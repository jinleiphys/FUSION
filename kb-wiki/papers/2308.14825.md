# Spacetime dynamics of chiral magnetic currents in a hot non-Abelian plasma

> The correlations of electric currents in hot non-Abelian plasma are responsible for the experimental manifestations of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions. We evaluate these correlations using holography, and show that they are driven by large-scale topological fluctuations. In a non-Abelian plasma with chiral fermions, local axial charge can be generated either by topological fluctuations (creating domains with nonzero Chern-Simons number) or by thermal fluctuations. Within holography, we investigate the dynamical creation of the axial charge and isolate the imprint of the topological dynamics on the spatial correlations of electric current. In particular, we show that the spatial extent of the current correlation is quite large ($\sim 1\ {\rm fm}$) and grows with time, which is consistent with sphaleronlike dynamics. We provide numerical estimates for this spatial size that can be used as an input in phenomenological analyses.

## Key claim

The paper uses holography to show that electric current correlations in a hot non-Abelian plasma are driven by large-scale topological fluctuations, and that the spatial extent of these correlations is approximately 1 fm and grows with time, consistent with sphaleron-like dynamics. The hedge is that this result applies specifically to strongly coupled plasmas and depends on the parameters chosen to match QCD conditions (T ≈ 300 MeV, B ≈ 1 m_π²).

## Method

The authors employ a holographic U(1)_A × U(1)_V Stückelberg model with a mixed Chern-Simons term to compute retarded two-point correlators of the electric current along the magnetic field direction. They numerically construct the magnetic black brane background geometry, solve linearized fluctuation equations using pseudospectral methods, and isolate topological contributions by subtracting correlators with the non-Abelian anomaly switched off (m_s = 0). The subtracted correlators are then inverse Fourier transformed to real space, and the spatial extent is characterized by the root mean square of the absolute value.

## Key numbers

- Spatial extent of current correlations at T = 300 MeV, B = 1 m_π², and time interval 𝒯 = 10 fm: x_⟂ = 1.25 fm and x_∥ = 1.94 fm (for m_sL = 0.04)
- Axial charge relaxation rate Γ/T decreases linearly in B/T² at large magnetic field for α = 6/19 and α = 2
- Chern-Simons diffusion rate at B ≈ 0.22 T² increases with m_sL for all α values tested (α = 0, 6/19, 2)
- Transverse size growth at early times: x_⟂ T = 0.12 + 0.46 √(𝒯 T); at late times: x_⟂ T = 4.46 + 0.29 √(𝒯 T)
- Longitudinal size growth at early times: x_∥ T = 0.027 + 0.75 √(𝒯 T); at late times: x_∥ T = 7.14 + 0.024 𝒯 T
- At large magnetic field, transverse size scales as x_⟂/𝒯 ∝ 1/√B (exponent 0.50)
- At small magnetic field, longitudinal size scales as x_∥/𝒯 ∝ B² (exponent 2.00)
- Chern-Simons coupling matched to QCD: α = 6/19 ≈ 0.316; κ_5² = 24π²/19 ≈ 12.5

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) by computing the spatial and temporal correlations of electric currents that determine the experimental observable cos(Δφ_α + Δφ_β) proposed by Voloshin (2010). It builds on prior holographic calculations of the sphaleron rate in magnetized plasma by Basar (2012) and real-time CME dynamics by Lin (2013), Ammon (2016), and Grieninger (2021, 2023). The results provide input for phenomenological models of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the major source of uncertainty has been the spatial and temporal distribution of the chirality imbalance. The paper also connects to lattice QCD results for the sphaleron rate in 3-flavor QCD by Bonanno (2023).
