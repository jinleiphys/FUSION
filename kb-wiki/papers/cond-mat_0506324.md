# Systematic Low-Energy Effective Theory for Magnons and Charge Carriers in an Antiferromagnet

> By electron or hole doping quantum antiferromagnets may turn into high-temperature superconductors. The low-energy dynamics of antiferromagnets are governed by their Nambu-Goldstone bosons -- the magnons -- and are described by an effective field theory analogous to chiral perturbation theory for the pions in strong interaction physics. In analogy to baryon chiral perturbation theory -- the effective theory for pions and nucleons -- we construct a systematic low-energy effective theory for magnons and electrons or holes in an antiferromagnet. The effective theory is universal and makes model-independent predictions for the entire class of antiferromagnetic cuprates. We present a detailed analysis of the symmetries of the Hubbard model and discuss how these symmetries manifest themselves in the effective theory. A complete set of linearly independent leading contributions to the effective action is constructed. The coupling to external electromagnetic fields is also investigated.

## Key claim
The paper constructs a systematic low-energy effective field theory for magnons and charge carriers (electrons or holes) in an antiferromagnet, analogous to baryon chiral perturbation theory in QCD. The hedge is that the theory is universal and model-independent for the entire class of antiferromagnetic cuprates, but its applicability requires that magnons remain among the lightest degrees of freedom even when antiferromagnetic order is destroyed.

## Method
The authors perform a detailed symmetry analysis of the Hubbard model, including its $SU(2)_s$ spin symmetry (spontaneously broken to $U(1)_s$), $U(1)_Q$ fermion number symmetry, $SU(2)_Q$ symmetry at half-filling, and discrete lattice symmetries. They then construct a non-linear realization of the spontaneously broken $SU(2)_s$ symmetry on magnon fields (described by a unit-vector field $\e(x)$ in the coset space $S^2$) and on charge carrier fields, organizing the effective Lagrangian by the number of derivatives and constructing a complete set of linearly independent leading contributions to the effective action.

## Key numbers
- For the antiferromagnetic Heisenberg model, the spin stiffness is $\rho_s = 0.186(4) J$ and the spin-wave velocity is $c = 1.68(1) J a$, where $J$ is the exchange coupling and $a$ is the lattice spacing.
- The minimum of the single-hole dispersion corresponds to lattice momenta $(\pm \frac{\pi}{2},\pm \frac{\pi}{2})$ in the Brillouin zone, as indicated by angle resolved photoemission spectroscopy experiments and theoretical investigations.
- The exchange coupling in the Hubbard model at large $U$ is given by $J = 2 t^2/U$ to second order in perturbation theory.

## Context
This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory, previously used for antiferromagnetic magnons by Neuberger (1989), Fisher (1989), and others, to include charge carriers in analogy to baryon chiral perturbation theory in QCD developed by Weinberg (1979), Gasser and Leutwyler (1985), and others. The effective theory enables systematic analytic calculations of magnon-mediated forces between holes, magnon-hole scattering, and potential mechanisms for Cooper pair preformation, which are inaccessible to Hubbard-type models due to the fermion sign problem away from half-filling.
