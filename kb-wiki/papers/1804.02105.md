# Neutrinoless $ββ$ decay mediated by the exchange of light and heavy neutrinos: The role of nuclear structure correlations

> Neutrinoless $ββ$ decay nuclear matrix elements calculated with the shell model and energy-density functional theory typically disagree by more than a factor of two in the standard scenario of light-neutrino exchange. In contrast, for a decay mediated by sterile heavy neutrinos the deviations are reduced to about 50\%, an uncertainty similar to the one due to short-range effects. We compare matrix elements in the light- and heavy-neutrino-exchange channels, exploring the radial, momentum transfer and angular momentum-parity matrix element distributions, and considering transitions that involve correlated and uncorrelated nuclear states. We argue that the shorter-range heavy-neutrino exchange is less sensitive to collective nuclear correlations, and that discrepancies in matrix elements are mostly due to the treatment of long-range correlations in many-body calculations. Our analysis supports previous studies suggesting that isoscalar pairing correlations, which affect mostly the longer-range part of the neutrinoless $ββ$ decay operator, are partially responsible for the differences between nuclear matrix elements in the standard light-neutrino-exchange mechanism.

# Neutrinoless $\beta\beta$ decay mediated by the exchange of light and heavy neutrinos: The role of nuclear structure correlations

## Key claim

The shorter-range heavy-neutrino-exchange mechanism is less sensitive to collective nuclear correlations than the longer-range light-neutrino-exchange mechanism, and the discrepancies between shell-model and energy-density functional theory matrix elements are mostly due to the unequal treatment of long-range correlations in many-body calculations. The analysis supports previous suggestions that isoscalar pairing correlations, which affect the longer-range part of the neutrinoless $\beta\beta$ decay operator, are partially responsible for the differences between nuclear matrix elements in the standard light-neutrino-exchange mechanism.

## Method

The author compares shell-model nuclear matrix elements to recently published results obtained with covariant energy-density functional (EDF) theory for both light- and heavy-neutrino-exchange mechanisms across multiple $\beta\beta$ decay emitters ($^{48}$Ca, $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{124}$Sn, $^{130}$Te, $^{136}$Xe). The analysis explores radial, momentum transfer, and angular momentum-parity matrix element distributions, and considers transitions involving both correlated and uncorrelated (seniority-zero) nuclear states, using a separable collective Hamiltonian to isolate individual nuclear structure correlations.

## Key numbers

- Shell-model and EDF theory matrix elements for light-neutrino exchange typically disagree by more than a factor of two
- For heavy-neutrino exchange, deviations between the two methods are reduced to about 50%
- Shell-model light-neutrino matrix elements ($M^{0\nu}$) range from 0.93 (Argonne-type) / 1.02 (CD-Bonn-type) for $^{48}$Ca to 2.89/3.07 for $^{76}$Ge, 2.73/2.90 for $^{82}$Se, 2.76/2.96 for $^{124}$Sn, 2.76/2.96 for $^{130}$Te, and 2.28/2.45 for $^{136}$Xe
- Shell-model heavy-neutrino matrix elements ($M^{0N}$) range from 48/73 for $^{48}$Ca to 130/188 for $^{76}$Ge, 121/175 for $^{82}$Se, 141/203 for $^{124}$Sn, 146/210 for $^{130}$Te, and 116/167 for $^{136}$Xe
- For uncorrelated (seniority-zero) states, differences between shell-model and EDF theory are less than 30% for light-neutrino exchange and only about 10% for heavy-neutrino exchange
- For $^{136}$Xe, the light-neutrino-exchange total matrix element is about 35% of the $0^+$-pair matrix element, while for heavy-neutrino exchange it is about 70% of the $0^+$-pair matrix element
- Heavy-neutrino exchange probes momentum transfers up to about 500 MeV, while light-neutrino exchange is most relevant below about 200 MeV
- Heavy-neutrino exchange only allows contributions from internucleon distances up to about 1 fm, while light-neutrino exchange involves distances of a couple of fm

## Context

This work extends comparisons between shell-model and EDF theory calculations for neutrinoless $\beta\beta$ decay, which previously showed large disagreements for the standard light-neutrino-exchange mechanism (Engel 2017). The study builds on recent calculations for heavy-neutrino-exchange matrix elements (Song 2017; Hyvarinen 2015; Horoi 2016) and earlier work on the role of isoscalar pairing correlations (Menendez 2016). The finding that matrix elements for different decay channels are roughly proportional across isotopes, first observed in QRPA calculations (Faessler 2011), suggests that identifying the underlying decay mechanism from half-life measurements alone will be challenging and requires more accurate matrix elements than currently available.
