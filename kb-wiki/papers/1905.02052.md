# First observation of the directed flow of $D^{0}$ and $\overline{D^0}$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200~GeV

> We report the first measurement of rapidity-odd directed flow ($v_{1}$) for $D^{0}$ and $\overline{D^{0}}$ mesons at mid-rapidity ($|y| < 0.8$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200\,GeV using the STAR detector at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider. In 10--80\% Au+Au collisions, the slope of the $v_{1}$ rapidity dependence ($dv_{1}/dy$), averaged over $D^{0}$ and $\overline{D^{0}}$ mesons, is -0.080 $\pm$ 0.017 (stat.) $\pm$ 0.016 (syst.) for transverse momentum $p_{\rm T}$ above 1.5~GeV/$c$. The absolute value of $D^0$-meson $dv_1/dy$ is about 25 times larger than that for charged kaons, with 3.4$σ$ significance. These data give a unique insight into the initial tilt of the produced matter, and offer constraints on the geometric and transport parameters of the hot QCD medium created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first measurement of rapidity-odd directed flow for D0 and anti-D0 mesons in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV, finding that the slope of the v1 rapidity dependence for charm mesons is about 25 times larger in absolute value than that for charged kaons. The measurement is limited to mid-rapidity (|y| < 0.8) and transverse momentum above 1.5 GeV/c, with the result averaged over D0 and anti-D0 mesons in 10-80% centrality collisions.

## Method
The STAR detector at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider was used to reconstruct D0 and anti-D0 mesons via their hadronic decay channels at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The directed flow v1 was extracted from the first harmonic Fourier coefficient of the azimuthal distribution relative to the event plane, and the slope dv1/dy was determined from the linear dependence of v1 on rapidity. The measurement covered the centrality range 10-80% and transverse momentum pT above 1.5 GeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The slope dv1/dy averaged over D0 and anti-D0 mesons is -0.080 +/- 0.017 (stat.) +/- 0.016 (syst.) for pT above 1.5 GeV/c in 10-80% Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- The absolute value of D0-meson dv1/dy is about 25 times larger than that for charged kaons, with 3.4 sigma significance

## Context
This measurement extends prior work on directed flow of light hadrons at RHIC energies, where a negative v1 slope or "anti-flow" was observed for charged hadrons near midrapidity. The result provides new constraints on the initial tilt of the produced QGP matter and on transport parameters for heavy quarks, complementing previous measurements of D0 meson elliptic flow (v2) and nuclear modification factors (RAA) from STAR and ALICE that have been used to constrain drag and diffusion coefficients in the QGP. The large magnitude of the charm directed flow relative to light hadrons offers a unique probe of the early-stage dynamics of the collision system.
