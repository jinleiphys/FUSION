# Restoration of the Broken D2-Symmetry in the Mean Field Description of Rotating Nuclei

> Signature effects observed in rotational bands are a consequence of an inherent D2-symmetry. This symmetry is naturally broken by the mean field cranking approximation when a tilted (non-principal) axis orientation of the nuclear spin becomes stable. The possible tunneling forth and back between the two symmetry-related minima in the double-humped potential-energy surface appears as a typical bifurcation of the rotational band. We describe this many-body process in which all nucleons participate by diagonalizing the nuclear Hamiltonian within a selected set of tilted and non-tilted cranking quasiparticle states. This microscopic approach is able to restore the broken D2 symmetry and reproduce the quantum fluctuations between symmetry- related HFB states which emerge as splitting of the band energies and in parallel staggering in intraband M1 transitions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the broken D2 (signature) symmetry in tilted-axis cranking mean-field descriptions of rotating nuclei can be restored by diagonalizing the nuclear Hamiltonian within a small set of four selected quasiparticle states (two tilted and two non-tilted), rather than requiring a large set of generator-coordinate states. This approach reproduces the experimentally observed signature splitting in band energies and B(M1) staggering, but the authors note their calculations are exploratory and do not precisely match interaction strengths or run quasiparticle states to self-consistent deformations.

## Method

The authors construct four Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) quasiparticle states from the tilted-axis cranking (TAC) Routhian: two symmetry-related tilted minima at angles ±θ₀, and two non-tilted principal-axis cranking (PAC) states at θ=0 corresponding to favored and unfavored signature. They rotate all intrinsic states to a common laboratory frame and diagonalize the full many-body Hamiltonian H' in this 4×4 non-orthogonal basis using recently developed techniques for overlaps and Hamiltonian kernels of non-orthogonal HFB states. The method is applied to the K=7/2 ground-state band in ¹⁷⁵Hf using a modified oscillator basis with proton shells N=3,4,5 and neutron shells N=4,5,6, with fixed deformation parameters ε₂=0.258, ε₄=0, γ=0, and pairing gaps Δₚ=0.75 MeV and Δₙ=0.69 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for the K=7/2 band in ¹⁷⁵Hf: ε₂=0.258, ε₄=0, γ=0
- Pairing gaps: Δₚ=0.75 MeV (protons), Δₙ=0.69 MeV (neutrons)
- Calculations performed for rotational frequencies ℏω = 0.1 to 0.3 MeV
- At ℏω = 0.3 MeV, the DISQ mixing produces a signature splitting reaching a few hundred keV, comparable to the observed splitting
- The PAC Routhian signature splitting is too small by about a factor of three compared to experiment
- The TAC Routhian shows no signature effect

## Context

This work extends the Tilted Cranking (TAC) model of Frauendorf (1993) by addressing its inability to describe signature splitting, which arises from the spontaneous breaking of D2 symmetry when the rotational axis becomes tilted. The authors contrast their approach with an earlier generator-coordinate method by Onishi (1985) that used up to 30 quasiparticle states along the lowest potential-energy sheet but gave unsatisfactory results. The DISQ method enables the description of the smooth transition from the tilted-axis regime at low frequency to the principal-axis regime at high frequency, and the authors suggest it could be applied to more complex cases such as odd-odd nuclei, triaxial nuclei, K-isomeric decay, and band-crossing phenomena.
