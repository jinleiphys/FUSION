# Distribution of Stress Tensor around Static Quark--Anti-Quark from Yang-Mills Gradient Flow

> The spatial distribution of the stress tensor around the quark--anti-quark ($Q\bar{Q}$) pair in SU(3) lattice gauge theory is studied. The Yang-Mills gradient flow plays a crucial role to make the stress tensor well-defined and derivable from the numerical simulations on the lattice. The resultant stress tensor with a decomposition into local principal axes shows, for the first time, the detailed structure of the flux tube along the longitudinal and transverse directions in a gauge invariant manner. The linear confining behavior of the $Q\bar{Q}$ potential at long distances is derived directly from the integral of the local stress tensor.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first gauge-invariant, three-dimensional spatial distribution of the stress tensor around a static quark–anti-quark pair in SU(3) Yang-Mills theory, showing that the linear confining potential at long distances can be derived directly from the integral of the local stress tensor, but only after taking both the continuum limit (a → 0) and the zero flow-time limit (t → 0) with careful control of over-smearing and under-smearing artifacts.

## Method

The authors used SU(3) lattice gauge theory with the Wilson gauge action on 48⁴ and 64⁴ lattices at five lattice spacings from 0.029 fm to 0.058 fm. They employed the Yang-Mills gradient flow to define a renormalized energy-momentum tensor operator, then measured its expectation value around a static Q\bar{Q} system using Wilson loops with APE smearing and multi-hit noise reduction. The double limit of zero lattice spacing and zero flow time was taken using an extrapolation formula with linear terms in a² and t.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacings: a = 0.058, 0.046, 0.043, 0.038, and 0.029 fm, determined from the w₀-scale
- Q\bar{Q} distances studied: R = 0.46, 0.69, and 0.92 fm
- Flow time window used for analysis: 2 ≤ t/ā² ≤ 5, where ā = 0.029 fm, corresponding to flow radii ρ between 2ā ≈ 0.058 fm and √10 ā ≈ 0.092 fm
- Ground state saturation of the Wilson loop: |1 − P(R,T)| < 0.5% for T > 0.19 fm at a = 0.038 fm with N_APE = 160
- Temporal extent of Wilson loops: T = 0.46 fm for all R values
- Transverse radius of the flux tube: approximately 0.2 fm, with indication of increase for larger R (not quantified due to limited statistics)
- The stress tensor components at r=0 on the mid-plane show approximate degeneracy: ⟨T^R_44⟩ ≈ ⟨T^R_zz⟩ < 0 and ⟨T^R_rr⟩ ≈ ⟨T^R_θθ⟩ > 0

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the flux tube using action density, color electric fields, and plaquettes (DiGiacomo 1990, Bali 1994, Michael 1995, Green 1996, Gliozzi 2010, Meyer 2010, Cea 2012, Cardoso 2013) by providing the first gauge-invariant stress tensor distribution in three spatial dimensions. The Yang-Mills gradient flow method, developed by Narayanan and Neuberger (2006) and Luscher (2010), was previously applied to the equation of state of SU(3) Yang-Mills theory at finite temperature (Asakawa 2013, Kitazawa 2016, Kamata 2016, Kitazawa 2017); this work extends that formalism to the static Q\bar{Q} system. The results enable future studies of the three-quark (QQQ) system, the Q\bar{Q} system at finite temperature, and generalization to full QCD with dynamical quarks.
