# Combined analysis of eta meson hadro- and photo-production off nucleons

> The $η$-meson production in photon- and hadron-induced reactions, namely, $γp \to p η$, $π^- p \to n η$, $pp \to ppη$, and $pn \to pnη$, are investigated in a combined analysis in order to learn about the relevant production mechanisms and the possible role of nucleon resonances in these reactions. We consider the nucleonic, mesonic, and nucleon resonance currents constructed within an effective Lagrangian approach and compare the results with the available data for cross sections and spin asymmetries for these reactions. We found that the reaction $γp \to p η$ could be described well with the inclusion of the well-established $S_{11}(1535)$, $S_{11}(1650)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, and $D_{13}(1700)$ resonances, in addition to the mesonic current. Consideration of other well-established resonances in the same mass region, including the spin-5/2 resonances, $D_{15}(1675)$ and $F_{15}(1680)$, does not further improve the results qualitatively. For the reaction $π^- p \to n η$, the $P_{13}(1720)$ resonance is found to be important for reproducing the structure observed in the differential cross section data. Our model also improves the description of the reaction $NN \to NNη$ to a large extent compared to the earlier results by Nakayama \textit{et al.} [Phys.\ Rev.\ C \textbf{68}, 045201 (2003)]. For this reaction, we address two cases where either the $S_{11}(1535)$ or the $D_{13}$ dominates. Further improvement in the description of these reactions and the difficulty to uniquely determine the nucleon resonance parameters in the present type of analysis are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a combined analysis of $\eta$-meson production in photon- and hadron-induced reactions can describe the available data reasonably well, but the extracted nucleon resonance parameters are not uniquely determined and depend on the specific set of resonances included and the starting values used in the fitting procedure.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective Lagrangian model including nucleonic, mesonic, and nucleon resonance currents for the reactions $\gamma p \to p \eta$, $\pi^- p \to n \eta$, $pp \to pp\eta$, and $pn \to pn\eta$. The photoproduction amplitude was calculated with a generalized contact term to ensure gauge invariance, the $\pi N \to N \eta$ reaction was treated at tree level, and the $NN \to NN\eta$ process was calculated in the distorted-wave Born approximation with both initial-state and final-state interactions included using the Paris potential. Four different models (A, B, C, D) were considered, each containing different combinations of nucleon resonances with parameters obtained by global fitting to available cross section and spin asymmetry data.

## Key numbers

- The $S_{11}(1535)$ resonance mass was fitted to 1540 MeV in model (A) and 1527 MeV in model (B), compared to the PDG range of 1525–1545 MeV
- The $S_{11}(1535)$ branching ratio to $N\eta$ was extracted as 65% in model (A), compared to the PDG range of 45–60%
- The $S_{11}(1650)$ branching ratio to $N\eta$ was extracted as 19% in model (A), compared to the PDG range of 3–10%
- The $D_{13}(1520)$ branching ratio to $N\eta$ was extracted as 0.03% in model (A), compared to the PDG value of $0.23 \pm 0.04$%
- The $NN\eta$ coupling constant $g_{NN\eta}$ was fitted as 0.007 in model (A) and 0.088 in model (B)
- The cutoff parameter $\Lambda^*_v$ at the electromagnetic vector-meson exchange vertex was fitted to 1168 MeV in model (A) and 1202 MeV in model (B)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Nakayama et al. (2003) on $NN \to NN\eta$ production and the $\eta^\prime$-meson production study by Nakayama and Haberzettl (2004-2005), now performing a combined analysis of four reactions simultaneously. The approach is similar in spirit to the coupled-channels analyses of the Bonn-Gatchina-Giessen group and the $\eta$-MAID isobar model, but differs by including hadronic reactions $NN \to NN\eta$ alongside photoproduction to impose stricter constraints on resonance-nucleon-meson coupling strengths. The difficulty in uniquely determining resonance parameters motivates future coupled-channels calculations that include final-state interactions explicitly.
