# $η$ mesons in hot magnetized nuclear matter

> The $ηN$ interactions are investigated in the hot magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter using chiral SU(3) model and chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). In the chiral model, the in-medium properties of $η$-meson are calculated by the medium modified scalar densities under the influence of an external magnetic field. Further, in the combined approach of chiral model and ChPT, off-shell contributions of $ηN$ interactions are evaluated from the ChPT effective $ηN$ Lagrangian, and the in-medium effect of scalar densities are incorporated from the chiral SU(3) model. We observe a significant effect of magnetic field on the in-medium mass and optical potential of $η$ meson. We observe a deeper mass-shift in the combined approach of ChPT and chiral model compared to the effect of solo chiral SU(3) model. In both approaches, no additional mass-shift is observed due to the uncharged nature of $η$ mesons in the presence of magnetic field.

## Key claim

The authors investigate the in-medium mass and optical potential of the η meson in hot, magnetized, asymmetric nuclear matter using two approaches: the chiral SU(3) model alone and a combined approach of chiral SU(3) model with chiral perturbation theory (ChPT). The abstract's claim of a "significant effect of magnetic field" is qualified by the finding that no additional mass-shift arises from the magnetic field due to the uncharged nature of the η meson, meaning the magnetic field's influence enters only indirectly through modifications to the nucleon scalar densities.

## Method

The in-medium properties of the η meson were calculated by solving the dispersion relation derived from the ηN interaction Lagrangian, with the medium-modified scalar densities of protons and neutrons computed from the chiral SU(3) model under an external magnetic field along the Z-axis. In the combined approach, off-shell contributions from the ChPT effective ηN Lagrangian were included, with the scalar densities still taken from the chiral SU(3) model. The scalar and vector densities for protons incorporated Landau quantization due to the magnetic field, while neutron densities were computed without Landau quantization.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum mass of the η meson is calculated as 574.374 MeV, which is within 4.9% of the experimental mass of 547.862 MeV.
- At nuclear saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, the combined approach (chiral SU(3) model + ChPT) yields a mass-shift range of (-54.61) to -116.83 MeV for a scattering length a^{ηN} = 1.02 fm in non-magnetized nuclear matter.
- The magnetic field strengths considered are eB ∼ 15 m²_π (∼5×10¹⁹ gauss) at LHC and eB ∼ 2 m²_π (∼6.5×10¹⁸ gauss) at RHIC.
- The parameter d in the chiral model is set to 0.064, fitted to reproduce vacuum values of meson fields and nucleon masses.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of η mesons in non-magnetized nuclear matter (Kumar 2020c) by incorporating the effect of a strong external magnetic field, motivated by the transient magnetic fields produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC. The study builds on prior theoretical work using chiral SU(3) model and ChPT for meson-baryon interactions, including the work of Zhong (2006) who obtained an optical potential of -83 ± 5 MeV at saturation density using ChPT and relativistic mean-field model, and the coupled channel approach of Chiang (1991) who predicted an optical potential of -34 MeV at ρ₀. The results enable further exploration of η-nucleon bound state formation and η-mesic atoms in magnetized environments relevant to future experiments at CBM, PANDA, J-PARC, and NICA.
