# Renormalizing the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the one pion exchange potential

> We address the question whether the cut-off dependence, which has to be introduced in order to properly define the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the one pion exchange potential plus local (delta-function) potentials, can be removed (up to inverse powers of it) by a suitable tuning of the various (bare) coupling constants. We prove that this is indeed so both for the spin singlet and for the spin triplet channels. However, the latter requires such a strong cut-off dependence of the coupling constant associated to the non-local term which breaks orbital angular momentum conservation, that the renormalized amplitude lacks from partial wave mixing. We argue that this is an indication that this term must be treated perturbatively.

## Key claim

The cut-off dependence introduced to define the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the one pion exchange potential plus local delta-function potentials can be removed by tuning bare coupling constants in both the spin singlet and spin triplet channels, but the triplet channel requires such a strong cut-off dependence of the coupling constant associated to the non-local term that the renormalized amplitude loses partial wave mixing, indicating this term must be treated perturbatively.

## Method

The authors solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the leading-order nucleon-nucleon potential from chiral perturbation theory, which consists of one pion exchange plus local contact terms, using both hard cut-off and dimensional regularization. They analyzed the isovector-singlet and isosinglet-triplet channels separately, allowing the bare coupling constants of both local and non-local terms to have non-trivial cut-off dependence, and examined whether the resulting scattering amplitudes remained finite as the regulator was removed.

## Key numbers

- The inverse of the bare coupling constant \(c_0\) in the isovector-singlet channel renormalizes as \(1/c_0 = -M\Lambda/(2\pi^2) + (M^2 c_2)/(32\pi^2) \log(\Lambda^2/\mu^2) + 1/c_0^r(\mu)\) for a hard cut-off
- In dimensional regularization (D=3+2\(\epsilon\)), the renormalization gives \(1/c_0 = -M^2 c_2/(4(4\pi)^2)(1/\epsilon + \chi_{sch}) + 1/c_0^r(\mu)\)
- The renormalized coupling constant evolves as \(c_0^r(\mu) = c_0^r(\mu_0)/[1 + (M^2 c_2 c_0^r(\mu_0))/(16\pi^2) \log(\mu/\mu_0)]\)
- Using \(c_0^r(m_\pi) = -(1/79\ \text{MeV})^2\) gives \(c_0^r(M) = -(1/125\ \text{MeV})^2\)

## Context

This work extends the renormalization program for the Lippmann-Schwinger equation initiated by Kaplan (1996) and further developed by Bedaque (1999) and Frederico (2000), who addressed the spin singlet channel but left the spin triplet channel with unsatisfactory results. The authors argue that the standard assumption that only local terms should renormalize the LS equation (Lepage, 1997) is too restrictive, and their analysis of the triplet channel leads to a new proposal for counting rules where the spin symmetry breaking term must be treated perturbatively rather than non-perturbatively at leading order.
