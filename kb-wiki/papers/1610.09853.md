# Delayed versus accelerated quarkonium formation in a magnetic field

> Formation time of heavy quarkonia in a homogeneous magnetic field is analyzed by using a phenomenological ansatz of the vector current correlator. Because the existence of a magnetic field mixes vector quarkonia ($J/ψ$, $ψ^\prime$) and their pseudoscalar partners ($η_c$, $η_c^\prime$), the properties of the quarkonia can be modified through such a spin mixing. This means that the formation time of quarkonia is also changed by the magnetic field. We show the formation time of vector quarkonia is delayed by an idealized constant magnetic field, where the formation time of the excited state becomes longer than that of the ground state. As a more realistic situation in heavy-ion collisions, effects by a time-dependent magnetic field are also discussed, where delayed formation of $J/ψ$ and $ψ^\prime$ and very early formation of $η_c$ and $η_c^\prime$ are found.

# Delayed versus accelerated quarkonium formation in a magnetic field

## Key claim
The formation time of vector quarkonia (J/ψ, ψ′) is delayed by a constant magnetic field, while the formation time of their pseudoscalar partners (ηc, ηc′) is accelerated; however, these results depend on the specific ansatz for the spectral function and on the assumption that the continuum threshold is unchanged by the magnetic field.

## Method
The authors use a phenomenological ansatz for the vector current correlator in the space-time domain, decomposing it into pole contributions (J/ψ, ψ′, ηc, ηc′) plus a continuum. The magnetic field modifies the spectral function through spin mixing between vector and pseudoscalar states, with masses and mixing angles obtained from an effective Lagrangian approach using experimental radiative decay widths. Formation times are computed as the expectation value of the distribution derived from the fraction of each state in the correlator, both for constant and time-dependent magnetic fields.

## Key numbers
- In vacuum, formation times are ⟨τ_form⟩_J/ψ = 0.407 fm/c and ⟨τ_form⟩_ψ′ = 0.971 fm/c.
- In a time-dependent magnetic field at RHIC (Au+Au, √s = 200 GeV, b = 10 fm, eB_max ≈ 0.1 GeV²), formation times become ⟨τ_form⟩_ηc = 0.086 fm/c, ⟨τ_form⟩_J/ψ = 0.408 fm/c, ⟨τ_form⟩_ηc′ = 0.095 fm/c, and ⟨τ_form⟩_ψ′ = 0.980 fm/c.
- The maximal magnetic field perpendicular to the reaction plane is eB_max ∼ 5 m_π² ∼ 0.1 GeV², with lifetime parameters τ₀ = 0.05 fm/c and τ_B = 0.065 fm/c.
- The normalized fractions of pseudoscalar charmonia from the vector current reach saturation values of 0.01% for ηc and 0.2% for ηc′.
- The dimensionless coupling constants for spin mixing are g_{γ ηc J/ψ} = 2.0877 and g_{γ ηc′ ψ′} = 3.3762.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Kharzeev (1999) for quarkonium formation time from the space-time correlator, previously applied to finite temperature by Song (2013) and time-dependent temperature by Song (2015). It builds on mass spectroscopy studies in magnetic fields from potential models (Alford 2013, Bonati 2015, Suzuki 2016, Yoshida 2016) and effective Lagrangian approaches (Cho 2014, Yoshida 2016). The results enable quantitative estimates of magnetically-induced ηc and ηc′ production in dilepton spectra as probes of the early magnetic field in heavy-ion collisions, and can be combined with thermal effects from Song (2013, 2015) to study J/ψ suppression and heavy flavor flow under magnetic fields.
