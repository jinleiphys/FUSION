# Structure and EM form factors of purely relativistic systems

> The Bethe-Salpeter equation for two massive scalar particles interacting by scalar massless exchange has solutions of two types, which differ from each other by their behavior in the non-relativistic limit: the normal solutions which turn into the Coulomb ones and the "abnormal" solutions. The latter ones have no non-relativistic counterparts and disappear in the non-relativistic limit. We studied the composition of all these states. It turns out that the normal states, even for large binding energy, are dominated by two massive particles. Whereas, the contribution of the two-body sector into the abnormal states, even for small binding energy, is of the order of 1% only; they are dominated by an indefinite number of the massless particles. The elastic electromagnetic form factors for both normal and abnormal states, as well as the transition ones between them, are calculated.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Salpeter equation for two massive scalar particles with massless scalar exchange produces "abnormal" solutions that have no non-relativistic counterpart; these states are dominated by many-body contributions from exchanged massless particles rather than by the two constituent particles, even at very small binding energies.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the Wick-Cutkosky model using the Nakanishi integral representation, obtaining the weight function g(z) from a differential equation for states labeled by quantum numbers n and k. They computed the two-body Fock-sector contribution N₂ to the total norm by relating the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude to the light-front wavefunction ψ₂, and calculated elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors from the weight function g(z) for normal (k=0) and abnormal (k=2,4) states.

## Key numbers

- For the normal state (k=0) at α=0.02, binding energy B = 10⁻⁴ and two-body contribution N₂ = 0.992
- For the normal state (k=0) at α=5, binding energy B = 0.999 and two-body contribution N₂ = 0.651
- For the abnormal state (k=2) at α=2, binding energy B = 1.220 × 10⁻⁵ and two-body contribution N₂ = 7.7 × 10⁻³ (0.77%)
- For the abnormal state (k=2) at α=5, binding energy B = 3.512 × 10⁻³ and two-body contribution N₂ = 9.35 × 10⁻² (9.35%)
- For the abnormal state (k=4) at α=5, binding energy B = 0.217 × 10⁻⁴ and two-body contribution N₂ = 8.55 × 10⁻³ (0.855%)
- For the normal ground state at large coupling (α → 2π), N₂ tends to 9/14 ≈ 64%

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Wick-Cutkosky model (Wick 1954, Cutkosky 1954) and the earlier study by Dorkin et al. (2014) of two-body contributions in normal states, now applied to abnormal solutions first identified by Wick and Cutkosky. The results explain why abnormal states disappear in the non-relativistic limit: they are dominated by many-body Fock sectors involving exchanged massless particles, not by the two constituent particles. The finding that elastic form factors of abnormal states decrease roughly 1000 times faster than those of normal states, and that transition form factors between normal and abnormal states are orders of magnitude smaller than those between abnormal states, suggests these two classes of states represent weakly coupled "worlds" that barely communicate electromagnetically.
