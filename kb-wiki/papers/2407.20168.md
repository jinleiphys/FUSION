# Universal corrections to the superfluid gap in a cold Fermi gas

> A framework for computing the superfluid gap in an effective field theory (EFT) of fermions interacting via momentum-independent contact forces is developed. The leading universal corrections in the EFT are one-loop in-medium effects at the Fermi surface, and reproduce the well-known Gor'kov-Melik-Barkhudarov result. The complete subleading universal corrections are presented here, and include one-loop effects away from the Fermi surface, two-loop in-medium effects, as well as modifications to the fermion propagator. Together, these effects are found to reduce the gap at low densities. Applications to neutron superfluidity in neutron stars are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) framework for computing the superfluid gap in a cold Fermi gas with momentum-independent contact interactions, and presents the complete subleading universal corrections beyond the Gor'kov-Melik-Barkhudarov (GM) result. The authors find that these corrections reduce the gap at low densities, but the result is conditional on the expansion parameter \(k_F a\) being small, and the error band on the NNLO prediction is estimated assuming a next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^3\)LO) correction with a coefficient of magnitude 1.

## Method

The authors formulate an EFT for fermions interacting via momentum-independent contact forces, using dimensional regularization with the power-divergence subtraction (PDS) scheme and the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme. The superfluid gap is extracted from the singularity analysis of the in-medium four-point correlation function, computed order-by-order in perturbation theory with a power counting scheme that tracks powers of \(\lambda = 2 k_F a / \pi\) arising from BCS singularities in particle-particle loops. The leading order (LO) corresponds to \(c_{-1}\), the next-to-leading order (NLO) to \(c_0\), and the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) to \(c_1\) in the expansion of \(\log(\Delta / \omega_{k_F})\).

## Key numbers

* The GM suppression factor relative to BCS is \(\Delta_{\rm GM} = (4e)^{-1/3} \Delta_{\rm BCS} = 0.45138\ldots \times \Delta_{\rm BCS}\).
* The NNLO gap is \(\Delta = \Delta_{\rm GM} \left(1 + \frac{0.95238(40)}{\pi} k_F a + \mathcal{O}[(k_F a)^2]\right)\), where the coefficient \(0.95238(40)\) has an uncertainty from Monte Carlo integration.
* The expansion coefficients for \(\log(\Delta / \omega_{k_F})\) are \(c_{-1} = 1\), \(c_0 = \frac{7}{3}(\log 2 - 1) = -0.71599\ldots\), and \(c_1 = 0.47619(20)\).
* The effective mass and wavefunction renormalization are \(M^{\star}/M = 1 + \lambda^2 \frac{2}{15}(7\log 2 - 1) + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^3)\) and \(Z = 1 - \lambda^2 \log 2 + \mathcal{O}(\lambda^3)\).
* For neutron-neutron scattering, the scattering length is \(a^{-1} \approx -10.7\) MeV, and perturbation theory is expected to break down around \(k_F \approx 17\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the classic Gor'kov-Melik-Barkhudarov (GM) result (Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov, 1961) by computing the complete subleading universal corrections to the s-wave superfluid gap, which had not been fully calculated previously. The results are compared to Quantum Monte Carlo (QMC) simulations by Gezerlis and Carlson (2007), and the NNLO prediction shows improved agreement with QMC at \(k_F a = -1\) compared to the GM result. The framework enables future calculations of N\(^3\)LO corrections and can be extended to include effective range and other momentum-dependent modifications relevant for neutron superfluidity in neutron stars.
