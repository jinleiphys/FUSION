# Probing the densities of the Sn isotopes

> Proton and neutron densities have been obtained for the even-even isotopes of Sn from $^{100}$Sn to $^{176}$Sn using a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model with a Skyrme interaction. The matter densities so defined have been used with realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions in a folding model to specify optical potentials for the elastic scattering of protons with energies in the range 40 to 200 MeV. Those potentials have been used to make predictions of the differential cross sections and spin observables for proton scattering. As the target mass increases, the emergence of the neutron skin in the Sn isotopes is revealed by marked effects in the differential cross section. Comparisons with available data show how similar scattering data for the neutron-rich isotopes may provide constraints for the model structures.

## Key claim

Proton and neutron densities for even-even Sn isotopes from $^{100}$Sn to $^{176}$Sn, calculated using a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model with Skyrme interactions, predict that the emergence of a neutron skin as target mass increases produces marked effects in differential cross sections for elastic proton scattering at 40 to 200 MeV, but the authors note that only access to actual scattering data from neutron-rich isotopes will allow critique of the specific mean-field structures.

## Method

The authors used a spherical mean-field Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model with two Skyrme force parameterizations (SkP and SLy4) to generate proton and neutron densities and single-particle wave functions for all even-mass Sn isotopes from $^{100}$Sn to $^{176}$Sn. These densities were folded with realistic nucleon-nucleon effective interactions derived from BonnB $g$ matrices to construct microscopic optical potentials, and the resulting nonlocal Schrodinger equations were solved directly using the DWBA98 program to predict differential cross sections and spin observables for elastic proton scattering at 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The neutron skin thickness, defined as $R_{rms}(\text{neutron}) - R_{rms}(\text{proton})$, varies from 0 fm in $^{100}$Sn to approximately 0.5 fm in $^{170}$Sn.
- The half-density radius for protons ranges from approximately 5 fm in $^{100}$Sn to approximately 6 fm in $^{170}$Sn.
- The half-density radius for neutrons ranges from approximately 5 fm in $^{100}$Sn to approximately 6.5 fm in $^{170}$Sn.
- The central neutron density increases from approximately 0.08 neutrons/fm$^3$ in $^{100}$Sn to approximately 0.1 neutrons/fm$^3$ in $^{170}$Sn.
- The total reaction cross section for 200 MeV protons on Sn isotopes increases from approximately 1 barn to 1.63 barn for $^{170}$Sn.
- The proton central density decreases by approximately 40% from its value in $^{100}$Sn to $^{170}$Sn, while the neutron central density increases by approximately 25%.
- The distance over which the charge density falls from 90% to 10% of its central value is approximately 2 fm in all Sn isotopes studied.
- The pairing-force strengths used were $V_0 = -286.20$ MeV fm$^{-3}$ for SkP and $V_0 = -212.94$ MeV fm$^{-3}$ for SLy4, adjusted to the neutron pairing gap in $^{120}$Sn.
- The heaviest two-neutron-bound isotope is predicted to be mass 172 for SkP and mass 174 for SLy4.

## Context

This work extends previous mean-field studies of Sn isotopes (Mizutori et al., 2000) to a wider mass range from $^{100}$Sn to $^{176}$Sn, filling gaps in earlier calculations, and builds on the method of Karataglidis et al. (2002) which showed that 200 MeV proton scattering from $^{208}$Pb could extract a neutron skin thickness of approximately 0.17 fm and discriminate between model structures with identical root-mean-square radii. The predictions enable future experiments with radioactive ion beams at facilities such as TRIUMF/ISAC, NSCL, RIKEN, and CERN/ISOLDE to test the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model structures by comparing measured differential cross sections and total reaction cross sections for neutron-rich Sn isotopes against the calculated results.
