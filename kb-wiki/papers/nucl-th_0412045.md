# Nucleon correlations and Higher Twist effects in nuclear structure functions

> The overlap of the nucleons in nuclei plays an important role in understanding the nuclear dependence of deep inelastic scattering data. It is shown that the nuclear modification of the higher twist scale can be essentially determined by the overlapping volume per nucleon and this effect gives a large contribution to nuclear shadowing for small $x$ and low $Q^2$. In this kinematical region there is also a moderate enhancement of the longitudinal structure function in nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that higher twist (HT) contributions to nuclear shadowing in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) are significant at small $x$ and low $Q^2$, and that these contributions can be largely determined by the overlapping volume per nucleon in the nucleus, but the magnitude of the effect depends on the initial parametrization of the leading twist (LT) structure functions.

## Method

The author parametrizes the higher twist contribution to the nuclear structure function $F_{2A}$ per nucleon using an effective volume $V_A$ (reduced from the free nucleon volume $V_N$ by the overlapping volume per nucleon $V^{op}_A$) and an effective nucleon mass $M^*$ that accounts for binding energy. The nuclear HT correction is expressed as $F_{2A}^{HT} = F_{2D}^{HT} \Sigma_A$, where $\Sigma_A$ depends on the ratio of volumes and a single free parameter $\gamma$ that accounts for additional HT contributions from partons in different nucleons. The model is compared to NMC data on ratios of nuclear structure functions ($F_{2C}/F_{2D}$, $F_{2Ca}/F_{2D}$, $F_{2Pb}/F_{2C}$, $F_{2Sn}/F_{2C}$) using the leading twist results from the Gribov approach of Frankfurt and Strikman (2004).

## Key numbers

- The HT contribution to nuclear shadowing is about 50% of the whole suppression in the Gribov approach of Frankfurt and Strikman (2004).
- The phenomenological parametrization of the deuteron HT term is $F_{2D}^{HT} \simeq - (c_1 x + c_2 x^2)/Q^2$ with $c_1 = 1.509 \text{ GeV}^2$ and $c_2 = -8.553 \text{ GeV}^2$, valid in the small $x$ region.
- The overlapping volume per nucleon for different nuclei is taken as 65% of the values obtained by Close, Roberts, and Ross (1985) using a Reid-soft core potential.
- The effective HT nitrogen scale at $x \simeq 0.0045$ is much smaller than the value of $0.056 \text{ GeV}^2$ required to obtain the reported large enhancement of the longitudinal structure function.
- The leading twist result for $F_{2Sn}/F_{2C}$ at $x=0.0125$ is given as 0.975.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Close, Roberts, and Ross (1985) which applied the idea of nucleon overlap and $Q^2$-rescaling to the EMC effect, and also extends the author's own earlier work on nuclear shadowing. The paper directly addresses the debate between Frankfurt and Strikman (2004), who find large HT contributions (about 50% of shadowing), and Armesto, Capella, and others (2003), who report small HT corrections. The analysis suggests that a conclusive answer requires updated phenomenological fits including $O(1/Q^2)$ corrections, QCD evolution, and data on the longitudinal structure function, and it points toward a possible geometrical critical transition (percolation) in heavy ion collisions at high energy and density.
