# The spin-flip-induced quadrupole resonance in odd-$A$ exotic atoms

> We examine the possible existence of quadrupole resonances in exotic atoms containing odd-$A$ nuclei. We find that the spin-flip of the de-exciting exotic particle can induce a resonance, altering the orbital angular momentum of the nucleus by one quanta. This process results in an excited nucleus with a suppression in x-ray photon emissions during the exotic atom cascade. We provide specific cases of antiprotonic atoms of stable elements where this resonance effect is expected to occur. The study of this phenomena may provide insight into the strong interaction of deeply bound antiprotonic states in the proximity of the unpaired nucleon, and serve as a tool for probing short-lived excited nuclear states.

# The spin-flip-induced quadrupole resonance in odd-$A$ exotic atoms

## Key claim

The authors propose that in odd-$A$ exotic atoms, a quadrupole resonance can occur when the de-exciting exotic particle undergoes a spin-flip transition that changes its orbital angular momentum by two quanta, inducing a nuclear excitation with spin change of one quanta. This effect is predicted to be observable in specific antiprotonic atoms of stable odd-$A$ nuclei, provided the energy difference between antiproton states closely matches the nuclear excitation energy to within a few percent of the coupling matrix element.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian for the combined system of a deformed nucleus and an orbiting antiproton, consisting of separate nuclear and antiproton Hamiltonians plus a quadrupole interaction term. They use the Bohr-Mottelson collective rotational model for the odd-$A$ nucleus and Dirac spinor wavefunctions for the antiproton in a spherically symmetric Coulomb potential. The quadrupole interaction matrix elements are evaluated in a basis of combined states with well-defined total angular momentum, and the authors identify selection rules that allow spin-flip transitions ($\Delta j = -1$) of the antiproton coupled to $\Delta J = 1$ nuclear excitations, which are forbidden in even-$A$ systems.

## Context

This work extends the study of E2 nuclear resonance effects in exotic atoms, previously considered only for collective quadrupole excitations in even-$A$ nuclei (Leon, 1976; Batty, 1978; Leon, 1979; Reidy, 1985; Kanert, 1986), to odd-$A$ systems where single-particle excitations with $\Delta J = 1$ become possible. The spin-flip mechanism builds on earlier work by Karpeshin (1998) on spin-flip induced nuclear excitations in heavy odd-$A$ highly charged ions, but here the orbiting particle also changes its orbital angular momentum and principal quantum number, releasing much larger energies that can match nuclear gamma transitions. The predicted resonance effect could enable studies of strong interaction shifts and widths of deeply bound antiprotonic states in the proximity of unpaired nucleons, and serve as a complementary tool for determining the spin and quadrupole moment of short-lived excited nuclear states, particularly for isotopes where the excited-state spin assignments remain tentative (e.g., $^{101}$Ru, $^{123}$Sb, $^{165}$Ho).
