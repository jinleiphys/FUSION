# The Tetraquark Candidate Zc(3900) from Dynamical Lattice QCD Simulations

> The structure of the tetraquark candidate $Z_{c}(3900)$, which was experimentally reported in $e^+ e^-$ collisions, is studied by the s-wave meson-meson coupled-channel scattering on the lattice. The s-wave interactions among the $πJ/ψ$, $ρη_{c}$ and $D \bar{D}^{*}$ channels are derived from (2+1)-flavor dynamical QCD simulations at $m_π=410$--$700$ MeV. It is found that the interactions are dominated by the off-diagonal $πJ/ψ$-$D \bar{D}^{*}$ and $ρη_{c}$-$D \bar{D}^{*}$ couplings. With the interactions obtained, the s-wave two-body amplitudes and the pole position in the $πJ/ψ$-$ρη_{c}$-$D \bar{D}^{*}$ coupled-channel scattering are calculated. The results show that the $Z_{c}(3900)$ is not a conventional resonance but a threshold cusp. A semiphenomenological analysis with the coupled-channel interaction to the experimentally observed decay mode is also presented to confirm the conclusion.

## Key claim

The Zc(3900) is not a conventional resonance but a threshold cusp induced by the opening of the s-wave D Dbar* threshold, with the peak structure in the pi J/psi spectrum arising from the off-diagonal pi J/psi-D Dbar* coupling rather than from a genuine pole in the scattering amplitude.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor dynamical lattice QCD simulations at three pion masses (m_pi = 411, 570, 701 MeV) on a 32^3 x 64 lattice using the PACS-CS gauge configurations. They extracted s-wave coupled-channel potentials among the pi J/psi, rho eta_c, and D Dbar* channels using the time-dependent HAL QCD method from equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave functions. These potentials were then used as input to the Lippmann-Schwinger equation to compute two-body scattering amplitudes and search for pole positions in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses used: 411(1) MeV (case I, 450 configurations), 570(1) MeV (case II, 400 configurations), 701(1) MeV (case III, 399 configurations)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.0907(13) fm, spatial volume L^3 approx (2.9 fm)^3
- D meson mass: 1903(1) MeV (case I), 1947(1) MeV (case II), 2000(1) MeV (case III)
- Dbar* meson mass: 2056(3) MeV (case I), 2101(2) MeV (case II), 2159(2) MeV (case III)
- J/psi mass: 3097(1) MeV (case I), 3118(1) MeV (case II), 3143(1) MeV (case III)
- The pole position found in the complex energy plane is located far from the physical region relevant to the Zc(3900)

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method to coupled-channel systems, following earlier developments by Ishii (2007), Aoki (2010, 2011, 2012), and Sasaki (2015). It contradicts phenomenological models that interpret the Zc(3900) as a hadro-charmonium state, compact tetraquark, or D Dbar* molecule (e.g., Voloshin, Cleven), and instead supports kinematical threshold effect models (e.g., Matsuki, Swanson). The results are consistent with earlier lattice QCD studies using standard temporal correlation methods (Sasa, Lee, Chen 2014) that found no candidate for the Zc(3900) eigenstate. This work enables future studies of three-body decays such as Y(4260) to pi pi J/psi and pi D Dbar* using the scattering amplitudes obtained from lattice QCD.
