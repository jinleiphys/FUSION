# Hard Gammas from Low-Energy Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Single-particle radiative mechanism of $γ$ emission embedded into the pre-equilibrium exciton model is used to calculate the $γ$ emission from a decay of $^{160}$Er$^{*}$ created in two different ways. The initial stage of a reaction is described using momentum-space overlaps of colliding nuclei. We can reproduce the main features of the observed $γ$ energy spectra including the fact that the spectrum observed in more symmetric combination is slightly harder than in the other case.

## Key claim

The pre-equilibrium exciton model with a single-particle radiative mechanism can reproduce the main features of observed gamma energy spectra from decay of the same compound nucleus formed by two different projectile-target combinations, including the counterintuitive result that the spectrum from the more symmetric (Ni-induced) reaction is slightly harder than from the asymmetric (O-induced) case, but only when the initial exciton number is calculated from full momentum-space overlaps rather than from empirical systematics.

## Method

Gamma emission from the decay of \(^{160}\)Er\(^{*}\) at excitation energy \(E=53\) MeV, created by \(^{16}\)O + \(^{144}\)Nd and by \(^{64}\)Ni + \(^{96}\)Zr, was calculated using the pre-equilibrium exciton model with single-particle radiative mechanism. The initial exciton number \(n_0\) was determined from momentum-space overlaps of the colliding nuclei, yielding \(n_0=7\) for the O-induced reaction and \(n_0=5\) for the Ni-induced reaction. The updated code PEQAG solved the master equations coupling different nuclei and excitation energies to obtain the total gamma production spectra.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy of the compound system \(^{160}\)Er\(^{*}\): \(E=53\) MeV for both reactions.
- Beam energies: 86.3 MeV \(^{16}\)O ions and 236.6 MeV \(^{64}\)Ni ions.
- Initial exciton numbers from full overlap calculations: \(n_0=7\) for \(^{16}\)O-induced reaction; \(n_0=5\) for \(^{64}\)Ni-induced reaction.
- Empirical formula (Eq. 4) would give \(n_0=6\) for O-induced and \(n_0=11\) for Ni-induced reactions, but the Ni case is very close to the Coulomb barrier so the formula is not recommended.
- The calculated gamma spectra are somewhat below the experimental data at energies exceeding 16 MeV.
- Experimental data do not extend above 25 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier study (Betak, 1993) on the decay of \(^{132}\)Nd\(^{*}\) from two different projectile-target combinations, where lighter projectiles gave fewer degrees of freedom in the early stage. The present case of \(^{160}\)Er\(^{*}\) is intriguing because the more symmetric combination (Ni-induced) yields harder gamma spectra, opposite to naive expectations, and the pre-equilibrium exciton model reproduces this feature where bremsstrahlung and other approaches fail. The results enable further refinement of initial exciton number systematics for heavy-ion reactions near the Coulomb barrier, though the author notes that the model is too schematic for complete fits and that parameter adjustments would not add physical insight.
