# The Origin of Fermi Arcs in Cuprate Pseudogap States and Strong Constraints on Viable Theories of High-Temperature Superconductivity

> A full Fermi surface exists in underdoped high-temperature superconductors if the temperature T lies above the pseudogap temperature T*. Below T* only arcs of Fermi surface survive, scaling with T/T* as T -> 0, with T* displaying strong doping dependence. There is no accepted explanation for this behavior. We show that generalizing the BCS theory of normal superconductivity to include d-wave pairs and antiferromagnetism leads to the origin and doping dependence of the T* scale, and a quantitative description of Fermi arcs. These results place strong constraints on viable theories of high-temperature superconductivity.

## Key claim

The authors claim that generalizing BCS theory to include d-wave pairs and antiferromagnetism within an SU(4) framework can quantitatively describe both the pseudogap temperature scale T* and the temperature-dependent Fermi arcs observed in underdoped cuprates, with the caveat that the theoretical curve for arc length versus T/T* has no adjustable parameters beyond those fixed previously by global fits to cuprate data.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier SU(4) coherent-state model, which incorporates antiferromagnetism competing with d-wave superconductivity, to a k-dependent SU(4)_k formalism that treats individual momentum components as symmetry generators. They derive an anisotropic pseudogap closure temperature T*(k) proportional to the d-wave formfactor |g(k)|, and solve for the Fermi arc length by finding the angular region where thermal energy k_B T exceeds the momentum-dependent pseudogap correlation energy, using the Fermi surface constraint for a given hole doping P.

## Key numbers

- Critical hole doping P_q approximately 0.16-0.19, above which antiferromagnetic correlations vanish leaving a pure singlet d-wave condensate
- The d-wave formfactor g(k) = cos k_x - cos k_y defines the momentum anisotropy of the pseudogap
- Fermi arc length scales as T/T* and extrapolates to zero at T=0, with a full Fermi surface at T=T*
- The theoretical curve for fractional arc length versus T/T* shows a rapid drop near T ~ T* shifting to approximately linear decrease to zero at T=0

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier SU(4) model (Guidry 1999, Guidry 2004, Sun 2005, Sun 2006) that described ground-state properties and phase diagrams of cuprates, now applied to the momentum-resolved Fermi arc data of Kanigel (2006). It contradicts interpretations that nodal and antinodal Fermi surfaces are removed by different mechanisms (such as charge ordering or nodal liquids), and instead shows a unified mechanism can explain both regions. The results constrain viable theories of high-temperature superconductivity to those that can simultaneously predict the T* scale and its doping dependence along with the anisotropic Fermi arc structure.
