# Final-state interaction and recoil polarization in (e,e'p) reactions: comparison with the polarized target case

> A study of the total cross section for polarized proton knockout in $(e,e'{\vec p})$ reactions is carried out for the closed-shell nucleus $^{40}$Ca. The dependence of FSI effects on polarization observables viewed as functions of the nucleon polarization angles is analyzed and interpreted within the basis of a semi-classical model for the orbit of the struck nucleon and trajectory of the ejected nucleon. A comparison with the case of a $^{39}$K polarized target and unpolarized protons is performed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that final-state interaction (FSI) effects on recoil polarization observables in $(e,e'{\vec p})$ reactions with unpolarized targets can be interpreted using the same semi-classical picture of nucleon orbits previously developed for polarized targets, but with the important qualification that the induced polarization arises solely from FSI (being zero in the Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation), whereas polarization transfer observables from polarized electrons are non-zero even without FSI.

## Method

The authors applied a semi-relativistic Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (SR-DWIA) model to compute the cross section for polarized proton knockout from the $d_{3/2}$ shell of $^{40}$Ca at quasielastic kinematics ($q=500$ MeV/c, $\omega=133.5$ MeV, $\theta_e=30^\circ$). They systematically varied the final proton spin direction and decomposed the FSI contributions by selectively turning off the real central potential, the spin-orbit potential, and the imaginary central potential of the Schwandt optical potential parameterization. The results were compared with previous calculations for polarized $^{39}$K targets from Amaro et al. (1999) and interpreted using a semi-classical model relating the nucleon's position in its orbit to the absorption strength.

## Key numbers

- Kinematics: momentum transfer $q=500$ MeV/c, energy transfer $\omega=133.5$ MeV, electron scattering angle $\theta_e=30^\circ$
- Unpolarized PWIA cross section for $^{40}$Ca$(e,e'p)$ from the $d_{3/2}$ shell: $\Sigma_{unpol} \simeq 13.0$ nb/MeV (or $260 \times 10^{-6}$ fm$^3$) at the maximum
- Unpolarized PWIA cross section per particle in the $d_{3/2}$ shell: $\Sigma_{unpol}/4 \simeq 65 \times 10^{-6}$ fm$^3$
- Unpolarized PWIA cross section for $^{39}$K polarized target: $\Sigma_{unpol} \simeq 32.5 \times 10^{-6}$ fm$^3$ at the maximum
- Absorption reduction due to imaginary central potential alone: approximately 50% for polarization $\theta_s=90^\circ$, $\Delta\phi=270^\circ$ (Fig. 3), versus approximately 20% for the opposite polarization $\theta_s=90^\circ$, $\Delta\phi=90^\circ$ (Fig. 2)

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical interpretation of FSI effects developed by Amaro et al. (1999) for polarized $^{39}$K targets to the case of recoil polarization in $(e,e'{\vec p})$ reactions on unpolarized $^{40}$Ca. It connects to the experimental measurements of induced normal polarization by Woo et al. (1998) on $^{12}$C and the polarization transfer measurements by Malov et al. (2000) on $^{16}$O, as well as the theoretical framework of the Maris effect from Kelly (1996) and Boffi et al. (1996). The results enable future experiments to distinguish between initial-state (target) and final-state (recoil nucleon) polarization measurements, guiding the design of experiments to study medium modifications of nucleon form factors and reaction mechanisms.
