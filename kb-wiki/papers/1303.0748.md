# Quantal corrections to mean-field dynamics including pairing

> Extending the stochastic mean-field model by including pairing, an approach is proposed for describing evolutions of complex many-body systems in terms of an ensemble of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock Bogoliubov trajectories which is determined by incorporating fluctuations in the initial state. Non-linear evolution of the initial fluctuations provides an approximate description of quantal correlations and fluctuations of collective observables. Since the initial-state fluctuations break the particle-number symmetry, the dynamical description in which pairing correlations play a crucial role is greatly improved as compare to the mean-field evolution. The approach is illustrated for a system of particles governed by a pairing Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

Extending the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach to include pairing correlations, the authors propose that an ensemble of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) trajectories with initial-state fluctuations can approximately describe quantal correlations and fluctuations of collective observables, provided the initial fluctuations break the particle-number symmetry; the approach is illustrated only for a system governed by a pairing Hamiltonian, not for a general nuclear system.

## Method

The authors extend the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach by incorporating pairing, generating an ensemble of initial generalized density matrices whose normal and anomalous components are uncorrelated Gaussian random variables with specified first and second moments (Eqs. 4-5). Each member of the ensemble is then evolved independently using the TDHFB equation of motion, and observable quantities are obtained by averaging over the ensemble. The method is benchmarked against exact solutions for a pairing Hamiltonian (Richardson model) with K=10 doubly-degenerate levels and N=10 particles, using 2×10^5 events and a Runge-Kutta 2 algorithm with time step Δt = 0.005/Δε.

## Key numbers

- For the pairing Hamiltonian with K=10 doubly-degenerate levels (Ω_i = 1), constant level spacing Δε, and N=10 particles, the BCS threshold is at G_cr/Δε ≈ -0.3.
- For G/Δε = -0.05, -0.5, and -1.0, the quantity D(t) = Tr(ρ(t) - ρ²(t)) is computed; the curve for G/Δε = -0.05 is multiplied by 50 in the figure.
- For G/Δε = -0.5, occupation numbers as a function of time are shown, with the biggest deviation from exact results seen for levels near the initial Fermi energy.
- For σ_±(t), the curve for G/Δε = -0.05 is multiplied by a factor of 5 in the figure.
- Simulations for N=10, 20, 30, 40, and 50 particles (with K=N and G/Δε = -1) take "few minutes on a standard laptop."

## Context

This work extends the stochastic mean-field (SMF) approach previously developed for TDHF (Ayi08, Lac04) to include pairing correlations via TDHFB, addressing the limitation that mean-field descriptions underestimate quantum collective fluctuations and cannot spontaneously break symmetries. The approach is benchmarked against exact solutions of the Richardson pairing Hamiltonian (Ric64, Vol01) and is motivated by increasing interest in TDHFB for Fermi liquids in nuclear physics (Eba10, Sca13), condensed matter, and atomic physics (Ton06). The method enables the description of dissipative effects and correlations in superfluid mesoscopic systems where exact diagonalization becomes prohibitive (N ≥ 16), and is computationally feasible for large numbers of degrees of freedom.
