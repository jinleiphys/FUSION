# Quark mass dependence of s-wave baryon resonances

> We study the quark mass dependence of $J^P = \frac12^-$ s-wave baryon resonances. Parameter free results are obtained in terms of the leading order chiral Lagrangian. In the 'heavy' SU(3) limit with $m_π=m_K \simeq $ 500 MeV the resonances turn into bound states forming two octets plus a singlet representations of the SU(3) group. A contrasted result is obtained in the 'light' SU(3) limit with $m_π=m_K \simeq $ 140 MeV for which no resonances exist. Using physical quark masses our analysis suggests to assign to the $S=-2$ resonances $Ξ(1690)$ and $Ξ(1620)$ the quantum numbers $J^P=1/2^-$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that s-wave baryon resonances with \(J^P = \frac12^-\) are dynamically generated from the leading-order chiral Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction) without free parameters, but only under the condition that the Bethe-Salpeter equation is renormalized with a specific subtraction scheme that enforces approximate crossing symmetry; in the 'heavy' SU(3) limit with degenerate meson masses near 500 MeV the resonances become bound states, while in the 'light' SU(3) limit with masses near 140 MeV no resonances exist.

## Method

The authors solve a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation for s-wave meson-baryon scattering using an on-shell reduction scheme, with the interaction kernel taken at leading order in chiral perturbation theory (the Weinberg-Tomozawa term). The loop functions are renormalized by imposing that the scattering amplitude matches the perturbative kernel at subtraction points chosen to preserve crossing symmetry, specifically at the masses of the baryon octet members for each strangeness-isospin sector. The quark mass dependence is studied by varying the pion mass via a parameter \(x\) that interpolates between the physical point (\(x=0\)) and a SU(3)-symmetric point (\(x=1\)) where the pion mass equals the kaon mass, using the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation and leading-order baryon mass formulas.

## Key numbers

- In the SU(3)-symmetric limit (\(x=1\)), two degenerate octets of bound states appear at mass \(M_8 = 1691.83\) MeV (eigenvalue \(-3\)) and a singlet at \(M_0 = 1604.21\) MeV (eigenvalue \(-6\)).
- For the \(\Xi(1690)\) resonance, the calculated mass is 1663 MeV and width is 4 MeV, with a strong coupling to \(\bar K \Sigma\) (\(|g|^2 = 5.15\)) and a very small coupling to \(\pi \Xi\) (\(|g|^2 = 0.02\)), consistent with the experimental ratio \(\Gamma(\pi \Xi) / \Gamma(\bar K \Sigma) < 0.09\).
- For the \(\Xi(1620)\) resonance, the calculated mass is 1565 MeV and width is 247 MeV.
- The \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance is reproduced at mass 1409 MeV and width 34 MeV (experimental: \(1406 \pm 4\) MeV, \(50 \pm 2\) MeV).
- The \(N(1535)\) resonance is reproduced at mass 1500 MeV and width 64 MeV (experimental pole position: \(1505 \pm 10\) MeV, \(170 \pm 80\) MeV).
- The \(\Lambda(1670)\) resonance is reproduced at mass 1663 MeV and width 12 MeV (experimental: \(1670 \pm 10\) MeV, \(35 \pm 15\) MeV).
- The squared couplings for the SU(3) octet and singlet bound states are \(g_8^2 = 9.65\) and \(g_0^2 = 15.83\), respectively.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Lutz and Kolomeitsev (2000, 2002) that dynamically generates the \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(N(1535)\) resonances from the Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction with a crossing-symmetric renormalization scheme. It contrasts with the method of Oset and collaborators (2001), who used a three-momentum cutoff that fails to produce the \(N(1535)\) and gives only one resonance in the \(S=-2\) sector (identified with the \(\Xi(1620)\)), whereas the present scheme also generates the \(\Xi(1690)\) and assigns it \(J^P = 1/2^-\). The paper confirms the existence of a second \(\Lambda(1405)\) state first pointed out by Jido (2001) and by Garcia-Recio et al. (2002), and systematically maps the SU(3) structure of the dynamically generated resonances into two octets and a singlet.
