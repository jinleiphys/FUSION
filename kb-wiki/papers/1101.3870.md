# Non-Fermi liquid corrections to the neutrino mean free path in dense quark matter

> We calculate the neutrino mean free path with non-Fermi liquid (NFL) corrections in quark matter from scattering and absorption processes for both degenerate and nondegenerate neutrinos. We show that the mean free path decreases due to the non-Fermi liquid corrections leading to $l_{mean}^{-1}\sim[......+ .... C_F^2α_s^2\ln(m_D/T)^2]$. These reduction results in higher rate of scattering.

## Key claim

The neutrino mean free path in dense quark matter receives non-Fermi liquid (NFL) corrections that reduce its value, with the inverse mean free path acquiring a logarithmic term proportional to \(C_F^2 \alpha_s^2 \ln(m_D/T)^2\); the reduction is conditional on the inclusion of dynamically screened transverse gluon exchanges rather than bare propagators.

## Method

The authors calculated the neutrino mean free path from absorption (direct URCA: \(d + \nu_e \rightarrow u + e^-\) and its inverse) and scattering (\(q_i + \nu_e \rightarrow q_i + \nu_e\)) processes in two-flavor quark matter, using the weak interaction Lagrangian with Cabibbo angle \(\cos^2\theta_c \simeq 0.948\). They incorporated NFL corrections by replacing the free-quark dispersion relation with one derived from the on-shell one-loop quark self-energy, which contains a logarithmic singularity \(\Sigma \sim (g^2 C_F / 12\pi^2)(\omega - \mu) \ln(m_D / |\omega - \mu|)\), leading to a modified phase-space factor \(dp/d\omega \simeq [1 + (C_F \alpha_s / 3\pi) \ln(m_D/T)] (E_p/p)\). The mean free path was evaluated separately for degenerate neutrinos (\(\mu_\nu \gg T\)) and nondegenerate neutrinos (\(\mu_\nu \ll T\)), with the matrix element for the nondegenerate case requiring an additional factor of \(\alpha_s\) from quark interactions to obtain a nonvanishing phase space.

## Key numbers

* Degenerate neutrino absorption mean free path: without NFL correction, 3.62 m; with NFL correction, 1.85 m (quark chemical potential \(\mu_q \simeq 500\) MeV, electron chemical potential \(\mu_e = 11\) MeV, neutrino energy \(E_\nu = T\), quark mass \(m_q = 10\) MeV).
* Degenerate neutrino scattering mean free path: without NFL correction, 15.88 m; with NFL correction, 8.13 m (same conditions).
* Nondegenerate neutrino absorption mean free path: without NFL correction, 7.68 m; with NFL correction, 3.93 m (same conditions).
* Nondegenerate neutrino scattering mean free path: without NFL correction, 1585.18 m; with NFL correction, 811.06 m (same conditions).
* The correction factor to the mean free path from phase-space modification is \([1 + (C_F \alpha_s / 3\pi) \ln(m_D/T)]^2\), where \(C_F = (N_c^2 - 1)/(2N_c)\) and \(m_D\) is the Debye mass.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order calculations of the neutrino mean free path in quark matter by Iwamoto (1980, 1982), which assumed a free Fermi gas, by including non-Fermi liquid corrections arising from the exchange of dynamically screened transverse gluons. It parallels similar NFL studies of specific heat (Boyanovski et al., 2001; Ipp et al., 2004) and magnetic susceptibility (Tatsumi et al., 2009) in dense QCD, and generalizes the QED plasma results of Tubbs and Schramm (1975) and Lamb and Pethick (1976) to QCD matter. The reduced mean free path implies faster cooling of compact stars for nondegenerate neutrinos and supports the maintenance of local thermodynamic equilibrium for degenerate neutrinos, enabling more realistic models of neutron star thermal evolution.
