# Interaction of the single-particle and collective degrees of freedom in non-magic nuclei: the role of phonon tadpole terms

> A method of a consistent consideration of the phonon contributions to mass and gap operators in non-magic nuclei is developed in the so-called g^2 approximation, where g is the low-lying phonon creation amplitude. It includes simultaneous accounting for both the usual non-local terms and the phonon tadpole ones. The relations which allow the tadpoles to be calculated without any new parameters are derived. As an application of the results, the role of the phonon tadpoles in the single-particle strength distribution and in the single-particle energies and gap values has been considered. Relation to the problem of the surface nature of pairing is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper develops a consistent method for including phonon tadpole terms in the calculation of mass and gap operators for non-magic nuclei, showing that these tadpole terms are of the same order (g^2) as the usual non-local pole terms but were omitted in all prior work; the authors derive relations that allow tadpoles to be calculated without any new parameters, but the actual numerical impact on single-particle energies and gap values is presented only as a qualitative discussion rather than with explicit computed numbers.

## Method

The authors extend the g^2 approximation (where g is the low-lying phonon creation amplitude) from magic nuclei to non-magic nuclei with pairing by deriving a closed set of equations for the particle-hole (ph) and particle-particle (pp) phonon tadpole operators. They use the Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) framework, starting from the variation of the equation for the phonon creation vertex to obtain integral equations for the tadpole terms, then apply a "small d" approximation where the phonon-particle vertex g dominates over the pairing phonon vertices d^(1) and d^(2). The final equations for the tadpoles K^ph and K^pp are expressed in the single-particle lambda-representation with a diagonal approximation, and the solutions are to be added to the usual non-local phonon contributions to the mass and gap operators.

## Key numbers

- The perturbation parameter for the g^2 approximation is defined as alpha = (bar_g_L^2) / ((2j+1) * omega_L^2), where bar_g_L is the average matrix element of the L-phonon creation amplitude at the Fermi surface, omega_L is its excitation energy, and j is a typical single-particle angular momentum (j proportional to A^(1/3))
- For the tin region, prior calculations (Avekaev et al., 1999) showed the phonon contribution to the gap value Delta is about 30% of the experimental gap value, with the remaining 70% due to the "main" pairing force
- The Milan group (Baranco et al., 1999; Milan2) found about 50% of the gap value for the tin region comes from the initial NN-force and 50% from the phonon contribution
- Ab initio calculations (Pankratov et al., using Paris and Argonne v18 NN-forces) obtained close agreement with experimental Delta values, leaving room for not more than 20% phonon corrections

## Context

This work extends the consistent treatment of phonon tadpole terms from magic nuclei (Khodel, 1976; KhS) to non-magic nuclei with pairing, addressing a gap in prior calculations by Avekaev et al. (1999) and the Milan group (Baranco et al., 1999) that omitted the tadpole term. The method enables future calculations of single-particle strength distributions, single-particle energies, and gap values in superfluid nuclei that properly include all g^2-order contributions, and is directly relevant to the longstanding question of whether nuclear superfluidity has a volume or surface nature.
