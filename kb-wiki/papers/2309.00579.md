# Bayesian location of the QCD critical point from a holographic perspective

> A fundamental question in QCD is the existence of a phase transition at large doping of quarks over antiquarks. We present the first prediction of a QCD critical point (CP) from a Bayesian analysis constrained by first principle results at zero doping. We employ the gauge/gravity duality to map QCD onto a theory of dual black holes. Predictions for the CP location in different realizations of the model overlap at one sigma. Even if many prior samples do not include a CP, one is found in nearly 100\% of posterior samples, indicating a strong preference for a CP.

# Bayesian location of the QCD critical point from a holographic perspective

## Key claim

The paper presents the first Bayesian prediction of the QCD critical point location using holographic models constrained by lattice QCD results at zero baryon density, finding that even though many prior parameter samples do not include a critical point, nearly 100% of posterior samples contain one, indicating a strong preference for the existence of a critical point within the holographic framework.

## Method

The authors employ the gauge/gravity duality (Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton holographic models) to map QCD onto a theory of dual black holes in a 5-dimensional asymptotically anti-de Sitter spacetime. They test two different functional forms for the dilaton potential V(phi) and gauge coupling f(phi) (a polynomial-hyperbolic ansatz and a parametric ansatz), then use Bayesian inference with Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to constrain model parameters using lattice QCD results for the entropy density and second-order baryon number susceptibility at zero chemical potential from the Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration.

## Key numbers

* Critical point location for the polynomial-hyperbolic ansatz (PHA): T_c = 104 ± 3 MeV, mu_Bc = 589 (+36/-26) MeV at 95% confidence
* Critical point location for the parametric ansatz (PA): T_c = 107 ± 1 MeV, mu_Bc = 571 ± 11 MeV at 95% confidence
* The two model predictions overlap at one sigma, with combined 95% confidence region in the range T = 101-108 MeV and mu_B = 560-625 MeV
* Predicted center-of-mass energy for experimental detection: sqrt(s) = 4.4 ± 0.4 GeV for the PHA model and sqrt(s) = 4.6 (+0.2/-0.1) GeV for the PA model
* Gravitational constant posterior: kappa_5^2 = 11.4 ± 0.1 for both ansatze
* Correlation parameter between lattice data points: Gamma = 0.84 (+0.03/-0.06)

## Context

This work extends previous holographic predictions for the QCD critical point by Critelli et al. (2017) and Grefa et al. (2021, 2022) by introducing Bayesian inference to systematically quantify uncertainties from different functional forms of the holographic potentials. The results are compatible with those obtained by Cai et al. (2022) and fall within the extrapolated lattice QCD transition band from Borsanyi et al. (2020) based on the peak of the chiral susceptibility. The predicted collision energy range of 4.0-4.8 GeV is covered by the STAR Fixed Target program at RHIC and could be explored at FAIR, enabling experimental searches for the critical point through the Beam Energy Scan program.
