# Nuclear Structure and the Search for Collective Enhancement of P,T-Violation

> The discovery of the atomic electric dipole moment (EDM) would reveal the simultaneous violation of parity (P) and time-reversal (T) invariance. The EDM can be induced by T-odd forces between the nucleus and atomic electrons. As the nuclear dipole moment is screened according to the Schiff theorem, the appropriate nuclear operator is the Schiff moment that may exist in nuclei under PT-violation. We briefly review the current experimental situation and discuss more in detail the ideas concerning possible collective mechanisms for the enhancement of the nuclear Schiff moment. The most promising directions are related to the coexistence of octupole and quadrupole collective modes, either in the form of static deformation or as soft vibrational excitations. The search for enhancement is important for widening the pool of nuclei as candidates for the atomic EDM as well as for development of nuclear many-body theory beyond standard mean-field and random phase approximations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that collective enhancement of the nuclear Schiff moment, the key mediator of atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs), can arise from the coexistence of quadrupole and octupole collective modes, either as static deformation or soft vibrational excitations, but the authors note that the ideas regarding soft vibrational modes are "more speculative since the fully realistic calculations were not performed yet."

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework connecting PT-violating hadronic forces to the atomic EDM via the nuclear Schiff moment, deriving the Schiff moment operator from a multipole expansion of the nuclear charge density. They then analyze the single-particle contribution to the Schiff moment and compare it to collective mechanisms, focusing on axially symmetric deformed nuclei where quadrupole and octupole deformation coexist, using the intrinsic Schiff moment expression S_intr ≈ (9/(20√(35)π)) e Z R³ β₂ β₃ and the mixing of parity doublets induced by PT-odd weak interactions.

## Key numbers

- The Schiff moment enhancement from collective octupole-quadrupole deformation can reach "two to three orders of magnitude" compared to single-particle estimates, under the assumption of close opposite-parity levels with splitting Δ ≈ 50 keV.
- For radium isotopes: ²²⁵Ra has Δ = 55 keV (I = 1/2) and ²²³Ra has Δ = 50 keV (I = 3/2).
- The atomic EDM d is related to the Schiff moment S by d = ξ (S/(e·fm³)) × 10⁻¹⁷ e·cm, where ξ = 3.3 for radon and ξ = -8.5 for radium.
- Best experimental limits: |d(¹⁹⁹Hg)| < 2.1 × 10⁻²⁸ e·cm (Romalis 2001); d(¹²⁹Xe) = (0.7 ± 2.8) × 10⁻²⁷ e·cm (Rosenberry 2001).
- The electron EDM limit from ²⁰⁵Tl experiments: |d_e| ≤ 1.6 × 10⁻²⁷ e·cm.
- ²²⁵Ra has half-life t₁/₂ = 15 days; ²²³Ra has t₁/₂ = 11 days but I = 3/2.

## Context

This work extends earlier ideas on parity violation enhancement in neutron scattering (Sushkov and Flambaum 1980, 1982) and the role of octupole deformation in fission asymmetry (Sushkov and Flambaum 1982), applying similar collective concepts to the Schiff moment problem. The paper builds on calculations by Auerbach, Flambaum, and Spevak (1996) and Spevak, Auerbach, and Flambaum (1997) for static octupole deformation, and discusses the potential for soft vibrational modes in radioactive isotopes of radium, radon, and thorium. The work aims to guide experimental searches for atomic EDMs, particularly at TRIUMF (E-929 collaboration) and KVI, by identifying promising nuclear candidates beyond the current best limits from ¹²⁹Xe and ¹⁹⁹Hg, and to motivate development of nuclear many-body theory beyond mean-field and random phase approximations.
