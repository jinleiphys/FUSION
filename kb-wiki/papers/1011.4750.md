# Entropy Production in Gluodynamics in temporal axial gauge in 2+1 dimensions

> Entropy production in non-Abelian gauge theory is discussed in the limit of vanishing classical fields. Based on the Kadanoff-Baym equation with the leading order self-energy in the temporal axial gauge, the kinetic entropy is introduced and is proven to increase for a given self-energy. Numerical simulation of the Kadanoff-Baym equation for the non-Abelian gauge theory is performed in 2+1 dimensions. Starting from anisotropic distribution in momentum space, relaxation to isotropic distribution proceeds and the entropy is produced due to off-shell scattering of massive transverse polarization mode as expected from the proof of the H-theorem.

## Key claim

The paper proves an H-theorem for gluodynamics in temporal axial gauge using the Kadanoff-Baym equation with leading-order self-energy, showing that off-shell processes such as $g \leftrightarrow gg$ contribute to entropy production and thermalization; however, this proof relies on the gradient expansion (coarse graining in time) and the authors note that controlled gauge dependence under nonequilibrium conditions has not been proven.

## Method

The authors start from the 2PI effective action in temporal axial gauge with vanishing classical fields, derive the Kadanoff-Baym equations for transverse and longitudinal components of the gluon propagator, and approximate the self-energy at leading order in the coupling expansion $O(g^2)$. They introduce a kinetic entropy current and prove its divergence is positive semi-definite using the semi-positive definite coefficients $P_{\alpha\beta}$ and $Q_{\alpha\beta}$ and symmetry relations among them. Numerical simulations are performed in 2+1 dimensions for the transverse mode only, using a lattice discretization with $ma_s=0.3$, $N_s=30$, $a_t/a_s=0.1$, and $N_t=400$ time steps, replacing the tadpole mass shift by a constant mass $m^2$ to simulate thermal mass.

## Key numbers

* Spatial lattice spacing $a_s$ with $ma_s=0.3$, temporal step size $a_t/a_s=0.1$, $N_s=30$ momentum grid points, and $N_t=400$ time steps for memory integrals
* The leading-order self-energy includes $g\leftrightarrow gg$ processes at $O(g^2)$, while $gg\leftrightarrow gg$ and $g\leftrightarrow ggg$ are next-to-leading order at $O(g^4)$
* On-shell Boltzmann dynamics predicts equilibration time $\tau_{\rm eq}=2-3\ \mathrm{fm}/c$ for comparison

## Context

This work extends the Kadanoff-Baym approach previously applied to scalar $\phi^4$ theory in 1+1 dimensions (Berges and collaborators, 2002-2004), 2+1 dimensions (Juchem and collaborators, 2004), and 3+1 dimensions (Aarts and collaborators, 2002-2005) to non-Abelian gauge theory for the first time. The authors aim to address the early thermalization puzzle in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, where perturbative estimates (Baier and collaborators, 2001) predict longer thermalization times than the 0.6-1 fm/$c$ inferred from hydrodynamic models. The off-shell entropy production mechanism presented here is distinct from classical plasma instabilities such as the Weibel instability (Weibel, 1959) and Nielsen-Olsen instability (Nielsen and Olesen, 1978), and from classical statistical lattice approaches (Berges and collaborators, 2008).
