# Electron in a transverse harmonic cavity

> We employ Hamiltonian light-front quantum field theory in a basis function approach to solve the non-perturbative problem of an electron in a strong scalar transverse confining potential. We evaluate both the invariant mass spectra and the anomalous magnetic moment of the lowest state for this two-scale system. The weak external field limit of the anomalous magnetic moment agrees with the result of QED perturbation theory within the anticipated accuracy.

# Electron in a transverse harmonic cavity

## Key claim

The nonperturbative light-front Hamiltonian calculation of the electron anomalous magnetic moment in a transverse harmonic cavity agrees with the Schwinger one-loop QED result (α/2π) in the weak external field limit, but only after extrapolation to zero external field and within the anticipated numerical accuracy of 1–1.5% for nonrenormalized results; the renormalized results, however, extrapolate to a value approximately 20% above the Schwinger result, consistent with other truncated light-front calculations using Pauli-Villars regularization.

## Method

The authors solve the light-front QED Hamiltonian in light-front gauge using a basis of transverse two-dimensional harmonic oscillator states combined with discretized longitudinal plane waves (periodic boundary conditions for photons, antiperiodic for electrons). The Fock space is truncated to include only single-electron and electron-plus-photon states, with cutoffs on total light-front momentum K and maximum transverse quanta Nmax. The Hamiltonian matrix is diagonalized to obtain eigenvalues and eigenvectors, from which the anomalous magnetic moment is extracted via the spin-flip probability amplitude of the lowest mass eigenstate.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger one-loop QED result for the scaled anomalous magnetic moment √(δμ/g²) is 0.1125 (corresponding to α/2π).
- Nonrenormalized extrapolation to zero external field (Ω = 0 MeV) yields a = 0.1121, a deviation of less than 1% from the Schwinger value.
- Individual extrapolations for Nmax = K = 12–20 with Ω between 0.1 and 1.4 MeV give a values ranging from 0.1109 to 0.1134, within 1.5% of the Schwinger result.
- Renormalized results extrapolated to the continuum limit (Nmax = K → ∞) give a = 0.1362 (for Ω ≥ 0.2 MeV) and a = 0.1383 (for Ω ≥ 0.1 MeV), approximately 20% above the Schwinger value.
- With fixed Nmax = 20 and increasing K from 10 to 50, the Hamiltonian matrix dimension grows from d = 11,790 to d = 69,590; continuum extrapolation gives a = 0.1288–0.1290, approximately 15% above the Schwinger value.
- The harmonic oscillator frequency Ω is varied over a range spanning the electron mass (0.511 MeV), with specific calculations at Ω = 0.05 MeV and up to Ω = 1.4 MeV.
- The fine structure constant is taken as α = 1/137.036.

## Context

This work extends the basis-function light-front approach of Vary (2009) to QED in an external transverse confining potential, providing a nonperturbative framework for strong-field dynamics relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC and to next-generation laser facilities. The renormalized results show an enhancement of the anomalous magnetic moment similar to that found in the Pauli-Villars regulated light-front calculations of Brodsky (1998, 2004) and Chabysheva (2009). The method enables future extension to higher Fock sectors (including electron-positron pairs) for treating supercritical fields, and to light-front QCD using color-singlet basis enumeration techniques.
