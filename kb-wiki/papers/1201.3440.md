# Color-Singlet Three-Quark States in the su(4)-Algebraic Many-Quark Model

> As announced in last paper by the present authors, the color-singlet three-quark states are investigated for the case of the spherical j-j coupling and L-S coupling shell models. The latter case automatically leads to the su(4)*su(4)-model. For a certain type of Hamiltonian, the states of individual "nucleons" are characterized.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a specific Hamiltonian can produce color-singlet three-quark states (nucleon-like states) in either the \(L\)-\(S\) coupling scheme (yielding the \(su(4)\otimes su(4)\) form) or the \(j\)-\(j\) coupling scheme, but only under the condition that an additional interaction term \(\tilde H_2\) is included to resolve an energy-ordering problem that arises when spin-orbit coupling is present.

## Method

The authors construct color-singlet three-quark creation operators \(\tilde B^*\) in both the \(L\)-\(S\) coupling (where spin-orbit partner levels \(j_\pm = l_s \pm 1/2\) are strongly correlated) and the \(j\)-\(j\) coupling (where these levels are treated independently). They define four orthonormalized color-singlet three-quark states for a given \(l_s\) and act on them with a Hamiltonian \(\tilde H = \tilde H_0 + \tilde H_1 + \tilde H_2\), where \(\tilde H_0\) represents the spin-orbit interaction, \(\tilde H_1\) drives quark-triplet formation, and \(\tilde H_2\) is an additional term coupling the quark-number difference to the triplet-formation interaction. Eigenvalue equations are solved analytically for the two-level system to find the conditions under which the lower-energy eigenstate corresponds to the desired nucleon-like state.

## Key numbers

* For the case \(g_0 = 0, g_2 = 0\) (no spin-orbit, no \(\tilde H_2\)), the lower eigenstate energy for both \(j_+\) and \(j_-\) states is \(E_{j_\pm}^{(1)} = -4g_1(l_s+1)\), where \(g_1 \geq 0\) is the triplet-formation strength and \(l_s\) is the orbital quantum number.
* For the case \(g_1 = 3g_2\) and \(g_0 < 8g_2 (l_s+1)/(2l_s+1)\), the lower eigenstate for the \(j_+\) level has energy \(E_{j_+}^{(1)} = -3g_0 l_s - 24g_2(l_s+2)\), and for the \(j_-\) level \(E_{j_-}^{(1)} = 3g_0(l_s+1) - 24g_2(l_s+1)\).
* The \(\Delta\)-excitation energy relative to the nucleon state for the \(j_+\) level is \(E_{j_+}^{(\Delta)} - E_{j_+}^{(1)} = 24g_2(l_s+2) > 0\) when \(g_2 > 0\).
* The effective spin-orbit strength for the one-nucleon state is \(\xi_{l_0} = 3g_0 + 24g_2/(2l_s+1)\), which deviates from the bare three-quark value \(3g_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous paper (Tsue et al., 2011, referred to as (I)) which presented a systematic treatment of the \(su(4)\)-algebraic many-quark model. The paper specifically addresses the quark-triplet operator forms derived in that earlier work: one form (I\(\cdot\)6\(\cdot\)3) matching that of Petry et al. (year not given in text) and another form (I\(\cdot\)6\(\cdot\)6) based on the authors' own idea. The analysis shows that a Hamiltonian with only spin-orbit and triplet-formation terms is unsatisfactory because it can produce a lower-energy color-singlet state that is not the desired nucleon state; the addition of \(\tilde H_2\) resolves this. The resulting nucleon states (Eqs. 54a and 54b) were applied to nuclear magnetic moments in a separate work (reference [3] in the text, year not given). The paper enables future investigation of deviations from these idealized states.
