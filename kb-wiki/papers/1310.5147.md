# A Non-radial Oscillation Mode in an Accreting Millisecond Pulsar?

> We present results of targeted searches for signatures of non-radial oscillation modes (such as r- and g-modes) in neutron stars using {\it RXTE} data from several accreting millisecond X-ray pulsars (AMXPs). We search for potentially coherent signals in the neutron star rest frame by first removing the phase delays associated with the star's binary motion and computing FFT power spectra of continuous light curves with up to $2^{30}$ time bins. We search a range of frequencies in which both r- and g-modes are theoretically expected to reside. Using data from the discovery outburst of the 435 Hz pulsar XTE J1751$-$305 we find a single candidate, coherent oscillation with a frequency of $0.5727597 \times ν_{spin} = 249.332609$ Hz, and a fractional Fourier amplitude of $7.46 \times 10^{-4}$. We estimate the significance of this feature at the $1.6 \times 10^{-3}$ level, slightly better than a $3σ$ detection. We argue that possible mode identifications include rotationally-modified g-modes associated with either a helium-rich surface layer or a density discontinuity due to electron captures on hydrogen in the accreted ocean. Alternatively, the frequency could be identified with that of an inertial mode or an r-mode modified by the presence of a solid crust, however, the r-mode amplitude required to account for the observed modulation amplitude would induce a large spin-down rate inconsistent with the observed pulse timing measurements. For the AMXPs XTE J1814$-$338 and NGC 6440 X-2 we do not find any candidate oscillation signals, and we place upper limits on the fractional Fourier amplitude of any coherent oscillations in our frequency search range of $7.8\times 10^{-4}$ and $5.6 \times 10^{-3}$, respectively. We briefly discuss the prospects and sensitivity for similar searches with future, larger X-ray collecting area missions.

## Key claim

The paper reports a candidate coherent oscillation at 0.57276 times the spin frequency of the accreting millisecond pulsar XTE J1751-305, with a significance of 1.6 × 10⁻³ (slightly better than 3σ), but the authors caution that this is a single candidate among many searched frequencies and that possible mode identifications face theoretical challenges, including an r-mode interpretation that would imply a spin-down rate inconsistent with pulse timing measurements.

## Method

The authors used RXTE data from the discovery outburst of XTE J1751-305, corrected photon arrival times for binary orbital motion using known orbital solutions, and constructed a continuous light curve with 2³⁰ time bins sampled at 2048 Hz. They computed an FFT power spectrum and searched a frequency range of 0.4166 to 0.7567 times the spin frequency, corresponding to theoretically expected co-rotating frame frequencies for r-modes and g-modes. Similar searches were performed for XTE J1814-338 (two light curves of 2³⁰ bins each) and NGC 6440 X-2 (four shorter data segments averaged together).

## Key numbers

* XTE J1751-305: candidate oscillation at frequency 0.5727597 × ν_spin = 249.332609 Hz, with fractional Fourier amplitude 7.46 × 10⁻⁴
* XTE J1751-305: significance of candidate feature estimated at 1.6 × 10⁻³ level (single-trial probability 2 × 10⁻¹¹ after accounting for 77.6 × 10⁶ independent Fourier frequencies)
* XTE J1814-338: upper limit on fractional Fourier amplitude of any coherent oscillation in search range of 7.8 × 10⁻⁴ at full frequency resolution
* NGC 6440 X-2: upper limit on fractional Fourier amplitude of any coherent oscillation in search range of 5.6 × 10⁻³ at 3.125 × 10⁻⁴ Hz resolution
* XTE J1751-305: upper limits on broader-band signals of 1.64 × 10⁻⁴ (at 1/2048 Hz resolution) and 1.42 × 10⁻⁴ (at 1/128 Hz resolution)

## Context

This work extends targeted searches for non-radial oscillation modes in neutron stars, building on theoretical predictions that r-modes and g-modes could produce observable X-ray modulations by perturbing accretion hot-spots (Lamb et al. 2010; Nayakshin et al. 2010) or by modulating surface temperature (Lee and Strohmayer 2005; Heyl 2005). The candidate in XTE J1751-305 is discussed in the context of rotationally-modified g-modes associated with helium-rich surface layers or density discontinuities from electron captures, as well as inertial modes or crust-modified r-modes studied by Yoshida and Lee (2001) and Alford et al. (2012). The non-detections in XTE J1814-338 and NGC 6440 X-2 provide upper limits that constrain mode amplitudes in those systems, and the authors note that future missions with larger collecting area (such as LOFT and AXTAR) could improve sensitivity for similar searches.
