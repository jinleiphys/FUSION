# Gamow-Teller transitions and neutron-proton-pair transfer reactions

> We propose a schematic model of nucleons moving in spin--orbit partner levels, $j=l\pm{\sfrac12}$, to explain Gamow--Teller and two-nucleon transfer data in $N=Z$ nuclei above $^{40}$Ca. Use of the $LS$ coupling scheme provides a more transparent approach to interpret the structure and reaction data.We apply the model to the analysis of charge-exchange, $^{42}$Ca($^3$He,t)$^{42}$Sc, and np-transfer, $^{40}$Ca($^3$He,p)$^{42}$Sc, reactions data to define the elementary modes of excitation in terms of both isovector and isoscalar pairs, whose properties can be determined by adjusting the parameters of the model (spin--orbit splitting, isovector pairing strength and quadrupole matrix element) to the available data. The overall agreement with experiment suggests that the approach captures the main physics ingredients and provides the basis for a boson approximation that can be extended to heavier nuclei. Our analysis also reveals that the SU(4)-symmetry limit is not realized in $^{42}$Sc.

## Key claim

The authors propose a schematic model of nucleons in spin-orbit partner levels to explain both Gamow-Teller and two-nucleon transfer data in N=Z nuclei above ⁴⁰Ca, but they find that the SU(4)-symmetry limit is not realized in ⁴²Sc despite the concentration of GT strength in the lowest 1⁺ state, because the spin-orbit potential breaks the LS-coupling scheme.

## Method

The authors constructed a schematic model assuming nucleons occupy two orbitals with radial quantum number n, orbital angular momentum l, and total angular momentum j = l ± 1/2, analyzed in the LS coupling basis rather than the usual jj coupling. They applied the model to ⁴²Ca(³He,t)⁴²Sc charge-exchange and ⁴⁰Ca(³He,p)⁴²Sc np-transfer reactions, adjusting three parameters (spin-orbit splitting Δε, isovector pairing strength g₁, and quadrupole matrix element V₂₁₁₀) to fit experimental data, with the isoscalar pairing strength g₀ varied by ±15% around g₁.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit splitting fixed at Δε = 6 MeV, taken from Fujita et al. (2014, 2015)
- Experimental pairing gap Δ⁽³⁾ for ⁴¹Ca is 1.5585 MeV, which fixes the isovector pairing strength to g₁ ≈ -5 MeV
- B(GT;0⁺₁→1⁺₁) = 2.17(5) experimentally versus 2.11(8) from the schematic model
- B(M1;1⁺₁→0⁺₁) = 6.1(2.7) μ_N² experimentally versus 4.80(2) μ_N² from the schematic model
- Ratio of cross-sections σ(1⁺₁)/σ(0⁺₁) ≈ 2 experimentally versus 2.20(17) from the schematic model
- Excitation energy of the 1⁺₁ level in ⁴²Sc is 0.611 MeV experimentally
- Excitation energy of the 2⁺₁ level in ⁴²Ca is 1.525 MeV, used to fix the quadrupole matrix element

## Context

This work extends the experimental findings of Fujita et al. (2014, 2015), who reported a concentration of GT strength in the lowest 1⁺ state of ⁴²Sc and proposed it as a "low-energy super GT state" attributed to restoration of Wigner's SU(4) symmetry (Wigner, 1937). The authors also incorporate earlier two-nucleon transfer measurements using the ⁴⁰Ca(³He,p)⁴²Sc reaction by Zurmuhle (1966) and Puhlhofer (1968). The analysis contradicts the SU(4)-symmetry interpretation and provides the basis for a boson approximation that can be extended to heavier 0f₇/₂ nuclei using an interacting boson model, currently under study by the authors.
