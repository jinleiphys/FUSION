# Semi-inclusive single-jet production in DIS at next-to-leading order in the Color Glass Condensate

> Within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, we derive the next-to-leading order (NLO) cross-section for the single-jet semi-inclusive cross-section in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at small $x$, for both longitudinally and transversely polarized virtual photons. We provide analytic expressions, valid at finite $N_c$ and suitable for numerical evaluation, for both the cross-section differential in rapidity and transverse momentum and the cross-section differential in rapidity only. Our NLO formulae demonstrate that the very forward rapidity regime is plagued by large double logarithmic corrections coming from phase space constraints on soft gluons close to the kinematic threshold for jet production. A joint resummation of small-$x$ and threshold logarithms at single logarithmic accuracy is proposed to remedy the instability of the cross-section in this regime. By integrating over the single-jet phase space, we recover known results for the NLO DIS structure functions at small $x$, previously obtained using the optical theorem.

## Key claim

The paper derives the next-to-leading order (NLO) cross-section for semi-inclusive single-jet production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at small Bjorken x within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, but finds that the very forward rapidity regime is plagued by large double logarithmic corrections from soft gluon phase space constraints near the kinematic threshold for jet production, requiring a joint resummation of small-x and threshold logarithms to stabilize the cross-section.

## Method

The authors start from the NLO inclusive dijet production cross-section in DIS obtained in Caucal (2021) and integrate over the unmeasured parton in the final state to obtain the semi-inclusive single-jet cross-section, using dimensional regularization in the transverse plane to isolate ultraviolet and collinear divergences. They compute contributions from virtual diagrams (vertex corrections, self-energy corrections with gluons crossing the shockwave) and real diagrams (where either a fermionic jet or gluonic jet is tagged), demonstrating cancellations between diagrams sharing the same topology. The calculation is performed at finite N_c and includes contributions where the tagged jet originates from a gluon, with analytic expressions provided for both longitudinally and transversely polarized virtual photons.

## Key numbers

* The dominant double logarithmic correction in the forward rapidity regime is \(-\alpha_s C_F/(2\pi) \ln^2(1-z)\), which is negative and suppresses the cross-section near the kinematic threshold \(z=1\).
* The NLO cross-section recovers known results for NLO DIS structure functions at small x from Beuf (2017) and Hanninen (2017) when integrated over the single-jet phase space.
* The \(1/N_c^2\) suppressed corrections to the NLO impact factor depend on the quadrupole CGC correlator (trace of four Wilson lines), which does not appear in the leading-order cross-section.

## Context

This work extends the NLO calculation of inclusive dijet production in DIS by Caucal (2021) to the semi-inclusive single-jet case, and differs from the NLO calculation of single hadron production by Bergabo (2022) by considering jet production (avoiding fragmentation function uncertainties) and using dimensional regularization explicitly. The results enable future studies of whether transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization persists at NLO in the regime \(k_t^2, Q_s^2 \ll Q^2\), and provide the foundation for assessing the impact of QCD radiative corrections on the sensitivity of forward jet production to gluon saturation effects at the Electron-Ion Collider, as previously studied at leading order by Iancu (2020).
