# Anisotropic matching principle for the hydrodynamics expansion

> Following the recent success of anisotropic hydrodynamics we propose a new, general prescription for the hydrodynamics expansion around an anisotropic background. The anisotropic distribution is fixing exactly the complete energy-momentum tensor, just like the effective temperature is fixing the proper energy density in the ordinary expansion around local equilibrium. This means that momen- tum anisotropies are already included at the leading order, allowing for large pressure anisotropies without the need of a next to leading order treatment. The first moment of the Boltzmann equation (local four-momentum conservation) provides the time evolution of the proper energy density and the four velocity. Differently from previous prescriptions, the dynamic equations for the pressure corrections are not derived from the zeroth or second moment of the Boltzmann equation, but they are taken directly from the exact evolution given by the Boltzmann equation. We check the effec- tiveness of this new approach by matching with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in the Bjorken limit with the collisional kernel treated in relaxation time approximation, finding an unprecedented agreement.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new "anisotropic matching principle" for hydrodynamics expansions, where the anisotropic background distribution exactly reproduces the complete energy-momentum tensor (generalizing the Landau matching condition), thereby including momentum anisotropies at leading order and eliminating the need for next-to-leading-order corrections to describe large pressure anisotropies.

## Method

The author generalizes the Landau matching condition so that the anisotropic background distribution function (a generalized Romatschke-Strickland form) fixes all seven independent degrees of freedom of the energy-momentum tensor (proper energy density, three components of four-velocity, bulk pressure, and five components of shear stress) at leading order, rather than only the proper energy density as in conventional viscous hydrodynamics. The dynamical equations for the pressure corrections are taken directly from the exact evolution given by the Boltzmann equation (specifically from the first moment for energy-momentum conservation and from the exact convective derivatives of the pressure corrections), rather than from the zeroth or second moments of the Boltzmann equation as in previous anisotropic hydrodynamics approaches. The method is tested by matching with the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in the Bjorken (0+1-dimensional) limit with the collisional kernel treated in the relaxation-time approximation.

## Key numbers

- The paper demonstrates "unprecedented agreement" when matching the new leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics prescription against the exact solution of the Boltzmann equation in the Bjorken limit with the relaxation-time approximation, though no specific numerical values for the agreement are provided in the truncated text.
- The relaxation time is denoted as $\taueq$ (tau_eq), appearing in the collisional kernel $\coll[f] = (1/\taueq)(p\cdot U)[f(x,p) - f_{\rm eq}(x,p)]$.
- The paper references that viscous corrections combined with rapid longitudinal expansion result in substantial pressure asymmetry even at a very low viscosity to entropy density ratio of $4\pi \eta/{\cal S} \sim 1$.

## Context

This work extends the anisotropic hydrodynamics framework developed by Florkowski (2010), Martinez (2010), Ryblewski (2010-2013), and others, as well as the (2+1)-dimensional extension by Bazow, Heinz, and Strickland (2013). It addresses the ambiguity in choosing dynamical equations for anisotropic hydrodynamics, which was previously resolved for ordinary viscous hydrodynamics by Denicol (2014) using moments of the Boltzmann distribution. The new matching principle enables a consistent leading-order treatment that can be systematically extended to next-to-leading order, and its close-to-equilibrium limit reproduces second-order viscous hydrodynamics.
