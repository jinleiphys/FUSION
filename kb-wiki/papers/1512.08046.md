# Surprisingly large uncertainties in temperature extraction from thermal fits to hadron yield data at LHC

> The conventional hadron-resonance gas (HRG) model with the Particle Data Group (PDG) hadron input, full chemical equilibrium, and the hadron type dependent eigenvolume interactions is employed to fit the hadron mid-rapidity yield data of ALICE Collaboration for the most central Pb+Pb collisions. For the case of point-like hadrons the well-known fit result $T = 154 \pm 2$ MeV is reproduced. However, the situation changes if hadrons have different eigenvolumes. In the case when all mesons are point-like while all baryons have an effective hard-core radius of 0.3 fm the $χ^2$ temperature dependence of the $χ^2$ has a broad minimum in the temperature range of $155-210$ MeV, with fit quality comparable to the $T \sim 155$ MeV minimum in the point-particle case. Very similar result is obtained when only baryon-baryon eigenvolume interactions are considered, with eigenvolume parameter taken from previous fit to ground state of nuclear matter. Finally, when we apply the eigenvolume corrections with mass-proportional eigenvolume $v_i \sim m_i$, fixed to particular proton hard-core radius $r_p$, we observe a second minimum in the temperature dependence of the $χ^2$, located at the significantly higher temperatures. For instance, at $r_p = 0.5$ fm the fit quality is better than in the point-particle HRG case in a very wide temperature range of $170-320$ MeV, which gives an uncertainty in the temperature determination from the fit to the data of 150 MeV. These results show that thermal fits to the heavy-ion hadron yield data are very sensitive to the modeling of the short-range repulsion eigenvolume between hadrons, and that chemical freeze-out temperature can be extracted from the LHC hadron yield data only with sizable uncertainty.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out temperature extracted from thermal fits to LHC hadron yield data is subject to a surprisingly large uncertainty (up to 150 MeV) when different modeling assumptions for the short-range repulsive eigenvolume interactions between hadrons are considered, contrary to the narrow 154 ± 2 MeV result obtained in the conventional point-particle hadron-resonance gas model.

## Method

The authors performed thermal fits within the hadron-resonance gas (HRG) model to mid-rapidity hadron yield data from the ALICE Collaboration for 0-5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV. They compared the standard point-particle HRG with three different eigenvolume parametrizations: (1) point-like mesons with baryons of fixed hard-core radius 0.3 fm; (2) mass-proportional eigenvolumes (v_i ∝ m_i) fixed to a proton hard-core radius of 0.5 fm; (3) eigenvolume interactions only for baryon-baryon and antibaryon-antibaryon pairs, with radius 0.59 fm from nuclear matter fits. At each temperature, the system volume per unit rapidity was the only free parameter minimized to obtain χ².

## Key numbers

* Point-particle HRG fit yields T = 154 ± 2 MeV with χ²/N_dof = 30.1/10.
* With point-like mesons and baryons of radius r_B = 0.3 fm, the χ² has a broad minimum in the temperature range 155–210 MeV, with global minimum at T ≈ 163 MeV (χ²/N_dof = 25.6/10).
* With mass-proportional eigenvolume and proton radius r_p = 0.5 fm, the fit quality is better than the point-particle case in the temperature range 170–320 MeV, giving an uncertainty in temperature determination of 150 MeV; the global minimum is at T = 274 MeV (χ²/N_dof = 15.1/10).
* With only baryon-baryon eigenvolume interactions (r_B = 0.59 fm), the minimum occurs around T = 172 MeV (χ²/N_dof = 24.4/10).
* At the global minimum for r_p = 0.5 fm, the system volume radius per unit rapidity is R = 9.8 fm, compared to R = 10.8 fm for the point-particle case.
* The packing fraction at T = 270 MeV for the bag-like model is η ≈ 0.15.

## Context

This work extends the standard HRG thermal fit analyses that consistently yielded T ≈ 155 MeV for LHC data (SHARE, SABR2014, Floris, Becattini 2014) by systematically exploring the sensitivity to eigenvolume interactions. The authors show that the bag-model inspired eigenvolume parametrization (previously used by Hagedorn, Kapusta 1983, and Noronha-Hostler 2009) can produce a second, higher-temperature minimum, and that the two-component eigenvolume model with point-like mesons and finite baryons (compared to lattice QCD by EV-latt-1) gives a broad temperature range. The results indicate that chemical freeze-out temperature cannot be reliably extracted from LHC data until eigenvolume interactions are properly constrained, and suggest that similar sensitivity will appear at higher collision energies (e.g., ALICE at 5.02 TeV).
