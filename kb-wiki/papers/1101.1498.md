# Catalytic Reactions in Heavy-ion Collisions

> We discuss a new type of reactions of a phi meson production on hyperons, pi Y--> phi Y and anti-kaons bar-K N --> phi Y. These reactions are not suppressed according to Okubo-Zweig-Iizuka rule and can be a new efficient source of phi mesons in a nucleus-nucleus collision. We discuss how these reactions can affect the centrality dependence and the rapidity distributions of the phi yield.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that "catalytic reactions" on strange particles (πY → φY and K̄N → φY) can be a new efficient source of φ mesons in nucleus-nucleus collisions, but the authors qualify that these reactions are competitive with conventional ones only at later times in the fireball evolution (t ≳ 0.6 t₀) and depend on the fireball expansion time scaling as t₀/(10 fm).

## Method

The authors calculated tree-level cross sections for the catalytic reactions using an effective Lagrangian where φ mesons couple to strange hadrons via a coupling constant fixed by the φ → KK̄ decay. They then modeled the time evolution of a baryon-dominated fireball with scaling hydrodynamic parameters (temperature, baryon density) and computed φ production rates from catalytic reactions versus conventional πN → φN reactions. Finally, they compared parameterized centrality and rapidity distributions of φ yields against experimental data from Au+Au collisions at 11.7 AGeV (AGS) and In+In and Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV (SPS).

## Key numbers

- Maximal temperature T_m = 130 MeV and maximal density ρ_m = 5 ρ₀ (where ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density) used for fireball parameters corresponding roughly to 6A GeV beam energy
- Fireball expansion time t₀ = 10 fm used for rate calculations; catalytic reaction rates scale by factor t₀/(10 fm)
- Cross section parameterization coefficients for hyperon channels: a_ΛΣ = 4.24, b_ΛΣ = 1.66; a_ΣΣ = 2.16, b_ΣΣ = 0.851; a_ΣΛ = 1.40, b_ΣΛ = 0.682 (units: mb/GeV)
- Cross section parameterization coefficients for kaon channels: a_KΛ ≈ a_KΣ = 2.6, b_KΛ ≈ b_KΣ = 1.2, d_KΛ ≈ d_KΣ = 2.0 (units: mb/GeV)
- Catalytic reaction rates become comparable to conventional πN → φN rates for times ≳ 0.6 t₀
- Number of φ mesons scales as N_φ ∼ a_conv N_pp^(4/3) + a_cat N_pp^(5/3) for conventional and catalytic contributions respectively

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal in Kolomeitsev and Tomasik (2009) for catalytic φ production on strange particles, contrasting with the conventional OZI-suppressed reactions πN → φN and NΔ → φN that dominate transport models (Kämpfer, 2002). The mechanism differs from the strangeness coalescence approach of Ko (1991) (K̄K → φρ and KΛ → φN) because strangeness remains in the system and kaons are not required. The paper addresses the unexpectedly strong φ enhancement observed at beam energies around 2 GeV per nucleon (FOPI, 2003; Agakichev, 2009) and the anomalous rapidity distribution width rise at SPS energies (NA49, 2008) that cannot be explained by K⁺K⁻ → φ alone.
