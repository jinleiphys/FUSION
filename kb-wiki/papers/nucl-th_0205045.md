# Low energy behavior of astrophysical S factor in radiative captures to loosely bound final states

> The low-energy behavior of the astrophysical S-factor for E1 direct radiative captures a(p,gamma)b leading to loosely bound final states (b=a+p) is investigated. We derive a first-order integral representation for S(E) and focus on the properties around zero energy. We show that it is the competition between various effects, namely the remnant Coulomb barrier, the initial and final centrifugal barriers and the binding energy, that defines the behavior of the S(E->0). Contrary to previous findings, we prove that S(E->0) is not determined by the pole corresponding to the bound state. The derivative S'(0) increases with the increase of the centrifugal barrier, while it decreases with the charge of the target. For l_i=l_f+1 the increase of the binding energy of the final nucleus increases the derivative S'(0) while for l_i=l_f-1 the opposite effect is found. We make use of our findings to explain the low energy behavior of the S-factors related to some notorious capture reactions: 7Be(p, gamma)8B, 14N(p,gamma)15O, 16O}(p,gamma)17F, 20Ne(p, gamma)21Na and 22Mg(p, gamma)23Al.

# Low energy behavior of astrophysical S factor in radiative captures to loosely bound final states

## Key claim
Contrary to previous claims by Jennison (1998) that the low-energy rise of the astrophysical S-factor is governed by the pole at negative energy corresponding to the bound state, the authors prove that the pole actually causes the S-factor to decrease as energy approaches zero from above, and any rise must come from higher-order terms in the Whittaker expansion. The actual behavior of S(E) near zero energy is determined by the competition between the remnant Coulomb barrier, the initial and final centrifugal barriers, and the binding energy.

## Method
The authors derive a first-order integral representation for the E1 direct radiative capture S-factor a(p,gamma)b using the asymptotic expansion of the Whittaker function for the bound state and the regular Coulomb function for the scattering state, with the lower integration limit extended to zero. They then analyze the threshold behavior by separating the S-factor into contributions from the first term (containing the pole) and higher-order terms, and derive analytic expressions for S(0) and S'(0) for the two cases l_i = l_f + 1 and l_i = l_f - 1.

## Key numbers
- For the 7Be(p,gamma)8B system with l_i=1, l_f=0, binding energies of 0.06, 0.137, 0.35, and 0.6 MeV produce Coulomb parameters eta_f = 2.4, 1.6, 1.0, and 0.76 respectively, with S'(0) becoming positive only for the largest binding energies
- For l_i=2, l_f=1 captures in the same system, all binding energies from 0.06 to 0.6 MeV produce positive S'(0), with the derivative increasing as binding energy increases
- For l_i=0, l_f=1 captures, all binding energies produce S'(0) < 0, demonstrating that the initial centrifugal barrier is essential for producing a rising S-factor
- The binding energy threshold for the sign change in S'(0) for l_i=1, l_f=0 occurs between epsilon = 0.35 and 0.6 MeV for the 7Be(p,gamma)8B system

## Context
This work extends the analytical treatment of direct capture S-factors beyond the potential model approach of Baye (2000) and the pole-dominance argument of Jennison (1998), providing physical explanations for why different capture reactions show opposite low-energy behaviors. The findings explain the observed S-factor patterns for several astrophysically important reactions including 7Be(p,gamma)8B, 14N(p,gamma)15O, 16O(p,gamma)17F, 20Ne(p,gamma)21Na, and 22Mg(p,gamma)23Al, and enable more reliable extrapolations of measured capture cross sections to stellar energies by identifying which physical parameters control the slope of the S-factor.
