# Particle alignments and shape change in $^{66}$Ge and $^{68}$Ge

> The structure of the $N \approx Z$ nuclei $^{66}$Ge and $^{68}$Ge is studied by the shell model on a spherical basis. The calculations with an extended $P+QQ$ Hamiltonian in the configuration space ($2p_{3/2}$, $1f_{5/2}$, $2p_{1/2}$, $1g_{9/2}$) succeed in reproducing experimental energy levels, moments of inertia and $Q$ moments in Ge isotopes. Using the reliable wave functions, this paper investigates particle alignments and nuclear shapes in $^{66}$Ge and $^{68}$Ge. It is shown that structural changes in the four sequences of the positive- and negative-parity yrast states with even $J$ and odd $J$ are caused by various types of particle alignments in the $g_{9/2}$ orbit. The nuclear shape is investigated by calculating spectroscopic $Q$ moments of the first and second $2^+$ states, and moreover the triaxiality is examined by the constrained Hatree-Fock method. The changes of the first band crossing and the nuclear deformation depending on the neutron number are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that structural changes in the four sequences of positive- and negative-parity yrast states (even J and odd J) in 66Ge and 68Ge are caused by various types of particle alignments in the g9/2 orbit, but the specific backbending spin value differs between the two isotopes (J=10 in 66Ge versus J=8 in 68Ge), and the shell model calculations with an extended P+QQ Hamiltonian reproduce these changes only when the g9/2 single-particle energy is lowered to 2.50 MeV from the value of 3.7 MeV used in prior work.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell model calculations on a spherical basis using an extended P+QQ Hamiltonian (including single-particle energies, monopole corrections, J=0 and J=2 isovector pairing forces, quadrupole-quadrupole force, and octupole-octupole force) in the configuration space (2p3/2, 1f5/2, 2p1/2, 1g9/2). They determined force parameters for 66Ge and applied the same fixed parameters to 68Ge, then calculated energy levels, J-omega graphs, B(E2) and B(M1) transition probabilities, spectroscopic Q moments, and expectation values of proton and neutron numbers in each orbit. Additionally, they used the constrained Hartree-Fock (CHF) method to examine triaxiality and potential energy surfaces.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies used: εp3/2 = 0.00 MeV, εf5/2 = 0.77 MeV, εp1/2 = 1.11 MeV, εg9/2 = 2.50 MeV
- Force strengths for 66Ge: g0 = 0.27(64/A), g2 = 0.0, χ2 = 0.25(64/A)^(5/3) MeV, χ3 = 0.05(64/A)^2 MeV with A=66
- B(E2:2_1+ → 0_1+) for 66Ge: calculated 281 e^2fm^4, experimental 190 ± 36 e^2fm^4
- B(E2:2_1+ → 0_1+) for 68Ge: calculated 278 e^2fm^4, experimental 292 ± 33 e^2fm^4
- B(E2:2_2+ → 2_1+) for 66Ge: calculated 233 e^2fm^4, experimental 269 ± 127 e^2fm^4
- B(E2:2_2+ → 2_1+) for 68Ge: calculated 375 e^2fm^4, experimental 8.0 ± 3.5 e^2fm^4
- B(M1:2_2+ → 2_1+) for 66Ge: calculated 0.0023 μN^2, experimental 0.008 μN^2
- Spectroscopic Q moment for 10_1+ in 66Ge: -70.8 e fm^2
- Spectroscopic Q moment for 2_2+ in 66Ge: +20.9 e fm^2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous shell model study of 64Ge (Kaneko, prior paper) using the same extended P+QQ Hamiltonian approach, and addresses experimental data from Stefanova (for 66Ge) and Ward (for 68Ge) that revealed detailed band structures up to high spins (J ≤ 28). The calculations contradict some results from the particle-rotor model of Lima (which identified the 8_3+ state as continuation of the ground-state band in 68Ge) and disagree with the terminating-state interpretation of Ward for the negative-parity band in 68Ge. The results enable detailed comparison with the excited VAMPIR calculations of Petrovici et al. for 68Ge and provide a framework for understanding band crossings and shape changes in N≈Z Ge isotopes.
