# Isoscalar M1 and E2 Amplitudes in n+p -> d+gamma

> The low energy radiative capture process n+p -> d+gamma provides a sensitive probe of the two-nucleon system. The cross section for this process is dominated by the isovector M1 amplitude for capture from the 1S0 channel via the isovector magnetic moment of the nucleon. In this work we use effective field theory to compute the isoscalar M1 and isoscalar E2 amplitudes that are strongly suppressed for cold neutron capture. The actual value of the isoscalar E2 amplitude is expected to be within 15% of the value computed in this work. In contrast, due to the vanishing contribution of the one-body operator at leading order and next-to-leading order, the isoscalar M1 amplitude is estimated to have a large uncertainty. We discuss in detail the deuteron quadrupole form factor and SD mixing.

## Key claim

The isoscalar E2 amplitude in n+p -> d+gamma is expected to be within approximately 15% of the value computed in this work using effective field theory without pions, while the isoscalar M1 amplitude has a large uncertainty (estimated at roughly 60%) because the one-body operator vanishes at leading and next-to-leading order, making the result sensitive to higher-order contributions.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory without explicit pions (EFT without pions, denoted as nopi) with KSW power counting to compute the isoscalar M1 and isoscalar E2 amplitudes for the radiative capture process n+p -> d+gamma. They computed the S-D mixing parameter epsilon_1 up to N3LO, the deuteron quadrupole form factor up to N2LO, and the isoscalar E2 amplitude up to N2LO, fitting counterterms to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis and the experimental deuteron quadrupole moment.

## Key numbers

- The isoscalar M1 amplitude relative to the dominant isovector M1 amplitude: X_M1S / X_M1V = -5.0 x 10^-4, with an estimated uncertainty of approximately 60%
- The isoscalar E2 amplitude relative to the dominant isovector M1 amplitude: X_E2S / X_M1V = -2.5 x 10^-4, with an estimated uncertainty of approximately 15%
- The isovector E1 amplitude relative to the dominant isovector M1 amplitude at 2200 m/s neutron speed: X_E1V / X_M1V = -1.2 x 10^-4 (scales linearly with incident neutron speed)
- The photon circular polarization in the forward direction: P_gamma = -1.06 x 10^-3, compared to the experimental value P_gamma_expt = -(1.5 +/- 0.3) x 10^-3
- The S-D mixing parameter eta_sd = 0.02543 +/- 0.00007 from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis
- The LO quadrupole moment: mu_Q(LO) = 0.335 fm^2, with the difference from experiment delta_mu_Q = -0.0492 fm^2

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of the two-nucleon system to suppressed amplitudes in n+p -> d+gamma, following earlier EFT calculations of the dominant isovector M1 amplitude by Chen, Rupak, and Savage (1999) and the deuteron properties by the same authors. The calculation was motivated by a challenge from M. Rho for the community to predict these suppressed amplitudes, with parallel work being performed by Park, Kubodera, Min, and Rho using an alternative power counting. The results enable interpretation of upcoming spin-dependent measurements at the ILL reactor facility and are consistent with earlier potential model calculations by Burichenko and Kriplovich using the Reid soft-core potential.
