# Meson spectral functions at nonzero momentum in hot QCD

> We present preliminary results for meson spectral functions at nonzero momentum, obtained from quenched lattice QCD simulations at finite temperature using the Maximal Entropy Method. Twisted boundary conditions are used to have access to many momenta p~T. For light quarks, we observe a drastic modification when heating the system from below to above Tc. In particular, for the vector spectral density we find a nonzero spectral weight at all energies.

# Meson spectral functions at nonzero momentum in hot QCD

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary evidence that meson spectral functions at nonzero momentum undergo a drastic modification when the system is heated from below to above the critical temperature \(T_c\), with the vector spectral density showing nonzero spectral weight at all energies; however, the results are obtained using the Maximal Entropy Method (MEM) on quenched lattice QCD simulations, and the sharp peak near zero energy in the vector channel above \(T_c\) is identified as an artefact of the MEM analysis rather than a physical feature.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on two lattices, one below \(T_c\) (cold: \(48^3 \times 24\), \(\beta = 6.5\), \(T \sim 160\) MeV) and one above \(T_c\) (hot: \(64^3 \times 24\), \(\beta = 7.192\), \(T \sim 420\) MeV), using light staggered quarks with mass \(am = 0.01\) (\(m/T = 0.24\)). Twisted boundary conditions were employed to access many momenta in the range \(0 < p/T < 4\) on the hot lattice, and meson spectral functions were extracted from Euclidean correlators via the Maximal Entropy Method, with a separate MEM analysis on even and odd time slices to handle the staggered fermion signal mixing.

## Key numbers

- Cold lattice: \(48^3 \times 24\), \(\beta = 6.5\), inverse lattice spacing \(a^{-1} \sim 4\) GeV, temperature \(T \sim 160\) MeV, 100 propagators.
- Hot lattice: \(64^3 \times 24\), \(\beta = 7.192\), inverse lattice spacing \(a^{-1} \sim 10\) GeV, temperature \(T \sim 420\) MeV, 100 propagators.
- Critical temperature: \(T_c \approx 270\) MeV.
- Light quark mass: \(am = 0.01\), corresponding to \(m/T = 0.24\).
- Approximately 20 different momenta accessed in the range \(0 < p/T < 4\) on the hot lattice.
- In the cold phase, the relative effect of increasing momentum on the pseudoscalar correlator ratio \(G(\tau, \pv)/G(\tau, \vecnul)\) is up to 45% for the largest momentum shown.
- In the hot phase, the relative effect on the same ratio is up to 15% for the largest momentum shown.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of meson spectral functions at zero momentum (Asakawa 2003, Datta 2003, Umeda 2002, Morrin 2005) and of transport coefficients (Karsch 1986, Aarts 2002, Gupta 2003, Nakamura 2004, Petreczky 2005) by introducing nonzero momentum via twisted boundary conditions, following the formulation of Flynn (2005). The results enable future extraction of the electrical conductivity via the Kubo formula from the slope of the vector spectral function at vanishing energy, and allow comparison with analytical studies of spectral functions in strongly coupled gauge theories using the gauge-gravity duality (Teaney 2006, Kovtun 2006).
