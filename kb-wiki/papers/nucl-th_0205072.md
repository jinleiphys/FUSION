# Fully-Renormalized QRPA fulfills Ikeda sum rule exactly

> The renormalized quasiparticle-RPA is reformulated for even-even nuclei using restrictions imposed by the commutativity of the phonon creation operator with the total particle number operator. This new version, Fully-Renormalized QRPA (FR-QRPA), is free from the spurious low-energy solutions. Analytical proof is given that the Ikeda sum rule is fullfiled within the FR-QRPA.

## Key claim

The authors reformulate the renormalized quasiparticle-RPA (RQRPA) into a Fully-Renormalized QRPA (FR-QRPA) for even-even nuclei by imposing that the phonon creation operator commutes with the total particle number operator; they provide an analytical proof that this version fulfills the Ikeda sum rule exactly, unlike previous RQRPA formulations which violated it.

## Method

The authors start from the general RPA basis and construct the phonon operator using particle creation and annihilation operators (rather than quasiparticle operators), which automatically includes quasiparticle scattering terms without introducing additional degrees of freedom. They then reformulate the RQRPA equations by substituting the standard bifermionic operators \(A^\dagger\) with modified operators \(\bar A^\dagger\) that incorporate these scattering terms, and derive the corresponding renormalization matrix \({\cal U}_{\tau\tau'}\) and the modified forward- and backward-going amplitudes. The FR-QRPA equations are solved with the usual normalization and closure relations, and the Ikeda sum rule is proven analytically using these relations together with modified equations for the chemical potentials that account for quasiparticle occupation numbers in the RQRPA vacuum.

## Key numbers

* The Ikeda sum rule for Gamow-Teller transitions is \(3(N-Z)\); the paper proves this is fulfilled exactly in FR-QRPA.
* For Fermi transitions, the Ikeda sum rule is \(N-Z\); the paper proves this is fulfilled exactly in FR-QRPA.
* The renormalization matrix element is \({\cal D}_{\tau\tau'}=1+\left((u_{\tau'}^2-v_{\tau'}^2){\cal N}_{\tau'}-(u_{\tau}^2-v_{\tau}^2){\cal N}_{\tau}\right)/(v_{\tau'}^2-v_{\tau}^2)\), where \({\cal N}_{\tau}\) is the relative quasiparticle occupation number for level \(\tau\).
* The modified equations for the chemical potentials are \(\langle 0^+_{RPA}|\hat N|0^+_{RPA}\rangle=\sum_n \hat j_n^2(v_n^2+(u_n^2-v_n^2){\cal N}_n)=N\) and similarly for protons with \(Z\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA) developed by Hara (1964), Rowe (1968), and others, which was used in double beta decay studies by Faessler and Simkovic (1998) and others. It directly addresses the violation of the Ikeda sum rule found in previous RQRPA versions by Toivanen and Suhonen (1997), Stoica and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (2000-2001), and Bobyk et al. (2000), as well as the failure of self-consistent QRPA (Delion et al., 1997) and second-QRPA (Stoica and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus, 2001) to fully restore the sum rule. The FR-QRPA also avoids the spurious low-energy solutions that appeared in earlier attempts to include scattering terms (Raduta et al., 1998; Dang and Zelevinsky, 2001). This enables reliable calculations of nuclear matrix elements for double beta decay without the instability and sum-rule violation that plagued previous approaches.
