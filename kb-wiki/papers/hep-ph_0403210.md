# $J^P={1/2}^-$ Pentaquarks in Jaffe and Wilczek's Diquark Model

> If Jaffe and Wilczek's diquark picture for $Θ_5$ pentaquark is correct, there should also exist a $SU_F$(3) pentaquark octet and singlet with no orbital excitation between the diquark pair, hence $J^P={1/2}^-$. These states are lighter than the $Θ_5$ anti-decuplet and lie close to the orbitally excited (L=1) three-quark states in the conventional quark model. We calculate their masses and magnetic moments and discuss their possible strong decays using the chiral Lagrangian formalism. Among them two pentaquarks with nucleon quantum numbers may be narrow. Selection rules of strong decays are derived. We propose the experimental search of these nine additional $J^P={1/2}^-$ baryon states. Especially there are two additional $J^P={1/2}^-$ $Λ$ baryons around $Λ(1405)$. We also discuss the interesting possibility of interpreting $Λ(1405)$ as a pentaquark. The presence of these additional states will provide strong support of the diquark picture for the pentaquarks. If future experimental searches fail, one has to re-evaluate the relevance of this picture for the pentaquarks.

## Key claim

If Jaffe and Wilczek's diquark picture for the Θ₅⁺ pentaquark is correct, there must also exist a SU(3) flavor pentaquark octet and singlet with no orbital excitation between the diquark pair, yielding Jᴾ = 1/2⁻ states that are lighter than the Θ₅⁺ anti-decuplet; the paper explicitly states that if future experimental searches fail to find these states, one must re-evaluate the relevance of the diquark picture for pentaquarks.

## Method

The authors apply Jaffe and Wilczek's diquark model, in which pentaquarks consist of two scalar diquarks (each in color anti-triplet and flavor anti-triplet) plus an antiquark. They construct the flavor wave functions for the octet and singlet arising from the 3_F ⊗ 3̅_F combination of the diquark pair and antiquark, with no orbital excitation (L=0) giving negative parity. Masses are computed using a Hamiltonian with SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking terms, magnetic moments are derived from the antiquark contribution alone, and strong decay modes are analyzed using a chiral Lagrangian with the "fall-apart" decay mechanism.

## Context

This work extends Jaffe and Wilczek's diquark model (Jaffe, 2003) for the Θ₅⁺ pentaquark by predicting a lighter octet and singlet of Jᴾ = 1/2⁻ pentaquarks that have no orbital excitation between diquarks, unlike the positive-parity anti-decuplet. The paper proposes that Λ(1405) could be interpreted as a pentaquark candidate, and that two nucleon-like pentaquarks (p₈, n₈) may be narrow resonances. The results enable experimental searches for nine additional negative-parity baryon states in the 1360–1540 MeV mass range; failure to find them would challenge the diquark picture for pentaquarks.
