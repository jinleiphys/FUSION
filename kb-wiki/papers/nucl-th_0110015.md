# The deuteron: a mini-review

> We review some recent results for elastic electron deuteron scattering (deuteron form factors) and photodisintegration of the deuteron, with emphasis on the recent high energy data from Jefferson Laboratory (JLab).

## Key claim

Nonrelativistic quantum mechanics without exchange currents or relativistic effects is ruled out by the deuteron structure function A(Q²) data at high Q², but even the best relativistic models using only NN degrees of freedom are not entirely satisfactory; there is evidence that new physics (such as the ρπγ exchange current or quark degrees of freedom) begins to appear above Q² of 2–3 GeV².

## Method

The authors compare eight relativistic models (propagator, instant-form, front-form, point-form, and a quark cluster model) for elastic electron-deuteron scattering form factors (structure functions A, B, and T₂₀) against high-Q² data from Jefferson Laboratory. They also review high-energy deuteron photodisintegration cross sections and polarization observables from SLAC and CEBAF experiments at center-of-mass angles from 36° to 90° and photon energies from 0.7 to 5.5 GeV, comparing to meson-baryon and quark-model calculations.

## Key numbers

- Nonrelativistic models are a factor 4 to 8 smaller than the A(Q²) data for Q² > 2 GeV².
- The S- and D-state wave function components of five models (AV18, Paris, CD Bonn, IIB, W16) vary by less than 10% for momenta below about 400 MeV, and by less than a factor of 2 at momenta up to 1 GeV.
- Photodisintegration cross sections dσ/dt at θ_cm = 90° and 69° fall off as s⁻¹¹ (or dσ/dΩ as s⁻¹⁰) above about 1 GeV, consistent with perturbative QCD expectations.
- At forward angles (36° and 52°), cross sections fall off as approximately s⁻⁹ at lower energies, with onset of s⁻¹¹ behavior at about 4 GeV and 3 GeV beam energy respectively, corresponding to perpendicular momentum p_T² ≈ 1 GeV².
- The induced polarization p_y at θ_cm = 90° is consistent with zero above about 1 GeV photon energy.

## Context

This mini-review extends the discussion of deuteron form factors from earlier nonrelativistic potential models (Paris potential, Argonne V18, Nijmegen potentials, CD Bonn) and relativistic models (VGO, PWM, FSR, ARW, CK, LPS, AKP, DB) by comparing them to high-Q² JLab data. It also reviews high-energy photodisintegration data from SLAC experiments NE8 and NE17 and CEBAF experiments E89-012 and E96-003. The work highlights that meson theory works for elastic form factors (where only the NN channel is active) but fails for photodisintegration (where hundreds of N*N* channels are active), while perturbative QCD scaling laws appear qualitatively for photodisintegration but fail for elastic scattering. The results motivate further development of nonperturbative quark models and improved measurements of the B structure function minimum in the Q² range 1.5–4 GeV².
