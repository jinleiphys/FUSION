# Algebraic Models of Hadron Structure II. Strange Baryons

> The algebraic treatment of baryons is extended to strange resonances. Within this framework we study a collective string-like model in which the radial excitations are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of the strings. We derive a mass formula and closed expressions for strong and electromagnetic decay widths and use these to analyze the available experimental data.

## Key claim

The algebraic treatment of baryons, previously applied to nonstrange resonances, is extended to strange baryons using a collective string-like model where radial excitations are interpreted as rotations and vibrations of the strings, under the assumption that spin-flavor symmetry is broken only in a diagonal way through a dynamic symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct a U(7) spectrum generating algebra for the spatial degrees of freedom of three constituent quarks, combined with an SU_sf(6) ⊗ SU_c(3) internal spin-flavor-color algebra. They derive a mass formula incorporating vibrational quantum numbers (v₁, v₂), orbital angular momentum L, and a Gürsey-Radicati spin-flavor operator with Casimir terms for SU_sf(6), SU_f(3), SU_s(2), U_Y(1), and SU_I(2). Strong decay widths are computed using a transition operator for pseudoscalar meson emission, with spatial matrix elements expressed via collective form factors and spin-flavor matrix elements evaluated using SU(3) isoscalar factors.

## Key numbers

- The mass formula fit to 48 three- and four-star resonances yields an r.m.s. deviation of δ = 33 MeV.
- The nucleon mass squared is fixed at M₀² = 0.882 GeV².
- The combined flavor and isospin term for nonstrange baryons has strength b + (1/3)f = 0.031 GeV², close to the previously fitted value of 0.030 GeV² for nonstrange baryons alone.
- The Λ*(1405), Λ*(1520), and Λ*(2100) singlet resonances are overpredicted by 236, 121, and 97 MeV, respectively.
- The lowest four S₁₁ Σ states occur at 1711, 1755, 1822, and 1974 MeV.
- The lowest four D₁₃ Σ states occur at 1711, 1755, 1822, and 1974 MeV as spin-orbit partners of the S₁₁ states.
- A third low-lying P₁₁ state is found at 1740 ± 11 MeV by the Zagreb group; the calculation gives P₁₁ states at 1444, 1683, and 1713 MeV.
- A D₁₃ resonance at 1895 MeV from kaon photoproduction analysis has possible assignments at 1847, 1874, or 1909 MeV in the model.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic U(7) approach of Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan (1994) from nonstrange baryons to hyperons (Σ, Λ, Ξ, Ω families), providing a unified description of both strange and nonstrange resonances. The model reproduces the mass spectrum without needing separate energy shifts for positive and negative parity states, unlike the relativized quark model of Capstick and Isgur (1986). The results enable systematic searches for missing resonances predicted by three-quark models versus quark-diquark models, and provide a framework for analyzing strong and electromagnetic decay data to distinguish between conventional qqq configurations and exotic states such as hybrid qqq-g or multiquark meson-baryon bound states.
