# Upsilon decay widths in magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter

> The in-medium partial decay widths of $Υ(4S) \rightarrow B\bar B$ in magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter are studied using a field theoretic model for composite hadrons with quark (and antiquark) constituents. The medium modifications of the decay widths of $Υ(4S)$ to $B\bar B$ pair in magnetized matter arise due to the mass modifications of the decaying $Υ(4S)$ as well as of the produced $B$ and $\bar B$ mesons. The in-medium masses of the open bottom meson in magnetized nuclear matter are computed from their interactions with the nucleons and the scalar mesons within a chiral effective model. The mass modification of the $Υ(4S)$ arises due to the medium modification of a scalar dilaton field, which is introduced in the model to simulate the gluon condensates of QCD. The charged $B^{\pm}$ mesons have additional contributions from the Landau energy levels, leading to positive shifts in their masses in the presence of a magnetic field. In the presence of an external magnetic field, there are contributions to the masses of the $B$, $\bar B$ mesons and $Υ(4S)$ state (longitudinal component) due to the pseudoscalar meson-vector meson (PV) mixing ($B-B^*$, $\bar B- \bar {B^*}$ and $Υ(4S)-η_b(4S)$ mixings), which are also considered in the present study. The PV mixing effects are observed to be the dominant contributions to the mass shifts of these mesons, which lead to appreciable modifications in the decay widths of $Υ(4S)$ to the neutral ($B^0 \bar {B^0}$) and the charged ($B^+ B^-$) pairs in the presence of a magnetic field. These should have observable consequence in the production of open bottom mesons and bottomonium states at LHC and RHIC, where huge magnetic fields are produced in ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The partial decay widths of Υ(4S) → B\bar{B} in magnetized asymmetric nuclear matter are significantly modified by pseudoscalar-vector meson (PV) mixing effects, which dominate over other medium modifications and produce appreciable differences between the charged and neutral decay channels, with observable consequences for heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC.

## Method

The in-medium masses of B and \bar{B} mesons were computed from their interactions with nucleons and scalar mesons within a chiral effective model, including Landau energy level contributions for charged B± mesons and PV mixing (B-B*, \bar{B}-\bar{B}*, and Υ(4S)-ηb(4S) mixing) effects. The mass modification of Υ(4S) was calculated from the medium change of a scalar dilaton field that simulates the gluon condensates of QCD. The partial decay widths were then computed using a field theoretical model for composite hadrons with explicit quark and antiquark constituent operators, with the decay amplitude multiplied by a strength parameter fitted to the vacuum partial decay width of Υ(4S).

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field at RHIC is estimated to be eB ∼ 2 mπ² and at LHC eB ∼ 15 mπ²
- The mass of charged B± mesons including lowest Landau level contribution is given by m_eff(B±) = sqrt((m*_B±)² + |eB|)
- The PV mixing mass shift ΔM_PV = (ΔE/2)((1+Δ²)^(1/2)-1), where Δ = 2g|eB|((q1/m1)-(q2/m2))/ΔE and ΔE = mV - mP
- The decay width formula for Υ(4S) → B0\bar{B}0 is Γ = γ_Υ² (8π²/3) |p|³ (p0_B0 p0_\bar{B}0 / m_Υ(4S)) A_Υ(4S)(|p|)²

## Context

This work extends previous studies of heavy flavor mesons in magnetized matter using chiral effective models (Mishra et al., various years) and the field theoretical composite hadron model (Misra, 1978-1982), which had been applied to charmonium decay to D\bar{D} in magnetic fields. The results enable predictions for open bottom meson and bottomonium production in ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC, where the time evolution of the magnetic field remains an open question.
