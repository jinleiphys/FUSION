# Decomposition of EOS of Asymmetric Nuclear Matter into Different Spin-isospin Channels

> We investigate the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter and its isospin dependence in various spin-isospin $ST$ channels within the framework of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach extended to include a microscopic three-body force (TBF). It is shown that the potential energy per nucleon in the isospin-singlet T=0 channel is mainly determined by the contribution from the tensor SD coupled channel. At high densities, the TBF effect on the isospin-triplet T=1 channel contribution turns out to be much larger than that on the T=0 channel contribution. At low densities around and below the normal nuclear matter density, the isospin dependence is found to come essentially from the isospin-singlet SD channel and the isospin-triplet T=1 component is almost independent of isospin-asymmetry. As the density increases, the T=1 channel contribution becomes sensitive to the isospin-asymmetry and at high enough densities its isospin-dependence may even become more pronounced than that of the T=0 contribution. The present results may provide some microscopic constraints for improving effective nucleon-nucleon interactions in nuclear medium and for constructing new functionals of effective nucleon-nucleon interaction based on microscopic many-body theories.

## Key claim

The isospin dependence of the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter arises primarily from the isospin-singlet tensor SD coupled channel at low densities, but at high enough densities (around 0.4 fm⁻³) the isospin-triplet T=1 channel contribution may become even more sensitive to isospin asymmetry than the T=0 channel, due to the inclusion of a microscopic three-body force.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G-matrix within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach, using the Argonne V18 two-body interaction plus a microscopic three-body force (TBF) based on meson-exchange currents. The TBF was reduced to an effective density-dependent two-body interaction by averaging over the third nucleon degrees of freedom, with the defect function determined self-consistently. The potential energy per nucleon was decomposed into partial wave channels labeled by total spin S and total isospin T (ST = 00, 10, 01, 11) and into the T=0 and T=1 channels.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- At densities up to about 0.25 fm⁻³, the attraction of the potential energy in both T=0 and T=1 channels increases monotonically with density
- In the high-density region 0.25 ≤ ρ ≤ 0.4 fm⁻³, the T=0 channel potential energy becomes almost independent of density in both cases (with and without TBF)
- At ρ = 0.4 fm⁻³, the isospin dependence of the T=1 channel potential energy may become more pronounced than that of the T=0 channel when the TBF is included
- The kinetic part of the energy per nucleon increases monotonically with density as ρ^(2/3)

## Context

This work extends the microscopic BHF calculations of Zuo (2002, 2005) by decomposing the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter into individual spin-isospin channels, providing more detailed constraints than the total EOS alone. The results differ from those of Baldo et al. (2007), who used the phenomenological Urbana TBF and obtained different density dependences for the ST=10 and ST=11 components. The decomposition is intended to guide improvements to effective Skyrme-type interactions, which have been shown by Lesinski et al. (2006, 2007) to deviate significantly from microscopic results when decomposed into spin-isospin channels, even when they reproduce the total EOS.
