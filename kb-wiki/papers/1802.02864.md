# Gauge Invariant Noether's Theorem and The Proton Spin Crisis

> Due to proton spin crisis it is necessary to understand the gauge invariant definition of the spin and orbital angular momentum of the quark and gluon from first principle. In this paper we derive the gauge invariant Noether's theorem by using combined Lorentz transformation plus local gauge transformation. We find that the notion of the gauge invariant definition of the spin (or orbital) angular momentum of the electromagnetic field does not exist in Dirac-Maxwell theory although the notion of the gauge invariant definition of the spin (or orbital) angular momentum of the electron exists. We find that the gauge invariant definition of the spin angular momentum of the electromagnetic field in the literature is not correct because of the non-vanishing surface term in Dirac-Maxwell theory although the corresponding surface term vanishes for linear momentum. We also show that the Belinfante-Rosenfeld tensor is not required to obtain symmetric and gauge invariant energy momentum tensor of the electron and the electromagnetic field in Dirac-Maxwell theory.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a gauge-invariant definition of the spin (or orbital) angular momentum of the electromagnetic field does not exist in Dirac-Maxwell theory, contrary to claims in the literature, because a non-vanishing surface term prevents the separation of total angular momentum into gauge-invariant spin and orbital contributions for the photon field, although such a separation is possible for the electron.

## Method

The author derives a gauge-invariant Noether's theorem by applying a combined Lorentz transformation plus local gauge transformation to the Dirac-Maxwell Lagrangian density, rather than using the Lorentz transformation alone. The functional differentials of the fields are modified to include a gauge parameter that cancels the gauge-dependent terms, yielding gauge-invariant conserved currents. The resulting expressions for the energy-momentum tensor and angular momentum tensor are then compared with the standard gauge-non-invariant results obtained from the ordinary Noether procedure.

## Key numbers

- The total quark spin contribution to the proton spin is approximately 1/6, and the total gluon helicity contribution is approximately 1/10, based on experimental measurements cited in the paper.
- The RHIC experiment at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair sqrt(s_NN) = 510 GeV in polarized proton-proton collisions is referenced as providing recent spin measurements.
- The spin of the proton at rest is 1/2, and its helicity when in motion is conserved with quantized value ±1/2.

## Context

This work addresses the proton spin crisis, which began with the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) experiment at CERN showing that the total quark contribution to the proton spin is nearly zero, contradicting the naive parton model prediction. The paper extends prior attempts by Jaffe and Manohar (1990), Ji (1997), and others to define gauge-invariant spin and orbital angular momentum for gluons, but finds that the proposed gauge-invariant definitions of photon spin angular momentum in the literature are incorrect due to a non-vanishing surface term. The result implies that current experimental measurements of the spin-dependent gluon distribution function at RHIC and future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) lack a first-principles gauge-invariant definition in QCD.
