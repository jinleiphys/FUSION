# Polar polarization: a new method for polarimetry analysis

> We present a novel analysis method for measurements of polarization transferred in $A(\vec{e},e'\vec{N})$ experiments, which can be applied to other kinds of polarization measurements as well. In this method the polarization transfer components are presented in spherical coordinates using an efficient likelihood numerical maximization based on an analytic derivation. We also propose a formalism that accounts for multi-parameter models, and which yields a smooth and continuous representation of the data (rather than using standard binning). Applying this method on simulated data generates results with reduced statistical and systematic uncertainties and enables revealing physical information that is lost in standard binning of the data. The obtained results can be compared easily to theoretical models and other measurements. Furthermore, CPU time is significantly reduced using this method.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new analysis method for polarization-transfer measurements in \(A(\vec{e},e'\vec{N})\) experiments that uses spherical coordinates and a continuous (unbinned) representation of data, claiming reduced statistical and systematic uncertainties and significantly reduced CPU time compared to standard Cartesian binning methods, though the method is demonstrated only on simulated data.

## Method

The authors derive maximum likelihood estimators for polarization components in spherical coordinates (magnitude \(P\), polar angle \(\vartheta\), azimuthal angle \(\varphi\)) using an analytic linear approximation of the log-likelihood function as a starting point for Newton-Raphson numerical optimization. They extend the formalism to handle polarization that varies with a kinematic variable by implementing piecewise continuous linear interpolation and cubic spline interpolation weighting schemes, replacing the traditional discrete binning approach.

## Key numbers

- The linear approximation of the log-likelihood function is expanded up to \(\mathcal{O}(\gamma_k^3)\) where \(|\gamma_k| < 1\).
- One Newton-Raphson step (eq. P1) is sufficient to reach the absolute maximum of the likelihood function, limited only by the computer's precision.
- The derivatives required for the numerical optimization can be derived analytically, reducing computation time by approximately two orders of magnitude.
- The ratio \(R \equiv P_x/P_z\) in elastic electron-proton scattering is proportional to the ratio of electric to magnetic form factors: \(R_{^1\text{H}} = -\frac{2M_p}{(E+E')\tan(\theta_e/2)} \cdot \frac{G_E^p(Q^2)}{G_M^p(Q^2)}\).

## Context

This work extends the standard maximum likelihood method for polarization extraction from focal plane polarimeter data (Besset 1979) and addresses limitations of Cartesian coordinate analysis and arbitrary binning used in prior theses (Pospischil, Doria, Degrush) and experiments (A1 Recoil Polarization 2007, Doria 2015, Samo 2017). The method enables direct extraction of the polar angle \(\vartheta\) (whose cotangent relates to \(G_E/G_M\) similarly to \(R\)) with unbiased and nearly normal distributions, and allows continuous comparison of experimental results with theoretical models without binning artifacts.
