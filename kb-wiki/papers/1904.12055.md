# Short Range Operator Contributions to $0νββ$ decay from LQCD

> The search for neutrinoless double beta decay of nuclei is believed to be one of the most promising means to search for new physics. Observation of this very rare nuclear process, which violates Lepton Number conservation, would imply the neutrino sector has a Majorana mass component and may also provide an explanation for the universe matter-antimatter asymmetry of the universe. In the case where a heavy intermediate particle is exchanged in this process, QCD contributions from short range interactions become relevant and the calculation of matrix elements with four-quark operators becomes necessary. In these proceedings we will discuss our current progress in the calculation of these four-quark operators from LQCD.

## Key claim

The paper reports progress on lattice QCD calculations of the short-range four-quark operator matrix elements that contribute to neutrinoless double beta decay, specifically for the $\pi^- \rightarrow \pi^+$ process, but notes that the full $nn \rightarrow pp$ amplitude calculation is significantly more challenging and may be necessary due to a potential breakdown of effective field theory power counting.

## Method

The authors compute three-point correlation functions on the lattice with the four-quark operator inserted between two pion interpolating fields, using $N_f=2+1+1$ HISQ gauge configurations from the MILC collaboration with Möbius Domain Wall valence fermions. From these correlation functions, ratio functions are formed to extract the bare matrix elements, which are then renormalized and extrapolated to the continuum, infinite volume, and physical pion mass limits. For the more challenging two-nucleon contact interaction, the authors are exploring a Feynman-Hellmann method combined with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to compute matrix elements more efficiently.

## Key numbers

* The matrix elements are renormalized and expressed in the $\overline{MS}$-scheme at a scale of 3 GeV.
* For operator $O_1$ in the $\overline{MS}$ scheme: $-0.0193(14)$ [GeV]$^4$
* For operator $O_1^\prime$ in the $\overline{MS}$ scheme: $-0.0785(59)$ [GeV]$^4$
* For operator $O_2$ in the $\overline{MS}$ scheme: $-0.0356(30)$ [GeV]$^4$
* For operator $O_2^\prime$ in the $\overline{MS}$ scheme: $0.0109(10)$ [GeV]$^4$
* For operator $O_3$ in the $\overline{MS}$ scheme: $0.000179(08)$ [GeV]$^4$

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculations of short-range contributions to $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay, which have received less attention than the light Majorana exchange mechanism. The leading short-range operators were identified by Prezeau (2003) and Graesser (2016), and the present results build on earlier lattice calculations by Nicholson (2016, 2018). The computed $\pi^- \rightarrow \pi^+$ matrix elements can be used to construct $nn \rightarrow pp e^- e^-$ potentials for many-body nuclear calculations, while the ongoing work on the $nn \rightarrow pp$ amplitude addresses a potential breakdown of chiral EFT power counting identified by Cirigliano (2018).
