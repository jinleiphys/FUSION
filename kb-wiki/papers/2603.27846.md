# Full energy fraction and angular dependence of medium-induced splittings in the large-$N_c$ limit

> Jets produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are modified by their interactions with the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), making jet substructure observables sensitive probes of QGP dynamics. A quantitative description of these modifications requires understanding how the medium affects elementary parton splittings with full dependence on both their energy fraction $z$ and splitting angle $θ$, beyond the widely used soft emitted-gluon approximation. Here, we study medium-induced $1 \to 2$ splittings double-differential in $z$ and $θ$, with full resummation of multiple scatterings, and show that in the large-$N_c$ limit and under the harmonic oscillator (HO) approximation, all path integrals can be evaluated analytically for any splitting channel, providing a computationally efficient semi-analytical result. We also revisit the semi-hard approximation (SHA), extending it to include leading corrections in inverse powers of the partons energies, which we denote the improved semi-hard approximation (ISHA), and assess its validity through a comparison with the large-$N_c$-HO results. Our analysis shows that while the SHA is found to be unreliable across most of phase space, even for high-energy emitters, the ISHA provides a robust approximation for splittings where all partons are sufficiently energetic.

## Key claim

The authors derive fully analytic expressions for medium-induced $1 \to 2$ parton splittings double-differential in energy fraction $z$ and splitting angle $\theta$ under the large-$N_c$ limit and harmonic oscillator approximation, but only for the case where all partons involved are sufficiently energetic; the previously used semi-hard approximation (SHA) is shown to be unreliable across most of phase space, while the improved semi-hard approximation (ISHA) provides a robust alternative when all partons are energetic.

## Method

The authors work within the BDMPS-Z framework, resumming multiple scatterings via path integrals over transverse trajectories in a background field, with medium averages taken using a dipole cross section that is local in time. They evaluate all path integrals analytically in the large-$N_c$ limit under the harmonic oscillator (HO) approximation for any splitting channel, reducing the double-differential splitting spectrum to expressions involving only two time integrals that are straightforward to evaluate numerically. They also derive the improved semi-hard approximation (ISHA) by adding leading finite-energy corrections (in inverse powers of parton energies) to the existing semi-hard approximation (SHA), and compare both approximations against the exact large-$N_c$-HO results.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum spectrum is recovered for emissions occurring entirely outside the medium: $\frac{dI^{\text{out-out}}}{dz d^2\vec{l}} = \frac{\alpha_s}{2\pi^2 \vec{l}^2} P_{a\to bc}(z)$.
- The splitting angle is related to relative transverse momentum by $\theta = |\vec{l}| / (z(1-z)E)$.
- The vertex factor for all splitting channels contains the combination $\vec{l} = \vec{k}_1 - z\vec{p}_1 = (1-z)\vec{k}_1 - z\vec{q}_1$ and the Altarelli-Parisi splitting function $P_{a\to bc}(z)$.
- The medium length is denoted $L$, with time integrations separated into in-in ($t_1 < t_2 < L$), in-out ($t_1 < L < t_2$), and out-out ($L < t_1 < t_2$) regions.

## Context

This work extends the formal derivation of double-differential in-medium splitting spectra from Blaizot et al. (2012) and Apolinario et al. (2014) by providing explicit analytic evaluations in the large-$N_c$ limit under the harmonic oscillator approximation, which was previously only available for the $\gamma \to q\bar{q}$ channel in Isaksen et al. (2023) and only for static media up to 2 fm. The semi-hard approximation (SHA) was introduced by Dominguez et al. (2019) and Isaksen et al. (2020) for use beyond the harmonic oscillator, but Isaksen et al. (2023) found it overestimates the spectrum; the ISHA corrects this by including finite-energy corrections. The results enable efficient semi-analytical calculations of jet substructure observables such as energy correlators, as previously pursued by Andres et al. (2022, 2023) and Barata et al. (2023, 2024) using the SHA.
