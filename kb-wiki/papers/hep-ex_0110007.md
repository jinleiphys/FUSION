# $Q^2$ Dependence of Quadrupole Strength in the $纬^*p\to螖^+(1232)\to p 蟺^0$ Transition

> Models of baryon structure predict a small quadrupole deformation of the nucleon due to residual tensor forces between quarks or distortions from the pion cloud. Sensitivity to quark versus pion degrees of freedom occurs through the $Q^2$ dependence of the magnetic ($M_{1+}$), electric ($E_{1+}$), and scalar ($S_{1+}$) multipoles in the $纬^* p \to 螖^+ \to p 蟺^0$ transition. We report new experimental values for the ratios $E_{1+}/M_{1+}$ and $S_{1+}/M_{1+}$ over the range $Q^2$= 0.4-1.8 GeV$^2$, extracted from precision $p(e,e 'p)蟺^{\circ}$ data using a truncated multipole expansion. Results are best described by recent unitary models in which the pion cloud plays a dominant role.

## Key claim
The paper reports new experimental values for the ratios \(E_{1+}/M_{1+}\) and \(S_{1+}/M_{1+}\) over \(Q^2 = 0.4-1.8\) GeV\(^2\), extracted from precision \(p(e,e'p)\pi^0\) data using a truncated multipole expansion. The results are best described by recent unitary models in which the pion cloud plays a dominant role, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the truncated expansion introduces systematic truncation errors of up to 0.7% for \(R_{EM}\) and 0.5% for \(R_{SM}\), and that no single quark or chiral soliton model successfully describes both ratios simultaneously.

## Method
The CLAS Collaboration measured the \(p(e,e'p)\pi^0\) reaction at two beam energies (1.645 and 2.445 GeV) using the CEBAF Large Acceptance Spectrometer at Jefferson Lab. A partial wave analysis of the center-of-mass differential cross sections was performed, extracting the structure functions \(\sigma_T + \epsilon_L \sigma_L\), \(\sigma_{TT}\), and \(\sigma_{LT}\) from fits to the \(\phi^*_\pi\) and \(\cos\theta^*_\pi\) distributions. The ratios \(R_{EM} = \text{Re}(E_{1+} M_{1+}^*)/|M_{1+}|^2\) and \(R_{SM} = \text{Re}(S_{1+} M_{1+}^*)/|M_{1+}|^2\) were then obtained from the Legendre coefficients \(A_2\), \(C_0\), and \(D_1\) using a truncated multipole expansion that retains only terms involving \(M_{1+}\).

## Key numbers
- \(R_{EM} = -3.4 \pm 0.4 \pm 0.4\%\) at \(Q^2 = 0.40\) GeV\(^2\) and beam energy 1.645 GeV.
- \(R_{SM} = -5.6 \pm 0.4 \pm 0.6\%\) at \(Q^2 = 0.40\) GeV\(^2\) and beam energy 1.645 GeV.
- \(R_{EM} = -0.9 \pm 1.1 \pm 0.7\%\) at \(Q^2 = 1.80\) GeV\(^2\) and beam energy 2.445 GeV.
- \(R_{SM} = -11.6 \pm 1.6 \pm 1.5\%\) at \(Q^2 = 1.80\) GeV\(^2\) and beam energy 2.445 GeV.
- The overall \(R_{EM}\) is approximately \(-2\%\) across the measured \(Q^2\) range, showing no significant \(Q^2\) dependence.
- The truncation error (including model dependence) was typically 0.3% (worst 0.7%) for \(R_{EM}\) and 0.1% (worst 0.5%) for \(R_{SM}\) over the \(Q^2\) range.

## Context
This work extends previous measurements of the \(\gamma^* N \to \Delta(1232)\) quadrupole transition, which had large systematic and statistical errors or limited kinematic coverage, including photo-pion measurements from LEGS (Blanpied et al., 2000) and MAMI (Beck et al., 2001) at \(Q^2 = 0\), and electroproduction data from JLAB/Hall C (Frolov et al., 1999) at higher \(Q^2\). The results enable tests of dynamical models such as Sato-Lee (2001) and Dubna-Mainz-Taipei (Kamalov et al., 2001), which incorporate non-resonant meson exchange and pion rescattering, and provide constraints for chiral perturbation theory (Gellas et al., 1999) and lattice QCD calculations at low \(Q^2\).
