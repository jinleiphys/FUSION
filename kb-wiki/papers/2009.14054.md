# Cone-size dependent jet spectrum in heavy-ion collisions

> Jets in the vacuum correspond to multi-parton configurations that form via a branching process sensitive to the soft and collinear divergences of QCD. In heavy-ion collisions, energy loss processes that are stimulated via interactions with the medium, affect jet observables in a profound way. Jet fragmentation factorizes into a three-stage process, involving vacuum-like emissions above the medium scale, induced emissions enhanced by the medium length and, finally, long-distance vacuum-like fragmentation. This formalism leads to a novel, non-linear resummation of jet energy loss. In this talk we present new results on the combined effects of small-$R$ resummation and energy loss to compute the $R$-dependent jet spectrum in heavy-ion collisions.

# Cone-size dependent jet spectrum in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper presents a formalism that combines small-$R$ resummation (DGLAP evolution) with a non-linear resummation of jet energy loss to compute the $R$-dependent jet spectrum in heavy-ion collisions, finding a weak $R$-dependence of the nuclear modification factor that arises from color coherence effects, only splittings at angles larger than the medium scale $\theta_c$ are resolved and affect the cone-size dependence.

## Method

The authors factorize jet fragmentation into three stages: vacuum-like emissions above the medium scale, medium-induced emissions enhanced by the medium length, and long-distance vacuum-like fragmentation. They merge two angular-ordered showers via a generating functional approach, where the single-parton quenching factor $Q^{(0)}(\nu)$ acts as an initial condition for the complete resummed quenching factor of the jet. The initial hard spectrum was matched to PYTHIA8 simulations at $R=1$ with EPS09 LO nPDFs for LHC conditions and evolved using DGLAP evolution equations to the measured jet cone size, with medium parameters fixed to describe existing data on jet suppression at $R=0.4$.

## Key numbers

- Medium length fixed to $L = 3.5$ fm and jet quenching parameter $\hat q = 1.5$ GeV$^2$/fm, chosen to describe existing data on jet suppression at $R=0.4$
- The phase space for vacuum-like emissions on time-scales shorter than the medium resolution time is given by $2 \frac{\alpha_s C_R}{\pi} \ln\frac{R}{\theta_c} \left( \ln\frac{\pT}{\omega_c} +\frac{2}{3} \ln \frac{R}{\theta_c}\right)$, with $\omega_s \sim \hat q L^2$ and $\theta_c \sim (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}$
- The error bands in the computed nuclear modification factor correspond to varying the parameter $\theta_c$ by a factor of 2

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Mehtar-Tani (2017) on the impact of vacuum-like emissions in the medium on the inclusive jet spectrum, and the factorization approach of Caucal (2018). It enables further constraint of medium transport properties by combining jet substructure observables with the inclusive jet spectrum, and addresses the multi-prong structure of jets interacting with the quark-gluon plasma, going beyond single-parton quenching models.
