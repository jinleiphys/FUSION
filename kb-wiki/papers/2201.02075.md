# How well do we know the gluon polarization in the proton?

> We perform the first simultaneous global QCD analysis of spin-averaged and spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs), including single jet production data from unpolarized and polarized hadron collisions. We critically assess the impact of SU(3) flavor symmetry and PDF positivity assumptions on the quark and gluon helicity PDFs, and find strong bias from these, particularly on the gluon polarization. The simultaneous analysis allows for the first time extraction of individual helicity-aligned and antialigned PDFs with a consistent treatment of uncertainties.

## Key claim

The authors find that existing polarized data allow solutions containing negative gluon polarization in addition to the standard positive gluon solutions found in previous analyses, giving equally acceptable descriptions of the data, particularly when SU(3) flavor symmetry and PDF positivity assumptions are not imposed.

## Method

The authors performed the first simultaneous global QCD analysis of spin-averaged and spin-dependent parton distribution functions (PDFs) at next-to-leading order, fitting inclusive deep-inelastic scattering data, Drell-Yan lepton-pair production data, and inclusive jet production data from both unpolarized and polarized hadron collisions. They used a Bayesian Monte Carlo methodology with a multi-step strategy to explore the highly dimensional parameter space, and systematically compared scenarios with and without SU(3) flavor symmetry constraints and positivity assumptions on the PDFs.

## Key numbers

* The analysis used 2680 unpolarized and 365 polarized inclusive DIS data points, with cuts of W² > 10 GeV² and Q² > m_c²
* The Drell-Yan dataset from Fermilab E866 included 250 data points after applying a cut of M_{ℓ+ℓ-} > 6 GeV
* For jet production, the authors included data with jet transverse momenta p_T > 8 GeV from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC, and from D0 and CDF at the Tevatron
* The strong coupling was fixed at α_s(M_Z) = 0.118 at the Z-boson mass scale, with PDF evolution starting at input scale μ_0 = 1.27 GeV
* The total parameter space explored was approximately 80 parameters, including 30 for spin-averaged PDFs, 18 for spin-dependent PDFs, and 33 normalizations

## Context

This work extends the Jefferson Lab Angular Momentum (JAM) Collaboration's previous advances in simultaneous Bayesian Monte Carlo determination of co-dependent distributions, such as PDFs and fragmentation functions (Ethier 2017, Sato 2019, Moffat 2021), and polarized and unpolarized PDFs (Cocuzza 2021). The analysis critically assesses the theoretical assumptions made in previous global analyses by de Florian et al. (2014) and Nocera et al. (2014), particularly the imposition of SU(3) flavor symmetry and positivity constraints. The simultaneous analysis enables for the first time extraction of individual helicity-aligned and antialigned PDFs with a consistent treatment of uncertainties, and the authors conclude that further input from higher-precision measurements is needed to draw firmer conclusions about gluon polarization and its contribution to the proton spin budget.
