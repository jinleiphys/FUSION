# Dihadron azimuthal correlations at large pseudo-rapidity difference in multiplicity-selected d+Au collisions by STAR

> The long-range pseudo-rapidity correlations observed in pp and p+Pb collisions at the LHC have stimulated extensive interest. The tantalizing observation of similar effect by PHENIX calls for careful examination of the RHIC d+Au data. In this talk, we present dihadron correlations in multiplicity-selected d+Au collisions by STAR, in both $Δϕ$ and $Δη$ and from both TPC and FTPC.

## Key claim

The long-range pseudo-rapidity correlations ("ridge") observed in d+Au collisions at RHIC, when examined via the "central minus peripheral" difference technique, appear to be mainly consistent with jet phenomenology and likely caused by centrality biases rather than requiring new physics such as hydrodynamic flow or color glass condensate effects.

## Method

The STAR Collaboration analyzed dihadron correlations in minimum-bias d+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV using approximately 10 million events from the 2003 run. Two sets of correlations were formed: TPC-TPC correlations with both particles within |η| < 1, and TPC-FTPC correlations with trigger particles from TPC and associated particles from forward TPCs covering |η| = 1.8-4.8. The correlations were analyzed both by subtracting a uniform combinatorial background using the Zero-Yield-At-Minimum (ZYAM) assumption and by decomposing into Fourier series coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Approximately 10 million minimum-bias d+Au events were used, with primary vertex position |z_vtx| < 30 cm from the TPC center
- Trigger and associated particle pT ranges are both 1 < pT < 3 GeV/c
- Tracking efficiency corrections: 85% ± 4% (syst.) for TPC associated particles, 70% ± 4% (syst.) for FTPC associated particles
- Gaussian+constant fits to near-side correlations indicate a 20% difference in Gaussian area between central and peripheral collisions
- The first harmonic Fourier coefficient V1 varies approximately as (dN/dη)^(-1)
- The second harmonic Fourier coefficient V2 is finite at forward/backward rapidity of |Δη| ≈ 3

## Context

This work extends the investigation of long-range pseudo-rapidity correlations first observed in pp collisions by CMS (2010) and p+Pb collisions by CMS, ALICE, and ATLAS at the LHC, following the PHENIX observation (2013) of a double ridge in d+Au collisions using the same "central minus peripheral" technique. The STAR results with larger acceptance (|Δη| < 2 from TPC, and up to |Δη| = 4.8 with FTPC) enable detailed investigation of centrality biases that may explain the observed differences, and the next step is to quantify these biases to potentially isolate contributions unrelated to jets.
