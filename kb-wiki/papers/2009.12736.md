# Unified description of the coupled-channels and statistical Hauser-Feshbach nuclear reaction theories for low energy neutron incident reactions

> We incorporate the coupled-channels optical model into the statistical Hauser-Feshbach nuclear reaction theory, where the scattering matrix is diagonalized by performing the Engelbrecht-Weidenmüller transformation. This technique has been implemented in the coupled-channels optical model code ECIS by J. Raynal, and we extend this method so that all the open channels in a nucleon-induced reaction on a deformed nucleus can be calculated consistently.

# Unified description of the coupled-channels and statistical Hauser-Feshbach nuclear reaction theories for low energy neutron incident reactions

## Key claim

The paper presents a unified framework that incorporates the coupled-channels optical model into the statistical Hauser-Feshbach theory via the Engelbrecht-Weidenmüller transformation, but the author qualifies that the approach is limited to low-energy neutron-induced reactions where compound elastic scattering plays a more important role than in charged-particle cases, and that the width fluctuation correction must be handled carefully when off-diagonal S-matrix elements are non-negligible.

## Method

The author implemented the Engelbrecht-Weidenmüller transformation in the CoH₃ code to diagonalize the coupled-channels S-matrix, producing a set of single-channel transmission coefficients in a transformed channel space. The width fluctuation correction is then calculated in this diagonalized space using the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble model, and the cross sections are transformed back to the original channel space. The method was applied to neutron-induced reactions on deformed nuclei ²³⁸U and ¹⁶⁹Tm, comparing results obtained with generalized transmission coefficients from the coupled-channels S-matrix against the conventional approximation of using energy-shifted ground-state transmission coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For a 100-keV neutron induced reaction on ²³⁸U, the generalized transmission coefficient gives an inelastic scattering cross section of 553 mb to the 45-keV 2⁺ level; using the energy-shifted ground-state approximation gives 488 mb, which is 9% smaller and closer to the JENDL-4 evaluated value of 461 mb.
- The difference between the Engelbrecht-Weidenmüller transformation and the single-channel width fluctuation correction for ²³⁸U is at most 15%.
- For ²³⁸U, the coupled levels included are at 0, 45, 148, 307, and 518 keV (0⁺ to 8⁺) in the ground state rotational band.
- For ¹⁶⁹Tm, the coupled levels are at 0, 8.4, 118, 139, and 332 keV from (1/2)⁺ to (9/2)⁺.
- The p-wave transmission coefficient difference between the generalized and approximated methods for ²³⁸U reaches about 10%.

## Context

This work extends the earlier implementation by J. Raynal in the ECIS code, which was limited to binary reactions only, to a full multi-stage Hauser-Feshbach framework. The paper directly addresses approximations made by existing Hauser-Feshbach codes such as EMPIRE, TALYS, and CCONE, which replace transmission coefficients for excited states with energy-shifted ground-state values and ignore direct reaction effects in the statistical theory. The results enable more accurate predictions of neutron inelastic scattering cross sections for deformed nuclei, particularly for applications such as nuclear data evaluation where the code comparison by Capote (2017) revealed systematic differences of about 10% at 100 keV for ²³⁸U.
