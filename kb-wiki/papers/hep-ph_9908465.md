# Constraints on Neutrino Mixing

> We explore the implications of imposing the constraint that two neutrino flavors (which for definiteness we take to be the mu and tau neutrinos) are similarly coupled to the mass basis in addition to the unitarity constraints. We allow three active and an arbitrary number of sterile neutrinos. We show that in this scheme one of the mass eigenstates decouples from the problem, reducing the dimension of the flavor space by one.

## Key claim

Imposing the constraint that muon and tau neutrinos are similarly coupled to the mass basis (proportional mixing for all but one mass eigenstate), together with unitarity, forces one mass eigenstate to decouple from all other flavors, reducing an N-flavor mixing problem to an (N-1)-flavor problem. This result holds for three active flavors plus an arbitrary number of sterile neutrinos, but only if the proportionality condition applies to all mass eigenstates except the third.

## Method

The authors impose the condition that the mixing matrix elements for muon and tau neutrinos are proportional for all mass eigenstates except the third: sinφ Uμi = e^{iη} cosφ Uτi for i ≠ 3. Using unitarity constraints (∑i |Uαi|² = 1 and ∑i Uαi Uβi* = 0), they derive that the sum A = ∑i≠3 [|Uμi|² + |Uτi|²] must equal 1, and that Uα3 = 0 for all flavors α other than μ and τ. They then construct rotated flavor states that isolate the decoupled mass eigenstate and write the reduced mixing matrix for the remaining flavors, including explicit forms for the special case φ = π/4 (maximal μ-τ mixing) and for general N flavors using SU(2) rotation parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The constraint forces A = ∑i≠3 [|Uμi|² + |Uτi|²] = 1, exactly.
- For the special case φ = π/4, the mixing matrix elements for the third mass eigenstate become |Uμ3|² = 1/2 and |Uτ3|² = 1/2.
- The parameter reduction: the original N(N-1)/2 mixing parameters are reduced to (N² - 3N + 4)/2 parameters under the similar-coupling constraint.
- In the three-active-flavor case with φ = π/4, the remaining mixing is described by a single angle θ, with Ue1 = cosθ, Ue2 = -sinθ, Uμ1 = sinθ, Uμ2 = cosθ.

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological approach of Elwood, Irges, and Ramond (1998), who suggested a family symmetry leading to a specific MNS matrix structure with large μ-τ entries, and is motivated by the SuperKamiokande atmospheric neutrino results (Fukuda et al., 1998) showing maximal νμ mixing. The formalism enables simplified treatment of matter-enhanced neutrino oscillations in supernova environments, as illustrated by applying the constraints to the 4×4 mixing scheme of Caldwell, Fuller, and Qian (1999) for r-process nucleosynthesis, where the decoupled mass eigenstate acts as a "filter" in MSW resonances. The results also connect to the bi-maximal mixing matrices of Barger et al. (1998) and Georgi and Glashow (1998), and show that standard MSW analyses for νe → νμ conversion remain valid for νe → ν̃μ conversion in solar neutrino experiments.
