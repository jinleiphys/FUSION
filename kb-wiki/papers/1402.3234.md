# $ﾎｳ^\ast N \to N(1710)$ transition at high momentum transfer

> In a relativistic quark model we study the structure of the $N(1710)$ resonance, and the $ﾎｳ^\ast N \to N(1710)$ reaction focusing on the high momentum transfer region, where the valence quark degrees of freedom are expected to be dominant. The $N(1710)$ resonance, a state with spin 1/2 and positive parity ($J^P = \frac{1}{2}^+$), can possibly be interpreted as the second radial excitation of the nucleon, after the Roper, $N(1440)$. We calculate the $ﾎｳ^\ast N \to N(1710)$ helicity amplitudes, and predict that they are almost identical to those of the $ﾎｳ^\ast N \to N(1440)$ reaction in the high momentum transfer region. Thus, future measurement of the helicity amplitudes for the $ﾎｳ^\ast N \to N(1710)$ reaction can give a significant hint on the internal structure of the $N(1710)$ state.

## Key claim

The authors predict that the $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1710)$ helicity amplitudes are almost identical to those of the $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1440)$ reaction in the high momentum transfer region ($Q^2 > 4$ GeV$^2$), under the working hypothesis that the $N(1710)$ state is the second radial excitation of the nucleon. This prediction is expected to be valid only in the high $Q^2$ region where valence quark degrees of freedom dominate, and the model does not include meson cloud effects important at low $Q^2$.

## Method

The authors used the covariant spectator quark model, describing baryons as quark-diquark systems in an S-state configuration. The $N(1710)$ radial wave function was determined uniquely by imposing orthogonality conditions with both the nucleon and the Roper ($N(1440)$) radial wave functions, introducing no new adjustable parameters beyond those already fixed by nucleon form factor data. The transition form factors and helicity amplitudes for $\gamma^\ast N \to N(1710)$ were then calculated from the overlap integrals of the radial wave functions.

## Key numbers

- The orthogonality conditions for the $N(1710)$ radial wave function yielded parameters $\beta_4 = 0.3377$ and $\beta_5 = 0.00855$.
- For the $N(1710)$ parametrization of $A_{1/2}$ at high $Q^2$: $A = 122.17 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ and $\Lambda_1^2 = 2.783$ GeV$^2$.
- For the $N(1710)$ parametrization of $S_{1/2}$ at high $Q^2$: $S = 210.71 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$ and $\Lambda_2^2 = 1.531$ GeV$^2$.
- The PDG result for $N(1710)$ at $Q^2=0$ is $A_{1/2}(0) = (24 \pm 10) \times 10^{-3}$ GeV$^{-1/2}$.
- The predicted asymptotic falloffs are $A_{1/2} \propto 1/Q^3$ and $S_{1/2} \propto 1/Q^5$ for very large $Q^2$.

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously applied to the nucleon and the Roper resonance $N(1440)$ (Ramalho and Tsushima, 2010) to the second radial excitation $N(1710)$. The predictions are intended for testing at the Jefferson Lab 12-GeV upgrade, which will enable study of resonances in the third resonance region ($W \approx 1.7$ GeV). The results contrast with models where $N(1710)$ is dynamically generated from baryon-meson interactions (such as the EBAC model of Suzuki et al., 2010), which would predict a faster falloff with $Q^2$ than the valence quark dominance scenario presented here.
