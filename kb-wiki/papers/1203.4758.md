# Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering

> Starting from hyperbolic dispersion relations, we derive a closed system of Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering that respects analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry. We work out analytically all kernel functions and unitarity relations required for the lowest partial waves. In order to suppress the dependence on the high-energy regime we also consider once- and twice-subtracted versions of the equations, where we identify the subtraction constants with subthreshold parameters. Assuming Mandelstam analyticity we determine the maximal range of validity of these equations. As a first step towards the solution of the full system we cast the equations for the $ππ\to\bar NN$ partial waves into the form of a Muskhelishvili-Omnès problem with finite matching point, which we solve numerically in the single-channel approximation. We investigate in detail the role of individual contributions to our solutions and discuss some consequences for the spectral functions of the nucleon electromagnetic form factors.

## Key claim

Starting from hyperbolic dispersion relations, the authors derive a closed system of Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering that respects analyticity, unitarity, and crossing symmetry, but note that the full solution of the system is not yet achieved, only the t-channel subproblem is solved numerically in the single-channel approximation as a first step.

## Method

The authors derive Roy-Steiner equations for pion-nucleon scattering by expanding hyperbolic dispersion relations (HDRs) in s-channel and t-channel partial waves, then projecting the full HDRs onto both sets of partial waves. They work out analytically all kernel functions and unitarity relations for the lowest partial waves (S- and P-waves, totaling six s-channel and three t-channel waves). The t-channel equations are cast into a Muskhelishvili-Omnès problem with a finite matching point and solved numerically in the single-channel approximation, using known s-channel partial waves and ππ phase shifts as input. The authors also consider once- and twice-subtracted versions of the equations, identifying subtraction constants with subthreshold parameters.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon and pion masses used are the isospin limit values: m ≡ m_p (proton mass) and M_π ≡ M_{π^±} (charged pion mass), with the kaon mass M_K ≡ M_{K^±} used for inelasticity considerations.
- The s-channel threshold is at s_+ = (m + M_π)^2 and pseudothreshold at s_- = (m - M_π)^2.
- The t-channel thresholds are t_π = 4M_π^2 for ππ and t_N = 4m^2 for NN.
- The Cheng-Dashen point is at (s = u = m^2, t = 2M_π^2) = (ν = 0, ν_B = 0), with z_s^CD ≈ -5.56 × 10^{-3}.
- The subthreshold point is at (s = u = s_0, t = 0) = (ν = 0, ν_B = -M_π^2/(2m)).
- The s-channel threshold point is at (s = s_+, t = 0, u = s_-) = (ν = M_π, ν_B = -M_π^2/(2m)).
- The matching points for separating low- and high-energy regions are denoted s_m and t_m in the s- and t-channel, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the Roy-Steiner equation formalism previously applied to ππ scattering (Roy, 1971) and πK scattering (piK:comparison, piK:RS) to the pion-nucleon system, which involves spin-1/2 nucleons and thus more partial waves. The approach improves upon fixed-t dispersion relations used in earlier πN analyses (Becher and Leutwyler) and the KH80 solution (Koch and Höhler, 1980), which does not include more recent data and lacks theoretical uncertainty estimates. The t-channel solutions obtained here are directly applicable to dispersive analyses of nucleon electromagnetic form factors and the scalar form factor needed for the πN σ-term extraction, and the full system will ultimately enable a precise determination of low-energy πN scattering parameters with reliable theoretical errors.
