# Meson-Baryon Couplings Revisited

> The theoretical evaluation of the coupling constants gpiNN , gKNLambda and gKNSigma is undertaken using QCD sum rules. These quantities were previously calculated with exponential (Borel) kernels used to suppress the unknown contributions of the hadronic continua. This method however introduces arbitrariness and instability in the calculation. In order to avoid these I redo the calculation using polynomial kernels tailored to vanish at the baryonic resonance masses. The results are gpiNN = 12.5 +- 1.0, gKNSigma = 5.45 +- .4, gKNSigma= -(12.7 - 15.0) which are close to experiment and to the predictions of SU(3) and which , with the corresponding Goldberger-Treiman Discrepancy satisfy quite well the Dashen-Weinstein relation.

# Meson-Baryon Couplings Revisited

## Key claim

The paper claims that QCD sum rules with polynomial kernels tailored to vanish at baryonic resonance masses yield coupling constants for pi-N, K-N-Lambda, and K-N-Sigma that are close to experiment and SU(3) predictions, while avoiding the arbitrariness and instability problems of the traditional Borel (exponential kernel) method. The results depend on the choice of polynomial coefficients and on the value of the 4-quark condensate factorization parameter kappa for the Lambda channel.

## Method

The author replaces the standard exponential (Borel) damping kernel in QCD sum rule dispersion integrals with a polynomial kernel P(t) = 1 - a1*t - a2*t^2, whose coefficients are chosen to minimize the kernel's magnitude over the baryonic resonance region (2.0-3.0 GeV^2 for the nucleon case, 1.5-3.5 GeV^2 for the kaon case). The contour integral method is used, where the hadronic continuum contribution is rendered negligible by the polynomial's vanishing at resonance masses, and the QCD side is evaluated from the operator product expansion. For the kaon-baryon couplings, the Goldberger-Treiman relation is used together with the polynomial kernel method applied to the three-point function.

## Key numbers

- g_piNN = 12.4 +/- 0.6 (final result from Section 4; intermediate result in Section 2 gives 11.5 +/- 1.0)
- g_KNSigma = 5.45 +/- 0.4
- g_KNLambda = -(12.7 - 15.0) (range depending on kappa, the 4-quark condensate factorization parameter, taken as 1 <= kappa <= 3)
- Polynomial coefficients for pi-N case: a1 = 0.807 GeV^{-2}, a2 = -0.160 GeV^{-4}
- Polynomial coefficients for kaon case: a0 = 0.77 GeV^{-2}, a1 = -0.14 GeV^{-4}
- Goldberger-Treiman discrepancies: Delta_Sigma ~ 0.18, Delta_Lambda ~ 0.29
- Lambda_N^2 from nucleon 2-point function: (2pi)^4 * lambda_N^2 * P(m_N^2) = 0.444 GeV^6
- Lambda_Lambda^2 from Lambda 2-point function: (2pi)^4 * lambda_Lambda^2 * P_Lambda(m_Lambda^2) = 0.481 GeV^6
- C constant in OPE: C ~ -0.0117 GeV^3 (using standard values <a_s GG> ~ 0.012 GeV^4, m_0^2 ~ 0.8 GeV^2)

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach of Reinders, Rubinstein and Yazaki (1985) and Shifman, Vainshtein and Zakharov (1979) for meson-baryon couplings, directly addressing the arbitrariness and stability problems identified in earlier Borel-type calculations by Kim (1998), Shiomi and Hatsuda (1995), and Belyaev and Kogan (1984). The polynomial kernel method was previously applied by Nasrallah (2015) to the nucleon mass sum rule. The results are compared to SU(3) predictions (with alpha = D/(D+F) ~ 0.635) and to experimental analyses by Haberzettl (2008), and the Goldberger-Treiman discrepancies are tested against the Dashen-Weinstein relation (1969). The kaon-baryon results are contrasted with heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Goity (1998) which gave much smaller values than SU(3) expectations.
