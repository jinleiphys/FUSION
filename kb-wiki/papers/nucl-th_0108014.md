# Local Density Approximation for Systems with Pairing Correlations

> I formulate a local density approximation for fermion systems with pairing correlations based on a rapidly converging renormalization scheme for the pairing gap.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a local density approximation (LDA) for fermion systems with pairing correlations by introducing a rapidly converging renormalization scheme for the pairing field, which resolves the ultraviolet divergence that previously prevented such an extension. The key condition is that the regularization converges for cut-off energies significantly smaller than the Fermi energy, unlike previous schemes that required cut-offs of order the Fermi energy.

## Method

The author derives a regularized anomalous density by subtracting divergent terms from the HFB quasiparticle sum, using a Thomas-Fermi approximation for single-particle wave functions and energies to evaluate the regulator. The renormalization introduces two local cut-off momenta (one above and one below the Fermi surface) and defines an effective position-dependent coupling constant, leading to a cut-off independent pairing gap. The scheme is implemented by extracting a density-dependent bare coupling constant from homogeneous infinite matter calculations and using it in a Kohn-Sham energy density functional with a pairing term \( g(\rho(\mathbf{r})) |\nu_c(\mathbf{r})|^2 \).

## Key numbers

- The regularization scheme converges for cut-off energies \( E_c \) significantly smaller than the Fermi energy \( \epsilon_F = \hbar^2 k_F^2 / 2m \), while the scheme from Ref. [5] (Bruun et al.) converges only at energies of order \( \epsilon_F \).
- The traditional \(\delta\)-function approach with cut-off energy \( E_c \) (Henning, 1995) converges extremely slowly, remaining about 20% off the converged value even at \( E_c = 1000 \) MeV.
- The calculation for homogeneous neutron matter used density \( \rho = 0.08\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3} \) and coupling constant \( g = -250\ \mathrm{MeV} \cdot \mathrm{fm}^3 \).

## Context

This work extends the Kohn-Sham LDA framework (Hohenberg and Kohn, 1964) to systems with pairing correlations, overcoming the divergence problem identified in earlier attempts by Oliveira et al. and Bruun et al. (2000). The regularization scheme improves upon previous approaches for infinite homogeneous systems (Yang, 1962; Gorkov, 1958; Randeria, 1995) and finite systems (Bruun, 2000; Bulgac and Yu, 2000) by achieving faster convergence. The formalism enables practical LDA calculations for nuclei, fermionic atomic condensates, \( ^3 \)He, and neutron matter using density-dependent coupling constants extracted from homogeneous matter.
