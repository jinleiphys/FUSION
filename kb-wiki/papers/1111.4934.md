# Relativistic chiral representation of the $πN$ scattering amplitude II: The pion-nucleon sigma term

> We present a determination of the pion-nucleon sigma-term based on a novel analysis of the $πN$ scattering amplitude in Lorentz covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory renormalized in the extended-on-mass-shell scheme. This amplitude, valid up-to next-to-next-leading order in the chiral expansion, systematically includes the effects of the $Δ(1232)$, giving a reliable description of the phase shifts of different partial wave analyses up to energies just below the resonance region. We obtain predictions on some observables that are within experimental bounds and phenomenological expectations. In particular, we use the center-of-mass energy dependence of the amplitude adjusted with the data above threshold to extract accurately the value of $σ_{πN}$. Our study indicates that the inclusion of modern meson-factory and pionic-atom data favors relatively large values of the sigma term. We report the value $σ_{πN}=59(7)$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper reports a value of the pion-nucleon sigma term σπN = 59(7) MeV, but this result is conditional on the inclusion of modern meson-factory and pionic-atom data; the value obtained from the older Karlsruhe-Helsinki partial-wave analysis is significantly lower at 43(5) MeV, and the authors argue that the larger value is favored only when the more recent data sets are used.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) calculation of the πN scattering amplitude in Lorentz covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT), renormalized in the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme, with explicit inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance via the δ-counting. They fitted the center-of-mass energy dependence of the S- and P-wave phase shifts from three different partial-wave analyses (Karlsruhe-Helsinki, George-Washington, and Matsinos/EM) up to Wmax = 1.2 GeV, extracting the low-energy constant c1, which directly determines σπN through the scalar form factor relation without requiring extrapolation to the unphysical Cheng-Dashen point.

## Key numbers

- σπN = 59(7) MeV, the central result from averaging the George-Washington and EM partial-wave analyses, with the error including higher-order uncertainties and dispersion of fits.
- σπN = 43(5) MeV from the Karlsruhe-Helsinki partial-wave analysis, using the same chiral framework.
- σπN = 59(2) MeV from the EM (Matsinos) analysis, and σπN = 59(4) MeV from the George-Washington analysis, showing mutual consistency.
- The isoscalar scattering length a0+⁺ from the GW analysis is −0.4(7) × 10⁻² mπ⁻¹, and from the EM analysis is 0.2(3) × 10⁻² mπ⁻¹, both compatible with the pionic-atom value of 0.0076(31) mπ⁻¹ (i.e., 0.76(31) × 10⁻² mπ⁻¹).
- The Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy ΔGT is 2.1(8)% for GW and 1.9(4)% for EM, compared to 4.9(8)% for KH.
- The πN coupling constant gπN from the GW fit is 13.15(10), consistent with the pionic-atom value of 13.12(9).

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analyses of the Karlsruhe-Helsinki group (1985) and the George-Washington group (2008) by providing a chiral perturbation theory framework that avoids the extrapolation to the unphysical Cheng-Dashen point, instead extracting σπN directly from the energy dependence of scattering data. The results confirm the longstanding discrepancy between the KH value (σπN ≈ 45 MeV, from Gasser et al., 1990) and the larger GW value (σπN ≈ 64 MeV, from Pavan et al., 2001), and the authors argue that modern data from meson factories and pionic-atom experiments (Baru et al., 2010) favor the larger value. The work enables a re-evaluation of the strangeness content of the nucleon and of chiral symmetry restoration in nuclear matter, noting that a σπN of 60 MeV is compatible with negligible strangeness when analyzed in Lorentz covariant BχPT (Martin Camalich et al., 2010), contrary to earlier heavy-baryon χPT relations.
