# Quantum groups and Cabibbo mixing

> Treating the issue of hadron masses and mass relations by the use of quantum groups U_q(su_n) taken as hadron flavor symmetries suggests, at least in the case of baryons, a direct connection of the deformation parameter q with the Cabibbo angle. We discuss possible manifestations of the Cabibbo mixing implied by such connection, including unusual ones.

# Quantum groups and Cabibbo mixing

## Key claim

The deformation parameter q of the quantum group U_q(su_n), when used as a hadron flavor symmetry, is directly linked to the Cabibbo angle θ_C, with the specific relation θ_8 = 2θ_C for octet baryons and θ_10 = θ_C for decuplet baryons, suggesting an exact value of π/14 for the Cabibbo angle. This connection is conditional on the particular choice of q = exp(iπ/7) for the octet mass sum rule, which yields the best agreement with data at the 0.07% level.

## Method

The author derives q-deformed mass sum rules for octet and decuplet baryons using representations of the dynamical quantum algebra U_q(u_{4,1}) and its compact counterpart U_q(u_5), where the deformation parameter q is fixed to discrete values q_m = exp(iπ/m) that cause the polynomial A_q in the mass relation to vanish. For pseudoscalar mesons, the q-deformed Gell-Mann-Okubo formula is compared with the generalization involving decay constants f_π and f_K, whose ratio is expressible through the Cabibbo angle. The resulting link between q and θ_C is then extended to the anyonic statistics parameter ν via q = exp(iπν), to the parameters of the LTK diquark-quark model, and to the intercepts of two- and three-particle correlation functions of pions and kaons treated as q-deformed bosons.

## Key numbers

- The best octet baryon mass sum rule (q7) agrees with data at the 0.07% level, using q_7 = exp(iπ/7)
- The decuplet mass relation holds remarkably for θ_10 ≃ π/14, which is argued to equal the Cabibbo angle
- The exact value suggested for the Cabibbo angle is θ_C = π/14
- The q-number [2]_7 = 2cos(π/7) ≈ 1.80194
- Solving the LTK-q relation yields ξ_ts^(+) ≈ 0.741 and ξ_ts^(-) ≈ -6.705 for the diquark-quark model parameter ratio
- The asymptotic intercept for BM-type q-bosons at θ = π/7 is λ^BM_asymp = 2cos(π/7) - 1 ≈ 0.80194
- The combination r_0 at θ = 2θ_C is approximately 0.8955
- Decuplet mass splittings deviate from equal spacing rule: 152.6 MeV, 148.8 MeV, 139.0 MeV for successive isoplet differences

## Context

This work extends the program of using quantum groups U_q(su_n) as hadron flavor symmetries, previously developed by Gavrilik and collaborators (Gavrilik 1990s), to establish a direct connection between the deformation parameter and the Cabibbo angle. It builds on the LTK diquark-quark model (Lichtenberg, Tassie, and Kang 1970s) and the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula, providing improved mass sum rules that account for higher-order SU(3) breaking in a nonpolynomial fashion. The results enable predictions for the intercepts of two- and three-pion (kaon) correlation functions in relativistic heavy ion collisions, testable against RHIC/STAR data, and connect the Cabibbo angle to the anyonic statistics parameter from lattice fermion constructions (Lerda and collaborators 1990s). The work also relates to the Weinberg angle through the relation θ_W = 2(θ_12 + θ_23 + θ_13) from Palle (1990s), linking bosonic and fermionic mixing sectors.
