# Quark-antiquark Scattering Phase Shift and Meson Spectral Function in Pion Superfluid

> We study the quark-antiquark scattering phase shift and meson spectral function in the pion superfluid described by the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. The meson mixing in the pion superfluid changes dramatically the full scattering phase shift and spreads strongly the spectral function of some of the collective modes.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the pion superfluid phase, the strong mixing among the $\pi_+$, $\pi_-$, and $\sigma$ mesons makes it impossible to define separate quark-antiquark scattering phase shifts or spectral functions for individual channels, and the full phase shift and spectral function are not a simple sum of contributions from all meson channels.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a hard cutoff regularization, solving the gap equations for the chiral and pion condensates self-consistently at finite temperature and isospin chemical potential. Meson properties are obtained via the random phase approximation (RPA) by computing the polarization matrix in the $(\pi_+, \pi_-, \pi_0, \sigma)$ basis, and the full quark-antiquark scattering phase shift is extracted from the determinant of the inverse meson propagator. The meson spectral function is then calculated from the full mixed meson propagator, including both the pole contributions and the continuous background from the quark-antiquark continuum.

## Key numbers

- Model parameters: cutoff $\Lambda = 0.653$ GeV, coupling $G = 4.93$ GeV$^{-2}$, current quark mass $m_0 = 0.005$ GeV, fitted to vacuum pion mass $M_\pi = 0.134$ GeV, pion decay constant $F_\pi = 0.093$ GeV, and chiral condensate $\langle\sigma\rangle = 2(-0.25\text{ GeV})^3$.
- At $\mu_I = 0.15$ GeV and $T = \mu_B = 0$ in the pion superfluid, the Goldstone mode $\pi_+$ has mass $M_{\pi_+} = 0$, the $\pi_-$ mode has mass $M_{\pi_-} = 0.25$ GeV, and the $\sigma$ mode has mass $M_\sigma = 0.64$ GeV (hard cutoff) or $m_\sigma = 0.48$ GeV (Pauli-Villars regularization).
- At $\mu_I = 0.25$ GeV and $T = \mu_B = 0$ in the pion superfluid, the $\pi_+$ mode is the Goldstone mode, the $\sigma$ mode has mass $M_\sigma = 0.27$ GeV, and the $\pi_-$ mode has mass $M_{\pi_-} = 0.72$ GeV.
- In the normal phase at $\mu_I = 0.1$ GeV and $T = 0$, the mass splitting is $\Delta M_\pi = M_{\pi_0} - M_{\pi_+} = M_{\pi_-} - M_{\pi_0} = \mu_I = 0.1$ GeV.
- The critical isospin chemical potential for pion superfluidity at $T = 0$ is $\mu_I = M_\pi = 0.134$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier NJL model studies of pion superfluidity by He et al. (2005) and Xia et al., which focused on meson masses and the phase diagram, by computing the full scattering phase shift and spectral function including the continuous background contribution. The approach builds on the Beth-Uhlenbeck formula for the second virial coefficient (Beth and Uhlenbeck, 1937) and its relativistic generalization by Dashen et al. (1969), as well as the RPA treatment of mesons in the NJL model by Hufner et al. (1994) and Zhuang et al. The results enable a more complete understanding of meson properties in the pion superfluid phase, particularly the breakdown of the pole approximation for the $\sigma$ mode due to meson mixing, and provide input for thermodynamic calculations using the phase shift approach.
