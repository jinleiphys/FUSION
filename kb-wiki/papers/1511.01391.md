# Glueball decay patterns in top-down holographic QCD

> We discuss our results on scalar glueball decay in the top-down holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model for low-energy QCD and compare with available experimental data, which appear to disfavor the glueball candidate $f_0(1500)$ but seem to be perfectly consistent with interpreting $f_0(1710)$ as a nearly unmixed glueball. The holographic model moreover makes definite predictions for future experiments.

## Key claim

The top-down holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto (WSS) model for low-energy QCD, when applied to scalar glueball decay, disfavors identifying the $f_0(1500)$ meson as a nearly pure glueball, but is perfectly consistent with interpreting $f_0(1710)$ as a nearly unmixed glueball, provided one ignores mixing with $q\bar q$ states (which is absent at the model's level of approximation in the 't Hooft limit).

## Method

The authors computed tree-level decay amplitudes for the lightest dilatonic scalar glueball mode ($G_D$) of the WSS model, using the effective action for scalar and tensor glueball modes derived in their prior work (Brunner:2015oqa). They evaluated the decay pattern with the glueball mass set alternatively to that of $f_0(1500)$ and $f_0(1710)$, and compared the resulting partial widths to experimental data from the Particle Data Group. For finite quark masses, additional couplings of the glueball to pseudoscalar mass terms were included under the assumption of a universal coupling (1504.05815), and an upper limit on $\Gamma(\eta\eta')$ was derived from the requirement that the predicted flavor asymmetry in $\Gamma(\pi\pi)/\Gamma(KK)$ remains within experimental error (1510.07605).

## Key numbers

*   The lightest dilatonic glueball mode $G_D$ has a mass of approximately $1.567 M_{\rm KK} \approx 1487$ MeV, close to the mass of $f_0(1500)$ and 14% lighter than $f_0(1710)$.
*   For a pure glueball with the mass of $f_0(1500)$, the predicted total width $\Gamma/M$ is $0.027\ldots0.039$ (massless WSS) or $0.057\ldots0.079$ (massive WSS), compared to the experimental value of $0.072(5)$.
*   For $f_0(1500)$, the predicted decay rate into four pions is $\Gamma(4\pi)/M = 0.003\ldots0.005$ (massless) or $0.005\ldots0.007$ (massive), while the experimental value is $0.036(3)$, a discrepancy of roughly an order of magnitude.
*   For $f_0(1500)$, the predicted decay into $4\pi^0$ is $\Gamma(4\pi^0)/M = 4\times 10^{-6}\ldots 3\times 10^{-5}$, which is two orders of magnitude smaller than the experimental ratio $\Gamma(f_0(1500)\to4\pi^0)/\Gamma(f_0(1500)\to2\eta)=0.8(3)$ from Alde (1987).
*   For a pure glueball with the mass of $f_0(1710)$, the predicted total width $\Gamma/M$ is $0.059\ldots0.076$ (massless) or $0.083\ldots0.106$ (massive), compared to the experimental value of $0.081(5)$.
*   For $f_0(1710)$, the predicted decay into $2\omega$ is $\Gamma(2\omega)/M = 0.011\ldots 0.014$, consistent with the experimental ratio $\Gamma(2\omega)/\Gamma(2\pi)=0.78(32)$ from the Particle Data Group.
*   For $f_0(1710)$, the predicted ratio $\Gamma(4\pi)/\Gamma(2\pi) \approx 2.4$, with a specific prediction of $\Gamma(2\pi^+2\pi^-)/\Gamma(2\pi)\approx 0.8$.

## Context

This work extends the top-down holographic approach of the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, which has previously reproduced decay rates for $\rho$ and $\omega$ mesons (Sakai 2004, Rebhan 2014), to the scalar glueball sector. It contradicts the interpretation of $f_0(1500)$ as a nearly pure glueball, which is often assumed in phenomenological mixing models (Amsler 1995, Close 2001, Janowski 2011), and instead supports the identification of $f_0(1710)$ as the preferred glueball candidate, consistent with models that assign it small $q\bar q$ components (Janowski 2014, Cheng 2015). The holographic model makes definite predictions for future experiments, particularly for the decay $f_0(1710)\to 2\rho^0$ currently being studied by the CMS-TOTEM collaboration (Osterberg 2014).
