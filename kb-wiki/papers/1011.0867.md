# Formation of η-Mesic Nuclei

> In this paper, we briefly introduce the interests and the recent research activities on the η-mesic nuclei. We also mention the activities on the η' (958)-mesic nucleus formation.

# Formation of η-Mesic Nuclei

## Key claim

The paper argues that η-mesic nuclei can serve as a laboratory to study the in-medium properties of the N*(1535) resonance, but the existence and nature of such bound states depend critically on which theoretical model of N* is correct, the chiral doublet model predicts a repulsive core inside the nucleus (for certain values of chiral restoration strength C), while the chiral unitary model predicts an attractive potential with little medium modification.

## Method

The authors calculate η-nucleus optical potentials using two distinct theoretical models of the N*(1535) resonance: the chiral doublet model (where N* is a chiral partner of the nucleon) and the chiral unitary model (where N* is dynamically generated in coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering). They then apply the Green's function method of Morimatsu and Yazaki to compute formation spectra for (π⁺,p) reactions on ¹²C and for d+d → α+η reactions, evaluating the nuclear response function with the complex optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The ηNN* coupling constant g_η is determined to be approximately 2.0 to reproduce the partial width Γ_{N*→ηN} ≈ 75 MeV at tree level
- The chiral restoration parameter C is taken as 0.0 and 0.2, with the empirical value of C lying between 0.1 and 0.3 at nuclear saturation density ρ₀
- For the ¹²C(π⁺,p)¹¹C⊗η reaction, the incident pion kinetic energy T_π is 820 MeV, corresponding to recoilless condition at the η threshold
- The neutron separation energy difference for a (0s_{1/2})_n⁻¹ hole-state is 18 MeV, shifting the η production threshold to E_ex − E_0 = 18 MeV
- For the d+d → α+η reaction, the momentum transfer is approximately 1 GeV/c, and the assumed optical potential depth at nuclear center is V_η(0) = (−50 − 40i) MeV

## Context

This work extends the research line of deeply bound mesic systems, following the successful observation of deeply bound pionic atoms by Suzuki et al. (2004) which provided information on chiral symmetry restoration in nuclei. The authors build on earlier theoretical studies of η-mesic nuclei by Haider and Liu (1986) and by Chiang, Oset and Liu (1991), and respond to experimental attempts including the negative result from the (π⁺,p) reaction by Chrien et al. (1988) and more recent photoproduction experiments on ¹²C by Sokol (2000) and on ³He by Pfeiffer (2004). The calculations enable future experiments planned by Moskal (2010) and Itahashi (2010) to distinguish between competing models of the N*(1535) resonance through spectral shape measurements.
