# A Model Study of Discrete Scale Invariance and Long-Range Interactions

> We investigate the modification of discrete scale invariance in the bound state spectrum by long-range interactions. This problem is relevant for effective field theory descriptions of nuclear cluster states and manifestations of the Efimov effect in nuclei. As a model system, we choose a one dimensional inverse square potential supplemented with a long-range Coulomb interaction. We study the renormalization and bound-state spectrum of the system as a function of the Coulomb interaction strength. Our results indicate, that the counterterm required to renormalize the inverse square potential alone is sufficient to renormalize the full problem. However, the breaking of the discrete scale invariance through the Coulomb interaction leads to a modified bound state spectrum. The shallow bound states are strongly influenced by the Coulomb interaction while the deep bound states are dominated by the inverse square potential.

## Key claim

The counterterm required to renormalize the pure inverse square potential alone is sufficient to renormalize the full problem when a long-range Coulomb interaction is added, but the Coulomb interaction breaks the discrete scale invariance of the bound state spectrum, with shallow states strongly influenced by Coulomb while deep states remain dominated by the inverse square potential.

## Method

The authors solved the one-dimensional Schrödinger equation with an attractive inverse square potential supplemented by an attractive Coulomb potential, using a momentum-space Lippmann-Schwinger equation regularized with a sharp momentum cutoff Λ. They renormalized the system by including a momentum-independent counterterm H_S(Λ) and fixed one bound state energy as the cutoff was varied, then studied the cutoff dependence of the remaining bound states for several values of the Coulomb strength α and the inverse-square parameter ν.

## Key numbers

- For the pure inverse square potential (α=0) with ν=1, the ratio of consecutive binding energies is a constant given by (e^{π/ν})² = (e^{π})².
- For α=0.1, 0.3, and 0.5 with ν=Λ_*=1, the potential energy at the distance where both interactions have equal strength is \bar{E}_pot = 1.6×10⁻², 1.44×10⁻¹, and 4.0×10⁻¹ respectively (in dimensionless units).
- For the shallowest state shown with α=0.1 (ν=Λ_*=1), first-order perturbation theory gave E_B^(1) = 4.4×10⁻³ compared to the exact value 6.3×10⁻³; for α=0.5, perturbation theory gave 1.9×10⁻² compared to the exact value 5.2×10⁻².
- The fixed binding energy used for renormalization was E_fixed ≈ 0.308 (dimensionless units).

## Context

This work extends the study of limit cycles and discrete scale invariance in the inverse square potential (Hammer 2005) to include long-range Coulomb interactions, which are relevant for effective field theory descriptions of nuclear cluster states and the Efimov effect in nuclei. The results provide a first step toward understanding the breaking of discrete scale invariance in realistic systems such as the αα system (Higa 2008) and the triple-α system that produces the Hoyle state in ¹²C, where exact Efimov features are broken by Coulomb but remnants may remain.
