# Patterns of $C$- and $CP$-violation in hadronic $η$ and $η'$ three-body decays

> We construct hadronic amplitudes for the three-body decays $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^+π^-π^0$ and $η'\toηπ^+π^-$ in a non-perturbative fashion, allowing for $C$- and $CP$-violating asymmetries in the $π^+π^-$ distributions. These amplitudes are consistent with the constraints of analyticity and unitarity. We find that the currently most accurate Dalitz-plot distributions taken by the KLOE-2 and BESIII collaborations confine the patterns of these asymmetries to a relative per mille level. Our dispersive representation allows us to extract the individual coupling strengths of the $C$- and $CP$-violating contributions arising from effective isoscalar and isotensor operators in $η^{(\prime)}\toπ^+π^-π^0$ and an effective isovector operator in $η'\toηπ^+π^-$, while the strongly different sensitivities to these operators can be understood from chiral power counting arguments.

## Key claim

The paper constructs dispersive amplitudes for eta and eta-prime three-body decays that incorporate C- and CP-violating effects, finding that current Dalitz-plot data from KLOE-2 and BESIII constrain these asymmetries to the relative per mille level, though the actual sensitivity differs strongly between decay channels depending on the isospin structure of the violating operators.

## Method

The authors build non-perturbative hadronic amplitudes for eta -> pi+ pi- pi0, eta' -> pi+ pi- pi0, and eta' -> eta pi+ pi- using dispersive representations that satisfy analyticity and unitarity constraints. They introduce C- and CP-violating contributions through effective isoscalar and isotensor operators for the eta-prime to three-pion decays and an isovector operator for eta' -> eta pi+ pi-, and fit the resulting amplitude parameters to existing Dalitz-plot distributions from the KLOE-2 and BESIII collaborations.

## Key numbers

* The currently most accurate Dalitz-plot distributions from KLOE-2 and BESIII confine C- and CP-violating asymmetries in eta-prime to pi+ pi- pi0 and eta' to eta pi+ pi- to a relative per mille level.
* The dispersive representation extracts coupling strengths for C- and CP-violating contributions arising from effective isoscalar and isotensor operators in eta-prime to pi+ pi- pi0 and an effective isovector operator in eta' to eta pi+ pi-.
* The strongly different sensitivities to these operators can be understood from chiral power counting arguments.

## Context

This work extends the dispersive treatment of eta and eta-prime three-body decays previously developed for the CP-conserving case by the same group (Kubis and collaborators) to include C- and CP-violating effects. It provides a theoretical framework for interpreting future high-statistics measurements from experiments such as KLOE-2 and BESIII, enabling searches for new sources of CP violation beyond the Standard Model in the light meson sector.
