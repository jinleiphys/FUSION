# Continuum and lattice meson spectral functions at nonzero momentum and high temperature

> We analyse discretization effects in the calculation of high-temperature meson spectral functions at nonzero momentum and fermion mass on the lattice. We do so by comparing continuum and lattice spectral functions in the infinite temperature limit. Complete analytical results for the spectral densities in the continuum are presented, along with simple expressions for spectral functions obtained with Wilson and staggered fermions on anisotropic lattices. We comment on the use of local and point split currents.

## Key claim

The paper provides complete analytical results for free meson spectral functions at nonzero momentum and fermion mass in the infinite temperature limit, serving as a reference point for lattice studies; however, the analysis is restricted to the free (non-interacting) case and the infinite temperature limit, meaning it does not capture nonperturbative medium effects or interactions that become important at realistic temperatures near the deconfinement transition.

## Method

The authors computed meson spectral functions analytically in the continuum using the spectral representation of fermion propagators at lowest order in the loop expansion, then derived analogous expressions for Wilson and staggered fermions on anisotropic lattices using the mixed representation of Carpenter and Baillie. They compared continuum and lattice spectral functions by taking the thermodynamic limit (N_sigma to infinity) while focusing on the effect of finite temporal lattice extent N_tau, using block functions to approximate delta functions in the spectral decomposition.

## Key numbers

- External momentum p = 4T and fermion mass m = T were used in all comparison figures
- For Wilson fermions, the maximal energy is given by omega_max^Wilson / T approximately equals 2 N_tau ln(1 + (6 + am)/xi), where xi is the anisotropy parameter
- For staggered (naive) fermions, the maximal energy is omega_max^naive / T approximately equals 2 N_tau ln((sqrt(xi^2 + 3 + a^2 m^2) + sqrt(3 + a^2 m^2))/xi)
- In the large omega limit, continuum spectral functions increase as omega^2 with coefficient N_c/(8 pi) (a_H^(1) - a_H^(2)), except for gamma^0 and gamma^0 gamma_5 channels where the limit is N_c/(6 pi) p^2 and N_c/(6 pi)(p^2 + 6m^2) respectively
- The charge susceptibility at leading order is chi = 2 N_c I_1, where I_1 = -2 integral_k n'_F(omega_k) equals T^2/6 in the massless case
- The zero-momentum euclidean correlator for the conserved current G^00(tau, 0) equals N_c/L^3 sum_k 1/cosh^2(E_k/2T) for both Wilson and naive fermions

## Context

This work extends the zero-momentum analysis of Karsch (2003) to nonzero momentum and fermion mass, providing complete analytical expressions for continuum meson spectral functions that can be used as benchmarks for lattice calculations using the Maximal Entropy Method (MEM). The results enable systematic studies of discretization effects in lattice investigations of hadronic bound states in the quark-gluon plasma (Asakawa 2003, Datta 2003, Umeda 2002), thermal dilepton rates (Karsch 2001), and transport coefficients (Gupta 2003), while also providing a foundation for future studies incorporating interactions via hard thermal loop calculations (Braaten 1990, Karsch 2000, Alberico 2004) and bremsstrahlung effects (Aurenche 1998).
