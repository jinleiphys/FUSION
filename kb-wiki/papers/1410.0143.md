# Heavy-quark expansion for D and B mesons in nuclear matter

> The planned experiments at FAIR enable the study of medium modifications of $D$ and $B$ mesons in (dense) nuclear matter. Evaluating QCD sum rules as a theoretical prerequisite for such investigations encounters heavy-light four-quark condensates. We utilize an extended heavy-quark expansion to cope with the condensation of heavy quarks.

# Heavy-quark expansion for D and B mesons in nuclear matter

## Key claim
The heavy-quark expansion (HQE) for heavy-light four-quark condensates in nuclear matter does not exhibit a simple expansion in inverse powers of the heavy-quark mass \(m_Q\), even in vacuum; therefore, the lowest-order terms are not suppressed by \(1/m_Q\) as they are for the heavy two-quark condensate \(\langle \bar{Q}Q \rangle\), challenging the common practice of omitting heavy-light four-quark condensates in previous sum rule analyses.

## Method
The authors extend the vacuum heavy-quark expansion method, originally developed for the heavy two-quark condensate \(\langle \bar{Q}Q \rangle\) in a weak classical gluonic background field in Fock-Schwinger gauge, to in-medium heavy-light four-quark condensates. They derive a general formula for the HQE of in-medium heavy-light four-quark condensates and evaluate the two leading-order terms for the complete list of two-flavor four-quark condensates, incorporating medium-specific gluonic condensates and additional heavy-quark condensate structures such as \(\langle \bar{Q} \slashed{v} Q \rangle\) and \(\langle \bar{Q} \slashed{v} \sigma G Q \rangle\).

## Key numbers
- Leading-order term for \(\langle \bar{q} \gamma^\nu t^A q \bar{Q} \gamma_\nu t^A Q \rangle^{(0)} = -\frac{2}{3}\frac{g^2}{(4\pi)^2}\left( \log\frac{\mu^2}{m_Q^2} + \frac{1}{2} \right) \langle \bar{q} \gamma^\nu t^A q \sum_f \bar{q}_f \gamma_\nu t^A q_f \rangle\)
- Leading-order term for \(\langle \bar{q} \slashed{v} t^A q \bar{Q} \slashed{v} t^A Q \rangle^{(0)} = -\frac{2}{3}\frac{g^2}{(4\pi)^2}\left( \log\frac{\mu^2}{m_Q^2} + \frac{2}{3} \right) \langle \bar{q} \slashed{v} t^A q \sum_f \bar{q}_f \slashed{v} t^A q_f \rangle\)
- Leading-order term for \(\langle \bar{q} t^A q \bar{Q} \slashed{v} t^A Q \rangle^{(0)} = -\frac{4}{3}\frac{g^2}{(4\pi)^2}\left( \log\frac{\mu^2}{m_Q^2} - \frac{1}{8} \right) \langle \bar{q} t^A q \sum_f \bar{q}_f \slashed{v} t^A q_f \rangle\)
- Next-to-leading-order term for \(\langle \bar{q}q \bar{Q}Q \rangle^{(1)} = -\frac{1}{3}\frac{g^2}{(4\pi)^2} \frac{1}{m_Q} \langle \bar{q}q G^A_{\mu\nu} G^{A\, \mu\nu} \rangle\)
- Next-to-leading-order term for \(\langle \bar{q} \sigma_{\mu\nu} t^A q \bar{Q} \sigma^{\mu\nu} t^A Q \rangle^{(1)} = -\frac{5}{6}\frac{g^2}{(4\pi)^2} \frac{1}{m_Q} \langle f^{ABC} \bar{q} \sigma_{\mu\nu} t^A q G^{B\,\nu}{}_\lambda G^{C\, \lambda\mu} \rangle\)

## Context
This work extends the vacuum heavy-quark expansion method of Generalis and Broadhurst (1984) to in-medium situations, going beyond previous approaches such as Narison (2013). The results provide the necessary theoretical foundation for QCD sum rule evaluations of \(D\) and \(B\) meson properties in dense nuclear matter, which are relevant for planned experiments at FAIR. The derived formulas reduce the number of independent condensates entering sum rule evaluations for heavy-light mesons and challenge the common omission of heavy-light four-quark condensates in previous analyses, enabling more complete in-medium studies of open-charm and open-bottom meson spectroscopy.
