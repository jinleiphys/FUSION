# Chain configurations in light nuclei

> The model of nuclear matter built from alpha-particles is proposed. The strong deformed shape for doubly even N=Z nuclides from carbon to magnesium has been determined according to this model. In this paper we undertake very simple approach, which assumes the existence of low lying chain configurations.

# Chain configurations in light nuclei

## Key claim
The authors propose that low-lying chain configurations of alpha-particles exist in doubly even N=Z nuclei from carbon to magnesium, not only at high excitation above the alpha-fragmentation threshold as traditionally assumed. The model uses a simple one-parameter Yukawa potential and frozen alpha-particle positions, with the caveat that the approach cannot derive maximum allowed angular momentum or fluctuations of the moment of inertia.

## Method
The nucleus is modeled as a rigid arrangement of interacting alpha-particles whose positions are fixed by minimizing binding energy while maintaining a nearly spherical form. The interaction potential is a Yukawa form V(r) = -V₀ exp(-γr)/r with V₀ = 106.7 MeV fitted to fragmentation energies and γ taken as the inverse Compton wavelength of the neutral pion. Excitation energies for chain configurations are calculated from the difference between fragmentation energy and binding energy, and rotational band levels are obtained using the classical moment of inertia of the linear alpha-particle chain.

## Key numbers
- For ¹²C (Nα=3): predicted excitation energy E* = 3.0 MeV (MBA model), compared to 1.0 MeV (liquid drop model) and 7.27 MeV (chain fragmentation energy)
- For ¹⁶O (Nα=4): predicted E* = 8.98 MeV (MBA), compared to 9.0 MeV (LD) and 14.44 MeV (chain fragmentation)
- For ²⁰Ne (Nα=5): predicted E* = 12.99 MeV (MBA), compared to 13.0 MeV (LD) and 19.17 MeV (chain fragmentation)
- For ²⁴Mg (Nα=6): predicted E* = 21.87 MeV (MBA), compared to 20.0 MeV (LD) and 28.48 MeV (chain fragmentation)
- Alpha-particle radius Rα = 1.52 fm
- Potential depth V₀ = 106.7 MeV
- For ²⁰Ne rotational band K=0⁺₈: 0⁺ state at 13.22 MeV (width 40 keV), 2⁺ at 13.53/13.57 MeV (width 61/12 keV), 4⁺ at 14.27 MeV (width 92 keV), 6⁺ at 15.35 MeV, 8⁺ at 16.75 MeV (width 160 keV)
- Angular frequencies for maximal angular momenta are approximately 0.8-1.0 MeV

## Context
This work extends the Ikeda diagram approach (Ikeda, 1968) for alpha-cluster states by proposing that chain configurations may exist at lower excitation energies than previously expected, not only above the alpha-fragmentation threshold. The model builds on the Hafstad-Teller alpha-particle binding model (Hafstad and Teller, 1938) and the quasi-crystal model of the nucleus, and compares its predictions with the macroscopic-microscopic model of Gridnev et al. (2005). The results suggest that the 0⁺₂ state in ¹²C (Hoyle state at 7.65 MeV) may be related to chain configurations, and the approach could be extended to describe all cluster levels in the Ikeda diagram, though experimental confirmation is needed for the predicted low-lying 0⁺ states in ¹²C and ¹⁶O.
