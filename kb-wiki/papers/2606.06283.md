# Diffusion of multiple conserved charges from entropy production

> We derive dissipative relativistic hydrodynamic equations in the presence of multiple conserved charges, i.e., baryon number ($B$), electric charge ($Q$), and strangeness ($S$), using the Chapman-Enskog (CE) method within the kinetic theory approach. The relativistic Boltzmann equation is solved within the relaxation-time approximation with a momentum-independent relaxation time in the collision term. We derive both first-order (Navier-Stokes limit) and second-order dissipative hydrodynamic equations. Within the kinetic theory framework, using the Boltzmann's H-theorem, and by demanding that for a dissipative system, the entropy must be produced, we find different transport coefficients at the first-order and second-order gradient expansion of the out-of-equilibrium distribution function around the local equilibrium. Apart from the well-known transport coefficients, the shear ($η$) and the bulk ($ζ$) viscosities , we also find the diffusion matrix elements ($κ_{qq^{\prime}}$) for the conserved charges $B$, $Q$ and $S$. The diffusion matrix elements ($κ_{qq^{\prime}}$) are important to model the multi-component diffusion dynamics sourced by inhomogeneous baryon stopping in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions. We estimate the temperature ($T$) and chemical potential dependence of diagonal and off-diagonal elements of the diffusion matrix elements for the (2+1) flavor quark-gluon plasma. We further estimate the ratio $κ_{qq^{\prime}}T/η$ for a wide range of temperature and chemical potentials to show the relative importance of the diffusion matrix elements compared to other transport coefficients.

## Key claim

The authors derive first-order and second-order dissipative relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a system with multiple conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness) using the Chapman-Enskog method with a momentum-independent relaxation time, and they obtain the diffusion matrix elements by demanding entropy production via Boltzmann's H-theorem, which ensures that the diagonal components of the diffusion matrix are manifestly positive, unlike some previous studies.

## Method

The relativistic Boltzmann equation is solved within the relaxation-time approximation using a Chapman-Enskog-like expansion of the distribution function around local equilibrium, ordered in powers of spacetime gradients. The entropy production is computed from Boltzmann's H-theorem, and by requiring that entropy must be produced in a dissipative system, the transport coefficients at first-order (Navier-Stokes limit) and second-order gradient expansion are determined. The system considered is a (2+1) flavor quark-gluon plasma consisting of up, down, and strange quarks (with their antiparticles) and gluons, where the quarks carry baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity is given by \(\eta = \frac{\beta \tau_R}{15} {\sum_a}' \int_p \frac{(p\cdot\Delta\cdot p)^2}{\epsilon} (f_a^0 \tilde{f}_a^0 + \bar{f}_a^0 \tilde{\bar{f}}_a^0)\), where \(\tau_R\) is the momentum-independent relaxation time and \(\beta = 1/T\).
* The bulk viscosity is \(\zeta = \tau_R T {\sum_a}' \int_p \epsilon [\{(\beta_\theta - \beta/3)\epsilon + \beta m^2/(3\epsilon) - \xi_\theta^{(a)}\}^2 f_a^0 \tilde{f}_a^0 + \{(\beta_\theta - \beta/3)\epsilon + \beta m^2/(3\epsilon) + \xi_\theta^{(a)}\}^2 \bar{f}_a^0 \tilde{\bar{f}}_a^0]\).
* The diffusion matrix elements are \(\kappa_{qq'} = -\frac{\tau_R}{3} \sum_a \int_p \frac{(p\cdot\Delta\cdot p)}{\epsilon} [\{q_a - n_q \epsilon/(\epsilon+P)\}\{q'_a - n_{q'} \epsilon/(\epsilon+P)\} f_a^0 \tilde{f}_a^0 - \{q_a + n_q \epsilon/(\epsilon+P)\}\{q'_a + n_{q'} \epsilon/(\epsilon+P)\} \bar{f}_a^0 \tilde{\bar{f}}_a^0]\), where \(q, q' = B, Q, S\).
* The ratio \(\kappa_{qq'} T / \eta\) is estimated for a wide range of temperature and chemical potentials to show the relative importance of diffusion matrix elements compared to shear viscosity.

## Context

This work extends the multi-component dissipative hydrodynamic framework developed in studies such as Fotakis (2019) and Hu (2022) by using the Chapman-Enskog method rather than the moment-expansion method, and it ensures that diagonal components of the diffusion matrix are manifestly positive, unlike some previous approaches. The results enable modeling of multi-component diffusion dynamics in heavy-ion collisions at finite baryon density, particularly relevant for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and the Compressed Baryonic Matter experiment at FAIR, where baryon-rich QCD plasma is produced and the diffusion of conserved charges may affect fluctuations near the QCD critical point.
