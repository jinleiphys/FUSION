# Configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected triaxial HFB states using the Skyrme energy density functional

> We present a method based on mean-field states generated by triaxial quadrupole constraints which are projected on particle number and angular momentum and mixed by the generator coordinate method on the quadrupole moment. This method is equivalent to a seven-dimensional GCM calculation, mixing all five degrees of freedom of the quadrupole operator and the gauge angles for protons and neutrons. A first application to Mg24 permits to analyze in detail the effects of triaxial deformations and of K-mixing.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method that generalizes previous axial-only generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations by allowing triaxial quadrupole deformations and full angular-momentum projection, but the authors hedge that the first application to ²⁴Mg is only an illustration and that the results must be taken "cum grano salis" because deformation has limited meaning after projection and the projected states from different intrinsic shapes can have large overlaps.

## Method

The method proceeds in three steps: first, a nonorthogonal basis of self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) states is generated with constraints on two quadrupole coordinates (q₁, q₂) covering the first sextant of the β-γ plane; second, particle-number and angular-momentum projected matrix elements are calculated between all mean-field states using a six-point trapezoidal rule for α, 18-point Gauss-Legendre quadrature for β, and 12-point trapezoidal rule for γ; third, these matrix elements are used in a Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation to mix all K components and all deformations, with redundant states eliminated through a four-step procedure involving diagonalization of norm kernels.

## Key numbers

* The mean-field ground state of ²⁴Mg without angular-momentum projection is axial prolate, more bound than the spherical configuration by about 700 keV.
* After J=0 projection, the lowest energy is obtained for a triaxial configuration with γ ≈ 16° and β₂ around 0.6; the energy gain for this triaxial point is 3.1 MeV larger than that of the axial point.
* The overlap between the lowest-energy J=0 projected states from axial and triaxial configurations is close to 0.9.
* The discretization for Euler angles uses 6 points for α, 18 for β, and 12 for γ (corresponding to 24, 36, 24 points in the full 8π² volume), yielding expectation values of J² and J_z accurate to order 10⁻⁴.
* Particle-number projection using a nine-point formula gives matrix elements with absolute deviation lower than 10⁻⁸ and dispersion lower than 10⁻⁸.

## Context

This work extends the axial-only GCM with angular-momentum projection developed by Val et al. (2000), Bender et al. (2003, 2004) and others using Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic functionals, by allowing triaxial deformations and K-mixing. It enables fully microscopic five-dimensional quadrupole dynamics without the approximations of the Bohr-Hamiltonian approach used by Girod and Grammaticos (1978) and Libert et al. (1999) for the Gogny force, and by Prochniak et al. (2004) for Skyrme interactions. The method is a first step toward a full seven-dimensional GCM calculation mixing all five degrees of freedom of the quadrupole operator and the gauge angles for protons and neutrons.
