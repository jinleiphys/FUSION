# Single-pole Nature of the Detectable Lambda(1405)

> We have investigated the single-pole nature of the detectable Lambda(1405) in detail, by employing a chiral model having double poles of a KbarN-piSigma coupled channel T-matrix. The effects of the 1st pole and the 2nd pole on the observed piSigma mass spectrum in the Lambda(1405) region were analyzed separately by means of the Generalized Optical Potential. It is concluded that the 1st pole is responsible for both peak structures seen in the T(piSigma leftarrow KbarN)(T(21)) and T(piSigma leftarrow piSigma)(T(22)) invariant-mass spectra, and the 2nd pole due to an energy-dependent chiral interaction provides continuum amplitudes affecting the shape of peak structures in experimentally observable spectrum of the Lambda(1405).

# Single-pole Nature of the Detectable Lambda(1405)

## Key claim

The experimentally observable Λ(1405) resonance is of single-pole nature, formed as a Feshbach resonance from the 1st pole of the coupled-channel T-matrix, despite the chiral model having double poles. The 2nd pole provides only continuum amplitudes that modify the peak shape but does not itself produce any peak structure in the detectable πΣ invariant-mass spectra.

## Method

The authors employed a two-channel (KbarN and πΣ) treatment of the I=0 part of the chiral dynamics from Hyodo and Weise (2008), using the leading-order Weinberg-Tomozawa interaction and loop functions. They introduced generalized optical potentials to decompose the T-matrix elements T_{21} (πΣ ← KbarN) and T_{22} (πΣ ← πΣ) into contributions from the 1st pole, the 2nd pole, and the remaining part. By varying arbitrary factors f_{11}, f_{22}, and f_{12} that scale the interaction strengths, they traced how the pole positions and spectral shapes change, and analyzed the formation mechanism of peak structures.

## Key numbers

- 1st pole position: Ξ_{1st pole} = 1432 − i17 MeV
- 2nd pole position: Ξ_{2nd pole} = 1398 − i73 MeV (in the full coupled-channel model)
- 2nd pole position at f_{12}=0 (no coupling via KbarN): Ξ_{pole} = 1388 − i96 MeV
- PDG value of Λ(1405): mass = 1405.1^{+1.3}_{-1.0} MeV/c², width = 50.5 ± 2.0 MeV (Patrignani, 2016)
- Best-fit single-pole parameters from Hassanvand et al. (2013) for HADES data: (M_{pole}c², Γ) = (1405^{+11}_{-9}, 62 ±10) MeV
- Double-pole fit parameters from Siebenson and Fabbietti (2013) for HADES data: (1418, 58) MeV and (1375, 146) MeV
- Residue of 1st pole: A_{1st pole} ∝ 0.01 + i1.33
- Residue of 2nd pole: A_{2nd pole} ∝ 3.37 − i8.71

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether the Λ(1405) has a single-pole or double-pole nature, which was raised by chiral dynamics calculations (Oller, 2001; Jido et al., 2003) that found two poles in the T-matrix. The authors directly challenge the double-pole interpretation used in analyses of CLAS photoproduction data (Roca, 2013; Mai, 2015; Nakamura, 2014) and HADES data (Siebenson and Fabbietti, 2013), as well as the claim by Magas et al. (2005) that different reaction spectra provide evidence for two poles. The paper supports the single-pole phenomenological analyses of Hassanvand et al. (2013) that contributed to the PDG value, and argues that the 2nd pole cannot produce observable peak structures because its energy-dependent chiral interaction yields continuum amplitudes incompatible with Breit-Wigner-type resonance formation. This conclusion provides a basis for studies of anti-kaonic nuclei (Akaishi, 2002; Yamazaki, 2002).
