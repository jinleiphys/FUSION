# On the Color-Singlet States in Many-Quark Model with the su(4)-Algebraic Structure. III

> A phase structure and phase transitions are investigated in the modified Bonn quark model. The force strength, which represents the particle-hole type interaction, is devised so as to depend on the quark number, namely quark number density. As a result, it is shown that the phase transition from the quark-triplet state as a nucleon to the quark-pair state as a color-superconducting state occurs as the density increases.

## Key claim

By introducing a quark-number-dependent force strength \(\chi\) (rather than a fixed value), the modified Bonn quark model can be made consistent with the common understanding that the quark-triplet phase (nucleonic matter) occurs at low density and the quark-pair phase (color-superconducting state) occurs at high density, with the phase transition proceeding through intermediate phases or directly depending on a parameter \(z\).

## Method

The authors treat the force strength \(\chi\) as an operator \(\hat{\chi}\) that depends on the quark number operator \(\hat{N}\) and the order parameter operator \(\hat{n}^0\), with a free parameter \(z\) that classifies the phase transition patterns. They then compute the eigenvalue \(\chi\) as a function of the quark number \(N^0\) and the total degeneracy \(\Omega^0\), and trace the ground-state energy and order parameter \(n^0\) across the phase diagram in the \(\chi\)-\(N^0\) plane for various values of \(z\), using \(\Omega^0=6\) for numerical examples.

## Key numbers

* For \(\Omega^0=6\), the critical points on the phase diagram are specified by \(\chi_0(\Omega^0)\), \(\chi_1(\Omega^0)\), and \(\chi_2(\Omega^0)\); for example, \(\chi_1(6) = -0.041099\) and \(\chi_2(6) = -0.032811\).
* For \(z=1.0\) (case 1), the quark-triplet phase is realized for \(N^0 \leq 5\) (with \(\Omega^0=6\)), and the order parameter changes continuously through the intermediate phase.
* For \(z=1.8\) (case 2), the curve crosses the line P\(_0\)P\(_2\) at \(N^0=2\Omega^0 = 12\).
* For \(z=2.2\) (case 3), the curve crosses the line P\(_1\)P\(_2\); the approximate linear fit for \(\Omega^0=6\) gives \(A=1.040754\) and \(B=0.00886469\), with an error of 0.95% at \(N^0=13\).
* For \(z=3.2\) (case 4), the phase transition proceeds from quark-triplet directly to the intermediate phase \(Q_{i_2}\) and then to quark-pair.
* For \(z=5.0\) (case 5), the phase transition occurs directly from quark-triplet to quark-pair, with a discontinuous order parameter.

## Context

This paper is Part III of a series extending the Bonn quark model, originally introduced to describe the nucleon and \(\Delta\)-resonance as quark-triplet states. It builds on the exact eigenstate constructions in Part I and the ground-state energy analysis in Part II, where the order parameter \(n^0\) was identified. The work addresses the contradiction found in earlier parts (A, C, II) where fixed \(\chi\) gave phase transitions opposite to the common understanding from QCD, namely that color-superconducting phases (studied by various authors around 2000) should appear at high density. The introduction of a density-dependent force strength enables the model to reproduce the expected ordering of phases and opens the way to studying the nature (continuous or discontinuous) of the phase transition between nucleonic matter and color-superconducting matter in this schematic \(su(4)\) model.
