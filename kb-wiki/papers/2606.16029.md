# Asymmetric binary Bose mixtures: a Functional Renormalisation Group study

> Zero-temperature binary Bose mixtures with mass and intraspecies couplings imbalances are studied using the functional renormalisation group (FRG) framework. We find that this dual asymmetry induces a spontaneous redistribution of quantum fluctuations between the components. This effect leads to a significant divergence in the quasiparticle residues of the two species, a phenomenon not captured in mean-field treatments. Critically, our FRG analysis predicts a systematic reduction of the density-channel sound velocity with increasing asymmetry -- a result that qualitatively contradicts the behaviour predicted by standard Bogoliubov theory. The ratio of the renormalisation factors is proposed as a robust quantitative probe of many-body correlations and fluctuation transfer. These findings provide a consistent non-perturbative framework for interpreting future experiments on heterogeneous mixtures.

# Asymmetric binary Bose mixtures: a Functional Renormalisation Group study

## Key claim
The paper claims that mass and intraspecies coupling asymmetries in zero-temperature binary Bose mixtures induce a spontaneous redistribution of quantum fluctuations between components, leading to a systematic reduction of the density-channel sound velocity with increasing asymmetry, a result that qualitatively contradicts the behaviour predicted by standard Bogoliubov theory. The authors hedge that these findings are obtained within a specific FRG truncation that is reliable in the dilute regime (gas parameter $\sqrt{n a^3} \ll 1$) and that beyond this regime, higher-order terms may become important and quantitative results should be interpreted with caution.

## Method
The authors use the functional renormalisation group (FRG) framework based on the average effective action, employing a derivative expansion truncated at quadratic order in the effective potential and assuming mass renormalisation factors do not run with scale. They solve the system of flow equations for the running couplings, condensate densities, and wave-function renormalisation factors at fixed chemical potentials, using the optimised Litim cutoff regulator, with initial conditions extracted from the classical action at an ultraviolet scale $\Lambda = 1$ and density $n/\Lambda^3 = 0.1$.

## Key numbers
- The mass ratio range studied is $R = m_1/m_2$ from $R=1$ to $R=4$, corresponding to physical systems such as $^{87}\mathrm{Rb}\text{-}^{85}\mathrm{Rb}$ ($R \simeq 1$) and $^{6}\mathrm{Li}\text{-}^{23}\mathrm{Na}$ ($R \simeq 4$).
- The relative dynamical renormalisation factor $\sigma = (Z_2/Z_1 - 1)$ decreases almost monotonically as the mass ratio approaches unity, dropping by almost 6 times when going from $R=4$ to $R=1$ at fixed interspecies coupling $\gamma = -0.13$.
- At $s = \beta_2/\beta_1 = 2$, the dynamical renormalisation factor $\sigma$ is found to be between $4\%$ and $4.5\%$, scaling approximately linearly with $s$ in the region $s \simeq 1$.
- Varying the interspecies coupling $\gamma$ by a factor of three results in a relatively minor upward shift of approximately $5\%$ in the corresponding curve for $\sigma$.
- The starting values of the stability factor $\tau = \sqrt{\beta_1\beta_2 - \gamma^2}$ are $4.38$ and $2.36$ for the two initial interspecies couplings $\gamma_{st} = -0.5$ and $\gamma_{st} = -1.5$ respectively.

## Context
This work extends previous FRG studies by Isa1 and Isa2 that focused on symmetric Bose mixtures, providing the first non-perturbative analysis of how mass and coupling-constant imbalances shape dynamical properties. The results qualitatively contradict standard Bogoliubov theory (Bo, Sto, Chi) predictions for the density-channel sound velocity, and go beyond mean-field treatments (Pet, Cik, Sta, Ota) that cannot capture the species-dependent renormalisation of quasiparticle residues. The findings enable a quantitative framework for interpreting future experiments on heterogeneous mixtures such as $^{6}\mathrm{Li}\text{-}^{23}\mathrm{Na}$ and $^{41}\mathrm{K}\text{-}^{87}\mathrm{Rb}$, and suggest that the ratio of renormalisation factors serves as a robust probe of many-body correlations and fluctuation transfer between components.
