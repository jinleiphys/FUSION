# Identical Bands in Superdeformed Nuclei: A Relativistic Description

> Relativistic Mean Field Theory in the rotating frame is used to describe superdeformed nuclei. Nuclear currents and the resulting spatial components of the vector meson fields are fully taken into account. Identical bands in neighboring Rare Earth nuclei are investigated and excellent agreement with recent experimental data is observed.

# Identical Bands in Superdeformed Nuclei: A Relativistic Description

## Key claim

Relativistic Mean Field Theory with full inclusion of nuclear currents (spatial components of vector meson fields) can reproduce identical bands in neighboring superdeformed nuclei to within 1 keV transition-energy differences, but only when both self-consistent polarization of the core and nuclear magnetism are included; a delicate cancellation between these two effects is required, and the precise mechanism is not fully understood.

## Method

The authors solve the relativistic self-consistent cranking (RSCC) equations for superdeformed nuclei, expanding Dirac spinors and meson fields in deformed oscillator bases up to 13 major shells. They use the NL1 parameter set (adjusted to nuclear matter and spherical nuclei) and include sigma, omega, and rho mesons plus the electromagnetic field, with nonlinear sigma self-coupling. For the odd-mass nucleus 151Tb, they remove one proton from the 152Dy core in four different single-particle orbits and solve fully self-consistently for each configuration, comparing results with and without nuclear magnetism and with and without core polarization.

## Key numbers

- Transition energy differences between 151Tb and 152Dy for the identical band (quantum numbers -+) are of order 1 keV, giving Delta E_gamma/E_gamma ~ 10^{-3}
- Without nuclear magnetism, the dynamic moment of inertia of 152Dy is much too small; a semiclassical correction overestimates it by roughly 10%
- Mass quadrupole moment of 152Dy decreases from 4350 to 4260 fm^2 over spin range 20 to 60 hbar; hexadecupole moment changes from 20600 to 19600 fm^4 over the same range
- Average charge quadrupole moment is 18.6 eb, compared to experimental value of 19 eb
- Hill-Wheeler deformation parameters: beta = 0.72, gamma = 0.7 degrees, corresponding to axis ratio 1:1.9
- At angular momentum I = 50 hbar, the static moment of inertia J^(1) for 152Dy is 86.41 (units not specified but in hbar^2/MeV); the identical band in 151Tb shows a relative change of only +0.10% in J^(1) and -0.001% in J^(2)
- The simple A^(5/3) rule would predict changes in moment of inertia of approximately 11 per mille; the calculated changes for the identical band are at least an order of magnitude smaller

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic calculations using the Strutinski method with rotating Nilsson or Saxon-Woods potentials (Werner and Dudek 1991, Ragnarsson 1990) and the pseudo-spin coupling scheme (Nazarewicz et al. 1990). It directly follows a first fully self-consistent investigation using the Skyrme III force in the Hg region (Chen et al. 1992), but focuses on the Dy region where pairing is negligible at high spin. The authors had previously performed cranked relativistic mean field calculations without nuclear magnetism (Koepf and Ring 1990) that reproduced quadrupole deformations but gave moments of inertia far too small. The present work demonstrates that full self-consistency and inclusion of nuclear currents are both essential, and that the NL1 parameter set (Reinhard et al. 1986) can reproduce identical bands without any free parameters. The results enable systematic studies of the cancellation mechanism between polarization and nuclear magnetism, and raise the question of whether similar cancellations occur in other mass regions.
