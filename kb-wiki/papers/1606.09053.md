# Triplet Pairing in pure neutron matter

> We study the zero temperature BCS gaps for the triplet channel in pure neutron matter using Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) evolved interactions. We use the dependence of the results on the SRG resolution scale, as a tool to analyze medium and many-body corrections. In particular, we study the effects of including the three-body interactions at leading order, which appear at N2LO in the Chiral EFT, as well as that of the first-order self-energy corrections on the zero temperature gap. In addition we also extract the transition temperature as a function of densities and verify the BCS scaling of the zero temperature gaps to the transition temperature. We observe that the self-energy effects are very crucial in order to reduce the SRG resolution scale dependence of the results, while the three-body effects at the leading order do not change the two-body resolution scale dependence. On the other hand, the results depend strongly on the three-body cut-off, emphasizing the importance of the missing higher-order three-body effects. We also observe that self-energy effects reduce the overall gap as well as shift the gap closure to lower densities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the SRG resolution scale dependence of triplet pairing gaps in pure neutron matter can be used as a diagnostic tool for missing many-body physics, but the results remain dependent on the bare interaction and the three-body cutoff, meaning the gaps are not yet model-independent predictions.

## Method

The authors solve the angle-averaged BCS gap equation for the coupled $^3P_2-^3F_2$ channel in pure neutron matter using SRG-evolved two-body interactions from both the chiral N3LO EM 500 and the phenomenological AV$_{18}$ potentials. They augment the chiral interactions with leading-order three-body forces at N2LO as a density-dependent two-body force, and include first-order self-energy corrections to the single-particle spectrum. As an alternative to solving the gap equation directly, they also extract gaps via the Weinberg eigenvalue method (stability analysis) and extend this approach to finite temperatures using the Thouless criterion to obtain transition temperatures.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the zero-temperature gap to the transition temperature follows the BCS result $\Delta(k_F)/T_c \sim 1.76$ for both singlet and triplet channels.
- The triplet gaps are studied for Fermi momenta in the range $1.0\ \text{fm}^{-1} < k_F < 3.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}$, where the triplet channel remains open.
- The N3LO EM 500 interaction has a chiral cutoff of $500\ \text{MeV} \sim 2.5\ \text{fm}^{-1}$, and results beyond $k_F \sim 2.0\ \text{fm}^{-1}$ are interpreted with caution.
- The SRG evolution parameter $\lambda$ is varied; results are independent of $\lambda$ near $k_F \approx 1.3\ \text{fm}^{-1}$.
- The $^1S_0$ channel gap closes for $k_F < 1.7\ \text{fm}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the study of Maurizio et al. (2014) on triplet pairing in pure neutron matter using chiral interactions and SRG-evolved potentials, and complements the approaches of Dong et al. (2013), Ding et al. (2015, 2016) who included short-range and long-range correlations via self-consistent Green's functions and Fermi liquid theory. The use of SRG evolution to isolate missing many-body effects, combined with leading-order chiral three-body forces, provides a systematic framework for assessing uncertainties in neutron star cooling models, particularly for interpreting the rapid cooling of Cassiopeia-A as reported by Page et al. (2010) and Ho et al. (2009).
