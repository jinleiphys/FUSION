# pi-pi Scattering and the Meson Resonance Spectrum

> A pi-pi, Kbar-K, and rho-rho(omega-omega) fully coupled channel model is used to predict the lowest isospin S, P, D, F-wave phase shifts and inelasticities for elastic pi-pi scattering from threshold to 2.0 GeV. As input the S-matrix is required to exhibit poles corresponding to the meson resonance table of the Particle Data Group. As expected, the pi-pi inelasticity is very strongly related to the opening of the Kbar-K channel near 1 GeV, and the opening of rho-rho(4pi) and omega-omega(6pi) channels in the 1.5 GeV region. The predictions of this model are compared to the various elastic pi-pi to pi-pi amplitudes, that were obtained from analyses of pi(-) p to pi(-) pi(+) n data. The role of the various resonances, in particular the glueball candidate f_0(1500) and the f_J(1710) is investigated.

## Key claim

A three-channel coupled model (pi-pi, Kbar-K, and rho-rho/omega-omega) with S-matrix poles fixed to known meson resonances can reproduce pi-pi phase shifts and inelasticities from threshold to 2.0 GeV, but the agreement depends critically on placing certain resonances on non-standard Riemann sheets. The f_0(1500) resonance must be placed on a non-standard pi-pi sheet (treated as a virtual resonance) to match data, and the f_J(1710) resonance is favored to have J=0 rather than J=2.

## Method

A separable-potential Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved for three coupled channels (pi-pi, Kbar-K, and rho-rho/omega-omega) for each angular momentum l=0,1,2,3. The S-matrix was constrained to have poles at the complex energies of known meson resonances from the Particle Data Group compilation, and the resulting elastic pi-pi phase shifts and inelasticities were compared to experimental amplitudes extracted from pi- p -> pi- pi+ n data. For S-waves, an additional attractive term was added to the pi-pi potential to reproduce low-energy behavior.

## Key numbers

- S-wave resonances imposed at complex energies (0.980, -0.025) GeV for f_0(980), (1.300, -0.200) GeV for f_0(1370), and (1.503, -0.060) GeV for f_0(1500)
- When f_0(1500) was placed on the non-standard pi-pi sheet (Im p_pi > 0), the model phase shifts showed significant improvement over the standard-sheet placement
- For J=2, the f_2(1525) resonance at (1.525, -0.038) GeV was placed on the non-standard pi-pi sheet, consistent with its 0.8% branching fraction to pi-pi
- For J=1, the rho(1700) at (1.700, -0.118) GeV was placed on the non-standard Kbar-K sheet to improve phase shift agreement above 1.3 GeV
- For J=3, resonances were imposed at (1.691, -0.080) GeV for rho_3(1690), (2.250, -0.125) GeV, and (2.700, -0.300) GeV
- Channel masses used: m_pi = 0.1396 GeV, m_K = 0.4937 GeV, m_rho = 0.7680 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channel models for S-wave pi-pi scattering (Cannata et al., 1989; Kaminski et al., 1994) and the authors' own three-channel S-wave model (Kloet and Loiseau, 1995) to all partial waves S, P, D, and F from threshold to 2.0 GeV. The model enables investigation of the role of the glueball candidate f_0(1500) and the disputed J value of f_J(1710) in pi-pi scattering, and can serve as a basis for extrapolation to higher energies for final-state interaction studies in reactions such as antiproton-proton annihilation.
