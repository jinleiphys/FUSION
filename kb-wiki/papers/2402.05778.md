# $П•^n$ trajectory bootstrap

> We perform an extensive bootstrap study of Hermitian and non-Hermitian theories based on the novel analytic continuation of $\langleП•^n\rangle$ or $\langle(iП•)^n\rangle$ in $n$. We first use the quantum harmonic oscillator to illustrate various aspects of the $П•^n$ trajectory bootstrap method, such as the large $n$ expansion, matching conditions, exact quantization condition, and high energy asymptotic behavior. Then we derive highly accurate solutions for the anharmonic oscillators with the parity invariant potential $V(П•)=П•^2+П•^{m}$ and the $\mathcal{PT}$ invariant potential $V(П•)=-(iП•)^{m}$ for a large range of integral $m$, showing the high efficiency and general applicability of this new bootstrap approach. For the Hermitian quartic and non-Hermitian cubic oscillators, we further verify that the non-integer $n$ results for $\langleП•^n\rangle$ or $\langle(iП•)^n\rangle$ are consistent with those from the wave function approach. In the $\mathcal{PT}$ invariant case, the existence of $\langle(iП•)^n\rangle$ with non-integer $n$ allows us to bootstrap the non-Hermitian theories with non-integer powers, such as fractional and irrational $m$.

## Context

This work extends the principle of minimal singularity proposed by Li (2023) from zero-dimensional scalar field theories to one-dimensional quantum mechanics, and generalizes the bootstrap approach to non-Hermitian PT symmetric systems studied by Bender (1998, 1999). The method enables the study of non-integer power potentials relevant to multi-critical Yang-Lee edge singularity models (Lencses 2022, 2023) and provides a framework that may apply to higher-dimensional non-positive bootstrap studies (Gliozzi 2013, 2014; Li 2017, 2021).
