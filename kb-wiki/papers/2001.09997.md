# Shape isomers in Pt, Hg and Pb isotopes with N $\le$ 126

> Deformation-energy surfaces of 54 even-even isotopes of Pt, Hg and Pb nuclei with neutron numbers up to 126 are investigated within a macroscopic-microscopic model based on the Lublin-Strasbourg-Drop macroscopic energy and shell plus pairing-energy corrections obtained from a Yukawa-folded mean-field potential at the desired deformation. A new, rapidly converging Fourier shape parametrization is used to describe nuclear shapes. The stability of shape isomeric states with respect to non-axial and higher-order deformations is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that shape isomers and shape coexistence in Pt, Hg, and Pb isotopes with neutron numbers up to 126 can be reliably predicted using a macroscopic-microscopic model with a new Fourier shape parametrization, but only after checking the stability of all identified minima against non-axial (triaxial) deformations, which often reduces barrier heights or eliminates apparent minima seen in axial-only calculations.

## Method

The authors computed deformation-energy surfaces for 54 even-even isotopes (170-204Pt, 172-206Hg, 174-208Pb) using the macroscopic-microscopic Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) model for the macroscopic energy, with shell and pairing corrections from a Yukawa-folded single-particle potential. They employed a new Fourier shape parametrization with optimal coordinates (q2, q3, q4, q6) plus a non-axial parameter eta, and minimized the total energy in a four-dimensional deformation space (q2, q3, q4, eta) and separately in (q2, q4, q6) to test higher-order effects. Pairing was treated with BCS plus an approximate GCM+GOA particle-number projection.

## Key numbers

- The largest ground-state deformation energy (E_def = E_tot^g.s. - E_tot^sph.) of approximately 3.5 MeV is predicted for 178-184Pt isotopes.
- In 170Pt, the oblate ground-state minimum lies only about 200 keV below the prolate minimum.
- The barrier separating the super-deformed (SD) isomer at q2 ≈ 0.6 from the normal-deformed minimum in Pt isotopes grows with neutron number to reach about 3 MeV in 204Pt.
- SD isomeric states in Pt are typically about 6 MeV above the ground state (E_tot^isomer - E_tot^g.s. ≈ 6 MeV).
- In Hg isotopes, the SD isomer at q2 ≈ 0.6 is located between 4 MeV (in 190Hg) and 16 MeV (in 206Hg) above the ground state.
- Strongly deformed oblate minima at q2 ≈ -0.45 in Pb isotopes lie at about 5 MeV above ground in 174Pb and 2.5 MeV in 186Pb.
- The barrier between oblate and prolate minima in 174-180Pt isotopes is about 1.5 to 2.5 MeV in axial calculations, but is significantly reduced when non-axial degrees of freedom are included.
- For the four heaviest Pt isotopes (202-204Pt), inclusion of non-axial deformations reduces the barrier height between the SD isomer and ground state by up to 0.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Pomorski et al., 2017) on shape isomers in the same mass region by using a larger set of isotopes, a denser deformation grid, inclusion of non-axial degrees of freedom, and an improved pairing treatment with approximate particle-number projection. It differs from the systematic study of Moller et al. (2012), which used a more restricted (epsilon2, gamma, epsilon4) deformation space and the finite-range liquid droplet model, by employing the LSD macroscopic energy and a more general Fourier shape parametrization that reveals super-deformed minima not present in that earlier work. The results enable comparison with experimental data on super-deformed bands from the compilation of Ljungvall et al. (2016) and provide predictions for electric quadrupole moments that can be tested in future Coulomb-excitation or lifetime measurements.
