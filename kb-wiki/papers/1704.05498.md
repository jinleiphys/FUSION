# Critical parameters of consistent relativistic mean-field models

> In the present work, the critical temperature, critical pressure and critical density, known as the critical parameters related to the liquid-gas phase transition are calculated for 34 relativistic mean-field models, which were shown to satisfy nuclear matter constraints in a comprehensive study involving 263 models. The compressibility factor was calculated and all 34 models present values lower than the one obtained with the van der Waals equation of state. The critical temperatures were compared with experimental data and just two classes of models can reach values close to them. A correlation between the critical parameters and the incompressibility was obtained.

# Critical parameters of consistent relativistic mean-field models

## Key claim

The critical temperature, pressure, and density of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter are calculated for 34 relativistic mean-field (RMF) models that previously passed seven nuclear matter constraints; only the Z271 family of parametrizations and the density-dependent (DD) models approach the experimental critical values, with the Z271 family matching all three experimental critical parameters from Elliott et al. (2013).

## Method

The critical parameters (T_c, P_c, rho_c) are obtained by solving the conditions P_c = P(rho_c, T_c), (dP/drho)|_{rho_c,T_c}=0, and (d^2P/drho^2)|_{rho_c,T_c}=0 for symmetric nuclear matter (proton fraction 0.5) using the thermodynamical pressure derived from each RMF Lagrangian. The 34 models include 30 nonlinear models with sigma and omega self-interactions and cross terms, and 4 density-dependent coupling models (DD-F, TW99, DDHdelta, DD-MEdelta); one additional point-coupling model (FA3) was excluded because it fails to produce neutron star mass-radius curves at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 17.97 MeV for all eight Z271 parametrizations (Z271s2 through Z271v6), matching the experimental value of 17.9 +/- 0.4 MeV from Elliott et al. (2013)
- Critical pressure P_c = 0.303 MeV/fm^3 for all Z271 parametrizations, within the experimental range of 0.31 +/- 0.07 MeV/fm^3
- Critical density rho_c = 0.0509 fm^{-3} for all Z271 parametrizations, within the experimental range of 0.06 +/- 0.01 fm^{-3}
- Compressibility factor Z_c = P_c/(rho_c T_c) ranges from 0.284 (BKA24) to 0.331 (all Z271 models), all lower than the van der Waals value of 0.375
- Density-dependent models give T_c = 15.17-15.32 MeV, rho_c = 0.0491-0.0509 fm^{-3}, P_c = 0.235-0.245 MeV/fm^3
- Nonlinear models (excluding Z271) give T_c = 13.83-14.92 MeV, rho_c = 0.0439-0.0468 fm^{-3}, P_c = 0.176-0.209 MeV/fm^3

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Lourenco et al. (2017) which identified 35 consistent RMF parametrizations from 263 models by applying seven nuclear matter constraints. It confirms and generalizes the finding of earlier work (Ref. vdw5, 2005) that RMF models show model-dependent behavior in the liquid phase but scaling in the gaseous phase at T=T_c. The correlation between critical parameters and incompressibility K_0, previously observed for 128 Boguta-Bodmer parametrizations (Ref. rmft), is also found for the more sophisticated CRMF models, while no correlation with symmetry energy or its slope is observed. The results enable future investigations of strangeness effects on the liquid-gas phase transition in neutron-Lambda matter.
