# A massive high density effective theory

> We derive an effective theory for dense, cold and massive quark matter. To this end, we employ a general effective action formalism where antiquarks and quarks far from the Fermi surface, as well as hard gluons, are integrated out explicitly. We show that the resulting effective action depends crucially on the projectors used to separate quarks from antiquarks. If one neglects the quark masses in these projectors, the Feynman rules of the effective theory involve quark mass insertions which connect quark with antiquark propagators. Including the quark masses into these projectors, mass insertions do not appear and the Feynman rules are identical to those found in the zero-mass limit.

## Key claim

The way quark masses enter effective theories for dense, cold quark matter depends crucially on the choice of projection operators used to separate quarks from antiquarks; if one uses projectors based on solutions of the massive Dirac equation (the mHDET), the Feynman rules remain identical to the zero-mass limit and mass insertions do not appear, whereas if one uses massless projectors (the chiral HDET), mass insertions connect quark with antiquark propagators and additional diagrams are required.

## Method

The author employs a general effective action formalism where antiquarks, quarks far from the Fermi surface, and hard gluons are integrated out explicitly. Two sets of projection operators are considered: the standard massless projectors \(\Lambda^e_{\mathbf{k},0} = \frac{1}{2}(1 + e \gamma_0 \boldsymbol{\gamma} \cdot \hat{\mathbf{k}})\) and massive projectors \(\Lambda^e_{\mathbf{k},m} = \frac{1}{2E_k}[E_k + e \gamma_0 (\boldsymbol{\gamma} \cdot \mathbf{k} + m)]\) that remain regular in the \(m \to 0\) limit. The resulting effective actions are derived and compared, with the quark propagators and vertices analyzed in both the chiral HDET and the non-chiral mHDET.

## Key numbers

- The quark energy in the mHDET is given by \(E_k = \sqrt{\mathbf{k}^2 + m^2}\), and mass corrections are obtained by the Taylor expansion \(E_k = k + m^2/2k - m^4/8k^3 + \cdots\).
- Cutoffs considered are \(\Lambda_{\rm q} \sim g k_F\) for quarks and \(\Lambda_{\rm gl} \sim k_F\) for gluons, with \(\Lambda_{\rm q} \ll \Lambda_{\rm gl}\).
- For hard gluons, the self-energy \(\Pi_{22}\) from quark loops is of order \(g^2 k_F^2\), which is small compared to the free inverse propagator \(\Delta_{0,22}^{-1}\) of order \(k_F^2\).

## Context

This work extends the general effective action formalism developed by Reuter (2004) for dense quark matter, which provides a common basis for hard thermal loop (HTL), hard dense loop (HDL), and high density effective theories (HDET). It addresses the problem that in the chiral HDET used by Son (1999), Savage (2002), Schafer (2002), and Kryjevski (2003) for massive quarks, mass insertions connecting quark and antiquark propagators proliferate at higher orders in \(m/k_F\). The mHDET introduced here avoids this complication, enabling systematic organization of mass corrections to any order in \(m/k_F\) without additional Feynman diagrams, which should facilitate future calculations of color superconducting gap parameters and other observables.
