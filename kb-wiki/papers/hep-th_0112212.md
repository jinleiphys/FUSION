# Improved variational description of the Wick-Cutkosky model with the most general quadratic trial action

> We generalize the worldline variational approach to field theory by introducing a trial action which allows for anisotropic terms to be induced by external 4-momenta of Green's functions. By solving the ensuing variational equations numerically we demonstrate that within the (quenched) scalar Wick-Cutkosky model considerable improvement can be achieved over results obtained previously with isotropic actions. In particular, the critical coupling associated with the instability of the model is lowered, in accordance with expectations from Baym's proof of the instability in the unquenched theory. The physical picture associated with a different quantum mechanical motion of the dressed particle along and perpendicular to its classical momentum is discussed. Indeed, we find that for large couplings the dressed particle is strongly distorted in the direction of its four-momentum. In addition, we obtain an exact relation between the renormalized coupling of the theory and the propagator. Along the way we introduce new and efficient methods to evaluate the averages needed in the variational approach and apply them to the calculation of the 2-point function.

## Key claim

By introducing a trial action that allows separate retardation functions for longitudinal and transverse directions relative to the external four-momentum, the variational description of the Wick-Cutkosky model yields considerable improvement over previous isotropic results; in particular, the critical coupling for the quenched theory is lowered, consistent with expectations from Baym's proof of instability in the unquenched theory.

## Method

The authors generalize Feynman's worldline variational approach by using a quadratic trial action with two independent retardation functions \(f_L(\sigma)\) and \(f_T(\sigma)\) for the longitudinal and transverse components relative to the external momentum \(p_\mu\). They derive variational equations for the profile functions \(A_L(E)\) and \(A_T(E)\) and the parameter \(\lambda\) from Mano's equation, then solve these equations numerically by iteration for the quenched scalar Wick-Cutkosky model with physical nucleon mass \(M = 939\) MeV and pion mass \(m = 140\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling in the isotropic case was \(\alpha_c \approx 0.815\); the anisotropic trial action lowers this critical coupling.
- The dimensionless coupling is defined as \(\alpha = g^2 / (4\pi M^2)\).
- Physical masses used: nucleon mass \(M = 939\) MeV, pion mass \(m = 140\) MeV.
- The variational equations were solved with a convergence criterion of 1 part in \(10^6\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous variational studies of the Wick-Cutkosky model (WC1, WC2) which used isotropic trial actions, and parallels similar anisotropic treatments for the polaron by the same group (polaron_aniso). The improved description enables more reliable non-perturbative calculations of propagators and effective couplings in scalar field theories, and provides a benchmark for Monte-Carlo studies by Tjon and coworkers (Tjon1, Tjon2) and Dyson-Schwinger analyses by Ahlig and Alkofer (Alkofer).
