# On collective octupole degrees of freedom -- next pieces of the formal background

> The concept of an intrinsic system can be extended to the case of collective octupole degrees of freedom by exploiting the symmetry properties with respect to transformations of the octahedral group O_h. Explicit formulas for scalar invariants as polynomials of intrinsic variables are presented. A method of constructing a basis in the space of functions on the octupole intrinsic space is proposed.

# On collective octupole degrees of freedom, next pieces of the formal background

## Key claim
The paper extends the concept of an intrinsic (principal axes) reference frame to collective octupole degrees of freedom using the octahedral group \(O_h\), but shows that the two previously introduced variants of intrinsic variables (F₁⁻-type and F₂⁻-type) are not fully equivalent, specifically, the F₂⁻ variant does not cover the whole octupole space, as demonstrated by a contradiction in the values of rotational invariants for certain points.

## Method
The author exploits the decomposition of the seven-dimensional octupole space into irreducible representations of the octahedral group \(O_h\) (A₂⁻ ⊕ F₁⁻ ⊕ F₂⁻) to define intrinsic coordinates \((b, \mathbf{f}, \mathbf{g})\). Explicit polynomial formulas for the four elementary rotational scalars \(\eta_2, \eta_4, \eta_6, \eta_{10}\) (and one pseudoscalar of order 15) are derived for both the F₁⁻ and F₂⁻ intrinsic variants. A basis in the Hilbert space of functions on the octupole intrinsic space is constructed by symmetrizing polynomials in the deformation variables \((b, \mathbf{f})\) times rotation matrices \(\mathcal{D}^J_{MK}\), then imposing that the resulting functions belong to the domain of the Laplace-Beltrami operator; the Maxima computer algebra system is used to find linear combinations satisfying this condition for total polynomial order \(N \le 4\) and angular momentum \(J \le 6\).

## Key numbers
- The octupole space decomposes into irreducible representations of \(O_h\) with dimensions 1 (A₂⁻), 3 (F₁⁻), and 3 (F₂⁻).
- There are 4 elementary scalar invariants of orders 2, 4, 6, and 10, plus one pseudoscalar of order 15.
- The Laplace-Beltrami operator in the intrinsic frame contains a deformation Jacobian factor \(d = 8\left(b^3 - (15/16) b(f_x^2+f_y^2+f_z^2) + 2(15/16)^{3/2} f_x f_y f_z\right)\).
- The basis construction has been carried out for total polynomial order \(N \le 4\) and angular momentum \(J \le 6\), with procedures applicable up to \(N \le 10\), \(J \le 12\).
- For \(N=4, J=0\), 5 functions survive symmetrization, from which 2 linear combinations satisfy the Laplace-Beltrami domain condition, one proportional to \(\eta_2^2\) and one proportional to \(\eta_4\).

## Context
This work extends the intrinsic-system formalism for octupole degrees of freedom previously introduced by the same author in Prochniak (Acta Phys. Pol. B, 2015) and in collaboration with Greiner (2015). It builds on the quadrupole-case basis construction methods of Prochniak (1999) and the general theory of invariants of binary forms from Olver (1999). The results provide formal tools needed to construct a complete basis in the combined quadrupole-plus-octupole collective space, which is required for describing nuclei with static octupole deformation in the actinide region (Gaffney et al., 2013) and lighter N=82 nuclei (Komorowska et al., 2019), as well as for theoretical treatments combining quadrupole and octupole deformations (Dobrowolski et al., 2019). Open questions remain: there is no formal proof that the F₁⁻ variant covers the whole octupole space, and the integration measure containing the factor \(|d|\) appears to preclude fully analytical results for scalar products of basis functions.
