# Effective field theory calculation of nd radiative capture at thermal energies

> The cross section for the thermal neutron capture by the deuteron is calculated with pionless Effective Field Theory(EFT). No new Three-Nucleon forces are needed up to next-to-next-to-leading order in order to achieve cut-off independent results, besides those fixed by the triton binding energy and Nd scattering length in the triton channel. The cross-section is accurately determined to be $σ_{tot}=[0.503\pm 0.003]mb$. At zero energies, the magnetic $M1$-transition gives the dominant contribution and is calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^2$LO). Close agreement between the available experimental data and the calculated cross section is reached. We demonstrate convergence and cutoff independence order by order in the low-energy expansion.

## Key claim

The cross section for thermal neutron capture by the deuteron is calculated using pionless Effective Field Theory (EFT) and determined to be 0.503 ± 0.003 mb, with no new three-nucleon forces required beyond those already fixed by the triton binding energy and Nd scattering length in the triton channel, contrary to what some potential-model calculations might suggest.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev integral equation for nd-scattering and the triton bound state up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) in pionless EFT, using a cutoff regulator between 150 and 500 MeV. The magnetic M1 transition amplitude was computed by sandwiching photon-interaction kernels between the Faddeev amplitudes, with the three-body forces H0 and H2 fixed respectively by the doublet-S-wave nd scattering length and the triton binding energy. The two-nucleon parameters were determined from the deuteron binding momentum γt = 45.7025 MeV, triplet effective range ρt = 1.764 fm, singlet scattering length as = -23.714 fm, and singlet effective range rs = 2.73 fm.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section at thermal energy: σtot = [0.503 ± 0.003] mb, with contributions of 0.485 mb (LO), 0.011 mb (NLO), and 0.007 mb (N²LO)
- Experimental value for comparison: 0.508 ± 0.015 mb (Jurney et al., for 2200 m/sec neutrons)
- Cutoff variation of cross section at E = 2.65 × 10⁻⁸ MeV: 0.0012 (LO), 0.00060 (NLO), 0.0000090 (N²LO) between Λ = 150 MeV and Λ = 500 MeV
- Expansion parameter estimate: γt/mπ ≈ 1/3, giving estimated N³LO uncertainty of about 0.003 mb
- Input parameters: deuteron binding energy B = 2.225 MeV, nucleon mass M = 938.918 MeV, isoscalar nucleon magnetic moment k0 = 0.4399, isovector nucleon magnetic moment k1 = 2.35294, L1 ≈ -4.5 fm

## Context

This work extends the application of pionless EFT to the nd radiative capture process at thermal energies, following earlier calculations for np → dγ by Rupak (1999) and Ando (2006) and for nd capture at higher energies by Sadeghi et al. The results contradict the findings of potential-model calculations by Viviani et al., which used Argonne v18/Urbana IX interactions and obtained cross sections of 0.578 mb (with explicit Delta admixtures) and 0.558 mb (with gauge-invariant currents plus three-body currents), overpredicting experiment by 14% and 10% respectively. The EFT result shows that no new three-nucleon forces beyond those in nd scattering are needed, and enables model-independent predictions with systematically improvable theoretical uncertainties for this low-energy observable.
