# Ordinary muon capture as a probe of virtual transitions of $ββ$ decay

> A reliable theoretical description of double-beta-decay processes needs a possibility to test the involved virtual transitions against experimental data. Unfortunately, only the lowest virtual transition can be probed by the traditional electron-capture or $β^-$-decay experiments. In this article we propose that calculated amplitudes for many virtual transitions can be probed by experiments measuring rates of ordinary muon capture (OMC) to the relevant intermediate states. The first results from such experiments are expected to appear soon. As an example we discuss the $ββ$ decays of $^{76}$Ge and $^{106}$Cd and the corresponding OMC for the $^{76}$Se and $^{106}$Cd nuclei in the framework of the proton-neutron QRPA with realistic interactions. It is found that the OMC observables, just like the $2νββ$-decay amplitudes, strongly depend on the strength of the particle-particle part of the proton-neutron interaction.

## Key claim

Ordinary muon capture (OMC) rates can probe the nuclear wave functions of many virtual intermediate states involved in double-beta decay, not just the lowest one as in traditional electron-capture or beta-decay experiments; however, the OMC results cannot be directly interpreted as amplitudes of the virtual transitions because the muon capture process also activates induced parts of the nucleonic current (weak-magnetism and pseudoscalar terms).

## Method

The authors performed proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pnQRPA) calculations with realistic interactions (Bonn one-boson-exchange potential) for the A=76 and A=106 isobaric systems, using Woods-Saxon single-particle energies. They computed partial ordinary muon capture rates for transitions from the ground state of ⁷⁶Se to 1⁺ and 2⁻ states of ⁷⁶As, and from ¹⁰⁶Cd to 1⁺ states of ¹⁰⁶Ag, and compared these with corresponding beta-decay log ft values as a function of the particle-particle strength parameter g_pp.

## Key numbers

- For ⁷⁶As(2⁻₁) → ⁷⁶Se(0⁺_g.s.), the experimental log ft = 9.7; the calculation with g_pp = 1.0 for the 2⁻ channel reproduces this value.
- For ¹⁰⁶Ag(1⁺₁) → ¹⁰⁶Cd(0⁺_g.s.), the experimental log ft > 4.2; the calculation with g_pp = 0.8 gives reasonable agreement.
- The pnQRPA breaking point for the A=76 system occurs around g_pp ≈ 1.2, where the recoil matrix element grows unphysically large.
- For the A=76 system, the OMC rate to the first 1⁺ state becomes very small for g_pp values beyond 1.1.
- The OMC rate distribution for 1⁺ capture peaks strongly between 10 MeV and 20 MeV for the A=106 system, and below 4 MeV for the A=76 system.

## Context

This work extends the use of ordinary muon capture, previously applied to 1s-0d shell nuclei by Gorringe et al. (1999), to heavier double-beta-decay candidates as a probe of virtual transitions. It addresses the limitation that traditional electron-capture or beta-decay experiments can only test the lowest intermediate state, while OMC can access higher-lying states. The first experimental proposal for measuring partial muon-capture rates in double-beta-decay candidates (A=48 to A=150) has been submitted to the Paul Scherrer Institut by the Dubna-Louvain-Orsay-PSI-Jyväskylä collaboration, and the results are expected to enable testing of the pnQRPA wave functions used in calculations of both 2νββ and 0νββ decay rates.
