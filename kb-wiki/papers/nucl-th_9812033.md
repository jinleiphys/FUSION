# Nonextensive statistical effects in nuclear physics problems

> Recent progresses in statistical mechanics indicate the Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics as the natural generalization of the standard classical and quantum statistics, when memory effects and long-range forces are not negligible. In this framework, weakly nonextensive statistical deviations can strongly reduce the puzzling discrepancies between experimental data and theoretical previsions for solar neutrinos and for pion transverse-momentum correlations in Pb-Pb high-energy nuclear collisions.

# Nonextensive statistical effects in nuclear physics problems

## Key claim

Weakly nonextensive statistical deviations (with entropic parameter |q-1| < 0.02) can strongly reduce the puzzling discrepancies between experimental data and theoretical predictions for both solar neutrino fluxes and pion transverse-momentum correlations in Pb-Pb high-energy nuclear collisions, but the mechanism cannot fully solve the solar neutrino problem (best-fit chi-squared of 35 remains large).

## Method

The authors apply Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics, which generalizes Boltzmann-Gibbs entropy via a parameter q, to two distinct nuclear-physics problems. For the solar neutrino problem, they model the solar core as a weakly non-ideal plasma (plasma parameter Gamma ~ 0.1) where non-Markovian memory effects and long-range forces produce small deviations from the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution, and they compute the resulting changes in thermonuclear reaction rates and neutrino fluxes using homology relations. For high-energy heavy-ion collisions, they calculate the event-by-event transverse-momentum fluctuation measure Phi_{p_perp} for a pion gas using the generalized Bose-Einstein distribution and compare it to NA49 experimental data from central Pb+Pb collisions at 158 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- Solar core plasma parameter: Gamma_sun ~ 0.1, indicating a weakly non-ideal plasma where Debye-Huckel conditions are only approximately verified
- Solar core thermal energy: kT_sun ~ 1.36 keV
- Solar plasma frequency timescale: t_pl ~ 10^{-17} sec, comparable to collision time t_coll ~ 10^{-17} sec
- Best-fit single-delta model for solar neutrino fluxes: delta = 0.005 with chi-squared = 35 (compared to SSM chi-squared > 74)
- Best-fit two-delta model: delta_{(17)} = -0.018 and delta_{(34)} = 0.030 with chi-squared = 20
- Boron flux can change by up to 50% and beryllium flux by up to 30% due to small delta values
- Standard extensive thermodynamics prediction for pion gas: Phi_{p_perp} = 6.5 MeV at T = 120 MeV
- Experimental NA49 value for Pb-Pb collisions: Phi_{p_perp} = 0.7 +/- 0.5 MeV
- Experimental value for p-p collisions: Phi_{p_perp} = 4.2 +/- 0.5 MeV
- Best-fit nonextensive parameter for pion gas: q = 1.015 (q-1 = 0.015) at freeze-out temperature T = 120 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Tsallis nonextensive thermostatistics framework (Tsallis, 1988) to two longstanding problems in nuclear astrophysics and heavy-ion physics. For the solar neutrino problem, it builds on prior work by Quarati and collaborators showing that non-Maxwellian distributions affect reaction rates, and uses homology relations from Castellani (1997) to estimate flux variations. For heavy-ion collisions, it addresses the discrepancy between the standard thermodynamic calculation of Phi_{p_perp} by Mrowczynski (1998) and the NA49 experimental data from Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV. The results suggest that nonextensive statistics could serve as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and predict stronger suppression effects for heavier mesons and baryons that could be tested in future experiments.
