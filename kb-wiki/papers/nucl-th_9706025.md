# Polarization Observables in $A(d,p)$ Breakup and Quark Degrees of Freedom in the Deuteron

> The differential cross section, the tensor analyzing power, $T_{20}$, and the polarization transfer, $κ_0$, in $^{12}C(d,p)$ breakup at relativistic energy are calculated within a model which incorporates multiple scattering and Pauli principle at quark level. It is shown that the rescattering and quark exchange affect drastically the polarization observables for kinematical region corresponding to high internal momentum in the deuteron.

# Polarization Observables in $A(d,p)$ Breakup and Quark Degrees of Freedom in the Deuteron

## Key claim
Multiple scattering and quark exchange effects drastically alter the differential cross section, tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$, and polarization transfer $\kappa_0$ in $^{12}C(d,p)$ breakup at relativistic energy, but only for the kinematical region corresponding to high internal momentum in the deuteron; the Melosh transformation does not significantly affect the final results.

## Method
The author modified the Bertocchi-Treleani approach based on Sitenko-Glauber multiple scattering theory by incorporating a relativistic deuteron wave function in the infinite momentum frame and applying the Pauli principle at the constituent quark level via the Resonating Group Method. The calculation included elastic rescattering of deuteron constituents, inelastic collision of the constituent neutron, and contributions from nucleon resonance states generated by quark exchange, using the lowest 10 resonance states with effective numbers larger than $10^{-4}$. The invariant differential cross section, $T_{20}$, and $\kappa_0$ for $0^{\circ}$ inclusive $^{12}C(d,p)$ breakup were computed from these modified wave functions.

## Key numbers
- Total cross section $\sigma^T_{n^{12}C}$ taken as experimental value for $\sigma^T_{p^{12}C} = 340$ mb.
- Differential cross sections approximated by universal curve $A_1 e^{-B_1k^2_{\perp}} + A_2 e^{-B_2k^2_{\perp}}$ with $A_1 = 2611.3$ mb/(GeV/c)$^2$, $B_1 = 69.3$ (GeV/c)$^{-2}$, $A_2 = 119.3$ mb/(GeV/c)$^2$, $B_2 = 4.3$ (GeV/c)$^{-2}$.
- Parameters for average resonance chosen as $\sigma^T_{N_R\ ^{12}C} = 400$ mb, $A_1^R = 2373.9$ mb/(GeV/c)$^2$, $B_1^R = 77.0$ (GeV/c)$^{-2}$, with $A_2^R = A_2$ and $B_2^R = B_2$.
- Renormalization of the $S$ wave shown in Fig. 1 for several deuteron wave functions; the corresponding effect for the $D$ wave is less than 1%.

## Context
This work extends earlier nonrelativistic calculations of multiple scattering effects in $A(d,p)$ breakup by Bertocchi and Treleani (1972) and by others such as Perdrisat and Punjabi, and incorporates quark exchange effects previously studied by Kobushkin, Syamtomov, and Glozman (1995). The calculations address the systematic difference in $T_{20}$ measurements for $^1H$ and $^{12}C$ targets observed by Azhgirey and collaborators, and leave open the question of $np$ spin-flip amplitude contributions and detailed $A$-dependence of polarization observables for future work.
