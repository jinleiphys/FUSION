# Relativistic effects on the neutron charge form factor in the constituent quark model

> The neutron charge form factor GEn(Q**2) is investigated within a constituent quark model formulated on the light-front. It is shown that, if the quark initial motion is neglected in the Melosh rotations, the Dirac neutron form factor F1n(Q**2)$ receives a relativistic correction which cancels exactly against the Foldy term in GEn(Q**2), as it has been recently argued by Isgur. Moreover, at the same level of approximation the ratio of the proton to neutron magnetic form factors GMp(Q**2) / GMn(Q**2) is still given by the naive SU(6)-symmetry expectation, -3/2. However, it is also shown that the full Melosh rotations break SU(6) symmetry, giving rise to GEn(Q**2) =/ 0 and GMp(Q**2)/GMn(Q**2) =/ -3/2 even when a SU(6)-symmetric canonical wave function is assumed. It turns out that relativistic effects alone cannot explain simultaneously the experimental data on GEn(Q**2)$ and GMp(Q**2)/GMn(Q**2).

## Key claim

Within a light-front constituent quark model, if the quark initial transverse motion is neglected in the Melosh rotations, the Dirac neutron form factor receives a relativistic correction that cancels exactly against the Foldy term in the neutron charge form factor, preserving the SU(6)-symmetry prediction GEn(Q2) = 0; however, when full Melosh rotations are included, relativistic effects alone cannot simultaneously explain the experimental data on GEn(Q2) and the ratio GMp(Q2)/GMn(Q2).

## Method

The authors formulated a constituent quark model on the light-front, using a pure SU(6)-symmetric canonical S-wave wave function for the three-quark system. They computed the nucleon Dirac and Pauli form factors from the plus component of the electromagnetic current, including one-body currents with constituent quark form factors, and then derived the Sachs form factors GEn and GMp/GMn. The analysis compared two approximations: (1) neglecting the initial transverse motion of quarks in the Melosh rotations, and (2) including the full Melosh rotations, to isolate the role of relativistic effects.

## Key numbers

- The experimental neutron charge radius is rn2 = -0.113 ± 0.005 fm2, while the Foldy contribution alone is approximately -0.126 fm2, implying a small intrinsic charge radius r1n2 ≈ 0.013 ± 0.005 fm2.
- In the approximation neglecting initial transverse motion, the ratio GMp(Q2)/GMn(Q2) remains at the SU(6)-symmetry value of -3/2.
- For a constituent quark mass m = 220 MeV, when the average transverse momentum ⟨p⊥⟩ exceeds m (e.g., ⟨p⊥⟩ ≈ 0.58 GeV from the quark potential model of Capstick and Isgur (1986)), the neutron charge radius from relativistic effects alone does not exceed about 40% of its experimental value.
- The experimental ratio GMp/GMn is approximately μp/μn ≈ -1.46, with only 10-15% uncertainty up to Q2 ∼ 1 (GeV/c)2.

## Context

This work extends the argument of Isgur (1999) that the Foldy term in GEn cancels against a relativistic correction to F1n, by demonstrating this cancellation explicitly within a light-front constituent quark model. It also contradicts the claim by Coester (1992) that the leading term in GEn at low Q2 is given by its Foldy term, showing that claim ignores the Isgur cancellation mechanism. The results show that relativistic effects from Melosh rotations alone cannot explain the experimental data, pointing to the need for additional mechanisms such as mixed-symmetry S-wave components from spin-spin forces, flavour-dependent constituent quark form factors, or many-body electromagnetic currents.
