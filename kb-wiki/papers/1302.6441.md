# Effect of jets on $v_4/v_2^2$ ratio and constituent quark scaling in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The Monte Carlo HYDJET++ model, that combines parametrized hydrodynamics with jets, is employed to study formation of second v_2 and fourth v_4 components of the anisotropic flow in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at energies of the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), sqrt{s}=200 AGeV and sqrt{s}=2.76 ATeV, respectively. It is shown that the quenched jets contribute to the soft part of the v_2(p_T) and v_4(p_T) spectra. The jets increase the ratio v_4/v_2^2 thus leading to deviations of the ratio from the value of 0.5 predicted by the ideal hydrodynamics. Together with the event-by-event fluctuations, the influence of jets can explain quantitatively the ratio v_4/v_2^2 at p_T < 2 GeV/c for both energies and qualitatively the rise of its high-p_T tail at LHC. Jets are also responsible for violation of the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling at LHC despite the fact that the scaling is fulfilled for the hydro-part of particle spectra.

## Key claim

Jets increase the ratio \(v_4/v_2^2\) above the ideal-hydrodynamic value of 0.5, and together with event-by-event fluctuations can explain the ratio quantitatively for \(p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\) at both RHIC and LHC energies; jets are also responsible for violating number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling at LHC, even though the scaling holds for the pure hydrodynamic part of the particle spectra.

## Method

The authors used the Monte Carlo event generator HYDJET++, which combines a parametrized hydrodynamic description of the soft (thermal) particle production (via the FASTMC module) with a hard-process module (HYDJET) that simulates jet production and quenching in the quark-gluon plasma. They generated approximately 60,000 minimum-bias Au+Au events at \(\sqrt{s}=200A\) GeV and 50,000 Pb+Pb events at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76A\) TeV, then computed the second (\(v_2\)) and fourth (\(v_4\)) Fourier harmonics of the azimuthal particle distribution as functions of transverse momentum \(p_T\) and centrality, separating the contributions from the hydrodynamic part, resonance decays, and jet fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- The ideal hydrodynamic prediction for the ratio \(v_4/v_2^2\) is 0.5; the HYDJET++ model with parametrized hydrodynamics and an extended resonance table already gives \(v_4/v_2^2 \approx 0.6\).
- Including jets raises the ratio to \(v_4/v_2^2 \approx 0.65\) at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s}=200A\) GeV) and to \(v_4/v_2^2 \approx 0.7\) at LHC (\(\sqrt{s}=2.76A\) TeV) for centralities 20-30%.
- At RHIC, resonance decays change the elliptic flow of directly produced hadrons with \(p_T \leq 3\) GeV/\(c\) by 1-2%; at LHC the change is less than 1%.
- The elliptic flow from jet hadrons with \(p_T \leq 2\) GeV/\(c\) is almost zero; with rising \(p_T\) it increases to 3-5% due to jet quenching.
- At RHIC, the NCQ scaling holds within 5-10% accuracy for pions, protons, and kaons after including resonance decays and jets; at LHC, the scaling is violated when jets are included, in agreement with experimental observations.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic studies of the \(v_4/v_2^2\) ratio, which had shown that ideal hydrodynamics predicts a value of 0.5 (Borghini and Ollitrault, 2006) while experiments at RHIC (STAR and PHENIX Collaborations) measured values close to 1. The authors build on the suggestion by Gombeaud and Ollitrault (2010) that event-by-event fluctuations can increase the measured ratio, and on hydrodynamic calculations by Luzum, Gombeaud, and Ollitrault (2010) that underpredicted the STAR data. The paper also addresses the violation of NCQ scaling observed at LHC by the ALICE Collaboration, showing that jets are the primary cause of the breaking, whereas resonance decays help restore scaling in the hydrodynamic sector. The results enable a clearer separation of soft and hard contributions to anisotropic flow and suggest that at higher collision energies the NCQ scaling will worsen further.
