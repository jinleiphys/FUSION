# Kinematical higher-twist corrections in $γ^* γ\to M \bar M $

> We estimate kinematical higher-twist (up to twist 4) corrections to the $γ^*(q_1) γ(q_2) \to M(p_1) \bar{M}(p_2)$ amplitudes at large $Q^2=-q_1^2$ and small $s=(q_1+q_2)^2$, where $M$ is a scalar or pseudoscalar meson. This process is known to factorize at leading twist into a perturbatively calculable coefficient function and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs). The kinematical higher-twist contributions of order $s/Q^2$ and $m^2/Q^2$ turn out to be important in the cross section, considering the kinematics accessible at Belle and Belle II. We present numerical estimates for the cross section for $γ^* γ\to π^0 π^0$ with the $ππ$ GDA extracted from Belle measurements and with the asymptotic $ππ$ GDA as inputs to study the magnitude of the kinematical corrections. To see how the target mass corrections of order $m^2/Q^2$ affect the cross section, we also perform the calculation for $γ^* γ\to ηη$ by using a model $ηη$ GDA.In the range $s> 1$ GeV$^2$, the kinematical higher-twist corrections account for $\sim 15 \%$ of the total cross section, an effect which is not negligible. Since $ππ$ GDAs are the best way to access the pion energy-momentum tensor (EMT), our study demonstrates that an accurate evaluation of EMT form factors requires the inclusion of kinematical higher-twist contributions.

## Key claim

Kinematical higher-twist corrections of order \(s/Q^2\) and \(m^2/Q^2\) to the \(\gamma^* \gamma \to M \bar M\) amplitudes are estimated up to twist-4 accuracy, and are found to account for approximately 15% of the total cross section in the range \(s > 1\) GeV\(^2\), meaning they are not negligible for the kinematics accessible at Belle and Belle II.

## Method

The authors apply the operator product expansion separation of kinematical and dynamical higher-twist contributions, previously developed for deeply-virtual Compton scattering (Braun 2011, 2012), to the crossed channel \(\gamma^* \gamma \to M \bar M\). They derive helicity amplitudes in terms of generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) including kinematical twist-3 and twist-4 corrections, and compute the differential cross section for \(e \gamma \to e \pi^0 \pi^0\) using both the \(\pi\pi\) GDA extracted from Belle measurements (Kumano 2017) and an asymptotic \(\pi\pi\) GDA model. The calculation is also performed for \(\gamma^* \gamma \to \eta\eta\) using a model \(\eta\eta\) GDA to study target mass corrections of order \(m^2/Q^2\).

## Key numbers

* Kinematical higher-twist corrections account for \(\sim 15\%\) of the total cross section for \(\gamma^* \gamma \to \pi^0 \pi^0\) in the range \(s > 1\) GeV\(^2\) at Belle kinematics.
* Cross sections are calculated at \(Q^2 = 9, 16, 25\) GeV\(^2\) with \(s_{e\gamma} = 30\) GeV\(^2\) and \(s \in (0.25, 4)\) GeV\(^2\).
* For the asymptotic \(\pi\pi\) GDA, kinematical corrections can account up to about \(40\%\) of the cross section.
* The meson mass \(m\) appears in the target mass corrections of order \(m^2/Q^2\), with \(\Delta_T^2 = 4m^2 - (1-\zeta_0^2)s\).

## Context

This work extends the separation of kinematical and dynamical higher-twist contributions, previously applied to deeply-virtual Compton scattering (Braun 2011, 2012), to the timelike crossed channel \(\gamma^* \gamma \to M \bar M\). It builds on leading-twist factorization of this process using GDAs (Diehl 2000) and on the extraction of \(\pi\pi\) GDAs from Belle measurements (Kumano 2017). The results enable more accurate extraction of pion energy-momentum tensor form factors from \(\pi\pi\) GDA studies, which is important because pion GPDs cannot be directly measured in experiments. The Belle II experiment with higher luminosity is expected to provide more precise measurements of \(\gamma^* \gamma \to M \bar M\) reactions.
