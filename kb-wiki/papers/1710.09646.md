# Evidence for the role of proton shell closure in quasi-fission reactions from X-ray fluorescence of mass-identified fragments

> The atomic numbers and the masses of fragments formed in quasi-fission reactions have been simultaneously measured at scission in 48 Ti + 238 U reactions at a laboratory energy of 286 MeV. The atomic numbers were determined from measured characteristic fluorescence X-rays whereas the masses were obtained from the emission angles and times of flight of the two emerging fragments. For the first time, thanks to this full identification of the quasi-fission fragments on a broad angular range, the important role of the proton shell closure at Z = 82 is evidenced by the associated maximum production yield, a maximum predicted by time dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. This new experimental approach gives now access to precise studies of the time dependence of the N/Z (neutron over proton ratios of the fragments) evolution in quasi-fission reactions.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first simultaneous measurement of atomic number (Z) and mass (A) of quasi-fission fragments at scission, demonstrating that the maximum production yield is associated with the proton shell closure at Z = 82, not the neutron shell closure at N = 126 as previously suggested. The authors hedge that further experiments are needed to determine whether this maximum results from shell effects during fragment formation or from enhanced stability against fission of primary fragments with Z near 82.

## Method

A 48Ti beam at 286 MeV bombarded a 238U target; the two emerging fragments were detected in coincidence by two large-area position-sensitive multi-wire proportional counters covering a broad angular range. Fragment masses were determined from emission angles and times of flight with a mass resolution of 2.6 amu, while atomic numbers were extracted from characteristic K X-ray fluorescence measured by three planar germanium detectors, after subtracting contributions from internal conversion. The Z distributions at scission were inferred from the X-ray yields, assuming K vacancies created during the collision are adiabatically transferred to the heavier fragment.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 286 MeV (5.75 MeV/nucleon) for 48Ti + 238U reactions
- Mass resolution: sigma = 2.6 amu, with average masses determined to better than ±1 amu
- Germanium detector energy resolution: sigma = 400 eV after Doppler correction
- Time calibration precision: better than 200 ps
- Secondary maximum in fragment yield observed at A = 212, corresponding to Z = 82
- For fragments with mass 210 ≤ A ≤ 214, the most probable atomic number is unambiguously Z = 82
- K X-ray energies from fits agree with tabulated values within ±0.2 keV
- Yield ratios between different K transitions agree with tabulated values within less than 5%

## Context

This work extends previous quasi-fission studies that measured only fragment masses (e.g., Kozulin et al. 2016, Wakhle et al. 2014) and had interpreted maximum yields around A = 208 as arising from the N = 126 neutron shell closure. By simultaneously measuring Z and A, the authors provide clear evidence that the proton shell closure at Z = 82 dominates, consistent with predictions from time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations by Simenel et al. (2012). The new experimental approach enables precise studies of N/Z evolution in quasi-fission and can be applied to various systems to improve models for super-heavy element synthesis.
