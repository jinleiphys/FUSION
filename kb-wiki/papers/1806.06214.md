# Weyl systems: anomalous transport normally explained

> The chiral kinetic theory is derived from exact spinor mean field equations without symmetry-breaking terms for large classes of SU(2) systems with spin-orbit coupling. The influence of the Wigner function's off-diagonal elements is worked out. The decoupling of the diagonal elements renormalizes the drift according to Berry connection which is found as an expression of the meanfield, spin-orbit coupling and magnetic field. As special limit, Weyl systems are considered. The anomalous term $\sim\V E\V B$ in the balance of the chiral density appears consequently by an underlying conserving theory. The experimental observations of this term and the anomalous magneto-transport in solid-sate physics usually described by chiral kinetic theory are therefore not a unique signal for mixed axial-gravitational or triangle anomaly and no signal for the breaking of Lorentz-invariance. The source of the anomalous term is by two thirds the divergence of Berry curvature at zero momentum which can be seen as Dirac monopole and by one third the Dirac sea at infinite momentum. During the derivation of the chiral kinetic theory this source by the Dirac sea is transferred exclusively to the Dirac monopole due to the projection of the spinor Wigner functions to the chiral basis. The dynamical result is shown to suppress the anomalous term by two thirds.

## Key claim

The anomalous term proportional to **E·B** in the chiral density balance, usually interpreted as a unique signature of the Adler-Bell-Jackiw chiral anomaly or Lorentz-symmetry breaking, can instead arise from an underlying conserving theory without symmetry-breaking terms, meaning its observation in solid-state experiments is not a definitive signal of such anomalies.

## Method

The author derives the chiral kinetic theory from exact spinor mean-field equations for SU(2) systems with spin-orbit coupling, using the Wigner function formalism with off-diagonal elements. The transformation to the helicity basis is performed, and the resulting coupled kinetic equations for scalar and vector Wigner functions are mapped onto the chiral kinetic equation form. The infinite-mass limit of nonrelativistic particles is then applied to produce the chiral dispersion characteristic of Weyl systems.

## Key numbers

- The anomalous term in the chiral density balance is sourced by two thirds from the divergence of Berry curvature at zero momentum (Dirac monopole) and one third from the Dirac sea at infinite momentum.
- The dynamical result suppresses the anomalous term by two thirds relative to the full source.
- The chiral anomaly rate from the phenomenological model is \(d n_5/d t = e^2/(2 \pi^2 \hbar^2) \mathbf{E} \cdot \mathbf{B}\).
- The topological charge integral yields \(\xi = \pm 1\) as the monopole charge inside the Fermi surface at zero temperature.

## Context

This work extends the quantum kinetic approach of Morawetz (2015) and provides a conserving-theory alternative to the symmetry-breaking assumptions used in prior treatments of chiral anomaly in Weyl semimetals, such as those by Zyuzin and Burkov (2012) and Buividovich (2015). It challenges the interpretation of negative longitudinal magnetoresistance as a unique signature of chiral anomaly, consistent with recent experimental doubts raised by Arnold (2018) regarding conventional conductors. The derivation enables a unified description of anomalous transport across large classes of spin-orbit coupled systems listed in the paper, including Rashba, Dresselhaus, graphene, and Weyl materials.
