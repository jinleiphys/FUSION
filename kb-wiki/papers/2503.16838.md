# Investigation of $Δ(1232)$ resonance substructure in $pγ^*\to Δ(1232)$ process through helicity amplitudes

> This work investigates the substructure of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance in the $pγ^*\to Δ(1232)$ process through helicity transition amplitudes within the quark model framework. We consider the involved baryons composed of three quarks, and both the quark core and meson cloud contribute to the transition amplitudes. The comparison of theoretical results with experimental data reveals that, rather than the $L=0$ component of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance, it is the $L=2$ component that significantly affects its $S_{1/2}$ amplitude. These findings indicate that the $Δ(1232)$ resonance likely contains a substantial $L=2$ component, challenging the conventional view of the $Δ(1232)$ resonance as an $L=0$ baryon.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Δ(1232) resonance contains a substantial L=2 component (approximately 47% combined symmetric and mixed-symmetric L=2 admixture), challenging the conventional view that it is primarily an L=0 state; this conclusion is hedged by the model dependence of the quark-model framework and the fitting procedure to experimental helicity amplitude data.

## Method

The authors computed the transverse (A₁/₂, A₃/₂) and longitudinal (S₁/₂) helicity amplitudes for the pγ* → Δ(1232) process within a three-quark model using harmonic oscillator basis wave functions in Jacobi coordinates. The transition amplitudes included both a direct quark-core contribution (impulse approximation for photon-quark interaction) and a meson-cloud contribution (pion loop via chiral quark model with monopole form factor cutoff Λπ = 0.732 GeV and Pauli-Villars regularization). The Δ(1232) wave function coefficients (B, C, D for L=0, symmetric L=2, and mixed-symmetric L=2 components) were obtained by fitting the calculated helicity amplitudes to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1232) resonance consists of 53% L=0 component (B = 0.725), 23% symmetric L=2 component (C = 0.485), and 24% mixed-symmetric L=2 component (D = 0.487)
- The current quark mass for up and down quarks was set to m = 5 MeV
- The pion decay constant used in the chiral quark model Lagrangian was F = 88 MeV
- The monopole form factor cutoff parameter for the pion-photon interaction was Λπ = 0.732 GeV
- The real photon momentum in the resonance rest frame is given by K = (MΔ² - Mp²)/(2MΔ), where MΔ and Mp are the Δ(1232) and proton rest masses

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group on the helicity transition amplitudes of N(1440), N(1520), and N(1535) (Kaewsnod 2022a, Kaewsnod 2022b), which revealed admixtures of higher orbital momentum and exotic quark configurations in those resonances. The finding that the Δ(1232) contains a significant L=2 component contradicts the traditional classification of this resonance as a pure S-wave (L=0) three-quark state, and supports earlier suggestions from studies of D-wave admixtures in nucleon and Δ wave functions (Julia-Diaz 2005) and from lattice QCD analyses of Δ form factors (Ramalho 2010). The results enable future investigations of higher resonances such as N(1875) and N(1895) using the same framework, and motivate refinement of quark models beyond the simple quark-core picture.
