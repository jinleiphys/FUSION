# Double parton correlations in constituent quark models

> Double parton correlations, having effects on the double parton scattering processes occurring in high-energy hadron-hadron collisions, for example at the LHC, are studied in the valence quark region, within constituent quark models. In this framework, two particle correlations are present without any additional prescription, at variance with what happens, for example, in independent particle models, such as the MIT bag model in its simplest version. From the present analysis, conclusions similar to the ones obtained recenty in a modified version of the bag model can be drawn: correlations in the longitudinal momenta of the active quarks are found to be sizable, while those in transverse momentum are much smaller. However, the used framework allows to understand clearly the dynamical origin of the correlations. In particular, it is shown that the small size of the correlations in transverse momentum is a model dependent result, which would not occur if models with sizable quark orbital angular momentum were used to describe the proton. Our analysis permits therefore to clarify the dynamical origin of the double parton correlations and to establish which, among the features of the results, are model independent. The possibility to test experimentally the studied effects is discussed.

## Key claim

Double parton correlations in the valence quark region are studied within constituent quark models, where two-particle correlations arise naturally from the interquark potential without additional prescriptions, unlike in independent particle models such as the MIT bag model; the analysis shows that sizable longitudinal momentum correlations are a model-independent feature, while the small size of transverse momentum correlations is model-dependent and would not hold in models with significant quark orbital angular momentum.

## Method

The authors evaluate spin-averaged double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) for two valence u quarks in the proton using two non-relativistic constituent quark models: a simple harmonic oscillator (HO) model with SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry, and the Isgur-Karl (IK) model which includes a one-gluon-exchange admixture of states up to the 2ħω shell. The dPDFs are computed at the hadronic scale Q₀ using momentum-space wave functions, with the quark on-mass-shell approximation to relate longitudinal momentum fractions to three-momenta, and the results are compared to the factorized forms commonly assumed in double parton scattering analyses.

## Key numbers

- The HO model parameter α² = 1.35 fm⁻², fixed to reproduce the slope of the proton charge form factor at zero momentum transfer.
- The IK model parameter α² = 1.23 fm⁻², with wave function coefficients a_S = 0.931, a_S' = -0.274, a_M = -0.233 determined from spectroscopic properties.
- Support violation (fraction of quark momentum in the non-physical region x₁+x₂>1) at k⊥=0 in the HO (IK) model: 5% (4%) at x₂=0.2, 9% (7%) at x₂=0.4, 18% (15%) at x₂=0.6.
- The dPDF uu(x₁,x₂,k⊥=0) integrates to N_u(N_u-1)=2 for the proton, consistent with the number density interpretation.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of double parton correlations previously performed in a modified MIT bag model by Manohar and collaborators (2012), using constituent quark models that intrinsically include correlations through the interquark potential rather than adding them via prescriptions. The results confirm that the x₁-x₂ factorization assumption is strongly violated in a model-independent way, consistent with findings by Gaunt and Stirling (2009), while the x-k⊥ factorization holds exactly in the SU(6)-symmetric HO model but is mildly violated in the IK model due to orbital angular momentum contributions. The analysis enables future studies where these model results at the hadronic scale could be evolved using perturbative QCD to make contact with LHC data, though the authors note that the current calculations are only reliable in the valence region (x>0.1) at low energy scales.
