# Electric dipole polarizability of $^{40}$Ca

> The electric dipole strength distribution in $^{40}$Ca between 5 and 25 MeV has been determined at RCNP, Osaka, from proton inelastic scattering experiments at very forward angles. Combined with total photoabsorption data at higher excitation energy, this enables an extraction of the electric dipole polarizability $α_\mathrm{D}$($^{40}$Ca) = 1.92(17) fm$^3$. Together with the measured $α_{\rm D}$ in $^{48}$Ca, it provides a stringent test of modern theoretical approaches, including coupled cluster calculations with chiral effective field theory interactions and state-of-the art energy density functionals. The emerging picture is that for this medium-mass region dipole polarizabilities are well described theoretically, with important constraints for the neutron skin in $^{48}$Ca and related equation of state quantities.

## Key claim

The electric dipole polarizability of $^{40}$Ca is measured as 1.92(17) fm$^3$, and when combined with the previously measured value for $^{48}$Ca, it provides a stringent test of modern theoretical approaches; the abstract's claim that "dipole polarizabilities are well described theoretically" is qualified by the finding that only the NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ interaction in coupled-cluster calculations simultaneously describes both isotopes well, while other interactions show systematic deviations.

## Method

The electric dipole strength distribution in $^{40}$Ca between 5 and 25 MeV was determined from proton inelastic scattering at 295 MeV incident energy using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at RCNP, Osaka, at laboratory scattering angles from 0.4$^\circ$ to 14.0$^\circ$. A multipole decomposition analysis separated the $E1$ contributions from other multipoles and a pre-equilibrium background, and the resulting $E1$ cross sections were converted to equivalent photoabsorption cross sections using the virtual photon method in an eikonal approach. The dipole polarizability was obtained by integrating the photoabsorption cross section from 10 to 25 MeV (contributing 1.60(14) fm$^3$) and extending to 60 MeV using total photoabsorption data from Ahr (1975) and Ahr (1985) for the higher-energy region.

## Key numbers

- Electric dipole polarizability $\alpha_\mathrm{D}(^{40}{\rm Ca}) = 1.92(17)$ fm$^3$, with a total uncertainty of 9.0% dominated by the background parameterization (8.3%)
- Contribution to $\alpha_\mathrm{D}$ from the energy range 10-25 MeV: 1.60(14) fm$^3$
- Incident proton energy: 295 MeV; energy resolution: about 30 keV (full width at half maximum)
- Coupled-cluster calculations with the NNLO$_{\rm sat}$ interaction predict symmetry energy parameters at the lower end: $J = 27$ MeV, $L = 41$ MeV
- The set of chiral interactions used in coupled-cluster calculations predict a range of symmetry energy parameters $J = 27-33$ MeV, $L = 41-49$ MeV
- Energy density functionals refit including $\alpha_\mathrm{D}$ of $^{208}$Pb narrow predictions to $J = 30-32$ MeV and $L = 35-52$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of measuring electric dipole polarizabilities via proton inelastic scattering at forward angles, previously applied to $^{48}$Ca by Birkhan (2017) and $^{208}$Pb by Tamii (2011). The new $^{40}$Ca data, combined with the $^{48}$Ca result, provide a benchmark for coupled-cluster calculations with chiral effective field theory interactions (building on work by Bacca (2013), Hagen (2016), and Simonis (2019)) and for energy density functionals (as studied by Reinhard (2021) and Reinhard (2022)). The results support the robustness of current theoretical approaches for describing dipole polarizabilities in medium-mass nuclei and their constraints on symmetry energy parameters, while also highlighting tensions with parity-violating asymmetry measurements from the PREX and CREX collaborations.
