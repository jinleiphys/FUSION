# Parity and time reversal violating nuclear polarizability

> We propose a nuclear mechanism which can induce an atomic electric dipole moment (EDM). Parity and time reversal violating (P,T-odd) nuclear forces generate a mixed P,T-odd nuclear polarizability beta_{ik} (defined by an energy shift U = -beta_{ik} E_{i}H_{k}, E is electric field and H magnetic field). The interaction of atomic electrons with beta_{ik} produces an atomic EDM. We performed an analytical calculation of the P,T-odd nuclear polarizability and estimated the value for the induced atomic EDM. The measurements of atomic EDMs can provide information about P,T-odd nuclear forces and test models of CP-violation.

# Parity and time reversal violating nuclear polarizability

## Key claim

The paper proposes that parity- and time-reversal-violating (P,T-odd) nuclear forces generate a mixed P,T-odd nuclear polarizability, which can induce an atomic electric dipole moment (EDM) through the interaction of atomic electrons with the perturbed nucleus. However, the induced atomic EDM is estimated to be only about 1% of the contribution from the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment (MQM), making this mechanism subdominant to previously studied effects.

## Method

The authors performed an analytical calculation of the P,T-odd nuclear polarizability for a heavy spherical nucleus with a single unpaired nucleon, using first-order perturbation theory in the P,T-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction. They derived expressions for the scalar and tensor components of the polarizability, then used these to construct an effective Hamiltonian describing the interaction between the polarizability and atomic electrons. The induced atomic EDM was estimated by comparing this effective Hamiltonian to the known Hamiltonian for the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment interaction, evaluating the ratio of their matrix elements for s and p3/2 electron state mixing.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of the effective Hamiltonian for the P,T-odd nuclear polarizability to that for the nuclear magnetic quadrupole moment is approximately 1% for heavy atoms, given by the expression $\frac{Ze^{2}S_{\rm rel}}{a_{B}\omega _{\rm fs}}$
- For the scalar polarizability, the expression $\beta_{s}=\xi e_{N}\mu _{N}(g_{s}-g_{l})(\frac{1}{\omega _{\rm fs}})(\frac{2I-1}{3I})$ is derived, where $\xi$ characterizes the P,T-odd interaction strength, $e_N$ is the effective nucleon charge, $\mu_N$ is the nuclear magneton, and $\omega_{\rm fs}$ is the fine-structure splitting energy
- The tensor polarizability is related to the scalar by $\beta_{t}=-\frac{3\beta_{s}}{2I(2I-1)}$, where $I$ is the nuclear total angular momentum
- The relativistic enhancement factor for heavy atoms is $S_{\rm rel}\approx \frac{4}{3}\frac{1}{Z^{2}\alpha ^{2}}(\frac{ZR_{N}}{a_{B}})^{-\frac{3}{4}Z^{2}\alpha ^{2}}$, where $Z$ is the atomic number, $\alpha$ is the fine-structure constant, $R_N$ is the nuclear radius, and $a_B$ is the Bohr radius

## Context

This work extends the study of P,T-odd nuclear moments as sources of atomic EDMs, following earlier calculations of atomic EDMs induced by nuclear magnetic quadrupole moments (MQM) in cesium (Khriplovich 1976, Sushkov et al. 1984), dysprosium (Dzuba et al. 1986, Flambaum 1994), and radium (Flambaum 1999, Dzuba et al. 2000). The mechanism proposed here is distinct from the MQM mechanism and produces a smaller effect, but it provides an additional channel through which atomic EDM measurements can constrain P,T-odd nuclear forces and test models of CP-violation. The authors note that in deformed nuclei, the collective polarizability from many nucleons in open shells could produce enhanced contributions, similar to the enhancement of the P,T-odd nuclear quadrupole moment discussed by Flambaum (1994).
