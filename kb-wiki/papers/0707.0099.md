# Energy distributions from three-body decaying many-body resonances

> We compute energy distributions of three particles emerging from decaying many-body resonances. We reproduce the measured energy distributions from decays of two archetypal states chosen as the lowest $0^{+}$ and $1^{+}$-resonances in $^{12}$C populated in $β$-decays. These states are dominated by sequential, through the $^{8}$Be ground state, and direct decays, respectively. These decay mechanisms are reflected in the ``dynamic'' evolution from small, cluster or shell-model states, to large distances, where the coordinate or momentum space continuum wavefunctions are accurately computed.

# Energy distributions from three-body decaying many-body resonances

## Key claim

The energy distributions of fragments from three-body decaying many-body resonances are insensitive to the short-distance many-body structure and can be accurately computed using a three-body cluster model, provided the dynamic evolution from small to large distances is properly accounted for. The authors show this holds for specific archetypal states in 12C, but note that the distinction between sequential and direct decay becomes artificial when intermediate two-body states have large widths.

## Method

The authors use the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method of the Faddeev equations combined with complex scaling to compute resonance wavefunctions from small to asymptotically large distances. Two-body interactions are chosen to reproduce low-energy scattering data, and a three-body Gaussian potential is adjusted to reproduce the energy position of each many-body resonance. The energy distribution of each emitted particle is obtained by integrating the square of the resonance wavefunction over unobserved momenta, with coordinate-space and momentum-space computations compared for consistency.

## Key numbers

- The 1+ resonance in 12C is at 5.43 MeV above threshold (excitation energy 12.71 MeV); the computed alpha-particle energy distribution converges for hyperradii larger than about 60 fm, with negligible variation between 70 fm and 100 fm.
- The first 0+ resonance in 12C is at 0.38 MeV above threshold (excitation 7.63 MeV); its decay is almost entirely sequential (direct decay only about 1%), in agreement with the experimental upper limit.
- The second 0+ resonance in 12C is at 3.95 MeV above threshold (excitation 11.2 MeV) in the calculation, but the beta-feeding process shifts the peak to about 2.8 MeV; the decay is about 75% sequential and 25% direct.
- The three-body Gaussian potential parameters are b = 6 fm and -S = 20 MeV for the 0+ resonance and -S = 92 MeV for the 1+ resonance.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of three-body decays beyond phenomenological approaches such as Dalitz plots (Dalitz, 1958) and R-matrix theory (Fynbo, 2003) by providing a microscopic calculation that includes the dynamic evolution from small-distance cluster or shell-model states to asymptotic three-body continuum wavefunctions. The method is tested on the well-studied 0+ and 1+ resonances in 12C, which have been measured by Freer (1994), Fynbo (2003), and Diget (2005, 2006), and which are known to exhibit different decay mechanisms. The approach enables accurate computation of energy distributions for three-body decaying resonances in nuclear, molecular, and particle physics systems, including cases where the initial state has no three-body cluster structure.
