# High-order baryon number fluctuations within the fRG approach

> We compute high-order baryon number fluctuations at finite temperature and density within a QCD-assisted low energy effective field theory. Quantum, thermal and density fluctuations are incorporated with the functional renormalization group approach. Quantum and in-medium fluctuations are encoded via the evolution of renormalization group flow equations. The resulting fourth- and sixth-order baryon number fluctuations meet the lattice benchmark results at vanishing density. They are consistent with experimental measurements, and in particular, the non-monotonic dependence of the kurtosis of net-baryon number distributions on the collision energy is observed in our calculations. This non-monotonicity arises from the increasingly sharpened chiral crossover with the decrease of collision energy.

## Key claim
The non-monotonic dependence of the kurtosis of net-baryon number distributions on collision energy is observed in the fRG-LEFT calculations, arising from the increasingly sharpened chiral crossover as collision energy decreases. This result is consistent with experimental measurements by STAR, but the theoretical errors are highly correlated and the non-monotonic behavior for higher-order fluctuations (sixth, eighth) cannot be definitively confirmed due to large uncertainties at low collision energies.

## Method
The authors constructed a QCD-assisted low-energy effective field theory (LEFT) for two quark flavors, incorporating quantum, thermal, and density fluctuations via functional renormalization group (fRG) flow equations. They performed a scale matching between 2-flavor and 2+1-flavor QCD to map their results to physical QCD, using coefficients c_T = 1.247(12) and c_muB = 1.110(66). Baryon number fluctuations up to eighth order were computed from the thermodynamic potential density, and the collision-energy dependence was extracted using a freeze-out curve derived from STAR experimental parameters.

## Key numbers
- Fourth-order baryon number fluctuations R^B_42 at zero chemical potential are in quantitative accordance with Wuppertal-Budapest lattice results and qualitatively consistent with HotQCD results.
- The sixth-order fluctuation R^B_62 crosses zero and becomes negative at a temperature close to the pseudo-critical temperature T_c = 156 MeV at zero baryon chemical potential.
- The scale-matching coefficients between 2-flavor and 2+1-flavor QCD are c_T = 1.247(12) and c_muB = 1.110(66).
- Theoretical R^B_32 results agree with experimental net-proton skewness R^p_32 for collision energies sqrt(s_NN) >= 14.5 GeV, with significant discrepancy at lower energies.
- R^B_62 is negative at the three collision energies where experimental data exist (200 GeV, 54.4 GeV, 27 GeV).
- The non-monotonic dependence of R^B_42 on collision energy is observed in the low energy regime, consistent with STAR measurements at 3.1 sigma significance for central collisions.

## Context
This work extends functional QCD computations by Fu (2019) and lattice QCD results from the HotQCD collaboration (Bazavov, 2020) and Wuppertal-Budapest collaboration (Borsanyi, 2018) to finite baryon chemical potential. It provides theoretical support for the non-monotonic collision-energy dependence of net-proton kurtosis observed by STAR (Adam, 2020) and extends predictions to sixth-order cumulants measured by STAR (2021). The results enable future comparisons with the full STAR, CBM, and HADES energy ranges through ongoing extensions to functional QCD computations.
