# Pure Quark and Gluon Observables in Collinear Drop

> We construct a class of pure quark and gluon observables by using the collinear drop grooming technique. The construction is based on linear combinations of multiple cumulative distributions of the jet mass in collinear drop, whose specific weights are fully predicted perturbatively. This yields observables which obtain their values purely from quarks (or purely from gluons) in a wide region of phase space. We demonstrate this by showing that these observables are effective in two phase space regions, one dominated by perturbative resummation and one dominated by nonperturbative effects. The nonperturbative effects are included using shape functions which only appear as a common factor in the linear combinations constructed. We test this construction using a numerical analysis with next-to-leading logarithmic resummation and various shape function models, as well as analyzing these observables with Pythia and Vincia. Choices for the collinear drop parameters are optimized for experimental use.

## Key claim

The authors construct pure quark and gluon observables using linear combinations of multiple cumulative distributions of the jet mass in collinear drop, where the weights are fully predicted perturbatively, and these observables obtain values purely from quarks or purely from gluons in a wide region of phase space, provided the nonperturbative effects factorize as a common factor in the linear combinations.

## Method

The construction uses the collinear drop grooming technique, which applies two sequential soft drop procedures with parameters (z_cut1, β1) and (z_cut2, β2) to define a groomed jet mass Δm² = m_SD1² - m_SD2². The authors form linear combinations of multiple cumulative distributions of this jet mass, with coefficients determined perturbatively, such that the resulting observables isolate pure quark or pure gluon contributions. The method is tested using next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) resummation with various shape function models for nonperturbative effects, and validated with Pythia and Vincia event generators.

## Key numbers

- The collinear drop jet mass observable is defined as Δm² = m_SD1² - m_SD2², where m_SD1 and m_SD2 are the masses of jets after the first and second soft drop grooming steps.
- The critical jet mass for nonperturbative effects to become important is Δm_Λi² = Λ_QCD Q (Λ_QCD / Q_cuti)^(1/(1+βi)), where Q_cuti = 2^βi Q z_cuti.
- The global-soft scale is Q_gsi = p_T R z_cuti (R/R0)^βi, with R0 = 1 throughout the paper.
- The collinear-soft scale is Q_csi = (Δm²/Q)^((1+βi)/(2+βi)) Q_cuti^(1/(2+βi)).
- The jet radius used for the construction is R = 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the jet topics method of Metodiev (2018) and Komiske (2018) by constructing observables that directly yield pure quark or gluon contributions without requiring mutual irreducibility or anchor bins in distribution tails. The construction exploits the unique feature of the collinear drop jet mass spectrum, which goes to a constant as the cumulative jet mass approaches zero, unlike most jet distributions that vanish due to Sudakov suppression. This enables experimental extraction of quark and gluon fractions from two jet samples (e.g., Z-jet and dijet events) by taking ratios of the pure observables, with a built-in consistency test from y-independence of the ratios.
