# Energy of vanishing flow in heavy-ion collisions: Role of Coulomb interactions and asymmetry of a reaction

> We aim to understand the role of Coulomb interactions as well as of different equations of state on the disappearance of transverse flow for various asymmetric reactions leading to same total mass. For the present study, the total mass of the system is kept constant (ATOT = 152) and asymmetry of the reaction is varied between 0.2 and 0.7. We find that the contribution of mean-field at low incident energies is more for nearly symmetric systems, while the trend is opposite at higher incident energies. The Coulomb interactions as well as different equations of state are found to affect the balance energy significantly for larger asymmetric reactions.

## Key claim

The balance energy (energy of vanishing flow) increases with the asymmetry of the reaction, and Coulomb interactions affect the balance energy more significantly than the choice of equation of state, especially for larger asymmetric reactions. The abstract's claim that Coulomb interactions "affect the balance energy significantly for larger asymmetric reactions" is hedged by the finding that the relative percentage difference in balance energy due to Coulomb interactions grows from 30 MeV/nucleon at asymmetry η=0.2 to 115 MeV/nucleon at η=0.7.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate semi-central heavy-ion collisions for six asymmetric reactions, all with total mass A_TOT = 152 and asymmetry parameter η ranging from 0.2 to 0.7. Incident energies were varied from 50 to 250 MeV/nucleon in 50 MeV/nucleon steps, using both hard (compressibility 380 MeV) and soft (compressibility 200 MeV) equations of state, with and without Coulomb interactions. The directed transverse flow was calculated in the center-of-mass frame, and the balance energy was extracted from the excitation function of the flow.

## Key numbers

- Total mass of the system was kept constant at A_TOT = 152 for all reactions.
- Asymmetry parameter η was varied from 0.2 to 0.7 across six reactions: η=0.2 (Fe-56 + Ru-96), η=0.3 (Cr-50 + Ru-102), η=0.4 (Ca-40 + Sn-112), η=0.5 (S-32 + Sn-120), η=0.6 (Si-28 + Xe-124), η=0.7 (O-16 + Xe-136).
- Incident energies were studied from 50 to 250 MeV/nucleon with 50 MeV/nucleon increments.
- Hard equation of state compressibility: 380 MeV; soft equation of state compressibility: 200 MeV.
- Power-law fit exponent τ for balance energy vs. asymmetry: τ = 0.375 (soft EoS, no Coulomb), τ = 0.282 (hard EoS, no Coulomb), τ = 0.06067 (soft EoS, with Coulomb).
- Relative percentage difference in balance energy due to Coulomb interactions: ΔE_bal^Coul = 30 MeV/nucleon at η=0.2 and 115 MeV/nucleon at η=0.7, a difference of 90 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of balance energy in heavy-ion collisions, which had focused on system mass dependence (Sood, 2004) and isospin effects via symmetry energy and NN cross sections (Chen, 2001; Li, 2001; Pak, 1997; Gautam, 2010), by systematically varying the asymmetry of the reaction while keeping total mass constant. The authors note that previous studies of asymmetry effects had been performed for multifragmentation (Liu, 2001; Zhang, 2001; Donangelo, 2001) but not for transverse flow or balance energy. This work enables future experimental verification of the predicted strong Coulomb-induced shift in balance energy for highly asymmetric reactions, and clarifies that isospin studies must control for total mass to isolate genuine isospin effects.
