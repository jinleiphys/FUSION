# Two-particle azimuthal correlations as a probe of collective behaviour in deep inelastic $\textit{ep}$ scattering at HERA

> Two-particle azimuthal correlations have been measured in neutral current deep $\mbox{inelastic}$ $\textit{ep}$ scattering with virtuality $Q^2 > 5$ GeV$^2$ at a centre-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=318$ GeV recorded with the ZEUS detector at HERA. The correlations of charged particles have been measured in the range of laboratory pseudorapidity $-1.5 < η< 2.0$ and transverse momentum $0.1 < p_{\textrm{T}}~< 5.0$ GeV and event multiplicities $N_{\textrm{ch}}$ up to six times larger than the average $\left< N_{\textrm{ch}} \right>\approx5$. The two-particle correlations have been measured in terms of the angular observables $c_n\{2\}=\left< \left< \cos{ n Δφ} \right> \right>$, where $n$ is between 1 and 4 and $Δφ$ is the relative azimuthal angle between the two particles. Comparisons with available models of deep inelastic scattering, which are tuned to reproduce inclusive particle production, suggest that the measured two-particle correlations are dominated by contributions from multijet production. The correlations observed here do not indicate the kind of collective behaviour recently observed at the highest RHIC and LHC energies in high-multiplicity hadronic collisions.

## Key claim

Two-particle azimuthal correlations in neutral current deep inelastic scattering at HERA do not indicate collective behaviour of the kind observed at RHIC and LHC in high-multiplicity hadronic collisions; the measured correlations are dominated by contributions from multijet production, with no evidence of a near-side ridge that would signal hydrodynamic collectivity.

## Method

The ZEUS Collaboration measured two-particle azimuthal correlations using 45 million neutral current DIS events with Q² > 5 GeV² at √s = 318 GeV from HERA II data (2003-2007, 366 pb⁻¹). Charged particles were selected in the range -1.5 < η < 2.0 and 0.1 < pT < 5.0 GeV, and the angular observables c_n{2} = ⟨⟨cos(nΔφ)⟩⟩ for n = 1-4 were computed as functions of charged-particle multiplicity Nch, pseudorapidity difference |Δη|, and mean transverse momentum. Efficiency corrections were derived from ARIADNE and LEPTO Monte Carlo generators, and comparisons were made between data and model predictions at reconstruction and generator levels.

## Key numbers

- Centre-of-mass energy √s = 318 GeV with 27.5 GeV electron/positron beams and 920 GeV proton beams
- Virtuality Q² > 5 GeV², with mean Q² ≈ 30 GeV²
- Integrated luminosity 366 ± 7 pb⁻¹
- Average reconstructed track multiplicity ⟨Nrec⟩ ≈ 5, with event multiplicities Nch up to six times larger than average
- Track selection: 0.1 < pT < 5.0 GeV and -1.5 < η < 2.0
- Scattered electron energy cut: Ee > 10 GeV
- Monte Carlo non-closure uncertainty on c_n{2} versus Nch without Δη cut: < 15%
- Systematic uncertainty from secondary-particle contamination: < 10%
- Systematic uncertainty from choice of Monte Carlo generator: < 10%

## Context

This work extends searches for collective behaviour to the smallest interaction regions available, complementing studies at RHIC and LHC in heavy-ion, p+A, and pp collisions (Abelev 2013, Khachatryan 2010, Aad 2012, Adare 2013) and a corresponding analysis of e+e- collisions at the Z pole (Badea 2019). The absence of a near-side ridge in ep DIS contrasts with observations in high-multiplicity pp and p+Pb collisions, suggesting that fluid-dynamic descriptions may break down for interaction regions smaller than those in hadronic collisions. The results enable future investigations of the transition between parton-fragmentation-dominated and collective regimes as a function of system size.
