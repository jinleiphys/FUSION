# Compton scattering on the proton, neutron, and deuteron in chiral perturbation theory to O(Q^4)

> We study Compton scattering in systems with A=1 and 2 using chiral perturbation theory up to fourth order. For the proton we fit the two undetermined parameters in the O(Q^4) $γ$p amplitude of McGovern to experimental data in the region $ω,\sqrt{|t|} \leq 180$ MeV, obtaining a chi^2/d.o.f. of 133/113. This yields a model-independent extraction of proton polarizabilities based solely on low-energy data: alpha_p=12.1 +/- 1.1 (stat.) +/- 0.5 (theory) and beta_p=3.4 +/- 1.1 (stat.) +/- 0.1 (theory), both in units of 10^{-4} fm^3. We also compute Compton scattering on deuterium to O(Q^4). The $γ$d amplitude is a sum of one- and two-nucleon mechanisms, and contains two undetermined parameters, which are related to the isoscalar nucleon polarizabilities. We fit data points from three recent $γ$d scattering experiments with a chi^2/d.o.f.=26.6/20, and find alpha_N=13.0 +/- 1.9 (stat.) +3.9/-1.5 (theory) and a beta_N that is consistent with zero within sizeable error bars.

## Key claim

The authors extract proton polarizabilities from a fit of the O(Q⁴) chiral perturbation theory amplitude to experimental Compton scattering data with ω, √|t| ≤ 180 MeV, obtaining a model-independent determination based solely on low-energy data, with the hedge that the theory uncertainty is estimated from the variation of the cutoff and higher-order effects.

## Method

The authors compute Compton scattering on the proton, neutron, and deuteron using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) to fourth order (O(Q⁴)). For the proton, they fit the two undetermined low-energy constants (LECs) in the γp amplitude to experimental differential cross-section data with photon energy and momentum transfer up to 180 MeV. For the deuteron, they construct the γd amplitude as a sum of one- and two-nucleon mechanisms, using the NLO (ν=2) chiral potential to generate the deuteron wave function, and fit the two isoscalar LECs to data from three recent γd scattering experiments.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability: α_p = 12.1 ± 1.1 (stat.) ± 0.5 (theory) in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Proton magnetic polarizability: β_p = 3.4 ± 1.1 (stat.) ± 0.1 (theory) in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- χ²/d.o.f. for the proton fit: 133/113
- Isoscalar nucleon electric polarizability: α_N = 13.0 ± 1.9 (stat.) +3.9/−1.5 (theory) in units of 10⁻⁴ fm³
- β_N consistent with zero within sizeable error bars
- χ²/d.o.f. for the deuteron fit: 26.6/20
- O(Q³) chiral prediction for α_p = α_n = 12.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_p = β_n = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from pion-loop effects

## Context

This work extends the O(Q³) chiral perturbation theory calculations of γp scattering (McGovern, 2001; Beane et al., 2002) and γd scattering (Beane et al., 2002) to O(Q⁴), enabling the first model-independent extraction of proton polarizabilities from low-energy data alone. The results for γd cross sections and extracted isoscalar polarizabilities agree qualitatively with potential-model treatments (Wilbois et al., 1998; Levchuk et al., 1999; Karakowski et al., 1999), but the chiral EFT approach provides a consistent framework where one- and two-nucleon mechanisms are derived from the same Lagrangian. This work enables future extraction of neutron polarizabilities from deuteron Compton data and can be extended by including an explicit Delta-isobar field to improve convergence at higher energies.
