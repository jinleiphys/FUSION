# Phenomenological Scaling of Rapidity Dependence for Anisotropic Flows in 25 MeV/nucleon Ca + Ca by Quantum Molecular Dynamics Model

> Anisotropic flows ($v_1$, $v_2$, $v_3$ and $v_4$) of light fragments up till the mass number 4 as a function of rapidity have been studied for 25 MeV/nucleon $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca at large impact parameters by Quantum Molecular Dynamics model. A phenomenological scaling behavior of rapidity dependent flow parameters $v_n$ (n = 1, 2, 3 and 4) has been found as a function of mass number plus a constant term, which may arise from the interplay of collective and random motions. In addition, $v_4/{v_2}^2$ keeps almost independent of rapidity and remains a rough constant of 1/2 for all light fragments.

## Key claim

The paper claims a phenomenological scaling behavior for rapidity-dependent anisotropic flows ($v_1$, $v_2$, $v_3$, $v_4$) of light fragments in 25 MeV/nucleon Ca + Ca collisions, where the flows scale with a linear function of mass number plus a constant term $C(A)$ rather than with mass number $A$ alone; the scaling holds excellently except for slight deviations in the large rapidity (spectator) region.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IDQMD) model to simulate approximately 60000 events of $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca collisions at 25 MeV/nucleon with impact parameters from 4 fm to 6 fm. Anisotropic flow coefficients $v_1$, $v_2$, $v_3$, and $v_4$ were extracted from the Fourier expansion of the azimuthal distribution of emitted fragments (neutrons, protons, and clusters up to mass number 4) at a freeze-out time of 200 fm/c. The flows were studied as functions of rapidity normalized to the projectile rapidity in the center-of-mass frame, and a mass-dependent scaling function $C(A)$ was empirically determined to collapse the per-nucleon flow curves for different fragment masses.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear equation of state used a hard EOS with incompressibility $K = 380$ MeV, with parameters $\alpha = 124$ MeV, $\beta = 70.5$ MeV, and $\gamma = 2$.
- The symmetry energy strength was set to $C_{sym} = 32$ MeV (though noted as trivial for the symmetric system).
- The coalescence condition for cluster formation required spatial distance $\Delta r < 3.5$ fm and momentum difference $\Delta p < 300$ MeV/c between two nucleons.
- For $v_1$ and $v_2$, the scaling function was $C(A) = \frac{5}{8}(A + \frac{3}{5})$; for $v_3$ and $v_4$, $C(A) = 3(A - \frac{2}{3})$.
- The ratio $v_4/v_2^2$ was found to be approximately constant at 0.5 across rapidity for all light fragments up to $A = 4$, with some fluctuations at large rapidities.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of nucleon-number scaling for transverse momentum dependent elliptic flow by Yan et al. (2006) and Ma et al. (2006) to the rapidity dependence of higher-order flows ($v_3$, $v_4$) in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions. The observed $v_4/v_2^2 \approx 0.5$ matches the value predicted by relativistic fluid dynamics at RHIC energies (STAR Collaboration, 2003), suggesting that a thermal equilibrium and ideal fluid-like behavior may apply even at this low energy. The results enable further theoretical investigations and experimental checks of collective flow scaling and thermalization in the intermediate energy regime.
