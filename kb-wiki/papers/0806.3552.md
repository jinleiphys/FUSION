# Binding Energies and Radii of the Nuclei with N >=Z in an Alpha-Cluster Model

> Using the surface tension energy put in dependence on the number of alpha-clusters in the core in a phenomenological model representing a nucleus as a core and a nuclear molecule on its surface leads to widening the number of isotopes to be described from the narrow strip of beta-stability to the isotopes with N >= Z. The number of alpha-clusters in the molecule is obtained from the analysis of experimental binding energies and the specific density of the core binding energy ρand the radii are calculated. It is shown that for the isotopes of one Z with growing A the number of alpha-clusters of the molecule decreases mostly to 3 and ρincreases to reach a saturation value within ρ= 2.5 ÷2.7 MeV/fm^3 at the beta - stable isotopes, so the narrow strip of the binding energies of the beta - stable isotopes with Z <= 84 is outlined by a function of one variable Z.

# Binding Energies and Radii of the Nuclei with N ≥ Z in an Alpha-Cluster Model

## Key claim

The paper claims that a phenomenological alpha-cluster model, which represents a nucleus as a core plus a nuclear molecule on its surface, can describe binding energies and radii for isotopes with N ≥ Z, not just the narrow strip of beta-stability. The hedge is that the model works with an average deviation of calculated binding energies from experimental values of about 2.5 MeV, and the specific density of core binding energy ρ reaches a saturation value of ρ = 2.5–2.7 MeV/fm³ only at the beta-stable isotopes.

## Method

The author uses a phenomenological alpha-cluster model where a nucleus is treated as a core composed of alpha-clusters and excess nn-pairs, with a peripheral nuclear molecule on the core's surface. Binding energies are calculated as the sum of nuclear force energy from short-range links, surface tension energy, Coulomb energy, and the binding energy of excess nn-pairs, using formulas from prior work (Nie 2005, 2006). The number of alpha-clusters in the molecule (Nα^ml) is determined by fitting experimental binding energies from the Audi-Wapstra mass table, and charge radii are calculated using several geometric formulas (adding volumes, weighted radii, or liquid-drop-like expressions) and fitted to experimental radii from muonic atom transition data (Fricke 1995).

## Key numbers

- The nuclear force energy between two alpha-clusters is εαα^nuc = 4.350 MeV, composed of εαα = 2.425 MeV and Coulomb repulsion εαα^C = 1.925 MeV.
- The nuclear force energy of one alpha-cluster is εα^nuc = 29.060 MeV, composed of εα = 28.296 MeV (the binding energy of ⁴He) and εα^C = 0.764 MeV.
- The specific density of core binding energy ρ reaches a saturation value of ρ = 2.5–2.7 MeV/fm³ at the beta-stable isotopes, with a central value of ρ = 2.55 MeV/fm³ used for the binding energy formula.
- The charge radius of a core alpha-cluster rα = 1.595 fm was obtained from fitting 116 data points for isotopes with Z ≥ 24, with rms deviation δ = 0.030 fm; for 147 isotopes with Z ≥ 6, δ = 0.064 fm.
- The radius of a peripheral alpha-cluster r_⁴He = 1.71 fm.
- The liquid-drop formula R = r₀A^{1/3} with r₀ = 0.95 fm gives δ = 0.067 fm for Z ≥ 24 and δ = 0.12 fm for Z ≥ 6, considerably worse than the cluster-model formulas.
- Predicted binding energies include: E_{123(45,78)} = 1007 MeV, E_{126(46,80)} = 1034 MeV, E_{139(51,88)} = 1132 MeV, E_{145(53,92)} = 1171 MeV, E_{148(54,94)} = 1193 MeV, E_{189(73,116)} = 1500 MeV, E_{193(75,118)} = 1529 MeV, E_{175(81,94)} = 1348 MeV, E_{261(101,160)} = 1929 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the alpha-cluster model previously developed by Nie (2005, 2006) for symmetrical (N=Z) nuclei and beta-stable isotopes to cover all isotopes with N ≥ Z. The model uses experimental binding energies from the Audi-Wapstra compilation and charge radii from muonic atom data (Fricke 1995, Table IIIA). The key advance is that the number of alpha-clusters in the peripheral molecule (Nα^ml) is determined from binding energy analysis, and the specific density of core binding energy ρ saturates at beta-stability, allowing a simple one-variable function to outline the beta-stability strip for Z ≤ 84. The model enables predictions of binding energies for neutron-rich isotopes beyond the heaviest beta-stable isotope, and suggests that nuclear radii first decrease with decreasing Nα^ml toward beta-stability, then slowly increase for heavier isotopes as excess nn-pairs accumulate on the core surface.
