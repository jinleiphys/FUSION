# What happens in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions when causality is violated?

> We summarize our recent investigations on how causality violations in Israel-Stewart-type relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations can give rise to both analytical and numerical instabilities. The classification of spacetime regions into causal and stable ("good"), acausal but stable ("bad"), and acausal and unstable ("ugly") is reviewed. We compare the predictions of the MUSIC hydrodynamic solver with an analytical solution, and demonstrate how the acausality-driven instabilities develop in a simple one-dimensional scenario.

# What happens in hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions when causality is violated?

## Key claim

Causality violations in Israel-Stewart-type relativistic viscous hydrodynamic simulations do not automatically lead to instabilities in all reference frames; instabilities arise only when the acoustic cone becomes past-directed (vw ≥ 1) or when w² becomes negative, and numerical regulators can suppress these instabilities even when the underlying equations become ill-posed.

## Method

The authors classify fluid cells into three regimes based on the local propagation speed w and flow speed v: "good" (causal, 0 ≤ w ≤ 1), "bad" (acausal but stable in the simulation frame, w > 1 but vw < 1), and "ugly" (acausal and unstable, w² < 0 or vw ≥ 1). They construct an analytical solution of Israel-Stewart theory with bulk viscosity alone for a homogeneous fluid undergoing a specific flow, and compare its behavior to numerical solutions computed with the MUSIC hydrodynamic solver using different initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- The threshold for instability in the benchmark solution occurs at flow speed v ≈ 0.838, where vw = 1.
- The analytical solution bifurcates into two branches at v ≈ 0.838: one branch (v < 0.838) evolves from bad to good and reaches equilibrium at late times, while the other branch is entirely ugly and exhibits unstable behavior.
- The bulk viscosity is set to ζ = P τΠ with τΠ = 1 in code units, and the equation of state is ε = 3P.

## Context

This work extends recent studies showing that Israel-Stewart theory can violate causality in far-from-equilibrium regimes (Causality_bulk, BemficaCausalityShear2020xym) and that superluminal signal propagation occurs at early times in state-of-the-art simulations (Plumberger 2022). The classification into good, bad, and ugly regimes builds on the connection between causality, stability, and well-posedness established by Gavassino (2021) and Gavassino (2021). The findings provide guidance for future implementations of relativistic hydrodynamics in heavy-ion collision simulations, particularly regarding the treatment of initial conditions and numerical regulators that can suppress Hadamard instabilities when w² < 0.
