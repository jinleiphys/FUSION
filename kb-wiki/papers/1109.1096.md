# QCD Chiral restoration at finite $T$ under the Magnetic field: Studies based on the instanton vacuum model

> We investigate the chiral restoration at finite temperature $(T)$ under the strong external magnetic field $\vec{B}=B_{0}\hat{z}$ of the SU(2) light-flavor QCD matter. We employ the instanton-liquid QCD vacuum configuration accompanied with the linear Schwinger method for inducing the magnetic field. The Harrington-Shepard caloron solution is used to modify the instanton parameters, i.e. the average instanton size $(\barρ)$ and inter-instanton distance $(\bar{R})$, as functions of $T$. In addition, we include the meson-loop corrections (MLC) as the large-$N_{c}$ corrections because they are critical for reproducing the universal chiral restoration pattern. We present the numerical results for the constituent-quark mass as well as chiral condensate which signal the spontaneous breakdown of chiral-symmetry (SB$χ$S), as functions of $T$ and $B$. Besides we find that the changes for the $F_π$ and $m_π$ due to the magnetic field is relatively small, in comparison to those caused by the finite $T$ effect.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a strong external magnetic field enhances spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking (SBχS) via magnetic catalysis, shifting the critical temperature for chiral restoration upward, but that the changes to the pion decay constant and pion mass due to the magnetic field are relatively small (a few percent) compared to those caused by finite temperature.

## Method

The authors employ the instanton-liquid QCD vacuum model, using the Harrington-Shepard caloron solution to modify the average instanton size and inter-instanton distance as functions of temperature. The external magnetic field is introduced via the linear Schwinger method, and meson-loop corrections are included as large-Nc corrections to reproduce the universal chiral restoration pattern. Numerical results for the constituent-quark mass, chiral condensate, pion weak-decay constant, and pion mass are computed as functions of temperature and magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

* Critical temperature for chiral restoration in the chiral limit with no magnetic field: T_c = 170.4 MeV for both u and d quarks (n=0, where n = eB_0/m^2_π).
* Critical temperature for the u quark in the chiral limit at n=20: T_c^u = 183.1 MeV, showing a shift upward due to the magnetic field.
* Critical temperature for the d quark in the chiral limit at n=20: T_c^d = 174.1 MeV, a smaller shift than for the u quark.
* At T ≈ 100 MeV, the pion weak-decay constant and pion mass change by about 10% compared to T=0, while changes due to the magnetic field are only a few percent.

## Context

This work extends prior instanton-vacuum studies of chiral symmetry breaking under magnetic fields by Nam (2009, 2010) and incorporates the Harrington-Shepard caloron solution (Harrington and Shepherd, 1976) for finite-temperature instanton parameters. It also builds on the linear Schwinger method (Schwinger, 1951) for magnetic field inclusion and meson-loop corrections (Nam, 2008, 2010) to achieve the correct chiral restoration pattern. The results enable future studies of chiral restoration at finite temperature and magnetic field, with the next step being the inclusion of finite quark chemical potential.
