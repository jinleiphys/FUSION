# Perturbative Renormalizability of Chiral Two Pion Exchange and Power Counting in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

> We show how to renormalize chiral two pion exchange perturbatively if one pion exchange has already been fully iterated at leading order. This particular choice corresponds to the implementation of the counting proposal of Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck at subleading orders. We illustrate why the perturbative treatment of the two pion exchange contributions is mandatory in order to avoid certain inconsistencies in Weinberg's counting. In addition, renormalizability implies modifications of the power counting which we explore for the particular case of the singlet channel.

# Perturbative Renormalizability of Chiral Two Pion Exchange and Power Counting in Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

## Key claim
The paper shows that chiral two-pion exchange (TPE) can be renormalized perturbatively when one-pion exchange (OPE) is fully iterated at leading order, but only if the power counting is modified by promoting a C4 contact operator from order Q^4 to order Q^2. This modification is necessary to remove ultraviolet divergences that appear when only the Weinberg-prescribed counterterms (C0 and C2) are used, and the resulting counting is compatible with the renormalization group analysis of Birse (2005).

## Method
The author implements the power counting proposal of Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck (NTvK) at next-to-leading (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (N^2LO) for the singlet 1S0 channel. The leading-order potential (OPE plus the C0 contact term) is iterated non-perturbatively in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, while subleading TPE contributions and additional contact terms are treated in perturbation theory. The counterterms are fitted to reproduce Nijmegen II phase shifts for momenta k ≤ 200 MeV, using a coordinate-space cutoff rc to regularize short-range singularities.

## Key numbers
- The chiral potential at order Q^ν behaves in coordinate space as 1/(Λ0^ν r^(ν+3)) g(mπ r), where g(x) contains an exponential factor e^(-n x) with n the number of exchanged pions.
- In the perturbative Weinberg counting without the additional C4 counterterm, the N^2LO (order Q^3) contribution to the phase shifts diverges as k^4/rc, where k is the center-of-mass momentum and rc is the coordinate-space cutoff.
- When the C4 counterterm is included, the residual cutoff dependence of the phase shifts becomes proportional to k^6 rc.
- The perturbative treatment fails for cutoff radii below rc ≈ 0.6–0.7 fm due to the appearance of the first deeply bound state, which generates a pole in the renormalization group evolution of the C0 counterterm.
- The KSW counting requires a regularization scale μ < Λ_NN ≈ 300 MeV (or equivalently rc > 1/Λ_NN) for the counting to work.
- The lattice EFT calculations of Borasoy et al. (2007) use a spatial lattice spacing of as = 1.97 fm and a temporal lattice spacing of at = 1.32 fm, providing an acceptable description of NN phase shifts for k ≤ 100 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the NTvK counting proposal (Nogga, Timmermans and van Kolck, 2005) to subleading orders, demonstrating that perturbative renormalizability of chiral TPE requires modifications to Weinberg's original power counting (Weinberg, 1990, 1991). The resulting counting is consistent with the renormalization group analysis of Birse (2005) and with the short-range physics extracted from deconstruction of phenomenological phase shifts with perturbative TPE (Birse, 2007; Birse, 2010; Ipson, 2010). The paper also shows that the non-perturbative Weinberg counting suffers from a "power counting extravaganza" where an order Q^3 operator behaves as order Q^(-1), a problem that perturbative treatment avoids. The results enable future calculations of NLO and N^2LO phase shifts for central waves in the NTvK counting, which have been obtained for the first time in Valderrama (2009).
