# Multiparticle production in nuclear collisions using effective-energy approach

> The dependencies of charged particle pseudorapidity density and transverse energy pseudorapidity density at midrapidity on the collision energy and on the number of nucleon participants, or centrality, measured in nucleus-nucleus collisions are studied in the energy range spanning a few GeV to a few TeV per nucleon. The study is based on the earlier proposed model, combining the constituent quark picture together with Landau relativistic hydrodynamics and shown to interrelate the measurements from different types of collisions. Within this picture, the dependence on the number of participants in heavy-ion collisions are found to be well described in terms of the effective energy defined as a centrality-dependent fraction of the collision energy. The effective energy approach is shown to reveal a similarity in the energy dependence for the most central and centrality data in the entire available energy range. Predictions are made for the forthcoming higher-energy measurements in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the centrality dependence of charged particle pseudorapidity density and transverse energy pseudorapidity density in heavy-ion collisions can be described by an effective-energy approach, where the effective energy is defined as a centrality-dependent fraction of the collision energy, revealing a similarity in energy dependence across all centralities. The hedge is that this description works well for most data but shows some deviations for the most peripheral collisions at low energy (19.6 GeV) and for the most central collisions at LHC energy, attributed to experimental limitations and lack of pp data at corresponding energies.

## Method

The authors extend a previously proposed model combining constituent quark picture with Landau relativistic hydrodynamics to describe the centrality dependence of bulk observables in heavy-ion collisions. They define an effective center-of-mass energy ε_NN = √s_NN (1 - α), where α is the average centrality for a given centrality interval, and use this effective energy in the relationship between midrapidity density per participant pair in heavy-ion collisions and that in pp/pp̅ collisions. The approach is applied to both charged particle pseudorapidity density and transverse energy pseudorapidity density data from RHIC and LHC experiments, with fits using hybrid functions combining logarithmic and power-law terms.

## Key numbers

- Charged particle pseudorapidity density per participant pair at midrapidity in most central collisions at √s_NN = 5.13 TeV predicted to be about 11.5 within 10% uncertainty
- Transverse energy pseudorapidity density per participant pair at midrapidity in most central collisions at √s_NN = 5.13 TeV predicted to be about 16.1 GeV with about 10% uncertainty
- Hybrid fit to central collision charged particle density: ρ(0) = (-0.306 ± 0.027) + (0.364 ± 0.009) ln(s_NN) + (0.0011 ± 0.0011) s_NN^(0.50 ± 0.06)
- Hybrid fit to centrality data charged particle density: ρ(0) = (0.002 ± 0.080) + (0.646 ± 0.022) ln(ε_NN) + (0.0003 ± 0.0001) ε_NN^(1.158 ± 0.034)
- Hybrid fit to central collision transverse energy density: ρ_T(0) = (-0.447 ± 0.014) + (0.327 ± 0.011) ln(s_NN) + (0.002 ± 0.003) s_NN^(0.50 ± 0.08)
- Hybrid fit to centrality data transverse energy density: ρ_T(0) = (-0.387 ± 0.090) + (0.574 ± 0.032) ln(ε_NN) + (0.011 ± 0.005) ε_NN^(0.818 ± 0.064)
- Transition from logarithmic to power-law behavior observed at √s_NN approximately 600-700 GeV for charged particle density and approximately 500-700 GeV for transverse energy density

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark plus Landau hydrodynamics model previously proposed by two of the authors (Sarkisyan and Sakharov, 2010) from central collisions to centrality-dependent measurements in heavy-ion collisions. The approach addresses the failure of the wounded nucleon model (Bialas, Bleszynski, and Czyz, 1976) to describe RHIC and LHC data, and builds on constituent quark scaling observed in nuclear overlap model Monte Carlo simulations (Eremin and Voloshin, 2003; Nouicer, 2006). The results enable predictions for future LHC heavy-ion runs at √s_NN = 5.13 TeV and support the universality of multihadron production across different collision systems when effective energy is properly accounted for.
