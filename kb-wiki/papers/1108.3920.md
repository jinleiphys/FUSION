# Exclusive production of lepton, quark and meson pairs in peripheral ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> We discuss exclusive production of lepton-antilepton, quark-antiquark, $ππ$ and $ρ^0 ρ^0$ pairs in ultraperipheral, ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. The cross sections for exclusive production of pairs of particles is calculated in Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA). Realistic (Fourier transform of charge density) charge form factors of nuclei are used and the corresponding results are compared with the cross sections calculated with monopole form factor used in the literature. Absorption effects are discussed and quantified. The cross sections obtained with realistic form factors are significantly smaller than those obtained with the monopole form factors.

## Key claim

The cross sections for exclusive production of lepton, quark, and meson pairs in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions, calculated using realistic (Fourier transform of charge density) nuclear form factors, are significantly smaller than those obtained with the monopole form factor commonly used in the literature; the effect is more pronounced at larger invariant masses and transverse momenta.

## Method

The total cross sections for exclusive pair production in nucleus-nucleus collisions were calculated using the Equivalent Photon Approximation (EPA) in impact-parameter space, with realistic charge form factors derived from the two-parameter Fermi model of nuclear charge density. The elementary subprocess cross sections for muon pairs were taken from QED, for pion pairs from the Brodsky-Lepage pQCD formalism and the hand-bag model, for rho-rho pairs from a fit to low-energy data and a Regge-VDM model at high energy, and for heavy quark pairs from Born-level QED plus leading-order QCD corrections and a color dipole model for multi-quark final states. Absorption effects were included via a step function excluding impact parameters smaller than twice the nuclear radius.

## Key numbers

- For exclusive muon pair production at LHC with ALICE cuts (pseudorapidity 4 < eta < 5, p_t > 2 GeV), the invariant mass distribution shows increasing difference between realistic and monopole form factor results at larger invariant masses.
- The nuclear cross section for pion pair production shows an enhancement of order 10^4 relative to the elementary gamma-gamma cross section, which is less than the naive Z1^2 Z2^2 expectation.
- For heavy quark production, the charm quark mass was taken as m_c = 1.5 GeV and bottom quark mass as m_b = 4.75 GeV.
- The cross section for exclusive heavy quark pair production was calculated for LHC energy sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of rho-rho pair production (Szczurek et al., 2010), muon pair production (Szczurek and collaborators, 2010), heavy quark pair production (Szczurek and collaborators, 2010), and D meson pair production (Szczurek and collaborators, 2010) by providing a unified treatment with realistic nuclear form factors. The use of realistic form factors corrects the overestimates from the monopole form factor approximation used in earlier literature, and the results enable more accurate predictions for LHC and RHIC experiments, particularly for the ALICE muon detector acceptance.
