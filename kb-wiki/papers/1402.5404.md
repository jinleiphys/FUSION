# Why is GeV physics relevant in the age of the LHC?

> The contribution that Jefferson Lab has made, with its 6 GeV electron beam, and will make, with its 12 GeV upgrade, to our understanding of the way the fundamental interactions work, particularly strong coupling QCD, is outlined. The physics at the GeV scale is essential even in TeV collisions.

## Key claim

GeV-scale physics, as studied at Jefferson Lab, is essential for interpreting TeV-scale collisions at the LHC because the hadronic debris that triggers LHC detectors consists of pions, kaons, and nucleons, objects of fermi size, and the processes of hadron breakup and recombination at TeV energies are governed by physics at the GeV scale.

## Method

The paper reviews the experimental program at Jefferson Lab's CEBAF facility, which uses electron beams at 6 GeV (completed) and 12 GeV (upgraded) to study hadron spectroscopy and structure via electromagnetic probes. It discusses partial wave analyses of photoproduction data performed by the EBAC (ANL-Osaka) collaboration using a Lagrangian-based approach and by the Bonn-Gatchina collaboration using multi-channel amplitude analysis, as well as lattice QCD calculations and Schwinger-Dyson equation studies of hadronic properties.

## Key numbers

- The 6 GeV program at JLab has added significantly to knowledge of the light baryon spectrum, revealing that previously "missing" baryon states were not absent but "dark" in πN and ππN channels.
- The Bonn-Gatchina analysis suggests parity doublets in N* and Δ* spectra appear around 1.9-2 GeV.
- Lattice calculations with heavy pions (400-500 MeV) produce a spectrum resembling the constituent quark model, while physical-mass pions (140 MeV) are expected to yield larger meson-cloud components.
- The EBAC analysis indicates the stable core of the Roper resonance (N*(1440)) is many hundreds of MeV heavier than 1440 MeV.
- Hints of exotic 1⁻⁺ mesons have been seen at 1400 and 1600 MeV by GAMS, VES, BNL-E852, CLEO-c, and COMPASS.
- Lattice calculations predict hybrid baryons with glue contributions are ~1 GeV heavier than their largely qqq companions.
- The ratio of d to u parton distributions at x ~ 1 and Q² ~ 10 GeV² has large current uncertainties that 12 GeV JLab running should dramatically improve.
- The total angular momentum carried by quarks in the nucleon is ~0.15 ħ, leaving a gap to be filled by gluonic angular momentum.

## Context

This paper extends the long-established program of deep inelastic scattering that started at SLAC (Holt, year not given) and continues the work of the CTEQ-JLab group (CJ11) on parton distribution functions. It builds on baryon spectroscopy results from the EBAC/ANL-Osaka collaboration (Lee et al., year not given) and Bonn-Gatchina team (year not given), and on studies of exotic mesons by GAMS, VES, BNL-E852 (Chung, Dzierba, years not given), CLEO-c, and COMPASS. The 12 GeV upgrade enables precision mapping of transverse momentum distributions (TMDs) and generalized parton distributions (GPDs), following results from HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab, and will support the GlueX experiment in Hall D to search for hybrid mesons with exotic quantum numbers.
