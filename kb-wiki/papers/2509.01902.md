# On $Ω_{3c}NN$ and $Ω_{3c} Ω_{3c} N$ systems with HAL QCD potentials

> This study employs the Faddeev formalism in configuration space to investigate the $Ω_{3c}NN$ cluster containing a triply charmed Omega baryon ($Ω_{3c}$). Using the recently reported HAL QCD $S$-wave $Ω_{3c}N$ potentials in the $^3S_1$ and $^5S_2$ channels, together with the MT-I--III nucleon--nucleon potential and neglecting the Coulomb force, we find no bound state for the $Ω_{3c}np$ system. We predict near-threshold resonances in the $J^π=5/2^{+}$ (maximal total spin) and $J^π=1/2^{+}$ (minimal total spin) states, with resonance energies of $1.1~\mathrm{MeV}$ below and $0.0~\mathrm{MeV}$ at the three-body breakup threshold, respectively, at Euclidean time $t/a = 16$. A similar analysis of the $Ω_{3c}Ω_{3c}N$ system likewise reveals no bound states, though a possible resonance is indicated. The short-distance behavior of the HAL QCD $Ω_{3c}N$ potential is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper finds no bound states for the $\Omega_{3c}NN$ or $\Omega_{3c}\Omega_{3c}N$ systems using HAL QCD potentials, but predicts near-threshold resonances for the $\Omega_{3c}NN$ system at specific Euclidean times, with the caveat that the resonance energies depend on the $t/a$ parameter of the potential and exhibit some degree of instability.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for three-body systems with two identical fermions, employing an $S$-wave model with total orbital angular momentum zero. They used the HAL QCD $\Omega_{3c}N$ potentials in the $^3S_1$ and $^5S_2$ channels at Euclidean times $t/a = 16, 17, 18$, the MT-I-III nucleon-nucleon potential, and the HAL QCD $\Omega_{3c}\Omega_{3c}$ potential in the $^1S_0$ channel at $t/a = 26$, neglecting the Coulomb force. Resonance energies were estimated by scaling the $\Omega_{3c}N$ potential depth and extrapolating three-body energies using an exponential interpolation function.

## Key numbers

- $\Omega_{3c}N$ scattering lengths: $a_0 \simeq 0.56~\text{fm}$ in the $^3S_1$ channel and $a_0 \simeq 0.38~\text{fm}$ in the $^5S_2$ channel
- $\Omega_{3c}N$ effective ranges: $r_{\rm eff} \simeq 1.60~\text{fm}$ in the $^3S_1$ channel and $r_{\rm eff} \simeq 2.04~\text{fm}$ in the $^5S_2$ channel
- For the $\Omega_{3c}NN$ system at $t/a = 16$: resonance energy of $-1.1~\mathrm{MeV}$ (below three-body breakup threshold) for the $J^\pi=5/2^{+}$ state and $0.0~\mathrm{MeV}$ (at threshold) for the $J^\pi=1/2^{+}$ state
- For the $\Omega_{3c}NN$ system at $t/a = 17$: resonance energies of $-0.7~\mathrm{MeV}$ for $J^\pi=1/2^{+}$ and $-0.5~\mathrm{MeV}$ for $J^\pi=5/2^{+}$
- For the $\Omega_{3c}NN$ system at $t/a = 18$: resonance energies of $0.1~\mathrm{MeV}$ for $J^\pi=1/2^{+}$ and $0.2~\mathrm{MeV}$ for $J^\pi=5/2^{+}$
- $\Omega_{3c}\Omega_{3c}$ dibaryon binding energy: $5.54~\mathrm{MeV}$
- Estimated resonance energy for $\Omega_{3c}\Omega_{3c}N$ system: approximately $9.5~\mathrm{MeV}$ above three-body threshold
- $\Omega_{3c}$ mass: $4796~\mathrm{MeV}/c^2$
- Lattice spacing: $a \simeq 0.084372~\mathrm{fm}$, spatial extent $L \simeq 8.1~\mathrm{fm}$
- Pion mass: $m_\pi = 137.1~\mathrm{MeV}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of tribaryon systems with strangeness $-3$, such as $\Omega NN$ investigated by Garcilazo (2018, 2019), Zhang (2022), Etminan (2023), and Filikhin (2025), to the charm sector using the recently reported HAL QCD $\Omega_{3c}N$ potentials from Zhang and the HAL QCD Collaboration. The study builds on lattice QCD simulations of $\Omega_{3c}N$ interactions by the HAL QCD Collaboration and $\Omega_{3c}\Omega_{3c}$ interactions by Lyu (2021), and enables future investigations of charmed hypernuclei and exotic multiquark states, while also raising questions about the short-distance behavior of the $\Omega_{3c}N$ potential and the validity of pair-potential descriptions at distances where baryon overlap occurs.
