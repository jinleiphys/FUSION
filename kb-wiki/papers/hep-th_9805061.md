# Time-Dependent Variational Principle for $ϕ^4$ Field Theory: RPA Approximation and Renormalization (II)

> The Gaussian-time-dependent variational equations are used to explored the physics of $(ϕ^4)_{3+1}$ field theory. We have investigated the static solutions and discussed the conditions of renormalization. Using these results and stability analysis we show that there are two viable non-trivial versions of $(ϕ^4)_{3+1}$. In the continuum limit the bare coupling constant can assume $b\to 0^{+}$ and $b\to 0^{-}$, which yield well defined asymmetric and symmetric solutions respectively. We have also considered small oscillations in the broken phase and shown that they give one and two meson modes of the theory. The resulting equation has a closed solution leading to a ``zero mode'' and vanished scattering amplitude in the limit of infinite cutoff.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Gaussian time-dependent variational approach yields two viable non-trivial versions of \((\phi^4)_{3+1}\) field theory in the continuum limit, but only when the bare coupling constant approaches zero from either the positive or negative side; the resulting scattering amplitude vanishes in the limit of infinite cutoff, and a stable "zero mode" appears.

## Method

The authors linearize the time-dependent variational equations around stationary points of the Gaussian effective Hamiltonian to obtain RPA (random phase approximation) equations for one- and two-particle wavefunctions. They solve the coupled channel scattering problem analytically by treating the two-particle propagator as a separable potential, and renormalize the gap equation by introducing a mass scale \(\mu\) and a dimensionless renormalized coupling constant \(g_\mu\). The stability of the static solutions is analyzed via the eigenvalues of the Hessian matrix of the energy density \(u(\varphi,m)\).

## Key numbers

* The gap equation in the broken phase yields the relation \(b\varphi^2 = 3\bar m^2\), where \(\bar m\) is the mass at the minimum of the energy density.
* The renormalized gap equation takes the form \(m^2 = \epsilon\mu^2 + \frac{\bar g_\mu}{1+\bar g_\mu} m^2 \log(m^2/\mu^2)\), with \(\bar g_\mu = g_\mu/16\pi^2\) and \(\epsilon = 0, \pm 1\).
* For the broken phase, the eigenvalues of the stability matrix are \(\lambda^+ = \bar m^2 L_\mu \{3 + \mathcal{O}(1/L_\mu)\}\) and \(\lambda^- = \frac{2\bar m^2}{3L_\mu} \bigl(-\frac{1}{g_\mu} + \frac{1}{16\pi^2}\log\frac{\bar m^2}{\mu^2}\bigr) + \mathcal{O}(1/L_\mu^2)\), where \(L_\mu = \frac{1}{8\pi^2}\log(2\Lambda_{\bf p}/e\mu)\) diverges with the cutoff \(\Lambda_{\bf p}\).
* The scattering amplitude in the broken phase vanishes as \(\mathcal{O}(1/L_\mu)\) in the limit of infinite cutoff, as shown in Eq. (5.11): \(\Omega^2 - {\bf P}^2 - \bar m^2 \mathcal{O}(1/L_\mu) = 0\).

## Context

This paper is a direct continuation of the authors' earlier work (Kerman and Lin, 1998, referred to as I), which derived the RPA equations for the symmetric vacuum of \(\phi^4\) theory. The present work extends that analysis to the asymmetric (broken) vacuum and to the massless solution. The Gaussian effective potential (GEP) obtained here recovers the result of the authors of Ref. [3] (unidentified in the truncated text) after a change of variables. The results enable a nonperturbative treatment of one- and two-meson scattering in \((\phi^4)_{3+1}\) and suggest that only two distinct renormalized theories exist, distinguished by the sign of the bare coupling constant.
