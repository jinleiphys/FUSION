# Implications of the measurement of pulsars with two solar masses for quark matter in compact stars and HIC. A NJL model case study

> The precise measurement of the high masses of the pulsars PSR J1614-2230 (M_{1614}=1.97 +- 0.04 solar masses) and PSR J0348-0432 (M_{0348}=2.01 +- 0.04 solar masses) provides an important constraint for the equation of state of cold, dense matter and is suited to give interesting insights regarding the nature and existence of the possible phase transition to deconfined quark matter in the cores of neutron stars. We analyze the stability and composition of compact star sequences for a class of hybrid nuclear - quark-matter equations of state. The quark matter phase is described in the framework of a standard color superconducting 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model and the hadronic phase is given by the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state for the Bonn-A potential. The phase transition is obtained by a Maxwell construction. Within this model setup we aim to constrain otherwise not strictly fixed parameters of the NJL model, namely the coupling strengths in the vector meson and diquark interaction channels. We perform this investigation for two different parameterizations characterized by a different scalar coupling constant. The analysis of flow data obtained in heavy-ion collisions resulted in a further constraint which we account for in our discussion. Massive hybrid stars with extended quark matter cores can be obtained in accordance with all of the considered constraints.

## Key claim

Within the specific model setup (NJL quark matter with DBHF nuclear matter and a Maxwell construction), hybrid stars with extended quark matter cores can reach masses of 2 solar masses or higher, contradicting the claim that such massive pulsars rule out the existence of deconfined quark matter in neutron star cores. The result depends on choosing sufficiently large vector coupling and appropriate diquark coupling in the NJL model.

## Method

The authors constructed hybrid equations of state by combining a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) nuclear matter equation of state for the Bonn-A potential with a standard color-superconducting 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for quark matter, connected via a Maxwell construction. They systematically scanned the NJL parameter space by varying the vector meson coupling strength (eta_V from 0 to 0.7) and diquark coupling strength (eta_D from 0.8 to 1.15) for two different parameter sets (Set A and Set B) that differ in scalar coupling constant and constituent quark mass, computing a total of 928 different quark matter equations of state and solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for each.

## Key numbers

- Pulsar PSR J1614-2230 mass: 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses
- Pulsar PSR J0348-0432 mass: 2.01 +/- 0.04 solar masses
- Previously highest well-known neutron star mass: 1.667 +/- 0.021 solar masses for PSR J1903+0327
- Maximum mass of the purely hadronic DBHF Bonn-A equation of state: 2.4 solar masses
- For any hybrid equation of state reproducing the maximum mass of PSR J1614-2230, the radius of the most massive configuration is around 12 km
- Set A parameters: scalar coupling G_S * Lambda^2 = 2.319, cutoff Lambda = 602.30 MeV, light constituent quark mass M_u = 367.5 MeV
- Set B parameters: scalar coupling G_S * Lambda^2 = 2.176, cutoff Lambda = 629.54 MeV, light constituent quark mass M_u = 330.0 MeV
- Fierz-transformed ratios: eta_V = 0.5, eta_D = 0.75 for vector and diquark couplings relative to scalar coupling

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Klahn 2006) and responds to claims by Lattimer (2010) and Ozel (2006) that 2-solar-mass pulsars rule out quark matter in neutron star cores, demonstrating instead that hybrid stars with quark cores remain viable. The study uses the same NJL model framework as earlier work by Blaschke (2005), Ruester (2005), and Abuki (2005) but systematically scans the vector and diquark coupling parameter space, and additionally checks consistency with heavy-ion collision flow constraints from Danielewicz (2002). The results enable future Bayesian analyses of neutron star mass-radius data to constrain the quark matter equation of state parameters.
