# Photon radiation in hot nuclear matter by means of chiral anomalies

> A new mechanism of photon emission in the quark-gluon plasma is proposed. Photon dispersion relation in the presence of the $CP$-odd topological regions generated by the chiral anomaly acquires an imaginary mass. It allows photon radiation through the decay $q\to qγ$ and annihilation $q\bar q\to γ$ processes closely related to the chiral Cherenkov radiation. Unlike previous proposals this mechanism does not require an external magnetic field. The differential photon emission rate per unit volume is computed and shown to be comparable to the rate of photon emission in conventional processes.

## Key claim

A new mechanism for photon emission from the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is proposed, operating via the chiral anomaly without requiring an external magnetic field. The mechanism relies on the photon acquiring an imaginary mass from interaction with CP-odd topological domains, enabling decay and annihilation processes, but the effect is conditional on the photon energy and plasma temperature satisfying |m_A| > ω_pl, which may only occur at sufficiently high temperatures where the sphaleron transition rate rises as T^4.

## Method

The author computes the photon emission rate from QGP using the axion-photon coupling term in the Lagrangian, where the axion field θ is sourced by topological charge density fluctuations from sphaleron-mediated transitions between QCD vacua. The photon dispersion relation acquires an imaginary mass m_A from the chiral conductivity σ_χ, and the differential photon emission rate per unit volume is calculated for two channels: the decay channel q → qγ and the annihilation channel qq̅ → γ, using standard thermal field theory with quark equilibrium distribution functions and the high-energy approximation ω ≫ m.

## Key numbers

- Plasma frequency at T = 400 MeV: ω_pl ≈ 35 MeV
- Chiral conductivity σ_χ estimated as 1–10 MeV, leading to infrared photon emission threshold ω_0 ∼ 0.1–1 GeV
- For ω ≪ T, the decay channel spectrum scales as 1/ω², with explicit form ω dΓ_D/d³k ∝ 3ζ(3)/(βω)³
- For ω ≫ T, the decay channel spectrum gives ω dΓ_D/d³k ∝ (1/βω) e^{-βω}
- For ω ≪ T, the annihilation channel spectrum gives ω dΓ_A/d³k ∝ 1/6 (constant in ω)
- For ω ≫ T, the annihilation channel spectrum gives ω dΓ_A/d³k ∝ (2/3) e^{-βω}
- The total photon emission rate is comparable to conventional contributions (referencing Fig. 3 of Paquet (2015))

## Context

This work extends the chiral Cherenkov radiation mechanism previously developed for single quarks in Tuchin (2018) and Huang (2018) to the bulk QGP, and contrasts with earlier proposals that required external magnetic fields, such as synchrotron radiation (Tuchin, 2012) and radiation via the chiral anomaly in a magnetic field (Fukushima, 2012). The result enables a new contribution to the direct photon spectrum in heavy-ion collisions that may help explain the observed photon enhancement (Adare, 2008; Adam, 2015), and the chiral conductivity parameter σ_χ can potentially be extracted from charge separation measurements (Kharzeev, 2007).
