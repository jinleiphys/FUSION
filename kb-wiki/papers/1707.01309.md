# Periodic orbit bifurcations and local symmetry restorations in exotic-shape nuclear mean fields

> The semiclassical origins of the enhancement of shell effects in exotic-shape mean-field potentials are investigated by focusing attention on the roles of the local symmetries associated with the periodic-orbit bifurcations. The deformed shell structures for four types of pure octupole shapes in the nuclear mean-field model having a realistic radial dependence are analyzed. Remarkable shell effects are shown for a large Y32 deformation having tetrahedral symmetry. Much stronger shell effects found in the shape parametrization smoothly connecting the sphere and the tetrahedron are investigated from the view point of the classical-quantum correspondence. The local dynamical symmetries associated with the bridge orbit bifurcations are shown to have significant roles in emergence of the exotic deformed shell structures for certain combinations of the surface diffuseness and the tetrahedral deformation parameters.

# Periodic orbit bifurcations and local symmetry restorations in exotic-shape nuclear mean fields

## Key claim

The anomalously strong shell effects found in tetrahedral-shape nuclear mean-field potentials arise from local dynamical symmetry restorations associated with bridge orbit bifurcations, where families of quasi-periodic orbits connect pairs of periodic orbits that are distant from each other in phase space. This mechanism explains the emergence of deformed magic numbers identical to those of the spherical harmonic oscillator for certain combinations of surface diffuseness and tetrahedral deformation parameters.

## Method

The author analyzed single-particle level structures in the radial power-law (RPL) potential model, which approximates the Woods-Saxon potential with a tunable surface sharpness parameter α. Classical periodic orbits were computed for tetrahedral deformations using two shape parametrizations: a pure Y₃₂ octupole deformation and a shape smoothly interpolating between sphere and tetrahedron. Fourier transforms of the quantum level density were compared with classical periodic orbit periods to identify bifurcation-enhanced contributions to shell effects.

## Key numbers

- Shell closures at large Y₃₂ deformation (β₃₂ ≈ 0.3–0.4) occur at particle numbers 2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112, 168, 240, identical to spherical harmonic oscillator magic numbers.
- Bridge orbit bifurcation occurs between β_td = 0.502 (diameter orbit DA bifurcation) and β_td = 0.574 (three-dimensional rotational orbit TB submergence) for α = 5.0.
- Strong shell effect found at tetrahedral parameter β_td ≈ 0.8 for the sphere-to-tetrahedron shape parametrization.
- Fourier spectra peaks at scaled periods τ = 5.06 (diameter orbit) and τ = 5.84 (circle orbit) in the spherical case, merging into a single enhanced peak at τ ≈ 5.6 for β_td ≈ 0.6.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical analysis of deformed shell structures beyond the quadrupole deformations studied by Nazarewicz (1990s) and the octupole shell effects examined by Hamamoto et al. (1990s) using modified oscillator potentials. It builds on the author's previous work on bridge orbit bifurcations (Arita 2012, 2016) and provides a semiclassical explanation for the tetrahedral shell effects found in Woods-Saxon calculations by Dudek et al. (2006, 2007). The results enable future investigations of how hidden dynamical symmetries in exotic nuclear shapes can be identified through classical periodic orbit analysis, potentially guiding searches for tetrahedral-shape nuclei in regions far from stability.
