# Screening Effects in Superfluid Nuclear and Neutron Matter within Brueckner Theory

> Effects of medium polarization are studied for $^1S_0$ pairing in neutron and nuclear matter. The screening potential is calculated in the RPA limit, suitably renormalized to cure the low density mechanical instability of nuclear matter. The selfenergy corrections are consistently included resulting in a strong depletion of the Fermi surface. All medium effects are calculated based on the Brueckner theory. The $^1S_0$ gap is determined from the generalized gap equation. The selfenergy corrections always lead to a quenching of the gap, which is enhanced by the screening effect of the pairing potential in neutron matter, whereas it is almost completely compensated by the antiscreening effect in nuclear matter.

# Screening Effects in Superfluid Nuclear and Neutron Matter within Brueckner Theory

## Key claim

Medium polarization effects on $^1S_0$ pairing in neutron and nuclear matter produce opposite outcomes: screening quenches the gap in neutron matter, while antiscreening enhances it in nuclear matter, but selfenergy corrections almost completely compensate the antiscreening in nuclear matter, leaving the gap close to the bare-interaction value.

## Method

The authors solved the generalized gap equation for $^1S_0$ pairing using the Argonne V18 two-body force plus a microscopic three-body force, with the screening potential calculated in the RPA limit renormalized via the Babu-Brown induced interaction theory to avoid the low-density mechanical instability. The G-matrix from Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory provided the particle-hole vertices, approximated by Landau parameters extracted from a Skyrme-like fit to the BHF energy functional. Selfenergy corrections, including Fermi surface depletion via the Z-factor, were consistently incorporated.

## Key numbers

- In neutron matter, the screening effect reduces the gap by about 10% in the peak region (with Z=1 and free single-particle spectrum).
- In nuclear matter, the antiscreening effect raises the gap from 3 MeV to 5 MeV at Fermi momentum $k_F = 0.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ (with Z=1 and free spectrum).
- The Z-factor at $k_F = 0.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ is 0.84 in neutron matter and 0.68 in nuclear matter.
- The maximum gap in the complete calculation (including selfenergy and screening) is $1.5 - 2$ MeV at $k_F \approx 0.8\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ for neutron matter.
- At $k_F = 0.6\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ in nuclear matter, the pairing interaction strength changes from -13 $\mathrm{MeV}\cdot\mathrm{fm}^3$ (bare) to -27 $\mathrm{MeV}\cdot\mathrm{fm}^3$ (with one-bubble screening).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Shen et al., 2005) by replacing the Gogny force with the realistic Argonne V18 interaction and correcting a phase error that had produced an overly strong antiscreening effect in symmetric nuclear matter. The results confirm earlier findings that screening quenches pairing in neutron matter (Clark, 1979; Schulze et al., 2001) but show that in nuclear matter the isospin modes reverse the competition between attractive density and repulsive spin-density excitations, producing antiscreening. The near-complete cancellation between vertex corrections and selfenergy effects in nuclear matter explains why the full medium-modified gap remains close to the bare-interaction result, consistent with the observation that Gogny-force gaps match experimental gaps in finite nuclei (Schuck, 1999). The work enables future studies of the transition from screening in neutron-rich matter to antiscreening in symmetric nuclear matter, and of pairing in channels where the bare interaction is repulsive.
