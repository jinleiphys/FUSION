# The Tjon Band in Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory

> We explore the lattice spacing dependence in Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory for few-body systems up to next-to-next-to leading order in chiral effective field theory including all isospin breaking and electromagnetic effects, the complete two-pion-exchange potential and the three-nucleon forces. We calculate phase shifts in the neutron-proton system and proton-proton systems as well as the scattering length in the neutron-neutron system. We then perform a full next-to-next-to-leading order calculation with two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces for the triton and helium-4 and analyse their binding energy correlation. We show how the Tjon band is reached by decreasing the lattice spacing and confirm the continuum observation that a four-body force is not necessary to describe light nuclei.

## Key claim

By decreasing the lattice spacing in Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory, the binding energies of triton and helium-4 converge to the Tjon band, confirming that an explicit four-body force is not required to describe light nuclei at next-to-next-to-leading order, provided the lattice spacing is chosen small enough.

## Method

The authors performed lattice calculations at three spatial lattice spacings (a = 1.97 fm, 1.64 fm, and 1.32 fm) using chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-leading order, including all isospin breaking and electromagnetic effects, the complete two-pion-exchange potential, and three-nucleon forces. Two-body phase shifts were computed using a radial projection method with a spherical wall boundary, with leading order solved non-perturbatively and higher-order corrections included perturbatively. Three- and four-body binding energies were calculated using the Lanczos method and Monte Carlo simulations respectively, with the three-nucleon forces parameterized by the low-energy constants c_D and c_E fitted to triton properties.

- Reference scale for three-nucleon forces: Lambda = 700 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory framework of Epelbaum (2009) and Lahde (2013), which previously required an effective four-nucleon force to correct overbinding of helium-4 on coarse lattices. The study confirms the Tjon band correlation between triton and helium-4 binding energies, originally observed by Tjon (1975) and refined by Platter (2004), and demonstrates that this correlation is naturally recovered as lattice spacing decreases. The results enable future calculations of medium-mass nuclei without the need for ad hoc four-body counterterms, and support the use of finer lattices for improved accuracy in nuclear structure calculations.
