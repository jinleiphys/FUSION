# Energy-momentum tensor of the dilute (3+1)D Glasma

> We present a succinct formulation of the energy-momentum tensor of the Glasma characterizing the initial color fields in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. We derive concise expressions for the (3+1)D dynamical evolution of symmetric nuclear collisions in the weak field approximation employing a generalized McLerran-Venugopalan model with non-trivial longitudinal correlations. Utilizing Monte Carlo integration, we calculate in unprecedented detail non-trivial rapidity profiles of early-time observables at RHIC and LHC energies, including transverse energy densities and eccentricities. For our setup with broken boost invariance, we carefully discuss the placement of the origin of the Milne frame and interpret the components of the energy-momentum tensor. We find longitudinal flow that deviates from standard Bjorken flow in the (3+1)D case and provide a geometric interpretation of this effect. Furthermore, we observe a universal shape in the flanks of the rapidity profiles regardless of collision energy and predict that limiting fragmentation should also hold at LHC energies.

## Key claim

The authors derive concise analytic expressions for the (3+1)D Glasma field strength tensor in the weak field approximation, reducing the number of integrals from six to three compared to the gauge field formulation, and find that longitudinal flow deviates from standard Bjorken flow with a universal shape in the flanks of rapidity profiles regardless of collision energy.

## Method

The authors solve the Classical Yang-Mills equations in covariant gauge to leading order in the color charge densities of the two colliding nuclei, obtaining the Glasma field strength tensor components as three-dimensional integrals over lightlike paths in the causal past of the evaluation point. They employ a generalized McLerran-Venugopalan model with a longitudinal correlation length parameter and perform Monte Carlo integration with optimized sampling bounds to compute the energy-momentum tensor, local rest frame energy density, and fluid velocity via the Landau condition in a shifted Milne frame.

## Key numbers

- The longitudinal correlation length parameter xi ranges from 0 to 2 R_l, where R_l = R/(sqrt(2)*gamma_beam) is the boosted Woods-Saxon radius
- The MV scale mu is related to the saturation momentum by Q_s approximately equal to g^2 mu in the non-perturbative case
- The normalization condition integral dx^pm T(x^pm, zero_perp) = 1 ensures recovery of the two-dimensional MV model at the center of the nuclei
- The field strength tensor integrals are evaluated for RHIC and LHC energy ranges, with the Woods-Saxon profile parameters R (radius) and d (skin depth) used for nuclear modeling

## Context

This work extends the first analytical calculation of energy deposition in high-energy heavy-ion collisions from Ipp et al. (2021) by formulating the energy-momentum tensor of the dilute Glasma, showing excellent agreement with non-perturbative lattice simulations from Ipp et al. (2021, 2022). The results enable more efficient computation of (3+1)D initial conditions for heavy-ion collisions compared to previous approaches such as IP-Glasma (Schenke et al., 2012) or lattice-based methods (Gelfand et al., 2016; Ipp et al., 2017), and provide a framework for testing limiting fragmentation predictions at LHC energies against experimental measurements of flow decorrelations from CMS (2015) and ATLAS (2017).
