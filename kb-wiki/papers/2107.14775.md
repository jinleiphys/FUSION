# $η/s-\hat{q}/T^3$ Relation at Next-to-Leading Order in QCD

> The relation between the specific shear viscosity $η/s$ and the dimensionless jet quenching parameter $\hat{q}/T^3$ in perturbative QCD is explored at next-to-leading order in the coupling constant. It is shown that the relation changes little, although both transport coefficients independently are subject to large modifications at the NLO level. This finding confirms that the relationship is robust.

# η/s-ˆq/T³ Relation at Next-to-Leading Order in QCD

## Key claim

The product (η/s)·(ˆq/T³) remains approximately constant and changes by only a factor ~1.6 at next-to-leading order, even though each transport coefficient individually changes by more than a factor 10 relative to its leading-logarithmic value. This robustness holds for coupling strengths corresponding to mD/T ~ 2–2.5 (αs ≈ 0.3), which are relevant for the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The author computes the specific shear viscosity η/s and the dimensionless jet quenching parameter ˆq/T³ at next-to-leading order (NLO) in perturbative QCD using the effective kinetic theory framework of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002) and its NLO extension by Ghiglieri, Moore, and Teaney (2015, 2018). For ˆq, the NLO result combines the leading-order expression with a correction δˆq that incorporates full dynamical screening of one-gluon exchange; for η/s, the author uses an analytical fit to the NLO numerical results of Ghiglieri et al. (2018) that includes NLO corrections to diffusion coefficients, conversion rates, and collinear splitting processes. The double ratio is then evaluated as a function of mD/T for both a pure gluon plasma (Nf=0) and a three-flavor quark-gluon plasma (Nf=3).

## Key numbers

- For a pure gluon plasma at leading-logarithmic order: (η_g/s_g)·(ˆq_A/T³) = 1.84 (Eq. 13).
- For a three-flavor quark-gluon plasma at leading-logarithmic order: (η/s)_{Nf=3}·(ˆq_F/T³) = 1.63 (Eq. 16).
- At NLO for realistic couplings (mD/T ~ 2–2.5), the double ratio differs from the LL result by only a factor ~1.6 and is weakly dependent on the coupling strength.
- The NLO correction increases ˆq by up to a factor 15 at the upper end of the coupling range (mD/T < 2.5, corresponding to 0 < αs < 1/π).
- The NLO correction suppresses η/s by more than a factor 10 at the upper end of the same coupling range.
- The ultraviolet cut-off is set to μ/T = 2.765 for pure gauge theory and μ/T = 2.957 for three-flavor QCD.

## Context

This work extends the original relation proposed by Majumder, Müller, and Wang (2007), who argued that (η/s)·(ˆq/T³) ≈ constant in any perturbative gauge theory with small-angle scattering dominated by massless or low-mass quanta. The NLO analysis uses the transport coefficient calculations of Ghiglieri, Moore, and Teaney (2015, 2018) for both η and ˆq. The finding that the double ratio is robust against large NLO corrections confirms the MMW relation as a reliable constraint for simultaneous extraction of η/s and ˆq/T³ from experimental data, motivating combined model-data comparisons rather than independent extractions.
