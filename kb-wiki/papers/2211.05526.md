# Phenomenological description of the $π^-π^+$ $S$-waves in $D^+\toπ^-π^+π^+$ and $D^+_s\toπ^-π^+π^+$ decays: The problem of phases

> We present a phenomenological description of the LHCb data for the magnitudes and phases of the $π^-π^+$ $S$-wave amplitudes in the $D^+\toπ^-π^+π^+$ and $D^+_s\toπ^-π^+π^+$ decays. We operate within a simple model that takes into account the known pair interactions of particles in coupled channels. The seed complex amplitudes for various intermediate state production are assumed to be independent of the energy. Their values are determined by fitting. This model gives the satisfactorily description of virtually all features of the energy dependence of the experimentally measured $S$-wave amplitudes in the $D^+\toπ^-π^+ π^+$ and $D^+_s\to π^-π^+π^+$ decays in the regions $2m_π<m_{π^-π^+}<1.39\mbox{ GeV}$ and $2m_π<m_{π^-π^+} <1.29\mbox{ GeV}$, respectively.

## Key claim

The paper presents a phenomenological model that can satisfactorily describe the LHCb data for the magnitudes and phases of the π⁻π⁺ S-wave amplitudes in D⁺→π⁻π⁺π⁺ and D⁺_s→π⁻π⁺π⁺ decays, but only in the restricted mass regions 2m_π < m_{π⁻π⁺} < 1.39 GeV and 2m_π < m_{π⁻π⁺} < 1.29 GeV, respectively; the model does not describe the oscillations observed above these regions, which would require additional mechanisms for f₀(1370) and f₀(1500) production.

## Method

The authors model the S-wave amplitude as a sum of seed complex constants (λ_ab) plus loop integrals I_ab(s) multiplied by known ππ→ππ and K̄K→ππ scattering amplitudes T(s), with the ππ and K̄K scattering amplitudes taken from Achasov and Kiselev (2006) and Achasov (2003). The seed amplitudes λ_ab and subtraction constants C_ab are free parameters determined by fitting to the LHCb data on the magnitude a₀(s) and phase δ₀(s) of the S-wave amplitude in each decay channel.

## Key numbers

- The S-wave contribution accounts for approximately 62% of the full decay rate of D⁺→π⁻π⁺π⁺ and approximately 85% of the full decay rate of D⁺_s→π⁻π⁺π⁺.
- For D⁺→π⁻π⁺π⁺, the fit including K̄K contributions yields χ²=162 with parameters λ_{π⁺π⁻} = -1.21 + i11.21, λ_{π⁰π⁰} = 20.40 + i4.47, C_{ππ} = 0.68, λ_{K⁺K⁻} = 39.11 + i27.43, λ_{K⁰K̄⁰} = -32.93 - i29.98, C_{K̄K} = 0.46.
- For D⁺_s→π⁻π⁺π⁺, the fit yields χ²=129 with parameters λ_{π⁺π⁻} = 5.37 - i2.30, C_{ππ} = 1.69, λ_{K⁺K⁻} = 20.18 - i8.94, C_{K̄K} = 0.60.
- The fit without K̄K contributions for D⁺→π⁻π⁺π⁺ gives χ²=278 with parameters λ_{π⁺π⁻} = -1.72 + i11.30, λ_{π⁰π⁰} = 17.86 + i6.59, C_{ππ} = 0.77.
- The amplitude a₀(s) reaches its minimum at √s = m_{π⁻π⁺} ≈ 0.9 GeV, where the ππ-scattering amplitude T⁰₀(s) reaches the unitary limit.

## Context

This work extends the isobar model approach previously applied to D⁺→π⁻π⁺π⁺ by Boito et al. (2007) and to D/D_s→π⁺π⁻e⁺ν_e by Achasov et al. (2020), using known ππ and K̄K scattering amplitudes instead of Breit-Wigner resonance forms. The measured S-wave phases deviate from the Watson final-state interaction theorem, as noted by the LHCb collaboration (Aaij et al., 2022a), indicating the importance of three-body interactions. The model enables predictions for the π⁰π⁰ S-waves in D⁺→π⁺π⁰π⁰ and D⁺_s→π⁺π⁰π⁰ decays, which the authors identify as crucial tests of their approach.
