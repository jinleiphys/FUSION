# Gauge Invariances and Phases of Massive Higher Spins in (A)dS

> The (m^2,Λ) plane of spin s>1 massive fields in (A)dS backgrounds is shown to consist of separate phases, divided by lines of novel ``partially massless'' gauge theories that successively remove helicities, starting from the lowest, 0 or +/-(1/2). The norms of the excluded states flip as the gauge lines are crossed and only the region containing the massive Minkowski theory is unitary. The partially massless gauge theories are unitary or not, depending on the ordering of the gauge lines. This ``level splitting'' of massless Minkowski gauge theories is specific to non-zero Λ.

## Key claim

The (m², Λ) plane for massive spin s > 1 fields in (A)dS backgrounds is partitioned into separate phases by lines of "partially massless" gauge theories that successively remove helicities starting from the lowest (0 or ±1/2); the norms of the excluded states flip sign as these gauge lines are crossed, and only the region containing the massive Minkowski theory is unitary, with the partially massless gauge theories being unitary or not depending on the ordering of the gauge lines.

## Method

The authors analyze the field equations for spins s = 3/2, 2, 5/2, and 3 in (A)dS backgrounds, computing divergences of the field equations to identify where constraints become Bianchi identities (indicating gauge invariances). They examine the coefficients of these constraints in canonical (anti)commutators to determine the sign of norms for each helicity state, thereby mapping the unitary and non-unitary regions of the (m², Λ) plane. A counting argument for the number of propagating degrees of freedom (PDoF) is given for general higher spins, showing how successive gauge invariances remove lower helicities.

## Key numbers

- Spin 3/2: gauge line at m² = -Λ/3 (DoF = 2); unitary region is m² ≥ -Λ/3; the anticommutator coefficient controlling unitarity is (m² + Λ/3).
- Spin 2: gauge lines at m² = 0 (DoF = 2, linearized cosmological Einstein gravity) and m² = 2Λ/3 (DoF = 4, partially massless Weyl-like theory); the dS region m² < 2Λ/3 is unitarily forbidden.
- Spin 5/2: gauge lines at m² = -Λ/3 (DoF = 4, partially massless) and m² = -4Λ/3 (DoF = 2, strictly massless); the unitary region is m² > -4Λ/3; the partially massless AdS theory is non-unitary.
- Spin 3: gauge lines at m² = 2Λ (DoF = 6, partially massless), m² = 4Λ/3 (DoF = 4, partially massless), and m² = 0 (DoF = 2, strictly massless); the unitary region is m² > 2Λ; the partially massless dS theory at m² = 2Λ is unitary.
- General counting: for bosons, a partially massless theory with t = s scalar divergences (maximal) yields 2s PDoF; with t = s-1 vector divergences yields 2s-2 PDoF; the strictly massless case t = 1 yields 2 PDoF.

## Context

This work extends the known result for spin 2 (Deser 1983) that a partially massless gauge theory exists along m² = 2Λ/3, and the unitarity analysis of Higuchi (1987) for spin 2 in dS. It generalizes the pattern to all higher spins, showing that the (m², Λ) plane is divided into phases by gauge lines, with only the region containing the Minkowski massive theory being unitary. The paper enables the systematic classification of unitary higher-spin theories in (A)dS and raises the question of the precise location of gauge lines for s > 3, suggesting a possible distinction between bosons and fermions for higher spins.
