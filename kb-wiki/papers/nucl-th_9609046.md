# No-core shell-model calculations with starting- energy-independent multi-valued effective interactions

> Large-space no-core shell model calculations have been performed for A=3-6 nuclei, using a starting-energy-independent two-body effective interaction derived by application of the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation. This transformation can be performed by direct calculation or by different iteration procedures, which are described. A possible way of reducing the auxiliary potential influence on the two-body effective interaction has also been introduced. The many-body effects have been partially taken into account by employing the recently introduced multi-valued effective interaction approach. Dependence of the He5 energy levels on the harmonic-oscillator frequency as well as on the size of the model space has been studied. The Reid 93 nucleon-nucleon potential has been used in the study, but results have also been obtained using the Nijmegen II potential for comparison.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a starting-energy-independent two-body effective interaction derived via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation can be used in no-core shell-model calculations for light nuclei (A=3-6), but the unmodified two-body Hamiltonian overbinds the system; an *ad hoc* modification of the Q-space part of the auxiliary harmonic-oscillator potential (with parameter \(k_Q=0.6\)) is required to bring calculated binding energies into reasonable agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors performed large-space no-core shell-model calculations for \(^3\)H, \(^4\)He, \(^5\)He, \(^6\)Li, and \(^6\)He using a complete \(N\hbar\Omega\) model space (up to \(N=8\)). The two-body effective interaction was derived from the Reid 93 nucleon-nucleon potential (and the Nijmegen II potential for comparison) by three methods: the Krenciglowa-Kuo iteration, the vertex-renormalization iteration using exact G-matrix derivatives, and a direct construction of the transformation operator \(\omega\) from the solutions of the two-body Schrödinger equation. Many-body effects were partially included via the multi-valued effective interaction approach, where different effective interactions are used for different \(\hbar\Omega\) excitations. To reduce the spurious influence of the auxiliary harmonic-oscillator potential, the Q-space part of the relative-coordinate two-nucleon Hamiltonian was modified by introducing a parameter \(k_Q\) (set to 0.6 for most calculations).

## Key numbers

- \(^3\)H binding energy: 8.482 MeV (experimental); exact Reid 93 calculation gives 7.63 MeV; the 8\(\hbar\Omega\) calculation with \(\hbar\Omega=19.2\) MeV and \(k_Q=0.6\) reproduces the exact result reasonably.
- \(^4\)He: 8\(\hbar\Omega\) calculation for positive-parity states and 7\(\hbar\Omega\) for negative-parity states with \(\hbar\Omega=18.4\) MeV; the modified Hamiltonian (\(k_Q=0.6\)) gives very reasonable agreement with experimental results, while the unmodified Hamiltonian overbinds.
- \(^5\)He: 7\(\hbar\Omega\) (\(\pi=+\)) and 6\(\hbar\Omega\) (\(\pi=-\)) calculations with \(\hbar\Omega=17.8\) MeV; the \(\frac{3}{2}^+_2\) state, dominated by the \((0s)^3(0p)^2\) configuration, is identified as a candidate for the experimental \(\frac{3}{2}^+\) state.
- \(^6\)Li: 6\(\hbar\Omega\) calculation for positive-parity states with \(\hbar\Omega=17.2\) MeV; the calculation with \(k_Q=0.6\) gives very reasonable agreement with experiment for all properties shown.
- \(^6\)He: results are presented in Table 5 (not shown in truncated text); energies are the same as for \(^6\)Li due to isospin symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the no-core shell-model approach previously developed by Zheng et al. (1994, 1995) and others, which used starting-energy-dependent G-matrix methods or approximate folded-diagram treatments. The paper introduces a starting-energy-independent effective interaction via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation (Lee and Suzuki, 1980) and addresses the overbinding problem observed in earlier calculations (Zheng et al., 1995) by modifying the auxiliary potential in the Q-space. The multi-valued effective interaction approach (Zheng et al., 1995) is employed to partially account for many-body effects. The results enable more reliable no-core shell-model calculations for light nuclei and provide a framework for studying the dependence of nuclear states on model-space size and harmonic-oscillator frequency, particularly for nuclei like \(^5\)He where the nature of excited states remains controversial (Csótó and Lovas, 1996).
