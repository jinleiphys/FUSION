# Nuclear isotope shifts within the local energy-density functional approach

> The foundation of the local energy-density functional method to describe the nuclear ground-state properties is given. The method is used to investigate differential observables such as the odd-even mass differences and odd-even effects in charge radii. For a few isotope chains of spherical nuclei, the calculations are performed with an exact treatment of the Gor'kov equations in the coordinate-space representation. A zero-range cutoff density-dependent pairing interaction with a density-gradient term is used. The evolution of charge radii and nucleon separation energies is reproduced reasonably well including kinks at magic neutron numbers and sizes of staggering. It is shown that the density-dependent pairing may also induce sizeable staggering and kinks in the evolution of the mean energies of multipole excitations. The results are compared with the conventional mean field Skyrme-HFB and relativistic Hartree-BCS calculations. With the formulated approach, an extrapolation from the pairing properties of finite nuclei to pairing in infinite matter is considered, and the dilute limit near the critical point, at which the regime changes from weak to strong pairing, is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a local energy-density functional (EDF) approach with a zero-range, density-dependent pairing interaction containing a density-gradient term can reproduce the fine structure of charge radii and nucleon separation energies across isotope chains, including kinks at magic neutron numbers and odd-even staggering, provided the Gor'kov equations are solved exactly in coordinate space with an energy cutoff of 40 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct a local energy-density functional for spherical superfluid nuclei, separating it into normal and anomalous (pairing) parts. The normal part includes volume, surface, Coulomb, and spin-orbit terms with no momentum dependence (effective mass equals bare mass), while the pairing part uses a zero-range cutoff density-dependent interaction with a density-gradient term. The Gor'kov equations are solved exactly in coordinate-space representation for spherical nuclei, and the generalized Green's function is integrated up to an energy cutoff of 40 MeV to obtain normal and anomalous densities self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- Energy cutoff: \(\epsilon_{\rm c} = 40\) MeV, chosen to be larger than the Fermi energy in symmetric nuclear matter \(\epsilon_{0\rm F} \approx 37\) MeV
- Equilibrium density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Yukawa range parameter for surface term: \(R = 0.35\) fm
- Pairing energy per particle in infinite matter: \(E_{\rm pair}/N = -\frac{3}{8} \frac{\Delta^2(\vec p_{\rm F})}{\epsilon_{\rm F}}\)
- The pairing gap vanishes at or slightly above the equilibrium density \(\approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent finite Fermi systems (FFS) theory and the EDF method previously applied to magic nuclei (Fayans et al., 1994) to open-shell spherical nuclei with pairing. It contrasts with conventional Skyrme-HFB and relativistic Hartree-BCS calculations by using a density-dependent pairing interaction that is not directly connected to the particle-hole channel interaction, following the philosophy of Migdal's theory of finite Fermi systems. The approach enables extrapolation from pairing properties of finite nuclei to pairing in infinite nuclear matter, relevant for understanding pairing phenomena in neutron stars, and allows study of the dilute limit near the critical point where the regime changes from weak to strong pairing.
