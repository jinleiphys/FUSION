# An Analytic Yang-Mills Vacuum Calculation in $3+1d$

> I present a novel analytic framework for $SU(N)$ Yang-Mills theory in the four-dimensional continuum. Background and effective field theory techniques are used to include non-perturbative contributions from cubic and quartic interactions. This approach is inspired by Savvidy who claims first-order contributions from quartic interactions stabilize IR divergence found at one-loop order, making possible IR finite Yang-Mills calculations. I assess the validity of this claim and discuss the implications of my findings.

# An Analytic Yang-Mills Vacuum Calculation in \(3+1d\)

## Key claim

The paper claims that including first-order contributions from quartic interactions via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation can regulate the infrared divergence found in one-loop Yang-Mills calculations, but only under the condition that cubic interactions do not contribute at first order and that the auxiliary field saddle point is constrained to the positive real axis. The author assesses Savvidy's claim that quartic interactions stabilize the IR divergence and finds that the resulting effective theory yields a finite vacuum with a stable minimum.

## Method

The author uses the background field method with a covariantly constant self-dual field strength tensor for SU(N) Yang-Mills theory in Euclidean space. A modified Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation introduces an auxiliary field \(\Delta\) that captures quartic interactions at first order, while cubic interactions are discarded as they vanish under the gauge fixing condition. The effective action is computed using zeta function regularization and heat kernel methods, with the running coupling renormalized to absorb UV-divergent and scale-dependent terms. Gap equations for the background field \(B\) and the auxiliary field \(\Delta\) are solved via saddle point approximation in the large \(\beta V\) limit.

## Key numbers

- The saddle point value of the auxiliary field is \(\bar{\Delta} = 2.82898\), which is identical for all color indices.
- The non-trivial saddle point for the background field is \(\bar{B} \to 2.25885 \Lambda_{\text{YM}}^2 / \lambda^a\), with a trivial unstable solution at \(\bar{B} \to 0\).
- The vacuum free energy density summed over SU(3) color indices is \(\ln Z / (\beta V) = 8 \times 0.0592377 \Lambda_{\text{YM}}^4\).
- The effective scalar glueball mass per component is \(m^a_{\text{Glue}} = 2.52789 \Lambda_{\text{YM}}\).
- For a Landau pole scale \(\Lambda_{\text{YM}}\) in the range 400–700 MeV, the glueball mass range is approximately 1011 to 1770 MeV.
- The beta function at first loop order is \(dg(\mu)/d\ln(\mu) = -11 g^3(\mu) / (48\pi^2)\), matching the perturbative result times a factor of 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the line of non-perturbative Yang-Mills calculations initiated by Savvidy (1977, 2023), who first identified the IR divergence in one-loop effective theories and proposed that quartic interactions could stabilize it. The author builds on prior background field analyses by Leutwyler (1981), Nielsen (1978), and Yildiz (1980), who studied vacuum instabilities from zero modes. The Hubbard-Stratonovich technique is similar to large-N methods used by Romatschke (2022, 2024) and Grable (2022). The resulting glueball mass range agrees with quenched lattice QCD predictions from Morningstar (1999), Ochs (2013), and Mathieu (2009). The work enables future extensions to finite temperature, chemical potential, and confinement parameters such as the Wilson loop.
