# Microscopically-constrained Fock energy density functionals from chiral effective field theory. I. Two-nucleon interactions

> The density matrix expansion (DME) of Negele and Vautherin is a convenient tool to map finite-range physics associated with vacuum two- and three-nucleon interactions into the form of a Skyme-like energy density functional (EDF) with density-dependent couplings. In this work, we apply the improved formulation of the DME proposed recently in arXiv:0910.4979 by Gebremariam {\it et al.} to the non-local Fock energy obtained from chiral effective field theory (EFT) two-nucleon (NN) interactions at next-to-next-to-leading-order (N$^2$LO). The structure of the chiral interactions is such that each coupling in the DME Fock functional can be decomposed into a cutoff-dependent coupling {\it constant} arising from zero-range contact interactions and a cutoff-independent coupling {\it function} of the density arising from the universal long-range pion exchanges. This motivates a new microscopically-guided Skyrme phenomenology where the density-dependent couplings associated with the underlying pion-exchange interactions are added to standard empirical Skyrme functionals, and the density-independent Skyrme parameters subsequently refit to data. A Mathematica notebook containing the novel density-dependent couplings is provided.

## Key claim

The authors apply an improved density matrix expansion (DME) to the non-local Fock energy from chiral effective field theory two-nucleon interactions at next-to-next-to-leading-order (N²LO), showing that each coupling in the resulting functional decomposes into a cutoff-dependent constant from zero-range contact interactions and a cutoff-independent density-dependent function from long-range pion exchanges. This decomposition motivates a new microscopically-guided Skyrme phenomenology where only the density-dependent couplings from pion exchanges are added to standard empirical Skyrme functionals, while the density-independent Skyrme parameters are subsequently refit to data, rather than constructing a fully ab initio functional.

## Method

The authors start from the Hartree-Fock energy expression for even-even nuclei using chiral EFT NN interactions at N²LO, separating the direct (Hartree) and exchange (Fock) contributions. They apply the improved phase-space-averaging density matrix expansion (PSA-DME) to approximate the spatially non-local Fock energy as a generalized Skyrme functional with density-dependent couplings, using the local Fermi momentum as the expansion scale. The DME is truncated at second order for the scalar part of the one-body density matrix and first order for the vector part, with the vector Π-functions modified to account for anisotropy of the local momentum distribution in finite Fermi systems.

## Key numbers

- The chiral EFT NN interaction is taken at N²LO, with the UV cutoff Λ between approximately 2.5 and 3.0 fm⁻¹ in most implementations.
- The DME truncation uses n_max = 2 for the scalar part and m_max = 1 for the vector part of the one-body density matrix.
- The local Fermi momentum is defined as k_F(R) = (3π²ρ₀(R)/2)^(1/3), relating the expansion scale to the isoscalar density.
- The PSA-DME vector Π-function uses a modified Fermi momentum given by k̃_F(R) = ((2 + 2P₂(R))/(2 - P₂(R)))^(1/3) k_F(R), where P₂(R) is the quadrupole anisotropy of the local momentum distribution.

## Context

This work extends the density matrix expansion approach originally formulated by Negele and Vautherin (1972, 1975) to incorporate chiral EFT interactions, building on the improved PSA-DME formulation by Gebremariam et al. (2009). The approach complements empirical efforts to improve Skyrme functionals (Lesinski 2006, Carlsson 2009, Goriely 2009) and previous microscopic EDF constructions from many-body perturbation theory (Bogner 2008, Duguet 2009). The resulting density-dependent couplings from pion exchanges can be added to existing empirical Skyrme functionals, enabling systematic improvement of isovector structure and predictive power away from known data, while a companion paper will extend the treatment to three-nucleon interactions at the same chiral order.
