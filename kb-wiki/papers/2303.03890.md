# Improved Superscaling in Quasielastic Electron Scattering with Relativistic Effective Mass

> Superscaling in electron scattering from nuclei is re-examined paying special attention to the definition of the averaged single-nucleon responses. The validity of the extrapolation of nucleon responses in the Fermi gas has been examined, which previously lacked a theoretical foundation. To address this issue, we introduce new averaged responses with a momentum distribution smeared around the Fermi surface, allowing for momenta above the Fermi momentum. This approach solves the problem of negativity in the extrapolation away from the scaling region and, at the same time, validates its use in the scaling analysis. This work has important implications for the interpretation of scaling data and contributes to the development of a more complete understanding of the scaling approach.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the traditional extrapolation of single-nucleon responses from the relativistic Fermi gas to kinematic regions outside the Pauli-blocking allowed range (|ψ*| > 1) can produce unphysical negative responses, but this extrapolation remains valid within the quasielastic scaling region (−2 < ψ* < 2) because it yields results nearly identical to those obtained from a new, theoretically justified averaging over a smeared momentum distribution.

## Method

The authors re-examine the averaged single-nucleon responses in the superscaling formalism by replacing the sharp Fermi gas momentum distribution with a Fermi-type distribution n(h) = a/[1 + e^{(h−k_F)/b}] that allows momenta above the Fermi momentum. They compute the longitudinal and transverse response functions for protons and neutrons in ¹²C using both the traditional extrapolated Fermi gas method and the new smeared-distribution average, with parameters k_F = 225 MeV/c and effective mass m_N* = 0.8 m_N fitted to quasielastic data. The nuclear responses are then obtained by multiplying the averaged single-nucleon responses by the phenomenological scaling function f*_QE(ψ*) extracted from experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momentum k_F = 225 MeV/c used for ¹²C calculations
- Effective mass m_N* = 0.8 m_N used for ¹²C calculations
- Smearing parameter b = 50 MeV/c used for the Fermi-type momentum distribution
- The extrapolated transverse response becomes negative for ψ* > 4 at q = 500 MeV/c, ψ* > 5 at q = 700 MeV/c, and ψ* > 7 at q = 1000 MeV/c
- The extrapolated longitudinal neutron response becomes negative for ψ* > 2

## Context

This work extends the SuSA (superscaling approach) and SuSAM* (superscaling approach with relativistic effective mass) models developed by Donnelly (1999), Amaro (2004, 2018), and Martinez-Consentino (2017) by providing a theoretical foundation for the previously ad-hoc extrapolation of single-nucleon responses. The new averaging method with a smeared momentum distribution resolves the negativity problem identified in the extrapolation and validates the use of the traditional approach within the scaling region. This enables more robust applications of superscaling models to neutrino scattering and improves the interpretation of quasielastic electron scattering data from nuclei like ¹²C.
