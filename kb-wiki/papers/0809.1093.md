# Reparameterization Invariant Collinear Operators

> In constructing collinear operators, which describe the production of energetic jets or energetic hadrons, important constraints are provided by reparameterization invariance (RPI). RPI encodes Lorentz invariance in a power expansion about a collinear direction, and connects the Wilson coefficients of operators at different orders in this expansion to all orders in alphas. We construct reparameterization invariant collinear objects. The expansion of operators built from these objects provides an efficient way of deriving RPI relations and finding a minimal basis of operators, particularly when one has an observable with multiple collinear directions and/or soft particles. Complete basis of operators are constructed for pure glue currents at twist-4, and for operators involving multiple collinear directions, including those appearing in e+e- -> 3 jets, and for pp-> 2 jets initiated via gluon-fusion.

## Key claim

Reparameterization invariance (RPI) encodes Lorentz invariance in a power expansion about a collinear direction and connects Wilson coefficients of operators at different orders in this expansion to all orders in $\alpha_s$, but the paper's construction of RPI-invariant collinear objects provides an efficient way to derive these relations and find a minimal operator basis without first constructing a complete basis and then deriving connections order by order, which becomes cumbersome at higher orders or with multiple directions.

## Method

The authors construct a set of reparameterization-invariant and gauge-invariant collinear objects for SCET, including a quark field operator $\Psi_n$, a gluon field strength operator ${\cal G}_n^{\mu\nu}$, and $\delta$-function operators that pick out large momenta of collinear fields, with gauge invariance ensured by a "reparameterization invariant Wilson line" operator ${\cal W}_n$. These objects are then used to build operators that automatically encode RPI relations at any order in the power expansion; when expanded, they generate a series of operators with connected Wilson coefficients. The procedure is applied to construct complete bases of operators for pure glue currents at twist-4, for operators with multiple collinear directions including those in $e^+e^-\to 3$ jets, and for $pp\to 2$ jets initiated via gluon-fusion.

## Key numbers

- The collinear momentum scaling is $(n\cdot p, \bar n\cdot p, p_{n\perp}) \sim Q(\lambda^2, 1, \lambda)$ where $\lambda \ll 1$ and $Q^2$ is a large perturbative momentum scale (the jet-energy).
- The five infinitesimal RPI parameters are $\{\Delta^\bot_\mu, \epsilon^\bot_\mu, \alpha\}$ with power counting $\{\Delta^\bot_\mu, \epsilon^\bot_\mu, \alpha\}\sim \{\lambda^1,\lambda^0,\lambda^0\}$.
- For SCETa, the power counting for fields includes: $\xi_n \sim \lambda$, $(n\cdot A_n,\bar n\cdot A_n,A_n^{\perp}) \sim (\lambda^2, 1,\lambda)$, $q_{us} \sim h_v^{us} \sim \lambda^3$, $A_{us} \sim \lambda^2$, $i\partial^\mu \sim \lambda^2$, $(in\cdot\partial, \bar n\cdot \mathcal{P}, \mathcal{P}_{n\perp}) \sim (\lambda^2, 1,\lambda)$, $W_n \sim Y_n \sim \lambda^0$.
- For SCETb, the power counting includes: $\xi_n \sim \eta$, $(n\cdot A_n,\bar n\cdot A_n,A_n^{\perp}) \sim (\eta^2, 1,\eta)$, $q_s \sim h_v^s \sim \eta^{3/2}$, $A_s \sim \eta$, $i\partial_s^\mu \sim \eta$, $(in\cdot\partial, \bar n\cdot \mathcal{P}, \mathcal{P}_{n\perp}) \sim (\eta^2, 1,\eta)$, $W_n \sim S_n \sim \eta^0$.

## Context

This work extends the reparameterization invariance formalism from HQET (Luke 1992) to SCET, building on the complete set of RPI transformations for collinear fields first discussed by Chay (2002) and generalized by Manohar (2002). The paper provides a systematic method for constructing RPI-invariant operators that is more efficient than previous approaches, which required constructing a full operator basis first and then deriving RPI relations order by order (as done for heavy-to-light currents by Chay 2002, Beneke 2002, Pirjol 2002, and Arnesen 2005, and for light-light SCET currents by Hardmeier 2003). The construction enables future work on factorization theorems for processes with multiple jets and hadrons, including power corrections at higher orders in the twist or $\lambda$ expansion.
