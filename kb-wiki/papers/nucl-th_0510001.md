# Theoretical Description of K-Isomers

> A proper treatment of K-mixing is the key to understanding K-isomers. Here, we present a method based on the projected shell model. This method differs from the usual description of multi-quasiparticle states by introducing a transformation to the laboratory frame and a subsequent configuration mixing in that frame. It allows a quantitative study on the degree of K-violation through direct calculations of electromagnetic transitions.

## Key claim
The projected shell model (PSM) can provide a quantitative description of K-mixing in multi-quasiparticle states by transforming basis states to the laboratory frame and mixing them there, but the method has so far only been applied to a few selected nuclei and requires further extensions to handle configurations with up to 10-quasiparticles and high-order multipole transitions.

## Method
The PSM constructs a deformed basis from Nilsson single-particle states with BCS pairing, then builds multi-quasiparticle configurations (2-qp, 4-qp, etc.) for even-even, odd-odd, and odd-mass nuclei. Angular momentum projection restores rotational symmetry, transforming these body-fixed states to the laboratory frame, and a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian (including quadrupole pairing with strength proportional to monopole pairing by a factor of 0.16) is diagonalized in the projected basis to produce mixed wavefunctions that encode the degree of K-violation.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameters for ¹⁷⁸Hf calculation: ε₂ = 0.251, ε₄ = 0.056
- The 2.45 MeV, 31-year isomer in ¹⁷⁸Hf is identified as a candidate for triggered decay via external electromagnetic radiation
- Quadrupole pairing strength G_Q is set to 0.16 times the monopole pairing strength G_M
- The ¹⁷⁸Hf calculation reproduces known data for most states, except the first 8⁻ bandhead and the 14⁻ bandhead are calculated too low

## Context
This work extends the projected shell model approach of Sun (2005) and earlier PSM formulations to the specific problem of K-isomers, which have been studied experimentally by Walker (2001) and theoretically by Jain (1995), Soloviev, Zeng (2002), and Xu (2004). The method enables direct calculation of electromagnetic transition probabilities between high-K isomeric states and low-lying states, with planned applications to ¹⁷⁸Hf, ¹³⁴Nd, and ¹⁷⁴Hf. Future work requires enlarging the configuration space beyond the current 4-qp limit, adding B(E3) and higher multipole transition calculations, and possibly including octupole-octupole interactions.
