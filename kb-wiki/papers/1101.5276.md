# "Weak Quantum Chaos" and its resistor network modeling

> Weakly chaotic or weakly interacting systems have a wide regime where the common random matrix theory modeling does not apply. As an example we consider cold atoms in a nearly integrable optical billiard with displaceable wall ("piston"). The motion is completely chaotic but with small Lyapunov exponent. The Hamiltonian matrix does not look like one taken from a Gaussian ensemble, but rather it is very sparse and textured. This can be characterized by parameters $s$ and $g$ that reflect the percentage of large elements, and their connectivity, respectively. For $g$ we use a resistor network calculation that has a direct relation to the semi-linear response characteristics of the system, hence leading to a novel prediction regarding the rate of heating of cold atoms in optical billiards with vibrating walls.

## Key claim

Weakly chaotic systems, such as cold atoms in a nearly integrable optical billiard with a displaceable wall ("piston"), have a Hamiltonian matrix that is very sparse and textured rather than resembling a Gaussian random matrix, leading to a suppression of the energy absorption rate that can be characterized by a resistor network parameter g_s.

## Method

The authors model a two-dimensional optical billiard with a fixed deformed wall (characterized by deformation parameter u = L/R) and a movable flat piston wall. They numerically diagonalize the Hamiltonian using the boundary element method to obtain eigenstates and eigenenergies within an energy window defined by the scaled Planck constant hbar = lambda_E/L. They then compute the perturbation matrix F_nm due to piston displacement, characterize its sparsity via parameters s (participation number ratio) and g_s (resistor network measure), and analyze the classical power spectrum of the force fluctuations to obtain the classical absorption coefficient g_c.

## Key numbers

- The ballistic time is t_L = L_x/v_E, where v_E = (2E/m)^(1/2) is the particle velocity
- The Lyapunov time is t_R = R/v_E, with the dimensionless deformation parameter u = L/R = t_L/t_R
- The mean level spacing is Delta_0 = 2pi/(m L_x L_y)
- For large frequencies, the classical power spectrum approaches C_infinity = (8/(3pi)) (m^2 v_E^3 / L_x)
- For small deformation, the zero-frequency power spectrum is enhanced by factor 1/u: tilde{C}(omega=0) approx (1/u) C_infinity
- For a strictly uniform matrix g_s = s = 1, for a Gaussian matrix s = 1/3 and g_s ~ 1, while for sparse matrix s, g_s << 1

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum chaos in billiard systems beyond the standard random matrix theory (RMT) framework, which applies to strongly chaotic systems with short correlation times. The authors build on prior studies of nuclear friction (Wall formula) and optical billiards, and specifically address the regime of weak quantum chaos (WQC) where the correlation time t_R is much larger than the ballistic time t_L. The resistor network approach connects to semi-linear response theory (SLRT) developed in earlier work by the same group, and enables predictions for the energy absorption rate of cold atoms in optical billiards with vibrating walls, which differs from both the classical result and the standard RMT prediction.
