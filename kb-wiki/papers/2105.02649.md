# Nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double-$β$ decay in the triaxial projected shell model

> The nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double-$β$ decay for nuclei $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd are studied within the triaxial projected shell model, which incorporates simultaneously the triaxial deformation and quasiparticle configuration mixing. The low-lying spectra and the $B(E2:0^+\rightarrow2^+)$ values are reproduced well. The effects of the quasiparticles configuration mixing, the triaxial deformation, and the closure approximation on the nuclear matrix elements are studied in detail. For nuclei $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd, the nuclear matrix elements are respectively reduced by the quasiparticle configuration mixing by 6%, 7%, 2%, 3%, and 4%, and enhanced by the odd-odd intermediate states by 7%, 4%, 11%, 20%, and 14%. Varying the triaxial deformation $γ$ from $0^\circ$ to $60^\circ$ for the mother and daughter nuclei, the nuclear matrix elements change by 41%, 17%, 68%, 14%, and 511% respectively for $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd, which indicates the importance of treating the triaxial deformation consistently in calculating the nuclear matrix elements.

# Nuclear matrix elements of neutrinoless double-$\beta$ decay in the triaxial projected shell model

## Key claim

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) reproduces low-lying spectra and $B(E2)$ values for five $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay candidates, but the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) depend strongly on triaxial deformation: varying $\gamma$ from $0^\circ$ to $60^\circ$ changes NMEs by 14% to 511% across the studied nuclei, and the commonly used closure approximation systematically underestimates NMEs by 4% to 20% compared to explicit inclusion of odd-odd intermediate states.

## Method

The TPSM is applied to calculate NMEs for $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd using a Hamiltonian with spherical single-particle terms, quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and monopole plus quadrupole pairing. The intrinsic vacuum is obtained from a Nilsson mean field, and two-quasiparticle configurations are constructed for both even-even and odd-odd nuclei; angular momentum projection restores rotational symmetry, and the Hill-Wheeler equation is solved for the projected wavefunctions. The NME is evaluated using the nonrelativistic reduction of the decay operator with three harmonic-oscillator major shells, and the Pfaffian algorithm handles the rotational overlaps between mother and daughter nuclei.

## Key numbers

- For $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd, quasiparticle configuration mixing reduces NMEs by 6%, 7%, 2%, 3%, and 4%, respectively.
- Odd-odd intermediate states enhance NMEs by 7%, 4%, 11%, 20%, and 14% for $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd, respectively, compared to the closure approximation.
- Varying triaxial deformation $\gamma$ from $0^\circ$ to $60^\circ$ changes NMEs by 41%, 17%, 68%, 14%, and 511% for $^{76}$Ge, $^{82}$Se, $^{100}$Mo, $^{130}$Te, and $^{150}$Nd, respectively.
- The TPSM NME values are: $^{76}$Ge = 3.17, $^{82}$Se = 2.59, $^{100}$Mo = 3.92, $^{130}$Te = 2.92, $^{150}$Nd = 3.29 (with axial deformation parameters).
- For $^{76}$Ge and $^{76}$Se with triaxial deformation $\gamma = 30^\circ$ and $40^\circ$, the NME becomes 3.72, which is 17% larger than the axial value of 3.17.
- Gamow-Teller transitions contribute approximately 85% of the total NME, Fermi transitions about 14%, and Tensor transitions about 1%.
- The $B(E2: 0^+ \rightarrow 2^+)$ values from TPSM range from 0.199 $e^2$b$^2$ ($^{82}$Se) to 3.018 $e^2$b$^2$ ($^{150}$Nd), compared to experimental values from 0.180 $e^2$b$^2$ to 2.707 $e^2$b$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) approach of Chaturvedi (2008) and Rath (2010) by incorporating triaxial deformation and quasiparticle configuration mixing beyond the HFB vacuum, enabled by the Pfaffian algorithm of Bertsch (2012) and Hu (2014). The TPSM results are compared with NMEs from nonrelativistic DFT (Vaquero 2013), relativistic DFT (Song 2017), the interacting boson model (Barea 2015), QRPA (Hyvarinen 2015), and the shell model (Horoi 2016), showing that TPSM values lie between the smaller shell-model/PHFB values and the larger DFT values. The systematic enhancement from odd-odd intermediate states (4% to 20%) is consistent with the approximately 10% enhancement found in shell-model calculations for $^{48}$Ca by Senkov (2013). The strong sensitivity to triaxial deformation, especially the 511% variation for $^{150}$Nd, demonstrates that consistent treatment of triaxiality is essential for reliable NME predictions, and the finding that $^{76}$Ge and $^{76}$Se have triaxially deformed $0^+$ states despite axial predictions from relativistic DFT (Zhao 2010, 2015; Wang 2017, 2018) motivates further work including four-quasiparticle configurations.
