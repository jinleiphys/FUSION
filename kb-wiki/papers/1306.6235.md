# Universality of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations and nucleon momentum distributions

> By analyzing recent microscopic many-body calculations of few-nucleon systems and complex nuclei performed by different groups in terms of realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions, it is shown that NN short-range correlations (SRCs) have a universal character, in that the correlation hole that they produce in nuclei appears to be almost A-independent and similar to the correlation hole in the deuteron. The correlation hole creates high-momentum components, missing in a mean-field (MF) description and exhibiting several scaling properties and a peculiar spin-isospin structure. In particular, the momentum distribution of a pair of nucleons in spin-isospin state $(ST)=(10)$, depending upon the pair relative ($k_{rel}$) and center-of-mass (c.m.) ($K_{c.m.}$) momenta, as well as upon the angle $Θ$ between them, exhibits a remarkable property: in the region $k_{rel}\gtrsim 2\,fm^{-1}$ and $K_{c.m.}\lesssim 1\,fm^{-1} $, the relative and c.m. motions are decoupled and the two-nucleon momentum distribution factorizes into the deuteron momentum distribution and an A-dependent momentum distribution describing the c.m. motion of the pair in the medium. The impact of these and other properties of one- and two-nucleon momentum distributions on various nuclear phenomena, on ab initio calculations in terms of low-momentum interactions, as well as on ongoing experimental investigations of SRCs, are briefly commented.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations (SRCs) have a universal character across nuclei, meaning the correlation hole in coordinate space is nearly independent of mass number A and closely resembles that of the deuteron, but this universality is strictly established only for the region where the pair relative momentum \(k_{rel} \gtrsim 2\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) and center-of-mass momentum \(K_{c.m.} \lesssim 1\,\text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Method

The authors analyzed recent microscopic many-body calculations of few-nucleon systems and complex nuclei performed by different groups using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions such as AV18 and AV8'. They examined one- and two-body densities and momentum distributions from ab initio solutions (e.g., Green Function Monte Carlo, Variational Monte Carlo, cluster expansion methods) to extract the spin-isospin structure and scaling properties of SRCs. The two-nucleon momentum distribution for pairs in spin-isospin state (ST)=(10) was studied as a function of relative momentum \(k_{rel}\), center-of-mass momentum \(K_{c.m.}\), and the angle \(\Theta\) between them.

## Key numbers

- The correlation hole in the relative two-nucleon density appears at distances \(r \lesssim 1.5\,\text{fm}\), with a sharp damping relative to mean-field densities.
- The factorization property of the two-nucleon momentum distribution holds for \(k_{rel} \gtrsim 2\,\text{fm}^{-1}\) and \(K_{c.m.} \lesssim 1\,\text{fm}^{-1}\), where relative and center-of-mass motions decouple.
- The number of NN pairs in different spin-isospin states (ST) is tabulated for various nuclei, showing deviations from the shell model due to SRCs (e.g., creation of (00) and (11) states in \(A \leq 4\) nuclei).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of SRCs by Jastrow (1955) and Gottfried (1963) and builds on modern ab initio calculations by groups such as Pieper (GFMC) and Forest (1996) using realistic interactions like AV18 and AV8'. It enables a unified understanding of high-momentum components in nuclei, which is relevant for interpreting ongoing experimental investigations of SRCs and for improving ab initio calculations with low-momentum interactions. The universality claim supports the use of the deuteron as a reference for SRCs in heavier nuclei, simplifying the analysis of nuclear phenomena at short distances.
