# Maximally local two-nucleon interactions at N$^3$LO in $Δ$-less chiral effective field theory

> We present new maximally local two-nucleon interactions derived in $Δ$-less chiral effective field theory up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N$^3$LO), which include all contact and pion-exchange contributions to the nuclear Hamiltonian up to this order. Our interactions are fit to nucleon-nucleon phase shifts using a Bayesian statistical approach, and explore a wide cutoff range from $0.6-0.9$ fm ($\approx 660 - 440$ MeV). These interactions can be straightforwardly employed in quantum Monte Carlo methods, such as the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo method. Together with local three-nucleon forces, calculations with these new interactions will provide improved benchmarks for the structure of atomic nuclei and serve as crucial input to analyses of astrophysical phenomena of neutron stars, such as binary neutron-star mergers.

## Key claim
The paper presents new maximally local two-nucleon interactions at N$^3$LO in $\Delta$-less chiral EFT that include all contact and pion-exchange contributions, but the authors note that four nonlocal contact operators remain necessary and that Fierz rearrangement freedom is violated when local regulators are applied, inducing regulator artifacts that are expected to be small at the cutoff ranges studied.

## Method
The authors constructed maximally local two-nucleon interactions in $\Delta$-less chiral EFT up to N$^3$LO by selecting a complete set of 21 contact operators (17 local and 4 nonlocal) using Fierz rearrangement freedom and unitary transformations, combined with all local pion-exchange contributions including two-pion exchange with spectral-function regularization at a cutoff of 1 GeV. The low-energy constants were fit to nucleon-nucleon phase shifts using both a Bayesian statistical approach that models EFT truncation uncertainties and a least-squares approach, exploring coordinate-space cutoffs from 0.6 to 0.9 fm (approximately 660 to 440 MeV).

## Key numbers
- Cutoff range explored: $R_0 = 0.6-0.9$ fm (approximately $660-440$ MeV)
- Total of 21 contact operators at N$^3$LO, out of which 4 are nonlocal
- Spectral-function regularization cutoff fixed at $\tilde{\Lambda} = 1$ GeV for two-pion exchange contributions
- The interactions are named N$^3$LO$_{\rm LA}$-09 through N$^3$LO$_{\rm LA}$-06, where the number refers to the coordinate-space cutoff in fm

## Context
This work extends previous local chiral EFT interactions developed up to NNLO by Gezerlis (2013, 2014) and the maximally local approach of Huth (2017), while also building on the $\Delta$-full N$^3$LO interactions of Piarulli (2014, 2017) and the local N$^3$LO interactions of Saha (2022) that neglected nonlocalities. The new interactions are designed for use in quantum Monte Carlo methods such as auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo, and together with forthcoming local three-nucleon forces at N$^3$LO, they will enable improved benchmarks for nuclear structure and provide input for neutron-star astrophysics analyses, including binary neutron-star mergers.
