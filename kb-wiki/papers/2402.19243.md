# Revisiting $O(N)$ $σ$ model at unphysical pion masses and high temperatures

> Roy-equation analyses on lattice data of $ππ$ scattering phase shifts at $m_π=391$MeV reveals that the lowest $f_0$ meson becomes a bound state under this condition. In addition, there is a pair of complex poles below threshold generated by crossing symmetry [X.-H. Cao et al., Phys. Rev. D 108, 034009 (2023)]. We use the $N/D$ method to partially recover crossing symmetry of the $O(N)$ $σ$ model amplitude at leading order of $1/N$ expansion, and qualitatively reproduce the pole structure and pole trajectories with varying pion masses as revealed by Roy-equation analyses. The $σ$ pole trajectory with varying temperature is also discussed and found to be similar to its properties when varying $m_π$. As the temperature increases, the complex $σ$ poles firstly move from the second Riemann sheet to the real axis becoming two virtual state poles, and then one virtual state pole moves to the first sheet turning into a bound state pole and finally tends to the pion pole position at high temperature which is as expected from the chiral symmetry restoration. Our results provide further evidences that the lowest $f_0$ state extracted from experiments and lattice data plays the role of $σ$ meson in the spontaneous breaking of chiral symmetry. Finally, we also briefly discuss the problems of the effective potential in the situation when $m_π$ and temperature get large.

## Key claim
Using the N/D method to partially restore crossing symmetry in the O(N) sigma model at leading order of 1/N expansion, the authors qualitatively reproduce the pole structure and pole trajectories of the sigma meson with varying pion masses as revealed by Roy-equation analyses of lattice data, though the match is qualitative rather than quantitative and requires parameter tuning.

## Method
The authors start from the O(N) linear sigma model at leading order in 1/N expansion, which breaks crossing symmetry because only the s-channel contributes to the IJ=00 partial wave. They then apply the N/D method with twice-subtracted dispersion relations to incorporate the left-hand cut generated by the sigma bound state in crossed t- and u-channels, thereby partially restoring crossing symmetry while preserving exact partial wave unitarity. The subtraction constants are fixed by requiring the amplitude to match the leading order result at threshold and the sigma bound state pole position to remain consistent with the crossed-channel left-hand cut. The pole trajectories are then studied by varying the pion mass and temperature.

## Key numbers
- At m_pi = 391 MeV, the leading order O(N) model gives a bound state pole at sqrt(s) = 780 MeV and a virtual state pole at sqrt(s) = 489 MeV
- At m_pi = 391 MeV, the N/D modified O(N) model gives a bound state pole at sqrt(s) = 658 MeV and a subthreshold pole at sqrt(s) = 466 - i 77 MeV
- The critical pion mass at which the sigma pole reaches threshold in the leading order O(N) model is m_c = 337 MeV
- The intrinsic scale M is set to 550 MeV for the leading order calculation and 1.5 GeV for the N/D modified calculation, with the latter giving m_c = 214 MeV
- At physical pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV, the leading order O(N) model gives a sigma pole at sqrt(s) = 356 - i 148 MeV

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Cao et al. (2023), who performed Roy-equation analyses on lattice pi-pi scattering phase shifts at m_pi = 391 MeV and found a bound state f0 meson with a pair of complex subthreshold poles generated by crossing symmetry. It also relates to lattice results from Briceno et al. (2016) and the HadSpec collaboration (Rodas et al., 2023), which used K-matrix parametrizations to extract sigma poles at various unphysical pion masses. The authors aim to demonstrate that the linear sigma model, despite not being QCD, provides a qualitative description of low-energy QCD in the IJ=00 channel and supports the interpretation that the f0(500) particle plays the role of the sigma meson in spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. The thermal analysis extends the model to high temperatures, showing chiral symmetry restoration as the sigma pole tends to the pion pole position.
