# Stiff phases in strongly coupled gauge theories with holographic duals

> According to common lore, Equations of State of field theories with gravity duals tend to be soft, with speeds of sound either below or around the conformal value of $v_s=1/\sqrt{3}$. This has important consequences in particular for the physics of compact stars, where the detection of two solar mass neutron stars has been shown to require very stiff equations of state. In this paper, we show that no speed limit exists for holographic models at finite density, explicitly constructing examples where the speed of sound becomes arbitrarily close to that of light. This opens up the possibility of building hybrid stars that contain quark matter obeying a holographic equation of state in their cores.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the common lore that holographic equations of state are universally soft (with speed of sound at or below the conformal value \(v_s = 1/\sqrt{3}\)) is false at finite density; by constructing explicit examples in both top-down and bottom-up holographic models, the authors show the speed of sound can become arbitrarily close to the speed of light, provided the scalar field in the gravity dual possesses self-interactions beyond a simple quadratic potential.

## Method

The authors study charged black hole solutions in five-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell-scalar theories that are holographically dual to strongly coupled gauge theories at finite density and temperature. They consider a top-down model derived from a consistent truncation of type IIB supergravity on \(AdS_5 \times S^5\) (dual to a deformation of \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM with a gaugino mass) and a family of bottom-up models with a canonically normalized scalar and a quartic self-interaction term \(V_4\). For each model, they numerically solve the equations of motion for the metric, gauge field, and scalar field, extract the thermodynamic quantities via holographic renormalization, and compute the speed of sound from the equation of state.

## Key numbers

* The conformal speed of sound is \(v_s = 1/\sqrt{3} \approx 0.577\); the paper constructs models where \(v_s\) becomes arbitrarily close to 1 (the speed of light).
* In the top-down model, the scalar operator has conformal dimension \(\Delta = 3\) and mass \(m^2 L^2 = -3\).
* For bottom-up models, the scalar mass is in the interval \(0 > m^2 L^2 \geq -3\), corresponding to operator dimensions \(4 > \Delta \geq 3\).
* The quartic coupling \(V_4 = -2/3\) is identified as a special value where logarithmic terms in the renormalized expectation values vanish and the conformal anomaly disappears.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous holographic description of dense QCD matter (Hoyos et al., 2016), where a D3-D7 brane setup produced a soft equation of state (\(v_s^2 < 1/3\)) that made hybrid stars unstable. It contradicts the earlier conjecture by Hohler and Stephanov (2009) and Cherman et al. (2009) that any field theory with a gauge/gravity dual has a speed of sound at most the conformal value. The results open the possibility of constructing hybrid neutron stars with quark matter cores described by a stiff holographic equation of state, enabling future work on matching such holographic equations of state to nuclear matter descriptions like those of Hebeler et al. (2013).
