# Total Hadron-Hadron Cross Sections at High Energies

> We calculate total hadron-hadron cross sections at high energies using the Low-Nussinov two-gluon model of the Pomeron. The gluon exchange is represented by a phenomenological potential including screened color-Coulomb, screened confining, and spin-spin interactions. We use bound-state wave functions obtained from a potential model for mesons, and a Gaussian wave function for a proton. We evaluate total cross sections for collisions involving pion, kaon, rho, phi, D, J/psi, psi', Upsilon, Upsilon', and the proton. We find that the total cross sections increase with the square of the sum of the root-mean square radii of the colliding hadrons, but there are variations arising from the spin-spin interaction. We also calculate the total cross sections of a mixed-color charmonium state on a pion and on a proton. The dependence of the total cross section on the size and the color state of the charmonium is investigated.

# Total Hadron-Hadron Cross Sections at High Energies

## Key claim

The total hadron-hadron cross section at high energies can be calculated using the Low-Nussinov two-gluon model of the Pomeron with a phenomenological potential that includes screened color-Coulomb, screened confining, and spin-spin interactions; the cross sections increase with the square of the sum of the root-mean-square radii of the colliding hadrons, but with variations arising from the spin-spin interaction, and the model is calibrated to reproduce experimental pp, πp, and Kp cross sections at √s = 20 GeV before being extended to other systems.

## Method

The authors model Pomeron exchange as two-gluon exchange between constituents of colliding hadrons, representing the gluon exchange by a phenomenological potential containing screened color-Coulomb (Yukawa), screened confining (exponential), and spin-spin interactions. They use bound-state meson wave functions obtained from a non-relativistic quark model with Gaussian basis states, and a simple Gaussian wave function for the proton. The total cross section is obtained from the forward elastic scattering amplitude via the optical theorem, with the screening mass and interaction strength first fitted to reproduce experimental pp, πp, and Kp cross sections at √s = 20 GeV, then applied to other hadron-hadron systems.

## Key numbers

- The model is calibrated to reproduce experimental pp, πp, and Kp total cross sections at √s = 20 GeV.
- Total cross sections are evaluated for collisions involving π, K, ρ, φ, D, J/ψ, ψ′, Υ, Υ′, and the proton.
- The total cross section increases with the square of the sum of the root-mean-square radii of the colliding hadrons.
- For an unscreened linear-confining interaction (μ = 0 in the 6πb₀/(k_T² + μ²)² term), the total cross section becomes singular due to divergence of the k_T integral.
- The spin-spin interaction term in the potential includes a factor (8πα_s)²/(9 m_i m_k m_j m_l) with a Gaussian form factor e^{-k_T²/2d²}.

## Context

This work extends the two-gluon model of the Pomeron originally proposed by Low (1975) and Nussinov (1975), and further developed by Gunion and Soper (1977), Landshoff and Nachtmann (1987), Dolejši and Hüfner (1992), and Wong (1996). It improves upon earlier studies by using meson wave functions from a full potential model calculation rather than simple form factors, and by incorporating a more complete phenomenological potential including screened confining and spin-spin interactions beyond the single screened color-Coulomb potential used previously. The results are relevant for understanding J/ψ absorption in heavy-ion collisions, where distinguishing hadronic absorption from quark-gluon plasma effects requires theoretical cross sections for reactions such as J/ψ + hadron that are not experimentally available.
