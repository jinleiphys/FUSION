# Bottomonium Continuous Production from Unequilibrium Bottom Quarks in Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We employ the Langevin equation and Wigner function to describe the bottom equark dynamical evolutions and their formation into a bound state in the expanding Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP). The additional suppressions from parton inelastic scatterings are supplemented in the regenerated bottomonium. Hot medium modifications on $Υ(1S)$ properties are studied consistently by taking the bottomonium potential to be the color-screened potential from Lattice results, which affects both $Υ(1S)$ regeneration and dissociation rates. Finally, we calculated the $Υ(1S)$ nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}^{\rm rege}$ from bottom quark combination with different diffusion coefficients in Langevin equation, representing different thermalization of bottom quarks. In the central Pb-Pb collisions (b=0) at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, we find a non-negligible $Υ(1S)$ regeneration, and it is small in the minimum bias centrality. The connections between bottomonium regeneration and bottom quark energy loss in the heavy ion collisions are also discussed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that $\Upsilon(1S)$ regeneration from bottom quark combination in central Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV is non-negligible, contributing 10–20% of the inclusive nuclear modification factor, but this contribution is small (∼4%) in minimum bias centrality; the exact magnitude depends on the assumed heavy quark potential (V=U vs V=F) and the diffusion coefficient.

## Method
The authors simulate bottom quark diffusion in an expanding quark-gluon plasma using the Langevin equation, with the drag force determined by the fluctuation-dissipation relation. The probability of $b$ and $\bar b$ quarks combining into $\Upsilon(1S)$ is computed via a Wigner function whose Gaussian width depends on the temperature-dependent mean radius from a Schrödinger equation with a color-screened potential (taken from lattice free energy $F$ or internal energy $U$). After regeneration, the $\Upsilon(1S)$ is evolved with a rate equation including gluon dissociation and quasi-free dissociation, with decay rates depending on the temperature-dependent binding energy. The QGP expansion is modeled with (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics.

## Key numbers
- At $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions, the initial maximum temperature is set to $T_0=510$ MeV, with thermalization time $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c.
- The differential cross section for $b\bar b$ production at central rapidity at 5.02 TeV is extracted as $d\sigma_{pp}^{b\bar b}/dy=47.5\ \mu$b.
- The inclusive $\Upsilon(1S)$ cross section at central rapidity at 5.02 TeV is extracted as $d\sigma_{pp}^{\Upsilon(1S)}/dy=59.8$ nb, giving a hidden-to-open-bottom ratio of $0.13\%$.
- With the internal energy potential (V=U) and diffusion coefficient $D(2\pi T)=4$, the regeneration $R_{AA}^{\rm rege}$ is $0.1$–$0.2$ in most central collisions and ∼$4\%$ at minimum bias centrality ($N_p\approx 200$).
- With the free energy potential (V=F) and $D(2\pi T)=4$, the regeneration $R_{AA}^{\rm rege}$ is around $6\%$ in most central collisions.

## Context
This work extends the Langevin-plus-Wigner approach previously applied to charmonium regeneration by Chen (2016) to the bottomonium system, incorporating hot medium modifications to the $\Upsilon(1S)$ properties via a temperature-dependent potential from lattice results. It builds on transport model calculations by Zhao (2012) and Emerick (2011) that suggested non-negligible bottomonium regeneration at LHC energies, and discusses connections to experimental data from ALICE (Abelev 2014) and CMS (2017). The results enable quantitative comparison of regeneration contributions to the $\Upsilon(1S)$ nuclear modification factor and motivate studies of rapidity dependence as a test of the regeneration mechanism.
