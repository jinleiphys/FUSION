# Realistic Transverse Images of the Proton Charge and Magnetic Densities

> We develop a technique, denoted as the finite radius approximation (FRA), that uses a two-dimensional version of the Shannon-Nyquist sampling theorem to determine transverse densities and their uncertainties from experimental quantities. Uncertainties arising from experimental uncertainties on the form factors and lack of measured data at high $Q^2$ are treated. A key feature of the FRA is that a form factor measured at a given value of $Q^2$ is related to a definite region in coordinate space. An exact relation between the FRA and the use of a Bessel series is derived. The proton Dirac form factor is well enough known such that the transverse charge density is very accurately known except for transverse separations $b$ less than about 0.1 fm. The Pauli form factor is well known to $Q^2$ of about 10 GeV$^2$, and this allows a reasonable, but improvable, determination of the anomalous magnetic moment density.

## Key claim

The paper develops a model-independent technique, the finite radius approximation (FRA), to extract transverse charge and magnetization densities of the proton from experimental form factor data, but the accuracy is limited by experimental uncertainties and the lack of data at high momentum transfer, particularly for the Pauli form factor above 10 GeV².

## Method

The authors apply a two-dimensional version of the Shannon-Nyquist sampling theorem, assuming the transverse density vanishes beyond a finite radius R, to relate form factors measured at discrete Q² values to Bessel series coefficients for the transverse density. They extract the Sachs form factors G_E and G_M from a global fit to world cross section and polarization data up to Q² of about 30 GeV², including two-photon exchange corrections, then compute the Dirac (F₁) and Pauli (F₂) form factors and their uncertainties. The transverse charge density ρ_ch is obtained from the Fourier transform of F₁, while the magnetization density ρ_m is derived from F₂ via a derivative relation.

## Key numbers

- The proton Dirac form factor F₁ is well measured up to Q² = 31 GeV², allowing extraction of ρ_ch with N = 30 terms in the Bessel series, corresponding to R₁ = 3.29 fm.
- The Pauli form factor F₂ is known only up to Q² ≈ 10 GeV², limiting the extraction of ρ₂ to N = 20 terms with R₂ = 3.62 fm; uncertainties on F₂ reach 25% by 13 GeV².
- The transverse charge density ρ_ch is very accurately known except for transverse separations b less than about 0.1 fm.
- The experimental uncertainty in ρ_ch at b = 0 is about 1.5% of the central density value.
- The mean-square transverse radius for the charge density from the dipole model is ⟨b²⟩_D = 0.439 fm², with R = 3.31 fm.
- The electric radius R_E was constrained to 0.878 fm and the magnetic radius R_M to 0.860 fm in the global fit.

## Context

This work extends the transverse density formalism of Miller (2007, 2010) by providing a practical, model-independent extraction method that handles experimental uncertainties and incomplete high-Q² data, in contrast to earlier Fourier transform approaches that suffered from relativistic boost complications. The technique enables realistic uncertainty bands for the proton's transverse charge and magnetization densities, and can be extended to other quantities such as generalized parton distributions and transverse momentum distributions, as noted by Burkert (2008). The method also establishes an exact relation between the FRA and Bessel series expansions, improving upon the traditional Hofstadter interpretation of form factors as Fourier transforms of static densities.
