# Scissors Modes:The first overtone

> Scissors modes were predicted in the framework of the Two-Rotor Model. This model has an intrinsic harmonic spectrum, so that the level above the Scissors Mode, the first overtone, has excitation energy twice that of the Scissors Mode. Since the latter is of the order of 3 MeV in the rare earth region, the energy of the overtone is below threshold for nucleon emission, and its width should remain small enough for the overtone to be observable. We find that $B(E2)\uparrow_{overtone} = {3 /over 64 θ_0^{2}}B(E2)\uparrow_{scissors}$, where $θ_0$ is the zero-point oscillation amplitude, which in the rare earth region is of order $ 10^{-1}$.

# Scissors Modes: The first overtone

## Key claim

The first overtone of the Scissors Mode, predicted within the Two-Rotor Model, should have a substantial E2 excitation amplitude despite the small zero-point oscillation amplitude θ₀ (of order 10⁻¹ in rare earth nuclei), because B(E2)↑_overtone is of zero order in the expansion with respect to θ₀ rather than being suppressed by powers of θ₀ as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors quantized the Two-Rotor Model using a reformulated intrinsic Hamiltonian with a quadratic potential approximation, separating the angular variable θ into two regions (I and II) related by reflection about π/4. They constructed the total wavefunctions for the ground state, Scissors Mode, and first overtone while enforcing r-symmetry (invariance under independent rotations by π around the ξ-axis of the neutron and proton axes), then computed the E2 excitation matrix element from the ground state to the overtone and the M1 decay matrix element from the overtone to the Scissors Mode using the appropriate multipole operators in the intrinsic frame.

## Key numbers

- The Scissors Mode excitation energy is of order 3 MeV in the rare earth region, making the first overtone energy of order 6 MeV, below the nucleon emission threshold.
- The zero-point oscillation amplitude θ₀ in the rare earth region is of order 10⁻¹.
- B(E2)↑_overtone = (3 / (64 θ₀²)) × B(E2)↑_scissors, where θ₀ is the zero-point oscillation amplitude.
- B(M1; overtone → scissors) ≈ (1/4) × B(M1)↑_scissors.
- The intrinsic spectrum follows ε_nK = ω(2n + K + 1), with ω = √(C/ℐ).

## Context

This work extends the Two-Rotor Model originally developed by LoIudice and Palumbo (1979) for predicting Scissors Modes, and addresses the possible occurrence of the first overtone previously considered by Sun (year not specified in text) but not thoroughly investigated. The result is relevant to other electrically charged systems where Scissors Modes have been predicted, including metal clusters (Lipparini and Stringari, year not specified), quantum dots (Serra et al., year not specified), and crystals (Hatada et al., year not specified). The authors suggest that microscopic approaches such as the Random Phase Approximation (Suzuki and Rowe, year not specified), the Interacting Boson Model (Iachello, year not specified), sum rule methods (Lipparini and Stringari, year not specified), and geometrical models (Rohozinski and Greiner, year not specified) should be investigated to confirm the overtone structure found here.
