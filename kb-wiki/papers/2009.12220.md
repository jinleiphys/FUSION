# Parton distributions in radiative corrections to the cross section of electron-proton scattering

> The structure function approach and the parton picture, developed for the theoretical description of the deep inelastic electron-proton scattering, also proved to be very effective for calculation of radiative corrections in Quantum Electrodynamics. We use them to calculate radiative corrections to the cross section of electron-proton scattering due to electron-photon interaction, in the experimental setup with the recoil proton detection, proposed by A.A. Vorobev to measure the proton radius. In the one-loop approximation, explicit expressions for these corrections are obtained for arbitrary momentum transfers. It is shown that, at momentum transfers small compared with the proton mass, various contributions to the corrections mutually cancel each other with power accuracy. In two loops, the corrections are obtained in the leading logarithmic approximation.

# Parton distributions in radiative corrections to the cross section of electron-proton scattering

## Key claim
The radiative corrections to electron-proton scattering in the recoil-proton detection setup proposed by Vorobev exhibit cancellation of the dominant electron-photon interaction contributions at low momentum transfers, with power accuracy rather than merely logarithmic accuracy in the one-loop approximation. The paper refines earlier results from Fadin (2018) and extends them to a wider kinematic range using the structure function and parton distribution approach.

## Method
The authors treat the electron-proton scattering cross section with radiative corrections as an inclusive process, expressing it via the proton current tensor and the deep inelastic scattering tensor for the electron. They calculate the electron structure functions \(F_1(x, Q^2)\) and \(F_2(x, Q^2)\) in the one-loop approximation for arbitrary momentum transfers, and in the two-loop leading logarithmic approximation using the parton evolution equations of Gribov-Lipatov-Altarelli-Parisi type adapted to QED. The radiative correction \(\delta^e\) associated with electron structure is obtained by integrating the differential cross section over the Bjorken variable \(x\) and normalizing to the Born cross section.

## Key numbers
- The experiment aims to measure the proton radius with 0.6 percent precision, requiring 0.2 percent accuracy in the cross section \(d\sigma/(d Q^2)\) measurement.
- The momentum transfer range of interest is \(0.04\;\mathrm{GeV}^2 > Q^2 > 0.001\;\mathrm{GeV}^2\).
- The one-loop correction \(\delta^e_{one-loop}\) at small \(x_-\) (low \(Q^2\)) is given by an expression proportional to \(\alpha/(2\pi)\) times terms of order \(x_-\) and \(x_-^2\), demonstrating power suppression; for example, the leading term is \(\frac{\alpha}{2\pi(1+2\rho x_-)}\big[(-2x_- -x_-^2(1-\ln x_-+\rho(3-2\ln x_-)))\ln(Q^2/m^2) + x_-(2\ln x_- -1) + \dots\big]\).
- The two-loop leading logarithmic correction at small \(x_-\) when electron-positron pair production is allowed is \(\delta^e_{two-loop, LLA} = \left(\frac{\alpha}{2\pi}\right)^2 \frac{L^2}{2}\bigg[ -\frac83 \ln x_- -\frac{44}{9}- \frac{4}{3(1+2\rho x_-)}\bigg]\), where \(L = \ln(Q^2/m^2)\).
- The one-loop vertex correction at \(Q^2 \gg m^2\) is \(\delta_{vertex}^{e} = \frac{\alpha}{\pi}\left[-\left(\ln\frac{Q^2}{m^2} - 1\right) \ln \frac{m^2}{\lambda^2} -\frac{1}{2}\ln^2\frac{Q^2}{m^2} +\frac{3}{2}\ln\frac{Q^2}{m^2} +\frac{\pi^2}{6} -2\right]\).

## Context
This work extends the earlier analysis by Fadin (2018) which first demonstrated the cancellation of infrared and collinear singularities in the recoil-proton detection setup. It provides a systematic calculation using the structure function approach originally developed for deep inelastic scattering by Gribov and Lipatov (1972) and Dokshitzer (1977), and adapted to QED radiative corrections by Kuraev (1985). The results are relevant to the ongoing "proton radius puzzle", the discrepancy between proton radius values from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy (Pohl, 2010; Antognini, 2013) and from electron-proton scattering and hydrogen spectroscopy (Mohr, 2008; Bernauer, 2010). The paper enables precise calculation of radiative corrections for the proposed Vorobev experiment at MAMI, which aims to resolve this puzzle by measuring the recoil proton with high precision.
