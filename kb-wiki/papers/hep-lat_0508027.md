# Two Color QCD beyond the BEC regime

> We present results of simulations of Two Color QCD using two flavors of Wilson quark in the fundamental representation, at non-zero quark chemical potential mu, on an 8^3x16 lattice. Results for the quark number density, quark and gluon energy densities, and superfluid condensate are qualitatively distinct from the behaviour expected on the assumption that the dominant degrees of freedom are tightly bound scalar diquarks which Bose condense; rather the scaling with mu is more suggestive of a Fermi surface disrupted by a Cooper pair condensate. We also present evidence both for screening of the static potential, and color deconfinement, arising solely as a result of a non-zero quark density.

# Two Color QCD beyond the BEC regime

## Key claim
The simulations of Two Color QCD with Wilson fermions at non-zero quark chemical potential yield results qualitatively distinct from the Bose-Einstein Condensate (BEC) behavior predicted by chiral perturbation theory, and instead show scaling consistent with a Fermi surface disrupted by a Cooper pair condensate (BCS-type superfluidity), though the evidence is preliminary and requires further confirmation with smaller diquark source terms.

## Method
The authors performed lattice gauge theory simulations of SU(2) gauge theory with two flavors of Wilson quarks in the fundamental representation on an 8³×16 lattice at β=1.7 and κ=0.1780, using a Hybrid Monte Carlo (HMC) algorithm with a diquark source term ja=0.04 to reduce infrared fluctuations. They measured the quark number density, quark and gluon energy densities, superfluid diquark condensate, Polyakov loop, and static quark potential for chemical potential values μa in the range 0.3 to 0.9, accumulating roughly 300 HMC trajectories of mean length 0.5.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a=0.220 fm, determined from μ=0 string tension measurements.
- Pion mass mπa=0.800(2) and pion-to-rho mass ratio mπ/mρ=0.920(3) at μ=0.
- Diquark source strength ja=0.04 used in all μ≠0 simulations.
- Quark number density at saturation: lim_{μ→∞} nq = 2NfNc = 4 for Nf=2, Nc=2.
- Onset transition predicted by chiral perturbation theory at μoa≈0.4.
- Polyakov loop data suggest deconfining transition may occur at μa≈0.6(1).
- Static potential shows clear evidence for screening for μa≥0.4.
- Quark energy density scaling with μ⁴ shows a significant fraction (O(30%)) from gluons, indicating a strongly-interacting medium.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of Two Color QCD using staggered fermions (AADGG, KSHM) and chiral perturbation theory (KTSVZ), which predicted BEC behavior with nq∝fπ²(μ-μo) and a condensate approaching a constant at large μ. The results contradict those predictions and instead suggest BCS-type behavior with nq∝μ³ and ⟨qq⟩∝Δμ², placing the system in a regime more analogous to high-density QCD. The use of Wilson fermions allows Nf=2 simulations with asymptotic freedom and a controllable continuum limit, unlike earlier work by the Hiroshima group which used an improved gauge action. Future work will focus on the hadron spectrum and gluon propagator, inspired by evidence for vector state mass decrease reported by Hiroshima.
