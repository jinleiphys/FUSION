# Cold Neutron-Deuteron Capture and Wigner-SU(4) Symmetry

> We calculate the cold neutron-deuteron ($nd$) capture cross section, $σ_{nd}$, to next-to-next-to leading order (NNLO) using the model-independent approach of pionless effective field theory (EFT($π\!\!\!/$)). At leading order we find $σ_{nd} = 0.315 \pm 0.217$ mb, while the experimental result is 0.508(15) mb [Jurney, Bendt and Browne in Phys. Rev. C 25, 2810 (1982)] for a laboratory neutron velocity of 2200 m/s. At next-to-leading-order (NLO), we show that $σ_{nd}$ is sensitive to the low energy constant (LEC), $L_1^{(0)}$, of the two-nucleon isovector current appearing at NLO. A fit of $L_1^{(0)}$ at NLO to the triton magnetic moment yields a NLO prediction of $σ_{nd}=0.393 \pm 0.164$ mb, where the error comes from propagating the error from the $L_1^{(0)}$ fit. At next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO), we find that a new three-nucleon magnetic moment counterterm is required for renormalization group invariance of both $σ_{nd}$ and the triton magnetic moment. Fitting the NNLO correction to $L_1^{(0)}$ (denoted $L_1^{(1)}$) to cold neutron-proton capture ($σ_{np}$) yields a NNLO prediction of $σ_{nd}=0.447 \pm 0.130$ mb, where the error comes from propagating the error from the $L_1^{(1)}$ fit. We also study different fittings of $L_1^{(0)}$ and $L_1^{(1)}$ to $σ_{np}$, $σ_{nd}$, and/or the triton magnetic moment. For example, fitting $L_1^{(0)}$ simultaneously to $σ_{np}$, $σ_{nd}$, and the triton magnetic moment at NLO, and fitting $L_1^{(1)}$ simultaneously to $σ_{np}$ and $σ_{nd}$ at NNLO, yields $σ_{nd} = 0.480 \pm 0.114$ mb and $0.511 \pm 0.042$ mb, respectively, where errors are naively estimated from EFT($π\!\!\!/$) power counting. In addition, we discuss how Wigner-SU(4) symmetry may alter the naive EFT($π\!\!\!/$) expansion of $σ_{nd}$.

## Key claim

The cold neutron-deuteron capture cross section is calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)), but the convergence of the EFT(π̸) expansion is slower than naive power counting suggests, with the leading-order result (0.315 ± 0.217 mb) significantly below the experimental value (0.508 ± 0.015 mb), and the NNLO result depends on fitting choices for low-energy constants, with Wigner-SU(4) symmetry providing an explanation for the small one-nucleon current contribution.

## Method

The authors performed a fully perturbative calculation of the cold neutron-deuteron capture process nd → tγ using pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) in the auxiliary field formalism, working to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). They solved integral equations for the two-body triton photo-disintegration amplitude, incorporating one-nucleon, two-nucleon, and three-nucleon magnetic current operators, and fit the low-energy constants L₁⁽⁰⁾ and L₁⁽¹⁾ to combinations of the cold neutron-proton capture cross section, the nd capture cross section, and the triton magnetic moment.

## Key numbers

- Leading-order (LO) prediction for σ_nd: 0.315 ± 0.217 mb (experimental value: 0.508 ± 0.015 mb at neutron laboratory velocity 2200 m/s, from Jurney, Bendt and Browne (1982))
- Next-to-leading-order (NLO) prediction (fitting L₁⁽⁰⁾ to triton magnetic moment): σ_nd = 0.393 ± 0.164 mb
- Next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) prediction (fitting L₁⁽¹⁾ to σ_np): σ_nd = 0.447 ± 0.130 mb
- Simultaneous fit at NLO to σ_np, σ_nd, and triton magnetic moment: σ_nd = 0.480 ± 0.114 mb
- Simultaneous fit at NNLO to σ_np and σ_nd: σ_nd = 0.511 ± 0.042 mb
- Wigner-SU(4) breaking parameter δ = 27 MeV, defined as half the difference between inverse scattering lengths in the ³S₁ and ¹S₀ channels

## Context

This work extends previous EFT(π̸) calculations of nd capture by Sadeghi (2006) and Arani (2014), which used partial resummation techniques and lacked the three-nucleon magnetic moment counterterm found necessary here for renormalization group invariance. It also complements potential model calculations by Viviani (1996) and Marcucci (2005) using AV18/UIX, and chiral effective field theory calculations by Girlanda (2010) and Song (2008). The identification of a new three-nucleon magnetic moment counterterm at NNLO enables future calculations of other three-nucleon electromagnetic processes, including proton-deuteron capture (important for Big Bang nucleosynthesis), photo-disintegration, and parity-violating asymmetries.
