# Regge phenomenology of the $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ poles

> We use Regge phenomenology to study the structure of the poles of the $N^*$ and $Δ^*$ spectrum. We employ the available pole extractions from partial wave analysis of meson scattering and photoproduction data. We assess the importance of the imaginary part of the poles (widths) to obtain a consistent determination of the parameters of the Regge trajectory. We compare the several pole extractions and show how Regge phenomenology can be used to gain insight in the internal structure of baryons. We find that the majority of the states in the parent Regge trajectories are compatible with a mostly compact three-quark state picture.

## Key claim

The paper uses Regge phenomenology to assess whether the majority of states in the parent $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ Regge trajectories are compatible with a mostly compact three-quark state picture, finding that they are, but only under the condition that the imaginary part of the poles (resonance widths) is properly accounted for in the trajectory fits rather than using the narrow-width approximation.

## Method

The authors fit complex Regge trajectories of the form $\alpha(s) = \alpha_0 + \alpha' s + i\, \gamma \, \phi(s,s_t)$ to the pole positions extracted from seven different partial wave analyses of pion-nucleon scattering and photoproduction data (CMB, JüBo, BnGa, SAID(SE), SAID(ED), KH80, KA84). They compare three models for the imaginary part: model 0 (linear, zero-width approximation), model I (square-root phase space), and model II (Chew-Mandelstam dispersive approach with analytic continuation of two-body phase space), fitting simultaneously to the real and imaginary parts of the pole positions using least-squares minimization with bootstrap error estimation.

## Key numbers

- For the $\frac{1}{2}^+_{(+)}$ trajectory (containing the nucleon), the slope parameter $\alpha'$ from model I ranges from 0.72(6) GeV$^{-2}$ (JüBo) to 0.98(6) GeV$^{-2}$ (KH80), while model 0 gives slopes from 1.00(8) to 1.10(2) GeV$^{-2}$
- For the $\frac{1}{2}^+_{(+)}$ trajectory, the intercept $\alpha_0$ from model I ranges from -0.1(2) (KH80) to 0.6(1) (JüBo), while model 0 gives intercepts from -0.50(4) to -0.3(1)
- The effective threshold parameter $s_t$ for the $\frac{1}{2}^+_{(+)}$ trajectory in model I ranges from 1.8(4) GeV$^2$ (KH80) to 2.65(5) GeV$^2$ (JüBo)
- The $\Delta(1232)$ pole mass is 1210(1) MeV with width 100(2) MeV (CMB), and 1211(0) MeV with width 100(2) MeV (SAID(SE))
- The $N(939)$ nucleon pole mass is 939(1) MeV with zero width across all analyses

## Context

This work extends the Regge phenomenology analysis previously applied to strange baryons by Fernandez-Ramirez (2015) to the non-strange $N^*$ and $\Delta^*$ spectrum, using pole extractions from multiple partial wave analyses including the Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley (CMB) analysis by Cutkosky (1979, 1980), the Jülich-Bonn coupled-channel model by Rönchen (2018), and the Bonn-Gatchina analysis by Anisovich (2011) and Sokhoyan (2015). The results enable future pole extractions to be guided by Regge constraints and provide a benchmark for distinguishing compact three-quark states from states with additional hadronic components, which is relevant for understanding nonperturbative QCD and confinement as indicated by the approximate linearity of Chew-Frautschi plots first noted by Chew and Frautschi (1962).
