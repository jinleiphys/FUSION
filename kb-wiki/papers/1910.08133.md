# Electroweak properties of kaons in a nuclear medium

> Kaon electroweak properties in symmetric nuclear matter are studied in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model using the proper-time regularization. The valence quark properties in symmetric nuclear matter are calculated in the quark-meson coupling model, and they are used as inputs for studying the in-medium kaon properties in the NJL model. We evaluate the kaon decay constant, kaon-quark coupling constant, and $K^+$ electromagnetic form factor by two different approaches. Namely, by two different ways of calculating the in-medium constituent quark masses of the light quarks. We predict that, in both approaches, the kaon decay constant and kaon-quark coupling constant decrease as nuclear density increases, while the $K^+$ charge radius increases by 20-25\% at normal nuclear density.

## Key claim

The kaon decay constant and kaon-quark coupling constant decrease with increasing nuclear density, while the \(K^+\) charge radius increases by 20–25% at normal nuclear density, but these predictions depend on the specific approach used to calculate the in-medium constituent quark masses (QMC-based or NJL-based), which yield similar but not identical results.

## Method

The valence quark properties in symmetric nuclear matter were calculated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, and these were used as inputs to study in-medium kaon properties within the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with proper-time regularization. Two approaches were adopted: the QMC-based approach, where the vacuum constituent quark masses from the NJL model (400 MeV for light quarks) were used to compute their density dependence in the QMC model; and the NJL-based approach, where the in-medium current quark properties from the QMC model (light-quark mass 16.4 MeV in vacuum) were used to solve the NJL gap equations for the dynamical quark masses. The kaon electromagnetic form factor was then evaluated from the sum of two Feynman diagrams in the NJL model.

## Key numbers

- Light-quark constituent mass reduction: 30–35% at nuclear matter saturation density \(\rho_0\) relative to vacuum, with the largest difference between the two approaches about 10%.
- In-medium light-quark chiral condensate decreases by 13–16% at \(\rho_0\) (from 0.171 GeV in vacuum to 0.149 GeV in the NJL-based approach and 0.144 GeV in the QMC-based approach).
- Kaon decay constant \(f_K^*\) at \(\rho_0\): 0.088 GeV (NJL-based) and 0.087 GeV (QMC-based), compared to 0.091 GeV in vacuum.
- Kaon-quark coupling constant \(g_{Kqq}^*\) at \(\rho_0\): 4.408 (NJL-based) and 4.376 (QMC-based), compared to 4.570 in vacuum.
- Effective kaon mass \(m_K^*\) at \(\rho_0\): 0.386 GeV (NJL-based) and 0.371 GeV (QMC-based), compared to 0.495 GeV in vacuum.
- \(K^+\) charge radius increases by 20–25% at normal nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the approach used for in-medium pion properties by Hutauruk et al. (2018) to kaons, improving upon an earlier study by Yabusaki et al. (2017) that used a light-front constituent quark model without dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The NJL model employed here incorporates chiral symmetry and its partial restoration in medium, which the light-front approach lacked. The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on kaon electroproduction at JLab and provide predictions for kaon property changes in dense nuclear matter relevant to neutron star studies.
