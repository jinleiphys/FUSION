# Charge symmetry breaking in the A=4 hypernuclei

> Charge symmetry breaking (CSB) in the $Λ$-nucleon strong interaction generates a charge dependence of $Λ$ separation energies in mirror hypernuclei, which in the case of the $A=4$ mirror hypernuclei $0^+$ ground states is sizable, $ΔB^{J=0}_Λ\equiv B^{J=0}_Λ (_Λ^4{\rm He}) -B^{J=0}_Λ(_Λ^4{\rm H})=230\pm 90$ keV, and of opposite sign to that induced by the Coulomb repulsion in light hypernuclei. Recent ab-initio calculations of the ($_Λ^4{\rm H},{_Λ^4{\rm He}}$) mirror hypernuclei $0^+_{\rm g.s.}$ and $1^+_{\rm exc}$ levels have demonstrated that a $Λ- Σ^0$ mixing CSB model due to Dalitz and von Hippel (1964) is capable of reproducing this large value of $ΔB^{J=0}_Λ$. These calculations are discussed here with emphasis placed on the leading-order chiral EFT hyperon-nucleon strong-interaction Bonn-Juelich potential model used and the no-core shell-model calculational scheme applied. The role of one-pion exchange in producing sizable CSB level splittings in the $A=4$ mirror hypernuclei is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the large charge symmetry breaking (CSB) splitting of the Λ separation energy between the A=4 mirror hypernuclei ground states, ΔBΛJ=0 = 230 ± 90 keV, can be reproduced by ab initio no-core shell-model calculations using a Λ-Σ0 mixing CSB model due to Dalitz and von Hippel (1964), but only when the strong-interaction ΛN↔ΣN coupling potential is sufficiently strong, as in the leading-order chiral EFT Bonn-Juelich potential with cutoff momentum Λ between 600 and 650 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed no-core shell-model (NCSM) calculations for the A=4 mirror hypernuclei 4ΛH and 4ΛHe using translationally invariant harmonic-oscillator bases in relative Jacobi coordinates, with N3LO NN and N2LO NNN chiral interactions plus leading-order chiral EFT coupled-channel YN potentials from the Bonn-Juelich group. The CSB potential VCSB was generated from the strong-interaction ΛN↔ΣN coupling potentials using the Dalitz-von Hippel Λ-Σ0 mass-mixing model, with the CSB scale coefficient 0.0297 derived from baryon mass differences, and the Coulomb interaction between charged baryons was included.

## Key numbers

- ΔBΛJ=0 ≡ BΛJ=0(4ΛHe) − BΛJ=0(4ΛH) = 233 ± 92 keV from experiment (MAMI 2016 and emulsion work)
- Λ-Σ0 mass-mixing matrix element ⟨Σ0|δM|Λ⟩ = 1.143 ± 0.040 MeV, derived from baryon mass differences
- CSB scale coefficient 2⟨Σ0|δM|Λ⟩/(MΣ0 − MΛ) = 0.0297 ± 0.0010
- For cutoff Λ = 650 MeV: ΔBΛJ=0 = 244 keV and ΔBΛJ=1 = −237 keV from the NCSM calculations with VCSB
- For cutoff Λ = 600 MeV: ΔBΛJ=0 = 136 keV and ΔBΛJ=1 = −193 keV
- For cutoff Λ = 550 MeV: ΔBΛJ=0 = 30 keV and ΔBΛJ=1 = −172 keV
- For cutoff Λ = 700 MeV: ΔBΛJ=0 = 296 keV and ΔBΛJ=1 = −256 keV
- Mean CSB splittings over cutoff range: ΔBΛJ=0 = 177+119−147 keV and ΔBΛJ=1 = −215+43−41 keV
- Calculated 3H binding energy: 8.482 MeV; 3He binding energy: 7.720 MeV
- Calculated BΛ(4ΛH) for cutoff 600 MeV: 2.308 MeV (J=0) and 1.359 MeV (J=1)
- Calculated BΛ(4ΛHe) for cutoff 600 MeV: 2.444 MeV (J=0) and 1.166 MeV (J=1)
- Meson-mixing CSB contributions found negligible: ΔBΛJ=0(π0η+ρ0ω) ∼ −20 keV, ΔBΛJ=1(π0η+ρ0ω) ∼ −10 keV (Coon et al. 1999)
- For Λ = 650 MeV: contact-term contribution ΔBΛJ=0(CT) = 313 keV, octet meson exchange contribution ΔBΛJ=0(8f) = −76 keV

## Context

This work extends earlier ab initio calculations using Nijmegen NSC97e,f models (Haidenbauer 2007, Nogga 2013, 2014) which could only reach ΔBΛJ=0 ≈ 100 keV at most, by employing leading-order chiral EFT YN potentials with stronger ΛN↔ΣN coupling. The calculations confirm the Dalitz and von Hippel (1964) Λ-Σ0 mixing mechanism as the dominant source of CSB in A=4 hypernuclei, with one-pion exchange playing a key role through the ΛN↔ΣN coupling potential. The results enable future work on CSB in heavier hypernuclei and motivate inclusion of YNN three-body forces in chiral EFT approaches, as the NLO calculations without such forces substantially underbind the A=4 hypernuclei.
