# Quartet structure of $N=Z$ nuclei in a boson formalism: the case of $^{28}$Si

> The structure of the $N=Z$ nucleus $^{28}$Si is studied by resorting to an IBM-type formalism with $s$ and $d$ bosons representing isospin $T=0$ and angular momentum $J=0$ and $J=2$ quartets, respectively. $T=0$ quartets are four-body correlated structures formed by two protons and two neutrons. The microscopic nature of the quartet bosons, meant as images of the fermionic quartets, is investigated by making use of a mapping procedure and is supported by the close resemblance between the phenomenological and microscopically derived Hamiltonians. The ground state band and two low-lying side bands, a $β$ and a $γ$ band, together with all known $E2$ transitions and quadrupole moments associated with these states are well reproduced by the model. An analysis of the potential energy surface places $^{28}$Si, only known case so far, at the critical point of the U(5)-$\overline{\rm SU(3)}$ transition of the IBM structural diagram.

## Key claim

The nucleus \(^{28}\)Si is placed at the critical point of the U(5)-\(\overline{\rm SU(3)}\) transition of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) structural diagram, based on an analysis of the potential energy surface; this is stated as the only known case so far, with the caveat that the system has only three bosons (\(N=3\)), so the transitional patterns are smoothed out but still show significant fingerprints of a precursor to a phase transition.

## Method

The authors describe \(^{28}\)Si using an IBM-type formalism with \(s\) and \(d\) bosons representing isospin \(T=0\), angular momentum \(J=0\) and \(J=2\) quartets (four-body correlated structures of two protons and two neutrons). A mapping procedure links the fermionic quartet space to the boson space, and the boson Hamiltonian parameters are partly derived from a shell-model calculation using the USDB interaction and partly fitted to experimental data. The potential energy surface is then analyzed using coherent states to determine the geometric shape and the location relative to the U(5)-\(\overline{\rm SU(3)}\) transition.

## Key numbers

* Single-boson energies from shell-model calculation with USDB interaction: \(\epsilon(0) = -37.713\) MeV and \(\epsilon(2) = -36.158\) MeV.
* Fitted two-body boson matrix elements (in MeV): (1) = -3.374, (2) = -0.859, (3) = -3.875, (4) = -14.298, (5) = -2.348, (6) = -6.746, (7) = -9.316.
* Experimental band heads: \(\beta\) band at 4.98 MeV, \(\gamma\) band at 7.42 MeV.
* \(E2\) operator parameters: \(e_B = 1.45\) (W.u.)\(^{1/2}\) and \(\chi = 2.1\).
* Ratio \(B(E2; 0^+_2 \rightarrow 2^+_1) / B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\) experimental value: 0.72(0.05).
* Ratio \(R_{42} = E(4^+_1) / E(2^+_1)\) experimental value for \(^{28}\)Si: 2.6.
* Critical control parameter for the schematic Hamiltonian: \(\eta_{cr} = 0.129\); the two minima coexist in the interval 0.127–0.143.
* Scale factor for the schematic Hamiltonian: \(\rho = 2.45\) MeV; \(E2\) operator scale factor: \(\tau = 1.68\) (W.u.)\(^{1/2}\).

## Context

This work extends the microscopic quartet model, previously applied to \(N=Z\) nuclei for proton-neutron pairing and general two-body interactions (e.g., Chasman (year), Sandulescu et al. (year)), to a boson formalism that simplifies the treatment of larger systems. It contrasts with the standard IBM-4 approach (Elliott (year)), which requires 10 different types of pair bosons for even-even \(N=Z\) nuclei, and with a prior phenomenological quartet-boson application (Dukelsky (year)) to nuclei in different major shells. The finding that \(^{28}\)Si sits at the critical point of the U(5)-\(\overline{\rm SU(3)}\) transition is distinct from well-established critical nuclei such as \(^{150}\)Nd, \(^{152}\)Sm, and \(^{154}\)Gd, which lie along isotopic chains exhibiting a spherical-prolate transition (Iachello (year), Scholten (year), Garcia-Ramos (year)). The quartet boson model is expected to enable studies of heavier unstable \(N=Z\) nuclei with data from radioactive beam facilities.
