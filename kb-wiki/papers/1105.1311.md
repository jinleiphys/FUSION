# Elementary excitations in homogeneous superfluid neutron star matter: Role of the proton component

> The thermal evolution of neuron stars depends on the elementary excitations affecting the stellar matter. In particular, the low-energy excitations, whose energy is proportional to the transfered momentum, can play a major role in the emission and propagation of neutrinos. In this paper, we focus on the density modes associated with the proton component in the homogeneous matter of the outer core of neutron stars (at density between one and three times the nuclear saturation density, where the baryonic constituants are expected to be neutrons and protons). In this region, it is predicted that the protons are superconductor. We study the respective roles of the proton pairing and Coulomb interaction in determining the properties of the modes associated with the proton component. This study is performed in the framework of the Random Phase Approximation, generalized in order to describe the response of a superfluid system.The formalism we use ensures that the Generalized Ward's Identities are satisfied. An important conclusion of this work is the presence of a pseudo-Goldstone mode associated with the proton superconductor in neutron-star matter. Indeed, the Goldstone mode, which characterizes a pure superfluid, is suppressed in usual superconductors due to the long-range Coulomb interaction, which only allows a plasmon mode. However, for the proton component of stellar matter, the Coulomb field is screened by the electrons and a pseudo-Goldstone mode occurs, with a velocity increased by the Coulomb interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the outer core of neutron stars, where protons are superconducting, a pseudo-Goldstone mode exists for the proton component because the Coulomb field is screened by electrons, unlike in ordinary superconductors where the Goldstone mode is suppressed and only a plasmon mode appears.

## Method

The authors formulate a generalized Random Phase Approximation (RPA) within a conserving approximation scheme that satisfies Generalized Ward's Identities, extending the formalism to describe the response of a superfluid system with both pairing and Coulomb interactions. They solve the coupled RPA equations for the proton and electron polarization tensors, treating the proton pairing in the BCS approximation with a constant gap and including the electron screening of the Coulomb interaction through a relativistic Lindhard function.

## Key numbers

- Proton Fermi momentum at nuclear saturation density (rho = 0.16 fm^-3): k_Fp = 0.56 fm^-1, corresponding to a proton fraction Y_p = 3.7%
- Electron plasmon frequency at nuclear saturation density: omega_0e = 6.15 MeV
- For a pairing gap Delta = 0.5 MeV, the Goldstone mode velocity in the pure pairing case is v_F/sqrt(3), where v_F is the Fermi velocity
- The pseudo-Goldstone mode velocity is about three times larger than the pure Goldstone mode velocity at nuclear saturation density
- The ratio N_p/N_e = m_p c/(hbar k_Fp) is approximately 8 at the considered density

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective excitations in normal neutron star matter using non-relativistic RPA (paper1, paper2) to the superfluid case, and addresses controversial results in the literature regarding whether the proton superfluid presents a Goldstone mode (Reddy, Kundu, Leinson1, Armen, Leinson2, Vosk). The findings enable improved calculations of neutrino emission and mean free path in neutron star cores, and provide a foundation for future work including the neutron component and entrainment effects when both neutrons and protons are superfluid (chamel, haensel).
