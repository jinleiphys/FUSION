# Irreducible pionic effects in nucleon-deuteron scattering below 20 MeV

> The consequences of a recently introduced irreducible pionic effect in low energy nucleon-deuteron scattering are analyzed. Differential cross-sections, nucleon (vector) and deuteron (vector and tensor) analyzing powers, and four different polarization transfer coefficients have been considered. This 3NF-like effect is generated by the pion-exchange diagram in presence of a two-nucleon correlation and is partially cancelled by meson-retardation contributions. Indications are provided that such type of effects are capable to selectively increase the vector (nucleon and deuteron) analyzing powers, while in the considered energy range they are almost negligible on the differential cross sections. These indications, observed with different realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, provide additional evidences that such 3NF-like effects have indeed the potential to solve the puzzle of the vector analyzing powers. Smaller but non negligible effects are observed for the other spin observables. In some cases, we find that the modifications introduced by such pionic effects on these spin observables (other than the vector analyzing powers) are significant and interesting and could be observed by experiments.

## Key claim

The irreducible pionic effect generated by a one-pion-exchange diagram in the presence of a two-nucleon correlation can selectively increase the vector analyzing powers in nucleon-deuteron scattering below 20 MeV without appreciably affecting differential cross sections, but this effect depends on an adjustable parameter that controls the partial cancellation against meson-retardation contributions and must be tuned for each underlying nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Method

The authors incorporated a three-nucleon-force-like operator derived from a dynamical pion-exchange diagram with a two-nucleon correlation directly into the Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) three-nucleon scattering equation. They used finite-rank separable representations (PEST, BBEST, CDBEST) of three realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Paris, Bonn B, and CD-Bonn) and solved the resulting multichannel integral equations for neutron-deuteron scattering observables at energies from 3 to 20 MeV, including all three-nucleon states with total angular momenta up to J = 19/2.

## Key numbers

- At 3 MeV incident neutron energy, the OPE-3NF effect raises the neutron analyzing power Ay to match experimental data without sensible modification of the differential cross section; the cancellation parameter for the Paris potential was set to 0.385.
- For the deuteron vector analyzing power iT11 at 3.3 MeV (equivalent to 4 MeV pd data with Coulomb slow-down correction of about 0.7 MeV), the OPE-3NF effect provides the necessary increase suggested by data.
- At 8.5 MeV, the same mechanism raises the neutron-deuteron Ay to match experimental data; at 12 MeV the effect increases Ay significantly in the backward hemisphere while slightly suppressing it in the forward hemisphere.
- The parameter governing the cancellation of the 3NF effect evolves with a perfectly linear dependence on energy when fitting pd Ay data at 12, 14, 16, and 18 MeV (compared to nd calculations at 11.3, 13.3, 15.3, and 17.3 MeV respectively).
- For the polarization transfer coefficient Ky^y' at 15, 17, and 19 MeV, the OPE-3NF effect with the Bonn B potential corrects the underprediction of the minimum at 110 degrees, but the CD-Bonn results show smaller modifications that tend in the opposite direction.

## Context

This work extends the study of Canton et al. (2000, 2001) on irreducible pionic effects in three-nucleon systems by testing the OPE-3NF diagram with three different realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (Paris, Bonn B, CD-Bonn) and by comparing with a wider set of experimental data including differential cross sections, vector and tensor analyzing powers, and spin-transfer coefficients. The approach addresses the long-standing puzzle of vector analyzing powers in nucleon-deuteron scattering below 30 MeV, which has also been approached through alternative explanations such as phenomenological spin-orbit three-body operators (Kievsky et al., 1999), Brown-Rho scaling (Sammarruca et al., 2000), and modifications to triplet P-wave phase shifts (Tornow et al., 1998). The results enable future experimental tests of these pionic effects, particularly through measurements of the T21 tensor analyzing power in the 10-20 MeV range, where no data currently exist.
