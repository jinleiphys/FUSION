# Analysis of combined HERA data in the Impact-Parameter dependent Saturation model

> The Impact-Parameter dependent Saturation Model (IP-Sat) is a simple dipole model that incorporates key features of the physics of gluon saturation and matches smoothly to the perturbative QCD dipole expression at large Q^2 for a given x. It was previously shown that the model gives a good description of HERA data suggesting evidence for gluon saturation effects at small x. The model has also been applied to proton-proton and proton-nucleus collisions and provides the basis for the IP-Glasma model of initial conditions in heavy ion collisions. Here we present a reanalysis of available data in electron-proton collisions at small Bjorken-x, including the recently released combined data from the ZEUS and H1 collaborations. We first confront the model to the high precision combined data for the reduced cross-section and obtain its parameters. With these parameters fixed, we compare model results to data for the structure function F_2, the longitudinal structure function F_L, the charm structure function F_2^{c\bar{c}}, exclusive vector meson (J/ψ, ϕand ρ) production and Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS). Excellent agreement is obtained for the processes considered at small x in a wide range of Q^2. Our results strongly hint at universality of the IP-Sat dipole amplitude and the extracted impact-parameter distribution of the proton. They also provide a benchmark for further refinements in studies of QCD saturation at colliders.

## Key claim

The IP-Sat dipole model, with only four free parameters, provides an excellent description of a wide range of HERA data at small Bjorken-x, strongly hinting at the universality of the extracted dipole amplitude and impact-parameter distribution of the proton, though the model lacks the same grounding in fundamental theory as the rcBK and JIMWLK frameworks.

## Method

The authors performed a chi-squared fit of the IP-Sat model parameters to the combined H1 and ZEUS reduced cross-section data for neutral current unpolarized e±p scattering in the kinematic range Q² ∈ [0.75, 650] GeV² and x ≤ 0.01, using 263 data points. With the parameters fixed from this fit, they then compared model predictions for the structure functions F₂, F_L, F₂^{c̄c}, exclusive vector meson production (J/ψ, φ, ρ), and Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) against experimental data without further adjustment.

## Key numbers

- The fit to the reduced cross-section σ_r yields χ²/d.o.f = 298.89/259 = 1.15 for charm mass m_c = 1.27 GeV, and χ²/d.o.f = 316.61/259 = 1.22 for m_c = 1.4 GeV.
- The extracted initial gluon distribution parameters for m_c = 1.27 GeV are: μ₀² = 1.51 GeV², A_g = 2.308, λ_g = 0.058, with B_G fixed at 4 GeV⁻².
- The light quark mass preferred by the fit is m_u ≈ 10⁻⁴ GeV, in contrast to earlier IP-Sat analyses which required m_u = 50 or 140 MeV.
- Using the old IP-Sat parameter set from Watt (2007) against the new combined data gives χ²/d.o.f = 825.86/259 = 3.19, indicating the new fit supersedes the earlier one.

## Context

This work extends the IP-Sat model analyses of Kowalski and Teaney (2003) and Watt, Motyka, and Kowalski (2007) by confronting the model with the newly released combined H1 and ZEUS data, which have unprecedentedly high precision and are free from normalization discrepancies between the two experiments. The results provide a benchmark for further refinements in studies of QCD saturation at colliders, including predictions for a future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and applications to proton-proton, proton-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions.
