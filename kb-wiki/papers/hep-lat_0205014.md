# Connecting the Quenched and Unquenched Worlds via the Large N_c World

> In the large N_c(number of colors) limit, quenched QCD and QCD are identical. This implies that, in the effective field theory framework, some of the low energy constants in (N_c=3) quenched QCD and QCD are the same up to higher-order corrections in the 1/N_c expansion. Thus the calculation of the nonleptonic kaon decays relevant for the Delta I=1/2 rule in the quenched approximation is expected to differ from the unquenched one by an O(1/N_c) correction. However, the calculation relevant to the CP-violation parameter epsilon'/epsilon would have a relatively big higher-order correction due to the large cancellation in the leading order. Some important weak matrix elements are poorly known that even constraints with 100% errors are interesting. In those cases, quenched calculations will be very useful.

## Key claim

In the large \(N_c\) limit, quenched QCD and full QCD become identical, implying that for \(N_c=3\) some low-energy constants in quenched and unquenched chiral perturbation theory differ only by \(O(1/N_c)\) corrections. However, for observables like \(\epsilon'/\epsilon\) that involve large cancellations at leading order, the quenching error can be significantly larger than the naive \(\sim 30\%\) estimate.

## Method

The author uses effective field theory (chiral perturbation theory and quenched chiral perturbation theory) combined with a \(1/N_c\) expansion to compare low-energy constants and observables in quenched and unquenched QCD. By analyzing the large \(N_c\) scaling of the low-energy constants and the chiral logarithms in both theories, the size of quenching errors is estimated for various weak matrix elements and hadronic quantities.

## Key numbers

- The quenching error from the difference in low-energy constants is \(O(1/N_c) \sim 30\%\), consistent with the \(\sim 25\%\) quenching error seen in simulations (e.g., for \(f_{B_s}/f_B\)).
- For the \(\Delta I=1/2\) rule, quenched simulations give \(25.3 \pm 1.8\) (RBC Collaboration) and \(10 \pm 4\) (CP-PACS Collaboration) compared to the experimental value \(22.2\).
- For \(\epsilon'/\epsilon\), the quenched calculation yields \((-4.0 \pm 2.3) \times 10^{-4}\) (RBC Collaboration), compared to the experimental average of \((17.2 \pm 1.8) \times 10^{-4}\).
- The proton strange magnetic moment from experiment is \(\mu_s^{\exp} = [0.01 \pm 0.29(\text{stat}) \pm 0.31(\text{sys}) \pm 0.07(\text{theor.})]\) n.m., and the kaon cloud contribution is \(\sim 2.0\) n.m., implying a quenching error of \(\sim 0.8\) n.m.

## Context

This work extends the connection between quenched and unquenched QCD established by Sharpe (1990, 1992) and Bernard and Golterman (1992) in quenched chiral perturbation theory, and by 't Hooft (1974) for large \(N_c\) QCD. The paper provides a systematic framework for estimating quenching errors using \(1/N_c\) expansions, enabling lattice practitioners to assess the reliability of quenched calculations for specific observables. It identifies promising targets for quenched simulations with \(100\%\) error tolerance, such as the parity-violating pion-nucleon coupling \(h_\pi\) and parity-violating \(\gamma N\Delta\) couplings, which would be valuable for interpreting experiments at LANSCE and elsewhere.
