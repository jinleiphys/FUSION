# Electron Capture and Bound-State $β$ Decays in Ions and Plasma

> We present a new theory describing the variation of electron capture and bound-state $β$-decays in atomic ions and (non) local thermodynamic equilibrium ((N)LTE) plasmas. We adopt the Takahashi-Yokoi nuclear model with added corrections to first calculate the decay rate for each atomic configuration of the isotope, and then evaluate the in-plasma decay rate by combining them with the charge state distribution (CSD) consistent with plasma density and temperature. Our approach expands the thermodynamic parameter space in which in-plasma $β$-decays can be studied, opening the possibility to validate the model in low-density laboratory magnetoplasmas before application to stellar nucleosynthesis. The model is explained using $^{7}$Be, and then applied to higher mass isotopes such as $^{140}$Pr$^{0+,57+,58+}$, $^{142}$Pm$^{0+,59+,60+}$ and $^{163}$Dy$^{66+}$. Our model is therefore amenable to isotopes in a wide range of masses, in both single charge state or in a plasma-generated CSD.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalized model for electron capture and bound-state beta decay rates in atomic ions and plasmas that splits the calculation into an atomic component (configuration-dependent decay rate) and a plasma component (charge state distribution), enabling application to both low-density laboratory magnetoplasmas in non-local thermodynamic equilibrium and stellar plasmas, whereas the original Takahashi-Yokoi (1983) model was designed only for stellar plasmas.

## Method

The authors adopt the Takahashi-Yokoi nuclear model with added corrections, replacing electron radial wavefunctions with Coulomb amplitudes from Behrens and Buhring (1982) to account for multi-electron screening, and incorporating exchange/overlap corrections via polynomial interpolation from Bahcall (1963) and Vatai (1970) for isotopes in the range Z=6-37. They first calculate the decay rate for each atomic configuration (charge state and excitation level) of the isotope using the lepton phase volume formalism, then evaluate the in-plasma decay rate by combining these configuration-dependent rates with the charge state distribution consistent with plasma density and temperature. The model is demonstrated using Be-7 and validated against experimental storage-ring data for Pr-140, Pm-142, and Dy-163.

## Key numbers

* Be-7 neutral ground state decay rate: lambda(0) = 1.507 x 10^-7 s^-1 (from Tilley 2002)
* Be-7 configuration-dependent decay rate changes: |delta-lambda*(ij)| <= 3.5% for Be-7(0+) and Be-7(1+); approximately 50% drop in Be-7(2+) when K-shell occupancy drops from 100% to 50%; approximately 50% drop in Be-7(3+) when electron excites from K-shell to L-shell
* Be-7 model prediction for true ground state (1s^2 2s^2): half-life = 53.39 days, branching ratio = 10.43% (compared to ENSDF values of 53.22 +/- 0.06 days and 10.44%)
* Dy-163(66+) bound-state beta decay half-life: predicted 49.77 days (measured 47 +5/-4 days from Jung 1992)
* Pr-140(57+) EC decay rate: predicted 0.00153 s^-1 (measured 0.00147 +/- 0.00007 s^-1 from Litvinov 2007)
* Pr-140(58+) EC decay rate: predicted 0.00240 s^-1 (measured 0.00219 +/- 0.00006 s^-1 from Litvinov 2007)
* Pr-140 hyperfine ratio lambda*(H)/lambda*(He): predicted 1.57 (measured 1.49 +/- 0.08)
* Pm-142(0+) EC decay rate: predicted 0.0040 s^-1 (measured 0.0039 +/- 0.0005 s^-1 from Winckler 2009)
* Pm-142(59+) EC decay rate: predicted 0.0035 s^-1 (measured 0.0036 +/- 0.0001 s^-1)
* Pm-142(60+) EC decay rate: predicted 0.0049 s^-1 (measured 0.0051 +/- 0.0001 s^-1)
* Pm-142 hyperfine ratio lambda*(H)/lambda*(He): predicted 1.39 (measured 1.44 +/- 0.06)

## Context

This work extends the Takahashi-Yokoi (1983) theory of in-plasma beta decays, which was originally formulated for stellar plasmas, to also apply to low-density laboratory magnetoplasmas in non-local thermodynamic equilibrium, such as those produced by electron cyclotron resonance ion sources at the PANDORA facility under construction at INFN-LNS. The model enables experimental validation of plasma-modified decay rates in controlled laboratory conditions before application to stellar nucleosynthesis models, which currently rely on half-lives measured in neutral atoms that may differ significantly from stellar plasma values. The atomic component is validated against storage-ring measurements of highly charged ions by Litvinov (2007) for Pr-140 and Winckler (2009) for Pm-142, and against Dirac-Hartree-Fock calculations by Taioli (2022) and Simonucci (2013).
