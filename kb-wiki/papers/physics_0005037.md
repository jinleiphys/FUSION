# Algebraic Models: Coordinates, Scales, and Dynamical Symmetries

> We discuss the variety of coordinates often used to characterize the coherent state classical limit of an algebraic model. We show selection of appropriate coordinates naturally motivates a procedure to generate a single particle Schrödinger hamiltonian which, for low energy states, gives equivalent results to a bosonic algebraic model to leading order in $N$. The process is used to study the associated geometries of the dynamical symmetries of U(3). By demanding that the inner product be preserved in the Schrödinger picture we conclude that different dynamical symmetries correspond to different scales.

## Key claim

Different dynamical symmetries of the U(3) algebraic model correspond not only to different geometries but also to different intrinsic scales, as demonstrated by demanding that the inner product be preserved when mapping between the algebraic and Schrödinger pictures. This scale dependence is necessary to make the Schrödinger and algebraic prescriptions commensurate for the leading asymptotic in N, the symmetric representation label.

## Method

The author develops a generic procedure to convert an algebraic Hamiltonian into a single-particle Schrödinger Hamiltonian by traveling counter-clockwise around a correspondence diagram: taking the coherent state classical limit, selecting Darboux coordinates in a patch of the coherent state phase space, and then re-quantizing canonically. For the U(3) model, the classical Hamiltonian is expanded near the fixed point of the Hamiltonian flow (where dH=0 and p_r=0), and the resulting approximate harmonic oscillator is quantized to obtain energy spacings and wavefunctions that agree with the algebraic results to leading order in N. The scale ratio between the U(2) and O(3) dynamical symmetry chains is then fixed by comparing the analytically calculated wavefunction overlaps from the re-quantization procedure with the exact algebraic overlaps.

## Key numbers

- For the U(2) chain with Hamiltonian H = ε n̂ + δ n̂(n̂+1), the low-energy level spacing from re-quantization is ΔE ≈ ε + 2δ, which matches the algebraic result for the lowest levels.
- On the O(3) chain (X=Y=0), the re-quantized energy spacing is ΔE ≈ Z ≈ 4AN, compared to the exact algebraic expression ΔE = A(4N-2), agreeing to leading order in N.
- The scale ratio ζ = (mω̄)_U(2) / (mω̄)_O(3) is fixed to leading order as ζ ≈ (2 ln 2)/(2 - ln 2).
- The radial displacement r* for the O(3) chain is given by (r*)^2 ≈ (N ħ)/(mω̄) (1 + (X/Z)(l²/(Nħ)² - 1) - Y/Z), where X=ε+2δ+β, Y=δ(N-1), and Z=4A(N-1).
- The shift ε in the minimum position due to the centrifugal barrier term is ε ≈ 1/(16N²-3) [1 - (16N²/(16N²-3))(4(X/Z)(l²/N² - 1) - (Y/Z)(2l²/N² + 3))], with leading contribution O(1/N²).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid algebraic-Schrödinger approaches introduced by Iachello (1990s) for studying transition intensities in molecules, specifically the U(2) vibron model and the U(3) two-dimensional model. The procedure is most similar to the re-quantization used by Roos (1980s) to study spectra, but differs from studies by Wulfman (1990s) that relied on particular dynamical symmetries. The results enable a generic correspondence between algebraic parameters and geometric quantities in configuration space, independent of a specific dynamical symmetry, which is needed for analyzing molecular structure in hybrid algebraic-Schrödinger approaches.
