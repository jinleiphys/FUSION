# Multi-messenger and multi-physics Bayesian inference for GW170817 binary neutron star merger

> The tidal deformability probability distribution extracted from GW170817 alone, or including multi-messenger information, is confronted to astrophysical and nuclear physics additional constraints within a semi-agnostic approach for the dense matter equation of state. We use Bayesian statistics to combine together low density nuclear physics data, such as the ab-initio predictions based on $χ$EFT interactions or the isoscalar giant monopole resonance, and astrophysical constraints from neutron stars, such as the maximum mass of neutron stars or the probability density function of the tidal deformability $\tildeΛ$ obtained from the GW170817 event. The posteriors probability distribution functions are marginalized over several nuclear empirical parameters ($L_\textrm{sym}$, $K_\textrm{sym}$, $Q_\textrm{sat}$ and $Q_\textrm{sym}$), as well as over observational quantities such as the $1.4M_\odot$ radius $R_{1.4}$ and the pressure at twice the saturation density $P(2n_\textrm{sat})$. The correlations between $L_\textrm{sym}$ and $K_\textrm{sym}$ and between $K_\textrm{sat}$ and $Q_\textrm{sat}$ are also further analyzed. Tension is found between the posteriors: the first one is localized in the tidal deformability probability distribution itself, depending whether multi-messenger analysis is included or not, and the second one is between the observational data and the nuclear physics inputs. These tensions impact the predictions for $L_\textrm{sym}$, $K_\textrm{sym}$ and $R_{1.4}$ with centroids which differ by 2-3$σ$. Implications for the nuclear equation of state are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper finds tension between posterior probability distributions obtained from different combinations of constraints: the tidal deformability probability distribution itself differs depending on whether multi-messenger information is included, and there is tension between observational data and nuclear physics inputs, with centroids for the symmetry energy slope \(L_\textrm{sym}\), the symmetry energy curvature \(K_\textrm{sym}\), and the canonical neutron star radius \(R_{1.4}\) differing by 2–3\(\sigma\).

## Method

The authors performed a Bayesian statistical analysis using a semi-agnostic meta-model (ELFc) for the nucleonic equation of state, which is parameterized by nuclear empirical parameters. They combined low-density nuclear physics constraints (chiral effective field theory predictions from Drischler (2016) and isoscalar giant monopole resonance data) with astrophysical constraints (the maximum neutron star mass and the tidal deformability probability distribution from GW170817, with and without multi-messenger information from Coughlin (2019)). The posterior probability distributions were marginalized over the empirical parameters \(L_\textrm{sym}\), \(K_\textrm{sym}\), \(Q_\textrm{sat}\), and \(Q_\textrm{sym}\), as well as over the radius \(R_{1.4}\) and the pressure at twice saturation density \(P(2n_\textrm{sat})\).

## Key numbers

- The tidal deformability from GW170817 alone is \(70 < \tilde{\Lambda} < 720\) at 90% confidence level from LIGO-Virgo (2019), and \(70 < \tilde{\Lambda} < 500\) from De (2018).
- The multi-messenger analysis from Coughlin (2019) suggests \(\tilde{\Lambda} \ge 300\).
- The maximum neutron star mass constraints used are \(M_\mathrm{max}^\mathrm{obs} \approx 2 M_\odot\), based on observations including \(2.01(4) M_\odot\) for PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis, 2013) and \(2.14^{+0.10}_{-0.09} M_\odot\) for MSP J0740+6620 (Cromartie, 2019).
- The kinetic energy per nucleon at saturation is \(t^{FG}_{\mathrm{sat}} = 22.1\) MeV.
- The saturation density is fixed at \(n_{\mathrm{sat}} = 0.155\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The fixed empirical parameters include \(E_{\mathrm{sat}} = -15.8\) MeV and \(E_{\mathrm{sym}} = 32.0\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of the neutron star equation of state that used piece-wise polytropes (Annala, 2018; Most, 2018), sound-speed models (Tews, 2018; Tews, 2019), and Skyrme-type functionals (Malik, 2018; Kim, 2018) by employing a semi-agnostic meta-model that retains information about matter composition. It confronts the tidal deformability probability distributions from LIGO-Virgo (2019) and De (2018) with the multi-messenger analysis of Coughlin (2019), and it explicitly investigates the role of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance constraint. The identified tensions between nuclear physics and astrophysical constraints call for future extensions of the analysis to include possible phase transitions in dense matter.
