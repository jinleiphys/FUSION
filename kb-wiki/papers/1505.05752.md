# Polyakov loop fluctuations in Dirac eigenmode expansion

> We investigate correlations of the Polyakov loop fluctuations with eigenmodes of the lattice Dirac operator. Their analytic relations are derived on the temporally odd-number size lattice with the normal non-twisted periodic boundary condition for the link-variables. We find that the low-lying Dirac modes yield negligible contributions to the Polyakov loop fluctuations. This property is confirmed to be valid in confined and deconfined phase by numerical simulations in SU(3) quenched QCD. These results indicate that there is no direct, one-to-one correspondence between confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in QCD in the context of different properties of the Polyakov loop fluctuation ratios.

## Key claim

The authors derive exact analytic relations between the Polyakov loop fluctuations and Dirac eigenmodes on a temporally odd-number lattice, and find that low-lying Dirac modes contribute negligibly to these fluctuations in both confined and deconfined phases, indicating no direct one-to-one correspondence between confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.

## Method

The authors derived analytic relations linking the Polyakov loop and its fluctuations (real, imaginary, and modulus) to Dirac eigenmodes on a temporally odd-number lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using the modified Kogut-Susskind formalism to reduce computational cost. They performed SU(3) quenched lattice QCD simulations on a 10³×5 lattice at β=5.6 (confined phase, T≈160 MeV) and β=6.0 (deconfined phase, T≈400 MeV) with 20 gauge configurations each, diagonalizing the KS Dirac operator to obtain eigenvalues and eigenfunctions, then introduced an infrared cutoff Λ to separate low-lying from higher-lying Dirac modes.

## Key numbers

- In confined phase (β=5.6, a≈0.25 fm, T≈160 MeV): original Polyakov loop susceptibility ratio R_A = 0.6548; with IR cutoff Λ≈0.4 GeV, R_A = 0.6549
- In deconfined phase (β=6.0, a≈0.10 fm, T≈400 MeV): original R_A = 0.9834; with IR cutoff Λ≈0.4 GeV, R_A = 0.9834
- In confined phase: original transverse susceptibility ratio R_T = 1.131; with IR cutoff Λ≈0.4 GeV, R_T = 1.131
- In deconfined phase: original R_T = 0.2603; with IR cutoff Λ≈0.4 GeV, R_T = 0.2603
- Chiral condensate ratio R_chiral ≈ 0.02 with Λ≈0.4 GeV and quark mass m≈5 MeV in confined phase
- Creutz ratio χ(3,3) ≈ 0.35(2) at β=5.6, consistent with string tension σ=0.89 GeV/fm

## Context

This work extends the Dirac-mode expansion method developed by the same group (GIS, DSI, SDI) to Polyakov loop fluctuations, building on earlier studies showing that low-lying Dirac modes have little contribution to the Polyakov loop and confining force. The results contradict the apparent coincidence of deconfinement and chiral crossover temperatures observed in lattice QCD (Karsch, LFKRS13f), and support findings from hadron spectroscopy (LS11) that chiral symmetry restoration does not dictate deconfinement. The work enables systematic separation of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking effects in QCD using Polyakov loop fluctuation ratios as deconfinement probes.
