# Frozen and $β$-equilibrated $f$ and $p$ modes of cold neutron stars: nuclear metamodel predictions

> When the chemical re-equilibration timescale is sufficiently long, the normal and quasi-normal mode frequencies of neutron stars should be calculated in the idealized limit that the internal composition of each fluid element is fixed over the oscillation period. However, many studies rely on a barotropic equation of state, which implicitly overlooks potential out-of-$β$-equilibrium effects. To investigate possible biases arising from this assumption, we calculate the non-radial fundamental ($f$) and first pressure ($p_1$) modes for a wide range of neutron star structures, each governed by different nucleonic equations of state. This ensemble is generated using the metamodel technique, a phenomenological framework that incorporates constraints from experimental nuclear physics and chiral effective field theory. The metamodel also provides the internal composition of $β$-equilibrated $npeμ$ matter, allowing us to calculate oscillation modes beyond those supported by a purely barotropic fluid. Thus, we systematically assess the impact of assuming a barotropic equation of state across various equations of state and provide a distribution of expected $f$ and $p_1$ mode frequencies that may be detectable by next-generation gravitational wave interferometers.

## Key claim

The paper systematically quantifies the bias introduced by assuming a barotropic (β-equilibrated) equation of state when calculating neutron star oscillation modes, finding that for the fundamental (f) mode the difference between frozen and barotropic frequencies is below 0.5%, while for the first pressure (p1) mode the difference remains below 5% for masses not too close to the maximum TOV mass, confirming that barotropic models can be trusted within these error bounds.

## Method

The authors generate a large ensemble of nucleonic equations of state using the nuclear metamodel technique, which provides both the β-equilibrated equation of state and the internal composition of npeμ matter for cold neutron stars. They apply Bayesian filtering with constraints from chiral effective field theory calculations of pure neutron matter, nuclear mass measurements from the AME2020 table, the maximum mass of PSR J0348+0432, the tidal deformability from GW170817, and NICER mass-radius estimates for three pulsars. For each metamodel instance that passes all filters, they solve the relativistic Cowling approximation perturbation equations for quadrupolar (l=2) f and p1 modes in both the frozen composition and β-equilibrated regimes.

## Key numbers

- f-mode frequency difference between frozen and barotropic limits: smaller than 0.5%
- p1-mode frequency difference between frozen and barotropic limits: less than 5% for masses below approximately 2 solar masses
- Dispersion of metamodel instances around the quasi-universal relation fit for the f-mode: errors smaller than 2.5%
- Dispersion of metamodel instances around the quasi-universal relation fit for the p1-mode: errors ranging from approximately 5% to 10%
- Maximum TOV mass constraint: must exceed 2.01 ± 0.04 solar masses (PSR J0348+0432)

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of the nuclear metamodel by Zhang (2020), Carreau (2019), Guven (2020), and Davis (2024) to include oscillation mode calculations, and systematically tests quasi-universal relations proposed by Andersson (1997), Tsui (2004), Deb (2021), Deb (2022), and Sotani (2021) using a much larger set of nucleonic equations of state than previously employed. The results provide posterior predictive distributions for f and p1 mode frequencies that can be used to guide searches by next-generation gravitational wave interferometers such as the Einstein Telescope and Cosmic Explorer, and suggest that distinguishing hadronic from strange stars using p1-mode frequencies may be harder than suggested by Flores (2023) due to the dispersion from nuclear model uncertainties.
