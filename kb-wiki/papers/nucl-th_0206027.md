# The Electromagnetic Form Factors of the Proton and Neutron: Fundamental Indicators of Nucleon Structure

> We present a relativistic interpretation for why the proton's ratio of electric to magnetic form factor falls and for why the ratio of the Pauli to Dirac form factor varies as 1/Q. Reproducing the observed electric form factor of the neutron then mandates the inclusion of the effects of the pion cloud.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed fall of the proton's \(G_E/G_M\) and the approximate constancy of \(QF_2/F_1\) arise naturally from a relativistic treatment using ordinary Dirac spinors, which carry light-front orbital angular momentum and violate the hadron helicity conservation rule; reproducing the neutron's electric form factor then requires the additional inclusion of pion cloud effects.

## Method

The author constructs a relativistic three-quark model of the nucleon using light-front dynamics, with a spatial wave function taken from Schlumpf (1994) and parameters \(\beta = 0.607\) GeV, \(\gamma = 3.5\), and quark mass \(m = 0.267\) GeV. Form factors are computed from matrix elements of the \(J^+\) current using light-front spinors, where the Melosh rotation accounts for the lower components of Dirac spinors and introduces spin-flip terms. For the neutron, the model is extended to a light-front cloudy bag model that includes pionic contributions from a bare nucleon plus a pion cloud.

## Key numbers

* The spatial wave function parameters are \(\beta = 0.607\) GeV, \(\gamma = 3.5\), and quark mass \(m = 0.267\) GeV.
* The model predicts \(QF_2/F_1\) is constant for \(Q^2\) between 2 and about 20 GeV\(^2\).
* The neutron charge radius squared from the relativistic quark model alone is \(R^2_{\rm model} = -0.025\) fm\(^2\), about five times smaller than the experimental value of \(R^2 = -0.113 \pm 0.005\) fm\(^2\).
* The Foldy contribution to the neutron charge radius is \(R_F^2 = -0.111\) fm\(^2\), while the model's \(F_1\) contribution gives \(R_1^2 = +0.086\) fm\(^2\).
* The Galster parameterization represents neutron data for \(Q^2 < 0.7\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier relativistic model of Frank, Jennings, and Miller (1995), which predicted a strong decrease of \(G_E/G_M\) with \(Q^2\) that was later confirmed by experiments at Jefferson Laboratory. The analysis contradicts the hadron helicity conservation rule, which would predict \(Q^2F_2/F_1\) constant, and instead shows that Poincare invariance leads to a constant \(QF_2/F_1\) over the measured range. The inclusion of the pion cloud via the light-front cloudy bag model, building on the cloudy bag model of Thomas and collaborators, enables a description of the neutron electric form factor at both low and high \(Q^2\), and the author states this approach can describe all four nucleon electromagnetic form factors.
