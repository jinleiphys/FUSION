# R-ratios and moments of nuclear structure functions

> We study implications of a model, which links nuclear and nucleon structure functions. Computed Callen-Gross functions $κ^A(x,Q^2)= 2xF_1^A(x,Q^2)/F_2^A(x,Q^2)$ appear for finite $Q^2$ to be close to their asymptotic value 1. Using those $κ$, we compure $R$ ratios for $Q^2\ge 5 GeV^2$. We review approximate methods in use for the extraction of $R$ from inclusive scattering and ENC data. Further we calcuate ratios of the moments of $F_k$ and find these to describe the data, in particular their $Q^2$ dependence. The above observables, as well as inclusive cross sections, are sensitive tests for the underlying relation between nucleonic and nuclear structure functions. In view of the overall agreement, we speculate that the above relation effectively circumvents a QCD calculation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a specific model linking nuclear and nucleon structure functions can reproduce experimental data for the ratio \(R^A\) of longitudinal to transverse virtual photon cross sections and for moments of nuclear structure functions, effectively circumventing a full QCD calculation for the tested kinematic region.

## Method

The authors compute nuclear Callen-Gross functions \(\kappa^A(x,Q^2) = 2xF_1^A/F_2^A\) and the corresponding \(R\) ratios using a convolution relation (Eq. a3) that connects nuclear structure functions to weighted free-nucleon structure functions and a point-nucleon spectral function \(f^{PN}\). They then compare three approximate methods for extracting \(R\) (the high-\(Q^2\) approximation, the nucleon-elastic approximation, and an empirical constant-over-\(Q^2\) form) against their "exact" computed values. Additionally, they compute ratios of moments of structure functions \(\mu^A(m;Q^2)\) from the same convolution model and compare them to experimental data.

## Key numbers

* For \(0.2 \lesssim x \lesssim 0.75\) and \(Q^2 \ge 5\) GeV\(^2\), the computed nuclear Callen-Gross function satisfies \(|\kappa(x,Q^2)-1| \approx 0.11-0.12\).
* The "exact" \(R\) for \(x=0.12\) at \(Q^2=5\) GeV\(^2\) is 0.216; at \(Q^2=50\) GeV\(^2\) it is 0.176.
* The "exact" \(R\) for \(x=0.5\) at \(Q^2=5\) GeV\(^2\) is 0.140; at \(Q^2=50\) GeV\(^2\) it is approximately 0.
* The nucleon-elastic approximation \(R_{NE}^{(2)}\) gives \(0.31/Q^2\) (with \(Q^2\) in GeV\(^2\)) for \(x\approx 1\).
* The ratio \(R(1.05,5)/R^{NE}(1,5) = 1.86\) for the nucleon-elastic approximation near the quasielastic peak.
* The empirical constant approximation \(R_C\) uses \(\delta/Q^2\) with \(0.2 \lesssim \delta \lesssim 0.5\).

## Context

This work extends the convolution model of Gurvitz and Rinat (1995) for relating nuclear and nucleon structure functions, previously applied to inclusive cross sections by Rinat and Taragin (1996, 1997). The paper directly compares its computed \(R\) ratios to the approximate methods of Bosted et al. (1992) and Dasu et al. (1988, 1994), and its moment calculations to the work of Cothran, Day, and Liutti (1998), which used a \(Q^2\)-independent PWIA and produced incorrect \(Q^2\) dependence. The results enable tests of the underlying relation between nucleonic and nuclear structure functions across wide \(x\) and \(Q^2\) ranges, and the authors speculate the relation may represent an effective theory mimicking QCD.
