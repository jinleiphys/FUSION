# Constraints on Galactic Wino Densities from Gamma Ray Lines

> We systematically compute the annihilation rate for neutral winos into the final state gamma + X, including all leading radiative corrections. This includes both the Sommerfeld enhancement (in the decoupling limit for the Higgsino) and the resummation of the leading electroweak double logarithms. Adopting an analysis of the HESS experiment, we place constraints on the mass as a function of the wino fraction of the dark matter and the shape of the dark matter profile. We also determine how much coring is needed in the dark matter halo to make the wino a viable candidate as a function of its mass. Additionally, as part of our effective field theory formalism, we show that in the pure-Standard Model sector of our theory, emissions of soft Higgses are power-suppressed and that collinear Higgs emission does not contribute to leading double logs.

## Key claim

The authors systematically compute the leading-log (LL) semi-inclusive wino annihilation rate into gamma + X, including both Sommerfeld enhancement and resummation of electroweak double logarithms, and find that the leading-log radiative corrections lead to only a modest, few-percent reduction in the semi-inclusive rate compared to tree level plus Sommerfeld enhancement, contradicting earlier fixed-order NLO calculations that found a 75% reduction.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid effective field theory combining NRQCD (for the nonrelativistic wino pair) with Soft-Collinear Effective Theory (SCET-II) for the final-state collinear jet containing the observed photon. They derive a factorization theorem for the semi-inclusive annihilation cross section, compute the anomalous dimensions for the soft and collinear operators using rapidity renormalization group techniques, and resum the leading double logarithms by running the matching coefficients from the high scale M_chi down to M_W. The Sommerfeld enhancement is computed in the decoupling limit for the Higgsino, using a quantum mechanical potential derived from the underlying relativistic field theory.

## Key numbers

- The thermal-relic wino mass window for pure wino dark matter is M_Wino = 2.7–2.9 TeV (from Fan:2013faa and constraint references).
- The wino-nucleon scattering cross section is sigma ~ 10^(-47) cm^2, far below current direct detection limits (Hisano:2011cs, Hill:2013hoa).
- The HESS experiment energy resolution at 3 TeV is approximately 400 GeV, too poor to distinguish two-body from n-body annihilation.
- The leading-log Sudakov suppression factor for the nonsinglet operator C_2 at the low scale is exp[-(3g^2/(4pi^2)) log^2(M_chi/M_W)], which for M_chi ~ 3 TeV gives a few-percent reduction relative to tree level plus Sommerfeld enhancement.
- The Sommerfeld enhancement can increase the rate by as much as O(10^4) relative to a perturbative calculation.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous factorization theorem (Baumgart:2014vma) for semi-inclusive wino annihilation and provides a systematic calculation of all leading radiative corrections. It contradicts the fixed-order NLO calculation of Hryczuk:2011vi and Cohen:2013ama, which found a 75% reduction relative to tree level plus Sommerfeld enhancement and motivated the need for resummation. The results enable more reliable constraints on wino dark matter from HESS observations, allowing the authors to determine how much coring in the dark matter halo profile is needed to make the wino a viable candidate as a function of its mass and wino fraction.
