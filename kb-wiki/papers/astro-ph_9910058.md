# Electron Capture Rates of Mid-fp Shell Nuclei for Supernova and Stellar Evolution

> A detailed model is constructed for the calculation of electron capture rates of some fp-shell nuclei for situations prevailing in pre-supernova and collapse phases of the evolution of the core of massive stars leading to supernova explosion. The model uses explicitly the Gamow-Teller strength function obtained through (n,p) reaction studies wherever available. The rates include contribution from the excited states of the mother as well as from the resonant states in equilibrium with the back reaction i.e. the beta decay of the daughter nucleus. Comparisons are made with the shell model results and the earlier calculations by Aufderheide et al. and Fuller, Fowler and Newman. For the nuclei $^{56}$Fe, $^{55}$Mn and $^{60}$Ni with negative Q-values one observes large contribution from the excited states.

# Electron Capture Rates of Mid-fp Shell Nuclei for Supernova and Stellar Evolution

## Key claim

The authors construct a detailed model for electron capture rates on fp-shell nuclei that uses experimentally measured Gamow-Teller strength distributions from (n,p) reaction data directly, rather than theoretical strength functions, and find that for nuclei with negative Q-values (56Fe, 55Mn, 60Ni) the contribution from excited states of the mother nucleus becomes dominant at low densities where the electron chemical potential is too small to drive capture from the ground state.

## Method

The electron capture rate is calculated using the standard phase-space integral with the Gamow-Teller strength distribution taken directly from (n,p) reaction histograms for the ground state of the mother nucleus, while excited-state contributions are modeled using Gaussian strength distributions based on the Brink hypothesis with centroids and widths from fits to (n,p) data. The model also includes resonant-state contributions from the back reaction (beta decay of the daughter nucleus) using the formalism of Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1982), with a quenching factor of 0.6 applied to the Gamow-Teller matrix elements. Rates are computed for four densities (log rho10 from -2.5 to 0.5) and five temperatures (T9 = 2 to 6) at fixed electron fraction Ye = 0.45.

## Key numbers

* For 56Fe at T9 = 2 and log rho10 = 0.5, total electron capture rate = 231.1 s^-1 (ground state contribution 227.7 s^-1); at log rho10 = -2.5, total rate = 8.21 x 10^-11 s^-1 (ground state 1.13 x 10^-11 s^-1), showing excited states dominate at low density.
* For 55Mn at T9 = 2 and log rho10 = 0.5, total rate = 110.6 s^-1 (ground state 108.1 s^-1); at log rho10 = -2.5, total rate = 1.46 x 10^-9 s^-1 (ground state 4.00 x 10^-10 s^-1).
* For 60Ni at T9 = 2 and log rho10 = 0.5, total rate = 416.1 s^-1 (ground state 415.5 s^-1); at log rho10 = -2.5, total rate = 4.99 x 10^-9 s^-1 (ground state 9.64 x 10^-10 s^-1).
* For 56Fe at density 1.07 x 10^8 g/cc and T9 = 3.65, the present model gives 1.42 x 10^-5 s^-1, compared to 4.68 x 10^-7 s^-1 from Aufderheide et al. (1994) and 2.1 x 10^-6 s^-1 from Martinez-Pinedo et al. (1999).
* For 60Ni at density 5.86 x 10^7 g/cc and T9 = 3.40, the present model gives 2.17 x 10^-5 s^-1, compared to 1.49 x 10^-6 s^-1 from Aufderheide et al. (1994) and 1.09 x 10^-3 s^-1 from Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1982).
* For 55Mn at density 1.07 x 10^8 g/cc and T9 = 3.65, the present model gives 1.52 x 10^-5 s^-1, compared to 9.23 x 10^-6 s^-1 from Aufderheide et al. (1994).
* The Gamow-Teller back resonance contributions are essentially zero for all three nuclei (e.g., 0.10 s^-1 for GT and 0.00 s^-1 for Fermi in 60Ni at T9 = 6 and log rho10 = 0.5), because the resonance excitation energies (5.38, 6.71, and 5.29 MeV for 56Fe, 55Mn, and 60Ni respectively) are too high to contribute significantly.

## Context

This work extends the beta-decay model of Kar, Ray and Sarkar (1994) to the electron capture sector, incorporating the key improvement of using experimentally measured (n,p) Gamow-Teller strength distributions directly rather than theoretical forms. The results are compared with the earlier calculations of Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1982) and Aufderheide et al. (1994), as well as with the shell-model calculations of Martinez-Pinedo, Langanke and Dean (1999). The authors find that for even-even nuclei (56Fe, 60Ni) their rates are more than an order of magnitude higher than those of Aufderheide et al. (1994), while for the odd-A nucleus 55Mn the rates agree within a factor of two. The work enables future identification of the sets of important nuclei for presupernova evolution using these rates together with nuclear statistical equilibrium, which may show important differences from the sets prescribed by Aufderheide et al. (1994).
