# Quantum deformation of the Dirac bracket

> The quantum deformation of the Poisson bracket is the Moyal bracket. We construct quantum deformation of the Dirac bracket for systems which admit global symplectic basis for constraint functions. Equivalently, it can be considered as an extension of the Moyal bracket to second-class constraints systems and to gauge-invariant systems which become second class when gauge-fixing conditions are imposed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a quantum deformation of the Dirac bracket for second-class constraint systems that admit a global symplectic basis for the constraint functions, extending the Moyal bracket formalism to constrained systems. The construction is valid locally for any second-class constraint system, but the global existence of the symplectic basis remains an open question in the general case.

## Method

The authors define skew-gradient projections of phase-space functions onto the constraint submanifold using phase flows generated by constraint functions in a symplectic basis. They then construct equivalence classes of functions in the unconstrained phase space, where functions are equivalent if their projections coincide, and show that the Moyal bracket between projected functions serves as the quantum deformation of the Dirac bracket. The formalism is applied to the Weyl association rule and the Groenewold star-product to establish the quantum evolution equation in terms of the Moyal bracket of the projected Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The constraint functions satisfy the Poisson bracket relation {G_a, G_b} = I_ab, where I_ab is the 2m x 2m symplectic matrix with block form [[0, E_m], [-E_m, 0]].
- The lowest-order quantum correction to the projected function f_t is O(hbar^2), given explicitly as f_t = f - (hbar^2/8){f, G^{a1}} P^2 G_{a1} - ... + O(hbar^4) on the constraint submanifold.
- The projection operator P is expressed as an integral over 2m variables: P = integral d^{2m}lambda/(2pi hbar)^m prod_a exp(i/hbar G^a lambda_a).

## Context

This work extends the Moyal quantization formalism of Groenewold (1946) and Moyal (1949) to second-class constraint systems, building on Dirac's Hamiltonian approach to gauge theories and constrained systems (Dirac, 1950). The construction enables the formulation of quantum evolution equations in the unconstrained phase space for systems such as the O(n) non-linear sigma model and the n-1-dimensional spherical pendulum, which are second-class from the start. The paper also relates to the path integral quantization methods of Batalin and Fradkin for second-class constraints.
