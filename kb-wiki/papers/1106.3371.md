# Longitudinal Fluctuations in Partonic and Hadronic Initial State

> Collective flow in collisions between Lead nuclei at LHC are influenced by random initial state fluctuations, especially for odd harmonics. Here we extend fluctuation studies to longitudinal fluctuations, which may have significant effect on the rapidity distribution of odd harmonics. Furthermore center of mass rapidity fluctuations are measurable, but not yet analysed. Here in the PACIAE parton and hadron molecular dynamics model we make an analysis of initial state fluctuations. As previous analyses discussed mainly the effects of fluctuations on eccentricity and the elliptic flow we pay particular attention to the fluctuations of the Center of Mass rapidity of the system, which is conservatively estimated in our model as Δy_{CM} = 0.1, by neglecting all pre-equilibrium emission effects that are increasing the y_{CM} fluctuations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that center-of-mass rapidity fluctuations in the initial state of heavy-ion collisions are a conservative lower limit of $\Delta y_{CM} = 0.1$ in the PACIAE model, because all pre-equilibrium emission effects that would increase the fluctuations are neglected.

## Method

The authors used the PACIAE (Parton And Hadron Cascade) model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies (1.38+1.38 A·TeV) and Au+Au collisions at RHIC energies (100+100 A·GeV). The model geometrically determines participant nucleons from projectile and target nuclei, then decomposes the collision into nucleon-nucleon pairs using PYTHIA with string fragmentation switched off, producing a partonic initial state of quarks, anti-quarks, and gluons. The CM-rapidity fluctuations were computed from the asymmetry in participant nucleon numbers between projectile and target.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at 1.38+1.38 A·TeV, the relative number fluctuation $|\delta N| / \langle N_{part} \rangle$ is approximately 1.4%.
- For peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at 1.38+1.38 A·TeV (60-70% centrality), the relative number fluctuation is approximately 6%.
- For central Au+Au collisions at 100+100 A·GeV, the relative number fluctuation is approximately 1.5%.
- For peripheral Au+Au collisions at 100+100 A·GeV (70-80% centrality), the relative number fluctuation is approximately 5%.
- The CM-rapidity fluctuation for central Pb+Pb collisions at 1.38+1.38 A·TeV is approximately $\pm 0.03$ units.
- The CM-rapidity fluctuation for peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at 1.38+1.38 A·TeV (60-70% centrality) is approximately $\pm 0.1$ units.
- In the PACIAE model for 60-70% peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, 57.6% of total collision energy is shared by quarks and anti-quarks and 42.4% by gluons; for 0-5% central collisions the values are 57.9% and 42.1% respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of initial state fluctuations that focused on eccentricity and elliptic flow (e.g., Qin (2010), Petersen (2011), Grassi (2011)) to longitudinal fluctuations in the beam direction. The analysis provides a conservative lower limit estimate of CM-rapidity fluctuations using the PACIAE model, which is about 5-10 times smaller than assumptions used in earlier work by the authors (Csernai et al., 2011) on directed flow. The results enable experimental measurement of event-by-event CM-rapidity fluctuations as a tool to distinguish between tightly bound and loosely connected initial state models, and are essential for separating global directed flow from fluctuation-driven directed flow in $v_1$ analyses at RHIC and LHC.
