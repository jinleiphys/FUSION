# Electromagnetic Single-nucleon Response involving Polarized Targets

> This work is an extension of our past study focused on a covariant representation of the electromagnetic (EM) current of spin-1/2 Dirac particles, specifically, nucleons. In the past study the EM responses that occur in unpolarized electron scattering from unpolarized nucleons were derived; however, scattering of polarized electrons from polarized nucleons was beyond the scope of that earlier work. Here such extensions are studied in detail. While in other work the EM response has already been developed for the double-polarization scattering problem, that effort was focused on high-energy collider physics. In the present study the formalism is recast into a set of EM response functions that have transparent dependencies on the relevant kinematic variables, especially on how these behave with respect to the momentum p of the (moving) struck, polarized nucleon. The motivation for such a reformulation of the problem is the desire to see a clear path to expansions in $p$ of the EM response for use in devising ``prescriptions for nuclear physics''. Results are provided where comparisons of the full (unexpanded) responses with various approximations that are frequently employed in studies of EM nuclear physics are made, demonstrating that under some circumstances such approximations are reasonable, whereas in other circumstances the expanded results are likely to be invalid. In addition, the EM current operators and approximations to them are discussed in detail.

## Key claim

This work extends a previous covariant formalism for electromagnetic single-nucleon responses from unpolarized scattering to the case of polarized electrons scattering from polarized nucleons, but with the crucial condition that the formalism is recast to expose transparent dependencies on the momentum **p** of the struck, polarized nucleon, specifically to enable expansions in \(p\) for use in nuclear-physics prescriptions. The authors demonstrate that commonly used approximations for the electromagnetic current operators are reasonable only under specific kinematic circumstances and are likely invalid in the quasielastic regime relevant to modern GeV-scale electron scattering and neutrino reactions.

## Method

The authors derive the full, unexpanded electromagnetic current matrix elements for an on-shell spin-1/2 nucleon using the covariant representation with Dirac and Pauli form factors \(F_1, F_2\) (or Sachs form factors \(G_E, G_M\)), expressed in terms of dimensionless variables \(\tau, \eta, \theta\). They then construct the hadronic tensor and all nine response functions (unpolarized and polarized) in both the standard 123-coordinate system and the rotated 1'2'3'-system aligned with the hadron plane, using the bilinear products of the current operators. The exact results are compared with expansions to linear order in \(\eta = p/m_N\), and the authors evaluate the validity of the "prescription for nuclear physics" where the momentum operator is replaced by \(-i \nabla\) in coordinate space.

## Key numbers

* For the isovector sector, the leading-order charge contribution is proportional to \(G_E^v = G_D\), while the next-to-leading-order (linear in \(\eta\)) contribution is proportional to \(G_M^v - \frac{1}{2} G_E^v = 4.20 G_D\), using \(\mu^v = \mu_p - \mu_n = 4.70\).
* With typical Fermi momentum \(\eta_F \approx 0.25\), the linear-order contribution is roughly \(4.20 \times 0.25 \sim 1.05\) relative to the leading-order \(\sim 1\), giving approximately 25% effects from \({\cal O}(\eta^1)\) versus \({\cal O}(\eta^0)\) contributions, while \({\cal O}(\eta^2)\) contributions are only a few percent.
* For a 250 GeV proton colliding with an energetic electron, \(\eta \cong 266\), and the quantity \(\Xi = \sqrt{\tau/(1+\tau)} \delta\) becomes unity at a scattering angle of about \(0.22^\circ\), indicating that at larger angles the expansion in \(\eta\) fails and all terms must be retained.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Donnelly and Jeschonnek, 2024) which derived only unpolarized electromagnetic responses, and it recasts the double-polarization formalism previously developed for high-energy collider physics (Sofiatti, 2011) into a form suitable for nuclear-physics applications. The results enable systematic expansions in the nucleon momentum \(p\) for use in quasielastic electron scattering and neutrino reaction calculations, and they clarify the limitations of older approximations such as those in DeForest (1966) and McVoy, which assumed \(\omega \sim m_N^{-1}\) and expanded only to \({\cal O}(m_N^{-2})\), making them invalid for the modern GeV-scale regime where \(\lambda\) and \(\tau\) are not small.
