# Interplay of projectile breakup and target excitation in reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei

> In this work, we reexamine the extension of the CDCC formalism to include target excitation and apply it to a variety of reactions to study the effect of breakup on inelastic cross sections. We use a transformed oscillator basis to discretize the continuum of the projectiles in the different reactions and use the extended CDCC method developed in this work to solve the resulting coupled differential equations. A new code has been developed to perform the calculations. Reactions 58Ni(d, d) 58Ni*, 24Mg(d, d) 24Mg* , 144Sm( 6Li, 6Li) 144Sm* and 9Be( 6Li, 6Li) 9Be* are studied. Satisfactory agreement is found between experimental data and extended CDCC calculations. The studied CDCC method is proved to be an accurate tool to describe target excitation in reactions with weakly-bound nuclei. Moderate effects of breakup on inelastic observables are found for the reactions studied. Cross section magnitudes are not modified much, but angular distributions present smoothing when opposed to calculations without breakup.

## Key claim

The extended CDCC formalism that treats projectile breakup and target excitation on equal footing produces satisfactory agreement with experimental data for several reactions, but the effect of breakup on inelastic observables is only moderate: cross section magnitudes are not modified much, while angular distributions present smoothing when compared to calculations without breakup.

## Method

The authors extended the Continuum-Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) method to include target excitation by deriving coupling potentials that treat projectile breakup and collective target excitation consistently, using a j-j coupling scheme. The projectile continuum was discretized using a transformed harmonic oscillator basis (pseudo-state method), and the resulting coupled differential equations were solved with a newly developed code. The method was applied to four reactions: 58Ni(d,d)58Ni* at 80 MeV, 24Mg(d,d)24Mg* at 70 MeV, 144Sm(6Li,6Li)144Sm* at 23, 28, 30, and 35 MeV, and 9Be(6Li,6Li)9Be*.

## Key numbers

- 58Ni(d,d)58Ni* at Ed=80 MeV: deformation parameter beta=0.179 for 58Ni; only p-n states up to 40 MeV and orbital angular momentum l=0-2 were considered, with l=1,4 contributions found negligible.
- 24Mg(d,d)24Mg* at Ed=70 MeV: deformation parameter beta=0.5 for 24Mg; hexadecapolar deformation beta4=-0.017 for the 4+ state; inclusion of the 4+ state produced a small reduction of the inelastic cross section at the peak.
- 144Sm(6Li,6Li)144Sm* at four energies (23, 28, 30, 35 MeV): deformation parameters beta2=0.087 and beta3=0.130 for 144Sm; 6Li continuum discretized up to 10 MeV considering only s and d waves; ground state of 6Li at E=-1.47 MeV and d-wave resonance at E=0.7 MeV.
- For 144Sm(6Li,6Li)144Sm* at 23 MeV, a deuteron-target imaginary potential renormalization factor of Nid=0.5 was needed for good agreement, while Nid=0.2 worked for 28, 30, and 35 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC formalism originally developed by the Kyushu-Pittsburg group (Yahiro, 1986) for deuteron scattering to more general reactions with heavier projectiles, using a full j-j coupling scheme without the approximations previously employed. The method enables consistent treatment of both breakup and target excitation in reactions such as 144Sm(6Li,6Li)144Sm*, which had previously been studied by decoupling these effects (Woodard, 2012). The calculations are compared with Faddeev/AGS calculations by Deltuva (2016) for the 24Mg(d,d)24Mg* reaction, and the extended CDCC method is found to be computationally less demanding while producing comparable results.
