# Stability of multicomponent Israel-Stewart-Maxwell theory for charge diffusion

> We obtain stability criteria for diffusive inviscid multicomponent Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics with and without background or dynamic electromagnetic fields. Our analysis is grounded on the maximum entropy principle, and it provides stability conditions that are valid around all thermodynamic equilibria, including rotating equilibria, charged equilibria, and equilibria in a background gravitational field. We prove that the electromagnetic part of the information current is stable and causal by construction and, therefore, the stability criteria found for Israel-Stewart theories of hydrodynamics automatically extend to similar formulations of magnetohydrodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the stability criteria for multicomponent Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics automatically extend to formulations with dynamic electromagnetic fields (Israel-Stewart-Maxwell theory), because the electromagnetic part of the information current is stable and causal by construction for non-polarizable and non-magnetizable media.

## Method

The authors apply the information current method, grounded on the maximum entropy principle, to derive linear stability criteria for diffusive inviscid multicomponent Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics. They first derive the information current for the multicomponent Israel-Stewart theory without electromagnetic fields, then show it can be transformed into the information current of Carter's multifluid theory via a change of variables. Finally, they extend the analysis to include background and dynamic electromagnetic fields, proving that the electromagnetic contribution to the information current does not alter the stability conditions.

## Key numbers

- The adiabatic sound speed squared must satisfy \(c_s^2 = \partial P / \partial \varepsilon |_{n_A/s} \in (0,1]\) for stability
- The specific heat at constant particle densities must satisfy \(c_v = T \partial s / \partial T |_{n_A} > 0\)
- The enthalpy density must satisfy \(\varepsilon + P > 0\) for stability against spontaneous acceleration
- The three \(l \times l\) symmetric matrices \(\partial \mu^A / \partial n_B |_T\), \(b^{AB}\), and \(b^{AB} - \partial \mu^A / \partial n_B |_T\) must be positive definite for diffusive stability (the third matrix may be semi-definite)

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics to multicomponent fluids with charge diffusion, building on the information current method developed by Hiscock (1983) and Olson (1990). It generalizes the single-species stability results of Olson to multiple chemical species and proves that the stability conditions remain valid when electromagnetic fields are included, agreeing with results of Biswas (2022) while extending them to resistive fluids and inhomogeneous equilibria. The analysis enables numerical simulations of relativistic heavy-ion collisions at lower energies and neutron star mergers where multiple conserved charges (baryon number, electric charge, strangeness) must be tracked simultaneously.
