# Unexpected Rise in Nuclear Collectivity from Short-Range Physics

> We discover a surprising relation between the collective motion of nucleons within atomic nuclei, traditionally understood to be driven by long-range correlations, and short-range nucleon-nucleon interactions. Specifically, we find that quadrupole collectivity in low-lying states of $^6$Li and $^{12}$C, calculated with state-of-the-art ab initio techniques, is significantly influenced by two opposing $S$-wave contact couplings that subtly alter the surface oscillations of one largely deformed nuclear shape, without changing that shape's overall contribution within the nucleus. The results offer new insights into the nature of emergent nuclear collectivity and its link to the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction at short distances.

# Unexpected Rise in Nuclear Collectivity from Short-Range Physics

## Key claim
The authors find that quadrupole collectivity in low-lying states of ⁶Li and ¹²C can be enhanced by up to ~50% through two opposing S-wave contact couplings in the chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction, but this enhancement occurs without changing the overall mixing of nuclear shapes, instead, it subtly alters the surface oscillations (polar versus equatorial modes) of one dominant deformed shape.

## Method
The authors performed ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model (SA-NCSM) calculations for ⁶Li and ¹²C using the NNLO_opt chiral nucleon-nucleon potential, varying 11 low-energy constants (LECs) associated with S-wave, P-wave, and tensor contact interactions uniformly within ±10% of their NNLO_opt values. They conducted global sensitivity analysis (GSA) using 300,000 samples generated via Saltelli's procedure, computing first-order and total-order sensitivity indices for quadrupole moments and energies. Individual LEC response curves were obtained by varying one LEC at a time while fixing others to their NNLO_opt values, using 300 samples per LEC via Latin hypercube design.

## Key numbers
- The dominant nuclear shape contributes 82.2%–86.6% to the ⁶Li 1⁺_g.s. state across the 10% LEC variations, showing resilience of shape probabilities.
- Quadrupole moments of the ⁶Li 3⁺_1 and ¹²C 2⁺_1 states can be enhanced by up to ~50% through the two S-wave contact interactions.
- Many-body model uncertainties in the GSA calculations are 2%–15%, compared to a 13% many-body model uncertainty reported in prior ab initio estimates for Q₂.
- The 8-shell model spaces were sufficient for GSA, with 14 shapes recovering about 97% of total probabilities for ⁶Li and about 80% for ¹²C.
- Calculations used a single harmonic oscillator energy ℏΩ = 15 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the finding by Dytrych et al. (2020) that collective phenomena emerge from ab initio calculations, and contradicts the traditional assumption that collectivity is driven solely by long-range correlations and shape mixing. The results show that short-range S-wave contact interactions, specifically the attractive LO ³S₁ and repulsive NLO ¹S₀ couplings, can significantly alter collectivity through surface oscillation dynamics rather than shape deformation changes. This enables the use of quadrupole moments as observables to constrain chiral potentials, and provides a direct link between QCD-rooted short-range physics and emergent nuclear collectivity, building on the chiral EFT framework developed by Bedaque and van Kolck (2002), Entem and Machleidt (2003), and Epelbaum et al. (2014).
