# Pseudogauge freedom and the SO(3) algebra of spin operators

> The energy-momentum and spin tensors for a given theory can be replaced by alternative expressions that obey the same conservation laws for the energy, linear momentum, as well as angular momentum but, however, differ by the local redistribution of such quantities (with global energy, linear momentum, and angular momentum remaining unchanged). This arbitrariness is described in recent literature as the pseudogauge freedom or symmetry. In this letter, we analyze several pseudogauges used to formulate the relativistic hydrodynamics of particles with spin 1/2 and conclude that the canonical version of the spin tensor has an advantage over other forms as only the canonical definition defines the spin operators that fulfill the SO(3) algebra of angular momentum. This result sheds new light on the results encountered in recent papers demonstrating pseudogauge dependence of various physical quantities. It indicates that for spin-polarization observables, the canonical version is fundamentally better suited for building a connection between theory and experiment.

## Key claim

The canonical version of the spin tensor has a specific advantage over other pseudogauges (GLW and HW) used in relativistic spin hydrodynamics because only the canonical definition yields spin operators that satisfy the fundamental SO(3) angular momentum commutation relations, making it fundamentally better suited for connecting theory with experiment for spin-polarization observables.

## Method

The authors analyzed three different pseudogauges (canonical, de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Weert (GLW), and Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen (HW)) used to formulate relativistic hydrodynamics of spin-1/2 particles described by the Dirac equation. They explicitly computed the equal-time commutation relations for the total spin operators defined in each pseudogauge, using the fundamental anticommutation relations of the Dirac field operators, to test whether the spin operators satisfy the SO(3) algebra.

## Key numbers

- The canonical spin operator satisfies [S^i(t), S^j(t)] = 2i ε^{ijk} S^k(t), reproducing the SO(3) algebra via the relation [Σ^i, Σ^j] = 2i ε^{ijk} Σ^k for the Σ matrices
- The GLW spin operator commutator [Φ^m_GLW(t), Φ^n_GLW(t)] yields a non-vanishing term (2i/m^2) ε^{mnj} ∫ d^3x (∂_x^j ψ†) Σ^l ∂_x^l ψ, which generally does not vanish for spin-polarized systems
- The HW and GLW total spin operators are equal: S^k_GLW(t) = S^k_HW(t), so only the canonical case needs to be checked against the GLW case

## Context

This work extends the discussion of pseudogauge freedom in relativistic spin hydrodynamics, which was reviewed by Florkowski (2018), Speranza (2020), and Bhadury (2021). It directly addresses the pseudogauge dependence of physical quantities found in quantum calculations by Buzzegoli (2021) and Das (2021), suggesting that quantum treatments using pseudogauges that break the SO(3) algebra are fundamentally inconsistent. The results indicate that while all pseudogauges are equivalent at the level of field equations, the canonical pseudogauge is uniquely suitable for describing spin degrees of freedom when comparing theory to experiment, whereas other pseudogauges (like GLW) may be more convenient for determining system dynamics due to their conserved spin tensors.
