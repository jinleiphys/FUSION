# Bayesian inference of nucleus resonance and neutron skin

> In this proceeding, we have presented some highlight results on the constraints of the nuclear matter equation of state (EOS) from the data of nucleus resonance and neutron-skin thickness using the Bayesian approach based on the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock model and its extension. Typically, we have discussed the anti-correlation and positive correlation between the slope parameter and the value of the symmetry energy at the saturation density under the constraint of, respectively, the neutron-skin thickness and the isovector giant dipole resonance. We have shown that the Bayesian analysis can help to find a compromise for the ``PREXII puzzle'' and the ``soft Tin puzzle". We have further illustrated the possible modifications on the constraints of lower-order EOS parameters as well as the relevant correlation when higher-order EOS parameters are incorporated as independent variables. For a given model and parameter space, the Bayesian approach serves as a good analysis tool suitable for multi-messengers versus multi-variables, and is helpful for constraining quantitatively the model parameters as well as their correlations.

# Bayesian inference of nucleus resonance and neutron skin

## Key claim

The Bayesian analysis of nucleus resonance and neutron-skin data can find a compromise for both the "PREXII puzzle" and the "soft Tin puzzle", but this compatibility relies on the large experimental error bars; if the errors were reduced while the mean values remained unchanged, the puzzles would become more significant.

## Method

The study employed the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) model and its extension (the KIDS model) to compute nuclear structure observables, then used Bayesian inference with Markov-Chain Monte Carlo sampling to obtain posterior probability distribution functions of nuclear matter equation of state parameters. The theoretical results for neutron-skin thickness, isovector giant dipole resonance centroid energy and electric polarizability, and isoscalar giant monopole resonance excitation energy were compared to experimental data via a likelihood function with 1-sigma experimental errors.

## Key numbers

- Neutron-skin thickness of 208Pb: Δr_np = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm from parity-violating electron scattering (PREXII)
- Neutron-skin thickness of 48Ca: Δr_np = 0.121 ± 0.026 (model) ± 0.024 (theo) fm from parity-violating electron scattering (CREX)
- Slope parameter L from combined neutron-skin data: L = 51.6 +28.7 -19.9 MeV (68% confidence, mean value) or L = 61.5 +18.8 -29.8 MeV (68% confidence, MAP value)
- The neutron-skin data constrains the slope parameter L* at subsaturation density ρ* = 0.10 fm^-3, where L* becomes approximately independent of E_sym^0
- The IVGDR data constrains the symmetry energy E_sym(ρ*) at subsaturation density ρ* = 0.05 fm^-3, where E_sym(ρ*) becomes approximately independent of L

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses by Xu and collaborators (2020-2022) that extracted nuclear matter EOS information from ISGMR, IVGDR, and neutron-skin data using the SHF model. It addresses the "soft Tin puzzle" (where ISGMR in Sn isotopes gives smaller incompressibility than in heavy nuclei, noted by Piekarewicz 2010 and Garg 2007) and the "PREXII puzzle" (where the large Δr_np in 208Pb from PREXII implies a large slope parameter L, contradicting results from electric dipole polarizability and CREX data on 48Ca). The work enables future studies incorporating astrophysical data to constrain the EOS from low to high densities, and calls for similar studies using relativistic mean-field models to explore model dependence.
