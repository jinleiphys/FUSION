# Nucleon numbers for nuclei with shape coexistence

> We consider two competing sets of nuclear magic numbers, namely the harmonic oscillator (HO) set (2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112, 168, 240,...) and the set corresponding to the proxy-SU(3) scheme, possessing shells 0-2, 2-4, 6-12, 14-26, 28-48, 50-80, 82-124, 126-182, 184-256... The two sets provide 0+ bands with different deformation and band-head energies. We show that for proton (neutron) numbers starting from the regions where the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, as derived by the HO, becomes weaker than the one obtained in the proxy-SU(3) scheme, to the regions of HO shell closure, the shape coexistence phenomenon may emerge. Our analysis suggests that the possibility for appearance of shape coexistence has to be investigated in the following regions of proton (neutron) numbers: 8, 18-20, 34-40, 60-70, 96-112, 146-168, 210-240,...

## Key claim

The paper proposes that shape coexistence in nuclei emerges in specific proton or neutron number regions where the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength calculated from the harmonic oscillator (HO) magic numbers becomes weaker than that obtained from the proxy-SU(3) scheme, with the phenomenon persisting up to the HO shell closure.

## Method

The authors compare two competing sets of nuclear magic numbers: the harmonic oscillator set (2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112, 168, 240, ...) and the proxy-SU(3) set (shells 0-2, 2-4, 6-12, 14-26, 28-48, 50-80, 82-124, 126-182, 184-256, ...). For each set, they calculate the eigenvalues of the second-order Casimir operator of SU(3), which is proportional to the quadrupole-quadrupole interaction strength and thus to nuclear deformation. They identify the nucleon numbers where the HO-derived Casimir eigenvalue falls below the proxy-SU(3) value, and define these as the onset regions for shape coexistence.

## Key numbers

* The harmonic oscillator magic numbers are: 2, 8, 20, 40, 70, 112, 168, 240, ...
* The proxy-SU(3) magic numbers below 50 are: 2, 4, 6, 12, 14, 26, 28, 48
* Shape coexistence is predicted to appear in the following proton or neutron number regions: 8, 18-20, 34-40, 60-70, 96-112, 146-168, 210-240, ...
* The proxy-SU(3) shells close at 48, 80, 124, ... which are close to the nuclear magic numbers 50, 82, 126, ...

## Context

This work extends the proxy-SU(3) scheme developed by Bonatsos et al. (2017) and the earlier review of shape coexistence by Heyde and Wood (2011). The predicted nucleon number regions for shape coexistence are shown to correspond to experimentally observed cases, such as Hg isotopes (96-110 neutrons) and Sn isotopes (60-70 neutrons), as documented in the review by Wood (1992) and the work of Heyde and Wood (2011). The analysis enables targeted experimental searches for shape coexistence in the newly identified regions, particularly at higher nucleon numbers.
