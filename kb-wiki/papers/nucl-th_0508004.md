# Global properties of the Skyrme-force-induced nuclear symmetry energy

> Large scale calculations are performed to establish the global mass dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, $a_{sym}(A)$, which in turn depends on two basic ingredients: the mean-level spacing, $ε(A)$, and the effective strength of the isovector mean-potential, $κ(A)$. Surprisingly, our results reveal that in modern parameterizations including SLy4, SkO, SkXc, and SkP these two basic ingredients of $a_{sym}$ are almost equal after rescaling them linearly by the isoscalar and the isovector effective masses, respectively. This result points toward a new fundamental property of the nuclear interaction that remains to be resolved. In addition, our analysis determines the ratio of the surface-to-volume contributions to $a_{sym}$ to be $\sim$1.6, consistent with hydrodynamical estimates for the static dipole polarizability as well as the neutron-skin.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for modern Skyrme-force parameterizations (SLy4, SkO, SkXc, SkP), the two basic ingredients of the nuclear symmetry energy, the mean-level spacing and the effective strength of the isovector mean-potential, are nearly equal after rescaling by the isoscalar and isovector effective masses, respectively. This near-equality is presented as a surprising, apparently fundamental property of the nuclear interaction that remains unexplained, but the authors note it does not hold for older parameterizations like SIII and SkM*.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale Skyrme-Hartree-Fock (SHF) calculations for all even-even nuclei with 20 ≤ A ≤ 128, from N=Z to near the neutron drip line, using six different Skyrme force parameterizations (SkP, SkXc, SLy4, SkO, SkM*, SIII). They extracted the mean-level spacing ε(A) by setting all isovector coupling constants to zero and comparing excitation energies, then extracted the effective isovector mean-potential strength κ(A) by comparing total SHF binding energies with and without isovector contributions. Coulomb and pairing effects were disregarded to focus on the strong interaction in the particle-hole channel.

## Key numbers

- The surface-to-volume ratio of the symmetry energy, r_{S/V} = a_{sym}^{(S)} / a_{sym}^{(V)}, is approximately 1.6, consistent across modern parameterizations (e.g., 1.51 for SLy4, 1.53 for SkXc, 1.57 for SkP and SkO).
- The volume expansion coefficient ε_V* (isoscalar-effective-mass-scaled mean-level spacing) is approximately 100 MeV for modern forces, corresponding to the pure Fermi gas estimate; specific values include 94.5 MeV (SLy4), 108.6 MeV (SkXc), 108.8 MeV (SkP), 107.2 MeV (SkO).
- The infinite nuclear matter symmetry energy a_{sym}^{(∞)} is 32.0 MeV for SLy4, 30.1 MeV for SkXc, 30.0 MeV for SkP, and 32.0 MeV for SkO.
- The semi-classical estimate for the ratio r_ε = ε_S*/ε_V* gives approximately 1.52, close to the calculated values (e.g., 1.56 for SLy4, 1.51 for SkXc, 1.61 for SkP).
- The experimental value of σ_{-2} A^{-5/3} is approximately 2.7 ± 0.2 μb/MeV for A ≥ 100, yielding r_{S/V} ~ 1.65 from hydrodynamical estimates.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous letter (Satula 2003) that demonstrated the SHF symmetry energy follows a specific formula involving ε and κ. The results contradict the wide variation in surface-to-volume ratios quoted in the literature, for example, Danielewicz (2003) estimated 2.0 ≤ r_{S/V} ≤ 2.8, while the mass formula of Moller (1995) gave r_{S/V} ≈ 1.6. The finding that ε* ≈ κ* for modern forces points toward a new fundamental property of the nuclear mean field that requires further theoretical explanation, and the established r_{S/V} ≈ 1.6 enables consistent hydrodynamical estimates for the static dipole polarizability and neutron-skin thickness.
