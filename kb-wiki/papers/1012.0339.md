# Search for effects beyond the Born approximation in polarization transfer observables in $\vec{e}p$ elastic scattering

> Intensive theoretical and experimental efforts over the past decade have aimed at explaining the discrepancy between data for the proton electric to magnetic form factor ratio, $G_{E}/G_{M}$, obtained separately from cross section and polarization transfer measurements. One possible explanation for this difference is a two-photon-exchange (TPEX) contribution. In an effort to search for effects beyond the one-photon-exchange or Born approximation, we report measurements of polarization transfer observables in the elastic $H(\vec{e},e'\vec{p})$ reaction for three different beam energies at a fixed squared momentum transfer $Q^2 = 2.5$ GeV$^2$, spanning a wide range of the virtual photon polarization parameter, $ε$. From these measured polarization observables, we have obtained separately the ratio $R$, which equals $μ_p G_{E}/G_{M}$ in the Born approximation, and the longitudinal polarization transfer component $P_\ell$, with statistical and systematic uncertainties of $ΔR \approx \pm 0.01 \mbox{(stat)} \pm 0.013 \mbox{(syst)}$ and $ΔP_\ell/P^{Born}_{\ell} \approx \pm 0.006 \mbox{(stat)}\pm 0.01 \mbox{(syst)}$. The ratio $R$ is found to be independent of $ε$ at the 1.5% level, while the $ε$ dependence of $P_\ell$ shows an enhancement of $(2.3 \pm 0.6) %$ relative to the Born approximation at large $ε$.

## Key claim
The ratio \(R = \mu_p G_E/G_M\) is found to be independent of the virtual photon polarization parameter \(\epsilon\) at the 1.5% level at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\), while the longitudinal polarization transfer component \(P_\ell\) shows a \((2.3 \pm 0.6)\%\) enhancement relative to the Born approximation at large \(\epsilon\), indicating possible two-photon-exchange effects beyond the one-photon-exchange approximation.

## Method
The experiment used a longitudinally polarized electron beam (82-86% polarization) scattered elastically off a 20 cm liquid hydrogen target at Jefferson Lab Hall C, at a fixed \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) for three beam energies (1.87, 2.84, and 3.63 GeV) spanning a wide range of \(\epsilon\) (0.15 to 0.785). Electrons were detected by a lead-glass electromagnetic calorimeter (BigCal), and coincident protons were detected in the High Momentum Spectrometer (HMS) with its Focal Plane Polarimeter (FPP) measuring the transferred polarization via proton re-scattering in CH\(_2\) analyzer blocks. The spin transport matrix of the HMS was calculated using the differential-algebra based program COSY to extract the transverse (\(P_t\)) and longitudinal (\(P_\ell\)) polarization components at the target.

## Key numbers
- Ratio \(R = \mu_p G_E/G_M\) at \(\epsilon = 0.152\): \(0.696 \pm 0.009\) (stat) \(\pm 0.006\) (point-to-point syst), total syst \(\pm 0.013\)
- Ratio \(R\) at \(\epsilon = 0.635\): \(0.688 \pm 0.011\) (stat) \(\pm 0.001\) (point-to-point syst), total syst \(\pm 0.009\)
- Ratio \(R\) at \(\epsilon = 0.785\): \(0.692 \pm 0.011\) (stat) \(\pm 0.000\) (point-to-point syst), total syst \(\pm 0.009\)
- One-parameter fit result for \(R\): \(0.6923 \pm 0.0058\)
- \(P_\ell / P^{\text{Born}}_\ell\) at \(\epsilon = 0.635\): \(1.007 \pm 0.005\) (stat) \(\pm 0.005\) (point-to-point syst), total syst \(\pm 0.010\)
- \(P_\ell / P^{\text{Born}}_\ell\) at \(\epsilon = 0.785\): \(1.023 \pm 0.006\) (stat) \(\pm 0.005\) (point-to-point syst), total syst \(\pm 0.011\)
- Enhancement of \(P_\ell\) relative to Born approximation at large \(\epsilon\): \((2.3 \pm 0.6)\%\)

## Context
This work addresses the long-standing discrepancy between proton form factor ratios \(G_E/G_M\) extracted from Rosenbluth cross section measurements and those from polarization transfer experiments, which has been hypothesized to arise from two-photon-exchange (TPEX) contributions. The results extend prior polarization transfer measurements by Jones (2000), Punjabi (2005), Gayou (2002), and Puckett (2010) to a systematic study of \(\epsilon\) dependence at fixed \(Q^2\). The observed \(\epsilon\)-independent \(R\) and the enhancement in \(P_\ell\) provide new constraints on theoretical TPEX models such as those by Blunden (2005), Afanasev (2005), Kivel and Vanderhaeghen (2009), and Bystritskiy (2007), none of which predicted the \(P_\ell\) enhancement. The data enable further extraction of the real part of TPEX amplitudes and motivate complementary measurements of the Rosenbluth plot non-linearity, single-spin asymmetries, and the \(e^+p / e^-p\) cross section ratio.
