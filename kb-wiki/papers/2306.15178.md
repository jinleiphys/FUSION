# $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering as the reference system for reaction cross section

> In our previous paper, we tested the chiral (Kyushu) folding model for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering, since the profile function in the Glauber mode is constructed for the system. We found that the folding model is reliable for reaction cross sections $σ_{\rm R}$ in $30 \lsim E_{\rm lab} \lsim 100 $~MeV and $250 \lsim E_{\rm lab} \lsim 400 $~MeV. Accurate data are available for $^{12}$C scattering on $^{9}$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{27}$Al targets in $30 \lsim E_{\rm lab} \lsim 400 $~MeV. We determine matter radius $r_{m}({\rm exp})$ of $^{12}$C from the accurate $σ_{\rm R}({\rm exp})$, using the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model. Our result is $r_{\rm m}^{12}({\rm exp}) =2.352 \pm 0.013$~fm for $^{12}$C. The model is applied for the accurate data on $^{12}$C+$^{27}$Al scattering, and yields $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp}) =2.936 \pm 0.012$~fm for $^{27}$Al. Our conclusion is that $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp}) =2.352 \pm 0.013$~fm agrees with $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp}) =2.35 \pm 0.02$~fm determined from interaction cross sections by Tanihata {\it et. al.}.

## Key claim

The chiral (Kyushu) folding model is reliable for reaction cross sections of $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering in specific energy ranges, and the matter radius of $^{12}$C determined from accurate reaction cross section data using this model agrees with the value previously determined from interaction cross sections, but only when no fine-tuning factor is introduced, unlike the case for proton scattering.

## Method

The authors used the Kyushu $g$-matrix folding model, which is based on a chiral N$^{3}$LO two-nucleon force and NNLO three-nucleon force treated with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method and localized into three-range Gaussian forms. They calculated reaction cross sections for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C and $^{12}$C+$^{27}$Al scattering by folding the projectile and target densities with the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, then scaled the phenomenological proton and neutron densities to match the experimental reaction cross sections and extracted matter radii. The scaling was performed under the condition that the scaled proton radius equals the experimental proton radius determined from charge radii.

## Key numbers

- Matter radius of $^{12}$C: $r_{\rm m}^{12}({\rm exp}) = 2.352 \pm 0.013$ fm, determined from reaction cross section data in the ranges $30 \lesssim E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 100$ MeV and $250 \lesssim E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 373$ MeV
- Matter radius of $^{27}$Al: $r_{\rm m}({\rm exp}) = 2.936 \pm 0.012$ fm, determined from reaction cross section data in the ranges $40 \lesssim E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 87$ MeV and $250 \lesssim E_{\rm lab} \lesssim 373$ MeV
- Neutron radius of $^{12}$C: $r_{\rm n}({\rm exp}) = 2.377 \pm 0.027$ fm
- Neutron radius of $^{27}$Al: $r_{\rm n}({\rm exp}) = 2.924 \pm 0.023$ fm
- Neutron skin of $^{12}$C: $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp}) = 0.050 \pm 0.027$ fm
- Neutron skin of $^{27}$Al: $r_{\rm skin}({\rm exp}) = -0.024 \pm 0.023$ fm
- Scaling factor for $^{12}$C neutrons: $\alpha_{\rm n} = 1.017$; for $^{12}$C protons: $\alpha_{\rm p} = 0.996$
- Scaling factor for $^{27}$Al neutrons: $\alpha_{\rm n} = 0.999$; for $^{27}$Al protons: $\alpha_{\rm p} = 0.991$

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous test of the chiral (Kyushu) folding model for $^{12}$C+$^{12}$C scattering (Tagami et al., 2020) by using high-accuracy reaction cross section data from Takechi et al. (2009) to determine matter radii. The result for $^{12}$C ($2.352 \pm 0.013$ fm) agrees with the value $2.35 \pm 0.02$ fm determined from interaction cross sections by Tanihata et al. (1988), and is more reliable than the value $2.340 \pm 0.009$ fm obtained from proton scattering (Wakasa et al., 2022) because the latter required a fine-tuning factor due to the transparency of proton scattering. The matter radius for $^{27}$Al ($2.936 \pm 0.012$ fm) fills a gap in the compilation of experimental matter radii from Ozawa et al. (2001).
