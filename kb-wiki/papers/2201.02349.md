# Chiral condensates and screening masses of neutral pseudoscalar mesons in thermomagnetic QCD medium

> We point out that chiral condensates at nonzero temperature and magnetic fields are in strict connection to the space-time integral of corresponding two-point neutral meson correlation functions in the pseudoscalar channel via the Ward-Takahashi identity. Screening masses of neutral pseudoscalar mesons, which are defined as the exponential decay of the corresponding spatial correlation functions in the long distance, thus are intrinsically connected to (inverse) magnetic catalysis of chiral condensates. To study this we performed lattice simulations of $(2+1)$-flavor QCD on $32^3\times N_t$ lattices with pion mass $M_π\simeq 220$ MeV in a fixed scale approach having temperature $T\in[17, 281]$ MeV and magnetic field strength $eB\in[0, 2.5]$ GeV$^2$. We find that screening lengths, i.e. inverses of screening masses of $π^0$, $K^0$ and $η^0_{s\bar{s}}$, turn out to have the similar complex $eB$ and $T$ dependences of the corresponding chiral condensates. Although the transition temperature is found to always decrease as $eB$ grows, we show that the suppression due to magnetic fields becomes less significant for hadron screening length and chiral condensates with heavier quarks involved, and ceases to occur for $η^0_{s\bar{s}}$ and strange quark chiral condensate. The complex $eB$ and $T$ dependences of both screening masses and chiral condensates, reflecting the crossover nature of the QCD transition, are attributed to the competition between sea and valence quark effects. These findings could be useful to guide low-energy models and effective theories of QCD.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that chiral condensates and screening masses of neutral pseudoscalar mesons in a thermomagnetic QCD medium are intrinsically connected via the Ward-Takahashi identity, but the relationship is conditional: the suppression due to magnetic fields becomes less significant for hadron screening length and chiral condensates with heavier quarks, and ceases to occur for the strange quark chiral condensate and the eta meson, even though the transition temperature always decreases with increasing magnetic field.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations of (2+1)-flavor QCD using highly improved staggered quarks (HISQ) and a tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action on 32^3 x N_t lattices with a fixed scale approach (lattice spacing a approximately 0.117 fm). They computed chiral condensates and spatial correlation functions of neutral pseudoscalar mesons (pi^0, K^0, eta^0_s\bar{s}) at temperatures from 17 to 281 MeV and magnetic field strengths from 0 to 2.5 GeV^2, extracting screening masses from the exponential decay of spatial correlators at large distances. To separate sea and valence quark effects, they computed correlation functions where the magnetic field appears only in the fermion matrix (sea) or only in the quark propagator (valence).

## Key numbers

- Pion mass M_pi approximately 220 MeV, kaon mass M_K approximately 507 MeV, eta^0_s\bar{s} mass approximately 684 MeV at zero magnetic field and zero temperature
- Temperature range: T in [17, 281] MeV; magnetic field range: eB in [0, 2.5] GeV^2
- Pseudocritical temperature at zero magnetic field: T_pc(eB=0) approximately 170 MeV
- At T = 169 MeV and eB approximately 2.5 GeV^2, the screening mass ratio M_H(T,B)/M_H(T,0) for pi^0 is about 1.7 and for K^0 is about 1.2
- At T = 17 MeV and eB approximately 2.5 GeV^2, the screening mass ratio for pi^0 is about 0.6 and for K^0 is about 0.68
- Lattice spacing a approximately 0.117 fm, spatial lattice size N_s = 32, temporal lattice sizes N_t from 96 to 6

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of inverse magnetic catalysis and the reduction of the pseudocritical temperature in magnetic fields, previously observed by Bali (2011) and Bali (2012) with physical pion masses. It connects to studies by D'Elia (2018) and Endrodi (2019) showing that T_pc reduction occurs even when inverse magnetic catalysis is absent for heavy pions. The paper provides first-principles lattice results that can guide low-energy effective theories and models of QCD, such as chiral perturbation theory and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models, which have previously studied pion pole masses in magnetic fields (Andersen 2012, Fayazbakhsh 2012, Kamikado 2013) but lacked a connection between screening masses and chiral condensates.
