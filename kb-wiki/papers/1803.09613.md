# Three-nucleon force in chiral EFT with explicit $Δ(1232)$ degrees of freedom: Longest-range contributions at fourth order

> We analyze the longest-range two-pion exchange contributions to the three-nucleon force at leading-loop order in the framework of heavy-baryon chiral effective field theory with explicit $Δ(1232)$ degrees of freedom. All relevant low-energy constants which appear in the calculation are determined from pion-nucleon scattering. Comparing our results with the ones obtained in the $Δ$-less theory at N4LO, we find effects of the $Δ$ isobar for this particular topology to be rather well represented in terms of resonance saturation of various low-energy constants in the $Δ$-less approach.

## Key claim

The longest-range two-pion exchange contributions to the three-nucleon force at leading-loop order (N³LO) in chiral effective field theory with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom are computed, and the effects of the Δ isobar for this particular topology are found to be rather well represented in terms of resonance saturation of various low-energy constants in the Δ-less approach, though the authors note that this topology already shows good convergence in the standard Δ-less expansion.

## Method

The authors employed heavy-baryon chiral effective field theory with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom (the small-scale expansion), treating the delta-nucleon mass splitting Δ as a soft scale alongside the pion mass and external momenta. They calculated the one-loop two-pion exchange contributions to the three-nucleon force at N³LO (order ε³ in the small-scale expansion), determined all relevant low-energy constants from pion-nucleon scattering, and carried out renormalization of the effective Lagrangian to leading loop order including nucleon mass, axial coupling, and delta mass and field renormalization.

## Key numbers

- The delta-nucleon mass splitting Δ is defined as Δ := m_Δ − m_N, with m_Δ ≈ 1232 MeV and m_N the physical nucleon mass
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ is taken as Λ_χ ∼ 1 GeV
- The small expansion parameter ε is defined as ε ∈ {Q/Λ_χ, M_π/Λ_χ, Δ/Λ_χ}
- The nucleon axial coupling g_A is defined as g_A = G_A(0), with the physical value determined from the axial-vector form factor
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is denoted F, with the physical value F_π used after renormalization

## Context

This work extends the small-scale expansion for nuclear forces to N³LO for the first time, concentrating on the longest-range two-pion exchange three-nucleon force topology. It builds on prior calculations of the three-nucleon force at N⁴LO in the Δ-less theory by Krebs (2012) and Krebs (2013), and on the pioneering Δ-full calculations at N²LO by Krebs (2007) and Epelbaum (2007). The calculation is a necessary prerequisite for analyzing Δ contributions to the more problematic intermediate-range diagrams (two-pion-one-pion exchange and ring topologies), which will be reported in a separate publication.
