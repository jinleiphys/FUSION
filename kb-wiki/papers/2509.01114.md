# Non-diagonal DVCS and studies of hadronic structure with $N \to N^*$ transition GPDs

> Transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs) describe matrix elements of nonlocal partonic QCD operators between the ground and excited baryon states and provide new tools for quantifying and interpreting the structure of baryon resonances in QCD. We discuss a description of non-diagonal Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering process involving a transition between a nucleon and a nucleon resonance in the $πN$ system within the framework of transition GPDs. We address the physical content of $N \to N^*$ and $N \to Δ$ transition GPDs, review the existing theoretical models and present theoretical estimates of related observables for the kinematic conditions corresponding to the experimental studies with JLab@12GeV. We also discuss the perspective of exploring resonance production with help of transition GPDs and consider the application of the Froissart-Gribov projections to study excitation of nucleon resonances by means of QCD probes with spin-$J$.

# Non-diagonal DVCS and studies of hadronic structure with $N \to N^*$ transition GPDs

## Key claim

Transition generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for nucleon-to-resonance processes provide a framework for probing the internal structure of baryon resonances, but the resulting tomographic images do not represent simple probability densities; instead, they reflect symmetry-breaking effects (such as SU(6) breaking) and require careful interpretation in the infinite momentum frame, especially for non-diagonal transitions where initial and final states differ.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical formalism for non-diagonal Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) involving transitions from a nucleon to resonances in the $\pi N$ system, including $\Delta(1232)$, $P_{11}(1440)$, $D_{13}(1520)$, and $S_{11}(1535)$. Phenomenological models for the transition GPDs are constructed using the double distribution Ansatz with two parameterizations (model I with $b=\infty$ and model II with $b=1$), constrained by electromagnetic transition form factor data from CLAS@6 GeV and by the Partial Conservation of the Axial Current (PCAC) principle for polarized GPDs. The cross sections and beam spin asymmetries for the reaction $e^- p \to e^- \gamma R \to e^- \gamma \pi^+ n$ are computed for kinematics accessible at CLAS@12 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The $N \to \Delta(1232)$ transition magnetic form factor at zero momentum transfer is related to the proton magnetic moment by $G_M(0) = \frac{2 \sqrt{2}}{3} \mu_p$ under SU(6) symmetry.
- The 7-fold cross section for the $\pi \gamma$-electroproduction reaction is given as $\frac{d^7 \sigma}{d Q^2 d x_B d t d \Phi d M_{\pi N}^2 d \Omega_\pi^*}$, with $Q^2$ and $x_B$ describing the lepton side, $t$ and $\Phi$ characterizing the hard subprocess, and $M_{\pi N}^2$ and two decay angles describing the resonance decay.
- The beam spin asymmetry (BSA) is defined as $\frac{d \sigma^{+}-d \sigma^{-}}{d \sigma^{+}+d \sigma^{-}}$, where $d \sigma^{\pm}$ correspond to electron beam helicity $\pm 1/2$.
- The analysis of the $N \to \Delta$ transition requires 4 vector and 4 axial-vector transition GPDs, with only 3 relevant in the large-$N_c$ limit.
- The phenomenological model for $N \to \Delta$ transition GPDs uses the VGG model for nucleon GPDs $\tilde{H}$ and $E$, with cross channel pion exchange for $\tilde{E}$.

## Context

This work extends the GPD framework from diagonal (nucleon-to-nucleon) processes, reviewed by Goeke (2001) and Diehl (2023), to non-diagonal transitions involving excited baryon states, building on the formalism introduced by Semenov-Tian-Shansky (2023) and the white paper by Diehl (2024). The approach connects to earlier work by Guichon (2003) on $N \to \Delta$ transitions and to the CLAS collaboration's experimental program (2023) for exclusive reactions with $N \to \Delta, N^*$ transitions. The paper enables future studies of baryon spectroscopy using non-local QCD probes of arbitrary spin-$J$, including the potential to investigate the nature of the Roper resonance $P_{11}(1440)$ and to apply Froissart-Gribov projections for discriminating between phenomenological GPD models, as demonstrated by Semenov-Tian-Shansky (2023) for the diagonal case.
