# Scaling of multiplicity and flow: pre-LHC trends and LHC surprises

> We examine the scaling trends in particle multiplicity and flow observables between SPS, RHIC and LHC, and discuss their compatibility with popular theoretical models. We examine the way scaling trends between SPS and RHIC are broken at LHC energies, and suggest experimental measurements which can further clarify the situation

# Scaling of multiplicity and flow: pre-LHC trends and LHC surprises

## Key claim
The simple logarithmic scaling of multiplicity with center-of-mass energy observed between SPS and RHIC energies breaks at LHC energies (multiplicity grows faster, as ~s^0.11), while flow observables like elliptic flow v2 continue to scale approximately logarithmically, suggesting a possible "hard" (non-flowing) component contributing to multiplicity without contributing to flow.

## Method
The author qualitatively examines scaling trends in particle multiplicity (dN/dy) and elliptic flow (v2) across SPS (√s=19.6 GeV), RHIC (√s=200 GeV), and LHC (√s=2760 GeV) energies. The analysis compares experimental data to predictions from hydrodynamic models, string-based models, and limiting fragmentation scaling, then evaluates two extreme scenarios: (i) a "soft" background obeying pre-LHC scaling with an unthermalized extra "jetty" contribution, versus (ii) full thermalization of the extra multiplicity affecting flow.

## Key numbers
- Multiplicity at LHC grows as ~s^0.11, faster than the logarithmic ~ln √s trend from SPS to RHIC, but far slower than Landau hydrodynamics (~s^1/4) or AdS/CFT shockwave predictions (~s^1/3)
- Elliptic flow v2 at LHC has grown approximately logarithmically with √s, remaining in the region predicted by pre-LHC scaling or slightly below it
- The eccentricity of 20-30% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at LHC is ~20-30% higher than the eccentricity of 0-40% centrality used in pre-LHC scaling studies
- Below √s ~ 62 GeV, v2(pT) saturates and the increase of integrated v2 is driven by higher average pT rather than increased v2(pT)

## Context
This work extends the scaling analyses of Busza (2009) and the "mescaling" framework, examining how the simple logarithmic trends observed between SPS and RHIC are broken at LHC energies. The author questions the hydrodynamic paradigm (which predicts v2 should not scale monotonically with dN/dy across the phase transition) and suggests that far-from-equilibrium models of flow generation (Borghini, 2006; Koch, 2007) may need serious consideration. The paper enables experimental tests: measuring limiting fragmentation of v2, average pT, and HBT radii at moderate rapidity at LHC to distinguish between thermalized and unthermalized contributions to the extra multiplicity.
