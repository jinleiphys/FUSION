# $\bar{K}$ -induced formation of the $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2^{'}(1525)$ resonances on proton targets

> We investigate the productiong of $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2^{'}(1525)$ mesons in the $K^{-}p\toΛf_2(1270)$,$K^{-}p\toΛf^{'}_2(1525)$ and $K^{-}p\to{}K^{+}K^{-}Λ$ reactions within an effective Lagrangian approach. For $K^{-}p\to{}f_2Λ$ reaction, by considering the contributions from the $t$-channel $K^{+}$ exchange and $u$-channel nucleon pole, we get a fairly good description of the experimental measurements about the total and differential cross sections. Based on the studies of the $K^{-}p\to{}f_2Λ$ reaction, we investigate $K^{-}p\to{}K^{+}K^{-}Λ$ reaction including the contributions from the $f_2(1270)$ and $f'_2(1525)$ mesons decaying into $K^+K^-$ pair. The total cross sections and invariant mass distribution of the $K p \to K^+K^- Λ$ reaction are predicted. The results can be tested in future experiments and therefore offer new clues on the nature of these tensor states.

## Key claim
The $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2^{'}(1525)$ tensor mesons can be produced in $K^{-}p$ scattering via $t$-channel $K^{+}$ exchange and $u$-channel nucleon pole contributions, and their subsequent decay into $K^{+}K^{-}$ pairs yields observable peaks in the invariant mass spectrum; however, the model relies on a cutoff parameter $\Lambda_K$ tuned between 0.51 and 0.53 GeV to match existing data, and the predictions for the $K^{-}p\to K^{+}K^{-}\Lambda$ reaction have not yet been experimentally tested.

## Method
The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach with tree-level Feynman diagrams for the reactions $K^{-}p\to\Lambda f_2(1270)$ and $K^{-}p\to\Lambda f_2^{'}(1525)$, including $t$-channel $K^{+}$ exchange and $u$-channel nucleon pole contributions. Coupling constants are determined from flavor SU(3) symmetry ($g_{Kp\Lambda}=13.24$) and from the partial decay widths of $f_2\to K^{+}K^{-}$ using the tensor meson dominance (TMD) model. The $K^{-}p\to K^{+}K^{-}\Lambda$ reaction is then computed by including the $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2^{'}(1525)$ intermediate states decaying into $K^{+}K^{-}$, with a Monte Carlo multiparticle phase space integration for cross sections and invariant mass distributions.

## Key numbers
- $g_{Kp\Lambda}=13.24$ (determined from flavor SU(3) symmetry).
- $g_{f_2K^{+}K^{-}}=9.96$ for $f_2(1270)$ and $15.78$ for $f_2^{'}(1525)$ (derived from partial decay widths).
- $G_{f_2NN}=2.19$ for $f_2(1270)$; $F_{f_2NN}=0$ for both resonances (from tensor meson dominance).
- $m_{f_2(1270)}=1.275$ GeV, $\Gamma_{f_2(1270)}=0.185$ GeV, Br($f_2(1270)\to K^{+}K^{-})=0.046$.
- $m_{f_2^{'}(1525)}=1.525$ GeV, $\Gamma_{f_2^{'}(1525)}=0.073$ GeV, Br($f_2^{'}(1525)\to K^{+}K^{-})=0.887$.
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda_K$ taken between 0.51 and 0.53 GeV for both reactions.
- Predicted $K^{+}K^{-}$ invariant mass peaks at $M^{2}_{K^{+}K^{-}}\approx 1.63$ GeV$^{2}$ (from $f_2(1270)$) and $\approx 2.33$ GeV$^{2}$ (from $f_2^{'}(1525)$) at beam momenta $P_{K^{-}}=3.9$ and 4.6 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of tensor meson photoproduction on protons, such as the $\gamma p\to p f_2(1270)$ reaction by Xie (2014) and the $\gamma p\to f_2^{'}(1525)p$ reaction by Xie (2015), into the $\bar{K}N$ scattering channel. It also builds on the interpretation of $f_2(1270)$ and $f_2^{'}(1525)$ as dynamically generated states from vector-meson-vector-meson interactions, as proposed by Geng (2008) and supported by $J/\psi$ decay studies by Martinez Torres (2009) and Geng (2009). The predictions for the $K^{-}p\to K^{+}K^{-}\Lambda$ reaction provide testable signals for future experiments, offering new clues on the nature of these tensor states.
