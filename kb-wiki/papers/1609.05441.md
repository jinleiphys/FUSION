# Theory of hard probes in PbPb collisions

> The jet quenching phenomenon in heavy ion collisions provides a strong evidence of the modification of parton shower in the quark-gluon plasma. This contribution focuses on the hard probes of QGP using jets and summarizes the new theoretical progress of jet substructure modification studies using effective field theory techniques. We emphasize the important role of jet substructure observables as they probe various aspects of the jet formation mechanism and allow us to study the medium properties in great details. The precise calculations require the systematic resummation and consistently including medium modifications. Specifically, we discuss the calculations of jet shapes and cross sections in proton-proton and lead-lead collisions at the LHC using soft-collinear effective theory, with Glauber gluon interactions in the medium. In the end we present the comparison between our calculations and the recent measurements at the LHC with very good agreement. We conclude that precise jet modification studies in heavy ion collisions has been initiated.

# Theory of hard probes in PbPb collisions

## Key claim

Precise calculations of jet substructure modification in heavy ion collisions require systematic resummation and consistent inclusion of medium modifications using effective field theory; the non-trivial jet shape modification pattern observed at the LHC is due to the combination of cross section suppression (increased quark jet fraction) and jet-by-jet broadening, not solely from medium-induced energy loss.

## Method

The authors use soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) extended with Glauber gluon interactions (SCET_G) to factorize jet production and substructure into hard, jet, and soft functions. The jet shape is resummed at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy using renormalization-group evolution between the jet scales μ_jr and μ_jR, and medium modifications are incorporated via medium-induced splitting functions that capture the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, using a Bjorken-expanding hydrodynamic medium model with Debye-screened quasi-particles.

## Key numbers

- Jet shape resummation at NLL accuracy is performed for anti-kT jets with R=0.3, pT>100 GeV, and 0.3<|y|<2 in proton-proton collisions at 2.76 TeV LHC, showing significant improvement over fixed-order calculations.
- The nuclear modification factor R_AA for anti-kT jets with R=0.3, |η|<2 is calculated as a function of pT in central lead-lead collisions at √s_NN=2.76 TeV, with theoretical uncertainty estimated by varying the jet-medium coupling g.
- The ratio of differential jet shapes ρ^AA(r)/ρ^pp(r) is computed for lead-lead versus proton-proton collisions at √s_NN=2.76 TeV, showing the separate contributions from cold nuclear matter effects, cross section suppression, and jet-by-jet shape modification.
- Predictions are made for inclusive and photon-tagged jets at √s_NN=5 TeV LHC Run 2, where photon-tagged jets show less cross section suppression and more manifest jet broadening due to higher quark jet fraction.

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based jet shape calculations of Chien (2014) from proton-proton to lead-lead collisions, building on the SCET_G framework for jet-medium interactions developed by Idilbi and Ovanesyan (2008-2011) and the medium-induced splitting functions of Ovanesyan and Fickinger (2011-2013). The calculations are compared with ALICE (2013), ATLAS (2012, 2014), and CMS (2013) measurements at the LHC, showing good agreement. This enables precise jet substructure studies that can disentangle cold nuclear matter effects from final-state medium modifications and test each stage of parton shower evolution in the quark-gluon plasma.
