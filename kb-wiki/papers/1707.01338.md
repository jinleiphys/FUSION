# Hyperons in the pasta phase

> We have investigated under which conditions hyperons (particularly $Λ$s and $Σ^-$s) can be found in the pasta phase. The larger the density and the temperature and the smaller the electron fraction the higher the probability that these particles appear but always in very small amounts. $Λ$-hyperons only occur in the gas and in smaller amounts than would occur if matter were homogeneous, never with abundancies above 10$^{-5}$. The amount of $Σ^-$ in the gas is at least two orders of magnitude smaller and can be disregarded in practical calculations.

## Key claim

Hyperons (particularly Λ and Σ⁻) can appear in the pasta phase of core-collapse supernova matter, but only in very small amounts: Λ-hyperons never exceed abundances of 10⁻⁵ and occur only in the gas phase, not in the dense clusters, while Σ⁻ abundances are at least two orders of magnitude smaller and can be disregarded in practical calculations.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic non-linear Walecka model (NL3ωρ parametrisation) with hyperon-ω couplings from SU(6) symmetry and hyperon-σ couplings tuned to reproduce empirical hyperon potentials in symmetric nuclear matter. The pasta phase was constructed using the coexistence phase (CP) method, applying Gibbs conditions for phase coexistence between a dense clustered phase and a dilute gas phase, with electrons as the only leptons and fixed electron fraction as an input parameter.

## Key numbers

- Λ-hyperon fraction in the pasta phase never exceeds 10⁻⁵ under any conditions studied
- Λ-hyperons appear in the gas phase only for temperatures above 10 MeV, with fractions above 10⁻⁷ possible only for T > 10 MeV
- At T = 10 MeV and Yₑ = 0.3, the Λ fraction in the gas phase is approximately 2 × 10⁻⁸
- Σ⁻ fractions are at least two orders of magnitude smaller than Λ fractions, typically 10⁻¹⁰ to 10⁻⁷
- The critical temperature for pasta phase disappearance in this model is approximately 14.45 MeV
- The NL3ωρ model predicts saturation density at 0.148 fm⁻³, binding energy of -16.2 MeV, compressibility of 271.6 MeV, symmetry energy of 31.7 MeV, and slope of 55.5 MeV
- At zero temperature, no hyperons appear at sub-saturation densities; Λ onset occurs at 0.31 fm⁻³ for β-equilibrium matter
- Largest Λ fractions occur for temperatures above 10 MeV, electron fractions between 0.3 and 0.5, and densities between 0.025 and 0.035 fm⁻³

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the pasta phase, which had only been treated with nucleonic degrees of freedom (Avancini 2008, Xu 2009, Guilherme 2017, Lena 2016), by investigating whether strangeness-carrying baryons appear in non-homogeneous matter. The results contradict the expectation that hyperons might contribute significantly to the low-density equation of state, showing instead that their role is negligible in matter with heavy clusters. The authors note that larger Λ fractions might be expected in calculations that explicitly include light clusters, as suggested by Oertel (2017), and recommend such a study as the next step.
