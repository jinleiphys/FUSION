# First-forbidden transitions in the reactor anomaly

> We describe here microscopic calculations performed on the dominant forbidden transitions in reactor antineutrino spectra above 4 MeV using the nuclear shell model. By taking into account Coulomb corrections in the most complete way, we calculate the shape factor with the highest fidelity and show strong deviations from allowed approximations and previously published results. Despite small differences in the ab initio electron cumulative spectra, large differences on the order of several percents are found in the antineutrino spectra. Based on the behaviour of the numerically calculated shape factors we propose a parametrization of forbidden spectra. Using Monte Carlo techniques we derive an estimated spectral correction and uncertainty due to forbidden transitions. We establish the dominance and importance of forbidden transitions in both the reactor anomaly and spectral shoulder analysis. Based on these results, we conclude that a correct treatment of forbidden transitions is indispensable in both the normalization anomaly and spectral shoulder.

## Key claim

Microscopic shell-model calculations show that first-forbidden transitions in reactor antineutrino spectra above 4 MeV produce large deviations from allowed approximations and previously published results, with differences on the order of several percent in the antineutrino spectra, despite small differences in the electron cumulative spectra. The authors conclude that a correct treatment of forbidden transitions is indispensable for both the normalization anomaly and the spectral shoulder, but note that the shape factors used for allowed decays in reactor antineutrino anomaly analysis suffer from missing terms and uncertain evaluation.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations of the dominant first-forbidden beta transitions in reactor antineutrino spectra above 4 MeV, using the Behrens-Buhring formalism with complete Coulomb corrections including the lambda_k functions and Coulombic convolution distortion. They combined nuclear level energies and branching ratios from the ENDF/B-VIII.0 decay data library with spin-parity information from the Evaluated Nuclear Structure Data File (ENSDF), and used cumulative fission yields from the JEFF3.3 database. The shape factors for forbidden transitions were calculated microscopically and compared against allowed approximations, and Monte Carlo techniques were used to derive estimated spectral corrections and uncertainties.

## Key numbers

* For a fictional first-forbidden unique transition with Z=50 and endpoint energy E0=6 MeV, setting the Coulomb function lambda_2 to unity instead of using its correct value introduces discrepancies rising to 10-20% in the spectral shape.
* The weak magnetism correction used in previous analyses gives dN/dE = 0.67% per MeV, extracted from mirror decays for low masses.
* The Coulomb energy parameter 2xi = alpha Z / R is approximately equal to the endpoint energy W0 for typical fission fragment Z values and endpoint energies of a few MeV, indicating the xi approximation is of questionable validity.
* The induced tensor term delta_it, previously neglected, is generally of comparable magnitude as the weak magnetism term b/Ac and can easily exceed it by a factor of (-)5 on a case-by-case basis.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Hayen 2019) with more data and more sophisticated methods, and addresses the reactor antineutrino anomaly and the "5 MeV bump" spectral disagreement identified by multiple experiments (Abe 2014, Seo 2018). The original treatments by Huber (2011) and Mueller (2011) introduced strong approximations for forbidden transitions, and previous microscopic calculations were performed on only three nuclei (Fang 2015). This work enables improved summation calculations for reactor antineutrino spectra and provides a parametrization of forbidden spectra based on numerically calculated shape factors.
