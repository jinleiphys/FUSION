# Role of the conserved charges in the chiral symmetry restoration phase transition

> The effect of conserved baryon, isospin and strangeness charges on the behavior of phase transitions in dense matter is studied. Baryonic matter is described within the three-flavor Polyakov$-$Nambu$-$Jona-Lasinio model and several charge fractions $Y_Q$ are considered. The role of the vector interaction, which can be important to describe dense systems, is discussed. Special attention is given to the case with charge fraction $Y_Q=0.4$, due to its importance in heavy-ion collisions and core-collapse supernova matter. It is shown that the possible formation of chiral-symmetric quark matter in the laboratory will be favored in asymmetric matter. Besides, the inclusion of the vector interaction reinforces the formation of quark matter at lower densities.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the three-flavor Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, the formation of chiral-symmetric quark matter is favored in asymmetric matter (charge fraction \(Y_Q < 0.5\)) and that including a repulsive vector interaction reinforces the formation of quark matter at lower densities, but these conclusions are conditional on the specific choice of vector coupling strength \(\zeta = G_V/G_S\) and the charge fraction considered.

## Method

The authors study the chiral symmetry restoration phase transition in dense baryonic matter using the (2+1)-flavor PNJL model with a sharp momentum cutoff regularization (\(\Lambda = 602.3\) MeV). They impose Gibbs conditions for phase equilibrium with fixed charge fraction \(Y_Q\) and zero strangeness density, considering both \(\zeta = 0\) and \(\zeta = 0.5\) for the ratio of vector to scalar coupling constants. The phase diagrams are mapped in the temperature–baryonic chemical potential and temperature–baryon density planes for several charge fractions between \(Y_Q = 0\) (neutral matter) and \(Y_Q = 0.5\) (symmetric matter).

## Key numbers

* For \(\zeta = 0\), the critical end point (CEP) chemical potential \(\mu_B^{\mathrm{CEP}}\) varies by approximately 89 MeV when going from \(Y_Q = 0.5\) to \(Y_Q = 0\), while the CEP temperature \(T^{\mathrm{CEP}}\) is reduced by approximately 16 MeV.
* For \(\zeta = 0.5\), the CEP temperature experiences a reduction of approximately 78 MeV with decreasing \(Y_Q\), while the change in \(\mu_{BQ}^{\mathrm{CEP}}\) is not larger than 54 MeV; for \(Y_Q = 0\) the CEP occurs at zero temperature.
* At \(T = 50\) MeV and \(Y_Q = 0.4\) with \(\zeta = 0\), the phase transition spans from baryon density \(\rho_B = 0.013\) fm\(^{-3}\) (pressure 0.565 MeV fm\(^{-3}\)) to \(\rho_B = 0.397\) fm\(^{-3}\) (pressure 0.613 MeV fm\(^{-3}\)).
* At \(T = 50\) MeV and \(Y_Q = 0.4\) with \(\zeta = 0.5\), the phase transition spans from \(\rho_B = 0.076\) fm\(^{-3}\) (pressure 2.56 MeV fm\(^{-3}\)) to \(\rho_B = 0.292\) fm\(^{-3}\) (pressure 2.73 MeV fm\(^{-3}\)).
* For \(\zeta = 0.5\) and \(Y_Q = 0.4\), chiral-symmetric matter can appear at approximately twice saturation density for small temperatures.
* The model parameters used are: cutoff \(\Lambda = 602.3\) MeV, \(m_u = m_d = 5.5\) MeV, \(m_s = 140.7\) MeV, \(G_S \Lambda^2 = 1.835\), \(G_D \Lambda^5 = 12.36\), and Polyakov loop potential parameters \(a_0 = 3.51\), \(a_1 = -2.47\), \(a_2 = 15.2\), \(b_3 = -1.75\) with \(T_0 = 210\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of phase transitions in multicomponent systems with conserved charges, building on the Gibbs construction formalism applied to hadron–quark transitions in neutron stars by Glendenning (1992) and to the liquid–gas phase transition in asymmetric nuclear matter by Muller and Serot (1995). The results are relevant for heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Au-Au or Pb-Pb collisions with \(Y_Q \approx 0.4\)) and core-collapse supernova matter, and suggest that experiments at FAIR or NICA using neutron-rich nuclei could produce conditions favorable for chiral-symmetric quark matter formation at intermediate densities. The authors note that future work should incorporate quantum fluctuations via the functional renormalization group approach, as applied to the quark meson model by Schaefer and Wambach (2004) and others.
