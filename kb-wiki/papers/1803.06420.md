# Exclusive vector meson production at an electron-ion collider

> Coherent exclusive vector meson electroproduction is a key physics channel at an electron-ion collider. It probes the gluon structure of nuclei over a wide range of $Q^2$, and can be used to measure nuclear shadowing and to search for gluon saturation and/or the colored glass condensate. In this paper, we present calculations of the kinematic distributions for a variety of exclusive vector meson final states: the $蟻$, $蠒$, J/$蠄$, $蠄'$ and the $违$ states. The cross-sections for light and $c\overline c$ mesons are large, while $违$ states should be produced in moderate numbers at a medium energy EIC (the proposed U.S. designs) and in large numbers at the LHeC. We investigate the acceptances for these states, as a function of detector rapidity coverage. A large-acceptance detector is needed to cover the full range photon-nucleon collision energies produced at an EIC; a forward detector is required to observe vector mesons from the most energetic photon interactions, and thereby probe gluons at the lowest possible Bjorken-$x$ values.

## Key claim

Coherent exclusive vector meson electroproduction at an electron-ion collider can probe gluon structure, nuclear shadowing, and gluon saturation, but the cross-section calculations rely on theoretically informed extrapolations for high-\(Q^2\) electroproduction on ion targets, where no experimental data exist.

## Method

The authors calculated kinematic distributions and cross-sections for exclusive vector meson production (\(\rho\), \(\phi\), \(J/\psi\), \(\psi'\), \(\Upsilon\)) in \(ep\) and \(eA\) collisions at eRHIC, JLEIC, and LHeC, using parameterizations of HERA data for photonuclear cross-sections and a quantum Glauber calculation for nuclear targets. They implemented the calculations in an event generator (eSTARlight) that samples photon flux, photon virtuality \(Q^2\), and center-of-mass energy \(W\) via rejection sampling, and models final-state angular distributions using phenomenological fits assuming \(s\)-channel helicity conservation.

## Key numbers

* Photoproduction (\(Q^2 < 1\) GeV\(^2\)) cross-section for \(\rho^0\) in \(ep\) collisions at eRHIC: 5.0 \(\mu\)b; at LHeC: 10.0 \(\mu\)b.
* Photoproduction cross-section for \(J/\psi\) in \(eA\) collisions at eRHIC: 1.9 \(\mu\)b; at LHeC: 15.0 \(\mu\)b.
* Electroproduction (\(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\)) cross-section for \(\rho^0\) in \(ep\) collisions at eRHIC: 14.0 nb; at LHeC: 26.0 nb.
* Electroproduction cross-section for \(\Upsilon(1S)\) in \(ep\) collisions at LHeC: 18.0 pb; in \(eA\) collisions at LHeC: 5.3 nb.
* Expected number of \(J/\psi\) photoproduction events at eRHIC in \(ep\) collisions (10 fb\(^{-1}\)): 85 million; in \(eA\) collisions (10 fb\(^{-1}\)/nucleon): 100 million.
* Expected number of \(\Upsilon(1S)\) electroproduction events at LHeC in \(eA\) collisions: 250 kilo.
* Coefficients for \(Q^2\) dependence of cross-section: for \(\rho\), \(c_1 = 2.09 \pm 0.10\), \(c_2 = 0.73 \pm 0.18 \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^{-2}\); for \(J/\psi\), \(c_1 = 2.36 \pm 0.20\), \(c_2 = 0.29 \pm 0.43 \times 10^{-2}\) GeV\(^{-2}\).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations from the STARlight Monte Carlo generator (Klein, 2016) and the Sartre generator (Toll, 2012; Toll, 2013) by adding electroproduction capabilities, a wider range of vector meson final states, and detailed angular distributions. It enables detector design and experimental planning for the proposed U.S. EIC designs (eRHIC, JLEIC) and the LHeC, and provides a tool to study nuclear shadowing and saturation by comparing \(ep\) and \(eA\) cross-sections, where many theoretical uncertainties cancel in the ratio.
