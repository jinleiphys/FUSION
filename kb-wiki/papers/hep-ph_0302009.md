# The universal psi-prime-prime to psi-prime ratio as unambiguous signature for hard physics in nuclear reactions and in decays of beauty hadrons

> We suggest the ratio of the $ψ''(3770)/ψ'(3686)$ production cross sections as an unambiguous probe of the dominance of hard, point-like physics in charmonium production. If hard physics dominates the charmonium production the ratio : ${σ(ψ'')\overσ(ψ')}\approx {Γ(ψ''\to l^+l^-)\over Γ(ψ'\to l^+l^-)} \approx 0.1$ is independent of the process. We argue that since the dominant $D\bar D$ decay mode of the $ψ''$-meson can be detected with a high efficiency at the current facilities the effective counting rate for the $ψ''$ production should be comparable to that of the $ψ'$ and hence it could be observed at the $B$-factories. Even in hadron induced processes the ratio will not be changed significantly by final state interactions due to the similar mass and size of these two charmonium states. A possible exception are the charmonium state interactions with low energy particles -- comovers -- which are especially relevant for the heavy ion collisions.

# The universal psi-prime-prime to psi-prime ratio as unambiguous signature for hard physics in nuclear reactions and in decays of beauty hadrons

## Key claim
The ratio of ψ″(3770) to ψ′(3686) production cross sections is predicted to be approximately 0.1 and universal across all hard, point-like production processes, but this universality will be lost if nonperturbative QCD physics influences production. A possible exception to the robustness of the ratio is charmonium state interactions with low-energy comovers, especially relevant for heavy ion collisions.

## Method
The authors use a nonrelativistic charmonium mixing model where ψ′ is primarily a 2S state with a small 1D admixture and ψ″ is primarily a 1D state with a small 2S admixture, with a mixing angle θ = 19 ± 2° determined from the measured e⁺e⁻ decay widths. They argue that in hard processes, only the S-wave component contributes to production (proportional to the square of the wavefunction at zero separation), so the ψ″/ψ′ ratio equals tan²(θ). They compare this prediction with existing data from B-factories (BABAR, CLEO, BELLE), Tevatron (CDF), and fixed-target experiments (E789, E772), and contrast it with NRQCD predictions that allow significant D-wave production.

## Key numbers
- Predicted universal ratio σ(ψ″)/σ(ψ′) ≈ tan²(θ) ≈ 0.1, derived from mixing angle θ = 19 ± 2°
- Predicted branching fraction for B-meson decays into ψ″: approximately 0.03%, compared to the measured B → ψ(2S) branching fraction of 0.297 ± 0.020 ± 0.020% (BABAR) and 0.316 ± 0.014 ± 0.023 ± 0.016% (CLEO)
- Predicted inclusive cross section for p̄p → ψ″ + X at Tevatron: approximately 3 nb, based on CDF measurement of σ(p̄p → ψ′ + X) = 30.1 ± 6.6 +3.8 −4.2 ± 6.6 nb
- Predicted integrated cross section for pp → ψ″ + X on hydrogen target: 40 ± 19 nb, derived from E789/E772 data at 800 GeV
- BELLE measured σ(e⁺e⁻ → ψ′ + X) = 0.67 ± 0.09 pb at 10.6 GeV; NRQCD predicts σ(e⁺e⁻ → δ₁ + X) = 0.043 pb for the D-wave state
- Thermal model prediction for heavy ion collisions: σ(ψ″)/σ(ψ′) = 0.63 to 0.65 at temperature T = 170 to 180 MeV, much larger than the hard production prediction of 0.1

## Context
This work extends the charmonium mixing model of Eichten (1974) and Richard (1979) to propose a universal experimental signature distinguishing hard perturbative QCD production from soft nonperturbative mechanisms. It directly contrasts with the NRQCD approach (Bodwin, 1994) which predicts significant direct D-wave charmonium production and thus a different ψ″/ψ′ ratio. The paper enables experimental tests at B-factories (BABAR, BELLE), Tevatron (CDF), fixed-target experiments (HERA-B), and heavy ion facilities (NA60 at CERN-SPS, PHENIX at RHIC) by reanalyzing existing data for ψ″ production via its dominant D̄D decay channel rather than dilepton channels.
