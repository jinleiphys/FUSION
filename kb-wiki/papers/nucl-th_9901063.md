# Rotational Invariance In Nuclear Light-Front Mean Field Theory

> A light-front treatment for spherical nuclei is developed from a relativistic effective Lagrangian and employing the mean field approximation. Minimizing the nuclear minus momentum subject to the constraint that, in the rest frame, the expectation values of the plus- and minus-momenta are the same leads to a formalism in which rotational invariance is recovered.

## Key claim

The authors show that rotational invariance can be recovered in a light-front mean-field treatment of spherical nuclei by minimizing the expectation value of the nuclear minus momentum subject to the constraint that the expectation values of plus- and minus-momenta are equal in the rest frame, rather than by minimizing the minus momentum alone.

## Method

The authors start from a relativistic effective Lagrangian with nucleons, scalar mesons, and vector mesons, using the meson-nucleon coupling constants and meson masses of Horowitz and Serot (1981). They construct a light-front Hamiltonian \(H_{LF} \equiv \frac{1}{2}(P^+ + P^-)\) and minimize its expectation value in a mean-field approximation, using a Slater determinant of nucleon fields with a Fock-space basis restricted to positive \(p^+\) values. The single-particle states are expanded in a basis of B-splines of degree five in the transverse coordinate \(x_\perp\) and discrete Fourier components in the longitudinal variable, and the coupled equations for nucleons and meson fields are solved iteratively until stable solutions are obtained.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{40}\)Ca, the single-particle energies \(\epsilon_n = p_n^-/2\) from the light-front calculation match those from the equal-time formalism to within numerical accuracy; for example, the \(0s_{1/2}\) state gives \(-55.39\) MeV (LF) versus \(-55.40\) MeV (ET), and the \(0p_{3/2}\) state gives \(-38.90\) MeV in both formalisms.
* The nucleon plus-momentum distribution peaks at \(y \approx 0.76\), corresponding to \(p^+\) of 710 MeV per nucleon.
* The average value of \(p^+\) per nucleon is 672.56 MeV (\( \langle y \rangle = 0.72\)), out of a total expectation value \(P^+/A\) of 930.56 MeV.
* Scalar mesons carry 4.70 MeV of the plus-momentum, while vector mesons carry 253.30 MeV (27% of the nuclear plus-momentum).
* The calculation uses a box size of \(2L = 24\) fm, 20 splines, and 24 Fourier components.

## Context

This work extends the light-front mean-field treatment of nuclear matter by Miller (1997) to finite spherical nuclei, and it reproduces the standard equal-time results for nuclear binding energy and density from Horowitz and Serot (1981). The formalism enables computation of nucleon and meson plus-momentum distributions needed for analyzing deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering and high-momentum transfer nuclear reactions, though the authors note that the mean-field approximation yields a nucleon effective mass of about 670 MeV that is too small to reproduce the observed reduction in nuclear structure functions near \(x \sim 0.5\), suggesting that effects beyond the mean field (such as tensor coupling of the isoscalar vector meson) may be needed.
