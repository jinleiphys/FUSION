# Quadrupole shape dynamics in view from a theory of large amplitude collective motion

> Low-lying quadrupole shape dynamics is a typical manifestation of large amplitude collective motion in finite nuclei. To describe the dynamics on a microscopic foundation, we have formulated a consistent scheme in which the Bohr collective Hamiltonian for the five dimensional quadrupole shape variables is derived on the basis of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory. It enables us to incorporates the Thouless-Valatin effect on the shape inertial functions, which has been neglected in previous microscopic Bohr Hamiltonian approaches. Quantitative successes are illustrated for the low-lying spectra in $^{68}$Se, $^{30-34}$Mg and $^{58-64}$Cr, which display shape-coexistence, -mixing and -transitional behaviors.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their CHFB + LQRPA approach, which incorporates the Thouless-Valatin effect on both rotational and vibrational inertial functions, solves the problem of stretched excitation spectra that arises when the Inglis-Belyaev cranking approximation is used, but the quantitative success is demonstrated only for specific nuclei and the approach relies on a pairing plus quadrupole model rather than a fully selfconsistent energy density functional.

## Method

The authors first perform constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) calculations to obtain mean-field states with various quadrupole deformations in the beta-gamma plane, using a pairing plus quadrupole model with parameters adjusted to reproduce Skyrme-HFB results. They then solve local quasiparticle random phase approximation (LQRPA) equations at each deformation point to determine the displacement operators and inertial functions, including the time-odd induced fields that constitute the Thouless-Valatin effect. The resulting Bohr collective Hamiltonian, with collective potential from the CHFB deformation energy and inertial functions from the LQRPA solutions, is requantized using the Pauli prescription and diagonalized on a 60 x 60 mesh in the beta-gamma plane to obtain excitation spectra.

## Key numbers

- In $^{68}$Se, the ratio of LQRPA to Inglis-Belyaev vibrational inertia $D_{\beta\beta}/D_{\beta\beta}^{(IB)}$ is typically 1.3-1.5 in a large part of the beta-gamma plane, and around 2 at larger deformation $\beta \gtrsim 0.4$.
- In $^{68}$Se, the rotational moment of inertia ratio ${\cal J}_1 /{\cal J}_1^{(IB)}$ is around 1.2-1.5 at small and modest deformation, decreasing to about 1.1 at large deformations.
- In $^{68}$Se, the CHFB+LQRPA calculation yields yrast energies $E(2_1^+)=0.810$ MeV, $E(4_1^+)=1.951$ MeV, $E(6_1^+)=3.348$ MeV, compared to experimental values 0.854, 1.942, 3.304 MeV; the Inglis-Belyaev calculation gives 0.991, 2.310, 3.891 MeV.
- In $^{68}$Se, the CHFB+LQRPA calculation gives the yrare $2_2^+$ state at $E(2_2^+)=1.536$ MeV, consistent with the experimental 1.593 MeV, while the Inglis-Belyaev calculation gives 1.883 MeV.
- In $^{32}$Mg, the CHFB+LQRPA calculation yields $E(2_1^+)=0.744$ MeV, $E(4_1^+)=2.099$ MeV, $E(0_2^+)=0.986$ MeV, compared to experimental values 0.885, 2.322, and 1.058 MeV respectively.
- The energy ratio $E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)$ in Mg isotopes is 2.23, 2.64, and 3.21 in experiment and 2.37, 2.82, and 3.26 in theory for A=30, 32, and 34.
- In $^{62}$Cr, the CHFB+LQRPA calculation gives $E(2_1^+)=0.502$ MeV and $E(4_1^+)=1.228$ MeV, compared to experimental values 0.446 and 1.180 MeV.
- The energy ratio $E(4_1^+)/E(2_1^+)$ in Cr isotopes increases from 2.12 at N=34 to 2.68 at N=40.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent collective coordinate (SCC) method of Marumori (1980) and Matsuo (2000) to construct a microscopic Bohr Hamiltonian that includes the Thouless-Valatin effect on all inertial functions, which had been neglected in previous microscopic Bohr Hamiltonian approaches such as those by Pomorski (1977), Dudek (1980), Libert (1999), Prochniak (2004), and Niksik (2009). The approach improves upon calculations that used the Inglis-Belyaev cranking approximation, which systematically overestimates excitation energies as shown by Dudek (1980) and Prochniak (2009), and goes beyond treatments that included the Thouless-Valatin effect only for rotational moments of inertia as in Bertsch (2007), Girod (2009), and Delaroche (2010). The method enables quantitative description of shape coexistence, shape mixing, and shape transitional behaviors in nuclei such as $^{68}$Se, neutron-rich Mg isotopes around $^{32}$Mg, and neutron-rich Cr isotopes, and the authors anticipate future applications using selfconsistent Skyrme energy density functionals and extension to spontaneous fission.
