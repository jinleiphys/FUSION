# Dynamical scheme for computing the mass parameter of a system in a medium

> We present a new method for extracting a mass parameter using time-dependent density functional theory for an arbitrary physical system, provided the adiabatic limit is achievable. This approach works for collective variables also in the presence of a medium, in particular for the nuclei interacting with a neutron background. We apply the method to extract mass parameters of impurities in the neutron star crust, like their inertial masses and quadrupole mass parameters. The extracted mass parameters at various depths of the inner crust are compared with other methods, including the hydrodynamic approach. The presented method opens avenues for the construction of an effective model of neutron star crust grounded in microscopic calculations.

# Dynamical scheme for computing the mass parameter of a system in a medium

## Key claim

The authors present a method for extracting mass parameters from time-dependent density functional theory that works for collective variables in the presence of a medium (such as a neutron background), provided the adiabatic limit is achievable; the method extracts the mass parameter by adding an external harmonic potential coupled to the collective variable and measuring the resulting shift in oscillation frequency, circumventing the need for explicit linearization or construction of collective subspaces.

## Method

The method solves time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations arising from a modified energy functional that includes an additional harmonic potential term (1/2)k(Q(t)-Q0)^2 coupled to the chosen collective variable Q. For several values of the control parameter k, the frequency of small-amplitude oscillations in Q(t) is extracted and fitted to the relation ω^2(k) = ω_Q^2 + k/M_Q, where M_Q is the mass parameter and ω_Q is the mode energy. The authors apply this to a system of a zirconium nucleus (40 protons) immersed in superfluid neutron matter, using the BSk31 Skyrme functional with microscopically deduced pairing, solving on a 32×32×64 spatial grid with 1.25 fm lattice spacing and periodic boundary conditions.

## Key numbers

- The effective mass M_cm (in units of neutron mass m_n) was extracted for average nuclear densities ranging from 0.002 fm^{-3} to 0.051 fm^{-3}
- For the center-of-mass mode, the extracted frequency follows ω^2 = 7×10^{-6} k (in units where the slope gives the inverse mass parameter) for the system at average density 0.015 fm^{-3}
- The method's results for effective mass are consistent with an independent benchmark scheme within numerical uncertainties below 5%
- For large-amplitude oscillations (A = 10 fm), the method overestimates M_cm by approximately 10-15% compared to small-amplitude results
- The damping coefficient γ_CM increases monotonically with average nuclear density, vanishing in the low-density limit corresponding to a nucleus oscillating in vacuum
- The Landau velocity used to characterize dissipation is defined as v_l = Δ_n/(ħ k_F_n), where Δ_n and k_F_n are bulk values of the pairing field and Fermi wave-vector of neutrons

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to extract effective masses of impurities in neutron star crusts, particularly the dynamical scheme of Pecak (2024) which was specific to center-of-mass motion and could not be generalized to other collective variables. The method is benchmarked against that earlier approach and shows consistent results. The technique enables extraction of mass parameters for quadrupole vibrations and other collective modes in the inner crust, which is relevant for constructing effective models of neutron star crust properties such as phonon spectra, thermal and electric conductivities (as studied by Gnedin and others), and heat capacity (as studied by Urban in 2015). The approach complements hydrodynamic models and quasiparticle random phase approximation studies (PhysRevC.71.042801, PhysRevC.99.045801) that have examined quadrupole cluster vibrations in the inner crust.
