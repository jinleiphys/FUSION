# Semiclassical calculations of complete and incomplete fusion in collisions of weakly bound nuclei

> We use an improved version of the semiclassical method described in Refs. [1,2,3] to evaluate fusion cross sections in collisions of weakly bound nuclei. This version takes into account the static effects of the low breakup threshold, uses better bin states in the discretization of the continuum and avoids the excitation of closed channels. The population of these channels is a consequence of the violation of energy conservation, which is inherent in the semiclassical method. The method is employed to evaluate complete fusion and total fusion cross section in collisions of the weakly bound 6,7Li projectiles with 159Tb and 197Au targets, for which data is available. The overall agreement between theory and experiment is fairly good.

## Key claim

The improved semiclassical method, which incorporates static effects of the low breakup threshold, better bin states for continuum discretization, and avoids excitation of closed channels (a consequence of energy conservation violation inherent in the semiclassical approach), achieves fairly good overall agreement between theory and experiment for complete fusion and total fusion cross sections in collisions of weakly bound 6,7Li projectiles with 159Tb and 197Au targets.

## Method

The projectile-target relative motion is treated classically using an optical potential, while the intrinsic dynamics of the projectile (including breakup) is handled by time-dependent quantum mechanics. The continuum of unbound states is discretized using energy bins (wave packets in k-space) within the continuum discretized coupled channel (CDCC) approximation. The optical potential is taken as the expectation value of the total fragment-target potential in the projectile ground state, rather than assuming both clusters are located at the projectile center as in prior work (Marta et al., 2002, 2008, 2014). The time-dependent Schrodinger equation is solved via coupled-channel equations for the amplitudes of bound and bin states.

## Key numbers

* Breakup thresholds: 1.47 MeV for 6Li, 2.47 MeV for 7Li, 1.57 MeV for 9Be (context: light weakly bound nuclei).
* Coulomb radius parameter: r0C = 1.06 fm used in calculations.
* Systems studied: 6,7Li projectiles on 159Tb and 197Au targets, compared with data from Pires et al. (2011), Mukherjee et al. (2006), and Palshetkar et al. (2014).

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical approach of Marta, Canto and Donangelo (2002, 2008, 2014) by improving the optical potential choice (using ground-state expectation values rather than the r=0 approximation) and using better bin states for continuum discretization. It contrasts with full quantum CDCC methods (Hagino et al., 2000; Diaz-Torres and Thompson, 2002) which cannot easily separate complete from incomplete fusion for projectiles breaking into fragments of comparable masses, and with classical trajectory models (Diaz-Torres et al., 2007, 2010, 2011) that treat breakup stochastically. The method enables evaluation of individual complete fusion and incomplete fusion cross sections for weakly bound projectiles that break into fragments of comparable masses, which is difficult with purely quantum approaches.
