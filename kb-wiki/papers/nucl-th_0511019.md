# Properties of hadron and quark matter studied with a molecular dynamics

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition in a molecular dynamics (MD) of quark degrees of freedom. The hadron state at low density and temperature, and the deconfined quark state at high density and temperature are observed in our model. We investigate the equations of state and draw the phase-diagram at wide baryon density and temperature range. We also discuss the transport property, e.g. viscosity, of $q\bar{q}$ matter. It is found that the ratio of the shear viscosity to the entropy density is less than one for quark matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density for quark matter is less than one, but this result is preliminary and based on a molecular dynamics model that does not consistently include the number of quarks and anti-quarks, preventing quantitative discussion of the critical temperature.

## Method

The authors performed molecular dynamics simulations of quark matter in a cubic box with periodic boundary conditions, using a Hamiltonian with color-dependent interactions and a Pauli potential. They simulated two systems: quark matter with finite baryon density (no anti-quarks) and q-qbar matter with zero baryon density, solving equations of motion for several thousand fm/c after equilibrium. The shear viscosity was calculated using the Green-Kubo relation from the stress tensor, and the entropy density was derived from thermodynamic quantities.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the baryon-quark transition is defined by maximum-specific-heat points and becomes smaller as density increases; at 0.5 rho_0 and 1 rho_0 the energy per baryon shows a change in specific heat.
- For q-qbar matter, the ratio eta/s is larger than one in the low temperature region where quarks form mesons, and takes a value smaller than one around 130 MeV where quarks are deconfined.
- The radial distribution function of q-qbar matter is shown at temperatures of 120, 140, 160, and 180 MeV, with bumps near zero distance due to meson-like correlation.
- The simulation used box sizes of 10 fm for low temperature region and 4 fm for high temperature region.
- The quark mass assumed in the calculation is 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous molecular dynamics model for zero temperature (Akimura et al., 2005) to finite temperature, and aims to study both static and dynamic aspects of the hadron-quark phase transition. The results are compared to quenched lattice QCD studies of viscosity above the critical temperature (Nakamura and Sakai, 2005), which also found eta/s much smaller than 1. The paper is preliminary and notes that longer simulation time, larger box size, and consistent treatment of quark-antiquark numbers are needed for quantitative conclusions; investigation of viscosity for matter at finite baryon density is stated to be in progress.
