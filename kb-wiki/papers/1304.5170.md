# Scissors Modes: The elusive breathing overtone

> The Two-Rotor Model predicts two levels above the Scissors Modes with degenerate intrinsic energy. They have $J^π= 0^+,2^+$ and are referred to as overtones. Their energy is below threshold for nucleon emission, which should make them observable. The $J^π=0^+$ overtone, that has the structure of an isovector breathing mode, has vanishing $E0$ amplitude so that cannot be directly excited, but it could be reached in the decay of the $J=2^+$ overtone. We discuss such a process and evaluate the $B(E2)$ strength, which, however, turns out to be very small.

# Scissors Modes: The elusive breathing overtone

## Key claim

The Two-Rotor Model predicts two overtone states above the Scissors Modes with degenerate intrinsic energy at approximately 6 MeV in the rare earth region, but the $J^\pi=0^+$ breathing overtone cannot be directly excited because its $E0$ amplitude vanishes, and the $B(E2)$ strength for its population via decay of the $J=2^+$ overtone turns out to be negligibly small due to an apparently accidental cancellation.

## Method

The authors evaluate the $B(E2)$ transition strength for the decay of the $2^+$ overtone ($2_1^+$) into the $0^+$ breathing overtone ($0_1^+$) within the Two-Rotor Model, using the quadrupole operator expanded to zero order in the zero-point oscillation amplitude $\theta_0$. They also compute the $B(M1)$ strength for the subsequent decay of the breathing overtone into the $1^+$ Scissors Mode, using the magnetic dipole operator of the model.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic energy of the Scissors Modes is of order 3 MeV in the rare earth region.
- The intrinsic energy of the overtones ($J^\pi=0^+,2^+$) is of order 6 MeV.
- $B(E2, 0^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+) = \frac{3}{64} e^2 Q_{20}^2$, which is nearly a factor of 2 larger than the $B(E2)$ strength for the $J=2^+$ Scissors Mode in the rare earth region.
- $B(E2, 2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+) = \frac{1}{32} \left(1 - \sqrt{\frac{3}{2}}\right)^2 e^2 Q_{20}^2$, which is negligibly small.
- $B(M1, 0_1^+ \rightarrow 1^+) = \frac{1}{\theta_0^2} |\mathcal{M}|^2$, equal to the $B(M1)$ strength for excitation of the $1^+$ Scissors Mode from the ground state.
- The energy of photons from the $2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+$ decay is $E = 3\hbar^2 / \mathcal{I}$, where $\mathcal{I}$ is the moment of inertia.

## Context

This work extends the Two-Rotor Model predictions originally developed by LoIudice and Palumbo for the Scissors Modes, and builds on the recent determination of the $2^+$ overtone structure by Hatada, Hayakawa, and Palumbo (2013). The overtones lie below the nucleon emission threshold, which should make them observable, but the calculated $E2$ decay strength from the $2^+$ overtone to the breathing overtone is negligible. The authors note that recent experimental investigations of Scissors Modes in Gd isotopes up to about 6 MeV by Chyzh et al. show no evidence of these overtones, but those experiments use neutron-photon reactions that cannot be analyzed within the Two-Rotor Model. The authors suggest that microscopic models such as the Fermion-Boson Interacting Model, which reproduces the Two-Rotor Model Hamiltonian in the semiclassical limit (Dieperink and Scholten, 1980), and experiments with electromagnetic interactions are needed to assess the existence of these states.
