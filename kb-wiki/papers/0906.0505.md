# Experimental confirmation of the Lambda(1405) Ansatz from resonant formation of a K-p quasi-bound state in K- absorption by 3He and 4He

> The Sigma-pi invariant-mass spectra in the resonant capture of K- at rest in 4He, 3He and d are calculated by a coupled-channel procedure for a K-p quasi-bound state of an arbitrary chosen mass (M) and width (Gamma). A chi2 analysis of old 4He bubble chamber data shows a dominance of the s-orbit absorption, and yielded M = 1405.5 ^(+1.4)_(-1) MeV/c2 and Gamma = 26 ^(+4)_(-3) MeV, where a possible population of Sigma0(1385) and also a small p-orbit capture contribution are taken into account. This result confirms the Lambda(1405) ansatz, whereas recent chiral-SU(3) predictions (M ~ 1420 MeV/c2) are excluded. A more stringent test by using a 3He target is proposed .

# Experimental confirmation of the Lambda(1405) Ansatz from resonant formation of a K-p quasi-bound state in K- absorption by 3He and 4He

## Key claim

The old 4He bubble chamber data for Sigma-pi invariant-mass spectra from stopped K- absorption are consistent with a K-p quasi-bound state mass of 1405.5 MeV/c2 and width of 26 MeV, confirming the Lambda(1405) ansatz that the resonance is a bound I=0 Kbar N state; recent chiral-SU(3) predictions placing the mass near 1420 MeV/c2 are excluded at the 99.9% confidence level.

## Method

The authors formulated K- absorption by 4He, 3He, and deuterium as a resonant capture process where the K- forms a quasi-bound K-p state (treated as a Feshbach resonance) that decays to Sigma + pi, using a coupled-channel separable potential model with Yukawa-type form factors. They calculated the projected Sigma-pi invariant-mass distributions for s-orbit and p-orbit atomic capture, including direct (quasi-free) capture contributions, and performed chi-squared fitting to the 4He bubble chamber data of Riley et al. with the resonance mass and width as free parameters, accounting for possible Sigma0(1385) population and p-orbit mixing.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit resonance mass: M = 1405.5 (+1.4 / -1.0) MeV/c2 from chi-squared analysis of 4He data with 10% p-orbit mixing and 10% Sigma0(1385) population taken into account
- Best-fit resonance width: Gamma = 25.6 (+4 / -3) MeV from the same analysis
- The chi-squared minimum value was approximately 11 for the best-fit s-orbit capture case with M = 1405 MeV/c2 and Gamma = 40 MeV, compared to chi-squared = 388 for p-orbit capture
- The energy of K- + 4He at rest is 4221 MeV, nearly coinciding with Lambda(1405) + 3H at 4216 MeV (5 MeV difference), while Lambda*(1420) + 3H at 4229 MeV is 15 MeV higher (off-tuned)
- The energy of K- + 3He at rest is 3302 MeV, close to Lambda*(1420) + 2H at 3296 MeV (6 MeV difference), while Lambda(1405) + 2H at 3283 MeV is 19 MeV lower (off-tuned)
- The I=1 component contribution to the spectral intensity was found to be about 4%, affecting M by 0.2 MeV and Gamma by 0.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) interaction approach (2002-2004), which treats Lambda(1405) as a bound I=0 Kbar N state and predicts deeply bound kaonic nuclear states, and directly contradicts the "Chiral-Weak" models from chiral SU(3) dynamics (Oset-Ramos-Bennhold, Hyodo-Weise, and others) that predict a K-p resonance mass around 1421-1434 MeV/c2 with a second pole coupled to Sigma-pi. The analysis resolves the "entrance-channel ambiguity" present in the earlier Dalitz-Deloff (1991) analysis of Hemingway's data, which fitted the Sigma-pi to Sigma-pi transition amplitude rather than the K-p to Sigma-pi amplitude used here. The results enable future experiments at J-PARC and DAΦNE using 3He targets to provide a more stringent test, since 3He offers better resonant tuning to distinguish between the two scenarios.
