# Dwarf (Twin) Neutron Stars I: Did GW170817 Involve One?

> Dwarf neutron stars are stable twins of neutron stars but with a maximum mass less than that of neutron stars. Their existence brings into concordance the seemingly conflicting information on the size of neutron stars inferred from gravitational waves from GW170817, from the NICER mission, and from the PREX-II experiment. Their distinctive characteristics lead to rich and falsifiable predictions that are expected to be tested in the near future. If corroborated, the existence of dwarf neutron stars would substantially improve our understanding of the QCD phase diagram and offer valuable insights into the dark sector.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that "dwarf neutron stars" (stable twin stars with lower maximum mass than ordinary neutron stars) could resolve the apparent tension between neutron star radius measurements from GW170817, NICER, and PREX-II, but only if at least one component of the GW170817 binary was a dwarf twin rather than both components being ordinary neutron stars.

## Method

The author constructs equations of state (EOS) that produce two stable branches in the mass-radius relation: a normal nucleonic branch supporting stars up to >2 solar masses, and a dwarf twin branch with maximum masses between ~1.2-1.7 solar masses. Using approximately 5000 EOS samples that support dwarf twins, the author computes tidal deformabilities for binary configurations matching the GW170817 mass ratio (q=0.85) and chirp mass (1.188 solar masses), then compares the resulting binary tidal deformability to observational constraints.

## Key numbers

- Dwarf branches accommodate a mass range typically less than ~0.4 solar masses
- The TOV limit (maximum mass of dwarf twins) of astrophysical interest lies in the range ~1.2-1.7 solar masses
- Normal branch radii are ~12-16 km; dwarf branch radii are ~8-12 km
- For the 1.44 solar mass pulsar J0030+0451, NICER finds radius = 13.02 +1.24 -1.06 km (68% confidence)
- For the 2.08 solar mass pulsar J0740+6620, NICER finds radius = 13.7 +2.6 -1.5 km (68% confidence)
- The constraint on 1.4 solar mass star radius from GW170817 is R = 11.0 +0.9 -0.6 km at 90% level (Capano 2019)
- For scenario I (primary is dwarf), binary tidal deformability = 280; for scenario II (secondary is dwarf), binary tidal deformability = 550
- For the 5000-sample ensemble, scenario I yields binary tidal deformability in range [360, 2030] and scenario II yields [230, 2100]
- Imposing binary tidal deformability less than 800 (600) gives normal branch R(1.4 solar masses) less than 14 km (13.5 km)
- Jumps in energy density associated with the phase transition are ~500-1200 MeV/fm^3 (ratio ~2-5 relative to onset density)
- Central densities at the TOV limit reach ~2.5-2.7 GeV
- Gravitational binding energy released during dwarfnovae is ~0.5-5 x 10^53 ergs

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of twin stars (third-family compact stars arising from first-order phase transitions in QCD) to propose "dwarf" twins with lower maximum masses than the nucleonic branch, a scenario previously dismissed as unphysical. The paper reconciles the tension between the relatively small radii inferred from GW170817 (Capano 2019) and the larger radii from NICER measurements (Miller 2019, 2021) and PREX-II results (2021). The work enables falsifiable predictions testable by future LIGO and NICER observations, and suggests that if confirmed, dwarf twins would provide insights into the QCD phase diagram and potentially dark sector physics.
