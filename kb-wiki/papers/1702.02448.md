# Nuclear matrix element of neutrinoless double-$β$ decay: Relativity and short-range correlations

> Background: The discovery of neutrinoless double-beta ($0νββ$) decay would demonstrate the nature of neutrinos, have profound implications for our understanding of matter-antimatter mystery, and solve the mass hierarchy problem of neutrinos. The calculations for the nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ of $0νββ$ decay are crucial for the interpretation of this process. Purpose: We study the effects of relativity and nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations on the nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ by assuming the mechanism of exchanging light or heavy neutrinos for the $0νββ$ decay. Methods: The nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ are calculated within the framework of covariant density functional theory, where the beyond-mean-field correlations are included in the nuclear wave functions by configuration mixing of both angular-momentum and particle-number projected quadrupole deformed mean-field states. Results: The nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ are obtained for ten $0νββ$-decay candidate nuclei. The impact of relativity is illustrated by adopting relativistic or nonrelativistic decay operators. The effects of short-range correlations are evaluated. Conclusions: The effects of relativity and short-range correlations play an important role in the mechanism of exchanging heavy neutrinos though the influences are marginal for light neutrinos. Combining the nuclear matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ with the observed lower limits on the $0νββ$-decay half-lives, the predicted strongest limits on the effective masses are $|\langle m_ν\rangle|<0.06~\mathrm{eV}$ for light neutrinos and $|\langle m_{ν_h}^{-1}\rangle|^{-1}>3.065\times 10^8~\mathrm{GeV}$ for heavy neutrinos.

## Key claim

The effects of relativity and short-range correlations (SRCs) are marginal for the nuclear matrix element of neutrinoless double-beta decay mediated by light neutrinos, but play an important role for the mechanism of exchanging heavy neutrinos, where the SRCs reduce the matrix element by up to 40% and the relativistic corrections are compensated by the SRC implementation.

## Method

The nuclear matrix elements \(M^{0\nu}\) for ten candidate nuclei were calculated within covariant density functional theory (CDFT) using the relativistic energy functional PC-PK1, with beyond-mean-field correlations included via configuration mixing of angular-momentum and particle-number projected quadrupole deformed mean-field states (generator coordinate method). The decay operators were evaluated in both full relativistic and nonrelativistic-reduced forms, and short-range correlations were incorporated using a Jastrow function with the Argonne V18 parametrization.

## Key numbers

* For the \(0\nu\beta\beta\) decay of \({}^{150}\)Nd \(\to\) \({}^{150}\)Sm with light-neutrino exchange, the total NME is \(M^{0\nu}=5.46\) (full relativistic operator with Argonne SRCs).
* For the same decay with heavy-neutrino exchange, the total NME is \(M^{0\nu}=218.2\) (full relativistic operator with Argonne SRCs).
* The predicted strongest limits on effective masses are \(|\langle m_\nu\rangle|<0.06~\mathrm{eV}\) for light neutrinos and \(|\langle m_{\nu_h}^{-1}\rangle|^{-1}>3.065\times 10^8~\mathrm{GeV}\) for heavy neutrinos.
* For heavy-neutrino exchange, the Argonne-parametrized SRCs reduce the NME by 40%–44% across the ten candidate nuclei (e.g., from 365.3 to 218.2 for \({}^{150}\)Nd).
* For light-neutrino exchange, the Argonne-parametrized SRCs reduce the NME by only 1%–3% (e.g., from 5.59 to 5.46 for \({}^{150}\)Nd).
* The nonrelativistic approximation for the bare heavy-neutrino NME of \({}^{150}\)Nd gives a reduction of 12% (from 365.3 to 320.3), but this difference is eliminated after SRC implementation (218.2 vs. 220.8).

## Context

This work extends previous CDFT calculations by Song (2014) and Yao (2015) that studied light-neutrino exchange, now generalizing to heavy-neutrino exchange and including SRC corrections that were neglected earlier. The results confirm the validity of the nonrelativistic approximation for light-neutrino NMEs found in those prior studies, while revealing significant SRC effects for heavy neutrinos. The systematic NME values for ten candidate nuclei enable improved constraints on neutrino masses when combined with experimental half-life limits from KamLAND-Zen (2016) and NEMO-3 (2016), and the work highlights the need for further investigation of finite nucleon size effects and alternative decay mechanisms such as pion-exchange, as discussed by Simkovic (1992) and Vergados (1982).
