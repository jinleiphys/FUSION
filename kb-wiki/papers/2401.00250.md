# In-medium mass shift of two-flavored heavy mesons, $B_c$, $B^*_c$, $B_s$, $B^*_s$, $D_s$ and $D^*_s$

> For the first time, we estimate the in-medium mass shift of the two-flavored heavy mesons $B_c, B_c^*, B_s, B_s^*, D_s$ and $D_s^*$ in symmetric nuclear matter. The estimates are made by evaluating the lowest order one-loop self-energies. The enhanced excitations of intermediate state heavy-light mesons in symmetric nuclear matter are the origin of their negative mass shift. This negative mass shift may be regarded as a signature of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in an empirical sense because the origin of the negative mass shift in the study is not directly related to the chiral symmetry mechanism. ## Key claim

The in-medium mass shift of two-flavored heavy mesons is estimated for the first time, but the negative mass shift is not directly related to chiral symmetry mechanisms; it may only be regarded as a signature of partial restoration of chiral symmetry in an empirical sense because the origin of the negative mass shift in the study is not directly related to the chiral symmetry mechanism.

## Method

The authors evaluate the lowest order one-loop self-energies for the mesons $B_c, B_c^*, B_s, B_s^*, D_s$ and $D_s^*$ in symmetric nuclear matter using an SU(5) effective Lagrangian density with minimal substitutions. The in-medium Lorentz-scalar effective masses of the intermediate state heavy-light mesons ($B, B^*, D, D^*, K, K^*$) are calculated by the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, and these are used as inputs to compute the self-energies of the two-flavored heavy mesons.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density $\rho_0 = 0.15$ fm$^{-3}$, the QMC model predicts mass shifts for intermediate mesons: $\Delta m_B = -61.13$ MeV, $\Delta m_{B^*} = -61.05$ MeV, $\Delta m_D = -61.97$ MeV, $\Delta m_{D^*} = -61.66$ MeV, $\Delta m_K = -63.20$ MeV, $\Delta m_{K^*} = -61.97$ MeV.
- The free space masses used as input are: $m_{B_c} = 6274.5$ MeV, $m_{B^*_c} = 6333.0$ MeV (average from Martin-Gonzalez (2022)), $m_{B_s} = 5366.9$ MeV, $m_{B^*_s} = 5415.4$ MeV, $m_{D_s} = 1968.4$ MeV, $m_{D^*_s} = 2112.2$ MeV.
- The coupling constant for the $B_c B^* D$ interaction is $g_{B_c B^* D} = g/2\sqrt{2} \approx 11.9$, derived from $g_{\Upsilon BB} \approx 13.2$ using the vector meson dominance model.

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to heavy quarkonia by Krein (2010), Krein (2017), and Zeminiani (2020) to two-flavored heavy mesons, enabling comparison of meson-nuclear matter interaction strengths among quarkonia and two-flavored mesons. The results contradict the naive expectation that the $B_c$ meson mass shift would be intermediate between those of $\eta_c$ and $\eta_b$, and instead show the $B_c$ mass shift is larger than both. The study enables future investigation of how these in-medium mass shifts affect decay rates of processes such as $B_c \rightarrow D_s \ell^+ \ell^-$ in nuclear matter, which are relevant for searches beyond the Standard Model.
