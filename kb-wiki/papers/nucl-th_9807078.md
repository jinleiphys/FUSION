# Collective coordinates, shape transitions and shape coexistence: a microscopic approach

> We investigate a description of shape-mixing and shape-transitions using collective coordinates. To that end we apply a theory of adiabatic large-amplitude motion to a simplified nuclear shell-model, where the approximate results can be contrasted with exact diagonalisations. We find excellent agreement for different regimes, and contrast the results with those from a more standard calculation using a quadrupole constraint. We show that the method employed in this work selects diabatic (crossing) potential energy curves where these are appropriate, and discuss the implications for a microscopic study of shape coexistence.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the local harmonic approximation (LHA) to adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion can self-consistently select either diabatic (crossing) or adiabatic potential energy curves depending on the interaction parameters, and that this method provides useful predictions about shape mixing and shape coexistence in a fully microscopic Hamiltonian, with the caveat that the method fails in the pure quadrupole force limit (G=0) where the mass parameter vanishes.

## Method

The authors apply the local harmonic approximation (LHA) to a simplified nuclear shell model based on the O(4) algebra, which is isomorphic to su(2)⊕su(2) and describes nucleons interacting through a pairing-plus-quadrupole force. They construct classical Hamiltonians from coherent-state representations of the time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations, then truncate to second order in momenta to obtain an adiabatic Hamiltonian. The collective path is found by solving self-consistent equations that combine a force condition with a local RPA equation, and the resulting one-dimensional collective Hamiltonian is requantised to obtain excitation energies and transition matrix elements, which are compared with exact diagonalisations.

## Key numbers

- For the single-j shell O(4) model with 40 particles in a shell with Ω=100, excitation energies are computed as a function of the ratio 2κ/G, covering three regimes: weak quadrupole force (2κ/G=0.079), medium quadrupole force (2κ/G=1.63), and strong quadrupole force (2κ/G=12.7)
- The decoupling measure D is defined as D = (Č_B_{11})·B̄^{11} - 1, where values closer to zero indicate better decoupling of collective from intrinsic motion
- The model uses a single-j shell with pair multiplicity Ω = j+1/2 required to be an even integer for the algebra to close
- The basis dimension for exact diagonalisation of the single-j shell model is n₀+1, where n₀ = N₀/2 is half the particle number

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semi-microscopic study of collective motion with pairing (Nakatsukasa and Walet, 1998) to a fully microscopic Hamiltonian, building on the formalism reviewed in KWD91. The O(4) model was originally developed to describe K^π=0^+ excitations in deformed nuclei (PSS80) and has been used as a test-bed for the boson expansion method (Mat82), the selfconsistent collective coordinate method (Mat86), and a semiclassical method (SM88). The results enable future microscopic studies of shape coexistence phenomena, particularly addressing the long-standing question raised by Hill and Wheeler (1953) about whether nuclear collective potentials are adiabatic or diabatic, and complement configuration-constrained Nilsson-Strutinsky calculations by Bengtsson and Nazarewicz (1989) that suggested diabatic potential-energy curves give more accurate pictures for shape coexistence in Sn and Pb isotopes.
