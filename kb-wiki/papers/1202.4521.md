# Nuclear structure study with core excitations in Ni region: for $fpg_{9/2}$ space

> Shell model calculations for Ni, Cu and Zn isotopes by modifying $fpg$ interaction due to Sorlin {\it et. al.,} [Phys. Rev. Lett. 88, 092501 (2002)] have been reported. In the present work 28 two body matrix elements of the earlier interaction have been modified. Present interaction is able to explain new experimental results for this region.

## Key claim

The paper reports that modifying 28 two-body matrix elements of the Sorlin et al. (2002) $fpg$ interaction, specifically by subtracting 100 keV from $g_{9/2}f_{7/2}$ matrix elements and adding 100 keV to $g_{9/2}f_{5/2}$ matrix elements, yields a new interaction (named $pfg9a$) that can reproduce experimental ground-state spins and low-lying level ordering for neutron-rich Ni, Cu, and Zn isotopes in the $fpg_{9/2}$ model space, though the agreement is not perfect for all states.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed for Ni, Cu, and Zn isotopes with neutron numbers $40 \leq N \leq 50$ using a $^{40}$Ca core with eight frozen $f_{7/2}$ neutrons, including the $0f_{7/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$ proton orbitals and $0f_{7/2}$, $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, $0g_{9/2}$ neutron orbitals. The authors modified 28 two-body matrix elements of the Sorlin et al. (2002) $fpg$ interaction by subtracting 100 keV from $g_{9/2}f_{7/2}$ matrix elements and adding 100 keV to $g_{9/2}f_{5/2}$ matrix elements, creating the $pfg9a$ interaction. Calculations used the ANTOINE code with truncation allowing neutron jumps from ($p_{3/2}$, $f_{5/2}$, $p_{1/2}$) to $g_{9/2}$ and proton jumps from $f_{7/2}$ to ($p_{3/2}$, $f_{5/2}$, $p_{1/2}$).

- Ground-state binding energies for $^{68-76}$Ni: experimental values range from -248.3 MeV ($^{68}$Ni) to -293.7 MeV ($^{76}$Ni); shell-model values range from -227.7 MeV to -267.3 MeV (Table 1).
- Ground-state binding energies for $^{69-79}$Cu: experimental values range from -257.9 MeV ($^{69}$Cu) to -315.2 MeV ($^{79}$Cu); shell-model values range from -235.1 MeV to -287.2 MeV (Table 1).
- Ground-state binding energies for $^{70-80}$Zn: experimental values range from -269.0 MeV ($^{70}$Zn) to -332.0 MeV ($^{80}$Zn); shell-model values range from -242.6 MeV to -302.4 MeV (Table 1).
- For $^{70}$Ni, the calculated $2^+$ state at 1.516 MeV is 257 keV above the experimental level; the $4^+$, $6^+$, and $8^+$ states are predicted at 2.677 MeV, 2.958 MeV, and 3.095 MeV respectively (Section 3.2).
- For $^{76}$Ni, the calculated $2^+$ state is 557 keV above the experimental value; $4^+$, $6^+$, and $8^+$ states are predicted at 2.169 MeV, 2.420 MeV, and 2.532 MeV (Section 3.2).
- For $^{70}$Zn, the calculated $2^+$ state at 0.603 MeV is 282 keV below the experimental value (Section 3.3).
- $B(E2; 1/2^- \rightarrow 3/2^-)$ for $^{69}$Cu: experimental 10.4 W.u., calculated 11.5 W.u. (Table 2).
- $B(E2; 5/2^- \rightarrow 3/2^-)$ for $^{69}$Cu: experimental 3.0 W.u., calculated 9.9 W.u. (Table 2).
- $B(E2; 7/2^- \rightarrow 3/2^-)$ for $^{73}$Cu: experimental 14.9 W.u., calculated 0.0038 W.u. (Table 2).
- Single-particle energies used: $0f_{7/2}$ at 0.0 MeV, $1p_{3/2}$ at 2.0 MeV, $0f_{5/2}$ at 6.5 MeV, $1p_{1/2}$ at 4.0 MeV, $0g_{9/2}$ at 9.0 MeV (Section 2.1).

## Context

This work extends shell-model studies in the Ni region by modifying the Sorlin et al. (2002) $fpg$ interaction, which was built using $fp$ two-body matrix elements from Poves et al. (2001) and $1p_{3/2}$, $0f_{5/2}$, $1p_{1/2}$, $0g_{9/2}$ matrix elements from Nowacki (1996). The author notes that previous interactions (Jensen et al. 1995, Nowacki 1996, Smith et al. 2004, Lisetskiy et al. 2004, Honma et al. 2009) gave unsatisfactory results for very neutron-rich nuclei, including large $E(2^+)$ values for $^{76}$Ni and $^{80}$Zn, small $B(E2)$ values, and incorrect ground-state spins for $^{75-79}$Cu. The new $pfg9a$ interaction enables improved description of yrast levels and $B(E2)$ systematics for Ni, Cu, and Zn isotopes, with the author suggesting further modification and inclusion of the $1d_{5/2}$ orbit may be important, as also attempted by Sieja (2010) and Lenzi et al. (2010).
