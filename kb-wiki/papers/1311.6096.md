# Neutrino scattering from hydrodynamic modes in hot and dense neutron matter

> We calculate the scattering rate of low energy neutrinos in hot and dense neutron matter encountered in neutrons stars and supernova in the hydrodynamic regime. We find that the Brillouin peak, associated with the sound mode, and the Rayleigh peak, associated with the thermal diffusion mode, dominate the dynamic structure factor. Although the total scattering cross section is constrained by the compressibility sum rule, the differential cross-section calculated using the hydrodynamic response function differs from results obtained in approximate treatments often used in astrophysics such as random phase approximations (RPA). We identified these differences and discuss its implications for neutrino transport in supernova.

## Key claim

The total neutrino scattering cross section in hot dense neutron matter is tightly constrained by the compressibility sum rule, so both the hydrodynamic approach and random phase approximation (RPA) yield similar total cross sections; however, the differential cross section from the hydrodynamic response differs from RPA results, particularly at forward scattering angles where collective mode features appear.

## Method

The authors calculated the density-density response function for neutron matter in the hydrodynamic regime using a moment expansion method to solve the linearized Boltzmann equation, with an effective zero-range S-wave interaction whose strength was fixed by matching to the compressibility from the NL3 nuclear equation of state. They then obtained the dynamic structure factor via the fluctuation-dissipation theorem and computed neutrino scattering cross sections, comparing results with those from RPA and free Fermi gas calculations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron matter density used: n = 10^{-2} fm^{-3}, temperature T = 5 MeV, with incident neutrino energy E_nu = 3T
- Total cross section per volume for free Fermi gas: 2.97 x 10^{-4} m^{-1}; for Hartree: 2.75 x 10^{-4} m^{-1}; for RPA: 8.20 x 10^{-4} m^{-1}; for hydrodynamic response: 8.82 x 10^{-4} m^{-1}
- Ratio of hydrodynamic to free Fermi gas total cross section: 2.97; ratio of RPA to Hartree total cross section: 2.98
- The ratio of hydrodynamic to free Fermi gas transport cross section is about 3 for almost all incident energies above 1 MeV
- Ambient conditions in the neutrino-sphere: densities 10^{12} - 10^{14} g/cm^3 and temperatures T = 3-8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino scattering in dense neutron matter beyond the quasi-particle picture (RPA) used by Horowitz (1991), Reddy (1998, 1999), and Burrows (1998, 1999) into the hydrodynamic regime where multiple collisions between nucleons become relevant. The authors used transport coefficients (shear viscosity and thermal conductivity) from Benhar (2010) and followed the moment expansion method developed by Watabe (2009, 2010). The results enable improved modeling of neutrino transport in supernova simulations, particularly for understanding the neutrino spectrum formation in the neutrino-sphere region of proto-neutron stars.
