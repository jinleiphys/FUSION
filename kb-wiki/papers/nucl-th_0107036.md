# The effect of surface and Coulomb interaction on the liquid-gas phase transition of finite nuclei

> By means of the Furnstahl, Serot and Tang's model, the effects of surface tension and Coulomb interaction on the liquid-gas phase transition for finite nuclei are investigated. A limit pressure p-lim above which the liquid-gas phase transition cannot take place has been found. It is found that comparing to the Coulomb interaction, the contribution of surface tension is dominate in low temperature regions. The binodal surface is also addressed.

## Key claim

Using the Furnstahl-Serot-Tang (FST) model, the authors find that for finite nuclei a limit pressure \(p_{\lim}\) exists above which the liquid-gas phase transition cannot take place, and that in low temperature regions the surface tension effect dominates over the Coulomb interaction, contrary to what might be expected from infinite nuclear matter treatments.

## Method

The authors employ the FST relativistic mean-field model to compute the thermodynamic potential and pressure for finite nuclear matter, treating the nucleus as a liquid droplet. They incorporate the Coulomb interaction via a uniformly charged sphere approximation and the surface tension via a temperature-dependent surface energy term, then solve the two-phase coexistence equations (equality of neutron chemical potentials, proton chemical potentials including Coulomb term, and pressures including both Coulomb and surface contributions) to construct the binodal surface and determine the limit pressure.

## Key numbers

- Limit pressure \(p_{\lim} = 0.018\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-3}\) at temperature \(T = 5\ \text{MeV}\), above which the liquid-gas phase transition cannot occur.
- Critical temperature of the liquid-gas phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter is \(T_c = 14.75\ \text{MeV}\).
- Limit temperature \(T_{\lim}\) for the FST model is in the range \(5.4-8.8\ \text{MeV}\).
- Surface tension parameterization: \(\gamma(T) = (1.14\ \text{MeV}\,\text{fm}^{-2})\left[1 + \frac{3T}{2T_c}\right]\left[1 - \frac{T}{T_c}\right]^{3/2}\).
- FST model parameter set T1: \(g_s^2 = 99.3\), \(g_v^2 = 154.5\), \(g_\rho^2 = 70.2\), \(m_s = 509\ \text{MeV}\), \(S_0 = 90.6\ \text{MeV}\), \(\zeta = 0.0402\), \(\eta = -0.496\), \(d = 2.70\).

## Context

This work extends the study of liquid-gas phase transitions from infinite nuclear matter to finite nuclei, building on the earlier work of Muller and Serot (1995) who identified a second-order phase transition in multi-component charged systems, and on the authors' own previous paper (Qian, Su, Wang, 2000) which considered density-dependent nucleon-nucleon-rho meson coupling. The finding of a limit pressure parallels the earlier discovery of a limit temperature for the FST model by Zhang, Song, Wang, and Su (1999), and the results enable future studies of Coulomb instability and phase coexistence in finite nuclear systems.
