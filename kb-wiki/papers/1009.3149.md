# Effective interactions and long distance symmetries in the Nucleon-Nucleon system

> Effective interactions, when defined in a coarse grained sense, such as Vlowk at the scale 450 MeV, display a remarkable symmetry pattern. Serber symmetry works with high accuracy for spin triplet states. Wigner SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry with nucleons in the fundamental representation works only for even partial waves exactly as predicted by large Nc limit of QCD with accuracy O(1/Nc^2). This suggests tailoring the very definition of effective interactions to provide a best possible fulfillment of long distance symmetries. With the Vlowk definition Wigner symmetry requires that chiral potentials have low cut-offs (450 MeV). Perturbative saturation of heavy mesonic resonances does not faithfully display the Serber symmetry pattern.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the coarse-grained effective interaction \(V_{\rm low k}\) (defined at a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda = 450\ \mathrm{MeV}\)) displays Wigner SU(4) spin-isospin symmetry for even partial waves and Serber symmetry for spin-triplet states, but these symmetries are not properties of the bare NN potential; they emerge only after renormalization and are broken by short-distance counterterms.

## Method

The authors analyze phase shifts from the Nijmegen group (Stoks 1993) and the Argonne V18 potential (Wiringa 1994), decomposing them into central, tensor, and spin-orbit components using first-order perturbation theory around a central potential. They compare the bare potentials and their corresponding \(V_{\rm low k}\) versions (obtained by integrating out high-momentum components above \(\Lambda\)) to test whether Wigner and Serber symmetries hold. The large-\(N_c\) expansion of QCD is used to interpret the symmetry pattern, and the resonance saturation hypothesis (matching heavy meson exchanges to chiral potentials) is tested against the \(V_{\rm low k}\) results.

## Key numbers

- The \(V_{\rm low k}\) cutoff is set at \(\Lambda = 450\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (or equivalently \(\Lambda \le 2.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\)).
- Wigner symmetry holds for even partial waves with accuracy \(\mathcal{O}(1/N_c^2)\) as predicted by the large-\(N_c\) limit of QCD.
- For the \(^1S_0\) channel, the low-energy constant \(C_0(\Lambda)\) reproduces the exact \(V_{\rm low k}\) value at \(\Lambda \sim 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\); for the \(^3S_1\) channel, at \(\Lambda \sim 200\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- Inclusion of tensor force mixing and range corrections saturates the \(V_{\rm low k}\) to almost \(100\%\) at \(\Lambda \sim 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and yields \(C_{^1S_0} = C_{^3S_1}\).
- Chiral potentials at N\(^3\)LO require a cutoff \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 450\ \mathrm{MeV}\) to preserve Wigner symmetry; larger cutoffs \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 600\ \mathrm{MeV}\) produce increasing violations.

## Context

This work extends the observation by Holt (2003) that all high-precision NN potentials reduce to a universal \(V_{\rm low k}\) when \(\Lambda \le 2.1\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), and it builds on earlier partial-wave analyses (Holinde 1975) that noted statistical correlations among channels. The authors show that the \(V_{\rm low k}\) approach acts as a symmetry filter, revealing Wigner and Serber symmetries that are hidden in the bare potentials. This enables a cleaner separation of long-distance physics from short-distance fine-tuning, and suggests that effective interactions should be defined to maximize these symmetries, with implications for nuclear structure calculations and the construction of chiral potentials.
