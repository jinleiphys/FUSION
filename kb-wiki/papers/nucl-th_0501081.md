# Formal Relation among Various Hermitian and non-Hermitian Effective Interactions

> A general definition of the model-space effective interaction is given. The energy-independent effective hamiltonians derived in a time-independent way are classified systematically.

## Key claim

The paper provides a general, unified definition of the model-space effective hamiltonian via a two-parameter transformation \(X(n)\) and the mapping operator \(\omega\), showing that all energy-independent effective interactions derived in a time-independent approach, both hermitian and non-hermitian, can be systematically classified as special cases of the general form \({\cal H}(m,n)\), with the condition that the decoupling equation \(Q{\overline H}(m,n)P=0\) is satisfied.

## Method

The authors introduce an operator \(\omega = Q\omega P\) that maps between the model space \(P\) and its complement \(Q\), and define a transformation \(X(n) = (1+\omega-\omega^\dagger)(1+\omega^\dagger\omega+\omega\omega^\dagger)^n\). They then construct a transformed hamiltonian \({\overline H}(m,n) = X^{-1}(m) H X(n)\) and an overlap operator \(\chi(m,n) = (P+\omega^\dagger\omega)^{n-m}\). The \(P\)-space effective hamiltonian is given by \({\cal H}(m,n) = P{\overline H}(m,n)P\). By choosing specific integer or half-integer values of \(m\) and \(n\), the formalism reproduces known effective hamiltonians: the Bloch-Horowitz form (\(m=n=0\)), the Kato form (\(m=0, n=-1\)), and the hermitian forms of des Cloizeaux, Okubo, and the Van Vleck (canonical) form (\(m=n=-1/2\)). The relation to the \(Z\)-transformation method of Holt, Kuo and Brown (2004) is also established, showing that different choices of the \(Z\) operator (Okubo, Andreozzi, Suzuki-Okamoto) generate a family of hermitian effective interactions from the same non-hermitian \(V_{LS}\).

## Key numbers

- The effective hamiltonian \({\cal H}(0,0) = P H (P+\omega)\) is non-hermitian and corresponds to the standard Bloch-Horowitz form.
- The effective hamiltonian \({\cal H}(0,-1) = P H (P+\omega)(P+\omega^\dagger\omega)^{-1}\) is hermitian and equivalent to Kato's effective hamiltonian.
- The effective hamiltonian \({\cal H}(-1/2,-1/2) = (P+\omega^\dagger\omega)^{-1/2}(P+\omega^\dagger)H(P+\omega)(P+\omega^\dagger\omega)^{-1/2}\) is hermitian and equivalent to the des Cloizeaux, Okubo, and Van Vleck (canonical) forms.
- The overlap operator for \({\cal H}(0,0)\) is \(\chi(0,0)=P\); for \({\cal H}(0,-1)\) it is \(\chi(0,-1)=(P+\omega^\dagger\omega)^{-1}\); for \({\cal H}(-1/2,-1/2)\) it is \(\chi(-1/2,-1/2)=P\).
- The transformation \(X(-1/2)\) is shown to equal \(e^G\) where \(G = \operatorname{arctanh}(\omega-\omega^\dagger)\), establishing the canonical (Van Vleck) form.

## Context

This work extends the formal classification of effective interactions previously discussed by Suzuki and Okamoto (1994, 1984) and by Holt, Kuo and Brown (2004), who studied hermitian effective interactions via the \(Z\)-transformation method. The paper provides a unified framework showing that the non-hermitian Bloch-Horowitz form, the hermitian Kato form, and the hermitian des Cloizeaux/Okubo/Van Vleck forms are all special cases of a single two-parameter family \({\cal H}(m,n)\). This enables practitioners to systematically choose or compare different effective interactions for nuclear many-body calculations, and clarifies the formal relationship between the \(Z\)-transformation approach and the older operator-based methods.
