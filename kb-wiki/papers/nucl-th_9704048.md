# Stochastic Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock for heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi energy domain

> We present the first realization of Stochastic TDHF, a theory which goes beyond pure mean-field dynamics, embracing dissipation as well as fluctuations. Applications to heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi energy domain are given and analyzed in terms of dissipative features and Intermediate Mass Fragment production.

## Key claim

Stochastic Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (STDHF) can be practically implemented for heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi energy domain using an intuitive jump algorithm inspired by the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) approach, and this first realization produces physically reasonable dissipation and fragment formation, though the simplified version using global rather than local averages may overestimate collision rates after the compound stage.

## Method

The authors implemented a simplified STDHF scheme in a 3D nuclear TDHF code without symmetry restrictions, using a Skyrme force with density-dependent zero-range interaction. The method generates an ensemble of TDHF trajectories by occasional stochastic jumps: for each pair of single-particle wavefunctions, local average positions and momenta are computed, and if the particles approach within a scattering length derived from an effective nucleon-nucleon cross section, their momenta are changed while conserving local momentum and kinetic energy. Pauli blocking is evaluated from the overlap of old and new TDHF states, and the new wavefunctions are orthogonalized and the total energy is restored via iterative imaginary time steps.

## Key numbers

* Head-on collision of 16O on 16O at 50 MeV/u beam energy: STDHF ensemble of 100 trajectories shows damping of the quadrupole moment Q20 to zero, indicating compound nucleus formation, while pure TDHF shows transparency.
* Two-body collisions in STDHF peak around t = 30 fm/c, corresponding to maximum overlap of the two colliding nuclei.
* At 50 MeV/u beam energy with impact parameters b = 0 and 2 fm, both cases lead to almost 100% incomplete fusion with average mass <A> ~ 22 +/- 1 and charge <Z> ~ 11 +/- 1.
* At 75 MeV/u beam energy, fission and fragmentation mechanisms take over, with fission still dominating but a sizeable number of events producing three fragments.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) beyond pure mean-field dynamics, which is known to be insufficient for central heavy-ion collisions in the Fermi energy domain as discussed by Davies et al. (1985). The approach provides a quantal alternative to the semiclassical Boltzmann-Langevin equation (BLE) introduced by Abe et al. (1996), which has faced huge computational and formal difficulties in large-scale applications. The results enable future studies of Intermediate Mass Fragment (IMF) production and fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, with the authors noting that the computational effort per STDHF event is comparable to an acceptable BUU calculation while providing richer physical content including full fluctuations.
