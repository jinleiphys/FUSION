# Collective excitations of a hot QCD medium in a time dependent background magnetic field

> Collective modes within a hot Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) medium are obtained from the polarization tensor, considering both constant and time-varying electromagnetic fields. In both scenarios, five complex modes emerge, reliant on the wave vector ($k$), with electrical conductivity exerting significant influence. The impact of the modes on the energy loss of heavy quarks in the hot QCD medium with a background electromagnetic field has been studied by obtaining the induced electric field in terms of the polarization tensor while invoking Wong's equations. The findings are seen to be consistent with analogous approaches, reinforcing the significance of the results.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a hot QCD medium with a weak, time-varying background magnetic field, five complex collective modes emerge from the polarization tensor, with two of the purely imaginary modes showing positive growth that may indicate instabilities; this result is conditional on the use of the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) for the collision kernel and on the assumption that the cyclotron frequency is approximately equal to the decay frequency of the magnetic field.

## Method

The authors derived the polarization tensor for a hot QCD medium using semi-classical transport theory with the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) for the collision kernel, considering both constant and time-dependent electromagnetic fields. They decomposed the polarization tensor into longitudinal, transverse, magnetic-field-direction, and antisymmetric projection components, then solved for the poles of the propagator to obtain dispersion relations. The energy loss of heavy quarks was computed by solving Maxwell's equations for the induced electric field and applying Wong's equations.

## Key numbers

* Five complex collective modes emerge from the dispersion relations, with two modes (\(\omega_2\) and \(\omega_5\)) showing positive imaginary parts that may lead to instabilities.
* The dispersion relations are plotted at temperature \(T = 2T_C\) with \(T_C = 0.17\) GeV.
* Time-dependent magnetic fields with decay rates \(\tau_b = 10\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(\tau_b = 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) are compared against the constant magnetic field case.
* The energy loss of charm quarks is greater than that of bottom quarks at the same momentum, consistent with the heavier quark traveling at lower velocity.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of collective modes in hot QCD media that considered constant magnetic fields (Formanek 2021) and momentum anisotropy (Carrington 2014, Romatschke 2003, 2004), as well as earlier calculations of electrical conductivity in time-dependent electromagnetic fields (Feng 2017, Thakur 2019, Dey 2019, Hattori 2016, Fukushima 2017, Dash 2020, Kurian 2017, 2019, Ghosh 2019, Gowthama 2020, K 2021). The results enable future investigations into the effects of the equation of state, momentum anisotropy, and more realistic collision kernels (BGK and modified BGK) on collective behavior, as well as calculations of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) to connect theoretical predictions with experimental data from RHIC and LHC.
