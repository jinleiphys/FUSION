# Single-Transverse-Spin Asymmetries in Exclusive Photo-production of J/psi in Ultra-Peripheral Collisions in the Fixed-Target Mode at the LHC and in the Collider Mode at RHIC

> We investigate the potentialities offered by the study of J/psi exclusive photo-production in ultra-peripheral collisions at a fixed-target experiment using the proton and lead LHC beams (generically denoted as AFTER@LHC) on hydrogen targets and at RHIC in the collider mode. We compare the expected counting rates in both set-ups. Studying Single-Transverse-Spin Asymmetries (A_N) in such a process provides a direct path to the proton Generalised Parton Distribution (GPD) E_g(x,xi,t). We evaluate the expected precision on A_N for realistic conditions with the LHCb detector in pH(pol) and PbH(pol) collisions. We also discuss prospects with polarised deuterium and helium targets in the case of AFTER@LHC.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Single-Transverse-Spin Asymmetries (STSA or \(A_N\)) in exclusive \(J/\psi\) photo-production via ultra-peripheral collisions can provide a direct path to the gluon Generalised Parton Distribution \(E_g(x,\xi,t)\), and that the fixed-target mode at the LHC (AFTER@LHC) can achieve the first measurement of this GPD by 2025, with the caveat that the predictions rely on a specific model (variant V4 of Koempel and Kroll) for the largely unknown \(E^g\) and that the asymmetry magnitude is sizeable only for moderate \(W_{\gamma p}\) values.

## Method

The authors evaluated expected cross sections and counting rates for exclusive \(J/\psi\) photo-production in ultra-peripheral collisions using the Starlight Monte Carlo generator for four collision systems: \(p\)H\(^\uparrow\) and PbH\(^\uparrow\) in fixed-target mode at AFTER@LHC (with LHCb-like detector conditions), and \(p^\uparrow p^\uparrow\) and Au\(^\uparrow p^\uparrow\) in collider mode at RHIC (with STAR detector conditions). They then used the Equivalent Photon Approximation to convert photonic STSAs (derived from a GPD-based formalism involving the gluon form factors \(\mathcal{H}^g\) and \(\mathcal{E}^g\)) into hadronic STSAs, folding in the expected statistical yields and target polarisation to estimate the achievable precision on \(A_N\).

## Key numbers

* For \(p\)H\(^\uparrow\) collisions at AFTER@LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 115 GeV), the total \(J/\psi \to \ell^+\ell^-\) cross section after pseudo-rapidity and \(P_T\) cuts is 20.64 pb, with an integrated luminosity of \(1.0 \times 10^4\) pb\(^{-1}\)yr\(^{-1}\).
* For PbH\(^\uparrow\) collisions at AFTER@LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 72 GeV), the total \(J/\psi \to \ell^+\ell^-\) cross section after cuts is \(9.81 \times 10^3\) pb, with an integrated luminosity of 0.12 pb\(^{-1}\)yr\(^{-1}\).
* The expected statistical precision on the STSA \(A_N\) is 1 to 4% for \(p\)H\(^\uparrow\) collisions and 10 to 40% for PbH\(^\uparrow\) collisions, over an accessible \(x_F\) range from 0 down to -0.35.
* The maximum photon-nucleon centre-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s_{\gamma N}^{\rm max}}\) is 44 GeV for \(p\)H\(^\uparrow\) and 12 GeV for PbH\(^\uparrow\) at AFTER@LHC, compared to 77 GeV for \(p^\uparrow p^\uparrow\) at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) = 510 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the study of exclusive vector quarkonium photo-production as a probe of gluon GPDs, following the theoretical framework of Koempel and Kroll (2011) who first derived the STSA expression in terms of \(E^g\). It builds on the AFTER@LHC proposal of Hadjidakis et al. (2018) for fixed-target experiments using LHC beams, and compares directly with the capabilities of RHIC collider-mode measurements (Aschenauer et al., 2016). The results enable a first experimental determination of the gluon GPD \(E_g\), which is crucial for the Ji sum rule decomposition of the proton spin, and open the possibility to study GPDs of light nuclei (deuterium, helium-3, helium-4) using polarised targets.
