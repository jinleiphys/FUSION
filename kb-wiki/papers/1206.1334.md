# Conformal anomaly as a source of soft photons in heavy ion collisions

> We introduce a novel photon production mechanism stemming from the conformal anomaly of QCDxQED and the existence of strong (electro)magnetic fields in heavy ion collisions. Using the hydrodynamical description of the bulk modes of QCD plasma, we show that this mechanism leads to the photon production yield that is comparable to the yield from conventional sources. This mechanism also provides a significant positive contribution to the azimuthal anisotropy of photons, $v_2$, as well as to the radial "flow". We compare our results to the data from the PHENIX Collaboration.

## Key claim

The conformal anomaly of QCD×QED, combined with strong magnetic fields in heavy ion collisions, provides a novel photon production mechanism whose yield is comparable to conventional sources and which can account for a large fraction of the observed azimuthal anisotropy \(v_2\) of direct photons, potentially resolving the "\(v_2\) puzzle." The authors hedge that their calculations are schematic and rely on crude approximations, using the most conservative estimates for bulk viscosity and other input parameters, yet still find a substantial contribution.

## Method

The authors compute the photon production rate from the triangle diagram coupling the trace of the energy-momentum tensor (the conformal anomaly) to two photons via a scalar dilaton \(\sigma\), using the effective Lagrangian \(\mathcal{L}_{\sigma\gamma\gamma} = g_{\sigma\gamma\gamma}\,\sigma\,F_{\mu\nu}F^{\mu\nu}\). They evaluate the imaginary part of the photon self-energy in a magnetic field, using a hydrodynamic spectral function for the bulk mode \(\theta\) dominated by bulk viscosity \(\zeta\), and approximate the time evolution of the magnetic field and temperature using eikonal and Bjorken hydrodynamics, respectively. The resulting photon yield and azimuthal anisotropy are compared to PHENIX data for minimum bias Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* The dilaton mass \(m_\sigma = 550\) MeV and decay constant \(f_\sigma = 100\) MeV yield a two-photon decay width \(\Gamma(\sigma \to \gamma\gamma) \simeq 5\) KeV, consistent with the PDG range of \(2 \div 10\) KeV for the \(f_0(600)\) meson.
* The coupling constant \(g_{\sigma\gamma\gamma} \simeq 0.02\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\) is fixed from the decay width.
* The bulk viscosity is parameterized as \(\zeta/\eta = C_\zeta (1/3 - c_s^2)^2\) with \(C_\zeta = 2.5 \div 5\), using \(\eta/s = 1/4\pi\) and lattice-based speed of sound \(c_s^2 = 0.175 \div 0.221\).
* The initial time for Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV is \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm/\(c\).
* The magnetic field components are approximated as \(e B_{x,y}(t) \simeq e B^0_{x,y} / (1 + (t/t_B)^2)\), with \(B^0_x\) approximately independent of impact parameter and \(B^0_y\) linear in impact parameter.

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum anomalies in relativistic plasmas, previously focused on the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2004, 2007; Fukushima 2008) and the chiral separation effect (Son 2004, 2009), to the conformal (scale) anomaly. It addresses the "\(v_2\) puzzle" for soft direct photons observed by PHENIX (Adare 2011), where conventional thermal photon production (Chatterjee 2007) predicts much smaller elliptic flow than measured, and jet-medium interactions (Turbide 2005) even predict a negative contribution. The mechanism enables future tests via photon polarization, low-mass dilepton pairs, and uranium-uranium collisions (Voloshin 2010) to separate initial eccentricity from magnetic field effects.
