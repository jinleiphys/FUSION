# Order, Collectivity, Structured Yrast Lines, and Correlated Energies in Random Bosonic Systems

> Signatures of order and collectivity appear in a broad range of one and two level boson systems. Toy systems of $N$ particles on a single $j=3,\,4\dots 7$ level, and on 2 levels $(j_1,\,j_2)$ with $j_1+j_2 \leq 6$ were studied. Surprising novel features in the yrast lines were consistent with boson condensates. Strong signatures of order and collectivity were the norm. These included the usual energy ratios and quadrupole transition strengths for vibrational and rotational bands. There were pronounced correlations in energy ratios, sharp peaks in the Alga ratios and fractional collectivity across all the 1-level systems, as well as signatures of triaxiality

## Key claim

Signatures of order and collectivity, including rotational and vibrational bands, appear universally in random bosonic systems with one or two single-particle levels, not just in the special case of the interacting boson model (IBM). The yrast lines show novel step-like structures consistent with boson condensates, and the correlations in energy ratios previously seen in the IBM persist across a much broader range of single-particle spins.

## Method

The author constructed ensembles of random two-body interactions conserving angular momentum for systems of N bosons on a single j-level (j = 3 through 7) and on two levels (j1, j2) with j1 + j2 ≤ 6. Hamiltonians were diagonalized in the M = 0 subspace, and J labels were assigned using the J² operator. For each ensemble of 2 × 10⁴ to 5 × 10⁴ spectra, the author computed ground-state spin distributions, yrast energies, B(E2) transition strengths, quadrupole moments, fractional collectivity, Alaga ratios, and triaxiality measures, then examined correlations among these observables.

## Key numbers

- For (N,j) = (8,4), the ground-state spin distribution p(J) shows pronounced peaks at J = 0, 4, 24, and 32, with f₀ (probability of spin-0 ground state) and f_{Jmax} together accounting for around 20% of total spectra
- The 0-2 subset (0gs spectra with a J=2 first excited state) has f_{0-2} values ranging from 0.06 for (N,j)=(6,6) to 0.20 for (N,j)=(12,3)
- R₄₂ shows a sharp peak at 3.3 (the rigid rotor value of 10/3) for N=8, 10, and 12 systems, coming solely from the 0-2 and Jgs=Jmax subsets
- For (N,j)=(8,4), fractional collectivity fc peaks at 1.0 for the 0-2 subset, and the Alaga ratio A shows large sharp peaks at 0.43 for both the 0-2 and Jgs=Jmax subsets
- The triaxiality measure R_{triA} has a prominent peak at 1.02 for (N,j)=(8,4), and R_{triB} shows peaks at 2 for Jgs=Jmax spectra and at 5 for 0gs spectra
- For Jgs=Jmax systems with (N,j)=(10,4), the yrast energy at maximum angular momentum is E(Jmax) = (1/2)N(N-1)V_{2j} = 45V₈

## Context

This work extends the study of random interactions in bosonic systems beyond the IBM (j=0,2) and vibron model (j=0,1) studied by Bijker et al. (1999, 2000) and the correlated energy ratios seen by Johnson and Nam (2007) and Lei et al. (2011). The author shows that the same correlations (labeled α, β, γ, δ, and a new ρ correlation) appear in a much broader range of single-particle spins, and that the yrast lines for Jgs=0, j, Jmax-N, and Jmax subsets all show systematic step-like structures interpretable as boson condensates. The results enable future work connecting random ensemble phenomenology to specific dynamical symmetries of the Hamiltonian, as previously done by Fu et al. (2018) for the IBM.
