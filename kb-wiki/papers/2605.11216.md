# The mean-field theory of superfluid-superconducting vortex states in the outer core of neutron stars

> Purpose: Characterize superfluid-superconducting vortex states at arbitrary pressures with $T_{cp}\neq T_{cn}$, assuming both proton and neutron mean-fields are formed by spin-0 Cooper pairs. Method: The existing mean-field theory is extended to account for $T_{cp}\neq T_{cn}$. The pressure dependence of the pairing gap energy $Δ_{\alpha0}$ is quantitatively established on the basis of the effective chiral field theory. To link $T_{cα}$ with $Δ_{\alpha0}$, I use the weak-coupling result $T_{cα}\approx0.57Δ_{\alpha0}$. A quadratic scaled-temperature ($T/T_{cp}$) dependence of the thermodynamic magnetic field is postulated in analogy with pure superconductors. The $T/T_{cα}$-dependence of the gap $Δ_{αT}$ is inferred from the many-body approximations for the pure neutron matter. Results: An empirical $T/T_{cα}$-dependence for the mean-field is constructed to account for the interplay between the condensation and the magnetic energies. The superfluid entrainment is found to increase the size of the vortex core and to decrease the effective magnetic penetration depth. The size of the neutron vortex core is found to be larger than the magnetic penetration depth in the outer core. Conclusions: The usual approximation of infinitely thin vortex line (the London's approximation) for the neutron vortex is found to be irrelevant in the entire outer core and for the proton vortex is found to be limited to vicinity of the crust-core transition. The developed mean-field theory paves the way to study the vortex microscopic structure, the angular momentum, the magnetization and the vortex-fluxtube interaction energy.

# The mean-field theory of superfluid-superconducting vortex states in the outer core of neutron stars

## Key claim

The paper extends the mean-field theory of superfluid-superconducting vortex states in neutron star outer cores to the general case where the proton and neutron pairing critical temperatures are unequal ($T_{cp} \neq T_{cn}$), relaxing the earlier assumptions that either $T_{cp}=T_{cn}$ or that temperature is negligible compared to both critical temperatures. The London approximation of infinitely thin vortex lines is found to be irrelevant for neutron vortices throughout the entire outer core and for proton vortices only near the crust-core transition.

## Method

The author extends the existing mean-field theory (Kobyakov 2020) by incorporating temperature-dependent coefficients and order parameters, using the weak-coupling relation $T_{c\alpha} \approx 0.57 \Delta_{\alpha 0}$ to link critical temperatures to zero-temperature pairing gaps. The pressure dependence of the pairing gap energies $\Delta_{\alpha 0}$ is established from effective chiral field theory calculations (Drischler et al. 2017; Lim and Holt 2021), and the temperature dependence of the gap $\Delta_{\alpha T}$ is inferred from many-body approximations for pure neutron matter (Drissi and Rios 2022). A quadratic scaled-temperature dependence of the thermodynamic magnetic field is postulated in analogy with terrestrial superconductors, and empirical fits to the gap temperature dependence are constructed using two functional forms.

## Key numbers

- The weak-coupling relation used is $T_{c\alpha} \approx 0.57 \Delta_{\alpha 0}$, linking critical temperature to the zero-temperature pairing gap.
- The superfluid entrainment coupling parameter $c_{np}$ lies in the range $-0.5 \lesssim c_{np} \lesssim -0.245$, based on earlier work (Kobyakov et al. 2017).
- The nuclear saturation density used is $n_0 = 0.1561$ fm$^{-3}$, obtained from the Sk$\chi$450 nuclear interaction parametrization.
- The fit parameters for the scaled gap functions are $c_1 = 1.4$ (for Eq. fTTc) and $c_2 = 1.5$ (for Eq. fT0).
- The coherence length is given by $\xi_{\alpha T} = \hbar v_{F\alpha} / (\pi \Delta_{\alpha T})$, where $v_{F\alpha} = \hbar k_{F\alpha} / m_p$ is the Fermi velocity.

## Context

This work extends the mean-field theory developed by Kobyakov (2020), which was limited to the special case $T_{cp}=T_{cn}$, and generalizes earlier studies by Alpar et al. (1984) that assumed $T \ll T_{c\alpha}$ and neglected temperature dependence of the mean fields. The paper addresses the fact that modern pairing calculations (Drischler et al. 2017; Lim and Holt 2021) predict significant pressure dependence of both critical temperatures, meaning the conditions $T \ll T_{c\alpha}$ and $T_{cp}=T_{cn}$ do not hold universally in the outer core. The developed framework enables future studies of vortex microscopic structure, angular momentum, magnetization, and vortex-fluxtube interaction energy, and provides a foundation for incorporating spin-1 neutron triplet superfluidity (as studied by Yasui et al. 2019; Mizushima et al. 2021; Kobayashi and Nitta 2022, 2023) into a more complete description.
