# Neutrino Magnetic Moment, CP Violation and Flavor Oscillations in Matter

> We consider collective oscillations of neutrinos, which are emergent nonlinear flavor evolution phenomena instigated by neutrino-neutrino interactions in astrophysical environments with sufficiently high neutrino densities. We investigate the symmetries of the problem in the full three flavor mixing scheme and in the exact many-body formulation by including the effects of CP violation and neutrino magnetic moment. We show that, similar to the two flavor scheme, several dynamical symmetries exist for three flavors in the single-angle approximation if the net electron background in the environment and the effects of the neutrino magnetic moment are negligible. Moreover, we show that these dynamical symmetries are present even when the CP symmetry is violated in neutrino oscillations. We explicitly write down the constants of motion through which these dynamical symmetries manifest themselves in terms of the generators of the SU(3) flavor transformations. We also show that the effects due to the CP-violating Dirac phase factor out of the many-body evolution operator and evolve independently of nonlinear flavor transformations if neutrino electromagnetic interactions are ignored. In the presence of a strong magnetic field, CP-violating effects can still be considered independently provided that an effective definition for neutrino magnetic moment is used.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that dynamical symmetries exist for three-flavor collective neutrino oscillations in the single-angle approximation when the net electron background and neutrino magnetic moment effects are negligible, and that these symmetries persist even when CP symmetry is violated. The CP-violating Dirac phase can be factored out of the full many-body evolution operator and evolves independently of nonlinear flavor transformations when neutrino electromagnetic interactions are ignored, but this factorization requires an effective definition of the neutrino magnetic moment when a strong magnetic field is present.

## Method

The authors employed an operator formalism based on SU(3) generators to describe three-flavor mixing in the exact many-body formulation, extending the isospin-type formalism previously used for two-flavor schemes. They constructed the full Hamiltonian including vacuum oscillations, matter refraction, and neutrino self-interactions, then analyzed the dynamical symmetries by identifying constants of motion in terms of the generators of SU(3) flavor transformations under the single-angle approximation. The factorization of the CP-violating Dirac phase was examined by applying the transformation operator Q to the Hamiltonian and evolution operator, both with and without the inclusion of neutrino magnetic moment effects.

## Key numbers

- The Standard Model minimally extended with right-handed neutrinos yields neutrino dipole moments of order 10^{-19} Bohr magnetons or smaller
- Current experimental upper limit on the anomalous magnetic moment of the neutrino is of order 10^{-11} Bohr magnetons (Beda 2013)
- An upper limit of 10^{-12} Bohr magnetons can be obtained from constraints on additional cooling mechanisms for red giant stars due to plasmon decay into neutrinos (Raffelt 1999, Viaux 2013)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical symmetry analysis of Pehlivan (2011) from two-flavor to three-flavor mixing schemes, incorporating CP violation and neutrino magnetic moment effects that were previously examined separately. The factorization of CP-violating effects from flavor evolution was originally shown in the mean field approximation by Gava (2008), but this paper demonstrates it is more general and holds in the full many-body formulation. The results enable future studies of collective neutrino oscillations in core-collapse supernovae and other astrophysical environments where both CP violation and magnetic fields may be important, particularly for understanding r-process nucleosynthesis conditions.
