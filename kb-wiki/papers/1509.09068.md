# Testing two-nucleon transfer reaction mechanism with elementary modes of excitation in exotic nuclei

> Nuclear Field Theory of structure and reactions is confronted with observations made on neutron halo dripline nuclei, resulting in the prediction of a novel (symbiotic) mode of nuclear excitation, and on the observation of the virtual effect of the halo phenomenon in the apparently non-halo nucleus $^7$Li. This effect is forced to become real by intervening the virtual process with an external (t,p) field which, combined with accurate predictive abilities concerning the absolute differential cross section, reveals an increase of a factor 2 in the cross section due to the presence of halo ground state correlations, and is essential to reproduce the value of the observed $d σ(^7$Li(t,p)$^9$Li)/d$Ω$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that ground state correlations (gsc) associated with the neutron halo phenomenon in the exotic nucleus \(^{11}\)Li produce a measurable effect even in the apparently non-halo nucleus \(^7\)Li, increasing the absolute differential cross section of the \(^7\)Li(t,p)\(^9\)Li reaction by a factor of 2; this effect is essential to reproduce the experimental data, but the claim is conditional on the accuracy of the Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) framework and the specific treatment of pairing vibrations and particle-vibration coupling.

## Method

The authors applied Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) to calculate the structure of pairing vibrational modes in N=6 magic number isotopes, solving the monopole dispersion relation in the Random Phase Approximation (RPA) to obtain two-nucleon spectroscopic amplitudes for the pair removal mode of \(^9\)Li. They then computed absolute differential cross sections for the \(^7\)Li(t,p)\(^9\)Li reaction using second-order Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) that includes simultaneous and successive transfer corrected for non-orthogonality, comparing the results with experimental data from Young (1971) and Fortune (1994).

## Key numbers

- The quantality parameter for single-particle motion is \(q \approx 0.4\), based on \(\hbar^2/(ma^2) / |V_0|\) with \(V_0 = -100\) MeV and \(a \approx 1\) fm.
- The Cooper pair coherence length is \(\xi \approx 20\) fm, derived from \(\hbar v_F/(2E_{corr})\) with \(E_{corr} \approx 1-1.5\) MeV.
- The generalized quantality parameter for Cooper pairs is \(q' \approx 0.02\), implying localization.
- In \(^{10}\)Li, the \(s_{1/2}\) orbital lies at \(\epsilon_{s_{1/2}} \approx 0.2\) MeV and the \(p_{1/2}\) orbital at \(\epsilon_{p_{1/2}} \approx 0.5\) MeV, both near threshold.
- The Giant Dipole Pygmy Resonance (GDPR) in \(^{11}\)Li has excitation energy \(E_x \leq 1\) MeV and carries approximately 8% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule.
- The first excited \(0^{+*}\) state in \(^{12}\)Be lies at 2.24 MeV excitation energy.
- The two-neutron separation energy of \(^7\)Li is \(S_{2n} = 12.91\) MeV.
- Eliminating ground state correlations in the calculation of the \(^7\)Li(t,p)\(^9\)Li(gs) cross section underpredicts experiment by about 50%, corresponding to a factor of 2 increase due to halo ground state correlations.

## Context

This work extends the Nuclear Field Theory approach developed by Bes and Broglia (1966) and the two-nucleon transfer reaction formalism of Broglia (1973, 1999) and Potel (2013) to exotic neutron-rich nuclei at the drip line. It builds on earlier studies of parity inversion in light nuclei by Sagawa and collaborators and on the description of pairing vibrations in closed-shell systems. The results enable systematic tests of the predicted symbiotic (composite) elementary modes of excitation, such as the monopole and dipole pair addition modes in \(^{11}\)Li and \(^{12}\)Be, through planned experiments including \(^{10}\)Be(p,t)\(^8\)Be, \(^8\)Be(t,p)\(^{10}\)Be, \(^{10}\)Be(t,p)\(^{12}\)Be, and \(^{14}\)Be(p,t)\(^{12}\)Be reactions, as well as knockout reactions and heavy-ion induced two-neutron transfer.
