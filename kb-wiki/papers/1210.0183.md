# Isospin Non-Conservation in $sd$-Shell Nuclei

> The question of isospin-symmetry breaking in nuclei of the $sd$ shell is addressed. We propose a new global parameterization of the isospin-nonconserving (INC) shell-model Hamiltonian which accurately describes experimentally known isobaric mass splittings. The isospin-symmetry violating part of the Hamiltonian consists of the Coulomb interaction and effective charge-dependent forces of nuclear origin. Particular attention has been paid to the effect of the short-range correlations. The behavior of $b$ and $c$ coefficients of the isobaric-mass-multiplet equation (IMME) is explored in detail. In particular, a high-precision numerical description of the staggering effect is proposed and contribution of the charge-dependent forces to the nuclear pairing is discussed. The Hamiltonian is applied to the study of the IMME beyond a quadratic form in the A=32 quintet, as well as to calculation of nuclear structure corrections to superallowed $0^+ \to 0^+$ Fermi $β$ decay, and to amplitudes of Fermi transitions to non-analogue states in sd-shell nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new global parameterization of the isospin-nonconserving (INC) shell-model Hamiltonian for sd-shell nuclei that accurately describes experimentally known isobaric mass splittings, but the fit is performed only for the isovector and isotensor parts of the charge-dependent interaction, while the isoscalar part is assumed to be already contained in the isospin-conserving Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors start from the isospin-conserving USD, USDA, or USDB Hamiltonians for the sd shell (0d5/2, 1s1/2, 0d3/2 orbitals) and add a charge-dependent term consisting of the Coulomb interaction and effective charge-dependent nuclear forces represented either by scaled T=1 matrix elements of the isospin-conserving interaction or by a linear combination of Yukawa-type potentials corresponding to pion and rho meson exchange. The strength parameters of the isovector and isotensor components are determined by a least-squares fit to experimental IMME b and c coefficients, with particular attention paid to the effect of short-range correlations via Jastrow-type and UCOM approaches.

## Key numbers

* The difference between proton-proton and neutron-neutron scattering lengths in the 1S0 channel is aN_pp - aN_nn = 1.65 ± 0.60 fm, indicating charge-symmetry breaking of the NN interaction.
* The difference between the average of pp and nn scattering lengths and the np scattering length is (aN_pp + aN_nn)/2 - aN_np = 5.6 ± 0.6 fm, indicating charge-independence breaking.
* The Yukawa potentials use mu_pi = 0.7 fm^-1 and mu_rho = 3.9 fm^-1, corresponding to pion and rho meson exchange respectively.
* The ft-values for thirteen 0+ to 0+ beta+ transitions among T=1 analogue states are known with a precision better than 0.1%, while the largest uncertainty on the extracted Ft value (about 0.4%) is due to ambiguous calculation of the nuclear structure correction.

## Context

This work extends the earlier shell-model studies of isospin mixing by Ormand and Brown (1985, 1989), who constructed realistic INC effective Hamiltonians constrained by experimental mass splittings of isobaric multiplets, and by Zuker and collaborators (2002) who analyzed mirror energy differences in pf-shell nuclei. The paper also builds on the more recent USDA and USDB Hamiltonians by Brown and collaborators (2006) and incorporates new approaches to short-range correlations from the UCOM method and the work of Simkovic (2009). The new Hamiltonian enables improved calculations of nuclear structure corrections to superallowed 0+ to 0+ Fermi beta decay, which are needed to test the conserved vector current hypothesis and extract the Vud matrix element of the CKM matrix.
