# Low-lying Dipole Modes in $^{26,28}$Ne in the Quasiparticle Relativistic Random Phase Approximation

> The low-lying isovector dipole strengths in neutron rich nuclei $^{26}$Ne and $^{28}$Ne are investigated in the quasiparticle relativistic random phase approximation. Nuclear ground state properties are calculated in an extended relativistic mean-field theory plus BCS method where the contribution of the resonant continuum to pairing correlations is properly treated. Numerical calculations are tested in the case of isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole modes in the neutron rich nucleus $^{22}$O. It is found that in present calculation low-lying isovector dipole strengths at $E_x < 10$ MeV in nuclei $^{26}$Ne and $^{28}$Ne exhaust about 4.9% and 5.8% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn dipole sum rule, respectively. The centroid energy of the low-lying dipole excitation is located at 8.3 MeV in $^{26}$Ne and 7.9 MeV in $^{28}$Ne.

## Key claim

The low-lying isovector dipole strengths in neutron-rich nuclei $^{26}$Ne and $^{28}$Ne exhaust about 4.9% and 5.8% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn dipole sum rule, respectively, but the authors note that a spherical symmetry is assumed despite these nuclei being deformed and strongly anharmonic, and the low-lying strengths are dominated by single-particle transitions rather than collective excitations.

## Method

The authors used the quasiparticle relativistic random phase approximation (QRRPA) formulated in the response function method, with ground states calculated in an extended relativistic mean-field theory plus BCS approach that treats the resonant continuum with a scattering boundary condition. The calculations were performed with the NL3 parameter set, and the method was tested on the isovector dipole and isoscalar quadrupole modes in $^{22}$O before being applied to $^{26}$Ne and $^{28}$Ne.

## Key numbers

- Low-lying isovector dipole strengths at $E_x < 10$ MeV in $^{26}$Ne exhaust 4.9% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn dipole sum rule
- Low-lying isovector dipole strengths at $E_x < 10$ MeV in $^{28}$Ne exhaust 5.8% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn dipole sum rule
- Centroid energy of the low-lying dipole excitation in $^{26}$Ne is 8.3 MeV (8.34 MeV from QRRPA calculation)
- Centroid energy of the low-lying dipole excitation in $^{28}$Ne is 7.9 MeV (7.94 MeV from QRRPA calculation)
- Centroid energy of the normal IVGDR (10-60 MeV) in $^{26}$Ne is 22.3 MeV
- Centroid energy of the normal IVGDR (10-60 MeV) in $^{28}$Ne is 21.1 MeV
- Energy weighted moment $m_1$ at $E_x < 10$ MeV in $^{26}$Ne is 4.525 $e^2fm^2$MeV
- Energy weighted moment $m_1$ at $E_x < 10$ MeV in $^{28}$Ne is 5.606 $e^2fm^2$MeV
- Low-lying dipole excitations in both nuclei exhaust about 26.3% of the TRK cluster dipole sum rule (17.2 $e^2fm^2$MeV for $^{26}$Ne and 21.3 $e^2fm^2$MeV for $^{28}$Ne)

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle relativistic random phase approximation (QRRPA) approach previously applied to Sn isotopes by Paar and co-workers (2003) to the neutron-rich Ne isotopes $^{26}$Ne and $^{28}$Ne, which were being studied experimentally via Coulomb excitation at RIKEN by Beaumel and collaborators. The results are consistent with experimental observations in $^{18}$O, $^{20}$O and $^{22}$O where low-lying isovector dipole strengths exhaust about 5% of the TRK sum rule (Aumann 1996), and the finding that low-lying strengths are dominated by single-particle excitations of loosely bound neutrons agrees with the RRPA study of neutron-rich O, Ca, Ni, Zr, and Sn isotopes by Vretenar and collaborators (2001). The calculations enable comparison with upcoming experimental data from RIKEN on $^{26}$Ne and $^{28}$Ne inelastic scattering.
