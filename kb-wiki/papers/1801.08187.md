# From Heavy-Ion Collisions to Compact Stars: Equation of State and Relevance of the System Size

> In this article, we start by presenting state-of-the-art methods allowing us to compute moments related to the globally conserved baryon number, by means of first principle resummed perturbative frameworks. We focus on such quantities for they convey important properties of the finite temperature and density equation of state, being particularly sensitive to changes in the degrees of freedom across the quark-hadron phase transition. We thus present various number susceptibilities along with the corresponding results as obtained by lattice quantum chromodynamics collaborations, and comment on their comparison. Next, omitting the importance of coupling corrections and considering a zero-density toy model for the sake of argument, we focus on corrections due to the small size of heavy-ion collision systems, by means of spatial compactifications. Briefly motivating the relevance of finite size effects in heavy-ion physics, in opposition to the compact star physics, we present a few preliminary thermodynamic results together with the speed of sound for certain finite size relativistic quantum systems at very high temperature.

## Key claim

The paper argues that finite-size corrections to the thermodynamics of the quark-gluon plasma may be non-negligible for heavy-ion collision systems (where the dimensionless parameter \(T \times R\) ranges from a few to roughly ten), in contrast to compact-star systems where the infinite-volume limit is an excellent approximation. However, the finite-size results presented are explicitly preliminary and based on a zero-density, non-interacting scalar field toy model that omits coupling corrections.

## Method

The authors first review two resummed perturbative frameworks for infinite-volume QCD at finite temperature and density: Dimensional Reduction (DR) and Hard-Thermal-Loop perturbation theory (HTLpt). They compare the resulting quark and baryon number susceptibilities and kurtoses with lattice QCD data. For finite-size effects, they consider a single non-interacting massless scalar field at zero chemical potential, spatially compactified between two infinite parallel planes separated by a distance \(L\) with Dirichlet boundary conditions. They derive an exact analytic expression for the free-energy density and compute the speed of sound and entropy non-additivity as functions of \(T\) and \(L\).

## Key numbers

* The pseudo-critical temperature for vanishing quark chemical potentials is \(T_c = 154 \pm 9\) MeV (from lattice QCD).
* Characteristic sizes of heavy-ion collision fireballs are \(R \sim 4\)–8 fm for lead–lead collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 2.76\) TeV and \(R \sim 1\)–2 fm for proton–lead collisions at \(\sqrt{s} = 5.02\) TeV.
* The zero-temperature Casimir free-energy density for the scalar field toy model is \(f(T=0, L) = -\pi^2 / (1440 L^4)\), giving the Casimir pressure \(p_{\rm Cas} = -\pi^2 / (480 L^4)\).
* For the infinite-volume QCD results, both DR and HTLpt agree with lattice data down to \(T \sim 200\)–400 MeV for second- and fourth-order susceptibilities.
* For the finite-size toy model, corrections to the speed of sound become non-negligible starting at about 6–10% for system sizes relevant to lead–lead collisions at a few hundred MeV.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative QCD thermodynamics framework developed by Blaizot (2001), Andersen (2012), and Mogliacci (2013) for infinite-volume systems, comparing with lattice results from the BNL–Bielefeld and Wuppertal–Budapest collaborations. It introduces a novel finite-size correction framework motivated by the observation that heavy-ion collision systems are much smaller than compact stars, following earlier finite-volume studies by Karsch (2015) for hadronic systems. The preliminary results suggest that thermodynamic quantities may require finite-size refinement, but the authors leave open the question of whether more dynamical quantities are less sensitive to system size.
