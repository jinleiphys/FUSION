# Symmetry preserving truncations of the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations

> Ward-Green-Takahashi (WGT) identities play a crucial role in hadron physics, e.g. imposing stringent relationships between the kernels of the one- and two-body problems, which must be preserved in any veracious treatment of mesons as bound-states. In this connection, one may view the dressed gluon-quark vertex, $Γ_μ^a$, as fundamental. We use a novel representation of $Γ_μ^a$, in terms of the gluon-quark scattering matrix, to develop a method capable of elucidating the unique quark-antiquark Bethe-Salpeter kernel, $K$, that is symmetry-consistent with a given quark gap equation. A strength of the scheme is its ability to expose and capitalise on graphic symmetries within the kernels. This is displayed in an analysis that reveals the origin of $H$-diagrams in $K$, which are two-particle-irreducible contributions, generated as two-loop diagrams involving the three-gluon vertex, that cannot be absorbed as a dressing of $Γ_μ^a$ in a Bethe-Salpeter kernel nor expressed as a member of the class of crossed-box diagrams. Thus, there are no general circumstances under which the WGT identities essential for a valid description of mesons can be preserved by a Bethe-Salpeter kernel obtained simply by dressing both gluon-quark vertices in a ladder-like truncation; and, moreover, adding any number of similarly-dressed crossed-box diagrams cannot improve the situation.

# Symmetry preserving truncations of the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations

## Key claim

There are no general circumstances under which a Bethe-Salpeter kernel obtained simply by dressing both gluon-quark vertices in a ladder-like truncation can preserve the Ward-Green-Takahashi identities essential for a valid description of mesons; adding any number of similarly-dressed crossed-box diagrams cannot improve the situation because such kernels omit H-diagrams, which are two-particle-irreducible contributions generated as two-loop diagrams involving the three-gluon vertex.

## Method

The authors use a novel representation of the dressed gluon-quark vertex in terms of the gluon-quark scattering matrix to develop a mechanical method that elucidates the unique quark-antiquark Bethe-Salpeter kernel symmetry-consistent with a given quark gap equation. They apply functional differentiation of the quark self-energy with respect to the quark propagator, following the cutting procedure of Munczek (1994) and Bender (1996), to generate the kernel that preserves all Ward-Green-Takahashi identities, and analyze the diagrammatic content of the resulting kernel for systems with nonzero isospin.

## Key numbers

- The pion mass-squared is shown to vanish at zero current-quark mass only when the complete symmetry-consistent kernel is used; with the Ball-Chiu vertex Ansatz and the kernel restricted to the dressed-vertex ladder-like kernel, the dot-dashed (red) curve in Fig. 3 demonstrates that this kernel generates "far too much attraction."
- Using a modified Ball-Chiu Ansatz with the coefficient of the delta_B term doubled and interaction strength reduced from D = 0.5 GeV^2 to D = 0.29 GeV^2, one can tune to obtain m_pi^2 = 0 at m_q = 0, but the quark-level Goldberger-Treiman relation is violated: the ratio E_pi(k^2)/B(k^2) is not constant, with B(k^2 = 0) = 0.28 GeV and the ratio falling to 0.47 at x = 9 (where x = k^2/B^2(0)).
- The complete symmetry-consistent kernel preserves the Goldberger-Treiman relation without fine tuning, yielding E_pi(k^2, k·P = 0; P^2 = 0) proportional to B(k^2) in the chiral limit.

## Context

This work extends the symmetry-preserving truncation scheme introduced by Munczek (1994) and Bender (1996), which generates a Bethe-Salpeter kernel from any gap equation whose diagrammatic content is known, and the improved scheme of Chang (2009) that enables more realistic kernels without requiring knowledge of the gap equation's diagrammatic content. The analysis invalidates claims made in Williams (2015) regarding the sufficiency of vertex-dressed ladder kernels, and demonstrates that H-diagrams constitute a distinct class of essentially non-Abelian two-particle-irreducible contributions that cannot be absorbed as vertex dressings or expressed as crossed-box diagrams. The results enable validation of any newly-proposed Bethe-Salpeter kernel based on a skeleton expansion of the gap equation and expose the full complexity demanded of symmetry-consistent kernels.
