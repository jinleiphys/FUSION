# Structure and dynamics of open-shell nuclei from spherical coupled-cluster theory

> We extend the spherical coupled-cluster ab initio method for open-shell nuclei where two nucleons are removed from a shell subclosure. Following the recent implementation of the two-particle attached approach [Phys. Rev.C 110 (2024) 4, 044306], we focus on the two-particle-removed method. Using the equations-of-motion framework, we address both nuclear structure and dipole response functions by coupling coupled-cluster theory with the Lorentz integral transform technique. We perform calculations using chiral interactions, including three-nucleon forces, and estimate many-body uncertainties by comparing different coupled-cluster truncation schemes. We validate our approach by studying ground-state energies, excited states, and electric dipole polarizabilities in the oxygen and calcium isotopic chains. For binding energies and selected low-lying excited states, we achieve an accuracy comparable to that of the established closed-shell coupled-cluster theory and generally agree with experiment. Finally, we underestimate experimental data for electric dipole polarizabilities, particularly in calcium isotopes.

## Key claim

The spherical coupled-cluster method is extended to open-shell nuclei with two nucleons removed from a shell subclosure, achieving accuracy comparable to closed-shell coupled-cluster theory for binding energies and low-lying excited states, but the method systematically underestimates experimental electric dipole polarizabilities, particularly in calcium isotopes.

## Method

The authors implement a two-particle-removed (2PR) equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (EOM-CC) approach, where an open-shell nucleus is treated as an excited state of its closed-shell neighbor by removing two fermions. They couple this with the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) technique to compute dipole response functions, using chiral interactions including three-nucleon forces at next-to-next-to-leading order (the ΔNNLOGO(394) interaction). Many-body uncertainties are estimated by comparing different coupled-cluster truncation schemes, including CCSD and CCSDT-1 for the ground state, and 0p-2h versus 1p-3h for the EOM excitation operators.

## Key numbers

* Ground-state energy of ²²O: -161.2(3) MeV at the T-1/1p-3h level, compared to the experimental value of -162.0 MeV from wang2021ame.
* Ground-state energy of ³⁸Ca: -314.6(4) MeV at the T-1/1p-3h level, compared to the experimental value of -313.1 MeV from wang2021ame.
* For ²²O, the D/0p-2h scheme recovers approximately 85% of the experimental binding energy, with the D/1p-3h scheme adding about 8 MeV of additional correlation energy.
* For ³⁸Ca, the T-1/3p-1h (two-particle-attached) prediction is -311.1(5) MeV, while the T-1/1p-3h (two-particle-removed) prediction is -314.6(4) MeV.
* The reference energy E₀* for ²⁴O at the T-1 level is -169.5 MeV, with a 2PR excitation energy ω₀ of 8.3 MeV for ²²O.
* The reference energy E₀* for ⁴⁰Ca at the T-1 level is -346.0 MeV, with a 2PR excitation energy ω₀ of 31.38 MeV for ³⁸Ca.

## Context

This work extends the two-particle-attached (2PA) method recently implemented by Bonaiti (2024) to the two-particle-removed (2PR) case, building on the EOM-CC framework developed by Jansen (2011) and Piecuch (2013). The approach provides a symmetry-preserving alternative to methods that break and restore symmetries, such as Bogoliubov coupled-cluster (Signoracci 2015, Tichai 2024) and deformed coupled-cluster (Novario 2020, Hagen 2022). The 2PR-LIT-CC technique enables future calculations of electromagnetic response functions in neutron-deficient open-shell nuclei near shell closures, complementing existing studies of dipole polarizabilities in closed-shell and two-particle-attached systems.
