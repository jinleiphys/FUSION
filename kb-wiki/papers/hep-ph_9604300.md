# Dilepton and Photon Productions from a Coherent Pion Oscillation

> Since the electromagnetic current for a pion system coincides with the third component of the isovector current, the isospin angular oscillation of a coherent field can be a significant source for the electromagnetic emissions. We study the characteristic dilepton and photon emissions from the classical pion field oscillation in the QCD vacuum. The general analytical solution obtained in the nonlinear sigma model is used to calculate the electromagnetic current density, which exhibits a light-front singularity and decreases rapidly as inverse square of the proper time due to a longitudinal expansion. The momentum and invariant mass spectra of the direct photon and dilepton are found to be a sensitive probe of the space-time evolution of the chiral condensate field.

# Dilepton and Photon Productions from a Coherent Pion Oscillation

## Key claim

The electromagnetic emissions from a classical pion field oscillation in the QCD vacuum can be a significant additional source of dileptons and photons, particularly in the low mass (low transverse momentum) region below the pion mass threshold, provided the field evolves according to a boost-invariant solution with a light-front singularity and inverse-square proper-time decay.

## Method

The authors use the nonlinear sigma model to obtain an analytical solution for a classical pion field undergoing isospin angular oscillation, assuming boost invariance and a Gaussian transverse profile for a single DCC domain. They compute the Fourier transform of the third component of the isovector current (which coincides with the electromagnetic current) and derive the dilepton invariant mass spectrum and direct photon transverse momentum spectrum from the classical current, integrating the proper-time evolution from an initial time tau_0 to infinity.

## Key numbers

- The electromagnetic current density decreases as inverse square of the proper time: proportional to 1/tau^2.
- The typical DCC domain size is taken as R_D = 1 to 2 fm.
- The initial proper time tau_0 is taken as 5 or 10 fm/c.
- The initial energy density from internal chiral oscillations is estimated as epsilon_0 ~ 0.5 T_c^4 ~ 0.1 GeV/fm^3.
- The average value of the squared isospin constant a_3^2 is about 4.8 for epsilon_0 = 0.1 GeV/fm^3 and tau_0 = 5 fm/c.
- The dilepton spectrum contains the factor exp[-q_T^2 R_D^2 / 2] providing a strong transverse momentum cutoff.
- The photon spectrum contains the factor (1/q_T) exp[-q_T^2 R_D^2 / 2] times the Bessel function combination [J_0^2(q_T tau_0) + N_0^2(q_T tau_0)].

## Context

This work extends the study of disoriented chiral condensates (DCC) proposed by Bjorken, Kowalski, and Taylor (1993) and the analytical solutions for classical pion fields obtained by Blaizot and Krzywicki (1992) and by Huang and Suzuki (1996). It provides a formalism for using electromagnetic probes to access the space-time evolution of the chiral condensate field, which is difficult to obtain from final hadron spectra alone. The results suggest a possible mechanism for the low-mass dilepton enhancement observed by the CERES and HELIOS collaborations at CERN SPS, and the photon spectrum is proposed as a sensitive probe of the DCC domain size R_D.
