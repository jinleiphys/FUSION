# Understanding Nuclei in the upper sd - shell

> Nuclei in the upper-$sd$ shell usually exhibit characteristics of spherical single particle excitations. In the recent years, employment of sophisticated techniques of gamma spectroscopy has led to observation of high spin states of several nuclei near A$\simeq$ 40. In a few of them multiparticle, multihole rotational states coexist with states of single particle nature. We have studied a few nuclei in this mass region experimentally, using various campaigns of the Indian National Gamma Array setup. We have compared and combined our empirical observations with the large-scale shell model results to interpret the structure of these nuclei. Indication of population of states of large deformation has been found in our data. This gives us an opportunity to investigate the interplay of single particle and collective degrees of freedom in this mass region.

## Key claim
Nuclei in the upper-sd shell near A≈40, previously thought to exhibit only spherical single-particle excitations, show coexisting multiparticle-multihole rotational states; the authors find indications of large deformation in their data, but the claim is hedged by stating that the evidence comes from preliminary analysis and that further detailed analysis is needed for firm conclusions.

## Method
The authors performed fusion-evaporation reactions at various Indian accelerator facilities using the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) with Clover detectors in addback mode, employing inverse kinematics. They constructed symmetric and asymmetric coincidence matrices for gamma-gamma coincidence, directional correlation (DCO), and integrated polarization asymmetry (IPDCO) measurements, and extracted level lifetimes using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM) via centroid shift and lineshape analysis. Experimental results were compared with large-scale shell model calculations using the OXBASH code with the sdpfmw interaction over a valence space including sd and fp orbitals above a ¹⁶O inert core.

## Key numbers
- The first negative parity state in ³⁵Cl is at 3163 keV, and the first positive parity state is at 1219 keV.
- For the candidate deformed band in ³⁵Cl, the quadrupole moment extracted from preliminary centroid shift (F(τ)) analysis is around 0.7 eb.
- In inverse kinematics, recoils have velocities β = v/c = 5-6% and move within a narrow cone of half angle 13° in the forward direction; the spread in recoil velocities is within 7% in inverse kinematics compared to 17% in forward kinematics.
- The ⁶⁶Ga source used for calibration emits gamma rays with energies ranging from 833 to 4806 keV.
- For the candidate 7⁺ state in ³⁰P, the 2858 keV transition de-excites the 7202 keV state.

## Context
This work extends studies of upper-sd shell nuclei near A≈40 that began in the 1960s, where earlier experiments were limited to low spins. It builds on observations of superdeformation in ⁴⁰Ca and ³⁶Ar, and on discussions of isospin symmetry violation in mirror pairs by Ekman and colleagues. The authors compare their shell model results with those using the SDPF-M and sdfp interactions, noting that Bender and Steppenbeck used the WBP-a Hamiltonian with reduced fp-shell energies, while Ionescu-Bujor used two different interactions with opposite adjustments to the sd-fp gap. The work enables future investigation of how the sd-fp shell gap evolves with proton number and how collectivity and single-particle modes interplay in light-mass nuclei, with implications for understanding the "island of inversion" far from stability.
