# Phenomenology of the Pentaquark Antidecuplet

> We consider the mass splittings and strong decays of members of the lowest-lying pentaquark multiplet, which we take to be a parity-odd antidecuplet. We derive useful decompositions of the quark model wave functions that allow for easy computation of color-flavor-spin matrix elements. We compute mass splittings within the antidecuplet including spin-color and spin-isospin interactions between constituents and point out the importance of hidden strangeness in rendering the nucleon-like states heavier than the S=1 state. Using recent experimental data on a possible S=1 pentaquark state, we make decay predictions for other members of the antidecuplet.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the recently observed strangeness +1 baryon (Z⁺, mass 1540 MeV) is a member of a parity-odd pentaquark antidecuplet, but the authors qualify that the parity, spin, and isospin of the experimental state are currently unmeasured, so the assignment remains a theoretical interpretation within constituent quark models.

## Method

The authors constructed color-flavor-spin wave functions for the pentaquark antidecuplet using two complementary decompositions: one based on two quark pairs combined with an antiquark, and another based on a three-quark state combined with a quark-antiquark pair. They computed mass splittings within the antidecuplet using the MIT bag model with color-spin interactions from single gluon exchange, and separately considered spin-isospin interactions from pseudoscalar meson exchange. Using the observed Z⁺ mass and SU(3) flavor symmetry, they predicted decay widths for other antidecuplet members into ground state octet baryons and mesons.

## Key numbers

- Z⁺ mass: 1540 MeV (experimental observation), with decay width less than 21 MeV
- Antidecuplet mass splitting from color-spin interactions: approximately 52 MeV
- Predicted masses using color-spin interactions (with Z⁺ fixed at 1542 MeV): p₅ = 1594 MeV, Σ₅ = 1646 MeV, Ξ₅ = 1698 MeV
- Predicted masses using spin-isospin interactions (with Z⁺ fixed at 1542 MeV): p₅ = 1697 MeV, Σ₅ = 1869 MeV, Ξ₅ = 2058 MeV
- Hidden strangeness expectation values: ⟨Z⁺|S_h|Z⁺⟩ = 1, ⟨N₅|S_h|N₅⟩ = 4/3, ⟨Σ₅|S_h|Σ₅⟩ = 5/3, ⟨Ξ₅|S_h|Ξ₅⟩ = 2
- Spin-color matrix elements: quark-quark = 16/3, quark-antiquark = 40/3
- Spin-isospin matrix elements: Z⁺ = 10, p₅ = 20/3, Σ₅ = 25/9, Ξ₅ = -5/3
- Relative decay widths for Z⁺ → p K⁰: Γ/Γ₀ = 0.99 (both spin-color and spin-isospin cases)

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark model studies of pentaquarks by Hogaasen and Strottman (1970s) and by Roiesnel (1980s), which had predicted a Z⁺ mass of 1615 MeV but concluded no such state existed at low mass. The paper contrasts its results with the Skyrme model prediction by Diakonov et al. (1997), which assumed positive parity and found antidecuplet mass splittings of about 180 MeV between levels. The authors' analysis enables experimental searches for other antidecuplet members (p₅, Σ₅, Ξ₅) and sets the groundwork for studying other pentaquark multiplets, including a potentially light nucleon-like octet state.
