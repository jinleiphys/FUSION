# Advanced extraction of the deuteron charge radius from electron-deuteron scattering data

> To extract the charge radius of the proton, $r_{p}$, from the electron scattering data, the PRad collaboration at Jefferson Lab has developed a rigorous framework for finding the best functional forms - the fitters - for a robust extraction of $r_{p}$ from a wide variety of sample functions for the range and uncertainties of the PRad data. In this paper we utilize and further develop this framework. Herein we discuss methods for searching for the best fitter candidates as well as a procedure for testing the robustness of extraction of the deuteron charge radius, $r_{d}$, from parametrizations based on elastic electron-deuteron scattering data. The ansatz proposed in this paper for the robust extraction of $r_{d}$, for the proposed low-$Q^{2}$ DRad experiment at Jefferson Lab, can be further improved once there are more data.

## Key claim

The paper develops and extends the PRad collaboration's rigorous framework for robust extraction of the deuteron charge radius from elastic electron-deuteron scattering data, but notes that the bias from fitting functions is a non-negligible source of systematic uncertainty for the proposed DRad experiment, and the ansatz can be further improved once more data become available.

## Method

The authors generated pseudo-data for the deuteron charge form factor \(G_{C}^{d}\) using four parametrizations (Abbott1, Abbott2, Parker, and SOG) at 67 \(Q^{2}\) bins in the DRad kinematic range of \(2 \times 10^{-4}~\text{(GeV/c)}^2 < Q^{2} < 0.05~\text{(GeV/c)}^2\), added bin-by-bin Gaussian fluctuations to mimic statistical and systematic uncertainties, and fitted the pseudo-data with various functional forms (rational functions, continued fractions, and polynomial expansions of the Z transformation). They then extracted the deuteron charge radius from the slope of the fitted function at \(Q^{2}=0\) and evaluated the bias and root-mean-square error (RMSE) for each fitter. A data-driven method was used to fix two parameters of the Rational(1,3) function by fitting existing higher-\(Q^{2}\) experimental data from Abbott et al. (2000), creating a "fixed Rational(1,3)" fitter with improved asymptotic behavior.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron charge radius from muonic deuterium spectroscopy by the CREMA collaboration is \(r_{d} = 2.12562 \pm 0.00078~\text{fm}\), which is 7.5-\(\sigma\) smaller than the CODATA-2010 world-average value.
* The deuteron charge radius from electronic spectroscopy is \(r_{d} = 2.1415 \pm 0.0045~\text{fm}\), which is 3.5-\(\sigma\) larger than the muonic deuterium value.
* The PRad collaboration measured the proton charge radius as \(r_{p} = 0.831 \pm 0.007_{\rm stat} \pm 0.012_{\rm syst}~\text{fm}\) from elastic electron-proton scattering down to \(Q^{2} = 2.1 \times 10^{-4}~\text{GeV}^2/c^2\).
* The DRad experiment aims for a subpercent precision of \(\leq 0.25\%\) on the deuteron charge radius.
* The effects of choosing different \(G_{M}^{d}\) and \(G_{Q}^{d}\) form-factor models on the deuteron radius extraction are \(0.03\%\) and \(0.009\%\), respectively.
* The pseudo-data generation used 30 bins at 1.1 GeV beam energy and 37 bins at 2.2 GeV beam energy, with bin-by-bin statistical uncertainties from \(0.02\%\) to \(0.07\%\) and systematic uncertainties from \(0.06\%\) to \(0.16\%\).
* The fixed Rational(1,3) fitter had parameters \(b_{2} = 0.0416 \pm 0.0152\) and \(b_{3} = 0.00474 \pm 0.000892\) determined from fitting existing data with \(\chi^2/\text{NDF} \simeq 1.25\).

## Context

This work extends the rigorous fitting framework developed by the PRad collaboration for the proton charge radius (Yan et al., 2018) to the deuteron case, addressing the "deuteron charge radius puzzle" that emerged from discrepancies between muonic spectroscopy results (CREMA collaboration, Pohl et al., 2016) and electronic spectroscopy measurements. The paper enables the proposed DRad experiment at Jefferson Lab to perform a model-independent extraction of the deuteron charge radius with controlled systematic uncertainties, and provides a data-driven method for selecting robust fitting functions that can be applied to future low-\(Q^{2}\) electron-deuteron scattering data.
