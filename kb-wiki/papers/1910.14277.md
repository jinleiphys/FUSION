# Prediction of $NΩ$-like dibaryons with heavy quarks

> Possible $NΩ$-like dibaryons $NΩ_{ccc}$ and $NΩ_{bbb}$ with quantum numbers $IJ^P=\frac{1}{2}2^+$ are investigated within the framework of quark delocalization color screening model. We find both of these two states are bound, and the binding energy increases as the quarks of the system become heavier. The attraction between $N$ and $Ω_{ccc}$ (or $Ω_{bbb}$) mainly comes from the kinetic energy term due to quark delocalization and color screening. The effect of the channel-coupling provides more effective attraction to $NΩ_{ccc}$ and $NΩ_{bbb}$ systems. Besides, the scattering length, the effective range, and the binding energy, obtained from the calculation of the low-energy scattering phase shifts, also supports the existence of the $NΩ_{ccc}$ and $NΩ_{bbb}$ states. All these properties can provide necessary information for experimental search for the $NΩ$-like dibaryons with heavy quarks. And the experimental progress can also check the mechanism of the intermediate-range attraction of the baryon-baryon interaction in quark models.

# Prediction of $N\Omega$-like dibaryons with heavy quarks

## Key claim
Both $N\Omega_{ccc}$ and $N\Omega_{bbb}$ states with quantum numbers $IJ^P=\frac{1}{2}2^+$ are bound in the quark delocalization color screening model, with binding energy increasing as the constituent quarks become heavier; however, the single-channel binding energies are very small (less than 3 MeV), and the full binding arises only after including channel-coupling effects, which provide additional effective attraction.

## Method
The authors employed the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) with a Hamiltonian including kinetic energy, confinement, one-gluon-exchange, and chiral meson-exchange potentials. They performed dynamical calculations using the resonating group method (RGM) for the $uudccc$ and $uudbbb$ systems, expanding the relative motion wave function in Gaussians. Low-energy scattering phase shifts were computed via the Kohn-Hulthen-Kato variational method, from which scattering length, effective range, and binding energy were extracted using the effective range expansion.

## Key numbers
- $N\Omega_{ccc}$ single-channel binding energy: $-0.6$ MeV; after five-channel coupling: $-30.9$ MeV below $N\Omega_{ccc}$ threshold
- $N\Omega_{bbb}$ single-channel binding energy: $-2.8$ MeV; after five-channel coupling: $-50.7$ MeV below $N\Omega_{bbb}$ threshold
- $N\Omega_{ccc}$ scattering length: $a_0 = 1.3347$ fm; effective range: $r_0 = 0.43343$ fm; binding energy from scattering: $B' = -21.6$ MeV
- $N\Omega_{bbb}$ scattering length: $a_0 = 1.1608$ fm; effective range: $r_0 = 0.53617$ fm; binding energy from scattering: $B' = -40.1$ MeV
- $\Sigma_{c}\Xi^{*}_{cc}$ single-channel binding energy: $-1.1$ MeV (disappears in channel-coupling calculation)

## Context
This work extends previous QDCSM studies of the $N\Omega$ dibaryon (Huang et al., 2019) to the heavy-quark sector, following lattice QCD results (HAL QCD Collaboration, 2018) that found $N\Omega$ bound at nearly physical quark masses and lattice studies of deuteron-like dibaryons with heavy quarks (Lattice1, 2019) which showed stronger binding for heavier systems. The results are consistent with the trend that binding increases with quark mass, and provide scattering parameters that could be used for experimental correlation analyses at RHIC and LHC, similar to the $p\Omega$ correlation measurements by the STAR experiment.
