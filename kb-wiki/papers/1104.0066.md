# Further improvements on a global nuclear mass model

> The semi-empirical macroscopic-microscopic mass formula is further improved by considering some residual corrections. The rms deviation from 2149 known nuclear masses is significantly reduced to 336 keV, even lower than that achieved with the best of the Duflo-Zuker models. The alpha-decay energies of super-heavy nuclei, the Garvey-Kelson relations and the isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME) can be reproduced remarkably well with the model, and the predictive power of the mass model is good. With a systematic study of 17 global nuclear mass models, we find that the quadratic form of the IMME is closely related to the accuracy of nuclear mass calculations when the Garvey-Kelson relations are reproduced reasonably well. Fulfilling both the IMME and the Garvey-Kelson relations seems to be two necessary conditions to improve the quality of the model prediction. Furthermore, the alpha-decay energies of super-heavy nuclei should be used as an additional constraint on the global nuclear mass models.

# Further improvements on a global nuclear mass model

## Key claim

The improved macroscopic-microscopic mass formula (WS3) achieves an rms deviation of 336 keV for 2149 known nuclear masses, lower than the best Duflo-Zuker models, but this accuracy depends on incorporating residual corrections including mirror-nuclei effects, a Wigner-like term for heavy nuclei, and triaxial deformation contributions, while the model's predictive power for super-heavy nuclei and drip-line nuclei is demonstrated separately from the fit.

## Method

The authors extend their earlier Weizsäcker-Skyrme (WS) mass formula by adding a liquid-drop energy with a modified Bethe-Weizsäcker form, Strutinsky shell corrections from an axially deformed Woods-Saxon potential with isospin-dependent spin-orbit strength, and three residual correction terms: a mirror-nuclei term Δ_M based on shell-correction differences between mirror pairs, a residual pairing correction Δ_P from second differences of shell energies, and a triaxial/tetrahedral deformation term Δ_T. Model parameters are fitted to 2149 measured nuclear masses from AME2003 for nuclei with N and Z ≥ 8, and the resulting model is labeled WS3.

## Key numbers

- rms deviation σ(M) = 336 keV for 2149 measured nuclear masses (AME2003), compared to 360 keV for the best Duflo-Zuker model (DZ28) and 656 keV for FRDM
- rms deviation σ(S_n) = 286 keV for 1988 measured neutron separation energies
- rms deviation σ(Q_α) = 248 keV for α-decay energies of 46 super-heavy nuclei (Z ≥ 106), compared to 936 keV for DZ28
- Average deviation of 118 keV for four recently measured masses (⁶³Ge, ⁶⁵As, ⁶⁷Se, ⁷¹Kr), versus 321 keV for DZ28 and 680 keV for FRDM
- rms deviation σ(GK) = 131 keV from Garvey-Kelson mass estimates
- rms deviation σ(b) = 449 keV from the empirical IMME formula b_fit = 0.710A^{2/3} − 0.946 for 179 neutron drip line nuclei

## Context

This work extends the macroscopic-microscopic mass model approach of Wang et al. (2010) (labeled WS and WS*) by adding residual corrections that reduce the rms deviation from 441 keV to 336 keV. It systematically compares 17 global nuclear mass models including the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (1995), the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov model HFB-17, and the Duflo-Zuker models DZ10, DZ28, and DZ31. The study demonstrates that fulfilling both the Garvey-Kelson relations and the quadratic form of the isobaric multiplet mass equation (IMME) are necessary conditions for improving mass model reliability, and proposes that α-decay energies of super-heavy nuclei should be used as an additional constraint on global mass models.
