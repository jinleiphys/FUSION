# Effective Nucleon Masses in Symmetric and Asymmetric Nuclear Matter

> The momentum and isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon mass are studied. Two definitions of the effective mass, i.e. the Dirac mass $m^*_D$ and the nonrelativistic mass $m^*_{NR}$ which parameterizes the energy spectrum, are compared. Both masses are determined from relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations. The nonrelativistic mass shows a distinct peak around the Fermi momentum. The proton-neutron mass splitting in isospin asymmetric matter is $m^*_{D,n} <m^*_{D,p}$ and opposite for the nonrelativistic mass, i.e. $m^*_{NR,n} >m^*_{NR,p}$, which is consistent with nonrelativistic approaches.

## Key claim
The Dirac mass and the nonrelativistic effective mass, both derived from relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations using projection techniques, show opposite proton-neutron mass splittings in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter: the Dirac mass gives \(m^*_{D,n} < m^*_{D,p}\) while the nonrelativistic mass gives \(m^*_{NR,n} > m^*_{NR,p}\), resolving the apparent controversy between relativistic and nonrelativistic approaches.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter using the Bonn A nucleon-nucleon potential. The Lorentz structure of the T-matrix was decomposed into covariant amplitudes using a subtracted projection scheme, from which the scalar and vector components of the nucleon self-energy were extracted. The Dirac mass was computed from the scalar and spatial-vector self-energy components, while the nonrelativistic effective mass was derived from the Schroedinger-equivalent single-particle potential obtained from the DBHF self-energies.

## Key numbers
- At nuclear saturation density \(n_0 = 0.166\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the nonrelativistic effective mass shows a pronounced peak slightly above the Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.35\ \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\).
- The Dirac mass decreases continuously with increasing Fermi momentum; the nonrelativistic mass initially decreases but rises again at high Fermi momenta (shown for \(k_F\) values of 1.07, 1.35, and 1.7 \(\mathrm{fm}^{-1}\), corresponding to densities \(0.5 n_0\), \(n_0\), and \(2 n_0\)).
- In asymmetric matter at fixed density \(n_B = 0.166\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\), the neutron Dirac mass decreases with increasing asymmetry parameter \(\beta = (n_n - n_p)/n_B\), while the neutron nonrelativistic mass increases with \(\beta\).

## Context
This work extends previous DBHF calculations using projection techniques (Schiller 2001, de Jong 1998, van Dalen 2004) and resolves a controversy between relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory and nonrelativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations. RMF theory (Baran 2004, Liu 2002) predicts \(m^*_{NR,n} < m^*_{NR,p}\), while nonrelativistic BHF calculations (Zuo 1999, Muether 2004) predict \(m^*_{NR,n} > m^*_{NR,p}\); the present DBHF results show that both predictions are correct for different definitions of the effective mass. The results enable consistent comparisons between relativistic and nonrelativistic approaches to nuclear matter and are relevant for interpreting experiments at radioactive beam facilities.
