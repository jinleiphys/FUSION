# Coherence Phenomena in Charmonium Production off Nuclei at the Energies of RHIC and LHC

> In the energy range of RHIC and LHC the mechanisms of nuclear suppression of charmonia are expected to be strikingly different from what is known for the energy of the SPS. One cannot think any more of charmonium produced on a bound nucleon which then attenuates as it passes through the rest of the nucleus. The coherence length of charmonium production substantially exceeds the nuclear radius in the new energy range. Therefore the production amplitudes on different nucleons, rather than the cross sections, add up and interfere, i.e. shadowing is at work. So far no theoretical tool has been available to calculate nuclear effects for charmonium production in this energy regime. We develop a light-cone Green function formalism which incorporates the effects of the coherence of the production amplitudes and of charmonium wave function formation, and is the central result of this paper. We found a substantial deviation from QCD factorization, namely, gluon shadowing is much stronger for charmonium production than it is in DIS. We predict for nuclear effects $x_2$ scaling which is violated at lower energies by initial state energy loss which must be also included in order to compare with available data. In this paper only the indirect J/Psi originating from decay of P-wave charmonia are considered. The calculated x_F-dependence of J/Psi nuclear suppression is in a good accord with data. We predict a dramatic variation of nuclear suppression with x_F in pA and a peculiar peak at x_F=0 in AA collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

At RHIC and LHC energies, the coherence length of charmonium production exceeds the nuclear radius, so production amplitudes on different nucleons add coherently and interfere (shadowing), rather than the classical picture of charmonium produced on a single nucleon then attenuating. The authors develop a light-cone Green function formalism to calculate these coherence effects, and find that gluon shadowing for charmonium production is much stronger than in deep-inelastic scattering, violating QCD factorization.

## Method

The authors employ a light-cone dipole formalism in the target rest frame, representing the gluon-nucleon collision amplitude as a convolution of light-cone wave functions of the incident gluon and the final charmonium (specifically P-wave χ states) with a dipole transition cross section. For nuclear targets, they use a path integral technique where the evolution of a color-octet or color-singlet \(\bar cc\) wave packet through the nucleus is described by light-cone Green functions satisfying two-dimensional Schrödinger equations, incorporating both coherence length and formation length effects. They also include contributions from higher Fock states containing additional gluons (\(|\bar cc G\rangle\), \(|\bar cc 2G\rangle\)) to account for gluon shadowing, and correct for initial-state energy loss by projectile partons.

## Key numbers

- The formation length for charmonium is given by \(t_f = 2E_{\Psi} / (M_{\Psi'}^2 - M_{J/\Psi}^2)\); the coherence length is \(t_c = 2E_{\Psi} / M_{J/\Psi}^2\), which is much shorter than the formation length.
- The mean transverse size of the \(\bar cc\) pair is \(\langle r_T^2 \rangle \sim 1/m_c^2\).
- The width of the \(\chi\) momentum fraction distribution is \(\langle (\alpha - 1/2)^2 \rangle = 0.01\).
- Nuclear suppression from shadowing and absorption is nearly a factor of 0.5 for heavy nuclei (see Fig. e-dep).
- The total \(J/\Psi\)-nucleon cross section rises with energy approximately as \(s^{0.2}\).
- At 200 GeV, energy loss is the dominant effect shaping the \(x_F\) dependence; at 800 GeV, the steep \(x_F\) dependence is a combined effect of quark and gluon shadowing and energy loss.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of charmonium suppression from the SPS energy range (where classical attenuation models apply) to the higher energies of RHIC and LHC, where coherence effects dominate. It builds on prior work by Kopeliovich et al. (1991) predicting shadowing from coherence length effects, confirmed by NMC measurements of exclusive \(J/\Psi\) photoproduction off nuclei, and by HERMES data for \(\rho\) meson electroproduction. The approach contrasts with phenomenological fits by Capella et al. and by Y. A. et al., and provides a parameter-free QCD calculation that enables realistic predictions for RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collisions, where charmonium is used as a probe of quark-gluon plasma formation.
