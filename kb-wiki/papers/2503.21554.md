# Internal structure of $T_{cc}$ and $X(3872)$ by using compositeness

> The internal structure of the near-threshold exotic hadrons, $T_{cc}$ and $X(3872)$, are studied by respecting the decay and coupled-channel contributions. The effective field theory model is introduced to calculate the compositeness, the probability of finding the hadronic molecular component. Applying the new interpretation scheme for the complex compositeness of unstable states and using the bare energies of the compact component estimated by the constituent quark model, we find that approximately 50 % of the $T_{cc}$ wavefunction is occupied by the $D^{0}D^{*+}$ molecular component. This indicates that the structure of $T_{cc}$ is primarily dominated by the $D^0D^{*+}$ component, having the nearest threshold. However, other components, such as the isospin partner $D^{*0}D^{+}$ channels, also provide non-negligible contributions. On the other hand, about 90 % of $X(3872)$ is composed of the $D^{0}\bar{D}^{0*}$ molecular component. This is because the non-composite bare state and the charged $D\bar{D}^{*}$ threshold are located relatively far from the $X(3872)$ and contribute very little to its internal structure.

## Key claim

The paper finds that approximately 50% of the \(T_{cc}\) wavefunction is the \(D^{0}D^{*+}\) molecular component, but with non-negligible contributions from the isospin partner \(D^{*0}D^{+}\) channel and the compact component; for \(X(3872)\), about 90% is the \(D^{0}\bar{D}^{0*}\) molecular component, making it an almost pure molecule. These conclusions depend on using a new interpretation scheme for complex compositeness and on bare energies estimated from constituent quark models.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic effective field theory (EFT) model with two scattering channels (the threshold channel and a coupled channel) coupled to a discrete bare state, using a complex coupling constant to effectively incorporate decay contributions. The cutoff is set to 140 MeV based on pion-exchange range, and the bare-state energies are taken from constituent quark model calculations (7 MeV for \(T_{cc}\), 78.36 MeV for \(X(3872)\)). The coupling constant is then fixed to reproduce the measured binding energy and decay width, and the complex compositeness is computed and interpreted via a probabilistic scheme that yields three quantities: \(\mathcal{X}\) (probability of composite component), \(\mathcal{Y}\) (uncertain identification), and \(\mathcal{Z}\) (probability of elementary component).

## Key numbers

* \(T_{cc}\) eigenenergy: \(E = -0.36 - i0.024\) MeV (binding energy \(B = 0.36\) MeV, decay width \(\Gamma = 0.048\) MeV).
* \(X(3872)\) eigenenergy: \(E = -0.04 - i0.595\) MeV (\(B = 0.04\) MeV, \(\Gamma = 1.19\) MeV).
* Threshold energy difference for \(T_{cc}\): \(\Delta\omega = 1.41\) MeV (between \(D^{0}D^{*+}\) and \(D^{*0}D^{+}\) thresholds).
* Threshold energy difference for \(X(3872)\): \(\Delta\omega = 8.23\) MeV (between \(D^{0}\bar{D}^{0*}\) and \(D^{*-}D^{+}\) thresholds).
* For \(T_{cc}\): compositeness of threshold channel \(X_1 = 0.541 - 0.007i\); after interpretation, \(\mathcal{X} = 0.537\), \(\mathcal{Z} = 0.456\).
* For \(X(3872)\): compositeness of threshold channel \(X_1 = 0.919 - 0.079i\); after interpretation, \(\mathcal{X} = 0.890\), \(\mathcal{Z} = 0.081\).

## Context

This work extends the compositeness analysis of near-threshold exotic hadrons by explicitly including decay and coupled-channel effects, building on the low-energy universality framework of Braaten (2004) and Hyodo (2014). It applies the new probabilistic interpretation scheme for complex compositeness developed by Kinugawa (2024) to the specific cases of \(T_{cc}\) (discovered by LHCb in 2021) and \(X(3872)\) (discovered by Belle in 2003). The results enable quantitative comparison of molecular versus compact components in these states and can be extended to other near-threshold exotic hadrons with similar decay and coupled-channel structures.
