# Elastic and inelastic pion-nucleon scattering to fourth order in chiral perturbation theory

> We extend our previous study of elastic pion-nucleon scattering in the framework of chiral perturbation theory by performing a combined analysis of the reactions $πN \to πN$ and $πN \to ππN$. The calculation is carried out to fourth order in the chiral expansion using the heavy baryon approach and the covariant formulation supplemented with a modified version of the extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme. We demonstrate that a combined fit to experimental data in both channels leads to a reduced amount of correlations between the low-energy constants. A satisfactory description of the experimental data in both channels is obtained, which is further improved upon including tree-level contributions of the $Δ$(1232) resonance. We also explore a possibility of using the empirical information about $πN$ subthreshold parameters obtained recently by means of the Roy-Steiner equations to stabilize the fits.

## Key claim

A combined chiral perturbation theory analysis of elastic πN → πN and inelastic πN → ππN scattering to fourth order reduces correlations among low-energy constants compared to fits using only elastic data, but the description of πN → ππN data at fourth order deteriorates relative to third order except in the covariant approach below 300 MeV, indicating that the large values of certain LECs preferred by elastic scattering conflict with the production channel.

## Method

The authors computed the πN → πN and πN → ππN amplitudes to fourth order (Q⁴) in chiral perturbation theory using both heavy-baryon and covariant formulations with a modified extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme. They performed combined fits to experimental πN scattering data below Tπ < 100 MeV from the GWU-SAID database and πN → ππN total and double-differential cross section data up to Tπ < 350 MeV, including tree-level Δ(1232) contributions and constraints from Roy-Steiner subthreshold parameters.

## Key numbers

- Fits used 1704 πN experimental data points with Tπ < 100 MeV; πN → ππN data sets included 87–140 data points depending on maximum energy cutoff (Tπ,ππN < 250–350 MeV)
- At Q⁴, including subthreshold constraints reduced correlations: in HB-NN scheme, c₁–c₂ correlation dropped from 0.90 to 0.73, c₂–e₁₆ from −0.94 to −0.62, c₂–d₁₊₂ from 0.94 to 0.87
- The linear combination d₁₄₋₁₅ changed from ∼ −6 GeV⁻² at Q³ to ∼ −10 GeV⁻² at Q⁴ in the covariant approach
- Δ(1232) contributions used hA = 1.35 and g₁ = 2.29 (large-Nc values)
- Breakdown scale Λb ∼ 600 MeV used for naturalness estimates: ci ∼ 2 GeV⁻¹, di ∼ 3 GeV⁻², ei ∼ 5 GeV⁻³

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous fourth-order study of elastic πN scattering (Siemens et al., 2016) by adding the inelastic πN → ππN channel and subthreshold constraints from the Roy-Steiner analysis of Hoferichter et al. (2015). It builds on earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations of πN → ππN at leading one-loop order (Bernard et al., 1995; Fettes et al., 1999) and tree-level covariant studies (Siemens et al., 2014). The results enable more constrained extractions of low-energy constants for use in few-nucleon calculations and provide a benchmark for future studies with explicit Δ and Roper resonance degrees of freedom.
