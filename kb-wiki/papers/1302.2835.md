# Non-congruence of the nuclear liquid-gas and deconfinement phase transitions

> First order phase transitions (PTs) with more than one globally conserved charge, so-called non-congruent PTs, have characteristic differences compared to congruent PTs (e.g., dimensionality of phase diagrams, location and properties of critical points and endpoints). In the present article we investigate the non-congruence of the nuclear liquid-gas PT at sub-saturation densities and the deconfinement PT at high densities and/or temperatures in Coulomb-less models, relevant for heavy-ion collisions and neutron stars. For the first PT, we use the FSUgold relativistic mean-field model and for the second one the relativistic chiral SU(3) model. The chiral SU(3) model is one of the few models for the deconfinement PT, which contains quarks and hadrons in arbitrary proportions (i.e. a "solution") and gives a continuous transition from pure hadronic to pure quark matter above a critical point. The study shows the universality of the applied concept of non-congruence for the two PTs with an upper critical point, and illustrates the different typical scales involved. In addition, we find a principle difference between the liquid-gas and the deconfinement PTs: in contrast to the ordinary Van-der-Waals-like PT, the phase coexistence line of the deconfinement PT has a negative slope in the pressure-temperature plane. As another qualitative difference we find that the non-congruent features of the deconfinement PT become vanishingly small around the critical point.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition (LGPT) and the deconfinement (quark-hadron) phase transition (QHPT) are non-congruent first-order phase transitions when the system has more than one globally conserved charge and is isospin asymmetric, but the non-congruent features of the QHPT become vanishingly small near its critical point, unlike the LGPT.

## Method

The authors used the FSUgold relativistic mean-field model to describe the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition at sub-saturation densities, and the relativistic chiral SU(3) model (a unified equation-of-state approach containing both hadrons and quarks in arbitrary proportions) for the deconfinement phase transition. Both models were applied in a Coulomb-less approximation, and phase coexistence was computed under conditions of global charge neutrality for heavy-ion collisions and neutron star scenarios, with the chiral SU(3) model employing a Polyakov-loop-like field \(\Phi\) as the order parameter for deconfinement.

## Key numbers

- The chiral SU(3) model's first-order deconfinement phase transition line is calibrated to terminate on the zero temperature axis at four times saturation density for charge-neutral beta-equilibrated matter.
- The FSUgold model reproduces nuclear saturation properties and its neutron matter equation of state agrees with recent experimental and observational constraints.
- The phase coexistence line of the deconfinement phase transition has a negative slope in the pressure-temperature plane, in contrast to the ordinary Van-der-Waals-like liquid-gas phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the concept of non-congruent phase transitions, previously studied in terrestrial high-temperature plasmas (Iosilevskiy, 2003) and introduced to astrophysics for neutron stars (Iosilevskiy, 2010), to both the nuclear liquid-gas and deconfinement transitions. It contradicts earlier claims (Muller and Serot, 1995; Tatsumi et al., 2011) that these transitions become second-order in asymmetric matter, instead showing they remain first-order but exhibit non-congruent behavior. The study enables a unified thermodynamic treatment of both phase transitions in heavy-ion collisions and neutron star interiors, and highlights the need for proper treatment of non-congruence in future equation-of-state modeling.
