# Radiative capture reaction for $^{17}$Ne formation within a full three-body model

> Background: The breakout from the hot Carbon-Nitrogen-Oxigen (CNO) cycles can trigger the rp-process in type I x-ray bursts. In this environment, a competition between $^{15}\text{O}(α,γ){^{19}\text{Ne}}$ and the two-proton capture reaction $^{15}\text{O}(2p,γ){^{17}\text{Ne}}$ is expected. Purpose: Determine the three-body radiative capture reaction rate for ${^{17}\text{Ne}}$ formation including sequential and direct, resonant and non-resonant contributions on an equal footing. Method: Two different discretization methods have been applied to generate $^{17}$Ne states in a full three-body model: the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator method and the hyperspherical adiabatic expansion method. The binary $p$--$^{15}$O interaction has been adjusted to reproduce the known spectrum of the unbound $^{16}$F nucleus. The dominant $E1$ contributions to the $^{15}\text{O}(2p,γ){^{17}\text{Ne}}$ reaction rate have been calculated from the inverse photodissociation process. Results: Three-body calculations provide a reliable description of $^{17}$Ne states. The agreement with the available experimental data on $^{17}$Ne is discussed. It is shown that the $^{15}\text{O}(2p,γ){^{17}\text{Ne}}$ reaction rates computed within the two methods agree in a broad range of temperatures. The present calculations are compared with a previous theoretical estimation of the reaction rate. Conclusions: It is found that the full three-body model provides a reaction rate several orders of magnitude larger than the only previous estimation. The implications for the rp-process in type I x-ray bursts should be investigated.

## Key claim

The full three-body model provides a reaction rate for the $^{15}\text{O}(2p,\gamma){^{17}\text{Ne}}$ radiative capture that is several orders of magnitude larger than the only previous theoretical estimation by Grigorenko et al., but the authors hedge that the implications for the rp-process in type I x-ray bursts "should be investigated" rather than claiming a definitive astrophysical conclusion.

## Method

The authors applied two different discretization methods to describe $^{17}$Ne states within a full $^{15}\text{O}+p+p$ three-body model: the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) method within the hyperspherical harmonic framework, and the hyperspherical adiabatic (HA) expansion method with a box boundary condition. The binary $p$--$^{15}$O interaction was adjusted to reproduce the known spectrum of the unbound $^{16}$F nucleus using an $l$-dependent potential with central, spin-orbit, and spin-spin terms. The dominant $E1$ contributions to the $^{15}\text{O}(2p,\gamma){^{17}\text{Ne}}$ reaction rate were calculated from the inverse photodissociation process, treating sequential and direct, resonant and non-resonant contributions on an equal footing.

## Key numbers

- The $^{17}$Ne ground state two-proton separation energy was fixed to the experimental value of -0.943 MeV, requiring a three-body force strength of $v_{3b}=-1.94$ MeV in the THO case and $v_{3b}=-2.05$ MeV in the HA case.
- The computed matter radius of $^{17}$Ne was 2.69 fm (THO) and 2.66 fm (HA), in good agreement with the experimental value of $r_{mat}=2.75(7)$ fm from Ozawa et al.
- The computed charge radius of $^{17}$Ne was 2.95 fm (THO) and 2.99 fm (HA), slightly underestimating the experimental value of $r_{ch}=3.042(21)$ fm from Geithner et al.
- The $l$-content analysis showed that $d$ waves contribute roughly 63% (THO) and 62% (HA) of the norm, while $s$ waves contribute 30% (THO) and 31% (HA).
- The $p$--$^{15}$O potential parameters included central strengths of $v_c^{(0)}=-50.0$ MeV, $v_c^{(1)}=-11.0$ MeV, and $v_c^{(2)}=-48.4$ MeV, with a spin-orbit strength of $v_{so}=-30$ MeV fm$^2$ for $l=1,2$.

## Context

This work extends previous three-body calculations by the same group for Borromean nuclei such as $^{6}$He and $^{9}$Be (Casal et al., 2013, 2014), and directly contradicts the earlier theoretical estimation of the $^{15}\text{O}(2p,\gamma){^{17}\text{Ne}}$ reaction rate by Grigorenko et al. (2005, 2006), which computed resonant and non-resonant contributions separately. The present unified treatment provides a reaction rate several orders of magnitude larger, which could affect the competition between the $^{15}\text{O}(\alpha,\gamma){^{19}\text{Ne}}$ breakout reaction and the two-proton capture path in type I x-ray bursts, potentially altering the trigger conditions of the rp-process.
