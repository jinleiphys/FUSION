# Electromagnetic and weak probes: theory

> An overview of the latest theoretical developments and results on electromagnetic and weak probes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is presented. The possibilities to use electromagnetic probes, i.e., photons and dileptons, as a spectrometer, thermometer, chronometer, polarimeter, barometer, and multimeter of the collision system, as well as to explore the QCD phase diagram and to locate phase transitions such as the critical endpoint, are discussed.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic and weak probes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions can provide undistorted information about their production site, but the theoretical description of thermal photon and dilepton rates still faces significant uncertainties, particularly from lattice QCD calculations and from the need to include baryonic degrees of freedom and viscous corrections before quantitative comparisons to experimental data can be made.

## Method

The paper reviews theoretical approaches for calculating photon and dilepton emission rates using thermal field theory (based on the electromagnetic current-current correlation function) and relativistic kinetic theory. For photons, the review covers perturbative QCD calculations at leading and next-to-leading order, lattice QCD analyses of the vector-current correlator, and effective model calculations for the hadron gas phase using the Massive Yang-Mills approach and hadronic many-body theory. For dileptons, the review discusses in-medium vector-meson spectral functions calculated via hadronic many-body theory (Rapp-Wambach spectral functions), the Functional Renormalization Group approach, and combined analyses of QCD and Weinberg sum rules.

## Key numbers

- The NLO correction to the thermal QGP photon rate represents a 20% increase over the LO result for alpha_s = 0.3
- The suppression of direct photon v2 by shear and bulk viscous corrections is of the order of 20-30%
- The inverse slope parameter T_slope and integrated low-mass dilepton excess yields are compared across collision energies from HADES (2.42 GeV) through RHIC (200 GeV) to LHC (2.76 TeV)
- Z0 boson production in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV is compared to calculations using EPPS16 and nCTEQ15 nuclear PDFs

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework for electromagnetic probes developed by McLerran and Toimela (1985), Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001), and Ghiglieri et al. (2013), and builds on the hadronic many-body theory approach of Rapp and Wambach (1999) and Rapp (2011). The paper discusses recent comparisons of theoretical calculations to experimental data from PHENIX, STAR, HADES, NA60, and ALICE collaborations, and identifies open questions including the need for high-precision measurements to distinguish between models and to constrain in-medium properties of the rho meson, as well as the need for future lattice QCD calculations with larger lattices or higher statistics to obtain photon rates with small uncertainties.
