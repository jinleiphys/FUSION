# Energy per particle of nuclear and neutron matter from subleading chiral three-nucleon interactions

> We derive from the subleading contributions to the chiral three-nucleon interaction [published in Phys.~Rev.~C77, 064004 (2008) and Phys.~Rev.~C84, 054001 (2011)] their first-order contributions to the energy per particle of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter in an analytical way. For the variety of short-range and long-range terms that constitute the subleading chiral 3N-force the pertinent closed 3-ring, 2-ring, and 1-ring diagrams are evaluated. While 3-ring diagrams vanish by a spin-trace and the results for 2-ring diagrams can be given in terms of elementary functions of the ratio Fermi-momentum over pion mass, one ends up in most cases for the closed 1-ring diagrams with one-parameter integrals. The same treatment is applied to the subsubleading chiral three-nucleon interactions as far as these have been constructed up to now.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytical calculation of the first-order contributions to the energy per particle of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter arising from the subleading chiral three-nucleon forces, with the caveat that the results are restricted to first-order many-body perturbation theory and that for many terms the final expressions involve one-parameter integrals rather than fully closed analytic forms.

## Method

The author evaluates the closed 3-ring, 2-ring, and 1-ring diagrams (Figure 2) obtained by concatenating the three nucleon lines of each subleading chiral three-nucleon interaction term in its given spin-isospin-momentum operator form. After taking spin- and isospin-traces over the closed rings, the momentum-space loops are evaluated as Fermi sphere integrals with the Fermi momentum \(k_f\) for nuclear matter or \(k_n\) for neutron matter. The 3-ring diagrams vanish by a spin-trace, the 2-ring diagram results are expressed in terms of elementary functions of the ratio \(u = k_f/m_\pi\) or \(u = k_n/m_\pi\), and the 1-ring diagrams typically reduce to one-parameter integrals over the dimensionless variable \(x\).

## Key numbers

* The 2-ring diagram contribution to the energy per particle of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter from the \(2\pi\)-exchange-contact topology (first term) is given by \(\bar E(\rho) = \frac{g_A^2 C_T m_\pi^7}{560 \pi^5 f_\pi^4} \left\{ 3u^2 + \frac{11u^4}{2} - 6u^6 - 2u^5(7+3u^2)\arctan u - (3+7u^2)\ln(1+u^2) \right\}\), with \(u = k_f/m_\pi\).
* The 2-ring diagram contribution to the energy per particle of pure neutron matter from the same interaction is \(\bar E_n(\rho_n) = \frac{g_A^2 C_T m_\pi^7}{1680 \pi^5 f_\pi^4} \left\{ 3u^2 + \frac{11u^4}{2} + \frac{23u^6}{2} - 2u^5(7+3u^2)\arctan u - (3+7u^2)\ln(1+u^2) \right\}\), with \(u = k_n/m_\pi\).
* The 2-ring diagram contribution from the leading relativistic \(1/M\)-correction to the \(1\pi\)-exchange-contact topology for nuclear matter is \(\bar E(\rho) = \frac{g_A^2(C_S-C_T) m_\pi^6}{512 \pi^4 M f_\pi^2} \left\{ 3u^2 - 18u^4 + 8u^6 + 24u^3\arctan 2u - \left(\frac{3}{4}+9u^2\right)\ln(1+4u^2) \right\}\).
* The 2-ring diagram contribution from the \(2\pi\)-exchange topology (long-range terms) for nuclear matter is \(\bar E(\rho)^{2r} = \frac{3g_A^4 m_\pi^7}{(4\pi)^5 f_\pi^6} \left\{ \frac{3u^2}{4} - \frac{7u^4}{2} + \frac{4u^6}{3} + 5u^3\arctan 2u - \left(\frac{3}{16}+2u^2\right)\ln(1+4u^2) \right\}\).
* The 2-ring diagram contribution from the \(1/M\)-correction with explicit \(\Delta(1232)\)-isobars for nuclear matter is \(\bar E(\rho)^{2r} = \frac{g_A^4 m_\pi^8}{64\pi^4 f_\pi^4 M \Delta^2} \left\{ \frac{5u^2}{2} - 9u^4 + \frac{8u^6}{3} - \frac{8u^8}{5} + 14u^3\arctan 2u - \left(\frac{5}{8}+6u^2\right)\ln(1+4u^2) \right\}\), with \(\Delta = 293\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analytical treatment of chiral three-nucleon forces in nuclear matter beyond the leading N\(^2\)LO contributions, using the subleading 3N-force operator forms derived by the Darmstadt group in Phys. Rev. C77, 064004 (2008) and Phys. Rev. C84, 054001 (2011). It provides semi-analytical expressions that can serve as a check on the numerical normal-ordering and partial-wave decomposition approaches previously applied by the Darmstadt group (e.g., in calculations of the equation of state and pairing gaps). The results enable future studies to test the accuracy of approximations used in numerical many-body perturbation theory and to incorporate subsubleading (N\(^4\)LO) chiral 3N-forces as they become fully constructed.
