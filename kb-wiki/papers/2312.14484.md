# Entanglement in few-nucleon scattering events

> We investigate the spin entanglement in few-nucleon scattering processes involving nucleons and deuterons. For this purpose, we consider the entanglement power introduced by Beane et al. We analyze different entanglement entropies as a basis to define the entanglement power of the strong interaction and calculate the corresponding entanglement powers for proton-neutron, neutron-deuteron, proton-deuteron, and deuteron-deuteron scattering. For the latter two processes, we also take into account the modification from the Coulomb interaction. In contrast to proton-neutron scattering, no universal low-energy features are evident in the spin entanglement in neutron-deuteron, proton-deuteron, and deuteron-deuteron scattering.

## Key claim

The spin entanglement generated in neutron-deuteron, proton-deuteron, and deuteron-deuteron scattering shows no universal low-energy features, in contrast to the proton-neutron case where entanglement suppression correlates with an emergent Wigner SU(4) symmetry; the Coulomb interaction modifies the entanglement only indirectly through its effect on the Coulomb-subtracted nuclear phase shifts, not through direct spin-dependent coupling.

## Method

The authors computed the entanglement power of the strong interaction for s-wave scattering by averaging various entanglement entropies (von Neumann entropy, its Taylor expansions, and Rényi entropies) over all possible initial spin states using the Fubini-Study volume element. They constructed the scattering operator S in terms of spin scalar products and phase shifts for spin-1/2-spin-1/2 (proton-neutron), spin-1-spin-1/2 (nucleon-deuteron), and spin-1-spin-1 (deuteron-deuteron) systems, then evaluated the resulting entanglement powers using phase shift data from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis PWA93, pionless effective field theory calculations, and experimental data.

## Key numbers

* For proton-neutron scattering, the entanglement power epsilon_1 based on the leading Taylor expansion is given by (1/6) sin^2[2(delta_0 - delta_1)], where delta_0 and delta_1 are the spin-singlet and spin-triplet s-wave phase shifts.
* Using scattering lengths a_0 = 5 fm and a_1 = -20 fm, the minima of epsilon_1 occur at k_min,1 = 0 MeV and k_min,2 approximately 20 MeV, while maxima occur at k_max,+ approximately 7 MeV and k_max,- approximately 57 MeV.
* For nucleon-deuteron scattering, the entanglement power epsilon_1 = (8/243)(17 + 10 cos[2(delta_1/2 - delta_3/2)]) sin^2[delta_1/2 - delta_3/2], with a minimum at relative momenta of order 150 MeV.
* The total volume of the Fubini-Study manifold for spin-1/2 states is pi, and for spin-1 states is pi^2/2.

## Context

This work extends the entanglement-based approach introduced by Beane et al. (2018) for nucleon-nucleon scattering to systems involving deuterons, and incorporates the Coulomb interaction treatment developed by Bai and Ren (2022) for charged-particle scattering. The results confirm the emergent Wigner SU(4) symmetry interpretation for proton-neutron scattering but show that no analogous universal low-energy behavior governs spin entanglement in nucleon-deuteron or deuteron-deuteron scattering. The formalism enables future studies of entanglement in other few-nucleon systems and could be applied to investigate whether dynamical entanglement suppression is a general property of the strong interaction at low energies.
