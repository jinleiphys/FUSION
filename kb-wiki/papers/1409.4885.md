# Fission induced by nucleons at intermediate energies

> Monte Carlo calculations of fission of actinides and pre-actinides induced by protons and neutrons in the energy range from 100 MeV to 1 GeV are carried out by means of a recent version of the Liège Intranuclear Cascade Model, INCL++, coupled with two different evaporation-fission codes, GEMINI++ and ABLA07. In order to reproduce experimental fission cross sections, model parameters are usually adjusted on available (p,f) cross sections and used to predict (n,f) cross sections for the same isotopes.

## Key claim

The authors test whether the same set of fission model parameters can simultaneously reproduce both proton-induced and neutron-induced fission cross sections for the same target nuclei in the 100 MeV to 1 GeV range, finding that for most actinides the same parameters work well, but for some nuclei (notably $^{232}Th$ and $^{233}U$) different barrier-height adjustments are needed for the proton and neutron channels.

## Method

The authors coupled the Liège Intranuclear Cascade Model (INCL++) with two different evaporation-fission codes, GEMINI++ (for actinides) and ABLA07 (for lead and bismuth), to simulate fission induced by protons and neutrons on targets from $^{208}Pb$ to $^{239}Pu$. They adjusted two fission-model parameters, the ratio of level density parameters at saddle and ground-state deformation ($\tilde a_f/\tilde a_n$ in GEMINI++) or a scale factor $k_f$ on the saddle-point level density parameter (in ABLA07), plus a global shift $\Delta B_f$ of the liquid-drop fission barriers, to fit the experimental $(p,f)$ cross sections of Kotov (2006), then used the same or very close parameter values to predict $(n,f)$ cross sections for the same isotopes.

## Key numbers

* For $^{235}U(p,f)$, the best fit used $\tilde a_f/\tilde a_n = 1.050$ and $\Delta B_f = -0.2$ MeV, yielding $\chi^2_0 = 2.75$ with $Q = 0.91$ (9 data points, 2 parameters).
* For $^{238}U(p,f)$, the best fit used $\tilde a_f/\tilde a_n = 1.038$ and $\Delta B_f = -0.3$ MeV, yielding $\chi^2_0 = 4.50$ with $Q = 0.72$.
* For $^{209}Bi(p,f)$ with INCL++/ABLA07, the best fit used $k_f = 1.01$ and $\Delta B_f = +0.18$ MeV, yielding $\chi^2_0 = 2.53$ with $Q = 0.93$.
* For $^{232}Th$, the $(p,f)$ fit used $\tilde a_f/\tilde a_n = 1.040$ and $\Delta B_f = -0.3$ MeV, but the $(n,f)$ calculation required $\Delta B_f = +0.1$ MeV with the same $\tilde a_f/\tilde a_n$ ratio.
* The ratio $\sigma_{pf}/\sigma_{nf}$ for $^{232}Th$ at $E_{proj} \simeq 180$ MeV is predicted by the systematics of Smirnov (2001) to be 1.55.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Baznat et al. (2003), who used CEM2k+GEM2 and LAQGSM+GEM2 to fit $(p,f)$ cross sections from Prokofiev's systematics and then predict $(n,f)$ cross sections, but here the authors use the INCL++ cascade model (which includes a self-consistent stopping time and avoids a separate pre-equilibrium stage) coupled with GEMINI++ or ABLA07. The results enable prediction of $(n,f)$ cross sections for targets where only $(p,f)$ data exist, which is important for applications such as accelerator-driven systems, radioactive waste transmutation, and radiation shielding design. The work also provides a direct comparison with the JENDL/HE-2007 evaluations and with recent n_TOF measurements at CERN.
