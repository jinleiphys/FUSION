# Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal effect in a quark-gluon plasma and the Boltzmann equation

> We show how the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect on photon production rates in a quark-gluon plasma can be derived via the usual Boltzmann equation. To do this, we first derive the electromagnetic polarization tensor using linear response theory, and then formulate the Boltzmann equation including the collisions mediated by soft gluon exchanges. We then identify the resulting expression for the production rate with that obtained by the field-theoretic formalism recently proposed by Arnold, Moore and Yaffe. To illustrate the LPM effect we solve the Boltzmann equation in the diffusion approximation.

## Key claim

The Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect on photon production rates in a quark-gluon plasma can be derived via the usual linearized Boltzmann equation, not only through the explicit summation of Feynman diagrams as in the field-theoretic formalism of Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (2001-2002). The Boltzmann equation captures the relevant multiple-scattering diagrams and kinematical approximations for small-angle scatterings, providing a simpler and more intuitive derivation, though the resulting rates are not different from those obtained by the earlier formalism.

## Method

The authors first derive the electromagnetic polarization tensor using linear response theory, relating it to the retarded current-current correlation function. They then formulate the linearized Boltzmann equation for quark and antiquark distribution functions perturbed by a weak external electromagnetic field, including a collision term that describes scatterings mediated by soft gluon exchanges at the Born approximation level with hard thermal loop (HTL) corrections. The solution of the Boltzmann equation, which takes into account repeated small-angle scatterings, is identified with the vertex function in the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe formalism, and the LPM effect is illustrated by solving the Boltzmann equation in the diffusion approximation.

## Key numbers

- The photon production rate per unit volume and frequency for soft photons ($\omega \ll T$) is given by $\frac{dN_\gamma}{d^4 x d\omega} = -\frac{T}{2\pi^2}\sum_{i=1}^{2} \;{{\rm Im}\,\Pi^{\rm ret}_{ii}(\omega,\bk)}$.
- The quark thermal mass $m_\infty \sim gT$ and the Debye mass $m_D$ satisfy $m_\infty^2 = g^2 T^2 C_f/4$ and $m_D^2 = (2N_c+N_f)g^2T^2/6$, with $C_f = (N_c^2-1)/(2N_c)$ and $N_c=3$.
- The collision rate $\gamma \sim g^2 T$ for soft momentum transfers $Q \sim gT$, derived from the scattering cross section $\sigma \sim \int dQ^2 (d\sigma/dQ^2)$ with $d\sigma/dQ^2 \sim g^4/Q^4$ and density $n \sim T^3$.
- In the diffusion approximation, the Bethe-Heitler rate is $\frac{dN_\gamma^{BH}}{d^4 x} = C_{BH} \frac{d\omega}{\omega}$ with $C_{BH} = \frac{\bar e^2 g^2 C_f T^4}{72\pi^3} \frac{m_{_D}^2}{m_\infty^2}\ln\frac{m_\infty}{m_{_D}}$, where $\bar e^2 \equiv 4N_c\sum_f e_f^2$.
- The LPM-suppressed rate for small $\omega$ is $\frac{dN_\gamma^{LPM}}{d^4 x} = C_{BH}\frac{d\omega}{\sqrt{\omega\omega_c}}$, with $\omega_c = \frac{ \pi^3 g^2 C_f T^3}{162 (\ln 2)^2} \frac{m_{_D}^2 }{m_\infty^4} \ln\left(\frac{m_\infty}{m_{_D}}\right)$.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretic derivation of the LPM effect by Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (2001-2002) by showing that the same physics is captured by the standard linearized Boltzmann equation, which is simpler and more intuitive. It also generalizes Migdal's original derivation (1956) from fixed scattering centers to the case of two-body collisions in a quark-gluon plasma. The approach enables future calculations of photon production in non-equilibrated plasmas and provides a connection to transport calculations such as electrical conductivity (Baym 1997, Arnold 2000).
