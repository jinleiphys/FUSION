# D_{sJ}^+(2317)\to D_s^+π^0 decay width

> We use the QCD sum rules to analize the hadronic decay $D_{sJ}^{+}(2317)\to D_s^+π^0$, in the hypotesis that the $D_{sJ}^{+}(2317)$ can be identified as a four-quark state. We use a diquak-antidiquark current and work to the order of $m_s$ in full QCD, without relying on $1/m_c$ expansion. We find that the partial decay width of the hadronic isospin violating mode is proportional to the isovector quark condensate, $\langle0|\bar{d}d-\bar{u}u|0\rangle$. The estimated partial decay width is of the order of 6 keV.

## Key claim

The partial decay width of the isospin-violating hadronic decay \(D_{sJ}^{+}(2317)\to D_s^{+}\pi^{0}\) is proportional to the isovector quark condensate \(\langle0|\bar{d}d-\bar{u}u|0\rangle\) and is estimated to be of order 6 keV, but this result depends critically on the value of the isospin-breaking parameter \(\gamma\), which introduces a large uncertainty; the width could range from 0.2 keV to 40 keV depending on \(\gamma\).

## Method

The author used the QCD sum rule formalism with a three-point correlation function, employing a diquark-antidiquark interpolating current for the \(D_{sJ}^{+}(2317)\) meson as a four-quark state. The calculation was performed at leading order in full QCD, working to order \(m_s\) without relying on a \(1/m_c\) expansion, and the pion pole method was used to extract the coupling constant \(g_{D_{sJ}D_s\pi}\). The isospin violation was introduced through the difference between the \(u\) and \(d\) quark condensates, parameterized by \(\gamma = \langle0|\bar{d}d-\bar{u}u|0\rangle / \langle0|\bar{u}u|0\rangle\).

## Key numbers

- The coupling constant \(g_{D_{sJ}D_s\pi}\) was determined to be \(50\ \text{MeV}\) for \(\sqrt{s_0}=2.7\ \text{GeV}\) and \(\gamma=-1\times10^{-2}\), with a range of \(45\ \text{MeV}\leq g_{D_{sJ}D_s\pi}\leq 55\ \text{MeV}\) when varying \(\sqrt{s_0}\) between \(2.6\) and \(2.8\ \text{GeV}\).
- The partial decay width for \(\gamma=-1\times10^{-2}\) is \(\Gamma(D_{sJ}^{+}(2317)\rightarrow D_s^{+}\pi^0) = (6\pm2)\ \text{keV}\).
- Using the largest (in module) value \(\gamma=-2.6\times10^{-2}\) from Kim, Musakhanov, and Siddikov (2005), the width could be as large as \(\sim 40\ \text{keV}\); using the smallest value \(\gamma=-2\times10^{-3}\) gives \(g_{D_{sJ}D_s\pi}=10\ \text{MeV}\).
- The current-meson coupling \(\lambda_S\) was taken as \((2.9\pm0.3)\times10^{-3}\ \text{GeV}^5\) from the two-point function analysis of Bracco et al. (2005).

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule analysis of the \(D_{sJ}^{+}(2317)\) mass as a four-quark state by Bracco et al. (2005) to the calculation of its strong decay width. The result is compared with other theoretical approaches: calculations assuming a conventional \(c\bar{s}\) state by Bardeen, Eichten, and Hill (2003) (21.5 keV), Colangelo and De Fazio (2003) (7\(\pm\)1 keV), Godfrey (2003) (\(\sim\)10 keV), Fayyazuddin and Riazuddin (2004) (16 keV), and Wei, Huang, and Zhu (2005) (39\(\pm\)5 keV), as well as a four-quark picture by Cheng and Hou (2003) (10-100 keV). The author concludes that the strong decay width alone cannot definitively distinguish between the \(c\bar{s}\) and four-quark interpretations, as both pictures yield overlapping ranges from a few keV to a hundred keV.
