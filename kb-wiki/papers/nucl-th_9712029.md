# Two photons decay of the glueball and scalar isoscalar mesons in a scaled NJL model

> We use a modified version of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model which implements the QCD trace anomaly to calculate the two photons decay width of the glueball (f_0(1500)) and of the two scalar mesons (f_0(1370), f_{J=0}(1710)) to which it is mixed. We investigate the effect of this mixing over the coupling constants of the f_0 states to the quarks.

## Key claim
Using a scaled Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model that implements the QCD trace anomaly, the authors calculate two-photon decay widths for the glueball candidate f0(1500) and the scalar mesons f0(1370) and f0(1710), finding that the relative magnitudes of these widths depend strongly on the value of the vacuum gluon condensate χ0, and that their results disagree qualitatively with the earlier mixing scheme of Close et al. (1997).

## Method
The authors use a modified SU(3) NJL model with a dilaton field χ that couples to quarks and implements the QCD trace anomaly, where the vacuum expectation value χ0 is identified with the gluon condensate. The model parameters are fixed to reproduce the pion and kaon masses, the pion decay constant, and the masses of a0(1450) and f0(1500), leaving χ0 as a free parameter that controls the mixing strength between the glueball and scalar meson fields. The two-photon decay widths are computed from the transition amplitudes involving quark loops, using the coupling constants of each f0 state to u and s quarks obtained after diagonalizing the 3x3 mixing matrix of the scalar isoscalar fields.

## Key numbers
- For χ0 = 400 MeV: Γ(f0(1370)) = 348 eV, Γ(f0(1500)) = 102 eV, Γ(f0(1710)) = 6.7 eV; masses: f0(1370) = 1341 MeV, f0(1500) = 1500 MeV, f0(1710) = 1753 MeV.
- For χ0 = 350 MeV: Γ(f0(1370)) = 617 eV, Γ(f0(1500)) = 112 eV, Γ(f0(1710)) = 8.85 eV; masses: f0(1370) = 1305 MeV, f0(1500) = 1500 MeV, f0(1710) = 1755 MeV.
- For χ0 = 300 MeV: Γ(f0(1370)) = 1258 eV, Γ(f0(1500)) = 123 eV, Γ(f0(1710)) = 12.3 eV; masses: f0(1370) = 1250 MeV, f0(1500) = 1500 MeV, f0(1710) = 1757 MeV.
- The experimental value for f0(1370) → 2γ is Γ = 5.4 ± 2.3 keV (from Ref. [14]).
- The constituent up quark mass is fixed at Mu = 725 MeV to reproduce a0(1450).
- The ratio Γ(f0(1370))/Γ(f0(1500)) varies from 3 to 10 in the model, compared to 6 to 11 in the work of Ref. [14bis] for Γ(f0(980))/Γ(f0(1590)).

## Context
This work extends the scaled NJL model developed by the authors in earlier papers (Jaminon and Bossche, 1996) to calculate two-photon decays of scalar mesons mixed with a glueball, following the mixing scheme proposed by Close et al. (1997) for f0(1370), f0(1500), and f0(1710). The results contradict the relative width predictions of Close et al. (1997), who found f0(1500) always had the smallest width, whereas this model finds f0(1710) always has the smallest width; the authors attribute this discrepancy to the chiral-symmetry-related factor (m² − 4M²) that suppresses the widths of f0(1370) and f0(1710) near threshold. The work enables future comparison with experimental measurements of the two-photon widths, which could test the mixing hypothesis, and the authors suggest that other models using dilatons coupled to scalar states should investigate this problem.
