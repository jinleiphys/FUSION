# An introductory lecture on Generalised Parton Distributions

> These lecture notes on Generalised Parton Distributions aim at providing a general picture of the field on the theoretical and phenomenological sides to master and Ph.D. students. They go along with the lecture given at the Baryon International School of Physics in 2021.

## Key claim

These lecture notes provide a pedagogical introduction to Generalised Parton Distributions (GPDs) for master and Ph.D. students, covering both theoretical foundations and phenomenological applications, with a focus on the pion case to simplify the discussion without losing substantial content relevant to other hadrons.

## Method

The lecture defines GPDs as non-local matrix elements off-diagonal in momentum space, parameterised by the average momentum fraction \(x\), the skewness \(\xi\), and the momentum transfer \(t\). It then systematically derives the key properties of GPDs: their forward limits relating them to Parton Distribution Functions (PDFs), their connection to electromagnetic form factors through \(x\)-integration, the soft pion theorem linking them to Distribution Amplitudes (DAs), their interpretation in impact parameter space, and their Mellin moments connecting them to the Energy-Momentum Tensor (EMT) form factors. The lecture also presents two complementary modelling strategies: the Double Distribution (DD) approach and the Lightfront Wave Function (LFWF) formalism.

## Key numbers

- The GPD support is limited to the square \((x,\xi) \in [-1,1]^2\) as shown in Diehl (1998).
- The forward limit relates the pion GPD to PDFs: \(H^q(x,0,0) = q(x)\Theta(x) - \bar{q}(-x)\Theta(-x)\) and \(H^g(x,0,0) = x g(x)\Theta(x) - x g(-x)\Theta(-x)\).
- The soft pion theorem for the non-singlet combination gives \(H^q_{NS}(x,1,0) = \varphi\left(\frac{1+x}{2}\right)\), where \(\varphi\) is the pion Distribution Amplitude.
- The first Mellin moment of the quark GPD connects to EMT form factors: \(\int_{-1}^1 dx\, x H^{q}(x,\xi,t) = A^q(t) + 4\xi^2 C^q(t)\).
- The Ji sum rule gives the total angular momentum of each quark flavour: \(J^q = \frac{1}{2}\left( A^q(0) + B^q(0)\right)\).
- Conservation laws require \(\sum_f A^{q_f}(0) + A^g(0) = 1\) and \(\sum_f B^{q_f}(0) + B^g(0) = 0\).
- The positivity condition for quark GPDs in the DGLAP region: \(|H^q(x,\xi)|_{x \ge \xi}| \le \sqrt{q\left(\frac{x-\xi}{1-\xi}\right) q\left(\frac{x+\xi}{1+\xi}\right)}\).

## Context

This lecture extends the foundational work on GPDs introduced in the 1990s by Mueller (1998), Ji (1996, 1997), and Radyushkin (1996, 1997), and follows the notation convention of Diehl (2003). It builds upon the standard DGLAP evolution equations from Gribov, Lipatov, Altarelli, and Parisi (1972-1977) and ERBL evolution from Efremov, Radyushkin, Brodsky, and Lepage (1978-1980). The lecture enables students to understand the role of GPDs in the physics case of current and future experimental facilities such as the US Electron Ion Collider (AbdulKhalek et al., 2021) and the Chinese Electron Ion Collider (Anderle et al., 2021), and highlights recent progress in covariant extension techniques (Dutrieux et al., 2021) that reconcile the Double Distribution and Lightfront Wave Function modelling approaches.
