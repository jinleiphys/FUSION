# Effective Field Theory for the Crystalline Colour Superconductive Phase of QCD

> We present an effective field theory for high density, low temperature QCD in the crystalline colour superconductive phase (LOFF phase). This interesting phase of QCD is characterized by a gap parameter with a crystalline pattern, breaking traslational and rotational invariance, and could have astrophysical applications. In the effective theory the fermions have a Majorana mass, which, besides colour, breaks translation and rotation symmetries. Fermions couple to the three phonons arising from the breaking of rotation and translation invariance. Integrating out the fermions leads eventually to an effective lagrangian in terms of the phonon fields only, which satisfies an anisotropic dispersion relation.

## Key claim

The paper presents an effective field theory for the crystalline colour superconductive (LOFF) phase of QCD at high density and low temperature, where the gap parameter has a crystalline pattern that breaks translational and rotational invariance. The authors construct the theory under the condition that the difference in chemical potentials between the two quark flavours lies in the range δμ ∈ (δμ₁, δμ₂), where this phase is energetically favoured over the BCS phase.

## Method

The authors start from QCD with two massless quark flavours at high density, introduce two chemical potentials μ₁ and μ₂ with δμ = μ₁ - μ₂, and integrate out gluons and negative-energy fermion fields near the Fermi surface. They then introduce velocity-dependent positive-energy fermion fields and construct an effective Lagrangian containing a Majorana mass term that arises from scalar and vector condensates breaking translational and rotational invariance. The theory is coupled to the three phonon fields (Nambu-Goldstone bosons) that arise from the breaking of space symmetries, and after integrating out the fermions, an effective Lagrangian for the phonon field alone is obtained.

## Key numbers

- The LOFF phase exists for δμ in the interval (δμ₁, δμ₂), where δμ₁ = 0.71Δ and δμ₂ = 0.744Δ, for μ = 0.4 GeV and Δ = 40 MeV.
- The scalar condensate gap parameter is Δ^(s) and the vector condensate gap parameter is Δ^(v), with the total momentum of a Cooper pair given by 2q.
- The phonon field φ satisfies the condition |φ| ≤ f, where f is the decay constant.
- The effective phonon Lagrangian contains an anisotropic dispersion relation with parallel velocity v_∥ and transverse velocity v, where the explicit values of these constants are not given numerically in the paper.

## Context

This work extends the effective theory approach of Gatto (2000) to the crystalline colour superconductive phase, which was first studied in QCD by Alford, Bowers, and Rajagopal (2000) based on the earlier LOFF mechanism from Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964) and Fulde and Ferrell (1964) in quantum electrodynamics. The paper enables further study of the phonon dynamics in the LOFF phase and provides a framework for investigating astrophysical applications such as neutron star glitches, quark star oscillations, and cooling of neutron stars.
