# The Large Hadron-Electron Collider at the HL-LHC

> The Large Hadron electron Collider (LHeC) is designed to move the field of deep inelastic scattering (DIS) to the energy and intensity frontier of particle physics. Exploiting energy recovery technology, it collides a novel, intense electron beam with a proton or ion beam from the High Luminosity--Large Hadron Collider (HL-LHC). The accelerator and interaction region are designed for concurrent electron-proton and proton-proton operation. This report represents an update of the Conceptual Design Report (CDR) of the LHeC, published in 2012. It comprises new results on parton structure of the proton and heavier nuclei, QCD dynamics, electroweak and top-quark physics. It is shown how the LHeC will open a new chapter of nuclear particle physics in extending the accessible kinematic range in lepton-nucleus scattering by several orders of magnitude. Due to enhanced luminosity, large energy and the cleanliness of the hadronic final states, the LHeC has a strong Higgs physics programme and its own discovery potential for new physics. Building on the 2012 CDR, the report represents a detailed updated design of the energy recovery electron linac (ERL) including new lattice, magnet, superconducting radio frequency technology and further components. Challenges of energy recovery are described and the lower energy, high current, 3-turn ERL facility, PERLE at Orsay, is presented which uses the LHeC characteristics serving as a development facility for the design and operation of the LHeC. An updated detector design is presented corresponding to the acceptance, resolution and calibration goals which arise from the Higgs and parton density function physics programmes. The paper also presents novel results on the Future Circular Collider in electron-hadron mode, FCC-eh, which utilises the same ERL technology to further extend the reach of DIS to even higher centre-of-mass energies.

## Key claim

The LHeC, operating concurrently with the HL-LHC, can reduce PDF uncertainties in precision electroweak and Higgs measurements to sub-leading levels, enabling a factor of two or more improvement in electroweak precision tests and Higgs coupling determinations compared to HL-LHC stand-alone projections, provided the energy-recovery electron linac and interaction region are built as described.

## Method

The paper presents prospective studies using simulated pseudodata for 1 ab⁻¹ of electron-proton scattering at E_e = 60 GeV and E_p = 7 TeV, combined with projections for 3 ab⁻¹ of pp collisions at √s = 14 TeV. PDF uncertainties are evaluated by performing QCD fits to LHeC pseudodata and comparing the resulting PDF sets against existing sets (CT14, MMHT2014) and HL-LHC PDF projections in template fits for m_W and sin²θ_eff measurements. Higgs coupling fits in the κ-framework are performed using the HEPfit and GFitter programs, combining HL-LHC rate projections for production and decay channels with LHeC projections for Higgs production in ep collisions.

## Key numbers

- Total uncertainty on sin²θ_eff: 8 × 10⁻⁵ with LHeC PDFs versus 15 × 10⁻⁵ with HL-LHC PDFs and 36 × 10⁻⁵ for current ATLAS 8 TeV measurement (Table 1)
- PDF uncertainty on m_W: 1.6 MeV with LHeC PDFs and |η|<4 acceptance versus 5.8 MeV with HL-LHC PDFs and 12 MeV with CT14 (Table 2)
- Total uncertainty on m_W: 4.1 MeV with LHeC PDFs and |η|<4 acceptance versus 7.3 MeV with HL-LHC PDFs (Table 2)
- PDF+α_s uncertainty on gluon-fusion Higgs cross section: 0.4% with LHeC PDFs versus 3.1% with HL-LHC PDFs (Table 3)
- PDF+α_s uncertainty on t̅tH cross section: 0.4% with LHeC PDFs versus 3.5% with HL-LHC PDFs (Table 3)
- κ_W precision: 0.50% for HL-LHC+LHeC combined versus 1.7% for HL-LHC alone (Table 4)
- κ_b precision: 1.1% for HL-LHC+LHeC combined versus 3.6% for HL-LHC alone (Table 4)
- κ_c precision: 3.6% for HL-LHC+LHeC combined (not measurable at HL-LHC alone) (Table 4)

## Context

This paper updates the 2012 LHeC Conceptual Design Report (Klein et al., 2012) and extends the physics case for concurrent ep and pp operation at the HL-LHC. It demonstrates that LHeC PDFs resolve the ambiguity between new physics effects and PDF uncertainties that limits interpretation of LHC data alone, as previously noted by ATLAS (2018) and CMS (2018) in their weak mixing angle studies. The combined HL-LHC+LHeC Higgs coupling precision approaches that projected for ILC or CLIC (de Blas et al., 2019), while the energy-recovery linac technology is validated through the PERLE facility at Orsay. The paper also presents first results for the FCC-eh mode, extending DIS to even higher centre-of-mass energies.
