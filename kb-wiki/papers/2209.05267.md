# Enhanced dilepton emission from a phase transition in dense matter

> It is demonstrated that the presence of a phase transition in heavy ion collisions, at beam energies that probe dense QCD matter, leads to a significant enhancement of the dilepton yield per produced pion due to the extended emission time. In addition, the temperature of low mass dileptons shows a modest decrease due to the mixed phase. The emission of dileptons in the SIS18-SIS100 beam energies range is studied by augmenting the UrQMD transport model with a realistic density dependent equation of state, as well as two different phase transitions. This is achieved by extending the molecular dynamics interaction part of the UrQMD model to a density dependent interaction potential with a high density minimum leading to a phase transition and metastable coexisting high density states. Together with a high precision measurement these simulations will be able to constrain the existence of a phase transition in QCD up to densities of several times nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The presence of a first-order phase transition in dense QCD matter, as probed by heavy-ion collisions in the SIS18–SIS100 beam energy range, leads to a significant enhancement of the dilepton yield per produced pion (by a factor of 2–3 for the emission rate at low mass) and a modest decrease (about 5 MeV) in the temperature extracted from low-mass dileptons, but only when the created system reaches densities within the coexistence region of the transition.

## Method

The authors augmented the UrQMD hadronic transport model (v3.5) with a density-dependent interaction potential derived from the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) equation of state, then created two modified versions (PT1 and PT2) by cutting the CMF potential at baryon densities of 2.4 \(n_0\) and 3.0 \(n_0\) respectively, shifting it, and interpolating with a third-order polynomial to produce a second minimum representing a phase transition. They simulated 50,000 central Au-Au collisions (\(b < 2\) fm) at several beam energies, used a coarse-graining method on a space-time grid to extract local baryon density and temperature from the transport output, and calculated dilepton emission rates using the Rapp-Wambach-van-Hees in-medium spectral function with a pion fugacity correction.

## Key numbers

*   The CMF baseline nuclear incompressibility is \(K_0 = 267\) MeV.
*   The cut density for the PT1 phase transition is \(n_B^{cut} = 2.4\,n_0\); for PT2 it is \(3.0\,n_0\).
*   The shift in density for PT1 is \(\Delta n_B = 2.0\,n_0\); for PT2 it is \(3.0\,n_0\).
*   The coarse-graining grid uses cells of \(\Delta x, \Delta y, \Delta z, \Delta ct = 0.5\) fm, spanning \(-25 < x < 25\) fm in space and \(0 < t < 50\) fm/\(c\) in time.
*   The enhancement factor for the dilepton emission rate at mass \(M \approx 50\) MeV is 2–3 when the system enters the phase transition region.
*   The enhancement factor for the integrated dilepton yield in the low-mass region (0.3–0.7 GeV) is about 1.5.
*   The temperature reduction observed in the low-mass range (0.3–0.5 GeV) is about 5 MeV.
*   Beam energies studied range from \(E_{\mathrm{lab}} = 1.23\) to 10 \(A\)GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous fluid-dynamic simulations by Seck (2020) which predicted an increased dilepton emission up to a factor of 2 due to a phase transition, but here the effect is studied within a fully microscopic non-equilibrium transport model (UrQMD) that naturally includes finite viscosity and finite-size effects. The predicted enhancement factor of 2–3 for the emission rate and 1.5 for the integrated yield is sizeable enough to be measured by upcoming experiments at SIS100, and together with high-precision measurements these simulations can constrain the existence of a phase transition in QCD up to densities of several times nuclear saturation density.
