# Dimuonium $(μ^+μ^-)$ Production in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We study dimuonium $(μ^+μ^-)$ production in the quark-gluon plasma created in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The production is controlled by the process $q\bar q\rightarrow (μ^+μ^-)g$, and the dimuonium motion in the plasma is described by a transport equation. While the electrodynamics dominated dimuonium yield is not high enough, the transverse energy distribution carries the information of the plasma at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key claim

Dimuonium production in a quark-gluon plasma via the process \(q\bar q \rightarrow (\mu^+\mu^-)g\) yields a transverse energy distribution that inherits the thermodynamic behavior of the hot medium, but the overall electrodynamics-dominated yield is not high enough for easy detection; the distribution can nevertheless serve as an electromagnetic probe of the QGP at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Method

The authors calculated the tree-level scattering amplitude for \(q\bar q \rightarrow (\mu^+\mu^-)g\) with light quarks (N_f=2, zero current mass) and an effective strong coupling \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), then integrated over the relative momentum of the muon pair using the non-relativistic ground-state wave function of dimuonium. They coupled a classical Boltzmann-type transport equation for the dimuonium phase-space distribution to ideal hydrodynamic equations for the QGP evolution (with Bjorken hydrodynamics and a lattice-QCD equation of state, deconfinement temperature \(T_c = 190\) MeV), solving for central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV and Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV.

## Key numbers

- Dimuonium ground-state binding energy: 1.4 KeV; Bohr radius: 512 fm.
- Dimuonium lifetimes: \(^1S_0\) state decays to \(\gamma\gamma\) in 0.602 ps; \(^3S_1\) state decays to \(e^+e^-\) in 1.81 ps (both much shorter than the muon lifetime of 2.197 \(\mu\)s).
- Dimuonium mass used in calculations: \(m_d \simeq 2 m_\mu = 211\) MeV (binding energy neglected).
- Effective strong coupling: \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) (corresponding to \(T/T_c \simeq 1.5-2\)).
- Deconfinement phase transition temperature: \(T_c = 190\) MeV.
- For central collisions, dimuonium number: \(1.3 \times 10^{-9}\) at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV); about 3 times larger at LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.5\) TeV).
- Low transverse energy region (\(E_t < 1\) GeV) thermal slope parameter: \(T_{eff} = 195\) MeV at RHIC; \(T_{eff} = 240\) MeV at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the study of dimuonium production mechanisms beyond purely electromagnetic channels (e.g., Ginzburg et al. via photon fusion in heavy ion collisions) to include production inside a quark-gluon plasma via the QCD process \(q\bar q \rightarrow (\mu^+\mu^-)g\). It enables the use of dimuonium transverse energy distributions as a new electromagnetic probe of the QGP, complementing existing probes like direct photons and dileptons, and suggests that future experimental searches at RHIC and LHC could test the thermodynamic properties of the deconfined medium.
