# Model of pp and AA collisions for the description of long-range correlations

> Soft processes in pp and AA interactions are considered in the framework of phenomenological model with color strings formation and fusion. Elementary parton collisions are realized in the model as the interaction of two colour dipoles from projectile and target nucleons. Modeling of the exclusive distributions of parton momentum fractions and transverse coordinates is performed. The interaction of colour strings in transverse plane is carried out in the framework of local string fusion model with the introduction of the lattice in the impact parameter plane and taking into account the finite rapidity length of strings. The parameters were fixed with experimental data on pp total inelastic cross section and charged multiplicity. The model was used for the calculation of long-range correlations between the multiplicities (n) and the mean transverse momenta (pt) of charged particles. The dependence of n-n, pt-n, pt-pt correlations on the width and position of the backward and forward rapidity windows was studied. Note that the model enables to describe the AA interactions without referring to the Glauber picture based on the concept of elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions. In this connection the charged multiplicity, the mean numbers of participant nucleons and binary collisions and their variances in the case of PbPb collisions were calculated and compared with the predictions of alternative models and the experimental data. The influence of different ways of centrality determination on the multiplicity fluctuations and long-range correlations was also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a Monte Carlo model of soft proton-proton (pp) and nucleus-nucleus (AA) collisions based on color string formation and fusion that can describe AA interactions without referring to the Glauber picture of elementary nucleon-nucleon collisions, but the model's parameters are fixed using pp data and the description of AA observables is achieved with no additional parameter tuning.

## Method

The model treats elementary parton collisions as interactions between color dipoles from projectile and target nucleons, using eikonal approximation with confinement effects. Exclusive distributions of parton momentum fractions and transverse coordinates are modeled, and strings are formed between interacting partons. The interaction of color strings in the transverse plane is handled by a local string fusion model with a lattice in the impact parameter plane, accounting for finite rapidity length of strings. For nucleus-nucleus collisions, nucleons are arranged according to a Woods-Saxon distribution and treated as sets of dipoles, with no additional parameter turning beyond the pp case.

## Key numbers

- For pp collisions at 7 TeV, the n-n correlation coefficient as a function of forward window width follows the fit b = beta * Delta_y_F / (Delta_y_F + k) with beta = 1.000 +/- 0.002 and k = 0.34 +/- 0.01
- For pp collisions at 7 TeV, the pt-n correlation coefficient fit gives beta = 0.088 +/- 0.002 and k = 0.41 +/- 0.02
- For pp collisions at 7 TeV, the pt-pt correlation coefficient fit gives beta = 0.53 +/- 0.05 and k = 4.6 +/- 0.6
- Three parameter sets are used: r_str = 0.2 fm with alpha_S = 0.7 and mu_0 = 0.92; r_str = 0.3 fm with alpha_S = 0.5 and mu_0 = 1.02; r_str = 0.4 fm with alpha_S = 0.5 and mu_0 = 1.12
- The parameter gamma for transverse momentum variance is set to 0.61, independent of energy
- For PbPb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, the number of binary collisions in the model is almost twice less than in the Glauber model at sigma_NN = 34 mb

## Context

This work extends the string fusion model approach of Braun (2000) and Vechernin (various years) by implementing a detailed Monte Carlo simulation that accounts for finite rapidity length of strings and exclusive parton distributions. The model enables calculation of long-range correlations (n-n, pt-n, pt-pt) in both pp and AA collisions without relying on the Glauber picture used in models like HIJING (Wang, 1991) or the modified Glauber model of Feofilov and Ivanov. The results can be compared with experimental data from ALICE (Aamodt, 2010) and CMS (Khachatryan, 2010) collaborations, and the dependence of correlation coefficients on rapidity window widths provides predictions testable in future experiments.
