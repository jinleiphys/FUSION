# Diagrammatic calculation of thermodynamical quantities in nuclear matter

> In medium T-matrix calculations for symmetric nuclear matter at zero and finite temperatures are presented. The internal energy is calculated from the Galitskii-Koltun's sum rule and from the summation of the diagrams for the interaction energy. The pressure at finite temperature is obtained from the generating functional form of the thermodynamic potential. The entropy at high temperature is estimated and compared to expressions corresponding to a quasiparticle gas.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a thermodynamically consistent in-medium T-matrix approximation for symmetric nuclear matter can yield the internal energy, pressure, and entropy at finite temperatures, but only when using a diagrammatic summation for the interaction energy rather than relying solely on the Galitskii-Koltun sum rule, which shows a dependence on the energy cutoff and differs by about 0.9 MeV at saturation density.

## Method

The authors perform self-consistent in-medium T-matrix calculations for symmetric nuclear matter using the CD-Bonn two-body potential, iterating between the T-matrix, the self-energy, and the Dyson equation to obtain dressed single-particle propagators with broad spectral functions. The internal energy is computed both from the Galitskii-Koltun sum rule and from a direct diagrammatic summation of the kinetic and potential energy expectation values. The pressure is obtained from the generating functional form of the thermodynamic potential by integrating over an artificial coupling constant λ that multiplies the interaction lines while keeping the propagators fixed at full strength, and the entropy is then extracted via the thermodynamic relation TS = E + PV − μN.

## Key numbers

- Internal energy per particle at zero temperature and saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³): −15.80 MeV from Galitskii-Koltun sum rule, −16.63 MeV from diagram summation; difference of about 0.9 MeV.
- Pressure per density at zero temperature and ρ₀: −7.69 MeV (total), compared to −5.58 MeV from Baldo and Ferreira using Argonne v₁₄ interaction.
- Pressure per density at T = 20 MeV and ρ₀: 6.49 MeV (total), compared to 4.74 MeV from Baldo and Ferreira.
- Entropy per baryon at T = 10 MeV: 1.04 (from Galitskii-Koltun energy) and 0.98 (from diagram summation energy), compared to 1.22 for a free Fermi gas and 1.07 for a free Fermi gas with in-medium effective mass m* = 802 MeV.
- Entropy per baryon at T = 20 MeV: 1.76 from both energy methods, compared to 2.02 for free Fermi gas and 1.74 for free Fermi gas with effective mass m* = 745 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamically consistent in-medium T-matrix approach previously applied at zero temperature by Bozek (2001, 2002) to finite temperatures, where no estimates for pressure or entropy with short-range correlations were available. It contrasts with the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations at finite temperature by Baldo and Ferreira (using Argonne v₁₄) and with the Bloch-De Dominicis approach, finding qualitatively similar negative pressure at zero temperature and ρ₀. The key finding, that the entropy of a free Fermi gas with in-medium effective mass closely matches the full calculation, simplifies modeling of protoneutron star evolution as noted by Prakash (1996).
