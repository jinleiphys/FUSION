# Consistent high-energy constraints in the anomalous QCD sector

> The anomalous <VVP> Green function and related form-factors (pi^0 to gamma^* gamma^* and tau^- to X^- nu_tau vector form-factors, with X^-=(KKpi)^-, phi^- gamma, (phi V)^-) are analyzed in this letter in the large-N_C limit. Within the single (vector and pseudoscalar) resonance approximation and the context of Resonance Chiral Theory, we show that all these observables over-determine in a consistent way a unique set of compatible high-energy constraints for the resonance couplings. This result is in agreement with analogous relations found in the even intrinsic-parity sector of QCD like, e.g., F_V^2 = 3 F^2. The antisymmetric tensor formalism is considered for the spin-one resonance fields. Finally, we have also worked out and provide here the relation between the two bases of odd intrinsic-parity Lagrangian operators commonly employed in the literature.

## Key claim

Within the single (vector and pseudoscalar) resonance approximation of Resonance Chiral Theory, all anomalous QCD observables studied (the ⟨VVP⟩ Green function and the vector form-factors for τ⁻ → (KKπ)⁻ ν_τ, τ⁻ → φ⁻ γ ν_τ, τ⁻ → (φ V)⁻ ν_τ, and π⁰ → γ*γ*) over-determine a unique set of compatible high-energy constraints for the resonance couplings, provided the lightest pseudoscalar resonance multiplet is included in the Lagrangian.

## Method

The authors analyzed the odd intrinsic-parity sector of Resonance Chiral Theory (RχT) in the large-N_C limit, using the antisymmetric tensor formalism for spin-one resonance fields. They derived high-energy constraints from the Operator Product Expansion (OPE) for the ⟨VVP⟩ Green function and from the requirement that the vector form-factors for τ⁻ → (KKπ)⁻ ν_τ, τ⁻ → φ⁻ γ ν_τ, τ⁻ → (φ V)⁻ ν_τ, and the π⁰ → γ*γ* transition form-factor vanish at large momentum transfer (Brodsky-Lepage counting rules). The constraints from the two different operator bases (Ruiz-Femenia (2003) and Kampf (2011)) were compared and shown to be equivalent after including the pseudoscalar resonance contributions.

## Key numbers

*   The consistent set of constraints includes the relation \(F_V^2 = 3 F^2\), which was also independently derived in the even intrinsic-parity sector.
*   The constraint from the ⟨VVP⟩ Green function yields \(c_5 - c_6 = \frac{N_C M_V}{64 \sqrt{2} \pi^2 F_V}\).
*   The constraint from the ⟨VVP⟩ Green function yields \(d_3 = -\frac{N_C}{64\pi^2} \frac{M_V^2}{F_V^2}\).
*   The compatibility between the τ⁻ → (KKπ)⁻ ν_τ and ⟨VVP⟩ constraints requires \(1 + \frac{32 \sqrt{2} F_V d_m \kappa_3^{PV}}{F^2} = 0\).
*   The fitted value of \(F_V\) from τ⁻ → (πππ)⁻ ν_τ decays is consistent with the prediction \(F_V^2 = 3 F^2\) at one sigma, with the quoted error slightly below 5%.

## Context

This work resolves inconsistencies between the high-energy constraints derived from the ⟨VVP⟩ Green function by Ruiz-Femenia (2003) and those from τ⁻ → (KKπ)⁻ ν_τ decays by Dumm (2009), which had yielded conflicting predictions for the π⁰ → γγ* transition form-factor. The resolution required including the lightest pseudoscalar resonance multiplet, as done in the Lagrangian basis of Kampf (2011), which was absent in the earlier treatment by Ruiz-Femenia (2003). The resulting consistent set of relations is analogous to the consistent high-energy constraints found in the even intrinsic-parity sector by Cirigliano (2006), and it enables a unified description of multiple anomalous QCD processes within the single-resonance approximation.
