# Gravitational form factors of nuclei in the Skyrme model

> We compute the gravitational form factor $D(t)$ of various nuclei in the generalized Skyrme model where nuclei are described as solitonic field configurations each with a definite baryon number $B$. We separately discuss the cases $B=1$ (nucleons), $B=2$ (deuteron), $B=3$ (helium-3 and tritium) and extrapolate to larger $B$-values. Configurations with $B>1$ are in general not spherically symmetric, and we demonstrate how group theory helps to extract the form factor. Numerical results are presented for the configurations with $B=1,2,3,4,5,6,7,8,32,108$. We find that the $B$-dependence is consistent with a power-law $D(0)\propto B^β$ with $β=1.7\sim 1.8$. Other gravitational form factors can be calculated in the same framework, and we show the result for the $J(t)$ form factor associated with angular momentum for the $B=3$ solution.

## Key claim

The paper computes the gravitational form factor \(D(t)\) for nuclei with baryon numbers up to \(B=108\) in the generalized Skyrme model, finding that the \(B\)-dependence follows a power-law \(D(0)\propto B^{\beta}\) with \(\beta=1.7\sim 1.8\); however, this result relies on neglecting \(\mathcal{O}(I^2,J^2)\) terms from semiclassical quantization and on spin-averaging or group-theoretic arguments to extract the monopole part of the form factor for non-spherical configurations.

## Method

The authors use static Skyrmion solutions obtained by minimizing the energy functional of the generalized Skyrme model (including quadratic, quartic, sextic, and pion-mass terms) via gradient flow. For each baryon number \(B\), the classical energy-momentum tensor is computed from the soliton field, and the \(D(t)\) form factor is extracted via a Fourier-transform formula involving the shear-force distribution, with the nuclear mass replacing the nucleon mass. For non-spherical configurations (\(B>1\)), group theory (tetrahedral, octahedral, and other discrete symmetry groups) is used to show that the relevant tensor integrals reduce to the same spherically-symmetric form after accounting for rotational collective coordinates and spin averaging.

## Key numbers

* \(D(0)\) for \(B=1\) (nucleons): computed numerically from the spherically-symmetric Skyrmion solution.
* \(D(0)\) for \(B=2\) (deuteron): computed from the toroidal \(B=2\) Skyrmion solution.
* \(D(0)\) for \(B=3\) (helium-3/tritium): computed from the tetrahedral \(B=3\) Skyrmion solution.
* \(D(0)\) for \(B=4,5,6,7,8,32,108\): computed from the corresponding Skyrmion solutions.
* Power-law fit: \(D(0)\propto B^{\beta}\) with \(\beta=1.7\sim 1.8\) over the range \(B=1\) to \(B=108\).
* \(J(t)\) form factor: shown for the \(B=3\) solution (angular momentum distribution).

## Context

This work extends previous Skyrme-model calculations of gravitational form factors, which were limited to the \(B=1\) sector (nucleons) by Cebulla et al. (2007), Perevalova et al. (2016), and Kim et al. (2020). It adapts the group-theoretic method used by Carson (1991) for electromagnetic form factors of \(B=3\) Skyrmions to the gravitational case. The results enable comparison with earlier nuclear D-term estimates from the liquid drop model (Polyakov 2002), the Walecka model (Guzey 2005), and nonrelativistic spectral functions (Liuti 2005), and provide predictions for future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider.
