# Ab Initio Structure of p-Shell Nuclei with Chiral Effective Field Theory and Daejeon16 Interactions

> We present No-Core Full Configuration results for the ground state energies of all particle-stable p-shell nuclei, as well as the excitation energies of more than 40 narrow states, excluding isobaric analog states. We used the chiral LENPIC nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interaction at N2LO with semi-local coordinate space regulators, and also the phenomenological Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon potential. With simple exponential extrapolations of the total energies of each state, binding energies and spectra are found to be in good agreement with experiment. Both interactions produce a trend towards some overbinding of nuclei at the upper end of the p-shell.

## Key claim
Both the chiral LENPIC nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interaction at N2LO and the phenomenological Daejeon16 nucleon-nucleon potential produce ground state and excitation energies for p-shell nuclei that agree well with experiment after exponential extrapolation, but both interactions show a trend toward overbinding at the upper end of the p-shell.

## Method
The authors performed No-Core Full Configuration (NCFC) calculations, which combine No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations in finite harmonic oscillator bases with exponential extrapolations to the infinite-basis limit. They used two internucleon interactions: the chiral LENPIC NN+3N interaction at N2LO with semi-local coordinate space regulators (SRG-evolved to alpha=0.08 fm^4) and the phenomenological Daejeon16 NN potential (derived from chiral N3LO with SRG evolution and unitary phase-equivalent transformations). For each nuclear state, total energies were computed at a sequence of Nmax values and extrapolated using a three-parameter exponential form, with uncertainties estimated from sensitivity to the highest Nmax and to the harmonic oscillator energy parameter.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energies and excitation energies for 74 states in 22 nuclei from A=4 to A=16 were computed, excluding isobaric analog states.
- For Daejeon16, Nmax values ranged from 8 (for 14N, 15N, 16O) up to 20 (for 4He); for LENPIC NN+3N, Nmax values ranged from 8 up to 14 (for 4He).
- Daejeon16 overbinds 12C by almost 1% and overbinds 16O by about 3.8 MeV.
- The LENPIC interaction used LEC values c_D=7.2 and c_E=-0.671 for the 3N interactions at N2LO with regulator R=1.0 fm.
- Excited states shown are narrow with width less than 300 keV, except for the 2+ and 4+ rotational excitations of 8Be.

## Context
This work extends prior NCFC studies with the JISP16 interaction (Maris 2013, Shirokov 2014) which reported results for 57 states from A=6 through A=16 and 120 states from A=3 through A=16 respectively, including isobaric analog states. The results confirm that Daejeon16 produces the correct parity-inverted ground state in 11Be and the correct 3+ ground state in 10B without explicit three-nucleon forces, challenging the conventional wisdom that 3N interactions are essential for these features (Navratil 2007, Jurgenson 2013). The LENPIC NN+3N interaction fails to reproduce the parity inversion in 11Be and reverses the ordering of the lowest two states in 12B, while Daejeon16 succeeds in both cases. These results enable detailed benchmarking of chiral interactions against phenomenological potentials and identify overbinding at the upper p-shell as a target for future interaction improvements.
