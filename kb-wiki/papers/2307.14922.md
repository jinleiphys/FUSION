# Single inclusive particle production at next-to-leading order in proton-nucleus collisions at forward rapidities: hybrid approach meets TMD factorization

> We revisit the calculation of the cross section for forward inclusive single hadron production in $pA$ collisions within the hybrid approach. We show that the proper framework to perform this calculation beyond leading order is not the collinear factorization, as has been assumed so far, but the TMD factorized framework. Within the TMD factorized approach we show that all the large transverse logarithms appearing in the fixed order calculation, are resummed into the evolution of the TMD PDFs and TMD FFs with factorization scale. The resulting expressions, when written in terms of TMDs evolved to the appropriate, physically well understood factorization scale, contain no additional large logarithms. The absence of any large logarithms in the resummed result should ensure positivity of the cross section and eradicate the persistent problem that have plagued the previous attempts at calculating this observable in the hybrid approach.

## Key claim
The authors show that the proper framework for calculating forward inclusive single hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions beyond leading order is not collinear factorization (as previously assumed) but transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization, and that resumming all large transverse logarithms into TMD parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions should ensure positivity of the cross section and eliminate the persistent negativity problem that plagued earlier hybrid-approach calculations.

## Method
The authors revisit the NLO calculation of the cross section for forward inclusive single hadron production in pA collisions within the hybrid approach, working in a frame where the target Wilson lines are evolved to a rapidity where no additional rapidity evolution is needed and the Ioffe time constraint limits soft gluon fluctuations in the projectile wave function. They demonstrate that all large transverse logarithms appearing in fixed-order calculations are resummed into the evolution of TMD PDFs and TMD FFs with the factorization scale, rather than using collinear subtraction as in previous works. The analysis focuses on the q→q→H channel for simplicity, with other channels (gluon-initiated or gluon-producing) discussed separately, and the TMDs are defined perturbatively through their relation to collinear PDFs and FFs with a longitudinal cutoff regulated by the Ioffe time parameter.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale of the target is assumed to satisfy Q_s^2 ≫ Λ_QCD^2, with Q_s at most 1–2 GeV at RHIC and LHC.
- The hadron is produced with large transverse momentum p_T^2 ≫ Λ_QCD^2, and the phenomenologically relevant regime is p_T^2 ≫ Q_s^2.
- The physically relevant choice for the factorization scales is μ_T^2 = μ_F^2 = μ^2 = max{Q_s^2, (p_⟂/ζ)^2}, where ζ is the momentum fraction from parton to hadron.
- The Ioffe time cutoff parameter s_0 is related to the projectile momentum via P^+/τ = s_0/2, and the longitudinal cutoff ξ_0(l) = l^2/(x s_0) depends on the gluon virtuality l.

## Context
This work extends the hybrid approach originally formulated at LO by Dumitru (2005) and the full NLO calculation by Chirilli (2011, 2012), which suffered from negative cross sections at large transverse momentum in numerical implementations by Stasto (2013, 2014) and Zaslavsky (2014). It builds on the Ioffe-time restriction approach of Altinoluk (2014) and the kinematic considerations of Watanabe (2015), which alleviated but did not solve the negativity problem. The paper also relates to attempts using SCET by Liu (2019) and threshold/Sudakov resummations by Liu (2020), Xiao (2018), and Shi (2021). The TMD factorized framework presented here enables a consistent resummation that should yield positive physical cross sections and provides a foundation for future phenomenological studies of forward particle production at RHIC and LHC.
