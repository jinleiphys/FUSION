# Superfluid density in linear response theory : pulsar glitches from the inner crust of neutron stars

> The question of whether there are enough superfluid neutrons in the inner crust of neutron stars to explain pulsar glitches remains a topic of debate. Previous band structure calculations suggest that the entrainment effect significantly reduces the superfluid density. In this letter, a new derivation of the BCS expression for the superfluid density is given. We compute it in the superfluid band theory framework through linear response theory, for a small relative velocity between superfluid and normal components, under the assumption that the pairing gap in the rest frame of the superfluid is constant and not affected by the perturbation. Our result suggests that a formula extensively used in neutron star physics is incomplete. Numerical evaluations for two realistic configurations reveal that the previously neglected contribution drastically alters the picture of the superfluid reservoir in the inner crust of neutron stars, suggesting that about 90% of the neutrons are effectively superfluid.

# Superfluid density in linear response theory: pulsar glitches from the inner crust of neutron stars

## Key claim

The paper derives a new BCS expression for the superfluid density in the inner crust of neutron stars, showing that a previously neglected off-diagonal contribution in the linear response of the density matrix drastically increases the superfluid fraction, suggesting that about 90% of neutrons are effectively superfluid under the assumption that the pairing gap in the rest frame of the superfluid is constant and unaffected by the perturbation.

## Method

The authors compute the superfluid density through linear response theory within the superfluid band theory framework, treating the term -v·p (where v is the normal fluid velocity) as a perturbation to the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) matrix. They solve the Hartree-Fock equations with Bloch boundary conditions using discrete Fourier transform to the momentum basis with 22^3 points in the cubic cell and 24^3 points in the first Brillouin zone, then apply the BCS approximation with a constant pairing gap to evaluate the diagonal and off-diagonal contributions to the superfluid density tensor.

## Key numbers

- At baryon density ρ_b = 0.030 fm^{-3} (lattice spacing L = 34.75 fm), adding the off-diagonal contribution results in a two times larger superfluid density at Δ ≈ 0.01 MeV and more than ten times larger at Δ ≈ 1 MeV compared to the diagonal-only result
- At baryon density ρ_b = 0.055 fm^{-3} (lattice spacing L = 27.99 fm), similar qualitative behavior is observed
- The superfluid fraction reaches about 90% of the average neutron density at realistic pairing gap values
- Fully self-consistent HFB calculations with coupling constants g/g_phys = 0.6 and 0.7 confirm the trend

## Context

This work extends previous band structure calculations by Carter, Chamel, and Haensel (2005, 2006) and Chamel (2012) that found strong entrainment effects drastically reducing superfluid density, and by Chamel (2025) who concluded pairing had no significant effect. It resolves contradictions with HFB calculations by Almirante and Urban (2024) in pasta phases and with superfluid hydrodynamics results by Martin and Urban (2016) that predicted superfluid fractions above 90%. The result enables explaining Vela pulsar glitches with crust superfluidity alone, without invoking core superfluidity as suggested by Andersson (2012), and has implications for phonon properties, thermal evolution, and oscillation modes of neutron stars.
