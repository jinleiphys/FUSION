# Optical Potential Approach to $K^{+}d$ Scattering at Low Energies

> We study the $K^{+}d$ scattering at low energies using the optical potential. Our optical potential consists of the first-order and second-order terms. The total, integrated elastic and elastic differential cross sections at incident kaon momenta below 800 MeV/c are calculated using our optical potential. We found that our results are consistent with the Faddeev calculation as well as the data and especially the second-order optical potential is essential to reproduce them at low energies. We also discuss the multiple scattering effects in this process.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an optical potential including both first-order and second-order terms can successfully describe K+d scattering data at low incident kaon momenta (below 800 MeV/c), and that the second-order term is essential for reproducing both the data and the Faddeev calculation results at low energies, despite the weak K+N interaction.

## Method

The author constructed an optical potential for K+d scattering using the Watson multiple scattering theory, consisting of first-order and second-order terms. The second-order optical potential included double scattering, nucleon-nucleon (N-N) interaction, and coherent rescattering corrections. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved in momentum space with partial wave decomposition, using separable forms for the kaon-nucleon T matrix (with phase shifts from Martin or Hyslop et al.) and the nucleon-nucleon T matrix (via the Ernst-Shakin-Thaler method with the Paris potential). Only S-wave contributions were included for the second-order potential, and the calculations incorporated full three-body kinematics without factorization.

## Key numbers

* Total and integrated elastic cross sections were calculated for incident kaon momenta up to 800 MeV/c.
* The second-order potential effect was most important at lower energies, especially near threshold; for the total cross section it decreased the magnitude slightly at Plab above approximately 150 MeV/c but increased it at Plab below approximately 150 MeV/c.
* The second-order potential effect was small at Plab above approximately 500 MeV/c for both total and elastic cross sections.
* The single scattering impulse approximation explicitly violated unitarity at low momenta where the elastic cross section became larger than the total cross section.
* The coherent rescattering effect had significant contribution for both total and elastic cross sections at momenta below approximately 500 MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the optical potential approach to K+d scattering by including second-order terms for the first time, building on the Watson multiple scattering theory (Watson) and the Kerman-McManus-Thaler (KMT) theory (Kerman, McManus, and Thaler). The results are compared with Faddeev calculations (Garcilazo) and experimental data from multiple sources. The approach enables a simpler calculation than the Faddeev method while maintaining unitarity, and is relevant for studying the possible pentaquark resonance Theta(1540) in the K+N channel at incident momenta near 450 MeV/c.
