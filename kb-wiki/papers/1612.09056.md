# $α$-decay properties of $^{296}118$ from double-folding potentials

> $α$-decay properties of the yet unknown nucleus $^{296}$118 are predicted using the systematic behavior of parameters of $α$-nucleus double-folding potentials. The results are $Q_α= 11.655 \pm 0.095$ MeV and $T_{1/2} = 0.825$ ms with an uncertainty of about a factor of 4.

## Key claim
The alpha-decay energy Q_alpha of the unknown nucleus 296118 is predicted to be 11.655 ± 0.095 MeV and its half-life 0.825 ms with an uncertainty of about a factor of 4, based on extrapolating the smooth systematic behavior of double-folding potential parameters rather than using mass models.

## Method
The author calculated the alpha-nucleus interaction using a double-folding procedure with an effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, where the daughter nucleus density was taken from a 2-parameter Fermi distribution with radius and diffuseness from average values of 232Th and 238U. The strength parameter lambda of the double-folding potential was extrapolated from neighboring nuclei in two known alpha-decay chains (chain-1 and chain-2), then the eigenstate energy was calculated from the resulting potential. The half-life was computed from barrier transmission using the semi-classical formalism of Gurvitz and Kalbermann (1987), and divided by an average preformation factor P = 0.0885 from the known decays in both chains.

## Key numbers
- Q_alpha = 11.655 ± 0.095 MeV for 296118, derived from extrapolated strength parameter lambda = 1.1458 ± 0.0010
- Predicted half-life T_1/2 = 0.825 ms for 296118, with an uncertainty factor of about 4 (range 0.2 ms to 3.3 ms)
- Calculated barrier transmission half-life T_1/2^calc = 73.0 μs for 296118
- Preformation factor P_av = 0.0885, averaged from the 5 known decays in chain-1 and chain-2, with individual values ranging from 0.0424 (for 286Fl in chain-1) to 0.193 (for 290Lv in chain-2)
- For comparison, the three selected mass models from Sobiczewski (2016) gave Q_alpha values of 11.62 MeV (WS3+), 11.73 MeV (WS4+), and 12.06 MeV (HN), with corresponding half-lives of 4.8 ms, 2.7 ms, and 0.50 ms

## Context
This work extends the systematic double-folding potential approach of Mohr (2006) to predict properties of the yet-unsynthesized nucleus 296118, using the smooth behavior of volume integrals and strength parameters from two known alpha-decay chains. The results are independent of mass models but agree well with the selected mass formulae of Sobiczewski (2016) (WS3+, WS4+, HN), and are consistent with recent calculations by Budaca et al. (2016) and Shin et al. (2016). The predictions provide guidance for ongoing experiments attempting to synthesize this heaviest nucleus to date, and can be tested when experimental data become available.
