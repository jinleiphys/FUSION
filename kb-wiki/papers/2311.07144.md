# Nuclear physics inputs for dense-matter modelling in neutron stars. The nuclear equation of state

> In this contribution, we briefly present the equation-of-state modelling for application to neutron stars and discuss current constraints coming from nuclear physics theory and experiments. To assess the impact of model uncertainties, we employ a nucleonic meta-modelling approach and perform a Bayesian analysis to generate posterior distributions for the equation of state with filters accounting for both our present low-density nuclear physics knowledge and high-density neutron-star physics constraints. The global structure of neutron stars thus predicted is discussed in connection with recent astrophysical observations.

## Key claim
The paper argues that a nucleonic meta-modelling approach, combined with Bayesian inference using both low-density nuclear physics constraints and high-density neutron-star physics filters, can generate posterior distributions for the equation of state that are compatible with current astrophysical observations, but only under the assumption that no non-nucleonic degrees of freedom appear in the neutron-star core.

## Method
The authors employ a nucleonic meta-modelling approach using a Taylor expansion of the energy functional around saturation density up to order N=4, with 13 bulk parameters and 5 surface parameters. They perform a Bayesian analysis with flat non-informative priors, applying low-density filters from chiral effective field theory calculations of pure neutron matter (interpreted as a 90% confidence interval) and high-density filters imposing stability, causality, positive symmetry energy, and a maximum neutron-star mass above 1.97 solar masses. The surface parameters are determined from a chi-squared fit to experimental masses from the Atomic Mass Evaluation.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n_sat approximately 0.16 fm^-3
- Low-density filter applied over density range n in [0.02-0.2] fm^-3, based on chiral EFT calculations by Drischler (2016)
- High-density filter requires M_max > 1.97 M_sun (solar masses)
- Taylor expansion performed up to order N=4 around saturation point
- The posterior shows 50% and 90% confidence intervals for the equation of state, with tight constraints at low density and greater spread at high density
- The mass-radius posterior shows 1-sigma, 2-sigma, and 3-sigma confidence intervals compatible with NICER observations of NSs J0740+6620 and J0030+0451, and with GW170817 constraints at 2-sigma

## Context
This work extends the meta-modelling approach previously developed by Margueron (2018) and applied to crust properties by Carreau (2019, 2020a). It builds on constraints from chiral effective field theory calculations (Drischler 2016, 2020; Hebeler 2013; Gandolfi 2012; Lynn 2016) and experimental constraints on the symmetry energy parameters J and L from various measurements including heavy-ion collisions (Tsang 2009), neutron skin thickness (Chen 2010), electric dipole polarizability (RocaMaza 2015), and isobaric analog states (Daniel 2014). The results enable comparison of unified equation-of-state models (BSk24, SLy4, BCPM) against astrophysical observations, and can serve as a null hypothesis to infer the presence of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom from future data.
