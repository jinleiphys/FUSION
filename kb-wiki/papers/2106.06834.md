# The Angular Correlations in the $e^+e^-$ Decay of Excited States in 8Be

> Motivated by the recent observation of anomalous electron-positron angular correlations in the decay of the 18.15 MeV 1+ excited states in 8Be, we reexamine in detail the Standard Model expectations for these angular correlations. The 18.15 MeV state is above particle threshold, and several multipoles can contribute to its $e^+e^-$ decay. We present the general theoretical expressions for $e^+e^-$ angular distributions for nuclear decay by C0, C1, C2 M1, E1, and E2 multipoles, and we examine their relative contribution to the $e^+e^-$ decay of 8Be at 18.15 MeV. We find that this resonance is dominated by M1 and E1 decay, and that the ratio of M1 to E1 strength is a strong function of energy. This is in contract to the original analysis of the $e^+e^-$ angular distributions, where the M1/E1 ratio was assumed to be a constant over the energy region Ep = 0:8-1:2 MeV. We find that the existence of a `bump' in the measured angular distribution is strongly dependent on the assumed M1/E1 ratio, with the present analysis finding the measured large-angle contributions to the $e^+e^-$ angular distribution to be lower than expectation. Thus, in the current analysis we find no evidence for axion decay in the 18.15 MeV resonance region of 8Be.

## Key claim

The paper reexamines the Standard Model expectations for electron-positron angular correlations in the decay of the 18.15 MeV 1+ excited state in 8Be and finds that the previously reported anomalous "bump" in the angular distribution is strongly dependent on the assumed ratio of M1 to E1 multipole strength; when the correct energy-dependent M1/E1 ratio from an R-matrix analysis is used, the measured large-angle contributions fall below Standard Model expectations, yielding no evidence for axion decay.

## Method

The authors derived general theoretical expressions for e+e- angular distributions from nuclear decay by C0, C1, C2, M1, E1, and E2 multipoles, then performed an R-matrix analysis of existing 7Li(p,gamma)8Be cross section data from Zahnow et al. and Fisher et al., along with angular distribution measurements from Mainsbridge and Schlueter, to extract the energy-dependent M1 and E1 contributions to the 18.15 MeV resonance region. They used these extracted multipole ratios to compute the expected e+e- angular distributions by numerically integrating the decay rate formula over the kinematic variable y, and compared the results to the measured angular distributions from the anomaly experiment.

## Key numbers

- The M1 resonance at 18.15 MeV has a width of 138 +/- 6 keV, centered at Ep = 1.03 MeV
- At the peak of the resonance (Ep = 1.03 MeV), the cross section is approximately M1 + 0.45 E1
- Above Ep = 1.16 MeV, the E1 contribution starts to dominate over M1
- The minimum M1/E1 ratio in the proton energy range 0.8-1.2 MeV is 0.4
- The R-matrix fit yields R = 0.82 +/- 0.029 for the ratio of 90-degree to integrated cross section, while the experimental data give R_exp = 0.59 +/- 0.037 (below the theoretical minimum of 0.75)
- The original anomaly analysis assumed a constant M1 + 0.23 E1 over Ep = 0.8-1.2 MeV

## Context

This work directly challenges the interpretation of the anomaly reported by the ATOMKI collaboration (2016), which claimed evidence for a new axion-like particle from e+e- angular correlations in 8Be. The authors extend the formalism of Rose (1949) for e+e- decay rates and use R-matrix analysis of photon data from Zahnow et al. (1995), Fisher et al. (1986), Mainsbridge (1960), and Schlueter et al. (1965), consistent with the analysis of Barker (1995). The paper demonstrates that the claimed axion signal disappears when the correct energy-dependent nuclear structure is accounted for, and calls for a detailed re-measurement of the M1 and E1 yields in the (p,gamma) reaction before claiming evidence for non-Standard Model particles.
