# Improved quark coalescence model for spin alignment and polarization of hadrons

> We propose an improved quark coalescence model for spin alignment of vector mesons and polarization of baryons by spin density matrix with phase space dependence. The spin density matrix is defined through Wigner functions. Within the model we propose an understanding of spin alignments of vector mesons $ϕ$ and $K^{*0}$ (including $\bar{K}^{*0}$) in the static limit: a large positive deviation of $ρ_{00}$ for $ϕ$ mesons from 1/3 may come from the electric part of the vector $ϕ$ field, while a negative deviation of $ρ_{00}$ for $K^{*0}$ may come from the electric part of vorticity tensor fields. Such a negative contribution to $ρ_{00}$ for $K^{*0}$ mesons, in comparison with the same contribution to $ρ_{00}$ for $ϕ$ mesons which is less important, is amplified by a factor of the mass ratio of strange to light quark times the ratio of $\left\langle \mathbf{p}_{b}^{2}\right\rangle $ on the wave function of $K^{*0}$ to $ϕ$ ($\mathbf{p}_{b}$ is the relative momentum of two constituent quarks of $K^{*0}$ and $ϕ$). These results should be tested by a detailed and comprehensive simulation of vorticity tensor fields and vector meson fields in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the large positive deviation of the spin alignment parameter ρ₀₀ for φ mesons from 1/3 may originate from the electric part of the vector φ field, while a negative deviation of ρ₀₀ for K*⁰ mesons may come from the electric part of vorticity tensor fields, with the negative contribution to K*⁰ being amplified by a factor of the strange-to-light quark mass ratio times the ratio of average squared relative momentum in the K*⁰ wave function to that in the φ wave function.

## Method

The authors formulate an improved quark coalescence model using spin density matrices defined through Wigner functions in phase space, which allows space-time dependence of quark polarization to be treated systematically. They derive expressions for the spin density matrix elements of vector mesons (φ and K*⁰) and baryons (Λ) by projecting the quark-antiquark or three-quark spin density operator onto hadron states with Gaussian wave functions. The quark polarization is then related to vorticity tensor fields and vector meson fields, and the model is applied to compute ρ₀₀ for vector mesons and polarization for Λ hyperons in the static limit.

## Key numbers

- For φ mesons, ρ₀₀ ≈ 1/3 − (4/9)⟨Pₛ(x₁,p₁)P_{\bar{s}}(x₂,p₂)⟩_φ, where the average is taken over the meson wave packet with Gaussian width parameter a_M
- For K*⁰ mesons, ρ₀₀ ≈ 1/3 − (4/9)⟨P_d(x₁,p₁)P_{\bar{s}}(x₂,p₂)⟩_{K*}
- For Λ hyperons, the polarization P_Λ(x,p) ≈ (1/3)⟨Pₛ(x₁,p₁) + Pₛ(x₂,p₂) + Pₛ(x₃,p₃)⟩_Λ, where the average is taken over the baryon wave packet with width parameters a_{Λ1} and a_{Λ2}
- The Gaussian meson wave function is given as φ_M(k) = (2√π/a_M)^{3/2} exp(−k²/(2a_M²))

## Context

This work extends the quark coalescence model of Yang (2017) by introducing phase-space-dependent spin density operators via Wigner functions, enabling a rigorous treatment of space-time-dependent quark polarization. The model provides a framework to explain the significant positive deviation of ρ₀₀ for φ mesons observed in preliminary STAR data at lower energies (Zhou, 2017), which was beyond conventional understanding based on the earlier coalescence model of Sheng (2019). The results are intended to be tested by detailed simulations of vorticity tensor fields and vector meson fields in heavy ion collisions, and the improved model can be implemented in real-time simulations to provide insights into vector meson spin alignments.
