# Complete Sets of Polarization Observables in Electromagnetic Deuteron Break-up

> For deuteron photo- and electrodisintegration the selection of complete sets of polarization observables is discussed in detail by applying a recently developed new criterion for the check of completeness of a chosen set of observables. The question of ambiguities and their resolution by considering additional observables is discussed for a numerical example, for which the role of experimental uncertainties is also investigated. Furthermore, by inversion of the expressions of the observables as hermitean forms in the $t$-matrix elements a bilinear term of the form $t_{j'}^*t_j$ can be given as a complex linear form in the observables from which an explicit solution for $t_j$ in terms of observables can be obtained. These can also be used to select sets of observables for the explicit representation of the $t$-matrix.

# Complete Sets of Polarization Observables in Electromagnetic Deuteron Break-up

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that complete sets of polarization observables for deuteron photo- and electrodisintegration can be selected using a recently developed criterion, but the reconstruction of the t-matrix from a minimal set of 2n-1 observables generally contains discrete ambiguities that require additional "check observables" to resolve, and experimental uncertainties of 10% in the observables lead to large uncertainties (average 44%) in the extracted t-matrix elements.

## Method

The authors apply a general completeness criterion based on constructing real quadratic forms from the hermitean forms representing each observable, then checking whether the resulting matrices have nonvanishing determinants for at least one combination of column indices. For the transverse observables (12 independent t-matrix elements in photodisintegration, 18 in electrodisintegration), they derive explicit groups of possible complete sets and test them numerically using a Monte Carlo simulation with randomly assigned errors of 10% on each observable, solving the system of nonlinear equations and comparing check observables to their "measured" values.

## Key numbers

- Number of independent t-matrix elements: 12 for photodisintegration, 18 for electrodisintegration
- For a complete set, at least 2n-1 observables are needed: 23 for transverse (photodisintegration) and 35 for electrodisintegration
- In the Monte Carlo simulation with 10% statistical error on each of 11 observables, about 1500 accepted solutions were obtained out of 100,000 trials
- The standard deviations of the extracted t-matrix elements from their mean values averaged 44%
- The simulation used kinematics with internal excitation energy E_np = 100 MeV and momentum transfer q^2_cm = 5 fm^{-2} at np angle theta_np = 50 degrees

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous completeness criterion (Arenhoevel, Leidemann, Tomusiak, 1998) from longitudinal observables of deuteron electrodisintegration to all polarization observables including transverse and mixed longitudinal-transverse types. It builds on the general formalism for polarization observables developed by the same authors (Arenhoevel 1988, Arenhoevel and Schwamb 1990, Arenhoevel, Leidemann, Tomusiak 1993) and provides a framework for experimental determination of the full t-matrix, enabling detailed comparison with theoretical models of the NN system. The paper also relates its formalism to the hybrid basis approach of Dmitrasinovic and Gross (1989).
