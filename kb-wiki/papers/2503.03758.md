# Classical periodic orbits in extended phase space for spherical harmonic oscillator with spin-orbit coupling

> A complete analysis of classical periodic orbits (POs) and their bifurcations was conducted in spherical harmonic oscillator system with spin-orbit coupling. The motion of the spin is explicitly considered using the spin canonical variables derived by semiclassical approximation to the spin coherent state path integral representation. In addition to the diametric and two circular PO families with frozen spin, solutions that bridge two circular POs are found in which orbital motion is coupled to spin precession. In addition, each bridge encounters a secondary bifurcation on the way from one circular PO to the other and generates a new PO, that survives at higher energies while maintaining a constant period. The generic expressions for those POs are obtained explicitly, and all the above peculiar bifurcation scenarios are described fully analytically.

# Classical periodic orbits in extended phase space for spherical harmonic oscillator with spin-orbit coupling

## Key claim

The paper claims to provide a complete analytical classification of all classical periodic orbits and their bifurcations in a spherical harmonic oscillator with spin-orbit coupling, but this completeness holds only for the integrable spherical case; the analysis explicitly excludes the anisotropic case where chaotic dynamics emerge, and the applicability of the results to semiclassical trace formulas for nuclear systems remains an open question due to unresolved inconsistencies in the semiclassical order of periodic orbit contributions.

## Method

The author introduces classical spin canonical variables via a semiclassical approximation to the spin coherent state path integral representation, then solves the coupled nonlinear equations of motion for orbital and spin degrees of freedom analytically. A time-dependent transformation of variables converts the system into time-independent homogeneous equations, whose eigenvalues yield the three normal mode frequencies ω, ω+κl, and ω-κl. Periodic orbits are constructed by imposing commensurability conditions among the spin precession frequency ω_p=κj and the orbital frequencies, with bifurcation points determined analytically from the energy-dependent relations between these frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit coupling strength κ is expressed as κ = κ̅ω/s, where κ̅ is a dimensionless parameter and s is the spin quantum number (in units of ℏ)
- Diametric periodic orbits have period T = 2π/ω
- Circular periodic orbits C₊ and C₋ have angular frequencies ω₊ = ω - κs and ω₋ = ω + κs, respectively
- Circular orbit radii are r± = √(E/(Mωω±))
- Bifurcation energies for circular orbits are given by E±_{k,m} = ω±(m/k · ω± - κs_z)/κ, where s_z = ±s
- Bridge orbit periods satisfy T = 2πm/ω_p = 2πn/(ω+κl) = 2π(n±m)/(ω+κl±ω_p)
- Secondary bifurcation energy is E_{bif}^{(n,m,k)} = (n-k)/k · ω²/κ + ½κs²
- For nuclear applications, the dimensionless spin-orbit parameter κ̅ ≈ 0.06

## Context

This work extends the numerical studies of Rozmej and Arview (1992), who investigated classical motions in the same Hamiltonian using Poincaré plots for two-dimensional orbits in angular-momentum projected space and found integrability in the spherical case. The paper also addresses difficulties raised by Amann et al. (2002), who applied the WKB method to a similar Hamiltonian with anisotropy and found inconsistencies between classical periodic orbit contributions and the exact quantum spectrum in the spherical case. The results enable future application of the semiclassical trace formula in extended phase space, potentially resolving the mode conversion problem that plagued earlier WKB approaches, though the author notes that the semiclassical order of contributing periodic orbits remains problematic and may require uniform approximations or alternative resummation schemes.
