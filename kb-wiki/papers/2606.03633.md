# Mechanical properties of the nucleon in the chiral confining model. II -- in-medium evolution of the nucleon properties

> This article is devoted to the study of the evolution of the properties of nucleons bound in nuclear matter within the framework of the chiral confining model. The in-medium nucleon trial states (either localized factorized wave functions or momentum-projected states) are determined by imposing the von Laue stability condition, according to the formal results established in a preliminary companion paper (labeled as I). The main results concern the response of the composite nucleon to the scalar field, as well as the respective roles of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in the evolution of the in-medium nucleon mass. This evolution governs the repulsive three-body forces required for the nuclear saturation mechanism. We also analyze the modification of the energy density distribution and the pressure distribution inside the in-medium nucleon. We also draw some perspectives concerning the mapping between bound nucleon properties and the equation of state of dense matter as realized in the deep interior of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the mechanical stability of the in-medium nucleon, enforced via the von Laue condition, determines the nucleon's response to the nuclear scalar field and that the resulting scalar susceptibility parameter \(C\) and the enriched chiral potential parameter \(C_{\chi,\mathrm{NJL}}\) together provide the repulsive three-body forces necessary for nuclear saturation, with the specific claim that agreement with lattice QCD data requires a non-vanishing \(C_{\chi,\mathrm{NJL}}\) parameter.

## Method

The authors construct in-medium nucleon trial states (localized factorized wave functions and momentum-projected states) within the chiral confining model (CCM), which combines a confining potential derived from the field correlator method (FCM) with a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) based chiral effective potential and a pion cloud coupling. The nucleon size parameter \(b\) is fixed by imposing the von Laue stability condition on the energy-momentum tensor, and the resulting scalar coupling constant \(g_S\) and dimensionless scalar susceptibility \(C\) are computed from the evolution of the nucleon mass and internal pressure distributions with the scalar field \(\mathcal{S}\).

## Key numbers

* The string tension is \(\sigma = 0.18\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and the gluon correlation length is \(T_g = 0.283\ \mathrm{fm}\) (corresponding to gluon condensate \(\mathcal{G}_2 = 0.025\ \mathrm{GeV}^4\)).
* The vacuum constituent quark mass is \(M_0 = 356.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the vacuum pion mass is \(M_\pi^{\mathrm{vac}} = 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and the vacuum pion decay constant is \(F_\pi^{\mathrm{vac}} = 91.7\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The lattice QCD expansion parameters for the nucleon mass in terms of \(M_\pi^2\) are in the range \(a_2 \simeq 1.0\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) to \(1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) and \(a_4 \simeq -0.25\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3}\) to \(-0.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3}\); the simplest linear sigma model without nucleonic response gives \(a_4 \simeq -3.5\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-3}\).
* The dimensionless scalar susceptibility parameter \(C\) is expected to be of order \(C \sim 0.5\) as in the MIT bag model used in the QMC framework.
* The NJL parameter \(C_{\chi,\mathrm{NJL}}\) is in the range \(0.4\) to \(0.5\) for typical NJL parameters.
* The isoscalar radius of the nucleon is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2_S\rangle} \simeq 0.77\ \mathrm{fm}\), and the quark core baryon number radius is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2_B\rangle} = 0.46\ \mathrm{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral confining model (CCM) developed by Chanfray, Hansen, and collaborators over the 2000s, which itself is a chiral version of the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model pioneered by Guichon (1988). The paper builds on the formal stability results of the companion paper (Paper I) and on previous CCM studies of nuclear matter and neutron stars (Chanfray 2005, 2007; Massot 2008, 2009, 2012; Chanfray 2023, 2024). It enables a microscopic mapping between nucleon structure properties (internal pressure and energy density) and the equation of state of dense matter in neutron stars, and it provides a framework to connect lattice QCD data on the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass to nuclear saturation mechanisms.
