# Measurements of $d_{2}^{n}$ and $A_{1}^{n}$: Probing the neutron spin structure

> We report on the results of the E06-014 experiment performed at Jefferson Lab in Hall A, where a precision measurement of the twist-3 matrix element $d_2$ of the neutron ($d_{2}^{n}$) was conducted. This quantity represents the average color Lorentz force a struck quark experiences in a deep inelastic electron scattering event off a neutron due to its interaction with the hadronizing remnants. This color force was determined from a linear combination of the third moments of the spin structure functions $g_1$ and $g_2$ on $^{3}$He after nuclear corrections had been applied to these moments. The kinematics included two average $Q^{2}$ bins of $3.2$ GeV$^{2}$ and $4.3$ GeV$^{2}$, and Bjorken-$x$ $0.25 \leq x \leq 0.90$ covering the DIS and resonance regions. We found $d_2^n$ to be small and negative for $<Q^{2}> = 3.2$ GeV$^{2}$, and smaller for $<Q^{2}> = 4.3$ GeV$^{2}$, consistent with a lattice QCD calculation. The twist-4 matrix element $f_{2}^{n}$ was extracted by combining our $d_{2}^{n}$ with the world data on $Γ_{1}^{n} = \int_{0}^{1} g_{1}^{n} dx$. We found $f_{2}^{n}$ to be roughly an order of magnitude larger than $d_{2}^{n}$. Utilizing the extracted $d_{2}^{n}$ and $f_{2}^{n}$ data, we separated the color force into its electric and magnetic components, $F_{E}^{y,n}$ and $F_{B}^{y,n}$, and found them to be equal and opposite in magnitude, in agreement with instanton model predictions but not with those from QCD sum rules. Additionally, we have extracted the neutron virtual photon-nucleon asymmetry $A_{1}^{n}$, the structure function ratio $g_{1}^{n}/F_{1}^{n}$, and the quark ratios $(Δu + Δ\bar{u})/(u + \bar{u})$ and $(Δd + Δ\bar{d})/(d + \bar{d})$. These results were found to be consistent with DIS world data and with the prediction of the constituent quark model but at odds with those of perturbative QCD at large $x$.

## Key claim

The twist-3 matrix element \(d_{2}^{n}\) of the neutron was measured to be small and negative at \(\langle Q^{2}\rangle = 3.2\) GeV\(^{2}\), and even smaller at \(\langle Q^{2}\rangle = 4.3\) GeV\(^{2}\), consistent with lattice QCD calculations; the extracted color Lorentz force components \(F_{E}^{y,n}\) and \(F_{B}^{y,n}\) were found equal and opposite in magnitude, agreeing with instanton model predictions but not with QCD sum rules.

## Method

The experiment (E06-014) was performed at Jefferson Lab Hall A using a longitudinally polarized electron beam scattering from a transversely and longitudinally polarized \(^{3}\)He target. Unpolarized cross sections and double-spin asymmetries were measured to extract the spin structure functions \(g_{1}\) and \(g_{2}\) on \(^{3}\)He, which were then corrected for nuclear effects to obtain neutron structure functions. The third moments of \(g_{1}\) and \(g_{2}\) were combined to determine \(d_{2}^{n}\), and the twist-4 matrix element \(f_{2}^{n}\) was extracted by combining \(d_{2}^{n}\) with world data on \(\Gamma_{1}^{n} = \int_{0}^{1} g_{1}^{n} dx\).

## Key numbers

* \(d_{2}^{n}\) at \(\langle Q^{2}\rangle = 3.2\) GeV\(^{2}\) was found to be small and negative (explicit numerical value not provided in the truncated text).
* \(d_{2}^{n}\) at \(\langle Q^{2}\rangle = 4.3\) GeV\(^{2}\) was found to be smaller than at 3.2 GeV\(^{2}\) (explicit numerical value not provided in the truncated text).
* \(f_{2}^{n}\) was found to be roughly an order of magnitude larger than \(d_{2}^{n}\) (explicit numerical value not provided in the truncated text).
* The kinematics covered two average \(Q^{2}\) bins of 3.2 GeV\(^{2}\) and 4.3 GeV\(^{2}\), with Bjorken-\(x\) range \(0.25 \leq x \leq 0.90\).
* The fit to world \(\Gamma_{1}^{n}\) data for extracting \(f_{2}^{n}\) yielded parameters \(A = 1.936 \times 10^{-2}\) and \(B = -1.675 \times 10^{-2}\) with associated error matrix elements \(2.240 \times 10^{-4}\), \(-1.653 \times 10^{-4}\), and \(1.351 \times 10^{-4}\).

## Context

This work extends measurements of the neutron spin structure to higher precision in the twist-3 matrix element \(d_{2}^{n}\), building on prior experiments such as SLAC E142 (Anthony 1996), E143 (Abe 1998), E154 (Abe 1997), SMC (Adeva 1998), HERMES (Airapetian 2002), and JLab RSS (Slifer 2008) and E94-010 (Slifer 2008). The results enable tests of lattice QCD calculations and instanton model predictions for color Lorentz forces, and the extracted \(A_{1}^{n}\) and quark polarization ratios provide constraints on constituent quark models versus perturbative QCD at large \(x\).
