# Chemical freeze-out parameters at RHIC from microscopic model calculations

> The relaxation of hot nuclear matter to an equilibrated state in the central zone of heavy-ion collisions at energies from AGS to RHIC is studied within the microscopic UrQMD model. It is found that the system reaches the (quasi)equilibrium stage for the period of 10-15 fm/$c$. Within this time the matter in the cell expands nearly isentropically with the entropy to baryon ratio $S/A = 150 - 170$. Thermodynamic characteristics of the system at AGS and at SPS energies at the endpoints of this stage are very close to the parameters of chemical and thermal freeze-out extracted from the thermal fit to experimental data. Predictions are made for the full RHIC energy $\sqrt{s} = 200$ AGeV. The formation of a resonance-rich state at RHIC energies is discussed.

## Key claim

The UrQMD transport model predicts that in central Au+Au collisions at full RHIC energy (sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV), the hot nuclear matter in the central cell reaches a quasi-equilibrium stage that yields chemical freeze-out parameters of T = 195 +/- 5 MeV and mu_B = 43 +/- 2 MeV, but the matter freezes before reaching complete chemical equilibrium, complicating standard thermal model fits.

## Method

The authors used the microscopic UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at AGS, SPS, and RHIC energies. They defined a central cubic cell of volume V = 125 fm^3 and tracked the time evolution of thermodynamic quantities including pressure, energy density, baryon density, and strangeness density, comparing them to grand-canonical statistical model predictions. The microscopic results were used to extract temperature T, baryochemical potential mu_B, and strangeness chemical potential mu_S via the statistical model of an ideal hadron gas.

## Key numbers

- Entropy per baryon ratio at RHIC: S/A = 150 - 170 (compared to S/A = 12 at AGS and S/A = 32 at SPS)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature at RHIC: T_chem.FO = 195 +/- 5 MeV
- Baryochemical potential at chemical freeze-out at RHIC: mu_chem.FO = 43 +/- 2 MeV
- Thermal freeze-out temperature at RHIC: T_therm.FO = 130 +/- 5 MeV
- Baryochemical potential at thermal freeze-out at RHIC: mu_therm.FO = 43 +/- 3 MeV
- Pressure to energy density ratio: P/epsilon = 0.12 at AGS, and 0.15 at both SPS and RHIC
- Energy density in the RHIC cell at t = 5 fm/c: epsilon = 2.33 GeV/fm^3
- Energy per hadron at beginning of kinetic equilibrium at RHIC: 1.08 GeV (t = 5 fm/c) and 1.01 GeV (t = 6 fm/c)
- Strangeness to entropy ratio at RHIC: R_S = 0.05 (compared to 0.025 at AGS and 0.04 at SPS)
- Baryon resonance fraction at RHIC: approximately 70% of all baryons in the cell for 5 <= t <= 19 fm/c
- Particle composition in RHIC cell at t >= 10 fm/c: 90% mesons, 7% baryons, 3% antibaryons

## Context

This work extends earlier UrQMD studies at AGS and SPS energies (Bravina et al., 1999) that established the central cell method for studying equilibration in heavy-ion collisions. The predictions for RHIC are compared to thermal fit results from Cleymans and Redlich (1999) and to preliminary STAR Collaboration data. The finding of a resonance-rich state at RHIC, with baryon resonances (mostly Delta(1232)) comprising about 70% of all baryons, suggests that the matter freezes out before reaching complete chemical equilibrium, which challenges the standard application of thermal model fits and motivates experimental searches for resonance-rich matter at RHIC.
