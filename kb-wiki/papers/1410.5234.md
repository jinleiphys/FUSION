# Semileptonic $B \rightarrow \bar{D} $ transition in nuclear medium

> We study the semileptonic tree-level $B \rightarrow \bar{D}$ transition in the framework of QCD sum rules in nuclear medium. In particular, we calculate the in-medium form factors entering the transition matrix elements defining this decay channel. It is found that the interactions of the participating particles with the medium lead to a considerable suppression in the branching ratio compared to the vacuum.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the in-medium form factors for the semileptonic tree-level \(B \rightarrow \bar{D}\) transition using QCD sum rules and find that the nuclear medium suppresses the branching ratio by roughly 50% for all lepton channels when central values are considered, though the errors on the form factors at \(q^2=0\) can partially obscure the difference between medium and vacuum results.

## Method

The authors compute the three-point in-medium correlation function for the \(B^+ \rightarrow \bar{D}^0 \ell^+ \nu_\ell\) transition using QCD sum rules, expanding the OPE side in terms of nuclear matter condensates (quark, gluon, and mixed condensates) at saturation density \(\rho_N = (0.11)^3\) GeV\(^3\). They equate the hadronic and OPE representations, apply double Borel transformation with respect to the squared momenta of the initial and final mesons, and perform continuum subtraction to extract the two transition form factors \(f_1(q^2)\) and \(f_2(q^2)\). The form factors are then fitted to a rational function of \(q^2\) and used to compute the decay width and branching ratio.

## Key numbers

- Form factor \(f_1(0) = 0.55 \pm 0.06\) in nuclear matter, compared to \(0.64 \pm 0.07\) in vacuum.
- Form factor \(f_2(0) = -0.11 \pm 0.01\) in nuclear matter, compared to \(-0.15 \pm 0.02\) in vacuum.
- Branching ratio for \(B^+ \rightarrow \bar{D}^0 \ell^+ \nu_\ell\) (\(\ell = e, \mu\)) in nuclear matter: \((1.04 \pm 0.29) \times 10^{-2}\); in vacuum: \((2.03 \pm 0.57) \times 10^{-2}\).
- Branching ratio for \(B^+ \rightarrow \bar{D}^0 \tau^+ \nu_\tau\) in nuclear matter: \((0.48 \pm 0.13) \times 10^{-2}\); in vacuum: \((0.92 \pm 0.26) \times 10^{-2}\).
- Ratio \(\mathcal{R}(D) = \mathcal{B}(B^+ \rightarrow \bar{D}^0 \tau^+ \nu_\tau) / \mathcal{B}(B^+ \rightarrow \bar{D}^0 \ell^+ \nu_\ell) = 0.461 \pm 0.009\) in nuclear matter.
- Borel parameter working regions: \(8 \text{ GeV}^2 \leq M^2 \leq 12 \text{ GeV}^2\) and \(4 \text{ GeV}^2 \leq M'^2 \leq 6 \text{ GeV}^2\).
- Continuum thresholds: \(s_0 = (32.0 \pm 1.5) \text{ GeV}^2\) and \(s'_0 = (5.0 \pm 0.5) \text{ GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends previous in-medium QCD sum rule studies of \(B\) and \(D\) meson spectroscopic properties (Hayashigaki, Azizi et al., Hilger, Wang) to the first calculation of hadronic transition form factors in nuclear medium. The vacuum results are consistent with experimental data from the Particle Data Group (PDG) for both \(\ell = e, \mu\) and \(\tau\) channels. The findings are relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments at facilities such as CEBAF, RHIC, FAIR, CBM, and PANDA, and suggest that medium modifications must be accounted for when comparing theoretical predictions with future in-medium decay measurements.
