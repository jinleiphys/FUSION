# Heavy-dense QCD, sign optimization and Lefschetz thimbles

> We study the heavy-dense limit of QCD on the lattice with heavy quarks at high density. The effective three dimensional theory has a sign problem which is alleviated by sign optimization where the path integration domain is deformed in complex space in a way that minimizes the phase oscillations. We simulate the theory via a Hybrid-Monte-Carlo, for different volumes, both to leading order and next-to-next-to leading order in the hopping expansion, and show that sign optimization successfully mitigates the sign problem at large enough volumes where usual re-weighting methods fail. Finally we show that there is a significant overlap between the complex manifold generated by sign optimization and the Lefschetz thimbles associated with the theory.

## Key claim
Sign optimization successfully mitigates the sign problem in heavy-dense QCD on the lattice for volumes up to 6^3, where standard re-weighting methods fail, but only within the specific "mixing" ansatz for the complexified integration domain and under the heavy-dense and strong-coupling limits.

## Method
The authors simulate the effective three-dimensional theory of heavy-dense QCD using a Hybrid-Monte-Carlo algorithm, with the path integration domain deformed into complex space via a "mixing" ansatz parameterized by auxiliary variables. The optimal deformation is found by gradient ascent that maximizes the average phase, and the theory is simulated both to leading order and to next-to-next-to leading order in the hopping expansion for various volumes and chemical potentials.

## Key numbers
- The average sign on the real manifold for volume V=6^3 at mu=0.998, kappa=0.01, N_t=100 is too small for reweighting; sign optimization lifts it to values where reweighting is possible.
- For V=4^3 at h=0.45 (kappa=0.01, mu=0.998, N_t=100), the average sign increases with gradient ascent steps, stabilizing the Polyakov loop measurement.
- The Silver Blaze phenomenon is reproduced: density sharply rises at mu=1 (chemical potential in units of constituent quark mass).
- For volumes 5^3 and 6^3, sign optimization significantly reduces statistical uncertainty in density and Polyakov loop across the chemical potential range.
- For next-to-next-to leading order simulations at V=4^3, N_t=100, the average sign improves with sign optimization for different values of mu and hopping parameter kappa up to 0.05.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of sign optimization in one-dimensional QCD (Basar 2022) to the three-dimensional heavy-dense effective theory, which inherits the sign problem from full QCD. It builds on prior studies of heavy-dense QCD via Lefschetz thimbles (Zambello 2018) and Complex Langevin (Attanasio 2015, Aarts 2016). The method enables future application of sign optimization to full QCD at finite density, and the comparison with Lefschetz thimbles provides physical insight into how the optimization alleviates the sign problem.
