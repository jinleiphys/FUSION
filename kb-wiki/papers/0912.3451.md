# Transverse hydrodynamics with sudden hadronization -- production of strangeness

> We consider a physical scenario for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions where, at the early stage, only transverse degrees of freedom of partons are thermalized, while the longitudinal motion is described by free streaming. When the energy density of the partonic system drops to a certain critical value, the partons hadronize and the newly formed hadronic system freezes out. This sudden change is described with the help of the Landau matching conditions followed by the simulations done with THERMINATOR. The proposed scenario reproduces well the transverse-momentum spectra, the elliptic flow coefficient v2, and the HBT radii of pions and kaons studied at RHIC (Au+Au collisions at the top beam energy). It also reproduces quite well the transverse-momentum spectra of hyperons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a model where only transverse degrees of freedom are thermalized at early times, followed by a sudden hadronization and freeze-out via Landau matching, can reproduce RHIC data for pions, kaons, and hyperons, but the authors acknowledge this sudden transition is a simplified approximation that may effectively represent more gradual isotropization processes.

## Method

The authors solved the equations of transverse hydrodynamics (where longitudinal pressure vanishes) for Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV, using a 2D equation of state P₂ = ε₂/2 with ν_g = 16. At a critical energy density corresponding to a 3D freeze-out temperature T₃f, they applied Landau matching conditions to instantaneously convert the 2D partonic system into a 3D isotropic hadron resonance gas, then used the THERMINATOR Monte Carlo code to simulate hadron decays and freeze-out.

## Key numbers

- Initial 2D temperature: T₂ᵢ = 590 MeV at τᵢ = 1 fm
- 3D freeze-out/hadronization temperature: T₃f = 165 MeV
- Normalization parameter: n₀ = 0.43
- Mixing parameter for initial conditions: κ = 0.14 (following PHOBOS studies)
- Saturation scale at RHIC: Qₛ ∼ 1 GeV
- Theoretical HBT radii are about 20% smaller than experimental STAR data values

## Context

This work extends the transverse hydrodynamics approach proposed by Bialas (2007) and Chojnacki (2007), which previously described only parton-level observables, by adding a sudden hadronization transition modeled with Landau matching. It contrasts with standard perfect-fluid hydrodynamics (Kolb 2003, Huovinen 2003, Shuryak 2004) that assumes full 3D thermalization from early times. The results enable further studies of gradual isotropization using kinetic theory or dissipative hydrodynamics, as suggested by Kovchegov (2005), Bozek (2007), and Zhang (2008).
