# Scattering of light nuclei

> The exact treatment of nuclei starting from the constituent nucleons and the fundamental interactions among them has been a long-standing goal in nuclear physics. Above all nuclear scattering and reactions, which require the solution of the many-body quantum-mechanical problem in the continuum, represent an extraordinary theoretical as well as computational challenge for {\em ab initio} approaches. We present a new {\em ab initio} many-body approach which derives from the combination of the {\em ab initio} no-core shell model with the resonating-group method [4]. By complementing a microscopic cluster technique with the use of realistic interactions, and a microscopic and consistent description of the nucleon clusters, this approach is capable of describing simultaneously both bound and scattering states in light nuclei. We will discuss applications to neutron and proton scattering on $s$- and light $p$-shell nuclei using realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, and outline the progress toward the treatment of more complex reactions.

## Key claim

The authors present a new *ab initio* many-body approach that combines the no-core shell model (NCSM) with the resonating-group method (RGM) to describe both bound and scattering states in light nuclei simultaneously, but the approach is currently limited to binary-cluster channels and the treatment of more complex reactions is only outlined as future progress.

## Method

The NCSM/RGM approach expands the many-body wave function in binary-cluster channel states, where the internal wave functions of the nucleon clusters are obtained from NCSM calculations using realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials in a harmonic oscillator basis up to a maximum excitation \(N_{max}\hbar\Omega\). The unknown relative-motion wave functions are determined by solving non-local integral-differential coupled-channel equations that contain Hamiltonian and norm kernels, which incorporate the inter-cluster antisymmetrizer and all nuclear structure information. For single-nucleon projectile systems (a=1), the kernels are computed using both Jacobi-coordinate and Slater-determinant channel states, with the latter enabling efficient calculations for p-shell nuclei through second-quantization techniques.

## Key numbers

- The harmonic oscillator basis is truncated at a maximum excitation \(N_{max}\hbar\Omega\) above the minimum energy configuration, with \(\Omega\) the HO frequency
- For the \(n+\alpha\)(g.s.) system, calculations use the N\(^3\)LO NN potential at \(\hbar\Omega=19\) MeV
- The norm kernel for the \(n+\alpha\) system is shown for the \(^2S_{1/2}\) channel (strongly influenced by Pauli exclusion) and the \(^2P_{3/2}\) channel (corresponding to the \(^5\)He resonance)
- The general HO brackets for two particles with mass ratio \(a/(A-a)\) are used, with specific examples for mass ratio 3 (for A=3 systems) and mass ratio \(a/(A-a)\) for general cases

## Context

This work extends the traditional resonating-group method (RGM), which previously used semirealistic NN interactions like the Volkov (1970) or Minnesota (1977) potentials with adjusted parameters, by incorporating realistic interactions and a consistent microscopic description of clusters from the *ab initio* NCSM. The approach enables *ab initio* calculations of low-energy neutron and proton scattering on s-shell and light p-shell nuclei, going beyond the few-nucleon sector where exact methods like Faddeev (Witala, 2001), Faddeev-Yacubovski (Lazauskas, 2005), and hyperspherical harmonics (Viviani, 2009) are available. The method opens the path toward treating more complex reactions, including those relevant to solar neutrino production such as \(^7\)Be\((p,\gamma)^8\)B radiative capture.
