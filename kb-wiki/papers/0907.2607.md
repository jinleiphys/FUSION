# Quark matter under strong magnetic fields in the su(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model

> In the present work we use the mean field approximation to investigate quark matter described by the su(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model subject to a strong magnetic field. We consider two cases: pure quark matter and quark matter in beta-equilibrium possibly present in magnetars. The results are compared with the ones obtained with the su(2) version of the model. The energy per baryon of magnetized quark matter becomes more bound than nuclear matter made of iron nuclei, for B around 2x10^(19)G. When the su(3) NJL model is applied to stellar matter, the maximum mass configurations are always above 1.45 solar masses and may be as high as 1.86 solar masses for a central magnetic field of 5x10^(18)G. These numbers are within the masses of observed neutron stars.

## Key claim

For magnetic fields around 2 × 10^19 G, the energy per baryon of magnetized quark matter becomes more bound than nuclear matter made of iron nuclei, but this result depends on the specific parameter set used and the mean-field approximation applied to the su(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

## Method

The authors used the mean-field approximation to solve the su(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a constant external magnetic field, computing the thermodynamic potential, effective quark masses, and equation of state at zero temperature. They considered two scenarios: pure quark matter with equal chemical potentials for all flavors, and quark matter in beta-equilibrium with electrons and muons, as possibly present in magnetars. The quark condensates and pressures were evaluated including vacuum, magnetic, and medium contributions, with the magnetic field effects incorporated through Landau level quantization and the Riemann-Hurwitz zeta function.

## Key numbers

- Energy per baryon of magnetized quark matter becomes more bound than iron nuclei (E/A ~ 930 MeV) for B around 2 × 10^19 G
- Maximum mass configurations in su(3) NJL are always above 1.45 solar masses and may reach 1.86 solar masses for a central magnetic field of 5 × 10^18 G
- For su(3) NJL with B = 5 × 10^19 G, the maximum gravitational mass is 1.86 solar masses with radius 8.81 km and central energy density 9.26 fm^-4
- For su(3) NJL with B = 0, the maximum gravitational mass is 1.46 solar masses with radius 8.93 km
- The u quark vacuum effective mass becomes larger than that of the s quark for B > 1.5 × 10^20 G
- For su(2) NJL with B = 0, the maximum gravitational mass is 1.29 solar masses with radius 7.09 km
- The largest radius for maximum mass stars in su(3) NJL may be as high as 9.5 km
- Chiral symmetry is approximately restored for chemical potential μ = 400 MeV for the magnetic field intensities considered

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the su(2) version of the NJL model (Menezes et al., 2009) to include strange quarks, addressing questions raised by Buballa (1996, 1999) about the stability of quark matter described by the NJL model. The results show that the su(2) version predicts too small star masses except for very large magnetic fields, while the su(3) version produces maximum masses within the range of observed neutron stars. The paper enables future investigations of color superconductivity effects on quark stars under strong magnetic fields, noting that noticeable effects from color superconductivity likely require fields above approximately 10^19 G.
