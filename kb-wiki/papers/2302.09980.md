# Study of the threshold anomaly in the elastic scattering of d+$^{197}$Au

> Measurements of the elastic scattering angular distribution for the d+$^{197}$Au system were carried out covering deuteron incident energies in the range from 5 to 16 MeV, i.e. approximately 50% below and above the Coulomb barrier. A critical interaction distance of $d_I$= 2.49 fm was determined from these distributions, which is comparable to that of the radioactive halo nucleus $^{6}$He. The experimental angular distributions were systematically analyzed using two alternative models: the semi-microscopic Sao Paulo and the effective Woods-Saxon optical potentials, for which the best-fitting parameters were determined. These potentials, integrated in the vicinity of the sensitivity radius, were calculated for each energy. For both models, the energy dependence of these integrals presented the breakup threshold anomaly around the coulomb barrier, a typical signature of weakly bound nuclei.

# Study of the threshold anomaly in the elastic scattering of d+$^{197}$Au

## Key claim

The deuteron projectile on $^{197}$Au exhibits the breakup threshold anomaly (BTA) rather than the usual threshold anomaly (TA), as evidenced by the energy dependence of the optical potential integrals around the Coulomb barrier. This conclusion is conditional on the choice of optical model and the sensitivity radius analysis, and the authors note that their result differs from a prior study of d+$^{208}$Pb (Ref. [36] in the paper) which found the usual TA.

## Method

Elastic scattering angular distributions for d+$^{197}$Au were measured at ten deuteron incident energies from 5 to 16 MeV using the TANDAR tandem accelerator, with two silicon detector telescopes for particle identification. The experimental angular distributions were analyzed using two optical models: the phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential (WSP) and the semi-microscopic São Paulo potential (SPP), with parameters optimized via $\chi^2$ minimization using the code SFRESCO. The energy dependence of the optical potentials was studied by calculating Gaussian-weighted radial integrals in the vicinity of the sensitivity radius (determined via the notch method), and the dispersion relation was applied to connect the real and imaginary components.

## Key numbers

- Coulomb barrier: $V_B = 10.5$ MeV in the center-of-mass system
- Reduced critical interaction distance: $d_I = 2.49(7)$ fm
- Reduced strong-absorption distance: $d_S = 1.30(2)$ fm
- Sensitivity radius: $R_S = 11.0 \pm 0.5$ fm (average of real part at 10.5 fm and imaginary part at 11.5 fm)
- Global estimator $\chi_G^2 = 1.18$ for the fully optimized Woods-Saxon potential (all parameters free)
- Global estimator $\chi_G^2 = 1.97$ for the São Paulo potential (two free parameters)
- At 8 MeV, the SPP imaginary normalization factor $N_I = 32.1$, more than an order of magnitude larger than at higher energies
- Critical interaction distance corresponds to $17.6$ fm; strong-absorption distance corresponds to $9.2$ fm

## Context

This work extends the study of threshold anomalies to deuteron projectiles, which are the simplest nuclear laboratory for reactions. Prior work on weakly bound projectiles such as $^6$Li, $^7$Li, $^9$Be, and $^6$He (e.g., FNA07, PAD04, GLP07, SEA08) found the BTA signature, while strongly bound projectiles like $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O show the usual TA. The only prior deuteron study, on d+$^{208}$Pb (Ref. [36] in the paper), found the usual TA, which the present result contradicts. The measured $d_I$ value is comparable to that of the radioactive halo nucleus $^6$He (2.20 fm) and $^8$He (2.24 fm), suggesting the deuteron behaves similarly to weakly bound systems due to its extended wave function. This work enables further analysis of transfer and breakup reactions in the d+$^{197}$Au system, as well as studies of the polarization potential.
