# Glasma properties in small proper time expansion

> In a series of works by two of us, various characteristics of the glasma from the earliest phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions have been studied using a proper time expansion. These characteristics include: energy density, longitudinal and transverse pressures, collective flow, angular momentum and parameters of jet quenching. In this paper we extend the proper time interval where our results are reliable by working at higher order in the expansion. We also generalize our previous study of jet quenching by extending our calculations to consider inhomogeneous glasma. Inhomogeneities are an important aspect of physically realistic systems that are difficult to include in calculations and are frequently ignored.

## Key claim

The paper extends the proper time interval over which glasma properties from the earliest phase of relativistic heavy-ion collisions are reliable by working to eighth order in the proper time expansion, and generalizes previous jet quenching studies to inhomogeneous glasma produced by finite nuclei with Woods-Saxon density distributions; the results hold for approximately \(0 < \tau \lesssim 0.06\) fm/c for energy-momentum tensor quantities and about \(0.08\) fm/c for the momentum broadening coefficient \(\hat{q}\).

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the forward light-cone using an expansion in powers of the proper time \(\tau\), treating it as a small parameter. Post-collision chromoelectric and chromomagnetic fields are expressed in terms of pre-collision gauge potentials, which are then related to color charge distributions via the McLerran-Venugopalan model with a Gaussian average (Glasma Graph approximation). A hybrid computational procedure using Mathematica and Julia handles the enormous number of terms (e.g., approximately \(2.6 \times 10^{18}\) terms for a correlator at order \(\tau^8\)), and field correlators are computed up to eighth order for the energy-momentum tensor and up to seventh order for jet quenching quantities, including a first-order gradient expansion to incorporate nuclear density profiles.

## Key numbers

- The energy-momentum tensor results from the eighth-order expansion are valid for \(0 < \tau \lesssim 0.06\) fm/c, extending the previous sixth-order range of about \(0.05\) fm/c.
- The momentum broadening coefficient \(\hat{q}\) calculated to seventh order is reliable for \(0 < \tau \lesssim 0.08\) fm/c, compared to the previous fifth-order range of about \(0.07\) fm/c.
- At eighth order, the \(z\)-component of the magnetic field has \(15\,964\,128\) terms; a correlator of two field components at order \(\tau^8\) involves approximately \(2.6 \times 10^{18}\) terms.
- The initial anisotropy measure \(A_{TL}\) equals \(6\) at \(\tau=0\) (since \(p_T = \mathcal{E}_0 = -p_L\)).
- The elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) at \(\tau=0.06\) fm and the initial eccentricity \(\varepsilon\) are shown as functions of impact parameter from \(b=1\) fm to \(b=6\) fm; the ratio \(v_2/\varepsilon\) changes less than \(v_2\) alone over this range.
- Angular momentum per unit rapidity is computed for impact parameters from \(b=0.5\) fm to \(b=2.5\) fm and proper times from \(\tau=0.02\) fm to \(\tau=0.06\) fm, with the integral over \(R^x\) taken up to \(R^x_{\rm max} = 5.9\) fm.
- The calculation uses SU(3) gauge group, coupling constant \(g=1\), saturation scale \(Q_s = 2\) GeV, infrared cutoff \(m=0.2\) GeV, Woods-Saxon parameters \(a=0.5\) fm, \(r_0=1.25\) fm, and \(A=207\) for Pb.

## Context

This work extends a series of papers by Carrington et al. (2020-2022) that studied glasma characteristics using a small proper time expansion up to sixth order for the energy-momentum tensor and fifth order for jet quenching. The new eighth-order results extend the reliable time interval and confirm earlier findings that the glasma angular momentum is very small compared to the incoming nuclear angular momentum, consistent with the absence of global polarization observed by STAR (2017). The inclusion of inhomogeneous glasma via a gradient expansion (following Chen (2015) and Fries (2017)) enables more realistic modeling of jet quenching in finite nuclei, showing that the glasma contribution to jet suppression is comparable to that of the equilibrium quark-gluon plasma phase despite its much shorter lifetime. The results also demonstrate that glasma collective flow mimics hydrodynamic behavior (e.g., \(v_2\) increases while eccentricity decreases with time), which may help explain the success of hydrodynamic models applied to far-from-equilibrium systems.
