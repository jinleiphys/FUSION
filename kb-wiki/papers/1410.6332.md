# Consistent neutron star models with magnetic field dependent equations of state

> We present a self-consistent model for the study of the structure of a neutron star in strong magnetic fields. Starting from a microscopic Lagrangian, this model includes the effect of the magnetic field on the equation of state, the interaction of the electromagnetic field with matter (magnetisation), and anisotropies in the energy-momentum tensor, as well as general relativistic aspects. We build numerical axisymmetric stationary models and show the applicability of the approach with one example quark matter equation of state (EoS) often employed in the recent literature for studies of strongly magnetised neutron stars. For this EoS, the effect of inclusion of magnetic field dependence or the magnetisation do not increase the maximum mass significantly in contrast to what has been claimed by previous studies.

## Key claim
For the specific quark matter equation of state (MCFL phase) studied, including magnetic field dependence and magnetisation does not significantly increase the maximum neutron star mass, contrary to claims in earlier studies. The effect of the magnetic field on the equation of state becomes significant only at very large fields (B ≳ 10¹⁹ G), and the magnetisation in this model is too small for magnetic field values reachable in magnetars to induce any considerable change in neutron star structure.

## Method
The authors constructed a self-consistent general relativistic model of axisymmetric stationary neutron stars with poloidal magnetic fields, starting from a microscopic fermion Lagrangian and including magnetic field effects on the equation of state, magnetisation, and anisotropies in the energy-momentum tensor. They solved the coupled Einstein-Maxwell equations using spectral methods from the LORENE library, employing a quark matter equation of state in the Magnetic Colour-Flavor-Locked (MCFL) phase based on a massless three-flavor MIT bag model with NJL-type pairing interaction. The equation of state and magnetisation were pre-computed as functions of both enthalpy and magnetic field amplitude, then interpolated using Hermite polynomials during the global model computation.

## Key numbers
- The quark matter equation of state used a bag constant B_bag = 60 MeV/fm³ and pairing strength G_P = 5.15 GeV⁻² with cutoff Λ = 1 GeV
- Magnetic field effects on the equation of state become significant only at B ≳ 10¹⁹ G
- A static star configuration with gravitational mass 2.22 M⊙ was computed with a polar magnetic field of 8.16 × 10¹⁷ G
- The maximum magnetic field in magnetar interiors is estimated via the virial theorem at ∼ 10¹⁸ G
- Observed neutron star masses reach ∼ 2 M⊙ from recent astrophysical reports (Demorest; Antoniadis)
- Surface magnetic fields of typical pulsars are ∼ 10¹²–10¹³ G, while magnetars show surface fields up to ∼ 10¹⁵ G

## Context
This work extends previous general relativistic models of magnetised neutron stars (Bocquet, 1995; Bonazzola, 1993) by including for the first time a magnetic-field-dependent equation of state and magnetisation in fully axisymmetric numerical models. It contradicts earlier claims (Noronha, 2007; Paulucci, 2011) that magnetic field effects significantly increase the maximum mass, showing instead that for the MCFL quark matter model the effect is negligible at magnetar-strength fields. The approach enables future studies with different equations of state (hadronic or other quark models) and more realistic current distributions, as well as investigations of the interplay between rotation and strong magnetic fields in neutron star structure.
