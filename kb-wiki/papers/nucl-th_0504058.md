# Low momentum shell model effective interactions with all-order core polarizations

> An all-order summation of core polarization diagrams using the low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction, V_low-k is presented. The summation is based on the Kirson-Babu-Brown (KBB) induced interaction approach in which the vertex functions are obtained self consistently by solving a set of non-linear coupled equations. It is found that the solution of these equations is simplified by using V_low-k, which is energy independent, and by employing Green functions in the particle-particle and particle-hole channels. We have applied this approach to the sd-shell effective interactions and find that the results calculated to all orders using the KBB summation technique are remarkably similar to those of second-order perturbation theory, average differences being less than 10%.

# Low momentum shell model effective interactions with all-order core polarizations

## Key claim

The all-order summation of core polarization diagrams using the Kirson-Babu-Brown (KBB) induced interaction approach yields effective interactions for the sd-shell that are remarkably similar to those from second-order perturbation theory, with average differences of less than 10%. This result is conditional on using the energy-independent low-momentum nucleon-nucleon interaction V_low-k and on restricting the calculation to TDA diagrams for the particle-particle and particle-hole Green functions.

## Method

The authors solve the KBB self-consistent equations for the particle-hole vertex function f and the particle-particle vertex function Gamma, using V_low-k as the driving term and employing the true particle-particle and particle-hole Green functions to simplify the coupled equations. The vertex functions are obtained iteratively starting from second-order approximations, and the resulting vertex function Gamma is used as the Q-hat term in a folded-diagram expansion for the effective interaction V_eff, which is summed to all orders using the Lee-Suzuki iteration method. The calculation is restricted to a model space of 10 orbits from 0s_1/2 to 1p_1/2 with an oscillator constant of hbar-omega = 14 MeV, including only TDA diagrams and core excitations within this space.

## Key numbers

- Average differences between all-order KBB results and second-order perturbation theory results are less than 10% for the sd-shell effective interaction matrix elements.
- The decimation momentum cutoff for V_low-k is chosen as Lambda approximately 2.0 fm^{-1}, corresponding to the pion production threshold at E_lab approximately 350 MeV.
- The oscillator constant used in the shell model space is hbar-omega = 14 MeV.
- The model space includes 10 orbits from 0s_1/2 to 1p_1/2.

## Context

This work extends the earlier core polarization studies of Bertsch (early works) and Kuo and Brown (early works), who calculated core polarization to second order in the G-matrix, and the third-order studies of Barrett and Kirson (Barrett and Kirson, year not specified) for the sd-shell. It addresses the conflicting results between Kirson's finite-nucleus study, which found all-order core polarization effects close to the bare interaction, and Sjoberg's nuclear matter study (Sjoberg, year not specified), which found significant effects on Fermi liquid parameters. The method enables future calculations of effective interactions in other nuclear regions and effective operators such as magnetic moments, and the authors plan to extend the treatment to include RPA diagrams.
