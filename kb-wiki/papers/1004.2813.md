# Strangeness in the Meson Cloud Model

> I review progress in calculating strange quark and antiquark distributions of the nucleon using the meson cloud model. This progress parallels that of the meson cloud model, which is now a useful theoretical basis for understanding symmetry breaking in nucleon parton distribution functions. I examine the breaking of symmetries involving strange quarks and antiquarks, including quark - antiquark symmetry in the sea, SU(3) flavour symmetry and SU(6) spin-flavour symmetry.

## Key claim

The meson cloud model (MCM) predicts a strange sea asymmetry \(x(s-\bar{s})\) whose second moment is of order \(10^{-4}\), which is too small to explain the NuTeV anomaly in the weak mixing angle; the sign of the asymmetry depends on whether \(K^*\) meson Fock states are included.

## Method

The author calculates strange and antistrange quark distributions in the nucleon using time-ordered perturbation theory in the infinite momentum frame, with on-shell structure functions for the intermediate hadrons. The strange quark distribution in hyperons (\(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\)) is taken from a bag model calculation evolved to \(Q^2 = 16\) GeV\(^2\) via next-to-leading order QCD evolution, while the antistrange distribution in the kaon is taken from the Dortmund group parameterization. Fluctuation functions for the \(N \to K H\) and \(N \to K^* H\) vertices are included, with form-factor cutoffs \(\Lambda_c \sim 1\) GeV.

## Key numbers

- The second moment of the strange sea asymmetry \(\langle x(s-\bar{s})\rangle\) has magnitude around \(10^{-4}\), positive without \(K^*\) states and negative with \(K^*\) states included.
- The CTEQ group bounds on the second moment of the asymmetry are \(-0.001 < \langle x(s-\bar{s})\rangle < 0.005\).
- The NuTeV measurement of \(\sin^2\theta_W = 0.2277 \pm 0.0013 (\text{stat}) \pm 0.0009 (\text{syst})\) is 2% smaller than the world average, a \(3\sigma\) discrepancy; a symmetry-breaking term of \(-0.0038\) in the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio would explain the discrepancy.
- Removing \(K^*\) states changes the total \(s(x)+\bar{s}(x)\) by about 10%.
- The MCM calculation for \(x(\Delta s + \Delta \bar{s})\) is consistent with HERMES data at \(Q^2 = 2.5\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the original meson cloud model calculation by Signal and Thomas (1987), which used covariant perturbation theory and off-shell structure functions, to a time-ordered perturbation theory formulation following Melnitchouk and Thomas (1993) and Zoller (1992). The updated calculation addresses the NuTeV anomaly (NuTeV collaboration, 2002) and compares with HERMES polarized data (2004, 2008) and CTEQ global fits (CTEQ6.5S0, CTEQ6.6). The model enables estimates of SU(3) flavour symmetry breaking in the strange sea and provides a non-perturbative framework for understanding parton distribution function asymmetries.
