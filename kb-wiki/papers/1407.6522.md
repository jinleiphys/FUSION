# Astrophysical reaction rate for $^9$Be formation within a three-body approach

> The structure of the Borromean nucleus $^9$Be ($α+α+n$) is addressed within a three-body approach using the analytical transformed harmonic oscillator method. The three-body formalism provides an accurate description of the radiative capture reaction rate for the entire temperature range relevant in Astrophysics. At high temperatures, results match the calculations based on two-step sequential processes. At low temperatures, where the particles have no access to intermediate two-body resonances, the three-body direct capture leads to reaction rates larger than the sequential processes. These results support the reliability of the method for systems with several charged particles.

## Key claim
The three-body direct capture mechanism for $^9$Be formation yields reaction rates larger than those from sequential two-step processes at low temperatures, where intermediate two-body resonances cannot be populated. The analytical transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) method provides a reliable description of the radiative capture reaction rate across the entire astrophysical temperature range for systems with multiple charged particles.

## Method
The authors describe $^9$Be as a three-body system ($\alpha+\alpha+n$) using hyperspherical coordinates and diagonalize the three-body Hamiltonian in a discrete basis of transformed harmonic oscillator (THO) functions. The THO basis is constructed via an analytical local scale transformation of harmonic oscillator functions, with parameters adjusted to concentrate pseudostates near the breakup threshold. Electromagnetic transition probabilities $B(E1)$ and $B(M1)$ are computed from the resulting eigenstates, and the energy-averaged reaction rate $\langle R_{abc}(\varepsilon)\rangle(T)$ is obtained by integrating the photodissociation cross section weighted by a Poisson distribution to smooth the discrete pseudostate energies.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state binding energy of $^9$Be: $\varepsilon_B = -1.5736$ MeV (converged with $K_{\rm max}=30$, $i_{\rm max}=20$)
- Charge radius of $^9$Be: $r_{\rm ch} = 2.508$ fm (compared to experimental $2.519\pm0.012$ fm from Angeli (2013))
- Matter radius of $^9$Be: $r_{\rm mat} = 2.466$ fm
- Quadrupole moment of $^9$Be: 4.91 e fm$^2$ (compared to experimental $5.29\pm0.04$ e fm$^2$ from Sundholm (1991))
- Sum rule for electric dipolar transitions: $S_T(E1) = 0.5762$ e$^2$fm$^2$ (converged value)
- $^8$Be two-body resonance position: 0.092 MeV above the three-body threshold
- $^9$Be 1/2$^+$ three-body resonant state: around 0.11 MeV with relatively large width

## Context
This work extends the analytical THO method previously applied to $^6$He (Casal et al., 2013) to the Borromean nucleus $^9$Be, which contains two charged $\alpha$ particles. The three-body approach directly computes the radiative capture reaction $\alpha(\alpha n,\gamma)^9$Be without separating sequential and direct processes, in contrast to traditional two-step sequential models (Fowler, 1967; Angulo, 1999; Sumiyoshi, 2002). The results confirm that at low temperatures the direct three-body capture dominates over sequential processes as suggested by Garrido (2011), and the method enables reliable reaction rate calculations for $r$-process nucleosynthesis in type II supernovae.
