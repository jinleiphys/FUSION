# Calculation of shear viscosity using Green-Kubo relations within a parton cascade

> The shear viscosity of a gluon gas is calculated using the Green-Kubo relation. Time correlations of the energy-momentum tensor in thermal equilibrium are extracted from microscopic simulations using a parton cascade solving various Boltzmann collision processes. We find that the pQCD based gluon bremsstrahlung described by Gunion-Bertsch processes significantly lowers the shear viscosity by a factor of 3-8 compared to elastic scatterings. The shear viscosity scales with the coupling as 1/(alpha_s^2\log(1/alpha_s)). For a constant coupling constant the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio has no dependence on temperature. Replacing the pQCD-based collision angle distribution of binary scatterings by an isotropic form decreases the shear viscosity by a factor of 3.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity of a gluon gas is calculated using the Green-Kubo relation, and the authors find that including pQCD-based gluon bremsstrahlung (Gunion-Bertsch processes) lowers the shear viscosity by a factor of 3-8 compared to elastic scatterings alone, but this result depends on the coupling constant and the angular distribution of collisions.

## Method

The shear viscosity was extracted from microscopic simulations using the parton cascade BAMPS, which solves the ultrarelativistic Boltzmann equation via Monte Carlo techniques. Time correlations of the volume-averaged shear stress tensor component \(\bar \pi^{xy}(t)\) in thermal equilibrium were computed, and the relaxation time \(\tau\) was obtained by fitting the exponentially decaying correlation function. The Green-Kubo relation \(\eta = \frac{V}{10 T} \int_0^\infty dt \langle \bar \pi^{xy}(t) \bar \pi^{xy}(0) \rangle\) was then used to determine the shear viscosity for a gluon gas with both elastic \(gg \to gg\) and inelastic \(gg \leftrightarrow ggg\) pQCD-based interactions.

## Key numbers

- For pure elastic gluon interactions at \(\alpha_s = 0.3\), \(\eta/s = 1.342 \pm 0.035\); including bremsstrahlung reduces this to \(\eta/s = 0.166 \pm 0.025\) (a factor of approximately 8).
- For \(\alpha_s = 0.01\), elastic-only \(\eta/s = 215 \pm 19\); with bremsstrahlung \(\eta/s = 60 \pm 5\) (a factor of approximately 3.6).
- For \(\alpha_s = 0.6\), elastic-only \(\eta/s = 0.61 \pm 0.02\); with bremsstrahlung \(\eta/s = 0.0664 \pm 0.002\) (a factor of approximately 9.2).
- Replacing the pQCD-based collision angle distribution of binary scatterings by an isotropic form decreases the shear viscosity by a factor of 3 (at \(\alpha_s = 0.3\) and \(T = 400\) MeV).
- For constant isotropic cross sections, the shear viscosity for inelastic \(2 \leftrightarrow 3\) processes is a factor of 1.5 smaller than for elastic \(2 \to 2\) processes when \(\sigma_{22} = \sigma_{23}\).
- The shear viscosity scales as \(\eta \sim 1/(\alpha_s^2 \log(1.5/\alpha_s))\) when both elastic and inelastic processes are included.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of shear viscosity for a gluon gas using the BAMPS parton cascade, which had employed gradient expansion methods (PhysRevLett.100.172301) and entropy production approaches (El 2008). The Green-Kubo method provides an independent cross-check that does not rely on model assumptions about the form of equilibrium deviations. The results agree well with those of El (2008) and Reining et al. (2011), and show discrepancies with the temperature dependence reported by Fuini (2010) at lower temperatures. This work enables more reliable extraction of transport coefficients for the quark-gluon plasma and supports the conclusion that pQCD inelastic interactions can drive gluon matter toward a strongly coupled system with \(\eta/s\) as low as the AdS/CFT lower bound.
