# Z(5): Critical point symmetry for the prolate to oblate nuclear shape phase transition

## Key claim

A critical point symmetry for the prolate to oblate shape phase transition, called Z(5), is introduced by approximately separating variables in the Bohr Hamiltonian at γ=30°, assuming an infinite well potential in β and a harmonic oscillator potential in γ. The parameter-free predictions (up to overall scale factors) are found to be in good agreement with experimental data for ¹⁹⁴Pt, which is supposed to lie very close to the prolate to oblate critical point, as well as for its neighbours ¹⁹²Pt and ¹⁹⁶Pt.

## Method

The Bohr Hamiltonian is separated into β and γ equations by fixing γ=30°, where the angular momentum projection α on the body-fixed x'-axis becomes a good quantum number instead of the usual K projection. An infinite well potential in β (zero for β ≤ β_W, infinite otherwise) yields Bessel function solutions for the β-part, while a harmonic oscillator potential with minimum at γ=30° gives simple harmonic oscillator eigenfunctions for the γ-part. The total energy is given by E(s, n_w, L, n_γ̃) = E₀ + A (x_{s,ν})² + B n_γ̃, where x_{s,ν} are zeros of Bessel functions and the index ν depends on L and the wobbling quantum number n_w = L - α.

## Key numbers

- Ground state band levels (s=1, n_w=0) and γ₁-band levels (s=1, n_w=2 for even, n_w=1 for odd) are given as ratios normalized to the 2₁,₀ first excited state; all bands are parameter-free
- The β₁-band bandhead (s=2, n_w=0) is predicted and compared to experimental data for ¹⁹⁴Pt, ¹⁹²Pt, and ¹⁹⁶Pt
- B(E2) transition rates are normalized to B(E2; 2₁,₀ → 0₁,₀); transitions L₁,₂ → L₁,₀ are strong while (L+2)₁,₂ → L₁,₀ transitions are weaker by two or three orders of magnitude
- The ratio B(E2; 2₁,₂ → 2₁,₀) is enhanced relative to B(E2; 4₁,₂ → 4₁,₀) due to a mathematical detail in the Clebsch-Gordan coefficients

## Context

This work extends the family of critical point symmetries that includes E(5) for the U(5) to O(6) transition (Iachello, 2000), X(5) for the U(5) to SU(3) transition (Iachello, 2001), and Y(5) for the axial to triaxial transition (Iachello, 2003). It addresses the prolate to oblate shape phase transition previously studied in the Hf-Hg mass region by Jolie and collaborators (2001), who identified ¹⁹⁴Pt as the nucleus closest to the critical point. The Z(5) model crosses the γ-soft pool by fixing γ=30° and moving from a triaxial vibrator to a triaxial rotator, in contrast to earlier work that crossed from γ=0° to γ=60° in the β≠0 region and identified O(6) as the critical point. The model enables parameter-free predictions for nuclei near the prolate-oblate transition and can be extended using Davidson potentials and variational procedures to prove Z(5) is the critical point symmetry of the triaxial vibrator to triaxial rotator transition.
