# Gluon propagator in two-color dense QCD: Massive Yang-Mills approach at one-loop

> We study the Landau gauge gluon propagators in dense two-color QCD at quark chemical potential, $μ_q$, in the range from 0.5 to 1.0 GeV not reachable by the perturbative method at weak coupling. In order to take into account the non-perturbative effects, at tree level we use a massive Yang-Mills model for the Yang-Mills theory (or the Curci-Ferrari model) which has successfully described the lattice results of the gluon and ghost propagators in the Landau gauge. We couple quarks to this theory and compute the one-loop polarization effects in medium. The presence of the gluon mass significantly tempers the medium effects and uncertainties associated with the strong coupling constant $α_s$. The diquark condensate in two-color QCD is color-singlet, for which neither electric nor magnetic screening masses should appear at the scale less than the diquark gap. The presence of the gap helps to explain the lattice results which are not very sensitive to the quark density. Meanwhile we also found the limitation of the one-loop estimate as well as the lack of some physics in perturbative medium corrections.

## Key claim

The one-loop gluon polarization effects in dense two-color QCD are significantly tempered by the presence of a non-perturbative gluon mass in the vacuum propagator, making the gluon propagator relatively insensitive to the strong coupling constant and to quark density variations, provided the system is in a color-singlet diquark condensed phase where quarks are gapped.

## Method

The authors use the Curci-Ferrari (massive Yang-Mills) model at tree level for the Landau gauge gluon and ghost propagators, coupling quarks with a diquark gap parameter Delta (treated as given in the range 0-200 MeV) to compute the one-loop quark polarization effects in medium. They handle regularization artifacts that violate the Ward-Takahashi identity by using a subtraction scheme that preserves the conservation law, and they decompose the polarization tensor into electric and magnetic components using the projectors P^E and P^M.

## Key numbers

* Quark chemical potential mu_q studied in the range from 0.5 to 1.0 GeV, a region not reachable by weak-coupling perturbation theory.
* Gluon mass m_g in the vacuum propagator is taken in the range 0.4 to 0.7 GeV, based on lattice and functional results for pure Yang-Mills theory.
* Diquark gap Delta estimated from lattice critical temperature T_c approx 80-120 MeV using the BCS formula T_c approx 0.57 Delta, yielding Delta approx 140-210 MeV.
* Strong coupling constant alpha_s varied from 0 to 3 to cover the uncertainty in the infrared regime.
* The diquark condensate in two-color QCD is color-singlet, so neither electric nor magnetic screening masses appear at scales less than the diquark gap.

## Context

This work extends the massive Yang-Mills (Curci-Ferrari) approach, which successfully described lattice gluon and ghost propagators in vacuum (Tissier 2011, Reinosa 2017) and at finite temperature (Reinosa 2013, 2016), to finite quark chemical potential in two-color QCD. It builds on the classification of screening mass effects in different phases (normal, Higgs, singlet) from Kojo (2014) and aims to explain lattice results for QC2D gluon propagators at mu_q = 0.5-1 GeV (Boz 2018), which show little sensitivity to quark density. The approach enables quasi-particle descriptions for strongly correlated quark matter in a regime where perturbative QCD does not converge well.
