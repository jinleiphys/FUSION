# Skyrmions, half-skyrmions and nucleon mass in dense baryonic matter

> We explore the hadron properties in dense baryonic matter in a unified way by using a Skyrme model constructed with an effective Lagrangian which includes the $ρ$ and $ω$ vector mesons as hidden gauge bosons and is valid up to $O(p^4)$ in chiral expansion including the homogeneous Wess-Zumino terms. With the two input values of pion decay constant and the lowest lying vector meson mass which can be fixed in free space, all the other low energy constants in the effective Lagrangian are determined by their master formulas derived from holographic QCD models, which allows us to study the baryonic matter properties with no additional free parameters and thus without ambiguities. We find that the $ω$ field that figures in the homogeneous Wess-Zumino term plays a crucial role in the skyrmion structure and its matter properties. The most striking and intriguing observation is that the pion decay constant that smoothly drops with increasing density in the Skyrmion phase stops decreasing at $n_{1/2}^{}$ at which the skyrmions in medium fractionize into half-skyrmions and remains nearly constant in the half-skyrmion phase. In accordance with the large $N_c$ consideration, the baryon mass also stays non-scaling in the half-skyrmion phase. This feature is supported by the nuclear effective field theory with the parameters of the Lagrangian scaling modified at the skyrmion--half-skyrmion phase transition. Our exploration also uncovers the crucial role of the $ω$ meson in multi-baryon systems as well as in the structure of a single skyrmion.

## Key claim

The pion decay constant and nucleon mass drop smoothly with increasing density in the Skyrmion phase but stop decreasing at a transition density \(n_{1/2}\) where skyrmions fractionize into half-skyrmions, remaining nearly constant in the half-skyrmion phase; this behavior is supported by nuclear effective field theory with scaling parameters modified at the phase transition, but the authors note that some results (e.g., nucleon mass, binding energy) deviate from nature, indicating missing effects such as the dilaton and irreducible three-body interactions.

## Method

The authors use a Skyrme model constructed from a hidden local symmetry (HLS) Lagrangian expanded to chiral order \(O(p^4)\), including \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) vector mesons as hidden gauge bosons and homogeneous Wess-Zumino terms. All low-energy constants except the HLS parameter \(a\) are determined by master formulas derived from holographic QCD models, using only the free-space pion decay constant and \(\rho\) meson mass as inputs. Dense baryonic matter is simulated by placing skyrmions on a face-centered cubic (FCC) crystal, and in-medium hadron properties are studied by expanding meson fields as fluctuations around the classical crystal background.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant input: \(f_\pi = 92.4\ \text{MeV}\); \(\rho\) meson mass input: \(m_\rho = 775.5\ \text{MeV}\).
- In the full HLS(\(\pi,\rho,\omega\)) model, the single-skyrmion mass is \(M_{\text{sol}} = 1184\ \text{MeV}\), the \(\Delta\)-\(N\) mass splitting is \(\Delta_M = 448\ \text{MeV}\), the winding number radius is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_W} = 0.433\ \text{fm}\), and the energy radius is \(\sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle_E} = 0.608\ \text{fm}\).
- In the HLS(\(\pi,\rho,\omega\)) model, the half-skyrmion transition density \(n_{1/2}\) is approximately equal to the normal nuclear density \(n_0\); in the HLS(\(\pi,\rho\)) model (without \(\omega\)), \(n_{1/2} \cong 6 n_0\).
- In the half-skyrmion phase, the in-medium pion decay constant ratio \(f_\pi^* / f_\pi\) stays at a non-vanishing value of approximately 0.65.
- In the HLS(\(\pi,\rho,\omega\)) model, the in-medium soliton mass approaches approximately \(0.6\, M_{\text{sol}}\) in the half-skyrmion phase.

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to dense baryonic matter by including \(\rho\) and \(\omega\) vector mesons via hidden local symmetry, building on prior studies such as the minimal model of Park, Rho, and Vento (2003) and the crystal lattice approach of Perapechka and Vento (2009). The authors confirm the observation by Sutcliffe (2011) that incorporating isovector vector mesons brings the soliton mass close to the BPS bound, but find that the isoscalar \(\omega\) meson obstructs this approach. The results enable further study of the parity-doublet baryon model and the chiral bag model, and suggest that missing effects such as the dilaton and three-body interactions are needed to improve quantitative agreement with nature.
