# Dilepton decays of baryon resonances

> Dalitz decay of baryon resonances is studied and expressions for the decay width are derived for resonances with arbitrary spin and parity. Contributions of the various terms in the transition matrix element are compared and relevance of spin-parity and the resonance mass is discussed. Explicite algebraic expressions are cited for spin$\le$5/2 resonances. The results can be used in models of dielectron production in elementary reactions and heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper derives general expressions for the Dalitz decay width of baryon resonances with arbitrary spin and parity, but the authors ultimately discard one of the three independent matrix-element terms (for spin ≥ 3/2) because it does not contribute to the real photonic decay width and would require an unnaturally large coupling constant to be significant; they also show that the remaining two terms give nearly indistinguishable dilepton spectra when the resonance mass is close to its Breit-Wigner mass.

## Method

The authors construct the electromagnetic transition matrix element between a nucleon and a spin-\(J\) baryon resonance using generalized Rarita-Schwinger spinor-tensor fields for \(J \ge 3/2\) and standard Dirac spinors for \(J = 1/2\). They impose current conservation, the Dirac equation, and the Rarita-Schwinger constraints to reduce the number of independent form factors to three (for \(J \ge 3/2\)) or two (for \(J = 1/2\)). The differential Dalitz decay width is then obtained from the photonic decay width to a virtual photon via the standard relation \(d\Gamma/dM^2 = (\alpha/3\pi)(1/M^2)\Gamma_{R\to N\gamma}(M)\). The coupling constants are fixed by fitting to the real photonic decay widths of known resonances listed in the Review of Particle Physics.

* For the N(1680) resonance (spin-parity 5/2+), the fitted coupling constant for the \(p\gamma\) channel is \(g_1 = 2.74\) using a full width of 0.13 GeV and a photonic branching ratio of 0.27%.

## Context

This work extends the treatment of Dalitz decays beyond the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance, which was previously the only baryon resonance included in models of dielectron production in \(pp\) collisions (e.g., the Giessen and Frankfurt models cited in the paper). The derived expressions enable inclusion of higher-mass baryon resonances (spin \(\le 5/2\)) in transport models of heavy ion collisions, particularly relevant for the dilepton mass range near the \(\phi\) meson mass where the \(\rho^0\) and \(\Delta(1232)\) channels become negligible. The results are intended for use in interpreting data from the DLS collaboration at BEVALAC and future HADES measurements at GSI, and for assessing background contributions to \(\phi\) meson studies via the dilepton channel.
