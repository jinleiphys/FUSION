# Soft breathing modes in neutron--rich nuclei with the subtracted second random--phase approximation

> We analyze the isoscalar response related to breathing modes with particular attention being paid to low-lying excitations in neutron--rich nuclei. We use the subtracted second random--phase approximation (SSRPA) to describe microscopically the response. By increasing the neutron excess, we study the evolution of the response in Ca isotopes going from $^{40}$Ca to $^{48}$Ca and to $^{60}$Ca as well as in $N=20$ isotones going from $^{40}$Ca to $^{36}$S and to $^{34}$Si. Finally, the case of $^{68}$Ni is investigated. We predict soft monopole modes in neutron--rich nuclei which are driven by neutron excitations. At variance with dipole pygmy modes, these neutron excitations are not only strongly dominant at the surface of the nucleus but over its entire volume. The effect of the mixing with two particle-two hole configurations induced by the SSRPA model is analyzed. The properties of such soft neutron modes are investigated in terms of their excitation energies, transition densities and wave--function components. Their collectivity is also discussed as a function of the isospin asymmetry and of the mass of the nucleus. The link between such low--energy compression modes and a compressibility modulus introduced for neutron--rich infinite matter is finally studied.

## Key claim
The paper predicts the existence of soft monopole (breathing) modes in neutron-rich nuclei, driven predominantly by neutron excitations that extend over the entire nuclear volume rather than being confined to the surface. The authors qualify that these modes are distinct from dipole pygmy resonances and that their collectivity depends more strongly on neutron excess than on nuclear mass.

## Method
The authors employed the subtracted second random-phase approximation (SSRPA), a beyond-mean-field model that couples one particle-one hole (1p1h) configurations with two particle-two hole (2p2h) configurations, using the Skyrme parametrization SGII. The calculation scheme was fully self-consistent, with a cutoff of 80 MeV for 1p1h configurations and 60 MeV for 2p2h configurations. The isoscalar monopole response was computed for Ca isotopes (40Ca, 48Ca, 60Ca), N=20 isotones (40Ca, 36S, 34Si), and 68Ni, analyzing transition densities, wave-function components, and energy-weighted sum rule fractions.

## Key numbers
- For 40Ca, the SSRPA low-lying peak at 14.2 MeV has 2.13% of the isoscalar energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) up to 15 MeV, with 60% 2p2h contribution to the state norm.
- For 48Ca, the SSRPA peak at 13.8 MeV has 2.5% EWSR up to 15 MeV, dominated by the neutron 1p1h configuration [ν2f7/2, ν1f7/2]J=0.
- For 60Ca, the peak at 8.8 MeV has 5.13% EWSR up to 11 MeV, and the peak at 14.1 MeV has 26.81% EWSR up to 16 MeV with 86% 2p2h nature.
- For 34Si, the peak at 11.07 MeV has 4.5% EWSR up to 15 MeV; for 36S, the peak at 12.99 MeV has 3.15% EWSR up to 15 MeV.
- For 68Ni, the EWSR below 14 MeV is 5.37%, with the three main peaks at 11.02, 12.53, and 12.92 MeV having very weak 2p2h contributions (0.18%, 0.49%, and 0.29% of EWSR respectively).
- The SSRPA centroid energies for the giant monopole resonance are 20.7 MeV for 40Ca and 20.4 MeV for 48Ca, compared to experimental values of 18.3 and 19.0 MeV respectively.

## Context
This work extends previous mean-field predictions of soft monopole modes in neutron-rich Ca, Ni, Pb, and Sn isotopes (Capelli 2009, Khan 2011, Khan 2013) by incorporating beyond-mean-field effects through the SSRPA model. The study is motivated by experimental attempts to observe soft monopole excitations in 68Ni using active targets (Charlotte 2013, Marine 2015), which could not provide clear evidence. The results enable future experimental searches for these modes in neutron-rich nuclei, particularly in heavier systems where collectivity is enhanced, and establish a qualitative link between soft mode energies and the compressibility modulus of neutron-rich infinite matter.
