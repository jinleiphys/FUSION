# Three-particle finite-volume formalism for $π^+π^+ K^+$ and related systems

> We consider three-particle systems consisting of two identical particles and a third that is different, with all being spinless. Examples include $π^+π^+ K^+$ and $K^+K^+π^+$. We derive the formalism necessary to extract two- and three-particle infinite-volume scattering amplitudes from the spectrum of such systems in finite volume. We use a relativistic formalism based on an all-orders diagrammatic analysis in generic effective field theory, adopting the methodology used recently to study the case of three nondegenerate particles. We present both a direct derivation, and also a cross-check based on an appropriate limit and projection of the fully nondegenerate formalism. We also work out the threshold expansions for the three-particle K matrix that will be needed in practical applications, both for systems with two identical particles plus a third, and also for the fully nondegenerate theory.

## Key claim

The paper derives the finite-volume quantization condition for three-particle systems containing two identical spinless particles and a third distinct spinless particle (the "2+1" case), such as π⁺π⁺K⁺, extending the relativistic RFT formalism to systems where existing methods for three identical or fully nondegenerate particles do not apply. The derivation assumes that only a single three-particle channel can go on shell, which constrains the allowed center-of-momentum energy range depending on the particle masses and symmetries.

## Method

The authors use time-ordered perturbation theory within a generic relativistic effective field theory, following the diagrammatic approach developed in Blanton and Sharpe (2020) for nondegenerate particles and Blanton et al. (2020) for identical particles. They first derive an asymmetric form of the quantization condition containing a Lorentz-noninvariant three-particle K matrix, then convert it to a symmetric form with a Lorentz-invariant kernel using symmetrization identities. The results are expressed using two-dimensional flavor matrices corresponding to the two choices of spectator particle flavor. A cross-check is performed by taking the appropriate limit and isospin projection of the fully nondegenerate formalism for the π⁺π⁺Dₛ⁺ system.

## Key numbers

- The range of validity for the formalism when m₁ < m₂ and there are separate φⱼ → −φⱼ symmetries is m₂ < E* < 4m₁ + m₂, where E* is the center-of-momentum frame energy.
- The symmetry factor associated with each relevant cut in the 2+1 theory is 1/2! (one-half), compared to 1/3! for three identical particles.
- The two-particle kernel for spectator flavor 2 in the 2+1 theory contains a factor of 1/2 in the loop due to the symmetry of the two identical particles, as shown in Eq. (M2L22p1).
- The matrix form of the cut factor uses the vector α = (1, 1/√2) and its transpose, with the two-dimensional matrix for the two-particle kernel given as diag( B̄₂,L⁽¹⁾, ½ B̄₂,L⁽²⁾ ).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic finite-volume formalism (RFT approach) developed by Hansen and Sharpe (2014, 2015), Briceno et al. (2017, 2018), and Blanton et al. (2020) for three identical and fully nondegenerate particles to the phenomenologically important case of two identical particles plus a third distinct particle. The formalism enables lattice QCD calculations of systems such as π⁺π⁺K⁺, π⁺π⁺Dₛ⁺, and K⁺K⁺π⁺, which were previously inaccessible except for the recent nonrelativistic EFT treatment of the DDK system by Pang et al. (2020). The threshold expansions provided for the three-particle K matrix are the analog of the effective range expansion for two-particle scattering and are needed for practical applications to nonresonant systems.
