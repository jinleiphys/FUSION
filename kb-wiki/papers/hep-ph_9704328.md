# Color Anomaly and Flavor-Singlet Axial Charge of the Proton in the Chiral Bag: The Cheshire Cat Revisited

> Quantum effects inside the chiral bag induce a color anomaly which requires a compensating surface term to prevent breakdown of color gauge invariance. We show that the presence of this surface term first discovered several years ago allows one to derive in a gauge-invariant way a chiral-bag version of the Shore-Veneziano two-component formula for the flavor-singlet axial charge of the proton. This has relevance to what is referred to as the ``proton spin problem" on the one hand and to the Cheshire-Cat phenomenon in hadron structure on the other. We show that when calculated to the leading order in the color gauge coupling and for a specific color electric monopole configuration in the bag, one can obtain a striking Cheshire-Cat phenomenon with a negligibly small singlet axial charge.

# Color Anomaly and Flavor-Singlet Axial Charge of the Proton in the Chiral Bag: The Cheshire Cat Revisited

## Key claim

The authors show that a color anomaly induced by quantum effects inside the chiral bag, when compensated by a specific surface counter term (the NRWZ counter term), allows a gauge-invariant derivation of a chiral-bag version of the Shore-Veneziano two-component formula for the flavor-singlet axial charge of the proton. When calculated to leading order in the color gauge coupling and for a specific color electric monopole configuration with the boundary condition satisfied locally (Gamma=R), the model yields a negligibly small singlet axial charge consistent with the Cheshire Cat phenomenon, but this result depends critically on the choice of the lower limit Gamma in the electric field definition.

## Method

The authors construct the flavor-singlet axial current (FSAC) in the chiral bag model as a sum of a quark-plus-eta-prime contribution inside and outside the bag and a gluon contribution, imposing gauge invariance via the NRWZ surface counter term that cancels gauge-non-invariant terms from the Chern-Simons current. They evaluate the quark contribution using bagged quark wave functions with hedgehog pion Casimir effects, the eta-prime contribution via a static-source approximation with a Goldberger-Treiman-like relation from the boundary condition, and the gluon contribution to lowest order in alpha_s using color electric and magnetic fields in the MIT bag model with two choices for the monopole field lower limit (Gamma=0 and Gamma=R).

## Key numbers

- For bag radius R=0.6 fm and chiral angle theta/pi=-0.383, the quark-plus-eta-prime contribution g_{A_1}^0 = 0.321, the gluon contribution with Gamma=R is g_{A_2}^0 = -0.236, giving total g_A^0 = 0.085.
- For R=0.6 fm with Gamma=0, the gluon contribution is g_{A_2}^0 = 0.123, giving total g_A^0 = 0.444.
- For R=1.0 fm and theta/pi=-0.194, the total g_A^0 with Gamma=R is 0.040, while with Gamma=0 it is 1.027.
- In the MIT bag limit (R=infinity, no pions), the total g_A^0 with Gamma=R is -0.297, and with Gamma=0 is 1.784.
- The eta-prime mass is taken as m_eta = 958 MeV, the pion decay constant f = 93 MeV, and alpha_s = 2.2.
- The empirical values quoted for the flavor-singlet structure function Delta-Sigma are 0.27 +/- 0.04 from one analysis and 0.10 +/- 0.05 (exp) +/- 0.17/0.11 (th) from another.

## Context

This work extends the Shore-Veneziano two-component formula (Shore and Veneziano, 1990s) for the flavor-singlet axial charge to the chiral bag model, correcting a conceptual error in earlier work by the same authors with Park and Brown (Park, Rho, and Brown, 1990s) where the Chern-Simons current was introduced inside the bag without proper gauge-invariant treatment. The results contradict Dreiner, Ellis, and Flores (Dreiner, Ellis, and Flores, 1990s), who obtained a large singlet axial charge by ignoring perturbative gluon effects inside the bag; the present work shows that the Dreiner-Ellis-Flores scenario corresponds to the Gamma=0 configuration which breaks the Cheshire Cat. The calculation enables future full Casimir calculations and suggests that the singlet axial charge being small arises from cancellation between matter and gluon contributions, with implications for the "proton spin problem" and the Cheshire Cat principle in hadron structure.
