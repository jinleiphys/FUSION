# Study of the alpha-particle monopole transition form factor

> The 4He monopole form factor is studied by computing the transition matrix element of the electromagnetic charge operator between the 4He ground-state and the p+3H and n+3He scattering states. The nuclear wave functions are calculated using the hyperspherical harmonic method, by starting from Hamiltonians including two- and three-body forces derived in chiral effective field theory. The electromagnetic charge operator retains, beyond the leading order (impulse approximation) term, also higher order contributions, as relativistic corrections and meson-exchange currents. The results for the monopole form factor are in fairly agreement with recent MAMI data. Comparison with other theoretical calculations are also provided.

## Key claim

The monopole form factor of 4He, computed from the transition matrix element between the ground state and the p+3H and n+3He scattering states using chiral Hamiltonians with two- and three-body forces, shows fairly good agreement with recent MAMI data, though the agreement depends on the specific Hamiltonian and the inclusion of higher-order contributions to the charge operator beyond the impulse approximation.

## Method

The authors computed the 4He monopole form factor by evaluating the transition matrix element of the electromagnetic charge operator between the 4He ground state and the p+3H and n+3He scattering states. Nuclear wave functions were obtained using the hyperspherical harmonic (HH) method with Hamiltonians including two- and three-nucleon forces derived from chiral effective field theory at N3LO (NN) and N2LO (3N). The charge operator included, beyond the leading-order impulse approximation, relativistic corrections and meson-exchange currents derived within chiral effective field theory.

## Key numbers

- The first excited 0+ state of 4He lies approximately 20 MeV above the ground state, below the opening of the n+3He channel.
- The MAMI experiment used electron beams with energies of 450, 690, and 795 MeV directed at cryogenic helium gas.
- The proton form factor used in the calculation is f_p(q) = 1/(1 + 0.056 q^2)^2, with q given in fm^-1.
- The recoil factor f_R was approximated to 1, with a worst-case value of approximately 0.99 for a 795 MeV beam at a scattering angle of 18.3 degrees.
- The monopole form factor is defined as |F_M(q)|^2 = (1/16π) Σ_γ ∫ dE_γ 8 p_γ μ_γ |C_0^000(q,E_γ)|^2, summing over the p+3H and n+3He channels.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of the 4He monopole form factor, including calculations using bound-state techniques with Gaussian bases (Hiyama, 2004), the Lorentz Integral Transform method (Bacca, 2013, 2014; Kegel, 2023), the no-core Gamow shell model (Michel, 2023), and nuclear lattice effective field theory (M, 2023). The present calculation differs by using explicit four-nucleon scattering wave functions computed with the HH method and modern chiral Hamiltonians, following the experimental technique where electrons scatter 4He nuclei into final states composed of different clusters. This approach enables direct comparison with the precise MAMI data and can be extended to study other reactions involving four-nucleon systems.
