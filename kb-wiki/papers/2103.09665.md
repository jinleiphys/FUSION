# Hadronic Rescattering in pA and AA Collisions

> In a recent article we presented a model for hadronic rescattering, and some results were shown for pp collisions at LHC energies. In order to extend the studies to pA and AA collisions, the Angantyr model for heavy-ion collisions is taken as the starting point. Both these models are implemented within the general-purpose Monte Carlo event generator Pythia, which makes the matching reasonably straightforward, and allows for detailed studies of the full space--time evolution. The rescattering rate is significantly higher than in pp, especially for central AA collisions, where the typical primary hadron rescatters several times. We study the impact of rescattering on a number of distributions, such as pT and eta spectra, and the space--time evolution of the whole collision process. Notably rescattering is shown to give a significant contribution to elliptic flow in XeXe and PbPb, and to give a nontrivial impact on charm production.

## Key claim

Hadronic rescattering in pA and AA collisions produces significantly larger effects than in pp collisions, with the number of rescatterings rising faster than particle multiplicity, and can generate elliptic flow comparable to experimental PbPb data at large multiplicities, even without invoking a Quark-Gluon Plasma phase.

## Method

The Angantyr model for heavy-ion collisions is used to set up the nuclear collision geometry and select colliding nucleon-nucleon pairs, with each nucleon-nucleon collision modeled using the Multiparton Interactions framework in Pythia. The resulting strings are fragmented using the Lund string fragmentation model, which provides space-time production vertices for each primary hadron. A native hadronic rescattering framework implemented directly in Pythia then checks all hadron pairs for potential interactions, orders them by time, and simulates scatterings and decays iteratively until no further interactions remain.

## Key numbers

- In central PbPb collisions, the typical primary hadron rescatters several times, with the fraction of not-rescattered hadrons being small
- The string tension used in hadronization is approximately 1 GeV/fm
- Most rescatterings occur at low invariant masses, typically only a few GeV, with the number of interactions above 10 GeV being small enough to disregard
- Rescattering gives a significant contribution to elliptic flow in XeXe and PbPb collisions, matching experimental PbPb numbers at large multiplicities
- The model shows a clear centrality dependence on particle production rates, including a J/psi depletion in central collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous hadronic rescattering model for pp collisions (Sjostrand 2020) to pA and AA collisions using the Angantyr framework (Bierlich 2016, 2018). It provides an alternative to QGP-based approaches like the core-corona model in EPOS (Werner 2007, Pierog 2013) and differs from traditional afterburner approaches using UrQMD (Bass 1998) or SMASH (Weil 2016) by having no QGP phase, allowing rescattering to start earlier. The framework enables detailed studies of whether some signals attributed to QGP formation might have alternative explanations, and provides a straw-man model for sharpening experimental tests.
