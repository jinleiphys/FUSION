# Impact of Quarks and Pions on Dynamics and Neutrino Signal of Black Hole Formation in Non-rotating Stellar Core Collapse

> In the formation process of black holes, the density and temperature of matter become sufficiently high for quarks and pions to appear. In this study we numerically investigate stellar core collapse and black hole formation taking into account the equations of state involving quarks and/or pions. In our simulations, we utilize a code that solves the general relativistic hydrodynamics and neutrino transfer equations simultaneously, treating neutrino reactions in detail under spherical symmetry. Initial models with three different masses, namely, 40, 100 and 375Msolar, are adopted. Our results show that quarks and pions shorten the duration of neutrino emission if the collapse bounces before black hole formation. In addition, pions increase the luminosity and average energy of neutrinos before black hole formation. We also find that the hadron-quark phase transition leads to an interesting evolution of temperature. Moreover, the neutrino event number is evaluated for the currently operating neutrino detector, SuperKamiokande, to confirm that it is not only detectable but also affected by the emergence of quarks and pions for Galactic events. While there are some issues, such as hyperons, beyond the scope of this study, this is the first serious attempt to assess the impact of quarks and pions in dynamical simulations of black hole formation and will serve as an important foundation for future studies.

## Key claim

Quarks and pions shorten the duration of neutrino emission and pions increase the luminosity and average energy of neutrinos before black hole formation, but only if the collapsing core bounces before black hole formation; for stars that collapse directly to a black hole without a bounce, quarks and pions have a limited effect on the observable neutrino signal.

## Method

The authors performed spherically symmetric general relativistic hydrodynamic simulations coupled with detailed neutrino Boltzmann transport, using equations of state (EOS) that include hadronic matter with or without thermal pions and a hadron-quark mixed phase via the MIT bag model with bag constant B = 250 MeV fm⁻³. They simulated the collapse of three progenitor models: a 40 M☉ solar-metallicity star, a 100 M☉ Population III star, and a 375 M☉ Population III star, comparing four EOS variants (OO: no pions/no quarks; OQ: quarks only; PO: pions only; PQ: both pions and quarks).

## Key numbers

- For the 40 M☉ model, the time interval from bounce to black hole formation is 1.049 s (PQ), 1.086 s (OQ), 1.145 s (PO), and 1.345 s (OO), showing that quarks and pions shorten this interval.
- The maximum masses of cold neutron stars for the four EOS variants are 1.8 M☉ (PQ), 1.8 M☉ (OQ), 2.0 M☉ (PO), and 2.2 M☉ (OO).
- For the 40 M☉ model with EOS PQ, the central density at the time of black hole formation reaches 1.5 × 10¹⁶ g cm⁻³.
- The central density at bounce is ~3.2 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³ for the 40 M☉ models and ~2 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³ for the 100 M☉ models.
- The central entropy per baryon is ~1 kB for the 40 M☉ star and ~3.5 kB for the 100 M☉ star.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Self08a) on the collapse of a 100 M☉ star with quark/pion EOS to a broader mass range (40, 100, and 375 M☉), and builds on earlier simulations of black hole formation without exotic matter by Sumiyoshi (2007, 2008) and Fischer (2009). The results show that for stars that bounce (40 and 100 M☉), quarks and pions significantly alter the neutrino signal, while for the 375 M☉ star that collapses directly, the earlier results of Self06 remain valid. The study does not include hyperons, which have been shown important by Ishizuka (2008) and Sumiyoshi (2009), and treats pions in a maximum-production approximation, so future work should address these limitations.
