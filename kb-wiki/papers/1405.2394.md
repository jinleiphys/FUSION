# $^6$He nucleus in halo effective field theory

> Background: In recent years properties of light rare isotopes have been measured with high accuracy. At the same time, the theoretical description of light nuclei has made enormous progress, and properties of, e.g., the Helium isotopes can now be calculated {\it ab initio}. These advances make those rare isotopes an ideal testing ground for effective field theories (EFTs) built upon cluster degrees of freedom. Purpose: Systems with widely separated intrinsic scales are well suited to an EFT treatment. The Borromean halo nucleus $^6$He exhibits such a separation of scales. In this work an EFT in which the degrees of freedom are the valence neutrons ($n$) and an inert $^4$He-core ($α$) is employed. The properties of ${}^6$He can then be calculated using the momentum-space Faddeev equations for the $αnn$ bound state to obtain information on ${}^6$He at leading order (LO) within the EFT. Results: The $nn$ virtual state and the $^2$P$_{3/2}$ resonance in $^5$He give the two-body amplitudes which are input to our LO three-body Halo EFT calculation. We find that without a genuine three-body interaction the two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}$ of ${}^6$He is strongly cutoff dependent. We introduce a $nn α$ "three-body" operator which renormalizes the system, adjusting its coefficient to reproduce the $S_{2n}$ of $^6$He. The Faddeev components are then cutoff independent for cutoffs of the order of, and above, the breakdown scale of the Halo EFT. Conclusions: As in the case of a three-body system where only resonant s-wave interactions are present, one three-body input is required for the renormalization of the EFT equations that describe $^6$He at LO. However, in contrast to the s-wave-only case, the running of the LO $nnα$ counterterm does not exhibit discrete scale invariance, due to the presence of the p-wave $nα$ interaction.

## Key claim

The two-neutron separation energy of $^6$He cannot be predicted from two-body input alone in leading-order halo EFT; a three-body contact interaction is mandatory to renormalize the cutoff dependence, and the running of this three-body counterterm does not exhibit discrete scale invariance because of the p-wave nature of the n-alpha interaction.

## Method

The authors constructed a leading-order halo effective field theory with an inert $^4$He core and two valence neutrons, using momentum-space Faddeev equations for the alpha-n-n bound state. The two-body input consisted of the s-wave nn virtual state (scattering length $a_0=-18.7$ fm) and the p-wave $^2$P$_{3/2}$ n-alpha resonance (scattering volume $a_1=-62.951$ fm$^3$, effective range $r_1=-0.8819$ fm$^{-1}$), with the n-alpha interaction treated under the narrow-resonance power counting of Bedaque et al. (2003). The three-body equations were regularized with a hard momentum cutoff and renormalized by adjusting the coefficient of a nn-alpha contact interaction to reproduce the experimental two-neutron separation energy.

## Key numbers

* Two-neutron separation energy of $^6$He: $S_{2n} = 0.975$ MeV (experimental value used to fix the three-body counterterm).
* nn scattering length: $a_0 = -18.7$ fm; nn effective range: $r_0 = 2.75$ fm.
* n-alpha $^2$P$_{3/2}$ scattering volume: $a_1 = -62.951$ fm$^3$; n-alpha effective range: $r_1 = -0.8819$ fm$^{-1}$.
* Low-momentum scale: $M_{lo} = \sqrt{m_N S_{2n}} \approx 30$ MeV; high-momentum scale: $M_{hi} \approx \sqrt{m_N E^*_\alpha} = 140$ MeV, where $E^*_\alpha \approx 20$ MeV is the excitation energy of $^4$He.

## Context

This work extends the halo EFT framework, previously applied to s-wave two-neutron halos by Canham and Hammer (2008, 2009), to the Borromean nucleus $^6$He where the n-alpha interaction is dominated by a p-wave resonance. It contrasts with the approach of Rotureau and van Kolck (2012), who used the alternative power counting of Bertulani et al. (2002) with two fine-tunings in the p-wave, and with the work of Braaten et al. (2011) who studied a p-wave unitary limit that the authors argue is unphysical. The results enable systematic calculations of $^6$He properties within a controlled EFT expansion and provide a benchmark for comparing cluster EFT descriptions with ab initio methods.
