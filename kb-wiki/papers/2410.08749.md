# Entanglement and coherence of the wobbling mode

> The entanglement and coherence of the wobbling mode are studied in the framework of the particle plus triaxial rotor model for the one-quasiparticle nucleus $^{135}$Pr and the two-quasiparticles nucleus $^{130}$Ba. The focus lies on the coupling between the total and the particle angular momenta. Using the Schmidt decomposing, it is quantified in terms of the von Neumann entropy of the respective sub-systems, which measures their mutual entanglement. The entropy and the entanglement increase with spin $I$ and number of wobbling quanta $n$. The coherence of the wobbling mode is studied by means of the eigenstate decomposition of its reduced density matrix. To a good approximation, the probability distributions of the total angular momentum can be interpreted as the incoherent combination of the coherent contributions from the first two pairs of eigenvectors with the largest weight of the reduced density matrix. Decoherence measures are defined, which, in accordance, scatter between 0.1 to 0.2 at low spin and between 0.1 and 0.3 at high spin. Entanglement in the framework of the adiabatic approximation is further analyzed. In general, the coherent eigenstates of the effective collective Hamiltonian approximate the reduced density matrix with the limited accuracy of its pair of eigenstates with the largest weight. As the adiabatic approximation becomes more accurate with decreasing excitation energy, the probability distribution of the angle of the total angular momentum around a principal axis approaches the one of the full reduced density matrix. The $E2$ transition probabilities and spectroscopic quadrupole moments reflect this trend.

## Key claim

The entanglement between the particle and rotor subsystems in wobbling nuclei, quantified by von Neumann entropy, increases with total spin \(I\) and wobbling quantum number \(n\), but the coherence of the wobbling mode is partially preserved because the probability distributions of total angular momentum can be approximated as an incoherent combination of coherent contributions from only the first two eigenvector pairs of the reduced density matrix.

## Method

The particle plus triaxial rotor (PTR) model was diagonalized in a product basis of rotor states \(|IIK\rangle\) and high-\(j\) particle states \(|jk\rangle\) for the one-quasiparticle nucleus \(^{135}\)Pr and the two-quasiparticle nucleus \(^{130}\)Ba. The Schmidt decomposition of the eigenstates yielded reduced density matrices for the particle angular momentum \(\bm{j}\) and total angular momentum \(\bm{J}\) subsystems. Entanglement was quantified via the von Neumann entropy \(S = -\sum_m p_m \ln p_m\) of these reduced density matrices, and coherence was assessed by comparing the probability distributions \(P(K)\) and \(P(\phi)\) from the full density matrix with those from its normalized square, as well as by the \(l_1\) norm of off-diagonal matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{135}\)Pr, the von Neumann entropy \(S\) (in units of maximal entropy \(\ln 12\)) ranges from approximately 0.28 (the minimal entropy \(\ln 2 / \ln 12\)) at low spin to higher values at high spin, with the purity scattering between 0.1 and 0.2 at low spin and between 0.1 and 0.3 at high spin.
- The decoherence measure \(\Delta_K\) (absolute deviation between \(P(K)\) and \(P_2(K)\)) and \(\Delta_{\text{SSS}}\) (same for the spin squeezed state representation) scatter between 0.1 to 0.2 at low spin and between 0.1 and 0.3 at high spin for \(^{135}\)Pr.
- The PTR parameters for \(^{135}\)Pr are \(\beta = 0.17\), \(\gamma = -26^\circ\), and moments of inertia \(\mathcal{J}_{m,s,l} = 21, 13, 4~\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\) for the medium, short, and long axes, respectively.
- For \(^{130}\)Ba, the deformation parameters are \(\beta = 0.24\), \(\gamma = 21.5^\circ\), with spin-dependent moments of inertia \(\mathcal{J}_i = \Theta_i(1 + cI)\) where \(\Theta_{s,m,l} = 1.09, 1.50, 0.65~\hbar^2/\text{MeV}\) and \(c = 0.59\).

## Context

This work extends the topological classification of wobbling motion into transverse wobbling (TW) and longitudinal wobbling (LW) previously developed by Frauendorf and Donau (2014) and Chen and Frauendorf (2022, 2024) using spin coherent state maps and spin squeezed state plots. By applying quantum information concepts (von Neumann entropy, purity, coherence measures) to the PTR model, the authors quantify the degree to which the particle-rotor coupling causes information loss and decoherence, and they test the validity of the adiabatic approximation where the system is described by an effective collective Hamiltonian. This enables future studies to assess how well simplified collective models capture the full quantum dynamics of wobbling nuclei.
