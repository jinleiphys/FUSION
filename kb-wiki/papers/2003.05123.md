# Investigation of contributions of the $2_2^+$ resonance in $^6$He via analysis of $^6$He($p$, $p'$)

> We investigate the contribution of the $2^{+}_{2}$ resonance in $^6$He to observables via analysis of the $^6$He($p,p'$) reaction by using the continuum-discretized coupled channels method combined with the complex-scaling method. In this study, we obtain the $2^{+}_{2}$ state with the resonant energy 2.25 MeV and the decay width 3.75 MeV and analyse contributions of resonances and nonresonant continuum states to the cross section separately. It is found that the $2^{+}_{2}$ state plays an important role in the energy spectrum. Furthermore, contributions of nonresonant continuum states are also important to clarify the properties of the $2^{+}_{2}$ state.

## Key claim

The $2_2^+$ resonance in $^6$He, with resonant energy 2.25 MeV and decay width 3.75 MeV, contributes to the breakup cross section as a shoulder peak rather than a distinct peak, and its extracted properties are significantly affected if nonresonant continuum contributions are neglected.

## Method

The authors used the continuum-discretized coupled channels method (CDCC) combined with the complex-scaling method (CSM) to analyze the $^6$He($p,p'$) reaction as a $^4$He + $n$ + $n$ + $p$ four-body system. The internal Hamiltonian of $^6$He employed the Minnesota interaction for $n$-$n$ and the KKNN potential for $n$-$^4$He, with a phenomenological three-body potential to reproduce ground and $2^+_1$ state energies. The Jeukenne-Lejeune-Mahaux (JLM) effective NN interaction was used for the projectile-target interaction, with antisymmetrization treated via the Kerman-McManus-Thaler (KMT) theory.

## Key numbers

- The $2_2^+$ state in $^6$He has resonant energy $E_r = 2.25$ MeV and decay width $\Gamma = 3.75$ MeV, obtained via CSM.
- The $2_1^+$ state has $E_r = 0.848$ MeV and $\Gamma = 0.136$ MeV.
- The ground state energy of $^6$He was obtained as $-0.972$ MeV.
- Nonresonant continuum contributions account for about 30% of the total inelastic cross section integrated over $\varepsilon$ up to 1.5 MeV at $E/A_{\rm{P}} = 41$ MeV.
- Fitting the breakup cross section with Breit-Wigner functions neglecting nonresonant continuum yields $E_r = 1.71$ MeV and $\Gamma = 1.97$ MeV for the $2_2^+$ state (fit up to 3 MeV), and $E_r = 1.92$ MeV and $\Gamma = 2.72$ MeV (fit up to 4 MeV), both smaller than the CSM values.

## Context

This work extends the CDCC+CSM approach previously applied to $^{11}$Li($p,p'$) reactions by Matsumoto et al. (2019) to the case of $^6$He. The calculated $2_2^+$ resonance parameters are consistent with structural calculations by Myo et al. (2007). The analysis shows that the $2_2^+$ state, which does not produce a clear peak in experimental spectra, can be identified through the increase in cross section around $\varepsilon = 2$ MeV, similar to observations in $^8$He($p,t$)$^6$He reactions by Mougeot et al. (2012). The work demonstrates that accurate determination of the $2_2^+$ properties requires treating both resonant and nonresonant continuum contributions, and calls for new experimental data.
