# Testing chiral unitary models for the $Λ(1405)$ in $K^{+}πΣ$ photoproduction

> We adopt a novel formalism for the low-energy analysis of the $γp \to K^{+}πΣ$ photoproduction reaction to calculate the $πΣ$ invariant mass distributions in the $Λ(1405)$ resonance region. The approach adheres to constraints arising from unitarity, gauge invariance and chiral perturbation theory, and is used without adjusting any model parameters. It is found that the meson-baryon rescattering in the final state has a major impact on the magnitude and structure of the generated spectra that are compared with the experimental data from the CLAS collaboration. We demonstrate a large sensitivity of the theoretical predictions to the choice of the coupled-channel $πΣ- \bar{K}N$ model amplitudes which should enable one to constrain the parameter space of these models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the calculated $\pi\Sigma$ invariant mass distributions in $\gamma p \to K^{+}\pi\Sigma$ photoproduction are highly sensitive to the choice of the coupled-channel $\pi\Sigma - \bar{K}N$ model amplitudes used for the final-state interaction, but the predictions are made without adjusting any model parameters, serving only as a test of existing models rather than a new determination of the $\Lambda(1405)$ parameters.

## Method

The authors adopt a novel formalism for the $\gamma p \to K^{+}\pi\Sigma$ reaction that respects unitarity, gauge invariance, and chiral perturbation theory constraints, constructing the photoproduction amplitude from 20 tree-level diagrams (Weinberg-Tomozawa, Born, and anomalous graphs) at leading order. The final-state interaction of the $S=-1$ meson-baryon pair is implemented via a coupled-channel unitarization scheme that uses pre-existing $f_{0+}$ scattering amplitudes from four different $\bar{K}N$ models (Prague model P, Bonn models B2, B4, and BW) without any adjustment of their parameters. The s-wave projected photoproduction amplitudes are then used to compute double-differential cross sections for comparison with CLAS experimental data at center-of-mass energies $W = 2.0$ and $2.4$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- At $W = 2.0$ GeV, the kaon momentum is $|\vec{q}_{K}|(W=2\,\mathrm{GeV}, M_{\pi\Sigma}=1.35\,\mathrm{GeV}) \approx 350$ MeV, considered reasonable for three-flavor chiral perturbation theory
- At $W = 2.4$ GeV, the kaon momentum is $|\vec{q}_{K}| \approx 717$ MeV, deemed too large for the leading-order chiral perturbation theory photoproduction kernel
- The matching relation between regularization scales gives $\alpha_{\pi\Sigma} \approx 460$ MeV and $\alpha_{\bar{K}N} \approx 715$ MeV for a natural dimensional regularization scale $\mu \approx 1$ GeV
- The regularization scale modification study shows that $\alpha \approx 400$ MeV or $\mu \approx 1.0$ GeV produces $\pi\Sigma$ mass distribution magnitudes comparable to CLAS data
- The peak cross sections predicted by several models reach approximately $5-10\;\mu\mathrm{b/GeV}$, consistent with earlier predictions by Nacher (1998) and Lutz (2004)

## Context

This work extends the formalism developed by Bruns (2020) for two-meson photoproduction and tests it against CLAS experimental data from Moriya (2013). The approach contrasts with earlier phenomenological treatments by Roca (2013) and Mai (2014) that used simplified energy-dependent constants for the photoproduction part, and with the fits by Mai (2014) that included the CLAS photoproduction data directly in the parameter determination. The results demonstrate that the current formalism can discriminate between different $\bar{K}N$ coupled-channel models (Prague model by Bruns (2021) and Bonn models by Mai (2014) and Sadasivan (2018)), and the sensitivity to the loop function regularization suggests that future combined fits to both $K^{-}p$ reaction data and photoproduction data could constrain the parameter space of these models.
