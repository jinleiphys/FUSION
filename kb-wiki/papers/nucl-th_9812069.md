# Improved Cascade-Exciton Model of Nuclear Reactions

> Recent improvements to the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) of nuclear reactions are briefly described. They concern mainly the cascade stage of reactions and a better description of nuclei during the preequilibrium and evaporation stages of reactions. The development of the CEM concerning fission is given in a separate talk at this conference. The increased accuracy and predictive power of the CEM are shown by several examples. Possible further improvements to the CEM and other models are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors report incremental improvements to the Cascade-Exciton Model (CEM) that enhance its accuracy for nuclear reactions, but they caution that many results remain preliminary and that the work is not yet complete; the improved model (CEM97a) still shows discrepancies with some data by more than a factor of two, and the authors have not yet fixed several key parameters.

## Method

The authors modified the Dubna intranuclear cascade (INC) stage by updating elementary cross-section approximations for 34 types of nucleon, pion, and gamma interactions using a new compilation of experimental data. They tested the effects of including or ignoring refraction/reflection at nuclear potential boundaries, varied the pion-nucleus potential depth, introduced pion transparency distances, and revised pion absorption cross sections on nucleon pairs using isospin-dependent ratios and an A-dependent scaling factor W. They also incorporated an experimental mass table extended by Moller-Nix calculations, new level-density approximations, and improved treatment of rotational energy and fission barriers.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear matter density uses a Fermi distribution with parameters c = 1.07 A^(1/3) fm and a = 0.545 fm.
- The mean binding energy of nucleons epsilon is approximately 7 MeV.
- The standard Dubna INC uses a square-well pion-nucleus potential depth V_pi = 25 MeV.
- The effective real potential for incident nucleons is V_N(r) = T_F(r) + epsilon, where T_F(r) is the local Fermi energy.
- The authors analyzed more than 600 excitation functions for proton-induced reactions on 19 targets from C to Au at energies from 10 MeV to 5 GeV.
- For pion absorption on nuclear pairs, the standard CEM95 uses W = 4 in the relation sigma(pi- + "np" -> nn) = W * sigma(pi- + d -> nn).
- Using W = 2 for light targets like C, W = 3 for medium targets like Fe and Ni, and W = 4 for heavy targets like Pb significantly improved agreement with neutron and proton spectra at backward angles around 100-200 MeV.
- For Xe isotope production from p+133Cs, the difference between CEM97a and CEM95 predictions reaches a factor of 3 for 120Xe.
- The upper recommended energy for the present version of the CEM is 5 GeV, but the new elementary cross-section approximations are reliable up to 50-100 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the Cascade-Exciton Model developed at JINR, Dubna over the previous two decades, which had been used to analyze reactions induced by nucleons, pions, and photons. The improvements respond to findings from the International Code Comparison for Intermediate Energy Nuclear Data (1994), which showed CEM had good predictive power for double differential cross sections but left room for improvement. The work is motivated by needs for accurate nuclear data for Accelerator Transmutation of Waste (ATW) and Accelerator Production of Tritium (APT) projects. The authors compare their results with calculations from LAHET (Bertini INC), the Liege INC model by Cugnon et al. (1997), HETC-KFAII, HMS-ALICE, and INUCL. The improved CEM97a code has been incorporated into the MCNPX transport code.
