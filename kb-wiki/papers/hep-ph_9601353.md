# Light Baryons in a Constituent Quark Model with Chiral Dynamics

> It is shown from rigorous three-body Faddeev calculations that the masses of all 14 lowest states in the $N$ and $Δ$ spectra can be described within a constituent quark model with a Goldstone-boson-exchange interaction plus linear confinement between the constituent quarks.

## Key claim
The paper claims that rigorous three-body Faddeev calculations show the masses of all 14 lowest states in the N and Δ spectra can be described by a constituent quark model with Goldstone-boson-exchange (GBE) interaction plus linear confinement, using only four free parameters. The hedge is that tensor forces and fine-structure splittings in LS-multiplets are not yet included, and the Yukawa part of the interaction is only of secondary importance.

## Method
The authors solved the three-body Faddeev equations for baryon bound states using a quark-quark potential consisting of linear confinement plus a spin-spin component from octet and singlet pseudoscalar meson exchange (π, K, η, η′). The δ-function part of the meson-exchange potential was smeared with a Gaussian of range parameter α = 2.91 fm⁻¹ and a cut-off radius r₀ = 0.43 fm, while the Yukawa part was cut off at r₀ using a step function. The octet coupling constant was fixed from the phenomenological pion-nucleon coupling, while the singlet coupling and confinement strength were treated as free parameters.

## Key numbers
- Nucleon ground state mass: 939 MeV (absolute value reproduced without an arbitrary constant V₀)
- Free parameters: r₀ = 0.43 fm, α = 2.91 fm⁻¹, (g₀/g₈)² = 1.8, C = 0.474 fm⁻²
- Constituent quark mass: m = 340 MeV (from nucleon magnetic moments)
- Meson masses used: μ_π = 139 MeV, μ_η = 547 MeV, μ_η′ = 958 MeV
- Octet coupling constant: g₈²/4π = 0.67
- Nucleon root mean square matter radius: √⟨r_N²⟩ = 0.465 fm
- Delta root mean square matter radius: √⟨r_Δ²⟩ = 0.54 fm
- Maximum deviation from experimental values: does not exceed 3% for any of the 14 states

## Context
This work extends the GBE model proposed by Glozman and collaborators (1995-1996) from first-order perturbation theory to rigorous Faddeev calculations, confirming that the spin-flavor symmetry of GBE resolves long-standing problems in baryon spectroscopy, particularly the ordering of positive- and negative-parity states. The authors contrast their approach with one-gluon-exchange (OGE) models, arguing that OGE with a coupling α_s > 0.2-0.3 would spoil the spectra, and cite lattice QCD results by Liu and collaborators showing that N-Δ splitting disappears in the valence approximation, supporting the role of qq̅ excitations over gluon exchange. The work enables future inclusion of tensor forces and extension to strange baryon spectra.
