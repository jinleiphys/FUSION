# Analytic structure and power-series expansion of the Jost function for the two-dimensional problem

> For a two-dimensional quantum mechanical problem, we obtain a generalized power-series expansion of the S-matrix that can be done near an arbitrary point on the Riemann surface of the energy, similarly to the standard effective range expansion. In order to do this, we consider the Jost-function and analytically factorize its momentum dependence that causes the Jost function to be a multi-valued function. The remaining single-valued function of the energy is then expanded in the power-series near an arbitrary point in the complex energy plane. A systematic and accurate procedure has been developed for calculating the expansion coefficients. This makes it possible to obtain a semi-analytic expression for the Jost-function (and therefore for the S-matrix) near an arbitrary point on the Riemann surface and use it, for example, to locate the spectral points (bound and resonant states) as the S-matrix poles. The method is applied to a model simlar to those used in the theory of quantum dots.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized power-series expansion of the Jost function (and therefore the S-matrix) for two-dimensional quantum scattering that can be performed near an arbitrary point on the Riemann surface of the energy, not only at threshold. The expansion is valid within a domain of the complex energy plane determined by the exponential decay rate of the potential, and the method explicitly separates the logarithmic branching point at threshold from the single-valued analytic part of the Jost function.

## Method

The authors start from the radial Schrödinger equation for a circularly symmetric two-dimensional potential and express the wave function in terms of Riccati-Bessel and Riccati-Neumann functions. They use the variation of parameters method to derive first-order differential equations for the Jost functions, then factor out the explicit momentum dependence (including the logarithmic term \(h(k) = \frac{2}{\pi}\ln(kR/2)\) for half-integer \(\lambda\)) to obtain "tilded" functions \(\tilde{A}_\ell(E,r)\) and \(\tilde{B}_\ell(E,r)\) that are entire functions of the energy \(E\). These tilded functions are expanded in Taylor series around an arbitrary complex energy \(E_0\), and the expansion coefficients are obtained by solving a system of coupled first-order differential equations derived from the original equations, integrated from \(r=0\) to a large radius.

## Key numbers

- The analyticity domain \(\mathcal{D}\) for the tilded functions is defined by \(|\mathrm{Im}\,E|^2 < \frac{\hbar^4\eta^4}{16\mu^2} + \frac{\hbar^2\eta^2}{2\mu}\mathrm{Re}\,E\), where \(\eta\) is the exponential decay rate of the potential \(V(r) \sim \exp(-\eta r)\).
- For the model potential used in the example, the domain boundary crosses the real axis at \(E \approx -0.0332\) (in donor Hartree units).
- With complex rotation angle \(\theta = 0.05\pi\), the extended domain is given by \(\sin^2(\chi/2 + \theta) < \hbar^2\eta^2\cos^2\theta / (8\mu|E|)\) for \(E = |E|e^{i\chi}\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach for three-dimensional single-channel and multi-channel problems (Rakityansky and Elander, 2009 and 2011) to the two-dimensional case, where the threshold has a logarithmic rather than square-root branching point. It resolves a controversy in the literature regarding the two-dimensional effective range expansion: some authors (Verhaar et al., Hammer) include the scattering length inside the logarithmic term, while others (Bolle et al.) move the logarithmic term to the left-hand side. The method enables semi-analytic calculation of the Jost function and S-matrix near any complex energy, allowing efficient location of bound and resonant states as S-matrix poles, and is applied to a model similar to those used in quantum dot theory.
