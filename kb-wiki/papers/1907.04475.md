# Hyperon production in $Λ_c^+\to K^-pπ^+$ and $Λ_c^+\to K^0_Spπ^0 $

> We investigate $S=-1$ hyperon production from the $Λ_c^+\to K^-pπ^+$ and $Λ_c^+\to K^0_Spπ^0$ decays within the effective Lagrangian approach. We consider the $Σ/Λ$ ground states, $Λ(1520)$, $Λ(1670)(J^p=1/2^-)$, $Λ(1890)(J^p=3/2^+)$; $Λ/Σ$-pole contributions from the combined resonances between 1800 MeV and 2100 MeV; and $N/Δ$-pole and $K^\ast$-pole contributions, which include the proton, $Δ(1232)$, and $K(892)$. We calculate the Dalitz plot density $(d^2Γ/dM_{K^-p}dM_{K^-π^+}$) for the $Λ_c^+\to K^-pπ^+$ decay. The calculated result is in good agreement with experimental data from the Belle Collaboration. Using the parameters from the fit, we present the Dalitz plot density for the $Λ_c^+\to K^0_Spπ^0$ decay. In our calculation, a sharp peak-like structure near 1665 MeV is predicted in the $Λ_c^+\to K^-pπ^+$ decay because of the interference effects between the $Λ(1670)$ resonance and $η$-$Λ$ loop channels. We also demonstrate that we can access direct information regarding the weak couplings of $Λ(1670)$ and $Σ(1670)$ from the $Λ_c^+\to K^0_Spπ^0$ decay. Finally, a possible interpretation for the 1665 MeV structure beyond our prediction is briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a sharp peak-like structure near 1665 MeV in the $\Lambda_c^+\to K^-p\pi^+$ decay, but this structure arises from interference effects between the $\Lambda(1670)$ resonance and $\eta$-$\Lambda$ loop channels rather than from a new hyperon resonance, although the authors acknowledge that a missing hyperon resonance interpretation remains possible.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach to compute Dalitz plot densities for the charged ($\Lambda_c^+\to K^-p\pi^+$) and neutral ($\Lambda_c^+\to K^0_Sp\pi^0$) decay channels. They included contributions from $\Sigma/\Lambda$ ground states, $\Lambda(1520)$, $\Lambda(1670)(J^p=1/2^-)$, $\Lambda(1890)(J^p=3/2^+)$, combined resonances between 1800 MeV and 2100 MeV, $N/\Delta$-pole contributions (proton and $\Delta(1232)$), $K^\ast$-pole contributions ($K(892)$), and an $\eta$-$\Lambda$ loop diagram. Unknown weak coupling constants and phase factors were determined by reproducing Belle Collaboration experimental data for the charged channel, with the $\eta$-$\Lambda$ loop regularized via dimensional regularization using a subtraction scale of $\mu=630$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- $\Lambda(1670)$ has a full width of $\Gamma=35$ MeV and $J^p=1/2^-$
- $\Lambda(1520)$ has a full width of $\Gamma=15.6$ MeV and $J^p=3/2^-$
- The $\eta$-$\Lambda$ threshold is at $(M_\eta+M_\Lambda)^2=2.767$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to approximately 1663.5 MeV
- The $\Lambda(1670)$ band appears at $M^2_{K^-p}\approx2.79$ GeV$^2$ in the Dalitz plot
- The charged channel decay width is $\Gamma_{\Lambda_c^+\to \pi^+K^-p}=2.09\times10^{-10}$ MeV
- The neutral channel decay width is $\Gamma_{\Lambda_c^+\to \pi^0\overline{K}^0p}=1.31\times10^{-10}$ MeV
- The $\Lambda_c^+(2286,1/2^+)$ has a lifetime of $\tau_{\Lambda_c^+}=2\times10^{-13}$ s, corresponding to $\Gamma_{\Lambda_c^+}=3.29\times10^{-9}$ MeV
- The $\Lambda(1670)$ band width is similar to $\Gamma_{\Lambda(1520)}\approx 15$ MeV
- The subtraction scale for loop regularization is $\mu=630$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperon resonances in $\Lambda_c^+$ decays by the Belle Collaboration (Yang 2015) and addresses the possible observation of a narrow structure near 1665 MeV reported by Tanida (2018). The calculation builds on the heavy-quark effective field theory framework of Sharma (1998) for weak couplings and the coupled-channel chiral-unitary model of Hyodo (2011) for meson-baryon interactions. The results enable future experimental measurements of the neutral $\Lambda_c^+\to K^0_Sp\pi^0$ decay channel, which has not yet been reported, and provide a test of isospin symmetry in non-leptonic charmed baryon decays by isolating $I=1$ $\Sigma^\ast$ hyperon contributions from $I=0$ $\Lambda^\ast$ contributions.
