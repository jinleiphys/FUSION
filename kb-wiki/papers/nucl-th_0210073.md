# Constraining the Leading Weak Axial Two-body Current by SNO and Super-K

> We analyze the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory (SNO) and Super-Kamiokande (SK) data on charged current (CC), neutral current (NC) and neutrino electron elastic scattering (ES) reactions to constrain the leading weak axial two-body current parameterized by L_1A. This two-body current is the dominant uncertainty of every low energy weak interaction deuteron breakup process, including SNO's CC and NC reactions. Our method shows that the theoretical inputs to SNO's determination of the CC and NC fluxes can be self-calibrated, be calibrated by SK, or be calibrated by reactor data. The only assumption made is that the total flux of active neutrinos has the standard ^8B spectral shape (but distortions in the electron neutrino spectrum are allowed). We show that SNO's conclusion about the inconsistency of the no-flavor-conversion hypothesis does not contain significant theoretical uncertainty, and we determine the magnitude of the active solar neutrino flux.

## Key claim

The leading weak axial two-body current parameterized by \(L_{1,A}\) can be constrained by combining SNO charged-current (CC), neutral-current (NC), and Super-Kamiokande elastic scattering (ES) data, under the sole assumption that the total active neutrino flux retains the standard \({}^{8}\)B spectral shape (while allowing distortions in the electron neutrino spectrum). The analysis shows that SNO's conclusion ruling out the no-flavor-conversion hypothesis is robust against theoretical uncertainties in the two-body current.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory (EFT) without pions, EFT(\(\pi\hskip-0.6em/\)), to compute the CC and NC cross sections for neutrino-deuteron breakup up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), parameterizing the dominant isovector axial two-body current by \(L_{1,A}\). They then solved a system of three equations linking the measured event rates \(R_{NC}\), \(R_{CC}\), and \(R_{ES}\) (the latter from Super-Kamiokande) to the total active neutrino flux \(\Phi_{\nu_x}\), the flavor-conversion integral \(\int dE\rho P_{\nu_e/\nu_x}\), and \(L_{1,A}\), using the approximation that the normalized response functions \(\rho_{CC}\), \(\rho_e\), and \(\rho_{\mu,\tau}\) can be related by choosing appropriate detection thresholds (5 MeV for CC and NC, 6.8 MeV for ES).

## Key numbers

*   \(L_{1,A} = 4.0 \pm 4.7\ (\text{stat.}) \pm 4.5\ (\text{syst.})\ \text{fm}^3\) at renormalization scale \(\overline{\mu}=m_\pi\), from the combined CC, NC, and ES analysis.
*   Total active neutrino flux \(\Phi_{\nu_x} = (6.4 \pm 1.4 \pm 0.6) \times 10^6\ \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\).
*   Flavor-conversion integral \(\int dE\rho P_{\nu_e/\nu_x} = 0.25_{-0.07}^{+0.12} \pm 0.03\).
*   The no-flavor-conversion hypothesis is ruled out at 4.3\(\sigma\) when \(L_{1,A}\) is allowed to float, and at 5.3\(\sigma\) when using the reactor-antineutrino determination of \(L_{1,A}\).
*   The scaling parameters for the effective cross sections are \(\alpha_{NC}=0.013\) and \(\alpha_{CC}=0.010\).
*   The ratio of neutral-current to electron-neutrino ES cross sections at 6.8 MeV threshold is \(\overline{\sigma}_{\mu,\tau}/\overline{\sigma}_e = 0.153\).

## Context

This work extends the EFT(\(\pi\hskip-0.6em/\)) calculations of Butler, Chen, and Kong (2001) for neutrino-deuteron reactions, showing that the same two-body current \(L_{1,A}\) can be constrained by solar neutrino data rather than relying solely on reactor antineutrino experiments or potential model calculations (e.g., Ying, Haxton, and Henley; Nakamura, Sato, Gudkov, and Kubodera). The result enables a self-calibration of SNO's theoretical inputs and confirms that the no-flavor-conversion hypothesis is ruled out independent of the short-distance physics uncertainty, while also providing a cross-check for the proton-proton fusion process in the solar \(pp\) chain.
