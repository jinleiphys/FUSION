# Renormalization of the P- and T-odd nuclear potentials by the strong interaction and enhancement of P-odd effective field

> Approximate analytical formulas for the self-consistent renormalization of P,T-odd and P-odd weak nuclear potentials by the residual nucleon-nucleon strong interaction are derived. The contact spin-flip nucleon-nucleon interaction reduces the constant of the P,T-odd potential 1.5 times for the proton and 1.8 times for the neutron. Renormalization of the P-odd potential is caused by the velocity dependent spin-flip component of the strong interaction. In the standard variant of $π+ ρ$-exchange, the conventional strength values lead to anomalous enhancement of the P-odd potential. Moreover, the $π$-meson exchange contribution seems to be large enough to generate an instability (pole) in the nuclear response to a weak potential.

## Key claim

The residual strong interaction renormalizes P,T-odd nuclear potentials moderately (reducing them by factors of 1.5–1.8), but the tensor velocity-dependent component of the π+ρ-exchange interaction can produce a substantial enhancement of the P-odd potential, potentially driving the self-consistent solution toward a pole (instability) in the nuclear response, depending on the values of the meson coupling constants.

## Method

The authors derived approximate analytical formulas for the self-consistent renormalization of P,T-odd and P-odd weak nuclear potentials by the residual nucleon-nucleon strong interaction, using the Landau-Migdal parametrization for the contact interaction and explicit π+ρ-exchange potentials for the tensor components. They solved linear algebraic equations for the renormalized coupling constants by computing the correction to the single-particle weak potential from the commutator of the strong interaction with the perturbed wave functions, summing over occupied nucleon states in a Fermi-gas approximation.

## Key numbers

- Contact spin-flip interaction reduces the P,T-odd potential constant by a factor of 1.5 for the proton and 1.8 for the neutron.
- The Landau-Migdal interaction parameters used: C = 300 MeV·fm³, g = 0.575, g′ = 0.725, g₁ = −0.5, g′₁ = −0.26.
- For the π+ρ-exchange calculation: f²_π = 0.08, f²_ρ ranging from 1.86 to 4.86; nonlocality factors W_π = 0.11 and W_ρ = 0.69 at p_F = 1.3 fm⁻¹.
- The critical value of the parameter k for the pole in the P-odd response is k_c = 0.67; π-meson alone gives k_π ≈ 1, producing instability.
- With "strong" ρ-meson coupling (f²_ρ = 4.86), k = 0.4, yielding enhancement factors g^W_p / g^{W0}_p = 1.6 and g^W_n / g^{W0}_p = 0.7 (for g^{W0}_p = 4, g^{W0}_n ≈ 0).
- With "weak" ρ-meson coupling (0.4 × f²_ρ), k ≈ 0.7 ≈ k_c, giving "infinite" enhancement.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study (Flambaum and Vorov, 1993) on induced parity nonconserving interaction (IPNCI) by considering the part of the strong interaction that renormalizes the single-particle weak potential, rather than the two-nucleon IPNCI which did not contribute to the single-particle potential. The results connect to the broader literature on π-condensation in nuclei (Migdal, 1978; Friman et al., 1979) and to the stability of Fermi liquids under tensor interactions (Khalatnikov and Shikin, 1972; Pines and Nozieres, 1966). The finding that the π-meson exchange contribution can drive the nuclear response toward a pole suggests a possible mechanism for the unexplained large parity nonconservation effects observed in neutron capture by ²³²Th (Frankle et al., 1991) and in Mössbauer transitions (Tsin, 1992), and motivates further experimental and theoretical study of P-odd nuclear effects as probes of both weak and strong interaction physics.
