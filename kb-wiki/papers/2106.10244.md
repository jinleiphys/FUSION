# Study of neutrino-nucleus reactions with CRISP Program (0 < $E_ν$ < 3 GeV)

> The neutrino-nucleus reactions are studied at energies from 0 to 3 GeV, using the CRISP program. To simulate these reactions, CRISP uses the Monte Carlo method through an intranuclear cascade model. Quase-elastic and baryonic resonance formation channels for the neutrino-nucleon interaction are considered. The total and differential particle emission cross-sections were obtained, obtaining a good agreement with the values reported by the MiniBooNE experiment. The influence of nuclear effects on the studied reactions, such as fermionic motion, the Pauli blocking mechanism, and the nucleonic separation energy, was shown. It was not possible to simultaneously reproduce the $ν_μ+ D$ and $ν_μ+ ^{12}C$ reactions using the same axial mass value. For the charged current quasi-elastic channel, $M_A = 0.95 \ GeV$ for the $ν_μ+ D$ reaction, and $M_A = 1.35 \ GeV$ for the $ν_μ+ ^{12}C$ reaction. This can be solved if one considers, in addition to the neutrino-nucleon interaction, the neutrino interaction with a pair of nucleons, just as we demonstrate in the last part of this work.

## Key claim

The CRISP intranuclear cascade model can reproduce MiniBooNE charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) cross-section data for neutrino-carbon reactions, but only if the axial mass parameter is treated as nucleus-dependent: \(M_A = 0.95\) GeV for \(\nu_\mu + D\) and \(M_A = 1.35\) GeV for \(\nu_\mu + ^{12}C\). The authors argue this discrepancy can be resolved by including neutrino interactions with nucleon pairs rather than single nucleons alone.

## Method

The CRISP program simulates neutrino-nucleus reactions using a Monte Carlo intranuclear cascade model with three stages: primary neutrino-nucleon interaction (quasi-elastic, baryonic resonance formation, and deep inelastic scattering channels), intranuclear cascade with particle-particle collisions and Pauli blocking, and evaporation-fission of the residual nucleus. The target nucleus is modeled as a Fermi gas of protons and neutrons in a square nuclear potential, and the MiniBooNE neutrino flux predictions for \(0 < E_\nu < 3\) GeV are used as input to generate incident neutrino energies. Cross sections are computed for \(\nu_\mu + ^{12}C\) and \(\bar{\nu}_\mu + ^{12}C\) reactions and compared to MiniBooNE data on hydrocarbon (CH\(_2\)) targets.

## Key numbers

- Axial mass \(M_A = 0.95\) GeV for the \(\nu_\mu + D\) CCQE reaction
- Axial mass \(M_A = 1.35\) GeV for the \(\nu_\mu + ^{12}C\) CCQE reaction
- Best agreement for \(d\sigma/dQ^2\) in \(\nu_\mu + CH_2\) CCQE obtained with proton separation energy \(S_p = 3\) MeV
- Best agreement for \(\bar{\nu}_\mu + ^{12}C\) CCQE obtained with neutron separation energy \(S_N = 3\) MeV
- \(^{12}N\) proton separation energy cited as 0.6 MeV (from Wang 2017)
- Binding energy per nucleon \(B = 8\) MeV used as default separation energy in CRISP
- Fermi gas model uses nuclear potential depth \(V_0 = E_f + B\) with \(B = 8\) MeV
- Vector mass parameter \(M_V = 0.843\) GeV used in form factor parameterizations
- Axial coupling constant \(g_A = -1.267\)

## Context

This work extends the CRISP model, previously applied to photon-, electron-, and proton-induced reactions (Deppman 2004, 2006; Andrade-II 2012), to neutrino-nucleus interactions in the 0–3 GeV energy range, continuing earlier work by Vargas (2017). The results are compared directly to MiniBooNE experimental data (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010, 2013), and the inability to simultaneously fit deuterium and carbon data with a single axial mass echoes a known tension in the field. The proposed resolution via two-nucleon interactions points toward more sophisticated treatment of nuclear correlations, which could inform future analyses for experiments such as DUNE and Hyper-Kamiokande.
