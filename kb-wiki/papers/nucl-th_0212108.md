# Flavor independent systematics of excited baryons and intra-band transition

> Transitions among excited nucleons are studied within a non-relativistic quark model with a deformed harmonic oscillator potential. The transition amplitudes are factorized into the $l$-th moment and a geometrical factor. This fact leads to an analogous result to the ``Alaga-rule'' for baryons.

## Key claim

The authors show that intra-band pion transitions among excited nucleons within a deformed oscillator quark model obey a geometrical scaling relation analogous to the Alaga rule in nuclear rotational bands, with the ratio of decay widths determined purely by angular momentum factors independent of the detailed dynamics.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic quark model with a deformed harmonic oscillator potential (DOQ model) where the oscillator frequencies are determined by energy minimization with a volume conservation condition. They calculate transition amplitudes for pion emission between excited nucleon states using a semi-classical cranking method, factorizing the amplitudes into an l-th moment and a geometrical factor. The decay widths are then computed by integrating over the pion momentum and summing over spin states.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass taken as 300 MeV (insensitive to calculations)
- Oscillator parameter omega = 644 MeV, chosen to reproduce the excitation energy of the first 1/2+ excited state
- Decay width for 9/2+ to 5/2+ transition: 5.8 MeV
- Decay width for 9/2+ to 3/2+ transition: 10.8 MeV
- Decay width for 7/2+ to 5/2+ transition: 35.3 MeV
- Decay width for 7/2+ to 3/2+ transition: 1.7 MeV
- Decay width for 5/2+ to 1/2+ transition: 0.1 MeV
- Decay width for 3/2+ to 1/2+ transition: 0.2 MeV
- Decay width for 7/2- to 3/2- transition: 7.9 MeV
- Decay width for 7/2- to 1/2- transition: 9.9 MeV
- Decay width for 5/2- to 3/2- transition: 39.3 MeV
- Decay width for 5/2- to 1/2- transition: 0.7 MeV
- Alaga-rule ratio for baryons: W(7/2 to 5/2) / W(3/2 to 1/2) = 9/7

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous finding of flavor-independent systematics in baryon excitation spectra (Koma, Hosaka, Toki, 1999) and their deformed oscillator quark model that reproduces these spectra with a single parameter. The paper draws an analogy to the Alaga rule from nuclear rotational band theory (Alaga, 1976) and applies it to baryon transitions. The results enable future study of inter-band transitions with pions that could be compared with experimental data from N* to N pi pi processes.
