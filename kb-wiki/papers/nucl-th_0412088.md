# Nuclear matter symmetry energy from generalized polarizabilities: dependences on momentum, isospin, density and temperature

> Symmetry energy terms from macroscopic mass formulae are investigated as generalized polarizabilities of nuclear matter. Besides the neutron-proton (n-p) symmetry energy the spin dependent symmetry energies and a scalar one are also defined. They depend on the nuclear densities ($ρ$), neutron-proton asymmetry ($b$), temperature ($T$) and exchanged energy and momentum ($q$). Based on a standard expression for the generalized polarizabilities, a differential equation is proposed to constrain the dependence of the symmetry energy on the n-p asymmetry and on the density. Some solutions are discussed. The q-dependence (zero frequence) of the symmetry energy coefficients with Skyrme-type forces is investigated in the four channels of the particle-hole interaction. Spin dependent symmetry energies are also investigated indicating much stronger differences in behavior with $q$ for each Skyrme force than the results for the neutron-proton one.

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficients of nuclear matter can be interpreted as generalized static polarizabilities, and their dependence on neutron-proton asymmetry and density is constrained by a differential equation derived from the polarizability formalism, though the specific form of the asymmetry dependence (such as the parabolic approximation) may need modification for very asymmetric systems or densities far from saturation.

## Method

The author derives a differential equation from the standard expression for generalized polarizabilities to constrain the simultaneous dependence of the symmetry energy coefficients on neutron-proton asymmetry (b) and density (ρ). The static (zero-frequency) limit of the dynamical polarizability is then calculated for symmetric nuclear matter using Skyrme-type forces (SLyb and SKM) in the four channels of the particle-hole interaction (scalar, isovector, spin, and spin-isovector), with the q-dependence (exchanged momentum) investigated at finite temperatures up to 7 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The neutron-proton symmetry energy coefficient at zero momentum and zero temperature is approximately 32 MeV for both Skyrme forces SLyb and SKM.
- The neutron-proton symmetry energy coefficient decreases with increasing exchanged momentum up to q ≈ 500 MeV (nearly twice the nucleon momentum at the Fermi surface) but does not reach negative values.
- The symmetry energy coefficient in asymmetric matter can reach up to 1.5 times the symmetric value for b = 2 (neutron density three times larger than proton density), and up to 2 times the symmetric value in the limit of neutron matter (b → ∞).
- The parameter γ in the density dependence expression Esym = Esym(ρ0)(ρ/ρ0)^γ ranges from approximately 0.6 (from variational calculations) to approximately 2 (from analysis of low-energy heavy ion collisions).

## Context

This work extends previous investigations of symmetry energy as a generalized polarizability (Braghin, 2000-2001) and the linear response method for dynamical polarizabilities (Braghin, Vautherin, 1994). It provides a framework to constrain the simultaneous density and isospin dependence of symmetry energy coefficients, which is relevant for understanding neutron star structure and the equation of state at high densities. The results enable future work connecting the momentum dependence of symmetry energies to experimental observables in heavy ion collisions and radioactive beam facilities such as RIA and GSI.
