# Minimizing Selection Bias in Inclusive Jets in Heavy-Ion Collisions with Energy Correlators -- arXiv note

> This note serves as a companion to a Letter, where we introduce a new energy correlator-based observable designed to minimize the impact of selection bias due to energy loss in inclusive jets in heavy-ion collisions. Here, we apply the method outlined in the Letter to the first-ever measurement of energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions, recently released by the CMS Collaboration.

## Key claim

The new observable E2C/C2, designed to correct for selection bias due to energy loss in inclusive jets, significantly reduces the suppression of the Pb-Pb energy correlator relative to the p-p baseline, but the error bands presented are likely overestimated because correlations between the unbiasing function and the E2C distribution are not accounted for in the error propagation.

## Method

The authors apply the unbiasing function C2, derived in a companion Letter (Andres and Holguin, 2024), to the CMS measurement of the two-point energy correlator (E2C) in inclusive jets from lead-lead (Pb-Pb) collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV. Jets are reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with R=0.4 for charged particles with transverse momenta between 120 GeV and 200 GeV, and the E2C distribution is measured within a R=0.4 cone around the winner-takes-all axis. The ratio E2C/C2 is then compared across different centrality classes and jet pT ranges to assess the reduction of selection bias.

## Key numbers

*   Pb-Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.
*   Inclusive jets with reconstructed transverse momenta between 120 GeV and 200 GeV.
*   Jets reconstructed using anti-kT algorithm with R = 0.4.
*   For jets with 120 < pT < 140 GeV in the 0-10% centrality class, the Pb-Pb E2C is suppressed relative to p-p for 0.03 less than R_L less than 0.2; after applying C2, the ratio becomes completely flat in the hadronization region (R_L less than 0.03).
*   For the 50-90% centrality class with 120 < pT < 140 GeV, the E2C/C2 Pb-Pb/p-p ratio becomes completely flat, indicating selection bias was the sole cause of deviation from vacuum baseline.

## Context

This work extends the method introduced in a companion Letter (Andres and Holguin, 2024) to the first-ever measurement of energy correlators in heavy-ion collisions, released by the CMS Collaboration (CMS-PAS-HIN-23-004). The results confirm qualitative predictions for medium-induced radiation from prior theoretical work (Andres et al., 2022; Andres et al., 2023; Andres et al., 2024) and show that the observed modifications in the deep hadronization region are fully attributable to selection bias. The analysis enables future experimental measurements to disentangle medium-induced radiation from other effects such as medium response (Yang, 2023; Bossi et al., 2024) and to identify signatures of color coherence dynamics.
