# Bottomonia suppression in heavy-ion collisions

> The thermal suppression of heavy quark bound states represents an ideal observable for determining if one has produced a quark-gluon plasma in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. In recent years, a paradigm shift has taken place in the theory of quarkonium suppression due to new first principles calculations of the thermal widths of these states. These thermal widths are large, e.g. O(20-100 MeV) for the Upsilon(1S), and cause in-medium suppression of the states at temperatures below their traditionally defined disassociation temperatures. In order to apply the newly developed understanding to phenomenology, however, one must make detailed 3+1d dissipative hydrodynamical models of the plasma including the effects of finite shear viscosity. These effects include not only the modification of the time evolution of the temperature of the system, flow, etc., but also non-equilibrium modifications of the heavy quark potential itself. In this proceedings contribution, we briefly review the setup for these model calculations and present comparisons of theory with data from RHIC 200 GeV/nucleon Au-Au collisions, LHC 2.76 TeV/nucleon Pb-Pb, and LHC 5.02 TeV/nucleon Pb-Pb collisions as a function of number of participants, rapidity, and transverse momentum.

## Key claim

The thermal suppression of bottomonium states in heavy-ion collisions can be described by a model that includes large in-medium thermal widths (e.g., 20-100 MeV for Upsilon(1S)) and non-equilibrium corrections from pressure anisotropy, without requiring cold nuclear matter effects or regeneration, but the model shows tension with STAR data at RHIC 200 GeV that may indicate the importance of cold nuclear matter effects or energy-dependent feeddown fractions at lower energies.

## Method

The authors used a 3+1d dissipative anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) framework to model the quark-gluon plasma evolution, incorporating finite shear viscosity and non-equilibrium modifications of the heavy quark potential. In-medium decay widths from a complex-valued potential were folded over the full hydrodynamic evolution to compute survival probabilities of Upsilon states as a function of space-time position, transverse momentum, and rapidity, with comparisons made to experimental data from RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperatures for 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb collisions: T0 = {0.552, 0.546, 0.544} GeV for 4π η/s = {1, 2, 3} respectively, with hydrodynamic initialization at τ0 = 0.3 fm/c
- Initial temperatures for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions: T0 = {0.641, 0.632, 0.629} GeV for 4π η/s = {1, 2, 3} respectively, with hydrodynamic initialization at τ0 = 0.3 fm/c
- Thermal widths for Upsilon(1S) are O(20-100 MeV)
- LHC initial temperatures quoted as T0 = 600-700 MeV at τ0 = 0.25 fm/c from prior work (Alqahtani 2017)

## Context

This work extends the paradigm shift in quarkonium suppression theory following new first-principles calculations of thermal widths (Dumitru 2007, Burnier 2009, Strickland 2011), which showed that in-medium suppression occurs below traditional dissociation temperatures. The model builds on earlier anisotropic hydrodynamics work (Strickland 2014) and provides comparisons to CMS, ALICE, and STAR data, enabling future inclusion of bulk viscous corrections and non-conformal effects (Alqahtani 2017, Du 2016).
