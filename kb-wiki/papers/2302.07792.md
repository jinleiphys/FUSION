# Generation, dynamics, and correlations of the fission fragments' angular momenta

> The generation of angular momentum in fissioning nuclei is not well understood. The predictions of different models disagree, particularly concerning the correlation between the fragments' angular momenta. In this article, a time-dependent collective Hamiltonian model is proposed to treat the generation of the angular momentum in the fission fragments due to the quantum uncertainty principle as well as the dynamics of the collective wave function during and after scission. The model is constructed in the framework of the frozen Hartree-Fock approximation using a Skyrme energy functional to extract deformations of the fission fragments as well as the interactions in a derived collective Hamiltonian. The fission reactions studied are $^{240}$Pu $\rightarrow$ $^{132}$Sn+$^{108}$Ru and $^{240}$Pu $\rightarrow$ $^{144}$Ba+$^{96}$Sr. The model can account for a large part of the angular momentum found in experimental data. The orientation of the angular momentum of each fragment is found to be mainly in the plane perpendicular to the fission axis, in agreement with the experiment. The magnitudes of the angular momenta in the two fragments are nearly uncorrelated, in agreement with the recent experimental data of Wilson et al., Nature (London) 590, 566 (2021). Some of the conclusions of the traditional collective vibration model are supported by the present model but some are not. Surprisingly, it is found that the angular momenta of the fragments are slightly correlated positively as in a wriggling mode. It is also found that the presence of an octupole deformation in a fragment can significantly increase the generated angular momentum.

# Generation, dynamics, and correlations of the fission fragments' angular momenta

## Key claim

The time-dependent collective Hamiltonian model can account for a large part of the angular momentum found in experimental data, but the model's predictions depend on treating the fragments as rigid rotors and on a 4 fm shift of the frozen Hartree-Fock nuclear potential to account for missing neck degrees of freedom. The angular momenta of the two fragments are found to be slightly correlated positively as in a wriggling mode, contrary to some expectations from traditional collective vibration models.

## Method

The authors construct a time-dependent collective Hamiltonian in the frozen Hartree-Fock approximation using a Skyrme energy functional (Sly4d interaction) to extract fragment deformations and interactions. The model treats the orientation angles of the fission fragments as quantum degrees of freedom while the separation coordinate between fragments is treated as an external time-dependent parameter taken from time-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations. The system is evolved with the time-dependent Schrodinger equation using a Runge-Kutta method at order 4 with a time step of 0.25 fm/c, starting from the ground eigenstate at a separation distance of 14 fm and evolving until the fragments reach 100 fm separation.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction $^{240}$Pu $\rightarrow$ $^{132}$Sn+$^{108}$Ru: the average spin $\langle L^2 \rangle^{1/2}$ of $^{108}$Ru is 9.28 $\hbar$ at scission (D=21 fm) and 12.31 $\hbar$ at large distances (final)
- For $^{240}$Pu $\rightarrow$ $^{144}$Ba+$^{96}$Sr: the average spin of $^{144}$Ba is 10.04 $\hbar$ at scission and 10.95 $\hbar$ at large distances; for $^{96}$Sr it is 7.74 $\hbar$ at scission and 9.30 $\hbar$ at large distances
- When the moment of inertia is divided by two, the average spin of $^{108}$Ru reduces to 7.24 $\hbar$ at scission and 10.38 $\hbar$ at large distances
- The correlation coefficient $r_{L_{Hx},L_{Lx}}$ between the projections of angular momentum on a transverse axis is 0.22 initially and 0.06 at the final time
- The probability that both projections on the x-axis have the same sign (wriggling mode population) is 0.58 initially and 0.53 at the end of the evolution
- The average opening angle between the fragments' angular momentum vectors is 86.7 degrees (78.4 degrees for the initial distribution)
- The probability of $m \neq 0$ (projection on the fission axis) is of the order of 1 percent
- The rigid moments of inertia used: $^{132}$Sn = 50.0 $\hbar^2$/MeV, $^{108}$Ru = 51.4 $\hbar^2$/MeV, $^{144}$Ba = 63.1 $\hbar^2$/MeV, $^{96}$Sr = 37.1 $\hbar^2$/MeV
- Deformations: $^{108}$Ru has $\beta_2$ = 0.82, $^{144}$Ba has $\beta_2$ = 0.22 and $\beta_3$ = 0.16, $^{96}$Sr has $\beta_2$ = 0.53

## Context

This work extends the frozen Hartree-Fock approach previously used for fusion tunneling (Simenel 2017, Umar 2021) to the problem of fission fragment angular momenta. It addresses disagreements between existing models: statistical fluctuation models (Moretto 1980, Dossing 1985, Randrup 2021, 2022), quantum effects at scission (Mikhailov 1999, Bonneau 2007, Shneidman 2002, Rasmussen 1969, Zielinska-Pfabe 1974), and projection methods (Bertsch 2019, Marevic 2021, Bulgac 2021, 2022, Schunck 2022). The results agree with the experimental finding of Wilson (2021) that the magnitudes of angular momenta in the two fragments are nearly uncorrelated. The model enables future work on the role of octupole deformation in generating angular momentum and on the dynamics of the opening angle distribution, which differs from predictions in Randrup (2021, 2022) and Bulgac (2022).
