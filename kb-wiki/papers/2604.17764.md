# Soft mode dynamics associated with QCD critical point and color superconductivity -- pseudogap, anomalous dilepton production and electric conductivity

> We give a systematic account of the soft mode dynamics of QCD critical point and the two-flavor color-superconductivity based on the 2-flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model, and investigate their effects on electromagnetic observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (HIC). We first demonstrate that the collective excitations coupled to the fluctuations of the respective order parameters are the soft modes associated with the phase transitions, in the sense that they acquire a prominent spectral strength in the low-energy and low-momentum region near the phase transitions, and the peak energy goes down, i.e., gets softened, and eventually vanishes at the critical point. It is shown that the diquark soft mode of the 2SC gives rise to the pseudogap, i.e., a depression in the density of states of the quark spectra around the Fermi surface above but in the vicinity of the critical temperature. Then, exploiting the ideas that were developed in condensed matter physics for describing the `para-conductivity' in the normal phase of metal superconductors, we show that the soft modes cause an anomalous enhancement of electric conductivity and the dilepton production rate, and discuss their relevance to HIC.

## Key claim

The soft modes (collective excitations) associated with the QCD critical point and two-flavor color superconductivity (2SC) cause an anomalous enhancement of electric conductivity and dilepton production rates in the normal phase above the critical temperature, analogous to "paraconductivity" in condensed-matter superconductors; the enhancement is a direct consequence of the spectral strength of these modes becoming prominent at low energy and low momentum near the phase transition.

## Method

The authors use the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to study the soft mode dynamics of both the QCD critical point and the 2SC phase transition. They first identify the collective excitations coupled to the order-parameter fluctuations and show that these modes soften (peak energy goes to zero) at the critical point. They then compute the diquark soft mode's effect on the quark spectral function, demonstrating a pseudogap in the density of states near the Fermi surface. Finally, they adapt the condensed-matter formalism for "paraconductivity" to calculate the soft-mode contributions to electric conductivity and dilepton production rates in the normal phase.

## Key numbers

- The diquark soft mode of the 2SC phase transition causes a depression (pseudogap) in the density of states of quark spectra around the Fermi surface above but near the critical temperature T_c.
- The soft-mode peak energy goes down and eventually vanishes at the critical point (the paper does not provide a numerical value for this energy in the text).
- No explicit numerical values for electric conductivity or dilepton production rates are given in the truncated text.

## Context

This work extends the concept of soft mode dynamics from condensed matter physics (where paraconductivity above T_c in metallic superconductors is well known) to QCD phase transitions, specifically the critical point and color superconductivity. It builds on prior studies of the QCD phase diagram using the NJL model and connects to experimental observables in relativistic heavy-ion collisions (HIC). The results enable predictions for anomalous dilepton yields and enhanced electrical conductivity that could be tested against HIC data from facilities such as RHIC and LHC.
