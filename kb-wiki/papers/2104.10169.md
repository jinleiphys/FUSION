# An Exact Integral-to-Sum Relation for Products of Bessel Functions

> A useful identity relating the infinite sum of two Bessel functions to their infinite integral was discovered in Dominici et al. (2012). Here, we extend this result to products of $N$ Bessel functions, and show it can be straightforwardly proven using the Abel-Plana theorem, or the Poisson summation formula. For $N=2$, the proof is much simpler than that of Dominici et al., and significantly enlarges the range of validity.

## Key claim

The paper extends a known identity relating the infinite integral of two Bessel functions to their infinite sum (Dominici et al., 2012) to products of N Bessel functions, with a simpler proof and a significantly enlarged range of validity that includes cases where the sum of the absolute values of the arguments can be up to 2π (and beyond via rescaling), subject to specific conditions on the parameters.

## Method

The authors prove the integral-to-sum relation for products of N Bessel functions of the first kind using the Abel-Plana theorem, which relates sums over integers to integrals plus a correction term involving an imaginary-axis integral. They verify the five Abel-Plana validity conditions for the function f(z) = z^{-λ} ∏ J_{ν_j}(a_j z), showing the correction term vanishes when the exponent λ equals Σ ν_j − 2k for integer k. An alternative proof using the Poisson summation formula is also sketched, relying on the band-limited nature of Bessel functions expressed in cosine integral form.

## Key numbers

- The main result (Equation 4) holds for integer k, real {a_j}, and complex {ν_j} subject to Σ|a_j| ≤ 2π and Σ Re(ν_j) > 2k − N/2, with stricter conditions when Σ|a_j| = 2π or when a beat-frequency sum Σ s_j a_j = 0 exists.
- For the N=2 case, the result requires |a|+|b| ≤ 2π and Re(μ+ν) > 2k−1, with the additional condition Re(μ+ν) > 2k if |a|+|b| = 2π or a = ±b.
- The sum converges absolutely when Σ Re(ν_j) > 2k − N/2 + 1, with truncation error scaling as O(M^{1−[Re(λ)+N/2]}) for large truncation M.
- For conditionally convergent cases (0 < Re(λ)+N/2 < 1), the truncation error scales as O(M^{-[Re(λ)+N/2]}).

## Context

This work extends the identity of Dominici et al. (2012), which was proven for two Bessel functions using hypergeometric function manipulations, to arbitrary N Bessel functions with a simpler proof via the Abel-Plana theorem. The result is relevant to fields where Bessel function integrals appear, including atomic physics, classical mechanics, and cosmology (e.g., the FFTLog algorithm of Hamilton, 2000). The enlarged domain of validity and the ability to handle products of many Bessel functions enables more efficient computation of integrals arising in spherical harmonic expansions and Fourier transforms of isotropic functions.
