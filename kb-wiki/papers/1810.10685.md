# Photoproduction of J/$ψ$ in non-single-diffractive p+p collisions

> Recently, significant enhancements of J/$ψ$ production at very low transverse momenta were observed by the ALICE and STAR collaboration in peripheral hadronic A+A collisions. The anomaly excesses point to evidence of coherent photon-nucleus interactions in violent hadronic heavy-ion collisions, which were conventionally studied only in ultra-peripheral collisions. Assuming that the coherent photoproduction is the underlying mechanism which is responsible for the excess observed in peripheral A+A collisions, its contribution in p+p collisions with nuclear overlap, i.e. non-single-diffractive collisions, is of particular interest. In this paper, we perform a calculation of exclusive J/$ψ$ photoproduction in non-single-diffractive p+p collisions at RHIC and LHC energies base on the pQCD motivated parametrization from world-wide experimental data, which could be further employed to improve the precision of phenomenal calculations for photoproduction in A+A collisions. The differential rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of J/$ψ$ from photoproduction are presented. In comparison with the J/$ψ$ production from hadronic interactions, we find that the contribution of photoproduction is negligible.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that exclusive J/ψ photoproduction in non-single-diffractive (NSD) p+p collisions at RHIC and LHC energies is negligible compared to hadronic production, meaning the low-pT excess observed in peripheral A+A collisions is not expected to appear in p+p collisions. This conclusion depends on the assumption that coherent photoproduction is the mechanism responsible for the excess in A+A collisions.

## Method

The calculation uses the equivalent photon approximation, factorizing the cross section into a photon flux from the proton (modeled with a dipole form factor for the proton's charge distribution) and the elementary γp → J/ψ p cross section. The photonuclear cross section and the slope parameter for the pT distribution are parametrized from world-wide experimental data (including ALICE, LHCb, H1, ZEUS, and fixed-target experiments) using pQCD-motivated and Regge-motivated functional forms, with all measurements updated to the latest J/ψ branching fractions. The NSD condition is implemented via a geometrical cut on impact parameter, using NSD cross sections of 30, 50, and 56 mb at √s = 0.2, 2.76, and 5.02 TeV respectively.

## Key numbers

- The parametrization of the γp → J/ψ p cross section yields C0 = 80.2 ± 0.9 nb and δ = 0.321 ± 0.005, with χ²/NDF = 113.6/116.
- The proton charge radius used in the dipole form factor is rp = 0.8768 ± 69 fm.
- The J/ψ photoproduction cross section in NSD p+p collisions is several orders of magnitude lower than the hadronic production cross section at all three energies studied (√s = 0.2, 2.76, and 5.02 TeV), including at pT → 0.
- The NSD cross sections used are 30 mb at √s = 0.2 TeV, 50 mb at √s = 2.76 TeV, and 56 mb at √s = 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent photoproduction from ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) and peripheral A+A collisions (where ALICE and STAR observed low-pT J/ψ excesses) into the p+p collision regime. The calculation uses the same photoproduction mechanism proposed by PhysRevC.93.044912, PhysRevC.97.044910, and SHI2018399 for A+A collisions. The result that photoproduction is negligible in p+p collisions is good news for nuclear modification factor (RAA) measurements, since the pp baseline used for low-pT regions (extrapolated from higher pT measurements) does not need correction for photoproduction contributions.
