# Transport properties of a charged drop in an external electric field

> Transport properties of a charged droplet of weakly interacting particles in an external field are investigated. A non-equilibrium distribution function which describes a process of the droplet transverse evolution with constant entropy in an external electric field is calculated. With the help of this distribution function, shear viscosity coefficients in the transverse plane are calculated as well. They are found to be very small and depend on the time of the droplet's expansion in a hydrodynamical regime and external field value. An applicability of the results to the description of initial states of quark-gluon plasma obtained in high-energy interactions of nuclei is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the shear viscosity of a charged droplet of weakly interacting particles in an external electric field is very small, and that this smallness arises from the interaction between the charged droplet and the external field rather than from strong coupling between the particles themselves. The result is conditional on the droplet expanding with constant entropy in a high-energy regime where the dimensionless parameter Lambda_0 is less than 1.

## Method

The authors solve the Vlasov equation for a non-equilibrium distribution function describing the transverse evolution of a charged droplet in an external electric field, treating the external field as a perturbation that acts only at the initial time. They expand the distribution function as a power series in the time parameter tau and calculate the first-order term, then use this distribution function to compute the averaged radial velocity and the non-diagonal stress-energy tensor components needed for shear viscosity.

## Key numbers

- The dimensionless parameter Lambda_0 = 2 q Q_T / (m c^2 r_0D) is defined, with Lambda_0 less than 1 in the high-energy regime of interest.
- The first-order radial velocity expression contains terms proportional to 2 k T_bot r / (m c^2 r_b^2) and 2 pi q^2 n r / (m c^2), both small due to the large m c^2 denominator.
- The validity condition for the perturbative expansion is r^2 Lambda tau less than 1, which translates to E_pot less than (r / l_0) m c^2.
- The Debye length is defined as r_D^2 = k_B T_0 / (4 pi q^2 n_0).

## Context

This work extends the approach of AnVis1 and AnVis2 (2013-2014) by proposing a different mechanism for the smallness of shear viscosity in quark-gluon plasma, based on weakly interacting particles rather than strong coupling. The model considers a small, dense charged droplet similar to the hot spots described in HotSp and HotSp1 (2010-2012), and uses the constant-entropy expansion framework from Land1 (1987). The results are intended to apply to the initial states of quark-gluon plasma produced in high-energy nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the shear viscosity/entropy ratio is known to be very small from experimental data compared to perturbative calculations in PerVis (2003).
