# Baryon masses at second order in large-$N$ chiral perturbation theory

> We consider flavor breaking in the the octet and decuplet baryon masses at second order in large-$N$ chiral perturbation theory, where $N$ is the number of QCD colors. We assume that $1/N \sim 1/N_F \sim m_s / Λ\gg m_{u,d}/Λ, α_{EM}$, where $N_F$ is the number of light quark flavors, and $m_{u,d,s} / Λ$ are the parameters controlling $SU(N_F)$ flavor breaking in chiral perturbation theory. We consistently include non-analytic contributions to the baryon masses at orders $m_q^{3/2}$, $m_q^2 \ln m_q$, and $(m_q \ln m_q) / N$. The $m_q^{3/2}$ corrections are small for the relations that follow from $SU(N_F)$ symmetry alone, but the corrections to the large-$N$ relations are large and have the wrong sign. Chiral power-counting and large-$N$ consistency allow a 2-loop contribution at order $m_q^2 \ln m_q$, and a non-trivial explicit calculation is required to show that this contribution vanishes. At second order in the expansion, there are eight relations that are non-trivial consequences of the $1/N$ expansion, all of which are well satisfied within the experimental errors. The average deviation at this order is $7 \MeV$ for the $\De I = 0$ mass differences and $0.35 \MeV$ for the $\De I \ne 0$ mass differences, consistent with the expectation that the error is of order $1/N^2 \sim 10\%$.

## Key claim

The paper claims that at second order in a combined large-$N$ and chiral expansion, the baryon octet and decuplet masses satisfy eight non-trivial relations that follow from the $1/N$ expansion, all of which agree well with experiment; however, the $m_q^{3/2}$ chiral loop corrections to the large-$N$ relations are large and have the wrong sign, requiring cancellation by higher-order counterterms to achieve agreement.

## Method

The authors perform a simultaneous expansion in $1/N$ (where $N$ is the number of QCD colors) and flavor breaking controlled by quark masses and electromagnetism, assuming $1/N \sim 1/N_F \sim m_s/\Lambda \gg m_{u,d}/\Lambda, \alpha_{EM}$. They use an effective Lagrangian in a spin-flavor Fock space formalism that keeps $SU(N_F)$ flavor symmetry manifest order-by-order in $1/N$, and consistently include chiral loop contributions at orders $m_q^{3/2}$, $m_q^2 \ln m_q$, and $(m_q \ln m_q)/N$. A 2-loop calculation is performed to verify that a potentially allowed $m_q^2 \ln m_q$ contribution vanishes.

## Key numbers

- Average deviation for $\Delta I = 0$ mass differences at second order: $7\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Average deviation for $\Delta I \ne 0$ mass differences at second order: $0.35\ \mathrm{MeV}$ (or $0.20\ \mathrm{MeV}$ omitting the model-dependent $\Delta^0 - \Delta^{++}$ value)
- Leading-order $m_q^{3/2}$ correction to the large-$N$ relation $(\Sigma - N) - (\Lambda - N)$: $-400 \pm 160\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Leading-order $m_q^{3/2}$ correction to the large-$N$ relation $(\Sigma^* - \Delta) - (\Lambda - N)$: $200 \pm 80\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Leading-order $m_q^{3/2}$ corrections to the Gell-Mann-Okubo, decuplet equal spacing, and related relations: $-2 \pm 1\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Quark mass ratio extracted at leading order: $R \equiv (m_s - (m_u+m_d)/2)/(m_d - m_u) = 110 \pm 30$ (from a fit, $R \simeq 90$)
- Leading-order $\Delta I = 0$ mass relation deviations: Gell-Mann-Okubo $8\%$, decuplet equal spacing $3\%$ and $7\%$, large-$N$ relations $40\%$ and $15\%$

## Context

This work extends the large-$N$ baryon analysis of Dashen, Jenkins, and Manohar (1994-1995) and the spin-flavor formalism of Luty, March-Russell, and Manohar (1995) to second order in baryon masses, following the same expansion previously applied to baryon magnetic moments by Luty, March-Russell, and Manohar (1995). It differs from the analysis of Jenkins and Lebed (1995) by consistently including chiral loop contributions. The results enable systematic comparison of large-$N$ predictions with baryon mass data at subleading order, and demonstrate that the $1/N$ expansion yields testable relations beyond those from flavor symmetry alone, with remaining deviations consistent with $1/N^2 \sim 10\%$ corrections.
