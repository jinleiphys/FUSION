# Relativity violations and beta decay

> In some quantum theories of gravity, deviations from the laws of relativity could be comparatively large while escaping detection to date. In the neutrino sector, precision experiments with beta decay yield new and improved constraints on these countershaded relativity violations. Existing data are used to extract bounds of $3 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV on the magnitudes of two of the four possible coefficients, and estimates are provided of future attainable sensitivities in a variety of experiments.

## Key claim

Existing data from tritium beta-decay endpoint experiments (Troitsk and Mainz) can be used to extract constraints on two of the four "countershaded" Lorentz-violating neutrino operators, improving the limit on one coefficient by over an order of magnitude and providing the first measurement of another; the constraints are conservative and assume only one nonzero coefficient at a time, with the possibility of fortuitous cancellations excluded by comparing experiments with different orientations.

## Method

The authors analyze published tritium beta-decay endpoint spectra from the Troitsk and Mainz experiments, modeling the Lorentz-violating modifications as a shift in the endpoint energy that depends on the coefficients $\aof 3 {00}$ and $\aof 3 {10}$ (for time-averaged data) and on $\Re \aof 3 {11}$ and $\Im \aof 3 {11}$ (for sidereal-time-binned data). They extract constraints by comparing measured endpoint energies to theoretical expectations, accounting for experimental geometry (colatitude, magnetic field orientation, acceptance cone aperture) and using the Standard-Model Extension (SME) framework. They also estimate future sensitivities for KATRIN, neutron decay, and double beta decay experiments.

## Key numbers

- Constraint on $|\aof 3 {j0}| < 3 \times 10^{-8}$ GeV for both $j=0$ and $j=1$ at better than 90% confidence level, from combined Troitsk and Mainz data.
- For the Troitsk experiment: $|\aof 3 {j0}| < 2\times10^{-8}$ GeV for both $j=0$ and $j=1$, from a $\pm 5$ eV upper limit on a possible constant endpoint shift.
- For the Mainz experiment run Q12: $\aof 3 {00} = 8.2\pm6.1\times10^{-9}$ GeV and $\aof 3 {10} = -2.4\pm1.8\times10^{-8}$ GeV; conservative constraints $|\aof 3 {00}| < 2\times10^{-8}$ GeV and $|\aof 3 {10}| < 5\times10^{-8}$ GeV from a $\pm 5$ eV observable shift.
- Improvement over the previous best constraint on $\aof 3 {00}$ from IceCube data ($|\acfc 3{}|<1.9\times 10^{-7}$ GeV) by more than an order of magnitude.
- For KATRIN: estimated sensitivity about two orders of magnitude beyond the new constraints, with 30 days of data.
- For neutron decay: estimated reach $|\acfc 3{}| < 2\times 10^{-7}$ GeV from a 0.1% measurement of the differential spectrum at 0.41 MeV.
- For neutron decay asymmetry parameter $a$: estimated reaches $|\aof 3{10}| < 5\times 10^{-8}$ GeV and $|\Re \aof 3{11}|, |\Im \aof 3{11}| < 4\times 10^{-8}$ GeV from a 0.1% measurement near 0.35 MeV.
- For neutron decay correlation parameter $B$: estimated reaches $|\aof 3{10}| < 2\times 10^{-6}$ GeV and $|\Re \aof 3{11}|, |\Im \aof 3{11}| < 1\times 10^{-6}$ GeV from a 0.1% measurement near 0.35 MeV.
- For $^{136}$Xe double beta decay (two-neutrino mode): estimated reach $|\acfc 3{}| < 2\times 10^{-7}$ GeV from a 0.1% measurement near $K_m \simeq 1.02$ MeV.
- For $^{136}$Xe neutrinoless double beta decay: constraint $|\geff|\lsim 10^{-9}$ from the current limit on $m_\nu^2$.

## Context

This work extends the search for Lorentz violation in the neutrino sector using the Standard-Model Extension (SME) framework developed by Kostelecky and collaborators, focusing on four "countershaded" operators of mass dimension $d=3$ that are odd under CPT and cannot be probed by neutrino oscillations or time-of-flight experiments. It improves on the previous best constraint on the isotropic coefficient $\acfc 3{}$ from IceCube data (Kostelecky and Mewes, 2012) by more than an order of magnitude and provides the first constraint on $\aof 3 {10}$. The results enable future dedicated analyses by the Troitsk, Mainz, and KATRIN collaborations, as well as neutron decay and double beta decay experiments, to further probe these operators and potentially reveal or exclude countershaded Lorentz violation at the eV scale.
