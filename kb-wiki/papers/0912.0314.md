# Modified parameter-sets of M3Y-type semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction for nuclear structure studies

> New parameter-sets of the M3Y-type semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction are obtained, by taking into account the whole contribution of the interaction to the pairing. The interactions are applicable to nuclear structure studies via mean-field calculations and their extensions. Implementing self-consistent mean-field calculations for the spherical nuclei, we confirm that the results do not change significantly from the previous ones, in which the parameters were determined by partly discarding influence of the density-dependent repulsion on the pairing.

## Key claim

New parameter-sets M3Y-P4' and M3Y-P5' of the M3Y-type semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction are obtained by fully including the density-dependent contact force contribution to pairing, whereas the previous sets M3Y-P4 and M3Y-P5 had partly discarded this influence. The results for spherical nuclei do not change significantly from the previous parameter-sets.

## Method

The author refitted the central-channel parameters of the M3Y-type interaction to the measured binding energies of 16O and 208Pb and to the even-odd mass differences of Sn isotopes (66 ≤ N ≤ 80), using Hartree-Fock (HF) and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) approximations with the Gaussian expansion method. The density-dependent contact force contribution to pairing was fully taken into account in the new fits, unlike in the previous M3Y-P4 and M3Y-P5 sets. The non-central channels (spin-orbit and tensor) were kept identical to the previous sets.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: k_F0 = 1.340 fm^-1 for both M3Y-P4' and M3Y-P5'
- Saturation energy: E_0 = -16.05 MeV (M3Y-P4') and -16.14 MeV (M3Y-P5')
- Incompressibility: K = 230.4 MeV (M3Y-P4') and 239.1 MeV (M3Y-P5')
- Volume symmetry energy: a_t = 28.49 MeV (M3Y-P4') and 28.42 MeV (M3Y-P5')
- Effective k-mass: M*_0/M = 0.653 (M3Y-P4') and 0.637 (M3Y-P5')
- Binding energy of 208Pb: 1634.7 MeV (M3Y-P4') and 1635.7 MeV (M3Y-P5'), compared to experimental 1636.4 MeV
- Binding energy of 16O: 127.8 MeV (M3Y-P4') and 124.1 MeV (M3Y-P5'), compared to experimental 127.6 MeV
- Landau-Migdal parameter g'_0 = 1.033 (M3Y-P4') and 1.007 (M3Y-P5'), close to the known value g'_0 ≈ 1
- The heaviest bound Ca nucleus has N = 50 and the heaviest bound Ni nucleus has N = 66 for both M3Y-P4' and M3Y-P5'

## Context

This work extends the author's previous development of semi-realistic M3Y-type interactions (Nakada, 2003; Nakada, 2008) by correcting the treatment of the density-dependent contact force in pairing calculations. The new parameter-sets are compared with the Gogny D1S (Berger et al.) and D1M (Goriely et al.) interactions. The results enable improved nuclear structure studies via mean-field calculations and their extensions, particularly for investigating shell structure evolution and neutron drip line predictions in medium-mass nuclei.
