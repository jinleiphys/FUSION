# $KΛ(1405)$ configuration of the $K\bar{K}N$ system

> We study the $KΛ(1405)$ configuration of the $K\bar{K}N$ system by considering $KπΣ$ as a coupled channel. We solve the Faddeev equations for these systems and find confirmation of the existence of a new $N^{*}$ resonance around 1920 MeV with $J^π=1/2^{+}$ predicted in a single-channel potential model and also found in a Faddeev calculation as an $a_{0}(980)N$ state, with the $a_{0}(980)$ generated in the $K\bar{K}$, $πη$ interaction.

## Key claim
The paper confirms the existence of a new \(N^{*}\) resonance around 1920 MeV with \(J^{\pi}=1/2^{+}\) in the \(K\bar{K}N\) system, but the key hedge is that the \(K\pi\Sigma\) and \(K\pi\Lambda\) coupled channels do not alter the peak position or width found when only the \(K\bar{K}N\) channel is considered, meaning the resonance is primarily a \(K\bar{K}N\) state rather than being significantly modified by the additional channels.

## Method
The authors solved the Faddeev equations for the three-body \(K\bar{K}N\), \(K\pi\Sigma\), and \(K\pi\Lambda\) systems with total charge zero, using two-body \(t\)-matrices obtained from chiral Lagrangians via the Bethe-Salpeter equation in a unitary coupled-channel approach. The three-body \(T\)-matrix was projected onto S-wave and total isospin \(I=1/2\), and the squared amplitude \(|T_R|^2\) was evaluated as a function of the total three-body energy \(\sqrt{s}\) and the invariant mass of the \(\bar{K}N\) or \(K\bar{K}\) subsystem.

## Key numbers
- The \(N^{*}\) resonance appears at \(\sqrt{s} \sim 1922\) MeV when the \(\bar{K}N\) subsystem (isospin 0) has an invariant mass close to 1428 MeV.
- The resonance also appears at \(\sqrt{s} \sim 1923\) MeV when the \(K\bar{K}\) subsystem (isospin 1) has an invariant mass around 987 MeV.
- The ratio of \(|T_R|^2\) for \(I_{\bar{K}N}=1\) to \(I_{\bar{K}N}=0\) at \((\sqrt{s},\sqrt{s_{\bar{K}N}})=(1923,1428)\) MeV is \(\sim 0.008\).
- The ratio of \(|T_R|^2\) for \(I_{\bar{K}K}=0\) to \(I_{\bar{K}K}=1\) at \((\sqrt{s},\sqrt{s_{\bar{K}K}})=(1923,987)\) MeV is \(\sim 1\).
- In the \(K\pi\Sigma\) channel alone (without \(K\bar{K}N\)), a weaker signal appears at \(\sqrt{s}\sim 1915\) MeV with \(\sqrt{s_{\pi\Sigma}}\sim 1415\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends prior predictions of a new \(N^{*}\) resonance around 1920 MeV found in a single-channel variational method by Jido (2010) and in a Faddeev calculation by Martinez Torres (2010) as an \(a_{0}(980)N\) state. The present study explicitly includes the \(K\pi\Sigma\) and \(K\pi\Lambda\) coupled channels to test whether the \(\Lambda(1405)\) double-pole structure (with a lower pole around 1390 MeV coupling strongly to \(\pi\Sigma\) and an upper pole around 1426 MeV coupling to \(\bar{K}N\)) affects the resonance. The finding that the \(K\pi\Sigma\) channel does not alter the resonance confirms the dominance of the \(K\bar{K}N\) configuration and enables future searches for this state in reactions such as \(\gamma p \to K^{+}\Lambda\), where its existence remains controversial.
