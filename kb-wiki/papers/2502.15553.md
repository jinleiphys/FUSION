# Examination of the multiple-scattering expansion in the four-nucleon system

> The elastic neutron-${}^3\mathrm{H}$ scattering at intermediate energies is studied using rigorous integral equations solved in the momentum-space partial-wave basis. The four-particle transition operators are expanded into multiple-scattering series in terms of subsystem transition operators. Various approximations resulting from truncation of the series in different ways are evaluated and their validity is investigated. They fail at lower energies but at higher energies provide a rough reproduction of exact results at small scattering angles. In the large-angle region all approximations fail heavily, indicating that the scattering amplitude results from a delicate interplay of many multiple-scattering terms. The partial-wave analysis reveals that the developed approximations are reliable in higher partial waves, and for practical calculations an efficient ``hybrid'' approach is proposed, combining exact amplitudes in lower partial waves with approximations in higher partial waves. The implications for often used approximation of the first order in two-body transition matrix and development of microscopic optical potentials are discussed.

# Examination of the multiple-scattering expansion in the four-nucleon system

## Key claim

The multiple-scattering series truncated at low order (first, second, or third order in subsystem transition operators, or first order in the two-nucleon transition operator) fails at lower energies (50 MeV) but at higher energies (100–150 MeV) provides a rough reproduction of exact results at small scattering angles (up to about 50 degrees); however, in the large-angle region all approximations fail heavily, indicating that the scattering amplitude results from a delicate interplay of many multiple-scattering terms.

## Method

The elastic neutron-³H scattering is studied using the Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) integral equations solved in the momentum-space partial-wave basis. The four-particle transition operators are expanded into a multiple-scattering series in terms of subsystem (3+1 and 2+2) transition operators, and also in terms of the two-nucleon transition operator. The truncated series approximations (U1, U2, U3 for subsystem operators up to first, second, and third order; T1 for first order in the two-nucleon operator) are compared with the exact solution of the AGS equations at beam energies of 50, 100, and 150 MeV.

## Key numbers

- At 150 MeV beam energy, the U3 approximation approaches exact results at angles up to about Θ_c.m. = 50°; the U2 and T1 approximations at 150 MeV still lead to 10–20% and 30–40% overestimation of the differential cross section, respectively.
- At 50 MeV beam energy, all approximations fail to reproduce even the shape of the nucleon analyzing power.
- The partial-wave analysis shows that for total angular momentum J ≤ 4, none of the approximations U2, U3, or T1 is accurate; for higher J, all three approximations approach the exact results.
- The hybrid approach combining exact amplitudes for J ≤ 4 with T1 or U2 approximations for higher partial waves (up to J = 11) reproduces the exactly calculated observables well at 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends rigorous four-nucleon scattering calculations (Deltuva 2013, 2015) to higher energies (up to 150 MeV) and systematically tests the convergence of the multiple-scattering expansion. The study is motivated by the common use of Born-type approximations and first-order optical potentials in nucleon-nucleus scattering (Crespo 2006, PhysRevC.74.044616). The results provide guidance for developing microscopic optical potentials (Vorabbi, in progress) and suggest that a hybrid approach combining exact low-partial-wave amplitudes with approximate high-partial-wave amplitudes is accurate and computationally efficient.
