# Symmetry violations in nuclear and neutron $β$ decay

> The role of $β$ decay as a low-energy probe of physics beyond the Standard Model is reviewed. Traditional searches for deviations from the Standard Model structure of the weak interaction in $β$ decay are discussed in the light of constraints from the LHC and the neutrino mass. Limits on the violation of time-reversal symmetry in $β$ decay are compared to the strong constraints from electric dipole moments. Novel searches for Lorentz symmetry breaking in the weak interaction in $β$ decay are also included, where we discuss the unique sensitivity of $β$ decay to test Lorentz invariance. We end with a roadmap for future $β$-decay experiments.

## Key claim

β decay provides a unique low-energy probe of physics beyond the Standard Model, but the sensitivity of traditional searches for exotic couplings must be re-evaluated in light of constraints from the LHC and neutrino mass measurements, while novel searches for Lorentz symmetry breaking in the weak interaction offer signals with no Standard Model background.

## Method

The authors review the effective field theory (EFT) framework for β decay, parametrizing deviations from the Standard Model V-A structure through dimension-6 operators that generate scalar, tensor, and right-handed vector couplings. They compare bounds from β-decay correlation coefficients (such as the β-ν asymmetry a, the Fierz interference term b, and the triple-correlation coefficients D and R) with constraints from LHC data, neutrino mass measurements, and electric dipole moment searches. For Lorentz violation, they employ the Standard Model Extension (SME) to describe how constant background tensor fields would produce sidereal variations in β-decay observables, focusing on the minimal SME with dimension-3 and dimension-4 operators.

## Key numbers

- Neutron decay axial-vector coupling: |g_A| = 1.2723(23) from neutron β-decay experiments (PDG 2014)
- The vector charge g_V(0) = 1, up to small corrections from conserved vector current (CVC) hypothesis
- Weak magnetism form factor g_M given by (μ_p - μ_n)/2, the difference between proton and neutron magnetic moments
- The Fierz interference term b is zero in the Standard Model; its measurement constrains scalar and tensor couplings
- Triple-correlation coefficient D is T-odd and P-even; its nonzero value would indicate time-reversal violation

## Context

This review extends the traditional analysis of β-decay searches for new physics (Severijns et al., Holstein) by incorporating constraints from LHC experiments and neutrino oscillation measurements. It also introduces the novel application of β decay to test Lorentz invariance, building on the SME framework developed by Kostelecký and collaborators (1990s-2000s) and recent theoretical extensions to β decay by Nooij et al. (2013). The paper provides a roadmap for future β-decay experiments, identifying which measurements remain most competitive given bounds from other frontiers such as electric dipole moment searches and collider data.
