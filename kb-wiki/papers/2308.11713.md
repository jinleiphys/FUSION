# The McDIPPER: A novel saturation-based 3+1D initial state model for Heavy Ion Collisions

> We present a new 3D resolved model for the initial state of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, based on the $k_\perp$-factorized Color Glass Condensate hybrid approach. The McDIPPER framework responds to the need for a rapidity-resolved initial-state Monte Carlo event generator which can deposit the relevant conserved charges (energy, charge and baryon densities) both in the midrapidity and forward/backward regions of the collision. This event-by-event generator computes the gluon and (anti-) quark phase-space densities using the IP-Sat model, from where the relevant conserved charges can be computed directly. In the present work we have included the leading order contributions to the light flavor parton densities. As a feature, the model can be systematically improved in the future by adding next-to-leading order calculations (in the CGC hybrid framework), and extended to lower energies by including sub-eikonal corrections the channels included. We present relevant observables, such as the eccentricities and flow decorrelation, as tests of this new approach.

## Key claim

The McDIPPER framework provides a 3+1D initial-state model for heavy-ion collisions based on leading-order \(k_\perp\)-factorized Color Glass Condensate (CGC) cross-sections, but the authors explicitly note that the current version includes only leading-order contributions to light flavor parton densities and can be systematically improved by adding next-to-leading order calculations and sub-eikonal corrections for lower energies.

## Method

The model computes event-by-event initial energy and conserved charge (u, d, s quark) densities by evaluating leading-order single-inclusive gluon and quark production cross-sections in the dilute-dense CGC hybrid approach, using the IP-Sat model for the dipole scattering amplitude and collinear parton distributions from the LHAPDF library. The phase-space distributions are identified with space-time rapidity via \(\delta(y-\eta_s)\), and moments of the spectra yield the 3+1D profiles of energy density \((e\tau)_0\) and net quark densities \((n_f\tau)_0\). Nucleon positions are sampled from a Wood-Saxon distribution with rejection sampling of the nucleon overlap probability, and the relevant integrals are pre-tabulated as functions of nuclear thickness and rapidity for efficient event generation.

## Key numbers

* The nucleon size parameter \(B_G = 0.156\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) is determined through fits to HERA data.
* The GBW saturation scale uses \(Q_{p,0}^2 = 0.152\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and \(\lambda = 0.215\).
* The IP-Sat model uses a charm quark mass of either \(m_c = 1.27\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (Set 1) or \(m_c = 1.4\ \mathrm{GeV}\) (Set 2).
* The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross-section parametrization is \(\sigma_{\mathrm{inel}} = 25.2 + 0.05\log(\sqrt{s_{NN}}) + 0.56\log^2(\sqrt{s_{NN}}) + 45.2(\sqrt{s_{NN}})^{-0.9} + 33.8(\sqrt{s_{NN}})^{-1.1}\) mb.
* Pre-tabulation for a 3D grid with 101 points in each of the \(T_1\), \(T_2\), and \(\eta_s\) directions requires 2-3 CPU hours.

## Context

This work extends saturation-based initial-state models such as IP-Glasma (Schenke, 2012), EKRT (Eskola, 1999), and MC-KLN (Drescher, 2006), which were primarily designed for 2+1D midrapidity simulations, to a full 3+1D framework that includes forward/backward rapidity regions. It responds to the need for rapidity-resolved initial conditions for 3+1D hydrodynamic codes like MUSIC (Schenke, 2010) and VHLLE (Karpenko, 2013), and aims to eventually connect saturation-based models at high energies with hadronic transport approaches such as SMASH (Schafer, 2021) at lower energies. The model enables future studies of longitudinal decorrelation of flow harmonics and the rapidity dependence of conserved charge densities.
