# Cluster model of 12C in density functional theory framework

> We employ the constrained density functional theory to investigate cluster phenomena for the $^{12}$C nucleus. The proton and neutron densities are generated from the placement of three $^{4}$He nuclei (alpha particles) geometrically. These densities are then used in a density constrained Hartree-Fock calculation that produces an antisymmetrized state with the same densities through energy minimization. In the calculations no \textit{a priori} analytic form for the single-particle states is assumed and the full energy density functional is utilized. The geometrical scan of the energy landscape provides the ground state of $^{12}$C as an equilateral triangular configuration of three alphas with molecular bond like structures. The use of the nucleon localization function provides further insight to these configurations. One can conclude that these configurations are a hybrid between a pure mean-field and a pure alpha particle condensate. This development could facilitate DFT based fusion calculations with a more realistic $^{12}$C ground state.

## Key claim

The constrained density functional theory approach yields a ground state of ¹²C as an equilateral triangular configuration of three alpha particles with molecular bond-like structures, which the authors describe as a hybrid between a pure mean-field and a pure alpha particle condensate, rather than a pure alpha condensate.

## Method

The authors placed three ⁴He nuclei (alpha particles) geometrically on a numerical grid, using their proton and neutron densities obtained from independent Hartree-Fock calculations. They then performed a density constrained Hartree-Fock calculation that produced an antisymmetrized Slater determinant state with the same densities through energy minimization, using the full Skyrme SLy4d energy density functional with no assumed analytic form for single-particle states. The energy landscape was scanned by varying the spacing between two alphas (d₂ from 1.5 to 6.6 fm) and the vertical position of the third alpha (z₃ from 0.7 to 5.0 fm), and the nucleon localization function (NLF) was used to characterize spatial distributions.

## Key numbers

- Minimum energy configuration found at d₂ = 2.65 fm and z₃ = 2.3 fm, corresponding to an equilateral triangle placement of alpha particles
- Calculated mass radius of ¹²C: 2.57 fm (compared to experimentally deduced 2.43 fm and cluster model range 2.40-2.53 fm)
- Calculated quadrupole deformation: β₂ = -0.42 and γ = 29.7° (compared to experimental β₂ = -0.4 and cluster model β₂ = -0.41, γ = 27.5°)
- Bent-arm configuration found at d₂ = 5.0 fm and z₃ = 2.2 fm
- Overlap between ground state and bent-arm state Slater determinants: on the order of 10⁻³
- Angular momentum projection shows major component J=0 with significant J=2 component and little contribution from J>2

## Context

This work extends the density functional theory approach to cluster phenomena in light nuclei, addressing the limitation that standard non-relativistic DFT calculations (e.g., Reinhard (2011)) produce a spherical ground state for ¹²C without clusterization, while experimental evidence shows oblate deformation. The method contrasts with cluster wavefunction approaches such as the Bloch-Brink alpha cluster model (Brink, 1966), antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (Kanada-En'yo, 1995), and fermionic molecular dynamics (Neff, 2004), which assume Gaussian wave-packets and often neglect the full effective interaction and spin-orbit force. This development could enable DFT-based fusion calculations using a more realistic ¹²C ground state, and the preparation of alpha clustering configurations can be used for developing and quantifying new energy density functionals.
