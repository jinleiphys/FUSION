# A quark-meson coupling model based on Bogoliubov's model of the nucleon

> The quark-meson coupling model due to Guichon is formulated on the basis of the independent quark model of the nucleon proposed by Bogoliubov and is applied to a phenomenological description of symmetric nuclear matter. The model predicts, at saturation density, the compressibility $K=249$ MeV and the quark effective mass $m_q^*= 249.1$ MeV, {the effective nucleon mass being $M^*=747.3$ MeV}. The predicted nucleon mass radius is $r=0.93$ fm.

## Key claim

The paper reformulates Guichon's quark-meson coupling (QMC) model using Bogoliubov's independent quark model with a linearly rising potential, rather than the MIT bag model, and finds that this approach predicts a slightly less stiff equation of state for symmetric nuclear matter than the original QMC model, with the compressibility at saturation density being 249.1 MeV.

## Method

The authors start from Bogoliubov's Hamiltonian for three independent valence quarks bound by a linear potential with string tension κ, then introduce an external scalar σ field (as in the QMC model) that couples to the quarks via a term g_σ σ added to the linear potential. They compute the quark effective mass m_q(σ) by solving the squared Dirac Hamiltonian in a truncated basis of ℓ=0 and ℓ=1 wavefunctions, including center-of-mass corrections, and then minimize the nuclear matter energy density functional (with scalar σ and vector ω meson fields) to obtain saturation properties.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility K = 249.1 MeV at saturation density (from the full model with center-of-mass corrections, eq. (10) in Table 1)
- Quark effective mass at saturation m_q* = 249.1 MeV (from the abstract; Table 1 gives m_q*/m_q = 0.80)
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation M* = 747.3 MeV (abstract) or 755.49 MeV (Table 1, eq. (10))
- Nucleon mass radius r = 0.93 fm (abstract; more precisely 0.930548 fm from the text)
- Binding energy at saturation: E/ρ_B - M_N = -15.7 MeV (input)
- Free nucleon mass M_N = 939 MeV (input)
- String tension κ = 4.60 × 10^4 MeV^2 (Table 1, eq. (10))
- Scalar meson mass m_σ = 550 MeV, vector meson mass m_ω = 783 MeV (inputs)
- Quark mass in vacuum m_q = 313 MeV (input)

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model originally formulated by Guichon (1988) using the MIT bag model, by instead adopting Bogoliubov's independent quark model (1967) with a linearly rising potential inspired by QCD string theory. The approach is similar in spirit to earlier QMC implementations based on a quadratically rising potential by Batista and Barik. The model predicts a compressibility (249 MeV) close to that of the derivative-coupling Zimanyi-Moszkowski model (225 MeV), but with a slightly larger K/|W_0| ratio. The authors note that the string tension obtained (≈0.46 GeV/fm) is less than one quarter of lattice QCD values, and suggest that a one-dimensional oscillator treatment would yield a string tension closer to 0.655 GeV/fm, leaving refinement of the hyperfine structure for future work.
