# Baryon transition form factors at the pole

> Electromagnetic resonance properties are uniquely defined at the pole and do not depend on the separation of the resonance from background or the decay channel. Photon-nucleon branching ratios are nowadays often quoted at the pole, and we generalize the considerations to the case of virtual photons. We derive and compare relations for nucleon to baryon transition form factors both for the Breit-Wigner and the pole positions. Using the MAID2007 and SAID SM08 partial wave analyses of pion electroproduction data, we compare the $G_M$, $G_E$, and $G_C$ form factors for the $Δ(1232)$ resonance excitation at the Breit-Wigner resonance and pole positions up to $Q^2=5$ GeV$^2$. We also explore the $E/M$ and $S/M$ ratios as functions of $Q^2$. For pole and residue extraction, we apply the Laurent + Pietarinen method.

## Key claim
Electromagnetic resonance properties for baryon transitions are uniquely defined at the pole and do not depend on separating resonance from background, but the continuation of fit amplitudes to the pole is itself a possible source of error; the authors extract pole-valued transition form factors for the Δ(1232) resonance from two partial-wave analyses (MAID2007 and SAID SM08) to quantify the uncertainty arising from differences in those analyses and their underlying experimental databases.

## Method
The authors applied the Laurent-plus-Pietarinen (L+P) method to extract pole positions and residues from the MAID2007 and SAID SM08 energy-dependent partial-wave analyses of pion electroproduction data. They fitted the E1+, M1+, and S1+ multipoles for the Δ(1232) resonance simultaneously from threshold up to 2 GeV center-of-mass energy, at Q² values from 0 to 5 GeV². The L+P method uses a Laurent expansion for the pole part and conformal-mapping expansions (Pietarinen series) for the regular background, with branch points fixed to represent physical thresholds.

## Key numbers
- Pole position used for Δ(1232): W_p = (1210 − 50 i) MeV with πN residue Res_πN = 50 e^(−i 47°)
- At Q² = 0, magnetic form factor G_M (BW) = 2.97 (MAID) and 3.11 (SAID); G_M (pole absolute value) = 3.20 (MAID) and 3.38 (SAID)
- At Q² = 0, E/M ratio R_EM (BW) = −0.022 (MAID) and −0.016 (SAID); R_EM (pole absolute value) = −0.063 (MAID) and −0.054 (SAID)
- At Q² = 0, S/M ratio R_SM (BW) = −0.042 (MAID) and −0.044 (SAID); R_SM (pole absolute value) = −0.067 (MAID) and −0.069 (SAID)
- At Q² = 0, helicity amplitude A_1/2 (BW) = −0.131 × 10⁻³/GeV^(1/2) (MAID) and −0.139 (SAID); A_1/2 (pole absolute value) = −0.131 (MAID) and −0.142 (SAID)
- At Q² = 0, helicity amplitude A_3/2 (BW) = −0.247 × 10⁻³/GeV^(1/2) (MAID) and −0.258 (SAID); A_3/2 (pole absolute value) = −0.261 (MAID) and −0.273 (SAID)
- At Q² = 0, helicity amplitude S_1/2 (BW) = 0.016 × 10⁻³/GeV^(1/2) (MAID) and 0.018 (SAID); S_1/2 (pole absolute value) = 0.027 (MAID) and 0.030 (SAID)
- Analysis covers Q² from 0 to 5 GeV², with finer steps of 0.1 GeV² near the real-photon point and 1 GeV² steps above

## Context
This work extends a previous study by Workman et al. (2013) of photo-decay couplings at the pole to the regime of non-zero Q² for virtual photons. It provides pole-valued transition form factors for the Δ(1232) that can be compared with lattice QCD simulations (Alexandrou et al., 2013; 2010) and chiral effective field theory calculations (Gail and Hemmert, 2006), where the finite resonance width complicates extraction. The results enable future comparisons with the method proposed by Agadjanov et al. (2014) for determining transition form factors at the pole in lattice simulations near the physical point, and update earlier MAID2003 and SAID2002 fits that had indicated a cross-over to positive E/M values which has now disappeared with new data.
