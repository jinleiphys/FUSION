# N* Masses from QCD Sum Rules

> We report N* masses in the spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 sectors using the method of QCD Sum Rules. They are based on three independent sets derived from generalized interpolating fields. The predictive ability of each sum rule is examined by a Monte-Carlo based analysis procedure in which all three phenomenological parameters (mass, coupling, threshold) are extracted simultaneously. A parity projection technique is also studied.

# N* Masses from QCD Sum Rules

## Key claim

QCD sum rules can predict low-lying N* masses in the spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 sectors with an accuracy of about 100 MeV (5 to 10%), provided that a Monte-Carlo based analysis extracts all three phenomenological parameters (mass, coupling, threshold) simultaneously and that a parity projection technique is applied for negative-parity states.

## Method

The authors constructed three independent sets of correlation functions from generalized interpolating fields for spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 baryons, varying the mixing parameter beta to achieve maximal overlap with states of a given parity. They applied a Monte-Carlo based numerical analysis procedure that treats mass, coupling, and continuum threshold as free parameters extracted simultaneously with error bars, and implemented a parity projection technique using a time-ordered correlation function in complex p0-space in the rest frame to separate positive and negative parity contributions exactly.

## Key numbers

- N(1/2+) mass predicted as 1.06 ± 0.11 GeV (beta = +1.0) compared to experimental 0.938 GeV, from chiral-odd sum rules at the gamma_mu p_nu p-hat structure
- N(3/2-) mass predicted as 1.44 ± 0.13 GeV compared to experimental 1.520 GeV, from chiral-odd sum rules at the g_mu_nu structure
- N(1/2-) mass predicted as 1.53 ± 0.05 GeV (beta = +1.1) compared to experimental 1.535 GeV, using the parity projection method with Borel region 0.80 to 1.80 GeV
- Sigma(1/2-) mass predicted as 1.63 ± 0.07 GeV compared to experimental 1.620 GeV, with Borel region 0.80 to 1.90 GeV
- Xi(1/2-) mass predicted as 1.61 ± 0.08 GeV compared to experimental 1.620 GeV, with Borel region 0.80 to 1.80 GeV
- Lambda(1/2-) mass predicted as 1.67 ± 0.05 GeV compared to experimental 1.670 GeV, with Borel region 0.90 to 1.80 GeV
- The physical point for the quark condensate parameter a = -(2pi)^2 <q-bar q> is 0.52 GeV^3

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to baryon masses, originally developed by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and Ioffe (1981), by focusing on excited N* states rather than ground-state baryons. It builds on the Monte-Carlo based analysis procedure introduced by Leinweber (1997) and applies a parity projection technique analogous to that used in lattice QCD calculations of N* masses by Zhou and Lee (2001). The results enable systematic study of parity partner splittings in terms of the quark condensate as the order parameter of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, with the authors reporting a clear decrease in the N(1/2-)-N(1/2+) splitting as the quark condensate decreases.
