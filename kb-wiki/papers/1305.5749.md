# Nuclear effective charge factor originating from covariant density functional theory

> Guiding by the relativistic local density approximation, we explore a phenomenological formula for the coupling strength of Coulomb field to take into account the Coulomb exchange term effectively in the relativistic Hartree approximation. Its validity in finite nuclei is examined by comparing with the exact treatment of the Coulomb exchange energies in the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach. It is found that the exact Coulomb exchange energies can be reproduced by employing the phenomenological formula with the relative deviations less than 1% for semi-magic Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes. Furthermore, we check the applicability of the phenomenological formula for the effective interactions in the relativistic Hartree approach by investigating the binding energy differences of mirror nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors propose a phenomenological formula for an effective charge factor \(\eta(Z,A)\) that can incorporate Coulomb exchange effects into the relativistic Hartree (RH) approximation without performing the full, computationally expensive Fock term. The formula reproduces exact Coulomb exchange energies from relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) calculations with relative deviations under 1% for semi-magic isotopes, but the paper's abstract does not emphasize that the formula is fitted specifically to RHFB results with the PKA1 interaction and that its parameters are not derived from first principles.

## Method

The authors first compute exact Coulomb direct and exchange energies for semi-magic Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes using the RHFB approach with the PKA1 effective interaction in the particle-hole channel and D1S in the particle-particle channel. From these exact energies, they extract an effective charge factor \(\eta = \sqrt{1 + E_{\text{Cex}}/E_{\text{Cdir}}}\) for each nucleus. Guided by the relativistic local density approximation (RLDA), they fit a four-parameter formula \(\eta(Z,A) = 1 - aZ^b + cA^d\) to these exact factors. They then test the formula in one-step (non-self-consistent) and self-consistent RH calculations, comparing the resulting Coulomb exchange energies and binding energy differences of mirror nuclei against the exact RHFB results.

## Key numbers

* The fitted parameters for the effective charge factor formula \(\eta(Z,A) = 1 - aZ^b + cA^d\) are \(a = 0.366958\), \(b = -0.645775\), \(c = 0.030379\), \(d = -0.398341\).
* For Pb isotopes, the relative deviation of Coulomb exchange energies from exact RHFB results is smaller than \(0.5\%\) when using the phenomenological effective charge factor, compared to \(5.2\%\sim 8.1\%\) for the nonrelativistic LDA (NRLDA) and \(1.7\%\sim 3.6\%\) for the RLDA.
* For self-consistent calculations across semi-magic Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopes, the relative deviations of Coulomb exchange energies with the phenomenological effective charge factor are less than \(1\%\), while the NRLDA shows a maximum deviation exceeding \(8\%\).
* For the example nucleus \(^{132}\)Sn, the absolute ratio between the exact Coulomb exchange energy and the Coulomb direct energy is about \(0.05\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic local density approximation (RLDA) for Coulomb exchange developed by Gu et al. (2012), which achieved relative deviations under 5% for semi-magic isotopes but still showed systematic deviations for heavy nuclei like Sn and Pb. The proposed phenomenological formula eliminates those systematic deviations and achieves sub-1% accuracy, making it suitable for efficient RH calculations of isospin-sensitive observables. The approach enables improved treatment of Coulomb exchange in RH models such as NL3 (Lalazissis 1997), PKDD (Long 2004), and DD-ME2 (Lalazissis 2005), as demonstrated by the improved description of binding energy differences of mirror nuclei.
