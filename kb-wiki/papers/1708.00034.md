# Probing the Efimov discrete scaling in atom-molecule collision

> The discrete Efimov scaling behavior, well-known in the low-energy spectrum of three-body bound systems for large scattering lengths (unitary limit), is identified in the energy dependence of atom-molecule elastic cross-section in mass imbalanced systems. That happens in the collision of a heavy atom with mass $m_H$ with a weakly-bound dimer formed by the heavy atom and a lighter one with mass $m_L \ll m_H$. Approaching the heavy-light unitary limit the $s-$wave elastic cross-section $σ$ will present a sequence of zeros/minima at collision energies following closely the Efimov geometrical law. Our results open a new perspective to detect the discrete scaling behavior from low-energy scattering data, which is timely in view of the ongoing experiments with ultra-cold binary mixtures having strong mass asymmetries, such as Lithium and Caesium or Lithium and Ytterbium.

# Probing the Efimov discrete scaling in atom-molecule collision

## Key claim
The discrete Efimov scaling factor, previously observed in three-body bound-state spectra and recombination rates, can be identified in the energy dependence of the s-wave elastic cross-section for atom-molecule collisions in mass-imbalanced systems, specifically when a heavy atom collides with a weakly-bound dimer formed by that heavy atom and a much lighter one, provided the heavy-light scattering length is tuned near the unitary limit.

## Method
The authors computed s-wave phase shifts for the H-(HL) collision system using the three-body Faddeev formalism with both renormalized zero-range interactions and finite-range Gaussian potentials, as well as the Born-Oppenheimer approximation. They analyzed the positions of zeros and minima in the elastic cross-section as functions of collision energy for various mass ratios A = mL/mH ranging from 0.01 to 0.08, with fixed ratios of two-body to three-body binding energies B2/B3 = 0.01, 0.03, and 0.05. The scaling function relating successive minima energies was extracted and compared to the Efimov geometric factor exp(2π/s0).

## Key numbers
- For mass ratio A = 0.01, the Efimov geometric factor exp(2π/s0) = 4.698, decreasing from the identical-boson value of approximately 515.
- For A = 0.01 with B2/B3 = 0.01, up to six zeros/minima in the s-wave cross-section appear within the energy range E/B3 < 1.
- For A = 0.08, only one zero in the cross-section appears within the same energy range.
- The Gaussian potential calculations used aHL/r0 = 50 and B2/B3 = 0.0012.
- The ratio between successive minima energies approaches the Efimov limit exp(2π/s0) as 1/(E_{n+1}^{1/2} aHL) approaches zero.
- For the Li-Yb system, the mass ratio A = 0.034.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of Efimov physics from bound-state spectra (Braaten and Hammer, 2006) and three-body recombination rate measurements in Cs-Li mixtures (Tung et al., 2014; Ulmanis et al., 2016) to the scattering regime of atom-molecule collisions. It builds on earlier studies of poles in k cot δ0 for tri-nucleon systems (Delves, 1960; van Oers, 1967; Whiting, 1976; Girard, 1979) and for neutron-19C scattering (Yamashita, 2008; Shalchi, 2016; Deltuva, 2017). The results suggest that cold-atom experiments with mass-imbalanced mixtures such as Li-Cs (A = 0.045) or Li-Yb (A = 0.034) could observe the Efimov discrete scaling by measuring cross-section minima at geometrically spaced collision energies, complementing existing trap setups with Yb-Li mixtures (Hara, 2011; Hansen, 2011).
