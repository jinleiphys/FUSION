# Multiplicative renormalizability of quasi-parton operators

> Extracting parton distribution functions (PDFs) from lattice QCD calculation of quasi-PDFs has been actively pursued in recent years. We extend our proof of the multiplicative renormalizability of quasi-quark operators in Ref. [1] to quasi-gluon operators, and demonstrated that quasi-gluon operators could be multiplicatively renormalized to all orders in perturbation theory, without mixing with other operators. We find that using a gauge-invariant UV regulator is essential for achieving this proof. With the multiplicative renormalizability of both quasi-quark and quasi-gluon operators, and QCD collinear factorization of hadronic matrix elements of there operators into PDFs, extracting PDFs from lattice QCD calculated hadronic matrix elements of quasi-parton operators could have a solid theoretical foundation.

## Key claim

The authors extend their previous proof of multiplicative renormalizability for quasi-quark operators to quasi-gluon operators, demonstrating that all 36 independent quasi-gluon operators can be multiplicatively renormalized to all orders in perturbation theory without mixing with other operators, provided a gauge-invariant UV regulator (such as dimensional regularization) is used. The key qualifier is that using a cutoff regularization that breaks gauge symmetry would leave uncanceled linear divergences that would make multiplicative renormalization impossible.

## Method

The authors perform an explicit one-loop calculation of quasi-gluon operators for an asymptotic gluon of momentum p using dimensional regularization (DR) to regularize both logarithmic and linear UV divergences. They then analyze all possible UV divergent topologies of higher-order diagrams using power counting and generalized Ward identities to demonstrate that linear UV divergences from the gluon-gauge-link vertex cancel to all orders. The proof relies on showing that UV divergences are localized in coordinate spacetime and that gauge invariance removes the potentially problematic linear divergences.

## Key numbers

- One-loop diagram (a) contributes both linear and logarithmic UV divergences: \(-\frac{\pi \mu_r\xi_z}{3-d}\) (linear) and \(\frac{1}{4-d}\) (logarithmic), where \(\mu_r\) is a renormalization scale and \(d\) is the spacetime dimension
- One-loop diagram (b) contributes only linear UV divergence: \(\frac{-i}{p_z \xi_z} \frac{\pi \mu_r \xi_z}{3-d}\)
- One-loop diagram (c) contributes both linear and logarithmic UV divergences: \(\frac{i}{p_z \xi_z} \frac{\pi \mu_r \xi_z}{3-d}\) (linear) and \(\frac{3}{4} \frac{1}{4-d}\) (logarithmic)
- The linear UV divergences from diagrams (b) and (c) cancel when summed, with no cancellation if a cutoff regularization is used instead of DR
- The gluon-gauge-link vertex \(\Gamma^{\lambda \mu \nu}(p,n)\) has four possible tensor structures \(\Pi_i^{\lambda \mu \nu}\), with coefficients \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) having mass dimension 0 (at most logarithmic divergent), \(c_3\) having mass dimension -1 (UV finite), and the potentially linearly divergent coefficient \(c_4\) removed by gauge invariance

## Context

This work extends the proof of multiplicative renormalizability from quasi-quark operators (Ishikawa et al., 2017) to quasi-gluon operators, completing the theoretical foundation for quasi-parton operators. The proof is essential for the approach proposed by Ma and Qiu (2014, 2017) to extract PDFs from lattice QCD calculations of "lattice cross sections" via QCD collinear factorization. The result enables future lattice QCD calculations of hadronic matrix elements of quasi-gluon operators to be used in global QCD analyses for extracting PDFs, similar to how experimental cross sections constrain PDFs. The paper notes that Zhang et al. (2018) reached similar conclusions using a different approach.
