# Chiral Perturbation Theory and Nuclear Physics

> Chiral symmetry serves as a guiding principle in low-energy hadron dynamics. An effective lagrangian, which explicitly breaks chiral symmetry via a small mass term, allows for a systematic method of calculating higher order corrections to tree-level results. This is applied to radiative muon capture on hydrogen, %to extract the weak pseudoscalar form factor, and to proton-proton $π^0$ production near threshold. Both processes pose theoretical challenges in light of recent experimental results.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) provides a systematic, gauge-invariant framework for low-energy hadronic processes, but its application to nuclei requires a two-step procedure (nuclear ChPT) because naive chiral counting fails for loosely bound many-body systems due to purely nucleonic intermediate states with very low excitation energies.

## Method

The author constructs an effective chiral Lagrangian for pions and heavy nucleons (using the heavy fermion formalism) as an expansion in powers of pion momentum and mass, with chiral order indices assigned to each term and each irreducible Feynman diagram. This Lagrangian is applied to two processes: radiative muon capture on the proton (RMC) at next-to-leading chiral order (NLO) using tree diagrams only, and proton-proton π⁰ production near threshold using the same chiral order to generate one- and two-body diagrams.

## Key numbers

- For RMC at lowest chiral order (ν=0): Γ_RMC = 0.061 s⁻¹ (excluding pion-pole diagrams: 0.043 s⁻¹)
- Including ν=1 (1/M recoil terms): Γ_RMC = 0.075 s⁻¹ (no pion-pole: 0.053 s⁻¹)
- The 1/M recoil corrections account for about 20% of the leading order contribution
- About 30% of the total Γ_RMC value comes from pion-pole exchange diagrams
- For the pp → ppπ⁰ reaction, the exchanged particle at threshold has k₀ = 70 MeV and |k| ≈ 370 MeV/c, giving k² = -m_π M
- The s-wave πN coupling constants from phenomenological Hamiltonians are λ₁ ≈ 0.005 and λ₂ ≈ 0.05
- Early calculations based on phenomenological vertices underestimate measured π⁰ production cross sections by a factor of about 5

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and Weinberg (1990) to two specific low-energy hadronic processes that pose theoretical challenges in light of recent experimental results. For RMC, the author notes that the TRIUMF group extracted a pseudoscalar form factor about 50% larger than expected from PCAC, and the ChPT calculation gives a harder photon spectrum than the phenomenological approach of Beder and Fearing (1987). For pp → ppπ⁰, the work addresses the discrepancy between early calculations by Kroll and Ruderman (1966) and the high-precision measurements by Meyer et al. (1990), where heavy-meson exchange contributions as shown by Lee and Riska (1993) may be needed to explain the observed cross section.
