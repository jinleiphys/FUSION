# Comment on "Valence QCD: Connecting QCD to the Quark Model"

> I criticize certain conclusions about the physics of hadrons drawn from a "valence QCD" approximation to QCD.

# Comment on "Valence QCD: Connecting QCD to the Quark Model"

## Key claim

The claim by Liu et al. (1999) that baryon hyperfine interactions are largely attributed to Goldstone boson exchanges between quarks rather than to standard one-gluon-exchange forces is unjustified, because the reduced Delta-N splitting observed in valence QCD may stem from a dramatically different spectrum and internal hadronic structure rather than from a diminished hyperfine interaction.

## Method

Isgur extracted meson and baryon spectra from the lattice data presented in Liu et al. (1999) for valence QCD and compared them to the spectra of nature and of standard quenched QCD on the same lattices. He examined the orbital excitation energy (the a1-rho mass difference) and the hyperfine splittings in both mesons and baryons as functions of quark mass, and analyzed the scaling behavior of hyperfine splittings in heavy-light systems from the heavy quark limit down to light quarks.

## Key numbers

- The Delta-N splitting in valence QCD is only about 50 MeV, compared to 180 MeV found on the same lattices in standard quenched QCD
- The physical Delta-N splitting is about 300 MeV
- The D*-D splitting is 141 MeV (used as calibration for meson hyperfine scale)
- The Sigma_c-Lambda_c splitting is 169 MeV (used as calibration for baryon hyperfine scale)
- The chi_c2-psi splitting is 459 MeV (used as calibration for quarkonium spectra)
- The OZI-violating amplitude in meson nonets is empirically of order 10 MeV, while the Sigma*-Sigma splitting driven by kaon exchange is about 200 MeV

## Context

This comment criticizes the conclusions of Liu et al. (1999) regarding valence QCD, which was proposed as a field-theoretic approximation to QCD intended to connect to the valence quark model by suppressing Z-graphs. Isgur argues that the valence QCD spectrum is dramatically different from both quenched QCD and nature, making it unreliable for drawing conclusions about the physics of hadrons. The comment extends the long-standing debate between the one-gluon-exchange model (De Rujula, Georgi, and Glashow, 1975; Isgur-Karl model) and Goldstone boson exchange models of hyperfine interactions. Isgur shows that heavy quark symmetry (Isgur and Wise, 1989, 1990) demands hyperfine splittings scale as 1/m_Q in both mesons and baryons, consistent with one-gluon-exchange but not with pseudoscalar meson exchange which would scale as 1/m_Q^2.
