# QCD sum rules with two-point correlation function

> We construct three different sum rules from the two-point correlation function with pion, $i\int d^4x e^{iq\cdot x} <0| T J_N(x) {\bar J}_N(0)|π(p)>$, beyond the soft-pion limit. The PS and PV coupling schemes in the construction of the phenomenological side are carefully considered in each sum rule. We discuss the dependence of the result on the specific Dirac structure and identify the source of the dependence by making specific models for higher resonances.

# QCD sum rules with two-point correlation function

## Key claim
The $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$ Dirac structure provides a sum rule for $g_{\pi N}$ that is independent of the pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) coupling schemes and yields a value close to the empirical coupling, while the $i\gamma_5 \not\!p$ structure yields unreliable results contaminated by unknown single-pole terms and continuum threshold sensitivity.

## Method
Three different QCD sum rules were constructed from the two-point correlation function with a pion, $i\int d^4x e^{iq\cdot x} \langle 0| T J_N(x) {\bar J}_N(0)|\pi(p)\rangle$, beyond the soft-pion limit. The operator product expansion (OPE) was calculated keeping only the quark-antiquark component of the pion wave function, using vacuum saturation to factor higher-dimensional operators. The phenomenological side was constructed using both PS and PV coupling schemes, and the sum rules were analyzed for the $i\gamma_5 \not\!p$, $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$, and $i\gamma_5$ Dirac structures.

## Key numbers
- For the $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$ sum rule, the extracted value of $c = g_{\pi N} \lambda_N^2$ is 0.00308 GeV$^6$ (with continuum threshold $S_\pi = 2.07$ GeV$^2$) and 0.002906 GeV$^6$ (with $S_\pi = 2.57$ GeV$^2$), giving $|\Delta c| = 0.00017$ GeV$^6$.
- For the $i\gamma_5 \not\!p$ sum rule, $c = -0.00022$ GeV$^6$ ($S_\pi = 2.07$ GeV$^2$) and $-0.0023$ GeV$^6$ ($S_\pi = 2.57$ GeV$^2$), giving $|\Delta c| = 0.0021$ GeV$^6$, with $b = 0.011$ GeV$^4$ ($S_\pi = 2.07$ GeV$^2$).
- For the $i\gamma_5$ sum rule, $c = -0.00033$ GeV$^6$ ($S_\pi = 2.07$ GeV$^2$) and $-0.00016$ GeV$^6$ ($S_\pi = 2.57$ GeV$^2$), giving $|\Delta c| = 0.00017$ GeV$^6$.
- The $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$ sum rule combined with the nucleon chiral odd sum rule yields $g_{\pi N} \sim 10$.
- All fits were performed within the Borel window $0.8 \le M^2 \le 1.2$ GeV$^2$.

## Context
This work extends the earlier QCD sum rule calculations of the $\pi NN$ coupling constant by Shiomi and Hatsuda (1994) in the soft-pion limit and by Birse and Krippa (1996) beyond the soft-pion limit using the $i\gamma_5 \not\!p$ structure. The authors had previously shown (Kim, Lee, Oka, 1998) that the $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$ structure yields a coupling-scheme-independent sum rule. The present study systematically compares all three Dirac structures, finding that the $i\gamma_5 \not\!p$ sum rule is unreliable due to contamination from unknown single-pole terms and continuum threshold sensitivity, while the $i\gamma_5$ and $\gamma_5 \sigma_{\mu\nu} q^\mu p^\nu$ sum rules show similar stability but yield different numerical values for $g_{\pi N} \lambda_N^2$, raising questions about OPE convergence or limitations of the sum rule method itself. This work enables future studies of the $i\gamma_5$ sum rule without the kinematical condition $p^2 = 2 p \cdot q$, which would reveal coupling-scheme dependence and provide further insights into the pion-nucleon coupling.
