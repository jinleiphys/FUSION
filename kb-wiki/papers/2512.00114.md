# Characterizing the Neutron Skin of $^{48}$Ca Through Collective Flow at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> The recently developed ``imaging-by-smashing" technique has emerged as a powerful approach to connect final-state collective flow phenomena in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions with the intrinsic structure of the colliding nuclei. While most efforts have focused on constraining nuclear shape properties such as deformation and triaxiality, less attention has been given to the neutron skin, primarily in heavy nuclei such as $^{208}$Pb. In this work, a novel study of the neutron-skin thickness in $^{48}$Ca is presented, based on comparative analyses of $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca and $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV. Simulations within the AMPT framework are employed to investigate the impact of varying neutron-skin thicknesses $Δr_{np}$ for $^{48}$Ca on collective flow observables, including anisotropic flow coefficients and mean transverse momentum ($[p_{\rm T}]$) fluctuations. The triangular flow $v_3$, quadrangular flow $v_4$, as well as the variance and skewness of $[p_{\rm T}]$ fluctuations, display notable sensitivity to $Δr_{np}$. These findings indicate that calcium-isotope runs at the LHC could provide an independent and complementary approach to constraining $Δr_{np}$, with the potential to help resolve the current tension between PREX and CREX measurements.

## Key claim

The paper claims that collective flow observables in ultra-relativistic calcium isotope collisions at the LHC can constrain the neutron-skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca, but only if the differences between $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca collisions are measured under matched experimental conditions, because the predicted signal differences are on the order of 1-2% for flow coefficients and up to 8% for mean transverse momentum fluctuations.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT model with string melting to simulate $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca and $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV, implementing distinct Woods-Saxon distributions for neutrons and protons to model varying neutron-skin thicknesses for $^{48}$Ca. Three scenarios were considered: no neutron skin ($\Delta r_{np}=0$), the CREX lower bound ($\Delta r_{np}=0.068$ fm), and the CREX upper bound ($\Delta r_{np}=0.181$ fm), with the proton radius kept identical between $^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca. Anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n\{2\}$ were extracted using two-particle correlations with an $\eta$-gap of $|\Delta\eta|>0.2$, and mean transverse momentum cumulants $\kappa_1$, $\kappa_2$, and $\kappa_3$ were computed using the Generic Algorithm within $|\eta|<0.8$ and $0.2 < p_{\rm T} < 5.0$ GeV/$c$.

## Key numbers

- $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV with $\Delta r_{np}=0$, $0.068$ fm, and $0.181$ fm; $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca at same energy as baseline
- $v_2\{2\}$ in $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca is approximately 4% lower than in $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca for central collisions (0-20% centrality)
- $v_3\{2\}$ ratio of $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca with $\Delta r_{np}=0$ to $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca deviates from unity by approximately 2% in central collisions, increasing to about 8% in peripheral collisions
- $\tilde{R}(v_3\{2\})$ for $\Delta r_{np}$(CREX, HB) relative to $\Delta r_{np}=0$ shows deviation of 1.5-2% across presented centrality ranges
- $\frac{v_2\{2\}}{v_3\{2\}}$ ratio differs by approximately 5% between $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca in central collisions
- $v_4\{2\}$ from $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca with $\Delta r_{np}=0$ is approximately 2-3% larger than from $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca in 0-30% centrality
- $\kappa_1$ ratios show differences of less than 0.5% across neutron-skin scenarios
- $\kappa_2$ ratios between $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca with $\Delta r_{np}=0$ and $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca deviate from unity by more than 30% in central collisions
- $\kappa_3$ ratios between $^{48}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca with $\Delta r_{np}=0$ and $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca deviate from unity by more than 20% in central collisions
- $\tilde{R}(\kappa_2)$ difference between $\Delta r_{np}$(CREX, LB) and $\Delta r_{np}=0$ is about 3% in 0-30% centrality; difference between $\Delta r_{np}=0$ and $\Delta r_{np}$(CREX, HB) reaches about 8% in central collisions

## Context

This work extends the "imaging-by-smashing" technique, previously applied to nuclear shape properties of $^{76}$Zr, $^{76}$Ru, $^{238}$U, $^{129}$Xe, and $^{208}$Pb, to the neutron-skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca. It addresses the tension between PREX-II (2021) measurements for $^{208}$Pb ($\Delta r_{np}=0.283\pm0.071$ fm) and CREX (2022) measurements for $^{48}$Ca ($\Delta r_{np}=0.121\pm0.05$ fm), as well as discrepancies with ab initio calculations predicting $\Delta r_{np}=0.14-0.20$ fm (Hagen 2015, Hu 2021). Previous high-energy attempts using global Bayesian fits to Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC within the Trajectum hydrodynamic framework (Giacalone 2023) extracted $\Delta r_{np}=0.217\pm0.058$ fm but did not resolve the tension. The present study proposes that future calcium isotope runs at the LHC, with matched experimental conditions similar to the isobaric Ru-Zr runs at RHIC (Jia 2022), could provide complementary constraints on the neutron-skin thickness of $^{48}$Ca.
