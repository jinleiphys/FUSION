# Improved Nuclear Physics Near $A=61$ Refines Urca Neutrino Luminosities in Accreted Neutron Star Crusts

> We performed a Penning trap mass measurement of $^{61}{\rm Zn}$ at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory and NuShellX calculations of the $^{61}{\rm Zn}$ and $^{62}{\rm Ga}$ structure using the GXPF1A Hamiltonian to obtain improved estimates of the $^{61}{\rm Zn}(p,γ)^{62}{\rm Ga}$ and $^{60}{\rm Cu}(p,γ)^{61}{\rm Zn}$ reaction rates. Surveying astrophysical conditions for type-I X-ray bursts with the code MESA, implementing our improved reaction rates, and taking into account updated nuclear masses for $^{61}{\rm V}$ and $^{61}{\rm Cr}$ from the recent literature, we refine the neutrino luminosity from the important mass number $A=61$ urca cooling source in accreted neutron star crusts. This improves our understanding of the thermal barrier between deep heating in the crust and the shallow depths where extra heat is needed to explain X-ray superbursts, as well as the expected signature of crust urca neutrino emission in light curves of cooling transients.

## Key claim

The paper refines the neutrino luminosity from A=61 urca cooling in accreted neutron star crusts by combining a new Penning trap mass measurement of 61Zn with shell-model reaction rate calculations, but the resulting luminosity can be roughly two times lower or one-third higher than previous estimates depending on the specific X-ray burst model conditions assumed.

## Method

A Penning trap mass measurement of 61Zn was performed at the National Superconducting Cyclotron Laboratory using the LEBIT facility, determining the mass excess via time-of-flight ion cyclotron resonance. Shell-model calculations using NuShellX with the GXPF1A Hamiltonian provided level properties of 61Zn and 62Ga, from which the 61Zn(p,γ)62Ga and 60Cu(p,γ)61Zn reaction rates were computed using the narrow resonance approximation. These rates were implemented in multi-zone X-ray burst models using the MESA code for three different astrophysical conditions, and the resulting mass fraction X(61) was combined with updated nuclear masses for 61V and 61Cr from recent literature to compute urca neutrino luminosities.

## Key numbers

- Mass excess of 61Zn: ME(61Zn) = -56355.5(5.0) keV, a factor of three more precise than the most recent atomic mass evaluation
- Q-value for 60Cu(p,γ): 5299.2(5.3) keV, compared to the AME value of 5293(16) keV
- Q-value for 61Zn(p,γ): 2920.5(5.1) keV, compared to the AME value of 2921(16) keV
- At 0.5 GK, the 60Cu(p,γ) reaction rate is nearly ten times larger than other rate estimates
- At 0.5 GK, the 61Zn(p,γ) rate is roughly four times smaller than the Fisker (2001) rate currently recommended in the REACLIB database
- The neutrino luminosity from A=61 urca pairs can be roughly two times lower or one-third higher than the results of Ong (2020), depending on X-ray burst model conditions

## Context

This work extends the survey of urca cooling sources in accreted neutron star crusts by Ong (2020), who identified A=61 as producing the fourth largest neutrino luminosity among roughly one hundred mass numbers present in X-ray burst ashes. The updated nuclear physics reduces the uncertainty in the neutrino luminosity by a factor of two compared to previous work, and the remaining nuclear physics uncertainties are now smaller than the uncertainty from unknown X-ray bursting conditions. The improved reaction rates and neutrino luminosities will refine calculations of X-ray superbursts and cooling transients, and may also impact νp-process nucleosynthesis calculations.
