# Kaon and Pion Fluctuations from Small Disoriented Chiral Condensates

> Enhancement of omega and antiomega baryon production in Pb+Pb collisions at a c.m. energy of 17 A GeV can be explained by the formation of many small disoriented chiral condensate regions. This explanation implies that neutral and charged kaons as well as pions must exhibit novel isospin fluctuations. We compute the distribution of the fraction of neutral pions and kaons from such regions. We then propose robust statistical observables that can be used to extract the novel fluctuations from background contributions in pion and kaon measurements at RHIC and LHC.

# Kaon and Pion Fluctuations from Small Disoriented Chiral Condensates

## Key claim

If the enhancement of Omega and anti-Omega baryon production in Pb+Pb collisions at 17 A GeV is due to many small disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) regions of roughly 2 fm size, then neutral and charged kaons as well as pions must exhibit novel isospin fluctuations that can be extracted from background using robust statistical observables, provided the number of DCC domains is large enough for the central limit theorem to apply.

## Method

The authors compute probability distributions for the fraction of neutral kaons and neutral pions from multiple independent DCC domains, starting from single-domain distributions (flat for kaons, 1/(2*sqrt(f)) for pions) and deriving the multi-domain distributions via Fourier transform methods. They then fold these DCC distributions with Gaussian thermal background distributions to obtain net distributions for the neutral fraction, and derive expressions for the dynamic isospin observable nu_dyn^{c0} that isolates DCC-induced isospin fluctuations from other sources.

## Key numbers

- DCC domain size is about 2 fm, consistent with dynamical simulations of the two-flavor linear sigma model
- For kaons from n domains, the distribution centroid is at f = 1/2 with width sqrt(1/(12n)) in the large-n limit
- For pions from n domains, the distribution centroid is at f = 1/3 with width sqrt(2/(45n)) in the large-n limit
- The dynamic isospin observable for kaon DCC is nu_dyn^{c0}(K DCC) = 4*beta_K * (beta_K/(3n) - 1/N_K), where beta_K is the fraction of kaons from DCC and N_K is total kaon number
- The dynamic isospin observable for pion DCC is nu_dyn^{c0}(pi DCC) = 4.5*beta_pi * (beta_pi/(5n) - 1/N_pi)
- HIJING simulations of central Au+Au at 200 A GeV yield nu_dyn^{c0} approximately -0.002 for 47 K+ and 44 K_S^0 on average

## Context

This work extends the DCC framework of Anselm, Blaizot, and Bjorken (early 1990s) from large single domains to many small domains, motivated by the Kapusta and Wong (2001) explanation of Omega baryon enhancement at SPS energies. It is the first study to address kaon fluctuations from DCC, going beyond the two-flavor pion-only treatments of Amado and Lu and Chow and Cohen. The robust observables proposed enable experimental searches at RHIC and LHC, complementing baryon enhancement measurements to build a circumstantial case for DCC production.
