# Transport coefficients in large N_f gauge theories with massive fermions

> We compute the shear viscosity and the electrical conductivity in gauge theories with massive fermions at leading order in the large N_f expansion. The calculation is organized using the 1/N_f expansion of the 2PI effective action to next-to-leading order. We show explicitly that the calculation is gauge fixing independent and consistent with the Ward identity. We find that these transport coefficients depend in a nontrivial manner on the coupling constant and fermion mass. For large mass, both the shear viscosity and the electrical conductivity go to zero.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity and electrical conductivity in large \(N_f\) gauge theories with massive fermions depend nontrivially on both the coupling constant and the fermion mass, and both transport coefficients vanish in the large-mass limit, but the calculation is explicitly gauge-fixing independent and consistent with the Ward identity only at leading order in the \(1/N_f\) expansion.

## Method

The authors computed the shear viscosity and electrical conductivity using the \(1/N_f\) expansion of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action truncated at next-to-leading order, which yields a closed integral equation for the fermionic 4-point function. They worked in the generalized Coulomb gauge, evaluated the gauge boson self energy with free fermion propagators, and used the fermion spectral density in the large \(N_f\) limit to handle pinching poles. The resulting integral equation for the effective 3-point vertex was solved numerically via a variational method.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity \(\eta\) and electrical conductivity \(\sigma\) are proportional to \(N_f^2\) in the large \(N_f\) limit (apart from external charges for conductivity).
* For massless fermions, the shear viscosity at leading order in the \(1/N_f\) expansion was previously computed using kinetic theory by Moore (2001).
* The fermion thermal width \(\Gamma_\mathbf{p}\) is of order \(1/N_f\), as given by Eq. (2.27) in the paper.
* The gauge boson self energy is computed with free fermion propagators, and the running coupling constant obeys \(\beta(e^2) = e^4/(6\pi^2)\).
* The renormalization scale is chosen as \(\mu = \mu_{\rm DR} = \pi e^{-\gamma_E} T\) for massless fermions, following the dimensional reduction value.

## Context

This work extends the leading-order large \(N_f\) calculation of transport coefficients from massless fermions (Moore, 2001) to the massive case, and it generalizes the 2PI effective action approach previously applied to the \(O(N)\) model (Aarts, 2004). The results enable future studies of transport in gauge theories at finite fermion mass, relevant for heavy-ion physics and early-universe applications, and provide a nontrivial check of gauge invariance in self-consistent truncations of the 2PI effective action.
