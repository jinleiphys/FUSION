# Simulating jets and heavy quarks in the Glasma using the colored particle-in-cell method

> We explore the impact of strong classical color fields, which occur in the earliest stages of heavy-ion collisions and are known as the Glasma, on the classical transport of hard probes, namely heavy quarks and jets. To achieve this, we simulate SU(3) color fields using classical real-time lattice gauge theory and couple them to an ensemble of test particles whose dynamics are described by Wong's equations. We provide an overview of how classical color algebras are constructed and introduce a method to generate random classical SU(3) color charges. We extensively test our numerical particle solver in the limits of infinitely massive heavy quarks and ultra-relativistic light-like jets and obtain excellent quantitative agreement with previous studies. Going towards realistic masses and initial moment, we extract longitudinal and transverse momentum broadening for heavy quarks and jets. The resulting accumulated momenta and the anisotropy of these dynamical hard probes exhibit deviations from limiting scenarios, showing that the full dynamics have a significant effect.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the full dynamical treatment of heavy quarks and jets propagating through the Glasma, beyond the limiting approximations of infinitely massive quarks or ultra-relativistic light-like jets, yields significant deviations in momentum broadening and anisotropy, showing that the approximations used in previous studies miss important effects.

## Method

The authors simulate SU(3) color fields of the Glasma using classical real-time lattice gauge theory in Milne coordinates with boost-invariant initial conditions from the McLerran-Venugopalan model. They couple these fields to an ensemble of test particles whose dynamics are governed by Wong's equations, using a colored particle-in-cell method where particle positions are approximated to the nearest grid point on the transverse lattice and color charges are rotated with gauge links during propagation. The particle solver is implemented on GPUs and uses Euler's method for the proper time evolution of positions, momenta, and color charges.

## Key numbers

* Heavy quark momentum broadening in the longitudinal (rapidity) direction oscillates as a function of proper time, indicating possible plasmon modes in the Glasma
* The paper reports "excellent quantitative agreement" with previous studies in the limiting cases of infinitely massive heavy quarks and ultra-relativistic light-like jets, though no specific numerical comparison values are given in the truncated text
* The MV model parameter \(\mu^2_{A,B}\) represents the variance of color charge density fluctuations for each nucleus, with units of energy squared
* The transverse lattice uses \(N^2\) points with lattice spacing \(a = L/N\) where \(L\) is the side length of the square transverse plane

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of jets in the Glasma by Ipp et al. (2020) that evaluated jet momentum broadening from field correlators without solving particle equations of motion, and studies by Boguslavski et al. (2018, 2020) that extracted heavy quark transport coefficients from electric field correlators assuming infinitely massive static quarks. It also connects to work by Das et al. (2015, 2017), Ruggieri et al. (2018), and others who studied heavy quark diffusion in the Glasma using Langevin descriptions. The framework enables systematic study of the full dynamics of hard probes with finite masses and initial momenta, and can be extended to investigate the back-reaction of particles on the Glasma fields.
