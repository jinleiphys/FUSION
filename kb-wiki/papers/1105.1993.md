# Neutral pion electroproduction in $p(e,e'π^0)p$ above $\sqrt{s}>2$ GeV

> Electroproduction of neutral pions in exclusive reaction $p(e,e'π^0)p$ is studied above the resonance region, $\sqrt{s}>2$ GeV. The reaction amplitude is described by exchanges of vector $ω(782)$, $ρ(770)$ and axial-vector $h_1(1170)$ and $b_1(1235)$ Regge trajectories. The residual effect of $s$- and $u$-channel nucleon resonances is taken into account using a dual connection between the exclusive form factors and inclusive deep inelastic structure functions. In photoproduction at forward angles the exchange of Regge trajectories dominates and the dip region is filled by the resonances. In electroproduction the excitation of nucleon resonances explains the high $Q^2$ data from JLAB. The results for the beam spin azimuthal asymmetry measured at CLAS/JLAB are presented. Model calculations in the deep inelastic region at HERMES/DESY are given.

# Neutral pion electroproduction in $p(e,e'\pi^0)p$ above $\sqrt{s}>2$ GeV

## Key claim

The paper claims that the large transverse cross section observed in neutral pion electroproduction at JLAB, which cannot be explained by Regge pole exchanges alone, is dominated by the excitation of nucleon resonances in the $s$- and $u$-channels, with the residual resonance contributions modeled via a Bloom-Gilman duality connection between exclusive form factors and inclusive deep inelastic structure functions.

## Method

The reaction amplitude is constructed from $t$-channel exchanges of vector $\omega(782)$ and $\rho(770)$ Regge trajectories and axial-vector $h_1(1170)$ and $b_1(1235)$ Regge trajectories, combined with $s$- and $u$-channel nucleon resonance contributions. The resonance contributions are parameterized using generalized form factors derived from a dual connection between exclusive form factors and inclusive deep inelastic structure functions, with the parameter $\xi=0.4$ fixed from charged pion electroproduction. The model is applied to photoproduction data at $E_\gamma = 6$, 9, 12, and 15 GeV and to electroproduction data from JLAB at $\sqrt{s} \simeq 2.1$ GeV and $Q^2 \simeq 2$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

- The $\omega(782)$ Regge trajectory is given by $\alpha_\omega(t) \simeq 0.4 + \alpha_\omega' t$ with slope $\alpha_\omega' = 0.85$ GeV$^{-2}$, producing a nonsense wrong-signature zero at $-t = 0.47$ GeV$^2$
- The $\rho(770)$ Regge trajectory is $\alpha_\rho(t) = 0.53 + \alpha_\rho' t$ with slope $\alpha_\rho' = 0.85$ GeV$^{-2}$, producing a zero at $-t = 0.62$ GeV$^2$
- The $\omega NN$ coupling constant is $G_{\omega NN} = 18 \pm 1$ from fits to high energy photoproduction data
- The $\rho NN$ vector and tensor coupling constants are $G_{\rho NN} = 3.4$ and $\kappa_\rho = 6.1$
- The $\gamma^*\omega\pi^0$ transition form factor cutoff is $\Lambda_{\gamma\omega\pi^0} = 1.2$ GeV from fits to electroproduction data
- The $\rho\gamma\pi$ coupling constants are $G_{\rho\gamma\pi} = 0.84$ GeV$^{-1}$ and $G_{\omega\gamma\pi} = 2.4$ GeV$^{-1}$, derived from radiative decay widths $\Gamma_{\rho^0 \to \gamma\pi^0} = (89 \pm 11)$ keV and $\Gamma_{\omega \to \gamma\pi^0} = (764 \pm 51)$ keV
- The electroproduction data from JLAB correspond to $Q^2 = 1.94$ GeV$^2$, $x_B = 0.37$, and $E_e = 5.75$ GeV
- The axial-vector meson coupling used for the photoproduction asymmetry calculation is $G_B = \pm 11$ GeV$^{-2}$

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kaskulov and collaborators (2009) for charged pion electroproduction to the neutral pion channel, where the Regge model based on single trajectory exchanges alone largely underestimates the measured cross sections, as noted by the CLAS Collaboration (2010). The dip-filling mechanism via nucleon resonances follows the earlier suggestion by Laget (2010) regarding Regge-cut unitary corrections. The model enables interpretation of color transparency signals in exclusive pion electroproduction off nuclei, as discussed by Kaskulov (2008), and provides predictions for beam spin asymmetries measurable at CLAS/JLAB and HERMES/DESY.
