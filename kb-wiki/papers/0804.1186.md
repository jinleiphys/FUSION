# Properties of nuclear and Coulomb breakup of 8B

> The dependence of breakup cross sections of 8B at 65 MeV/nucleon on the target mass number A_T is investigated by means of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) with more reliable distorting potentials than those in the preceding study. The A_T^(1/3) scaling law of the nuclear breakup cross section is found to be satisfied only in the middle A_T region of 40 < A_T < 150. The interference between nuclear and Coulomb breakup amplitudes vanishes in very forward angle scattering, independently of the target nucleus. The truncation of the relative energy between the p and 7Be fragments slightly reduces the contribution of nuclear breakup at very forward angles, while the angular region in which the first-order perturbation theory works well does not change essentially.

## Key claim

The $A_{\rm T}^{1/3}$ scaling law of the nuclear breakup cross section for $^8$B at 65 MeV/nucleon holds only in the middle target-mass region $40 \la A_{\rm T} \la 152$, not universally as previously assumed. The interference between nuclear and Coulomb breakup amplitudes vanishes at very forward scattering angles ($\theta_8 \sim 0^\circ$) for all target nuclei, contrary to an earlier conclusion that it vanished only for $^{208}$Pb.

## Method

The authors used the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method (CDCC) within a $^7$Be$+p+$A three-body model to calculate breakup cross sections of $^8$B at 65 MeV/nucleon on seven targets: $^{12}$C, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, $^{152}$Sm, and $^{208}$Pb. They employed more realistic $p$-target and $^7$Be-target optical potentials than in the preceding study by Hussein (2006), using a global Dirac-based potential for $p$-A and a modified $^7$Li global optical potential for $^7$Be-A. For partial waves with orbital angular momentum $L>1000$, the eikonal-CDCC method was used to obtain $S$-matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Proton separation energy of $^8$B: $S_p = 137$ keV
- Nuclear breakup cross section scaling formula: $\bar{\sigma}_{\rm N}(A_{\rm T}^{1/3}) = 12.8 A_{\rm T}^{1/3} + 16.5$ (fitted to $^{40}$Ca, $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{152}$Sm targets)
- For $^{208}$Pb target, the first-order perturbation theory error is less than 10% for $\theta_8 \le 2.4^\circ$
- Critical angles $\theta_{\rm cr}$ (where nuclear-Coulomb interference is less than 10%): $0.30^\circ$ for $^{12}$C, $0.30^\circ$ for $^{16}$O, $0.40^\circ$ for $^{40}$Ca, $0.60^\circ$ for $^{58}$Ni, $0.90^\circ$ for $^{90}$Zr, $1.65^\circ$ for $^{152}$Sm, and $2.30^\circ$ for $^{208}$Pb
- Maximum wave number between $p$ and $^7$Be: 0.66 fm$^{-1}$; maximum radius: 200 fm
- Integration radii $R_{\rm max}$ for nuclear+Coulomb breakup: 210 fm ($^{12}$C, $^{16}$O), 250 fm ($^{40}$Ca), 330 fm ($^{58}$Ni), 450 fm ($^{90}$Zr), 670 fm ($^{152}$Sm), 880 fm ($^{208}$Pb)

## Context

This work extends the systematic CDCC analysis of breakup reactions by Hussein (2006) using more reliable distorting potentials, correcting the earlier conclusion that nuclear-Coulomb interference vanished only for $^{208}$Pb targets. The study shows that the $A_{\rm T}^{1/3}$ scaling law for nuclear breakup is not universal but restricted to middle-mass targets, and that truncation of the relative energy between fragments does not rescue first-order perturbation theory at angles beyond $\theta_8 \sim 0.5^\circ$ for $^{12}$C or $2.0^\circ$ for $^{208}$Pb. The results enable extraction of B(E1) strength of $^8$B with approximately 10% error from $^{208}$Pb breakup data at very forward angles, and establish the need for accurate optical potentials in future CDCC analyses of $^{11}$Be, $^7$Be, $^6$He, and $^{11}$Li breakup reactions.
