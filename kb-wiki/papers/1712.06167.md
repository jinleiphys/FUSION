# Proton skins, neutron skins, and proton radii of mirror nuclei

> We present predictions for proton skins based on isospin-asymmetric equations of state derived microscopically from high-precision chiral few-nucleon interactions. Moreover, we investigate the relation between the neutron skin of a nucleus and the difference between the proton radii of the corresponding mirror nuclei.

# Proton skins, neutron skins, and proton radii of mirror nuclei

## Key claim
Using microscopic equations of state derived from chiral effective field theory, the neutron skin of a neutron-rich nucleus is linearly related to the difference between the proton radii of the corresponding mirror pair, with the relation holding across different isotopic and isotonic chains. This correlation is fundamentally distinct from phenomenological correlations obtained by varying parameters in Skyrme models, as it emerges from a single microscopic prediction rather than from a family of models tuned to produce a chosen neutron skin in 208Pb.

## Method
The author derives equations of state for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter from high-precision chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) with the leading three-nucleon force included as an effective density-dependent force. Nuclear densities for protons and neutrons are obtained by minimizing the energy of a liquid droplet model (volume, surface, and Coulomb terms) with respect to Thomas-Fermi distribution parameters, using the microscopic EoS as input. Neutron and proton skins are then computed as the difference between the root-mean-square radii of the neutron and proton density distributions.

## Key numbers
- Proton skin of 16O: 0.422 ± 0.022 fm
- Proton skin of 20Ne (Z=10, A=20): 0.032 ± 0.006 fm
- Proton skin of 48Ca (Z=20, A=48): -0.181 ± 0.010 fm (negative skin, meaning neutron skin is positive)
- Proton skin of 48Ni (Z=28, A=48): 0.316 ± 0.021 fm
- Neutron skin of 48Ca (Z=20, N=28): 0.181 ± 0.010 fm, with corresponding ΔRp = 0.309 ± 0.023 fm
- Neutron skin of 54Fe (Z=26, N=28): -0.008 ± 0.006 fm, with corresponding ΔRp = 0.066 ± 0.007 fm
- Linear relation parameters: slope a = 0.78 ± 0.05, intercept b = -0.044 ± 0.016 fm
- Surface constant f0 in the range 60-70 MeV fm5

## Context
This work extends the microscopic approach of Alonso and Sammarruca (2003) using chiral EFT interactions, contrasting with phenomenological studies by Brown (2017) and Yang and Piekarewicz (2017) who used large sets of Skyrme functionals or relativistic energy density functionals to correlate neutron skins with the slope of the symmetry energy. The predicted linear relation between neutron skin and mirror-pair proton radius difference offers a potential alternative to parity-violating experiments (such as PREX on 208Pb and CREX on 48Ca) for extracting neutron skin information, though the author notes that measuring proton radii of neutron-deficient mirror nuclei remains an enormous experimental challenge that may be met at future radioactive beam facilities.
