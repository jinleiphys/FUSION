# Total 4He Photoabsorption Cross Section Revisited: Correlated HH versus Effective Interaction HH

> Two conceptually different hyperspherical harmonics expansions are used for the calculation of the total 4He photoabsorption cross section. Besides the well known method of CHH the recently introduced effective interaction approach for the hyperspherical formalism is applied. Semi-realistic NN potentials are employed and final state interaction is fully taken into account via the Lorentz integral transform method. The results show that the effective interaction leads to a very good convergence, while the correlation method exhibits a less rapid convergence in the giant dipole resonance region. The rather strong discrepancy with the experimental photodisintegration cross sections is confirmed by the present calculations.

# Total 4He Photoabsorption Cross Section Revisited: Correlated HH versus Effective Interaction HH

## Key claim

The effective interaction hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) method yields much faster convergence for the 4He total photoabsorption cross section than the correlated hyperspherical harmonics (CHH) method, but even with improved convergence the theoretical cross section still shows a considerably stronger giant dipole resonance peak than most modern photoabsorption experiments, confirming a persistent discrepancy.

## Method

The total 4He photoabsorption cross section in the dipole approximation was calculated using two different hyperspherical harmonics expansion methods: the correlated HH (CHH) approach, which multiplies basis functions by short-range two-body correlation functions, and the effective interaction HH (EIHH) approach, which applies a Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation to decouple low- and high-momentum subspaces. Both methods employed semi-realistic NN potentials (MT-I/III and TN) and treated final state interactions exactly via the Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method, with the response function obtained by inverting the LIT.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy: -30.69 MeV (CHH) and -30.71 MeV (EIHH) with the MT-I/III potential
- RMS matter radius: 1.421 fm (CHH) and 1.422 fm (EIHH) with the MT-I/III potential
- The CHH calculation required Kmax up to 11 without reaching full convergence, while EIHH showed good convergence already at Kmax=11
- The Lorentz integral transform was calculated with a width parameter sigma_I = 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the earlier CHH calculation of Efros, Leidemann, and Orlandini (1997) for 4He photodisintegration, which used Kmax=7 and found a strong discrepancy with experimental data. The authors compare the CHH approach with the recently introduced EIHH method of Barnea, Leidemann, and Orlandini (2000), which had shown improved convergence for ground state energies of A=3-6 nuclei. The results confirm that the discrepancy with photoabsorption experiments persists even with better convergence, though the cross section extracted from Compton scattering by Wells et al. agrees much better with theory. The work enables future calculations with fully realistic NN forces and three-nucleon forces, and highlights the need for new experimental data from Lund to resolve the experimental situation.
