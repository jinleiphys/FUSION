# Modeling Neutron Star Matter in the Age of Multimessenger Astrophysics

> The interpretation of the available and forthcoming data obtained from multimessenger astrophysical observations -- potentially providing unprecedented access to neutron star properties -- will require the development of novel, accurate theoretical models of dense matter. Of great importance, in this context, will be the capability to devise a description of thermal effects applicable to the study of quantities other than the equation of state, such as the transport coefficients and the neutrino mean free path in the nuclear medium. The formalism based on correlated basis states and the cluster expansion technique has been previously employed to derive a well-behaved effective interaction -- suitable for use in standard perturbation theory -- from a state-of-the-art nuclear Hamiltonian, including phenomenological two- and three-nucleon potentials. Here, we provide a comprehensive and self-contained account of the extension of this approach to the treatment of finite-temperature effects, and report the results of numerical calculations of a number of properties of nuclear matter with arbitrary neutron excess and temperature up to 50 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Correlated Basis Function (CBF) effective interaction formalism to finite temperature, showing that thermal modifications of the correlation functions are negligibly small up to T ~ 50 MeV, so that the zero-temperature effective interaction can be used for calculations of hot nuclear matter properties.

## Method

The authors derive a finite-temperature perturbation theory based on the Bloch equation, using the CBF effective interaction obtained from the AV6P+UIX Hamiltonian via cluster expansion of correlated ground states. The effective interaction is defined by equating the expectation value of the Hamiltonian in the correlated ground state to the Fermi gas expectation value plus the effective interaction, with correlation functions determined from Euler-Lagrange equations at zero temperature. Thermodynamic quantities are then computed from the grand canonical potential at first order in the perturbation.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range considered: up to 50 MeV
- Pion mass m_pi ≈ 140 MeV, setting the scale for the temperature regime where nucleons are the dominant degrees of freedom
- Equilibrium density of isospin-symmetric matter: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- The AV6P potential reproduces deuteron binding energy with 1% accuracy and electric quadrupole moment with 4% accuracy
- The AV6P potential describes NN scattering phase shifts up to beam energies ~600 MeV, beyond the pion production threshold at E_thr ≈ 280 MeV
- The CBF effective interaction is computed at baryon densities of 0.04, 0.32, and 0.48 fm^{-3} (shown in Figure 2)
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda_chi ~ 0.8-1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the CBF effective interaction approach previously developed by Benhar, Lovato, and others (e.g., Lovato et al. 2014, Benhar et al. 2020) from zero temperature to finite temperature, providing a unified framework for computing both equilibrium and dynamical properties of neutron star matter. The approach is conceptually analogous to renormalization-group evolution methods used with chiral effective field theory potentials (e.g., Gezerlis et al. 2013, Piarulli et al. 2015), but operates in coordinate space rather than momentum space and is applicable to higher densities. The results enable calculations of transport coefficients, neutrino mean free paths, and other properties needed to interpret multimessenger observations of neutron star mergers and proto-neutron star evolution.
