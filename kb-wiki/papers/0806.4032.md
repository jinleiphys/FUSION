# Electric and magnetic response to the continuum for A=7 isobars in a dicluster model

> Mirror isobars $^7$Li and $^7$Be are investigated in a dicluster model. The magnetic dipole moments and the magnetic dipole response to the continuum are calculated in this framework. The magnetic contribution is found to be small with respect to electric dipole and quadrupole excitations even at astrophysical energies, at a variance with the case of deuteron. Energy weighted molecular sum rules are evaluated and a formula for the molecular magnetic dipole sum rule is found which matches the numerical calculations. Cross-sections for photo-dissociation and radiative capture as well as the S-factor for reactions of astrophysical significance are calculated with good agreement with known experimental data.

# Electric and magnetic response to the continuum for A=7 isobars in a dicluster model

## Key claim

The magnetic dipole contribution to the electromagnetic response of ⁷Li and ⁷Be is small compared to electric dipole and quadrupole excitations even at astrophysical energies, contrary to the case of the deuteron where magnetic contributions dominate at low energies. The dicluster model reproduces the astrophysical S-factors for the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be and ³H(α,γ)⁷Li reactions with good agreement to experimental data, though the ⁷Be result has a non-optimal slope and the ⁷Li result overestimates the data.

## Method

The authors describe ⁷Li and ⁷Be as dicluster systems (α + t and α + ³He, respectively) and solve the radial Schrödinger equation with an intercluster potential consisting of a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential, a Coulomb potential, and a spin-orbit interaction. They calculate the differential reduced transition probabilities dB(E1)/dE, dB(E2)/dE, and dB(M1)/dE for transitions from the ground state to continuum states, then use these response functions to compute photo-dissociation and radiative capture cross-sections and the astrophysical S-factors. Energy-weighted molecular sum rules (EWMSR) are evaluated and a new formula for the molecular magnetic dipole sum rule is derived.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energies for ⁷Be: −1.58 MeV (3/2⁻) and −0.98 MeV (1/2⁻)
- Woods-Saxon potential depth for ⁷Be: V_WS = −73.851 MeV; spin-orbit strength: V_ls = 1.275 MeV
- Magnetic moment of ⁷Be: calculated −1.53 μ_N, experimental −1.398 ± 0.015 μ_N
- Magnetic moment of ⁷Li: calculated 3.37 μ_N, experimental 3.256427(2) μ_N
- B(M1, 3/2⁻ → 1/2⁻) for ⁷Be: calculated 1.86 μ², experimental 1.87 ± 0.25 μ²
- Total integrated non-energy weighted B(E1) for ⁷Be: 0.226 e²fm²
- Total integrated non-energy weighted B(E2) for ⁷Be: 113.5 e²fm⁴
- Total integrated non-energy weighted B(M1) to continuum for ⁷Be: 0.00408 μ² (up to 50 MeV)
- Total integrated B(M1) to continuum for ⁷Li: 0.0029 μ²
- EWMSR for M1 in ⁷Li: 1.36 μ_N²MeV; calculated: 1.20 μ_N²MeV (88% exhausted)
- EWMSR for M1 in ⁷Be: 0.87 μ_N²MeV; calculated: 0.82 μ_N²MeV (94% exhausted)
- S(0) from parabolic fit: 0.42 keV·b for ⁷Be, 0.13 keV·b for ⁷Li
- Width of 7/2⁻ resonance in ⁷Be: calculated ~90 keV, experimental 175 ± 7 keV
- Width of 5/2⁻ resonance in ⁷Be: calculated ~0.8 MeV, experimental 1.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the dicluster model previously applied to ⁷Li by the same authors (Forvit, year not specified) to include ⁷Be and adds magnetic dipole excitations to the continuum that were neglected in earlier studies. The approach contrasts with computationally-heavy ab initio Monte Carlo shell model calculations (Noll, year not specified) and with the Multichannel Algebraic Scattering approach applied by Canton and Levchuk (year not specified) to the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be reaction. The results enable improved modeling of the ³He(α,γ)⁷Be reaction, which influences solar neutrino production and primordial light element abundance determinations, and demonstrate that the simple cluster picture has comparable predictive power to more refined approaches like R-matrix calculations.
