# Symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities via the Gravitational Waves from GW170817

> Motivated by the historical detection of gravitational waves from GW170817, the neutron star and the neutron drop, i.e., a certain number of neutrons confined in an external field, are systematically investigated by ab initio calculations as well as the nonrelativistic and relativistic state-of-art density functional theories. Strong correlations are found among the neutron star tidal deformability, the neutron star radius, the root-mean-square radii of neutron drops, and the symmetry energies of nuclear matter at supra-saturation densities. From these correlations and the upper limit on the tidal deformability extracted from GW170817, the neutron star radii, the neutron drop radii, and the symmetry energy at twice saturation density are respectively constrained as $R_{1.4M_{\odot}}\leqslant 12.94$ km, $R_{\rm nd} \leqslant 2.36$ fm, and $E_{\mathrm{sym}}(2ρ_0) \leqslant 53.2$ MeV.

## Key claim

The paper uses the upper limit on the tidal deformability from GW170817 to constrain the symmetry energy at twice saturation density to \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(2\rho_0) \leqslant 53.2\) MeV, but this result depends on the assumption that strong linear correlations among neutron star tidal deformability, neutron star radius, neutron drop radii, and symmetry energy hold across a wide range of density functionals and are supported by ab initio calculations.

## Method

The authors performed systematic calculations of neutron star tidal deformabilities and radii using a set of nonrelativistic and relativistic density functional theories (DFTs) as well as ab initio relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with Bonn potentials. They also computed root-mean-square radii of neutron drops (20 neutrons in a harmonic oscillator potential with \(\hbar\omega = 25\) MeV) using the same theoretical frameworks. Linear correlations among these quantities were established by fitting the DFT results, and the upper limit \(\Lambda_{1.4M_{\odot}} \leqslant 580\) from GW170817 was propagated through these correlations to constrain neutron star radii, neutron drop radii, and the symmetry energy at twice saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on tidal deformability from GW170817: \(\Lambda_{1.4M_{\odot}} \leqslant 580\) at 90% credible level for a \(1.4M_{\odot}\) neutron star.
- Constrained neutron star radius: \(R_{1.4M_{\odot}} \leqslant 12.94\) km, derived from the correlation \(\Lambda_{1.4M_{\odot}} = a R_{1.4M_{\odot}}^b\) with \(a \approx 7.29 \times 10^{-5}\) km\(^{-b}\) and \(b \approx 6.21\), correlation coefficient \(r \approx 0.99\).
- Constrained neutron drop radius: \(R_{\rm nd} \leqslant 2.36\) fm for \(N=20\) neutrons in a harmonic oscillator potential with \(\hbar\omega = 25\) MeV, from linear correlation with \(R_{1.4M_{\odot}}\) (Pearson's \(r = 0.95\)).
- Constrained symmetry energy at twice saturation density: \(E_{\mathrm{sym}}(2\rho_0) \leqslant 53.2\) MeV, from linear correlation with \(R_{\rm nd}\) (Pearson's \(r = 0.96\)).
- Chirp mass of GW170817: \(\mathcal{M} = 1.188_{-0.002}^{+0.004} M_{\odot}\).

## Context

This work extends the use of gravitational wave observations from GW170817 to constrain nuclear matter properties at supra-saturation densities, building on earlier studies that linked tidal deformability to the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation density (Fattoyev 2018, Lim 2018, Malik 2018). The novel contribution is the introduction of neutron drops as a bridge between astrophysical observables and the high-density symmetry energy, following the proposal by Zhao (2016) that neutron drop properties correlate with the nuclear equation of state. The results are consistent with the ASY-EOS experimental constraint \(50.8\) MeV \(\leqslant E_{\mathrm{sym}}(2\rho_0) \leqslant 60.4\) MeV (Russotto 2016) and with ab initio RBHF and APR calculations (Akmal 1998), and they enable future constraints from more precise gravitational wave measurements or neutron star radius observations from X-ray bursts and quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries (Steiner 2010, Bogdanov 2016).
