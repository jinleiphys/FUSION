# Magnetic field effects in peripheral heavy-ion collisions around 1 GeV/nucleon

> Magnetic field effects on free nucleons are studied in peripheral collisions of $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au at energies ranging from 600 to 1500 MeV/nucleon by utilizing an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. With the help of angular distributions and two-particle angular correlators, the magnetic field effect at an impact parameter of 11 fm is found to be more obvious than at an impact parameter of 8 fm. Moreover, the results suggest that with an increase in the number of peripheral collisions, protons are more easily condensed with the magnetic field. Magnetic field effects are further investigated by the ratio of free neutrons to free protons as functions of a two-particle correlator $C_{2}$, four-particle correlator $C_{4}$ and six-particle correlator $C_{6}$ of angle $ϕ$, rapidity $Y$ and transverse momentum $p_{T}$. The results show that weak magnetic field effects could be revealed more clearly by these multiple-particle correlators, with the larger number of particle correlators demonstrating a clear signal. The results highlight a new method to search for weak signals using multi-particle correlators.

# Magnetic field effects in peripheral heavy-ion collisions around 1 GeV/nucleon

## Key claim

Magnetic field effects on free nucleons in peripheral Au+Au collisions are detectable via multi-particle correlators, but only when nuclear interactions are sufficiently weak (impact parameter 11 fm); at smaller impact parameters (8 fm), stronger nuclear interactions wash out the magnetic field signal even when the magnetic field itself is artificially amplified by a factor of 1.5.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate 197Au+197Au collisions at beam energies from 600 to 1500 MeV/nucleon, incorporating magnetic fields via Liénard-Wiechert potentials. They compared two impact parameters (8 fm and 11 fm) and analyzed angular distributions, two-particle angular correlators, and multi-particle correlators (C2, C4, C6) of angle φ, rapidity Y, and transverse momentum pT, as well as the ratio of free neutrons to free protons as functions of these correlators.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 600 to 1500 MeV/nucleon for 197Au+197Au collisions
- Impact parameters compared: 11 fm (more peripheral) and 8 fm (moderate peripheral)
- For b = 8 fm, the magnetic field was artificially multiplied by a factor of 1.5 to compensate for the lower intrinsic field strength at smaller impact parameter
- The relative ratio P for free protons at b = 11 fm reached 20% for the two-particle angle correlator, considerably larger than at b = 8 fm
- Gaussian wave packet width L fixed at 2.18 fm² in the IQMD model
- Mid-rapidity selection used |Y/Y₀| < 0.5, where Y₀ is the initial projectile rapidity
- A relation P(C₂) < P(C₄) < P(C₆) was found for both angle and rapidity correlators at b = 11 fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck model (Deng and Ma, 2017, 2018) on electromagnetic effects in heavy-ion collisions, and addresses the limited exploration of magnetic field effects at intermediate energies compared to ultra-relativistic collisions where fields up to 10¹⁸ G are produced (Voronov et al., 2009; Skokov et al., 2009). The study introduces multi-particle correlators as a new method to detect weak magnetic field signals, which could be applied to search for other tiny effects such as the chiral magnetic effect, equation of state properties, and nuclear α-clustering. The results contradict the naive expectation that a stronger magnetic field (at b = 8 fm) would produce larger observable effects, demonstrating instead that nuclear interactions in the participant zone dominate and suppress magnetic field signals.
