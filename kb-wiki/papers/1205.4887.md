# Symmetries of hadrons after unbreaking the chiral symmetry

> We study hadron correlators upon artificial restoration of the spontaneously broken chiral symmetry. In a dynamical lattice simulation we remove the lowest lying eigenmodes of the Dirac operator from the valence quark propagators and study evolution of the hadron masses obtained. All mesons and baryons in our study, except for a pion, survive unbreaking the chiral symmetry and their exponential decay signals become essentially better. From the analysis of the observed spectroscopic patterns we conclude that confinement still persists while the chiral symmetry is restored. All hadrons fall into different chiral multiplets. The broken U(1)_A symmetry does not get restored upon unbreaking the chiral symmetry. We also observe signals of some higher symmetry that includes chiral symmetry as a subgroup. Finally, from comparison of the Δ- N splitting before and after unbreaking of the chiral symmetry we conclude that both the color-magnetic and the flavor-spin quark-quark interactions are of equal importance.

## Key claim

After artificially restoring chiral symmetry by removing the lowest eigenmodes of the Dirac operator from valence quark propagators, all hadrons except the pion survive and confinement persists, contradicting the idea that hadron masses are entirely due to the quark condensate; the authors observe that both color-magnetic and flavor-spin quark-quark interactions contribute equally to the Delta-N splitting.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical lattice QCD simulation with two degenerate light chirally improved fermions at a pion mass of 322(5) MeV on a 16^3 x 32 lattice. They removed the lowest 2 to 128 eigenmodes of the Hermitian Dirac operator D5 from the valence quark propagators, then used the variational method with multiple Gaussian-smeared and derivative sources to extract ground and excited state masses for mesons (rho, a1, b1, rho') and baryons (nucleon and Delta of both parities) as a function of the number of removed eigenmodes.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass before truncation: m_pi = 322(5) MeV (corresponding to the dynamical configurations used)
- Lattice spacing: a = 0.144(1) fm
- Lattice volume: 16^3 x 32
- Number of gauge configurations: 161
- Number of removed eigenmodes studied: k = 0, 2, 4, 8, 12, 16, 20, 32, 64, 128
- Gaussian source sizes: narrow source 0.27 fm, wide source 0.55 fm

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Lang, Glozman, and Schroeck (2011) on low-lying mesons (pi, rho, a0, a1) to include radially and orbitally excited mesons (b1, rho') and baryons (nucleon and Delta states), providing stronger evidence that confinement survives chiral symmetry restoration. The results contradict the popular assertion that the rho mass is entirely due to the quark condensate, with implications for the expected behavior of the rho mass in dense medium as discussed by Brown and collaborators (1991). The observed higher symmetry beyond SU(2)R x SU(2)L may relate to the parity doubling seen in highly excited hadrons reported by Glozman (1999) and Cohen (2001).
