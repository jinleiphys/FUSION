# Equation of state and hybrid star properties with the weakly interacting light U-boson in relativistic models

> It has been a puzzle whether quarks may exist in the interior of massive neutron stars, since the hadron-quark phase transition softens the equation of state (EOS) and reduce the neutron star (NS) maximum mass very significantly. In this work, we consider the light U-boson that increases the NS maximum mass appreciably through its weak coupling to fermions. The inclusion of the U-boson may thus allow the existence of the quark degrees of freedom in the interior of large mass neutron stars. Unlike the consequence of the U-boson in hadronic matter, the stiffening role of the U-boson in the hybrid EOS is not sensitive to the choice of the hadron phase models. In addition, we have also investigated the effect of the effective QCD correction on the hybrid EOS. This correction may reduce the coupling strength of the U-boson that is needed to satisfy NS maximum mass constraint. While the inclusion of the U-boson also increases the NS radius significantly, we find that appropriate in-medium effects of the U-boson may reduce the NS radii significantly, satisfying both the NS radius and mass constraints well.

## Key claim
The inclusion of a weakly interacting light U-boson can stiffen the hybrid equation of state (EOS) enough to allow quark degrees of freedom in the interior of massive neutron stars, even though the hadron-quark phase transition alone softens the EOS and reduces the maximum mass. The stiffening role of the U-boson in hybrid matter is not sensitive to the choice of hadron phase model, unlike in pure hadronic matter, and appropriate in-medium effects can reduce the NS radius to satisfy both mass and radius constraints.

## Method
The authors used relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory with parameter sets NL3 and FSUGold to describe hadronic matter, and the MIT bag model for quark matter. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated using Gibbs construction to determine the mixed phase. The U-boson was included as an additional vector boson coupling weakly to fermions, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation was solved to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- PSR J1614-2230 mass: \(M = 1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\) (Demorest 2010)
- PSR J0348+0432 mass: \(M = 2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\) (Antoniadis 2013)
- Bag constant range used: \(B^{1/4} = 180\) to 220 MeV
- Transition density range used: \(2\rho_0\) to \(3\rho_0\), where \(\rho_0 = 0.148\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- With NL3 and \(B^{1/4}=180\) MeV: transition density from hadronic to mixed phase \(\approx 0.20\) fm\(^{-3}\), extent of mixed phase \(\approx 0.57\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- With FSUGold and \(B^{1/4}=180\) MeV: onset transition density \(0.28\) fm\(^{-3}\), extent of mixed phase \(1.47\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- With NL3 and \(B^{1/4}=180\) MeV, no U-boson: NS maximum mass \(1.39 M_\odot\); with \((g_u/m_u)^2 = 25\) GeV\(^{-2}\): maximum mass increases to \(1.71 M_\odot\)
- With NL3 and \(B^{1/4}=200\) MeV: NS maximum mass \(1.86 M_\odot\)
- QCD correction parameter \(c = 0.3\) used for perturbative QCD correction

## Context
This work extends previous studies on the U-boson in pure hadronic matter (Zhang 2011, Wen 2009, Krivoruchenko 2009) to hybrid stars containing both hadronic and quark phases. It addresses the tension between the softened EOS from hadron-quark phase transitions and the observed massive neutron stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013). The results enable future investigations of how in-medium effects of the U-boson can simultaneously satisfy mass and radius constraints from observations, and suggest that the U-boson may allow quark cores in massive neutron stars without violating observational limits.
