# Nucleon form factors: From the space-like to the time-like region

> I discuss how dispersion relations can be used to analyse the nucleon electromagnetic form factors, with particular emphasis on the constraints from unitarity and pQCD. Results for nucleon radii, vector-meson couplings, the onset of pQCD and bounds on the strangeness form factors are presented. The em form factors in the time-like region reveal some interesting physics which is not yet understood in full detail. The need for a better data basis at low, intermediate and large momentum transfer and also in the time-like region is stressed.

## Key claim

Dispersion relations can be used to analyze nucleon electromagnetic form factors in both the space-like and time-like regions, but the time-like data reveal interesting structure near the two-nucleon threshold that is not yet understood, and the extraction of strange vector form factors depends strongly on whether one assumes maximal OZI violation or imposes the OZI rule.

## Method

The author uses unsubtracted dispersion relations to construct spectral functions for the isoscalar and isovector nucleon electromagnetic form factors, imposing constraints from unitarity (particularly the two-pion cut enhancement for the isovector channel), perturbative QCD scaling and superconvergence relations, and the measured neutron charge radius. The spectral functions are parametrized as a sum of vector meson poles plus a two-pion continuum contribution, with the number of poles determined by a stability criterion, and the free parameters (three isoscalar and three isovector poles, 19 parameters reduced to 8 by 11 constraints) are fitted to the available space-like and time-like data.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: \(r_E^p = 0.847\) fm, with about 1% uncertainty
- Proton magnetic radius: \(r_M^p = 0.853\) fm, with about 1% uncertainty
- Neutron magnetic radius: \(r_M^n = 0.889\) fm, with about 1% uncertainty
- \(\omega NN\) coupling: \(g_{\omega NN}^2 / 4\pi = 34.6 \pm 0.8\), tensor-to-vector ratio \(\kappa_\omega = -0.16 \pm 0.01\)
- \(\phi NN\) coupling: \(g_{\phi NN}^2 / 4\pi = 6.7 \pm 0.3\), tensor-to-vector ratio \(\kappa_\phi = -0.22 \pm 0.01\)
- Onset of pQCD: \(\Lambda^2 \simeq 10\) GeV\(^2\) for the best fit to space-like data; \(\Lambda^2 = 12.0\) GeV\(^2\) when time-like data are included
- Strange magnetic moment (maximal OZI violation): \(\mu_s = -0.24\) n.m.
- Strangeness radius (maximal OZI violation): \(r_s^2 = 0.17 \ldots 0.21\) fm\(^2\)
- Strange magnetic moment (OZI rule imposed): \(\mu_s = 0.003\) n.m.
- Strangeness radius (OZI rule imposed): \(r_s^2 = 0.002\) fm\(^2\)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive analysis of Hohler et al. (1976) by incorporating new constraints from perturbative QCD and updated data, particularly the FENICE measurements of the neutron form factor in the time-like region. The analysis enables improved determinations of nucleon radii and vector-meson couplings, and it sets the stage for interpreting parity-violation experiments at SAMPLE, TJNAF, and MAMI that aim to measure strange quark contributions to the nucleon form factors. The unresolved discrepancy between the maximal OZI violation scenario and the OZI-respecting scenario for strange form factors highlights the need for both better data and more theoretical work on the role of \(\pi\rho\) correlations and kaon loops.
