# The Deflation of SU(3)_c at High Temperatures

> The ideas of "local" and "global" colour-singletness are not well understood within QCD. We use a group theoretical technique to project out the partition function for a system of quarks, antiquarks and gluons to a particular representation of the internal symmetry group $SU(3)_c$: colour-singlet, colour-octet and colour 27-plet at finite temperature. For high temperatures and large size it is shown that colour-singlet is degenerate with colour-octet, colour 27-plet states etc. For the composite system it is shown that $SU(3)_c$ appears to be a good symmetry only at low temperatures and at higher temperatures it gets submerged into a larger group $U(12)_q \otimes U(12)_{\bar{q}}$ (2-flavour). At high enough temperatures this conclusion is model independent. This means that a phase transition from the hadronic matter to the quark-gluon phase implies a transition from the group $SU(3)_c$ to $U(12)_q \otimes U(12)_{\bar q}$. Ideas of extensions beyond the standard model would have to be reviewed in the light of this result.

# The Deflation of SU(3)_c at High Temperatures

## Key claim

At sufficiently high temperatures and/or large system size, the colour-singlet representation becomes degenerate with colour-octet, colour-27-plet, and other higher representations, meaning that SU(3)_c ceases to be a good symmetry for the composite system and becomes submerged into a larger group U(12)_q ⊗ U(12)_\bar{q} (for 2 flavours). The authors claim this conclusion is model independent at high enough temperatures, but the calculation uses non-interacting quarks and gluons, and the neglect of perturbative interactions may not be justified at low temperatures.

## Method

The authors apply a group-theoretical colour projection technique to the partition function of a system of non-interacting quarks, antiquarks, and gluons at finite temperature, projecting onto specific SU(3)_c representations: colour-singlet (0,0), colour-octet (1,1), and colour-27-plet (2,2). They compute the projected partition functions using character orthogonality relations for SU(3)_c, with the generating function expressed in terms of the diagonal generators I_z and Y, and evaluate the resulting energies for different representations as a function of the dimensionless parameter TV^{1/3}.

## Key numbers

- The unprojected energy is given by E_0 = 3 a_q V T^4 with a_q = (37 π^2)/90.
- For the colour-singlet projection, the correction term E_corr is significant only when TV^{1/3} is small (< 2) and vanishes when TV^{1/3} becomes large (> 2).
- For a temperature T = 160 MeV, the colour-singletness restriction only affects systems with size approximately 1.0 fm.
- The larger group into which SU(3)_c submerges is U(12)_q ⊗ U(12)_\bar{q} for 2 flavours, U(18)_q ⊗ U(18)_\bar{q} for 3 flavours, and U(6)_q ⊗ U(6)_\bar{q} for 0 flavours.

## Context

This work extends earlier colour projection calculations by Elze et al. (1990s) and Gorenstein et al. (1990s), who had imposed colour-singletness on the quark-gluon plasma and found that finite-size corrections vanish for large TV^{1/3}. The present paper goes beyond those studies by projecting onto higher representations (octet, 27-plet) and showing that all representations become degenerate at high temperatures, contradicting the assumption that global colour-singletness automatically holds for large QGP systems. This result challenges the standard picture of the early universe above the QCD phase transition temperature, where the unbroken gauge group is usually taken as SU(3)_c ⊗ SU(2)_L ⊗ U(1)_Y, and suggests that ideas of extensions beyond the standard model need to be reviewed.
