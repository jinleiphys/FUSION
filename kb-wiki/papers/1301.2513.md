# A new type of nuclear collective motion - the spin scissors mode

> The coupled dynamics of low lying modes and various giant resonances are studied with the help of the Wigner Function Moments method on the basis of Time Dependent Hartree-Fock equations in the harmonic oscillator model including spin-orbit potential plus quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin residual interactions. New low lying spin dependent modes are analyzed. Special attention is paid to the spin scissors mode.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the spin-scissors mode, rotational oscillations of "spin-up" nucleons against "spin-down" nucleons, is a new type of nuclear collective motion, and that the spin-spin residual interaction strongly redistributes M1 strength in favor of this mode over the conventional orbital scissors mode, potentially explaining experimental B(M1) inversions in nuclei like 233Th.

## Method

The authors solve the Time Dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) equations using the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method in a harmonic oscillator model that includes spin-orbit potential plus separable quadrupole-quadrupole and spin-spin residual interactions. They linearize the resulting dynamical equations for collective variables (phase space moments up to quadratic order) around equilibrium for an axially symmetric nucleus, then separate the equations into isoscalar and isovector components to extract excitation energies and transition probabilities.

## Key numbers

- The spin-spin interaction pushes all excitation levels upward proportionally to its strength without changing their order.
- For rare earth nuclei, the WFM method with pair correlations reproduces conventional scissors mode energies with approximately 10% accuracy.
- B(M1) values for the conventional scissors mode in rare earth nuclei were reduced by about a factor of two with respect to non-superfluid values when pair correlations were included, but remained about two times too high compared to experiment.
- In 233Th, the B(M1) values of the two low-lying magnetic states show an inversion in strength favoring the lowest state (the spin scissors mode) when the spin-spin interaction is increased.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Balbutsev, Molodtsova, and Schuck, 2012) which first predicted the spin scissors mode using only the spin-orbit interaction, by now including the spin-spin residual interaction that was previously neglected. The paper aims to resolve the longstanding discrepancy between theoretical B(M1) values for the conventional orbital scissors mode and experimental data, which has been a puzzle since the mode was predicted by Hilton (1980) and studied by various groups including Zawischa (1990) and Sushkov (1990). The results offer a tentative explanation for the experimental finding in 233Th by Siem (2002), where the B(M1) inversion could not be explained by triaxial deformation alone.
