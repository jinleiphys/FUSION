# Heavy flavor angular correlations as probes of the glasma

> We study the effect of the glasma fields, formed in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions, on the transport of $Q\bar{Q}$ pairs produced back-to-back. We find that for pairs with moderate initial transverse momentum $p_T$ evolving in glasma fields with sufficiently large saturation momentum $Q_s$, the azimuthal correlation $C({Δϕ})$ is quickly affected. The decorrelation widths $σ_{Δϕ}$ during the glasma and Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) phases are comparable.

## Key claim

The glasma fields formed in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions can decorrelate the azimuthal angle of heavy quark-antiquark pairs produced back-to-back, and the decorrelation width during the glasma phase is comparable to that accumulated during the subsequent Quark-Gluon Plasma phase, but only for pairs with moderate initial transverse momentum evolving in glasma fields with sufficiently large saturation momentum.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the sourceless classical Yang-Mills equations on a real-time lattice to generate glasma fields, with initial conditions sampled from the McLerran-Venugopalan model where the saturation momentum \(Q_s\) sets the scale. Heavy quark and antiquark test particles were evolved in these glasma fields using Wong's classical transport equations, initialized at the same transverse coordinate with opposite transverse momenta (simulating leading-order perturbative QCD production via gluon fusion) and random color charges. The two-particle correlation function \(\mathcal{C}(\Delta\eta,\Delta\phi)\) was extracted from many test particles across multiple glasma events as a function of relative proper time \(\Delta\tau\).

## Key numbers

- For \(c\bar{c}\) pairs with \(p_T\in[5,10]\,\mathrm{GeV}\) in glasma with \(Q_s=2\,\mathrm{GeV}\) at \(\Delta\tau=0.3\,\mathrm{fm/c}\), the decorrelation width is \(\sigma_{\Delta\phi}^\mathrm{glasma}\approx 0.3\).
- For \(c\bar{c}\) pairs with \(p_T\in[4,10]\,\mathrm{GeV}\) during the QGP phase (including collisional and radiative energy loss), the decorrelation width is \(\sigma_{\Delta\phi}^\mathrm{QGP}\approx 0.36\) (Nahrgang 2013).
- The correlation magnitude drops rapidly after \(\Delta\tau=1/Q_s\approx 0.1\,\mathrm{fm/c}\) for \(Q_s=2\,\mathrm{GeV}\).
- The decorrelation width at \(Q_s=2\,\mathrm{GeV}\) for \(c\bar{c}\) pairs with initial \(p_T=2\,\mathrm{GeV}\) was studied at \(\Delta\tau\in\{0.05, 0.1, 0.2, 0.5\}\,\mathrm{fm/c}\).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy quark transport in glasma fields that focused on the nuclear modification factor in pA collisions (Ruggieri 2018) and AA collisions (Sun 2019, Gale 2024), as well as \(Q\bar{Q}\) pair dissociation in glasma (Pooja 2024, Oliva 2024). By demonstrating that the glasma-induced azimuthal decorrelation is comparable to QGP effects, the results enable future theoretical predictions for \(D\bar{D}\) correlations planned for measurement at ALICE3 (ALICE 2022), and highlight the need to disentangle initial-state glasma effects from later QGP contributions.
