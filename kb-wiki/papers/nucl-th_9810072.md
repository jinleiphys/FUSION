# Weak Magnetism in Two Neutrino Double Beta Decay

> We have extended the formalism for the two-neutrino double beta decay by including the weak-magnetism term, as well as other second-forbidden corrections. The weak magnetism diminishes the calculated half-lives in $\sim 10%$, independently of the nuclear structure. Numerical computations were performed within the pn-QRPA, for $^{76}Ge$, $^{82}Se$, $^{100} Mo$, $^{128}Te$ and $^{130}Te$ nuclei. No one of the second-forbidden corrections modifies significantly the spectrum shapes. The total reduction in the calculated half lives varies from 6% up to 32%, and strongly depend on the nuclear interaction in the particle-particle $S=1,T=0$ channel. We conclude that the higher order effects in the weak Hamiltonian would hardly be observed in the two-neutrino double beta experiments.

## Key claim

The weak-magnetism term and other second-forbidden corrections reduce calculated two-neutrino double beta decay half-lives by 6% to 32%, with the exact reduction depending strongly on the nuclear interaction in the particle-particle S=1,T=0 channel; however, none of these higher-order effects modify the electron energy spectrum shapes significantly enough to be observed in current experiments.

## Method

The authors extended the standard allowed-approximation formalism for two-neutrino double beta decay by including the weak-magnetism term and other second-forbidden corrections (velocity-dependent operators and the r² term) in the non-relativistic weak Hamiltonian. They computed the nuclear matrix elements within the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) for five nuclei: ⁷⁶Ge, ⁸²Se, ¹⁰⁰Mo, ¹²⁸Te, and ¹³⁰Te. The half-lives and spectrum shapes were evaluated by including interference terms between the Gamow-Teller operator and the second-forbidden operators at the amplitude level.

## Key numbers

- Weak magnetism alone renormalizes the Gamow-Teller matrix element by a factor of approximately 1.05 for medium-heavy nuclei, independent of nuclear structure.
- Total half-life reduction ranges from 6% in ¹²⁸Te up to approximately 32% in ¹⁰⁰Mo.
- Calculated half-lives within the allowed approximation (T^A_2ν) and with second-forbidden corrections (T^A+SFC_2ν): ⁷⁶Ge: 7.3×10²¹ y and 6.8×10²¹ y; ⁸²Se: 1.5×10²⁰ y and 1.4×10²⁰ y; ¹⁰⁰Mo: 9.7×10¹⁹ y and 6.6×10¹⁹ y; ¹²⁸Te: 8.1×10²³ y and 7.6×10²³ y; ¹³⁰Te: 2.2×10²⁰ y and 2.0×10²⁰ y.
- The Gamow-Teller matrix element M^(0)_2ν values: 0.050 (⁷⁶Ge), 0.060 (⁸²Se), 0.051 (¹⁰⁰Mo), 0.059 (¹²⁸Te), 0.048 (¹³⁰Te) in natural units with g_A=1.

## Context

This work completes a series of studies by Barbero, Krmpotic, Mariano, and Tadic (1995, 1998) on higher-order corrections in two-neutrino double beta decay, following earlier examinations of first-forbidden non-unique and unique transitions. The authors note that Williams and Haxton (1988) had previously considered higher-order terms, but most other calculations had ignored them. The results show that while first-forbidden transitions through 0⁻,1⁻ virtual states and second-forbidden corrections both affect transition rates significantly, the theoretical uncertainties within QRPA are comparable to or larger than experimental errors, and the spectral distortions are too small to interfere with searches for neutrinoless double beta decay or Majoron-emitting modes.
