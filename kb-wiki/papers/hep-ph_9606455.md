# Heavy Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory and the Spin 3/2 Delta Resonances

> Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory is briefly reviewed, paying particular attention to the role of the spin 3/2 delta resonances. The concept of resonance saturation for the baryonic sector is critically discussed. Starting from a relativistic formulation of the pion-nucleon-delta system, the heavy baryon chiral lagrangian including spin 3/2 resonances is constructed by means of a 1/m-expansion. The effective theory obtained admits a systematic expansion in terms of soft momenta, the pion mass $M_π$ and the delta-nucleon mass difference $Δ$.

## Key claim

Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) can be systematically extended to include spin 3/2 delta resonances as explicit degrees of freedom via a 1/m-expansion, but only if one treats the delta-nucleon mass difference Δ and the pion mass M_π as comparable small scales in a simultaneous expansion, rather than treating the delta as a heavy resonance.

## Method

The author starts from a relativistic chiral Lagrangian for the pion-nucleon-delta system, including a Rarita-Schwinger spin 3/2 field. By decomposing the nucleon and delta fields into light and heavy components using a fixed-velocity projection, and then integrating out the heavy degrees of freedom, the heavy baryon chiral Lagrangian is constructed order by order in a 1/m-expansion. The resulting effective theory admits a systematic expansion in soft momenta, M_π, and Δ, with the delta propagator counting as order ε^{-1} in this small-scale expansion.

## Key numbers

- The delta-nucleon mass difference is Δ ≈ 300 MeV, comparable to M_π ≈ 140 MeV.
- For the nucleon mass shift at order ε^3, the delta-loop contribution gives R(Δ, M_π) = -0.0044 GeV^3, corresponding to a nucleon mass shift of -10 MeV, comparable to the -15 MeV shift from the M_π^3 term.
- For neutral pion photoproduction at threshold, the order ε^3 delta contribution yields E_{0+} = 0.80 × 10^{-3}/M_π, far from the experimental value of (-1.33 ± 0.08) × 10^{-3}/M_π from Fuchs (1995).
- The coupling constants used are b_1 = -2.30 ± 0.35 from BDM (1989) and g_{πNΔ} = 1.5 ± 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory framework of Jenkins and Manohar (1991) by deriving it systematically from a relativistic formulation, clarifying how the delta resonances should be included as dynamical degrees of freedom rather than only through resonance saturation of counterterms as in the approach reviewed by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995). The formalism enables consistent calculations of processes such as nucleon Compton scattering and pion photoproduction, where the small mass difference Δ makes the chiral expansion converge slowly; the author identifies the need for complete one-loop renormalization and extension to chiral SU(3) as next steps.
