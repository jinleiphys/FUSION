# $Q^2$ Independence of $QF_2/F_1$, Poincare Invariance and the Non-Conservation of Helicity

> A relativistic constituent quark model is found to reproduce the recent data regarding the ratio of proton form factors, $F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2)$. We show that imposing Poincare invariance leads to substantial violation of the helicity conservation rule, as well as an analytic result that the ratio $F_2(Q^2)/F_1(Q^2)\sim 1/Q$ for intermediate values of $Q^2$.

## Key claim

A relativistic constituent quark model that imposes Poincare invariance reproduces the recent data showing that the ratio \(QF_2/F_1\) is approximately constant for \(2 \le Q^2 \le 6\) GeV\(^2\), but the paper's analytic argument that the ratio scales as \(1/Q\) is a leading-order approximation valid only for intermediate \(Q^2\); the exact model calculation shows a broad maximum near \(Q^2 \approx 10\) GeV\(^2\) followed by a slow falloff at asymptotically large \(Q^2\), not a strict \(1/Q\) behavior.

## Method

The authors use the Bakamjian-Thomas light-front formalism with a relativistic constituent quark model based on Schlumpf's power-law wave function, where the proton wave function is a product of Dirac spinors and a symmetric momentum-space wave function \(\Phi(M_0) = N/(M_0^2 + \beta^2)^\gamma\). Form factors \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) are computed from the plus-component of the electromagnetic current in the Drell-Yan frame (\(q^+ = 0\)), with the spin structure handled via Melosh rotations that mix light-front helicities; the key analytic step is to approximate the boosted mass operator \(M_0' \approx Q \sqrt{\eta/(1-\eta)}\) for large \(Q\) and retain only the leading \(Q\)-dependent terms in the Melosh-rotation overlaps.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark mass \(m = 0.267\) GeV; confinement scale parameter \(\beta = 0.607\) GeV; power-law exponent \(\gamma = 3.5\).
- The ratio \(Q\kappa F_2/F_1\) is predicted to be approximately constant at \(\approx 0.8\) for \(Q^2\) up to 20 GeV\(^2\).
- The integrands for \(F_1\) and \(F_2\) peak at \(x_3 = 0.85\) (the momentum fraction of the struck quark), indicating the Feynman mechanism dominates.
- A 10% increase in \(\gamma\) (from 3.5 to 3.9) causes about a 50% decrease in \(F_2\) but only a 5% change in the ratio \(QF_2/F_1\).
- The ratio \(Q^2 \kappa F_2/F_1\) falls at asymptotic \(Q^2\) but does not follow the perturbative QCD prediction of \(1/\log(Q^2)\) for \(80 < Q^2 < 600\) GeV\(^2\).

## Context

This work extends the relativistic constituent quark model of Schlumpf (1992-1994) and the earlier calculation by Frank, Jennings, and Miller (1995) that first reproduced the falling \(G_E/G_M\) ratio. It directly contradicts the perturbative QCD helicity-conservation rule of Brodsky and Lepage (1980) and Mueller (1979), which predicted \(Q^2 F_2/F_1\) constant rather than \(Q F_2/F_1\) constant. The results support the arguments of Ralston and collaborators that helicity non-conservation is important, and align with the soft non-factorizable terms discussed by Braun et al. (2001). The paper enables future experimental tests of helicity non-conservation in exclusive proton-proton scattering at high momentum transfer, particularly to explain the large analyzing power \(A_{NN}\) observed at \(s \approx 20\) GeV\(^2\) by Cosbie and collaborators and by Krisch and collaborators.
