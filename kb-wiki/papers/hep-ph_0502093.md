# Phenomenology of spin 3/2 baryons with pentaquarks

> We examine several assignments of spin and parity for the pentaquark Theta+ state (J^P=1/2^{+,-}, 3/2^{+,-}) in connection with known baryon resonances. Assuming that the Theta+ belongs to an antidecuplet representation which mixes with an octet, we calculate the mass spectra of the flavor partners of the Theta+ based on the SU(3) symmetry. The decay widths of the Theta+ and nucleon partners are analyzed for the consistency check of the mixing angle obtained from the masses. It is found that a suitable choice of the mixing angle successfully reproduces the observed masses of Theta(1540) and Xi_{3/2}(1860), when their spin and parity are assigned to be J^P=3/2^-, together with other J^P=3/2^- resonances. The decay widths of Theta -> KN, N(1520) -> pi N, and N(1700) -> pi N are also reproduced simultaneously.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the pentaquark Theta+ state and its flavor partner Xi_{3/2}(1860) can be consistently assigned spin and parity J^P=3/2^- within an octet-antidecuplet mixing scenario, provided a suitable mixing angle is chosen; this assignment simultaneously reproduces the observed masses and decay widths of known baryon resonances, whereas the J^P=1/2^+ assignment faces inconsistency between mass spectra and decay widths.

## Method

The authors employed SU(3) flavor symmetry with Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formulae for the antidecuplet and octet representations, allowing mixing between the two representations for nucleon and Sigma states via a 2x2 mass matrix with an off-diagonal mixing parameter delta. They fixed the six free parameters (M_8, M_10bar, a, b, c, delta) by inputting known masses of Theta(1540), Xi_{3/2}(1860), and selected nucleon, Lambda, and Sigma resonances, then computed predicted masses for the remaining multiplet members and decay widths using SU(3) coupling relations.

## Key numbers

* Theta+ mass: 1540 MeV (input), Xi_{3/2} mass: 1860 MeV (input)
* For J^P=3/2^- set 1: predicted Sigma_2 mass = 1834 MeV, predicted Xi_8 mass = 1837 MeV (close to known Xi(1820) with J^P=3/2^-)
* For J^P=3/2^- set 4: predicted Xi_8 mass = 1659 MeV (close to known Xi(1690), predicting its J^P to be 3/2^-)
* Nucleon mixing angle from mass spectrum for J^P=3/2^-: theta_N = 33.0 degrees
* Decay width of Theta+ from nucleon decays for J^P=3/2^- with mass-determined mixing angle: 3.1 MeV (phase 1) or 20.0 MeV (phase 2)
* For J^P=1/2^+: decay width of Theta+ from nucleon decays with mass-determined mixing angle (theta_N=29 degrees): 29.1 MeV (phase 1) or 103.3 MeV (phase 2)
* For J^P=1/2^- pure antidecuplet: predicted Theta+ decay width = 156.1 MeV (unrealistically large)

## Context

This work extends the octet-antidecuplet mixing scenario originally proposed by Jaffe and Wilczek (2003) for J^P=1/2^+ pentaquarks, which assigned N(1440) and N(1710) as nucleon partners with ideal mixing near 35.2 degrees. The authors show that the J^P=1/2^+ assignment faces a known inconsistency between mass spectra and decay widths, as previously noted by Cohen (2004) and Pakvasa (2004). The J^P=3/2^- assignment resolves this inconsistency and enables future experimental determination of Theta+ quantum numbers, with predictions for a new Sigma state around 1840 MeV and possible identification of Xi(1690) as a 3/2^- resonance.
