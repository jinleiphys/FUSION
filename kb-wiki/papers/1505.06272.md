# Effective Field Theory and Time-Reversal Violation in Light Nuclei

> Thanks to the unnaturally small value of the QCD vacuum angle $\barθ< 10^{-10}$, time-reversal ($T$) violation offers a window into physics beyond the Standard Model (SM) of particle physics. We review the effective-field-theory framework that establishes a clean connection between $T$-violating mechanisms, which can be represented by higher-dimensional operators involving SM fields and symmetries, and hadronic interactions, which allow for controlled calculations of low-energy observables involving strong interactions. The chiral properties of $T$-violating mechanisms leads to a pattern that should be identifiable in measurements of the electric dipole moments of the nucleon and light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while a single EDM measurement (e.g., the neutron EDM) can always be attributed to any one time-reversal-violating source with an appropriately tuned coupling, combined measurements of the electric dipole moments of the neutron, proton, deuteron, helion, and triton can provide increasingly detailed information to identify the dominant source of time-reversal violation, under the assumption that lower-dimension operators are most important.

## Method

The authors review the effective-field-theory framework connecting time-reversal-violating mechanisms at the quark-gluon level (represented by higher-dimensional operators involving Standard Model fields) to hadronic and nuclear interactions. They describe the renormalization-group evolution of these operators from the new-physics scale down to the QCD scale, the matching onto chiral effective field theory for hadronic interactions, and the subsequent calculation of electric dipole moments for the nucleon and light nuclei (deuteron, helion, triton) using chiral perturbation theory and nuclear EFTs.

## Key numbers

- The QCD vacuum angle is constrained to $\bar{\theta} \lesssim 10^{-10}$ from the neutron EDM bound
- The existing bound on the neutron EDM is $|d_n| < 2.9 \cdot 10^{-13} e$ fm (from Baker et al., 2006)
- The expected CKM "background" for the neutron EDM is at $\sim 10^{-19} e$ fm
- The deuteron EDM might be probed at the level of $\sim 10^{-16} e$ fm in storage-ring experiments
- The Jarlskog invariant for CKM CP violation is $J \simeq 3 \cdot 10^{-5}$
- The Higgs vacuum expectation value is $v \simeq 246$ GeV
- The up-down CKM element is $V_{ud} \simeq 0.97$
- The relative light-quark mass splitting is $\varepsilon \sim 1/3$

## Context

This review extends the classic studies of the neutron EDM from the QCD $\bar{\theta}$ term by Baluni (1978) and Crewther et al. (1979), which first used chiral symmetry to analyze time-reversal violation. It builds on more recent work by Mereghetti et al. (2010), Maekawa et al. (2011), de Vries et al. (2012), and Bsaisou et al. (2014) that extended the chiral-symmetry analysis to additional time-reversal-violating sources and multi-nucleon systems. The framework enables a model-independent connection between beyond-Standard-Model physics at the TeV scale and low-energy electric dipole moment measurements, with the goal of identifying the dominant source of time-reversal violation from a combination of light-nuclear EDM measurements.
