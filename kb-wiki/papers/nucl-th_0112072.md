# Multiplicity Moments and Hard Processes in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The normalized multiplicity moments and their relation with soft and hard processes in relativistic heavy ion collisions are analyzed in a general two-component model. It is found that, the strong fluctuations in binary collision number N_c in minimum-bias events can enhance the hard component, especially for the higher order moments. This enhancement can not be effectively described by modifying the participant number in the one-component model.

## Key claim

The normalized multiplicity moments \(C_i\) in relativistic heavy ion collisions are sensitive to hard scattering processes primarily through the strong fluctuations in binary collision number \(N_c\) in minimum-bias events, not through the average ratio of hard to soft component; this enhancement cannot be fully reproduced by modifying the participant number in an effective one-component model.

## Method

The paper uses a general two-component model where the total multiplicity at fixed impact parameter is decomposed into a soft component proportional to the participant number \(N_p\) and a hard component proportional to the binary collision number \(N_c\). The normalized multiplicity moments \(C_i = \langle n^i \rangle / \langle n \rangle^i\) are derived analytically in terms of nuclear geometry moments (fluctuations in \(N_p\) and \(N_c\)) and the average ratio \(x\) of hard to soft component, neglecting higher-order elementary dynamics for large \(\langle N_p \rangle\). The model is applied to Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies (\(\sqrt{s} = 56, 130, 200\) A GeV) using a Wood-Saxon nuclear density distribution and experimental data for central rapidity densities to determine \(x\) and the minimum participant number \(N_{min}\) for centrality selection.

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) A GeV for central Au-Au collisions, the average ratio of hard to soft component is \(x = 0.74\), with \(\langle N_p \rangle = 344\) and \(N_{min} = 314\).
- At \(\sqrt{s} = 130\) A GeV, \(x = 0.58\), \(\langle N_p \rangle = 343\), \(N_{min} = 312\).
- At \(\sqrt{s} = 56\) A GeV, \(x = 0.32\), \(\langle N_p \rangle = 330\), \(N_{min} = 287\).
- For minimum-bias events at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) A GeV, the hard contribution to the fifth normalized moment \(C_5\) is almost 50% (ratio \(r_5 \approx 1.5\)).
- In the effective one-component model, the power-law exponent \(\alpha\) needed to mimic the hard component's effect on average multiplicity is \(\alpha = 1.09\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) A GeV, \(\alpha = 1.07\) at 130 A GeV, and \(\alpha = 1.04\) at 56 A GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of normalized multiplicity moments by Zhuang (2000) at SPS energies, where only soft processes (wounded nucleon model) were considered, to include hard scattering processes relevant at RHIC energies. It builds on the two-component decomposition of multiplicity into soft and hard parts used by Gyulassy and Wang (1995) and Kharzeev and Nardi (2001). The paper shows that while the average multiplicity can be effectively described by a one-component power-law model as done by Aggarwal et al. (2002), the normalized moments in minimum-bias events reveal the distinct fluctuation pattern of binary collisions that the effective model cannot capture. This enables using higher-order multiplicity moments as a more sensitive probe of hard processes and nuclear geometry fluctuations in heavy ion collisions.
