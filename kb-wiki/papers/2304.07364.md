# Dark matter effects in modified teleparallel gravity

> This work investigates dark matter (DM) effects in compact objects in modified teleparallel gravity (MTG) in which a modification of Teleparallel Equivalent to General Relativity is used. We applied a tetrad to the modified field equations where a set of relations is found. The conservation equation allows us to rewrite our Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with an effective gravitational coupling constant. As input to these new equations, we use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with dark matter content included, obtained from a Lagrangian density with both, hadronic and dark particle degrees of freedom, as well as the Higgs boson, used as a mediator in both sectors of the theory. Through numerical calculations, we analyze the mass-radius diagrams obtained from different parametrizations of the RMF-DM model, generated by assuming different values of the dark particle Fermi momentum and running the free parameter coming from the MTG. Our results show that it is possible for the system simultaneously support more DM content, and be compatible with recent astrophysical data provided by LIGO and Virgo Collaboration, as well as by NASA's Neutron star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER).

# Dark matter effects in modified teleparallel gravity

## Key claim

The authors show that in modified teleparallel gravity (MTG) with a free parameter β₃ modulating the effective gravitational coupling constant, neutron stars can simultaneously accommodate more dark matter content than in General Relativity while remaining compatible with recent astrophysical observations from LIGO/Virgo and NICER, but only when β₃ > 1, which counteracts the mass reduction caused by increasing dark matter Fermi momentum.

## Method

The authors derive modified Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations from a teleparallel gravity Lagrangian with three free parameters (β₁, β₂, β₃), imposing the constraint 2β₃ − β₁ − β₂ = 0 from energy-momentum conservation to obtain an effective gravitational coupling constant χ̄ = χ/β₃. They solve these equations using equations of state from a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the NL3* parametrization, incorporating dark matter as a 200 GeV neutralino fermion interacting with nucleons via Higgs boson exchange, with the dark particle Fermi momentum k_F^DM treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Dark fermion mass M_χ = 200 GeV (lightest neutralino); Higgs boson mass m_h = 125 GeV; Higgs vacuum expectation value v = 246 GeV
- For k_F^DM = 0 (no dark matter) and β₃ = 0.82, maximum neutron star mass ≈ 2.5 M_⊙; for β₃ = 1.00, maximum mass ≈ 2.8 M_⊙
- For k_F^DM = 0.08 GeV and β₃ = 0.82, maximum mass ≈ 1.6 M_⊙; for β₃ = 1.00, maximum mass ≈ 1.7 M_⊙
- For GW190814 compatibility with k_F^DM = 0.08 GeV, β₃ must lie in the range 2.19–2.50, yielding maximum masses 2.50–2.67 M_⊙ and radii 11.16–11.92 km
- For PSR J0740+6620 (lines constraint) with k_F^DM = 0.08 GeV, β₃ range is 1.41–1.62, giving maximum masses 2.01–2.15 M_⊙ and radii 8.96–9.60 km
- For GW170817 with k_F^DM = 0.08 GeV, β₃ range is 1.23–3.63, with maximum masses 1.87–3.22 M_⊙

## Context

This work extends the modified teleparallel gravity framework of Silas et al. (2020) by incorporating dark matter effects into neutron star structure, combining two usually competing approaches: modified gravity and particle dark matter. It builds on prior RMF-DM models (Lourenço et al., 2019; Dutra et al., 2014) that included dark matter through Higgs-mediated interactions, and on the NL3* parametrization (da Silva et al., 2020) validated against finite nuclei properties. The results enable future Bayesian inference studies to establish confidence regions in the k_F^DM versus β₃ parameter space using available astrophysical data.
