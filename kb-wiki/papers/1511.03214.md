# Numerical assessment of post-prior equivalence for inclusive breakup reactions

> We address the problem of the post-prior equivalence in inclusive breakup reactions induced by weakly-bound nuclei. The problem is studied within the DWBA model of Ichimura, Austern, Vincent [Phys. Rev. C32, 431 (1985)]. The post and prior formulas obtained in this model are briefly recalled, and applied to several breakup reactions induced by deuterons and $^6$Li projectiles, to test their actual numerical equivalence. The different contributions of the prior-form formula are also discussed. A critical comparison with the prior-form distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) model of Udagawa and Tamura [Phys. Rev. C24, 1348 (1981)] is also provided.

## Key claim

The post and prior forms of the NEB cross section within the IAV model are numerically equivalent when the full prior-form expression (including the non-orthogonality and interference terms) is used, contrary to earlier claims by Udagawa and Tamura that the additional terms are unphysical and that the IAV model overestimates data.

## Method

The authors applied the post-form and prior-form DWBA formulas of the IAV model to inclusive breakup reactions induced by deuterons on 62Ni at 25.5 MeV and by 6Li on 209Bi at 36 MeV. To overcome the marginal convergence of the post-form integrals, the distorted waves of the outgoing fragment were averaged over small momentum bins (down to 0.04 fm−1), producing square-integrable source terms; the same averaged waves were used in the prior-form calculations for consistency. The separate contributions of the prior-form expression (UT term, non-orthogonality term, and interference term) were evaluated and compared with the post-form result and with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For 62Ni(d,pX) at Ed = 25.5 MeV, the post-form IAV calculation converged for a bin width of approximately 0.04 fm−1.
- The angle-integrated NEB differential cross section from the post-form and prior-form IAV calculations agreed very satisfactorily, with only small differences attributed to numerical inaccuracies.
- For 62Ni(d,pX) at Ed = 25.5 MeV and θp = 20° in the LAB frame, the sum of EBU (calculated with CDCC) plus NEB(IAV) reproduced the data reasonably well, while the sum EBU + NEB(UT) largely underpredicted the data.
- For 209Bi(6Li,αX) at E = 36 MeV, calculations were also performed (results shown in the paper but not fully detailed in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the IAV model (Ichimura, Austern, Vincent 1985) and addresses the long-standing controversy with the prior-form DWBA model of Udagawa and Tamura (1981), who argued that the IAV model overestimates data based on calculations by Mastroleo, Udagawa and Tamura (1989). The present numerical demonstration of post-prior equivalence supports the formal arguments of Ichimura et al. (1986, 1988, 1990) and Hussein et al. (1990), and validates the binning regularization procedure used in earlier work by Lei and Moro (2015). This enables reliable application of the IAV model to inclusive breakup reactions for weakly-bound projectiles such as deuterons and 6Li.
