# D-state configurations in the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon and the Delta(1232) resonance

> The $Δ-N$ electromagnetic transition form factors are calculated in the Poincaré covariant quark model in three forms of relativistic kinematics. Addition of $D-$state components to pure $S-$state model wave functions, chosen so as to reproduce the empirical elastic electromagnetic nucleon form factors with single constituent currents, brings the calculated $R_{EM}$ ratio for the $Δ(1232)\to Nγ$ transition closer to the empirical values in instant and point form kinematics. The calculated $R_{SM}$ ratio is insensitive to the $D-$state component. In front form kinematics the substantial violation of the angular condition for the spin 3/2 resonance transition amplitude in the impulse approximation prevents a unique determination of $R_{EM}$ and $R_{SM}$, both of which are very sensitive to $D-$state components. In no form of kinematics do $D-$state deformations of the rest frame baryon wave functions alone suffice for a description of the empirical values of these ratios.

## Key claim

Adding D-state components to the rest-frame baryon wave functions brings the calculated \(R_{EM}\) ratio for the \(\Delta(1232)\to N\gamma\) transition closer to empirical values in instant and point form kinematics, but in no form of kinematics do D-state deformations alone suffice to describe the empirical values of both \(R_{EM}\) and \(R_{SM}\); the notable empirical negative peak in \(R_{EM}\) below 0.5 GeV\(^2\) cannot be described by D-state deformations alone.

## Method

The authors calculated the \(\Delta\)-\(N\) electromagnetic transition form factors in the Poincaré covariant quark model using three forms of relativistic kinematics (instant, point, and front form). They added D-state components to pure S-state model wave functions of the nucleon and \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance, with the S-state parameters chosen to reproduce empirical elastic nucleon form factors with single constituent currents for pointlike quarks. The matrix elements of single quark current operators were evaluated in the Breit frame, accounting for Lorentz boosts and Wigner rotations.

## Key numbers

*   The S-state wave function parameters for instant form kinematics were \(a=6\), \(b=600\) MeV; for point form \(a=9/4\), \(b=640\) MeV; for front form \(a=4\), \(b=500\) MeV.
*   The D-state amplitude \(b_N\) in the nucleon wave function was varied among values 0, 0.2, and \(-0.2\) to study its effect on form factors.
*   The empirical structure in \(R_{EM}\) at momentum transfers below 0.5 GeV\(^2\) could not be described by D-state deformations alone.
*   In front form kinematics with the \(Q^+ \neq 0\) frame, the magnetic transition form factor \(G_M^*\) drops to 0 at \(Q^2=0\), in disagreement with empirical values.

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré covariant quark model calculations of elastic nucleon form factors by Bruno (2004) and the \(\Delta\)-\(N\) transition studies by Weber (1990) to include explicit D-state admixtures in the baryon wave functions. It directly addresses the question of whether D-state deformations can explain the nonvanishing \(E2/M1\) and \(C2/M1\) ratios observed in \(\Delta(1232)\) decay, which have also been attributed to meson cloud effects in the coupled-channel model of Sato and Lee (2001). The results enable future work to quantify the relative contributions of D-state configurations versus quark-antiquark (meson cloud) components, and highlight the fundamental limitation of front form kinematics with single quark currents for spin-3/2 transitions, as previously noted by Coester (1992) and Simula (1995).
