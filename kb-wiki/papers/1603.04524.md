# Searching for $Ξ_{cc}^+$ in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We study the doubly charmed baryon $Ξ_{cc}^+$ in high energy nuclear collisions. We solve the three-body Schroedinger equation with relativistic correction and calculate the $Ξ_{cc}^+$ yield and transverse momentum distribution via coalescence mechanism. For $Ξ_{cc}^+$ production in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy, the yield is extremely enhanced, and the production cross section per binary collision is one order of magnitude larger than that in p+p collisions. This indicates that, it is most probable to discover $Ξ_{cc}^+$ in heavy ion collisions and its discovery can be considered as a probe of the quark-luon plasma formation.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the production cross section of the doubly charmed baryon $\Xi_{cc}^+$ per binary collision in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy is one order of magnitude larger than in p+p collisions, but this comparison is hedged: the heavy-ion result is an *effective* cross section per binary collision (412 nb) derived from the total yield divided by the number of binary collisions, while the p+p reference value (61 nb) is from a different calculation at a different center-of-mass energy (14 TeV vs. 2.76 TeV).

## Method

The authors solve the three-body Schrodinger equation for the $ccq$ bound state using the hyperspherical method with a Cornell potential, including a relativistic correction to the light quark motion via perturbation theory. They then construct the Wigner function of the $\Xi_{cc}^+$ from the relativistically corrected wave function and use it as the coalescence probability in a coalescence model applied on the hadronization hypersurface determined by hydrodynamic evolution of the quark-gluon plasma. The charm quark distribution is taken as kinetically equilibrated but not chemically equilibrated, with its space-time evolution governed by a conservation law.

## Key numbers

- $\Xi_{cc}^+$ mass: 3.584 GeV (from the three-body calculation with relativistic correction)
- $\Xi_{cc}^+$ average radius: 0.41 fm
- Most probable relative distance and momentum in the Wigner function: $(\langle r\rangle, \langle p\rangle) = (0.41\ \text{fm}, 1.4\ \text{GeV})$ with standard deviations $\sigma_r = 0.16\ \text{fm}$ and $\sigma_p = 0.39\ \text{GeV}$
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV with $N_{coll} = 2000$: total effective cross section $\tilde\sigma_{AA}^{\Xi_{cc}^+} = 8.24 \times 10^5$ nb, effective cross section per binary collision $\tilde\sigma_{pp}^{\Xi_{cc}^+} = 412$ nb
- Production cross section in p+p collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 14$ TeV: $\sigma_{pp}^{\Xi_{cc}^+} = 61$ nb (from Berezhony, cited in text)
- Maximum initial temperature in central Pb+Pb collisions: $T_0 = 484$ MeV at $\tau_0 = 0.6$ fm/c
- Critical temperature for hadronization: $T_c = 165$ MeV
- Charm quark cross section used: $d\sigma_{pp}^{c\bar c}/dy = 0.7$ mb
- Average transverse momentum of $\Xi_{cc}^+$ from coalescence: $\langle P_T\rangle \simeq 2$ GeV
- Predicted $\Xi_{cc}^+$ lifetime: between 110 and 250 fs

## Context

This work extends the study of multicharmed baryon production in heavy ion collisions, following the authors' prior calculation for the triply charmed $\Omega_{ccc}$ (He, 2015). It builds on the coalescence mechanism previously used for light hadrons (Fries, 2003; Molnar, 2002; Greco, 2003) and on the hydrodynamic description of the quark-gluon plasma (Hirano, 2010). The paper addresses the long-standing experimental search for $\Xi_{cc}^+$, where the SELEX collaboration (2003) claimed observation but FOCUS, Belle, BaBar, and LHCb failed to confirm it. The results suggest that heavy ion collisions at LHC energy provide a favorable environment for discovering $\Xi_{cc}^+$, which could serve as a probe of quark-gluon plasma formation.
