# Scaling and higher twist in the nucleon Compton amplitude

> The partonic structure of hadrons plays an important role in a vast array of high-energy and nuclear physics experiments. It also underpins the theoretical understanding of hadron structure. Recent developments in lattice QCD offer new opportunities for reliably studying partonic structure from first principles. Here we report on the use of the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to study the forward Compton amplitude in the unphysical region. We demonstrate how this amplitude provides direct constraint on hadronic inelastic structure functions. The use of external momentum transfer allows us to study the $Q^2$ evolution to explore the onset of asymptotic scaling and reveal higher-twist effects in partonic structure.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the forward Compton amplitude can be extracted from lattice QCD using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem in the unphysical region below the inelastic threshold, and that this amplitude provides direct access to partonic structure functions and their moments, with the caveat that the statistical precision is currently limited and the predicted logarithmic QCD evolution is too mild over the accessible Q^2 range to definitively separate leading-twist from higher-twist contributions.

## Method

The authors use the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to compute the second-order energy shift of a nucleon in a spatially-varying external vector potential on a 32^3 x 64 lattice at the SU(3) symmetric point (kappa=0.12090) with a non-perturbatively improved clover action. The Compton amplitude T_33 is extracted from the quadratic energy shift for five different Q^2 values ranging from 2.7 to 6.9 GeV^2, and the low moments of the structure function are obtained by Bayesian fits to the Taylor series expansion in omega=2p·q/Q^2, with positivity and monotonicity constraints on the moments.

## Key numbers

- Q^2 values studied: 2.7 to 6.9 GeV^2
- Lattice size: 32^3 x 64
- The lowest two moments of T_1 are resolved at each Q^2 value, with 68% confidence level error bars shown in the moment extraction plots
- The interference (ud) structure function leading moment shows clear suppression at higher Q^2, consistent with a higher-twist interpretation

## Context

This work extends the exploratory study of Chambers (2017) by performing a more extensive analysis of the Compton amplitude across multiple Q^2 values, and introduces a novel method to isolate the ud interference structure function as a signature of higher-twist effects. The approach is complementary to other lattice QCD strategies for partonic structure such as quasi-PDFs (Ji, 2013) and the Euclidean hadron tensor (Liu, 1993), and avoids the need to compute 4-point functions by exploiting the efficiency of the Feynman-Hellmann approach. The results enable future studies of x-dependent parton distributions through reconstruction methods, as discussed in a companion proceeding by Somfleth.
