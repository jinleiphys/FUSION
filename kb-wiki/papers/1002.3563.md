# Collective nuclear excitations with Skyrme-Second RPA

> Second RPA calculations with a Skyrme force are performed to describe both high- and low-lying excited states in $^{16}$O. The coupling between 1 particle-1 hole and 2 particle-2 hole as well as that between 2 particle-2 hole configurations among themselves are fully taken into account and the residual interaction is never neglected, not resorting therefore to a generally used approximate scheme where only the first kind of coupling is considered. The issue of the rearrangement terms in the matrix elements beyond standard RPA is addressed and discussed. As a general feature of second RPA results, a several-MeV shift of the strength distribution to lower energies is systematically found with respect to RPA distributions. A much more important fragmentation of the strength is also naturally provided by second RPA due to the huge number of 2 particle-2 hole configurations. A better description of the excitation energies of the low-lying 0$^+$ and 2$^+$ states is obtained with second RPA with respect to RPA.

## Key claim
Second RPA (SRPA) calculations with a Skyrme force, fully including all couplings between 1p1h and 2p2h configurations as well as among 2p2h configurations themselves, produce a several-MeV shift of strength distributions to lower energies compared to standard RPA, but the authors note that the proper treatment of rearrangement terms for density-dependent forces remains unresolved and that the results obtained with the currently used prescription for these terms may be unreliable.

## Method
The authors solved Hartree-Fock equations in coordinate space for 16O using the SGII Skyrme parametrization, then performed full SRPA calculations (without the diagonal approximation and with nonzero B matrix) including 2p2h configurations up to an energy cutoff of 120 MeV. The SRPA eigenvalue problem involved matrices of order 5-6 x 10^4, solved with a Krylov-Schur iteration procedure from the SLEPC package, retaining only the first 1-2 x 10^3 eigenvalues. Two approaches were tested for beyond-RPA matrix elements: without rearrangement terms, and with rearrangement terms calculated using the usual RPA prescription.

## Key numbers
- Energy cutoff of 120 MeV on 2p2h configurations was used to achieve stable results for monopole and quadrupole strength distributions
- SRPA matrices had dimensions of N = 5-6 x 10^4
- Only the first n ~ 1-2 x 10^3 eigenvalues were calculated
- For the monopole isoscalar case, the SRPA first moment m1 reached 671.6515 (compared to RPA value 671.6516) when including states up to omega_max = 100 MeV
- For the monopole isovector case, the SRPA first moment m1 reached 201.2473 (compared to RPA value 201.2494) at omega_max = 100 MeV
- The spurious state in RPA was found at 1.02 MeV, exhausting more than 96% of the isoscalar EWSR; a renormalizing factor of 1.006 brought it to 0.02 MeV
- In SRPA, the spurious state appeared at imaginary energy

## Context
This work extends earlier SRPA calculations that used the diagonal approximation (neglecting residual interaction in the A22 matrix) or approximated the equations as a second Tamm-Dancoff model, as done by Adachi (1988), Schwesinger (1982), and others. It also builds on recent full SRPA implementations by Roth (2004) using Argonne V18 with the Unitary Correlation Operator Method, and by Gambacurta (2009) for metallic clusters. The results enable future applications of full Skyrme-SRPA to neutron-rich nuclei where pygmy resonances appear, and to double phonon excitation modes that have been studied experimentally but previously required boson mapping procedures with high-order Pauli corrections as discussed by Lanza (2008).
