# N to Delta electromagnetic and axial form factors in full QCD

# N to Delta electromagnetic and axial form factors in full QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations of N to Δ electromagnetic, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar form factors, using both dynamical Wilson fermions and a hybrid approach with staggered sea quarks and domain wall valence quarks, achieve sufficient accuracy at pion masses down to 350 MeV to exclude zero values for the quadrupole-to-dipole ratios EMR and CMR for the first time in full QCD, though the magnetic dipole form factor remains below experimental values at low Q² and requires further chiral extrapolation to determine if the gap can be bridged.

## Method

The authors compute two-point and three-point correlation functions on gauge configurations generated with two degenerate flavors of dynamical Wilson fermions and with MILC configurations using three flavors of staggered sea quarks (Asqtad action) combined with domain wall valence quarks. Sequential inversions are performed with fixed sink and source quantum numbers, and form factors are extracted from plateau regions of ratios of three-point to two-point functions after Gaussian smearing at source and sink, with HYP-smearing applied to gauge links in the unquenched Wilson and hybrid cases.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses range from 580–350 MeV across all simulation types; the lightest hybrid approach pion mass is 354(2) MeV on a 28³×64 lattice
- Quenched Wilson simulations use a 32³×64 lattice with a⁻¹ = 2.14(6) GeV (a = 0.09 fm); unquenched Wilson use 24³×40 and 24³×32 lattices with a⁻¹ = 2.56(10) GeV (a = 0.08 fm)
- Hybrid scheme uses a⁻¹ = 1.58 GeV (a = 0.125 fm) from MILC configurations
- The ratio 2C₅^A/G_A is found to be approximately 1.6, and G_{πNΔ}/G_{πNN} is also approximately 1.6, both independent of Q² and quark mass
- The ratio 8C₆^A/G_p is approximately 1.7, a few percent larger than G_{πNΔ}/G_{πNN}
- The experimental value of G_{πNN}(0) is 13.21(11), while lattice results at the simulated pion masses give smaller values at Q² → 0
- Momentum transfer is limited to Q² ∼ 2 GeV² due to noise at larger values

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of N to Δ transition form factors by Alexandrou (2004) and the LHP collaboration (2007) to include both dynamical Wilson fermions and a hybrid staggered/domain-wall approach, enabling comparison of unquenching effects. The results provide lattice predictions for the parity-violating asymmetry ratio C₅^A/C₃^V to be measured by the G0 collaboration, and test the Goldberger-Treiman relations by comparing the pseudoscalar form factors G_{πNN} and G_{πNΔ} with the axial form factors G_A and C₅^A. The observed deviations from experiment for G_m^* and the Q² → 0 values of G_{πNN} and G_{πNΔ} motivate further study of finite lattice spacing effects and chiral extrapolation to the physical pion mass.
