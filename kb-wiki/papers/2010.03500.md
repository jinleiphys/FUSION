# Numerical Simulation of GUE Two-Point Correlation and Cluster Functions

> Numerical simulations of the two-point eigenvalue correlation and cluster functions of the Gaussian unitary ensemble (GUE) are carried out directly from their definitions in terms of deltas functions. The simulations are compared with analytical results which follow from three analytical formulas for the two-point GUE cluster function: (i) Wigner's exact formula in terms of Hermite polynomials, (ii) Brezin and Zee's approximate formula which is valid for points with small enough separations and (iii) French, Mello and Pandey's approximate formula which is valid on average for points with large enough separations. It is found that the oscillations present in formulas (i) and (ii) are reproduced by the numerical simulations if the width of the function used to represent the delta function is small enough and that the non-oscillating behaviour of formula (iii) is approached as the width is increased.

# Numerical Simulation of GUE Two-Point Correlation and Cluster Functions

## Key claim
Numerical simulations of the GUE two-point correlation and cluster functions, performed directly from their delta-function definitions, reproduce the oscillations of Wigner's exact formula and Brezin and Zee's approximate formula only when the width of the Gaussian used to represent the delta function is sufficiently small; the non-oscillating smooth behavior of French, Mello and Pandey's approximate formula is approached as that width is increased.

## Method
The author numerically computed the two-point correlation function R₂(Eₓ, Eᵧ) and cluster function T₂(Eₓ, Eᵧ) for the Gaussian unitary ensemble (GUE) by representing delta functions as Gaussians of variable width Γ, then ensemble-averaging over large numbers of random matrix realizations (up to 18×10⁷ for N=5). The numerical results were compared with three analytical formulas: Wigner's exact Hermite-polynomial expression (T_H2), Brezin and Zee's small-separation approximation (T_S2), and French, Mello and Pandey's large-separation approximation (T_L2). Simulations were performed for matrix sizes N=5, 10, 100, and 1000, with the variance of matrix elements chosen so that the Wigner semicircle radius a = N/2.

## Key numbers
- For N=5 with a=2.5, the ensemble-averaged level density at the spectrum center is approximately ρ_W(0) = 4/π ≈ 1.27, and T₂(0,0) ≈ (4/π)² ≈ 1.62.
- Numerical simulations for N=5 used ensemble size k_max = 18×10⁷ and Gaussian delta-function width Γ = 0.05 to reproduce the oscillatory exact cluster function T_H2.
- For N=10 (a=5), simulations with Γ = 0.05 and k_max = 9×10⁷ reproduced oscillations; increasing Γ to 0.4 made the numerical results follow the smooth T_L2 behavior for large |Eᵧ − Eₓ|.
- For N=100 (a=50), simulations with Γ = 1 and k_max = 9×10⁶ followed the smooth T_L2 behavior rather than oscillations.
- For N=1000 (a=500), simulations with Γ = 20 and k_max = 9×10⁵ also followed the smooth T_L2 behavior.
- To reproduce oscillatory behavior for N=100, a Gaussian width of no more than Γ ∼ 1/20 and an ensemble size of k_max ∼ 10⁸ would be required, which would occupy 80 GB of memory.

## Context
This work extends numerical studies of random-matrix eigenvalue correlations beyond the traditional spacing distributions and number variance (Dyson 1962, 1963; Bohigas 1991) by directly simulating the correlation and cluster functions themselves. The method reproduces Wigner's exact Hermite-polynomial formula (Wigner 1962; Mehta 1967) and the approximations of Brezin and Zee (1993, 1995) and French, Mello and Pandey (1978). The approach enables investigation of the validity range of analytical results for other ensembles such as deformed ensembles (Bertuola 2005; Guhr 1998) and may assist in studying ensembles that are currently intractable analytically.
