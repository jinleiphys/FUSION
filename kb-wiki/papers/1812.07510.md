# $β^-$ decay study of the $^{66}$Mn - $^{66}$Fe - $^{66}$Co - $^{66}$Ni chain

> Background: Shell evolution can impact the structure of the nuclei and lead to effects such as shape coexistence. The nuclei around $^{68}$Ni represent an excellent study case, however, spectroscopic information of the neutron-rich, $Z<28$ nuclei is limited. Purpose: The goal is to measure $γ$-ray transitions in $^{66}$Fe, $^{66}$Co and $^{66}$Ni populated in the $β^-$ decay of $^{66}$Mn, to determine absolute $β$-feedings and relative $γ$-decay probabilities and to compare the results with Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations in order to study the influence of the relevant single neutron and proton orbitals occupancies around $Z=28$ and $N=40$. Method: The low-energy structures of $^{65,66}$Fe, $^{66}$Co and $^{66}$Ni were studied in the $β^-$ decay of $^{66}$Mn produced at ISOLDE, CERN. The beam was purified by means of laser resonance ionization and mass separation. The $β$ and $γ$ events detected by three plastic scintillators and two MiniBall cluster germanium detectors, respectively, were correlated in time to build the low-energy excitation schemes and to determine the $β$-decay half-lives of the nuclei. Results: The relative small $β$-decay ground state feeding of $^{66}$Fe obtained in this work is at variant to the earlier studies. Spin and parity $1^+$ was assigned to the $^{66}$Co ground state based on the strong ground state feeding in the decay of $^{66}$Fe as well as in the decay of $^{66}$Co. Experimental log(ft) values, $γ$-ray deexcitation patterns and energies of excited states were compared to Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations. Based on this comparison, spin and parity assignments for the selected number of low-lying states in the $^{66}$Mn to $^{66}$Ni chain were proposed. Conclusions: The $β$-decay chain starting $^{66}$Mn towards $^{66}$Ni, crossing $N=40$, evolves from deformed nuclei to sphericity...

## Key claim

The β-decay chain from ⁶⁶Mn to ⁶⁶Ni, crossing N=40, evolves from deformed nuclei to sphericity, with the selective population of 0⁺ and 2⁺ states in ⁶⁶Ni understood within a shape coexistence framework; however, the β-feeding values for excited states should be treated as upper limits and the log(ft) values as lower limits due to the pandemonium effect, and the ground-state feeding of ⁶⁶Fe obtained in this work is at variance with earlier studies.

## Method

The low-energy structures of ⁶⁵,⁶⁶Fe, ⁶⁶Co, and ⁶⁶Ni were studied via β⁻ decay of ⁶⁶Mn produced at ISOLDE, CERN, using 1.4 GeV protons on a UCₓ target with laser resonance ionization and mass separation. β and γ events were detected by three plastic scintillators and two MiniBall cluster germanium detectors, respectively, and correlated in time to build excitation schemes and determine β-decay half-lives; a Bayesian statistical analysis using the SATLAS code with Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling was employed. Experimental log(ft) values, γ-ray deexcitation patterns, and excited-state energies were compared to Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations to propose spin and parity assignments.

## Context

This work extends the decay scheme of ⁶⁶Mn presented by Liddick (2013) and is consistent with results from Olaizola (2017a), while resolving a misassignment of a level at 2121 keV previously quoted at 2130 keV. The study addresses the increase of collectivity at N=40, shape coexistence in the ⁶⁸Ni region, and the onset of deformation in the A=66 chain, building on prior work by Tsunoda (2014), Otsuka (2016), and Leoni (2017) regarding shell evolution and the role of the tensor force. The results enable further tests of Monte Carlo Shell Model calculations for neutron-rich nuclei around Z=28 and N=40, and clarify the β-decay feeding patterns that distinguish deformed from spherical configurations.
