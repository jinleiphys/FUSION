# Thermal photon $v_2$ with slow quark chemical equilibration

> Elliptic flow of direct photons in high-energy heavy ion collisions has been a topic of great interest as it is experimentally found larger than most hydrodynamic expectations. I discuss the implication of possible late formation of quark component in the hot QCD medium on the photon elliptic flow as quarks are the source of thermal photons in the deconfined phase. Hydrodynamic equations are numerically solved with the evolution equations for quark and gluon number densities. The numerical results suggest that thermal photon $v_2$ is visibly enhanced by the slow chemical equilibration of quarks and gluons, reducing the aforementioned problem.

## Key claim

The numerical results suggest that thermal photon elliptic flow \(v_2^\gamma\) is visibly enhanced by slow chemical equilibration of quarks and gluons, reducing the discrepancy between experimental direct photon \(v_2\) and most hydrodynamic predictions. The enhancement is conditional on the quark component forming later in the medium evolution, so that thermal photons are emitted mainly in the middle-to-late stages when the momentum anisotropy is already sizable.

## Method

The author develops a (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic model coupled to evolution equations for quark and gluon number densities, representing chemical non-equilibration via parametrized reaction rates for gluon splitting, quark pair production, and gluon emission from quarks. Thermal photon emission rates from the QGP and hadron phases are calculated based on Arnold (2001) and Turbide (2003), with the QGP rate scaled by the factor \(n_q/n_q^\mathrm{eq}\) to account for the underpopulated quark density. The hydrodynamic equations are solved numerically with a smooth initial condition based on the wounded nucleon density profile from Kolb (2000), and the differential photon elliptic flow \(v_2^\gamma(p_T)\) is extracted from the azimuthal photon spectra.

## Key numbers

- Initial time \(\tau_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\); initial energy density normalization \(e_0 = 30\) GeV/fm\(^3\) for impact parameter \(b = 0\) fm.
- Crossover temperature \(T_c = 0.17\) GeV with interpolation width \(\Delta T = 0.017\) GeV; hadronic freeze-out temperature \(T_f = 0.15\) GeV.
- Reaction rate parameters chosen as \(c_a = c_c = 1.5\) and \(c_b = 0.5\) to represent slow quark chemical equilibration.
- Effective chemical relaxation time \(\tau_\mathrm{chem} = 1/c_b T \sim 2\) fm/\(c\) for \(T \sim 0.2\) GeV.
- Photon elliptic flow \(v_2^\gamma(p_T)\) is shown for the transverse momentum range \(0.5 < p_T < 4\) GeV.
- Quark number density at initial time set to zero; parton degeneracies \(d_q = 24\) and \(d_g = 16\) for \(N_f = 2\).

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic analyses of direct photon elliptic flow, which have historically under-predicted the large \(v_2^\gamma\) observed experimentally by Adare (2011) and Lohner (2012). It builds on the idea from Geiger (1992), Biro (1993), and collinear-splitting models that chemical equilibration of quarks and gluons may be slower than thermalization, and on prior photon emission studies in chemically non-equilibrated systems by Kampfer (1994) and Strickland (1994). The results suggest that late quark production can help resolve the photon \(v_2\) puzzle, and the author notes that future work should include viscous effects, more realistic off-equilibrium equations of state, and triangular flow \(v_3^\gamma\) to distinguish this mechanism from alternatives such as strong magnetic fields.
