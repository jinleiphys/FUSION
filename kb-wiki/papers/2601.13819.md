# $\mathbf{γZ}$ Box at Low Energy

> We calculate the 1-loop $γZ$ box-graph correction to electron-quark scattering at low energy and low momentum transfer. Both electron and quark masses are kept non-zero. From our result, we extract coupling constants for the low-energy effective Lagrangian with parity-violating 4-fermion interaction terms. We study the zero-mass limits and show that a non-zero electron mass is sufficient to obtain finite, well-defined couplings which are insensitive to a hadronic mass cutoff. We finally discuss the impact of our results on the determination of the weak charge of the proton from polarized electron-proton scattering.

## Key claim

The authors show that a non-zero electron mass alone is sufficient to obtain finite, well-defined low-energy couplings from the 1-loop γZ box graph, contradicting the previous assumption that a hadronic mass cutoff or non-zero quark mass is required to regulate the result.

## Method

The authors performed an exact 1-loop calculation of the γZ box-graph correction to electron-quark scattering, keeping both electron and quark masses non-zero throughout. They used FeynCalc and Package-X to compute the Passarino-Veltman scalar integrals, then extracted the effective low-energy coupling constants by taking the threshold limit (s → (m+M)², t → 0) after subtracting the singular Sommerfeld enhancement terms proportional to απ/β.

## Key numbers

- The box-graph correction to the parity-violating coupling C₁q (g_AV^eq) for zero quark mass is δ_box C₁q(M=0) = -(α/4π) Q_e Q_q · 6 g_VA^eq [ln(M_Z²/m²) + 5/6], where m is the electron mass
- The box-graph correction to C₂q (g_VA^eq) for zero electron mass is δ_box C₂q(m=0) = -(α/4π) Q_e Q_q · 6 g_AV^eq [ln(M_Z²/M²) + 5/6], where M is the quark mass
- The effective weak charge of the proton obtained using the previous prescription with Λ = m_ρ and C_γZ(m_ρ) = 3/2 gives Q_W^p,eff = 0.0694
- Taking the limit M_Z → ∞ introduces small errors on the order of 1 to 2% for energies of interest

## Context

This work revisits the classic calculation by Marciano and Sirlin (1982) of γZ box-graph corrections to atomic parity violation, which introduced a hadronic mass scale M with a logarithm L_M = ln(M_Z²/M²). The authors show that the prescription of Marciano and Sanda (1978), using a finite quark mass to regularize the loop integral, is not justified, as it replaces an infrared divergence with a non-universal quark-mass singularity. The result enables a cleaner separation of perturbative and non-perturbative contributions to the weak charge of the proton, relevant for the Qweak experiment and the future P2 experiment at MESA in Mainz.
