# Screening of nucleon electric dipole moments in atomic systems

> The electric dipole moments (EDMs) of diamagnetic atoms are expected to be sensitive to charge-parity violation particularly in nuclei through the nuclear Schiff moment. I explicitly demonstrate that the well-known form of the Schiff moment operator originating from the nucleon EDM is obtained by considering the screening of the nucleon EDMs in a neutral atom. Consequently, an additional contribution to the Schiff moment arises from the screening of the nuclear EDM induced by the interaction of the nucleon EDMs with the protons. This correction to the Schiff moment of $^{199}$Hg is evaluated in the independent particle model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the well-known form of the Schiff moment operator originating from the nucleon electric dipole moment (EDM) is obtained by considering the screening of nucleon EDMs in a neutral atom, and that an additional contribution to the Schiff moment arises from the screening of the nuclear EDM induced by the interaction of the nucleon EDMs with the protons.

## Method

The author uses perturbation theory to examine the screening of intrinsic nucleon EDMs and the nuclear EDM in a neutral atom, introducing Hermitian operators to transform the external and internal interactions. The finite-size effects are evaluated in the point-like nucleus limit, and the resulting Schiff moment contributions are computed using the independent particle model (IPM) for the nucleus $^{199}$Hg.

## Key numbers

* The leading order Schiff moment from nucleon EDMs in $^{199}$Hg is $S_2(^{199}\text{Hg}) = 2.8 \, d_n \, (\text{fm}^2)$ in the independent particle model, consistent with a previous evaluation of $S_2 \simeq 2.2 \, d_n \, (\text{fm}^2)$.
* The next-to-leading order Schiff moment from nucleon EDMs in $^{199}$Hg is $S_3(^{199}\text{Hg}) = -0.15 \, d_n \, (\text{fm}^2)$ in the independent particle model, representing a correction of about 5%.
* The random phase approximation calculation gives $S_2 = (1.895 \pm 0.035) \, d_n \, (\text{fm}^2)$ for $^{199}$Hg, as reported by Dmitriev and Sen'kov (2003).
* An upper bound on the neutron EDM from the experimental limit on the atomic EDM of $^{199}$Hg is $d_n < 1.6 \times 10^{-26} \, e \, \text{cm}$, competitive with the direct measurement $d_n < 1.8 \times 10^{-26} \, e \, \text{cm}$ from Abel et al. (2020).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Schiff moment screening originally developed by Schiff (1963) and later elaborated by Sushkov, Flambaum, and Khriplovich (1984) and Spevak, Auerbach, and Flambaum (1997). The paper resolves a controversy between claims of complete screening of nucleon EDMs in neutral atoms (Oshima 2007, Fujita 2012) and the standard Schiff moment approach used to extract neutron EDM bounds from atomic EDM experiments (Dmitriev and Sen'kov 2003, Graner et al. 2016). The results enable more precise interpretation of ongoing atomic EDM searches, particularly for octupole-deformed nuclei like $^{225}$Ra where the $S_3$ contribution may dominate over $S_2$.
