# The QCD equation of state with 2+1 flavors of Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ)

> One of the fundamental properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), the equation of state, is a subject of extensive studies in lattice QCD and an essential requirement for the correct hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions. Lattice QCD provides first-principle calculations for the physics in the non-perturbative regime. In this contribution, we report on recent progress by the HotQCD collaboration in studying the 2+1 flavor equation of state on lattices with the temporal extent N_tau=6, 8, 10 and 12 in Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) discretization scheme. Comparisons with equation of state calculations with different fermion actions are also discussed.

## Key claim

The HotQCD collaboration reports progress toward a continuum-extrapolated 2+1 flavor QCD equation of state using the HISQ/tree action, but the results on the finest lattices (Nτ=12) still require higher statistics before a continuum extrapolation can be attempted, and the HISQ/tree interaction measure appears to disagree with the stout continuum estimate in the temperature range 170–350 MeV.

## Method

The interaction measure (ε-3p)/T⁴ was computed on Euclidean space-time lattices with temporal extents Nτ=6, 8, 10, and 12 using a tree-level improved gauge action and the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) fermion action. The strange quark mass was tuned to its physical value while the two degenerate light quarks had masses m_l=m_s/20 (slightly heavier than the physical m_l≈m_s/27). The lattice spacing was set in physical units using either the Sommer scale r₁=0.3106 fm or the kaon decay constant f_K=156.1 MeV, and the β-functions R_β and R_m were determined for both scale choices.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark mass m_s is tuned to the physical value; the two light quark masses are m_l=m_s/20, compared to the physical ratio m_l≈m_s/27.
- The Sommer scale used is r₁=0.3106 fm; the alternative scale is f_K=156.1 MeV.
- On Nτ=6 lattices, the temperature difference between using the r₁ and f_K scales is within 9 MeV; on Nτ=12 lattices, the difference is within 2 MeV.
- The HISQ/tree results disagree with the stout continuum estimate in the temperature range T=170–350 MeV.
- On Nτ=10 and Nτ=12 lattices, cutoff effects for both HISQ/tree and asqtad actions are comparable with statistical errors, and agreement between the two actions is good for both the r₁ and f_K scales.
- The lattice results begin to disagree with the hadron resonance gas (HRG) model at T≈150–160 MeV.
- For temperatures up to 800 MeV, cutoff effects in the T→∞ limit are observed only for the p4 action.

## Context

This work extends the HotQCD collaboration's ongoing lattice QCD calculations of the 2+1 flavor equation of state, comparing the HISQ/tree action results with earlier stout action continuum estimates (Ref. stout_eos) and with asqtad action results. The study enables future continuum-extrapolated equation of state calculations needed for hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions, but requires higher statistics on the Nτ=10 and Nτ=12 ensembles before such an extrapolation can be attempted. The paper also compares lattice data to hadron resonance gas (HRG) model predictions at low temperature and to perturbative QCD calculations at high temperature.
