# The axion-baryon coupling in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

> In the past, the axion-nucleon coupling has been calculated in the framework of SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory up to third order in the chiral power counting. Here, we extend these earlier studies to the case of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with SU(3) flavor symmetry and derive the axion coupling to the full SU(3) baryon octet, showing that the axion also significantly couples to hyperons. As studies on dense nuclear matter suggest the possible existence of hyperons in stellar objects such as neutron stars, our results should have phenomenological implications related to the so-called axion window.

## Key claim
The paper extends previous SU(2) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations of the axion-nucleon coupling to SU(3) flavor symmetry, deriving the axion coupling to the full baryon octet up to third chiral order, and showing that axions couple significantly to hyperons, particularly the Λ and Σ⁻, at levels comparable to or potentially larger than the neutron coupling, depending on the axion model.

## Method
The authors use SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBCHPT) to compute the axion-baryon coupling constant \(g_{aAB}\) for all members of the ground-state baryon octet (nucleons, Σ, Λ, Ξ) up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) in the chiral power counting. They include leading-order tree-level contributions from the \(\mathcal{O}(p)\) Lagrangian, \(1/m_B\) and \(1/m_B^2\) corrections from the heavy-baryon expansion, next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) tree-level terms proportional to \(\chi_-\) and \(\chi_+\), and leading one-loop contributions, with renormalization via low-energy constants from the \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) Lagrangian.

## Key numbers
- The axion decay constant window is estimated as \(10^9\,\text{GeV} \lesssim f_a \lesssim 10^{12}\,\text{GeV}\) (traditional axion window).
- The axion mass is given by \(m_a \approx 5.7 \left(10^{12}\,\text{GeV}/f_a\right) \times 10^{-6}\,\text{eV}\).
- The axion lifetime is \(\tau_a \approx \left(1\,\text{eV}/m_a\right)^5 \times 10^{24}\,\text{s}\).
- The neutron electric dipole moment upper limit is \(|d_n| < 1.8\times 10^{-26}\,e\,\text{cm}\) (90% C.L.), implying \(\bar{\theta} \lesssim 10^{-11}\).
- The \(\chi_-\) contribution to the axion-baryon coupling includes a factor \((1+w/(1+z))^{-1}\) where \(w = m_u/m_s\), which gives a correction of about two percent relative to the SU(2) case.
- The leading-order meson masses are expressed in terms of \(B_0\) and quark masses: \(M_{\pi^\pm}^2 = B_0(m_u+m_d)\), \(M_{K^\pm}^2 = B_0(m_u+m_s)\), \(M_{K^0}^2 = B_0(m_d+m_s)\).

## Context
This work extends previous SU(2) HBCHPT calculations of the axion-nucleon coupling by Vonk, Guo, and Meißner (2020) to SU(3) flavor symmetry, and generalizes earlier leading-order axion-baryon coupling derivations by Donnelly (1978), Kaplan (1985), Srednicki (1985), Georgi (1986), and Chang (1993). The results enable revised constraints on axion parameters from neutron star cooling, since hyperons (particularly Λ and Σ⁻) may exist in dense stellar cores as suggested by Glendenning (1982, 1984) and others, and the axion coupling to these hyperons is of similar magnitude to the nucleon coupling, implying that axion-nucleon bremsstrahlung alone may be insufficient for constraining the axion window.
