# The "Hole" In He

> The measurement and analysis of electron scattering from He-3 and He-4 by Sick and collaborators reported 20 years ago remains a matter of current interest. By unfolding the measured free-proton charge distribution, they deduced a depression in the central point nucleon density, which is not found in few-body calculations based on realistic potentials. We find that using wave functions from such calculations we can obtain good fits to the He charge distributions under the assumption that the proton charge size expands toward the center of the nucleus. The relationship to 6-quark Chromo-Dielectric Model calculations, is discussed. The expansion is larger than than the predictions of mean field bag calculations by others or our CDM calculations in the independent pair approximation. There is interest here in the search for a "smoking gun" signal of quark substructure.

# The "Hole" In He

## Key claim

The central depression in the point-nucleon density of He-3 and He-4, previously extracted from electron scattering data by Sick and collaborators, can be explained without invoking a true hole in the point-proton distribution if the proton charge radius expands toward the nuclear center. The expansion required to fit the data is larger than predictions from mean-field bag calculations or from the authors' own Chromo-Dielectric Model calculations in the independent pair approximation.

## Method

The authors used wave functions from Green's function Monte Carlo (GFMC) and variational few-body calculations to construct charge distributions for He-4, then introduced a density-dependent proton form factor whose Gaussian width parameter b(r') varies with distance from the nuclear center. They also employed an independent pair approximation (IPA) using two-body correlation functions from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions, with the proton size parameter b(r_12) depending on inter-nucleon separation, and performed chi-squared fits to the experimental charge distribution data.

## Key numbers

- The experimentally extracted central depression in the point-proton density is about 30% extending to about 0.8 fm for both He-3 and He-4.
- The free proton rms charge radius is 0.83 fm, used as the baseline b_0 = sqrt(2/3) * 0.83 fm.
- A fairly good fit to the He-4 charge distribution was obtained with a density-dependent Gaussian parameterization using A = 0.45 and s = 0.65 fm for the radial falloff of the swelling.
- Best-fit IPA parameters for the inter-nucleon separation dependence with n=2 were A = 2.185 and s = 0.883 fm; with n=4, A = 0.976 and s = 1.245 fm; with n=6, A = 0.774 and s = 1.34 fm.
- The electron scattering experiments by McCarthy, Sick and Whitney reached momentum transfers up to 4.5 fm^-1, yielding a spatial resolution of about 0.3 fm.

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing discrepancy between the charge distributions of He-3 and He-4 extracted by McCarthy, Sick and Whitney (1977) and Sick (1978) and the predictions of few-body calculations based on realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, which do not produce a significant central depression. The authors propose that the apparent hole arises from a swelling of the proton charge size in the high-density nuclear interior, relating this to 6-quark Chromo-Dielectric Model calculations by Koepf, Pepin, Stancu and Wilets (1994-1996). The approach contrasts with that of Kisslinger, Ma and Hoodbhoy (1986, 1991), who used 3-quark/6-quark/9-quark bag models with a step-function transition, and suggests that future tests should combine charge form factor data with quasi-elastic scattering predictions to distinguish between nucleon-only and quark-substructure models.
