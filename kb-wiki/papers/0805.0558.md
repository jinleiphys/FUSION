# Scalar-vector Lagrangian without nonlinear self-interactions of bosonic fields in the relativistic mean-field theory

> A new Lagrangian model without nonlinear scalar self-interactions in the relativistic mean-field (RMF) theory is proposed. Introducing terms for scalar-vector interactions (SVI), we have developed a RMF Lagrangian model for finite nuclei and nuclear matter. It is shown that by inclusion of SVI in the basic RMF Lagrangian, the nonlinear sigma^3 and sigma^4 terms can be dispensed with. The SVI Lagrangian thus obtained provides a good description of ground-state properties of nuclei along the stability line as well as far away from it. This Lagrangian model is also able to describe experimental data on the breathing-mode giant monopole resonance energies well.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that nonlinear scalar self-couplings (sigma^3 and sigma^4 terms) can be removed from the relativistic mean-field Lagrangian and replaced by scalar-vector interaction terms (sigma omega^2 and sigma^2 omega^2) while still providing a good description of ground-state properties of finite nuclei and breathing-mode giant monopole resonance energies, provided the incompressibility is around 272 MeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a new RMF Lagrangian by adding scalar-vector interaction terms U_mm = (1/2)g_4 sigma omega_mu omega^mu + (1/2)g_5 sigma^2 omega_mu omega^mu to the linear Walecka model Lagrangian, while setting the conventional nonlinear sigma self-coupling constants g_2 and g_3 to zero. They performed a multi-dimensional parameter search by fitting experimental binding energies and charge radii of six nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 90Zr, 116Sn, 124Sn, 208Pb) without imposing any constraints on nuclear matter properties. The resulting parameter sets SVI-1 and SVI-2 were then tested on ground-state properties of spherical and deformed nuclei, isotopic chains of Sn and Pb, and breathing-mode giant monopole resonance energies using constrained Generator Coordinate Method calculations.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter incompressibility: K = 263.9 MeV for SVI-1 and K = 271.5 MeV for SVI-2
* Nuclear matter saturation density: rho_0 = 0.149 fm^-3 for both SVI-1 and SVI-2
* Nuclear matter asymmetry energy: J = 37.6 MeV for SVI-1 and J = 37.0 MeV for SVI-2
* Effective nucleon mass in nuclear matter: m* = 0.616 for SVI-1 and m* = 0.621 for SVI-2
* RMS deviation of binding energies for spherical nuclei: delta = 1.33 MeV for SVI-1 and delta = 1.20 MeV for SVI-2, compared to 2.70 MeV for NL-SH and 2.00 MeV for NL3
* RMS deviation of charge radii for spherical nuclei: delta = 0.021 fm for SVI-1 and delta = 0.019 fm for SVI-2
* RMS deviation of binding energies for Sn isotopes: 1.04 MeV for SVI-1 and 1.07 MeV for SVI-2, compared to 1.83 MeV for NL3
* RMS deviation of binding energies for Pb isotopes: 0.88 MeV for SVI-1 and 0.69 MeV for SVI-2, compared to 1.82 MeV for NL3
* RMS deviation of binding energies for deformed nuclei away from stability: approximately 0.62 MeV for both SVI-1 and SVI-2
* GMR energy for 208Pb: 13.3 MeV (SVI-1), 13.5 MeV (SVI-2), compared to experimental value 13.96 +/- 0.28 MeV
* GMR energy for 90Zr: 17.2 MeV (SVI-1), 17.5 MeV (SVI-2), compared to experimental value 17.81 +/- 0.30 MeV
* GMR energy for 120Sn: 15.2 MeV (SVI-1), 15.4 MeV (SVI-2), compared to experimental value 15.52 +/- 0.15 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Savushkin et al. (1997) and Furnstahl et al. (1997) who incorporated various meson-meson interactions, and builds on the earlier exploration of scalar-vector interactions in nuclear matter by Moncada (1994). It contrasts with the standard RMF models NL-SH (Sharma et al., 1993) and NL3 (Lalazissis et al., 1997) which rely on nonlinear sigma self-couplings introduced by Boguta and Bodmer (1977). The SVI model enables a simpler Lagrangian without scalar self-couplings that still reproduces ground-state properties and GMR energies, while also addressing the overly stiff equation of state problem associated with the sigma^3+sigma^4 models that is problematic for neutron star masses.
