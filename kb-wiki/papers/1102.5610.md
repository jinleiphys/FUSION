# An effective thermodynamic potential from the instanton vacuum with the Polyakov loop

> In this talk, we report our recent studies on an effective thermodynamic potential (Omega_eff) at finite temperature (T>0) and zero quark-chemical potential (mu_R=0), using the singular-gauge instanton solution and Matsubara formula for N_c=3 and N_f=2 in the chiral limit, i.e. m_q=0. The momentum-dependent constituent-quark mass is computed as a function of T, together with the Harrington-Shepard caloron solution in the large-N_c limit. In addition, we take into account the imaginary quark-chemical potential mu_I = A_4, indentified as the traced Polayakov-loop (Phi) as an order parameter for the Z(N_c) symmetry, characterizing the confinement (intact) and deconfinement (spontaneously broken) phases. As a consequence, we observe the crossover of the chiral (chi) order parameter sigma^2 and Phi. It also turns out that the critical temperature for the deconfinement phase transition, T^Z_c is lowered by about (5~10) % in comparison to the case with the constant constituent-quark mass. This behavior can be understood by considerable effects from the partial chiral restoration and nontrivial QCD vacuum on the Phi. Numerical results show that the crossover transitions occur at (T^chi_c,T^Z_c) ~ (216,227) MeV.

# An effective thermodynamic potential from the instanton vacuum with the Polyakov loop

## Key claim

The crossover transitions for chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement occur at (T^chi_c, T^Z_c) ≈ (216, 227) MeV respectively for N_c=3 and N_f=2 in the chiral limit, but the critical temperature for deconfinement is lowered by about 5-10% when using a momentum- and temperature-dependent constituent-quark mass compared to the case with a constant constituent-quark mass.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective thermodynamic potential at finite temperature and zero quark-chemical potential by combining the singular-gauge instanton solution with the Matsubara formula for fermions, using the Harrington-Shepard caloron solution in the large-N_c limit to compute the instanton density and average size as functions of temperature. They introduced an imaginary quark-chemical potential identified as the traced Polyakov loop to incorporate the Z(N_c) symmetry order parameter, then solved the saddle-point equations with respect to the mean fields sigma and Phi to obtain the temperature dependence of both order parameters.

## Key numbers

- Crossover transition temperatures: T^chi_c = 216 MeV and T^Z_c = 227 MeV for N_f=2, N_c=3 in the chiral limit using the full momentum- and temperature-dependent constituent-quark mass
- T^Z_c lowered from 240 MeV (constant mass M_0,0 ≈ 350 MeV) to 227 MeV (momentum- and temperature-dependent mass), a reduction of about 5-10%
- For the pure-glue potential, the critical temperature T_0 = 270 MeV at which the first-order deconfinement phase transition occurs
- With M_k,0 (momentum-dependent but temperature-independent mass), T^Z_c = 225 MeV and T^chi_c = 265 MeV
- With M_0,T (temperature-dependent but momentum-independent mass), T^Z_c = 230 MeV
- With M_0,0 (constant mass), T^Z_c = 240 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Polyakov-loop-augmented Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (pNJL) model approach of Fukushima (2003) and Ratti et al. (2004-2006) by replacing the local four-fermion interaction with a nonlocal interaction derived from the instanton vacuum, providing an intrinsic ultraviolet regulator through the momentum-dependent constituent-quark mass. The results are compared with lattice QCD simulations by Maezawa et al. (2007) and Ali Khan et al. (2000) which found T^chi_c ≈ 171-180 MeV for N_f=2, and with Kaczmarek et al. (2005) which found T^Z_c ≈ 202 MeV for full QCD. The approach enables future studies of how the nontrivial QCD vacuum and partial chiral restoration affect the deconfinement phase transition, and suggests that additional temperature dependence in the instanton distribution function or a lower T_0 value (e.g., 200 MeV giving T^chi_c = 194 MeV) may be needed to match lattice results.
