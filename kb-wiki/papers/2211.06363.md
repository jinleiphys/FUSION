# The impact of fluctuating initial conditions on bottomonium suppression in 5.02 TeV heavy-ion collisions

> We compute bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow within the pNRQCD effective field theory using an open quantum systems approach. For the hydrodynamical background, we use 2+1D MUSIC second-order viscous hydrodynamics with IP-Glasma initial conditions and evolve bottom/antibottom quantum wave packets in real time in these backgrounds. We find that the impact of fluctuating initial conditions is small when compared to results obtained using smooth initial conditions. Including the effect of fluctuating initial conditions, we find that the Upsilon(1S) integrated elliptic flow is v_2[1S] = 0.005 +/- 0.002 +/- 0.001, with the first and second variations corresponding to statistical and systematic theoretical uncertainties, respectively.

## Key claim

The impact of fluctuating IP-Glasma initial conditions on bottomonium suppression and elliptic flow is small compared to results obtained using smooth initial conditions, with differences smaller than the systematic theoretical uncertainties associated with variations in the transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors evolved bottom/antibottom quantum wave packets in real time using a next-to-leading order complex effective Hamiltonian derived from pNRQCD effective field theory and open quantum systems. The hydrodynamic background was provided by 2+1D MUSIC second-order viscous hydrodynamics with IP-Glasma fluctuating initial conditions for 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions. Survival probabilities were computed by projecting the final time-evolved wave functions onto vacuum bottomonium eigenstates, and feed-down from excited states was included using a matrix of branching fractions from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- Upsilon(1S) integrated elliptic flow: v2[1S] = 0.005 ± 0.002 ± 0.001, with the first variation corresponding to statistical uncertainty and the second to systematic theoretical uncertainty, for 10-90% centrality and pT < 50 GeV
- Heavy quark mass: M = M1S = 4.73 GeV
- Strong coupling at inverse Bohr radius scale: αs(1/a0) = 0.468
- Lower temperature cutoff for medium evolution: TF = 190 MeV
- Onset of medium evolution: τmed = 0.6 fm
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: η/s = 0.12
- Lattice parameters: NUM = 2048 points, L = 40 GeV⁻¹, dt = 0.001 GeV⁻¹
- Hydrodynamic box size: L = 30 fm with 512 grid points in x and y directions
- Sampled trajectories: 200,000 wave packet trajectories per centrality bin
- Sampled IP-Glasma events: approximately 100 in 0-5% and 5-10% bins, 200 in other centrality bins

## Context

This work extends the open quantum systems approach of Brambilla et al. (2020, 2021, 2022) which used smooth optical Glauber initial conditions, by incorporating fluctuating IP-Glasma initial conditions for the first time in such a framework. It contrasts with earlier studies by Song et al. (2011) that used eikonal transport with dissociation rates, and Kim et al. (2022) that used Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions with the SONIC hydrodynamics code and an adiabatic approximation. The results enable future predictions for higher-order anisotropic flow coefficients (v3, v4) and studies of off-diagonal quantum jump effects on both RAA and vn.
