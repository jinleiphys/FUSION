# Chiral symmetry restoration effects onto the meson spectrum from a Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter approach

> Light meson spectra are studied in a Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to QCD. By varying the interaction strength of three sets of models for the quark-antiquark interaction, the transition from the chiral symmetric to the chirally broken regime in the vacuum is studied. The simplest type of these models leads to degenerate meson spectra for a large domain of the strength parameter. The more sophisticated and thus more realistic models show significantly smaller parameter domains for which degenerate meson spectra are obtained. The underlying mechanism for obtaining and then lifting degeneracies is traced back to the location of the quark propagators' poles, in particular, whether they are beyond or within the domain of integration in the Bethe-Salpeter equation. In view of this mechanism the potential relation of the obtained degeneracies to the dynamical emergence of symmetries is discussed, adding thereby another point of view on the conjectured chiral spin symmetry of QCD in the temperature domain right above the crossover.

## Key claim

By varying the interaction strength in three Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter models, the authors find that meson mass degeneracies emerge not only from chiral symmetry restoration but also from the location of quark propagator poles relative to the integration domain of the Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the simplest model showing degenerate spectra over a much larger parameter range than more realistic models.

## Method

The authors solved the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states in rainbow-ladder truncation, using three interaction models: a Gaussian-type model (Model I), the Maris-Tandy model (Model II), and a model with coupled ghost and gluon propagators (Model III). They varied the interaction strength parameters (D_I, D_II, D_III) to scan from the chirally broken regime to the chirally symmetric regime, tracking the pole locations of the quark propagator in the complex momentum plane and computing meson masses for pseudoscalar, scalar, vector, and axial-vector channels.

## Key numbers

- For Model I, dynamical chiral symmetry breaking occurs at D_I/omega_I^2 >= 1.92 (corresponding roughly to D_I ≈ 0.332 GeV^2), with poles remaining real in the interval 0.332 GeV^2 ≤ D_I ≤ 0.801 GeV^2
- For Model I at the physical point: D_I = 1 GeV^2, omega_I = 0.5 GeV, with D_I/omega_I^6 = 64 GeV^{-4} kept constant during variation
- For Model II at the physical point: D_II = 0.93 GeV^2, omega_II = 0.4 GeV, with current quark mass m[μ = 19 GeV] = 3.7 MeV
- For Model III: D_III varied between 1 and 0.1, with current quark mass m[μ = 31 GeV] = 1.9 MeV
- For Model I in the chiral limit, at D_I = 0.23 GeV^2 the pole locations are at p^2 = 0 and p^2 = -0.249 GeV^2 (the latter being a degenerate pair)

## Context

This work extends the study of meson degeneracies from finite-temperature lattice QCD calculations by Rohrhofer (2019) and the chiral spin symmetry framework of Glozman (2022) to a vacuum Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach, where the interaction strength replaces temperature as the symmetry-restoring parameter. The observed degeneracy patterns, where axial-vector states become degenerate first, followed by vector mesons, then scalars, and finally pions, match the extended multiplets predicted by chiral spin symmetry scenarios. The link between quark propagator analytic structure and meson mass degeneracies has not been observed before and provides a new perspective on the nature of chiral spin symmetry in QCD.
