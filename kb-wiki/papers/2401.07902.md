# Unleashing the Power of EFT in Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> Neutrino physics is advancing into a precision era with the construction of new experiments, particularly in the few GeV energy range. Within this energy range, neutrinos exhibit diverse interactions with nucleons and nuclei. This study delves in particular into neutrino--nucleus quasi-elastic cross sections, taking into account both standard and, for the first time, non-standard interactions, all within the framework of effective field theory (EFT). The main uncertainties in these cross sections stem from uncertainties in the nucleon-level form factors, and from the approximations necessary to solve the nuclear many-body problem. We explore how these uncertainties influence the potential of neutrino experiments to probe new physics introduced by left-handed, right-handed, scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor interactions. For some of these interactions the cross section is enhanced, making long-baseline experiments an excellent place to search for them. Our results, including tabulated cross sections for all interaction types and all neutrino flavors, can serve as the foundation for such searches.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that neutrino-nucleus quasi-elastic cross sections can be computed for all Standard Model and non-standard effective field theory interactions (left-handed, right-handed, scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor), but the precision of these calculations is fundamentally limited by uncertainties in nucleon-level form factors, particularly the axial form factor, and by approximations in nuclear many-body methods, which together determine how well future experiments can constrain new physics.

## Method
The authors compute neutrino-nucleon scattering amplitudes within the Weak Effective Field Theory (WEFT) framework, parameterizing hadronic matrix elements using Lorentz-invariant form factors for vector, axial-vector, scalar, pseudoscalar, and tensor currents. They then incorporate nuclear effects using the spectral function formalism, which accounts for relativistic kinematics and nuclear dynamics, to obtain neutrino-nucleus cross sections for quasi-elastic scattering on carbon-12 and oxygen-16 targets.

## Key numbers
- The axial mass parameter used in the dipole parametrization is \(m_A \simeq 0.961\) GeV, as employed by the GENIEv3 neutrino generator for their nominal \(10a\_02\_11a\) model.
- The nucleon axial coupling at zero momentum transfer is \(g_A = 1.2728 \pm 0.0017\).
- The vector dipole mass parameter is \(M_V = 0.84\) GeV.
- The proton magnetic moment is \(\mu_p = 2.79284\) and the neutron magnetic moment is \(\mu_n = -1.91304\), both in units of the nuclear magneton.
- The average light quark mass is \(m_q = 3.410(43)\) MeV from lattice QCD (2019 FLAG review, \(N_f=2+1+1\)).
- The QCD contribution to the neutron-proton mass difference is \(\delta M_N^{QCD} = 2.58(18)\) MeV, and the up-down quark mass difference is \(\delta m_q = 2.527(47)\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory framework for neutrino oscillations developed by Falkowski et al. (2019) and applied to FASER\(\nu\) by Falkowski et al. (2021), by computing for the first time neutrino-nucleus cross sections for all WEFT operator types including nuclear effects. The spectral function formalism used here has been previously applied by Rocco et al. (2018, 2020) to describe meson exchange currents and pion production, and the results enable systematic searches for new physics in long-baseline experiments like DUNE, with tabulated cross sections publicly available on GitHub.
