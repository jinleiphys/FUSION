# Magnetic moment, vorticity-spin coupling and parity-odd conductivity of chiral fermions in 4-dimensional Wigner functions

> We demonstrate the emergence of the magnetic moment and spin-vorticity coupling of chiral fermions in 4-dimensional Wigner functions. In linear response theory with space-time varying electromagnetic fields, the parity-odd part of the electric conductivity can also be derived which reproduces results of the one-loop and the hard-thermal or hard-dense loop. All these properties show that the 4-dimensional Wigner functions capture comprehensive aspects of physics for chiral fermions in electromagnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the magnetic moment and spin-vorticity coupling of chiral fermions emerge naturally from the 4-dimensional Wigner function formalism, and that the parity-odd electric conductivity derived from this approach reproduces one-loop and hard-thermal-loop results under the condition that the external frequency and momentum are much smaller than the internal momentum.

## Method

The authors solve the quantum kinetic equations for massless chiral fermions in a background electromagnetic field using a perturbation expansion in powers of the gradient operator and field strength. They extract the magnetic moment and spin-vorticity energy shifts from the first-order Wigner function solution by allowing the chemical potential to depend on 3-momentum, and they compute the parity-odd conductivity via linear response theory in space-time varying electromagnetic fields using spherical Bessel function operators.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic moment of a chiral fermion is given by \(\boldsymbol{\mu}_m = \frac{sQ}{2|\mathbf{p}|}\mathbf{v}\), with g-factor \(g=2\) and spin \(\mathbf{S} = \frac{1}{2}es\mathbf{v}\)
- The energy shift from spin-vorticity coupling is \(\Delta E_\omega = -\frac{1}{2}es(\boldsymbol{\omega}\cdot\mathbf{v}) = -\boldsymbol{\omega}\cdot\mathbf{S}\)
- The static limit of the parity-odd conductivity for chirality \(s\) is \(\sigma_{\chi,s}^{(0)} = sQ\frac{\mu_s}{4\pi^2}\)
- The total chiral magnetic conductivity in the static limit is \(\sigma_{\chi}^{(0)} = \frac{1}{2\pi^2}Q\mu_5\)
- The CVE coefficient from the vorticity term is \(\frac{1}{4\pi^2}\left(\frac{\pi^2}{3}T^2 + \mu_s^2\right)\omega^\rho\)

## Context

This work extends the covariant quantum kinetic theory based on 4D Wigner functions developed by Gao (2012) and Chen (2012) to demonstrate the emergence of magnetic moment and spin-vorticity coupling, which were previously shown in the 3D chiral kinetic equation by Son and Yamamoto (2012) and by Manuel (2013). The parity-odd conductivity result reproduces the one-loop calculation of Kharzeev (2009) and the hard-thermal-loop result of Braaten (1989) and Laine (2005). This formalism enables a unified treatment of the Chiral Magnetic Effect, Chiral Vortical Effect, and related phenomena in heavy ion collisions.
