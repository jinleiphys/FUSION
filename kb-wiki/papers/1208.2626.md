# Highly anisotropic dissipative hydrodynamics

> The quark gluon plasma generated in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions may possess sizable momentum-space anisotropies that cause the longitudinal and transverse pressures in the local rest frame to be significantly different. We review recent attempts to derive a dynamical framework that can reliably describe systems that possess a high degree of momentum-space anisotropy. The dynamical framework that has been developed can describe the evolution of the quark gluon plasma ranging from the longitudinal free-streaming limit to the ideal hydrodynamical limit.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the derivation of anisotropic hydrodynamics, a framework that can reliably describe quark-gluon plasma systems with large momentum-space anisotropies, ranging from the longitudinal free-streaming limit to the ideal hydrodynamical limit, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the framework assumes azimuthal symmetry in the local rest frame and that the full (2+1)-dimensional derivation relies on a specific ansatz for the one-particle distribution function.

## Method

The author derives dynamical equations for the energy density, pressures, and four-velocity by taking the zeroth and first moments of the Boltzmann equation with a relaxation-time approximation for the collision kernel. The one-particle distribution function is parameterized using a single scale Λ and a dimensionless anisotropy parameter ξ, which defines a deformation of an isotropic distribution function. For a boost-invariant system with an ideal equation of state, the resulting coupled equations for Λ and ξ are solved to obtain the spatio-temporal evolution of the energy-momentum tensor.

## Key numbers

- Initial isotropic temperature of Λ₀ = T₀ = 0.6 GeV at τ₀ = 0.25 fm/c, with ξ₀ = 0 and 4πη/S = 1, used for a non-central b = 7 fm collision with Monte-Carlo Glauber initial conditions.
- After a very short time, the system develops a pressure anisotropy on the order of P_L/P_T ∼ 0.4–0.5, which slowly relaxes back towards isotropy.
- The relaxation rate is given by Γ = 2R^(1/4)(ξ)Λ/5η̄, where η̄ = η/S with η being shear viscosity and S entropy density.

## Context

This work extends earlier viscous hydrodynamics approaches (Israel 1979, Muronga 2006, Luzum 2008, Dusling 2007) by reorganizing the expansion around large momentum-space anisotropies rather than small shear corrections, motivated by findings that anisotropies can persist for a few fm/c (Ryblewski 2012) and are particularly large at the longitudinal and transverse edges of the plasma (Martinez 2009). The framework enables future work to relax the assumption of azimuthal symmetry in the local rest frame one-particle distribution function.
