# Testing Lepton Flavor Universality with Pion, Kaon, Tau, and Beta Decays

> We present an overview of searches fo violation of lepton flavor universality with focus on low energy precision probes using pions, kaons, tau leptons, and nuclear beta decays. The current experimental results are reviewed, the theoretical status within the context of the Standard Model is summarized, and future prospects (both experimental and theoretical) are discussed. We review the implications of these measurements for physics beyond the Standard Model by performing a global model-independent fit to modified $W$ couplings to leptons and four-fermion operators. We also discuss new physics in the context of simplified models and review Standard Model extensions with focus on those which can explain a possible deviation from unitarity of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa quark mixing matrix.

## Key claim

This review argues that low-energy precision probes of lepton flavor universality (LFU) using pion, kaon, tau, and beta decays provide complementary and highly sensitive tests of physics beyond the Standard Model, with particular emphasis on the Cabibbo angle anomaly (CAA) which shows a 3.7-sigma deviation from first-row CKM unitarity that could be explained by modified W-muon couplings.

## Method

The authors performed a comprehensive review of experimental measurements and Standard Model predictions for LFU tests in charged-current processes, including ratios of leptonic and semileptonic decay rates of pions, kaons, and tau leptons, as well as nuclear beta decays and neutron decay. They then interpreted the experimental constraints in terms of effective field theory, performing a global model-independent fit to modified W-lepton couplings and four-fermion operators, and discussed simplified new physics models with focus on those that can explain the Cabibbo angle anomaly.

## Key numbers

- Standard Model prediction for pion ratio: R(SM)^pi_{e/mu} = 1.23524(15) x 10^{-4}
- Experimental PDG average for pion ratio: R(Exp)^pi_{e/mu} = (1.2327 ± 0.0023) x 10^{-4}
- Resulting muon-electron universality test from pions: (A_mu/A_e)_{R^pi_{e/mu}} = 1.0010 ± 0.0009
- Standard Model prediction for kaon ratio: R(SM)^K_{e/mu} = (2.477 ± 0.001) x 10^{-5}
- Experimental PDG average for kaon ratio: R(Exp)^K_{e/mu} = (2.488 ± 0.009) x 10^{-5}
- Resulting muon-electron universality test from kaons: (A_mu/A_e)_{R^K_{e/mu}} = 0.9978 ± 0.0018
- Global fit to CKM unitarity: Delta_CKM = (-19.5 ± 5.3) x 10^{-4}, a 3.7-sigma effect
- V_ud from nuclear 0+ to 0+ decays: V_ud = 0.97373(31)
- V_ud from neutron decay: V_ud = 0.97338(47)
- V_us from K_ell3 decays: V_us = 0.2231(6)
- V_us/V_ud from R_A: 0.2313(5)
- Tau LFU ratios from HFLAV combination: (A_tau/A_mu)_tau = 1.0010 ± 0.0014, (A_tau/A_e)_tau = 1.0029 ± 0.0014, (A_mu/A_e)_tau = 1.0018 ± 0.0014

## Context

This review extends the ongoing program of LFU tests in low-energy precision physics, building on the theoretical framework of chiral perturbation theory developed by Cirigliano (2007) and the experimental measurements from the PIENU, NA62, KLOE, BaBar, and CLEO collaborations. The paper connects the Cabibbo angle anomaly, first highlighted by Coutinho (2019) and Crivellin (2020), to LFU violation through modified W-lepton couplings, and discusses how future experiments like PIONEER can improve precision. The work enables a coherent interpretation of multiple LFU probes within effective field theory and simplified models, particularly those addressing the tension between V_ud determinations from nuclear beta decays and the unitarity deficit.
