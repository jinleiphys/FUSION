# Torsional oscillations of magnetized neutron stars: Impacts of Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion

> Torsional oscillations of magnetized neutron stars have been well studied since they may be relevant to the physical interpretation of some of the observed quasiperiodic oscillations in the magnetar giant flares. In the crustal region of a magnetar, the strong magnetic field can alter the equation of state and composition due to the Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion. In this paper, we study this effect on the crust-confined, torsional oscillation modes of neutron stars with mixed poloidal-toroidal magnetic fields in general relativity under the Cowling approximation. Furthermore, the inner and outer crusts are treated consistently based on the nuclear-energy density functional theory. Depending on the magnetic-field configurations, we find that the Landau-Rabi quantization of electrons can change the frequencies of the fundamental torsional oscillation mode of $1.4 M_\odot$ neutron star models with a normal fluid core by about 10% when the magnetic field strength at the pole reaches the order of $10^{16}$ G. The shift can even approach 20% at a field strength of $10^{15}$ G for neutron stars with a simple model of superconducting core where the magnetic field is assumed to be expelled completely.

## Key claim

The Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion in magnetar crusts can alter torsional oscillation frequencies by about 10% for 1.4 solar mass neutron star models with a normal fluid core when the polar magnetic field strength reaches the order of 10^16 G, and the shift can approach 20% at 10^15 G for stars with a simple superconducting core model where the magnetic field is expelled completely.

## Method

The authors constructed magnetized neutron star models by iteratively solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with a magnetic-field-dependent equation of state, using the nuclear-energy density functional BSk24 for both inner and outer crusts consistently. They imposed mixed poloidal-toroidal magnetic fields via the linearized relativistic Grad-Shafranov equation and computed torsional oscillation modes under the Cowling approximation in general relativity. The shear modulus of the crust was calculated using the isotropic Coulomb solid model with the magnetic-field-dependent composition.

## Key numbers

- Torsional mode frequency shift of about 10% for 1.4 solar mass neutron star models with normal fluid core when polar magnetic field reaches ~10^16 G
- Frequency shift approaching 20% at field strength of 10^15 G for neutron stars with a simple superconducting core model
- Magnetic energy to gravitational energy ratio scales as ~10^-4 times (B/10^16 G)^2
- The reference magnetic field B_rel = 4.41 x 10^13 G, defined as m_e^2 c^3 / (e hbar)
- Shear modulus formula: mu = 0.1194 n_N Z^2 e^2 / a_i, where a_i is the ion-sphere radius

## Context

This work extends previous studies of torsional oscillations in magnetized neutron stars by Sotani (2007, 2018, 2022) and de Souza (2019) by incorporating the effects of Landau-Rabi quantization of electron motion on the crust equation of state, following the treatment of Chamel (2012) and Mutafchieva (2019). The unified equation of state based on BSk24 functional matches smoothly with the core equation of state from Pearson (2018), avoiding the errors from ad hoc crust-core matching shown by Ferreira (2020) and PhysRevC.104.015801. The results enable more accurate modeling of quasiperiodic oscillations observed in magnetar giant flares from sources such as SGR 1806-20 and SGR 1900+14.
