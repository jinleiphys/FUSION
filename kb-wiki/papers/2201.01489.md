# The $Λ(1405)$ resonance as a genuine three-quark or molecular state

> The mechanism for the formation of the $Λ(1405)$ resonance is studied in a chiral quark model that includes quark-meson as well as contact (four point) interactions. The negative-parity $S$-wave scattering amplitudes for strangeness $-1$ and $1$ are calculated within a unified coupled-channel framework that includes the $KN$, $\bar{K}N$, $πΣ$, $ηΛ$, $KΞ$, $πΛ$, and $ηΣ$ channels and possible genuine three-quark bare singlet and octet states corresponding to $\frac12^-$ resonances. We show that in order to reproduce the scattering amplitudes in the $S_{01}$ partial wave it is important to include the pertinent three-quark octet states as well as the singlet state, while the inclusion of the contact term is not mandatory. The Laurent-Pietarinen expansion is used to determine the $S$-matrix poles. Following their evolution as a function of increasing interaction strength, the mass of the singlet state is strongly reduced due to the attractive self-energy in the $πΣ$ and $\bar{K}N$ channels; when it drops below the $KN$ threshold, the state acquires a dominant $\bar{K}N$ component which can be identified with a molecular state. The attraction between the kaon and the nucleon is generated through the $\bar{K}NΛ^*$ interaction rather than by meson-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance can be explained either as a dynamically generated (molecular) state or as a genuine three-quark state depending on the interaction strength, but that reproducing the full scattering amplitudes in the $S_{01}$ partial wave requires including both the singlet and octet three-quark bare states, while the contact (four-point) interaction is not mandatory.

## Method

The authors used a chiral quark model based on an SU(3)-extended Cloudy Bag Model with quark-meson and contact interactions, solving coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the $S_{01}$ and $S_{11}$ partial waves. The channels included $KN$, $\bar{K}N$, $\pi\Sigma$, $\eta\Lambda$, $K\Xi$, $\pi\Lambda$, and $\eta\Sigma$, with bare three-quark states corresponding to the $\Lambda(1405)$, $\Lambda(1670)$, $\Lambda(1800)$, $\Sigma(1750)$, and $\Sigma(1900)$ resonances. The Laurent-Pietarinen expansion was used to determine $S$-matrix poles and their evolution as a function of interaction strength.

## Key numbers

- Bag radius $R = 0.83$ fm and meson decay constants $f_\pi = f_\eta = f_K = 73$ MeV were used as standard parameters.
- For dynamically generated poles without bare states (two channels, $\pi\Sigma$ and $\bar{K}N$), the lower pole was at Re$W = 1348$ MeV with $-2$Im$W = 33$ MeV (for $R=0.83$ fm, $f_\pi=73$ MeV), and the upper pole at Re$W = 1433$ MeV with $-2$Im$W = 20$ MeV.
- With bare three-quark states included, the $\Lambda(1405)$ pole in the $\pi\Sigma$ channel was at Re$W = 1417$ MeV with $-2$Im$W = 30$ MeV; the bare singlet mass was $m^0 = 1667$ MeV.
- The $\Lambda(1670)$ pole in the $\pi\Sigma$ channel was at Re$W = 1667$ MeV with $-2$Im$W = 29$ MeV; the $\Lambda(1800)$ pole in the $\bar{K}N$-$\pi\Sigma$ channel was at Re$W = 1814$ MeV with $-2$Im$W = 198$ MeV.
- The scattering length for $\bar{K}N$ in the $S_{01}$ partial wave was $a_{0\,\mathrm{LSE}} = (-1.32 + i\,1.00)$ fm from the full LSE solution.
- For the $S_{11}$ partial wave, the $\Sigma(1750)$ pole in the $\bar{K}N$-$\bar{K}N$ channel (five channels) was at Re$W = 1738$ MeV with $-2$Im$W = 48$ MeV; the $\Sigma(1900)$ pole was at Re$W = 1924$ MeV with $-2$Im$W = 96$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Cloudy Bag Model approach of Thomas (1984, 1985) and Jennings (1986) to include multiple bare three-quark states and a full coupled-channel framework with seven channels, building on the authors' previous analyses of non-strange resonances such as the Roper resonance (PRC2018) and the $\Delta(1600)$ (PRC2019). The results contradict the purely dynamical (molecular) picture from chiral unitary approaches (Weise 1995, Oset 1998, Lutz 2002) by showing that bare three-quark octet states are essential to reproduce the $\Lambda(1670)$ and $\Lambda(1800)$ resonances, while the $\Lambda(1405)$ can arise from either mechanism. The findings enable future studies of the interplay between quark and molecular degrees of freedom in strange baryon resonances, and provide a framework for analyzing lattice QCD results such as those from Leinweber (2012, 2015, 2018) and the Graz group (Lang 2013).
