# Possible Evidence for Radial Flow of Heavy Mesons in d+Au Collisions

> Recent measurements of particle correlations and the spectra of hadrons at both RHIC and the LHC are suggestive of hydrodynamic behavior in very small collision systems (p+Pb, d+Au and p+p collisions). The measurements are both qualitatively and quantitatively similar to what is seen in central heavy ion collisions where low viscosity hot nuclear matter is formed. While light quarks and gluons are thought to make up the bulk matter, one of the most surprising results in heavy ion collisions is that charm quarks also have a large $v_2$, suggesting that they too participate in the matter. Measurements of the transverse momentum spectra of electrons from the decay of $D$ and $B$ mesons in d+Au show an enhancement in central collisions relative to p+p. We employ the blast-wave model to determine if the flow of heavy quarks in d+Au and p+Pb data is able to explain the enhancement observed in the data. We find a reasonable description of the data with blast-wave parameters extracted from fits to the light hadron spectra, suggesting hydrodynamics as a possible explanation.

# Possible Evidence for Radial Flow of Heavy Mesons in d+Au Collisions

## Key claim
The enhancement of electrons from heavy flavor decay observed in central d+Au collisions at RHIC could be caused by radial flow, as a blast-wave model parameterized by light hadron spectra provides a qualitative description of the data; however, the uncertainties on both the data and the calculation are large, so hydrodynamics is only one possible explanation.

## Method
Blast-wave model parameters (maximum surface velocity β_max and freeze-out temperature T_fo) were extracted via simultaneous fits to π±, K±, p, and p̅ transverse momentum spectra in the 0-20% most central d+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV, using data from PHENIX. These parameters were then used to generate D and B meson spectra via the same blast-wave functional form, which were normalized to the integrated yield from Fixed-Order-Next-to-Leading-Log (FONLL) calculations for p+p collisions. The resulting meson spectra were decayed into electrons using PYTHIA 8 to produce the expected electron R_dAu, with binary scaling enforced at high p_T where the blast-wave expectation falls below FONLL.

## Key numbers
- β_max = 0.70 and T_fo = 139 MeV from the best fit to light hadron spectra in 0-20% central d+Au collisions
- Systematic variation of blast-wave parameters yields T_fo = 145 MeV and β_max = 0.66 (steeper spectra scenario) or T_fo = 127 MeV and β_max = 0.74 (flatter spectra scenario)
- D meson enhancement reaches approximately a factor of 2 over FONLL at p_T ≈ 2 GeV/c
- B meson enhancement reaches approximately a factor of 1.8 at p_T ≈ 5 GeV/c
- In p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the predicted D meson enhancement reaches a maximum of approximately 20% at p_T ≈ 3 GeV/c, and electrons are enhanced by 10-20% over p_T = 1-6 GeV/c
- In √s = 200 GeV p+p collisions, extracted ⟨β⟩ values are 0.244±0.081 and 0.28±0.02 from prior work

## Context
This work extends the investigation of possible hydrodynamic behavior in small collision systems, following observations of elliptic flow and radial flow signatures in p+Pb and d+Au collisions by ALICE (Abelev 2013), ATLAS (Aad 2013), PHENIX (Adare 2013), and STAR (Abelev 2008). The specific heavy flavor enhancement in d+Au collisions was previously reported by PHENIX (Adare 2012) and was consistent with Cronin enhancement. The blast-wave approach used here follows the formalism of Kolb (2000) and Schnedermann (1993), and the extracted parameters are compared to those from STAR (Abelev 2008) for the same collision system. The results suggest that if hydrodynamics describes light hadrons in d+Au, it may also apply to heavy flavor, and the paper calls for future measurements with vertex detectors at STAR and PHENIX to separate charm and bottom contributions and for multiplicity-selected p+p studies at √s = 200 GeV.
