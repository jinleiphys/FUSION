# Non-Zero Magnetic Screening Mass in QED and QCD at One Loop Level in Non-Equilibrium

> Using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path integral formalism we show that the magnetic screening mass in QED and QCD at one loop level is non-zero as long as the single particle distribution function f(\vec{k}) is non-isotropic, {i.e.} it depends on the direction of the momentum. For isotropic distribution functions such as those corresponding to thermal equilibrium the magnetic screening mass at one loop level is found to be zero which is consistent with finite temperature field theory. The non-zero magnetic screening mass in non-isotropic non-equlibrium situations has fundamental importance in that it acts as a natural cut-off to remove infrared divergences in the magnetic sector. Thus it allows one to avoid infrared problems which previously made it difficult to use a transport theory approach using perturbative QCD or QED scattering kernels to study the thermalization of a QED or QCD plasma.

# Non-Zero Magnetic Screening Mass in QED and QCD at One Loop Level in Non-Equilibrium

## Key claim

Using the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path integral formalism, the authors show that the magnetic screening mass in QED and QCD at one loop level is non-zero only when the single particle distribution function f(k) is non-isotropic, i.e., depends on the direction of momentum; for isotropic or equilibrium distributions the magnetic screening mass remains zero at one loop, consistent with finite temperature field theory.

## Method

The authors compute the retarded photon and gluon self-energies at one loop using the closed time path (CTP) formalism, extracting the transverse (magnetic) and longitudinal (Debye) screening masses in the static limit (p0 = 0, |p| → 0). For QED, they evaluate the electron loop contribution to the photon self-energy; for QCD, they combine the quark loop contribution with the gluon loop contribution from their earlier work (Cooper, 2002) using the frozen ghost formalism, summing over quark flavors and including the gluon distribution function with color factor Nc.

## Key numbers

- For an equilibrium distribution in QED, the Debye screening mass is m_D^2 = e^2 T^2 / 3, matching finite temperature QED results.
- For an equilibrium distribution in QCD, the Debye screening mass is m_D^2 = g^2 T^2 [N_c/3 + N_f/6], matching finite temperature QCD results.
- The magnetic screening mass m_g^2 is exactly zero for any isotropic distribution function f(|k|) or equilibrium distribution f_eq(k0) in both QED and QCD at one loop.
- The magnetic screening mass in QED at one loop is given by m_g^2(hat p) = 2e^2 ∫ d^3k/(2π)^3 { [1 + (hat p·hat k)^2] f(k)/|k| + [1 - (hat p·hat k)^2] [(hat p·∇_k)/(hat p·hat k) f(k)] }.
- The magnetic screening mass in QCD at one loop is m_g^2(hat p) = g^2 ∫ d^3k/(2π)^3 { [1 + (hat p·hat k)^2] [N_c f_g(k) + Σ_q f_q(k)]/|k| + [1 - (hat p·hat k)^2] [(hat p·∇_k)/(hat p·hat k) [N_c f_g(k) + Σ_q f_q(k)]] }.

## Context

This work extends the finite temperature field theory results of Pisarski (1989), Braaten and Pisarski (1990), and Thoma (1995), who found zero magnetic screening mass at one loop in equilibrium, to non-equilibrium non-isotropic plasmas. The non-zero magnetic screening mass in non-isotropic situations provides a natural infrared cutoff for the magnetic sector, removing the infrared divergences that previously hindered transport theory approaches using perturbative QCD or QED scattering kernels to study thermalization of QED or QCD plasmas. This is particularly relevant for heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the initial parton system is highly non-isotropic and may not thermalize before hadronization.
