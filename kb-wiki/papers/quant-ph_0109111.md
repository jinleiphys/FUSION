# Single and double slit scattering of wave packets

> The scattering of wave packets from a single slit and a double slit with the Schrödinger equation, is studied numerically and theoretically. The phenomenon of diffraction of wave packets in space and time in the backward region, previously found for barriers and wells, is encountered here also. A new phenomenon of forward diffraction that occurs only for packets thiner than the slit, or slits, is calculated numerically as well as, in a theoretical approximation to the problem. This diffraction occurs at the opposite end of the usual diffraction phenomena with monochromatic waves.

# Single and double slit scattering of wave packets

## Key claim

Contrary to the intuitive expectation that a thin wave packet (with a broad energy-momentum spectrum) should produce no diffraction pattern in the forward direction, numerical solutions of the Schrödinger equation show that diffraction patterns reappear for packets initially thinner than the slit dimensions, provided the packet width in the direction perpendicular to the slit is also sufficiently small. The effect is attributed to the excitation of cavity modes inside the slit, which are not captured by the standard Kirchhoff approximation.

## Method

The two-dimensional time-dependent Schrödinger equation was solved numerically using a direct extension of methods from prior work (Kälbermann, 1997–2000), with a repulsive screen potential of order 10¹⁹ (effectively infinite) and flux conservation verified to better than 10⁻⁹. A Gaussian minimal-uncertainty wave packet was launched from the left toward single-slit and double-slit geometries, and the wave amplitude was computed in both the backward and forward regions. An analytical approximation was developed using the Kirchhoff integral with Dirichlet boundary conditions, supplemented by a cavity-mode expansion (standing waves with Dirichlet conditions on the walls and an antinode at the slit exit) to account for discrete spectral components inside the slit.

## Key numbers

- Packet width parameters σ₁ = σ₂ = 1 (thin) and σ₁ = σ₂ = 2 (wide) for single-slit scattering at t = 300, with slit dimensions a = 2, b = 3 (thin) and a = b = 1 (wide), showing disappearance of the backward multiple-peak structure for the wide packet.
- For forward diffraction, thin packet (σ₁ = σ₂ = 1, a = 2, b = 3) at x = 29.76, t = 300, v_x = 0.05, m = 20, produces a structured diffraction pattern; wide packet (σ₁ = 2, σ₂ = 1, a = 1, b = 2) under the same conditions shows no pattern.
- The critical ratio for forward diffraction appearance is σ₁/a > 1 (no pattern) versus σ₁/a < 1 (pattern present); the ratio σ₂/b is less sensitive, with pattern persisting for σ₂ up to 5 when b = 1.
- Long-time analytical prediction for thin packet (σ₁ = σ₂ = 1, a = 2, b = 3) at t = 50000, x = 5000, shows a jagged multi-peak structure, while a wide packet (σ₁ = σ₂ = 100) at the same time and distance reproduces the plane-wave diffraction pattern.

## Context

This work extends the author's prior studies of "wavepacket diffraction in space and time" (Kälbermann, 1997–2000), previously demonstrated for barriers and wells in one and two dimensions, to the classic paradigm of single- and double-slit scattering. It contradicts the standard expectation, based on the Kirchhoff approximation (Jackson, 1975; Zeilinger, 1986), that thin packets (broad spectrum) should produce only a single central peak in the forward direction. The new "cavity mode diffraction" mechanism, involving transient excitation of standing waves inside the slit, is proposed as an improvement over the Kirchhoff approximation, which fails for thin packets. The work suggests experimental tests using cold Bose gas packets or liquid helium, and calls for a renewed theoretical effort on matter-wave slit diffraction beyond the monochromatic plane-wave treatment.
