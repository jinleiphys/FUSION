# Unitarity potentials and neutron matter at the unitary limit

> We study the equation of state of neutron matter using a family of unitarity potentials all of which are constructed to have infinite $^1S_0$ scattering lengths $a_s$. For such system, a quantity of much interest is the ratio $ξ=E_0/E_0^{free}$ where $E_0$ is the true ground-state energy of the system, and $E_0^{free}$ is that for the non-interacting system. In the limit of $a_s\to \pm \infty$, often referred to as the unitary limit, this ratio is expected to approach a universal constant, namely $ξ\sim 0.44(1)$. In the present work we calculate this ratio $ξ$ using a family of hard-core square-well potentials whose $a_s$ can be exactly obtained, thus enabling us to have many potentials of different ranges and strengths, all with infinite $a_s$. We have also calculated $ξ$ using a unitarity CDBonn potential obtained by slightly scaling its meson parameters. The ratios $ξ$ given by these different unitarity potentials are all close to each other and also remarkably close to 0.44, suggesting that the above ratio $ξ$ is indifferent to the details of the underlying interactions as long as they have infinite scattering length. A sum-rule and scaling constraint for the renormalized low-momentum interaction in neutron matter at the unitary limit is discussed.

## Key claim

The universal constant ξ (the ratio of the true ground-state energy to that of the non-interacting system) for neutron matter at the unitary limit is approximately 0.44, and this value is independent of the detailed structure of the underlying interaction potentials as long as they have infinite scattering length, as demonstrated by comparing results from hard-core square-well potentials and a tuned CDBonn potential.

## Method

The authors constructed a family of hard-core square-well (HCSW) potentials with analytically determined scattering lengths, selecting three sets of parameters (HCSW01, HCSW02, HCSW03) that satisfy the condition for infinite scattering length (A=0). They also used a unitarity CDBonn potential obtained by slightly scaling its meson parameters to achieve a very large scattering length of -12070 fm. All potentials were transformed into low-momentum interactions Vlow-k using a renormalization group method that preserves scattering lengths, and the neutron matter equation of state was calculated using both an all-order ring-diagram method (summing pphh ring diagrams) and a model-space Hartree-Fock (MSHF) method.

## Key numbers

- The universal ratio ξ is expected to approach 0.44(1) at the unitary limit (as → ±∞)
- The unitarity CDBonn potential has as = -12070 fm, obtained by adjusting meson parameters by about 2%
- Three HCSW unitarity potentials have as values of 15.2×10^6 fm (HCSW01), 3.38×10^6 fm (HCSW02), and -4.58×10^6 fm (HCSW03)
- The effective ranges re for the HCSW potentials are 2.36 fm (HCSW01), 2.21 fm (HCSW02), and 2.20 fm (HCSW03), all close to the unitarity-CDBonn's re of 2.54 fm
- Ring-diagram calculations give ξ values stable to within less than 0.002 variation with Λ in the range 2.0 to 2.4 fm^-1
- MSHF calculations give ξ values nearly constant for kF between 0.9 and 1.4 fm^-1 (density range 0.016 to 0.092 fm^-3)
- For kF = 1.2 fm^-1, the MSHF ξ values from the four potentials range from 0.434 to 0.440
- First-order HF calculations (including only the 1st-order ring diagram) give ξHF ≈ 0.55 for kF = 0.8 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Siu 2008) where ξ was calculated for neutron matter using a tuned CDBonn potential, by now testing universality with a family of analytically tractable HCSW potentials. The results confirm the universal constant ξ ≈ 0.44, consistent with quantum Monte Carlo estimates from Carlson (2003) giving 0.44(1), Astrakharchik (2004) giving 0.42(1), and Gezerlis (2008) giving 0.40(1), as well as experimental values for trapped ultracold Fermi gases (Partridge 2006, Stewart 2006, Luo 2007). The work enables further studies of the sum-rule and scaling constraint for the renormalized low-momentum interaction at the unitary limit, and suggests that the MSHF method, while simpler, provides results nearly identical to the more complex ring-diagram method for pure neutron matter.
