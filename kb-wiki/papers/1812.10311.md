# Proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors from lattice QCD

> The electromagnetic form factors of the proton and the neutron are computed within lattice QCD using simulations with quarks masses fixed to their physical values. Both connected and disconnected contributions are computed. We analyze two new ensembles of $N_f = 2$ and $N_f = 2 + 1 + 1$ twisted mass clover-improved fermions and determine the proton and neutron form factors, the electric and magnetic radii, and the magnetic moments. We use several values of the sink-source time separation in the range of 1.0 fm to 1.6 fm to ensure ground state identification. Disconnected contributions are calculated to an unprecedented accuracy at the physical point. Although they constitute a small correction, they are non-negligible and contribute up to 15% for the case of the neutron electric charge radius.

## Key claim

The paper computes proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors from lattice QCD at physical quark masses, including both connected and disconnected contributions, and finds that while disconnected contributions are small corrections, they are non-negligible and contribute up to 15% for the neutron electric charge radius.

## Method

The authors analyze two ensembles of twisted mass clover-improved fermions: an \(N_f = 2+1+1\) ensemble (cB211.072.64) and an \(N_f = 2\) ensemble (cA2.09.64), both with quark masses tuned to their physical values. They compute connected three-point functions using sequential inversions through the sink for five sink-source time separations from 0.96 fm to 1.60 fm, and evaluate disconnected contributions using hierarchical probing combined with deflation of the 200 lowest eigenvalues of the squared Dirac operator. The form factors are extracted using plateau, two-state, summation, and derivative summation methods to control excited-state contamination, and fitted with dipole, Galster-like, and z-expansion parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing for the \(N_f=2+1+1\) ensemble: \(a = 0.0801(4)\) fm
- Pion mass for the \(N_f=2+1+1\) ensemble: \(m_\pi = 0.1393(7)\) GeV
- Nucleon-to-pion mass ratio for the \(N_f=2+1+1\) ensemble: \(m_N/m_\pi = 6.74(3)\), compared to the physical ratio of 6.8
- Sink-source time separations used: 0.96 fm to 1.60 fm (corresponding to \(t_s/a = 12\) to 20)
- Disconnected contributions contribute up to 15% for the neutron electric charge radius
- For the \(N_f=2+1+1\) ensemble, 750 configurations were analyzed with up to 64 source positions per configuration for the largest time separation, yielding 48000 measurements for \(t_s/a = 20\)
- For disconnected loops, 200 low modes were deflated and 512 Hadamard vectors were used, resulting in 6144 inversions per configuration

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of nucleon electromagnetic form factors by Alexandrou et al. (2017, 2018) and others such as Capitani et al. (2015), Green et al. (2014), Hasan et al. (2017), and Ishikawa et al. (2018), by performing simulations at the physical pion mass with unprecedented accuracy for disconnected contributions. The results enable direct comparison with experimental determinations of the proton charge radius, which is relevant to the "proton radius puzzle" arising from the discrepancy between muonic hydrogen measurements by Pohl et al. (2010) and electron-proton scattering experiments by Bernauer et al. (2013). The inclusion of disconnected contributions at the physical point provides a more complete first-principles calculation of nucleon structure, and the comparison between \(N_f=2\) and \(N_f=2+1+1\) ensembles allows assessment of unquenching effects from strange and charm quarks.
