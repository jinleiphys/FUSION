# Tensor part of the Skyrme energy density functional. II: Deformation properties of magic and semi-magic nuclei

> We study systematically the impact of the time-even tensor terms of the Skyrme energy density functional, i.e. terms bilinear in the spin-current tensor density, on deformation properties of closed shell nuclei corresponding to 20, 28, 40, 50, 82, and 126 neutron or proton shell closures. We compare results obtained with three different families of Skyrme parameterizations whose tensor terms have been adjusted on properties of spherical nuclei: (i) TIJ interactions proposed in the first paper of this series [T. Lesinski et al., Phys. Rev. C 76, 014312 (2007)] which were constructed through a complete readjustment of the rest of the functional (ii) parameterizations whose tensor terms have been added perturbatively to existing Skyrme interactions, with or without readjusting the spin-orbit coupling constant. We analyse in detail the mechanisms at play behind the impact of tensor terms on deformation properties and how studying the latter can help screen out unrealistic parameterizations. It is expected that findings of the present paper are to a large extent independent of remaining deficiencies of the central and spin-orbit interactions, and will be of great value for the construction of future, improved energy functionals.

## Key claim

The impact of time-even tensor terms on deformation properties of magic and semi-magic nuclei is systematically studied, but the authors caution that the current form of the central Skyrme interaction is not flexible enough to allow a satisfactory description of single-particle spectra even when tensor terms are included; the findings on deformation are expected to be largely independent of remaining deficiencies in the central and spin-orbit parts.

## Method

The authors performed constrained Hartree-Fock calculations using the Skyrme energy density functional with time-even tensor terms bilinear in the spin-current tensor density, comparing three families of parameterizations: (i) TIJ interactions from Lesinski et al. (2007) with a complete refit of all parameters, (ii) parameterizations where tensor terms were added perturbatively to existing Skyrme interactions with or without readjusting the spin-orbit coupling constant, and (iii) the standard SLy4 and SLy5 parameterizations without tensor terms. Deformation energy curves were computed for even-even nuclei with neutron or proton shell closures at 20, 28, 40, 50, 82, and 126, and the mechanisms behind the tensor terms' influence on deformation properties were analyzed.

## Key numbers

- The parameterizations T26, T44, and T62 share the same isoscalar tensor coupling constant C0^J = 120 MeV fm^5, and differ by the isovector one C1^J which takes values 120, 0, and -120 MeV fm^5 respectively.
- The coupling constants alpha and beta are related to the TIJ indices by alpha = 60(J-2) MeV fm^5 and beta = 60(I-2) MeV fm^5.
- Parameterization T22 has by construction vanishing tensor terms at sphericity and should have properties close to those of SLy4.
- The study covers shell closures at neutron or proton numbers 20, 28, 40, 50, 82, and 126.

## Context

This work extends the study of tensor terms in the Skyrme energy density functional from spherical nuclei, reported in Lesinski et al. (2007) (Article I), to deformed nuclei, addressing a gap noted by Zalewski et al. (2008) who considered deformation but did not study the impact of tensor terms on deformation properties as such. The findings are intended to guide the construction of future improved energy functionals by showing how studying deformation properties can help screen out unrealistic parameterizations, and the authors note that the results are consistent with Kortelainen et al. (2008) who found it impossible to satisfactorily describe single-particle levels of doubly-magic nuclei with a standard Skyrme functional including tensor terms.
