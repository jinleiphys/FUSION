# Comparison of the Effective Interaction to Various Orders in Different Mass Regions

> The convergence of the perturbation expansion for the effective interaction to be used in shell-model calculations is investigated as function of the mass number $A$, from $A=4$ to $A=208$. As the mass number increases, there are more intermediate states to sum over in each higher-order diagram which contributes to the effective interaction. Together with the fact that the energy denominators in each diagram are smaller for larger mass numbers, these two effects could largely enhance higher-order contributions to the effective interaction, thereby deteriorating the order-by-order convergence of the effective interaction. This effect is counterbalanced by the short range of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, which implies that its matrix elements are weaker for valence single-particle states in ``large'' nuclei with large mass number as compared to those in light nuclei. These effects are examined by comparing various mean values of the matrix elements. It turns out that the contributions from higher-order terms remain fairly stable as the mass number increases from $A=4$ to $A=208$. The implications for nuclear structure calculations are discussed.

# Comparison of the Effective Interaction to Various Orders in Different Mass Regions

## Key claim
The convergence of the perturbation expansion for the effective shell-model interaction remains fairly stable as mass number increases from A=4 to A=208, because the weakening of the G-matrix matrix elements for heavier nuclei counterbalances the effects of more intermediate states and smaller energy denominators that would otherwise enhance higher-order contributions.

## Method
The authors calculated all non-folded valence-linked diagrams through third order in the G-matrix, using the Bonn A one-boson-exchange potential, for model spaces corresponding to closed-shell cores at A=4, A=16, A=40, A=100, and A=208. They then summed higher-order folded diagrams to infinite order using the folded-diagram method, with intermediate state excitations limited to 2-hbar-omega in oscillator energy, and evaluated mean values of matrix elements weighted by (2J+1)(2T+1) degeneracy factors.

## Key numbers
- Harmonic oscillator energies used: 18.4 MeV for A=4, 13.9 MeV for A=16, 11.0 MeV for A=40, 8.5 MeV for A=100, and 6.9 MeV for A=208, derived from hbar-Omega = 45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3}
- For A=4 (He-4 core), diagonal mean value of V^(1) (bare G-matrix) = -3.42 MeV for all isospins, -5.98 MeV for T=0, -2.23 MeV for T=1
- For A=16 (O-16 core), diagonal mean value of V^(1) = -1.39 MeV for all isospins, -2.67 MeV for T=0, -0.88 MeV for T=1
- For A=40 (Ca-40 core), diagonal mean value of V^(1) = -0.68 MeV for all isospins, -1.35 MeV for T=0, -0.43 MeV for T=1
- For A=100 (Sn-100 core), diagonal mean value of V^(1) = -0.28 MeV for all isospins, -0.57 MeV for T=0, -0.17 MeV for T=1
- For A=208 (Pb-208), diagonal mean value of V^(1) = -0.09 MeV for proton-proton model space, -0.06 MeV for neutron-neutron model space
- Ratio of V^(2) to V^(1) for T=1: 0.66 for A=4, 0.49 for A=16, 0.42 for A=40, 0.49 for A=100, 0.44 for pp and 0.50 for nn in A=208
- Ratio of V^(3) to V^(2) for T=1: 1.11 for A=4, 1.02 for A=16, 0.94 for A=40, 0.94 for A=100, 1.00 for both pp and nn in A=208

## Context
This work extends the long-standing investigation of perturbative effective interactions initiated by Barrett and Kirson (1970), who showed that third-order contributions were substantial and questioned convergence, and by Schucan and Weidenmuller (1972), who pointed out ultimate divergence from intruder states. The study systematically compares convergence across mass regions from A=4 to A=208, demonstrating that the methods previously applied mainly to O-16 and Ca-40 can be reliably extended to heavy nuclei such as Sn-132 and Pb-208, as used in recent calculations by Hjorth-Jensen, Engeland, Holt, and Osnes (1995).
