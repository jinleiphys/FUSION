# A Consistent Meson-Field-Theoretical Description of PP-Bremsstrahlung

> A parameter-free and relativistic extension of the RuhrPot meson-baryon model is used to define the dominant isoscalar meson-exchange currents. We compute pp-bremsstrahlung observables below the $π-$production threshold using a relativistic hadronic current density that includes impulse, wave function re-orthonormalization, meson-recoil, $\bar{\rm N}$N creation and annihilation, $ρπγ$ + $ωπγ$ + $ρηγ$ + $ωηγ$ vector-meson decay and N$Δγ(π,ρ)$ exchange currents. We obtain a good description of the available data. The N$Δγ(π)$ current is shown to dominate the large two-body contributions and closed-form expressions for various non-relativistic approximations are analyzed. An experimental sensitivity to the admixture of pseudo-scalar and pseudo-vector admixture of the NN$π$ interaction is demonstrated. We examine the Lorentz invariance of the NN$\rightleftharpoons$NN $t$-matrices and show a dominantly pseudo-vector NN$π$ coupling renders impulse approximation calculations without boost operators to be essentially exact. Conversely, a similar analysis of the $Δ{\rm N}\rightleftharpoons$NN transitions shows that boost operators and the two-body $NΔγ$ wave function re-orthonormalization meson-recoil currents are required in NN, $Δ$N and $ΔΔ$ coupled channel $t$-matrix applications. The need for additional data is stressed.

# A Consistent Meson-Field-Theoretical Description of PP-Bremsstrahlung

## Key claim
The RuhrPot meson-baryon model, extended consistently to include relativistic meson-exchange currents, provides a parameter-free description of pp-bremsstrahlung observables below the pion-production threshold that agrees well with available data, but only if a dominantly pseudo-vector NNpi coupling is assumed; a large pseudo-scalar admixture is ruled out. The authors stress that additional data are needed to further constrain the theory.

## Method
The authors computed pp-bremsstrahlung observables using a relativistic hadronic current density derived from the RuhrPot effective meson field theory, including impulse current, wave function re-orthonormalization, meson-recoil, NbarN creation and annihilation, vector-meson decay (rho-pi-gamma, omega-pi-gamma, rho-eta-gamma, omega-eta-gamma), and N-Delta-gamma (pi and rho exchange) currents. They employed a momentum-space t-matrix expansion of two-nucleon wave functions and performed calculations below the pion-production threshold, comparing their results with data from the 1990 TRIUMF pp-bremsstrahlung experiment.

## Key numbers
- The NNpi coupling constant used is g_NNpi = 12.922, with the N-Delta-pi coupling constant g_Ndeltapi = 28.85, consistent with Chew-Low and strong-coupling models.
- The vector-meson decay coupling constants adopted are g_rhopigamma = 0.53, g_omegapigamma = 2.58, g_rhoetagamma = 1.39, and g_omegaetagamma = 0.15.
- The N-Delta-rho coupling constant is fixed at g_Ndeltarho = 20.73, and the N-Delta magnetic transition moment is mu_NDelta = 3.993, determined from SU(6) and vector-meson dominance.
- The isoscalar and isovector anomalous magnetic moments used are kappa_is = -0.12 and kappa_iv = 3.706.
- Meson masses adopted include: pi (136.5 MeV), eta (548.8 MeV), rho (776.9 MeV), omega (782.4 MeV), delta (983.0 MeV), and epsilon (975.0 MeV).
- Form factor regularization scales are Lambda_1 = 0.8 GeV for all mesons except the epsilon, which requires Lambda_1 = 0.6 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the RuhrPot meson-baryon model, previously applied to NN scattering (RuhrPot, 1990s) and the triton binding energy (DH95), to pp-bremsstrahlung for the first time with full consistency between form factors, the NN interaction, and exchange currents. It contrasts with earlier calculations by Brown (1969, 1971) and more recent works (HF86, VH91, VH92, HF93a, HF93b, JE93, JE94, KA93, MJ93a, MJ93, MJ94, JO94) that used inconsistent combinations of interactions or introduced free parameters for double-counting subtraction. The results enable future tests of off-shell t-matrix models and coupled-channel treatments of NN, DeltaN, and DeltaDelta transitions, provided boost operators and relativistic exchange currents are properly included.
