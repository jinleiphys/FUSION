# Generalized parton distributions in the deuteron

> We introduce generalized quark and gluon distributions in the deuteron, which can be measured in exclusive processes like deeply virtual Compton scattering and meson electroproduction. We discuss the basic properties of these distributions, and point out how they probe the interplay of nucleon and parton degrees of freedom in the deuteron wave function.

## Key claim

The paper introduces generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for the deuteron, extending the nucleon GPD framework to a spin-1 target, and argues that these distributions can be accessed in hard exclusive processes such as deeply virtual Compton scattering and meson electroproduction, provided that the invariant momentum transfer \(Q^2\) is large and factorization holds.

## Method

The authors define the deuteron GPDs through non-diagonal matrix elements of quark-antiquark and gluon operators on the light cone, decomposing them into five vector (\(H_i\)) and four axial-vector (\(\tilde{H}_i\)) distributions using tensor structures constrained by parity and time-reversal invariance. They derive sum rules connecting the first \(x\)-moments of the GPDs to the deuteron elastic form factors (\(G_1, G_2, G_3, \tilde{G}_1, \tilde{G}_2\)), and show that the forward limit recovers the usual parton distributions \(F_1, b_1, g_1\) for a spin-1 target. The paper also presents helicity amplitudes for quark and gluon scattering, and discusses the phenomenology of exclusive processes, including the role of the deuteron’s isosinglet nature and the suppression of pion exchange.

## Key numbers

- The deuteron mass \(M\) is used as the scale in the form factor decomposition, e.g., in the term \(P^\mu/(2M^2)\) for \(G_3\).
- The variable \(t_0 = -4 M^2 \xi^2 /(1-\xi^2)\) defines the kinematic boundary for the momentum transfer \(t\).
- The sum rule \(\int_{-1}^1 dx H_5(x,0,0) = 0\) recovers the parton model sum rule \(\int_0^1 b_1(x) = 0\) from Close and Kumano (1990).
- The GPDs vanish for \(\xi > w\), where \(w\) parameterizes the width of the deuteron light-cone wave function in longitudinal momentum fraction.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of generalized parton distributions, previously developed for nucleons by Mueller et al. (1994) and Radyushkin (1997), to the deuteron as a spin-1 target. It builds on earlier studies of deuteron parton distributions in deep inelastic scattering by Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1989), and on elastic form factor measurements by Brodsky, Ji, and Lepage (1983) and Garcon and Van Orden (2001). The paper enables future experimental studies of the interplay between nucleon and parton degrees of freedom in the deuteron, particularly at facilities like DESY and Jefferson Lab, and suggests that exclusive electroproduction on deuteron targets can probe short-distance QCD dynamics beyond the convolution model of Frankfurt and Strikman (1981).
