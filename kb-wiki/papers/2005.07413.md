# Quartet condensation induced by the isovector pairing force

> The phenomenon of quartet condensation in the ground state of an isovector pairing Hamiltonian for an even-even $N=Z$ system is investigated. For this purpose we follow the evolution of the ground state from an unperturbed regime up to a strongly interacting one in a formalism of collective pairs. These pairs are those resulting from the diagonalization of the pairing Hamiltonian in a space of two particles coupled to isospin $T=1$. The ground state is found to rapidly evolve from a product of distinct $T=0$ quartets, each one formed by two of the above pairs, to a condensate of identical quartets built only with the pair corresponding to the lowest energy. This finding establishes a link between the complicated structure of the exact ground state and the simple approximation scheme of the Quartet Condensation Model. The mechanism at the basis of this quartet condensation turns out to be the same which is responsible for the development of a pair condensate in the ground state of a like-particle pairing Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The ground state of an isovector pairing Hamiltonian for even-even N=Z systems evolves from a product of distinct T=0 quartets to a condensate of identical quartets built only with the lowest-energy collective pair, but this evolution is conditional on the increasing energy gap between the lowest pair and the remaining pairs; the overlap with the quartet condensate approaches unity only in the strong-coupling regime (g ≥ 1.25 in the like-particle case).

## Method

The authors diagonalized the isovector pairing Hamiltonian in a space of two particles coupled to isospin T=1 to obtain collective pairs, then used these pairs to construct basis states for the full N=Z system. They calculated the overlaps between the exact ground state and two trial states: one composed of distinct quartets built from the lowest three collective pairs, and one composed of a condensate of identical quartets built only from the lowest pair. The energies of the Richardson pairs defining the exact ground state were tracked as a function of the pairing strength g.

## Key numbers

- For a system of 6 protons and 6 neutrons over 6 levels, the weak-coupling region where the exact ground state overlaps with a product of distinct quartets is approximately 0 ≤ g ≤ 0.2 (in units of level spacing d).
- The overlap between the exact ground state and the quartet condensate state |Θ_s⟩ approaches unity in the strong-coupling regime (g ≥ 1.25 d for the analogous like-particle system).
- The relative error in the QCM ground state correlation energy and the deviation from unity of the overlap between exact and QCM ground states are both well below 1% throughout the entire range of g studied.
- For the like-particle system with Ω = 2N = 12 particles over 12 equispaced levels, the weak-coupling region where the exact ground state overlaps with a product of distinct pairs is approximately 0 ≤ g ≤ 0.25 d.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of exact T=0 seniority-zero eigenstates of the isovector pairing Hamiltonian (Sambataro and Sandulescu, 2019) and establishes a direct link between that exact solution and the simpler Quartet Condensation Model (QCM) developed by Sandulescu and collaborators (2012, 2013). The mechanism of quartet condensation is shown to be analogous to the pair condensation phenomenon in like-particle pairing, first studied by Bayman (1960) with the PBCS approximation and by Richardson (1963) with the exact solution. This enables the use of the computationally simpler QCM wave function for describing ground states of N=Z nuclei with isovector pairing, with accuracy that can be further improved by releasing constraints to allow products of distinct quartets.
