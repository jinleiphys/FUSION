# Why fluctuations of conserved charges in the confining regime above $T_{ch}$ behave as if the quarks were free?

> Some cumulants of the fluctuations of conserved charges soon above the chiral crossover behave as if the quarks were free. This was taken by many as evidence of deconfinement. At the same temperatures the mesonic correlators reveal the chiral spin and SU(4) symmetries, indicating that the propagating degrees of freedom are massless quarks connected into color singlets by the chromoelectric confining string. These correlators are qualitatively different from the free quark gas. Here we clarify the reason for the difference. The conserved quark number densities do not propagate in time but do propagate in spatial directions. The mesonic propagators calculated in full QCD differ radically from the free quark loop (quark gas) above T_ch. In contrast, the quark number density spatial propagator in full QCD at T > 220 MeV is very close to the free quark loop. In other words, the conserved charges do not see confinement, in contrast to the mesonic correlators. This is consistent with the well understood quark-hadron duality at T=0 in e^+e^- -> hadrons, where at invariant masses above 2 GeV the cross-section in the confining regime is represented by the free quark loop plus small perturbative corrections. All these features above T_ch but below the deconfinement temperature T_d can be combined within the following microscopic picture of the stringy fluid matter. It is a medium of the overlapping strongly interacting color singlet clusters. The quark interchanges between the clusters, required by Paili principle, make the quarks quasifree, which is reflected in fluctuations of conserved charges.

# Why fluctuations of conserved charges in the confining regime above \(T_{ch}\) behave as if the quarks were free?

## Key claim

The fluctuations of conserved charges above the chiral crossover temperature \(T_{ch}\) appear free-quark-like not because the system is deconfined, but because this observable is insensitive to the confining infrared contributions, analogous to quark-hadron duality at \(T=0\) in \(e^+e^- \rightarrow \text{hadrons}\). The conserved charge density spatial propagator in full QCD at \(T \geq 220\) MeV is very close to the free quark loop, while mesonic correlators at the same temperatures remain qualitatively different from the free quark gas and reveal confining dynamics.

## Method

The author compares spatial correlation functions of isovector quark bilinears computed in \(N_F=2\) full QCD at physical quark masses with those obtained from free noninteracting quarks on the same lattice, using a chirally symmetric Dirac operator. The analysis distinguishes between mesonic correlators (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, tensor channels) and conserved charge density correlators (the \(\gamma_4\) component), examining their behavior across temperatures above the chiral crossover \(T_{ch} \sim 155\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The chiral restoration temperature is \(T_{ch} \sim 155\) MeV.
- The quark number density spatial propagator in full QCD at \(T \geq 220\) MeV is very close to the free quark loop.
- The deconfinement temperature \(T_d\) is estimated at \(\sim 300\) MeV, consistent with the Hagedorn temperature \(T_H \sim 300\) MeV from string descriptions.
- The Stefan-Boltzmann \(\sim T^4\) behavior of pressure, energy density, and entropy density sets in at much larger temperatures \(T > 400\)–\(500\) MeV.
- At \(T=0\), the inclusive \(e^+e^- \rightarrow \text{hadrons}\) cross-section above invariant mass 2 GeV is accurately reproduced by a free quark loop plus small perturbative corrections.
- Full QCD correlators from the \(E_3\) multiplet (conserved charge densities) at \(T=193\) MeV already show insignificant difference from free quark gas correlators.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous demonstration (Glozman, 2026) that fluctuations of conserved charges above \(T_{ch}\) scale as \(N_c^1\) and are not directly sensitive to deconfined gluons. It contradicts the interpretation (Asakawa, 2000; Jeon, 2000; Ejiri, 2005) that free-quark-like fluctuations signal deconfinement just above \(T_{ch}\). The paper reconciles these observations with the chiral spin and \(SU(4)\) symmetries found in mesonic propagators (Glozman, 2018; 2020; Chiu, 2020) and with the three-regime picture of the QCD phase diagram (hadron gas below \(T_{ch}\), stringy fluid at \(T_{ch} < T < 3T_{ch}\), quark-gluon plasma at higher temperatures). The microscopic picture of overlapping color-singlet clusters with quark interchanges required by the Pauli principle provides a consistent framework for all available lattice data.
