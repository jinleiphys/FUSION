# Non-Markovian effects in quantum system: an exact stochastic mean-field treatment

> A quantum Monte-Carlo is proposed to describe fusion/fission processes when fluctuation and dissipation, with memory effects, are important. The new theory is illustrated for systems with inverted harmonic potentials coupled to a heat-bath.

# Non-Markovian effects in quantum system: an exact stochastic mean-field treatment

## Key claim
The proposed quantum Monte-Carlo method exactly reproduces the dynamics of an open quantum system with memory effects, but the numerical results shown for the inverted harmonic oscillator (the first step toward fusion/fission applications) are limited to a specific Caldeira-Leggett model with a Drude spectral density, and the method's accuracy depends on sufficient trajectory sampling.

## Method
The authors replace the exact evolution of the system density matrix with a stochastic integro-differential master equation containing Gaussian noises with zero mean and specified variances. They solve this equation by stochastically evolving the first and second moments of position and momentum for a system coupled to a harmonic oscillator bath via a Caldeira-Leggett model with a Drude spectral density. The method is tested against exact solutions for both harmonic and inverted harmonic potentials, and compared to second-order and fourth-order Time ConvolutionLess (TCL) master equation approaches.

## Key numbers
- For the harmonic case test, parameters were set to \(k_b T = \hbar \omega_0\), \(\eta = 0.5 \hbar \omega_0\), \(\hbar \Omega = 5 \hbar \omega_0\), and \(\hbar \omega_0 = 14\) MeV.
- The passing probability error \(\Delta P/P\) was computed as a function of \(\Delta E\) (total system energy minus barrier height) for three temperatures: \(T = 5\hbar \omega\), \(\hbar \omega\), and \(0.1\hbar \omega\).
- At low \(\Delta E\) (below the barrier), the second-order TCL method (TCL2) is a "rather poor approximation," while the Markovian limit gives better results than TCL2 in that regime.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previously proposed stochastic mean-field method for open quantum systems to treat non-Markovian (memory) effects relevant to nuclear fusion and fission dynamics. It directly compares the quantum Monte-Carlo approach to the Time ConvolutionLess (TCL) expansion method, which is the standard tool for non-Markovian effects in nuclear physics. The inverted harmonic oscillator application is the first step toward realistic fusion/fission calculations, and the method enables exact treatment of fluctuation and dissipation with memory effects that are important near the Coulomb barrier.
