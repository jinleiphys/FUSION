# Solar Neutrinos Before and After Neutrino 2004

> We compare, using a three neutrino analysis, the allowed neutrino oscillation parameters and solar neutrino fluxes determined by the experimental data available Before and After Neutrino 2004. New data available after Neutrino2004 include refined KamLAND and gallium measurements. We use six different approaches to analyzing the KamLAND data. We present detailed results using all the available neutrino and anti-neutrino data for Delta m^2_{12}, tan^2 theta_{12}, sin^2 theta_{13}, and sin^2 eta (sterile fraction). Using the same complete data sets, we also present Before and After determinations of all the solar neutrino fluxes, which are treated as free parameters, an upper limit to the luminosity fraction associated with CNO neutrinos, and the predicted rate for a 7Be solar neutrino experiment. The 1 sigma (3 sigma) allowed range of Delta m^2_{21} = (8.2 +- 0.3) (^+1.0_-0.8)times 10^{-5} eV^2 is decreased by a factor of 1.7 (5), but the allowed ranges of all other neutrino oscillation parameters and neutrino fluxes are not significantly changed. Maximal mixing is disfavored at 5.8 sigma and the bound on the mixing angle theta_{13} is slightly improved to sin^2 theta_{13}<0.048 at 3 sigma. The predicted rate in a 7Be neutrino-electron scattering experiment is (0.665 +-0.015) of the rate implied by the BP04 solar model in the absence of neutrino oscillations. The corresponding predictions for p-p and pep experiments are, respectively, 0.707 {+0.011}{-0.013} and 0.644 {+0.011}{-0.013}. We derive upper limits to CPT violation in the weak sector by comparing reactor anti-neutrino oscillation parameters with neutrino oscillation parameters. We also show that the recent data disfavor at 91 % CL a proposed non-standard interaction description of solar neutrino oscillations.

## Key claim

The new KamLAND and gallium data released at Neutrino 2004 improve the precision of the solar mass-squared difference Δm²₂₁ by a factor of 1.7 (1σ) to 5 (3σ), but do not significantly change the allowed ranges of other neutrino oscillation parameters or solar neutrino fluxes, contrary to expectations that new data might reveal non-standard physics.

## Method

The authors performed a three-neutrino oscillation analysis comparing experimental data available before and after the Neutrino 2004 conference, using six different approaches to analyze the KamLAND reactor antineutrino energy spectrum (varying binning schemes, Poisson vs. Gaussian statistics, and normalization treatments). Solar neutrino data from chlorine, gallium (SAGE, GALLEX/GNO), Super-Kamiokande, and SNO (pure D₂O and salt phases) were analyzed with all solar neutrino fluxes treated as free parameters subject only to the luminosity constraint, and the results were marginalized over all other parameters.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-neutrino analyses (Post-KamLAND, 2003; CNO paper; Roadmap paper) by incorporating the refined KamLAND spectrum showing spectral distortion and the revised gallium average rate (68.1 ± 3.75 SNU) released at Neutrino 2004. The results confirm the LMA solution and rule out the non-standard interaction description proposed by Friedland, Lunardini, and Pena-Garay (2004) at 91% CL, while also establishing upper limits on CPT violation by comparing reactor antineutrino and solar neutrino oscillation parameters. The analysis enables more precise predictions for future ⁷Be solar neutrino experiments and tighter constraints on sterile neutrino admixtures and CNO neutrino luminosity.
