# Spectroscopy of the hidden-charm $[qc][\bar q \bar c]$ and $[sc][\bar s \bar c]$ tetraquarks

> We calculate the spectrum of $q\bar q c \bar c$ and $s\bar s c \bar c$ tetraquarks, where $q$, $s$ and $c$ stand for light ($u,d$), strange and charm quarks, respectively, in a relativized diquark model, characterized by one-gluon-exchange (OGE) plus confining potential. In the diquark model, a $q\bar q c \bar c$ ($s\bar s c \bar c$) tetraquark configuration is made up of a heavy-light diquark, $q c$ ($sc$), and anti-diquark, $\bar q \bar c$ ($\bar s \bar c$). According to our results, 13 charmonium-like observed states can be accommodated in the tetraquark picture, both in the hidden-charm ($q\bar q c \bar c$) and hidden-charm hidden-strange ($s\bar s c \bar c$) sectors.

# Spectroscopy of the hidden-charm $[qc][\bar q \bar c]$ and $[sc][\bar s \bar c]$ tetraquarks

## Key claim
The authors claim that 13 observed charmonium-like exotic states can be accommodated within a tetraquark picture using a relativized diquark model, but the assignments are tentative and model-dependent, with the model parameters partly fitted to the same experimental data they aim to reproduce.

## Method
The authors compute the tetraquark spectrum in a two-step process: first, diquark masses are obtained by solving the Schrodinger equation with a relativized quark-quark potential based on one-gluon-exchange (OGE) plus confining interactions; second, the tetraquark spectrum is calculated by solving the eigenvalue problem of a relativized diquark-antidiquark Hamiltonian using a numerical variational procedure with harmonic oscillator trial wave functions. Only color-triplet diquarks in the $\bar{\bf 3}_{\rm c}$ representation are considered, as color-sextet diquarks are expected to be higher in energy or unbound.

## Context
This work extends the diquark-antidiquark tetraquark approach previously applied to fully-heavy tetraquarks by Anwar (2017) and to hidden-charm tetraquarks by Maiani (2004) and Ebert (2008), using a relativized Hamiltonian based on the Godfrey-Isgur model (Godfrey 1985). The results compete with molecular model interpretations (e.g., Tornqvist (1993), Swanson (2003), Guo (2013)) and hadro-charmonium models (Voloshin (2013)) for the same $XYZ$ states. The next step identified by the authors is an analysis of ground-state energies and dominant decay modes, including estimates of total decay widths and production cross-sections.
