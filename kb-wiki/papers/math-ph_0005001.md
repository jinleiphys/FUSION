# Classical phase space structure induced by spontaneous symmetry breaking

> The collective dynamics of a many-body system is described as a special case of low-energy quantum dynamics, occurring when the ground state breaks a continuous symmetry of the Hamiltonian. This approach is applied to the spontaneous breaking of the rotational symmetry of a nuclear Hamiltonian. It is shown that the excitation operator of the isovector low-lying angular oscillations in deformed nuclei is a linear combination between angular momentum operators, which generate static rotations, and "angle" operators, which generate the transition to a rotating frame.

# Classical Phase Space Structure Induced by Spontaneous Symmetry Breaking

## Key claim
The paper argues that collective dynamics in many-body systems arises as a special case of low-energy quantum dynamics when the ground state breaks a continuous symmetry of the Hamiltonian, and that the excitation operator for isovector low-lying angular oscillations in deformed nuclei is a linear combination of angular momentum operators and "angle" operators that generate transitions to a rotating frame.

## Method
The author constructs a symplectic manifold of trial wavefunctions (Hartree-Fock or Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov states) and identifies the critical submanifold Q generated by the symmetry group action on a symmetry-breaking ground state. When Q is isotropic, the tangent space decomposition yields a classical phase-space structure with collective coordinates and canonically conjugate momenta. The method is applied to a nuclear Hamiltonian with a spherical oscillator term and quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, using cranked anisotropic oscillator eigenstates as trial functions, and extended to separate proton and neutron manifolds for treating scissors vibrations.

## Key numbers
- The angle operator parameter λ satisfies tan 2λ = 2ω / (ω₀ η), where ω is the cranking frequency, ω₀² = (ω₂² + ω₃²)/2, and η = (ω₂² − ω₃²)/(2ω₀²)
- The deformation parameters satisfy sinh 2θₖ = ω₀(1 − ωₖ²/ω₀²)/(2Ωₖ), with Ω₁ = ω₁ and Ω₂,₃² = (ω₀ + ε₂,₃)² − (ω₀η/2)²
- The energy shift parameters are ε₂ = −ε₃ = ω₀η/(2 cos 2λ)

## Context
This work extends the analogy between the Poincaré group of space-time and the CM(3) group of nuclear collective coordinates, as previously discussed by Rowe (1970) and others. The geometrical construction of collective phase space from symmetry breaking generalizes the standard cranking model of nuclear rotation (Rowe and Spring, 1970) and provides a framework for treating the low-lying isovector "scissors" vibrations in deformed nuclei, which are relevant to nuclear magnetism. The approach enables a microscopic description of these modes using separate proton and neutron manifolds with isovector and isoscalar quadrupole-quadrupole interactions.
