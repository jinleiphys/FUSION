# The Delta-Delta Intermediate State in 1S0 Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering From Effective Field Theory

> We examine the role of the Delta-Delta intermediate state in low energy NN scattering using effective field theory. Theories both with and without pions are discussed. They are regulated with dimensional regularization and MSbar subtraction. We find that the leading effects of the Delta-Delta state can be absorbed by a redefinition of the contact terms in a theory with nucleons only. It does not remove the requirement of a higher dimension operator to reproduce data out to moderate momentum. The explicit decoupling of the Delta-Delta state is shown for the theory without pions.

## Key claim

The leading effects of the Delta-Delta intermediate state in low-energy 1S0 nucleon-nucleon scattering can be absorbed into a redefinition of the four-nucleon contact terms, but the Delta-Delta state does not remove the need for a higher-dimension operator to reproduce data out to moderate momentum; the explicit decoupling of the Delta-Delta state is shown for the theory without pions.

## Method

The computation was performed at lowest order in an effective field theory involving local four-fermion operators and one-pion exchange, using dimensional regularization with MSbar subtraction. The scattering amplitude was computed by summing bubble chains of contact interactions and, when pions were included, by solving the coupled-channel Schrodinger equation for the NN and Delta-Delta wavefunctions with a Yukawa potential. The three unknown coefficients of the local four-baryon contact terms (C^{NN}, C^{Delta N}, C^{Delta Delta}) were fit to reproduce the scattering length and, in some cases, the effective range.

## Key numbers

- The experimental scattering length in the 1S0 channel is a_0 = -23.7 fm.
- The experimental effective range is r_eff = 2.73 fm.
- In the theory without pions, for C^{NN} = C^{Delta N} = C^{Delta Delta} = C_0, fitting to the scattering length gives C_0 ~ +1/(234 MeV)^2, and the predicted effective range is r_eff ~ -0.26 fm.
- In the theory without pions, for C^{NN} = 1/2 C^{Delta N} = 1/4 C^{Delta Delta} = C_0, fitting to the scattering length gives C_0 ~ +1/(470 MeV)^2, and the predicted effective range is r_eff ~ -1.05 fm.
- In the theory with pions, for C^{NN} = C^{Delta N} = C^{Delta Delta} = C_0, fitting to the scattering length gives C_0 = -1/(106.2 MeV)^2, and the predicted effective range is r_eff = 4.3 fm.
- In the theory with pions, for parameters that reproduce both the scattering length and effective range (C^{Delta Delta}/1.17 = C^{Delta N}/1.17 = C^{NN} = -1/(110.995 MeV)^2), the phase shift is not well reproduced over a larger momentum range.
- The scale of the contact terms in the theory with pions is ~100 MeV.
- The scale sqrt(2 M_N (M_Delta - M_N)) sets the scale for coefficients in the theory without pions when Deltas are included.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering pioneered by Weinberg (1990-1992) and the subsequent calculations including pions by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996). It specifically examines whether including the Delta-Delta intermediate state can improve the power counting and remove the need for fine-tuned contact terms. The paper finds that the Delta-Delta state effectively decouples and does not resolve the need for higher-dimension operators, suggesting that alternative approaches such as the inclusion of an explicit dibaryon field (Kaplan, 1996) may be more promising for recovering reasonable power counting.
