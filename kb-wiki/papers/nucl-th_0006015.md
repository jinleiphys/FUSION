# Relativistic Contributions to Deuteron Photodisintegration in the Bethe-Salpeter Formalism

> In plane wave one-body approximation the reaction of deuteron photodisintegration is considered in the framework of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for two-nucleon system. Results are obtained for deuteron vertex function, which is the solution of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation with a multi-rank separable interaction kernel, with a given analytical form. A comparison is presented with predictions of non-relativistic, quasipotential approaches and the equal time approximation. It is shown that important contributions come from the boost in the arguments of the initial state vertex function and the boost on the relative energy in the one-particle propagator due to recoil.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that fully relativistic treatment of deuteron photodisintegration within the Bethe-Salpeter formalism yields significant corrections to the differential cross section compared to non-relativistic and quasipotential approaches, with the most important contributions arising from the boost transformation of the deuteron vertex function and the boost on the relative energy in the one-particle propagator due to recoil, but only within the plane-wave one-body approximation that neglects final-state interactions.

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation for the deuteron bound state using a multi-rank separable interaction kernel (rank three, based on a relativistic generalization of the Graz-II potential) to obtain the deuteron vertex function in Minkowski space. They compute the differential cross section for the reaction γ + d → p + n in the plane-wave one-body approximation, where the photon couples to a single nucleon and final-state interactions between the outgoing nucleons are dropped, and compare results from the full Bethe-Salpeter formalism with those from the Blankenbecler-Sugar quasipotential reduction and the equal-time approximation.

## Key numbers

- The separable interaction kernel parameters are chosen to reproduce the ³S₁–³D₁ NN scattering phase shifts up to a laboratory energy of E_Lab = 500 MeV
- The parameter β₁₁ = 231 MeV sets the energy scale up to which the T-matrix satisfies exact two-body unitarity, corresponding to nucleon kinetic energies E_Lab = (2/m)β₁₁(m + β₁₁/4) ≈ 500 MeV
- The ³D₁⁺-state probability P₂ is set to 4% and 6% in different parameterizations of the interaction kernel
- The photon energy range considered for comparison of relativistic effects is 0.1–0.7 GeV for the separate effect of the pair current term

## Context

This work extends the Bethe-Salpeter formalism approach to deuteron photodisintegration, which had previously been applied to elastic electron-deuteron scattering (RT1, 1990s), elastic pd-backward scattering (RT7, 1990s), and inclusive quasi-elastic electron-deuteron scattering (UmnK, Ciofi degli Atti, 1990s), but not to the non-diagonal matrix element of photodisintegration. The paper contrasts its fully relativistic results with non-relativistic models, the Logunov-Tavkhelidze and Blankenbecler-Sugar quasipotential approaches (Logunov and Tavkhelidze, 1963; Blankenbecler and Sugar, 1966), and the equal-time approximation (Hummel and Tjon, 1990; Wallace, 1991). The neglect of final-state interactions is acknowledged as a shortcoming, with planned future work to include rescattering from J=0 (¹S₀) and J=1 (³S₁–³D₁) channels.
