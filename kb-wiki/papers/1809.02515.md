# Total hadronic cross sections at high energies in holographic QCD

> We present our analysis on high energy hadron-hadron scattering in the framework of the holographic QCD. Combining the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan Pomeron exchange kernel and gravitational form factors of the involved hadrons which are obtained by using the bottom-up AdS/QCD models in the five-dimensional AdS space, we calculate the total cross sections at high energies. We show that our calculations for the nucleon-nucleon scattering agree with the experimental data including the recent ones taken by the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC. The present framework is applicable to any high energy process, in which the strong interaction can be approximated by the Pomeron exchange. We present the results for the pion-nucleon and pion-pion scattering as examples, which can be obtained without any additional parameter because all the adjustable parameters are fixed via the analysis on the nucleon-nucleon scattering. The resulting total cross section ratios are $σ_{tot}^{πN} / σ_{tot}^{N N} = 0.63$ and $σ_{tot}^{ππ} / σ_{tot}^{N N} = 0.45$, respectively.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel, combined with gravitational form factors from bottom-up AdS/QCD models, can reproduce total hadronic cross sections for nucleon-nucleon scattering including recent LHC data, but only after including a confinement-modified kernel and only in the energy range \(10^2 < \sqrt{s} < 10^5\) GeV; the agreement with the COMPETE empirical fit deviates substantially at \(\sqrt{s} > 10\) TeV, which the authors attribute to a possible lack of saturation effects.

## Method

The authors combine the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan (BPST) Pomeron exchange kernel, modified to include confinement effects via a cutoff in the fifth-dimensional AdS coordinate, with gravitational form factors of the hadrons obtained from bottom-up hard-wall AdS/QCD models. The total cross section is computed using the eikonal approximation and the optical theorem, integrating over impact parameter and the AdS bulk coordinates \(z\) and \(z'\), with the density distributions of nucleons and pions derived from solutions to the five-dimensional Dirac equation and the AdS/QCD model of mesons, respectively. The two adjustable parameters \(g_0^2\) and \(\rho\) in the BPST kernel are fixed by fitting to nucleon-nucleon scattering data, after which predictions for pion-nucleon and pion-pion scattering require no additional parameters.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit parameters for the BPST kernel: \(g_0^2 = 6.27 \times 10^2\) and \(\rho = 0.824\).
- Nucleon cutoff parameter: \(z_0^N = 1 / (245\ \text{MeV})\), with nucleon mass \(m_N = (m_p + m_n)/2\).
- Pion cutoff parameter: \(z_0^\pi = 1/(322\ \text{MeV})\), fixed by the \(\rho\) meson mass \(m_\rho\).
- Predicted total cross section ratio: \(\sigma_{tot}^{\pi N} / \sigma_{tot}^{N N} = 0.63\).
- Predicted total cross section ratio: \(\sigma_{tot}^{\pi \pi} / \sigma_{tot}^{N N} = 0.45\).
- The analysis covers the energy range \(10^2 < \sqrt{s} < 10^5\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel, previously used to describe deep inelastic scattering (DIS) structure functions at small Bjorken \(x\) (Brower et al., 2007; Watanabe et al., 2012), to hadron-hadron scattering. It contrasts with the soft Pomeron approach of Donnachie and Landshoff (1992), whose extracted ratio \(\sigma_{tot}^{\pi N} / \sigma_{tot}^{N N} = 0.63\) agrees with the present prediction. The framework enables predictions for any high-energy process dominated by Pomeron exchange, such as deeply virtual Compton scattering or vector meson production, and the observed deviation from the COMPETE empirical fit at \(\sqrt{s} > 10\) TeV suggests that including saturation effects may be necessary.
