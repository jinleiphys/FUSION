# On the spin-observables in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction

> Spin-observables in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction is discussed. This work is complementary to the earlier works on this topic. Here, the reaction amplitude is expressed in Pauli-spin basis which allows to calculate all the observables straightforwardly. We make use of the fact that the underlying reflection symmetry about the reaction plane can be most conveniently and easily exploited in this basis to help finding the non-vanishing and independent observables in this reaction. The important issue of complete experiments is reviewed. By expressing the reaction amplitude in Pauli-spin basis, many sets of eight observables - distinct from those found in earlier works from the amplitude in transversity basis - capable of determining the reaction amplitude up to an overall phase are found. It is also shown that some of the combinations of the spin observables are suited for studying certain aspects of the reaction dynamics. We, then, carry out a (strictly) model-independent partial-wave analysis, in particular, of the peculiar angular behavior of the beam asymmetry observed in eta' photoproduction very close to threshold [P. Levi Sandri et al. 2015 Eur. Phys. J. A 51, 77]. This work should be useful, especially, for newcomers in the field of baryon spectroscopy, where the photoproduction reactions are a major tool for probing the baryon spectra.

## Key claim

The paper claims that expressing the pseudoscalar meson photoproduction amplitude in the Pauli-spin basis, rather than the transversity basis used in prior work, yields many new sets of eight observables capable of determining the full reaction amplitude up to an overall phase, and that this representation is particularly convenient for exploiting reflection symmetry about the reaction plane. The hedge is that these new complete-experiment sets are "distinct from those found in earlier works" and that the completeness condition from prior transversity-basis analyses holds only when the magnitudes of the relative phases differ from each other.

## Method

The author expresses the photoproduction amplitude in the Pauli-spin basis using the Chew-Goldberger-Low-Nambu (CGLN) form, then derives all single-, double-, and triple-spin observables from the Pauli-spin amplitudes using trace formulas. The reflection symmetry about the reaction plane is exploited to identify non-vanishing and independent observables. A model-independent partial-wave analysis is then applied to the beam asymmetry in eta-prime photoproduction near threshold, using the multipole decomposition of the amplitude.

## Key numbers

- The beam asymmetry Sigma (B^l) is given by (1/2)(|M_0|^2 + |M_2|^2 - |M_1|^2 - |M_3|^2), where M_m are Pauli-spin amplitudes.
- The target asymmetry T_y equals Re[M_0 M_2*] + Im[M_1 M_3*], and the recoil asymmetry R_y equals Re[M_0 M_2*] - Im[M_1 M_3*].
- The diagonal target-recoil asymmetries are: K_xx = (1/2)(|M_0|^2 + |M_1|^2 - |M_2|^2 - |M_3|^2), K_yy = (1/2)(|M_0|^2 - |M_1|^2 + |M_2|^2 - |M_3|^2), K_zz = (1/2)(|M_0|^2 - |M_1|^2 - |M_2|^2 + |M_3|^2).
- The beam asymmetry for a general linear polarization angle phi is (1/2)cos(2phi)(|M_0|^2 - |M_1|^2 + |M_2|^2 - |M_3|^2).
- The multipole expansion for F_4 contains no partial waves lower than D-wave, and F_1 contains the final S-wave.

## Context

This work extends the earlier treatments of spin observables in pseudoscalar meson photoproduction by Fasano, Tabakin, and Saghai (1992) and Chiang and Tabakin (1997), who used the transversity basis. It also revisits the complete-experiment problem addressed by Nakayama (2018), where it was shown that the completeness condition of four observables in transversity representation holds only when relative phase magnitudes differ. The model-independent partial-wave analysis of eta-prime photoproduction near threshold addresses the peculiar angular behavior of the beam asymmetry observed by Levi Sandri et al. (2015), which the Bonn-Gatchina group (2018) attributed to P13-D13 interference with a narrow D13(1900) resonance and the Mainz group (2018) attributed to S11-F15 interference with a narrow S11(1900) resonance. The work enables identification of new complete-experiment sets and provides a framework for model-independent baryon resonance extraction.
