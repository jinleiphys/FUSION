# Chiral Dynamics of the Two Λ(1405) States

> Using a chiral unitary approach for the meson--baryon interactions, we show that two octets of J^π=1/2^- baryon states, which are degenerate in the limit of exact SU(3) symmetry, and a singlet are generated dynamically. The SU(3) breaking produces the splitting of the two octets, resulting in the case of strangeness S=-1 in two poles of the scattering matrix close to the nominal Λ(1405) resonance. These poles are combinations of the singlet state and the octets. We show how actual experiments see just one effective resonance shape, but with properties which change from one reaction to another.

## Key claim

Using a chiral unitary approach for meson-baryon interactions, the authors show that the SU(3) breaking of two degenerate octets of \(J^\pi = 1/2^-\) baryon states produces two poles of the scattering matrix close to the nominal \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance, but actual experiments see only one effective resonance shape whose properties change from one reaction to another.

## Method

The authors employ a chiral unitary coupled-channel approach based on the lowest-order meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian (Weinberg-Tomozawa term) and solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation \(T = [1 - V G]^{-1} V\) with a loop function \(G\) regularized via dimensional regularization. They compute the poles of the \(T\)-matrix in the complex plane for strangeness \(S=-1\) by gradually breaking SU(3) symmetry from the symmetric limit (\(x=0\)) to physical masses (\(x=1\)), using the models of Bennhold (2003) and Oller (2000) with physical baryon and meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Pole positions for \(I=0\) from the model of Bennhold (2003): \(1390 + 66i\) MeV, \(1426 + 16i\) MeV, and \(1680 + 20i\) MeV.
- Pole positions for \(I=0\) from the model of Oller (2000): \(1379 + 27i\) MeV, \(1434 + 11i\) MeV, and \(1692 + 14i\) MeV.
- In the SU(3) symmetric limit, the singlet pole is at 1486 MeV and the octet pole at 1556 MeV (Bennhold model) or 1447 MeV and 1516 MeV (Oller model).
- The subtraction constants used in the Bennhold model are: \(a_{\bar{K}N} = -1.84\), \(a_{\pi\Sigma} = -2.00\), \(a_{\pi\Lambda} = -1.83\), \(a_{\eta\Lambda} = -2.25\), \(a_{\eta\Sigma} = -2.38\), \(a_{K\Xi} = -2.67\).
- The averaged meson decay constant used is \(f = 1.123 f_\pi\) with \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding of two poles near the \(\Lambda(1405)\) in the chiral unitary approach of Oller (2000) and in the cloudy bag model of Fink (1990), and is consistent with results from Nieves (2001) and the magnetic model. It clarifies that the \(\Lambda(1405)\) is not a pure singlet as assumed in Goity (2002) but a mixture of a singlet and two octets, and it enables future experiments to distinguish the two poles by measuring reactions such as \(\gamma p \to K^+ \Lambda(1405)\) where the \(I=1\) component produces different cross sections in various charge channels, as confirmed experimentally by Ahn (2003).
