# Light-front interpretation of Proton Generalized Polarizabilities

> We extend the recently developed formalism to extract light-front quark charge densities from nucleon form factor data to the deformations of these quark charge densities when applying an external electric field. We show that the resulting induced polarizations can be extracted from proton generalized polarizabilities. The available data for the generalized electric polarizabilitiy of the proton yield a pronounced structure in its induced polarization at large transverse distances, which will be pinned down by forthcoming high precision virtual Compton scattering experiments.

## Key claim

The paper extends the light-front formalism for extracting quark charge densities from nucleon form factors to the case of deformations induced by an external electric field, showing that the resulting induced polarizations can be obtained from proton generalized polarizabilities (GPs). The available data for the generalized electric polarizability yield a pronounced structure in the induced polarization at large transverse distances (0.5–1 fm), but this structure is conditional on the parameterization used to fit the data and will be pinned down by forthcoming high-precision virtual Compton scattering experiments.

## Method

The authors use the light-front frame to define induced polarizations from the linear response of the nucleon to a quasi-static electromagnetic field, expressed through the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) tensor at low outgoing photon energy. They extract the functions A, B, and C that describe the induced polarization for helicity-averaged and transversely polarized protons, expressed in terms of six generalized polarizabilities (GPs). The GPs are parameterized using a dispersive part from πN intermediate states (via MAID2007) plus an asymptotic part fitted to VCS data from MAMI, MIT-Bates, and JLab, with two parameterizations (GP I and GP II) for the electric GP.

## Key numbers

- Asymptotic electric GP at real photon point: P^(L1,L1)0_asy(0) = -14.37 GeV^{-3}
- Asymptotic magnetic GP at real photon point: P^(M1,M1)0_asy(0) = 21.82 GeV^{-3}
- Magnetic GP dipole parameter: Λ_β = 0.5 GeV
- Electric GP dipole parameter (both GP I and GP II): Λ_α = 0.7 GeV
- Gaussian term coefficient for electric GP (GP II only): C_α = -150 GeV^{-7}
- Pronounced structure in induced polarization at large transverse distances of 0.5–1 fm
- Q^2 values smaller than 0.1 GeV^2 where chiral effective field theory describes VCS data well

## Context

This work extends the light-front density formalism developed by Miller (2007) and Carlson (2007) for nucleon form factors to the case of induced polarizations from generalized polarizabilities, building on the GP framework of Guichon (1995) and Drechsel (1997). The results enable a spatial visualization of how quark charge densities deform under an external electromagnetic field, complementing the picture obtained from elastic form factors. Forthcoming VCS precision experiments at MAMI in the intermediate Q^2 region will test the large-distance structure seen in the induced polarization, which cannot be described by chiral effective field theory and requires dispersion relation approaches.
