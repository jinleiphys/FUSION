# Chiral Corrections to Matrix Elements of Twist-2 Operators

## Key claim
The leading non-analytic contributions of the form m_q log(m_q) to matrix elements of twist-2 operators in the nucleon and pion are computed using chiral perturbation theory, including previously omitted one-loop contributions that are related to tree-level matrix elements by chiral symmetry. The result for the nucleon non-singlet matrix element differs from prior computations (Thomas, Melnitchouk and Steffens, 2000; Detmold et al., 2001) primarily due to the inclusion of a two-pion-nucleon interaction associated with the tree-level matrix element, and also in the numerical coefficient of the g_A^2 contribution.

## Method
The authors construct the low-energy effective field theory (chiral perturbation theory) for twist-2 operators by determining their transformation properties under SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R and writing the corresponding operators in the pion-nucleon EFT. They compute one-loop diagrams for both pion and nucleon states, including vertex corrections and wavefunction renormalization, and also include contributions from Delta intermediate states using the Heavy-Baryon formulation of Jenkins and Manohar (1991).

## Key numbers
- The pion decay constant is taken as f = 132 MeV.
- The axial-vector coupling constant is g_A = 1.25.
- The N-Delta coupling is g_{NDelta} ~ 1.8, from the observed width for Delta -> N pi.
- The non-singlet nucleon matrix element receives a correction factor of [1 - (3 g_A^2 + 1) * (1 - delta^{n 1})/(8 pi^2 f^2) * m_pi^2 log(m_pi^2 / Lambda_chi^2)].
- The non-singlet pion matrix element for n-odd receives a correction factor of [1 - (1 - delta^{n 1})/(8 pi^2 f^2) * m_pi^2 log(m_pi^2 / Lambda_chi^2)].
- The Delta contribution to the nucleon matrix element involves the function J_1(Delta M, m_pi) = (m^2 - 2 Delta^2) log(m^2 / Lambda_chi^2) + 2 Delta sqrt(Delta^2 - m^2) log((Delta - sqrt(Delta^2 - m^2 + i epsilon))/(Delta + sqrt(Delta^2 - m^2 + i epsilon))), where Delta M = M_Delta - M_N.
- The singlet nucleon matrix element receives no non-analytic corrections of the form m_q log m_q from nucleon intermediate states, but does receive contributions from Delta intermediate states.

## Context
This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory to twist-2 operators relevant for deep-inelastic scattering, following the framework established by Jenkins and Manohar (1991) for heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. It corrects and extends prior calculations by Thomas, Melnitchouk and Steffens (2000) and Detmold et al. (2001), which omitted certain contributions related to chiral symmetry. The results enable model-independent extrapolation of unquenched lattice QCD calculations of nucleon matrix elements of twist-2 operators from the quark masses used on the lattice (producing pions of mass ~500 MeV) to the physical values (m_u ~ 5 MeV, m_d ~ 10 MeV). The work can be straightforwardly extended to off-forward matrix elements relevant for deeply-virtual Compton scattering.
