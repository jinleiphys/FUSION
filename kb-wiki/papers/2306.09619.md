# Early-times Yang-Mills dynamics and the characterization of strongly interacting matter with statistical learning

> In ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, a plasma of deconfined quarks and gluons is formed within $1$ fm/c of the nuclei's impact. The complex dynamics of the collision before $\approx 1$ fm/c is often described with parametric models, which affect the predictivity of calculations. In this work, we perform a systematic analysis of LHC measurements from Pb-Pb collisions, by combining an \emph{ab-initio} model of the early stage of the collisions with a hydrodynamic model of the plasma. We obtain state-of-the-art constraints on the shear and bulk viscosity of quark-gluon plasma. We mitigate the additional cost of the ab-initio initial conditions by combining Bayesian model averaging with transfer learning, allowing us to account for important theoretical uncertainties in the hydrodynamics-to-hadron transition. We show that, despite the apparent strong constraints on the shear viscosity, metrics that balance the model's predictivity with its degree of agreement with data do not prefer a temperature-dependent specific shear viscosity over a constant value. We validate the model by comparing with discriminating observables not used in the calibration, finding excellent agreement.

## Key claim

Despite obtaining state-of-the-art constraints on the shear and bulk viscosity of quark-gluon plasma from a Bayesian analysis combining an ab-initio early-stage model with hydrodynamic simulations, metrics that balance model predictivity with agreement to data (Bayes factors) do not prefer a temperature-dependent specific shear viscosity over a constant value.

## Method

The authors simulated Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using a three-stage model: the ab-initio IP-Glasma model for the early stage (color glass condensate collision evolved with classical Yang-Mills equations), followed by relativistic viscous hydrodynamics, and finally hadronic transport with the SMASH model. They performed Bayesian inference using Gaussian process emulators trained on 350 parameter sets sampled with a Maximum Projection Latin hypercube, and used transfer learning to account for uncertainty in the hydrodynamics-to-hadron transition by comparing two different models (Grad 14-moment and Chapman-Enskog) at reduced computational cost.

## Key numbers

- The Bayesian analysis used 350 sets of parameters sampled with a Maximum Projection Latin hypercube
- 20 principal components captured 90.6% of the model's information for emulation
- The Bayes factor between constant and temperature-dependent eta/s was ln B = 0.2 +/- 2.4 for the Grad model, and ln B = 1 +/- 4 for the Chapman-Enskog RTA model
- The model with constant eta/s and ab-initio initial conditions uses 8 parameters, approximately half the number used in other modern models
- The specific shear viscosity eta/s was constrained to intermediate values approximately 0.1 to 0.2 with modest temperature dependence
- At low temperature (T approximately 150 MeV), large values of specific bulk viscosity zeta/s were disfavored; significant bulk viscosity was favored between 200 and 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of heavy-ion collisions (Bernhard 2019, JETSCAPE 2020, Nijs 2020-2023) by replacing parametric early-stage models with the ab-initio IP-Glasma model (Schenke 2012), which describes nuclei as color glass condensates. The finding that temperature-dependent eta/s is not preferred by Bayes factors confirms similar results from JETSCAPE (2020). The transfer learning approach builds upon Liyanage (2022) to study model uncertainties at reduced computational cost, enabling future exploration of a broader range of theoretical uncertainties in the transport coefficients of Quantum Chromodynamics.
