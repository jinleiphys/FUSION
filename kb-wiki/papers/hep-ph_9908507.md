# Light quark distributions in the proton sea

> We use the meson cloud model to calculate $\bar{d}(x) - \bar{u}(x)$ and $ \bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)$ in the proton. We show that a modification of the symmetric, perturbative part of the light quark sea provides better agreement with the ratio $ \bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x).

## Key claim
The meson cloud model can describe the flavor asymmetry \(\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)\) in the proton, but to also match the measured ratio \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\), especially its broad maximum near \(x \approx 0.18\) and return to unity at larger \(x\), the symmetric (perturbative) part of the light quark sea must be modified to a harder distribution, motivated by its origin in gluon splitting.

## Method
The authors use a pion cloud model in which the proton wavefunction is expanded in Fock states containing a baryon (\(p\) or \(n\)) and a pion (\(\pi^0\) or \(\pi^+\)). Quark distribution functions are obtained by convoluting splitting functions (derived from the \(\pi N\) coupling constant \(g_\pi^2/4\pi = 13.6\) and an exponential cutoff with \(\Lambda = 0.83\) GeV) with the quark distributions of the pion and the bare nucleon. Two forms of the bare symmetric sea are compared: a soft parametrization \(x\bar{Q}_{\rm bare}(x)=0.11(1-x)^{15.8}\) and a harder gluonic form \(x\bar{Q}'_{\rm bare}(x)=0.0124 x^{-0.36}(1-x)^{3.8}\).

## Key numbers
- The integrated flavor asymmetry \(D = \int_0^1 (\bar{d}(x)-\bar{u}(x)) dx = 0.100\) is reproduced by choosing the cutoff constant \(\Lambda = 0.83\) GeV.
- The soft bare sea parametrization is \(x\bar{Q}_{\rm bare}(x)=0.11(1-x)^{15.8}\).
- The harder gluonic bare sea parametrization is \(x\bar{Q}'_{\rm bare}(x)=0.0124 x^{-0.36}(1-x)^{3.8}\).
- The pion valence quark distribution used is \(x q_v(x)=0.99x^{0.61}(1-x)^{1.02}\) and the pion sea quark distribution is \(x q_{\rm sea}(x)=0.2(1-x)^{5.0}\).
- The \(\pi N\) coupling constant is taken as \(g_\pi^2/4\pi = 13.6\).

## Context
This work extends meson cloud model calculations of the proton sea, which were first suggested by Thomas (1983) and later by Henley and Miller (1990), and which have been applied by several groups (e.g., Speth and collaborators, Kumano, and others) to explain the flavor asymmetry observed in NMC, NA51, E866, and HERMES experiments. The key advance is showing that the ratio \(\bar{d}(x)/\bar{u}(x)\), not just the difference, can be better described by using a harder distribution for the perturbative symmetric sea, motivated by gluon splitting. This enables future tests with forthcoming E866 data and proposed experiments, and the authors note that other symmetric contributions (e.g., from the \(\omega\) or \(\sigma\) meson) could produce similar improvement.
