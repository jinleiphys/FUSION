# Analysis of Spectroscopic Factors in 11Be and 12Be in the Nilsson Strong Coupling Limit

> Spectroscopic factors in 10Be, 11Be and 12Be, extracted from (d,p), one neutron knockout, and (p,d) reactions are interpreted within the rotational model. Assuming that the ground state and first excited state of 11Be can be associated with the 1/2[220] and 1/2[101] Nilsson levels, the strong coupling limit gives simple expressions that relate the amplitudes of these wavefunctions (in the spherical basis) with the measured cross-sections and derived spectroscopic factors. We obtain good agreement with both the measured magnetic moment of the ground state in 11Be and the reaction data.

# Analysis of Spectroscopic Factors in 11Be and 12Be in the Nilsson Strong Coupling Limit

## Key claim

Spectroscopic factors extracted from transfer and knockout reactions on 10Be, 11Be, and 12Be can be quantitatively described by the Nilsson strong coupling limit, provided the ground and first excited states of 11Be are associated with the 1/2[220] and 1/2[101] Nilsson levels and the wavefunction amplitudes are empirically adjusted to fit the combined reaction data and the measured magnetic moment of the 11Be ground state.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for spectroscopic factors in the (d,p), one-neutron knockout, and (p,d) reactions using the Nilsson strong coupling formalism, where the deformed wavefunctions are expanded in the spherical |j,l> basis with amplitudes C_{j,l}. They performed a weighted chi-squared minimization over 12 experimental constraints (relative spectroscopic factors from four reaction datasets plus the absolute magnetic moment of the 11Be ground state) to determine four unknown amplitudes: the C_{j,l} coefficients for the 1/2[220] and 1/2[101] Nilsson orbitals, plus the mixing parameters alpha and beta for the two 0+ states in 12Be.

## Key numbers

- 11Be ground state magnetic moment: mu = -1.6813(5) mu_N (used as a constraint)
- Fitted wavefunction for 1/2[220]: approximately -0.72 |s_{1/2}> - 0.09 |d_{3/2}> + 0.69 |d_{5/2}>
- Fitted wavefunction for 1/2[101]: approximately 0.68 |p_{1/2}> + 0.73 |p_{3/2}>
- Mixing parameters for 12Be 0+ states: alpha = 0.73, beta = 0.69
- 10Be(d,p)11Be: S(0+,1/2+) = 1 (normalized), S(0+,1/2-) = 0.87 +/- 0.08 experimental vs 0.88 +/- 0.06 calculated
- 12Be(-1n)11Be: S(0+,5/2+) = 0.90 +/- 0.25 experimental vs 0.91 +/- 0.16 calculated; S(0+,3/2-) = 0.85 +/- 0.25 experimental vs 0.93 +/- 0.17 calculated
- 11Be(p,d)10Be: S(1/2+,2+_1) = 1 +/- 0.38 experimental vs 0.91 +/- 0.16 calculated
- 11Be(d,p)12Be: S(1/2+,0+_2) = 2.61 +/- 1.34 experimental vs 0.90 +/- 0.19 calculated
- Rotational constant A = 0.35 MeV determined from the 2+ state in 12Be
- Decoupling parameter a = 1.92 from magnetic moment calculation, compared to a = 1.85 from rotational energy estimate

## Context

This work extends the Nilsson model interpretation of neutron-rich Be isotopes previously developed by Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and by Hamamoto and Shimoura (2008), who used deformed Woods-Saxon potentials to explain energy levels and electromagnetic data in 11Be and 12Be. The present analysis provides a simpler analytic framework that simultaneously reproduces spectroscopic factors from multiple reaction types (transfer, knockout, pickup) and the magnetic moment, whereas earlier work by Fortune (2016) had proposed a shell-model-inspired solution. The results enable predictions for future experiments on 12Be(d,p)13Be and 13B(d,3He)12Be reactions, which the authors provide as calculated spectroscopic factors in their Table II.
