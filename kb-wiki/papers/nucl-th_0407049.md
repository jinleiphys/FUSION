# Helicity asymmetries in double pion photoproduction on the proton

> Based on a prior model on double pion photoproduction on the proton, successfully tested in total cross sections and invariant mass distributions, we make a theoretical study of the angular dependence of helicity asymmetries from the interaction of circularly polarized photons with unpolarized protons. We show that this observable is sensitive to details of the internal mechanisms and, thus, represents a complementary test of the theoretical model.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the helicity asymmetry \(A\) for double pion photoproduction is highly sensitive to the internal mechanisms and interferences of the theoretical model, but the comparison with preliminary experimental data is only qualitative because the data are integrated over the CLAS acceptance while the calculations cover full phase space.

## Method

The author uses a previously published tree-level model (Roca, 2003) containing about 25 Feynman diagrams with baryon resonances (\(\Delta(1232)\), \(N^*(1440)\), \(N^*(1520)\), \(\Delta(1700)\)) and \(\rho\)-meson terms, with no free parameters fitted to double pion photoproduction data. The helicity asymmetry \(A = (d\sigma^+ - d\sigma^-)/(d\sigma^+ + d\sigma^-)\) is computed for circularly polarized photons on unpolarized protons in the helicity frame, evaluating the \(\phi\) angular dependence for the \(\gamma p \to \pi^+\pi^- p\) and \(\gamma p \to \pi^0\pi^0 p\) channels at several center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- For \(\gamma p \to \pi^+\pi^- p\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 1500\) MeV (laboratory photon energy \(E_\gamma \approx 730\) MeV), the \(\Delta\)KR term alone gives a very small asymmetry (multiplied by 100 to be visible in the plot), while the full model shows a strong \(\phi\) dependence.
- Nucleon intermediate mechanisms contribute only about 10% to the total cross section but produce a dramatic influence on the angular distribution of the helicity asymmetry.
- For \(\gamma p \to \pi^0\pi^0 p\) at \(\sqrt{s} = 1400\) MeV, removing the \(k\) diagram from the full model makes the angular distribution of the asymmetry negligible; at \(\sqrt{s} = 1500\) MeV, removing the same diagram produces a very large effect.
- The \(\pi^0\pi^0\) channel obeys the symmetry \(A(\phi) = A(\phi+\pi)\) due to identical particles, restricting the angular dependence to \(\sin(2\phi)\) and forbidding \(\sin(\phi)\), \(\sin(3\phi)\), etc.

## Context

This work extends the unpolarized model of Roca (2003), which successfully reproduced total cross sections and invariant mass distributions for double pion photoproduction on the proton and in nuclei, to polarization observables. It complements earlier studies of beam asymmetries at GRAAL (Assafiri, 2003) and spin amplitudes for the GDH sum rule (Nacher, 2003). The predictions are motivated by preliminary CLAS data (Strauch, 2003) for \(\gamma p \to \pi^+\pi^- p\) and prospects for \(\gamma p \to \pi^0\pi^0 p\) measurements at Mainz (Beck, 2003). The results enable future tests that can discriminate between theoretical models by revealing details invisible in unpolarized observables.
