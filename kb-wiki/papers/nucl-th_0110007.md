# Non-abelian anomaly and the Lagrangian for radiative muon capture

> 7 pages, in Proceedings of the conference Mesons and Light Nuclei '98, Prague-Pruhonice, Czechia, 31 August-4 September 1998, eds. J. Adam et al., World Scientific, Singapore, 1999, p.490; 1 reference updated, errors removed from Eqs.50-53

## Key claim

The authors construct an anomalous Lagrangian for the π ρ ω a₁ system that includes full electroweak interactions, extending the earlier work of Kaiser and Meissner (1997) who had dropped weak interactions and the D meson. This Lagrangian is designed to produce a radiative muon capture amplitude that satisfies gauge invariance, CVC, and PCAC constraints at tree level, but the actual construction of the amplitude is stated as "in progress."

## Method

The authors start from the general Wess-Zumino-Witten anomalous action and the 14-term homogeneous Lagrangian of Kaiser and Meissner (1997) for the π ρ ω a₁ D system. They retain only the terms L₉–L₁₄ (which contain fewer than four particles per vertex), set the D meson field to zero, and include both electromagnetic and weak external gauge fields. After forming linear combinations of these Lagrangians and removing π–a₁ mixing by redefining the axial meson field, they obtain four 3-point Lagrangians (L̅₇–L̅₁₀) that contain the strong vertices ρ ω π and ρ ω a₁ together with all additional terms from external electroweak fields.

## Key numbers

- The experimental value of the weak induced pseudoscalar constant g_P at q² ≈ 0.88 m_μ² is (9.8 ± 0.7 ± 0.3) g_A(0), which exceeds the PCAC/pion-pole prediction by a factor of about 1.5.
- The covariant WZW action contains a factor N_c/(240 π²) where N_c is the number of colours.
- The 3-point Lagrangian from the WZW action contains a factor e²/(8π² f_π).
- The external weak vector fields are related to Standard Model fields via factors 1/sin Θ_w and cos Θ_c, and the neutral field combination includes cotg(2Θ_w) and 1/sin(2Θ_w).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kaiser and Meissner (1997), who constructed an anomalous action for the π ρ ω a₁ D system but omitted weak interactions. The authors' earlier work (Smejkal, Truhlík, Khanna, 1998) derived a radiative muon capture amplitude from a Lagrangian based on SU(2)_L × SU(2)_R hidden local symmetry, which coincided with low-energy-theorem amplitudes at leading order but differed at next order. The present Lagrangian provides the framework to construct an amplitude that satisfies gauge invariance, CVC, and PCAC constraints exactly at tree level, which is needed to analyze the TRIUMF measurement of radiative muon capture that gave a g_P value exceeding the PCAC prediction.
