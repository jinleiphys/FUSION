# Coupled channel Faddeev calculations of a (Kbar-N-pi) quasibound state

> The (Kbar-N-pi) system is studied using separable interactions fitted to data available on the s-wave (Kbar-N)--(pi-Y) subsystem and the p-wave (pi-N),(pi-Y),(pi-pi) and (pi-Kbar) subsystems. Three-body (Kbar-N-pi)--(pi-Y-pi) coupled channel Faddeev equations with relativistic kinematics are solved in search for quasibound poles in the complex plane, leading to a I(JP)=1(3/2-) (Kbar-N-pi) quasibound state at M ~ (1570 +/- 10) MeV and width greater than 60 MeV near the (Kbar-N-pi) threshold. A D13 Sigma(1570) resonance of molecular structure is thus predicted.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a \(I(J^P)=1(3/2^-)\) \(\bar K N \pi\) quasibound state near the \(\bar K N \pi\) threshold, but the precise location and width depend sensitively on the poorly known shape of the \(p\)-wave \(\pi Y\) interaction, specifically the form factor parameter \(A\) for the \(\Sigma(1385)\) resonance.

## Method

The authors solved coupled-channel Faddeev equations with relativistic kinematics for the \(\bar K N \pi\)–\(\pi Y \pi\) system, using separable two-body interactions fitted to data. The \(s\)-wave \(\bar K N\)–\(\pi Y\) subsystem was modeled with a rank-one separable potential reproducing the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance, while the \(p\)-wave \(\pi N\), \(\pi \bar K\), \(\pi Y\), and \(\pi\pi\) subsystems were each fitted to phase shifts for the \(\Delta(1232)\), \(K^*(892)\), \(\Sigma(1385)\), and \(\rho(770)\) resonances, respectively. The Faddeev equations were solved by searching for poles of the Fredholm determinant in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers

- The \(\bar K N \pi\) quasibound state mass is \(M \approx (1570 \pm 10)\) MeV.
- The partial decay width (excluding meson absorption modes) is \(\Gamma_{\downarrow} \approx (60 \pm 10)\) MeV.
- For the \(\pi Y\) form factor parameter \(A = 0.25\) fm\(^2\), the energy eigenvalue relative to the \(\bar K N \pi\) threshold is \(-7.6 - i24.6\) MeV.
- For the \(\pi Y\) form factor parameter \(A = 0.45\) fm\(^2\), the energy eigenvalue relative to the \(\bar K N \pi\) threshold is \(+10.9 - i35.2\) MeV.
- The \(\pi N\) form factor has a zero \(r_0 = 1.36\) fm, while the \(\pi Y\) form factor zero \(r_0\) ranges from 1.11 fm (for \(A=0.05\) fm\(^2\)) to 1.54 fm (for \(A=0.50\) fm\(^2\)).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson-assisted dibaryons by Gal and Garcilazo (2008, 2010) to two-meson assisted baryons, replacing a baryon with a meson in the \(\bar K N \pi\) system. It contrasts with earlier Faddeev calculations for \(\bar K N \pi\) configurations by Jido et al. (2008) and others that used only \(s\)-wave two-body interactions and found higher-mass resonances; the inclusion of \(p\)-wave interactions here yields a lower-mass quasibound state. The predicted state corresponds to a \(D_{13}\) \(\Sigma\) resonance near the \(\bar K N \pi\) threshold, potentially matching the one-star \(\Sigma(1580)\) resonance listed by the Particle Data Group, and enables future experimental searches for this exotic resonance.
