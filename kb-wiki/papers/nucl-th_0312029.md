# Nucleon-nucleon interaction in the $J$-matrix inverse scattering approach and few-nucleon systems

> The nucleon-nucleon interaction is constructed by means of the $J$-matrix version of inverse scattering theory. Ambiguities of the interaction are eliminated by postulating tridiagonal and quasi-tridiagonal forms of the potential matrix in the oscillator basis in uncoupled and coupled waves, respectively. The obtained interaction is very accurate in reproducing the $NN$ scattering data and deuteron properties. The interaction is used in the no-core shell model calculations of $^3$H and $^4$He nuclei. The resulting binding energies of $^3$H and $^4$He are very close to experimental values.

## Key claim

The nucleon-nucleon interaction constructed via the J-matrix inverse scattering approach, with the ambiguity resolved by postulating a tridiagonal (or quasi-tridiagonal) potential matrix in the oscillator basis, reproduces NN scattering data and deuteron properties accurately and yields 3H and 4He binding energies very close to experimental values without introducing three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors use the J-matrix version of inverse scattering theory to construct the NN potential matrix in the harmonic oscillator basis for each partial wave independently. The ambiguity inherent in phase-equivalent transformations is eliminated by imposing a tridiagonal form for the potential matrix in uncoupled partial waves and a quasi-tridiagonal form in coupled partial waves, producing what they call Inverse Scattering Tridiagonal Potentials (ISTP). The resulting potential matrices, with rank chosen such that 2N+l=8 for even l and 2N+l=7 for odd l (using an oscillator parameter hbar-omega=40 MeV), are then used directly in no-core shell model calculations of 3H and 4He.

## Key numbers

* The oscillator basis parameter used throughout is hbar-omega = 40 MeV.
* The ISTP matrix rank N is chosen so that 2N+l=8 in partial waves with even orbital angular momentum l and 2N+l=7 in partial waves with odd l.
* Non-zero diagonal and off-diagonal potential matrix elements are tabulated for multiple partial waves: for example, in the 1s0 partial wave, V_{00} = -0.3706925910512869 and V_{01} = 0.1340546812405571 (in hbar-omega units).
* The 3H and 4He binding energies obtained from no-core shell model calculations using ISTP are very close to experimental values (specific numerical binding energies are not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the J-matrix inverse scattering approach (Ztmf1, Zret, Zret2) to construct NN interactions that are both realistic (reproducing scattering data) and directly usable as effective interactions in no-core shell model calculations without additional truncation or renormalization. It contrasts with meson-exchange realistic potentials like Bonn, Argonne, and Nijmegen (which require three-nucleon forces to reproduce light-nuclei binding) and with the V_{low-k} approach (Kuo-rev), which still requires construction of a shell-model effective interaction. The ISTP approach enables direct no-core shell model studies of few-nucleon systems and, with modern computing, can be extended to nuclei up to A~12.
