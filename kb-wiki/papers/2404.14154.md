# Ab initio description of monopole resonances in light- and medium-mass nuclei: III. Moments evaluation in ab initio PGCM calculations

> The paper is the third of a series dedicated to the ab initio description of monopole giant resonances in mid-mass closed- and open-shell nuclei via the so-called projected generator coordinate method. The present focus is on the computation of the moments $m_k$ of the monopole strength distribution, which are used to quantify its centroid energy and dispersion. First, the capacity to compute low-order moments via two different methods is developed and benchmarked for the $m_1$ moment. Second, the impact of the angular momentum projection on the centroid energy and dispersion of the monopole strength is analysed before comparing the results to those obtained from consistent quasi-particle random phase approximation calculations. Next, the so-called energy weighted sum rule (EWSR) is investigated. First, the appropriate ESWR in the center-of-mass frame is derived analytically. Second, the exhaustion of the intrinsic EWSR is tested in order to quantify the (unwanted) local-gauge symmetry breaking of the presently employed chiral effective field theory ($χ$EFT) interactions. Finally, the infinite nuclear matter incompressibility associated with the employed $χ$EFT interactions is extracted by extrapolating the finite-nucleus incompressibility computed from the monopole centroid energy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) can compute monopole strength moments with internal consistency at the few-percent level, but this agreement is conditional on the completeness of the PGCM subspace with respect to the monopole operator; the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) must be corrected for center-of-mass effects to properly quantify local-gauge symmetry breaking in chiral effective field theory interactions.

## Method

The authors compute moments \(m_k\) of the monopole strength distribution for \(^{16}\text{O}\), \(^{24}\text{Mg}\), \(^{28}\text{Si}\), and \(^{46}\text{Ti}\) using two approaches: the sum-over-excited-states (SOES) method, which sums over PGCM eigenstates, and the ground-state-expectation-value (GSEV) method, which evaluates commutator-based moment operators in the ground state. Two-dimensional PGCM calculations mix constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states using the root-mean-square radius and axial quadrupole deformation as generator coordinates, with a chiral Hamiltonian at N\(^3\)LO evolved via similarity renormalization group to \(\lambda = 1.88\) fm\(^{-1}\). The \(m_1\) moment is benchmarked between SOES and GSEV, and the intrinsic EWSR is derived analytically and tested against the computed strength.

*   \(m_1\) for \(^{24}\text{Mg}\): GCM SOES = 16676 fm\(^4\)MeV, GCM GSEV = 17850 fm\(^4\)MeV; PGCM SOES = 17178 fm\(^4\)MeV, PGCM GSEV = 17978 fm\(^4\)MeV.
*   \(m_1\) for \(^{46}\text{Ti}\): GCM SOES = 43185 fm\(^4\)MeV, GCM GSEV = 46776 fm\(^4\)MeV; PGCM SOES = 44392 fm\(^4\)MeV, PGCM GSEV = 47046 fm\(^4\)MeV.
*   Centroid energy \(\bar{E}_1\) for \(^{16}\text{O}\): GCM = 21.51 MeV, PGCM = 22.26 MeV (3.4% shift due to angular momentum projection).
*   Centroid energy \(\bar{E}_1\) for \(^{24}\text{Mg}\): GCM = 17.46 MeV, PGCM = 17.72 MeV (1.5% shift).
*   Centroid energy \(\bar{E}_1\) for \(^{46}\text{Ti}\): GCM = 19.40 MeV, PGCM = 19.60 MeV (1.1% shift).
*   Dispersion \(\sigma\) for \(^{16}\text{O}\): GCM = 5.67 MeV, PGCM = 7.02 MeV (19.3% shift).
*   Dispersion \(\sigma\) for \(^{46}\text{Ti}\): GCM = 3.89 MeV, PGCM = 3.63 MeV (7.1% shift).
*   GSEV \(m_1\) from QFAM for \(^{16}\text{O}\): 8356 fm\(^4\)MeV; for \(^{24}\text{Mg}\): 17478 fm\(^4\)MeV; for \(^{46}\text{Ti}\): 46387 fm\(^4\)MeV.

## Context

This paper is the third in a series extending the ab initio PGCM framework for monopole resonances, following Paper I (Porro 2024) on uncertainty quantification and Paper II (Porro 2024) on strength functions in light and medium-mass nuclei. It develops moment evaluation techniques that enable comparison with quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations via the finite amplitude method (QFAM) from Beaujeault (2023). The work enables extraction of the infinite nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_\infty\) from finite-nucleus centroid energies, providing a test of chiral Hamiltonians against empirical expectations for the nuclear equation of state.
