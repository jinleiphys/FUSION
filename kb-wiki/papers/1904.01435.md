# Wounded nucleon, quark and quark-diquark emission functions versus experimental results from RHIC

> Using the wounded nucleon, quark, and quark-diquark models, we extract the wounded source emission functions from the PHOBOS data for d+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{NN}}}=200$ GeV. We apply these models to compute charged particle multiplicity distributions as functions of pseudorapidity for p+p, p+Al, p+Au, d+Au, $^3$He+Au, Cu+Cu, Cu+Au, Au+Au, and U+U collisions at the same energy and compare them with experimental data from the PHOBOS and PHENIX collaborations. In symmetric collisions of heavy nuclei, the obtained distributions differ among the tested models. On the other hand, in asymmetric collisions, all three models give essentially the same distributions. The wounded quark-diquark and quark models are in reasonable agreement with data for all the investigated systems.

# Wounded nucleon, quark and quark-diquark emission functions versus experimental results from RHIC

## Key claim

The wounded quark and quark-diquark models are in reasonable agreement with experimental data for all investigated collision systems at √s_NN = 200 GeV, while the wounded nucleon model is unsuitable for central symmetric collisions of heavy nuclei. However, for asymmetric collisions (p, d, ³He on Au), all three models give essentially identical distributions, meaning the data cannot distinguish between them in those systems.

## Method

The authors extracted wounded source emission functions F(η) from minimum-bias PHOBOS data for d+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV using the wounded nucleon model (WNM), wounded quark model (WQM), and wounded quark-diquark model (WQDM). They then applied these parameter-free emission functions via Glauber Monte Carlo simulations to compute charged particle pseudorapidity distributions dNch/dη for nine collision systems (p+p, p+Al, p+Au, d+Au, ³He+Au, Cu+Cu, Cu+Au, Au+Au, U+U) at the same energy, comparing with PHOBOS and PHENIX data across multiple centrality classes. Constituent-constituent cross-sections were determined by trial and error: σ_qq = 6.65 mb for WQM and σ'_qq = 5.75 mb for WQDM (with σ_qq:σ_qd:σ_dd = 1:2:4).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon cross-section at √s_NN = 200 GeV: σ_nn = 41 mb
- Wounded quark model constituent cross-section: σ_qq = 6.65 mb
- Wounded quark-diquark model constituent cross-section: σ'_qq = 5.75 mb (with σ_qq:σ_qd:σ_dd = 1:2:4)
- Mean number of wounded constituents per nucleon in p+p collisions: 1.00 (WNM), 1.14 (WQDM), 1.27 (WQM)
- Mean number of wounded quarks in minimum-bias d+Au: 3.68 (deuteron side), 8.70 (gold side)
- Mean number of wounded nucleons in minimum-bias Au+Au: 50.2 per nucleus
- Mean number of wounded quarks in minimum-bias Au+Au: 115.7 per nucleus
- Mean number of wounded quarks in 0-6% central Au+Au: 449.1 per nucleus
- Largest disagreement with data: approximately 20% for most central ³He+Au collisions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Barej, 2017) where the wounded quark emission function was extracted from d+Au data and predictions were made for p+Al, p+Au, and ³He+Au that were later verified by PHENIX (Adare, 2018). The paper systematically compares three wounded-source models originally proposed by Bialas (1976, 1977) and the quark-diquark model developed by Bzdak and collaborators. The results enable parameter-free predictions for pseudorapidity distributions across all RHIC collision systems at 200 GeV, and the authors note they are working to extend these models to include fragmentation regions and unwounded sources. The paper calls for testing these results against more sophisticated models such as color glass condensate approaches (Iancu, 2002; Gelis, 2010; Kharzeev, 2002) and event generators like HIJING (Wang, 1991) and EPOS (Pierog, 2009).
