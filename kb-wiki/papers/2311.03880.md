# Chiral Soliton Lattice turns into 3D crystal

> Chiral perturbation theory predicts the chiral anomaly to induce a so-called Chiral Soliton Lattice at sufficiently large magnetic fields and baryon chemical potentials. This state breaks translational invariance in the direction of the magnetic field and was shown to be unstable with respect to charged pion condensation. Improving on previous work by considering a realistic pion mass, we employ methods from type-II superconductivity and construct a three-dimensional pion (and baryon) crystal perturbatively, close to the instability curve of the Chiral Soliton Lattice. We find an analogue of the usual type-I/type-II transition in superconductivity: Along the instability curve for magnetic fields $eB > 0.12\, {\rm GeV}^2$ and chemical potentials $μ< 910\, {\rm MeV}$, this crystal can continuously supersede the Chiral Soliton Lattice. For smaller magnetic fields the instability curve must be preceded by a discontinuous transition.

## Key claim
The paper claims that, for magnetic fields \(eB > 0.12\, {\rm GeV}^2\) and chemical potentials \(\mu < 910\, {\rm MeV}\), a three-dimensional pion (and baryon) crystal can continuously supersede the Chiral Soliton Lattice along the instability curve; for smaller magnetic fields, the transition must be discontinuous, analogous to the type-I/type-II transition in superconductivity.

## Method
The authors used leading-order chiral perturbation theory with a Wess-Zumino-Witten term and a realistic pion mass \(m_\pi = 140\, {\rm MeV}\). They expanded the equations of motion for the electromagnetic gauge field, neutral pion field, and charged pion field in a small parameter \(\epsilon\) near the critical magnetic field \(B_{c2}\) of the Chiral Soliton Lattice instability, employing methods from Ginzburg-Landau theory for type-II superconductors. The resulting three-dimensional crystal was constructed perturbatively in Fourier space, solving the expanded equations to determine the spatial variation of magnetic field, baryon number, and all pion condensates.

## Key numbers
- The type-I/type-II transition point occurs at \(\mu \simeq 910\, {\rm MeV}\) and \(e\langle B\rangle \simeq 0.12\, {\rm GeV}^2 \simeq 6.3\, m_\pi^2 \simeq 6.1\times 10^{18}\, {\rm G}\).
- The critical magnetic field for the onset of the Chiral Soliton Lattice is \(eB_{\rm CSL} = 16\pi m_\pi f_\pi^2 / \mu\).
- The instability curve for the Chiral Soliton Lattice ends at the domain wall limit with \(eB_{c2} = 3 m_\pi^2\).
- The chiral limit gives \(eB_{c2} = 16\pi^4 f_\pi^4 / \mu^2\).
- The baryon onset at zero magnetic field is at \(\mu_0 \simeq 922.7\, {\rm MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Evans and Schmitt, 2022) which considered the chiral limit \(m_\pi = 0\) and found a two-dimensional Abrikosov lattice; here they generalize to a realistic pion mass, yielding a three-dimensional crystal. It builds on the prediction of the Chiral Soliton Lattice phase by Brauner and collaborators (2016) and the observation of its instability toward charged pion condensation. The results enable future studies of the QCD phase diagram at high magnetic fields and baryon chemical potentials, including comparisons with first-principle lattice QCD calculations (currently out of reach due to the chemical potential) and with model approaches such as nucleon-meson models or gauge-gravity duality.
