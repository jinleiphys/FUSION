# Locating the critical end point using the linear sigma model coupled to quarks

> We use the linear sigma model coupled to quarks to compute the effective potential beyond the mean field approximation, including the contribution of the ring diagrams at finite temperature and baryon density. We determine the model couplings and use them to study the phase diagram in the baryon chemical potential-temperature plane and to locate the Critical End Point.

## Key claim

Using the linear sigma model coupled to quarks with ring diagram contributions beyond mean field, the authors locate the critical end point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram under the condition that the model couplings are fixed using both the lattice QCD critical temperature at zero chemical potential and the conjectured maximum baryon chemical potential of the transition line at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors compute the finite-temperature effective potential for the linear sigma model with two light quark flavors, including one-loop boson and fermion contributions plus ring diagrams to account for plasma screening effects. They work in both the high-temperature approximation (T >> m_b, mu_q) and the low-temperature approximation (T << m_b, mu_q), using a Taylor series expansion around T=0 for the latter. The model couplings lambda and g are determined by imposing five conditions: the pion thermal mass vanishes at the second-order transition point at mu_q=0 and T=T_c, and the effective potential has two degenerate minima at the first-order transition point at T=0 and mu_q=mu_q^c, with counter-terms added to the tree-level potential.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum pion mass: 139 MeV; vacuum sigma mass: 475 MeV (used as inputs for both parameter sets)
- For the first parameter set: T_c(mu_q=0) = 170 MeV, mu_q^c(T=0) = 340 MeV, yielding (lambda, g) = (0.87, 1.57)
- For the second parameter set: T_c(mu_q=0) = 165 MeV, mu_q^c(T=0) = 330 MeV, yielding (lambda, g) = (0.91, 1.61)
- The critical end point is located in the region of low temperatures and high quark chemical potential (exact coordinates not given numerically in the text)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the QCD phase diagram using effective models, specifically the linear sigma model with quarks, by including ring diagram contributions beyond the mean field approximation. The approach follows the method of fixing couplings using lattice QCD results for the critical temperature at zero chemical potential (LQCD computations, circa 2017) and the Hagedorn limiting temperature analysis for the critical chemical potential at zero temperature (Hagedorn). The results enable a more robust determination of the CEP location, though the authors note that including temperature and density modifications to the couplings would be needed for greater precision, as has been shown useful for describing inverse magnetic catalysis.
