# Heavy Hadrons and QCD Instantons

> Heavy hadrons are analyzed in a random and dilute gas of instantons. We derive the instanton-induced interactions between heavy and light quarks at next to leading order in the heavy quark mass and in the planar approximation, and discuss their effects on the hadronic spectrum. The role of these interactions in the formation of exotic hadrons is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives instanton-induced interactions between heavy and light quarks at next-to-leading order in the heavy quark mass and in the planar approximation, but the results are limited to a random and dilute gas of instantons with a diluteness factor of order \(n\rho^4 \sim 10^{-3}\), and the spectrum calculations rely on a variational approach with harmonic confinement that is outside the instanton model itself.

## Method

The authors perform a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation of the heavy quark field to expand the QCD action in inverse powers of the heavy quark mass \(m_Q\), keeping terms up to order \(1/m_Q\). They then analyze heavy-light meson correlation functions in a random instanton gas using the planar approximation, where the inverse correlator is expressed in terms of the 't Hooft zero mode of the Dirac operator in a one-instanton background. Effective local interactions between heavy and light quarks are derived from the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the long-wavelength limit, and the resulting Hamiltonians are used in a variational calculation with harmonic oscillator trial wavefunctions to estimate hadron spectra.

## Key numbers

- Instanton-induced shift in heavy quark mass from leading order: \(\Delta_0 M_Q \sim 70\) MeV
- Recoil correction to heavy quark mass for a charm quark (\(m_c = 1350\) MeV): \(\Delta_1 M_Q \simeq 16\) MeV
- Total instanton-induced heavy quark mass shift: \(\Delta M_Q = \Delta_0 M_Q + \Delta_1 M_Q \sim 86\) MeV
- Average light quark mass shift from instantons: \(\Delta M_q \sim 420\) MeV
- Chromomagnetic spin correction for a charm quark: \(\Delta M_Q^{spin} \simeq 3\) MeV
- Expectation value of instanton-induced two-body interaction for heavy-light mesons \(Q\overline{q}\): \(\langle H^{(2)}_{qQ} \rangle \sim -183\) MeV
- Expectation value of instanton-induced two-body interaction for heavy-heavy mesons \(Q\overline{Q}\): \(\langle H^{(2)}_{QQ} \rangle \sim -103\) MeV
- Spin correction for heavy-light mesons: \(\langle H_{qQ}^{2,s} \rangle \simeq -42\) MeV
- Spin induced interaction in charmonium: \(\langle H_{QQ}^{2,s} \rangle \sim -0.7\) MeV
- Diluteness factor: \(n\rho^4 \sim 10^{-3}\)
- Size of heavy-light meson ground state: \(r_{qQ} = 0.6\) fm
- Size of heavy-heavy system: \(r_{QQ} \simeq 0.4\) fm
- Confining energy for mesons: \({\cal E}_\alpha \simeq 250\) MeV
- Size of heavy-light baryons: \(r_{qqQ} = 1\) fm
- Confining energy for baryons: \({\cal E}_\alpha \sim 500\) MeV

## Context

This work extends the instanton-based approach to heavy-light systems previously developed by the same authors (Chernyshev, Nowak, Zahed, 1995) and by Diakonov and collaborators, combining heavy quark symmetry with the random instanton gas model of the QCD vacuum. The results are compared to the constituent quark model of De Rujula, Georgi, and Glashow (1975) and to bag model estimates, showing overall agreement. The paper enables further study of exotic hadrons such as tetraquarks and pentaquarks with heavy quarks, and suggests that the H-dibaryon may be unbound by three-body instanton forces if strangeness is treated as light, but may be bound if strangeness is treated as heavy.
