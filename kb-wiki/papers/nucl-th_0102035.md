# Asymmetric Nuclear Matter from Extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock Approach

> The properties of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter have been investigated in the framework of the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation at zero temperature. Self-consistent calculations using the Argonne $V_{14}$ interaction are reported for several asymmetry parameters $β= \frac{N - Z}{A}$ ranging from symmetric nuclear matter to pure neutron matter. The binding energy per nucleon fulfills the $β^2$ law in the whole asymmetry range. The symmetry energy is calculated for different densities and discussed in comparison with other predictions. At the saturation point it is in fairly good agreement with the empirical value. The present approximation, based on the Landau definition of quasiparticle energy, is investigated in terms of the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem, which is proved to be fulfilled with a good accuracy at various asymmetries. The isospin dependence of the single-particle properties is discussed, including mean field, effective mass, and mean free path of neutrons and protons. The isospin effects in nuclear physics and nuclear astrophysics are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The binding energy per nucleon of isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter obeys the empirical parabolic law (β² law) over the entire asymmetry range from symmetric matter to pure neutron matter, at least up to twice saturation density, with no significant β⁴ contribution expected from higher-order terms in the Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone expansion.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently for the complex G-matrix using the Argonne V₁₄ nucleon-nucleon potential with partial waves up to L=6, for three densities (ρ₀/2, ρ₀, and 2ρ₀, where ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³) and asymmetry parameters β = (N−Z)/A from 0 to 1. They went beyond the standard Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approximation by including the second-order rearrangement term M₂ and renormalization corrections from partial depletion of the Fermi seas (third- and fourth-order terms in the G-matrix), calling this the extended Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (EBHF) approximation. The single-particle properties (mean field, effective mass, mean free path) were computed separately for neutrons and protons using the Landau definition of quasiparticle energy.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³ (used as reference density throughout the paper).
- Binding energy per nucleon B(ρ,β) obeys the parabolic law B(ρ,β) = B(ρ,0) + E_sym(ρ) β² for all β from 0 to 1 at the three densities studied (ρ₀/2, ρ₀, 2ρ₀).
- The symmetry energy E_sym(ρ) increases with density and shows no saturation up to ρ = 0.5 fm⁻³, in contrast to some variational calculations.
- The crossing point in the momentum dependence of the neutron and proton mean fields occurs at a momentum where the average G-matrix components ⟨G^pp⟩ = ⟨G^pn⟩ for protons and ⟨G^np⟩ = ⟨G^nn⟩ for neutrons, independent of β.
- The self-consistent solution of the Bethe-Goldstone equation required five iterations to reach satisfactory convergence.

## Context

This work extends the earlier BHF study of asymmetric nuclear matter by Lombardo et al. (using the separable Paris potential) by employing the full Argonne V₁₄ potential with additional partial waves (L=3 to 6) that contribute significantly at high density, and by including higher-order correlation terms beyond BHF. The results are compared with variational calculations by Wiringa, Fiks, and Fabrocini (1988) using the same Argonne V₁₄ potential, and with lowest-order constrained variational calculations by Pandharipande and collaborators. The EBHF approach enables more realistic predictions for neutron star structure, supernova dynamics, and heavy-ion collisions with neutron-rich nuclei, and provides a microscopic test of the Lane potential form for the isospin dependence of the nuclear mean field.
