# Nonextensive thermodynamics for hadrons with finite chemical potentials

> The non extensive thermodynamics of an ideal gas composed by bosons and/or fermions is derived from its partition function for systems with finite chemical potentials. It is shown that the thermodynamical quantities derived in the present work are in agreement with those obtained in previous works when $μ\le m$. However some inconsistencies of previous references are corrected when $μ> m$. A discontinuity in the first derivatives of the partition function and its effects are discussed in detail. We show that at similar conditions, the non extensive statistics provide a harder equation of state than that provided by the Boltzmann-Gibbs statistics.

## Key claim

The paper derives a thermodynamically consistent formulation of Tsallis non-extensive statistics for ideal quantum gases of bosons and fermions at finite chemical potentials, correcting previous works by including p-independent terms in the average particle number and energy that arise from a discontinuity in the partition function integrand when the chemical potential exceeds the particle mass (μ > m).

## Method

The authors start from the grand-canonical partition function defined using q-exponential and q-logarithm functions with separate branches for positive and negative arguments, then derive the average occupation number, particle number, energy, and entropy via standard thermodynamic relations. They compare their results with prior formulations by Cleymans and Worku (2012) and Conroy, Miller, and Plastino (2010), identifying discrepancies in the x < 0 (high chemical potential) sector, and apply the formalism to a hadron resonance gas containing 808 meson and 1168 baryon states to compute phase transition lines and equations of state.

## Key numbers

- The entropic index is set to q = 1.14, determined from analysis of pT-distributions and hadronic mass spectrum in prior work by Lucas (2013).
- For μ_B = 0, the effective temperature from the energy-per-particle condition (⟨E⟩/⟨N⟩ = 1 GeV) is T_o = 45.6 MeV for q = 1.14, while for q = 1.12 it becomes T_o = 61.0 MeV.
- The chemical freeze-out line spans the region 0 < μ_B < 1039.2 MeV for the energy-per-particle condition with q = 1.14.
- The ratio of Boltzmann-Gibbs temperature T to Tsallis effective temperature τ is approximately constant at 3.65 for the energy-per-particle condition up to μ_B ≈ 0.8 GeV.
- The maximum baryonic chemical potential for the transition line is μ_B = 1039.2 MeV, corresponding to zero critical temperature.

## Context

This work extends the non-extensive self-consistent thermodynamics developed by Deppman (2012) from zero chemical potential to finite chemical potentials, which is important for studying nucleus-nucleus collisions and protoneutron stars. It corrects inconsistencies in prior formulations by Cleymans and Worku (2012) and Conroy, Miller, and Plastino (2010) for the regime μ > m, and provides the necessary formalism to investigate how Tsallis statistics affects stellar matter equations of state. The results enable future comparisons with lattice QCD data at finite baryon chemical potential and with experimental freeze-out data from RHIC, SPS, AGS, HADES, and FOPI collaborations.
