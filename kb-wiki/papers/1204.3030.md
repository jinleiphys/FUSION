# One possible mechanism for massive neutron star supported by soft EOS

> The recently discovery of a massive neutron star (PSR J1614-2230 of $1.97\pm0.04M_{\odot}$) rules out the soft equation of states (EOSs) such as those included hyperons or kaon condensates at high densities, while the nuclear theory or the terrestrial laboratory data prefer a soft EOS. Here we propose one possible mechanism to allow that the observed massive neutron star can be supported by a soft EOS, that is, if the the gravitational constant $G$ varies at super strong field, a soft EOS can support the massive neutron stars.

# One possible mechanism for massive neutron star supported by soft EOS

## Key claim
If the gravitational constant \(G\) decreases at super-strong field strengths (to about \(G/G_0 = 0.87\) or lower), a soft equation of state that includes hyperons can support the observed mass of PSR J1614-2230 (\(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\)), even though such soft EOSs are normally ruled out by that observation.

## Method
The authors solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for static, spherically symmetric neutron stars, treating the gravitational constant \(G\) as a variable parameter denoted by the ratio \(G/G_0\) (where \(G_0\) is the Newtonian constant on Earth). They adopt a soft equation of state derived from the relativistic \(\sigma-\omega-\rho\) model that includes hyperons at high densities, with parameters fitted to reproduce nuclear saturation properties. The mass-radius relation and moment of inertia are computed for several values of \(G/G_0\).

## Key numbers
- PSR J1614-2230 has a mass of \(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\); with the standard gravitational constant (\(G/G_0 = 1\)), the soft hyperon EOS yields a maximum mass of only about \(1.61 M_\odot\), which cannot support this observation.
- To support a mass of \(1.97 M_\odot\), the gravitational constant must decrease to \(G/G_0 = 0.87\).
- To also support the observation of EXO 0748-676 (mass \(\geq 2.10 \pm 0.28 M_\odot\), radius \(\geq 13.8 \pm 1.8\) km), the gravitational constant should decline to about \(G/G_0 = 0.80\).
- For a canonical neutron star of \(1.4 M_\odot\), the moment of inertia increases by about 33% when \(G/G_0\) is reduced from 1.0 to 0.80.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Dobado (2011), who used the two-solar-mass neutron star observation to constrain the Newtonian gravitational constant in neutron stars. It also builds on earlier studies by Wen et al. (2009, 2011) that considered non-Newtonian gravity as a way to stiffen soft EOSs. The proposed mechanism offers an alternative to making the EOS itself stiffer (as attempted by Bednarek and Bonanno), and suggests that future accurate measurements of neutron star moment of inertia could probe variations of the gravitational constant at strong fields.
