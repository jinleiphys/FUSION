# Improved radiative corrections for (e,e'p) experiments: Beyond the peaking approximation and implications of the soft-photon approximation

> Analysing (e,e'p) experimental data involves corrections for radiative effects which change the interaction kinematics and which have to be carefully considered in order to obtain the desired accuracy. Missing momentum and energy due to bremsstrahlung have so far always been calculated using the peaking approximation which assumes that all bremsstrahlung is emitted in the direction of the radiating particle. In this article we introduce a full angular Monte Carlo simulation method which overcomes this approximation. The angular distribution of the bremsstrahlung photons is reconstructed from H(e,e'p) data. Its width is found to be underestimated by the peaking approximation and described much better by the approach developed in this work.

## Key claim
The peaking approximation (PA) underestimates the width of the bremsstrahlung photon angular distribution in (e,e'p) experiments, particularly between the radiation peaks in the incident and scattered electron directions; the full angular Monte Carlo (FAMC) method developed in this work reproduces the measured angular distribution more accurately, while the impact of the soft-photon approximation (SPA) on the photon angular distribution is found to be minor compared to the impact of the PA.

## Method
The authors developed a full angular Monte Carlo (FAMC) simulation that generates multi-photon bremsstrahlung events according to the modified soft-photon approximation (mSPA) photon angular distribution, overcoming the peaking approximation which assumes all bremsstrahlung is emitted in the direction of the radiating particle. The simulation uses probability density functions for photon energies, angular distributions, and photon multiplicity (a Poisson distribution) to generate events, with the angular distribution reconstructed from the SPA expression A(Ω_γ). The FAMC method was implemented into the SIMC analysis code for Hall C at TJNAF and tested against H(e,e'p) experimental data from the E97-006 experiment at a beam energy of 3.12 GeV, using 600,000 successful Monte Carlo events per run to compare PA and FAMC results.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 3.12 GeV for the H(e,e'p) test kinematics (Q² = 2 GeV²)
- The total radiated energy simulated with the FAMC method is about 0.3% smaller than that from the peaking approximation
- The ee interference term accounted for roughly 20% of all events at the kinematic setting with Q² = 2 GeV²
- A sample kinematic configuration at Q² = 15 GeV² is given: incident electron energy 21.00 GeV, scattered electron momentum 13.00 GeV, outgoing proton momentum 8.882 GeV, electron scattering angle 13.5°, proton scattering angle -19.9°

## Context
This work extends previous radiative correction procedures for (e,e'p) experiments that relied on the peaking approximation, first proposed by Schiff (1952) for (e,e') experiments and extended to (e,e'p) by Makins and collaborators. The need for removing the PA became evident from H(e,e'p) data showing discrepancies between the PA and measured bremsstrahlung angular distributions, as noted by Maximon and Isabelle. A similar FAMC code was described by Afanasev and collaborators but did not handle multi-photon bremsstrahlung or get inserted into data analysis codes. This work enables more accurate radiative corrections for current experiments at TJNAF and MAMI, particularly for studies of short-range correlations in nuclei, and indicates that for beam energies envisaged for the TJNAF upgrade, a calculation going beyond the SPA might become necessary.
