# The Infrared Sensitivity of Screening and Damping in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

> All the next-to-leading order contributions to the quasi-particle dispersion laws of a quark-gluon plasma which due to infrared singularities are sensitive to the magnetic-mass scale are calculated using Braaten-Pisarski resummation. These relative-order-$g\ln(g)$ corrections are shown here to generally contribute to the dynamical screening of gluonic fields with frequencies below the plasma frequency as well as to the damping of propagating gluonic and fermionic quasi-particles. In the limit of vanishing wave-vector the infrared singularities disappear, but in a way that raises the possibility for formally higher orders of the Braaten-Pisarski scheme to equally contribute at next-to-leading order when the wave-vector is of the order of or less than the magnetic-mass scale. This is argued to be a problem in particular for the fermionic damping rate.

# The Infrared Sensitivity of Screening and Damping in a Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim
All next-to-leading order contributions to quasi-particle dispersion laws that are sensitive to the magnetic-mass scale are calculated using Braaten-Pisarski resummation, yielding relative-order-\(g\ln(g)\) corrections to dynamical screening and damping. However, in the limit of vanishing wave-vector the infrared singularities disappear only in a way that raises the possibility for formally higher orders of the Braaten-Pisarski scheme to equally contribute at next-to-leading order when the wave-vector is of the order of or less than the magnetic-mass scale, which is argued to be a problem in particular for the fermionic damping rate.

## Method
The authors compute the infrared-singular parts of the resummed one-loop gluon and quark self-energies on the mass-shell of the leading-order quasi-particle modes, using Braaten-Pisarski resummation of hard thermal loops. They isolate the contributions that involve a mass-shell singularity from the static (\(n=0\)) Matsubara mode of the transverse gluon propagator, which couples to the mode under study via dressed vertices derived from differential Ward identities. The singular integrals are evaluated with an infrared cutoff \(\lambda\) that is identified with the magnetic-mass scale \(\mu \sim g^2 T\), and the results are expressed as corrections to the dispersion laws for both propagating modes (damping) and screening modes (dynamical screening lengths).

## Key numbers
- The leading-order thermal gluon mass is \(m^2 = (N+N_f/2) (gT/3)^2\) for gauge group SU(\(N\)) with \(N_f\) flavors.
- The leading-order fermion thermal mass is \(M^2 = C_F (gT)^2/8\) with \(C_F = (N^2-1)/(2N)\).
- The damping coefficients for gluonic modes are \(\gamma_{t,\ell} = (g^2 N T / 4\pi) \, v_{t,\ell} \, \ln(1/g)\), where \(v_\ell = (\omega/q) [3(m^2-Q^2)/(3m^2-Q^2)]\) and \(v_t = (\omega/q) [3(m^2\omega^2 - Q^4)/(3m^2\omega^2 - Q^4)]\).
- The damping coefficients for fermionic modes are \(\gamma_\pm = (g^2 C_F T / 4\pi) \, |v_\pm| \, \ln(1/g)\), where \(v_\pm = [\omega(Q^2-M^2) \mp q Q^2] / (q M^2)\).
- The correction to the dynamical screening length for gluonic modes is \(\delta|q|_{t,\ell} = (g^2 N T / 4\pi) \ln(1/g)\), a constant independent of the mode.
- For \(q \to 0\), the singular integral \({\cal S}\) becomes linearly divergent as \(T/(4\pi^2 \lambda)\) rather than logarithmically, and the prefactors vanish as \(q^2\) (gluons) or \(q\) (fermions), making the strict \(q=0\) results infrared-finite.

## Context
This work extends the Braaten-Pisarski resummation scheme (Braaten and Pisarski, 1990) to next-to-leading order corrections that are sensitive to the magnetic-mass scale, which had previously been found to cause logarithmic singularities in the damping of gluonic and fermionic excitations with non-zero wave vector (Pisarski, 1993; Lebedev and Smilga, 1991; Peigne et al., 1993). It provides a unified treatment of these infrared singularities for both screening and damping, showing they originate from mass-shell singularities involving the static transverse gluon propagator. The results enable future calculations to determine the coefficient under the \(\ln(1/g)\) once the magnetic-mass scale is known, but also highlight a limitation of the Braaten-Pisarski scheme for wave-vectors of order or smaller than the magnetic-mass scale, particularly for the fermionic damping rate at \(q=0\) previously computed by Klimov, Karsch, and Matsui (1987).
