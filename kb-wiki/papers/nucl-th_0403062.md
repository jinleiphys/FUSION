# Chaos driven fusion enhancement factor at astrophysical energies

> We perform molecular dynamics simulations to assess the screening effects by bound target electrons in low energy nuclear reactions in laboratories. Quantum effects corresponding to the Pauli and Heisenberg principle are enforced by constraints. We show that the enhancement of the average cross section and of its variance is due to the perturbations induced by the electrons.This gives a correlation between the maximum amplitudes of the inter-nuclear oscillational motion and the enhancement factor. It suggests that the chaotic behavior of the electronic motion affects the magnitude of the enhancement factor.

# Chaos driven fusion enhancement factor at astrophysical energies

## Key claim

The enhancement of the low-energy fusion cross section and its variance arises from perturbations induced by bound target electrons, with the chaotic behavior of the electronic motion affecting the magnitude of the enhancement factor. The averaged enhancement factor does not exceed the adiabatic limit, although fluctuations from individual events can exceed that limit.

## Method

The authors performed constrained molecular dynamics (CoMD) simulations of the D+d reaction, enforcing the Heisenberg uncertainty principle as a constraint on the classical equations of motion for the deuterium atom. They treated the tunneling process by switching to imaginary time when the collective momentum of the two nuclei becomes zero, computing the penetrability from the action integral. For each incident energy, they generated an ensemble of events with different initial conditions in phase space to obtain distributions of the enhancement factor.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of deuterium atom obtained from simulation: -13.56 eV, compared to experimental value -13.59811 eV
- Mean square radius of deuterium atom from simulation: 0.5327 Angstrom, compared to Bohr radius 0.529 Angstrom
- Averaged screening potential from fit to simulation data: U_e = 15.9 +/- 2.0 eV
- Adiabatic limit screening potentials used for comparison: U_e^(g) = 40.7 eV and U_e^(u) = 0.0 eV
- Enhancement factor values for two individual events at E_cm = 0.15 keV: f_e = 170.8 (event A) and f_e = 6.5 (event B)
- Power law exponent for the ratio Sigma^2 / f_e versus incident energy: -2.6
- In the low energy region, the enhancement factor exceeds 50

## Context

This work extends prior time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) studies of electron screening by Shoppa, Koonin, Langanke, and Seki (1993) and by Kimura, Takigawa, Abe, and Brink (2003), which suggested the screening potential does not exceed the adiabatic limit. The molecular dynamics approach captures fluctuations and correlations beyond the mean-field TDHF scheme, which is cylindrically symmetric and cannot describe the chaotic behavior identified here. The results enable future experimental investigations of fusion at very low energies using polarized targets, where the enhancement factor could be controlled by changing the polarization direction relative to the beam axis.
