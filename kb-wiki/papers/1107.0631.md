# Jet shower evolution in medium and dijet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

> We study the evolution of a partonic jet shower propagating through a quark-gluon plasma. Combining the in-medium evolutions of the leading parton and shower gluons, we compute the depletion of the energy from the jet cone by dissipation through elastic collisions with medium constituents, by scattering of shower partons to larger angles, and by radiation outside the jet cone. Numerical results are presented for the nuclear modification of dijet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.

# Jet shower evolution in medium and dijet asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC

## Key claim
The observed enhancement of dijet energy asymmetry in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC can be described by a model combining elastic collisional energy loss, scattering of shower partons to larger angles, and medium-induced radiation outside the jet cone, using transport coefficients similar to those describing jet quenching data at RHIC. The model assumes that vacuum radiation occurs before the jet interacts with the medium, and that the medium undergoes one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion.

## Method
The evolution of a partonic jet shower is described by two coupled equations: one for the energy of the leading parton and one for the double-differential distribution of shower gluons in energy and transverse momentum. These equations incorporate elastic collisional energy loss, transverse momentum broadening via the transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\), and a medium-induced gluon radiation rate taken from the higher-twist formalism of jet quenching. The initial gluon distribution before medium interaction is generated from PYTHIA, and the medium temperature profile is modeled with a one-dimensional boost-invariant expansion starting at \(t_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) with a central temperature \(T_0 = 520\) MeV.

## Key numbers
- The medium in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV is assumed to thermalize at \(t_0 = 0.6\) fm/\(c\) with a hottest-point temperature \(T_0 = 520\) MeV in central collisions.
- A lower cut-off of 2 GeV is imposed on the energy of shower gluons when solving the radiated gluon distribution.
- The length-averaged transport coefficient in central collisions is \(\langle \hat{q} \rangle \approx 0.9\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, with about 20% difference between ATLAS and CMS for the best descriptions of the data.
- This corresponds to \(\hat{q} \approx 2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at \(T = 400\) MeV, the highest temperature available in Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- The local temperature of the medium drops below \(T_c = 160\) MeV, at which point jet-medium interaction stops.

## Context
This work extends the study of jet quenching in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, which has been investigated by Armesto (2011) and Bass (2008), by providing a simplified but realistic model for the in-medium evolution of a full partonic jet shower rather than only the leading parton. The model enables quantitative comparison with dijet asymmetry measurements from ATLAS (2010) and CMS (2011) at the LHC, and the extracted transport coefficients are consistent with those describing jet quenching data at RHIC. Future directions include complete Monte-Carlo simulation of jet shower evolution and reconstruction, and realistic event-by-event hydrodynamical simulation of fluctuating background as studied by Qin (2010) and Cacciari (2011).
