# Stability of the manifold boundary approximation method for reductions of nuclear structure models

> The framework of nuclear energy density functionals has been employed to describe nuclear structure phenomena for a wide range of nuclei. Recently, statistical properties of a given nuclear model, such as parameter confidence intervals and correlations, have received much attention, particularly when one tries to fit complex models. We apply information-theoretic methods to investigate stability of model reductions by the manifold boundary approximation method (MBAM). In an illustrative example of the density-dependent point-coupling model of the relativistic energy density functional, utilizing Monte Carlo simulations, it is found that main conclusions obtained from the MBAM procedure are stable under variation of the model parameters. Furthermore, we find that the end of the geodesic occurs when the determinant of the Fisher information metric vanishes, thus effectively separating the parameter space into two disconnected regions.

## Key claim

The main conclusions obtained from the manifold boundary approximation method (MBAM) procedure for reducing the density-dependent point-coupling (DD-PC1) model are stable under variation of the model parameters within the 1-sigma confidence interval of the best-fitting model, but the method is sensitive to the choice of reparametrization because different parametrizations describe similar but inherently different physical models.

## Method

The authors applied the manifold boundary approximation method (MBAM) to a seven-parameter density-dependent point-coupling model (DD-PC1) of the relativistic energy density functional, constrained by pseudodata for infinite symmetric nuclear matter including Dirac mass and binding energy. They solved the geodesic equation using algorithmic differentiation via the AUTOGRAD package and the SCIPY ordinary differential equation integrator, then investigated stability by extrapolating geodesics beyond singular regions, propagating parameter uncertainties via Monte Carlo simulations using the Jacobi equation, and testing an exponential reparametrization of the coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The pseudodata set includes 8 data points: one Dirac mass measurement (0.58 at density 0.152 fm^-3, with 2% error of 0.055) and 7 binding energy measurements (ranging from -6.48 MeV at 0.04 fm^-3 to -5.03 MeV at 0.32 fm^-3, each with 10% error)
- 1300 Monte Carlo sample points were used to sample the DD-PC1 parameter space for uncertainty propagation
- 200 Markov chains were used in the MCMC sampling of the reparametrized model posterior distribution
- The geodesic singularity occurs at tau approximately 1.3, where the Fisher information metric determinant vanishes and the Ricci scalar diverges

## Context

This work extends previous information-geometric studies of nuclear energy density functionals by Niksic (2016) and Niksic (2017), which demonstrated that nuclear EDFs are "sloppy" models and applied MBAM to deduce effective functional forms. The present study investigates the stability of those MBAM reductions, finding that while the main conclusions are robust to parameter variations within confidence intervals, the method is sensitive to reparametrization choices. This enables future applications of MBAM to more complex nuclear structure models constrained by finite-nuclei observables, while cautioning practitioners about the need for careful integration step sizes and awareness of metric singularities.
