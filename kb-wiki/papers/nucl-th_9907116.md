# Strange matter in the string-flip model

> We employ variational Monte Carlo methods to study the transition to strange matter in a simple one-dimensional string-flip model with two flavors and two colors of quarks. The dynamics of the system are described in terms of a many-body potential that confines quarks within hadrons, yet enables the hadrons to separate without generating unphysical long-range van der Waals forces. The model has ``natural'' low- and high-density limits: it behaves as a system of isolated hadrons at low density and as a Fermi gas of quarks at high density. We show that the system exhibits a transition to strange matter characterized by an increase in the length-scale for confinement. Yet the small increase at the transition region --- of only ten percent --- suggests that clustering correlations remain strong well into the strange-matter domain. Our results put into question descriptions of strange matter in terms of noninteracting, or weakly interacting, quarks.

## Key claim

Using a one-dimensional string-flip model with two flavors and two colors of quarks, the authors show that the transition to strange matter is characterized by only a ten percent increase in the confinement length-scale, suggesting that clustering correlations remain strong well into the strange-matter domain and putting into question descriptions of strange matter in terms of noninteracting or weakly interacting quarks.

## Method

The authors employed variational Monte Carlo methods to compute ground-state properties of a one-dimensional string-flip model with up to 80 quarks (40 baryons). The many-body potential confines quarks within color-neutral two-quark composites (nucleons, lambdas, and cascades) while enabling hadrons to separate without generating long-range van der Waals forces, with the optimal pairing determined by minimizing the flux-tube energy among all possible configurations. A one-parameter variational wavefunction was used, consisting of a free Fermi-gas wavefunction multiplied by an exponential clustering factor, and the optimal variational parameter was extracted by minimizing the total energy at each density and strangeness-per-baryon ratio.

## Key numbers

- Critical density for transition to strange matter in the Fermi-gas model: ρ_c = 0.697 (in units where ħ, spring constant k, and up-quark mass are set to 1)
- Constituent strange-quark mass ratio: m_s/m_u = 1.6
- Transition to strange matter in the string-flip model occurs at approximately ρ = 1.3 ρ_c (ρ = 0.9)
- At the transition density, the length-scale for confinement increases by only 10% relative to its isolated-hadron value
- At the highest density shown, the variational energy remains about 5% larger than the Fermi-gas result
- Hartree-Fock estimate at the transition density overestimates the variational energy by about 20%
- Optimal variational parameter values: λ_0 = 1/√2 (≈0.707) in the isolated-hadron limit, approaching λ = 0 in the Fermi-gas limit
- Variational parameters for different hadron types: α_uu = 1, α_us ≈ 1.109, α_ss ≈ 1.265

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional string-flip model originally proposed by Lenz and collaborators (1986) and first applied to nuclear matter by Horowitz, Moniz, and Negele (1985), incorporating strange quarks to study the transition from hadronic to quark matter. The results contradict the traditional description of strange matter as a Fermi gas of noninteracting quarks (Witten, 1984; Farhi and Jaffe, 1984) and instead support a hadronic picture where clustering correlations remain dominant even at the transition density. The findings enable future work on the dynamic response of the system, including calculation of the Coulomb sum and extension to three-dimensional models with three flavors and three colors of quarks.
