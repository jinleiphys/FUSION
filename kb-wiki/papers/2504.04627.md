# Harmonic Oscillator Representation of Scattering Theory in the Presence of Coulomb Potential

> Considering the problem of scattering of charged particles, we introduce a new approach of taking the Coulomb interaction into account within the HORSE formalism. Compared to the conventional HORSE approach for uncharged particles, we add a diagonal Coulomb term to} the three-term recurrent relation for expansion coefficients in the asymptotic region. The method simplifies calculations and demonstrates a good agreement with numerical solution.

## Key claim

The authors introduce a modification to the HORSE formalism for charged-particle scattering by adding a diagonal Coulomb term to the three-term recurrence relation for expansion coefficients in the asymptotic region, and they demonstrate that this asymptotic recurrence relation remains highly accurate even for small radial quantum numbers, enabling a simplified calculation of scattering phase shifts.

## Method

The authors expand the radial wave function in a harmonic oscillator basis and modify the standard three-term recurrence relation (TRR) for the expansion coefficients in the asymptotic region by adding a diagonal Coulomb term V_{nn}^{ad,l}. They use the asymptotic expressions for the regular and irregular Coulomb wave function expansion coefficients S_{nl}(k) and C_{nl}(k) as starting values for the TRR, then propagate these coefficients down to small n. The nuclear potential is smoothly truncated at N=10 using a smoothing parameter alpha=5, while the Coulomb potential is sharply truncated at the same N. The phase shifts are then obtained from the matching condition at the truncation boundary using the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the truncated Hamiltonian matrix.

## Key numbers

- The starting value n_s=40 was used for the TRR propagation in the test calculation for p-alpha scattering at E=20 MeV with hbar-omega=20 MeV
- For the p-15N scattering test case, the nuclear interaction was smoothly truncated at N=10 with hbar-omega=18 MeV
- The method used n_s=200 for the p-15N scattering phase shift calculation
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used were V_0=-55.91 MeV, R_0=3.083 fm, alpha_0=0.53 fm, V_ls=0.9 MeV·fm^2, R_1=3.083 fm, and alpha_1=0.53 fm
- The reduced mass for p-15N scattering was mu=881.2 MeV
- The Kiev group method required summation of Coulomb matrix elements up to M=70

## Context

This work extends the HORSE formalism, previously applied to uncharged particles and nuclear resonance states, to handle the long-range Coulomb interaction in charged-particle scattering. It builds on the theoretical framework of the Kiev group (Okhrimenko, 1984) but simplifies their approach by showing the asymptotic TRR works accurately even at small n, avoiding the need to compute sums of Coulomb matrix elements up to large radial quantum numbers. The method is compared to the approach of Bang (1999) which cuts the Coulomb potential at a finite radius b=7.0 fm, and shows comparable accuracy while being simpler to implement in many-body applications like continuum extensions of the No-Core Shell Model.
