# Can the nucleon axial charge be O(Nc^0)?

> The nucleon self-energy and its relation to the nucleon axial charge gA are discussed at large Nc. The energy is compared for the hedgehog, conventional, and recently proposed dichotomous nucleon wavefunctions which give different values for gA. We consider their energies at both perturbative and non-perturbative levels. In perturbative estimates, we take into account the pion exchanges among quarks up to the third orders of axial charge vertices, including the many-body forces such as the Wess-Zumino terms. It turns out that the perturbative pion exchanges among valence quarks give the same leading Nc contributions for three wavefunctions, while their mass differences are O(Lamba_qcd). The signs of splittings flip for different orders of the axial charge vertices, so it is hard to conclude which one is the most energetically favored. For non-perturbative estimates involving the modification of quark bases, we use the chiral quark soliton model as an illustration. With the hedgehog quark wavefunctions with gA of O(Nc), we investigate whether solutions with coherent pions are energetically favored. Again it is hard to give decisive conclusions, but it is likely that adding the confining effects disfavors the solution with the coherent pions, making a pion cloud around a nucleon quantum rather than coherent. The nuclear matter at large Nc is also discussed in light of the value of gA.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether the nucleon axial charge \(g_A\) could be \(O(N_c^0)\) rather than the usual \(O(N_c)\), but concludes that it is very hard to determine which of the three candidate nucleon wavefunctions (hedgehog, conventional, or dichotomous) is energetically favored, because perturbative pion exchanges give the same leading \(N_c\) contributions for all three and the signs of mass splittings flip at different orders of the axial charge vertices.

## Method

The author compares the self-energies of three types of valence baryon wavefunctions (hedgehog, conventional, and dichotomous) at large \(N_c\) using both perturbative and non-perturbative approaches. Perturbatively, pion exchanges among quarks are evaluated up to third order in the axial charge operators, including many-body forces such as the Wess-Zumino term, using \(SU(4)\) spin-flavor algebra and \(N_c\)-counting. Non-perturbatively, the chiral quark soliton model is used as an illustration to examine whether hedgehog quark wavefunctions with coherent pions are energetically favored when confining effects are included.

## Key numbers

* The hedgehog wavefunction gives \(g_A^{\text{val}} = -N_c\).
* The conventional nucleon wavefunction gives \(g_A^{\text{val}} = \pm (N_c+2)/3\) at odd \(N_c\), and zero at even \(N_c\).
* The dichotomous wavefunction gives \(g_A^{\text{val}} = \pm 1\).
* The mass differences between the three wavefunctions are \(O(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}})\), where \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} \sim 200\) MeV.
* The kinetic energy of nucleons at large \(N_c\) is \(\sim \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}/N_c\), which is \(\sim 60\) MeV at \(N_c=3\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Hidaka and Kojo, 2010) which proposed constructing a nucleon wavefunction with \(g_A \sim 1\) to resolve the large binding energy problem in dilute nuclear matter at large \(N_c\). The paper contrasts the hedgehog wavefunction used in Skyrme-type models (Skyrme, 1961; Adkins, Nappi, and Witten, 1983) and chiral quark soliton models (Diakonov and Petrov, 1987; Reinhardt and Wunsch, 1988) with the conventional and dichotomous wavefunctions. The results suggest that confining effects likely disfavor the coherent pion cloud solution, making the pion cloud around a nucleon quantum rather than coherent, which would enable a more realistic description of nuclear matter at large \(N_c\) where binding energies are \(O(1-10)\) MeV.
