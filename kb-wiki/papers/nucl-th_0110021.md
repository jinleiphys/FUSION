# Pseudospin symmetry as a relativistic dynamical symmetry in the nucleus

> Pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is investigated by solving the Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector radial potentials, and studying the correlation of the energy splittings of pseudospin partners with the nuclear potential parameters. The pseudospin interaction is related to a pseudospin-orbit term that arises in a Schroedinger-like equation for the lower component of the Dirac spinor. We show that the contribution from this term to the energy splittings of pseudospin partners is large. The near pseudospin degeneracy results from a significant cancelation among the different terms in that equation, manifesting the dynamical character of this symmetry in the nucleus. We analyze the isospin dependence of the pseudospin symmetry and find that its dynamical character is behind the different pseudospin splittings observed in neutron and proton spectra of nuclei.

## Key claim

Pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is not exact but arises from a dynamical cancellation among several terms in a Schroedinger-like equation for the lower component of the Dirac spinor, with the pseudospin-orbit term contributing a large amount that is offset by kinetic and potential terms. The near degeneracy depends sensitively on the shape parameters (radius, diffuseness, depth) of the Woods-Saxon mean-field potentials, and the symmetry is therefore dynamical rather than geometrical.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation numerically with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector radial potentials for neutrons in 208Pb, 40Ca, and 48Ca, fitting the potential parameters to reproduce experimental single-particle energies. They then varied the diffuseness, radius, and depth of the Σ = S+V potential systematically and computed the energy splittings of pseudospin partner states. They also evaluated the expectation values of the kinetic term, pseudospin-orbit term, Darwin-like term, and Σ potential separately from a Schroedinger-like equation for the lower Dirac component, checking the sum rule that relates these contributions.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the fitted Woods-Saxon parameters were R = 7 fm, Δ0 = 650 MeV, Σ0 = -66 MeV, and a = 0.6 fm.
- For 40Ca, the fitted parameters were R = 3.8 fm, a = 0.8 fm, Σ0 = -78 MeV, Δ0 = 770 MeV; calculated energies for the (2s1/2, 1d3/2) pseudospin partners were -16.0 MeV and -15.1 MeV (experimental: -18.2 MeV and -15.6 MeV).
- For 48Ca, the fitted parameters were R = 4.0 fm, a = 0.8 fm, Σ0 = -64 MeV, Δ0 = 650 MeV; calculated energies for the same pseudospin partners were -12.6 MeV and -12.2 MeV (experimental: -12.4 MeV and -12.4 MeV).
- The pseudospin energy splitting in 40Ca was 0.9 MeV; in 48Ca it decreased to 0.4 MeV.
- Varying the diffuseness a from about 0.5 fm to 0.8 fm caused the pseudospin splittings in 208Pb to decrease and eventually change sign (level crossing).
- Varying the radius R from about 6.5 fm to 8.0 fm caused the pseudospin splittings to increase, with level crossing observed for the (2f5/2, 3p3/2) doublet.
- Varying |Σ0| from about 55 MeV to 75 MeV showed that decreasing |Σ0| reduces the splitting; for sufficiently low |Σ0|, level inversion occurred for deeper doublets.
- Keeping the product |Σ0|R2 fixed while varying R and Σ0 kept the pseudospin splittings almost constant.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic interpretation of pseudospin symmetry initiated by Ginocchio (1997), who showed that exact pseudospin symmetry requires Σ = S+V = 0, and by Meng et al. (1998), who related the symmetry to dΣ/dr = 0. The authors demonstrate that neither condition is met in real nuclei; instead, the near degeneracy results from a dynamical cancellation among terms in the lower-component equation, making pseudospin a dynamical symmetry in the sense defined by Arima. The study enables a systematic understanding of why pseudospin splittings differ between neutrons and protons (isospin asymmetry) and how they vary with mass number, surface diffuseness, and nuclear radius across isotopic chains such as Sn and Zr.
