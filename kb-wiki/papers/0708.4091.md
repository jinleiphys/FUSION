# Non-Abelian Global Strings at Chiral Phase Transition

> We construct non-Abelian global string solutions in the U(N)_L x U(N)_R linear sigma model. These strings are the most fundamental objects which are expected to form during the chiral phase transitions, because the Abelian eta' string is marginally decomposed into N of them. We point out Nambu-Goldstone modes of CP^{N-1} for breaking of U(N)_V arise around a non-Abelian vortex.

## Key claim

The paper constructs non-Abelian global string solutions in the U(N)_L × U(N)_R linear sigma model, claiming these are the most fundamental objects expected to form during chiral phase transitions because the Abelian eta' string can be marginally decomposed into N of them without energy cost, but only in the absence of the anomaly term.

## Method

The authors solve the static field equations of the linear sigma model for an N×N complex scalar field matrix Φ, using axially symmetric ansätze with multiple winding numbers. They numerically compute profile functions for the (1,0,0) and (1,1,0) vortex configurations in the N=3 model, rescaling to dimensionless variables with κ = λ₁/λ₂. The solutions are obtained by shooting methods with boundary conditions at small and large radius.

## Key numbers

- The tension of a non-Abelian (1,0,...,0) string is E = const. + 2π v log Λ, which is 1/N of the Abelian eta' string tension E = const. + 2π N v log Λ
- The transverse size of the strings is of order x ∼ 1 in rescaled units (x ∼ m̂⁻¹ where m̂ = √(-m²))
- For the (1,0,0)-vortex, the asymptotic expansion coefficients are a₃ = (1/8)(4κ b₀² - 1)a₁ and b₂ = (1/4)[2(1+2κ)b₀² - 1]b₀
- For the (1,1,0)-vortex, the asymptotic coefficients are a₃ = (1/8)(2κ b₀² - 1)a₁ and b₂ = (1/4)[2(1+κ)b₀² - 1]b₀

## Context

This work extends the seminal paper by Balachandran and Digal (2002) who first pointed out the existence of non-Abelian global strings but only constructed solutions with domain walls in the presence of the anomaly, not pure string solutions without it. The paper also builds on studies of non-Abelian local strings in supersymmetric QCD by Auzzi et al. (2003) and the moduli matrix formalism by Isozumi et al. (2004). The results enable future calculations of string interactions, radiation signatures detectable in heavy-ion colliders or the early Universe, and extensions to include the anomaly term and pion masses.
