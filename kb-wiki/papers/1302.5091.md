# GFMC calculations of electromagnetic moments and M1 transitions in $A\leq 9$ nuclei

> We present recent Green's function Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments and M1 transitions in $A \leq 9$ nuclei, which include corrections arising from two-body meson-exchange electromagnetic currents. Two-body effects provide significant corrections to the calculated observables, bringing them in excellent agreement with the experimental data. In particular, we find that two-body corrections are especially large in the A=9, $T=3/2$ systems, in which they account for up to $\sim 20%$ ($\sim 40 %$) of the total predicted value for the $^9$Li ($^9$C) magnetic moment.

## Key claim

Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations of magnetic moments and M1 transitions in A ≤ 9 nuclei, which include two-body meson-exchange current corrections, bring the theoretical predictions into excellent agreement with experimental data; however, the two-body corrections are especially large in the A=9, T=3/2 systems, where they account for up to ∼20% of the total predicted value for ⁹Li and ∼40% for ⁹C magnetic moments.

## Method

The authors used Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) methods to compute nuclear wave functions from a Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne-v₁₈ two-nucleon potential and Illinois-7 three-nucleon potential. Electromagnetic transition matrix elements were evaluated between these wave functions using current operators that include both the standard one-body impulse approximation (convection and spin-magnetization terms) and two-body meson-exchange currents constructed within two frameworks: standard nuclear physics approach (SNPA) and pionful chiral effective field theory (χEFT) up to N3LO. The χEFT currents involve low-energy constants, some fixed to NN scattering data and others fitted to the deuteron, ³He, and ³H magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

- Two-body corrections account for ∼20% of the total predicted magnetic moment for ⁹Li in the A=9, T=3/2 systems
- Two-body corrections account for ∼40% of the total predicted magnetic moment for ⁹C in the A=9, T=3/2 systems
- The χEFT contact currents use a cutoff Λ = 600 MeV in the regularization function exp(-Q⁴/Λ⁴)
- The MEC SNPA (χEFT) correction provides ∼20% (∼30%) of the total calculated isovector contribution for the A=9, T=3/2 nuclei's magnetic moments

## Context

This work extends previous GFMC calculations by Pastore et al. (2012) and builds on the Argonne-v₁₈ potential from Wiringa, Stoks, and Schiavilla (1995) and the Illinois-7 three-nucleon potential from Pieper (2008). The two-body current operators are constructed within the SNPA framework described by Marcucci et al. (2005, 2008) and the χEFT formulation developed by Pastore et al. (2008, 2009) and Piarulli et al. (2012). The results enable improved predictions for electromagnetic observables in light nuclei and provide a test of chiral effective field theory currents against the more traditional SNPA approach, with the χEFT model generally giving better agreement for isoscalar magnetic moments.
