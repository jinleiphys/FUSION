# Accurate bulk properties of nuclei from $A = 2$ to $\infty$ from potentials with $Δ$ isobars

> We optimize $Δ$-full nuclear interactions from chiral effective field theory. The low-energy constants of the contact potentials are constrained by two-body scattering phase shifts, and by properties of bound-state of $A=2$ to $4$ nucleon systems and nuclear matter. The pion-nucleon couplings are taken from a Roy-Steiner analysis. The resulting interactions yield accurate binding energies and radii for a range of nuclei from $A=16$ to $A=132$, and provide accurate equations of state for nuclear matter and realistic symmetry energies. Selected excited states are also in agreement with data.

## Key claim

The authors claim that chiral interactions including Delta isobar degrees of freedom, when optimized against both few-nucleon observables and nuclear matter properties, can accurately reproduce binding energies (within about 2%) and radii for nuclei from A=16 to A=132, but the potentials still fail to explain the unusually large charge radii of neutron-rich calcium isotopes.

## Method

The authors optimized Delta-full chiral effective field theory interactions at NLO and NNLO with momentum cutoffs of 450 MeV and 394 MeV, constraining low-energy constants by nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts from the Granada partial wave analysis up to 200 MeV laboratory energy, bound-state observables of A=2 to 4 systems computed with the no-core shell model, and nuclear matter saturation properties computed with coupled-cluster methods in the CCD(T) approximation. The pion-nucleon couplings c1, c2, c3, and c4 were held fixed at central values from a Roy-Steiner analysis. Heavier nuclei up to A=132 were computed using coupled-cluster singles, doubles, and leading triples (CCSDT-1) starting from spherical Hartree-Fock states.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for Delta NNLO_GO(450): rho_0 = 0.169 fm^{-3}; for Delta NNLO_GO(394): rho_0 = 0.163 fm^{-3}
- Symmetry energy S_0 = 32.0 MeV with slope L = 65.2 for Delta NNLO_GO(450); S_0 = 31.5 MeV with L = 58.4 for Delta NNLO_GO(394)
- Binding energy of O-16: 128.1(23) MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(450) and 127.5(19) MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(394), compared to experimental 127.62 MeV
- Binding energy of Ca-40: 348(7)(1) MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(450) and 346(6) MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(394), compared to experimental 342.05 MeV
- Binding energy of Sn-132: 1043(20)(30) MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(394), compared to experimental 1102.84 MeV
- Binding energy of He-4: 28.2975 MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(450) and 28.3028 MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(394), compared to experimental 28.2957 MeV
- Excited state O-16 3^-_1: 6(1) MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(450) and 5.6(3)(1) MeV for Delta NNLO_GO(394), compared to experimental 6.13 MeV

## Context

This work extends the approach of Ekstrom (2017) by including nuclear matter properties as calibration data and optimizing interactions with Delta isobar degrees of freedom, addressing the long-standing difficulty of chiral potentials to simultaneously reproduce binding energies, charge radii, and nuclear matter saturation. The resulting potentials improve upon NNLO_sat (Ekstrom 2015), which accurately described binding energies and radii up to calcium isotopes but was less accurate for spectra, and upon the 1.8/2.0(EM) interaction (Hebeler 2011), which reproduced binding energies but yielded too small radii. The new potentials enable ab initio calculations of medium-mass and heavy nuclei up to tin isotopes with controlled uncertainties, and have already been applied to open-shell and deformed nuclei such as F-29, Ar-40, and neon and magnesium isotopes.
