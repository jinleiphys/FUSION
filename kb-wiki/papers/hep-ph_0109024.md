# Relations between N and Delta electromagnetic form factors

> The inclusion of two-body exchange currents in the constituent quark model leads to several new relations between the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon and Delta(1232). These are:(i) the neutron charge form factor can be expressed as the difference between proton and Delta+ charge form factors, and (ii) the N->Delta charge quadrupole (C2) transition form factor is connected to the charge monopole (C0) form factor of the neutron. The latter relation is used to estimate the charge radius of constituent quarks. Furthermore, we find that exchange currents do not modify the SU(6) relation between the magnetic N->Delta and the magnetic neutron form factors. Consequently, after including exchange currents, the C2/M1 ratio in the N->Delta transition can be expressed as a ratio of the elastic charge and magnetic form factors of the neutron.

# Relations between N and Delta electromagnetic form factors

## Key claim

The paper claims that two-body exchange currents in the constituent quark model produce new relations between nucleon and Delta(1232) electromagnetic form factors, specifically that the neutron charge form factor equals the difference between proton and Delta+ charge form factors, and that the N→Delta charge quadrupole (C2) transition form factor is directly connected to the neutron charge monopole (C0) form factor. The hedge is that these relations hold when three-body operators and strange quark loops are neglected, and that the C2/M1 ratio prediction agrees "astonishingly well" with direct pion electroproduction data but relies on the specific exchange current model.

## Method

The author evaluates one-body and two-body quark charge and current operators between three-quark wave functions in a constituent quark model that includes gluon and pion exchange currents. The two-body operators correspond to Feynman diagrams for photon coupling to quark-antiquark pair exchange currents, which are required to satisfy current conservation. The resulting form factor relations are derived by comparing the multipole expansions of the charge and current operators for nucleon and Delta states, and the C2/M1 ratio is obtained by combining the charge quadrupole and magnetic dipole transition form factor relations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron charge radius: r_n^2 = -b^2 * (M_Delta - M_N)/M_N, where M_Delta - M_N is the N-Delta mass splitting
- Proton charge radius: r_p^2 = b^2 + r_gammaq^2 + (b^2/(2 m_q)) * (delta_g(b) - delta_pi(b))
- Delta+ charge radius: r^2_Delta+ = b^2 + r^2_gammaq + (b^2/(6 m_q)) * (5 delta_g(b) - delta_pi(b))
- N→Delta quadrupole transition moment: Q_{p→Delta+} = r_n^2 / sqrt(2)
- Intrinsic quadrupole moment from neutron charge radius: Q_0^p = -Q_0^{Delta+} = -r_n^2 = +0.113 fm^2
- N→Delta quadrupole transition radius: r^2_{Q, p→Delta+} = 0.84(21) fm^2 from fit to G_C^n data
- Constituent quark charge radius extracted: r^2_gammaq = 0.64 fm^2
- Fourth moment of neutron charge distribution: r_n^4 = -0.32 fm^4
- Delta magnetic moments (quark model): Delta++ = 5.28 mu_N, Delta+ = 2.64 mu_N, Delta0 = 0.000 mu_N, Delta- = -2.64 mu_N
- N→Delta transition magnetic moment (quark model): mu_{p→Delta+} = 2.70 mu_N
- Experimental range for mu_{p→Delta+}: (3.5-4.2) mu_N
- Experimental range for mu_{Delta++}: (3.7-7.5) mu_N

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model with exchange currents developed by Buchmann (1991) and Buchmann (1997) to derive new relations between nucleon and Delta electromagnetic form factors. The relations are shown to be consistent with the general SU(6) spin-flavor symmetry analysis of Dillon and Morpurgo (1999), who proved that the charge radius relation r_p^2 - r_Delta+^2 = r_n^2 is a general consequence of SU(6) and quark-gluon dynamics when three-body operators are neglected. The paper enables predictions of the C2/M1 ratio in the N→Delta transition directly from elastic neutron form factor data, which can be tested against pion electroproduction experiments such as those by Bartsch et al. (1999) and earlier measurements. The work also provides a method to extract the constituent quark charge radius from the N→Delta quadrupole transition radius, which is measurable in future experiments.
