# Rescattering in Meson Photoproduction off Few Body Systems

> Exclusive reactions induced at high momentum transfer in few body systems provide us with an original way to study the production and propagation of hadrons in cold nuclear matter. In very well defined parts of the phase space, the reaction amplitude develops a logarithmic singularity. It is on solid ground since it depends only on on-shell elementary amplitudes and on low momentum components of the nuclear wave function. This is the best window to study the propagation of exotic configurations of hadrons such as, for instance, the onset of color transparency. It may appear earlier in meson photo-production reactions, more particularly in the strange sector, than in more classical quasi elastic scattering of electrons. More generally, those reactions provide us with the best tool to determine the cross section of the scattering of various hadrons (strange particles, vector mesons) from the nucleon and to access the production of possible exotic states.

## Key claim

Exclusive meson photoproduction off few-body systems, particularly in kinematics where the reaction amplitude develops a logarithmic singularity, provides a more controlled environment to search for color transparency than semi-exclusive electron scattering, because the rescattering peak is a well-defined structure whose suppression with increasing momentum transfer would signal the effect, rather than a change in a flat attenuation level.

## Method

The author computes the fully differential cross section for the $^2$H$(\gamma,\pi^-p)p$ reaction using a diagrammatic approach that includes quasi-free production, pion-nucleon rescattering, and nucleon-nucleon rescattering amplitudes. The elementary photoproduction amplitude uses a Regge model with saturating trajectories, the deuteron wave function is taken from the Paris potential, and the high-energy $\pi N$ and $NN$ scattering amplitudes are parameterized as purely absorptive with forward slopes. The calculation is implemented in a Monte Carlo code that simulates events within the fiducial acceptance of the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab.

## Key numbers

- The elementary $n(\gamma,\pi^-)p$ cross section is computed at $E_{\gamma}=4$ GeV, with the Regge model using a hadronic form factor cutoff mass $\Lambda=0.7$ GeV$^2$ (changed from 0.8 GeV$^2$ in the original reference).
- The $\pi N$ total cross section above $Q_s\sim 2$ GeV is taken as $\sigma_{\pi^-p}=30$ mb, with slope parameter $\beta_{\pi}=6$ GeV$^{-2}$.
- The mass of the fast $\pi p$ pair is kept constant at $W=\sqrt{(p_2+p_{\pi})^2}=2.896$ GeV, corresponding to a 4 GeV photon on a nucleon at rest.
- The four-momentum transfer is kept constant at $t=(k-p_{\pi})^2=-3$ GeV$^2$ for the coplanar kinematics study.
- At the top of the $\pi p$ rescattering peak, the photon energy is $E_{\gamma}=3.432$ GeV for one peak and $E_{\gamma}=5.436$ GeV for the other.
- The characteristic evolution distance for a 3 GeV proton is $l_f=1.5$ fm, estimated from $l_f=2\hbar c p/\Delta m^2$ with $\Delta m^2\sim 0.8$ GeV$^2$.
- The CLAS simulation uses a beam endpoint of $E_{\gamma}=6$ GeV, with the mass of the fast $p\pi$ pair restricted to $2.85<W_{p_2\pi}<2.95$ GeV and momentum transfer $-t>1$ GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic approach of Laget (1981) for meson production in few-body systems to higher energies accessible at Jefferson Lab, and updates the nucleon sector already revisited in Laget (2005). It provides a baseline calculation for the $^2$H$(\gamma,\pi^-p)p$ reaction against which deviations from conventional nuclear physics, such as the onset of color transparency, can be measured. The method enables determination of scattering cross sections for unstable hadrons (hyperons, vector mesons) and access to exotic states, building on earlier studies of color transparency in $A(e,e'p)$ reactions (Ent et al., Onley et al.) and $A(e,e'\rho)$ reactions at Fermilab and DESY (HERMES).
