# Medium Modifications of Vector Mesons and NA60

> We confront different models for medium modifications of the electromagnetic correlation function to recent dimuon spectra from the NA60 collaboration for central In-In collisions at the CERN-SPS. In the low-mass region (M \lesssim 1.0 GeV), we evaluate medium modifications of the spectral properties of ρ-, ω- and ϕ-mesons. In the intermediate-mass region (M>1.0 GeV), we employ schematic models for the mixing of vector and axialvector current correlators, which provide a mechanism for chiral-symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

The hadronic many-body approach, which predicts a strongly broadened ρ-meson spectral function, quantitatively describes the NA60 dimuon spectra in central In-In collisions at the SPS, while a dropping-mass scenario is disfavored unless a large component of free ρ decays can be argued for.

## Method

The authors convoluted thermal dilepton rates from several models over a space-time evolving fireball model for central In(158 AGeV)-In collisions, using a cylindrical expansion with transverse acceleration and a hadron-resonance gas equation of state. They compared the resulting dimuon spectra to NA60 data, evaluating medium modifications of ρ, ω, and φ mesons via hadronic many-body theory in the low-mass region (M ≲ 1.0 GeV), and employing schematic chiral mixing models for four-pion contributions in the intermediate-mass region (M > 1.0 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Initial QGP temperature: T₀ = 197 MeV
- Chemical freezeout conditions: (μ_N^ch, T_ch) = (232, 175) MeV
- Thermal freezeout time: t_fo = 7 fm/c at T_fo ≃ 120 MeV
- Total fireball entropy: S = 2630, translating to dN_ch/dy ≃ 195
- Transverse acceleration: a_⟂ = 0.08 c²/fm; initial transverse radius: r_⟂ = 5.15 fm
- In-medium ω-meson width: Γ_ω^med ≃ 100 MeV
- Assumed in-medium φ-meson width: Γ_φ^med ≃ 80 MeV
- Strangeness-suppression factor: γ_s ≃ 0.75
- Chiral mixing parameter from pion tadpole integral: ε = (1/2) I_π(T, μ_π) / I_π(T_c, μ_π=0)
- Dropping-mass parameterization: m_ρ* = m_ρ (1 - C ρ_B/ρ_0)[1 - (T/T_c)²]^α with C = 0.15 and α = 0.3

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic many-body calculations by Rapp and Wambach (1999) and confronts them with the first NA60 dimuon data from Damjanovic and Specht (2005), enabling discrimination between competing theoretical mechanisms for chiral-symmetry restoration. The results favor a "melting" ρ-meson with strong broadening over a dropping-mass scenario previously used to explain CERES/NA45 data (1990s), and support the notion of a reduced quark-hadron duality scale near T_c as proposed by Rapp (1999). Future studies within chirally symmetric models including baryon effects, and model-independent constraints from chiral sum rules (Klingl and Weise, 1993) or QCD sum rules (Ruppert, 2005), are needed to corroborate these findings.
