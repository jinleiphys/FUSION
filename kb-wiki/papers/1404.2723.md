# Limiting P-odd interactions of cosmic fields with electrons, protons and neutrons

> We propose methods for extracting limits on the strength of P-odd interactions of pseudoscalar and pseudovector cosmic fields with electrons, protons and neutrons. Candidates for such fields are dark matter (including axions) and dark energy, as well as several more exotic sources described by standard-model extensions. Calculations of parity nonconserving amplitudes and atomic electric dipole moments induced by these fields are performed for H, Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Ba+, Tl, Dy, Fr, and Ra+. From these calculations and existing measurements in Dy, Cs and Tl, we constrain the interaction strengths of the parity-violating static pseudovector cosmic field to be 7*10^(-15) GeV with an electron, and 3*10^(-8) GeV with a proton.

## Key claim

The paper derives limits on the strength of parity-violating interactions of a static pseudovector cosmic field with electrons and protons by combining new atomic-structure calculations with existing experimental data, but these limits apply only to the temporal component of such a field and assume the field is static rather than oscillatory.

## Method

The authors calculated atomic structure coefficients \(K_{\rm PNC}\) and \(K_{\rm EDM}\) for H, Li, Na, K, Rb, Cs, Ba+, Tl, Dy, Fr, and Ra+ using the correlation potential method and time-dependent Hartree-Fock method for single-valence systems, and a configuration interaction method for the nearly degenerate levels in Dy. These coefficients link the fundamental coupling constants of pseudoscalar and pseudovector cosmic fields to observable parity nonconserving (PNC) amplitudes and atomic electric dipole moments (EDMs). The calculated coefficients were then combined with existing experimental PNC measurements in Cs, Tl, and Dy to extract limits on the static pseudovector field coupling to electrons and protons.

## Key numbers

- Limit on static pseudovector cosmic field interaction strength with electrons: \(|b_0^e| < 7 \times 10^{-15}\) GeV, derived from Dy measurements.
- Limit on static pseudovector cosmic field interaction strength with protons: \(|b_0^p| < 3.1 \times 10^{-8}\) GeV, derived from Cs measurements.
- Calculated \(K_{\rm PNC}\) coefficient for Cs \(6s\)-\(7s\) transition: \(0.256(5) \times 10^{-6}\) a.u.
- Calculated \(K_{\rm PNC}\) coefficient for Tl \(6p_{1/2}\)-\(6p_{3/2}\) transition: \(0.22(5) \times 10^{-6}\) a.u.
- Calculated matrix element \(\langle B| \gamma^5 |A \rangle\) for Dy: \(0.7(2) \times 10^{-8} b_0\) a.u. = \(50(20) b_0\) MHz.
- Existing limits on spatial components of static cosmic fields: \(|\mathbf{b}| < 1.3 \times 10^{-31}\) GeV for electrons (Heckel 2008), \(6 \times 10^{-32}\) GeV for protons (Brown 2010), and \(8.4 \times 10^{-34}\) GeV for neutrons (Allmendinger 2014).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for using atomic systems to probe dark matter and dark energy candidates, including axions, by calculating the parity-violating effects of pseudoscalar and pseudovector cosmic fields. The results are complementary to existing limits on spatial components of such fields from spin-gravity experiments (Heckel 2008, Brown 2010, Allmendinger 2014) and to conventional atomic PNC tests of the electroweak theory (Wieman 1997, Dzuba 2012). The method enables future experiments to search for oscillating PNC amplitudes and EDMs, which could determine the mass and coupling constants of cosmic field particles, and can be directly applied to extract limits for neutrons when experimental data from ongoing Yb measurements (Tsigutkin 2009, 2010) become available.
