# The axial anomaly in QCD at finite temperature

> We study flavor mixing and the axial anomaly in QCD at zero and finite temperature. Using the instanton liquid model, we show that the strength of the anomaly is essentially unchanged near the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration. We demonstrate that nevertheless, chiral symmetry restoration has important consequences for the eta and eta prime. In particular, the strange and non-strange components of the eta unmix near T_c. The anomaly does not affect the strange eta, so we expect a light purely strange pseudoscalar meson near the phase transition.

# The axial anomaly in QCD at finite temperature

## Key claim

Using the instanton liquid model, the strength of the axial anomaly (measured by the expectation value of the 't Hooft operator) is essentially unchanged near the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration, and even peaks near T_c; however, chiral symmetry restoration still causes the strange and non-strange components of the eta to unmix, producing a light purely strange pseudoscalar meson near the phase transition, contrary to scenarios where the anomaly itself disappears.

## Method

The author studies flavor mixing and the U(1)_A anomaly in QCD at zero and finite temperature using the instanton liquid model, comparing random (RILM), quenched (QILM), and unquenched (IILM) instanton ensembles. Temporal correlation functions for the singlet and octet pseudoscalar currents are computed at various temperatures, and the spectrum of the Dirac operator is determined for N_f=2 at several temperatures and quark masses. The temperature dependence of the 't Hooft operator expectation value and the quark condensate are extracted from finite-temperature instanton ensembles obtained in prior work (Schaefer and Shuryak, 1996).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration: T_c ≃ 140 MeV
- At T=126 MeV (near T_c), the mass of the strange component of the eta is m_η_S = (0.420 ± 0.120) GeV and the non-strange component mass is m_η_NS = (1.250 ± 0.400) GeV, assuming ideal mixing above T_c and a perturbative continuum threshold at 1 GeV
- At zero temperature in the streamline ensemble, the eta mass is m_η = (0.66 ± 0.12) GeV with a mixing angle θ = (1 ± 3)°
- The eta-prime mass in the streamline ensemble is m_η' ≃ 2 GeV
- Quark masses used in finite-temperature simulations: m_u = m_d = 22 MeV, m_s = 155 MeV
- The ratio (m_u + m_d)/(2m_s) = 1/7 in the simulation, about three times larger than the physical mass ratio

## Context

This work extends the instanton liquid model approach to the eta-eta' system at finite temperature, building on earlier studies of the anomaly at finite temperature by Pisarski and Wilczek (1984), and more recent work by Shuryak (1994), Kharzeev, Kharzeev and Lee (1996), and Huang and Wang (1996). The author argues that contrary to the common expectation from effective lagrangians that the anomaly weakens near T_c (based on semi-classical estimates of the instanton density), the instanton liquid model shows the anomaly persists. This enables predictions for a light strange pseudoscalar meson near the phase transition, relevant for strangeness production in relativistic heavy ion collisions at AGS and CERN.
