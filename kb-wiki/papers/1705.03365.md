# Dynamical dissociation of quarkonia by wave function decoherence

> We investigate the real-time evolution of quarkonium bound states in a quark-gluon plasma in one dimension using an improved QCD based stochastic potential model. This model describes the quarkonium dynamics in terms of a Schrödinger equation with an in-medium potential and two noise terms encoding the residual interactions between the heavy quarks and the medium. The probabilities of bound states in a static medium and in a boost-invariantly expanding quark-gluon plasma are discussed. We draw two conclusions from our results: One is that the outcome of the stochastic potential model is qualitatively consistent with the experimental data in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The other is that the noise plays an important role in order to describe quarkonium dynamics in medium, in particular it causes decoherence of the quarkonium wave function. The effectiveness of decoherence is controlled by a new length scale $l_{\rm corr}$. It represents the noise correlation length and its effect has not been included in existing phenomenological studies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that wave function decoherence, driven by a finite noise correlation length \(l_{\rm corr}\) in a stochastic potential model, provides an important dynamical mechanism for quarkonium suppression in a quark-gluon plasma, and that this effect has not been included in existing phenomenological studies. The authors hedge that their results are from a simplified one-dimensional model and are qualitatively, not quantitatively, consistent with experimental data.

## Method

The authors numerically solved a stochastic Schrödinger equation for a heavy quark-antiquark pair in one dimension, using an operator splitting method with a Debye-screened potential and a Gaussian-correlated noise term. The noise correlation length \(l_{\rm corr}\) was varied to study its effect on the survival probability of quarkonium bound states, both in a static medium and in a boost-invariantly (Bjorken) expanding quark-gluon plasma with a time-dependent temperature \(T(t) = T_0 (t_0/(t_0+t))^{1/3}\). Ensemble averages over 1000 events were taken to compute occupation probabilities of the initial bound states.

## Key numbers

* Bottomonium ground state in a static QGP at \(T = 0.4\) GeV: the ground state occupation probability after 10 fm is sensitive to the noise correlation length, with a transition between weak and strong decoherence occurring at \(l_{\rm corr} \sim l_{\Psi} \sim 0.2\) fm.
* For bottomonium in a static QGP, the noise correlation length was varied over \(l_{\rm corr} = 0.04, 0.16, 0.32, 0.48, 0.96\) fm (Table 1), with the heavy quark mass \(M = 4.8\) GeV, Debye mass \(m_D = 0.4\) GeV, and noise strength \(\gamma = 0.012\) GeV.
* For bottomonium in a Bjorken-expanding QGP, the initial temperature was \(T_0 = 0.4\) GeV and the QGP formation time was \(t_0 = 1\) fm; the noise correlation length was set to \(l_{\rm corr} = 1/T\) and the noise strength to \(\gamma = 0.3 T\) (Table 2).
* For charmonium in a Bjorken-expanding QGP, the heavy quark mass was \(M = 1.18\) GeV, with the same parameter scalings \(l_{\rm corr} = 1/T\) and \(\gamma = 0.3 T\) (Table 2).

## Context

This work extends the stochastic potential model of Akamatsu (2011) and the open quantum system approach to quarkonia from QCD by Akamatsu (2014) by explicitly demonstrating the role of a finite noise correlation length in wave function decoherence. It contrasts with prior phenomenological studies that used a complex potential (e.g., Strickland 2011, Krouppa 2015) or only a screened potential without noise, showing that the complex potential underestimates the survival probability while the screened potential alone overestimates it. The results enable future three-dimensional simulations with more realistic QGP evolution and extensions to color octet states, as discussed in Akamatsu (2014).
