# $R_{AA}$ of $J/ψ$ near mid-rapidity in heavy ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV

> We build up a model to reproduce the experimentally measured $R_{AA}$ of $J/ψ$ near midrapidty in Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. The model takes into account the $J/ψ$ suppression from the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas as well as the nuclear absorption of primordial charmonia and the regeneration effects at the hadronization stage, and hence is a generalization of the two component model introduced by Grandchamp and Rapp. The improvements in this work are twofold; the addition of the initial local temperature profile and a consistent use of QCD NLO formula for both the dissociation cross section in the hadron gas and the thermal decay widths in the quark-gluon plasma for the charmonium states. The initial local temperature profile is determined from the assumption that the local entropy density is proportional to a formula involving the number densities of the number of participants and of the binary collisions that reproduces the multiplicities of charged particles at chemical freeze-out. The initial local temperature profile brings about a kink in the $R_{AA}$ curve due to the initial melting of $J/ψ$. The initially formed fireball, composed of weakly interacting quarks and gluons with thermal masses that are extracted from lattice QCD, follows an isentropic expansion with cylindrical symmetry. The fit reproduces well the Au+Au as well as the Cu+Cu data. The same method is applied to predict the $R_{AA}$ expected from the Pb+Pb collision at LHC energy.

## Key claim

The paper presents a generalized two-component model for $J/\psi$ suppression that reproduces experimental $R_{AA}$ data in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV, but only under the assumption that the initial local temperature profile creates a kink in the $R_{AA}$ curve due to initial melting of $J/\psi$ when the local temperature exceeds the dissociation temperature.

## Method

The model generalizes the two-component model of Grandchamp and Rapp by adding an initial local temperature profile determined from the assumption that local entropy density is proportional to a linear combination of the number densities of participants and binary collisions, reproducing charged particle multiplicities at chemical freeze-out. The fireball, composed of weakly interacting quarks and gluons with thermal masses extracted from lattice QCD, follows an isentropic expansion with cylindrical symmetry. The authors use QCD NLO formulas consistently for both the dissociation cross section in the hadron gas and the thermal decay widths in the quark-gluon plasma for charmonium states, with the coupling constant related to the screening mass and sequential melting temperatures rather than being a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section used is $\sigma_{in}=42$ mb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV
- The nuclear absorption cross section of primordial charmonia by a nucleon is $\sigma_{nuc}=1.5$ mb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV
- Thermalization time is taken as $\tau_0=0.6$ fm/c
- The lifetime of the quark-gluon plasma phase in head-on Au+Au collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV is 5.63 fm/c, with a mixed phase lasting 1.44 fm/c
- The temperature for chemical freeze-out is set at 161 MeV
- The transverse acceleration of the quark-gluon plasma is set to $a_{\bot}=0.1$ $c^2$/fm, turned off when transverse velocity reaches 0.6 c
- The parameter $x$ in the entropy formula is 0.11 at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV
- The Woods-Saxon parameters for Au nucleus are $r_o=6.38$ fm and $C=0.535$ fm
- The transverse radius of quark-gluon plasma in head-on collision is about 7.0 fm at thermalization time

## Context

This work extends the two-component model introduced by Grandchamp and Rapp (2002) by incorporating an initial local temperature profile and consistently applying QCD NLO formulas for dissociation cross sections in both the quark-gluon plasma and hadron gas phases. The model reproduces experimental data from the PHENIX collaboration for both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at RHIC, and the same method is applied to predict $R_{AA}$ for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy. The approach enables discrimination between different charmonium melting temperatures by relating sudden drops in the $R_{AA}$ curve to the dissociation of excited charmonia ($\chi_c$, $\psi'$) and $J/\psi$ itself at distinct initial temperatures.
