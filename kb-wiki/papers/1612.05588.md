# Collective excitations of a quantized vortex in $^3P_2$ superfluids in neutron stars

> We discuss collective excitations (both fundamental and solitonic excitations) of quantized superfluid vortices in neutron $^3P_2$ superfluids, which likely exist in high density neutron matter such as neutron stars. Besides the well-known Kelvin modes (translational zero modes), we find a gapfull mode whose low-energy description takes the simple form of a double sine-Gordon model. The associated kink solution and its effects on spontaneous magnetization inside the vortex core are analyzed in detail.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a gapful (massive) collective mode in addition to the well-known gapless Kelvin modes on quantized vortices in neutron \(^3P_2\) superfluids; this mode is described by a double sine-Gordon model, but the abstract does not specify that the kink solutions are only \(2\pi\) kinks because the numerically evaluated coefficients yield \(|\lambda_1|/(4\lambda_2) > 1\), preventing additional local minima.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the Ginzburg-Landau (GL) equations for an axially symmetric integer vortex in a \(^3P_2\) superfluid in the weak-coupling limit, neglecting sixth-order terms and external magnetic fields. They introduce a constant phase \(\delta\) in the off-diagonal elements of the tensor order parameter and treat it as a slowly varying function of the \(z\)-coordinate, deriving an effective free energy functional via the moduli approximation. The resulting low-energy effective theory is a double sine-Gordon model, whose kink solutions are analyzed using a Bogomol'nyi-type method.

## Key numbers

- The coherence length \(\xi\) is approximately \(30\) fm.
- The dimensionless parameter \(\eta\) at spatial infinity is \(\eta = 6 - \sqrt{43} \sim -0.557\).
- The magnetic moment per unit length for vanishing \(\delta\) is \(m(z) \approx 4.196 \times 10^{-19} \, \text{MeV} \, \text{fm}^{-1} \, \text{G}^{-1} \approx 0.6 \times 10^{-27} \, \text{J} \, \text{T}^{-1} \, \text{fm}^{-1}\); the magnetic moment of a \(10\) fm string is comparable to the neutron magnetic moment.
- The numerically evaluated coefficients in the effective free energy are \(\lambda_1 = -4.889\), \(\lambda_2 = 0.411\), and \(\lambda_3 = 17.748\), yielding \(\lambda = 2\lambda_2/|\lambda_1| = 0.168735\).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of \(^3P_2\) vortex structure by Richardson (1972), Muzikar (1980), Sauls (1982), and Masuda (2015), which did not consider the phase parameter \(\delta\) or its associated collective excitations. The results enable future investigation of vortex dynamics in neutron star interiors, including the effect of kink solutions on spontaneous magnetization reversal in the vortex core, and can be generalized to include external magnetic fields and sixth-order terms in forthcoming work.
