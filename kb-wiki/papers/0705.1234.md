# Early Time Dynamics in Heavy Ion Collisions from AdS/CFT Correspondence

> We study the matter produced in heavy ion collisions assuming that this matter is strongly interacting and employing AdS/CFT correspondence to investigate its dynamics. At late proper times we show that Bjorken hydrodynamics solution, obtained recently by Janik and Peschanski using gauge-gravity duality [hep-th/0512162], can be singled out by simply requiring that the metric tensor is a real and single-valued function of the coordinates everywhere in the bulk, without imposing any constraints on the curvature invariant. At early proper times we use similar strategy to show that the energy density approaches a constant as proper time goes to zero. We therefore demonstrate that the strong coupling dynamics incorporates the isotropization transition in heavy ion collisions. By matching our early-time regime with the late-time one of Janik and Peschanski we estimate the isotropization time at RHIC to be approximately 0.3 fm/c, in good agreement with results of hydrodynamic simulations.

## Key claim

The authors show that requiring the metric tensor to be a real and single-valued function everywhere in the bulk (without imposing constraints on curvature invariants) forces the energy density to approach a constant as proper time goes to zero, demonstrating that strong-coupling dynamics incorporates an isotropization transition in heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors solve Einstein equations with a negative cosmological constant in AdS$_5$ space using the holographic renormalization technique, expanding the metric in Fefferman-Graham coordinates near the $z=0$ boundary. They assume a boost-invariant Bjorken geometry and impose the condition that the metric is a real and single-valued function of the scaling variable $u = z/\tau$ for all values, which constrains the allowed power $\Delta$ in the energy density scaling $\epsilon \sim \tau^\Delta$ to $\Delta = 0$ at early times.

## Key numbers

* The isotropization time at RHIC is estimated to be $\tau_{\text{iso}} \approx 0.3$ fm/c, obtained by matching the early-time constant energy density regime with the late-time Bjorken hydrodynamic solution.
* At late proper times, the energy density scales as $\epsilon(\tau) \propto \tau^{-4/3}$, characteristic of ideal Bjorken hydrodynamics.
* The allowed range for the power $\Delta$ in the energy density scaling $\epsilon \sim \tau^\Delta$ is $-4 \le \Delta \le 0$ from positivity conditions, and $-1 \le \Delta \le 0$ from finite total energy at early times.

## Context

This work extends the late-time AdS/CFT analysis of Janik and Peschanski (2005), who derived Bjorken hydrodynamics from gauge-gravity duality, by applying similar methods to early proper times. The authors demonstrate that the strong-coupling dynamics produces an anisotropic initial state with negative longitudinal pressure, similar to the Color Glass Condensate framework of Lappi (2003), and that isotropization necessarily occurs at intermediate times. This enables matching the early-time regime to late-time hydrodynamics to estimate the isotropization time, which agrees with hydrodynamic simulations of RHIC data.
