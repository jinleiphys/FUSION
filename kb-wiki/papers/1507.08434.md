# Radial Flow in Non-Extensive Thermodynamics and Study of Particle Spectra at LHC in the Limit of Small $(q-1)$

> We expand the Tsallis distribution in a Taylor series of powers of (q-1), where q is the Tsallis parameter, assuming q is very close to 1. This helps in studying the degree of deviation of transverse momentum spectra and other thermodynamic quantities from a thermalized Boltzmann distribution. After checking thermodynamic consistency, we provide analytical results for the Tsallis distribution in the presence of collective flow up to the first order of (q-1). The formulae are compared with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper expands the thermodynamically consistent Tsallis distribution in a Taylor series in powers of (q-1), assuming q is very close to 1, and provides analytical results for the Tsallis distribution with collective flow up to first order in (q-1); the expansion is valid only when the conditions |1-q|E/T < 1 and |1-q|(E/T)^2 < 2 are satisfied for first order, with stricter conditions for higher orders.

## Method

The authors performed a Taylor expansion of the Tsallis distribution function f^q (with the thermodynamically consistent form having an extra power of q) in powers of (q-1) up to second order, then derived analytical expressions for particle density, energy density, and pressure to first order in (q-1) with non-zero chemical potential. They incorporated radial flow by substituting E → p^μ u_μ in the expanded distribution and integrating over a space-like freeze-out surface with constant flow velocity β, obtaining an analytical formula for the invariant yield. The expanded distributions were fitted to ALICE data for π^+ in p-p collisions at √s = 0.9 TeV and π^- in Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV using the MINUIT package.

## Key numbers

- For the Tsallis fit to π^+ in p-p collisions at √s = 0.9 TeV: T = 70.8 MeV, q = 1.1474, spherical radius = 4.81 fm
- For the Boltzmann fit to π^+ in p-p collisions at √s = 0.9 TeV: T = 150.2 MeV, radius = 2.65 fm
- For the Boltzmann plus first order in (q-1) fit to π^+ in p-p collisions at √s = 0.9 TeV: T = 138.4 MeV, q = 1.035, radius = 2.80 fm
- For the Boltzmann plus first and second orders in (q-1) fit to π^+ in p-p collisions at √s = 0.9 TeV: T = 121.2 MeV, q = 1.065, radius = 3.09 fm
- For the Tsallis distribution with flow (first order in (q-1)) fit to π^- in (0-5)% Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV: β = 0.609, T = 146 MeV, q = 1.030, radius = 29.8 fm
- For (q-1) = 0.15 with T = 0.1 GeV, the expansion is reliable up to E ≈ 0.3 GeV; for (q-1) = 0.01 with T = 0.1 GeV, the expansion is reliable up to E ≈ 1.4 GeV
- The expansion in (q-1) deviates from the full Tsallis distribution by about 10% for particle density and about 20% for energy density and pressure when (q-1) ≈ 0.1

## Context

This work extends the Tsallis distribution analysis pioneered by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations at RHIC and the ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations at LHC, building on the thermodynamically consistent formalism developed by Worku and Azmi. The Taylor expansion approach was previously considered by Osada and Wilk (2008) and Alberico and Lavagno (2009), but the present paper provides a more systematic analysis using a thermodynamically consistent form of the Tsallis distribution with an extra power of q. The inclusion of flow extends the Tsallis-Blast Wave formalism of Tang et al. (2009) by providing an analytical formula rather than a numerical treatment, and the results enable systematic study of how collective flow breaks m_T scaling and reduces the extracted q value, as previously noted by Dhananjay et al.
