# Bayesian modeling of the nuclear equation of state for neutron star tidal deformabilities and GW170817

> We present predictions for neutron star tidal deformabilities obtained from a Bayesian analysis of the nuclear equation of state, assuming a minimal model at high-density that neglects the possibility of phase transitions. The Bayesian posterior probability distribution is constructed from priors obtained from microscopic many-body theory based on realistic two- and three-body nuclear forces, while the likelihood functions incorporate empirical information about the equation of state from nuclear experiments. The neutron star crust equation of state is constructed from the liquid drop model, and the core-crust transition density is found by comparing the energy per baryon in inhomogeneous matter and uniform nuclear matter. From the cold $β$-equilibrated neutron star equation of state, we then compute neutron star tidal deformabilities as well as the mass-radius relationship. Finally, we investigate correlations between the neutron star tidal deformability and properties of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper presents predictions for neutron star tidal deformabilities from a Bayesian analysis that assumes a minimal model at high density, explicitly neglecting phase transitions to exotic matter such as hyperons or deconfined quark matter, meaning the results apply only if no such transitions occur.

## Method

The authors constructed a Bayesian posterior probability distribution for parameters of an energy density functional, using priors from chiral effective field theory calculations of symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter up to twice saturation density, and likelihood functions from empirical nuclear matter properties derived from 205 Skyrme interactions and symmetry energy constraints. The neutron star crust equation of state was computed using the liquid drop model, and the core-crust transition density was determined by comparing the energy per baryon in inhomogeneous and uniform matter. From the cold beta-equilibrated equation of state, the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

* The mean values for the symmetric nuclear matter expansion parameters were {a_i} = {-3.48 fm^2, 6.15 fm^2, -1.51 fm^2, 39.58 fm^2}, with covariance matrix elements ranging from 0.05 fm^4 to 696.53 fm^4.
* The mean values for the pure neutron matter expansion parameters were {b_i} = {-1.70 fm^2, 3.87 fm^2, 4.61 fm^2, 16.85 fm^2}, with covariance matrix elements ranging from 0.04 fm^4 to 227.42 fm^4.
* The theoretical uncertainty on the nuclear symmetry energy at n=2n_0 reached approximately 50 MeV.
* The correlation between K_sym and (3J-L) was R_xy = -0.955, with mean values <3J-L> = 43.1 MeV and <K_sym> = -112 MeV, and standard deviations sigma_{3J-L} = 6.02 MeV and sigma_{K_sym} = 40.3 MeV.
* The symmetry energy at saturation density was constrained to J = 31 +/- 1.5 MeV from the likelihood function.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of the nuclear equation of state by Lim and Holt (2018) and others, incorporating chiral effective field theory constraints from Hebeler (2010), Drischler (2014, 2016), and Holt (2017) together with empirical nuclear data from Skyrme interactions benchmarked by Dutra (2012). The results enable predictions for neutron star tidal deformabilities that can be compared directly to gravitational wave observations such as GW170817, and the method provides a framework for incorporating future constraints from the NICER mission and additional merger events.
