# Web-page on UrQMD Model Validation

> A WEB-page containing materials of comparing experimental data and UrQMD model calculations has been designed. The page provides its user with a variety of tasks solved with the help of the model, accuracy and/or quality of experimental data description, and so on. The page can be useful for new experimental data analysis, or new experimental research planning. The UrQMD model is cited in more than 272 publications. Only 44 of them present original calculations. Their main results on the model are presented on the page.

## Key claim

The paper describes the creation of a web page that compiles and organizes results from 44 publications containing original calculations with the UrQMD model, selected from over 272 publications that cite the model. The page is intended to support the second trend of Monte Carlo event generator application: analysis of new experimental data and planning of new investigations, rather than claiming the model itself is validated.

## Method

The authors searched the SPIRES electronic database of scientific publications for papers citing the two milestone UrQMD papers, reviewed all 272 publications found at the beginning of 2006, and selected 44 that presented original calculations using the model. Graphical materials from these selected papers were extracted and organized into a web page with 11 thematic sections (production cross sections, particle multiplicities, multiplicity ratios, rapidity distributions, mT-distributions, PT-distributions, flow, event-by-event fluctuations, dileptons/J/Psi production, and others), each subdivided into hadron-nucleon, hadron-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus interactions.

## Key numbers

- 272 publications cited the UrQMD model at the beginning of year 2006
- 44 of those publications presented original calculations with the model and were selected for the web page
- The model uses a hard equation of state; only the hard equation of state has been implemented into the current UrQMD model
- The model contains 55 different baryon species (including nucleon, delta and hyperon resonances with masses up to 2.25 GeV/c^2) and 32 different meson species
- Resonance formation cross sections are calculated from measured decay properties up to c.m. energies of 2.25 GeV/c^2 for baryon resonances and 1.7 GeV/c^2 for meson-meson and meson-baryon reactions
- The fragmentation function for leading nucleons uses parameters A=0.275 and B=0.42
- The model underestimates pion multiplicities and overestimates strange baryon and antiproton multiplicities in NN-interactions (see pages 1_3_11, 3_t89, 14_2)
- The K+/pi+ ratio in nucleus-nucleus interactions at energies larger than 10 GeV shows a problem that suggests additional transverse pressure not implemented in UrQMD 1.3

## Context

This work extends the utility of the UrQMD model (Bass et al., 1998; Bleicher et al., 1999) by providing a centralized, organized repository of validation results for experimentalists planning new measurements or analyzing data, particularly for the future PANDA and CBM experiments at GSI. The page enables users to quickly assess where the model agrees or disagrees with data across many observables, which is important because the model is cited in over 272 publications but only 44 contain original calculations. The compilation reveals specific systematic discrepancies, such as the model's failure to describe baryon rapidity distributions in pp-interactions and nucleus-nucleus collisions at energies above 6 GeV/nucleon, and the poor description of antiproton production in PbPb interactions at 160 GeV/nucleon, which may motivate further theoretical developments including the possible need for quark-gluon plasma formation or additional transverse pressure effects.
