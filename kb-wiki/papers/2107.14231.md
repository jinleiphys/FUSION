# Relativistic spin hydrodynamics with torsion and linear response theory for spin relaxation

> Using the second law of local thermodynamics and the first-order Palatini formalism, we formulate relativistic spin hydrodynamics for quantum field theories with Dirac fermions, such as QED and QCD, in a torsionful curved background. We work in a regime where spin density, which is assumed to relax much slower than other non-hydrodynamic modes, is treated as an independent degree of freedom in an extended hydrodynamic description. Spin hydrodynamics in our approach contains only three non-hydrodynamic modes corresponding to a spin vector, whose relaxation time is controlled by a new transport coefficient: the rotational viscosity. We study linear response theory and observe an interesting mode mixing phenomenon between the transverse shear and the spin density modes. We propose several field-theoretical ways to compute the spin relaxation time and the rotational viscosity, via the Green-Kubo formula based on retarded correlation functions.

## Key claim

Using the second law of local thermodynamics and the first-order Palatini formalism, the authors formulate relativistic spin hydrodynamics for quantum field theories with Dirac fermions (such as QED and QCD) in a torsionful curved background, treating spin density as an independent degree of freedom only in the regime where its relaxation time is much slower than other non-hydrodynamic modes.

## Method

The authors start from the Ward-Takahashi identity for local Lorentz symmetry in a torsionful background geometry, defining the spin current via variation of the QCD action with respect to the spin connection. They perform an entropy-current analysis in a general torsionful background to derive constitutive relations, introducing a new transport coefficient called the rotational viscosity. Linear response theory is then applied to obtain dispersion relations for dynamical modes, and Green-Kubo formulae for the spin relaxation time and rotational viscosity are derived from retarded correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation rate of spin modes is given by \(\Gamma_s = 2 \etas / \chi_s\), where \(\etas\) is the rotational viscosity and \(\chi_s\) is the spin susceptibility.
- The spin hydrodynamics contains only three non-hydrodynamic modes corresponding to a spin vector.
- The regime of validity requires \(\omega \gtrsim \Gamma_s\) (where spin modes are independent) and \(\omega \ll \Gamma\) (where \(\Gamma\) is the relaxation rate of other non-hydrodynamic modes), which is possible only when \(\Gamma_s\) is parametrically smaller than \(\Gamma\).
- In a weakly coupled quark-gluon plasma with fermion mass \(M\) much larger than temperature \(T\), \(\Gamma_s\) is expected to be smaller than \(\Gamma\) by additional powers of \(T/M\).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of relativistic spin hydrodynamics based on the second law of thermodynamics (Hattori 2019, Fukushima 2020, Li 2020, She 2021, Gallegos 2021) and quantum kinetic theory (Florkowski 2017, Gao 2019, Hattori 2019, Li 2019, Yang 2020, Weickgenannt 2020, Liu 2020, Bhadury 2020, Shi 2020, Peng 2021). It also builds on the Hydro+ framework (Stephanov 2017) for including slow non-hydrodynamic modes. The paper enables computation of the spin relaxation time in relativistic plasmas via Green-Kubo formulae, which is directly relevant to interpreting spin polarization measurements in heavy-ion collisions at STAR (2017, 2019) and ALICE (2019).
