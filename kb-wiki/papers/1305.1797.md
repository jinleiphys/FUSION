# Elliptic flow and nuclear modification factors of D-mesons at FAIR in a Hybrid-Langevin approach

> The Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at the Facility for Anti-proton and Ion Research (FAIR) will provide new possibilities for charm-quark ($D$-meson) observables in heavy-ion collisions at low collision energies and high baryon densities. To predict the collective flow and nuclear modification factors of charm quarks in this environment, we apply a Langevin approach for the transport of charm quarks in the UrQMD (hydrodynamics + Boltzmann) hybrid model. Due to the inclusion of event-by-event fluctuations and a full (3+1) dimensional hydrodynamical evolution, the UrQMD hybrid approach provides a realistic evolution of the matter produced in heavy-ion collisions. As drag and diffusion coefficients we use a resonance approach for elastic heavy-quark scattering and assume a decoupling temperature of the charm quarks from the hot medium of $130\, \MeV$. Hadronization of the charm quarks to $D$-mesons by coalescence is included. Since the initial charm-quark distribution at FAIR is unknown, we utilize two different initial charm-quark distributions in our approach to estimate the uncertainty of these predictions. We present calculations of the nuclear modification factor, $R_{AA}$, as well as for the elliptic flow, $v_2$, in Pb+Pb collisions at $E_{lab}=25\,\text{AGeV}$. The different medium modifications of $D$-mesons and $\bar{D}$-mesons at high baryon-chemical potential are explored by modified drag- and diffusion-coefficients using the corresponding fugacity factor. Here we find a considerably larger medium modification for $\bar{\mathrm{D}}$- than for $D$-mesons.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that at FAIR energies, the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) and elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(D\)-mesons depend strongly on the unknown initial charm-quark momentum distribution, and that including a fugacity factor to account for high baryon chemical potential leads to a substantially larger medium modification for \(\bar{D}\)-mesons than for \(D\)-mesons, but only when using the resonance-model drag and diffusion coefficients.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic Langevin approach to simulate charm-quark transport through a bulk medium generated by the UrQMD hybrid model, which combines a (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic evolution with a Boltzmann transport afterburner. Drag and diffusion coefficients were taken from a resonance-scattering model assuming \(D\)-meson-like resonances in the quark-gluon plasma, and charm quarks were hadronized into \(D\)-mesons via a coalescence mechanism. Two different initial charm-quark transverse-momentum distributions (a HSD parametrization and a PYTHIA parametrization) were used to estimate uncertainties, and a fugacity factor \(e^{\pm \mu_B/T}\) was applied to the drag/diffusion coefficients for charm and anti-charm separately.

## Key numbers

* The calculations were performed for Pb+Pb collisions at \(E_{lab}=25\,\text{AGeV}\).
* The decoupling temperature of charm quarks from the hot medium was set to \(130\,\text{MeV}\).
* For the HSD initial-state parametrization, the elliptic flow \(v_2\) of \(D\)-mesons reached up to about \(6\%\) in the \(20\%\)-\(40\%\) centrality range.
* For the PYTHIA initial-state parametrization, the elliptic flow \(v_2\) reached up to about \(10\%\).
* When fugacity factors were included, the elliptic flow for \(\bar{D}\)-mesons reached up to \(15\%\).
* The initial charm-quark production in pp collisions at this energy is kinematically allowed only up to \(p_T\approx 2\,\text{GeV}\); results above \(p_T\approx 1.5\,\text{GeV}\) should be interpreted with caution.
* The HSD initial distribution used fit parameters \(A_1=0.870/\text{GeV}^2\) and \(A_2=3.062\).
* The PYTHIA initial distribution used fit parameters \(B_1=1.144\), \(B_2=-0.586\,\text{GeV}\), and \(B_3=0.646\,\text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends previous Langevin studies of heavy-quark transport at RHIC and LHC energies (van Hees 2007, He 2012) to the lower collision energies and high baryon densities accessible at FAIR, using a more realistic (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamical background than the simplified fireball models employed earlier. The predictions are intended for the Compressed Baryonic Matter (CBM) experiment at FAIR, and the authors note that the predicted difference between \(D\)-meson and \(\bar{D}\)-meson modification could discriminate between the resonance-scattering model and the \(T\)-Matrix approach (van Hees 2007) for calculating heavy-quark coupling to the QGP.
