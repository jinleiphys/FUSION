# Constraining the EOS of neutron-rich nuclear matter and properties of neutron stars with heavy-ion reactions

> Heavy-ion reactions especially those induced by radioactive beams provide useful information about the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, thus the Equation of State of neutron-rich nuclear matter, relevant for many astrophysical studies. The latest developments in constraining the symmetry energy at both sub- and supra-saturation densities from analyses of the isopsin diffusion and the $π^-/π^+$ ratio in heavy-ion collisions using the IBUU04 transport model are discussed. Astrophysical ramifications of the partially constrained symmetry energy on properties of neutron star crusts, gravitational waves emitted by deformed pulsars and the w-mode oscillations of neutron stars are presented briefly.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy is partially constrained by combining analyses of isospin diffusion data (probing sub-saturation densities) and the π⁻/π⁺ ratio in heavy-ion collisions (probing supra-saturation densities), but the constraints are model-dependent and the symmetry energy at densities above about 2ρ₀ may be much softer than the APR prediction, with the extracted slope parameter L = 88 ± 25 MeV from isospin diffusion alone.

## Method

The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model IBUU04 to analyze isospin diffusion data from MSU (Tsang et al.) and π⁻/π⁺ ratio data from FOPI/GSI at 0.4 AGeV beam energy. They varied the density dependence of the symmetry energy by adjusting the x parameter in a modified Gogny force (MDI interaction) and compared calculations with experimental data for reactions including ⁴⁰Ca+⁴⁰Ca, ⁹⁶Ru+⁹⁶Ru, ⁹⁶Zr+⁹⁶Zr, and ¹⁹⁷Au+¹⁹⁷Au. The constrained symmetry energy was then used to compute neutron star crust properties via dynamical and thermodynamical methods, and to calculate gravitational wave signatures from deformed pulsars and axial w-mode oscillations.

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter L = 88 ± 25 MeV extracted from isospin diffusion data using IBUU04 analysis.
- Maximum density reached in isospin diffusion reactions is about 1.2ρ₀.
- Maximum central density reached in π⁻/π⁺ reactions at 400 MeV/A is about 2.5ρ₀.
- Core-crust transition density ρₜ constrained between 0.040 fm⁻³ and 0.065 fm⁻³ using the experimentally constrained L values with the full EOS and dynamical method.
- Transition pressure Pₜ limited between 0.01 MeV/fm³ and 0.26 MeV/fm³ for the MDI interaction.
- Fiducial value of ρₜ ≈ 0.08 fm⁻³ and Pₜ ≈ 0.65 MeV/fm³ often used in the literature are significantly above the constrained ranges.
- Crustal fraction of moment of inertia ΔI/I > 0.014 from Vela pulsar glitch observations.
- Minimum radius constraint for Vela pulsar: R ≥ 4.7 + 4.0 M/M⊙ km, giving R ≥ 10.5 km for a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star.
- Neutron star quadrupole moment scaling: Φ₂₂,max = 2.4 × 10³⁸ g cm² (σ/10⁻²) (R/10 km)⁶·²⁶ (1.4 M⊙/M)¹·².
- Moment of inertia empirical relation: I ≈ (0.237 ± 0.008) MR² [1 + 4.2 (M km/M⊙ R) + 90 (M km/M⊙ R)⁴].

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining the nuclear symmetry energy from heavy-ion reactions, building on prior analyses by Tsang et al. (2004) for isospin diffusion and Reisdorf et al. (2007) for pion ratios. The partially constrained symmetry energy is applied to neutron star crust properties, following the theoretical framework of Lattimer and Prakash (2007) and the dynamical method of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971). The results enable tighter constraints on neutron star radii from pulsar glitch observations and provide predictions for gravitational wave signals from deformed pulsars and w-mode oscillations that can be tested by LIGO and VIRGO.
