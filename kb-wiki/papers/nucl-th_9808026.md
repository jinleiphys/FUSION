# The pseudo-spin symmetry in Zr and Sn isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line

> Based on the Relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory, the pseudo-spin approximation in exotic nuclei is investigated in Zr and Sn isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line. The quality of the pseudo-spin approximation is shown to be connected with the competition between the centrifugal barrier (CB) and the pseudo-spin orbital potential (PSOP). The PSOP depends on the derivative of the difference between the scalar and vector potentials $dV/dr$. If $dV/dr = 0$, the pseudo-spin symmetry is exact. The pseudo-spin symmetry is found to be a good approximation for normal nuclei and to become much better for exotic nuclei with highly diffuse potential, which have $dV/dr \sim 0$. The energy splitting of the pseudo-spin partners is smaller for orbitals near the Fermi surface (even in the continuum) than the deeply bound orbitals. The lower components of the Dirac wave functions for the pseudo-spin partners are very similar and almost equal in magnitude.

## Key claim

The pseudo-spin symmetry approximation is shown to be connected with the competition between the centrifugal barrier (CB) and the pseudo-spin orbital potential (PSOP), which depends on the derivative of the difference between the scalar and vector potentials \(dV/dr\); the symmetry becomes exact when \(dV/dr = 0\) and is found to be better for exotic nuclei with highly diffuse potentials where \(dV/dr \sim 0\), rather than being universally good for all nuclei.

## Method

The authors used the Relativistic Continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov (RCHB) theory with the non-linear Lagrangian parameter set NLSH to self-consistently calculate the scalar and vector potentials for Zr and Sn isotopes from the proton drip line to the neutron drip line. They solved the Dirac equation in the canonical basis of RCHB, examined the radial equations for the lower component of the Dirac spinor, and compared the effective centrifugal barrier and effective pseudo-spin orbital potential to analyze the energy splitting of pseudo-spin partner orbitals. Pairing correlations were included using a zero-range pairing force with strength renormalized by comparison with Gogny force D1S calculations.

## Key numbers

- In \(^{120}\)Zr, the single particle energy of \(3s_{1/2}\) is \(-6.00\) MeV and its partner \(2d_{3/2}\) is \(-5.86\) MeV, giving a splitting of \(0.14\) MeV; for the more deeply bound \(2s_{1/2}\) (\(-31.62\) MeV) and \(1d_{3/2}\) (\(-33.23\) MeV), the splitting is \(1.61\) MeV, larger by a factor of 10.
- In \(^{150}\)Sn, the single particle energies for the \(3s_{1/2}\) and \(2d_{3/2}\) partners are \(-9.645\) and \(-10.11\) MeV respectively; for \(2d_{5/2}\) and \(1g_{7/2}\) they are \(-11.74\) and \(-13.87\) MeV; for \(2p_{3/2}\) and \(1f_{5/2}\) they are \(-22.46\) and \(-25.50\) MeV; for \(2s_{1/2}\) and \(1d_{3/2}\) they are \(-33.63\) and \(-36.58\) MeV.
- In \(^{150}\)Sn, near the threshold, the \(3p_{3/2}\) and \(2f_{5/2}\) partners have energies \(-1.581\) and \(-1.031\) MeV, with a splitting \(\Delta E = (E_{\tilde lj=\tilde l-1/2} - E_{\tilde lj=\tilde l+1/2})/(2\tilde l+1)\) of \(-0.1100\) MeV; the \(2f_{7/2}\) and \(1h_{9/2}\) partners have energies \(-2.549\) and \(-2.620\) MeV, with a splitting of \(0.7889 \times 10^{-2}\) MeV.
- For the \(2s_{1/2}\) and \(1d_{3/2}\) partners, the pseudo-spin splitting in \(^{170}\)Sn is only half of that in \(^{96}\)Sn.

## Context

This work extends the previous investigation by Meng et al. (1998) on the pseudo-spin symmetry in real nuclei using RCHB theory, applying it to exotic Zr and Sn isotopes across the entire range from proton drip line to neutron drip line. It builds on the relativistic mean field (RMF) framework where Bahri et al. (1992) first noted the relation between pseudo-spin symmetry and RMF, and on Ginocchio (1997) who revealed that pseudo-orbital angular momentum corresponds to the orbital angular momentum of the lower component of the Dirac wave function. The results enable further study of pseudo-spin symmetry in highly neutron-rich nuclei and its connection to diffuse nuclear potentials near the drip lines.
