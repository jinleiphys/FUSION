# Investigation of the MicroBooNE neutrino cross sections on Argon

> Experimental data of charged current inclusive neutrino cross sections on argon as a function of different variables have recently appeared. We have compared them to our theoretical approach. Overall we find an agreement in spite of a tendency of underestimation in some specific regions. A new aspect is the availability of data in terms of the energy transfer to the nucleus, which allows a better separation of the different reaction mechanisms. We explain the deterioration of agreement in specific kinematical conditions by the absence in our model of two-pion production and other inelastic channels, more important for MicroBooNE than for T2K.

# Investigation of the MicroBooNE neutrino cross sections on Argon

## Key claim
The authors' theoretical model, which includes quasielastic, multinucleon emission, and single-pion production channels, shows overall agreement with MicroBooNE inclusive neutrino-argon cross section data, but systematically underestimates the data in specific kinematic regions, particularly at low muon momentum and at transferred energies above approximately 0.2 GeV, which the authors attribute to the absence of two-pion production and other inelastic channels that are more important for MicroBooNE than for T2K due to differences in the neutrino energy profiles.

## Method
The authors calculated nuclear response functions using a random phase approximation (RPA) on top of a local Fermi gas model, including quasielastic scattering, multinucleon (2p-2h) excitations, and coherent plus incoherent single-pion production. For argon-40, they approximated the nuclear density profiles using those of calcium-40, rescaling the quasielastic results by the ratio of active neutrons (22/20) and rescaling the short-range correlation contributions from carbon-12 by a factor of 40/12. They compared their flux-integrated predictions to MicroBooNE double-differential cross sections in muon momentum and angle, single-differential cross sections in muon energy and transferred energy, and the total cross section as a function of neutrino energy, applying additional smearing matrices provided by the collaboration where necessary.

## Key numbers
- The model achieves a chi-squared per degree of freedom of 27.9/11 for the flux-integrated differential cross section as a function of muon energy, compared to 16.7/11 for GiBUU and 32.4/11 for GENIEv3.
- For the flux-integrated differential cross section as a function of transferred energy, the model with RPA effects gives chi-squared per degree of freedom of 17.2/8, comparable to GiBUU at 17.0/8 and lower than MicroBooNE MC at 33.8/8.
- Without RPA effects, the chi-squared per degree of freedom for the transferred energy cross section decreases to 13.4/8.
- The quasielastic cross section for argon-40 is obtained by multiplying the calcium-40 result by 22/20 to account for the number of active neutrons.
- The short-range pair ratio between argon-40 and carbon-12 is approximately 4.2 in the full calculation of Mosel (2016), while the authors' simple A-scaling gives 40/12 = 3.33.
- The model calculations extend only up to transferred energy omega = 1 GeV, limiting the total cross section comparison to neutrino energies below 1.2 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous successful comparisons of their RPA-based model to T2K inclusive neutrino-carbon cross sections (Martini 2014) and MiniBooNE CCQE-like data (Martini 2011, 2013) to the new MicroBooNE measurements on argon-40. The analysis is enabled by recent MicroBooNE publications (Abratenko 2019, MicroBooNE 2021) that provide inclusive cross sections as functions of muon momentum, muon energy, transferred energy, and neutrino energy, the transferred energy data being a first for this energy range since MINERvA (2015) at higher energies. The results highlight that the missing two-pion and inelastic channels, which are negligible for T2K due to its lower-energy flux, become important for MicroBooNE and will need to be included for future DUNE analyses. The authors note that their multinucleon contribution is about a factor of 2 larger than that of Nieves (2011) used by GENIEv3, MicroBooNE MC, NEUT, and NuWro, which may explain their better agreement with data.
