# A dependence of the enhancement factor in energy-weighted sums for isovector giant resonances

> We consider the energy weighted sums (EWS) for isovector giant dipole resonances (IVGDR) in finite nuclei within Landau kinetic theory. The dependence of both IVGDR energy, $E_{IVGDR}$, and the EWS enhancement factor, $κ(A)$, on the mass number $A$ occurs because of the boundary condition on the moving nuclear surface. The values of $E_{IVGDR}A^{1/3}$ and $κ(A)$ increase with $A$. The obtained value of the enhancement factor is about 10% for light nuclei and reaches approximately 20% for heavy nuclei. A fit of the enhancement factor to the proper experimental data provides a value for the isovector Landau amplitude of $F'_1\simeq 1.1$.

## Key claim
The enhancement factor κ(A) for the isovector giant dipole resonance energy-weighted sum rule increases with mass number A, from about 10% for light nuclei to approximately 20% for heavy nuclei, due to the boundary condition on the moving nuclear surface. This A-dependence is obtained within Landau kinetic theory, and a fit to experimental data yields an isovector Landau amplitude of F'₁ ≈ 1.1, which exceeds the range of 0.5-0.7 derived from Skyrme forces for infinite nuclear matter.

## Method
The authors used the collisionless Landau-Vlasov kinetic equation to derive the isovector response function for finite nuclei, including both the isovector Landau amplitudes F'₀ and F'₁. They supplemented the dispersion relation with a boundary condition that balances internal forces from the isovector sound wave against surface tension forces, leading to a secular equation for the wave number q that depends on the mass number A. The IVGDR eigenenergy and the enhancement factor were then calculated by solving the dispersion relation and secular equation consistently, with parameters F'₁ = 1.1 and surface stiffness Q = 10.5 MeV chosen to fit experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Enhancement factor κ(A) is about 10% for light nuclei and reaches approximately 20% for heavy nuclei.
- The isovector Landau amplitude obtained from fitting is F'₁ ≈ 1.1.
- The bulk symmetry energy C_sym is set to 60 MeV, with r₀ = 1.2 fm, F₁ = -0.3, and F'₀ = 1.41.
- For A > 100, experimental data center at about 1.2 Thomas-Reiche-Khun sum-rule units (i.e., κ ≈ 0.2).
- For the nucleus ²⁰⁸Pb, a best value for κ of 0.22 ± 0.04 was deduced from a fit to experimental data in prior work (trco08).
- The surface stiffness parameter Q is estimated as Q ≈ 11 MeV from fitting the energy dependence on mass number.

## Context
This work extends the Landau kinetic theory approach to finite nuclei, going beyond the Steinwedel-Jensen model (Bohr and Mottelson) and the scaling approximation of Stringari (1983). The results are compared with Random Phase Approximation calculations (LiSt, tror87, krtr80, sabo04) which overestimate the enhancement factor, and with experimental data from the Livermore group (BeFu). The approach enables simultaneous description of the A-dependence of both the IVGDR energy and the enhancement factor, and provides a method to extract the isovector Landau amplitude F'₁ from finite-nucleus data, which is relevant for constraining the velocity-dependent part of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.
