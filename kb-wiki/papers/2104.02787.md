# Exploring the flavor content of light and heavy-light pseudoscalars

> The electroweak properties of light and charmed D and Ds pseudoscalar mesons are investigated within a unified covariant constituent quark model. The quark-antiquark-meson vertices are assumed to have a symmetric form by the exchange of quark momenta, which is successful in describing the light pseudoscalar meson properties. The flavor decomposition of the elastic electromagnetic form factors, electromagnetic charge radii, and weak decay constants are calculated. Based on the results a discussion on the SU(3) and SU(4) symmetry breaking is made and a comparison with the pion and kaon properties to highlight the Higgs contribution to the structure of these mesons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified covariant constituent quark model, with a symmetric quark-antiquark-meson vertex and a single scale parameter per meson fitted to the weak decay constant, can simultaneously describe the electromagnetic form factors, charge radii, and flavor decomposition of light pseudoscalars (pion, kaon) and heavy-light pseudoscalars (D+, Ds+), thereby revealing the transition from dynamical chiral symmetry breaking in light mesons to Higgs-dominated mass generation in heavy-light systems.

## Method

The authors construct a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude model for pseudoscalar mesons using an effective SU(4) flavor Lagrangian, with a vertex function that behaves as 1/k^2 at large momentum and contains one mass scale parameter (μ_M) per meson. The elastic electromagnetic form factors are computed via the Mandelstam formula (triangle Feynman diagram) in the Breit frame with Drell-Yan condition (q^+=0), using the plus component of the electromagnetic current to eliminate instantaneous terms. The flavor decomposition of form factors is performed by separating contributions from quarks (charge +2/3) and antiquarks (charge +1/3) in the current operator.

## Key numbers

* Pion (π+) charge radius: 0.665 fm (model A), compared to experimental 0.672(8) fm
* Kaon (K+) charge radius: 0.551 fm (model C), compared to experimental 0.560(3) fm
* D+ charge radius: 0.505 fm (model E)
* Ds+ charge radius: 0.377 fm (model F)
* Pion decay constant: 92.55 MeV (model A), compared to experimental 92.28(7) MeV
* Kaon decay constant: 110.8 MeV (model C), compared to experimental 110(1) MeV
* D+ decay constant: 144.5 MeV (model E), compared to experimental 144(3) MeV
* Ds+ decay constant: 182.7 MeV (model F), compared to experimental 182(3) MeV
* Constituent quark masses used: u/d = 384 MeV, s = 508 MeV, c = 1623 MeV (from Suisso (2002))
* Vertex mass scale parameters: μ_π = 225 MeV, μ_K = 420 MeV, μ_D = 1607 MeV, μ_Ds = 1685 MeV

## Context

This work extends the covariant constituent quark model of de Melo (2002) and Yabusaki (2015), previously applied to pion and kaon electroweak properties, to include charmed D and Ds mesons within a unified SU(4) framework. The results are compared with lattice QCD calculations by Can (2012) and the 2017 EPJA collaboration, which found D+ charge radii around 0.4 fm, and with the vector meson dominance model. The flavor decomposition of form factors enables study of how the charge distribution changes from light-light to heavy-light pseudoscalars, and the model provides predictions for D+ and Ds+ electromagnetic form factors that can be tested by future experiments.
