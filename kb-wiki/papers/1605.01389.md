# Heavy ions at the Future Circular Collider

> The Future Circular Collider (FCC) Study is aimed at assessing the physics potential and the technical feasibility of a new collider with centre-of-mass energies, in the hadron-hadron collision mode, seven times larger than the nominal LHC energies. Operating such machine with heavy ions is an option that is being considered in the accelerator design studies. It would provide, for example, Pb-Pb and p-Pb collisions at sqrt{s_NN} = 39 and 63 TeV, respectively, per nucleon-nucleon collision, with integrated luminosities above 30 nb^-1 per month for Pb-Pb. This is a report by the working group on heavy-ion physics of the FCC Study. First ideas on the physics opportunities with heavy ions at the FCC are presented, covering the physics of the Quark-Gluon Plasma, of gluon saturation, of photon-induced collisions, as well as connections with other fields of high-energy physics.

## Key claim

The Future Circular Collider (FCC) could provide Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV and p-Pb collisions at 63 TeV, with integrated luminosities of 33 nb^-1 per month for Pb-Pb and 8 pb^-1 per month for p-Pb, but these projections assume a single heavy-ion experiment and an idealised 30-day run with 50% performance efficiency; the physics opportunities described are based on extrapolations from LHC data and may evolve significantly with future LHC results and theoretical advances.

## Method

The study extrapolates global event characteristics (charged particle multiplicity, transverse energy, femtoscopic correlations) from LHC measurements at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV to FCC energies using power-law scaling dN_ch/dη ∝ (sqrt(s_NN))^0.3. Fluid dynamic simulations with a realistic QCD equation of state and minimal shear viscosity (η/s = 1/4π) were run for central Pb-Pb collisions to estimate freeze-out hypersurfaces, energy densities, and temperature evolution. Accelerator performance projections were based on an injection scenario using the LHC as the final injector, with parameters from LHC Run-1 heavy-ion operation, updated to include filling the entire FCC ring with up to 4 LHC injection cycles.

## Key numbers

- Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 39 TeV and p-Pb collisions at 63 TeV
- Integrated luminosity per month: 33 nb^-1 for Pb-Pb, 8 pb^-1 for p-Pb
- Charged hadron multiplicity per unit rapidity at η=0 in central Pb-Pb: 3600 at 39 TeV (extrapolated from 1600 at 2.76 TeV)
- Total charged multiplicity in central Pb-Pb: 50000 at 39 TeV (extrapolated from 17000 at 2.76 TeV)
- Transverse energy per unit rapidity at η=0 in central Pb-Pb: 5.2-5.8 TeV at 39 TeV (extrapolated from 1.8-2.0 TeV at 2.76 TeV)
- Homogeneity volume in central Pb-Pb: 11000 fm^3 at 39 TeV (extrapolated from 5000 fm^3 at 2.76 TeV)
- Decoupling time in central Pb-Pb: 13 fm/c at 39 TeV (extrapolated from 10 fm/c at 2.76 TeV)
- Energy density at τ=1 fm/c in central Pb-Pb: 35-40 GeV/fm^3 at 39 TeV (extrapolated from 12-13 GeV/fm^3 at 2.76 TeV)
- Initial temperature estimate: up to 800-1000 MeV if thermalization time decreases to O(0.02 fm/c) at FCC energies
- Peak luminosity for Pb-Pb: 57.8 × 10^27 cm^-2 s^-1; for p-Pb: 9918 × 10^27 cm^-2 s^-1
- Number of bunches in FCC: 2072; particles per bunch: 2.0 × 10^8 for Pb, 164 × 10^8 for protons

## Context

This paper extends the heavy-ion physics programme from the LHC to the FCC, building on LHC measurements by Aamodt (2010), Abbas (2013), Chatrchyan (2012), and Adam (2015-2016) for global event characteristics. It references the CGC-rcBK model by Albacete (2010) for saturation-based multiplicity estimates and fluid dynamic frameworks by Gale (2012), Niemi (2014-2015), and Denicol (2014) for collective flow calculations. The work enables future experimental design studies for heavy-ion detectors at FCC-hh, particularly regarding requirements for charged-hadron identification, low-pT tracking, forward coverage up to η≈6, and calorimetry that persists at higher event multiplicities. The Chinese SppC project (with Pb-Pb at sqrt(s_NN) ~20-30 TeV) is noted as a complementary effort.
