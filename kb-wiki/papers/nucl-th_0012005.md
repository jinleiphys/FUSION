# Pion-Lambda-Sigma Coupling Extracted from Hyperonic Atoms

> The latest measurements of the atomic level width in Sigma-hyperonic Pb atom offer the most accurate datum in the region of low-energy Sigma-hyperon physics. Atomic widths are due to the conversion of Sigma-nucleon into Lambda-nucleon. In high angular momentum states this conversion is dominated by the one-pion exchange. A joint analysis of the data of the scattering of negative-Sigma on proton converting into a Lambda and a neutron and of the atomic widths allows to extract a pseudovector pion-hyperon-Sigma coupling constant of 0.048 with a statistical error of +-0.005 and a systematic one of +-0.004. This corresponds to a pseudoscalar coupling constant of 13.3 with a statistical uncertainty of 1.4 and a systematic one of 1.1.

# Pion-Lambda-Sigma Coupling Extracted from Hyperonic Atoms

## Key claim

The pion-hyperon coupling constant is extracted from a joint analysis of Sigma-hyperonic atomic level widths and Sigma-proton scattering data, yielding a pseudovector coupling of 0.048 with combined statistical and systematic uncertainties of about 0.009. The extraction relies on the dominance of one-pion exchange in high angular momentum atomic states, but requires corrections for short-range background amplitudes (contributing approximately 10% to atomic widths) and careful treatment of nuclear density uncertainties.

## Method

The authors analyze the conversion reaction Sigma- + p -> Lambda + n occurring at the nuclear surface in Sigma-hyperonic atoms, where high angular momentum states (l >= 9) suppress short-range interactions and enhance the one-pion exchange contribution. Atomic level widths are calculated using a microscopic optical potential approach with unitarity relations, folding the OPE amplitude over nuclear proton densities and atomic wave functions, with corrections for nucleon binding energy, nuclear recoil, and finite-range effects. The coupling constant is determined by simultaneously fitting the atomic width data from Pb, Al, and Si Sigma atoms together with the Massachusetts scattering data (8 data points at momenta 200-580 MeV/c) using several models of short-range background amplitudes (two-pion, rho meson, K meson exchange) to estimate systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Pseudovector coupling constant f²_{πΛΣ}/4π = 0.048 ± 0.005 (statistical) ± 0.004 (systematic)
- Corresponding pseudoscalar coupling constant G²_{πΛΣ} = 13.3 ± 1.4 (statistical) ± 1.1 (systematic)
- Atomic level width of Sigma- Pb atom in n=10, l=9 state: 17(3) eV from Powers et al.
- Momentum transferred by the pion: central value approximately 260 MeV/c
- Energy excess Q from hyperon mass difference minus nucleon binding: 70-80 MeV
- Nuclear recoil energy: approximately 10 MeV, with spread of about 5 MeV half-width
- Best fit to atomic data alone gave f²_{πΛΣ}/4π = 0.050(6) with chi-squared/data = 2.38/4
- Systematic error from short-range interaction uncertainty: 0.003 in f²_{πΛΣ}/4π
- Nuclear density uncertainty contributes 3.4% error for Ca, 6.4% for Pb, 10% for Al, 11% for Si atomic widths
- Non-perturbative optical potential corrections reduce the coupling constant by approximately 4%

## Context

This work extends the approach used for antiprotonic atoms by Wycech (1997) to the Sigma-hyperonic system, where spin and isospin averaging does not cancel the long-range pionic effects as it does in antiprotonic atoms. The extracted coupling constant provides a test of SU(3) symmetry predictions and can be compared with meson-exchange models of hyperon-nucleon interactions such as those by Swart (1997), Stoks (1999), Rijken (1999), and Reuber (1994). The result enables improved descriptions of low-energy Sigma-hyperon physics and constrains the short-range components in hyperon-nucleon potential models, though the data sample remains too limited for a definitive determination of background amplitudes.
