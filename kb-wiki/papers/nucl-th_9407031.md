# Production of Eta-Mesons in Collisions of Nucleons and Delta-Resonances

> We calculate the cross section for the production of $η$-mesons via \mbox{$ΔN \to N N η$} in a relativistic One-Boson-Exchange-Model. Using this cross section we then determine the probability for the production of an $η$-meson by a $Δ$-resonance moving in nuclear matter. The result is compared to prescriptions in BUU-calculations in which \et-production proceeds both through a direct channel and through the sequential process $Δ\to N π; πN \to N η$.

## Key claim

The cross section for $\Delta N \to N N \eta$ is calculated microscopically and found to be dominated by pion exchange, with the exchanged pion becoming on-shell over a wide energy range; the resulting cross section is density-dependent and finite only when in-medium pion width is included, and the standard BUU-model prescription of adding separate direct and sequential production channels does not involve double counting and reproduces the exact result surprisingly well.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic One-Boson-Exchange Model with empirical interaction vertices for $\pi$ and $\rho$ exchange, where the $\eta$ is produced via an intermediate $N(1535)$ resonance. The pion propagator was regularized by including an in-medium width derived from the pion selfenergy, using a local-density approximation with the total pion-nucleon cross section. The resulting density-dependent cross section for $\Delta N \to N N \eta$ was then inserted into rate equations for a single $\Delta^-$ moving in homogeneous proton matter, and the $\eta$ production probability was compared to the BUU-model's additive treatment of sequential ($\Delta \to N \pi; \pi N \to N \eta$) and direct production channels.

## Key numbers

- $g_{\Delta N \pi} = 2.15$, corresponding to a $\Delta$-width of 115 MeV.
- $g_{N N^* \pi} = 1.37$ and $g_{N N^* \eta} = 1.53$, determined by fitting $\pi N \to N \eta$ data.
- $g_{\Delta N \rho} = 13.3$ and $g_{N N^* \rho} = 0.61$, taken from other groups.
- For normal nuclear density $\rho_0$, $\Gamma_{\Delta p \rightarrow n n \eta} \approx 0.01 \;\Gamma_\Delta$.
- At $\rho = \rho_0$, the sequential $\eta$-production probability is about 30% smaller than the direct production probability from the microscopic calculation.
- The on-shell pion condition applies for cm-energies of the $\Delta N$-system from about 50 MeV above threshold up to about 7 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier BUU-model simulations (Mosel, 1993; Wolf, 1993) that used an ad-hoc recipe for the direct $\Delta N \to NN\eta$ channel and an explicit sequential channel, by providing a microscopically calculated cross section. It confirms the dominance of resonance channels for sub-threshold particle production found in those simulations. The result enables transport calculations to use a density-dependent cross section for $\Delta N \to N N \eta$ and validates the existing BUU prescription, putting the enhancement of far-subthreshold particle yields on a firmer theoretical basis. The authors note that Salcedo et al. (1993) had shown for a similar problem that sequential and direct production coincide in the zero-density limit, which the present results confirm.
