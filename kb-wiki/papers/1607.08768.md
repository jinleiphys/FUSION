# Influence of differential elastic nucleon-nucleon cross section on stopping and collective flows in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies

> By considering three different Nucleon-Nucleon (NN) elastic differential cross sections: the Cugnon \emph{et al.} parameterized differential cross section [Nucl. Instrum. Methods Phys. Res., Sect. \textbf{B111}, 215 (1996)], and the differential cross section derived from the collision term of the self-consistent relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation proposed by Mao \emph{et al.} [Z.\ Phys.\ A {\bf 347}, 173 (1994)], as well as the isotropic differential cross section, within the newly updated version of the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model, the influence of the differential elastic NN cross section on various observables (e.g., nuclear stopping, both the rapidity and transverse-velocity dependence of the directed and elliptic flows) in Au+Au collisions at beam energies 150, 250, 400, and 800 MeV$/$nucleon is investigated. By comparing calculations with those three differential cross sections, it is found that the nuclear stopping power, the directed and elliptic flows are affected to some extent by the differential cross sections, and the impact of differential cross section on those observables becomes more visible as the beam energy increases. The effect on the elliptic flow difference $v_{2}^{n}$-$v_{2}^{H}$ and ratio $v_{2}^{n}$/$v_{2}^{H}$ of neutrons versus hydrogen isotopes ($Z=1$), which have been used as sensitive observables for probing nuclear symmetry energy at high densities, is weak.

## Key claim

The differential elastic nucleon-nucleon cross section affects nuclear stopping, directed flow, and elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions, with the effect growing more visible as beam energy increases; however, the impact on the elliptic flow ratio and difference of neutrons versus hydrogen isotopes (observables used to probe the nuclear symmetry energy at high densities) is weak.

## Method

The authors used the ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) model with three different elastic nucleon-nucleon differential cross sections: the isotropic differential cross section, the Cugon et al. (1996) parameterization, and the analytical expression from Mao et al. (1994) derived from the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation. They simulated Au+Au collisions at beam energies of 150, 250, 400, and 800 MeV per nucleon and compared the resulting nuclear stopping (varxz), directed flow (v1), and elliptic flow (v2) of free protons, as well as the elliptic flow ratio and difference of neutrons versus hydrogen isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The stopping power varxz calculated with the Cugnon parameterization is about 8% less than that with the isotropic differential cross section at 800 MeV per nucleon.
- The slope of directed flow obtained with the isotropic differential cross section is about 15% larger than that obtained with the Cugnon parameterization at 800 MeV per nucleon.
- The elliptic flow at midrapidity (y0=0) obtained with the isotropic differential cross section is about 20% larger than that obtained with the Cugnon parameterization at 800 MeV per nucleon.
- Beam energies studied: 150, 250, 400, and 800 MeV per nucleon for Au+Au collisions.
- Centrality range: reduced impact parameter b0 between 0 and 0.55, with specific analyses at 0.25 less than b0 less than 0.45 for flow and b0 less than 0.15 for stopping.

## Context

This work extends studies of the influence of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections on heavy-ion collision observables, where prior work by Cugnon et al. (1996) and Mao et al. (1994) provided the differential cross section parameterizations used. The results show that while the differential cross section affects stopping and collective flows, the elliptic flow ratio and difference of neutrons versus hydrogen isotopes (used by Russotto et al. (2011) to probe the nuclear symmetry energy) are robust against this uncertainty, supporting the use of these observables for constraining the high-density symmetry energy.
