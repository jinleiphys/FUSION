# The properties of kaonic nuclei in relativistic mean-field theory

> The static properties of some possible light and moderate kaonic nuclei, from C to Ti, are studied in the relativistic mean-field theory. The 1s and 1p state binding energies of $K^-$ are in the range of $73\sim 96$ MeV and $22\sim 63$ MeV, respectively. The binding energies of 1p states increase monotonically with the nucleon number A. The upper limit of the widths are about $42\pm 14$ MeV for the 1s states, and about $71\pm 10$ MeV for the 1p states. The lower limit of the widths are about $12\pm 4$ MeV for the 1s states, and $21\pm 3$ MeV for the 1p states. If $V_{0}\leq 30$ MeV, the discrete $K^-$ bound states should be identified in experiment. The shrinkage effect is found in the possible kaonic nuclei. The interior nuclear density increases obviously, the densest center density is about $2.1ρ_{0}$.

# The properties of kaonic nuclei in relativistic mean-field theory

## Key claim

Using a modest K-sigma coupling constant (gσK ≈ gσN/5) fitted to experimental KN scattering lengths, the authors predict that discrete K⁻ bound states should be identifiable in experiment only if the imaginary potential depth V₀ ≤ 30 MeV; for V₀ ≥ 50 MeV, the widths become too large to resolve discrete states for most nuclei studied.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled equations of relativistic mean-field theory self-consistently for N=Z even-even nuclear cores from ¹²C to ⁴⁴Ti, using the NL-SH parameter set for nucleon-meson couplings and a one-meson-exchange motivated Lagrangian for the K⁻-meson interactions with σ and ω mesons. The K⁻ self-energy is modified by introducing a phenomenological imaginary part with a "tρ" form, where the imaginary potential depth V₀ is varied between 15 and 50 MeV, and a suppression factor accounting for reduced phase space in mesonic and non-mesonic decay channels is applied.

## Key numbers

- 1s state K⁻ binding energies (BK⁻¹ˢ): 73–96 MeV across all studied nuclei
- 1p state K⁻ binding energies (BK⁻¹ᵖ): 22–63 MeV, increasing monotonically with nucleon number A
- Upper limit widths (V₀ = 50 MeV): 42 ± 14 MeV for 1s states, 71 ± 10 MeV for 1p states
- Lower limit widths (V₀ = 15 MeV): 12 ± 4 MeV for 1s states, 21 ± 3 MeV for 1p states
- Dense central nuclear density for ¹²CK⁻: approximately 2.1 ρ₀ (about 0.31 fm⁻³)
- R.m.s. radius reduction for ¹²CK⁻: approximately 0.12 fm compared to ordinary ¹²C
- Separation between 1s and 1p states: 27–62 MeV, decreasing with nucleon number

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF studies of in-medium K⁻N interactions (e.g., Schaffner-Bielich et al., 1997; Pal et al., 2000) and kaonic nuclei calculations by Mares et al. (2000) and Gal (2000). The predicted binding energies (73–96 MeV) are compatible with the FINUDA experiment's K⁻pp candidate (binding energy ~88 MeV after subtracting pp binding) and with calculations by Akaishi et al. (2002) and Dote et al. (2004). However, the central nuclear densities found here (about 2.1 ρ₀) are significantly lower than the 4–10 ρ₀ predicted by Dote et al. (2004) using antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, indicating substantial model dependence in the predicted compression of nuclear matter by the antikaon.
