# Two-neutron overlap functions for 6He from a microscopic structure model

> A fully antisymmetrized microscopic model is developed for light two-neutron halo nuclei using a hyper-spherical basis to describe halo regions. The many-body wavefunction is optimized variationally. The model is applied to 6He bound by semi realistic Minnesota nucleon-nucleon forces. The two-neutron separation energy and the radius of the halo are reproduced in agreement with experiment. Antisymmetrization effects between 4He and halo neutrons are found to be crucial for binding of 6He. We also properly extract two-neutron overlap functions and find that there is a significant increase of 30%-70% in their normalization due to microscopic effects as compared to the results of three-body models.

## Key claim

The authors develop a fully antisymmetrized microscopic model for two-neutron halo nuclei that reproduces the two-neutron separation energy and halo radius of 6He, but they note that the interaction mixture parameter is the only free parameter in their model and must be tuned (from its default value 1.0 to 1.015 for the Minnesota plus spin-orbit force) to achieve the correct separation energy, meaning the absolute binding of 4He and 6He is not reproduced without this adjustment.

## Method

The model describes 6He as an antisymmetrized product of a microscopic 4He core (built using the stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussians in Jacobi coordinates) and a valence part consisting of two neutrons expressed in a hyper-spherical basis with Laguerre polynomials for the hyper-radial part. The many-body wavefunction is optimized variationally by minimizing the expectation value of the Hamiltonian, which includes the semi-realistic Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction with a spin-orbit force. Two-neutron overlap functions are then extracted from the optimized wavefunction and expressed in hyper-spherical coordinates for direct comparison with three-body models.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy of 6He: 0.97 MeV (reproduced by tuning the Minnesota interaction mixture parameter to 1.015 with spin-orbit force, or 1.15 without spin-orbit force)
- Increase of 30%–70% in the normalization of two-neutron overlap functions due to microscopic effects compared to three-body model results
- The hyper-radial basis uses associated Laguerre polynomials \(L^5_{n_{lag}}\) with \(n_{lag}=0,1,2,\ldots\) and a scale parameter \(\rho_0\)

## Context

This work extends microscopic cluster models of light nuclei (such as the stochastic variational method of Varga (1995) and the SVM book) by incorporating a hyper-spherical basis that correctly captures the exponential asymptotic decay of halo wavefunctions, which three-body models (e.g., Zhukov (1993), Thompson (2004)) treat with an inert-core approximation. The authors aim to provide microscopic two-neutron overlap functions that can be used directly in reaction calculations, addressing a gap where previous microscopic structure models (e.g., GFMC by Pieper (2001, 2005), no-core shell model by Navratil (2009)) did not produce few-body-like input for reactions. This enables future reaction studies of two-neutron halo nuclei to incorporate antisymmetrization effects missing in three-body models.
