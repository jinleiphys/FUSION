# The decay $ρ^{0}\to π^{0}π^{0}γ$ and the role of $σ$-meson

> We study the radiative decay $ρ^{0}\toπ^{0}π^{0}γ$ within the framework of a phenomenological approach in which the contributions of $σ$-meson and $ω$-meson intermediate states and of the pion-loops are considered. We conclude that the $σ$-meson amplitude provides the dominant contribution.

## Key claim

The authors conclude that the σ-meson intermediate state provides the dominant contribution to the decay ρ⁰ → π⁰π⁰γ, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific values chosen for the σ-meson mass and width (M_σ and Γ_σ) and on the sign of the coupling constant g_{ρσγ}, which they estimate from the experimental branching ratio of ρ⁰ → π⁺π⁻γ.

## Method

The authors calculate the decay rate for ρ⁰ → π⁰π⁰γ using a phenomenological approach that includes three contributions: a σ-meson pole diagram, an ω-meson pole diagram, and charged-pion loop diagrams. They describe the vertices with effective Lagrangians, determine coupling constants from experimental data (e.g., g_{ρσγ} from ρ⁰ → π⁺π⁻γ, g_{ωπγ} from ω → π⁰γ, and g_{ρππ} from ρ⁰ → π⁺π⁻), and treat the σ-meson propagator with a complex mass M_σ − iΓ_σ/2. The decay width is obtained by integrating the differential decay probability over photon and pion energies for an unpolarized ρ⁰ at rest.

## Key numbers

- For M_σ = 478 MeV, Γ_σ = 324 MeV, and g_{ρσγ} = 5.92, the total decay rate is Γ(ρ⁰ → π⁰π⁰γ) = 289 KeV, with contributions: σ-meson amplitude = 323 KeV, ω-meson amplitude = 1.46 KeV, and pion-loop amplitude = 2.7 KeV.
- For the same σ-meson parameters but with g_{ρσγ} = −4.49, the total decay rate is Γ(ρ⁰ → π⁰π⁰γ) = 215 KeV.
- Using σ-meson parameters M_σ = 500 MeV, Γ_σ = 250 MeV, and g_{ρσγ} = 2.71, the decay rate is 56.2 KeV; using M_σ = 500 MeV, Γ_σ = 500 MeV, and g_{ρσγ} = 2.71, the rate is 31 KeV.
- The maximum photon energy in the decay is E_{γ,max} = 338 MeV.
- Previous calculations by Bramon et al. (1992) gave Γ(ρ⁰ → π⁰π⁰γ) = 1.6 KeV and branching ratio B = 1.1 × 10⁻⁵; Bramon et al. (1992) later obtained Γ = 3.88 KeV and B = 26 × 10⁻⁶ including charged-pion loops.
- Marco et al. (1999) obtained B(ρ⁰ → π⁰π⁰γ) = 1.4 × 10⁻⁵ using chiral unitary theory, and B = 1.0 × 10⁻⁴ for the opposite sign of the product F_V G_V.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of radiative ρ⁰ decays by Singer (1963), Renard (1969), and Bramon et al. (1992), who considered vector meson dominance (VDM) and pion-loop contributions but neglected or downplayed the σ-meson. It also relates to the chiral unitary approach of Marco et al. (1999), who found that π⁰π⁰ interactions are dominated by the σ-pole. The authors' larger decay rates (up to 334 KeV depending on parameters) suggest that future measurements at high-luminosity e⁺e⁻ machines (Novosibirsk, Frascati) could constrain the σ-meson mass and width and determine the sign of the coupling constant g_{ρσγ}, which is important for analyzing photoproduction reactions near threshold.
