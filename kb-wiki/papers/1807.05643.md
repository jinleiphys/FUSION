# Pairing of the Pöschl-Teller gas

> We report calculations of equation of state of a model system, representative for a cold Fermi gas, of particles interacting via the Pöschl-Teller interaction. In successively more sophisticated calculations, we determine the importance of correlations and non-localities. We show that the gas displays, at relatively low density, an instability indicated by a divergence of the in-medium scattering length which occurs well before the divergence of the vacuum scattering length and the spinodal density. We also calculate the pairing gap and show that non-local correlations can change the pairing gap by almost an order of magnitude.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a cold Fermi gas interacting via the Pöschl-Teller potential exhibits an instability at relatively low density, indicated by a divergence of the in-medium scattering length that occurs well before the divergence of the vacuum scattering length and the spinodal density; additionally, non-local correlations can change the pairing gap by almost an order of magnitude, calling into question the quality of locally correlated wave functions for pairing phenomena.

## Method

The authors used the Jastrow-Feenberg variational approach with the Fermi-hypernetted chain Euler-Lagrange (FHNC-EL) method, truncated at the FHNC//0 level, to compute the equation of state, stability, and pairing properties of a Fermi gas with the attractive Pöschl-Teller interaction. They extended the calculation beyond local correlations using correlated basis functions (CBF) theory, replacing the collective approximation with the proper Lindhard function to obtain an energy-dependent effective interaction for the pairing gap. The pairing gap was calculated from a correlated BCS (CBCS) state, with the pairing interaction derived from the correlated wave function.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum scattering length at interaction strength V0 = 1.8 is a0 = -4.524 σ.
- Calculations were performed up to densities KFσ = 0.3, corresponding to a density of ρ = 0.0009 σ^-3.
- The Landau parameter F0^s values obtained from the equation of state and from the particle-hole interaction can differ by about 30 percent for strong coupling strengths V0 > 1.7.
- The pairing gap in CBF approximation and FHNC//0-EL approximation can differ by almost an order of magnitude, as shown for a range of coupling strengths.
- The low-density limit of the gap at the Fermi surface is given by Δ/EF = (8/e^2) exp(π/(2a0 KF)).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pairing in cold Fermi gases using the Pöschl-Teller interaction, notably the Monte Carlo study by Gezerlis and Carlson (2008), and is similar to studies using an attractive square-well potential by Astrakharchik et al. (2004) and the authors' own earlier work (cbcs). The paper demonstrates that local correlation functions perform poorly for pairing phenomena, consistent with earlier work by the authors (shores, CCKS86), and that the instability from the in-medium scattering length divergence prevents reaching the large values of -KF a0 reported by Gezerlis and Carlson (2008). The results enable further investigation into the transition from BCS to BEC phases and the need for self-consistently determined pairing interactions in diagram summation.
