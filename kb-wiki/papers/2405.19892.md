# Hyperon-Nucleus/Nucleon Scattering at BESIII

> Utilizing the large quantity of hyperons and antihyperons produced by the decay of 10 billion $J/ψ$ and 2.7 billion $ψ(3686)$ collected at BESIII, the cross sections of several specific elastic or inelastic (anti-)hyperon-nucleus/nucleon rections have been measured via the scattering between the (anti-)hyperons and the nucleus in the dense objects of BESIII detector. The novel method developed in these works extends the research field and opens a new era for the experiments at $e^+ e^-$ colliders. The results of such measurements will definitely benefit a lot the precise probe of the (anti-)hyperon-nucleus/nucleon interactions and provide constraints for the studies of the potential of strong interaction, the origin of color confinement, the unified model for baryon-baryon interactions, and the internal structure of neutron stars. The desirable prospects of corresponding studies in the future Super Tau-Charm Factory (STCF) are also discussed in this report.

## Key claim

Using the large sample of hyperons from charmonium decays at BESIII, cross sections for several (anti-)hyperon-nucleus/nucleon reactions have been measured via a novel "Double Tag" method that treats the detector material as a target, but the results are limited to specific incident momenta (around 1.074 GeV/c for Lambda and 0.818 GeV/c for Xi0) and rely on effective luminosities that account for target properties and beam behavior.

## Method

The BESIII detector at the BEPCII e+e- collider collected 10 billion J/psi and 2.7 billion psi(3686) events. Hyperons produced in charmonium decays travel through the beam pipe and inner detector material (primarily beryllium and hydrogen), where they may scatter elastically or inelastically with nuclei. A "Double Tag" method reconstructs the incident hyperon via its recoil mass from the other decay products (single-tag), then reconstructs the scattered hyperon or its decay products (double-tag). The cross section is extracted using an effective luminosity that accounts for the target material distribution and hyperon flux, determined from signal Monte Carlo samples.

## Key numbers

- Cross section of Lambda + Be-9 -> Sigma+ + X: (37.3 ± 4.7_stat ± 3.5_syst) mb at p_Lambda ≈ 1.074 GeV/c
- Cross section of Lambda p -> Sigma+ X (per single proton): (19.3 ± 2.4_stat ± 1.8_syst) mb at p_Lambda ≈ 1.074 GeV/c
- Cross section of Xi0 + Be-9 -> Xi- + p + Be-8: (22.1 ± 5.3_stat ± 4.5_syst) mb at p_Xi0 ≈ 0.818 GeV/c
- Cross section of Xi0 n -> Xi- p (per neutron): (7.4 ± 1.8_stat ± 1.5_syst) mb at p_Xi0 ≈ 0.818 GeV/c
- Cross section of Lambda p -> Lambda p: (12.2 ± 1.6_stat ± 1.1_syst) mb at p_Lambda ≈ 1.074 GeV/c within -0.9 < cosθ < 0.9
- Cross section of anti-Lambda p -> anti-Lambda p: (17.5 ± 2.1_stat ± 1.6_syst) mb at p_anti-Lambda ≈ 1.074 GeV/c within -0.9 < cosθ < 0.9
- Signal yield for Xi0 n -> Xi- p: 22.9 ± 5.5 events with 7.1 sigma significance
- Double-tag yield for Lambda + Be-9 -> Sigma+ + X: 795 ± 101 events

## Context

This work extends the sparse experimental data on hyperon-nucleon scattering, which previously came primarily from fixed-target experiments at J-PARC and CLAS (Eisele 1971, Hauptman 1977, CLAS 2021), and provides the first measurements of antihyperon-nucleon scattering. The results constrain theoretical models of baryon-baryon interactions (Polinder 2007, Haidenbauer 2015, Haidenbauer 2018) and address the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron star structure, where hyperon appearance in the core softens the equation of state. The method opens a new direction for hyperon-nucleon studies at e+e- colliders, and the authors discuss prospects for the future Super Tau-Charm Factory (STCF), which could produce 10^12 to 10^13 J/psi per year and enable measurements of Omega- nucleon interactions and polarization-dependent cross sections.
