# Data-driven evaluations of Euclidean windows to scrutinize hadronic vacuum polarization

> In this paper, we discuss how windows in Euclidean time can be used to isolate the origin of potential conflicts between evaluations of the hadronic-vacuum-polarization (HVP) contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon in lattice QCD and from $e^+e^-\to\text{hadrons}$ cross-section data. We provide phenomenological comparison numbers evaluated from $e^+e^-\to\text{hadrons}$ data for the window quantities most commonly studied in lattice QCD, complete with the correlations among them. We discuss and evaluate modifications of window parameters that could be useful in dissecting the energy dependence of tensions in the HVP integral and emphasize that further optimizations require a precise knowledge of the full covariance matrix in lattice-QCD calculations as well.

# Data-driven evaluations of Euclidean windows to scrutinize hadronic vacuum polarization

## Key claim

The paper provides phenomenological comparison numbers from e+e- to hadrons data for Euclidean window quantities used in lattice QCD, complete with correlations, to help isolate the origin of the 2.1 sigma tension between the BMWc lattice result for hadronic vacuum polarization (HVP) and the data-driven determination, but notes that further optimization of window parameters requires precise knowledge of the full covariance matrix in lattice-QCD calculations.

## Method

The authors evaluate window quantities by applying smooth weight functions in Euclidean time to the time-momentum representation of the HVP integral, then map these to corresponding weight functions in center-of-mass energy using the known kernel functions. They compute the standard short-distance, intermediate, and long-distance windows with parameters t0=0.4 fm, t1=1.0 fm, Delta=0.15 fm, and extend this to a finer seven-window decomposition with 0.3 fm intervals, using the merging procedure from the White Paper (Aoyama 2020) to account for tensions in the e+e- database.

## Key numbers

* Total HVP from e+e- data: 693.0(3.9) x 10^{-10}, consistent with the White Paper value 693.1(4.0) x 10^{-10}
* Standard intermediate window (0.4 to 1.0 fm) from e+e- data: 229.4(1.4) x 10^{-10} for all channels
* BMWc lattice result for intermediate window: 236.7(1.4) x 10^{-10}, producing a 3.7 sigma tension with the e+e- value in this window
* Standard long-distance window from e+e- data: 395.1(2.4) x 10^{-10}, dominated by the 2pi channel at 342.3(2.3) x 10^{-10} (87 percent)
* Standard short-distance window from e+e- data: 68.4(5) x 10^{-10}
* The 2pi channel contributes 494.3(3.6) x 10^{-10} to the total HVP, with 69.2 percent in the long-distance window

## Context

This work extends the window method first suggested by Blum (2018) and further developed by Lehner (2020) for comparing lattice QCD and data-driven HVP evaluations. It provides the phenomenological reference numbers needed to interpret the tension between the BMWc lattice result (Borsanyi 2020) and the e+e- based determination, which is 2.1 sigma for the total HVP but increases to 3.7 sigma in the intermediate window. The paper enables more detailed scrutiny of the energy dependence of this tension through the finer window decomposition and correlation matrices, and encourages lattice collaborations to compute these windows and make covariance matrices available for further optimization.
