# Stability of the $β$-equilibrated dense matter and core-crust transition in neutron stars

> The stability of the $β$-equilibrated dense nuclear matter is analyzed with respect to the thermodynamic stability conditions. Based on the density dependent M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, the effects of the nuclear incompressibility on the proton fraction in neutron stars and the location of the inner edge of their crusts and core-crust transition density and pressure are investigated. The high-density behavior of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter satisfies the constraints from the observed flow data of heavy-ion collisions. The neutron star properties studied using $β$-equilibrated neutron star matter obtained from this effective interaction for a pure hadronic model agree with the recent observations of the massive compact stars. The density, pressure and proton fraction at the inner edge separating the liquid core from the solid crust of neutron stars are determined to be $ρ_t=$ 0.0938 fm$^{-3}$, P$_t=$ 0.5006 MeV fm$^{-3}$ and x$_{p(t)}=$ 0.0308, respectively.

## Key claim

Using the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, the core-crust transition density, pressure, and proton fraction in neutron stars are determined via thermodynamic stability conditions, with the results depending on the nuclear incompressibility parameter \(n\) and agreeing with recent observations of massive compact stars.

## Method

The authors calculate the equation of state (EoS) of asymmetric nuclear matter using the DDM3Y effective interaction, with density dependence parameters fixed by the saturation density and energy of symmetric nuclear matter. The \(\beta\)-equilibrium proton fraction is obtained both approximately (via symmetry energy) and exactly (by solving the chemical potential condition). The core-crust transition is located by finding the density at which the thermodynamic stability condition \(V_{\text{thermal}} = 0\) is violated, using the condition that the uniform npe matter becomes unstable against small density fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Transition density \(\rho_t = 0.0938\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) for \(n = 2/3\).
- Transition pressure \(P_t = 0.5006\ \text{MeV fm}^{-3}\) for \(n = 2/3\).
- Proton fraction at transition \(x_{p(t)} = 0.0308\) for \(n = 2/3\).
- Symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_\infty = 274.7 \pm 7.4\ \text{MeV}\).
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation \(E_{\text{sym}}(\rho_0) = 30.71 \pm 0.26\ \text{MeV}\).
- Slope of symmetry energy \(L = 45.11 \pm 0.02\ \text{MeV}\).
- Isospin-dependent part of isobaric incompressibility \(K_\tau = -408.97 \pm 3.01\ \text{MeV}\).
- Maximum \(\beta\)-equilibrium proton fraction from exact calculation \(x_p \sim 0.0422\) at \(\rho \sim 1.35 \rho_0\).
- Saturation density \(\rho_0 = 0.1533\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Saturation energy per nucleon \(\epsilon_0 = -15.26\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic method for locating the neutron star core-crust transition, previously applied by Kubis (2004, 2007) and Lattimer (2007), using the DDM3Y interaction which also satisfies heavy-ion flow data constraints from Danielewicz (2002). The results for transition density and pressure are in close agreement with those obtained using the SLy4 interaction by Moustakidis (2012) and with other theoretical calculations by Seif (2014) for high nuclear incompressibility. The EoS is stiff enough at high densities to support massive compact stars observed by Demorest (2010) and Antoniadis (2013), while the symmetry energy remains supersoft as preferred by FOPI/GSI experimental data.
