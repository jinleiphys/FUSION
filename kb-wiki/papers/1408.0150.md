# The Proton Radius from Bayesian Inference

> The methods of Bayesian statistics are used to extract the value of the proton radius from the elastic $ep$ scattering data in a model independent way. To achieve that goal a large number of parametrizations (equivalent to neural network schemes) are considered and ranked by their conditional probability $P(\mathrm{parametrization}\,|\,\mathrm{data})$ instead of using the minimal error criterion. As a result the most probable proton radii values ($r_E^p=0.899\pm 0.003$ fm, $r_M^p=0.879\pm 0.007$ fm) are obtained and systematic error due to freedom in the choice of parametrization is estimated. Correcting the data for the two photon exchange effect turns out to be important to obtain the agreement between the $r_E^p$ and $r_M^p$ values. The results disagree with recent muonic atom measurements.

## Key claim

Using Bayesian model comparison to rank thousands of neural-network parametrizations of the proton form factors, the authors extract the most probable proton charge radius as \(r_E^p = 0.899 \pm 0.003\) fm and magnetic radius as \(r_M^p = 0.879 \pm 0.007\) fm from elastic electron-proton scattering data, but only after subtracting the two-photon exchange correction; these values disagree with the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift result of Pohl (2010).

## Method

The authors fit elastic \(ep\) cross-section data (parametrized by the one-photon exchange formula) and polarization transfer data using feed-forward neural networks with one hidden layer, where the electric and magnetic form factors are jointly parametrized as \(G_E = (1 - Q^2 o_E) G_D\) and \(G_M = \mu_p (1 - Q^2 o_M) G_D\) with \(G_D = (1 + Q^2/0.71\ \mathrm{GeV}^2)^{-2}\). Models are grouped by the number of hidden units (up to 40), and each model is ranked by its Bayesian evidence \(P(\mathcal{D}|\mathcal{N})\) rather than by \(\chi^2\) minimization; the two-photon exchange correction calculated in Graczyk (2013) is subtracted from the cross-section data before fitting.

## Key numbers

* Proton charge radius: \(r_E^p = 0.899 \pm 0.003\) fm, obtained with \(Q^2_{\mathrm{cutoff}} = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and TPE correction applied.
* Proton magnetic radius: \(r_M^p = 0.879 \pm 0.007\) fm, obtained with \(Q^2_{\mathrm{cutoff}} = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\) and TPE correction applied.
* Without TPE correction, the difference \(r_E^p - r_M^p\) is \(0.09\) fm; with TPE correction it reduces to \(0.02\) fm.
* The expected value of \(r_E^p\) from the evidence-weighted average over models is \(0.8989 \pm 0.0001\) fm (systematic uncertainty from parametrization choice) for \(Q^2_{\mathrm{cutoff}} = 1\ \mathrm{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the Bayesian neural-network approach previously applied to nucleon form factors by Graczyk (2010, 2011) and to two-photon exchange corrections by Graczyk (2013), now focused on the low-\(Q^2\) region for proton radius extraction. The results agree with earlier frequentist analyses by Sick (2003) and Blunden (2005) but disagree with the muonic hydrogen measurement of Pohl (2010), leaving the "proton radius puzzle" unresolved. The Bayesian framework enables future quantitative studies of the dependence on data set selection and \(Q^2\) cutoff.
