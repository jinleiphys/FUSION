# Spontaneous fission half-lives of nuclei in a phenomenological model

> A simple phenomenological model, based on the Świ{\c a}tecki idea for evaluation of the spontaneous fission half-lives is proposed. The model contains only one adjustable parameter fixed to the data for even-even nuclei and two additional hindrance factors to the life-times, which give the effect of odd particles. A good agreement with the experimental data for all fissioning nuclei is achieved.

## Key claim

The authors propose a simple phenomenological model for spontaneous fission half-lives that requires only one adjustable parameter for even-even nuclei plus two constant hindrance factors for odd-A and odd-odd nuclei, achieving "surprisingly good agreement" with experimental data across all fissioning nuclei, but with the caveat that the quality for odd nuclei is "much worse" because a single constant cannot capture angular momentum and parity effects.

## Method

The authors follow the Swiatecki (1955) idea of subtracting the experimental ground-state microscopic energy correction δM (the difference between experimental mass and the LSD macroscopic mass) from the logarithm of the spontaneous fission half-life. They fit a global trend function f(Z) as two straight lines in proton number Z (one for Z < 104, a constant for Z ≥ 104) using 62 even-even fissioning isotopes, then add constant hindrance factors of 10.3 for odd-A and 17.8 for odd-odd nuclei to account for odd-particle effects.

## Key numbers

- The adjustable parameter k = 7.5 MeV⁻¹ multiplies the microscopic energy correction δM in the half-life formula.
- The global trend function f(Z) is given by −4·Z + 371.2 for Z < 104 and −42.8 for Z ≥ 104.
- Hindrance factors: 10.3 for odd-A nuclei and 17.8 for odd-odd nuclei.
- Root mean square deviation of log₁₀(T₁/₂^sf) for even-even isotopes with Z < 104 is 1.52.
- RMS deviation increases to 2.02 when odd-A and odd-odd nuclei are included.
- RMS deviation reaches 2.47 when super-heavy isotopes (where the liquid drop barrier vanishes) are included.

## Context

This work extends the original Swiatecki (1955) idea of correlating spontaneous fission half-lives with ground-state mass deviations, using the modern Lublin-Strasbourg Drop (LSD) mass formula from Pomorski and Dudek (2003) and microscopic corrections from Möller et al. (1995). The model enables rough estimates of fission half-lives for undiscovered isotopes when combined with binding energy predictions from existing mass tables, and can be paired with the alpha decay formula from Zdeb and Warda (2013) to assess stability of unknown nuclei.
