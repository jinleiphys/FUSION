# Variational Calculations using Low-Momentum Potentials with Smooth Cutoffs

> Recent variational calculations of the deuteron and the triton illustrate that simple wave function ansatze become more effective after evolving the nucleon-nucleon potential to lower momentum (``V_lowk''). However, wave function artifacts from the use of sharp cutoffs in relative momentum decrease effectiveness for small cutoffs (< 2 fm^-1) and slow down convergence in harmonic oscillator bases. These sharp cutoff artifacts are eliminated when V_lowk is generated using a sufficiently smooth cutoff regulator.

# Variational Calculations using Low-Momentum Potentials with Smooth Cutoffs

## Key claim

Simple variational wave function ansätze for the deuteron and triton become more effective after evolving the nucleon-nucleon potential to lower momentum, but only if the low-momentum potential is generated with a sufficiently smooth cutoff regulator rather than a sharp cutoff; sharp cutoffs below 2 fm⁻¹ introduce wave function artifacts that degrade variational estimates and slow convergence in harmonic oscillator bases.

## Method

The authors construct low-momentum potentials (V_lowk) using an exponential smooth regulator f(k) = exp[-(k²/Λ²)⁴] applied in each partial wave, starting from the Argonne v₁₈ potential. They use an energy-dependent renormalization group equation to evolve the bare potential to lower cutoffs, then convert to an energy-independent hermitian potential via a Gram-Schmidt procedure. Variational calculations for the deuteron use two analytic trial wave function forms (a Salpeter-type ansatz and a multi-pole expansion), while triton calculations diagonalize the Hamiltonian in a truncated harmonic oscillator basis built from Jacobi coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy E_d ≈ -2.2246 MeV (reference value used for comparison)
- For the Salpeter-type ansatz with sharp cutoff, best variational energy reaches a minimum deviation of less than 3 keV at Λ ≈ 1.5 fm⁻¹, but worsens at smaller cutoffs
- For the multi-pole ansatz with smooth cutoff, variational estimates improve monotonically to the eV level at low cutoffs
- Triton convergence with smooth cutoff at Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹: energy within 2 keV of converged result at N_max = 20 and within 1 keV at N_max = 30
- Sharp cutoff triton calculations at Λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹ reach within about 200 keV with small basis but then converge extremely slowly beyond the 50 keV level

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Bogner (2005) which showed that V_lowk with sharp cutoffs improves variational calculations down to moderate cutoffs but suffers from degradation below 2 fm⁻¹ and slow basis convergence. The results are consistent with Viviani (2005), who demonstrated faster convergence for nonlocal interactions in hyperspherical harmonics calculations of 3- and 4-body nuclei. The smooth cutoff V_lowk enables more efficient variational calculations for few- and many-body systems, and allows unambiguous density matrix expansions for nuclear energy density functional theory.
