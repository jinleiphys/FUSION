# Jets in nuclear collisions

> Ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC open exciting new possibilities for jet physics studies in the presence of hot and dense nuclear matter. Recent theoretical advances in understanding the QCD multi-parton dynamics provide a good description of the quenching in the single and double inclusive high-pT hadron spectra. Measurement of the redistribution of the lost energy and the corresponding increase in the soft hadron multiplicities is the next critical step in elucidating the modification of the jet properties in the nuclear environment.

# Jets in Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim

The paper argues that while current theoretical models based on non-Abelian energy loss (GLV approach) successfully describe the observed suppression of single and double inclusive high-\(p_T\) hadron spectra in heavy ion collisions, the next critical step is measuring the redistribution of lost energy into soft hadrons, which will test whether the medium-induced increase in soft multiplicities scales as \(C_A/C_F\) rather than the vacuum bremsstrahlung ratio.

## Method

The author reviews the perturbative QCD formalism for jet production in vacuum and in nuclear matter, comparing analytic results for vacuum parton showers (including Sudakov form factors and color coherence) with medium-modified calculations using the GLV approach to first order in opacity with 1+1D Bjorken expansion. Numerical predictions for the nuclear modification factor \(R^{h_1}_{AA}\) and the away-side correlation function \(C_2(\Delta\phi)\) are compared to existing data from RHIC and SPS, and the redistribution of lost energy is estimated via Poisson-distributed medium-induced gluon emission with a plasmon frequency cutoff.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of charged hadron multiplicities for gluon versus quark jets in vacuum: \(\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle_{\rm g-jet} / \langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle_{\rm q-jet} = 1.4 - 1.6\) over \(E^*_g = 5 - 40\) GeV, compared to analytic expectation of 1.5 from \(\sqrt{C_A/C_F}\).
- Typical charged hadron multiplicity for gluon jets of \(E^*_g = 10 - 20\) GeV: \(\langle N_{\rm ch}\rangle_{\rm g-jet} = 6 - 10\).
- Transverse momentum broadening from elastic scattering in cold nuclear matter at midrapidity: \(\langle {\bf k}_T^2 \rangle \simeq 0.7\) GeV\(^2\) per jet.
- Initial effective gluon rapidity density in hot nuclear matter: \(dN^g/dy \sim 1000\).
- Medium-induced increase in parton multiplicity relative to vacuum bremsstrahlung: \(\sim 25 - 35\%\).
- Bremsstrahlung gluons from secondary rescattering appear at transverse momenta \(p_T \sim 600\) MeV.
- Attenuation in double inclusive hadron production is \(25\% - 50\%\) larger than suppression in single inclusive spectra.

## Context

This conference paper reviews the theoretical framework for jet modification in heavy ion collisions, extending the vacuum QCD parton shower formalism (Dokshitzer 1978, Field) to include medium effects via the GLV approach (Gyulassy 2000). It builds on earlier work on nuclear shadowing and dynamical parton mass (Qiu 2003) and Cronin effect studies (Cronin 1974, Vitev 2002). The paper enables future experimental searches at RHIC and LHC for the redistribution of lost jet energy into soft hadrons, with preliminary RHIC results (Wang 2004) already hinting at energy recovery into \(p_T \leq 2 - 3\) GeV hadrons.
