# A new criterion for the existence of dark matter in neutron stars

> The tidal deformability and the radius of neutron stars are observables, which have been used to constrain the neutron star equation of state and explore the composition in neutron stars. We investigated the radius and tidal deformability of dark matter admixed neutron stars (DANSs) by utilizing the two-fluid TOV equations. Assuming that the dark matter modeled as ideal fermi gas or self-interacting bosons, for a series of DANSs at a fixed mass, it is shown that there exists the DANSs with smaller normal matter radii but larger tidal deformabilities. This negative correlation does not exist in the normal neutron stars.In other words, if the observation finds that the neutron stars with a fixed mass exists such a situation, that is, having a smaller observed radius but a larger tidal deformability, it will indicate the existence of dark matter in neutron stars.In addition, the relevant neutron star observations can also be used to constrain the dark matter parameters.

# A new criterion for the existence of dark matter in neutron stars

## Key claim

If a series of neutron stars with the same or extremely similar mass are observed, and those with smaller normal matter radii have larger tidal deformabilities, this negative correlation would indicate the presence of dark matter in those neutron stars. This criterion holds for both self-interacting bosonic and fermionic dark matter models, but the specific mass range where the negative correlation appears depends on the dark matter particle mass and the normal matter equation of state.

## Method

The authors solve the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for dark matter admixed neutron stars (DANSs), where normal matter and dark matter interact only through gravity. Dark matter is modeled either as self-interacting bosons (with coupling constant fixed to λ = π) or as an ideal Fermi gas at zero temperature, while normal matter is described by the APR3 (soft) or DDME2 (stiff) equations of state. The tidal deformability and normal matter radius are computed for DANSs at fixed stellar masses with varying dark matter mass fractions.

## Key numbers

- For self-interacting bosonic dark matter with particle mass mχ,b = 275 MeV and the APR3 EOS, the dimensionless tidal deformability of a 1.4 M⊙ DANS at high dark matter mass fraction (FX ∼ 0.8) can reach Λ1.4 ∼ 8000, compared to Λ1.4 ∼ 400 for normal neutron stars with the APR3 EOS.
- For self-interacting bosonic dark matter with mχ,b = 200 MeV, the negative correlation between Λ and RN appears for DANSs with mass 1.75 M⊙; for mχ,b = 275 MeV, it appears only for masses below 1.5 M⊙; for mχ,b > 350 MeV, only DANSs with mass around 1 M⊙ or less show the negative correlation.
- For fermionic dark matter modeled as ideal Fermi gas, a larger particle mass (mχ,f = 600 MeV) is needed to support DANSs with similar mass compared to the bosonic case.
- The GW170817 event constrained the tidal deformability of a 1.4 M⊙ neutron star to Λ1.4 = 190^{+390}_{-120} (Abbott 2018).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of dark matter admixed neutron stars using two-fluid TOV equations (Ciarcelluti 2011, Giangrandi 2022, Leung 2022) and investigations of how dark matter affects neutron star observables (Deliyergiyev 2019, Nelson 2019). The key advance is identifying a specific observational signature, a negative correlation between radius and tidal deformability at fixed mass, that distinguishes DANSs from normal neutron stars and from twin stars (Blaschke 2020), which only allow two radii at a given mass. The criterion could enable future gravitational wave and X-ray observations (such as from NICER and LIGO/Virgo) to both detect dark matter in neutron stars and constrain dark matter particle parameters.
