# Review of Neutron Yield from ($α$, n) Reactions: Data, Methods, and Prospects

> Understanding the radiogenic neutron production rate through the ($α$, n) reaction is crucial in many areas of physics, including dark matter searches, neutrino studies, and nuclear astrophysics. In addition to its relevance for fundamental research, the ($α$, n) reaction also plays a significant role in nuclear energy technologies, for example by contributing to neutron production in subcritical systems using UO$_{2}$, as well as in applications such as medical physics. This review examines the current state of ($α$, n) yield calculations and neutron spectra, describes the computational tools used for their estimation, and discusses the available cross-section data. We explore the uncertainties affecting ($α$, n) yield estimations and propose a strategy to enhance their accuracy. Furthermore, this paper discusses and emphasizes the need for new measurements of ($α$, n) cross-sections for a variety of relevant materials. Such measurements are essential for improving neutron flux predictions, which are crucial for reducing uncertainties in sensitivity estimates for next-generation physics experiments operating in the keV--MeV range.

## Key claim

Accurate predictions of radiogenic neutron yields from (alpha,n) reactions are critical for reducing uncertainties in sensitivity calculations for next-generation physics experiments in the keV-MeV range, but significant uncertainties remain, typically estimated at 30% for many materials, driven by missing cross-section measurements, limited energy coverage, and inconsistencies among different experimentally measured cross-sections.

## Method

The authors conducted a comprehensive review of (alpha,n) yield calculations, computational tools, and experimental cross-section databases, comparing results from the TALYS and EMPIRE nuclear model codes with evaluated libraries (JENDL-5 and TENDL-2021) and experimental data from the EXFOR database. They catalogued which isotopes have measured or evaluated cross-sections, identified gaps for mid-Z materials, and assessed uncertainties arising from uranium/thorium contamination measurements, material chemical composition, cross-section data, and theoretical models.

## Key numbers

- Uncertainties in neutron yield calculations are typically estimated to be within 30% for many nuclides and materials.
- JENDL-5 includes 18 nuclides for alpha-induced reactions in the energy range up to 15 MeV.
- TENDL-2021 contains evaluations for about 2800 isotopes and up to energies of 200 MeV for some cases.
- The EXFOR library contains numerical data sets and bibliographical information from 22,000 experiments.
- For heavy isotopes, NeuCBOT predicts an (alpha,n) yield of 6.9 x 10^-10 n/alpha at 10 MeV (for an unspecified isotope).
- For 40Ar(alpha,n)43Ca, one experimental value is available at E_alpha = 7.4 MeV.
- For 42Ca(alpha,n)45Ti, experimental cross sections are available for E_alpha between 5.7 MeV and 12 MeV.
- For 116Cd(alpha,n)50Sn^m, two experimental cross sections are available at E_alpha = 6.18 MeV and 7 MeV.

## Context

This review extends earlier comparisons between TALYS and EMPIRE codes reported by Zakhary and Kudryavtsev (2020), and builds on the detailed comparison of JENDL and TENDL versions performed by Mendoza (2020). The paper identifies specific priorities for new (alpha,n) cross-section measurements on mid-Z materials commonly used in rare event physics experiments (such as dark matter searches and neutrino studies), and emphasizes the need for improved understanding of correlated gamma-ray emissions and (alpha,ngamma) cross-sections for low-background detector applications.
