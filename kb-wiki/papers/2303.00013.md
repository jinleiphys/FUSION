# What neutron stars tell about the hadron-quark phase transition: a Bayesian study

> The existence of quark matter inside the heaviest neutron stars has been the topic of numerous recent studies, many of them suggesting that a phase transition to strongly interacting conformal matter inside neutron stars is feasible. Here we examine this hybrid star scenario using a soft and a stiff hadronic model, a constituent quark model with three quark flavours, and applying a smooth crossover transition between the two. Within a Bayesian framework, we study the effect of up-to-date constraints from neutron star observations on the equation-of-state parameters and various neutron star observables. Our results show that a pure quark core is only possible if the maximum mass of neutron stars is below $\sim2.35~M_\odot$. However, we also find, consistently with other studies, that a peak in the speed of sound, exceeding $1/3$, is highly favoured by astrophysical measurements, which might indicate the percolation of hadrons at $\sim3-4n_0$. Even though our prediction for the phase transition parameters varies depending on the specific astrophysical constraints utilized, the position of the speed of sound peak only changes slightly, while the existence of pure quark matter below $\sim4 n_0$, using our parameterization, is disfavoured. On the other hand, the preferred range for the EoS shows signs of conformality above $\sim4n_0$. Additionally, we present the difference in the upper bounds of radius estimates using the full probability density data and sharp cut-offs, and stress the necessity of using the former.

## Key claim

The authors find that a pure quark core in neutron stars is only possible if the maximum mass of neutron stars is below approximately 2.35 solar masses, and that a peak in the speed of sound exceeding 1/3 is highly favoured by astrophysical measurements, which might indicate hadron percolation at around 3-4 times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state by combining two hadronic relativistic mean field models (the soft SFHo model and the stiff DD2 model) with a three-flavor constituent quark-meson model (the extended linear sigma model), using a smooth fifth-order polynomial interpolation between the hadronic and quark sectors in the intermediate density region. They perform a Bayesian analysis on a four-dimensional parameter space (sigma meson mass, vector meson-quark coupling, and the central density and width of the interpolation region) using a pre-sampled grid of approximately 18,000 equations of state, incorporating constraints from pulsar mass measurements, NICER mass-radius observations, the tidal deformability from GW170817, and compatibility with perturbative QCD calculations.

## Key numbers

- The maximum mass of neutron stars must be below approximately 2.35 solar masses for a pure quark core to be possible.
- The speed of sound peak exceeding 1/3 is highly favoured, occurring at approximately 3-4 times nuclear saturation density (n0).
- Pure quark matter below approximately 4 times nuclear saturation density is disfavoured by the authors' parameterization.
- The hadronic SFHo model has a maximum stable neutron star mass of approximately 2.05 solar masses, while the DD2 model reaches approximately 2.42 solar masses.
- The sigma meson mass is varied between 290 and 700 MeV, and the vector meson-quark coupling gV is varied between 0 and 10.
- The interpolation central density is varied between 2 n0 and 5 n0, with the interpolation width between 1 n0 and 4 n0.
- The perturbative QCD matching point uses a baryon chemical potential of 2.6 GeV, a baryon density of 6.47 per cubic femtometer, and a pressure of 3823 MeV per cubic femtometer.
- The chirp mass of GW170817 is (1.186 plus or minus 0.001) solar masses.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars using effective quark models, particularly the authors' own extended linear sigma model (Kovacs 2021), by incorporating a Bayesian framework with the most recent astrophysical constraints from NICER, pulsar timing, and gravitational wave observations. The results are consistent with model-agnostic studies (Annala 2019, Jiang 2022) that find a peak in the speed of sound above the conformal limit, and with studies suggesting that strong first-order phase transitions are disfavoured (Christian 2019, Gorda 2022). The analysis enables future work to combine these constraints with additional observations from upcoming gravitational wave detectors and X-ray missions to further narrow the allowed parameter space for the hadron-quark phase transition.
