# Fock exchange terms in non-linear Quantum Hadrodynamics

> We propose a method to introduce Fock term contributions in relativistic models of fermions coupled to mesons, including self-interactions for the mesonic fields. We show that effects on equilibrium properties and on the dynamical response of the fermionic system can be consistently accounted for. Some implications on equilibrium properties of asymmetric nuclear matter are discussed.

# Fock exchange terms in non-linear Quantum Hadrodynamics

## Key claim

The authors propose a method to include Fock exchange contributions in relativistic quantum hadrodynamics models that include non-linear self-interactions of the scalar meson field, using a truncation scheme based on an expansion in powers of 1/N (where N=4 for nucleon spin-isospin degrees of freedom). The method is not perturbative in the coupling constants, but the abstract's claim that effects can be "consistently accounted for" is qualified by the approximation that derivatives of meson fields are neglected relative to mass terms, which is not justified for light mesons like pions.

## Method

The authors start from the QHD-I sigma-omega model with non-linear scalar field self-interactions (phi^3 and phi^4 terms) and derive a kinetic equation for the Wigner transform of the one-body fermionic density matrix. They eliminate the meson fields in favor of fermionic operators by neglecting derivative terms in the meson field equations, then expand the scalar field operator in powers of the scalar density operator and truncate at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion. The resulting Hartree-Fock kinetic equation and energy-momentum tensor are applied to infinite nuclear matter at equilibrium, with parameters fitted to symmetric nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Binding energy at saturation: E/A = -15.75 MeV
- Nucleon effective (Dirac) mass at saturation: M_0^* = 0.7 M (where M is the bare nucleon mass)
- Incompressibility modulus: K_0 = 250 MeV
- Fitted parameters: f_S = 10.1, f_V = 4.1, A = 0.06, B = 0.01
- Symmetry energy coefficient from non-linear Hartree-Fock (NLHF) calculation at normal density: a_4 = 24 MeV (compared to accepted value of 28-30 MeV)
- Kinetic contribution to a_4: approximately 12 MeV (approximately epsilon_F/3)
- Of the remaining approximately 16 MeV, almost two thirds come from exchange terms
- The truncation scheme is shown to converge up to densities of order 4 rho_0

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field (RMF) approximation of Quantum Hadrodynamics (Walecka, 1974; Serot and Walecka, 1986) to include Fock exchange terms while retaining the non-linear scalar self-interactions introduced by Boguta and Bodmer (1977) to fix the nuclear compressibility. Previous Hartree-Fock calculations for QHD had been limited to linear models (Horowitz and Serot, 1983; Bouyssy et al., 1987) or had linearized the scalar field equation (Bernardos et al., 1993). The method enables self-consistent treatment of both equilibrium and dynamical properties of asymmetric nuclear matter, and indicates that exchange contributions significantly reduce the required strength of charged meson (rho meson) contributions to the symmetry energy around normal density. The approach is also applicable to other fermion-boson models such as the Quark Meson Coupling model (Guichon, 1988) and the Linear Quark Meson model.
