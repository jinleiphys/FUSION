# Long Range Correlations and the Soft Ridge in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider experiments exhibit correlations peaked in relative azimuthal angle and extended in rapidity. Called the ridge, this peak occurs both with and without a jet trigger. We argue that the untriggered ridge arises when particles formed by flux tubes in an early glasma stage later manifest transverse flow. Combining a blast wave model of flow fixed by single-particle spectra with a simple description of the glasma, we find excellent agreement with current data.

# Long Range Correlations and the Soft Ridge in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

## Key claim
The untriggered soft ridge observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions arises from early-stage rapidity correlations from Glasma flux tubes combined with late-stage transverse flow, but the model only addresses long-range correlations with pair separations greater than one unit in rapidity and does not explain the rapidity dependence within one to two units.

## Method
The authors combine a Color Glass Condensate (CGC) Glasma description of flux tubes with a blast wave model of transverse flow. The Glasma provides initial-state correlations from flux tubes of transverse size ~Q_s^{-1} that are uniform in rapidity, while the blast wave model describes how transverse flow focuses particles into a narrow azimuthal region. The correlation function is computed by integrating the spatial correlation function at freeze-out over the Cooper-Frye freeze-out surface, using blast wave parameters fixed by single-particle spectra from Kiyomichi (2005). The centrality and energy dependence of the ridge height and azimuthal width are then compared to preliminary STAR data at 62 and 200 GeV for Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers
- The saturation scale Q_s is approximately 1-2 GeV.
- The rapidity density of gluons scales as dN/dy ~ alpha_s^{-1} Q_s^2 R_A^2.
- The integrated correlation strength in the Glasma scales as R ~ (Q_s R)^{-2}.
- The product R dN/dy scales as kappa alpha_s(Q_s)^{-1}, where kappa is an energy-independent constant fixed by 200 GeV data.
- At 62 GeV, Q_s^2 drops by approximately 1/2 relative to the 200 GeV value.
- Blast wave velocities at 62 GeV are reduced by 5% and temperatures by 10% relative to 200 GeV values, following Kiyomichi (2005).

## Context
This work extends the Color Glass Condensate framework of McLerran and Venugopalan (1994) and Lappi (2006) to explain the soft ridge observed by STAR (Daugherity, 2006) and PHOBOS (Wenger, 2008) without a jet trigger. It builds on Voloshin's (2003) connection between flow and momentum correlations, and uses blast wave parameters from Schnedermann (1993) and Kiyomichi (2005). The model enables future work incorporating quantum corrections to the Glasma and viscous hydrodynamic corrections to describe the rapidity dependence within one to two units, as well as extensions to jet-triggered ridge measurements.
