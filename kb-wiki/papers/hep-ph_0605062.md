# Stable TeV - Black Hole Remnants at the LHC: Discovery through Di-Jet Suppression, Mono-Jet Emission and a Supersonic Boom in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The production of Large Extra Dimension (LXD) Black Holes (BHs), with a new, fundamental mass scale of $M_f = 1$ TeV, has been predicted to occur at the Large Hadron Collider, LHC, with the formidable rate of $10^8$ per year in p-p collisions at full energy, 14 TeV, and at full luminosity. We show that such LXD-BH formation will be experimentally observable at the LHC by the complete disappearance of all very high $p_t$ ($> 500$ GeV) back-to-back correlated Di-Jets of total mass $M > M_f = 1$ TeV. We suggest to complement this clear cut-off signal at $M > 2*500$ GeV in the di-jet-correlation function by detecting the subsequent, Hawking-decay products of the LXD-BHs, namely either multiple high energy ($> 100$ GeV) SM Mono-Jets (i.e. away-side jet missing), sprayed off the evaporating BHs isentropically into all directions or the thermalization of the multiple overlapping Hawking-radiation in a Heckler-Kapusta-Plasma. Microcanonical quantum statistical calculations of the Hawking evaporation process for these LXD-BHs show that cold black hole remnants (BHRs) of Mass $\sim M_f$ remain leftover as the ashes of these spectacular Di-Jet-suppressed events. Strong Di-Jet suppression is also expected with Heavy Ion beams at the LHC, due to Quark-Gluon-Plasma induced jet attenuation at medium to low jet energies, $p_t < 200$ GeV. The (Mono-)Jets in these events can be used to trigger for Tsunami-emission of secondary compressed QCD-matter at well defined Mach-angles, both at the trigger side and at the awayside (missing) jet. The Machshock-angles allow for a direct measurement of both the equation of state EoS and the speed of sound $c_s$ via supersonic bang in the "big bang" matter.

## Key claim

The paper claims that Large Extra Dimension (LXD) black holes with a fundamental mass scale of 1 TeV will be observable at the LHC through the complete disappearance of back-to-back correlated di-jets with total mass above 1 TeV, and that this signal can be complemented by detecting multiple high-energy mono-jets from Hawking evaporation or a thermalized Heckler-Kapusta-Plasma; however, the claim depends on the validity of the ADD model with extra dimensions and on the assumption that the classical geometric cross section for black hole production remains valid at energies near the fundamental scale.

## Method

The author computes black hole production cross sections using the classical geometric formula sigma(M) approximately equal to pi times the Schwarzschild radius squared, integrated over parton distribution functions (CTEQ tables) for proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 14 TeV. Microcanonical quantum statistical calculations of the Hawking evaporation process are performed, incorporating back-reaction of emitted quanta, to determine the mass evolution and particle emission spectra for black holes with initial masses near 1 TeV. For the heavy ion collision analysis, the author applies linearized fluid-dynamical calculations of Mach shock cones produced by jets propagating through quark-gluon plasma, including the effects of collective flow on the Mach angles.

## Key numbers

- Fundamental mass scale M_f = 1 TeV
- Predicted black hole production rate: 10^8 per year in p-p collisions at 14 TeV and full luminosity
- Di-jet suppression threshold: complete disappearance of di-jets with total mass M > 1 TeV, corresponding to individual jet p_t > 500 GeV each
- Mono-jet emission energy: average particle energy approximately 100 GeV from Hawking radiation
- Black hole production cross section: approximately 10 nanobarn (400 pb to 10 nb for d = 2 to 7)
- Schwarzschild radius for M_BH approximately M_f: R_H approximately 10^{-4} fm
- Energy density of Hawking radiation: greater than or equal to 10^9 TeV fm^{-3}
- QGP energy density for comparison: approximately 500 GeV fm^{-3}
- Mach cone angle for ideal QGP (c_s = 1/sqrt(3)): Delta-phi approximately pi plus or minus 0.96 radians
- Average radial flow velocity at RHIC: u_f approximately 0.6
- Speed of sound in deconfined phase: c_s = 1/sqrt(3) for ideal QGP

## Context

This work extends the ADD model of Arkani-Hamed, Dimopoulos, and Dvali (1998) for large extra dimensions and the black hole production predictions of Dimopoulos and Landsberg (2001) and Giddings and Thomas (2002) to specific LHC observables. The paper connects black hole phenomenology with heavy ion physics by proposing that di-jet suppression from black hole formation can be distinguished from quark-gluon plasma-induced jet attenuation studied at RHIC by Gyulassy, Vitev, and Wang (2003). The Mach shock analysis builds on earlier predictions by Stoecker (2004) and the linearized fluid-dynamical approach of Casalderrey-Solana (2004), and enables experimental determination of the QGP equation of state and speed of sound through measurement of Mach cone angles in heavy ion collisions at the LHC.
