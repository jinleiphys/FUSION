# Hyperon-nucleon interactions in effective field theory

> We have constructed the leading order hyperon-nucleon potential in a chiral Effective Field Theory approach. The chiral potential consists of one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms. The hyperon-nucleon interactions are derived using SU(3) symmetry, the nucleon-nucleon interaction is not considered explicitly since it can not be described well with a leading order chiral Effective Field Theory. We solve a regularized Lippmann-Schwinger equation and show that a good description of the available hyperon-nucleon scattering data is possible with five free parameters. The chiral potential can be used as further input for hypernucleus and hypernuclear matter calculations. Preliminary hypertriton calculations yielded the correct hypertriton binding energy.

# Hyperon-nucleon interactions in effective field theory

## Key claim

A leading-order chiral effective field theory potential, with five free parameters fitted to 35 low-energy hyperon-nucleon scattering data points, can describe the available YN scattering data reasonably well for cutoff values between 550 and 700 MeV, but the description is limited by the fact that leading-order phase shifts become too large at higher energies, a problem expected to be resolved at next-to-leading order.

## Method

The leading-order hyperon-nucleon potential was constructed from one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms, with SU(3) symmetry relating the couplings across different baryon-baryon channels. A regularized coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved in momentum space using a regulator function of the form exp[-(p'^4 + p^4)/Lambda^4], and the five low-energy constants were fitted directly to total cross sections and the inelastic capture ratio at rest, rather than to phase shifts, because no unique partial-wave analysis exists for YN scattering.

## Key numbers

- The fit used 35 YN data points (low-energy total cross sections and the inelastic capture ratio at rest from Swa62), yielding chi-squared values of 28.3 to 34.6 for cutoff masses Lambda = 550 to 700 MeV.
- The Lambda p singlet scattering length a_s^{Lambda p} = -1.91 fm and triplet scattering length a_t^{Lambda p} = -1.23 fm (for Lambda = 600 MeV).
- The Sigma+ p singlet scattering length a_s^{Sigma+ p} = -2.32 fm and triplet scattering length a_t^{Sigma+ p} = 0.65 fm (for Lambda = 600 MeV).
- The hypertriton binding energy was predicted as E_B = -2.34 to -2.36 MeV for cutoffs 550-700 MeV, compared to the experimental value of -2.354(50) MeV.
- The Lambda p cross section shows a cusp at the Sigma+ n threshold, peaking at 60 mb.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach previously applied to the nucleon-nucleon interaction by Epelbaum et al. (2005) to the hyperon-nucleon system, using the same Weinberg power-counting scheme. It differs from the next-to-leading order EFT analysis by Korpa et al. (2001), which used the Kaplan-Savage-Wise resummation scheme with 12 parameters and did not include explicit pseudoscalar-meson exchanges. The resulting potential enables calculations of hypernuclei and hypernuclear matter, with preliminary Faddeev calculations by Nogga (2006) showing that the chiral EFT yields the correct hypertriton binding energy, and four-body calculations for ^4_Lambda H and ^4_Lambda He giving reasonable Lambda separation energies though not reproducing the charge dependence.
