# The phi(1020) a0(980) S-wave scattering and hints for a new vector-isovector resonance

> We have studied the phi(1020)a0(980) S-wave scattering at threshold energies employing chiral Lagrangians coupled to vector mesons by minimal coupling. The interaction is described without new free parameters by considering the scalar isovector a0(980) resonance as dynamically generated in coupled channels, and demanding that the recently measured e+ e- -> phi(1020) f0(980) cross section is reproduced. For some realistic choices of the parameters, the presence of a dynamically generated isovector companion of the Y(2175) is revealed. We have also investigated the corrections to the e+ e- -> phi(1020) pi0 eta reaction cross section that arise from phi(1020)a0(980) re-scattering in the final state. They are typically large and modify substantially the cross section. For a suitable choice of parameters, the presence of the resonance would manifest itself as a clear peak at sqrt{s}~2.03 GeV in e+ e- -> phi(1020) pi0 eta.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether an isovector companion of the isoscalar Y(2175) resonance exists in the phi(1020) a0(980) S-wave channel, finding that for some realistic parameter choices a dynamically generated resonance appears, but the conclusion is conditional on the uncertain properties of the a0(980) resonance and the local term in the interaction kernel.

## Method

The authors employed chiral Lagrangians coupled to vector mesons by minimal coupling to describe phi(1020) a0(980) S-wave scattering at threshold energies. The a0(980) resonance was treated as dynamically generated in coupled channels (K anti-K and pi0 eta), and the interaction was constrained by requiring reproduction of the measured e+ e- -> phi(1020) f0(980) cross section. The phi(1020) a0(980) scattering amplitude was obtained by extracting the interaction kernel from triangular kaon-loop diagrams and solving a Bethe-Salpeter equation, with the loop function regularized either by a dispersion relation or a three-momentum cutoff.

## Key numbers

- The a0(980) pole position from the Bethe-Salpeter approach is 1.009 + i 0.056 GeV, with residue gamma^2_{K anti-K} = 24.73 - i 10.82 GeV^2
- The a0(980) pole position from the N/D approach is 1.055 + i 0.025 GeV, with residue gamma^2_{K anti-K} = 17.37 - i 24.77 GeV^2
- Fit 1 to e+ e- -> phi(1020) f0(980) data yields sqrt(-g^2) = 7.33 +/- 0.30 and subtraction constant a1 = -2.41 +/- 0.14
- Fit 2 yields sqrt(-g^2) = 5.21 +/- 0.12 and subtraction constant a1 = -2.61 +/- 0.14
- The Y(2175) resonance mass from BABAR is 2175 +/- 10 +/- 15 MeV with width 58 +/- 16 +/- 20 MeV
- The Belle Collaboration measured M_Y = 2079 +/- 13 +79/-28 MeV and Gamma_Y = 192 +/- 23 +25/-61 MeV
- A combined fit to BABAR and Belle data yields M_Y = 2117 +0.59/-0.49 MeV and Gamma_Y = 164 +69/-80 MeV
- For suitable parameters, a resonance peak appears at sqrt(s) ~ 2.03 GeV in e+ e- -> phi(1020) pi0 eta

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Alvarez-Ruso, Oller, Alarcon, 2010) of the phi(1020) f0(980) channel, where the Y(2175) was found to have a large mesonic phi(1020) f0(980) component. The present investigation searches for an isovector companion of the Y(2175) in the phi(1020) a0(980) system, contrasting with a Faddeev-type calculation by Torres et al. (2009) that found no isovector resonance. The results enable experimental searches for this possible resonance in e+ e- -> phi(1020) pi0 eta and e+ e- -> phi(1020) K+ K- reactions, where final state interactions had not been previously considered.
