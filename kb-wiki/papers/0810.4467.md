# Renormalization group and bound states

> Similarity renormalization group procedure identifies the role of bound states in the low-energy rate of change of effective coupling constant in a model Hamiltonian with asymptotic freedom.

## Key claim

The Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) procedure can control the low-energy growth of the effective coupling constant in an asymptotically free model, but only if the generator of the SRG transformations includes the interactions responsible for bound-state formation; otherwise, the coupling constant increases to huge values (e.g., \(g_\lambda \rightarrow |E_B|/b^M \sim 10^{10}\)) when the scale parameter \(\lambda\) drops below the binding energy scale.

## Method

The author constructs a soluble model Hamiltonian with factorized matrix elements \(H_{mn} = E_m \delta_{mn} - g \sqrt{E_m} \sqrt{E_n}\), where \(E_n\) are discrete positive eigenvalues of the free Hamiltonian \(H_0\). The SRG procedure is applied by rotating the basis states to produce a Hamiltonian \(H_\lambda\) with vanishing matrix elements between states differing in energy by more than \(\lambda\), using a generator \(T_\lambda = [G_\lambda, H_\lambda]\) where \(G_\lambda = f H_0 + (1-f) D_\lambda\) and \(D_\lambda\) is the diagonal part of \(H_\lambda\). The effective coupling constant \(g_\lambda\) is extracted from the lowest-energy matrix elements of \(H_\lambda\) as a function of the scale parameter \(\lambda\), for various values of the mixing parameter \(f\) (which controls how much interaction is included in the generator).

## Key numbers

- The model uses a geometric energy spectrum \(E_n = b^n\) with \(b = 4\), a cutoff \(N = 16\) and lower bound \(M = -25\), giving \(\Lambda = 4^{16} \sim 4 \times 10^9\).
- The initial coupling constant at infinite cutoff is \(g_{\lambda = \infty} = g_\Lambda \sim 4/100\).
- The bound-state energy is \(E_B \simeq -8 \times 10^{-6}\) (in the same units as the energy eigenvalues).
- For the generator with \(f=1\) (no interactions included), \(g_\lambda\) increases to \(\sim 10^{10}\) when \(\lambda\) decreases below the binding energy scale (around \(\ln \lambda / \ln b \sim -8\)).
- For the generator with \(f=0\) (full interactions included), the huge increase of \(g_\lambda\) is absent and the coupling constant never significantly exceeds 1.

## Context

This work extends the standard Wilsonian renormalization group approach (Wilson 1965, 1970) by applying the SRG procedure (Wegner 1994; Perry & Szpigel 2000; Bogner et al. 2006, 2007) to a soluble model with asymptotic freedom. The results show that the SRG can handle the non-perturbative regime where the coupling constant becomes order 1, which is a basic obstacle to describing bound states in asymptotically free theories like QCD. The model also suggests a connection between asymptotic freedom and RG limit cycles (Wilson 1970; Braaten 2008), where a small imaginary perturbation leads to a limit cycle with period \(b^p \sim e^{\pi/h}\), predicting bound states with binding energies forming a geometric series.
