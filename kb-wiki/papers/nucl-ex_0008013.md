# Spectral Measurements of Neutrons from Pb, W, and Na Targets Irradiated by 0.8 AND 1.6 GeV Protons

> Preliminary results of neutron spectra measurements from Pb, W, and Na targets irradiated by 0.8 and 1.6 GeV protons are presented. Measurements have been carried out using the proton beam extracted from the ITEP synchrotron and the TOF technique. Neutron registration has been carried out using BICRON MAB-511 liquid scintillation counters. Spectra measured at angles of 30, 60, 90, 120, and 150 degrees have been compared with results of their simulation using the LAHET code system and the code CEM2k. The results are of interest both from data gathering viewpoint and as a benchmark of the up-to-the-date predictive powers of codes applied to design the hybrid Accelerator Driven Systems (ADS) using lead or lead-bismuth targets and sodium-cooled targets.

## Key claim
The paper presents preliminary neutron spectra from Pb, W, and Na targets at 0.8 and 1.6 GeV proton energies, showing that while calculations with LAHET and CEM2k agree reasonably well with data for heavy targets (Pb, W), agreement worsens for the light Na target, particularly at high-energy tails and around 20 MeV at forward angles.

## Method
Neutron double-differential spectra were measured using the time-of-flight (TOF) technique at the ITEP proton synchrotron, with BICRON MAB-511 liquid scintillation detectors placed at angles of 30°, 60°, 90°, 120°, and 150°. Neutron-gamma separation was performed using amplitude-amplitude analysis for recoil proton energies of 2.5–10 MeV and amplitude-time analysis for 10–300 MeV. Measured spectra were compared with simulations using the LAHET code system (including low-energy neutron transport via HMCNP) and the CEM2k code.

## Key numbers
- Proton beam energies: 0.8 GeV and 1.6 GeV
- Target sizes: Pb (0.8 GeV: ∅6.0 cm × 2.0 cm; 1.6 GeV: 15×15×20 cm), W (∅5.0 cm × 3.0 cm), Na (∅6.0 cm × 20 cm)
- Neutron detector: BICRON MAB-511, ∅12.7 cm × 15.2 cm
- Detector distance from target: 1.5 m to 3 m
- Beam intensity: approximately 10⁵ protons per pulse
- Beam profile: ellipse with axes of 2 cm × 2.5 cm
- Impurity content: tungsten < 0.2%, sodium < 0.02%
- Neutron counter efficiency error: 10% below 80 MeV, 15% above 80 MeV
- Recoil proton energy ranges for n-γ separation: 2.5–10 MeV (amplitude-amplitude), 10–300 MeV (amplitude-time)

## Context
This work extends previous neutron spectrum measurements from Pb, W, and Na targets at proton energies up to 2 GeV, comparing with prior data from LANL (1992), KEK (1997), and SATURNE (1998). The results serve as a benchmark for codes used in designing Accelerator Driven Systems (ADS) with lead or lead-bismuth targets and sodium-cooled targets, and highlight remaining discrepancies in modeling high-energy neutron production from light nuclei.
