# Generalized Skyrme model with the loosely bound potential

> We study a generalization of the loosely bound Skyrme model which consists of the Skyrme model with a sixth-order derivative term - motivated by its fluid-like properties - and the second-order loosely bound potential - motivated by lowering the classical binding energies of higher-charged Skyrmions. We use the rational map approximation for the Skyrmion of topological charge B=4, calculate the binding energy of the latter and estimate the systematic error in using this approximation. In the parameter space that we can explore within the rational map approximation, we find classical binding energies as low as 1.8% and once taking into account the contribution from spin-isospin quantization we obtain binding energies as low as 5.3%. We also calculate the contribution from the sixth-order derivative term to the electric charge density and axial coupling.

## Key claim

The generalized Skyrme model combining the loosely bound potential (second-order in sigma) with a sixth-order BPS-Skyrme term can produce classical binding energies as low as 1.8% and total binding energies (including spin-isospin quantization) as low as 5.3% for the B=4 Skyrmion, but these values are only valid within the rational map approximation and the systematic error from this approximation means the true binding energies are expected to be 1-2% higher.

## Method

The authors used the rational map approximation for the B=4 Skyrmion, employing a holomorphic rational map of degree 4 with cubic symmetry, and solved the radial ordinary differential equations for the Skyrmion profile functions using the relaxation method. They calibrated the model by fitting the mass and size of the B=4 Skyrmion to experimental data for helium-4 (mass 3727 MeV, charge radius 8.492 x 10^-3 MeV^-1), then calculated the classical binding energy, the total binding energy including spin-isospin quantization, and the electric charge density and axial coupling. The systematic error of the rational map approximation was estimated by comparing to full PDE solutions from prior work at c_6=0, yielding a linear correction function delta_4^corrected = delta_4 + 0.01538 + 0.01142 m_2.

## Key numbers

- Classical binding energy as low as 1.8% for the B=4 Skyrmion in the parameter space explored within the rational map approximation
- Total binding energy (including spin-isospin quantization) as low as 5.3% for the B=4 Skyrmion
- Lowest total binding energy found: 3.03% at (m_2, c_6) = (0.8, 1) with m_1 = 0.25, and marginally less at (m_1, c_6) = (0.1, 1) with m_2 = 0.7
- Systematic error correction: rational map approximation underestimates binding energy by 1.5% for m_2 = 0 and by 2.5% for m_2 = 0.8
- Corrected rational map approximation matches full PDE classical binding energy within 0.0042-0.065% and total binding energy within 0.18-0.29%
- Calibration used experimental values: M_4He = 3727 MeV, r_4He = 8.492 x 10^-3 MeV^-1

## Context

This work extends the loosely bound Skyrme model of Gudnason (2016), which used a second-order potential to lower binding energies, by adding the BPS-Skyrme term (sixth-order derivative term) motivated by fluid-like properties from the BPS-Skyrme model of Adam (2010). The study is part of a line of research attempting to reduce the notoriously large binding energies of multi-Skyrmions (about one order of magnitude too large compared to experimental data), following earlier attempts including the lightly bound Skyrme model of Harland (2013) and the BPS subsector approach. The results enable future exploration of region B of parameter space (larger m_2 with medium c_6) using full PDE calculations, and may improve the Skyrme model's description of nuclear clustering, particularly for carbon-12 cluster states as studied by Lau (2014).
