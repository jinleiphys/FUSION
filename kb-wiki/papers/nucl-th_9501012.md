# Propagation of a $K$-body force into $A$-body space

> The calculation of the spreading width of a compound nuclear reaction caused by a symmetry breaking $K$-body force acting in an $A$-body system ($K << A$, usually $K=2$) involves the determination of the local average square matrix element in $A$-body space. This problem is reduced to finding the global mean square matrix element $v^2$ in $K$-body space. The result is a compact formula for the spreading width which contains $v^2$ as an input. Our method is based on the dilute gas approximation for excitons close to the Fermi edge. The relative strength of the contributions of operators with different exciton rank as well as the connection between the energy dependence of the spreading width and the body rank of the underlying interaction are established.

## Key claim

The paper derives a compact formula for the spreading width of a compound nuclear reaction caused by a symmetry-breaking K-body force, reducing the problem of finding the local average square matrix element in A-body space to finding the global mean square matrix element v² in K-body space, under the condition that the dilute gas approximation for excitons close to the Fermi edge is valid.

## Method

The authors decompose the A-body space into exciton subspaces of fixed particle-hole number, express the K-body interaction operator in the exciton picture as a sum of operators with different exciton rank k, and use the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) assumption for the K-body matrix elements to relate the ensemble average of squared matrix elements to a single parameter v². They then separate the strength function into actor and spectator contributions, apply the dilute gas approximation to treat the sums as independent, and express the strength function as a convolution of partial level densities. Finally, they sum over all exciton subspaces to obtain the spreading width in A-body space.

## Key numbers

* The spreading width is given by Γ↓(E) = 2π ≪V²≫ ρ(E), where ρ(E) is the level density of the system.
* The Bethe formula for the total level density is ρ^(B)(E) = exp[2(π²gE/6)^(1/2)] / √(48)E, where g is the single body density at the Fermi edge.
* The Blatt-Weisskopf expression is ρ^(BW)(E) = C exp[2(aE)^(1/2)].
* The constant temperature approximation (CTA) gives ρ^(CTA)(y) = ρ(y₀) e^(y - y₀)/T, with nuclear temperature T = √(6y₀/π²g).
* The Ericson formula for the partial level density with fixed exciton number is ρ^(Er)_hp(E) = g[g(E - S)]^(h+p-1) / [h! p! (h+p-1)!].
* The Gilbert-Cameron comparison found CTA fits experimental data up to approximately 10 MeV excitation energy, while Bethe or Blatt-Weisskopf expressions must be used above this value.

## Context

This work generalizes earlier results presented in a prior paper by the same group (referenced as "yo" in the text) and extends the approach of Wong (referenced as "Wong") for propagating K-body matrix elements into A-body space. The method builds on the statistical theory of compound nucleus reactions where matrix elements are assumed to follow the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble, and it provides a connection between the energy dependence of the spreading width and the body rank of the underlying interaction. The results enable calculation of symmetry breaking effects (such as isospin or parity violation) in compound nuclear reactions for arbitrary K-body forces, not just the usual two-body force.
