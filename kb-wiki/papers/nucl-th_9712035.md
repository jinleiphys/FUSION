# The ground state of medium-heavy nuclei with non central forces

> We study microscopically the ground state properties of 16O and 40Ca nuclei within correlated basis function theory. A truncated version of the realistic Urbana v14 (U14) potential, without momentum dependent terms, is adopted with state dependent correlations having spin, isospin and tensor components. Fermi hypernetted chain integral equations and single operator chain approximation are used to evaluate one- and two-body densities and ground state energy. The results are in good agreement with the available variational MonteCarlo data, providing a first substantial check for the accuracy of the cluster expansion method with state dependent correlations. The finite nuclei treatment of non central interactions and correlations has, at least, the same level of accuracy as in infinite nuclear matter. The binding energy for the full U14+TNI interaction is computed, addressing its small momentum dependent contributions in local density approximation. The nuclei are underbound by about 1 MeV per nucleon. Further energy lowering might be achieved by a more accurate variational minimization.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that correlated basis function (CBF) theory with Fermi hypernetted chain (FHNC) integral equations and state-dependent correlations (including spin, isospin, and tensor components) can achieve an accuracy for finite nuclei comparable to that already established for infinite nuclear matter, though the nuclei remain underbound by about 1 MeV per nucleon when using the full Urbana v14 plus three-nucleon interaction (U14+TNI) potential.

## Method

The author adopts a truncated Urbana v14 potential (U6) without momentum-dependent terms and constructs a correlated ground state wave function as a symmetrized product of two-body correlation operators (with spin, isospin, and tensor components) acting on a shell-model mean-field state for 16O and 40Ca. The expectation value of the Hamiltonian is expanded in Mayer-like cluster diagrams, and infinite classes of these diagrams are summed using FHNC integral equations in the single operator chain approximation to evaluate one- and two-body densities and the ground state energy. The binding energy for the full U14+TNI interaction is then computed by addressing the small momentum-dependent contributions in a local density approximation.

## Key numbers

*   Ground state energy of 16O and 40Ca computed with the truncated U6 potential: results are in good agreement with available variational Monte Carlo data, providing a check of the cluster expansion method.
*   Binding energy for the full U14+TNI interaction: the nuclei are underbound by about 1 MeV per nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the CBF and FHNC approach, previously applied to doubly closed shell nuclei with semirealistic central interactions (e.g., in papers by the author and collaborators from 1995-1997), to include non-central (spin, isospin, tensor) forces and correlations for 16O and 40Ca. It provides a first substantial check of the cluster expansion method with state-dependent correlations against variational Monte Carlo data (Pieper et al., 1992) for these medium-heavy nuclei. The results enable future improvements through more accurate variational minimization and set the stage for applying these techniques to heavier systems where Monte Carlo methods become increasingly difficult.
