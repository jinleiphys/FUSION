# Correlated $ππ$ and $K\bar K$ exchange in the baryon-baryon interaction

> A dynamical model for correlated two-pion and two-kaon exchange in the baryon- baryon interaction is presented, both in the scalar-isoscalar ($σ$) and the vector-isovector ($ρ$) channel. The correlations between the two pseudoscalar mesons are taken into account by means of $ππ- K\bar K$ amplitudes derived from a meson-exchange model, which is in line with the empirical $ππ$ data. It is found that correlated $K\bar K$ exchange plays an important role in the $σ$-channel for baryon-baryon states with non- vanishing strangeness. The strength of correlated $ππ$ plus $K\bar K$ exchange in the $σ$-channel decreases with the strangeness of the baryon- baryon system becoming more negative. The results for correlated $ππ$- exchange in the vector-isovector channel deviate from what is expected in the naive SU(3) picture for genuine $ρ$-exchange. Shortcomings of a simplified description in terms of sharp mass $σ$- and $ρ$-exchange are pointed out.

## Key claim

Correlated $K\bar K$ exchange plays an important role in the scalar-isoscalar ($\sigma$) channel for baryon-baryon states with non-vanishing strangeness, and the strength of correlated $\pi\pi$ plus $K\bar K$ exchange in the $\sigma$-channel decreases as the strangeness of the baryon-baryon system becomes more negative; the results for correlated $\pi\pi$ exchange in the vector-isovector ($\rho$) channel deviate from what is expected in the naive SU(3) picture for genuine $\rho$ exchange.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical model for correlated two-pion and two-kaon exchange in the baryon-baryon interaction by deriving the contribution from dispersion theory and unitarity, starting from a field-theoretic model for the baryon-antibaryon ($B\bar B'$) to $\pi\pi$ or $K\bar K$ amplitudes in the pseudophysical region. The Born terms included baryon-exchange and $\rho$-pole diagrams, and the correlations between the two pseudoscalar mesons were taken into account using $\pi\pi$-$K\bar K$ amplitudes derived from a meson-exchange model consistent with empirical $\pi\pi$ data. The parameters of the $B\bar B'\to\pi\pi, K\bar K$ model, related by SU(3) symmetry, were determined by adjustment to quasiempirical $N\bar N\to\pi\pi$ amplitudes in the pseudophysical region.

## Key numbers

- The $\epsilon$-meson used in one-boson-exchange models has mass $m_\epsilon=760$ MeV and width $\Gamma_\epsilon=640$ MeV, hidden under the strong $\rho^0$ signal in experiment.
- The mass difference between $\Lambda$ and $\Sigma$ hyperons is $77$ MeV, which the authors treat as small on the baryonic scale.
- The $\pi\pi$ threshold is at $t=4m_\pi^2$, and the $K\bar K$ threshold is at $t=4m_K^2$, with the pseudophysical region for the $t$-channel process lying between $4m_\pi^2$ and the baryon-antibaryon threshold.
- For c.m. energies below $1$ GeV, the $\pi\pi$ interaction is dominated by the $JI=00$ and $JI=11$ partial waves.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation of correlated $\pi\pi$ exchange in the $NN$ system by Kim (1994) to include the $K\bar K$ channel on equal footing, which is essential for hyperon-nucleon and strangeness $S=-2$ baryon-baryon channels. It replaces the phenomenological $\sigma'$ and $\rho$ exchange used in the Bonn $NN$ potential and the Julich $YN$ models (Holinde et al., 1994) with microscopically derived correlated processes, thereby eliminating undetermined model parameters such as the $\sigma'$ coupling constants at strange vertices. The results enable a more predictive treatment of $S=-2$ baryon-baryon channels relevant to searches for the H-dibaryon predicted by Jaffe (1977).
