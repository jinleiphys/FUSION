# Astrophysical constraints on neutron star $f$-modes with a nonparametric equation of state representation

> We constrain the fundamental-mode ($f$-mode) oscillation frequencies of nonrotating neutron stars using a phenomenological Gaussian process model for the unknown dense-matter equation of state conditioned on a suite of gravitational-wave, radio and X-ray observations. We infer the quadrupolar $f$-mode frequency preferred by the astronomical data as a function of neutron star mass, with error estimates that quantify the impact of equation of state uncertainty, and compare it to the contact frequency for inspiralling neutron-star binaries, finding that resonance with the orbital frequency can be achieved for the coalescences with the most unequal mass ratio. For an optimally configured binary neutron star merger, we estimate the gravitational waveform's tidal phasing due to $f$-mode dynamical tides as $7^{+2}_{-3}$ rad at merger. We assess prospects for distinguishing $f$-mode dynamical tides with current and future-generation gravitational-wave observatories.

## Key claim

The paper constrains neutron star fundamental-mode ($f$-mode) frequencies using a nonparametric Gaussian process model for the equation of state, finding that resonance between the $f$-mode and orbital frequency can be achieved in approximately 25% of binary neutron star mergers, but only for the most unequal-mass-ratio coalescences and contingent on the specific observational dataset used to condition the equation of state.

## Method

The authors compute quadrupolar $f$-mode frequencies for nonrotating neutron stars by solving the perturbed Einstein-Euler equations (the Zerilli equation) for each of 10,000 equation of state realizations drawn from a Gaussian process posterior conditioned on pulsar mass measurements, gravitational-wave tidal deformability data from GW170817 and GW190425, and X-ray pulse profile modeling from NICER. They then evaluate the resulting $f_2(m)$ distribution against the Keplerian contact frequency for a simulated binary neutron star population, and compute the dynamical tidal phase using the fmtidal frequency-domain model to assess distinguishability with current and future gravitational-wave detectors.

## Key numbers

- For a canonical 1.4 $M_\odot$ neutron star with the PSR+GW+NICER dataset: $f_{1.4} = 1.74^{+0.23}_{-0.15}$ kHz
- For a maximum-mass TOV star with the PSR+GW+NICER dataset: $f_{\rm TOV} = 2.17^{+0.26}_{-0.28}$ kHz
- Mean dynamical tidal phase at contact for the simulated population: $|\Psi_{\rm dyn}(f_c)| = 2.8^{+2.8}_{-2.0}$ rad
- Maximum dynamical tidal phase for 1 $M_\odot$--1 $M_\odot$ coalescences: $7^{+2}_{-3}$ rad at merger
- Distinguishability SNR required for a 1.4 $M_\odot$--1.4 $M_\odot$ coalescence: 75 at LIGO design sensitivity, 90 at A+ sensitivity, 270 for Einstein Telescope, 155 for Cosmic Explorer
- Canonical radius with PSR+GW+NICER: $R_{1.4} = 12.51^{+0.95}_{-1.18}$ km
- TOV mass with PSR+GW+NICER: $M_{\rm TOV} = 2.25^{+0.35}_{-0.22} M_\odot$

## Context

This work extends the nonparametric equation of state inference framework of Legred et al. (2021) to compute $f$-mode frequencies, contrasting with studies that assume specific microscopic frameworks such as relativistic mean-field theory (Jaiswal and Chatterjee, 2021; Keshari Pradhan and Chatterjee, 2022) or empirical quasi-universal relations linking $f$-mode frequency to tidal deformability (Chan et al., 2014; Chirenti et al., 2015; Zhao and Lattimer, 2022). The distinguishability analysis is inspired by Williams and Pratten (2022) but marginalizes over equation of state uncertainty rather than fixing a specific equation of state. The results enable direct marginalization over $f$-mode frequency uncertainty in waveform models such as SEOBNRTv4 and IMRPhenomPv2_NRTidalv3, and can inform detector design for dedicated high-frequency sensitivity to postmerger oscillations.
