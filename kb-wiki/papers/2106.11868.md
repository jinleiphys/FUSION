# Doubly Heavy Tetraquark Resonant States

> Spectrum of the doubly heavy tetraquarks, $bb\bar q\bar q$, is studied in a constituent quark model. Four-body problem is solved in a variational method where the real scaling technique is used to identify resonant states above the fall-apart decay thresholds. In addition to the two bound states that were reported in the previous study we have found several narrow resonant states above the $BB^*$ and $B^*B^*$ thresholds. Their structures are studied and are interpreted by the quark dynamics. A narrow resonance with spin-parity $J^P=1^+$ is found to be a mixed state of a compact tetraquark and a $B^*B^*$ scattering state. This is driven by a strong color Coulombic attraction between the $bb$ quarks. Negative-parity excited resonances with $J^P=0^-$, $1^-$ and $2^-$ form a triplet under the heavy-quark spin symmetry. It turns out that they share a similar structure to the $λ$-mode of a singly heavy baryon as a result of the strongly attractive correlation for the doubly heavy diquark.

## Key claim

The paper reports that, in addition to previously known bound states, the doubly heavy tetraquark system \(bb\bar q\bar q\) hosts several narrow resonant states above the \(BB^*\) and \(B^*B^*\) thresholds, with the caveat that the quark model used is quenched (no \(q\bar q\) creation or annihilation) and therefore the discussion is limited to low energies where pion emission is suppressed.

## Method

The four-body problem for \(bb\bar q\bar q\) was solved using a constituent quark model Hamiltonian with a color Coulomb, linear confinement, and spin-spin interaction. A variational Gaussian expansion method was employed, and resonant states above the fall-apart decay thresholds were identified using the real-scaling technique, where the spatial range of the Jacobi coordinate for the two-meson channel is scaled to distinguish resonances from scattering states.

## Key numbers

- Two bound states with \(I(J^P)=0(1^+)\) were confirmed: a deeply bound state at 173 MeV below the \(BB^*\) threshold and a shallow bound state at 4 MeV below the \(BB^*\) threshold.
- A narrow resonant state \(1^+_3\) was found at 10667 MeV, which is 50 MeV above the \(BB^*\) threshold and 5 MeV below the \(B^*B^*\) threshold; its estimated decay width is 9 MeV.
- Negative-parity resonant states with \(J^P=0^-\), \(1^-\), and \(2^-\) were found at 10762 MeV, which is 145 MeV above the \(BB^*\) threshold; their estimated decay widths are about 3 MeV each.
- The ratio \(P(B^*B^*)/P(BB^*)\) for the \(1^+_3\) state is about 2.4, while for the shallow bound state \(1^+_2\) it is about 0.02.
- The expectation value of the color operator \(\langle\lambda_{b1}\cdot\lambda_{b2}\rangle\) for the \(bb\) subsystem is about 0 for the \(1^+_2\) state and \(-0.63\) for the \(1^+_3\) state.

## Context

This work extends the previous bound-state study of Meng (2020) on \(bb\bar q\bar q\) tetraquarks by identifying resonant states above threshold. The narrow \(1^+\) resonance is interpreted as a mixed state of a compact tetraquark (with a radially excited \(bb(2S)\) diquark) and a \(B^*B^*\) molecular component, driven by strong color Coulomb attraction between the two bottom quarks. The negative-parity triplet (\(0^-,1^-,2^-\)) is the first observation of a heavy-quark spin triplet in tetraquark systems, analogous to the \(\lambda\)-mode excitation of a singly heavy baryon; its existence in pentaquark systems was previously pointed out by Shimizu (2018). These results enable future studies of the internal structure of multi-quark resonances and their decay dynamics.
