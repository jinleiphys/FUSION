# Quark Hadron Continuity in QCD with one Flavor

> We study QCD with one flavor at finite baryon density. In the limit of very high baryon density the system is expected to be a color superconductor. In the case of one flavor, the order parameter is in a $\bar 3$ of color and has total angular momentum one. We show that in weak coupling perturbation theory, the energetically preferred phase exhibits ``color-spin-locking'', i.e. the color and spin direction of the condensate are aligned. We discuss the properties of this phase and argue that it shares important features of the hadronic phase at low density. In particular, we find an unbroken rotational symmetry, spin 3/2 quasiparticles, and an unusual mechanism for quark-anti-quark condensation. Our results are relevant to three flavor QCD in the regime where the strange quark mass is bigger than the critical value for color-flavor-locking. We find that the gaps in this case are on the order of one MeV.

# Quark Hadron Continuity in QCD with one Flavor

## Key claim

The paper argues that high-density QCD with one flavor exhibits a "color-spin-locked" phase that shares important features with the low-density hadronic phase, suggesting the two phases may be continuously connected without a phase transition. This claim is conditional on weak coupling perturbation theory and applies specifically to the regime where the strange quark mass is larger than the critical value for color-flavor-locking in three-flavor QCD.

## Method

The authors study the phase structure of one-flavor QCD at finite baryon density using weak coupling perturbation theory, first in the non-relativistic limit (large quark mass) and then in the ultra-relativistic limit (massless quarks). They derive gap equations from the Dyson-Schwinger equation in the Nambu-Gorkov formalism, considering several possible order parameters for color anti-triplet pairs with total angular momentum one, and compare their thermodynamic potentials to determine the energetically preferred phase. The analysis includes both same-chirality and opposite-chirality pairing channels, as well as mixed condensates.

## Key numbers

- The gap in the color-spin-locked (CSL) phase in the non-relativistic limit is given by Δ_CSL = 2^{-2/3}(2Λ)exp(-1/G) with G = (2/3)(g²/8π²)log(4p_F²/m_D²)
- The gaps in the polar, planar, and A-phases are larger by a factor 2^{2/3} relative to the CSL gap: Δ(polar) = Δ(planar) = Δ(A-phase) = 2^{2/3}Δ(CSL)
- The thermodynamic potential comparison shows Ω(polar) = Ω(A-phase) = (2^{4/3}/3)Ω(CSL), with the CSL phase favored by a factor of approximately 1.2
- In the ultra-relativistic limit, the p-wave (l=1) gap is suppressed relative to the s-wave gap: Δ_{l=1} = exp(-6)Δ_{l=0} ≈ 0.004Δ_{l=0}
- The opposite-chirality (LR) pairing gap is larger than the same-chirality (LL) gap: Δ^{LR} = exp(-9/2)Δ_0 ≈ 0.01Δ_0, bigger by a factor of about 4.5
- The mixed LL+LR pairing gap is Δ^{LL+LR} = exp(-5)Δ_0
- The gaps in the three-flavor case with strange quark mass are on the order of one MeV

## Context

This work extends the concept of quark-hadron continuity previously proposed for three-flavor QCD by Schafer (1999) to the one-flavor case, and builds on earlier studies of color superconductivity by Frautschi (1978), Barrois (1977), and Bailin and Love (1984). The paper contrasts the color-spin-locked phase with the color-flavor-locked phase of Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999), noting that while both exhibit unbroken rotational symmetry and gapped excitations, the symmetry groups involved are different. The results are relevant to understanding the phase structure of three-flavor QCD when the strange quark mass exceeds the critical value for color-flavor-locking, a regime studied by Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (1999) and Schafer (1999). The work enables further investigation of whether the high-density color-spin-locked phase can be continuously connected to low-density nuclear matter composed of spin-3/2 baryons, as suggested by the similarity of their spectra.
