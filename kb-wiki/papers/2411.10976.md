# Measurement of enhanced electric dipole transition strengths at high spin in $^{100}$Ru: Possible observation of octupole deformation

> The majority of atomic nuclei have deformed shapes and nearly all these shapes are symmetric with respect to reflection. There are only a few reflection asymmetric pear-shaped nuclei that have been found in actinide and lanthanide regions, which have static octupole deformation. These nuclei possess an intrinsic electric dipole moment due to the shift between the center of charge and the center of mass. This manifests in the enhancement of the electric dipole transition rates. In this article, we report on the measurement of the lifetimes of the high spin levels of the two alternate parity bands in $^{100}$Ru through the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method. The estimated electric dipole transition rates have been compared with the calculated transition rates using the triaxial projected shell model without octupole deformation, and are found to be an order of magnitude enhanced. Thus, the observation of seven inter-leaved electric dipole transitions with enhanced rates establish $^{100}$Ru as possibly the first octupole deformed nucleus reported in the A $\approx$ 100 mass region.

## Key claim

The paper reports that seven interleaved electric dipole (E1) transitions in \(^{100}\)Ru have measured strengths an order of magnitude larger than those calculated by the triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) without octupole deformation, suggesting that \(^{100}\)Ru is "possibly the first octupole deformed nucleus reported in the A ≈ 100 mass region." The claim is hedged because the TPSM calculations that included octupole-octupole interaction as a perturbation still produced rates "quite weak in comparison to the observed rates," so the authors state that "more detailed calculations with broken reflection symmetry" are needed to confirm stable octupole deformation.

## Method

Lifetimes of high-spin levels in the two alternate parity bands (Band 2 and Band 3) of \(^{100}\)Ru were measured using the Doppler Shift Attenuation Method (DSAM). The nucleus was populated via the \(^{100}\)Mo(\(^{4}\)He,4n)\(^{100}\)Ru reaction at 45 MeV, and gamma rays were detected with the Indian National Gamma Array (INGA) of 11 Compton-suppressed clover detectors. Lineshape analyses were performed simultaneously at three angles (40°, 90°, and 125°) using the LINESHAPE package with stopping powers from SRIM, and the extracted B(E1) rates were compared to TPSM calculations both without and with an octupole-octupole interaction term added to the Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Measured lifetimes for Band 2: 14\(^+\) level at 1.02(9) ps, 16\(^+\) at 0.32(3) ps, 18\(^+\) at 0.17(1) ps, and 20\(^+\) with an upper limit < 0.14 ps.
- Measured lifetimes for Band 3: 13\(^-\) level at 0.40(3) ps, 15\(^-\) at 0.31(2) ps, 17\(^-\) at 0.29(2) ps, and 19\(^-\) with an upper limit < 0.12 ps.
- B(E1) values for Band 2: 1.4(4) × 10\(^{-4}\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (14\(^+\)), 2.4(6) × 10\(^{-4}\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (16\(^+\)), 7.7(25) × 10\(^{-4}\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (18\(^+\)).
- B(E1) values for Band 3: 2.0(4) × 10\(^{-4}\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (13\(^-\)), 2.7(6) × 10\(^{-4}\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (15\(^-\)), 4.4(15) × 10\(^{-4}\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\) (17\(^-\)).
- Weighted average quadrupole moment Q\(_0\) = 264(8) efm\(^2\) for both bands.
- Weighted average D\(_0\) value of 0.04(1) efm for spins up to 16\(\hbar\), increasing to 0.07(2) efm at higher spin.
- The single-particle B(E1) rate in neighboring \(^{110}\)Cd is 1.8(4) × 10\(^{-6}\) e\(^2\)fm\(^2\), making the observed \(^{100}\)Ru rates two orders of magnitude larger.

## Context

This work extends the search for octupole deformation into the A ≈ 100 mass region, where no such nucleus had previously been reported. Prior studies of octupole-deformed nuclei have been concentrated in the actinide region (Ra–Th isotopes, e.g., Gaffney et al., 2013; Chishti et al., 2020) and the lanthanide region (Sm–Ba isotopes, e.g., Butler et al., 2020). The present measurement follows a prior observation of seven interleaved E1 transitions in \(^{100}\)Ru (Karmakar et al., 2023) where the B(E1)/B(E2) ratios were too small to confirm octupole collectivity; the direct lifetime measurement here provides the absolute B(E1) rates needed for comparison. The results enable future theoretical work with broken-reflection-symmetry mean-field calculations and may motivate searches for octupole deformation in other nuclei near A ≈ 100.
