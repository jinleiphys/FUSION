# Forward-backward correlations between intensive observables

> We demonstrate that the investigations of the forward-backward correlations between intensive observables enable to obtain more clear signal about the initial stage of hadronic interaction, e.g. about the process of string fusion, compared to usual forward-backward multiplicity correlations. As an example, the correlation between mean-event transverse momenta of charged particles in separated rapidity intervals is considered. We performed calculations in the framework of dipole-based Monte Carlo string fusion model. We obtained the dependence of the correlation strength on the collision centrality for different initial energies and colliding systems. It is shown that the dependence reveals the decline of the correlation coefficient for most central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy. We compare the results both with the ones obtained in alternative models and with the ones obtained by us using various MC generators.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that forward-backward correlations between intensive observables (specifically mean-event transverse momentum) provide a clearer signal of initial-stage string fusion than traditional multiplicity correlations, but only when the string density is high enough, the decline of the correlation coefficient for most central collisions is achieved only in Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy, not in Au-Au at RHIC or p-Pb at LHC.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo calculations using a dipole-based string fusion model (SFM) that implements a cellular lattice in the transverse plane, where strings fuse if their centers occupy the same cell. They computed the correlation coefficient \(b_{p_t-p_t}\) between mean transverse momenta in forward and backward rapidity intervals as a function of collision centrality for Au-Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, p-Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, and Pb-Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV. The results were compared with predictions from THERMINATOR 2, HIJING, DPMJET, and AMPT Monte Carlo generators.

## Key numbers
- For Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76\) TeV, the correlation coefficient \(b_{p_t-p_t}\) shows a maximum in mid-central collisions and then declines for most central collisions, reflecting attenuation of color field fluctuations at large string density.
- For Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV and p-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02\) TeV, the maximal string density is not enough to provide a decline of the correlation coefficient for most central collisions.
- The string fusion model calculations used a string radius \(r_\mathrm{str}=0.2\) fm.
- The dipole interaction probability amplitude uses a confinement scale \(r_{\text{max}}\) and effective coupling \(\alpha_s^2/2\) with modified Bessel functions \(K_0\).

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of forward-backward correlations proposed by Amelin (1994) as a signature of string fusion and percolation, following the realization by Braun (2000), Alessandro (2006), and Vechernin (2007) that intensive observables give a cleaner signal than multiplicity correlations. The cellular variant of string fusion used here was shown by Lakomov (2013) to give results very close to the original continuous string fusion model. The results enable future comparisons with fully event-by-event hydrodynamical models such as iEBE-VISHNU (Shen, 2014) or EKRT (Niemi, 2015) to further study sensitivity to initial-stage properties.
