# Parity-projected Skyrme Hartree-Fock and angular momentum projection approach to Mg isotopes

> We perform calculations of the variation after parity projection with Skyrme interaction for ground and excited states of even-even Mg isotopes. Using the 3D real-space representation, we can take into account any kind of deformation; e.g., cluster structure, gamma-deformation. We also perform the full 3D angular momentum projection to obtained rotational spectra and B(E2). In 24Mg, the ground K^pi=0+ and several odd-parity bands are calculated. In 30,32,34Mg, our calculation reproduces a breaking of the N=20 magic number, so-called "Island of inversion" phenomena. Properties of both the ground and excited states are reasonably reproduced.

# Parity-projected Skyrme Hartree-Fock and angular momentum projection approach to Mg isotopes

## Key claim

The variation after parity projection (VAPP) method with Skyrme interaction, combined with full 3D angular momentum projection (AMP), can reproduce ground and excited state properties of Mg isotopes including the "Island of inversion" phenomena at N=20, but the calculated band-head energies for negative-parity bands in 24Mg are slightly higher than experiments by 1–2.5 MeV and moments of inertia are somewhat overestimated in 30,32Mg due to neglected pairing correlation.

## Method

The authors perform variation after parity projection (VAPP) calculations using the Skyrme interaction with the SGII parameter set on a 3D Cartesian real-space mesh, allowing any deformation including cluster and gamma-deformation. They then apply full 3D angular momentum projection (AMP) to the resulting parity-projected states to obtain rotational spectra and B(E2) transition strengths for even-even Mg isotopes from A=24 to A=34.

## Key numbers

- 24Mg ground state deformation: β₂ = 0.52, with octupole components β₃₀ = 0.17 and β₃₁ = 0.12
- 24Mg ground band: AMP reproduces experimental spectra and B(E2) up to J^π = 6^+
- 24Mg negative-parity band-head energies: calculated values are 1–2.5 MeV higher than experimental data
- 30Mg ground state deformation: β₂ = 0.22 and β₃₃ = 0.14
- 32Mg ground state deformation: β₂ = 0.44 and β₃₃ = 0.12
- 34Mg ground state deformation: β₂ = 0.53 with β₃₀ = 0.17
- For neutron-rich isotopes, two positive-parity solutions exist: one with large quadrupole deformation β ≈ 0.4–0.6 and one with small deformation β ≈ 0.1–0.3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous VAPP method (Ohta, Nakatsukasa, Yabana 2004) which successfully described cluster structures in 20Ne (α+16O) and 12C (three α) to the Mg isotopic chain. It addresses the failure of standard mean-field Skyrme calculations to reproduce the anomalous deformation and quenching of the N=20 shell gap observed experimentally in neutron-rich Mg isotopes by Motobayashi (1995), Iwasaki (2001), and Pritychenko (1999). The method enables systematic description of both ground and excited states across the "Island of inversion" region, though the neglect of pairing correlation leads to overestimated moments of inertia in 30,32Mg.
