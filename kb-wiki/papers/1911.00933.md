# Chiral magnetic response to arbitrary axial imbalance

> The response of chiral fermions to time and space dependent axial imbalance & constant magnetic field is analyzed. The axialvector-vector-vector (AVV) three-point function is studied using a real-time approach at finite temperature in the weak external field approximation. The chiral magnetic conductivity is given analytically for noninteracting fermions. It is pointed out that local charge conservation plays an important role when the axial imbalance is inhomogeneous. Proper regularization is needed which makes the constant axial imbalance limit delicate: for static but spatially oscillating chiral charge the current of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) vanishes. In the homogeneous (but possible time-dependent) limit of the axial imbalance the CME current is determined solely by the chiral anomaly. As a phenomenological consequence, the observability of the charge asymmetry caused by the CME turns out to be a matter of interplay between various scales of the system. Possible plasma instabilities resulting from the gradient corrections to the CME current are also pointed out.

## Key claim

The chiral magnetic effect (CME) current vanishes for static but spatially oscillating chiral charge, and only in the homogeneous (but possibly time-dependent) limit is the current determined solely by the chiral anomaly; the sign of the current depends on whether a Pauli-Villars regulator (or Chern-Simons term) is included to enforce local vector charge conservation.

## Method

The authors use the Schwinger-Keldysh real-time formalism at finite temperature to compute the axial-vector-vector-vector (AVV) three-point function in the weak external field approximation. They evaluate the retarded response of the electric current to a space-time dependent axial-vector potential (representing axial imbalance) in the presence of a constant magnetic field, employing Pauli-Villars regularization to maintain electromagnetic gauge invariance. The calculation yields an analytic expression for the chiral magnetic conductivity for noninteracting fermions, and the Ward-Takahashi identities are used to constrain the vertex function.

## Key numbers

* The chiral magnetic current for a homogeneous (time-dependent) axial imbalance is given by **J = (e²/2π²) A₅,₀ B** (Eq. CME1), which differs by a sign from the standard static formula **J = (e²/2π²) μ₅ B** (Eq. CME).
* The anomalous Ward identity for the AVV vertex yields **q₂,ρ Γ^{ρμν}_{AVV} = i ε^{μναβ} q₁,α q₂,β · (e²/2π²)** (Eq. gradJ5), fixing the anomaly coefficient to **e²/2π²**.
* For static but spatially oscillating chiral charge, the CME current vanishes entirely, as a consequence of proper regularization and local charge conservation.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretic analysis of the CME beyond the static homogeneous limit, addressing the subtle order-of-limits issue raised by Ren (2017) where the static limit and the homogeneous limit give opposite results for the CME current. It contrasts with earlier kinetic theory approaches (e.g., Shovkovy and collaborators) and field theory analyses (Kharzeev, 2008) that omitted the Pauli-Villars regulator or Chern-Simons term, which led to a delayed response current. The results clarify that for realistic systems like the quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, the observability of the CME charge asymmetry depends on the interplay between the spatial and temporal variation scales of the axial imbalance, and the work points to possible plasma instabilities from gradient corrections to the CME current.
