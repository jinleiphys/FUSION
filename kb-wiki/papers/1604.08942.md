# Early Time Dynamics of Gluon Fields in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Nuclei colliding at very high energy create a strong, quasi-classical gluon field during the initial phase of their interaction. We present an analytic calculation of the initial space-time evolution of this field in the limit of very high energies using a formal recursive solution of the Yang-Mills equations. We provide analytic expressions for the initial chromo-electric and chromo-magnetic fields and for their energy-momentum tensor. In particular, we discuss event-averaged results for energy density and energy flow as well as for longitudinal and transverse pressure of this system. Our results are generally applicable if $τ< 1/Q_s$. The transverse energy flow of the gluon field exhibits hydrodynamic-like contributions that follow transverse gradients of the energy density. In addition, a rapidity-odd energy flow also emerges from the non-abelian analog of Gauss' Law and generates non-vanishing angular momentum of the field. We will discuss the space-time picture that emerges from our analysis and its implications for observables in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors present an analytic calculation of the early-time evolution of classical gluon fields in high-energy nuclear collisions, valid only for proper times τ < 1/Q_s, showing that the transverse energy flow contains hydrodynamic-like contributions and a rapidity-odd component that generates non-vanishing angular momentum.

## Method

The authors solve the classical Yang-Mills equations in the McLerran-Venugopalan model with boost invariance using a formal recursive Taylor series expansion in proper time τ, where the color charge densities of the two colliding nuclei are allowed to vary in the transverse plane. They compute the gluon field coefficients order by order in τ, with all odd powers vanishing, and then average over events using Gaussian weight functions for the color charge densities with widths given by the local charge densities squared μ₁ and μ₂.

## Key numbers

* Initial energy density at Qτ ≪ 1, neglecting transverse gradients: ε₀(x_⊥) = [2π N_c α_s³/(N_c²-1)] μ₁(x_⊥) μ₂(x_⊥) ln(Q₁²/ˆm²) ln(Q₂²/ˆm²)
* The analytic approximation gives acceptable results up to Qτ ≈ 1, which coincides with the time when the classical field approximation starts to break down
* Transverse energy flow up to order τ²: T⁰ⁱ_even = (τ/2) αⁱ [1 - (1/2a)(Qτ)²] cosh η and T⁰ⁱ_odd = (τ/2) βⁱ [1 - (9/16a)(Qτ)²] sinh η
* For slab-on-slab collisions with no transverse gradients, the longitudinal and transverse pressure ratios to energy density are computed up to and including terms of order τ⁴
* The system attempts to become isotropic; if P_L = P_T ≡ P then necessarily P = ε/3, the equation of state for a weakly interacting gas of massless particles

## Context

This work extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1993) by allowing small but non-vanishing gradients in the average color charge in the transverse plane, enabling discussion of flow phenomena in event-averaged collisions. The analytic results for pressure evolution match well with real-time lattice simulations by Gelis et al. (2013) for SU(2) gauge group up to Qτ ≈ 1. The results enable matching to 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic codes and suggest that the characteristic glasma flow pattern could contribute to directed flow measured at RHIC and LHC.
