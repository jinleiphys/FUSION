# Symmetric correlations as seen at RHIC

> We analyze the forward-backward multiplicity correlation coefficient as measured by STAR. We show that in the most central Au+Au collisions bins located symmetrically around η= 0 with large separation in pseudorapidity are more strongly correlated than bins located asymmetrically with smaller separation. In proton-proton collisions the opposite effect is observed. It suggests a qualitatively different behavior of the two-particle correlation as a function of pseudorapidity sum in p+p and Au+Au collisions.

## Key claim

In the most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, bins located symmetrically around η=0 with a large pseudorapidity separation are more strongly correlated than bins located asymmetrically with a smaller separation, which is the opposite of what is observed in p+p collisions and contradicts the natural expectation that correlation strength should decrease with increasing pseudorapidity distance.

## Method

The author analyzed the STAR forward-backward multiplicity correlation coefficient b_BF|_STAR, which is measured by fixing the number of particles in a third reference window R to remove impact parameter fluctuations. A general formula was derived relating b_BF|_STAR to the correlation coefficients b_BF and b_BR measured without fixing the reference window, assuming the average multiplicity in B at a given n_R is a linear function of n_R. Using published STAR data for the covariance and variance in the most central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions and the raw multiplicity distribution in the reference window, the author extracted the underlying correlation coefficients b_BF and b_BR.

## Key numbers

- In the most central Au+Au collisions at √s=200 GeV, the STAR-measured correlation coefficient b_BF|_STAR ≈ 0.58 (with uncertainty ±0.06) for the configuration with maximum pseudorapidity gap Δη=1.8 between bins B and F, violating the bound of 1/2 expected from the natural ordering b_BR ≥ b_BF.
- The extracted underlying correlation coefficients for central Au+Au collisions are b_BR ≈ 0.58 and b_BF ≈ 0.72, showing that b_BR is significantly smaller than b_BF even though the average distance between B and R (one unit of η) is smaller by a factor of two than that between B and F.
- For p+p collisions, using a negative binomial distribution for P(n_R) with ⟨n_R⟩=2.3 and k=2, and taking c_1=0.1, the author obtains b_BR ≈ 0.28 and b_BF ≈ 0.13, showing the standard ordering b_BR > b_BF.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of STAR data on forward-backward multiplicity correlations (STAR Collaboration, 2009) and the theoretical discussion initiated by Lappi and McLerran (2011) regarding the influence of the reference window procedure. The result contradicts the natural expectation that correlation strength decreases with pseudorapidity distance, which holds in p+p collisions and in models such as HIJING (Wang, 1991) and the Parton String Model (Amelin, 2001), both of which predict b_BF|_STAR < 1/2 for central Au+Au collisions. The finding suggests a qualitatively different two-particle correlation function in central Au+Au collisions that strongly depends on the pseudorapidity sum η_1+η_2, indicating a specific mechanism that correlates bins symmetrically around η=0 more strongly than asymmetric bins, and points toward a strong violation of boost invariance in the midrapidity region (Bialas, 2011).
