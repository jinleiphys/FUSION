# $Δ$ self-energy at finite temperature and density and the $πN$ cross-section

> The self energy of $Δ$-baryon is evaluated at finite temperature and density using the real time formalism of thermal field theory. The Dyson-Schwinger equation is used to get the exact thermal propagator followed by the spectral function of $Δ$. The $πN$ scattering cross section obtained using explicit $Δ$ exchange is normalized to the experimental data in vacuum and its medium modification is implemented by means of the exact thermal propagator. A significant suppression of the peak of the cross-section is observed at higher temperature and baryon density. Effects on the mean relaxation time of nucleons and the temperature dependence of the shear viscosity of a pion nucleon gas are demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the in-medium modification of the Δ-baryon propagator, computed via thermal field theory, leads to a significant suppression of the πN scattering cross-section peak at higher temperature and baryon density, which in turn reduces the shear viscosity of a pion-nucleon gas; however, the calculation is limited to Δ(1232) exchange only and does not include contributions from other resonances such as the Roper(1440) or Δ(1600).

## Method

The Δ self-energy was evaluated at finite temperature and baryon density using the real-time formalism of thermal field theory, computing one-loop diagrams with π, ρ, N, and Δ in the internal lines. The Dyson-Schwinger equation was solved to obtain the exact thermal propagator and spectral function of the Δ. The πN elastic scattering cross-section was then computed by replacing the free vacuum Δ propagator with the in-medium one, and the resulting cross-section was used as input in the collision integral of the transport equation to evaluate the relaxation times and shear viscosity of a pion-nucleon gas mixture.

## Key numbers

- The Δ spectral function shows a gradual suppression of the peak with increasing temperature and density; at T=70 MeV and μ_N=727 MeV (with μ_π=105 MeV), the spectral function was compared with results from vanHees (2011) using a monopole form factor with Λ=600 MeV.
- The πN phase shift δ_33 was fitted using m_Δ=1234 MeV and Λ=600 MeV, yielding reasonable agreement with experimental data from Koch (1980).
- The πN elastic scattering cross-section, normalized to vacuum data from Prakash (1993) up to about 1.5 GeV, shows a significant suppression of the peak at higher temperature and baryon density.
- The mean relaxation time of nucleons decreases with increasing temperature; for μ_N=200 MeV and μ_N=500 MeV, the relaxation time is reduced when medium effects are included compared to using vacuum cross-sections.
- The shear viscosity of the πN gas decreases with increasing temperature; at a given temperature, the viscosity is lower for higher baryon chemical potential (μ_N=500 MeV vs. μ_N=200 MeV) when medium-modified cross-sections are used.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of in-medium hadron properties, particularly the Δ self-energy in nuclear matter studied by Oset (1987), Wehrberger (1994), and Korpa (2001), and the modification of the Δ spectral function at finite temperature and density by vanHees (2011). It also builds on the authors' prior work on ππ scattering in a hot pion gas (Mitra 2012, Ghosh 2013), where medium-modified ρ and σ propagators led to reduced cross-sections and altered transport coefficients. The results enable more realistic estimates of shear viscosity for use in viscous hydrodynamic simulations of the later stages of heavy-ion collisions, particularly relevant for the CBM experiment at FAIR.
