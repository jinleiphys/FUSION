# Double Lambda He-6 in cluster effective field theory

> The bound state of \nuclide[6][ΛΛ]{He} is studied as a three-body ($ΛΛα$) system in a cluster effective field theory at leading order (LO). We find that the system exhibits the limit cycle which is associated with the formation of bound states called the Efimov states. This implies that the three-body contact interaction should be promoted to LO. The relationship of the binding energy and the $ΛΛ$ scattering length is discussed as well as the role of the scale in this system.

# Double Lambda He-6 in cluster effective field theory

## Key claim
The bound state of ⁶_ΛΛHe, treated as a three-body (ΛΛα) system in cluster effective field theory at leading order, exhibits a limit cycle that requires promoting the three-body contact interaction to leading order for proper renormalization; however, the system is not close to the unitary limit, so the presence of an imaginary solution in the asymptotic integral equation implies the existence of a bound state but does not guarantee Efimov states.

## Method
The authors studied the bound state of ⁶_ΛΛHe as a three-body ΛΛα system using cluster effective field theory at leading order, treating the α particle as an elementary field. They examined the homogeneous part of the coupled integral equations for S-wave Λ + ⁵_ΛHe scattering in the asymptotic limit to test for limit cycle behavior, and solved for the two-Λ separation energy B_ΛΛ as a function of the ΛΛ scattering length a_ΛΛ and the sharp cutoff Λ_c introduced in the integral equations.

## Key numbers
- The two-Λ separation energy of ⁶_ΛΛHe from the NAGARA event: B_ΛΛ = 6.93 ± 0.16 MeV
- The Λ separation energy of ⁵_ΛHe: B_Λ = 3.12 MeV
- The binding momentum of ⁵_ΛHe as a Λα cluster: γ_Λα = 73.2 MeV
- The large momentum scale of the α-cluster theory: Λ_H ≈ 170 MeV, giving expansion parameter Q/Λ_H ≈ 0.43
- The S-wave ΛΛ scattering length from ¹²C(K⁻,K⁺ΛΛX) data: a_ΛΛ = -1.2 ± 0.6 fm
- The imaginary solution indicating the limit cycle: s₀ = 1.0496
- The first divergence of the three-body coupling g(Λ_c) appears at Λ_c ∼ 1 GeV
- For renormalization at B_ΛΛ = 6.93 MeV and 1/a_ΛΛ = -2.0 fm⁻¹, the three-body coupling values are g(Λ_c) ≈ -0.715, -0.447, -0.254 for Λ_c = 170, 300, 430 MeV respectively

## Context
This work extends the cluster effective field theory approach previously applied to ⁴_ΛΛH as a three-body (ΛΛd) system by Ando, Oh, and collaborators (2013). The method follows the renormalization group analysis of Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck (1999), who showed that three-body contact interactions must be promoted to leading order to handle the limit cycle in three-body systems. The results reproduce potential model calculations from Filikhin and Gal (2002) with a cutoff of Λ_c = 300 MeV, suggesting a nontrivial role of two-pion exchange in the ΛΛ interaction, though including such exchanges would require cutoffs above Λ_H ≈ 170 MeV and inclusion of higher-energy degrees of freedom such as the first excited state of the α particle.
