# Confinement, DCSB, Bound States, and the Quark-Gluon Vertex

> Aspects of the dressed-quark-gluon vertex and their role in the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations are briefly surveyed using an intuitive model. The model allows one to elucidate why a linear extrapolation to the chiral limit of extant lattice data on the dressed-quark mass-function overestimates this function and hence the value of the vacuum quark condensate. The diagrammatic content of the vertex described is explicitly enumerable. This property is essential to the symmetry preserving study of bound state properties. It facilitates a realistic analysis of vector and pseudoscalar meson masses, and also allows the accuracy of standard truncations to be gauged. The splitting between vector and pseudoscalar meson masses is observed to vanish as the current-quark mass increases. That argues for the mass of the pseudoscalar partner of the Upsilon(1S) to be above 9.4GeV. Moreover, in this limit the rainbow-ladder truncation provides an increasingly accurate estimate of a bound state's mass.

# Confinement, DCSB, Bound States, and the Quark-Gluon Vertex

## Key claim
A linear extrapolation of lattice data on the dressed-quark mass-function to the chiral limit overestimates the true mass-function and hence the inferred vacuum quark condensate, because the chiral susceptibility peaks in the transition domain between infrared and ultraviolet momenta (0.5 to 1.0 GeV²). The splitting between vector and pseudoscalar meson masses vanishes as current-quark mass increases, arguing that the pseudoscalar partner of the Upsilon(1S) lies above 9.4 GeV.

## Method
The authors construct a closed system of Dyson-Schwinger equations using a simple model for the dressed-quark-gluon vertex defined by an integral equation with a global parameter C, combined with a confining delta-function model for the dressed-gluon interaction. They solve the coupled gap equation and vertex equation algebraically, then use the resulting vertex in a symmetry-preserving Bethe-Salpeter kernel to compute vector and pseudoscalar meson masses across a range of current-quark masses.

## Key numbers
- The chiral susceptibility peak occurs on the momentum domain 0.5 to 1.0 GeV², marking the transition between infrared and ultraviolet regimes.
- The pseudoscalar meson mass squared follows m_{0^-}² ≈ 1.33 m (in GeV) for m much less than the model scale G.
- For two pseudoscalar mesons with unlike-flavour quarks of mass m_s and 2 m_s, the ratio m²_{0^-_{2m_s}} / m²_{0^-_{m_s}} = 2.4.
- At the charm quark mass (m_c = 1.33 GeV), the vector-to-pseudoscalar mass ratio m_{1^-}/m_{0^-} = 1.04, with a splitting of 130 MeV.
- At the bottom quark mass (m_b = 4.62 GeV), the vector-pseudoscalar splitting drops to 40 MeV, only 5% of its chiral-limit value.
- The predicted masses are m_{η_c} = 2.97 GeV and m_{η_b} = 9.42 GeV (experimental values: 2.9797 ± 0.00015 GeV and 9.30 ± 0.03 GeV respectively).
- At the s-quark mass, the rainbow-ladder truncation relative error for vector meson mass is 20%; at the c-quark mass it falls below 4%.
- The absolute difference between truncated and exact pseudoscalar mass peaks at approximately 60 MeV near m ∼ 4 m_s, where the relative error is only 3%.

## Context
This work extends Dyson-Schwinger equation studies of QCD's Schwinger functions (Roberts and Williams, 1994; Lane and Politzer, 1977) and lattice-QCD simulations of gluon and quark propagators (Bowman et al., 2002; Mandar et al., 2001). It builds on the vertex model introduced by Mandar et al. (2003) and complements a later three-gluon vertex ansatz by Mandar et al. (2004). The results enable systematic assessment of the rainbow-ladder truncation accuracy and support the interpretation that the renormalisation-group-improved rainbow-ladder truncation becomes exact in the static source limit, consistent with potential models like Richardson (1979). The methods are well suited for studying radial excitations, axial vector mesons, and missing nucleon resonances.
