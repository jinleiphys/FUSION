# Effect of nuclear deformation on J/Psi suppression in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Using a hadron-string cascade model, JPCIAE, we study the effect of nuclear deformation on J/Psi suppression in the collision of uranium nuclei at 200A GeV/c. We find that the J/Psi survival probability is much smaller if the major axes of both deformed nuclei are along the beam direction than if they are perpendicular to the beam direction.

# Effect of Nuclear Deformation on J/Psi Suppression in Relativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim
The J/Psi survival probability in uranium-uranium collisions at 200A GeV/c is significantly smaller when the major axes of both deformed nuclei are aligned with the beam direction (tip-tip) than when they are perpendicular to it (body-body), but this effect only becomes pronounced when the color electric dissociation coefficient is scaled by the orientation-dependent initial energy density.

## Method
The authors used the JPCIAE hadron-string cascade model, an extension of the LUND string model with PYTHIA routines, to simulate U-U collisions at 200A GeV/c. They modeled the uranium nuclei as ellipsoids with deformation parameter delta=0.29 and compared four collision geometries: body-body, spherical-spherical, tip-body, and tip-tip. The J/Psi suppression was calculated from three mechanisms: hadronic absorption by baryons and mesons, energy degradation of leading nucleons, and dissociation of the pre-J/Psi c-cbar pair in the color electric field of strings.

## Key numbers
- Deformation parameter: delta=0.29, with major semi-axis a=8.4 fm and minor semi-axis b=6.5 fm, using equivalent spherical radius R=7.0 fm
- Nuclear passing time ratios: t(B-B):t(S-S):t(T-B):t(T-T) = 0.93:1.0:1.07:1.2
- J/Psi absorption cross sections: sigma(J/Psi-B)=6 mb for baryons, sigma(J/Psi-M)=3 mb for mesons
- Effective color electric dissociation coefficient: c_s=6.0x10^-7 GeV^-1 (determined from Pb-Pb data at 158A GeV/c)
- Formation time of produced particles: tau=1.2 fm/c
- Initial energy density in tip-tip collision is about 35% higher than in body-body collision
- With energy-density-scaled dissociation coefficients (1.2 for T-T, 1.07 for T-B, 0.93 for B-B), J/Psi suppression in tip-tip is twice stronger than in body-body

## Context
This work extends prior studies of J/Psi suppression in spherical nuclei collisions at CERN SPS energies, where anomalous suppression in Pb-Pb collisions (NA38, NA50 collaborations) could not be fully explained by hadronic absorption alone. The authors build on their earlier JPCIAE model results (Sa et al., 1998) and on a suggestion by Shuryak (1993) to study deformed nuclei collisions. The paper also references Bao et al. (1998) who found 38% higher maximum energy density in tip-tip collisions at AGS energies. This study enables experimental verification of non-hadronic dense matter formation by comparing J/Psi suppression across different nuclear orientations in U-U collisions.
