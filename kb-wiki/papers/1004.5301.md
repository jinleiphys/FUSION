# The QCD chiral transition temperature in a Dyson-Schwinger-equation context

> We analyze the chiral phase transition with the help of the QCD gap equation. Various models for the effective interaction in rainbow truncation are contrasted with regard to the resulting chiral transition temperatures. In particular, we investigate possible systematic relations of the details of the effective interaction and the value of T_c. In addition, we quantify changes to the transition temperature beyond the rainbow truncation.

## Key claim

The chiral transition temperature \(T_c\) in the rainbow-truncated Dyson-Schwinger-equation approach is not universally determined by simple integrated interaction strength or the zero-temperature chiral condensate, but instead shows a systematic dependence on the presence and relative weight of a delta-function term in the effective interaction, with models lacking such a term yielding lower \(T_c\) values.

## Method

The authors solved the QCD gap equation (quark Dyson-Schwinger equation) at finite temperature and zero chemical potential in the Matsubara formalism, using four different model Ansatze for the effective quark-gluon interaction in rainbow truncation (Munczek-Nemirovsky, Alkofer-Watson-Weigel, Maris-Tandy, and Maris-Roberts). For each interaction and parameter set, they extracted the chiral transition temperature by analyzing the scaling behavior of chiral susceptibilities \(\chi_T\) and \(\chi_h\) as functions of reduced mass \(h = m/T\), following the method of Blaschke (1998). They also estimated corrections beyond rainbow truncation via phenomenological parameter rescaling, pion-loop vertex corrections, and Polyakov-loop effects.

## Key numbers

* For the Munczek-Nemirovsky (MN) interaction: \(T_c = 169\) MeV (single parameter set, \(D = 0.5618\) GeV\(^2\)).
* For the Alkofer-Watson-Weigel (AWW) interaction: \(T_c = 82\) MeV (\(\omega = 0.3\) GeV), 94 MeV (\(\omega = 0.4\) GeV), 101 MeV (\(\omega = 0.5\) GeV).
* For the Maris-Tandy (MT) interaction: \(T_c = 82\) MeV (MT1, \(\omega = 0.3\) GeV), 94 MeV (MT2, \(\omega = 0.4\) GeV), 96 MeV (MT3, \(\omega = 0.5\) GeV).
* For the Maris-Roberts (MR) interaction: \(T_c = 120\) MeV (MR1, \(\omega = 0.3\) GeV), 133 MeV (MR2, \(\omega = 0.4\) GeV), 144 MeV (MR3, \(\omega = 0.5\) GeV).
* Phenomenological rescaling to estimate beyond-rainbow corrections (using parameter sets A and B from Nicmorus (2008)): \(T_c = 94\) MeV for set A (MT2) and 129 MeV for set B, giving a ratio of 0.73, consistent with the ratio of \(\approx 0.74\) found for hadron masses.
* Pion-loop vertex corrections (from Mueller (2008)) reduce \(T_c\) by about 5%.
* A first estimate of vertex corrections in an extended MN study gives shifts in \(T_c\) of order 15%.
* Inclusion of a Polyakov-loop potential (from Horvatic, private communication) shifts \(T_c\) up by approximately 70 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier DSE studies of the chiral phase transition by Holl (1998) and Maris (1999), which established mean-field critical exponents for the MT and AWW interactions, by systematically comparing four interaction forms and quantifying the non-universal \(T_c\) values. The results enable future studies to use \(T_c\) as a diagnostic tool to discriminate between effective interaction models, analogous to how excited-state meson masses were used by Holl (2004, 2005) and Krassnigg (2008, 2009) to probe interaction details. The paper also provides a baseline for incorporating corrections beyond rainbow truncation, such as those from the quark-gluon vertex DSE (Alkofer, 2008) and Polyakov-loop effects (Schaefer, 2007).
