# Heavy Flavor and Jet Studies for the Future Electron-Ion Collider to Explore the Hadronization Process

> Heavy flavor production at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) will allow us to precisely determine the quark/gluon fragmentation processes in vacuum and the nuclear medium especially within the poorly constrained kinematic region. Heavy flavor hadron and jet reconstruction with the recent EIC detector design have been studies in simulation. Results of corresponding physics projections such as the flavor dependent hadron nuclear modification factor $R_{eA}$ in electron+nucleus collisions will be shown. The statistical precision obtained by these proposed heavy flavor measurements for the future EIC provides a strong discriminating power in separating different theoretical predictions.

## Key claim

Heavy flavor measurements at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) will provide precise determination of quark/gluon fragmentation processes in vacuum and nuclear medium, particularly in the poorly constrained kinematic region, with statistical precision sufficient to discriminate between different theoretical predictions for parton energy loss mechanisms.

## Method

Simulation studies were performed using PYTHIA8 for event generation and GEANT4 within the Fun4All framework, incorporating the performance of a proposed Forward Silicon Tracker (FST) and other EIC detector sub-systems. Heavy flavor hadrons (D-mesons, B-mesons, and Lambda_c) were reconstructed via topological cuts including charged track transverse displaced vertex matching and track transverse momentum thresholds, while jets were reconstructed using the anti-kT algorithm with cone radius R=1.0. The nuclear modification factor R_eA was projected by extracting hadron yields from reconstructed mass spectra in e+p collisions and scaling e+Au cross-sections by nuclear mass number A.

## Key numbers

- Center of mass energies for EIC operations: 20 to 140 GeV, with simulation performed at 63.2 GeV (10 GeV electron on 100 GeV proton/nucleus)
- Integrated luminosity: 10 fb^-1 for e+p collisions and 500 pb^-1 for e+Au collisions
- Forward Silicon Tracker pseudorapidity coverage: 1.0 < eta < 3.5
- Jet cone radius: R = 1.0
- Less than 10% statistical uncertainties obtained for reconstructed heavy flavor R_eAu measurements with approximately one year of EIC operation
- Nuclear species mass numbers: from 2 to 208

## Context

This work extends simulation studies for the EIC detector design described in the EIC Yellow Report (2021) and prior detector R&D by the Los Alamos group (Li, 2020). The projected flavor-dependent nuclear modification factor R_eA measurements will enable discrimination between different theoretical models of parton energy loss in nuclear medium, building on the factorization framework where fragmentation functions D_b(z_h, nu) describe hadronization from partons of flavor b. The results provide a pathway to constrain nuclear parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions across a wide kinematic region that is poorly constrained by existing data.
