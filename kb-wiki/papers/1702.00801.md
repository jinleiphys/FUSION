# Bayesian analysis for a new class of hybrid EoS models using mass and radius data of compact stars

> We present a Bayesian analysis for a new class of realistic models of two-phase equations of state (EoS) for hybrid stars and demonstrate that the observation of a pair of high-mass twin stars would have a sufficient discriminating power to favor hybrid EoS with a strong first order phase transition over alternative EoS. Such a measurement would provide evidence for the existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that simultaneous mass and radius measurements of a pair of high-mass twin stars (two stars with similar masses but different radii) would have sufficient discriminating power to favor hybrid equations of state with a strong first-order phase transition over alternative models, provided the radius uncertainties are small enough (1.5 km or better).

## Method
The authors constructed a two-parameter family of hybrid equations of state using a density-dependent relativistic mean-field model (DD2F−) for hadronic matter with an excluded volume correction (parameter p) and a Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with multiquark interactions (parameter η₄) for quark matter. They performed a Bayesian analysis by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for each parameter pair and computing posterior probabilities given existing mass constraints (PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230) and a radius constraint (PSR J0437-4715), then extended the analysis to include fictitious radius measurements for the two high-mass pulsars.

## Key numbers
- Mass of PSR J0348+0432: M_A = 2.01 M_⊙ ± 0.04 M_⊙
- Mass of PSR J1614-2230: M_D = 1.94 M_⊙ ± 0.04 M_⊙
- Radius of PSR J0437-4715: R_B = 15.5 km with σ_RB = 1.5 km
- Fictitious radii assumed for high-mass twins: R_A = 13 km, R_D = 15 km
- Radius uncertainties tested: σ_R = 1.5 km and σ_R = 0.5 km
- Most probable excluded volume parameter range: 40 < p < 80 (dimensionless)
- Saturation density: n_sat = 0.16 fm⁻³

## Context
This work extends earlier Bayesian analyses by the same group (Blaschke 2014, Alvarez-Castillo 2014, 2015, 2016) that used mass and radius constraints to discriminate hybrid star models. It builds on the Alford-Han-Prakash classification (2013) of mass-radius sequences for hybrid stars and the concept of a "third family" of compact stars (Benic 2014, Alvarez-Castillo 2016). The results suggest that future high-precision radius measurements of massive pulsars could provide evidence for a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram. The next planned steps include expanding the parameter space to include purely hadronic cases and modeling pasta phases in the transition region (Yasutake 2014, Alvarez-Castillo 2014).
