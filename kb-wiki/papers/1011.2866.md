# The anti-quark--quark potential from Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes on lattice

> Potentials of quark--anti-quark pairs are studied from the anti-q--q Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions in quenched lattice QCD. With the use of a method which has been recently developed in the derivation of nuclear forces from QCD, we derive the anti-q--q potentials with finite quark masses from the NBS wave functions. We calculate the anti-q--q NBS wave functions in pseudo-scalar and vector channels for several quark masses. The derived potentials in both channels reveal linear confinement plus Coulomb potentials. We also discuss the quark-mass and channel dependence of the anti-q--q potentials.

## Key claim

The anti-quark–quark potential derived from Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions in quenched lattice QCD reveals a linear confinement plus Coulomb form for finite quark masses, similar to the infinitely heavy quark potential from Wilson loops, but the method assumes that the equal-time NBS amplitudes satisfy the Bethe-Salpeter equation with constant quark masses due to the absence of asymptotic fields for confined quarks.

## Method

The authors computed equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions for anti-quark–quark systems in pseudo-scalar and vector channels using quenched lattice QCD on a \(32^3 \times 48\) lattice with \(\beta = 6.0\) (lattice spacing \(a = 0.10\) fm, physical volume \(V = (3.2\ \text{fm})^3\)). They extracted the potentials by applying the effective Schrödinger equation to the NBS wave functions, using the Lévy-Klein-Macke (LKM) formalism to derive a leading-order local potential \(V(r) = \frac{1}{2\mu}\frac{\nabla^2 \phi(r)}{\phi(r)} + E\), with quark masses \(m_q = m_V/2\) determined from vector meson masses.

## Key numbers

- String tension \(\sigma = 950\ \text{MeV/fm}\) in the pseudo-scalar channel and \(\sigma = 1011\ \text{MeV/fm}\) in the vector channel for the heaviest quark mass (\(m_{\text{PS}} = 2.53\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_{\text{V}} = 2.55\ \text{GeV}\))
- String tension \(\sigma = 762\ \text{MeV/fm}\) in the pseudo-scalar channel and \(\sigma = 914\ \text{MeV/fm}\) in the vector channel for the lightest quark mass (\(m_{\text{PS}} = 0.94\ \text{GeV}\), \(m_{\text{V}} = 1.04\ \text{GeV}\))
- Coulomb coefficient \(A = 155\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}\) in the pseudo-scalar channel and \(A = 123\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}\) in the vector channel for the heaviest quark mass
- Coulomb coefficient \(A = 329\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}\) in the pseudo-scalar channel and \(A = 182\ \text{MeV}\cdot\text{fm}\) in the vector channel for the lightest quark mass
- Lattice spacing \(a = 0.10\ \text{fm}\) and physical volume \(V = (3.2\ \text{fm})^3\) from the lattice setup with \(\beta = 6.0\)
- Four quark masses studied with hopping parameters \(\kappa = 0.1520, 0.1480, 0.1420, 0.1320\), corresponding to pseudo-scalar meson masses \(m_{\text{PS}} = 0.94, 1.27, 1.77, 2.53\ \text{GeV}\) and vector meson masses \(m_{\text{V}} = 1.04, 1.35, 1.81, 2.55\ \text{GeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the method developed for deriving nuclear forces from QCD (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2010) to anti-quark–quark systems with finite quark masses, in contrast to the traditional Wilson loop approach which yields potentials only for infinitely heavy quarks (Bali 2001). The results confirm that the NBS wave function method reproduces the linear plus Coulomb potential form previously obtained from Wilson loops, and the string tension at the heaviest quark mass is roughly consistent with the heavy quark limit value of about 0.89 GeV/fm from Bali (2001). This enables future studies of dynamical quark effects, finite-temperature potentials, three-quark potentials with finite quark masses, and color non-singlet quark–quark potentials.
