# Has the neutral double hypernucleus $_{ΛΛ}^{4}$n been observed?

> The BNL-AGS E906 experiment was the first fully electronic experiment to produce and study double hypernuclei with large statistics. Two dominant structures were observed in the correlated $π^-$--$π^-$ momentum matrix at (p$_{π-H}$,p$_{π-L}$) = (133,114) MeV/c and at (114,104) MeV/c. In this work we argue that the interpretation of the structure at (133,114) MeV/c in terms of $^3_Λ$H+$^4_Λ$H pairs is questionable. We show, that neither a scenario where the hypernuclei are produced after capture of a stopped $Ξ^-$ by a $^9$Be nucleus nor interactions of energetic $Ξ^-$ with $^9$Be nuclei in the target material can produce a sufficient amount of such twin pairs. We have therefore explored the conjecture of Avraham Gal that decays of the $_{ΛΛ}^{4}$n may be responsible for the observed structure. Indeed, the inclusion of $_{ΛΛ}^{4}$n with a two-body $π^-$ branching ratio of 50 % in a statistical multifragmentation model allows to describe the E906 data remarkably well. On the other hand, a bound $^{3}_Λ$n nucleus would cause a striking structure in the momentum correlation matrix which is clearly inconsistent with the observation of E906.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the structure observed at (133,114) MeV/c in the BNL-AGS E906 experiment, previously interpreted as $^3_{\Lambda}$H+$^4_{\Lambda}$H twin hypernuclei, cannot be produced in sufficient quantity by either stopped $\Xi^-$ capture on $^9$Be or in-flight $\Xi^-$+$^9$Be reactions. They instead propose that the sequential weak decay of a hypothetical neutral double hypernucleus $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{4}$n, with a two-body $\pi^-$ branching ratio of 50%, can reproduce the observed signal.

## Method

The authors used a statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to simulate the production of hyperfragments following $\Xi^-$ capture by a $^9$Be target, assuming an initial excitation energy of 29.5 MeV for the $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{10}$Li$^*$ system. They also employed the Giessen Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (GiBUU) transport model to estimate the contribution from in-flight $\Xi^-$+$^9$Be nuclear interactions, using two different YN-interaction models (Rijken and Fujiwara). The SMM predictions were then folded with pionic two-body weak decay branching ratios and the experimental momentum resolution of 3.5 MeV/c to compare with the E906 data.

## Key numbers

- The two dominant structures in the E906 correlated $\pi^-$--$\pi^-$ momentum matrix were observed at (p$_{\pi-H}$,p$_{\pi-L}$) = (133,114) MeV/c and (114,104) MeV/c.
- The corrected relative signal strength for the (133,114) MeV/c region in the E906 data is 0.22 $\pm$ 0.04$_{stat}$, while the SMM without neutral hypernuclei gives a factor of 25 smaller value (8.7 $\times$ 10$^{-3}$).
- Including $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{4}$n with a two-body $\pi^-$ branching ratio of 50% yields a ratio N$_{\Box}$/N$_{total}$ = 0.23, close to the experimental value.
- The probability for $^3_{\Lambda}$H+$^4_{\Lambda}$H twin production via $\Xi^-$+$^9$Be interactions is of the order 10$^{-4}$ per interaction.
- The $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{4}$n nuclei produced in the SMM have an average kinetic energy of about 2.4 MeV, causing an additional broadening of about 9 MeV/c (FWHM) in horizontal direction.

## Context

This work addresses the "E906 puzzle" from the BNL-AGS E906 experiment (PhysRevLett.87.132504, 2001), which observed double hypernuclei via sequential pionic decay. The authors challenge the earlier interpretation of the (133,114) MeV/c structure as $^3_{\Lambda}$H+$^4_{\Lambda}$H twins, noting that statistical decay models (Lorente, 2011) predict $^4_{\Lambda}$H+$^4_{\Lambda}$H production exceeding $^3_{\Lambda}$H+$^4_{\Lambda}$H production. They explore a conjecture by Avraham Gal (HYP-2003 conference) that the sequential weak decay of $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{4}$n could explain the signal. The stability of $_{\Lambda\Lambda}^{4}$n remains controversial (PhysRevC.91.014003, 2015; 1674-1137-41-7-074102, 2017), and the authors note that a bound $^{3}_{\Lambda}$n nucleus would produce a structure inconsistent with the E906 observation.
