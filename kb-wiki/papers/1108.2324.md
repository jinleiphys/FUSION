# Properties of Nambu-Goldstone Bosons in a Single-Component Bose-Einstein Condensate

> We theoretically study the properties of Nambu-Goldstone bosons in an interacting single-component Bose-Einstein condensate (BEC). We first point out that the proofs of Goldstone's theorem by Goldstone, et al. [Phys. Rev. {\bf 127} (1962) 965] may be relevant to distinct massless modes of the BEC: whereas the first proof deals with the poles of the single-particle Green's function $\hat{G}$, the second one concerns those of the two-particle Green's function. Thus, there may be multiple Nambu-Goldstone bosons even in the single-component BEC with broken U(1) symmetry. The second mode turns out to have an infinite lifetime in the long-wavelength limit in agreement with the conventional viewpoint. In contrast, the first mode from $\hat{G}$, i.e., the Bogoliubov mode in the weak-coupling regime, is shown to be a "bubbling" mode fluctuating temporally out of and back into the condensate. The substantial lifetime originates from an "improper" structure of the self-energy inherent in the BEC, which has been overlooked so far and will be elucidated here, and removes various infrared divergences pointed out previously.

## Key claim

Goldstone's theorem, as proven by Goldstone, Salam, and Weinberg (1962), may predict two distinct massless Nambu-Goldstone bosons in a single-component Bose-Einstein condensate with broken U(1) symmetry, contrary to the widely accepted view of a single phonon branch. The first mode from the single-particle Green's function is a short-lived "bubbling" mode, not a well-defined quasi-particle, while the second mode from the two-particle Green's function has an infinite lifetime in the long-wavelength limit.

## Method

The author develops a self-consistent perturbation expansion based on a functional Phi that simultaneously satisfies Goldstone's theorem and conservation laws, using the Dyson-Beliaev equation and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the condensed Bose system. The poles of the single-particle Green's function G and the two-particle Green's function K are analyzed separately, with the self-energy shown to have an "improper" structure that has been overlooked in prior work. The analysis is performed for a homogeneous system of identical spin-0 bosons with mass m and a two-body interaction potential V, using the Matsubara formalism at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- The second proof of Goldstone's theorem yields a finite spectral weight of -2π√n₀ δ(ω) at ω=0 for the density-operator correlation function ρ_{nψ}(0,ω), where √n₀ is the condensate wave function amplitude.
- The first mode (Bogoliubov mode in the weak-coupling regime) is shown to have a substantial lifetime originating from the improper self-energy structure, removing various infrared divergences pointed out by Gavoret and Nozières (1964).
- The second mode has a vanishing imaginary part in the long-wavelength limit, making it an observable propagating mode with infinite lifetime.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Goldstone's theorem by Goldstone, Salam, and Weinberg (1962) and challenges the standard treatment by Brauner (2010) and others who did not distinguish between the two proofs. It contradicts the widely accepted viewpoint of a unique phonon branch due to density fluctuations from Feynman (1954), Gavoret and Nozières (1964), and Griffin (1993). The results enable a safe calculation of the two-particle Green's function without artificial procedures to remove infrared singularities, and clarify the nature of the Nambu-Goldstone bosons in single-component BECs.
