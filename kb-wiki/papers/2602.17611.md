# Evidence for Multimodal Superfluidity of Neutrons

> We present theoretical and experimental evidence for a new phase of matter in neutron-rich systems that we call multimodal superfluidity. Using ab initio lattice calculations, we show that the condensate consists of coexisting s-wave pairs, p-wave pairs in entangled double pair combinations, and quartets composed of bound states of two s-wave pairs. We identify multimodal superfluidity as a general feature of single-flavor spin-1/2 fermionic systems with attractive s-wave and p-wave interactions, provided the system is stable against collapse into a dense droplet. Beyond neutrons at sub-saturation densities, we demonstrate that this phase appears in generalized attractive extended Hubbard models in one, two, and three dimensions. We elucidate the mechanism for this coexistence using self-consistent few-body Cooper models and compare with Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer theory. We also derive the form of the effective action and show that spin, rotational, and parity symmetries remain unbroken. Finally, we analyze experimental data to show that p-wave pair gaps and quartet gaps are present in atomic nuclei, and we discuss the consequences of this new phase for the structure and dynamics of neutron star crusts.

## Key claim

The paper claims that neutron matter below saturation density exhibits a new quantum phase called "multimodal superfluidity," where the condensate simultaneously contains s-wave pairs, p-wave pairs in entangled double-pair combinations, and quartets (bound states of two s-wave pairs). This phase is claimed to be a general feature of single-flavor spin-1/2 fermionic systems with attractive s-wave and p-wave interactions, provided the system is stable against collapse into a dense droplet, and the authors hedge that this contradicts the prevailing assumption that the ground state is a pure s-wave superfluid.

## Method

The authors performed ab initio auxiliary-field projection Monte Carlo lattice calculations of generalized attractive extended Hubbard models in one, two, and three dimensions, and of realistic neutron matter using next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) chiral effective field theory interactions with the wavefunction matching method to overcome the Monte Carlo sign problem. They extracted condensate momentum distributions for s-wave pairs, p-wave pairs, and quartets by computing cumulants that subtract background contributions from disconnected processes. They also derived a low-energy effective action and analyzed experimental nuclear binding energies using staggered k-point difference formulas applied to AME2020 masses and ENSDF data.

## Key numbers

- For the 3D GAE Hubbard model at densities between 0.0086 fm^-3 and 0.0097 fm^-3, thermodynamic limits for condensate fractions are approximately 1.8(4)% for s-wave pairs, 1.3(3)% for p-wave pairs, and 45(3)% for quartets (from A=38, 66, 114 systems)
- For realistic neutron matter at density 0.033 fm^-3 (A=38 on L=8 lattice), condensate fractions are 1.40(5)% for ^1S_0 pairs, 0.02(1)% for ^3P_0 pairs, 0.26(8)% for ^3P_2 pairs, and 48(1)% for quartets
- For realistic neutron matter at density 0.085 fm^-3 (A=66 on L=7 lattice), condensate fractions are 0.68(1)% for ^1S_0 pairs, 0.00% for ^3P_0 pairs, 0.12(2)% for ^3P_2 pairs, and 13(1)% for quartets
- Neutron matter s-wave pair binding (2Δ_S) is 5.58(92) MeV at 0.033 fm^-3 and 2.61(76) MeV at 0.085 fm^-3
- Neutron matter quartet binding (4Δ_Q) is 3.90(167) MeV at 0.033 fm^-3 and 2.01(86) MeV at 0.085 fm^-3
- Empirical p-wave pair binding in nuclei: 0.172(12) MeV for {^17O(3/2+), ^18O(1+)}; 0.139(3) MeV for {^55Fe(5/2-), ^56Fe(1+), ^57Fe(5/2-)}; 0.245(1) MeV for {^57Ni(5/2-), ^58Ni(1+), ^59Ni(5/2-)}
- Empirical quartet binding in nuclei: 1.150 MeV for {^6He, ^8He}; 0.142(28) MeV for {^18O, ^20O, ^22O}; 0.236(1) MeV for {^42Ca, ^44Ca, ^46Ca}; 0.332(3) MeV for {^44Ca, ^46Ca, ^48Ca}

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of neutron matter superfluidity (Gezerlis 2014, Gandolfi 2022) that previously treated s-wave and p-wave pairing channels in isolation, and contradicts the prevailing assumption that the ground state is a pure s-wave superfluid. The results are consistent with existing energy observables from other ab initio calculations using chiral effective field theory (Hebeler 2009, Gandolfi 2015) and with ^1S_0 pairing gaps in the literature, while revealing new condensate structure. The findings have implications for neutron star crust physics, including explaining low thermal inertia in transient sources like KS 1731-260, and could be realized in ultracold fermionic dipolar molecules or atoms on optical lattices, as well as in condensed matter systems with multiple attractive pairing channels.
