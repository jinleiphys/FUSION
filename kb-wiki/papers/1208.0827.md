# New Sensitivity to Solar WIMP Annihilation using Low-Energy Neutrinos

> Dark matter particles captured by the Sun through scattering may annihilate and produce neutrinos, which escape. Current searches are for the few high-energy neutrinos produced in the prompt decays of some final states. We show that interactions in the solar medium lead to a large number of pions for nearly all final states. Positive pions and muons decay at rest, producing low-energy neutrinos with known spectra, including nuebar through neutrino mixing. We demonstrate that Super-Kamiokande can thereby provide a new probe of the spin-dependent WIMP-proton cross section. Compared to other methods, the sensitivity is competitive and the uncertainties are complementary.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that low-energy neutrinos (MeV range) from pion multiplication and decay following WIMP annihilation in the Sun are detectable by Super-Kamiokande, providing a new probe of the spin-dependent WIMP-proton cross section that is competitive with existing methods and nearly insensitive to the choice of annihilation final state, unlike high-energy neutrino searches which depend strongly on the final state.

## Method

The authors used GEANT4 to simulate hadronic showers in the solar core (density 160 g/cm³ with solar elemental abundances), injecting pions with initial multiplicities from e⁺e⁻ collider data and tracking their interactions and decays. They calculated the resulting low-energy neutrino yield from positive pions and muons decaying at rest, then estimated the detectable signal in Super-Kamiokande via inverse beta decay (ν̄ₑ p → e⁺ n), accounting for matter-enhanced neutrino mixing in the Sun and detector energy resolution. The sensitivity to the spin-dependent WIMP-proton cross section was derived by comparing expected signal rates to measured background spectra from 4.1 livetime years of Super-K-I data.

## Key numbers

- Low-energy neutrino yield scales as N_ν ≈ 3 N_π⁺, with N_π ∼ 0.1 × (2 m_χ / m_π), giving near-linear scaling with WIMP mass
- For a WIMP mass of 10 GeV, the signal shown corresponds to a cross section of 4.5 × 10⁻³⁷ cm² (at the edge of exclusion)
- Expected signal and background events in the 25–50 MeV energy range: 15 signal events and 135 background events for the case shown
- Super-Kamiokande has 1.5 × 10³³ free protons in the 22.5 kton fiducial volume
- The neutrino interaction cross section rises as σ_ν ∼ 10⁻⁴³ × (E_ν / MeV)² cm²
- The sensitivity extends to WIMP masses m_χ ≳ 4 GeV (evaporation becomes important below this)
- Future improvements: neutron tagging with gadolinium could improve sensitivity by ∼15; Hyper-Kamiokande (25 times larger) could improve by up to ∼375

## Context

This work extends the standard approach of searching for high-energy neutrinos from prompt decays of WIMP annihilation products (e.g., W⁺W⁻, τ⁺τ⁻, b b̅) by showing that low-energy neutrinos from pion showers in the solar medium are detectable and provide complementary sensitivity. It builds on earlier work by Gaisser (1986) and Ritz (1987) that considered such MeV neutrinos undetectable, and is similar to an independent simultaneous study by Bernal, Martin-Albo, and Palomares-Ruiz (2012). The method enables probing spin-dependent WIMP-proton scattering at low WIMP masses (down to ∼4 GeV), a region challenging for both direct detection experiments (e.g., Archambault 2012, Kim 2012, Felizardo 2010) and high-energy neutrino searches (e.g., Tanaka 2011, Aartsen 2012), and provides a test of the DAMA/LIBRA signal region (Bernabei 2010, Savage 2008).
