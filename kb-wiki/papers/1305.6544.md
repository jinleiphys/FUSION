# Influence of hadronic bound states above $T_c$ on heavy-quark observables in Pb+Pb collisions at at the CERN Large Hadron Collider

> We investigate how the possible existence of hadronic bound states above the deconfinement transition temperature $T_c$ affects heavy-quark observables like the nuclear modification factor, the elliptic flow and azimuthal correlations. Lattice QCD calculations suggest that above $T_c$ the effective degrees of freedom might not be exclusively partonic but that a certain fraction of hadronic degrees of freedom might already form at higher temperatures. This is an interesting questions by itself but also has a strong influence on other probes of the strongly interacting matter produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. A substantial fraction of hadronic bound states above $T_c$ reduces on average the interaction of the heavy quarks with colored constituents of the medium. We find that all studied observables are highly sensitive to the active degrees of freedom in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim
The paper investigates whether hadronic bound states existing above the deconfinement temperature $T_c$ can explain heavy-quark observables, finding that all studied observables are highly sensitive to the active degrees of freedom in the quark-gluon plasma. The authors model the fraction of partonic degrees of freedom via an exponential interpolation between $T_c$ and $c\,T_c$ (with $c=1.3$ or $c=1.5$), noting that a quantitative description of hadronic degrees of freedom above $T_c$ is not available.

## Method
The authors use the Monte-Carlo propagation of heavy quarks including collisional and radiative energy loss (MC@sHQ) within a 3+1d fluid dynamically expanding plasma from EPOS initial conditions. Heavy quarks are initialized randomly at nucleon-nucleon scattering points according to FONLL $p_T$-distributions, then evolved via the Boltzmann equation using pQCD cross sections with running coupling, HTL propagators, and bremsstrahlung gluon emission including the LPM effect. Hadronization occurs at $T_c$ via coalescence for low-$p_T$ and fragmentation for intermediate- and high-$p_T$ heavy quarks. The fraction of partonic degrees of freedom $\lambda(T)$ is modeled as an exponential function from 0 at $T_c$ to 1 at $c\,T_c$, with $T_c=155$ MeV and two scenarios $c=1.3$ and $c=1.5$.

## Key numbers
- $T_c = 155$ MeV, chosen as the middle of the lattice QCD range of 145-165 MeV
- Two scenarios for the upper bound of the mixed region: $c=1.3$ (i.e., up to $1.3 \times 155 = 201.5$ MeV) and $c=1.5$ (i.e., up to $1.5 \times 155 = 232.5$ MeV)
- For the nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ at $p_T > 7$ GeV, the scenario with $c=1.3$ gives the same result as a global temperature-independent rescaling $K=0.8$
- The $R_{\rm AA}$ increases by a factor of 2 at intermediate and larger transverse momentum when going from no reduction to the largest reduction scenario
- The elliptic flow $v_2$ is calculated for $D$ mesons in 30-50% centrality Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV
- Azimuthal correlations are studied for three $p_T$-trigger classes: [1-4] GeV, [4-10] GeV, and [10-20] GeV

## Context
This work extends the MC@sHQ model previously coupled to a 2+1d fluid dynamical background with a first-order phase transition (Kolb 2003) to a 3+1d fluid dynamical medium from EPOS initial conditions (Werner 2010, 2012). The investigation is motivated by lattice QCD calculations (Borsanyi 2010) and the PNJL model (Ratti 2011) suggesting hadronic bound states may exist above $T_c$. The results contrast with models that use a global temperature-independent rescaling parameter $K$ (Molnar 2004, Gossiaux 2008, Uphoff 2010) and with approaches that include resonant scatterings from in-medium $D$-meson-like resonances (vanHees 2004, 2005). The findings enable future work distinguishing between global rescaling and temperature-dependent reduction of partonic degrees of freedom, particularly through elliptic flow measurements where final hadronic interactions are expected to contribute.
