# Entropic destruction of a moving heavy quarkonium

> Recently it has been shown that the peak of the quarkonium entropy at the deconfinement transition is related to the emergent entropic force which destructs the quarkonium. Using the AdS/CFT correspondence, we consider dissociation of a moving heavy quarkonium by entropic force. We find that the entropic force destructs the moving quarkonium easier than the static case which is expected from perturbative weakly coupled plasma. By considering the Maxwell charge, we study the effect of medium on the destruction of heavy quarkonium. It is shown that the quarkonium dissociates easier in the medium.

## Key claim

The entropic force, which emerges from the growth of quarkonium entropy with inter-quark distance, destroys a moving heavy quarkonium more easily than a static one, and this effect is stronger when the quarkonium moves orthogonal to the plasma wind compared to parallel motion.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to model a moving heavy quark-antiquark pair in a strongly coupled plasma. They boost the background metric (either N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills, confining Yang-Mills from D4 branes, or AdS Reissner-Nordstrom black hole) to describe motion with rapidity beta, then compute the free energy of the U-shaped fundamental string connecting the quarks and the entropy via S = -dF/dT. The entropy is compared for transverse (theta=pi/2) and parallel (theta=0) alignments of the quarkonium relative to the plasma wind.

## Key numbers

- In N=4 SYM, the screening length for static quarkonium is L = c/T with c = 0.28; for L greater than this value, the quarks are completely screened and the entropy becomes S = sqrt(lambda) * theta(L - c/T).
- The critical temperature of the confining Yang-Mills theory is T_c = 1/(2 pi R).
- In the AdS Reissner-Nordstrom background, the black hole temperature is T = (u_h/pi) * (1 - q^2/(2 u_h^6)), where u_h is the horizon location and q is the black hole charge.
- For the finite density study, the chemical potential is mu = 55 MeV with n = N_F/N_C = 1 (left and right plots of Fig. 4), and mu = 30 MeV with n = 2/3 (right plot of Fig. 3).

## Context

This work extends the holographic study of static quarkonium entropy by Hashimoto (2014) to the case of moving quarkonium, motivated by the fact that experimentally observed charmonium at RHIC and LHC moves with relativistic velocities through the QGP. The authors previously studied finite 't Hooft coupling corrections to the thermal width and imaginary potential of moving charmonium (Fadafan, 2015). The results confirm that the peak of quarkonium entropy at the deconfinement transition is a generic feature of holographic confining models, and that the entropic force provides a mechanism for the charmonium suppression puzzle at RHIC and LHC raised by Kharzeev (2014). The work enables future lattice QCD comparisons and studies of competition between entropic force and imaginary potential for meson melting.
