# Neutron capture rates and r-process nucleosynthesis

> Simulations of r-process nucleosynthesis require nuclear physics information for thousands of neutron-rich nuclear species from the line of stability to the neutron drip line. While arguably the most important pieces of nuclear data for the r-process are the masses and beta decay rates, individual neutron capture rates can also be of key importance in setting the final r-process abundance pattern. Here we consider the influence of neutron capture rates in forming the A~80 and rare earth peaks.

## Key claim
Individual neutron capture rates can significantly influence the final r-process abundance pattern in the A~80 and rare earth peak regions, even though masses and beta-decay rates are generally the most important nuclear data for r-process simulations. The influence depends on whether (n,gamma)-(gamma,n) equilibrium holds and on the specific freezeout mechanism (photodissociation-limited vs. capture-limited).

## Method
The authors performed sensitivity studies using nuclear network calculations with hydrodynamic trajectories from black hole-neutron star mergers, realistic supernova neutrino-driven winds, and parameterized wind trajectories. For the A~80 region, they individually varied neutron capture rates of approximately 300 nuclei in about fifty baseline weak r-process simulations, increasing each rate by a factor of 100 and comparing the resulting abundance patterns. For the rare earth region, they used hot and cold main r-process baseline simulations and quantified local changes using an F-measure when capture rates were increased by a factor of 10.

## Key numbers
- In the A~80 region, the most influential capture rates produce changes of >5%, 10-15%, or >15% in the overall r-process abundance pattern when increased by a factor of 100 over baseline simulations.
- The weak r-process example used entropy s/k=10, timescale tau=100 ms, and electron fraction Y_e=0.250.
- The hot main r-process simulation used entropy s/k=85, timescale tau=85 ms, and initial electron fraction Y_e=0.25.
- The cold r-process simulation used entropy s/k=40, timescale tau=50 ms, and Y_e=0.25.
- In the rare earth region, capture rates of nuclei with A between 160 and 168 showed heightened sensitivity, with F-measure values of 100-200, 200-300, or >300 when rates were increased by a factor of 10.

## Context
This work extends the earlier sensitivity study of Surman (2009) that focused on the A~130 region, now examining the A~80 and rare earth peak regions. The authors find that in the weak r-process, an early-time neutron capture effect can occur (unlike the photodissociation effect seen in main r-process simulations), where capture rates of nuclei near the N=50 closed shell can control how far the process proceeds. The results enable experimental and theoretical efforts to prioritize neutron capture rate measurements for specific nuclei identified as most influential, particularly those along beta-decay pathways of closed-shell nuclei and even-N nuclei farther from stability in cold r-process scenarios.
