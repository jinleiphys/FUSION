# Ab initio many-body calculation of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B radiative capture

> We apply the ab initio no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) approach to calculate the cross section of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B radiative capture. This reaction is important for understanding the solar neutrino flux. Starting from a selected similarity-transformed chiral nucleon-nucleon interaction that accurately describes two-nucleon data, we performed parameter-free many-body calculations that simultaneously predict both the normalization and the shape of the S-factor. We study the dependence on the number of 7Be eigenstates included in the coupled-channel equations and on the size of the harmonic oscillator basis used for the expansion of the eigenstates and of the localized parts of the integration kernels. Our S-factor result at zero energy is on the lower side of, but consistent with, the latest evaluation.

## Key claim

The authors present the first parameter-free *ab initio* many-body calculation of the 7Be(p,gamma)8B radiative capture S-factor, finding S17(0) = 19.4(7) eV b, which is on the lower side of but consistent with the latest evaluation of 20.8 ± 0.7 (expt) ± 1.4 (theory) eV b, with the qualification that the result depends on the number of 7Be eigenstates included and the harmonic oscillator basis size.

## Method

The authors applied the *ab initio* no-core shell model/resonating group method (NCSM/RGM) approach, starting from a similarity-renormalization-group (SRG)-evolved chiral N3LO nucleon-nucleon potential with evolution parameter Λ = 1.86 fm⁻¹. They solved integro-differential coupled-channel equations for bound and scattering states of the p-7Be system, using the five lowest Nmax=10 eigenstates of 7Be calculated within the NCSM, and computed the radiative capture cross section using a one-body E1 transition operator.

## Key numbers

- S17(0) = 19.4(7) eV b, the calculated S-factor at zero energy, on the lower side of the latest evaluation of 20.8 ± 0.7 (expt) ± 1.4 (theory) eV b.
- Calculated 8B ground state separation energy of 136 keV, close to the experimental value of 137 keV, using the SRG-N3LO NN potential with Λ = 1.86 fm⁻¹.
- 7Be ground state energy of -38.46 MeV from NCSM calculation (Nmax up to 14), compared to the experimental value of -37.60 MeV.
- 7Be charge radius of 2.46(2) fm from NCSM, compared to the experimental value of 2.647(17) fm.
- 7Be ground state magnetic moment of -1.15 μN from NCSM, compared to the experimental value of -1.3995(5) μN.
- B(M1; 1/2⁻ → 3/2⁻) transition strength of 3.14 μN² from NCSM, compared to the experimental value of 3.71(48) μN².

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* NCSM/RGM approach developed by the same group (Navratil et al., 2009-2011) to the astrophysically important 7Be(p,gamma)8B reaction, which is crucial for interpreting solar neutrino flux measurements from the Sudbury Neutrino Observatory. The calculation improves upon previous theoretical approaches such as the R-matrix parametrization (Barker, 1995), potential model (Robertson, 1983; Typel, 1997; Davids, 2003), microscopic cluster models (Descouvemont, 1994; Csoto, 1995; Descouvemont, 2004), and earlier *ab initio* work using NCSM wave functions only for the 8B bound state (Navratil et al., 2006). The results enable future improvements by including three-nucleon interactions and coupling the NCSM/RGM binary-cluster basis with NCSM calculations for 8B.
