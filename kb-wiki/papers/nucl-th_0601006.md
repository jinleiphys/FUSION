# Perfect fluidity of sQGP core and dissipative hadronic corona

> We can establish a new picture, the perfect fluid sQGP core and the dissipative hadronic corona, of the space-time evolution of produced matter in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC. It is also shown that the picture works well also in the forward rapidity region through an analysis based on a new class of the hydro-kinetic model and is a manifestation of deconfinement.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the agreement of ideal hydrodynamic simulations with experimental data at RHIC is not evidence for perfect fluidity throughout the entire evolution, but rather supports a hybrid picture: a perfect fluid strongly-coupled Quark Gluon Plasma (sQGP) core surrounded by a dissipative hadronic corona, where the apparent success of purely ideal hydrodynamic models in the hadron phase is an accidental consequence of neglecting particle ratio data.

## Method

The author performed systematic comparisons of three different assumptions for the hadron phase in hydrodynamic simulations of Au+Au collisions at RHIC: chemical equilibrium (CE), chemically frozen or partial chemical equilibrium (PCE), and hadronic cascade models (HC). A hybrid dynamical model was then developed, combining a fully three-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic description of the QGP phase with a kinetic hadronic cascade model (JAM) for the hadron phase, and the pseudorapidity dependence of elliptic flow was computed and compared to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Mean transverse momentum for pions was calculated as a function of thermal freezeout temperature at impact parameter b=5 fm in sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV Au+Au collisions, showing qualitatively different behavior between chemical equilibrium and chemically frozen assumptions.
- Critical temperature and chemical freezeout temperature were taken as T_c = T^ch = 170 MeV in the hydrodynamic model.
- Switching temperature from hydrodynamic to kinetic description was taken as T_sw = 169 MeV.
- Shear viscosities of both QGP and hadron phases were found to give eta ~ 0.1 GeV/fm^2 around T_c.
- The minimum possible value of eta/s is stated as 1/(4 pi), which the QGP phase is said to be comparable with.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework of Huovinen (2003) and the experimental findings from the STAR (Ackermann 2000), PHENIX (Adcox 2002), and PHOBOS (Back 2002) collaborations at RHIC. The hybrid model builds upon the dynamical framework of Hirano and Nara and incorporates the JAM hadronic cascade model. The picture enables future extraction of transport properties of the QGP from experimental data and identifies the rapid increase of entropy density near the QCD critical temperature, rather than a sudden reduction of viscosity, as the key signature of deconfinement.
