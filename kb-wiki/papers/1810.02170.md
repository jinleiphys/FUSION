# Description of phi-meson production in hadronic and nuclear collisions at very high energies

> We expose the current experimental and theoretical situation of the interesting case of the production of phi mesons in up to very high energy collisions of hadrons on both nucleon and nuclear targets, and we present a quantitavely good theoretical description of the corresponding experimental data, based on the formalism of the well established Quark-Gluon String Model, that has proved to be valid for a wide energy range. All the available experimental data for phi-meson production in hadron-nucleon collisions on the spectra of secondary phi, and on the ratios of phi/pi(-) and phi/K(-) production cross sections, as well the corresponding ones for phi-meson production on nuclear targets, are considered. In particular, it is seen that the production of phi-mesons on nuclear targets presents unusually small shadow corrections for the inclusive density in the central rapidity region.

# Description of phi-meson production in hadronic and nuclear collisions at very high energies

## Key claim

The Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM) provides a quantitatively good description of phi-meson production data across a wide energy range, but the model overpredicts the midrapidity density measured by ALICE in pp collisions at 2.76 and 7 TeV, possibly due to the experimental extrapolation to zero transverse momentum from data measured only at pT >= 0.4 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors use the Quark-Gluon String Model (QGSM), based on Dual Topological Unitarization and Regge phenomenology, to calculate inclusive spectra of phi mesons produced in hadron-nucleon, hadron-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions. The model treats high-energy collisions as exchanges of one or several Pomerons, with inclusive spectra determined by convolutions of diquark, valence quark, and sea quark distribution functions with fragmentation functions into phi mesons, using a normalization parameter a_phi = 0.11 fixed from low-energy data. For nuclear targets, Gribov-Glauber multiple-scattering theory is used, and at LHC energies inelastic-shadowing corrections are included by imposing a maximal number of cut Pomerons (n_max = 21) to account for percolation effects.

## Key numbers

- Normalization parameter a_phi = 0.11, determined from low-energy experimental data and used for all energies
- In pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV: experimental dn/dy (|y| <= 0.5) = 0.0260 +/- 0.0004 +/- 0.003, QGSM calculation gives 0.032
- In pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7.00 TeV: experimental dn/dy (|y| <= 0.5) = 0.032 +/- 0.0004 +/- 0.004, QGSM calculation gives 0.05
- In p + C collisions at sqrt(s) = 41.6 GeV: experimental dσ_pA/dy (|y| <= 0.5) = 1.74 +/- 0.15 mb, QGSM gives 1.5 mb
- In p + Ti collisions at sqrt(s) = 41.6 GeV: experimental dσ_pA/dy (|y| <= 0.5) = 6.85 +/- 0.7 mb, QGSM gives 7.1 mb
- In p + W collisions at sqrt(s) = 41.6 GeV: experimental dσ_pA/dy (|y| <= 0.5) = 23.5 +/- 2.1 mb, QGSM gives 19.1 mb
- In central Pb+Pb collisions (0-5%) at sqrt(s) = 17.3 GeV: experimental dn/dy (|y| <= 0.5) = 2.35 +/- 0.15, QGSM gives 2.764
- In central Au+Au collisions (0-5%) at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV: experimental dn/dy (|y| <= 0.5) = 7.95 +/- 0.11 +/- 0.73, QGSM gives 8.12
- Maximal number of cut Pomerons n_max = 13 at RHIC energies, n_max = 21 at LHC energies

## Context

This work extends previous QGSM analyses of pseudoscalar meson (pi, K) and baryon production (KaPi, Sh, softpPb, AMPS, AMPSk) to the specific case of phi mesons, updating earlier results (AMShPhi1, AMShPhi2) with new experimental data from ALICE at LHC energies. The paper shows that phi-meson production on nuclear targets exhibits unusually small shadow corrections at RHIC energies compared to pions and kaons, but that inelastic-shadowing effects become sizable at LHC energies, consistent with the percolation approach of Capella, Kaidalov, and Tran Thanh Van (CKTr). The results enable further tests of the transition between soft and hard QCD regimes using phi mesons as a probe.
