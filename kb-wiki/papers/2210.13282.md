# Perturbative Color Correlations in Double Parton Scattering

> We study the contribution of color correlations to Double Parton Scattering (DPS). We show that there is a specific class of Feynman diagrams related to so called 1 \textrightarrow{} 2 processes when the contribution of these color correlations is not Sudakov suppressed with the transverse scales. The split scale increases with the transverse scale Q, with color correlations being up to 5-10 percent of singlet ones. The effective absence of Sudakov suppression gives hope that although they are small relative to color singlet correlations, they eventually can be observed.

## Key claim

The authors show that for a specific class of Feynman diagrams (the so-called 1→2 processes), color correlations in double parton scattering are not Sudakov suppressed with the transverse scale, unlike the conventional 2→2 processes where such suppression is severe; this means color correlations, though small relative to color singlet correlations (5–10%), may eventually be observable at Tevatron and LHC energies.

## Method

The authors use a pQCD formalism based on the DGLAP equation modified for non-singlet color representations, employing a "DDT-like" regularization scheme. They compute the two-particle generalized parton distribution (₂GPD) for 1→2 processes by solving the non-singlet DGLAP equation in Mellin space, separating the solution into a Sudakov factor and a "tree level" factor, then numerically inverting the Mellin transform. The analysis focuses on gluonic ₂GPDs, which dominate due to large color factors, and uses analytical continuation for the x→1 asymptotic region where the saddle point method applies.

## Key numbers

- Color correlations are predicted to be up to 5–10 percent of singlet correlations in the ₂GPD for 1→2 processes.
- The Sudakov suppression factor for non-singlet states is approximately exp[−(α_s/2π)(C_A^A − ᵅC̄_A^A) ln²(Q²/k₀²)], with corrections from non-singular kernel parts contributing less than 10 percent numerically.
- The strong coupling constant is taken as α_s(k²) = 12π/[β₀ · ln(k²/Λ_QCD²)] with Λ_QCD = 0.22 GeV, β₀ = 11·N − 2·n_f, and N = n_f = 3.
- The split scale for color non-singlet ladders increases with the hard scale Q, unlike the singlet split scale which is of order several GeV and does not depend on energy.

## Context

This work extends the study of color correlations in DPS beyond the early work of Mekhfi (1988) and Artru (1980s), who showed that such correlations are strongly Sudakov suppressed for conventional 2→2 processes. It builds on the formalism for ₂GPDs developed by Diehl (2016), Manohar (2012), and others, and addresses the problem raised in Diehl (2021) that non-singlet ₂GPDs can be negative. The results enable future experimental searches for color correlations at LHC and Tevatron, and suggest that 1v1 processes with two singlet ladders splitting into color ladders may be distinguishable from conventional singlet DPS and 1→1 hard processes.
