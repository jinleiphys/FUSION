# Study of Baryon Resonances in the Photoproduction gamma p ---> K* Sigma(1190)

> In this talk, we report the theoretical studies of baryon resonances for the gamma p ---> K*0 Sigma+ and gamma p ---> K*+ Sigma0 reaction processes by making use of the effective Lagrangian method in the tree-level Born approximation. The numerical results for the total and differential cross sections are shown with the presently available data. It turns out that the contributions from the N* and Delta* resonances are almost negligible near the threshold energy. On the other hand, the effects of the K exchange in the t channel and Delta(1232) in the s channel are considerably larger than other ones in the scattering process, being in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The contributions from higher-mass N* and Delta* resonances (such as D13(2080), S11(2090), G17(2190), D15(2200), S31(2150), G37(2200), and F37(2390)) are almost negligible near the threshold energy for gamma p -> K* Sigma(1190) photoproduction, while the K-exchange in the t channel and Delta(1232) in the s channel dominate the scattering process.

## Method

The authors employed the effective Lagrangian method in the tree-level Born approximation to calculate total and differential cross sections for the gamma p -> K*0 Sigma+ and gamma p -> K*+ Sigma0 reactions. They included all possible N* and Delta* resonances with masses below about 2500 MeV in a full relativistic manner, with coupling constants determined from experimental data and the SU(6) relativistic quark model, and used phenomenological form factors with cutoff masses tuned to reproduce experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Cutoff masses for form factors: Lambda_K = Lambda_kappa = 1.15 GeV for t-channel meson exchange; Lambda_N = Lambda_Delta = 1.50 GeV for s-channel baryon poles; Lambda_N* = Lambda_Delta* = 1.00 GeV for s-channel resonances; Lambda_Sigma = Lambda_Sigma* = 0.95 GeV for u-channel hyperons; Lambda_Lambda = 0.70 GeV for u-channel Lambda
- Photon energy range for K*0 Sigma+ differential cross sections: 1.925 to 2.9125 GeV
- Photon energy range for K*+ Sigma0 differential cross sections: 1.85 to 3.75 GeV
- Resonance states considered only for sqrt(s) less than or equal to 2500 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of K* Sigma photoproduction using the effective Lagrangian method by Oh (2006) and the chiral quark model by Zhao (2001), which the authors note had difficulty reproducing recent experimental data. The study contrasts with K* Lambda photoproduction (Kim, 2012), where resonance contributions were found to be more significant. The results enable future comparisons with data from the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration at ELSA, the CLAS collaboration at Jefferson Lab, and the LEPS collaboration at SPring-8, and suggest that higher-mass baryon resonances do not play a major role in this reaction channel near threshold.
