# Directed flow of light flavor hadrons for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}=$ 7.7-200 GeV

> We have studied the directed flow of light-flavor hadrons for Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{S_{NN}}=$ 7.7 - 200 GeV. The initial condition is taken from a suitable Glauber model which is further evolved within the framework of relativistic hydrodynamics. Model calculations of the rapidity-odd directed flow ($v_1$) of identified light-flavor hadrons are compared with the available experimental data after suitably calibrating the initial condition to describe the rapidity dependence of charged particle multiplicity and net-proton yield. For reasonable choice of the initial condition, we are able to describe the measured rapidity and beam energy dependence of identified hadron $v_{1}$ including the observed $v_1$ splitting between baryons and anti-baryons.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a multistage hybrid framework (hydrodynamics + hadronic transport) with a tilted initial condition for both energy density and net baryon density can simultaneously describe the rapidity and beam energy dependence of directed flow for identified light-flavor hadrons across Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV, including the observed splitting between baryon and anti-baryon directed flow. However, the model parameters cannot be uniquely constrained by the available data, and the baryon diffusion coefficient remains a free parameter that must be adjusted separately at the lowest beam energies.

## Method

The initial condition is constructed from a Monte Carlo Glauber model with asymmetric matter and baryon deposition along rapidity, creating tilted profiles in the reaction plane that are evolved using the relativistic hydrodynamic code MUSIC with a lattice QCD-based equation of state (NEoS-BQS) at finite baryon density. The fluid evolution includes shear viscosity with specific shear viscosity parameter C_eta = 0.08 and baryon diffusion with coefficient kappa_B controlled by a free parameter C_B (set to 1.0 for energies above 11.5 GeV and 0.5 at 11.5 and 7.7 GeV). The Cooper-Frye particlization is performed on a constant energy density hypersurface at epsilon_f = 0.26 GeV/fm^3, followed by hadronic transport using UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 7.7, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Initial proper time tau_0 ranges from 0.6 fm at 200 GeV to 3.6 fm at 7.7 GeV
- Initial energy density normalization epsilon_0 ranges from 8.0 GeV/fm^3 at 200 GeV to 0.55 GeV/fm^3 at 7.7 GeV
- Hardness factor alpha ranges from 0.14 at 200 GeV to 0.1 at 7.7 GeV
- Freeze-out energy density: epsilon_f = 0.26 GeV/fm^3
- Specific shear viscosity parameter C_eta = 0.08
- Baryon diffusion parameter C_B = 1.0 for energies above 11.5 GeV, C_B = 0.5 at 11.5 and 7.7 GeV
- The model captures the sign change in the slope of proton directed flow at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed initial baryon profile (Parida and Chatterjee, 2022) from studies at 19.6 and 200 GeV to the full RHIC Beam Energy Scan range down to 7.7 GeV. It builds on the tilted initial condition approach of Bozek (2010) and the baryon deposition ansatz of Denicol (2018) and Shen (2020). The results enable future studies that evolve strangeness and electric charge independently in hydrodynamic simulations rather than constraining them via the equation of state, and highlight the need for centrality-dependent measurements of proton directed flow to constrain baryon stopping mechanisms at lower energies.
