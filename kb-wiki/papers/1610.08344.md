# Charge conservation effects for high order fluctuations

> The exact charge conservation significantly impacts multiplicity fluctuations. The result depends strongly on the part of the system charge carried by the particles of interest. Along with the expected suppression of fluctuations for large systems, charge conservation may lead to negative skewness or kurtosis for small systems.

# Charge conservation effects for high order fluctuations

## Key claim

Exact charge conservation in the canonical ensemble can produce negative skewness or kurtosis for small systems, depending on the fraction of the system's total charge carried by the particles of interest, contrary to the positive values expected from the grand canonical ensemble.

## Method

The author calculates normalized skewness and normalized kurtosis for multiplicity fluctuations in a canonical ensemble (CE) hadron gas model with one conserved charge, total charge Q=2, for primary particles without resonance decays. The fraction of charge carried by the particles of interest, k=z_j/z, is varied (k=0.95 and k=0.5) to study its effect on fluctuations of plus, minus, net, and total charged particle multiplicities as a function of the mean number of neutral particles.

## Key numbers

- For k=0.95 (particles carry 95% of the total charge Q=2), the normalized skewness S·σ in the canonical ensemble can become negative for small systems.
- For k=0.5, the normalized kurtosis κ·σ² in the canonical ensemble can become negative for small systems.
- The total charge of the system is Q=2, representing a small system analogous to p+p collisions.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of charge conservation effects on fluctuations beyond the grand canonical ensemble (GCE), where fluctuations are either Poisson (S·σ=1, κ·σ²=1) or Gaussian (S·σ=0, κ·σ²=0). The results are relevant for interpreting non-monotonous behavior of net-proton normalized kurtosis observed by STAR (Thader 2016) and for the system size and energy scan by NA61/SHINE (Mackowiak-Pawlowska 2016) in the search for the critical point of strongly interacting matter. The calculations are based on the formalism of Begun (2004) for comparing CE and GCE multiplicity fluctuations.
