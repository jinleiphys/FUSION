# Proton and pion distribution functions in counterpoint

> Working with proton and pion valence distribution functions (DFs) determined consistently at the same, unique hadron scale and exploiting the possibility that there is an effective charge which defines an evolution scheme for DFs that is all-orders exact, we obtain a unified body of predictions for all proton and pion DFs - valence, glue, and four-flavour-separated sea. Whilst the hadron light-front momentum fractions carried by identifiable parton classes are the same for the proton and pion at any scale, the pointwise behaviour of the DFs is strongly hadron-dependent. All calculated distributions comply with quantum chromodynamics constraints on low- and high-$x$ scaling behaviour and, owing to emergent hadron mass, pion DFs are the most dilated. These results aid in elucidating the sources of similarities and differences between proton and pion structure.

## Key claim

Working with proton and pion valence distribution functions determined at the same hadron scale and using an effective charge that defines an all-orders exact evolution scheme, the authors obtain a unified set of predictions for all proton and pion parton distribution functions; the key hedge is that the effective charge need not be process-independent and therefore is not unique, though a suitable process-independent charge is available.

## Method

The authors use continuum Schwinger function methods to obtain hadron-scale valence parton distribution functions for the proton and pion, then evolve these to higher scales using one-loop DGLAP equations integrated with an effective running coupling that provides an infrared completion of the perturbative running coupling. The evolution includes a threshold function that suppresses gluon-to-quark splitting for heavier quark flavors until the resolving energy scale exceeds the quark's infrared mass, and a Pauli blocking factor in the gluon splitting function that shifts momentum between light quark flavors while preserving baryon number.

## Key numbers

- Hadron scale value: $\zeta_{\cal H} = 0.331(2)\,$GeV (results reported with $\pm 5\%$ variation for uncertainty)
- At hadron scale, valence momentum fractions: $\langle x \rangle_{{\mathpzc u}_p}^{\zeta_{\cal H}}=0.687$, $\langle x \rangle_{{\mathpzc d}_p}^{\zeta_{\cal H}} = 0.313$, $\langle x \rangle_{{\mathpzc u}_\pi}^{\zeta_{\cal H}} =0.5$
- At $\zeta_2=2\,$GeV, proton glue momentum fraction: $\langle x \rangle_{{\mathpzc g}^p}^{\zeta_2}=40.9(1.1)\%$; pion glue: $\langle x \rangle_{{\mathpzc g}^\pi}^{\zeta_2}=41.0(1.2)\%$
- At $\zeta_3=m_{J/\psi}=3.097\,$GeV, proton valence $u$ momentum fraction: $\langle x \rangle_{{\mathpzc u}^p}^{\zeta_3}=30.4(1.3)\%$; pion valence $u$: $\langle x \rangle_{{\mathpzc u}^\pi}^{\zeta_3}=22.1(1.0)\%$
- Large-$x$ exponent for proton valence DFs at hadron scale: $(1-x)^3$; for pion valence DFs: $(1-x)^2$
- At $\zeta_2=2\,$GeV, glue large-$x$ exponent for proton: $\beta_{\rm proton}^{\rm glue} = 5.33(5)$; for pion: $\beta_{\rm pion}^{\rm glue} = 3.75(5)$
- Pauli blocking strength parameter: ${\mathpzc g}_{d,\bar d}= 0.34 = -{\mathpzc g}_{u,\bar u}$

## Context

This work extends continuum Schwinger function method calculations of hadron-scale valence DFs from Cui (2020, 2021) and Chang (2022) to produce a unified set of predictions for both proton and pion DFs including glue and sea distributions. The results contradict typical phenomenological fits such as those from Accardi (2016), NNPDF (2017), and Hou (2019) that fail to satisfy QCD large-$x$ scaling constraints, and provide predictions testable at upgraded facilities including Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider. The isovector distribution agrees with lattice QCD results from Lin (2020), and the pion glue DF agrees with lattice calculations by Fan (2021).
