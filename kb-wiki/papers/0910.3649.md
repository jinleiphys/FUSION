# Hadronic parity violation in pionless effective field theory

> We present results for two-body observables that are sensitive to the parity-violating component of nucleon-nucleon interactions. These interactions are studied using an effective field theory in which the only dynamic degrees of freedom are nucleon fields. The observables we study are cross-section asymmetries in nucleon-nucleon scattering and asymmetries and induced polarizations in the process np \to dγ.

## Key claim

The parity-violating component of nucleon-nucleon interactions can be described model-independently using pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)), where all interactions are contact terms and the five unknown low-energy constants (LECs) can in principle be determined from a sufficient set of experimental observables, though the results presented are a step toward that determination rather than a complete extraction.

## Method

The authors construct the leading-order parity-violating Lagrangian in pionless effective field theory, containing five contact terms each with an unknown low-energy constant. They then calculate the longitudinal asymmetry in nucleon-nucleon scattering (for pp, nn, and np systems) and the photon asymmetry and induced circular polarization in the radiative neutron capture process np → dγ, all at leading order in the EFT expansion, using the power-divergence subtraction scheme.

## Key numbers

- The pp longitudinal asymmetry at 13.6 MeV yields the extracted combination 𝒜_pp(μ=m_π) = (1.3 ± 0.3) × 10⁻¹⁴ MeV⁻³.
- The experimental result for A_L^pp at 45 MeV (Kistryn, 1987) is noted to lie outside the domain of applicability of EFT(π̸).
- The ongoing experimental effort at the Spallation Neutron Source aims to measure A_γ to a sensitivity of δA_γ = 1 × 10⁻⁸.
- The leading-order result for the photon asymmetry A_γ is expressed as A_γ = (32/3) (M / [κ₁(1 − γ a_Sing)]) (eR / CTrip), with no numerical value quoted.
- The leading-order result for the induced circular polarization P_γ is given as an expression involving aR, bR, dR, and the strong couplings, with no numerical value quoted.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to hadronic parity violation pioneered by Kaplan (1992, 1998), Savage (1998, 2000), and Zhu (2004), providing a systematic, model-independent alternative to the traditional one-meson exchange model of Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein (1979) and the Danilov amplitude approach. The results enable future extraction of the five parity-violating low-energy constants by combining these two-body observables with few-body processes such as asymmetries in n d → t γ or n α spin rotation, for which experiments are ongoing or being considered.
