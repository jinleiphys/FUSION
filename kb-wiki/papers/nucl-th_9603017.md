# Deriving the quark condensate within a finite Fermi system from the generating functional of chiral perturbation theory

> The generating functional of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at order O(Q^2) in the mean field approximation (with a pseudoscalar source coupling which is consistent with the PCAC-Ward identities on the current quark level) has been exploited to derive Migdal's in--medium pion propagator. It is shown that the prediction for the density dependence of the quark condensate obtained on the composite hadron level by embedding PCAC within the framework of Migdal's approach to finite Fermi systems is identical to that resulting from QCD.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when Migdal's in-medium pion propagator is derived from the generating functional of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at order O(Q²) in the mean field approximation, the S-wave pion self-energy contains an infinite number of higher powers of matter density rather than being linear in density; nevertheless, the prediction for the density dependence of the quark condensate obtained on the composite hadron level by embedding PCAC within Migdal's approach to finite Fermi systems is identical to the linear decrease predicted by QCD.

## Method

The authors derive Migdal's in-medium pion propagator from the generating functional of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at order O(Q²) in the mean field approximation, using a pseudoscalar source coupling consistent with PCAC-Ward identities on the current quark level. They then evaluate the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GOR) relation within a finite Fermi system in two ways: first by assuming PCAC as an operator relation and exploiting the Migdal propagator, and second by calculating the PCAC condition on the matrix element level within Migdal's theory. Both approaches are compared with the direct result from the generating functional formalism.

## Key numbers

- The pion-nucleon sigma term σ is identified with σ_N, and the combination (c₂ + c₃)m_π² is extracted from the empirical isospin even pion-nucleon scattering length as (c₂ + c₃)m_π² ≈ -26 MeV.
- The nucleon mass in the SU(2) chiral limit is m₀ = 890 MeV, with m_N = m₀ + σ > m₀.
- The in-medium pion decay constant is given by (f_π^t(ρ))² = f_π² (1 + 2(c₂+c₃)ρ/f_π²) + O(m_π).
- The in-medium quark condensate is ⟨0̃|q̄q|0̃⟩ = ⟨0|q̄q|0⟩ (1 - σ_Nρ/(f_π² m_π²)) + O(m_π).
- The in-medium pion mass is (m_π*)² = m_π² (1 - σ_Nρ/(f_π² m_π²))/(1 + 2(c₂+c₃)ρ/f_π²) ≈ m_π².
- The S-wave pion self-energy at zero momentum satisfies Π^S(0,0)/m_π² = 1/(1 - σ_Nρ/(f_π² m_π²)) - 1, which expands as (σ_Nρ/(f_π² m_π²))(1 + σ_Nρ/(f_π² m_π²) + (σ_Nρ/(f_π² m_π²))² + ...).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Birse (1990s) and others who used PCAC in combination with Migdal's pion propagator to study the density dependence of the quark condensate. The paper contradicts the claim by DEE (1992) and CEK (1994) that the quark condensate would show a hyperbolic decrease with density when using approximate forms of the S-wave pion self-energy. The authors show that a consistent treatment of PCAC, as enabled by the generating functional formalism of Gasser, Sainio and Svarc (1988), recovers the linear decrease predicted by QCD and rules out the possibility of learning more about the evolution of the quark condensate with density from composite hadron physics.
