# The Kohn-Luttinger Effect in Dense Matter and its Implications for Neutron Stars

> Repulsive short-range interactions can induce p-wave attraction between fermions in dense matter and lead to Cooper pairing at the Fermi surface. We investigate this phenomenon, well-known as the Kohn-Luttinger effect in condensed matter physics, in dense matter with strong short-range repulsive interactions. We find that repulsive interactions required to stabilize massive neutron stars can induce p-wave pairing in neutron and quark matter. When massive vector bosons mediate the interaction between fermions, the induced interaction favors Cooper pairing in the 3P2 channel. For the typical strength of the interaction favored by massive neutron stars, the associated pairing gaps in neutrons can be in the range of 10 keV to 10 MeV. Strong and attractive spin-orbit and tensor forces between neutrons can result in repulsive induced interactions that greatly suppress the 3P2 pairing gap in neutron matter. In quark matter, the induced interaction is too small to result in pairing gaps of phenomenological relevance.

## Key claim
Repulsive short-range interactions required to stabilize massive neutron stars can induce p-wave Cooper pairing in neutron and quark matter via the Kohn-Luttinger effect, but the resulting pairing gaps are highly sensitive to the interplay between central, spin-orbit, and tensor components of the nuclear force, with strong attractive spin-orbit and tensor forces potentially suppressing the \(^3P_2\) pairing gap in neutron matter.

## Method
The authors calculated the induced interaction between fermions at the Fermi surface using second-order perturbation theory, evaluating the screening, vertex, and exchange diagrams (zero-sound and exchange zero-sound diagrams) for a bare potential expanded up to order \((k_F/\Lambda)^2\) with central, spin-orbit, and tensor components. They derived analytic expressions for the induced \(^1S_0\) and \(^3P_J\) potentials up to \(\mathcal{O}(mk_F^5)\) and estimated pairing gaps using the BCS approximation, assuming the bare interaction is mediated by heavy vector meson exchange. Three scenarios were considered: (A) pure vector meson exchange, (B) variable central p-wave and spin-orbit strengths, and (C) inclusion of tensor interactions to match nucleon-nucleon phase shift data.

## Key numbers
- Pairing gaps in neutrons from the Kohn-Luttinger effect are estimated to be in the range of **10 keV to 10 MeV** for the typical interaction strength favored by massive neutron stars.
- For a dimensionless Fermi liquid parameter \(F_0 \gtrsim 2\) at baryon density \(n_B \gtrsim 3 n_{\mathrm{sat}}\), the induced p-wave gap is predicted to be \(\Delta_p \gtrsim 1\) MeV.
- The maximum \(^3P_2\) gap from bare nuclear potentials in earlier work is \(\Delta_{^3P_2} \simeq 1-2\) MeV between \(2-3 n_{\mathrm{sat}}\), while chiral EFT calculations give a maximum \(\Delta_{^3P_2} \simeq 0.4\) MeV at \(n_B \simeq 1.3 n_{\mathrm{sat}}\).
- In scenario A with \(F_0 = 3\) at \(n_B = 3 n_{\mathrm{sat}}\), the \(^3P_2\) gap from the induced interaction is shown for coupling strengths corresponding to \(F_0 = 2, 3, 4\).
- The induced \(^3P_2\) potential at leading order is \(V^{\mathrm{ind}}_{^3P_J} = m k_F \bar{C}_0^2 \frac{1}{5\pi^2} (1 - 2\log 2)\), with higher-order corrections scaling as \(m k_F^3\) and \(m k_F^5\).

## Context
This work extends the Kohn-Luttinger mechanism, originally discovered in condensed matter physics by Kohn and Luttinger (1965), to dense nuclear and quark matter relevant for neutron star cores. It builds on early nuclear physics studies by Fay (1968), Pines (1971), and Clark (1976) that recognized polarization-induced interactions could alter pairing gaps, and more recent work by Schwenk (2003) which found that spin-orbit interactions suppress the \(^3P_2\) gap at modest densities. The results enable future studies of neutron star cooling and transport properties, as the presence or absence of \(^3P_2\) pairing significantly affects neutrino emission and thermal evolution. The authors note that in quark matter, the induced interaction is too small to produce pairing gaps of phenomenological relevance.
