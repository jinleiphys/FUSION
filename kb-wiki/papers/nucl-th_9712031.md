# $NNK$ and $ΛΛK$ Amplitudes in Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We explore the S-wave and P-wave amplitudes for $NNK$ and $ΛΛK$ interactions using chiral perturbation theory to ${\cal O}(m_s\log m_s)$. In contrast to the large corrections found in P-wave amplitudes in the nonleptonic decay of octet baryons, the $NNK$ amplitudes receive only small corrections to their tree-level values. The uncertainty in the $ΛΛK$ amplitudes is found to be large.

# NNK and ΛΛK Amplitudes in Chiral Perturbation Theory

## Key claim

The S-wave and P-wave amplitudes for NNK interactions receive only small corrections from chiral loop effects at O(m_s log m_s), in contrast to the large corrections found in P-wave amplitudes for nonleptonic octet baryon decays; however, the ΛΛK amplitudes remain poorly constrained with large uncertainties after including the same loop corrections.

## Method

The authors computed the S-wave and P-wave amplitudes for NNK and ΛΛK interactions using chiral perturbation theory to O(m_s log m_s), including one-loop graphs with pseudo-Goldstone bosons. Tree-level amplitudes were constructed from the weak Lagrangian assuming octet dominance, with couplings h_D, h_F, h_C, and h_π determined by fitting to the measured S-wave amplitudes of B → B'π decays, and the non-analytic corrections from loop graphs were evaluated to determine the SU(3) breaking effects.

## Key numbers

- Tree-level S-wave amplitudes for NNK: ppK = 2.0, npK = 0.8, nnK = 2.8 (dimensionless units)
- Loop-level S-wave amplitudes for NNK: ppK = 1.5 ± 0.1, npK = 0.4 ± 0.1, nnK = 1.9 ± 0.4
- Tree-level P-wave amplitudes for NNK (× Λ_χ / 1 GeV): ppK = -2.4, npK = 9.1, nnK = 6.7
- Loop-level P-wave amplitudes for NNK (× Λ_χ / 1 GeV): ppK = -1.7 ± 0.2, npK = 7 ± 1, nnK = 6 ± 1
- Tree-level S-wave amplitude for ΛΛK: 0.6; loop-level range: -0.5 to 1.1
- Tree-level P-wave amplitude for ΛΛK (× Λ_χ / 1 GeV): -5.2; loop-level range: -9 to -0.5
- Tree-level fit values: h_D = -0.58, h_F = 1.40, D = 0.8, F = 0.5

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory from the nonleptonic decays of octet baryons (B → B'π), where P-wave amplitudes were poorly described at leading order as noted by Jenkins (1991), to the NNK and ΛΛK interactions relevant for hypernuclear decay. The results enable improved predictions for the decay of Λ-hypernuclei and doubly-Λ hypernuclei, with the NNK amplitudes providing a test of whether the failure of chiral perturbation theory for B → B'π P-waves is accidental or systematic. The impact on hypernuclear decay rates was explored by Ramos and collaborators (1997), who found modifications at the 10-15% level from the reduced NNK amplitudes.
