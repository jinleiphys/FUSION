# Sub-threshold $ϕ$ and $Ξ^-$ production by high mass resonances with UrQMD

> We present a possible explanation for the deep sub-threshold, $ϕ$ and $Ξ^-$ production yields measured with the HADES experiment in Ar+KCl reactions at $E_{\mathrm{lab}}=1.76$ A GeV and present predictions for Au+Au reactions at $E_{\mathrm{lab}}=1.23$ A GeV. To explain the surprisingly high yields of $ϕ$ and $Ξ^-$ hadrons we propose new decay channels for high mass baryon resonances. These new decay channels are constrained by elementary $\mathrm{p+p}\rightarrow \mathrm{p+p+}ϕ$ cross sections, and $Ξ^-$ production in p+Nb. Based on the fits to the elementary reactions one obtains a satisfactorily description of $ϕ$ and $Ξ^-$ production in deep sub-threshold Ar+KCl reactions as well as the observed nuclear transparency ratio in proton induced $ϕ$ production in cold nuclear matter. The results implicate that no new medium effects are required to describe the rare strange particle production data in low energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that deep sub-threshold production of φ mesons and Ξ⁻ baryons in nuclear collisions can be explained by introducing new decay channels of high-mass baryon resonances (N* → N + φ and N* → Ξ + K + K), with branching fractions constrained by elementary reaction data, thereby eliminating the need for new medium effects to describe the HADES data.

## Method

The authors modified the UrQMD transport model to include new decay channels for five high-mass N* resonances (N*(1990), N*(2080), N*(2190), N*(2220), N*(2250)): N* → N + φ with a branching fraction of 0.2% and N* → Ξ + K + K with a branching fraction of 10%. The φ branching fraction was fitted to ANKE data on p+p → p+p+φ cross sections near threshold, while the Ξ branching fraction was fitted to HADES data on Ξ⁻ production in p+Nb collisions at 3.5 GeV. The model was then applied to simulate Ar+KCl collisions at 1.76 A GeV and Au+Au collisions at 1.23 A GeV, comparing with HADES experimental results.

## Key numbers

- Branching fraction for N* → N + φ decays: 0.2% for all five N* resonances, fitted to ANKE p+p → p+p+φ data
- Branching fraction for N* → Ξ + K + K decays: 10% for all N* states with sufficient mass, fitted to HADES p+Nb data at Elab = 3.5 GeV
- Total φ yield in Ar+KCl collisions at Elab = 1.76 A GeV: 2.7 × 10⁻⁴ from UrQMD, compared to HADES measurement of (2.6 ± 0.8) × 10⁻⁴
- Ξ⁻ production yield in p+Nb at Elab = 3.5 GeV: (1.44 ± 0.05) × 10⁻⁴ from UrQMD, compared to HADES data of (2.0 ± 0.3 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴
- Ξ⁻/Λ ratio in p+Nb at Elab = 3.5 GeV: (0.71 ± 0.03) × 10⁻² from UrQMD, compared to HADES data of (1.2 ± 0.3 ± 0.4) × 10⁻²
- Inelastic φ + p cross section from the model: less than 1 mb, much smaller than the 14-35 mb values inferred from other experiments

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strangeness production in low-energy nuclear collisions, where thermal models had significant problems describing the set of strange particle ratios measured by HADES (Agakishiev 2015, Agakishiev 2009). The authors note that earlier transport model studies successfully described kaon production by modifying medium properties, but the UrQMD model includes a significantly larger number of high-mass resonances than those models. The paper contradicts the interpretation of earlier experiments (LEPS-SPring8, ANKE) that inferred large φ+N absorption cross sections (14-35 mb), arguing instead that the apparent suppression arises from inelastic rescattering of the mother resonance before it decays. The results enable predictions for Au+Au collisions at 1.23 A GeV currently being studied by HADES, and highlight the need for measurements of Ξ production in near-threshold elementary collisions to directly constrain heavy resonance production and decays.
