# Diffusion processes with Non-Markovian Transport Coefficients

> Employing time-dependent projection formalism, a Fokker-Planck equation with non-Markovian transport coefficients is derived for large amplitude collective motion. Properties of transport coefficients for diffusion processes in a potential well and along a potential barrier are discussed, and their connection with the fluctuation-dissipation theorem is investigated. In the case of diffusion in a potential well, the formalism naturally leads to the generalization of the well known Einstein relation by including quantum fluctuations in addition to thermal fluctuations. Furthermore, it is shown that at low temperatures diffusion along potential barrier is significantly modified by quantum fluctuations. Explicit expressions for transport coefficients are presented for the case of the Caldeira-Leggett model.

## Key claim

The paper derives a generalized Fokker-Planck equation with non-Markovian transport coefficients for large amplitude collective motion, showing that quantum fluctuations significantly modify diffusion along a potential barrier at low temperatures, but only within the weak-coupling limit where memory times are much shorter than the relaxation time of the collective motion.

## Method

Using a time-dependent projection formalism and a Galilei transformation to separate mean collective motion from fluctuations, the authors derive a Fokker-Planck equation for the Wigner distribution function of the collective coordinate. They treat the coupling between collective and microscopic degrees of freedom to second order in perturbation theory, defining response and correlation functions whose moments serve as non-Markovian transport coefficients. The Caldeira-Leggett model with Ohmic dissipation and a cut-off function is used to obtain explicit expressions for these transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- Memory time of the correlation function is approximately τ_c^(+) ≈ (0.5–3) × 10^(-22) seconds for cut-off frequencies ℏω_c > 5 MeV, nearly independent of temperature
- For diffusion in a potential well, the fluctuation-dissipation relation yields effective temperature T* = (1/2)ℏΩ · coth((1/2)βℏΩ), which reduces to T for T >> ℏΩ and to (1/2)ℏΩ for T << ℏΩ
- The curvature of the fission barrier is of order 1 MeV, motivating the need to include quantum fluctuations at low temperatures

## Context

This work extends the classical Kramers (1940) Fokker-Planck approach to nuclear fission and chemical reactions by incorporating quantum statistical effects through non-Markovian transport coefficients. It generalizes the quasi-linear response theory of Takigawa (1981) to include quantum fluctuations of the collective motion, and provides a first step toward describing fusion of very heavy nuclei for superheavy element synthesis, where the fission barrier curvature (~1 MeV) requires treatment of quantum fluctuations at low temperatures. The formalism enables future studies of nuclear collective excitation stability (Ayik et al., 1998) and fusion-fission dynamics.
