# Are there eta-Helium bound states?

> Using multiple scattering theory the scattering lengths of $η$ mesons on helium nuclei are calculated and checked against final state $η$ interactions from the $pd \rightarrow η^3$He and $dd \rightarrow η^4$He reactions. The existence of an $η^4$He quasibound state is indicated.

## Key claim

Using multiple scattering theory, the authors calculate that the $\eta^4$He system likely has a quasibound state near threshold, while the $\eta^3$He system does not, contradicting earlier suggestions that a rapid slope in $pd \rightarrow \eta^3$He data indicated a quasibound state in the $A=3$ system.

## Method

The authors compute $\eta$-helium scattering lengths using a multiple scattering expansion that sums the series generated by an optical potential, then apply two corrections: replacing the nucleon number $A$ with $A-1$ to prevent multiple collisions on the same nucleon, and introducing an off-shell $\eta$-nucleon scattering matrix via an energy extrapolation using separation and recoil energies. These corrected scattering lengths are then used to model final state interactions in $pd \rightarrow \eta^3$He and $dd \rightarrow \eta^4$He reactions, employing a separable potential wave function with a Gaussian production amplitude.

## Key numbers

- For $\eta^4$He with the best estimate $a_{A-1}(\eta A,{\rm off})$, the condition $(1 - ip a)=0$ yields a pole in the upper complex momentum half-plane, indicating a quasibound state for all Re $a(\eta N)$ in the range 0.3 to 0.6 fm
- The $\eta N$ scattering lengths used are: 0.476+i0.279 fm (potential I), 0.579+i0.399 fm (II), 0.430+i0.394 fm (III), and 0.28+i0.19 fm (IV)
- For $^3$He [$^4$He] the separation energy is 7 [21] MeV and the recoil energy is 12 [12] MeV
- The spin-averaged experimental value $|f({\rm expt})|^2 = 0.63 \pm 0.02$ $\mu$b/sr at $p_\eta = 0.246$ fm$^{-1}$ for $pd \rightarrow \eta^3$He
- For potential IV with $\beta=1/R_{\rm RMS}$ and $\lambda=0.88$, the $\chi^2$/data point = 0.35 for $^3$He, compared to $\chi^2$/dp = 6.63 using only the simple final state interaction factor $|F_1|^2$

## Context

This work extends the prediction of $\eta$-nuclear quasibound states by Haider and Liu (1986) and detailed calculations by Li, Cheung, and Kuo (1987), and directly addresses the suggestion by Wilkin (1993) that the rapid slope in $pd \rightarrow \eta^3$He data signals a quasibound state in the $A=3$ system. The authors find the opposite interpretation: the $\eta^3$He scattering length has a positive real part (virtual state), while $\eta^4$He has a negative real part indicating a quasibound state, contradicting the interpretation of Frascaria et al. (1994) who saw no structure in $dd \rightarrow \eta^4$He. The results enable future measurements at proton storage rings like Celsius to constrain the $\eta N$ scattering length by comparing with the $^3$He data, complementing elementary photo- or electroproduction.
