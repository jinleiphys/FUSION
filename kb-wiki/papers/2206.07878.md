# Inverse Primakoff Scattering for Axionlike Particle Coupling

> Axionlike particles (ALPs) can be produced in the Sun, and are considered viable candidates for the cosmological dark matter (DM). It can decay into two photons or interact with matter. We identify new inelastic channels of inverse Primakoff processes due to atomic excitation and ionization. Their cross sections are derived by incorporating full electromagnetic fields of atomic charge and current densities, and computed by well-benchmarked atomic many-body methods. Complementing data from the underground XENONnT and surface TEXONO experiments are analyzed. Event rates and sensitivity reaches are evaluated with respect to solar- and DM-ALPs. New parameter space in ALP couplings with the photons versus ALP masses in (1~eV$-$10~keV) not previously accessible to laboratory experiments are probed and excluded with solar-ALPs. However, at regions where DM-ALPs have already decayed, there would be no ALP-flux and hence no interactions at the detectors in direct search experiments. No physics constraints can be derived. Future projects would be able to evade the stability bound and open new observable windows in (100~eV$-$1~MeV) for DM-ALPs.

## Key claim

The paper identifies new inelastic inverse Primakoff scattering channels (atomic excitation and ionization) for axionlike particles (ALPs) and derives their cross sections using full atomic many-body calculations, claiming that new parameter space in ALP-photon coupling versus ALP mass (1 eV–10 keV) can be probed and excluded with solar-ALPs, but that for dark matter ALPs, regions where ALPs have already decayed yield no observable flux and thus no physics constraints can be derived.

## Method

The authors derived double differential cross sections for three inverse Primakoff channels (elastic scattering, atomic excitation, and atomic ionization) by incorporating the full electromagnetic fields of atomic charge and current densities, using well-benchmarked atomic many-body methods with the frozen core approximation. They regulated the Coulomb pole via atomic screening and the transverse photon pole via the complex refractive index of the detector material, then computed event rates for solar-ALPs and dark matter ALPs in liquid xenon targets, analyzing data from the underground XENONnT and surface TEXONO experiments.

## Key numbers

- For solar-ALPs at g_agg = 10^{-10} GeV^{-1} and m_a = 1 meV, the elastic IP event rate is 6.6 × 10^{-2} ton^{-1} year^{-1}, the excitation IP rate is 2.9 × 10^{-4} ton^{-1} year^{-1}, and the ionization IP rate is 7.8 × 10^{-3} ton^{-1} year^{-1}
- For solar-ALPs at m_a = 5 keV, the ionization IP rate (5.1 × 10^{-1} ton^{-1} year^{-1}) dominates over the elastic IP rate (2.4 × 10^{-2} ton^{-1} year^{-1})
- For DM-ALPs at m_a = 1 keV, the two-photon decay rate is 250 ton^{-1} year^{-1} and the ionization IP rate is 500 ton^{-1} year^{-1}
- The equivalent photon approximation for non-relativistic DM-ALPs gives a total ionization cross section approximately proportional to g_agg^2 m_a^3 / (32 pi n_A v_a), where n_A is the atomic number density
- The solar-ALP flux integration uses the BS05-AGSOP solar model with the Sun-Earth distance d_sun = 1.5 × 10^{13} cm

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of inverse Primakoff scattering previously studied by Barshay (1981), Avignone (1988), Buchmuller (1989), and others, correcting earlier over-predictions of elastic cross sections that used universal screening lengths of order Angstroms, as first pointed out by Abe (2021). The atomic many-body methods build on the authors' earlier work for low-energy neutrino interactions (Chen 2013, 2014) and dark matter interactions (Pandey 2018, Liu 2021). The results enable laboratory experiments to probe ALP-photon coupling parameter space in the 1 eV–10 keV mass range for solar-ALPs, and future projects could open observable windows in the 100 eV–1 MeV range for dark matter ALPs, complementing helioscope experiments like CAST which are strongest for masses below 10^{-2} eV.
