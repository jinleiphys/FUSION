# Far-off-equilibrium expansion trajectories in the QCD phase diagram

> We consider the hydrodynamic evolution of a quark-gluon gas with non-zero quark masses and net baryon number in its phase diagram. For far-off-equilibrium initial conditions the expansion trajectories appear to violate simple rules based on the second law of thermodynamics that were previously established for ideal or weakly dissipative fluids. For Bjorken flow we present a detailed analysis within kinetic theory that provides a full microscopic understanding of these macroscopic phenomena and establishes their thermodynamic consistency. We point out that, for certain far-off-equilibrium initial conditions, the well-known phenomenon of "viscous heating" turns into "viscous cooling" where, driven by dissipative effects, the temperature decreases faster than in adiabatic expansion.

## Key claim

For far-off-equilibrium initial conditions with negative shear stress, the well-known phenomenon of "viscous heating" can reverse into "viscous cooling," where dissipative effects cause the temperature to decrease faster than in adiabatic expansion; this apparent violation of thermodynamic intuition is resolved by recognizing that only the total entropy (including non-equilibrium corrections) must obey the second law, while the equilibrium entropy can temporarily decrease.

## Method

The authors studied 1-dimensional Bjorken expansion of a quark-gluon gas with non-zero quark masses and net baryon number using both second-order relativistic hydrodynamics and the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA). They solved the coupled evolution equations for energy density, net quark density, and shear and bulk viscous stresses, converting these into trajectories in the temperature-chemical potential plane via Landau matching conditions. For the kinetic theory analysis, they used Romatschke-Strickland type initial momentum distributions with a common anisotropy parameter and solved the RTA Boltzmann equation iteratively to obtain the time evolution of temperature, chemical potential, and shear stress.

## Key numbers

- Initial Milne time for all calculations: $\tau_0 = 0.1$ fm/$c$
- Quark degeneracy factor: $g_q = 12$ (with $N_c=3$ colors and $N_f=2$ flavors); gluon degeneracy factor: $g_g = 16$
- For the non-conformal case, quark mass set to $m_q = m_{\bar q} = 1$ GeV (gluons massless)
- Relaxation time parametrized as $T\tau_R = 5C$ with $C = 10/4\pi$ for the illustrative dissipative calculations
- Two sets of initial conditions shown: $(T_0, \mu_0) = (0.8, 0.5)$ GeV and $(0.7, 0.83)$ GeV, with initial normalized shear stress values $(\pi/4p)_0 = 0.2$ and $(\pi/4p)_0 = -0.3$

## Context

This work extends the numerical observations of Dore et al. (2020, 2022), who first found that dissipative expansion trajectories in the QCD phase diagram could show decreasing equilibrium entropy, and provides a full microscopic explanation using kinetic theory. The study builds on decades of work on isentropic expansion trajectories in ideal hydrodynamics (Heinz 1987, Lee 1992, Cho 1993, Hung 1997) and on the development of second-order viscous hydrodynamics. The results enable a more complete understanding of how far-off-equilibrium initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions at the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC can produce expansion trajectories that differ qualitatively from ideal fluid expectations, with implications for the interpretation of critical point search observables.
