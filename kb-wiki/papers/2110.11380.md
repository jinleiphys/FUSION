# Chiral anomalous processes in magnetospheres of pulsars and black holes

> We propose that chirally asymmetric plasma can be produced in the gap regions of the magnetospheres of pulsars and black holes. We show that, in the case of supermassive black holes situated in active galactic nuclei, the chiral charge density and the chiral chemical potential are very small and unlikely to have any observable effects. In contrast, the chiral asymmetry produced in the magnetospheres of magnetars can be substantial. It can trigger the chiral plasma instability that, in turn, can lead to observable phenomena in magnetars. In particular, the instability should trigger circularly polarized electromagnetic radiation in a wide window of frequencies, spanning from radio to near-infrared. As such, the produced chiral charge has the potential to affect some features of fast radio bursts.

## Key claim

Chirally asymmetric plasma can be produced in the gap regions of magnetar magnetospheres via the chiral anomaly, but only if the magnetic field is sufficiently strong; for supermassive black holes in active galactic nuclei, the chiral charge density and chiral chemical potential are negligible and unlikely to produce any observable effects.

## Method

The authors model the production of chiral charge in intermittent gap regions of pulsar and black hole magnetospheres using the anomalous continuity equation for chiral charge, including a chirality-flipping rate due to the nonzero electron mass. They estimate the parallel electric field in polar gaps from standard pulsar gap models (Ruderman 1975), then compute the stationary chiral charge density and chiral chemical potential by balancing anomalous production against chirality flipping. For magnetars, they further analyze the resulting chiral plasma instability by solving the coupled Maxwell equations with the chiral magnetic effect current.

## Key numbers

* For a magnetar with surface magnetic field \(B = 10^{15}~\text{G}\), the chiral chemical potential reaches \(\mu_5 \simeq 2.4 \times 10^{-3} m_e\) (about \(1.2 \times 10^{-3}~\text{MeV}\)).
* For a magnetar with \(B = 10^{14}~\text{G}\), the chiral chemical potential is \(\mu_5 \simeq 9.0 \times 10^{-5} m_e\).
* For a supermassive black hole with \(B \simeq 10^2~\text{G}\), the chiral chemical potential is \(\mu_5 \simeq 3.8 \times 10^{-24}~\text{MeV}\) (assuming \(T = 1~\text{MeV}\)).
* The polar gap size for a magnetar with \(B = 10^{14}~\text{G}\) is \(h \simeq 3.6~\text{m}\).
* The parallel electric field in the gap for \(B = 10^{14}~\text{G}\) is \(E_\parallel \simeq 2.7 \times 10^{-8} E_c\), where \(E_c \equiv m_e^2/e \approx 1.3 \times 10^{18}~\text{V/m}\).
* The chiral flip rate is \(\Gamma_m \simeq \alpha^2 m_e^2 / T\), giving a characteristic time \(t^\star \simeq \Gamma_m^{-1} \approx 10^{-17}~\text{s}\) at \(T \simeq 1~\text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral anomalous phenomena from heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2007, 2013), the early Universe (Joyce 1997, Boyarsky 2011), and Dirac/Weyl semimetals (Armitage 2017, Gorbar 2021) into astrophysical magnetospheres. It contradicts the possibility of observable chiral effects in black hole magnetospheres (Beskin 2010) while supporting the scenario that magnetar magnetospheres can host chiral plasma instability, which may produce circularly polarized electromagnetic radiation from radio to near-infrared frequencies and potentially affect features of fast radio bursts, as observed from magnetar SGR 1935+2154 by CHIME/FRB (2020) and STARE2 (2020).
