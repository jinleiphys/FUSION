# Hadronic structure of the photon at small x in holographic QCD

> We present our analysis on the photon structure functions at small Bjorken variable x in the framework of the holographic QCD. In the kinematic region, a photon can fluctuate into vector mesons and behaves like a hadron rather than a pointlike particle. Assuming the Pomeron exchange dominance, the dominant hadronic contribution to the structure functions is computed by convoluting the probe and target photon density distributions obtained from the wave functions of the U(1) vector field in the five-dimensional AdS space and the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan Pomeron exchange kernel. Our calculations are in agreement with both the experimental data from OPAL collaboration at LEP and those calculated from the parton distribution functions of the photon proposed by Glück, Reya, and Schienbein. The predictions presented here will be tested at future linear colliders, such as the planned International Linear Collider.

## Key claim

The holographic QCD model, using the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel and 5D U(1) vector field wave functions, can reproduce the photon structure functions at small Bjorken x (x ≤ 0.025) in agreement with OPAL data, but only after refitting the overall normalization factor g₀² separately for the photon case because the target photon is a non-normalizable mode unlike the nucleon case.

## Method

The authors compute the quasi-real photon structure functions F₂^γ and F_L^γ by convoluting the BPST Pomeron exchange kernel with density distributions of the probe and target photons derived from the wave functions of the massless 5D U(1) vector field in AdS space. They use both the conformal and modified (confinement-corrected) versions of the BPST kernel, with parameters for the nucleon structure functions fixed in prior work (Watanabe 2013), and fit the overall normalization g₀² to 9 OPAL data points with x ≤ 0.025. For the modified kernel, they also explore a larger confinement parameter z₀ = 6 GeV⁻¹ motivated by the number of active quark flavors (f = 6 for the photon versus f = 4 for the nucleon).

## Key numbers

- The overall normalization factor g₀² is fitted to 17.51 for the conformal kernel and 49.01 for the modified kernel with z₀ = 4.25 GeV⁻¹.
- For the modified kernel with z₀ = 6 GeV⁻¹, the refitted normalization is g₀² = 21.60.
- The target photon is treated as quasi-real with P² = 0.01 GeV².
- The analysis covers the kinematic region x ≤ 10⁻² and Q² ≤ 10 GeV².
- The confinement parameter z₀ takes values 4.25 GeV⁻¹ (from nucleon studies) and 6 GeV⁻¹ (for the photon case with f = 6).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach to photon structure functions, following prior applications of the BPST Pomeron kernel to nucleon structure functions by Brower, Polchinski, Strassler, and Tan (2006) and subsequent phenomenological studies by Watanabe (2012, 2013). The results are consistent with the GRS parton distribution functions of the photon proposed by Glück, Reya, and Schienbein (1999), and the agreement with the GRS set may indicate that vector meson dominance is realized in the holographic setup. The predictions for very low x (below 10⁻³) will be testable at future linear colliders such as the International Linear Collider.
