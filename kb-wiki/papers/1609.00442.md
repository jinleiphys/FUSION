# The role of $a_1(1260)$ in $蟺^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p$ and $蟺^- p \to 蟺^- 蟻^0 p$ reactions near threshold

> We report on a theoretical study of the $蟺^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p$ and $蟺^- p \to 蟺^- 蟻^0 p$ reactions near threshold within an effective Lagrangian approach. The production process is described by $t$-channel $蟻^0$ meson exchange. For the $蟺^- p \to 蟺^- 蟻^0 p$ reaction, the final $蟺^- 蟻^0$ results from the decay of the $a_1(1260)$ resonance which is assumed as a dynamically generated state from the $K^* \bar K$ and $蟻蟺$ coupled channel interactions. We calculate the total cross section of the $蟺^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p$ reaction. It is shown that, with the coupling constant of the $a_1(1260)$ to $蟻蟺$ channel obtained from the chiral unitary theory and a cut off parameter $螞_蟻\sim 1.5$ GeV in the form factors, the experimental measurement can be reproduced. Furthermore, the total and differential cross sections of $蟺^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p \to 蟺^- 蟻^0 p$ reaction are evaluated, and it is expected that our model calculations can be tested by future experiments. These reactions are important for the study of the $a_1(1260)$ resonance and would provide further clue for the nature of $a_1(1260)$ state.

## Key claim

The $a_1(1260)$ resonance, treated as a dynamically generated state from $K^* \bar K$ and $\rho \pi$ coupled channel interactions, can reproduce the measured total cross section of $\pi^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p$ via $t$-channel $\rho^0$ exchange, but only when the cutoff parameter $\Lambda_\rho$ is set to $1.5 \pm 0.1$ GeV and the coupling constant $g_{a_1\rho\pi}$ is taken from chiral unitary theory as $(-3795, 2330)$ MeV.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach with $t$-channel $\rho^0$ meson exchange to compute the invariant scattering amplitudes for both reactions. For $\pi^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p$, the amplitude included the $\rho NN$ vertex with coupling $g_{\rho NN}=3.36$ and tensor coupling $\kappa_\rho=6.1$, plus the $a_1\rho\pi$ vertex with coupling $g_{a_1\rho\pi}$ from chiral unitary theory. For $\pi^- p \to \pi^- \rho^0 p$, the $a_1(1260)$ propagator was included with a Breit-Wigner form, and the total and differential cross sections were obtained by integrating over the $\pi^- \rho^0$ invariant mass and angular phase space.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constant $g_{a_1\rho\pi} = (-3795, 2330)$ MeV, taken from Roca (2005) chiral unitary theory.
- Cutoff parameter $\Lambda_\rho = 1.5 \pm 0.1$ GeV in the $\rho NN$ form factor reproduces experimental data.
- Mass of $a_1(1260)$ taken as $m_{a_1} = 1230$ MeV; width $\Gamma_{a_1}$ varied as 250, 425, and 600 MeV for comparison.
- Mass of $\rho$ meson taken as $m_\rho = 775.26$ MeV; proton mass $m_p = 938.27$ MeV; pion mass $m_{\pi^-} = 139.57$ MeV.
- Experimental data points for $\pi^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p$ total cross section exist at beam momenta $p_{\rm lab} = 3.2$, 4.2 GeV (Chung 1968) and 3.89 GeV (Wohlmut 1970).
- Predicted total cross section for $\pi^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p \to \pi^- \rho^0 p$ is shown for beam momenta from threshold (2.0 GeV) to 5.0 GeV.
- Differential cross section $d\sigma/dM_{\rho\pi}$ is predicted at $p_{\rm lab} = 4$ GeV for the three width values.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach of Roca (2005), which treats $a_1(1260)$ as a dynamically generated resonance from $K^* \bar K$ and $\rho \pi$ coupled channels, into hadronic production reactions. It provides a test of that picture by comparing with existing data from Chung (1968) and Wohlmut (1970) for $\pi^- p \to a^-_1(1260) p$, and makes predictions for the $\pi^- p \to \pi^- \rho^0 p$ reaction that could be tested by future experiments, potentially clarifying the nature of the $a_1(1260)$ and related structures such as the $a_1(1420)$ observed by COMPASS (Adolph 2015).
