# Quark matter and the astrophysics of neutron stars

> Some of the means through which the possible presence of nearly deconfined quarks in neutron stars can be detected by astrophysical observations of neutron stars from their birth to old age are highlighted.

# Quark matter and the astrophysics of neutron stars

## Key claim
Astrophysical observations of neutron stars from birth to old age (via neutrino, photon, and gravitational-wave signals) can potentially reveal the presence of nearly deconfined quarks, but definitive conclusions are prevented by the multitude of high-density phases, cooling mechanisms, superfluid effects, and unknown envelope composition.

## Method
The paper reviews how the appearance of quarks in neutron star cores affects observable signals across the stellar lifetime: neutrino emission during proto-neutron star evolution, thermal photon emission during cooling, mass measurements from binary pulsar timing, and gravitational-wave signatures from binary mergers. For each signal type, the author compares predictions for matter containing quarks versus matter with only nucleons or other exotic components such as hyperons or Bose condensates.

## Key numbers
- Gravitational binding energy released in core-collapse supernovae: of order 300 bethes (1 bethe = 10^51 erg)
- Neutrino mean free path in dense matter: lambda ≈ 10 cm (with cross section sigma ≈ 10^-40 cm^2 and density n ≈ 2 to 3 n_s, where n_s ≈ 0.16 fm^-3)
- Proto-neutron star radius: exceeds 20 km
- Stars with baryonic mass M_B ~ 1.7 solar masses are metastable when quarks appear; stars with M_B ≤ 1.1 solar masses do not contain quarks (for the equation of state used)
- PSR J0751+1807 estimated mass: 2.1 ± 0.2 solar masses (1-sigma error)
- Ter 5 I and J: one pulsar has mass larger than 1.68 solar masses at 95% confidence
- Simple mean mass of neutron stars with white dwarf companions exceeds those with neutron star companions by 0.25 solar masses
- For Tolman VII solution: central density rho_c,T_VII = 1.5 × 10^16 (M_sun/M)^2 g cm^-3
- A measured mass of 2.2 solar masses would imply maximum density < 3.1 × 10^15 g cm^-3 (about 8 n_s) under causal constraints, or about 4.2 n_s under the c/sqrt(3) sound-speed limit
- Binary pulsar PSR B1913+16 merger timescale: about 250 million years
- Binary pulsar PSR J0737-3039 merger timescale: about 85 million years

## Context
This review extends the original suggestion by Collins and Perry (1975) that neutron star cores may contain deconfined quarks due to asymptotic freedom in QCD. It synthesizes results from Pons (2001) on proto-neutron star evolution with quarks, Page (2004) and Lattimer and Prakash (2004) on cooling simulations, and Lattimer and Prakash (2005, 2006) on mass measurements and equation-of-state constraints. The work enables future gravitational-wave observations from LIGO, VIRGO, GEO600, and TAMA to distinguish between normal neutron stars and strange quark stars through merger signatures, and motivates continued mass measurements to eliminate families of equations of state that predict substantial softening at high densities.
