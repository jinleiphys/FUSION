# Coherent Neutron Scattering in Polycrystalline Deuterium and its Implications for Ultracold Neutron Production

> This paper presents a calculation of the neutron cross-sections in solid materials (used in practical neutron sources) with a large coherent scattering contribution. In particular, the dynamic structure function S(Q, $ω$) of polycrystalline ortho-D$_2$ is evaluated using a Monte-Carlo calculation that performs an average over scattering angles relative to crystal axes in random orientations. This method uses an analytical dispersion function with force constants derived from neutron scattering data of single crystal D$_2$ in the framework of an axially symmetric force tensor. The resulting two dimensional map of S(Q, $ω$) captures details of the phonon branches as well as the molecular rotations, that can be compared directly to data from inelastic neutron scattering on polycrystalline D$_2$. This high resolution information is used to calculate the absolute cross-sections of production and upscattering loss of ultracold neutron (UCN). The resulting scattering cross-sections are significantly different, especially for UCN upscattering, from the previous predictions using the approach centered on the incoherent approximation.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first full treatment of coherent neutron scattering in polycrystalline ortho-D₂, showing that the ultracold neutron (UCN) upscattering cross-sections are significantly different from previous predictions that used the incoherent approximation, particularly for neutrons with energies below 350 neV where the incoherent approximation breaks down.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte-Carlo algorithm that samples the dispersion curve of each single crystallite oriented at random angles, using an axially symmetric force tensor model with force constants derived from single crystal D₂ neutron scattering data by Nielsen (1973). For each magnitude of momentum transfer, up to 10⁵ different angles are sampled isotropically over 4π to construct a two-dimensional map of the dynamic structure function S(Q, ω) for both neutron downscattering and upscattering, including 1-phonon and 2-phonon contributions from coherent and incoherent processes, as well as rotational transitions.

## Key numbers

- Coherent scattering length for deuterium: σ_D^{coh} = 5.592 b, σ_D^{inc} = 2.050 b (compared to hydrogen where σ_H^{coh} = 1.7583 b, σ_H^{inc} = 80.27 b)
- Lattice constant for HCP D₂: d = 3.607 Å; deuteron separation in molecule: a = 0.71 Å
- Debye temperature for solid D₂: ω_D ≈ 110 K
- Rotational transition energy J=0→1 in ortho-D₂: 7.1 meV
- Thermal wavelength of D₂: 4.6 Å at 5 K and 3.0 Å at 18 K
- Scattering lengths: a_coh = 6.67 fm, a_inc = 4.04 fm for deuteron
- For Q < 2.5 Å⁻¹ (relevant to UCN production), the Debye-Waller factor agreement with cubic approximation is within 5%

## Context

This work extends the treatment of neutron scattering in solid deuterium beyond the incoherent approximation used in standard codes such as MCNP and NJOY (MacFarlane, 1994; Granada, 2009; Liu, 2000; Atchison, 2007), which assumed negligible interference between wavefunctions scattered from different lattice sites. The full treatment enables more accurate predictions of UCN production and upscattering loss in superthermal UCN sources, and provides a methodology that can be applied to other materials with large coherent scattering contributions, such as solid oxygen where spin form factors are less well-known.
