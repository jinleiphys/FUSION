# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Kernel for the Lightest Pseudoscalar Mesons

> Starting from a phenomenologically successful, numerical solution of the Dyson-Schwinger equation that governs the quark propagator, we reconstruct in detail the interaction kernel that has to enter the instantaneous approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation to allow us to describe the lightest pseudoscalar mesons as quark-antiquark bound states exhibiting the (almost) masslessness necessary for them to be interpretable as the (pseudo) Goldstone bosons related to the spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking of quantum chromodynamics.

# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter Kernel for the Lightest Pseudoscalar Mesons

## Key claim
The authors reconstruct the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter interaction kernel that yields massless pseudoscalar meson solutions (consistent with Goldstone boson interpretation) from a numerical quark mass function obtained via rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger equation solutions, but only under the condition that the effective quark mass is sufficiently small; for larger quark masses the resulting potential does not produce massless bound states.

## Method
The authors start from a parametrized quark mass function M(k^2) in the chiral limit, taken from a published rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger solution (Maris and Tandy, 1997). They insert this mass function into the axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity relation linking the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude to the quark propagator, integrate over the fourth Euclidean momentum component to obtain the Salpeter amplitude, and then perform Fourier-Bessel transformations to configuration space. The instantaneous potential V(r) is extracted by dividing the Fourier-Bessel transform of the kinetic term E(p) φ₂(p) by the configuration-space Salpeter component φ(r), following from the spinless Salpeter equation in the massless pseudoscalar limit.

## Key numbers
- Quark mass function at zero momentum: M(0) = 0.778572 GeV (from the fit parameters a = 0.0705896 GeV and c = 0.707983 GeV)
- For the massless quark case (m = 0), the potential at the origin: V(0) = -0.926813 GeV (for the simplified parametrization with b = 1.69334 GeV, γ = 6.49292)
- For the case m = b = 1.69334 GeV, the potential at the origin: V(0) = -1.96928 GeV
- Maximum error of the Salpeter amplitude parametrization relative to numerical integration: less than 0.0036 GeV^{-3/2}
- The exponent γ in the simplified Salpeter amplitude parametrization: γ = 6.49292 (approximately 13/2)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous inversion program (Lucha and Schöberl, 2013, 2015, 2016) that aims to reconstruct instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter kernels from known bound-state solutions, moving from analytic models to input derived from a realistic Dyson-Schwinger equation solution. It relies on the rainbow-ladder truncation results of Maris and Tandy (1997) for the quark propagator. The extracted potential shows confinement-betraying rise to infinity for small quark masses but approaches finite nonpositive values for larger masses, consistent with the authors' earlier findings (Lucha and Schöberl, 2015). This enables future extension to Minkowski-space analysis and timelike quark momenta.
