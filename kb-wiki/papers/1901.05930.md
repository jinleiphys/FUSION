# Superheavy objects composed of nuclear and dark matter

> We consider a model of $O$He atomic dark matter formed by Coulomb binding of the hypothetical stable double-charged massive $O^{--}$ particles with nuclei of primordial helium. Such a dark matter can be captured by ordinary matter forming superheavy nuclei. We propose a possibility of self-bound by nuclear and electromagnetic interaction $O$-nuclearites as well as more massive gravitating ones and discuss effect of accumulation of $O$He atoms in stars and their effect in stellar evolution.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that hypothetical $O$He atomic dark matter (formed by a stable double-charged $O^{--}$ particle Coulomb-bound to a helium nucleus) can be captured by ordinary nuclei to form superheavy nuclei, and can also form self-bound $O$-nuclearites via nuclear and electromagnetic interactions, provided the number of $O$-particles satisfies $N_O \geq A/4$ for a nucleus of atomic number $A$.

## Method

The authors model the energy of an $O$-nucleus using a volume term ($-16$ MeV per nucleon), an electromagnetic term from the proton-$O$ interaction, and a kinetic term for the $O$-fermions (negligible for $m_O \sim$ TeV). They assume the most energetically favorable distribution has $O$-particles following the proton density, with $n_O = n_p/2$ inside the nucleus. For gravitating $O$-nuclearites, they balance internal nucleon pressure against gravitational pressure to estimate size and stability, and they compute the capture rate of $O$He onto neutron stars and red giants using a scattering formalism that accounts for baryon degeneracy.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear saturation density: $n_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$; the condition for $O$-nuclearite stability requires nucleon density $n < (2-2.5) n_0$.
- For an individual gravitating $O$-nuclearite with $m_O \simeq 10^3 m_\mathrm{N}$, mass $M \sim 3 \cdot 10^{-8} M_\odot$ and radius $R \sim 30$ m.
- Maximum number of $O$-particles accumulated in a neutron star of age $10^{10}$ yr: $N_O^{\rm max} \sim 10^{39}$, corresponding to a maximum $O$-nuclearite mass $M_{O{\rm -nuclearite}}^{\rm max} \sim 10^{18}$ g.
- For a red giant of age $10^8$ yr: $N_O^{\rm max} \sim 10^{46}$, $M_{O{\rm -nuclearite}}^{\rm max} \sim 10^{25}$ g.
- To accumulate a solar-mass $O$-nuclearite in an old neutron star, the $O$He flux must be $\sim 10^7$ times enhanced over the baseline estimate.

## Context

This work extends the $O$He dark matter hypothesis of Khlopov (2005) and the earlier literature on superheavy nuclei and nuclearites bound by pion condensate (Migdal 1971, 1977, 1978, 1990), sigma condensate (Lee 1974), or strange quarks (Bodmer 1971, Witten 1984, DeRujula 1984, Alcock 1986). It enables future studies of the observable astrophysical signatures of $O$He accumulation in stars, including possible effects on stellar evolution and the formation of gravitating $O$-nuclearites that could precede black hole formation in neutron stars.
