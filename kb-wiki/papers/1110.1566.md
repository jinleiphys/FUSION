# Curved and diffuse interface effects on the nuclear surface tension

> We redefine the surface tension coefficient for a nuclear Fermi-liquid drop with a finite diffuse layer. Following Gibbs-Tolman concept, we introduce the equimolar radius R_e of sharp surface droplet at which the surface tension is applied and the radius of tension surface R_s which provides the minimum of the surface tension coefficient σ. This procedure allows us to derive both the surface tension and the corresponding curvature correction (Tolman length) correctly for the curved and diffuse interface. We point out that the curvature correction depends significantly on the finite diffuse interface. This fact is missed in traditional nuclear considerations of curvature correction to the surface tension. We show that Tolman's length ξis negative for nuclear Fermi-liquid drop. The value of the Tolman length is only slightly sensitive to the Skyrme force parametrization and equals ξ=-0.36 fm.

## Key claim

The curvature correction to the nuclear surface tension (Tolman length) depends significantly on the finite diffuse interface of the nuclear surface, a fact missed in traditional nuclear considerations. The Tolman length is negative for a nuclear Fermi-liquid drop and equals ξ = -0.36 fm, with only slight sensitivity to the Skyrme force parametrization.

## Method

The authors apply the Gibbs-Tolman concept to a nuclear Fermi-liquid drop with a finite diffuse layer, introducing both an equimolar radius R_e (the physical size of a sharp-surface droplet) and a radius of tension R_s (where the surface tension coefficient σ is minimized). Using the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation (ETFA) with effective Skyrme-like forces and a direct variational method, they calculate the free energy and chemical potential for symmetric (N=Z) uncharged droplets. The surface tension is then evaluated as a function of the dividing surface radius, and the Tolman length is extracted from the asymptotic difference between R_e and R_s in the limit of large particle number.

## Key numbers

- Tolman length ξ = -0.36 fm for the SkM Skyrme force parametrization
- Planar surface tension σ_∞ = 0.92 MeV/fm² for SkM force
- Tolman length ξ = -0.26 fm for SIII force; ξ = -0.37 fm for SLy230b force; ξ = -0.36 fm for T6 force
- Planar surface tension σ_∞ = 0.93 MeV/fm² (SIII), 1.01 MeV/fm² (SLy230b), 1.02 MeV/fm² (T6)
- Volume particle density for A=208 using SkM force: ϱ_V = 0.171 fm⁻³, while bulk density ρ_+,0 = 0.170 fm⁻³
- Ratio of curvature correction to surface coefficient in droplet model: a₃/a₂ ≈ 0.63 for SkM interaction

## Context

This work extends the Gibbs-Tolman thermodynamic approach (Gibbs, Tolman 1949) to nuclear Fermi-liquid drops, correcting earlier phenomenological treatments such as Myers and Swiatecki (1969) and the droplet model expansions that neglected the role of the finite diffuse layer. The results enable improved calculations of nuclear binding energies and fission barriers, particularly for light nuclei, and suggest that the negative Tolman length could hinder the yield of light fragments in nuclear multifragmentation processes in heavy ion collisions. The approach also provides a thermodynamic alternative to the "geometrical" definition of nuclear size (Myers 1973) that does not rely on details of the particle density profile.
