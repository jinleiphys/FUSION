# Effect of strong magnetic field on competing order parameters in two-flavor dense quark matter

> We study the effect of strong magnetic field on competing chiral and diquark order parameters in a regime of moderately dense quark matter. The inter-dependence of the chiral and diquark condensates through nonperturbative quark mass and strong coupling effects is analyzed in a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. In the weak magnetic field limit, our results agree qualitatively with earlier zero-field studies in the literature that find a critical coupling ratio $G_D/G_S\sim 1.1$ below which chiral or superconducting order parameters appear almost exclusively. Above the critical ratio, there exists a significant mixed broken phase region where both gaps are non-zero. However, a strong magnetic field $B\gtrsim 10^{18}$ G disrupts this mixed broken phase region and changes a smooth crossover found in the weak-field case to a first-order transition for both gaps at almost the same critical density. Our results suggest that in the two-flavor approximation to moderately dense quark matter, strong magnetic field enhances the possibility of a mixed phase at high density, with implications for the structure, energetics and vibrational spectrum of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper finds that a strong magnetic field \(B \gtrsim 10^{18}\) G disrupts the mixed broken phase region (where both chiral and diquark condensates coexist) that exists for weak or zero magnetic field when the diquark-to-scalar coupling ratio exceeds a critical value \(\rho_c \approx 1.09\). Specifically, a strong magnetic field changes the smooth crossover found in the weak-field case into a first-order transition for both gaps at almost the same critical density, contrary to the abstract's implication that the mixed phase is simply "disrupted."

## Method

The authors study the competition between chiral and diquark condensates in a two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model at zero temperature, including a constant external magnetic field via rotated \(\mathrm{U}(1)\) charges. They derive the thermodynamic potential in the mean-field approximation, regularize the divergent integrals using a smooth Fermi-Dirac-type cutoff, and solve the coupled gap equations for the constituent quark mass \(m\) and the diquark gap \(\Delta\) self-consistently as functions of chemical potential \(\mu\), magnetic field strength \(\tilde{e}B\), and the coupling ratio \(\rho = G_D/G_S\).

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling ratio for zero magnetic field: \(\rho_c \approx 1.09\), below which chiral or superconducting order parameters appear almost exclusively, and above which a mixed broken phase exists.
- For \(\rho = 0.75\) at \(\tilde{e}B = 0\): jump in chiral gap \(\delta_m = 0.250\) GeV, jump in diquark gap \(\delta_\Delta = 0.077\) GeV, critical chemical potential \(\mu_c = 0.332\) GeV (first-order transition).
- For \(\rho = 1.05\) at \(\tilde{e}B = 0\): \(\delta_m = 0.133\) GeV, \(\delta_\Delta = 0.063\) GeV, \(\mu_c = 0.295\) GeV (first-order transition).
- For \(\rho = 1.09\) at \(\tilde{e}B = 0\): \(\delta_m = 0\), \(\delta_\Delta = 0\), \(\mu_c = 0.289\) GeV (second-order transition).
- For \(\rho = 1.15\) at \(\tilde{e}B = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\): \(\delta_m = 0.185\) GeV, \(\delta_\Delta = 0.051\) GeV, \(\mu_c = 0.295\) GeV (first-order transition, compared to smooth crossover at zero field).
- For \(\rho = 1.25\) at \(\tilde{e}B = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\): \(\delta_m = 0.122\) GeV, \(\delta_\Delta = 0.038\) GeV, \(\mu_c = 0.284\) GeV (first-order transition).
- Constituent quark mass at zero chemical potential increases with magnetic field: \(m \sim 0.35\) GeV for \(\tilde{e}B = 0.15\) GeV\(^2\) and \(m \sim 0.48\) GeV for \(\tilde{e}B = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) (magnetic catalysis).
- Spread of metastable region for \(\tilde{e}B = 0.5\) GeV\(^2\) is about 0.22 GeV; for \(\tilde{e}B = 0.25\) GeV\(^2\) it is about 0.12 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the zero-magnetic-field study of Huang et al. (2001) on the competition between chiral and diquark condensates in the two-flavor NJL model, finding qualitative agreement in the weak-field limit but significant differences at strong fields. It also builds on earlier studies of magnetized quark matter (Mandal 2012, Allen 2013, Sinha 2013, Denke 2013, Grunfeld 2014, Chatterjee 2014, Allen 2015, Denke 2015, Mandal 2016) by focusing on the coupled gap equations rather than treating the condensates independently. The results suggest that strong magnetic fields in neutron star cores (up to \(10^{18}-10^{19}\) G) can alter the phase structure of dense quark matter, with implications for the structure, energetics, and vibrational spectrum of neutron stars.
