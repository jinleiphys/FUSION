# Transverse-momentum dependent parton distribution functions beyond leading twist in quark models

> Higher-twist transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) are a valuable probe of the quark-gluon dynamics in the nucleon, and play a vital role for the explanation of sizable azimuthal asymmetries in hadron production from unpolarized and polarized deep-inelastic lepton-nucleon scattering observed in experiments at CERN, DESY and Jefferson Lab. The associated observables are challenging to interpret, and still await a complete theoretical explanation, which makes guidance from models valuable. In this work we establish the formalism to describe unpolarized higher-twist TMDs in the light-front framework based on a Fock-space expansion of the nucleon state in terms of free on-shell parton states. We derive general expressions and present numerical results in a practical realization of this picture provided by the light-front constituent quark model. We review several other popular quark model approaches including free quark ensemble, bag, spectator and chiral quark-soliton model. We discuss how higher-twist TMDs are described in these models, and obtain results for several TMDs not discussed previously in literature. This study contributes to the understanding of non-perturbative properties of subleading twist TMDs. The results from the light-front constituent quark model are also compared to available phenomenological information, showing a satisfactory agreement.

## Key claim

Higher-twist transverse momentum dependent parton distribution functions (TMDs) can be described in quark models without gauge-field degrees of freedom, but the relations among them depend on whether the model includes interactions; in the light-front constituent quark model, some free equations-of-motion relations hold while the Lorentz-invariance relation is not supported, indicating that the approach is not fully consistent due to the neglect of higher Fock states.

## Method

The authors established a formalism to describe unpolarized higher-twist TMDs in the light-front framework using a Fock-space expansion of the nucleon state in terms of free on-shell parton states. They derived general expressions and presented numerical results using the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM), and also reviewed several other quark model approaches including the free quark ensemble, bag, spectator, and chiral quark-soliton models. They derived equations-of-motion relations among TMDs and a Lorentz-invariance relation (LIR) valid in frameworks without gauge degrees of freedom, then tested the theoretical consistency of each model framework.

## Key numbers

- The unpolarized T-even TMD sector contains 4 TMDs: twist-2 \(f_1^q(x,p_T)\), twist-3 \(e^q(x,p_T)\) and \(f^{\perp q}(x,p_T)\), and twist-4 \(f_4^q(x,p_T)\)
- In quark models, 3 amplitudes \(\{A^q_1, A^q_2, A^q_3\}\) describe these 4 TMDs, leading to the LIR: \(f_4^q(x) = \tfrac{1}{2}f_1^q(x) + \frac{d}{dx}f^{\perp q(1)}(x)\)
- The twist-4 TMD satisfies the positivity constraint \(f_4^q(x,p_T) \ge 0\) and the sum rule \(2\int dx\,f_4^q(x) = N_q\) where \(N_q\) is the valence quark number of flavor \(q\)
- In free quark models, the EOM relations are: \(x\,e^q(x,p_T) = \frac{m_q}{M_N}f_1^q(x,p_T)\), \(x\,f^{\perp q}(x,p_T) = f_1^q(x,p_T)\), and \(x^2 f_4^q(x,p_T) = \frac{p_T^2 + m_q^2}{2M_N^2}f_1^q(x,p_T)\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of leading-twist TMDs in quark models (Pasquini 2008, Boffi 2009) to higher-twist TMDs, and reviews earlier work on subleading-twist TMDs by Jaffe (1991), Signal (1996), Jakob (1997), Schweitzer (2003), Wakamatsu (2003), and Avakian (2010). The results provide guidance for interpreting azimuthal asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering observed at CERN, DESY, and Jefferson Lab, and enable comparisons between model predictions and phenomenological extractions such as the first extraction of \(e^q(x)\) reported by Courtoy (2014). The study also identifies limitations of the light-front constituent quark model approach, particularly the violation of the Lorentz-invariance relation due to the neglect of higher Fock states.
