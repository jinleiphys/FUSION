# The role of the quantum dispersion in the Coulomb correction of Bose-Einstein correlations

# The role of the quantum dispersion in the Coulomb correction of Bose-Einstein correlations

## Key claim

The expected Coulomb distortion in the two-pion momentum correlation function is obliterated by the quantum dispersion of localized wavepacket states, and therefore becomes unobservable for charged pions; this conclusion is conditional on the pion source size (rms radius ~5 fm) and the initial wavepacket width (σ₀ = 1.8 fm), and the authors state that experimental data published after "Coulomb correction" are wrong for small momentum differences.

## Method

The time-dependent Schrödinger equation for two identical charged pions was solved using a basis expansion of symmetrized two-particle states built from time-dependent Gaussian wavepackets (s-, p-, d-, and f-type functions). The source was initialized as a static Gaussian with rms radius R_s = 5 fm and temperature T = 50 MeV, with initial wavepacket width σ₀ = 1.8 fm; 2000 events were propagated for an average of 250 fm/c, and from each event 5000 correlated pairs were sampled via Monte Carlo to construct the two-particle correlation function. As a second method, the authors employed a molecular dynamics procedure based on Bohm's quantum theory of motion using only Gaussian s-functions with a quantum potential.

## Key numbers

- Initial wavepacket width σ₀ = 1.8 fm
- Source rms radius R_s = 5 fm, temperature T = 50 MeV
- For two pions separated by r₀ = 4 fm: Coulomb energy E_coul ≈ 0.4 MeV, zero-point quantum energy E_q ≈ 26 MeV (nearly two orders of magnitude larger)
- Only for distances r₀ > 290 fm does the Coulomb energy exceed the localization energy
- For Z = 1 (realistic charge): only 0.4% of the final state weight is deposited into p-basis functions (99.6% remains in the s-function)
- For Z = 5: 8% of the weight is put into p-functions; in 10% of events this weight exceeds 20%
- Average system propagation time: 250 fm/c until Coulomb potential decayed below 5% of its initial value
- 2000 events generated with sp-basis; 10⁷ total pairs used for correlation functions
- For the Bohm molecular dynamics method: 3 × 10⁶ pion pairs generated for each charge

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier wavepacket description (Merlitz and Pelte, 1997) and contradicts the long-standing practice of applying Coulomb corrections derived from scattering theory, beginning with the Gamow factor approach (Gyulassy, 1979). Prior models progressively reduced the predicted Coulomb suppression by including finite source size (Bowman, 1991), exchange terms (Biyajima, 1995), long-lived resonances (Sinyukov, 1996), Coulomb screening (Baym, 1996), and finite momentum resolution (Barz, 1997). The paper argues that all such semiclassical treatments fail because they neglect the quantum potential and dispersion, and that experimental data should not be "corrected" for a nonexistent effect. The result enables a re-evaluation of pion source size extraction from Bose-Einstein correlation measurements without Coulomb corrections for small momentum differences.
