# Tensor-polarized structure functions: Tensor structure of deuteron in 2020's

> We explain spin structure for a spin-one hadron, in which there are new structure functions, in addition to the ones ($F_1$, $F_2$, $g_1$, $g_2$) which exist for the spin-1/2 nucleon, associated with its tensor structure. The new structure functions are $b_1$, $b_2$, $b_3$, and $b_4$ in deep inelastic scattering of a charged-lepton from a spin-one hadron such as the deuteron. Among them, twist-two functions are related by the Callan-Gross type relation $b_2 = 2 x b_1$ in the Bjorken scaling limit. First, these new structure functions are introduced, and useful formulae are derived for projection operators of $b_{1-4}$ from a hadron tensor $W_{μν}$. Second, a sum rule is explained for $b_1$, and possible tensor-polarized distributions are discussed by using HERMES data in order to propose future experimental measurements and to compare them with theoretical models. A proposal was approved to measure $b_1$ at the Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility (JLab), so that much progress is expected for $b_1$ in the near future. Third, formalisms of polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes are explained for probing especially tensor-polarized antiquark distributions, which were suggested by the HERMES data. The studies of the tensor-polarized structure functions will open a new era in 2020's for tensor-structure studies in terms of quark and gluon degrees of freedom, which is much different from ordinary descriptions in terms of nucleons and mesons.

# Tensor-polarized structure functions: Tensor structure of deuteron in 2020's

## Key claim

The tensor-polarized structure functions $b_1$, $b_2$, $b_3$, and $b_4$ of a spin-one hadron such as the deuteron can probe dynamical aspects including orbital motion within the hadron, and the HERMES data suggest a finite tensor polarization for antiquarks, but the sum rule for $b_1$ is not rigorous and depends on the parton model, so the interpretation of a finite $b_1$ integral as evidence for tensor-polarized antiquark distributions remains conditional on further theoretical and experimental work.

## Method

The paper derives projection operators for extracting the four tensor-polarized structure functions $b_{1-4}$ from the hadron tensor $W_{\mu\nu}$ for a spin-one hadron, using explicit combinations of the metric tensor, momentum vectors, and polarization vectors with spin-state Kronecker deltas. A parton-model sum rule for $b_1$ is derived by relating the integral of $b_1$ over Bjorken $x$ to the electric quadrupole form factor of the deuteron and to tensor-polarized antiquark distributions. A phenomenological parametrization of the tensor-polarized parton distributions is fitted to the HERMES data using a functional form $\delta_{_T} w(x) = a x^b (1-x)^c (x_0-x)$ with a node, assuming flavor symmetry and isospin symmetry, with the MSTW LO unpolarized PDFs at $Q^2 = 2.5$ GeV$^2$.

## Key numbers

* HERMES measurement: $\int_{0.002}^{0.85} dx \, b_1(x) = [1.05 \pm 0.34 \text{ (stat)} \pm 0.35 \text{ (sys)}] \times 10^{-2}$ for the full range.
* HERMES measurement: $\int_{0.02}^{0.85} dx \, b_1(x) = [0.35 \pm 0.10 \text{ (stat)} \pm 0.18 \text{ (sys)}] \times 10^{-2}$ for $Q^2 > 1$ GeV$^2$.
* Fit Set 1 (no tensor-polarized antiquarks, $\alpha_{\bar q}=0$): $\chi^2$/d.o.f. = 2.83, $a = 0.378 \pm 0.212$, $b = 0.706 \pm 0.324$, $c = 1.0$ (fixed), $x_0 = 0.229$.
* Fit Set 2 (finite tensor-polarized antiquarks allowed): $\chi^2$/d.o.f. = 1.57, $a = 0.221 \pm 0.174$, $\alpha_{\bar q} = 3.20 \pm 2.75$, $b = 0.648 \pm 0.342$, $c = 1.0$ (fixed), $x_0 = 0.221$.
* The analysis uses the average $Q^2 = 2.5$ GeV$^2$ of the HERMES experiment for the unpolarized PDFs.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for spin-one hadron structure functions originally introduced by Frankfurt and Strikman (1983) and Hoodbhoy, Jaffe, and Manohar (1989), and the $b_1$ sum rule proposed by the author in a parton-model approach. The paper interprets the HERMES (2005) data on $b_1$ and provides a parametrization to guide the approved JLab experiment to measure $b_1$ and future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). The formalism for polarized proton-deuteron Drell-Yan processes enables probing tensor-polarized antiquark distributions at Fermilab, J-PARC, and GSI-FAIR, opening a new era of tensor-structure studies in terms of quark and gluon degrees of freedom rather than the traditional nucleon and meson descriptions.
