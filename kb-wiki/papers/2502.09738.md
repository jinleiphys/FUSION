# Dirac fermions under imaginary rotation

> In the present study, we investigate the properties of an ensemble of free Dirac fermions, at finite inverse temperature $β$ and finite chemical potential $μ$, undergoing rigid rotation with an imaginary angular velocity $Ω=iΩ_I$. Our purpose is to establish the analytical structure of such states, as well as the prospects (and dangers) of extrapolating results obtained under imaginary rotation to the case of real rotation. We show that in the thermodynamic limit, the state of the system is akin to a stationary system with modified inverse temperature $β_q = qβ$ and the same chemical potential, where $q$ is the denominator of the irreducible fraction $ν= βΩ_I / 2π= p/q$. The temperature of the system becomes a fractal function of the rotation parameter, as in the case of the scalar field. The chemical potential breaks the fractalization of fermions. We also compute the thermodynamic potential $Φ$ and associated thermodynamic functions, showing that they also exhibit fractal behavior. Finally, we evaluate the axial and helical fluxes through the transverse plane, generated through the vortical effects, and show that they diverge in the thermodynamic limit, in the case when $ν= 1/q$ and $q \to \infty$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that an ensemble of free Dirac fermions under imaginary rotation (angular velocity \(\Omega = i\Omega_I\)) behaves in the thermodynamic limit like a stationary system with a modified inverse temperature \(\beta_q = q\beta\) (where \(q\) is the denominator of the irreducible fraction \(\nu = \beta\Omega_I / 2\pi = p/q\)), but this fractalization is broken by the presence of a finite chemical potential \(\mu\).

## Method

The authors analyze free Dirac fermions in Minkowski spacetime using relativistic kinetic theory (RKT) and thermal quantum field theory (QFT) in cylindrical coordinates. They construct mode solutions of the Dirac equation that are eigenfunctions of the Hamiltonian, vertical momentum, angular momentum, and helicity, then compute thermal expectation values of the vector, axial, and helical charge currents, the thermodynamic potential \(\Phi\), and related thermodynamic functions for the system under imaginary rotation. The transition from real to imaginary rotation is made by replacing \(\Omega\) with \(i\Omega_I\) and symmetrizing over clockwise and counterclockwise rotations.

## Key numbers

* The rotation parameter is defined as \(\nu = \beta\Omega_I / 2\pi = p/q\), where \(p\) and \(q\) are integers and \(q\) is the denominator of the irreducible fraction.
* In the massless limit, the pressure under real rotation scales as \(P = P_0 \gamma^4\) with \(\gamma = 1/\sqrt{1 - \rho^2 \Omega^2}\); under imaginary rotation, it scales as \(P = P_0 \gamma_I^4\) with \(\gamma_I = 1/\sqrt{1 + \rho^2 \Omega_I^2}\).
* The grand potential per unit volume in the massless limit under real rotation is \(\overline{\Phi} = -\gamma^2(R) P_0\), where \(P_0 = 7\pi^2 T^4/180 + T^2 \mu^2/6 + \mu^4/(12\pi^2)\).
* The axial and helical fluxes through the transverse plane diverge in the thermodynamic limit when \(\nu = 1/q\) and \(q \to \infty\).

## Context

This work extends the fractal behavior previously discovered for scalar fields under imaginary rotation by Chernodub (2022) and confirmed by Ambrus et al. (2023) to the case of free Dirac fermions. It also connects to lattice QCD studies of rotating systems (Yamamoto and Hirono, 2013; Braguta et al., 2021; Chernodub, 2022) where imaginary rotation is used to keep observables real. The results enable further investigation of how rotation affects the QCD phase diagram and whether the fractal features survive in interacting theories.
