# Deeply Bound Kaonic Clusters

> In this talk I make a review on the theoretical and experimental situation around the deeply bound kaon clusters, the possible bound kaonic states of nuclear, rather than atomic nature. At the same time I discuss novel developments around other kind of bound kaon clusters, which include states of two mesons and one baryon, with either one or two kaons, and states of a vector meson and two kaons, which explain naturally the observed properties of the X(2175) and Y(4260) resonances.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that deeply bound kaonic nuclear states predicted by chiral dynamical models are unlikely to be observable as distinct peaks because their calculated widths (around 100 MeV) far exceed the energy separation between levels, contradicting claims from several experiments that interpreted peaks as evidence for such states.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical framework based on chiral unitary dynamics in coupled channels, using Bethe-Salpeter equations to describe the $\bar{K}N$ interaction and its extrapolation into nuclear matter. They analyze experimental claims by performing detailed simulations of the reactions, including a Monte Carlo calculation of three-nucleon kaon absorption for the $K^{-}_{stop} A \to \Lambda d A'$ reaction, incorporating Fermi motion, nuclear density distributions, and experimental momentum cuts.

## Key numbers

- The chiral self-consistent approach yields a $K^-$ optical potential with about 60 MeV attraction at nuclear matter density, producing a 1s deeply bound state around 30 MeV with a width of about 100 MeV
- The $\Lambda d$ invariant mass distribution from the three-nucleon absorption model peaks at $M_{\Lambda d}= 3252$ MeV with a width of about 36 MeV, matching the experimental peak
- The $\bar{K}NN$ system binding energy is predicted in a range of 20 to 70 MeV with widths from 50 to 100 MeV across different calculations
- The energy gap parameter $\Delta=7.8$ MeV was determined for the $^6$Li nucleus to respect the reaction threshold for three-nucleon kaon absorption
- Experimental momentum cuts applied: 140 MeV/c $< p_{\Lambda}<$ 700 MeV/c and 300 MeV/c $< p_d <$ 800 MeV/c

## Context

This work reviews the controversy between chiral dynamics approaches (Lutz 1998, Ramos and Oset 2000, Schaffner-Bielich 2000, Tolos 2006) that predict moderate kaon-nucleus potentials of about 60 MeV attraction, and phenomenological models (Akaishi and Yamazaki 2002, 2003) that claim much stronger potentials of up to 600 MeV attraction. The paper demonstrates that the FINUDA experiment's claimed deeply bound kaon cluster from $\Lambda d$ pairs can be explained by conventional three-nucleon kaon absorption without invoking exotic bound states, extending the earlier two-nucleon absorption analysis by Magas (2006) and Ramos (2007). The review also discusses novel three-body systems of two mesons and one baryon that explain the X(2175) and Y(4260) resonances, and notes that recent experimental work by Hayano (2008) on $^4$He kaonic atoms rules out the superstrong potentials.
