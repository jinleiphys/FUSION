# Can tetraneutron exist from theoretical point of view?

> A theoretical possibility is shown for the bound state of a tetraneutron to exist in the case of the proposed neutron-neutron potentials in the singlet state with two attractive wells separated by a repulsive barrier. The anomalous behaviours are revealed for the calculated size, density distribution, and pair correlation functions of a hypothetical tetraneutron.

# Can tetraneutron exist from theoretical point of view?

## Key claim

A bound tetraneutron can theoretically exist, but only if the neutron-neutron singlet interaction potential has a specific exotic shape: two attractive wells separated by a repulsive barrier. The authors do not claim this potential is realistic; they state it is constructed only to demonstrate the possibility of a bound tetraneutron, and note that the required potential produces an anomalously high singlet scattering phase shift (about 160 degrees at 100-150 MeV) that is not observed experimentally.

## Method

The authors solve the four-neutron Schrodinger equation for the S=0, L=0 state using a variational method with a translationally invariant Gaussian basis antisymmetrized under particle permutations, employing up to 400 basis functions with optimization of nonlinear parameters. They construct phenomenological neutron-neutron potentials as superpositions of Gaussian functions, first in a spinless model where singlet and triplet potentials are identical, then in a more realistic case with separate singlet and triplet potentials, fitting the low-energy neutron-neutron scattering length (-18.9 fm) and effective range (2.75 fm).

## Key numbers

- For a single-Gaussian attractive potential, the critical coupling for tetraneutron binding is g_cr(4) = 3.911, which is 1.46 times the two-particle critical coupling g_cr(2) = 2.684 (dimensionless units with hbar^2/m = 41.4425 MeV·fm^2)
- For the proposed four-component singlet potential (E5) with r_0 = 0.488519 fm, the tetraneutron binds at g = 322.40 = 0.976 g_cr(2), where g_cr(2) = 330.42, while the dineutron remains unbound
- With the realistic triplet potential (E6) and singlet potential (E5), the tetraneutron binding energy is B(4n) >= 0.5 MeV (variational estimate using about 400 Gaussian functions)
- The root-mean-square radius of the hypothetical tetraneutron is 0.83 fm (1.704 r_0)
- The average singlet potential energy contribution is -1296.7 MeV, triplet potential energy is -158.7 MeV, and kinetic energy is 1454.9 MeV, yielding total energy E(4n) <= -0.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies (reviewed in the paper) that concluded tetraneutron binding is impossible with standard nuclear forces, including calculations by other groups (2000-2005) using modern four-particle methods. The paper contradicts the longstanding consensus that few-neutron systems cannot form bound states, following experimental reports of tetraneutron registration in reactions with 14Be. The authors propose that their exotic potential class could also produce tetraneutron resonances if the binding condition is not met, and suggest that heavier multineutron systems (e.g., 8 neutrons) might be even more stable due to magic number effects and cluster structure.
