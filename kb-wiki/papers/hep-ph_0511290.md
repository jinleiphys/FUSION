# Decays of eta and eta-prime within a chiral unitary approach

> Various decays of eta and eta-prime are investigated within the framework of U(3) chiral effective field theory in combination with a relativistic coupled-channels approach. Final state interactions are included by deriving s- and p-wave interaction kernels for meson-meson scattering from the chiral effective Lagrangian and iterating them in a Bethe-Salpeter equation. Very good agreement with experimental data is achieved.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a chiral unitary approach, combining U(3) chiral effective field theory with a relativistic coupled-channels Bethe-Salpeter equation, achieves very good agreement with experimental data for hadronic and anomalous decays of eta and eta-prime, but the authors note that four different classes of fits describe the available data equally well while differing in predictions for unmeasured quantities such as the eta-prime to pi+ pi- pi0 decay width.

## Method

The authors derive s-wave and p-wave interaction kernels for meson-meson scattering from the U(3) chiral effective Lagrangian and iterate them in a Bethe-Salpeter equation to generate infinite meson-meson rescattering processes. They apply this framework to hadronic decays (eta to 3 pi, eta-prime to eta pi pi, eta-prime to 3 pi) and anomalous decays (eta, eta-prime to gamma* gamma*, eta-prime to pi+ pi- gamma) by treating the Bethe-Salpeter T-matrix as an effective vertex for meson-meson scattering. The approach is constrained by fitting chiral couplings up to fourth order to experimental decay widths, branching ratios, Dalitz plot parameters, and meson-meson scattering phase shifts simultaneously.

## Key numbers

*   Theoretical partial decay width for eta to 3 pi0: 422 +- 13 eV, compared to experimental value 419 +- 27 eV (Particle Data Group, 2004).
*   Theoretical partial decay width for eta to pi+ pi- pi0: 290 +- 8 eV, compared to experimental value 292 +- 21 eV (Particle Data Group, 2004).
*   Theoretical branching ratio r1 = Gamma(eta to 3 pi0) / Gamma(eta to pi+ pi- pi0): 1.46 +- 0.02, compared to experimental value 1.44 +- 0.04 (Particle Data Group, 2004).
*   Theoretical Dalitz parameter a for eta to pi+ pi- pi0: -1.20 +- 0.07, compared to experimental value -1.22 +- 0.07 (Abele, 2001).
*   Theoretical Dalitz parameter g for eta to 3 pi0: -0.062 +- 0.006, compared to experimental value -0.062 +- 0.008 (Abele, 2001).
*   Theoretical decay width for eta-prime to 3 pi0: 330 +- 33 eV, compared to experimental value 315 +- 78 eV (Particle Data Group, 2004).
*   Theoretical branching ratio r2 = Gamma(eta-prime to 3 pi0) / Gamma(eta-prime to eta pi0 pi0): (71 +- 7) x 10^{-4}, compared to experimental value (74 +- 12) x 10^{-4} (Particle Data Group, 2004).
*   Theoretical partial decay width for eta-prime to eta pi+ pi-: 81 +- 4 keV, compared to experimental value 89 +- 11 keV (Particle Data Group, 2004).
*   Theoretical partial decay width for eta-prime to eta pi0 pi0: 46 +- 3 keV, compared to experimental value 42 +- 6 keV (Particle Data Group, 2004).
*   Theoretical ratio r3 = Gamma(eta-prime to eta pi+ pi-) / Gamma(eta-prime to eta pi0 pi0) with isospin-symmetric amplitude but physical masses: 1.78 +- 0.02; with isospin-breaking in amplitude: 1.77 +- 0.02; experimental value from Particle Data Group (2004): 2.12 +- 0.19.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral unitary approach (Borasoy and Nissler, 2004; Borasoy and Nissler, 2005) to provide a unified description of eta and eta-prime decays, building on earlier chiral perturbation theory calculations (Gasser and Leutwyler, 1985; Gasser and Leutwyler, 1985) and extended Khuri-Treiman equation studies (Kambor, 1996). The approach enables future experimental tests at WASA@COSY and MAMI-C facilities, particularly for the unmeasured eta-prime to 3 pi Dalitz parameters and decay widths, which can distinguish between the four fit classes and clarify the role of the a0(980) resonance in eta-prime to eta pi pi decays.
