# The Nuclear Response in Delta-Isobar Region in the ($^3\!$He,t) Reaction

> The excitation of a $Δ$-isobar in a finite nucleus in charge--exchange ($^3\!$He,t) reaction is discussed in terms of a nuclear response function. The medium effects modifying a $Δ$- and a pion propagation were considered for a finite size nucleus. The Glauber approach has been used for distortion of a $^3\!$He and a triton in the initial and the final states. The effects determining the peak positions and its width are discussed. Large displacement width for the $Δ$ - h excitations and considerable contribution of coherent pion production were found for the reaction on $^{12}$C.

# The Nuclear Response in Delta-Isobar Region in the (³He,t) Reaction

## Key claim

Medium effects from pion propagator renormalization in the one-pion-exchange mechanism, combined with finite-nucleus effects including multipole displacement and coherent pion production, produce strong deviations from the impulse approximation in the (³He,t) reaction on ¹²C at 2 GeV, but the collective enhancement from the real part of the pion self-energy is largely cancelled by the imaginary part from incoherent Delta decay, leaving the cross-section close to its quasifree magnitude.

## Method

The author calculated the absolute cross-section for the ¹²C(³He,t) reaction at 0 degrees and 2 GeV kinetic energy using a nuclear response function formalism with a finite-nucleus pionic response. The elementary charge-exchange amplitude was described by one-pion exchange with soft monopole form factors (cutoff Lambda = 650 MeV), and distortion of the incoming ³He and outgoing triton was treated via the Glauber approach with an inelastic distortion factor. The Delta-hole response was expanded in multipoles using a Woods-Saxon optical potential for the Delta, and the pion self-energy included forward and backward Delta-hole loops, short-range correlations with a Landau-Migdal parameter g', a sigma-term from the sigma-commutator, and a two-nucleon absorption term from the Ericson-Ericson optical potential.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff parameter for the monopole form factors is Lambda = 650 MeV, used for both piNN and piNDelta vertices.
- The imaginary part of the two-nucleon absorption potential uses ImC = 0.08 x (hbar/mu c)^6, from fits to pionic atom level widths.
- The Glauber distortion parameter is bar-gamma = (2.1 - i 0.26) fm², taken from elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering.
- The real part of the Delta-nucleus optical potential is V_Delta = -35 MeV, with imaginary part W_Delta = 40 MeV.
- The coherent pion production contribution peaks at about 240 MeV excitation energy.
- The attraction from the real part of the Delta-nucleus potential shifts the peak position by 15-20 MeV downward.

## Context

This work extends the study of medium effects in Delta-isobar excitation from nuclear matter calculations (Chanfray and Ericson 1984; Dmitriev and Suzuki 1985; Hong, Osterfeld, and Udagawa 1990) to finite nuclei, addressing the observation from experiments by Ellegaard et al. (1983), Ableev et al. (1984), and Contardo et al. (1986) that the Delta resonance properties differ in complex nuclei compared to free nucleon targets. The finite-nucleus treatment enables the inclusion of coherent pion production, which is absent in infinite nuclear matter, and demonstrates that the observed width of the resonance peak arises partly from the displacement of different Delta-hole multipoles. The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental triton spectra and provide a framework for extending the analysis to heavier targets and higher projectile energies.
