# Effect of Nucleon Dressing on the Triton Binding Energy

> The effect of nucleon dressing by pions, on the binding energy of three nucleons interacting via two-body forces, is calculated for the first time within a conventional nuclear physics approach. It is found that the dressing increases the binding energy of the triton by an amount approximately in the range from 0.3 MeV to 0.9 MeV, depending on the model used for dressing. This suggests that nucleon dressing may help explain the underestimation of the triton binding energy in previous calculations using only two-nucleon forces.

## Key claim

Nucleon dressing by pions increases the triton binding energy by 0.3 to 0.9 MeV, depending on the dressing model, suggesting that this mechanism may help explain the longstanding underestimation of the triton binding energy in calculations that include only two-nucleon forces, without invoking three-nucleon forces.

## Method

The authors formulated non-relativistic three-nucleon bound state equations in time-ordered perturbation theory, using dressed nucleon propagators and neglecting all three-nucleon forces. They expressed the dressed three-nucleon propagator and the disconnected three-nucleon t-matrix in terms of convolution integrals of the dressed nucleon propagator and the two-nucleon t-matrix, then solved the resulting Faddeev-type equations numerically using separable two-nucleon potentials and a partial wave decomposition in the J-J coupling scheme.

## Key numbers

- The triton binding energy increase from nucleon dressing ranges from 0.3 MeV to 0.9 MeV, depending on the dressing model used (models M6, M7, M8 with different parameters).
- The nucleon wave function renormalisation constant Z takes values 0.603483 (model M6), 0.699705 (model M7), and 0.799532 (model M8).
- The bare nucleon mass m0 is 6.56284 fm (model M6), 5.71685 fm (model M7), and 5.3317 fm (model M8).
- Previous non-relativistic calculations using only two-nucleon forces underestimate the triton binding energy by approximately 0.5 to 1.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the long line of research on the triton binding energy discrepancy, which has previously been addressed through relativistic corrections (Glockle 1986, Kondratyuk 1988, Sammarruca 1992, Stadler 1996, Stadler 1997, Kamada 2008) and through three-nucleon forces (Hajduk 1983, Ishikawa 1984, Friar 1984, Picklesimer 1992, Stadler 1995, Deltuva 2003, Skibinski 2011). The authors note that in the unitary transformation approach used in effective field theory (Epelbaum 1998, Bernard 2007), nucleon dressing contributes to two-nucleon and three-nucleon forces, whereas in their time-ordered perturbation theory approach, part of the dressing resides in the three-nucleon propagator. This work enables future calculations that combine nucleon dressing with three-nucleon forces to fully resolve the triton binding energy puzzle.
