# Leading Modes of the 3pi0 production in proton-proton collisions at incident proton momentum 3.35GeV/c

> This work deals with the prompt pp-->pp3pi0 reaction where the 3pi0 do not origin from the decay of narrow resonances like η(547), ω(782), η'(958). The reaction was measured for the proton beam momentum of 3.35GeV/c with the WASA-at-COSY detector setup. The dynamics of the reaction is investigated by Dalitz and Nyborg plots studies. The reaction is described by the model assuming simultaneous excitation of two baryon resonances Δ(1232) and N*(1440) where resonances are identified by their unique decays topology on the missing mass of two protons MMpp dependent Dalitz and Nyborg plots. The ratio R=Γ(N*(1440)->Nππ)/Γ(N*(1440)->Δ(1232)π->Nππ)= 0.039 +- 0.011(stat.) +- 0.008(sys.) is measured for the first time in a direct way. It shows that the {N*(1440)->Δ(1232)π->Nππ} decay is a leading mode of 3pi0 production. It is also shown that the MMpp is very sensitive to the structure of the spectral line shape of the N*(1440) resonance as well as on the interaction between the Δ(1232) and N*(1440) resonances. The multipion spectroscopy - a precision tool to directly access the properties of baryon resonances is considered. The pp-->ppη(3pi0) reaction was also measured simultaneously. It is shown that the η production mechanism via N*(1535) is 43.4 +- 0.8(stat.) +- 2.0(sys.) of the total production, for the η momentum in the CM system q_η^CM=0.45-0.7GeV/c. First time momentum dependence of the η angular distribution is seen, the strongest effect is observed for the cos(θ_η^CM) distribution.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the dominant mechanism for prompt 3π0 production in pp collisions at 3.35 GeV/c is the simultaneous excitation of Δ(1232) and N*(1440) baryon resonances, but this conclusion is conditional on the assumption that no interaction between the two resonances is included in the Monte-Carlo model, and the missing mass population function f(MMpp) must be extracted empirically from data to achieve agreement.

## Method

The pp→pp3π0 reaction was measured at the WASA-at-COSY detector with a proton beam momentum of 3.35 GeV/c, and events were selected via kinematic fitting with cuts on the missing mass of two protons (MMpp<0.5 GeV/c² and MMpp>0.6 GeV/c²) to exclude η meson decays. The dynamics were investigated using Dalitz plots (M²(pp) vs M²(p3π0) and M²(2π0) vs M²(2π0)) and Nyborg plots (M(pπ0) vs M(pπ0π0)) across five MMpp bins, comparing experimental data to Monte-Carlo simulations of two decay models for N*(1440): direct decay (Model₁: N*(1440)→pπ0π0) and sequential decay (Model₂: N*(1440)→π0Δ(1232)→pπ0π0). A chi-square fit of 19752 data points simultaneously across 15 plots determined the fraction β of Model₁ to the sum of both models, and a missing mass population function f(MMpp) was derived by dividing the η-subtracted experimental MMpp distribution by the Monte-Carlo model sum.

## Key numbers

- Ratio R = Γ(N*(1440)→Nππ) / Γ(N*(1440)→Δ(1232)π→Nππ) = 0.039 ± 0.011 (stat.) ± 0.008 (sys.), measured for the first time in a direct way
- β = 0.0548 ± 0.0015 (stat.) ± 0.0011 (sys.), the fraction of Model₁ (direct N*(1440) decay) to the sum of Model₁ and Model₂
- α = 0.1035 ± 0.0026 (stat.) ± 0.0003 (sys.), the fraction of normalized Model₁* to Model₁*+Model₂* from the chi-square fit
- χ²_min/NDF = 14813.29/19751 = 0.750 ± 0.010 for the full fit
- The η production mechanism via N*(1535) constitutes 43.4 ± 0.8 (stat.) ± 2.0 (sys.) of the total production for η momentum in the CM system q_η^CM = 0.45-0.7 GeV/c
- Missing mass population function f(MMpp) values range from 3.8 ± 1.3 at MMpp = 0.408 ± 0.008 GeV/c² to 0.159 ± 0.025 at MMpp = 0.984 ± 0.008 GeV/c²

## Context

This work extends studies of baryon resonance production in pp collisions, building on earlier measurements of Δ(1232) and N*(1440) excitation cross sections (Blair, 1970s) and total cross section data for pp→pX reactions. The paper provides the first direct measurement of the ratio of N*(1440) decay widths via its two dominant decay branches, which enables future studies of the Roper resonance's internal structure and its spectral line shape. The demonstrated sensitivity of MMpp to the N*(1440) line shape and to possible Δ(1232)-N*(1440) interactions opens the possibility of using multipion spectroscopy as a precision tool for accessing baryon resonance properties.
