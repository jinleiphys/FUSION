# Hybrid stars in the quark-meson coupling model with superconducting quark matter

> The phase transition to a deconfined phase is studied and the consequences in the formation of neutron stars are investigated. We use the quark-meson-coupling model for the hadron matter and the MIT Bag model for the quark matter in order to build the appropriate equations of state for the hybrid stars. The properties of the stars are then calculated. The differences between unpaired and color-flavor locked quark matter are discussed.

# Hybrid stars in the quark-meson coupling model with superconducting quark matter

## Key claim

The paper claims that hybrid neutron stars built from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model for hadronic matter and the MIT Bag model for quark matter can produce stable configurations, but only for certain combinations of bag constant and gap parameter; for a gap parameter Δ = 100 MeV, the bag constant must satisfy B^{1/4} ≥ 185 MeV to obtain a phase transition to a deconfined color-flavor locked (CFL) phase, and for B^{1/4} = 190 MeV with the CFL phase the maximum stellar mass (1.32 M⊙) is too small to account for known radio-pulsar masses.

## Method

The authors construct equations of state for hybrid stars by combining the QMC model (including hyperons) for the hadron phase with the MIT Bag model for unpaired quark matter (UQM) and for the color-flavor locked (CFL) superconducting phase. The mixed phase is built by enforcing Gibbs criteria for chemical, thermal, and mechanical equilibrium with global charge neutrality, and the resulting equations of state are integrated using the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain stellar mass, radius, and composition.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for QMC+UQM: 1.41 M⊙ (B^{1/4}=180 MeV), 1.58 M⊙ (B^{1/4}=190 MeV), 1.73 M⊙ (B^{1/4}=200 MeV), 1.85 M⊙ (B^{1/4}=210.854 MeV)
- Maximum mass for QMC+CFL: 1.32 M⊙ (B^{1/4}=190 MeV), 1.49 M⊙ (B^{1/4}=200 MeV), 1.76 M⊙ (B^{1/4}=211.303 MeV)
- Radius of maximum mass star for QMC+UQM: 11.95 km (B^{1/4}=190 MeV), 12.47 km (B^{1/4}=200 MeV)
- Radius of maximum mass star for QMC+CFL: 8.79 km (B^{1/4}=190 MeV), 13.31 km (B^{1/4}=200 MeV)
- For QMC+CFL with B^{1/4}=200 MeV, quarks appear at 2.67 times nuclear saturation density (ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³)
- Nuclear matter saturation properties used: binding energy E_B = -15.7 MeV at ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, symmetry energy a_sym = 32.5 MeV, incompressibility K = 257 MeV, effective nucleon mass M* = 0.774 M
- Bag constant for QMC hadron phase: B_N^{1/4} = 211.303 MeV (m_u=m_d=0) or 210.854 MeV (m_u=m_d=5.5 MeV)
- Gap parameter for CFL phase: Δ = 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies by two of the authors (Menezes and Providência) that used the non-linear Walecka model (NLWM) for hadronic matter with MIT Bag and Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models for unpaired quark matter (2003, 2003). It contrasts the QMC model with the NLWM, finding that hyperons appear only for very high bag constants in QMC, unlike in NLWM where hyperons appear at lower densities. The results are compared with those of Alford and collaborators (2001) for CFL quark matter, and the paper notes that only one of the studied equations of state satisfies the gravitational redshift constraint of 0.35 from the X-ray binary EXO0748-676 measured by Cottam and collaborators (2002). The work enables future investigation of the inclusion of strange meson fields (f₀(975) and φ(1020)) and neutrino trapping effects on hybrid star properties.
