# Superfluid Phase Transitions in Dense Neutron Matter

> The phase transitions in a realistic system with triplet pairing, dense neutron matter, have been investigated. The spectrum of phases of the $^3P_2-^3F_2$ model, which adequately describes pairing in this system, is analytically constructed with the aid of a separation method for solving BCS gap equation in states of arbitrary angular momentum. In addition to solutions involving a single value of the magnetic quantum number (and its negative), there exist ten real multicomponent solutions. Five of the corresponding angle-dependent order parameters have nodes, and five do not. In contrast to the case of superfluid $^3$He, transitions occur between phases with nodeless order parameters. The temperature dependence of the competition between the various phases is studied.

## Key claim

The phase diagram of dense neutron matter contains ten real multicomponent superfluid phases beyond the familiar single-component solutions, and transitions occur between phases with nodeless order parameters, in contrast to superfluid \(^3\)He, but these results depend on the assumption that the coupling parameter \(\eta\) (the ratio of \(^3F_2\) to \(^3P_2\) pairing matrix elements) remains small and that strong-coupling corrections near \(T_c\) are ignored.

## Method

The authors solve the BCS gap equations for the \(^3P_2\)–\(^3F_2\) pairing model in dense neutron matter using a separation method that factorizes the gap into a universal shape factor \(\chi(p)\) and coefficients \(D_1^{2M}\). They treat the \(^3F_2\) channel perturbatively via the small parameter \(\eta\), reducing the problem to three coupled equations for the coefficient ratios \(\lambda_1\) and \(\lambda_2\) and the gap \(\Delta_F\). The resulting system is solved analytically by a coordinate rotation that eliminates angular integrals, yielding isolated solutions in the \((\lambda_1,\lambda_2)\) plane.

## Key numbers

- The coupling parameter \(\eta\) varies around \(0.3\) in the density interval \(\rho_0 < \rho < 3\rho_0\) (where \(\rho_0\) is nuclear saturation density).
- The phase transition between the lowest-energy groups occurs at \(T \simeq 0.7 T_c\).
- The nodeless multicomponent solutions include two-component solutions with \(\lambda_2 = \pm 3\) and three-component solutions with \(\lambda_2 = 3(\sqrt{21}-4)/5\), \(\lambda_2 = 3\), and \(\lambda_2 = -3(\sqrt{21}+4)/5\).
- The nodal solutions include two-component solutions with \(\lambda_2 = \pm 1\) and three-component solutions with \(\lambda_2 = 13-2\sqrt{35}\), \(\lambda_2 = 3\), and \(\lambda_2 = 13+2\sqrt{35}\).

## Context

This work extends the pure \(^3P_2\) pairing problem studied by Khodel, Clark, and Zverev (2000) and earlier numerical treatments by Takatsuka (1972), Tamagaki (1970), and others, by including the \(^3F_2\) channel that lifts the parametric degeneracy of the pure \(^3P_2\) solutions. The results enable predictions of multiple superfluid phase transitions in cooling neutron stars, which may be detectable through changes in the star's moment of inertia or angular velocity, though the fine structure of the phase diagram remains unresolved without better knowledge of the in-medium neutron-neutron interaction.
