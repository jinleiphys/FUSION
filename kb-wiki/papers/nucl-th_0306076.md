# Two-photon exchange and elastic electron-proton scattering

> Two-photon exchange contributions to elastic electron-proton scattering cross sections are evaluated in a simple hadronic model including the finite size of the proton. The corrections are found to be small in magnitude, but with a strong angular dependence at fixed $Q^2$. This is significant for the Rosenbluth technique for determining the ratio of the electric and magnetic form factors of the proton at high $Q^2$, and partly reconciles the apparent discrepancy with the results of the polarization transfer technique.

## Key claim

Two-photon exchange corrections to elastic electron-proton scattering are small in magnitude but have a strong angular dependence at fixed momentum transfer, which partly reconciles the discrepancy between the Rosenbluth (longitudinal-transverse separation) and polarization transfer techniques for measuring the proton form factor ratio, though the calculation uses a simplified hadronic model with elastic intermediate states only.

## Method

The authors computed the two-photon exchange amplitude for elastic electron-proton scattering using a hadronic model with a finite-size proton, including both box and crossed-box diagrams. They expressed the Dirac and Pauli form factors in terms of Sachs form factors with a common monopole form factor (cutoff mass 0.84 GeV) to regulate ultraviolet divergences, and evaluated the loop integrals analytically using Passarino-Veltman functions with the FF program, obtaining identical numerical results from FeynCalc and FormCalc.

## Key numbers

- The difference between the model-independent infrared divergent contribution and the commonly used Mo-Tsai treatment yields roughly a 1% change in the cross section over the range of epsilon at Q^2 = 3 GeV^2 and Q^2 = 6 GeV^2.
- This infrared treatment alone gives a reduction of order 3% in the ratio R = mu_p G_E/G_M at Q^2 = 3 GeV^2 and 7% at Q^2 = 6 GeV^2.
- The full two-photon exchange correction parameter b (from fitting the correction to a linear function of epsilon) behaves roughly as b ≈ 0.014 ln(Q^2/0.65) with Q^2 in GeV^2, over the range 1-6 GeV^2.
- The finite proton vertex correction was found by Maximon and Tjon (2000) to be less than 0.5% for Q^2 < 6 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of two-photon exchange corrections beyond the standard treatment of Mo and Tsai (1969), which had been used in existing experimental analyses by Arrington (2003) and Walker (1994). The calculations show that two-photon exchange corrections have the proper sign and magnitude to resolve a large part of the discrepancy between Rosenbluth separation results (which gave R ≈ 1 for Q^2 < 6 GeV^2) and Jefferson Lab polarization transfer measurements by Jones (2000) (which gave R ≈ 1 - 0.135 (Q^2 - 0.24)). The authors note that additional contributions from inelastic nucleon excitation (e.g., the Delta+) remain to be studied, and that direct experimental evidence could come from comparing e+ p and e- p cross sections at backward angles.
