# Resonances: Universality and Factorization on Higher Sheets

> Most particles in nature are unstable, manifesting as resonances in scattering processes. Using analyticity and unitarity, we show nonperturbatively that resonances, defined as poles on higher Riemann sheets of scattering amplitudes, share basic properties with stable particles: (i) Universality, that a resonance generically appears in every S-matrix element; and (ii) Factorization, that amplitudes factorize on resonance poles. Our framework applies in any spacetime dimension and across arbitrarily many two-particle cuts, including cases where the kinematic Riemann surface becomes infinitely sheeted. Importantly, we find that resonance data (mass, width, couplings, and sheet index) are fully encoded on the physical sheet, where causality can impose additional constraints. These results are relevant for extending S-matrix bootstrap studies beyond elastic scattering.

## Key claim

Using analyticity and unitarity, the authors show nonperturbatively that resonances (poles on higher Riemann sheets) share universality and factorization properties with stable particles, but only under the condition that the determinant of the kernel develops a simple zero at the resonance pole; higher-order zeros would not imply factorization and correspond to colliding poles.

## Method

The authors derive a generalized K-matrix solution for partial-wave amplitudes that separates two-particle branch-cut contributions (encoded in the diagonal matrix Sigma) from all other singularities (encoded in the K-matrix). They then analytically continue this solution across two-particle cuts to higher Riemann sheets using monodromy relations, obtaining the amplitude on any sheet as T_{(n)} = [K^{-1} - Sigma_{(n)}]^{-1}. By analyzing the determinant condition for poles on higher sheets, they derive factorization of residues and relations connecting resonance data (mass, width, couplings, sheet index) to the physical-sheet amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The two-body phase-space volume in d spacetime dimensions is given by rho_{ij}(s) = b_{ij} [s - (m_i - m_j)^2]^{(d-3)/2} [s - (m_i + m_j)^2]^{(d-3)/2} / s^{(d-2)/2}, where b_{ij} = 1 - delta_{ij}/2 is the Bose symmetry factor.
* For square-root type branch points (d even with two massive particles, or d odd with two massless particles), the Riemann surface has exactly 2 sheets per threshold, so n_{ij} in {0,1}.
* For logarithmic type branch points (d odd with two massive particles, d even with two massless particles, or any d with exactly one massless particle), the Riemann surface is infinitely sheeted with n_{ij} in Z.
* N distinct square-root thresholds produce a Riemann surface with 2^N sheets total.

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix bootstrap program (Paulos 2016, Paulos 2017, Kruczenski 2022) beyond elastic scattering by showing that resonance data is fully encoded on the physical sheet, where causality imposes constraints. It generalizes the standard K-matrix formalism used in nuclear phenomenology (JPAC 2021, Mai 2022) and lattice QCD (Luscher 1990, Briceno 2017) by not assuming any particular parametrization of the K-matrix. The results enable bootstrap studies of inelastic processes and systems with multiple coupled channels, including cases with massless particles or odd-dimensional spacetimes where the Riemann surface becomes infinitely sheeted.
