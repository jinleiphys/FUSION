# Angular distributions in $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}π^{0}(η)$ decays

> The differential decay rates of the processes $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}π^{0}$ and $J/ψ\to p\bar{p}η$ close to the $p\bar{p}$ threshold are calculated with the help of the $N\bar{N}$ optical potential. The same calculations are made for the decays of $ψ(2S)$. We use the potential which has been suggested to fit the cross sections of $N\bar{N}$ scattering together with $N\bar{N}$ and six pion production in $e^{+}e^{-}$ annihilation close to the $p\bar{p}$ threshold. The $p\bar{p}$ invariant mass spectra is in agreement with the available experimental data. The anisotropy of the angular distributions, which appears due to the tensor forces in the $N\bar{N}$ interaction, is predicted close to the $p\bar{p}$ threshold. This anisotropy is large enough to be investigated experimentally. Such measurements would allow one to check the accuracy of the model of $N\bar{N}$ interaction.

## Key claim

The anisotropy of angular distributions in \(J/\psi\to p\bar{p}\pi^{0}(\eta)\) and \(\psi(2S)\to p\bar{p}\pi^{0}(\eta)\) decays near the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold is predicted to be large enough to be measured experimentally, but the predictions are valid only in a narrow energy region above threshold and cannot be compared with existing data that integrate over the full invariant mass range.

## Method

The authors calculated differential decay rates using an \(N\bar{N}\) optical potential model (Dmitriev 2016) that was previously fitted to \(N\bar{N}\) scattering cross sections, \(N\bar{N}\) production in \(e^{+}e^{-}\) annihilation, and six-pion production near the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold. They solved coupled-channels radial Schrodinger equations for the \(p\bar{p}\) system in the \(^{3}S_{1}\) and \(^{3}D_{1}\) partial waves, including tensor forces, and derived the decay amplitude and angular distributions for both isospin channels (\(I=1\) for \(p\bar{p}\pi^{0}\), \(I=0\) for \(p\bar{p}\eta\)). A refitted version of the potential was also tested against the invariant mass spectra.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropy parameter \(\gamma^{I}\) is predicted to be significant for \(p\bar{p}\) invariant mass about 100-200 MeV above the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold.
- The anisotropy in the distribution over the angle \(\vartheta_{pk}\) between proton and meson momenta is expected to be two times larger than in the distribution over the angle \(\vartheta_{p}\) between proton and beam direction.
- The decay rate formula includes the factor \(pk^{3}/(2^{11}\pi^{5}m_{J/\psi}^{4})\) for the differential distribution, where \(p\) is the proton momentum in the \(p\bar{p}\) center-of-mass frame and \(k\) is the meson momentum in the \(J/\psi\) rest frame.
- The invariant mass spectrum formula contains the enhancement factor \((|u_{1}^{I}(0)|^{2}+|u_{2}^{I}(0)|^{2})\) which equals unity when final-state interaction is turned off.

## Context

This work extends the potential model of Dmitriev (2016) for \(N\bar{N}\) interactions to the decays \(J/\psi\to p\bar{p}\pi^{0}(\eta)\) and \(\psi(2S)\to p\bar{p}\pi^{0}(\eta)\), where the tensor part of the interaction had been neglected in the chiral model calculation of Kang (2015). The calculations enable experimental tests of the \(N\bar{N}\) interaction model through angular distribution measurements near the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold, which would complement existing data on \(e^{+}e^{-}\to p\bar{p}\) and \(e^{+}e^{-}\to 6\pi\) processes. The results are also applicable to \(\psi(2S)\) decays with the replacement of the \(J/\psi\) mass by the \(\psi(2S)\) mass.
