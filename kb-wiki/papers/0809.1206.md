# Inflationary Axion Cosmology Beyond Our Horizon

> In theories of axion dark matter with large axion decay constant, temperature variations in the CMB are extremely sensitive to perturbations in the initial axion field, allowing one to place a lower bound on the total amount of inflation. The most stringent bound comes from axion strings, which for axion decay constant f=10^17 GeV would currently be observable at a distance of 6 x 10^16 light-years, nearly ten million times as far away as our horizon.

# Inflationary Axion Cosmology Beyond Our Horizon

## Key claim

The paper argues that ultralight axions with large decay constants ($f_a \gg 10^{12}$ GeV) can serve as dark matter if inflation homogenizes the initial misalignment angle, and that preinflationary thermal and topological fluctuations in the axion field produce observable isocurvature signatures in the CMB dipole and quadrupole, but only if the total amount of inflation exceeds a certain threshold, quantified by the ratio $R_X$ of the horizon size at the start of inflation to the patch destined to become our present-day horizon.

## Method

The authors compute the isocurvature perturbation spectrum from three sources: scale-invariant quantum fluctuations during inflation, preinflationary thermal fluctuations of the axion field (assuming thermal equilibrium at the Peccei-Quinn scale), and topological defects (axion cosmic strings) formed at the PQ phase transition. They derive constraints on the inflationary Hubble parameter $H_I$ and the number of e-foldings $N$ by comparing the predicted CMB multipole moments to the COBE and WMAP bounds on isocurvature perturbations, and estimate the detectability of a dipole asymmetry from axion strings using future large-scale surveys.

## Key numbers

- Axion mass: $m_a \approx 6 \times 10^{-9} \text{ eV} \times f_{16}^{-1}$, where $f_{16} \equiv (f_a / \mathcal{N}) / (10^{16} \text{ GeV})$.
- For $f_a = 10^{17}$ GeV, axion strings would be observable at a distance of $6 \times 10^{16}$ light-years, nearly $10^7$ times the current horizon.
- The bound on the inflationary Hubble parameter from isocurvature fluctuations: $H_I \lesssim 5 \times 10^8 \text{ GeV} \times f_{16}^{5/12}$ for $f_{16} \lesssim 10^{-1}$, and $H_I \lesssim 3 \times 10^8 \text{ GeV} \times f_{16}^{1/4}$ for $f_{16} \gtrsim 10$.
- For $f_{16} = 1/10$ and $H_I = H_I^{\max} = 2 \times 10^8$ GeV, the quadrupole bound gives $R_X \gtrsim 3 \times 10^5$.
- For $f_{16} \sim 10$, a constraint on the isocurvature dipole $q_1$ at the $10^{-4}$ level would be sensitive to a cosmic string more than $10^7$ horizon lengths away.
- The observed CMB dipole asymmetry is of order $10^{-3}$ (Kogut 1993).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of isocurvature constraints on axion dark matter from Fox (2004) and Beltran (2006) by considering preinflationary thermal fluctuations and topological defects, which produce scale-dependent signatures not captured by the standard scale-invariant bound. It also builds on the "tilted universe" scenario of Turner (1991), where a dipole isocurvature mode causes the CMB rest frame to differ from the matter rest frame. The paper enables future searches for ultralight axions through CMB dipole measurements and large-scale structure surveys, and provides a way to probe the total duration of inflation beyond what is accessible from flatness or the CMB quadrupole alone.
