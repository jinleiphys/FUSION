# Local CP-violation and electric charge separation by magnetic fields from lattice QCD

> We study local CP-violation on the lattice by measuring the local correlation between the topological charge density and the electric dipole moment of quarks, induced by a constant external magnetic field. This correlator is found to increase linearly with the external field, with the coefficient of proportionality depending only weakly on temperature. Results are obtained on lattices with various spacings, and are extrapolated to the continuum limit after the renormalization of the observables is carried out. This renormalization utilizes the gradient flow for the quark and gluon fields. Our findings suggest that the strength of local CP-violation in QCD with physical quark masses is about an order of magnitude smaller than a model prediction based on nearly massless quarks in domains of constant gluon backgrounds with topological charge. We also show numerical evidence that the observed local CP-violation correlates with spatially extended electric dipole structures in the QCD vacuum.

## Key claim

The local CP-violating correlation between topological charge density and the electric dipole moment of quarks in full QCD with physical quark masses is about an order of magnitude smaller than a model prediction based on nearly massless quarks in constant selfdual or antiselfdual gluon backgrounds, with the coefficient of proportionality depending only weakly on temperature.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with 2+1 flavors of dynamical quarks at physical masses using a tree-level improved Symanzik gauge action and stout-smeared staggered fermions, with a constant external magnetic field applied in the z-direction. They measured the local correlation between the topological charge density and the electric dipole moment operator for u, d, and s quarks, using the gradient flow to renormalize the observables and extrapolating results to the continuum limit across multiple lattice spacings (Nt = 6, 8, 10, 12) at temperatures of 113 MeV and 163 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The coefficient Cu for the up quark at T = 113 MeV is 0.132(10) in the continuum limit
- The coefficient Cd for the down quark at T = 113 MeV is 0.130(14) in the continuum limit
- The coefficient Cs for the strange quark at T = 113 MeV is 0.096(7) in the continuum limit
- At T = 163 MeV, Cu = 0.14(2), Cd = 0.12(3), and Cs = 0.09(2) in the continuum limit
- The magnetic fields studied range up to eB < 0.5 GeV^2
- The transition temperature Tc is approximately 150 MeV
- The smearing range used for continuum extrapolation was up to Rs^2 ≈ 0.5 fm^2

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of the chiral magnetic effect by measuring local correlations rather than global fluctuations, using dynamical quarks with physical masses instead of the quenched SU(2) approach of Buividovich (2009). The results contradict the model prediction of Basar (2011) based on nearly massless quarks in constant gluon backgrounds, finding the effect to be an order of magnitude smaller. The observed spatially extended electric dipole structures in the QCD vacuum provide direct numerical evidence for charge separation that could be relevant for interpreting charge asymmetry measurements from the STAR experiment at RHIC and the ALICE experiment at the LHC.
