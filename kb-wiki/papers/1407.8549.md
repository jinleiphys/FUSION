# Structure of chromomagnetic fields in the glasma

> The initial stage of a heavy ion collision is dominated by nonperturbatively strong chromoelectric and -magnetic fields. The spatial Wilson loop provides a gauge invariant observable to probe the dynamics of the longitudinal chromomagnetic field. We discuss recent results from a real time lattice calculation of the area-dependence of the expectation value of the spatial Wilson loop. We show that at relatively early times after the collision, a universal scaling as a function of the area emerges at large distances for very different initial conditions, with a nontrivial critical exponent. A similar behavior has earlier been seen in calculations of the gluon transverse momentum spectrum, which becomes independent of the initial spectrum of gauge fields. We also show the distribution of eigenvalues of the spatial Wilson loop and the fluctuations of its real and imaginary parts.

## Key claim
The spatial Wilson loop in the glasma exhibits a universal scaling as a function of area at large distances after a time of approximately 3/Qs, with a nontrivial critical exponent, but only for large loops; small loops retain sensitivity to the initial condition.

## Method
The authors performed real-time lattice simulations of classical Yang-Mills equations for SU(3) gauge fields, starting from three different Color Glass Condensate initial conditions: the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model, and solutions of the JIMWLK renormalization group equation with fixed and running coupling. They computed the expectation value of the spatial Wilson loop as a function of its area in the transverse plane, and extracted scaling exponents by fitting the area dependence to an exponential form with a power-law exponent.

## Key numbers
- Universal scaling exponent for large-area Wilson loops: gamma ≈ 1.2, reached after time τ ∼ 3/Qs
- Large-area fitting range: e^0.5 < A Qs^2 < e^5
- Small-area fitting range: e^{-3.5} < A Qs^2 < e^{-0.5}
- Variance of real and imaginary parts of Wilson loop trace approaches σ^2 = 1/(2 Nc^2) for large areas, with Nc=3

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Dumitru et al. (2013) from SU(2) to SU(3) colors, over a larger range of areas, and with a more systematic comparison of initial conditions from the MV model and JIMWLK evolution. The universal scaling behavior parallels earlier findings for the gluon transverse momentum spectrum by Lappi et al. (2011), which also becomes independent of initial conditions in the infrared. The results enable a gauge-invariant characterization of the glasma's soft field modes and provide a foundation for studying the approach to isotropization, though the present work does not include unstable quantum fluctuations.
