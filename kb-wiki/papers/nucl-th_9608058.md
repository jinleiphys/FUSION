# Multiquark Systems in a Constituent Quark Model with Chiral Dynamics

> We discuss the stability of multiquark systems within the recent model of Glozman et al. where the chromomagnetic hyperfine interaction is replaced by pseudoscalar-meson exchange. We find that such an interaction binds a heavy tetraquark system $QQ\bar q\bar q$ ($Q=c, b$ and $q=u, d)$ by $0.2-0.4$ GeV. This is at variance with results of previous models where $cc\bar q\bar q$ is unstable.

# Multiquark Systems in a Constituent Quark Model with Chiral Dynamics

## Key claim
Within the model of Glozman et al. where the chromomagnetic hyperfine interaction is replaced by pseudoscalar-meson exchange, a heavy tetraquark system $QQ\bar q\bar q$ ($Q=c, b$ and $q=u, d$) is bound by 0.2–0.4 GeV, provided the light diquark is in a spin–isospin $S=0$, $I=0$ state. This result contradicts previous models where $cc\bar q\bar q$ is unstable, but the binding depends on the specific choice of diquark quantum numbers and the inclusion of a Coulomb term in the confining potential.

## Method
The authors adopt the Hamiltonian of Glozman et al. (1996), which replaces the chromomagnetic hyperfine interaction with pseudoscalar-meson exchange ($\pi$, $\eta$, $\eta'$) between constituent quarks, omitting the one-gluon-exchange spin-spin term entirely. They perform crude variational estimates with Gaussian wave functions for each internal Jacobi coordinate, extending the model to systems containing heavy quarks by restricting meson exchange to light quarks only. The confining potential is a linear term, supplemented in some cases by a Coulomb term.

## Key numbers
- Binding energy of $(\bar c \bar c qq)$ tetraquark: 0.18 GeV, defined as $2(\bar c q) - (\bar c \bar c qq)$.
- Binding energy of $(\bar b \bar b qq)$ tetraquark: 0.22 GeV, defined as $2(\bar c q) - (\bar c \bar c qq)$ (note: the text appears to contain a typo, repeating the $\bar c q$ notation for the $b$ case).
- Binding energy for $(\bar b \bar b qq)$ becomes about twice larger (approximately 0.44 GeV) when a Coulomb term is included in the confining potential.
- Constituent quark mass for light quarks: $m = 0.337$ GeV.
- Charmed quark mass: $m_c \simeq 1.35$ GeV.
- Predicted masses: $\Lambda_c = 2.32$ GeV, $\Sigma_c = \Sigma_c^* = 2.48$ GeV.
- $\Delta$–$N$ splitting reproduced at approximately 0.3 GeV.
- Pion coupling constant at quark level: $g^2/4\pi = 0.67$.

## Context
This work extends the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman, Papp, and Plessas (1996), originally applied to the baryon spectrum, to multiquark systems containing heavy quarks. It contradicts earlier results from Ader et al. (1982), Zouzou et al. (1986), and Silvestre-Brac (1993), who found that in flavour-independent potentials with no hyperfine interaction, $(\bar b \bar b qq)$ is more likely stable than $(\bar c \bar c qq)$. The authors also note that Tornqvist (1991) and Manohar and Wise (1993) proposed one-pion exchange could bind heavy-flavour configurations, but with different diquark quantum numbers (colour 6 rather than colour $\bar 3$), leading to different predictions. The work enables further study of other multiquark systems such as $(ccqqqq)$, which the authors indicate is under investigation.
