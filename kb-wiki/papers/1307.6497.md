# The Heine-Stieltjes correspondence and a new angular momentum projection for many-particle systems

> A new angular momentum projection for systems of particles with arbitrary spins is formulated based on the Heine-Stieltjes correspondence, which can be regarded as the solutions of the mean-field plus pairing model in the strong pairing interaction G ->Infinity limit. Properties of the Stieltjes zeros of the extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomials, of which the roots determine the projected states, and the related Van Vleck zeros are discussed. The electrostatic interpretation of these zeros is presented. As examples, applications to n nonidentical particles of spin-1/2 and to identical bosons or fermions are made to elucidate the procedure and properties of the Stieltjes zeros and the related Van Vleck zeros. It is shown that the new angular momentum projection for n identical bosons or fermions can be simplified with the branching multiplicity formula of U(N) supset O(3) and the special choices of the parameters used in the projection. Especially, it is shown that the solutions for identical bosons can always be expressed in terms of zeros of Jacobi polynomials. However, unlike non-identical particle systems, the n-coupled states of identical particles are non-orthogonal with respect to the multiplicity label after the projection.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new angular momentum projection method for many-particle systems based on the Heine-Stieltjes correspondence, which reformulates the Bethe ansatz equations for angular momentum projection as a problem of finding zeros of extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomials; the method is claimed to be more efficient than traditional projection techniques because it avoids solving a matrix eigenvalue problem whose dimension grows combinatorially, but the authors note that the Bethe ansatz method is advantageous only if there is a simple way to solve the Bethe ansatz equations.

## Method

The authors start from the Bethe ansatz method for angular momentum projection, where n-coupled states with total angular momentum J are constructed using lowering operators J_-(x_i) acting on the SU(2) highest weight state, with the parameters x_i satisfying Bethe ansatz equations. They then map these Bethe ansatz equations onto a second-order Fuchsian equation whose polynomial solutions are the extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomials, with the Stieltjes zeros (roots of these polynomials) providing the required x_i values. The Van Vleck polynomial associated with each Heine-Stieltjes polynomial is also determined, and the method is applied to systems of n nonidentical spin-1/2 particles and to identical bosons or fermions, with the parameters epsilon_alpha chosen to have a symmetric reflection pattern to simplify the search for solutions.

## Key numbers

- For n=8 nonidentical spin-1/2 particles with t=1 (S=3), there are d(8,1)=7 different solutions for the extended Heine-Stieltjes polynomials, with Stieltjes zeros at values including x=0, x=plus or minus 3.679, x=plus or minus 2.59, and x=plus or minus 1.502.
- For n=8 with t=4 (S=0), there are d(8,4)=14 different solutions; example Stieltjes zeros include (x^2+0.379415)(x^2+10.53874) for zeta=1 and (x^2-12.56879)(x^2+0.57829) for zeta=2.
- For n=7 with t=2 (S=3/2), there are d(7,2)=6 different solutions; example Stieltjes zeros include (x-2.646)(x+2.646) for zeta=1 and (x-1.5275)(x+1.5275) for zeta=2.
- The multiplicity formula for the spin-1/2 case gives d(n,t) = (1+n-2t)n! / ((1+n-t)(n-t)!t!), which equals the dimension of the irrep [n-t,t] of the permutation group S_n.
- The constant c_{n,k} in the Van Vleck polynomials for the spin-1/2 case is c_{n,k} = -(n-k+1)k.

## Context

This work extends the Bethe ansatz approach to angular momentum projection previously developed by Pan and collaborators (2011) and Guan and collaborators, by providing a practical solution method via the Heine-Stieltjes correspondence. The method contrasts with traditional projection techniques such as Lowdin's projection operator method (1955), the Biedenharn and Louck Wigner operator method (1968) which only works analytically for spin-1/2 systems, and the M-scheme or J-coupled scheme used in nuclear shell model calculations; it also avoids the numerical difficulties of the Wheeler-Hill integral in projection operator methods. The results enable efficient construction of many-body states with definite angular momentum for arbitrary particle spins and can be applied to the mean-field plus pairing model in the strong pairing interaction limit, as well as to the construction of eigenstates of the pure pairing Hamiltonian in a deformed Nilsson basis.
