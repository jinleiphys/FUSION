# Magnetised neutron star crust within effective relativistic mean-field model

> Even though the crystallize nature of the neutron star crust plays a pivotal role in describing various fascinating astrophysical observations, its microscopic structure is not fully understood in the presence of a colossal magnetic field. In the present work, we study the crustal properties of a neutron star within an effective relativistic mean field framework in the presence of magnetic field strength $\sim 10^{17}$G. We calculate the equilibrium composition of the outer crust by minimizing the Gibbs free energy using the most recent atomic mass evaluations. The magnetic field significantly affects the equation of state (EoS) and the properties of the outer crust, such as neutron drip density, pressure, and melting temperature. For the inner crust, we use the compressible liquid drop model for the first time to study the crustal properties in a magnetic environment. The inner crust properties, such as mass and charge number distribution, isospin asymmetry, cluster density, etc., show typical quantum oscillations (De Haas-van Alphen effect) sensitive to the magnetic field's strength. The density-dependent symmetry energy influences the magnetic inner crust like the field-free case. We study the probable modifications in the pasta structures and it is observed that their mass and thickness changes by $\sim 10-15 \%$ depending upon the magnetic field strength. The fundamental torsional oscillation mode frequency is investigated for the magnetized crust in the context of quasiperiodic oscillations (QPO) in soft gamma repeaters. The magnetic field strengths considered in this work influences only the EoS of outer and shallow regions of the inner crust, which results in no significant change in global neutron star properties. However, the outer crust mass and its moment of inertia increase considerably with increase in magnetic field strength.

## Key claim

The paper claims that magnetic field strengths up to ~10^17 G significantly alter the outer crust composition, neutron drip density, and pasta structure properties, but these changes do not substantially affect global neutron star properties such as mass and radius; the hedge is that the magnetic field influences only the equation of state of the outer and shallow inner crust regions.

## Method

The authors used an effective relativistic mean-field (E-RMF) model to describe nuclear matter, incorporating Landau quantization for charged particles (protons, electrons, muons) in a magnetic field. The outer crust equilibrium composition was determined by minimizing the Gibbs free energy using experimental atomic masses from AME2020 supplemented by theoretical mass models (HFB24, HFB26, FRDM). For the inner crust, they applied the compressible liquid drop model (CLDM) within the Wigner-Seitz cell approximation, considering spherical, cylindrical, and planar pasta geometries, and solved for equilibrium via variational minimization including mechanical, dynamical, beta-equilibrium, and virial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strengths considered: up to B* = 5000, corresponding to B ~ 10^17 G (where B_c ~ 4.414 x 10^13 G)
- Outer crust surface density increases by a factor of ~7 between B* = 1000 (1.0554 x 10^-6 fm^-3) and B* = 5000 (7.5783 x 10^-6 fm^-3)
- Neutron drip density increases up to ~3 times between B* = 1000 (2.9791 x 10^-4 fm^-3 for HFB26) and B* = 5000 (7.9545 x 10^-4 fm^-3 for HFB26)
- The 56Fe plateau extends up to 1.0706 x 10^-5 fm^-3 for B* = 5000, compared to 4.9729 x 10^-9 fm^-3 for the field-free case
- Pasta structure mass and thickness change by ~10-15% depending on magnetic field strength
- Maximum isospin asymmetry alpha does not exceed ~0.4 for field strength as high as B* = 5000

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous unmagnetized neutron star crust studies using CLDM (Parmar 2022) to include magnetic fields, addressing the lack of comprehensive magnetized crust investigations. It builds on prior outer crust calculations in magnetic fields (Chamel 2012, Chamel 2015, Basilico 2015, Stein 2016) and inner crust studies using Thomas-Fermi approximations (Bao 2021, Nandi 2011, Lima 2013), but applies CLDM for the first time to magnetized inner crust. The results enable more realistic modeling of magnetar crust properties relevant to quasiperiodic oscillations in soft gamma repeaters, crustal transport, and magnetic field evolution.
