# Dibaryons with two strange quarks and one heavy flavor in a constituent quark model

> We investigate the symmetry property and the stability of dibaryons containing two strange quarks and one heavy flavor with $I=\frac{1}{2}$. We construct the wave function of the dibaryon in two ways. First, we directly construct the color and spin state of the dibaryon starting from the four possible SU(3) flavor state. Second, we consider the states composed of five light quarks, and then construct the wave function of the dibaryon by adding one heavy quark. The stability of the dibaryon against the strong decay into two baryons is discussed by using variational method in a constituent quark model with confining and hyperfine potential. We find that for all configurations with S=0,1,2, the ground states of the dibaryons are the sum of two baryons, and there are no compact bound state that is stable against the strong decay.

## Key claim

The authors find that for all configurations with total spin S=0,1,2, the ground states of the dibaryons containing two strange quarks and one heavy flavor (uudssQ) are simply the sum of two baryons, and there are no compact bound states that are stable against strong decay. This negative result holds despite the fact that the color-spin interaction in these systems is more attractive than in the corresponding two-baryon thresholds, because the additional repulsion from kinetic energy and confinement potential when bringing all six quarks into a compact configuration dominates over the hyperfine attraction.

## Method

The authors used a nonrelativistic constituent quark model with a Hamiltonian containing confinement and hyperfine potentials, with parameters fitted to reproduce experimental baryon masses via a variational method with single Gaussian trial wave functions. They constructed the color, flavor, and spin wave functions for the uudssQ dibaryon system with isospin I=1/2 using two complementary approaches: direct construction from SU(3) flavor states of the six-quark system, and construction from the five-light-quark subsystem plus one heavy quark. The spatial wave function was taken as a symmetric Gaussian with four variational parameters, and the stability against strong decay into two baryons was evaluated by comparing the variational ground state energy of the compact dibaryon with the sum of masses of the lowest two-baryon threshold.

## Key numbers

- Quark masses used in the model: m_q = 0.343 GeV (light u/d), m_s = 0.632 GeV, m_c = 1.93 GeV, m_b = 5.3 GeV
- Fitted baryon masses from the model: nucleon 0.977 GeV (exp. 0.938 GeV), Lambda 1.12 GeV (exp. 1.115 GeV), Sigma 1.2 GeV (exp. 1.189 GeV), Lambda_c 2.285 GeV (exp. 2.286 GeV), Lambda_b 5.608 GeV (exp. 5.619 GeV)
- For the H-dibaryon with flavor singlet, the expectation value of the hyperfine operator -sum(lambda_i^c lambda_j^c sigma_i dot sigma_j) is -24, while for Lambda Lambda it is -16
- For the five-quark subsystem in the H-dibaryon, the hyperfine expectation value is -16, identical to that of Lambda Lambda

## Context

This work extends the long-standing investigation of dibaryon stability initiated by Jaffe (1976) for the H-dibaryon, and generalizes it to systems containing one heavy quark (charm or bottom). Previous studies using chromomagnetic models (Leandri 1993, 1995) and chiral constituent quark models (Pepin 1998, Carames 2015) had examined heavy dibaryons, while Huang et al. (2013) studied H-like dibaryons with heavy quarks in the quark delocalization color screening model. The present work provides a systematic variational calculation within a consistent nonrelativistic framework that treats kinetic and interaction terms on equal footing, finding that the additional kinetic repulsion from compactification prevents binding even in the most attractive color-spin channels. This result suggests that future searches for stable multiquark states with heavy quarks may need to consider alternative configurations or additional dynamical mechanisms beyond the simple color-spin hyperfine interaction.
