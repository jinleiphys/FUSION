# The unified Skyrmion profiles and Static Properties of Nucleons

> An unified approximated solution for symmetric Skyrmions was proposed for the SU(2) Skyrme model for baryon numbers up to 8,which take the hybrid form of a kink-like solution and that given by the instanton method. The Skyrmion profiles are examined by computing lowest soliton energy as well as the static properties of nucleons within the framework of collective quantization, with a good agreement with the exact numeric results. The comparisons with the previous computations as well as the experimental data are also given.

# The unified Skyrmion profiles and Static Properties of Nucleons

## Key claim
The paper proposes a unified analytical approximation for Skyrmion profiles in the SU(2) Skyrme model with pion mass term for baryon numbers up to 8, claiming good agreement with exact numerical solutions. The hedge is that this is an approximate solution that approaches the real solution "as better as possible," not an exact analytical solution, and the variational parameters must be optimized for each baryon number.

## Method
The authors construct a hybrid profile function F(x) combining a kink-like arctan term with a hyperbolic term derived from the instanton holonomy method, then minimize the static energy functional using the downhill simplex (Nelder-Mead) algorithm to determine four variational parameters (a, c, d, w) for baryon numbers B=1 through 8. The optimized profiles are compared to numerical solutions obtained via the relaxation method for the rational-map ansatz of the Skyrme model with reduced pion mass m=0.526. Static properties of nucleons are then computed using semiclassical collective quantization with the B=1 solution.

## Key numbers
- Reduced pion mass used: m = 0.526 (dimensionless, defined as m = m_pi/(e f_pi))
- Baryon numbers considered: B = 1 through 8
- Sigma term computed: 38.099 MeV (compared to experimental "world average" estimate of 36 ± 20 MeV)
- Parameter fits given for lambda, c, d, w as functions of B, with numerical coefficients such as lambda = 0.4639 + 1.488 exp(15.038 - 15.361B) - 0.5466 exp(1.7442 - 1.7816B)
- Nucleon and Delta masses computed via formulas M_N = M^SK + 3/(8 I_0 Lambda) and M_Delta = M^SK + 15/(8 I_0 Lambda)

## Context
This work extends the rational-map approach of Battye and Sutcliffe (2000s) for Skyrmion solutions by providing an analytical profile form rather than purely numerical solutions. It builds on the instanton method of Atiyah and Manton (1989) and the kink-like solutions for the massless Skyrme model from Jia et al. (2009). The unified profile enables computation of static nucleon properties following the collective quantization framework of Adkins, Nappi, and Witten (1983), and the results are compared to those earlier computations as well as experimental data. This provides a practical analytical tool for baryon phenomenology where exact analytical solutions are unavailable due to the model's high nonlinearity.
