# Entropy production in quantum Yang-Mills mechanics in semi-classical approximation

> We discuss thermalization of isolated quantum systems by using the Husimi-Wehrl entropy evaluated in the semiclassical treatment. The Husimi-Wehrl entropy is the Wehrl entropy obtained by using the Husimi function for the phase space distribution. The time evolution of the Husimi function is given by smearing the Wigner function, whose time evolution is obtained in the semiclassical approximation. We show the efficiency and usefullness of this semiclassical treatment in describing entropy production of a couple of quantum mechanical systems, whose classical counter systems are known to be chaotic. We propose two methods to evaluate the time evolution of the Husimi-Wehrl entropy, the test-particle method and the two-step Monte-Carlo method. We demonstrate the characteristics of the two methods by numerical calculations, and show that the simultaneous application of the two methods ensures the reliability of the results of the Husimi-Wehrl entropy at a given time.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Husimi-Wehrl entropy, evaluated via a semiclassical approximation where the Wigner function is evolved using classical Hamilton equations and then Gaussian-smeared to obtain the Husimi function, can reliably describe entropy production in isolated quantum systems whose classical counterparts are chaotic, provided that two complementary numerical methods (test-particle and two-step Monte-Carlo) are applied simultaneously to ensure result reliability.

## Method

The authors compute the time evolution of the Wigner function in the semiclassical approximation by solving the classical Hamilton equation, ignoring terms of order hbar-squared. The Husimi function is then obtained by Gaussian smearing of the time-evolved Wigner function. Two numerical methods are proposed to evaluate the Husimi-Wehrl entropy integral: a test-particle method where phase-space points follow classical trajectories, and a two-step Monte-Carlo method that sequentially applies Monte-Carlo integration. The methods are tested on quantum mechanical systems whose classical counterparts exhibit chaotic behavior.

## Key numbers

- The Husimi-Wehrl entropy has a minimum value of S_HW = 1 for a one-dimensional system in the ground state, in contrast to the von Neumann entropy which gives S_vN = 0.
- The Gaussian smearing parameter Delta appears in the Husimi function definition: f_H(q,p) = integral (dq' dp' / pi hbar) exp[-Delta(q-q')^2/hbar - (p-p')^2/(Delta hbar)] f_W(q,p).
- The semiclassical equation of motion for the Wigner function retains terms up to O(hbar) and is exact for linear systems such as the harmonic oscillator and inverted harmonic oscillator.

## Context

This work extends the analytical results for Husimi-Wehrl entropy in one-dimensional systems (such as the inverted harmonic oscillator studied by Kunihiro et al.) to numerically tractable methods applicable to systems with more degrees of freedom. The approach builds on the test-particle method previously proposed by Tsai and Muller for solving the Husimi function equation of motion, but avoids ambiguities inherent in moment-matching approaches. The methods are designed to be applicable to quantum field theories and systems relevant to early universe cosmology and relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where thermalization of isolated quantum systems remains an open problem.
