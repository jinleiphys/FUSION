# Resonance energy and wave functions of $^{31}$Ne: a calculation using supersymmetric quantum mechanics

> In this communication, we present an efficient method for computation of energy and wave function of weakly bound nuclei by the application of supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SSQM) and bound states in continuum (BIC) technique. As a case study the scheme is implemented to the two-body ($^{30}$Ne + n) cluster model calculation of neutron-rich nucleus $^{31}$Ne. Woods-Saxon central potential with spin-orbit component is used as the core-nucleon interaction. The two-body Schrödinger equation in relative coordinate is solved numerically to get the energy and wave function of the low-lying bound states. A one-parameter family of isospectral potential (IP) is constructed from the bound state solutions following algebra of SSQM to find energies and wave functions of the resonance states. In addition to the 2p$_{3/2^-}$ (-0.33 MeV) ground state, two bound excited states: s$_{1/2}$ (-0.30 MeV), $p_{1/2}$ (-0.15 MeV) are also obtained. Few low-lying resonance states: f$_{7/2_1}$ (2.57 MeV), f$_{7/2_2}$ (4.59 MeV), f$_{5/2_1}$ (5.58 MeV), p$_{1/2_1}$(1.432 MeV), p$_{1/2_2}$ (4.165 MeV), p$_{3/2_1}$ (1.431 MeV), p$_{3/2_2}$ (4.205 MeV) are predicted. Among the predicted resonance states, the f$_{7/2^{-}}$ state having resonance energy $E_R \simeq 4.59$ MeV is in excellent agreement with the one found in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper claims that supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SSQM) combined with the bound states in continuum (BIC) technique provides an efficient method for computing resonance energies and wave functions of weakly bound nuclei, demonstrated for the two-body (30Ne + n) cluster model of 31Ne. The method is claimed to have an edge over other approaches because it can extract resonant state wave functions and reproduce the experimental observable width, though the resonance energy is independent of the tuning parameter eta and the width acquires computational error as eta approaches zero.

## Method

The authors solved the two-body Schrodinger equation in relative coordinate using the re-normalized Numerov method with a Woods-Saxon central potential including a spin-orbit component to obtain bound state energies and wave functions for the 30Ne + n system. They then constructed a one-parameter family of isospectral potentials (IP) following the algebra of SSQM, using the bound state solutions for states of the same spin-parity and combining with the BIC technique for states of different spin-parity. The tuning parameter eta was adjusted to deepen the potential well and raise the barrier simultaneously, enabling trapping of the system; resonance energies were extracted from peaks in the trapping probability versus energy plot, and resonance widths were computed using the WKB approximation in terms of barrier transmission coefficient and time of flight between classical turning points.

## Key numbers

- Ground state 2p3/2- binding energy: 0.33 MeV (separation energy Sn = 0.3317 MeV)
- Bound excited state s1/2 binding energy: 0.30 MeV (Sn = 0.3005 MeV)
- Bound excited state p1/2 binding energy: 0.15 MeV (Sn = 0.1503 MeV)
- Resonance f7/2_1: ER = 2.570 MeV, width = 0.863 +/- 0.087 MeV
- Resonance f7/2_2: ER = 4.59 MeV, width = 0.803 +/- 0.083 MeV (in excellent agreement with 4.592 MeV from Liu (2012))
- Resonance f5/2_1: ER = 5.580 MeV, width = 0.821 +/- 0.138 MeV
- Resonance p1/2_1: ER = 1.432 MeV, width = 0.866 +/- 0.282 MeV
- Resonance p1/2_2: ER = 4.165 MeV, width = 2.080 +/- 0.565 MeV
- Resonance p3/2_1: ER = 1.431 MeV, width = 0.828 +/- 0.265 MeV
- Resonance p3/2_2: ER = 4.205 MeV, width = 1.586 +/- 0.523 MeV
- Calculated RMS matter radius for 3/2- ground state: 3.61 fm (compared to experimental 3.47 fm from AMD and 3.62 fm from AMD-RGM in Minomo (2012))
- For the 7/2- resonance, the isospectral potential well depth changed from -6.79 MeV (eta = infinity) to -268.81 MeV (eta = 5e-5), and barrier height from 6.15 MeV to 130.44 MeV

## Context

This work extends the SSQM-based method previously applied by the same group to 19C (Khan 2021), 22C (Hasan 2019), and 42,44Mg (Khan 2021), and by Dutta (2018) to 15Be. It addresses the known p-f shell inversion in 31Ne, where the ground state is 2p3/2 (3/2-) rather than the shell model prediction of 1f7/2 (7/2-), consistent with findings from Nakamura (2014), Hamamoto (2010), and Liu (2012). The method enables prediction of resonance energies and widths for weakly bound halo nuclei near the neutron dripline, where traditional approaches such as the complex scaling method (Moiseyev 1998), R-matrix method (Descouvemont and Vincke 1990), and ACCC method (Kukulin 1989) face numerical difficulties.
