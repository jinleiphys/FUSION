# Strong decays of the $Ξ(1620)$ as a $Λ\bar{K}$ and $Σ\bar{K}$ molecule

> In this work, we study the strong decays of the newly observed $Ξ(1620)^0$ assuming that it is a meson-baryon molecular state of $Λ\bar{K}$ and $Σ\bar{K}$. We consider four possible spin-parity assignments $J^P=1/2^{\pm}$ and $3/2^{\pm}$ for the $Ξ(1620)^0$, and evaluate its partial decay width into $Ξπ$ via hadronic loops with the help of effective Lagrangians. In comparison with the Belle data, the calculated decay width favors the spin-party assignment $1/2^-$ while the other spin-parity assignments do not yield a decay width consistent with data in the molecule picture. We find that about $52\%$-$68\%$ of the total width comes from the $\bar{K}Λ$ channel, while the rest is provided by the $\bar{K}Σ$ channel. As a result, both channels are important in explaining the strong decay of the $Ξ(1620)^0$. This information is helpful to further understand the nature of the $Ξ(1620)^0$.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the newly observed Ξ(1620)⁰ is most likely an S-wave meson-baryon molecular state composed of ΛK̅ and ΣK̅ with spin-parity Jᴾ = 1/2⁻, but only if the probability of finding the ΛK̅ component lies in the range 52%–68%; other spin-parity assignments (1/2⁺, 3/2⁺, 3/2⁻) are excluded because they cannot reproduce the experimental total decay width.

## Method
The authors assumed the Ξ(1620)⁰ to be a hadronic molecule of ΛK̅ and ΣK̅ and considered four possible spin-parity assignments (Jᴾ = 1/2⁺, 1/2⁻, 3/2⁺, 3/2⁻). They computed the partial decay width into Ξπ via hadronic loop diagrams (exchanging K*, Λ, and Σ) using effective Lagrangians, with coupling constants determined by the compositeness condition (Weinberg-Salam type) and a Gaussian correlation function with size parameter β = 1.0 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The experimental mass of Ξ(1620)⁰ is M = 1610.4 ± 6.0 (stat) +5.9/−3.5 (syst) MeV; the experimental total width is Γ = 59.9 ± 4.8 (stat) +2.8/−3.0 (syst) MeV (Belle Collaboration).
- For the favored Jᴾ = 1/2⁻ assignment with x(ΛK̅) between 0.52 and 0.68, the predicted total decay width is 50.39–68.79 MeV, consistent with the Belle data.
- About 52%–68% of the total width comes from the K̅Λ channel; the rest comes from the K̅Σ channel.
- For Jᴾ = 1/2⁺, the predicted total width ranges from 0.011 MeV to 6.787 MeV, far below the experimental value.
- The coupling constant g for the K* → Kπ vertex was fixed as g = 4.64 using the experimental K*⁺ width of 50.3 ± 0.8 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies that identified the Ξ(1620) as a dynamically generated resonance in chiral unitary approaches (Ramos et al., 2002; Garcia-Recio et al., 2003) or as a bound state in the Bethe-Salpeter equation (Wang et al., 2019) and the One-Boson-Exchange model (Chen et al., 2019). Unlike those studies, the present work explicitly tests all four spin-parity assignments and finds that only the S-wave 1/2⁻ molecular picture with mixed ΛK̅ and ΣK̅ components can reproduce the Belle decay width. The result enables future experimental searches to focus on confirming the Jᴾ = 1/2⁻ assignment and the molecular nature of the Ξ(1620)⁰.
