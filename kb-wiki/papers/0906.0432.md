# Proton radioactivity within a generalized liquid drop model

> The proton radioactivity half-lives of spherical proton emitters are investigated theoretically. The potential barriers preventing the emission of protons are determined in the quasimolecular shape path within a generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) including the proximity effects between nuclei in a neck and the mass and charge asymmetry. The penetrability is calculated with the WKB approximation. The spectroscopic factor has been taken into account in half-life calculation, which is obtained by employing the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory combined with the BCS method with the force NL3. The half-lives within the GLDM are compared with the experimental data and other theoretical values. The GLDM works quite well for spherical proton emitters when the spectroscopic factors are considered, indicating the necessity of introducing the spectroscopic factor and the success of the GLDM for proton emission. Finally, we present two formulas for proton emission half-life calculation similar to the Viola-Seaborg formulas and Royer's formulas of alpha decay.

# Proton radioactivity within a generalized liquid drop model

## Key claim
The generalized liquid drop model (GLDM) including proximity effects, centrifugal potential, mass asymmetry, and spectroscopic factors from RMF+BCS theory reproduces experimental proton radioactivity half-lives for spherical emitters with an average discrepancy less than 40%, but only when the experimental Q value is used as input and the spectroscopic factor is included; for nuclei at the end of a proton shell, the spectroscopic factor becomes very small (e.g., 0.022 and 0.011) and neglecting it leads to large deviations.

## Method
The potential barrier for proton emission was constructed along a quasimolecular shape path within the GLDM, which includes volume, surface, Coulomb, and proximity energies. The barrier penetrability was calculated using the WKB approximation, and the spectroscopic factor (the probability that the spherical orbit of the emitted proton is empty in the daughter nucleus) was obtained from relativistic mean field (RMF) theory combined with the BCS method using the NL3 force. The partial half-life was then computed as T_p = ln2 / (nu_0 * P * S_p) with an assault frequency of 8e20 s^-1.

## Key numbers
- The penetrability P for the studied spherical proton emitters ranges from 1.280e-23 (for 105Sb) to 7.542e-17 (for 164Ir), which is relatively large compared to alpha-decay penetrabilities (10^-39 to 10^-14).
- The GLDM reproduces experimental half-lives with an average discrepancy less than 40%; for specific nuclei where DDM3Y and JLM models had difficulty, the GLDM deviations are: 4% for 147Tm, 26% for 150Lu, 5% for 156Ta, 10% for 156Ta (isomeric state), and 33% for 177Tl (isomeric state).
- For 155Ta (l=5, Q=1.453 MeV), the GLDM gives T_p = 3.9^{+1.4}_{-1.0} ms compared to the experimental value of 2.9^{+1.5}_{-1.1} ms.
- For 159Re (l=5, Q=1.816 MeV), the GLDM gives 23^{+8}_{-6} microseconds compared to the experimental value of 21^{+4}_{-4} microseconds.
- The spectroscopic factor S_p ranges from 0.999 (105Sb) down to 0.011 (185Bi) and 0.022 (177Tl isomeric state).
- Two fitted formulas for proton emission half-life are presented: one Viola-Seaborg type with parameters a=0.3437, b=4.9628, c=-31.1253, c0=2.5950 (average deviation sigma=0.153 for spherical emitters); and one Royer type with parameters a=-23.0632, b=-0.4225, c=0.4170, c0=2.5989 (sigma=0.183 for spherical emitters).

## Context
This work extends the GLDM, previously applied to fusion, fission, and alpha decay by Royer and collaborators (2000s), to proton radioactivity for the first time. It improves upon calculations using the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction by Basu (2003) and the JLM interaction by Bhattacharya and Gangopadhyay (2007), which did not include spectroscopic factors and consequently showed large discrepancies for nuclei like 177Tl and 185Bi. The inclusion of spectroscopic factors from RMF+BCS theory demonstrates that shell structure significantly affects proton emission half-lives. The two proposed formulas, analogous to the Viola-Seaborg and Royer formulas for alpha decay, enable determination of the angular momentum transfer l from measured half-lives and Q values, and show that proton emission is more sensitive to both Q and l than alpha decay due to the smaller reduced mass.
