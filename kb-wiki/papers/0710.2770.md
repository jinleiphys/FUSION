# Renormalizing the Schrodinger Equation for NN scattering

> The renormalization of the Schr"odinger equation with regular One Boson Exchange and singular chiral potentials including One and Two-Pion exchanges is analyzed within the context of NN scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that renormalization of the Schrödinger equation for nucleon-nucleon (NN) scattering with singular chiral potentials is possible and provides a framework for short-distance insensitivity, but only when the potential is attractive at short distances; for repulsive singular potentials no low-energy parameters can be fixed.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation in coordinate space for NN scattering, imposing a boundary condition at a cutoff radius \(r_c\) that matches the zero-energy wave function (or its energy-dependent generalization) to the finite-energy wave function. They then take the limit \(r_c \to 0\) to remove cutoff dependence, using the scattering length and effective range as renormalization conditions. For coupled channels such as the deuteron, they diagonalize the potential to handle the tensor-force singularity.

## Key numbers

- Scattering length in the \(^1S_0\) channel: \(\alpha_0 = -23.74(2) \, \text{fm}\) (from Stoks et al. 1994).
- Effective range in the \(^1S_0\) channel: \(r_0 = 2.77(4) \, \text{fm}\) (from Stoks et al. 1994).
- For the renormalized OBE potential with \(g_{\omega NN}=0\), a fit to the effective range yields \(m_\sigma = 493(12) \, \text{MeV}\), \(g_{\sigma NN}=8.8(2)\), and \(g_{\omega NN}=0(5)\) with \(\chi^2/\text{DOF}=0.24\).
- For the OBE potential with all mesons included, a fit yields \(g_{\omega NN}=12.876(2)\), \(g_{\sigma NN}=12.965(2)\), and \(m_\sigma=554.0(4) \, \text{MeV}\) with \(\chi^2/\text{DOF}=0.26\).
- The OPE-only deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio is \(\eta_{\text{OPE}}=0.0263\) (experimental value \(0.0256(4)\)).
- The TPE potential at NNLO yields \(r_0 = 2.87 \, \text{fm}\) at a cutoff \(r_c \sim 0.5 \, \text{fm}\), compared to the Nijmegen value \(r_0=2.67 \, \text{fm}\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization approach previously applied to OBE potentials (Pavon Valderrama et al. 2003) to chiral one-pion exchange (OPE) and two-pion exchange (TPE) potentials from Kaiser (1997) and Rentmeester (1999). It contrasts with the traditional approach of Machleidt (1987, 2000) where the potential is assumed valid down to the origin and parameters are fine-tuned. The results enable a systematic, model-independent description of the NN force based on chiral interactions, and the authors note that the renormalization of a chiral quark model with \(N\Delta\) splitting as a small scale deserves further investigation.
