# Nuclear Fusion via Triple Collisions in Solar Plasma

> We consider several nuclear fusion reactions that take place at the center of the sun, which are omitted in the standard pp-chain model. More specifically the reaction rates of the nonradiative production of ^3He, ^7Be, and ^8B nuclei in triple collisions involving electrons are estimated within the framework of the adiabatic approximation. These rates are compared with those of the corresponding binary fusion reactions.

# Nuclear Fusion via Triple Collisions in Solar Plasma

## Key claim

Triple collisions involving electrons in the solar plasma can produce \(^3\)He, \(^7\)Be, and \(^8\)B nuclei via nonradiative pathways omitted in the standard pp-chain model, but the relative contribution of these triple processes to the total fusion rates is rather small, with ratios to binary rates on the order of \(10^{-4}\) to \(10^{-3}\) across the relevant temperature range.

## Method

The authors calculated reaction rates for three triple-collision processes (e+p+d → \(^3\)He+e, e+p+\(^7\)Be → \(^8\)B+e, and e+\(^3\)He+\(^4\)He → \(^7\)Be+e) using an adiabatic approximation for nuclear motion, justified by the small electron-to-nucleus mass ratio, and a perturbative treatment of the electron-nucleus Coulomb interaction, justified by small Sommerfeld parameters at solar temperatures. Initial and final nuclear wave functions were obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with phenomenological short-range potentials and screened Coulomb interactions, with final states for two processes computed using two-cluster models and the \(^3\)He wave function from a Faddeev-type integro-differential equation. The triple rates were averaged over a Maxwell distribution of collision momenta and compared to the corresponding binary reaction rates at various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- For process (1) (e+p+d → \(^3\)He+e): \(\Lambda_1(T) \times 10^3\) ranges from 0.218 at \(T_6 = 1\) (1 × \(10^6\) K) to 0.055 at \(T_6 = 100\).
- For process (2) (e+p+\(^7\)Be → \(^8\)B+e): \(\Lambda_2(T) \times 10^3\) ranges from 0.106 at \(T_6 = 14\) to 0.089 at \(T_6 = 20\).
- For process (3) (e+\(^3\)He+\(^4\)He → \(^7\)Be+e): \(\Lambda_3(T) \times 10^3\) ranges from 0.098 at \(T_6 = 10\) to 0.193 at \(T_6 = 20\).
- The electron density used in calculations was \(n_e = 100 N_A\) cm\(^{-3}\), where \(N_A\) is Avogadro’s number.
- The average Coulomb interaction \(\langle V_e \rangle\) in the solar center is of order 10–20 eV, much smaller than the average kinetic energy of plasma particles (∼1.5 keV).

## Context

This work extends the standard pp-chain model of solar fusion by considering triple collisions involving electrons, which are omitted in the standard treatment. The authors note that the factorization of the three-body wave function into a product of two-body subsystem wave functions, as investigated in Refs. [1, 2], is only valid when all three particles are far apart, which does not contribute significantly to the reactions due to the short range of strong and weak interactions. The results suggest that while triple collisions are not negligible in principle, their contribution to solar fusion rates is small, and the mutual dependence of the binary processes (e+\(^7\)Be and p+\(^7\)Be) via the triple channel means that neutrino fluxes from \(^8\)B and \(^7\)Be are not independent. This work enables more refined modeling of solar neutrino production and could be extended to systems where all three particles are positively charged, such as p+p+\(^7\)Be, where different temperature dependence is expected.
