# Coulomb corrections in the calculation of ultrarelativistic heavy ion production of continuum e+ e- pairs

> Coulomb corrections to perturbation theory for producing electron-positron pairs in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions are considered in a part-analytical, part-numerical approach. Production probabilities are reduced from perturbation theory with increasing charge of the colliding heavy ions, as has been previously argued in the literature. It is shown here that the reduction from perturbation theory comes from the appropriate physical spatial cutoff of the electromagnetic potentials arising from the colliding ultrarelativistic heavy ions.

## Key claim

Coulomb corrections to perturbation theory for continuum electron-positron pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions exist and arise from the appropriate physical spatial cutoff of the transverse electromagnetic potentials, not from the regularization of the integral itself; the reduction from perturbation theory is a function of the combination variable \(k\gamma/\omega\) and vanishes for large values of that variable.

## Method

The author starts from the semiclassical Dirac equation solution for continuum pair production, which involves two countermoving delta-function potentials. He replaces the unphysical logarithmic transverse potential with a physically motivated Yukawa-type potential cut off at impact parameter \(\gamma/\omega\) (the equivalent-photon cutoff), leading to a modified Bessel function form \(K_0(\rho\omega/\gamma)\). He then numerically evaluates the transverse integral \(F(\mathbf{k})\) and the Coulomb correction integral \(G\) (the difference between the exact and perturbative \(k^2|F|^2\) integrals) for various nuclear charges \(Z\), and compares the results with the analytic expression of Lee and Milstein (2000).

## Key numbers

* For Au + Au at RHIC, the exact semiclassical solution produced a reduction of a little less than 10% in the predicted cross section for bound-electron positron pair production.
* The Coulomb correction integral \(G\) was evaluated numerically and found to converge to the Lee and Milstein result to one part in \(10^6\).
* The main contribution to the Coulomb correction integral \(G\) for \(Z=82\) is peaked at \(\gamma k / \omega = 2.8\) and spreads between half-maxima at 1.3 and 7.5.
* For \(k > \sim 100\ \omega / \gamma\), the magnitude of \(F(\mathbf{k})\) goes over into the original unregularized form.

## Context

This work extends the debate on Coulomb corrections to continuum pair production initiated by Ivanov, Schiller, and Serbo (1999) using the Weizsacker-Williams method, and by Lee and Milstein (2000, 2001) using an approximate analysis of the closed-form Dirac solution. The author shows that the universal function \(f(Z\alpha)\) derived by Bethe and Maximon (1954) for photoproduction Coulomb corrections also appears in the heavy-ion case at lowest order in transverse momentum, but that full calculations beyond the two-peak approximation used by Lee and Milstein will restore dependence on \(\gamma/\omega\). The CERN data of Vane et al. (1997), which scale perturbatively as \(Z_1^2 Z_2^2\), remain a puzzle and motivate full calculations of the uncorrelated positron cross section \(d\sigma(q)\) using the physically cut-off transverse integrals.
