# Phenomenological NLO analysis of eta(c) production at the LHC in the collider and fixed-target modes

> In view of the good agreement between the LHCb prompt-eta(c) data at sqrt(s)=7 and 8 TeV and the NLO colour-singlet model predictions --i.e. the leading v^2 NRQCD contribution--, we provide predictions in the LHCb acceptance for the forthcoming 13 TeV analysis bearing on data taken during the LHC Run2. We also provide predictions for sqrt(s)=115 GeV for proton-hydrogen collisions in the fixed-target mode which could be studied during the LHC Run3. Our predictions are complemented by a full theoretical uncertainty analysis. In addition to cross section predictions, we elaborate on the uncertainties on the p bar-p branching ratio --necessary for data-theory comparison-- and discuss other usable branching fractions for future studies.

## Key claim

The NLO colour-singlet model (the leading \(v^2\) NRQCD contribution) agrees well with LHCb prompt-\(\eta_c\) data at \(\sqrt{s}=7\) and 8 TeV, and the authors provide predictions for \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV in the LHCb acceptance and for \(\sqrt{s}=115\) GeV in fixed-target mode, but these predictions depend on the precise value of the \(\eta_c \to p\bar{p}\) branching fraction and on the decay channel used for reconstruction.

## Method

The authors computed the prompt \(\eta_c\) production cross section at next-to-leading order (NLO) in QCD within the colour-singlet model (CSM), which corresponds to the leading \(v^2\) contribution in the NRQCD factorization approach. They performed the calculation for the LHCb kinematic acceptance in collider mode at \(\sqrt{s}=13\) TeV and for proton-hydrogen fixed-target collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=115\) GeV. A full theoretical uncertainty analysis was carried out, including variations of the factorization and renormalization scales and of the parton distribution functions (PDFs). The authors also compiled and discussed the available branching fractions of charmonium decays to hadronic final states (such as \(p\bar{p}\), \(\phi\phi\), \(\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\)) and radiative decays, evaluating their suitability for \(\eta_c\) reconstruction at LHCb.

## Key numbers

- The branching fraction of \(\eta_c \to p\bar{p}\) is \((1.52 \pm 0.16) \times 10^{-3}\), known to about 10% precision.
- The branching fraction of \(\eta_c \to \phi\phi\) is \((1.79 \pm 0.20) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The branching fraction of \(\eta_c \to \Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\) is \((1.09 \pm 0.24) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The branching fraction of \(\eta_c \to \Xi^+\Xi^-\) is \((0.90 \pm 0.26) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The branching fraction of \(\eta_c \to p\bar{p}\pi^+\pi^-\) is \((5.3 \pm 1.8) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The branching fraction of \(\eta_c \to \phi K^+K^-\) is \((2.9 \pm 1.4) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The branching fraction of \(\eta_c' \to p\bar{p}\) is indirectly determined to be about \(0.7 \times 10^{-4}\).
- The branching fraction of \(h_c \to p\bar{p}\) has an upper limit \(< 0.15 \times 10^{-3}\) from BES III.
- The branching fraction of \(h_c \to \eta_c \gamma\) is \((510 \pm 60) \times 10^{-3}\).
- The branching fraction of \(h_c \to p\bar{p}\pi^+\pi^-\) is \((2.89 \pm 0.32 \pm 0.55) \times 10^{-3}\) from BES III.
- The LHCb \(\eta_c\) hadroproduction measurement required \(\eta_c\) transverse momentum \(P_T > 6.5\) GeV due to trigger bandwidth.

## Context

This work extends the NLO colour-singlet model analysis previously applied to LHCb \(\eta_c\) data at 7 and 8 TeV (Aaij 2014) to the upcoming 13 TeV Run 2 data and to fixed-target collisions at 115 GeV for Run 3. The discussion of decay channels builds on the LHCb measurement of \(\eta_c \to p\bar{p}\) (Aaij 2014) and on the observation of \(\eta_c' \to p\bar{p}\) in exclusive \(B^+ \to p\bar{p} K^+\) decays (Aaij 2016). The paper enables future \(\eta_c\) production measurements at the LHC using multiple decay channels (\(p\bar{p}\), \(\phi\phi\), \(\Lambda\bar{\Lambda}\), etc.) and provides a basis for using \(\eta_c\) production in proton-nucleus collisions to probe gluon distributions in nuclei, as proposed by Lansberg (2016) and Kusina (2017, 2018).
