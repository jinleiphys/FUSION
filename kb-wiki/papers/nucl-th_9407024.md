# Constraint Correlation Dynamics of SU(N) Gauge Theories

> A constraint correlation dynamics up to 4-point Green functions is proposed for SU(N) gauge theories which reduces the N-body quantum field problem to the two-body level. The resulting set of nonlinear coupled equations fulfills all conservation laws including fermion number, linear and angular momenta as well as the total energy. Apart from the conservation laws in the space-time degrees of freedom the Gauss law is conserved as a quantum expectation value identically for all times. The same holds for the Ward identities as generated by commutators of Gauss operators. The constraint dynamical equations are highly non-perturbative and thus applicable also in the strong coupling regime, as e.g. low-energy QCD problems.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a constraint correlation dynamics for SU(N) gauge theories that truncates the infinite hierarchy of Green function equations at the 4-point level, yielding a closed set of nonlinear equations that preserve all conservation laws (fermion number, linear and angular momenta, total energy) and also conserve the Gauss law as a quantum expectation value identically for all times, with the same holding for higher-order Ward identities generated by commutators of Gauss operators.

## Method

The authors start from the SU(N) gauge Lagrangian in the temporal gauge (A0 = 0) and derive Heisenberg equations of motion for equal-time Green functions constructed from gluon field operators, their conjugate momenta, and quark field operators. They apply a cluster expansion to decompose full Green functions into products of connected (correlated) Green functions, then truncate the hierarchy by setting all connected n-point functions with n >= 5 to zero. The resulting closed system of equations for connected 1-point, 2-point, 3-point, and 4-point Green functions is presented explicitly for gluon, quark, and mixed quark-gluon sectors.

## Key numbers

* The truncation scheme neglects all correlated terms of order n >= 5, i.e. G^{{a,alpha}}_{n c} = 0 for n >= 5.
* The gauge group is SU(N) with N x N generators T^a satisfying Tr{T^a T^b} = 1/2 delta^{ab} and [T^a, T^b] = i f^{abc} T^c, where a,b run from 1 to (N^2 - 1).
* The canonical quantization conditions are [A^a_i(r,t), Pi^b_j(r',t)] = i delta_{ij} delta^{ab} delta^3(r - r') and {Psi(r,t), Psi^dagger(r',t)} = delta^3(r - r').

## Context

This work extends the relativistic two-body correlation dynamics previously developed for baryons and mesons (Cassing 1990, Wang 1991) to non-abelian SU(N) gauge theories, addressing the novel complications of gluon self-interactions and internal gauge symmetry. The approach provides a non-perturbative alternative to lattice calculations and Dyson-Schwinger equation methods for problems such as low-energy QCD, confinement, hadron spectra, and the deconfinement transition. It enables the formulation of transport theories for quarks and gluons that go beyond the semi-classical abelian approximation of Elze et al. (1986) and the high-temperature perturbative limit.
