# Charge splitting of directed flow and charge-dependent effects in pion spectra in heavy ion collisions

> The large and rapidly varying electric and magnetic fields induced by the spectator systems moving at ultrarelativistic velocities induce a charge splitting of directed flow, $v_1$, of positive and negative pions in the final state of the heavy ion collision. The same effect results in a very sizeable distortion of charged pion spectra as well as ratios of charged pions ($π^+/π^-$) emitted at high values of rapidity. Both phenomena are sensitive to the actual distance between the pion emission site and the spectator system. This distance $d_E$ appears to decrease with increasing rapidity of the pion, and comes below $\sim$1~fm for pions emitted close to beam rapidity. In this paper we discuss how these findings can shed new light on the space-time evolution of pion production as a function of rapidity, and on the longitudinal evolution of the system created in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The electromagnetic fields from spectator systems induce a charge splitting of directed flow and distort charged pion spectra, and the magnitude of these effects is sensitive to the longitudinal distance \(d_E\) between the pion emission site and the spectator system; this distance decreases with increasing pion rapidity, falling below \(\sim\)1 fm for pions near beam rapidity, and increases to \(\approx\)3 fm for pions at central rapidities.

## Method

The authors analyze experimental data from the NA49 experiment at the SPS (Pb+Pb at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV) and the STAR experiment at RHIC (Au+Au at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV) to study charge-dependent distortions in pion spectra and directed flow. They compare these data to a phenomenological model where the spectator system is treated as Lorentz-contracted homogeneously charged spheres, with the longitudinal distance \(d_E\) between the pion emission point and the spectator center as the single free parameter. The model also incorporates a geometric picture of nuclear matter subdivided into longitudinal "strips" to relate \(d_E\) to the space-time evolution of the collision.

## Key numbers

- The distance \(d_E\) between the pion emission site and the spectator system is in the range 0.5–1 fm for pions at high rapidity (near beam rapidity) in Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV, as deduced from NA49 data on \(\pi^+/\pi^-\) ratios.
- For pions at central rapidities (\(-1 < y < 1\)) in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7\) GeV, the distance \(d_E\) is approximately 3 fm, as extracted from STAR directed flow data.
- The longitudinal extent of the Lorentz-contracted spectator system at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV is of the order of about 1 fm.
- The \(\pi^+/\pi^-\) ratio approaches zero in the region \(y \approx y_\mathrm{beam}\) (corresponding to \(x_F = 0.15 = m_\pi/m_N\) at low \(p_T\)) in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Rybicki (2007) on electromagnetic distortion of charged pion ratios and connects them to more recent findings on spectator-induced directed flow. The results are relevant to the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2014, Voloshin 2014, Hirono 2012) and the electromagnetic properties of the quark-gluon plasma. The analysis demonstrates that the distance \(d_E\) evolves with pion rapidity, reflecting the longitudinal expansion of the system, which is poorly constrained by hydrodynamical calculations at high baryochemical potential (Ryblewski). The findings enable future studies using existing fixed-target experiments (NA49, NA61/SHINE) and collider experiments to trace the space-time evolution of particle production.
