# Spurious states in the Faddeev formalism for few-body systems

> We discuss the appearance of spurious solutions of few-body equations for Faddeev amplitudes. The identification of spurious states, i.e., states that lack the symmetry required for solutions of the Schroedinger equation, as well as the symmetrization of the Faddeev equations is investigated. As an example, systems of three and four electrons, bound in a harmonic-oscillator potential and interacting by the Coulomb potential, are presented.

# Spurious states in the Faddeev formalism for few-body systems

## Key claim

Spurious solutions of the Faddeev equations, eigenstates that lack the antisymmetry required by the Schrödinger equation, appear generically and can even be the lowest-energy eigenstates in systems with repulsive interactions, as demonstrated for three and four electrons bound in a harmonic-oscillator potential with Coulomb interactions. The spurious states can be identified by the action of the metric operator 𝒯 (or 𝒯₄), which annihilates them, and can be eliminated by symmetrizing the Faddeev Hamiltonian on the physical subspace.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations for three and four identical fermions (electrons) bound by a harmonic-oscillator potential and interacting via the Coulomb potential, using a harmonic-oscillator basis truncated by total oscillator quanta N ≤ N_max. For the three-body system, the basis states are antisymmetrized only for particles 1 and 2; for the four-body system, the Faddeev amplitude is antisymmetrized for the first three particles. The metric operators 𝒯 (three-body) and 𝒯₄ (four-body) are diagonalized to separate physical (eigenvalue 3 or 4) from spurious (eigenvalue 0) subspaces, and the Hamiltonian matrix structure is analyzed in this basis.

## Key numbers

- For the three-electron system (J^π = 1/2^-) with ℏΩ = 0.5 a.u.: the physical ground-state energy converges to 3.2634 a.u. at N_max = 39 (after subtracting 0.75 a.u. center-of-mass energy); the first excited state energy is 4.0446 a.u.
- For the three-electron system: the number of physical states is about one-third of the total basis states; e.g., at N = 21, D = 253 total states, with 84 physical and 169 spurious.
- For the four-electron system (J^π = 0^+) with ℏΩ = 0.5 a.u.: the physical ground-state energy is 5.6006 a.u. at N_max = 14 (after subtracting 0.75 a.u. center-of-mass energy); the first excited state energy is 5.6979 a.u.
- For the four-electron system: at N = 14, D = 1390 total states, with 366 physical and 1024 spurious; the spurious ground state has energy 3.25 a.u. (unperturbed HO energy).
- Using an effective interaction approach, the three-electron ground state reaches the Stochastic Variational Method result of 3.26324 a.u. at N_max = 27; the four-electron ground state reaches 5.5991 a.u. at N_max = 14.

## Context

This work extends earlier analytical findings of spurious components in Faddeev amplitudes for three identical particles in a harmonic-oscillator potential by Friar, Gibson, and Payne (1980) and subsequent studies by Barnea and Mandelzweig (1992, 1994) and Rudnev and Yakovlev (1995). It generalizes the analysis to four identical fermions using a formulation with Faddeev amplitudes antisymmetrized for the first three particles, which differs from the traditional Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations. The identification method using the metric operator 𝒯 is analogous to the 𝒯-operator test for spurious states in Dyson boson mapping. The results enable systematic elimination of spurious states in Faddeev calculations for few-nucleon systems and provide a foundation for extending shell-model calculations to four nucleons using effective interactions.
