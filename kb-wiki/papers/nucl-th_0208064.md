# Probing the Isospin Dependent In-Medium Nucleon-Nucleon Cross Section by Nucleon Emissions

> The effects of the symmetry potential and the isospin dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross section on the number of proton(neutron) emissions N$_p(N_n$) are studied respectively within an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. The isospin dependent in-medium NN cross section is found to have a strong influence on N$_p(N_n$) but N$_p(N_n$) is not sensitive to the symmetry potential for the neutron-deficient colliding system at relatively high energies.We propose to make use of the N$_p(N_n$) as a probe to extract information on the isospin dependent in-medium NN cross section.

# Probing the Isospin Dependent In-Medium Nucleon-Nucleon Cross Section by Nucleon Emissions

## Key claim
The number of emitted protons N$_p$ and neutrons N$_n$ in heavy ion collisions depends sensitively on whether the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section is isospin dependent (with $\sigma_{np} \geq \sigma_{nn} = \sigma_{pp}$) or isospin independent, but depends only weakly on the symmetry potential, but this holds only for neutron-deficient colliding systems at relatively high beam energies (approximately 50 to 400 MeV/nucleon). Below about 50 MeV/nucleon, N$_p$ and N$_n$ depend on both the isospin dependence of the NN cross section and the symmetry potential.

## Method
The authors used an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model with a Skyrme-type mean field, Coulomb, Yukawa, momentum-dependent, Pauli, and symmetry potentials, together with a density-dependent in-medium NN cross section $\sigma^{med}_{NN} = (1 + \alpha \rho/\rho_0) \sigma^{free}_{NN}$ with $\alpha \approx -0.2$. They simulated collisions of various neutron-deficient systems (e.g., $^{76}$Kr+$^{40}$Ca, $^{74}$Kr+$^{74}$Se, $^{76}$Kr+$^{76}$Kr, $^{74}$Se+$^{74}$Se) at beam energies from 50 to 400 MeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 4.0 fm, comparing cases with isospin-dependent ($\sigma^{iso}$) versus isospin-independent ($\sigma^{noiso}$) NN cross sections and with different symmetry potentials ($U_1^{sym}$, $U_2^{sym}$, $U_0^{sym}$). Free nucleons were identified at 200 fm/c using a phase-space coalescence method with spatial distance $\Delta r = 3$ fm and momentum distance $\Delta p = 300$ MeV/c.

## Key numbers
- The free neutron-proton cross section $\sigma^{free}_{NN}$ is about a factor of 3 times larger than the free proton-proton or free neutron-neutron cross section below about 400 MeV (lab frame).
- The density-dependent in-medium NN cross section uses the parameter $\alpha \approx -0.2$ in $\sigma^{med}_{NN} = (1 + \alpha \rho/\rho_0) \sigma^{free}_{NN}$.
- The coalescence parameters for cluster identification are relative momentum $p_0 = 300$ MeV/c and relative distance $R_0 = 3.5$ fm.
- The beam energy range where N$_p$(N$_n$) depends sensitively on isospin-dependent NN cross section but weakly on symmetry potential is approximately 50 to 400 MeV/nucleon.
- The neutron-proton ratios of the colliding systems studied are 1.04 ($^{76}$Kr+$^{40}$Ca), 1.06 ($^{74}$Kr+$^{74}$Se), 1.11 ($^{76}$Kr+$^{76}$Kr), and 1.18 ($^{74}$Se+$^{74}$Se).
- The IQMD model was validated by comparing intermediate mass fragment multiplicities for $^{58}$Fe+$^{58}$Fe and $^{58}$Ni+$^{58}$Ni at beam energy $E = 75$ MeV/u, with fragments defined as having charge numbers from 3 to 18.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Bao-An Li et al. (2000), who found that the neutron-to-proton ratio of preequilibrium emissions at relatively low energies is sensitive to the symmetry potential and weakly sensitive to the isospin dependence of the in-medium NN cross section. The present study shows the opposite behavior for the absolute numbers of emitted protons and neutrons at higher beam energies (50-400 MeV/nucleon) for neutron-deficient systems, where two-body collisions dominate over the mean field. This enables a new experimental probe: measuring N$_p$ and N$_n$ separately (or their sum) in neutron-deficient heavy ion collisions to extract information on the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section, complementing the symmetry potential probe of Li et al. The authors note that sequential decays of heavier particle-unstable nuclei (as studied by M.B. Tsang et al.) do not affect their final conclusion, since the final total number of nucleon emissions already includes such decay contributions.
