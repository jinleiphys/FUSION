# R-matrix and K-matrix analysis of elastic alpha-alpha scattering

> The R- and K-matrix parametrizations are analyzed and compared for the elastic alpha-alpha scattering at center-of-mass energies below 40 MeV. The two parametrizations differ in their definitions of the resonance energy which can lead to quite different results. The physical values of the best-fit parameters are compared with those computed for a potential model. The existence of a broad resonance near 9 MeV is not supported by the data or by the potential model. We discuss the positive and negative aspects for both parametrizations.

# R-matrix and K-matrix analysis of elastic alpha-alpha scattering

## Key claim
The R-matrix and K-matrix parametrizations give different definitions of resonance energy that can lead to quite different results, and a broad resonance near 9 MeV in alpha-alpha scattering is not supported by either the data or a potential model. The physical interpretation of fit parameters as resonance parameters is often unjustified, with many pole terms serving only as ad hoc background contributions.

## Method
The authors performed R-matrix and modified K-matrix (cal-K) parametrizations of elastic alpha-alpha scattering phase shifts for s-wave scattering at center-of-mass energies below 40 MeV, using 43 data points below the first reaction threshold (Li-7 + p at 17.3 MeV) supplemented by 5 complex phase shifts from Darriulat et al. (1965) in the 26-40 MeV range. They compared best-fit parameters with those computed from a potential model using a Gaussian nuclear potential and Coulomb potential (Buck, Friedrich and Wheatley 1977; Friedrich 1981), with the potential depth adjusted so that the first pole lies at the Be-8 ground state energy of 92.08 keV. For the R-matrix, they used a channel radius of 6 fm following Barker (1994) and examined the dependence of resonance parameters on the choice of boundary condition constant B and channel radius a.

## Key numbers
- The Be-8 ground state resonance energy is 92.08 keV (center-of-mass), used as the fixed first pole energy in both parametrizations
- The potential model best fit to 48 data points gives chi-squared = 84.1 with channel radius a = 5.5 fm, and chi-squared = 93.9 with a = 6 fm
- The best R-matrix fit below 18 MeV gives chi-squared = 18.05 with three poles at E1 = 0.09208 MeV (fixed), E2 = 9.787 MeV, and E3 = 32.954 MeV
- The best K-matrix fit up to 40 MeV gives chi-squared = 27.0 using three real poles (at 0.09208, 2.809, and 31.865 MeV) plus a pair of complex conjugate poles at -1.026 +/- i 0.199 MeV
- The second R-matrix pole at 9.787 MeV has a formal reduced width of 0.848 MeV and formal width of 9.366 MeV, but does not satisfy the resonance condition d(delta)/dE greater than 0
- The potential model K-matrix has real poles at e1 = 92.08 keV, e2 = 2.808641 MeV, and e3 = 31.881031 MeV, with reduced widths of 46.138, -10.801, and -6.364 MeV respectively
- The R-matrix observed width of the ground state is 5.57 x 10^{-6} MeV (5.57 eV)
- The 3-pole R-matrix approximation to the potential model gives chi-squared = 9202, and the 5-pole approximation gives chi-squared = 3897, showing very slow convergence

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Barker (1994) on alpha-alpha scattering and addresses discrepancies between R-matrix and K-matrix fits observed in studies of the C-12(alpha,gamma)O-16 reaction (Azuma et al. 1994; Barker 1971, 1987; Barker and Kajino 1991; Ji et al. 1990; Humblet et al. 1976; Filippone et al. 1989; Humblet et al. 1991; Brune 1996). The paper demonstrates that while both parametrizations can fit data equally well, the physical interpretation of pole parameters as resonance parameters is problematic: the R-matrix has a strong dependence on the arbitrarily chosen channel radius, and the K-matrix background parameters (such as echo poles below threshold or complex poles) are ad hoc without physical meaning. This cautions against overinterpreting fit parameters in extrapolations for astrophysical reactions like C-12(alpha,gamma)O-16, though careful application of either method can give similarly good parametrizations.
