# Hybrid Quark Stars With Strong Magnetic Field

> Discovery of huge magnetic field in magnetars has stimulated a renewed interest about the magnetic field and physics of compact stars, where microphysics such as QED or QCD may play active parts. Here we discuss the equation of state (EOS) of quark matter in the core of compact stars by taking into account the strong magnetic field. We show that quark EOS becomes very stiff in the presence of the strong magnetic field, and becomes stiffest under the causality condition beyond the threshold strength of $B_c\sim O(10^{19})$ G. This is because quarks make the Landau levels in the presence of the magnetic field and thereby only the lowest Landau level is occupied in the extreme case beyond $B_c$. Thus quarks can freely move along the magnetic field with localization in the perpendicular plane, which resembles the quasi-one dimensional systems and gives rise to a stiff EOS. Consequently, we may easily produce high-mass stars beyond two solar mass. As another interesting possibility, we discuss the appearance of the third family of compact stars, succeeding white dwarfs and neutron stars, before collapsing into black holes. We demonstrate an example, which is specified by a discontinuous increase of the adiabatic index at the hadron-quark phase transition. Such new family may affect the supernova explosions or the gravitational wave emitted from the neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The quark equation of state becomes extremely stiff when the magnetic field exceeds a critical strength of about \(10^{19}\) G, because quarks occupy only the lowest Landau level and behave as a quasi-one-dimensional system; this stiffness can produce hybrid stars with masses above two solar masses, but only if the transition density from hadronic to quark matter is not too large.

## Method

The authors model quark matter as massless \(u,d,s\) quarks in a strong magnetic field using the MIT bag model, with Landau quantization giving single-particle energies that depend on the magnetic field strength. They derive the equation of state for the case where only the lowest Landau level is occupied, obtaining \(P = \varepsilon - 2B_{\text{bag}}\), and connect this quark EOS to hadronic EOSs (Brueckner-Hartree-Fock with hyperons, or the Shen EOS) at a chosen transition density. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation is then solved to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars.

## Key numbers

- The critical magnetic field strength for occupation of only the lowest Landau level is \(B_c \sim O(10^{19})\) G, derived from \(eB_c = (6\pi^4)^{1/3} n_b^{2/3}\).
- The maximum magnetic field from the virial theorem for a neutron star with \(R = 10\) km and \(M = 1.4 M_\odot\) is \(\sim 10^{18}\) G.
- Hybrid stars with quark cores can reach masses well above \(2 M_\odot\); for a \(2 M_\odot\) star, the quark core radius ratio is \(R_Q/R \approx 0.8\).
- The third family of compact stars appears for transition densities \(\varepsilon_c = 1.4 \times 10^{15}\) g/cm\(^3\) and \(2.0 \times 10^{15}\) g/cm\(^3\) when using the Shen hadronic EOS.
- The adiabatic index \(\Gamma\) asymptotically approaches 2 in the high-density limit, satisfying the gravitational stability condition \(\Gamma > \frac{4}{3}(1 + K R_s / R)\).

## Context

This work extends studies of magnetars with surface fields of \(O(10^{15})\) G (discovered in the 1990s) and addresses the observational constraint from massive neutron stars beyond \(2 M_\odot\) (Demorest et al., 2010; Antoniadis et al., 2013). The authors contrast their stiff quark EOS with previous hybrid star models where quark matter softens the EOS and cannot reach \(2 M_\odot\) (Kemper et al.; Schaffner-Bielich et al.). The possibility of a third family of compact stars (quark stars) is discussed, which could produce twin stars and affect supernova explosions or gravitational waves from neutron star mergers.
