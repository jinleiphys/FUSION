# Quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering off medium-heavy nuclei: 40Ca and 40Ar

> The charged-current quasi-elastic scattering of muon neutrinos on calcium and argon targets is calculated for neutrino energy up to 2.8 GeV. The calculations are done within the framework of the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation, which was earlier successfully applied to describe electron-nucleus data. The model is first tested against experimental data for electron scattering off calcium and then it is applied to calculate (anti)neutrino cross sections on 40Ca and 40Ar. We show that reduced exclusive cross sections for neutrino and electron scattering are similar. A significant nuclear model dependence of both inclusive and total cross sections for energy about 1 GeV was found. From the comparison of the (anti)neutrino differential and total cross sections per (proton)neutron, calculated for the carbon, oxygen, and argon targets it is evident that the cross sections decrease slowly with the mass-number of the target due to nuclear effects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) cross sections for neutrino and antineutrino scattering on 40Ca and 40Ar, calculated within the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA), show significant nuclear model dependence for energies around 1 GeV, and that cross sections per (proton)neutron decrease slowly with target mass number due to nuclear effects. The abstract's claim of similarity between reduced exclusive cross sections for neutrino and electron scattering is qualified by the finding that electron cross sections at the maximum are systematically higher than those for (anti)neutrino, attributed to Coulomb distortion of the incident electron wave function.

## Method

The author calculated exclusive, inclusive, and total CCQE cross sections for muon neutrino and antineutrino scattering on 40Ca and 40Ar targets using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA). The model was first tested against experimental data for electron scattering off 40Ca, including exclusive (e,e'p) data from NIKHEF and inclusive (e,e') data from SLAC and Bates, using the LEA program with the EDAD1 parametrization of the relativistic optical potential for final-state interactions. For inclusive cross sections, the approach included short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations in the ground state, with the high-momentum component of the spectral function incorporating 13% of the independent-particle shell model sum-rule limit.

## Key numbers

- Normalization factors for 40Ca(e,e'p) reduced cross sections: 0.68 for the 3/2+ ground state transition, 0.51 for the 1/2+ state (Ex=2.522 MeV), 0.11 for the 5/2+ state (Ex=5.258 MeV), and 0.13 for the 5/2+ state (Ex=6.328 MeV)
- For 40Ar(e,e'p) reduced cross sections at the maximum, values are systematically lower than for 40Ca by less than 12%
- Inclusive cross sections for electron scattering on 40Ca calculated with RDWIA generally agree with data within 14%
- For neutrino scattering, the RFGM results at the maximum are higher than RDWIA results by 13% for neutrino energy 0.5 GeV, decreasing to 7% for 2.5 GeV; for antineutrino, the discrepancy is 20% at 0.5 GeV and 12% at 2.5 GeV
- Ratio of RFGM to RDWIA total cross sections for neutrinos: about 1.26 at 0.3 GeV, 1.16 at 1 GeV, and 1.09 at 2.4 GeV; for antineutrinos: about 2.17 at 0.3 GeV, 1.3 at 1 GeV, and 1.16 at 2.4 GeV
- Axial mass MA = 1.032 GeV used in calculations
- Fermi momentum pF = 249 MeV and nuclear binding energy epsilon_b = 33 MeV used in the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM)

## Context

This work extends the RDWIA approach previously applied to carbon and oxygen targets (Butkevich 2002, 2003, 2005) to the medium-heavy nuclei 40Ca and 40Ar, motivated by the growing interest in liquid argon time projection chamber detectors for long-baseline neutrino experiments. The calculations are compared with the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM) and with experimental data from NIKHEF (CaN1, CaN3), SLAC (Whit), and Bates (Will) for electron scattering, as well as with neutrino data from various experiments (Mann, Baker, Pohl, Brunn, MiniB, Sci1). The results enable improved predictions for CCQE cross sections on argon targets, which are needed for neutrino oscillation experiments such as those proposed at LBNE, and demonstrate that the ratio of exclusive to inclusive CCQE cross sections decreases with target mass number, providing a quantity that may be insensitive to neutrino flux uncertainties.
