# More on nucleon-nucleon cross sections in symmetric and asymmetric matter

> Following a recent work, we present numerical results for total two-nucleon effective cross sections in isospin symmetric and asymmetric matter. The present calculations include the additional effect of Pauli blocking of the final states.

# More on nucleon-nucleon cross sections in symmetric and asymmetric matter

## Key claim

The inclusion of Pauli blocking of final states in the calculation of total in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections produces significant suppression compared to the authors' earlier work (Sammarruca and Krastev, 2005), with the effect being particularly strong for nn cross sections in asymmetric matter where the neutron Fermi momentum is larger than the proton Fermi momentum.

## Method

The authors compute total effective two-nucleon cross sections by integrating the in-medium differential cross section multiplied by a Pauli operator Q that restricts the angular integration domain based on the Fermi momentum. The calculation uses the Bonn-B potential within the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) approach to nuclear matter, with in-vacuum kinematics assumed for defining the total two-nucleon momentum in the nuclear matter rest frame. Results are tabulated for pp and np channels in symmetric matter and for pp and nn channels in asymmetric matter at various densities and relative momenta q0.

## Key numbers

- For pp in symmetric matter at kF=0.0 fm^-1 (free space) and q0=100 MeV: sigma_pp = 171.2 mb; at kF=0.8 fm^-1 and same q0: sigma_pp = 0.00 mb (fully suppressed)
- For np in symmetric matter at kF=0.0 fm^-1 and q0=100 MeV: sigma_np = 453.3 mb; at kF=0.8 fm^-1 and same q0: sigma_np = 0.00 mb
- For pp in symmetric matter at kF=1.0 fm^-1 and q0=400 MeV: sigma_pp = 17.51 mb; at kF=1.7 fm^-1 and same q0: sigma_pp = 17.40 mb
- For np in symmetric matter at kF=1.0 fm^-1 and q0=400 MeV: sigma_np = 18.35 mb; at kF=1.7 fm^-1 and same q0: sigma_np = 12.60 mb
- In asymmetric matter with alpha=0.6, at q0=100 MeV and kF=0.6 fm^-1: sigma_pp = 95.88 mb, sigma_nn = 10.11 mb; at kF=0.7 fm^-1: sigma_pp = 75.14 mb, sigma_nn = 0.00 mb
- In asymmetric matter with alpha=0.6, at q0=400 MeV and kF=1.7 fm^-1: sigma_pp = 20.91 mb, sigma_nn = 14.50 mb
- The q0 values cover a range corresponding to in-vacuum laboratory kinetic energies from approximately 20 MeV to 350 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculation (Sammarruca and Krastev, 2005) by adding Pauli blocking of final states, which was omitted in that earlier study. The results are intended for use in Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equations, where total effective cross sections with properly Pauli-blocked final states provide a better indication of the suppression the interaction undergoes in the medium. The low-density enhancement observed in the np channel, traced to the 3S1 partial wave, connects to discussions of bound-state signatures by Alm, Ropke, and Schmidt (1994) and Bohnet, Ohtsuka, Aichelin, Linden, and Faessler (1989). The authors note that empirical constraints on these in-medium cross sections can only be obtained indirectly through heavy-ion observables, with recent work by Li and Chen (2005) suggesting that transverse flow of neutrons and protons may be a reliable probe.
