# Correlation between the nuclear structure and reaction dynamics of Ar-isotopes as projectile using the relativistic mean-field approach

> This theoretical study is devoted to bridging the gap between the nuclear structure and reaction dynamics and unravelling their impact on each other, considering the neutron-rich light mass 30-60Ar isotopes. Using the relativistic mean-field with the NL3* parameter set, several bulk properties such as binding energies, charge radii, quadrupole deformation parameter, two neutron separation energy, and differential two neutron separation energy with the shell closure parameter are probed for the mentioned isotopic chain. For validation, the RMF (NL3*) results are compared with those obtained from the finite range droplet model (FRDM), Weizsacker-Skyrme model with WS3, WS* parameters and the available experimental data. Most of the participating isotopes are found to be prolate in structure and neutron shell closures are conspicuously revealed at N=14, 20, 40 but weakly shown at N=24, 28, 34. From our analysis, a central depletion in the nucleonic density is identified in 32Ar and 42-58Ar, indicating them as possible candidates for a semi-bubble-like structure. Interestingly, these results are consistent with recent theoretical and experimentally measured data. Besides, using the Glauber model, the reaction cross-sections are determined by taking 26-48Ar as projectiles and stable targets such as 12C, 16O, 40Ca, 90Zr, 124,132Sn, 208Pb and 304120. Although there is no experimental evidence for the stability of 304120, it has been predicted in Ref. [Mod. Phys. Lett. A {\bf 27}, 1250173 (2012)] as a stable nucleus. A relatively higher cross-section value is noticed between 30Ar and 32Ar which infers that 32Ar is the most stable isotope among the considered chain. Moreover, we noticed that the profile of the differential cross-sections and scattering angle are highly influenced by the mass of the target nuclei and the magnitude of the incident energy of the projectile nucleus.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nuclear structure and reaction dynamics of neutron-rich Ar isotopes are correlated, with the RMF (NL3*) model identifying central density depletion (semi-bubble structure) in 32Ar and 42-58Ar, and the Glauber model showing that 32Ar is the most stable isotope in the chain based on a relatively higher reaction cross-section between 30Ar and 32Ar. The abstract glosses over the fact that the shell closures at N=24, 28, 34 are described as "weakly shown" rather than definitive, and that the identification of 32Ar as most stable is an inference from cross-section trends, not a direct measurement.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism with the NL3* parameter set, incorporating the Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (BCS) pairing theory, to calculate binding energies, charge radii, quadrupole deformation parameters, two-neutron separation energies, and shell closure parameters for 30-60Ar isotopes. These structural results were then used as input for the Glauber multiple scattering model to compute total reaction cross-sections and differential elastic scattering cross-sections for 26-48Ar projectiles incident on stable targets (12C, 16O, 40Ca, 90Zr, 124,132Sn, 208Pb, and the predicted stable nucleus 304120) at various incident energies.

## Key numbers

* Binding energies for 30-60Ar from RMF (NL3*) range from 206.41 MeV (30Ar) to 419.67 MeV (58Ar), with 60Ar at 418.26 MeV; comparison with experimental data shows ΔBE (Exp. - Th.) values from 0.18 MeV (30Ar) to 5.004 MeV (48Ar).
* Charge radii (Rch) from RMF (NL3*) range from 3.471 fm (40Ar) to 3.711 fm (30Ar); experimental values for 32Ar, 34Ar, 36Ar, 38Ar, 40Ar, 42Ar, 44Ar, 46Ar are 3.347, 3.365, 3.391, 3.403, 3.427, 3.435, 3.445, 3.438 fm respectively.
* Quadrupole deformation parameter β2 from RMF (NL3*) ranges from -0.149 (44Ar, oblate) to 0.257 (30Ar, prolate); experimental β2 values for 34Ar, 36Ar, 38Ar, 40Ar, 42Ar, 44Ar, 46Ar are 0.238, 0.256, 0.163, 0.251, 0.275, 0.240, 0.175 respectively.
* Depletion factor (D.F.) from RMF (NL3*) is 0.048 for 32Ar, 0.106 for 42Ar, 1.251 for 44Ar, 2.897 for 46Ar, 1.890 for 48Ar, 0.053 for 50Ar and 52Ar, 0.393 for 54Ar, 0.766 for 56Ar, and 1.282 for 58Ar.
* Shell closures are conspicuously revealed at N=14, 20, 40 and weakly shown at N=24, 28, 34 based on the Dn(N) parameter.
* Two-neutron separation energy S2n shows a drop of about 2-6 MeV at 32Ar, 38Ar, and 58Ar, confirming shell/sub-shell closures at N=14, 20, and 40.
* Total reaction cross-section decreases to 550 MeV/A incident energy, then rises gently to about 760 MeV/A, then becomes nearly constant; a relatively higher cross-section is found between 30Ar and 32Ar for all targets.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of Ar isotopes by Saxena et al. (2019), Thakur et al. (2019, 2020), and Adriani et al. (2020) by using the RMF (NL3*) approach to probe both structure and reaction dynamics, and by comparing with the finite range droplet model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (2015) and the Weizsacker-Skyrme mass formulas WS3 of Liu et al. (2011) and WS* of Wang et al. (2010). The identification of semi-bubble structure in 32Ar and 42-58Ar is consistent with recent theoretical and experimental data, and the use of the Glauber model with RMF densities follows the methodology of Patra et al. (2009) and Sharma et al. (2006). The results enable future studies of halo structure in neutron-rich Ar isotopes, particularly 32Ar, and provide a framework for correlating shell closures with reaction cross-section trends across the isotopic chain.
