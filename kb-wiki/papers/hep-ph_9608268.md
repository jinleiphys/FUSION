# Meson photoproduction at threshold in three flavor soliton models

> In Skyrme-type models, the leading term of the low-energy photoproduction amplitude is identical to the standard expression and independent of the number of flavors considered, but subleading terms are not.

## Key claim

In Skyrme-type soliton models extended to three flavors, the leading (Kroll-Ruderman) term of the low-energy photoproduction amplitude is identical to the standard two-flavor expression and independent of the number of flavors, but the subleading terms (linear in meson mass) are modified by the inclusion of strangeness degrees of freedom, with the novel anomalous term depending on an integral over the winding number density of the soliton.

## Method

The author derives the photoproduction amplitude at threshold from the vector and axial vector currents of a chirally symmetric mesonic Lagrange density, using collective coordinates for flavor rotations and a Galilean transformation for center-of-mass motion. The meson field is introduced as a linear combination of a chiral rotation and a translation, orthogonal to the purely translational zero mode, and the amplitude is expanded to linear order in meson masses and zeroth order in rotational velocities. Numerical results for pion-nucleon amplitudes are computed using SU(3)-symmetric baryon wave functions and compared to the standard low-energy theorem, SU(2) soliton model, and experimental data.

## Key numbers

* Kroll-Ruderman amplitude for n(gamma, pi-)p: -31.3 x 10^{-3} m_pi+^{-1} in the SU(3) soliton model, compared to -31.8 (standard LET) and -31.4 +/- 1.3 (experiment, Mainz).
* Kroll-Ruderman amplitude for p(gamma, pi+)n: -27.9 x 10^{-3} m_pi+^{-1} in the SU(3) soliton model, compared to -27.4 (standard LET) and -27.9 +/- 0.5 (experiment, Mainz).
* Kroll-Ruderman amplitude for p(gamma, pi0)p: -0.8 x 10^{-3} m_pi+^{-1} in the SU(3) soliton model, compared to -2.5 (standard LET) and -2.0 +/- 0.2 (experiment, Mainz).
* Kroll-Ruderman amplitude for n(gamma, pi0)n: 0.6 x 10^{-3} m_pi+^{-1} in the SU(3) soliton model, compared to 0.4 (standard LET) and 1.6 (SU(2) soliton model).
* Born coupling g_{K Lambda N}/sqrt(4 pi): 3.74 for SU(3) symmetry, reduced to 2.69 with broken SU(3), and suppressed to approximately 0 with form factor effects.
* Born coupling g_{K Sigma N}/sqrt(4 pi): -1.08 for SU(3) symmetry, reduced to -0.89 with broken SU(3), and suppressed to approximately 0 with form factor effects.
* The novel anomalous term L_{gamma phi}^{(ano,2)} amounts to roughly 10% of L_{gamma phi}^{(ano,1)}.

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) soliton model results of Baenst (1970) and the Skyrme-type low-energy theorem derived in a previous paper (Skyrme LET, 1995) to three flavors, showing that the leading Kroll-Ruderman term is unchanged but subleading terms differ. The SU(3) generalization follows the rigid rotator approach of RRA (1995) for baryon wave functions. The paper enables future calculations of strangeness photoproduction, but cautions that for kaons and eta mesons the constant chiral rotation approximation fails due to strongly coupled bound states (e.g., a bound state roughly 450 MeV above the nucleon mass in the K Lambda, K Sigma, eta N system), which also explains the strong eta decay branching of the S11(1535) resonance as noted by Weise (1996).
