# Reconciling the lattice background field method with nonrelativistic QED: Spinor case

> We show that inconsistency between background field methods, which are relevant for lattice QCD spectroscopy, and effective field theory matching conditions, which are obtained from scattering amplitudes, can be resolved by augmenting nonrelativistic QED with operators related by the equations of motion. To determine the coefficients of such operators, we perform the nonrelativistic expansion of QED for a spin-half hadron including non-minimal electromagnetic couplings. As an effective field theory framework could provide a valuable tool to analyze lattice QCD correlation functions in external fields, we investigate whether nonrelativistic QED can be used to this end. We argue, however, that the most desirable approach is a hybrid one, which combines a relativistic hadron theory with operator selection based on nonrelativistic QED power counting. In this hybrid framework, new results are obtained for charged spin-half hadrons in uniform magnetic fields, including a proper treatment of Landau levels both in infinite volume and on a torus.

## Key claim

The inconsistency between the lattice background field method and nonrelativistic QED matching conditions for spin-half hadrons can be resolved by retaining equation-of-motion operators that are ordinarily eliminated from the effective Lagrangian, but the authors argue that a hybrid approach combining a relativistic hadron theory with operator selection based on nonrelativistic QED power counting is the most desirable framework for analyzing lattice QCD correlation functions in external fields.

## Method

The authors performed a Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation on the relativistic QED Lagrangian for a spin-half hadron, including non-minimal electromagnetic couplings up to operators of order 1/M^3, to derive the nonrelativistic QED Lagrangian augmented with equation-of-motion operators. They then matched the coefficients of this augmented nonrelativistic QED to the underlying relativistic theory by expanding the relativistic action order-by-order in inverse powers of the hadron mass M, and computed correlation functions for charged spin-half hadrons in uniform electric and magnetic fields both in infinite volume and on a torus.

## Key numbers

- The nonrelativistic QED Lagrangian is developed to order 1/M^3, with the leading odd operator eliminated via the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation using S_1 = - (gamma vector dot D vector) / (2M)
- The charge radius relation: <r_E^2> = (3/(2M^2)) (4C_1 + kappa), where kappa is the anomalous magnetic moment
- The magnetic radius relation: <r_M^2> = (6/M^2) (C_1 - 2C_2)
- The electric polarizability relation: pi M^3 alpha_E = -C_3 - C_6 + (1/4)(C_4 + C_5)
- The magnetic polarizability relation: pi M^3 beta_M = C_3 + C_6 + (1/4)(C_4 + C_5)
- The matching condition for the linear combination c_{A_1} + (1/2)c_{A_2} = Z^2 + kappa^2 - 16 pi M^3 alpha_E + (4/3) Z M^2 <r_E^2>
- The coefficient c_F = Z + kappa = mu (the magnetic moment) at O(M^{-1})
- The coefficient c_D = Z + 2kappa + 8C_1 at O(M^{-2}), which equals Z + (4/3)M^2 <r_E^2>

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis for scalar hadrons (Lee, 2013) to the spin-half case, resolving an inconsistency between the background field method used in lattice QCD spectroscopy and standard nonrelativistic QED matching conditions obtained from scattering amplitudes. The paper builds on the nonrelativistic QED framework developed by Caswell (1985), Kinoshita (1995), Manohar (1997), and Hill (2012), and enables the proper treatment of Landau levels for charged spin-half hadrons in uniform magnetic fields on a torus, which is relevant for lattice QCD computations of hadronic polarizabilities and electromagnetic properties.
