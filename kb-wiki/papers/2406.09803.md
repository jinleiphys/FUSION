# Triaxial shape of the one-proton emitter $^{149}$Lu

> We revisit the proton emitter $^{149}$Lu utilizing the recently developed triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (TRHBc). By incorporating the microscopic nuclear structure properties from the TRHBc theory into the WKB approximation, we successfully reproduce the measured proton-emission half-life of $^{149}$Lu within experimental uncertainties. A triaxial ground state characterized by ($β=0.17,γ=31^\circ$) has been clarified for $^{149}$Lu. The inclusion of triaxiality significantly changes nuclear density distributions and potentials, which results in enhanced binding of both the nuclear system and the proton-emitting orbital. As a result, a slightly extended half-life for the proton emission of $^{149}$Lu is achieved after considering triaxial deformation degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the proton emitter ¹⁴⁹Lu has a triaxial ground state with deformation parameters (β=0.17, γ=31°), and that including triaxiality in the theoretical model yields a slightly extended half-life for proton emission compared to the axially symmetric calculation, while still reproducing the experimental value within uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used the triaxial relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (TRHBc) to self-consistently calculate nuclear structure properties of ¹⁴⁹Lu and its daughter ¹⁴⁸Yb, including density distributions, potentials, and spectroscopic factors. They then incorporated these microscopic inputs into the WKB approximation to compute the proton-emission half-life, averaging the decay width over all emission angles (θ, φ). The density functional NL3* was employed, and results were compared with those from the axially symmetric deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state deformation of ¹⁴⁹Lu: β = 0.17, γ = 31°
- Ground-state deformation of ¹⁴⁸Yb (daughter): β = 0.17, γ = 35°
- Experimental half-life of ¹⁴⁹Lu: T₁/₂ = 450⁺¹⁷⁰₋₁₀₀ nanoseconds
- DRHBc + WKB predicted half-life: T₁/₂ = 438⁺¹³⁶₋₁₀₂ ns
- TRHBc + WKB predicted half-life: T₁/₂ = 504⁺¹⁶⁰₋₁₁₆ ns
- Spectroscopic factor S_F from DRHBc: 0.80; from TRHBc: 0.70
- Logarithm of decay width lgΓ from DRHBc: −14.86⁺⁰·¹⁴₋₀·₀₉; from TRHBc: −14.89⁺⁰·¹²₋₀·₁₂

## Context

This work extends previous studies of ¹⁴⁹Lu that used the axially symmetric DRHBc theory (Xiao 2023) and the nonadiabatic quasiparticle model (PhysRevLett.128.112501, 2022), both of which indicated possible coexistence of prolate and oblate shapes but did not consider triaxiality. The TRHBc theory (Zhang 2023) was recently developed to include triaxial deformation degrees of freedom, and this paper applies it for the first time to a proton emitter. The results enable future studies of triaxial effects on proton emission in other exotic nuclei near the dripline.
