# Influence of Impact Parameter on Thermal Description of Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions at GSI/SIS

> Attention is drawn to the role played by the size of the system in the thermodynamic analysis of particle yields in relativistic heavy ion collisions at SIS energies. This manifests itself in the non-linear dependence of K+ and K- yields in $AA$ collisions at 1 -- 2 A.GeV on the number of participants. It is shown that this dependence can be quantitatively well described in terms of a thermal model with a canonical strangeness conservation. The measured particle multiplicity ratios (pi+/p, pi-/pi+, d/p, K+/pi+ and K+/K- but not eta/pi0) in central Au-Au and Ni-Ni collisions at 0.8 -- 2.0 A.GeV are also explained in the context of a thermal model with a common freeze-out temperature and chemical potential. Including the concept of collective flow a consistent picture of particle energy distributions is derived with the flow velocity being strongly impact-parameter dependent.

## Key claim

The thermal model with canonical strangeness conservation can quantitatively describe the non-linear dependence of kaon yields on the number of participants in heavy ion collisions at SIS energies (1-2 A.GeV), but only when the system size is explicitly included as a parameter; the grand canonical ensemble overestimates the K+/pi+ ratio by more than an order of magnitude for central Au-Au collisions at 1 A.GeV.

## Method

The authors applied a thermal model with exact canonical treatment of strangeness conservation, using an ideal gas approximation with Boltzmann statistics including all particles and resonances from the Particle Data Group. The model has three independent parameters: temperature T, baryon chemical potential mu_B, and volume V (related to the number of participating nucleons via V ~ 1.9 pi A_part). Particle multiplicity ratios (pi+/p, pi-/pi+, d/p, K+/pi+, K+/K-) measured in central Au-Au and Ni-Ni collisions at 0.8-2.0 A.GeV were used to extract freeze-out parameters, and the impact parameter dependence was studied by comparing K+/pi+ and K+/A_part data from the KaoS collaboration with model predictions.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature T ~ 50 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B ~ 822 MeV extracted for central Au-Au collisions at 1 A.GeV
- Radius R ~ 6.2 fm needed to describe K+/pi+ ratio in central Au-Au at 1 A.GeV, corresponding to A_part ~ 330 participating nucleons
- Radius R ~ 4 fm for central Ni-Ni collisions, compatible with the smaller size of Ni
- Grand canonical ensemble would give K+/pi+ ~ 0.04 for Au-Au at 1 A.GeV, overestimating data by more than an order of magnitude
- Canonical suppression factor F_S can be larger than an order of magnitude at SIS energies
- K+ yield scales as A_part^alpha with alpha > 1, while pion yield is proportional to A_part
- Freeze-out temperature increases with incident energy E/A while mu_B shows opposite trend
- Theory and experiment for eta/pi0 ratio deviate by a factor of 2 to 8 depending on incident energy

## Context

This work extends the thermal model approach of Hagedorn (1960s) and the canonical strangeness conservation formalism developed by Redlich, Turko, and others to the GSI/SIS energy range, where system sizes are small and temperatures are low. The paper builds on observations by the KaoS collaboration (MIS94, barth) that K+ yields show a non-linear dependence on centrality. The results connect to the freeze-out curve in the (T-mu_B) plane established at AGS energies by Stachel, Elliot and at SPS energies by Sollfrank, Stock, and others, showing that SIS points lie on the same universal freeze-out curve where energy per hadron is approximately 1 GeV. The work enables systematic studies of impact parameter dependence in thermal models and raises open questions about eta meson production, which remains unexplained by the thermal approach.
