# Hadronization of Dense Partonic Matter

> The parton recombination model has turned out to be a valuable tool to describe hadronization in high energy heavy ion collisions. I review the model and revisit recent progress in our understanding of hadron correlations. I also discuss higher Fock states in the hadrons, possible violations of the elliptic flow scaling and recombination effects in more dilute systems.

# Hadronization of Dense Partonic Matter

## Key claim
The parton recombination model successfully describes hadronization in high energy heavy ion collisions, but its validity is restricted to intermediate transverse momentum (approximately 2–6 GeV/c) where thermal parton recombination dominates; at higher transverse momenta, fragmentation takes over, and the model's momentum-conserving formalism is only safely applied when mass effects are small (P_T >> M).

## Method
The author reviews the recombination model formalism, which computes hadron spectra by convolving two-particle Wigner functions for partons with the Wigner function of the hadron, typically approximating the parton Wigner function as a product of single-particle phase space distributions. The model is applied to central Au+Au collisions at RHIC by combining hadron production from recombination at intermediate transverse momentum with perturbative QCD calculations using fragmentation and energy loss in the medium. The author also examines extensions of the model to include parton correlations for dihadron yields and higher Fock states for elliptic flow scaling violations.

## Key numbers
- Hadron spectra from recombination of thermal quarks dominate up to about 4 GeV/c for mesons and up to about 6 GeV/c for baryons in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV
- Elliptic flow scaling law: v_2(P_T) = n v_2^p(P_T/n), where n is the number of valence quarks (2 for mesons, 3 for baryons)
- Energy loss estimated at up to 14 GeV/fm for a 10 GeV parton
- Correlation enhancement factor Q = n_A * n_B, giving Q=4 for meson-meson pairs, Q=6 for meson-baryon pairs, and Q=9 for baryon-baryon pairs
- Scaling violations from higher Fock states are smaller than 5% for realistic wave functions with up to 50% probability of an additional parton
- Kinematic windows for correlation analysis: trigger particles 2.5–4.0 GeV/c, associated particles 1.7–2.5 GeV/c, with |y| < 0.35

## Context
This review extends the recombination model work of Fries, Muller, Nonaka, and Bass (2003) and the independent formulations by Greco, Ko, and Levai (2003) and Hwa and Yang (2002). The model was developed to explain RHIC data that fragmentation alone could not describe, particularly the large baryon/meson ratio observed by PHENIX (2003), nuclear modification factors near unity from PHENIX and STAR (2003), and the quark number scaling of elliptic flow from PHENIX and STAR (2003). The work enables future investigations into gluon recombination mechanisms, soft-hard recombination in dilute systems like d+Au collisions, and more detailed studies of dihadron correlations that go beyond the factorization approximation used for single-particle spectra.
