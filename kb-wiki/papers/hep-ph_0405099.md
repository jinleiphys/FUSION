# New results on the limit for the width of the exotic Theta^+ resonance

> We investigate the impact of the Θ^+(1540) resonance on differential and integrated cross sections for the reaction K^+d{\to}K^0pp, where experimental information is available at kaon momenta below 640 MeV/c. The calculation utilizes the Jülich KN model and extensions of it that include contributions from a Θ^+(1540) state with different widths. The evaluation of the reaction K^+d{\to}K^0pp takes into account effects due to the Fermi motion of the nucleons within the deuteron and the final three-body kinematics. We conclude that the available data constrain the width of the Θ^+(1540) to be less than 1 MeV.

## Key claim

The available data on the reaction \(K^+d \to K^0pp\) at kaon momenta below 640 MeV/c constrain the width of the \(\Theta^+(1540)\) resonance to be less than 1 MeV, but this conclusion depends critically on two specific data sets (at 434 MeV/c and 470 MeV/c); if those data are excluded, a width of about 5 MeV could be accommodated without increasing the \(\chi^2\)/dof by more than 10%.

## Method

The authors used the Jülich meson-exchange model for the \(KN\) interaction and added a \(\Theta^+(1540)\) resonance with spin and parity \(1/2^+\) at 1540 MeV, with widths of 1, 5, 10, and 20 MeV. They calculated differential and integrated cross sections for the reaction \(K^+d \to K^0pp\) by integrating the elementary \(KN\) amplitude over the full three-body phase space, including Fermi motion of nucleons in the deuteron (using the CD-Bonn wave function) and final-state kinematics, and compared these calculations to experimental data at kaon momenta from 252 to 640 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The \(\chi^2\)/dof for the full data set (138 points) is 1.8 for no \(\Theta^+\) (width 0 MeV), 7.4 for \(\Gamma_\Theta = 5\) MeV, 27.7 for \(\Gamma_\Theta = 10\) MeV, and 42.2 for \(\Gamma_\Theta = 20\) MeV.
- When the data at \(k_0 = 434\) MeV/c (Damerell) and \(k_0 = 470\) MeV/c (Glasser) are excluded, the \(\chi^2\)/dof becomes 1.4 for no \(\Theta^+\), 1.7 for \(\Gamma_\Theta = 5\) MeV, 2.4 for \(\Gamma_\Theta = 10\) MeV, and 4.8 for \(\Gamma_\Theta = 20\) MeV.
- The \(\Theta^+\) resonance mass is assumed to be 1540 MeV; the experimental masses reported by other collaborations range from 1521 to 1555 MeV.
- The incident kaon momentum range relevant for the \(\Theta^+\) is 417 to 476 MeV/c.
- The total \(K^+d \to K^0pp\) cross section data show that widths larger than 1 MeV are incompatible with the data; widths of 1 MeV or less can be accommodated.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Haidenbauer (2004) by performing a direct calculation of the \(K^+d \to K^0pp\) reaction rather than relying on extracted \(KN\) cross sections. It contradicts the earlier rough estimate by Nussinov (2004) of an upper limit of 6 MeV and refines the limits from Cahn (2004) (1.1 MeV) and Gibbs (2004) (0.9 MeV) by using a more rigorous treatment of Fermi motion and three-body kinematics. The result enables future searches for the \(\Theta^+\) to focus on resonances with widths below 1 MeV and provides a methodology for extracting resonance parameters from deuteron breakup reactions.
