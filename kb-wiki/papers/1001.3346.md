# Color-magnetic flux tubes in quark matter cores of neutron stars

> We argue that if color-superconducting quark matter exists in the core of a neutron star, it may contain a high density of flux tubes, carrying flux that is mostly color-magnetic, with a small admixture of ordinary magnetic flux. We focus on the two-flavor color-superconducting ("2SC") phase, and assume that the flux tubes are energetically stable, although this has not yet been demonstrated. The density of flux tubes depends on the nature of the transition to the color-superconducting phase, and could be within an order of magnitude of the density of magnetic flux tubes that would be found if the core were superconducting nuclear matter. We calculate the cross-section for Aharonov-Bohm scattering of gapless fermions off the flux tubes, and the associated collision time and frictional force on a moving flux tube. We discuss the other forces on the flux tube, and find that if we take in to account only the forces that arise within the 2SC core region then the timescale for expulsion of the color flux tubes from the 2SC core is of order 10^10 years.

## Key claim

If color-superconducting quark matter exists in neutron star cores, it may contain a high density of color-magnetic flux tubes, but this depends on the assumption that such flux tubes are energetically stable (which has not yet been demonstrated) and on the nature of the transition to the color-superconducting phase.

## Method

The authors calculated the Ginzburg-Landau parameter for two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) quark matter to determine whether it is a type-II superconductor with respect to the broken "X" gauge symmetry. They then computed the Aharonov-Bohm scattering cross-section for gapless fermions (electrons, muons, and unpaired blue quarks) scattering off color-magnetic flux tubes, and used the Boltzmann kinetic equation to derive the associated collision time and frictional force on a moving flux tube. The expulsion timescale for flux tubes from the 2SC core was estimated by considering forces within the core region only.

## Key numbers

* The 2SC phase is type-II if the pairing gap satisfies Δ ≳ μ_q/16; for typical quark chemical potentials μ_q ∼ 400 MeV, this requires Δ ≳ 25 MeV.
* The Ginzburg-Landau parameter for 2SC quark matter is κ_2SC ≈ 11 Δ/μ_q.
* The maximum flux tube density for an internal field B = 10^14 Gauss is n_v = 8.1 × 10^19 cm^−2.
* The flux tube tension is of order 60 to 130 MeV/fm for μ_q in the 350 to 500 MeV range.
* The timescale for expulsion of color flux tubes from the 2SC core, considering only forces within the core region, is of order 10^10 years.

## Context

This work extends the study of color-superconducting quark matter in neutron stars, building on prior analyses of the 2SC phase structure by Alford (2007) and of type-II color superconductivity by Iida (2002) and Giannakis (2003). The paper investigates the possibility that color-magnetic flux tubes analogous to "Z-strings" in the standard model (Vachaspati, 1992) could exist in 2SC quark matter, and calculates their transport and dynamical properties. This enables future work on the observational signatures of quark matter in neutron stars through the effects of flux tubes on thermal and magnetic evolution.
