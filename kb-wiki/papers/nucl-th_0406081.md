# From Solar Proton Burning to Pionic Deuterium through the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei

> Within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei (the NNJL model), describing strong low-energy nuclear interactions, we compute the width of the energy level of the ground state of pionic deuterium. The theoretical value fits well the experimental data. Using the cross sections for the reactions nu_e + d -> p + p + e^- and nu_e + d -> p + n + nu_e, computed in the NNJL model, and the experimental values of the events of these reactions, detected by the SNO Collaboration, we compute the boron neutrino fluxes. The theoretical values agree well with the experimental data and the theoretical predictions within the Standard Solar Model by Bahcall. We argue the applicability of the constraints on the astrophysical factor for the solar proton burning, imposed by helioseismology, to the width of the energy level of the ground state of pionic deuterium. We show that the experimental data on the width satisfy these constraints. This testifies an indirect measurement of the recommended value of the astrophysical factor for the solar proton burning in terrestrial laboratories in terms of the width of the energy level of the ground state of pionic deuterium.

## Key claim

Within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model of light nuclei (NNJL model), the width of the ground state of pionic deuterium is computed and agrees with experimental data; the same model links this width to the astrophysical factor for solar proton burning, allowing helioseismological constraints on that factor to be applied to the pionic deuterium width, provided the NNJL model's description of low-energy nuclear interactions is valid.

## Method

The authors compute the width of the 1s state of pionic deuterium using the Deser-Goldberger-Baumann-Thirring (DGBT) formula, evaluating the imaginary part of the S-wave π⁻d scattering amplitude near threshold from the two dominant channels: π⁻d → nnγ and π⁻d → nn. The matrix elements for these transitions are calculated within the NNJL model, using the anomaly of the one-nucleon loop diagram that also governs the solar proton burning reaction. Separately, the cross sections for νₑ + d → p + p + e⁻ and νₑ + d → p + n + νₑ, computed in the NNJL model, are averaged over the ⁸B solar neutrino spectrum and combined with SNO event rates to extract ⁸B solar neutrino fluxes.

## Key numbers

- Total width of the ground state of pionic deuterium: Γ₁ₛ = (1.15 ± 0.12) eV (theoretical, NNJL model).
- Partial width for π⁻d → nnγ: Γ₁ₛ^(nnγ) = (0.30 ± 0.04) eV.
- Partial width for π⁻d → nn: Γ₁ₛ^(nn) = (0.85 ± 0.11) eV.
- Ratio D = Γ₁ₛ^(nn) / Γ₁ₛ^(nnγ) = 2.83 ± 0.50 (theoretical), compared to experimental D = 2.83 ± 0.04.
- Astrophysical factor for solar proton burning: S_pp(0) = 4.08 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV·b (NNJL model), compared to the Standard Solar Model recommended value S_pp^SSM(0) = 4.00 × 10⁻²⁵ MeV·b.
- ⁸B solar neutrino flux from neutral-current reaction: φ(⁸B)_NC = (6.15 ± 1.01) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ (NNJL model), compared to SNO measurement φ^SNO_NC(⁸B) = (4.90 ± 0.38) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ and SSM prediction φ^SSM(⁸B) = (5.82 ± 1.34) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹.
- ⁸B solar neutrino flux from charged-current reaction: φ(⁸B)_CC = (2.33 ± 0.38) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹ (NNJL model), compared to SNO measurement φ^SNO_CC(⁸B) = (1.70 ± 0.12) × 10⁶ cm⁻² s⁻¹.
- Helioseismological constraint on the astrophysical factor: 0.94 ≤ S_pp(0) / S_pp^SSM(0) ≤ 1.18, translated to pionic deuterium width: (1.08 ± 0.11) eV ≤ Γ₁ₛ ≤ (1.36 ± 0.14) eV.

## Context

This work extends the NNJL model, previously applied to deuteron properties and low-energy astrophysical reactions (Ivanov et al., earlier papers), to the exotic atom pionic deuterium. The computed width agrees with experimental data from the PSI and TRIUMF collaborations (PD1, PD2, PD3). The link between the pionic deuterium width and the solar proton burning astrophysical factor S_pp(0) is established through the common one-nucleon loop anomaly in the NNJL model. The analysis of SNO data on ⁸B solar neutrino fluxes confirms the model's reliability for low-energy deuteron reactions. The helioseismological constraints on S_pp(0) from Degl'Innocenti, Fiorentini, and Ricci (1998) are applied to the pionic deuterium width, suggesting that terrestrial measurements of this width can serve as an indirect probe of the solar proton burning rate.
