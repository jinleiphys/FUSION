# Holographic charm and bottom pentaquarks III: Excitations through photo-production of heavy mesons

> We consider the photo-excitation of charm and bottom pentaquarks with the holographic assignments $[\frac 12\frac 12^-]_{S=0,1}$ and $[\frac 12\frac 32^-]_{S=1}$, in the photo-production of heavy vector mesons such as charmonia and bottomonia near threshold. We use a Witten diagram to combine the s-channel photo-excitation of holographic pentaquarks with a massive t-channel graviton or tensor glueball exchange, to extract the scattering amplitude for heavy meson photo-production in the threshold region. The pentaquark signal is too weak to be detected at current electron facilities.

## Key claim

The holographic pentaquark signal in photo-production of charmonium and bottomonium near threshold is too weak to be detected at current electron facilities, even though the three predicted charm pentaquark states (with specific spin-isospin-parity assignments) produce resonant enhancements in the s-channel that are in principle distinguishable from the dominant t-channel graviton or tensor glueball exchange.

## Method

The authors constructed a Witten diagram combining s-channel photo-excitation of holographic pentaquarks (with assignments [1/2 1/2^-]_{S=0,1} and [1/2 3/2^-]_{S=1}) with a massive t-channel graviton or tensor glueball exchange to extract the scattering amplitude for heavy meson photo-production in the threshold region. They used the soft-wall holographic QCD model to compute the transition form factors for the processes gamma + p -> X and X -> V + p, fixing the Pauli coupling constants using theoretically computed partial decay widths from the Sakai-Sugimoto model. The differential and total cross sections were evaluated numerically for both charm and bottom pentaquark cases.

## Key numbers

- Charm pentaquark masses fixed to LHCb values: m = 4311.9 +/- 0.7 MeV (Gamma = 9.8 +/- 2.7 MeV), m = 4440.3 +/- 1.3 MeV (Gamma = 20.6 +/- 4.9 MeV), m = 4457.3 +/- 0.6 MeV (Gamma = 6.4 +/- 2.0 MeV)
- Predicted bottom pentaquark masses: m = 11155 MeV (Gamma = 71 +/- 18 MeV), m = 11163 MeV (Gamma = 43 +/- 11 MeV), m = 11167 MeV (Gamma = 33 +/- 8 MeV)
- Transition magnetic moment for charm pentaquark at 4440 MeV: mu_X/mu_N ~ 0.78, with Pauli coupling |eta_4440| = 0.134
- Pauli couplings for charm pentaquarks: eta_X(4312) = 0.044 +/- 0.005, eta_X(4440) = 0.134 +/- 0.017, eta_X(4457) = 0.099 +/- 0.012
- Photo-decay widths: Gamma_{Xgamma}(4312) = (3.2 +/- 0.8) x 10^{-8} GeV, Gamma_{Xgamma}(4440) = (1.05 +/- 0.26) x 10^{-7} GeV, Gamma_{Xgamma}(4457) approximately 0
- Branching ratios for X -> J/Psi + p: Br(4312) = 0.3%, Br(4440) = 1.8%, Br(4457) = 0.9%
- Soft-wall parameters: 4 kappa_N^2 = 4 kappa_rho^2 = m_rho^2, tau_N = 1 + m_N^2/m_rho^2 = 2.465, kappa_V = kappa_{J/Psi} = 1.03784 GeV

## Context

This work extends the holographic pentaquark construction by Liu and Zahed (2017) and the subsequent analysis of strong two-body decay channels by Liu et al. (2021), applying it to photo-production processes currently pursued at JLab (Meziani, 2016). The holographic approach contrasts with molecular constructions by Chen (2015), Rossi (2016), Eides (2017), and Guo (2019), and with solitonic molecules by Scoccola (2015) and string-based molecules by Sonnenschein (2016). The results enable experimental searches at JLab and future facilities to distinguish between holographic and molecular pentaquark interpretations, though the predicted signals require significantly higher luminosity than currently available.
