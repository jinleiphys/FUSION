# Solving the three-body bound-state Bethe-Salpeter equation in Minkowski space

> The scalar three-body Bethe-Salpeter equation, with zero-range interaction, is solved in Minkowski space by direct integration of the four-dimensional integral equation. The singularities appearing in the propagators are treated properly by standard analytical and numerical methods, without relying on any ansatz or assumption. The results for the binding energies and transverse amplitudes are compared with the results computed in Euclidean space. A fair agreement between the calculations is found.

## Key claim

The three-body Bethe-Salpeter equation for scalar bosons with zero-range interaction is solved directly in Minkowski space by numerical integration, without relying on any ansatz or three-dimensional reduction; the eigenvalues obtained are close to unity but acquire small imaginary parts (up to about 10%) due to numerical truncation of the integration domain and finite precision.

## Method

The Faddeev component of the vertex function for three equal-mass scalar bosons interacting via a zero-range contact force is obtained by solving the four-dimensional integral equation in the center-of-mass frame. The pole singularities in the propagators are treated by a principal-value subtraction technique, and the logarithmic singularities in the angular-integrated kernel are handled by standard numerical integration. The resulting non-singular integral equation is solved by a spline decomposition of the vertex function, using the three-body binding energies and scattering lengths from prior Euclidean-space calculations as inputs.

## Key numbers

- For binding energy \(B_3/m = 0.006\) and scattering length \(am = -1.280\), the eigenvalue is \(\lambda = 0.999 - 0.054i\).
- For \(B_3/m = 0.395\) and \(am = -1.500\), the eigenvalue is \(\lambda = 1.000 + 0.002i\).
- For \(B_3/m = 1.001\) and \(am = -1.705\), the eigenvalue is \(\lambda = 0.997 + 0.106i\).
- The integration domain for the spectator momentum was truncated at \(q_v^{\text{max}}/m = 6.0\) for all cases; for \(B_3/m = 0.006\) and \(0.395\) the time-component cutoff was \(q_0^{\text{max}}/m = 13.0\), and for \(B_3/m = 1.001\) it was \(q_0^{\text{max}}/m = 15.0\).

## Context

This work extends the direct Minkowski-space integration method previously applied to two-body systems (Carbonell and Karmanov, 2015) to the three-body case, which had only been solved in Euclidean space (Ydrefors et al., 2018) or via light-front projection (Tobias et al., 2014; Carbonell and Karmanov, 2013). The Minkowski-space solution provides the physical Bethe-Salpeter amplitude needed for computing observables such as parton distribution functions and electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region, which are inaccessible from Euclidean-space calculations. The authors note that extending the Nakanishi integral representation approach to three-body systems is a work in progress.
