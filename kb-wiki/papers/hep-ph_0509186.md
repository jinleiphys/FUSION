# Manifestly Lorentz-Invariant Baryon Chiral Perturbation Theory

> A successful effective field theory program requires besides the most general effective Lagrangian a perturbative expansion scheme for observables in terms of a consistent power counting method. We discuss a renormalization scheme for manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory generating a simple and consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams. The approach may be used in an iterative procedure to renormalize higher-order loop diagrams and also allows for implementing a consistent power counting when vector mesons are explicitly included.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a consistent power counting for renormalized diagrams in manifestly Lorentz-invariant baryon chiral perturbation theory can be achieved through a renormalization scheme that subtracts terms violating power counting, provided those terms are analytic in small quantities and can be absorbed into counterterms.

## Method

The author compares two renormalization schemes: infrared regularization (Becher and Leutwyler 1999), which splits Feynman-parameter integrals into infrared and regular parts, and the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme (Gegelia 1999, Fuchs 2003), which performs additional subtractions beyond the MS-bar scheme by expanding the integrand in small quantities and subtracting terms whose order is smaller than the power counting suggests. Both schemes are illustrated using a one-loop integral relevant to the nucleon self-energy, and their application to multiloop diagrams is demonstrated with a two-loop self-energy toy model (Schindler 2003).

## Key numbers

- The one-loop contribution to the nucleon self-energy in the MS-bar scheme yields a result of order \( q^2 \), which violates the desired power counting of \( D = n-1 \) (where \( n \) is the number of dimensions and \( q \) is a small expansion parameter such as the pion mass).
- In the EOMS scheme, the renormalized integral \( H^R = H - H^{\text{subtr}} \) is of order \( q \) as \( n \to 4 \), consistent with the power counting expectation of \( D = n-3 \).
- The EOMS scheme predicts the mass difference \( M_{\rho^0} - M_{\rho^\pm} \sim 1 \) MeV at tree order from consistency conditions for the QED of vector mesons.

## Context

This work extends the manifestly Lorentz-invariant approach to baryon chiral perturbation theory, which had previously been thought to lack systematic power counting when using the MS-bar scheme (Gasser 1983, Gasser 1987). The EOMS scheme has been applied to calculations of the nucleon mass, the pion-nucleon sigma term, the scalar form factor (Fuchs 2003), ground-state baryon octet masses (Lehnhart 2004), and nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Fuchs 2003, Schindler 2005). The approach enables consistent inclusion of vector mesons (Fuchs 2003) and the Delta(1232) resonance (Hacker 2005) as explicit degrees of freedom, and has been used to derive consistency conditions among renormalized parameters of the effective Lagrangian (Djukanovic 2004).
