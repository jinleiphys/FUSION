# Nucleon resonances in polarized omega photoproduction

> The role of the nucleon resonances ($N^*$) in $ω$ photoproduction is investigated by using the resonance parameters predicted by Capstick and Roberts. The contributions from the nucleon resonances are found to be significant in various spin asymmetries. In particular, we found that a crucial test of our predictions can be made by measuring the parity asymmetry and beam-target double asymmetry at forward scattering angles.

## Key claim

The authors predict that measuring the parity asymmetry and beam-target double asymmetry at forward scattering angles can provide a crucial test of their quark-model-based resonance parameters, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that the non-resonant background (particularly one-pion exchange) is well understood and that the dominant resonance contributions come specifically from the \(N\frac32^+(1910)\) and \(N\frac32^-(1960)\) states.

## Method

The authors construct a model for \(\gamma p \to p\omega\) by combining a non-resonant background amplitude (Pomeron exchange via the Donnachie-Landshoff model, pseudoscalar-meson exchange, and direct/crossed nucleon terms) with resonant amplitudes from 22 nucleon resonances (12 positive parity, 10 negative parity, up to spin-9/2). The resonance photo-excitation and decay amplitudes are taken from the relativised quark model predictions of Capstick and Roberts (1992, 1994), and the total decay widths are approximated as 300 MeV based on Particle Data Group averages.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections are calculated at \(E_\gamma = 1.68\) GeV and found to describe data for \(E_\gamma \leq 5\) GeV.
- The cut-off parameters for pseudoscalar-meson exchange are \(\Lambda_\pi = 0.6\) GeV and \(\Lambda_{\omega\gamma\pi} = 0.7\) GeV.
- The \(\omega NN\) coupling is taken as \(g_{\omega NN} = 10.35\) with \(\kappa_\omega = 0\), and the nucleon form factor cut-off is \(\Lambda_N = 0.5\) GeV.
- The two dominant resonances are \(N\frac32^+(1910)\) (a missing resonance) and \(N\frac32^-(1960)\) (identified as the two-star \(D_{13}(2080)\)), contributing most strongly in the invariant mass range \(W = 1.79 \sim 2.12\) GeV.
- The parity asymmetry \(P_\sigma\) at \(\theta = 0\) is predicted to deviate significantly from the one-pion-exchange value of \(-1\) when resonances are included.
- The beam-target double asymmetry \(C^{BT}_{zz}\) at \(\theta = 0\) shows a striking difference between calculations with and without \(N^*\) contributions.

## Context

This work extends the study of nucleon resonances in vector meson photoproduction by Zhao et al. (1998, 1999), who used an SU(6) x O(3) constituent quark model and found single polarization observables sensitive to resonances. The present paper uses the different quark model predictions of Capstick and Roberts (1992, 1994), which include configuration mixing and the \({}^3P_0\) model for meson decays, and finds that the dominant resonances differ (e.g., \(N\frac32^+(1910)\) and \(N\frac32^-(1960)\) instead of \(F_{15}(2000)\)). The results enable experimental tests at facilities such as LEPS (SPring-8), TJNAF, ELSA-SAPHIR, and GRAAL to help resolve the "missing resonance problem" by distinguishing between quark model predictions.
