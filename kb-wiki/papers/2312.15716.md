# Isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-Delta elastic cross section

> In heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, the production and propagation of $Δ$ particles are crucial to understanding the nuclear equation of state and inferring the properties of nuclear matter at high densities. Based on the self-consistent relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport theory, by introducing the isovector $ρ$ meson exchange into the effective Lagrangian and adopting the density-dependent coupling constants, the detailed expressions for isospin-dependent in-medium $NΔ\rightarrow NΔ$ elastic cross sections $σ_{NΔ}^{*}$ have been calculated. The energy and density dependence of the isospin-related $σ_{NΔ}^{*}$ as well as the total contributions of $σ$, $ω$ and $ρ$ meson fields are analyzed. It is found that the total $σ_{NΔ}^{*}$ has a sensitive center-of-mass energy dependence at lower energies while exhibiting a slight increase as the center-of-mass energy increases. The isospin effect between different isospin-separated channels weakens as the energy and/or density increases. The isospin effect on the density- and energy-dependent in-medium $NΔ$ elastic cross sections is dominantly caused by the delicate balance of the isovector $ρ$ meson exchange.

## Key claim

The paper calculates isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-Delta elastic cross sections using density-dependent coupling constants (DD-ME2 parameter set) within the self-consistent relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport theory, finding that the isospin effect weakens as energy and/or density increases and is dominantly caused by the delicate balance of the isovector rho meson exchange.

## Method

The authors extended the RBUU transport framework by introducing isovector rho meson exchange into the effective Lagrangian alongside sigma and omega mesons, adopting density-dependent coupling constants from the DD-ME2 parameter set (Lalazissis, 2005). They derived the detailed expressions for the in-medium NDelta to NDelta elastic cross sections by computing the transition probability from the Born terms, including contributions from sigma-sigma, sigma-omega, omega-omega, rho-rho, sigma-rho, and omega-rho meson exchange channels, with isospin matrices tabulated for individual reaction channels. The effective masses of nucleon and Delta were determined from the mean-field sigma field, and a phenomenological form factor with meson-dependent cutoff masses (Lambda_sigma=1200 MeV, Lambda_omega=808 MeV, Lambda_rho=800 MeV) was introduced at each vertex.

## Key numbers

* At saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3) and center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 2.5 GeV, the total in-medium NDelta elastic cross section is 13.07 mb with density-dependent coupling constants, compared to 54.76 mb from previous work (Mao, 1996) using constant coupling constants.
* The coupling constant ratios adopted are: chi_sigma = 1.0, chi_omega = 0.8, chi_rho = 0.7 for the Delta-to-nucleon coupling ratios.
* The rho meson exchange contribution to the total cross section approaches zero at 2 to 3 times saturation density.
* The canonical momenta correction from omega and rho mesons has a relatively weak effect on the cross section, especially at center-of-mass energies above 2.3 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous RBUU-based calculations of in-medium nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections (Li, 2000) and inelastic NN to NDelta cross sections (Li, 2016; Li, 2017) by now computing the isospin-dependent NDelta elastic cross section with density-dependent coupling constants, improving upon the constant-coupling approach of Mao (1996). The results are intended for implementation in transport models such as the UrQMD model, where NDelta elastic scattering accounts for about 5% of all collisions in central Au+Au collisions at 1A GeV beam energy at 25 fm/c (Liu, 2020), and may help improve the description of pion yields from HADES Collaboration measurements at 1.23A GeV.
