# Chiral dynamics in the gamma p --> pi^0 eta p and gamma p --> pi^0 K^0 Sigma^+ reactions

> Using a chiral unitary approach for meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness zero sector, where the $N^*(1535)$ resonance is dynamically generated, we study the reactions $γp \to π^0 ηp$ and $γp \to π^0 K^0 Σ^+$ at photon energies at which the final states are produced close to threshold. Among several reaction mechanisms, we find the most important is the excitation of the $Δ^*(1700)$ state which subsequently decays into a pseudoscalar meson and a baryon belonging to the $Δ(1232)$ decuplet. Hence, the reaction provides useful information with which to test current theories of the dynamical generation of the low-lying $3/2^-$ states. The first reaction is shown to lead to sizable cross sections and the $N^*(1535)$ resonance shape is seen clearly in the $ηp$ invariant mass distribution. The same dynamical model is shown to lead to much smaller cross sections at low energies in the second reaction. Predictions are made for cross sections and invariant mass distributions which can be compared with forthcoming experiments at ELSA.

## Key claim

The reactions \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 \eta p\) and \(\gamma p \to \pi^0 K^0 \Sigma^+\) are studied using a chiral unitary approach where the \(N^*(1535)\) resonance is dynamically generated, and the most important reaction mechanism is found to be the excitation of the \(\Delta^*(1700)\) state which subsequently decays into a pseudoscalar meson and a baryon belonging to the \(\Delta(1232)\) decuplet; the first reaction yields sizable cross sections while the second leads to much smaller cross sections at low energies, providing a test of theories of dynamical generation of low-lying \(3/2^-\) states.

## Method

The authors use a chiral unitary approach for meson-baryon scattering in the strangeness zero sector, where the \(N^*(1535)S_{11}\) resonance is dynamically generated via the Bethe-Salpeter equation with a kernel from the lowest-order chiral Lagrangian. For the photoproduction reactions, they include mechanisms such as the Kroll-Ruderman term, meson pole terms, and contributions from explicit baryonic resonances including \(\Delta(1232)P_{33}\), \(N^*(1520)D_{13}\), and \(\Delta^*(1700)D_{33}\), with the final state interaction unitarized through coupled channels. The model is first tested against \(\gamma p \to \eta p\) data and then extended to predict observables for the two-meson final states.

## Key numbers

* The cut-off for the loop integrals is chosen as \(\Lambda = 1400\) MeV, fixed to reproduce the maximum of the \(\gamma p \to \eta p\) total cross section data.
* The \(\pi N \to \pi\pi N\) channel increases the width of the \(N^*(1535)\) by about 10% and changes its position by about 10 MeV.
* The contribution of the magnetic moment diagram (b) to the \(\gamma p \to \eta p\) amplitude changes \(|t|^2\) by 6% when added coherently to the Kroll-Ruderman term, and by about 2% when the \(\pi^0 p\) intermediate state is included.
* The coefficients for the anomalous magnetic moment Lagrangian are \(b_6^D = 2.40\) and \(b_6^F = 1.82\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach previously applied to the \(\gamma p \to K^+ \pi \Sigma\) reaction (Nacher, 1998) where the \(\Lambda(1405)\) resonance was studied, and builds on the Valencia model for two-meson photoproduction (Gomez Tejedor, 1993; Nacher, 2000). The study enables predictions for cross sections and invariant mass distributions that can be compared with ongoing experiments at ELSA (Metag), and tests the dynamical generation of the \(\Delta^*(1700)\) resonance as proposed by Kolomeitsev (2003) and Sarkar (2004).
