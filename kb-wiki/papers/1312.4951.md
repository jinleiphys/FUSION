# Accurate evaluation of hadronic uncertainties in spin-independent WIMP-nucleon scattering: Disentangling two- and three-flavor effects

> We show how to avoid unnecessary and uncontrolled assumptions usually made in the literature about soft SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking in determining the two-flavor nucleon matrix elements relevant for direct detection of WIMPs. Based on SU(2) Chiral Perturbation Theory, we provide expressions for the proton and neutron scalar couplings $f_u^{p,n}$ and $f_d^{p,n}$ with the pion-nucleon sigma-term as the only free parameter, which should be used in the analysis of direct detection experiments. This approach for the first time allows for an accurate assessment of hadronic uncertainties in spin-independent WIMP-nucleon scattering and for a reliable calculation of isospin-violating effects. We find that the traditional determinations of $f_u^p-f_u^n$ and $f_d^p-f_d^n$ are off by a factor of 2.

## Key claim

The paper shows that traditional determinations of the proton and neutron scalar couplings \(f_u^{p,n}\) and \(f_d^{p,n}\) using soft SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking are off by a factor of 2 for the isospin-violating differences \(f_u^p - f_u^n\) and \(f_d^p - f_d^n\), and that a rigorous SU(2) Chiral Perturbation Theory approach avoids uncontrolled assumptions about strangeness input, allowing for reliable uncertainty estimates.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for the scalar couplings \(f_u^{p,n}\) and \(f_d^{p,n}\) from SU(2) Chiral Perturbation Theory using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem applied to the chiral expansion of the nucleon mass, with the pion-nucleon sigma-term \(\sigma_{\pi N}\) as the only free parameter. They incorporate isospin-violating effects via the quark mass ratio \(\mu_u/\md = 0.47 \pm 0.04\) and the electromagnetic proton-neutron mass difference, and compare their results to the traditional approach that reconstructs \(f_u^{p,n}\) and \(f_d^{p,n}\) from the strangeness content \(y\) and the quantity \(z\).

## Key numbers

- The quark mass ratio is \(\mu_u/\md = 0.47 \pm 0.04\), giving \(\xi = (\md - \mu_u)/(\md + \mu_u) = 0.36 \pm 0.04\).
- The electromagnetic contribution to the proton-neutron mass difference is \((m_p - m_n)^\text{em} = (0.76 \pm 0.3)\ \text{MeV}\), leading to \(B c_5(\md - \mu_u) = (-0.51 \pm 0.08)\ \text{MeV}\).
- The isospin-violating differences are \(f_u^p - f_u^n = (1.9 \pm 0.4) \cdot 10^{-3}\) and \(f_d^p - f_d^n = (-4.1 \pm 0.7) \cdot 10^{-3}\), compared to the traditional estimate of \(4.3 \cdot 10^{-3}\) and \(-8.2 \cdot 10^{-3}\) from Belanger (2013), showing a factor of 2 discrepancy.
- The strangeness coupling from lattice QCD is \(f_s^N = 0.043 \pm 0.011\).
- The shift terms from isospin violation are \(\Delta f_u^p = (1.0 \pm 0.2) \cdot 10^{-3}\), \(\Delta f_u^n = (-1.0 \pm 0.2) \cdot 10^{-3}\), \(\Delta f_d^p = (-2.1 \pm 0.4) \cdot 10^{-3}\), and \(\Delta f_d^n = (2.0 \pm 0.4) \cdot 10^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the use of SU(2) Chiral Perturbation Theory to the determination of scalar couplings for WIMP-nucleon scattering, correcting the traditional approach based on SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking from Cheng (1988) and Ellis (2000) that used the quantity \(z \approx 1.49\) without reliable uncertainty estimates. The results enable accurate assessment of hadronic uncertainties in spin-independent direct detection and reliable calculation of isospin-violating effects, which is crucial for interpreting tensions between experiments such as DAMA, CoGeNT, CRESST, CDMS II, XENON, and LUX. The framework also clarifies the role of the pion-nucleon sigma-term \(\sigma_{\pi N}\), whose precise determination remains an open issue requiring improved analyses of \(\pi N\) scattering and lattice QCD calculations.
