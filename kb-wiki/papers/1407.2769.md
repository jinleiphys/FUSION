# Multiple chiral doublet bands of identical configuration in 103Rh

> Three sets of chiral doublet band structures have been identified in the 103Rh nucleus. The properties of the observed chiral doublet bands are in good agreement with theoretical results obtained using constrained covariant density functional theory and particle rotor model calculations. Two of them belong to an identical configuration, and provide the first experimental evidence for a novel type of multiple chiral doublets, where an "excited" chiral doublet of a configuration is seen together with the "yrast" one. This observation shows that the chiral geometry in nuclei can be robust against the increase of the intrinsic excitation energy.

# Multiple chiral doublet bands of identical configuration in 103Rh

## Key claim

The paper reports the first experimental evidence for multiple chiral doublet bands (MχD) that belong to the same configuration in a single nucleus, specifically in 103Rh. This demonstrates that chiral geometry in nuclei can be robust against an increase in intrinsic excitation energy, as the "excited" chiral doublet of a configuration is observed together with the "yrast" one.

## Method

Medium- and high-spin states of 103Rh were populated using the 96Zr(11B,4n) fusion-evaporation reaction at 40 MeV beam energy, with gamma rays detected by the Gammasphere spectrometer. The data were analyzed using coincidence relations and relative intensities to construct the level scheme, with spin assignments from directional correlation of oriented states (DCO) measurements. The experimental results were compared with theoretical calculations using constrained covariant density functional theory (CDFT), tilted axis cranking CDFT (TAC-CDFT), and quantum particle rotor model (PRM) calculations.

## Key numbers

- Quadrupole deformation of the ground state of 103Rh: β = 0.25 with triaxiality parameter γ = 20°
- For the positive-parity configuration π(g9/2)⁻¹⊗ν(h11/2)²: β = 0.29, γ = 11.0°
- For the negative-parity configuration π(g9/2)⁻¹⊗ν(h11/2)¹(2d5/2)¹: β = 0.27, γ = 18.7°
- For the negative-parity configuration π(g9/2)⁻¹⊗ν(h11/2)¹(1g7/2)⁻¹: β = 0.26, γ = 14.5°
- Energy separation between positive-parity chiral bands 1 and 2: approximately 500 keV at I = 29/2 ħ, decreasing to approximately 360 keV at I = 39/2 ħ
- Energy differences between same-spin levels: bands 3 and 4 about 300 keV, bands 6 and 5 about 100 keV
- Alignment value approximately 9 ħ for all four negative-parity bands (bands 3-6) over a wide rotational frequency interval
- DCO ratio of 0.93(11) for the 674 keV transition and 1.07(4) for the 1007 keV transition linking band 5 to band B
- DCO ratio of 0.89(18) for the 574 keV transition and 0.88(19) for the 700 keV transition linking band 4 to band 6
- DCO ratios of 0.55(2) and 0.54(13) for the 396 and 391 keV M1 transitions linking band 4 to band 3 and band 5 to band 6, respectively
- PRM moment of inertia: J₀ = 23 ħ²/MeV for positive-parity bands, J₀ = 25 ħ²/MeV for negative-parity bands
- Coriolis attenuation factor ξ = 0.85 used for negative-parity bands

## Context

This work extends the search for multiple chiral doublet bands (MχD) predicted by Meng et al. (2006) and first experimentally observed in 133Ce by Ayangeakaa et al. (2013) and possibly in 107Ag by Qi et al. (2013). Unlike those cases where each chiral doublet corresponded to a distinct configuration, the present result in 103Rh shows two chiral doublets arising from the same π(g9/2)⁻¹⊗ν(h11/2)¹(g7/2)¹ configuration, providing the first evidence for this novel type of MχD. The observation enables further study of how chiral geometry persists at higher intrinsic excitation energies and motivates searches for similar structures in neighboring nuclei such as 105Rh, where three ΔI=1 bands were previously reported by Alcántara-Núñez et al. (2004).
