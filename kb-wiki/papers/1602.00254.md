# Generalized Valon Model for Double Parton Distributions

> We show how the double parton distributions may be obtained consistently from the many-body light-cone wave functions. We illustrate the method on the example of the pion with two Fock components. The procedure, by construction, satisfies the Gaunt-Stirling sum rules. The resulting single parton distributions of valence quarks and gluons are consistent with a phenomenological parametrization at a low scale.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that double parton distributions (dPDFs) can be constructed consistently from many-body light-cone wave functions using a generalized valon model, but notes that single parton distributions (sPDFs) alone cannot uniquely determine dPDFs, as the authors explicitly show by constructing perturbations that leave sPDF sum rules unchanged.

## Method

The authors model the pion using two Fock components, \(|\pi^+\rangle = A |\bar{u}d\rangle + B |\bar{u}d gg\rangle\), with the generalized valon ansatz that the only correlations in the wave function come from longitudinal momentum conservation. They assume a factorized form for the squared wave functions, \(|\psi_N|^2 \propto \phi_{f_1}(x_1)\dots\phi_{f_N}(x_N)\), with single-parton functions \(\phi_f(x) = x^{\alpha_f-1}(1-x)^{\beta_f}\), and constrain the parameters by matching the asymptotic behavior to the GRV\(_\pi\) parametrization at a low scale \(Q_0 = 500\) MeV.

## Key numbers

* The momentum fractions from the GRV\(_\pi\) parametrization at \(Q_0 = 500\) MeV are: \(2\int dx\, x V(x) = 0.58\) for valence quarks and \(\int dx\, x g(x) = 0.42\) for gluons.
* The average number of gluons inferred from the GRV\(_\pi\) parametrization is \(\int dx\, g(x) = 1.46\).
* The two-component model yields normalization constants \(A^2 = 0.15\) and \(B^2 = 0.85\), indicating strong dominance of the component with gluons.
* The single-parton function parameters used are: \(a+b=0.5\), \(\alpha_q=0.5\), \(\beta_q=-0.09\), \(\alpha_g=0.48\), \(\beta_g=-0.09\).

## Context

This work extends the valon model approach of Broniowski (2013) for the nucleon, which previously considered only a single valence Fock component, to include higher Fock states and thereby treat gluon and sea content. The authors contrast their top-down method with bottom-up attempts such as the product ansatz, which violates Gaunt-Stirling sum rules (Gaunt 2009), and with earlier model calculations in the MIT bag model (Chang 2012) and constituent quark model (Rinaldi 2013) that implemented relativity only approximately. The construction enables systematic determination of dPDFs for phenomenological use at the LHC, with the authors noting that QCD evolution washes out correlations at low \(x_1\) and \(x_2\) but correlations remain substantial elsewhere.
