# S=I=0 Pion Pairs in the A(pi,pi pi)X Reaction

> Recent experimental results show a large enhancement of the 2 pion emission on the scalar isoscalar channel, which had been predicted by some theoretical estimates. We present here a detailed calculation of the A(pi, 2pi)X process incorporating a microscopic model for the elementary reaction in vacuum, pion-pion final state interaction in the nuclear medium, and other nuclear medium effects.

## Key claim
The paper presents a detailed calculation of the A(π, 2π)X reaction that reproduces the experimental enhancement of scalar-isoscalar pion pairs in medium nuclei, but the model fails to reproduce the observed peak for heavier nuclei in the π⁻ → π⁺π⁻ channel, finding only a small effect from in-medium pion-pion final state interaction.

## Method
The authors solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation for ππ scattering in the scalar isoscalar channel, including coupled ππ and K̅K channels, with a potential from lowest-order chiral Lagrangians and a 1 GeV cutoff. Nuclear medium effects are incorporated by substituting vacuum meson propagators with medium propagators containing meson selfenergies from particle-hole and Δ-hole excitations. The elementary πN → ππN amplitude is taken from a microscopic model with no free parameters, and nuclear distortion of initial and final pions (absorption and quasielastic scattering) is implemented following Oset (1986) and Abs (1994).

## Key numbers
- The average effective density at which pion production occurs is ρ_av = ρ_0/4, considerably lower than used in prior work by Rap (1999).
- The theoretical ππ scattering amplitude agrees with experimental phase shifts and inelasticities from threshold up to energies around 1.2 GeV.
- The imaginary part of T_ππ shows a large enhancement around 300 MeV invariant mass, where CHAOS data show a well marked peak.
- A cutoff of 1 GeV is used to regularize the momentum integral in the two-meson propagator G.

## Context
This work extends prior calculations by Rap (1999) that used a simpler elementary amplitude and found a clear peak in M_ππ distributions for medium nuclei, but overestimated the deuterium threshold region. The present model improves agreement for deuterium but fails for heavier nuclei in the scalar-isoscalar channel, contradicting the earlier predictions of strong medium enhancement by Sch (1988) and Hatsuda et al. The results suggest that lepton-induced reactions (γ, ππ) may be a better probe of in-medium pion-pion interactions, as they avoid the strong initial-state absorption that limits the effective density to ρ_0/4.
