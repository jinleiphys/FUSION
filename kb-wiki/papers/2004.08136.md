# Femtoscopic correlations and the $Λ_c N$ interaction

> We study the prospects for deducing constraints on the interaction of charmed baryons with nucleons from measurements of two-particle momentum correlation functions for $Λ_c p$. The correlation functions are calculated for $Λ_c N$ and $Σ_c N$ interactions that have been extrapolated from lattice QCD simulations at unphysical masses of $m_π=410-570$ MeV to the physical point using chiral effective field theory as guideline. In addition, we consider phenomenological $Y_c N$ models from the literature to explore the sensitivity of the results to the properties of the interaction in detail. We find that a measurement of the $Λ_c p$ correlation functions could indeed allow one to discriminate between strongly attractive $Λ_c N$ forces, as predicted by some phenomenological models, and a weakly attractive interaction as suggested by the presently available lattice simulations.

## Key claim

A measurement of the $\Lambda_c p$ correlation function could allow one to discriminate between strongly attractive $\Lambda_c N$ forces (as predicted by some phenomenological models) and a weakly attractive interaction (as suggested by presently available lattice simulations), provided the repulsive Coulomb interaction is properly accounted for and the source size is small enough.

## Method

The authors computed $\Lambda_c p$ correlation functions using the Koonin-Pratt formula with a Gaussian source distribution, solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the $S$-wave components of the $\Lambda_c N$ interaction. They used a chiral EFT-based potential extrapolated from HAL QCD lattice simulations at unphysical pion masses ($m_\pi = 410-570$ MeV) to the physical point, and also simulated phenomenological potentials from Maeda et al. (2015), Vidana et al. (2019), and Garcilazo et al. (2019) by adjusting contact terms to match their effective range parameters. The repulsive Coulomb interaction between $\Lambda_c$ and the proton was included via the Vincent-Phatak method.

## Key numbers

* The $\Lambda_c N$ scattering lengths from the LQCD-extrapolated interaction are $-0.85$ fm (singlet) and $-0.81$ fm (triplet) for cutoff $\Lambda=500$ MeV, and $-1.01$ fm (singlet) and $-0.98$ fm (triplet) for $\Lambda=600$ MeV.
* The nearest threshold to $\Lambda_c N$ ($\Sigma_c N$) is separated by $M_{\Sigma_c} - M_{\Lambda_c} = 168$ MeV, compared to $M_\Sigma - M_\Lambda = 78$ MeV for the strange sector.
* The singlet scattering length changes from $-1.01$ fm to $-0.97$ fm when Coulomb is added to the LQCD-e (600) potential.
* Source radii considered were $R \approx 1.2$ fm (motivated by ALICE $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV) and $R \approx 2.5, 5$ fm (motivated by STAR Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV).
* The CTNN-d potential from Maeda et al. (2015) predicts bound states with scattering lengths of $5.31$ fm (singlet) and $5.01$ fm (triplet).

## Context

This work extends the femtoscopic methodology previously applied to $\Lambda p$, $\Sigma^0 p$, and $\Xi^- p$ systems (Adams et al., 2005; Acharya et al., 2018, 2019) to the charmed baryon sector, motivated by recent ALICE and STAR measurements showing larger-than-expected $\Lambda_c$ yields. The $\Lambda_c N$ interaction is constructed in analogy to the $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ interaction developed by the Julich-Bonn-Munich group (Polinder et al., 2006; Haidenbauer et al., 2013, 2019). The results enable future femtoscopic experiments at facilities such as J-PARC, KEK, and FAIR to test whether the $\Lambda_c N$ interaction is weakly attractive (as suggested by HAL QCD lattice simulations) or strongly attractive (as predicted by phenomenological models like those of Vidana et al. (2019) and Maeda et al. (2015)).
