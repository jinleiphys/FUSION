# Elliptic and triangular flows in dAu collisions at 200 GeV in the fusing color string model

> In the color string picture with fusion and percolation the elliptic and triangular flows are studied for p-Au and d-Au collisions at 200 GeV. The ordering $v_n(d-Au)>v_n(p-Au)$ observed experimentally for central collisions is reproduced.The calculated elliptic flow $v_2$ at central collisions agrees satisfactorily with thedata. The triangular flow $v_3$ is found to be greater than the experimental values, similar to the resultsobtained in the approach based on the Color Glass Condensate initial conditions with subsequenthydrodynamical evolution.

## Key claim

The fusing color string model reproduces the experimentally observed ordering \(v_n(d\text{-}Au) > v_n(p\text{-}Au)\) for central collisions and gives a satisfactory description of the elliptic flow \(v_2\), but the triangular flow \(v_3\) is found to be greater than the experimental values, similar to results from Color Glass Condensate initial conditions with subsequent hydrodynamical evolution.

## Method

The authors performed Monte-Carlo simulations of p-Au and d-Au collisions at 200 GeV, locating interacting nucleons based on a distance criterion and distributing strings randomly in the transverse plane with probability proportional to hadron matter. Overlapping strings were fused, and the quenching of particles passing through the gluon field of fused strings was described by a formula derived from QED, with a single adjustable quenching coefficient \(\kappa = 0.6\) fixed from mid-central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.13 TeV. The azimuthal distribution of emitted particles was then used to compute the flow coefficients \(v_2\) and \(v_3\) after averaging over events, with centrality selection based on multiplicity cuts.

## Key numbers

- The quenching coefficient \(\kappa = 0.6\) was adjusted to give the experimental value for \(v_2\) in mid-central Pb-Pb collisions at 5.13 TeV, integrated over transverse momenta.
- For central d-Au collisions at 200 GeV, the calculated \(v_2\) values are nearly 50% larger than for central p-Au collisions.
- The statistical error of the Monte-Carlo simulations is estimated as around 5%.
- Central collisions were defined by selecting events with multiplicity \(\mu\) in the range \(0.9\mu_{\text{max}} < \mu < \mu_{\text{max}}\), where \(\mu_{\text{max}}\) is the largest multiplicity for all impact parameters and runs.

## Context

This work extends the fusing color string model, previously applied to larger systems, to small collision systems (p-Au and d-Au) where azimuthal anisotropies have been observed. It addresses the speculation by Aidala (2019) that the enhanced number of primary sources in d-Au collisions should lead to smaller \(v_2\) values, showing instead that strings communicate via the gluonic field. The results for \(v_3\) overshoot the data similarly to models based on gluonic initial conditions with hydrodynamical evolution by Mace (2018) and Schenke (2016), suggesting that improved treatment of hadronization may be needed.
