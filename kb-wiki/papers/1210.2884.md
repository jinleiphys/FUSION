# Cluster Model Analysis of Kaon Scattering from $^{12}$C

> Angular distributions of differential cross sections for the interaction of $K^{+}$ mesons with $^{^{12}}$C nucleus at beam momenta of 635, 715, and 800 MeV/c have been analyzed using 3$α$-particle model of $^{^{12}}$C. Differential cross sections for inelastic transitions to the (2$^{^{+}}$; 4.44 MeV) and (3$^{-}$; 9.64 MeV) states in $^{^{12}}$C are calculated, and deformation lengths $δ_{2}$ and $δ_{3}$ are extracted and consistent with other works. Good agreement with experimental data of elastic and inelastic $K^{\pm}$-$^{12}$C scattering is obtained.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a simple nonrelativistic optical potential based on a 3α-cluster model of ¹²C, using Woods-Saxon folding potentials for the kaon-alpha interaction, can reproduce elastic and inelastic K⁺ and K⁻ scattering data at beam momenta of 635, 715, and 800 MeV/c, but the agreement is qualified by the fact that the predicted diffraction minima are much deeper than those observed, and the calculations converge to smaller values than the data at very forward angles due to neglect of Coulomb interaction.

## Method

The author used the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) with the DWUCK4 code, folding a Woods-Saxon kaon-alpha optical potential over the 3α-cluster wave function of ¹²C to produce a single-folding optical potential for K⁺-¹²C and K⁻-¹²C scattering. The internal wave function of ¹²C was taken as a Gaussian with a range parameter μ = 0.04667 fm⁻², and the six parameters of the real and imaginary Woods-Saxon potentials were varied systematically to minimize χ² against experimental angular distributions. For inelastic transitions to the 2⁺ (4.44 MeV) and 3⁻ (9.64 MeV) states, the radial transition potential was taken as the derivative of the folded optical potential multiplied by a deformation length δₗ.

## Key numbers

- Beam momenta analyzed: 635, 715, and 800 MeV/c for K⁺ on ¹²C; 800 MeV/c for K⁻ on ¹²C.
- The range parameter of ¹²C chosen for the calculations: μ = 0.04667 fm⁻² (with alternative values 0.02967 and 0.0884 fm⁻² also tested).
- Deformation lengths extracted for the 2⁺ and 3⁻ states fall between 0.802 and 1.429 fm across different momenta.
- For a 635 MeV/c (T_K = 310.638 MeV) charged kaon on ¹²C, the surrogate mass used in DWUCK4 was M_K = 0.82852 amu = 771.6 MeV, and the effective beam energy was E_L = 260.54 MeV.
- The α-particle density parameters: ρ₀ = 0.4229 fm⁻³ and β = 0.7014 fm⁻².
- The present model's reaction and total cross sections are smaller than those from the Watanabe superposition model by 3% for total and 3–5% for reaction cross sections.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channels calculations by Ebrahim (2012) using the Watanabe superposition model for K⁺ scattering from ⁶Li and ¹²C, and by previous optical potential analyses of K⁺-¹²C scattering that required in-medium modifications such as a 10–15% increase in the S11 K⁺N phase shift (Siegel et al.) or changes in ρ and ω meson masses (Brown et al.). The present 3α-cluster folding model provides a parameter-free alternative to Woods-Saxon potentials fitted directly to data (AAB) and enables extraction of deformation lengths for the 2⁺ and 3⁻ states in ¹²C that are consistent with those from other probes (BOH, SMI), allowing future investigation of possible differences between proton and neutron distributions in N=Z nuclei.
