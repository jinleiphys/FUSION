# Generating Tensor Polarization from Shear Stress

> We derive an expression for the tensor polarization of a system of massive spin-1 particles in a hydrodynamic framework. Starting from quantum kinetic theory based on the Wigner-function formalism, we employ a modified method of moments which also takes into account all spin degrees of freedom. It is shown that the tensor polarization of an uncharged fluid is determined by the shear-stress tensor. In order to quantify this novel polarization effect, we provide a formula which can be used for numerical calculations of vector-meson spin alignment in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The tensor polarization of an uncharged fluid of massive spin-1 particles arises from shear stress, not from the nonlocal collision terms that generate vector polarization; this effect is derived from quantum kinetic theory using a modified method of moments that includes all spin degrees of freedom, and the resulting formula can be used for numerical calculations of vector-meson spin alignment in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

Starting from the Wigner-function formalism for a Proca field, the authors define a scalar distribution function on an extended phase space that includes a spin variable, and derive a Boltzmann equation for it. They then split the distribution function into a local-equilibrium part and a dissipative part, expand the dissipative part in irreducible moments of momentum and spin (including new rank-2 spin moments for tensor polarization), and obtain the Navier-Stokes limit of the equations of motion for those moments by neglecting terms of order inverse Reynolds number times gradient. The resulting linearized collision integrals are evaluated for a simple four-point interaction to extract the proportionality coefficient between the tensor-polarization moment and the shear-stress tensor.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic spin-hydrodynamics framework previously developed for spin-0 particles (Denicol, 2012) and spin-1/2 particles (Weickgenannt, 2022) to the case of massive spin-1 particles, introducing new rank-2 spin moments that describe tensor polarization. It proposes a mechanism for vector-meson spin alignment that is distinct from the thermal-shear contribution proposed by Liu (2021), Fu (2021), and Becattini (2021) and from the spectral-function imbalance mechanism of Baym (2017) and Speranza (2018). The result enables quantitative predictions for the spin alignment of \(\phi\) and \(K^{*0}\) mesons measured by the ALICE and STAR collaborations, which currently exceed theoretical predictions based on local equilibrium.
