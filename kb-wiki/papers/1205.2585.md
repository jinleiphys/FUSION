# Elliptic flow and the symmetry energy at supra-saturation density

> The elliptic flow in collisions of neutron-rich heavy-ion systems at intermediate energies emerges as an observable sensitive to the strength of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities. First results obtained by comparing ratios or differences of neutron and hydrogen flows with predictions of transport models favor a moderately soft to linear density dependence, consistent with ab-initio nuclear-matter theories. Comprehensive data sets of high accuracy can be expected to improve our knowledge of the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The elliptic flow of neutrons and protons in collisions of neutron-rich heavy-ion systems at intermediate energies is sensitive to the strength of the symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, with first results from comparing ratios or differences of neutron and hydrogen flows to transport model predictions favoring a moderately soft to linear density dependence, consistent with ab-initio nuclear-matter theories, though the statistical uncertainty of the existing data is larger than the investigated systematic effects.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed existing FOPI/LAND data for $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon, applying equal acceptances for neutrons and hydrogen isotopes with regard to particle energy, rapidity, and transverse momentum. They compared the measured elliptic flow parameters and their ratios to predictions from the UrQMD transport code using power-law coefficients $\gamma = 0.5$ and $\gamma = 1.5$ for the density dependence of the symmetry energy, and also compared with independent QMD transport calculations from the Tübingen group.

## Key numbers

- The power-law exponent for the potential term of the symmetry energy was determined as $\gamma = 0.9 \pm 0.4$ from the elliptic-flow analysis of $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions at 400 MeV/nucleon
- The corresponding slope parameter at saturation density is $L = 83 \pm 26$ MeV
- Linear interpolations between UrQMD predictions for $\gamma = 0.5$ and $\gamma = 1.5$, averaged over $0.3 < p_t/A \le 1.0$ GeV/c, yielded $\gamma = 1.01 \pm 0.21$ for the FP1 parameterization and $\gamma = 0.98 \pm 0.35$ for the FP2 parameterization of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections
- The relative strengths of neutron and proton elliptic flows varied on the level of 15% between calculations with $\gamma = 0.5$ and $\gamma = 1.5$
- The maximum squeeze-out for $^{197}$Au + $^{197}$Au collisions occurs near 400 MeV/nucleon, with the excitation function showing $v_2 < 0$ between approximately 150 MeV/nucleon and 4 GeV/nucleon

## Context

This work extends the search for high-density probes of the symmetry energy beyond the directed flow approach proposed by Li (2002) and addresses the conflicting conclusions from analyses of $\pi^-/\pi^+$ production ratios measured by the FOPI Collaboration (Reisdorf et al., 2007), which had suggested either super-stiff or super-soft behavior. The elliptic-flow result is consistent with the range spanned by DBHF and variational calculations using realistic nuclear potentials (Fuchs et al., 2006) and with constraints from isospin-diffusion data and neutron-skin thickness (Li and Chen, 2005). The analysis enables future dedicated measurements with improved statistical accuracy, such as the 2011 experiment with LAND and CHIMERA detectors, to better constrain the equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter at supra-saturation densities.
