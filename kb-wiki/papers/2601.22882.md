# Heavy quark polarization anisotropy as a novel probe of fireball geometry

> We propose a new approach to probe the initial fireball geometry in relativistic heavy-ion collisions using spin polarization. Specifically, we introduce polarization harmonics of open heavy hadrons as a novel observable sensitive to geometric anisotropies. Heavy quarks are produced in early hard scatterings and can acquire spin polarization from the strong, transient electromagnetic fields present at early times. As they propagate through the anisotropic quark-gluon plasma, medium-induced interactions lead to path-length dependent depolarization, imprinting an azimuthally anisotropic polarization pattern. Within the framework of rotational Brownian motion, we show that the resulting polarization harmonics are directly related to the initial spatial eccentricities, thereby establishing heavy-flavor polarization anisotropies as a sensitive and complementary probe of the early-time collision geometry. We present quantitative estimates of the second polarization harmonic associated with the recently observed $D^{*+}$ spin alignment reported by the ALICE Collaboration.

# Heavy quark polarization anisotropy as a novel probe of fireball geometry

## Key claim
Heavy-flavor polarization harmonics, derived from path-length-dependent spin depolarization of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, are directly proportional to the initial-state spatial eccentricities within a rotational Brownian motion framework. The quantitative estimates rely on a static fireball approximation with constant average temperature, so the numerical values provide qualitative order-of-magnitude guidance rather than precision predictions.

## Method
The author models heavy-quark spin evolution using the stochastic Landau-Lifshitz-Gilbert equation, treating the spin dynamics as rotational Brownian motion on a sphere. The ensemble-averaged path length of heavy quarks through the medium is expanded in Fourier harmonics, and the relation between path-length anisotropy coefficients and spatial eccentricities is derived for weakly anisotropic geometries. The resulting Fokker-Planck equation for the spin probability distribution is solved analytically, yielding polarization harmonics that are expressed in terms of the spatial eccentricities, the spin relaxation time, and the heavy-quark kinematics.

## Key numbers
- The second polarization harmonic for charm quarks, \(p_2\), decreases with increasing transverse momentum; at \(p_T \approx 2\) GeV the value is approximately 0.02, while at \(p_T \approx 10\) GeV it falls below 0.005 (read from Figure 1).
- The \(p_T\)-averaged second polarization harmonic is \(\langle p_2 \rangle \simeq 0.17\) for Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02\) TeV in the 30-50% centrality class.
- The fit parameters used are: \(L_0 = 10\) fm, \(\tau_s = 1.31\) fm, charm-quark mass \(m_Q = 1.27\) GeV, and elliptic eccentricity \(\epsilon_2 = 0.38\) from Monte Carlo Glauber calculations.
- The spin relaxation time for mesons uses \(\alpha = 6\), and the rapidity window is \(0.3 < |y| < 0.8\) with central value \(y = 0.55\).

## Context
This work extends the rotational Brownian motion framework for heavy-quark spin polarization developed by Dey et al. (2025), which was used to explain the ALICE Collaboration's observation of \(D^{*+}\) meson spin alignment in Pb-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV. The proposed polarization harmonics provide a new observable that is complementary to established probes of fireball geometry such as flow harmonics, Hanbury-Brown-Twiss femtoscopy, jet quenching, and heavy-flavor suppression. The approach enables future studies that embed spin dynamics in realistic hydrodynamic simulations and incorporate first-principles calculations of the heavy-quark spin relaxation time in external magnetic fields.
