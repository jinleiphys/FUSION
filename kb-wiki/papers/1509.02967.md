# Multi-particle long-range rapidity correlations from fluctuation of the fireball longitudinal shape

> We calculate the genuine long-range multi-particle rapidity correlation functions, $C_{n}(y_1,...,y_n)$ for $n=3,4,5,6$, originating from fluctuations of the fireball longitudinal shape. In these correlation functions any contribution from the short-range two-particle correlations, and in general up to $(n-1)$-particle in $C_n$, is suppressed. The information about the fluctuating fireball shape in rapidity is encoded in the cumulants of coefficients of the orthogonal polynomial expansion of particle distributions in rapidity.

## Key claim

The paper derives formulas for genuine long-range multi-particle rapidity correlation functions \(C_n\) for \(n=3,4,5,6\) that originate solely from event-by-event fluctuations of the fireball longitudinal shape, under the condition that short-range correlations of order \(n\) or lower can be neglected or subtracted via cumulant techniques.

## Method

The authors expand the event-by-event single-particle rapidity distribution in Legendre polynomials \(T_i(y)\) with fluctuating coefficients \(a_i\). They then write the multi-particle rapidity densities \(\rho_n\) as products of these fluctuating single-particle distributions plus short-range terms. By taking event averages and subtracting lower-order correlation contributions, they isolate the genuine \(n\)-particle correlation function \(C_n\) as a sum over cumulants of the expansion coefficients \(\langle a_{i_1}\dots a_{i_n}\rangle_{[n]}\) multiplied by the corresponding products of Legendre polynomials. They show how to compute these cumulants experimentally from sums over \(n\)-tuples of particles in each event.

## Key numbers

* The orthogonalization condition for the Legendre polynomials is \(\int dy\, T_i(y) T_k(y) = \delta_{ik}\), with \(T_k(y) = \sqrt{k+\frac{1}{2}} P_k(\eta/Y)\) where \(y = \eta/Y\) and \(\eta\) is rapidity in the range \([-Y,Y]\).
* For the four-particle cumulant of the leading term: \(\langle a_i^4\rangle_{[4]} \equiv \langle a_i^4\rangle - 3\langle a_i^2\rangle^2\).
* For the five-particle cumulant of the leading term: \(\langle a_i^5\rangle_{[5]} \equiv \langle a_i^5\rangle - 10\langle a_i^2\rangle\langle a_i^3\rangle\).
* For the six-particle cumulant of the leading term: \(\langle a_i^6\rangle_{[6]} \equiv \langle a_i^6\rangle - 15\langle a_i^2\rangle\langle a_i^4\rangle - 10\langle a_i^3\rangle^2 + 30\langle a_i^2\rangle^3\).

## Context

This work extends the two-particle rapidity correlation method of Bzdak (2012) to higher-order correlations, analogous to how multi-particle flow cumulants (Borghini 2000, 2001) remove non-flow effects in azimuthal correlations. The proposed cumulant technique suppresses short-range backgrounds from resonance decays and jets, enabling direct extraction of genuine multi-particle correlations from fluctuations of the fireball longitudinal shape. The method is applicable to symmetric A+A collisions, asymmetric p+A collisions, and p+p collisions, and its predictions can be tested against models of energy deposition in hadronic collisions.
