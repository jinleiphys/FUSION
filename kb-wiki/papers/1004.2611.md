# Computation of spectroscopic factors with the coupled-cluster method

> We present a calculation of spectroscopic factors within coupled-cluster theory. Our derivation of algebraic equations for the one-body overlap functions are based on coupled-cluster equation-of-motion solutions for the ground and excited states of the doubly magic nucleus with mass number $A$ and the odd-mass neighbor with mass $A-1$. As a proof-of-principle calculation, we consider $^{16}$O and the odd neighbors $^{15}$O and $^{15}$N, and compute the spectroscopic factor for nucleon removal from $^{16}$O. We employ a renormalized low-momentum interaction of the $V_{\mathrm{low-}k}$ type derived from a chiral interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order. We study the sensitivity of our results by variation of the momentum cutoff, and then discuss the treatment of the center of mass.

## Key claim

The paper presents a proof-of-principle calculation of spectroscopic factors within coupled-cluster theory, showing that the method yields well-converged results for nucleon removal from 16O that depend very weakly on model-space size and oscillator frequency, but the authors note that the spectroscopic factor is not an observable and depends on the employed Hamiltonian or model.

## Method

The authors derived algebraic and diagrammatic expressions for one-body overlap functions using equation-of-motion coupled-cluster theory with singles and doubles (EOM-CCSD) for the ground and excited states of the doubly magic nucleus 16O and its odd-mass neighbors 15O and 15N. They employed a renormalized low-momentum interaction of the Vlow-k type derived from a chiral interaction at next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order, truncating the cluster operator at the two-particle-two-hole excitation level (CCSD). The spectroscopic factor was computed from the norm of the radial overlap function using similarity-transformed creation and annihilation operators in an uncoupled m-scheme basis.

## Key numbers

- The CCSD ground-state energy for 16O is overbound by approximately 15 MeV compared to experiment, using Vlow-k with cutoff λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹
- The energy difference between the ground states of 15O and 15N is about 4 MeV, close to the experimental value of 3.5 MeV
- The spectroscopic factor SF(1/2⁻) for proton removal from 16O varies less than 1% over a wide range of oscillator frequencies ℏω, using Vlow-k with cutoff λ = 2.0 fm⁻¹
- Momentum cutoffs studied range from λ = 1.6 to 2.2 fm⁻¹, with the spectroscopic factor increasing as the cutoff decreases
- The spectroscopic factor SF(3/2⁻) for proton removal is similar in size to SF(1/2⁻), while the spectroscopic factor for the deeply bound Jπ = 1/2⁺ state is very small

## Context

This work extends coupled-cluster theory to the computation of spectroscopic factors, which had previously been addressed within other ab initio methods such as the self-consistent Green's function approach of Barbieri and Dickhoff (2009). The authors note that Barbieri and Dickhoff (2009) also found SF(1/2⁻) ≈ SF(3/2⁻) for nucleon removal from 16O, consistent with their own results. The method enables future calculations for heavier nuclei such as 22,24O, 48,52Ca, and 56,78Ni, and the authors plan to implement a spherical formulation to access larger model spaces.
