# Softer Hard Scattering and Noncommutative Gauge-String Duality

> We study exclusive scattering of `hadrons' at high energy and fixed angle in (nonconformal) noncommutative gauge theories. Via gauge-string duality, we show that the noncommutativity renders the scattering soft, leading to exponential suppression. The result fits with the picture that, in noncommutative gauge theory, fundamental partons consist of extremely soft constituents and `hadrons' are made out of open Wilson lines.

## Key claim

Noncommutative gauge theory exhibits exponentially suppressed (soft) exclusive scattering at high energy and fixed angle, in contrast to the power-law behavior of commutative gauge theory. This result is conditional on the noncommutativity scale \(\sqrt{\Theta}\) being large compared to the string scale (\(\Theta \gg \ell_{\rm st}^2\)), and the scattering kinematics being restricted to the noncommutative subspace.

## Method

The authors use gauge-string duality to compute exclusive scalar glueball scattering in noncommutative \({\cal N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory with noncommutativity on the \((23)\)-plane. The gauge theory amplitude is obtained by convoluting the Virasoro-Shapiro string scattering amplitude (approximated in the flat-space limit \(R \to \infty\)) with the normalized dilaton wave function in the dual ten-dimensional background, evaluated via a saddle-point method. The wave function is derived from the dilaton equation of motion in the noncommutative background metric, and its asymptotic form is obtained via the WKB method.

## Key numbers

* The noncommutativity parameter is defined by \([\widehat{x}_2, \widehat{x}_3] = i \Theta\), with the noncommutative scale set by \(a^4 = \Theta^2 / R^4\).
* The classical turning point for the dilaton wave function at high energy is located at \(u_c \sim u_* = R / \sqrt{\Theta}\).
* The final gauge theory amplitude for \(\vert {\bf p} \vert \rightarrow \infty\) is \({\cal A}_{\rm NCYM}({\bf p}) \sim { (g_{\rm YM}^2 N)^{1/2} \over N^2} (\Theta s)^3 \sin^2 \theta \left[ e^{f(\theta)} \right]^{-\Theta s}\), where \(f(\theta) = -\sin^2(\theta/2) \ln(\sin^2(\theta/2)) - \cos^2(\theta/2) \ln(\cos^2(\theta/2))\).
* The stringy behavior is pronounced for energy-momentum exceeding the noncommutativity scale: \(\vert {\bf p} \vert \gsim 1 / \sqrt{\Theta}\).
* The size of the open Wilson line grows linearly with center-of-mass momentum: \({\bf d} = \Theta \wedge {\bf p}\), with \(\vert {\bf d} \vert \gsim \sqrt{\Theta}\).

## Context

This work extends the gauge-string duality analysis of exclusive glueball scattering in commutative gauge theory by Polchinski and Strassler (2002), who found power-law behavior. It contradicts the earlier supergravity-based result of Gross et al. (2001), which also found exponential suppression but from the bulk-to-boundary propagator rather than from the full string amplitude. The result enables future study of deep inelastic scattering in noncommutative gauge theory, extending the work of Polchinski and Strassler (2002) on structure function evolution, and suggests that open Wilson lines are the fundamental constituents of noncommutative gauge theory.
