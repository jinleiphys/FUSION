# Polarization and dilepton anisotropy in pion-nucleon collisions

> Hadronic polarization and the related anisotropy of the dilepton angular distribution are studied for the reaction $πN \rightarrow Ne^+ e^-$. We employ consistent effective interactions for baryon resonances up to spin-5/2, where non-physical degrees of freedom are eliminated, to compute the anisotropy coefficients for isolated intermediate baryon resonances. It is shown that the spin and parity of the intermediate baryon resonance is reflected in the angular dependence of the anisotropy coefficient. We then compute the anisotropy coefficient including the $N(1520)$ and $N(1440)$ resonances, which are essential at the collision energy of the recent data obtained by the HADES collaboration on this reaction. We conclude that the anisotropy coefficient provides useful constraints for unravelling the resonance contributions to this process.

## Key claim

The anisotropy coefficient λθ in dilepton angular distributions from πN → Ne+e− reflects the spin and parity of the intermediate baryon resonance, but the actual angular dependence is model-dependent because different choices for the interaction Lagrangians (e.g., the number of derivatives in the resonance-nucleon-ρ vertices) can alter the results, though the qualitative features should remain valid as long as the lowest angular momentum states dominate.

## Method

The authors employed effective Lagrangians for baryon resonances up to spin-5/2, using gauge-invariant Rarita-Schwinger fields to eliminate non-physical lower-spin degrees of freedom following the scheme of Pascalutsa (1998) and Vrancx et al. (2011). They computed the hadronic density matrix elements for the reaction πN → R → Ne+e− via s-channel and u-channel diagrams, with the ρ0 meson mediating the electromagnetic coupling through vector meson dominance. The anisotropy coefficient λθ was then extracted from the angular distribution of the dilepton decay products in the helicity frame, first for isolated hypothetical resonances at √s = 1.49 GeV, then for the physical resonances N(1440), N(1520), N(1535), N(1650), N(1675), N(1680), Δ(1600), and Δ(1620) using coupling constants determined from Particle Data Group partial widths.

## Key numbers

- At √s = 1.49 GeV and dilepton invariant mass M = 0.5 GeV, the N(1520) resonance contributes dσ/dM = 0.44 μb/GeV and the N(1440) contributes dσ/dM = 0.33 μb/GeV (including both s- and u-channel diagrams)
- The combined cross section for N(1520) and N(1440) with interference is dσ/dM = 0.84 μb/GeV (same phase) or 0.70 μb/GeV (opposite phase)
- The N(1520) contribution may vary by about 40% due to uncertainties in resonance widths and branching ratios from the Particle Data Group
- The combined total cross section including both N(1440) and N(1520) can be up to a factor of 2 larger than the central values
- Sub-leading contributions: N(1535) yields 0.011 μb/GeV and Δ(1600) yields 0.0078 μb/GeV at the same kinematics
- For the πN → Nππ test channel at 0.5 GeV invariant mass of the pion pair, the N(1520) contribution gives dσ/dM between 5 and 10 mb/GeV
- The hypothetical resonance calculations used mR = 1.49 GeV and ΓR = 0.15 GeV for all spin-parity combinations

## Context

This work extends the study of dilepton anisotropy coefficients from Bratkovskaya (1995), who computed λθ for dilepton sources in heavy-ion collisions, and builds on the effective Lagrangian framework for baryon resonances developed by Zétényi (2012). The calculations are motivated by preliminary HADES collaboration data on pion-induced dilepton production presented at the NSTAR2015 conference, and the cross section results are consistent with those of Zétényi (2012) within uncertainties. The approach enables future extraction of resonance quantum numbers from HADES angular distribution data, similar to how the quantum numbers of the X(3872) meson were determined by Aaij et al. (2013) using angular distributions.
