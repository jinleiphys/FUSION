# $D\bar{D}^*$ scattering and $χ_{c1}(3872)$ in nuclear matter

> We study the behaviour of the $χ_{c1}(3872)$, also known as $X(3872)$, in dense nuclear matter. We begin from a picture in vacuum of the $X(3872)$ as a purely molecular $(D \bar D^*-c.c.)$ state, generated as a bound state from a heavy-quark symmetry leading-order interaction between the charmed mesons, and analyze the $D \bar D^*$ scattering $T-$matrix ($T_{D \bar D^*}$) inside of the medium. Next, we consider also mixed-molecular scenarios and, in all cases, we determine the corresponding $X(3872)$ spectral function and the $D \bar D^*$ amplitude, with the mesons embedded in the dense environment. We find important nuclear corrections for $T_{D \bar D^*}$ and the pole position of the resonance, and discuss the dependence of these results on the $D \bar D^*$ molecular component in the $X(3872)$ wave-function. These predictions could be tested in the finite-density regime that can be accessed in the future CBM and PANDA experiments at FAIR.

## Key claim

The paper studies the behavior of the χc1(3872) (X(3872)) in dense nuclear matter, finding important nuclear corrections to the D D̄* scattering T-matrix and the pole position of the resonance, with the magnitude of these corrections depending on the fraction of D D̄* molecular component in the X(3872) wave-function.

## Method

The authors start from a vacuum picture of the X(3872) as a purely molecular (D D̄* - c.c.) state generated from a heavy-quark spin symmetry (HQSS) leading-order contact interaction, then implement the changes of the D and D̄* meson propagators in nuclear matter using self-consistent unitarized coupled-channel calculations of the meson-nucleon interaction. The in-medium D D̄* scattering T-matrix and X(3872) spectral function are obtained by modifying the two-meson loop functions through the D(*) and D̄(*) self-energies in symmetric nuclear matter, with the interaction potential assumed unchanged. The authors also consider mixed-molecular scenarios where the X(3872) is not a purely molecular state.

## Key numbers

- The X(3872) Breit-Wigner width measured by LHCb is Γ = 1.39(24)(10) MeV or Γ = 0.96(19)(21) MeV from two different works
- A sharp momentum cut-off Λ = 0.7 GeV is used to regularize the UV behavior of the loop function integration
- The D and D̄* spectral functions are computed at densities ρ = 0.5ρ0 and ρ = ρ0 (where ρ0 is nuclear saturation density)
- The X(3872) mass in vacuum is denoted m0, used to fix the coupling constant C0X via the condition C0X = 1/Σ0(m0²)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the X(3872) in vacuum and in hot pionic baths (Cleven 2019) to the case of dense nuclear matter, complementing earlier in-medium studies of other charmed mesons such as the Ds0*(2317) and X(3700) scalar mesons (Molina 2008). The predictions could be tested in the finite-density regime accessible at future CBM and PANDA experiments at FAIR, where the A-dependence of ψ(2S) and X(3872) production near threshold in antiproton-nucleus collisions may expose an extended D*D̄ component of the X(3872) wave-function (Lutz 2015).
