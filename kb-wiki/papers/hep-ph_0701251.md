# Paramaterizations of inclusive cross sections for pion production in proton-proton collisions. II. Comparison to new data

> A set of new, precise data have recently been made available by the NA49 collaboration for charged pion production in proton-proton and proton-Carbon reactions at 158 GeV. The current paper compares this new data to five currently available arithmetic parameterizations. Although a precise fit is not expected, two of the parameterizations do not work very well but the other three are able to provide a moderately good, but not precise fit to the proton-proton data. The best two of these three parameterizations are scaled to the proton-Carbon data and again provide a moderately good, but not precise fit.

# Parameterizations of inclusive cross sections for pion production in proton-proton collisions. II. Comparison to new data

## Key claim

Although a precise fit is not expected, three of five arithmetic parameterizations (Badhwar, Mokhov, and Carey for negative pions) provide a moderately good, but not precise, description of the new NA49 charged pion production data in proton-proton collisions at 158 GeV beam momentum; the Alper and Ellis parameterizations do not work well in this energy region.

## Method

The authors compared five existing arithmetic parameterizations (Badhwar, Alper, Ellis, Mokhov, and Carey) to new NA49 data for inclusive charged pion production in proton-proton (pp) and proton-Carbon (pC) collisions at a beam momentum of 158 GeV (center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 17 GeV). Parameterizations originally expressed in variables such as rapidity or total momentum were transformed to the (xF, pT) variables used by NA49. The two best parameterizations for pp data (Badhwar and Mokhov) were then scaled by a constant Cronin enhancement factor A^alpha and compared to the pC data.

## Key numbers

- Beam momentum of 158 GeV, corresponding to sqrt(s) = 17 GeV, for both pp and pC reactions
- Badhwar parameterization gives reasonable fit for pp data at pT > 0.3 GeV for positive pions, but fails for negative pions at large xF values (xF > 0.45)
- Mokhov parameterization gives reasonable description of shape at low pT (below 0.5 GeV) for pp data
- For pC data, both Badhwar and Mokhov parameterizations give best fit with Cronin enhancement factor alpha = 0.9
- The Carey parameterization for pi- uses normalization N = 13 mb/GeV^2, with exponent 4.5 on (pT^2 + 0.86) and exponent 4 on (1 - xR)

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Blattnig et al. (2004), who compared parameterizations to an extensive data set for neutral and charged pion production in pp collisions and concluded that the Badhwar parameterization provided the best overall description. The new NA49 data surpasses previous data in precision and quality, allowing more stringent tests. The results enable users of these parameterizations (in cosmic ray simulations, astrophysics, spacecraft radiation prediction, and nuclear modification factor calculations) to understand their limitations in light of the new data, and indicate the absence of nuclear collective effects for this pC reaction at 158 GeV.
