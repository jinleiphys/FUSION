# Anomalous diffusion of pions at RHIC

> After pointing out the difference between normal and anomalous diffusion, we consider a hadron resonance cascade (HRC) model simulation for particle emission at RHIC and point out, that rescattering in an expanding hadron resonance gas leads to a heavy tail in the source distribution. The results are compared to recent PHENIX measurements of the tail of the particle emitting source in Au+Au collisions at RHIC. In this context, we show, how can one distinguish experimentally the anomalous diffusion of hadrons from a second order QCD phase transition.

# Anomalous diffusion of pions at RHIC

## Key claim

Rescattering in an expanding hadron resonance gas with a time-dependent mean free path leads to a heavy (power-law) tail in the source distribution of pions, which can be described by Levy stable source distributions; however, the transverse momentum dependence of the source function in central collisions is not reproduced by the HRC model, indicating that rescattering effects may be too large at higher transverse momentum.

## Method

The authors used the Hadronic Resonance Cascade (HRC) model of Humanic to simulate Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, generating events at impact parameters of 4.45 fm (0-20% centrality) and 12.5 fm (50-90% centrality). They compared the simulated two-particle relative coordinate source distributions S(r) to PHENIX imaging data, decomposing the source into core (primordial and short-lived resonance decay pions), omega decay products, and halo (long-lived resonance decay pions) components. The Levy index of stability alpha was extracted from the power-law tail of S(r) on log-log plots.

## Key numbers

- Levy index of stability alpha = 1.15 +/- 0.1 from both HRC simulations and PHENIX data for 0-20% centrality and 0.2 GeV/c < p_t < 0.36 GeV/c
- Average multiplicities of 3400 particles for b=4.45 fm (0-20% centrality) and 38 particles for b=12.5 fm (50-90% centrality)
- Pion mass m_pi = 140 MeV, kaon mass m_K = 494 MeV
- For Levy stable sources in d=3 spatial dimensions, S(r) proportional to r^{-(d+alpha)} for r >> R

## Context

This work extends the study of non-Gaussian source structures beyond the Gaussian fits used in standard HBT analyses, following the PHENIX imaging measurement (PHENIX-imaging, 2005) that found heavy tails in Au+Au collisions. The authors connect anomalous diffusion in a time-dependent mean free path system to Levy stable distributions, contrasting with the Gaussian distributions from normal diffusion. The results enable experimental discrimination between anomalous diffusion of hadrons and a second-order QCD phase transition: in the latter case, the Levy index would be universal (independent of momentum, centrality, and particle type), while in anomalous diffusion the rescattering might show sensitivity to these controls, though the HRC simulations show surprisingly little centrality dependence of the tail slope.
