# Quantum computing of the $^6$Li nucleus via ordered unitary coupled clusters

> The variational quantum eigensolver (VQE) is an algorithm to compute ground and excited state energy of quantum many-body systems. A key component of the algorithm and an active research area is the construction of a parametrized trial wavefunction -- a so called variational ansatz. The wavefunction parametrization should be expressive enough, i.e. represent the true eigenstate of a quantum system for some choice of parameter values. On the other hand, it should be trainable, i.e. the number of parameters should not grow exponentially with the size of the system. Here, we apply VQE to the problem of finding ground and excited state energies of the odd-odd nucleus $^6$Li. We study the effects of ordering fermionic excitation operators in the unitary coupled clusters ansatz on the VQE algorithm convergence by using only operators preserving the $J_z$ quantum number. The accuracy is improved by two order of magnitude in the case of descending order. We first compute optimal ansatz parameter values using a classical state-vector simulator with arbitrary measurement accuracy and then use those values to evaluate energy eigenstates of $^6$Li on a superconducting quantum chip from IBM. We post-process the results by using error mitigation techniques and are able to reproduce the exact energy with an error of 3.8% and 0.1% for the ground state and for the first excited state of $^6$Li, respectively.

## Key claim
The ordering of fermionic excitation operators in the unitary coupled clusters ansatz strongly affects VQE convergence for the odd-odd nucleus $^6$Li, with descending order by magnitude improving accuracy by two orders of magnitude compared to ascending order; the ground state energy was reproduced on quantum hardware with 3.8% error and the first excited state with 0.1% error after error mitigation.

## Method
The authors employed a shell-model description of $^6$Li as a valence proton and neutron added to an inert $^4$He core, using the Cohen-Kurath interaction in a $0p_{3/2}$ and $0p_{1/2}$ harmonic oscillator basis (12 qubits after Jordan-Wigner mapping). They implemented the variational quantum eigensolver with unitary coupled clusters singles and doubles (UCCSD) ansatze, comparing different operator orderings (descending, ascending, random shuffle, ADAPT-VQE, and layerwise learning) on a classical state-vector simulator, then executed the qubit-based excitation (QBE-UCC) ansatz on the IBM 27-qubit device ibmq_mumbai with measurement error mitigation.

## Key numbers
- Ground state energy error on ibmq_mumbai after measurement error mitigation: 3.81% (mean energy -5.319 MeV vs exact -5.529 MeV, standard deviation 0.24 MeV)
- First excited state energy error on ibmq_mumbai after measurement error mitigation: 0.12% (mean energy -3.424 MeV vs exact -3.420 MeV, standard deviation 0.08 MeV)
- State-vector simulation of first excited state achieved error ratio of 10^{-11} using fermionic UCC ansatz
- Descending ordering improved accuracy by two orders of magnitude compared to ascending ordering
- QBE-UCC circuit after transpilation: 209 CNOT gates for ground state (9 parameters), 41 CNOT gates for first excited state (3 parameters)
- Hamiltonian consisted of 975 Pauli terms, grouped into 250 sets of qubit-wise commuting operators

## Context
This work extends prior VQE applications in nuclear physics from even-even nuclei (Be8-VQE, 2021; Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model studies) to the more complex odd-odd nucleus $^6$Li. The authors build on the same Cohen-Kurath interaction framework used for $^6$He computations (Be8-VQE, 2021) and demonstrate that operator ordering strategies, particularly descending order by magnitude and layerwise learning, outperform ADAPT-VQE (Grimsley et al., 2019) for this system. The results enable future VQE studies of heavier nuclei with more valence nucleons, though the authors note that the factorial growth of nuclear states makes scaling impractical without further symmetry reductions.
