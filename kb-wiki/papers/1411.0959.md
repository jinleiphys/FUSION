# Equation of state and sound velocity of hadronic gas with hard-core interaction

> Thermodynamic properties of hot and dense hadronic systems with a hard-sphere interaction are calculated in the Boltzmann approximation. Two parametrizations of pressure as a function of density are considered: the first one, used in the excluded volume model and the second one, suggested earlier by Carnahan and Starling. The results are given for one-component systems containing only nucleons or pions, as well as for chemically equilibrated mixtures of pions, nucleons and delta resonances. It is shown that the Carnahan-Starling approach can be used in a much broader range of hadronic densities as compared to the excluded volume model. In this case superluminal sound velocities appear only at very high densities, in the region where the deconfinement effects should be already important.

## Key claim

The Carnahan-Starling approximation (CSA) for hard-sphere interactions yields a much softer equation of state than the excluded volume model (EVM), pushing superluminal sound velocities to baryon densities above about 1 fm⁻³, where deconfinement effects should already be important; the EVM becomes inaccurate when the total volume of constituents exceeds 10–20% of the system volume.

## Method

The authors calculate thermodynamic properties of hot, dense hadronic systems using the Boltzmann approximation with hard-sphere repulsion, comparing two parametrizations of the compressibility factor Z(n): the excluded volume model (EVM) with Z = 1/(1−4η) and the Carnahan-Starling approximation (CSA) with Z = (1+η+η²−η³)/(1−η)³, where η is the packing fraction. They derive analytic expressions for pressure, chemical potential, energy density, entropy density, and sound velocity for one-component nucleon and pion systems, then extend the formalism to chemically equilibrated mixtures of nucleons, Δ resonances, and pions using the condition μ_π = 0 and μ_N = μ_Δ = μ_B.

## Key numbers

- For nucleon matter with R = 0.39 fm (excluded volume b = 1 fm³), the CSA sound velocity remains below unity up to n ≈ 0.9 fm⁻³ at T ≤ 200 MeV, while the EVM gives superluminal velocities at much lower densities.
- The EVM pressure diverges at η = 0.25 (packing fraction), whereas the CSA reproduces the first eight terms of the virial expansion and works up to η ≈ 0.5.
- For pion matter with R = 0.39 fm, sound velocities exceed the ideal gas value c_s = 1/√3 ≈ 0.577 at T ≳ 200 MeV, but become superluminal only at unrealistically high temperatures where hadrons should melt.
- A 20% reduction in nucleon radius from 0.39 fm to 0.31 fm corresponds to a two-fold decrease in excluded volume b.

## Context

This work extends earlier excluded volume model calculations by Cleymans (1983), Rischke (1991), Satarov (2009), and Bugaev (2008, 2013) that showed strong sensitivity of the hadronic equation of state to short-range repulsion parameters and the need for finite hadron sizes to obtain a reasonable phase diagram. The CSA, originally developed by Carnahan and Starling (1969) for condensed matter and validated by Hansen (2006) and Mulero (2008) for hard-sphere liquids, is shown to be applicable to hadronic systems at much higher densities than the EVM. The results enable more realistic modeling of dense hadronic matter in heavy-ion collisions and compact stars, and suggest that acausal behavior in the CSA appears only in the density region where a quark-gluon phase should already form.
