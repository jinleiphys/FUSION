# Anisotropic hydrodynamic modeling of 200 GeV Au-Au collisions

> We compare phenomenological results from 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) with experimental data collected in RHIC 200 GeV Au-Au collisions. We present comparisons of identified particle spectra in different centrality clases, charged particle multiplicity versus pseudorapidity, identified particle multiplicity versus centrality across a wide range of particle species, identified particle elliptic flow versus transverse momentum, and charged particle elliptic flow as a function of transverse momentum and rapidity. We use the same aHydroQP and hadronic production/feed down codes that were used previously to describe LHC 2.76 TeV data. The aHydroQP hydrodynamic model includes the effects of both shear and bulk viscosities in addition to an infinite number of transport coefficients computed self-consistently in the relaxation time approximation. To convert to the final state hadrons, we use anisotropic Cooper-Frye freeze-out performed on a fixed-energy-density hypersurface and compute the production/feed down using a customized version of Therminator 2. We find good agreement with many heavy-ion collision observables using only smooth Glauber initial conditions parameterized by an initial central temperature of T_0 = 455 MeV, a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s= 0.179, and a switching (freeze-out) temperature of T_FO=130 MeV.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) model, using smooth Glauber initial conditions with a single set of parameters (initial central temperature T0 = 455 MeV, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.179, and freeze-out temperature TFO = 130 MeV), reproduces many RHIC 200 GeV Au-Au observables, but the agreement is poorer than for LHC 2.76 TeV data, particularly for kaon elliptic flow and for charged particle v2 at high transverse momentum in mid-central collisions.

## Method

The authors solved the 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) equations of motion, which include shear and bulk viscosities and an infinite number of transport coefficients computed in the relaxation time approximation, using smooth Glauber initial conditions with a tilted profile and a binary-collision mixing factor of χ = 0.145. They performed anisotropic Cooper-Frye freeze-out on a fixed-energy-density hypersurface corresponding to TFO = 130 MeV, then used a customized version of Therminator 2 to handle hadronic production, decays, and resonance feed-downs for 370 hadron species.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature T0 = 455 MeV at proper time τ0 = 0.25 fm/c for 200 GeV Au-Au collisions
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.179 (assumed temperature-independent)
- Freeze-out (switching) temperature TFO = 130 MeV
- Binary-collision mixing factor χ = 0.145 for the Glauber profile
- Initial plateau width parameter Δς = 1.4 and fragmentation Gaussian width σς = 1.4 for the rapidity profile
- Approximately 20% difference in proton multiplicity in the 0-5% centrality class and approximately 35% difference in Ω+ + Ω- multiplicity in the most central bin
- Between 10,500 and 100,500 Monte-Carlo sampled hadronic production/decay events per observable and centrality class

## Context

This work extends the aHydroQP formalism previously applied to LHC 2.76 TeV collisions (Alqahtani et al., 2017) to RHIC 200 GeV Au-Au collisions, using the same code and freeze-out prescription. The extracted parameters provide a baseline for future studies including off-diagonal momentum-space anisotropies, fluctuating initial conditions, and temperature-dependent η/s, and are intended for use in other contexts such as bottomonium suppression calculations (Strickland, 2011; Krouppa et al., 2015-2017).
