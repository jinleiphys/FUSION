# Symmetry Conserving Configuration Mixing description of odd mass nuclei

> We present a self-consistent theory for the description of the spectroscopic properties of odd nuclei which includes exact blocking, particle-number and angular-momentum projection and configuration mixing. In our theory the pairing correlations are treated in a variation-after-projection approach and the triaxial deformation parameters are explicitly considered as generator coordinates. The angular-momentum and particle-number symmetries are exactly recovered. The use of the effective finite-range density-dependent Gogny force in the calculations provides an added value to the theoretical results. We apply the theory to the textbook example of 25Mg and, although this nucleus has been thoroughly studied in the past, we still provide a novel view of nuclear phenomena taking place in this nucleus. We obtain an overall good agreement with the known experimental energies and transition probabilities without any additional parameter such as effective charges. In particular, we clearly identify six bands, two of which we interpret as collective gamma bands.

## Key claim

The paper presents a self-consistent theory for odd-mass nuclei that includes exact blocking, particle-number and angular-momentum projection, and configuration mixing using the generator-coordinate method with triaxial deformation parameters as coordinates, but the approach is demonstrated only for the single nucleus 25Mg and the authors note that the overall good agreement with experimental energies and transition probabilities is achieved without any additional parameters such as effective charges.

## Method

The authors solve particle-number variation-after-projection (PN-VAP) constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations on a grid of triaxial deformation parameters (β,γ) for blocked neutron states of positive and negative parity, using the finite-range density-dependent Gogny D1S interaction. They then perform simultaneous particle-number and angular-momentum projection on each intrinsic state, followed by configuration mixing via the generator-coordinate method (GCM) to solve the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation, yielding final wave functions as linear combinations of projected states with different deformations.

## Key numbers

- The positive-parity PN-VAP energy minimum lies at β ≈ 0.42 with axial symmetry (γ = 0°)
- After angular-momentum projection, the absolute minimum corresponds to I = 5/2⁺, with relative energies of 0.637 MeV (I = 1/2⁺), 1.023 MeV (I = 3/2⁺), and 1.522 MeV (I = 7/2⁺)
- For I ≥ 5/2 ħ, the projected minima shift to β ≈ 0.51, γ ≈ 25° with K = 5/2 purity of at least 98%
- For I = 1/2 ħ and 3/2 ħ, the minima are at β ≈ 0.55, γ ≈ 7.6° and β ≈ 0.64, γ ≈ 6.6° respectively, with pure K = 1/2
- The negative-parity PN-VAP minimum lies 4.237 MeV above the positive-parity minimum, at β = 0.68 with axial symmetry
- After angular-momentum projection, negative-parity minima shift to β ≈ 0.77, γ ≈ 11° for all I values, with relative energies of 3.567 MeV (I = 3/2⁻), 4.228 MeV (I = 7/2⁻), 4.605 MeV (I = 1/2⁻), and 6.131 MeV (I = 11/2⁻) relative to the I = 5/2⁺ energy

## Context

This work extends symmetry-conserving configuration mixing (SCCM) approaches, previously applied only to even-even nuclei with Gogny (Egido 2016), Skyrme (Bender 2003), and relativistic (Nikšić 2011) interactions, to odd-mass nuclei. It builds on the authors' earlier blocked HFB studies of odd nuclei in the magnesium chain (Borrajo and Egido 2016, 2017) by adding full configuration mixing over the (β,γ) plane. The method enables the description of collective bands and transition probabilities in odd nuclei without effective charges, and the authors identify six bands in 25Mg, including two interpreted as collective gamma bands, providing a benchmark for future calculations in other odd-mass systems.
