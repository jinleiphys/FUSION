# In-medium polarization tensor in strong magnetic fields (I): Magneto-birefringence at finite temperature and density

> We investigate in-medium polarization effects of the fermion and antifermion pairs at finite temperature and density in strong magnetic fields within the lowest Landau level approximation. Inspecting the integral representation of the polarization tensor by analytic and numerical methods, we provide both the real and imaginary parts of the polarization tensor obtained after delicate interplay between the vacuum and medium contributions essentially due to the Pauli-blocking effect. Especially, we provide a complete analytic form of the polarization tensor at zero temperature and finite density that exhibits an exact cancellation and associated relocation of the singular threshold behaviors for a single photon decay to a fermion and antifermion pair. As a physical application of the in-medium polarization tensor, we discuss the magneto-birefringence that is polarization-dependent dispersion relations of photons induced by the strong magnetic fields.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete analytic form of the in-medium polarization tensor at zero temperature and finite density in strong magnetic fields, showing that the threshold for single-photon decay into a fermion-antifermion pair shifts from the fermion pair mass to the chemical potential due to Pauli blocking, and that the vacuum and medium contributions exhibit a delicate interplay rather than simple constructive addition.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop polarization tensor within the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation using the Ritus-basis formalism and real-time thermal field theory. They perform analytic and numerical evaluation of the integral representation of the polarization tensor, separating vacuum and medium contributions, and obtain the photon dispersion relation from the pole of the resummed photon propagator. The medium contribution is derived from the retarded correlator using the r-a basis propagators with Fermi-Dirac distribution functions at finite temperature T and chemical potential μ.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger mass parameter is defined as m_B² = |q_f B|/(2π) · q_f²/π, which sets the scale for the vacuum photon mass
- The threshold condition for fermion-antifermion pair creation in vacuum is q_∥² ≥ (2m)², where m is the fermion mass
- The LLL approximation requires the magnetic field strength to satisfy |e B| ≫ T, μ (strong field condition)
- The I function describing the vacuum polarization has limiting behaviors I(0) = 1 and I(∞) = 0
- In the massless limit, the vacuum scalar function becomes Π̃_∥^vac(q_∥²) = 1

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the polarization tensor in strong magnetic fields within the LLL approximation by Fukushima (2015) and provides a complete analytic expression that was previously only known in integral form. It complements recent investigations of the polarization tensor in all Landau levels focusing on the imaginary part by Wang (2020, 2021). The results enable quantitative predictions for magneto-birefringence in neutron-star physics and laser experiments, and provide the foundation for studying the axial Ward identity and chiral anomaly in magnetized media, which will be addressed in the second paper of this series.
