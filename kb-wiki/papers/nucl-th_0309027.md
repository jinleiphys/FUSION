# Pion parameters in nuclear medium from chiral perturbation theory and virial expansion

> We consider two methods to find the effective parameters of the pion traversing a nuclear medium. One is the first order chiral perturbation theoretic evaluation of the pion pole contribution to the two-point function of the axial-vector current. The other is the exact, first order virial expansion of the pion self-energy. We find that, although the results of chiral perturbation theory are not valid at normal nuclear density, those from the virial expansion may be reliable at such density. The latter predicts both the mass-shift and the in-medium decay width of the pion to be small, of about a few MeV.

# Pion parameters in nuclear medium from chiral perturbation theory and virial expansion

## Key claim

The authors find that first-order chiral perturbation theory (χPT) results for pion parameters in nuclear matter are not valid at normal nuclear density due to large cancellations and the presence of baryonic resonances near threshold, but the first-order virial expansion using experimental πN scattering data may be reliable at such densities, predicting both the mass shift and in-medium decay width of the pion to be small (about a few MeV).

## Method

The authors compute the pion self-energy in nuclear matter at zero temperature using two approaches: (1) one-loop chiral perturbation theory evaluation of the axial-vector current two-point function, using in-medium nucleon propagators with the 11-component replaced by the vacuum propagator plus a density-dependent term; (2) an exact first-order virial expansion that expresses the pion self-energy as an integral over the product of the nucleon density distribution function and the forward πN scattering amplitude taken from experimental data (Hohler). The virial formula is evaluated in the pion rest frame, with the integration variable transformed to the pion energy in the nucleon rest frame.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant in vacuum: F_π = 92.4 MeV
- Axial-vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.27
- Low-energy constants from πN scattering: c_1 = -0.81 ± 0.12 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 3.2 ± 0.25 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -4.66 ± 0.36 GeV^{-1}
- Normal nuclear density: n_0 = (110 MeV)^3
- At normal nuclear density, the virial expansion predicts both the pion mass shift and decay width to be about a few MeV
- The mean distance between nucleons at normal nuclear density is about 2 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier applications of chiral perturbation theory to pions in nuclear matter by Thorsson and Meissner, and compares with the virial expansion approach previously used by Smilga, Jeon, and Mallik. The authors note that earlier applications of the virial formula to this problem (by Ericson, Migdal, and Waas) used an approximated version that is not justified when the scattering amplitude varies appreciably and changes sign over the integration range. The results enable reliable determination of pion parameters in nuclear matter up to normal nuclear density, and the authors suggest the virial expansion method could be applied to the more interesting case of nucleon mass shifts in nuclear medium, where chiral Lagrangians produce absurdly large values (Montano).
