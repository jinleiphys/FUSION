# Incoherent diffractive production of jets in electron DIS off nuclei at high energy

> We study incoherent diffractive production of two and three jets in electron-nucleus deep inelastic scattering (DIS) at small $x_{\scriptscriptstyle \rm Bj}$ using the color dipole picture and the effective theory of the Color Glass Condensate (CGC). We consider color fluctuations in the CGC weight-function as the source of the nuclear break-up and the associated momentum transfer $\sqrt{|t|}$. We focus on the regime in which the two jets are almost back-to-back in transverse space and have transverse momenta $P_{\perp}$ much larger than both the momentum transfer and the saturation scale $Q_s$. The cross section for producing such a hard dijet is parametrically dominated by large size fluctuations in the projectile wave-function that scatter strongly and for which a third, semi-hard, jet appears in the final state. The 2 + 1 jets cross section can be written in a factorized form in terms of incoherent quark and gluon diffractive transverse momentum distributions (DTMDs) when the third jet is explicit, or incoherent diffractive parton distribution functions (DPDFs) when the third jet is integrated over. We find that the DPDFs and the corresponding cross section saturate logarithmically when $|t| \ll Q_s^2$, while they fall like $1/|t|^2$ in the regime $Q_s^2 \ll |t| \ll P_{\perp}^2$. We further show that there is no angular correlation between the hard jet momentum and the momentum transfer. For typical EIC kinematics the 2 jets and 2 + 1 jets cross sections are of the same order.

## Key claim

The cross section for producing a hard, almost back-to-back dijet in incoherent diffractive electron-nucleus DIS is parametrically dominated by large-size fluctuations in the projectile wavefunction that scatter strongly, leading to a third semi-hard jet in the final state; the 2+1 jets cross section can be written in a factorized form in terms of incoherent diffractive transverse momentum distributions (DTMDs) or diffractive parton distribution functions (DPDFs), but only when the third jet is either explicit or integrated over, respectively.

## Method

The authors use the color dipole picture and the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory to compute cross sections for incoherent diffractive dijet and trijet production in electron-nucleus DIS at small Bjorken x. They treat color fluctuations in the CGC weight-function as the source of nuclear breakup and momentum transfer, focusing on the regime where the two hard jets have transverse momenta much larger than both the momentum transfer and the saturation scale. The cross sections are derived by expanding the relevant QCD correlator in the Gaussian approximation and separating hard (P⊥-dependent) and semi-hard (Δ⊥-dependent) factors.

## Key numbers

- The saturation momentum Qs is approximately 1–1.5 GeV for typical heavy nuclei at small x.
- For a large nucleus like Pb or Au (A ≈ 200), the momentum transfer from geometrical fluctuations is Δ⊥ ≈ 30 MeV, while from color fluctuations it is Δ⊥ ∼ Qs ∼ 1–1.5 GeV.
- The DPDFs and corresponding cross section saturate logarithmically when |t| ≪ Qs², and fall like 1/|t|² in the regime Qs² ≪ |t| ≪ P⊥².
- For typical EIC kinematics, the 2 jets and 2+1 jets cross sections are of the same order.

## Context

This work extends the study of coherent diffractive jet production in photon-nucleus collisions (Iancu 2021, Iancu 2022, Iancu 2023, Hauksson 2024) to the incoherent case by incorporating color fluctuations in the target. It builds on the CGC framework for inclusive dijet production (Marquet 2007, Dominguez 2011, Iancu 2020) and on the Good-Walker picture of diffraction (Good 1960), where incoherent diffraction is proportional to fluctuations in the target. The results enable quantitative predictions for incoherent diffractive jet production at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), particularly for studying gluon saturation and the transition between dilute and dense regimes.
