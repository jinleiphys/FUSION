# Ab Initio Complex Scaling and Similarity Renormalization Group for Continuum Properties of Nuclei

> We introduce a novel \abinitio many-body method designed to compute the properties of nuclei in the continuum. This approach combines well-established techniques, namely the Complex Scaling (CS) and Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) methods while employing the translationally invariant No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) as a few-body solver. We demonstrate that this combination effectively overcomes numerical limitations previously encountered in exploring continuum properties of light nuclei with standard many-body techniques, and at the same time makes less imperative the need for a continuous set of basis states for the continuum. To benchmark the method for applications in the many-body sector, we apply it to the \textsuperscript{4}He system, where semi-exact calculations within a finite basis are feasible. Our extrapolated results agree with exact calculations already published in the literature. We argue that different NN parametrizations of chiral EFT Hamiltonians will not permit to reproduce evaluated resonance properties of \textsuperscript{4}He. As an application, we showcase the case of the tetraneutron. This work enables the application of the method to $A>4$-mass systems, providing a reliable representation of the initial Hamiltonian and its continuum properties.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a novel *ab initio* many-body method that combines Complex Scaling (CS) and Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) techniques to compute continuum properties of nuclei, overcoming previous numerical limitations that restricted CS to very light systems and small rotation angles (below about 0.3 rad). The authors demonstrate that applying SRG evolution *after* complex rotation, with a modified generator, can accelerate convergence for many-body calculations, but this approach is currently limited to CS angles of approximately 0.3 rad due to practical convergence considerations.

## Method

The method applies Complex Scaling to the Hamiltonian via a functional fitting of the NN interaction in momentum space, using a discrete basis of Gaussian-type functions to avoid numerical instabilities from oscillatory integrands at large rotation angles. The Similarity Renormalization Group evolution is then applied to the complex-scaled Hamiltonian using a modified generator that includes a factor of e^(4iθ) to compensate for the CS phase of the kinetic energy, enabling off-diagonal suppression. The translationally invariant No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) serves as the few-body solver for the resulting effective Hamiltonian, with calculations performed for the 4He system as a benchmark and the tetraneutron as an application.

## Key numbers

- The functional fitting approach with N_f=6 basis functions achieves approximately 2% error for the deuteron binding energy; with N_f=12, precision is below the keV threshold for all eigenvectors.
- The CS method is demonstrated to be stable for rotation angles up to 0.57 rad (72% of the harmonic oscillator basis θ_max of π/4) for the np system in the 3SD1 channel.
- For the triton ground state, SRG evolution with λ=2.0 fm^-1 at θ=0.3 rad accelerates convergence from requiring N_max=20 (bare) to N_max=10 (SRG-evolved).
- The 4He resonance calculations use SRG-evolved Hamiltonians with λ=2.1 fm^-1, θ=0.3 rad, and N_max values between 15 and 20.
- For the 0+;0 resonance in 4He, the extrapolated energy at θ=21° is 0.40 MeV with a width of 2.00 MeV, compared to R-matrix evaluated values of 0.39 MeV (energy) and 0.5 MeV (width).
- The 3N-induced interactions from SRG evolution affect resonance positions by a few hundred keV, less than the shift on binding energies (approximately 2 MeV for 4He, 500 keV for 3H and 3He).
- For the tetraneutron system, calculations at θ=0.3 rad with λ=2.1 fm^-1 and N_max=18 show no resonance at the hypothesized energy.

## Context

This work extends the Complex Scaling approach beyond previous applications by Papadimitriou (2015) and others, which were limited to small CS angles (θ ≤ 0.3 rad) and very light systems due to numerical instabilities. The method combines CS with SRG evolution (Jurgenson, 2011) using a modified generator, enabling application to A>4 mass systems. The benchmarks for 4He show agreement with exact few-body calculations by Lazauskas (2017) and with R-matrix evaluations from the TUNL Nuclear Data Project (Tilley, 1992). The tetraneutron results support the conclusions of numerous theoretical studies (Lazauskas, 2005; Hiyama, 2016; Carbonell, 2017; Deltuva, 2018; Higgins, 2020) that rule out a tetraneutron resonance, contradicting some experimental claims (Kisamori, 2016; Duer, 2022). The method enables future studies of continuum properties for nuclei up to A≈16 using exact spectroscopic methods, and can be extended to heavier nuclei with other *ab initio* techniques.
