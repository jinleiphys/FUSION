# The Phase Diagram of Neutral Quark Matter

> In this thesis, I study the phase diagram of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter as a function of the strange quark mass, the quark chemical potential, and the temperature, employing a general nine-parameter ansatz for the gap matrix. I also study the phase diagram of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter within the framework of a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. In the analysis, dynamically generated quark masses are taken into account self-consistently. The phase diagram in the plane of temperature and quark chemical potential is presented. In addition, I study the effect of neutrino trapping on the phase diagram of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter within the same NJL model. The phase diagrams in the plane of temperature and quark chemical potential, as well as in the plane of temperature and lepton-number chemical potential are presented. The implications of these results for the evolution of protoneutron stars are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

This thesis systematically maps the phase diagram of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter, showing that the ground state transitions from the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase to the gapless CFL (gCFL) phase at a critical strange quark mass, and that neutrino trapping favors two-flavor color superconductivity over the CFL phase at intermediate densities, but the abstract's claim of a "general nine-parameter ansatz" is hedged by the fact that the analysis is performed within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, not from first-principles QCD.

## Method

The author employs a nine-parameter ansatz for the color-flavor gap matrix to study the phase diagram of dense, locally neutral three-flavor quark matter as a function of strange quark mass, quark chemical potential, and temperature. Calculations are performed within a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, with dynamically generated quark masses taken into account self-consistently. The effect of neutrino trapping is also studied within the same NJL model, and phase diagrams are presented in the plane of temperature versus quark chemical potential and temperature versus lepton-number chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature, the transition point between the gaseous nuclear phase and the liquid nuclear phase occurs at a quark chemical potential of approximately 308 MeV, derived from the baryon chemical potential of 924 MeV for infinite isospin-symmetric nuclear matter bound by 16 MeV per baryon at normal nuclear density n0 ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³.
- The critical endpoint of the quark-hadron transition line from lattice QCD is at (T, μ) = (162 ± 2, 120 ± 14) MeV.
- The critical temperature for a color superconductor is given by Tc_BCS = (e^γ/π) Δ0 ≈ 0.567 Δ0, where γ ≈ 0.577 is the Euler-Mascheroni constant and Δ0 is the zero-temperature gap.
- A typical value for the bag constant used in the thesis is B^(1/4) = 0.17 GeV.
- The pressure difference for each gapped quasiparticle at weak coupling and zero temperature is δp = μ² Δ² / (4π²).
- In the CFL phase with massless quarks at zero temperature, the pressure is approximately p_CFL ≈ 3μ⁴/(4π²) + 3μ²Δ²/π² − B, using Δ₁ = 2Δ₂ ≡ 2Δ.
- Spin-one color-superconducting gaps are of the order of 100 keV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier schematic phase diagram of strongly interacting matter reviewed by Rischke (2003), which showed only the hadronic phase, quark-gluon plasma, and generic color superconductivity, by systematically mapping the neutral quark matter regime with multiple competing phases (CFL, gCFL, mCFL, uSC, 2SC, g2SC). The thesis builds on the discovery of the CFL phase by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and on the NJL-model studies of color superconductivity at moderate densities. The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star interiors, particularly for protoneutron star evolution where neutrino trapping is important, and provide a framework for future work to incorporate additional phases such as meson-condensed CFL matter or spin-one color superconductivity.
