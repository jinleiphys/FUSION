# Integrable models for confined fermions: applications to metallic grains

> We study integrable models for electrons in metals when the single particle spectrum is discrete. The electron-electron interactions are BCS-like pairing, Coulomb repulsion, and spin exchange coupling. These couplings are, in general, nonuniform in the sense that they depend on the levels occupied by the interacting electrons. By using the realization of spin 1/2-operators in terms of electrons the models describe spin 1/2 models with nonuniform long range interactions and external magnetic field. The integrability and the exact solution arise since the model Hamiltonians can be constructed in terms of Gaudin models. Uniform pairing and the resulting orthodox model correspond to an isotropic limit of the Gaudin Hamiltonians. We discuss possible applications of this model to a single grain and to a system of few interacting grains.

## Key claim

The paper constructs exactly solvable (integrable) generalizations of the universal Hamiltonian for metallic grains by allowing nonuniform coupling constants that depend on the single-particle levels involved, provided these couplings take a specific functional form derived from anisotropic Gaudin models; the authors do not claim that all physically relevant nonuniform couplings are integrable, only that a particular class parametrized by hyperbolic or trigonometric functions can be solved exactly.

## Method

The authors start from the second-quantized Hamiltonian for electrons in a confining potential with two-body interactions, separate the disorder-averaged "universal" part from fluctuations, and rewrite the Hamiltonian in terms of spin and charge su(2) operators. They then construct integrable generalizations by expressing the spin and charge Hamiltonians as linear combinations of commuting integrals of motion derived from anisotropic (XXZ-type) Gaudin models, which yields specific functional forms for the nonuniform coupling constants. The exact eigenstates are obtained as products of Bethe-type states, with rapidities satisfying generalized Richardson-Gaudin equations.

## Key numbers

- The nonuniversal corrections to the universal Hamiltonian are of relative order δ/E_Th, where δ is the average level spacing and E_Th is the Thouless energy.
- For the charge Hamiltonian with constant A_ij ≡ A, the eigenenergy is E_NK = p u Σ_jα η_j coth[p(ω_α - u_j)] - A N_K (Ω_K - N_K) - E_K.
- In the isotropic limit p→0, the coupling constants become g_ij = -u(η_i - η_j)/(u_i - u_j) and 4U_ij = A + u(η_i - η_j)/(u_i - u_j) for i≠j.
- For the spin Hamiltonian with uniform Zeeman splitting ζ_i ≡ μ_B B, the eigenenergy is E_NS = μ_B B (2N_S - Ω_S) + Σ_iα φ_i/(ν_α - v_i) - (1/4)Σ_ij J_ij - (1/2)Σ_i J_ii.

## Context

This work extends the universal Hamiltonian approach for metallic grains developed by Glazman (1990s), Altshuler (1990s), and others, which assumes uniform coupling constants. The exact solution of the uniform BCS-type Hamiltonian was previously found by Richardson and Sherman (1964) and later shown to be integrable via Gaudin models by Cambiaggio, Rivas, and Saraceno (1997) and by Amico, Di Lorenzo, and Osterloh (2001). The present paper generalizes these integrability results to nonuniform couplings by using anisotropic Gaudin Hamiltonians, enabling exact treatment of disorder-induced fluctuations that become important in ultra-small grains (as observed in non-equilibrium transport experiments by Agam and collaborators). The model can also describe systems of several weakly coupled grains, with inter-grain pair tunneling exponentially suppressed relative to intra-grain pairing.
