# Di-$J/ψ$ structures from the quark Pauli-blocking effect

> The double-charmonium scattering states such as $J/ψJ/ψ$, $η_cJ/ψ$, and $η_cη_c$ are investigated by a simplified quark cluster model. It is found that the quark Pauli-principle over the $c\bar c c\bar c$ system causes a rapid increase and a node in the two-meson phase shifts. The increase is not large enough to be regarded as a resonance, but if it is seen experimentally, that is most likely the quark Pauli-blocking effect.

## Key claim

The quark Pauli-blocking effect in the $c\bar c c\bar c$ system causes a rapid increase and a node in the two-meson phase shifts, but this increase is not large enough to be regarded as a resonance; if a structure is seen experimentally in the di-$J/\psi$ spectrum, it is most likely due to this quark many-body effect rather than a genuine resonance.

## Method

The authors use a simplified nonrelativistic quark cluster model with a resonating group method (RGM) approach, where $c$ and $\bar c$ quarks form meson clusters that are antisymmetrized to account for the quark Pauli principle. The Hamiltonian includes kinetic terms, a spin-independent color term, and color-spin interactions, with the quark interchange between mesons assumed to occur only when the four quarks form the $(0s)^4$ configuration. The RGM equation is solved to obtain scattering phase shifts and cross sections for the $J/\psi J/\psi$, $\eta_c J/\psi$, and $\eta_c \eta_c$ channels in $S$-wave with total angular momenta $J=0,1,2$.

## Key numbers

- The size parameter $\tilde b$ of the harmonic oscillator wave function for the $c\bar c$ meson is 0.385 fm, determined from the $m(\psi(2S))-m(J/\psi)\sim 600$ MeV splitting and a $c$-quark mass of 1500 MeV.
- The $J=2$ phase shift has a node at ${4\over 3}\omega_0$ above threshold, corresponding to approximately 6.5 GeV, with a rapid increase at around 6.3-6.4 GeV.
- For the $J=1$ channel ($\eta_c J/\psi$), the quark Pauli-blocking effect is expected to appear at around 6.2-6.3 GeV.
- The $\bar \nu$ matrix elements (quark interchange in spin-flavor-color space) are: $-{1\over 3}$ for $J=1$ and $J=2$; for $J=0$ the matrix is $\left(\begin{array}{cc} -{1\over 6} & \sqrt{1\over 12} \\ \sqrt{1\over 12} & {1\over 6} \end{array}\right)$.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical investigation of $c\bar c c\bar c$ exotic hadrons observed by LHCb, ATLAS, and CMS (2020-2024) at masses above the $2m(J/\psi)$ threshold (X(6200, 6600, 6900)), where previous quark model studies by Santowsky (2021), Ortega (2023), Meng (2024), and Wang (2023) found it difficult to produce resonances below 7 GeV due to repulsive or zero color-spin interactions and no long-range pion exchange. The quark Pauli-blocking effect, which has not been focused on in scattering states before, produces a well-and-barrier type potential that can cause rapid phase shift increases; this effect has already been implicitly included in some RGM scattering calculations such as Ortega (2023), where resonances below the $\eta_c\eta_c'$ threshold may correspond to the phase shift increases found here. The work enables experimental searches for these structures in di-$J/\psi$ and $\eta_c J/\psi$ channels as a direct probe of quark many-body effects in systems free from pion interactions.
