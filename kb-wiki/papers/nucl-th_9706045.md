# Peripheral Nucleon-Nucleon Phase Shifts and Chiral Symmetry

> Within the one-loop approximation of baryon chiral perturbation theory we calculate all one-pion and two-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction. In fact we construct the elastic NN-scattering amplitude up to and including third order in small momenta. The phase shifts with orbital angular momentum $L\geq2 $ and the mixing angles with $J\geq2$ are given parameterfree and thus allow for a detailed test of chiral symmetry in the two-nucleon system. We find that for the D-waves the $2π$-exchange corrections are too large as compared with empirical phase shifts, signaling the increasing importance of shorter range effects in lower partial waves. For higher partial waves, especially for G-waves, the model independent $2π$-exchange corrections bring the chiral prediction close to empirical NN phase shifts. We propose to use the chiral NN phase shifts with $L\geq 3$ as input in a future phase shift analysis. Furthermore, we compute the irreducible two-pion exchange NN-potentials in coordinate space. They turn out to be of van-der-Waals type, with exponential screening of two-pion mass range.

## Key claim

Within the one-loop approximation of baryon chiral perturbation theory, the authors calculate all one-pion and two-pion exchange contributions to the nucleon-nucleon interaction up to third order in small momenta, finding that for D-waves the two-pion-exchange corrections are too large compared to empirical phase shifts, while for higher partial waves (especially G-waves) the model-independent two-pion-exchange corrections bring the chiral prediction close to empirical NN phase shifts.

## Method

The authors used covariant perturbation theory with dimensional regularization to evaluate all one-pion and two-pion exchange diagrams contributing to the elastic NN-scattering amplitude up to third order in small momenta (p, q, and m_pi). They separated the planar box diagram into iterated one-pion exchange (enhanced by a factor M) and irreducible two-pion exchange by performing the l0-integration via contour methods before expanding in 1/M. The phase shifts with orbital angular momentum L >= 2 and mixing angles with J >= 2 were then computed perturbatively from the resulting T-matrix elements projected onto the LSJ-basis.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- Axial vector coupling constant: g_A = 1.32 (from Goldberger-Treiman relation with g_piN = 13.4)
- Average nucleon mass: M = 939 MeV
- Average pion mass: m_pi = 138 MeV
- Low-energy constants (in GeV^-1): c1 = -0.9 +/- 0.1, c2 = 3.3 +/- 0.2, c3 = -5.3 +/- 0.2, c4 = 3.6 +/- 0.1
- NNpi0-threshold: T_lab = 280 MeV
- S-wave scattering lengths: a(3S1) = -5.42 fm, a(1S0) = 23.75 fm
- Deuteron pole at T_lab = -4.45 MeV
- Empirical mixing angles epsilon_J do not exceed 7 degrees up to the NNpi0-threshold
- P- and D-wave phase shifts change by at most 30 degrees from threshold to T_lab = 280 MeV
- F- and G-waves do not exceed values of 7 degrees up to T_lab = 280 MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to the NN interaction pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and applied by Ordonez, Ray and van Kolck (1994), but differs by using covariant perturbation theory with dimensional regularization rather than time-ordered perturbation theory with a Gaussian cut-off. The authors aim to identify the kinematical window in energy and angular momentum where the NN-interaction is governed by chiral symmetry alone, proposing that chiral NN phase shifts with L >= 3 can serve as input in future phase shift analyses. The results contrast with the one-boson-exchange model (Bonn potential) which uses adjustable cut-offs and form factors, and the irreducible two-pion exchange potentials computed in coordinate space are found to be of van-der-Waals type with exponential screening of two-pion mass range.
