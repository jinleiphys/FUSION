# Nuclear Spin-Isospin Correlations, Parity Violation, and the $f_π$ Problem

> The strong interaction effects of isospin- and spin-dependent nucleon-nucleon correlations observed in many-body calculations are interpreted in terms of a one-pion exchange mechanism. Including such effects in computations of nuclear parity violating effects leads to enhancements of about 10%. A larger effect arises from the one-boson exchange nature of the parity non-conserving nucleon- nucleon interaction, which depends on both weak and strong meson-nucleon coupling constants. Using values of the latter that are constrained by nucleon-nucleon phase shifts leads to enhancements of parity violation by factors close to two. Thus much of previously noticed discrepancies between weak coupling constants extracted from different experiments can be removed.

## Key claim

Including spin-isospin correlations from one-pion exchange enhances parity-violating nuclear observables by about 10%, but a larger effect (enhancements by factors close to two) arises from using strong meson-nucleon coupling constants constrained by nucleon-nucleon phase shifts rather than the original DDH values, which can remove much of the previously noticed discrepancies between weak coupling constants extracted from different experiments.

## Method

The author constructs a model of the parity-violating single-particle potential for a nucleon outside a core approximated as infinite nuclear matter, using a Brueckner-type reaction matrix that incorporates one-pion exchange correlations via a two-potential theorem. The strong interaction correlation function is modeled with a Miller-Spencer type function plus a one-pion exchange correction, with parameters chosen to reproduce the pair-correlation functions of Akmal and Pandharipande (1997). The resulting parity-violating single-particle potential is evaluated numerically, and the strong coupling constants are then replaced with values from the Bonn OBEPR potential that fit nucleon-nucleon phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The spin-isospin correlation effects enhance the coefficients of each weak coupling constant term in the parity-violating single-particle potential by about 10% (e.g., the coefficient of \(f_\pi\) increases from 24 to 26.43, and of \(h_\rho^0\) from -6.9 to -8.1)
- Using Bonn OBEPR strong coupling constants (e.g., \(g_\omega = 20\) instead of DDH's 8.37, \(\chi_V = 6.1\) instead of 3.7) increases the vector meson contributions to the parity-violating potential by a factor of 1.9
- The parity-violating neutron spin rotation coefficient \(g_n\) increases from 0.2 (with DDH parameters) to 1.9 (with Bonn parameters), and to 3.2 if \(f_\pi = 1\)
- The correlation model uses \(\omega = 2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.36\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)

## Context

This work extends the standard DDH parity-violating nucleon-nucleon potential framework (Desplanques, Donoghue and Holstein, 1980) by incorporating spin-isospin correlations from variational nuclear matter studies (Akmal and Pandharipande, 1997; S. C. Pieper et al., 1992) and by using strong coupling constants from the Bonn potential (Machleidt, 1989) that fit nucleon-nucleon phase shifts. The results suggest that the long-standing "\(f_\pi\) problem", where the weak pion-nucleon coupling extracted from the \({}^{133}\)Cs anapole moment (Wood et al., 1997) disagrees with that from \({}^{18}\)F circular polarization data, can be largely resolved, though the \({}^{133}\)Cs result remains somewhat inconsistent. The paper calls for an updated effective field theory treatment of parity violation in nucleon-nucleon interactions, as advocated by Holstein.
