# Local Bayesian Dirichlet mixing of imperfect models

> To improve the predictability of complex computational models in the experimentally-unknown domains, we propose a Bayesian statistical machine learning framework utilizing the Dirichlet distribution that combines results of several imperfect models. This framework can be viewed as an extension of Bayesian stacking. To illustrate the method, we study the ability of Bayesian model averaging and mixing techniques to mine nuclear masses. We show that the global and local mixtures of models reach excellent performance on both prediction accuracy and uncertainty quantification and are preferable to classical Bayesian model averaging. Additionally, our statistical analysis indicates that improving model predictions through mixing rather than mixing of corrected models leads to more robust extrapolations.

## Key claim

The authors propose a Bayesian Dirichlet model mixing framework (Local Bayesian Model Mixing, LBMM) that improves prediction accuracy and uncertainty quantification for nuclear mass extrapolations compared to Bayesian Model Averaging (BMA), but the local mixtures fall slightly behind global mixtures on the testing dataset, which the authors attribute to difficult tuning of the statistical model and a limited testing dataset.

## Method

The authors develop a Bayesian framework using Dirichlet distributions to combine predictions from multiple nuclear mass models, where model weights can be either global (constant across the nuclear landscape) or local (varying with proton number Z and neutron number N). For local mixtures, they implement two variants: a Generalized Linear Dirichlet model (GLD) with linear dependence of log-parameters on coordinates, and a Gaussian Process Dirichlet model (GPD) using squared exponential kernels. The method is applied to predict two-neutron separation energies of even-even nuclei using seven nuclear density functional theory models plus two phenomenological mass models, trained on 521 nuclei from AME2003 and tested on 59 nuclei from AME2020.

## Key numbers

- Training dataset: 521 even-even nuclei from AME2003; testing dataset: 59 nuclei from AME2020
- GBMM+D (global Dirichlet mixture) of uncorrected models achieves 0.31 MeV test rms deviation, compared to 0.32 MeV for BMA(ex) on uncorrected models
- LBMM+GPD achieves 0.25 MeV training rms and 0.33 MeV testing rms for uncorrected models, with noise scale sigma = 0.26(1) MeV
- Individual model test rms values range from 0.40 MeV (HFB-24) to 1.14 MeV (SkM*) for uncorrected models
- BMA(ex) on corrected models gives 0.35 MeV test rms, while GBMM+L on corrected models gives 0.41 MeV test rms
- The GBMM+D global weights for uncorrected models assign 0.57 to HFB-24, 0.29 to FRDM-2012, and 0.07 to UNEDF2

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous BMA studies of nuclear masses (Neufcourt 2019, 2020a, 2020b; Kejzlar 2020; Hamaker 2021) by introducing a Bayesian stacking approach that does not assume any single model is true, unlike BMA. The method builds on Bayesian stacking frameworks (Le Clarke 2017; Gelman 2018, 2022) and addresses the limitation that BMA weights are global and cannot capture local model preferences. The results enable more robust extrapolations of nuclear masses to experimentally unknown regions, which is important for understanding nucleosynthesis in stars and for applications in atomic and high-energy physics.
