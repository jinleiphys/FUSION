# Investigating excited $Ω_c$ states from pentaquark perspective

> Inspired by the recent observation of new $Ω_c^0$ states by the LHCb Collaboration, we explore the excited $Ω_{c}$ states from the pentaquark perspective in the quark delocalization color screening model. Our results indicate that the $Ω_c(3185)$ can be well interpreted as a molecular $ΞD$ predominated resonance state with $J^P=1/2^-$. The $Ω_c(3120)$ can also be interpreted as a molecular $Ξ_c^* \bar{K}$ state with $J^P=3/2^-$ and a new molecular state $Ξ^*_c \bar{K}^*$ with $J^P=5/2^-$ and a mass of 3527 MeV is predicted, which is worth searching in the future. Other reported $Ω_c$ states cannot be well described in the framework of pentaquark systems in present work. The three-quark excited state, or the unquenched picture may be a good explanation, which is worth further exploration.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the newly observed $\Omega_c(3185)$ can be well interpreted as a molecular $\Xi D$ resonance state with $J^P=1/2^-$, and $\Omega_c(3120)$ as a molecular $\Xi_c^* \bar{K}$ bound state with $J^P=3/2^-$, but only within the specific framework of the quark delocalization color screening model; other reported $\Omega_c$ states could not be described as pentaquark systems in this work.

## Method
The authors used the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM) to systematically investigate $ssc\bar{q}q$ pentaquark systems with quantum numbers $I=0$, $J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-$ and $5/2^-$. They performed dynamic bound-state calculations using the resonating group method and studied scattering phase shifts of open channels to identify genuine resonance states, considering three spatial structures ($qss-\bar{q}c$, $qsc-\bar{q}s$, $ssc-\bar{q}q$) and their couplings.

## Key numbers
- $\Omega_c(3185)$: corrected resonance mass 3174 to 3182 MeV and decay width 42 MeV from the $\Xi D$ resonance state with $J^P=1/2^-$, consistent with experimental mass $3185.1 \pm 1.7_{-0.9}^{+7.4} \pm 0.2$ MeV and width $50 \pm 7_{-20}^{+10}$ MeV
- $\Omega_c(3120)$: corrected bound state mass 3138 MeV from the $\Xi_c^* \bar{K}$ state with $J^P=3/2^-$, close to experimental mass $3119.1 \pm 0.3 \pm 0.9 \pm 0.3$ MeV
- New predicted molecular state $\Xi^*_c \bar{K}^*$ with $J^P=5/2^-$ and mass 3527 MeV
- Root mean square of cluster spacing: 1.9 fm for the $\Xi D$ resonance state and 1.8 fm for the $\Xi_c^* \bar{K}$ bound state

## Context
This work extends previous studies of excited $\Omega_c$ states observed by LHCb (2017, 2023) using the QDCSM, which had previously been applied to hidden-charm pentaquark systems and successfully predicted $P_c$ states (Huang et al.). The results contradict interpretations of $\Omega_c(3185)$ and $\Omega_c(3327)$ as conventional three-quark excitations proposed by Luo (2023) and Yu (2023), but support molecular assignments similar to those from Feng (2023) and Xin (2023). The study enables future searches for the predicted $\Xi^*_c \bar{K}^*$ state and suggests that other $\Omega_c$ states may require three-quark excited state or unquenched picture explanations.
