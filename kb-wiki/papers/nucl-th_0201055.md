# Dynamical Effects beyond Mean Field in Drip Line Nuclei

> New structure problems are raised in nuclei near drip lines by recent RIB experiments. We pointed out the importance of dynamical effect beyond mean field theories to the new structure problems.

## Key claim

Dynamical correlations beyond mean field, specifically particle-vibration coupling, can substantially modify single-particle energies in drip-line nuclei, but the effects are qualitatively different from those in heavy nuclei because of phase-space limitations and selection rules that can block or reverse the usual sign of the energy shifts.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with Skyrme interactions to obtain zeroth-order single-particle energies and wave functions for the cores of \(^{10}\)Be and \(^{24}\)O. They then coupled these to natural parity vibrational states (\(J^\pi = 1^-, 2^+, 3^-\)) using a particle-vibration coupling Hamiltonian derived from the same Skyrme interaction, calculating second-order energy shifts from the real part of the four diagrams shown in Fig. 1. For \(^{10}\)Be, a phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential was used instead of HF, with empirical phonon energies and transition strengths.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{10}\)Be with the SIII Skyrme force, the HF single-particle energies are \(\varepsilon(1p_{1/2}) = -3.80\) MeV and \(\varepsilon(1p_{3/2}) = -11.13\) MeV; with a standard Woods-Saxon potential they become \(\varepsilon(1p_{1/2}) = -3.93\) MeV and \(\varepsilon(1p_{3/2}) = -9.36\) MeV.
- The lowest \(2^+_1\) state in \(^{10}\)Be has an excitation energy of 3.37 MeV and a reduced transition probability \(B(E2, 0\rightarrow 2^+_1) = 52\) e\(^2\)fm\(^4\).
- Coupling to the \(2^+_1\) phonon in \(^{10}\)Be reduces the \(2s_{1/2}\)-\(1p_{1/2}\) gap from 5 MeV to 2 MeV, melting the N=8 shell closure.
- For \(^{24}\)O, the self-consistent RPA calculation gives the lowest \(2^+\) state at \(E = 3.90\) MeV with proton moment \(M_p = 1.8\) fm\(^2\) and neutron moment \(M_n = 10\) fm\(^2\).
- Two low-lying \(3^-\) states in \(^{24}\)O at 6.07 MeV and 7.33 MeV absorb 22% and 19% of the energy-weighted sum rule strength, respectively.

## Context

This work extends the study of dynamical correlations beyond mean field in light neutron-rich nuclei, building on earlier particle-vibration coupling studies in heavy nuclei near \(^{208}\)Pb (Mahaux 1985; Bohr and Mottelson 1975). It directly addresses the melting of the N=8 shell closure observed experimentally in \(^{11}\)Be (Simon 1999), \(^{11}\)Li (Keller 1994), and \(^{12}\)Be (Iwa 2000), and the disappearance of the N=20 shell closure in \(^{32}\)Mg (Mg32). The results differ from those of NVM (Nunes, Thompson, and Varley) who obtained inversion of the \(2s_{1/2}\) and \(1p_{1/2}\) levels in \(^{11}\)Be using a non-standard Woods-Saxon parameterization with large diffuseness \(a = 0.9\) fm and modified spin-orbit coupling. The present work enables future experimental searches for the predicted collective \(3^-\) states in \(^{24}\)O to test the validity of the calculated shell gaps.
