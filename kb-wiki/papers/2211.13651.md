# Transverse momentum decorrelation of the flow vector in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV

> The individual studies of the anisotropic flow vector, flow angle and flow magnitude fluctuations with multi-particle correlations provide insight into the initial conditions and properties of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) created in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. Recent measurements of these fluctuations have been available and the comparison to hydrodynamic models shows unique sensitivities to the initial conditions of the system, but also a puzzling dependence on the specific shear viscosity $η/s$. In this paper, a systematic study with A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model using different tunings of the initial conditions, partonic cross section and hadronic interactions investigates the $p_{\rm T}$-dependent flow vector, flow angle and flow magnitude fluctuations. It is found that the transport model reasonably describes the flow vector, flow angle and flow magnitude fluctuations observed in data and that the fluctuations are driven by fluctuations in the initial state. The comparison of data and model presented in this paper enables further constraints on the initial conditions of the heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the AMPT transport model reasonably describes the pT-dependent flow vector, flow angle, and flow magnitude fluctuations observed in ALICE data, but the model overestimates the deviation from unity for the flow vector ratio v2{2}/v2[2] and the factorization ratio r2, especially in non-central collisions, indicating that further tuning of the initial conditions is needed.

## Method
The authors performed a systematic study using the AMPT model with five different parameter tunings, varying the Lund string parameters (a and b) for initial conditions, the partonic cross section (1.5 mb vs 3.0 mb, corresponding to specific shear viscosities of approximately 0.28 and 0.18), and the hadronic rescattering time (ART ON vs OFF). They calculated two-particle correlations (v2{2}/v2[2] and factorization ratio r2) and four-particle correlations (flow angle fluctuations A2f and flow magnitude fluctuations M2f) using the Generic Framework with subevent pseudorapidity gaps to suppress non-flow.

## Key numbers
- The flow vector ratio v2{2}/v2[2] shows deviation from unity largest in 0-5% central collisions, decreasing with centrality; changing Lund string parameters affects the ratio by up to 35% in central collisions and up to 30% in non-central collisions.
- The factorization ratio r2 shows deviation from unity largest in 0-5% central collisions; changing Lund string parameters affects r2 by up to 40% in central collisions and up to 30% in non-central collisions for large |pta-ptt|.
- Flow angle fluctuations A2f show deviation from unity for pT > 2.5 GeV/c for most configurations, with Par3 (a=0.5, b=0.9) showing deviation for pT > 1.5 GeV/c; changing initial conditions affects A2f by up to 70% in the 20-30% centrality range.
- Flow magnitude fluctuations M2f show strongest deviation from unity in 0-5% central collisions; changing Lund string parameters affects M2f by up to 20%.
- The partonic cross section change from 1.5 mb to 3.0 mb affects v2{2}/v2[2] by at most 10% and r2 by up to 10% at high |pta-ptt|.
- Hadronic rescattering affects v2{2}/v2[2] by at most 10-20% and r2 by around 10% at high |pta-ptt|.

## Context
This work extends the study of pT-dependent flow vector fluctuations from hydrodynamic models (Bozek 2018, Bozek 2021) and ALICE measurements (ALICE 2022) by providing the first systematic transport model study using AMPT. The results show that flow angle and flow magnitude fluctuations are driven primarily by initial state fluctuations rather than transport properties, contradicting the puzzling eta/s-dependence observed in hydrodynamic models by Bozek (2021) at low pT. The comparison enables further constraints on the initial conditions of heavy-ion collisions, particularly the Lund string parameters in HIJING.
