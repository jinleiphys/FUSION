# Phases of Quench Dynamics in the Presence of Fluctuation

> We study the effect of thermal fluctuations on a sourced quench in a system with $Z_2$ symmetry. By ignoring the fluctuation of finite momentum modes and tracing the dynamics of zero momentum mode driven by a spatially homogeneous source near the critical point, we map out a phase diagram for the quench dynamics. The phase diagram consists of three different phases. Phase I occurs for large fluctuations with the relaxation time set by fluctuation induced inverse effective mass square. Phase II occurs for small fluctuation and slow quench rate. The dynamics is characterized by a modified Kibble-Zurek scaling. Phase III corresponds to small fluctuations and rapid quench rate. The relaxation time tends to a finite value in the rapid quench limit. We also estimate the fluctuations of finite momentum modes, finding significant enhancement of the effective mass square. We speculate the qualitative features of the phase diagram may remain the same, but enhanced fluctuations can lead to shrinkage of phase II and III.

## Key claim

The paper claims that thermal fluctuations can fundamentally alter the scaling behavior of quench dynamics near a critical point, producing a phase diagram with three distinct dynamical phases. The key hedge is that the analysis ignores fluctuations of finite momentum modes and truncates to only the zero momentum mode, and the authors speculate the qualitative features may remain the same but enhanced fluctuations could shrink phases II and III.

## Method

The authors study a stochastic model (model A in the Hohenberg-Halperin classification) with Z2 symmetry, described by a Langevin equation for the order parameter field with Gaussian white noise. They consider a sourced quench driven by a spatially homogeneous source J(t) = tanh(vt) and trace only the dynamics of the zero momentum mode, truncating the cubic coupling term to its zero-momentum contribution. They solve the resulting stochastic differential equation numerically for different values of quench rate v and noise strength kappa, generating 10^4 independent solutions per parameter set to obtain statistical averages, and also perform analytic calculations in limiting regimes.

## Key numbers

- For phase I (large fluctuations, slow quench), the dynamical relaxation time scales as t0 ~ kappa^{-1/2}, with the mean field crossing time given by t0 = 1/[(3+c)a*kappa^{1/2}] where a ≈ 0.48 and ac ≈ -0.4 from numerical solutions.
- For the noise-free rapid quench limit (v → infinity), the crossing time is t0 = (1/18)(2*sqrt(3)*pi + 6*ln 2) ≈ 0.84.
- For large noise and rapid quench (kappa >> 1, v → infinity), the crossing time is t0 = ln(1+dC)/(C*kappa^{1/2}) ≈ 0.66*kappa^{-1/2}, with C ≈ 1.046 and d = 2*sqrt(2)*Gamma(3/4)/Gamma(1/4).
- For large noise and slow quench (kappa >> 1, v << kappa^{1/2}), the crossing time is t0 ≈ 0.96*kappa^{-1/2}.
- The transition between phase I and phase II is estimated to occur at kappa ~ v^{4/5}.
- For kappa = 0.1 in phase II, the modified Kibble-Zurek exponent is b ≈ 0.29, with t_KZ ~ v^{-b}.

## Context

This work extends the Kibble-Zurek mechanism (Kibble 1976, Zurek 1985) by introducing thermal fluctuations as a new ingredient in quench dynamics near a critical point. It builds on prior studies of deviation from KZ scaling in experiments (Dodd 1998, Ko 2019, Goo 2021) and numerical simulations (Chesler 2014, del Campo 2021), where no consensus on the explanation had been reached. The paper also connects to the hydrodynamic effective field theory framework (Kovtun 2012, Akamatsu 2016) where fluctuations modify long-time behavior through corrections to dispersion relations. The results enable future work to include finite momentum mode fluctuations and to test the predicted phase diagram in cold atom or heavy ion collision experiments.
