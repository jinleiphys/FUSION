# Cranked Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov Theory: probing the gateway to superheavy nuclei

> The cranked relativistic Hartree+Bogoliubov theory has been applied for a systematic study of the nuclei around 254No, the heaviest elements for which detailed spectroscopic data are available. The deformation, rotational response, pairing correlations, quasi-particle and other properties of these nuclei have been studied with different parametrizations for the effective mean-field Lagrangian. Pairing correlations are taken into account by a finite range two-body force of Gogny type. While the deformation properties are well reproduced, the calculations reveal some deficiencies of the effective forces both in the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. For the first time, the quasi-particle spectra of odd deformed nuclei have been calculated in a fully self-consistent way within the framework of the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The energies of the spherical subshells, from which active deformed states of these nuclei emerge, are described with an accuracy better than 0.5 MeV for most of the subshells with the NL1 and NL3 parametrizations. However, for a few subshells the discrepancies reach 0.7-1.0 MeV. In very heavy systems, where the level density is high, this level of accuracy is not sufficient for reliable predictions of the location of relatively small deformed shell gaps. The calculated moments of inertia reveal only small sensitivity to the RMF parametrization and, thus, to differences in the single-particle structure. However, in contrast to lighter systems, it is necessary to decrease the strength of the D1S Gogny force in the pairing channel in order to reproduce the moments of inertia.

## Key claim

The cranked relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (CRHB) theory, with approximate particle number projection via the Lipkin-Nogami method, reproduces deformation properties of nuclei around 254No well, but the effective forces show deficiencies in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels; specifically, the strength of the D1S Gogny force in the pairing channel must be reduced to reproduce the moments of inertia, in contrast to lighter systems where the original strength works.

## Method

The CRHB theory with the Lipkin-Nogami method (CRHB+LN) was applied to systematically study nuclei around 254No, using five different RMF parametrizations (NL1, NL-Z, NL3, NLSH, NL-RA1) for the effective mean-field Lagrangian. Pairing correlations were treated with a finite-range two-body Gogny force (D1S set), and the scaling factor of this force was adjusted to reproduce the experimental kinematic moment of inertia of 254No at rotational frequency 0.15 MeV. The equations were solved in a basis of an anisotropic three-dimensional harmonic oscillator with shells up to N_F=14 for fermions and N_B=16 for bosons.

## Key numbers

- The energies of spherical subshells are described with accuracy better than 0.5 MeV for most subshells with NL1 and NL3 parametrizations; for a few subshells discrepancies reach 0.7-1.0 MeV.
- The kinematic moment of inertia J^(1) for 254No in CRMF calculations without pairing is approximately 87 MeV^{-1} up to Omega_x ~ 0.26 MeV.
- CRHB+LN calculations with the original D1S force (f=1.0) underestimate experimental kinematic and dynamic moments of inertia by about 25% for 254No.
- The total angular momentum gain at the band crossing in 254No is approximately 17 hbar, with proton and neutron contributions of about 7 hbar and 10 hbar, respectively.
- The scaling factor f for the Gogny D1S force was chosen to reproduce the experimental kinematic moment of inertia of 254No at rotational frequency 0.15 MeV; the values found for various RMF parametrizations are nearly the same (given in Table I, not fully shown in text).
- The difference between two prescriptions for center-of-mass motion correction in the A~250 mass region does not exceed 0.3 MeV, affecting two-particle separation energies by at most 0.030 MeV.
- Basis truncation at N_F=14 and N_B=16 yields kinematic moment of inertia values differing by less than 0.75% from values with N_F=18 and N_B=18; charge quadrupole moment by less than 0.9%; mass hexadecapole moment by less than 3.4%; binding energies by less than 0.1%; separation energies S_{2n} and delta_{2n} by less than 40 keV each.

## Context

This work extends the application of self-consistent relativistic mean-field theory to the heaviest deformed nuclei with detailed spectroscopic data, specifically the region around 254No. It goes beyond the earlier RMF comparison by Ref. BRBRMG (1998), which was restricted to binding energies and used BCS pairing without particle number projection. The results enable a critical test of RMF parametrizations for predicting superheavy nuclei properties, showing that while deformation is well described, the single-particle energies are not accurate enough (0.7-1.0 MeV discrepancies for some subshells) to reliably predict deformed shell gaps in the superheavy region where level density is high. The need to reduce the Gogny D1S pairing strength contrasts with successful applications in the A~75, A~160-170, and A~190 mass regions, and suggests that pairing properties of the Gogny force need adjustment far from the Sn isotopes where they were fitted.
