# Quasiparticle Coupled Cluster Theory for Pairing Interactions

> We present an extension of the pair coupled cluster doubles (p-CCD) method to quasiparticles and apply it to the attractive pairing Hamiltonian. Near the transition point where number symmetry gets spontaneously broken, the proposed BCS-based p-CCD method yields significantly better energies than existing methods when compared to exact results obtained via solution of the Richardson equations. The quasiparticle p-CCD method has a low computational cost of $\mathcal{O}(N^3)$ as a function of system size. This together with the high quality of results here demonstrated, points to considerable promise for the accurate description of strongly correlated systems with more realistic pairing interactions.

## Key claim

The paper extends the pair coupled cluster doubles (p-CCD) method to a quasiparticle (BCS-based) reference, showing that this BCS-based p-CCD yields significantly better energies than existing methods near the Hartree-Fock to BCS transition point for the attractive pairing Hamiltonian, though the method's accuracy degrades somewhat in the immediate vicinity of the transition and the energy exhibits a first-order (derivative-discontinuous) behavior there.

## Method

The authors formulate a quasiparticle coupled cluster theory using a BCS mean-field vacuum as the reference, with a cluster operator restricted to pair excitations (two-quasiparticle creation operators). They solve the projective coupled cluster equations for the cluster amplitudes and energy, and optionally supplement the amplitude equations with a Brueckner condition that self-consistently adjusts the BCS reference (yielding BCS p-BCCD). The method is applied to the reduced BCS (pairing) Hamiltonian with equally spaced single-particle levels, and results are compared to exact Richardson solutions, Hartree-Fock-based p-CCD, and number-projected BCS (PBCS).

## Key numbers

- For the half-filled pairing Hamiltonian with 100 levels, BCS p-CCD and BCS p-BCCD recover a higher fraction of correlation energy than HF p-CCD near the transition point G_c, where HF p-CCD breaks down and predicts a complex energy.
- The computational cost of the quasiparticle p-CCD method scales as O(N^3) as a function of system size.
- For the 100-level half-filled system at G/Δε = 1, BCS p-CCD reduces errors in single-particle occupation probabilities by roughly a factor of two compared to BCS.
- For the 100-level half-filled system, BCS p-CCD reduces the particle-number fluctuation σ_N^2 by approximately half compared to BCS.
- For the 100-level half-filled system, the BCS p-CCD pairing parameter Δ_c is more accurate than PBCS for both weakly attractive and strongly attractive cases.
- As system size increases (with G scaled to keep the BCS gap constant), PBCS degrades in accuracy while the relative error from BCS p-CCD decreases; for 100 levels at G=0.24 and G=0.40, the CC method shows improving relative accuracy with increasing number of levels.

## Context

This work extends the pair coupled cluster doubles (p-CCD) method, previously shown by Ayers and coworkers (AP1roG) to be equivalent to zero-seniority diagonalization (DOCI) for molecular systems, to a BCS quasiparticle reference. It addresses the breakdown of HF-based p-CCD and self-consistent RPA methods near the Hartree-Fock to BCS transition, which was documented by Jorge and coworkers for the pairing Hamiltonian. The method enables accurate description of strongly correlated pairing systems with mild computational scaling, and the authors note that the approach can be generalized to any two-body Hamiltonian expressible in terms of number and pair operators, with a more general Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov-based coupled cluster technique to be presented in future work by Duguet and coworkers.
