# Mass and radius constraints for compact stars and the QCD phase diagram

> We suggest a new Bayesian analysis using disjunct M-R constraints for extracting probability measures for cold, dense matter equations of state. One of the key issues of such an analysis is the question of a deconfinement transition in compact stars and whether it proceeds as a crossover or rather as a first order transition. The latter question is relevant for the possible existence of a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram under scrutiny in present and upcoming heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Bayesian analysis method that uses three statistically independent mass-radius constraints (none from bursting sources) to extract probability measures for cold dense matter equations of state, with the specific aim of determining whether a first-order deconfinement phase transition occurs in compact stars and whether such a transition could indicate a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram.

## Method

The authors construct a three-parameter hybrid equation of state (EoS) combining the hadronic APR model with a bag-like quark matter EoS, varying the transition energy density, the energy density jump (latent heat), and the squared speed of sound in quark matter. They generate 1000 hybrid EoS by sampling these parameters, solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to obtain mass-radius and mass-baryon mass sequences, and then apply Bayesian analysis using three observational constraints: the maximum mass from PSR J0348+0432, a radius constraint from PSR J0437-4715, and a gravitational binding energy constraint from the neutron star B in J0737-3039.

## Key numbers

- Pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD: \(T_c = (154 \pm 9)\) MeV (Bazavov 2011)
- Chemical freeze-out temperature from ALICE at \(\sqrt{s}=2.7\) TeV: \(T_{\rm fo} = (156 \pm 5)\) MeV (Becattini 2012)
- Parameter ranges sampled: transition density \(400 < \epsilon_c < 1000\) MeV/fm\(^3\), energy density jump ratio \(0 < \gamma < 1.0\), squared speed of sound \(0.3 < c^2_{\rm QM} < 1.0\)
- Maximum mass constraint: \(\mu_A = 2.01 M_\odot\) with \(\sigma_A = 0.04 M_\odot\) from PSR J0348+0432 (Antoniadis 2013)
- Radius constraint: \(\mu_B = 15.5\) km at mass \(1.7 M_\odot\) with \(\sigma_B = 1.5\) km from PSR J0437-4715 (Bogdanov 2012)
- Gravitational binding constraint: mean mass \(\mu = 1.249 M_\odot\), mean baryon mass \(\mu_B = 1.360 M_\odot\), with standard deviations \(\sigma_M = 0.001 M_\odot\) and \(\sigma_{M_B} = 0.002 M_\odot\) (Kitaura 2006)
- Maximum probabilities for the most likely EoS are about 1%

## Context

This work extends the pioneering Bayesian analysis of Steiner (2010) by using three statistically independent constraints that avoid the systematic bias toward smaller radii suspected in burst-source analyses (Miller 2010, Trumper 2011). The method is designed to test whether a strong first-order phase transition in massive neutron stars around \(2 M_\odot\) can produce a "third family" (mass twins) of hybrid stars, which would provide astrophysical evidence for a critical endpoint in the QCD phase diagram (Blaschke 2013, Alvarez-Castillo 2013). The approach enables future observational campaigns to distinguish between equations of state with and without phase transitions through precise radius measurements of massive neutron stars.
