# Transverse momentum dependent transversely polarized distributions at next-to-next-to-leading-order

> We calculate the matching of the transversity and pretzelosity transverse momentum dependent distributions (TMD) on transversity collinear distribution at the next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO). We find that the matching coefficient for pretzelosity distribution is zero, despite the matrix element for it is nontrivial. This result suggests that the pretzelosity matches a twist-4 distribution. The matching for transversity TMD distributions is provided for both parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions cases.

## Key claim

The matching coefficient for the pretzelosity transverse momentum dependent distribution (TMD) onto the transversity collinear distribution is zero at both one-loop and two-loop order, despite the matrix element for pretzelosity being nontrivial; this result suggests that pretzelosity matches a twist-4 distribution rather than a twist-2 one.

## Method

The authors evaluate the operator product expansion (OPE) matching of transversity and pretzelosity TMDs onto the collinear transversity parton distribution function (PDF) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in QCD perturbation theory, using the delta-regulator scheme for rapidity divergences and dimensional regularization for ultraviolet divergences. They compute quark matrix elements of the TMD operators with free quark states, project onto the appropriate tensor structures for transversity and pretzelosity, and extract the matching coefficients by comparing with the OPE expression. The calculation follows the same diagrammatic approach as the unpolarized case, with the pretzelosity projector involving a traceless tensor structure that simplifies many diagrams.

## Key numbers

- The NLO matching coefficient for transversity TMD contains a term \(-C_F \zeta_2 \delta(\bar x)\) as the finite part, where \(\zeta_2 = \pi^2/6\).
- The NNLO matching coefficient for transversity TMD in the quark-to-quark channel includes a constant term \(C_F^2 (5\zeta_4/4) + C_F C_A (5\zeta_4 - 77\zeta_3/9 - 67\zeta_2/6 + 1214/81) + C_F N_f (14\zeta_3/9 + 5\zeta_2/3 - 164/81)\), where \(\zeta_3 \approx 1.20206\), \(\zeta_4 = \pi^4/90\), and \(C_F = 4/3\), \(C_A = 3\), \(N_f\) is the number of active quark flavors.
- The matching coefficient for pretzelosity distribution is zero at both NLO and NNLO, confirming the null result found in phenomenological analyses.

## Context

This work extends the NNLO matching calculations previously performed for unpolarized TMDs by Catani et al. (2013) and Echevarria et al. (2016) to the polarized case of transversity, making it the first NNLO evaluation of a polarized TMD distribution in the TMD factorization formalism. The zero matching coefficient for pretzelosity confirms the phenomenological and experimental analyses by Lefky and Prokudin (2014) and Parsamyan (2018), which found the pretzelosity distribution to be very small and consistent with zero. The results enable precision phenomenology for transversity-related observables at the same level as unpolarized distributions, relevant for future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider and LHCSpin.
