# Heavy neutron stars from light scalars

> We study how light scalar fields can change the stellar landscape by triggering a new phase of nuclear matter. Scalars coupled to nucleons can develop a non-trivial expectation value at finite baryon density. This sourcing of a scalar reduces the nucleon mass and provides an additional energy density and pressure source. Under generic conditions, a new ground state of nuclear matter emerges, with striking implications for the configuration of stellar remnants. Notably, neutron stars in the new ground state can be significantly heavier than QCD equations of state currently predict. We also find hybrid stellar compositions and stable self-bound objects with sizes as small as the Compton wavelength of the scalar. We discuss several specific realizations of this scenario: the QCD axion and lighter generalizations thereof and linearly or quadratically coupled scalar fields effectively equivalent to a class of scalar-tensor modification of gravity. Lastly, we explore phenomenological signatures relevant to electromagnetic and gravitational wave observations of neutron stars, such as atypical compactness and instability gaps in radii.

## Key claim

Light scalar fields coupled to nucleons can develop a non-trivial expectation value at finite baryon density, triggering a new ground state of nuclear matter that allows neutron stars to be significantly heavier than predictions from QCD equations of state, provided the scalar potential does not dominate over the reduction of the nucleon mass.

## Method

The authors model nuclear matter as a free Fermi gas of neutrons coupled to a real scalar field, with the scalar-dependent nucleon mass and scalar potential encoded in the Lagrangian. They solve the coupled system of the generalized Klein-Gordon equation and Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static, spherically symmetric stars, using a shooting method to find viable solutions. In the limit where scalar gradient energy is negligible, they derive an effective equation of state by minimizing the total energy density with respect to the scalar field at fixed baryon density or chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Neutron stars in the new ground state can exceed the maximal mass predicted by standard causal bounds, with the merger event GW190814 measuring a stellar remnant of approximately 2.6 solar masses that could be explained by such heavy scalarized neutron stars.
- Self-bound objects can have sizes as small as the Compton wavelength of the scalar field, which is set by the scalar mass m_phi.
- The scalarization condition translates into an upper bound on the scale M_phi (controlling scalar-matter coupling strength) and an upper bound on the scalar mass m_phi.

## Context

This work extends the study of scalar-tensor theories of gravity (Damour and Esposito-Farese, 1993) and chameleon screening (Khoury and Weltman, 2004) by showing that under generic conditions a new ground state of nuclear matter emerges, not just modified macroscopic properties. It enables predictions for electromagnetic and gravitational-wave observations of neutron stars, including atypical compactness and instability gaps in radii, and provides a potential explanation for the mass gap between neutron stars and black holes highlighted by the GW190814 event (LIGO Scientific Collaboration, 2020).
