# Statistical Assemblies of Particles with Spin

> Spin, $s$ in quantum theory can assume only half odd integer or integer values. For a given $s$, there exist $n=2s+1$ states $|s,m\rangle$, $m=s,s-1,........,-s$. A statistical assembly of particles (like a beam or target employed in experiments in physics) with the lowest value of spin $s=\frac {1}{2}$ can be described in terms of probabilities $p_m$ assigned to the two states $m=\pm \frac {1}{2}$. A generalization of this concept to higher spins $s>\frac {1}{2}$ leads only to a particularly simple category of statistical assemblies known as `Oriented systems'. To provide a comprehensive description of all realizable categories of statistical assemblies in experiments, it is advantageous to employ the generators of the Lie group $SU(n)$. The probability domain then gets identified to the interior of regular polyhedra in $\Re^{n-1},$ where the centre corresponds to an unpolarized assembly and the vertices represent `pure' states. All the other interior points correspond to `mixed' states. The higher spin system has embedded within itself a set of $s(2s+1)$ independent axes, which are determinable empirically. Only when all these axes turn out to be collinear, the simple category of `Oriented systems' is realized, where probabilities $p_m$ are assigned to the states $|s,m\rangle$. The simplest case of higher spin $s=1$ provides an illustrative example, where additional features of `aligned' and more general `non oriented' categories are displayed.

# Statistical Assemblies of Particles with Spin

## Key claim
For statistical assemblies of particles with spin s > 1/2, the simple "oriented" description using probabilities p_m assigned to |s,m⟩ states is only one special category; a comprehensive description requires the SU(n) group representation, where the probability domain corresponds to the interior of regular polyhedra in ℝ^(n-1), and higher-spin systems contain s(2s+1) independent axes that are only collinear in the oriented case.

## Method
The paper generalizes the density matrix formalism for spin-1/2 statistical assemblies to arbitrary spin s by expressing the spin density matrix ρ in terms of the generators of the Lie group SU(n), where n = 2s+1. The Fano statistical tensors t^k_q are related to the SU(n) parameters h_α and e^(γ)_αβ, and the density matrix is diagonalized via unitary transformation to obtain eigenvalues corresponding to statistical probabilities p_α. The bounds on these probabilities define a polyhedral probability domain in ℝ^(n-1).

## Key numbers
- For spin s, the number of states is n = 2s+1, and the number of independent axes embedded in the system is s(2s+1)
- The number of real independent parameters needed to characterize a statistical assembly of particles with spin s is n²-1, while an oriented system requires only n+1 parameters
- For spin-1 (n=3), the probability domain forms an equilateral triangle in ℝ², with vertices at (√(3/2), √(1/2)), (-√(3/2), √(1/2)), and (0, -√2) in the h₁⁰-h₂⁰ plot
- For spin-3/2 (n=4), the probability domain forms a regular tetrahedron in ℝ³, with vertices at (√2, √(2/3), √(1/3)), (-√2, √(2/3), √(1/3)), (0, -2√(2/3), √(1/3)), and (0, 0, -√3)
- The bounds on the SU(n) parameters satisfy -√(n-1) ≤ h⁰_(n-1) ≤ 1/√(n-1) for the highest index, with analogous bounds for lower indices

## Context
This work extends the density matrix formalism of von Neumann and the Fano statistical tensor approach (Fano, 1950s) for describing spin assemblies. It builds on the Madison convention for spin-1 normalization and the SU(n) parameterization introduced by Ramachandran and Murthy. The paper provides a unified geometrical interpretation of the probability domain for any spin, generalizing earlier treatments for specific cases like s=1 and s=3/2 by other authors. This enables a complete classification of statistical assemblies into oriented, aligned, and non-oriented categories, with implications for describing beams and targets in nuclear and particle physics experiments.
