# Many-body forces in magnetic neutron stars

> In this work, we study in detail the effects of many-body forces on the equation of state and the structure of magnetic neutron stars. The stellar matter is described within a relativistic mean field formalism that takes into account many-body forces by means of a non-linear meson field dependence on the nuclear interaction coupling constants. We assume that matter is at zero temperature, charge neutral, in beta-equilibrium, and populated by the baryon octet, electrons, and muons. In order to study the effects of different degrees of stiffness in the equation of state, we explore the parameter space of the model, which reproduces nuclear matter properties at saturation, as well as massive neutron stars. Magnetic field effects are introduced both in the equation of state and in the macroscopic structure of stars by the self-consistent solution of the Einstein-Maxwell equations. In addition, effects of poloidal magnetic fields on the global properties of stars, as well as density and magnetic field profiles are investigated. We find that not only different macroscopic magnetic field distributions, but also different parameterizations of the model for a fixed magnetic field distribution impact the gravitational mass, deformation and internal density profiles of stars. Finally, we also show that strong magnetic fields affect significantly the particle populations of stars

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that many-body forces parameterized by the coupling constant \(\zeta\) significantly affect the global properties of magnetic neutron stars, but only when the macroscopic stellar structure is computed with full two-dimensional deformed solutions rather than with the commonly used spherical Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) approximation; the authors find that using spherical TOV solutions for highly magnetized stars systematically overestimates the maximum mass and underestimates the equatorial radius.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean-field model with many-body forces (MBF model) where the scalar meson couplings depend on the scalar fields via a parameter \(\zeta\), generating different equations of state that reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties. They solve the coupled Einstein-Maxwell equations self-consistently using the LORENE library for stationary, axisymmetric spacetimes with poloidal magnetic fields, assuming zero-temperature, charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated matter composed of the full baryon octet, electrons, and muons. The magnetic field effects on the equation of state are included via Landau quantization of charged particles, and the macroscopic stellar structure is computed for different \(\zeta\) parameterizations and different magnetic field configurations.

## Key numbers

* Maximum baryon mass for the \(\zeta=0.040\) parametrization with hyperons: \(M_{b}^{\text{max}} = 2.57\,M_{\odot}\) for the full 2D (LORENE) solution versus \(M_{b}^{\text{max}} = 2.98\,M_{\odot}\) for the spherical TOV solution with magnetic fields, both using current amplitude \(j_0 = 3.5 \times 10^{15}\,\mathrm{A/m^2}\)
* Maximum gravitational mass for the same parametrization: \(M_{g}^{\text{max}} = 2.23\,M_{\odot}\) (2D) versus \(M_{g}^{\text{max}} = 2.50\,M_{\odot}\) (TOV)
* Equatorial radius for a star with baryon mass \(M_b = 1.4\,M_{\odot}\): \(R_{1.4M_{\odot}} = 14.95\,\mathrm{km}\) (2D) versus \(R_{1.4M_{\odot}} = 11.88\,\mathrm{km}\) (TOV)
* Equatorial radius for a star with gravitational mass \(M_g = 1.4\,M_{\odot}\): \(R_{1.4M_{\odot}} = 15.11\,\mathrm{km}\) (2D)
* The magnetic field limit for stable solutions within this approach is of order \(\sim 10^{18}\,\mathrm{G}\)
* Saturation density fixed at \(\rho_0 = 0.15\,\mathrm{fm^{-3}}\) and binding energy per nucleon \(B/A = -15.75\,\mathrm{MeV}\) for all parameterizations
* Compressibility modulus \(K_0\) ranges from 211 MeV (\(\zeta = 0.129\)) to 297 MeV (\(\zeta = 0.040\))

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent calculations of magnetic neutron stars that were performed for quark stars by Chatterjee (2014) and hybrid stars by Franzon (2015), now applying the same formalism to hyperon-rich hadronic matter using the many-body forces model. The authors cross-check earlier findings that Landau quantization effects barely impact global stellar properties for central fields below \(\sim 5\times 10^{18}\,\mathrm{G}\), but they show that the common practice of using spherical TOV solutions with isotropic magnetic field contributions leads to systematic errors of up to \(\sim 0.4\,M_{\odot}\) in maximum mass and several kilometers in radius. The work enables future studies of how different equation-of-state stiffnesses and magnetic field configurations affect neutron star observables such as cooling, gravitational wave emission, and electromagnetic signatures.
