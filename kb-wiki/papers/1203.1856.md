# N=4 Super Yang-Mills Plasma

> The N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma is studied in the regime of weak coupling. Collective excitations and collisional processes are discussed. Since the Keldysh-Schwinger approach is used, the collective excitations in both equilibrium and non-equilibrium plasma are under consideration. The dispersion equations of gluon, fermion, and scalar modes are written down and the self-energies, which enter the equations, are computed in the Hard Loop Approximation. The self-energies are discussed in the context of effective action which is also given. The gluon modes and fermion ones appear to be the same as those in the QCD plasma of gluons and massless quarks. The scalar modes are as of a free relativistic massive particle. The binary collisional processes, which occur at the lowest nontrivial order of the coupling constant, are reviewed and then the transport properties of the plasma are discussed. The N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma is finally concluded to be very similar the QCD plasma of gluons and light quarks. The differences mostly reflect different numbers of degrees of freedom in the two systems.

## Key claim

The N=4 super Yang-Mills plasma (SYMP) is concluded to be very similar to the QCD plasma of gluons and light quarks (QGP), with differences mostly reflecting different numbers of degrees of freedom; however, this conclusion is explicitly restricted to the regime of weak coupling where perturbative methods are applicable, and the authors deliberately avoid comparing their results to those obtained in the strong coupling regime via AdS/CFT duality or lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors computed the retarded self-energies of gluon, fermion, and scalar fields in the Hard Loop Approximation using the Keldysh-Schwinger (real-time) formalism, which allows treatment of both equilibrium and non-equilibrium plasmas. They wrote down the dispersion equations for each field type and computed the self-energies from the one-loop diagrams of N=4 super Yang-Mills theory, including contributions from fermion loops, gluon loops, ghost loops, scalar loops, and tadpole diagrams. The binary collisional processes at the lowest nontrivial order of the coupling constant were reviewed, and transport properties were discussed.

## Key numbers

* Energy density of equilibrium non-interacting SYMP: $\varepsilon = \frac{\pi^2}{60} \cdot 30 (N_c^2 -1) T^4$; for $N_c=3$, this is approximately 2.5 times larger than that of QGP at the same temperature.
* Particle density in SYMP: $n = \frac{2\zeta(3)}{\pi^2} \cdot 7 (N_c^2 -1) T^3$; for $N_c=3$, $n_{\rm SYMP}/n_{\rm QGP} \approx 1.3$ at the same temperature.
* Debye mass squared in SYMP: $m_D^2 = \frac{g^2}{6} \cdot 12 N_c T^2$; for $N_c=3$, the ratio of Debye masses squared is 2.4 at the same value of $gT$.
* Plasma parameter for SYMP with $N_c=3$: $\lambda \approx 0.257 g^3$, compared to $\lambda \approx 0.042 g^3$ for QGP.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous systematic comparison of supersymmetric perturbative plasmas to non-supersymmetric counterparts, which began with N=1 SUSY QED (Czajka and Mrowczynski, 2010, 2011). The paper directly confronts weakly coupled SYMP with perturbative QGP, finding that gluon and fermion collective modes are the same as in QCD plasma of gluons and massless quarks, while scalar modes behave as free relativistic massive particles. The results enable further perturbative studies of transport properties in supersymmetric plasmas and provide a baseline for comparison with strong-coupling results from AdS/CFT duality (Maldacena, 1997; Son, 2007; Janik, 2010) and lattice QCD, though the authors explicitly avoid such comparisons due to difficulties in defining collective excitations in those frameworks.
