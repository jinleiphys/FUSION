# Shear and Bulk Viscosities of Quark Matter from Quark-Meson Fluctuations in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> We have calculated the temperature dependence of shear $η$ and bulk $ζ$ viscosities of quark matter due to quark-meson fluctuations. The quark thermal width originating from quantum fluctuations of quark-$π$ and quark-$σ$ loops at finite temperature is calculated with the formalism of real-time thermal field theory. Temperature-dependent constituent-quark and meson masses, and quark-meson couplings are obtained in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We found a non-trivial influence of the temperature-dependent masses and couplings on the Landau-cut structure of the quark self-energy. Our results for the ratios $η/s$ and $ζ/s$, where $s$ is the entropy density (also determined in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model in the quasi-particle approximation), are in fair agreement with results of the literature obtained from different models and techniques. In particular, our result for $η/s$ has a minimum very close to the conjectured AdS/CFT lower bound, $η/s = 1/4π$.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s calculated from quark-meson fluctuations in the NJL model reaches a minimum very close to the conjectured AdS/CFT lower bound of 1/(4π), but this result is only physically meaningful for temperatures above the Mott temperature (T_M ≈ 0.187 GeV) and below approximately 0.240 GeV, where the on-shell Landau cut contribution to the quark thermal width is nonzero.

## Method

The authors computed the quark thermal width Γ_Q from one-loop quark self-energy diagrams involving quark-π and quark-σ fluctuations using real-time thermal field theory, with temperature-dependent constituent-quark and meson masses and quark-meson couplings obtained from the two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the quasi-particle (Hartree) approximation. The shear and bulk viscosities were then evaluated from Kubo formulas expressed in terms of the on-shell, momentum-dependent quark thermal width, using expressions identical to those from the relaxation time approximation. The entropy density and speed of sound required for the bulk viscosity were also computed within the NJL quasi-particle approximation.

## Key numbers

- Mott temperature T_M = 0.187 GeV, defined by the condition m_π(T_M) = 2 M_Q(T_M)
- Vacuum constituent quark mass M_Q = 328 MeV; vacuum pion mass m_π = 142 MeV; vacuum sigma mass m_σ = 663 MeV
- NJL model parameters: current quark mass m_Q = 5 MeV, coupling GΛ² = 2.14, cutoff Λ = 653 MeV; vacuum quark condensate ⟨ūu⟩ = (−252 MeV)³, pion decay constant f_π = 94 MeV
- The η/s ratio has a minimum very close to the AdS/CFT lower bound η/s = 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08
- The quark thermal width Γ_Q for momentum k = 0.5 GeV shows a peak near T ≈ 0.19–0.20 GeV and decreases toward zero at higher temperatures
- The bulk viscosity ζ shows a non-monotonic temperature dependence with a single peak (when using the full momentum-dependent Γ_Q) in the range T_M < T < 0.240 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations of shear viscosity from quark-meson fluctuations, notably complementing the recent study by Lang, Krieg, and Weise (2014) by using a different formalism, including bulk viscosity, and employing different quark-meson couplings that reflect dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The results for η/s and ζ/s show fair agreement with earlier estimates from other models and techniques, including those of Redlich et al., Cassing, Purnendu et al., Plumari et al., and Weise et al. The calculation enables future investigations of quark-quark and quark-gluon scattering contributions to viscosities, which become important at higher temperatures where the NJL model's lack of asymptotic freedom limits its applicability.
