# On the S-wave πD-scattering length in the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron

## Key claim
The S-wave pion-deuteron scattering length is calculated to be -0.057 M_π^{-1} in a relativistic field theory model where the deuteron is treated as a one-nucleon loop bound state, and this value agrees with the experimental result of -0.052^{+0.022}_{-0.017} M_π^{-1} only when the Δ-resonance contribution is included. The calculation is performed in a leading long-wavelength approximation and neglects the mass difference M_Δ - M_N as small compared to the remaining contributions.

## Method
The authors compute the elastic πD-scattering amplitude using a relativistic field theory model where the deuteron field couples to nucleons via a one-nucleon loop mechanism, with the coupling constant g_V = 11.3 fixed by the deuteron electric quadrupole moment Q_D = 0.286 fm^2. The pion-nucleon interaction is described using a nonlinear realization of chiral SU(2)×SU(2) symmetry with g_πNN = 13.4, and the πNΔ interaction is included with g_πNΔ = 1.90 g_πNN. The scattering amplitude is obtained from box-diagram calculations in the leading long-wavelength approximation, with an ultraviolet cutoff Λ_D = 64.843 MeV corresponding to the effective deuteron radius.

## Key numbers
- S-wave πD-scattering length: a_πD = -0.057 M_π^{-1} (theoretical)
- Experimental S-wave πD-scattering length: (a_πD)_exp = -0.052^{+0.022}_{-0.017} M_π^{-1}
- Deuteron binding energy: ε_D = 2.225 MeV
- Deuteron mass: M_D = 2M_N - ε_D, with M_N = 938 MeV
- Pion mass: M_π = M_π^0 = 134.976 MeV
- Δ-resonance mass: M_Δ = 1232 MeV
- PCAC constant: F_0 ≈ 92 MeV
- πNN coupling constant: g_πNN = 13.4 ± 0.2
- πNΔ coupling constant: g_πNΔ = 1.90 g_πNN
- Deuteron electric quadrupole moment: Q_D = 0.286 fm^2
- Phenomenological coupling constant: g_V = 11.3
- Ultraviolet cutoff: Λ_D = 64.843 MeV
- Effective deuteron radius: r_D = 1/Λ_D = 3.043 fm
- Deuteron radius from binding energy: r_D = (ε_D M_N)^{-1/2} = 4.319 fm
- Average deuteron radius from wavefunction: \bar{r} = 3.140 fm

## Context
This work extends the relativistic field theory model of the deuteron developed by the same authors (Ivanov et al., 1997) to the calculation of πD-scattering, following the approach that all deuteron interactions are determined by one-nucleon loops, similar to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model treatment of hadrons as collective quark-antiquark excitations. The result that the scattering length is of order O(M_π^2) agrees with prior work by Robilotta (year not given) and Weinberg (year not given). The calculation confirms the important role of the Δ-resonance in elastic πD-scattering and demonstrates that the model can describe low-energy πD dynamics at energies very close to the πD-threshold, enabling further applications of the relativistic deuteron model to other low-energy processes.
