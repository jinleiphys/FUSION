# The role of local and global strangeness neutrality at the inhomogeneous freeze-out in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The decoupling surface in relativistic heavy-ion collisions may not be homogeneous. Rather, inhomogeneities should form when a rapid transition from high to low entropy density occurs. We analyze the hadron "chemistry" from high-energy heavy-ion reactions for the presence of such density inhomogeneities. We show that due to the non-linear dependence of the particle densities on the temperature and baryon-chemical potential such inhomogeneities should be visible even in the integrated, inclusive abundances. We analyze experimental data from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS and Au+Au collisions at BNL-RHIC to determine the amplitude of inhomogeneities and the role of local and global strangeness neutrality.

## Key claim

The paper shows that inhomogeneities on the freeze-out surface in relativistic heavy-ion collisions are visible in integrated, inclusive hadron abundances due to the non-linear dependence of particle densities on temperature and baryon-chemical potential, and that imposing global strangeness neutrality (rather than local) yields a statistically significant improvement in describing experimental data across SPS energies without adding extra parameters.

## Method

The authors model an inhomogeneous freeze-out surface as a superposition of grand-canonical ensembles with Gaussian distributions of temperature T and baryon-chemical potential μB, characterized by their means (T̄, μ̄B) and widths (δT, δμβ). They compute hadron densities in the ideal gas approximation with an excluded volume correction (radius R0 = 0.3 fm for all species), and fit the four parameters (T̄, μ̄B, δT, δμβ) to experimental particle multiplicity ratios from NA49 (Pb+Pb at Elab/A = 20, 30, 40, 80, 158 GeV) and STAR (Au+Au at √sNN = 130 and 200 GeV) by minimizing χ². Strangeness neutrality is imposed either locally (μS fixed per domain to give zero net strangeness) or globally (a single μ̄S fixed by requiring zero net strangeness over the entire ensemble).

## Key numbers

- At intermediate SPS energies (Elab/A ≈ 30-160 GeV), the inhomogeneous model with global strangeness neutrality yields χ²/dof ≈ 1, compared to χ²/dof values between 2 and 4 for the inhomogeneous model with local strangeness neutrality.
- The excluded volume correction uses R0 = 0.3 fm for all hadron species.
- The correlation length scale for freeze-out domains is estimated at ∼ 1/T ∼ 1-2 fm.
- Lattice QCD estimates locate the critical point at T ≈ 160 MeV, μB ≈ 360 MeV.
- At RHIC energies (√sNN = 200 GeV), the χ² contours are flat in both δT and δμβ directions, indicating no statistically significant improvement over the homogeneous model.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Zschiesche, Portugal, and Dumitru, PRC 2005) by comparing local versus global strangeness neutrality conditions in the inhomogeneous freeze-out model. It builds on the homogeneous grand-canonical freeze-out model used extensively by Braun-Munzinger et al. (2005) and others, and addresses the possibility that inhomogeneities arise from a first-order phase transition near the critical endpoint predicted by lattice QCD (Fodor and Katz, 2001) and effective chiral Lagrangians. The results enable future studies to establish quantitative relations between the amplitudes of T and μB inhomogeneities and phase transition properties such as latent heat and interface tension, and motivate further experimental data from GSI-FAIR, RHIC low-energy program, and CERN-LHC.
