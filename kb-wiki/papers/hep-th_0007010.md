# Tutorial on quasi-sparse eigenvector diagonalization

> 8 pages, uses ws-p8-50x6-00.cls included, to appear in the proceedings of the International Workshop on Nonperturbative Methods and Lattice QCD, Guangzhou, May 2000

## Key claim

The quasi-sparse eigenvector (QSE) method can find low-lying eigenvalues and eigenvectors for quantum field Hamiltonians, including infinite-dimensional systems, provided that the splitting between low-lying eigenvalues is not too small compared to the size of off-diagonal Hamiltonian matrix entries, so that the low-lying eigenvectors are dominated by a small fraction of their largest components.

## Method

The QSE algorithm iteratively selects a subset of orthonormal basis vectors to form a subspace S, diagonalizes the Hamiltonian restricted to S to obtain an eigenvector v, sorts the basis components of v by magnitude and discards the least important ones, then replaces discarded vectors with new ones selected at random from a pool of candidate basis vectors connected to the retained vectors through non-vanishing Hamiltonian matrix elements. The process is repeated until the eigenvector becomes a stable fixed point of the update. The paper illustrates the method using phi^4 theory in 1+1 dimensions with a momentum cutoff scheme, where ladder operators are defined with respect to an arbitrary mass parameter mu' to construct Fock states and compute matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- The Hamiltonian for phi^4 theory in 1+1 dimensions uses a periodic box of length 2L and momentum modes restricted to |n| ≤ N_max.
- The free-particle energy for mode n is ω_n(μ) = sqrt(n^2 π^2 / L^2 + μ^2).
- The mass counterterm coefficient is b(μ) = Σ_n 1/(2 ω_n(μ)).
- The sample MATLAB code uses niter = 30 iterations, nactive = 100 basis states in the subspace, and nretain = 80 basis states kept after each truncation.

## Context

This tutorial extends the QSE method introduced in Lee (2000) for quantum field Hamiltonians, providing implementation details for phi^4 theory in 1+1 dimensions. The work enables practical diagonalization of Hamiltonians that would otherwise be intractable due to exponential growth of Fock space, and the paper mentions that stochastic error correction techniques will be presented in a forthcoming work. The method is contrasted with approaches that require auxiliary cutoffs to render Fock space finite, which QSE diagonalization avoids.
