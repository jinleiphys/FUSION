# Measuring the finite width and unitarity corrections to the phi-omega mixing amplitude

> It is shown that the phase of phi-omega interference in the reaction e^+e^- -->pi^+ pi^- pi^0 at energies close to the phi(1020) peak can be calculated in a way that is practically independent of the model of phi-omega mixing. The magnitude of the presently measured interference phase, still of poor accuracy, is in agreement with the predictions based on extending the omega(782) resonance tail from the peak position to the phi mass upon assuming the omega -->rho pi -->3pi model. The calculated omega width at the phi mass is about 200 MeV.

## Key claim

The deviation of the phi-omega interference phase from the canonical 180 degrees can be explained mainly by finite width effects, and the phase can be calculated in a way that is practically independent of the specific model of phi-omega mixing, due to compensation between the rho-pi state contribution to the mixing amplitude and the direct transition.

## Method

The authors calculate the phi-omega interference phase in the reaction e+e- → π+π-π0 by evaluating the unitarity corrections to the coupling constants and the phi-omega mixing amplitude from real intermediate states, primarily the ρπ and KK channels. They use the Dyson-like equation approach for the vertex function to account for ρπ rescattering effects, and relate the physical coupling constants of the phi and omega mesons to their ideally mixed counterparts through the complex mixing parameter εφω(s).

## Key numbers

- The measured phi-omega interference phase from CMD-2 is χφω = 162° ± 17°
- The calculated omega width at the phi mass is about 200 MeV
- The combination g(m²φ) = Re g⁰φρπ / Re g̃⁰ωρπ(m²φ) - Re Πφω / Δm²φω is approximately 0.06, obtained from the 3π branching ratios of omega and phi
- The contribution from the first term in the phase deviation formula gives 6° ± 1° to Δχφω, with the uncertainty from the 20% uncertainty of SU(3) predictions for vector meson couplings to KK
- The calculated phase is χφω = 165° ± 3° at Rρπ = 0 GeV⁻¹ and χφω = 172° ± 3° at Rρπ = 1 GeV⁻¹
- The difference between predictions of strong and weak phi-omega mixing models for χφω at the phi mass is only 0.6°, too small to be measured

## Context

This work extends the analysis of phi-omega interference previously developed by Achasov, Kozhevnikov, and collaborators (1992, 1993, 1995) to include finite width and unitarity corrections. The authors show that the phase deviation from 180° observed by the CMD-2 collaboration (Akhmetshin et al., 1998) can be explained by the imaginary part of the phi-omega mixing parameter arising mainly from the ρπ intermediate state. The results enable future precise measurements of the interference phase to determine the form factor parameter Rρπ that controls the omega width growth with energy, and to test the applicability of field theoretical methods to hadronic resonances at energies far from their mass shells.
