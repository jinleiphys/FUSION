# Applying Bayesian parameter estimation to relativistic heavy-ion collisions: simultaneous characterization of the initial state and quark-gluon plasma medium

> We quantitatively estimate properties of the quark-gluon plasma created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions utilizing Bayesian statistics and a multi-parameter model-to-data comparison. The study is performed using a recently developed parametric initial condition model, TRENTO, which interpolates among a general class of particle production schemes, and a modern hybrid model which couples viscous hydrodynamics to a hadronic cascade. We calibrate the model to multiplicity, transverse momentum, and flow data and report constraints on the parametrized initial conditions and the temperature-dependent transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma. We show that initial entropy deposition is consistent with a saturation-based picture, extract a relation between the minimum value and slope of the temperature-dependent specific shear viscosity, and find a clear signal for a nonzero bulk viscosity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that Bayesian parameter estimation can simultaneously constrain both the initial-state entropy deposition and the temperature-dependent transport coefficients of the quark-gluon plasma, showing that initial entropy deposition is consistent with a saturation-based picture and finding a clear signal for a nonzero bulk viscosity, though the quantitative estimate of bulk viscosity is precluded by the neglect of bulk viscous corrections at particlization.

## Method

The authors coupled the parametric initial condition model TRENTO, which uses a generalized mean of participant thickness functions to interpolate among different entropy deposition schemes, to the VISH2+1 viscous hydrodynamic code and the UrQMD hadronic cascade. They calibrated the model to experimental data on charged particle multiplicity, transverse momentum spectra, and anisotropic flow coefficients from Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC and Au+Au collisions at RHIC, using a Gaussian process emulator to interpolate between a limited number of model evaluations and Markov chain Monte Carlo to extract posterior probability distributions for the model parameters.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian parameter estimation studies of heavy-ion collisions (Petersen 2010, Novak 2013, Pratt 2015, Sangaline 2015, Bernhard 2015, Heinz 2015) by using the flexible TRENTO initial condition model, which can mimic various specific models including the wounded nucleon model (p=1), KLN saturation model (p approximately -0.67), and EKRT model (p approximately 0). The results enable systematic model comparison using Bayes factors and provide simultaneous constraints on initial conditions and transport coefficients that can inform further refinements of both the initial-state theory and the temperature dependence of QGP viscosities.
