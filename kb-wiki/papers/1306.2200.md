# High-Accuracy Analysis of Compton Scattering in Chiral Effective Field Theory: Status and Future

> Theory and prospects of Compton scattering on nucleons and light nuclei below 500 MeV are outlined; cf. Refs. [1-3]. Invited contribution at the Workshop to Explore Physics Opportunities with Intense, Polarized Electron Beams with Energy up to 300 MeV, MIT, Cambridge (USA), 14th-16th March 2013.

## Key claim

Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChiEFT) with explicit Delta(1232) degrees of freedom can describe Compton scattering on nucleons and light nuclei from zero energy to about 400 MeV, but the accuracy of the predictions depends on the energy regime: for omega less than about 140 MeV, contributions are included at order e^2 delta^4 (accuracy delta^5 less than 2 percent), while for omega near the Delta resonance at 293 MeV, only order e^2 delta^0 is achieved (accuracy delta^2 less than 20 percent).

## Method

The authors construct unified single-nucleon Compton amplitudes in ChiEFT using the delta-expansion of Pascalutsa and Phillips (2003), which relates the Delta-nucleon mass splitting and pion mass to a single expansion parameter delta approximately 0.4. They fit the Delta(1232) M1 coupling b1 to MAMI data at photon energies 200-325 MeV, and fit the static scalar polarisabilities alpha_E1 and beta_M1 to low-energy data below 170 MeV, iterating between both regions until convergence. For the neutron, polarisabilities are extracted from elastic deuteron data at energies 49-94 MeV, using the Baldin sum rule constraint to reduce statistical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Static scalar proton polarisabilities: alpha_E1(p) = [10.7 +/- 0.4_stat +/- 0.2_Baldin +/- 0.3_theory] x 10^{-4} fm^3; beta_M1(p) = [3.1 +/- 0.4_stat +/- 0.2_Baldin +/- 0.3_theory] x 10^{-4} fm^3 (fit with chi^2/d.o.f. = 113.2/135)
- Baldin sum rule value: alpha_E1(p) + beta_M1(p) = [13.8 +/- 0.4] x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Delta(1232) parameters: Delta_M = 293 MeV; g_pi N Delta = 1.425; E2/M1 coupling ratio b2/b1 = -0.34
- Isoscalar scalar polarisabilities: alpha_E1(s) = [10.9 +/- 0.9_stat +/- 0.2_Baldin +/- 0.8_theory] x 10^{-4} fm^3; beta_M1(s) = [3.6 +/- 0.9_stat +/- 0.2_Baldin +/- 0.8_theory] x 10^{-4} fm^3 (fit with chi^2/d.o.f. = 24.3/25)
- Neutron scalar polarisabilities: alpha_E1(n) = [11.1 +/- 1.8_stat +/- 0.2_Baldin +/- 0.8_theory] x 10^{-4} fm^3; beta_M1(n) = [4.2 +/- 1.8_stat +/- 0.2_Baldin +/- 0.8_theory] x 10^{-4} fm^3
- Expansion parameter delta approximately 0.4

## Context

This work extends the parameter-free leading-order ChiEFT prediction of Bernard et al. (1991) that alpha_E1 = 10 beta_M1 = 12.4 x 10^{-4} fm^3, and provides the most complete ChiEFT analysis of nucleon polarisabilities to date. The results enable future extraction of the four dipole spin-polarisabilities (gamma_E1E1, gamma_M1M1, gamma_E1M2, gamma_M1E2) from polarised Compton experiments, which are identified as a top priority by the 2007 NSAC and 2010 NuPECC Long-Range Plans. The authors note that ongoing experiments at MAMI (Mainz), HIgammaS at TUNL, and MAX-Lab at Lund, as well as future high-intensity electron beam facilities like MESA, can provide the necessary accuracy to measure asymmetries at the 10^{-2} level for spin polarisability extraction.
