# A Novel Approach in Solving the Spinor-Spinor Bethe-Salpeter Equation

> To solve the spinor-spinor Bethe-Salpeter equation in Euclidean space we propose a novel method related to the use of hyperspherical harmonics. We suggest an appropriate extension to form a new basis of spin-angular harmonics that is suitable for a representation of the vertex functions. We present a numerical algorithm to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation and investigate in detail the properties of the solution for the scalar, pseudoscalar and vector meson exchange kernels including the stability of bound states. We also compare our results to the non relativistic ones and to the results given by light front dynamics.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel method for solving the spinor-spinor Bethe-Salpeter equation in Euclidean space using hyperspherical harmonics, but the solution only exists below a critical coupling constant; above this critical value the bound state solution disappears entirely, analogous to the collapse phenomenon for potentials like \(-\alpha/r^2\) in nonrelativistic quantum mechanics.

## Method

The authors expand the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function into a new basis of spin-angular harmonics and then expand the interaction kernel into four-dimensional hyperspherical harmonics. By performing the integration over hyper-angles analytically, the original four-dimensional integral equation reduces to an infinite system of one-dimensional integral equations, which is then transformed into an algebraic system of linear equations using Gaussian quadrature. The method is applied to the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) bound states for scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector meson exchange kernels with monopole form factors.

## Key numbers

- For a scalar meson exchange with \(\mu = 0.15\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) and coupling \(g^2 = 15\), the bound state mass converges to \(M = 1.9368\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) at \(M_\mathrm{max} = 4\) and \(N_G = 64\).
- For a scalar meson exchange with \(\mu = 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) and coupling \(g^2 = 15\), the bound state mass converges to \(M = 1.9982\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) at \(M_\mathrm{max} = 4\) and \(N_G = 64\).
- For a scalar meson exchange with \(\mu = 0.15\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) and coupling \(g^2 = 30\), the bound state mass converges to \(M = 1.7870\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) at \(M_\mathrm{max} = 4\) and \(N_G = 96\).
- For a scalar meson exchange with \(\mu = 0.5\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) and coupling \(g^2 = 30\), the bound state mass converges to \(M = 1.9122\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) at \(M_\mathrm{max} = 4\) and \(N_G = 96\).
- The critical coupling constant is estimated analytically as \(g^2_\mathrm{cr} \sim 4\pi^2\).
- The constituent nucleon mass is taken as \(m = 1.0\ \mathrm{GeV/c}^2\) throughout.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Umnikov, Kaptari, and Khanna (1997), who solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for spinor particles using a two-dimensional Gaussian mesh, by providing a more efficient method that avoids cumbersome two-dimensional arrays. The results are compared to nonrelativistic calculations and to the light front dynamics approach of Mangin-Brinet, Carbonell, and Karmanov (2003), showing agreement at low coupling but increasing differences at higher coupling due to the fourth component of the vertex function present in the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. The method enables a simple analytical parametrization of the solution, facilitating Wick rotation back to Minkowski space and practical calculations of matrix elements for systems such as the deuteron, positronium, and mesons.
