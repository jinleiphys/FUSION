# Hydrodynamic Parameterization of NA44 Data on Kaon and Pion Correlations and Spectra

> An analytically calculable model has been proposed recently for the simultaneous description of particle spectra and correlations, which is able to explain in a natural manner the $m_t$ scaling of the radius parameters of the Bose--Einstein correlation functions. We present here preliminary results on fitting this model to data sampled by NA44 collaboration in central S + Pb reactions at CERN SPS, which indicate that both pions and kaons freeze-out at a surprisingly low temperature of T approximately 108 MeV.

## Key claim

The authors present a hydrodynamical model that can simultaneously describe particle spectra and Bose-Einstein correlation functions, and they find that both pions and kaons freeze-out at a surprisingly low temperature of approximately 108 MeV in central S + Pb reactions at CERN SPS. However, the results are explicitly labeled as preliminary because systematic errors on the spectra were not yet available, the effects of Bose-Einstein statistics were not included in the fitting, and the chi-squared hypersurface has a complicated shape with multiple almost degenerate minima.

## Method

The authors fit an analytically calculable hydrodynamical model with transverse flow and temperature profiles to NA44 data on pion and kaon invariant momentum distributions (IMDs) and Bose-Einstein correlation function (BECF) radius parameters (R_side, R_out, R_long) from central S + Pb collisions at the CERN SPS. They performed both analytical (saddle-point approximation in Boltzmann approximation) and numerical integration fits using the CERN MINUIT multidimensional fitting algorithm, comparing the results to data points for both pions and kaons simultaneously and separately.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out temperature from numerical integration fit: T0 = 108 ± 10 MeV (central value from simultaneous fit to pions and kaons)
- Mean emission time from numerical integration fit: tau0 = 7.0 ± 0.5 fm/c
- Geometrical radius from numerical integration fit: RG = 4.4 ± 0.2 fm
- Intercept parameter for pions: lambda_pi+ = 0.56 ± 0.02 at mean transverse momentum 150 MeV, and 0.55 ± 0.02 at 450 MeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for full numerical fit: 237/126
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for full analytical fit: 207/126
- Transverse flow parameter from numerical fit: b^2 = 0.68 ± 0.14
- Temperature parameter from fit to pions only (numerical): T0 = 112 ± 2 MeV
- Temperature parameter from fit to kaons only (numerical): T0 = 133 ± 6 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analytically calculable model of Csorgo and Lorstad (1995) for simultaneous description of spectra and correlations, which naturally explains the m_t scaling of HBT radius parameters observed by the NA44 collaboration. The results are consistent with a similar low freeze-out temperature of T0 = 92.9 ± 4.4 MeV found by Chapman et al. at AGS energies from analysis of BECF and spectra of pions and kaons. The authors note that including systematic errors on the spectra and the effects of Bose-Einstein statistics (as done for NA49 data by another group) will be necessary to obtain final results and resolve ambiguities between multiple minima in the parameter space.
