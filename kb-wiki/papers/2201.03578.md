# Evidence of Two-Source King Plot Nonlinearity in Spectroscopic Search for New Boson

> Optical precision spectroscopy of isotope shifts can be used to test for new forces beyond the Standard Model, and to determine basic properties of atomic nuclei. We measure isotope shifts on the highly forbidden ${}^2S_{1/2} \rightarrow {}^2F_{7/2}$ octupole transition of trapped $^{168,170,172,174,176}$Yb ions. When combined with previous measurements in Yb$^+$ and very recent measurements in Yb, the data reveal a King plot nonlinearity of up to 240$σ$. The trends exhibited by experimental data are explained by nuclear density functional theory calculations with the Fayans functional. We also find, with 4.3$σ$ confidence, that there is a second distinct source of nonlinearity, and discuss its possible origin.

## Key claim

The paper reports a King plot nonlinearity of up to 240 standard deviations when combining their new measurements with previous data, and finds evidence at the 4.3 sigma level for a second distinct source of nonlinearity that cannot be explained by the quadratic field shift alone, though the dominant nonlinearity is consistent with nuclear structure effects calculated using density functional theory rather than a new boson.

## Method

The authors performed laser spectroscopy on the highly forbidden 467 nm octupole transition (²S₁/₂ → ²F₇/₂) in trapped single Yb⁺ ions of five even isotopes (168, 170, 172, 174, 176), measuring isotope shifts with approximately 500 Hz accuracy by alternating between isotopes and using a frequency-doubled Ti:Sapphire laser locked to an ultralow-thermal-expansion cavity. They combined these new measurements with previously measured quadrupole transitions in Yb⁺ and recent measurements in neutral Yb, then analyzed the nonlinearity patterns using a basis decomposition method (λ₊, λ₋) to distinguish between possible sources, and compared the results to nuclear density functional theory calculations using four different energy density functionals including the Fayans functional.

## Key numbers

- Isotope shift for the (168,170) pair on the octupole transition: -4 438.160 30(50) MHz
- Isotope shift for the (172,174) pair on the octupole transition: -3 132.321 60(50) MHz
- King plot nonlinearity of 41 standard deviations using only Yb⁺ transitions (γ and α)
- King plot nonlinearity of 240 standard deviations when including neutral Yb data
- Evidence for a second source of nonlinearity at 4.3 sigma significance (χ² = 25.4 for four transitions)
- Absolute frequency of the ²S₁/₂ → ²F₇/₂ transition for ¹⁷²Yb⁺: 642.116 785 150 879 5(24) THz
- Measurement accuracy of approximately 500 Hz for isotope shifts on the octupole transition
- Nonlinearity 20 times larger than previously observed when comparing two quadrupole transitions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 3-sigma observation of King plot nonlinearity in Yb⁺ quadrupole transitions (Counts 2020) by adding the octupole transition, which increases the observed nonlinearity 20-fold due to the very different electronic structure of the F state. The results contradict the null result in Ca⁺ (Solaro 2020) and are consistent with recent large nonlinearities reported in neutral Yb (Ono 2021, Figueroa 2021). The analysis enables improved constraints on new boson coupling constants that approach or exceed bounds from electron g-2 and neutron scattering experiments, and demonstrates that the Fayans functional provides better agreement with nuclear radius data than other energy density functionals, which can guide further development of nuclear models.
