# Squeezed spectra and back-to-back correlations of protons and antiprotons at RHIC energies

> This study constrains the range of in-medium mass modification through a comparison of theoretical calculations with experimental transverse momentum spectra and the yield ratio {\bar{p}}/p of protons and antiprotons. Based on the constrained range and a Gaussian source model with radial ow, the theoretical predictions for the fermion back-to-back correlation (fBBC) of p{\bar{p}} pairs at RHIC energies are presented. The results reveal a strong sensitivity of the fBBC signal to the assumed source time distribution: a Lorentzian form generates a pronounced high-momentum signal, whereas an α-stable Lévy form leads to a marked low-momentum signal. Moreover, the in-medium mass modification is shown to enhance the yield ratio {\bar{p}}/p. Therefore, events characterized by a larger {\bar{p}}/p ratio are predicted to have a significantly higher probability of exhibiting a detectable fBBC signal. This study may propose a promising new direction for the experimental observation of this phenomenon

# Squeezed spectra and back-to-back correlations of protons and antiprotons at RHIC energies

## Key claim
The study constrains the range of momentum-dependent in-medium mass modification of protons and antiprotons by comparing theoretical calculations with experimental transverse momentum spectra and yield ratios at six RHIC collision energies, finding that the data are "not inconsistent" with such a modification. The fermion back-to-back correlation (fBBC) signal is predicted to be highly sensitive to the source time distribution, with observable signals possible only under specific conditions of source shape, momentum range, and time distribution width.

## Method
The author employs a Gaussian source model with radial flow to describe particle emission, incorporating a momentum-dependent in-medium mass modification parameterized as δm(k) = δm₀ exp[-k²/Λ²]. The Bogoliubov-Valatin transformation connects vacuum and in-medium fermion operators, and the fBBC correlation function is computed for proton-antiproton pairs using both Lorentzian and α-stable Lévy source time distributions. Theoretical single-particle spectra and yield ratios are fitted to STAR Collaboration experimental data at six collision energies (√sNN = 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV) to extract the mass modification parameters and source properties.

## Key numbers
- Proton vacuum mass m₀ = 938.27 MeV, freeze-out temperature T = 140 MeV (fixed throughout)
- In-medium mass modification magnitude at zero momentum δm₀ = 20 MeV (fixed for all collision energies)
- Momentum-dependence parameter Λ = 1400 MeV at √sNN = 27, 39, 62.4, and 200 GeV; 1300 MeV at 19.6 GeV; 1250 MeV at 11.5 GeV
- Source radius R = 7 fm at √sNN = 200 GeV, decreasing to 4.15 fm at 11.5 GeV
- Radial flow velocity v₀ = 0.7 at 200 GeV, decreasing to 0.51 at 11.5 GeV
- In-medium proton chemical potential μp = 20 MeV at 200 GeV, increasing to 270 MeV at 11.5 GeV
- For Lorentzian time distribution, Δt = 2 fm/c; for α-stable Lévy distribution, Δt = 1 fm/c and α = 1 (or α = 1.35 for comparison)
- Previous experimental fBBC measurement at √sNN = 200 GeV in 0-25% centrality showed approximately 2% signal in transverse momentum range 0.6-0.9 GeV/c

## Context
This work extends previous studies of squeezed back-to-back correlations for boson-antiboson pairs (Padula, 2006; Padula, 2010) to fermion-antifermion pairs, building on the author's earlier work on momentum-independent mass modification (Zhang, 2025). The study is motivated by indirect evidence from the PHENIX Collaboration (2024) suggesting significant in-medium mass reduction of the η' meson. The results enable experimental searches for fBBC signals in specific momentum regions depending on source time distribution, and suggest that non-central collisions may provide more favorable conditions due to narrower source time distributions, as indicated by studies of Lévy-stable source parameters (EPJC, 2016).
