# The Ay Problem for p-3He Elastic Scattering

> We present evidence that numerically accurate quantum calculations employing modern internucleon forces do not reproduce the proton analyzing power, A_y, for p-3He elastic scattering at low energies. These calculations underpredict new measured analyzing powers by approximately 30% at E_{c.m.} = 1.20 MeV and by 40% at E_{c.m.} = 1.69 MeV, an effect analogous to a well-known problem in p-d and n-d scattering. The calculations are performed using the complex Kohn variational principle and the (correlated) Hyperspherical Harmonics technique with full treatment of the Coulomb force. The inclusion of the three-nucleon interaction does not improve the agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim

Numerically accurate quantum calculations using modern internucleon forces (AV18 and AV18UR) systematically underpredict the proton analyzing power \(A_y\) for \(p\)-\(^3\)He elastic scattering at low energies, with the discrepancy persisting even after including the three-nucleon interaction (Urbana IX). This failure mirrors the well-known "\(A_y\) puzzle" in \(N\)-\(d\) scattering, suggesting a common deficiency in the nuclear Hamiltonian.

## Method

The authors solved the four-nucleon scattering problem using the complex Kohn variational principle with a correlated hyperspherical harmonic (CHH) expansion of the wave function, including full treatment of the Coulomb force. Calculations were performed for the Argonne V18 (AV18) nucleon-nucleon potential alone and with the addition of the Urbana IX three-nucleon potential (AV18UR). New measurements of the proton analyzing power were obtained at the University of Wisconsin tandem accelerator using polarized protons on a \(^3\)He gas target at two center-of-mass energies.

## Key numbers

- At \(E_{c.m.} = 1.20\) MeV, theoretical calculations underpredict the measured analyzing power by approximately 30%.
- At \(E_{c.m.} = 1.69\) MeV, theoretical calculations underpredict the measured analyzing power by approximately 40%.
- The combination of phase shifts \(\Delta = 0.5[\delta(^3P_1) + \delta(^3P_2)] - \delta(^3P_0)\) at \(E_{c.m.} = 1.20\) MeV is \(3.9^\circ\) (AV18) and \(3.8^\circ\) (AV18UR), compared to the experimental phase shift analysis value of \(6.9^\circ \pm 0.9^\circ\).
- At \(E_{c.m.} = 1.20\) MeV, the theoretical \(^3P_2\) phase shift is \(7.7^\circ\) (AV18) and \(6.9^\circ\) (AV18UR), while the phase shift analysis gives \(8.9^\circ \pm 0.5^\circ\); the \(^3P_1\) phase shift is \(8.1^\circ\) (AV18) and \(7.4^\circ\) (AV18UR), compared to \(10.1^\circ \pm 0.5^\circ\) from the phase shift analysis.
- The beam polarization measured at 4.0 MeV ranged between 0.79 and 0.84 with typical statistical errors of \(\pm 0.01\), contributing a 3% scale factor uncertainty to the analyzing power data.

## Context

This work extends the known "\(A_y\) puzzle" from three-nucleon systems (\(p\)-\(d\) and \(n\)-\(d\) scattering, documented by Glockle et al. (1996) and Kievsky et al. (1996)) to the four-nucleon system for the first time. The calculations demonstrate that the same deficiency in the nuclear Hamiltonian that causes the underprediction of \(N\)-\(d\) analyzing powers also affects \(p\)-\(^3\)He scattering, and that the three-nucleon force (Urbana IX) does not resolve the discrepancy. This result motivates further investigation into the spin-orbit components of the nuclear interaction and enables future studies of four-nucleon reactions such as \(d+d \rightarrow ^4\)He\(+\gamma\) and the \(hep\) process (\(p+^3\)He \(\rightarrow ^4\)He\(+e^++\nu_e\)).
