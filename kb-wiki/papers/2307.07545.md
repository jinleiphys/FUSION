# Prescaling relaxation to nonthermal attractors

> We study how isotropic and homogeneous far-from-equilibrium quantum systems relax to nonthermal attractors, which are of interest for cold atoms and nuclear collisions. We demonstrate that a first-order ordinary differential equation governs the self-similar approach to nonthermal attractors, i.e., the prescaling. We also show that certain natural scaling-breaking terms induce logarithmically slow corrections that prevent the scaling exponents from reaching the constant values during the system's lifetime. We propose that, analogously to hydrodynamic attractors, the appropriate mathematical structure to describe such dynamics is the transseries. We verify our analytic predictions with state-of-the-art 2PI simulations of the large-N vector model and QCD kinetic theory.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the approach to nonthermal attractors (prescaling) is governed by a first-order ordinary differential equation, but that scaling-breaking terms such as the Debye mass in QCD kinetic theory induce logarithmically slow corrections that prevent the scaling exponents from reaching constant values during the system's lifetime.

## Method

The authors derive a first-order ODE for the prescaling factor \(B(t)\) from the Boltzmann equation under the assumption of a homogeneous collision kernel, yielding power-law corrections to constant scaling exponents. They test this analytic prediction against ab initio 2PI simulations of the large-\(N\) vector model and QCD kinetic theory simulations, the latter spanning eight orders of magnitude in time. For QCD kinetic theory, where the Debye mass breaks the homogeneity of the collision kernel, they use a Fokker-Planck approximation to derive a second-order ODE for the time-dependent scaling exponent \(\beta(t)\) and analyze its late-time behavior.

## Key numbers

* Large-\(N\) vector model: IR fixed point scaling exponents \((\alpha_\infty, \beta_\infty) = (3/2, 1/2)\) in \(3+1\) dimensions, with coupling \(\lambda = 0.01\) and initial occupation \(f_0 = 100/\lambda\).
* QCD kinetic theory: direct energy cascade fixed point \((\alpha_\infty, \beta_\infty) = (-4/7, -1/7)\) from energy conservation with \(\sigma = d+z = 4\).
* QCD kinetic theory simulations run from \(t_i Q = 0\) to \(t_f Q = 10^8\) with coupling \(g^2 = 10^{-8}\) and initial occupation \(n_0 = 1\).
* At the time when typical occupancy becomes \(\mathcal{O}(1)\) (when \(t_f Q \geq \alpha_S^{-7/4}\)), the deviation \(\delta\beta(t)/\beta \sim 1/\log(\alpha_S^{-7/4}) \lesssim 0.03\) for \(g^2 = 10^{-8}\).
* The leading logarithmic correction to the scaling exponent in QCD kinetic theory is \(\beta(t) \approx \beta_\infty [1 + 1/\log(t Q)]\).

## Context

This work extends the concept of prescaling proposed by Mazeliauskas and collaborators (2018) for nonthermal attractors, which appear in ultracold quantum gases (Prufer 2018, Erne 2018, Glidden 2020), ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions (Baier 2000, Berges 2013, Kurkela 2015), and early universe cosmology (Micha 2002, Berges 2008). The authors show that prescaling dynamics in QCD kinetic theory exhibits a transseries structure analogous to that found for hydrodynamic attractors in boost-invariant expansion by Heller and collaborators (2015), Basar and collaborators (2015), and Aniceto and collaborators (2015, 2018). The results enable experimental verification in cold atom systems and suggest that scaling exponents in QCD kinetic theory will show percent-level deviations from fixed-point values throughout the system's lifetime.
