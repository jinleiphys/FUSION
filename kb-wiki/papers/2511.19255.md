# Magnetic susceptibility of a hot hadronic medium and quark degrees of freedom near the QCD cross-over point

> The lattice QCD results for the temperature-dependent magnetic susceptibility of the medium below the cross-over temperature are not possible to reconcile with the widely used Hadron Resonance Gas model, also amended with the physical magnetic moments of hadrons or the pion--vector-meson loops. As noticed earlier, one observes a substantially too strong diamagnetism at temperatures in the range above $\approx 120$~MeV compared to the lattice. This hints at a presence of quarks significantly below the QCD cross-over temperature, which are needed as a source of paramagnetism. However, the pions must be retained to describe the diamagnetism data at low temperatures. Therefore, we consider here a quark-meson approach, where the temperature-dependent quark masses are fixed in a model-free way using the baryon-baryon and baryon-strangeness susceptibilities from the lattice at zero magnetic field. The constituent quarks possess anomalous magnetic moments estimated from the octet baryon magnetic moments. The vacuum quark-loop and meson-loop contributions are duly incorporated. We show that in such a framework, one can describe the magnetic susceptibility up to the cross-over point. The qualitative conclusion is that the QCD degrees of freedom must extend far below the cross-over temperature, down to $\approx 120$~MeV.

## Key claim

The widely used Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model, even when augmented with physical magnetic moments of hadrons or pion-vector-meson loops, cannot reproduce lattice QCD results for the magnetic susceptibility above approximately 120 MeV because it predicts too strong diamagnetism; a quark-meson model with temperature-dependent quark masses fixed from baryon-baryon and baryon-strangeness susceptibilities can simultaneously describe the magnetic susceptibility, baryon-baryon, and baryon-strangeness lattice data up to the QCD cross-over temperature.

## Method

The authors constructed a non-interacting quark-meson model below the cross-over temperature, incorporating constituent quarks (u, d, s) with temperature-dependent masses fixed model-independently from lattice data for baryon-baryon and baryon-strangeness susceptibilities at zero magnetic field, plus pions and kaons to retain diamagnetism at low temperatures. The vacuum quark-loop and meson-loop contributions to the magnetic susceptibility were included with a regulator for the quark vacuum part, and the constituent quarks were assigned anomalous magnetic moments estimated from octet baryon magnetic moments.

## Key numbers

* The pseudo-critical temperature of the QCD cross-over is approximately 155 MeV.
* The mismatch between HRG and lattice data for the magnetic susceptibility becomes apparent above approximately 120 MeV.
* The light quark mass at the cross-over temperature is approximately 550 MeV, and the strange quark mass is approximately 600 MeV, with the splitting m_s - m_l stable around 120-130 MeV.
* The pion-vector-meson loop contribution to the magnetic susceptibility is paramagnetic and ranges from 10% to 20% of the total HRG value across the temperature range.
* The magnetic polarizability contributions from hadrons are negligible, with 100 times the polarizability contribution estimated at approximately -0.002.

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD studies of magnetic susceptibility by Bali (2014, 2020) and Brandt (2024), which first demonstrated diamagnetism at low temperatures and the failure of HRG above approximately 120 MeV. It contradicts the assumption that HRG is adequate up to the cross-over temperature for all observables, as it works well for conserved charge susceptibilities (Bollweg 2021). The quark-meson approach enables simultaneous description of magnetic, baryon-baryon, and baryon-strangeness susceptibilities, a task not accomplished within HRG, and suggests that quark degrees of freedom must be present significantly below the cross-over temperature, down to approximately 120 MeV.
