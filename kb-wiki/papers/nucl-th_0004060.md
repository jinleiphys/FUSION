# Probing the effectiveness: chiral perturbation theory calculations of low-energy reactions on the deuteron

> I provide a brief, and therefore necessarily biased, review of the application of effective field theory to electromagnetic processes on the deuteron. Electron-deuteron scattering is discussed for virtual photon momenta up to 700 MeV, and photon-deuteron scattering is examined for incident photon energies of order the pion mass.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (χPT) provides a systematically improvable, model-independent framework for calculating electromagnetic reactions on the deuteron at low energies, but the approach requires careful handling of the deuteron wave function and the power-counting for the NN scattering amplitude, which remains an open issue.

## Method

The author reviews the application of χPT to electron-deuteron scattering (virtual photon momenta up to 700 MeV) and Compton scattering on the deuteron (incident photon energies of order the pion mass). The method divides the calculation into two parts: a systematic Q-expansion for the transition operator Ô, and construction of the deuteron wave function using one of three approaches: a full chiral wave function from a Q³ potential, a wave function from one-pion exchange plus a short-distance potential tuned to reproduce the deuteron binding energy and asymptotic normalizations, or a potential-model wave function such as Nijm93.

## Key numbers

- Electron-deuteron scattering is discussed for virtual photon momenta up to 700 MeV
- The deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d varies from 0.258 fm² (short-distance potential radius R=2.5 fm) to 0.269 fm² (R=1.5 fm), compared to the experimental value of 0.286 fm²
- For the proton, experimental polarizabilities are α_p + β_p = 13.23 ± 0.86^{+0.20}_{-0.49} × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and α_p - β_p = 10.11 ± 1.74^{+1.22}_{-0.86} × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- χPT at leading loop order (O(e²Q)) predicts α_p = 12.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³ and β_p = 1.2 × 10⁻⁴ fm³
- Compton scattering data are compared at photon energies of 69 MeV and 95 MeV

## Context

This review extends the application of effective field theory from the one-nucleon sector (where χPT is well-established, as in Bernard (1995)) to light nuclei, specifically the deuteron. It builds on Weinberg's (1992) proposal to use χPT for nuclear potentials and wave functions, and addresses the power-counting issues raised by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998). The work enables systematic inclusion of two-body currents (meson-exchange currents) in electromagnetic reactions, which is critical for extracting neutron polarizabilities from Compton scattering on deuterium, as pursued experimentally by Lucas (1994) and Hornidge (2000).
