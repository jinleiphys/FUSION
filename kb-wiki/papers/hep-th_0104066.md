# Shear viscosity of strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma

> Using the anti-de Sitter/conformal field theory correspondence, we relate the shear viscosity ηof the finite-temperature N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory in the large N, strong-coupling regime with the absorption cross section of low-energy gravitons by a near-extremal black three-brane. We show that in the limit of zero frequency this cross section coincides with the area of the horizon. From this result we find η=π/8 N^2T^3. We conjecture that for finite 't Hooft coupling (g_YM)^2N the shear viscosity is η=f((g_YM)^2N) N^2T^3, where f(x) is a monotonic function that decreases from O(x^{-2}\ln^{-1}(1/x)) at small x to π/8 when x\to\infty.

# Shear viscosity of strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills plasma

## Key claim
The shear viscosity of strongly coupled N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory at large N and large 't Hooft coupling is computed exactly as η = (π/8) N² T³, and the authors conjecture that for finite 't Hooft coupling the viscosity takes the form η = f(g²N) N² T³ where f(x) is a monotonic function decreasing from O(x⁻² ln⁻¹(1/x)) at small x to π/8 as x → ∞.

## Method
The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to relate the shear viscosity of the finite-temperature N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory to the absorption cross section of low-energy gravitons by a near-extremal black three-brane. They solve the s-wave radial wave equation for a minimally coupled scalar on the black three-brane metric using a matching method across three regions (near-horizon, intermediate, and asymptotic), and show that in the zero-frequency limit the absorption cross section equals the area of the horizon. The shear viscosity is then extracted via the Kubo formula, which relates the viscosity to the graviton absorption cross section.

## Key numbers
- η = (π/8) N² T³ for the strongly coupled N=4 SYM theory in the large N, large 't Hooft coupling limit
- The zero-frequency graviton absorption cross section σ(0) = π³ r₀³ R², which equals the area of the horizon
- The Hawking temperature of the black three-brane is T = r₀/(π R²)
- At weak coupling (g²N ≪ 1), the shear viscosity has parametric form η = C N² T³ / [(g²N)² ln(1/g²N)]
- The absorption probability for low-energy gravitons is P = (π/32) ω⁵ r₀³ R²

## Context
This work extends the AdS/CFT correspondence (Maldacena 1998, Gubser-Klebanov-Polyakov 1998, Witten 1998) to compute transport coefficients nonperturbatively in a strongly coupled gauge theory, a regime inaccessible to lattice simulations. The result that the shear viscosity approaches a constant value at infinite coupling contrasts with the weak-coupling behavior (Arnold-Moore-Yaffe 2000) where η diverges as the coupling goes to zero. The finding that η is proportional to the entropy density (Gubser-Klebanov-Peet 1996, Gubser-Klebanov-Tseytlin 1998) and related to the horizon area parallels the universal result for black holes (Das-Gibbons-Mathur 1997). This work enables future calculations of other transport coefficients (such as R-charge diffusion constants) in strongly coupled plasmas using the AdS/CFT correspondence, and provides a benchmark for understanding the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions.
