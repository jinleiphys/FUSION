# Resonance production in a thermal model

> We discuss the pi+ pi- invariant-mass correlations and the resonance production in a thermal model with single freeze-out. The predictions are confronted with the recent data from the STAR Collaboration.

## Key claim
The thermal model with single freeze-out can reproduce the gross features of the STAR pi+ pi- invariant-mass correlation data, but thermal effects and kinematic cuts shift the rho peak downward by only about 30 MeV, leaving a few tens of MeV unexplained unless the rho mass is also reduced by 9%.

## Method
The authors computed pi+ pi- invariant-mass spectra from decays of neutral resonances (rho, K_S^0, omega, eta, eta', f0/sigma, f2) using a phase-shift formula for the volume density of resonances. They applied a single-freeze-out thermal model with a Hubble-like flow, four parameters (T=160 MeV, mu_B=26 MeV at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, invariant time tau, transverse size rho_max), and compared the full calculation including flow, kinematic cuts, and detector efficiency to STAR data.

## Key numbers
- Freeze-out temperature T = 160 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B = 26 MeV at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, fixed by fitting particle abundance ratios.
- Thermal effects and kinematic cuts shift the rho peak downward by about 30 MeV.
- The rho peak position was moved down by 9% in the model to improve agreement with data.
- Static-source calculations at T = 165 MeV and T = 110 MeV show a much steeper fall-off at the lower temperature.

## Context
This work extends the single-freeze-out model of Broniowski and Florkowski (2002) to analyze resonance production in pi+ pi- invariant-mass correlations, confronting predictions with STAR data from Fachini and the STAR Collaboration (2003). The same phase-shift formalism was also applied by Pratt and Bauer in a similar analysis. The results enable further study of the "cooling" effect induced by resonances and the unexplained shift of the rho peak in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC.
