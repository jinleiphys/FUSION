# Hadron Properties and Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> An overview of the theory and phenomenology of hadrons and QCD is provided from a Dyson-Schwinger equation viewpoint. Following a discussion of the definition and realisation of light-quark confinement, the nonperturbative nature of the running mass in QCD and inferences from the gap equation relating to the radius of convergence for expansions of observables in the current-quark mass are described. Some exact results for pseudoscalar mesons are also highlighted, with details relating to the U_A(1) problem, and calculated masses of the lightest J=0,1 states are discussed. Studies of nucleon properties are recapitulated upon and illustrated: through a comparison of the ln-weighted ratios of Pauli and Dirac form factors for the neutron and proton; and a perspective on the contribution of quark orbital angular momentum to the spin of a nucleon at rest. Comments on prospects for the future of the study of quarks in hadrons and nuclei round out the contribution.

# Hadron Properties and Dyson-Schwinger Equations

## Key claim
Confinement can be identified through violation of the axiom of reflection positivity in QCD's Schwinger functions, but this is a sufficient and not necessary condition; the detailed structure of the dressed-quark-gluon vertex can influence the analytic properties of the quark propagator, so a firm conclusion on whether the dressed-quark propagator itself violates reflection positivity has not yet been reached.

## Method
The paper reviews the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) framework for nonperturbative QCD, focusing on the gap equation for the dressed-quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states. Confinement is diagnosed via the Euclidean-space Schwinger function and the confinement test based on reflection positivity, while dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) is studied through the momentum-dependent quark mass function obtained from solutions of the renormalised gap equation. The axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identity is used to derive exact results for pseudoscalar mesons, including the role of the non-Abelian anomaly in the U_A(1) problem.

## Key numbers
- The infrared quark mass function from DSE studies is approximately 100 times larger than the current-quark mass, with DCSB responsible for 98% of a proton's mass.
- The radius of convergence for chiral perturbation theory expansions in current-quark mass corresponds to a pseudoscalar meson mass of 0.45 GeV; chiral perturbation theory is only valid for pion masses squared less than 0.2 GeV^2.
- The model predicts eta-eta' mixing angles of approximately -15 degrees, pi0-eta angles of approximately 1 degree, and a strong neutron-proton mass difference of 0.75 times the difference between down and up current-quark masses.

## Context
This review extends the DSE-based approach to hadron physics developed by Roberts (1994) and collaborators, building on the confinement test formulated by Krein (1990) and the Munczek (1983) confining model. The discussion of the U_A(1) problem and eta' mass follows the work of Bhagwat (2007) and connects to the topological susceptibility arguments of Witten (1979) and Veneziano (1979). The results enable systematic truncations of DSEs for meson spectroscopy and provide constraints on the domain of applicability of chiral perturbation theory for lattice QCD simulations.
