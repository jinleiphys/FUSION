# Nuclear collective excitations in Landau Fermi liquid theory

> The nuclear collective excitations are studied within Landau Fermi liquid theory. By using the nucleon-nucleon interaction of the linear sigma-omega model, the nuclear collective excitation energies of different values of $l$ are obtained, which are fitted with the centroid energies of the giant resonances of spherical nuclei, respectively. In addition, it is pointed out that the isovector giant resonances except l=1 correspond to the modes that protons are in the creation state and neutrons are in the annihilation state, and vice versa. Some mixtures of the nuclear collective excitation states with different values of l are predicted.

## Key claim

Landau Fermi liquid theory, using a Fermi liquid function derived from the linear sigma-omega model, can reproduce the centroid energies of giant resonances in spherical nuclei, but only after fixing the effective nucleon mass to \(M^\ast_N = 0.742 M_N\) by fitting to the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance of \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) at 10.9 MeV; the agreement is less satisfactory for light nuclei without adjusting this parameter further.

## Method

The author extended the reduced Boltzmann equation for a Fermi liquid to three dimensions with spin, using the linear sigma-omega model Lagrangian to derive the Fermi liquid function from the static nucleon-nucleon potential. The quasi-nucleon density was expanded in spherical harmonics, and the resulting matrix equation for collective modes was diagonalized to obtain isolated eigenvalues corresponding to collective excitation energies for given angular momentum \(l\). Calculations were performed with parameters \(g_\sigma = 10.47\), \(g_\omega = 13.80\), \(m_\sigma = 520\ \text{MeV}\), \(m_\omega = 783\ \text{MeV}\), \(M_N = 939\ \text{MeV}\), and Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.36\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), with the effective nucleon mass varied to fit experimental data.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) with \(M^\ast_N = 0.742 M_N\), the calculated collective excitation energies are: \(l=0\): 12.28 MeV (experimental centroid 14.17 ± 0.28 MeV); \(l=1\): 13.73 MeV (experimental 13.5 ± 0.2 MeV); \(l=2\): 10.92 MeV (experimental 10.9 ± 0.1 MeV).
- For \(l=3\) with \(M^\ast_N = 0.8 M_N\), the positive isolated eigenvalue is 3.12 MeV, interpreted as a low-energy octupole resonance.
- For the isoscalar giant monopole in \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) at \(M^\ast_N = 0.742 M_N\): proton excitation energy \(E_0(p) = 16.28\ \text{MeV}\), neutron excitation energy \(E_0(n) = 7.05\ \text{MeV}\), sum \(E_0(p)+E_0(n) = 23.33\ \text{MeV}\) (experimental isovector centroid 26.0 ± 3.0 MeV).
- For the isovector giant dipole in \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) at \(M^\ast_N = 0.742 M_N\): \(E_1(p) = 16.97\ \text{MeV}\), \(E_1(n) = 8.93\ \text{MeV}\), sum 25.9 MeV (experimental isovector centroid 13.5 ± 0.2 MeV); adjusting to \(M^\ast_N = 0.755 M_N\) gives \(E_1(p) = 15.53\ \text{MeV}\), \(E_1(n) = 6.57\ \text{MeV}\), sum 22.1 MeV.
- For the isoscalar giant quadrupole in \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) at \(M^\ast_N = 0.742 M_N\): \(E_2(p) = 15.02\ \text{MeV}\), \(E_2(n) = 5.84\ \text{MeV}\), sum 20.86 MeV (experimental isovector centroid 22 MeV).
- Mixture energies for nuclear matter at \(k_F = 1.36\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) and \(M^\ast_N = 0.742 M_N\): \(l=0,1\) mixture: 19.14 MeV; \(l=0,1,2\) mixture: 19.89 MeV; \(l=0,1,2,3\) mixture: 19.91 MeV.

## Context

This work applies Landau Fermi liquid theory, a standard many-body method in condensed matter physics, to nuclear collective excitations, an approach the author notes has not been used successfully for nuclear many-body problems before. It extends the formalism of Wen (2004) to three-dimensional Fermi systems with spin and uses the linear sigma-omega model of Walecka (1974) for the effective interaction. The results are compared with experimental giant resonance data for spherical nuclei such as \(^{208}\text{Pb}\), \(^{90}\text{Zr}\), and \(^{40}\text{Ca}\), and with prior calculations using random phase approximation with Skyrme interactions (Zhang) and relativistic random phase approximation (Cao, Ma, Ring 2009). The paper predicts that isovector giant resonances (except \(l=1\)) correspond to proton creation and neutron annihilation modes (or vice versa), and that mixtures of different \(l\) states yield higher excitation energies, which could be tested in future experiments.
