# Dissecting Deuteron Compton Scattering I: The Observables with Polarised Initial States

> A complete set of linearly independent observables in Compton scattering with arbitrarily polarised real photons off an arbitrarily polarised spin-1 target is introduced, for the case that the final-state polarisations are not measured. Adopted from the one widely used e.g. in deuteron photo-dissociation, it consists of 18 terms: the unpolarised cross section, the beam asymmetry, 4 target asymmetries and 12 asymmetries in which both beam and target are polarised. They are expressed by the helicity amplitudes and -- where available -- related to observables discussed by other authors. As application to deuteron Compton scattering, their dependence on the (isoscalar) scalar and spin dipole polarisabilities of the nucleon is explored in Chiral Effective Field Theory with dynamical Delta(1232) degrees of freedom at order $e^2δ^3$. Some asymmetries are sensitive to only one or two dipole polarisabilities, making them particularly attractive for experimental studies. At a photon energy of 100 MeV, a set of 5 observables is identified from which one may be able to extract the spin polarisabilities of the nucleon. These are experimentally realistic but challenging and mostly involve tensor-polarised deuterons. Relative to Compton scattering from a nucleon, sensitivity to the "mixed" spin polarisabilities $γ_{E1M2}$ and $γ_{M1E2}$ is increased because of interference with the D wave component of the deuteron and with its pion-exchange current. An interactive Mathematica 9.0 notebook with results for all observables at photon energies up to 120 MeV is available from hgrie@gwu.edu. ## Key claim

The paper introduces a complete set of 18 linearly independent observables for deuteron Compton scattering with arbitrarily polarised initial states and no final-state polarisation detection, but the abstract's claim that some asymmetries are "particularly attractive for experimental studies" is qualified by the finding that the set of 5 observables identified for extracting nucleon spin polarisabilities at 100 MeV are "experimentally realistic but challenging and mostly involve tensor-polarised deuterons."

## Method

The author adopts the parametrisation of the differential cross section from deuteron photo-disintegration (Arenhovel, 1990), expressing it in terms of the unpolarised cross section, one beam asymmetry, four target asymmetries, and twelve double asymmetries involving both beam and target polarisation. These observables are expressed in terms of helicity amplitudes for Compton scattering on a spin-1 target, and their sensitivity to the isoscalar scalar and spin dipole polarisabilities of the nucleon is explored using Chiral Effective Field Theory with dynamical Delta(1232) degrees of freedom at order e^2 δ^3.

## Key numbers

- At a photon energy of 100 MeV, a set of 5 observables is identified from which one may be able to extract the nucleon spin polarisabilities.
- The static scalar polarisabilities of the proton from a recent fit are α_E1^(p) = 10.7 ± 0.3(stat) ± 0.2(Baldin) ± 0.3(theory) and β_M1^(p) = 3.1 ∓ 0.3(stat) ± 0.2(Baldin) ∓ 0.3(theory), in units of 10^(-4) fm^3.
- The isoscalar scalar polarisabilities from deuteron data are α_E1 = 10.9 ± 0.9(stat) ± 0.2(Baldin) ± 0.8(theory) and β_M1 = 3.6 ∓ 0.9(stat) ± 0.2(Baldin) ∓ 0.8(theory), in units of 10^(-4) fm^3.
- The isoscalar Baldin sum rule gives α_E1 + β_M1 = 14.5 ± 0.3, in units of 10^(-4) fm^3.
- The linear combination γ_π is approximately in the range [5 to 15], in units of 10^(-4) fm^4.

## Context

This work extends the classification of Compton scattering observables from the spin-1/2 case discussed by Babusci et al. (1998) to the spin-1 deuteron target, and builds upon prior studies of deuteron Compton scattering by Chen, Ji and Li (2004) and Griesshammer (2010). The paper enables future experimental programmes at facilities like HIγS, MAMI and MAX-Lab to extract the isoscalar nucleon spin polarisabilities, particularly the mixed spin polarisabilities γ_E1M2 and γ_M1E2, which show enhanced sensitivity due to interference with the deuteron D-wave component and pion-exchange currents.
