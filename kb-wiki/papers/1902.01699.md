# Resistive dissipative magnetohydrodynamics from the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation

> We derive the equations of motion of relativistic, resistive, second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics from the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation using the method of moments. We thus extend our previous work [Phys. Rev. D 98, 076009 (2018)], where we only considered the non-resistive limit, to the case of finite electric conductivity. This requires keeping terms proportional to the electric field $E^μ$ in the equations of motions and leads to new transport coefficients due to the coupling of the electric field to dissipative quantities. We also show that the Navier-Stokes limit of the charge-diffusion current corresponds to Ohm's law, while the coefficients of electrical conductivity and charge diffusion are related by a type of Wiedemann-Franz law.

## Key claim

The authors derive the equations of motion for relativistic, resistive, second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamics from the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation using the method of moments, extending their previous non-resistive work (Denicol et al., Phys. Rev. D 98, 076009, 2018) to the case of finite electric conductivity by keeping terms proportional to the electric field in the equations of motion.

## Method

The authors start from the Boltzmann-Vlasov equation for a single-component system of spin-zero particles with electric charge undergoing binary elastic collisions. They apply the method of moments to derive an infinite set of equations of motion for irreducible moments of the deviation of the single-particle distribution function from local equilibrium, then truncate this system using the 14-moment approximation to obtain closed fluid-dynamical equations. The electric field is included explicitly, leading to new transport coefficients from the coupling of charged particles to the electric field.

## Key numbers

- For a massless Boltzmann gas with constant cross section: the electrical conductivity coefficient in the diffusion equation is $\delta_{VE} = P_0 \beta_0^2 / 12$ (in units of $\tau_V$)
- For a massless Boltzmann gas with constant cross section: the shear viscosity is $\eta = 4/(3\sigma \beta_0)$ where $\sigma$ is the cross section
- For a massless Boltzmann gas with constant cross section: the relaxation time for shear is $\tau_\pi = 5/3$ (in units of mean free path $\lambda_{\mathrm{mfp}}$)
- For a massless Boltzmann gas with constant cross section: the relaxation time for diffusion is $\tau_V = 9/4$ (in units of $\lambda_{\mathrm{mfp}}$)
- For a massless Boltzmann gas with constant cross section: the thermal conductivity is $\kappa = 3/(16\sigma)$
- The electric conductivity is related to thermal conductivity by $\sigma_E \equiv \textswab{q}^2 \kappa/T$, a type of Wiedemann-Franz law

## Context

This work extends the non-resistive magnetohydrodynamic framework developed by Denicol et al. (2018) to include finite electric conductivity, which is necessary because taking the infinite conductivity limit while keeping other transport coefficients finite is inconsistent with the underlying Boltzmann equation. The derivation provides a complete second-order dissipative magnetohydrodynamic framework that can be applied to describe the evolution of strongly interacting matter in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, where strong electromagnetic fields are created in non-central collisions and give rise to phenomena like the Chiral Magnetic Effect. The equations are compatible with both Landau and Eckart definitions of the fluid rest frame.
