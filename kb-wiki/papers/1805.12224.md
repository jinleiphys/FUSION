# Measuring Color Memory in a Color Glass Condensate at Electron-Ion Colliders

> The color memory effect is the non-abelian gauge theory analog of the gravitational memory effect, in which the passage of color radiation induces a net relative SU(3) color rotation of a pair of nearby quarks. It is proposed that this effect can be measured in the Regge limit of deeply inelastic scattering at electron-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The color memory effect, a permanent relative SU(3) color rotation of a quark dipole after passage of color radiation, can be measured in the Regge limit of deeply inelastic scattering at electron-ion colliders, provided that the Lorentz-contracted ion radius is small compared to the QCD length scale, analogous to how LIGO's pendulum restoring force must be separated from the gravitational wave timescale.

## Method

The authors map the color memory effect derived at future null infinity onto the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory formulated in the infinite momentum frame, showing that the same differential equation for large gauge transformations appears in both frameworks. They then relate the color rotation of a quark dipole traversing a shockwave, described by the eikonal phase U(x), to the dipole scattering amplitude S(x,y) = (1/N_c) Tr(U(x)U†(y)), which enters the inclusive deeply inelastic scattering cross-section through factorization into a photon splitting wavefunction and the dipole cross-section.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale Q_s^2 = (g_YM^2/4π) C_F log(1/(Λ_QCD^2 |r|^2)) ∫ dx⁻ μ²(x⁻), where C_F is the quadratic Casimir in the fundamental representation and μ² is the color charge squared per unit rapidity per unit area.
- The dipole scattering amplitude in the Gaussian source approximation gives ⟨S(r)⟩ ≈ exp{-(1/4) |r|² Q_s²}, with the transition between weak and strong absorption occurring at |r| ~ 1/Q_s.
- The Regge limit is defined by taking Bjorken x_Bj ~ Q²/s → 0 at fixed large Q² ≫ Λ_QCD², where Λ_QCD is the QCD scale.

## Context

This work extends the equivalence between soft theorems, asymptotic symmetries, and memory effects, previously established for gravity (Strominger, 2014) and Yang-Mills theory (Pate et al., 2017), to a concrete collider observable. It connects the formal color memory effect to the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory (Iancu et al., 2003; Gelis et al., 2010; Kovchegov et al., 2012) and shows that existing and future electron-ion collider data can test whether the dipole scattering amplitude is determined by the color memory effect, with the JIMWLK renormalization group evolution and the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation governing the x-dependence of the saturation scale.
