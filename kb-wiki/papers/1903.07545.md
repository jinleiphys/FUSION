# Naive versus mirror assignment at finite density: correlator mixing and chiral symmetry restoration

> Chiral symmetry imposes some constrains on hadron correlation functions in dense medium. These constraints imply a certain general structure of the two- and three-point correlation functions of chiral partners and lead to the effect of mixing arising from the interaction of the long-ranged nuclear pions with corresponding interpolating currents. It reflects the phenomena of partial restoration of chiral symmetry. We consider soft pion corrections for quark condensates and several possible pairs of chiral partners for both mesons and nucleons and analyse their mixing patterns. We explored both naive and mirror assignments for the chiral partner of nucleon and discussed possible phenomenological consequence.

# Naive versus mirror assignment at finite density: correlator mixing and chiral symmetry restoration

## Key claim

Chiral symmetry constraints on hadron correlation functions in dense medium imply a mixing effect from long-ranged nuclear pions interacting with interpolating currents, reflecting partial chiral symmetry restoration; however, the naive assignment for the nucleon chiral partner yields a correlator difference that grows with density rather than decreasing, contradicting the expectation that chiral partners become degenerate, whereas the mirror assignment produces a mixing pattern that can push the system toward restoration.

## Method

The author derives soft-pion corrections to two-point and three-point hadron correlation functions in nuclear matter by expanding the nuclear wave function to include virtual pion states and applying the soft-pion theorem to evaluate commutators of the axial charge with interpolating fields. The analysis is performed for both the naive assignment (where nucleon and its chiral partner transform identically under chiral rotations) and the mirror assignment (where they transform into each other), using the Ioffe choice for the nucleon interpolating field and the standard chiral perturbation theory expression for the pion-nucleon sigma term.

## Key numbers

- The leading nonanalytic part in the chiral expansion of the pion-nucleon sigma term is taken to be -20 MeV, following chiral perturbation theory results from Gas (year not specified in text).
- The parameter xi = (rho * sigma_piN) / (f_pi^2 * m_pi^2) controls the size of the soft-pion corrections, where rho is nuclear density.
- For the in-medium quark condensate, the soft-pion corrections partially compensate the linear-density drop; the full calculation from We (year not specified) and the author's model-independent estimate agree up to a density of approximately 0.07-0.08 fm^{-3}.
- The rho to pi pi coupling constant in vacuum is approximately 6.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis of chiral mixing in the naive assignment (Krippa, year not specified) and contrasts it with the mirror assignment originally proposed by Kun (year not specified) in the parity doublet model. The paper provides model-independent chiral constraints that any effective model of chiral symmetry restoration should satisfy, complementing model-dependent approaches such as the quark-meson model (Sch, year not specified), dilaton models (Mish, year not specified), and chiral models with hidden local symmetry (Moto, year not specified). The results enable future studies of the in-medium nucleon self-energy via QCD sum rules with proper mixing patterns, and suggest that at sufficiently large densities the mirror assignment leads to two mismatched Fermi surfaces, opening possibilities for LOFF and Sarma phases.
