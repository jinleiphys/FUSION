# Gauge-Invariant Formulation of Adiabatic Self-Consistent Collective Coordinate Method

> The adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method is a practical microscopic theory of large-amplitude collective motions in nuclei with superfluidity. We show that its basic equations are invariant against transformations involving the gauge angle in the particle-number space. By virtue of this invariance, a clean separation between the large-amplitude collective motion and the pairing rotational motion can be achieved, enabling us to restore the particle-number symmetry broken by the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approximation. We formulate the ASCC method explicitly in a gauge-invariant form. In solving the ASCC equations, it is necessary to fix the gauge. Applying this new formulation to the multi-O(4) model, we compare different gauge-fixing procedures and demonstrate that calculations using different gauges indeed yield the same results for gauge-invariant quantities, such as the collective path and quantum spectra. We suggest a gauge-fixing prescription that seems most convenient in realistic calculations.

## Key claim

The ASCC method's basic equations are invariant under gauge transformations involving the gauge angle in particle-number space, and this gauge invariance explains a previously observed numerical redundancy that was treated with an ad hoc condition in Hinohara (2006). The paper demonstrates that different gauge-fixing choices yield identical gauge-invariant quantities such as the collective path and quantum spectra.

## Method

The authors reformulate the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method to make its gauge invariance with respect to the pairing rotational degree of freedom manifest, requiring the collective coordinate operator \(\Qhat(q)\) to commute with the number operator. They apply this gauge-invariant formulation to the multi-O(4) model, solving the moving-frame HFB equation and local harmonic equations iteratively while comparing different gauge-fixing procedures, including the "QRPA gauge" where \(\partial\lambda/\partial q = 0\).

## Key numbers

- The multi-O(4) model Hamiltonian contains three residual interactions with strengths \(G_0\), \(G_2\), and \(\chi\) for monopole-pairing, quadrupole-pairing, and quadrupole particle-hole interactions, respectively.
- The particle number constraint is \(N_0 = 2\sum_{i>0} v_i^2(q)\) (Eq. 4.7).
- The quadrupole deformation is \(D(q) = 2\sum_{i>0} d_i \sigma_i v_i^2(q)\) (Eq. 4.8).
- The monopole-pairing gap is \(\Delta_0(q) = G_0 \sum_{i>0} u_i(q)v_i(q)\) (Eq. 4.9).
- The quadrupole-pairing gap is \(\Delta_2(q) = G_2 \sum_{i>0} d_i \sigma_i u_i(q) v_i(q)\) (Eq. 4.10).

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method originally proposed by Matsuo (2000) and addresses a numerical instability encountered in shape-coexistence studies by Kobayashi (2004). The gauge-invariant formulation provides a theoretical justification for the ad hoc condition used in Hinohara (2006) and enables clean separation between large-amplitude collective motion and pairing rotational motion, which is essential for particle-number restoration in systems with superfluidity. The multi-O(4) model tests follow previous benchmark calculations by Matsuyanagi (1982), Mizobuchi (1981), Suzuki (1988), Fukui (1991), and Kobayashi (2003).
