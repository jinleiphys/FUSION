# Constraining nuclear matter parameters from correlation systematics:a mean-field perspective

> The nuclear matter parameters define the nuclear equation of state (EoS), they appear as coefficients of expansion around the saturation density of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. We review their correlations with several properties of finite nuclei and of neutron stars within mean-field frameworks. The lower order nuclear matter parameters such as the binding energy per nucleon, incompressibility and the symmetry energy coefficients are found to be constrained in narrow limits through their strong ties with selective properties of finite nuclei. From the correlations of nuclear matter parameters with neutron star observables, we further review how precision knowledge of the radii and tidal deformability of neutron stars in the mass range $1 - 2 M_\odot$ may help cast them in narrower bounds. The higher order parameters such as the density slope and the curvature of the symmetry energy or the skewness of the symmetric nuclear matter EoS are, however, plagued with larger uncertainty. From inter-correlation of these higher order nuclear matter parameters with lower order ones, we explore how they can be brought to more harmonious bounds.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how lower-order nuclear matter parameters (binding energy per nucleon, incompressibility, symmetry energy coefficient) can be constrained within narrow limits through their strong correlations with finite-nucleus properties, while higher-order parameters (density slope and curvature of symmetry energy, skewness of symmetric nuclear matter EoS) remain more uncertain but can be brought to more harmonious bounds through inter-correlation with lower-order parameters.

## Method

The authors employ a mean-field framework using Skyrme and relativistic energy density functionals (EDFs) to compute nuclear matter parameters as Taylor expansion coefficients around saturation density. They analyze correlation systematics between these parameters and observables from finite nuclei (ISGMR energies, neutron-skin thickness, symmetry energies from double-difference methods) and neutron star properties (radii, tidal deformability). The analysis uses a thermodynamic approach to the EDF that relates lower-order and higher-order density derivatives through iterative relations derived from the Euler equation and the density-dependent effective nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- ISGMR data yields $K_0 \sim 212$ MeV from correlation analysis at crossing density $\rho_c = (0.710 \pm 0.005)\rho_0$, with $K_c \approx 35 \pm 4$ MeV and $M_c \approx 1050 \pm 100$ MeV
- Volume symmetry energy $C_{\rm sym}^v = 32.10 \pm 0.31$ MeV and surface symmetry energy $C_{\rm sym}^s = 58.91 \pm 1.08$ MeV extracted from double-difference analysis of experimental symmetry energies
- $K_0$ value of $230 \pm 20$ MeV from earlier ISGMR analysis, contrasted with softer $K_0 \sim 200$ MeV from Sn and Cd isotope data
- Neutron-skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb from PREX: $\Delta r_{np} = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}$ fm
- $L_0 = 75 \pm 25$ MeV from neutron-skin correlation analysis using hadronic probes
- $K_{\rm sym}^0$ ranges from -700 MeV to +400 MeV and $Q_{\rm sym}^0$ from -800 MeV to 1500 MeV across different Skyrme parameterizations
- Correlation relation: $K_{\rm sym}^0 \approx 3.41 L_0 - 306 \pm 40$ MeV from Tews et al. (2017)
- $Q_0$ (skewness parameter) calculated as $\sim -378$ MeV from the correlation chain

## Context

This review extends the correlation systematics approach pioneered by Blaizot (1980) for ISGMR-incompressibility correlations and by Centelles et al. (2009) for neutron-skin-symmetry energy correlations. It builds on work by Khan et al. (2012, 2013) showing that ISGMR energy correlates with incompressibility at a crossing density rather than at saturation density, and by Jiang et al. (2012) on double-difference extraction of symmetry energy coefficients. The paper enables tighter constraints on higher-order nuclear matter parameters through inter-correlation with better-known lower-order parameters, and shows how precision measurements of neutron star radii and tidal deformability from gravitational wave events like GW170817 (Abbott et al., 2017) can further narrow these bounds.
