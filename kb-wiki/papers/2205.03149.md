# Conductivity, diffusivity, and violation of Wiedemann-Franz Law in a hadron resonance gas with van der Waals interactions

> In this work, a hadron resonance gas under van der Waals (VDW) interactions has been studied. Both attractive and repulsive interactions between the meson-meson and (anti)baryon-(anti)baryon have been taken into consideration. Various transport properties such as electrical conductivity ($σ_{\rm el}$) and thermal conductivity ($κ_{\rm th}$) have been estimated by solving the Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation. The effect of baryochemical potential ($μ_{\rm B}$) and temperature is also explicitly explored for the mentioned observables. Comparisons have been made with the results obtained from other existing theoretical models. We observe the violation of Wiedemann-Franz law in a hadron resonance gas at a high-temperature regime. The corresponding diffusivities have also been estimated, which can help us to understand the system in a better way.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the Wiedemann-Franz law is violated in a hadron resonance gas at high temperature, but this violation is conditional on the inclusion of van der Waals interactions (both attractive and repulsive) between meson-meson and (anti)baryon-(anti)baryon pairs, and is demonstrated specifically within the relaxation time approximation of the Boltzmann transport equation.

## Method
The authors solve the Boltzmann transport equation under the relaxation time approximation for a hadron resonance gas with van der Waals interactions. They include both attractive and repulsive interactions between meson-meson and (anti)baryon-(anti)baryon pairs, using hardcore radii of 0.2 fm for mesons and 0.62 fm for (anti)baryons, with the van der Waals parameter a = 0.926 GeV fm³. Electrical conductivity (σ_el) and thermal conductivity (κ_th) are computed from the resulting distribution functions, and the Lorenz number (L = κ/(σT)) is evaluated to test the Wiedemann-Franz law.

## Key numbers
- Hardcore radius for mesons: r_M = 0.2 fm; for (anti)baryons: r_B = 0.62 fm.
- Van der Waals parameter a = 0.926 GeV fm³.
- Baryochemical potentials studied: μ_B = 0.0, 0.025, 0.2, 0.436, and 0.630 GeV, corresponding to collision energies from LHC down to √s_NN = 3 GeV (NICA).
- Mass cut-off for included hadrons and resonances: 2.25 GeV.
- Scaled electrical conductivity σ_el/T decreases with increasing temperature at all μ_B values, with VDWHRG results being higher than ideal HRG at high temperatures.
- Scaled thermal conductivity κ/T² shows a similar decreasing trend with temperature, compared against results from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, excluded volume HRG model, chiral perturbation theory, color string percolation model, and pion gas calculations.

## Context
This work extends the van der Waals hadron resonance gas model (Vovchenko 2016, Sarkar 2018) by computing transport coefficients beyond thermodynamics, specifically electrical and thermal conductivities. It contrasts with ideal HRG and excluded volume HRG models (Kadam 2017) by including both attractive and repulsive interactions, and it compares results with parton-hadron-string dynamics (Cassing 2013), color string percolation model (Sahoo 2018), and lattice QCD-inspired approaches. The violation of the Wiedemann-Franz law at high temperature provides a new observable for characterizing the hadronic phase, and the computed diffusivities (charge diffusion D_el and thermal diffusivity D_Th) can inform hydrodynamic simulations for heavy-ion collisions at LHC, RHIC, FAIR, and NICA energies.
