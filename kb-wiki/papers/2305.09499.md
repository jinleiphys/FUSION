# Energy and Angle Dependence of Neutrino Scattering Rates in Proto-Neutron Star and Supernova Matter within Skyrme RPA

> Supernova explosions are the most powerful neutrino sources. The neutrino emission is also the dominating cooling mechanism for a proto-neutron star, whose interior is mainly composed of extremely dense and hot nuclear matter. Neutrino transport is an essential part of the simulation of these phenomena, and modern codes are able to implement inelastic neutrino scattering and also to some extent its angle distribution. We therefore study the energy and angle dependence of neutrino scattering rates in proto-neutron star and supernova matter with the full Skyrme RPA response functions. We confirm earlier findings obtained in the Landau approximation that the RPA reduces neutrino scattering, but the detailed differential scattering rates in hot and dense matter depend sensitively on the adopted interaction. The scattering angle distribution is different for different interactions because it depends strongly on the neutron Fermi velocity. We also find that many Skyrme interactions present an unphysical feature that the Fermi velocity of neutrons exceeds the speed of light already at relatively low densities.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that random phase approximation (RPA) reduces neutrino scattering rates compared to Hartree-Fock calculations, but the detailed differential scattering rates in hot and dense matter depend sensitively on the adopted Skyrme interaction, and many Skyrme interactions exhibit an unphysical feature where the neutron Fermi velocity exceeds the speed of light at relatively low densities.

## Method

The authors derived the neutrino-nucleon scattering rate from the nuclear density and spin-density correlation functions using Fermi's golden rule, then computed the full Skyrme RPA response functions in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature by solving the Bethe-Salpeter-like equation for the particle-hole interaction. They compared these full RPA results with the Hartree-Fock response and the lowest-order Landau approximation, and studied the energy and angle dependence of neutrino scattering rates for different Skyrme interactions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: $\rho_0 = 0.16$ nucleons/fm$^3$ (corresponding to $2.7 \times 10^{14}$ g/cm$^3$)
- Fermi weak interaction constant: $G \approx 8.975 \times 10^{-5}$ MeV fm$^3$
- Neutral current coupling constants: $C_{V,n} = -1$, $C_{A,n} = -1.23$ for neutrons; $C_{V,p} = 0.08$, $C_{A,p} = 1.23$ for protons
- Response functions computed at density $\rho = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$, proton fraction $Y_p = 0.2$, temperature $T = 10$ MeV, and momentum transfer $q = 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$
- Proto-neutron star initial internal temperature: $10^{11}-10^{12}$ K ($10-100$ MeV)
- Typical proto-neutron star mass: $1-2 M_{\odot}$, radius: about 10 km

## Context

This work extends earlier RPA studies of neutrino responses by Burrows (1998), Reddy (1999), and Horowitz (2003), which used the Landau approximation rather than full RPA, and by Pastore (2015), which discussed full RPA response functions with Skyrme interactions but did not show neutrino mean-free paths for asymmetric matter or discuss angle and energy distributions. The results enable improved neutrino transport in supernova simulations and proto-neutron star evolution calculations, particularly for implementing inelastic neutrino scattering and its angle distribution in modern simulation codes.
