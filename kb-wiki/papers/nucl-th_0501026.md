# Unintegrated CCFM parton distributions and pion production in proton-proton collisions at high energies

> Inclusive cross sections for pion production in proton-proton collisions are calculated for the first time based on unintegrated parton (gluon, quark, antiquark) distributions (uPDF). We use Kwieciński uPDF's and phenomenological fragmentation functions from the literature. In addition to the $gg \to g$ diagram used recently for applications at RHIC we include also $g q \to q$ and $q g \to q$ diagrams. We find that the new contributions are comparable to the purely gluonic one at midrapidities and dominate in the fragmentation region. The new mechanisms are responsible for $π^+ - π^-$ asymmetry. We discuss how the asymmetry depends on $x_F$ and $p_t$. Inclusive distributions in $x_F$ (or rapidity) and transverse momentum for partons and pions are shown for illustration. In contrast to standard collinear approach in our approach the range of applicability can be extended towards much lower transverse momenta.

# Unintegrated CCFM parton distributions and pion production in proton-proton collisions at high energies

## Key claim

Inclusive cross sections for pion production in proton-proton collisions are calculated for the first time using unintegrated parton distributions (uPDFs) from the Kwieciński approach, including not only the previously considered $gg \to g$ diagram but also $gq \to q$ and $qg \to q$ diagrams. The new contributions are comparable to the purely gluonic one at midrapidities and dominate in the fragmentation region, but the description of experimental data remains imperfect at very low transverse momenta, with the agreement depending sensitively on the choice of factorization scale for fragmentation functions and the treatment of nonperturbative form factors.

## Method

The authors solve the CCFM evolution equations in impact-parameter space using the Kwieciński formalism, with leading-order GRV parton distributions (1998) as initial conditions, to obtain unintegrated gluon, quark, and antiquark distributions. Inclusive parton cross sections are computed for three diagrams ($gg \to g$, $qg \to q$, $gq \to q$) using a convolution of uPDFs with a delta function for transverse momentum conservation, then converted to pion distributions via phenomenological Kretzer fragmentation functions (2000). Nonperturbative effects are included via a Gaussian form factor in impact-parameter space, and the running coupling uses an analytic model from Shirkov and Solovtsov (1997) with freezing at low scales.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy W = 17.3 GeV is used for parton distributions (energy of NA49 experiments at CERN SPS).
- Parton transverse momenta are integrated over the interval 0.2 GeV < $p_t$ < 4 GeV for $x_F$ distributions.
- The nonperturbative Gaussian form factor parameter $b_0$ is constrained to $b_0 >$ 0.5 GeV$^{-1}$ to avoid exceeding experimental data in the perturbative regime ($p_t >$ 2 GeV).
- Pion production results are shown at W = 27.4 GeV for comparison with experimental data.
- The Kretzer fragmentation functions are frozen below $\mu_D = p_t <$ 1 GeV, with the initial evolution scale $\mu_0^2$ = 0.26 GeV$^2$.
- The approach achieves reasonable description of data down to pion transverse momenta $\sim$ 0.5 GeV.
- The $\pi^+ - \pi^-$ asymmetry is well described by the model, depending only weakly on the $b_0$ parameter.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Kharzeev and Levin (2001), who used only gluonic mechanisms to describe particle production at RHIC, by adding quark and antiquark degrees of freedom that are necessary to explain the $\pi^+ - \pi^-$ asymmetry observed by the BRAHMS collaboration. The authors build on the CCFM evolution framework developed by Kwieciński and collaborators, previously tested for gauge boson production (Szczurek and Slominski, 2004) and charm photoproduction (Luszczak and Szczurek, 2004), and apply it to a lower-scale regime ($\mu \sim p_t \sim$ 0.5-1.0 GeV) where perturbative and nonperturbative effects mix. The results enable future studies of charge asymmetries at RHIC and further refinement of nonperturbative form factors and hadronization treatments.
