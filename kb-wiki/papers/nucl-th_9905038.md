# Differential flow in heavy-ion collisions at balance energies

> A strong differential transverse collective flow is predicted for the first time to occur in heavy-ion collisions at balance energies. We also give a novel explanation for the disappearance of the total transverse collective flow at the balance energies. It is further shown that the differential flow especially at high transverse momenta is a useful microscope capable of resolving the balance energy's dual sensitivity to both the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections in the reaction dynamics.

# Differential flow in heavy-ion collisions at balance energies

## Key claim

The paper predicts for the first time that a strong differential transverse collective flow exists at balance energies where the normal transverse collective flow disappears, and claims that this differential flow, especially at high transverse momenta, can resolve the dual sensitivity of the balance energy to both the nuclear equation of state and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, which the standard flow analysis cannot separate.

## Method

The authors used the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model to simulate Au+Au collisions at an impact parameter of 5 fm and several beam energies. They performed standard transverse momentum analysis to identify the balance energy, then computed the differential flow as the first-order Fourier coefficient v1(y,pt) = <cos(phi)>(y,pt) for nucleons in a selected rapidity bin, and studied its dependence on transverse momentum for different nuclear equations of state and in-medium cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Balance energy for Au+Au at impact parameter 5 fm is 50 MeV/nucleon, identified using a stiff nuclear EOS with compressibility K=380 MeV and free-space nucleon-nucleon cross sections.
- At the balance energy of 50 MeV/nucleon, the differential flow changes from attractive to repulsive at a balance transverse momentum of about p_bal = 0.2 GeV/c.
- A soft EOS with compressibility K=210 MeV combined with a 30% reduction in nucleon-nucleon cross sections gives approximately the same balance energy of 50 MeV/nucleon as the stiff EOS with free-space cross sections.
- For the stiff EOS with free-space cross sections, the average in-plane transverse momentum per nucleon in the rapidity range 0.5 <= (y/y_proj)_cms <= 1.0 is 0.86 MeV/c; for the soft EOS with reduced cross sections it is -0.02 MeV/c, both equivalent to zero within statistical errors.
- The soft EOS with reduced in-medium cross sections lowers the differential flow at high transverse momenta (above about 0.2 GeV/c) by about a factor of 2 compared to the stiff EOS with free-space cross sections.

## Context

This work extends the extensive experimental and theoretical studies of balance energies in heavy-ion collisions reviewed by Gary (1999) and others, which had been hindered by the dual sensitivity of the balance energy to both the nuclear EOS and in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections (as noted by Molitoris (1985), Bertsch (1987), Tsang (1989), and others). The differential flow analysis proposed here enables separation of these two effects, and the authors suggest it can be applied to existing experimental data on balance energies as a function of mass, isospin, and impact parameter, as well as to studies of "zero" flow of secondary particles like pions and kaons.
