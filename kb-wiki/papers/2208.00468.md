# Effect of global momentum conservation on longitudinal flow decorrelation

> We calculate the longitudinal flow decorrelation coefficients, i.e., $r_n(η,η_r)$ for $n=2,3$, in the presence of hydro-like flow and the global momentum conservation (GMC) constraint. The longitudinal flow decorrelation is weakened due to the GMC constraint. The GMC effect is sensitive to the total number of particles involved in GMC, the average longitudinal momentum, the transverse momentum, and the reference pseudorapidity. Our results of the $r_2(η,η_{rA})/r_2(η,η_{rB})$ ratio between two reference pseudorapidity bins are consistent with the experimental measurements. We predict that the modification effect of GMC on longitudinal flow decorrelation is more noticeable at BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider energies than at CERN Large Hadron Collider energies. Our finding provides a new perspective for understanding the longitudinal flow decorrelation in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the global momentum conservation (GMC) constraint weakens longitudinal flow decorrelation coefficients \(r_n(\eta,\eta_r)\) for \(n=2,3\), but this effect is conditional on the total number of particles involved in GMC, the average longitudinal momentum, the transverse momentum, and the reference pseudorapidity.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for two-particle azimuthal cumulants \(c_n\{2\}\) by combining a hydro-like flow model with a GMC constraint, expanding the joint probability distribution to first order in \(1/N\). They then compute the longitudinal flow decorrelation coefficients \(r_n(\eta,\eta_r)\) as ratios of these cumulants, and estimate the key parameters \(\langle p_z^2\rangle_\Gamma\) and \(N_\Gamma\) by fitting a longitudinally accelerating perfect-fluid hydrodynamic model to charged-particle pseudorapidity distributions from ALICE for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76\(A\) TeV.

## Key numbers

- The central pseudorapidity density \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta|_{\eta=0}\) for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76\(A\) TeV is 422.0 (30-40% centrality), 259.1 (40-50%), 147.1 (50-60%), and 74.7 (60-70%).
- The freeze-out temperature \(T_f\) is taken as 0.09 GeV and the average mass \(\bar{m}\) of all charged hadrons as 0.24 GeV.
- The effective temperature \(T_{\rm eff}\) is 0.27 GeV for all four centrality bins studied.
- The longitudinal acceleration parameter \(\lambda\) is 1.04 for all four centrality bins.
- The average number of particles for the 30-40% centrality bin is approximately 1.7 times that of 40-50%, 3 times that of 50-60%, and 6 times that of 60-70% centrality bins.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of transverse momentum conservation (TMC) effects on multi-particle cumulants at mid-rapidity by Bzdak (2017, 2018) and Xie (2022) to the longitudinal direction, incorporating full 3D momentum conservation. It also builds on the 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic framework with fluctuating initial conditions developed by Schenke (2010), Karpenko (2013), and Molnar (2014). The results enable more precise interpretation of longitudinal flow decorrelation measurements from CMS (2015), ATLAS (2017, 2020), and STAR (2020, 2022) by isolating the non-flow contribution from GMC, and predict that the GMC modification is more noticeable at RHIC energies than at LHC energies.
