# Creation of the inverse population in the $^{229}$Th ground-state doublet

> A new method for obtaining of inverse population between quadrupole sublevels of the ground state $5/2^+(0.0)$ and the isomeric state $3/2^+(7.6$ eV) of the $^{229}$Th nucleus in a dielectric crystal with a large band gap by means of external source of the VUV radiation is proposed. The method is based on an efficient depopulation of the upper sublevels of the ground state of $^{229}$Th by resonant photons from narrowband laser or broader tunable free electron laser. Sublevels of the isomeric state play the role of intermediate states. In addition, we have considered a case of excitation of the isomeric state (without creation of inverse population) by broadband source of light with the anti-Stokes scattering. The proposed scheme of optical pumping results in (1) inverse population of nuclear sublevels without using extremely low temperatures, and (2) shows a new way for the creation of the gamma-ray laser of optical range at the nuclear transition in $^{229}$Th (a) in crystals with isovalent substitution of host ions (eg Si$^{4+}$ ions in the SiO$_2$ crystal replaced by the $^{229}$Th$^{4+}$ ions) and (b) in crystals such as Na$_2$ThF$_6$, where the substitution is not necessary.

# Creation of the inverse population in the $^{229}$Th ground-state doublet

## Key claim
The authors propose that inverse population between quadrupole sublevels of the $^{229}$Th ground state and isomeric state can be achieved via optical pumping with a narrowband laser, without requiring the extremely low temperatures (0.01–0.1 K) needed in prior relaxation-based schemes. The method works specifically for crystals with isovalent substitution (e.g., Th$^{4+}$ replacing Si$^{4+}$ in SiO$_2$) or crystals like Na$_2$ThF$_6$ where substitution is not necessary, and the inverse population is sufficient for a VUV gamma-ray laser at the 7.6 eV nuclear transition.

## Method
The method uses two sequential stages of narrowband laser irradiation on $^{229}$Th nuclei in a dielectric crystal with large electric field gradient (EFG) at the thorium sites. First, a laser tuned to a specific quadrupole sublevel transition depopulates one of the ground state sublevels ($|5/2^+,\pm3/2\rangle$) via the isomeric state sublevels ($|3/2^+,\pm3/2\rangle$) over a few half-lives. Second, a laser excites nuclei from the remaining populated ground state sublevel ($|5/2^+,\pm5/2\rangle$) to the isomeric state, creating inverse population between the isomeric sublevel and the depleted ground state sublevel. The authors also consider simultaneous depopulation of two sublevels when the laser linewidth exceeds the energy splitting between transitions, and examine broadband laser excitation with anti-Stokes scattering (which does not produce inverse population).

## Key numbers
- Isomeric state energy: $E_{is} = 7.6 \pm 0.5$ eV (Beck 2007)
- Ground state quadrupole moment: $Q_{gr} = 3.15$ $e$b (Bemis 1988)
- Isomeric state quadrupole moment: $Q_{is} \simeq 1.8$ $e$b (Tkalya 2011)
- Effective electric field gradient at $^{229}$Th nucleus: $\varphi_{zz}^{eff} \simeq -2 \times 10^{19}$ V cm$^{-2}$ (or $\simeq -2 \times 10^{20}$ eV cm$^{-2}$)
- Interaction energy for ground state: $6 \times 10^{-5}$ eV; for isomeric state: $3.3 \times 10^{-5}$ eV
- Quadrupole splittings: $\Delta E_{gr_1} = 0.9 \times 10^{-5}$ eV, $\Delta E_{gr_2} = 1.8 \times 10^{-5}$ eV, $\Delta E_{is} = 1.6 \times 10^{-5}$ eV
- Radiative width of isomeric transition: $\Gamma_{rad} \simeq 3 \times 10^{-19}$ eV
- Decay constant: $\Lambda \simeq 4.5 \times 10^{-4}$ s$^{-1}$
- Excitation cross section (for $\Delta\omega_L \sim 10^{-7}$ eV): $\sigma \simeq 10^{-22}$ cm$^{-2}$
- For $\sigma\varphi/\Lambda = 10$: inverse population $n^{is}_{\pm3/2} - n^{gr}_{\pm3/2} \simeq 0.8$ at $t \simeq 0.2 T_{1/2}^{is}$
- For $\sigma\varphi/\Lambda = 1$: inverse population $n^{is}_{\pm3/2} - n^{gr}_{\pm3/2} \simeq 0.1$ at $t = T_{1/2}^{is}$
- Achievable inverse population density in $^{229}$Th:LiCaAlF$_6$: $2.5 \times 10^{17}$ cm$^{-3}$ (from initial density $n^{gr}_0 \simeq 10^{18}$ cm$^{-3}$, Rellergert 2010)
- Photon flux densities: $\varphi \simeq 10^{19}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$ for $\sigma\varphi/\Lambda = 1$, $\varphi \simeq 10^{20}$ cm$^{-2}$s$^{-1}$ for $\sigma\varphi/\Lambda = 10$

## Context
This work extends the prior proposal by Tkalya (2011, 2012) for a gamma-ray laser using the $^{229}$Th ground-state doublet, which required Boltzmann distribution of nuclear sublevels at 0.01–0.1 K via spin relaxation through metallic coatings (with relaxation times of tens to hundreds of days). The new optical pumping method avoids these low temperatures and slow relaxation, enabling use of crystals with isovalent substitution (e.g., $^{229}$Th:SiO$_2$) where EFG may be smaller. The approach also addresses the problem of charge compensation in non-isovalent substitution crystals like $^{229}$Th:LiCaAlF$_6$, which can cause additional line broadening. The results enable experimental progress toward a VUV laser at the 7.6 eV nuclear transition in $^{229}$Th, with applications including tests of exponential decay law, nuclear superfluorescence, enhanced sensitivity to temporal variation of fundamental constants, and nuclear frequency standards with accuracy at $1 \times 10^{-19}$ (Campbell 2012).
