# Heavy quark production in deep inelastic electron-nucleus scattering

> Heavy quark production has been very well studied over the last years both theoretically and experimentally. Theory has been used to study heavy quark production in $e p$ collisions at HERA, in $pp$ collisions at Tevatron and at RHIC, in $pA$ and $dA$ collisions at RHIC and in $AA$ collisions at CERN-SPS and at RHIC. However, to the best of our knowledge, heavy quark production in $eA$ has received almost no attention. With the possible construction of a high energy electron-ion collider, updated estimates of heavy quark production are needed. We address the subject from the perspective of saturation physics and compute the heavy quark production cross section with the dipole model. We isolate shadowing and non-linear effects, showing their impact on the charm structure function and on the transverse momentum spectrum.

## Key claim

Heavy quark production in electron-nucleus collisions, which has received almost no attention compared to other collision systems, is predicted to show saturation effects that can be observed in the charm structure function and transverse momentum spectra, with the difference between linear and non-linear predictions reaching a factor of up to 4 depending on energy and kinematics.

## Method

The authors compute the heavy quark production cross section in deep inelastic electron-nucleus scattering using the color dipole model, where the dipole-nucleus cross section is obtained from the Glauber-Gribov formalism with the b-CGC model for the dipole-proton cross section fitted to HERA data. They compare these saturation-model predictions (bCGC) with linear QCD predictions using the GRV98 gluon distribution (CT) and the EKS98 nuclear gluon distribution with shadowing (CT+Shad). The charm and bottom quark masses are taken as 1.5 GeV and 4.5 GeV respectively, and the Peterson fragmentation function is used to compute D-meson spectra.

## Key numbers

- The difference between bCGC and linear CT predictions for the charm structure function \(F_2^{c}(x,Q^2)/A\) can reach a factor up to 4
- The ratio of linear (CT+Shad) to bCGC predictions for the charm transverse momentum spectrum goes from 1.5 at \(W=100\) GeV to 4 at \(W=1400\) GeV, for \(Q^2 = 2\) GeV\(^2\)
- The b-CGC model parameters used: \(\gamma_s = 0.46\), \(B_{CGC} = 7.5\) GeV\(^{-2}\), \(\mathcal{N}_0 = 0.558\), \(x_0 = 1.84 \times 10^{-6}\), \(\lambda = 0.119\)
- Charm quark mass \(m_c = 1.5\) GeV, bottom quark mass \(m_b = 4.5\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the dipole model calculations of Floter et al. (2008) from electron-proton to electron-ion collisions, continuing the authors' previous studies of nuclear structure functions and diffractive observables at a future Electron-Ion Collider (Goncalves et al., 2007-2009). The results contradict earlier estimates by other groups (e.g., Peters et al., 2006) which found weaker non-linear effects, and suggest that heavy quark production in eA collisions is a promising signature of saturation physics that could be tested at a future EIC.
