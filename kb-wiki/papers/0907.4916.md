# Overview of Issues Surrounding Strangeness in the Nucleon

> The calculation of the strangeness content of the nucleon and its experimental verification is a fundamental step in establishing non-perturbative QCD as the correct theory describing the structure of hadrons. It holds a role in QCD analogous to the correct calculation of the Lamb shift in QED. We review the latest developments in the vector and scalar matrix elements of the strange quarks in the proton, where there has recently been considerable progress.

## Key claim

The strange quark contributions to nucleon properties (vector and scalar matrix elements) have been determined with significantly higher precision from theory than from experiment, and the strange sigma commutator \(\sigma_s\) is an order of magnitude smaller than the classic belief of about one third of the nucleon mass, with profound implications for dark matter searches.

## Method

The paper reviews parity-violating electron scattering experiments at MIT-Bates, Mainz, and JLab to extract the strange vector form factors, and compares them to lattice QCD calculations using both indirect methods (Leinweber et al., 2004) and direct numerical methods for disconnected quark loops (Deka et al., 2008; Doi et al., 2009). For the scalar form factors, the author describes a lattice QCD analysis of nucleon octet masses within full 2+1 flavor QCD, using a mass formula with SU(3) expansion to first order in quark masses plus one-loop chiral corrections with finite range regularization, from which sigma terms are extracted via the Feynman-Hellman theorem (Young and Thomas, 2009).

## Key numbers

- Strangeness magnetic moment from indirect lattice QCD: \(-0.046 \pm 0.019 \, \mu_N\) (Leinweber et al., 2004)
- Direct lattice QCD result for strangeness magnetic form factor at zero momentum transfer: \(G_M^s(Q^2=0) = -0.017 \pm 0.025 \pm 0.07 \, \mu_N\) (Doi et al., 2009)
- Best experimental determination of strange magnetic form factor at \(Q^2 = 0.1\) GeV\(^2\) from global analysis: \(-0.01 \pm 0.25 \, \mu_N\) (Young et al., 2007)
- A4 Collaboration measurement at Mainz at \(Q^2 = 0.22\) GeV\(^2\): \(-0.14 \pm 0.11 \pm 0.11 \, \mu_N\) (Baunack et al., 2009)
- Indirect method result at \(Q^2 = 0.22\) GeV\(^2\): \(G_M^s = -0.034 \pm 0.031 \, \mu_N\) (Wang et al., 2009)
- Momentum fraction carried by strange and anti-strange quarks from direct lattice QCD: \(\langle x(s + \bar{s}) \rangle = 0.027 \pm 0.006\) (Deka et al., 2008)
- Pion-nucleon sigma commutator from lattice QCD analysis: \(\sigma_{\pi N} = 47 \pm 10\) MeV (Young and Thomas, 2009)
- Strange sigma commutator from lattice QCD analysis: \(\sigma_s = 31 \pm 16\) MeV (Young and Thomas, 2009)
- Direct lattice QCD calculation of \(\sigma_s\): \(59 \pm 11\) MeV (Toussaint and Freeman, 2009)

## Context

This review extends the long-standing effort to measure strange quark contributions to nucleon structure, which began with parity-violating electron scattering experiments at MIT-Bates, Mainz, and JLab (Armstrong et al., 2005; Aniol et al., 2005; Maas et al., 2004; Hasty et al., 2001). The theoretical calculations now exceed experimental precision by an order of magnitude, reversing the usual situation in strong interaction physics, and the newly determined small value of \(\sigma_s\) (Young and Thomas, 2009) directly impacts the interpretation of dark matter searches within minimal supersymmetric extensions of the Standard Model (Ellis et al., 2008). The work enables future experimental efforts to improve parity-violating electron scattering measurements and provides a crucial test of non-perturbative QCD analogous to the Lamb shift in QED.
