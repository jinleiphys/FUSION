# Jet Quenching at RHIC and LHC and the Fragmentation Function in Vacuum

> The value of the parton to hadron fragmentation function in QCD in vacuum (for example from proton-proton collisions at high energy colliders) is directly/indirectly used in the literature to study the jet quenching and the hadron production from quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC. In this paper we show that this is not possible because, unlike the perturbative propagator in non-equilibrium QCD, the parton to hadron fragmentation function is a non-perturbative quantity in QCD and hence it is not possible to decompose the fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD into the vacuum part and the medium part.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the parton-to-hadron fragmentation function measured in vacuum (e.g., from proton-proton collisions) cannot be used, even indirectly, to study jet quenching or hadron production from quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC, because the fragmentation function is a non-perturbative QCD quantity and therefore cannot be decomposed into a vacuum part and a medium part, unlike the perturbative propagator in non-equilibrium QCD.

## Method

The author uses the closed-time path integral formalism of non-equilibrium QCD to derive gauge-invariant definitions of the quark and gluon fragmentation functions in a non-equilibrium medium, and proves the factorization of infrared divergences at all orders in the coupling constant using the background field method with SU(3) pure gauge background fields. The proof shows that the non-equilibrium fragmentation function must be defined with respect to the non-equilibrium ground state |in>, not the vacuum state |0>, and that the vacuum fragmentation function cannot be separated from a medium contribution.

## Key numbers

- RHIC Au-Au collisions at center-of-mass energy √s = 200 GeV
- LHC Pb-Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy √s = 5.5 TeV (stated for nuclei) and √s = 2.76 TeV (stated for Pb-Pb jet quenching observations)
- Quark-gluon plasma phase transition temperature ≥ 200 MeV (10^12 K)
- Hadronization time scale in QCD is approximately 10^(-24) seconds

## Context

This work extends the author's prior development of factorization theorems in non-equilibrium QCD (Nayak, several papers from 2004-2017) and directly contradicts the common practice of using vacuum fragmentation functions in medium-modified DGLAP equations, as done by Guo and Wang (2000) and others. The paper argues that lattice QCD at finite temperature is inapplicable because the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC may be in non-equilibrium, and that hydrodynamics cannot correctly implement hadronization from first principles. The result implies that a fully consistent treatment of jet quenching requires measuring or computing the fragmentation function in non-equilibrium QCD, which has not been done experimentally.
