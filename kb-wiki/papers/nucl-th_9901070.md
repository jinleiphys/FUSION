# Pair correlations in high-K bands

> The tilted axis cranking model is used in combination with the random phase approximation and particle number projection to analyze the influence of dynamical pair correlations in the high-K bands of 178-W and their effect on relative energy and angular momentum. The calculations show the importance of dynamical pair correlations to describe the experiment as well as advantages and problems with the different models in the superfluid and normal state regions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including dynamical pair correlations (beyond the static mean-field pairing) is essential for reproducing the experimental energies of high-K bands in 178W, but the success depends on the method used: the random phase approximation works well when the static pair gap has collapsed, while particle number projection is more stable in the transitional region where the static gap is small but nonzero.

## Method

The tilted axis cranking (TAC) model was combined with either the random phase approximation (RPA) for pairing vibrations or particle number projection (PNP) to include dynamical pair correlations in multi-quasiparticle high-K bands of 178W. The HFB mean field was solved with a monopole pairing interaction, using fixed deformations ε2=0.229 and ε4=0.034 (except ε2=0.255, ε4=0.038 for the Kπ=25+ band), and the pairing strengths were fixed to Gν=0.093 MeV and Gπ=0.121 MeV to match even-odd mass differences. The RPA correlation energy was calculated using a complex integration method, and the PNP energy was minimized with respect to the gap parameter Δ while keeping the chemical potential fixed.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strengths: Gν = 0.093 MeV for neutrons, Gπ = 0.121 MeV for protons, fixed to match even-odd mass differences giving Δν = 1.15 MeV and Δπ = 1.23 MeV
- Ground state deformations: ε2 = 0.229, ε4 = 0.034 for all bands except Kπ=25+ which uses ε2 = 0.255, ε4 = 0.038
- For the Kπ=25+ and 30+ bands, ΔHFB = 0 while ΔPNP is about 30% smaller than ground state values
- For Kπ=7- and 15+ bands, the neutron HFB gap ΔHFBν becomes zero at ω ≈ 0.3 MeV
- The tilt angle ϑ increases monotonically from 0° at the band head to near 90° at highest frequency, with deviations of 0°-10° between the J⊥=0 condition and the energy minimum in PNP calculations

## Context

This work extends earlier TAC calculations on 178,179W by Frauendorf and collaborators (1999) that only considered static pair fields, and addresses problems noted in studies of high-K band heads by Jain and collaborators (1995). The paper compares two methods for including dynamical pair correlations: RPA, which works well when the static gap has collapsed (ΔHFB=0), and PNP, which is more stable in the transitional region near the superfluid-to-normal phase transition. The results enable improved description of high-K isomers and their rotational bands, though the authors note that the cranking model remains unreliable near band crossings and that the quasiboson approximation in RPA can break down for certain phonon modes.
