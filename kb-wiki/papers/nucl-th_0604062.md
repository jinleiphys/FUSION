# From Dimensional to Cut-Off Regularization

> We extent the standard approach of dimensional regularization of Feynman diagrams: we replace the transition to lower dimensions by a 'natural' cut-off regulator. Introducing an external regulator of mass Lambda^(2e), we regain in the limit e -> 0 and e > 0 the results of dimensional and cut-off regularization, respectively. We demonstrate the versatility and adequacy of the different regularization schemes for practical examples (such as non covariant regularization, the axial anomaly or regularization in effective field theories).

# From Dimensional to Cut-Off Regularization

## Key claim
The paper extends dimensional regularization (DR) by replacing the transition to lower dimensions with a "natural" cut-off regulator, introducing an external regulator of mass $\lambda^{2\epsilon}$ such that in the limit $\epsilon \rightarrow 0$ one recovers DR results and for $\epsilon > 0$ one recovers cut-off regularization (CR) results, thereby unifying both schemes while preserving the technical simplicity of DR.

## Method
The author starts from the standard DR expression for a Feynman integral in $d-2\epsilon$ dimensions and formally transforms it back to $d$-dimensional space via a change of variable $k^2/m^2 = x^{2+\epsilon}$. A regulating factor $(\lambda^2/(k^2+M^2))^\epsilon$ is then introduced, where the scales $\lambda$ and $M$ are identified with external scale parameters. By choosing $\lambda^2 = \mu^2$, $M^2 = m^2$ with $\epsilon \rightarrow 0$, the method reproduces DR; by choosing $\lambda^2 = M^2 = \Lambda^2$ with $\epsilon > 0$, it reproduces CR. The approach is demonstrated on one-loop integrals, multi-loop integrals, non-covariant regularization, the axial anomaly, and effective field theories.

## Key numbers
- For the "setting sun" multi-loop diagram in 4 dimensions, the regularized result contains a factor $\Gamma(\epsilon)$ from which the double singularity is extracted.
- For the integral $I(m^2,M^2) = \int d^4k \, 1/(k^2+m^2) \cdot 1/(k^2+M^2)$, NR yields $\pi^2 \, \Gamma(-1+\epsilon)/\Gamma(1+\epsilon) \cdot 1/(M^2-m^2) \left(m^2 \ln(m^2/\mu^2) - M^2 \ln(M^2/\mu^2)\right)$.
- For a 3-dimensional example $J(\lambda,M,\epsilon) = \int d^3k \, k^2/(k^2+m^2) \cdot (\lambda^2/(k^2+M^2))^\epsilon$, the result is $\pi^{3/2} (3/2) \, \Gamma(-3/2+\epsilon)/(\epsilon \, \Gamma(\epsilon)) \, (\lambda^2/m^2)^\epsilon \, m^3 \, F(\epsilon-3/2,\epsilon,\epsilon+1, -(M^2-m^2)/m^2)$.
- For a sharp momentum cut-off $\Lambda$, the integral $\int_0^\Lambda d^4k (k^2)^n$ yields $\pi^2 \, (\Lambda^2)^{n+2}/(n+2)$.
- In NR, the same integral regularized with regulator mass $\mu$ gives $\pi^2 \, \Gamma(n+2)/\Gamma(2) \cdot \Gamma(\epsilon-(n+2))/\Gamma(\epsilon) \, (\mu^2)^{n+2}$, which agrees with CR upon identifying $\Lambda = \mu$ for $\epsilon = n+3$.
- Comparing DR and CR for a quadratic divergence yields $\Lambda^2 = - (2/\epsilon) (\mu^2/m^2)^\epsilon \, m^2$ for $\Lambda \rightarrow \infty$ and $\epsilon \rightarrow 0$.

## Context
This work extends the standard dimensional regularization formalism of 't Hooft (1972), Bollini and Giambiagi (1972), and Ashmore (1972) by providing a direct link to cut-off regularization, which is frequently favored in phenomenological effective field theories. The method addresses known shortcomings of DR in contexts such as the axial anomaly (Adler 1969, Jackiw 1972), non-covariant regularization (Leibbrandt 1975, Holstein 1974), and effective field theories like chiral perturbation theory (Young 2002, Meissner 2005). The approach enables a transparent unification of DR and CR, allowing the introduction of external mass scales via conventional form factors characteristic of phenomenological low-energy nuclear physics, and provides a consistent regularization for the axial anomaly without requiring formal extensions of $\gamma_5$ to dimensions greater than 4.
