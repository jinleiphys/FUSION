# Double $π^0$ Photoproduction off the Proton at Threshold

> The reaction $γp \to π^0 π^0 p$ has been measured using the TAPS BaF$_2$ calorimeter at the tagged photon facility of the Mainz Microtron accelerator. Chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) predicts that close to threshold this channel is significantly enhanced compared to double pion final states with charged pions. In contrast to other reaction channels, the lower order tree terms are strongly suppressed in 2$π^0$ photoproduction. The consequence is the dominance of pion loops in the 2$π^0$ channel close to threshold - a result that opens new prospects for the test of ChPT and in particular its inherent loop terms. The present measurement is the first which is sensitive enough for a conclusive comparison with the ChPT calculation and is in agreement with its prediction. The data also show good agreement with a calculation in the unitary chiral approach.

## Key claim

The measurement confirms that the total cross section for the reaction γp → π⁰π⁰p near threshold is dominated by pion loop contributions, as predicted by chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), but the data are not precise enough to discriminate between the ChPT prediction and the unitary chiral approach of Roca et al. (2003).

## Method

The reaction was measured at the Mainz Microtron (MAMI) using the Glasgow tagged photon facility and the TAPS BaF₂ calorimeter, covering photon energies from 285 to 820 MeV with an average energy resolution of 2 MeV. The two π⁰ mesons were detected via their two-photon decay channels, providing kinematical overdetermination for unambiguous identification, while the proton was not detected. The cross section was extracted from the rate of 2π⁰ events, corrected for detector acceptance and efficiency using GEANT3 simulations, and normalized absolutely using the photon beam flux determined from tagged electron counting and a BGO detector.

## Key numbers

- The total cross section near threshold follows the ChPT prediction σ_tot(Eγ) = 0.6 nb × ((Eγ − 308.8 MeV) / 10 MeV)², where 308.8 MeV is the production threshold.
- The upper limit for the cross section, obtained by varying the Roper coupling to the s-wave ππ channel, corresponds to increasing the constant in the formula from 0.6 nb to 0.9 nb.
- The photon flux was approximately 0.5 MHz MeV⁻¹ at photon energies of 300 MeV.
- The average detection efficiency was 1.0%, with systematic errors estimated at 6%.
- The TAPS detector covered approximately 40% of the full solid angle.

## Context

This work extends earlier measurements by Haerter et al. and Wolf et al., which suffered from large statistical uncertainties in the threshold region, and provides the first data set sensitive enough for conclusive comparison with the ChPT calculation of Bernard et al. (1997). The results enable future constraints on the Roper resonance coupling to the s-wave ππ channel and provide a test bed for distinguishing between ChPT and the unitary chiral model of Roca et al. (2003), which dynamically generates pion loops but predicts a significantly smaller cross section near threshold.
