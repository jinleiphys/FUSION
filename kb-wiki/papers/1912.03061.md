# Rearrangement term in the nonlocal folding model of the nucleon optical potential

> Based on the mean-field determination of the single-particle energy in nuclear matter that contains naturally a rearrangement term (RT) implied by the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, the folding model of the nucleon optical potential (OP) is extended to take into account the RT using the effective, density dependent CDM3Yn interaction. With the exchange part of the nucleon folded OP treated exactly in the Hartree-Fock manner, a compact nonlocal version of the folding model is suggested in the present work to determine explicitly the isospin-dependent, nonlocal central term of the nucleon OP. To solve the optical model (OM) equation with a complex nonlocal OP, the calculable $R$-matrix method is used to analyse the elastic neutron and proton scattering on $^{40,48}$Ca, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{208}$Pb targets at low energies. The inclusion of the RT into the folding model calculation of the nonlocal nucleon OP was shown to be essential for the overall good OM description of elastic nucleon scattering. To validate the nonlocal version of the folding model, the OM results given by the nonlocal folded nucleon OP are also compared with those given by the global parametrization of the nonlocal OP using the analytical nonlocal form factor suggested by Perey and Buck.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the rearrangement term (RT) in the nonlocal folding model of the nucleon optical potential is essential for a good optical model description of elastic nucleon scattering at low energies (up to 45 MeV), but the nonlocal folded OP with the RT included gives a noticeably better description of backward-angle data than the local folded OP with the RT, especially for proton scattering on 40Ca at 45 MeV.

## Method

The authors extended the folding model of the nucleon optical potential by incorporating the rearrangement term using the density-dependent CDM3Yn effective interaction, with the density dependence modified to reproduce the single-particle potential obtained from the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem in nuclear matter. The exchange part of the folded nucleon OP was treated exactly in the Hartree-Fock manner, yielding a nonlocal central term, and the resulting integro-differential optical model equation was solved using the calculable R-matrix method with Lagrange mesh and Gauss-Legendre quadrature integration. Elastic neutron and proton scattering on 40,48Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb targets were analyzed at energies up to 45 MeV, and the results were compared with those from the global parametrization of the nonlocal OP using the Perey-Buck nonlocal form factor and its recent energy-dependent versions.

## Key numbers

* The nuclear matter saturation density used is approximately 0.16 fm^-3, with nuclear incompressibility K around 230 MeV.
* Elastic neutron scattering on 208Pb was analyzed at incident energies of 26, 30.4, and 40 MeV.
* Elastic neutron scattering on 40,48Ca and 90Zr was analyzed at 17 and 24 MeV.
* Elastic proton scattering on 208Pb was analyzed at 30.4, 35, and 45 MeV.
* Elastic proton scattering on 40Ca was analyzed at 30, 35, and 45 MeV.
* An overall renormalization factor of approximately 0.8 was needed for the imaginary neutron folded potential to achieve good OM description of elastic neutron scattering data.
* The energy range of the study was E ≤ 45 MeV for the folding model analysis.

## Context

This work extends the earlier local folding model study by Loan et al. (2015) that first showed the strong impact of the rearrangement term on the strength and shape of the folded nucleon OP, but used a local approximation for the exchange term. The present paper treats the exchange kernel exactly, producing a nonlocal folded OP, and demonstrates that the RT is essential for good OM description across the whole angular range, with the nonlocal version outperforming the local one at backward angles. The results are compared with the global nonlocal parametrizations of Perey and Buck (1962) and the recent energy-dependent versions PBE and TPME by Lovel et al., and the nonlocal folding model can be used in future studies of charge-exchange (p,n) reactions to isobar analog states.
