# Collective neutrino oscillations and heavy-element nucleosynthesis in supernovae: exploring potential effects of many-body neutrino correlations

> In high-energy astrophysical processes involving compact objects, such as core-collapse supernovae or binary neutron star mergers, neutrinos play an important role in the synthesis of nuclides. Neutrinos in these environments can experience collective flavor oscillations driven by neutrino-neutrino interactions, including coherent forward scattering and incoherent (collisional) effects. Recently, there has been interest in exploring potential novel behaviors in collective oscillations of neutrinos by going beyond the one-particle effective or "mean-field" treatments. Here, we seek to explore implications of collective neutrino oscillations, in the mean-field treatment and beyond, for the nucleosynthesis yields in supernova environments with different astrophysical conditions and neutrino inputs. We find that collective oscillations can impact the operation of the $νp$-process and $r$-process nucleosynthesis in supernovae. The potential impact is particularly strong in high-entropy, proton-rich conditions, where we find that neutrino interactions can nudge an initial $νp$ process neutron rich, resulting in a unique combination of proton-rich low-mass nuclei as well as neutron-rich high-mass nuclei. We describe this neutrino-induced neutron capture process as the "$ν$i process". In addition, nontrivial quantum correlations among neutrinos, if present significantly, could lead to different nuclide yields compared to the corresponding mean-field oscillation treatments, by virtue of modifying the evolution of the relevant one-body neutrino observables.

## Key claim

Collective neutrino oscillations, particularly beyond-mean-field many-body correlations, can significantly alter nucleosynthesis yields in supernova environments by modifying the electron fraction, potentially nudging an initial νp-process toward neutron-rich conditions and producing a unique combination of proton-rich low-mass and neutron-rich high-mass nuclei, a process the authors term the "νi process." The authors emphasize that their calculations use a simplified toy model and are intended as an illustration of potential effects rather than a precise representation of supernova neutrino streams.

## Method

The authors performed many-body quantum calculations of neutrino flavor evolution for small ensembles (N=4 and N=8 neutrinos) using the full Schrödinger equation with a Hamiltonian including vacuum oscillations and neutrino-neutrino interactions, and compared these to mean-field calculations using the standard polarization vector evolution equations. The resulting neutrino capture rates on nucleons (νe + n → p + e− and ν̄e + p → n + e+) were then used as inputs to the nuclear reaction network code PRISM to compute nucleosynthetic yields for supernova neutrino-driven wind trajectories with different initial neutrino energy distributions and electron fractions.

## Key numbers

- Reference vacuum oscillation frequency ω0 = 1.22×10−16 MeV, based on mass-squared splitting δm2 = 2.44×10−3 eV2 and reference energy E0 = 10 MeV
- Neutrino mixing angle sin2(2θ) = 0.10
- Protoneutron star radius Rν = 15 km
- Neutrino-neutrino coupling at emission surface μ(Rν) = 6.954 × 105 ω0
- Initial electron fractions Ye range from 0.634 (symmetric case) down to 0.366 (asymmetric case with ν̄e energy 16 MeV)
- Neutrino energies used: 10–20 MeV for νe and νx species; ν̄e energies from 10–16 MeV depending on asymmetry
- Luminosities: Lν,e = 9.091 × 1051 erg/s, Lν,x = 1.818 × 1052 erg/s for N=4 cases; Lν̄,e varies from 9.091 × 1051 to 1.455 × 1052 erg/s
- Flavor evolution calculations initiated at ri ≈ 100 km where μ(ri) = 100 ω0 (N=4) or 50 ω0 (N=8)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective neutrino oscillations in supernovae (Duan 2006, Balantekin 2006, Pehlivan 2011) and their impact on nucleosynthesis (Frohlich 2006, Pruet 2006, Wanajo 2006b) by exploring beyond-mean-field many-body correlations. The authors build on recent theoretical work quantifying many-body effects in simplified neutrino ensembles (Cervia 2019, Patwardhan 2021, Roggero 2021) and connect these to observable nucleosynthetic signatures. The results enable future studies to assess whether beyond-mean-field neutrino correlations could leave distinct imprints on the elemental abundance patterns from supernova ejecta, though the authors note that more realistic treatments including finite wave-packet effects and three-flavor oscillations (Shalgar 2023, Johns 2023) are needed before drawing firm conclusions.
