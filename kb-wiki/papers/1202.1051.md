# Temperature dependence of symmetry energy of finite nuclei

> The temperature dependence of the symmetry energy and the symmetry free energy coefficients of atomic nuclei is investigated in a finite temperature Thomas-Fermi framework employing the subtraction procedure. A substantial decrement in the symmetry energy coefficient is obtained for finite systems,contrary to those seen for infinite nuclear matter at normal and somewhat subnormal densities. The effect of the coupling of the surface phonons to the nucleonic motion is also considered; this is found to decrease the symmetry energies somewhat at low temperatures.

# Temperature dependence of symmetry energy of finite nuclei

## Key claim

The symmetry energy coefficient of finite nuclei decreases substantially with temperature, contrary to the behavior seen in infinite nuclear matter at normal and subnormal densities; however, this decrease is sensitive to the definition used (difference method vs. local density approximation) and to the choice of nuclear pairs, and can even become negative at high temperatures due to Coulomb polarization effects.

## Method

The authors performed finite-temperature Thomas-Fermi (FTTF) calculations using the subtraction procedure to separate the hot nucleus from the surrounding gas phase, employing two effective interactions: the modified Seyler-Blanchard (SBM) interaction and the SkM* interaction. The coupling of surface phonons to nucleonic motion was included through a phenomenological parametric form of the nucleon omega-mass, and symmetry coefficients were computed both via the difference method using isobaric nuclear pairs and via the local density approximation (LDA).

## Key numbers

- The SBM interaction parameters yield a volume symmetry coefficient of 31 MeV at saturation density, with isoscalar volume incompressibility K_infinity = 240 MeV, symmetry incompressibility K_sym = -101 MeV, and symmetry pressure L = 59.8 MeV.
- The SkM* interaction gives K_infinity = 216.7 MeV, K_sym = -155.9 MeV, and L = 45.8 MeV.
- The effective k-mass of the nucleon for symmetric matter is 0.62 m, where m is the nucleon mass.
- The phenomenological omega-mass is given by m_omega/m = 1 - 0.4 A^(1/3) exp[-(T/(21 A^(-1/3)))^2] * (1/rho(0)) * (d rho(r)/dr).
- For infinite nuclear matter at density rho_0/8, e_sym decreases with temperature while f_sym increases, with the kinetic component e_sym(k) falling faster than the potential component e_sym(v).
- For finite nuclei, e_sym is nearly constant up to approximately 2 MeV, then falls substantially with temperature in the difference method; in the LDA, the temperature dependence is much weaker.

## Context

This work extends earlier finite-temperature calculations by Donati et al. (low temperature domain T less than or equal to 2 MeV) and Dean et al. (shell model Monte Carlo framework) to a broader temperature range up to 8 MeV, and contrasts with results for infinite nuclear matter reported by Xu et al. and Moustakidis. The findings enable improved modeling of stellar core-collapse and bounce phases where matter is nucleated rather than homogeneous, and may inform future finite-temperature Hartree-Fock calculations to resolve ambiguities in defining symmetry coefficients for finite nuclei.
