# Softening of the nuclear equation-of-state by kinetic non-equilibrium in heavy ion collision

> Highly compressed nuclear matter created in relativistic heavy collisions is to large extent governed by local non-equilibrium. As an idealised scenario colliding nuclear matter configurations are studied with an effective in-medium interaction based on the microscopic DBHF model. It is found that on top of the repulsive momentum dependence of the nuclear forces kinetic non-equilibrium leads to an effective softening of the equation of state as compared to ground state nuclear matter. The separation of phase space which is the basic feature of such anisotropic configurations has thereby a similar influence as the introduction of a virtual new degree of freedom.

## Key claim

Kinetic non-equilibrium in colliding nuclear matter, caused by the separation of phase space into two Fermi ellipsoids, leads to an effective softening of the equation of state compared to ground state nuclear matter, even on top of the repulsive momentum dependence of nuclear forces. This softening is a leading-order effect that should be accounted for when extracting the equilibrium EOS from heavy ion collision data.

## Method

The authors studied idealized colliding nuclear matter (CNM) configurations, representing the local phase space in the participant zone of relativistic heavy ion collisions, using an effective in-medium interaction based on the microscopic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) model. They computed the energy-momentum tensor and binding energy per particle for symmetric CNM (equal densities in the two currents) at various streaming velocities, subtracting the kinetic energy of relative motion to obtain an effective EOS. The results were compared to a simplified density-dependent relativistic mean field (RMF) approximation to isolate the effect of phase space separation.

## Key numbers

- Saturation properties of the DBHF model (Bonn A potential): saturation density $\rho_{\rm sat}= 0.185~{\rm fm}^{-3}$, binding energy $E^{\rm bind} = -16.15$ MeV, compression modulus $K= 230$ MeV.
- Streaming velocities studied: $u= |{\bf u}_i| =$ 0.2, 0.4, 0.6, 0.8 (in units of $c$), corresponding to incident laboratory energies $E_{\rm lab}$=0.08, 0.36, 1.05, 3.34 GeV/nucleon.
- The quadrupole moment $Q_{zz}$ of the energy-momentum tensor reaches an asymptotic value of 2 at 6.0 A.GeV beam energy, indicating maximal anisotropy where the Fermi momentum is negligible compared to the beam velocity.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of the nuclear EOS using transport models like BUU (Cassing, 1999) and QMD (Aichelin, 1991), which typically use phenomenological mean fields parameterized for locally equilibrated nuclear matter. The authors show that the standard approach, which decouples the local density-dependent part of the mean field from phase space anisotropy, misses a significant softening effect. The paper enables a more accurate interpretation of heavy ion collision data, such as recent precision measurements of $K^+$ production at SIS energies (Sturm, 2000; Fuchs, 2000) that support a soft EOS, by clarifying that the observed softness may partly arise from non-equilibrium rather than the equilibrium EOS itself.
