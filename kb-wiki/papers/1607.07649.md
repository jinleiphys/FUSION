# Siegert State Approach to Quantum Defect Theory

> The Siegert states are approached in framework of Bloch-Lane-Robson formalism for quantum collisions. The Siegert state is not described by a pole of Wigner R- matrix but rather by the equation $1- R_{nn}L_n = 0$, relating R- matrix element $R_{nn}$ to decay channel logarithmic derivative $L_n$. Extension of Siegert state equation to multichannel system results into replacement of channel R- matrix element $R_{nn}$ by its reduced counterpart ${\cal R}_{nn}$. One proves the Siegert state is a pole, $(1 - {\cal R}_{nn} L_{n})^{-1}$, of multichannel collision matrix. The Siegert equation $1 - {\cal R}_{nn} L_{n} = 0$, ($n$ - Rydberg channel), implies basic results of Quantum Defect Theory as Seaton's theorem, complex quantum defect, channel resonances and threshold continuity of averaged multichannel collision matrix elements.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Siegert state, defined by an outgoing-wave boundary condition, is not described by a pole of the Wigner R-matrix but rather by the equation \(1 - R_{nn}L_n = 0\) relating the R-matrix element to the channel logarithmic derivative; this equation, when extended to multichannel systems using the reduced R-matrix element \(\mathcal{R}_{nn}\), yields the basic results of Quantum Defect Theory (QDT), including Seaton's theorem, complex quantum defects, channel resonances, and threshold continuity of averaged collision matrix elements.

## Method

The authors develop the Siegert state within the Bloch-Lane-Robson formalism for quantum collisions, using the Bloch outgoing-wave boundary condition operator \(\mathcal{L}\) to define a purely outgoing eigenstate. They rewrite the Schroedinger equation for the Siegert state in channel space in terms of the \(\mathcal{R}\)-matrix (Green operator) and the R-matrix, deriving the implicit equation \(1 - \mathcal{R}_{nn}L_n = 0\) for the complex energy pole. This formalism is then applied to electron scattering on atoms, where the logarithmic derivative of a Coulombic Rydberg channel is specified below threshold (\(L_e^< = -\cot\pi\nu\)) and above threshold (\(L_e^> = i\)), and the resulting equations are shown to reproduce the standard MQDT relations for the collision matrix.

## Key numbers

* The logarithmic derivative for an attractive Coulomb channel below threshold is given by \(L_e^< = -\cot\pi\sqrt{e_1^2 e_2^2 m / 2\hbar^2 (E_\pi - E)}\); for pure Coulomb states the level equation \((L_e^<)^{-1}=0\) yields eigenenergies \(E_\pi - E_n = (1/n^2) e_1^2 e_2^2 m / 2\hbar^2\) with integer principal quantum number \(n\).
* The logarithmic derivative for an attractive Coulomb channel above threshold is \(L_e^> = i\); in the zero-energy limit the channel penetration factor is \(P_e = 1\) and the shift factor is \(S_e = 0\).
* For \(s\)-wave scattering on neutral fields, \(L_{n>} = iP_n = i\rho\), \(L_{n<} = S_{n<} = -\rho\), \(\Delta S_n = \rho\) (with \(\rho = k_n r\)), giving \(\tau = P_n / \Delta S_n = 1\) and \(\arctan\tau = \pi/4\).
* The energy average of the effective term below threshold yields \(\overline{(L_{e<}^{-1} - \mathcal{R}_{ee})^{-1}} = (1/i - \mathcal{R}_{ee})^{-1} = (1/L_e^> - \mathcal{R}_{ee})^{-1}\), establishing continuity of the averaged collision matrix across threshold: \(\overline{W_N^<} = W_N^>\).

## Context

This work extends the Siegert state concept, originally introduced by Gamow and Siegert (1939) for radioactive decay and resonant states, into the Bloch-Lane-Robson formalism (Lane and Robson 1966, Robson and Lane 1967) to provide a unified description of bound and resonant states in multichannel scattering. It reproduces key results of Multichannel Quantum Defect Theory (MQDT) as developed by Seaton (1983), Burke (2011), and Lane (1986), including Seaton's theorem relating the quantum defect below threshold to the scattering phase shift above threshold, and the threshold continuity theorem (an alternative to Gailitis' theorem). The approach enables a direct derivation of MQDT from the Siegert state equation, distinguishing "channel resonances" (from the Siegert pole) from "inner resonances" (from R-matrix poles), and generalizes the Wigner-Breit-Baz threshold cusp theory (Baz, Zeldovich and Perelomov 1971) by relating threshold effects to reaction dynamics.
