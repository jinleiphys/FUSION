# Neutron-proton bremsstrahlung from intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions as a probe of the nuclear symmetry energy?

> Hard photons from neutron-proton bremsstrahlung in intermediate energy heavy-ion reactions are examined as a potential probe of the nuclear symmetry energy within a transport model. Effects of the symmetry energy on the yields and spectra of hard photons are found to be generally smaller than those due to the currently existing uncertainties of both the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and the photon production probability in the elementary process $pn\to pnγ$. Very interestingly, nevertheless, the ratio of hard photon spectra $R_{1/2}(γ)$ from two reactions using isotopes of the same element is not only approximately independent of these uncertainties but also quite sensitive to the symmetry energy. For the head-on reactions of $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ and $^{112}Sn+^{112}Sn$ at $E_{beam}/A=50$ MeV, for example, the $R_{1/2}(γ)$ displays a rise up to 15% when the symmetry energy is reduced by about 20% at $ρ=1.3ρ_0$ which is the maximum density reached in these reactions.

## Key claim

Hard photon yields and spectra from individual heavy-ion reactions are too contaminated by uncertainties in the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections and the elementary \(pn\to pn\gamma\) probability to serve as a clean probe of the nuclear symmetry energy; however, the ratio of hard photon spectra \(R_{1/2}(\gamma)\) from two reactions using isotopes of the same element is approximately independent of those uncertainties and remains sensitive to the symmetry energy, especially for very energetic photons.

## Method

The authors used the IBUU04 transport model, which includes an isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section and a momentum- and isospin-dependent single nucleon potential (MDI) with a parameter \(x\) that varies the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy while keeping other properties of the equation of state fixed. Hard photon production was treated perturbatively by summing the photon production probability over each neutron-proton collision, using both a semi-classical formula (Eq. intc) and a quantum-mechanical one-boson-exchange model (Eq. QFT) for the elementary \(pn\to pn\gamma\) probability. Head-on collisions of \(^{132}\text{Sn}+^{124}\text{Sn}\) and \(^{112}\text{Sn}+^{112}\text{Sn}\) at \(E_{\text{beam}}/A = 50\) MeV were simulated, and the ratio of hard photon spectra \(R_{1/2}(\gamma)\) was computed for symmetry energies corresponding to \(x=1\) and \(x=-1\).

## Key numbers

- The maximum central density reached in head-on \(^{132}\text{Sn}+^{124}\text{Sn}\) reactions at 50 MeV/A is about \(1.3\rho_0\).
- At \(\rho = 1.3\rho_0\), the symmetry energy with \(x=-1\) is higher by about 20% compared to that with \(x=1\).
- The ratio \(R_{1/2}(\gamma)\) displays a rise up to 15% when the symmetry energy is reduced by about 20% at \(\rho = 1.3\rho_0\).
- The supranormal density phase lasts from about 10 to 35 fm/c, during which most hard photons with \(50\ \text{MeV} \leq \varepsilon_\gamma \leq 125\ \text{MeV}\) are produced.
- The inclusive photon production cross sections for \(^{12}\text{C}+^{12}\text{C}\) calculated with both \(p^a_\gamma\) and \(p^b_\gamma\) agree with experimental data to within about a factor of 2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of hard photon production in heavy-ion reactions (Bertsch 1988, Nifenecker 1990, Cassing 1990) and of the nuclear symmetry energy using hadronic probes (Tsang 2004, Chen 2005, Li 2005, Shetty 2007). It directly addresses a question raised by experimentalists (Wieleczko) about whether hard photons can probe the symmetry energy at supranormal densities. The proposed ratio \(R_{1/2}(\gamma)\) offers a probe that is free of final-state strong interactions, unlike hadronic probes such as the \(K^0/K^+\) ratio (Di Toro), and is more sensitive to the symmetry energy than the kaon ratio, which shows about a 15% change when the symmetry energy is changed by at least 50%. The results motivate experimental measurements of the hard-photon spectra ratio using neutron-rich and neutron-deficient tin isotopes.
