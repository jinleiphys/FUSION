# Signals of Disoriented Chiral Condensate

> If a disoriented chiral condensate is created over an extended space-time region following a rapid cooling in hadronic or nuclear collisions, the misalignment of the condensate with the electroweak symmetry breaking can generate observable effects in the processes which involve both strong and electromagnetic interactions. We point out the relevance of the dilepton decay of light vector mesons as a signal for formation of the disoriented condensate. We predict that the decay ρ^0 to dileptons will be suppressed and/or the ρresonance peak widens, while the decay ωto dileptons will not be affected by the condensate.

# Signals of Disoriented Chiral Condensate

## Key claim

If a disoriented chiral condensate (DCC) is created over an extended space-time region following rapid cooling in hadronic or nuclear collisions, the misalignment of the condensate with the electroweak symmetry breaking direction will suppress the dilepton decay of the rho meson (by a factor of two on average) and/or widen the rho resonance peak, while the dilepton decay of the omega meson will remain unaffected. This prediction depends on the assumption that the DCC space-time spread grows much larger than 1/m_pi and that the DCC is not in local thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors analyze the chiral transformation properties of the electromagnetic current in a DCC background, using a gauged sigma model with vector mesons treated as massive gauge bosons of SU(2)_L x SU(2)_R and U(1)_V. They compute the effective photon-meson couplings in the DCC by averaging over all possible chiral orientations of the condensate, and then estimate the space-time variation effects by parametrizing the DCC size and interior variation scale in terms of kappa (the DCC size in units of 1/m_pi).

## Key numbers

- The rho dilepton decay rate in the DCC is reduced by a factor of 1/2 relative to the normal vacuum: Gamma(rho(770 MeV) -> l+l-)_DCC = (1/2) Gamma(rho^0 -> l+l-)_normal
- The a_1(1260 MeV) dilepton decay rate in the DCC is predicted as Gamma(a_1(1260 MeV) -> l+l-)_DCC = (m_rho/m_a1)^3 Gamma(rho(770 MeV) -> l+l-)_DCC
- For a slowly varying DCC with kappa >= 5, the rho mass smearing is delta m_rho <= 25 MeV
- For a rapidly varying DCC with kappa ~ O(1), the rho mass smearing is delta m_rho^2 ~ 30 m_pi^2/kappa^2
- The rho production cross section in pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 27.5 GeV is sigma(rho^0) = (12.6 +/- 0.6) mb, and sigma(omega) = (12.8 +/- 0.8) mb
- At sqrt(s) = 5 GeV, sigma(rho^0)/sigma(omega) = 1.0 +/- 0.2; at sqrt(s) = 6.8 GeV, sigma(rho^0)/sigma(omega) = 1.07 +/- 0.2

## Context

This work extends the DCC proposal of Bjorken, Kowalski, and Taylor (1993) and the quenching mechanism of Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993) by identifying a new electromagnetic signal for DCC formation. It contrasts with quark-gluon plasma (QGP) signatures, noting that QGP affects rho and omega equally (except for lifetime differences), while the DCC affects them differently due to their distinct chiral properties. The prediction enables experimental searches for DCC in hadronic and heavy-ion collisions by comparing the dilepton spectra of rho and omega mesons, using the omega as a standard unaffected by the condensate.
