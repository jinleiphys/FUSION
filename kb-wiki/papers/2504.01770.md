# $\mathbf{γ^{(*)} + N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+ \to N(1520)\frac{3}{2}^{-}}$ helicity amplitudes and transition form factors

> We recently reported new results on the $γ^{(*)} + N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+ \to Δ(1700)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ transition form factors using a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector$\,\otimes\,$vector contact interaction (SCI) within a coupled formalism based on the Dyson-Schwinger, Bethe-Salpeter, and Faddeev equations. In this work, we extend our investigation to the $γ^{(*)} + N(940)\frac{1}{2}^+ \to N(1520)\frac{3}{2}^{-}$ transition. Our computed transition form factors show reasonable agreement with experimental data at large photon virtualities. However, deviations emerge at low $Q^2$, where experimental results exhibit a sharper variation than theoretical predictions. This discrepancy is expected, as these continuum QCD analyses account only for the quark-core of baryons, while low photon virtualities are dominated by meson cloud effects. We anticipate that these analytical predictions, based on the simplified SCI framework, will serve as a valuable benchmark for more refined studies and QCD-based truncations that incorporate quark angular momentum and the contributions of scalar and vector diquarks.

## Key claim

The authors extend their symmetry-preserving contact interaction (SCI) framework from the previously studied gamma(*) + N(940)1/2+ -> Delta(1700)3/2- transition to the gamma(*) + N(940)1/2+ -> N(1520)3/2- transition, finding that the computed transition form factors agree with experimental data at large photon virtualities but deviate at low Q2 where meson cloud effects dominate. The authors explicitly state that these discrepancies are expected because their continuum QCD analyses account only for the quark-core of baryons.

## Method

The authors employ a symmetry-preserving treatment of a vector-tensor-vector contact interaction (SCI) within a coupled formalism based on Dyson-Schwinger, Bethe-Salpeter, and Faddeev equations, using a quark-diquark picture of baryons. The Faddeev amplitude for the N(1520)3/2- is taken to be identical to that of the Delta(1700)3/2- within the SCI approach, differing only by an overall normalization constant and flavor structure coefficients arising from Clebsch-Gordan coefficients. The microscopic electromagnetic current is computed from three types of contributions: photon coupling to a dressed-quark (D1), elastic photon-diquark coupling (D2), and photon-induced transitions between axial-vector and scalar diquarks (D3), with the results expressed in terms of Jones-Scadron form factors (magnetic dipole G*_M, electric quadrupole G*_E, and Coulomb quadrupole G*_C) and subsequently converted to helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The N(1520)3/2- mass is given by m_N(1520) = 0.89 x m_Delta(1700) within the SCI approach
- The isoscalar-vector diquark contributes approximately 3.4% to the N(1520)3/2- Faddeev amplitude according to the QCD-kindred analysis of Liu (2022)
- The isovector-axial-vector Faddeev amplitude accounts for 91.60% of the mass of the N(1520)3/2- and 99.98% of the mass of the Delta(1700)3/2- based on the continuum approach of Liu (2022)
- The nucleon mass used in the positive-energy projector is m_N = 1.14 GeV
- The fine-structure constant alpha = 1/137 is used in the helicity amplitude normalization factor F
- The dressed-quark anomalous magnetic moment parameter eta is varied over the range eta in [0, 2/3], with the central value at eta = 1/3

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the gamma(*) + N(940)1/2+ -> Delta(1700)3/2- transition (Albino, 2025) to the N(1520)3/2- resonance, using the same SCI-DSE framework that has been applied to other nucleon resonances including N(1440)1/2+ (Wilson, 2011; Segovia, 2015), N(1535)1/2- (Raya, 2021), and Delta(1600)3/2+ (Lu, 2019). The results build upon the QCD-kindred analyses of Liu (2022) which demonstrated that the same isovector-axial-vector diquark correlation dominates both resonances. The predictions are intended to serve as a benchmark for more refined QCD-based truncations that incorporate quark angular momentum and scalar/vector diquark contributions, and can be compared with experimental measurements from CLAS at JLab (Burkert, 2002; CLAS, 2009; Mokeev, 2015, 2023) and other facilities.
