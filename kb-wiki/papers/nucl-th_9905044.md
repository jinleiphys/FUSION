# Probing nucleon strangeness structure with phi electroproduction

> We study the possibility to constrain the hidden strangeness content of the nucleon by means of the polarization observables in phi meson electroproduction. We consider the OZI evading direct knockout mechanism that arises from the non-vanishing s\bar{s} sea quark admixture of the nucleon as well as the background of the dominant diffractive and the one-boson-exchange processes. Large sensitivity on the nucleon strangeness are found in several beam-target and beam-recoil double polarization observables. The small \sqrt{s} and W region, which is accesible at some of the current high-energy electron facilities, is found to be the optimal energy region for extracting out the OZI evasion process.

## Key claim

The paper argues that double polarization observables in phi electroproduction are sensitive to the hidden strangeness content of the nucleon, but this sensitivity is conditional on operating at low center-of-mass energies (near threshold) and forward scattering angles, where the OZI-evading knockout process can interfere with the dominant diffractive process.

## Method

The authors model phi electroproduction using three mechanisms: diffractive production via vector-meson dominance with Pomeron exchange, one-boson-exchange (pi and eta exchange) as background, and a direct knockout process arising from a non-vanishing s\bar{s} sea quark admixture in the nucleon. The knockout amplitudes are computed using a relativistic harmonic oscillator quark model for the nucleon wavefunction, with the strange sea quark admixture parameterized by amplitudes b_0 and b_1 for spin-0 and spin-1 s\bar{s} clusters. Beam-target and beam-recoil double polarization asymmetries are then calculated for longitudinally polarized electron beams.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical upper bound on the admixture of strange sea quarks in the proton allowed by existing electroproduction cross section data is less than 5%.
- For the diffractive process at W ~ 2 GeV, the parameters are sigma_phi(0,W) = 0.20 microbarn and b_phi = 4.01 GeV^{-2}; for W <= 3 GeV, sigma_phi(0,W) = 0.22 microbarn and b_phi = 3.46 GeV^{-2}.
- The phenomenological factor xi^2 for the ratio of longitudinal to transverse cross sections is fixed at 0.328.
- The pi NN coupling constant used is g^2_{pi NN}/4pi = 14.0, and the eta NN coupling constant is set by the SU(3) relation g_{eta NN}/g_{pi NN} = 0.26 with F/D = 0.565.
- The double polarization asymmetries are shown for sqrt(s) = 2.55 GeV, W = 2.15 GeV, and Q^2 = 0.135 GeV^2, with strangeness admixture fractions |B|^2 = 0%, 0.5%, and 1.0%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies on phi photoproduction (Oh et al., 1997; Oh et al., 1998) to the electroproduction case with longitudinally polarized electron beams. It builds on earlier estimates of the knockout process using a nonrelativistic harmonic oscillator quark model (Henley et al., 1992) and the authors' own relativistic version (Titov et al., 1997). The paper enables experimental searches for nucleon strangeness at facilities like Jefferson Lab, where a proposal (Burkert et al., 1998) targets the low-energy region near threshold.
