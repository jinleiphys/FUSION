# Charge-Symmetry Breaking and the Two-Pion-Exchange Two-Nucleon Interaction

> Charge-symmetry breaking in the nucleon-nucleon force is investigated within an effective field theory, using a classification of isospin-violating interactions based on power-counting arguments. The relevant charge-symmetry-breaking interactions corresponding to the first two orders in the power counting are discussed, including their effects on the 3He-3H binding-energy difference. The static charge-symmetry-breaking potential linear in the nucleon-mass difference is constructed using chiral perturbation theory. Explicit formulae in momentum and configuration spaces are presented. The present work completes previously obtained results.

# Charge-Symmetry Breaking and the Two-Pion-Exchange Two-Nucleon Interaction

## Key claim

Charge-symmetry-breaking (CSB) two-pion-exchange potentials arising from the nucleon-mass difference are constructed using chiral perturbation theory, but their contribution to observables such as the ³He–³H binding-energy difference is estimated to be somewhat smaller than the dominant CSB mechanisms (short-range forces and CSB one-pion-exchange), and the separate quark-mass and electromagnetic components of the nucleon-mass difference remain unknown, so only the sum of all CSB potentials is currently constrained by experiment.

## Method

The authors classify isospin-violating interactions using power-counting arguments within an effective field theory based on chiral perturbation theory, identifying CSB terms at orders n=1, 2, and 3 in the Lagrangian. They compute the two-pion-exchange CSB nucleon-nucleon potential by evaluating the crossed-box diagram, the triangle diagram with a mass insertion, and the seagull triangle diagram (Figs. 2a–2c), using the leading-order chiral Lagrangian for pions and nucleons together with CSB terms from the nucleon-mass difference. The loop integrals are regularized and renormalized by contact terms, and explicit results are given in both momentum space and configuration space.

## Key numbers

- The ³He–³H binding-energy difference is 764 keV, of which 648 keV comes from the Coulomb energy difference and approximately 29 keV from Breit corrections plus vacuum polarization, leaving about 87 keV to be explained by hadronic CSB mechanisms.
- The kinetic-energy modification from the nucleon-mass difference (Eqn. 1c) contributes a robust 14 keV to the ³He–³H binding-energy difference.
- The short-range CSB force is estimated to contribute about 45 keV to the ³He–³H binding-energy difference, based on a 34-channel calculation using the AV18 potential giving −7.6 MeV for the short-range interaction expectation value.
- The CSB one-pion-exchange potential contribution is estimated as ⟨V_π^CSB⟩ ∼ 0(50) keV using the experimental value (β₁ + β̄₃) = 0(9)·10⁻³ from the Nijmegen phase-shift analysis.
- The experimental scattering-length difference a_nn − a_pp = −1.5(5) fm is attributed to CSB in the nuclear force, and adjusting the short-range CSB force to reproduce this gives a contribution of approximately 65(22) keV to the ³He–³H binding-energy difference.
- The CSB two-pion-exchange potential is estimated via dimensional analysis to be of order V_2π^CSB ∼ (m_π⁴/8 f_π² Λ²) δM_N, about half the estimate for the kinetic-energy modification of 25 keV.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of CSB in the nucleon-nucleon force within chiral perturbation theory, completing the long-range component of the class (III) potential up to order n=3. It builds on earlier calculations of the crossed-box contribution by Friar et al. (csb1) and the CSB seagull contribution by van Kolck et al. (csb2), and provides the previously uncalculated triangle diagram with mass insertion. The results are intended for incorporation into the Nijmegen phase-shift analysis of Robilotta and Timmermans (rob_mart) and into few-nucleon calculations within effective field theory, potentially enabling a model-independent disentanglement of CSB components of different ranges (one-pion, two-pion, and short-range).
