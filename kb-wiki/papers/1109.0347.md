# On Angular Correlations and High Energy Evolution

> We address the question to what extent JIMWLK evolution is capable of taking into account angular correlations in a high energy hadronic wave function. Our conclusion is that angular (and indeed other) correlations in the wave function cannot be reliably calculated without taking into account Pomeron loops in the evolution. As an example we study numerically the energy evolution of angular correlations between dipole scattering amplitudes in the framework of the large $N_c$ approximation to JIMWLK evolution (the "`projectile dipole model"'). Target correlations are introduced via averaging over (isotropic) ensemble of anisotropic initial conditions. We find that correlations disappear very quickly with rapidity even inside the saturation radius. This is in accordance with our physical picture of JIMWLK evolution. The actual correlations inside the saturation radius in the target QCD wave function, on the other hand should remain sizable at any rapidity.

## Key claim

JIMWLK evolution cannot reliably account for angular correlations in a high-energy hadronic wave function because it only describes color field modes with transverse momenta smaller than the saturation momentum, whereas the correlations that produce angular structure reside in modes at or above the saturation scale; proper treatment requires including Pomeron loops in the evolution.

## Method

The authors numerically evolved an ensemble of anisotropic initial dipole scattering amplitude configurations using the BK equation (the large \(N_c\) limit of JIMWLK evolution, also called the projectile dipole model). Each configuration was given a \(\cos^2(\theta)\) angular modulation to mimic scattering on a constant chromo-electric field, and the ensemble was made isotropic by uniformly randomizing the orientation angle. The rapidity evolution of angular correlations was then studied by averaging observables over the ensemble of BK-evolved configurations.

## Key numbers

- The anisotropy measure \(A(Y,r) = (N(Y,r,0)-N(Y,r,\pi/2))/(N(Y,r,0)+N(Y,r,\pi/2))\) decays exponentially with rapidity as \(A(r_{\text{max}}) \sim e^{-\lambda_A Y}\) with \(\lambda_A \simeq 0.6\).
- The fluctuation \(\Delta(Y,r)\) of the dipole amplitude decays with the same exponent \(\lambda_A \simeq 0.6\) at the scale \(r_{\text{max}} \simeq 0.5\ \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- The normalized angular correlation \(\Delta_\theta(Y,R_s(Y),\theta)\) at the saturation scale decays approximately as \(e^{-2\lambda_A Y}\).
- The saturation radius \(R_s\) loses its initial strong angular dependence after about 5 units of rapidity.
- The scale \(a(Y)\) at which anisotropy equals 10% shrinks approximately as \(a(Y) \simeq 8.5 - Y\) (in \(\text{GeV}^{-1}\)).

## Context

This work extends the discussion of angular correlations in high-energy QCD initiated by the CMS observation of the "ridge" in proton-proton collisions, following the approach of Kovner and Lublinsky (2011) which argued that such correlations arise from anisotropic target field configurations. The numerical results contradict the expectation from the target dipole model of Mueller (1994) and the analytical work of Yoshioka (2006), which found significant correlations even at leading order in \(1/N_c\). The authors conclude that future quantitative studies must include both JIMWLK and KLWMIJ evolutions simultaneously, i.e., explicit Pomeron loop effects, as argued earlier by private communication (2009).
