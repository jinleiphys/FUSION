# Dissociation of Relativistic Projectiles with the Continuum-Discretized Coupled-Channels Method

> Relativistic effects in the breakup of weakly-bound nuclei at intermediate energies are studied by means of the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with eikonal approximation. Nuclear coupling potentials with Lorentz contraction are newly included and those effects on breakup cross sections are investigated. We show that relativistic corrections lead to larger breakup cross sections. Coupled-channel effects on the breakup cross sections are also discussed.

## Key claim

Relativistic corrections to both Coulomb and nuclear coupling potentials increase breakup cross sections of weakly-bound projectiles at intermediate energies, but the enhancement is almost entirely due to the Coulomb potential modification; the relativistic correction to the nuclear potential alone produces negligible effects.

## Method

The authors applied the continuum-discretized coupled-channels method with eikonal approximation (E-CDCC) to breakup reactions of \(^8\)B and \(^{11}\)Be on \(^{208}\)Pb targets at 100 and 250 MeV/nucleon. They incorporated Lorentz-contracted Coulomb potentials (E1 and E2 multipoles) and a zero-range approximation for relativistic nuclear potentials following Feshbach and Zabek (1977), then solved the coupled-channels equations to compute differential breakup cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For \(^8\)B + \(^{208}\)Pb at 250 MeV/nucleon (\(\gamma = 1.268\)), the dynamical relativistic correction increases the breakup cross section by about 10–15% around the peak for scattering angles \(\theta \lesssim 0.7\) degrees.
- For \(^{11}\)Be + \(^{208}\)Pb at 250 MeV/nucleon, the relativistic enhancement around the peak is also about 10–15%, with differences appearing below about 0.3 degrees.
- For both \(^8\)B and \(^{11}\)Be on \(^{208}\)Pb at 100 MeV/nucleon, the relativistic enhancement at the peak is below the 10% level.
- The maximum impact parameter was set to 500 fm for \(^8\)B and 450 fm for \(^{11}\)Be at 100 MeV/nucleon, and to 400 fm for both at 250 MeV/nucleon.
- The maximum internal coordinate \(\xi\) was taken to be 200 fm.

## Context

This work extends the eikonal CDCC method developed by the Kyushu group (Ogata et al., 2003, 2005) to include relativistic dynamics, following earlier treatment of relativistic Coulomb potentials by Bertulani (2005). It contradicts the common assumption that relativistic corrections are negligible at 100–250 MeV/nucleon, showing 10–15% effects that are important for quantitative extraction of astrophysical factors such as \(S_{17}\) for the \(^7\)Be\((p,\gamma)^8\)B reaction. The results enable future studies that must address quantum mechanical corrections and the far-field approximation used in deriving the relativistic potentials.
