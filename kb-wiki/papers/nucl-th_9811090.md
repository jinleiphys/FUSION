# Lambda collective flow in heavy ion reactions

> Collective flow of Lambda hyperons in heavy ion reactions at SIS energies is investigated. It is found that a $Λ$ mean field constructed on the basis of the quark model leads to a good description of the experimental data of the in-plane transverse flow of $Λ$'s. The attractive mean field can also give rise to an additional "virtual" $Λ$ radial flow directed inwards, which is reflected by a "concave" structure of the transverse mass spectrum of the $Λ$ hyperons emitted at midrapidity. The $Λ$ radial flow is found to exhibit a strong mass dependence: The flow is visible in the Ni+Ni system, but is strongly reduced in the system of Au on Au.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a Lambda hyperon mean field constructed from the quark model (scaling nucleon potentials by 2/3) reproduces the experimental in-plane transverse flow data from the FOPI Collaboration, but this agreement is conditional on the beam energy being near the Lambda production threshold (1-2 GeV/nucleon) where the attractive potential dominates and the non-covariant treatment of the Lambda dynamics is justified.

## Method

The authors used the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) transport model to simulate heavy ion reactions at SIS energies (1-2 GeV/nucleon). Lambda hyperons were produced via baryon-baryon and pion-baryon reactions with experimentally fitted cross sections, and their propagation was governed by a Hamiltonian including a mean field derived from the quark model (scalar and vector potentials taken as 2/3 of the nucleon values from the nonlinear sigma-omega model). The in-plane transverse flow was analyzed as the average Lambda momentum projected onto the reaction plane as a function of rapidity, while radial flow was studied through the transverse mass spectrum of Lambdas emitted at midrapidity.

## Key numbers

- Lambda-nucleon elastic cross section is about 100 mb at a Lambda laboratory momentum of 0.2 GeV/c, becoming larger at lower momenta.
- The Lambda potential at saturation density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3) agrees roughly with hypernuclei data; the potential is attractive below three times saturation density (rho < 3 rho_0).
- For the Ni+Ni reaction at 1.93 GeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 0-4 fm, the average transverse mass of midrapidity Lambdas is reduced by the attractive potential from <m_T> - m_Lambda = 127 MeV to <m_T> - m_Lambda = 115 MeV, corresponding to a radial velocity beta = 0.025.
- The extracted temperature of the Lambda sources from the calculation without potential is T = 115 MeV.
- For the Au+Au reaction at 1 GeV/nucleon and b = 3 fm, the average transverse mass is reduced by only about 1 MeV due to the potential, making the radial flow invisible.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of collective flow in heavy ion reactions, particularly the analysis of nucleon flow (Pan 1993) and kaon flow (Li 1995, Wang 1997, Wang 1998) to Lambda hyperons. It directly compares with the RBUU model calculations of Li (1996) for the same reaction, finding good agreement for Lambda and kaon flow but small discrepancies for proton flow. The study enables future experimental tests of hyperon-nucleus potentials in dense nuclear matter, noting that the EOS Collaboration data for Ni+Cu at 2 GeV/nucleon had error bars too large to confirm the predicted concave structure in the Lambda transverse mass spectrum.
