# Nonequilibrium Weak Processes in Kaon Condensation II - Kinetics of condensation ---

> The kinetics of negatively charged kaon condensation in the early stages of a newly born neutron star is considered. The thermal kaon process, in which kaons are thermally produced by nucleon-nucleon collisions, is found to be dominant throughout the equilibration process. Temporal changes of the order parameter of the condensate and the number densities of the chemical species are obtained from the rate equations, which include the thermal kaon reactions as well as the kaon-induced Urca and the modified Urca reactions. It is shown that the dynamical evolution of the condensate is characterized by three stages: the first, prior to establishment of a condensate, the second, during the growth and subsequent saturation of the condensate, and the third, near chemical equilibrium. The connection between the existence of a soft kaon mode and the instability of the noncondensed state is discussed. Implications of the nonequilibrium process on the possible delayed collapse of a protoneutron star are also mentioned.

## Key claim

The thermal kaon process (KT), in which kaons are thermally produced by nucleon-nucleon collisions, is the dominant mechanism throughout the entire equilibration process of kaon condensation in a protoneutron star, including both the onset of condensation from a noncondensed state and the approach to chemical equilibrium, contrary to earlier work that required an ad hoc seed of condensate.

## Method

The authors solve coupled rate equations for the number densities of electrons, kaons, and protons, including the thermal kaon process (KT), kaon-induced Urca process (KU), and modified Urca process (MU), assuming the system remains in thermal equilibrium at constant temperature. The thermodynamic potential for the kaon-condensed phase is constructed from chiral symmetry, and the order parameter (chiral angle θ) is determined at each time step from the field equation ∂Ω/∂θ=0, while the kaon chemical potential is obtained from the total kaon number density relation that includes both condensed and thermal contributions.

- Critical density for kaon condensation: n_B^C = 0.49 fm^{-3} (= 3.0 n_0, where n_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}) for Σ_KN = 300 MeV
- For n_B = 0.55 fm^{-3} and T = 1.0 × 10^{11} K: initial kaon chemical potential μ_K^0 = -139 MeV, initial proton fraction x_p^0 = 0.14; equilibrium chiral angle θ^{eq} = 0.48 rad, equilibrium kaon chemical potential μ_K^{eq} = 203 MeV, equilibrium proton fraction x_p^{eq} = 0.23
- Initial chemical disequilibrium parameters at n_B = 0.55 fm^{-3}, T = 1 × 10^{11} K: ξ^{(KT)}(t=0) = -46 and ξ^{(KU)}(t=0) = 46
- KT forward reaction rate prefactor: 4.0 × 10^{30} cm^{-3}·s^{-1} times dimensionless factors including cos^6(θ/2) and T_9^5
- KU forward reaction rate prefactor: 6.6 × 10^{29} cm^{-3}·s^{-1} times sin^2θ(t) and T_9^5
- MU forward reaction rate prefactor: 5.9 × 10^{23} cm^{-3}·s^{-1} times (n_e/n_0)^{1/3}, cos^2(θ/2), and T_9^7

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Paper I, Muto et al. 1999) which calculated the KT reaction rate and showed it dominates over KU and MU rates, by now solving the full coupled rate equations to obtain the temporal evolution of the condensate. It addresses a gap in earlier treatments (Muto et al. 1997) that required manually inserting a seed condensate because KU and MU reactions cannot create a condensate from the noncondensed state. The results enable quantitative comparison of kaon condensation time scales with the gravitational collapse time scale (milliseconds), deleptonization time scale (seconds), and cooling time scale (tens of seconds) in protoneutron star evolution, relevant to the delayed collapse scenario of Brown and Bethe (1994) and the numerical simulations of Baumgarte et al. (1996).
