# Pseudo Nambu-Goldstone modes in neutron stars

> If quarks and gluons are either gapped or confined in neutron stars (NSs), the most relevant light modes are Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes. We study NG modes within a schematic quark model whose parameters at high density are constrained by the two-solar mass constraint. Our model has the color-flavor-locked phase at high density, with the effective couplings as strong as in hadron physics. We find that strong coupling effects make NG modes more massive than in weak coupling predictions, and would erase several phenomena caused by the stressed pairings in mismatched Fermi surfaces. For instance, we found that charged kaons, which are dominated by diquark and anti-diquark components, are not light enough to condense at strong coupling. Implications for gravitational wave signals for NS-NS mergers are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Strong coupling effects in the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase of dense quark matter, constrained by the two-solar-mass neutron star observation, make pseudo Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes more massive than weak-coupling predictions predict, and can prevent charged kaon condensation at densities above about five times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The author uses a 3-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type quark model with additional vector and color-magnetic interactions, treated in the mean-field approximation for chiral and diquark condensates. The properties of NG modes (π, K, η, η') are then computed within the random phase approximation (RPA) using 3×3 coupled-channel equations for charged mesons and 9×9 equations for neutral mesons, including both quark-antiquark and diquark-antidiquark fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The Hatsuda-Kunihiro parameter set is used: Λ = 631.4 MeV, G_s Λ² = 3.67, K Λ⁵ = 9.29, m_{u,d} = 5.5 MeV, m_s = 135.7 MeV.
- Three parameter sets (g_V, H)/G_s are considered: (0.5, 1.4) for set (I), (0.8, 1.5) for set (II), and (1.0, 1.6) for set (III).
- For the preferred set (II), typical excitation energies for the K_+ mode are ω_{K_+} = 5–15 MeV at n_B ≳ 5n_0.
- For set (III), ω_{K_+} = 25–30 MeV.
- For set (I), K_+ condenses at μ_c ≃ 0.51 GeV (n_B ≃ 6.45 n_0).
- The lowest quark-hole threshold is ~200 MeV, above the NG mode poles.
- The color chemical potential μ_8^c is 50–100 MeV in the CFL region.
- Dirac and Majorana masses for quarks are shown as functions of chemical potential; typical gap values are Δ_{us}, Δ_{ds} ≃ 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of NG modes in the CFL phase (Casalbuoni 1999, Son 1999, Rho 1999, Beane 2000, Bedaque 2001) by incorporating strong coupling effects consistent with the two-solar-mass neutron star constraint from Kojo (2014, 2015). It also extends model studies by Forbes (2004), Kleinhaus (2007), and Ebert (2007) by including vector interactions, residual chiral condensates, and U_A(1) breaking. The results enable construction of thermal equations of state for neutron star mergers, contrasting with gapless quark matter models such as those by Masuda (2015) and Kurkela (2016).
