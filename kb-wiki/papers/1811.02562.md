# Matrix Model of Strength Distribution: Extension and Phase Transition

> In this work we extend a previous study of matrix models of strength distributions. We still retain the nearest neighbor coupling mode but we extend the values the coupling parameter v. We consider extremes, from very smal v to very large v. We first use the same transiiton operator as before \textless{}n T(n+1)\textgreater{} =constat(=1). For this case we get an exponential decreasefor small v but we get a phase transition beyond v=10. In that case we get an even-odd effect-separate exponentials for even n and for odd n. We now also consider also the dipole choice--where \textless{}nT(n+1)\textgreater{} = $\sqrt{(n+1)}$ .

## Key claim
The paper extends a previous matrix model of strength distributions by exploring a much wider range of the coupling parameter v, from very small (v=0.01) to very large (v=1,000,000), and finds that while exponential decrease of strength persists for small v, a phase transition occurs beyond v=10 for the constant transition operator case, producing separate exponential decays for even and odd n.

## Method
The authors use an 11×11 tridiagonal Hamiltonian matrix with diagonal elements E_n = n (with E=1 MeV) and nearest-neighbor off-diagonal coupling v. They diagonalize this matrix for v values from 0.01 to 1,000,000 and compute transition strengths from the ground state to excited states using two different transition operators: one with constant matrix elements ⟨n|T|n+1⟩ = 1, and one with dipole-like matrix elements ⟨n|T|n+1⟩ = √(n+1). The logarithm of the transition strength is plotted against excitation energy and fitted with linear least squares.

## Key numbers
- For the constant transition operator with v=0.01, the approximate analytic expression for the nth excited state transition amplitude is O = v^(n+1)[(-1)^(n+1)/n! - 1/(n+1)!]
- For v=0.01 with constant transition, the logarithm of the squared transition strength to the second excited state is approximately -28.4419 (exact value -29.8287)
- For v=0.01 with constant transition, the logarithm of the squared transition strength to the third excited state is approximately -41.0002 (exact value -43.1978)
- For v=0.01 with constant transition, the logarithm of the squared transition strength to the fourth excited state is approximately -52.0431 (exact value -56.4379)
- The phase transition to separate even-odd exponentials occurs for v values beyond 10, and persists for v=100, 1,000, 10,000, 100,000, and 1,000,000

## Context
This work extends the matrix model study of Kingan and Zamick (2018) by exploring a much wider range of coupling parameters than the previous work, which only considered v=0.5, 1, 2, and 3. The exponential decrease of strength distributions connects to physical problems of magnetic dipole excitations studied by Schwengner et al. and Brown et al., and to the schematic model of giant dipole resonances by Brown and Bolsterli. The results enable further investigation of how strength distributions behave under extreme coupling conditions and how the choice of transition operator affects the emergence of phase transitions.
