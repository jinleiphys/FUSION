# Spectrum of the molecular pentaquarks

> We investigate the mass spectrum of the molecular pentaquarks composed of a baryon and a meson. We establish the underlying relations among the near-threshold interactions of the molecular tetraquark and pentaquark systems. We find the existence of the molecule candidates in the $Σ_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $DD^\ast$, and $D\bar{D}^\ast$ systems indicates a substantial presence of the hadronic molecules in the {\it heavy} baryon plus {\it heavy} meson systems ({\it heavy} refers to the hadrons with the $c$ and/or $s$ quarks). We make an exhaustive prediction of the possible bound/virtual molecular states in the systems: $Σ_{c}^{(\ast)}\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $Σ_{c}^{(\ast)}D^{(\ast)}$, $Ξ_{c}^{(\prime,\ast)}\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $Ξ_{c}^{(\prime,\ast)}D^{(\ast)}$, $Σ_{c}^{(\ast)}K^{\ast}$, $Σ_{c}^{(\ast)}\bar{K}^{\ast}$, $Ξ_{c}^{(\prime,\ast)}K^{\ast}$, $Ξ_{c}^{(\prime,\ast)}\bar{K}^{\ast}$, $Ξ_{cc}^{(\ast)}\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $Ξ_{cc}^{(\ast)}D^{(\ast)}$, $Ξ_{cc}^{(\ast)}K^{\ast}$, $Ξ_{cc}^{(\ast)}\bar{K}^{\ast}$, $Σ^{(\ast)}\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $Σ^{(\ast)}D^{(\ast)}$, $Ξ^{(\ast)}\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $Ξ^{(\ast)}D^{(\ast)}$, $Σ^{(\ast)} K^\ast$, $Σ^{(\ast)} \bar{K}^\ast$, $Ξ^{(\ast)} K^\ast$, $Ξ^{(\ast)} \bar{K}^\ast$. Hunting for the predicted states in experiments will significantly deepen our understanding of the formation mechanism of the hadronic molecules, and shed light on the manifestation of flavor symmetry in the low-energy residual strong interactions.

## Key claim

The existence of observed molecular candidates in the $\Sigma_c\bar{D}^{(\ast)}$, $DD^\ast$, and $D\bar{D}^\ast$ systems implies a substantial presence of hadronic molecules in heavy baryon plus heavy meson systems, where "heavy" refers to hadrons containing $c$ and/or $s$ quarks, and the strange quark is treated as an inert source due to its limited involvement in near-threshold residual strong interactions.

## Method

The authors derive near-threshold S-wave effective potentials for di-hadron systems from quark-level potentials $V_{qq}$ and $V_{\bar{q}q}$, which encode light quark pair correlations. These potentials are projected onto specific isospin and spin states of the hadron pairs, and the Lippmann-Schwinger equation is solved with a sharp cutoff $\Lambda = 0.4$ GeV to search for bound and virtual states. The low-energy constants $c_s$, $c_a$, $\tilde{c}_s$, and $\tilde{c}_a$ are determined from well-established experimental states, and correspondences among different systems are established under heavy quark symmetry.

## Key numbers

- $c_s = 146.4 \pm 10.8$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $c_a = -7.3 \pm 10.5$ GeV$^{-2}$ for the $qq$ type interactions
- $184.3$ GeV$^{-2} < \tilde{c}_a + \tilde{c}_s < 187.5$ GeV$^{-2}$ and $78.1$ GeV$^{-2} < \tilde{c}_a - \tilde{c}_s < 180.3$ GeV$^{-2}$ for the $\bar{q}q$ type interactions
- $\Sigma_c\bar{D}$ bound state at $4311.9 \pm 0.7_{-0.6}^{+6.8}$ MeV (threshold $4320.7$ MeV)
- $\Sigma_c\bar{D}^\ast$ bound states at $4440.3 \pm 1.3_{-4.7}^{+4.1}$ MeV ($\frac{1}{2}^-$) and $4457.3 \pm 0.6_{-1.7}^{+4.1}$ MeV ($\frac{3}{2}^-$) (threshold $4462.0$ MeV)
- $\Sigma_c^\ast\bar{D}$ bound state at $4380.3_{-1.8}^{+1.7}$ MeV (threshold $4385.6$ MeV)
- $\Xi_c\bar{D}$ bound state at $4334.8_{-1.1}^{+0.8}$ MeV (threshold $4336.3$ MeV)
- $\Xi_c\bar{D}^\ast$ bound states at $4473.9_{-4.5}^{+3.1}$ MeV ($\frac{1}{2}^-$) and $4476.3_{-2.3}^{+1.3}$ MeV ($\frac{3}{2}^-$) (threshold $4477.6$ MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous framework for molecular tetraquarks (Wang et al., 2023) to the pentaquark sector, establishing connections among observed states such as $P_{\psi}^N$, $T_{cc}$, $T_{cs}$, $X(3872)$, and $Z_c(3900)$. The predictions for $\Xi_c\bar{D}^\ast$ molecular states are consistent with the observed $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4459)$ and $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ signals from LHCb (2019, 2022), and the framework explains the near-threshold nature of $T_{cc}(3875)$ and $P_{\psi s}^\Lambda(4338)$ through a unified $qq$ coupling mechanism. The results enable experimental searches for predicted double-charm pentaquarks in $\Sigma_c^{(\ast)}D^{(\ast)}$ and $\Xi_c^{(\prime,\ast)}D^{(\ast)}$ systems, which could serve as sources for double-charm baryon production.
