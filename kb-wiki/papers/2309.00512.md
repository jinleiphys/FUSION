# Relativistic hydrodynamic fluctuations from an effective action: causality, stability, and the information current

> Causality is necessary for retarded Green's functions to remain retarded in all inertial frames in relativity, which ensures that dissipation of fluctuations is a Lorentz invariant concept. For first-order BDNK theories with stochastic fluctuations, introduced via the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, we show that imposing causality and stability leads to correlation functions of hydrodynamic fluctuations that only display the expected physical properties at small frequencies and wavenumber, i.e., within the expected regime of validity of the first-order approach. For second-order theories of Israel and Stewart type, constructed using the information current such that entropy production is always non-negative, a stochastic formulation is presented using the Martin-Siggia-Rose approach where imposing causality and stability leads to correlators with the desired properties. We also show how Green's functions can be determined from such an action. We identify a $\mathbb{Z}_2$ symmetry, analogous to the Kubo-Martin-Schwinger symmetry, under which this Martin-Siggia-Rose action is invariant. This modified Kubo-Martin-Schwinger symmetry provides a new guide for the effective action formulation of hydrodynamic systems with dynamics not solely governed by conservation laws. Furthermore, this symmetry ensures that the principle of detailed balance is valid in a covariant manner. We employ the new symmetry to further clarify the connection between the Schwinger-Keldysh and Martin-Siggia-Rose approaches, establishing a precise link between these descriptions in second-order theories of relativistic hydrodynamics. Finally, the modified Kubo-Martin-Schwinger symmetry is used to determine the corresponding action describing diffusion in Israel-Stewart theories in a general hydrodynamic frame.

## Key claim

For first-order BDNK theories with stochastic fluctuations introduced via the Schwinger-Keldysh formalism, imposing causality and stability leads to correlation functions that only display the expected physical properties at small frequencies and wavenumber, i.e., within the expected regime of validity of the first-order approach; for second-order Israel-Stewart theories constructed using the information current, a stochastic formulation using the Martin-Siggia-Rose approach yields correlators with the desired properties when causality and stability are imposed.

## Method

The authors constructed effective actions for stochastic relativistic hydrodynamic fluctuations using both the Schwinger-Keldysh (closed time path) formalism for first-order BDNK theories and the Martin-Siggia-Rose approach for second-order Israel-Stewart theories. For the first-order case, they expanded the effective action around equilibrium to study Gaussian fluctuations and computed tree-level propagators. For the second-order case, they built the effective action using the information current formalism, which guarantees non-negative entropy production off-shell, and identified a modified Kubo-Martin-Schwinger (KMS) symmetry that leaves the action invariant under a discrete transformation involving time reversal and parity.

## Key numbers

- Causality condition for first-order BDNK diffusion: \(0 \leq \lambda/\kappa \leq 1\), where \(\lambda\) is the BDNK frame coefficient and \(\kappa\) is the conductivity
- The non-hydrodynamic mode in the local rest frame has dispersion relation \(\omega(\mathbf{k}\to 0) = - i\chi/\lambda\), where \(\chi = (\partial n/\partial\mu)_T > 0\) is the susceptibility
- The diffusion constant is \(D = \kappa/\chi\)
- For \(\lambda > 0\) (causal case), the Gaussian weight in the path integral becomes positive when \(|\omega| > \sqrt{\kappa/\lambda}\, |k|\), causing the path integral to lose convergence
- For \(\lambda = 0\) (acausal case), the number of modes from propagator poles changes from one in the local rest frame to two in a boosted frame, with the new mode being unstable

## Context

This work extends the framework developed by Mullins et al. (2023) for studying thermal fluctuations in relativistic hydrodynamics, which generalized the non-relativistic results of Fox and Uhlenbeck using the information current formalism of Gavassino (2021). It addresses the fundamental tension between causality and stability in first-order fluctuating hydrodynamics, showing that first-order BDNK theories cannot produce covariantly stable stochastic fluctuations within the Schwinger-Keldysh approach. The paper establishes a precise link between Schwinger-Keldysh and Martin-Siggia-Rose descriptions for second-order Israel-Stewart theories and identifies a modified KMS symmetry that provides a new guide for constructing effective actions for hydrodynamic systems with dynamics not solely governed by conservation laws.
