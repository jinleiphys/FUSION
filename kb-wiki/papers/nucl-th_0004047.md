# Photoproduction of electron-positron pairs on the proton in the resonance region

> Production of lepton pairs in gamma p -> e-e+ p reaction is studied at photon energies up to 1 GeV. We show that even if lepton charge is not measured there are extensive kinematical regimes where the nuclear process dominates over the Bethe-Heitler contribution. The decomposition is performed of the unpolarized cross section for the virtual Compton scattering in terms of response functions. These are expressed in terms of a polarization density matrix of the virtual photon and are shown to be sensitive to the properties of baryon resonances in the first and the second resonance regions. In the analysis a unitary K-matrix model is used, based on an effective Lagrangian including nucleon, mesons, and baryon resonances with masses up to 1.7 GeV. Results of the model are compared with data for real-photon Compton scattering and predictions are given for observables in photoproduction of lepton pairs.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the photoproduction of electron-positron pairs on the proton, even when lepton charges are not distinguished (so the interference between virtual Compton scattering and Bethe-Heitler amplitudes vanishes), there exist extensive kinematical regimes where the nuclear (virtual Compton scattering) process dominates over the Bethe-Heitler contribution, particularly at backward angles for the virtual photon.

## Method

The authors use a unitary K-matrix model based on an effective Lagrangian that includes nucleon, mesons, and baryon resonances with masses up to 1.7 GeV, applied to the reaction gamma p -> e- e+ p at photon energies up to 1 GeV. They decompose the unpolarized cross section for virtual Compton scattering in terms of response functions (W_T, W_L, W_TT, W_LT) and relate these to elements of the polarization density matrix of the virtual photon. The model is first compared to data for real-photon Compton scattering, then predictions are made for observables in dilepton photoproduction.

## Key numbers

- Photon energies studied: up to 1 GeV, with specific calculations at E_gamma^lab = 320 MeV (Delta resonance region) and E_gamma^lab = 700 MeV (D_13(1520) resonance region)
- Baryon resonances included with masses up to 1.7 GeV: P_33(1232), D_13(1520), S_11(1535), S_11(1650), P_11(1440), S_31(1620), D_33(1700)
- Fitted couplings for P_33(1232): (g_1, g_2) = (4.9, 5.27); for D_13(1520) on proton: (7.25, 7.9); for D_33(1700): (1.74, 4.75)
- At E_gamma^lab = 700 MeV and theta_gamma = 135 degrees, maximal photon invariant mass M_gamma^max = 543 MeV
- In the Delta region, favorable conditions for nuclear dominance occur at invariant masses up to approximately 200 MeV
- At 700 MeV, larger invariant masses up to approximately 500 MeV become accessible
- For the Delta region, k^2 <= 0.08 GeV^2 is reached

## Context

This work extends the K-matrix model previously developed by the same authors (Scholten 1996, Korchin 1998) for pion-nucleon scattering, pion photoproduction, and real-photon Compton scattering to the time-like virtual Compton scattering regime. The study is motivated by experiments being performed at MAMI (Mainz) as reported by Bacelar (1999), and complements earlier work on virtual Compton scattering in the space-like region (Procee 1998, Guichon 1998) and on the Bethe-Heitler interference approach (Alv73, Lvov 1996, Korchin 1997, Diehl 1997). The results enable experimental extraction of response functions and polarization density matrix elements from dilepton production data, providing sensitivity to baryon resonance properties, particularly the G_3(k^2) coupling in the gamma N D_13(1520) vertex which is specific to virtual photons.
