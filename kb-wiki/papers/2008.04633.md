# Neutral-current background induced by atmospheric neutrinos at large liquid-scintillator detectors: I. model predictions

> The experimental searches for diffuse supernova neutrino background and proton decay in next-generation large liquid-scintillator (LS) detectors are competitive with and complementary to those in the water-Cherenkov detectors. In this paper, we carry out a systematic study of the dominant background induced by atmospheric neutrinos via their neutral-current (NC) interactions with the $^{12}{\rm C}$ nuclei in the LS detectors. The atmospheric neutrino fluxes at the location of Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory (JUNO) are used, as the JUNO detector is obviously a suitable representative for future LS detectors. Then, we implement the sophisticated generators \texttt{GENIE} and \texttt{NuWro} to simulate the neutrino interactions with the carbon nuclei, and the package \texttt{TALYS} to deal with the deexcitations of final-state nuclei. Finally, the event rates for the production of additional nucleons, $γ$'s, $α$'s, pions and kaons are obtained and categorized, and the systematic uncertainty of the NC background represented by a variety of data-driven nuclear models is estimated. The implications of the NC background from atmospheric neutrinos for the detection of diffuse supernova neutrino background and proton decay are also discussed.

## Key claim

The neutral-current (NC) interactions of atmospheric neutrinos with carbon-12 nuclei constitute the dominant irreducible background for diffuse supernova neutrino background (DSNB) and proton decay searches in large liquid-scintillator detectors, but the predicted background rates carry systematic uncertainties of order 15-20% arising from the choice of neutrino interaction generator, nuclear model (relativistic Fermi gas vs. spectral function), and axial mass parameter.

## Method

The authors used atmospheric neutrino fluxes at the Jiangmen Underground Neutrino Observatory (JUNO) site from the Honda group (2015), then simulated neutrino-carbon-12 neutral-current interactions with the Monte Carlo generators GENIE (version 2.12.0) and NuWro (version 17.01) across six different model configurations varying the axial mass (0.99, 1.03, and 1.35 GeV), nuclear model (relativistic Fermi gas or spectral function), and inclusion of transverse enhancement model for two-body currents. The deexcitation of final-state nuclei was handled with the TALYS package, and the resulting energy spectra of neutrons, protons, gamma rays, alpha particles, pions, and kaons were computed.

## Key numbers

* The DSNB search NC background rate from single-neutron final states is (3.1 ± 0.5) kt⁻¹ yr⁻¹ in the visible energy range 11 MeV to 30 MeV.
* The single π⁺ or π⁻ production rate for proton decay background in the energy transfer range [150, 650] MeV is (0.61 ± 0.14) kt⁻¹ yr⁻¹.
* The single K⁺ or K⁻ production rate for proton decay background in the energy transfer range [150, 1000] MeV is (4⁺⁵₋₄ × 10⁻⁴) kt⁻¹ yr⁻¹.
* The atmospheric neutrino flux uncertainty varies from about 30% at low energies to less than 10% in the range of 1-10 GeV.
* The spectral function approach (Model-N5) reduces total cross sections by about 20% at 1 GeV compared to the relativistic Fermi gas model.

## Context

This work extends the earlier DSNB background study by the KamLAND collaboration (2011) and the muon-spallation background studies for water-Cherenkov detectors by Li and Beacom (2014-2015), providing the first systematic multi-model comparison of neutral-current atmospheric neutrino backgrounds for next-generation liquid-scintillator detectors like JUNO and LENA. The results enable experimental collaborations to estimate signal sensitivity and design background-reduction strategies for DSNB and proton decay searches, and also inform searches for neutrinos from dark matter annihilation as noted by Palomares-Ruiz (2007) and Guo (2015).
