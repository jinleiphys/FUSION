# Deconfinement of non-strange hadronic matter with nucleons and $Δ$ baryons to quark matter in neutron stars

> We explore the possibility of formation of $Δ$ baryons (1232 MeV) in neutron star matter in an effective chiral model within the relativistic mean-field framework. With variation in delta-meson couplings, consistent with the constraints imposed on them, the resulting equation of state is obtained and the neutron star properties are calculated for static and spherical configuration. Within the framework of our model the critical densities of formation of $Δ$s and the properties of neutron stars are found to be very sensitive to the iso-vector coupling compared to the scalar or vector couplings. We revisit the $Δ$ puzzle and look for the possibility of phase transition from non-strange hadronic matter (including nucleons and $Δ$s) to deconfined quark matter, based on QCD theories. The resultant hybrid star configurations satisfy the observational constraints on mass from the most massive pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 in static condition obtained with the general hydrostatic equilibrium based on GTR. Our radius estimates are well within the limits imposed from observational analysis of QLMBXs. The obtained values of $R_{1.4}$ are in agreement with the recent bounds specified from the observation of gravitational wave (GW170817)from binary neutron star merger. The constraint on baryonic mass from study of binary system PSR J0737-3039 is also satisfied with our hybrid equation of state. \noindent{Keywords: Delta baryons, Quark matter, Phase transition, Equation of State, Neutron Stars, Hybrid Stars}

## Key claim

The paper claims that a phase transition from non-strange hadronic matter (including nucleons and Δ baryons) to deconfined quark matter can produce hybrid neutron star configurations that satisfy observational mass constraints from PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432, but only for specific choices of Δ-meson coupling strengths; the pure hadronic matter with Δ baryons alone fails to reach the 2 solar mass threshold.

## Method

The authors use an effective chiral model within the relativistic mean-field framework to describe neutron star matter containing nucleons and Δ baryons (1232 MeV), with Δ-meson couplings varied according to constraints from QCD sum-rule calculations. They then apply both Gibbs and Maxwell constructions to model a first-order phase transition from this hadronic phase to deconfined quark matter described by an MIT bag model with strong interaction corrections. The resulting hybrid equations of state are used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static, spherical neutron star configurations.

## Key numbers

- Δ baryon excitation energy: m_Δ − m_N = 293 MeV
- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ_0 ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³
- Saturation density of the model: ρ_0 = 0.153 fm⁻³
- Binding energy per nucleon at saturation: B/A = −16.3 MeV
- Symmetry energy coefficient: J = 32 MeV
- Slope parameter: L_0 = 87 MeV
- Nuclear incompressibility: K = 303 MeV
- Nucleon effective mass: m*_N/m_N = 0.85
- Maximum mass of pure nucleon star: M = 2.1 M_⊙
- Maximum mass with Δ baryons for x_ρ = 1 (CS-I and CS-III): M = 1.80 M_⊙ with radius ≈ 11.5 km
- Maximum mass with Δ baryons for x_ρ = 0.5 (CS-II and CS-IV): M = (1.44 and 1.35) M_⊙ with radius (10.3–10.8) km
- Δ baryons appear at ≈ 2ρ_0 when x_ρ = 0.5 (CS-II and CS-IV)
- Δ baryons appear at 6.5ρ_0 for CS-I and 5ρ_0 for CS-III when x_ρ = 1.0
- Second minimum of energy per baryon: ≈ 8 MeV at 2.5ρ_0 for x_σ = 1.35, x_ω = 1.0; ≈ −14 MeV at 2.8ρ_0 for x_σ = 1.2, x_ω = 0.8
- Δ potentials: −110 MeV and −102.5 MeV for the two coupling sets

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Δ baryons in neutron stars by DragoPRC (2014), Kolomeitsev (2017), and Maslov (2015) by using an effective chiral model rather than standard relativistic mean-field models, and by incorporating a hadron-quark phase transition as a mechanism to resolve the "delta puzzle" where Δ formation softens the equation of state too much. The paper follows the approach of Ozel (2016), Weissenborn (2011), and Bombaci (2016) who used phase transitions to deconfined quark matter to restore higher maximum masses. The results enable future work on hybrid star configurations with non-strange hadronic matter and quark matter, particularly for comparison with gravitational wave constraints from GW170817.
