# Anomaly Matching in Gauge Theories at Finite Matter Density

> We investigate the application of 't Hooft's anomaly matching conditions to gauge theories at finite matter density. We show that the matching conditions constrain the low-energy quasiparticle spectrum associated with possible realizations of global symmetries.

## Key claim

The authors show that 't Hooft's anomaly matching conditions, originally derived at zero density, remain valid at finite matter density and constrain the low-energy quasiparticle spectrum, despite the breaking of Lorentz symmetry and the large degeneracy of states near the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate that the axial anomaly equation is unchanged by a nonzero chemical potential, using both a path-integral measure argument and an explicit evaluation of the triangle diagram with finite-density propagators. They then analyze the infrared singularity structure of the three-current correlator at finite density using a general tensor decomposition that includes the chemical potential four-vector, and apply the Landau-Cutkowsky rules to show that the singularity arises from on-shell quasiparticle states in a manner identical to the zero-density case.

## Key numbers

* The anomaly equation at finite density takes the same form as at zero density: \(\partial_\mu j_5^{\mu} = - (e^2 / 32 \pi^2) \epsilon^{\mu\nu\rho\sigma} F_{\mu\nu}F_{\rho\sigma}\).
* The difference \(\delta\Delta_{\mu\nu}^{(1)}(\mu)\) between the finite-density and zero-density triangle diagram contributions vanishes identically, as shown by a momentum-shift argument yielding \(\delta{\Delta ^{(1)}_{\mu\nu}}(\mu)=0\).
* In the limit \(k^2\rightarrow 0; q_0\rightarrow 0\), the form factor \(F_3\) develops a singularity \(\lim F_3 = i/(2\pi^2 q^2)\), identical to the zero-density pion pole.
* The Fermi surface degeneracy does not enhance the anomaly prefactor; the on-shell kinematic conditions select a single point on the Fermi surface determined by the external momentum \(\vec{k}_1\).

## Context

This work extends 't Hooft's original anomaly matching conditions (Hooft, 1980) to finite-density gauge theories, building on earlier discussions of the anomaly at finite temperature by Pisarski, Tytgat, and collaborators (1997). The authors note that Sannino (1999) had previously observed that color-superconducting phases satisfy anomaly matching, and the present paper provides the general theoretical justification. The results enable rigorous constraints on the quasiparticle spectrum of dense QCD at intermediate densities, where perturbative calculations are unreliable, and provide a non-trivial check on asymptotic-density results such as color-flavor locking.
