# Equivalence of the long-wavelength approximation and the truncated Taylor expansion in relativistic Coulomb excitation

> The long-wavelength approximation and the truncated Taylor expansion are frequently used in the theory of relativistic Coulomb excitation to obtain multipole expansions of the interaction. It is shown in this note that these two approximations are exactly equivalent.

# Equivalence of the long-wavelength approximation and the truncated Taylor expansion in relativistic Coulomb excitation

## Key claim

The long-wavelength approximation (LWLA) and the truncated Taylor expansion approximation (TTEA) are exactly equivalent in relativistic Coulomb excitation, because both make the identical truncation of keeping only the ℓ = λ terms in the expansion of the retarded scalar potential in terms of r^ℓ Y^λ_μ(𝐫̂). The authors note that in the non-relativistic limit (γ ∼ 1) the ℓ = λ selection rule is exact, but for γ > 1 the truncation is a significant extra assumption.

## Method

The authors start from two formally exact expansions of the retarded scalar potential Φ(𝐫,t) for a projectile moving along a straight-line trajectory. The LWLA keeps only the first term in the expansion of the spherical Bessel function j_λ(|ω|r/c), while the TTEA keeps only the ℓ = λ terms in the full Taylor expansion of Φ(𝐫,t) about the origin. By writing both expansions in the common form Σ A^λ,ℓ_μ r^ℓ Y^λ_μ(𝐫̂) and Σ B^λ,ℓ_μ r^ℓ Y^λ_μ(𝐫̂), the authors show that the coefficients A^λ,ℓ_μ and B^λ,ℓ_μ are identical for all ℓ, λ, μ, so the ℓ = λ truncation yields the same approximate multipole expansion.

## Key numbers

- The scalar potential is given by Φ(𝐫,t) = Z_P e γ / √[(x - b_x)^2 + (y - b_y)^2 + γ^2(z - vt)^2], where Z_P is the projectile charge, γ is the Lorentz factor, v is the projectile speed, and b is the impact parameter.
- The Alder-Winther expansion coefficient includes the factor (1/(2γ))^μ, where μ is the azimuthal quantum number and γ is the Lorentz factor.
- The spherical Bessel function expansion is j_λ(|ω|r/c) = Σ_{ℓ=λ,λ+2,...} (|ω|r/c)^ℓ (-1/2)^{(ℓ-λ)/2} / [((ℓ-λ)/2)! (ℓ+λ+1)!!].
- In the non-relativistic limit γ ∼ 1, the Laplacian ∇_s^2 (1/√[(s_x - b_x)^2 + (s_y - b_y)^2 + (s_z - vt)^2]) = 0 at s = 0 for b ≠ 0, but for γ > 1 this Laplacian is nonzero.

## Context

This note resolves an ambiguity between two approximation schemes used in relativistic Coulomb excitation theory. The LWLA was used by Catara (year not given in text) and the TTEA was introduced by Bertulani et al. (year not given in text). The work extends the exact multipole expansion of the Fourier transform V(ω,b) derived by Alder and Winther (1979). The equivalence means that results calculated with the TTEA have exactly the same range of validity as those calculated with the LWLA, and the equivalence also applies to the vector potential 𝐀(𝐫,t) = (v/c)Φ(𝐫,t)𝐳̂. This enables practitioners to use whichever formulation is more convenient for coupled-channel calculations, with the TTEA offering explicit expressions for the potentials.
