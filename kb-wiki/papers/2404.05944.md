# Shape transition and coexistence in Te isotopes studied with the quadrupole collective Hamiltonian based on a relativistic energy density functional

> Evolution and coexistence of shape and the related spectroscopic properties of even-even Te isotopes are investigated within the quadrupole collective model that is based on the nuclear density functional theory. By means of the constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations performed within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov method with a choice of the energy density functional and pairing interaction, the deformation-dependent mass parameters and moments of inertia as well as collective potential of the triaxial quadrupole collective Hamiltonian are completely determined. The collective model produces for the near mid-shell nuclei, e.g., $^{116}$Te and $^{118}$Te, the low-energy $0^+_2$ state, which can be interpreted as the intruder state originating from the strongly deformed prolate minimum in the potential energy surface, along with the $0^+_1$ ground state that is attributed to the normal state based on a weakly oblate deformed global minimum. The collective model calculation suggests a parabolic behavior of the $0^+_2$ energy level near the neutron mid-shell $N=66$, as observed experimentally. Sensitivities of the calculated low-energy spectra to the pairing strength and collective mass parameters are analyzed.

## Key claim
The relativistic density functional theory plus quadrupole collective Hamiltonian (RHB+QCH) calculation suggests that the low-energy \(0^+_2\) state in near-mid-shell Te isotopes (e.g., \(^{116}\)Te and \(^{118}\)Te) can be interpreted as an intruder state originating from a strongly deformed prolate minimum, while the \(0^+_1\) ground state is attributed to a weakly oblate deformed global minimum; however, the calculation systematically underestimates the \(2^+_1\) and \(4^+_1\) yrast levels for nuclei with neutron numbers \(N=60-66\), likely because the mean-field overestimates quadrupole deformation and shape mixing.

## Method
The authors performed constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations using the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) method with the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable finite-range pairing force. From these calculations, they fully determined the deformation-dependent mass parameters, moments of inertia (scaled by a factor \(\alpha=0.3\) using the Inglis-Belyaev formula), and collective potential of the triaxial quadrupole collective Hamiltonian (QCH). The QCH was then solved to obtain excitation energies and collective wave functions for even-even \(^{104-132}\)Te isotopes, with sensitivity studies varying the pairing strength (default \(V=728\) MeV fm\(^3\), increased by 5%, and an \(N\)-dependent scaling factor) and the moment of inertia scaling.

## Key numbers
- The default pairing strength used is \(V=728\) MeV fm\(^3\) with a Gaussian range parameter \(a=0.644\) fm; the increased strength is \(1.05V = 764\) MeV fm\(^3\).
- The moment of inertia scaling factor is \(\alpha=0.3\) for all Te nuclei.
- The \(N\)-dependent pairing factor \(f(N)\) varies from 1.1 (at \(N=52\)) to 0.97 (at \(N=80\)), with parameters \(c_1=1.1\) and \(c_2=-0.58\).
- For \(^{116}\)Te and \(^{118}\)Te, the Gogny-D1S HFB plus 5DCH calculation obtained \(2^+_1\) excitation energies of 336 keV and 368 keV, respectively, which are close to the present results with default pairing and increased MOI.
- The calculated neutron pairing gaps with the increased pairing strength (\(f=1.05\)) are systematically larger than those with default strength (\(f=1.0\)) by typically 10–20%.
- The \(2^+_1\) and \(4^+_1\) energies are lowest at \(N=64\) in the fixed-pairing calculations, while experimental minima occur at \(N=56\) and \(N=70\).

## Context
This work extends the previous EDF-based QCH study of shape coexistence in Cd isotopes (Nomura, 2022) to the Te isotopic chain, where experimental evidence for intruder states and shape coexistence near the neutron mid-shell \(N=66\) has been accumulating (e.g., Mihai, 2011; von Spee, 2024). The results are compared with Gogny-D1S HFB plus 5DCH calculations (Delaroche, 2010) and with experimental data from the NNDC database and von Spee (2024). The study enables systematic theoretical predictions for low-energy spectra and shape coexistence in Te isotopes, and identifies the need for improved treatment of pairing strengths and deformation properties to better match experimental systematics.
