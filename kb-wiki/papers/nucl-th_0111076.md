# Two-potential approach to multi-dimensional tunneling

> We consider tunneling to the continuum in a multi-dimensional potential. It is demonstrate that this problem can be treated as two separate problems: a) a bound state and b) a non-resonance scattering problem, by a proper splitting of the potential into two components. Finally we obtain the resonance energy and the partial tunneling widths in terms of the bound and the scattering state wave functions. This result can be used in a variety of tunneling problems. As an application we consider the ionization of atomic states by an external field. We obtain very simple analytical expressions for the tunneling width and for the angular distribution of tunneling electrons. It is shown that the angular spread of electrons in the final state is determined by the semi-classical traversal tunneling time.

# Two-potential approach to multi-dimensional tunneling

## Key claim

The paper claims that multi-dimensional tunneling to the continuum can be reduced to two separate problems, a bound state and a non-resonance scattering problem, by splitting the potential into two components, yielding resonance energy and partial tunneling widths that are independent of the choice of the separation hyper-surface, up to terms of order Gamma/U_0.

## Method

The author splits the multi-dimensional potential V(x) into an inner component U(x) and an outer component W(x) separated by a hyper-surface Sigma placed inside the barrier. The bound state Phi_0 of the inner potential U is computed, and the outer potential W is treated via a Green's function expansion that converges faster than the Born series. The total and partial tunneling widths are then expressed as surface integrals over Sigma involving the bound state wave function and non-resonance scattering wave functions of the outer Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- The total width Gamma for ionization of a Hydrogen atom in an electric field is Gamma = (4/E) * exp(-2/3E) in atomic units, where E is the electric field strength.
- The angular distribution of ionized electrons from Hydrogen is Gamma_q = (4pi/E) * Gamma * exp(-q^2/E) in atomic units.
- For a short-range potential well, the total ionization width is Gamma = pi * A^2 * E * exp(-2/3E), where A is a dimensionless constant depending on the well form.
- The traversal tunneling time tau for both the Hydrogen atom and the short-range well is tau = 1/E in atomic units.
- The action S for the Hydrogen atom is S = 1/(3E) + ln(E * x_i / 2) - x_i/2, with x_i the inner turning point.
- The action S for the short-range well is S = 1/(3E).

## Context

This work extends the two-potential approach (TPA) previously developed by Gurvitz for one-dimensional tunneling (Gurvitz, 1990s) to multi-dimensional systems. The derived formulas for the Hydrogen atom ionization width reproduce the well-known result of Landau (1965), and the short-range potential result matches the work of Demkov and Drukarev (1960s). The paper enables calculation of angular distributions of tunneling particles in terms of the semi-classical traversal tunneling time, connecting to the work of Buttiker and Landauer (1980s) on tunneling time and to Brach (1990s) on lateral spread.
