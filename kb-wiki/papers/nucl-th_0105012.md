# Significance Of Deuteron Breakup In A Halo Transfer Reaction

> We discuss the quasi-adiabatic approximations to the three-body wavefunction in breakup processes, clarifying the assumptions underlying the model. This suggests alternative approximation schemes. Using different theoretical three-body models, calculated differential cross section angular distributions for the Be-11(p,d) reaction,for which new preliminary data have been reported at 35 MeV, are presented. We show that calculations are sensitive to the inclusion of deuteron breakup and to the breakup model used, particularly if used to deduce absolute spectroscopic information on the 0{+} and 2{+} Be-10 core state parentages. There is also considerable sensitivity to the model used in calculations of the relative cross sections to the two states.

# Significance Of Deuteron Breakup In A Halo Transfer Reaction

## Key claim

The inclusion of deuteron breakup degrees of freedom in the theoretical analysis of the ¹¹Be(p,d)¹⁰Be reaction at 35 MeV significantly affects calculated differential cross sections and the extracted spectroscopic information on the 0⁺ and 2⁺ states of ¹⁰Be, but the magnitude of these effects depends on the specific three-body breakup model used (adiabatic, quasi-adiabatic, or projection operator approach). The standard DWBA procedure for determining spectroscopic factors is not reliable for reactions involving weakly bound halo nuclei, as the extracted spectroscopic factors would be smaller than those obtained with conventional DWBA due to breakup effects.

## Method

The authors calculated differential cross section angular distributions for the ¹¹Be(p,d)¹⁰Be reaction at 35 MeV incident energy using four different theoretical approaches: the adiabatic (AD) model of Johnson-Soper, the quasi-adiabatic (QAD) model, the projection operator approach (POA), and the Coupled Discretized Continuum Channels (CDCC) method as a benchmark. The three-body wavefunctions for the n+p+¹⁰Be system in the exit channel were computed using these models, and the transfer amplitude was evaluated in the prior form using the zero-range approximation with the n-p interaction as the transition interaction. The QAD and POA calculations were iterated to determine mean breakup energies for the continuum in each partial wave, with the maximum relative breakup energy set to 25 MeV (k = 0.78 fm⁻¹).

## Key numbers

- Incident energy: 35 MeV/nucleon for the ¹¹Be beam in inverse kinematics
- Neutron separation energy of ¹¹Be: 0.5 MeV
- Excited state energy of ¹⁰Be 2⁺ core state: 3.368 MeV
- Maximum relative breakup energy for continuum discretization: 25 MeV, corresponding to k = 0.78 fm⁻¹
- Radial integration range: 0 to 35 fm in steps of 0.1 fm
- Maximum number of partial waves: 30 for both entrance and exit channels
- Woods-Saxon well parameters for transferred neutron: r = 1.25 fm, a = 0.65 fm
- Spin-orbit force in proton channel: 6 MeV
- Ratio of spectroscopic factors S(2⁺)/S(0⁺) from different models varies from 0.07 to 0.73 (cited from prior work)

## Context

This work extends the quasi-adiabatic model formulation of Amakawa et al. (1980s) and the adiabatic model of Johnson and Soper (1970) by clarifying the theoretical assumptions and providing an alternative projection operator formulation based on Feshbach's unified theory. The paper addresses the need for reliable reaction models to analyze transfer reactions with radioactive beams, specifically for the halo nucleus ¹¹Be, where prior work by various authors (cited as ref4 in the text) correctly reproduces parity inversion but makes very different predictions about the coupling of the ¹¹Be ground state with the 2⁺ excited state of ¹⁰Be. The results enable improved extraction of spectroscopic factors from future experimental data at GANIL and other facilities, and the authors indicate that the same models will be applied to study the halo nature of ¹⁹C.
