# Electroweak Hard Photon Bremsstrahlung in Electron-Nucleon Scattering

> One way to treat the infrared divergences of the electroweak Next-to-Leading-Order (NLO) differential cross sections to parity-violating (PV) electron-proton scattering is by adding soft-photon emission contribution. Although more physical, the results are left with a logarithmic dependence on the photon detector acceptance, which can only be eliminated by considering Hard Photon Bremsstrahlung (HPB) contribution. Here we present a treatment of HPB for PV electron-proton scattering. HPB differential cross sections for electron-proton scattering have been computed using the experimental values of nucleon form factors. The final results are expressed through kinematic parameters, making it possible to apply the computed PV HPB differential cross sections for the analysis of data of a range of current and proposed experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the logarithmic dependence on the photon detector acceptance parameter \(\Delta E\) left after soft-photon emission corrections to parity-violating electron-proton scattering can be eliminated by adding the Hard Photon Bremsstrahlung (HPB) differential cross section, provided the HPB contribution is computed using experimental nucleon form factors and expressed through kinematic parameters.

## Method

The authors computed the HPB differential cross section for parity-violating electron-proton scattering by evaluating the full set of \(2 \rightarrow 3\) bremsstrahlung diagrams, splitting the amplitude into a part with the photon momentum removed from the numerator (\(M_a\)) and a remainder of order \(O(k_5)\). They expressed the general electroweak couplings by inserting appropriate form factors into the vertices and constructed the HPB cross sections as functions of Mandelstam invariants, with the emitted photon phase space parametrized in the center-of-mass frame and integrated numerically using cuts on the photon energy.

## Key numbers

- The soft-photon approximation is valid for photon energies \(\lambda < \Delta E \leq 10^{-3} E_{cms}\), where \(\Delta E\) is the maximum soft-photon energy and \(E_{cms}\) is the center-of-mass energy.
- The universal nucleon form factor used is of monopole or dipole type: \(G_N(q) = \left( \frac{\Lambda^2}{\Lambda^2 - q^2} \right)^n\) with \(n = 1, 2\) and \(\Lambda^2 = 0.83 m_N^2\).
- The maximum hard-photon energy in the center-of-mass frame is \((k_5^0)_{\max} = \frac{\sqrt{s}}{2} - \frac{(m_e + m_N)^2}{2\sqrt{s}}\).
- The minimum hard-photon energy is \((k_5^0)_{\min} = \Delta E\), matching the soft-photon cutoff.

## Context

This work extends the earlier NLO calculations for parity-violating electron-nucleon scattering by Aleksejevs, Barkanova, and collaborators (2002), which computed one-quark NLO contributions including soft-photon emission but left a logarithmic dependence on the detector acceptance parameter \(\Delta E\). The present paper provides the missing HPB contribution needed to cancel that dependence, and the analytical results are designed to be applicable to several recent and proposed parity-violating experiments including SAMPLE, HAPPEX, G0, A4, and Qweak.
