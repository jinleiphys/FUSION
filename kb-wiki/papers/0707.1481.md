# Measurement of Nuclear Transparency for the A(e,e' pi^+) Reaction

> We have measured the nuclear transparency of the A(e,e' pi^+) process in ^{2}H,^{12}C, ^{27}Al, ^{63}Cu and ^{197}Au targets. These measurements were performed at the Jefferson Laboratory over a four momentum transfer squared range Q^2 = 1.1 - 4.7 (GeV/c)^2. The nuclear transparency was extracted as the super-ratio of $(σ_A/σ_H)$ from data to a model of pion-electroproduction from nuclei without pi-N final state interactions. The Q^2 and atomic number dependence of the nuclear transparency both show deviations from traditional nuclear physics expectations, and are consistent with calculations that include the quantum chromodynamical phenomenon of color transparency.

## Key claim

The nuclear transparency for the A(e,e' pi^+) reaction shows deviations from traditional nuclear physics expectations in both its Q^2 and atomic number dependence, and is consistent with calculations that include the quantum chromodynamical phenomenon of color transparency, though the authors note that the coherence length for pion production was smaller than the nucleon radius and essentially constant (0.2-0.5 fm) over the kinematic range.

## Method

The experiment was performed in Hall-C at Jefferson Laboratory using a continuous wave electron beam with energies between 4 and 5.8 GeV incident on solid foil targets of 12C, 27Al, 63Cu, 197Au and liquid hydrogen and deuterium targets. Scattered electrons and electroproduced pions were detected in coincidence using the Short Orbit Spectrometer (SOS) and the High Momentum Spectrometer (HMS) respectively. The nuclear transparency was extracted as the super-ratio of (sigma_A/sigma_H) from data to a model of pion-electroproduction from nuclei without pion-nucleon final state interactions, using the standard Hall C Monte Carlo simulation code SIMC.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear transparency measured over Q^2 range 1.1 to 4.7 (GeV/c)^2 for five targets: 2H, 12C, 27Al, 63Cu, and 197Au
* Point-to-point systematic uncertainty of 2.4-3.2%, dominated by spectral function uncertainty (1%) and iteration procedure (1%)
* Q^2 dependent model uncertainty of 7.6%, 5.7%, 3.5%, 3.8%, and 3.8% for Q^2 = 1.1, 2.1, 3.0, 3.9, and 4.7 (GeV/c)^2 respectively
* Additional normalization systematic uncertainty of 1.1% from pion absorption correction (0.5%) and target thickness (1%)
* Additional 7.0% normalization type model uncertainty, independent of Q^2
* The parameter alpha (from T = A^{alpha-1}) found to be approximately 0.76 in fits to pion-nucleus scattering cross sections from Carroll (1979)
* Coherence length for pion production ranged from 0.2-0.5 fm over the kinematic range

## Context

This work extends previous searches for color transparency using the A(p,2p) and A(e,e'p) reactions, which showed no increase of nuclear transparency up to Q^2 = 8.1 (GeV/c)^2 (Hall C CT experiment, 2006), and a pion-photoproduction experiment at JLab (2006) that supported earlier onset of CT in mesons compared to baryons. The results, together with previous meson transparency measurements from the Fermilab di-jet experiment (1995) and HERMES rho^0 production (2001), suggest a gradual transition to meson production with small inter-quark separation and put severe constraints on early models predicting a dramatic threshold-like transition. The authors note that extending these measurements to Q^2 approximately 10 (GeV/c)^2 is important to establish the onset of CT on a firm footing.
