# Electromagnetic Pion Form Factor and Neutral Pion Decay Width

> The electromagnetic pion form factor, $F_π(q^2)$, is calculated for spacelike-$q^2$ in impulse approximation using a confining quark propagator, $S$, and a dressed quark-photon vertex, $Γ_μ$, obtained from realistic, nonperturbative Dyson-Schwinger equation studies. Good agreement with the available data is obtained for $F_π(q^2)$ and other pion observables, including the decay $π^0 \rightarrow γ\,γ$. This calculation suggests that soft, nonperturbative contributions dominate $F_π(q^2)$ at presently accessible~$q^2$.

## Key claim

The calculation suggests that soft, nonperturbative contributions dominate the electromagnetic pion form factor \(F_\pi(q^2)\) at presently accessible spacelike-\(q^2\), and that the perturbative QCD factorisation Ansatz becomes valid only for spacelike-\(q^2 \gsim 10\) GeV\(^2\), which is currently inaccessible experimentally.

## Method

The electromagnetic pion form factor \(F_\pi(q^2)\) was calculated in impulse approximation using a confining dressed quark propagator \(S(p)\), a pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude \(\Gamma_\pi(p,P)\) approximated by the quark mass function, and a dressed quark-photon vertex \(\Gamma_\mu(p_1,p_2)\) that satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity. The quark propagator was modelled by algebraic forms (Eqs. SSM and SVM) that are entire functions in the complex-\(p^2\) plane, ensuring the absence of free-quark production thresholds. The six model parameters were fixed by fitting to experimental values of \(f_\pi\), the chiral condensate, \(m_\pi^2\), \(\pi\)-\(\pi\) scattering lengths, and a least-squares fit to \(F_\pi(q^2)\) on the spacelike domain \([0,4]\) GeV\(^2\).

## Key numbers

- The asymptotic behaviour of \(F_\pi(q^2)\) at large spacelike-\(q^2\) is \(F_\pi(q^2) \propto 1/q^4\), up to \(\ln[q^2]\)-corrections, and becomes dominant only for spacelike-\(q^2 \gsim 10\) GeV\(^2\).
- The neutral pion decay width \(\Gamma_{\pi^0\rightarrow\gamma\gamma}\) in the chiral limit yields \(g_{\pi^0\gamma\gamma}^0 = 1/2\), independent of the details of the quark propagator.
- Experimental values used for fitting include: \(f_\pi / \langle \overline{q}q\rangle^{1/3} = 0.423\), \(f_\pi r_\pi = 0.318\), \(m_\pi^2 / \langle \overline{q}q\rangle^{2/3} = 0.396\), and \(\pi\)-\(\pi\) scattering lengths \(a_0^0 = 0.26 \pm 0.05\), \(a_0^2 = -0.028 \pm 0.012\), \(a_1^1 = 0.038 \pm 0.002\), \(a_2^0 = 0.0017 \pm 0.0003\).
- The experimental neutral pion decay width is \(\Gamma_{\pi^0\rightarrow\gamma\gamma} = (7.74 \pm 0.56)\) eV, corresponding to \(g_{\pi^0\gamma\gamma} = 0.500 \pm 0.018\) using \(m_{\pi^0}=135\) MeV and \(f_\pi=92.4\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to QCD, reviewed in Roberts (1994), by providing a phenomenological application to the pion form factor and neutral pion decay. It contradicts the expectation that perturbative QCD factorisation (as in Efremov and Radyushkin (1980) and Lepage and Brodsky (1980)) applies at presently accessible \(q^2\), supporting instead the conclusion of Isgur, Llewellyn-Smith (1989) that soft nonperturbative contributions dominate. The calculation enables constraints on the effective quark-quark interaction in the infrared and demonstrates how the Abelian anomaly is realised in the DSE framework, with the chiral limit result for \(\pi^0\rightarrow\gamma\gamma\) being independent of model details.
