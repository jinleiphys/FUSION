# Leading Power Accuracy in Lattice Calculations of Parton Distributions

> In lattice-QCD calculations of parton distribution functions (PDFs) via large-momentum effective theory, the leading power (twist-three) correction appears as ${\cal O}(Λ_{\rm QCD}/P^z)$ due to the linear-divergent self-energy of Wilson line in quasi-PDF operators. For lattice data with hadron momentum $P^z$ of a few GeV, this correction is dominant in matching, as large as 30\% or more. We show how to eliminate this uncertainty through choosing the mass renormalization parameter consistently with the resummation scheme of the infrared-renormalon series in perturbative matching coefficients. An example on the lattice pion PDF data at $P^z = 1.9$ GeV shows an improvement of matching accuracy by a factor of more than $3\sim 5$ in the expansion region $x= 0.2\sim 0.5$.

# Leading Power Accuracy in Lattice Calculations of Parton Distributions

## Key claim
The leading power (twist-three) correction in lattice-QCD calculations of parton distribution functions via large-momentum effective theory can be eliminated by choosing the mass renormalization parameter consistently with the resummation scheme of the infrared-renormalon series in perturbative matching coefficients, provided the renormalization scheme for the linear divergence is defined consistently in both the renormalization and the perturbative matching.

## Method
The authors use perturbative matching coefficients accurate to next-to-next-to-leading order plus the leading renormalon contribution estimated from heavy quark pole mass calculations, adopting the principal value prescription to regulate renormalon poles in the Borel integral. They extract the finite renormalization twist-three mass parameter m0 from the Pz=0 lattice matrix element in the perturbative z region, using the renormalization condition that continuum-limit lattice matrix elements have a short-distance expansion consistent with Wilson coefficients resummed through the principal value prescription. The leading renormalon resummation is applied to the momentum-space matching kernel through Fourier transformation of coordinate-space corrections, with a small exponential decay introduced to regularize the long-tail behavior.

## Key numbers
- The leading power correction appears as O(Λ_QCD/P^z) and can be as large as 30% or more for lattice data with hadron momentum P^z of a few GeV.
- For the lattice pion PDF data at P^z = 1.9 GeV, the improvement of matching accuracy is by a factor of more than 3 to 5 in the expansion region x = 0.2 to 0.5.
- The mass renormalon strength for n_f=0 is N_m = 0.660(56) in the MSbar scheme, determined from lattice calculations; for n_f=3 the authors use N_m = 0.575.
- The extracted m0(τ) value is 0.161^{+0.025}_{-0.002} GeV for NLO with leading renormalon resummation, and 0.164^{+0.016}_{-0.003} GeV for NNLO with leading renormalon resummation.
- The fixed-order Wilson coefficient C0 at NLO is given by 1 + (α_s(μ)/3π)(3 ln(z^2 μ^2 e^{2γ_E}/4) + 5).

## Context
This work extends the large-momentum effective theory framework proposed by Ji (2013, 2014) for lattice calculations of parton distribution functions, addressing the twist-three power corrections that had not been properly handled in prior literature. It builds on the observation by Su et al. (2022) that the proper perturbative scale in LaMET matching is around 2xP^z, and uses the self-renormalization approach from the Lattice Parton Collaboration (2021) for eliminating power divergences. The leading renormalon resummation method draws on heavy quark pole mass calculations by Bali et al. (2013) and Pineda (2001), and the principal value prescription follows work by Ayala et al. (2019, 2020). This enables future high-precision lattice calculations of PDFs with controlled systematic uncertainties from power corrections.
