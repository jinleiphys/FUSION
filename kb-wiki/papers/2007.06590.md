# Valence parton distribution of pion from lattice QCD: Approaching continuum

> We present a high-statistics lattice QCD determination of the valence parton distribution function (PDF) of the pion, with a mass of 300 MeV, using two very fine lattice spacings of $a=0.06$ fm and 0.04 fm. We reconstruct the $x$-dependent PDF, as well as infer the first few even moments of the PDF using leading-twist 1-loop perturbative matching framework. Our analyses use both RI-MOM and ratio-based schemes to renormalize the equal-time bi-local quark-bilinear matrix elements of pions boosted up to 2.4 GeV momenta. We use various model-independent and model-dependent analyses to infer the large-$x$ behavior of the valence PDF. We also present technical studies on lattice spacing and higher-twist corrections present in the boosted pion matrix elements.

## Key claim

The valence pion PDF at a pion mass of 300 MeV is determined from lattice QCD using two fine lattice spacings (0.06 fm and 0.04 fm), with the continuum extrapolation remaining speculative rather than definitive because only two lattice spacings are available and the analysis does not perform a full continuum limit extrapolation.

## Method

The authors computed equal-time bi-local quark-bilinear matrix elements of pions boosted up to 2.4 GeV momentum on two 2+1 flavor HISQ ensembles with clover-improved valence quarks at lattice spacings a=0.06 fm and a=0.04 fm. They renormalized the matrix elements using both RI-MOM and ratio-based schemes, then applied leading-twist 1-loop perturbative matching to extract the x-dependent valence PDF and its first few even moments. Excited-state contamination in the three-point functions was controlled through two-state and three-state fits to the spectral decomposition.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass: 300 MeV (valence quark mass tuned to produce this value on both ensembles)
- Lattice spacings: a=0.06 fm and a=0.04 fm
- Maximum pion boost momentum: 2.15 GeV on a=0.06 fm ensemble (nz=5) and 2.42 GeV on a=0.04 fm ensemble (nz=5)
- Number of gauge configurations: 100-525 on a=0.06 fm ensemble and 314-564 on a=0.04 fm ensemble, depending on momentum
- Temporal extent in pion mass units: m_pi * L_t = 5.85 on a=0.06 fm and 3.89 on a=0.04 fm
- Wilson-Clover quark mass parameters: m_q a = -0.0388 on a=0.06 fm and m_q a = -0.033 on a=0.04 fm
- Clover coefficients: c_sw = 1.02868 on a=0.06 fm and 1.0336 on a=0.04 fm
- Gaussian smearing radii: 0.312 fm on a=0.06 fm and 0.208 fm on a=0.04 fm
- First excited state (radial excitation pi_1) mass at zero momentum: estimated at 1.46 GeV (PDG value of 1.30 GeV plus 0.16 GeV to account for the 300 MeV pion mass)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous lattice calculation of the valence pion PDF (Izubuchi 2019) by adding a finer lattice spacing (0.04 fm), more than doubling the statistics on the 0.06 fm ensemble, and combining RI-MOM and ratio-based renormalization schemes. The study addresses the ongoing debate about whether the pion valence PDF vanishes as (1-x)^2 or slower as x approaches 1, which has been investigated by various non-perturbative approaches including Dyson-Schwinger equations (Chen 2016, Bednar 2018), light-front holography (de Teramond 2018), and other lattice QCD calculations (Sufian 2019, Sufian 2020, Joo 2019, Lin 2020). The results enable improved comparisons with experimental data analyses (Aicher 2010) and provide a foundation for future continuum extrapolations with additional lattice spacings.
