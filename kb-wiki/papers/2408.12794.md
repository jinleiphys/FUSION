# Refining the isovector component of the Woods-Saxon potential

> We investigate the isovector component in the phenomenological mean field model of nuclei. Lane's isospin dependence, initially proposed for the nuclear optical potential, is reexamined within the context of bound states using the Woods-Saxon potential. We demonstrate that the original parametrization can be reexpressed in terms of parameters associated with the compound nucleus, enhancing its suitability for bound states. Comparisons with the conventional symmetry term are performed to assess how well each approach fits experimental data on single-particle/hole energies and reproduces charge-radius systematics. Our results indicate that Lane's formula provides better accuracy compared with the traditional approach to the nuclear potential. Additionally, we find that the isovector component of the nuclear potential favors a surface-peaked form factor, especially one described by the first derivative of the Fermi like function divided by the radial coordinate. This consideration is crucial for open-shell nuclei where Woods-Saxon eigenfunctions serve as a realistic basis for other many-body methods. Our findings also enable discrimination among various shell-model calculations of the isospin-symmetry breaking correction to superallowed $0^+\rightarrow0^+$ nuclear $β$ decays [I.~S. Towner and J.~C. Hardy, Phys. Rev. C {\bf 77}, 025501 (2008)]. This disparity currently constitutes the main source of theoretical uncertainty in subsequent tests of the standard model.

## Key claim

Lane’s isospin-dependent formula, originally proposed for unbound nucleons, can be re-expressed for bound states using compound-nucleus quantum numbers, yielding an extra attractive term proportional to 3/A that makes it more accurate than the conventional symmetry term, especially for light and N≈Z nuclei.

## Method

The authors re-derived Lane’s formula for bound states by decomposing the total isospin operator of the compound system and expressing the expectation value ⟨t·T_A-1⟩ in terms of the compound nucleus’s N and Z. They then performed least-squares fits of Woods-Saxon potential depths to experimental single-particle and single-hole energies near doubly magic cores, comparing the quality of fits using the conventional symmetry term versus the re-derived Lane formula. They also tested different radial form factors for the isovector component (volume form f(r), surface-peaked forms g(r) and h(r)) and evaluated each model’s ability to reproduce charge-radius systematics.

## Key numbers

* The re-derived Lane formula includes an additional term +κ·3/A compared to the conventional symmetry term, making it more attractive in light nuclei where this term is largest.
* Two data points (ν1d5/2 in ¹⁷O and π1d5/2 in ¹⁷F) deviate from the global Lane-formula trend by more than 5 MeV and were excluded from fits.
* Self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations gave energies of -7.686 MeV for ν1d5/2 in ¹⁷O and -4.0814 MeV for π1d5/2 in ¹⁷F, supporting the exclusion of these experimental points.
* The surface-peaked form factor g(r) (first derivative of the Fermi function divided by r) was found to be favored for the isovector component, especially for open-shell nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the Lane isospin formalism (Lane, 1962) from optical-model reactions to bound-state applications, correcting an earlier derivation by SWV that contained errors except for N=Z cases. It directly compares with the conventional symmetry-term parametrization used in standard Woods-Saxon parameter sets (WS1, WS2, WS3) and in shell-model calculations of the isospin-symmetry breaking correction δ_C for superallowed β decays (Towner and Hardy, 2008). The findings enable discrimination among different shell-model calculations of δ_C, which currently constitutes the main theoretical uncertainty in tests of the CKM matrix unitarity.
