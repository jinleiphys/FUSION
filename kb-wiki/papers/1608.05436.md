# First application of superconducting transition-edge-sensor microcalorimeters to hadronic-atom x-ray spectroscopy

> High-resolution pionic-atom x-ray spectroscopy was performed with an x-ray spectrometer based on a 240-pixel array of superconducting transition-edge-sensor (TES) microcalorimeters at the piM1 beam line of the Paul Scherrer Institute. X-rays emitted by pionic carbon via the 4f->3d transition and the parallel 4d->3p transition were observed with a full-width-at-half-maximum energy resolution of 6.8 eV at 6.4 keV. Measured x-ray energies are consistent with calculated electromagnetic values which considered the strong-interaction effect assessed via the Seki-Masutani potential for the 3p energy level, and favor the electronic population of two filled 1s electrons in the K-shell. Absolute energy calibration with an uncertainty of 0.1 eV was demonstrated under a high-rate hadron beam condition of 1.45 MHz. This is the first application of a TES spectrometer to hadronic-atom x-ray spectroscopy and is an important milestone towards next-generation high-resolution kaonic-atom x-ray spectroscopy.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a 240-pixel superconducting transition-edge-sensor (TES) microcalorimeter array can achieve 6.8 eV FWHM energy resolution at 6.4 keV under a high-rate hadron beam (1.45 MHz), enabling the first observation of the pionic carbon 4d→3p transition and establishing absolute energy calibration uncertainty below 0.1 eV, a necessary step toward future kaonic-atom measurements, though the strong-interaction shift of the 3p level (0.78 eV) is only assessed via the Seki-Masutani potential rather than directly extracted.

## Method
The experiment was performed at the πM1 beamline of the Paul Scherrer Institute using a 240-pixel TES microcalorimeter array with bismuth absorbers, cooled to 75 mK by an adiabatic demagnetization refrigerator. Pions at 173 MeV/c were stopped in a carbon graphite target, and x-rays from π−12C 4f→3d and 4d→3p transitions were detected with time-resolved coincidence using beamline counters. Energy calibration was performed in situ using an x-ray tube source fluorescing chromium and cobalt pieces, with recalibration every 2 hours to mitigate gain drift.

## Key numbers
- Energy resolution: 6.8 eV FWHM at 6.4 keV under a pion beam intensity of 1.45 MHz, with a TES hit rate of 4.8 Hz/pixel (0.4 Hz from the pion beam).
- Absolute energy calibration uncertainty: 0.1 eV, demonstrated under the 1.45 MHz beam condition.
- Timing resolution: 1.2 μs FWHM for the coincidence between pion arrival and x-ray detection.
- π−12C 4f→3d transition energy: 6428.39 ± 0.13 (stat.) ± 0.09 (syst.) eV.
- π−12C 4d→3p transition energy: 6435.76 ± 0.30 (stat.) +0.11/−0.07 (syst.) eV.
- Yield ratio I(4d→3p)/I(4f→3d): 0.30 ± 0.03 (stat.) ± 0.02 (syst.).
- Strong-interaction shift of the 3p level: 0.78 eV (calculated via Seki-Masutani potential); shifts for 4f, 4d, and 3d levels are below 10−4 eV.
- Stopped-π− trigger rate: 34.5 kHz; data accumulated for 13.5 hours.

## Context
This work extends the application of TES microcalorimeters, previously used in astrophysics and laboratory x-ray spectroscopy (Smith 2012, Uhlig 2015), to hadronic-atom x-ray spectroscopy for the first time. It directly addresses the need for improved energy resolution over silicon drift detectors (SDDs), which achieved only ~200 eV FWHM at 6 keV in prior kaonic-helium measurements at KEK-PS (E570) and DAΦNE (SidHe4, SidHe3). The demonstrated 0.1 eV calibration uncertainty and 6.8 eV resolution enable future measurements of kaonic-helium 3d→2p x-rays at J-PARC (E62), where predicted strong-interaction shifts of ~0.2 eV and widths of ~2 eV require resolution far beyond SDD capabilities.
