# Leading and higher twists in the proton polarized structure function at large Bjorken x

> A phenomenological parameterization of the proton polarized structure function has been developed for x > 0.02 using deep inelastic data up to ~ 50 (GeV/c)**2 as well as available experimental results on both photo- and electro-production of proton resonances. According to the new parameterization the generalized Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule is predicted to have a zero-crossing point at Q**2 = 0.16 +/- 0.04 (GeV/c)**2. Then, low-order polarized Nachtmann moments have been estimated and their Q**2-behavior has been investigated in terms of leading and higher twists for Q**2 > 1 (GeV/c)**2. The leading twist has been treated at NLO in the strong coupling constant and the effects of higher orders of the perturbative series have been estimated using soft-gluon resummation techniques. In case of the first moment higher-twist effects are found to be quite small for Q**2 > 1 (GeV/c)**2, and the singlet axial charge has been determined to be a0[10 (GeV/c)**2] = 0.16 +/- 0.09. In case of higher order moments, which are sensitive to the large-x region, higher-twist effects are significantly reduced by the introduction of soft gluon contributions, but they are still relevant at Q**2 ~ few (GeV/c)**2 at variance with the case of the unpolarized transverse structure function of the proton. Our finding suggests that spin-dependent correlations among partons may have more impact than spin-independent ones. As a byproduct, it is also shown that the Bloom-Gilman local duality is strongly violated in the region of polarized electroproduction of the Delta(1232) resonance.

## Key claim

The paper develops a phenomenological parameterization of the proton polarized structure function \(g_1^p(x, Q^2)\) that covers both deep inelastic scattering and resonance regions, and uses it to extract leading and higher twist contributions to polarized Nachtmann moments. The authors find that spin-dependent parton correlations (higher twists) remain relevant at \(Q^2 \sim\) few (GeV/c)² for higher moments, unlike the unpolarized case, but this conclusion depends on including soft-gluon resummation effects which significantly reduce the extracted higher-twist contributions.

## Method

The authors constructed a parameterization of \(g_1^p(x, Q^2)\) for \(x \gtrsim 0.02\) by fitting deep inelastic scattering data up to \(\sim 50\) (GeV/c)² and adding a Breit-Wigner model for proton resonance contributions, with parameters adjusted to reproduce photoproduction data and the Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule at \(Q^2 = 0\). They then computed low-order polarized Nachtmann moments (which remove target-mass corrections) and performed a twist analysis for \(Q^2 \gtrsim 1\) (GeV/c)², treating the leading twist at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling constant and estimating higher-order perturbative effects using soft-gluon resummation techniques.

## Key numbers

- Zero-crossing point of the generalized Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule: \(Q^2 = 0.16 \pm 0.04\) (GeV/c)²
- Singlet axial charge at \(Q^2 = 10\) (GeV/c)²: \(a_0[10\ (\text{GeV}/c)^2] = 0.16 \pm 0.09\)
- Non-singlet axial charge below charm threshold: \(\Delta q^{NS} = 1.095 \pm 0.007\) (derived from \(g_A = 1.2670 \pm 0.0035\) and \(a_8 = 0.579 \pm 0.025\))
- Transition point from Regge to partonic regime: \(Q_R^2 \simeq 4.498\) (GeV/c)²
- Proton Drell-Hearn-Gerasimov sum rule value at \(Q^2 = 0\): \(\int dE_\gamma (\sigma_{3/2} - \sigma_{1/2})/E_\gamma \simeq 204.5\ \mu\text{barn}\)
- Adjusted resonance widths: \(\Gamma_{\Delta(1232)}^{(0)} = 0.10\) GeV (PDG value 0.12 GeV), \(\Gamma_{D_{13}(1520)}^{(0)} = 0.20\) GeV (PDG value 0.13 GeV)
- Adjusted amplitude: \(C_{D_{13}(1520)} = 0.200\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) (PDG value 0.170 GeV\(^{-1/2}\))

## Context

This work extends the twist analysis previously applied to the unpolarized transverse structure function \(F_2^N\) by Ricco et al. (2000) and Simula et al. (2000) to the polarized case of \(g_1^p\), using the same Nachtmann moment formalism and soft-gluon resummation techniques. The finding that spin-dependent higher twists remain relevant at few (GeV/c)² for higher moments, unlike the unpolarized case, suggests that spin-dependent parton correlations may be more significant than spin-independent ones. The parameterization also enables tests of Bloom-Gilman local duality, which the authors find is strongly violated for polarized electroproduction of the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance.
