# The sigma meson from lattice QCD with two-pion interpolating operators

> In this article we describe our studies of the sigma meson, f_0(500), using two-pion correlation functions. We use lattice quantum chromodynamics in the quenched approximation with so-called clover fermions. By working at unphysical pion masses we are able to identify a would-be resonance with mass less than $2 m_π$, and then extrapolate to the physical point. We include the most important annihilation diagram, which is "partially disconnnected" or "single annihilation." Because this diagram is quite expensive to compute, we introduce a somewhat novel technique for the computation of all-to-all diagrams, based on momentum sources and a truncation in momentum space. In practice, we use only ${\bf p}=0$ modes, so the method reduces to wall sources. At the point where the mass of the pion takes its physical value, we find a resonance in the $0^{++}$ two-pion channel with a mass of approximately $609 \pm 80$ MeV, consistent with the expected properties of the sigma meson, given the approximations we are making.

## Key claim

Using quenched lattice QCD with clover fermions at unphysical pion masses, the authors identify a would-be resonance in the $0^{++}$ two-pion channel with mass below $2 m_\pi$, and extrapolate to the physical point to find a mass of approximately $609 \pm 80$ MeV, consistent with the sigma meson $f_0(500)$ given the approximations (quenched approximation, omission of the $C_1$ diagram, and truncation to zero-momentum wall sources).

## Method

The authors computed four-point pseudoscalar correlation functions for $\pi^+\pi^- \to \pi^+\pi^-$ on $10^3 \times 20$ lattices at gauge coupling $\beta=5.45$ using quenched QCD with clover fermions (tree-level $c_{\text{SW}}=1.0$). They included the partially disconnected (single annihilation) diagram $C_2$ but omitted the $C_1$ diagram due to noise, and used Jacobi-smeared wall sources (zero-momentum mode only) to approximate all-to-all propagators. Effective masses were extracted from plateaus in the correlation function at six different bare quark masses, and a linear extrapolation of the lighter-mass regime to the physical pion mass was performed, with the lattice scale set via the Sommer parameter $r_0$.

## Key numbers

* Extrapolated mass of the $0^{++}$ resonance at the physical point: $609 \pm 80$ MeV
* Extrapolated mass from the heavy quark regime (shown to be specious): $1237 \pm 80$ MeV
* Lattice spacing: approximately 0.414 fm and 0.363 fm from two prescriptions (mean used for scale setting, separation used for scale-setting error)
* Number of gauge configurations: 4000-4500 for all but the lightest pion mass; 2000 for the lightest mass
* Lattice volume: $10^3 \times 20$; pion masses satisfy $m_\pi L \geq 4$ for finite volume control
* Sommer parameter $r_0$ taken as 0.5 fm to convert to physical units

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of scalar correlation functions in the $I=0$, $J^{PC}=0^{++}$ channel, many of which omitted annihilation diagrams (e.g., DeTar (1987), Lee (1999), Alford (2000), Prelovsek (2008, 2010), and others). It is methodologically similar to Alford (2000) but includes the partially disconnected (single annihilation) diagram argued by Guo (2013) to be essential. The result enables future lattice studies using Luescher's method to extract the $I=0$, $L=0$ scattering phase shift $\delta_0^0$ for $\pi^+\pi^- \to \pi^+\pi^-$, as recently attempted by Briceno (2016), and provides a lattice-based estimate of the sigma meson mass that can be improved by unquenched calculations and inclusion of the omitted $C_1$ diagram.
