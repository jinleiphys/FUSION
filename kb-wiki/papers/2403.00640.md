# J/psi-pair production at NLL in TMD factorisation at the LHC

> J/psi-pair production at the LHC is currently one of the few tools available to probe gluon transverse momentum distributions (TMDs). In this context, data from LHCb in the collider mode have the potential to probe the evolution of the unpolarised-gluon TMDs and to measure the distribution of the linearly-polarised gluon in unpolarised protons for the first time. In this proceedings contribution, improved predictions obtained for the LHC (at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV) up to next-to-leading logarithm (NLL) in TMD factorisation are presented. We show the obtained predictions of transverse-momentum distributions at different invariant masses and rapidities computed in the LHCb acceptance along with PDF uncertainty. We predict the azimuthal modulations of the cross section that arise from linearly-polarised gluons.

## Key claim

The paper presents improved predictions for J/psi-pair production at the LHC at next-to-leading logarithm (NLL) accuracy in TMD factorisation, showing that the transverse-momentum distributions and azimuthal modulations are sensitive to both the unpolarised and linearly-polarised gluon TMDs, but the results carry large non-perturbative uncertainties and are preliminary.

## Method

The authors compute the hadronic cross section for double-J/psi production in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 13 TeV using TMD factorisation at NLL accuracy, implemented in impact-parameter space with a perturbative Sudakov factor and a Gaussian non-perturbative Sudakov factor. They evaluate the transverse-momentum (q_T) spectra of the J/psi pair at different invariant masses (M_QQ = 6.6, 7.9, 11.0 GeV) and rapidities (y = 2.50, 3.25, 4.00) within the LHCb acceptance, including PDF uncertainty from MSTW2008LO. They also compute the azimuthal modulations <cos(2phi_CS)> and <cos(4phi_CS)> arising from linearly-polarised gluons, binned in cos(theta_CS).

## Key numbers

- The hard scale Q is set to the invariant mass M_QQ of the J/psi pair, with the J/psi mass taken as M_Q = 3.1 GeV.
- The non-perturbative Sudakov parameter A is varied between 0.04 GeV^2 and 0.64 GeV^2, with Q_NP = 1 GeV.
- The b_T,max cutoff is estimated at 1.5 GeV^{-1}.
- The normalised q_T spectrum is shown for M_QQ = 6.6, 7.9, and 11.0 GeV at fixed rapidity y_QQ = 3.25.
- The normalised q_T spectrum is shown for y = 2.50, 3.25, and 4.00 at fixed M_QQ = 8 GeV.
- The <cos(2phi_CS)> modulation is of order a few percent, with the highest contribution in the cos(theta_CS) bin [0.50, 0.75].
- The <cos(4phi_CS)> modulation is of order 1%, with the highest absolute contribution in the cos(theta_CS) bin [0.50, 0.75].
- Experimental values from LHCb (2017 data with DPS subtracted) give <cos(2phi)> = -0.029 +/- 0.050 +/- 0.009 and <cos(4phi)> = -0.087 +/- 0.052 +/- 0.013 over the full cos(theta_CS) region.

## Context

This work extends the TMD evolution formalism for quarkonium-pair production first implemented by Scarpa (2020), now including x and rapidity dependence and PDF uncertainty for the first time. The predictions are compared to LHCb data from 2017 (LHCb, 2016) and to preliminary LHCb results presented at the same conference. The results enable future extractions of the gluon Sivers function from polarised proton collisions, as proposed by Hadjidakis (2021), and motivate improved modelling of the non-perturbative Sudakov factor beyond the simple Gaussian Ansatz.
