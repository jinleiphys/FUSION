# Constraining the low-energy S=-2 meson-baryon interaction with two-particle correlations

> The two-particle correlation technique applied to $K^-Λ$ pairs in pp collisions at LHC recently provided the most precise data on the strangeness $S=-2$ meson-baryon interaction. In this letter, we use for the first time femtoscopic data to constrain the parameters of a low-energy effective QCD Lagrangian. The tuned model delivers new insights on the molecular nature of the $Ξ(1620)$ and $Ξ(1690)$ states. This procedure opens the possibility to determine higher order corrections, directly constraining QCD effective models particularly in the multi-strange and charm sectors.

## Key claim

The authors use femtoscopic correlation data for K⁻Λ pairs in pp collisions at LHC for the first time to constrain the parameters of a next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian in the S=-2 meson-baryon sector, but the fit requires 13 free parameters (LECs and subtraction constants) and the resulting model describes the data with an average deviation of about 1.3 standard deviations across the fitted momentum range.

## Method

The authors fit a unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UχPT) model at next-to-leading order to the measured K⁻Λ correlation function from ALICE in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV. The model includes coupled-channel dynamics across the Ξπ, ΛK̄, ΣK̄, and Ξη channels, with the scattering amplitude obtained by solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation with an on-shell factorization. The fit uses a bootstrap technique with 1000 samplings, varying source parameters and production weights within their uncertainties, and covers the relative momentum range 0 ≤ k* ≤ 500 MeV/c.

## Key numbers

- The subtraction constants extracted from the fit are: a_{Ξπ} = -2.96 ± 0.11, a_{ΛK̄} = -1.87 ± 0.10, a_{ΣK̄} = -1.32 ± 0.02, a_{Ξη} = -2.42 ± 0.03 (at regularization scale μ = 1 GeV)
- The production weights (normalized to K⁻Λ = 1.0) are: ω_{π⁻Ξ⁰} = 1.53, ω_{π⁰Ξ⁻} = 1.58, ω_{K⁻Σ⁰} = 0.68, ω_{K̄⁰Σ⁻} = 0.63, ω_{ηΞ⁻} = 0.18
- The NLO LEC b₀ = -2.997 ± 0.002 GeV⁻¹, roughly one order of magnitude larger than corresponding S=-1 values
- The normalization constant N_D = 1.0015 ± 0.0004
- The average deviation between data and model is about 1.3 standard deviations across the fitted k* range

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Feijoo (2023) who developed the first unitarized effective meson-baryon chiral Lagrangian including NLO contributions in the S=-2 sector, but could not constrain the NLO LECs due to insufficient experimental data. The authors use the ALICE measurement of K⁻Λ correlations (2023) which provided the most precise data on this interaction. This methodology enables future constraints on effective chiral models for multi-strange and charm hadron interactions, where femtoscopic measurements at LHC will be the primary experimental input, particularly with the higher statistics expected from LHC Run 3 and Run 4 and dedicated experiments like ALICE 3.
