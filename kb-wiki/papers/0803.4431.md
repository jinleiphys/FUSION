# $α$-Decay Lifetime in Superheavy Nuclei With $A>282$

> Nuclei with $A>282$ have been studied in the Relativistic Mean Field approach using the force FSU Gold and a zero range pairing interaction. The Euler-Lagrange equations have been solved in the co-ordinate space. Alpha nucleus potential has been constructed with the DDM3Y1 interaction, which has an exponential density dependence, in the double folding model using the nucleon densities in the daughter nucleus and the $α$ particle. Half lives of $α$ decay have been calculated for tunneling of the $α$ particle through the potential barrier in the WKB approximation and assuming a constant preformation probability. The resulting values agree well with experimental measurements.

## Key claim
The authors show that alpha-decay half-lives for superheavy nuclei with A > 282, calculated using microscopic densities from the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) approach with the FSU Gold force and the DDM3Y1 interaction, agree well with experimental measurements, but only after assuming a constant preformation probability of 1.4 × 10⁻² fitted from the data, and using experimental Q-values rather than theoretical ones.

## Method
The authors performed Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) calculations in coordinate space using the FSU Gold Lagrangian density with a zero-range pairing interaction (strength 300 MeV·fm for both protons and neutrons) to obtain nucleon densities for the daughter nuclei. They constructed the alpha-nucleus potential via the double folding model using the DDM3Y1 interaction (with exponential density dependence and parameters C = 0.2845, α = 3.6391, β = 2.9605 fm²) and the theoretical densities of the alpha particle and daughter nucleus. The tunneling probability was calculated in the WKB approximation, the assault frequency from the decay energy, and the half-life was obtained assuming a constant spectroscopic factor of 1.4 × 10⁻² for all decays, with experimental Q-values used instead of theoretical ones.

## Key numbers
- Spectroscopic factor (preformation probability) taken as a constant value of 1.4 × 10⁻² for all decays, fitted from the half-life data.
- Pairing strength set to 300 MeV·fm for both protons and neutrons in the zero-range pairing interaction.
- DDM3Y1 interaction parameters: C = 0.2845, α = 3.6391, β = 2.9605 fm².
- Mass range studied: parent nuclei with 283 ≤ A ≤ 294.
- Half-life agreement spans over four orders of magnitude across the studied nuclei.
- Notable exception: decay of ²⁹⁴118 shows significant departure from experimental measurement.

## Context
This work extends the Super-Asymmetric Fission Model (SAFM) approach to alpha decay in superheavy nuclei by using microscopic densities from RMF calculations with the FSU Gold force, rather than phenomenological density profiles used in earlier work by Mohr (2006) and Roy Chowdhury et al. (2006). It contrasts with the RMF calculations of Gambhir et al. (2005) who used the NL3 force, and with Rashdan (2002) who used RMF to calculate nucleus-nucleus potentials for scattering. The results enable further testing of nuclear density predictions in the superheavy region where experimental scattering data are unavailable, and suggest that spherical shape assumptions are valid for nuclei near N ≈ 172, Z ≈ 114.
