# $J/ψ$ suppression and QCD phase diagram

> QCD phase diagram is obtained by analysing centrality dependence of $J/ψ$ suppression in $\snn$=17.3 Pb+Pb and $\snn$=200 GeV Au+Au collisions. $J/ψ$'s produced in initial interactions are assumed to dissolve if local temperature exceeds a threshold temperature. The threshold temperature depends on the (local) fluid temperature and baryonic chemical potential, which are obtained, under certain assumptions, from experimentally determined quantities e.g. rapidity density, net baryon density. QCD critical line with curvature parameter $κ\approx 0.018-0.23$ is consistent with experimental $J/ψ$ data. $κ$ is factor of $\sim$3 larger than in lattice QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The curvature parameter of the QCD critical line, extracted from experimental J/ψ suppression data, is κ ≈ 0.018–0.023, which is approximately three times larger than the value from lattice QCD calculations (κ ≈ 0.006), but the authors note that their static model neglects the possibility that J/ψ's could drift into hotter regions, meaning their result likely represents a lower limit on κ.

## Method

The authors used a threshold model where J/ψ mesons produced in initial interactions dissolve if the local fluid temperature exceeds a threshold temperature T_J/ψ = K T_c. Local temperature and baryonic chemical potential were obtained from experimentally measured charged particle rapidity density and net baryon rapidity density, converted via a parameterized QGP equation of state that matches lattice QCD at zero chemical potential. The model was fit to centrality-dependent J/ψ suppression data from √s_NN = 17.3 GeV Pb+Pb collisions (NA60) and √s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions (PHENIX), with cold nuclear matter effects accounted for separately using nuclear absorption cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Curvature parameter κ = 0.018–0.023 from J/ψ data, approximately 3 times larger than lattice QCD value κ ≈ 0.006
- J/ψ melting temperature T_J/ψ = (1.57–1.63) T_c, with T_c(μ=0) fixed at 160 MeV
- In √s_NN = 200 GeV Au+Au collisions, best fit threshold temperature T_J/ψ/T_c = 1.60 ± 0.03
- Average initial temperature and chemical potential in 0–5% central collisions: at √s_NN = 17.3 GeV, ⟨T⟩_i = 191.5 MeV, ⟨μ⟩_i = 390.2 MeV; at √s_NN = 200 GeV, ⟨T⟩_i = 242.4 MeV, ⟨μ⟩_i = 23.6 MeV
- Charged particle rapidity density at η=0: 308 (17.3 GeV Pb+Pb), 690 (200 GeV Au+Au); net baryon rapidity density at y=0: 75 (17.3 GeV), 7 (200 GeV)

## Context

This work extends the threshold model approach of Blaizot (2000) and Blaizot (1996) for J/ψ suppression by linking the melting temperature to the QCD phase diagram via the critical temperature T_c(μ). The extracted curvature parameter (κ ≈ 0.018–0.023) is closer to the chemical freeze-out curve from statistical model analyses by Becattini (2005) and Cleymans (2006) than to lattice QCD results from Fodor (2001) and Kaczmarek (2011), which gives κ ≈ 0.006. This result is encouraging for detection of the QCD critical end point because a critical line closer to chemical freeze-out would reduce dilution of CEP signals such as charge fluctuations.
