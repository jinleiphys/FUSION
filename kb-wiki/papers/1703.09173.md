# Bound states of skyrmions and merons near the Lifshitz point

> We study topological defects in anisotropic ferromagnets with competing interactions near the Lifshitz point. We show that skyrmions and bi-merons are stable in a large part of the phase diagram. We calculate skyrmion-skyrmion and meron-meron interactions and show that skyrmions attract each other and form ring-shaped bound states in a zero magnetic field. At the Lifshitz point merons carrying a fractional topological charge become deconfined. These results imply that unusual topological excitations may exist in weakly frustrated magnets with conventional crystal lattices.

# Bound states of skyrmions and merons near the Lifshitz point

## Key claim

Skyrmions in frustrated ferromagnets with easy-axis anisotropy attract each other and form ring-shaped bound states in zero magnetic field, but only when the spin stiffness is positive (on the underfrustrated side of the Lifshitz point) and the skyrmion helicities are opposite; at the Lifshitz point itself, merons carrying fractional topological charge become deconfined, though the deconfinement is incomplete at zero temperature because the meron-meron interaction potential retains a minimum even when the spin stiffness vanishes.

## Method

The authors studied a classical spin model on a square lattice with ferromagnetic nearest-neighbor exchange (J₁) and antiferromagnetic second- and third-neighbor exchanges (J₂, J₃), plus single-ion magnetic anisotropy (K). They mapped the lattice model to a continuum O(3) nonlinear sigma model with fourth-order gradient terms and numerically minimized the energy to find stable skyrmion and meron configurations, calculating interaction potentials and bound-state energies as functions of the frustration parameters and anisotropy.

## Key numbers

- Skyrmion radius scales as R ∼ [b/K]^(1/4), where b is a linear combination of the fourth-order coupling constants b₁ and b₂.
- For the skyrmion ring with large topological charge Q, the energy per skyrmion is E_Q/|Q| = 4π (ρ + √[K(2b₁ + b₂/3)]), where ρ = J₁ − 2J₂ − 4J₃ is the spin stiffness.
- The inverse domain wall width and the spiral wave vector for the ring are both given by κ = |q| = [K/(2b₁ + b₂/3)]^(1/4).
- The domain wall stability condition is 6b₁ + b₂ > 0, which is equivalent to 4J₂ + 16J₃ > J₁.
- For the meron pair, the two-dimensional Coulomb potential that confines vortex to antivortex is U_C(r) = 2πρ ln(r/r₀), which vanishes at the Lifshitz point ρ = 0.

## Context

This work extends the study of skyrmions in frustrated magnets from the strongly frustrated regime (ρ < 0), where oscillatory skyrmion-skyrmion interactions were reported by Leonov (2015) and Lin (2016), to the underfrustrated side (ρ > 0) near the Lifshitz point. It shows that skyrmion attraction and ring formation occur without the oscillatory potential found in the spiral phase. The meron deconfinement mechanism is related to the Kosterlitz-Thouless (1973) vortex unbinding, but with the Coulomb potential vanishing at the Lifshitz point. The square lattice of merons found for large Q is analogous to the simple cubic lattice of half-skyrmions in nuclear physics described by Kugler (1989). The results suggest that these topological excitations may exist in known weakly frustrated materials such as the square-lattice ferromagnets studied by Shannon (2004) and the easy-plane triangular antiferromagnet NiBr₂ reported by Day (1976).
