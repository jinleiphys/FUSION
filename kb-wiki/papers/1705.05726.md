# Spectral shapes of forbidden argon beta decays as background component for rare-event searches

> The spectral shape of the electrons from the two first-forbidden unique beta- decays of Ar-39 and Ar-42 were calculated for the first time to the next-to-leading order. Especially the spectral shape of the Ar-39 decay can be used to characterise this background component for dark matter searches based on argon. Alternatively, due to the low thresholds of these experiments, the spectral shape can be investigated over a wide energy range with high statistics and thus allow a sensitive comparison with the theoretical predictions. This might lead to interesting results for the ratio of the weak vector and axial-vector constants in nuclei.

## Key claim
The spectral shapes of the first-forbidden unique beta decays of Ar-39 and Ar-42 were calculated to next-to-leading order for the first time, but the dependence of the shape on the ratio g_A/g_V is very weak and the uncertainty from the Q-value dominates the theoretical prediction.

## Method
The beta-decay shape factors were computed using next-to-leading-order weak-interaction theory, expanding the nuclear form factors as power series in the kinematic quantities. Nuclear wave functions for the initial and final states were obtained from both the microscopic quasiparticle-phonon model (MQPM) and the nuclear shell model with the sdpfnow effective interaction, using the Bonn one-boson-exchange potential and Woods-Saxon single-particle energies.

## Key numbers
- Ar-39 beta-decay endpoint: 565 +/- 5 keV
- Ar-42 beta-decay endpoint: 599 +/- 6 keV
- Ar-39 half-life: 269 +/- 3 years (Sto65) and 265 +/- 30 years (Zel52)
- Ar-42 half-life: 32.9 +/- 1.1 years (Sto65)
- Specific activity of Ar-39 in natural argon: 1.01 +/- 0.02 +/- 0.08 Bq per kg (Ben07)
- Difference between MQPM and shell-model intensities at low energy: at most 0.5% of the intensity
- The integrated shape factor constant kappa: 6147 s

## Context
This work provides the first next-to-leading-order calculations of the beta spectra for Ar-39 and Ar-42, which are background components for argon-based dark matter experiments such as DEAP-3600 (DEAP Collaboration) and DarkSide-50 (DarkSide Collaboration). The predicted spectra can be compared with data from these experiments and from the GERDA experiment (GERDA Collaboration), which uses liquid argon as a coolant. The high statistics available in these detectors may enable sensitive tests of the weak coupling constant ratio g_A/g_V, extending previous work on forbidden beta-decay shapes by Haaranen (2017) and Kostensalo (2017).
