# Perfect Fluidity in Atomic Physics

> Experimental results obtained at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) have been interpreted in terms of a strongly interacting quark gluon plasma. The strongly interacting plasma is characterized by ``perfect fluidity'', i.e. a ratio of shear viscosity to entropy density that saturates a proposed lower bound. In this contribution we explore the possibility that a similar phenomenon takes place in a strongly coupled non-relativistic Fermi liquid in which the scattering length between the Fermions is infinitely large.

## Key claim
The paper explores whether a strongly coupled non-relativistic Fermi liquid at unitarity (infinite scattering length) exhibits a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio as low as the conjectured universal lower bound of \(\hbar/(4\pi)\), similar to the "perfect fluidity" observed in the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC.

## Method
The author extracts \(\eta/s\) from experimental damping data of the radial breathing mode in a trapped atomic Fermi gas at unitarity, using a hydrodynamic model that relates the damping rate to the volume-integrated shear viscosity. The analysis assumes the shear viscosity is proportional to the entropy density, and uses experimental thermodynamic data for the entropy per particle and the universal parameter \(\xi\) from Green function Monte Carlo calculations.

## Key numbers
- The conjectured universal lower bound for the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is \(\eta/s = \hbar/(4\pi)\).
- For the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma at RHIC, the extracted values are in the range \(\eta/s < 0.5\).
- In the unitarity limit, the universal parameter relating chemical potential to Fermi energy is \(\xi \simeq 0.44\) from Green function Monte Carlo calculations.
- The critical temperature for the superfluid/normal transition in the atomic gas is \(T_c/T_F \simeq 0.29\).
- The extracted \(\eta/s\) for the cold atomic gas in the unitarity limit is roughly \(0.5\), compatible with the conjectured viscosity bound.

## Context
This work extends the concept of "perfect fluidity" from the relativistic quark-gluon plasma studied at RHIC to a non-relativistic strongly coupled Fermi liquid at unitarity, drawing on the theoretical framework of the viscosity bound conjectured by Kovtun et al. (2004) from AdS/CFT duality. The analysis uses experimental damping data from the Duke group (Kinast, 2005) and thermodynamic data from Luo et al. (2006), and builds on prior theoretical work on collective oscillations by Heiselberg (2004), Stringari (2004), and Bulgac (2004). The results enable direct comparison of transport properties between these two very different strongly coupled quantum fluids.
