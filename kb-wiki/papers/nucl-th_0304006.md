# Antikaon production in A+A collisions at SIS energies within an off-shell G-matrix approach

> The production and propagation of antikaons -- described by dynamical spectral functions $A_h(X,\vec{P},M^2)$ as evaluated from a coupled channel $G$-matrix approach -- is studied for nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS energies in comparison to the conventional quasi-particle limit and the available experimental data using off-shell transport theory. We find that the $K^-$ spectra for $^{12}C + ^{12}C$ and $^{58}Ni + ^{58}Ni$ at 1.8 A$\cdot$GeV remain underestimated in the $G$-matrix approach as in the on-shell quasi-particle approximation whereas the preliminary spectra for $Au + Au$ at 1.5 A$\cdot$GeV are well described in both limits. This also holds approximately for the $K^-$ rapidity distributions in semi-central collisions of $Ni+Ni$ at 1.93 A$\cdot$GeV. However, in all limits considered there is no convincing description of all spectra simultaneously. Our off-shell transport calculations, furthermore, demonstrate that the strongest in-medium effects should be found for low antikaon momenta in the center-of-mass frame, since the deceleration of the antikaons in the attractive Coulomb and nuclear potentials and the propagation to the on-shell mass induces a net shift and squeezing of the $K^-$ spectra to the low momentum regime.

# Antikaon production in A+A collisions at SIS energies within an off-shell G-matrix approach

## Key claim

The authors find that K- spectra for 12C+12C and 58Ni+58Ni at 1.8 A·GeV remain underestimated in the G-matrix approach as in the on-shell quasi-particle approximation, while preliminary spectra for Au+Au at 1.5 A·GeV are well described in both limits; however, in all limits considered there is no convincing description of all spectra simultaneously.

## Method

The authors employed an off-shell transport theory based on the Kadanoff-Baym equations, incorporating antikaon spectral functions from a self-consistent coupled-channel G-matrix approach that includes the in-medium modification of pions via ph, Δh, and 2p2h excitations. The G-matrix calculations were performed at a fixed temperature T=70 MeV, and the resulting energy- and momentum-dependent self energies were used to propagate antikaons off-shell in nucleus-nucleus collisions at SIS energies (1-2 A·GeV). The transition probabilities for reactions such as K-N → K-N, K-N → πY, and πY → πY were determined from the same G-matrix approach.

## Key numbers

- K- spectra for 12C+12C and 58Ni+58Ni at 1.8 A·GeV remain underestimated in the G-matrix approach
- Preliminary spectra for Au+Au at 1.5 A·GeV are well described in both the G-matrix and quasi-particle limits
- K- rapidity distributions in semi-central collisions of Ni+Ni at 1.93 A·GeV are approximately described
- The G-matrix calculations were performed at a fixed temperature T=70 MeV, corresponding to an average temperature of fireballs produced in these collisions
- The antikaon potential is approximately linear in nuclear density from 0.5 ρ0 to 2 ρ0
- The K-N threshold in free space corresponds to √s ≈ 1.432 GeV, while thresholds for π0Λ and π0Σ are at 1.25 GeV and 1.332 GeV, respectively
- The Λ(1405) resonance appears 27 MeV below the K-N threshold in free space

## Context

This work extends the off-shell transport approach developed by Cassing (1999) and Cassing (1999b) by incorporating in-medium transition amplitudes from a coupled-channel G-matrix calculation for the strangeness sector, following the approach of Tolos (2001) for antikaon-nucleon interactions. The study contrasts with earlier work using chiral Lagrangians by Lutz (1998) and Oset (2000) that predicted moderate attractive depths of -50 to -80 MeV at normal nuclear matter density, as well as with analyses of kaonic-atom data by Friedman (1994) and Gal that favored strongly attractive potentials of about -200 MeV. The results enable further investigation of in-medium antikaon properties, particularly the prediction that the strongest in-medium effects should be found for low antikaon momenta in the center-of-mass frame due to deceleration in attractive Coulomb and nuclear potentials combined with propagation to the on-shell mass.
