# Scattering phases for meson and baryon resonances on general moving-frame lattices

> A proposal by Lüscher enables one to compute the scattering phases of elastic two-body systems from the energy levels of the lattice Hamiltonian in a finite volume. In this work we generalize the formalism to S--, P-- and D--wave meson and baryon resonances, and general total momenta. Employing nonvanishing momenta has several advantages, among them making a wider range of energy levels accessible on a single lattice volume and shifting the level crossing to smaller values of $m_πL$.

## Key claim

This work generalizes Lüscher's finite-volume formalism for extracting elastic two-body scattering phase shifts from lattice QCD energy levels to S-, P-, and D-wave meson and baryon resonances with arbitrary total momenta, including the specific cases of boost vectors proportional to (0,0,1), (1,1,0), and (1,1,1). The generalization is conditional on the assumption that the two-body interaction has finite range and vanishes outside a region much smaller than the lattice volume.

## Method

The authors derive quantization conditions for two-particle states in a finite cubic volume with periodic boundary conditions, starting from the Helmholtz equation for the center-of-mass wave function in the exterior region where interactions vanish. They construct the Green function and its partial-wave expansion using generalized zeta functions, then reduce the resulting phase shift formulae according to the irreducible representations of the little groups that remain after Lorentz-boosting the cubic lattice. For meson-baryon resonances, the spin-1/2 baryon is incorporated via spin spherical harmonics and Clebsch-Gordan coefficients.

## Key numbers

- For the rho resonance at the physical point, the ground state energy levels for the four lowest momenta cover the resonance region at lattice volumes of m_pi L = 2 to 2.5, assuming an effective range approximation with g_{rho pi pi} = 6.0
- For zero total momentum, the region of practical use is limited to m_pi L less than or approximately 2.5, while nonvanishing total momenta extend this to much larger values
- For the Sigma* (1385) resonance at a pion mass of m_pi = 230 MeV, with g_{Sigma* Lambda pi} = 9.2, the avoided level crossing shifts to much smaller m_pi L values when using nonzero total momentum compared to zero total momentum
- For zero total momentum and O(200) MeV pion masses, baryon resonances like Delta and Sigma* require volumes of L approximately 6 fm for the phase shift to cover the region delta approximately pi/2

## Context

This work extends the original finite-volume formalism of Lüscher (1991) and the generalization to nonzero total momentum by Gottlieb and Rummukainen (1995), as well as subsequent work by Davoudi and Savage (2011), Fu (2011), Feng et al. (2011), and Leskovec and Prelovsek (2012). It provides explicit phase shift formulae for the rho, Delta, and N*(1440) (Roper) resonances, enabling future lattice calculations of elastic and transition form factors of unstable particles such as the rho form factor and the Delta-to-nucleon electromagnetic transition form factors.
