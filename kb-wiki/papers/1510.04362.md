# From strangelets to strange stars: A unified description

> The conventionally separated treatments for strangelets and strange stars are now unified with a more comprehensive theoretical description for objects ranging from strangelets to strange stars. After constraining the model parameter according to the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis and observational mass-radius probability distribution of pulsars, we investigate the properties of this kind of objects. It is found that the energy per baryon decreases monotonously for increasing baryon number and reaches its minimum at the maximum baryon number, corresponding to the most massive strange star. Due to the quark depletion, an electric potential well for negatively charged particles is formed on the surface of the quark part, which may provide some unique observables. For a rotational bare strange star, a magnetic field with the typical strength of pulsars is generated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that strangelets and strange stars can be described in a unified theoretical framework, and that the energy per baryon decreases monotonically with increasing baryon number, reaching its minimum at the maximum baryon number corresponding to the most massive strange star. This result depends on constraining the bag constant B according to the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis and observational mass-radius probability distributions of pulsars.

## Method

The authors treat gravity and electrostatic interactions on the macroscopic scale while incorporating strong and weak interactions locally, using the bag model in the ideal Fermi-gas approximation at zero temperature. The quark-vacuum interface is treated with the multiple reflection expansion (MRE) method, and the internal structure of a spherically symmetric, charged, static strange quark matter sphere is determined by solving the differential equation for chemical potential gradients under thermodynamic equilibrium. The only free model parameter, the bag constant B, is constrained by the Witten-Bodmer hypothesis and by comparing the calculated mass-radius relations with observational data from eight pulsars.

## Key numbers

- The bag constant B is constrained to the range 144.37 < B^{1/4} < 159.26 MeV for strange quark matter to be absolutely stable at zero external pressure.
- For an SQM sphere with core radius R = 1000 fm and B^{1/4} = 152 MeV, the core contains 1.37x10^9 u-quarks, 1.48x10^9 d-quarks, 1.26x10^9 s-quarks, and 2.74x10^5 electrons, with total core charge Q(R) = 2.66x10^5 and mass M = 1.22x10^12 MeV.
- The minimum baryon numbers for absolutely stable strangelets are A_min = 24, 80, and 394849 for B^{1/4} = 145, 152, and 159 MeV respectively.
- In the region 200 fm < R < 1 km, the radius relates to baryon number as R = r_0 A^{1/3} with r_0 = 0.944, 0.901, and 0.862 fm for the three bag constant values.
- The constant surface charge density for R > 10^5 fm is sigma = 0.0144, 0.0135, and 0.0128 fm^{-2} for B^{1/4} = 145, 152, and 159 MeV.
- For a rotating bare strange star, the magnetic field strength approaches approximately 10^12 Gauss at rotational frequency f = 10 Hz.

## Context

This work extends previous separate treatments of strangelets and strange stars by Berger (1987), Madsen (1993), and Alcock (1986) into a unified description. It incorporates charge screening effects and the MRE method for the quark-vacuum interface, building on earlier work by Alford (2006) on electrostatic effects in strange quark matter and by Madsen (2008) on electron-positron pair creation bounds. The results enable more realistic modeling of strange quark matter objects across all baryon numbers and provide predictions for experimental detection of strangelets and for interpreting pulsar observations as possible strange stars.
