# Exclusive vector meson photoproduction in fixed - target collisions at the LHC

> The exclusive $ρ$, $ω$ and $J/Ψ$ photoproduction in fixed - target collisions at the LHC is investigated. We estimate, for the first time, the rapidity and transverse momentum distributions of the vector meson photoproduction in $p He$, $p Ar$, $Pb He$ and $Pb Ar$ fixed - target collisions at the LHC using the STARlight Monte Carlo and present our results for the total cross sections. Predictions for the kinematical range probed by the LHCb detector are also presented. Our results indicate that the experimental analysis of this process in fixed - target collisions at the LHC is feasible. Such future analysis will probe the QCD dynamics in a kinematical range complementary to that studied in the collider mode.

## Key claim
The paper claims that exclusive vector meson photoproduction in fixed-target collisions at the LHC is experimentally feasible, but this feasibility depends on the specific collision system: for PbA collisions the LHCb detector acceptance has only a small impact on total cross sections, while for pA collisions the LHCb requirements significantly reduce the predicted rates because the dominant \(\gamma p\) contribution peaks at very forward rapidities beyond the detector coverage.

## Method
The authors used the STARlight Monte Carlo to compute rapidity and transverse momentum distributions for exclusive \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(J/\Psi\) photoproduction in \(pHe\), \(pAr\), \(PbHe\), and \(PbAr\) fixed-target collisions at LHC energies. The photon flux was calculated using the equivalent photon approximation with a point-like form factor, and the photon-hadron cross sections were described by Regge-inspired parametrizations for proton targets and a classical Glauber approach with generalized vector dominance for nuclear targets. Predictions were made both for the full LHC kinematical range and for the LHCb detector acceptance (\(2 \le \eta \le 5\), \(p_T \ge 0.2\) GeV).

## Key numbers
- Total cross section for \(\rho^0 \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-\) in \(pAr\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=110\) GeV: 318.60 \(\mu\)b (full range), 16.50 \(\mu\)b (LHCb requirements).
- Total cross section for \(\rho^0 \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-\) in \(PbAr\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=69\) GeV: 42.50 mb (full range), 24.50 mb (LHCb requirements).
- Total cross section for \(J/\Psi \rightarrow \mu^+\mu^-\) in \(pAr\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=110\) GeV: 3.88 nb (full range), 0.14 nb (LHCb requirements).
- Total cross section for \(J/\Psi \rightarrow \mu^+\mu^-\) in \(PbAr\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=69\) GeV: 88.67 nb (full range), 39.68 nb (LHCb requirements).
- Total cross section for \(\rho^0 \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-\) in \(pHe\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=110\) GeV: 6.97 \(\mu\)b (full range), 1.09 \(\mu\)b (LHCb requirements).
- Total cross section for \(\rho^0 \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^-\) in \(PbHe\) collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=69\) GeV: 5.60 mb (full range), 2.44 mb (LHCb requirements).
- Typical center-of-mass energies: \(\sqrt{s} \approx 110\) GeV for \(pA\) collisions, \(\sqrt{s} \approx 69\) GeV for \(PbA\) collisions.
- Estimated event rates: approximately \(16 \times 10^9\) \(\rho\) events and 34000 \(J/\Psi\) events per year at expected luminosities of \(\mathcal{O}(100\ \text{nb}^{-1})\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of photon-induced interactions in collider mode at the LHC (e.g., ALICE and LHCb results for ultraperipheral collisions) to the fixed-target configuration proposed by the AFTER@LHC experiment and recently realized by the LHCb Collaboration using the SMOG device for beam-gas collisions. The results complement earlier analyses of exclusive dilepton and \(\eta_c\) photoproduction in fixed-target collisions by Landsberg (2003) and Goncalves and collaborators, and provide first estimates that enable experimental searches for vector meson photoproduction in the low-energy regime (\(\sqrt{s_{\gamma h}} \lesssim 44\) GeV) not probed by previous fixed-target experiments or current colliders.
