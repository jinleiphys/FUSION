# Final state interaction in the reaction NN -> eta NN

> The production of $η$ mesons in $NN$ collisions in the near threshold region is analyzed with special emphasis on the role of final state interaction which is calculated using three-body scattering theory. The three-body aspects are shown to be very important in interpreting experimental results for the total cross section of $pp\toηpp$. Furthermore, energy spectra and angular distributions of the emitted particles are calculated and compared to experimental data.

## Key claim

The three-body treatment of the $\eta NN$ final state interaction is essential for a quantitative description of $\eta$ production in $NN$ collisions near threshold, and the first-order approximation (including only single $\eta N$ and $NN$ rescatterings) is inadequate because the multiple scattering series converges slowly due to a virtual pole in the $\eta NN$ amplitude.

## Method

The authors solved Faddeev equations for the $\eta NN$ system using separable representations of the two-body $t$ matrices for the $\eta N$ and $NN$ interactions, restricting the full three-body solution to the lowest angular momentum states where a relative two-particle $S$ state couples with an $S$ state of the third particle. The driving $NN\to NN^*$ transition potential was modeled via $\pi$, $\rho$, and $\eta$ meson exchange, with the $\rho NN^*$ coupling constant treated as a free parameter normalized to experimental total cross sections at $Q = 36$ MeV. Higher partial waves in the final state were treated perturbatively up to first order in the $\eta N$ and $NN$ scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* Normalization factor $C_n = 0.18$ for the damping of the initial beam, compared to $C_n \approx 0.55$ from Wilkin (2001) and values 0.23 and 0.2 from Nakayama (2002) and Pena (2002).
* At excess energy $Q = 60$ MeV, the $^1S_0s$ configuration provides about 77% of the total cross section for $pp\to\eta pp$, whereas for $\gamma d\to\eta np$ the $S$ wave provides only a 10% fraction at the same energy.
* Ratio of enhancement factors for $T=1$ to $T=0$ transitions reduced from 1.35 to 0.98 at relative $NN$ momentum $p_r = 0.5$ fm$^{-1}$ when the $^3D_1\to^3S_1$ transition is included.
* Two parametrizations of the $\eta N$ interaction give scattering lengths $a_{\eta N} = (0.25+i0.16)$ fm and $a_{\eta N} = (0.52+i0.33)$ fm.
* Total cross section normalized to $\sigma = 5.6$ $\mu$b at $Q = 36$ MeV for $pp\to\eta pp$ (PROMISE-WASA data) and to $\sigma = 35$ $\mu$b for $np\to\eta np$ (CELSIUS data).
* At $Q = 15.5$ MeV, the three-body result normalized to experimental integrated cross section $\sigma = 3.24$ $\mu$b from Mosk (2003).

## Context

This work extends the three-body treatment of $\eta NN$ final state interaction previously applied to $\eta$ photoproduction on the deuteron by Fix and Arenhoevel (2002) to the $NN\to\eta NN$ reaction, where the shorter range of the production mechanism (characteristic radius $R \sim 0.3$ fm versus $R \sim 2$ fm in photoproduction) makes three-body effects even more important. The results contradict the findings of Nakayama (2003), who attributed a broad peak in the $pp$ invariant mass distribution at $Q = 41$ MeV to higher $NN$ partial waves, whereas the present calculation finds only a minor role for $P$ and $D$ waves up to $Q = 60$ MeV. The work enables future experiments to distinguish between competing models of the $NN\to NN^*$ transition mechanism and motivates inclusion of Coulomb effects and additional channels such as $\pi NN$ configurations.
