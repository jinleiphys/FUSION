# Microscopic in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections with improved Pauli blocking effects

> We present updated predictions of effective elastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections intended for use in nucleus-nucleus reactions. A novel characteristic of the present approach combines all microscopic medium effects included in the Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock G-matrix with a Pauli blocking mechanism which is more appropriate for applications in ion-ion reaction models as compared to a previous approach. The effective in-medium cross section is found to be quite sensitive to the description of Pauli blocking in the final configurations.

## Key claim

The paper presents updated predictions of microscopic in-medium elastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections that combine Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) G-matrix medium effects with a Pauli blocking mechanism that accounts for arbitrary directions of the relative momentum of two nucleons in two colliding Fermi spheres, which is more appropriate for ion-ion reaction models than the previous approach of Sammarruca and Krastev (2006) that assumed in-vacuum kinematics for the total momentum.

## Method

The authors start from a one-boson-exchange NN potential and perform a self-consistent DBHF calculation of nuclear matter to obtain the nucleon effective masses and the G-matrix. They then solve the Thompson equation for two nucleons scattering at positive energy in the presence of the medium, using the effective masses and an angle-averaged Pauli operator for intermediate states. The average in-medium cross section is computed by integrating the DBHF differential cross section over all possible directions of the relative momentum of two nucleons in the two colliding Fermi spheres, with the Pauli-allowed solid angle derived from a geometric analysis of the intersection of two excluded cones on a unit sphere.

## Key numbers

- The calculations are performed for incident laboratory energies in the range 20 MeV/nucleon to 300 MeV/nucleon.
- For the comparison of Pauli blocking descriptions, the authors use a representative case with Fermi momenta k_F1 = k_F2 = 1.0 fm^{-1}.
- The in-medium pp and np cross sections are shown for symmetric cases with equal Fermi momenta (k_F1 = k_F2) and asymmetric cases (k_F1 ≠ k_F2), with the cross sections plotted as a function of incident momentum.
- The cross sections calculated with the new Pauli blocking prescription (Eq. sigav) show a sharper rise from zero and a more pronounced peak structure compared to the previous approach (Eq. sigmed), with differences largest at low momenta near the onset of the cross section.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic DBHF calculations of in-medium NN cross sections reported by Sammarruca and Krastev (2006) by implementing a more realistic Pauli blocking geometry that accounts for arbitrary directions of nucleon momenta in two colliding Fermi spheres, rather than assuming the target nucleon is at rest. The improved cross sections are intended for use in Glauber model calculations of nucleus-nucleus reactions at intermediate energies, including direct reactions such as knockout, elastic scattering, charge-exchange, and excitation of giant resonances, as well as in transport simulations based on the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation and quantum molecular dynamics. The authors plan to apply these cross sections in future work to calculate reaction cross sections for stable and unstable nuclei using the eikonal formalism.
