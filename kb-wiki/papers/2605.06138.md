# Probing the density dependence of nuclear symmetry energy through isospin transport in heavy-ion reactions

> The density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy remains one of the key uncertainties in contemporary nuclear physics, with significant implications for the structure of exotic nuclei, the dynamics of heavy-ion collisions, and the properties of astrophysical objects such as neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae. However, extracting robust constraints requires observables that are minimally affected by final-state interactions and are reliably predicted by transport models. This review synthesizes recent theoretical and experimental advancements in constraining the symmetry energy by leveraging isospin diffusion in heavy-ion reactions within the Fermi energy domain. Recent results from the INDRA-FAZIA collaboration, including isospin transport ratio data, and Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model calculations are highlighted. Confidence regions for the symmetry energy are extracted from isospin transport ratios and isospin diffusion currents by utilizing state-of-the-art nuclear functionals, including both ab initio and phenomenological approaches, with a particular focus on the density regions probed by these experiments. The resulting constraints will aid future Bayesian studies of the nuclear equation of state and contribute to a more unified understanding of dense matter in both terrestrial experiments and astrophysical environments.

## Key claim

This review argues that isospin transport ratios (ITR) constructed from the quasiprojectile neutron-to-proton ratio in heavy-ion reactions at Fermi energies provide a more robust and model-independent constraint on the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy than free-nucleon observables, but only when the density region probed by the experiment is determined consistently from the transport dynamics rather than assumed.

## Method

The authors combine experimental data from the INDRA-FAZIA collaboration for the four reactions ⁵⁸,⁶⁴Ni+⁵⁸,⁶⁴Ni at 32 MeV/nucleon with Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model calculations using the BUU@VECC-McGill code. The isospin transport ratio is constructed from the average neutron-to-proton ratio ⟨N/Z⟩ of the quasiprojectile remnant, with impact parameter determined model-independently from charged-particle multiplicity. Theoretical calculations employ a metamodelling approach for the nuclear equation of state that allows using realistic functionals, including both phenomenological Skyrme and relativistic mean-field models and ab initio functionals from chiral effective field theory, with the isovector parameters Esym, Lsym, and Ksym varied systematically.

## Key numbers

- The four reactions studied are ⁵⁸,⁶⁴Ni+⁵⁸,⁶⁴Ni at 32 MeV/nucleon beam energy
- For the SLy5 EoS, the saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1604 fm⁻³, Esym = 32.03 MeV, Lsym = 48.3 MeV, Ksym = -112 MeV
- The relative sensitivity ΔRQP/⟨RQP⟩ to variations in Lsym is 0.62, compared to 0.31 for Esym and 0.48 for Ksym
- The relative sensitivity ΔRfree/⟨Rfree⟩ to Lsym is 0.18, compared to 0.08 for Esym and 0.19 for Ksym
- The transport calculations use Ntest = 100 test particles per nucleon and 500 events per reaction system
- The quasiprojectile freeze-out time is approximately 100 fm/c, with ITR values averaged over t = 150-300 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the pioneering isospin diffusion studies by the MSU group at NSCL (Tsang 2004, Tsang 2009, Tsang 2012) by using direct measurement of the quasiprojectile isotopic composition with FAZIA rather than surrogate observables, and by implementing a model-independent impact parameter reconstruction method (Frankland 2021). The results will enable future Bayesian studies of the nuclear equation of state and contribute to a more unified understanding of dense matter in both terrestrial experiments and astrophysical environments, particularly neutron star properties.
