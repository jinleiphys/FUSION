# Hadronic Observables from Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations

> In these proceedings we present a mini-review on the topic of the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach to the study of relativistic bound-states in physics. In particular, we present a self-contained discussion of their derivation, as well as their truncation such that important symmetries are maintained.

## Key claim

The Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter approach provides a systematic, step-by-step framework for studying relativistic bound states in QCD, where interaction mechanisms beyond the leading Rainbow-Ladder truncation can be included in a controlled manner while preserving important symmetries such as chiral symmetry.

## Method

The authors derive Bethe-Salpeter kernels from the 2PI and 3PI effective actions by taking functional derivatives of the quark self-energy with respect to the quark propagator, which diagrammatically corresponds to cutting quark lines. They show that the meson Bethe-Salpeter kernel is obtained as \(K_{ab;cd}(x,y;x',y') = -\delta\Sigma_{ab}(x,y)/\delta G_{cd}(x',y')\) evaluated at the stationary solution, and extend this procedure to three-body bound states and to the gauging of equations for form factor calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Rainbow-Ladder truncation follows from the 2PI effective action at two-loop order, yielding a self-energy and kernel with bare vertices that are renormalisation-group improved
- The 3PI effective action at three-loop order is required to obtain a non-trivial baryon Bethe-Salpeter kernel
- For a two-fermion bound state with total spin \(J=0\), the covariant decomposition uses Dirac matrices \(\{\mathds{1}, \gamma^\mu, \gamma^{[\mu}\gamma^{\nu]}, \gamma^{[\mu}\gamma^{\nu}\gamma^{\rho]}, \gamma^{[\mu}\gamma^{\nu}\gamma^{\rho}\gamma^{\sigma]}\}\) saturated with relative momentum \(k^\mu\) and positive parity projectors \(D_i = \{\mathds{1}, \slashed{k}\}\)
- For a spin-\(\frac{1}{2}\) baryon, the covariant decomposition uses \(D_i = \{\mathds{1}, \slashed{k}_T, \slashed{q}_t, \slashed{k}_T\slashed{q}_t\}\) with two relative momenta \(k^\mu\) and \(q^\mu\)

## Context

This mini-review extends the work of Fukuda (1987) and Komachiya (1989) on deriving Bethe-Salpeter kernels from the effective action, and contrasts the 2PI and 3PI approaches at three-loop order. The Rainbow-Ladder truncation, which provides accurate results for ground-state pseudoscalar and vector mesons but shows clear deficiencies in other channels, serves as the baseline that this systematic framework aims to improve upon. The formalism enables the inclusion of crossed-ladder exchanges and other interaction mechanisms beyond the leading order, with applications to glueball and tetraquark bound states as next steps.
