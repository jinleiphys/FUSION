# Hyperons analogous to the Λ(1405)

> The low mass of the $Λ(1405)$ hyperon with $j^P = 1/2^-$, which is higher than the ground state $Λ(1116)$ mass by 290 MeV, is difficult to understand in quark models. We analyze the hyperon spectrum in the bound state approach of the Skyrme model that successfully describes both the $Λ(1116)$ and the $Λ(1405)$. This model predicts that several hyperon resonances of the same spin but with opposite parity form parity doublets that have a mass difference of around 300 MeV, which is indeed realized in the observed hyperon spectrum. Furthermore, the existence of the $Ξ(1620)$ and the $Ξ(1690)$ of $j^P=1/2^-$ is predicted by this model. Comments on the $Ω$ baryons and heavy quark baryons are made as well.

## Key claim

The bound state approach of the Skyrme model predicts that several hyperon resonances of the same spin but opposite parity form parity doublets with a mass difference of around 300 MeV, which is realized in the observed hyperon spectrum; the model specifically predicts that the Ξ(1620) and Ξ(1690) with j^P = 1/2^- are analogous states to the Λ(1405), though the Ξ(1620) has only one-star rating in the PDG and lacks experimental confirmation since the early 1980s.

## Method

The hyperon spectrum was analyzed using the bound state approach of the Skyrme model, where hyperons are described as bound states of a soliton and mesons carrying strangeness (kaon and K* vector mesons). The mass formula includes contributions from the soliton mass (order N_c), bound kaon energy (order N_c^0), and hyperfine terms (order 1/N_c), with parameters fitted to known hyperon masses to predict masses of other hyperons. The parity of a hyperon changes when a P-wave kaon is replaced by an S-wave kaon, and the two kaon types can combine to give states with j_m = 0 or j_m = 1.

## Key numbers

- The Λ(1405) mass is 290 MeV above the ground state Λ(1116) mass
- The energy difference ω_2 - ω_1 between S-wave and P-wave kaons was estimated at about 200 MeV in the simple model of Callan and Klebanov (1985), while the empirical value is about 300 MeV
- The model predicts a Ξ(1/2^-) state at about 1618 MeV, with calculated masses of 1616 MeV (1614 MeV with mixing) and 1658 MeV (1660 MeV with mixing) for the two Ξ(1/2^-) states
- The model predicts an Ω(1/2^-) state at 1837 MeV and an Ω(3/2^-) state at 1978 MeV, which are much lower than quark model predictions of around 2 GeV
- The model predicts the Ξ(1950) has j^P = 1/2^+ and the Ξ(2120) has j^P = 3/2^+

## Context

This work extends the bound state approach of the Skyrme model developed by Callan and Klebanov (1985), which successfully describes both the Λ(1116) and Λ(1405) on the same footing. The predictions for Ξ and Ω spectra differ significantly from quark model predictions including those of Chao et al. (CIK, 1981), Capstick and Isgur (CI, 1986), and the Glozman-Riska model (GR, 1996). The model enables experimental tests at current accelerator facilities to resolve the status of the Ξ(1620) and to search for low-mass Ω excited states, and suggests similar patterns may exist in charm and bottom baryon spectra, where the energy difference between positive and negative parity states is again close to 300 MeV as seen in the Λ_c(2286) and Λ_c(2595) system.
