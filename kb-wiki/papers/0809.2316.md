# Nuclear stopping and sideward-flow correlation from 0.35A to 200A GeV

> The correlation between the nuclear stopping and the scale invariant nucleon sideward flow at energies ranging from those available at the GSI heavy ion synchrotron (SIS) to those at the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) is studied within ultrarelativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD). The universal behavior of the two experimental observables for various colliding systems and scale impact parameters are found to be highly correlated with each other. As there is no phase transition mechanism involved in the UrQMD, the correlation may be broken down by the sudden change of the bulk properties of the nuclear matter, such as the formation of quark-gluon plasma (QGP), which can be employed as a QGP phase transition signal in high-energy heavy ion collisions. Furthermore, we also point out that the appearance of a breakdown of the correlation may be a powerful tool for searching for the critical point on the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

Within the UrQMD model (which contains no phase transition mechanism), the nuclear stopping ratio and scale invariant nucleon sideward flow show a universal, highly correlated behavior across beam energies from 0.35A to 200A GeV and for various symmetric colliding systems; the authors propose that a breakdown of this correlation in experimental data could serve as a signal for quark-gluon plasma formation or for locating the QCD critical point.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, which includes hadronic and string degrees of freedom but no phase transition, to simulate symmetric heavy-ion collisions for five pairs of nuclei (40Ca, 58Ni, 96Ru, 129Xe, 197Au) at 30 beam energies from 0.35A to 200A GeV with the cascade equation of state, and at 12 beam energies from 0.35A to 3.9A GeV with the hard equation of state (incompressibility K=380 MeV). They computed the central nuclear stopping ratio R(0) and the scale invariant nucleon sideward flow F~(b0) for scaled impact parameters b0 from 0 to 0.8, then performed logarithmic transformations using a system-dependent normalization factor ρmb (meson-to-baryon ratio per unit volume per passing time) to reveal a universal linear correlation between ln[R(0)/ρmb] and ln[F~(b0)/ρmb].

## Key numbers

* Beam energies per nucleon studied: 30 values from 0.35 to 200 GeV (0.35, 0.5, 0.66, 0.83, 1.0, 1.5, 2.0, 3.0, 4.0, 5.3, 6.6, 8.0, 10.0, 10.93, 11.9, 12.9, 13.93, 15, 16.9, 17.92, 18.95, 20, 24.22, 36.0, 55.0, 76.92, 102.33, 131.33, 163.36, 200.0) for cascade EoS; 12 values from 0.35 to 3.9 GeV for hard EoS.
* Five symmetric colliding systems: 197Au+197Au, 129Xe+129Xe, 96Ru+96Ru, 58Ni+58Ni, 40Ca+40Ca.
* Total of 150 colliding systems (5 nuclei x 30 energies) for cascade EoS; 60 colliding systems (5 nuclei x 12 energies) for hard EoS.
* The hard EoS uses incompressibility K = 380 MeV, applied only for beam energies up to 4A GeV.
* The correlation function C(b0F, b0R) = exp[-m(b0F, b0R)] satisfies 0 < C < 1.

## Context

This work extends the correlation analysis of nuclear stopping and sideward flow from fixed impact parameters (as in a prior correlation study cited by the authors) to a continuous range of scaled impact parameters b0 from 0 to 0.8, using the UrQMD model across SIS/GSI to SPS/CERN energies. The authors propose that the universal correlation they observe (which arises from the variable separable properties of the two observables) could be broken by a first-order QGP phase transition, similar to how the collapse of the excitation function of sideward and elliptic flow was proposed as a phase transition probe in prior work (collape, cited in the text). The approach enables a systematic method to search for the QCD critical point by mapping the beam energies at which the correlation breaks down for different colliding nuclei.
