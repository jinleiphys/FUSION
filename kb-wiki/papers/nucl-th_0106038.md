# Spectral Red-Shift Versus Broadening from Photon and Dilepton Spectra

> We estimate the photon and dilepton emission rates from hot hadronic matter with in-medium spectral shift and broadening of vector mesons. It is observed that both the WA98 photon data and CERES/NA45 dilepton data can be well reproduced with similar initial conditions. The freeze-out condition has been constrained by the transverse mass spectra of pions and protons measured by the NA49 collaboration. We argue that simultaneous measurement of the $p_T$ spectra of single photons as well as invariant mass distribution of dileptons is crucial to understand the in-medium spectral function of the vector mesons.

## Key claim

The WA98 photon data and CERES/NA45 dilepton data can both be well reproduced with similar initial conditions (initial temperature ~200 MeV) only if the hadronic masses are allowed to decrease near the critical temperature (spectral red-shift), whereas a scenario with only broadening of the vector meson width (mass unchanged) fails to reproduce the photon excess in the region 1.5 ≤ pT (GeV) ≤ 2.5, even though it can reproduce the dilepton invariant mass distribution.

## Method

The authors calculated photon and dilepton emission rates from hot hadronic matter using three scenarios: (I) hadronic masses scaling to zero near the critical temperature with vacuum width, (II) vector meson width increasing with temperature while masses remain at vacuum values, and (III) both masses and widths at vacuum values. The space-time evolution was modeled using both (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamics and a transport-model cooling law, with the freeze-out temperature constrained by fitting the NA49 transverse mass spectra of pions and protons. Photon production included prompt hard QCD photons and thermal photons from reactions involving π, ρ, ω, η, a1 mesons and baryonic resonances; dilepton production included π+π− → e+e− and ω → e+e−.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature Ti ~ 200 MeV for central Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 17.3 GeV, obtained for τi = 1 fm/c and effective degeneracy gk ~ 30.
- Freeze-out temperature TF = 120 MeV, determined by fitting NA49 pion and proton mT spectra.
- Hadron multiplicity dN/dy = 725 at impact parameter b ~ 3.2 fm for Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 17.3 GeV.
- Average number of nucleon-nucleon collisions n(b) ~ 660 for the most central WA98 event (b ~ 3.2 fm).
- Photon yield with scenario I (mass reduction) is almost an order of magnitude larger than scenario II (broadening only) at T = 180 MeV.
- The WA98 photon excess in the region 1.5 ≤ pT (GeV) ≤ 2.5 is reproduced by scenario I but not by scenario II.
- Dilepton enhancement around M ~ 0.3 – 0.6 GeV is reproduced by both scenarios I and II when using the cooling law with parameters Ti = 200 MeV, T∞ = 120 MeV, τ = 8 fm/c.

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of in-medium modifications of vector mesons (Brown-Rho scaling, Nambu scaling, chiral model predictions) by comparing both photon and dilepton spectra simultaneously against WA98 and CERES/NA45 data, using freeze-out conditions constrained by NA49 hadron spectra. The authors argue that previous work (Alam et al. 2000, 2001) had shown that a simple hadronic model with vacuum properties is inadequate, and that simultaneous measurement of photon pT spectra and dilepton invariant mass distribution is crucial to distinguish between mass shift and broadening scenarios. The results enable future experiments at RHIC to potentially rule out or restrict one of the scenarios with better statistics.
