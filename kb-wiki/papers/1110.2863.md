# 0++ states in a large-Nc Regge approach

> Scalar-isoscalar states ($J^{PC}=0^{++}$) are discussed within the large-$N_c$ Regge approach. We find that the lightest $f_0(600)$ scalar-isoscalar state fits very well into the pattern of the radial Regge trajectory where the resonance nature of the states is advantageously used. We confirm the obtained mass values from an analysis of the pion and nucleon spin-0 gravitational form factors, recently measured on the lattice. We provide arguments suggesting an alternating meson-glueball pattern of the 0++ states, which is supported by the pseudoscalar-isovector 0+- excited spectrum and asymptotic chiral symmetry. Finally, matching to the OPE requires a fine-tuned mass condition of the vanishing dimension-2 condensate in the Regge approach with infinitely many scalar-isoscalar states.

# 0++ states in a large-Nc Regge approach

## Key claim
The lightest scalar-isoscalar state f0(600) fits into a single radial Regge trajectory with all other 0++ states, provided the resonance half-width is used as the mass uncertainty in the fit; the authors further argue for an alternating meson-glueball pattern among the 0++ states, supported by the pseudoscalar-isovector excited spectrum and asymptotic chiral symmetry, but note that this pattern is "not the only solution" to the matching conditions.

## Method
The authors performed a chi-squared fit of all scalar-isoscalar 0++ states listed in the Particle Data Group tables to a linear radial Regge trajectory of the form M_S(n)^2 = (a/2)n + m_sigma^2, using the half-width of each resonance as the mass uncertainty in the fit. They then compared the resulting mass values to those extracted from an analysis of pion and nucleon spin-0 gravitational form factors measured on the lattice, and examined the operator product expansion (OPE) matching conditions for the trace of the energy-momentum tensor correlator to constrain the infinite Regge spectrum.

## Key numbers
- Fitted Regge slope: a = 1.31(12) GeV^2, corresponding to string tension sqrt(sigma) = 456(21) MeV, which agrees with lattice determinations of sqrt(sigma) = 420 MeV (Kaczmarek 2005)
- Fitted sigma mass from Regge trajectory: m_sigma = 556(127) MeV
- Chi-squared per degree of freedom for the Regge fit: chi^2/DOF = 0.12
- From pion gravitational form factor analysis: m_sigma = 445(32) MeV with valence quark momentum fraction <x>_{u+d}^pi = 0.52(2)
- From nucleon gravitational form factor analysis: m_sigma = 550^{+180}_{-200} MeV with <x>_{u+d}^N = 0.447(14)
- Complex pole mass of sigma from Caprini (2005): m_sigma = 441^{+16}_{-8} MeV with Gamma_sigma = 544^{+18}_{-24} MeV
- Breit-Wigner parameters for sigma: (m_sigma, Gamma_sigma) = (841(5), 820(20)) MeV (Nieves 2011)
- Average width-to-mass ratio for all baryons and mesons in PDG: Gamma/m = 0.12(8)
- Holographic model yields m_sigma = 566 MeV and string tension sigma = 450 MeV when using m_f0 = 980 MeV
- Condition for vanishing dimension-2 condensate requires m_f0 > sqrt(a/2) = 810(40) MeV

## Context
This work extends the large-Nc Regge approach to scalar-isoscalar states, building on earlier Regge trajectory analyses by Anisovich (2000, 2005) who identified two parallel radial trajectories for scalar states. The authors' previous work (Ruiz Arriola 2010, Arriola 2010) showed all 0++ states fit a single trajectory. The alternating meson-glueball pattern extends the observation by Glozman (2003) of degenerate doublets between scalar and pseudoscalar states. The holographic connection relates to the AdS/CFT soft-wall approach (Erdmenger 2007) and the light-front interpretation of Brodsky and de Teramond (2008). The gravitational form factor analysis uses lattice data from the LHPC collaboration (Hagler 2007) and QCDSF collaboration (Gockeler 2003), and is compared to tetraquark probe lattice calculations by Prelovsek (2010). The dimension-2 condensate regularization follows the zeta-function method of Arriola (2006). This work enables future lattice studies at higher quark masses to distinguish whether the sigma state evolves as a meson or glueball, and provides a framework for understanding the fine-tuning required to match Regge spectra with OPE expectations.
