# Pion photo- and electroproduction and the partially-conserved axial current

> The relevance of the axial current for pion production processes off the nucleon with real or virtual photons is revisited. Employing the hypothesis of a partially conserved axial current (PCAC), it is shown that, when all of the relevant contributions are taken into account, PCAC does not provide any additional constraint for threshold production processes that goes beyond the Goldberger-Treiman relation. In particular, it is shown that pion electroproduction processes at threshold cannot be used to extract any information regarding the weak axial form factor. The relationships found in previous investigations are seen to be an accident of the approximations usually made in this context.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to previous claims, the partially-conserved axial current (PCAC) hypothesis does not provide any additional constraint for threshold pion production processes beyond the Goldberger-Treiman relation. Specifically, the author shows that pion electroproduction at threshold cannot be used to extract information about the weak axial form factor, and that previous relationships connecting them were an accident of incomplete approximations.

## Method

The author re-derives the PCAC relations for pion photo- and electroproduction using an operator-level Feynman diagram approach, splitting the axial current into a conserved weak part and a pion-pole-dominated hadronic part. By evaluating the divergence of the axial photoproduction current and carefully tracking all contributions, including contact terms and the interaction current, the author shows that the dependence on the axial form factor \(G_\sca\) cancels completely in the full production amplitude. The analysis is performed without assuming the soft-pion limit \(q\to 0\) before taking the chiral limit \(\mu\to 0\), which the author identifies as the source of previous errors.

## Key numbers

* The Goldberger-Treiman relation \(g_\sca/f_\pi \approx g_{\pi NN}/m\) is satisfied experimentally to better than 10%.
* The Goldberger-Treiman relation at \(t=0\) is given by \(G_\sca(0)/f_\pi = G_t(0)/m\), where \(G_\sca(0) \approx g_\sca\) and \(G_t(0) \approx g_{\pi NN}\) since the pion mass \(\mu\) is small.
* The pion mass \(\mu\) appears in the PCAC constraint equation \((p'-p)_\mu j^\mu_\sca = - (f_\pi \mu^2)/(t-\mu^2) \overline{u}_f \gamma_5 G_t u_i \tau\).

## Context

This work revisits the application of PCAC to pion production processes, which was pioneered by Adler (1965) and extended by de Baenst (1970), Ericson and Rho (1972), Vainshtein and Zakharov (1972), and Scherer and Koch (1991). These earlier works claimed that the axial form factor \(G_\sca\) could be extracted from threshold pion electroproduction data, as attempted by Choi et al. (1993) and Liesenfeld et al. (1999). The present paper contradicts those claims by showing that the dependence on \(G_\sca\) vanishes when all contributions are properly included. The author notes that reconciling this result with findings from chiral perturbation theory (Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner, 1992) remains an open question.
