# Comparison of the extended linear sigma model and chiral perturbation theory

> The pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes are calculated in tree approximation with the use of the extended linear sigma model (ELSM) as well as heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$χ$PT), and the non-relativistic forms of the ELSM results are compared with those of HB$χ$PT. We find that the amplitudes obtained in ELSM do not agree with those derived from the more fundamental effective approach, HB$χ$PT.

## Key claim

The extended linear sigma model (ELSM) fails to reproduce the pion-nucleon scattering amplitudes predicted by heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBχPT) at tree level, specifically because ELSM cannot generate the energy dependence of the isoscalar amplitude or the isovector spin-dependent term, due to the absence of Δ-resonance and ρ-meson degrees of freedom that are subsumed in the low-energy constants of HBχPT.

## Method

The authors calculated tree-level πN scattering amplitudes in both ELSM and HBχPT, then expanded the ELSM results in powers of 1/m_N and 1/m_σ to obtain non-relativistic forms. They decomposed the amplitudes into common parts (̃g^(±), ̃h^(±)) and residual parts (δg^(±), δh^(±)) for each approach, and compared the residual terms to identify which low-energy constants (c₁, c₂, c₃, c₄) of HBχPT could be reproduced by ELSM.

## Key numbers

- The chiral scale Λ_χ ≈ 4πf_π ≈ 1 GeV
- The sigma-meson mass scale m_σ ~ M ~ 1 GeV
- ELSM gives c₃ ~ -M/m_σ² ~ -1 GeV⁻¹ and c₁ ~ -1 GeV⁻¹, consistent with resonance saturation estimates
- ELSM yields c₂ = 0, meaning it fails to generate the energy dependence of g^(+) required by HBχPT
- ELSM yields c₄ = 0, meaning it cannot generate the isovector spin-dependent term in the πN scattering amplitude

## Context

This work extends the comparison of the extended linear sigma model (originally introduced by Nauenberg and Bjorken (1968) and Lee (1968)) with the more fundamental effective field theory approach of HBχPT (Weinberg (1990), Jenkins and Manohar (1991)). The study builds on earlier ELSM calculations by Delorme and Meißner (2000) that reproduced the πN isoscalar scattering length and sigma term, but shows that ELSM fails to capture the energy dependence and spin-dependent structure of πN amplitudes that HBχPT predicts. The results enable future work to incorporate Δ-resonance and ρ-meson degrees of freedom into sigma-model approaches to better approximate chiral effective field theory.
