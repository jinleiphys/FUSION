# Blast Wave Fits to Elliptic Flow Data at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =$ 7.7--2760 GeV

> We present blast wave fits to elliptic flow ($v_{2}(p_{\rm T})$) data in minimum bias collisions from the $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =$ 7.7--200 GeV at RHIC, and 2.76 TeV at LHC. The fits are performed separately for particles and corresponding anti-particles. The mean transverse velocity parameter $β$ shows an energy dependent difference between particles and corresponding anti-particles, which increases as the beam energy decreases. Possible effects of feed down, baryon stopping, anti-particle absorption, and early production times for anti-particles are discussed.

## Key claim

Blast wave fits to elliptic flow data, performed separately for particles and anti-particles, reveal an energy-dependent difference in the mean transverse velocity parameter β between the two groups that increases as the beam energy decreases; the authors discuss possible explanations including feed-down, baryon stopping, anti-particle absorption, and early production times for anti-particles, but note that no feed-down corrections were applied to the fits due to uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed simultaneous blast wave fits to v2(pT) data for identified hadrons (K+, K0s, p, φ, Λ and their anti-particles) from 0%–80% central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 7.7–200 GeV (RHIC) and 0%–60% central Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV (LHC), using a four-parameter blast wave model (kinetic freeze-out temperature T, transverse expansion rapidity ρ0, azimuthal variation amplitude ρa, and source density variation s2). Fits were restricted to pT < 1.2 GeV/c to avoid jet contributions, with the temperature fixed at T = 100, 120, and 140 MeV as input values since spectra were not available for most energies.

## Key numbers

- Transverse expansion velocity β for particles at √sNN = 7.7 GeV: ρ0 = (0.38 ± 0.04) × 10⁻² (corresponding to β = tanh(ρ0))
- Transverse expansion velocity β for anti-particles at √sNN = 7.7 GeV: ρ0 = (0.93 ± 0.14) × 10⁻²
- Transverse expansion velocity β for particles at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: ρ0 = (0.89 ± 0.02) × 10⁻²
- Transverse expansion velocity β for anti-particles at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: ρ0 = (0.89 ± 0.02) × 10⁻²
- ρa parameter for particles at √sNN = 7.7 GeV: (2.73 ± 0.28) × 10⁻²
- ρa parameter for particles at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: (3.02 ± 0.10) × 10⁻²
- s2 parameter for particles at √sNN = 7.7 GeV: (3.13 ± 0.67) × 10⁻²
- s2 parameter for particles at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: (4.62 ± 0.11) × 10⁻²
- χ²/ndf for particle group at √sNN = 39 GeV: up to 35 (1.5 with feed-down contributions included in error bars)
- χ²/ndf for anti-particle group at √sNN = 39 GeV: up to 17 (1.5 with feed-down contributions included)

## Context

This work extends the blast wave model fits previously applied to inclusive charged hadron data at BES energies (HydroV2) to identified particles and anti-particles separately, building on the observation of different v2 values for particles and anti-particles in BES experiments (Adamczyk 2013). The results enable future hybrid hydrodynamic calculations in the BES energy region to incorporate different flow fields for particles and anti-particles, and motivate the planned Beam Energy Scan II program at RHIC (BESII) with increased statistics at energies below √sNN = 20 GeV to distinguish between effects such as anti-particle absorption, early production times, and baryon stopping.
