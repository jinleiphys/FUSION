# Deconstructing 1S0 nucleon-nucleon scattering

> A distorted-wave method is used to analyse nucleon-nucleon scattering in the 1S0 channel. Effects of one-pion exchange are removed from the empirical phase shift to all orders by using a modified effective-range expansion. Two-pion exchange is then subtracted in the distorted-wave Born approximation, with matrix elements taken between scattering waves for the one-pion exchange potential. The residual short-range interaction shows a very rapid energy dependence for kinetic energies above about 100 MeV, suggesting that the breakdown scale of the corresponding effective theory is only 270MeV. This may signal the need to include the Delta resonance as an explicit degree of freedom in order to describe scattering at these energies. An alternative strategy of keeping the cutoff finite to reduce large, but finite, contributions from the long-range forces is also discussed.

## Key claim

After removing both one-pion exchange (to all orders) and two-pion exchange (in distorted-wave Born approximation) from empirical 1S0 nucleon-nucleon phase shifts, the residual short-range interaction shows a very rapid energy dependence for kinetic energies above about 100 MeV, suggesting the breakdown scale of the corresponding effective theory is only 270 MeV rather than the several hundred MeV one might hope for from chiral symmetry scales.

## Method

A distorted-wave method was used to remove one-pion exchange (OPE) from empirical 1S0 phase shifts to all orders by solving the Schrodinger equation with the OPE potential and constructing a modified effective-range expansion. Two-pion exchange (TPE) potentials at orders Q^2 and Q^3, along with the recoil correction to OPE and leading pi-gamma exchange, were then subtracted perturbatively using distorted-wave Born approximation, with matrix elements taken between scattering waves for the OPE potential. The residual short-range interaction was extracted directly from the phase shifts using a delta-shell potential at radius R0, with divergent pieces removed by subtracting zero-energy values.

## Key numbers

- The OPE strength scale lambda_{pi NN} = 290 MeV, derived from 16 pi F_pi^2 / (g_A^2 M_N)
- The breakdown scale of the effective theory after removing OPE and TPE is approximately 270 MeV
- The rapid energy dependence in the residual short-range interaction appears for kinetic energies above about 100 MeV
- The OPE coupling constant used was f_{pi NN}^2 = 0.075 (Nijmegen group preferred value)
- Cutoff radii R0 ranging from 0.1 fm to 1.6 fm were examined, with R0 = 0.1 fm nearly converged to the limiting form
- The most divergent term in the TPE matrix element behaves as 1/R0^5, with subleading divergences proportional to p^2/R0^3 and p^4/R0

## Context

This work extends the distorted-wave effective-range expansion previously applied to 1S0 scattering by Steele and Furnstahl (2000), who noted scales of order 2m_pi in short-distance coefficients and suggested TPE removal might improve convergence. It contrasts with peripheral partial waves studied by Birse and McGovern (2003) and Birse (2007) where scattering is weak and DWBA directly determines effective potentials. The strong energy dependence after TPE subtraction, consistent with observations by Pavon Valderrama (2009), suggests the breakdown scale may be only of order 2m_pi, potentially requiring inclusion of the Delta resonance as an explicit degree of freedom or use of finite cutoffs to improve convergence, as discussed by Beane et al. (2009) for S-wave NN scattering with perturbative OPE.
