# Two-meson cloud contribution to the baryon antidecuplet self-energy

> We study the self-energy of the SU(3) antidecuplet coming from two-meson virtual clouds. Assuming that the exotic Theta+ belongs to an antidecuplet representation with N(1710) as nucleon partner, we derive effective Lagrangians that describe the decay of N(1710) into N pi pi with two pions in s- or p-wave. It is found that the self-energies for all members of the antidecuplet are attractive, and the larger strangeness particle is more bound. From two-meson cloud, we obtain about 20 % of the empirical mass splitting between states with different strangeness.

# Two-meson cloud contribution to the baryon antidecuplet self-energy

## Key claim
The two-meson virtual cloud provides an attractive self-energy for all members of the SU(3) antidecuplet, with the larger-strangeness particle more bound, but the analysis is qualitative due to assumptions and experimental uncertainties; the mechanism accounts for about 20% of the empirical mass splitting between states of different strangeness, not the full splitting.

## Method
The authors constructed SU(3)-symmetric effective Lagrangians coupling the baryon antidecuplet, baryon octet, and two meson octets, with terms for s-wave (L^8s) and p-wave (L^8a, including a vector meson propagator for Nrho decay) interactions. They fixed the coupling constants g^8s = 1.9 and g^8a = 0.32 by fitting the partial decay widths of N(1710) to Npipi (s-wave, isoscalar: 25 MeV; p-wave, isovector: 15 MeV) from the Particle Data Group. The real part of the two-loop self-energy integral was computed with a three-momentum cutoff Lambda in the range 700-800 MeV.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constants: g^8s = 1.9, g^8a = 0.32, fixed from N(1710) partial decay widths of 25 MeV (s-wave) and 15 MeV (p-wave) to Npipi.
- Mass splitting between Theta_10bar and Xi_10bar states from two-meson cloud: 45-60 MeV, depending on cutoff (700 or 800 MeV).
- This splitting is about 20% of the experimental splitting of 320 MeV between Theta(1540) and Xi(1860).
- Input mass for antidecuplet baryon self-energy calculation: p^0 = 1700 MeV (average), with observed masses used for decay widths: M_N_10bar = 1710 MeV, M_Sigma_10bar = 1770 MeV, M_Xi_10bar = 1860 MeV.
- Predicted total three-body decay widths: Sigma(1770) about 24 MeV, Xi(1860) about 2.1 MeV, compatible with experimental total widths of about 70 and 18 MeV respectively.
- Pion decay constant used: f = 93 MeV.

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts to describe the Theta+ pentaquark as a K-pi-N bound state (Bicudo 2003, Kishimoto 2003, Llanes-Estrada 2003, Bicudo 2004), where an attractive interaction was found but insufficient to bind the three-body system. The two-meson cloud contribution provides about 20% of the empirical mass splitting, complementing the 60% naturally provided by constituent strange quark masses and leaving about 20% for quark correlation effects. The results are consistent with the Gell-Mann-Okubo mass formula for the antidecuplet and enable further study of the role of meson clouds in understanding the Theta+ and its antidecuplet partners.
