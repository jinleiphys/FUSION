# Generator Coordinate Truncations

> We investigate the accuracy of several schemes to calculate ground-state correlation energies using the generator coordinate technique. Our test-bed for the study is the $sd$ interacting boson model, equivalent to a 6-level Lipkin-type model. We find that the simplified projection of a triaxial generator coordinate state using the $S_3$ subgroup of the rotation group is not very accurate in the parameter space of the Hamiltonian of interest. On the other hand, a full rotational projection of an axial generator coordinate state gives remarkable accuracy. We also discuss the validity of the simplified treatment using the extended Gaussian overlap approximation (top-GOA), and show that it works reasonably well when the number of boson is four or larger.

## Key claim

The full rotational projection of an axially symmetric generator coordinate state provides remarkably accurate ground-state correlation energies for the sd interacting boson model, while the simplified projection using the S3 subgroup of the rotation group performs poorly across the parameter space studied. The extended Gaussian overlap approximation (top-GOA) works reasonably well when the boson number is four or larger.

## Method

The authors used the sd interacting boson model (IBM), equivalent to a 6-level Lipkin-type model, as a test system to compare several generator coordinate methods for calculating ground-state correlation energies. They computed exact solutions via matrix diagonalization and compared these to results from: full triaxial angular momentum projection, approximate triaxial projection using the S3 subgroup of the octahedral group, full axial projection (setting triaxiality gamma to zero), and the top-GOA approximation applied to the axial projection. The projected energies were minimized with respect to deformation parameters beta and gamma using variation-after-projection (VAP).

## Key numbers

- For N=4, epsilon=1, lambda1=0.5, and lambda2/lambda1 = -sqrt(7)/4: exact ground state energy relative to the pure s^4 configuration is -0.8193; full triaxial projection gives -0.8189 with beta=0.741 and gamma=17.64 degrees; axial projection gives -0.8017 with beta=0.723 and gamma=0.0 degrees.
- The axial projection provides about 80% of the correlation energy at lambda1=1 for the gamma-unstable case (lambda2/lambda1=0), with slightly larger fractions at smaller lambda1 values.
- For axial projection using Gauss-Legendre quadrature, the energy is almost converged at N_rot = 3 rotated wave functions for N=4.
- For triaxial projection, approximately 27 rotated wave functions are required for convergence at N=4.
- The top-GOA approximation requires only one slightly rotated wave function to evaluate second derivatives.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using the random phase approximation (RPA) for correlation energies (Hagino and Bertsch, 2000), which failed near the spherical-to-deformed phase transition point, and their development of the top-GOA method tested on the three-level Lipkin model (Hagino, Reinhard, and Bertsch, 2002). The paper addresses the practical challenge of treating all five quadrupole degrees of freedom in realistic nuclear systems, where full triaxial projection is computationally prohibitive. The results enable systematic calculation of ground-state correlation energies for deformation using axial projection with only a few Slater determinants, providing a path toward a microscopic global theory for nuclear binding energy systematics. The authors note that ambiguities remain in applying energy density functionals to multi-determinantal wave functions, which can be addressed either through the top-GOA approach or by specifying density-dependence in more detail as proposed by Duguet and Bonche (2003).
