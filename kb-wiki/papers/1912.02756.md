# Transverse wobbling in an even-even nucleus

> Two new bands built on the two-quasiparticle $π(h_{11/2})^2$ configuration of the even-even nucleus $^{130}$Ba are investigated using constrained triaxial covariant density functional theory combined with quantum particle rotor model calculations. The energy difference between the two bands, as well as the available electromagnetic transition probabilities $B(M1)_{\textrm{out}}/B(E2)_{\textrm{in}}$ and $B(E2)_{\textrm{out}}/B(E2)_{\rm{in}}$, are well reproduced. The analysis of the angular momentum geometry reveals that the higher band represents transverse wobbling motion of a two-quasiparticle configuration. This is the first example of two-quasiparticle wobbling bands in an even-even nucleus.

# Transverse wobbling in an even-even nucleus

## Key claim

The two newly observed bands S1 and S1′ in the even-even nucleus 130Ba, built on a two-quasiparticle π(h11/2)2 configuration, can be interpreted as the zero- and one-phonon states of transverse wobbling motion, provided the adopted δ values (the smaller χ2 solutions) for the M1/E2 mixing ratios are correct; the larger δ solutions would reinforce the conclusion but cannot be excluded based solely on angular distribution measurements.

## Method

Constrained triaxial covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the PC-PK1 effective interaction was used to determine the nuclear shape, yielding a minimum at (β=0.24, γ=21.5°) for the π(h11/2)2 configuration. Quantum particle rotor model (PRM) calculations with a single-j shell Hamiltonian for the protons and a triaxial rotor parametrized by spin-dependent moments of inertia were then performed to reproduce the energies and electromagnetic transition probabilities of bands S1 and S1′. The angular momentum geometry was analyzed via probability density distributions P(θ,φ) and root-mean-square angular momentum components to characterize the wobbling motion.

## Key numbers

- CDFT minimum for the π(h11/2)2 configuration: β=0.24, γ=21.5°, excitation energy 3.13 MeV relative to the ground state (β=0.23, γ=14°); experimental excitation energy of the I=10 state of band S1 is 3.79 MeV.
- PRM rotor moment of inertia parameters: Θs, Θm, Θl = 1.09, 1.50, 0.65 ℏ2/MeV with spin-dependence parameter c=0.59; fitted ratios Js/Jm/Jl = 0.73/1.00/0.43.
- Experimental mixing ratios δ for transitions from S1′ to S1 range from −0.58 to −0.60 for I=13–21 ℏ; PRM values range from −0.67 to −0.63.
- Experimental B(M1)out/B(E2)in ratios: 0.36 μN2/e2b2 at I=13 ℏ, decreasing to 0.25 μN2/e2b2 at I=19 ℏ; PRM values are larger (1.11 down to 0.67).
- Experimental B(E2)out/B(E2)in ratios: 0.32 at I=13 ℏ to 0.22 at I=19 ℏ; PRM values range from 0.51 to 0.29.
- The s-component of the valence proton angular momentum Jπ is constant at 9.5 ℏ for S1 and 9 ℏ for S1′.

## Context

This work extends the observation of transverse wobbling from odd-A nuclei (e.g., 135Pr studied by Matta (2015) and Sensharma (2019), 105Pd studied by Timar (2019), and Lu isotopes studied by Odegaard (2001), Jensen (2002), and others) to an even-even nucleus for the first time. Previous evidence for wobbling in even-even nuclei such as 112Ru and 114Pd was fragmentary and lacked electromagnetic transition data. The two-quasiparticle configuration in 130Ba provides a more stable transverse wobbling regime than the one-quasiparticle cases, with B(M1)out/B(E2)in about three times larger than in 135Pr and 105Pd. The results enable future searches for multi-phonon wobbling excitations (n=2,3) and tests of the coupling between wobbling and the scissor mode, as suggested by QRPA calculations of Frauendorf (2015).
