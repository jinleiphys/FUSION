# Nuclear spectra from low-energy interactions

> A method to describe spectra starting from nuclear density functionals is explored. The idea is based on postulating an effective Hamiltonian that reproduces the stiffness associated with collective modes. The method defines a simple form of such an effective Hamiltonian and a mapping to go from a density functional to the corresponding Hamiltonian. In order to test the method, the Hamiltonian is constrained using a Skyrme functional and solved with the generator-coordinate method to describe low-lying levels and electromagnetic transitions in $^{48,49,50,52}$Cr and $^{24}$Mg.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to construct an effective Hamiltonian from a nuclear energy density functional (EDF) that can reproduce the stiffness associated with collective modes, but the approach is limited by the approximation of using only a single quadrupole term, which restricts the range of deformations that can be accurately described and causes agreement to deteriorate for larger deformations where more complex shapes become important.

## Method

The authors define an effective Hamiltonian with three components: a spherical single-particle potential taken from a Skyrme functional, a quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and a seniority pairing interaction. The coupling constants are determined by fitting to the Skyrme SLy4 functional's Hartree-Fock energy as a function of deformation and using the uniform model for pairing strengths. The Hamiltonian is then solved using the generator-coordinate method with a basis of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov states generated on a grid of quadrupole deformation parameters, triaxiality, cranking frequency, and pairing strengths, with angular momentum and particle number projection applied to obtain spectra and electromagnetic transitions.

## Key numbers

- The Skyrme-based EDF describes ground-state masses with an error of around 0.7 MeV, as reported in prior work by Scamps (2021)
- The pairing strength parameters use an empirical average gap of \(\tilde{\Delta}_{0}=0.7\times 12/\sqrt{A}\) MeV, with a reduction factor of 0.7 to compensate for particle-number projection effects
- The pairing window is set to \(S=30\) MeV in the spherical basis
- The quadrupole interaction uses a Woods-Saxon diffuseness parameter \(a=0.9\) fm, a spin-orbit strength \(v_{s.o.}=32\) (MeV s)\(^{-2}\), and a central potential depth \(V_{0}=-49.6\) MeV with asymmetry parameter \(\kappa=0.86\)
- The truncation of the canonical basis for overlap calculations uses a criterion \(\sum_i v_i^2 - \sum_i^n v_i^2 < 0.01\)
- Calculations for \(^{48}\)Cr used 12 spherical oscillator shells (\(N_{max}=11\)) and a grid of \(N_{\phi}=191\) HFB vacua

## Context

This work extends the approach previously applied to superheavy nuclei by the same authors (Samark, 2021) and builds on the generator-coordinate method framework developed by Ring and Schuck (1980) and others. The method enables systematic description of low-lying spectra and electromagnetic transitions in medium-mass nuclei from a Skyrme functional without introducing free parameters, and it addresses the challenge of treating density-dependent interactions consistently by including all exchange terms to avoid spurious pole contributions that were identified by Fritz (1998) and others.
