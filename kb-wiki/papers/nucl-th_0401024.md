# Mean field and pairing properties in the crust of neutron stars

> Properties of the matter in the inner crust of a neutron star are investigated in a Hartree-Fock plus BCS approximation employing schematic effective forces of the type of the Skyrme forces. Special attention is paid to differences between a homogenous and inhomogeneous description of the matter distribution. For that purpose self-consistent Hartree Fock calculations are performed in a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell. The results are compared to predictions of corresponding Thomas Fermi calculations. The influence of the shell structure on the formation of pairing correlations in inhomogeneous matter are discussed.

# Mean field and pairing properties in the crust of neutron stars

## Key claim
Shell effects from the formation of quasi-nuclei in the inner crust of neutron stars substantially increase the proton abundance in beta-stable matter beyond what Thomas Fermi calculations predict, but the pairing gap for neutrons near the Fermi energy remains similar to that of homogeneous matter, so major differences in neutron pairing properties are not expected except at very low densities.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations in a spherical Wigner-Seitz cell using a basis of spherical Bessel functions, with Skyrme-type effective forces. For pairing, they solved the BCS equations with a zero-range density-dependent interaction fitted to reproduce CD Bonn pairing gaps, comparing plane-wave BCS in the box to full HF+BCS for inhomogeneous matter. The proton abundance was determined by the beta-equilibrium condition equating neutron, proton, and electron Fermi energies.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of nuclear matter: $\rho_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$
- For a Wigner-Seitz cell with radius $R = 21$ fm, $Z = 20$ protons and $N = 388$ neutrons yield a global density of $\rho = 0.0105$ fm$^{-3}$ and proton abundance $Y_p = 0.049$
- The energy per nucleon for this inhomogeneous HF solution is about 2 MeV per nucleon below the homogeneous beta-equilibrium energy
- At $\rho \approx 0.01$ fm$^{-3}$, the HF solution with localized quasi-nuclei yields about 3 MeV per nucleon below homogeneous matter; this gain decreases to around 1 MeV per nucleon at $\rho = 0.04$ fm$^{-3}$
- The gap function $\Delta(r)$ for plane-wave BCS at $\rho = 0.02$ fm$^{-3}$ fluctuates around 1.8 MeV
- The pairing gap in the region of quasi-nuclei is suppressed by about 25 percent compared to the homogeneous value
- Deeply bound single-particle states in the inhomogeneous system reach -42 MeV compared to -9.5 MeV in the homogeneous calculation

## Context
This work extends Thomas Fermi calculations of inhomogeneous baryonic matter in neutron star crusts (Oyamatsu 1993, Shen 1998, Watanabe 2000) by including shell effects through full Hartree-Fock treatment. It contrasts with homogeneous BCS gap calculations (Baldo 1998, Elgaroy 1996, Kuckei 2003) by examining how quasi-nuclei modify pairing locally. The results enable more refined models of neutron star cooling and glitch formation, though the authors note that a Cartesian basis would be needed to study non-spherical structures like rods or slabs.
