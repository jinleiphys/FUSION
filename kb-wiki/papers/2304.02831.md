# Electric-charge-dependent directed flow splitting of produced quarks in Au+Au collisions

> We report directed flow ($v_1$) of multistrange baryons ($Ξ$ and $Ω$) and improved $v_1$ data for $K^{-}$, $\bar{p}$, $\barΛ$ and $ϕ$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}=$27 and 200 GeV from the STAR experiment at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). We focus on particles whose constituent quarks are not transported from the incoming nuclei but instead are produced in the collisions. At intermediate impact parameters, we examine quark coalescence behavior for particle combinations with identical quark content, and search for any departure from this behavior (``splitting'') for combinations having non-identical quark content. Under the assumption of quark coalescence for produced quarks, the splitting strength appears to increase with the electric charge difference of the constituent quarks in the combinations, consistent with electromagnetic effect expectations.

## Key claim

Under the assumption of quark coalescence for produced quarks, the splitting of directed flow (v1) between hadron combinations with non-identical quark content appears to increase with the electric charge difference of the constituent quarks, consistent with electromagnetic effect expectations, though peripheral collision statistics are insufficient to draw firm conclusions.

## Method

The STAR experiment measured directed flow (v1) of multistrange baryons (Xi and Omega) and improved v1 data for K-, antiproton, anti-Lambda and phi mesons in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 and 200 GeV. The analysis focused on particles whose constituent quarks are produced in the collisions rather than transported from the incoming nuclei, examining quark coalescence behavior for hadron combinations with identical quark content and searching for deviations ("splitting") for combinations with non-identical quark content at intermediate impact parameters.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow (v1) measured for Xi and Omega baryons, and improved v1 data for K-, antiproton, anti-Lambda and phi mesons in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 27 and 200 GeV
- Splitting strength observed to increase with electric charge difference of constituent quarks in hadron combinations, consistent with electromagnetic effect expectations
- Peripheral collision statistics at both beam energies were insufficient to draw firm conclusions about the splitting behavior

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of directed flow for identified hadrons by including multistrange baryons (Xi and Omega) whose constituent quarks are entirely produced in the collision, avoiding complications from transported quarks. The results provide experimental constraints for theoretical models of electromagnetic field effects in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the expected charge-dependent splitting of directed flow from strong early-time electromagnetic fields produced at RHIC. The study enables future investigations of electromagnetic field-driven effects at RHIC energies, though the peripheral collision statistics remain a limitation.
