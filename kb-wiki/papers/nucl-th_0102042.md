# Continuum QRPA in the coordinate space representation

> We formulate a quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) in the coordinate space representation. This model is a natural extension of the RPA model of Shlomo and Bertsch to open-shell nuclei in order to take into account pairing correlations together with the coupling to the continuum. We apply it to the $^{120}$Sn nucleus and show that low-lying excitation modes are significantly influenced by the pairing effects although the effects are marginal in the giant resonance region. The dependence of the pairing effect on the parity of low-lying collective mode is also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper formulates a quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) in coordinate space that extends the continuum RPA method of Shlomo and Bertsch (1975) to open-shell nuclei by including pairing correlations, but the authors restrict the BCS approximation to cases where all states in the pairing active space are bound, leaving self-consistent treatment of pairing in the continuum for future work.

## Method

The authors generalize the response function formalism of RPA to QRPA by constructing a free response function that separates excitations within the pairing active space (treated with explicit two-quasiparticle configurations) from excitations between the active space and the continuum (treated with single-particle Green functions). The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the QRPA response function is solved in coordinate space using a zero-range Skyrme-type residual interaction, with the single-particle basis obtained from a Woods-Saxon mean-field potential. The method is applied to the nucleus 120Sn using a constant neutron pairing gap of 1.392 MeV and a proton pairing gap of zero due to the Z=50 magic number.

## Key numbers

- Neutron pairing gap: Delta = 1.392 MeV, estimated from experimental binding energies of neighboring nuclei
- Renormalization factors for the residual interaction: kappa = 0.638 for RPA and 0.660 for QRPA, adjusted so the spurious isoscalar dipole mode appears at zero excitation energy
- Lowest 2+ state energy: 5.2 MeV (RPA), 2.3 MeV (QRPA), compared to experimental value of 1.17 MeV
- B(E2) for lowest 2+ state: 0.031 e^2b^2 (RPA), 0.107 e^2b^2 (QRPA), compared to experimental value of 0.2 e^2b^2
- Lowest 3- state energy: 1.4 MeV (RPA), 3.0 MeV (QRPA), compared to experimental value of 2.4 MeV
- B(E3) for lowest 3- state: 0.159 e^2b^3 (RPA), 0.0771 e^2b^3 (QRPA), compared to experimental value of 0.09 e^2b^3
- QRPA second lowest 0+ state appears at 2.9 MeV, attributed to a pairing vibration mode
- Neutron Fermi energy for 120Sn: lambda_n = -8.149 MeV
- Single-particle energies for valence neutron levels: 1g7/2 at -11.184 MeV, 2d5/2 at -11.145 MeV, 2d3/2 at -9.280 MeV, 3s1/2 at -9.048 MeV, 1h11/2 at -6.949 MeV

## Context

This work extends the continuum RPA formalism of Shlomo and Bertsch (1975) to include pairing correlations via QRPA, building on earlier response function approaches by Bertsch and Tsai (1975) and applications to neutron-rich nuclei by Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang (1997, 1998, 1999). The method is closely related to the approach of Kamerdzhiev, Liotta, Litvinova, and Tselyaev (1998) but differs in details. The results enable future applications to neutrino-nucleus scattering and excitations of drip-line nuclei, though the authors note that a more sophisticated treatment using Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory would be needed for cases where the pairing active space includes continuum states.
