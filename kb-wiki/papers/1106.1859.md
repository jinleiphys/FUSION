# Properties of the partonic phase at RHIC within PHSD

> The dynamics of partons, hadrons and strings in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is analyzed within the novel Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model for partons (DQPM) matched to reproduce recent lattice-QCD results -- including the partonic equation of state -- in thermodynamic equilibrium. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is described by covariant transition rates for the fusion of quark-antiquark pairs or three quarks (antiquarks), respectively, obeying flavor current-conservation, color neutrality as well as energy-momentum conservation. In order to explore the space-time regions of 'partonic matter' the PHSD approach is applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions from SPS to RHIC energies. Detailed comparisons are presented for hadronic rapidity spectra and transverse mass distributions. The traces of partonic interactions are found in particular in the elliptic flow of hadrons as well as in an approximate quark-number scaling at the top RHIC energy.

## Key claim

The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which includes explicit partonic degrees of freedom and dynamical hadronization via covariant transition rates, reproduces hadron spectra and elliptic flow at RHIC energies and yields approximate quark-number scaling of elliptic flow, but the momentum independence of the quasiparticle width and mass is a rough approximation that will need refinement with higher statistics.

## Method

The authors apply the PHSD transport approach, based on the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) matched to lattice QCD results, to nucleus-nucleus collisions from SPS to RHIC energies. The transition from partonic to hadronic degrees of freedom is described by covariant transition rates for the fusion of quark-antiquark pairs or three quarks (antiquarks) into color-neutral hadronic resonances or strings, obeying flavor current conservation, color neutrality, and energy-momentum conservation. Results for hadronic rapidity spectra, transverse mass distributions, and elliptic flow are compared to experimental data from the NA49, PHENIX, STAR, BRAHMS, and PHOBOS Collaborations, as well as to calculations from the purely hadronic HSD model.

## Key numbers

- For 5% central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, the PHSD calculations show the elliptic flow v2 as a function of centrality (number of participating nucleons Npart) compares well to PHOBOS data for charged particles in the pseudorapidity window |eta| <= 1.
- The width parameter b for the pre-hadron phase-space distribution is fixed by sqrt(<r^2>) = b = 0.66 fm, corresponding to an average rms radius of mesons.
- The elliptic flow v2 for mesons is slightly underestimated for pT > 2 GeV in PHSD, while the proton (and antiproton) elliptic flow is slightly overestimated at low transverse momenta pT < 1.5 GeV.
- For KE_T > 0.5 GeV, the scaled elliptic flow v2/nq tends to underestimate experimental measurements for Lambda, Sigma, anti-Lambda, and anti-Sigma baryons.

## Context

This work extends the PHSD approach previously presented in CaBra09 and PHSD-RHIC, building on the DQPM framework from Cassing (2006, 2007) that fits lattice QCD results. The approach addresses the entropy problem identified in coalescence models by Ko (2001) and the AMPT model, where fusion of massless partons would violate the second law of thermodynamics. The results support the early suggestion by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (statistical hadronization model) that enhanced formation of strange antibaryons occurs via hadronization, and confirm the earlier suggestion by Bratkovskaya (2004) that the underestimation of kaon slopes by HSD and UrQMD was a signature for missing partonic degrees of freedom. The findings imply that neither purely hadronic nor purely partonic models can be used to extract physical conclusions when comparing to data, due to the presence of a "hadronic corona" even at top SPS energies.
