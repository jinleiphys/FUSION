# Initial state azimuthal anisotropies in small collision systems

> Strong multiparticle azimuthal correlations have recently been observed in high energy proton-nucleus collisions. While final state collective effects can be responsible for many of the observations, the domain structure in the classical color field of a high energy nucleus also naturally leads to such correlations. We describe recent calculations of the momentum space 2-particle cumulant azimuthal anisotropy coefficients v_n{2}, n=2,3,4 from fundamental representation Wilson line distributions describing the high energy nucleus. We find significant differences between Wilson lines from the MV model and from JIMWLK evolution. We also discuss the relation of this calculation to earlier work on the ridge correlation obtained in the "glasma graph" approximation, and to the "color electric field domain model."

## Key claim

Initial-state color field domain structure in a high-energy nucleus can generate significant azimuthal anisotropies directly in momentum space, even for a completely isotropic and homogeneous target, and these correlations are relatively stronger in smaller systems, contrary to the hydrodynamical explanation.

## Method

The calculation uses the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) formalism in the forward dilute-dense limit, where collinear partons from a dilute probe pass eikonally through the target color field and receive transverse momentum kicks described by fundamental representation Wilson lines. The two-particle cumulant anisotropy coefficients v_n{2} for n=2,3,4 are computed by Fourier-decomposing the two-particle correlation function obtained from squaring the single-particle spectrum, with the probe particles localized by an explicit Gaussian weight function representing their Wigner distribution. The target color field expectation values are evaluated using two different probability distributions for the Wilson lines: the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model of independent Gaussian-distributed color charges, and the result from leading-order running coupling JIMWLK nonlinear renormalization group evolution.

## Key numbers

- v_2{2}, v_3{2}, and v_4{2} coefficients are computed as functions of transverse momentum, rising up to a few times Q_s and then decreasing, with v_4 peaking at a larger momentum than v_2
- The parametric estimate for the correlation strength is ~ 1/(N_c^2 Q_s^2 S_perp), where N_c is the number of colors, Q_s is the saturation scale, and S_perp is the area of the probe
- The "nonlinear Gaussian" approximation (Gaussian distribution of color charges with full nonlinear treatment of the Wilson line exponential) is accurate to within at least 10% for the azimuthal harmonics
- The glasma graph approximation deviates from the full nonlinear JIMWLK result by a factor of ~2 for v_2 in the MV model

## Context

This work extends the CGC-based understanding of azimuthal correlations from color field domains, relating the calculation to earlier "glasma graph" approximation studies of the ridge correlation by Dumitru et al. (2010) and Dusling et al. (2012, 2013), and to the "color electric field domain model" proposed by Dumitru et al. (2014). The results show significant differences between the MV model and JIMWLK-evolved distributions, indicating a strong dependence on the details of the transverse momentum distribution of gluons in the target. This enables future work incorporating hadronization effects for more detailed comparison with experimental data and studying the rapidity structure of the correlations.
