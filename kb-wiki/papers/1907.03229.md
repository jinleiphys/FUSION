# Cross sections for 2-to-3 meson-meson scattering

> We study 2-to-3 meson-meson scattering based on the process that a gluon is created from a constituent quark or antiquark and subsequently the gluon creates a quark-antiquark pair. The transition potential for the process is derived in QCD. Eight Feynman diagrams at tree level are involved in the 2-to-3 meson-meson scattering. Starting from the $S$-matrix element, we derive the unpolarized cross section from the eight transition amplitudes corresponding to the eight Feynman diagrams. The transition amplitudes contain color, spin, and flavor matrix elements. The 2-to-3 meson-meson scattering includes $ππ\to πK\bar{K}$, $πK \to ππK$, $πK \to KK\bar{K}$, $KK \to πKK$, and $K\bar{K} \to πK\bar{K}$. Cross sections for the reactions are calculated. The cross sections depend on temperature obviously, and the cross section for $πK \to ππK$ for total isospin $I=3/2$ at zero temperature is compared to experimental data. By comparison with inelastic 2-to-2 meson-meson scattering, we find that 2-to-3 meson-meson scattering may be as important as inelastic 2-to-2 meson-meson scattering.

## Key claim

The authors claim that 2-to-3 meson-meson scattering may be as important as inelastic 2-to-2 meson-meson scattering, based on a comparison of calculated cross sections, but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the comparison is made only for specific channels and the 2-to-3 cross sections depend strongly on temperature.

## Method

The authors derive a transition potential in QCD for the process where a gluon is created from a constituent quark or antiquark and subsequently splits into a quark-antiquark pair. They construct eight tree-level Feynman diagrams for 2-to-3 meson-meson scattering, compute the corresponding transition amplitudes containing color, spin, and flavor matrix elements, and derive the unpolarized cross section from the S-matrix element. The cross sections are calculated for five specific reactions: ππ → πK̄K, πK → ππK, πK → KK̄K, KK → πKK, and K̄K → πK̄K.

## Key numbers

- The cross section for elastic ππ scattering with total isospin I=2 can go up to 12 mb.
- The cross section for elastic πK scattering with total isospin I=3/2 may become as large as 4.6 mb.
- The elastic cross section for K⁺K⁻ scattering obtained from π⁺p → K⁺K⁻Δ⁺⁺ decreases from 3.8 mb at 1 GeV to 2.9 mb at 1.38 GeV.
- The cross section for πK → ππK for total isospin I=3/2 at zero temperature is compared to experimental data (no numerical value for this cross section is given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of inelastic 2-to-2 meson-meson scattering, such as those by Li, De, H. S., et al. (2000) and by B. K. W. X. (2002) which used effective meson Lagrangians for reactions like ππ → K̄K and ρρ → K̄K. It also builds on the authors' own previous work (Y. X. W., 2005) on 2-to-1 meson-meson scattering via quark-antiquark annihilation into a gluon. The study is motivated by the need to understand chemical equilibrium in hadronic matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where meson momenta up to 1000 GeV/c have been measured by the ATLAS, CMS, and ALICE collaborations.
