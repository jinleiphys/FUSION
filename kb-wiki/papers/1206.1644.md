# The BCS-Bogoliubov and the su(2)-Algebraic Approach to the Pairing Model in Many-Fermion System

> The relation between two approaches to the su(2)-algebraic many-fermion model is discussed: (1) the BCS-Bogoliubov approach in terms of the use of the quasiparticles representing all the degrees of freedom except those related to the Cooper-pairs and (2) the conventional algebraic approach in terms of the use of the minimum weight states, from which the Cooper-pairs are excluded. In order to arrive at the goal, the idea of the quasiparticles is brought up in the conservation of the fermion number. Under the c-number replacement for the three su(2)-generators, the quasiparticles suggested in this paper are reduced to those in the BCS-Bogoliubov approach. It is also shown that the two approaches are equivalent through the c-number replacement. Further, a certain modification of the BCS-Bogoliubov approach is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the conventional su(2)-algebraic approach to the pairing model (approach A), which conserves fermion number, is equivalent to the BCS-Bogoliubov approach (approach B), which does not conserve fermion number, but only under the condition that the three su(2)-generators are replaced with c-numbers. This equivalence is established through an intermediate approach (approach C) that defines quasiparticles within the framework of fermion number conservation.

## Method

The authors construct a new set of quasiparticle operators (approach C) that are eigenstates of the fermion number operator, unlike the standard BCS-Bogoliubov quasiparticles. They show that under a c-number replacement of the su(2)-generators S_±,0, where the operators are replaced by their expectation values in the condensed Cooper-pair state, these new quasiparticles reduce to the standard BCS-Bogoliubov quasiparticles. The minimum weight state of the su(2)-algebraic approach is then expressed in terms of these new quasiparticles, and the orthogonal basis states are rewritten to reverse the order of Cooper-pair and quasiparticle operators, after which the c-number replacement yields the BCS-Bogoliubov basis.

## Key numbers

- The single-particle states are confined to 4Ω₀ states, where Ω₀ denotes an integer or half-integer.
- The condensed Cooper-pair state parameters u and v obey u² + |v|² = 1.
- The expectation values of the su(2)-generators in the condensed Cooper-pair state are S₊ = 2Ω₀uv*, S₋ = 2Ω₀uv, and S₀ = Ω₀(|v|² − u²).
- The minimum weight state has seniority number n = 2(p + q + r), where p, q, and r are non-negative integers or half-integers.
- The Cooper-pair state in approach C is |φ_σ⟩ = (S̃₊)^σ |0⟩ with σ = 0, 1, 2, …, 2Ω₀.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous two papers (referred to collectively as (I)) on the su(2)-algebraic many-fermion model, which provided a systematic method for determining the minimum weight state. The paper connects the conventional algebraic approach (approach A) with the BCS-Bogoliubov theory developed by Bardeen, Cooper and Schrieffer (1957) and applied to nuclear physics by Belyaev (1959), Bohr, Mottelson and Pines (1958), and others. It also discusses a modification of the BCS-Bogoliubov approach proposed by Suzuki and Matsuyanagi and later by Kuriyama and Yamamura. This work enables a clearer understanding of the relationship between number-conserving and number-non-conserving treatments of pairing correlations in nuclear structure.
