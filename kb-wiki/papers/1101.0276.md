# Symmetries, Supersymmetries, and Pairing in Nuclei

> These summer school lectures cover the use of algebraic techniques in various subfields of nuclear physics. After a brief description of groups and algebras, concepts of dynamical symmetry, dynamical supersymmetry, and supersymmetric quantum mechanics are introduced. Appropriate tools such as quasiparticles, quasispin, and Bogoliubov transformations are discussed with an emphasis on group theoretical foundations of these tools. To illustrate these concepts three physics applications are worked out in some detail: i) Pairing in nuclear physics; ii) Subbarrier fusion and associated group transformations; and iii) Symmetries of neutrino mass and of a related neutrino many-body problem.

## Key claim

These summer school lectures demonstrate that algebraic and group-theoretical methods, including dynamical symmetries, supersymmetries, and Gaudin algebras, provide a unified framework for solving exactly solvable pairing Hamiltonians in nuclear physics, with the caveat that the presented exact solutions are limited to specific cases such as monopole (s-wave) pairing, separable pairing, or two-level systems.

## Method

The lectures first introduce Lie groups, Lie algebras, and their bosonic and fermionic Fock-space realizations, then develop quasispin algebras and Bogoliubov transformations as SU(2) group operations. Dynamical symmetries are defined by writing Hamiltonians as sums of Casimir operators of a group chain, and supersymmetries are introduced via superalgebras (e.g., Osp(1/2)) and supersymmetric quantum mechanics with partner Hamiltonians H₁ = G†G and H₂ = GG†. The Gaudin algebra, an infinite-dimensional algebra with a spectral parameter λ, is realized using quasispin operators and connected to the Richardson solution of the pairing Hamiltonian through the Bethe ansatz.

## Key numbers

- For the SU(2) boson realization, the quadratic Casimir operator yields j(j+1) = (N/2)(N/2+1), where N is the total boson number.
- In the Nilsson Hamiltonian H = ω b_i† b_i – 2k L·S – kμ L², fits to data suggest μ ≈ 0.5, leading to near degeneracies in the 50–82 shell (principal quantum number N=4).
- The pairing Hamiltonian with separable pairing, Ĥ = –|G| Σ_{jj'} c*_j c_{j'} Ŝ⁺_j Ŝ⁻_{j'}, has eigenstates of the form Ŝ⁺(0)Ŝ⁺(z₁)…Ŝ⁺(z_{N-1})|0⟩ with energy E_N = –|G|( Σ_j Ω_j |c_j|² – Σ_{k=1}^{N-1} 2/z_k ), where Ω_j = j+1/2 is the maximum number of pairs in level j.
- For the two-level pairing Hamiltonian with single-particle energies ε_j, the eigenstates are constructed using operators 𝒥⁺(x) = Σ_j (c*_j / (2ε_j – |c_j|² x)) Ŝ⁺_j.

## Context

These lectures extend the algebraic approach pioneered by Racah (1940s) for pairing in LS-coupling and jj-coupling schemes, and by Kerman (1961) for the exact quasispin limit of pairing. The work connects the Gaudin algebra (Gaudin, 1976) to Richardson's exact solution of the pairing Hamiltonian (Richardson, 1963) and to the Interacting Boson Model of Arima and Iachello (1975–1979). The supersymmetric quantum mechanics framework of Witten (1981) is applied to relate the SU(3) shell model to pseudo-SU(3) symmetry, following Arima, Harvey, and Hecht (1969). These methods enable exact solutions for monopole and separable pairing models and open the door to studying collective neutrino oscillations and subbarrier fusion through similar group-theoretical techniques.
