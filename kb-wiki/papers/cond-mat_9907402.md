# Fixed-N Superconductivity: The Exact Crossover from the Bulk to the Few-Electron Limit

> We use two truly canonical approaches to describe superconductivity in ultrasmall metallic grains: (a) a variational fixed-N projected BCS-like theory and (b) an exact solution of the model Hamiltonian developed by Richardson in context with Nuclear Physics. Thereby we obtain a description of the entire crossover from the bulk BCS regime (mean level spacing $d\ll$ bulk gap $\tildeΔ$) to the `fluctuation-dominated' few-electron regime ($d\gg\tildeΔ$). A wave-function analysis shows in detail how the BCS limit is recovered and how for $d\gg\tildeΔ$ pairing correlations become delocalized in energy space.

# Fixed-N Superconductivity: The Exact Crossover from the Bulk to the Few-Electron Limit

## Key claim
The crossover from bulk BCS superconductivity to the fluctuation-dominated few-electron regime can be described exactly using Richardson's canonical solution, showing that pairing correlations persist for all grain sizes (no sharp breakdown of superconductivity), but the condensation energy changes from extensive to intensive at a crossover scale d ~ Δ̃, rather than at the parity-dependent critical spacings predicted by grand-canonical BCS theory.

## Method
The authors model ultrasmall metallic grains using a reduced BCS Hamiltonian with uniformly spaced single-particle levels and a pairing interaction cutoff at ω_D. They implement two canonical approaches: (1) a fixed-N projected BCS (PBCS) variational method, which projects the BCS wavefunction onto a definite electron number before variation, yielding N-1 variational parameters; and (2) Richardson's exact solution, which reduces the many-body problem to solving n_0 coupled algebraic equations for pair energies E_k, from which the exact ground state energy is obtained as the sum of the E_k. Both methods are applied to even and odd grains across the full range of level spacings d.

## Key numbers
- For a typical Al grain with radius r ≈ 5 nm, the mean level spacing is d ≃ 0.5 meV, the charging energy is E_C = 50 meV, and the bulk gap is Δ̃ ≃ 0.4 meV.
- The dimensionless pairing strength is λ = 0.224, close to that of aluminum.
- The PBCS results for the even-grain condensation energy E_0 agree with exact results to within about 6% for n_0 ≤ 10 (where n_0 is the number of electron pairs).
- In the bulk BCS limit d → 0, the condensation energy reaches the standard result −Δ̃²/(2d).
- For large d ≫ Δ̃, the condensation energy becomes practically intensive (almost d-independent), in contrast to the extensive 1/d scaling in the BCS regime.
- The pair-breaking energies cross at d ∼ Δ̃: in the bulk limit Ω_0 = Ω_1 = Δ̃, while for large d, Ω_0 ≃ d/2 and Ω_1 ≃ d.

## Context
This work extends Anderson's (1959) prediction that superconductivity breaks down when d > Δ̃, and improves upon earlier grand-canonical BCS studies by von Delft (1996) and Braun (1997, 1999) that found parity-dependent critical level spacings. The authors adapt a fixed-N projection method originally developed by Dietrich, Mang, and Pradal (1964) for nuclear shell models, and apply Richardson's exact solution from the 1960s, previously used in nuclear physics, to the problem of ultrasmall superconducting grains. The results enable quantitative comparison with the tunneling spectroscopy experiments of Ralph, Black, and Tinkham (1995, 1997) on Al grains, and provide an exact benchmark for assessing the accuracy of approximate canonical methods in the crossover regime.
