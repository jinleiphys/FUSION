# Bose-Einstein condensation in a rigidly rotating relativistic boson gas

> We study the Bose-Einstein condensation (BEC) of a free Bose gas under rigid rotation. The aim is to explore the impact of rotation on the thermodynamic quantities associated with BEC, including the Bose-Einstein (BE) transition temperature and condensate fraction. We begin by introducing the rotation in the Lagrangian density of free charged Klein-Gordon fields and determine the corresponding grand canonical partition function at finite temperature, chemical potential, and finite angular velocity. Assuming slow rotation, we derive analytical expressions for the pressure, energy, number, and angular momentum densities of a free Bose gas in nonrelativistic and ultrarelativistic limits in terms of the corresponding fugacities. We then focus on the phenomenon of BEC. We calculate the critical temperature of BEC transition and the condensate fraction in a slowly rotating Bose gas including only particles. Our findings indicate that the critical exponent associated with the BE transition in a rotating gas is lower compared to that in a nonrotating Bose gas. We also determine the fugacity in a rotating Bose gas in the aforementioned limits and examine how rotation affects its temperature dependence, both below and above the critical temperature. By analyzing the behavior of heat capacity at these temperatures, we demonstrate that in a nonrelativistic Bose gas, the rotation transforms the nature of the BE phase transition from a continuous to a discontinuous transition. In general, we find that a nonrelativistic Bose gas under rotation behaves similarly to a nonrotating Bose gas in ultrarelativistic limit.

## Key claim

The paper claims that in a nonrelativistic Bose gas under slow rigid rotation, the Bose-Einstein phase transition changes from a continuous (second-order) transition to a discontinuous (first-order) transition, as evidenced by a discontinuity in the heat capacity at the critical temperature. This claim is conditional on the assumption of slow rotation (beta Omega much less than 1) and on considering only the particle contribution (neglecting antiparticles).

## Method

The authors introduce rigid rotation into the Lagrangian density of free charged Klein-Gordon fields using a metric that describes a system rotating with constant angular velocity Omega around the z-axis. They compute the grand canonical partition function at finite temperature T, chemical potential mu, and angular velocity Omega using a mode expansion in cylindrical coordinates that separates the zero-mode contribution to study Bose-Einstein condensation. Assuming slow rotation (beta Omega much less than 1), they derive analytical expressions for thermodynamic quantities in the nonrelativistic (NR) and ultrarelativistic (UR) limits by expanding the logarithm in the pressure as a series and performing the momentum integrations and summations over the angular momentum quantum number.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature of BEC in a slowly rotating Bose gas is lower than that in a nonrotating Bose gas, although increasing the angular velocity increases the transition temperature (no explicit numerical value for the shift is given in the truncated text).
* The speed of sound in a slowly rotating nonrelativistic Bose gas is c_s = (2/5)^(1/2) approximately 0.63, compared to c_s^(0) = (2/3)^(1/2) approximately 0.82 in a nonrotating nonrelativistic Bose gas.
* The thermal de Broglie wavelength in the nonrelativistic limit is defined as lambda_T = (2pi/(mT))^(1/2), and the modified thermal length for a slowly rotating gas is lambda_{T,Omega} = lambda_T (beta Omega)^(1/3).
* The pressure of a slowly rotating nonrelativistic Bose gas is given by P_nr = (T / lambda_{T,Omega}^3) g_{7/2}(z), where g_s(z) is the Bose-Einstein function and z = exp(beta(mu - m)).
* The number density in the nonrelativistic limit is n_nr = (1 / lambda_{T,Omega}^3) g_{5/2}(z).

## Context

This work extends field-theoretical studies of rotating relativistic systems, such as those by Chernodub (2023) on gluon gases and Sadooghi (2024) on thermodynamic instabilities in rotating Bose gases, by specifically focusing on the Bose-Einstein condensation transition. The authors use the same field theoretical methods introduced by Kapusta (1979) and Haber and Weldon (1981, 1982). The results partly coincide with findings on fast rotation in anharmonic traps by Condat and colleagues (2006), although the framework and method of introducing rotation are completely different. This work enables further study of how rotation affects the equation of state and transport properties of compact stars, where both high densities and large angular frequencies (up to 10^3 Hz in neutron stars) are realized.
