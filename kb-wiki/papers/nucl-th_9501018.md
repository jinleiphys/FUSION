# SOME ASPECTS OF ANTIPROTON--NUCLEUS PHYSICS

> A brief review of antiproton--nucleus physics is presented. Some topics are related to early LEAR experiments, and others to more recent measurements or proposals. These include: exotic molecules, elastic and inelastic scattering, deep annihilation, strangeness production, neutron--antineutron oscillations, halo nuclei, antiproton production in nuclear reactions etc.

# Some Aspects of Antiproton–Nucleus Physics

## Key claim

Antiproton–nucleus experiments can probe both the elementary nucleon–antinucleon interaction and nuclear structure, but the field remains far from exhausted; many new experiments could be done at moderate cost if the antiproton source remains operational, and several astute experiments could be performed with very cold antiprotons on selected targets, including radioactive nuclear beams.

## Method

The paper reviews antiproton–nucleus physics across multiple topics: antiprotonic atoms and elastic scattering analyzed via optical potentials of Wood–Saxon type or KMT folding of the elementary antinucleon–nucleon amplitude; inelastic scattering and charge-exchange reactions used to filter spin and isospin components of the potential; integrated annihilation studies comparing data on neighboring isotopes to extract the isospin dependence of annihilation; and specific scenarios such as deep annihilation, cold annihilation on halo nuclei, and neutron–antineutron oscillations constrained by antiproton–nucleus optical potentials.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic width Im E of antiprotonic atoms is comparable to the shift Re E of the binding energy.
- For halo nuclei, the r.m.s. neutron radius exceeds the proton distribution radius by typically 0.5 fm.
- The critical coupling g3 for binding three bosons via Yukawa interaction is around 20% lower than the critical coupling g2 for two-body binding.
- The present limit on the stability of matter (T > 10^31 years) implies a limit tau > 10^8 s on the period of neutron–antineutron oscillations.
- A recent claim that the bound tau > 10^8 s should be revised by 31 orders of magnitude contains an error.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on antiproton–nucleus interactions performed at LEAR, including studies by Dover and collaborators on inelastic scattering and charge-exchange reactions, and by Yamazaki on antiprotonic atoms. The paper also connects to proposals for medium-energy antiproton physics from the SuperLEAR and KAON study groups, and to ongoing work on Pontecorvo reactions and strangeness production discussed by Golubeva at the same conference. The discussion of neutron–antineutron oscillations builds on calculations by several groups using the antiproton–nucleus optical potential measured at LEAR, and corrects a recent erroneous claim by Nazaruk.
