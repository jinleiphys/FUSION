# Strongly vs. weakly coupled in-medium showers: energy stopping in large-$N_f$ QED

> Inside a medium, showers originating from a very high-energy particle may develop via medium-induced splitting processes such as hard bremsstrahlung or pair production. During shower development, two consecutive splittings sometimes overlap quantum mechanically, so that they cannot be treated independently. Some of these effects can be absorbed into an effective value of a medium parameter known as $\hat q$. Previous calculations (with certain simplifying assumptions) have found that, after adjusting the value of $\hat q$, the leftover effect of overlapping splittings is quite small for purely gluonic large-$N_c$ showers but is very much larger for large-$N_f$ QED showers, at comparable values of $Nα$. Those works did not quite make for apples-to-apples comparisons: the gluon shower work investigated energy deposition from a gluon-initiated shower, whereas the QED work investigated charge-deposition from an electron-initiated shower. As a first step to tighten up the comparison, this paper investigates energy deposition in the QED case. Along the way, we develop a framework that should be useful in the future to explore whether the very small effect of overlapping splitting in purely gluonic showers is an artifact of having ignored quarks.

## Key claim

This paper investigates whether the drastically smaller effect of overlapping formation times in purely gluonic large-\(N_c\) QCD showers (a \(-2\% \times N_c\alpha_s\) correction) compared to large-\(N_f\) QED charge-deposition showers (a \(-87\% \times N_f\alpha\) correction) arises from the difference between energy deposition and charge deposition, rather than from fundamental differences between QCD and QED; the authors find that for large-\(N_f\) QED, overlap effects on energy deposition are comparable in size to those for charge deposition, ruling out this explanation.

## Method

The authors compute next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections to medium-induced splitting rates for large-\(N_f\) QED, using previously derived formulas for overlapping \(e \to e\gamma \to e e\bar e\) processes and virtual corrections to \(e \to e\gamma\) and \(\gamma \to e\bar e\). They then define net rates that incorporate both \(1\to2\) and effective \(1\to3\) splittings, numerically evaluate these rates, and fit them with analytic functions to compute the first moment (stopping length \(\ell_{\rm stop}^E\)) and width (\(\sigma^E\)) of the energy deposition distribution \(\varepsilon(z)\) for both electron-initiated and photon-initiated showers, expanding to first order in \(N_f\alpha\).

## Key numbers

* Overlap correction to \(\sigma/\ell_{\rm stop}\) for energy deposition from an electron-initiated shower: \(+113\% \times N_f\alpha\) (for renormalization scale \(\mu \propto (\hat q E)^{1/4}\) or \(\mu\) with explicit \(x_e\) dependence).
* Overlap correction to \(\sigma/\ell_{\rm stop}\) for energy deposition from a photon-initiated shower: \(+99\% \times N_f\alpha\) (for \(\mu \propto (\hat q E)^{1/4}\)) or \(+98\% \times N_f\alpha\) (for \(\mu\) with explicit \(x_e\) dependence).
* Overlap correction to \(\sigma/\ell_{\rm stop}\) for charge deposition from an electron-initiated shower: \(-87\% \times N_f\alpha\) (for \(\mu \propto (\hat q E_0)^{1/4}\)), \(-85\% \times N_f\alpha\) (for \(\mu \propto (\hat q E)^{1/4}\)), or \(-80\% \times N_f\alpha\) (for \(\mu\) with explicit \(x_e\) dependence).
* For comparison, the earlier large-\(N_c\) pure-gluon result for energy stopping \(\sigma/\ell_{\rm stop}\) was \(-2\% \times N_c\alpha_s\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of overlapping formation time effects in large-\(N_f\) QED (Arnold, Elgedawy, Iqbal, 2022) and large-\(N_c\) QCD (Arnold, Elgedawy, Iqbal, 2023), which found dramatically different correction sizes for charge deposition in QED versus energy deposition in pure-gluon QCD. The present paper tightens the comparison by computing energy deposition in QED, showing that the difference is not due to the type of deposition observable. The framework developed here is intended to enable future inclusion of quarks in QCD shower calculations, to test whether the small gluon result is an artifact of ignoring fermions.
