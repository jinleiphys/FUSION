# Strong coupling limit/region of lattice QCD

> We study the phase diagram of quark matter and nuclear properties based on the strong coupling expansion of lattice QCD. Both of baryon and finite coupling correction are found to have effects to extend the hadron phase to a larger mu direction relative to Tc. In a chiral RMF model with logarithmic sigma potential derived in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD, we can avoid the chiral collapse and normal and hypernuclei properties are well described.

## Key claim

The authors find that both baryon effects and finite coupling corrections (to order \(1/g^2\)) extend the hadron phase toward larger chemical potential \(\mu\) relative to the critical temperature \(T_c\), but each correction alone is insufficient to explain the empirical ratio \(R_{\mu T} = \mu_c(T=0)/T_c(\mu=0)\); the abstract glosses over this insufficiency.

## Method

The authors derive an effective potential for lattice QCD in the strong coupling limit (SCL) and include baryonic composite action via a mean-field approximation at zero diquark condensate, as well as finite coupling corrections up to \(1/g^2\) by bosonizing plaquette contributions. They then construct a chiral relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with a logarithmic sigma potential inspired by the SCL effective potential, and apply it to describe nuclear and hypernuclear properties.

## Key numbers

- In SCL-LQCD without baryon effects, the ratio \(R_{\mu T} = \mu_c(T=0)/T_c(\mu=0)\) is approximately 0.33, which is less than the empirical value of greater than two.
- The effective potential in SCL-LQCD at zero temperature takes the form \(\mathcal{F}_\mathrm{eff} = b_\sigma \sigma^2/2 - N_c \log \sigma\), where \(N_c = 3\).
- The spatial dimension used is \(d = 3\).
- The finite coupling parameters are \(\beta_t = d / (2 N_c^2 g^2)\) and \(\beta_s = (d-1) / (8 N_c^4 g^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the strong coupling expansion of lattice QCD (Kawamoto and Smit 1981; Bilic et al.) by incorporating baryon effects (KMOO 2007) and finite coupling corrections to improve the phase diagram of quark matter. It also addresses the "chiral collapse" problem in naive chiral RMF models based on \(\phi^4\) theory (Collapse), showing that the logarithmic sigma potential from SCL-LQCD stabilizes the normal vacuum. The results enable more realistic modeling of dense matter and hypernuclei, and suggest that gluons play a decisive role in avoiding chiral collapse (glueball).
