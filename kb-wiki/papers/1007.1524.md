# A new phenomenological formula for ground state binding energies

> A phenomenological formula based on liquid drop model has been proposed for ground state binding energies of nuclei. The effect due to bunching of single particle levels has been incorporated through a term resembling the one-body Hamiltonian. The effect of n-p interaction has been included through a function of valence nucleons. A total of 50 parameters has been used in the present calculation. The r.m.s. deviation for the binding energy values for 2140 nuclei comes out to be 0.376 MeV, and that for 1091 alpha decay energies is 0.284 MeV. The correspondence with the conventional liquid drop model is discussed.

# A new phenomenological formula for ground state binding energies

## Key claim

The paper proposes a phenomenological binding energy formula based on the liquid drop model that achieves an r.m.s. deviation of 0.376 MeV for 2140 nuclei, but this accuracy requires 50 free parameters and the nucleon numbers at which single-particle bunching occurs must be introduced from experimental mass values rather than derived from first principles.

## Method

The formula combines a conventional liquid drop model (LDM) macroscopic part with a bunching term that mimics the one-body Hamiltonian, using shell-model-like filling of orbits to assign occupation numbers at empirically determined neutron and proton numbers. A valence-nucleon term is included to account for n-p interaction and deformation effects, with valence particles or holes counted from the nearest major shells (8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126, 184). The 50 parameters were fitted in two steps using experimental masses from Audi et al. (2003) for 2140 nuclei with N, Z >= 8.

## Key numbers

- r.m.s. deviation for ground state binding energies: 0.376 MeV for 2140 nuclei
- r.m.s. deviation for alpha decay energies: 0.284 MeV for 1091 decays
- Only 39 nuclei show deviation larger than 1 MeV, none more than 2 MeV
- For nuclei with mass > 100, only 4 show deviation larger than 1 MeV, none more than 1.5 MeV
- Without modifications for neutron-rich nuclei, r.m.s. deviation becomes 0.400 MeV
- For 43 nuclei measured after 2003 (not in the Audi et al. table), r.m.s. deviation is 0.494 MeV (second fit) or 0.517 MeV (first fit)
- The volume coefficient a_v = 11.8906 MeV, surface coefficient a_surf = 7.3654 MeV, and r_0 = 1.0625 fm
- The pairing coefficient a_delta = 4.7686 MeV, close to the accepted value of 4.8 MeV

## Context

This work extends the microscopic-macroscopic approach of Myers and Swiatecki (1966), who included shell effects through bunching of single-particle levels, and the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) of Moller et al. (1995). The achieved accuracy (0.376 MeV) is comparable to the Duflo-Zuker (1995) ten-parameter formula (0.373 MeV for 2135 nuclei) and better than Skyrme Hartree-Fock (0.581 MeV) or FRDM (0.676 MeV). The formula enables direct analytic calculation of binding energies without external microscopic corrections, though the systematic deviation for Z=110 isotopes compared to other models (HFB, FRDM, Duflo-Zuker) indicates that modifications may be needed beyond N=159 and Z=108, the highest nucleon numbers in the fitting data.
