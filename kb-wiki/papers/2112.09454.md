# Zr Isotopes as a region of intertwined quantum phase transitions

> The zirconium isotopes with $A=$ 92$-$110 have one of the most complicated evolution of structure in the nuclear chart. In order to understand the structural evolution of these isotopes, we carry a detailed calculation in a definite symmetry-based framework, the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM). We compare our calculation to a large range of experimental data, such as energy levels, two neutron separation energies, $E2$ and $E0$ transition rates, isotope shifts and magnetic moments. The structural evolution of the low lying spectra of these isotopes is explained using the notion of intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), for which a QPT involving a crossing of two configurations (Type II) is accompanied by a QPT involving a shape evolution of each configuration separately (Type I). In our study, we find the occurrence of Type I QPT within the intruder configuration, changing from weakly deformed to prolate deformed and finally to $γ$-unstable, associated with the U(5), SU(3) and SO(6) dynamical symmetry limits of the IBM, respectively. Alongside the Type I QPT, we also find the occurrence of Type II QPT between the normal and intruder configurations, where both Types I and II have a critical-point near $A\approx100$. The good agreement of our calculation with the vast empirical data along the chain of isotopes demonstrates the relevance of IQPTs to the zirconium isotopes, and can serve as a case study to set path for new investigations of IQPTs in other nuclei and other physical systems.

## Key claim

The zirconium isotopes (A=92–110) exhibit intertwined quantum phase transitions (IQPTs), where a Type II QPT (crossing of normal and intruder configurations) occurs near A≈100 alongside a Type I QPT (shape evolution within the intruder configuration from weakly deformed to prolate deformed to γ-unstable), but only because the mixing between configurations is weak enough that the separate Type I transitions remain distinguishable.

## Method

The authors employed the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBM-CM), using a Hamiltonian with separate terms for the normal (A) and intruder (B) configurations plus a mixing term, with boson numbers N (for configuration A) and N+2 (for configuration B) corresponding to 0p-0h and 2p-2h proton excitations across the Z=40 subshell gap. They calculated energy levels, two-neutron separation energies, E2 and E0 transition rates, isotope shifts, and magnetic moments for ⁹²⁻¹¹⁰Zr, and analyzed wave functions by decomposing them into U(5), SU(3), and SO(6) dynamical symmetry bases to track configuration content and shape evolution.

## Key numbers

- Critical-point for both Type I and Type II QPTs occurs near A≈100.
- For ⁹⁶Zr, the calculated B(E2; 2⁺₁→0⁺₁)=2.3(3) W.u. matches the experimental single-particle value, while B(E2; 2⁺₂→0⁺₂)=36(11) W.u. and B(E2; 4⁺₁→2⁺₂)=56⁺²⁰₋₄₄ W.u. within the intruder configuration are reproduced well.
- For ⁹⁶Zr, the inter-configuration transition B(E2; 2⁺₂→0⁺₁)=0.26(8) W.u. (experimental) versus 0.001 W.u. (calculated) suggests the mixing parameter ω could be increased from 0.02 to 0.04 MeV.
- Boson numbers range from N=1 for ⁹²Zr to N=8 for ¹⁰⁶Zr, with hole bosons N̄=7,6 for ¹⁰⁸,¹¹⁰Zr.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Gavrielov 2019, 2020) on IQPTs in zirconium isotopes by providing a detailed comparison with a wide range of experimental data. It builds on earlier IBM-CM calculations for Mo isotopes (Sambataro 1982) and on the shell-model understanding of deformation onset via the proton-neutron interaction between π(1g₉/₂) and ν(1g₇/₂) spin-orbit partners (Federman 1977, 1979; Heyde 1985, 1987). The results demonstrate that the Zr chain serves as a case study for IQPTs, enabling future investigations of such intertwined transitions in other nuclei and physical systems.
