# Strangeness Production in AA and pp Collisions

> Boost-invariant hadron production in high energy collisions occurs in causally disconnected regions of finite space-time size. As a result, globally conserved quantum numbers (charge, strangeness, baryon number) are conserved locally in spatially restricted correlation clusters. Their size is determined by two time scales: the equilibration time specifying the formation of a quark-gluon plasma, and the hadronization time, specifying the onset of confinement. The expected values for these scales provide the theoretical basis for the suppression observed for strangeness production in elementary interactions ($pp$, $e^+e^-$) below LHC energies. In contrast, the space-time superposition of individual collisions in high energy heavy ion interactions leads to higher energy densities, resulting in much later hadronization and hence much larger hadronization volumes. This largely removes the causality constraints and results in an ideal hadronic resonance gas in full chemical equilibrium. In the present paper, we determine the collision energies needed for that; we also estimate when $pp$ collisions reach comparable hadronization volumes and thus determine when strangeness suppression should disappear there as well.

## Key claim

Strangeness suppression in elementary collisions (pp, e+e-) arises from causality constraints that force global quantum numbers to be conserved locally within small, causally disconnected spatial regions; this suppression should disappear in pp collisions at LHC energies (5-7 TeV) when the hadronization volume becomes large enough to approach the grand canonical limit.

## Method

The authors derive the size d of causally connected interaction regions at hadronization time using a boost-invariant production model, relating d to the ratio of equilibration time τ0 (quark-gluon plasma formation time, ~1 fm) and hadronization time τh (confinement time). They then connect τh/τ0 to the initial energy density ε0 via isentropic expansion and the lattice QCD value for the hadronization energy density εh ≈ 0.4 GeV/fm³. Using experimental charged hadron multiplicities per participant in AA and pp collisions as a function of collision energy √s, they compute ε0 for both systems and compare the resulting correlation scales d(√s).

## Key numbers

- Hadronization energy density from lattice QCD: εh ≈ 0.4 GeV/fm³
- Average transverse momentum per hadron: mT ≈ 0.5 GeV
- Proton radius used: Rp ≈ 0.8 fm
- Nuclear radius parameter: R0 = 1.25 fm
- Strangeness suppression in nuclear collisions disappears at √s ≈ 30-40 GeV (based on γs approaching 1)
- For strangeness suppression to vanish in pp collisions, required collision energies are approximately 5-7 TeV
- Strangeness correlation radius for recovering grand canonical behavior: Rc ≈ 2 fm (with d ≈ 2 Rc)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous causality-based approach (Castorina and Satz, 2015) that explained strangeness suppression via finite-size correlation clusters in boost-invariant production. It quantifies the transition from canonical strangeness conservation (Redlich, 2000) to grand canonical behavior in both AA and pp collisions, using the γs suppression factor framework of Letessier, Rafelski, and Tounsi. The results enable predictions for when strangeness suppression should vanish in pp collisions at LHC energies, with first indications already seen at 900 GeV and 7 TeV, and suggest extending the analysis to quarkonium suppression and jet quenching.
