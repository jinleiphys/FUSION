# Timelike vs spacelike DVCS from JLab, Compass to ultraperipheral collisions and AFTER@LHC

> Timelike and spacelike virtual Compton scattering in the generalized Bjorken scaling regime are complementary tools to access generalized parton distributions. We stress that the gluonic contributions are by no means negligible, even in the medium energy range which will be studied intensely at JLab12 and in the COMPASS-II experiment at CERN. Ultraperipheral collisions with proton or ion beams may also be used at RHIC and at collider or fixed target experiments at LHC.

## Key claim

Gluonic contributions to deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) amplitudes are by no means negligible even at medium energies accessible at JLab12 and COMPASS-II, and can even change the sign of the real part of the Compton form factor.

## Method

The authors performed a next-to-leading order (NLO) analysis of DVCS and TCS amplitudes using the collinear factorization framework, computing Compton form factors (CFFs) from two generalized parton distribution (GPD) models based on double distributions: the Goloskokov-Kroll (GK) model and a model using MSTW08 parton distribution function parametrizations. They then estimated cross sections and observables for TCS in ultraperipheral collisions at hadron colliders and in fixed-target experiments at the LHC (AFTER@LHC project), including Bethe-Heitler, Compton, and interference contributions.

## Key numbers

- The Bethe-Heitler cross section for TCS, integrated over $\theta \in [\pi/4, 3\pi/4]$, $\phi \in [0, 2\pi]$, $Q^2 \in [4.5, 5.5]$ GeV$^2$, $|t| \in [0.05, 0.25]$ GeV$^2$, is constant at large $s$ and equals 28.4 pb.
- The gluonic contribution to the imaginary part of the CFF remains sizeable for values of the skewness parameter up to 0.3.
- Calculations for DVCS observables at JLab used $E_e = 11$ GeV, $\mu_F^2 = Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$, and $t = -0.2$ GeV$^2$.
- Calculations for COMPASS used $\xi = 0.05$, $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$, $t = -0.2$ GeV$^2$.
- Lepton pair production differential cross sections for TCS were computed at $t = -0.2$ GeV$^2$, $Q^2 = 5$ GeV$^2$, for $s = 10^7$ GeV$^2$ and $s = 10^5$ GeV$^2$.
- For the AFTER@LHC fixed-target estimates, calculations used $Q^2 = 4$ GeV$^2$, $t = -0.1$ GeV$^2$, $\phi = 0$.

## Context

This work extends NLO calculations of DVCS coefficient functions (known from early GPD studies) to the TCS case, using the analyticity relation between the two reactions derived by the same authors (Moutarde, Pire, Szymanowski, Wagner, 2013). The results demonstrate that gluonic contributions must be included in analyses of upcoming JLab12 and COMPASS-II data, contrary to assumptions that they are negligible at medium energies. The study also enables experimental programs at RHIC and LHC (including the proposed AFTER@LHC fixed-target project) to extract GPDs through TCS in ultraperipheral collisions, particularly at very small skewness values.
