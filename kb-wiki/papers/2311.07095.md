# Revisit to the yield ratio of triton and $^3$He as an indicator of neutron-rich neck emission

> The neutron rich neck zone created in heavy ion reaction is experimentally probed by the production of the $A=3$ isobars. The energy spectra and angular distributions of triton and $^3$He are measured with the CSHINE detector in $^{86}$Kr +$^{208}$Pb reactions at 25 MeV/u. While the energy spectrum of $^{3}$He is harder than that of triton, known as "$^{3}$He-puzzle", the yield ratio $R({\rm t/^3He})$ presents a robust rising trend with the polar angle in laboratory. Using the fission fragments to reconstruct the fission plane, the enhancement of out-plane $R({\rm t/^3He})$ is confirmed in comparison to the in-plane ratios. Transport model simulations reproduce qualitatively the experimental trends, but the quantitative agreement is not achieved. The results demonstrate that a neutron rich neck zone is formed in the reactions. Further studies are called for to understand the clustering and the isospin dynamics related to neck formation.

## Key claim
The yield ratio of triton to ³He, R(t/³He), shows a robust rising trend with laboratory polar angle and is enhanced for out-plane emission relative to in-plane emission, indicating the formation of a neutron-rich neck zone in ²⁵ MeV/u ⁸⁶Kr + ²⁰⁸Pb reactions. However, transport model simulations (ImQMD05+GEMINI) reproduce the experimental trends only qualitatively, not quantitatively, suggesting that the clustering mechanism is more complicated than the coalescence picture currently used.

## Method
Energy spectra and angular distributions of triton and ³He were measured using four silicon-strip detector telescopes (SSDTs) of the CSHINE spectrometer, covering laboratory polar angles from 10° to 60°, in coincidence with fission fragments detected by three parallel plate avalanche counters (PPACs) to select semi-peripheral collisions. The yield ratio R(t/³He) was analyzed as a function of laboratory polar angle and as a function of the angle relative to the fission plane reconstructed from the two fission fragment velocities. Experimental data were compared to simulations using the improved quantum molecular dynamics model (ImQMD05) coupled with the statistical decay afterburner GEMINI.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 25 MeV/u for ⁸⁶Kr + ²⁰⁸Pb reactions.
- Energy peak positions (Ep) for triton: 45 MeV (SSDT2, 20°–30°), 40 MeV (SSDT3, 30°–40°), 19 MeV (SSDT4, 40°–60°).
- Energy peak positions (Ep) for ³He: 62 MeV (SSDT2), 58 MeV (SSDT3), 38 MeV (SSDT4).
- Average linear momentum transfer from fission fragment folding angle: ⟨LMT⟩ = 0.35, corresponding to an average impact parameter ⟨b⟩ ≈ 7 fm.
- Width of the distribution of the angle between the fission plane normal and the beam direction: σ_α1 ≈ 6°.
- Target thickness: about 1 mg/cm².
- Angular resolution of SSDTs: about 1°.
- Energy resolution of SSDTs: better than 2%.
- Track recognition efficiency: about 90%.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of the "³He-puzzle" (Poggi, 1995; Bougault, 1997; Liu, 2012) and the use of R(t/³He) as a probe of isospin dynamics and neutron-rich neck formation in heavy-ion reactions (INDRA, 2022; FOPI, 2010). The results confirm the formation of a neutron-rich neck zone in semi-peripheral collisions, as predicted by transport model simulations (Sobotka, 1994; Baran, 2001; Lionti, 2005), and demonstrate that the angular distribution of R(t/³He) relative to the fission plane provides a new experimental handle on neck emission. The quantitative disagreement with ImQMD05+GEMINI simulations calls for improved clustering models beyond the coalescence approach.
