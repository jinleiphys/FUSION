# Charged radiative pion capture on the nucleon in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory

> The differential cross sections and s-wave and p-wave multipoles for $π^- p \to γn$ and $π^+ n \to γp$ have been calculated through $O(p^3)$ in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT). Fits to existing data allow several of the low energy constants to be determined. Generally results of the calculation compare well with dispersion relation predictions.

## Key claim

The differential cross sections and multipoles for charged radiative pion capture on the nucleon have been calculated through O(p³) in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but the convergence for magnetic multipoles is slower than for electric ones, and the low-energy constant b₁₀ is double-valued from data fits alone, requiring comparison with dispersion relation calculations to select the preferred value.

## Method

The authors constructed the HBChPT Lagrangian to O(p³) and calculated all tree-level and one-loop diagrams in an arbitrary gauge to verify gauge invariance, then reduced to the v·ε=0 gauge. They evaluated amplitudes for the inverse photoproduction process and used detailed balance to connect to radiative capture data, projecting s-wave and p-wave multipoles from the four structure amplitudes F₁–F₄. Fits to differential cross section data from TRIUMF and SAL determined three unknown low-energy constants (b₁₀, b₂₁ʳ, b₂₂ʳ).

## Key numbers

- Low-energy constants from fits: b₂₁ʳ = –8.2 ± 0.7, b₂₂ʳ = 9.2 ± 0.6
- Preferred value of b₁₀ = 13.7 ± 4.5, selected by comparison with dispersion relation calculations (Tiator)
- The O(p) result alone does not fit the data; O(p²) improves the fit, but full O(p³) is necessary for a good fit
- The O(p³) magnetic multipoles are comparable to or only slightly smaller than the O(p²) terms, indicating slower convergence than for electric multipoles

## Context

This work extends previous HBChPT calculations of neutral pion photoproduction to the charged pion radiative capture channels (π⁻p → γn and π⁺n → γp), which have leading-order O(p) contributions. The results are compared with dispersion relation predictions (Tiator) and with earlier HBChPT s-wave multipole calculations (BKM). The extracted low-energy constants are needed for extending the program from ordinary muon capture (Fearing, Lewis, et al., 1999) to radiative muon capture, and the slower convergence of magnetic multipoles suggests that extending the calculation to O(p⁴) or including explicit Δ(1232) fields would be valuable.
