# Pion crystals hosting topologically stable baryons

> We construct analytic (3+1)-dimensional inhomogeneous and topologically non-trivial pion systems using chiral perturbation theory. We discuss the effect of isospin asymmetry with vanishing electromagnetic interactions as well as some particular configurations with non-vanishing electromagnetic interactions. The inhomogeneous configurations of the pion fields are characterized by a non-vanishing topological charge that can be identified with baryons surrounded by a cloud of pions. This system supports a topologically protected persistent superflow. When the electromagnetic field is turned on the superflow corresponds to an electromagnetic supercurrent.

## Key claim

Using chiral perturbation theory, the authors construct analytic (3+1)-dimensional inhomogeneous pion condensates that carry a non-vanishing topological charge identifiable with baryons, but only when the system is confined to a finite spatial volume with specific boundary conditions, which circumvents Derrick's no-go theorem against static solitons in flat topologically trivial space-time.

## Method

The authors employ zero-temperature two-flavor chiral perturbation theory with an SU(2) unimodular field ansatz, imposing a generalized hedgehog-like configuration where the angular fields depend linearly on the spatial coordinates and time. They enforce the condition K=0 (relating the isospin chemical potential to the finite volume size) to eliminate explicit time dependence, and solve the resulting sine-Gordon-like equation for the radial field α(r) numerically with Dirichlet boundary conditions that allow non-trivial topological charge.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_π ≈ 93 MeV; degenerate pion mass: m_π ≈ 135 MeV
- For the homogeneous broken phase, cos α_0 = m_π²/μ_I² and Θ_0 = π/2 when |μ_I| > m_π
- The normalized pressure in the homogeneous broken phase is p = (f_π²/(2 μ_I²)) (μ_I² - m_π²)²
- For the inhomogeneous solution with q=1, p=1, ℓ=1/m_π, and η_0 ≈ 0.186, the radial field satisfies α(2π) = 2π
- The total matter energy for this choice is E_m = 20.5 π² (f_π²/m_π)
- The system volume is 4π³ ℓ³, with ℓ = b/m_π where b is a real number

## Context

This work extends previous constructions of exact crystalline soliton solutions in chiral perturbation theory (Canfora 2013, 2014, 2015, 2018, 2019) by incorporating a non-vanishing isospin chemical potential, which allows static boundary conditions suitable for lattice QCD simulations. The results contrast with the chiral soliton lattice approach (Brauner 2016, Huang 2017) where the order parameter depends on only one spatial coordinate and cannot carry topological charge. The construction enables future lattice QCD studies of topologically stable inhomogeneous pion phases and suggests robustness beyond chiral perturbation theory based on earlier work (Alvarez 2020, Canfora 2020) showing the solutions survive subleading corrections in the 't Hooft expansion.
