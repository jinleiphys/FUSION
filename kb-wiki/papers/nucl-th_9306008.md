# Non-Linear Mean Field Dynamics in the Nuclear Spinodal Zone

> We demonstrate, by numerical simulations, that the dynamics of nuclear matter mean field inside the spinodal region is chaotic. Spontaneous symmetry-breaking - no explicit fluctuating term is considered - occurs leading to wild unpredictable density fluctuations. A proper recipe to calculate an average Lyapunov exponent in this multidimensional phase space is introduced. The latter is calculated for different values of the density in order to characterize in a quantitative way the chaotic and regular regions. It is argued that the mean field chaoticity can be the main mechanism of the nuclear multifragmentation occurring in the intermediate energy reactions.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate by numerical simulations that the mean field dynamics of nuclear matter inside the spinodal region is chaotic even without any explicit fluctuating term, and they argue that this chaoticity, rather than statistical or linear instability mechanisms, could be the main mechanism driving nuclear multifragmentation in intermediate energy reactions.

## Method

The authors solved the Vlasov equation on a two-dimensional lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using a simplified Skyrme interaction for the effective one-body field averaged over the y-direction. They initialized nuclear matter with a small density wave (amplitude 10^-2 ρ0) at a temperature of 3 MeV and varied initial conditions by changing the density by 10^-4 ρ0. They introduced a distance measure based on the norm difference between density profiles and calculated an average Lyapunov exponent for different densities to quantify chaotic versus regular regions.

## Key numbers

- The Lyapunov exponent λ∞ has a flat maximum around density ρ = 0.35 ρ0.
- At density ρ = 0.5 ρ0, the mean field divergence time τMF = 13.34 fm/c, compared to the single particle time τsp = 10.87 fm/c.
- At density ρ = 0.4 ρ0, τMF = 9.92 fm/c and τsp = 13.59 fm/c.
- At density ρ = 0.3 ρ0, τMF = 9.06 fm/c and τsp = 18.12 fm/c.
- At density ρ = 0.2 ρ0, τMF = 10.00 fm/c and τsp = 27.17 fm/c.
- At density ρ = 0.7 ρ0 (near the spinodal boundary), τMF = 1538 fm/c and τsp = 7.76 fm/c, indicating regular dynamics.
- The mixing of wave numbers takes place in an interval of time of about 20 fm/c.
- Total energy was conserved up to 1% and particle number remained constant during the whole evolution.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of mechanical instabilities in the spinodal zone, such as the Raleigh-type instabilities studied by Moretto (1992) and ring formation studied by Bauer, Bertsch, and Schultz (1992), as well as linear response analyses by Heiselberg, Pethick, and Ravenhall (1988, 1993). The finding that mean field dynamics is chaotic and produces strong mode coupling within about 20 fm/c challenges the validity of mean trajectory approaches used in kinetic equation simulations and suggests that concepts from chaotic dynamics, such as Kolmogorov entropy, may be more appropriate than thermodynamic quantities for characterizing multifragmentation. The authors note that extension to heavy ion collisions remains problematic and suggest that fragment size fluctuations and intermittency analysis, as pursued by Gross (1993) and Ploszajczak and Tucholski (1990), could provide signatures of chaotic dynamics.
