# Dynamics near the critical point: the hot renormalization group in quantum field theory

> The perturbative approach to the description of long wavelength excitations at high temperature breaks down near the critical point of a second order phase transition. We study the \emph{dynamics} of these excitations in a relativistic scalar field theory at and near the critical point via a renormalization group approach at high temperature and an $ε$ expansion in $d=5-ε$ space-time dimensions. The long wavelength physics is determined by a non-trivial fixed point of the renormalization group. At the critical point we find that the dispersion relation and width of quasiparticles of momentum $p$ is $ω_p \sim p^{z}$ and $Γ_p \sim (z-1) ω_p$ respectively, the group velocity of quasiparticles $v_g \sim p^{z-1}$ vanishes in the long wavelength limit at the critical point. Away from the critical point for $T\gtrsim T_c$ we find $ω_p \sim ξ^{-z}[1+(p ξ)^{2z}]^{1/2}$ and $Γ_p \sim (z-1) ω_p \frac{(p ξ)^{2z}}{1+(p ξ)^{2z}}$ with $ξ$ the finite temperature correlation length $ ξ\propto |T-T_c|^{-ν}$. The new \emph{dynamical} exponent $z$ results from anisotropic renormalization in the spatial and time directions. For a theory with O(N) symmetry we find $z=1+ ε \frac{N+2}{(N+8)^2}+\mathcal{O}(ε^2)$. Critical slowing down, i.e, a vanishing width in the long-wavelength limit, and the validity of the quasiparticle picture emerge naturally from this analysis.

## Key claim

The paper argues that near the critical point of a second-order phase transition in relativistic scalar field theory, the dynamics of long-wavelength excitations are governed by a nontrivial fixed point of the renormalization group, leading to a new dynamical critical exponent \(z\) that differs from the mean-field value \(z=1\) found in relativistic theories; the quasiparticle picture remains valid because the ratio \(\Gamma_p/\omega_p\) is small in the \(\epsilon\) expansion, contrary to the breakdown suggested by naive perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors implement a renormalization group approach at high temperature combined with an \(\epsilon\) expansion in \(d=5-\epsilon\) space-time dimensions, analyzing the high-temperature behavior of the relevant Feynman diagrams in the Matsubara formalism. They focus on the zero Matsubara mode contribution, which dominates the infrared physics, and compute the beta function for the effective dimensionless coupling \(g(\mu)=\lambda T \mu^{-\epsilon}\) to determine the infrared-stable fixed point. The anisotropic scaling between spatial and time directions, arising from the compactification of Euclidean time at finite temperature, is captured by introducing a bare speed of propagation and computing the dynamical exponent \(z\) via the renormalization group.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical critical exponent for O(N) symmetry: \(z = 1 + \epsilon \frac{N+2}{(N+8)^2} + \mathcal{O}(\epsilon^2)\); for a scalar theory with discrete symmetry (N=1) to lowest order: \(z = 1 + \frac{\epsilon}{27} + \mathcal{O}(\epsilon^2)\).
- At the critical point, dispersion relation \(\omega_p \sim p^{z}\) and width \(\Gamma_p \sim (z-1) \omega_p\), with group velocity \(v_g \sim p^{z-1}\) vanishing in the long-wavelength limit.
- Away from the critical point for \(T \gtrsim T_c\): \(\omega_p \sim \xi^{-z} [1 + (p\xi)^{2z}]^{1/2}\) and \(\Gamma_p \sim (z-1) \omega_p \frac{(p\xi)^{2z}}{1+(p\xi)^{2z}}\), where \(\xi \propto |T-T_c|^{-\nu}\) is the finite-temperature correlation length.
- In three spatial dimensions, the infrared-fixed point for the effective coupling is \(g^* = 8/3\) from the one-loop beta function; in the large N limit, the fixed point is \(\bar{g}_{eff}(\mu) \to 4\) as \(\mu \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the static renormalization group treatment of finite-temperature field theory, previously restricted to static quantities (e.g., Irla 1998, Senben 1999), to dynamical aspects including dispersion relations and relaxation rates. It contrasts with the phenomenological time-dependent Landau-Ginzburg approach (Hohenberg and Halperin 1977, Ma 1976), which yields \(z=2\) at mean-field level and automatically incorporates critical slowing down, whereas relativistic quantum field theory at tree level gives \(z=1\). The results complement studies of quantum phase transitions in condensed matter (Sachdev 1999) that focus on \(\omega, p \gg T\), by addressing the classical regime \(\omega, p \ll T\). The paper enables a microscopic understanding of critical slowing down in quantum field theory, relevant for the chiral phase transition in QCD as studied in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions, and provides a systematic \(\epsilon\)-expansion framework for computing dynamical critical exponents.
