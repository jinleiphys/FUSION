# Preparations for Quantum Simulations of Quantum Chromodynamics in 1+1 Dimensions: (I) Axial Gauge

> Tools necessary for quantum simulations of $1+1$ dimensional quantum chromodynamics are developed. When formulated in axial gauge and with two flavors of quarks, this system requires 12 qubits per spatial site with the gauge fields included via non-local interactions. Classical computations and D-Wave's quantum annealer Advantage are used to determine the hadronic spectrum, enabling a decomposition of the masses and a study of quark entanglement. Color edge states confined within a screening length of the end of the lattice are found. IBM's 7-qubit quantum computers, ibmq_jakarta and ibm_perth, are used to compute dynamics from the trivial vacuum in one-flavor QCD with one spatial site. More generally, the Hamiltonian and quantum circuits for time evolution of $1+1$ dimensional $SU(N_c)$ gauge theory with $N_f$ flavors of quarks are developed, and the resource requirements for large-scale quantum simulations are estimated.

## Key claim

The paper develops the tools for quantum simulations of 1+1 dimensional QCD in axial gauge, but the actual quantum computations on IBM's devices are limited to a single-flavor, single-site system, while the two-flavor results for hadronic spectra and entanglement are obtained from classical exact diagonalization and D-Wave's quantum annealer, not from gate-based quantum computers.

## Method

The authors map 1+1D SU(N_c) lattice gauge theory with N_f quark flavors onto qubits using a Kogut-Susskind staggered discretization with Jordan-Wigner transformation, formulated in axial gauge (A_x=0) where Gauss's law eliminates gauge links and produces non-local Coulomb interactions. Exact diagonalization of the Hamiltonian is performed for small lattices (L=1,2 spatial sites) with N_f=2 to extract hadronic spectra and wavefunctions, and D-Wave's quantum annealer Advantage is used to determine the hadronic spectrum. IBM's 7-qubit quantum computers ibmq_jakarta and ibm_perth are used to compute time evolution from the trivial vacuum in one-flavor QCD with one spatial site.

## Key numbers

- 12 qubits per spatial site are required for N_f=2 QCD with N_c=3 in axial gauge
- For L=1 with m=1 and g^2=1: vacuum energy E_Ω = -0.549, M_σ = 2.73, M_π = 2.74 (in lattice units)
- For L=2 with m=1 and g^2=1: vacuum energy E_Ω = -1.58, M_σ = 2.72, M_π = 2.74 (in lattice units)
- The σ- and π-meson mass splitting shows a clear maximum at g ~ 2.4
- For L=1 with m=1 and g^2=8: vacuum energy E_Ω = -0.205, M_σ = 5.73, M_π = 5.82

## Context

This work extends prior quantum simulations of 1+1D non-Abelian gauge theories, notably the recent quantum simulation of N_f=1 SU(2) lattice gauge theory by Atas et al. (2021), and builds upon the foundational Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian formulation from the 1970s. The paper provides the Hamiltonian and quantum circuits for general SU(N_c) with N_f flavors, enabling future large-scale quantum simulations of QCD dynamics. The results have been applied in a companion paper to quantum simulation of beta-decay of a single baryon in 1+1D QCD.
