# The rate of photon production in the quark-gluon plasma from lattice QCD

> We calculate the thermal rate of real-photon production in the quark-gluon plasma at a temperature of $T=254$ MeV using lattice QCD. The calculation is based on the difference between the spatially transverse and longitudinal parts of the polarization tensor, which has the advantage of falling off rapidly at large frequencies. We obtain this linear combination in the time-momentum representation from lattice QCD with two flavors of quarks in the continuum limit with a precision of about two parts per mille. Applying a theoretically motivated fit ansatz for the associated spectral function, we obtain values for the photon rate that are in line with QCD weak-coupling calculations; for photon momenta $ 1.0\leq k[{\rm GeV}]\leq 1.4$, our non-perturbative results constrain the rate to be no larger than twice the weak-coupling prediction. We also provide a physics interpretation of the electromagnetic spectral functions valid for all frequencies and momenta.

## Key claim

The authors non-perturbatively constrain the real-photon production rate from the quark-gluon plasma at T = 254 MeV using lattice QCD, finding that for photon momenta between 1.0 and 1.4 GeV the rate is no larger than twice the weak-coupling prediction, but they do not rule out the weak-coupling prediction itself.

## Method

The authors compute the Euclidean correlator of the isovector current in the time-momentum representation for a range of spatial momenta, using lattice QCD with two flavors of O(a)-improved Wilson fermions at T = 254 MeV. They take the continuum limit from four lattice spacings (1/(aT) = 12, 16, 20, 24) and then apply a theoretically motivated Padé-type fit ansatz for the associated spectral function, imposing the superconvergent sum rule and spectral positivity constraints. The effective diffusion coefficient D_eff(k), which is proportional to the photon rate, is determined by scanning the parameter space and identifying those values that yield a p-value above 0.32 for the Euclidean data.

## Key numbers

- Temperature: T = 254 ± 5 MeV
- Static susceptibility in the continuum limit: χ_s/T^2 = 0.880 (9)_stat (8)_syst
- Relative statistical precision of the continuum correlator: one to two parts per mille
- For photon momenta 1.0 ≤ k [GeV] ≤ 1.4, the effective diffusion coefficient D_eff(k) · GeV covers the interval [0, 0.7], compatible with both the weak-coupling prediction (0.3–0.5) and the strongly coupled SYM prediction
- Quark mass: m^MSbar ≈ 13 MeV (on three ensembles) and ≈ 16 MeV (on the finest ensemble) at renormalization scale μ = 2 GeV
- Pion mass on zero-temperature ensembles: 269 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quenched (gluon-only) lattice QCD calculation of Ghiglieri et al. (2016) to full QCD with two dynamical quark flavors, confirming the weak-coupling predictions of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2001) at a temperature typical of heavy-ion collisions. The results enable non-perturbative constraints on thermal photon emission in heavy-ion phenomenology and pave the way for calculating the production of lepton pairs and sterile neutrinos from the quark-gluon plasma. The method also provides a physics interpretation of electromagnetic spectral functions for all frequencies and momenta, connecting to the energy dissipation rate of external electromagnetic fields in the medium.
