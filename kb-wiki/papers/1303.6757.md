# Universal prohibited zones in the coordinate space of few-body systems

> In some special zones of the high-dimensional coordinate space of few-body systems with identical particles, the operation of an element (or a product of elements) of the symmetry groups of the Hamiltonian on a quantum state might be equivalent to the operation of another element. Making use of the matrix representations of the groups, the equivalence leads to a set of homogeneous linear equations imposing on the wave functions. When the matrix of these equations is non-degenerate, the wave functions will appear as nodal surfaces in these zones. In this case, these zones are prohibited. In this paper, tightly bound 4-boson systems with three types of interaction have been studied analytically and numerically. The existence of the universal prohibited zones has been revealed, and their decisive effect on the structures of the eigenstates is demonstrated.

## Key claim

The paper reveals a new kind of universality in tightly bound few-body systems: the existence of universal prohibited zones (PZ) in coordinate space where wave functions must vanish due to symmetry constraints, independent of the specific interaction or system type. The claim is hedged by the condition that the matrix of homogeneous linear equations derived from equivalent symmetry operations must be non-degenerate for the prohibition to occur.

## Method

The author analytically derived symmetry constraints for 4-boson systems by identifying zones in coordinate space where different symmetry operations (permutations, rotations, inversions) become equivalent, leading to homogeneous linear equations that force wave functions to zero. Numerical diagonalization of the Hamiltonian was performed for trapped 4-boson systems with three different interaction potentials (a short-ranged nuclear-type potential V_A, a Van der Waals-type potential V_B, and a repulsive hard-core potential V_C), using an isotropic harmonic trap to support bound states. The eigenstates were analyzed through root-mean-square radii, one-body densities, and shape-densities in selected coordinate zones.

## Key numbers

* Three interaction types were used: V_A(r) = 10(2e^{-(r/1.428)^2} - e^{-(r/2.105)^2}), V_B(r) = 1000e^{-3r} - 40/r^6 (with cutoff at r=1.2), and V_C(r) = 15 for r ≤ 1 and V_C(r) = 0 for r > 1, all in units where ℏω = 1 and √(ℏ/mω) = 1.
* For the 4-boson system with L ≤ 4, the ET-accessible states are 0⁺, 3⁻, and 4⁺; the SQ-accessible states are 0⁺, 2⁺, and 4⁺.
* For V_C, the maximum shape-density of 4⁻ in Z_{1-3} is 1.52 at a flattened regular pyramid, while the maximum in Z_{2-2} is 0.71 at a prolonged ET.
* The Z_{2-2} zone is a prohibited zone for 1⁺, 3⁺, 0⁻, and 1⁻ states; Z_{twi} is a PZ for 1⁺ and 1⁻ states.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical predictions on inherent nodal surfaces in 4-boson systems by Bao (earlier works cited as Bao, references 18 and 19 in the paper) from free systems to trapped systems, providing the first numerical confirmation of the universal prohibited zones. The study generalizes earlier findings on triply and quadruple excited intrashell states of atoms (Bao, several references) and the classification of states by inherent nodal structures (Moshinsky and collaborators). The results enable systematic prediction of geometric structures and oscillation modes in tetramers across nuclear, atomic, and molecular systems, and the paper notes that the similarity between V_A and V_B cases is high but weaker with V_C.
