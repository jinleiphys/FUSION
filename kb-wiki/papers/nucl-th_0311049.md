# A relation between proton and neutron asymptotic normalization coefficients for light mirror nuclei and its relevance to nuclear astrophysics

> We show how the charge symmetry of strong interactions can be used to relate the proton and neutron asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) of the one nucleon overlap integrals for light mirror nuclei. This relation extends to the case of real proton decay where the mirror analog is a virtual neutron decay of a loosely bound state. In this case, a link is obtained between the proton width and the squared ANC of the mirror neutron state. The relation between mirror overlaps can be used to study astrophysically relevant proton capture reactions based on information obtained from transfer reactions with stable beams.

# A relation between proton and neutron asymptotic normalization coefficients for light mirror nuclei and its relevance to nuclear astrophysics

## Key claim

Charge symmetry of strong interactions can be used to relate proton and neutron asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for one-nucleon overlap integrals in light mirror nuclei, with the relation extending to cases where the proton state is a resonance and its mirror analog is a virtual neutron decay of a loosely bound state. The ratio of squared ANCs is nearly independent of the nucleon-nucleon force choice, varying by less than 4% across different NN potentials, though the individual ANCs themselves can change by a factor of two.

## Method

The authors derived an analytical expression for the ratio of proton to neutron squared ANCs in mirror pairs using the charge symmetry of nuclear forces, starting from the definition of the ANC via the asymptotic behavior of the overlap integral and the many-body expression involving the NN potential. They performed microscopic calculations for several 0p-shell nuclei using fixed 0-omega shell model wave functions with a range of NN potentials (GPT, Volkov V1, Brink-Boeker B1, and four versions of M3Y), and also calculated single-particle ANC ratios using Woods-Saxon potentials that reproduce the binding energies. For unbound proton states, they extended the formalism to relate the proton resonance width to the squared ANC of the mirror neutron bound state.

## Key numbers

- The ratio of squared ANCs for the $^7$Be$|^{8}$B to $^7$Li$|^{8}$Li mirror overlap is 1.17-1.22 (depending on NN potential and total angular momentum j=1/2 or 3/2), with analytical estimate ${\cal R}_0 = 1.12$
- For the $^{11}$C$|^{12}$N to $^{11}$B$|^{12}$B mirror overlap, the ratio is 1.42-1.46, with analytical estimate ${\cal R}_0 = 1.37$
- The experimental ratio ${\cal R}^{exp} = |C_p^{exp}/C_n^{exp}|^2$ for the $^8$B - $^8$Li mirror pair is 1.08 $\pm$ 0.15, agreeing with the average single-particle ratio $\langle {\cal R}_b \rangle = 1.01 \pm 0.01$ and analytical estimate ${\cal R}_0 = 1.12$
- For the $^{12}$N - $^{12}$B mirror pair, ${\cal R}^{exp} = 1.28 \pm 0.29$, with $\langle {\cal R}_b \rangle = 1.30 \pm 0.02$ and ${\cal R}_0 = 1.37$
- The proton width of the $^{12}$N(2$^+$) resonance is predicted to be $5.9 \pm 1.0$ keV (using $\langle {\cal R}_{\Gamma}^{s.p.} \rangle = (1.22 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{-5}$) or $6.9 \pm 1.2$ keV (using ${\cal R}_0^{res} = 1.42 \times 10^{-5}$), compared to the experimental upper limit of 20 keV
- For the $^{22}$Mg$|^{23}$Al to $^{22}$Ne$|^{23}$Ne mirror overlap, the analytical estimate ${\cal R}_0 = 2.61 \times 10^4$ with $\langle {\cal R}_b \rangle = 2.67 \times 10^4$
- For the $^{26}$Si$|^{27}$P to $^{26}$Mg$|^{27}$Mg mirror overlap, ${\cal R}_0 = 43.3$ with $\langle {\cal R}_b \rangle = 40.3 \pm 1.1$

## Context

This work extends earlier microscopic calculations by Timofeyuk (2000) and Timofeyuk and Igashov (2001) that studied ANCs for the mirror pairs $^8$B - $^8$Li and $^{12}$N - $^{12}$B, where the ratios were found to be practically independent of the NN force choice. The present paper provides a model-independent analytical explanation for this observation based on charge symmetry, and generalizes the relation to bound-unbound mirror pairs where the proton state is a resonance. This enables the use of neutron ANCs obtained from transfer reactions with stable beams (which are more accurate than experiments with radioactive beams) to predict astrophysically relevant proton capture cross sections, including the $^7$Be(p,$\gamma)^8$B reaction relevant to the solar neutrino problem, the $^{14}$N(p,$\gamma)^{15}$O reaction responsible for energy production in the CNO cycle, and the $^{22}$Mg(p,$\gamma)^{23}$Al and $^{26}$Si(p,$\gamma)^{27}$P reactions relevant to nova nucleosynthesis.
