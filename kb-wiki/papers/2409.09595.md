# Neutral pion to two-photons transition form factor revisited

> Based upon a combined formalism of Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equations in quantum chromodynamics (QCD), we propose a QCD kindred algebraic model for the dressed quark propagator, for the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude of the pion and the electromagnetic quark-photon interaction vertex. We then compute the $γ^{*}π^0γ$ transition form factor $G^{γ^{*}π^0γ}(Q^2)$ for a wide range of photon momentum transfer squared $Q^2$. The quark propagator is expanded out in its perturbative functional form but with dynamically generated dressed quark mass. It has complex conjugate pole singularities in the complex-momentum plane which is motivated by the solution of the quark gap equation with rainbow-ladder truncation of the infinite set of Schwinger-Dyson equations. This complex pole singularity structure of the quark propagator can be associated with a signal of confinement which prevents quarks to become stable asymptotic states. The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is expressed without a spectral density function, which encapsulate its low and large momentum behaviour. The QCD evolution of the distribution amplitude is also incorporated into our model through the direct implementation of Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage evolution equations. We include the effects of the quark anomalous magnetic moment in the description of the quark-photon vertex whose infrared enhancement is known to dictate hadronic properties. Once the QCD kindred model is constructed, we calculate the form factor $G^{γ^{*}π^0γ}(Q^2)$ and find it consistent with direct QCD-based studies as well as most available experimental data. It slightly exceeds the conformal limit for large $Q^2$ which might be attributed to the scaling violations in QCD. The associated interaction radius and neutral pion decay width turn out to be compatible with experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors propose a QCD kindred algebraic model (QAM) for the pion that reproduces the neutral pion transition form factor consistently with most experimental data, but the computed form factor slightly exceeds the conformal limit for large momentum transfer squared \(Q^2\), which might be attributed to scaling violations in QCD rather than a genuine deviation from asymptotic behavior.

## Method

The authors construct an algebraic model for the dressed quark propagator with complex conjugate pole singularities (mimicking confinement), a Bethe-Salpeter amplitude without a spectral density function, and a quark-photon vertex that includes a transverse anomalous magnetic moment term. They compute the \(\gamma^{*}\pi^0\gamma\) transition form factor \(G^{\gamma^{*}\pi^0\gamma}(Q^2)\) using the impulse approximation within the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equation formalism, incorporating QCD evolution of the pion distribution amplitude through the Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage equations at leading order.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant is computed as \(f_\pi = 0.0924\) GeV for both parameter sets at the scale \(\zeta = 2\) GeV.
* The second moment of the pion distribution amplitude is \(<(2x-1)^2> = 0.24\) (parameter set 1) and \(0.26\) (parameter set 2) at \(\zeta = 2\) GeV.
* The strength parameter \(\eta\) of the anomalous magnetic moment term is fixed to \(\eta = 0.598\) (parameter set 1) and \(\eta = 0.615\) (parameter set 2) to reproduce the chiral anomaly value \(G(0,0,0) = 1/2\) at \(\zeta = 2\) GeV.
* The QCD scale parameter used for evolution is \(\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} = 0.234\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the line of algebraic modeling of hadron properties within the Schwinger-Dyson and Bethe-Salpeter equation framework, following earlier SDE-based studies such as Maris and Tandy (2002) and Raya et al. (2015). The model addresses the experimental discrepancy between the BABAR (2009) and Belle (2012) measurements of the pion transition form factor at large \(Q^2\), where BABAR suggested unexpected scaling violations while Belle indicated the form factor approaches a constant. The results enable more precise predictions for the hadronic light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, relevant for ongoing precision tests of the Standard Model.
