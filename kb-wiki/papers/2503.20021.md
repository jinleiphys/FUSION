# Viscous Gubser flow with conserved charges to benchmark fluid simulations

> We present semi-analytical solutions for the evolution of both the temperature and chemical potentials for viscous Gubser flow with conserved charges. Such a solution can be especially useful in testing numerical codes intended to simulate relativistic fluids with large chemical potentials. The freeze-out hypersurface profiles for constant energy density are calculated, along with the corresponding normal vectors and presented as a new unit test for numerical codes. We also compare the influence of the equation of state on the semi-analytical solutions. We benchmark the newly developed Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) code CCAKE that includes both shear viscosity and three conserved charges. The numerical solutions are in excellent agreement with the semi-analytical solution and also are able to accurately reproduce the hypersurface at freeze-out.

## Key claim

The paper presents semi-analytical solutions for viscous Gubser flow that include the evolution of both temperature and chemical potentials for systems with conserved charges, but the solutions are limited to cases where diffusion is prohibited by the symmetries of Gubser flow, and the coupling between temperature and chemical potentials arises only through the equation of state rather than through direct dynamical coupling.

## Method

The authors derive ordinary differential equations for temperature, chemical potentials, and shear stress components by transforming the relativistic hydrodynamic equations from Milne coordinates to de Sitter space using Weyl rescaling, exploiting the symmetries of Gubser flow. They solve these equations numerically using two analytic conformal equations of state (EoS1 for a massless quark-gluon plasma and EoS2 for a generalized conformal form) and compare the semi-analytical results against numerical simulations from the Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics code CCAKE, which includes shear viscosity and three conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge). The freeze-out hypersurface is defined by a constant energy density criterion, and the corresponding normal vectors are computed.

- Shear viscosity parameter: \(\bar \eta = 0.2\) (constant multiplying entropy density)

- Gubser flow parameter: \(q = 1\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)

- Number of colors: \(N_c = 3.0\); number of flavors: \(N_f = 2.5\)
- Initial chemical potential to temperature ratios studied: \(\hat{\mu}_{Y,0}/\hat{T}_0 = 0, 10, 15\)

## Context

This work extends prior semi-analytical solutions for Gubser flow with conserved charges, which previously addressed ideal flow (Denicol 2018) and viscous flow without calculating chemical potential evolution (Du 2019). It also builds on Bjorken flow studies at finite chemical potential (Dore 2020, Dore 2022, Chattopadhyay 2022). The solutions provide a new benchmark test for numerical relativistic hydrodynamic codes that include conserved charges, such as the SPH code CCAKE (Plumberg 2024), enabling verification of code accuracy for systems with large chemical potentials relevant to the QCD phase diagram and neutron star mergers.
