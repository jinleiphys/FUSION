# The charge and mass symmetry breaking in the $KK\bar{K}$ system

> In the framework of the Faddeev equations in configuration space, we investigate the $K$(1460) meson as a resonant state of the $KK\bar{K}$ kaonic system. We perform calculations for the particle configurations $K^{0}K^{+}K^{-}$ and $K^{0}K^{+}\overline{{K}^{0}}$ within two models: the $ABC $ model, in which all three particles are distinguishable, and the $AAC$ model when two particles are identical. The models differ in their treatment of the kaon mass difference and the attractive Coulomb force between the $K^{+}K^{-}$ pair. We found that the Coulomb shift adds over 1 MeV to the three-body binding energy. The expected correction to the binding energy due to mass redistribution from $AA$ to $AB$ is found to be negligible, up to a maximum of 6\% of the relative mass correction. At the same time, the symmetry of the wave function is distorted depending on the mass ratio value. We found that the repulsive $KK$ interaction plays essential role in the binding energy of the $KK\bar K$ system and report the mass of 1461.8 or 1464.1 MeV for the neutral $K^{0}$(1460) and 1466.5 or 1468.8 MeV for the charged $K^{+}$(1460) resonances, respectively, depending on the parameter sets for $KK$ and $K\bar{K}$ interactions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $K(1460)$ meson can be described as a resonant state of the $KK\bar{K}$ system using a single-channel non-relativistic potential model, but the validity of treating the system with two identical particles (the $AAC$ model) depends on the mass difference between kaons: the correction to the binding energy from mass redistribution is negligible (up to a maximum of 6% of the relative mass correction) only when the mass difference is small, while the Coulomb interaction adds over 1 MeV to the three-body binding energy.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for the $K^{0}K^{+}K^{-}$ and $K^{0}K^{+}\overline{K}^{0}$ particle configurations using both the $ABC$ model (three distinguishable particles) and the $AAC$ model (two identical particles). They employed $s$-wave effective Gaussian potentials for $KK$ and $K\bar{K}$ interactions from Torres (2011) with two parameter sets (A and B), included experimental kaon masses, and treated the Coulomb attraction between $K^{+}K^{-}$ as a perturbation on the left-hand side of the Faddeev equations.

## Key numbers

* Coulomb shift adds over 1 MeV to the three-body binding energy of the $KK\bar{K}$ system.
* Correction to the binding energy due to mass redistribution from $AA$ to $AB$ is negligible, up to a maximum of 6% of the relative mass correction.
* Mass of the neutral $K^{0}(1460)$ resonance is 1461.8 or 1464.1 MeV, depending on the parameter set for $KK$ and $K\bar{K}$ interactions.
* Mass of the charged $K^{+}(1460)$ resonance is 1466.5 or 1468.8 MeV, depending on the parameter set for $KK$ and $K\bar{K}$ interactions.
* The $K\bar{K}$ potential parameters for Set A: $v_0 = -630.0 - 210i$ MeV for both $I=0$ and $I=1$, with range $b = 0.66$ fm; $KK$ potential: $v_0 = 0$ MeV for $I=0$, $v_0 = 104$ MeV for $I=1$.
* The $K\bar{K}$ potential parameters for Set B: $v_0 = -1155.0 - 283i$ MeV for both $I=0$ and $I=1$, with range $b = 0.47$ fm; $KK$ potential: $v_0 = 0$ MeV for $I=0$, $v_0 = 313$ MeV for $I=1$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the $KK\bar{K}$ system that used the $AAC$ model with average kaon masses and no Coulomb force, such as Torres (2011) and Kezerashvili (2020). It also relates to the dynamic generation of the $K(1460)$ resonance studied by Albaladejo (2015), Sekihara (2019), and Meissner (2022). The paper enables more precise calculations of kaonic three-body resonances by demonstrating when the simpler $AAC$ model is valid and when the full $ABC$ model with mass differences and Coulomb effects is required.
