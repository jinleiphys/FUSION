# The pairing Hamiltonian for one pair of nucleons bound in a potential well

> The problem of one pair of identical nucleons sitting in ${\cal N}$ single particle levels of a potential well and interacting through the pairing force is treated introducing, in the Hamiltonian formalism, even Grassmann variables. The eigenvectors are analytically expressed solely in terms of these with coefficients fixed by the eigenvalues and the single particle energies. For these a specific model is needed: in the case of the harmonic oscillator well, for any strength of the pairing interaction, an accurate expression is derived for both the collective eigenvalue and for those trapped in between the single particle levels. Notably the latter are labelled through an index upon which they depend parabolically.

## Key claim

The paper provides an almost analytic solution for the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of one pair of identical nucleons interacting via the pairing force in a harmonic oscillator potential well, for any strength of the pairing interaction. The trapped eigenvalues (those lying between single-particle levels) are shown to depend parabolically on an index labeling them, though this parabolic dependence is a conjecture that the authors verify numerically for the specific case of the harmonic oscillator.

## Method

The authors introduce even (commuting, nilpotent) Grassmann variables to describe a pair of fermions with vanishing total angular momentum projection, rewriting the pairing Hamiltonian in terms of these variables. They diagonalize the resulting matrix for the case of one pair distributed over N single-particle levels, obtaining the secular equation whose solutions separate into N degenerate "free" eigenvalues (seniority v=2 states) and N non-degenerate eigenvalues affected by pairing (seniority v=0 states). For the harmonic oscillator well, they use Viete's formulas and a parabolic ansatz for the trapped eigenvalues, solving the resulting nonlinear system numerically after obtaining an accurate expression for the collective eigenvalue through iterative expansion.

## Key numbers

- Pairing strength for protons: G ≈ 27/A MeV; for neutrons: G ≈ 22/A MeV, with A the nuclear mass number
- Average distance between single-particle levels: ≈ 0.69 MeV in Pb and ≈ 0.74 MeV in Sn
- For N=5 levels and G̃=5, the exact collective eigenvalue is -341.013793 (in units of ħω), while the leading-order approximation gives -341.0
- For N=5 levels and G̃=0.05, the exact collective eigenvalue is 2.74028 (in units of ħω), with the cubic approximation giving 2.53580 at zeroth order
- The parabolic approximation for trapped eigenvalues agrees with exact numerical solutions to better than 0.27% for the cases studied (N=5, G̃=0.05, 0.1, 1, and 5)
- For N=5 and G̃=5, the trapped eigenvalues range from approximately 3.1493 (N̄=0) to 9.9157 (N̄=3) in units of ħω

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Grassmann algebra approach for n pairs in a single level (Barbaro 2000) to the multi-level case, and generalizes Richardson's (1964) technique for the pairing Hamiltonian. The analytic expressions for eigenvalues and eigenvectors in the harmonic oscillator case provide a foundation for treating any number of fermion pairs, and the parabolic dependence of trapped eigenvalues may hold for other one-body potentials with known degeneracy. The results enable nearly analytic solutions for nuclear pairing problems that previously required purely numerical treatment.
