# Bayesian analysis of properties of nuclear matter with the FOPI experimental data

> Based on the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) transport model, combined with experimental data of directed flow, elliptic flow, and nuclear stopping power measured by FOPI in $\rm ^{197}Au+^{197}Au$ collisions at beam energies ($E_{lab}$) of 0.25 and 0.4 GeV/nucleon, the incompressibility of the nuclear equation of state $K_0$, the nucleon effective mass $m^*$, and the in-medium correction factor ($F$, with respect to free-space values) on the nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections are studied by Bayesian analysis. It is found that both $m^*$ and $F$ can be tightly constrained with the uncertainty $\le$ 15\%, however, $K_0$ cannot be constrained tightly. We deduce $m^*/m_0 = 0.78^{+0.09}_{-0.10}$ and $F = 0.75^{+0.08}_{-0.07}$ with experimental data at $E_{lab}$ = 0.25 GeV/nucleon, and the obtained values increased to $m^*/m_0 = 0.88^{+0.03}_{-0.03}$ and $F = 0.88^{+0.06}_{-0.07}$ at $E_{lab}$ = 0.4 GeV/nucleon. The obtained results are further verified with rapidity-dependent flow data.

## Key claim

The nucleon effective mass ratio \(m^*/m_0\) and the in-medium correction factor \(F\) for nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections can be tightly constrained (uncertainty \(\le\) 15%) from FOPI experimental data using Bayesian analysis, but the incompressibility \(K_0\) cannot be tightly constrained at the studied beam energies, with its posterior distribution remaining close to the prior.

## Method

The authors used the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate \(\rm ^{197}Au+^{197}Au\) collisions at beam energies of 0.25 and 0.4 GeV/nucleon, generating 150 parameter sets with \(K_0\) ranging from 180 to 380 MeV, \(m^*/m_0\) from 0.6 to 0.95, and \(F\) from 0.6 to 1.0. A Gaussian process emulator was trained on these simulations and combined with Bayesian inference using Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling to constrain the parameters against FOPI experimental data on directed flow slope \(v_{11}\), elliptic flow at mid-rapidity \(v_{20}\), and nuclear stopping power \(vartl\) for free protons.

## Key numbers

- At \(E_{lab}\) = 0.25 GeV/nucleon: \(m^*/m_0 = 0.78^{+0.09}_{-0.10}\) and \(F = 0.75^{+0.08}_{-0.07}\).
- At \(E_{lab}\) = 0.4 GeV/nucleon: \(m^*/m_0 = 0.88^{+0.03}_{-0.03}\) and \(F = 0.88^{+0.06}_{-0.07}\).
- The posterior distribution of \(K_0\) had a central value around 230 MeV at both beam energies, but was not tightly constrained.
- Both \(m^*\) and \(F\) were constrained with uncertainty \(\le\) 15%.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian analyses of nuclear matter properties using heavy-ion collision data, such as those by Wang (2018) who extracted \(K_0 = 220\pm40\) MeV from the same FOPI data using UrQMD, and by Le Fevre (2015) who found \(K_0 = 190\pm30\) MeV using the IQMD model. The results are consistent with recent studies by Li (2025) and Song (2023) showing an increase of \(m^*\) and \(F\) at higher beam energies. The constrained parameters can be used to improve transport model simulations of heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies and to test predictions from nuclear structure and neutron star observations.
