# Heavy and light flavor jet quenching in different collision systems at the LHC energies

> Recent experiments have observed large anisotropic collective flows in high multiplicity proton-lead collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), which indicates the possible formation of mini quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in small collision systems. However, no jet quenching has been confirmed in such small systems so far. To understand this intriguing result, the system size scan experiments have been proposed to bridge the gap between large and small systems. In this work, we perform a systematic study on both heavy and light flavor jet quenching in different collision systems at the LHC energies. Using our state-of-the-art jet quenching model, which combines the next-to-leading-order perturbative QCD framework, a linear Boltzmann transport model and the (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics simulation, we provide a good description of nuclear modification factor $R_{\rm AA}$ for charged hadrons and $D$ mesons in central and mid-central Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions measured by CMS collaboration. We further predict the transverse momentum and centrality dependences of $R_{AA}$ for charged hadrons, $D$ and $B$ mesons in Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, Ar+Ar and O+O collisions at the LHC energies. Our numerical results show a clear system size dependence for both light and heavy flavor hadron $R_{AA}$ across different collision systems. Sizable jet quenching effect is obtained for both heavy and light flavor hadrons in central O+O collisions at the LHC energies. Our study provides a significant bridge for jet quenching from large to small systems, and should be helpful for finding the smallest QGP droplet and the disappearance of QGP in relativistic nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a systematic study of both heavy and light flavor jet quenching across different collision systems (Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe, Ar+Ar, O+O) shows a clear system size dependence, and that sizable jet quenching is predicted for central O+O collisions at LHC energies, which could serve as a bridge to understanding the absence of jet quenching in proton-nucleus collisions. The hedge is that the prediction for O+O collisions is a numerical result from their model that awaits experimental confirmation.

## Method

The authors combine a next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD framework to compute high-pT hadron spectra in p+p collisions, a linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model to simulate elastic and inelastic interactions of hard partons with the QGP medium, and a (3+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamics simulation (CLVisc) to model the space-time evolution of the QGP fireball. Initial conditions for hydrodynamics are generated using the TRENTo model with Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions, and the model is calibrated to describe charged hadron and D meson R_AA data from CMS and ALICE for Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions before making predictions for Ar+Ar and O+O collisions.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron R_AA in central (0-10%) O+O collisions at 6.5A TeV reaches about 0.5 at pT ~ 8-10 GeV.
- The model describes CMS data for charged hadron R_AA in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02A TeV and Xe+Xe collisions at 5.44A TeV across centralities (0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%, 50-80%).
- The model describes CMS data for D meson R_AA in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02A TeV across centralities (0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%, 50-80%).
- The model describes ALICE data for heavy flavor decayed electron R_AA in Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02A TeV across centralities (0-10%, 10-30%, 30-50%, 50-80%).
- The strong coupling constant used for interactions with thermal partons in the medium is alpha_s = 0.25.
- The shear-viscosity-to-entropy-density-ratio is set to eta_v/s = 0.16.
- The switching temperature for converting the QGP to hadrons is T_sw = 137 MeV.
- The initial proper time for hydrodynamics is tau_0 = 0.6 fm for all collision systems.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of jet quenching in large systems (Pb+Pb, Xe+Xe) to smaller systems (Ar+Ar, O+O), motivated by the observation of large anisotropic flows in high-multiplicity p+Pb collisions at the LHC (Abelev 2012, Aad 2012, Chatrchyan 2013) and the lack of confirmed jet quenching in such small systems. It builds on the system size scan proposal (Citron 2018, Huang 2019) and previous hydrodynamics predictions for O+O collisions (Sievert 2019, Lim 2018) and heavy flavor studies (Katz 2019). The results provide predictions for future O+O runs at the LHC and aim to help identify the smallest QGP droplet and the disappearance of QGP in relativistic nuclear collisions.
