# Conformal window in QCD for large numbers of colours and flavours

> We conjecture that the phase transitions in QCD at large number of colours N\gg 1 is triggered by the drastic change in the instanton density. As a result of it, all physical observables also experience some sharp modification in the θbehaviour. This conjecture is motivated by the holographic model of QCD where confinement -deconfinement phase transition indeed happens precisely at temperature T=T_c where $θ$ dependence of the vacuum energy experiences a sudden change in behaviour: from N^2\cos(θ/N) at T<T_c to \cosθ\exp(-N) at T>T_c. This conjecture is also supported by recent lattice studies. We employ this conjecture to study a possible phase transition as a function of κ\equiv N_f/N from confinement to conformal phase in the Veneziano limit N_f\sim N when number of flavours and colours are large, but the ratio κis finite. Technically, we consider an operator which gets its expectation value solely from nonperturbative instaton effects. When κexceeds some critical value κ> κ_c the integral over instanton size is dominated by small-size instatons, making the instanton computations reliable with expected \exp(-N) behaviour. However, when κ<κ_c, the integral over instaton size is dominated by large-size instantons, and the instanton expansion breaks down. This regime with κ<κ_c corresponds to the confinement phase. We also compute the variation of the critical κ_c(T, μ) when the temperature and chemical potential T, μ\ll Λ_{QCD} slightly vary. We also discuss the scaling (x_i-x_j)^{-γ_{\rm det}} in the conformal phase.

## Key claim

The paper conjectures that the confinement-deconfinement phase transition in QCD at large number of colours \(N\gg 1\) is triggered by a drastic change in the instanton density, and that this same mechanism drives the transition from confinement to a conformal phase when the ratio \(\kappa \equiv N_f/N\) exceeds a critical value \(\kappa_c\), with the caveat that the analysis is performed in the large-\(N\) limit and the instanton expansion remains reliable only when \((\kappa - \kappa_c) \gg 1/N\).

## Method

The author studies the expectation value of the 't Hooft determinant operator \(\langle \det \bar{\psi}_L^f \psi_R^f \rangle\), which receives contributions solely from instantons, as a function of \(\kappa = N_f/N\) in the Veneziano limit where \(N_f \sim N\) and both are large. The convergence of the integral over instanton size \(\rho\) is analysed using the one-loop instanton density formula; when the integral is dominated by small-size instantons the instanton expansion is reliable and the system is in the deconfined ("molecular") phase, whereas when it is dominated by large-size instantons the expansion breaks down and the system is in the confined ("plasma") phase. The critical value \(\kappa_c\) is identified as the point where this transition occurs, and its dependence on small temperature \(T\) and chemical potential \(\mu\) is computed.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for pure gauge theory at \(N=\infty\) is estimated as \(T_c (N=\infty) \simeq 0.53 \Lambda_{QCD}\), where \(\Lambda_{QCD}\) is defined in the Pauli-Villars scheme.
* The critical chemical potential for \(N_f \ll N\) is estimated as \(\mu_c (N=\infty) \simeq 2.6 \cdot \sqrt{N / N_f} \cdot T_c (N=\infty, \mu=0)\).
* The instanton density near the critical point scales as \(\exp[-\alpha N (T-T_c)/T_c]\) with \(\alpha = 11/3\) in leading order, valid when \(1/N \ll (T-T_c)/T_c \ll 1\).
* The instanton density near the critical chemical potential scales as \(\exp[-\alpha N (\mu-\mu_c)/\mu_c]\) with the same \(\alpha = 11/3\), valid when \(1/N \ll (\mu-\mu_c)/\mu_c \ll 1\).
* The phase transition line for small chemical potential is given by \(T_c(\mu) = T_c(\mu=0)[1 - (3 N_f \mu^2)/(4 N \pi^2 T_c^2(\mu=0))]\) for \(\mu \ll \pi T_c\) and \(N_f \ll N\).
* The phase transition line for small temperature is given by \(\mu_c(T) = \mu_c(T=0)[1 - (N \pi^2 T^2)/(3 N_f \mu_c^2(T=0))]\) for \(\sqrt{N} T \ll \mu_c\) and \(N_f \ll N\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous conjecture (Parnachev and Zhitnitsky, 2008; Zhitnitsky, 2008) that the confinement-deconfinement phase transition is accompanied by sharp changes in the \(\theta\)-dependence of the vacuum energy, which was originally applied to finite temperature and chemical potential in pure gauge theory and QCD with few flavours. The present paper generalises this framework to the Veneziano limit where \(N_f \sim N\), addressing the conformal window problem previously studied analytically by Banks and Zaks (1982), Miransky (1996), Appelquist et al. (1996, 1998), Sannino (1999), Dietrich (2006), Ryttov (2007), and Pica (2010), as well as by lattice computations (Appelquist et al., 2007, 2009, 2011; Deuzeman et al., 2009, 2013; Fodor et al., 2011). The results enable predictions for how the conformal window extends to non-zero temperature and chemical potential, and provide a framework for testing the instanton-dissociation picture using weakly coupled deformed QCD models (Shifman and Unsal, 2008; Unsal, 2008; Poppitz et al., 2009, 2012, 2013; Thomas, 2011; Anber, 2013).
