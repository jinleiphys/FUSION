# Branchings in the gamma-process path revisited

> The location of the (gamma,p)/(gamma,n) and (gamma,alpha)/(gamma,n) line at gamma-process temperatures is discussed, using updated reaction rates based on global Hauser-Feshbach calculations. The results can directly be compared to classic gamma-process discussions. The nuclei exhibiting the largest sensitivity to uncertainties in nuclear structure and reaction parameters are specified and suggestions for experiments are made. Additionally, the impact of employing two recent global alpha+nucleus potentials is discussed. It is found that branchings at higher mass depend more sensitively on these potentials. The case of 146Sm/144Sm production is addressed separately. Also in this case, the more recent alpha+nucleus potentials seem to improve the issues concerning the production of these Sm isotopes in massive stars. In conclusion it is found that it is unlikely that the calculated underproduction of p nuclides in the Mo-Ru region is due to nuclear physics deficiencies but that problems at higher mass number may still be cured by improved nuclear input.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the long-standing underproduction of p-nuclides in the Mo-Ru region is unlikely to be caused by deficiencies in nuclear physics input, but that problems at higher mass numbers (above A ~ 150) may still be resolved by improved nuclear data, particularly through the use of updated alpha+nucleus optical potentials.

## Method

The author calculated photodisintegration branch points for the gamma-process path using the NON-SMOKER Hauser-Feshbach code with three different global alpha+nucleus optical potentials (rate sets A, B, and C) across the temperature range T9 = 2.0 to 3.0. Branch points were identified as the first nucleus in each isotopic chain where the sum of (gamma,p) and (gamma,alpha) rates exceeds the (gamma,n) rate, and the sensitivity of these branchings to rate variations was assessed by identifying nuclei where competing rates are within factors of 3 (for proton emission) or 10 (for alpha emission) of each other.

## Key numbers

- Branch points were computed for 49 elements from Se (Z=34) to Pb (Z=82) at three temperatures: T9 = 2.0, 2.5, and 3.0
- The half-life of 146Sm is 1.03 x 10^8 years, used as a chronometer via the 144Sm/142Nd ratio in circumstellar grains
- For (gamma,p) rates, uncertainties are estimated at a factor of 3; for (gamma,alpha) rates, uncertainties are estimated at a factor of 10
- Neutron capture rates have an average uncertainty of 30% based on comparisons with stable targets
- Branch points shifted by 2 neutron numbers in elements Ba, W, Au, Hg compared to the classic work of Woosley and Howard (1978); Tl shifted by 4 neutrons
- Priority 1 experimental targets include 14 (p,gamma) reactions and 16 (alpha,gamma) reactions on stable or long-lived (T1/2 >= 10^6 years) targets

## Context

This work directly extends the classic gamma-process path analysis of Woosley and Howard (1978) by using updated Hauser-Feshbach reaction rates from Rauscher and Thielemann (2000) and comparing three global alpha+nucleus optical potentials (McFadden and Satchler 1966, Avrigeanu et al. 2005, and Frohlich and Rauscher 2005). The results enable experimentalists to prioritize measurements on the most sensitive branch-point nuclei rather than arbitrary nuclei near the gamma-process path, and provide guidance for future radioactive ion beam facilities (GSI, RIKEN, RIA) to address the remaining underproduction of p-nuclides in the mass range A = 151-167.
