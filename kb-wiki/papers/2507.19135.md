# Classification of flavor dependence of Chiral Magnetic Effect with Deep Neural Network using multiple correlators

> We study the flavor dependence of the Chiral Magnetic Effect (CME) by analyzing two key charge-separation correlators used to characterize the charge separation effect: the conventional $Δγ$ and the recently proposed $R_{ψ_2}$. Using the AMPT (A Multiphase Transport) model with an initial-state centrality-dependent charge separation, we evaluate the sensitivity of these correlators to 2-flavor ($u,d$) and 3-flavor ($u,d,s$) quark scenarios. While both correlators exhibit modest flavor dependence in mid-central (30-50\%) collisions, their discriminative power varies significantly with centrality and transverse momentum ($p_T$), limiting their utility disentangling the flavor dependent scenarios. To overcome these limitations, we develop a neural network classifier trained on final-state hadronic observables (e.g., $dN_{ch}/dη$, $p_T$ spectra). The model achieves $>90\%$ accuracy in flavor classification by leveraging multi-observable correlations, with $p_T$-differential features proving particularly discriminative. Crucially, by incorporating background contributions directly into the training data, our approach provides more reliable flavor estimates than correlator-only methods.

## Key claim

The paper claims that conventional charge-separation correlators ($\Delta\gamma$ and $R_{\psi_2}$) show only modest and centrality-dependent discriminative power for distinguishing 2-flavor ($u,d$) from 3-flavor ($u,d,s$) CME scenarios, but a deep neural network classifier trained on multiple final-state hadronic observables achieves over 90% accuracy in flavor classification, provided background contributions are incorporated directly into the training data.

## Method

The authors introduced initial-state centrality-dependent charge separation into the AMPT (A Multiphase Transport) model using a semi-empirical switching fraction formula, separately for 2-flavor (up and down quarks only) and 3-flavor (up, down, and strange quarks) scenarios in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. They computed the conventional $\Delta\gamma$ correlator and the $R_{\psi_2}$ correlator as functions of centrality and transverse momentum, then trained a convolutional neural network (CNN) on 2D histograms of correlated final-state observables (charged particle multiplicity, transverse momentum, elliptic flow, transverse energy, and azimuthal distributions) generated from $5\times10^5$ AMPT events per flavor class.

## Key numbers

- The neural network classifier achieves **>90% accuracy** in distinguishing 2-flavor from 3-flavor CME scenarios in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV for 30-50% centrality.
- The difference between 2-flavor and 3-flavor $\Delta\gamma$ values is approximately **$10^{-4}$** across all charge separation fractions studied ($f=5\%, 7.5\%, 10\%$).
- The 3-flavor scenario with $f=10\%$ agrees with STAR data for $\gamma_{SS}$ at low $P_+$ (<1.2 GeV/c), while the 2-flavor equivalent overpredicts it; for $P_+>1.2$ GeV/c, the 2-flavor model with $f=10\%$ or the 3-flavor model with $f=7.5\%$ provides better agreement.
- The 2-flavor scenario with $f=5\%$ gives the best match to STAR $\Delta\gamma$ data in 30-50% centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of flavor dependence in the CME by Huang et al., who used the $\gamma$ correlator in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV and found insufficient statistics to disentangle 2-flavor and 3-flavor scenarios. The authors provide the first theoretical calculation of the $R_{\psi_2}$ correlator for flavor-dependent CME in Au+Au collisions, finding that this correlator shows better separation between flavor scenarios than $\Delta\gamma$. The neural network approach, building on prior CNN applications in heavy-ion collisions, enables flavor classification from final-state observables without requiring the computationally expensive ensemble averaging needed for correlator-based methods, and could be applied to experimental data from RHIC and LHC to constrain the role of strange quarks in parity-violating currents.
