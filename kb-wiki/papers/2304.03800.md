# Towards $\it{ab}$-$\it{initio}$ nuclear theory calculations of $δ_\mathrm{C}$

> We propose a new theory framework to study the isospin-symmetry breaking correction $δ_\text{C}$ in superallowed nuclear beta decays, crucial for the precise determination of $|V_{ud}|$. Based on a general assumptions of the isovector dominance in ISB interactions, we construct a set of functions $F_{T_z}$ which involve nuclear matrix elements of isovector monopole operators and the nuclear Green's function. Via the functions $F_{T_z}$, a connection of $δ_\text{C}$ to measurable electroweak nuclear radii is established, providing an experimental gauge of the theory accuracy of $δ_\text{C}$. We outline a strategy to perform ab-initio calculations of $F_{T_z}$ based on the Lanczos algorithm, and discuss its similarity with other nuclear-structure-dependent inputs in nuclear beta decays.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the isospin-symmetry breaking correction δ_C in superallowed nuclear beta decays can be connected to measurable electroweak nuclear radii through a set of universal functions F_{T_z}(ζ), providing an experimental gauge of theory accuracy, but this connection relies on the assumption of isovector dominance in the ISB interaction, which is stated to be a good approximation only for nuclei with A ≥ 26 at the ~10% precision level.

## Method

The authors construct generating functions F_{T_z}(ζ) (for T_z = -1, 0, 1) that involve nuclear matrix elements of isovector monopole operators and the nuclear Green's function, then expand these functions around the ground-state energy E_{g,1} to obtain expansion coefficients α_{T_z} and β_{T_z}. These coefficients are related to the ISB observables ΔM_A^(1) and ΔM_B^(1) (constructed from electroweak nuclear radii) and to δ_C, establishing a sum-rule-like connection. The authors test the approach using conventional shell-model calculations for the A=38 isotriplet with the FSU interaction in the spsdpf model space, computing one-body density matrix elements and energy levels of ³⁸K(0⁺) states.

## Key numbers

- The ratio |b/c| ≥ 15 for A ≥ 26 in J^P = 0⁺, T = 1 isomultiplets, increasing with A, supporting isovector dominance
- For A=38, the relative error Δ_{nℓ} of the approximated Coulomb potential ranges from -0.004% (1s orbital) to -0.2% (2s orbital), well below the ~10% precision goal for δ_C
- The shell-model calculation for A=38 predicts only T=1 states in ³⁸K(0⁺) at excitation energies 0.000, 6.029, and 16.464 MeV, with vanishing generating functions due to the model space being limited to sd-shell orbitals

## Context

This work extends the idea from Seng (2022) that identified ISB observables ΔM_{A,B}^(1) from electroweak nuclear radii, moving from a model-dependent proportionality relation to a model-independent framework using generating functions. The approach contrasts with existing model calculations of δ_C based on Woods-Saxon potential (Towner 2002, 2007; Hardy 2008), Hartree-Fock (Ormand 1989, 1995), density functional theory (Satula 2011, 2016), random-phase approximation (Liang 2009), and isovector monopole resonance sum rules (Auerbach 2008), which show no sign of convergence. The framework enables future ab-initio calculations using the Lanczos algorithm that simultaneously predict both δ_C and the measurable radii observables, allowing direct experimental validation of theory uncertainties.
