# Antimagnetic Rotation Band in Nuclei: A Microscopic Description

> Covariant density functional theory and the tilted axis cranking method are used to investigate antimagnetic rotation (AMR) in nuclei for the first time in a fully self-consistent and microscopic way. The experimental spectrum as well as the B(E2) values of the recently observed AMR band in 105Cd are reproduced very well. This gives a further strong hint that AMR is realized in specific bands in nuclei.

# Antimagnetic Rotation Band in Nuclei: A Microscopic Description

## Key claim
Covariant density functional theory combined with the tilted axis cranking method reproduces the experimental spectrum and B(E2) values of the antimagnetic rotation band in 105Cd very well, providing a strong hint that AMR is realized in specific bands in nuclei. The authors qualify that this is the first fully self-consistent and microscopic description, and that polarization effects play a very important role in achieving agreement with data.

## Method
The tilted axis cranking (TAC) method based on covariant density functional theory with the point-coupling Lagrangian PC-PK1 was applied to the odd-A nucleus 105Cd. The Kohn-Sham equations in the form of a Dirac equation with effective fields were solved iteratively in a 3D Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis with N=10 major shells, allowing rotations around an axis in the (x,z)-plane (2D cranking). The calculations treated the configuration of three aligned neutrons (ν[h11/2(g7/2)2]) coupled to a pair of g9/2 proton holes, with no additional parameters and pairing correlations neglected.

## Key numbers
- The calculated B(E2) values for the AMR band in 105Cd are very small, less than 0.14 e²b², and decrease with increasing spin.
- The β deformation of 105Cd decreases from 0.2 to 0.14 with increasing rotational frequency, at a small and near-constant triaxiality (γ ≤ 9°).
- The bandhead angular momentum is I = 23/2 ħ for the negative-parity yrast band in 105Cd.
- The "core" contributes about 3 ħ of angular momentum when the rotational frequency increases from the bandhead to the maximum value.
- Without polarization, a maximal angular momentum of roughly 17 ħ is reached, beyond which higher values cannot be obtained even at rather high frequencies.

## Context
This work extends previous phenomenological studies of antimagnetic rotation, which had been investigated only with simple models such as the pairing plus quadrupole model (Frauendorf 2001) and the microscopic-macroscopic model (Simons 2003, Simons 2005, Datta 2005, Choudhury 2010). Prior theoretical treatments either neglected polarization effects entirely or included them only partially by minimizing the rotating energy surface with respect to a few deformation parameters. The present fully self-consistent microscopic approach using covariant density functional theory enables a proper treatment of nuclear currents and polarization effects, which are shown to be essential for reproducing the experimental B(E2) values and the deformation evolution along the band. This work enables future microscopic investigations of AMR in other candidate nuclei such as 109Cd and 100Pd, where the occurrence of AMR has not yet been fully confirmed.
