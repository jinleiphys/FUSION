# Phase Structure of Hot and/or Dense QCD with the Schwinger-Dyson Equation

> We investigate the phase structure of hot and/or dense QCD using the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) with the improved ladder approximation in the Landau gauge. We show that the phase transition from the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase is of second order, and that the scaling properties of the Majorana mass gap and the diquark condensate are consistent with mean field scaling. We examine the effect of the antiquark contribution and find that setting the antiquark Majorana mass equal to the quark one is a good approximation in the medium density region. We also study the effect of the Debye screening mass of the gluon and find that ignoring it causes the critical lines to move to the region of higher temperature and higher chemical potential.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the phase transition from the two-flavor color superconducting (2SC) phase to the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) phase is of second order, with scaling properties of the Majorana mass gap and diquark condensate consistent with mean field scaling, but this result depends on the improved ladder approximation in the Landau gauge and the specific regularization of the running coupling.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDEs) for the Dirac masses (B1, B3) and the quark and antiquark Majorana masses (Δ⁻, Δ⁺) using the improved ladder approximation in the Landau gauge, with the gluon propagator including Debye screening mass via the hard thermal/dense loop approximation. They used two types of running coupling with infrared regularization (type I with tf=0.5, tc=-2.0 and type II with tf=0.25) and determined the true vacuum by comparing effective potential values. The analysis was performed using the imaginary time formalism with Matsubara sums restricted to positive frequencies, and the spatial momentum was discretized using a logarithmic transformation with UV and IR cutoffs.

## Key numbers

- Λ_QCD = 583 MeV for running coupling (I) and Λ_QCD = 567 MeV for running coupling (II), determined by fitting the pion decay constant f_π = 88 MeV in the chiral limit via the Pagels-Stokar formula
- The antiquark Majorana mass is of the same order as the quark Majorana mass in the low and medium density region where μ ≲ 600 MeV
- The transition point between the two discretization schemes for momentum integration was set at μ₀/Λ_QCD = 1/3

## Context

This work extends the author's previous zero-temperature analysis (Takagi, 2002) of the hadron-to-2SC phase transition to non-zero temperature, and builds on earlier SDE studies of chiral symmetry restoration at finite temperature and chemical potential (Tani, 1998; Blaschke, 1997; Harada, 1998). The paper enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram including color superconducting phases, with implications for neutron star astrophysics and heavy ion collision phenomenology. The analysis also contrasts with approaches using contact 4-Fermi interactions (Berges, 1999; Rapp, 1998; Schwarz, 1999; Kitazawa, 2001) by including momentum-dependent gluon exchange and distinguishing quark from antiquark Majorana masses.
