# Towards the understanding of jet substructures and cross sections in heavy ion collisions using soft collinear effective theory

> The jet quenching phenomenon in heavy ion collisions provides a strong evidence of the modification of parton shower in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP). Jet substructure observables can probe various aspects of the jet formation mechanism. They contain useful information about the QGP and allow us to study the medium properties in great details. Here we present theoretical calculations of jet shapes and cross sections in proton-proton and lead-lead collisions at the LHC using soft-collinear effective theory, with Glauber gluon interactions in the medium. We find that resumming large logarithms in the jet substructure calculation is necessary for precise theoretical predictions. The resummation is performed using renormalization group evolution between characteristic jet scales. We also find that the medium induces power corrections to jet shapes. In the end we present the comparison between our calculations with the recent measurements at the LHC with very good agreement. Our calculations help initiate precise jet modification studies in heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim
Resummation of large logarithms in jet substructure calculations using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET) is necessary for precise theoretical predictions, and the medium induces power corrections to jet shapes; the non-trivial jet shape modification pattern observed in heavy ion collisions is due to both the increase of the quark jet fraction (which narrows the shape) and the broadening of the jet-by-jet shape.

## Method
The author calculated jet shapes and cross sections in proton-proton and lead-lead collisions at the LHC using SCET extended with Glauber gluon interactions (SCET_G) to describe medium interactions. Resummation was performed at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy using renormalization group evolution between characteristic jet scales, and medium-induced splitting functions were used to compute the modification of jet energy functions. The jet shape was defined as the fraction of transverse energy inside a subcone of size r relative to the full jet radius R, and the differential jet shape ρ(r) was derived from the integral jet shape Ψ(r).

## Key numbers
- Jet shape calculations were performed for anti-kT jets with R=0.3, pT>100 GeV, and 0.3<|y|<2 in proton-proton collisions at 2.76 TeV LHC (compared to CMS data).
- Jet shape calculations were also performed for anti-kT jets with R=0.7 at 7 TeV LHC (compared to CMS data).
- Nuclear modification factor R_AA was calculated for anti-kT jets with R=0.3, |η|<2 as a function of pT in central lead-lead collisions at √s_NN=2.76 TeV.
- The jet scale μ_jr was identified as ω tan(r/2) ≈ E_J × r, which eliminates large logarithms in fixed-order calculations.

## Context
This work extends the author's previous SCET calculations of jet shapes in proton-proton collisions (Chien 2014) and lead-lead collisions (Chien 2015) to a conference summary, and builds on the SCET framework developed by Bauer et al. (2000-2002) and Beneke (2002) with Glauber gluon interactions from Idilbi (2008) and Ovanesyan (2011). The calculations agree with measurements from ALICE (Abelev 2013), ATLAS (Aad 2012, 2014), and CMS (Chatrchyan 2013, CMS preliminary) at the LHC, and enable precise jet modification studies in heavy ion collisions, including predictions for inclusive and photon-tagged jets at LHC Run 2.
