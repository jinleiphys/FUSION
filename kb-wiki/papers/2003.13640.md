# Parameterization and applications of the low-$Q^2$ nucleon vector form factors

> We present the proton and neutron vector form factors in a convenient parametric form that is optimized for momentum transfers $\lesssim$ few GeV$^2$. The form factors are determined from a global fit to electron scattering data and precise charge radius measurements. A new treatment of radiative corrections is applied. This parametric representation of the form factors, uncertainties and correlations provides an efficient means to evaluate many derived observables. We consider two classes of illustrative examples: neutrino-nucleon scattering cross sections at GeV energies for neutrino oscillation experiments and nucleon structure corrections for atomic spectroscopy. The neutrino-nucleon charged current quasielastic (CCQE) cross section differs by 3-5% compared to commonly used form factor models when the vector form factors are constrained by recent high-statistics electron-proton scattering data from the A1 Collaboration. Nucleon structure parameter determinations include: the magnetic and Zemach radii of the proton and neutron, $[r_M^p, r_M^n] = [ 0.739(41)(23), 0.776(53)(28)]$ fm and $[r_Z^p, r_Z^n] = [ 1.0227(94)(51), -0.0445(14)(3)]$ fm; the Friar radius of nucleons, $[(r^p_F)^3, (r^n_F)^3] = [2.246(58)(2), 0.0093(6)(1)]$ fm$^3$; the electric curvatures, $[\langle r^4 \rangle^p_E, \langle r^4 \rangle^n_E ] = [1.08(28)(5), -0.33(24)(3)]$ fm$^4$; and bounds on the magnetic curvatures, $[ \langle r^4 \rangle^p_M, \langle r^4 \rangle^n_M ] = [ -2.0(1.7)(0.8), -2.3(2.1)(1.1)]$ fm$^4$. The first and dominant uncertainty is propagated from the experimental data and radiative corrections, and the second error is due to the fitting procedure.

## Key claim

The paper provides a compact parametric representation of proton and neutron vector form factors optimized for momentum transfers below a few GeV$^2$, with full covariance information, enabling systematic uncertainty propagation into derived observables; the neutrino-nucleon charged current quasielastic cross section differs by 3-5% compared to commonly used form factor models when vector form factors are constrained by recent high-statistics electron-proton scattering data from the A1 Collaboration.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit to elastic electron scattering data (including the Mainz A1 Collaboration dataset, world unpolarized cross-section data, and polarization data) combined with precise charge radius measurements from muonic hydrogen spectroscopy and neutron scattering, using a $z$-expansion parameterization of the Sachs electric and magnetic form factors with $k_{\rm max}=8$ coefficients and sum-rule constraints. A new treatment of radiative corrections was applied, including a dispersive evaluation of two-photon exchange corrections below $Q^2=1$ GeV$^2$ and a phenomenological correction above that scale, with the discrepancy between models treated as an additional uncertainty.

## Key numbers

* Proton electric charge radius: $r_E^p = 0.84089(39)$ fm (from the proton-only fit using Mainz data and muonic hydrogen constraint)
* Proton magnetic radius: $r_M^p = 0.739(41)(23)$ fm (first uncertainty from experimental data and radiative corrections, second from fitting procedure)
* Neutron magnetic radius: $r_M^n = 0.776(53)(28)$ fm
* Proton Zemach radius: $r_Z^p = 1.0227(94)(51)$ fm
* Neutron Zemach radius: $r_Z^n = -0.0445(14)(3)$ fm
* Proton Friar radius cubed: $(r^p_F)^3 = 2.246(58)(2)$ fm$^3$
* Neutron Friar radius cubed: $(r^n_F)^3 = 0.0093(6)(1)$ fm$^3$
* Proton electric curvature: $\langle r^4 \rangle^p_E = 1.08(28)(5)$ fm$^4$
* Neutron electric curvature: $\langle r^4 \rangle^n_E = -0.33(24)(3)$ fm$^4$
* Neutron electric charge radius squared: $\langle r^2 \rangle^n_E = -0.1161(22)$ fm$^2$ (from neutron scattering length measurements, used as input constraint)

## Context

This work extends the global form factor fit of Ye, Hill, and Arrington (2017) by providing a low-$Q^2$ optimized parameterization with full covariance matrices, enabling systematic uncertainty propagation into neutrino oscillation experiments and muonic atom spectroscopy. The analysis incorporates the muonic hydrogen charge radius measurement from Pohl (2010) and Antognini (2013), which was not included in the earlier fit, and applies a new dispersive treatment of two-photon exchange corrections. The results enable improved calculations of neutrino-nucleon charged current quasielastic cross sections for experiments like DUNE and T2HK, and provide nucleon structure parameters (Zemach radii, Friar radii, curvatures) needed for interpreting muonic atom spectroscopy measurements.
