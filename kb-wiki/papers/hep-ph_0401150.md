# A possibility to determine the P-parity of the $־˜^+$ pentaquark in the ${p}{n}\to ־›^0־˜^+$ reaction

> Spin structure of the reaction ${\vec p}{\vec n}\to {\vec ־›^0} {\vec ־˜}$ is analysed at the threshold in a model independent way under assumption that the $־˜^+$ is an isosinglet. We found that the sign of the spin-spin correlation parameter $C_{x,x}$ being measured in a double-spin experiment, determines the P-parity of the $־˜^+$ unambiguously. Furthermore we show that the polarization coefficients $K_x^x,K_y^y$ and $K_z^z$ which describe the polarization transfer from polarized beam or target to the final $־›^0$ and $־˜^+$ are nonzero for a positive parity of the $־˜^+$ and equal zero for a negative parity. It allows one to determine the P-parity of the $־˜^+$ in a single-spin measurement, since the polarization of the $־›^0$ can be measured via its decay $־›^0\to ֿ€^-+ p$.

## Key claim

The sign of the spin-spin correlation parameter \(C_{x,x}\) in the reaction \(\vec p \vec n \to \Lambda^0 \Theta^+\) near threshold determines the P-parity of the \(\Theta^+\) unambiguously, under the assumption that the \(\Theta^+\) is an isosinglet with spin \(J = \frac{1}{2}\). Additionally, the polarization transfer coefficients \(K_x^x, K_y^y, K_z^z\) are nonzero for positive parity and zero for negative parity, enabling parity determination in a single-spin measurement.

## Method

The author performed a model-independent analysis of the spin structure of the reaction \(pn \to \Lambda^0 \Theta^+\) near threshold, assuming conservation of P-parity, total angular momentum, and isospin, and applying the generalized Pauli principle for nucleons. The analysis considered only S-wave dominance in the final state at low excess energy (less than a few tens of MeV), and separately treated the cases of negative parity (\(^1P_1 \to ^3S_1\) transition) and positive parity (\(^3S_1-^3D_1 \to ^3S_1\) transition) of the \(\Theta^+\). The spin-spin correlation parameters and spin-transfer coefficients were derived from the transition matrix elements in nonrelativistic formalism.

## Key numbers

- For negative parity of the \(\Theta^+\), the spin-spin correlation parameters are \(C_{xx} = C_{yy} = C_{zz} = -1\).
- For positive parity of the \(\Theta^+\), the coefficients \(C_{x,x}\) and \(C_{y,y}\) are positive and equal to \(A = \frac{|G+F|^2}{|G+F|^2+2|G|^2}\).
- For positive parity, the spin-transfer coefficients are \(K_x^x = K_y^y = 2\frac{|G|^2 + \text{Re} GF^*}{|G+F|^2+2|G|^2}\) and \(K_z^z = 2\frac{|G|^2}{|G+F|^2+2|G|^2}\).
- For negative parity, all spin-transfer coefficients \(K_j^i = 0\) for \(i,j = x,y,z\).
- The proposed experiment at COSY would use a polarized proton beam on a deuteron target in the quasi-free region with spectator proton momentum less than \(\approx 50\) MeV/c, corresponding to excess energy less than 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent parity determination methods previously suggested by Thomas (2004) and Hanhart (2004) for the reaction \(\vec p \vec p \to \Sigma^+ \Theta^+\), and by Rekalo (2004) for photoproduction of the \(\Theta^+\). The author shows that the reaction \(pn \to \Lambda^0 \Theta^+\) offers an advantage because the polarization of the final \(\Lambda^0\) can be measured via its weak decay \(\Lambda^0 \to \pi^- + p\), enabling a single-spin experiment that is simpler than the double-spin measurement required in the \(pp\) reaction. The method also allows simultaneous determination of both the P-parity and isospin of the \(\Theta^+\) when combined with measurements from the \(pp \to \Sigma^+ \Theta^+\) reaction.
