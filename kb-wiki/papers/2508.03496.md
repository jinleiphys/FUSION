# Hyperon spin correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Recent experimental data show an unexpectedly large spin alignment of $ϕ$ mesons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, which can be explained by short-distance fluctuations of strong-force fields (vector $ϕ$ fields) within the constituent-quark model. We calculate the hyperon spin correlations within the same model, taking into account hydrodynamic effects and a $ϕ$ field fluctuating in space-time according to a Gaussian distribution. The $Λ\barΛ$ spin correlation induced by the $ϕ$ field is shown to be negative as opposed to that of $ΛΛ$ or $\barΛ\barΛ$. We thus propose a new net spin-correlation observable as a sensitive probe to separate strong-force effects from hydrodynamic ones. With the strength of the field fluctuations extracted from the observed $ϕ$ spin alignment, we predict the collision-energy dependence of the hyperon spin correlations and also investigate the dependence of the net spin correlation on azimuthal-angle and rapidity difference.

# Hyperon spin correlation in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a fluctuating strong-force field (vector phi field) can induce measurable spin correlations between Lambda and anti-Lambda hyperons in heavy-ion collisions, with the sign of the correlation being opposite for same-sign pairs (Lambda-Lambda or anti-Lambda-anti-Lambda) compared to opposite-sign pairs (Lambda-anti-Lambda). This net spin correlation is presented as a sensitive probe to separate strong-force field effects from conventional hydrodynamic contributions, provided that the field fluctuation strength extracted from phi meson spin alignment data is used to make quantitative predictions.

## Method

The authors employ the CLVisc (3+1)-D viscous hydrodynamic model with SMASH fluctuating initial conditions to generate freeze-out hypersurfaces and hydrodynamic fields for Au+Au collisions at multiple beam energies. They calculate hyperon spin polarization from four sources: thermal vorticity, thermal shear tensor, spin Hall effect, and a fluctuating phi field tensor with Gaussian space-time correlations. The spin correlations are computed via a Cooper-Frye type averaging over freeze-out surfaces, using the constituent-quark model relation that Lambda hyperon spin is carried entirely by the strange quark. The phi field fluctuation parameters are extracted from fits to STAR phi meson spin alignment data, and the field correlation length is taken as sigma_{t,x} = 0.6 fm (with variations of +/- 0.3 fm).

## Key numbers

- The phi field fluctuation parameters extracted from STAR data are parametrized as ln(F_{T,z}^2 / m_pi^2) = a_{T,z} - b_{T,z} ln(sqrt(s_NN)/GeV), with a_T = 3.90 +/- 1.11, b_T = 0.924 +/- 0.234, a_z = 3.33 +/- 0.917, b_z = 0.760 +/- 0.189, and m_pi = 0.138 GeV.

- The net spin correlation c_net is predicted to be of order 10^{-4} for sigma_{t,x} = 0.6 fm, requiring at least 10^8 pairs for measurement, achievable in the RHIC BES-II program.

- Spin correlations are calculated for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7, 14.5, 27, 39, and 62.4 GeV, within 20-50% centrality, for hyperons at central rapidity |Y| < 1 with transverse momentum p_T in (0.5, 3) GeV/c.

- The freeze-out hypersurface is determined by local energy density reaching 0.4 GeV/fm^3.

- The field correlation length is taken as sigma_{t,x} = 0.6 (+/- 0.3) fm, comparable to the radius of light mesons.

- When delta-phi = pi, the hydrodynamic contribution to spin correlation is approximately equal to the product of Lambda and anti-Lambda global polarizations, P_Lambda * P_anti-Lambda ~ O(10^{-4}).

## Context

This work extends the model previously applied to explain the unexpectedly large phi meson spin alignment observed by the STAR collaboration (STAR 2022), where conventional mechanisms such as fluid vorticity or magnetic field (Yang 2017, Sheng 2019, 2022) were two orders of magnitude too small. The authors build on their earlier studies of strong-force field fluctuations (Sheng 2019, 2022, 2023) that successfully described the transverse-momentum dependence of phi meson spin alignment. The net spin correlation observable is designed to isolate the strong-force field contribution from hydrodynamic effects (thermal vorticity, thermal shear, spin Hall effect) that produce nearly identical correlations for same-sign and opposite-sign hyperon pairs. The predicted azimuthal-angle and rapidity dependence of the net spin correlation can be tested in the RHIC BES-II program, providing constraints on the space-time scale of strong-force field fluctuations in the quark-gluon plasma.
