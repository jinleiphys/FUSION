# Improved structure of calcium isotopes from ab initio calculations

> The in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) is a powerful and flexible many-body method to compute the structure of nuclei starting from nuclear forces. Recent developments have extended the IMSRG from its standard truncation at the normal-ordered two-body level, the IMSRG(2), to a precision approximation including normal-ordered three-body operators, the IMSRG(3)-$N^7$. This improvement provides a more precise solution to the many-body problem and makes it possible to quantify many-body uncertainties in IMSRG calculations. We explore the structure of $^{44,48,52}$Ca using the IMSRG(3)-$N^7$, focusing on understanding existing discrepancies of the IMSRG(2) to experimental results. We find a significantly better description of the first $2^+$ excitation energy of $^{48}$Ca, improving the description of the shell closure at $N=28$. At the same time, we find that the IMSRG(3)-$N^7$ corrections to charge radii do not resolve the systematic underprediction of the puzzling large charge radius difference between $^{52}$Ca and $^{48}$Ca. We present estimates of many-body uncertainties of IMSRG(2) calculations applicable also to other systems based on the size extensivity of the method.

## Key claim

The IMSRG(3)-N7 truncation provides significantly better description of the first 2+ excitation energy in 48Ca compared to IMSRG(2), but does not resolve the systematic underprediction of the charge radius difference between 52Ca and 48Ca, indicating that higher-order many-body corrections alone cannot explain this discrepancy.

## Method

The authors performed IMSRG calculations at both the standard IMSRG(2) truncation (normal-ordered two-body level) and the improved IMSRG(3)-N7 truncation (including normal-ordered three-body operators) for 44,48,52Ca using the 1.8/2.0 (EM) chiral Hamiltonian. They employed both single-reference IMSRG and valence-space IMSRG (VS-IMSRG) formulations with a 40Ca core and neutron valence space consisting of the 1f7/2, 2p3/2, 2p1/2, and 1f5/2 orbitals, using a harmonic oscillator basis with emax=10 and hw=16 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The IMSRG(2) ground-state energy of 48Ca is -415.8 MeV, while the VS-IMSRG(2) gives -417.1 MeV, a difference of 1.3 MeV that reduces to 0.7 MeV at IMSRG(3)-N7 with emax3b=6, E3max=18
- The IMSRG(2) charge radius of 48Ca is 3.300 fm, while VS-IMSRG(2) gives 3.307 fm, a difference of -0.007 fm that reduces to -0.004 fm at IMSRG(3)-N7 with emax3b=6, E3max=18
- The experimental first 2+ excitation energy of 48Ca is 3.832 MeV, substantially overpredicted by both VS-IMSRG(2) and EOM-CCSD
- The ground-state energy of 48Ca is converged within 500 keV, the 2+ energy within 150 keV, the charge radius within 0.002 fm, and the neutron skin within 0.0015 fm at emax=10
- The IMSRG(3)-N7 correction to the neutron skin of 48Ca is about 0.005 fm, approximately 3-4% of the total neutron skin

## Context

This work extends the IMSRG methodology beyond the standard IMSRG(2) truncation used in prior studies by Hergert (2016) and Stroberg (2017, 2019), following the IMSRG(3) development by Heinz (2021). The results are compared with coupled-cluster calculations including CCSD, Lambda-CCSD(T), and CCSDT-1 from Hagen (2016). The improved description of the 48Ca 2+ excitation energy resolves a long-standing discrepancy, while the persistent underprediction of the 52Ca-48Ca charge radius difference points to the need for improved nuclear forces or additional physics beyond the many-body method.
