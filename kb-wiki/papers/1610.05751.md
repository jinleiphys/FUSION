# Metastable pions in dense media

> We study the leptonic decay of charged pions in a compact star environment. Considering leptons as a degenerated Fermi system, pions are tightly constrained to decay into these particles because their Fermi levels are occupied. Thus, pion decay is only possible through thermal fluctuations. Under these circumstances, pion lifetime is larger and hence can be considered to reach a metastable state. We explore restrictions under which such a metastability is possible. We also study conditions under which pions and leptons already in chemical equilibrium can reach simultaneously the thermal equilibrium, and obtain the neutrino emissivity from metastable pions. Scenarios that favor this metastable state are protoneutron stars.

## Key claim

Charged pions in a compact star environment can become metastable, their lifetime is significantly increased, because the leptons they would decay into are degenerate and their Fermi levels are occupied, so decay is only possible through thermal fluctuations; this metastability requires the lepton chemical potential to exceed a critical value (approximately 109.74 MeV for muons at chemical equilibrium) and the pion energy to be below a critical threshold.

## Method

The authors calculated the in-medium charged-pion decay rate (equilibrium deviation decay rate, EDDR) from the imaginary part of the retarded pion self-energy using the Matsubara formalism at finite temperature and density, including lepton chemical potentials and Fermi-Dirac statistics. They also computed the lepton EDDR from the lepton self-energy to find conditions where pion and lepton EDDRs are comparable, indicating simultaneous thermal equilibrium. The analysis considered pions at rest and leptons near the Fermi surface, with the neutrino chemical potential set to zero as a common approximation.

## Key numbers

* Critical lepton chemical potential for pion metastability at chemical equilibrium: \(\mu_c \approx 109.74\) MeV, for muons with \(m_\pi = 139.5\) MeV.
* Pion decay width in vacuum for \(\pi^- \to \ell + \bar{\nu}_\ell\): \(\bar{\Gamma}_{\pi\ell} = \frac{1}{\pi} f_\pi^2 G_F^2 m_\pi m_\ell^2 a_\ell^2\), where \(a_\ell \equiv (m_\pi^2 - m_\ell^2)/(2m_\pi^2)\) and \(G_F = 1.17 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* Lepton masses used: \(m_\mu = 105.6\) MeV, \(m_e = 0.5\) MeV; neutrinos treated as massless.
* Critical neutrino chemical potential for the rest-frame out-of-equilibrium case: \(\mu_{\nu_\ell}^c \equiv (m_\pi - \mu_\pi) + (\mu_\ell - m_\ell)\), which is positive under the assumptions \(\mu_\pi < m_\pi\) and \(\mu_\ell > m_\ell\).

## Context

This work extends the study of pions in dense matter, which has been explored in contexts such as pion condensation (Baym 1973, Sawyer 1973) and heavy-ion collisions (Xu 2009, Xiao 2013, Feng 2015), by focusing on the metastability of charged pions due to Pauli blocking in degenerate lepton environments. It builds on the concept of equilibrium deviation decay rates introduced by Weldon (1983) and applied to unstable particles by Kuznetsova and Rafelski (2008, 2010), and on the earlier suggestion of metastable pions by Loewe et al. (2011). The results enable estimates of neutrino emissivity from metastable pions in protoneutron stars, which can be compared with the standard URCA process, and provide a framework for assessing whether pions and leptons can reach simultaneous thermal equilibrium in such environments.
