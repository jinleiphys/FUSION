# Review on the determination of alpha_s from the QCD static energy

> We review the determination of the strong coupling alpha_s from the comparison of the perturbative expression for the Quantum Chromodynamics static energy with lattice data. We collect here all the perturbative expressions needed to evaluate the static energy at the currently known accuracy.

## Key claim

The strong coupling constant α_s can be determined by comparing perturbative QCD expressions for the static quark-antiquark energy with lattice data, but the extraction at N^3LL accuracy is not yet possible because the χ^2 as a function of r_0Λ_MS is very flat with current lattice data; the best result therefore comes from three-loop plus leading ultrasoft logarithmic resummation.

## Method

The author collects all known perturbative expressions for the QCD static energy E_0(r) at short distances, including terms up to three loops and resummation of ultrasoft logarithms via potential Non-Relativistic QCD (pNRQCD). Renormalon singularities are removed using the renormalon-subtracted (RS) scheme, and the resulting expressions are compared with n_f=2+1 lattice data from Bazavov (2011) for distances r < 0.5 r_0. A fitting procedure selects the ρ scale and r_0Λ_MS values for which the reduced χ^2 decreases when increasing perturbative order, and the central value is obtained as a weighted average over the selected ρ range.

## Key numbers

- r_0Λ_MS = 0.70 ± 0.07 (from three-loop plus leading ultrasoft logarithmic resummation, with errors from weighted standard deviation and difference with two-loop result)
- α_s(ρ = 1.5 GeV, n_f = 3) = 0.326 ± 0.019
- α_s(M_Z, n_f = 5) = 0.1156^{+0.0021}_{-0.0022} (evolved using 4-loop running with charm mass 1.6 GeV and bottom mass 4.7 GeV)
- Lattice data used in the distance range 0.065 fm ≲ r ≲ 0.234 fm (r < 0.5 r_0, with r_0 = 0.468 ± 0.004 fm)
- The analysis uses lattice data in the 0.8–2.9 GeV energy range

## Context

This review extends the approach of Brambilla (2010), who extracted r_0Λ_MS for the n_f=0 case using similar methods, and builds on perturbative three-loop calculations by Smirnov (2008, 2009) and Anzai (2009) as well as lattice data from Bazavov (2011). The result is mostly compatible with other recent lattice determinations of α_s (HPQCD, JLQCD, PACS-CS, ETM) and non-lattice determinations from τ decays, thrust, and PDF fits, though the central value is slightly lower. New lattice data at shorter distances are expected to enable an updated result with reduced errors.
