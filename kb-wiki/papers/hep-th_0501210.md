# Chiral Symmetry Breaking and the Dirac Spectrum at Nonzero Chemical Potential

> The relation between the spectral density of the QCD Dirac operator at nonzero baryon chemical potential and the chiral condensate is investigated. We use the analytical result for the eigenvalue density in the microscopic regime which shows oscillations with a period that scales as 1/V and an amplitude that diverges exponentially with the volume $V=L^4$. We find that the discontinuity of the chiral condensate is due to the whole oscillating region rather than to an accumulation of eigenvalues at the origin. These results also extend beyond the microscopic regime to chemical potentials $μ\sim 1/L$.

# Chiral Symmetry Breaking and the Dirac Spectrum at Nonzero Chemical Potential

## Key claim

The discontinuity of the chiral condensate at nonzero baryon chemical potential arises from the entire oscillating region of the Dirac eigenvalue density in the complex plane, not from an accumulation of eigenvalues at the origin as in the Banks-Casher mechanism at zero chemical potential. This result holds for chemical potentials up to μ ∼ 1/L, beyond the microscopic regime.

## Method

The authors use the analytical microscopic spectral density of the QCD Dirac operator at nonzero chemical potential, which is a complex oscillating function, and evaluate the chiral condensate via the integral relation Σ = lim_{V→∞} (1/V) ∫ dx dy ρ_{N_f}(x,y,m;μ)/(x+iy+m). They perform a saddle-point approximation of the integral over the imaginary eigenvalue coordinate y, using asymptotic expansions of the Bessel functions that appear in the exact microscopic density for one flavor with topological charge zero. The calculation separates the spectral density into a quenched part and an unquenched part, and the pole contribution from the unquenched part is shown to dominate.

## Key numbers

- The period of oscillations in the eigenvalue density scales as 1/V, where V = L⁴ is the spacetime volume.
- The amplitude of the oscillations diverges exponentially with the volume V.
- The support of the eigenvalue density in the microscopic limit is bounded by |x| < 2μ̂², where μ̂ = μ F_π √V is the rescaled chemical potential.
- The asymptotic result for the chiral condensate in the thermodynamic limit is Σ_{N_f=1}(m) = sg(m) Σ, valid for |m̂| ≫ 1 where m̂ = m Σ V.
- The example model density yields Σ_{Ex} = sg(m̂) Σ + (Σ/m̂) e^{-|m̂|} (e^{-|m̂|} - 1), approaching sg(m̂) Σ for large m̂.

## Context

This work extends the Banks-Casher relation (Banks and Casher, 1980) to nonzero chemical potential, where the Dirac operator becomes non-Hermitian and its eigenvalues spread into the complex plane. The authors use the microscopic spectral density derived from random matrix theory by Osborn (2004) and Splittorff and Verbaarschot (2004), and the asymptotic analysis builds on earlier work by Akemann et al. (2004). The results show that chiral symmetry breaking in full QCD at nonzero μ proceeds via a fundamentally different mechanism than in quenched or phase-quenched theories, where a diquark condensate forms instead. This analytical understanding is relevant to the sign problem in lattice QCD at finite baryon density.
