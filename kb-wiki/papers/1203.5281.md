# Impact of New beta-decay Half-lives on r-process Nucleosynthesis

> We investigate the effects of newly measured beta-decay half-lives on r-process nucleosynthesis. These new rates were determined by recent experiments at the radioactive isotope beam factory facility in the RIKEN Nishina Center. We adopt an r-process nucleosynthesis environment based on a magnetohydrodynamic supernova explosion model that includes strong magnetic fields and rapid rotation of the progenitor. A number of the new beta-decay rates are for nuclei on or near the r-process path, and hence they affect the nucleosynthesis yields and time scale of the r-process. The main effect of the newly measured beta-decay half-lives is an enhancement in the calculated abundance of isotopes with mass number A = 110 -- 120 relative to calculated abundances based upon beta-decay rates estimated with the finite-range droplet mass model. This effect slightly alleviates, but does not fully explain, the tendency of r-process models to underproduce isotopes with A = 110 -- 120 compared to the solar-system r-process abundances.

## Key claim

The newly measured beta-decay half-lives of 38 neutron-rich isotopes from krypton to technetium enhance the calculated r-process abundance of isotopes with mass number A = 110–120 relative to calculations using the finite-range droplet mass model, but this enhancement only slightly alleviates and does not fully explain the persistent underproduction of these isotopes compared to solar-system r-process abundances.

## Method

The authors performed r-process nucleosynthesis calculations using a magnetohydrodynamic supernova explosion model with strong magnetic fields and rapid rotation, evolving 23 tracer particles from the ejecta. They used three nuclear reaction networks: one with only theoretical FRDM rates from the REACLIB compilation, and two that incorporated the new experimental beta-decay half-lives from the RIKEN RIBF facility (RIBF and RIBF+ networks, the latter with modified Q-values for neutron capture reactions). The network included over 4000 isotopes with atomic number Z ≤ 100, and the calculations were post-processed from nuclear statistical equilibrium at T = 9 GK.

## Key numbers

- 38 neutron-rich isotopes had their beta-decay half-lives measured at the RIKEN RIBF facility, including 100Kr, 103–105Sr, 106–108Y, 108–110Zr, 111,112Nb, 112–115Mo, and 116,117Tc
- The secondary beam was produced by inflight fission of a 345 MeV/nucleon 238U beam in a beryllium target
- The nuclear reaction network contained more than 4000 isotopes with atomic number Z ≤ 100
- The switch from NSE to network calculation occurred at T = 9 GK (approximately 0.78 MeV)
- Modified Q-values for the RIBF+ network were: Q - 0.3 MeV for A = 97–103, Q + 0.5 MeV for A = 104–107, and Q + 1.0 MeV for A = 108–115

## Context

This work extends the study of r-process nucleosynthesis using a magnetohydrodynamic supernova model (Nishimura 2006) and incorporates new experimental beta-decay half-lives from the RIKEN RIBF facility (Nishimura 2011). The results confirm that the underproduction of isotopes near A = 120, a recurrent problem in r-process models noted by Woosley (1994), is not resolved by these new rates alone. The findings suggest that further measurements of masses and neutron separation energies in the A = 104–115 region are needed, and that the sensitivity of the A = 110–120 region to beta-decay rates may also be relevant to the light-element primary process (LEPP) proposed by Travaglio (2014) for ultra-metal-poor stars.
