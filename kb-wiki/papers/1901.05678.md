## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Garcilazo and Valcarce, 2018) of the Œ©d system using preliminary HAL QCD potentials, where a binding energy of about 17 MeV was found. The present results use the final HAL QCD interactions (Iritani et al., 2018; Gongyo et al., 2018) at nearly physical quark masses, which give stronger Œ©N attraction (1.54 MeV binding vs 0.3 MeV in the preliminary analysis). The study is motivated by the empirical pattern in the nonstrange sector where binding per baryon increases with baryon number, and by the recent STAR measurement of the proton-Œ© correlation function favoring a bound state hypothesis. The results suggest that these three-body states could be observed at J-PARC, FAIR, RHIC, or LHC experiments, though the authors note that coupled-channel calculations of the resonance widths require transition potentials not yet available from lattice QCD.
