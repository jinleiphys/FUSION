# Evaluation of the polarization observables I^S and I^C in the reaction gamma p --> pi^0 eta p

> We evaluate the polarization observables I^S and I^C for the reaction gamma p --> pi^0 eta p, using a chiral unitary framework developed earlier. The I^S and I^C observables have been recently measured for the first time by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration. The theoretical predictions of I^S and I^C, given for altogether 18 angle dependent functions, are in good agreement with the measurements. Also, the asymmetry dSigma/dcos(theta) evaluated here agrees with the data. We show the importance of the Delta(1700)D33 resonance and its S-wave decay into eta Delta(1232). The result can be considered as a further confirmation of the dynamical nature of this resonance. At the highest energies, deviations of the predictions from the data start to become noticeable, which leaves room for additional processes and resonances such as a Delta(1940)D33. We also point out how to further improve the calculation.

## Key claim

The chiral unitary framework developed earlier (Döring, 2005) successfully predicts the polarization observables I^S and I^C for the reaction γ p → π^0 η p, providing further confirmation that the Δ(1700) D33 resonance is dynamically generated, although at the highest energy (W = 1946 MeV) deviations from data become noticeable, leaving room for additional processes such as a Δ(1940) D33 resonance.

## Method

The authors evaluate the polarization observables I^S and I^C using a Monte Carlo integration of the three-body phase space, binning 4.8 × 10^5 random events into 16 × 16 bins for the angles φ and φ^*. The reaction amplitude is taken from the chiral unitary model of Döring (2005), which includes tree-level Δ(1700) → η Δ(1232) decay and Δ(1700) K Σ(1385) couplings as predictions from the chiral unitary approach. The double differential cross section is fitted with the φ dependence dσ/dφ = σ_0 [1 + I^S sin(2φ) + I^C cos(2φ)] separately for each φ^* bin.

## Key numbers

- The Δ(1700) → η Δ coupling constants from the chiral unitary model are g_{η Δ} = 1.7 − 1.4 i (original) and g_{η Δ} = −2.27 − 1.89 i (updated from Döring, 2007)
- The Δ(1700) → K Σ(1385) coupling constants are g_{K Σ(1385)} = 3.3 + 0.7 i (original) and g_{K Σ(1385)} = 3.01 + 1.95 i (updated)
- The γ N Δ(1700) helicity amplitudes are A_{1/2} = 0.104 ± 0.015 GeV^{-1/2} and A_{3/2} = 0.085 ± 0.022 GeV^{-1/2} at Q^2 = 0
- The ratio g_1'/g_2' = −0.26 M_N^{-1} / (+0.27 M_N^{-2}) has an estimated uncertainty of about 22%
- The Monte Carlo evaluation uses N = 4.8 × 10^5 events for each total energy W
- Results are presented at three energies: W = 1706 MeV, W = 1834 MeV, and W = 1946 MeV
- For the lowest energy bin (W = 1706 ± 64 MeV), the model was evaluated at W = 1740 MeV to correct for the finite experimental bin width

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary framework of Döring (2005) and Döring (2006) to polarization observables recently measured for the first time by the CBELSA/TAPS collaboration (Gutz, 2009). The results confirm the dominance of the Δ(1700) resonance and its dynamical nature, consistent with earlier findings from total cross sections and invariant mass distributions. The deviations at the highest energy (W = 1946 MeV) suggest the need for additional mechanisms such as a Δ(1940) D33 resonance, which was required in the Bonn-Gatchina partial wave analysis of Horn (2007), and the a_0(980) p final state not included in the present model. The work also points to the need for an upgrade incorporating the N(1650) S11 resonance, as addressed in Döring (2009), to improve the description of η p invariant masses.
