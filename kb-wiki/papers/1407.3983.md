# Shell structure and few-nucleon removal in intranuclear cascade

> It is well known that intranuclear-cascade models generally overestimate the cross sections for one-proton removal from heavy, stable nuclei by a high-energy proton beam, but they yield reasonable predictions for one-neutron removal from the same nuclei and for one-nucleon removal from light targets. We use simple shell-model calculations to investigate the reasons of this deficiency. We find that a correct description of the neutron skin and of the energy density in the nuclear surface is crucial for the aforementioned observables. Neither ingredient is sufficient if taken separately.

## Key claim

Intranuclear-cascade models overestimate one-proton removal cross sections from heavy stable nuclei because they neglect both the neutron skin and the quantum-mechanical penetration of deeply-bound nucleon wavefunctions into the nuclear surface; neither effect alone is sufficient to fix the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors coupled the Liège Intranuclear Cascade model (INCL) with the ABLA statistical de-excitation model and compared calculated one-nucleon-removal cross sections to experimental data for proton-induced reactions on stable targets from calcium to lead at energies above 500 MeV. They performed simple shell-model calculations with a central Saxon-Woods potential including spin-orbit and Coulomb terms to obtain single-particle wavefunctions and energies, then used these to compute the mean excitation energy deposited by a hole created at a given radial position. They introduced two modifications to the INCL nuclear model: a neutron skin (by decoupling neutron and proton density parameters) and "surface fuzziness" (making the square-well radius a random variable to mimic wavefunction penetration).

## Key numbers

- Standard INCL overestimates one-proton removal cross section for 1-GeV p+208Pb by a factor of 3–4 (calculated 59.5 mb vs experimental 17.6 ± 0.5 mb).
- With both neutron skin and surface fuzziness applied simultaneously to 1-GeV p+208Pb, the one-proton removal cross section drops to 33.6 mb (still a factor of ~2 above the experimental value).
- For 1-GeV p+208Pb one-neutron removal, standard INCL gives 82.1 mb; with both refinements the result is 83.8 mb, within two standard deviations of the experimental value 63.7 ± 9.6 mb.
- For 1-GeV p+40Ca one-proton removal, standard INCL gives 59.8 mb; with both refinements it gives 51.9 mb, within the experimental error bar of 54.7 ± 7.9 mb (at 763 MeV).
- For 1-GeV p+40Ca one-neutron removal, standard INCL gives 46.4 mb; with both refinements it gives 35.3 mb, in fair agreement with the experimental value 29.8 ± 6.4 mb (at 763 MeV).
- The fuzziness parameter was chosen as f=0.5 for protons and f=0.3 for neutrons in 208Pb, and f=0.3 for both in 40Ca.

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing puzzle in intranuclear-cascade modeling, where models such as INCL, Isabel (Yariv 1979), and Bertini (Bertini 1963, 1969) systematically overpredict one-proton removal from heavy stable nuclei while describing one-neutron removal reasonably. The authors show that the deficiency arises from the classical treatment of nucleon motion in the nuclear surface, which underestimates the excitation energy deposited when a proton is knocked out. The refined model improves agreement for both calcium and lead targets, but the remaining factor-of-two discrepancy for lead suggests that further refinements beyond the simple shell-model approach (e.g., Hartree-Fock methods) will be needed, especially for non-magic nuclei.
