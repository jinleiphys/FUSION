# QCD factorization with multihadron fragmentation functions

> Important aspects of QCD factorization theorems are the properties of the objects involved that can be identified as universal. One example is that the definitions of parton densities and fragmentation functions for different types of hadrons differ only in the identity of the nonperturbative states that form the matrix elements, but are otherwise the same. This leads to independence of perturbative calculations on nonperturbative details of external states. It also lends support to interpretations of correlation functions as encapsulations of intrinsic nonperturbative properties. These characteristics have usually been presumed to still hold true in fragmentation functions even when the observed nonperturbative state is a small-mass cluster of $n$ hadrons rather than simply a single isolated hadron. However, the multidifferential aspect of cross sections that rely on these latter types of fragmentation functions complicates the treatment of kinematical approximations in factorization derivations. That has led to recent claims that the operator definitions for fragmentation functions need to be modified from the single hadron case with nonuniversal prefactors. With such concerns as our motivation, we retrace the steps for factorizing the unpolarized semi-inclusive $e^+e^-$ annihilation cross section and confirm that they do apply without modification to the case of a small-mass multihadron observed in the final state. In particular, we verify that the standard operator definition from single hadron fragmentation, with its usual prefactor, remains equally valid for the small-mass $n$-hadron case with the same hard parts and evolution kernels, whereas the more recently proposed definitions with nonuniversal prefactors do not. Our results reaffirm the reliability of most past phenomenological applications of dihadron fragmentation functions.

## Key claim

The standard operator definition of single-hadron fragmentation functions, with its usual prefactor, remains equally valid for the case of a small-mass cluster of n hadrons (n-hadron) in semi-inclusive e+e- annihilation, contrary to recent claims that modifications with nonuniversal prefactors are required; the authors verify that the standard definition yields the same hard parts and evolution kernels as the single-hadron case.

## Method

The authors retrace the steps for factorizing the unpolarized semi-inclusive e+e- annihilation cross section, starting from the unfactorized hadronic tensor and applying collinear factorization in the limit of small hadron cluster mass M_h relative to Q, with fixed momentum fraction z. They work in a simplified theory of massless quarks with no color and one flavor, focusing on the leading region where a single parton fragments into an n-hadron cluster, and examine whether the kinematical approximations required for factorization are modified when the observed final state contains multiple hadrons rather than a single hadron.

## Key numbers

- The invariant mass of the hadron cluster is restricted to M_h^2 << Q^2, with M_h of order Λ_QCD.
- The momentum fraction variable z = 2 p_h · q / Q^2, and the light-cone momentum fraction ζ = p_h^+ / k^+ in the hadron frame.
- The variable z_n = z/2 + sqrt(z^2/4 - M_h^2/Q^2) = z + O(M_h^2/(z Q^2)) is used to express exact momentum components in terms of light-cone plus-momentum fractions.
- The cross section differential in z is given by dσ/dz = (π α^2 z / Q^2) [2 F_1(z,Q^2) + (z/3) F_2(z,Q^2)] + power-suppressed terms.

## Context

This work extends the standard collinear factorization framework for fragmentation functions, as described in Collins (2011), from single-hadron to multihadron final states, directly addressing claims by Pitonyak et al. (2023) that dihadron fragmentation functions require modified operator definitions with nonuniversal prefactors. The results reaffirm the validity of past phenomenological applications of dihadron fragmentation functions, such as those by Bianconi et al. (1999), Bacchetta et al. (2002), Boer et al. (2003), and Matevosyan et al. (2013, 2017, 2018), and enable continued use of the standard definition for extracting transversity distributions and related quantities from dihadron production data at Belle.
