# The deuteron-radius puzzle is alive: A new analysis of nuclear structure uncertainties

> To shed light on the deuteron radius puzzle we analyze the theoretical uncertainties of the nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift in muonic deuterium. We find that the discrepancy between the calculated two-photon exchange correction and the corresponding experimentally inferred value by Pohl et al. [1] remain. The present result is consistent with our previous estimate, although the discrepancy is reduced from 2.6 $σ$ to 2 $σ$. The error analysis includes statistic as well as systematic uncertainties stemming from the use of nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from chiral effective field theory at various orders. We therefore conclude that nuclear theory uncertainty is more likely not the source of the discrepancy.

## Key claim

The discrepancy between the calculated two-photon exchange correction and the experimentally inferred value from Pohl et al. (2016) remains, although it is reduced from 2.6 sigma to about 2 sigma; the authors conclude that nuclear theory uncertainty is more likely not the source of the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors computed the nuclear structure corrections to the Lamb shift in muonic deuterium using nucleon-nucleon interactions derived from chiral effective field theory at orders from leading order (LO) up to fifth order (N4LO). They propagated statistical uncertainties from the low-energy constants using covariance matrices from simultaneous fits to scattering data and ground-state observables, and estimated systematic uncertainties from both cutoff variation and the chiral EFT truncation error using the method of Epelbaum (2015) and Furnstahl (2015), with the chiral breakdown scale set to 600 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The final recommended value for the two-photon exchange correction is delta_TPE = -1.715 meV, with a total uncertainty range from -0.024 meV to +0.022 meV.
- The discrepancy between the calculated two-photon exchange correction and the experimentally inferred value from Pohl et al. (2016) is reduced from 2.6 sigma to about 2 sigma.
- The statistical uncertainty of delta^A_TPE from the N2LO_sim potentials is 0.001 meV, or 0.06 percent.
- The systematic uncertainty from variations of T_max_Lab from 125 to 290 MeV amounts to about 0.004 meV or 0.2 percent.
- The largest contribution of the chiral truncation uncertainty alone for the N2LO_sim potentials is 0.007 meV.
- The average energy value of the leading order dipole correction is about 7 MeV, corresponding to a momentum scale smaller than the pion mass.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Hernandez 2014, Hernandez 2016) by incorporating rigorous statistical uncertainty propagation from the low-energy constants and systematic chiral truncation error estimates following the methods of Epelbaum (2015) and Furnstahl (2015). The results are consistent with the calculation by Pachucki (2015) at the 0.6 percent level, and the recommended value from Krauth (2016) of delta_TPE = -1.7096(200) meV. The analysis enables improved extraction of the deuteron charge radius from muonic deuterium Lamb shift measurements and informs the ongoing deuteron radius puzzle, where the muonic deuterium result differs from the CODATA-2014 value by 5.6 sigma.
