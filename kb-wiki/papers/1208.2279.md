# Combined analysis of eta' production reactions: gamma N -> eta' N, N N -> N N eta', and pi N -> eta' N

> The production of eta' mesons in photon- and hadron-induced reactions has been revisited in view of the recent additions of high-precision data to the world data base. Based on an effective Lagrangian approach, we have performed a combined analysis of the free and quasi-free gamma N -> eta' N, N N -> N N eta', and pi N -> eta' N reactions. Considering spin-1/2 and -3/2 resonances, we found that a set of above-threshold resonances {S_{11}, P_{11}, P_{13}}, with fitted mass values of about M_R=1925, 2130, and 2050 MeV, respectively, and the four-star sub-threshold P_{13}(1720) resonance reproduce best all existing data for the eta' production processes in the resonance-energy region considered in this work. All three above-threshold resonances found in the present analysis are essential and indispensable for the good quality of the present fits.

# Combined analysis of eta' production reactions: gamma N -> eta' N, N N -> N N eta', and pi N -> eta' N

## Key claim
The authors find that a set of three above-threshold resonances {S11, P11, P13} with fitted masses of about 1925, 2130, and 2050 MeV, plus the four-star sub-threshold P13(1720) resonance, best reproduces all existing data for eta' production processes. However, this conclusion depends on the simultaneous analysis of multiple reaction channels; considering only free gamma p -> eta' p photoproduction data, multiple resonance sets can fit the cross-section data equally well, and the ambiguity is only resolved by including quasi-free gamma n -> eta' n and NN -> NN eta' data.

## Method
The authors performed a combined analysis of free and quasi-free gamma N -> eta' N, NN -> NN eta', and pi N -> eta' N reactions using an effective Lagrangian approach with spin-1/2 and -3/2 resonances. For photoproduction, they included nucleonic and meson-exchange (rho and omega) currents alongside resonance contributions, while for NN -> NN eta' they employed a distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) with explicit initial- and final-state NN interactions. The quasi-free photoproduction processes were evaluated by folding free cross sections with the Fermi momentum distribution of nucleons inside the deuteron.

## Key numbers
- Fitted masses of above-threshold resonances: S11 at 1924 +/- 4 MeV (CLAS fit), P11 at 2129 +/- 5 MeV (CLAS fit), P13 at 2050 +/- 4 MeV (CLAS fit)
- Total widths: S11(1925) = 112 +/- 7 MeV, P11(2130) = 205 +/- 12 MeV, P13(2050) = 140 +/- 10 MeV (CLAS fit values)
- Reduced helicity amplitudes for S11(1925): sqrt(beta_Neta') A_{1/2} = -11.84 +/- 0.41 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} (CLAS fit)
- Reduced helicity amplitudes for P11(2130): sqrt(beta_Neta') A_{1/2} = -11.34 +/- 0.62 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} (CLAS fit)
- Reduced helicity amplitudes for P13(2050): sqrt(beta_Neta') A_{1/2} = -5.71 +/- 0.17 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} and A_{3/2} = 9.89 +/- 0.30 x 10^{-3} GeV^{-1/2} (CLAS fit)
- Chi-squared per data point: 0.65 for CLAS free proton fit, 0.53 for CBELSA/TAPS free proton fit
- Pseudoscalar-pseudovector mixing parameter lambda = 0.53 +/- 0.06 (CLAS fit)
- Cutoff parameter Lambda_v = 1183 +/- 5 MeV (CLAS fit)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous studies of eta' photoproduction (Huang and Nakayama, 2004; Huang and Nakayama, 2006) by incorporating new high-precision data from CLAS (2009) and CBELSA/TAPS (2009, 2011) collaborations, as well as new invariant-mass distribution data from COSY-11 (2010). The combined analysis resolves ambiguities that existed when fitting only photoproduction cross-section data, which could not distinguish between three different resonance sets. The approach differs from the quark-model analysis by Zhong and Zhao (2011) who considered all nucleon resonances up to the n=3 harmonic-oscillator shell. The found resonances may be tentatively identified with PDG resonances S11(1895)**, P11(2100)*, and P13(2040)*. The work enables future extraction of spin observables (beam asymmetry, beam-target asymmetry) which are needed to further constrain the resonance parameters, and highlights the need to resolve discrepancies between CLAS and CBELSA/TAPS data sets that differ by factors up to 3 at higher energies.
