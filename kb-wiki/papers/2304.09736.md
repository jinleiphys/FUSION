# Khuri-Treiman analysis of $J/ψ\toπ^{+}π^{-}π^{0}$

> We study the decay $J/ψ\toπ^{+}π^{-}π^{0}$ within the framework of the Khuri-Treiman equations. We find that the BESIII experimental di-pion mass distribution in the $ρ(770)$-region is well reproduced with a once-subtracted $P$-wave amplitude. Furthermore, we show that $F$-wave contributions to the amplitude improve the description of the data in the $ππ$ mass region around 1.5 GeV. We also present predictions for the $J/ψ\toπ^{0}γ^{*}$ transition form factor.

## Key claim

The BESIII di-pion mass distribution in the $\rho(770)$ region is well reproduced by a once-subtracted Khuri-Treiman amplitude, but the description of data around $m_{\pi\pi}\sim1.5$ GeV requires the inclusion of an $F$-wave contribution from the $\rho_{3}(1690)$ resonance, with the caveat that the $\chi^{2}/$dof remains high (about 100) due to systematic uncertainties and the absence of full Dalitz plot data.

## Method

The decay amplitude was constructed using Khuri-Treiman equations that enforce unitarity, analyticity, and crossing symmetry for the $J/\psi\to\pi^{+}\pi^{-}\pi^{0}$ process. The $P$-wave $\pi\pi$ scattering phase shift from Pelaez (2019) was used as input, and the once-subtracted KT equations were solved iteratively with the subtraction constant $b$ fitted to the BESIII di-pion invariant mass projection. An $F$-wave contribution was added via a Breit-Wigner parametrization of the $\rho_{3}(1690)$ resonance, with a linear polynomial $p_{3}(s)$ for the energy dependence, and the full amplitude was fitted to 80 data points from BESIII.

## Key numbers

- Fitted $P$-wave subtraction constant: $|b| = 0.198(35)~\text{GeV}^{-2}$, $\phi_{b} = 2.675(300)$ from the once-subtracted fit
- Fitted $P$-wave subtraction constant with $F$-wave included: $|b| = 0.205(34)~\text{GeV}^{-2}$, $\phi_{b} = 2.784(298)$
- $F$-wave polynomial parameters: $|c| \times 10^{2} = 4.38(1.46)~\text{GeV}^{-4}$, $\phi_{c} = 3.80(5)$; $|d| \times 10^{2} = 1.58(46)~\text{GeV}^{-6}$, $\phi_{d} = 0.65(8)$
- Overall normalization from PDG branching ratio: $|a| \simeq 0.051~\text{GeV}^{-3}$ for unsubtracted case; $|a| = 0.0565(22)~\text{GeV}^{-3}$ for once-subtracted $P$-wave fit; $|a| = 0.0581(60)~\text{GeV}^{-3}$ for $P+F$-wave fit
- Sum-rule prediction for subtraction constant: $b_{\text{sum}} \simeq 0.141\,e^{2.321\,i}~\text{GeV}^{-2}$ (solution I); $0.129\,e^{2.640\,i}~\text{GeV}^{-2}$ (solution II); $0.124\,e^{2.811\,i}~\text{GeV}^{-2}$ (solution III)
- $\rho_{3}(1690)$ mass: $1688$ MeV, width: $161$ MeV from PDG
- BESIII branching ratio for $J/\psi\to\pi^{0}e^{+}e^{-}$: $(7.56 \pm 1.32 \pm 0.50) \times 10^{-7}$

## Context

This work extends the Khuri-Treiman formalism previously applied to $\omega\to3\pi$ (Niecknig, 2012; Danilkin, 2014; JPAC, 2020) and $\eta\to3\pi$ (Guo, 2014; Albaladejo, 2017; Gasser, 2018) to the charmonium decay $J/\psi\to3\pi$, building on earlier studies using Veneziano models (Szczepaniak, 2014) and unitarity constraints (Guo, 2010, 2011). The analysis enables future event-by-event likelihood fits to high-statistics BESIII data expected soon, which could help resolve the $\rho\pi$ puzzle comparing $J/\psi$ and $\psi(2S)$ decays, and provides predictions for the $J/\psi\to\pi^{0}\gamma^{*}$ transition form factor measurable in $J/\psi\to\pi^{0}e^{+}e^{-}$ and $J/\psi\to\pi^{0}\mu^{+}\mu^{-}$ decays.
