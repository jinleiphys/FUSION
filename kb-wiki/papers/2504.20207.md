# Application of the Holographic Equation of State for Numerical Modeling of the Evolution of Quark-Gluon Plasma

> In this paper, we propose a method for numerical modeling of the nuclear matter properties within the framework of relativistic heavy-ion collisions using a holographic equation of state. Model's free parameters are calibrated using lattice results for quark masses approximating physical values and adjusted to match the Regge spectra of $ρ$ mesons. Numerical simulations are performed using the iEBE-MUSIC framework, which incorporates the MUSIC relativistic hydrodynamics solver. We modify the code by implementing a tabulated holographic equation of state, enabling simulations of quark-gluon plasma evolution with dynamically generated initial conditions via the 3D Monte Carlo Glauber Model. Finally, the spectra of produced hadrons are computed using a hybrid iSS+UrQMD approach at the freeze-out stage.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that holographic equations of state (EoS) calibrated to lattice QCD results can be integrated into the iEBE-MUSIC relativistic hydrodynamics framework and produce rapidity spectra that qualitatively match experimental data from the NA49 experiment, though the overall scale remains highly sensitive to initial conditions and the authors note that further fine-tuning is needed for quantitative agreement.

## Method

The authors calibrate a bottom-up soft-wall holographic model with two dilaton fields by adjusting free parameters (a, b, c, and an energy scale L) to match lattice QCD results for the ratio s/T³ (isotropic case) and quark susceptibility (anisotropic case), as well as the Regge spectra of ρ mesons. They then implement the resulting tabulated holographic EoS into the MUSIC relativistic hydrodynamics solver within the iEBE-MUSIC framework, using 3D Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions and a hybrid iSS+UrQMD approach for hadronization and final-state interactions.

## Key numbers

- Parameter c = 0.227 GeV², adjusted to match the Regge spectra of ρ mesons.
- Simulations performed at collision energy √s = 8.9 GeV for comparison with NA49 experimental data.
- Central collisions defined with impact parameter b < 2.5 fm.
- The entropy density formula includes a factor (1/z_h)^(1+2/ν) with the anisotropy parameter ν (ν=1 for isotropic case).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach of Arefieva et al. (2018) by moving beyond purely theoretical studies to actual numerical simulations of quark-gluon plasma evolution in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It enables direct comparison of holographic EoS predictions with experimental data from the NA49 experiment, and provides a framework for testing alternative holographic models against lattice-based EoS such as NEOS (Monnai et al., 2019). The authors identify the need for more sophisticated calibration methods (e.g., machine learning) and more sensitive observables to distinguish between different EoS in future work.
