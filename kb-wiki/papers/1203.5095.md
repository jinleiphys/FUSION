# A method for studying initial geometry fluctuations via event plane correlations in heavy ion collisions

> A method is proposed to measure the relative azimuthal angle distributions involving two or more event planes of different order in heavy ion collisions using a Fourier analysis technique. The analysis procedure is demonstrated for correlations involving two and three event planes (Phi_n, Phi_m and Phi_h). The Fourier coefficients of these distributions are found to coincide with previously proposed correlators, such as cos(6Phi_2-6Phi_3) and cos(Phi_1+2Phi_2-3Phi_3) etc, hence the method provides a natural framework for studying these correlators at the same time. Using a Monte Carlo Glauber model to simulate Au+Au collisions with fluctuating initial geometry, we are able to identify several new two- or three-plane correlators that have sizable magnitudes and should be measured experimentally.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a Fourier-based method to measure correlations between event planes of different harmonic orders in heavy ion collisions, claiming that the Fourier coefficients of the relative azimuthal angle distributions coincide with previously proposed correlators. The hedge is that the method's predictions are based on Monte Carlo Glauber and CGC models of initial geometry fluctuations, and the actual experimental results may be distorted by hydrodynamic mixing during evolution, especially for higher-order planes.

## Method
The method constructs differential distributions of relative azimuthal angles between two or more event planes (e.g., k(Φ_n - Φ_m) for two planes, or 2D distributions in (Φ_2,1, Φ_3,1) for three planes) and expands them in Fourier series. The raw Fourier coefficients from measured event plane angles Ψ_n are corrected for finite event plane resolution using standard subevent methods. The approach is demonstrated for two-plane correlations (Φ_n, Φ_m) and three-plane correlations (Φ_1, Φ_2, Φ_3 and others), with the Fourier coefficients directly corresponding to previously proposed correlators such as cos(6Φ_2 - 6Φ_3) and cos(Φ_1 + 2Φ_2 - 3Φ_3).

## Key numbers
- 2.5 million Au+Au collisions simulated using Monte Carlo Glauber model with Woods-Saxon parameters: radius 6.38 fm, diffuseness 0.535 fm, nucleon-nucleon cross-section 42 mb
- Strong positive correlations observed for V_{2,4}^{1*} (cos 4(Φ_2* - Φ_4*)) in peripheral collisions, with the distribution requiring multiple Fourier components (j=1-5) to describe the full shape for N_part < 100
- The correlator cos(Φ_1* + 2Φ_2* - 3Φ_3*) shows the largest values among three-plane correlators in most centrality bins, except for r^n weighting in central and mid-central collisions where cos(Φ_1* + 3Φ_3* - 4Φ_4*) is largest
- Typical statistical error on Fourier coefficients in the 2D distributions is about 0.002

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of event plane correlations by Nagle (2010), Teaney and Yan (2010), and Bhalerao (2011) by providing a unified Fourier framework that simultaneously analyzes multiple correlators. The method enables experimental measurement of both two-plane correlators (e.g., between Φ_2 and Φ_4, which previous work by Adams (2003) and Adare (2010) found strong) and three-plane correlators (e.g., Φ_1 + 2Φ_2 - 3Φ_3 proposed by Teaney and Yan (2010)). The predictions from Glauber and CGC models provide testable signals that could distinguish between different initial geometry models and constrain the role of hydrodynamic evolution in generating anisotropic flow.
