# Core-corona interplay in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV

> A simple approach based on the separation of wounded nucleons in an A-A collision in two categories, those suffering single collisions - corona and the rest - core, estimated within a Glauber Monte-Carlo approach, explains the centrality dependence of the light flavor hadrons production in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV. The core contribution does not include any dependence of any process on the fireball shape as a function of the impact parameter. Therefore, the ratios of the $p_T$ distributions to the one corresponding to the minimum bias pp collisions at the same energy, each of them normalised to the corresponding charged particle density, the $\langle p_T\rangle$ and transverse energy per unit of rapidity are reproduced less accurate by such an approach. The results show that the corona contribution plays an important role also at LHC energies and it has to be considered in order to evidence the centrality dependence of different observables related to the core properties and dynamics.

## Key claim

A simple core-corona separation based purely on the number of collisions per wounded nucleon (single-scattering corona versus multiple-scattering core), estimated via a Glauber Monte-Carlo approach, can explain the centrality dependence of light flavor hadron yields in Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, but the approach reproduces the ratios of normalized pT distributions, average transverse momenta, and transverse energy per unit rapidity less accurately because it does not include any dependence of core processes on the fireball shape as a function of impact parameter.

## Method

The authors used a Glauber Monte-Carlo model with a Woods-Saxon nuclear density profile (radius 6.62 fm, diffuseness 0.546 fm) and an inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section of 64 mb at 2.76 TeV to classify wounded nucleons into corona (single collision) and core (more than one collision). For each centrality bin, the yield of a given hadron species per unit rapidity was constructed as a weighted sum of the corona contribution (taken from minimum-bias pp collisions at the same energy) and the core contribution (extracted from the most central 0-5% Pb-Pb data), with the weight fcore given by the Glauber-estimated fraction of wounded nucleons suffering multiple collisions. The same decomposition was applied to pT-differential spectra, average transverse momenta, and transverse energy.

## Key numbers

- For the 0-5% most central Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV: fcore = 0.942 ± 0.003, ⟨Npart⟩ = 382.5 ± 3.1
- For the 80-90% peripheral collisions: fcore = 0.325 ± 0.003, ⟨Npart⟩ = 6.3 ± 0.2
- Woods-Saxon parameters used: radius RA = 6.62 fm, diffuseness a = 0.546 fm
- Inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section at 2.76 TeV: 64 mb
- The percentage of wounded nucleons scattering only once for head-on Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV is very small (see Fig. 1a), decreasing with increasing nuclear mass and collision energy compared to Au-Au at 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the core-corona separation approach previously applied at SPS and RHIC energies (Bec0, Boz, Wer, Stei, Bec, Aich, Boz1, Gem, Schr) to LHC energies for the first time, using the specific geometrical method from Aich. The results show that the corona contribution remains important even at LHC energies and must be separated to study true core properties; the observed deviations from the simple model point to the need for including fireball shape effects such as azimuthally-dependent flow and suppression, as previously studied by the PHENIX Collaboration at RHIC for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV.
