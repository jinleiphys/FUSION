# A Search for various Double Beta Decay Modes of Cd, Te and Zn Isotopes

> Various double beta decay modes of Cd, Zn and Te isotopes are explored with the help of CdTe and CdZnTe semiconductor detectors. The data set is splitted in an energy range below 1 MeV having a statistics of 134.5 g$\cdot$d and one above 1 MeV resulting in 532 g$\cdot$d. No signals were observed in all channels under investigation. New improved limits for the neutrinoless double beta decay of Zn70 of $T_{1/2} > 1.3 \cdot 10^{16} yrs$ (90% CL), the longest standing limit of all double beta isotopes, and 0$νβ^+$EC of Te120 of $T_{1/2} > 2.2 \cdot 10^{16} yrs$ (90% CL) are given. For the first time a limit on the half-life of the 2$ν$ECEC of $^{120}$Te of $T_{1/2} > 9.4 \cdot 10^{15} yrs$ (90% CL) is obtained. In addition, limits on 2$ν$ECEC for ground state transitions of Cd106, Cd108 and Zn64 are improved. The obtained results even under rough background conditions show the reliability of CdTe semiconductor detectors for rare nuclear decay searches.

## Key claim

Using small CdTe and CdZnTe semiconductor detectors (2.89 g and 5.8 g) with modest exposure (0.53 kg·d above 1 MeV, 0.13 kg·d below 1 MeV), no signals were observed for any double beta decay mode; the authors demonstrate that even under rough background conditions, CdTe detectors are reliable for rare nuclear decay searches and that scaling to larger masses could probe neutrino masses below 1 eV.

## Method

Two semiconductor detectors, a 2.89 g CdZnTe (CPG) detector and a 5.8 g CdTe (ER) detector, were operated in a passive shielding. The data set was split into an energy range below 1 MeV (134.5 g·d from the CPG detector only) and above 1 MeV (532 g·d combined from both detectors). Efficiencies for full-energy detection and gamma escape were calculated with GEANT4 and cross-checked with MCNP. Half-life limits were extracted using a chi-squared method: the background around each candidate peak was fitted with an exponential, a Gaussian peak was grown at the expected energy, and the 90% CL limit was taken where the summed chi-squared increased by 2.71 from its minimum.

## Key numbers

- Neutrinoless double beta decay of 70Zn: T1/2 > 1.3 × 10^16 yrs (90% CL), improved limit for the isotope with the longest-standing limit among all double beta isotopes
- Neutrinoless β+/EC decay of 120Te: T1/2 > 2.2 × 10^16 yrs (90% CL)
- Two-neutrino double electron capture (2νECEC) of 120Te: T1/2 > 9.4 × 10^15 yrs (90% CL), first limit ever obtained for this mode
- Peak efficiency for 116Cd neutrinoless double beta decay (Q = 2805 keV): 55.5% (CPG detector), 60.6% (ER detector)
- Peak efficiency for 128Te neutrinoless double beta decay (Q = 868 keV): 92.7% (CPG), 94.0% (ER)
- Background in the 116Cd region (2.7–3.4 MeV, excluding peak): 3.3 × 10^(-4) [keV·g·d]^(-1)
- Half-life limits for various cadmium and tellurium 0νβ-β- modes: 0.7 × 10^18 to 3.3 × 10^19 yrs
- Half-life limits for various β+β+ modes: 8.5 × 10^15 to 1.0 × 10^18 yrs

## Context

This work is a first experimental step toward the COBRA project (Kiel et al., 2003), which proposes a large array of CdTe/CdZnTe detectors for double beta decay searches. It improves the limit on 70Zn 0νβ-β-, which had not been investigated experimentally in the previous fifty years, and provides the first limit on 2νECEC of 120Te. The results extend the survey of double beta decay modes compiled by Tretyak and Zdesenko (2002) and demonstrate that CdTe detectors, despite rough background conditions, are clean enough that an upgrade to 10 kg could probe neutrino masses below 1 eV, especially for 116Cd whose high Q-value (2805 keV) lies above all natural gamma lines.
