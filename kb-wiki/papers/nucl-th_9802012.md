# Computation of Two-Body Matrix Elements From the Argonne $v_{18}$ Potential

> We discuss the computation of two-body matrix elements from the Argonne $v_{18}$ interaction. The matrix elements calculation is presented both in particle-particle and in particle-hole angular momentum coupling. The procedures developed here can be applied to the case of other NN potentials, provided that they have a similar operator format.

# Computation of Two-Body Matrix Elements From the Argonne $v_{18}$ Potential

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete, detailed procedure for computing two-body matrix elements of the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential in both particle-particle and particle-hole angular momentum coupling, using Fourier transform techniques to separate the radial and angular dependencies of each operator component. The method is explicitly applicable to any NN potential with a similar operator format, but the derivations are specific to the 18-operator structure of the Argonne $v_{18}$ interaction.

## Method

The authors decompose the Argonne $v_{18}$ interaction into its 18 operator components (14 charge-independent, 3 charge-dependent, and 1 charge-asymmetric) plus electromagnetic terms. For each operator type, central, spin-orbit, tensor, $L^2$, and their combinations with spin and isospin operators, they use Fourier transforms to separate the two-body radial dependence into products of one-body Bessel functions, then recouple the angular and spin operators into scalar products of rank-$\lambda$ tensors acting on each particle separately. The resulting particle-hole coupled matrix elements are expressed in terms of reduced one-body matrix elements involving spherical harmonics, gradient operators, and spin operators, with explicit formulas for the radial integrals containing Bessel functions and derivative operators.

## Key numbers

- The Argonne $v_{18}$ potential was fit to the Nijmegen NN scattering database containing 1787 pp and 2514 np data in the range 0-350 MeV, with a $\chi^2$ per datum of 1.09.
- The strong interaction part has 14 charge-independent operator components (as in Argonne $v_{14}$) plus 3 charge-dependent operators ($T_{ij}$, $(\sigma_i\cdot\sigma_j)T_{ij}$, $S_{ij}T_{ij}$) and 1 charge-asymmetric operator ($\tau_{zi}+\tau_{zj}$).
- The tensor interaction form factor is defined as $\tilde V^T(q)=\int V^T(r_{12})j_2(qr_{12})r_{12}^2dr_{12}$.
- The spin-orbit form factor is $\tilde V^{LS}(q)=\int r_{12}^3V^{LS}(r_{12})j_1(qr_{12})dr_{12}$.
- The $L^2$ interaction form factors are $\tilde V^{L2,J}(q)=\int r_{12}^4V^{L2}(r_{12})j_J(qr_{12})dr_{12}$ for $J=0,2$ and $\bar V^{L2,1}(q)=\int r_{12}^3V^{L2}(r_{12})j_1(qr_{12})dr_{12}$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier Argonne $v_{14}$ potential formalism (which had 14 operator components) to the updated $v_{18}$ interaction that includes charge-independence breaking and charge-asymmetric terms, as well as a complete electromagnetic potential with Coulomb, Darwin-Foldy, vacuum polarization, and magnetic moment terms with finite-size effects. The paper provides the necessary angular momentum recoupling formulas (including the Ring phase for simplified symmetry relations) that enable practitioners to compute matrix elements for nuclear structure calculations using the Argonne $v_{18}$ potential, and the formalism can be adapted to other NN potentials with similar operator structure.
