# Relativistic Models of Quasielastic Electron and Neutrino-Nucleus Scattering

> Relativistic models developed for the exclusive and inclusive quasielastic (QE) electron scattering have been extended to charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) neutrino-nucleus scattering. Different descriptions of final-state interactions (FSI) are compared. For the inclusive electron scattering the relativistic Green's function approach is compared with calculations based on the use of relativistic purely real mean field potentials in the final state. Both approaches lead to a redistribution of the strength but conserving the total flux. Results for the differential cross section at different energies are presented. Scaling properties are also analyzed and discussed.

## Key claim

The relativistic Green's function (GF) approach and the relativistic mean field (RMF) model both redistribute strength while conserving total flux in inclusive quasielastic electron-nucleus scattering, but they produce increasingly divergent predictions for cross sections and scaling functions as momentum transfer increases above about 800 MeV/c, with the GF model showing sensitivity to the parameterization of the imaginary part of the optical potential.

## Method

The authors compare two relativistic models for inclusive quasielastic (QE) electron-nucleus scattering: the Green's function (GF) approach, which uses a complex energy-dependent optical potential and a biorthogonal expansion to treat final-state interactions (FSI), and the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, which uses the same real energy-independent potential for both bound and scattering states. For exclusive and semi-inclusive reactions, the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) is employed with complex optical potentials for the outgoing nucleon. The models are extended to charged-current (CC) and neutral-current (NC) neutrino-nucleus scattering using the same nuclear current and FSI treatments.

## Key numbers

- For the 12C(e,e') reaction at 1 GeV incident electron energy and q = 1000 MeV/c, the GF1 cross section at the peak overshoots the RMF cross section by up to 40%.
- For 12C(νμ,μ-) at incident neutrino energy Eν = 1000 MeV and muon scattering angle θμ = 45°, cross sections are shown as a function of muon kinetic energy Tμ for RPWIA, rROP, and GF with two optical potential parameterizations (EDAD1 and EDAD2).
- For 12C(e,e') at q = 500, 800, and 1000 MeV/c, the RMF, GF1, and GF2 cross sections are similar at q = 500 MeV/c, show moderate differences at q = 800 MeV/c, and larger differences at q = 1000 MeV/c.
- The imaginary part of the optical potential in the GF approach has its maximum strength around 500 MeV.

## Context

This work extends relativistic models previously developed and tested for exclusive (e,e'p) reactions (Meucci et al., Udias et al.) to inclusive electron scattering and to CC and NC neutrino-nucleus scattering. The comparison between the Pavia group's GF approach and the Madrid-Sevilla group's RMF model (Caballero et al.) addresses the treatment of FSI in the inclusive channel, where the GF approach conserves total flux through its biorthogonal expansion while the RMF model maintains the continuity equation. The analysis of scaling functions connects to the extensive superscaling phenomenology of Donnelly et al. and the phenomenological scaling function fL^exp extracted from longitudinal response data. The results enable improved estimates of theoretical uncertainties for neutrino oscillation experiments that use nuclear targets, though the authors note that contributions beyond the QE peak (meson exchange currents, Delta effects) must still be evaluated (B.C.D.M. 2004, Amaro et al. 2005, Ivanov et al. 2008).
