# Traces of non-equilibrium dynamics in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The impact of non-equilibrium effects on the dynamics of heavy-ion collisions is investigated by comparing a non-equilibrium transport approach, the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD), to a 2D+1 viscous hydrodynamical model, which is based on the assumption of local equilibrium and conservation laws. Starting the hydrodynamical model from the same non-equilibrium initial condition as in the PHSD, using an equivalent lQCD Equation-of-State (EoS), the same transport coefficients, i.e. shear viscosity $η$ and the bulk viscosity $ζ$ in the hydrodynamical model, we compare the time evolution of the system in terms of energy density, Fourier transformed energy density, spatial and momentum eccentricities and ellipticity in order to quantify the traces of non-equilibrium phenomena. In addition, we also investigate the role of initial pre-equilibrium flow on the hydrodynamical evolution and demonstrate its importance for final state observables. We find that due to non-equilibrium effects, the event-by-event transport calculations show large fluctuations in the collective properties, while ensemble averaged observables are close to the hydrodynamical results.

## Key claim

Non-equilibrium effects in heavy-ion collisions cause large event-by-event fluctuations in collective properties, but ensemble-averaged observables from the transport approach (PHSD) remain close to the results from viscous hydrodynamics (VISHNew) when both models start from the same non-equilibrium initial conditions and use equivalent equations of state and transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors compared the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which makes no equilibrium assumptions, to a 2D+1 viscous hydrodynamical model (VISHNew) based on local equilibrium and conservation laws. They extracted initial conditions for the hydrodynamical evolution from PHSD at tau = 0.6 fm/c using Landau matching of the energy-momentum tensor, and used the same temperature-dependent shear viscosity from PHSD simulations and a bulk viscosity from Bayesian analysis in the hydrodynamical model. The time evolution of energy density, Fourier transformed energy density, spatial and momentum eccentricities, and ellipticity were compared for peripheral Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Initial condition extraction time: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c for peripheral (b = 6 fm) Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Space-time grid cell size: Delta x = 1 fm, Delta y = 1 fm, Delta z proportional to 0.5 x t/gamma_NN
- Critical energy density for phase transition: approximately 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- Number of parallel ensembles (NUM) used for smoothing: 30, providing the same level of smoothing as in typical PHSD simulations
- String formation time: approximately 0.8 fm/c in the rest frame of the corresponding string
- Temperature range for blending lattice QCD EoS into hadron resonance gas: 110 to 130 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons by Kodama et al. (2011) and de Souza et al. (2015) who studied event-by-event fluctuations in coarse-grained hydrodynamical evolution versus PHSD dynamics, finding that ensemble averages approach the hydrodynamical limit despite large fluctuations. The study also builds on prior PHSD work by Cassing (2008) showing linear correlation of elliptic flow v_2 with initial spatial eccentricity epsilon_2. The results enable development of new observables with enhanced sensitivity to non-equilibrium components of bulk QCD matter evolution, particularly relevant for early-time probes such as heavy quarks, electromagnetic probes, and jets.
