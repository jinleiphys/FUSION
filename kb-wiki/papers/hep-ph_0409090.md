# Transport coefficients and the 2PI effective action in the large N limit

> We discuss the computation of transport coefficients in large N_f QCD and the O(N) model for massive particles. The calculation is organized using the 1/N expansion of the 2PI effective action to next-to-leading order. For the gauge theory, we verify gauge fixing independence and consistency with the Ward identity. In the gauge theory, we find a nontrivial dependence on the fermion mass.

## Key claim

The 2PI effective action framework, when truncated at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion, provides a consistent diagrammatic method for computing transport coefficients (shear viscosity, electrical conductivity) in both the O(N) model and large N_f QCD, but the gauge theory shows a nontrivial dependence on fermion mass that is absent in the scalar case.

## Method

The authors organize the calculation using the 1/N expansion of the 2PI effective action to next-to-leading order, generating Schwinger-Dyson equations for two-point functions and Bethe-Salpeter equations for four-point vertices. They solve the resulting integral equations for the effective vertex using a variational method with trial functions, performing one-dimensional integrals for the source term and three- to four-dimensional integrals for the kernel and self-energy contributions. For the gauge theory, they work in generalized Coulomb gauge and verify gauge fixing independence and consistency with the Ward identity.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscosity in the weakly coupled O(N) model in the ultrahard momentum limit (p >> T) for zero mass and vanishing coupling: η∞ = 27648ζ(5)/π × (N³/(N+2)) × T³/λ² ≈ 3041.9 × (3N³/(N+2)) × T³/λ²
* For N=1, the numerical prefactor (3041.9) is very close to Jeon's result (3040) and Arnold, Moore and Yaffe's result (3033.5)
* The asymptotic large-momentum solution for χ(p) in the O(N) model: lim_{p→∞} χ(p) = 6912π × (N²/(N+2)) × (1/λ²) × (p²/T²)
* Shear viscosity in large N_f QCD is normalized with η0 = d_F/(T_F C_F) × T³/g⁴, where d_F = N_c, C_F = (N_c²-1)/(2N_c), T_F = 1/2

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic calculation of transport coefficients by Jeon (1995) for a single-component scalar field to the O(N) model and large N_f QCD using the 2PI effective action framework. For massless fermions in large N_f QCD, transport coefficients were previously computed by Moore (2001) using kinetic theory. The results provide support for the applicability of 2PI effective action truncations to nonequilibrium quantum field theory and enable future studies of dynamics far from equilibrium, where contributions dropped in the leading-order transport analysis must be included.
