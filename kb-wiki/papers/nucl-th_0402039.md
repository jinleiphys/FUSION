# In-medium properties of the omega meson through omega photoproduction in nuclei

> The feasibility to study the in-medium properties of the omega meson in photon induced reactions on nuclei is investigated by means of a semiclassical transport model. The calculations are dedicated to an experiment presently being analyzed by the TAPS/Crystal Barrel collaborations. A full coupled-channel treatment of final state interactions allows a quantitative analysis of the influence of absorption and elastic scattering of the produced hadrons and likewise the application of kinematic cuts on the observables. Within the scope of our calculations, the preliminary data by the TAPS/Crystal Barrel collaborations indeed seem to indicate a modification of the isoscalar spectral density in the strongly interacting environment.

# In-medium properties of the omega meson through omega photoproduction in nuclei

## Key claim
Within the scope of the semiclassical transport model calculations, preliminary data from the TAPS/Crystal Barrel collaborations appear to indicate a modification of the isoscalar spectral density in the strongly interacting environment, but the authors note that a dispersive analysis of the real part of the omega self-energy cannot reproduce the experimental data, raising questions about the validity of the low-density approximation or the complexity of the omega-nucleon scattering amplitude.

## Method
The authors employed a semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model with a full coupled-channel treatment of final state interactions to simulate inclusive photoproduction of omega mesons in nuclei, detected via the semi-hadronic decay channel omega → π⁰γ. The medium modification of the omega meson was introduced via the low-density theorem with a collisional broadening of about 40 MeV at nuclear saturation density and a density-dependent mass shift, while the elementary production process γN → ωX was modeled using measured cross sections and the FRITIOF event generator coupled to the vector meson dominance model.

## Key numbers
- Collisional broadening of about 40 MeV at nuclear saturation density and vanishing momentum
- Mass shift parameterization: m* = m₀ + U_S(r), with U_S(r) = -0.16 m₀ × ρ(r)/ρ₀
- Branching ratio of omega to π⁰γ decay channel: 8.7 × 10⁻²
- Pion kinetic energies from rescattering accumulated around 100 MeV, allowing background reduction via a kinetic energy cutoff
- Photon energy range used in the TAPS/CB experiment: up to 2.5 GeV
- Averaged photon energy bin for comparison with preliminary data: E_γ = (0.8–1.2) GeV
- Omega momenta to which the measurement is sensitive: approximately (200–400) MeV

## Context
This work extends the theoretical investigation of in-medium vector meson properties motivated by the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis (Brown, 1991) and QCD sum-rule analyses (Hatsuda; Leupold, 1998) that predict either dropping masses or increased widths in nuclear matter. The calculations are dedicated to an experiment by the TAPS/Crystal Barrel collaborations at ELSA and build on prior hadronic model predictions (Klingl, 1997; Post, 2003) and earlier transport model developments (Muehlich, 2003; Effenberger, 1999). The results enable future experimental discrimination between mass-shift and broadening scenarios, though the authors note that more data with lower momentum cuts and better understanding of experimental background are required to unambiguously confirm the mass shift.
