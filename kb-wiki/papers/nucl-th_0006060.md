# Gluonic Correlations in Matter

> We derive the analogue of the QCD low energy theorems for the scalar and pseudoscalar gluonic correlators in nuclear matter. We find that the scalar correlations are depleted while the pseudoscalar correlations are enhanced to leading order in the nuclear matter density. We briefly discuss the consequences of these findings on the QCD spectrum.

# Gluonic Correlations in Matter

## Key claim

The scalar and pseudoscalar gluonic correlation functions in nuclear matter obey low-energy theorems (LET) analogous to those in vacuum; to leading order in nuclear matter density, scalar correlations are depleted while pseudoscalar correlations are enhanced, with the precise magnitude depending on whether one assumes self-dual gauge configurations or uses a heavy-quark expansion.

## Method

The authors derive the LET for the scalar gluonic two-point function at finite density by taking the density-dependent expectation value of the standard renormalization-group relation and expanding to linear order in nucleon density ρ. For the pseudoscalar gluonic correlator, they present two independent derivations: one assuming self-dual (instantonic) gauge field configurations dominate the functional integral, and another using a heavy-quark mass expansion combined with a chiral rotation. Both approaches yield differential-operator relations that connect the density dependence of the correlators to the nucleon matrix element of the gluon operator.

## Key numbers

- Scalar gluonic correlator change at zero momentum: ΔS(Q²=0) = (45/(4b)) ρ ⟨N| (α_s/π) G² |N⟩, where b = 11 - (2/3)N_f and ρ is the nucleon density.
- Pseudoscalar gluonic correlator change (self-dual approximation): ΔP_{NLQ}^{sd}(Q²=0) = -(225/(32b)) ρ ⟨N| (α_s/π) G² |N⟩.
- Pseudoscalar gluonic correlator change (heavy-quark expansion): ΔP_{NLQ}^{hq}(Q²=0) = -(5/b) ρ ⟨N| (α_s/π) G² |N⟩.
- Vacuum scalar LET: S(Q²=0) = (18/b) ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩.
- Vacuum pseudoscalar LET (self-dual): P_{NLQ}^{sd}(Q²=0) = -(18/b) ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩.
- Vacuum pseudoscalar LET (heavy-quark): P_{NLQ}^{hq}(Q²=0) = -(8/b) ⟨(α_s/π) G²⟩.

## Context

This work extends the QCD low-energy theorems originally derived by Novikov, Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1981) for gluonic correlators in vacuum to the case of nuclear matter at low density. It generalizes the finite-temperature and finite-density LET for scalar gluon operators previously obtained by Kapusta (1993). The results provide constraints that lattice QCD simulations or effective models of QCD in matter must satisfy, and suggest that in nuclear matter the η' mass should increase while the scalar glueball mass should decrease, with potential implications for low-mass dilepton emissions, pion thresholds, and the strong CP problem in matter.
