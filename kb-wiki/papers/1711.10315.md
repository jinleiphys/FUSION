# Higher cumulants of baryon number in critical QCD

> We study the higher moments of the baryon number in the immediate neighbourhood of the QCD critical endpoint within the framework of Ising-QCD thermodynamics (N.~G. Antoniou {\it et al}, arXiv:1705.09124 [hep-ph]). We show that the kurtosis, as a function of the freeze-out baryon chemical potential, attains a sharp minimum very close to the critical point. We argue that the sharpness of this minimum is due to the narrowness of the critical region in the chemical potential direction. Our analysis reveals that the broad minimum of the kurtosis observed in Au+Au central collisions at STAR (in RHIC-BES I) in the colliding energy region $17$ GeV $< ~\sqrt{s}~<$ $39$ GeV is apparently only a precursor of the critical point and not a signature of its location.

## Key claim

The broad minimum of the kurtosis times variance squared (κσ²) observed in Au+Au collisions at STAR (RHIC-BES I) for colliding energies 17 GeV < √s < 39 GeV is only a precursor of the QCD critical point and not a direct signature of its location; the true critical signal is a sharp minimum of the non-Gaussian kurtosis κ that appears very close to the critical chemical potential, but this sharp minimum is masked when data are plotted against collision energy rather than freeze-out baryochemical potential.

## Method

The authors employed the Ising-QCD effective action (Antoniou et al., arXiv:1705.09124) to calculate the partition function for baryon number fluctuations, summing over constant field configurations. They computed the non-Gaussian kurtosis κ = C₄/C₂² − 3 (where C₂ and C₄ are the second and fourth cumulants) at the critical temperature T = T_c as a function of ln ζ = (μ_B − μ_c)/(k_B T_c). Separately, they fitted a non-critical φ⁴ effective action with tunable coefficients a₂, a₃, a₄ to reproduce the STAR κσ² data as a function of freeze-out μ_B, and compared the resulting broad minima with the sharp Ising-QCD prediction.

## Key numbers

- The critical region along the baryochemical potential direction has width Δμ_B ≈ 5 MeV for the 3d-Ising case (at m̂ = 0), and a more precise calculation gives Δμ_B ≈ 4.5 MeV.
- The scaling exponent for the first moment ⟨N⟩ at the critical point is q = 5/6 (3d-Ising universality class).
- The critical chemical potential is estimated as μ_c = 256 MeV, with critical temperature T_c = 167 MeV.
- The non-Gaussian kurtosis κ was calculated for the range −0.05 < ln ζ < 0.05, with the critical region corresponding to −0.01 < ln ζ < 0.02.
- The broad minimum in κσ² from STAR data spans the colliding energy region 17 GeV < √s < 39 GeV, corresponding to freeze-out chemical potentials that show two minima when plotted against μ_B.
- The critical point is argued to lie near √s = 14.5 GeV (μ_B ≈ 260 MeV), where the simple φ⁴ description fails.

## Context

This work extends the Ising-QCD thermodynamics framework introduced by Antoniou et al. (2017) and applies it to interpret the STAR Beam-Energy Scan I results (Luo, 2017) for net-proton κσ². The authors contradict the common interpretation that a non-monotonic behavior of κσ²(√s) is a sufficient signature for critical fluctuations (Stephanov, 2009; Athanasiou, 2010). Instead, they show that the broad minimum observed experimentally can be reproduced by a standard φ⁴ effective action with no phase transition, while the true critical signal is a sharp minimum in the non-Gaussian kurtosis κ that requires plotting data against freeze-out μ_B rather than √s. This enables a refined search strategy for the QCD critical endpoint at future facilities like FAIR and NICA, where the distorted baryon-number multiplicity distribution near the first-order transition region could be detected.
