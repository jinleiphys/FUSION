# Improved Limit on Tensor Currents in the Weak Interaction from $^8$Li $β$ Decay

> The electroweak interaction in the Standard Model (SM) is described by a pure vector-axial-vector structure, though any Lorentz-invariant component could contribute. In this work, we present the most precise measurement of tensor currents in the low-energy regime by examining the $β$-$\barν$ correlation of trapped $^{8}$Li ions with the Beta-decay Paul Trap. We find $a_{βν} = -0.3325 \pm 0.0013_{stat} \pm 0.0019_{syst}$ at $1σ$ for the case of coupling to right-handed neutrinos $(C_T=-C_T')$, which is consistent with the SM prediction.

## Key claim

The paper reports the most precise single measurement of tensor currents in the weak interaction at low energy, finding a beta-neutrino correlation coefficient consistent with the Standard Model prediction, but this result assumes coupling to right-handed neutrinos (C_T = -C_T') and a vanishing Fierz term (b_Fierz = 0).

## Method

The beta-neutrino angular correlation was measured using trapped 8Li ions in the Beta-decay Paul Trap (BPT) at the Argonne Tandem-Linac Accelerator System (ATLAS). The experiment detected beta-alpha-alpha triple coincidences from the Gamow-Teller decay of 8Li to the broad 2+ excited state of 8Be, which immediately breaks into two alpha particles. The kinematic shift in alpha-particle energy difference (Delta E_alpha) was used to extract the tensor current contribution |C_T/C_A|^2 by fitting the measured Delta E_alpha spectrum to a linear combination of simulated pure axial-vector and pure tensor interaction spectra.

## Key numbers

- Measured beta-neutrino correlation coefficient: a_beta_nu = -0.3325 +/- 0.0013_stat +/- 0.0019_syst at 1 sigma, under the assumption C_T = -C_T' (right-handed neutrino coupling)
- Extracted tensor current strength: |C_T/C_A|^2 = 0.0012 +/- 0.0019_stat +/- 0.0028_syst at 1 sigma
- Upper limit at 95.5% confidence level: |C_T/C_A|^2 < 0.0076, or |C_T/C_A| < 0.087
- Total number of triple coincidence events used in analysis: 2.9 x 10^5, representing about 1% of all 8Li decays in the trap
- The measurement represents a 41% improvement in uncertainty over the previous 8Li measurement by the same group (Burkey et al., 2015)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous 8Li measurement (Burkey et al., 2015) and improves upon the 1963 corrected 6He measurement (Johnson et al., 1963) that gave a_beta_nu = -0.3308 +/- 0.0030. The result is consistent with the Standard Model prediction, in contrast with the global nuclear limits presented by Falkowski et al. (2021) that showed a 3.2 sigma deviation from the SM. The improved precision was enabled by an order-of-magnitude increase in trapped ion rate and the use of ab initio symmetry-adapted no-core shell model calculations for recoil-order form factors, replacing the earlier Sumikama et al. values. Analysis of the mirror nucleus 8B decay is underway to further probe non-SM physics and test the conserved vector current hypothesis via the weak magnetism term.
