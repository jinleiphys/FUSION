# Front Form Spinors in Weinberg-Soper Formalism and Melosh Transformations for any Spin

## Key claim

The paper constructs front form $(j,0)\oplus(0,j)$ spinors for arbitrary spin $j$ using the Weinberg-Soper formalism without directly invoking any wave equations, and obtains generalized Melosh transformations up to spin two. The construction reproduces spin one half front-form results of Melosh, Lepage and Brodsky, and Dziembowski as special cases, but the authors note that the spinors are constructed under the assumption that the center of mass of composite hadrons is "best described, in the phenomenological sense" by these fields.

## Method

The authors start from the Weinberg-Soper formalism, constructing right-handed $\phi^R(p^\mu)$ and left-handed $\phi^L(p^\mu)$ matter fields for the $(j,0)$ and $(0,j)$ representations, respectively. They derive the front-form boost operator $L$ using generators $G_1 = K_1 - J_2$ and $G_2 = K_2 + J_1$ of the $x^+ = 0$ plane stability group, then construct the spinor boost matrix $M(L)$ and the hadronic spinors ${\cal U}_h(p^\mu)$ (even spinor parity) and ${\cal V}_h(p^\mu)$ (odd spinor parity) for $j = 1/2, 1, 3/2, 2$. The generalized Melosh transformation $\Omega(j)$ is obtained by expressing instant form spinors as linear combinations of front form spinors and computing the overlap matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- For spin one half, the front form spinors ${\cal U}_{\pm 1/2}(p^\mu)$ are given explicitly with normalization factor $\frac{1}{2}\sqrt{1/p^+}$, and the Melosh transformation $\Omega(1/2)$ contains a prefactor $1/[2(E+m)p^+]^{1/2}$.
- For spin one, the generalized Melosh transformation $\Omega(1)$ has prefactor $1/[2(E+m)p^+]$.
- For spin three half, $\Omega(3/2)$ has prefactor $1/[2(E+m)p^+]^{3/2}$.
- For spin two, $\Omega(2)$ has prefactor $1/[2(E+m)p^+]^2$.
- The normalization constant for the rest-frame spinors is chosen as ${\cal N}(j) = m^j$, so that ${\overline {\cal U}}_h(p^\mu) {\cal U}_{h'}(p^\mu) = m^{2j} \delta_{hh'}$ and ${\overline {\cal V}}_h(p^\mu) {\cal V}_{h'}(p^\mu) = -m^{2j} \delta_{hh'}$.
- In the high momentum limit $p^+ \gg m$, the leading asymptotic behavior of ${\cal U}_h(p^\mu)$ and ${\cal V}_h(p^\mu)$ is $\sim (p^+)^{|h|}$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier instant-form $(j,0)\oplus(0,j)$ spinor constructions of Ahluwalia and Ernst (1992) to the front form, and generalizes the spin one half front-form results of Lepage and Brodsky (1980) and the Melosh transformation of Melosh (1974) and Dziembowski (1987) to arbitrary spin. The formalism is in the spirit of Dirac's (1936) original motivation for high-spin wave equations for "approximate application to composite particles," and opens up the possibility of constructing a QCD-based effective field theory of hadronic resonances along the lines of recent work by Cahill (1992). The authors note that for massive particles, spinors with $|h| < j$ do not vanish in the high momentum limit, which has physical significance for processes such as $e^+ e^- \rightarrow p_{\pm 1/2} + \overline{\Delta}_{\pm 3/2}$ being suppressed relative to $e^+ e^- \rightarrow p_{\pm 1/2} + \overline{\Delta}_{\mp 1/2}$.
