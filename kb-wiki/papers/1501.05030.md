# Transverse Wobbling in $^{135}$Pr

> A pair of transverse wobbling bands has been observed in the nucleus $^{135}$Pr. The wobbling is characterized by $ΔI$ =1, E2 transitions between the bands, and a decrease in the wobbling energy confirms its transverse nature. Additionally, a transition from transverse wobbling to a three-quasiparticle band comprised of strong magnetic dipole transitions is observed. These observations conform well to results from calculations with the Tilted Axis Cranking (TAC) model and the Quasiparticle Triaxial Rotor (QTR) Model.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first observation of transverse wobbling in the A~130 mass region, specifically in the nucleus $^{135}$Pr, where the wobbling energy decreases with increasing angular momentum. This decrease is the hallmark of transverse wobbling, as opposed to the increase expected for a purely collective wobbler, and the observation is conditioned on the presence of a high-j odd proton (h$_{11/2}$) coupled to a triaxial rotor core at low deformation ($\epsilon \sim 0.16$).

## Method

High-spin states in $^{135}$Pr were populated via the $^{123}$Sb($^{16}$O,4n) reaction at 80 MeV, and gamma-ray coincidence events were collected using the Gammasphere array at Argonne National Laboratory ($3.7 \times 10^9$ three- and higher-fold events) and the INGA clover array at the TIFR-BARC facility in Mumbai ($4.5 \times 10^8$ two- and higher-fold events). Angular distributions and polarization asymmetries were extracted to determine the multipolarity and electric/magnetic character of interband transitions, and the wobbling energy $E_{wob}$ was calculated from level energies as a function of spin. Experimental results were compared with Tilted Axis Cranking (TAC) model calculations and a modified Quasiparticle Triaxial Rotor (QTR) model.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for the one-quasiproton yrast band: $\epsilon = 0.16$, $\gamma = 26^\circ$ (from TAC calculations with pairing gaps $\Delta_p = 1.1$ MeV, $\Delta_n = 1.0$ MeV).
- Deformation parameters for the three-quasiparticle configuration: $\epsilon = 0.20$, $\gamma = 28^\circ$ (from TAC calculations with $\Delta_n = 0$, $\Delta_p = 0.8$ MeV).
- QTR model moments of inertia (fitted): ${\cal J}_m = 7.4$, ${\cal J}_s = 5.6$, ${\cal J}_l = 1.8$ $\hbar^2$/MeV, with $c = 0.116$; TAC model moments: ${\cal J}_m = 19$, ${\cal J}_s = 8$, ${\cal J}_l = 3$ $\hbar^2$/MeV.
- E2 fractions for interband transitions: 60.6% for the 747.0-keV transition ($\frac{17}{2}^- \rightarrow \frac{15}{2}^-$), 70.3% for the 812.8-keV transition ($\frac{21}{2}^- \rightarrow \frac{19}{2}^-$), 85.0% for the 754.6-keV transition ($\frac{25}{2}^- \rightarrow \frac{23}{2}^-$).
- Experimental intraband $B(M1)/B(E2)$ ratios for the dipole band: ranged from $3.0 \pm 0.4$ $\mu_N^2/(eb)^2$ to $18.8 \pm 0.9$ $\mu_N^2/(eb)^2$.
- Wobbling energy $E_{wob}$ decreases with spin, reaching a minimum near $I^\pi = \frac{29}{2}^-$.

## Context

This work extends the study of wobbling excitations, previously observed only in triaxial strongly deformed (TSD) nuclei around A~160 (e.g., Odegard et al., Schonwasser et al., Hartley et al.), to the A~130 region at lower deformation. It confirms the theoretical prediction of transverse wobbling by Frauendorf and Donau (2014), where a high-j proton aligns along the short axis of a triaxial rotor, causing a characteristic decrease in wobbling energy. The identification of a wobbler band in $^{135}$Pr, alongside a signature partner band and a three-quasiparticle magnetic dipole band, provides clear evidence for triaxiality in this mass region and enables systematic searches for similar structures in neighboring nuclei.
