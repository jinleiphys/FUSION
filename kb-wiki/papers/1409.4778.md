# Production and Elliptic Flow of Dileptons and Photons in the semi-Quark Gluon Plasma

> We consider the thermal production of dileptons and photons at temperatures above the critical temperature in QCD. We use a model where color excitations are suppressed by a small value of the Polyakov loop, the semi Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). Comparing the semi-QGP to the perturbative QGP, we find a mild enhancement of thermal dileptons. In contrast, to leading logarithmic order in weak coupling there are far fewer hard photons from the semi-QGP than the usual QGP. To illustrate the possible effects on photon and dileptons production in heavy ion collisions, we integrate the rate with a realistic hydrodynamic simulation. Dileptons uniformly exhibit a small flow, but the strong suppression of photons in the semi-QGP tends to bias the elliptical flow of photons to that generated in the hadronic phase.

## Key claim

In the semi-Quark Gluon Plasma (semi-QGP) model, where color excitations are suppressed by a small Polyakov loop near the critical temperature, thermal dilepton production is mildly enhanced (by about 20%) compared to the perturbative QGP, while hard photon production is strongly suppressed by a factor that can reach 1/27 in the confined phase; this suppression biases the elliptic flow of thermal photons toward that generated in the hadronic phase, potentially explaining the large photon elliptic flow observed at RHIC and LHC.

## Method

The authors compute thermal dilepton and photon production rates to leading order in the QCD coupling (for photons, only to leading logarithmic order) using the semi-QGP model, where a constant background field A0 acts as an imaginary chemical potential for color, modifying the Fermi-Dirac and Bose-Einstein distribution functions. They then integrate these rates with the 3+1D ideal hydrodynamic simulation MUSIC for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV in the 20-40% centrality class, including hadronic matter contributions from previous work.

## Key numbers

- For dileptons at E = 1 GeV and p = 0, the modification factor f_lbar-l(Q) in the semi-QGP relative to perturbative QGP shows a modest enhancement of about 20% above T_c.
- For photons, the suppression factor f_gamma(Q) = 1 - 4q + (10/3)q^2, where q = Q/(2 pi T) with 0 < q < 1; in the confined phase (q = 1/3), f_gamma = 1/27.
- The dilepton elliptic flow v2 from the semi-QGP plus hadronic matter ranges from about 0.01 to 0.06 across invariant masses up to 1.5 GeV.
- The calculation is performed for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV in the 20-40% centrality class.

## Context

This work extends the semi-QGP model developed by Pisarski (2006), Hidaka (2008-2009), and Lin (2013) to electromagnetic probes, and addresses the puzzle of large photon elliptic flow observed by PHENIX at RHIC (Adare, 2011) and ALICE at LHC (Lohner, 2012). The results show that the strong suppression of photon production near T_c in the semi-QGP biases the total thermal photon v2 toward the hadronic phase, which has large flow, offering a potential resolution to the discrepancy between earlier QGP-only calculations and experimental data. The authors note that previous computations in the semi-QGP also found strong suppression of shear viscosity (Hidaka, 2008-2009) and heavy quark collisional energy loss (Lin, 2013), suggesting a consistent picture of suppressed color excitations near T_c.
