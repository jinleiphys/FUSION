# Decay rates of medium-heavy Lambda-hypernuclei within the Propagator Method

> The Lambda decay rates in nuclei has been calculated in ref. 1 using the Propagator Method in Local Density Approximation. We have studied the dependence of the widths (including the one for the two-body induced process Lambda NN -> NNN) on the N-N and Lambda-N short range correlations. Using a reasonable parametrization of these correlations, as well as realistic nuclear densities and Lambda wave functions, we reproduce, for the first time, the experimental non-mesonic widths from medium to heavy hypernuclei.

# Decay rates of medium-heavy Lambda-hypernuclei within the Propagator Method

## Key claim
Using a reasonable parametrization of short-range correlations (Landau parameters g'=0.8, g'_Lambda=0.4) together with realistic nuclear densities and Lambda wave functions, the authors reproduce for the first time the experimental non-mesonic widths from medium to heavy hypernuclei, but only after adjusting the model parameters to fit the non-mesonic width of 12-Lambda-C as a testing ground.

## Method
The decay widths are calculated in nuclear matter using the Propagator Method, which obtains the Lambda self-energy from the imaginary part of the pion propagator in the medium, including 1p-1h, 2p-2h, and higher excitations. The results are extended to finite nuclei via the Local Density Approximation (LDA), using a local Fermi sea related to the nuclear density and Lambda wave functions from Wood-Saxon potentials that reproduce experimental s and p single-particle levels. Short-range correlations are incorporated through Landau-Migdal parameters g' (for N-N) and g'_Lambda (for Lambda-N), and the two-body induced process Lambda NN -> NNN is included.

## Key numbers
- For 12-Lambda-C: mesonic width Gamma_M = 0.25 (units not specified, presumably in units of the free Lambda decay width), one-body non-mesonic width Gamma_1 = 0.82, two-body induced width Gamma_2 = 0.16, total non-mesonic width Gamma_NM = 0.98, total width Gamma_T = 1.23
- Experimental total width for 12-Lambda-C: 1.25 +/- 0.18 (BNL and KEK) and 1.14 +/- 0.08 (KEK New)
- Landau parameters reproducing 12-Lambda-C non-mesonic rate: g' = 0.8, g'_Lambda = 0.4; without two-body induced channel, g' = 0.7
- For 208-Lambda-Pb: total width Gamma_T = 1.40, with mesonic width Gamma_M = 1 x 10^(-4), one-body width Gamma_1 = 1.21, two-body width Gamma_2 = 0.19
- Lambda wave functions from Wood-Saxon potentials with depth 28-32 MeV across medium to heavy hypernuclei
- Different Lambda wave functions give total decay widths differing by at most 15%

## Context
This work extends the Propagator Method of Oset and Salcedo (1985) to medium-heavy hypernuclei, building on the authors' previous calculation (Alberico et al., 1999). It addresses the longstanding puzzle that theoretical calculations of the neutron-to-proton ratio Gamma_n/Gamma_p (e.g., Oset et al., 1998; Parreno et al., 1997; Okamoto et al., 1998) systematically underestimate experimental values (0.5 to 2), while OPE models reproduce one-body non-mesonic rates for light and medium hypernuclei. The results enable improved Monte Carlo simulations of nucleon spectra (Ramos et al., 1997) that account for final state interactions, though direct comparison with experimental spectra from Noumi et al. (1995) and Bhang et al. (1998) remains limited because those spectra have not been corrected for energy losses in target and detector.
