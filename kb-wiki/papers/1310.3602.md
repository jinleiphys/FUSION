# Quark mean-field model for single and double $Λ$ and $Ξ$ hypernuclei

> The study of hypernuclei in the quark mean-field model are extended from single-$Λ$ hypernuclei to double-$Λ$ hypernuclei as well as $Ξ$ hypernuclei (double strangeness nuclei), with a broken SU(3) symmetry for the quark confinement potential. The strength of the potential for $u,d$ quarks is constrained by the properties of finite nuclei, while the one for $s$ quark is fixed by the single $Λ$-nucleus potential at the nuclear saturation density, which has a slightly different value. Compared to our previous work, we find that the introduction of such symmetry breaking improves efficiently the description of the single $Λ$ energies for a wide range of mass region, which demonstrates the importance of this effect on the hypernuclei study. Predictions for double-$Λ$ hypernuclei and $Ξ$ hypernuclei are then made, confronting with a few available experiential double-$Λ$ hypernuclei data and other model calculations. The consistency of our results with the available double-$Λ$ hypernucleus is found to be surprisingly good. Also, there is generally a bound state for $Ξ^0$ hypernuclei with both light and heavy core nuclei in our model.

## Key claim

The authors extend the quark mean-field (QMF) model to double-Λ and Ξ hypernuclei by introducing an effective SU(3) symmetry breaking, where the confinement potential strength for the s quark (k^s = 1000 MeV/fm²) is set different from that for u,d quarks, constrained by the single Λ-nucleus potential at saturation density rather than by strict SU(3) symmetry.

## Method

The QMF model treats baryons as composites of three constituent quarks satisfying Dirac equations with scalar-vector confinement potentials and meson mean fields (σ, ω, ρ). The confinement strength for u,d quarks (k^q) is fixed by finite nuclei properties, while the s-quark strength (k^s) is adjusted to reproduce the empirical single-Λ potential U_Λ^(N) ≈ −30 MeV at saturation density. The resulting baryon effective masses and meson-baryon couplings are then used in self-consistent mean-field calculations for spherical hypernuclei, with pairing treated via BCS theory and center-of-mass corrections included microscopically.

## Key numbers

- Single-Λ potential at saturation density: U_Λ^(N) = −28.58 MeV at ρ = 0.145 fm⁻³, obtained with k^s = 1000 MeV/fm²
- Single-Ξ potential at saturation density: approximately −10 MeV (weakly attractive)
- Deepest bound Ξ⁰ state: −7.78 MeV for the 1s₁/₂ state in ²⁰⁸_Ξ⁰Pb
- Λ binding energy for ⁴⁰_ΛCa 1s₁/₂: −18.64 MeV (experimental: −18.7 ± 1.1 MeV)
- Λ binding energy for ⁸⁹_ΛY 1s₁/₂: −22.60 MeV (experimental: −23.1 ± 0.5 MeV)
- Λ binding energy for ²⁰⁸_ΛPb 1s₁/₂: −25.37 MeV (experimental: −26.3 ± 0.8 MeV)
- Double-Λ binding energy B_ΛΛ for ⁶_ΛΛHe: 7.94 MeV (experimental: 6.91 ± 0.16 MeV)
- Double-Λ bond energy ΔB_ΛΛ for ⁶_ΛΛHe: 0.75 MeV (experimental: 0.67 ± 0.17 MeV)
- Double-Λ binding energy B_ΛΛ for ²¹⁰_ΛΛPb: 50.73 MeV (prediction)
- Double-Λ bond energy ΔB_ΛΛ saturates near zero for heavy systems, crossing zero around ⁴²_ΛΛCa

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QMF studies of single-Λ hypernuclei (Hu et al. 2013, Shen et al. 2000, 2002) by introducing SU(3) symmetry breaking in the quark confinement potential, improving agreement with experimental single-Λ energies compared to the symmetric case (k^q = k^s = 700 MeV/fm²). The results for double-Λ hypernuclei are compared with experimental data from the Nagara event and other emulsion experiments (Nakazawa 2010, Ahn 2013), as well as with RMF calculations (Shen et al. 2006) and Skyrme-Hartree-Fock studies (Schulze et al. 2013). The model predicts bound Ξ⁰ hypernuclei for both light and heavy core nuclei, providing testable predictions for future experiments at facilities studying strangeness physics.
