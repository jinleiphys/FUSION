# Direct observation of three-neutron emission from $^7$He$^*$ and the search for the trineutron

> Three-neutron emission from $^7$He has been directly measured for the first time, following neutron knockout from a $^8$He beam at 156 MeV/nucleon. A resonance-like structure at $2.08(4)$ MeV above the $^4$He+$3n$ threshold [$E_x=2.68(4)$ MeV] with a width of $3.9(2)$ MeV was observed and deduced to arise predominately from the predicted $J^π=3/2^{-}_2$ level. The three-neutron invariant-mass spectrum was reconstructed and found to peak at around 1 MeV and could, through complete simulations incorporating neutron-neutron correlations, be very well described by the sequential decay of $^7$He$^*$ via the $2_1^+$ excited state of $^6$He. No evidence was found for any significant three-neutron correlations beyond those expected from well-established two-body interactions, including a trineutron resonance.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first direct measurement of three-neutron emission from excited states of 7He, observing a resonance-like structure at 2.08(4) MeV above the 4He+3n threshold with a width of 3.9(2) MeV. However, the abstract's claim of "no evidence for any significant three-neutron correlations" is qualified by the fact that the analysis sets an upper limit of 0.8% (at 1 sigma) for a narrow trineutron resonance and 1.6% for a broader one, meaning the search is sensitive only to specific predicted trineutron parameters and cannot exclude all possible three-neutron resonances.

## Method

The experiment used a 156 MeV/nucleon 8He beam impinging on a 15 cm thick liquid hydrogen target, populating 7He excited states via the (p,pn) neutron knockout reaction. The charged fragments (4He, 6He) were momentum analyzed using the SAMURAI spectrometer, while forward-going neutrons were detected by the NeuLAND and NEBULA plastic scintillator arrays, enabling direct detection of three beam-velocity neutrons. Invariant-mass spectra for the 6He+n, 4He+2n, and 4He+3n decay channels were reconstructed and fit with Breit-Wigner line shapes incorporating energy-dependent widths, with all lineshapes processed through a complete GEANT4 simulation of the setup including detector acceptances, efficiencies, resolutions, and crosstalk rejection.

## Key numbers

- Resonance-like structure at 2.08(4) MeV above the 4He+3n threshold (excitation energy Ex=2.68(4) MeV) with a width of 3.9(2) MeV, observed in the 4He+3n channel
- 6He+n ground state resonance parameters: Er=379(2) keV, Gamma_r=137(10) keV, from the 6He+n relative-energy spectrum
- 6He(2_1^+) resonance parameters from the 4He+2n channel: Er=823(6) keV, Gamma_r=228(29) keV
- Three-neutron detection efficiencies at 1 MeV relative energy: approximately 40% for 1n, 14% for 2n, and 3% for 3n
- Upper limit of 0.8% (1 sigma) for a narrow trineutron resonance with parameters from Li (2019) (E_3n=1.3 MeV, Gamma_3n=0.9 MeV), and 1.6% for a broader trineutron (E_3n=3 MeV, Gamma_3n=5 MeV)
- Resolution in transverse momentum P_X of the knocked-out neutron: FWHM = 38 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of 7He excited states from experiments such as Kor (1999) and Skaza (2006), which had suggested broad structures at Ex approximately 2-3 MeV but without direct three-neutron detection. The measurement provides the first direct observation of three-neutron emission from a well-defined nuclear state and demonstrates that the decay proceeds sequentially via 6He(2_1^+), ruling out significant three-neutron correlations including a trineutron resonance as predicted by Gandolfi (2017), Li (2019), and Mazur (2024). The high-quality three-neutron emission data can serve as a benchmark for theoretical descriptions of multi-neutron correlations and decay mechanisms, and the experimental approach is being extended to other multineutron emitters such as 4n-unbound 7H.
