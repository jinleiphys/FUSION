# Study of Gamow-Teller strength and associated weak-rates on odd-A nuclei in stellar matter

> In a recent study by Cole et al., it was concluded that QRPA calculations show larger deviations and overestimate the total experimental Gamow-Teller (GT) strength. It was also concluded that QRPA calculated electron capture rates exhibit larger deviation than those derived from the measured GT strength distributions. The main purpose of this study is to probe the findings of the Cole et al. paper. This study gives useful information on the performance of QRPA-based nuclear models. As per simulation results, the capturing of electrons that occur on medium heavy isotopes have a significant role in decreasing the ratio of electron-to-baryon content of the stellar interior during the late stages of core evolution. We report the calculation of allowed charge-changing transitions strength for odd-A fp-shell nuclei (45Sc and 55Mn) by employing the deformed pn-QRPA approach. The computed GT transition strength is compared with previous theoretical calculations and measured data. For stellar applications the corresponding electron capture rates are computed and compared with rates using previously calculated and measured Gamow-Teller values. Our finding show that our calculated results are in decent accordance with measured data. At higher stellar temperature our calculated electron capture rates are larger than those calculated by Independent Particle Model (IPM) and shell model. It was further concluded that at low temperature and high density regions the positron emission weak-rates from 45Sc and 55Mn may be neglected in simulation codes.

## Key claim

The authors find that, contrary to the conclusions of Cole et al. (2012), not all QRPA calculations overestimate experimental Gamow-Teller strength; their deformed pn-QRPA model produces GT strength distributions in decent accordance with measured data for the odd-A nuclei 45Sc and 55Mn, and at higher stellar temperatures their calculated electron capture rates exceed those from the Independent Particle Model and shell model.

## Method

The authors employed the deformed proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with separable residual GT interactions in a Nilsson basis, incorporating pairing correlations via BCS approximation and quasi-particle phonon coupling. They computed allowed charge-changing GT transition strengths for the odd-A fp-shell nuclei 45Sc and 55Mn from 200 discrete parent levels to 300 daughter levels, using a quenching factor of 0.6. The corresponding stellar electron capture and positron emission weak rates were calculated over a temperature range of (0.7–30)×10^9 K and densities of 10^2 to 10^11 g cm^−3, using Boltzmann-distributed occupation probabilities for parent excited states.

## Key numbers

- For 45Sc at T9 = 2 and ρYe ∼ 10^7 g cm^−3, the pn-QRPA calculated weak rates are an order of magnitude greater than shell model calculation, but in decent comparison with measured data.
- At higher stellar temperature for 45Sc, the pn-QRPA calculated weak rates are bigger by a factor of approximately 5 than the data.
- For 55Mn at low temperature and ρYe ∼ 10^7 g cm^−3, pn-QRPA calculated weak rates are smaller than the data by a factor of about 12 and smaller than shell model calculation by a factor of about 7.
- At high stellar temperatures for 55Mn, pn-QRPA calculated weak rates exceed those from experimental GT strength and shell model calculations by factors of almost 3 and 4, respectively.
- The pairing gaps were calculated as Δ_n = Δ_p = 12/√A MeV.
- The Nilsson oscillator constant was taken as ħω = 41/A^(1/3) MeV.
- The constant D was taken as 6143 s and g_A/g_V as −1.2694.
- The quenching factor f_q = 0.6 was used for the pn-QRPA calculations.

## Context

This work directly probes the conclusions of Cole et al. (2012), who found that QRPA calculations overestimate total experimental GT strength and produce larger deviations in electron capture rates compared to shell model results. The study extends prior theoretical work by Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1980) using the Independent Particle Model, and by Aufderheide et al. (1994) for heavier nuclei, as well as large-scale shell model calculations by Langanke et al. (2001) used in presupernova evolution simulations. The results suggest that the deformed pn-QRPA model, which does not rely on the Brink-Axel hypothesis and incorporates a large model space of 5ħω, may provide enhanced electron capture rates that could have substantial impact during the late phases of presupernova evolution of high mass stars, and the authors suggest core-collapse simulators check the effect of these enhanced rates.
