# Overview on the theory and phenomenology of generalized parton distributions

> We give a brief overview on the theory and phenomenology of generalized parton distributions (GPDs), including the recently developed framework of single-diffractive hard exclusive process for matching GPDs to experimental observables. We concentrate on the extraction of GPDs from experimental processes, especially on the challenges and potential solutions regarding the separation of different GPDs and the extraction of $x$-dependence of GPDs, which is critically important for constructing the tomographic images and matching the $x$-moments of GPDs to various emergent hadron properties.

## Key claim

The paper argues that extracting the x-dependence of generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from experimental data is an ill-defined inversion problem because most known hard exclusive processes only yield integrals of GPDs over x, but a few specific processes (diphoton mesoproduction and photon-pion pair photoproduction) produce non-scaling hard kernels that can break this degeneracy and distinguish shadow GPDs from real ones.

## Method

The authors develop a unified framework called single-diffractive hard exclusive processes (SDHEPs), where a hadron diffracts into a final-state hadron plus a virtual state A* that then undergoes a hard 2-to-2 collision with another particle B. They factorize the amplitude into a GPD describing the diffraction and a hard scattering amplitude, with the leading channel (m=1) being the Bethe-Heitler process and the subleading channel (m=2) giving the GPD contribution. The azimuthal modulations in the diffractive frame and SDHEP frame are used to separate different GPD types, while the x-dependence extraction relies on processes where the hard kernel introduces a pole that depends on the scattering angle θ, breaking the leading-order scaling.

## Key numbers

- The soft scale is defined as Q_s ≡ √(-t) and must satisfy √(-t) ≪ Q, where Q ≡ q_T is the hard scale from the transverse momentum of produced particles.
- The contribution from the m-th channel scales as (√(-t)/q_T)^(m-1) relative to the m=1 channel.
- For the diphoton mesoproduction process, the new GPD integral contains a pole at x_p(θ) = ξ [(1 - z + tan²(θ/2) z)/(1 - z - tan²(θ/2) z)], which depends on the scattering angle θ.
- The differential cross section for real photon electroproduction (BH+DVCS) is given at next-to-leading-power accuracy (m ≤ 2) with azimuthal modulations including cosφ_S, sinφ_S, cosφ, sinφ, and combinations thereof.

## Context

This work extends the GPD factorization framework originally developed by Muller (1994), Ji (1996), and Radyushkin (1997), and builds on the two-scale exclusive process formalism. It addresses the challenge of separating different GPDs (H, E, H̃, Ẽ, and transversity GPDs) and the ill-defined inversion problem for x-dependence identified by Bertone (2021) regarding shadow GPDs. The SDHEP framework enables a consistent, process-independent definition of GPDs that paves the way for global analyses similar to those for parton distribution functions and TMDs, and it identifies specific processes (diphoton mesoproduction at J-PARC kinematics and photon-pion photoproduction at JLab Hall-D kinematics) that can distinguish shadow GPDs from real ones through both magnitude and shape of differential observables.
