# PSR J1231-1411 revisited: Pulse Profile Analysis of X-ray Observation

> One of the primary goals of Neutron Star Interior Composition Explorer (NICER)-like X-ray missions is to impose stringent constraints on the neutron star equation of state by precisely measuring their masses and radii. NICER has recently expanded the dataset of inferred mass-radius relations for neutron stars, including four rotation-powered millisecond pulsars PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, PSR J0437-4715, and PSR J1231-1411. In this work, the mass-radius relation and X-ray emitting region properties of PSR J1231-1411 are inferred with an independent pulse profile modeling based on the spherical star Schwarzschild-spacetime and Doppler approximation. With one single-temperature elongated hot spot and one single-temperature crescent hot spot, the inferred gravitational mass is $M = 1.12 \pm 0.07 M_{\odot}$ and the inferred equatorial radius is $R_{eq} = 9.91_{-0.86}^{+0.88}$ km (68% credible intervals). It provides an alternative geometry configuration of the X-ray emitting region for PSR J1231-1411 to sufficiently explain the observation data of NICER and XMM-Newton. The inferred radius is smaller than that derived by \citet{salmi2024nicer} ($M = 1.04_{-0.03}^{+0.05} M_{\odot}$, $R_{eq} = 12.6 \pm 0.3$ km), and the inferred mass is slightly higher in this work. The inferred geometry configurations of the X-ray emitting region in both works are non-antipodal, which is not consistent with a centered dipole magnetic field and suggests a complex magnetic field structure.

## Key claim

Using an independent pulse profile modeling code based on the spherical star Schwarzschild-spacetime and Doppler approximation (rather than the oblate-star formalism used in prior work), the authors infer a gravitational mass of \(M = 1.12 \pm 0.07 M_\odot\) and equatorial radius of \(R_{eq} = 9.91_{-0.86}^{+0.88}\) km for PSR J1231-1411, which yields a radius smaller than that found by Salmi (2024) (\(12.6 \pm 0.3\) km) and a slightly higher mass than their \(1.04_{-0.03}^{+0.05} M_\odot\).

## Method

The authors performed Bayesian pulse profile modeling of NICER and XMM-Newton data using a C++ code that implements light-bending via the lensing integral in a Schwarzschild spacetime with Doppler corrections, combined with the NSX hydrogen atmosphere model. They tested several hot-spot geometries and selected a model with one single-temperature elongated spot and one single-temperature crescent spot (STS-PST), fitting the joint NICER and XMM-Newton dataset with the MultiNest nested sampler using a background-marginalized likelihood for NICER and a single-background likelihood for XMM-Newton.

## Key numbers

* Gravitational mass: \(M = 1.12 \pm 0.07 M_\odot\) (68% credible interval) for PSR J1231-1411, using the STS-PST geometric model.
* Equatorial radius: \(R_{eq} = 9.91_{-0.86}^{+0.88}\) km (68% credible interval) for PSR J1231-1411, using the STS-PST geometric model.
* Distance to Earth: \(D = 621.72_{-58.07}^{+57.97}\) pc (68% credible interval) for PSR J1231-1411, using the STS-PST geometric model.
* Hydrogen column density: \(N_H = 2.31_{-0.40}^{+0.40} \times 10^{20}\) cm\(^{-2}\) (68% credible interval) for PSR J1231-1411, using the STS-PST geometric model.
* NICER effective exposure after filtering: 2.23 Ms, yielding 1,156,031 counts in the 0.3-1.5 keV energy range.
* XMM-Newton EPIC-pn effective exposure after filtering: 13.43 ks.

## Context

This work provides an alternative geometry configuration for the X-ray emitting regions of PSR J1231-1411 compared to the analysis by Salmi (2024), who used two dual-temperature overlapping regions and obtained a larger radius. The inferred non-antipodal hot-spot geometry in both studies is inconsistent with a centered dipole magnetic field, suggesting a complex magnetic field structure. The results extend the NICER mass-radius dataset for millisecond pulsars (including PSR J0030+0451, PSR J0740+6620, and PSR J0437-4715) and demonstrate that the spherical-star approximation yields consistent results for pulsars with spin frequencies below approximately 300 Hz, though discrepancies in inferred parameters for PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0437-4715 highlight the sensitivity to the choice of spacetime approximation and sampling settings.
