# The Nucleon Electric Dipole Moment in Light-Front QCD

> I present an exact relationship between the electric dipole moment and anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon in the light-front formalism of QCD and consider its consequences.

## Key claim
The paper derives an exact relationship between the electric dipole moment (EDM) and the anomalous magnetic moment of the nucleon in the light-front formalism of QCD, showing that both quantities should be computed with the same method to test for consistency; the relation holds for any spin-1/2 system and is independent of the mechanism of CP violation.

## Method
The authors analyze the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in the light-front QCD formalism, extending the Drell-Yan-West-Brodsky framework to parity- and time-reversal-odd observables. They construct parity and time-reversal operators on the light front, introduce a CP-violating phase parameter β_a in the light-front wave functions, and derive a proportionality between the electric dipole form factor F_3(q^2) and the Pauli form factor F_2(q^2) for each Fock component a.

## Key numbers
- The empirical anomalous magnetic moments used are κ^n = -1.91 and κ^p = 1.79, in units of μ_N.
- The ratio (d^n + d^p)/(d^p - d^n) is estimated as approximately -0.03 from the light-front relation, compared to approximately -0.3 from QCD sum rule calculations.
- The QCD sum rule method yields computed anomalous magnetic moments κ^n_th = -2 and κ^p_th = +2, in good agreement with experiment.
- The neutron EDM from the CKM mechanism is cited as d^n_CKM ≈ 10^{-32} e-cm from chiral Lagrangian treatments, and the d-quark EDM as d^d_KM ≈ 10^{-34} e-cm from three-loop leading-logarithmic approximation.

## Context
This work extends the light-front QCD formalism for electromagnetic form factors to include CP-violating observables, complementing earlier studies of Dirac and Pauli form factors. The derived relation provides a consistency test for methods used to compute nucleon EDMs, such as the QCD sum rule approach of Ioffe (1980s) and Pospelov and Ritz (2005), and the chiral Lagrangian estimates of Crewther et al. (1979). The result enables sharper constraints on non-CKM sources of CP violation by requiring that any model predicting the nucleon EDM must also successfully reproduce the anomalous magnetic moment.
