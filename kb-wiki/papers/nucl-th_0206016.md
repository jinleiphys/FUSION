# The Pairing interaction in nuclei: comparison between exact and approximate treatments

> As a model for a deformed nucleus the many level pairing model (picket fence model with ~100 levels) is considered in four approximations and compared to the exact solution given by Richardson long time ago. It is found that, as usual, the number projected BCS method improves over standard BCS but that it is much less accurate than the more sophisticated many-body-approaches which are Coupled Cluster Theory (CCT) in its SUB2 version or Self-Consistent Random Phase Approximation (SCRPA).

## Key claim

For a deformed nucleus modeled by a picket fence pairing Hamiltonian with approximately 100 levels, the number-projected BCS (PBCS) method improves over standard BCS but is significantly less accurate than Coupled Cluster Theory in its SUB2 version (exp S2) or Self-Consistent Random Phase Approximation (SCRPA), which achieve errors of only about 5% and 2% respectively in the normal phase, though these methods are not variational and overbind.

## Method

The authors compared four approximate treatments of the pairing Hamiltonian against the exact Richardson solution for a picket fence model with 100 equally spaced single-particle levels (spacing 300 keV) and 100 nucleons (half filling). They implemented standard BCS, number-projected BCS (PBCS), an exponential variational ansatz (Exp), Coupled Cluster Theory at the SUB2 level (exp S2), and Self-Consistent Random Phase Approximation (SCRPA), computing ground state correlation energies as a function of pairing strength G.

## Key numbers

- The model used 100 levels with a constant level spacing of 300 keV and 100 nucleons (half filling), representing typical level density and neutron numbers in the rare earth region (A ~ 170).
- The physical value of the pairing interaction for this region is G ~ 0.1 MeV, with a gap of Delta ~ 0.8 MeV.
- The critical pairing strength in the BCS approximation for this model is G_c ~ 0.055 MeV.
- In the worst case near the transition region, the exp S2 method has an error of about 5% and SCRPA has an error of about 2% in the correlation energy.
- PBCS and the Exp method show errors of the order of 15% to 20% in the correlation energy.
- The iterative solutions for CCT and SCRPA did not converge beyond G/G_c ~ 1.3 in the normal particle basis.

## Context

This work extends the exact Richardson solution (Richardson, 1963-1968) for the pairing Hamiltonian to test approximations commonly used in nuclear structure, specifically for deformed nuclei where the picket fence model is appropriate. The study enables quantitative assessment of which many-body methods are reliable for pairing correlations in the normal phase, and suggests that both exp S2 and SCRPA should be extended to the superfluid regime and to more realistic forces, building on earlier positive results for correlation functions in other models (Dukelsky and Schuck, 1990).
