# Equation of State within Gluon Dominated QGP Model in Relativistic Hydrodynamics Approach

> The dynamics of Quark-gluon plasma (QGP) as a lump of deconfined free quarks and gluons is elaborated. Based on the first principal we construct the Lagrangian that represents the dynamics of QGP. To induce a hydrodynamics approach, we substitute the gluon fields with flow fields. As a result, the derived equation of Motion (E.O.M) for gluon dominated QGP shows the form that similar to Euler equation, and the energy momentum tensor also represents explicitly the system of ideal fluid. Combining the E.O.M and energy momentum tensor, the pressure and energy density distribution as the equation of states are analytically derived.

## Key claim

The paper derives an equation of state for a gluon-dominated QGP by constructing a Lagrangian that, after substituting gluon fields with flow fields, yields an energy-momentum tensor identical to that of an ideal fluid; the resulting pressure and energy density are equal, implying a radiation-like state with \(P/\rho = 1\), but this result depends on the assumption of homogeneous gluon color states and a time-independent scalar field.

## Method

The authors start from a QCD-inspired Lagrangian with SU(3)\(_F\) ⊗ U(1)\(_G\) gauge symmetry and replace the gluon field \(U^a_\mu\) with a product of a relativistic 4-velocity \(u_\mu\) and a scalar field \(\phi^a\), effectively treating the gluon field as a flow field. Applying the Euler-Lagrange equation yields an equation of motion that reduces to the classical Euler equation in the non-relativistic limit. The energy-momentum tensor is then derived from the Lagrangian, and under the assumptions of homogeneous gluon color states (\(\phi^a = \phi\)) and a time-independent scalar field, it takes the ideal-fluid form \(\mathcal{T}_{\mu\nu} = (\mathcal{E} + \mathcal{P}) u_\mu u_\nu - \mathcal{P} g_{\mu\nu}\), giving \(\mathcal{E} = \mathcal{P} = 4 T g_F m_Q \phi\). The scalar field \(\phi\) is obtained by solving a simplified differential equation, and the pressure and energy density are integrated over a Friedmann–Robertson–Walker (FRW) background geometry.

## Key numbers

* The equation of state derived is \(\mathcal{E} = \mathcal{P} = 4 T g_F m_Q \phi\), where \(T\) is the sum of SU(3) generators, \(g_F\) is the strong coupling constant, \(m_Q\) is the quark mass, and \(\phi\) is the scalar field.
* The pressure-to-energy-density ratio is \(P/\rho = 1\), indicating a radiation-like state; this is contrasted with the general radiation condition \(P \sim 1/3 \rho\).
* For the case of constant \(r\) and \(\phi(t) \propto 1/t\), the pressure and energy scale as \(P = \rho \propto \lambda \zeta R^3 / t\), where \(\lambda = 4 T g_F f_Q m_Q\), \(\zeta\) is the spatial integral, and \(R\) is the scale factor.

## Context

This work extends the "fluid QCD model" previously developed by Sulaiman and Djun (no year given) and Djun (no year given) to derive an explicit equation of state for a gluon-dominated QGP. It contrasts with quark-dominated approaches (e.g., Romatschke, no year given) that require a very small shear-viscosity-to-entropy ratio to fit experimental data, and with lattice QCD calculations (e.g., Gottlieb, no year given; Petreczky, no year given). The results are intended to provide observables accessible in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, enabling future adjustments and verification of macroscopic QGP dynamics.
