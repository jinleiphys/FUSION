# Highlights of pion and kaon structure from continuum analyses

> One of the biggest challenges in contemporary physics is understanding the origin and dynamics of the internal structure of hadrons which, at a fundamental level, is described by quantum chromodynamics (QCD). Taking great prominence amongst hadrons are pions and kaons which, despite being the lightest hadrons in nature, their very existence is intimately connected to those mechanisms responsible for almost all of the mass of the visible matter. In this manuscript we discuss many aspects of the pion and kaon structure via light front wave functions and generalized parton distributions, and a collection of other distributions and structural properties that are inferred therefrom.

# Highlights of pion and kaon structure from continuum analyses

## Key claim
Pion and kaon structural properties, including generalized parton distributions (GPDs), electromagnetic and gravitational form factors, and impact parameter space distributions, can be derived from light-front wavefunctions (LFWFs) obtained via a perturbation theory integral representation (PTIR) approach that is informed by continuum Schwinger methods (CSM) predictions for parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) and parton distribution functions (PDFs). The approach is valid at the hadronic scale where fully dressed valence quarks carry all hadron momentum, and the resulting distributions reveal that emergent hadron mass (EHM) broadens distributions relative to asymptotic profiles while Higgs-generated flavor asymmetry produces skewed kaon distributions.

## Method
The authors construct leading-twist LFWFs for pions and kaons using a PTIR approach, where the quark propagator is parameterized with a constituent quark mass and the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is expressed via a spectral weight function. The spectral weight is informed by realistic CSM predictions for PDAs and PDFs from prior Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equation studies (Chang 2013, Ding 2019, Cui 2020). GPDs are then obtained via the overlap representation of LFWFs, and electromagnetic form factors (EFFs), gravitational form factors (GFFs), charge and mass distributions, pressure and shear distributions, and impact parameter space distributions are derived from the GPDs.

## Key numbers
- Pion charge radius: r_pi+ = 0.69 fm
- Kaon charge radius: r_K+ = 0.56 fm
- Ratio of gravitational to charge radii: r_P^theta2 approx 0.81 r_P, r_P^theta1 approx 1.18 r_P
- Kaon radii compressed by approximately 15% with respect to pion radii
- Constituent quark masses: M_u = 0.31 GeV, M_s = 1.2 M_u
- Kaon valence quark momentum fractions at hadronic scale: <x>_K^u = 0.47, <x>_K^s = 0.53
- Mean-squared transverse extent for K+: <b_perp^2>_K+^u = 0.69 r_K+^2, <b_perp^2>_K+^s = 0.51 r_K+^2
- Pressure changes sign at r_c = 0.45 fm for the pion

## Context
This work extends continuum Schwinger method analyses of meson structure (Chang 2013, Shi 2014, Ding 2019, Cui 2020) by constructing LFWFs and GPDs from previously computed 1-dimensional distributions. It provides predictions for upcoming experiments with meson targets (Arrington 2021, Aguilar 2019, Anderle 2021, Adams 2018). The PTIR approach enables algebraic relations between form factors and parton distributions, including a factorized LFWF model in the chiral limit that connects PDFs and PDAs via u^P(x;zeta_H) = [phi_P^u(x;zeta_H)]^2 / integral dx [phi_P^u(x;zeta_H)]^2. The work can be extended to transverse momentum dependent distributions and other hadrons.
