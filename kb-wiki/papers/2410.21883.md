# On the scalar $πK$ form factor beyond the elastic region

> Pion-kaon ($πK$) final states, often appearing in heavy-particle decays at the precision frontier, are important for Standard-Model tests, to describe crossed channels with exotic states, and for spectroscopy of excited kaon resonances. We construct a representation of the $πK$ $S$-wave form factor using the elastic $πK$ scattering phase shift via dispersion relations in the elastic region and extend this model into the inelastic region using resonance exchange, while maintaining unitarity and the correct analytic structure. As a first application, we successfully described the $τ\to K_S πν_τ$ spectrum to not only achieve a better distinction between $S$- and $P$-wave contributions, but also to provide an improved estimate of the $CP$ asymmetry produced by a tensor operator as well as the forward-backward asymmetry, both of which can be confronted with future data at Belle II.

## Key claim

The authors construct a representation of the πK S-wave form factor that extends beyond the elastic region by combining dispersion relations with resonance exchange, maintaining unitarity and correct analytic structure; the representation is applied to τ → K_S π ν_τ decays, but the fit scenarios become visibly indistinguishable in the total decay rate, with differences appearing mainly above the η′K threshold where phase space suppression and large data uncertainties limit discrimination.

## Method

The formalism builds upon the Bethe-Salpeter equation in a two-channel setup (πK and η′K), splitting the scattering potential into an elastic part V₀ (parameterized via the empirical πK scattering phase shift δ₀) and a resonance-exchange part V_R. The elastic T-matrix T₀ is constructed from δ₀ using an Omnès function, while the full T-matrix and scalar form factor are obtained by solving the coupled-channel equations with V_R parameterized as a sum over scalar resonances K₀*(1430) and K₀*(1950). The resulting scalar form factor is then fitted to the τ → K_S π ν_τ spectrum from Belle, with the vector form factor taken from resonance chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- The fit to πK scattering data covers the range from threshold up to 2.5 GeV, with the elastic phase guided smoothly to π at √s_m = 1.52 GeV.
- The branching ratio for τ → K_S π ν_τ is BR = 4.35(6)_st(3)_norm(7)_sys × 10⁻³ = 4.35(10) × 10⁻³, which is 2σ above the original Belle result 4.04(13) × 10⁻³ but agrees at 1.5σ with the more recent value 4.16(8) × 10⁻³.
- The BSM CP asymmetry produced by a tensor operator is A_CP^{τ,BSM} = -0.034(14) Im c_T.
- An upper bound on the branching ratio for τ → K₀*(1430) ν_τ is BR < 1.6 × 10⁻⁴ at 95% confidence level, a factor of 3 better than current literature values.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Hanhart (2012) and Ropertz (2018) for constructing πK form factors with unitarity and analytic structure, and applies it to the τ → K_S π ν_τ spectrum measured by Belle (Epifanov 2007). The approach improves upon simple Breit-Wigner parameterizations used by Belle, which violate unitarity and produce inconsistent phases at low energies, and provides a more flexible alternative to the coupled-channel Omnès matrix approach of El Bennich (2009), which requires unavailable scattering phase inputs for all channels. The results enable future analyses at Belle II to better distinguish S- and P-wave contributions, refine CP asymmetry measurements, and control πK final-state interactions in semi-leptonic D- and B-meson decays.
