# Transport coefficients from the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for $SU(3)_f$

> We calculate the shear $η(T)$ and bulk viscosities $ζ(T)$ as well as the electric conductivity $σ_e(T)$ and heat conductivity $κ(T)$ within the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for 3 flavors as a function of temperature as well as the entropy density $s(T)$, pressure $P(T)$ and speed of sound squared $c_s^2(T)$. We compare the results with other models such as the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) and confront these results with lattice QCD data whenever available. We find the NJL model to have a limited predictive power for the thermodynamic variables and various transport coefficients above the critical temperature whereas the PNJL model and DQPM show acceptable results for the quantities of interest.

## Key claim

The Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model for three flavors has limited predictive power for thermodynamic variables and transport coefficients above the critical temperature, whereas the Polyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) show acceptable results when compared to lattice QCD data.

## Method

The authors computed the shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, electric conductivity, and heat conductivity within the NJL model using the relaxation time approximation (RTA) for thermal distribution functions of partons. They calculated the equation of state (pressure, energy density, entropy density, speed of sound) from the stress-energy tensor for non-interacting particles at zero quark chemical potential. Results were compared with the PNJL model, DQPM, and available lattice QCD data from Borsanyi (2010), Cheng (2010), Meyer (2007, 2008), Nakamura (2005), Sakai (2007), Ding (2013), Aarts (2007), Gupta (2003), and Brandt (2013).

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity over entropy density ratio η/s in the NJL model shows a temperature dependence ∝ T⁻¹ for high temperatures, with order of magnitude agreement with lattice QCD data from 1.2 T_c up to 1.5 T_c
- The T⁻¹ behavior of η/s in the NJL model implies going below the Kovtun-Son-Starinets (KSS) bound of (η/s)_KSS = 1/(4π) above T ~ 1.7 T_c
- Bulk viscosity over entropy density ζ/s from the NJL model approaches zero at high temperatures, with no peak around T_c unlike lattice QCD and DQPM results
- Electric conductivity over temperature σ_e/T is approximately linear in T for T ≥ T_c up to about 2 T_c in both the NJL model and DQPM
- Heat conductivity κ/T² in the NJL model shows a rapid decrease with T for T > T_c, while DQPM shows a slightly rising ratio for T_c < T < 2 T_c

## Context

This work extends the calculation of transport coefficients from the NJL model for three flavors, building on prior work by Marty (2013) and comparing with the PNJL model of Fukushima (2003) and Ratti (2007) and the DQPM of Peshier (2004) and Cassing (2009). The results enable improved modeling of the partonic plasma expansion in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, though the NJL model's limitations above T_c suggest that PNJL or DQPM are more suitable for such applications.
