# Bulk Viscosity at Extreme Limits: From Kinetic Theory to Strings

> In this paper we study bulk viscosity in a thermal QCD model with large number of colors at two extreme limits: the very weak and the very strong 't Hooft couplings. The weak coupling scenario is based on kinetic theory, and one may go to the very strong coupling dynamics via an intermediate coupling regime. Although the former has a clear description in terms of kinetic theory, the intermediate coupling regime, which uses lattice results, suffers from usual technical challenges that render an explicit determination of bulk viscosity somewhat difficult. On the other hand, the very strong 't Hooft coupling dynamics may be studied using string theories at both weak and strong string couplings using gravity duals in type IIB as well as M-theory respectively. In type IIB we provide the precise fluctuation modes of the metric in the gravity dual responsible for bulk viscosity, compute the speed of sound in the medium and analyze the ratio of the bulk to shear viscosities. In M-theory, where we uplift the type IIA mirror dual of the UV complete type IIB model, we study and compare both the bulk viscosity and the sound speed by analyzing the quasi-normal modes in the system at strong IIA string coupling. By deriving the spectral function, we show the consistency of our results both for the actual values of the parameters involved as well for the bound on the ratio of bulk to shear viscosities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the ratio of bulk to shear viscosities in a thermal QCD model with large number of colors scales linearly with the deviation of the squared speed of sound from its conformal value at both weak and strong 't Hooft couplings, but the precise coefficient at strong string coupling is almost 9 times larger than the Buchel bound.

## Method

The authors study bulk viscosity in SU(M) gauge theory at three regimes of 't Hooft coupling: weak coupling using kinetic theory and diagrammatic analysis of the Boltzmann equation; intermediate coupling using lattice QCD results; and strong coupling using gauge/gravity duality. For the strong coupling regime, they employ type IIB string theory at weak string coupling and M-theory uplift of the type IIA mirror dual at strong string coupling, analyzing metric fluctuation modes, quasi-normal modes, and spectral functions to compute transport coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio bound from AdS/CFT is eta/s = 1/(4 pi) at strong 't Hooft coupling
- At weak coupling, bulk viscosity scales as zeta/eta proportional to (1/3 - c_s^2)^2 from kinetic theory
- At strong coupling, the ratio of bulk to shear viscosities satisfies zeta/eta greater than or equal to 2(1/3 - c_s^2) (Buchel bound)
- At strong string coupling, the lower bound on zeta/eta is almost 9 times larger than the Buchel bound

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory calculations of bulk viscosity by Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2006) to the 't Hooft coupling framework and contrasts them with strong coupling results from holography. The paper builds on the Klebanov-Strassler gravity dual and its UV completion, as well as the MQGP limit of thermal QCD proposed by the same group. The results enable direct comparison of bulk viscosity behavior across coupling regimes and provide analytic control over transport coefficients for both pure glue and flavored large-M thermal QCD.
