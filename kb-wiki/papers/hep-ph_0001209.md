# Anomalous f_1 exchange in vector meson photoproduction asymmetries

> We perform an analysis of the elastic production of vector mesons with polarized photon beams at high energy in order to investigate the validity of a recently proposed dynamical mechanism based on the dominance of the f_1 trajectory at large momentum transfer. The density matrix characterizing the angular distributions of the vector meson decays is calculated within an exchange model which includes the Pomeron and the f_1. The asymmetries of these decays turn out to be very useful to disentangle the role of these exchanges since their effect depends crucially on their quantum numbers which are different. The observables analyzed are accessible with present experimental facilities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the parity asymmetry \(P_\sigma\) and the photon-beam asymmetry \(\Sigma\) in elastic vector meson photoproduction can cleanly distinguish between the standard soft Pomeron exchange and a proposed anomalous \(f_1\) Regge trajectory, provided measurements extend to \(|t| \gtrsim 1\) GeV\(^2\). The claim is conditional: the \(f_1\) exchange dominates only at large momentum transfer (\(|t| \ge 1\) GeV\(^2\)), while the soft Pomeron governs the small-\(|t|\) region.

## Method

The authors calculate the density matrix for vector meson decay angular distributions in the Gottfried-Jackson frame, using an exchange model that includes both the soft Pomeron and the anomalous \(f_1\) trajectory. The Pomeron amplitude follows the Donnachie–Landshoff formalism with trajectory \(\alpha_P(t) = 1.08 + 0.25\,t\) (GeV\(^{-2}\)), while the \(f_1\) amplitude uses coupling constants extracted from \(f_1\) decay and form factors with cutoff masses \(\Lambda_\rho = 1.5\) GeV and \(\Lambda_\phi = 1.8\) GeV. The parity asymmetry \(P_\sigma\) and the linear-polarization asymmetry \(\Sigma\) are computed from the resulting density matrix elements for \(\rho\) and \(\phi\) photoproduction at a center-of-mass energy \(W = 94\) GeV.

## Key numbers

* The soft Pomeron trajectory is \(\alpha_P(t) = 1.08 + 0.25\,t\) with slope \(\alpha_P' = 0.25\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* The \(f_1\) trajectory has intercept \(\alpha_{f_1}(0) \approx 1\) and slope \(\alpha'_{f_1} \approx 0\).
* The \(f_1 \rho^0 \gamma\) coupling constant is \(g_{f_1 \rho^0 \gamma} = 0.94\) GeV\(^{-2}\); the \(f_1 \phi \gamma\) coupling is \(g_{f_1 \phi \gamma} = 0.18\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
* The \(f_1\) mass is \(m_{f_1} = 1.285\) GeV; form-factor cutoff masses are \(\Lambda_\rho = 1.5\) GeV and \(\Lambda_\phi = 1.8\) GeV.
* In \(\rho\) photoproduction, the Pomeron and \(f_1\) exchanges become comparable at \(|t| \approx 1\) GeV\(^2\), where \(P_\sigma\) vanishes; for \(|t| > 2\) GeV\(^2\), \(P_\sigma\) approaches \(-1\) (pure \(f_1\) dominance).
* The soft Pomeron alone yields \(P_\sigma = +1\) at all \(t\); the \(f_1\) alone yields \(P_\sigma = -1\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Kochelev et al., 1999) of an anomalous \(f_1\) Regge trajectory with unnatural parity to explain large-\(|t|\) vector meson photoproduction data from the ZEUS collaboration. It contrasts with the alternative explanation by Donnachie and Landshoff (1998, 1999), who introduced a hard Pomeron with trajectory \(\alpha_{P'} = 1.44 + 0.1\,t\) to describe data up to \(|t| \sim 2\) GeV\(^2\). The predicted asymmetries for the \(f_1\) model are drastically different from those of the hard Pomeron model, so measurements at facilities such as CERN, DESY, and Fermilab could discriminate between the two mechanisms and test the proposed connection to the QCD axial anomaly.
