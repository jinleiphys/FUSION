# Renormalization in conformal quantum mechanics

> The singular behavior of conformal interactions is examined within a comparative analysis of renormalization frameworks. The effective approach--inspired by the effective-field theory program--and its connection with the core framework are highlighted. Applications include black-hole thermodynamics, molecular dipole-bound anions, the Efimov effect, and various regimes of QED.

# Renormalization in conformal quantum mechanics

## Key claim
The paper argues that singular conformal interactions in quantum mechanics require renormalization due to additional ultraviolet physics, and that an effective-field-theory-inspired approach yields universal predictions for bound-state spectra that are independent of the details of the short-distance physics, provided the conformal coupling is fixed by the long-distance behavior.

## Method
The authors develop a real-space regularization procedure where a singular inverse-square potential \( V(r) = -g/r^2 \) is replaced inside a short-distance cutoff radius \( a \) by a regularizing core potential parametrized by a dimensionless depth \( \aleph \). They then match logarithmic derivatives of the radial wave functions at the boundary \( r=a \) and analyze the resulting bound-state condition in the limit \( \kappa a \ll 1 \), where \( \kappa \) is the bound-state momentum. Three renormalization frameworks are compared: an effective framework where the conformal coupling is fixed, an intrinsic framework where the conformal coupling runs with the cutoff, and a core framework where the core strength runs while the conformal coupling remains fixed.

## Key numbers
- Critical coupling for the inverse-square potential: \( \lambda^{(*)} = (l + \nu)^2 \), where \( \nu = d/2 - 1 \) and \( l \) is the angular momentum quantum number.
- Bound-state energy spectrum in the effective framework: \( E_n = E_0 \exp(-2\pi n / \Theta) \), with geometric ratio \( \eta = \exp(-2\pi / \Theta) \), where \( \Theta = \sqrt{\lambda - (l+\nu)^2} \) is the conformal parameter.
- Number of conformal bound states: \( N_{\text{conf}} \sim (\Theta / \pi) \ln(L_{\text{IR}} / L_{\text{UV}}) \).
- For molecular dipole-bound anions: critical dipole moment \( \lambda^{(*)}_{\text{conf}} \approx 1.279 \) (corresponding to 1.625 D in debye units).
- For QED\(_3\) with \( N_f \) flavors: critical fermion number \( N^{(*)} = 128/(3\pi^2) \approx 4.323 \).
- For quenched QED\(_4\): critical fine-structure constant \( \alpha^{(*)} = \pi/3 \).
- For the Efimov effect with identical bosons: conformal parameter \( \Theta \approx 1.006 \).
- Hawking temperature from conformal parameter: \( T_H = f'_+ / (4\pi) = \omega / (4\pi \Theta) \).

## Context
This work extends the renormalization interpretation of singular quantum mechanics, first discussed for the two-dimensional contact interaction by Jackiw (1991) and others, to the long-range inverse-square potential. It provides a unified real-space regularization that connects to prior treatments of the inverse-square potential by Gupta (1993) and Camblong (2000), and to the limit-cycle renormalization group for the three-body problem studied by Beane (2000) and Bawin (2003). The effective framework enables systematic analysis of physical realizations including black-hole thermodynamics (following 't Hooft 1985), molecular dipole-bound anions (with critical dipole moment from the conformal approach), the Efimov effect in three-body systems, and chiral symmetry breaking in QED\(_3\) and QED\(_4\) (following Gusynin 1995 and Nash 1990).
