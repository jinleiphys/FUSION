# Event generator to construct cross sections for the multiphonon excitation of a set of collective vibrational modes

> The construction of differential cross sections as a function of excitation energy for systems with a collection of low- and high-lying intrinsic vibrational modes has been attempted in the past. A prescription is proposed that simplifies the implementation of such calculation schemes with a remarkable reduction in computational time.

# Event generator to construct cross sections for the multiphonon excitation of a set of collective vibrational modes

## Key claim

The authors propose an event-generator method that constructs differential cross sections as a function of excitation energy for systems with multiple low-lying and high-lying collective vibrational modes, achieving a reduction in computational time of about two orders of magnitude compared to full coupled-channel calculations. The method relies on the approximation that different vibrational modes are independent from each other and that excitation probabilities are small for most impact parameters, though it is explicitly stated that the approach fails for very central impact parameters and cannot handle anharmonicities.

## Method

The method uses a semiclassical formalism where, for each impact parameter, the average number of phonons for each independent collective mode is pre-calculated using the formalism of Dasso et al. (2005). An event generator then samples a large number of "events" per impact parameter, assigning a Poisson-distributed number of phonons to each mode and accumulating weighted contributions into a histogram of excitation energy. The energy spread for each mode is assigned according to a width prescription: low-lying modes have zero width at the one-phonon level, a finite spread at the two-phonon level, and a width equal to the phonon energy at the three-phonon level; high-lying modes (giant resonances) have a spreading width that scales as the square root of the phonon number. The final differential cross section in mb/MeV is obtained by multiplying the accumulated histogram by factors involving the impact parameter mesh, energy mesh, and number of events.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 40Ca + 208Pb at 50 MeV per nucleon, the event generator yields a total excitation cross section for the low-lying 3- state (E=4.9 MeV) of 1.10 mb, compared to 1.05 mb from the full method of Lanza et al. (2009).
- For the giant quadrupole resonance (GQR, E=16.9 MeV) in the same system, the event generator gives 58.7 mb versus 58.6 mb from the full method.
- For the double-phonon state 3- x 3-, the event generator gives 0.11 x 10^{-3} mb versus 0.10 x 10^{-3} mb from the full method.
- For the double-phonon state GQR x GQR, the event generator gives 0.14 x 10^{-1} mb versus 0.14 x 10^{-3} mb from the full method (note the discrepancy of two orders of magnitude for this state).
- The example application for 38Ar + 208Pb at 40 MeV per nucleon uses impact parameters from 12 fm to 100 fm in steps of 0.5 fm, with 10^8 events per impact parameter, requiring only a few minutes of CPU time.
- The computational time saving is about two orders of magnitude compared to the method of Lanza et al. (2009).

## Context

This work extends the single-mode formalism of Dasso et al. (2005) to handle multiple independent collective vibrational modes simultaneously, addressing the practical difficulty that full coupled-channel calculations for heavy-ion reactions at modern beam energies require a very large number of impact parameters due to the long range of Coulomb coupling. The method is intended to complement the more sophisticated Hartree-Fock plus Random Phase Approximation based approach of Lanza et al. (2009), which is accurate but time-consuming; the authors suggest using the full method for the inner (grazing) region where nuclear interactions dominate and the event generator for the peripheral region where most impact parameters lie. The approach does not apply when anharmonicities need to be included, and it is explicitly limited to the regime where excitation probabilities follow a Poisson distribution and modes are independent.
