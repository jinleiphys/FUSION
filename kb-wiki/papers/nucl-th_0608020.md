# The Complex Gap in Color Superconductivity

> We solve the gap equation for color-superconducting quark matter in the 2SC phase, including both the energy and the momentum dependence of the gap, ϕ=ϕ(k_0,\vk). For that purpose a complex Ansatz for ϕis made. The calculations are performed within an effective theory for cold and dense quark matter. The solution of the complex gap equation is valid to subleading order in the strong coupling constant g and in the limit of zero temperature. We find that, for momenta sufficiently close to the Fermi surface and for small energies, the dominant contribution to the imaginary part of $ϕ$ arises from Landau-damped magnetic gluons. Further away from the Fermi surface and for larger energies the other gluon sectors have to be included into Imϕ. We confirm that Im$ ϕ$ contributes a correction of order g to the prefactor of ϕfor on-shell quasiquarks sufficiently close to the Fermi surface, whereas further away from the Fermi surface Imϕand Reϕare of the same order. Finally, we discuss the relevance of Imϕfor the damping of quasiquark excitations.

## Key claim

The paper solves the complex gap equation for the 2SC phase of color-superconducting quark matter including both energy and momentum dependence of the gap, and shows that for quarks exponentially close to the Fermi surface (within a distance of order \(\mu \exp(-c/g)\)), the imaginary part of the gap contributes to the real part only at sub-subleading order (beyond order \(g\)), thereby justifying previous calculations that neglected Im\(\phi\) at subleading order.

## Method

The gap equation was set up within an effective theory for cold, dense quark matter at zero temperature, using a complex Ansatz for the gap \(\phi = \phi(k_0, \mathbf{k})\) with energy and momentum treated as independent variables. The Matsubara sum in the gap equation was rewritten as a contour integral, deformed to introduce spectral densities of the gluon propagator and the gap function, and then decomposed into real and imaginary parts. The coupled equations for Re\(\phi\) and Im\(\phi\) were solved to subleading order in the strong coupling constant \(g\) by estimating contributions from Landau-damped magnetic gluons, electric gluons, and gluon poles in different energy-momentum regimes.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order gap on the Fermi surface scales as \(\phi \sim \mu \exp\left(-\frac{\pi}{2 \bar{g}}\right)\) where \(\bar{g} \equiv g/(3\sqrt{2}\pi)\).
* For momenta \(|k-\mu| \sim \Lambda_y\) with \(\Lambda_y \equiv \phi^y M^{1-y}\) and \(M \sim g\mu\), the scale \(\Lambda_1 = \phi\) and \(\Lambda_0 = M\); quarks with \(|k-\mu| \sim \Lambda_{1 \leq y < \bar{g}}\) are "exponentially close" to the Fermi surface.
* For on-shell quasiquarks sufficiently close to the Fermi surface, Im\(\phi\) contributes a correction of order \(g\) to the prefactor of \(\phi\); further away at \(|k-\mu| \sim g\mu\), Im\(\phi\) and Re\(\phi\) are of the same order.
* For energies just above the gluon mass \(m_g \sim g\mu\), Im\(\phi \sim g^2 \mu \gg\) Re\(\phi\) due to emission of on-shell electric gluons.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the color-superconducting gap in the 2SC phase by including the full energy and momentum dependence of the gap function, going beyond the approximations used in earlier studies (Son 1999, Schafer and Wilczek 1999, Pisarski and Rischke 1999) where the off-shell gap was assumed equal to the on-shell value or where the Eliashberg approximation (energy-only dependence) was employed. The analysis confirms the estimate of Pisarski and Rischke (1999) that Im\(\phi\) is of order \(g\) times Re\(\phi\) near the Fermi surface, and shows that the imaginary part does not feed back into the real part at subleading order for quarks exponentially close to the Fermi surface. The results enable systematic inclusion of subleading corrections to the gap and provide a framework for understanding damping of quasiquark excitations in color superconductors, with implications for neutron star cores where the coupling may be stronger.
