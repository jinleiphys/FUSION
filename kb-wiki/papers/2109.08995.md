# Spectral splits and entanglement entropy in collective neutrino oscillations

> In environments such as core-collapse supernovae, neutron star mergers, or the early universe, where the neutrino fluxes can be extremely high, neutrino-neutrino interactions are appreciable and contribute substantially to their flavor evolution. Such a system of interacting neutrinos can be regarded as a quantum many-body system, and prospects for nontrivial quantum correlations, i.e., entanglement, developing in a gas of interacting neutrinos have been investigated previously. In this work, we uncover an intriguing connection between the entropy of entanglement of individual neutrinos with the rest of the ensemble, and the occurrence of spectral splits in the energy spectra of these neutrinos, which develop as a result of collective neutrino oscillations. In particular, for various types of neutrino spectra, we demonstrate that the entanglement entropy is highest for the neutrinos whose locations in the energy spectrum are closest to the spectral split(s). This trend demonstrates that the quantum entanglement is strongest among the neutrinos that are close to these splits, a behavior that seems to persist even as the size of the many-body system is increased.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates a connection between the entanglement entropy of individual neutrinos and the occurrence of spectral splits in collective neutrino oscillations, showing that entanglement entropy is highest for neutrinos whose energies are closest to the spectral split frequencies, a trend that persists as the many-body system size increases.

## Method

The authors numerically integrated the time-dependent many-body Schrodinger equation for interacting neutrino ensembles using a fourth-order Runge-Kutta method with sparse-matrix representation, for total neutrino numbers from N=2 to N=16. They used the single-angle approximation with a bulb-model motivated form for the neutrino-neutrino interaction strength, and computed the reduced density matrix and entanglement entropy for each neutrino mode by tracing over all other neutrinos in the ensemble.

## Key numbers

- For a system of N neutrinos with evenly spaced oscillation frequencies all initially in the electron flavor state, the spectral split frequency is given by ω_s = ω_0 * N * cos^2(θ), where ω_0 is the reference frequency and θ is the mixing angle
- Calculations were performed with mixing angles θ = 0.161 (approximately equal to θ_13) and θ = 0.584 (approximately equal to θ_12)
- The neutrinosphere radius was chosen as R_ν = 32.2 ω_0^{-1}, with μ(R_ν) = 3.62 × 10^4 ω_0, and the starting radius was r_0 = 210.64 ω_0^{-1} giving μ(r_0) = 5 ω_0
- Time steps were chosen as 0.1 * [μ * (N/2)(N/2 + 1) + Σ_ω |ω|]^{-1}, dynamically adjusted as μ changed
- For N ≤ 9, the Runge-Kutta method was verified against Bethe-ansatz/homotopy-continuation results, with wave function coefficient discrepancies at a level of ≤ 10^{-6}

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies using Richardson-Gaudin (Bethe-Ansatz) techniques for exact eigenvalues and eigenstates of the many-body neutrino Hamiltonian (Patwardhan 2019, Cervia 2019), where they observed entanglement entropy reaching nearly maximal values of log(2) with small numbers of neutrinos. The paper connects these many-body entanglement effects to the phenomenon of spectral splits previously studied in the mean-field limit by Duan (2006), Raffelt (2007), and others. This enables future investigations into whether beyond-mean-field effects have significant implications for neutrino flavor evolution in core-collapse supernovae and neutron-star mergers, and may inform the validity of mean-field approximations for the large neutrino numbers present in these environments.
