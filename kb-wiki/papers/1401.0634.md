# Photocouplings at the Pole from Pion Photoproduction

> The reactions $γp\toπ^0 p$ and $γp\toπ^+ n$ are analyzed in a semi-phenomenological approach up to $E\sim2.3$ GeV. Fits to differential cross section and single and double polarization observables are performed. A good overall reproduction of the available photoproduction data is achieved. The Jülich2012 dynamical coupled-channel model -which describes elastic $πN$ scattering and the world data base of the reactions $πN\toηN$, $KΛ$, and $KΣ$ at the same time - is employed as the hadronic interaction in the final state. The framework guarantees analyticity and, thus, allows for a reliable extraction of resonance parameters in terms of poles and residues. In particular, the photocouplings at the pole can be extracted and are presented.

## Key claim

The paper extracts photocouplings at the pole for nucleon resonances from pion photoproduction data using a semi-phenomenological approach that preserves the analytic structure of the Juelich2012 dynamical coupled-channel model, but the authors note that the polynomial parameterization of the photo-excitation vertices cannot be used for sub-threshold extrapolations and that the approach is an intermediate step toward a more microscopic description.

## Method

The authors fit differential cross sections and single and double polarization observables for the reactions gamma p -> pi0 p and gamma p -> pi+ n up to E ~ 2.3 GeV using a semi-phenomenological approach. The hadronic final-state interaction is described by the Juelich2012 dynamical coupled-channel model, which solves a Lippmann-Schwinger-type equation in time-ordered perturbation theory and describes elastic pi N scattering and the reactions pi N -> eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma. The photo-excitation vertices are parameterized as polynomials at the multipole-amplitude level, with the photon coupling to pi N, eta N, and pi Delta channels, and the parameters are fitted to data using MINUIT on the JUROPA supercomputer.

## Key numbers

- The analysis covers data up to E = 2330 MeV for gamma p -> pi0 p and up to E = 2260 MeV for gamma p -> pi+ n
- Fit 1 uses 21,627 data points with chi^2 = 20,095 and chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.95
- Fit 2 uses 23,518 data points with chi^2 = 22,880 and chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.99
- The expansion point for the polynomial parameterization is E_s = 1077 MeV, close to the pi N threshold
- Polynomial orders are chosen as l_i, l_mu <= 3 in the parameterization
- Data with forward angles theta < 40 degrees for pi0 p at E > 2050 MeV and theta < 9 degrees for pi+ n at E > 1600 MeV are excluded due to the truncation of partial waves with J >= 11/2

## Context

This work extends the Juelich2012 dynamical coupled-channel model (Ronchen 2012) to pion photoproduction, building on earlier work by Huang (2011) that used a gauge-invariant framework. The approach is inspired by the GWU/SAID CM12 parameterization (Workman 2012) and provides a flexible tool to analyze the large and comprehensive set of pion photoproduction data from facilities like ELSA, GRAAL, JLab, MAMI, and SPring-8. The results enable future simultaneous fits to pion- and photon-induced reactions and planned extensions to eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma photoproduction, as well as electroproduction processes.
