# Quantum Monte Carlo with Coupled-Cluster wave functions

> We introduce a novel many body method which combines two powerful many body techniques, viz., quantum Monte Carlo and coupled cluster theory. Coupled cluster wave functions are introduced as importance functions in a Monte Carlo method designed for the configuration interaction framework to provide rigorous upper bounds to the ground state energy. We benchmark our method on the homogeneous electron gas in momentum space. The importance function used is the coupled cluster doubles wave function. We show that the computational resources required in our method scale polynomially with system size. Our energy upper bounds are in very good agreement with previous calculations of similar accuracy, and they can be systematically improved by including higher order excitations in the coupled cluster wave function.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a hybrid many-body method that combines quantum Monte Carlo with coupled-cluster (CC) wave functions to provide rigorous upper bounds to the ground state energy, where the CC wave functions serve as importance functions to circumvent the sign problem in a configuration-interaction Monte Carlo (CIMC) framework. The key qualifier is that the method yields strict upper bounds whose tightness can be systematically improved by including higher-order excitations in the CC wave function, and the computational resources scale polynomially with system size.

## Method

The authors implement a projection Monte Carlo algorithm in Fock space (configuration interaction Monte Carlo, CIMC) where the ground state wave function is filtered by repeated stochastic application of a projection operator on an ensemble of Slater determinants. A coupled-cluster doubles (CCD) wave function is used as the importance function to guide the random walk via a sign-problem-free propagator derived from a family of modified Hamiltonians parameterized by a positive parameter gamma. The method is benchmarked on the three-dimensional homogeneous electron gas (3DEG) in momentum space, using both full CCD amplitudes and amplitudes obtained from second-order Moller-Plesset perturbation theory (CCD(1)).

## Context

This work extends the configuration interaction Monte Carlo method introduced by Mukherjee (2013) by incorporating coupled-cluster wave functions as importance functions, addressing the lack of accurate and computationally efficient trial wave functions for Fock-space QMC algorithms. The method provides an alternative to the initiator full configuration interaction quantum Monte Carlo (i-FCIQMC) approach of Booth (2009) and Cleland (2010), with the advantage of providing rigorous upper bounds and polynomial scaling rather than exponential scaling with system size. The results enable systematic improvement toward exact ground state energies for fermionic systems in momentum space, particularly relevant for nuclear physics where non-local interactions are common and for electronic structure calculations with non-local pseudopotentials.
