# Effective field theory contribution to charge symmetry breaking NN scattering

> The effect of isospin breaking pion s-wave rescattering is included in elastic NN scattering at low energies using effective field theory. Although this mechanism gave a large contribution to charge symmetry breaking in np --> d pi0, the effect is rather small in pp vs. nn scattering parameters and in the 3H-3He binding energy difference. This smallness is caused by large cancellation of the up-down quark mass difference contribution and electromagnetic effects to the np mass difference.

# Effective field theory contribution to charge symmetry breaking NN scattering

## Key claim

The isospin-breaking pion s-wave rescattering mechanism, which gave a large contribution to charge symmetry breaking in np → dπ0, produces only a small effect in pp vs. nn scattering parameters and in the 3H-3He binding energy difference, because the up-down quark mass difference contribution and electromagnetic effects to the np mass difference largely cancel in this class III interaction.

## Method

The author computes the momentum-space potentials from the isospin-violating effective Lagrangian for both nucleonic (Fig. 1b) and Δ(1232) intermediate state (Fig. 1c) diagrams, then transforms them to coordinate space using Yukawa fits. These CSB potentials are added to the charge-symmetric Reid soft core potential, with explicit NΔ intermediate states included via coupled channels, and the resulting 1S0 scattering length and effective range differences are calculated. The contribution to the 3H-3He binding energy difference is estimated using the Gibson-Stephenson prescription relating binding energy to scattering parameters.

## Key numbers

- Δa = app - ann = 0.55 fm for the Reid SC model with both nucleonic and Δ contributions (Figs. 1b,c), compared to the experimental value of 1.5 ± 0.5 fm
- Δr0 = r0,pp - r0,nn = 0.012 fm for the same model, compared to the experimental value of 0.10 ± 0.12 fm
- ΔE = 41 keV contribution to the 3H-3He binding energy difference from the Reid SC model with both diagrams, compared to the empirical value of 76 ± 24 keV after removing Coulomb and np mass difference effects
- With the electromagnetic correction δmN = -0.76 ± 0.30 MeV from the Cottingham formula, the final results are reduced to approximately one quarter of the values in Table I (scaling factor about 0.25)
- The pion decay constant used is Fπ = 186 MeV, the πN coupling constant is f2/4π = 0.076, and the πNΔ coupling constant is f*2/4π = 0.35

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory treatment of charge symmetry breaking from the reaction np → dπ0 (Niskanen, 1999) to elastic NN scattering, using the same isospin-violating Lagrangian from van Kolck (1994) and Weinberg. It contrasts with earlier CSB studies by Coon and collaborators (1977, 1982) on two-meson exchange and by Henley and Miller (1979) on class III vs. class IV classification. The results show that the large CSB effect seen in pion production can coexist with the well-understood pp vs. nn differences, because the cancellation between quark mass and electromagnetic contributions is specific to the class III interaction.
