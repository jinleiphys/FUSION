# Impact of new results from the ultraperipheral collision on modeling the proton and neutron emission in photon-induced nuclear processes

> The ultrarelativistic collisions of heavy ions provide rich spectrum of possibilities to discuss the response of the nucleus to photons. Newly published neutron and proton multiplicities measured in the ALICE experiment in ultraperipheral collisions allow investigating the influence of the electromagnetic fields on colliding nuclei for the $^{208}$Pb+$^{208}$Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=5.02~TeV. The theoretical predictions are done within hybrid model including equivalent photon approximations (EPA), GiBUU modeling of pre-equilibrium processes and generation of the exited nuclear remnants, which decay is modeled by statistical approach: GEM2 or GEMINI++. The cross-sections of the mass-charge distributions of nuclear remnants as well as the neutron, proton and other charged particle multiplicities are estimated. We concentrate on production of protons and isotopes coming from the electromagnetic dissociation. Special attention is devoted to emission of a single proton. The cross section for $1p$ emission is very close to maximal available one based on reactions of photon with individual nucleons. Our pre-equilibrium processes explain simultaneously the tail of neutron energy distributions in the nuclear rest frame observed in $γ+ A$ collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a hybrid model combining the equivalent photon approximation (EPA), the GiBUU transport code for pre-equilibrium processes, and statistical de-excitation models (GEM2 or GEMINI++) can simultaneously describe both neutron and proton multiplicities measured by ALICE in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the agreement is conditional on the assumption that the cross section for single-proton emission is very close to the maximal value allowed by photon interactions with individual nucleons inside the nucleus.

## Method

The authors model ultraperipheral collisions of 208Pb+208Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV using a three-stage hybrid approach: (1) the photon flux from the electromagnetic fields is calculated with the equivalent photon approximation (EPA); (2) the photon-nucleus interaction is treated with the GiBUU transport model for photon energies above 200 MeV, and with the phenomenological Two-Component Model (TCM) or HIPSE for energies between 20-200 MeV; (3) the de-excitation of the hot nuclear remnants is simulated using either the GEM2 or GEMINI++ statistical codes. The GiBUU model provides the pre-equilibrium emission of nucleons and pions, yielding explicit distributions of excitation energies for the residual nuclei, which are then fed into the statistical de-excitation models.

## Key numbers

- The collision system is 208Pb+208Pb at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV, as measured by the ALICE experiment.
- The photon energy range considered extends from 200 MeV to almost 1 TeV for the GiBUU component, with the low-energy component below 200 MeV treated separately.
- For excitation energy E_exc=50 MeV (corresponding to average photon energy <E_gamma>=70 MeV), the average secondary neutron multiplicity <M_n^sec.>=5 and average secondary proton multiplicity <M_p^sec.>=0.
- For E_exc=150 MeV (<E_gamma>=200 MeV), <M_n^sec.>=13 and <M_p^sec.>=0.14.
- For E_exc=500 MeV (<E_gamma>=2000 MeV), <M_n^sec.>=25 and <M_p^sec.>=12.
- The E_0 parameter in the Two-Component Model was set to 50 MeV.
- The estimated uncertainty of GiBUU simulation results due to variation in model parameters is 20%.
- The intra-nuclear impact parameter for UPC is b > 14 fm (R_A1 + R_A2).

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Jucha et al. (2025) that successfully described ALICE neutron multiplicity data using a phenomenological Two-Component Model combined with GEMINI++, but which disregarded the details of the intranuclear cascade and pre-equilibrium emission. The new ALICE proton data (2025) provide additional constraints that require a microscopic description of the photon-nucleus collision, which the authors implement using the GiBUU transport model. The results enable improved modeling of electromagnetic dissociation in ultraperipheral collisions at LHC energies and can be compared with the RELDIS code (PhysRevC.60.044901) and experimental photoabsorption data from Mainz.
