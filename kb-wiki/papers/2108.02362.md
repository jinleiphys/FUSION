# Collision energy dependence of the critical end point from baryon number fluctuations in the Linear Sigma Model with quarks

> We show that the Linear Sigma Model with quarks produces an effective description of the QCD phase diagram and of the system's equilibrium distribution properties that deviate from those of the Hadron Resonance Gas Model. The deviation is due to the inclusion of plasma screening properties, encoded in the contribution of the ring diagrams and thus to the introduction of a key feature of plasmas near phase transitions, namely, long-range correlations. After fixing the model parameters using input from LQCD for the crossover transition at vanishing chemical potential, we study the location of the Critical End Point in the effective QCD phase diagram. We use the model to study baryon number fluctuations and show that in heavy-ion collisions, the CEP can be located for collision energies $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\sim 2$ GeV, namely, in the lowest NICA or within the HADES energy domain.

## Key claim

The Linear Sigma Model with quarks, when including plasma screening effects via ring diagrams, predicts that the Critical End Point (CEP) in the QCD phase diagram can be located at collision energies around sqrt(s_NN) ~ 2 GeV, specifically in the lowest NICA or within the HADES energy domain. This result depends on the high-temperature approximation and working in the chiral limit, which limits model accuracy but provides a useful guide for future studies.

## Method

The authors computed the effective potential of the Linear Sigma Model with quarks up to the ring diagrams contribution, which incorporates plasma screening effects through boson self-energies in the high-temperature approximation. Model parameters (a, lambda, g) were fixed by requiring that the phase transition line near zero baryon chemical potential matches the crossover transition found by Lattice QCD calculations, using the curvature parameters kappa_2 = 0.0153 and kappa_4 = 0.00032 from Borsanyi (2020). The probability distribution for the order parameter was then used to compute cumulant ratios of baryon number fluctuations as a function of collision energy, using a relation between sqrt(s_NN) and baryon chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The CEP location ranges between 786 MeV < mu_B^CEP < 849 MeV and 69 MeV < T^CEP < 70.3 MeV across the allowed parameter range.
- The pseudocritical temperature at zero baryon chemical potential is T_c(mu_B=0) ~ 158 MeV, taken from Lattice QCD input.
- The collision energy at the CEP is (sqrt(s_NN))_CEP ~ 2 GeV.
- The curvature parameters used from Lattice QCD are kappa_2 = 0.0153 and kappa_4 = 0.00032.
- The quark chemical potential at the CEP is mu^CEP ~ 1.7 T^CEP, justifying the high-temperature approximation.
- The volume used for cumulant calculations is Omega = (100 fm)^3.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Ayala (2019, 2017, 2015) that analyzed the effective potential of the Linear Sigma Model with quarks up to ring diagrams order but did not study higher-order fluctuations or the collision energy dependence of the CEP location. The model predicts deviations from the Hadron Resonance Gas Model expectations for cumulant ratios at collision energies around sqrt(s_NN) ~ 4-6 GeV, with the CEP itself appearing at sqrt(s_NN) ~ 2 GeV. The results are consistent with recent HADES data on net-proton number fluctuations and provide predictions for the NICA and HADES experimental programs.
