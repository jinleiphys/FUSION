# Triangle Diagram with Off-Shell Coulomb T-Matrix for (In-)Elastic Atomic and Nuclear Three-Body Processes

> The driving terms in three-body theories of elastic and inelastic scattering of a charged particle off a bound state of two other charged particles contain the fully off-shell two-body Coulomb T-matrix describing the intermediate-state Coulomb scattering of the projectile with each of the charged target particles. Up to now the latter is usually replaced by the Coulomb potential, either when using the multiple-scattering approach or when solving three-body integral equations. General properties of the exact and the approximate on-shell driving terms are discussed, and the accuracy of this approximation is investigated numerically, both for atomic and nuclear processes including bound-state excitation, for energies below and above the corresponding three-body dissociation threshold, over the whole range of scattering angles.

## Key claim

The common approximation of replacing the fully off-shell two-body Coulomb T-matrix by the Coulomb potential in three-body driving terms is systematically evaluated and found to be unreliable over wide parameter ranges, with the approximation always overestimating the exact elastic driving term for ground-state targets but producing values both larger and smaller than one for inelastic excitation processes.

## Method

The authors compute the ratio of the exact on-shell driving term (containing the full off-shell two-body Coulomb T-matrix) to the approximate Coulomb-Born driving term (with the Coulomb T-matrix replaced by the Coulomb potential) for elastic and inelastic scattering in three-body systems. They evaluate this ratio numerically for atomic systems (electron-hydrogen, positron-hydrogen, proton-hydrogen, and C6+-hydrogen) and nuclear systems (deuteron-nucleus scattering) over a range of energies from threshold to high energies, across the full angular range, using hydrogen-like wave functions for atomic cases and separable nuclear interactions for nuclear cases.

## Key numbers

- For elastic electron-hydrogen scattering, the minimum value of the ratio |R_ee,00| is approximately 0.27, reached at a projectile energy of around 70 eV and a scattering angle of about 60 degrees.
- For elastic positron-hydrogen scattering, the minimum ratio |R_pe+,00| deepens to approximately 0.054 around 60 eV projectile energy, occurring in the backward direction.
- For elastic proton-hydrogen scattering, the minimum ratio |R_pp,00| reaches values of order 10^-6 or less at 180 degrees, with the Coulomb-Born approximation failing completely nearly everywhere.
- For elastic C6+-hydrogen scattering, the minimum ratio |R_pC6+,00| is of order 10^-6 or less at 180 degrees.
- For inelastic electron-hydrogen excitation (1s to 2s), the ratio |R_ee,01| develops a minimum of 0.27 at 45 eV projectile energy and 90 degrees, coinciding with the elastic minimum value.
- The exact driving term for electron-hydrogen excitation ranges over 14 orders of magnitude in the parameter range considered.

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies by Sinfailam and Chen (1972), who found the Coulomb-Born approximation failed dramatically for atomic three-body processes at high energies, and theoretical studies by van Haeringen (1979) and Kok and colleagues (1979-1982), who restricted their investigations to negative total three-body energies and equal-mass particles. The present paper systematically covers energies both below and above the dissociation threshold, the full angular range, varying particle masses, and includes bound-state excitation, enabling more reliable assessment of when the Coulomb-Born approximation can be safely used in three-body integral equation approaches and multiple-scattering expansions for both atomic and nuclear reactions.
