# General Methods for Digital Quantum Simulation of Gauge Theories

> A general scheme is presented for simulating gauge theories, with matter fields, on a digital quantum computer. A Trotterized time-evolution operator that respects gauge symmetry is constructed, and a procedure for obtaining time-separated, gauge-invariant operators is detailed. We demonstrate the procedure on small lattices, including the simulation of a 2+1D non-Abelian gauge theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general, gauge-symmetry-preserving Trotterized time-evolution operator for digital quantum simulation of arbitrary gauge theories with matter fields, but the construction assumes ideal (infinite-precision) \(G\)-registers and does not address the additional gauge-invariance violations introduced by noisy gates or by the necessary discretization of continuous Lie groups.

## Method

The authors construct a Hamiltonian for pure gauge theories in the basis that diagonalizes the gauge field operators \(U\) (the "position" basis), then Trotterize the time-evolution operator into a product of commuting kinetic and potential terms, each implemented via primitive gates on \(G\)-registers (inversion, matrix multiplication, trace, and Fourier transform). The kinetic step is implemented by Fourier-transforming each link register, applying a diagonal phase, and transforming back; the potential step is implemented by computing the plaquette product into an ancillary register and applying a trace gate. Scalar and spinor matter fields are coupled using additional \(\mathbb{C}^N\)-registers with multiplication, inner-product, and Fourier gates. The method is demonstrated on small lattices for the non-Abelian discrete group \(D_4\) and for \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) with staggered fermions.

## Key numbers

*   For a pure-gauge simulation on a lattice with \(L\) links and \(N_P\) plaquettes, evolved for total time \(T\) with time-step \(\Delta t\), the gate count includes: \(2L \frac{T}{\Delta t}\) Fourier gates \(\mathfrak{U}_F\), \(L \frac{T}{\Delta t}\) phase gates \(\mathfrak{U}_{\text{phase}}\), \(6 N_P \frac{T}{\Delta t}\) inversion gates \(\mathfrak{U}_{-1}\), \(6 N_P \frac{T}{\Delta t}\) multiplication gates \(\mathfrak{U}_\times\), and \(N_P \frac{T}{\Delta t}\) trace gates \(\mathfrak{U}_{\Tr}\) (Table I).
*   The potential term in the Hamiltonian is \(H_V = -\frac{1}{g^2 a} \sum_p \Re \Tr \left[\prod_{\langle ij\rangle \in p} U_{ij}\right]\) and the kinetic term is \(H_K = \frac{4 g^2}{a} \sum_{\langle ij\rangle} \pi_{ij}^2\), where \(\pi_{ij}^2\) is the Laplace-Beltrami operator on the gauge group \(G\) (Eq. 1).
*   The transfer matrix \(T\) is constructed via the split-operator approximation \(T = T_V^{1/2} T_K T_V^{1/2}\), where the potential term contains \(\exp\left(\frac{a_0}{a g^2} \sum_x W_{\mu\nu}(x)\right)\) and the kinetic term contains \(\prod_{\langle ij\rangle} e^{\frac{a}{g^2 a_0} \Re \Tr [\tilde{U}_{ij}^\dagger U_{ij}]}\) (Eqs. 3-4).

## Context

This work extends prior proposals for simulating gauge theories on quantum computers, particularly those using Trotterized evolution for Abelian theories (Banerjee 2012, Hauke 2013, Martinez 2016, Klco 2018) and non-Abelian theories (Byrnes 2005, Banerjee 2012, Zohar 2012, Tagliacozzo 2012, Bender 2018), by providing a general framework that avoids gauge-fixing and exactly preserves gauge invariance at each Trotter step. The connection to the transfer matrix formalism (Luscher 1976, Creutz 1976, Caracciolo 2012) allows bare coupling constants to be determined classically via standard Euclidean lattice methods, enabling the quantum processor to be reserved for real-time evolution. The method enables the computation of time-separated, gauge-invariant correlators (e.g., glueball propagators and Wilson loops) using an ancillary-qubit technique (PhysRevLett.113.020505) combined with numerical differentiation of a perturbed Hamiltonian.
