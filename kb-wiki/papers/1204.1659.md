# Dynamical properties of the unitary Fermi gas: collective modes and shock waves

> We discuss the unitary Fermi gas made of dilute and ultracold atoms with an infinite s-wave inter-atomic scattering length. First we introduce an efficient Thomas-Fermi-von Weizsacker density functional which describes accurately various static properties of the unitary Fermi gas trapped by an external potential. Then, the sound velocity and the collective frequencies of oscillations in a harmonic trap are derived from extended superfluid hydrodynamic equations which are the Euler-Lagrange equations of a Thomas-Fermi-von Weizsacker action functional. Finally, we show that this amazing Fermi gas supports supersonic and subsonic shock waves.

# Dynamical properties of the unitary Fermi gas: collective modes and shock waves

## Key claim

The extended Thomas-Fermi–von Weizsäcker density functional, with parameters ξ = 0.40 and λ = 1/4 fitted to Monte Carlo data, accurately describes both static and dynamical properties of the unitary Fermi gas, including the prediction that this system supports supersonic and subsonic shock waves at zero temperature. The claim is conditional on the neglect of temperature effects (T ≪ T_c) and on the phenomenological choice of the gradient coefficient λ.

## Method

The author introduces an extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) density functional for the unitary Fermi gas, adding a von Weizsäcker gradient term λ ℏ²/(8m) (∇n)²/n to the standard Thomas-Fermi energy functional. The time-dependent version of this functional yields generalized superfluid hydrodynamic equations (Euler–Lagrange equations of an ETF action functional), which are solved numerically using a Crank-Nicolson finite-difference predictor-corrector algorithm. For shock waves, the author performs a dimensional reduction to one-dimensional axial dynamics, imposing periodic boundary conditions and an initial Gaussian density perturbation ρ(z, t=0) = 1 + 2η exp(−z²/(2σ²)) with zero initial velocity.

## Key numbers

- Universal parameter ξ = 0.40 and gradient coefficient λ = 1/4, fitted to Monte Carlo results for the energy of fermions in a spherical harmonic trap.
- First sound velocity of the uniform unitary Fermi gas: c₁ = √(ξ/3) v_F, where v_F = √(2ε_F/m) is the Fermi velocity.
- Monopole (breathing) mode frequency: Ω₀ = 2ω; dipole mode frequency: Ω₁ = ω, independent of atom number N.
- Quadrupole mode frequency Ω₂ depends on N and λ; in the Thomas-Fermi limit (N → ∞) Ω₂ = √2 ω, and in the non-interacting limit (N → 0) Ω₂ = 2ω.
- Mach number of density perturbations: M = 4(1+η)^(1/3) − 3, where η is the amplitude parameter; M > 1 for bright perturbations (η > 0, supersonic) and 0 ≤ M < 1 for dark perturbations (η < 0, subsonic).
- Breaking time for shock wave formation: T_s = σ / [2c_s ((1+η)^(1/3) − 1)], where σ is the perturbation half-width and c_s = √(ξ/3) v_F is the bulk sound velocity.
- Critical temperature of the superfluid-normal phase transition: T_c ≃ 0.2 T_F, with T_F ≃ 10⁻⁷ Kelvin for dilute alkali-metal atoms.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier density functional approach (Salasnich 2008, Salasnich and Ancilotto 2010) for the unitary Fermi gas, which itself builds on the Thomas-Fermi–von Weizsäcker method previously applied to electrons by March and Tosi and by Zaremba and Tso. The paper contrasts with orbital-based functionals such as the superfluid density functional of Bulgac and Yu (based on Bogoliubov–de Gennes) and the Kohn-Sham functional of Papenbrock and Bhattacharyya, which become numerically costly for large particle numbers. The shock wave predictions are motivated by the experimental observation of nonlinear hydrodynamic waves in collisions of strongly interacting ⁶Li Fermi gas clouds by Thomas and collaborators (2011). The results enable future simulations of colliding Fermi gas clouds without phenomenological viscous terms, and open the way to determining the surface tension of the unitary Fermi superfluid.
