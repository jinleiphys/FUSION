# Photo-absorption on deuteron contributed by d^*(2380) resonance

> In order to understand the possible physical nature of the newly observed resonance $d^*(2380)$, we calculate the real photo-absorption cross section on deuteron contributed by the resonance $d^*(2380)$ by considering the electromagnetic transition amplitude of $γ+d\rightarrow d^*(2380)$. In our interpretation, the $d^*(2380)$ is regarded as a compact six-quark system with mainly two components of $ΔΔ$ and hidden-color clusters $C_8C_8$. We find that only the next-to-leading terms contribute the $γ+d\rightarrow d^*(2380)$ and the obtained photo-absorption cross section is quite small which is in the order of 10 $nb$. Compared with data measured at ELPH and Mainz recently, it is almost about 20 times smaller.

## Key claim

The photo-absorption cross section on deuteron contributed by the d*(2380) resonance is calculated to be in the order of 10 nb, which is about 20 times smaller than recent experimental data from ELPH and Mainz, indicating that the direct photon coupling to one nucleon inside the deuteron is insufficient to describe the process.

## Method

The authors calculate the electromagnetic transition amplitude for γ + d → d*(2380) using the extended chiral SU(3) constituent quark model, where d*(2380) is treated as a compact six-quark system with approximately 31% ΔΔ configuration and 69% hidden-color C8C8 configuration. Since the leading-order photon-quark coupling vanishes due to the different structures of deuteron (pn) and d*(2380) (ΔΔ + C8C8), only next-to-leading order diagrams contribute, involving intermediate nucleon or Δ states and one-pion exchange between clusters. The helicity amplitudes and photo-absorption cross sections are computed in the Breit frame using non-relativistic approximations.

## Key numbers

- Total photo-absorption cross section at the d*(2380) resonance: σ^(d*)(M_d*; T) ~ 9.35 nb
- Individual cross sections for different deuteron polarizations: σ^(d*)(M_d*; m_d=+1) = 9.92 nb, σ^(d*)(M_d*; m_d=0) = 11.56 nb, σ^(d*)(M_d*; m_d=-1) = 6.49 nb
- Helicity amplitudes: |A_1| = 0.754 × 10^(-3) GeV^(-1/2), |A_0| = 0.812 × 10^(-3) GeV^(-1/2), |A_{-1}| = 0.608 × 10^(-3) GeV^(-1/2)
- The d*(2380) resonance has mass 2370-2380 MeV and width 70-80 MeV, with quantum numbers I(J^P) = 0(3^+)
- The ΔΔ component probability is about 31% and the C8C8 hidden-color component is about 69% in the d*(2380) wave function
- The calculated cross section is about 20 times smaller than the measured cross section of approximately 28 nb at the resonant point from ELPH data

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of d*(2380) as a compact hexaquark state (Dong et al., 2017; Huang et al., 2014, 2015) and contrasts with molecular-like interpretations such as the D12π model of Platonova and Belyaev (2012, 2014) or the ΔNπ three-body resonance approach of Gal (2013, 2014). The calculation is analogous to electromagnetic transition amplitude studies for nucleon resonances like Δ(1232), S11(1535), and D13(1520) performed by Capstick (1992), Close (1989), and others. The results suggest that the direct photon coupling mechanism alone cannot explain the experimental data from ELPH (Ishikawa et al., 2016, 2018) and Mainz (Gunther et al., 2017; Bashkanov et al., 2018), and further mechanisms such as pion cloud contributions may be needed.
