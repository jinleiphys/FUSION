# Quadrupole Deformation $β$ and $γ$ Constraint in a Framework of Antisymmetrized Molecular Dynamics

> We propose a new method of the $β$-$γ$ constraint for quadrupole deformation in antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) to describe various cluster and shell-model structures in the ground and excited states of light nuclei. We apply this method to N=6 isotones, $^{10}$Be, $^{12}$C, $^{9}$Li, and $^{11}$B, and find various structures as functions of the deformation parameters, $β$ and $γ$. In these nuclei, shell-model-like structures appear in the small $β$ region, while cluster structures develop well in the large $β$ region where various geometric configurations of clusters are obtained depending on the $γ$ parameter. For $^{10}$Be and $^{12}$C, we superpose the basis AMD wave functions obtained by the $β$-$γ$ constraint method to calculate energy spectra, and prove the advantages of the present method of the two-dimensional $β$-$γ$ constraint in the framework of AMD.

## Key claim

The authors propose a two-dimensional constraint on the quadrupole deformation parameters beta and gamma within the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) framework, claiming that this method efficiently generates basis wave functions describing both shell-model-like structures (at small beta) and various cluster configurations (at large beta) for light nuclei, though the success is demonstrated only for the specific N=6 isotones studied and the method's general applicability beyond these cases is not proven.

## Method

The authors performed variational calculations with constraints on the quadrupole deformation parameters beta and gamma using AMD wave functions, where the constraint potential enforces target (beta, gamma) values and zero off-diagonal quadrupole moments. They applied this beta-gamma constraint AMD to the N=6 isotones 10Be, 12C, 9Li, and 11B, generating wave functions at 196 mesh points on the beta-gamma plane. For 10Be and 12C, they superposed the total-angular-momentum projected basis wave functions using the generator coordinate method (GCM) to calculate energy spectra.

## Key numbers

- The width parameter of single-particle Gaussian wave packets was set to nu = 0.235 fm^-2, determined from a variational calculation for the ground state of 9Be.
- The central force used Volkov No.2 interaction with v1 = -60.65 MeV, v2 = 61.14 MeV, a1 = 1.80 fm, and a2 = 1.01 fm.
- The spin-orbit force used G3RS interaction with b1 = 0.600 fm, b2 = 0.477 fm, and strengths u1 = -1600 MeV and u2 = 1600 MeV.
- The deuteron binding energy in an exact calculation was 2.24 MeV, in agreement with the experimental value of 2.22 MeV.
- For 10Be, the minimum point of the positive-parity energy surface was at (beta cos gamma, beta sin gamma) = (0.35, 0.0), while after total-angular-momentum projection the 0+ energy surface minimum shifted to (0.55, 0.09).
- For 12C, the minimum point of the positive-parity energy surface was at (beta cos gamma, beta sin gamma) = (0.13, 0.22), and after total-angular-momentum projection the 0+ energy surface minimum became (0.35, 0.17).
- For 9Li, the minimum point of the 3/2- energy surface after total-angular-momentum projection was at (beta cos gamma, beta sin gamma) = (0.38, 0.13).
- For 11B, the minimum point of the 3/2- energy surface after total-angular-momentum projection was at (beta cos gamma, beta sin gamma) = (0.33, 0.13).

## Context

This work extends the earlier beta-constraint AMD method developed by Dote (1997) and Kimura (2001) by adding the gamma degree of freedom, enabling systematic study of triaxial deformations and cluster configurations. The approach is complementary to the beta-gamma constraint method used in Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations by Girod (1983) and Bender and Heenen (2008), but within a framework that can describe cluster structures unlike mean-field approaches. The method enables future systematic studies of cluster-shell competition in light unstable nuclei, particularly for describing exotic cluster structures in excited states of neutron-rich nuclei.
