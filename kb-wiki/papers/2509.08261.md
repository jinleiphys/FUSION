# Triaxial rotor modes in finite-N boson systems

> We propose an algebraic approach to elucidate the dynamic characteristics of triaxial rotor modes in nuclei by mapping a triaxial rotor Hamiltonian to the interacting boson model (IBM) one within a finite-$N$ framework. Our method unveils striking features not observed in conventional modes, exemplified by the $B(E2)$ anomaly, characterized by $B(E2;4_1--2_1^+)/B(E2;2_1--0_1^+)<<1$. Using specific examples, we demonstrate that the peculiar properties of low-lying states in both neutron-rich and neutron-deficient Os nuclei can be comprehensively understood through the proposed Hamiltonian, which incorporates both rigid and soft triaxial rotor modes. This algebraic method not only offers fresh insights into triaxial dynamics but also showcases its capability in uncovering emergent exotic collective modes in nuclear structure.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an algebraic mapping from a triaxial rotor Hamiltonian to the interacting boson model (IBM) within a finite-N framework, claiming that this approach can reproduce the B(E2) anomaly (B(E2;4_1+→2_1+)/B(E2;2_1+→0_1+) << 1) that conventional IBM modes cannot describe, but this anomaly only appears for specific choices of the SU(3) irreducible representation corresponding to γ_S = 30° and is absent for less triaxial deformations such as γ_S = 17.8°.

## Method

The authors construct an IBM Hamiltonian H_Tri = H_S + H_D, where H_S contains SU(3) Casimir operators to determine the ground-state deformation and H_D contains the rotor image operators L^2, (L×Q×L)^(0), and (L×Q)^(1)·(L×Q)^(1) derived from an exact mapping of the rigid rotor Hamiltonian. They then study the mixing of this triaxial rotor mode with the U(5) spherical vibrator mode using a Hamiltonian H = ε n_d + κ' H_Tri^χ, varying the control parameter η and the χ parameter to explore transitions between collective modes, and apply this to Os isotopes with boson numbers N=8 and N=9.

## Key numbers

- The B(E2) anomaly is characterized by B(E2;4_1+→2_1+)/B(E2;2_1+→0_1+) << 1, with the model producing B_{4/2} ~ 0.5 for the U(5)-rotor transition with γ_S = 30° and N=9
- For 168Os, the model gives B(E2;4_1+→2_1+)/B(E2;2_1+→0_1+) = 0.372 compared to the experimental value 0.34(18)
- For 170Os, the model gives B(E2;4_1+→2_1+)/B(E2;2_1+→0_1+) = 0.409 compared to the experimental value 0.38(11)
- The rotor Hamiltonian example uses A_1:A_2:A_3 = 3:1:4 with parameters t_1=3.0, t_2=0.1956, t_3=-0.00283 for N=9 and t_1=3.0, t_2=0.0548, t_3=-0.00022 for N=36
- The ground-state SU(3) IRREP for γ_S = 30° with N=9 is (λ_0, μ_0) = (6,6); for γ_S = 17.8° it is (10,4)
- For 190Os, the model reproduces B(E2;2_1+→0_1+) = 71.90 W.u. (experiment: 71.9(21)) and B(E2;4_1+→2_1+) = 98.90 W.u. (experiment: 105(6))
- For 192Os, the model reproduces B(E2;2_1+→0_1+) = 62.10 W.u. (experiment: 62.1(7)) and B(E2;4_1+→2_1+) = 83.15 W.u. (experiment: 75.6(20))

## Context

This work extends the algebraic mapping between triaxial rotor models and the SU(3) limit of the IBM established by Smirnov (2000) and Zhang (2014, 2022), which previously explained anomalous E2 transitions in neutron-deficient triaxial nuclei. The approach addresses the known limitation that IBM Hamiltonians with only two-body interactions cannot produce triaxial minima (VC 1981), requiring higher-order terms such as the cubic (d†×d†×d†)^3·(d̃×d̃×d̃)^3 operator. The method enables the description of both rigid and soft triaxial rotor modes within a unified IBM framework, and its application to 168,170Os (Grahn 2016, Goasduff 2019) and 190,192Os (Singh 2003, Baglin 2012) demonstrates that the B(E2) anomaly observed in neutron-deficient Os isotopes can be explained by finite-N effects in the triaxial rotor image, while the neutron-rich isotopes require a mixing of the triaxial rotor with the U(5) spherical vibrator mode.
