# Evolution of proto-neutron stars with the hadron-quark phase transition

> The Poyakov-Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model was developed recently, which includes both the chiral dynamics and (de)confinement effect and gives a good description of lattice QCD data. In this study we use the PNJL model to describe the quark phase, and first use it to study the evolution of proto-neutron star (PNS) with a hadron-quark phase transition. Along the line of a PNS evolution, we take several snapshots of PNS profiles, presenting the fractions of different species, the equations of state (EOS), and the mass-radius relations at different stages. The calculation shows the mixed phase may exist during the whole evolving process, and the onset density of quark phase decreases with the radiation of neutrinos in the heating stage. In the cooling stage, the EOS of the mixed phase softens and the center density increases. In this process a part of nuclear matter transforms to quark matter, which may lead to a PNS collapsing into a black hole.

# Evolution of proto-neutron stars with the hadron-quark phase transition

## Key claim

The hadron-quark phase transition may occur throughout the entire evolution of a proto-neutron star, and during the cooling stage a part of nuclear matter transforms to quark matter, which may lead to a PNS collapsing into a black hole if the equation of state in the core is not stiff enough. This possibility differs from results derived using the NJL model without the Polyakov loop confinement effect.

## Method

The hadronic phase is described by the non-linear Walecka model with the GM1 parameter set, while the quark phase is described by the three-flavor PNJL model that includes both chiral dynamics and the (de)confinement effect via the Polyakov loop. The mixed phase between hadronic and quark matter is determined using Gibbs criteria under thermal, chemical, and mechanical equilibrium with global electric charge neutrality. Four snapshots along the PNS evolution are taken at conditions (S=1, Y_Le=0.4), (S=1.5, Y_Le=0.3), (S=2, Y_nu_e=0), and (S=0, Y_nu_e=0), using an isentropic approximation.

## Key numbers

- The largest center densities of proto-neutron stars at the first three snapshots are 5.23, 5.00, and 4.54 rho_0, respectively, where rho_0 is the saturated nuclear density.
- The center density of the cold neutron star (S=0, Y_nu_e=0) is 4.88 rho_0.
- The maximum mass of cold neutron star with deconfined quarks is slightly larger than 2 M_sun.
- The PNJL model parameters used are: Lambda=603.2 MeV, G*Lambda^2=1.835, K*Lambda^5=12.36, m_u,d=5.5 MeV, m_s=140.7 MeV.
- The Polyakov loop effective potential parameters are: a0=-1.85, a1=-1.44e-3, a2=-0.08, a3=-0.4.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hadron-quark phase transitions in compact stars by using the PNJL model instead of the simpler MIT bag model or NJL model used in earlier work such as Steiner (2000) and Reddy (1998). The inclusion of the Polyakov loop confinement effect gives a stiffer mixed-phase EOS compared to the NJL model results of Steiner (2000), and the calculation shows that trapped neutrinos delay the onset of the hadron-quark phase transition to higher density. The results enable future studies of PNS collapse into black holes and motivate inclusion of vector interactions and color superconductivity in quark matter, as well as hyperon degrees of freedom.
