# A generalized definition of the isothermal compressibility in (2+1)-flavor QCD

> We introduce a generalized definition of the isothermal compressibility ($κ_{T,σ_Q^2}$) calculable by keeping net conserved charge fluctuations rather than total number densities constant. We present lattice QCD results for this isothermal compressibility, expressed in terms of fluctuations of conserved charges that are related to baryon ($B$), electric charge ($Q$) and strangeness ($S$) quantum numbers. This generalized isothermal compressibility is compared with hadron resonance gas model calculations as well as with heavy-ion collision data obtained at RHIC and the LHC. We find $κ_{T,σ_Q^2}=13.8(1.3)$~fm$^3$/GeV at $T_{pc,0}=156.5(1.5)$~MeV and $\hatμ_B=0$. This finding is consistent with the rescaled result of the ALICE Collaboration, where we replaced the number of charged hadrons ($N_{\rm ch}$) by the total number of hadrons ($N_{\rm tot}$) at freeze-out. Normalizing this result with the QCD pressure ($P$) we find that the isothermal compressibility on the pseudo-critical line stays close to that of an {\it ideal gas}, {\it i.e.} $P κ_{T,σ_Q^2}\simeq 1$.

# A generalized definition of the isothermal compressibility in (2+1)-flavor QCD

## Key claim
The authors introduce a generalized isothermal compressibility defined at fixed net-electric charge fluctuations rather than fixed particle number, which remains finite at vanishing chemical potential and yields a value of 13.8(1.3) fm^3/GeV at the pseudo-critical temperature; this result agrees with ALICE heavy-ion collision data only after rescaling to include neutral hadron contributions, which the original experimental analysis neglected.

## Method
The authors compute the generalized isothermal compressibility using a sixth-order Taylor expansion of the QCD pressure in terms of baryon, electric charge, and strangeness chemical potentials, with cumulants calculated from lattice QCD simulations of (2+1)-flavor QCD. They evaluate the compressibility at fixed net-electric charge fluctuations (sigma_Q^2) for strangeness-neutral, isospin-symmetric matter, and compare with non-interacting hadron resonance gas (HRG) model calculations using the QMHRG2020 hadron list. For comparison with heavy-ion data, they rescale experimental results from ALICE and STAR by including neutral hadron contributions using total hadron multiplicities from Braun-Munzinger (2014).

## Key numbers
- kappa_{T,sigma_Q^2} = 13.8(1.3) fm^3/GeV at T_{pc,0} = 156.5(1.5) MeV and hat{mu}_B = 0
- P kappa_{T,sigma_Q^2} = 0.80(12) + 0.06(1) hat{mu}_B^2 on the pseudo-critical line
- Leading-order compressibility kappa_T^{(0)} T^4 = 1.078(65) in QCD versus 1.713 in HRG at T_{pc,0}
- chi_{22}^{BQ}/chi_2^B = 0.41(3)(4) at T_{pc,0}
- HRG total hadron scaled variance omega_{tot} = 1.046 at (156.5, 0) MeV, decreasing to 1.032 at (144.3, 398) MeV
- ALICE rescaled result: kappa_{T,vec{N}} = 15.8(1.3) fm^3/GeV (original ALICE value was 27.9(3.18) fm^3/GeV neglecting neutral hadrons)

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD calculations of thermodynamic properties by Gupta (2014) that showed divergences in the standard isothermal compressibility at vanishing chemical potential. The generalized definition enables direct comparison with heavy-ion collision data from ALICE (2021) and STAR (2008, 2017), and with HRG model calculations. The finding that the compressibility stays close to the ideal gas value (P kappa approx 1) along the pseudo-critical line supports the use of HRG models for freeze-out phenomenology and provides a baseline for searches for critical point signatures, where the compressibility would diverge.
