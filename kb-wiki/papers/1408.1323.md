# Possibility of a new neutral hypernucleus (n,n,Lambda,Lambda)

> The status of the light hypernuclei is reviewed, and discussed with models based either on the Nijmegen-RIKEN baryon--baryon interaction or on recent studies using chiral effective field theory. The latter suggests a significantly shorter range for the $ΛΛ$ interaction, and this favours the formation of a Borromean state made of two neutrons and two hyperons. Various corrections are discussed, in particular the coupling of $NΛ$ to $NΣ$, or of $ΛΛ$ to $NΞ$, and the role of tensor forces. The new nucleus $(n,n,Λ,Λ)$ could be produced in various reactions, in particular deuteron--deuteron scattering with the simultaneous production of two charged kaons, for which an estimate of the cross section is provided.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (CEFT) suggests a significantly shorter range for the ΛΛ interaction than conventional meson-exchange models, and this favours the formation of a Borromean state made of two neutrons and two hyperons; the tetrabaryon (n,n,Λ,Λ) is found unbound with the Nijmegen-RIKEN ESC08 model but is at the edge of binding with CEFT, with a binding energy of about 1 MeV in a simulation using exponential potentials tuned to the CEFT low-energy parameters.

## Method

The authors solved the 2-body problem to fix parameters of simple potentials reproducing the deuteron binding energy, scattering lengths, and effective ranges for ΛN and ΛΛ from the Nijmegen-RIKEN group (ESC08) and the Bonn-Jülich EFT group (CEFT). The 3-body problem was solved using a variational wave function in perimetric coordinates and also by the Gaussian expansion method in the stochastic variant of Suzuki and Varga; the 4-body systems were solved using the Gaussian expansion method. The study was restricted to simple pairwise central potentials that reproduce the low-energy scattering parameters of each baryon-baryon pair, without explicit inclusion of tensor forces or coupling to ΣN and ΞN channels.

## Key numbers

- The nn scattering length is -16.51 fm (ESC08) and -18.9 fm (CEFT); the nn effective range is 2.85 fm (ESC08) and 2.75 fm (CEFT).
- The (nΛ)_{s=0} scattering length is -2.7 fm (ESC08) and -2.9 fm (CEFT); the effective range is 2.97 fm (ESC08) and 2.65 fm (CEFT).
- The (nΛ)_{s=1} scattering length is -1.65 fm (ESC08) and -1.51 fm (CEFT); the effective range is 3.63 fm (ESC08) and 2.64 fm (CEFT).
- The ΛΛ scattering length is -0.88 fm (ESC08) and -1.54 fm (CEFT); the effective range is 4.34 fm (ESC08) and 0.31 fm (CEFT).
- With CEFT parameters simulated by exponential potentials, the tetrabaryon (n,n,Λ,Λ) has a binding energy of about 1 MeV using about 80 terms in the Gaussian expansion scheme.
- The estimated cross section for the reaction d + d → T + K⁺ + K⁺ is of the order of 1–2 nb at an energy √s ∼ 5.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends studies of light hypernuclei using the Nijmegen-RIKEN baryon–baryon interaction (Rijken 2013) and recent chiral effective field theory calculations (Polinder 2007, Haidenbauer 2013). The authors agree with the conclusions of Nemura (2002) regarding (n,p,Λ,Λ) and with Lekala (2014) that (n,n,Λ,Λ) is unbound with ESC08, but find that the shorter ΛΛ effective range in CEFT may allow a Borromean state. The production reaction d + d → T + K⁺ + K⁺ is proposed as a promising tool to search for this new hypernucleus, and further investigations of 3-body forces and the couplings ΛN ↔ ΣN and ΛΛ ↔ ΞN are needed.
