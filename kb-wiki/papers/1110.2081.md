# Magnetism in Cold-Dense QCD

> In this talk I review some of the main findings on magnetism in color superconductivity. The physical characteristic of the different phases that are reached by increasing the applied magnetic field in a three-flavor color superconductor at asymptotically high densities are discussed. A mechanism to boost a seed magnetic field in high-dense quark matter is presented. Also, it is shown how a magnetic field can be generated in a color superconductor at moderate densities. Possible implications of these results for the astrophysics of magnetized compact objects are indicated.

# Magnetism in Cold-Dense QCD

## Key claim

The talk reviews how an applied magnetic field can modify color superconductivity in three-flavor quark matter at asymptotically high densities, producing distinct phases (MCFL and PCFL) that depend on the field strength relative to the CFL gap and the gluon Meissner mass; the abstract glosses over the fact that the MCFL phase is theoretically present for any nonzero field but only becomes effectively distinguishable from CFL at a threshold field proportional to the square of the CFL gap.

## Method

The analysis uses effective models of low-energy QCD to study diquark condensates in the Color-Flavor-Locked (CFL) phase under an applied rotated magnetic field, solving gap equations derived from the free energy to obtain the field-dependent gaps Δ, Δ_B, and Δ_M. The gluon sector is treated via an effective action for charged gluon fields, diagonalizing the mass matrix to identify a tachyonic mode that signals an instability, and then solving Ginzburg-Landau-type equations to find the vortex lattice structure of the gluon condensate.

## Key numbers

- Threshold field for CFL-to-MCFL transmutation: $\widetilde{B}_{MCFL} \sim \Delta^{2}_{CFL}$, where $\Delta_{CFL}$ is the CFL gap.
- Critical field for gluon condensation in the PCFL phase: $\widetilde{H}_{c} \simeq 0.7 \times 10^{17}$ G, obtained using $\mu \simeq 400$ MeV and $g(\mu) \simeq 3$, giving $m_{M}^{2} \simeq 16 \times 10^{-3}$ GeV².
- The Meissner mass of charged gluons in the CFL phase: $m_{M}^{2} = m_{g}^{2} (21 - 8 \ln 2)/54$, with $m_{g}^{2} = g^2 (\mu^2 / 2\pi^2)$.
- The Debye mass of charged gluons: $m_{D}^{2} = m_{g}^{2} (21 - 8 \ln 2)/18$.
- Magnetic flux quantum per vortex cell in the PCFL phase: $\widetilde{\Phi}_{0} = 2\pi / \widetilde{e}$.
- The magnetic-moment condensate $\Delta_M$ becomes comparable to $\Delta_B$ only when $\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B} \geq \mu^2$; at lower fields $\Delta_M$ is negligibly small.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of color superconductivity in the CFL phase (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek 1999) and the chromomagnetic instabilities found at intermediate densities (Inst-CFL). It builds on the rotated electromagnetic field formalism (rotated-EM) and the magnetic catalysis of chiral symmetry breaking (Miransky, Shovkovy 2002). The MCFL phase introduces a new gap structure with five neutral Goldstone bosons instead of nine, which has implications for transport and cooling of compact star cores. The PCFL phase provides a mechanism to boost seed magnetic fields inside neutron stars, with possible relevance for magnetar fields in the range $10^{14}-10^{15}$ G at the surface and estimated interior fields up to $10^{18}-10^{20}$ G (virial theorem for nuclear matter; EoS-H for quark matter).
