# Neutrino Degeneracy and Decoupling: New Limits from Primordial Nucleosynthesis and the Cosmic Microwave Background

> We reanalyze the cosmological constraints on the existence of a net universal lepton asymmetry and neutrino degeneracy. We show that neutrinos can begin to decouple at higher temperatures than previous estimates due to several corrections which diminish the neutrino reaction rate. These decoupled neutrinos are therefore not heated as the particle degrees of freedom change. The resultant ratio of the relic neutrino-to-photon temperatures after $e^\pm$ annihilation can then be significantly reduced by more than a factor of two from that of the standard nondegenerate ratio. This changes the expansion rate and subsequent primordial nucleosynthesis, photon decoupling, and structure formation. In particular we analyze physically plausible lepton-asymmetric models with large $ν_μ$ and $ν_τ$ degeneracies together with a moderate $ν_e$ degeneracy. We show that the nucleosynthesis by itself permits very large neutrino degeneracies $0 \le \xinum$, $\xinut \le 40$, $0 \le \xinue \le 1.4$ together with large baryon densities $0.1 \le Ω_b \h502 \le 1$ as long as some destruction of primordial lithium has occurred. We also show that structure formation and the power spectrum of the cosmic microwave background allows for the possibility of an $Ω= 1$, $Ω_Λ= 0.4$, cosmological model for which there is both significant lepton asymmetry ($| \xinum | = | \xinut | \approx 11$) and a relatively large baryon density ($Ω_b \h502 \approx 0.2$). Our best-fit neutrino-degenerate, high-baryon-content models are mainly distinguished by a suppression of the second peak in the microwave background power spectrum. This is consistent with the recent high resolution data from BOOMERANG and MAXIMA-1.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when neutrinos decouple at higher temperatures than previously estimated (due to corrections that diminish the neutrino reaction rate), the relic neutrino-to-photon temperature ratio can be reduced by more than a factor of two from the standard nondegenerate ratio, which in turn permits large neutrino degeneracies and baryon densities that would otherwise be ruled out by primordial nucleosynthesis constraints alone.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the neutrino decoupling temperature by including corrections for degeneracy effects on weakly-reacting species abundances, finite temperature corrections to electron and photon effective masses, and the use of Møller velocity for the collision term integration in the Boltzmann equations. They then computed the relic neutrino temperature by conserving relativistic entropy within a comoving volume, treating the energy densities of all particle species continuously rather than assuming instantaneous annihilation. Finally, they performed big bang nucleosynthesis calculations with these modified neutrino temperatures and compared the predicted light element abundances and cosmic microwave background power spectra against observational constraints.

## Key numbers

* Neutrino decoupling temperature for nondegenerate electron neutrinos: \(T_D(\xi_\nu = 0) \simeq 2.33\) MeV; for nondegenerate muon and tau neutrinos: \(T_D(\xi_\nu = 0) \simeq 3.90\) MeV
* Nucleosynthesis alone permits neutrino degeneracies in the ranges \(0 \le \xi_{\nu_\mu}, \xi_{\nu_\tau} \le 40\) and \(0 \le \xi_{\nu_e} \le 1.4\) together with baryon densities \(0.1 \le \Omega_b h_{50}^2 \le 1\)
* A best-fit cosmological model with \(\Omega = 1\), \(\Omega_\Lambda = 0.4\) allows significant lepton asymmetry (\(|\xi_{\nu_\mu}| = |\xi_{\nu_\tau}| \approx 11\)) and relatively large baryon density (\(\Omega_b h_{50}^2 \approx 0.2\))
* The relic neutrino temperature drops discontinuously when the degeneracy parameter reaches \(\xi_{\nu_{\mu,\tau}} \approx 11\) (compared to \(\approx 15\) in Kang and Steigman (1992)), corresponding to decoupling above the QCD phase transition temperature of 150 MeV
* For neutrinos decoupling just above the QCD transition, the relic neutrino temperature ratio is reduced by an additional factor \((9/60)^{1/3} \approx 0.53\) relative to the standard \((4/11)^{1/3}\) ratio

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of neutrino degeneracy in big bang nucleosynthesis, particularly the treatment by Kang and Steigman (1992), by including corrections that raise the neutrino decoupling temperature and by continuously tracking particle degrees of freedom rather than assuming instantaneous annihilation. The paper shows that these corrections allow neutrino-degenerate models with high baryon density to satisfy both nucleosynthesis constraints and cosmic microwave background data from BOOMERANG and MAXIMA-1, which hint at a suppression of the second acoustic peak. The results enable future work to constrain neutrino mass spectra from the implied neutrino contribution to the closure density and to explore models where neutrino degeneracy resolves apparent tensions between standard BBN predictions and observations of deuterium, helium-4, and lithium-7 abundances.
