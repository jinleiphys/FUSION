# Aspects of short range correlations in a relativistic model

> In the present work short range correlations are introduced for the first time in a relativistic approach to the equation of state of the infinite nuclear matter in the framework of the Hartree-Fock approximation using an effective Hamiltonian derived from the $σ-ω$ Walecka model. The unitary correlation method is used to introduce short range correlations. The effect of the correlations in the ground state properties of the nuclear matter is discussed.

# Aspects of short range correlations in a relativistic model

## Key claim

Short range correlations are introduced for the first time in a relativistic Hartree-Fock treatment of infinite nuclear matter using the σ-ω Walecka model, but the correlation parameters are not uniquely determined; the authors explore four different parameter choices (sets I–IV) and find that the equation of state is softened relative to Hartree or Hartree-Fock calculations in most cases, except when the Jastrow parameter α is fixed to 1, in which case the equation of state coincides with the Hartree result.

## Method

The authors start from the relativistic σ-ω Walecka model and apply the unitary correlation method (Villars approach) to introduce two-body short range correlations into the nuclear matter wave function. The correlated wave function is approximated by a Jastrow factor f(r) = 1 − (α + βr)e^(−γr), with parameters α, β, γ constrained by a normalization condition and by the healing distance concept. The ground state energy density is then computed in the Hartree-Fock approximation including direct and exchange contributions from both the kinetic and potential energy terms, with the correlation effects entering through modified matrix elements.

## Key numbers

- Saturation properties for set III (variational α, density-dependent γ): compressibility K = 280 MeV, relative effective mass M*/M = 0.65, kinetic energy T/ρ_B − M = 24.5 MeV, direct potential energy V_d/ρ_B = −47.5 MeV, exchange potential energy V_e/ρ_B = 8.4 MeV, correlation contribution to kinetic energy T^C/ρ_B = 5.5 MeV, correlation range R = 1.0 fm.
- Saturation properties for set IV (α = 1, γ = 750 MeV): compressibility K = 540 MeV, relative effective mass M*/M = 0.72, kinetic energy T/ρ_B − M = 23.0 MeV, direct potential energy V_d/ρ_B = −49.0 MeV, exchange potential energy V_e/ρ_B = 8.7 MeV, correlation contribution to kinetic energy T^C/ρ_B = 0.0 MeV, correlation range R = 0.9 fm.
- Meson masses used: m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV.
- The normalization condition ∫ (f²(r) − 1) d³r = 0 determines β; the healing distance is approximately 1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean field (RMF) approach of Walecka (1974) and the relativistic Hartree-Fock approximation of Horowitz and Serot (1981) and Bouyssy et al. (1987) by explicitly including short range correlations, which were previously absorbed into phenomenological parameters. The results are compared with the non-linear Walecka model NL3 and the quark-meson-coupling model (QMC). The authors note that the explicit inclusion of correlations reduces the coupling constants and softens the equation of state, playing a role similar to non-linear terms in relativistic models or three-body forces in non-relativistic potential models such as those by Coester (1970). The paper states that three-body correlations are under investigation.
