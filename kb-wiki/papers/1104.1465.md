# Gauge-independence of gluon spin in the nucleon and its evolution

> In recent papers, we have established the existence of gauge-invariant decomposition of nucleon spin, each term of which can be related to known high-energy deep-inelastic-scattering observables. A subtlety remains, however, for the intrinsic spin part of gluons at the quantum level. In fact, it was sometimes claimed that the evolution of gluon spin depends on the gauge choice and its physical interpretation makes sense only in the light-cone gauge. In the present paper, we will demonstrate explicitly that the gluon spin operator appearing in our decomposition evolves gauge-independently and that it properly reproduces the familiar evolution equation for the 1st moments of polarized quark and gluon distributions obtained with the Altarelli-Parisi method, which cannot directly be checked by the standard operator expansion method.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the gluon spin operator appearing in the author's gauge-invariant decomposition of nucleon spin evolves gauge-independently, and that its 1-loop anomalous dimensions in the Feynman gauge reproduce the standard Altarelli-Parisi evolution equations, contrary to earlier claims that the evolution depends on the gauge choice.

## Method

The author calculates the 1-loop anomalous dimensions of the quark and gluon spin operators within the Feynman gauge using a nonstandard Feynman rule: when one endpoint of a gluon propagator contracts with the physical part of the gluon field in the gluon spin operator, the propagator is replaced by a sum over only the two physical polarization states. The contributions from the three vertex pieces V_A, V_B, and V_C that constitute the gluon spin operator are computed separately, and the results are combined with field-strength renormalization contributions.

## Key numbers

- Quark spin operator anomalous dimension: Δγ^{(0)}_{qq} = 0, Δγ^{(0)}_{qG} = 0
- Gluon-to-quark mixing anomalous dimension: Δγ^{(0)}_{Gq} = (α_S / 2π) × (3/2) × C_F, with C_F = 4/3
- Gluon spin operator anomalous dimension: Δγ^{(0)}_{GG} = (α_S / 2π) × (11/6 × C_A - 1/3 × n_f), with C_A = 3 and n_f the number of quark flavors
- The final anomalous dimensions coincide with the lowest-order anomalous dimensions from the Altarelli-Parisi method

## Context

This work resolves a puzzle raised by Hoodbhoy, Ji, and Lu (1999), who claimed that gluon spin evolves differently in the light-cone gauge versus the Feynman gauge. It extends the author's previous gauge-invariant decomposition of nucleon spin (Wakamatsu 2010, 2011) by providing the operator definition of gluon spin that reproduces the Altarelli-Parisi evolution equations in any gauge. The result enables a consistent gauge-invariant treatment of gluon spin in nucleon structure calculations and confirms that the gluon spin operator provides the long-sought operator definition of gluon spin.
