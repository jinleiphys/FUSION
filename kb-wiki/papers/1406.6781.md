# Break-up channels in muon capture on 3He

> The mu + 2H -> nu + n + n, mu + 3He -> nu + 3H, mu + 3He -> nu + n + d and mu + 3He -> nu + n + n + p capture reactions are studied with various realistic potentials under full inclusion of final state interactions. Our results for the two- and three-body break-up of 3He are calculated with a variety of nucleon-nucleon potentials, among which is the AV18 potential, augmented by the Urbana~IX three-nucleon potential. Most of our results are based on the single nucleon weak current operator. As a first step, we have tested our calculation in the case of the mu + 2H -> nu + n + n and mu + 3He -> nu + 3H reactions, for which theoretical predictions obtained in a comparable framework are available. Additionally, we have been able to obtain for the first time a realistic estimate for the total rates of the muon capture reactions on 3He in the break-up channels: 544 1/s and 154 1/s for the n + d and n + n + p channels, respectively. Our results have also been compared with the most recent experimental data, finding a rough agreement for the total capture rates, but failing to reproduce the differential capture rates.

## Key claim
The authors present the first realistic estimates for total muon capture rates on ³He in the break-up channels (n+d and n+n+p) using only the single nucleon weak current operator, finding rough agreement with experimental total capture rates but failing to reproduce differential capture rates.

## Method
The authors employed the Faddeev equation approach in momentum space to calculate muon capture reactions on ²H and ³He, using various realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials (including AV18 and Bonn B) with the Urbana IX three-nucleon force. They performed a nonrelativistic reduction of the single nucleon weak current operator, including terms up to 1/M², and solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for final state interactions in the two- and three-body break-up channels.

## Key numbers
- Total capture rate for the μ⁻ + ³He → νμ + n + d channel: 544 s⁻¹
- Total capture rate for the μ⁻ + ³He → νμ + n + n + p channel: 154 s⁻¹
- For the μ⁻ + ²H → νμ + n + n reaction with AV18 potential including relativistic corrections: Γ_d^{F=1/2} = 392 s⁻¹ (full result) and Γ_d^{F=3/2} = 12.2 s⁻¹ (full result)
- For the μ⁻ + ²H → νμ + n + n reaction with Bonn B potential without relativistic corrections: Γ_d^{F=1/2} = 403 s⁻¹ (full result) and Γ_d^{F=3/2} = 11.7 s⁻¹ (full result)
- Relativistic and nonrelativistic maximal neutrino energies for the nn channel differ by only 0.0018 MeV (99.5072 MeV vs 99.5054 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the calculations of Romek (earlier reference) for the μ⁻ + ³He → νμ + n + d reaction to also cover the μ⁻ + ³He → νμ + n + n + p channel, and improves upon those calculations by including full final state interactions with both nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon forces. The results are compared with those of Marcucci et al. (2012) who used the hyperspherical harmonics formalism, which was not available for the A=3 full break-up channel. The authors establish a theoretical framework that can be extended to include meson-exchange currents in future work, and provide benchmark results for comparison with experimental data from Ackerbauer et al. (1998) and older measurements.
