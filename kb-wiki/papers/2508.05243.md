# Ab initio description of hypernuclei

> Hypernuclei are bound states of neutrons, protons and one or two hyperons, thus extending the nuclear landscape to a third dimension. They also encode information about the baryon-baryon and three-baryon interactions. Here, we review recent work on chiral effective field theory for two- and three-baryon interactions and their application in nuclei based on ab initio methods. These include the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, the no-core-shell-model (NCSM) and nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT). Besides of providing an overview of the formalisms explicit results for the separation energies of light $Λ$ hypernuclei are provided. Two-body and three-body forces are included consistently, in line with the underlying power counting. Calculations of $Λ$ hypernuclei within the NCSM, performed up to A=7 so far, suggest that agreement with the experimental binding energies can be achieved once appropriate three-body forces are taken into account. Similar conclusions are drawn from the study based on NLEFT, where even hypernuclei up to A=16 can be computed. Additionally, applications of ab initio approaches in calculations of $ΛΛ$ and $Ξ$ hypernuclei are discussed and possible candidates for the lightest systems that could be bound are identified, namely $^{\ \ 5}_{ΛΛ}{\rm He}$ and $^4_Ξ{\rm H}$.

## Key claim
The paper reviews how chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions for baryon-baryon systems, when combined with ab initio methods, can reproduce experimental binding energies of light hypernuclei, but only once appropriate three-body forces are included; agreement with experiment is not achieved with two-body forces alone.

## Method
The authors construct baryon-baryon potentials within SU(3) chiral EFT using the Weinberg power counting up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), with semilocal momentum-space (SMS) regularization. These potentials are then applied in ab initio calculations of hypernuclei using the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations, the no-core-shell-model (NCSM), and nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT). The low-energy constants are fitted to a set of 35 Lambda-N and Sigma-N cross sections plus the hypertriton binding energy.

## Key numbers
- Lambda-N singlet scattering length: a_s = -2.80 fm (SMS N2LO, cutoff 550 MeV)
- Lambda-N triplet scattering length: a_t = -1.58 fm (SMS N2LO, cutoff 550 MeV)
- Sigma-N (I=1/2) triplet scattering length: Re a_t = 2.38 fm, Im a_t = -3.26 fm (SMS N2LO, cutoff 550 MeV)
- Sigma-N (I=3/2) singlet scattering length: a_s = -4.19 fm (SMS N2LO, cutoff 550 MeV)
- Sigma-N (I=3/2) triplet scattering length: a_t = 0.44 fm (SMS N2LO, cutoff 550 MeV)
- Weak pion decay constant used: F_pi = 92.4 MeV (LO and NLO); at N2LO empirical values F_K = (1.19 +/- 0.01) F_pi and F_eta = (1.30 +/- 0.05) F_pi are used
- Axial-vector coupling: g_A = 1.29 (to account for Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy)
- Chi-squared values for YN data: approximately 15-16 at NLO, approximately 30 at LO
- Cutoff masses for SMS potentials: 500, 550, 600 MeV; for earlier non-local potentials: 550-700 MeV

## Context
This review extends the authors' previous work on chiral YN potentials (NLO13, NLO19) and the SMS N2LO potential (Haidenbauer 2023) by applying them to hypernuclei. It builds on the chiral EFT framework for NN interactions (Epelbaum 2008, Machleidt 2011) and extends it to the strangeness sector. The work enables systematic ab initio calculations of hypernuclei up to A=16 (via NLEFT) and identifies candidates for the lightest bound Lambda-Lambda and Xi hypernuclei (He-5 with two Lambdas and H-4 with a Xi), which can guide future experimental searches at facilities like J-PARC.
