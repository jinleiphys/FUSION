# Coulomb corrections to inclusive cross sections at the future Electron - Ion Collider

> The experimental results of the future electron -- ion ($e A$) collider are expected to constrain the dynamics of the strong interactions at small values of the Bjorken -- $x$ variable and large nuclei. Recently it has been suggested that Coulomb corrections can be important in inclusive and diffractive $eA$ interactions. In this paper we present a detailed investigation of the impact of the Coulomb corrections to some of the observables that will be measured in the future $eA$ collider. In particular, we estimate the magnitude of these corrections for the charm and longitudinal cross sections in inclusive and diffractive interactions. Our results demonstrate that the Coulomb corrections for these observables are negligible, which implies that they can be used to probe the QCD dynamics.

## Key claim

The Coulomb corrections to the charm and longitudinal cross sections in inclusive and diffractive electron-nucleus interactions at the future Electron-Ion Collider are negligible, meaning these observables can be used as clean probes of QCD dynamics, despite earlier suggestions that Coulomb corrections could reach 20-40% of the total cross sections.

## Method

The authors used the dipole approach to deep inelastic scattering, factorizing the electron-nucleus interaction into the fluctuation of a virtual photon into a quark-antiquark dipole and the dipole-nucleus scattering. They modeled the dipole-nucleus forward scattering amplitude using a Glauber multiple scattering approach that adds strong and Coulomb phase shifts separately, with the strong interaction described by a nuclear saturation model (Golec-Biernat–Wusthoff type) and the electromagnetic interaction treated via a single-photon exchange in the Weizsacker-Williams approximation. They then computed inclusive and diffractive cross sections for light, charm, and bottom quarks, and for longitudinal and transverse polarizations, comparing results with and without the Coulomb contribution.

## Key numbers

- For gold nucleus (A=197) at x=10^-4 and Q^2=1 GeV^2, the Coulomb contribution is approximately 10% of the inclusive cross section and 21% of the diffractive cross section.
- The ratio between diffractive and total cross sections is enhanced by approximately 10% at Q^2=1 GeV^2 due to Coulomb corrections.
- For charm production in diffractive interactions at Q^2=1 GeV^2, the Coulomb corrections are of order 2%.
- For the inclusive longitudinal cross section, the ratio of electromagnetic to strong contributions becomes smaller than 5% for Q^2 greater than 1.5 GeV^2.
- The charm contribution to the total cross section is expected to be approximately 20% at small x and low virtualities.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Tuchin (2011, 2013), who first suggested that Coulomb corrections could reach 20% of the inclusive cross section and 40% of the diffractive cross section in electron-nucleus scattering at small x and low Q^2. The present study improves upon that work by including heavy quark (charm and bottom) contributions and performing a flavor and polarization decomposition. The results show that while Coulomb corrections are important for total cross sections, they are negligible for charm and longitudinal observables, confirming that these channels can be used to probe QCD saturation effects as studied by Goncalves et al. (2014, 2015) and others. This enables future EIC measurements to cleanly constrain non-linear QCD dynamics without significant electromagnetic background.
