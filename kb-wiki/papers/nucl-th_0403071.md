# Relativistic effects and angular dependence in the reaction antiproton-proton -> pi^- pi^+

> We present a new fit to the LEAR data on antiproton-proton -> pi^- pi^+ differential cross sections and analyzing powers motivated by relativistic considerations. Within a quark model describing this annihilation we argue, since the pions are highly energetic, that relativistic effects cannot be neglected. The intrinsic pion wave functions are Lorentz transformed to the center of mass frame. This change in quark geometry gives rise to additional angular dependence in the transition operators and results in a relative enhancement of higher J \ge 2 partial wave amplitudes. The fit to the data is improved significantly.

## Key claim
Relativistic Lorentz transformation of the intrinsic pion wave functions from the pion rest frame to the center-of-mass frame introduces additional angular dependence in the transition operators, which enhances higher partial wave amplitudes with J >= 2 and significantly improves the fit to LEAR data without requiring the increased particle radii used in prior work.

## Method
The authors computed transition operators for the R2 quark-flow diagrams using Gaussian intrinsic wave functions for the proton, antiproton, and pions, with the pion wave functions boosted to the center-of-mass frame via a Lorentz transformation. They then performed a distorted-wave approximation fit to LEAR differential cross section and analyzing power data at five antiproton laboratory kinetic energies (66.7, 123.5, 219.9, 499.2, and 803.1 MeV), varying the relative strength of the 3S1 versus 3P0 annihilation mechanisms and the pi-pi final-state interaction phase shifts and inelasticities for J = 0 through 4.

## Key numbers
- Relativistic gamma factor for the pions ranges from gamma = 6.841 to 8.033 across the five energies studied (corresponding to pion speeds v approximately 0.98c).
- The pion size parameter beta is fixed at 3.23 fm^-2, corresponding to a pion radius of 0.48 fm.
- The proton size parameter alpha is fixed at 2.8 fm^-2.
- The D coefficient in the exponential of the transition operators is typically about 200 fm^-2, roughly two orders of magnitude larger than the C coefficient.
- The relative strength |lambda| of the 3S1 versus 3P0 mechanism averages about 1.17 across the five energies, ranging from 0.889 at 123.5 MeV to 1.377 at 499.2 MeV.
- The pi-pi inelasticity eta_2 drops to 0.538 at sqrt(s) = 2111 MeV and 0.624 at sqrt(s) = 2242 MeV, associated with the f2 resonance near 2010 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the constituent quark model approach of El-Bennich and Kloet (2004) and earlier studies by Kloet and collaborators, which had failed to reproduce the characteristic double-dip structure of the analyzing power A_0n and the forward peaks in differential cross sections at low momenta. Prior models by Moussallam (1983), Mull (1991, 1992), Muhm (1996), and Yan (1996) produced amplitudes dominated by J=0 and J=1 due to short annihilation ranges. The present approach achieves fit quality comparable to El-Bennich and Kloet (2004) but through relativistic effects rather than by increasing particle radii, demonstrating that the observed higher partial wave contributions can arise from Lorentz-boosted pion wave functions rather than from enlarged annihilation range.
