# Formation of Nuclear "Pasta" in Cold Neutron Star Matter

> Formation and decay of rod-like and slab-like nuclei, as may be encountered in the outer parts of neutron stars, are examined within a zero-temperature stability analysis with respect to perturbations inducing fission and proton clustering. The nonspherical nuclei are found to be stable against these perturbations, implying the possibility that the size of the stellar region containing such nuclei exceeds the equilibrium prediction.

## Key claim

Rod-like and slab-like nuclei in cold neutron star matter are stable against both fission-like deformations and proton clustering, implying that the stellar region containing such "nuclear pasta" phases may be larger than the equilibrium prediction, potentially extending up to a critical point where the stable uniform or bcc phase nucleates via quantum tunnelling.

## Method

The authors performed a zero-temperature stability analysis using a compressible liquid-drop model (modified from Baym, Bethe and Pethick 1971) with parameters \(C_1 = 400\) MeV fm\(^2\) and \(C_2 = 1\) for the surface tension and proton chemical potential. They derived instability conditions for quadrupole-type deformations of rods and slabs (generalizing the Bohr-Wheeler fission criterion for spherical nuclei) and for proton clustering via an extended Thomas-Fermi energy expansion, then evaluated these conditions for the ground-state neutron star matter phases.

## Key numbers

- The transition density from spherical to cylindrical nuclei is \(n_b \approx 0.079\) fm\(^{-3}\), close to the fission-like instability point \(u \approx 1/8\) or equivalently \(n_b \approx 0.061\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The critical density for proton clustering in uniform matter is \(\approx 0.120\) fm\(^{-3}\), agreeing with the phase-equilibrium point \(n_b \approx 0.123\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The critical wavelength for proton clustering in uniform matter is \(2\pi Q^{-1} \sim 20\) fm, comparable to an internuclear spacing of \(\sim 2R_c\).
- The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is \(\rho_s \simeq 2.7 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\), and the system dissolves into uniform matter at about \(\rho_s/2\).

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of Pethick and Ravenhall (1995) for spherical nuclei to rod-like and slab-like nuclei, and uses the compressible liquid-drop model previously developed by the authors (Watanabe, Iida, Sato 2000). The finding that nonspherical nuclei are stable against both fission and proton clustering contradicts the expectation that they would decay near equilibrium transition points, and enables future studies of metastability and quantum tunnelling nucleation in neutron star crusts, as well as finite-temperature effects that could melt the low-dimensional lattice via elastic deformations.
