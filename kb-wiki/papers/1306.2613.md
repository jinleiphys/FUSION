# Estimates for the Thermal Width of Heavy Quarkonia in Strongly Coupled Plasmas from Holography

> The gauge/gravity duality is used to investigate the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential (defined via the rectangular Wilson loop) in strongly coupled plasmas. This quantity can be used to estimate the width of heavy quarkonia in a plasma at strong coupling. In this paper the thermal worldsheet fluctuation method, proposed in [J.Noronha and A.Dumitru, Phys.\ Rev.\ Lett.\ {\bf 103}, 152304 (2009)], is revisited and general conditions for the existence of an imaginary part for the heavy quark potential computed within classical gravity models are obtained. We prove a general result that establishes the connection between this imaginary part of the potential determined holographically and the area law displayed by the Wilson loop in the vacuum of confining gauge theories. We also determine the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential in a strongly coupled plasma dual to Gauss-Bonnet gravity. This provides an estimate of how the thermal width of heavy quarkonia changes with the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, $η/s$, at strong coupling.

## Key claim

The thermal width of heavy quarkonia in strongly coupled plasmas can be estimated from the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential computed holographically via worldsheet fluctuations, but this imaginary part exists only when the bottom of the classical string worldsheet lies between the black brane horizon and a specific critical position determined by the metric; the authors prove a general connection between this imaginary part and the area law of the Wilson loop in confining gauge theories at zero temperature.

## Method

The authors revisit the thermal worldsheet fluctuation method proposed by Noronha and Dumitru (2009) for computing the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential via the gauge/gravity duality. They derive general conditions for the existence of an imaginary part within classical gravity models by analyzing the Nambu-Goto action for a rectangular Wilson loop in a general 5-dimensional metric with a black brane horizon, using a saddle point approximation to integrate out thermal long wavelength fluctuations. They then apply this formalism to compute the imaginary part of the potential in strongly coupled plasmas dual to Gauss-Bonnet gravity, which allows them to study how the thermal width depends on the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio, eta/s, is varied at strong coupling using Gauss-Bonnet gravity to estimate how the thermal width of heavy quarkonia changes.
* The black brane temperature T is related to the position of the horizon U_h, with the metric having G_00(U_h) = 0 and G_UU(U_h) going to infinity while G_00(U_h) G_UU(U_h) remains finite.
* The string tension sigma in confining theories at zero temperature is given by sigma = (1/(2 pi alpha')) sqrt(V(U_0)), where U_0 is a position where V(U) has a minimum or M(U_0) diverges.
* The heavy quark potential is defined via the rectangular Wilson loop in the limit of large temporal extent T, with the expectation value behaving as exp(i T V_QQbar(L,T)).

## Context

This work extends the method of Noronha and Dumitru (2009) for computing the thermal width of heavy quarkonia from holography, providing a more general derivation and proving a connection to the area law of Wilson loops in confining gauge theories. The authors compute the imaginary part of the potential in Gauss-Bonnet gravity, which goes beyond the standard N=4 Super Yang-Mills plasma dual to Einstein gravity, enabling estimates of how the thermal width changes with the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio. This enables future studies of heavy quarkonium suppression in strongly coupled plasmas with different transport properties, and may be useful for investigating the imaginary part of the heavy quark potential in confining gauge theories dual to gravity.
