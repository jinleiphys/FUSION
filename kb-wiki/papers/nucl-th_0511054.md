# Towards a field theoretic understanding of NN->NNpi

> We study the production amplitude for the reaction NN->NNpi up to next--to--leading order in chiral perturbation theory using a counting scheme that takes into account the large scale introduced by the initial momentum. In particular we investigate a subtlety that arises once the leading loop contributions are convoluted with the NN wavefunctions as demanded by the non--perturbative nature of the NN interaction. We show how to properly identify the irreducible contribution of loop diagrams in such type of reaction. The net effect of the inclusion of all next-to-leading order loops is to enhance the leading rescattering amplitude by a factor of 4/3, bringing its contribution to the cross section for pp->dpi+ close to the experimental value.

## Key claim

The net effect of including all next-to-leading order (NLO) loop contributions in chiral perturbation theory for the reaction NN→NNπ is to enhance the leading rescattering amplitude by a factor of 4/3, bringing its contribution to the cross section for pp→dπ⁺ close to the experimental value, but only after properly identifying and isolating the irreducible pieces of loop diagrams that would otherwise produce an unphysical linear growth with final NN relative momentum.

## Method

The authors evaluated the production amplitude for NN→NNπ up to NLO in chiral perturbation theory using a modified counting scheme that accounts for the large momentum scale p = √(m_π(M+m_π/4)) ≈ 362 MeV introduced by the initial nucleon momentum. They computed the leading loop diagrams (one-pion exchange with Weinberg-Tomozawa vertex, triangle diagrams, and crossed-box diagrams) and showed that the energy-dependent part of the Weinberg-Tomozawa vertex cancels one intermediate nucleon propagator, separating each diagram into an irreducible piece (which cancels with other loops) and a reducible piece (which is absorbed into the NN wavefunctions). The transition operators were then convoluted with NN wavefunctions from the CD-Bonn potential to compute the s-wave cross section parameter α for pp→dπ⁺.

## Key numbers

- Initial nucleon center-of-mass momentum required to produce a pion at rest: |p| = √(m_π(M+m_π/4)) = 362 MeV, using M = 939 MeV and m_π = 139.6 MeV
- Delta-nucleon mass difference: Δ = 293 MeV, comparable to the external momentum scale
- Leading order (LO) s-wave cross section parameter for pp→dπ⁺: α^LO = 131 μb (full result including direct term)
- Leading order rescattering-only s-wave parameter: α^{WT, LO} = 101 μb
- Next-to-leading order (NLO) rescattering-only s-wave parameter: α^{WT, NLO} = 182 μb (obtained by scaling the LO rescattering result by (4/3)²)
- Full NLO s-wave cross section parameter including direct term: α^{NLO} = 220 μb
- The sum of all irreducible NLO loops cancels exactly in both the A₁₁ channel (pp→ppπ⁰) and the A₁₀ channel (pp→dπ⁺), with the cancellation expressed as: A₁₀^{1a1(irr)+1a2(irr)+1b+1c+1d} = 0 and A₁₁^{1a1(irr)+1a2(irr)+1b+1c+1d} = 0

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of NN→NNπ beyond the leading-order calculations of Park et al. (1996) and the earlier NLO studies by the same authors (2004), which left unresolved the cancellation mechanism for pp→ppπ⁰ and the linear growth of loop amplitudes with final NN momentum. The paper resolves both issues by showing that the irreducible pieces of the Weinberg-Tomozawa rescattering diagrams cancel exactly with the other NLO loop contributions, while the reducible pieces correspond to putting the πN→πN vertex on-shell. This result contradicts the proposal by Anderstalk et al. (2005) to introduce a new leading-order counterterm, which the authors show is forbidden by chiral symmetry. The work enables a consistent NLO calculation of pion production cross sections that can be systematically improved, though a complete NLO calculation still requires evaluation of the Δ-isobar contribution (diagram (c) of Fig. 3) with a chiral NN→NΔ transition potential.
