# Potential model with bound-to-continuum approach for low-energy nucleon radiative capture by $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O

> The nucleon radiative capture reactions are important in pure and applied nuclear physics, especially in nuclear astrophysics. The keV-nucleon radiative capture reactions are studied with $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O targets using the bound-to-continuum potential model in which both scattering and bound states are treated simultaneously and based on the Skyrme Hartree-Fock approximation. The obtained results are shown to be in good agreement with the available experimental data. Alongside astrophysical aspects, the nuclear structure features were revisited for enlarging the prospect of adopting the nucleon radiative capture processes as a spectroscopic tool.

## Key claim

The bound-to-continuum potential model, based on a single Skyrme Hartree-Fock calculation with only two adjustable parameters (λ_b and λ_s) for the central potential depths, can simultaneously reproduce experimental cross-sections for both (p,γ) and (n,γ) reactions on ¹²C and ¹⁶O at keV energies, but the spectroscopic factors S_F must be treated as empirical values that deviate from unity for ¹²C due to core-polarization effects not captured by the mean-field approach.

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme Hartree-Fock equations using the SLy4 interaction to generate both bound and scattering wave functions from a single mean-field potential. For bound states, the well-depth method was applied by scaling the central potential with λ_b to match the experimental nucleon separation energy; for scattering states, λ_s was adjusted to reproduce the position of a low-lying resonance (0.42 MeV for ¹²C(p,γ)). The electric dipole (E1) capture cross-section was computed from the radial overlap integral of the scattering and bound wave functions, multiplied by angular-spin coefficients and an empirical spectroscopic factor S_F.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁶O(p,γ)¹⁷F: λ_b = 0.88 for the 1d₅/₂ bound state (separation energy e_s = -0.60 MeV) and λ_b = 0.95 for the 2s₁/₂ bound state (e_s = -0.11 MeV); S_F = 1.00 for both transitions.
- For ¹⁶O(n,γ)¹⁷O: λ_b = 0.90 for the 1d₅/₂ bound state (e_s = -4.14 MeV) and λ_b = 1.00 for the 2s₁/₂ bound state (e_s = -3.89 MeV); S_F = 1.00 for both.
- For ¹²C(p,γ)¹³N: λ_s = 1.02 to reproduce the resonance at 0.42 MeV; λ_b = 0.81 for the 1p₁/₂ bound state (e_s = -1.94 MeV); S_F = 0.20.
- For ¹²C(n,γ)¹³C ground state (1/2⁻): λ_b = 0.83 (e_s = -4.95 MeV); S_F = 0.45.
- For ¹²C(n,γ)¹³C* first excited state (1/2⁺ at 3.09 MeV): λ_b = 1.11 (e_s = -3.02 MeV); S_F = 1.00.
- For ¹²C(n,γ)¹³C* (3/2⁻ at 3.69 MeV): two contributions; λ_b = 0.45 for 1p₃/₂ (e_s = -1.26 MeV, S_F = 0.25) and λ_b = 1.30 for 1d₃/₂ (e_s = -1.26 MeV, S_F = 0.95).
- For ¹²C(n,γ)¹³C* (5/2⁺ at 3.85 MeV): λ_b = 0.94 (e_s = -1.09 MeV); S_F = 0.55.
- A linear correlation was found: (1 - λ_b) = 0.035 × Δe/MeV, where Δe = e_s - ε_α.

## Context

This work extends the bound-to-continuum approach previously applied to ¹⁶O(p,γ) in Nguyen (2021) to simultaneously treat both (p,γ) and (n,γ) reactions on ¹²C and ¹⁶O within a single Skyrme Hartree-Fock framework. It contrasts with earlier phenomenological potential models using Woods-Saxon or Gaussian potentials (Ho (1983, 1991), Baye (2004), Huang (2010), Dubovichenko (2013), Xu (2013)) and with folding model approaches (Kitaoka (2002), Nguyen (2021)) that treated bound and scattering states separately. The results enable consistent microscopic predictions for nucleon capture cross-sections at astrophysical energies and suggest that radiative capture reactions can serve as a spectroscopic tool for studying nuclear structure, particularly for weakly bound states and core-polarization effects.
