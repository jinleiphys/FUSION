# The non-perturbative constraint on sea quarks --the strange sea quarks in the nucleon and the soft pion contribution at high energy--

> The mean charge sum rule for the light sea quarks in the nucleon which holds under the same theoretical footing as the modified Gottfried sum rule shows that a usual parameterization of the strange sea quark distribution underestimate its contribution in the small $x$ region. We give a discussion of the soft pion contribution at high energy as a possible explanation of the saturation of the sum rule and show that it naturally explains why the strange sea quark is suppressed in the region above $x \sim 0.01$ while it becomes abundant below it.

## Key claim

The mean charge sum rule for light sea quarks in the nucleon, which holds under the same theoretical footing as the modified Gottfried sum rule, shows that standard parameterizations of the strange sea quark distribution underestimate its contribution in the small \(x\) region; the soft pion contribution at high energy naturally explains why the strange sea quark is suppressed above \(x \sim 0.01\) but becomes abundant below it.

## Method

The author derives sum rules from current anticommutation relations on the null plane, using chiral \(SU(N)\otimes SU(N)\) flavor symmetry and the Deser-Gilbert-Sudarshan (DGS) representation. The mean charge sum rule for light sea quarks in the proton is evaluated numerically using experimentally determined integrals \(I_\pi\), \(I_K^p\), and \(I_K^n\). The soft pion contribution to the strange sea quark distribution is then calculated in an \(SU(4)\otimes SU(4)\) model, using parton distributions from MRS and GS at \(Q_0^2 = 4\ \text{GeV}^2\), with the soft pion phase space defined by transverse momentum \(|\vec{k}^\perp| \leq b m_\pi\) and Feynman scaling variable \(|x_F| \leq c\), taking \(b=1\) and \(c=0.1\).

## Key numbers

* The mean charge sum rule for light sea quarks in the proton evaluates to approximately \(0.2\), using \(I_\pi \sim 5.17\), \(I_K^p \sim 2.39\), and \(I_K^n \sim 1.61\).
* The modified Gottfried sum rule predicts the Gottfried sum as \(0.26 \pm 0.03\).
* The soft pion contribution explains about \(-0.03\) of the Gottfried defect (total defect about \(-0.07\)), corresponding to about \(40\%\).
* The soft pion contribution to the strange sea quark becomes very large below \(x \sim 0.01\) and is suppressed above it.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous sum-rule analyses (Koretune, 1993, 1995) based on current anticommutation relations on the null plane, which had already explained the Gottfried sum model-independently. The soft pion contribution method follows the approach of Sakai and Yamada, and the calculation is compared with experimental data from the Harvard-Cornell group. The result enables a resolution of the apparent violation of the mean charge sum rule by showing that the strange sea quark distribution becomes flavor-symmetric with the non-strange sea in the very small \(x\) region, a region poorly constrained by existing phenomenological fits.
