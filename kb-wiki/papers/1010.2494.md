# Charmonium sum rules applied to a holographic model

> The heavy-quark QCD sum rules are applied to a model of charmonium based upon the gauge/gravity duality. We find that there is strong agreement between the moments of the polarization function calculated from the holographic model and the experimental data suggesting that the model is consistent with the heavy-quark QCD sum rules at zero temperature.

## Key claim

The holographic "shift and dip" model of charmonium is consistent with heavy-quark QCD sum rules at zero temperature, with agreement at the 80% level for the first moment and above 90% for higher moments, though the tower of excited states in the holographic model only marginally approximates the QCD continuum.

## Method

The author calculated the moments of the charm-quark polarization function from the shift and dip holographic model by solving the 5D gauge field equations of motion in a confining background with a piecewise holographic potential. These moments were compared to moments derived from experimental spectroscopic data for charmonium states (J/ψ, ψ', and a continuum above the D\(\bar{D}\) threshold) and to moments from perturbative QCD at leading order with αs corrections. The analysis used the ratio of consecutive moments and the ratio of holographic to experimental moments for n=1 through n=5.

## Key numbers

- For the shift and dip model (Model A), the ratio of holographic to experimental moments: \(\mathscr{M}_1^A/\mathscr{M}_1^{\rm Expt} = 0.79\), \(\mathscr{M}_2^A/\mathscr{M}_2^{\rm Expt} = 0.91\), \(\mathscr{M}_3^A/\mathscr{M}_3^{\rm Expt} = 0.96\), \(\mathscr{M}_4^A/\mathscr{M}_4^{\rm Expt} = 0.98\), \(\mathscr{M}_5^A/\mathscr{M}_5^{\rm Expt} = 0.99\).
- For the rescaled ρ model (Model B), the corresponding ratios: 0.54, 0.57, 0.59, 0.60, 0.61.
- Shift and dip model parameters: \(a = 0.970\ {\rm GeV}\), \(c = 2.781\ {\rm GeV}\), \(\alpha = 1.876\ {\rm GeV}\), \(z_d^{-1} = 2.211\ {\rm GeV}\).
- The polarization function from the shift and dip model agrees with QCD within 21% for \(Q^2 < 1\ {\rm GeV}^2\) and within 26% up to \(Q^2 = 10\ {\rm GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work applies heavy-quark QCD sum rules, originally developed by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (SVZ) (1978, 1979), to a holographic charmonium model constructed by Grigoryan (2010) that exactly reproduces the masses and decay constants of J/ψ and ψ'. It is the first application of heavy-quark QCD sum rules to a holographic model, extending earlier light-quark holographic analyses that used the operator product expansion (OPE) to constrain models such as those by Erlich (2005) and Da Rold (2005). The results enable future holographic models of heavy-quark systems to be tested against sum-rule constraints and suggest that improving the first-moment agreement requires adjusting the excited-state spectrum.
