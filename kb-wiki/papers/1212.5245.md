# Pion dissociation and Levinson's theorem in hot PNJL quark matter

> Pion dissociation by the Mott effect in quark plasma is described within the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck approach on the basis of the PNJL model which allows for a unified description of bound, resonant and scattering states. As a first approximation, we utilize the Breit-Wigner ansatz for the spectral function and clarify its relation to the complex mass pole solution of the pion Bethe-Salpeter equation. Application of the Levinson theorem proves that describing the pion Mott dissociation solely by means of spectral broadening of the pion bound state beyond T_Mott leaves out a significant aspect. Thus we acknowledge the importance of the continuum of scattering states and show its role for the thermodynamics of pion dissociation.

## Key claim

Describing pion Mott dissociation solely by spectral broadening of the bound state beyond the Mott temperature violates Levinson's theorem; the continuum of scattering states must be included to correctly account for the thermodynamics, because the vanishing bound state is exactly compensated by a resonance in the continuum.

## Method

The authors use the Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model at finite temperature to compute the thermodynamic potential in a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck form. They solve coupled gap equations for the constituent quark mass and Polyakov loop, then evaluate mesonic correlations via Bethe-Salpeter equations for pion and sigma masses and spectral widths. The scattering phase shift is decomposed into a resonant part (described by a Breit-Wigner ansatz above the Mott temperature) and a scattering continuum part, with the total phase shift constrained by Levinson's theorem.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass at zero temperature: \(M_\pi = 135\) MeV (input parameter)
- Pion decay constant: \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV (input parameter)
- Quark condensate: \(\langle \overline{q} q\rangle^{1/3} = -240.772\) MeV (input parameter)
- Constituent quark mass at zero temperature: \(m(0) = 367.5\) MeV
- Critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration in the chiral limit: \(T_c = 237\) MeV
- Critical temperature for finite current quark mass: \(T_c = 251\) MeV
- Mott temperature in the chiral limit: \(T_{\rm Mott} \simeq 231\) MeV
- Mott temperature away from the chiral limit: \(T_{\rm Mott} \simeq 256\) MeV
- Model parameters: current quark mass \(m_0 = 5.495\) MeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 602.472\) MeV, coupling \(G_S\Lambda^2 = 2.318\)

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model studies of pion dissociation (Hufner, 1994; Zhuang, 1994) by incorporating the Polyakov loop to suppress unphysical quark degrees of freedom at low temperatures and to recover the Stefan-Boltzmann limit at high temperatures. It improves on the work of Rossner (2007) by properly applying Levinson's theorem within the PNJL framework. The results enable a generalized PNJL-hadron resonance gas model that embodies Mott dissociation of hadrons, as suggested by Turko (2011).
