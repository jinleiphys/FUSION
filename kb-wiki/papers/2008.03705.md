# Hadronic effects on charmonium elliptic flows in heavy-ion collisions

> Transport and Langevin equations are employed to study hadronic medium effects on charmonium elliptic flows in heavy-ion collisions. In Pb-Pb collisions, the anisotropic energy density of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) in the transverse plane is transformed into hadron momentum anisotropy after the phase transition. Charmonia with high transverse momentum $p_T$ are produced via the primordial hard process and undergo different degrees of dissociation along different paths in the QGP. They then scatter with light hadrons in the hadron phase. Both contributions to the charmonium elliptic flows are studied at moderate and high transverse momenta. The elliptic flows of the prompt $J/ψ$ are found to be considerably enhanced at high transverse momentum when the charmonium diffusion coefficients in the hadronic medium are parametrized through the geometry scale approximation. This hadronic medium effect is negligible for quarkonia with larger mass such as bottomonia.

# Hadronic effects on charmonium elliptic flows in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The elliptic flow ($v_2$) of prompt $J/\psi$ at high transverse momentum is considerably enhanced by hadronic medium effects when charmonium diffusion coefficients are parametrized through the geometry scale approximation; this enhancement is negligible for bottomonia due to their larger mass and smaller geometry size, and the effect depends strongly on the assumed value of the spatial diffusion coefficient.

## Method

The authors employ a Boltzmann-type transport model to evolve charmonia through the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), where dissociation rates are calculated via operator product expansion for $J/\psi$ and via a geometry scale for excited states ($\chi_c$, $\psi^\prime$). After hadronization, charmonium trajectories in the hadronic medium are treated as Brownian motions described by a classical Langevin equation with drag and noise terms, where the spatial diffusion coefficients $\mathcal{D}_s^{\psi}$ for different charmonium states are obtained from the D meson diffusion coefficient $\mathcal{D}_s^D$ scaled by the ratio of squared charge radii. The QGP and hadronic medium evolution is simulated using $(2+1)$-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics with a first-order phase transition at $T_c=165$ MeV.

## Key numbers

- The $J/\psi$ prompt differential cross section at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV is taken to be $3.25\ \mu b$ in the forward rapidity region.
- The D meson spatial diffusion coefficient is taken as $\mathcal{D}_s^D(2\pi T)=8$ in the temperature region $0.8T_c < T < T_c$.
- The charge radius of the D meson is approximated as $\langle r_D \rangle = 0.41$ fm; the radii of the charmonium ground state and excited states ($\chi_c$, $\psi^\prime$) are 0.5 fm and 0.72 fm, respectively.
- In 40-60% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, hadronic effects increase $v_2^{J/\psi}$ from $\sim 2\%$ to $\sim 6\%$ at $p_T \sim 6$ GeV/c when using the geometry scale approximation.
- In 20-40% centrality, hadronic effects increase the elliptic flow of primordial $J/\psi$ from $\sim 2.5\%$ to $\sim 5\%$.
- The critical temperature for the phase transition is $T_c=165$ MeV; the initial maximum QGP temperature is fixed at $450$ MeV in the forward rapidity $2.5<y<4$.
- The kinetic freeze-out temperature is $T_{\rm kin}=0.8T_c$.

## Context

This work extends prior transport model studies of charmonium in heavy-ion collisions (Chen 2018) by adding hadronic medium effects that were previously neglected in analyses of the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ at LHC energies. The authors note that earlier theoretical calculations (Bhaduri 2018) found QGP path-length effects alone contribute only $\sim 2\%$ to $J/\psi$ elliptic flow at high $p_T$, which is considerably lower than experimental data from the ALICE collaboration (Acharya 2017). The study enables improved interpretation of charmonium collective flow data by showing that final-state interactions in the hadronic phase, rather than initial cold nuclear matter effects, can account for the observed difference between charmonium and bottomonium elliptic flows.
