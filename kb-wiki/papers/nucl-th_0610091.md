# The fractional symmetric rigid rotor

> Based on the Riemann fractional derivative the Casimir operators and multipletts for the fractional extension of the rotation group SO(n) are calculated algebraically. The spectrum of the corresponding fractional symmetric rigid rotor is discussed. It is shown, that the rotational, vibrational and $γ$-unstable limits of the standard geometric collective models are particular limits of this spectrum. A comparison with the ground state band spectra of nuclei shows an agreement with experimental data better than 2%. The derived results indicate, that the fractional symmetric rigid rotor is an appropriate tool for a description of low energy nuclear excitations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the fractional symmetric rigid rotor model, based on Riemann fractional derivatives of order α, can reproduce the ground state band spectra of even-even nuclei with an agreement better than 2% compared to experimental data, and that the rotational, vibrational, and γ-unstable limits of standard geometric collective models are particular cases of this fractional spectrum at specific α values.

## Method

The author algebraically calculates the Casimir operators and multiplets for a fractional extension of the rotation group SO(n) using the Riemann-Liouville fractional derivative of order α (0 ≤ α ≤ 1). The fractional angular momentum operators are defined via canonical quantization with fractional derivative operators, and the eigenvalues of the fractional symmetric rigid rotor Hamiltonian H = m₀ + A₀ Ĵ²(α) are derived analytically. The model is then fitted to experimental ground state band energies of even-even nuclei using a three-parameter formula (α, a₀, m₀) with J restricted to even non-negative integers.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁵⁶Gd: α = 0.863, a₀ = 31.90 keV, m₀ = -43.65 keV, J_max = 14, ΔE = 2.23%
- For ¹⁹⁶Pt: α = 0.710, a₀ = 175.06 keV, m₀ = -83.69 keV, J_max = 10, ΔE = 0.44%
- For ¹¹⁰Cd: α = 0.570, a₀ = 607.91 keV, m₀ = -405.80 keV, J_max = 6, ΔE = 0%
- For ²¹⁸Po: α = 0.345, a₀ = 1035.69 keV, m₀ = -671.03 keV, J_max = 8, ΔE = 0.12%
- For ¹⁷⁶Os: α = 0.808, a₀ = 51.231 keV, m₀ = -23.064 keV, J_max = 18, ΔE = 1.22%
- The rotational limit corresponds to α = 1, giving E = A₀ ħ² J(J+1)
- The vibrational limit corresponds to α = 1/2, giving E = m₀ + A₀ ħ² (τ + 1/2) with τ = J/2
- The γ-unstable Davidson potential limit corresponds to α ≈ 0.66
- The linear potential limit corresponds to α ≈ 0.33

## Context

This work extends the concept of fractional calculus, previously applied to anomalous diffusion and fractional wave equations (Raspini, Baleanu), into nuclear structure theory for the first time. The fractional symmetric rigid rotor unifies the rotational, vibrational, and γ-unstable limits of the Bohr-Mottelson geometric collective model (Bohr, Eisenberg and Greiner) as special cases at different α values, and also includes the linear potential model proposed by Fortunato (2005). The approach enables a smooth description of nuclear ground state band spectra across the transition from rotational to vibrational behavior without requiring the extensive numerical calculations of models like the Gneuss-Greiner model or the interacting boson model (IBA).
