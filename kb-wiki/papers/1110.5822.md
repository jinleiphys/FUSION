# Alpha Decay Processes under a Diproton, Dineutron and Deuteron Pairing Scheme

> In this paper we are able to justify the existence or no of photons emitted during $α$ decay, based on the average half life times using the BCS superconductivity and superfluidity theories. The proposed model suggests two possible channels. A first channel where the protons and neutrons interact separately through a spin coupling, giving rise to diprotons and dineutrons which in turn interact through an isospin coupling to form an $α$ particle, and a second channel where the protons and neutrons are treated as nucleons that interact through an isospin coupling, giving rise to deuterons, which in turn interact through a spin coupling to form an $α$ particle. Due to the nature of the involved particles, the systems considered are homogeneous. For the second channel, the photon play a very important role for the possible escape of the $α$ particle. Within these premises the half life times of a considerable number of heavy nuclei with spherical symmetry can be estimated with good experimental agreement.

## Key claim

The paper claims that alpha decay half-lives for heavy spherical nuclei can be estimated using a BCS-based pairing model with two possible formation channels, but the model's success depends on adjusting the nucleon occupation numbers to reproduce experimental data rather than predicting them from first principles.

## Method

The authors model alpha particle formation inside the nucleus using BCS superconductivity and superfluidity theories, treating nucleons as quasiparticles in a Landau Fermi liquid framework. They propose two channels: one where protons pair into diprotons and neutrons into dineutrons via spin coupling, which then combine through isospin coupling to form an alpha particle; and another where protons and neutrons first form deuterons through isospin coupling, which then pair through spin coupling. The half-life is computed from the imaginary part of the excitation energy combined with a probability factor for the alpha particle to escape the nucleus.

## Context

This work extends previous semiclassical models of bremsstrahlung emission during alpha decay (D'Arrigo 1994, Boie 2007, Jentschura 2008) and fully quantum approaches (Batkin, Papenbrock 1998) by applying BCS pairing theory to the alpha formation process. The model enables calculation of half-lives for even-even spherical nuclei and provides a framework for understanding when photons accompany alpha decay, specifically in the deuteron-pairing channel where the alpha particle requires a photon to carry away spin. The authors note that their calculated nucleon occupation numbers do not match those obtained from theory, indicating the need for better estimates of nucleon energies within different nuclei.
