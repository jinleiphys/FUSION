# Quantum Phase Transitions in a Finite System

> A general procedure for studying finite-N effects in quantum phase transitions of finite systems is presented and applied to the critical-point dynamics of nuclei undergoing a shape-phase transition of second-order (continuous), and of first-order with an arbitrary barrier.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic-oriented procedure for studying finite-N effects in quantum phase transitions of finite systems, applied to nuclei undergoing shape-phase transitions; the key hedge is that the method is "approximate" and relies on projection techniques that capture the essential features of critical-point dynamics but do not exactly reproduce the full numerical diagonalization.

## Method

The author employs the interacting boson model (IBM) with N bosons and constructs intrinsic energy surfaces from coherent states. For second-order (U(5)-O(6)) and first-order (U(5)-SU(3)) phase transitions, the method projects states of good O(5) or O(3) symmetry from the coherent state, defines projected energy surfaces, and uses the global minimum of these surfaces to obtain analytic estimates for excitation energies and B(E2) values. For first-order transitions with an arbitrary barrier, the Hamiltonian is decomposed into intrinsic and collective parts, and a 2x2 potential energy matrix is diagonalized to account for mixing between spherical and deformed L=0 states.

## Key numbers

- For the second-order (E(5)-type) critical point with N=5: the O(5) projected energy surface for the ground band has its global minimum at β² = 0.314.
- For the second-order case with N=5: the exact IBM calculation gives E(4⁺₁,₂) = 2.195 (in units of E(2⁺₁,₁)=1), compared to the E(5) model value of 2.20 and experimental data for ¹³⁴Ba of 2.32.
- For the second-order case with N=5: the exact IBM B(E2; 4⁺₁,₂ → 2⁺₁,₁) = 1.38 (in units of B(E2; 2⁺₁,₁ → 0⁺₁,₀)=1), compared to the E(5) model value of 1.68 and experimental 1.56(18).
- For the first-order (X(5)-type) critical point with N=10: the lowest L=0 eigenpotential has its global minimum at β = 0.591.
- For the first-order case with N=10: the exact IBM calculation gives E(4⁺₁) = 2.43 (in units of E(2⁺₁)=1), compared to the X(5) model value of 2.91 and experimental data for ¹⁵²Sm of 3.01.
- For the first-order case with N=10: the exact IBM B(E2; 4⁺₁ → 2⁺₁) = 1.61 (in units of B(E2; 2⁺₁ → 0⁺₁)=1), compared to the X(5) model value of 1.58 and experimental 1.45.
- For the general first-order transition with high barrier (β₀=1.3, N=10): the c₃/h₂=0.05 term gives E(4⁺₁) = 3.32, close to the rigid-rotor value of 3.33.
- For the general first-order transition with high barrier (β₀=1.3, N=10): the c₆/h₂=0.05 term gives E(4⁺₁) = 2.81, close to the X(5) value of 2.91.

## Context

This work extends the finite-N analytic approach to quantum phase transitions in nuclei previously studied numerically by several groups (references 5-8 in the paper) and builds on the author's earlier work (Leviatan 2003, 2005). The method provides analytic estimates that capture the essential features of the E(5) critical-point model of Iachello (2000) and the X(5) model of Iachello (2001), enabling systematic study of finite-N effects in both second-order and first-order shape-phase transitions, including cases with arbitrary barrier heights.
