# Quarkonium as relativistic bound state on the light front

> We study charmonium and bottomonium as relativistic bound states in a light-front quantized Hamiltonian formalism. The effective Hamiltonian is based on light-front holography. We use a recently proposed longitudinal confinement to complete the soft-wall holographic potential for the heavy flavors. The spin structure is generated from the one-gluon exchange interaction with a running coupling. The adoption of asymptotic freedom improves the spectroscopy compared with previous light-front results. Within this model, we compute the mass spectroscopy, decay constants and the r.m.s. radii. We also present a detailed study of the obtained light-front wave functions and use the wave functions to compute the light-cone distributions, specifically the distribution amplitudes and parton distribution functions. Overall, our model provides a reasonable description of the heavy quarkonia.

## Key claim

The authors claim that incorporating a running coupling (asymptotic freedom) into the light-front holographic Hamiltonian for heavy quarkonia improves the spectroscopy compared to previous fixed-coupling results, but the model remains phenomenological and the rotational symmetry is only approximate, as evidenced by mass spreads across different magnetic projections.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective light-front Hamiltonian combining a transverse "soft-wall" holographic confining potential, a longitudinal confining potential, and a one-gluon exchange interaction with a running strong coupling. They solved the eigenvalue equation using a basis function approach (Basis Light-Front Quantization, BLFQ) with transverse harmonic oscillator and longitudinal Jacobi polynomial basis states, truncating the basis by quantum number cutoffs \(N_{\max}\) and \(L_{\max}\). The model parameters (confining strength \(\kappa\) and quark masses \(m_c\), \(m_b\)) were fitted to experimental mass eigenvalues from the Particle Data Group for states below the open flavor thresholds.

## Key numbers

* Charmonium r.m.s. mass deviation from PDG data: 31 MeV at \(N_{\max}=L_{\max}=32\)
* Bottomonium r.m.s. mass deviation from PDG data: 38 MeV at \(N_{\max}=L_{\max}=32\)
* Mean mass spread (rotational symmetry violation) for charmonium: 17 MeV; for bottomonium: 8 MeV
* Confining strength \(\kappa = 0.966\) GeV for charmonium, \(\kappa = 1.389\) GeV for bottomonium
* Effective quark masses: \(m_c = 1.603\) GeV, \(m_b = 4.902\) GeV
* Fixed \(\alpha_s(0) = 0.6\) with \(\mu_{\text{ir}} = 0.55\) GeV for \(N_f=4\)
* Regularization mass \(\mu_g = 0.02\) GeV for the Coulomb singularity

## Context

This work extends the light-front holographic QCD approach of Brodsky and de Teramond (2014) by adding longitudinal confinement and a running coupling to the one-gluon exchange interaction, improving upon the fixed-coupling results of Li, Maris, and Vary (2015). The model enables computation of mass spectroscopy, decay constants, radii, and light-cone distributions (distribution amplitudes and parton distribution functions) for heavy quarkonia, and is competitive with other relativistic bound-state models such as those by Crater (2010), McNeile (2012), and Hilger (2014).
