# Selected results on hadron structure using state-of-the-art lattice QCD simulations

> We review progress on hadron structure using lattice QCD simulations at or near to physical values of the QCD parameters. In particular, we discuss recent results on hadron masses, the nucleon charges, spin, gluon and quark unpolarized moments, the axial charge of hyperons, and the pion unpolarized moment.

## Key claim

Lattice QCD simulations at or near physical quark masses now reproduce benchmark quantities like the nucleon axial charge, but systematic uncertainties from finite volume, lattice spacing, and excited-state contamination still require careful investigation, especially for quantities beyond hadron masses.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD results from multiple collaborations using various fermion discretizations (Wilson clover-improved, domain wall, twisted mass, staggered) with pion masses at or near the physical value and lattice spacings below 0.1 fm. Hadron masses are extracted from two-point Euclidean correlation functions, while matrix elements for charges, moments, and spin are obtained from three-point functions by constructing ratios that plateau at large Euclidean time separations. Disconnected quark loop contributions are included for spin and gluon observables using high-statistics ensembles.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon axial charge \(g_A\) from physical-pion-mass simulations agrees with the experimental value (previously underestimated at larger pion masses)
- Isovector tensor charge \(g_T^{u-d}\) from global analysis: \(0.54^{+0.30}_{-0.13}\) (HERMES, COMPASS, Belle) or \(0.81(44)\) (COMPASS, Belle reanalysis)
- Gluon unpolarized moment \(\langle x\rangle_g = 0.282(39)\) at \(\mu = 2\) GeV in \(\overline{\rm MS}\) from a physical ensemble (\(m_\pi = 132\) MeV, \(a = 0.093\) fm, \(\sim 155,800\) statistics)
- Nucleon spin decomposition at physical point: \(J^{u+d} = 0.273(22)\), \(\frac{1}{2}\Delta\Sigma^{u+d} = 0.229(20)\), \(\frac{1}{2}\Delta\Sigma^{u+d+s} = 0.211(21)\) including disconnected contributions
- SU(3) breaking parameter \(\delta_{\rm SU(3)}\) for octet baryons is about 10%–15% at the physical point
- Scalar charge extraction requires source-sink separation \(t_s - t_0 \gtrsim 1.5\) fm for convergence

## Context

This review extends the lattice QCD program from hadron masses (well-studied by PACS, BMW, MILC, QCDSF, CLS, RBC-UKQCD, ETMC) to structure observables including nucleon charges, spin, and parton distribution moments. The work enables direct comparison with experimental measurements from JLab, COMPASS, and Belle, and provides input for chiral Lagrangians and nuclear physics. New techniques for computing parton distribution functions via quasi-distributions (Ji 2013) and for resonances and ab initio nuclear physics (Savage 2015) are under development.
