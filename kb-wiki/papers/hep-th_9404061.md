# Second Order Derivative Supersymmetry and Scattering Problem

> Extensions of standard one-dimensional supersymmetric quantum mechanics are discussed. Supercharges involving higher order derivatives are introduced leading to an algebra which incorporates a higher order polynomial in the Hamiltonian. We study scattering amplitudes for that problem. We also study the role of a dilatation of the spatial coordinate leading to a q-deformed supersymmetric algebra. An explicit model for the scattering amplitude is constructed in terms of a hypergeometric function which corresponds to a reflectionless potential with infinitely many bound states.

## Key claim

The paper extends standard one-dimensional supersymmetric quantum mechanics (SSQM) by introducing supercharges that are second-order differential operators, leading to a polynomial superalgebra where the anticommutator of supercharges equals a quadratic polynomial in the Hamiltonian. The authors show that the Witten criterion for spontaneous supersymmetry breaking is not applicable to these higher-derivative systems, and they construct explicit scattering amplitudes for reflectionless potentials with infinitely many bound states.

## Method

The authors define second-order differential operators \(A^+ = (A^-)^\dagger = \partial^2 - 2f(x)\partial + b(x)\) as supercharges and derive the conditions for these operators to intertwine two Schrodinger Hamiltonians \(h^{(1)}\) and \(h^{(2)}\). They solve the intertwining relations to express the potentials \(V_{1,2}\) and the function \(b(x)\) solely in terms of \(f(x)\) and its derivatives, with the superalgebra taking the form \(\{Q^+, Q^-\} = (H - \alpha)^2 + d\) where \(\alpha\) and \(d\) are constants. The scattering problem is analyzed by relating the transmission and reflection coefficients of the partner Hamiltonians through the asymptotic values of the superpotential, and a \(q\)-deformed version of the algebra is introduced using a dilatation operator \(T_q f(x) = \sqrt{q} f(qx)\).

## Key numbers

- The superalgebra anticommutator is \(\{Q^+, Q^-\} = (H - \alpha)^2 + d\), where \(d = \beta - \alpha^2\) is a constant.
- For the shifted ladder case with \(c > 0\), the regularized Witten index is \(\Delta_W^{reg} = 1 - e^{-\gamma c}\), taking values between 0 and 1 for positive \(\gamma\).
- In the radial problem with \(\lambda = -1\), the parameter \(f_0\) takes values \(f_0 = -l_1 - 3/2\) for \(l_2 = l_1 + 2\) and \(f_0 = l_1 - 1/2\) for \(l_2 = l_1 - 2\) when \(l_1 \geq 2\).
- For the radial problem with \(\lambda = +1\) and \(d < 0\), the relation is \(f_0^2 = -d / [(2l+1)^2 + 4\gamma]\).
- In the \(q\)-deformed case, the spectra are related by \(E^{(1)} = q^2 E^{(2)}\).
- For the irreducible case with \(d > 0\) and equal asymptotics \(f_+ = f_- = f_\infty\), the transmission coefficients satisfy \(T^{(2)} = T^{(1)}\).
- For the radial case with \(d = -4\bar{f}_0^4 < 0\) and \(\lambda = -1\), the S-matrix relation is \(S_{l_2}^{(2)} = S_{l_1}^{(1)} (k - 2i\bar{f}_0)/(k + 2i\bar{f}_0)\).

## Context

This work extends the standard one-dimensional supersymmetric quantum mechanics introduced by Witten (1981) and the factorization method developed by Infeld and Hull. It builds upon the higher-derivative supersymmetric quantum mechanics (HSSQM) framework of Andrianov, Ioffe, and Spiridonov (1993) and the \(q\)-deformed SUSY approach of Spiridonov (1992). The paper enables the construction of new families of isospectral Hamiltonians that cannot be obtained by iterating standard Darboux transformations, and it provides a framework for generating reflectionless potentials with infinitely many bound states through self-similarity conditions on scattering amplitudes.
