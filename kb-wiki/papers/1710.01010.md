# Entropy and Multifractality in Relativistic Ion-Ion Collisions

> Entropy production in multiparticle systems is investigated by analysing the experimental data on ion-ion collision at AGS and SPS energies and comparing the findings with those reported earlier for hadron-hadron, hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions. It is observed that the entropy produced in limited and full phase space, when normalised to maximum rapidity exhibits a kind of scaling which is nicely supported by Monte Carlo model \hij. Using the Rényi's order-q information entropy, multifractal characteristics of particle production are examined in terms of Generalized dimensions, D$_{q}$. Nearly the same values of multifractal specific heat, \(c\) observed in hadronic and ion-ion collisions over a wide range of incident energies suggest that the quantity \(c\) may be used as a universal characteristic of multiparticle production in hadron-hadron, hadron-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

Entropy production in relativistic ion-ion collisions, when normalized to maximum rapidity, exhibits a scaling behavior that is independent of projectile and target mass as well as beam energy; the multifractal specific heat c is approximately 0.2 across hadron-hadron, hadron-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions over a wide energy range, suggesting it may be a universal characteristic of multiparticle production.

## Method

The authors analyzed six sets of experimental data from the EMU01 Collaboration on collisions of 16O, 28Si, 32S, and 197Au beams with AgBr nuclei in emulsion at AGS and SPS energies (10.6A to 200A GeV/c). Shannon entropy and Renyi's order-q information entropy were calculated from charged particle multiplicity distributions in pseudorapidity windows of varying width. Generalized dimensions Dq were extracted from the Renyi entropies normalized to maximum rapidity, and the multifractal specific heat c was obtained by fitting Dq versus ln(q)/(q-1) to a linear form. Results were compared with HIJING Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers

- Multifractal specific heat c = 0.20 ± 0.06 for 197Au-AgBr collisions at 10.6A GeV/c (experimental)
- Multifractal specific heat c = 0.22 ± 0.08 for 16O-AgBr collisions at 14.5A GeV/c (experimental)
- Multifractal specific heat c = 0.20 ± 0.07 for 28Si-AgBr collisions at 14.5A GeV/c (experimental)
- Multifractal specific heat c = 0.22 ± 0.07 for 16O-AgBr collisions at 60A GeV/c (experimental)
- Multifractal specific heat c = 0.20 ± 0.07 for 16O-AgBr collisions at 200A GeV/c (experimental)
- Multifractal specific heat c = 0.21 ± 0.06 for 32S-AgBr collisions at 200A GeV/c (experimental)
- Information dimension a ranges from 0.82 ± 0.08 to 0.92 ± 0.07 across all experimental data sets
- Entropy normalized to maximum rapidity saturates beyond Δη/Ym ∼ 0.5

## Context

This work extends earlier entropy scaling studies by Simak et al. (1988) in hadron-hadron collisions and by the authors' own previous work on nucleus-nucleus collisions (Ahmad et al., 2013). The multifractal specific heat values (c ∼ 0.2) are consistent with those reported by Bershadskii (1998, 1999) for 10.6A GeV/c 197Au-nucleus collisions and by Mukhopadhyay et al. (1993) for p-nucleus interactions at 200-800 GeV (c ∼ 0.25). The results enable the use of multifractal specific heat as a universal parameter for characterizing multiparticle production across all collision systems, and support the constant-specific-heat approximation for multiparticle production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
