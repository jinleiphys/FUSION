# SU(3)-flavour breaking in octet baryon masses and axial couplings

> The lightest baryon octet is studied within a covariant and confining Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. By solving the relativistic Faddeev equations including scalar and axialvector diquarks, we determine the masses and axial charges for ΔS = 0 transitions. For the latter the degree of violation of SU(3) symmetry arising because of the strange spectator quark(s) is found to be up to 10%.

## Key claim

The paper finds that SU(3)-flavour symmetry breaking in octet baryon axial charges, arising from the heavier strange spectator quark(s), reaches up to 10% in a covariant and confining Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, with the specific magnitude depending on the baryon and the diquark correlations involved.

## Method

The authors solve Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equations for the octet baryons within a three-flavour NJL model, using scalar and axialvector diquark correlations and employing proper-time regularization to simulate confinement. The static approximation is used for the quark exchange kernel, and the model parameters are constrained by reproducing the physical pion and rho meson masses, the pion decay constant, the nucleon and Xi masses, and the nucleon axial coupling. The axial charges for Delta S = 0 transitions are then computed from the sum of six Feynman diagrams representing quark and diquark contributions, including scalar-to-axialvector diquark transition vertices.

## Key numbers

- Dressed light quark mass M_ell = 0.40 GeV; dressed strange quark mass M_s = 0.59 GeV, giving a dressed quark mass ratio M_s/M_q approximately 1.5
- Octet baryon masses from the model: M_N = 0.940 GeV, M_Lambda = 1.120 GeV, M_Sigma = 1.234 GeV, M_Xi = 1.318 GeV (experimental comparison: M_Lambda = 1.116 GeV, M_Sigma = 1.193 GeV, M_Xi = 1.318 GeV)
- Diquark masses: M_[ell ell] = 0.68 GeV, M_[ell s] = 0.85 GeV, M_{ell ell} = 1.04 GeV, M_{ell s} = 1.17 GeV, M_{ss} = 1.30 GeV
- Axialvector diquark axial couplings: g_A^{ell ell} = -0.216, g_A^{underline ell s} = -0.194, g_A^{ell underline s} = -0.213, g_A^{ss} = -0.194
- Spin fractions: Delta u_n = 1.145, Delta d_n = 0.331, Delta d_Sigma- = -1.048, Delta d_Xi- = 0.313

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark model and MIT bag model studies of octet baryons (Le Yaouanc 1976, Isgur 1979, Chodos 1974) by using a covariant framework that respects QCD symmetries, and complements lattice QCD calculations of octet baryon masses (Durr 2008, Young 2009, Walker-Loud 2008, Aoki 2008) and electroweak matrix elements (Lin 2007). The results enable future investigations of hyperon electromagnetic and axial form factors as functions of momentum transfer, and provide a covariant basis for calculating parton distribution functions across the baryon octet.
