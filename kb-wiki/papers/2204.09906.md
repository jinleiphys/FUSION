# The Self-energy of Nucleon for the Pion-nucleon Scattering Parameter

> The scattering amplitude of the pion-nucleon elastic scattering is calculated by the lowest-order approximation of the perturbative expansion with the non-perturbative term. The self-energy of nucleon is determined so as to give the scattering parameters of the $S$-wave. It is shown that the scattering process mediated by the $σ$-meson plays a decisive role together with the pseudovector pion interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the low-energy pion-nucleon scattering parameters can be reproduced by determining the nucleon self-energy from a lowest-order perturbative expansion that includes a non-perturbative term, but only when the exchange of a sigma-meson is also included; without the sigma-meson, the self-energy alone fails to match the experimental S-wave and P-wave data.

## Method

The author calculates the pion-nucleon elastic scattering amplitude using the lowest-order approximation of perturbative expansion with a non-perturbative term, employing the pseudovector (PV) pion-nucleon coupling. The nucleon self-energy is expanded in a power series around the on-shell condition, and the unknown expansion coefficients are determined by solving a linear matrix equation that forces the calculated S-wave scattering parameters to match experimental values. The calculation is performed both with and without the inclusion of a sigma-meson exchange interaction, using standard values for the nucleon mass (0.939 GeV), pion mass (0.138 GeV), and coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- The self-energy coefficients determined with the sigma-meson interaction are: \(c = -0.39\), \(c_2^{(1)} = 0.28\), \(c_1^{(2)} = -8.39\), and \(c_2^{(2)} = 5.59\) (dimensionless, from matrix inversion).
- With the sigma-meson interaction and \(g_{\sigma\pi\pi} = 7\), the calculated S-wave scattering length \(a_0^{(+)} = -0.022\) (nuclear units) compares to the experimental value \(-0.015 \pm 0.015\); \(a_0^{(-)} = 0.098\) compares to \(0.097^{+0.003}_{-0.007}\).
- Without the sigma-meson, the S-wave scattering length \(a_0^{(+)} = 0.325\) (nuclear units), far from the experimental value, and the self-energy coefficients are \(c = -1.03\), \(c_2^{(1)} = 0.64\), \(c_1^{(2)} = -1.32\), and \(c_2^{(2)} = 2.14\).
- Increasing the sigma-pi-pi coupling to \(g_{\sigma\pi\pi} = 12\) yields \(c = 0.07\) and \(c_2^{(1)} = 0.02\), much smaller than the \(g_{\sigma\pi\pi} = 7\) case.
- The D-wave scattering length \(a_{2-}^{1/2}\) (isospin 1/2) is \(-0.14 \times 10^{-2}\) without sigma and \(0.15 \times 10^{-2}\) with sigma, compared to the experimental \(0.24 \pm 0.04 \times 10^{-2}\) (nuclear units).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of nucleon self-energy models applied to electromagnetic interactions (Kinpara, no year given) and intermediate-energy pion-nucleon scattering, where a different self-energy model succeeded. It contrasts with the standard pseudoscalar (PS) interaction approach (Serot, no year given) by using the PV coupling with a non-perturbative term. The results show that the sigma-meson exchange, as used in nuclear structure calculations (Serot), plays a role similar to the self-energy in the lowest-order process, and the method enables future work to search for relations between these two terms and to improve the linearization procedure for solving the matrix equation.
