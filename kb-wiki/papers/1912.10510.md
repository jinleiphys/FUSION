# A Survey of Nuclear Pasta in the Intermediate Density Regime: Structure Functions for Neutrino Scattering

> Background: Nuclear pasta matter, emerging due to the competition between the long-range Coulomb force and the short-range strong force, is believed to be present in astrophysical scenarios, such as neutron stars and core-collapse supernovae. Its structure can have a high impact e.g. on neutrino transport or the tidal deformability of neutron stars. Purpose: We investigate the impact of nuclear pasta on neutrino interactions and compare the results to uniform matter. Method: We calculate the elastic and inelastic static structure factors for nuclear pasta matter using density functional theory (DFT), which contain the main nuclear input for neutrino scattering. Results: Each pasta structure leaves a unique imprint in the elastic structure factor and it is largely enhanced. The inelastic structure factors are very similar for all configurations. Conclusion: Nuclear pasta has a noticeable impact on neutrino neutral-current scattering opacities. While for inelastic reactions the cross section is reduced, the elastic coherent scattering increases dramatically. The effect can be of importance for the cooling of neutron stars as well as for core-collapse supernova models.

# A Survey of Nuclear Pasta in the Intermediate Density Regime: Structure Functions for Neutrino Scattering

## Key claim

Nuclear pasta matter leaves a unique, largely enhanced imprint in the elastic structure factor for each pasta shape, while inelastic structure factors are nearly identical across all configurations; however, the impact on neutrino opacity is significant only when the elastic coherent scattering channel is included, and the enhancement relative to uniform matter is lower than previously reported by Horowitz (2004) because the present work also accounts for the axial current contribution.

## Method

The authors performed density functional theory (DFT) calculations using the Skyrme functional TOV-min in a periodic box with the Sky3D code, generating nuclear pasta configurations based on minimal surfaces (slab, Primitive, Gyroid, Diamond, and double Gyroid network-like and surface-like). They computed elastic and inelastic static structure factors for neutrons and protons at zero and finite temperature, then used these to evaluate neutrino neutral-current scattering opacities for proton fractions X_P = 1/2, 1/3, and 1/10 at mean densities between 0.04 fm^{-3} and 0.08 fm^{-3}.

## Key numbers

- Mean density range studied: 0.04 fm^{-3} to 0.08 fm^{-3}, in steps of 0.01 fm^{-3}
- First peak in the angular-averaged elastic structure factor appears at approximately q = 0.31 fm^{-1} for all pasta configurations at rho = 0.04 fm^{-3} and X_P = 1/3
- For X_P = 1/10 at a temperature of 4 MeV, pasta matter enhances the mean neutrino opacity by a factor of about three compared to uniform matter
- The opacity enhancement for pasta becomes significant for neutrino energies larger than 25 to 30 MeV
- Box lengths for slab configurations increase for T = 2 MeV and decrease for higher temperatures

## Context

This work extends prior DFT studies of nuclear pasta configurations by Schuetrumpf (2019) to compute neutrino scattering structure factors, which had previously been studied only using molecular dynamics frameworks by Horowitz (2004), Horowitz (2004b), Sonoda (2007), and Nandi (2018). The results enable more accurate modeling of neutrino transport in neutron star cooling and core-collapse supernova simulations, though the authors note that full inclusion of both Pauli effects and higher many-particle correlations would require RPA calculations as in Pastore (2015), Bedaque (2018), or Dzhioev (2018), or Monte Carlo methods as in Shen (2018).
