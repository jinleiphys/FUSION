# Isospin Character of the Pygmy Dipole Resonance in 124Sn

> The pygmy dipole resonance has been studied in the proton-magic nucleus 124Sn with the (a,a'g) coincidence method at E=136 MeV. The comparison with results of photon-scattering experiments reveals a splitting into two components with different structure: one group of states which is excited in (a,a'g) as well as in (g,g') reactions and a group of states at higher energies which is only excited in (g,g') reactions. Calculations with the self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation and the quasiparticle phonon model are in qualitative agreement with the experimental results and predict a low-lying isoscalar component dominated by neutron-skin oscillations and a higher-lying more isovector component on the tail of the giant dipole resonance.

## Key claim

The pygmy dipole resonance in 124Sn splits into two distinct groups of 1- states: a low-energy component (below about 6.5 MeV) that is excited by both alpha scattering and photon scattering, and a higher-energy component that is only excited by photon scattering, indicating that the low-lying group represents a more isoscalar neutron-skin oscillation while the higher-lying group belongs to the isovector tail of the giant dipole resonance.

## Method

The experiment used the (alpha,alpha'gamma) coincidence method with a 136 MeV alpha beam on a 124Sn target at the AGOR cyclotron, detecting scattered alpha particles with the big-bite spectrometer and coincident gamma rays with an array of seven HPGe detectors. Ground-state decays were selected to isolate Jpi=1- states of the PDR, and the resulting differential alpha-scattering cross sections were compared to B(E1)upward strength distributions from previous nuclear resonance fluorescence experiments. Theoretical calculations were performed using the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) and the relativistic quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (RQTBA).

## Key numbers

- Alpha beam energy: 136 MeV
- Target thickness: 7.02 mg/cm^2 of 124Sn enriched to 96.96%
- BBS central angle: 3.5 degrees with angular acceptance of 1.5 to 5.5 degrees
- Momentum transfer q approximately 0.33 fm^-1
- HPGe detector energy resolution: 10-15 keV for gamma-ray energies between 4 MeV and 9 MeV
- PDR energy region studied: 5.5-9.0 MeV
- Splitting threshold in 124Sn: about 6.5 MeV (compared to about 6 MeV for 140Ce and 138Ba)
- RQTBA calculations shifted by about 600 keV toward higher energies relative to experimental and QPM spectra
- Two RQTBA states at 7.13 and 8.58 MeV have almost equal electromagnetic B(E1) but differ in isoscalar B(E1) by a factor of 4

## Context

This work extends previous observations of PDR splitting in the N=82 isotones 138Ba and 140Ce (Savran 2006, Endres 2009) to the Z=50 isotope 124Sn, demonstrating that the splitting into two groups of states is a general feature of the PDR rather than a unique phenomenon. The results contradict simpler interpretations of the PDR as a single mode and support calculations by Paar (2009) showing isoscalar-isovector splitting of higher-lying E1 strength. The findings enable future experiments using isoscalar and surface-sensitive probes such as (p,p'gamma) reactions to further constrain the structure of the PDR and its relationship to the neutron skin and symmetry energy.
