# Long-range correlations in proton-nucleus collisions in MC model with string fusion

> The study of long-range correlations between observables in two rapidity windows was proposed as a signature of the string fusion and percolation phenomenon. In the present work we calculate the correlation functions and coefficients for p-Pb collisions in the framework of the Monte Carlo string-parton model, based on the picture of elementary collisions of color dipoles. It describes pA and AA scattering without referring to the Glauber picture of independent nucleons collisions and includes effects of string fusion. Different types of correlations are considered: n-n, pt-n, pt-pt, where n is the event multiplicity of charged particles in a given rapidity window and pt is their mean transverse momentum. The results, obtained in the present work for p-Pb collisions, are compared with available experimental results for pt-n correlations in one window and further predictions are made.

## Key claim

The Monte Carlo string-parton model with string fusion can reproduce the qualitative features of long-range correlations in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the comparison with experimental data on pt-n correlations is limited by the experimental restriction on transverse momentum (0.15 < pt < 10 GeV/c) and possible short-range effects in a single rapidity window.

## Method

The authors developed a Monte Carlo string-parton model that describes proton-nucleus collisions at the partonic level without using the Glauber picture of independent nucleon collisions. Nucleons are composed of valence quark-diquark pairs and sea quark-antiquark pairs, with elementary interactions modeled as color dipole collisions. When two dipoles interact, quark-gluon strings are stretched between their ends, and string fusion occurs when string centers occupy the same cell in a transverse lattice, with fused strings producing modified multiplicity and transverse momentum according to scaling relations.

## Key numbers

- p-Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV
- Woods-Saxon parameters for lead nucleus: R = 6.63 fm, d = 0.545 fm
- Three parameter sets used: (r_str = 0 fm, r_0 = 0.6 fm, alpha_s = 0.4, r_max/r_0 = 0.9, mu_0 = 1.010); (r_str = 0.2 fm, r_0 = 0.6 fm, alpha_s = 0.5, r_max/r_0 = 0.4, mu_0 = 1.152); (r_str = 0.3 fm, r_0 = 0.6 fm, alpha_s = 0.6, r_max/r_0 = 0.2, mu_0 = 1.308)
- Mean charged multiplicity from a single string per rapidity unit: mu_0 (values given in parameter sets above)
- Mean transverse momentum from a single string: p_0 (cancels out in normalized variables)
- String transverse area: S_str = pi * r_str^2

## Context

This work extends the string fusion and percolation approach previously applied to pp and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies (Kovalenko and collaborators, 2013-2014) to p-Pb collisions. The model differs from the Glauber picture of independent nucleon collisions (Glauber, 1959) by treating collisions at the partonic level with color dipoles. The results are compared with ALICE experimental data on pt-n correlations (ALICE Collaboration, 2013), and the predictions for pt-pt correlations could provide an experimental test to constrain the transverse radius of quark-gluon strings, which remains a free parameter in the model.
