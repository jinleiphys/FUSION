# Nuclear Reaction Screening, Weak Interactions, and r-Process Nucleosynthesis in High Magnetic Fields

> Coulomb screening and weak interactions in a hot, magnetized plasma are investigated. Coulomb screening is evaluated in a relativistic thermal plasma in which electrons and positrons are in equilibrium. In addition to temperature effects, effects on weak screening from a strong external magnetic field are evaluated. In high fields, the electron transverse momentum components are quantized into Landau levels. The characteristic plasma screening length at high temperatures and at high magnetic fields is explored. In addition to changes to the screening length, changes in weak interaction rates are estimated. It is found that high fields can result in increased $β$-decay rates as the electron and positron spectra are dominated by Landau levels. Finally, the effects studied here are evaluated in a simple r-process model. It is found that relativistic Coulomb screening has a small effect on the final abundance distribution. While changes in weak interaction rates in strong magnetic fields can have an effect on the r-process evolution and abundance distribution, the field strength required to have a significant effect may be larger than what is currently thought to be typical of the r-process environment in collapsar jets or neutron star mergers. If r-process sites exist in fields $\gtrsim 10^{14}$ G effects from fields on weak decays could be significant.

## Key claim

Relativistic Coulomb screening in hot, magnetized plasmas has a small effect on the final r-process abundance distribution, and while strong magnetic fields can alter weak interaction rates and r-process evolution, the field strength required for a significant effect may be larger than what is currently thought typical of r-process environments in collapsar jets or neutron star mergers; effects from fields on weak decays could be significant only if r-process sites exist in fields greater than approximately 10^14 G.

## Method

The authors evaluated Coulomb screening in a relativistic thermal electron-positron plasma by solving the Poisson-Boltzmann equation with Fermi-Dirac statistics and Landau level quantization of electron transverse momentum in strong external magnetic fields. They derived the Thomas-Fermi screening length using an Euler-MacLaurin expansion in momentum for high-field approximations, and computed weak interaction rates (beta-minus, beta-plus, electron capture, and positron capture) with modified phase space integrals that account for Landau level quantization of lepton energy spectra. The screening and weak interaction effects were then applied to a simple r-process model in a magnetohydrodynamic collapsar jet environment.

## Key numbers

- At a density times electron fraction of ρY_e = 5×10^5 g/cm^3, the positron-electron ratio becomes significant at high temperatures, with relativistic effects becoming increasingly important as positron number density becomes a significant fraction of electron number density.
- For fields of 10^15 G, the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation is valid and convergence of the chemical potential is immediate; for 10^14 G, convergence occurs rapidly at N_max = 1.
- At ρY_e = 5×10^4 g cm^-3 and T_9 = 2, the electron chemical potential with no field would be 0.76 MeV, and above 10^13 G the chemical potential decreases with field.
- For the Euler-MacLaurin approximation with p_max = 5, the uncertainty in the Thomas-Fermi screening length is less than 1% at T_9 = 7 and T_9 = 2 at ρY_e = 5×10^4 g cm^-3.
- At B = 10^16 G using only the LLL approximation, and at B = 10^13 G using the lowest 2000 Landau levels with p_max = 12, the Thomas-Fermi screening length is within about 10% of the exact value over a wide range of temperatures and densities.
- For fields below roughly 10^13 G, the magnetic field effect on the electron-positron population becomes negligible.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Coulomb screening in astrophysical plasmas, including the classical Debye-Huckel and Thomas-Fermi treatments by Salpeter (1954), the extended screening formalism of Jancovici (1977) and Itoh (1977), and recent relativistic screening evaluations by Famiano (2016) and Luo (2020). The authors also build on studies of weak interactions in magnetized plasmas by Grasso (2001) and Luo (2020), and on electron capture studies by Itoh (2002) and Liu (2007). The results enable more accurate modeling of r-process nucleosynthesis in magnetized environments such as collapsar jets and neutron star mergers, and provide a computationally efficient approximation (Euler-MacLaurin expansion) for including magnetic field effects in astrophysical reaction network codes like MESA and Libnucnet.
