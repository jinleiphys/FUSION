# No-core shell model in an effective-field-theory framework

> We present a new approach to the construction of effective interactions suitable for many-body calculations by means of the no-core shell model (NCSM). We consider an effective field theory (EFT) with only nucleon fields directly in the NCSM model spaces. In leading order, we obtain the strengths of the three contact terms from the condition that in each model space the experimental ground-state energies of 2H, 3H and 4He be exactly reproduced. The first (0^+;0) excited state of 4He and the ground state of 6Li are then obtained by means of NCSM calculations in several spaces and frequencies. After we remove the harmonic-oscillator frequency dependence, we predict for 4He an energy level for the first (0^+;0) excited state in remarkable agreement with the experimental value. The corresponding 6Li binding energy is about 70% of the experimental value, consistent with the expansion parameter of the EFT.

# No-core shell model in an effective-field-theory framework

## Key claim

The authors present a new approach combining the no-core shell model (NCSM) with effective field theory (EFT) principles, where the effective interaction is constructed directly in the model space without unitary transformations. In leading order of the pionless EFT, the first excited state of 4He is predicted within 10% of experiment, while the 6Li ground-state energy reaches only about 70% of the experimental value, consistent with the expected leading-order error of the EFT.

## Method

The authors start with a pionless EFT Hamiltonian in a harmonic-oscillator (HO) NCSM basis, with three contact terms (two NN and one 3N) whose strengths are fixed by reproducing the experimental ground-state energies of 2H, 3H, and 4He in each model space. They perform NCSM calculations for 4He and 6Li in multiple model spaces defined by the HO frequency and the maximum HO quantum number Nmax, then extrapolate the results to the continuum limit (hbar-omega to zero) and to infinite ultraviolet cutoff Lambda to remove the HO frequency dependence.

## Key numbers

- First (0+;0) excited state of 4He: predicted energy 18.5 MeV after extrapolation, compared to experimental value 20.21 MeV (about 10% agreement at leading order)
- 6Li ground-state binding energy: estimated at about 22.6 MeV after extrapolation, compared to experimental value 31.99 MeV (about 70% of experimental value)
- Largest model space for 4He: Nmax=16; for 6Li: Nmax=8 (6 hbar-omega excitations above lowest configuration)
- Estimated Coulomb underbinding in 6Li: about 2 MeV, a 10% effect consistent with subleading orders

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach, previously applied to 2H (Rupak, 2000), 3H (triton EFT), and the 4He ground state (Platter, 2004), to excited states of 4He and the six-nucleon system 6Li for the first time. It contrasts with the conventional NCSM approach (Navratil, 2000; Navratil, 2001) that uses unitary transformations and cluster approximations starting from realistic NN and 3N forces, which remain dependent on the input interactions. The method enables systematic improvement through higher EFT orders and offers a path to consistent treatment of external operators, though scattering calculations in the HO basis remain under development.
