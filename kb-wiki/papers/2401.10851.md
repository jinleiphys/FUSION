# Using Bayesian Inference to Distinguish Neutrino Flavor Conversion Scenarios via a Prospective Supernova Neutrino Signal

> The upcoming galactic core-collapse supernova is expected to produce a considerable number of neutrino events within terrestrial detectors. By using Bayesian inference techniques, we address the feasibility of distinguishing among various neutrino flavor conversion scenarios in the supernova environment, using such a neutrino signal. In addition to the conventional MSW, we explore several more sophisticated flavor conversion scenarios, such as spectral swapping, fast flavor conversions, flavor equipartition caused by non-standard neutrino interactions, magnetically-induced flavor equilibration, and flavor equilibrium resulting from slow flavor conversions. Our analysis demonstrates that with a sufficiently large number of neutrino events during the supernova accretion phase (exceeding several hundreds), there exists a good probability of distinguishing among feasible neutrino flavor conversion scenarios in the supernova environment.

## Key claim
Using Bayesian inference on a prospective supernova neutrino signal, the authors demonstrate that with more than several hundred neutrino events during the accretion phase, there exists a good probability of distinguishing among different neutrino flavor conversion scenarios, but this capability depends on the number of observed events and the specific spectral profiles assumed.

## Method
The authors generated synthetic neutrino event sets for seven flavor conversion scenarios (no flavor conversions, MSW with normal and inverted hierarchy, spectral swapping, slow flavor conversions, fast flavor conversions, flavor equipartition from non-standard self-interactions, and magnetically-induced flavor conversion) using two representative supernova accretion-phase spectra (large spectral difference and small spectral difference). They computed Bayes factors comparing each pair of scenarios by evaluating the likelihood of observed event energies from inverse beta decay and elastic scattering channels in water Cherenkov detectors, assuming fixed numbers of 100, 300, and 1000 observed events.

## Key numbers
- Bayes factor interpretation thresholds: ln BF10 in (0–1) not significant, (1–3) positive evidence, (3–5) strong evidence, >5 very strong evidence
- Two spectral profiles used: LSD with average energies ⟨E_νe⟩:⟨E_ν̄e⟩:⟨E_νx⟩ = 9:12:16.5 MeV and SSD with 9:12:14 MeV
- Assumed ES-to-IBD event ratio: N_ES = 0.05 N_IBD
- For spectral swapping width estimation: Δω ≈ 0.7 km⁻¹ or ΔE_ν = ±7.5 MeV per crossing
- Flux factor values assumed: F_νe = 0.5, F_ν̄e = 0.7, F_νx = 0.8

## Context
This work extends Bayesian model comparison techniques previously applied to supernova neutrino signals by Hyper-Kamiokande (2021) and Olsen (2022) to specifically distinguish between different neutrino flavor conversion mechanisms rather than supernova models. The analysis builds on prior studies of collective neutrino oscillations including spectral swapping (Duan 2006, Dasgupta 2009), fast flavor conversions (Sawyer 2005, Morinaga 2021), and magnetically-induced conversions (Abbar 2020, Sasaki 2021). The results enable future analyses to determine whether a detected supernova neutrino signal can reveal which flavor conversion physics operates in the supernova environment, though the authors note that unknown spectral parameters would make discrimination more challenging.
