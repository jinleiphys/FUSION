# Constraining the Surface Curvature of an Anisotropic Neutron Star

> The anisotropy of pressure arises due to the various complex phenomena that happen inside the neutron star (NS). In this study, we calculate the degree of anisotropy inside the NS using the scalar pressure anisotropy model. Macroscopic properties such as mass, radius, compactness, redshift, tidal deformability, the moment of inertia, and surface curvature (SC) are computed for the anisotropic NS with the equation of states spanning from relativistic to nonrelativistic cases. The variation of SC as the functions of the above-mentioned quantities are computed by changing the degree of anisotropy. Pressure anisotropy has significant effects on the magnitude of SC. The universal relations between the canonical SC$-Λ$ and SC$-\bar{I}$ are studied. From the GW170817 tidal deformability data constraints on SC are found to be SC$_{1.4}(10^{14}) = 3.44_{-1.0}^{+0.4}, 2.85_{-1.20}^{+0.62}, \ {\rm and} \ 2.52_{-1.02}^{+0.61}$ for $λ_{\rm BL} = 0.0, 1.0$, and $2.0$ respectively.

## Key claim

The paper constrains the surface curvature (SC) of neutron stars using the Bowers-Liang (BL) scalar pressure anisotropy model, finding that the canonical SC values depend on the degree of anisotropy parameter λ_BL, with the GW170817 tidal deformability data providing specific constraints only for positive values of λ_BL (0.0, 1.0, and 2.0) because higher negative values give unphysical solutions for tidal deformability.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for anisotropic neutron stars using the Bowers-Liang model, where the tangential pressure P_t is defined in terms of radial pressure P_r, energy density, and the anisotropy parameter λ_BL. They computed macroscopic properties including mass, radius, compactness, redshift, tidal deformability, moment of inertia, and surface curvature (defined as the ratio of the Kretschmann scalar at the neutron star surface to that of the Sun) for a set of 60 equations of state spanning from relativistic (RMF, DD-RMF) to nonrelativistic (Skyrme-Hartree-Fock) cases. The approximate relations between canonical SC and tidal deformability (Λ) and between SC and dimensionless moment of inertia (Ī) were fitted using a least-square method with a quadratic polynomial.

## Key numbers

* Canonical SC values from GW170817 tidal deformability data: SC₁.₄(10¹⁴) = 3.44₋₁.₀⁺⁰.⁴ for λ_BL = 0.0, 2.85₋₁.₂₀⁺⁰.⁶² for λ_BL = 1.0, and 2.52₋₁.₀₂⁺⁰.⁶¹ for λ_BL = 2.0
* The GW170817 tidal deformability limit used: Λ₁.₄ = 190₋₁₂₀⁺³⁹⁰
* The neutron star surface curvature is approximately 10¹⁴ times greater than the Sun's curvature
* The anisotropy parameter λ_BL is varied in the range -2 to +2, following Silva (2015)
* The mass of PSR J0952-0607 is M = 2.35 ± 0.17 M⊙
* The GW190814 secondary component mass range is M = 2.50–2.67 M⊙, reproduced for λ_BL = 1.8–2

## Context

This work extends previous studies of anisotropic neutron stars using the Bowers-Liang model, particularly building on the authors' prior calculation of I-Love-C relations for different degrees of anisotropy (Das, 2022). It also connects to the universal I-Love-Q relations established by Yagi (2015) and to constraints on anisotropy from GW170817 tidal deformability data by Biswas (2019). The results enable future constraints on surface curvature through Shapiro delay measurements or interferometry, as proposed by Rosi (2015), and provide approximate relations that can be used to infer SC from observable tidal deformability or moment of inertia values.
