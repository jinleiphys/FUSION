# Goldberger-Treiman constraint criteria for hyperon coupling constants

> The generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation is combined with the Dashen-Weinstein sum rule to provide a constraint equation between the g(KSigmaN) and g(KLambdaN) coupling constants. A comprehensive examination of the published phenomenological and theoretical hyperon couplings has yielded a much smaller set of values, spanning the intervals 0.80<=g(KSigmaN)/Sqrt(4Pi)<=2.72 and -3.90<=g(KLambdaN)/Sqrt(4Pi)<=-1.84, consistent with this criteria. The SU(3) and Goldberger-Treiman hyperon couplings satisfy the constraint along with predictions from a Taylor series extrapolation using the same momentum variation as exhibited by g(PiNN).

## Key claim

The paper derives a constraint equation between the two hyperon coupling constants \(g_{K\Sigma N}\) and \(g_{K\Lambda N}\) by combining the generalized Goldberger-Treiman (GT) relation with the Dashen-Weinstein (DW) sum rule, but the constraint only restricts an area in the coupling-constant plane rather than giving unique values because the \(\pi N\) coupling constant is not exactly known and its uncertainty is magnified by the large meson mass ratio.

## Method

The authors combine the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation, which defines GT coupling constants from axial charges and meson decay constants, with the Dashen-Weinstein sum rule that connects the nucleon and hyperon Goldberger-Treiman discrepancies. They rearrange the DW sum rule into a linear constraint equation \(g_{K\Lambda N} = g_{K\Sigma N}/\sqrt{3} + b\), where the intercept \(b\) depends on the \(\pi N\) coupling constant, the axial charges, and the meson decay constants. They then plot published phenomenological and theoretical hyperon coupling pairs in the \((g_{K\Sigma N}, g_{K\Lambda N})\) plane and identify which fall within the band defined by the uncertainty in \(b\).

## Key numbers

* The GT coupling constants are \(g_{\pi NN}^{GT} = 12.897 \pm 0.047\), \(g_{K\Lambda N}^{GT} = -9.228 \pm 0.209\), and \(g_{K\Sigma N}^{GT} = 3.215 \pm 0.163\) (dimensionless, not divided by \(\sqrt{4\pi}\)).
* The nucleon Goldberger-Treiman discrepancy is \(\Delta_N = 0.0094\), indicating the GT relation is satisfied to better than 1% for the \(\pi N\) system.
* The constraint band is defined by intercepts \(b_{max}/\sqrt{4\pi} = -3.106\) and \(b_{min}/\sqrt{4\pi} = -4.394\) for the full range \(12.90 \leq g_{\pi NN} \leq 13.20\), or a narrower band with intercepts \(-3.245\) and \(-4.011\) using the recommended value \(g_{\pi NN} = 13.02 \pm 0.08\).
* The filtered set of hyperon couplings consistent with the constraint spans the intervals \(0.80 \leq g_{K\Sigma N}/\sqrt{4\pi} \leq 2.72\) and \(-3.90 \leq g_{K\Lambda N}/\sqrt{4\pi} \leq -1.84\).
* The Taylor series extrapolation, assuming the same momentum variation as \(g_{\pi NN}\), yields \(g_{K\Lambda N}^{TS} = -10.77\) and \(g_{K\Sigma N}^{TS} = 4.75\) (dimensionless, not divided by \(\sqrt{4\pi}\)).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that implemented the Dashen-Weinstein sum rule to constrain the \(\pi N\) coupling constant (Goity, Dominguez, and others) by instead applying it to the hyperon sector. The constraint provides a criterion for evaluating the two hyperon couplings in tandem, which is useful for analyses of kaon electromagnetic production data from Jefferson Lab and for hyperon scattering and production models that involve both \(\Lambda\) and \(\Sigma\) intermediate states. The paper also compares with SU(3) flavor symmetry predictions (de Swart convention) and with theoretical calculations from Skyrme-type models (Gobbi et al.), QCD sum rules (Choe et al.), and chiral bag models (Jeong et al.).
