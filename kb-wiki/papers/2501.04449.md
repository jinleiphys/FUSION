# Isospin strikes back

> Assuming isospin conservation, the decay of a $c\bar c$ vector meson into the $Λ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.}$ final state is purely electromagnetic. At the leading order, the $c\bar c$ vector meson first converts into a virtual photon that, then produces the $Λ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.}$ final state. Moreover, such a mechanism, i.e., the virtual photon coupling to $Λ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.}$, is the solely intermediate process through which, in Born approximation, the reaction $e^+e^-\toΛ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.}$ does proceed. It follows that any significant difference between the amplitudes of the processes $c\bar c\toΛ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.}$ and $e^+e^-\toΛ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.}$ at the $c\bar c$ mass must be ascribed to an isospin-violating contribution in the $c\bar c$ decay. In Eur. Phys. J. C $\boldsymbol{80}$, 903 (2020) we studied the decay of the $ψ(2S)$ vector meson into $Λ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.}$ and, on the light of the large branching fraction ${\rm BR}_{18}(ψ(2S)\toΛ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.})=(1.23\pm0.24)\times 10^{-5}$, published in the 2018 edition of the Review of Particle Physics Phys. Rev. D $\boldsymbol{98}$, 030001 (2018), we claimed either the presence of a significant isospin-violating contribution or, with a lesser emphasis, a "not complete reliability of the only available datum". In any case, we propose a new measurement. Apparently, our second and considered less serious hypothesis was the right one, indeed the branching fraction published in the 2024 edition of the Review of Particle Physics Phys. Rev. D $\boldsymbol{110}$, 030001 (2024) is $ {\rm BR}(ψ(2S)\toΛ\barΣ^0+\mathrm{c.c.})=(1.6\pm0.7)\times 10^{-6}$, more than seven times lower with the error that increased from $\sim 20\%$ to $\sim 45\%$.

# Isospin strikes back

## Key claim

The large branching fraction for ψ(2S) → ΛΣ̄⁰ + c.c. reported in the 2018 Review of Particle Physics, which had suggested a significant isospin-violating contribution in the charmonium decay, has been superseded by a 2024 value more than seven times smaller, now consistent with isospin conservation within two standard deviations. The authors conclude that the earlier anomaly was due to unreliable data rather than a genuine isospin-violating effect.

## Method

The authors compare the decay rates of c̄c vector mesons (J/ψ and ψ(2S)) into the ΛΣ̄⁰ + c.c. final state with the cross sections for e⁺e⁻ → ΛΣ̄⁰ + c.c. at the same center-of-mass energies. Under isospin conservation, both processes proceed through the same virtual photon intermediate state, so any difference between the psionic coupling constants (from decay) and the Sachs form factors (from e⁺e⁻ annihilation) indicates isospin violation. The authors extract the effective form factor |A_{ΛΣ⁰}(q²)| by fitting a perturbative QCD-motivated power law to scaled cross section data from BaBar, Belle, and BESIII on five neutral baryon-antibaryon channels (nn̄, ΛΛ̄, Σ⁰Σ̄⁰, Ξ⁰Ξ̄⁰, and ΛΣ̄⁰), then compare the predicted scaled cross section at the ψ(2S) mass with the value derived from the measured branching fraction.

## Key numbers

- BR(ψ(2S) → ΛΣ̄⁰ + c.c.) from 2024 Review of Particle Physics: (1.6 ± 0.7) × 10⁻⁶, a factor >7 lower than the 2018 value of (1.23 ± 0.24) × 10⁻⁵
- BR(J/ψ → ΛΣ̄⁰ + c.c.) from 2024: (2.83 ± 0.23) × 10⁻⁵ (unchanged since 2018)
- Scaled cross section at ψ(2S) mass from fit: 0.825 ± 0.043 pb; from 2024 branching fraction: 1.64 ± 0.74 pb
- Scaled cross section at ψ(2S) mass from 2018 branching fraction: 12.6 ± 2.6 pb (red star in figure)
- Ratio R²_{ΛΣ⁰} for ψ(2S): 2.00 ± 0.88; for J/ψ: 1.15 ± 0.11
- Minimum relative isospin-violating-to-electromagnetic intensity for ψ(2S) at φ=0: 0.41 ± 0.31 (compatible with zero within <2σ); for J/ψ: 0.07 ± 0.06
- Fit parameter A = (3.92 ± 0.21) × 10⁸ GeV¹⁰ pb from 70 data points with q² ≥ (2.8 GeV)²

## Context

This work revises the conclusions of the authors' previous study (Ferroli et al., Eur. Phys. J. C 80, 903, 2020), which had interpreted the anomalously large ψ(2S) → ΛΣ̄⁰ branching fraction as evidence for significant isospin violation. The 2018 value had been derived from CLEO-c data but was not authored by the CLEO Collaboration; the 2024 value comes from a BESIII analysis (BESIII, 2021). The updated result now aligns with the expectation from perturbative QCD that the ratio of J/ψ to ψ(2S) decay rates into ΛΣ̄⁰, normalized by their leptonic widths, should scale as (M_{ψ(2S)}/M_{J/ψ})⁸ ≈ 4.0. The work demonstrates that the scaled cross section data from multiple neutral baryon-antibaryon channels can be described by a single power-law fit, enabling future tests of isospin symmetry in charmonium decays with improved precision.
