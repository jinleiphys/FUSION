# The axial N to Delta transition form factors from Lattice QCD

> We evaluate the N to Delta axial transition form factors in lattice QCD in the quenched theory, with two degenerate flavors of dynamical Wilson fermions and using domain wall valence fermions with staggered sea quark configurations. We predict the ratio $C_5^A(q^2)/C_3^V(q^2)$ relevant to the parity violating asymmetry and check the validity of the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation.

# The axial N to Delta transition form factors from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

The paper provides the first lattice QCD calculation of the axial N to Delta transition form factors, predicting that the ratio $C_5^A/C_3^V$, which approximates the parity violating asymmetry, is non-zero at $Q^2=0$ and increases roughly twofold by $Q^2\sim 1.5$ GeV$^2$, though the results are obtained at pion masses down to about 410 MeV and require extrapolation to the physical pion mass for a definitive comparison with experiment.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon and Delta two-point functions and three-point functions on the lattice, forming a ratio that becomes time-independent at large separations to extract the transition matrix element. They use three different lattice setups: quenched Wilson fermions on a $32^3\times 64$ lattice, dynamical two-flavor Wilson fermions on $24^3\times 40$ and $24^3\times 32$ lattices, and a hybrid approach combining domain wall valence fermions with three-flavor staggered sea quark configurations from the MILC collaboration. The Delta is produced at rest, and optimized sources are constructed to maximize the number of lattice momentum vectors contributing to a given $Q^2$.

## Key numbers

- Pion masses range from $am_\pi = 0.150(3)$ (dynamical Wilson, lightest) to $am_\pi = 0.373(3)$ (MILC hybrid, heaviest), corresponding to $m_\pi$ down to approximately 410 MeV in the quenched case.
- The axial mass from a dipole fit to $C_5^A$ at $m_\pi \sim 500$ MeV is $M_A \sim 1.8(1)$ GeV, compared to the experimental value of $1.28\pm 0.10$ GeV (Kitagaki).
- The ratio $(Q^2/M_N^2) C_6^A/C_5^A$ becomes consistent with unity for $Q^2 \gtrsim 0.5$ GeV$^2$ after linear extrapolation in $m_\pi^2$ in the quenched case.
- The off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation ratio $f_\pi g_{\pi N\Delta}/(2 M_N C_5^A)$ becomes consistent with unity for $Q^2 \gtrsim 1$ GeV$^2$.
- Lattice spacings: $a^{-1}=2.14(6)$ GeV (quenched), $a^{-1}=2.56(10)$ GeV (dynamical Wilson), $a^{-1}=1.58$ GeV (MILC).

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of nucleon structure, previously focused on electromagnetic N to Delta transition form factors (Alexandrou et al., 2005) and the axial charge $g_A$ (gaxial), to the axial sector of the N to Delta transition for the first time. The results provide input for parity violating asymmetry experiments planned at Jefferson Lab (Wells), where the ratio $C_5^A/C_3^V$ serves as the off-diagonal analogue of $g_A/g_V$ from neutron beta decay. The calculation tests the off-diagonal Goldberger-Treiman relation and enables future studies with lighter quarks to compare with experimental determinations of the axial mass.
