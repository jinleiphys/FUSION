# Unitary evolution with fluctuations and dissipation

> We outline an extension of the classical Langevin equation to a quantum formulation of the treatment of dissipation and fluctuations of all collective degrees of freedom with unitary evolution of a many-fermion system within an extension of the time-dependent density functional theory. We illustrate the method by computing the distribution of fission fragment yields for $^{258}$Fm in a quantum hydrodynamic approach and a typical trajectory with full unrestricted density functional theory augmented with dissipation and fluctuations.

## Key claim

The authors propose an extension of time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) that incorporates both dissipation and fluctuations while preserving unitary evolution, arguing that this approach avoids the particle-number non-conservation and computational intractability of Lindblad-type master equations for many-fermion systems.

## Method

The authors augment the TDDFT equations for single-quasiparticle wave functions with a "quantum friction" term proportional to the time derivative of the number density and stochastic scalar and vector fields with finite correlation times and lengths, rather than white noise. The stochastic fields are constructed as sums of smoothed delta-functions in space and time with random positions, widths, and amplitudes, allowing separate control of dissipation strength and fluctuation amplitude, with their ratio determining the temperature of the intrinsic system.

## Key numbers

- The nuclear surface tension reproduced by the SeaLL1 energy density functional is approximately 1 MeV/fm^2, with chemical potential mu = -15.6 MeV for infinite symmetric nuclear matter
- The gradient coefficients in the intrinsic energy functional are C = -2.8622 fm^3 and D = 9 fm^6
- The 1D harmonic oscillator demonstration uses hbar*omega = 6 MeV
- The fission fragment yield calculation for 258Fm is performed at an excitation energy E* approximately 14 MeV
- The correlation length for stochastic fields is of order pi/k_F (approximately 2 fm, the average interparticle distance)
- The correlation time for stochastic fields is of order (m r_0 A^(1/3))/(hbar k_F), with r_0 approximately 1.2 fm

## Context

This work extends the classical Langevin approach to a fully quantum framework for nuclear collective motion, addressing limitations of prior phenomenological models that restrict collective degrees of freedom to a small number of chosen variables (Moller 2001, Randrup 2011). The method builds on the authors' earlier finding (Bulgac 2018) that one-body dissipation dominates in low-energy nuclear dynamics and that TDDFT already transfers energy irreversibly from collective to intrinsic degrees of freedom. The approach enables fully microscopic simulations of fission fragment distributions without requiring pre-defined collective coordinates, inertia tensors, or potential energy surfaces.
