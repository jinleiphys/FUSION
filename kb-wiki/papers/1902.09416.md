# Progress in Multibaryon Spectroscopy

> Anchoring the nuclear interaction in QCD is a long-outstanding problem in nuclear physics. While the lattice community has made enormous progress in mesonic physics and single nucleon physics, continuum-limit physical-point multi-nucleon physics has remained out of reach. I will review CalLat's strategy for multi-nucleon spectroscopy and our latest results.

## Key claim

The CalLat collaboration presents a strategy for multi-nucleon spectroscopy on the lattice that combines "calm baryon" interpolating operators with spatially-displaced sources to achieve earlier plateaus in correlation functions, but the preliminary results at m_pi ~ 350 MeV show negative energy splittings that cannot be directly interpreted as binding energies without additional volumes or negative-energy points.

## Method

The authors generate an ensemble of 10,000 24^3 x 64 HISQ gauge configurations with a pion mass of about 350 MeV and lattice spacing of about 0.12 fm, using Möbius Domain Wall Fermions for the valence quarks. They apply the Matrix Prony method to construct "calm baryon" interpolating operators that remove single-nucleon inelastic excited state contamination, then combine these with spatially-displaced two-nucleon sources (local, face, edge, corner, and antipodal displacements) to project onto definite cubic irreducible representations. The finite-volume energy levels are extracted from two-exponential fits to correlation functions and interpreted via the Lüscher finite-volume formalism.

## Key numbers

* Pion mass of about 350 MeV with lattice spacing about 0.12 fm and m_pi L = 5.1 on a 24^3 x 64 lattice
* For the A_1^+ irrep at m_pi = 350 MeV: I=0 energy splitting of -8.3(3.4) MeV at n^2=0, -10.6(5.3) MeV at n^2=1, and -16(15) MeV at n^2=2
* For the A_1^+ irrep at m_pi = 350 MeV: I=1 energy splitting of -8.1(3.7) MeV at n^2=0, -18.8(5.3) MeV at n^2=1, and -42(17) MeV at n^2=2
* Box size L = 2.9 fm; characteristic length 1/sqrt(mu B) from n^2=0 energies is about 2.89 fm

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD calculations of two-nucleon systems by Yamazaki et al. (2015) at m_pi ~ 300 MeV and NPLQCD (Orginos et al., 2015) at m_pi ~ 450 MeV, who reported positive binding energies in the isosinglet and isovector channels. The CalLat approach introduces "calm baryon" operators combined with spatially-displaced sources to reduce excited state contamination, enabling earlier plateaus than local-source calculations. The results enable future application of the Lüscher finite-volume formalism to extract infinite-volume scattering amplitudes, and the methods can be extended to three-baryon systems as demonstrated in concurrent work on the three-neutron (isospin-3/2) system.
