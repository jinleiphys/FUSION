# Non-linear screening corrections of stellar nuclear reaction rates and their effects on solar neutrino fluxes

> Non-linear electron screening corrections of stellar nuclear fusion rates are calculated analytically in the framework of the Debye-Huckel model and compared with the respective ones of Salpeter's weak screening approximation. In typical solar conditions, the deviation from Salpeter's screening factor is less than one percent, while for hotter stars such corrections turn out to be of the order of one percent only over the limits of the Debye-Huckel model. Moreover, an investigation of the impact of the derived non-linear screening effects on the solar neutrino fluxes yields insignificant corrections for both the pp and CNO chain reactions.

# Non-linear screening corrections of stellar nuclear reaction rates and their effects on solar neutrino fluxes

## Key claim

Non-linear electron screening corrections to Salpeter's weak screening formula are analytically derived within the Debye-Huckel model and found to be less than 0.5% for all solar fusion reactions, contrary to earlier suggestions that such non-linearities might produce corrections of a few percent. The impact on solar neutrino fluxes is negligible, well below experimental errors of any currently imaginable neutrino detector.

## Method

The author analytically solves the screened Coulomb penetration integral using the Debye-Huckel potential, expanding the correction factor I(x) as a power series in x = r_c/r_D to sixth order. The method of steepest descent is then applied to derive the non-linear screening enhancement factor f_0 and the screened most probable energy of interaction E_0^sc, with the key improvement that x is determined self-consistently from the transcendental equation x e^x ξ^{2/3}(x) = U_e/E_0^nos rather than the linear approximation x = U_e/E_0^nos. These corrections are applied to specific solar fusion reactions and to the neutrino flux formulas of Ricci et al. (1995).

## Key numbers

- For the pp reaction in the solar interior (T_6 = 13.5, ρ = 93.3 g/cm^3): weak screening factor f_0^wes = 1.049, non-linear screening factor f_0^sc = 1.048, non-linear correction Δf = 0.030%
- For Be^7(p,γ)B^8: f_0^wes = 1.212, f_0^sc = 1.210, Δf = 0.161%
- For N^14(p,γ)O^15: f_0^wes = 1.400, f_0^sc = 1.396, Δf = 0.332%
- For alpha-alpha collisions at ρ = 10^6 g/cm^3, T_6 = 150: f_0^wes = 1.491, f_0^sc = 1.474, Δf = 1.136% (largest correction found, but at the limit of Debye-Huckel validity)
- Solar neutrino flux ratios Φ^sc/Φ^nos: pp neutrino = 1.000, Be^7 neutrino = 1.037, B^8 neutrino = 1.159, N^13 and O^15 neutrinos = 1.226
- Debye-Huckel radius in solar interior: r_D = 25719 fm; average internuclear distance: a = 11244 fm; thermal energy: kT = 1.161 keV

## Context

This work extends the weak screening formalism of Salpeter (1954) by providing an analytical treatment of non-linear corrections that had previously only been studied numerically by Gruzinov and Bahcall (1998), who found non-linear corrections of order 1%. The paper addresses the call by Adelberger et al. (1998) for an analytical study of non-linear screening effects. The results confirm the conclusions of Ricci et al. (1995) and Gruzinov and Bahcall (1998) that screening corrections cannot resolve the solar neutrino problem, while providing a more rigorous analytical framework. The formalism enables calculation of screening corrections for any stellar condition within the Debye-Huckel regime, though the author notes that for intermediate screening where corrections become significant (∼1%), the Debye-Huckel model itself begins to break down.
