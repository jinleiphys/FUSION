# Nucleon-nucleon scattering matrix and its Nc scaling

> This paper focuses on the scaling of the S-matrix for elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering at large Nc. It is argued that the logarithm of a typical S-matrix element is proportional to Nc in the regime where the large Nc limit is taken with momentum of the incident nucleons proportional Nc. The suggested scaling was previously derived within the framework of potential scattering models for purely elastic scattering in which the parameters scale the same way as in large Nc QCD. A variety of heuristic arguments strongly suggest that the same scaling holds in the realistic case where both elastic and inelastic scattering are possible.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the logarithm of a typical S-matrix element for elastic nucleon-nucleon scattering scales proportionally to \(N_c\) in the regime where the large \(N_c\) limit is taken with incident nucleon momentum proportional to \(N_c\) (Witten kinematics), and that this scaling holds even when inelastic channels are present, not just in the purely elastic potential scattering models where it was previously derived.

## Method

The authors present a series of heuristic arguments for the \(N_c\) scaling of the S-matrix logarithm, starting from a simple scaling argument based on the QCD Hamiltonian's \(N_c\) dependence and the interaction time. They then analyze a toy model with only elastic scattering and spinless nucleons, using classical equations of motion and the semi-classical limit to derive phase shifts proportional to \(N_c\). The analysis is extended to nonlocal and energy-dependent potentials, and finally to inelastic scattering by considering meson emission via a coherent state formalism and an analogy to meson bremsstrahlung.

## Key numbers

- The total nucleon-nucleon cross-section is given by \(\sigma^{\rm total} = \frac{2 \pi \, \log^2(N_c)}{m_{\pi}^2}\), with corrections of relative order \(\log(\log (N_c))/\log(N_c)\).
- The deuteron binding energy is only 2.2 MeV at \(N_c=3\), whereas by \(N_c\) counting rules one expects the deepest bound state to be bound by an energy of order \(N_c\).
- The number of mesons emitted on average, \(n_{\rm meson}\), is of order \(N_c^1\), as inferred from the Skyrme model where the pion field strength scales as \(f_\pi \sim N_c^{1/2}\).
- The probability that no meson is produced (elastic scattering) is \(P_{\rm elastic}=e^{-n_{\rm meson}}\), leading to \(\log{|S|} = -n_{\rm meson}/2 \sim N_c\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Witten (1979), who first proposed time-dependent mean-field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering at large \(N_c\) and noted the need for Witten kinematics (momentum proportional to \(N_c\)). It builds on a previous derivation by Cohen (2012) of the S-matrix scaling in a simplified model of spinless nucleons with elastic potentials, and generalizes that result to realistic QCD with spin, relativistic kinematics, and inelastic channels. The paper also connects to prior work on nucleon-nucleon potentials by Kaplan and Savage, Kaplan and Manohar, and the contracted \(SU(4)\) spin-flavor symmetry of baryons at large \(N_c\) developed by Dashen, Manohar, and Jenkins. The results enable a systematic understanding of how nuclear observables scale with \(N_c\) and provide a foundation for future studies of the semi-classical limit of nucleon-nucleon scattering.
