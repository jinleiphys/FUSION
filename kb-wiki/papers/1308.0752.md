# Energy and Transverse Momentum Fluctuations in the Equilibrium Quantum Systems

> The fluctuations in the ideal quantum gases are studied using the strongly intensive measures $Δ[A,B]$ and $Σ[A,B]$ defined in terms of two extensive quantities $A$ and $B$. In the present paper, these extensive quantities are taken as the motional variable, $A=X$, the system energy $E$ or transverse momentum $P_T$, and number of particles, $B=N$. This choice is most often considered in studying the event-by-event fluctuations and correlations in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. The recently proposed special normalization ensures that $Δ$ and $Σ$ are dimensionless and equal to unity for fluctuations given by the independent particle model. In statistical mechanics, the grand canonical ensemble formulation within the Boltzmann approximation gives an example of independent particle model. Our results demonstrate the effects due to the Bose and Fermi statistics. Estimates of the effects of quantum statistics in the hadron gas at temperatures and chemical potentials typical for thermal models of hadron production in high energy collisions are presented. In the case of massless particles and zero chemical potential the $Δ$ and $Σ$ measures are calculated analytically.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the strongly intensive fluctuation measures Δ and Σ, when normalized to equal unity for the independent particle model (Boltzmann approximation), deviate from unity due to Bose and Fermi statistics; for a pion gas at typical freeze-out temperatures (130–170 MeV) these deviations are about 25% for Δ and 10% for Σ, and are strongly enlarged for chemical non-equilibrium with μπ > 0.

## Method

The authors calculate the strongly intensive measures Δ[X,N] and Σ[X,N] for ideal quantum gases in the grand canonical ensemble, taking the extensive quantities X as either the system energy E or transverse momentum PT, and B as the particle number N. They use the normalization proposed by Gorenstein, Gazdzicki, and Pawlowska (2013) that makes Δ and Σ dimensionless and equal to unity for the independent particle model (Boltzmann approximation). Analytical results are derived for massless particles at zero chemical potential, and numerical evaluations using series expansions in modified Bessel functions are performed for a pion gas (Bose statistics) and a proton gas (Fermi statistics) at temperatures and chemical potentials typical for thermal models of high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- For massless particles at μ=0, Δ[E,N] ≈ 0.268 for bosons and 1.179 for fermions; Σ[E,N] ≈ 1.499 for bosons and 0.917 for fermions (these values are temperature-independent).
- For a pion gas (mπ ≈ 140 MeV) at μπ=0 and T=130–170 MeV, deviations from the Boltzmann value of 1 are about 25% for Δ[E,N] and about 10% for Σ[E,N].
- For a proton gas (mp ≈ 938 MeV) at T=150 MeV, μB=300 MeV: Δ[E,N] ≈ 1.030, Σ[E,N] ≈ 0.997.
- For a proton gas at T=100 MeV, μB=500 MeV: Δ[E,N] ≈ 1.040, Σ[E,N] ≈ 0.997.
- For massless particles at μ=0, Δ[PT,N] ≈ 0.433 for bosons and 1.125 for fermions; Σ[PT,N] ≈ 1.398 for bosons and 0.931 for fermions.

## Context

This work extends the study of strongly intensive fluctuation measures introduced by Gorenstein and Gazdzicki (2011) and the normalization scheme of Gorenstein, Gazdzicki, and Pawlowska (2013) to ideal quantum gases, providing analytical and numerical results for Bose and Fermi statistics. It supersedes the earlier Φ measure introduced by Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (1992), which was dimensional and lacked a characteristic scale, by using the dimensionless Δ and Σ measures. The results enable quantitative estimates of quantum statistics effects in event-by-event fluctuation analyses of high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions, particularly for the NA61/SHINE and RHIC low energy scan programs, and provide a baseline for comparison with dynamical effects such as resonance decays, transverse collective flow, and exact conservation laws.
