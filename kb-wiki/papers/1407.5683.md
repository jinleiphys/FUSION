# Model independent extraction of the proton magnetic radius from electron scattering

> We combine constraints from analyticity with experimental electron-proton scattering data to determine the proton magnetic radius without model-dependent assumptions on the shape of the form factor. We also study the impact of including electron-neutron scattering data, and $ππ\to N\bar{N}$ data. Using representative datasets we find for a cut of $Q^2\leq0.5$ GeV$^2$, $r_M^p=0.91^{+0.03}_{-0.06}\pm0.02$ fm using just proton scattering data; $r_M^p=0.87^{+0.04}_{-0.05}\pm0.01$ fm adding neutron data; and $r_M^p=0.87^{+0.02}_{-0.02}$ fm adding $ππ$ data. We also extract the neutron magnetic radius from these data sets obtaining $r_M^n=0.89^{+0.03}_{-0.03}$ fm from the combined proton, neutron, and $ππ$ data.

## Key claim
The paper extracts the proton magnetic radius from electron scattering data using a model-independent conformal mapping technique (the "z expansion") that avoids assumptions about the shape of the form factor. The authors find that including neutron and pion-pion data reduces the central value and uncertainty compared to using only proton data, but all results are consistent within errors.

## Method
The authors apply the conformal mapping technique of Hill and Paz (2010) to express the Sachs magnetic form factors as power series in a variable z that maps the cut complex plane onto the unit circle. They fit this series to tabulated electron-proton scattering data from Arrington (2007) and electron-neutron scattering data from multiple experiments, imposing conservative bounds on the expansion coefficients derived from vector dominance and explicit pion-pion continuum calculations. For the combined analysis, they incorporate the imaginary part of the isovector magnetic form factor from the two-pion threshold up to 40 pion masses squared, raising the effective cut for the isovector component from 4 to 16 pion masses squared.

## Key numbers
- Proton magnetic radius from proton data alone at Q^2 ≤ 0.5 GeV^2: r_M^p = 0.91^{+0.03}_{-0.06} ± 0.02 fm (bounds of 10 and 15 on expansion coefficients)
- Proton magnetic radius adding neutron data at Q^2 ≤ 0.5 GeV^2: r_M^p = 0.87^{+0.04}_{-0.05} ± 0.01 fm
- Proton magnetic radius adding both neutron and pion-pion data at Q^2 ≤ 0.5 GeV^2: r_M^p = 0.87^{+0.02}_{-0.02} fm (bounds of 10 and 15)
- Neutron magnetic radius from combined proton, neutron, and pion-pion data: r_M^n = 0.89^{+0.03}_{-0.03} fm
- For the isovector magnetic form factor, the vector dominance ansatz gives coefficients a_0 ≈ 4.7, a_1 ≈ 3.7, a_2 ≈ 2.7, a_3 ≈ 2.0, a_4 ≈ -0.36

## Context
This work extends the model-independent extraction method developed by Hill and Paz (2010) for the proton electric radius to the magnetic radius, addressing a potential "magnetic radius puzzle" arising from discrepancies between the A1 collaboration value of 0.777(16) fm (Bernauer, 2010) and earlier extractions of 0.876(19) fm (Borisyuk, 2009) and 0.854(5) fm (Belushkin, 2006). The method enables future comparisons with muonic hydrogen measurements and lattice QCD calculations, and the authors note that the proton and neutron magnetic radii are very similar, consistent with the linear behavior of the normalized form factors when plotted as a function of z.
