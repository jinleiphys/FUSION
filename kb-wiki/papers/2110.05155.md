# Observation of a structure in the M$_{pη}$ invariant mass distribution near 1700 MeV/$c^2$ in the $\mathbf{γp \rightarrow p π^0 η} $ reaction

> The reaction $γp \rightarrow p π^0 η$ has been studied with the CBELSA/TAPS detector at the electron stretcher accelerator ELSA in Bonn for incident photon energies from threshold up to 3.1 GeV. This paper has been motivated by the recently claimed observation of a narrow structure in the M$_{Nη}$ invariant mass distribution at a mass of 1678 MeV/$c^2$. The existence of this structure cannot be confirmed in the present work. Instead, for E$_γ$ = 1400 - 1500 MeV and the cut M$_{pπ^0} \le 1190 $ MeV/$c^2$ a statistically significant structure in the M$_{pη}$ invariant mass distribution near 1700 MeV/$c^2$ is observed with a width of $Γ\approx 35$ MeV/$c^2$ while the mass resolution is $σ_{res}$ = 5 MeV/$c^2$. Increasing the incident photon energy from 1420 to 1540 MeV this structure shifts in mass from $\approx$ 1700MeV/c$^2$ to $\approx$ 1725 MeV/$c^2$; the width increases to about 50 MeV/$c^2$ and decreases thereafter. The cross section associated with this structure reaches a maximum of $\approx$ 100 nb around E$_γ \approx$ 1490 MeV (W $\approx $ 1920 MeV), which coincides with the $p a_0$ threshold. Three scenarios are discussed which might be the origin of this structure in the M$_{pη}$ invariant mass distribution. The most likely interpretation is that it is due to a triangular singularity in the $γp \rightarrow p a_0 \rightarrow p π^0 η$ reaction

## Key claim

The paper reports a statistically significant structure in the \(M_{p\eta}\) invariant mass distribution near 1700 MeV/\(c^2\) in the \(\gamma p \rightarrow p \pi^0 \eta\) reaction, but explicitly cannot confirm a previously claimed narrow structure at 1678 MeV/\(c^2\). The observed structure is most likely interpreted as a triangular singularity in the \(\gamma p \rightarrow p a_0 \rightarrow p \pi^0 \eta\) reaction rather than a new resonance.

## Method

The CBELSA/TAPS detector at the ELSA accelerator in Bonn was used to measure the \(\gamma p \rightarrow p \pi^0 \eta\) reaction for incident photon energies from threshold up to 3.1 GeV. Events were selected via kinematic fits imposing energy and momentum conservation, with the proton treated as a missing particle, and confidence level cuts to suppress the dominant \(\gamma p \rightarrow p \pi^0 \pi^0\) background. The \(M_{p\eta}\) invariant mass distributions were studied under specific cuts on \(M_{p\pi^0}\) and incident photon energy ranges to isolate the structure.

## Key numbers

- Structure observed at \(M_{p\eta} = (1700 \pm 2)\) MeV/\(c^2\) with width \(\Gamma = (35 \pm 7)\) MeV/\(c^2\) for \(E_\gamma = 1400-1500\) MeV and cut \(M_{p\pi^0} \le 1190\) MeV/\(c^2\); mass resolution \(\sigma_{res} = 5\) MeV/\(c^2\)
- Significance of the structure is 6.5 \(\sigma\) (local \(p_0 = 2 \times 10^{-10}\))
- Cross section reaches a maximum of \(\approx 100\) nb around \(E_\gamma \approx 1490\) MeV (\(W \approx 1920\) MeV), coinciding with the \(p a_0\) threshold
- As incident photon energy increases from 1420 to 1540 MeV, the structure shifts in mass from \(\approx 1700\) MeV/\(c^2\) to \(\approx 1725\) MeV/\(c^2\); width increases to about 50 MeV/\(c^2\) and then decreases
- Upper limit of 60 counts (95% confidence level) for a narrow structure at 1678 MeV/\(c^2\) under conditions matching Kuznetsov et al.; upper limit of 9 nb for the cross section of such a structure

## Context

This work was motivated by the claim of Kuznetsov et al. of a narrow structure (\(\Gamma \approx 10\) MeV/\(c^2\)) in the \(M_{N\eta}\) invariant mass distribution at 1678 MeV/\(c^2\), which the present study cannot confirm with four times higher statistics. The results extend earlier CBELSA/TAPS studies by Gutz et al. and Sokhoyan et al., and are compared to the Bonn-Gatchina partial wave analysis by Klempt et al. The observation of a structure near 1700 MeV/\(c^2\) with a width of \(\approx 35\) MeV/\(c^2\) and its interpretation as a triangular singularity rather than a new resonance opens the question of whether similar kinematic enhancements appear in other reactions, and calls for an updated partial wave analysis incorporating the present data.
