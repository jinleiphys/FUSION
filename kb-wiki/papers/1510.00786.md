# Astrophysical S-factor for $^6$Li$(p,γ)$$^7$Be in the coupled-channel Gamow shell model

> We have applied the Gamow shell model (GSM) in the coupled-channel representation to study the astrophysical $S$-factor for the proton radiative capture reaction of $^6$Li. Reaction channels are built by coupling the proton wave function expanded in different partial waves with the GSM wave functions of the ground state ($1^+$) and the excited states ($3^+_1$, $0^+_1$ and $2^+_1$) of $^6$Li. All relevant $E1$, $M1$, and $E2$ transitions from the initial continuum states in $^7$Be to the final bound states (${3/2}^-_1$ and ${1/2}^-_1$) states are included. It is found that the calculated total astrophysical $S$ factor for this reaction agrees well with the experimental data.

# Astrophysical S-factor for $^6$Li$(p,\gamma)$$^7$Be in the coupled-channel Gamow shell model

## Key claim
The coupled-channel Gamow shell model (GSM-CC) reproduces the total astrophysical S-factor for the $^6$Li$(p,\gamma)$$^7$Be reaction across a wide energy range, but only after a small adjustment of the channel-channel coupling potentials to compensate for missing correlations from neglected non-resonant channels. The calculation does not confirm the recently reported sudden drop of the S-factor at low energies or the predicted new positive parity resonance at $E_\text{CM}\simeq 0.195$ MeV.

## Method
The authors applied the Gamow shell model in the coupled-channel representation using a translationally invariant Hamiltonian with an effective finite-range two-body interaction (Furutani-Horiuchi-Tamagaki). Reaction channels were built by coupling proton partial waves ($s_{1/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $p_{3/2}$, $d_{3/2}$, $d_{5/2}$) with GSM wave functions of the $^6$Li ground state ($1^+$) and excited states ($3^+_1$, $0^+_1$, $2^+_1$), using $^4$He as the core and a Woods-Saxon potential with spin-orbit term. All relevant $E1$, $M1$, and $E2$ transitions from initial continuum states in $^7$Be to the final bound states (${3/2}^-_1$ and ${1/2}^-_1$) were included.

## Key numbers
- $S_{\rm GSM-CC}(0) = 88.3423$ b$\cdot$eV, compared to the accepted experimental value $S_{\rm exp}(0) = 79 \pm 18$ b$\cdot$eV
- The calculation disagrees with the lowest experimental data point at $E_\text{CM} = 140$ keV
- Above $E_{\rm CM} = 1$ MeV, the agreement with experimental data deteriorates
- The predicted new resonance at $E_\text{CM}\simeq 0.195$ MeV reported by He et al. (2013) is not confirmed

## Context
This work extends the GSM-CC approach previously applied to low-energy elastic and inelastic proton scattering (Jaganathen, 2014) and proton/neutron radiative capture reactions (Fossez, 2015) to the specific astrophysically important $^6$Li$(p,\gamma)$$^7$Be reaction. The results are compared with experimental data from Switkowski et al. (1979), He et al. (2013), and Xu et al. (2013), as well as theoretical calculations by the potential model (Huang, 2010) and the microscopic cluster model (Arai, 2002). The GSM-CC results predict a lower value for $S(0)$ than these other theoretical studies and show a negative slope of $S(E_{\rm CM})$ towards low energies, consistent with Prior et al. (2004) but contradicting an earlier measurement by Cecil et al. (1992). The same Hamiltonian is used for both structure and reaction studies, enabling a unified description of the low-energy spectra of $^6$Li and $^7$Be alongside the capture cross section.
