# Recent Developments in relativistic models for exclusive (e,e'p) reactions

> A comparison of impulse approximation calculations for the (e,e'p) reaction, based on the Dirac equation and the Schrodinger one is presented. Trivial (kinematics) differences are indicated, as well as how to remove them from the standard nonrelativistic formalism. Signatures of the relativistic approach are found where the enhancement of the lower components (spinor distortion or negative energy contributions) modifies TL observables with respect to the nonrelativistic predictions, what seems to be confirmed by the experiment. Finally, the relativistic approach is used to analyze several experiments for the reaction 16O(e,e'p)15N taken at values of Q^2 from 0.2 to 0.8 (GeV/c)^2, not finding a significant Q^2 dependence of the scale factors over this range.

# Recent Developments in relativistic models for exclusive (e,e'p) reactions

## Key claim

Relativistic impulse approximation calculations for (e,e'p) reactions, based on the Dirac equation, show signatures, particularly in the TL asymmetry and response functions, that are confirmed by experiment, but the model's single-particle nature and the equivalence between Darwin factors from Dirac equation and Perey-type nonlocality from nonrelativistic analyses mean that disentangling relativistic from nonrelativistic approaches using only cross-sections or scale factors remains difficult.

## Method

The authors compare relativistic distorted wave impulse approximation (RDWIA) calculations, using Dirac equation solutions for both bound and outgoing nucleon wave functions with scalar-vector potentials, against nonrelativistic Schrodinger-based impulse approximation calculations for the (e,e'p) reaction. They compute the nuclear current matrix element using relativistic four-spinor wave functions and a relativistic nucleon current operator, and analyze the breakdown of factorization due to enhancement of the lower components of the Dirac spinors, particularly examining the TL asymmetry A_TL and the TL response function R^TL for the 16O(e,e'p)15N reaction across multiple kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Q^2 range analyzed for 16O(e,e'p)15N: from 0.2 to 0.8 (GeV/c)^2, with no significant Q^2 dependence of scale factors found over this range
- Missing momentum range of most data: p_m ≤ 300 MeV/c, with higher p_m regions being probed in newer experiments
- Nonlocality range parameters (β_nl) from Perey-Buck analysis of DEB potentials: between 0.82 and 1.0 fm, comparable to values from nonrelativistic analyses (0.82-0.95 fm)
- For 208Pb at T'=100 MeV, comparison of nonrelativistic central and spin-orbit potentials with those derived from Dirac equation based (DEB) procedure
- A_TL crossover observed between 200 and 300 MeV/c missing momentum for the three shells of 16O
- Scale factors derived from experiments at Q^2 around 0.2 (GeV/c)^2 (Spaltro et al.), 0.3 (GeV/c)^2 (Chinitz et al.), and 0.8 (GeV/c)^2 (Gao et al.), plus parallel kinematics from Leuschner et al. (Q^2 range 0 to 0.4 (GeV/c)^2)

## Context

This work extends the relativistic impulse approximation formalism developed by Udias et al. (1993-1999) and others (van Orden, Jin, Gardner) for (e,e'p) reactions, comparing directly with nonrelativistic approaches such as the DWEEPY code. The authors show that the enhancement of lower components in Dirac spinors produces observable signatures in the TL asymmetry that have been confirmed by experiments at higher Q^2 (Gao et al., 2000), and that the equivalence between Darwin factors and Perey-type nonlocality (Perey and Buck, 1961; Giannini and Rico, 1976) makes it difficult to distinguish relativistic from nonrelativistic approaches using only cross-sections. The analysis of scale factors across Q^2 addresses recent claims by Zhalov and Lapikas of possible Q^2 dependence of spectroscopic factors, finding no significant variation over the 0.2-0.8 (GeV/c)^2 range for 16O.
