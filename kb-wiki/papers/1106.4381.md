# QHD equation of state for strongly magnetized neutron stars

> We investigate the quantum hadrodynamic equation of state for neutron stars (with and without including hyperons) in the presence of strong magnetic fields. The deduced masses and radii are consistent with recent observations of high mass neutron stars even in the case of hyperonic nuclei for sufficiently strong magnetic fields. The calculated adiabatic index and the moments of inertia for magnetized neutron stars exhibit rapid changes with density. This may provide some insight into the mechanism of star-quakes and flares in magnetars.

## Key claim

The quantum hadrodynamic (QHD) equation of state for neutron stars with strong interior magnetic fields can produce masses and radii consistent with recent observations of high-mass neutron stars even when hyperons are present, provided the magnetic field is sufficiently strong, a condition that the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The authors constructed a relativistic mean-field QHD Lagrangian including the baryon octet, leptons, and five meson fields (σ, ω, ρ, σ*, φ), with Landau quantization for charged particles and anomalous magnetic moments (AMMs) for baryons. They solved the meson field equations under constraints of baryon number conservation, charge neutrality, and chemical equilibrium, then used the resulting equation of state in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to compute mass-radius relations and the slow-rotation approximation to compute moments of inertia. The magnetic field was modeled as density-dependent with a surface value of ~10^15 G and a saturation strength B0 at high density, parameterized as B(ρ/ρ0) = B^surf + B0[1 − exp{−β(ρ/ρ0)^γ}] with β = 0.02 and γ = 3.

## Key numbers

- Surface magnetic fields of magnetars are observed to be 10^14–10^15 G; interior fields may reach up to 10^18 G according to the scalar virial theorem.
- The critical magnetic field for the electron is B_e^c = 4.414 × 10^13 G; magnetic field strengths are expressed as dimensionless B* = B / B_e^c.
- For the hyperonic phase with B0* = 5 × 10^4 (i.e., B0 ≈ 2.2 × 10^18 G), a sudden large increase in the adiabatic index occurs at approximately 3ρ0, caused by the production of Ξ− hyperons.
- The largest observed magnetar flare (SGR 1806-20) released energy E_flare ~ 2 × 10^46 erg; a change in magnetic field of ΔB ~ 10^16 G corresponds to about a 1% decrease in the moment of inertia and rotation period.
- For typical AXP flares with energy ~10^40 erg, the implied ΔB ~ 10^13 G gives a rotation period decrease by a factor of ~10^−5, comparable to observed glitches in some AXPs.

## Context

This work extends the earlier cold free n,p,e Fermi gas study of Suh and Mathews (2001) by incorporating a realistic nuclear equation of state based on QHD, including hyperons and anomalous magnetic moments. It also builds on prior QHD studies of magnetized neutron matter by Bandyopadhyay et al. (1997), Broderick et al. (2000), and Ryu et al. (2010). The results enable a quantitative connection between magnetar flares, star-quakes, and pulsar glitches through the predicted rapid changes in the adiabatic index and moment of inertia with density, and they provide a mechanism to reconcile hyperonic equations of state with observed high neutron-star masses (~2 M⊙) if interior fields are sufficiently strong.
