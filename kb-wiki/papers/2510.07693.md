# Gamow shell model study of the 17Ne(p, p) reaction and of isospin symmetry breaking in 18Na

> The unbound nucleus 18Na, acting as an intermediate nucleus in the sequential decay of 19Mg, is situated beyond the proton drip line. We employ the coupled-channel Gamow shell model (GSM-CC) to investigate the properties of 18Na, as well as the 17Ne(p, p) cross section. GSM-CC treats the nucleus as an open quantum system and provides a unified framework for studying both nuclear structure and reaction cross sections. Our calculations reproduce the energies and partial decay widths of low-lying states in 18Na, as well as the 17Ne(p, p) cross section. Additionally, the mirror nucleus 18N is also described. The isospin symmetry breaking induced by the Coulomb interaction and continuum coupling is clearly obtained in our description of 18Na and 18N properties, arising from the extended s1/2 partial wave. The isospin symmetry breaking in the 18Na/18N pair is compared to that occurring in the mirror pair 16F/16N, whereby similarities and differences are analyzed.

## Key claim

The coupled-channel Gamow shell model (GSM-CC) reproduces the energies, partial decay widths, and the 17Ne(p,p) cross section for the unbound nucleus 18Na, but only after applying corrective factors (close to unity) to the two-body matrix elements of the nuclear interaction to reconcile discrepancies between the GSM and GSM-CC truncation schemes.

## Method

GSM-CC treats the nucleus as an open quantum system using the Berggren basis, which includes bound, resonant, and scattering states. The authors constructed reaction channels by coupling the ground and excited states of the 17Ne target with a proton projectile in partial waves up to l ≤ 3, using 14O as the inert core. The Hamiltonian employed a Woods-Saxon potential for the core-valence nucleon interaction and an effective field theory (EFT) nucleon-nucleon interaction, with the Coulomb interaction treated exactly.

## Key numbers

- The calculated width of the 2- state in 18Na is 9 keV, in excellent agreement with the experimental value of 5(3) keV and the shell model prediction of 8 keV.
- The 0- state in 18Na has a calculated width of 367 keV (via s1/2 decay to 17Ne ground state), matching the experimental value of 300(100) keV.
- The 1- state in 18Na has a calculated width of 428 keV (via s1/2 decay to 17Ne ground state), compared to the experimental value of 900(100) keV.
- The 3- state in 18Na has a calculated width of 28 keV (via d5/2 decay to 17Ne ground state), compared to the experimental value of 42(10) keV.
- The first resonance peak in the 17Ne(p,p) excitation function corresponds to the 2- state at a calculated energy of 1553 keV with a width of 9 keV, consistent with the experimental Er = 1.552(5) MeV and Γ = 5(3) keV.
- The calculated separation energy of the first excited state Sn*(2-) in 18N is 2.705 MeV, while the experimental value is 2.713 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the GSM-CC framework previously applied to mirror pairs such as 16F/16N (PhysRevC.106.L011301) and to reactions including 15O(p,p) and 18Ne(p,p). The study enables a unified description of nuclear structure and reaction observables for drip-line nuclei, and provides a quantitative assessment of isospin symmetry breaking in the 18Na/18N mirror pair, which is compared to the 16F/16N system. The results are relevant for understanding the sequential decay of 19Mg, where 18Na acts as an intermediate nucleus.
