# Electromagnetic fields in low-energy heavy-ion collisions with baryon stopping

> We investigate the impact of baryon stopping on the temporal evolution of electromagnetic fields in vacuum at low-energy Au+Au collisions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4$-$20$ GeV. Baryon stopping is incorporated into the Monte-Carlo Glauber model by employing a parameterized velocity profile of participant nucleons with non-zero deceleration. The presence of these decelerating participants leads to noticeable changes in the centrality and $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ dependence of electromagnetic fields compared to scenarios with vanishing deceleration. The influence of baryon stopping differs for electric and magnetic fields, also exhibiting variations across their components. We observe slight alteration in the approximate linear dependency of field strengths with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ in the presence of deceleration. Additionally, the longitudinal component of the electric field at late times becomes significant in the presence of baryon stopping.

## Key claim
The paper claims that incorporating baryon stopping (via a parameterized deceleration of participant nucleons) into the Monte-Carlo Glauber model produces noticeable changes in the centrality and energy dependence of electromagnetic fields in low-energy Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4\)–\(20\) GeV, but the effect is minimal for peripheral collisions and the approximate linear scaling of field strengths with \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) is only slightly altered.

## Method
The authors used a Monte-Carlo Glauber model with Wood-Saxon nucleon distributions for Au nuclei, sampling nucleon positions and identifying participants based on the inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section. They introduced baryon stopping by assigning a parameterized velocity profile \(\beta(\tau) = \mathcal{A}[1 - \tanh((\tau - \tau_h)/\Delta\tau)]\) to participant nucleons, with \(\mathcal{A} = \beta_{s_{NN}}/2\) and \(\Delta\tau\) controlling the deceleration timescale. Electromagnetic fields were then computed from the Liénard-Wiechert potentials including acceleration terms, evaluated at retarded times for an ensemble of 1000 events per centrality, with a cutoff of \(|\mathbf{R}| \approx 1\) fm to avoid divergences from nucleons near the observation point.

## Key numbers
- The study covers Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4\)–\(20\) GeV, with the maximum net baryon density at midrapidity achieved near \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \approx 6\) GeV.
- The Wood-Saxon parameters for Au are: \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), \(R = 6.34\) fm, \(a = 0.54\) fm.
- The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section is fitted as \(\sigma_{NN}^{f} = 7.63 (\sqrt{s_{NN}})^{0.22} + 17.36\) (with \(\chi^2 \approx 0.05\)).
- The deceleration parameter \(\Delta\tau\) is set to 1 fm or 3 fm; the starting time \(\tau_s\) for deceleration is defined when \(\beta_{s_{NN}}/\beta(\tau_s) \approx 0.98\).
- Temporal evolution is shown for impact parameters \(b = 3\) fm (central) and \(b = 12\) fm (peripheral) at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4\) GeV, with fields evaluated at \(\mathbf{r}_{\text{obs}} = (0,0,0)\).

## Context
This work extends the standard Glauber-model calculations of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions (Deng 2012, Skokov 2009) by incorporating baryon stopping, which is significant at low \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}\) where the assumption of near-perfect transparency breaks down. The study is motivated by the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC and the future FAIR facility, which aim to explore QCD matter properties and the chiral magnetic effect at lower collision energies. The authors note that a more realistic implementation of energy/rapidity loss (where baryons lose about one unit of rapidity per collision, as in Busza 2018) is beyond the present scope and left for future work.
