# Implicit schemes for real-time lattice gauge theory

> We develop new gauge-covariant implicit numerical schemes for classical real-time lattice gauge theory. A new semi-implicit scheme is used to cure a numerical instability encountered in three-dimensional classical Yang-Mills simulations of heavy-ion collisions by allowing for wave propagation along one lattice direction free of numerical dispersion. We show that the scheme is gauge covariant and that the Gauss constraint is conserved even for large time steps.

## Key claim

The paper develops a new semi-implicit numerical scheme for classical real-time lattice gauge theory that cures a numerical Cherenkov instability encountered in three-dimensional Yang-Mills simulations of heavy-ion collisions, but only for wave propagation along one specific lattice direction (the longitudinal direction) where the dispersion relation becomes linear.

## Method

The authors derive implicit and semi-implicit numerical schemes by modifying the standard Wilson gauge action, replacing selected spatial finite differences with temporally averaged expressions while maintaining lattice gauge invariance. For the semi-implicit scheme, they treat the longitudinal spatial direction explicitly and all transverse directions implicitly, then solve the resulting system of equations using damped fixed-point iteration. The scheme is tested on both a scalar wave equation toy model and Abelian gauge fields before generalization to non-Abelian lattice gauge theory.

## Key numbers

- The Courant-Friedrichs-Lewy (CFL) stability condition for the semi-implicit scheme is (a^0/a^1)^2 ≤ 1, allowing the "magic time-step" a^0 = a^1 for dispersion-free propagation along the longitudinal direction
- The fixed-point iteration convergence condition requires the damping parameter α to satisfy (2Δ - 1)/(2Δ + 1) < α < 1, where Δ = (a^0/a^2)^2 for the 2+1 dimensional case
- For the implicit scheme, the dispersion relation is sin²(ωa^0/2) = [Σ_i (a^0/a^i)² sin²(k^i a^i/2)] / [1 + 2 Σ_i (a^0/a^i)² sin²(k^i a^i/2)], which is unconditionally stable but always dispersive

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-dimensional heavy-ion collision simulations in the Color Glass Condensate framework (Gelfand 2016, Ipp 2017) that suffered from numerical Cherenkov instability. The instability is analogous to that encountered in Abelian particle-in-cell simulations of laser wakefield acceleration (Godfrey 1974, Greenwood 2004, Novokhatski 2012). The semi-implicit scheme enables stable simulations of nuclei with finite longitudinal extent propagating along the beam axis without requiring prohibitively fine lattice resolutions.
