# The CSM extension for description of the positive and negative parity bands in even-odd nuclei

> A particle-core Hamiltonian is used to describe the lowest parity partner bands $K^π=1/2^{\pm}$ in $^{219}$Ra, $^{237}$U and $^{239}$Pu, and three parity partner bands, $K^π=1/2^{\pm}, 3/2^{\pm}, 5/2^{\pm}$, in $^{227}$Ra. The core is described by a quadrupole and octupole boson Hamiltonian which was previously used for the description of four positive and four negative parity bands in the neighboring even-even isotopes. The particle-core Hamiltonian consists of four terms: a quadrupole-quadrupole, an octupole-octupole, a spin-spin and a rotational $\hat{I}^2$ interaction, with $\hat {I}$ denoting the total angular momentum. The single particle space for the odd nucleon consists of three spherical shell model states, two of positive and one of negative parity. The product of these states with a collective deformed ground state and the intrinsic gamma band state generate, through angular momentum projection, the bands with $K^π=1/2^{\pm},3/2^{\pm},5/2^{\pm}$, respectively. In the space of projected states one calculates the energies of the considered bands. The resulting excitation energies are compared with the corresponding experimental data as well as with those obtained with other approaches. Also, we searched for some signatures for a static octupole deformation in the considered odd isotopes. The calculated branching ratios in $^{219}$Ra agree quite well with the corresponding experimental data.

## Key claim

The Coherent State Model (CSM) is extended to describe both positive and negative parity bands in even-odd nuclei by coupling a particle-core Hamiltonian to a core described by quadrupole and octupole bosons, with the single-particle space restricted to only three spherical shell model states (two positive parity, one negative parity). The approach reproduces experimental excitation energies with root-mean-square deviations of 66.24 keV, 48.97 keV, 31.8 keV, and 23 keV for the four studied nuclei, though the agreement varies by nucleus and the model requires fitting of four interaction strengths to specific energy levels.

## Method

A particle-core Hamiltonian was constructed with four interaction terms: quadrupole-quadrupole, octupole-octupole, spin-spin (j·J), and rotational (I²) coupling. The core was described by a quadrupole and octupole boson Hamiltonian previously used for even-even neighbors, with the odd nucleon occupying three spherical shell model states (j₁, j₂ with positive parity; j₃ with negative parity). Angular momentum and parity projected states were generated from products of these single-particle states with deformed intrinsic core states (coherent states for K=1/2 bands, gamma-band intrinsic states for K=3/2 and K=5/2 bands), and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized in the space of projected states to obtain excitation energies. For K=1/2 bands, mixing of different j-states was included with small admixture amplitudes (0.001 for ²¹⁹Ra, 0.04 for ²³⁷U and ²³⁹Pu).

## Context

This work extends the Coherent State Model (CSM) developed by Raduta (1981) and later expanded to include octupole degrees of freedom (Raduta 1997, 2002, 2003, 2006) from even-even nuclei to even-odd nuclei. It contrasts with earlier particle-core approaches (Raduta and Ceausescu; Leander) that restricted the single-particle space to a single j-value, thereby eliminating octupole-octupole interaction contributions. The spectra of these odd isotopes were previously studied by Zubov, Denisov, and Bonatsos using a quadrupole-octupole Hamiltonian in intrinsic deformation variables β₂ and β₃ with kinetic energy, potential energy, and Coriolis interaction terms. The approach enables identification of signatures for static octupole deformation in odd isotopes, with the energy displacement function δE(I) for ²³⁹Pu suggesting possible static octupole deformation for states with angular momentum I ≥ 51/2.
