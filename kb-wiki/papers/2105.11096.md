# Systematic shell-model study of Rn isotopes with $A=$ 207 to 216 and isomeric states

> We present systematic large-scale shell-model calculations for Rn isotopes with $A=$ 207 to 216. For the $^{207-212}$Rn isotopes, we perform calculations with KHH7B interaction, while for $^{213-216}$Rn isotopes with KHPE and KHH7B interactions. The calculated energies and electromagnetic properties are compared with the available experimental data and predicted where experimental data are not available. We also suggest spins and parities of several unconfirmed states available from the recent experimental data. Comprehensive study of several isomeric states from the calculated shell-model configurations and half-lives is also reported.

# Systematic shell-model study of Rn isotopes with A = 207 to 216 and isomeric states

## Key claim

Large-scale shell-model calculations using the KHH7B interaction for 207-212Rn and both KHPE and KHH7B interactions for 213-216Rn can reproduce experimental energy levels and electromagnetic properties for low-lying states, but high-lying states require core-excitation beyond the Z=82 and N=126 shell closures that the current model spaces cannot fully accommodate due to computational limitations.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model diagonalizations using the NUSHELLX and KSHELL codes with two interactions: KHPE (using proton orbitals 1h9/2, 2f7/2, 2f5/2, 3p3/2, 3p1/2, 1i13/2 and neutron orbitals 1i11/2, 2g9/2, 2g7/2, 3d5/2, 3d3/2, 4s1/2, 1j15/2) and KHH7B (using four proton orbitals below and three above Z=82, and four neutron orbitals below and three above N=126). For 207-212Rn, calculations used KHH7B interaction; for 213-216Rn, both KHPE and KHH7B interactions were employed, with the highest matrix dimension reaching 1.3 x 10^9 for the ground state of 216Rn with KHPE.

## Key numbers

- For 212Rn, two new isomers with lifetimes tau = 25(2) ns and tau = 12(2) ns were placed at 12.211 MeV and 12.548 MeV, respectively, from the 204Hg(13C,5n)212Rn reaction
- In 210Rn, the calculated 18_1^- and 19_1^- states are lower in energy than experimental data by 436 keV and 572 keV, respectively
- In 212Rn, the calculated 18_1^- state is 662 keV lower and the 19_1^- state is 1.065 MeV higher than experimental data
- In 212Rn, the collective 3_1^- state is calculated at 2.856 MeV, which is 735 keV higher than the proposed experimental value
- In 214Rn, the calculated 2_2^+ state appears at 1.558 MeV (KHPE) and 1.509 MeV (KHH7B), and the 6_2^+ state at 1.883 MeV (KHPE) and 1.842 MeV (KHH7B)
- In 207Rn, the experimental 3/2_1^- state is at 0.123 MeV, while the calculated value is approximately twice that energy
- In 209Rn, the calculated 27/2_1^+ state is lower in energy by 1 MeV relative to experimental data
- In 211Rn, the calculated 5/2_1^- state is 147 keV higher than experimental data, and the tentative state at 0.833 MeV is reproduced with 31 keV difference
- The 8_1^+ isomeric state in 208,210,212Rn arises from the pi(h9/2)^4 configuration with seniority v=2, with increasing probability as mass number increases
- In 212Rn, the 14_1^+ and 17_1^- isomeric states have dominant configuration probabilities of 98% and 99.59%, respectively
- In 213Rn, the 55/2_1^+ isomeric state has a configuration probability of 99.95% from pi(h9/2)(i13/2)^3 x nu(j15/2)

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies in the lead region by McGrory and Kuo (several decades prior) for nuclei with few valence nucleons, and by Coraggio et al. using realistic effective interactions from the Bonn-A nucleon-nucleon potential for N=126 isotones. The Strasbourg group reported large-scale shell-model results for Po-Pu with N=126 using Kuo-Herling interaction, and the Yoshinaga group reported results for masses 210-217 using extended pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interactions. This systematic study of the full 207-216Rn chain enables comparison with recent experimental data from Legnaro National Laboratory on 212Rn and provides predictions for unconfirmed spin-parity assignments and isomeric state configurations that can guide future experiments.
