# Nucleon electromagnetic form factors on the lattice and in chiral effective field theory

> We compute the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon in quenched lattice QCD, using non-perturbatively improved Wilson fermions, and compare the results with phenomenology and chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The authors compute nucleon electromagnetic form factors using quenched lattice QCD with non-perturbatively improved Wilson fermions and find that, after a linear chiral extrapolation, the results show qualitative agreement with recent experimental deviations from the dipole parametrization, though the extrapolated magnetic moments and magnetic dipole masses agree less well than the electric dipole masses.

## Method

The authors performed quenched Monte Carlo simulations at three lattice spacings (β = 6.0, 6.2, 6.4) using non-perturbatively O(a)-improved Wilson fermions with Jacobi smearing and non-relativistic projection. They computed isovector form factors directly from proton matrix elements of the current ūγμu - d̄γμd, extracting G_e and G_m from ratios of three-point to two-point functions via uncorrelated MINUIT fits, and then fitted the Q² dependence with a dipole ansatz for each quark mass.

## Key numbers

- Isovector electric dipole mass M_e^v extrapolated to physical pion mass: 0.789(10) GeV (combined fit), compared to experimental value 0.75 GeV
- Isovector magnetic dipole mass M_m^v extrapolated: 0.93(7) GeV (combined fit), compared to experimental value 0.79 GeV
- Isovector magnetic moment μ^v extrapolated: 3.7(4) (combined fit), compared to experimental value 4.71
- Proton electric dipole mass M_e^p extrapolated: 0.807(15) GeV (combined fit), compared to experimental value 0.84 GeV
- Proton magnetic moment μ^p extrapolated: 2.3(2) (combined fit), compared to experimental value 2.79
- Neutron magnetic dipole mass M_m^n extrapolated: 0.89(8) GeV (combined fit), compared to experimental value 0.84 GeV
- Neutron magnetic moment μ^n extrapolated: -1.47(17) (combined fit), compared to experimental value -1.91
- Dipole mass parameter m_D ~ 0.84 GeV from phenomenological dipole fits
- Proton magnetic moment μ^p ~ 2.79 and neutron magnetic moment μ^n ~ -1.91 in nuclear magnetons

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice investigations of nucleon form factors by Liu and Ji (late 1990s) and addresses the need for improved chiral extrapolation methods, as earlier linear extrapolations by the authors (1998) gave results larger than experiment. The paper compares with chiral effective field theory formulae from Hemmert and collaborators (1998) and enables future unquenched calculations with dynamical fermions, as well as more sophisticated chiral extrapolation schemes that combine leading non-analytic behavior with linear mass dependence.
