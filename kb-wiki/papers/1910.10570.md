# Asymptotic normalization coefficients from transfer reaction and R-martix analysis of direct capture in $^{22}$Ne(p,$γ$)$^{23}$Na reaction

> The $^{22}$Ne(p,$γ$)$^{23}$Na reaction in NeNa cycle plays an important role in the production of only stable sodium isotope $^{23}$Na. This nucleus is processed by the NeNa cycle during hot bottom burning (HBB) in asymptotic giant branch (AGB) stage of low metallicity intermediate mass stats (4 M$_O$ $\leq$ M $\leq$ 6 M$_O$). Recent measurements have addressed the uncertainty in the thermonuclear reaction rate of this reaction at relevant astrophysical energies through the identification of low lying resonances at E$_p$ = 71,105, 156.2, 189.5 and 259.7 keV. In addition, precise measurements of low energy behaviour of the non-resonant capture has also been performed and the contribution of the sub-threshold resonance at 8664 keV excitation in $^{23}$Na has been established. Here, in this article, we have presented a systematic R-matrix analysis of direct capture to the bound states and the decay of the sub-threshold resonance at 8664 keV to the ground state of $^{23}$Na. A finite range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) calculation has been performed for $^{22}$Ne($^3$He,d)$^{23}$Na transfer reaction data to extract the asymptotic normalization coeeficients (ANC-s) required to estimate the non-resonant capture cross sections or astrophysical S-factor values in R-matrix analysis. Simultaneous R-matrix analysis constrained with ANC-s from transfer calculation reproduced the astrophysical S-factor data over a wide energy window. The S$_{tot}^{DC}$(0) = 48.8$\pm$9.5 keV.b compares well with the result of Ferraro, {\it et al.} and has a lower uncertainty. The resultant thermonuclear reaction is slightly larger in 0.1 GK $\le$ T $\le$ 0.2 GK temperature range but otherwise in agreeent with Ferraro, {\it et al.}.

# Asymptotic normalization coefficients from transfer reaction and R-matrix analysis of direct capture in $^{22}$Ne(p,$\gamma$)$^{23}$Na reaction

## Key claim

The total direct-capture astrophysical S-factor at zero energy is S$_{tot}^{DC}$(0) = 48.8$\pm$9.5 keV.b, which has a lower uncertainty than the value of 50$\pm$12 keV.b reported by Ferraro et al. (2018), but the resulting thermonuclear reaction rate is only slightly larger in the 0.1 GK to 0.2 GK temperature range and otherwise agrees with Ferraro et al. (2018).

## Method

A finite-range distorted wave Born approximation (FRDWBA) calculation was performed for $^{22}$Ne($^3$He,d)$^{23}$Na transfer reaction data from Power et al. (15 MeV) and Hale et al. (20 MeV) to extract asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for the ground state and six excited states of $^{23}$Na, including the sub-threshold state at 8664 keV. These ANCs were then used as constraints in a simultaneous R-matrix analysis (using the code AZURE2) of direct capture data from Rolfs et al. (1975), Gorres et al. (1983), Kelly et al. (2017), and Ferraro et al. (2018), with high-energy background poles placed at 15 MeV excitation to account for internal capture contributions.

## Key numbers

- Total direct-capture astrophysical S-factor at zero energy: S$_{tot}^{DC}$(0) = 48.8$\pm$9.5 keV.b for the $^{22}$Ne(p,$\gamma$)$^{23}$Na reaction
- ANC for the sub-threshold state at 8664 keV: 143.7$\pm$15.2 fm$^{-1/2}$ from transfer calculation; a better R-matrix fit required ANC = 166$\pm$4 fm$^{-1/2}$
- Spectroscopic factor for the 8664 keV sub-threshold state: C$^2$S = 0.32$\pm$0.05 from transfer analysis, corresponding to ANC = 144 fm$^{-1/2}$
- Spectroscopic factor for the ground state (3/2$^+$, 1d$_{3/2}$): C$^2$S = 0.082$\pm$0.012, with ANC = 1.96$\pm$0.5 fm$^{-1/2}$
- Spectroscopic factor for the 440 keV state (5/2$^+$, 1d$_{5/2}$): C$^2$S = 0.38$\pm$0.08, with ANC = 4.69$\pm$0.8 fm$^{-1/2}$
- Resonance strength for the 8945 keV doublet (151 keV resonance): $\omega\gamma$ = (2.0$\pm$0.5)$\times$10$^{-7}$ keV for the 7/2$^-$ component and (3.93$\pm$0.9)$\times$10$^{-9}$ keV for the 3/2$^+$ component
- Proton partial width for the 8944 keV state (3/2$^+$, $l$=2): $\Gamma_p$ = (9.99$\pm$2.50)$\times$10$^{-8}$ keV
- Proton partial width for the 8945 keV state (7/2$^-$, $l$=3): $\Gamma_p$ = (9.83$\pm$2.24)$\times$10$^{-10}$ keV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Ferraro et al. (2018), who measured off-resonance capture cross sections at proton energies of 188, 205, 250, and 310 keV and reported S$_{DC}$(0) = 50$\pm$12 keV.b, by providing a systematic R-matrix treatment constrained by ANCs from transfer reaction data. The results resolve discrepancies between the NACRE I compilation rate and those from Hale et al. (2001), Iliadis et al., and the STARLIB compilation in the 0.08 GK to 0.25 GK temperature window relevant to hot bottom burning in asymptotic giant branch stars. The extracted ANCs and resonance strengths enable more precise reaction rate calculations for the NeNa cycle, which is responsible for the observed anti-correlation of surface oxygen and sodium abundances in galactic globular clusters.
