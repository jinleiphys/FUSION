# The Proton Charge Radius from Dimuon Photoproduction off the Proton

> We investigate the feasibility of measuring the proton charge radius through dimuon photoproduction off a proton target. Our findings indicate that the Bethe-Heitler mechanism, which dominates at small momentum transfers, allows for an extraction of the proton electromagnetic form factors in the extremely low $Q^2$ region below $10^{-3}$ GeV$^2$ in the spacelike region, when the incident photon beam energy exceeds several hundred MeV. The optimal kinematical region and a sensitivity study of the proton charge radius from dimuon photoproduction are presented. Such a measurement is expected to provide an alternative to the elastic muon-proton scattering measurements such as MUSE at PSI and AMBER at CERN.

## Key claim

The Bethe-Heitler mechanism in dimuon photoproduction off a proton can provide an alternative extraction of the muon-proton scattering value of the proton charge radius, but only in the specific kinematic region where the momentum transfer squared is below 0.02 GeV² and the timelike Compton scattering background is suppressed to below 0.1%.

## Method

The authors computed the differential cross section for the reaction γ + p → p + μ⁺ + μ⁻, separating the Bethe-Heitler (BH) and timelike Compton scattering (TCS) contributions. They identified the optimal kinematic region by mapping the ratio dσ_TCS/dσ_BH in the (-t, m_{ll}²) plane for a photon lab energy of 1.2 GeV, then performed a Monte Carlo sensitivity study using the von Neumann rejection method to generate pseudodata and extract the proton charge radius via Bayesian fitting with the DRAM algorithm.

## Key numbers

- The optimal kinematic region is 0.001 GeV² < -t < 0.02 GeV² with E_γ = 1.2 GeV and cosθ_{p'}^{lab} = 0.7, where the TCS background is smaller than 0.1%
- The integrated BH cross section over -t from 0.001 to 0.02 GeV² is approximately 124 nb (order 100 nb)
- With a photon flux of 10⁷ photons/s and a 1 m TPC target at 20 bar, approximately 5 × 10⁶ events would be available after several months of data collection
- The extracted proton charge radius from the Monte Carlo simulation is 0.848(8) fm, corresponding to a 1% level measurement with 0.5% cross section uncertainty
- The minimal momentum transfer squared Q² = -t can reach 10⁻³ GeV² when the photon lab energy exceeds 0.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Pauk (2015) to test lepton universality using similar reactions and the approach by Carlson (2018) for measuring the deuteron charge radius. It provides a theoretical feasibility study for an alternative to the elastic muon-proton scattering experiments MUSE at PSI (Downie, 2014) and AMBER at CERN (Adams, 2018), which face challenges in constructing a clean muon beam. The proposed measurement would directly yield the muon-proton scattering value of the proton charge radius, which has not yet been measured, and would test lepton flavor universality in the Standard Model.
