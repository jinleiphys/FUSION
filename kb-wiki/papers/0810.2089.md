# Can stochastic quantization evade the sign problem? -- the relativistic Bose gas at finite chemical potential

> A nonperturbative study of field theories with a complex action, such as QCD at finite baryon density, is difficult due to the sign problem. We show that the relativistic Bose gas at finite chemical potential has a sign and `Silver Blaze' problem, similar to QCD. We then apply stochastic quantization and complex Langevin dynamics to study this theory with nonperturbative lattice simulations. Independence of chemical potential at small and a transition to a condensed phase at large chemical potential are found. Lattices of size N^4, with N=4,6,8,10, are used. We show that the sign problem is severe, however, we find that it has no negative effect using this approach. This improves the prospects of applying stochastic quantization to QCD at nonzero density.

## Key claim

Stochastic quantization via complex Langevin dynamics can successfully simulate the relativistic Bose gas at finite chemical potential, evading the sign problem and reproducing the expected "Silver Blaze" behavior (exact independence of observables from chemical potential below a critical threshold), provided the chemical potential remains below the critical value where the free theory becomes unstable.

## Method

The author discretizes a complex scalar field theory with quartic self-interaction on four-dimensional Euclidean lattices of size N^4 (N=4,6,8,10). The real and imaginary parts of the scalar field are complexified, and the resulting complex Langevin equations are integrated numerically using a step size of 5e-5 for 5 million timesteps. The density and field modulus are measured as functions of chemical potential, and the phase-quenched theory is also simulated for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Lattice sizes used: N^4 with N = 4, 6, 8, 10
- Langevin step size: eps = 5e-5
- Total Langevin timesteps: 5e6
- Parameters: m = 1, lambda = 1
- Critical chemical potential: mu_c approximately 1.15 (mean field estimate yields mu_c = 1.15 from 4 sinh^2(mu_c/2) = m^2 + 4 lambda <|phi|^2>)
- Free theory critical chemical potential: mu_c^0 = 0.9624 for the parameters used
- Chemical potential range studied: 0 <= mu <= 1.7

## Context

This work extends the application of complex Langevin dynamics, previously used on U(1) and SU(3) one-link models with mild sign problems (Aarts 2008), to a bosonic field theory that shares the sign and Silver Blaze problems with QCD at finite baryon density. The relativistic Bose gas provides a testbed where the exact mu-independence below mu_c is known theoretically, and the method successfully reproduces this behavior. The results improve prospects for applying stochastic quantization to QCD at nonzero density, where the sign problem has hindered nonperturbative studies using methods such as reweighting (Fodor 2001, Fodor 2002), Taylor expansion (Allton 2002, Gavai 2003), imaginary chemical potential (deForcrand 2002, D'Elia 2002), canonical ensemble (Kratochvila 2005, Ejiri 2008), and density of states (Fodor 2007).
