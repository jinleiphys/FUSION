# Study of the $DK$ interaction with femtoscopic correlation functions

> The $DK$ interaction in isospin zero is known to be attractive to such an extent that a bound state can be generated, which can be associated with the mysterious $D_{s0}^*(2317)$. In this work, we calculate the $DK$ femtoscopic correlation function in the coupled-channel framework for different source sizes that can directly probe the strongly attractive $DK$ interaction, which is otherwise inaccessible due to the unstable nature of $D$ and $K$ mesons, and therefore can help elucidate the nature of $D_{s0}^*(2317)$. We further generalize the study of source size dependence to various interactions, ranging from repulsive, weakly attractive, moderately attractive, and strongly attractive, in a square-well model. We hope that our study can motivate future experimental measurements of the $DK$ correlation function and other interactions relevant to the understanding of the nature of the many exotic hadrons discovered so far.

## Key claim

The paper argues that femtoscopic correlation functions of $DK$ pairs, particularly their dependence on the source size of the collision system, can provide a direct experimental probe of the strongly attractive $DK$ interaction responsible for the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ bound state, which is otherwise inaccessible due to the unstable nature of $D$ and $K$ mesons.

## Method

The authors employed leading-order chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) to describe the $DK$ interaction in a coupled-channel framework including $D_s^+\pi^0$, $D^0K^+$, $D^+K^0$, and $D_s^+\eta$ channels. They solved the coupled-channel scattering equation (Kadyshevsky equation) with a Gaussian regulator, tuning the cutoff parameter $\Lambda_F = 1107$ MeV to reproduce the $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ mass at 2317.8 MeV. The $S$-wave scattering wave functions were then used in the Koonin-Pratt formula with a static spherical Gaussian source to compute the femtoscopic correlation functions for various source sizes $R$.

## Key numbers

- The $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ mass is set to 2317.8 MeV, with the $D^0K^+$ threshold at 2358.52 MeV, yielding a binding energy of 40.72 MeV.
- The $D^0K^+$ scattering length is $a = 0.73$ fm and the effective range is $r_{\rm eff} = -2.11$ fm.
- The cutoff parameter $\Lambda_F$ is fine-tuned to 1107 MeV.
- The pion decay constant used is $f_0 = 92.2$ MeV.
- For the square-well model study, the reduced mass is 470 MeV, the range parameter $d = 2.5$ fm, and the depth parameters $V_0$ are 25 MeV (repulsive), -10 MeV (weakly attractive), -25 MeV (moderately attractive), and -75 MeV (strongly attractive), yielding exact binding energies of 2.07 MeV (moderate) and 38.08 MeV (strong).

## Context

This work extends the femtoscopy methodology previously applied to $DD^*$ and $D\bar{D}^*$ systems by Kamiya et al. (2022) for studying $T_{cc}^+(3875)$ and $X(3872)$, now targeting the deeper bound $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ state. The $DK$ interaction at leading-order ChPT has been established by Kolomeitsev (2003), Gamermann (2006), and Guo (2006), and the approach enables future experimental measurements by ALICE and other collaborations to directly test the hadronic molecular picture of $D_{s0}^*(2317)$ and other exotic hadrons through source-size-dependent correlation functions.
