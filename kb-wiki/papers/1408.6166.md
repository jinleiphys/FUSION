# Global phenomenological descriptions of nuclear odd-even mass staggering

> We examine the general nature of nuclear odd-even mass differences by employing neutron and proton mass relations that emphasize these effects. The most recent mass tables are used. The possibility of a neutron excess dependence of the staggering is examined in detail in separate regions defined by the main nuclear shells, and a clear change in this dependency is found at $Z=50$ for both neutrons and protons. A further separation into odd and even neutron (proton) number produces very accurate local descriptions of the mass differences for each type of nucleons. These odd-even effects are combined into a global phenomenological expression, ready to use in a binding energy formula. The results deviate from previous parametrizations, and in particular found to be significantly superior to a recent two term, $A^{-1}$ dependence.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear odd-even mass staggering exhibits a clear change in neutron-excess dependence at the proton shell closure Z=50 for both neutrons and protons, and that separating nuclei by odd/even neutron and proton numbers yields very accurate local descriptions, though the authors note that the overall RMSE values (always less than 0.20 MeV) are comparable to the underlying chaotic component of 0.1-0.2 MeV in nuclear masses, suggesting that division into individually fitted regions may be unnecessary.

## Method

The authors extracted neutron and proton odd-even staggering using three-point mass relations (Δ_n and Δ_p) from Eqs. (Dn) and (Dp), which subtract liquid-drop model contributions to isolate odd-even effects. They then fitted these extracted values to a scaled quadratic neutron-excess dependence A^(1/3) Δ_(n,p) = a ((N-Z)/A)^2 + b, dividing nuclei into regions defined by major nuclear shells (N=28, 50, 82, 126; Z=28, 50, 82) and further separating into even-even, even-odd, odd-even, and odd-odd configurations. Shell-affected nuclei and those with A<50 were excluded from the fits.

## Key numbers

- The RMSE for Δ_(n,p) values is always less than 0.20 MeV across all fitted regions and configurations, with the smallest RMSE of 0.09 MeV for Δ_n in the region 50<N<82, Z≥50 for even-even nuclei
- For even-even nuclei across all regions, the fitted parameter a for A^(1/3) Δ_n is -42(5) MeV and b is 6.7(2) MeV, with RMSE of 0.81 MeV for A^(1/3) Δ_n and 0.16 MeV for Δ_n
- For even-even nuclei in the region 82<N, Z≥50, the fitted a for A^(1/3) Δ_n is -60(7) MeV and b is 7.3(2) MeV, with RMSE of 0.71 MeV for A^(1/3) Δ_n and 0.12 MeV for Δ_n
- The constant shifts b for all fits lie between 5 and 8 MeV, each with uncertainties of about 0.2 MeV
- Comparing quadratic versus linear neutron-excess dependence yields a difference of only 0.01 MeV out of a value of 0.16 MeV in RMSE for Δ_(n,p)

## Context

This work extends earlier investigations of odd-even staggering by Vogelaar (1984) and Jensen et al. (Aksel) who suggested a neutron-excess dependence, and by Friedman and Bertsch (2009) who proposed a two-term expression Δ = c1 + c2/A. The authors use the recent mass compilation by Audi et al. and find their parametrization significantly superior to that two-term A^(-1) dependence. The results enable improved phenomenological descriptions of odd-even mass differences for use in semi-empirical mass models, and the observed decrease of staggering with neutron excess suggests possible vanishing of odd-even effects near the neutron dripline, though the authors caution this is based on extrapolation.
