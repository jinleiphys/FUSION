# Spin asymmetries in quasielastic charged current neutrino-nucleon scattering

> The work concerns the quasielastic charged current neutrino-neutron and antineutrino-proton interactions. Single, double, and triple spin asymmetries are computed and analyzed. The spin asymmetries are sensitive to the axial form factor of the nucleon. In particular, the target-recoil double spin asymmetry and the lepton-target-recoil triple spin asymmetry depend strongly on the axial form factor of the nucleon. Indeed, the sign and shape of these components depend on the axial mass parameter. All the asymmetries, except the lepton polarization, are observables well suited to study the nonstandard interactions described by the second class current contribution.

## Key claim

The paper claims that several spin asymmetries in quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleon scattering are sensitive to the axial form factor of the nucleon and to nonstandard interactions described by second class currents, but this sensitivity is conditional: the target-recoil double spin asymmetry and the lepton-target-recoil triple spin asymmetry depend strongly on the axial mass parameter, with their sign and shape changing as a function of \(M_A\), while the lepton polarization asymmetry is insensitive to both the axial form factor and second class currents.

## Method

The authors computed single, double, and triple spin asymmetries for the quasielastic charged-current processes \(\nu_\mu n \to \mu^- p\) and \(\overline{\nu}_\mu p \to \mu^+ n\) using the standard electroweak nucleon vertex with vector and axial form factors, including a dipole parametrization for the axial form factor \(F_A(t) = g_A / (1 - t/M_A^2)^2\) and a monopole parametrization for the second class current form factor \(F_3^A(t) = F_3^A(0) / (1 - t/M_A^2)\). The spin asymmetries were expressed in terms of longitudinal, transverse, and normal components relative to the scattering plane, and their dependence on neutrino energy and momentum transfer \(Q^2\) was evaluated numerically for varying values of \(M_A\) (0.8 to 1.2 GeV) and \(F_3^A(0)\) (-1, 0, 1).

## Key numbers

- The axial form factor dipole parametrization uses \(g_A = 1.2723 \pm 0.0023\) and a default axial mass \(M_A = 1.0\) GeV.
- The vector dipole mass is \(M_V = 0.84\) GeV; the Fermi constant is \(G_F = 1.16639 \times 10^{-5}\) GeV\(^{-2}\); the Cabibbo angle gives \(\cos\theta_C = 0.9737\).
- For \(\overline{\nu}_\mu p\) scattering at \(E = 1\) GeV, the roots of the equations \(\mathcal{C}_{NN'}^{TT} = 0\) and \(\mathcal{C}_{NN'}^{NN} = 0\) depend monotonically on \(M_A\), with the latter having no roots for too large \(M_A\) and the former having no roots for too small \(M_A\); at \(M_A \approx 1.0\) GeV the roots of \(\mathcal{C}_{NN'}^{NN} = 0\) vary monotonically with \(M_A\).
- The components \(\mathcal{C}_{NN'}^{TT}\) and \(\mathcal{C}_{NN'}^{NN}\) change sign from positive to negative as \(M_A\) is reduced from 1.2 GeV to 0.8 GeV for \(\overline{\nu}_\mu p\) scattering at \(E = 1\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of polarization in quasielastic neutrino-nucleon scattering by Adler (1963), Pais (1971), Block (1965), and Llewellyn-Smith (1971), and more recent work by Bilenky and Christova (2013) on axial contributions to nucleon polarization and by Fatima et al. (2018) on second class current contributions to lepton and recoil nucleon polarizations. The authors previously showed that spin observables contain information about nonresonant background in single pion production (Graczyk et al., 2017, 2019). The results enable future experimental extraction of the axial form factor and searches for second class currents using spin asymmetry measurements in neutrino-nucleon scattering, which could reduce systematic uncertainties in neutrino oscillation parameter determinations.
