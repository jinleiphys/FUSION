# Peripheral N$α$ Scattering: A Tool For Identifying The Two Pion Exchange Component Of The NN Potential

> We study elastic N$α$ scattering and produce a quantitative correlation between the range of the effective potential and the energy of the system. This allows the identification of the waves and energies for which the scattering may be said to be peripheral. We then show that the corresponding phase shifts are sensitive to the tail of the NN potential, which is due to the exchange of two pions. However, the present uncertainties in the experimental phase shifts prevent the use of N$α$ scattering to discriminate the existing models for the NN interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that peripheral N-alpha scattering phase shifts are sensitive to the tail of the NN potential arising from two-pion exchange, but the current experimental uncertainties in the phase shifts are too large to allow discrimination between existing NN potential models.

## Method

The authors construct an effective N-alpha potential by folding several realistic NN potentials (Paris, Bonn, supersoft core C, dTRS, Argonne, and chiral) into a Gaussian alpha-particle wave function, keeping only central and spin-orbit components. They then solve the Schrodinger equation using the variable phase method to calculate phase shifts for peripheral partial waves and determine the spatial region of the potential that contributes to each phase shift at various energies.

## Key numbers

- The alpha particle has a root-mean-square radius of about 1.6 fm, and the tail of the effective potential is defined as beginning at 2.0 fm
- For the Argonne potential, more than 95% of the phase shift for G_{7/2} and G_{9/2} waves comes from the tail region (r > 2.0 fm) for energies up to 50 MeV
- For the H_{9/2} and H_{11/2} waves, more than 95% of the phase shift comes from the tail region for energies up to more than 80 MeV
- The radii R_5 (where 95% of the phase shift is generated from r > R_5) for the G_{7/2} wave ranges from 3.24 fm at 5 MeV to 1.56 fm at 80 MeV
- The radii R_5 for the H_{9/2} wave ranges from 3.62 fm at 5 MeV to 2.26 fm at 80 MeV

## Context

This work extends the program of understanding the intermediate-range two-pion exchange component of the NN potential, which was developed through the Paris potential (Lacombe et al., 1980) and the Bonn potential (Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster, 1987), as well as chiral approaches (Ordonez and van Kolck, 1994; Robilotta and da Rocha, 1997). The paper shows that N-alpha scattering could in principle discriminate between these models, but the existing experimental phase shift analyses from Houdayer et al. (1978), Saito (1979), Krupp et al. (1984), and Burzynski et al. (1989) show large discrepancies that prevent such discrimination. The work enables future studies with more precise experimental phase shifts and more sophisticated alpha wave functions including D-wave components.
