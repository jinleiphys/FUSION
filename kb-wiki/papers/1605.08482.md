# Electric dipole response of neutron-rich Calcium isotopes in relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation

> New results for electric dipole strength in the chain of even-even Calcium isotopes with the mass numbers A = 40 - 54 are presented. Starting from the covariant Lagrangian of Quantum Hadrodynamics, spectra of collective vibrations (phonons) and phonon-nucleon coupling vertices for $J \leq 6$ and normal parity were computed in a self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA). These vibrations coupled to Bogoliubov two-quasiparticle configurations (2q$\otimes$phonon) form the model space for the calculations of the dipole response function in the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA). The results for giant dipole resonance in the latter approach are compared to those obtained in RQRPA and to available data. Evolution of the dipole strength with neutron number is investigated for both high-frequency giant dipole resonance (GDR) and low-lying strength. Development of a pygmy resonant structure on the low-energy shoulder of GDR is traced and analyzed in terms of transition densities. A dependence of the pygmy dipole strength on the isospin asymmetry parameter is extracted.

## Key claim

The relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) can describe the evolution of electric dipole strength across the calcium isotopic chain from A=40 to A=54, including the development of a pygmy dipole resonance on the low-energy shoulder of the giant dipole resonance, but the approach fails to reproduce the low-lying phonons in doubly-magic \({}^{40}\)Ca and yields GDR centroids that are systematically 1.0-1.5 MeV lower than experimental values at double shell closures.

## Method

The authors start from the covariant Lagrangian of Quantum Hadrodynamics and compute collective phonon spectra (multipolarities \(J \leq 6\), normal parity) and phonon-nucleon coupling vertices within a self-consistent relativistic quasiparticle random phase approximation (RQRPA) using the NL3* parameter set. These phonons are then coupled to Bogoliubov two-quasiparticle configurations (2q\(\otimes\)phonon) to form the model space for solving the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nuclear response function in the relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA). The dipole response is obtained from the strength function via the electromagnetic dipole operator, and transition densities are analyzed to separate pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) from giant dipole resonance (GDR) contributions.

## Key numbers

- The GDR centroid in \({}^{40}\)Ca and \({}^{42}\)Ca is approximately 2 MeV lower than the experimental centroid.
- In \({}^{208}\)Pb and \({}^{132}\)Sn, the GDR centroids from R(Q)TBA are also 1.0-1.5 MeV lower than experimental values.
- The pairing gap used for open-shell isotopes is \(\Delta_{max} = 2.0\) MeV, close to the empirical value \(\Delta_{emp} = 12/\sqrt{A}\) MeV.
- The low-lying dipole spectra shift by approximately 2-3 MeV when the pairing gap is varied between \(\Delta_{max}\) and \(\Delta_{min}\).
- The neutron emission threshold in \({}^{44}\)Ca is 11.13 MeV; in \({}^{48}\)Ca it is 9.95 MeV.
- The separation energy between PDR and GDR for the entire calcium isotopic chain is set at 13.0 MeV based on transition density analysis.
- For the isospin asymmetry parameter range \(0.04 \leq \alpha^2 \leq 0.06\), the pygmy strength has very similar values across Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb isotopic chains.
- In \({}^{50}\)Ca, the pygmy strength calculated with \(\Delta_{min}\) pairing gap is almost a factor of two larger than with \(\Delta_{max}\).

## Context

This work extends the RQTBA approach, previously applied successfully to medium-mass and heavy nuclei such as Pb, Sn, and Ni isotopes (Litvinova, Ring, Tselyaev, 2008, 2009), to the lighter calcium isotopic chain including neutron-rich isotopes beyond the closed-shell \({}^{48}\)Ca. The results enable systematic comparison of pygmy dipole strength versus isospin asymmetry across multiple isotopic chains, which is relevant for constraining the nuclear symmetry energy and equation of state (Roca-Maza, Vinas, Centelles et al., 2015). The study also identifies limitations of the standard RQTBA framework for doubly-magic nuclei like \({}^{40}\)Ca, where low-lying positive-parity phonons are not reproduced by RQRPA, motivating future extensions to include pairing vibrations and isovector phonons.
