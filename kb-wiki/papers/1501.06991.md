# Quantum decoherence in the entanglement entropy of a composite particle and its relationship to coarse graining in the Husimi function

> I investigate quantum decoherence in a one-body density matrix of a composite particle consisting of two correlated particles. Because of a two-body correlation in the composite particle, quantum decoherence occurs in the one-body density matrix that has been reduced from the two-body density matrix. As the delocalization of the distribution of the composite particle grows, the entanglement entropy increases, and the system can be well-described by a semi-classical approximation, wherein the center position of the composite particle can be regarded as a classical coordinate. I connect the quantum decoherence in the one-body density matrix of a composite particle to the coarse graining in a phase space distribution function of a single particle and associate it with the Husimi function.

## Key claim

Quantum decoherence in the one-body density matrix of a composite particle (two correlated particles) is shown to be equivalent to coarse graining in the phase-space distribution of a single particle, specifically the Husimi function, under the condition that the internal degrees of freedom of the composite particle are decoupled from its center-of-mass motion.

## Method

The author models a composite particle as a two-body bound state using a harmonic oscillator wave function in the generator coordinate method, with the two particles having masses \(m\) and \(um\). The one-body density matrix for particle 1 is obtained by tracing out particle 2 from the two-body density matrix. The Wigner function of this reduced density matrix is then expressed as a coarse-grained distribution of the center-of-mass Wigner function, with the smearing width determined by the intrinsic wave function of the composite particle. For the equal-mass case (\(u=1\)), this coarse-grained distribution is shown to be exactly twice the \(\hbar/2\)-Husimi function of the center-of-mass state.

## Key numbers

- For a free composite particle in a finite volume \(V\), the Rényi-2 entanglement entropy is \(\SR = \log_e V_{\rm eff} - \frac{1}{2}\log_e(2\pi)\), where \(V_{\rm eff} = V/\tilde{b}\) and \(\tilde{b} = b/\sqrt{u_2}\).
- For a composite particle in an external harmonic oscillator potential with Gaussian distribution \(F(\R) = e^{-\R^2/(2B^2)}/(B^2\pi)^{1/4}\), the Rényi-2 EE is \(\SR = \frac{1}{2}\log_e(1+v_{\rm eff}^2) - \frac{1}{2}\log_e\gamma\), where \(v_{\rm eff}=B/b\) and \(\gamma = (1+(u+1)v_{\rm eff}^2)/(1+uv_{\rm eff}^2)\).
- The semi-classical approximation for the Rényi-2 EE agrees with the exact result to within 10% error for \(u=1\) when \(v_{\rm eff} \ge 2\), with better agreement for larger mass ratio \(u=8\).
- The Wigner-Shannon EE has a constant shift of \(1-\log_e 2\) from the Rényi-2 EE.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of entanglement entropy in nuclear cluster systems (Kanada-En'yo, 2013, 2014), where the EE was enhanced by delocalization of cluster distributions. It connects the quantum decoherence from reduced density matrices to the coarse-graining approach of the Husimi function (Husimi, 1940; Takahashi, 1986; Nakamura, 1988) and the Wehrl and Rényi-Wehrl entropies (Wehrl, 1978; Gnutzmann, 2001). The correspondence established here enables interpreting entropy production in the one-body density matrix as thermalization, where the intrinsic kinetic energy of the composite particle is converted into heat, and provides a foundation for extending these ideas to time-dependent systems where internal and external degrees of freedom are decoupled.
