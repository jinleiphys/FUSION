# Large-mass neutron stars with hyperonization

> Within a density-dependent relativistic mean-field model using in-medium meson-hadron coupling constants and meson masses, we explore effects of in-medium hyperon interactions on properties of neutron stars. It is found that the hyperonic constituents in large-mass neutron stars can not be simply ruled out, while the recently measured mass of the millisecond pulsar J1614-2230 can constrain significantly the in-medium hyperon interactions. Moreover, effects of nuclear symmetry energy on hyperonization in neutron stars are also discussed.

# Large-mass neutron stars with hyperonization

## Key claim

Hyperonic constituents in large-mass neutron stars cannot be simply ruled out by the observation of the 2-solar-mass pulsar J1614-2230, provided that the in-medium hyperon interactions are chosen such that hyperons experience a different density dependence from nucleons (the separable case, SC), which suppresses hyperon fractions at high densities and restiffens the equation of state.

## Method

The authors extended a density-dependent relativistic mean-field (RMF) model based on Brown-Rho scaling, using parameter sets SLC and SLCd that differ in their symmetry energy slope (L = 92.3 MeV and 61.5 MeV, respectively), to include hyperonic degrees of freedom (Λ, Σ, Ξ). They considered two prescriptions for hyperon couplings: the usual case (UC) with density-independent coupling ratios, and the separable case (SC) where the meson-hyperon coupling constants are split into density-dependent and density-independent parts, with hyperon potentials at saturation density fixed to U^(N)_Λ = -30 MeV, U^(N)_Σ = -30 MeV, and U^(N)_Ξ = -18 MeV. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations were solved to obtain mass-radius relations for neutron stars.

## Key numbers

- The mass of pulsar J1614-2230 was accurately determined to be 1.976 ± 0.04 M⊙ (Demorest 2010).
- In the SC with the SLC model, maximum neutron star masses around 2 M⊙ were obtained, with corresponding radii ranging from 9.3 to 9.5 km.
- In the UC, the maximum neutron star mass reached only about 1.4 M⊙.
- The slope parameter L of the symmetry energy at saturation density is L = 92.3 MeV for SLC and L = 61.5 MeV for SLCd.
- The onset density of hyperons in the SC varied within the region 2.2–3ρ₀ as the ratio g⁰_ωΛ/g⁰_ωN increased from 0.2 to 0.8; a large rise up to 4.5ρ₀ could be obtained if the Λ potential at saturation density were reduced to 0 MeV.
- For the SC with SLCd, the onset density for hyperons was about 0.2ρ₀ larger than with SLC.
- For 1.4 M⊙ neutron stars in the SC, the radius ranged from 11.4 km (SLCd) to 12.3 km (SLC) for f_σY = 0.9.
- The maximum mass was reduced by only about 1% when softening the symmetry energy from SLC to SLCd.

## Context

This work extends earlier density-dependent RMF models (Jiang 2007, 2007b) that were constrained by nuclear giant monopole resonances, heavy-ion collective flow data, and isospin diffusion data. It directly addresses the claim that the 2 M⊙ pulsar J1614-2230 (Demorest 2010) rules out almost all hyperon equations of state, a conclusion supported by Brueckner approach calculations (Schulze 2011). The authors show that by using the separable case (SC) with different in-medium interactions for hyperons and nucleons, hyperonized neutron stars can reach 2 M⊙, similar to earlier efforts that invoked strong repulsions for hyperons (Hofmann 2001, Takatsuka 2002, Stoks 2007, Delfino 2008) or nonlinear self-interactions with hidden strangeness (Bednarek 2011). The results enable further studies of thermal evolution and direct Urca cooling with hyperon superfluidity, as discussed by Tsuruta (2009) and Page (2009).
