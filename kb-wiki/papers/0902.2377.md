# Exposing the Noncollectivity in Elliptic Flow

> We show that backward-forward elliptic anisotropy correlation provides an experimentally accessible observable which distinguishes between collective and non-collective contributions to the observed elliptic anisotropy $v_2$ in relativistic heavy ion collisions. The measurement of this observable will reveal the momentum scale at which collective expansion seizes and where the elliptic anisotropy is dominated by (semi)-hard processes.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the backward-forward correlation of elliptic anisotropy, \(\mathcal{C}_{FB}[p_T]\), can experimentally distinguish collective (hydrodynamic flow) from non-collective (jet-like) contributions to the measured \(v_2\) in relativistic heavy ion collisions, provided the rapidity gap between bins is chosen to prevent a single jet from contributing to both bins simultaneously.

## Method

The authors define a correlation observable \(\mathcal{C}_{FB}[p_T] = \langle V_2^F \cdot V_2^B \rangle / (\langle V_2^F \rangle \cdot \langle V_2^B \rangle)\), where \(V_2^{F/B}\) is the total elliptic anisotropy in forward/backward rapidity bins. They model the total particle yield as a sum of a collective hydrodynamic component (using a blast-wave parametrization from Teaney (2003)) and a non-collective jet component, with an interpolation function \(g(p_t)\) giving the relative jet weight. Using a two-component parametrization fitted to PHENIX \(v_2\) data for AuAu collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV (0-20% centrality), they compute \(\mathcal{C}_{FB}\) for three scenarios of the jet-only anisotropy \(v_2^{\mathcal{J}}(p_t)\) at low \(p_t\).

## Key numbers

- The transition from collective to non-collective anisotropy is expected around \(p_t \simeq 2-4\) GeV.
- The blast-wave model parameters used: \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV, \(T_o = 170\) MeV, \(R_o = 10\) fm, \(\tau_o = 7\) fm, \(u_o = 0.7\), \(u_2 = 0.06\).
- The jet-only anisotropy at high \(p_t\) is taken as \(v_2^{\mathcal{J}} = 0.07\) (constant for \(p_t > 6\) GeV).
- For scenario (a) (constant \(v_2^{\mathcal{J}}\) at all \(p_t\)): fit parameters \(P_C = 1.4\) GeV, \(P_W = 2\) GeV.
- For scenarios (b) and (c) (jet anisotropy dropping at low \(p_t\)): fit parameters \(P_C = 2.4\) GeV, \(P_W = 1.8\) GeV.
- The correlation \(\mathcal{C}_{FB}\) approaches 1 when the collective component dominates (\(g \to 0\)) and approaches 0 when the non-collective component dominates (\(g \to 1\)).

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow \(v_2\) at RHIC, where large \(v_2\) was interpreted as evidence for near-perfect fluidity with minimal shear viscosity (Csernai (2006), Lacey (2006)). It addresses the ambiguity in the \(p_t\) region \(p_t \lesssim 1.5\) GeV where both collective hydrodynamic expansion and non-collective jet-quenching contributions may coexist, potentially biasing viscosity extractions from viscous hydrodynamics comparisons. The proposed observable enables direct experimental measurement of the transition scale and the magnitude of non-collective \(v_2\) at low \(p_t\), which could constrain viscous corrections and clarify the origin of the decreasing \(v_2\) at intermediate \(p_t\).
