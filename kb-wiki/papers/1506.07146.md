# Light-Like Wilson Line in QCD Without Path Ordering

> Unlike the Wilson line in QED the Wilson line in QCD contains path ordering. In this paper we get rid of the path ordering in the light-like Wilson line in QCD by simplifying all the infinite number of non-commuting terms in the SU(3) pure gauge. We prove that the light-like Wilson line in QCD naturally emerges when path integral formulation of QCD is used to prove factorization of soft and collinear divergences at all order in coupling constant in QCD processes at high energy colliders.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the path ordering in the light-like Wilson line in QCD can be eliminated by simplifying all infinite non-commuting terms in the SU(3) pure gauge, yielding an expression that depends only on the endpoints and involves covariant derivatives and derivatives of the gauge field with respect to the coupling constant.

## Method

The author starts from the SU(3) pure gauge potential expressed in terms of an exponential series of non-commuting terms, then derives a relation between the covariant derivative of the gauge parameter and the derivative of the gauge field with respect to the coupling constant. Using this relation, the non-abelian phase factor is rewritten without path ordering. The result is then applied to show that the light-like Wilson line emerges naturally in the path integral formulation of QCD when proving factorization of soft and collinear divergences for the quark distribution function.

## Key numbers

* The SU(3) pure gauge is expressed as \(A^{\mu a}(x)=\partial^\mu \omega^b(x)\left[\frac{e^{gM(x)}-1}{gM(x)}\right]_{ab}\) where \(M_{ab}(x)=f^{abc}\omega^c(x)\), containing infinite powers of the coupling constant \(g\).
* The light-like Wilson line without path ordering is given by \({\cal P}{\rm exp}[ig\int_{x_i}^{x_f} dx^\mu { A}^a_\mu(x)T^a] = {\rm exp}[ig T^a[\frac{1}{2~l \cdot { D}[A(x_f)]} l \cdot \frac{d[gA(x_f)]}{dg}]^a] \times {\rm exp}[-ig T^b[\frac{1}{2~l \cdot { D}[A(x_i)]} l \cdot \frac{d[gA(x_i)]}{dg}]^b]\).
* The non-abelian phase or gauge link without path ordering is \({\cal P}e^{-ig \int_{0}^{\infty} d\lambda l\cdot { A}^a(x+l\lambda)T^a }={\rm exp}[ig T^a[\frac{1}{2~l \cdot { D}[A(x)]} l \cdot \frac{d[gA(x)]}{dg}]^a]\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous path integral proofs of factorization for NRQCD in heavy quarkonium production (Nayak 2013) and for the gluon distribution function (Nayak 2014), now providing the analogous proof for the quark distribution function. The result removes path ordering from the light-like Wilson line, which is the key object used to factorize soft and collinear divergences in QCD at high energy colliders. The derivation relies on the property that a light-like quark produces an SU(3) pure gauge field, and the resulting expression enables a cleaner path integral treatment of factorization theorems, complementing earlier diagrammatic approaches by Collins and Stermann (1991) and Nayak and Stermann (2006).
