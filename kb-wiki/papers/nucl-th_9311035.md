# Relativistic three-particle dynamical equations: I. Theoretical development

> Starting from the two-particle Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation and integrating over the time component of momentum, we rederive three dimensional scattering integral equations satisfying constraints of relativistic unitarity and covariance, first derived by Weinberg and by Blankenbecler and Sugar. These two-particle equations are shown to be related by a transformation of variables. Hence we show how to perform and relate identical dynamical calculation using these two equations. Similarly, starting from the Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equation for the three-particle system and integrating over the time component of momentum, we derive several three dimensional three-particle scattering equations satisfying constraints of relativistic unitarity and covariance. We relate two of these three-particle equations by a transformation of variables as in the two-particle case. The three-particle equations we derive are very practical and suitable for performing relativistic scattering calculations.

## Key claim

The paper derives three-dimensional three-particle scattering equations from the Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equation by integrating over the time component of momentum, under the assumption that the potential and t-matrix are independent of that time component. The resulting equations satisfy relativistic unitarity and covariance only below three- and four-particle thresholds, and the derivation is limited to a truncated Fock space with two or three particles in the ladder approximation.

## Method

Starting from the two-particle Bethe-Salpeter equation in the ladder approximation, the authors perform a contour integration over the time component of momentum (p0 for instant-form variables, p- for light-front variables) assuming the potential and t-matrix are independent of that component. This yields three-dimensional equations of the Blankenbecler-Sugar type (instant-form) and Weinberg type (light-front). The same procedure is then applied to the Bethe-Salpeter-Faddeev equation for three particles with separable two-particle potentials, producing several three-dimensional three-particle scattering equations. The equivalence between instant-form and light-front equations is demonstrated through a transformation of variables relating the momentum fraction xp to the third component of relative momentum p3.

## Key numbers

- The two-particle instant-form equation (Eq. G) contains the propagator factor [ (ω1+ω2) / (2ω1ω2[s - (ω1+ω2)^2 + i0]) ] in the integral over d3p.
- The two-particle light-front equation (Eq. N) contains the propagator factor 1 / [s - (p⊥^2+m2^2)/(1-xp) - (p⊥^2+m1^2)/xp + i0] with integration measure dxp/(2xp(1-xp)) d2p⊥.
- For equal-mass particles, the transformation relating light-front to instant-form variables is given by p^2 = (p⊥^2 + m^2)/(4xp(1-xp)) - m^2 (Eq. T).
- The S-wave separable potential form factor used is g(p^2) = 1/(p^2 + β^2)^(1/2) (Eq. X), with β as a parameter.

## Context

This work extends the three-dimensional reduction procedure first applied to the two-particle Bethe-Salpeter equation by Blankenbecler and Sugar (1966) and by Weinberg (1966), and later extended to three particles by Aaron, Amado, and Young (1968). The present derivation provides new three-particle instant-form equations distinct from those of Aaron, Amado, and Young, and establishes an equivalence between instant-form and light-front formulations that bypasses the difficulty of angular momentum decomposition in light-front variables. The equations are designed for practical numerical calculations of relativistic three-hadron scattering, with a numerical application to the three-nucleon system presented in a companion paper.
