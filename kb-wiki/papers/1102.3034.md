# Quark-Model Baryon-Baryon Interaction Applied to the Neutron-Deuteron Scattering (III) Breakup Differential Cross Sections

> The low-energy breakup differential cross sections of the neutron-deuteron (nd) scattering are studied by employing the energy-independent version of the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2. This interaction reproduces almost all the breakup differential cross sections predicted by the meson-exchange potentials for the neutron incident energies $E_n \leq$ 65 MeV. The space star anomaly of 13 MeV nd scattering is not improved even in our model. Some overestimation of the breakup differential cross sections at $E_n$=22.7 - 65 MeV implies that systematic studies of various breakup configurations are necessary both experimentally and theoretically.

## Key claim

The quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2 reproduces almost all breakup differential cross sections predicted by meson-exchange potentials for neutron incident energies up to 65 MeV, but the space star anomaly at 13 MeV remains unresolved and some overestimation of cross sections at 22.7-65 MeV indicates that systematic studies of various breakup configurations are still needed.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for three-nucleon breakup using the energy-independent version of the quark-model baryon-baryon interaction fss2, with partial wave truncation up to two-nucleon angular momentum I_max=3 for energies up to 19 MeV and I_max=4 for higher energies. Breakup differential cross sections were calculated for multiple kinematical configurations (quasi-free scattering, final-state interaction, collinear, symmetric space star, coplanar star, and non-standard configurations) at neutron incident energies from 8 to 65 MeV, using 35 discretization points for the momentum variable q. The Coulomb force was entirely neglected in the calculations.

## Key numbers

- Neutron incident energies studied: E_n = 8, 10.3, 10.5, 13, 16, 19, 22.7, and 65 MeV
- For E_n ≤ 19 MeV, partial waves up to I_max = 3 were sufficient; for E_n = 22.7 and 65 MeV, I_max = 4 was needed
- At E_n = 13 MeV, the symmetric space star configuration (θ1=θ2=50.5°, φ12=120°) showed cross sections between the lower pd data and higher nd data, with the space star 2 case (θ1=25°, θ2=50.5°, φ12=120°) about half the experimental values and the space star 3 case (θ1=39°, θ2=50.5°, φ12=120°) almost 30% smaller than experiment
- The pd data at 13 MeV for the symmetric space star were more than 30% smaller than the nd data
- For the H(d,2p)n reaction at E_d=16 MeV (equivalent to E_n=8 MeV), the calculated cross sections were somewhat too small in COLL1, COLL2, and NS1 configurations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nd elastic scattering (Fujiwara and Fukukawa, Papers I and II) using the quark-model interaction fss2, which had shown improved agreement for the A_y puzzle at low energies compared to standard meson-exchange potentials. The results are compared extensively with predictions from meson-exchange potentials such as the Paris potential (studied by Stolk and collaborators) and with experimental data from Correll (1987), Gebhardt (1993), and others. The paper shows that despite the different off-shell properties of the quark-model interaction, the space star anomaly at 13 MeV remains unresolved, and systematic overestimation of breakup cross sections at higher energies suggests that both experimental and theoretical studies of various breakup configurations are needed.
