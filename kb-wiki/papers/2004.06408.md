# Anomalous magnetohydrodynamics with constant anisotropic electric conductivities

> We study anomalous magnetohydrodynamics in a longitudinal boost invariant Bjorken flow with constant anisotropic electric conductivities as outlined in Ref. [1]. For simplicity, we consider a neutral fluid and a force-free magnetic field in the transverse direction. We derived analytic solutions of the electromagnetic fields in the laboratory frame, the chiral density, and the energy density as functions of proper time.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic solutions for anomalous magnetohydrodynamics (MHD) with constant anisotropic electric conductivities in a longitudinally boost-invariant Bjorken flow, but only for the simplified case of a neutral fluid, a force-free magnetic field in the transverse direction, and constant values of the Hall, longitudinal, and transverse conductivities (with temperature and magnetic field dependence deferred to future work).

## Method

The authors start from the energy-momentum conservation equations, current conservation equations (including the chiral anomaly), and Maxwell's equations, coupled with a constitutive relation for the anisotropic electric conductivity tensor. They assume a Bjorken flow velocity profile, a charge-neutral fluid (so the chiral electric conductivity vanishes), and a force-free electromagnetic field configuration where the electric and magnetic fields are parallel and lie in the transverse direction. The coupled differential equations are then solved analytically using the non-conserved charges method in the small \(\mu_5/T\) limit, yielding expressions for the electric field, chiral density, and energy density as functions of proper time.

## Key numbers

* The magnetic field in the laboratory frame decays as \(\mathbf{B}^y_{\mathrm{lab}} \propto 1/\tau\), while \(\mathbf{B}^x_{\mathrm{lab}} \propto \exp(-\sigma_\parallel \tau)/\tau\), showing slower decay than in vacuum.
* The analytic solution for the electric field is given by \(E(\tau) = E_{0}(\tau_{0}/\tau)\{ e^{-\sigma_\parallel(\tau-\tau_{0})} - a_{1}e^{-\sigma_\parallel\tau}[\mathrm{E}_{1-2c_{s}^{2}}(-\sigma_\parallel \tau_{0}) - (\tau/\tau_{0})^{2c_{s}^{2}}\mathrm{E}_{1-2c_{s}^{2}}(-\sigma_\parallel \tau)] + \mathcal{O}(a_{i}^{2})\}\), where \(\mathrm{E}_n(z)\) is the exponential integral and \(a_1 = eC\chi B_0 n_{5,0}/(a T_0^2 E_0) \tau_0\).
* The chiral density evolves as \(n_5(\tau) = n_{5,0}(\tau_0/\tau)\{ 1 + a_2 e^{\sigma_\parallel \tau_0}[\mathrm{E}_1(\sigma_\parallel \tau_0) - \mathrm{E}_1(\sigma_\parallel \tau)] + \mathcal{O}(a_i^2)\}\), with \(a_2 = e^2 C \chi E_0 B_0 / n_{5,0} \tau_0\).
* The energy density is \(\varepsilon(\tau) = \epsilon_0 (\tau_0/\tau)^{1+c_s^2} \{ 1 + \sigma_\parallel (E_0^2/\varepsilon_0) e^{2\sigma_\parallel \tau_0}[\tau_0 \mathrm{E}_{1-c_s^2}(2\sigma_\parallel \tau_0) - \tau (\tau/\tau_0)^{c_s^2-1} \mathrm{E}_{1-c_s^2}(2\sigma_\parallel \tau')] + (a_3/\tau_0) e^{\sigma_\parallel \tau_0}[\tau_0 \mathrm{E}_{2-3c_s^2}(\sigma_\parallel \tau_0) - \tau (\tau_0/\tau)^{2-3c_s^2} \mathrm{E}_{2-3c_s^2}(\sigma_\parallel \tau)] + \mathcal{O}(a_i^2, a_i E_0^2/\varepsilon_0)\}\), with \(a_3 = eC\chi n_{5,0} E_0 B_0/(a \varepsilon_0 T_0^2) \tau_0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytic solutions for ideal MHD with Bjorken flow (Pu 2016, Roy 2015) and for anomalous MHD with a constant isotropic electric conductivity (Siddique 2019) to the case of constant anisotropic electric conductivities (Hall, longitudinal, and transverse). The results are derived under the same framework as those earlier works but incorporate the conductivity tensor computed by Fukushima (2017). The analytic solutions enable future studies of anomalous MHD with temperature- and magnetic-field-dependent conductivities, which the authors state will be presented elsewhere.
