# Backbending in 50Cr

> The collective yrast band and the high spin states of the nucleus 50Cr are studied using the spherical shell model and the HFB method. The two descriptions lead to nearly the same values for the relevant observables. A first backbending is predicted at I=10\hbar corresponding to a collective to non-collective transition. At I=16\hbar a second backbending occurs, associated to a configuration change that can also be interpreted as an spherical to triaxial transition.

# Backbending in 50Cr

## Key claim

The paper predicts two backbendings in the yrast band of 50Cr: a first at I=10ℏ corresponding to a collective-to-non-collective shape transition from prolate to nearly spherical (slightly oblate), and a second at I=16ℏ associated with a configuration change from (1f7/2)^10-dominated states to aligned states with fewer than ten particles in the 1f7/2 shell, which can also be interpreted as a spherical-to-triaxial transition. The spherical shell model and HFB method give nearly identical results for the relevant observables, though the authors note that the HFB results are shifted downward in energy, indicating a larger static moment of inertia than in the shell model or experiment, which they attribute to deficient treatment of pairing correlations in the weak pairing regime.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale spherical shell model calculations in the full pf shell (approximately 10^7 configurations) using the KB3 effective interaction, with single-particle energies from the 41Ca experimental spectrum and effective charges qπ=1.5, qν=0.5. They also carried out self-consistent cranking Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the density-dependent Gogny force (DS1 parameter set) expanded in a triaxial harmonic oscillator basis with ten oscillator shells. The shell model secular problem was solved using the Lanczos method code ANTOINE, and both approaches were used to compute gamma-ray energies, quadrupole moments, B(E2) transition probabilities, and gyromagnetic factors as functions of angular momentum.

## Key numbers

- First backbending predicted at I=10ℏ, corresponding to a shape transition from prolate (β~0.23, γ~0) to nearly spherical oblate (β=0.08, γ=-67 degrees)
- Second backbending predicted at I=16ℏ, associated with a transition to a triaxial regime with increased β
- Preliminary experimental results from the Chalk-River McMaster collaboration show yrast 14+ and 16+ states at excitation energies 9.9 MeV and 13.9 MeV, with corresponding Eγ values of 2.3 MeV and 4.0 MeV
- Maximum angular momentum attainable in the (1f7/2)^10 configuration is 14ℏ; the band terminates at J=22ℏ as the maximum angular momentum with four protons and six neutrons in the fp shell is 23ℏ
- For the KB' interaction variant: g(2+)=0.53, g(4+)=0.60, g(6+)=0.62, g(8+)=0.66; B(E2,4+→2+)=415 e^2fm^4 compared to experimental value 159(21) e^2fm^4 and KB3 value 264 e^2fm^4

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of 48Cr (Caurier et al., 1995) where large-scale shell model calculations with KB3 predicted the same intrinsic state as mean-field calculations with the Gogny force for the ground state rotational band. The present paper applies the same complementary approach to 50Cr, where two additional neutrons contribute to new structures at higher angular momentum. The results are compared with truncated shell model calculations by Zamick and coworkers (1996), who interpreted positive quadrupole moments as belonging to a "high K prolate" band, while the authors rely on HFB results to conclude these states are oblate and non-collective. The paper enables future experimental tests of the predicted decay patterns, particularly the transition from collective E2 decays at low spin to M1 transitions at the first backbending and back to stretched E2 transitions at the second backbending, with experimental results expected from the McMaster-Chalk River collaboration and from Legnaro (Lenzi, private communication).
