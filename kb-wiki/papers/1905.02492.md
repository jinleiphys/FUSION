# Scattering phase shifts and mixing angles for an arbitrary number of coupled channels on the lattice

> We present a lattice method for determining scattering phase shifts and mixing angles for the case of an arbitrary number of coupled channels. Previous nuclear lattice effective field theory simulations were restricted to mixing of up to two partial waves for scattering of two spin-$1/2$ particles, which is insufficient for analyzing nucleon-nucleus or nucleus-nucleus scattering processes. In the proposed method, the phase shifts and mixing angles are extracted from the radial wave functions obtained by projecting the three-dimensional lattice Hamiltonian onto the partial wave basis. We use a spherical wall potential as a boundary condition along with a channel-mixing auxiliary potential to construct the full-rank $S$ matrix. Our method can be applied to particles with any spin, but we focus here on scattering of two spin-$1$ bosons involving up to four coupled channels. For a considered test potential, the phase shifts and mixing angles extracted on the lattice are shown to agree with the ones calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation in the continuum.

## Key claim

The paper presents a lattice method for extracting scattering phase shifts and mixing angles for an arbitrary number of coupled channels, generalizing previous work that was restricted to at most two coupled channels. The method is validated for a test potential using two spin-1 bosons with up to four coupled channels, showing agreement with continuum calculations for center-of-mass momenta up to 120 MeV.

## Method

The three-dimensional lattice Hamiltonian is projected onto partial wave basis states, and radial wave functions are obtained from the eigenvectors of the projected Hamiltonian. A spherical wall potential provides boundary conditions, while a channel-mixing auxiliary potential is added to break time-reversal symmetry and generate enough linearly independent solutions to construct the full-rank S matrix. The S matrix is then decomposed using the Blatt-Biedenharn parametrization to extract phase shifts and mixing angles.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = (100 MeV)^{-1} = 1.9733 fm
- Test potential parameters: C = -2 MeV, r0 = 0.02 MeV^{-1}
- Particle masses: m1 = m2 = 2 x 938.92 MeV (deuteron-like mass)
- Binding energies of bound states: -0.258 MeV (³SD₁), -0.204 MeV (¹S₀/⁵D₀), -0.198 MeV (⁵PF₂), -0.583 MeV (¹D₂/⁵SDG₂)
- Lattice cutoff momentum: Λ_latt ~ π/a ≈ 314 MeV
- Lattice and continuum results agree for center-of-mass momenta up to p = 120 MeV
- Wall potential strength: Λ = 10⁶ (dimensionless lattice units)
- Lattice lengths used: L = 35a, 41a, 47a (depending on number of channels)

## Context

This work extends the spherical wall method of Borasoy (2007) and Lu (2015), which was limited to uncoupled partial waves and at most two coupled channels for spin-1/2 particles. The new method enables lattice studies of nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering processes that require handling multiple coupled channels. It can be combined with the adiabatic projection method of Rokash (2015) and Elhatisari (2016) to access nuclear reactions via lattice simulations, building on the first ab initio study of alpha-alpha scattering by Elhatisari (2015).
