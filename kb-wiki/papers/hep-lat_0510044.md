# Fluctuations, strangeness and quasi-quarks in heavy-ion collisions from lattice QCD

> We report measurements of diagonal susceptibilities for the baryon number, chi_B, electrical charge, chi_Q, third component of isospin, chi_I, strangeness, chi_S, and hypercharge, chi_Y, as well as the off-diagonal chi_BQ, chi_BY, chi_BS, etc. We show that the ratios of susceptibilities in the high temperature phase are robust variables, independent of lattice spacing, and therefore give predictions for experiments. We also investigate strangeness production and flavour symmetry breaking matrix elements at finite temperature. Finally, we present evidence that in the high temperature phase of QCD the different flavour quantum numbers are excited in linkages which are exactly the same as one expects from quarks. We present some investigations of these quark-like quasi particles.

## Key claim

The paper claims that ratios of quark number susceptibilities (QNS) in the high-temperature phase of QCD are "robust variables" with little lattice-spacing dependence, and that these ratios can be directly compared to experimentally measurable event-by-event fluctuations of conserved quantum numbers in heavy-ion collisions. The hedge is that this robustness holds specifically in the high-temperature phase, not necessarily near or below the crossover temperature.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavours of dynamical sea quarks of bare mass m=0.1Tc on 4x16^3 lattices, covering a temperature range up to about 2Tc. They computed diagonal susceptibilities for baryon number (chi_B), electrical charge (chi_Q), third component of isospin (chi_I), strangeness (chi_S), and hypercharge (chi_Y), as well as off-diagonal susceptibilities such as chi_BQ, chi_BY, chi_BS, and chi_QY. The strange quark was treated as quenched with bare mass in the range ms/Tc=0.75-1, and partial quenching was used to explore valence quark mass dependence.

## Key numbers

- The ratio C_{s/u} (related to the Wroblewski parameter) at Tc is approximately 0.4, in agreement with experimental extraction when freeze-out temperature is close to Tc.
- The light quark masses used give m_rho/Tc=5.4 and m_pi/m_rho=0.3, described as the smallest quark mass used in a systematic study of fluctuations.
- The strange quark bare mass range used is ms/Tc=0.75-1, giving the correct physical value of m_K/m_rho.
- The robust variable C_BS equals unity immediately above Tc, indicating that unit strangeness is carried by objects with baryon number -1/3.
- The robust variable C_QS also reaches close to unity above Tc, indicating that unit strangeness is carried by objects with charge +1/3.
- The off-diagonal QNS chi_ud/T^2 is compared to weak coupling prediction chi_ud/T^2 = -(10/27 pi^3) alpha_s^3 log(c/alpha_s), with Tc/Lambda_MSbar = 0.49 +/- 0.05.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice computations of quark number susceptibilities by the same group (Gavai, Gupta, and collaborators, 2003-2005) and by the MILC collaboration, moving from quenched QCD to dynamical QCD with two light quark flavours. The paper introduces the concept of "robust observables" that are insensitive to lattice spacing, building on the observation from quenched studies that QNS depend quadratically on lattice spacing. The results enable direct comparison with experimental fluctuation measurements at RHIC and LHC, and provide the first lattice evidence that the high-temperature phase of QCD consists of "quasi-quarks" carrying the same quantum number linkages as elementary quarks, contradicting bound-state QGP models (Koch, 2005) that predicted different values for correlation measures like C_BS.
