# Jet suppression from small to intermediate to large radius

> We present predictions for jet suppression from small to intermediate to very large radius, for low and very high energy jets created in heavy ion collisions at the LHC. We use the hybrid strong/weak coupling model for jet quenching that combines perturbative shower evolution with an effective strongly coupled description of the energy and momentum transfer from the jet into the hydrodynamic quark-gluon plasma. Because of momentum conservation, the wake created by the jet enhances or depletes the amount of particles generated at the freeze-out hypersurface depending on their orientation with respect to the jet. Within such framework we find that jet suppression is surprisingly independent of the anti-$k_T$ radius $R$, first slightly increasing as one increases $R$, then at larger values of $R$ very slowly decreasing. This nearly independence of jet suppression with increasing values of $R$ arises from two competing effects, namely the larger energy loss of the hard jet components, which tends to increase suppression, versus the partial recovery of the lost energy due to medium response, reducing suppression. We also find that the boosted medium from the recoiling jet reduces the amount of plasma in the direction opposite to it in the transverse plane, increasing the amount of jet suppression due to an over-subtraction effect. We show that this characteristic signature of the hydrodynamization of part of the jet energy can be quantified by selecting samples of dijet configurations with different relative pseudorapidities between the leading and the subleading jet.

## Key claim

Jet suppression in heavy ion collisions is surprisingly independent of the anti-\(k_T\) radius \(R\) across a vast range in jet \(p_T\), first slightly increasing as one increases \(R\), then at larger values of \(R\) very slowly decreasing; this near-independence arises from two competing effects: larger energy loss of hard jet components (increasing suppression) versus partial recovery of lost energy due to medium response (reducing suppression).

## Method

The hybrid strong/weak coupling model was used, combining perturbative shower evolution via PYTHIA (with LO nuclear PDF modifications from Eskola (2009)) with a strongly coupled energy loss rate derived holographically for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM, where each parton transfers energy and momentum to the hydrodynamic quark-gluon plasma according to \(dE/dx = -(4/\pi) E_{\rm in} (x^2/x_{\rm therm}^2)(1/\sqrt{x_{\rm therm}^2-x^2})\). The wake created by the jet was computed using the Cooper-Frye prescription to translate perturbations in the energy-momentum tensor into modifications of the final hadron distribution at freeze-out, with local QGP properties read from event-averaged hydrodynamic profiles from Shen (2014). Jet suppression was quantified via \(R_{\rm AA}(p_T) \equiv (dN^{\rm AA}/dp_T)/(dN^{\rm pp}/dp_T)/\langle N_{\rm coll} \rangle\) for PbPb collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) ATeV, 0-10% centrality, using the anti-\(k_T\) algorithm in FastJet.

## Key numbers

* Jet suppression \(R_{\rm AA}\) for PbPb at \(\sqrt{s}=5.02\) ATeV, 0-10% centrality: for \(R=0.2\) and \(R=0.4\) jets, suppression is equally strong despite larger energy loss for \(R=0.4\); from \(R=0.4\) onwards, suppression decreases slowly, requiring \(R\sim 2\) to recover most lost energy (i.e., \(R_{\rm AA} \lesssim 1\)).
* For very large cones (\(R=2\)), \(R_{\rm AA}\) visibly decreases at high momentum starting from \(p_T \gtrsim 300\) GeV, largely due to nuclear PDF modifications (shown by dashed black line for \(R=2\) jets without quenching).
* A sizeable \(R\) dependence is predicted only for \(R\gtrsim 1\) and \(p_T\lesssim 200\) GeV.
* Leading jet suppression \(R_{\rm AA}^{\rm lead}\) for leading jets with \(p_T^L>250\) GeV and subleading jets with \(p_T^S>80\) GeV, \(\Delta\phi_D > 2\pi/3\): the effect of the QGP trough from the recoiling jet is gradually reduced as \(|\Delta\eta_D|\) increases, becoming almost irrelevant beyond \(|\Delta\eta_D| \gtrsim R + \sigma(\Delta\eta)\) where \(\sigma(\Delta\eta)\simeq 1\); a knee in the rate of increase of \(R_{\rm AA}^{\rm lead}\) occurs around \(|\Delta\eta_D|\sim R\).

## Context

This work extends the hybrid strong/weak coupling model (Casalderrey-Solana 2014, 2015, 2016) to study the angular dependence of jet suppression, showing that the near-independence of \(R_{\rm AA}\) on jet radius arises from competing effects of energy loss and medium response. The results contradict expectations that \(R_{\rm AA}\) should approach 1 for very large radius, and demonstrate that the QGP trough (depletion of fluid opposite the jet direction) causes an over-subtraction effect that increases suppression. The predictions for dijet configurations with varying pseudorapidity separation provide a testable signature of the hydrodynamization of jet energy, enabling experimental discrimination between perturbative and strongly coupled descriptions of the QGP.
