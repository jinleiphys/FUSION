# Compression modulus and symmetry energy of nuclear matter with KIDS density functional

> Equation of state of dense nuclear matter is explored in the KIDS density functional theory. Parameters of the equation of state which are coefficients of the energy density expanded in powers of $(ρ- ρ_0)/3ρ_0$ where $ρ$ is the nuclear matter density and $ρ_0$ is its density at saturation are constrained by using both nuclear data and the mass-radius relation of the neutron star determined from the modern astronomy. We find that the combination of both data can reduce the uncertainty in the equation of state parameters significantly. We confirm that the newly constrained parameters reproduce the basic properties of spherical magic nuclei with high accuracy. Neutron drip lines, on the other hand, show non-negligible dependence on the uncertainty of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

By combining nuclear data (binding energies and charge radii of spherical magic nuclei) with the mass-radius relation of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star, the uncertainty in the equation of state parameters K0, J, and L can be reduced significantly compared to experimental constraints alone, but the neutron drip line remains sensitive to the remaining uncertainty in the symmetry energy parameter Ktau.

## Method

The authors used the KIDS (Korea:IBS-Daegu-Sungkyunkwan) density functional, which expands the energy per nucleon in powers of the Fermi momentum, to describe infinite nuclear matter with seven density-dependent terms. They fixed the saturation density rho0 to 0.16 fm^-3, the binding energy EB to 16.0 MeV, and Qsym to 650 MeV, then varied K0 (220-260 MeV), J (30-34 MeV), L (40-70 MeV), and Ktau (-360, -420, -480 MeV) across 2250 combinations. For each combination, they fit two additional parameters (zeta and W0) to the energy per nucleon and charge radius of 40Ca, 48Ca, and 208Pb, computed chi-squared values, then selected the two best-fitting sets per K0 value. They solved the TOV equations for neutron star mass and radius and compared the results to observational data from LMXB, NICER, and gravitational wave events GW170817 and GW190425.

## Key numbers

*   Saturation density rho0 = 0.16 fm^-3 and binding energy EB = 16.0 MeV were fixed as model-independent values.
*   The compression modulus K0 was constrained to 230-250 MeV from nuclear data alone, narrower than the experimental range of 200-260 MeV.
*   From combined nuclear and neutron star constraints, the final parameter ranges were K0 = 230-250 MeV, J = 31-33 MeV, and L = 55-65 MeV.
*   The radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star was constrained to 11.8-12.5 km from the overlapping region of all observational data.
*   The two smallest chi-squared values for nuclear data were 7.5 x 10^-6 and 3.2 x 10^-4 across all 2250 parameter combinations.
*   Binding energies of 16O, 90Zr, and 132Sn were predicted with differences from experiment of 0.5% or less for all four selected EoS parameter sets.
*   Neutron drip lines for Ca isotopes differed significantly: N=54 for Ktau = -360 MeV and N=62 for Ktau = -420 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the KIDS density functional approach (previously developed by the same group in Kidsnm, Kidsnuclei1, Kidsnuclei2) by simultaneously constraining the equation of state with both nuclear data and modern neutron star observations. It reduces the uncertainty in K0, J, and L compared to the experimental ranges compiled by Dutra (2012) from 240 Skyrme force models. The results are consistent with a recent work (Arxiv2020) that fixed K0 to 240 MeV and used 13 nuclear data points, finding nearly identical L and Ktau ranges. The study enables more precise predictions for neutron-rich nuclei near the drip line and for neutron star properties, though it identifies that further reduction of L uncertainty (below +/- 5 MeV) is needed to better constrain Ktau and the neutron drip line.
