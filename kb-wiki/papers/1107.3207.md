# Light anti-nuclei production in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=7 and 14 TeV

> A dynamically constrained coalescence model based on the phase space quantization and classical limit method was proposed to investigate the production of light nuclei (anti-nuclei) in non-single diffractive (NSD) pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=7 and 14 TeV. This calculation was based on the final hadronic state in the PYTHIA and PACIAE model simulations, the event sample consisted of 1.2$\times 10^8$ events in both simulations. The PACIAE model calculated $\bar D$ yield of 6.247$\times 10^{-5}$ in NSD pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=7 TeV is well comparing with the ALICE rough datum of 5.456$\times 10^{-5}$. It indicated the reliability of proposed method in some extent. The yield, transverse momentum distribution, and rapidity distribution of the $\bar D$, $^3{\bar{He}}$, and $_{\barΛ} ^3{\bar H}$ in NSD pp collisions at $\sqrt{s} $=7 and 14 TeV were predicted by PACIAE and PYTHIA model simulations. The yield resulted from PACIAE model simulations is larger than the one from PYTHIA model. This might reflect the role played by the parton and hadron rescatterings.

## Key claim

The authors propose a dynamically constrained coalescence model based on phase space quantization to predict light anti-nuclei yields in pp collisions, and find that the PACIAE model calculated antideuteron yield of 6.247x10^-5 at 7 TeV is "well comparing" with the ALICE rough datum of 5.456x10^-5, but note the PACIAE value is larger, possibly due to the full rapidity phase space used versus the ALICE acceptance of |eta|<0.9.

## Method

The authors used the PYTHIA and PACIAE (parton and hadron cascade) models to simulate the final hadronic state in non-single diffractive (NSD) pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 and 14 TeV, generating 1.2x10^8 events for each model. They then applied a dynamically constrained coalescence model based on phase space quantization and classical limit method, where a configuration of antiparticles (e.g., antiproton, antineutron, antilambda) contributes to an anti-nucleus yield if the invariant mass falls within a mass window around the rest mass and the distances from the center-of-mass are within the radius of the nucleus. The model parameters were fitted to STAR data for kaons, lambda, and antilambda yields in pp collisions at sqrt(s)=0.2 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Antideuteron yield in NSD pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV: PACIAE model gives 6.247x10^-5, compared to ALICE rough datum of 5.456x10^-5 (estimated from Fig. 4 of the ALICE paper)
- Antideuteron yield in NSD pp collisions at sqrt(s)=14 TeV: PACIAE model gives 8.553x10^-5, PYTHIA model gives 6.048x10^-5
- Antihelium-3 yield in NSD pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV: PACIAE model gives 2.642x10^-7, PYTHIA model gives 1.417x10^-7
- Antihelium-3 yield in NSD pp collisions at sqrt(s)=14 TeV: PACIAE model gives 4.407x10^-7, PYTHIA model gives 2.419x10^-7
- Antihypertrinucleon (anti-lambda hypertriton) yield in NSD pp collisions at sqrt(s)=7 TeV: PACIAE model gives 2.453x10^-7, PYTHIA model gives 1.000x10^-7
- Antihypertrinucleon yield in NSD pp collisions at sqrt(s)=14 TeV: PACIAE model gives 3.305x10^-7, PYTHIA model gives 1.048x10^-7
- Average transverse momentum of antideuteron at 7 TeV: PACIAE model gives 0.999 GeV/c, PYTHIA model gives 0.955 GeV/c
- Average transverse momentum of antihelium-3 at 7 TeV: PACIAE model gives 0.939 GeV/c, PYTHIA model gives 1.05 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the study of light anti-nuclei production from heavy-ion collisions (where STAR observed antihypertrinucleon and antihelium-3 in Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV) to pp collisions at LHC energies. The dynamically constrained coalescence model based on phase space quantization provides an alternative to the analytical coalescence model using Wigner functions (as used by Greiner, Chen, Ma) and the statistical model (as used by Pop). The comparison between PACIAE and PYTHIA results enables assessment of the role of parton and hadron rescatterings, which the authors find to be non-negligible. The predictions for transverse momentum and rapidity distributions of antideuteron, antihelium-3, and antihypertrinucleon at 7 and 14 TeV can be tested against future LHC data.
