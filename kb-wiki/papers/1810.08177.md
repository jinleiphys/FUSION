# A modified-Boltzmann approach for modeling the hot QCD medium-induce splitting vertices in the deep LPM region

> Hard probes produced in perturbative processes are excellent probes for the study of the hot and dense QCD matter created in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Transport theory, allowing for coupling to an evolving medium with fluctuating initial conditions, has become a powerful tool in this endeavor. However, the implementation of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect for medium-induced parton bremsstrahlung and pair production, poses a challenge to semi-classical transport models based on Boltzmann-type transport equations. In this work, we investigate a possible solution to approximate the LPM effect in a "modified Boltzmann transport" approach, including a prescription for the running coupling constant. By fixing a numerical parameter, this approach quantitatively reproduces the rates of medium-induced parton splitting predicted by the next-to-lead-log solution of the AMY equation which is valid in the deep-LPM regime of an infinite medium. We also find qualitative agreement of our implementation with calculations in a finite and expanding medium, but future improvements are needed for added precision at small path length. This work benefits transport model-based studies and the usage of these models in the phenomenological extraction of the jet transport coefficient.

## Key claim

The authors propose a "modified Boltzmann transport" approach that approximates the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect in semi-classical transport models by using a suppression probability and a formation-time-based rejection scheme; this approach quantitatively reproduces next-to-leading-log (NLL) solutions of the AMY equation in the deep-LPM regime of an infinite medium, but only achieves qualitative agreement for finite and expanding media, with future improvements needed for small path lengths.

## Method

The authors start from a semi-classical Boltzmann transport model with independent (incoherent) elastic and inelastic collision rates, separating soft (diffusive) and hard (large momentum transfer) interactions using a switching scale Q_cut. They then modify the inelastic splitting rates by introducing an ansatz where the temporal two-point function F(t',t) is replaced by an ensemble of independent copies: each copy evolves with elastic broadening, the formation time tau_f is recalculated at each time step, and the splitting is accepted with probability p = min(1, a b lambda_tilde / tau_f) only when t - t' > a tau_f. The parameters a and b are tuned to match the NLL solution of the AMY equation, with a set to a process- and x-dependent color factor and b given by a logarithmic correction involving the ratio of the NLL-improved scale to the native transport scale.

## Key numbers

* The average inverse formation time in the deep-LPM regime scales as <tau_f^{-1}> ~ sqrt(hat_q / (2 x (1-x) E)), where hat_q is the transport coefficient.
* The leading-log (LL) splitting rate is dR_{bc}^{a,LL}/domega = (alpha_s P_{bc}^{a(0)})/(pi E sqrt(2)) * sqrt(hat_q_3(x, Q_0^2) / (2 x (1-x) E)).
* The NLL-improved scale Q_1^2 is determined self-consistently by Q_1^2 = sqrt(2 x (1-x) E alpha_s T m_D^2) times a logarithmic factor involving ln(2 xi Q_1^2 / m_D^2) and similar terms for x^2 and (1-x)^2.
* The tuned prefactor in the b parameter is 0.75, which is the only numerical constant tuned in the comparison to NLL solutions.
* The running coupling constant uses n_f = 3 and Lambda = 0.2 GeV, with a cutoff at Q_med = pi T to avoid the non-perturbative pole.

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical transport approach of Ghiglieri et al. (2015) by adding an approximate treatment of the LPM effect, which is a known challenge for Boltzmann-type transport models. The method is compared to the AMY formalism of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2002, 2003) and the NLL solutions of Arnold and Dogan (2008). The approach enables transport model-based studies to incorporate medium-induced parton splitting with quantitative accuracy in the deep-LPM regime, facilitating phenomenological extraction of the jet transport coefficient hat_q from heavy-ion collision data.
