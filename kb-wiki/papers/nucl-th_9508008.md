# The hole spectral function and the relationship between overlap functions, natural orbitals and the one-body density matrix in nuclei

> A method to calculate the hole spectral function in the discrete part of the spectrum is suggested within the natural orbital representation of the one-body density matrix of $A$-nucleon system using its relationship with the overlap functions of the eigenstates in the $(A-1)$-nucleon system.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to calculate the hole spectral function in the discrete part of the spectrum by using the natural orbital representation of the one-body density matrix (OBDM) of the A-nucleon system, exploiting the relationship between the OBDM and the overlap functions of the bound eigenstates of the (A-1)-nucleon system. The method is conditional on having a realistic OBDM from a given correlation method, and it applies only to the discrete (bound-state) part of the hole spectrum, not the continuum.

## Method

The authors diagonalize the one-body density matrix of the A-nucleon ground state to obtain natural orbitals and occupation numbers. They then extract the bound-state overlap functions and separation energies of the (A-1)-nucleon system from the asymptotic behavior of the radial OBDM, using an iterative procedure (Eqs. 19-23). Finally, they expand these overlap functions in the basis of natural orbitals to compute the amplitudes needed for the hole spectral function in the discrete part of the spectrum via Eq. (24).

## Key numbers

- The natural occupation numbers \(N_a\) satisfy \(0 \leq N_a \leq 1\) and \(\sum_a N_a = A\) (the total nucleon number).
- The separation energy for the lowest bound state is given by \(\varepsilon_0^{(qlj)} = \hbar^2 k_0^{(qlj)2} / 2m_q\), where \(k_0^{(qlj)}\) is obtained from the asymptotic decay constant of the OBDM.
- The asymptotic form of the radial neutron overlap function for bound states is \(\Phi_{\nu}^{(qlj)}(r) \rightarrow C_{\nu}^{(qlj)} \exp(-k_{\nu}^{(qlj)} r) / r\), with \(k_{\nu}^{(qlj)} = (1/\hbar) [2m_q (E_{\nu}^{(qlj)} - E_A^0)]^{1/2}\).
- The energy integral of the hole spectral function over the discrete part defines the one-body density matrix in momentum representation: \(\int_{-\infty}^{E_F^-} dE \, S(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}', E) = \rho(\mathbf{k}, \mathbf{k}')\), where \(E_F^-\) is a negative quantity whose absolute value equals the separation energy of the A-nucleon system.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework connecting the one-body density matrix to overlap functions, as previously developed by the authors and others (e.g., Antonov et al. 1993, 1994) using the Jastrow method, generator coordinate method, and coherent density fluctuation model. It provides a systematic procedure to compute the hole spectral function from correlated ground-state OBDMs, enabling tests of correlation methods against experimental nucleon removal cross-sections. The method is limited to the discrete part of the spectrum; the continuum part remains to be addressed, and the authors state that applications and comparisons with experimental data will be given in a future publication.
