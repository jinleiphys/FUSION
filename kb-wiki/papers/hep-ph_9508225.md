# Model gluon propagator and pion and rho-meson observables

> A one parameter, model confined-gluon propagator is employed in a phenomenological application of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations to the calculation of a range of $π$- and $ρ$-meson observables. Good agreement is obtained with the data. The calculated quark propagator does not have a singularity on the real-$p^2$ axis. A mass formula for the pion, involving only the vacuum, dressed quark propagator, is presented and shown to provide an accurate estimate of the mass obtained via a direct solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

# Model gluon propagator and pion and rho-meson observables

## Key claim
A one-parameter model confined-gluon propagator, when used as the kernel for the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and meson Bethe-Salpeter equations in rainbow/ladder approximation, can simultaneously describe a range of pion and rho-meson observables, but only if the mass scale parameter $m_t$ is approximately 700 MeV; the model explicitly excludes logarithmic corrections from QCD anomalous dimensions, so direct comparison of the renormalised current quark mass with standard QCD values is inappropriate.

## Method
The authors employed a one-parameter model gluon propagator in Landau gauge, consisting of an integrable infrared singularity plus a term ensuring correct large-momentum behaviour, as the kernel for the renormalised quark Dyson-Schwinger equation solved in rainbow approximation. The resulting dressed quark propagator was then used as input to the generalised ladder approximation Bethe-Salpeter equations for the pion and rho-meson, with the single gluon mass parameter $m_t$ and the renormalised current quark mass $m_R$ varied to fit pion observables. For the rho-meson, the Bethe-Salpeter equation was solved at spacelike total momenta and extrapolated to the timelike mass-shell point.

## Key numbers
- $m_t = 0.69$ GeV (the gluon propagator mass scale marking the perturbative/nonperturbative transition)
- $m_R = 1.1$ MeV (renormalised current quark mass at renormalisation point $\mu = 9.47$ GeV)
- The pion mass formula yields $m_\pi^2 N_\pi^2 = 2\,(0.45)^3\,m_R + (2.6)^2\,m_R^2 + 150\, m_R^3$ on the range $m_R \in [0, 0.02]$ GeV, with the linear term contributing almost 96% of the total at the fitted $m_R$
- The vacuum quark condensate inferred from the mass formula is $\langle \bar q q\rangle_{\mu} = -(0.45\ {\rm GeV})^3$
- The quark propagator shows no singularity on the real-$p^2$ axis, with a broad resonance-like peak in $1/[p^2 + M(p^2)^2]$ centred at $p^2 \approx -(0.55)$ GeV$^2$

## Context
This work extends earlier Dyson-Schwinger studies that employed parametrised quark propagators (Roberts et al., 1994; Roberts, 1994) by instead using a model gluon propagator as the fundamental interaction kernel, thereby correlating propagators for different quark flavours without introducing additional parameters. The approach builds on gluon Dyson-Schwinger studies (Baker, Ball and Zachariasen, 1983; Brown and Pennington, 1989) that found an enhanced gluon propagator at small momenta. The pion mass formula derived here provides an alternative to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation that avoids the approximations required to recover the standard form. The results enable future calculations of hadronic form factors and extension to systems involving strange quarks without new parameters.
