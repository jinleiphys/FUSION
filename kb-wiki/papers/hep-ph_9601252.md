# The rare decay $η\toππγγ$ in chiral perturbation theory

> We investigate the rare radiative eta decay modes $η\toπ^+π^-γγ$ and $η\toπ^0π^0γγ$ within the framework of chiral perturbation theory at $O(p^4)$. We present photon spectra and partial decay rates for both processes as well as a Dalitz contour plot for the charged decay.

# The rare decay $\eta\to\pi\pi\gamma\gamma$ in chiral perturbation theory

## Key claim

The leading-order chiral perturbation theory calculation at $O(p^4)$ predicts that the charged decay mode $\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\gamma$ is dominated by Wess-Zumino-Witten contact terms and internal bremsstrahlung, with the $\pi^0$-pole contribution confined to a very small region of phase space; the resulting branching ratio is well within the reach of upcoming experimental facilities, but the neutral mode $\eta\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\gamma$ will be much harder to detect.

## Method

The authors performed a tree-level calculation in chiral perturbation theory using the $O(p^2)$ Lagrangian and the anomalous Wess-Zumino-Witten Lagrangian at $O(p^4)$, incorporating $\eta$-$\eta'$ mixing via a phenomenological mixing angle $\theta = -20^\circ$ and using decay constants $F_\pi = 93$ MeV, $F_8 = 1.25 F_\pi$, and $\overline{F}_0 = 1.06 F_\pi$. For the charged mode, three classes of Feynman diagrams were evaluated: diagrams with a four-meson vertex and a neutral-meson propagator decaying to two photons (class 1), WZW contact terms (class 2), and internal bremsstrahlung from charged pions (class 3); for the neutral mode, only class 1 diagrams contribute at tree level. The differential decay rate was integrated numerically over five kinematic variables to obtain photon spectra and partial decay rates.

## Key numbers

- Upper limits from prior experiments: $\Gamma(\eta\to\pi^+\pi^-\gamma\gamma)/\Gamma_{\text{tot}} < 2.1\times10^{-3}$ (Particle Data Group, 1994) and $< 3.7\times10^{-3}$ (Baldini et al., 1960s), both for missing mass of neutral particles $> 195$ MeV.
- The $\pi^0$ pole appears at $s_\gamma = m_{\pi^0}^2 = 18219$ MeV$^2$.
- Total eta decay width used: $\Gamma_{\text{tot}} = 1.2\times10^{-3}$ MeV.
- For the neutral mode, a calculation based on old experimental data yields $\Gamma(\eta\to\pi^0\pi^0\gamma\gamma) = 1.38\times10^{-3}$ eV.
- Using an energy cut at $\delta m_{\text{brems}} = 195$ MeV, the predicted partial decay rate for the charged mode is smaller than the experimental upper limits by about three orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends chiral perturbation theory calculations of rare eta decays to the radiative two-photon channels, complementing earlier studies of anomalous processes such as $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-\pi^0$ (Bijnens, 1990s) and the Wess-Zumino-Witten contact term (Wess and Zumino, 1971; Witten, 1983). The charged mode provides a clean test of the three-meson-two-photon anomalous vertex, while the neutral mode is dominated by isospin-breaking effects through the $\pi^0$ pole, making extraction of the $G$-parity-conserving $\eta\eta\pi\pi$ coupling difficult. The predicted rates are accessible at facilities such as CELSIUS ($\sim 2.2\times10^9$ etas/year), ITEP ($\sim (0.27-2.7)\times10^9$), and DA$\Phi$NE ($\sim 3.2\times10^8$), enabling future experimental tests of the anomalous Lagrangian.
