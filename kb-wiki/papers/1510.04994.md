# Theory Considerations For Nucleosynthesis Beyond Fe With Special Emphasis On p-Nuclei In Massive Stars

> Nucleosynthesis of heavy elements requires the use of different experimental and theoretical methods to determine astrophysical reaction rates than light element nucleosynthesis. Additionally, there are also larger uncertainties involved in the astrophysical models, both because the sites are not well known and because of differing numerical treatments in different models. As an example for the latter, the production of p-nuclei is compared in two different stellar models, demonstrating that a model widely used for postproduction calculations may have a zone grid too coarse to follow the synthesis of p-nuclei in detail.

# Theory Considerations For Nucleosynthesis Beyond Fe With Special Emphasis On p-Nuclei In Massive Stars

## Key claim
The paper argues that a widely used set of stellar trajectories for modeling the gamma-process in massive stars may have a zone grid too coarse to follow the synthesis of p-nuclei in detail, and that this numerical inadequacy can distort both the final abundance predictions and their associated uncertainties. This claim is conditional on the specific comparison between the model of Hashimoto (1995) and the KEPLER model of Rauscher (2002).

## Method
The authors performed a full-scale Monte Carlo variation of reaction rates above the iron group, including beta decays, using the PizBuin framework. Temperature-dependent variation limits were assigned to different reaction types (neutron-induced, proton-induced, alpha-induced) based on experimental ground-state contributions and theoretical excited-state contributions. The method was applied to two different sets of stellar trajectories for the explosion of a 25 solar mass star of solar metallicity: one from Hashimoto (1995) and one from the KEPLER code by Rauscher (2002), and the resulting probability density distributions of final p-nucleus abundances were compared.

## Key numbers
- Uncertainty limits for predicted neutron-induced reaction rates: a factor of 2.0 (upper) and 0.5 (lower).
- Uncertainty limits for predicted proton-induced reaction rates: a factor of 3.0 (upper) and 0.33 (lower).
- Uncertainty limits for predicted alpha-induced reaction rates: a factor of 2.0 (upper) and 0.1 (lower).
- The ground-state contribution X0 for astrophysically relevant photodisintegration rates is less than 5 x 10^-5.
- The effective energy range for reactions dominated by alpha widths in p-nuclei production is up to approximately 9-10 MeV.
- The stellar model used was a 25 solar mass star of solar metallicity.

## Context
This work extends sensitivity studies of p-nucleosynthesis such as those by Rapp (2006) and the earlier postprocessing analysis by Rayet (1995) based on stellar models from Hashimoto (1995). The paper identifies a numerical problem in the widely used Hashimoto (1995) trajectories, showing that their coarse zone grid may not capture all relevant reaction flows for light p-nuclei, similar to a problem recently found in canonical thermonuclear supernova models where high-resolution 2D models by Travaglio (2011) were needed to produce all p-nuclei. The results enable future investigations of uncertainties in p-nuclei yields across a range of stellar masses for use in galactic chemical evolution studies.
