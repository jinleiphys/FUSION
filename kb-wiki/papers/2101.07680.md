# Second and fourth moments of the charge density and neutron-skin thickness of atomic nuclei

> A method is presented to extract the neutron-skin thickness of atomic nuclei from the second and fourth moments of the electric charge distribution. We show that the value of the proton fourth moment must be independently known in order to estimate the neutron skin thickness experimentally. To overcome this problem, we propose the use of a strong linear correlation among the second and fourth moments of the proton distribution as calculated with several energy density functionals of common use. We take special care in estimating the errors associated with the different contributions to the neutron radius and show, for the first time, the analytic expressions for the spin-orbit contribution to the charge fourth moments of neutrons and protons. To reduce the uncertainty on the extraction of the neutron radius, two neighboring even-even isotopes are used. Nevertheless, the error on the fourth moment of the proton distribution, even if determined or assumed with large accuracy, dominates and prevents the present method from being applied for a sound determination of the neutron skin thickness.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to extract the neutron-skin thickness of atomic nuclei from the second and fourth moments of the electric charge distribution, but the authors conclude that the error on the fourth moment of the proton distribution, even if determined or assumed with large accuracy, dominates and prevents the present method from being applied for a sound determination of the neutron skin thickness.

## Method

The authors derive analytic expressions for the second and fourth moments of the charge density, including spin-orbit contributions from both protons and neutrons, using a relativistic framework with the impulse approximation. They propose using a strong linear correlation among the second and fourth moments of the proton distribution as calculated with several energy density functionals, and apply the method to neighboring even-even isotopes to reduce uncertainty on the extracted neutron radius.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Kurasawa and Suzuki (2019) and Reinhard (2021) for relating charge moments to nuclear density moments, and builds on the spin-orbit contribution to the charge second moment derived by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2012). The method provides analytic expressions for the spin-orbit contribution to the charge fourth moment for the first time, but the dominant uncertainty from the proton fourth moment prevents practical application for determining neutron-skin thickness, pointing to the need for independent experimental or theoretical constraints on the proton fourth moment.
