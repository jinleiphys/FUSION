# Finite-Size Effects on Nucleation in a First-Order Phase Transition

> We discuss finite-size effects on homogeneous nucleation in first-order phase transitions. We study their implications for cosmological phase transitions and to the hadronization of a quark-gluon plasma generated in high-energy heavy ion collisions. Very general arguments allow us to show that the finite size of the early universe has virtually no relevance in the process of nucleation and in the growth of cosmological bubbles during the primordial quark-hadron and the electroweak phase transitions. In the case of high-energy heavy ion collisions, finite-size effects play an important role in the late-stage growth of hadronic bubbles.

## Key claim

Finite-size effects on the nucleation rate in first-order phase transitions are negligible for cosmological quark-hadron and electroweak transitions because the universe is large enough to be approximated as infinite, but these effects are important for the late-stage growth of hadronic bubbles in high-energy heavy ion collisions.

## Method

The authors analytically examine finite-size effects on homogeneous nucleation by considering two mechanisms: rounding of singularities in the nucleation rate due to an intrinsic uncertainty in supercooling, and influence on domain coarsening during late-stage bubble growth. They compare relevant length scales (system size, critical bubble radius, correlation length) for cosmological phase transitions using the particle horizon in a Robertson-Walker spacetime, and for heavy ion collisions using the Bjorken picture of longitudinal expansion with a cooling law.

## Key numbers

- For cosmological phase transitions, the typical length scale of the early universe is \(L_{univ}(T) \approx \mathcal{A}/T^2\sqrt{N(T)}\) with \(\mathcal{A} = 1.45 \times 10^{18}\) (in fermi units), making finite-size effects negligible for QCD (\(T_c^{QCD} \sim \mathcal{O}(10^{-1})\) GeV) and electroweak (\(T_c^{EW} \sim \mathcal{O}(10^{2})\) GeV) transitions; one would need \(T \sim 10^8\) GeV to see appreciable effects.
- For heavy ion collisions, the minimal supercooling due to finite size is \(\Delta T_{round}/T_c \approx 10^{-3}\) using \(T_c \approx 150\) MeV, \(\ell \approx 4 \times (150\) MeV\()^4\), and \(L \approx 10\) fm.
- The ratio of bubble growth velocity to plasma expansion velocity is \(v_b/v_L \approx (20/v_z)(T_c/T - 1)\), so bubble growth exceeds expansion for supercooling \(\theta \approx v_z/20 \leq 5\%\), with critical radius larger than 1 fm at such supercooling.

## Context

This work extends the study of finite-size scaling from equilibrium critical phenomena (Goldenfeld) to nonequilibrium processes like metastable decays, addressing a gap noted by Rikvold and collaborators. The authors contrast their conclusion that cosmological finite-size effects are negligible with arguments presented in Gavai, and they build on prior nucleation studies for QGP hadronization (Scavenius 2001, Shukla 2001) and the Polyakov Loop Model (explosive hadron production). The paper motivates future finite-size real-time lattice simulations and hydrodynamic studies of the chiral transition in expanding QGP.
