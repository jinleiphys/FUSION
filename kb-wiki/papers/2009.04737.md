# Constraints on the nuclear symmetry energy from asymmetric-matter calculations with chiral NN and 3N interactions

> The nuclear symmetry energy is a key quantity in nuclear (astro)physics. It describes the isospin dependence of the nuclear equation of state (EOS), which is commonly assumed to be almost quadratic. In this work, we confront this standard quadratic expansion of the EOS with explicit asymmetric nuclear-matter calculations based on a set of commonly used Hamiltonians including two- and three-nucleon forces derived from chiral effective field theory. We study, in particular, the importance of non-quadratic contributions to the symmetry energy, including the non-analytic logarithmic term introduced by Kaiser [Phys.~Rev.~C \textbf{91}, 065201 (2015)]. Our results suggest that the quartic contribution to the symmetry energy can be robustly determined from the various Hamiltonians employed, and we obtain 1.00(8) MeV (or 0.55(8) MeV for the potential part) at saturation density, while the logarithmic contribution to the symmetry energy is relatively small and model-dependent. We finally employ the meta-model approach to study the impact of the higher-order contributions on the neutron-star crust-core transition density, and find a small 5\% correction.

## Key claim

The paper tests the common assumption that the nuclear symmetry energy is purely quadratic in isospin asymmetry, finding that quartic contributions can be robustly determined at 1.00(8) MeV at saturation density, while the logarithmic term is small and model-dependent, leading to only a 5% correction to the neutron-star crust-core transition density.

## Method

The authors performed many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) calculations up to second order for asymmetric nuclear matter at 11 isospin asymmetries, using six different Hamiltonians that combine chiral NN interactions at N3LO with N2LO 3N forces evolved via similarity renormalization group (SRG). They analyzed the isospin-asymmetry expansion of the energy per particle, including quartic and logarithmic contributions, and employed a meta-model approach to study the impact on neutron-star crust-core transition densities.

## Key numbers

- Quartic contribution to the symmetry energy at saturation density: 1.00(8) MeV total, or 0.55(8) MeV for the potential part
- Free Fermi gas quartic contribution at saturation density: approximately 0.45 MeV
- Landau mass in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation density: m*_n/m(delta=0) = 0.64(2)
- Landau mass in pure neutron matter at saturation density: m*_n/m(delta=1) = 0.88(4)
- Difference between Landau masses in PNM and SNM at saturation density: D m*_n,sat = 0.24(5)
- Ratio of PNM energy to free Fermi gas energy at the plateau: approximately 0.42(1) at Fermi momenta around 1.3(2) fm^-1
- Correction to neutron-star crust-core transition density from higher-order contributions: 5%

## Context

This work extends previous studies of non-quadratic contributions to the symmetry energy, including the analytic MBPT calculations by Kaiser (2015) who found a logarithmic term, and the analysis by Wellenhofer et al. (2016) on the convergence of the asymmetry expansion. The results contradict the much larger quartic contribution of 7.2(2.5) MeV reported by Cai and Li (2015) using empirical constraints, and instead support earlier BHF calculations by Bombaci (1991) and others that found small non-quadratic contributions. The study enables improved modeling of neutron-star properties and provides benchmarks for future order-by-order chiral EFT calculations with statistically robust uncertainty estimates.
