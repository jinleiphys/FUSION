# Transverse phase space and its multipole decomposition

> Relativistic phase space distributions are very interesting objects as they allow one to gather the information extracted from various types of experiments into a single coherent picture. Focusing on the four-dimensional transverse phase space, we identified all the possible angular correlations providing at the same time a clear physical interpretation of all the leading-twist generalized and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions. We also developed a convenient representation of this four-dimensional space.

## Key claim

The paper identifies all possible angular correlations in the four-dimensional transverse phase space of quarks inside a spin-1/2 target, providing a clear physical interpretation of all leading-twist generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (TMDs) in terms of multipole structures, but only after integrating over the parton longitudinal momentum.

## Method

The authors start from the five-dimensional light-front Wigner distribution, defined as the Fourier transform of the generalized transverse-momentum dependent (GTMD) correlator to impact-parameter space. They integrate over the parton longitudinal momentum fraction x to obtain four-dimensional transverse phase-space distributions, then perform a multipole decomposition in both transverse momentum k_T and transverse position b_T spaces, classifying each component by its multipole orders (m_k, m_b). The decomposition is constrained by parity and time-reversal symmetries, and the resulting structures are illustrated using a light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) for up quarks.

## Key numbers

* The transverse phase-space distributions are evaluated at a fixed impact-parameter radius |b_T| = 0.4 fm for the graphical representation.
* There are 16 independent polarization configurations for the quark phase-space distributions at leading twist.
* The multipole decomposition yields contributions labeled by integer pairs (m_k, m_b), with the simplest being the (0,0) multipole appearing in ρ^e_X for X = UU, LL, TT.
* The (0,1) and (1,0) multipoles appear in ρ^e_X with X = UT, TU and in ρ^o_X with X = LT, TL, and vice versa for the (1,0) case.
* The (1,1) multipoles appear in ρ^e_X with X = UL, LU and in ρ^o_X with X = UU, LL, TT, TT'.

## Context

This work extends the phase-space distribution formalism introduced by Ji (2003) and Belitsky (2003) for infinitely massive targets, and the five-dimensional light-front version by Lorce (2011) for finite target mass. It provides a systematic multipole basis that connects GTMDs (the "mother distributions" of GPDs and TMDs) to experimentally accessible quantities, showing which angular correlations survive integration to GPDs (m_b = 0) or TMDs (m_k = 0) and which are lost entirely. The approach enables future experimental access to parton orbital angular momentum through the (1,1) multipole structures, which are not accessible via GPDs or TMDs alone.
