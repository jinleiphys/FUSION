# Statistical hadronization of charm at SPS, RHIC and LHC

> We study the production of charmonia and charmed hadrons for nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies within the framework of the statistical hadronization model. Results from this model are compared to the observed centrality dependence of J/psi production at SPS energy. We further provide predictions for the centrality dependence of the production of open and hidden charm mesons at RHIC and LHC.

# Statistical hadronization of charm at SPS, RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model can describe the centrality dependence of J/ψ production at SPS energy, but only when the charm cross section is increased by a factor of 2.8 compared to next-to-leading order perturbative QCD calculations. The model predicts much larger J/ψ yields at RHIC and LHC than conventional suppression models, with the largest differences expected at collider energies.

## Method

The model assumes all charm quarks are produced in primary hard collisions and equilibrate in quark-gluon plasma, with no J/ψ preformed (complete screening). All charmed hadrons are formed at freeze-out according to statistical laws, using a constant temperature of 170 MeV and baryonic chemical potential parametrized as μ_b = 1270/(1+√s_NN/4.3). The number of directly produced c-cbar pairs is taken from NLO pQCD calculations scaled to nucleus-nucleus collisions via the nuclear overlap function, and the charm enhancement factor g_c is extracted from the relation between direct pairs and thermally produced open charm hadrons.

## Key numbers

- For central collisions at SPS (√s_NN = 17.3 GeV, N_part = 350): dN_J/ψ/dy = 2.55×10^(-4), dN_D+/dy = 0.010, dN_D-/dy = 0.016, dN_D0/dy = 0.022, dN_Λ_c/dy = 0.014
- For central collisions at RHIC (√s_NN = 200 GeV, N_part = 350): dN_J/ψ/dy = 0.011, dN_D+/dy = 0.404, dN_D-/dy = 0.420, dN_D0/dy = 0.89, dN_Λ_c/dy = 0.153
- For central collisions at LHC (√s_NN = 5500 GeV, N_part = 350): dN_J/ψ/dy = 0.226, dN_D+/dy = 3.56, dN_D-/dy = 3.53, dN_D0/dy = 7.80, dN_Λ_c/dy = 1.16
- Charm enhancement factor g_c: 1.86 at SPS, 8.33 at RHIC, 23.2 at LHC
- Direct c-cbar pairs per unit rapidity: 0.064 at SPS, 1.92 at RHIC, 16.8 at LHC
- Volume for Δy=1 at central collisions: 861 fm^3 at SPS, 1663 fm^3 at RHIC, 4564 fm^3 at LHC
- To describe SPS J/ψ data, N_c-cbar^dir must be increased by a factor of 2.8 compared to NLO pQCD calculations

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model of Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (2000, 2001) to make predictions for RHIC and LHC energies, contrasting with the Matsui-Satz approach (1986) which predicted very strong J/ψ suppression (up to a factor of 20, as estimated by Vogt in 1999) for central Au-Au collisions at RHIC. The model enables tests of whether charm quarks travel significant distances in quark-gluon plasma, which would be a clear signal for a deconfined phase if precision data match the predictions. Preliminary PHENIX results at RHIC (Nagle, 2002) agree within large error bars, but high-statistics data are needed for a stringent test.
