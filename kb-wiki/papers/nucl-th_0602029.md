# Results from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> We describe the current status of the heavy ion research program at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The new suite of experiments and the collider energies have opened up new probes of the medium created in the collisions. Our review focuses on the experimental discoveries to date at RHIC and their interpretation in the light of our present theoretical understanding of the dynamics of relativistic heavy ion collisions and of the structure of strongly interacting matter at high energy density.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the experimental discoveries at RHIC and their interpretation, arguing that the data are consistent with the creation of a strongly coupled, nearly perfect fluid of quarks and gluons (the quark-gluon plasma), rather than a weakly interacting gas, but notes that definitive proof of deconfinement and thermalization remains an active area of interpretation.

## Method

The authors synthesize results from the four RHIC experiments (BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR) covering bulk particle production, collective flow (elliptic flow, HBT interferometry), high-\(p_T\) hadron suppression (jet quenching), and heavy quarkonium production. They compare these measurements to predictions from relativistic hydrodynamics, parton energy loss models, and lattice QCD thermodynamics, and discuss the implications for the equation of state and transport properties of the created medium.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron multiplicity in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is approximately \(dN_{\rm ch}/d\eta \approx 650\) at mid-rapidity (PHOBOS, BRAHMS).
- Elliptic flow coefficient \(v_2\) for charged hadrons in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV reaches about 0.05 at low \(p_T\) and scales with the number of constituent quarks for identified hadrons (STAR, PHENIX).
- Nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for high-\(p_T\) pions in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is suppressed to about 0.2 for \(p_T > 5\) GeV/\(c\) (PHENIX).
- Direct photon yield in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV is consistent with a thermal spectrum with an inverse slope of about 220 MeV for \(p_T < 3\) GeV/\(c\) (PHENIX).
- J/\(\psi\) production in central Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV shows a suppression factor \(R_{AA} \approx 0.2\) relative to p+p scaled by the number of binary collisions (PHENIX).

## Context

This review extends the earlier work of Harris and Muller (1996) on the search for the quark-gluon plasma and the RHIC white papers (BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR, all 2005) that presented the first comprehensive experimental evidence. It enables future studies at the LHC and the RHIC beam energy scan by establishing the baseline of a strongly coupled liquid phase, and it highlights open questions about the initial state (color glass condensate), the nature of the phase transition, and the role of dissipative effects.
