# Excited nucleons with chirally improved fermions

> We study positive and negative parity nucleons on the lattice using the chirally improved lattice Dirac operator. Our analysis is based on a set of three operators chi_i with the nucleon quantum numbers but in different representations of the chiral group and with different diquark content. We use a variational method to separate ground state and excited states and determine the mixing coefficients for the optimal nucleon operators in terms of the chi_i. We clearly identify the negative parity resonances N(1535) and N(1650) and their masses agree well with experimental data. The mass of the observed excited positive parity state is too high to be interpreted as the Roper state. Our results for the mixing coefficients indicate that chiral symmetry is important for N(1535) and N(1650) states. We confront our data for the mixing coefficients with quark models and provide insights into the physics of the nucleon system and the nature of strong decays.

## Key claim

The study identifies the negative parity resonances N(1535) and N(1650) with masses that agree well with experimental data, but the observed excited positive parity state has a mass too high to be interpreted as the Roper resonance N(1440), and the authors cannot decide from their fits whether the lowest positive parity excited state seen with smeared source and point sink corresponds to the Roper or not.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD calculations using the chirally improved Dirac operator on 16^3 × 32 and 12^3 × 24 lattices with Lüscher-Weisz gauge action at β_LW = 7.9. They implemented three nucleon interpolating operators χ_1, χ_2, and χ_3 with different diquark content and chiral representations, computed all cross-correlations, and applied a variational method to separate ground and excited states by diagonalizing the correlation matrix. Jacobi smeared sources and sinks were used, and the quark mass range covered a_m_q = 0.013 to 0.2, corresponding to pseudoscalar masses from 270 MeV to 866 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing: a = 0.15 fm, determined from the Sommer parameter
- Spatial extensions: 2.4 fm for 16^3 × 32 lattices, 1.8 fm for 12^3 × 24 lattices
- Residual quark mass: m_res = -0.0020(5) a^{-1} = -2.7(7) MeV
- Nucleon ground state mass at a_m_q = 0.013: a M_N = 0.770(23) on 16^3 × 32 lattice
- N(1535) mass at a_m_q = 0.06: a M_N(1535) = 1.457(18) on 16^3 × 32 lattice
- N(1650) mass at a_m_q = 0.06: a M_N(1650) = 1.577(38) on 16^3 × 32 lattice
- Excited positive parity state N'_+ mass at a_m_q = 0.08: a M_N'_+ = 1.873(41) on 16^3 × 32 lattice
- Pseudoscalar-to-vector mass ratio as low as m_PS/m_V = 0.28 achieved with the chirally improved operator

## Context

This work extends previous lattice studies of nucleon excitations that used mainly the χ_1 and χ_2 interpolators, such as those by Blum et al. (no splitting between N(1535) and N(1650)) and by Lee et al. and Dong et al. (who observed the Roper state using Bayesian priors). The inclusion of a third operator χ_3 and the variational method allows better separation of the two negative parity states N(1535) and N(1650), which previous work had not clearly resolved. The results enable future studies of the Roper resonance using more sophisticated operators and larger operator sets, as the authors note that optimizing the spatial structure of nonlocal correlators is a possibility for further lattice studies.
