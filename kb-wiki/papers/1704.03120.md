# Effects of enhanced bulk viscosity near the QCD critical point

> Search for the conjectured QCD critical point is one of the major scientific goals for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC. The growth of the correlation length is a universal feature for systems near criticality, and bulk viscosity exhibits the strongest dependence on the correlation length. We investigate its effects using a relativistic hydrodynamic model to find that rapidity distributions of charged particles and net baryon number are visibly modified if the fireball passes through the vicinity of the QCD critical point on the phase diagram. We also discuss how critical modification of dilepton emission rate may leave imprints on invariant mass spectra.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if the fireball created in heavy-ion collisions passes through the vicinity of the QCD critical point on the phase diagram, the enhanced bulk viscosity (which scales as the cube of the correlation length) can visibly modify rapidity distributions of charged particles and net baryon number, as well as dilepton invariant mass spectra, but the authors emphasize that their primary purpose is to demonstrate these critical effects rather than to determine the actual location of the critical point.

## Method

The authors extend a 1+1 dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model to finite baryon density, incorporating bulk viscosity that scales as ζ ∼ ξ³ and relaxation time τΠ ∼ ξ³ near the critical point, with the correlation length ξ mapped from the Ising model using a linear mapping of temperature and baryon chemical potential. They simulate medium evolution with initial conditions based on color glass condensate extrapolated to 17 GeV center-of-mass energy, using an equation of state constructed from lattice QCD data up to fourth order of baryon fluctuations matched to hadron resonance gas, and compute hadronic distributions via the Cooper-Frye formula at freeze-out with bulk viscous distortion of the phase-space distribution estimated using Grad's moment method.

## Key numbers

- The maximum of ξ/ξ₀ is normalized to 10 in the critical region
- The critical point location is taken as (T_c, μ_B^c) = (0.16, 0.22) GeV
- The size of the critical region is (ΔT, Δμ_B) = (0.02, 0.1) GeV
- Initial thermalization time τ_th = 1.5 fm/c
- Freeze-out energy density ε_f = 0.25 GeV/fm³
- The conjectured critical point is at space-time rapidity η ∼ 1.5

## Context

This work extends the search for the QCD critical point, a major goal of the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC, by incorporating the critical behavior of bulk viscosity into relativistic hydrodynamics, building on insights from the liquid-gas transition (model H) and holographic approaches (Buchel 2007, Natsuume 2007). The results enable future studies including derivation of full hadronic dilepton emission rates, comparison of different δf models, and estimation of radial flow effects, while also raising open questions about how the full hadronic processes would appear in dilepton spectra with bulk viscosity.
