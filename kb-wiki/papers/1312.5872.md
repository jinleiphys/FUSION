# Techniques to treat the continuum applied to electromagnetictransitions in $^8$Be

> Bremsstrahlung emission in collisions between charged nuclei is equivalent to nuclear gamma decay between continuum states. The way the continuum spectrum can be treated is not unique, and efficiency and accuracy of cross section calculations depend on the chosen method. In this work we describe, relate, and compare three different methods in practical calculations of inelastic cross sections, that is, by (i) treating the initial and final states as pure continuum states on the real energy axis, (ii) discretizing the continuum states on the real energy axis with a box boundary condition, and (iii) complex rotation of the hamiltonian(complex scaling method). The electric quadrupole transitions, $2^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ and $4^+ \rightarrow 2^+$, in $α+α$ scattering are taken as an illustration.

## Key claim

The three methods for treating continuum states in bremsstrahlung cross section calculations (full continuum, discretized continuum with box boundary, and complex scaling) are formally equivalent, but their practical applicability depends strongly on the width of the final energy window relative to the resonance energy; the discretized method becomes computationally prohibitive when the window width is orders of magnitude smaller than the central energy.

## Method

The authors computed electric quadrupole (E2) bremsstrahlung cross sections for the $2^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ and $4^+ \rightarrow 2^+$ transitions in $^8$Be, described as an $\alpha+\alpha$ two-body system using the Buck potential. They implemented three approaches: (i) pure continuum wave functions with energy normalization and Zel'dovich regularization, (ii) discretization of the continuum via box boundary conditions with box size $L$, and (iii) complex scaling of the Hamiltonian. The cross sections were integrated over specified final energy windows, with the Zel'dovich parameter set to $\eta = 0.01$ fm$^{-1}$ for regularization.

## Key numbers

- The $0^+$, $2^+$, and $4^+$ resonances in $^8$Be are found at 0.091 MeV, 2.88 MeV, and 11.78 MeV, with widths of 3.6 eV, 1.24 MeV, and 3.57 MeV, respectively (using the Buck potential).
- For the $2^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ transition, a final energy window of 0.5 keV around the $0^+$ resonance energy is sufficient for convergence.
- For the $4^+ \rightarrow 2^+$ transition, the final energy window is $2$ MeV $< E' < 4$ MeV, roughly corresponding to the $2^+$ resonance energy $\pm 1$ MeV.
- For the $4^+ \rightarrow 2^+$ transition with $E_i = 3$ MeV, achieving $\Delta E \sim 0.1$ MeV requires box size $L \sim 350$ fm.
- For the $2^+ \rightarrow 0^+$ transition with $E_i = 0.1$ MeV, achieving $\Delta E \sim 0.1$ keV requires $L \sim 65000$ fm, making the discretization method impractical.
- The differential cross section stabilizes for Zel'dovich parameter values smaller than about 0.02 fm$^{-1}$; an upper radial integration limit of 400-500 fm suffices for $\eta = 0.01$ fm$^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of bremsstrahlung in $\alpha+\alpha$ collisions by Langanke (1986) and Langanke (1986b), who used the full continuum method with the Buck and Ali-Bodmer potentials, and by Myo (1998) who applied complex scaling to Coulomb breakup of $^{11}$Be. The paper systematically compares three continuum treatment techniques for the same nuclear system, showing that while the full continuum and complex scaling methods are broadly applicable, the discretized continuum method becomes numerically intractable for narrow resonances such as the $0^+$ state in $^8$Be (width 3.6 eV). The results enable more reliable calculations of bremsstrahlung cross sections for continuum-to-continuum transitions in nuclear astrophysics, particularly for the triple-alpha process where $^8$Be plays a crucial role.
