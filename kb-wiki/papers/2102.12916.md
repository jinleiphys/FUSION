# Factorization and transverse phase-space parton distributions

> We first revisit impact-parameter dependent collisions of ultra-relativistic particles in quantum field theory. Two conditions sufficient for defining an impact-parameter dependent cross section are given, which could be violated in proton-proton collisions. By imposing these conditions, a general formula for the impact-parameter dependent cross section is derived. Then, using soft-collinear effective theory, we derive a factorization formula for the impact-parameter dependent cross section for inclusive hard processes with only colorless final-state products in hadron and nuclear collisions. It entails defining thickness beam functions, which are Fourier transforms of transverse phase-space parton distribution functions. By modelling non-perturbative modes in thickness beam functions of large nuclei in heavy-ion collisions, the factorization formula confirms the cross section in the Glauber model for hard processes. Besides, the factorization formula is verified up to one loop in perturbative QCD for the inclusive Drell-Yan process in quark-antiquark collisions at a finite impact parameter.

## Key claim

The paper derives a QCD factorization formula for impact-parameter dependent cross sections in inclusive hard processes with colorless final states, but only under two specific conditions: the high-energy limit where longitudinal momenta dominate over transverse and momentum dispersions, and the requirement that the fuzziness in transverse positions be much smaller than the impact parameter itself. These conditions could be violated in proton-proton collisions, meaning the impact-parameter dependent cross section may not be unambiguously definable for small colliding systems.

## Method

Using soft-collinear effective theory (SCET), the author derives a factorization formula for the impact-parameter dependent cross section of inclusive hard processes with only colorless final-state products in hadron and nuclear collisions. The derivation introduces thickness beam functions, defined as Fourier transforms of transverse phase-space parton distribution functions, which encode information on parton distributions in both transverse momentum and position space. The factorization formula is verified up to one loop in perturbative QCD for the inclusive Drell-Yan process in quark-antiquark collisions at a finite impact parameter, with the expansion parameter set by lambda = 1/(bQ) where b is the impact parameter and Q is the photon virtuality.

## Key numbers

* The expansion parameter for the Drell-Yan verification is lambda = 1/(bQ), with the physically interesting case lambda much less than 1 corresponding to the impact parameter being much larger than 1/Q.
* At the LHC, the crossing angle at the interaction point can be measured with an accuracy Delta theta_c less than 10 microradians; for a 7 TeV proton, the uncertainty principle dictates Delta x_T greater than or equal to 1/(2E Delta theta_c) greater than 1.4 fm.
* The transverse coherence length of the hard vertex is of order 1/p_{C,T}, where p_{C,T} is much greater than Lambda_QCD and the coherence length is much less than 1 fm.
* The one-loop spatial quark distribution in a fast moving quark is found to be inversely proportional to r^2, where r is the transverse distance from the original transverse location of the incoming quark.

## Context

This work extends the SCET-based factorization approach previously applied to Drell-Yan production by Bauer et al. (2002) and Becher and Neubert (2011) and to Higgs boson production by Mantry and Petriello (2010), generalizing it to impact-parameter dependent collisions. The factorization formula provides a QCD justification for the Glauber model used in heavy-ion collisions by Miller et al. (2007), showing that thickness beam functions reduce to a product of the Glauber thickness function and the corresponding beam function when the nucleus is treated as an assembly of uncorrelated nucleons. This enables systematic study of cold nuclear effects and nuclear modifications of PDFs, first revealed by the European Muon Collaboration (1983), and potentially allows measurement of transverse phase-space parton distributions in protons through inclusive hard processes in impact-parameter dependent pp collisions.
