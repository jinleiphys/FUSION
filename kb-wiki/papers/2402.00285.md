# Pion Electromagnetic Form Factor from Bethe-Salpeter Amplitudes with Appropriate Kinematics

> Within the framework provided by quantum chromodynamics's Schwinger-Dyson equations (SDEs), the pion's electromagnetic form factor is computed using solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) that have finite spacial momentum, and therefore allow the appropriate kinematics for a given momentum transfer. This removes, for the first time, a limiting approximation in previous SDE calculations where rest-frame solutions to the BSE are used for both the initial and final pion states. In performing these calculations, the rainbow-ladder truncation to the SDEs in the Landau gauge is used, with the quark-gluon interaction given by the Maris-Tandy model. Using Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes (BSAs) that have the correct spacial momentum for a given momentum transfer, $Q^2$, has a dramatic impact on results for the pion's electromagnetic form factor. The difference between results that use rest-frame BSAs and those with correct kinematics is less that 10\% for $0 \leqslant Q^2 \lesssim 1\,$GeV$^2$, however, these corrections grow with increasing momentum transfer and approach 100\% for $Q^2 \simeq 3\,$GeV$^2$.

## Key claim

The pion electromagnetic form factor is computed using Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes that carry the correct finite spatial momentum for a given momentum transfer, removing for the first time the limiting approximation in previous Schwinger-Dyson equation calculations where rest-frame solutions were used for both initial and final pion states. The corrections from using proper kinematics are less than 10% for momentum transfers up to approximately 1 GeV² but grow to approach 100% at Q² ≈ 3 GeV².

## Method

The authors solve the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in Euclidean space using the rainbow-ladder truncation of the Schwinger-Dyson equations in Landau gauge, with the quark-gluon interaction specified by the Maris-Tandy model. They first obtain dressed quark propagators from the gap equation, then solve the BSE for the pion's four-component Bethe-Salpeter amplitude on a discretized grid of momentum variables in both the rest frame and boost frames where the pion carries finite spatial momentum. The electromagnetic form factor is computed in the impulse approximation using two Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes each carrying half the photon momentum, with the quark-photon vertex approximated by the Ball-Chiu vertex plus an Ansatz transverse term rather than solving the inhomogeneous BSE.

## Key numbers

- The difference between form factor results using rest-frame Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes and those with correct kinematics is less than 10% for 0 ≤ Q² ≲ 1 GeV².
- The correction grows with increasing momentum transfer and approaches 100% for Q² ≈ 3 GeV².
- The pion mass is used to tune the light-quark mass such that the bound state mass becomes the physical pion mass, and the strength of the infrared term in the Maris-Tandy model is adjusted to reproduce the experimental decay constant.

## Context

This work extends the SDE/BSE calculations of Maris and Tandy (1999) and Maris (2000) by solving the BSE with finite spatial momentum for the pion, rather than using rest-frame solutions as in prior approaches. The improvement enables more accurate calculations of the pion electromagnetic form factor at large momentum transfers, and the method can be extended to timelike form factors and other pion transitions studied by Miramontes (2022) and Sauli (2022).
