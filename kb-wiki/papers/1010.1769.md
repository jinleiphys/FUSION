# Experimental status of deeply bound kaonic states in nuclei

> We review recent claims of the existence of deeply bound kaonic states in nuclei. Also we study in details the (K-,p) reaction on C12 with 1 GeV/c momentum kaon beam, based on which a deep kaon nucleus optical potential was claimed in [1]. In our Monte Carlo simulation of this reaction we include not only the quasi-elastic K- p scattering, as in [1], but also K- absorption by one and two nucleons followed by the decay of the hyperon in pi N, which can also produce strength in the region of interest. The final state interactions in terms of multiple scattering of the K-, p and all other primary particles on their way out of the nucleus is also considered. We will show that all these additional mechanisms allow us to explain the observed spectrum with a "standard" shallow kaon nucleus optical potential obtained in chiral models. [1] T. Kishimoto et al., Prog. Theor. Phys. 118, 181 (2007).

## Key claim
The authors argue that the observed spectrum in the (K-,p) reaction on 12C with a 1 GeV/c kaon beam, previously claimed as evidence for a deep kaon-nucleus optical potential of about 200 MeV, can instead be explained using a standard shallow optical potential of about 60 MeV when additional reaction mechanisms are included in the analysis.

## Method
The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation of the 12C(K-,p) reaction at 1 GeV/c kaon beam momentum, including not only quasi-elastic K- p scattering (as in the original analysis by Kishimoto (2007)) but also K- absorption by one and two nucleons followed by hyperon decay into pi N, as well as final state interactions from multiple scattering of all primary particles exiting the nucleus. The simulation used a shallow kaon-nucleus optical potential of (-60,-60) rho/rho0 MeV derived from chiral models, and selected events with fast protons emitted within 4.1 degrees in the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers
- The shallow optical potential used had strength Re V_opt = -60 rho/rho0 MeV and Im V_opt = -60 rho/rho0 MeV, as predicted by chiral models.
- The two-nucleon absorption process contributed to binding energies starting from approximately -300 MeV.
- The one-nucleon absorption and several rescattering processes contributed to the region -E_B > -50 MeV.
- The experiment used a 1 GeV/c momentum kaon beam on a 12C target.

## Context
This work extends the debate over the depth of the K- -nucleus optical potential, challenging the claim by Kishimoto (2007) of a deep potential of about 200 MeV based on the same reaction. The authors had previously shown that peaks observed by the FINUDA collaboration in (Lambda p) and (Lambda d) invariant mass distributions could be explained by K- absorption by two or three nucleons (Magas (2006), Ramos (2007)), rather than by deeply bound kaonic clusters. The study enables a re-evaluation of experimental claims for deeply bound kaonic states using conventional reaction mechanisms rather than invoking exotic new physics.
