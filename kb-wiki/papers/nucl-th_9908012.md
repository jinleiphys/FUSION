# Role of break-up processes in fusion enhancement of drip-line nuclei at energies below the Coulomb barrier

> We carry out realistic coupled-channels calculations for $^{11}$Be + $^{208}$Pb reaction in order to discuss the effects of break-up of the projectile nucleus on sub-barrier fusion. We discretize in energy the particle continuum states, which are associated with the break-up process, and construct the coupling form factors to these states on a microscopic basis. The incoming boundary condition is employed in solving coupled-channels equations, which enables us to define the flux for complete fusion inside the Coulomb barrier. It is shown that complete fusion cross sections are significantly enhanced due to the couplings to the continuum states compared with the no coupling case at energies below the Coulomb barrier, while they are hindered at above barrier energies.

## Key claim

Complete fusion cross sections for the reaction $^{11}$Be + $^{208}$Pb are significantly enhanced by couplings to continuum (break-up) states at energies below the Coulomb barrier, but are hindered at above-barrier energies, contrary to earlier simplified models that predicted only reduction.

## Method

The authors performed realistic coupled-channels calculations for the $^{11}$Be + $^{208}$Pb reaction, discretizing the $p_{3/2}$ neutron continuum states into ten energy bins up to 2 MeV with 200 keV steps. They constructed microscopic coupling form factors by folding single-particle transition densities from the $2s_{1/2}$ bound state to $p_{3/2}$ continuum states with the target's nuclear and Coulomb fields, and applied incoming boundary conditions inside the Coulomb barrier to separate complete fusion flux (entrance channel) from incomplete fusion flux (break-up channels).

## Key numbers

- The last neutron in $^{11}$Be occupies the $2s_{1/2}$ state bound by about 500 keV; the first excited $1p_{1/2}$ state is bound by about 180 keV.
- The nuclear form factor extends to the unusual distance of about 25 fm, dominating over the Coulomb form factor at those ranges.
- The Woods-Saxon nucleon-nucleus potential used parameters: $r_\circ=1.27$ fm, $a=0.67$ fm, $V=(-51+33~(N-Z)/A)$ MeV, and $V_{ls}=-0.44~V$.
- The ion-ion potential used $V_\circ= -152.5$ MeV, $r_\circ=1.1$ fm, and $a=0.63$ fm.
- The continuum distribution was considered up to 2 MeV, with a step of 200 keV, yielding 10 effective excited channels.

## Context

This work extends the earlier three-body model of Yabana (1997) and the simplified single-discrete-configuration approach of Dasso and Vitturi (1994), both of which predicted enhancement at sub-barrier energies, while contradicting the survival-factor models of Takigawa, Kuratani and Sagawa (1993) and Hussein, Pato, Canto and Donangelo (1992) that predicted only reduction. The results enable quantitative predictions for complete versus incomplete fusion in weakly-bound halo nuclei, and are consistent with the experimental measurements of Dasgupta et al. (1999) for the $^9$Be + $^{208}$Pb reaction at above-barrier energies.
