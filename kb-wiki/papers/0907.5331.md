# Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics: an off-shell transport approach for relativistic energies

> The dynamics of partons, hadrons and strings in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions is analyzed within the novel Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is applied to Pb+Pb collisions from 20 to 160 A GeV in order to explore the space-time regions of 'partonic matter'. We find that even central collisions at the top-SPS energy of 158 A GeV show a large fraction of non-partonic, i.e. hadronic or string-like matter, which can be viewed as a hadronic corona. Furthermore, we observe that the partonic phase has a very low impact on rapidity distributions of hadrons but a sizeable influence on the transverse mass distribution of final kaons due to the repulsive partonic mean fields. We also find a significant effect on the production of multi-strange antibaryons due to a slightly enhanced $s{\bar s}$ pair production in the partonic phase from massive time-like gluon decay and a larger formation of antibaryons in the hadronization process. All differential hadron spectra are analyzed in comparison to the data of experimental collaborations.

## Key claim

The Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) approach predicts that even central Pb+Pb collisions at the top SPS energy of 158 A GeV contain a large fraction of non-partonic (hadronic or string-like) matter, meaning that neither purely hadronic nor purely partonic models can be used to extract physical conclusions from data at these energies.

## Method

The authors construct a microscopic covariant transport model that combines effective partonic degrees of freedom from the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) with hadronic degrees of freedom from the Hadron-Strings Dynamics (HSD) approach. The DQPM is fitted to reproduce recent lattice QCD results for thermodynamic quantities, providing complex selfenergies for quarks and gluons, while the transition from partonic to hadronic matter is described by covariant transition rates for quark-antiquark fusion to mesons and three-quark fusion to baryons, obeying flavor current conservation, color neutrality, and energy-momentum conservation. The approach is applied to Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies from 20 to 160 A GeV.

## Key numbers

- The effective gluon-gluon elastic cross section reaches up to 10 mb at a scalar parton density of approximately 2 fm^-3, then drops rapidly with increasing density.
- The width parameter b for meson formation is fixed by sqrt(r^2) = b = 0.66 fm, corresponding to the average rms radius of mesons.
- The width parameter for baryon formation is taken as B = 1 fm, corresponding to the average rms radius of excited baryons.
- The strange quark mass is taken as M_s = M_u + 50 MeV, slightly suppressing gluon decay to s sbar relative to u ubar or d dbar.
- The branching ratio for gluon decay to quark-antiquark is approximately 0.8.

## Context

This work extends the DQPM framework of Cassing (2006, 2007) and the earlier PHSD calculations for idealized fireballs from Cassing and Bratkovskaya (2008) to actual nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS energies, using updated lQCD results from Cheng (2008) with smaller bare fermion masses. The approach enables systematic comparison with experimental data from the NA49 Collaboration and provides a framework to disentangle partonic and hadronic contributions to observables, addressing the entropy problem in hadronization models previously discussed by Koal (2002) and the AMPT model.
