# Strange quark helicity in the proton from chiral effective theory

> We compute the helicity-dependent strange quark distribution in the proton in the framework of chiral effective theory. Starting from the most general chiral SU(3) Lagrangian that respects Lorentz and gauge invariance, we derive the complete set of hadronic splitting functions at the one meson loop level, including the octet and decuplet rainbow, tadpole, Kroll-Ruderman and octet-decuplet transition configurations. By matching hadronic and quark level operators, we obtain generalized convolution formulas for the quark distributions in the proton in terms of hadronic splitting functions and quark distributions in the hadronic configurations, and from these derive model-independent relations for the leading nonanalytic behavior of their moments. Within the limits of parameters of the Pauli-Villars regulators derived from inclusive hyperon production, we find that the polarized strange quark distribution is rather small and mostly negative.

## Key claim

Within the framework of chiral effective theory, the polarized strange quark distribution in the proton is found to be rather small and mostly negative, but this result is conditional on the parameters of the Pauli-Villars regulators being derived from inclusive hyperon production data.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark polarization from direct lattice simulations is given as $\Delta s^+_{\rm latt} = -0.046(8)$ (Alexandrou 2020)
- The JAM global QCD analysis gives $\Delta s^+_{\rm JAM} = -0.03(10)$ at a scale of $Q^2=1$ GeV$^2$ (JAM 2017)
- The fraction of the proton's spin carried by quarks and antiquarks from JAM is $\Delta\Sigma = 0.36 \pm 0.09$ at $Q^2=1$ GeV$^2$ (JAM 2017)
- The difference $\Delta s - \Delta \bar s$ from perturbative QCD evolution at three-loop order is predicted to be negative and around 1% of the sum $\Delta s + \Delta \bar s$ (de Florian and Vogelsang 2019)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of chiral loop contributions to the nonperturbative strange quark PDF in the unpolarized sector (Salamu 2018, plb-2016, prd-2016) to the polarized sector. It provides a more systematic approach than earlier meson cloud models (Holtmann 1996, Cao 2003) by using chiral effective field theory to establish a direct connection between the meson cloud of the nucleon and underlying QCD theory. The results enable future comparisons with global QCD analyses such as those from the JAM Collaboration (JAM 2017) and with lattice QCD calculations of strange quark polarization (Alexandrou 2020).
