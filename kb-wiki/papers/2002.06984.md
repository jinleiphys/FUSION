# Model-independent determination of the nucleon charge radius from lattice QCD

> Lattice QCD calculations of nucleon form factors are restricted to discrete values of the Euclidean four-momentum transfer. Therefore, the extraction of radii typically relies on parametrizing and fitting the lattice QCD data to obtain its slope close to zero momentum transfer. We investigate a new method, which allows to compute the nucleon radius directly from existing lattice QCD data, without assuming a functional form for the momentum dependence of the underlying form factor. The method is illustrated for the case of the isovector mean square charge radius of the nucleon $\langle r^2_\mathrm{isov} \rangle$ and the quark-connected contributions to $\langle r^2_p\rangle$ and $\langle r^2_n \rangle$ for the proton and neutron, respectively. Computations are performed using a single gauge ensemble with $N_f=2+1+1$ maximally twisted mass clover-improved fermions at physical quark mass and a lattice spacing of $a=0.08\mathrm{fm}$.

# Model-independent determination of the nucleon charge radius from lattice QCD

## Key claim
The authors present a new method to compute the nucleon charge radius directly from existing lattice QCD data without assuming a functional form for the momentum dependence of the underlying form factor, but the method is demonstrated using only a single gauge ensemble at physical quark mass with a lattice spacing of a=0.08 fm, and the results include only quark-connected contributions while neglecting disconnected diagrams.

## Method
The method uses a discrete Fourier transform of the electric Sachs form factor data computed at discrete on-axis momenta to construct a position-space form factor, then evaluates the derivative at zero momentum transfer via an analytic expression involving Chebyshev polynomials. The authors compute two- and three-point correlation functions on a single N_f=2+1+1 twisted mass clover-improved fermion ensemble at physical pion mass, using five source-sink time separations from 0.97 fm to 1.62 fm, and extract the form factor using both the plateau method and the summation method for excited-state suppression.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing: a=0.0809(2)(4) fm
- Pion mass: M_pi=138.0(4)(6) MeV
- Spatial volume: (L/a)^3 = 64^3, temporal extent T/a = 128
- Five source-sink separations used: 0.97 fm, 1.13 fm, 1.29 fm, 1.46 fm, 1.62 fm
- 750 gauge configurations analyzed with total measurements ranging from 3000 to 48000 depending on the source-sink separation
- Momentum cutoff qmax = 4 * (2pi/L) used for the extrapolation bands

## Context
This work extends lattice QCD determinations of nucleon radii beyond the standard approach of fitting form factor data with parametrized functions such as dipole fits, which introduce model dependence. The method is related to the momentum elimination technique explored in Alexandrou (2016) for the magnetic form factor, and the authors compare their approach to the derivative method of de Divitiis (2012) which requires costly quark propagator expansions. The results enable future lattice calculations to extract radii with controlled systematic uncertainties from the infinite volume and continuum limits without relying on specific functional forms for the form factor.
