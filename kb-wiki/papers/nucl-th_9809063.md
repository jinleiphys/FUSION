# Superscaling in inclusive electron-nucleus scattering

> We investigate the degree to which the scaling functions $F(ψ')$ derived from cross sections for inclusive electron-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering define the same function for different nuclei. In the region where the scaling variable $ψ'< 0$, we find that this superscaling is experimentally realized to a high degree.

# Superscaling in inclusive electron-nucleus scattering

## Key claim

The scaling functions F(ψ') derived from inclusive electron-nucleus quasi-elastic scattering cross sections are essentially the same function for different nuclei (A ≥ 4) in the region where the scaling variable ψ' < 0, demonstrating that superscaling is experimentally realized to a high degree. The authors qualify that this holds specifically for the low-ω side of the quasi-elastic peak where quasi-free scattering dominates, and that the A-independence of the superscaling function is actually much better realized than the q-independence of the normal scaling function.

## Method

The authors analyzed existing inclusive electron-nucleus scattering data for nuclei with mass numbers A = 4 to 208, covering a large range in momentum transfer q (roughly 0.5 to several GeV/c) and energy transfer ω. They divided the measured cross sections by the single-nucleon electromagnetic cross section (weighted by proton and neutron numbers) and the Mott cross section with Rosenbluth kinematical factors, then plotted the resulting scaling function F(ψ') against the dimensionless scaling variable ψ', which incorporates an average removal energy shift E_rem (15-25 MeV) and the Fermi momentum k_F (220-240 MeV/c for the nuclei studied). The analysis focused on the region ψ' < 0 where quasi-elastic scattering dominates.

## Key numbers

- Fermi momenta used: 220 MeV/c for carbon, 230 MeV/c for aluminum, 235 MeV/c for iron, 240 MeV/c for gold, with intermediate values for other nuclei
- Average removal energy E_rem values ranged from about 15 to 25 MeV in the analysis
- Data analyzed covered momentum transfers q from roughly 0.5 GeV/c up to several GeV/c
- For ⁴He at ψ' = 0, F(ψ') is about 10% higher than for heavier nuclei, but agrees perfectly for ψ' < -0.2
- Cross sections at a given ψ' vary over more than three orders of magnitude, yet F(ψ') values are essentially universal for ψ' < 0

## Context

This work extends the concept of superscaling introduced by Alberico et al. (1988) within the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model, testing it for the first time with high-quality experimental data on finite nuclei. The paper builds on the established y-scaling analysis framework reviewed by Day et al. (1990) and demonstrates that different nuclei have a more or less universal momentum distribution once the dependence on Fermi momentum k_F is removed. The results enable future studies to use the universal scaling function as a baseline for isolating contributions from non-quasi-elastic processes such as meson exchange currents and Δ-excitation, and provide experimental support for the local density approximation approach to nuclear momentum distributions as discussed by Benhar et al. (1994).
