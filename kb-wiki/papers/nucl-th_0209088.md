# Electromagnetic structure of the deuteron: review of recent theoretical and experimental results

> This talk reviews recent theoretical and experimental results for elastic electron deuteron scattering (yielding the deuteron form factors), threshold electrodisintegration ($e+d\to e'+p+n$ where the mass of the final $np$ pair, $W$, is only a few MeV above the threshold value of $m_p+m_n$), and high energy deuteron photodisintegration ($γ+d\to p+n$). The talk is based on the complete reviews of Refs. [GVO,S,GG], with a few new results not previously reported.

# Electromagnetic structure of the deuteron: review of recent theoretical and experimental results

## Key claim
Recent high energy measurements of elastic electron-deuteron scattering support the use of a relativistic theory based on an accurate description of the NN channel, but the theory needed for understanding high energy deuteron photodisintegration cross sections and polarization observables is not yet mature.

## Method
The author reviews theoretical predictions for elastic electron-deuteron scattering (yielding deuteron form factors), threshold electrodisintegration (e+d to e'+p+n with final np pair mass only a few MeV above threshold), and high energy deuteron photodisintegration (gamma+d to p+n). The review compares nonrelativistic impulse approximation (NRIA) predictions from six potential models (Argonne AV18, Paris, CD Bonn, IIB, W16, and the Idaho potential) against experimental data, and examines seven relativistic calculations classified into propagator dynamics and hamiltonian dynamics approaches.

## Key numbers
- Nonrelativistic impulse approximation fails at large Q^2 by a factor of 4 to 8 for most models, and by a much larger factor for the Idaho model
- Relativistic effects grow rapidly for Q greater than or equal to 0.3 GeV
- For the VOG spectator calculation, choosing F_3 as a tripole (1+Q^2/Lambda_t^2)^{-3} with Lambda_t^2 = 5 GeV^2 brings all observables into excellent agreement with high Q^2 data measured at SLAC and JLab
- Alternatively, using a dipole form for F_3 with Lambda_d^2 = 0.71 GeV^2 and a dipole form for the rho-pi-gamma form factor with Lambda_d^2 = 1.5 GeV^2 gives agreement for structure function A but increases disagreement in B
- More accurate data is needed for both elastic scattering and threshold electrodisintegration, particularly in the region of minima near Q^2 approximately 1.5 GeV^2
- Recent JLab data generally confirm the perturbative QCD prediction that the photodisintegration cross section falls like s^{-11}, but a model with s^{-10} behavior does just as well
- The pQCD expectation that the polarized photon asymmetry Sigma(90 degrees) approaches -1 as E_gamma goes to infinity is not confirmed by recent Yerevan data

## Context
This review extends the complete reviews of Gross, Van Orden, and Sick; Sick; and Gilman and Gross (all 2001-2002), with a few new results not previously reported. The work compares six potential models (Argonne AV18, Paris, CD Bonn, IIB, W16, and the Idaho potential) and seven relativistic calculations (VOG, PWM, FSR, ARW, CK, LPS, AKP) against experimental data. The findings enable precision tests of relativistic theory, with a 1% measurement of G_C suggested to further constrain relativistic approaches, while high energy photodisintegration remains an open problem where quark degrees of freedom may eventually be required.
