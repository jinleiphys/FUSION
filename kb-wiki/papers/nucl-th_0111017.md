# A New Description of Nuclear Rotational Motion in terms of Intrinsic Pair Mode

> A new method describing nuclear rotational motion microscopically is proposed. We extract the rotational Hamiltonian by introducing the intrinsic pair modes which commute with the rotational mode. Thereby the rotational mode is not treated as zero energy mode in contrast with the conventional RPA formalism so that we circumvent the difficulty related with infrared divergence. The wave function is constructed by angular momentum projection on each intrinsic state. Without numerical integration for projection we calculate the matrix elements analytically under a certain approximation. The numerical calculations are carried out to illustrate the applicability of our method and they show that our method works well.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new microscopic method for describing nuclear rotational motion that avoids the infrared divergence problem of conventional RPA by not treating the rotational mode as a zero-energy eigenmode, instead introducing intrinsic pair operators that commute with the rotational mode in lowest order. The method claims to provide an analytic approximation for angular momentum projection matrix elements without numerical integration, but this analytic treatment relies on neglecting non-diagonal norm matrix elements and other approximations whose validity is tested only for axially deformed nuclei with a simple effective interaction.

## Method

The authors define intrinsic pair operators \(X_n^\dagger\) that commute with the angular momentum operators \(\hat J_\pm\) in leading order, extracted from the eigenvalue equation of the stability matrix after removing the collective rotational mode. The Hamiltonian is decomposed into rotational, intrinsic, and coupling parts expressed in terms of these pair operators, and the wave function is constructed by angular momentum projection onto multi-intrinsic pair states. Matrix elements are evaluated analytically under the approximation that non-diagonal norm matrix elements between different intrinsic states are neglected compared to the diagonal ones, and the Peierls-Yoccoz moment of inertia and Thouless-Valatin moment of inertia are derived, along with B(E2) transition probabilities.

## Context

This work extends the random-phase approximation (RPA) approach to nuclear rotation by circumventing the infrared divergence problem noted by Marshalek and Weneser (1969), where the zero-energy rotational mode leads to a divergent norm of the RPA ground state. The method provides an alternative to the angular momentum projection method used in the projected shell model (PSM) reviewed by Hara and Sun (1995), but with analytic expressions that avoid numerical integration. The approach enables calculation of moments of inertia and B(E2) transition probabilities for axially deformed nuclei without the computational cost of full projection, though the authors note that extension to high-spin states with K-mixing and application of more realistic interactions like the Gogny force remain for future work.
