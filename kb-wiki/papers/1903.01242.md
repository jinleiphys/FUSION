# Charged-lepton-flavor violation in $|\Delta S|=1$ hyperon decays

## Key claim
The authors investigate $|\Delta S|=1$ hyperon decays into different-flavor lepton pairs $e^\pm\mu^\mp$ in a model-independent effective-field-theory framework, finding that hyperon modes can provide complementary coverage of new-physics parameter space to that already available from kaon decays, but only if the LHCb experiment can achieve sufficient sensitivity on hyperon branching fractions, the existing limit ${\cal B}(\Sigma^+\to p\mu^+\mu^-) = (2.2^{+1.8}_{-1.3})\times 10^{-8}$ is not yet competitive with kaon constraints.

## Method
The authors construct the most general dimension-six effective Lagrangian invariant under the SM gauge group that induces $|\Delta S|=1$ transitions with charged-lepton-flavor violation, expressed in terms of vector, axial-vector, scalar, and pseudoscalar quark-lepton couplings. They compute the decay rates for spin-1/2 hyperon decays ($\Lambda\to n e^\mp\mu^\pm$, $\Sigma^+\to p e^\mp\mu^\pm$, $\Xi^0\to\Lambda e^\mp\mu^\pm$, $\Xi^0\to\Sigma^0 e^\mp\mu^\pm$, $\Xi^-\to\Sigma^- e^\mp\mu^\pm$) and the spin-3/2 decay $\Omega^-\to\Xi^- e^\mp\mu^\pm$ using baryonic matrix elements from leading-order chiral perturbation theory, with form-factor corrections from semileptonic hyperon decay phenomenology. They compare the resulting constraints with those from kaon modes ($K_L\to e^\pm\mu^\mp$, $K\to\pi e^\pm\mu^\mp$), $K\to\pi\nu\bar\nu$, leptonic meson decays, $\mu\to e$ conversion, and lepton-flavor-violating neutral meson decays.

## Key numbers
- Experimental limit ${\cal B}(K_L\to e^\pm\mu^\mp) < 4.7\times 10^{-12}$ (Ambrose 1998), probing energy scales above 100 TeV
- LHCb measurement ${\cal B}(\Sigma^+\to p\mu^+\mu^-) = (2.2^{+1.8}_{-1.3})\times 10^{-8}$ (Aaij 2017)
- Form-factor parameters: $M_V = 0.97(4)$ GeV and $M_A = 1.25(15)$ GeV for semileptonic hyperon decays
- Form-factor modifications increase decay rates by a few percent to about 20% for spin-1/2 hyperons, with rate uncertainty under 3% (6% for $\Omega^-$)

## Context
This work extends the long tradition of lepton-flavor-violation studies in the kaon sector (Ambrose 1998, Littenberg 1993) to hyperon decays, motivated by recent LHCb results on $\Sigma^+\to p\mu^+\mu^-$ and by hints of lepton-flavor universality violation in $B$-meson decays (Aaij 2014). The model-independent effective-Lagrangian approach follows the framework of Buchmuller (1985) and Grzadkowski (2010), and the analysis contrasts hyperon coverage of new-physics parameter space with that from kaon modes, $K\to\pi\nu\bar\nu$, $\mu\to e$ conversion, and lepton-flavor-violating decays of other neutral mesons. The results enable future LHCb searches for hyperon LFV decays to be interpreted in terms of specific new-physics scenarios such as leptoquarks, heavy neutrinos, $Z^\prime$ bosons, and multi-Higgs models.
