# The SVZ-Expansion and Beyond

> I discuss the standard SVZ-expansion of the QCD two-point correlators in terms of the QCD vacuum condensates and beyond it due to a new unflavoured 1/Q^2-term (tachyonic gluon mass squared λ^2) which modelizes the effects of uncalculated higher order perturbative terms. The approach is confronted with low-energy and lattice data. One can notice that, in the different examples studied here, high-dimension condensates are expected to deviate largely from their vacuum saturation (large N_c) values, while the new 1/Q^2-term due to λ^2 solves the hadronic mass scale hierarchy-puzzle encountered in the early SVZ-sum rule analysis and improves the low-energy phenomenology. From tau-decay data, one can extract the running strange quark mass m_s(2 GeV)=(93^{+29}_{-32}) MeV and a more accurate value of the QCD coupling α_s(M_Z)=0.117+- 0.002.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard SVZ operator product expansion (OPE) for QCD two-point correlators must be supplemented by a non-perturbative \(1/Q^2\) term, modeled as a tachyonic gluon mass squared \(\lambda^2\), which accounts for uncalculated higher-order perturbative contributions and resolves the hadronic mass scale hierarchy puzzle that plagued earlier SVZ sum-rule analyses.

## Method

The author reviews the SVZ expansion of QCD two-point correlators in terms of vacuum condensates of increasing dimension, then introduces a tachyonic gluon mass \(\lambda^2\) that generates a \(1/Q^2\) term in the OPE, motivated by string-inspired models and lattice data on the plaquette. The value of \(\lambda^2\) is extracted from fits to \(e^+e^- \to\) hadrons data and the pion channel using ratio-of-moments sum rules, and its effects are tested against lattice data for the scalar+pseudoscalar correlator and against tau-decay data for the strange quark mass and \(\alpha_s\).

## Key numbers

*   Tachyonic gluon mass squared: \((\alpha_s/\pi)\lambda^2 \simeq -(0.07 \pm 0.03)\ \text{GeV}^2\), corresponding to \(\lambda^2(\tau \approx 1\ \text{GeV}^{-2}) \simeq -(0.41 \pm 0.18)\ \text{GeV}^2\).
*   Running strange quark mass from tau-decay data: \(\overline{m}_s(2\ \text{GeV}) = (93^{+29}_{-32})\ \text{MeV}\).
*   QCD coupling at the \(Z\) mass: \(\alpha_s(M_Z) = 0.117 \pm 0.002\).
*   Light quark sum from (pseudo)scalar channels: \((\overline{m}_u + \overline{m}_d)(2\ \text{GeV}) = (8.6 \pm 2.1)\ \text{MeV}\).
*   Gluon condensate: \(\langle \alpha_s G^2 \rangle = (7.1 \pm 0.9) \times 10^{-2}\ \text{GeV}^4\).
*   Mixed quark-gluon condensate parameter: \(M_0^2 = (0.8 \pm 0.1)\ \text{GeV}^2\).

## Context

This work extends the original SVZ sum-rule approach (Shifman, Vainshtein, Zakharov, 1979) by adding a tachyonic gluon mass term that models the resummation of higher-order perturbative series (UV renormalon), following proposals by Zakharov (2000) and collaborators. The new term resolves the long-standing hierarchy puzzle where the optimal sum-rule scale for the pion channel (\(\sim 1.8\ \text{GeV}^2\)) was much larger than that for the \(\rho\) channel (\(\sim 0.6-0.8\ \text{GeV}^2\)), bringing both to about \(1\ \text{GeV}^2\). The analysis enables improved extractions of the strange quark mass and \(\alpha_s\) from tau-decay data, and provides a framework for testing higher-dimension condensates against lattice data, showing large violations of the vacuum saturation (large \(N_c\)) approximation.
