# Systematic trends of neutron skin thickness versus relative neutron excess

> Available experimental neutron skin thicknesses of even-even stable Ca, Ni, Sn, Pb, and Cd isotopes are evaluated, and separate trends of neutron skin thickness versus relative neutron excess $δ=(N-Z)/A$ are firstly observed for different isotopic chains. This phenomenon is quantitatively reproduced by the deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock $+$ BCS model with SLy4 force.

## Key claim

Separate, distinct trends of neutron skin thickness versus relative neutron excess are observed for different isotopic chains (Ca, Ni, Sn, Pb) when using evaluated data with improved precision, contradicting the previously reported single linear relationship that was based on data with larger statistical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors evaluated available experimental neutron skin thicknesses for even-even stable Ca, Ni, Sn, Pb, and Cd isotopes by taking weighted averages of measurements from multiple experimental methods including proton scattering, alpha scattering, antiprotonic atoms, and interaction cross sections. They compared these evaluated values with calculations from the deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus BCS model using the SLy4 force, and also examined the relationship between neutron skin thickness and the ratio of proton to neutron separation energies.

## Key numbers

- Normalized chi value from a linear fit to all Ca, Ni, Sn, and Pb data: χ_n = 1.32, outside the expected 1σ range of χ_n = 1 ± 0.17
- Normalized chi value from a global fit including Cd and Te isotopes: χ_n = 1.55, outside the expected 1σ range of χ_n = 1 ± 0.14
- Evaluated neutron skin thickness for ⁴⁸Ca: 0.191(13) fm
- Evaluated neutron skin thickness for ²⁰⁸Pb: 0.167(11) fm
- EOS parameters from SLy4 Skyrme force: symmetry energy E_s = 32.00 MeV, slope L = 45.94 MeV, incompressibility K_0 = 229.91 MeV
- Reported value of L = 106(37) MeV from ²⁰⁸Pb neutron skin thickness of 0.283(71) fm from parity violation electron scattering (Adhikari 2021), with deviation of 0.116(72) fm from the authors' evaluated value for ²⁰⁸Pb

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Trzcinska (2001) which reported a single linear relationship between neutron skin thickness and relative neutron excess using data with large uncertainties. The improved precision of evaluated data reveals separate trends for different isotopic chains, which are reproduced by the deformed Skyrme HF+BCS model with SLy4 force. The results enable better constraints on the equation of state parameters of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, and the authors note that the CREX experiment on ⁴⁸Ca (Horowitz 2014) would help clarify tensions between different determinations of the symmetry energy slope parameter L.
