# Description of $ρ(1700)$ as a $ρK \bar{K}$ system with the fixed center approximation

> We study the $ρK\bar{K}$ system with an aim to describe the $ρ(1700)$ resonance. The chiral unitary approach has achieved success in a description of systems of the light hadron sector. With this method, the $K \bar{K}$ system in the isospin sector $I=0$, is found to be a dominant component of the $f_0 (980)$ resonance. Therefore, by regarding the $K\bar{K}$ system as a cluster, the $f_0 (980)$ resonance, we evaluate the $ρK\bar{K}$ system applying the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations. We construct the $ρK$ unitarized amplitude using the chiral unitary approach. As a result, we find a peak in the three-body amplitude around 1739 MeV and a width of about 227 MeV. The effect of the width of $ρ$ and $f_0 (980)$ is also discussed. We associate this peak to the $ρ(1700)$ which has a mass of $1720 \pm 20$ MeV and a width of $250 \pm 100$ MeV.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the $\rho(1700)$ resonance can be described as a dynamically generated $\rho K\bar{K}$ three-body state, where the $K\bar{K}$ pair forms a cluster identified with the $f_0(980)$ resonance. The match to the experimental $\rho(1700)$ mass and width is achieved only when the finite widths of both the $\rho$ and $f_0(980)$ are included, with the best agreement found for an $f_0(980)$ width of 70 MeV.

## Method

The authors apply the fixed center approximation to the Faddeev equations, treating the $K\bar{K}$ system in isospin $I=0$ as a cluster (the $f_0(980)$ resonance) and the $\rho$ meson as the third particle. The $\rho K$ unitarized scattering amplitude is constructed using the chiral unitary approach with a Bethe-Salpeter equation, using the $SU(3)$ chiral Lagrangian for vector-pseudoscalar interactions. The three-body amplitude is then obtained by summing single and multiple scattering contributions, with the $f_0(980)$ cluster described by a form factor derived from the $K\bar{K}$ bound state wave function.

## Key numbers

- The three-body amplitude shows a peak around 1739 MeV with a width of about 227 MeV when both the $\rho$ width ($\Gamma_\rho \sim 150$ MeV) and the $f_0(980)$ width ($\Gamma_{f_0}=70$ MeV) are included.
- Without the $f_0(980)$ width but with the $\rho$ width, the peak is at 1748.0 MeV with a width of 160.8 MeV.
- The single scattering amplitude (no multiple scattering) gives a peak at 1777.9 MeV with a width of 144.4 MeV.
- The experimental $\rho(1700)$ has a mass of $1720 \pm 20$ MeV and a width of $250 \pm 100$ MeV (PDG).
- The $f_0(980)$ width is varied between 0 and 100 MeV; the resulting peak masses range from 1748.0 MeV ($\Gamma_{f_0}=0$) to 1734.8 MeV ($\Gamma_{f_0}=100$ MeV), and widths from 160.8 MeV to 224.6 MeV.
- The chiral unitary approach parameters used are: $\mu=900$ MeV, subtraction constant $a(\mu)=-1.85$, pion decay constant $f=115$ MeV.
- The $K\bar{K}$ cluster cutoff is $\Lambda = 1030$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary approach, previously successful for two-body systems such as the $f_0(980)$ resonance described as a $K\bar{K}$ molecule (Oller, 1997), to a three-body system using the fixed center approximation. It builds on prior applications of the fixed center approximation to three-body systems (Martinez Torres, 2010; Bayar, 2011) and on the chiral unitary description of vector-pseudoscalar interactions that generate the $K_1(1270)$ resonance (Roca, 2005; Geng, 2006). The results enable a description of the $\rho(1700)$ as a dynamically generated $\rho f_0(980)$ molecular state, suggesting that its dominant decay modes ($\rho\pi\pi$ and $4\pi$) originate from the decays of its constituent $\rho$ and $f_0(980)$ particles.
