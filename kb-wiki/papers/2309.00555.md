# Dilepton polarization as a signature of plasma anisotropy

> We propose the angular distribution of lepton pairs produced in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions as a probe of thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma. We focus on dileptons with invariant masses large enough that they are produced through quark-antiquark annihilation in the early stages of the collision. The angular distribution of the lepton in the rest frame of the pair then reflects the angular distribution of quark momenta. At early times, the transverse pressure of the quark-gluon plasma is larger than its longitudinal pressure as a result of the fast longitudinal expansion, which results in an oblate lepton distribution. By contrast, direct (Drell-Yan) production by quarks and antiquarks from incoming nuclei, whose momenta are essentially longitudinal, results in a prolate distribution. As the invariant mass increases, Drell-Yan gradually becomes the dominant source of dilepton production, and the lepton distribution evolves from oblate to prolate. The invariant mass at which the transition occurs is highly sensitive to the equilibration time of the quark-gluon plasma or, equivalently, the shear viscosity over entropy ratio $η/s$ in the early stages of the collision.

## Key claim

The angular distribution of lepton pairs from heavy-ion collisions can serve as a direct, model-independent probe of the pressure anisotropy of the quark-gluon plasma in its pre-equilibrium stage, with the sign of the quadrupole moment of the distribution distinguishing between plasma emission (oblate, negative) and Drell-Yan production (prolate, positive). The invariant mass at which the transition from oblate to prolate occurs is highly sensitive to the equilibration time or, equivalently, the shear viscosity over entropy ratio eta/s.

## Method

The authors calculate the dilepton production rate from quark-antiquark annihilation to leading order in perturbation theory, modeling the quark and antiquark phase-space distributions using QCD kinetic theory with an anisotropy parameter xi and a quark suppression factor q_s that evolve with time. They assume uniform longitudinal expansion (Bjorken flow) and neglect transverse flow and density inhomogeneity. The Drell-Yan contribution is computed at next-to-leading order using collinear factorization with nuclear parton distributions from EPPS, using the DrellYan Turbo software. The two contributions are summed and the angular distribution is characterized by the quadrupole moment of the Collins-Soper polar angle distribution.

## Key numbers

- For eta/s = 0.16, the variation of the angular distribution as a function of cos(theta) is approximately 16% for the invariant mass range 2.5 < M < 3 GeV/c^2 and approximately 30% for the range 4.5 < M < 5 GeV/c^2.
- The quadrupole moment for quark-gluon plasma production approaches a lower bound of -0.05 for large invariant masses.
- The quadrupole moment for Drell-Yan production is positive and smaller by a factor of about 2 than the upper bound of 0.1 expected from a 1+cos^2(theta) distribution.
- For the default scenario with no initial quarks, negative asymmetries are observed up to M approximately 4 GeV for smaller eta/s.
- If quarks are in chemical equilibrium at all times, the asymmetry remains negative up to M approximately 7-8 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Coquet 2021) on dilepton production in the pre-equilibrium stage and proposes a new observable to detect pressure anisotropy, which has been predicted theoretically (Baier 2000, Epelbaum 2013, Berges 2013, Kurkela 2015, Kurkela 2018) but has lacked experimental signatures. The measurement builds on the feasibility demonstrated by the NA60 collaboration (2008) for fixed-target collisions, and the authors argue that upcoming LHC detector upgrades (ALICE, LHCb) and future projects (LHCb Upgrade 2, ALICE 3) should enable the rejection of the correlated charm background, allowing first measurements of the quadrupole moment and providing direct experimental constraints on the isotropization time of the quark-gluon plasma.
