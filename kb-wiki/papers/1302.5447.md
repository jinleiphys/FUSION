# Bulk viscosity coefficients due to phonons in superfluid neutron stars

> We calculate the three bulk viscosity coefficients as arising from the collisions among phonons in superfluid neutron stars. We use effective field theory techniques to extract the allowed phonon collisional processes, written as a function of the equation of state of the system. The solution of the dynamical evolution of the phonon number density allows us to calculate the bulk viscosity coefficients as function of the phonon collisional rate and the phonon dispersion law, which depends on the neutron pairing gap. Our method of computation is rather general, and could be used for different superfluid systems, provided they share the same underlying symmetries. We find that the behavior with temperature of the bulk viscosity coefficients is dominated by the contributions coming from the collinear regime of the $2\leftrightarrow3$ phonon processes. For typical star radial pulsation frequencies of $ω\sim 10^{4} s^{-1}$, we obtain that the bulk viscosity coefficients at densities $n \gtrsim 4n_0$ are within 10% from its static value for $T \lesssim 10^9 K$ and for the case of strong neutron superfluidity in the core with a maximum value of the $^3P_2$ gap above 1 MeV, while, otherwise, the static solution is not a valid approximation to the bulk viscosity coefficients. Compared to previous results from Urca and modified Urca reactions, we conclude that at $T\sim 10^9$K phonon collisions give the leading contribution to the bulk viscosities in the core of the neutron stars, except for $n \sim 2n_0$ when the opening of the Urca processes takes place.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the three bulk viscosity coefficients (ζ₁, ζ₂, ζ₃) arising from phonon collisions in superfluid neutron stars, finding that for typical stellar radial pulsation frequencies (ω ∼ 10⁴ s⁻¹) and densities n ≳ 4n₀, the coefficients are within 10% of their static values for T ≲ 10⁹ K only when the neutron superfluidity in the core is strong (maximum ³P₂ gap above 1 MeV); otherwise the static approximation is invalid.

## Method

The authors used effective field theory (EFT) techniques to derive phonon self-interactions from the equation of state (EoS) of superfluid neutron star matter, specifically a causal parametrization of the Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (APR) EoS. They solved the dynamical evolution of the phonon number density to compute the frequency-dependent bulk viscosity coefficients, incorporating the phonon dispersion law beyond linear order (which curves downward for neutron pairing) and the collisional rate from 2↔3 phonon processes. The phonon decay rate was computed numerically from all Feynman diagrams at leading order in the EFT Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- For typical star radial pulsation frequencies ω ∼ 10⁴ s⁻¹, the bulk viscosity coefficients at densities n ≳ 4n₀ are within 10% of their static value for T ≲ 10⁹ K when the maximum ³P₂ gap is above 1 MeV.
- At T ∼ 10⁹ K, phonon collisions give the leading contribution to bulk viscosities in the neutron star core, except at n ∼ 2n₀ where Urca processes open.
- The phonon dispersion law correction parameter γ for ¹S₀ pairing is γ = -v_F²/(45Δ²), where v_F is the Fermi velocity and Δ is the pairing gap.
- The binding energy per nucleon parametrization uses ℰ₀ = 15.8 MeV, δ = 0.2, S₀ = 32 MeV, and β = 0.6, with nuclear saturation density n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of transport coefficients in superfluid neutron stars, particularly the phonon contribution to shear viscosity computed by Manuel, Tarrus, and Tolos (2011, 2012), and the bulk viscosity coefficients from Urca and modified Urca reactions by Haensel (2000, 2001). The results show that at T ∼ 10⁹ K, phonon collisions dominate over Urca processes for bulk viscosities in most of the core, except near n ∼ 2n₀. The method is general and applicable to other superfluid systems sharing the same underlying symmetries, enabling more accurate modeling of neutron star oscillation damping and rotational dynamics.
