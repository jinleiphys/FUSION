# Alpha Cluster Formation and Decay in Quartetting Wave Function Approach

> We present a microscopic calculation of alpha-cluster formation in heavy nuclei by using the quartetting wave function approach. The interaction of the quartet with the core nucleus is taken in local density approximation. The alpha-cluster formation is found to be particularly sensitive to the interplay of the mean field felt by the alpha-cluster and the Pauli blocking as a consequence of antisymmetrization. The striking feature of alpha-cluster formation probability across the major shell closures of 82 protons and 126 neutrons is reproduced. The shell (or subshell) effects on the alpha-cluster formation in superheavy nuclei are also analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a microscopic calculation of alpha-cluster formation in heavy and superheavy nuclei using the quartetting wave function approach, reproducing the striking change in formation probability across the major shell closures of 82 protons and 126 neutrons. The key hedge is that the approach uses a local-density approximation for the core nucleus and requires fitting the M3Y interaction strengths to experimental decay energies and half-lives for each nucleus, meaning the shell structure of the core is not explicitly calculated from first principles.

## Method

The authors use a quartetting (four-nucleon) wave function approach, separating the center-of-mass (c.o.m.) motion from the intrinsic motion of the alpha cluster. The c.o.m. Schrodinger equation is solved with an effective potential that includes a mean-field interaction from a double-folding model using M3Y nucleon-nucleon forces, a Pauli blocking term derived from the baryon density, and the Coulomb interaction. The alpha-cluster formation probability is obtained by integrating the bound state c.o.m. wave function from the critical radius (where the alpha-like bound state dissolves due to Pauli blocking) to infinity, and the decay width is calculated using the two-potential approach with Coulomb scattering wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Critical baryon density for alpha dissolution: n_B(r_c) = 0.02917 fm^{-3}
- Critical radii: r_c(210Po) = 7.432 fm and r_c(212Po) = 7.438 fm
- Alpha-cluster formation probability for 210Po: P_alpha = 0.054
- Alpha-cluster formation probability for 212Po: P_alpha = 0.142
- Bound state energy for 212Po: E_tunnel = -19.346 MeV, with Fermi energy mu_4 = -19.771 MeV
- Alpha-cluster formation probability for 210Pb: P_alpha = 0.081
- For superheavy nuclei, P_alpha jumps from 0.077 (268108, N=160) to 0.144 (270110, N=160)
- Free alpha-particle binding energy: E_alpha^(0) = -28.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quartetting wave function approach previously applied to 212Po (Xu 2016, Po) to a systematic study of even-even polonium isotopes and superheavy nuclei. It reproduces the empirical systematics of alpha-cluster formation probability extracted from measured data by Brown, Hatsukawa, Batchelder, and others, and provides a microscopic explanation for the shell effects at N=126 and Z=82. The approach enables predictions for superheavy nuclei and identifies Z=108 as a possible proton shell or subshell closure, consistent with experimental findings by Dvorak.
