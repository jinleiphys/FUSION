# Effective shear and bulk viscosities for anisotropic flow

> We evaluate the viscous damping of anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions for arbitrary temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosities. We show that the damping is solely determined by effective shear and bulk viscosities, which are weighted averages over the temperature. We determine the relevant weights for nucleus-nucleus collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ TeV and 200 GeV, corresponding to the maximum LHC and RHIC energies, by running ideal and viscous hydrodynamic simulations. The effective shear viscosity is driven by temperatures below $210$ MeV at RHIC, and below $280$ MeV at the LHC, with the largest contributions coming from the lowest temperatures, just above freeze-out. The effective bulk viscosity is driven by somewhat higher temperatures, corresponding to earlier stages of the collision. We show that at a fixed collision energy, the effective viscosity is independent of centrality and system size, to the same extent as the mean transverse momentum of outgoing hadrons. The variation of viscous damping is determined by Reynolds number scaling.

## Key claim

The damping of anisotropic flow in heavy-ion collisions is determined solely by effective shear and bulk viscosities, which are weighted averages over temperature, but this result holds strictly only to first order in viscosity (linear regime) and for integrated flow; the separation of shear and bulk contributions may break down at second order due to coupling terms.

## Method

The authors ran ideal and viscous hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with boost-invariant initial conditions and a deformed Gaussian entropy density profile, tuned to match Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV and 200 GeV. They implemented narrow Gaussian viscosity windows centered at various temperatures to extract the weighting functions w_n^(η)(T) and w_n^(ζ)(T) that define the effective viscosities, fitting the resulting Δ_n values with a combination of a smooth rational function and a discrete Dirac peak at freeze-out temperature T_f = 156.5 MeV.

## Key numbers

- For central Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, the effective shear viscosity is driven by temperatures below 280 MeV at LHC and below 210 MeV at RHIC.
- For constant η/s = 0.08, the predicted damping is Δ_2 = -0.11 (10% reduction in v_2) and Δ_3 = -0.19 (17% reduction in v_3).
- The integrated weight W for shear viscosity is -1.34 for v_2 and -2.33 for v_3; for bulk viscosity, -1.30 for v_2 and -2.61 for v_3.
- The freeze-out contribution w_f is only 5% of the total viscous suppression for v_2 and 21% for v_3 at LHC energy.
- The initial rms radius for 0-5% centrality is R_0 = 4.18 fm; for 20-30% centrality, R_0 = 2.97 fm.

## Context

This work extends the concept of effective viscosities introduced by Paquet et al. (2019) from entropy observables to anisotropic flow harmonics v_2 and v_3, and provides a framework to interpret Bayesian studies such as Auvinen et al. (2020) which found η/s is best constrained at T ~ 150-220 MeV. The results enable systematic comparison of different temperature-dependent viscosity parametrizations (e.g., those of Niemi et al. 2015) by reducing them to a single effective value per harmonic, and show that the centrality and system-size dependence follows Reynolds number scaling with 1/R, where R is the transverse size.
