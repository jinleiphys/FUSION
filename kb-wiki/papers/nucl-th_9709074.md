# Nucleon Sigma Term and In-medium Quark Condensate in the Modified Quark-Meson Coupling Model

> We evaluate the nucleon sigma term and in-medium quark condensate in the modified quark-meson coupling model which features a density-dependent bag constant. We obtain a nucleon sigma term consistent with its empirical value, which requires a significant reduction of the bag constant in the nuclear medium similar to those found in the previous works. The resulting in-medium quark condensate at low densities agrees well with the model independent linear order result. At higher densities, the magnitude of the in-medium quark condensate tends to increase, indicating no tendency toward chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

The modified quark-meson coupling model (MQMC), which features a density-dependent bag constant, can recover the empirical value of the nucleon sigma term (approximately 45 MeV) only when the bag constant is significantly reduced in nuclear matter to about 35-40% of its free-space value at saturation density, a condition that coincides with requirements from relativistic nuclear phenomenology and the EMC effect.

## Method

The authors evaluate the nucleon sigma term by computing the response of the nucleon mass to an external scalar field in the MQMC framework, using two parameterizations for the density-dependent bag constant: a scaling model where the bag constant scales with the in-medium nucleon mass, and a direct coupling model where the bag constant couples directly to the scalar mean field. The in-medium quark condensate is then calculated from the energy density of nuclear matter using the Feynman-Hellmann theorem, with the sigma term as input. The coupling constants are fixed by fitting the nuclear matter binding energy (-16 MeV) at saturation density (0.17 fm^-3).

## Key numbers

- Nucleon sigma term in the usual QMC (kappa=0): approximately 12 MeV, almost a factor of four smaller than the empirical value of 45 MeV (Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio, 1991)
- In the scaling model, kappa approximately 2.88 yields sigma_N approximately 45 MeV
- In the direct coupling model, g_sigma^B/g_sigma approximately 1.1 yields sigma_N approximately 45 MeV
- Bag constant reduction at saturation density: B/B_0 approximately 35-40% for both models when sigma_N = 45 MeV
- In-medium quark condensate ratio R_rho at low densities (rho_N < 1.2 rho_N^0) agrees with the linear order result R_rho = 1 - sigma_N rho_N / (m_pi^2 f_pi^2) when sigma_N = 45 MeV
- At higher densities, R_rho begins to increase at approximately 1.8 rho_N^0 (scaling model) or approximately 1.9 rho_N^0 (direct coupling model), indicating no chiral symmetry restoration

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling model (Guichon, 1988) by introducing a density-dependent bag constant, as previously developed by Jin and Jennings (1996). It directly addresses a shortcoming found by Saito and Thomas (1994, 1995), where the usual QMC predicted a nucleon sigma term much smaller than the empirical value extracted from pion-nucleon scattering (Gasser, Leutwyler, and Sainio, 1991). The results enable a consistent description of nuclear matter properties, the EMC effect, and the in-medium quark condensate within a single quark-level model, and provide constraints on the high-density behavior of the condensate that can be compared with hadronic models such as those of Li and Ko (1994), Brockmann and Weise (1996), and Delfino et al. (1995).
