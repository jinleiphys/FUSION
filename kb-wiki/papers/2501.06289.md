# Dynamics of Heavy Quarks in Strongly Coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM Plasma

> We calculate the probability distribution $P({\bf k})$ for a heavy quark with velocity $v$ propagating through strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM plasma in the 't Hooft limit at a temperature $T$ to acquire a momentum ${\bf k}$ due to interactions with the plasma. This distribution encodes the well-known drag coefficient $η_D$ and the transverse and longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficients $κ_T$ and $κ_L$. Furthermore, our calculation determines all of the higher order and mixed moments to leading order in $1/\sqrtλ$ for the first time. These non-Gaussian features of $P({\bf k})$ include qualitatively novel correlations between longitudinal energy loss and transverse momentum broadening at nonzero $v$. We demonstrate that these non-Gaussian characteristics can be sizable in magnitude and even dominant in physically relevant situations. We use these results to derive a Kolmogorov equation for the evolution of the probability distribution for the total momentum of a heavy quark that propagates through strongly coupled plasma. This evolution equation accounts for all higher order correlations between transverse momentum broadening and longitudinal energy loss, which we have calculated from first principles. It reduces to a Fokker-Planck (FP) equation when truncated to only include the effects of $η_D$, $κ_T$ and $κ_L$. Remarkably, while heavy quarks do not reach kinetic equilibrium with the plasma if evolved with this FP equation, we demonstrate that heavy quarks do reach kinetic equilibrium if evolved with the all-order Kolmogorov equation we have derived. Our results thus provide a dynamically complete framework for understanding the thermalization of a heavy quark that may be initially far from equilibrium in the strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM plasma -- as well as new insight into heavy quark transport and equilibration in quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the full probability distribution \(P({\bf k})\) for a heavy quark with velocity \(v\) propagating through strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM plasma to acquire a momentum \({\bf k}\), determining all higher-order and mixed moments to leading order in \(1/\sqrt{\lambda}\) for the first time; the authors demonstrate that the resulting Kolmogorov evolution equation, unlike the Fokker-Planck truncation, has the Boltzmann distribution as a static solution, implying heavy quarks do reach kinetic equilibrium when all non-Gaussian correlations are included.

## Method

The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the thermal expectation value of a Wilson loop on the Schwinger-Keldysh contour, which is related via Heavy Quark Effective Theory to the momentum transfer distribution \(P({\bf k})\). They solve the classical Nambu-Goto action for the trailing string configuration in the AdS black brane metric, imposing matching conditions at the horizon between the time-ordered and anti-time-ordered branches, and evaluate the resulting path integral to obtain an explicit analytic expression for \(P({\bf k})\) at leading order in \(1/\sqrt{\lambda}\). From this distribution they extract all moments and derive a Kolmogorov evolution equation for the heavy quark momentum probability distribution.

## Key numbers

- Drag coefficient: \(\eta_D = \tfrac{1}{2} \pi \sqrt{\lambda} T^2/M\)
- Transverse momentum diffusion coefficient: \(\kappa_T = \pi \sqrt{\lambda} \gamma^{1/2} T^3\)
- Longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficient: \(\kappa_L = \pi \sqrt{\lambda} \gamma^{5/2} T^3\)
- The position of the maximum of \(P({\bf k})\) is given by \(k_L = \pi \sqrt{\lambda} t T^2 \gamma v/2\)
- The jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) is extracted from \(P({\bf k})\) for \(v = 1\)
- The calculation is valid for \(\sqrt{\gamma} \ll M/(\sqrt{\lambda}T)\)

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing puzzle identified by Gubser (2006) regarding the failure of the Einstein relation for the trailing string results, where \(\kappa_L\) and \(\eta_D\) did not satisfy \(\kappa_L = 2 T E \eta_D\). Prior works by Herzog (2006), Gubser (2006), Casalderrey-Solana (2006, 2007) calculated only the drag and diffusion coefficients from the trailing string configuration, while subsequent investigations by de Boer (2008), Son (2009), and Caron-Huot (2011) attempted stochastic treatments but could not achieve equilibration to a Boltzmann distribution. The present work enables a complete description of heavy quark thermalization in strongly coupled plasma, from initial production through stopping, equilibration, and diffusion, and provides a framework that could be extended to study heavy quark transport in QCD quark-gluon plasma.
