# Exploring the effect of positive Q-value neutron transfer in coupled-channels calculations using microscopic nuclear potentials

> We investigated the effect of the degree of freedom of neutron transfer on the cross section of heavy-ion fusion reactions, using the relativistic mean-field formalism within the coupled channel approach (CCFULL). We obtain the microscopic nuclear interaction potential in terms of the density distributions for the targets and projectiles with the NL3$^*$ parameter set and corresponding R3Y nucleon-nucleon potential. The present analysis includes the $^{18}$O-induced reactions, for which experimental fusion cross-section is available around the Coulomb barrier. It is evident from the results that including vibrational and/or rotational degrees of freedom enhances the fusion cross-section at energies below the barrier. However, fusion hindrance persists in this energy region. To address this, we incorporated the two-neutron $(2n)$ transfer channels in the Coupled Channel calculation. A comparison with the Woods-Saxon potential (WS) shows that the R3Y nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential, with intrinsic degrees of freedom, is superior to it, especially at energies below the barrier. This superiority can be attributed to the observed higher barrier heights and lower cross-section of the WS potential compared to the relativistic R3Y NN potential for the considered reaction systems. Consequently, we employed the relativistic mean-field formalism to estimate fusion characteristics for the unknown $^{18}$O-induced reactions, namely $^{18}$O + $^{62}${Ni}, $^{18}$O + $^{70,72,76}${Ge}, $^{18}$O + $^{144,150}${Nd}, and $^{18}$O + $^{144,148,152,154}${Sm}. Our analysis highlights the significant role of positive $Q$-value neutron transfer in enhancing the sub-barrier fusion cross-section for the $^{18}$O + $^{148}${Nd} reaction with the R3Y NN potential. However, the effect of this transfer channel for the other considered reactions is comparatively less pronounced.

# Exploring the effect of positive Q-value neutron transfer in coupled-channels calculations using microscopic nuclear potentials

## Key claim

The inclusion of positive Q-value two-neutron (2n) transfer channels in coupled-channels calculations, combined with the microscopic R3Y nucleon-nucleon potential derived from relativistic mean-field formalism, enhances sub-barrier fusion cross-sections for 18O-induced reactions; however, this enhancement is significant only for the 18O + 148Nd reaction, while the effect is comparatively less pronounced for the other reactions studied.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-channels calculations using the CCFULL code, incorporating microscopic nuclear potentials obtained from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism with the NL3* parameter set and the corresponding R3Y nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential via the double-folding approach. They included vibrational and/or rotational degrees of freedom of the target nuclei up to 2+ states, and subsequently added two-neutron (2n) transfer channels with a macroscopic coupling form factor proportional to the derivative of the nuclear potential. The results were compared with calculations using the traditional Woods-Saxon (WS) potential with Akyuz-Winther parameterization.

## Key numbers

- For 18O + 148Nd, the 2n transfer Q-value is +0.225 MeV, the only positive Q-value among the 2n stripping channels listed for the systems studied
- For 18O + 148Nd, the quadrupole deformation parameter β2 changes from 0.164 (before 2n transfer) to 0.226 (after 2n transfer), and β4 changes from 0.049 to 0.079
- The 2n stripping Q-values for the other systems range from +8.20 MeV (18O + 58Ni) down to +0.11 MeV (18O + 186W), with many systems having negative Q-values
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used include depths V0 ranging from 55.25 MeV (18O + 64Ni) to 98.76 MeV (18O + 182,184,186W), with radius parameter r0 = 1.15-1.18 fm and diffuseness a0 = 0.52-0.73 fm
- The excitation energies E2+ of target nuclei range from 0.08 MeV (154Sm) to 1.66 MeV (144Sm)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Jain et al. (2023) that used microscopic RMF potentials in coupled-channels calculations including rotational excitations but still observed fusion hindrance. It builds on investigations by Rachkov et al. (2014) highlighting the role of neutron rearrangement in fusion enhancement, particularly when colliding nuclei possess magic proton or neutron numbers. The study also follows systematic work by Jiang et al. (2014) on neutron transfer effects and earlier experimental observations by Beckerman et al. (1980) on positive Q-value neutron transfer in Ni + Ni systems. The results enable predictions of fusion cross-sections for 18O-induced reactions on 62Ni, 70,72,76Ge, 144,150Nd, and 144,148,152,154Sm where experimental data are not yet available.
