# Matter induced $ρ$-$δ$ mixing : a source of dileptons

> We study the possibility of $ρ-δ$ mixing via N-N excitations in dense nuclear matter. This mixing induces a peak in the dilepton spectra at an invariant mass equal to that of the $δ$. We calculate the cross section for dilepton production through the mixing process and we compare its size with that of $π-π$ annihilation. In-medium masses and mixing angles are also calculated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that ρ-δ mixing via nucleon-nucleon excitations in dense nuclear matter opens a new dilepton production channel (π + η → e⁺e⁻) and induces an additional peak in the dilepton spectrum at the δ meson mass, but the authors hedge that this may appear only as a shoulder in the φ spectrum rather than a distinct peak due to the δ's vacuum width.

## Method

The authors calculate the ρ-δ mixing amplitude from the nucleon-loop polarization tensor using a relativistic mean-field hadronic Lagrangian with σ, δ, ω, and ρ mesons coupled to nucleons. They compute the in-medium dressed propagator for the coupled ρ-δ system, determine the dielectric function and its zeros to find dispersion relations and invariant masses, and evaluate the mixing angle. The cross section for dilepton production via π + η → e⁺e⁻ through the mixing process is then calculated and compared to the standard π⁺π⁻ annihilation channel.

## Key numbers

- Mixing angle reaches appreciable values for momenta beyond |q| ≈ 0.2 GeV/c at density 2.5ρ₀
- The δ → πη decay width at the δ mass is taken as Γ_δ(m_δ) = 59 MeV, using f_{δπη} = 0.44 from Kirchbach (1996)
- The experimental vacuum width of the δ meson is between 50-100 MeV
- Dispersion curves and mixing angles are calculated at nuclear density ρ = 2.5 ρ₀ (normal nuclear matter density ρ₀)
- The mixing angle vanishes at |q| = 0 and at ρ = 0

## Context

This work extends the study of vector-scalar meson mixing in dense matter, previously explored for ω-σ mixing by Chin (1977), Walecka (1986), Saito (1998), and Wolf (1998), and for ρ-ω mixing by Bhattacharya et al. (1997). The ρ-δ mixing is a pure density-dependent effect forbidden in free space by Lorentz symmetry. The calculation is motivated by upcoming experiments at TJNAF and HADES at GSI that will probe density-driven modifications of vector meson properties, and the authors note that further studies incorporating finite temperature effects and self-consistent many-body treatment are in progress.
