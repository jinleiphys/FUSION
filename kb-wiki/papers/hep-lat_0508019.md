# Baryons with Ginsparg-Wilson quarks in a staggered sea

> We determine the masses and magnetic moments of the octet baryons in chiral perturbation theory formulated for a mixed lattice action of Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and staggered sea quarks. Taste-symmetry breaking does not occur at next-to-leading order in the combined lattice-spacing and chiral expansion. Expressions derived for masses and magnetic moments are required for addressing lattice artifacts in mixed-action simulations of these observables.

## Key claim

The paper derives chiral perturbation theory expressions for octet baryon masses and magnetic moments in a mixed-action lattice setup with Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and staggered sea quarks, finding that taste-symmetry breaking does not appear at next-to-leading order in the combined lattice-spacing and chiral expansion, only entering at next-to-next-to-leading order through loops scaling as \(a^2 m_q \log m_q\) and \(m_q^2 \log m_q\).

## Method

The author constructs the baryon chiral Lagrangian for a mixed lattice action of three flavors of Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks and three flavors of staggered sea quarks, using a dual expansion in quark masses and lattice spacing with power counting \(\epsilon^2 \sim m_q/\Lambda_{\text{QCD}} \sim a^2 \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2\). The Symanzik Lagrangian is analyzed to identify dimension-6 operators that contribute to the effective theory, including taste-symmetry breaking terms in the sea sector and mixed valence-sea four-quark operators. Octet baryon masses are computed to \(\mathcal{O}(\epsilon^3)\) and magnetic moments to \(\mathcal{O}(\epsilon)\) using one-loop diagrams with intermediate baryons from the 1938-dimensional and 1086-dimensional supermultiplets, along with tree-level counterterms.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: \(f = 132\ \text{MeV}\) (used in meson mass formulas)
- Valence-valence meson mass: \(m_{QQ'}^2 = \frac{4\lambda}{f^2}(m_Q + m_{Q'})\) (vanishes in chiral limit)
- Mixed valence-sea meson mass: \(m_{Q_i Q'}^2 = \frac{4\lambda}{f^2}(m_Q + m_{Q'}) + \frac{16 a^2 C_{\text{mix}}}{f^2}\) (does not vanish in chiral limit)
- Taste-singlet sea-sea meson mass: \(m_{QQ}^2 = \frac{8\lambda}{f^2} m_Q + \frac{64 a^2}{f^2}(C_3 + C_4)\)
- Mass splitting parameter \(\Delta \sim \epsilon \Lambda_\chi\) between the 1938-dimensional and 1086-dimensional baryon multiplets in the chiral limit
- Total number of baryon states relevant for loop calculations up to next-to-next-to-leading order: 243 (out of 3024 total baryons in the theory)

## Context

This work extends mixed-action chiral perturbation theory from the meson sector (Bar, 2005) to the baryon sector, enabling analysis of lattice artifacts in mixed-action simulations of baryon observables using the publicly available MILC configurations (Bernard, 2001). The results provide the necessary expressions for extracting physical baryon masses and magnetic moments from simulations that combine computationally cheaper staggered sea quarks with chirally symmetric Ginsparg-Wilson valence quarks, and can be compared with quenched Ginsparg-Wilson studies (Renner, 2004; Bowler, 2004; Bonnet, 2004; Beane, 2005) and with staggered chiral perturbation theory (Aubin, 2003; Sharpe, 2004).
