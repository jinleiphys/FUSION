# On the Isovector Channels in Relativistic Point Coupling Models within the Hartree and Hartree-Fock Approximations

> We investigate the consequences of Fierz transformations acting upon the contact interactions for nucleon fields occurring in relativistic point coupling models in Hartree approximation, which yield the same models but in Hartree-Fock approximation instead. We find for four-fermion interactions occurring in two existing relativistic point coupling phenomenologies that whereas in Hartree the isovector-scalar strength, corresponding to delta-meson exchange, is unnaturally small, indicating a possible new symmetry, in Hartree-Fock it is instead comparable to the isovector-vector strength corresponding to rho-meson exchange, but the sum of the two isovector coupling constants appears to be preserved in both approaches. Furthermore, in Hartree-Fock approximation, both QCD-scaled isovector coupling constants are natural (dimensionless and of order 1) whereas in Hartree approximation only that of the isovector-vector channel is natural. This indicates that it is not necessary to search for a new symmetry and, moreover, that the role of the delta-meson should be reexamined.

## Key claim

The unnaturally small isovector-scalar coupling constant observed in relativistic point-coupling models in the Hartree approximation, which had suggested a possible new symmetry, becomes natural (dimensionless and of order 1) when the same models are transformed to the Hartree-Fock approximation via Fierz transformations, indicating that the smallness is an artifact of the Hartree representation rather than evidence for a symmetry.

## Method

The authors start with a relativistic point-coupling Lagrangian containing four-fermion contact interactions (isoscalar-scalar, isoscalar-vector, isovector-scalar, and isovector-vector) in the Hartree approximation. They apply Fierz transformations in Dirac-isospin space to the exchange densities, neglecting tensor and iso-tensor contributions, to derive a formally identical Lagrangian with redefined coupling constants that explicitly account for exchange processes. Using the inverse Fierz relations, they extract the corresponding Hartree-Fock coupling constants from the Hartree coupling constants of two existing phenomenological models (PC-LA and PC-F4), then compare the magnitudes and naturalness of both sets.

## Key numbers

- For model PC-LA, the Hartree isovector-scalar coupling constant is \(\tilde{\alpha}_{TS} = 7.421 \times 10^{-3} \; 10^{-4} \; \text{MeV}^{-2}\), while the Hartree-Fock value is \(\alpha_{TS} = 2.286 \; 10^{-4} \; \text{MeV}^{-2}\).
- For model PC-F4, the Hartree isovector-scalar coupling constant is \(\tilde{\alpha}_{TS} = -5.924 \times 10^{-2} \; 10^{-4} \; \text{MeV}^{-2}\), while the Hartree-Fock value is \(\alpha_{TS} = 1.345 \; 10^{-4} \; \text{MeV}^{-2}\).
- The ratio of absolute maximum to minimum QCD-scaled coupling constants for PC-LA is 152 in Hartree and 2.18 in Hartree-Fock; for PC-F4 it is 16.2 in Hartree and 1.98 in Hartree-Fock.
- In Hartree-Fock, all four QCD-scaled coupling constants are natural (dimensionless and of order 1) for both models, whereas in Hartree only three of four are natural.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic mean-field (RMF) studies of finite nuclei and nuclear matter, which have traditionally been performed in the Hartree approximation using either finite-range meson exchange or zero-range point couplings (Nikšić 1992, Bürvenich 2001). It directly addresses the finding by Furnstahl, Miller, and Lederman (1996) that the isovector-scalar coupling constant in the Hartree approximation is unnaturally small, which had suggested a possible new symmetry. The results enable a reexamination of the role of the delta-meson in asymmetric nuclear matter studies and suggest that the Hartree-Fock approximation may provide a more realistic framework for QCD scaling and power counting than the Hartree approximation.
