# Calculations of Nuclear Astrophysics and Californium Fission Neutron Spectrum Averaged Cross Section Uncertainties using ENDF/B-VII.1, JEFF-3.1.2, JENDL-4.0 and Low-Fidelity Covariances

> Nuclear astrophysics and californium fission neutron spectrum averaged cross sections and their uncertainties for ENDF materials have been calculated. Absolute values were deduced with Maxwellian and Mannhart spectra, while uncertainties are based on ENDF/B-VII.1, JEFF-3.1.2, JENDL-4.0 and Low-Fidelity covariances. These quantities are compared with available data, independent benchmarks, EXFOR library, and analyzed for a wide range of cases. Recommendations for neutron cross section covariances are given and implications are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper calculates Maxwellian-averaged and californium fission neutron spectrum averaged cross sections and their uncertainties for ENDF materials, but the abstract glosses over the finding that many evaluated covariance matrices produce uncertainties that are either unrealistically large (above 100%) or unrealistically small (below 1%), and that the spread of uncertainties should be constrained to 3-50% for practical applications.

## Method

Maxwellian-averaged cross sections at kT=30 keV and californium spectrum averaged cross sections were calculated using the Maxwellian (kT=1420 keV) and Mannhart evaluation spectra for neutron fission and capture reactions. Uncertainties were propagated from the covariance matrices of the ENDF/B-VII.1, JEFF-3.1.2, JENDL-4.0, and Low-Fidelity libraries, and the results were compared with the KADoNiS database and EXFOR experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Maxwellian-averaged neutron capture cross section uncertainties at kT=30 keV were calculated for ENDF/B-VII.1, Low-Fidelity, and KADoNiS libraries; stellar nucleosynthesis calculations require uncertainties below 3% to finalize s-process branching
- The $^{252}$Cf fission neutron spectrum has an average energy of 2.13 MeV
- Calculated absolute cross section values for linearized files are model dependent and may vary within 1-5% depending on the Mannhart evaluation group structure
- Covariance matrices produce cross section uncertainties within the range 0.5-120%, and the paper recommends this spread be kept within 3-50%
- For the Maxwellian spectrum (kT=30 keV), uncertainties below 1% were found for (n,fission) on $^{235}$U, $^{239}$Pu, $^{240}$Pu; uncertainties above 100% were found for (n,fission) on $^{225}$Ac, $^{226}$Ac, $^{233}$Th, $^{229}$Pa, $^{235}$Np, $^{246}$Pu, $^{250}$Cm, $^{247}$Bk, $^{250}$Bk, $^{246}$Cf, $^{248}$Cf, $^{250}$Cf, $^{254}$Cf, $^{251}$Es, $^{253}$Es, $^{255}$Es
- For the Mannhart spectrum, uncertainties below 1% were found for (n,fission) on $^{235}$U, $^{238}$U, $^{239}$Pu, $^{240}$Pu and for (n,$\gamma$) on $^{52}$Cr; uncertainties above 100% were found for (n,fission) on $^{225}$Ac, $^{226}$Ac, $^{234}$Th and for (n,$\gamma$) on $^{229}$Pa, $^{231}$U, $^{234}$Np, $^{235}$Np, $^{236}$Np, $^{237}$Pu

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of reaction rates and neutron cross sections by Pritychenko (2010) and Pritychenko (2012) that demonstrated the potential of ENDF/B-VII for applications such as the KADoNiS stellar nucleosynthesis library by Dillmann (2006). The results enable improved covariance recommendations for ENDF libraries and identify specific nuclides where covariance matrices need revision for both nuclear astrophysics and fast-neutron dosimetry applications.
