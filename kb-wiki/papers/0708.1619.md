# Magnetic Phases in Dense Quark Matter

> In this paper I discuss the magnetic phases of the three-flavor color superconductor. These phases can take place at different field strengths in a highly dense quark system. Given that the best natural candidates for the realization of color superconductivity are the extremely dense cores of neutron stars, which typically have very large magnetic fields, the magnetic phases here discussed could have implications for the physics of these compact objects.

## Key claim

The three-flavor color superconductor can exist in three distinct magnetic phases, CFL, MCFL, and PCFL, depending on the strength of an external magnetic field, but the transition from CFL to MCFL is not a true phase transition but a crossover or symmetry transmutation, while the transition from MCFL to PCFL is a genuine second-order phase transition.

## Method

The author analyzes the low-energy effective theory of the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase in the presence of a constant magnetic field, using the Ritus method to obtain the dispersion relation of charged mesons and determine the threshold field at which charged Goldstone bosons become unstable. For the MCFL-to-PCFL transition, a Ginzburg-Landau approach analogous to Abrikosov’s treatment of type-II superconductors is applied to study the formation of a gluon vortex condensate when the magnetic field exceeds the Meissner mass squared of charged gluons.

## Key numbers

- The threshold field for the CFL-to-MCFL transmutation is given by \(\widetilde{e}\widetilde{B}_{\mathrm{MCFL}} = \frac{4}{v_{\bot}^2}\Delta_{\mathrm{CFL}}^2 \simeq 12\Delta_{\mathrm{CFL}}^2\), where \(v_{\bot} \approx 1/\sqrt{3}\).
- Using \(\Delta_{\mathrm{CFL}} \sim 15\ \mathrm{MeV}\), the order of magnitude of the field strength at which charged mesons decouple is \(\sim 10^{16}\ \mathrm{G}\).
- The PCFL phase appears when \(\widetilde{B} \geq \widetilde{B}_{\mathrm{PCFL}} = m_M^2\), where \(m_M^2\) is the Meissner mass squared of the charged gluons, satisfying \(\mu^2 \gg m_M^2 \gg \Delta_{\mathrm{CFL}}^2\).
- At asymptotically large densities, \(\widetilde{B}_{\mathrm{MCFL}} \ll \widetilde{B}_{\mathrm{PCFL}}\) for each chemical potential \(\mu\).

## Context

This work extends the study of color superconductivity in three-flavor quark matter (Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek 1999) by incorporating the effects of strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetars (field range \(10^{14} - 10^{16}\ \mathrm{G}\)). It builds on prior analyses of magnetic instabilities in Yang-Mills fields (Skalozub, Olesen) and the electroweak theory, and it enables future investigations of the observable signatures of color-superconducting cores in highly magnetized compact stars, particularly magnetars where standard models face observational inconsistencies.
