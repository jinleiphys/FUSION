# Entrance channel effects on the evaporation residue yields in reactions leading to the $^{220}$Th compound nucleus

> The evaporation residue yields from compound nuclei $^{220}$Th formed in the $^{16}$O+$^{204}$Pb, $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf, $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba, $^{124}$Sn+$^{96}$Zr reactions are analyzed to study the entrance channel effects by comparison of the capture, fusion and evaporation residue cross sections calculated by the combined dinuclear system (DNS) and advanced statistical models. The difference between evaporation residue (ER) cross sections can be related to the stages of compound nucleus formation or/and at its surviving against fission. The sensitivity of the both stages in the evolution of DNS up to the evaporation residue formation to the angular momentum of DNS is studied. The difference between fusion excitation functions are explained by the hindrance to complete fusion due to the larger intrinsic fusion barrier $B^*_{\rm fus}$ for the transformation of the DNS into a compound nucleus and the increase of the quasifission contribution due to the decreasing of quasifission barrier $B_{\rm qf}$ as a function of the angular momentum. The largest value of the ER residue yields in the very mass asymmetric $^{16}$O+$^{204}$Pb reaction is related to the large fusion probability and to the relatively low threshold of the excitation energy of the compound nucleus. Due to the large threshold of the excitation energy (35 MeV) of the $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf reaction, it produces less the ER yields than the almost mass symmetric $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction having the lowest threshold value (12 MeV).

# Entrance channel effects on the evaporation residue yields in reactions leading to the $^{220}$Th compound nucleus

## Key claim

The evaporation residue yields from the $^{220}$Th compound nucleus depend strongly on the entrance channel mass asymmetry, with the largest yields occurring for the very mass asymmetric $^{16}$O+$^{204}$Pb reaction due to its large fusion probability and relatively low excitation energy threshold, while the $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf reaction produces less evaporation residue than the almost mass symmetric $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction at low excitation energies despite being more asymmetric, because the $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf reaction has a high threshold excitation energy of 35 MeV that makes low-energy compound nucleus formation inaccessible.

## Method

The authors calculated capture, fusion, and evaporation residue cross sections for four reactions forming $^{220}$Th ($^{16}$O+$^{204}$Pb, $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf, $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba, $^{124}$Sn+$^{96}$Zr) using the combined dinuclear system (DNS) model for the capture and fusion stages and an advanced statistical model for the de-excitation cascade. The capture probability was determined by solving the radial motion equations with friction forces, while the fusion probability was obtained from the competition between complete fusion and quasifission using the intrinsic fusion barrier $B^*_{\rm fus}$ and quasifission barrier $B_{\rm qf}$ calculated from the potential energy surface. The evaporation residue cross sections were computed by summing partial cross sections over angular momentum, with survival probabilities against fission calculated using a statistical model that includes the damping of shell corrections.

## Key numbers

- The $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction has the lowest threshold excitation energy for capture at 12 MeV, while the $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf reaction has a threshold of 35 MeV.
- The $Q_{gg}$ values are -180.516 MeV for $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba and -188.332 MeV for $^{124}$Sn+$^{96}$Zr, placing these reactions in valleys of the potential energy surface at charge numbers Z=34 and Z=40 respectively.
- The $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf reaction has $Q_{gg}$ = -99.49 MeV, which prevents capture at excitation energies below 35 MeV.
- The $xn$ evaporation residue excitation function for the $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf reaction reaches a maximum at $E^*_{\rm CN}$ = 44 MeV, while the total evaporation residue cross section peaks at $E^*_{\rm CN}$ = 50 MeV.
- The $^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba reaction shows maximum survival probability at excitation energies of 21-23 MeV with angular momentum range $\ell$ = 0-35.
- The capture excitation functions for the mass asymmetric reactions ($^{16}$O+$^{204}$Pb and $^{40}$Ar+$^{180}$Hf) are one order of magnitude higher than for the almost symmetric reactions ($^{82}$Se+$^{138}$Ba and $^{124}$Sn+$^{96}$Zr).
- The fusion excitation function for the almost symmetric reactions is two orders of magnitude lower than for the mass asymmetric reactions.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of entrance channel effects on evaporation residue yields previously studied by Sahm et al., Hinde et al. (1989), and Fazio et al. (2004) for reactions forming the same compound nucleus, where differences in evaporation residue cross sections were attributed to hindrance to complete fusion. The results contradict the classical classification that complete fusion always occurs for heavy ion collisions at angular momenta below $\ell_{\rm cr}$, as suggested by Froebrich, by showing that quasifission can occur even at $\ell=0$ for systems that are not very mass asymmetric, a phenomenon previously insinuated by Hinde et al. (1989) from comparisons of $^{30}$Si+$^{186}$W and $^{12}$C+$^{204}$Pb reactions. The work enables improved predictions for superheavy element synthesis by demonstrating that the angular momentum distribution of the compound nucleus differs for reactions with different mass asymmetry even at the same excitation energy, as shown earlier by Fazio et al. (2005).
