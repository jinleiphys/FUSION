# A Comparison between Relativistic and Semi-Relativistic Treatment in the Diquark-Quark Model

> In the diquark-quark model of the nucleon including scalar and axialvector diquarks we compare solutions of the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation in the instantaneous Salpeter approximation and in the fully covariant (i.e. four-dimensional) treatment. We obtain that the binding energy is severly underestimated in the Salpeter approximation. For the electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon we find that in both approaches the overall shapes of the respective form factors are reasonably similar up to $Q^2 \approx 0.4$ GeV^2. However, the magnetic moments differ substantially as well as results for the pion-nucleon and the axial coupling of the nucleon.

## Key claim

The Salpeter approximation (instantaneous, three-dimensional reduction) severely underestimates the binding energy of the nucleon compared to the fully covariant four-dimensional Bethe-Salpeter treatment, despite the binding energy being only 6% of the sum of constituent masses; the electromagnetic form factors are similar only up to Q² ≈ 0.4 GeV², while magnetic moments, pion-nucleon coupling, and axial coupling differ substantially.

## Method

The authors solved the ladder Bethe-Salpeter equation for the nucleon in a diquark-quark model including scalar and axialvector diquarks, using both the fully covariant four-dimensional treatment and the instantaneous Salpeter approximation. The vertex functions were expanded in Chebyshev polynomials in the angular variable z, and the coupled integral equations were solved iteratively. Electromagnetic form factors were computed via the impulse approximation diagrams (quark and diquark couplings) and, for the full calculation, additional exchange and seagull diagrams were included to ensure current conservation.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass fixed to M = 0.939 GeV for all calculations
- Set I (scalar diquark only): quark mass m_q = 0.35 GeV, scalar diquark mass m_sc = 0.65 GeV, diquark width λ = 0.18 fm; coupling g_s = 20.0 (Salpeter) vs. 16.5 (full)
- Set II (scalar + axialvector diquarks): m_q = 0.35 GeV, m_sc = 0.65 GeV, m_ax = 0.65 GeV, λ = 0.24 fm; coupling g_s = 12.3 (Salpeter, ref. 11.5) vs. 9.6 (full)
- Binding energy is only 6% of the sum of constituent masses
- Eigenvalue amplified by about 20-25% in the Salpeter approximation
- Form factors similar only up to Q² ≈ 0.4 GeV²; Salpeter approximation fails above 0.5 GeV²
- Proton magnetic moment (Set I): 1.58 (Salpeter) vs. 2.38 (full, all diagrams)
- Proton magnetic moment (Set II): 2.29 (Salpeter, ref. 2.78) vs. 2.45 (full, all diagrams)
- Proton charge radius (Set I): 0.89 fm (Salpeter) vs. 0.99 fm (full, all diagrams)
- Neutron charge radius squared (Set I): -0.28 fm² (Salpeter) vs. -0.23 fm² (full, all diagrams)
- Pion-nucleon coupling g_πNN (Set I): 8.96 (Salpeter) vs. 15.34 (full, all diagrams)
- Axial coupling g_A (Set I): 0.93 (Salpeter) vs. 1.46 (full, all diagrams)

## Context

This work extends the diquark-quark model calculations of Kei96a and Kei96b, which used the Salpeter approximation, by providing a fully covariant four-dimensional solution. The authors find discrepancies with Kei96b for the axialvector diquark case, attributing them partly to incomplete axialvector components and possibly a minor error in that work. The results enable future calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors at higher momentum transfers (several GeV²) using the full solution, and demonstrate that the Salpeter approximation is inadequate for quantitative predictions of magnetic moments, pion-nucleon coupling, and axial coupling.
