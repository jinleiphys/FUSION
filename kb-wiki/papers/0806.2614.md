# Radiative corrections to pion Compton scattering

> We calculate the one-photon loop radiative corrections to charged pion Compton scattering, $π^- γ\to π^- γ$. Ultraviolet and infrared divergencies are both treated in dimensional regularization. Analytical expressions for the ${\cal O}(α)$ corrections to the invariant Compton scattering amplitudes, $A(s,u)$ and $B(s,u)$, are presented for 11 classes of contributing one-loop diagrams. Infrared finiteness of the virtual radiative corrections is achieved (in the standard way) by including soft photon radiation below an energy threshold $λ$, and its relation to the experimental detection threshold is discussed. We find that the radiative corrections are maximal in backward directions, reaching e.g. -2.4% for a center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s}=4m_π$ and $λ=5 $MeV. Furthermore, we extend our calculation of the radiative corrections by including the leading pion structure effect (at low energies) in form of its electric and magnetic polarizability difference, $α_π- β_π\simeq 6\cdot 10^{-4} $fm$^3$. We find that this structure effect does not change the relative size and angular dependence of the radiative corrections to pion Compton scattering. Our results are particularly relevant for analyzing the COMPASS experiment at CERN which aims at measuring the pion electric and magnetic polarizabilities with high statistics using the Primakoff effect.

# Radiative corrections to pion Compton scattering

## Key claim
The one-photon loop radiative corrections to charged pion Compton scattering are calculated analytically, and the authors find that these corrections are maximal in backward directions (reaching e.g. -2.4% at a center-of-mass energy of √s = 4mπ with a soft-photon detection threshold λ = 5 MeV), but the abstract's claim that the pion structure effect does not change the relative size and angular dependence of the radiative corrections is conditional on including only the leading polarizability difference απ - βπ ≃ 6·10⁻⁴ fm³, with the pion-loop correction of chiral perturbation theory still expected to work partly against the polarizability effects.

## Method
The authors evaluate 11 classes of one-photon loop diagrams for π⁻γ → π⁻γ in scalar quantum electrodynamics using dimensional regularization for both ultraviolet and infrared divergences, working in Coulomb gauge in the center-of-mass frame. Analytical expressions for the O(α) corrections to the invariant Compton scattering amplitudes A(s,u) and B(s,u) are presented, and infrared finiteness is achieved by including soft photon radiation below an energy threshold λ. The calculation is extended by including the leading pion structure effect via a two-photon contact-vertex proportional to the polarizability difference απ - βπ.

## Key numbers
- Radiative corrections reach -2.4% at √s = 4mπ and λ = 5 MeV in backward directions (z ≃ -1).
- For λ = 10 MeV or 20 MeV at √s = 4mπ, the -2.4% value changes to -1.8% or -1.2%, respectively.
- The chiral perturbation theory prediction for the pion polarizability difference is απ - βπ = (5.7 ± 1.0)·10⁻⁴ fm³ (Gasser et al., two-loop order).
- Existing experimental determinations: απ - βπ = (15.6 ± 7.8)·10⁻⁴ fm³ from Serpukhov and (11.6 ± 3.4)·10⁻⁴ fm³ from Mainz.
- The radiative corrections simulate fake pion polarizabilities απ(rc) = -βπ(rc) ≃ 3·10⁻⁵ fm³, corresponding to about 10% of the chiral perturbation theory prediction.
- The non-vanishing polarizability sum from two-loop chiral perturbation theory is απ + βπ = (0.16 ± 0.1)·10⁻⁴ fm³.

## Context
This work extends the calculation of radiative corrections to scalar (spin-0) boson Compton scattering, filling a gap where previous results by Akhundov et al. were presented mainly in numerical form without accessible analytical expressions, and where radiative corrections were sometimes merely adapted from muon Compton scattering by replacing the mass mμ → mπ, which mistreats differences between scalar and spin-1/2 couplings. The results are particularly relevant for analyzing the COMPASS experiment at CERN, which aims to measure pion electric and magnetic polarizabilities using the Primakoff effect, and the authors note that the chiral prediction απ - βπ = (5.7 ± 1.0)·10⁻⁴ fm³ from two-loop chiral perturbation theory (Burgi, Gasser) is in conflict with existing experimental determinations from Serpukhov and Mainz that are more than twice as large.
