# Elliptic flow of thermal photons in chemically non-equilibrated QCD medium

> Heavy-ion experiments have recently revealed that azimuthal momentum anisotropy -- elliptic flow -- of direct photons from a QCD medium is a few times larger than hydrodynamic predictions. I present a possible explanation for the enhancement based on late quark chemical equilibration. The color glass theory indicates that the medium is initially gluon-rich. Thermal photons are then mainly produced after quarks are produced, at which point large flow anisotropy is developed. The numerical estimations indicate that slow chemical equilibration visibly enhances the elliptic flow of thermal photons.

## Key claim

Slow quark chemical equilibration in the quark-gluon plasma can visibly enhance the elliptic flow of thermal photons, offering a possible explanation for the "photon $v_2$ puzzle" where direct photon anisotropy is a few times larger than hydrodynamic predictions.

## Method

The author develops a (2+1)-dimensional boost-invariant hydrodynamic model coupled to rate equations for quark and gluon number densities, assuming local thermalization but not chemical equilibrium. The medium is initially gluon-rich based on color glass condensate theory, and thermal photon emission rates are computed using a hyperbolic interpolation between hadronic and QGP phase rates. The chemical reaction rates for gluon splitting, quark pair production, and gluon emission from a quark are parametrized as $r_i = c_i T$ with dimensionless parameters $c_i$, where $c_b$ controls the quark pair production rate and thus the chemical equilibration time.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV with impact parameter $b=7$ fm and thermalization time $\tau_\mathrm{th} = 0.4$ fm/$c$, the quark chemical equilibration parameters $c_b = 0.2, 0.5, 2.0$ correspond to chemical relaxation times $\tau_\mathrm{chem} \sim 5.0, 2.0, 0.5$ fm/$c$ respectively at average medium temperature $T \sim 0.2$ GeV.
- The crossover temperature $T_c = 0.17$ GeV and crossover width $\Delta T = 0.017$ GeV are used for the equation of state interpolation between parton gas and hadron resonance gas.
- The typical chemical equilibration time suggested in a pre-equilibrium model is $2$ fm/$c$.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic treatment of heavy-ion collisions by incorporating separate thermalization and chemical equilibration timescales, building on the author's prior work (Monnai 2014) on pre-equilibrium dynamics. It addresses the "photon $v_2$ puzzle" observed at RHIC (Adare 2011) and LHC (Lohner 2012), where direct photon elliptic flow is unexpectedly large compared to standard hydrodynamic predictions (Schenke 2010). The results suggest that the interplay of pre- and post-equilibrium physics is important for understanding photon observables, analogous to how thermal and chemical freeze-out must be treated separately for hadronic spectra. Future work requires more quantitative photon emission rates, initial conditions, equation of state, and inclusion of prompt photon contributions for direct comparison to experimental data.
