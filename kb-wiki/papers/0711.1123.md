# Quarkonia Photoproduction at Nucleus Colliders

> Exclusive photoproduction of heavy quarkonia in high-energy ultraperipheral ion-ion interactions (gamma A --> V A, where V = J/Psi,Upsilon and the nucleus A remains intact) offers a useful means to constrain the small-x nuclear gluon density. We discuss preliminary results on J/Psi photoproduction in Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, as well as full simulation-reconstruction studies of photo-produced Upsilon's in Pb-Pb interactions at the LHC.

## Key claim

Exclusive photoproduction of heavy quarkonia in ultraperipheral ion-ion collisions can constrain the small-x nuclear gluon density, but the preliminary PHENIX results for J/psi production at RHIC have experimental uncertainties too large to draw detailed conclusions about the nuclear gluon distribution, and the possible contribution of an incoherent gamma-nucleon component must also be considered.

## Method

The paper presents preliminary PHENIX measurements of exclusive J/psi photoproduction in Au-Au ultraperipheral collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, reconstructed in the dielectron channel using central tracking, RICH counters, and electromagnetic calorimetry, with event selection requiring an EMCal cluster above 0.8 GeV, a rapidity gap in one or both 3.0 < |eta| < 3.9 ranges, and at least 30 GeV deposited in the Zero-Degree-Calorimeters to select events with forward neutron emission. For the LHC, full simulation-reconstruction studies of Upsilon photoproduction in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV were performed using the STARLIGHT Monte Carlo generator with CMS detector response.

## Key numbers

- Maximum photon-nucleus center-of-mass energy at RHIC: W^max_gA ≈ 35 GeV; at LHC: W^max_gA ≈ 1 TeV
- Gluon distribution probed at x = M_V^2 / W_gA^2 ≈ 10^-2 for J/psi at RHIC and ≈ 10^-4 for Upsilon at LHC
- PHENIX J/psi yield: N_Jpsi = 10 ± 3 (stat) ± 3 (syst) events in the dielectron channel
- PHENIX J/psi cross-section at mid-rapidity: dsigma/dy at |y| < 0.5 = 48 ± 14 (stat) ± 16 (syst) microbarns
- Probability of forward neutron emission from electromagnetic dissociation at RHIC: P_Xn ≈ 0.64 at y = 0
- Upsilon photoproduction cross-section in Pb-Pb at LHC: approximately 150 microbarns without shadowing, 78 microbarns with leading-twist shadowing
- CMS reconstruction efficiency for Upsilon: epsilon_rec × Acceptance × epsilon_yield-extract ≈ 20%
- Expected number of Upsilon events in CMS at nominal 0.5 nb^-1 Pb-Pb integrated luminosity: approximately 500
- Coherent Upsilon p_T peak: p_T ≈ M_V / gamma ≈ 30 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the study of nuclear gluon distributions from deep inelastic scattering data (which only cover x above about 10^-2) into the small-x regime using ultraperipheral collisions, a technique reviewed in the UPC report. The PHENIX results are compared to theoretical predictions from STARLIGHT, Strikman (2005), Machado (2007), and Kopeliovich (2007), and the LHC projections build on calculations by Frankfurt (2003) and Goncalves (2006) that include gluon saturation effects. The measurements enable future constraints on the nuclear gluon density and tests of non-linear QCD evolution (Color Glass Condensate, black-disk limit) at the LHC.
