# Static $\bar Q Q$ Potentials and the Magnetic Component of QCD Plasma near $T_c$

> Static quark-anti-quark potential encodes important information on the chromodynamical interaction between color charges, and recent lattice results show its very nontrivial behavior near the deconfinement temperature $T_c$. In this paper we study such potential in the framework of the ``magnetic scenario'' for the near Tc QCD plasma, and particularly focus on the linear part (as quantified by its slope, the tension) in the potential as well as the strong splitting between the free energy and internal energy. By using an analytic ``ellipsoidal bag'' model, we will quantitatively relate the free energy tension to the magnetic condensate density and relate the internal energy tension to the thermal monopole density. By converting the lattice results for static potential into density for thermal monopoles we find the density to be very large around Tc and indicate at quantum coherence, in good agreement with direct lattice calculation of such density. A few important consequences for heavy ion collisions phenomenology will also be discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the strong splitting between the free energy and internal energy of a static quark-antiquark pair near the deconfinement temperature arises from the different response of the magnetic component of QCD plasma to slow versus fast separation of the charges, with the free energy tension related to the monopole condensate and the internal energy tension related to thermal monopole density.

## Method

The authors develop an analytic "ellipsoidal bag" model that solves the Laplace equation for the electric field between a static quark-antiquark pair with a tangent boundary condition on the bag surface, parameterized by rotational ellipsoids with the charges at the focal points. They then minimize the free energy for slow adiabatic separation (yielding the free energy) and the total energy for fast separation (yielding the internal energy), relating the resulting string tensions to the monopole condensate energy density and thermal monopole density respectively.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum string tension: sigma_vac approximately (426 MeV)^2 approximately 0.92 GeV/fm
- Internal energy tension sigma_V at T_c peaks at about 5 times the vacuum string tension sigma_vac
- Internal energy flattens to asymptotic value V(T,infinity) approximately 4 GeV at T_c
- Entropy at large separation at T_c: S(T_c,infinity) approximately 20, implying exp(20) states
- Free energy string tension formula: sqrt(sigma_F) = 2.32 x alpha_E^(1/4) x epsilon_C^(1/4)
- Internal energy tension survives to at least T = 1.3 T_c

## Context

This work extends the "magnetic scenario" for QCD plasma near T_c previously developed by the same authors (Liao and Shuryak, 2007-2008), where chromo-magnetic monopoles undergo Bose-Einstein condensation below T_c and exist as thermal quasiparticles above T_c. The model explains lattice results from Kaczmarek and Zantow (2005) showing the dramatic splitting between free and internal energies, and connects to earlier dual superconductor models of confinement by 't Hooft and Mandelstam. The results enable quantitative extraction of thermal monopole densities from lattice potential data and have implications for quarkonium survival, jet quenching near T_c, and the interpretation of the strongly coupled quark-gluon plasma as a liquid with plasma parameter Gamma approximately 3.
