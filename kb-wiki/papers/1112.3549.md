# Restriction on the Neutron-Antineutron Oscillations from the SNO Data on the Deuteron Stability

> Restriction on the neutron-antineutron oscillation time in vacuum is obtained from latest SNO data on the deuteron stability, $τ_D\,>\,3.01^.10^{31}$ years. Calculation performed within the quantum field theory based diagram technique reproduces satisfactorily results of the potential approach previously developed. The dependence of the obtained restriction on the total spin of the annihilating $N\bar N$ system and the deuteron wave function modifications is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper derives a restriction on the neutron-antineutron oscillation time in vacuum from the SNO deuteron stability limit, but the final numerical bound depends on the unknown ratio of triplet to singlet antineutron-proton annihilation cross sections, yielding a range of possible lower limits rather than a single value.

## Method

The authors compute the deuteron decay width into mesons using a quantum field theory diagram technique, where the neutron-antineutron transition vertex is inserted into a Feynman diagram for deuteron breakup followed by antineutron-proton annihilation. The amplitude is evaluated in the nonrelativistic approximation for nucleons, with the annihilation amplitude taken out of the momentum integration at an average point, and the resulting decay width is expressed in terms of the neutron-antineutron transition parameter μ_nn̅, the deuteron binding parameters, and the low-energy antineutron-proton annihilation cross section.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron lifetime lower limit from SNO: τ_D > 3.01 × 10^31 years.
- Neutron-antineutron oscillation time lower limit for spin-independent case (r=1): τ_nn̅ > 1.8 × 10^8 sec.
- Neutron-antineutron oscillation time lower limit for r ≫ 1: τ_nn̅ > 2.1 × 10^8 sec.
- Neutron-antineutron oscillation time lower limit using r_pp̅ ≈ 0.42 from antiproton-proton data: τ_nn̅ > 1.55 × 10^8 sec.
- Suppression factor R_D for the deuteron (spin-independent): R_D ≈ 2.94 × 10^22 sec^{-1}.
- Deuteron binding energy: ε_D ≈ 2.23 MeV; corresponding κ = √(m_N ε_D) ≈ 46 MeV.
- Hulthen parameter α ≈ 270 MeV; using the Hulthen wave function reduces the τ_nn̅ estimate by about 4%.

## Context

This work extends the potential approach of Dover, Gal, and Richard (1980s) by using a quantum field theory diagram technique that reproduces their suppression factors (R_D between 2.4 × 10^22 and 2.75 × 10^22 sec^{-1}) and provides a framework for studying relativistic corrections. The derived restriction from SNO data is comparable to the best existing limit from oxygen stability (τ_nn̅ > 3.3 × 10^8 sec from earlier work), and the method enables future inclusion of spin-dependent annihilation amplitudes and more realistic deuteron wave functions.
