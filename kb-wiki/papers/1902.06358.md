# Comparing proton momentum distributions in $A=2$ and 3 nuclei via $^2$H $^3$H and $^3$He $(e, e'p)$ measurements

> We report the first measurement of the $(e,e'p)$ reaction cross-section ratios for Helium-3 ($^3$He), Tritium ($^3$H), and Deuterium ($d$). The measurement covered a missing momentum range of $40 \le p_{miss} \le 550$ MeV$/c$, at large momentum transfer ($\langle Q^2 \rangle \approx 1.9$ (GeV$/c$)$^2$) and $x_B>1$, which minimized contributions from non quasi-elastic (QE) reaction mechanisms. The data is compared with plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculations using realistic spectral functions and momentum distributions. The measured and PWIA-calculated cross-section ratios for $^3$He$/d$ and $^3$H$/d$ extend to just above the typical nucleon Fermi-momentum ($k_F \approx 250$ MeV$/c$) and differ from each other by $\sim 20\%$, while for $^3$He/$^3$H they agree within the measurement accuracy of about 3\%. At momenta above $k_F$, the measured $^3$He/$^3$H ratios differ from the calculation by $20\% - 50\%$. Final state interaction (FSI) calculations using the generalized Eikonal Approximation indicate that FSI should change the $^3$He/$^3$H cross-section ratio for this measurement by less than 5\%. If these calculations are correct, then the differences at large missing momenta between the $^3$He/$^3$H experimental and calculated ratios could be due to the underlying $NN$ interaction, and thus could provide new constraints on the previously loosely-constrained short-distance parts of the $NN$ interaction.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first measurement of $(e,e'p)$ reaction cross-section ratios for $^3$He, $^3$H, and deuterium, finding that at missing momenta above the Fermi momentum ($k_F \approx 250$ MeV/$c$), the measured $^3$He/$^3$H ratio differs from plane-wave impulse approximation calculations by 20% to 50%, which could be due to the underlying nucleon-nucleon interaction if final state interaction calculations (showing less than 5% effect) are correct.

## Method

The experiment used a 4.326 GeV electron beam incident on gas targets of hydrogen, deuterium, $^3$He, and $^3$H in Jefferson Lab Hall A, detecting scattered electrons and knocked-out protons in the two high-resolution spectrometers at kinematics with $\langle Q^2 \rangle \approx 1.9$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$ and $x_B > 1$. The measurement covered a missing momentum range of $40 \le p_{miss} \le 550$ MeV/$c$ across two kinematic settings, with the angle between the recoil momentum and momentum transfer constrained to $\theta_{rq} < 37.5^\circ$ to minimize final state interactions. Cross-section ratios were extracted from normalized event yields corrected for radiative effects, bin migration, and finite missing energy acceptance using the SIMC Monte Carlo simulation with spectral functions calculated using the AV18 potential.

## Key numbers

* Missing momentum range covered: $40 \le p_{miss} \le 550$ MeV/$c$
* Average four-momentum transfer: $\langle Q^2 \rangle \approx 1.9$ (GeV/$c$)$^2$
* Bjorken scaling variable: $x_B = 1.4$ at central kinematics
* Measured $^3$He/$d$ and $^3$H/$d$ cross-section ratios differ from each other by $\sim 20\%$ at missing momenta up to about 270 MeV/$c$
* Measured $^3$He/$^3$H ratio agrees with PWIA calculations within about 3% for $p_{miss} \lesssim 250$ MeV/$c$
* At $p_{miss} > 250$ MeV/$c$, measured $^3$He/$^3$H ratio differs from calculation by $20\% - 50\%$
* Final state interaction calculations using generalized Eikonal Approximation change the $^3$He/$^3$H cross-section ratio by less than 5%
* Overall normalization uncertainty: $1.8\%$
* Point-to-point systematic uncertainties: $1\%$ to $8\%$
* Target areal densities: hydrogen $70.8 \pm 0.4$ mg/cm$^2$, deuterium $142.2 \pm 0.8$ mg/cm$^2$, $^3$He $53.4 \pm 0.6$ mg/cm$^2$, $^3$H $85.1 \pm 0.8$ mg/cm$^2$
* $2.78 \pm 0.18\%$ of target $^3$H nuclei decayed to $^3$He in six months since target filling

## Context

This work extends previous $(e,e'p)$ measurements on $^3$He by Benmokhtar (2004) and Rvachev (2004) that showed discrepancies up to a factor of five with PWIA calculations at high missing momenta, by using cross-section ratios of same-mass nuclei ($^3$He and $^3$H) to cancel final state interaction effects. The results enable new constraints on the short-distance parts of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, which have been loosely constrained by previous data, and can inform calculations of nucleon-nucleon short-range correlations in nuclei and neutron star structure.
