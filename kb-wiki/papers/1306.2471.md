# Connecting QGP-Heavy Ion Physics to the Early Universe

> We discuss properties and evolution of quark-gluon plasma in the early Universe and compare to laboratory heavy ion experiments. We describe how matter and antimatter emerged from a primordial soup of quarks and gluons. We focus our discussion on similarities and differences between the early Universe and the laboratory experiments.

## Key claim
The paper argues that while the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions shares key features with the QGP of the early Universe, the laboratory recreation is not exact; significant differences in size, baryon content, and expansion dynamics mean that certain big-bang phenomena, such as slow distillation of baryon number during the QGP-to-hadron transition, are not easily reproduced in the micro-bang.

## Method
The author compares the entropy-preserving expansion of QGP in the early Universe, governed by the Friedmann-Robertson-Walker-Lemaitre (FRWL) Hubble equation, with the relativistic hydrodynamic flow used to model QGP droplets in heavy-ion collisions. Analytical solutions for the energy density evolution are derived using a bag-model equation of state, and the entropy-per-baryon ratio is computed for both the Universe (from observed baryon-to-photon ratio) and for LHC heavy-ion collisions. The chemical potential evolution and the possibility of baryon-number distillation during a mixed QGP-hadron gas phase are discussed.

## Key numbers
- The QGP persisted in the early Universe down to a temperature \(T \simeq 150\) MeV.
- The entropy-per-baryon ratio in the early Universe is \(s/n_B \simeq 3 \times 10^{10}\), derived from the baryon-to-photon ratio \(\eta = 6.27 \pm 0.34 \times 10^{-10}\).
- The entropy-per-baryon ratio in LHC heavy-ion collisions is \(s/n_B|_{\rm lab} \simeq 5000\), meaning the laboratory QGP is about 6 orders of magnitude richer in baryons.
- The time constant for the quark Universe expansion is \(\tau_u^q = 36\ \mu\mathrm{s}\) for a bag constant \({\cal B}_0^{1/4} = 195\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (or \({\cal B}_0 = 0.19\ \mathrm{GeV/fm}^3\)).
- The entropy density at hadronization is \(s_h \simeq 5\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The redshift factor at hadronization is \(z_h = 0.9 \times 10^{12}\).
- The net baryon density at hadronization is \(n_B^h \simeq 1.67 \times 10^{-10}\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\).
- The radius of the Universe at hadronization is \(R_u \simeq 0.5 \times 10^{30}\ \mathrm{fm}\), corresponding to a time scale \(\tau_u = 1.5 \times 10^{6}\ \mathrm{s}\).
- The baryon chemical potential just before the QGP-hadron phase transition is \(\mu_B = 0.33^{+0.11}_{-0.08}\ \mathrm{eV}\).

## Context
This work extends the long-standing program, initiated in the late 1970s and documented in conference proceedings such as Bielefeld 1980 (Satz, 1981) and Rencontres de Moriond 1981 (Jacob, 1982), to connect QGP physics from heavy-ion collisions to the early Universe. It builds on quantitative studies of hadronization (Letessier and Rafelski, 2002; Fromerth et al., 2002, 2012) and recent LHC data analysis (Petran et al., 2013). The paper identifies open questions, such as the need for a realistic dynamical study of baryon-number distillation during the QGP-hadron transition in the early Universe, and the challenge of observing such distillation in the much smaller and baryon-richer laboratory micro-bang.
