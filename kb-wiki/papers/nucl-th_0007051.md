# Nucleon-nucleon interaction in the Skyrme model

> We consider the interaction of two skyrmions in the framework of the sudden approximation. The widely used product ansatz is investigated. Its failure in reproducing an attractive central potential is associated with terms that violate G-parity. We discuss the construction of alternative ansätze and identify a plausible solution to the problem.

## Key claim

The widely used product ansatz (PA) for two-skyrmion interactions fails to produce an attractive central nucleon-nucleon potential because it contains a term with wrong G-parity in the composite pion field; the symmetrized ansatz (SA) that removes this term can yield asymptotic attraction, though the precise amount depends on the quantization prescription for the normalization function \(\eta\).

## Method

The authors constructed \(B=2\) skyrmion configurations using the sudden approximation, starting from the product ansatz and then proposing a symmetrized ansatz (SA) where the composite pion field is \(\mathbf{P} = (s_{\mathbf{x}} \boldsymbol{\pi}_{\mathbf{y}} + s_{\mathbf{y}} \boldsymbol{\pi}_{\mathbf{x}})/f_\pi\) and the scalar function \(S\) is taken from the product ansatz, with unitarity enforced by a normalization factor \(\eta\). They computed the central potential \(V_C(d)\) by integrating the interaction Lagrangian density over space, using both a classical \(\eta_c\) and a quantized \(\eta_q\) (truncated at first order in the rotation matrices \(D\)), and compared the asymptotic forms to the Argonne potential and chiral perturbation theory results.

## Key numbers

* The product ansatz yields a net asymptotic central potential coefficient \(K = -1.23\) MeV (from \(\mathcal{L}_\sigma + \mathcal{L}_4\)), compared to the Argonne potential value \(K = 4.80\) MeV; the \(\mathcal{L}_\sigma\) contribution alone is attractive (\(K = 7.29\) MeV) but is overwhelmed by the repulsive \(\mathcal{L}_4\) contribution (\(K = -8.52\) MeV).
* The symmetrized ansatz with classical \(\eta_c\) gives \(K_c = 14\) MeV for the asymptotic central potential; with quantized \(\eta_q\) it gives \(K_q = 57\) MeV.
* Empirical values of \(K\) from subthreshold coefficients range from 21.6 MeV to 28.5 MeV across four determinations (Höhler 1983, Koch 1986, and two from the Karlsruhe-Helsinki group).
* The critical distance at which the product ansatz profile function develops a discontinuity is \(d_{\text{crit}} = 0.86\) fm.
* The pion decay constant used is \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, the pion mass \(m_\pi = 139\) MeV, and the Skyrme constant \(e = 4.0\).

## Context

This work extends the Skyrme model approach to nucleon-nucleon interactions initiated by Skyrme (1960s) and revived by Witten (1980s), directly addressing the failure of the product ansatz calculations by Jackson et al. (1985) and Vinh Mau et al. (1985) to produce central attraction. It builds on the symmetrized product ansatz of Nyman and Riska (1988), which had shown some attraction but was criticized by Sternheim and Kälbermann (1989) for baryon number violation. The results enable a more systematic study of how G-parity constraints and quantization procedures affect the central potential in topological soliton models, and suggest that further refinement of the quantization of the normalization function \(\eta\) could yield quantitatively accurate predictions.
