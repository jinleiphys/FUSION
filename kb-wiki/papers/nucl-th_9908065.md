# Baryons and Nuclei in the Large $N_c$ Limit

> The relation between the Skyrme model and the constituent quark model, which appears in the large $N_c$ limit is described. Examples of similarity in the predicted phenomenology for baryons are shown. Finally the application to nuclei is discussed.

# Baryons and Nuclei in the Large $N_c$ Limit

## Key claim
The large $N_c$ limit of QCD provides an equivalence between the Skyrme topological soliton model and the constituent quark model for baryons, but this equivalence is a limiting relation that holds only as $N_c \rightarrow \infty$ and requires systematic $1/N_c$ expansions to connect to phenomenology.

## Method
The paper derives the operator algebra of baryons in the large $N_c$ limit, showing that the spin-isospin operators satisfy a contracted SU(4) algebra that appears in both the Skyrme model and the constituent quark model. It then applies $1/N_c$ expansions to baryon mass operators and compares the resulting predictions for baryon spectra, heavy flavor hyperons, pentaquarks, and nuclear interactions from the Skyrme model product ansatz.

## Key numbers
- Baryon mass scales as $N_c \Lambda_{QCD}$ in the large $N_c$ limit, where $\Lambda_{QCD}$ is the QCD scale factor
- Baryon radius remains proportional to $1/\Lambda_{QCD}$ and $N_c$ independent
- Meson-baryon coupling constants are proportional to $\sqrt{N_c}$
- Pion decay constant $f_\pi$ scales as $\sim \sqrt{N_c}$
- The axial vector of a baryon scales as $N_c$
- The baryon mass operator expansion: $M = m_0 N_c + m_2 (1/N_c^2) \vec J^2 + m_3 (1/N_c^3) \vec J^4 + ...$
- The Skyrme model mass formula: $M = M_s + (1/2\Omega_s) \vec J^2$
- The sharp resonance propagator in the three-nucleon interaction appears as $-i(m_\Delta - m_N)$

## Context
This paper reviews and extends the connection between the Skyrme model and the constituent quark model, building on foundational work by 't Hooft (1974), Witten (1979), and Jenkins and Manohar (1991) on large $N_c$ QCD. It discusses how the operator algebra developed by Manohar (1998) and the contracted SU(4) algebra of the Skyrme model (Adkins, Nappi, Witten, 1983) yield equivalent results for baryon phenomenology. The paper enables further work on rational map approximations for multi-baryon systems (Houghton, Manton, Sutcliffe, 1998) and applications to pentaquark predictions and nuclear interactions.
