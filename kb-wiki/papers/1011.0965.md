# Ridge Formation Induced by Jets in $pp$ Collisions at 7 TeV

> An interpretation of the ridge phenomenon found in pp collisions at 7 TeV is given in terms of enhancement of soft partons due to energy loss of semihard jets. A description of ridge formation in nuclear collisions can directly be extended to pp collisions, since hydrodynamics is not used, and azimuthal anisotropy is generated by semihard scattering. Both the p_T and multiplicity dependencies are well reproduced. Some suggestions are made about other observables.

# Ridge Formation Induced by Jets in $pp$ Collisions at 7 TeV

## Key claim

The ridge phenomenon observed in $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV can be explained by transverse correlation between soft and semihard partons without invoking hydrodynamics or long-range longitudinal correlation, provided that the ridge yield is described by a factorizable form $R(1)R(2)$ where each soft particle responds independently to an unobserved semihard jet.

## Method

The authors extend their previous model of ridge formation in nuclear collisions to $pp$ collisions by assuming that semihard jets (with parton momentum $k_T$ in the 5–10 GeV/c range) enhance the transverse momentum distribution of soft partons from $\exp(-k_T/T)$ to $\exp(-k_T/T')$ with $T' > T$, independent of pseudorapidity. The two-particle correlation is taken as the factorized product of two single-particle ridge responses $R(p_T)$, where $R(p_T) = R_0(e^{-E_T/T'} - e^{-E_T/T})$. The ridge yield $Y_R(p_T,N)$ is then computed using the CMS definition involving integration over $\Delta\phi$ and $|\Delta\eta|$ from 2.0 to 4.8, with the single-particle distribution parametrized by a Tsallis fit to CMS data.

## Key numbers

- The inverse slope of the soft parton distribution in the absence of semihard scattering is $T = 0.294$ GeV.
- The logarithmic growth parameter relating $\Delta T/T$ to multiplicity is $\beta = 0.0175$, where $\Delta T/T = \beta \ln N$.
- The Tsallis fit to the CMS single-particle distribution uses $T_0 = 0.145$ GeV and $n = 6.6$, giving $\langle p_T \rangle = 0.545$ GeV/c.
- At $N=100$, $\Delta T/T$ is about 8%, slightly lower than in nuclear collisions at RHIC where $T=355\pm 6$ MeV and $T'=416\pm 22$ MeV for $4 < p_T^{\rm trig} < 6$ GeV/c.
- The ridge yield $Y_R$ jumps by nearly an order of magnitude between the $0.1 < p_T < 1$ GeV/c bin and the $1 < p_T < 2$ GeV/c bin.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous interpretation of ridge phenomena in Au-Au collisions at RHIC (Hwa and Yang, 2008; Chiu and Hwa, 2005) to $pp$ collisions at LHC energies, contrasting with models that invoke long-range longitudinal correlation from flux tubes (Gavin et al., 2009; Dumitru et al., 2008) or hydrodynamics. The approach reproduces the CMS ridge yield data (CMS Collaboration, 2010) across all $p_T$ and multiplicity bins using only two parameters, and suggests that a dense medium of soft partons may be created even in $pp$ collisions. The authors predict that the $p/\pi$ ratio in the ridge should increase with $p_T$ at low $p_T$ and exceed 0.2, which would distinguish recombination from fragmentation and motivate further experimental measurements of azimuthal anisotropy in small systems.
