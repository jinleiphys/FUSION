# Low-energy pions in nuclear matter and 2pi photoproduction within a BUU transport model

> A description of low-energy scattering of pions and nuclei within a BUU transport model is presented. Implementing different scenarios of medium modifications, the mean free path of pions in nuclear matter at low momenta and pion absorption reactions on nuclei have been studied and compared to data and to results obtained via quantum mechanical scattering theory. We show that even in a regime of a long pionic wave length the semi-classical transport model is still a reliable framework for pion kinetic energies greater than ~20-30 MeV. Results are presented on pion-absorption cross sections in the regime of 10 MeV < E(kin) < 130 MeV and on photon-induced double-pion production at incident beam energies of 400-500 MeV.

## Key claim

The semi-classical BUU transport model is a reliable framework for describing low-energy pions in nuclear matter for kinetic energies greater than approximately 20-30 MeV, even in the regime of long pionic wavelength, but the link from ππ photoproduction experiments to underlying effects like a possible softening of the in-medium ππ interaction is considerably complicated by the necessary presence of pion final state interactions.

## Method

The authors implemented a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model (GiBUU) to simulate low-energy pion propagation in nuclear matter, incorporating medium modifications including hadronic potentials for nucleons, Δ resonances, and pions, as well as density-dependent modifications of decay widths and Coulomb corrections. They extracted the pion mean free path and total width by performing Monte-Carlo simulations with pions and nucleons initialized in a box with continuous boundary conditions, and compared the results to experimental data on pion absorption cross sections and to quantum mechanical optical model calculations.

## Key numbers

- The mean free path and total width of pions in nuclear matter were studied at normal nuclear matter density ρ₀ = 0.168 fm⁻³.
- Pion absorption cross sections were presented in the kinetic energy regime of 10 MeV ≤ T_kin ≤ 130 MeV.
- Photon-induced double-pion production was studied at incident beam energies of 400-500 MeV, with new calculations also for 400 MeV ≤ E_γ ≤ 460 MeV.
- The hadronic potential for the pion at zero momentum in symmetric nuclear matter at normal density is about 18 MeV according to the Nieves et al. model, while experiments at GSI and MAMI hint at a value of about A⁰_π(p=0) ≈ 25 MeV.
- The Δ resonance potential depth is about -30 MeV at ρ₀, taken as 2/3 of the nucleon potential depth (approximately -50 MeV).
- The model is applicable for pion kinetic energies greater than approximately 20-30 MeV; below 20 MeV the semi-classical treatment becomes problematic.

## Context

This work extends earlier transport model studies by Salcedo et al. and Engel et al., which investigated pions with kinetic energies in the regime of 85-300 MeV, to even lower energies relevant for analyzing ππ photoproduction experiments by the TAPS collaboration and pion-induced experiments by the CHAOS collaboration. The results enable improved analysis of final-state effects in experiments searching for in-medium modification of the σ meson (f₀(600)) as a signal of partial chiral symmetry restoration, where the order parameter ⟨q̄q⟩ is expected to decrease by about 30% at normal nuclear matter density.
