# Semileptonic decays of $D_{(s)}$ mesons

> A symmetry-preserving continuum approach to meson bound-states in quantum field theory, employed elsewhere to describe numerous $π$- and $K$-meson electroweak processes, is used to analyse leptonic and semileptonic decays of $D_{(s)}$ mesons. Each semileptonic transition is conventionally characterised by the value of the dominant form factor at $t=0$ and the following results are obtained herein: $f_+^{D_s\to K}(0) = 0.673(40)$; $f_+^{D\to π}(0)=0.618(31)$; and $f_+^{D\to K}(0)=0.756(36)$. Working with the computed $t$-dependence of these form factors and standard averaged values for $|V_{cd}|$, $|V_{cs}|$, one arrives at the following predictions for the associated branching fractions: ${\cal B}_{D_s^+\to K^0 e^+ ν_e} = 3.31(33)\times 10^{-3}$; ${\cal B}_{D^0\to π^- e^+ ν_e} = 2.73(22)\times 10^{-3}$; and ${\cal B}_{D^0\to K^- e^+ ν_e} = 3.83(28)$%. Alternatively, using the calculated $t$-dependence, agreement with contemporary empirical results for these branching fractions requires $|V_{cd}|=0.221(9)$, $|V_{us}|=0.953(34)$. With all $D_{(s)}$ transition form factors in hand, the nature of SU$(3)$-flavour symmetry-breaking in this array of processes can be analysed; and just as in the $π$-$K$ sector, the magnitude of such effects is found to be determined by the scales associated with emergent mass generation in the Standard Model, not those originating with the Higgs mechanism.

## Key claim

The paper uses a symmetry-preserving continuum approach (rainbow-ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter equations) to compute semileptonic transition form factors for D and D_s mesons, finding that SU(3)-flavour symmetry-breaking effects are governed by emergent hadronic mass scales rather than Higgs-mechanism mass scales, with the caveat that the direct computational approach fails when the heavy-quark current-mass exceeds 2.7 times the s-quark mass, requiring extrapolation via the Schlessinger point method.

## Method

The authors computed dressed-quark propagators, Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and weak transition vertices at leading order in rainbow-ladder truncation of the continuum bound-state equations. They directly calculated transition form factors for fictitious pseudoscalar mesons with varying heavy-quark current masses up to 2.7 times the s-quark mass, then used the Schlessinger point method (a statistical Padé-based extrapolation technique) to determine the c-quark mass and predict form factors at the physical D and D_s masses. The form factors were parametrized using vector-meson and scalar-meson pole forms, with coefficients obtained from the SPM extrapolations.

## Key numbers

*   $f_+^{D_s\to K}(0) = 0.673(40)$; $f_+^{D\to \pi}(0) = 0.618(31)$; $f_+^{D\to K}(0) = 0.756(36)$ (dominant form factors at maximum recoil, t=0)
*   Predicted branching fractions: ${\cal B}_{D_s^+\to K^0 e^+ \nu_e} = 3.31(33)\times 10^{-3}$; ${\cal B}_{D^0\to \pi^- e^+ \nu_e} = 2.73(22)\times 10^{-3}$; ${\cal B}_{D^0\to K^- e^+ \nu_e} = 3.83(28)\%$
*   Extracted CKM matrix elements from comparison with experiment: $|V_{cd}| = 0.221(9)$; $|V_{us}| = 0.953(34)$
*   Determined renormalization-point-invariant c-quark mass: $\hat m_c = 1.93$ GeV (one-loop evolved to $\zeta_2=2$ GeV gives $m_c^{\zeta_2}=1.34$ GeV)
*   Ratio of leptonic decay constants: $f_{D_s}/f_D = 1.25(7)$; ratio of semileptonic form factors: $f_+^{D_d^s}(0)/f_+^{D_s^d}(0) = 1.12(09)$

## Context

This work extends the same continuum Schwinger function method previously applied to pi and K meson electroweak processes (Ji 2001, Chen 2012) to the D and D_s meson sector, providing a unified description across light and heavy pseudoscalar mesons. The results complement lattice QCD computations (Lubicz 2017) for D to pi and D to K transitions, and provide the first continuum predictions for D_s to K form factors where no lattice results are yet available. The analysis enables tighter constraints on CKM matrix elements |V_cd| and |V_cs| from BESIII, BaBar, Belle, and CLEO data, and demonstrates that SU(3)-flavour symmetry breaking in heavy-light systems is driven by emergent mass generation rather than Higgs-related current-quark masses, consistent with findings in the pi-K sector.
