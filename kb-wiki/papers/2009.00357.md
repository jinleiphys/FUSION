# Light clusters in dilute heavy-baryon admixed nuclear matter

> We study the composition of nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities, non-zero temperatures, and isospin asymmetry, under the conditions characteristic of binary neutron star mergers, stellar collapse, and low-energy heavy-ion collisions. The composition includes light clusters with mass number $A\le 4$, a heavy nucleus ($\isotope[56]{Fe}$), the $Δ$-resonances, the isotriplet of pions, as well as the $Λ$ hyperon. The nucleonic mean-fields are computed from a zero-range density functional, whereas the pion-nucleon interactions are treated to leading order in chiral perturbation theory. We show that with increasing temperature and/or density the composition of matter shifts from light-cluster to heavy baryon dominated one, the transition taking place nearly independent of the magnitude of the isospin. Our findings highlight the importance of simultaneous treatment of light clusters and heavy baryons in the astrophysical and heavy-ion physics contexts.

# Light clusters in dilute heavy-baryon admixed nuclear matter

## Key claim
The composition of warm dilute nuclear matter shifts from light-cluster dominated to heavy-baryon dominated with increasing temperature and/or density, and this transition occurs nearly independently of the magnitude of isospin asymmetry. However, the nucleons remain the dominant component at all temperatures in the parameter range studied, and the heavy nucleus Fe-56 does not play a significant role at temperatures above 10 MeV.

## Method
The composition of nuclear matter at sub-saturation densities, non-zero temperatures, and isospin asymmetry was computed using a quasiparticle gas model extended to include light clusters (A ≤ 4), Fe-56, Λ hyperons, Δ-resonances, and the isotriplet of pions. Nucleonic mean-fields were computed from a zero-range Skyrme density functional, pion-nucleon interactions were treated to leading order in chiral perturbation theory, and Pauli-blocking effects on cluster binding energies were included via fits to in-medium Bethe-Salpeter and Faddeev equation solutions.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- At fixed density n_B/n₀ = 10⁻² and charge fraction Y_Q = 0.1 or 0.4, nucleons are the dominant component at all temperatures, but for T ≥ 30 MeV the dominant mass fraction shifts to heavy baryons
- The Λ hyperon fraction exceeds 10⁻⁴ at density n_B/n₀ = 10⁻² for T ≥ 20 MeV in this model
- At T = 10 MeV, clusters abruptly disappear due to Pauli blocking when n_B/n₀ ≥ 0.1 (approximately 9 × 10⁻²)
- The critical inverse temperature for cluster dissolution is given by β_cr [MeV⁻¹] = 0.07835 + 0.00185 × (n₀/n_B)
- Pressure was computed for temperatures T = 20, 30, 40, and 50 MeV at charge fractions Y_Q = 0.1 and 0.4

## Context
This work extends the quasiparticle gas model approach of Wu (2017) to include heavy baryons and pions in the composition and equation of state of isospin-asymmetrical nuclear matter. It builds on extensive prior work using nuclear statistical equilibrium methods (e.g., Hempel 2010, Typel 2010, Raduta 2014) and virial expansions. The inclusion of hyperons and Δ-resonances connects to studies of dense matter in neutron stars (e.g., Li 2019, Raduta 2020) and finite-temperature hyperon abundances in supernova matter (Fortin 2018, Menezes 2017). The results enable improved transport coefficient calculations for binary neutron star mergers, stellar collapse, and heavy-ion collisions by providing a composition that simultaneously accounts for clustering and heavy baryon degrees of freedom.
