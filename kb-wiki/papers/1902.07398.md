# Impact of form factor uncertainties on interpretations of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering data

> The standard model coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CE$ν$NS) cross section is subject to nuclear form factor uncertainties, mainly driven by the root-mean-square radius of the neutron density distribution. Motivated by COHERENT phases I-III and future multi-ton direct detection dark matter searches, we evaluate these uncertainties in cesium iodide, germanium, xenon and argon detectors. We find that the uncertainties become relevant for momentum transfers $q\gtrsim 20$ MeV and are essentially independent of the form factor parameterization. Consequently, form factor uncertainties are not important for CE$ν$NS induced by reactor or solar neutrinos. Taking into account these uncertainties, we then evaluate their impact on measurements of CE$ν$NS at COHERENT, the diffuse supernova background (DSNB) neutrinos and sub-GeV atmospheric neutrinos. We also calculate the relative uncertainties in the number of COHERENT events for different nuclei as a function of recoil energy. For DSNB and atmospheric neutrinos, event rates at a liquid argon detector can be uncertain to more than 5%. Finally, we consider the impact of form factor uncertainties on searches for nonstandard neutrino interactions, sterile neutrinos and neutrino generalized interactions. We point out that studies of new physics using CE$ν$NS data are affected by neutron form factor uncertainties, which if not properly taken into account may lead to the misidentification of new physics signals. The uncertainties quantified here are also relevant for dark matter direct detection searches.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that neutron form factor uncertainties, driven by the poorly known root-mean-square radius of the neutron density distribution, become relevant for coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEvNS) at momentum transfers above about 20 MeV, but are essentially independent of the choice of form factor parameterization; consequently these uncertainties do not affect CEvNS from reactor or solar neutrinos but can impact interpretations of COHERENT data, diffuse supernova background (DSNB) neutrinos, and sub-GeV atmospheric neutrinos, potentially leading to misidentification of new physics signals if not properly accounted for.

## Method

The authors evaluated neutron form factor uncertainties for cesium iodide, germanium, xenon, and argon nuclei using three parameterizations: the Helm form factor, the Fourier transform of the symmeterized Fermi distribution, and the Klein-Nystrand form factor. They fixed the proton root-mean-square radius to experimental central values from Angeli (2013) and varied the neutron root-mean-square radius between the proton radius and an upper limit set by the neutron skin of lead-208 (0.3 fm for heavy nuclei, 0.1-0.2 fm for argon), then calculated the resulting uncertainties in CEvNS event rates for COHERENT phases I-III and for a future multi-ton liquid argon detector exposed to DSNB and atmospheric neutrino fluxes.

## Key numbers

* For cesium-133, the percentage uncertainty in the squared Helm form factor reaches about 5% at momentum transfer around 65 MeV; for argon-40 the uncertainty reaches about 4.5%
* For COHERENT phase-I (cesium iodide detector, 14.6 kg, 308.1 days of data), form factor uncertainties are about 4% at 7 photoelectrons (5.98 keV recoil energy) and increase to about 9% at 15 photoelectrons
* For DSNB and sub-GeV atmospheric neutrinos in a liquid argon detector with 1000 ton-year exposure, the relative uncertainty in the lowest energy bin is 5% and increases for larger recoil energies
* The proton root-mean-square radius for argon-40 is 3.427 fm; for cesium-133 it is 4.804 fm; for iodine-127 it is 4.750 fm
* The neutron skin of lead-208 is measured by PREX to be 0.33 +0.16 -0.18 fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear form factor uncertainties in CEvNS beyond the standard assumption of equal proton and neutron form factors used by the COHERENT collaboration (Akimov 2017). The authors build on prior measurements of neutron skins from PREX (Horowitz 2012, 2013) and theoretical calculations using relativistic mean field models (Cadeddu 2017). The quantified uncertainties are directly applicable to COHERENT phases I-III, future multi-ton dark matter detectors like Argo (Aalseth 2017), and searches for nonstandard neutrino interactions, sterile neutrinos, and neutrino generalized interactions, where they may prevent misidentification of nuclear physics effects as new physics signals.
