# A theoretical case for negative mass-square for sub-eV particles

> Electroweak gauge bosons have masses of the order of 10^2 GeV, while masses of additional bosons involved in gravito-electroweak unification are expected to be still higher. These are at least eleven orders of magnitude higher than sub-eV range indications for neutrino masses. Under these circumstances we suspect that the sub-eV particles are created in a spacetime where gravitational effects of massive gauge bosons may become important. The question that we thus ask is: What is the spacetime group around a gravito-electroweak vertex? Modeling it as de-Sitter we find that sub-eV particles may carry a negative mass square of the order of - (3/8 π^3) (M_unif./M_Planck)^4 M_Planck^2. Neutrino oscillation data then hints at 30-75 TeV scale for M_unif., where M_unif. characterizes gravito-electroweak unification scale.

## Key claim

Sub-eV particles (such as neutrinos) created in a de-Sitter spacetime region around a gravito-electroweak vertex may carry a negative mass-square, rather than the positive definite mass assumed in standard Poincaré-invariant particle physics. The negative mass-square arises because the notion of mass is modified when transitioning from Poincaré to de Sitter spacetime symmetry, and the magnitude depends on the gravito-electroweak unification scale.

## Method

The authors model the spacetime around a gravito-electroweak vertex as de Sitter-Schwarzschild geometry, imposing spherical symmetry, the dominant energy condition, regularity of density, and finiteness of mass. They then evaluate the first de Sitter Casimir invariant \(I_1\) for the \((1/2,0)\) and \((0,1/2)\) representation spaces (right-handed and left-handed fields) in the approximation \(r^2 - c^2 t^2 \ll r_0^2\), where \(r_0\) is the de Sitter radius determined by the central density \(\rho_0\). The eigenvalue of \(I_1\) yields a modified mass-square that includes a negative term proportional to \(\hbar^2 / r_0^2\).

## Key numbers

- The negative mass-square for sub-eV particles is given by \(m_{\mathrm{neg.}}^2 = - (3/8\pi^3) (M_{\mathrm{unif.}}/M_{\mathrm{Planck}})^4 M_{\mathrm{Planck}}^2\), where \(M_{\mathrm{Planck}}\) is the Planck mass.
- Setting \(M_{\mathrm{unif.}} = 100\) GeV (electroweak scale) gives \(m_{\mathrm{neg.}}^2 \simeq -8.4 \times 10^{-15}\) eV\(^2\), which is ruled out by neutrino oscillation data.
- Atmospheric neutrino data (\(\Delta m^2_{\mathrm{ATM}} = 2.5 \times 10^{-3}\) eV\(^2\)) implies \(M_{\mathrm{unif.}} \simeq 74\) TeV and \(r_0 \simeq 5 \times 10^{-4}\) cm.
- Solar neutrino data (\(\Delta m^2_{\mathrm{SOL}} = 6.9 \times 10^{-5}\) eV\(^2\)) implies \(M_{\mathrm{unif.}} \simeq 30\) TeV and \(r_0 \simeq 3 \times 10^{-3}\) cm.
- The electron neutrino mass-square from Lobashev is \(m_{\nu_e}^2 = -2.5 \pm 2.5 \pm 2.0\) eV\(^2\); the Particle Data Group gives \(m_{\nu_e}^2 = -2.5 \pm 3.3\) eV\(^2\).
- For muon neutrinos, two solutions exist: Solution A gives \(m_{\nu_\mu}^2 = -0.143 \pm 0.024\) MeV\(^2\); Solution B gives \(m_{\nu_\mu}^2 = -0.016 \pm 0.023\) MeV\(^2\).

## Context

This paper extends the idea that the notion of mass originates from spacetime symmetry groups, as established by Wigner (1939), and applies it to the de Sitter group in the context of gravito-electroweak unification. It builds on earlier work by Dymnikova (2002, 2003) on de Sitter-Schwarzschild geometry and by Ahluwalia-Khalilova and collaborators (2001, 2002) on representation spaces of the type \((j,0)\oplus(0,j)\). The results offer a theoretical explanation for the experimentally observed "negative mass squared problem" for electron and muon neutrinos, which has been reported by Lobashev (1999), Weinheimer (1999), Stoeffl, and Robertson, among others. The paper suggests that future experiments and global data analyses should allow for the possibility of negative mass-square rather than imposing \(m_\nu^2 > 0\) by hand.
