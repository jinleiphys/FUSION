# Shear Viscosity in Weakly Coupled N-Component Scalar Field Theories

> The rich phenomena of the shear viscosity (eta) to entropy density (s) ratio, eta/s, in weakly coupled N-component scalar field theories are studied. eta/s can have a "double dip" behavior due to resonances and the phase transition. If an explicit goldstone mass term is added, then eta/s can either decrease monotonically in temperature or, as seen in many other systems, reach a minimum at the phase transition. We also show how to go beyond the original variational approach to make the Boltzmann equation computation of eta systematic.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) in weakly coupled N-component scalar field theories can exhibit a "double dip" behavior due to resonances and the phase transition, or can decrease monotonically in temperature when an explicit goldstone mass term is added, contrary to the behavior seen in one-component theories and many other systems where η/s reaches a minimum at the phase transition.

## Method

The authors study a modified O(N) scalar field theory with a quartic coupling scaled as b/N, taking the large N limit to control the computation. They compute the effective potential using the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism and solve the linearized Boltzmann equation for shear viscosity using a systematic polynomial expansion method that goes beyond the standard variational approach. The ππ scattering amplitude includes s-, t-, and u-channel contributions with a σ resonance, and the authors add an explicit pion mass term to avoid on-shell σ production and maintain perturbative control.

## Key numbers

- η/s scales as O(N b^{-2}) in the large N limit, with s = O(N)
- At high T, η/s has the expansion η/s = (k0 N / b^2) (1 + k1/√b * (a+m^2)/T^2 + ...), with k0 ≃ 571 + 80√b + O(b) and k1 ≃ 0.84 - 0.12√b + O(b)
- For the numerical convergence test using y=1.89, η_trial increases by ~0.4% from r_max=0 to 1, ~0.03% from 1 to 2, and ~0.003% from 2 to 3 for T=30-300 (arbitrary units)
- The ratio l_sc/l_mfp ~ 1/N^2 in the large N case despite the σ resonance, ensuring Boltzmann equation applicability

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of η/s in one-component weakly interacting scalar field theories (Chen 2007) to N-component theories, and builds on prior work in the symmetric phase (Aarts 2004, Moore 2007) and linear sigma models with QCD-like parameters (Dobado 2009, Chakraborty 2010). The results show that the previously speculated universal behavior of η/s reaching a minimum at the phase transition is not universal, as the N-component theory can produce monotonic decrease or double-dip structures. The systematic variational method developed here can be applied to bulk viscosity computations as well.
