# Open problems in nuclear structure near drip lines

> Physics related to weakly bound nuclei and low-density asymmetric nuclear matter is discussed from a theoretical point of view. Especially, we focus our discussion on new correlations in nuclei near the drip lines and on open issues of the density functional theory with a proper account of the continuum.

# Open problems in nuclear structure near drip lines

## Key claim
The authors argue that while the neutron drip line is practically unknown experimentally beyond oxygen (Z=8), theoretical models also fail to reproduce even the heaviest oxygen isotope, and that a small error in correlation energy calculations can shift the predicted drip line by several mass units. They present a personal view that the density functional theory framework, particularly with proper treatment of the continuum and pair correlations, is the most promising approach but still faces fundamental open problems.

## Method
The paper reviews theoretical challenges in describing nuclei near the neutron and proton drip lines, focusing on two main areas: novel many-body correlations expected in dilute nuclear matter (such as BCS-BEC crossover of neutron pairing, deuteron condensation, and alpha-particle condensation) and open issues in nuclear density functional theory including the need for pairing functionals valid from low density to saturation density. The authors discuss time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) methods and quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) techniques as necessary frameworks for describing weakly bound systems with continuum coupling.

## Key numbers
- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ ≈ 0.17 fm⁻³
- Typical neutron skin thickness: around 1 fm
- Neutron skin density: ρ/ρ₀ ∼ 10⁻¹
- Neutron halo density: ρ/ρ₀ ≲ 10⁻²
- Neutron-neutron scattering length in ¹S channel: a ≈ -20 fm
- Effective range of nuclear force: rₑ ≈ 3 fm
- Density range for BCS-BEC crossover in dilute neutron matter: 10⁻⁴ ≲ ρ/ρ₀ ≲ 10⁻²
- Binding energy per nucleon in stable nuclei: B/A ≈ 8 MeV
- Number of confirmed isotopes: about 3,000
- Neutron drip line established experimentally only up to oxygen (Z=8)

## Context
This paper extends discussions of weakly bound nuclei and low-density nuclear matter, building on prior work on neutron halo and skin phenomena (Ozawa 2001), BCS-BEC crossover in fermion systems (Leggett; Engelbrecht), and di-neutron correlations in halo nuclei (Bertsch 1991; Zhukov; Matsuo 2005). The authors identify open problems including the need for pairing functionals covering low to saturation density (Dobaczewski 2002; Margueron), the role of three-body interactions in determining the oxygen drip line (Otsuka 2009), and the development of QRPA methods with continuum for deformed drip-line nuclei. The work enables future large-scale calculations of nuclear structure from stable to drip-line nuclei and connects to neutron star crust physics and nucleosynthesis reaction rates.
