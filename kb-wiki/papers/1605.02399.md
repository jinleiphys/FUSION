# Impacts of the tensor couplings of $ω$ and $ρ$ mesons and Coulomb exchange terms on super-heavy nuclei and their relation to symmetry energy

> We have studied the effects of tensor coupling of $ω$ and $ρ$ meson terms, Coulomb exchange term in local density approximation and various isoscalar-isovector coupling terms of relativistic mean field model on the properties of nuclear matter, finite nuclei, and super-heavy nuclei. We found that for the same fixed value of symmetry energy $J$ or its slope $L$ the presence of tensor coupling of $ω$ and $ρ$ meson terms and Coulomb exchange term yields thicker neutron skin thickness of $^{208}$Pb. We also found that the roles of tensor coupling of $ω$ and $ρ$ meson terms, Coulomb exchange term in local density approximation and various isoscalar-isovector coupling terms on the bulk properties of finite nuclei varies depending on the corresponding nucleus mass. However, on average, tensor coupling terms play a significant role in predicting the bulk properties of finite nuclei in a quite wide mass range especially in binding energies. We also observed that for some particular nuclei, the corresponding experimental data of binding energies is rather less compatible with the presence of Coulomb exchange term in local density approximation and they tend to disfavor the presence of isoscalar-isovector coupling term with too high $Λ$ value. Furthermore, we have found that these terms influence the detail properties of $^{292}$120 super-heavy nucleus such as binding energies, the magnitude of two nucleon gaps, single particle spectra, neutron densities, neutron skin thicknesses and mean square charge radii. However, the shell closure predictions of $^{208}$Pb and $^{292}$120 nuclei is not affected by the presence of these terms.

## Key claim

The authors find that for the same fixed value of symmetry energy \(J\) or its slope \(L\), the inclusion of tensor couplings of \(\omega\) and \(\rho\) mesons and the Coulomb exchange term in local density approximation yields a thicker neutron skin thickness of \(^{208}\)Pb, but the shell closure predictions for \(^{208}\)Pb and the super-heavy nucleus \(^{292}\)120 are not affected by these terms.

## Method

The authors generated seven parameter sets within the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, systematically including or excluding tensor couplings of \(\omega\) and \(\rho\) mesons, the Coulomb exchange term in relativistic local density approximation (RLDA), and various isoscalar-isovector coupling terms (parameter \(\Lambda\)). The parameter sets were obtained by fitting to binding energies of 31 nuclei, rms radii of 21 nuclei, diffraction radii of 18 nuclei, and surface thicknesses of 16 nuclei, using the same parametrization protocol. The effects of these terms were then studied on nuclear matter properties, finite nuclei bulk properties across a wide mass range, and the super-heavy nucleus \(^{292}\)120.

## Key numbers

*   Symmetry energy \(J\) ranges from 43.4 MeV (P0 set) down to 28.1 MeV (PTE06 set), with the slope \(L\) ranging from 130.0 MeV (P0) down to 42.3 MeV (PT55) and 43.6 MeV (PTE06).
*   The incompressibility coefficient \(K_0\) for symmetric nuclear matter varies from 231.2 MeV (P0) to 218.0 MeV (PTE06).
*   Binding energy at saturation density \(E\) ranges from -16.6 MeV (P0) to -15.8 MeV (PTE06).
*   For finite nuclei, the relative error in binding energies when tensor couplings are included is approximately between -1.0% and 0.5%, compared to between -1.0% and 1.0% when they are excluded.
*   The parameter \(\Lambda\) for isoscalar-isovector coupling was set to values of 0.00, 0.04, 0.055, and 0.06 across different parameter sets.

## Context

This work extends studies of super-heavy nuclei (SHN) within RMF models, which typically predict a double shell closure at \(Z=120\) and \(N=172\) (Bender, 1999; Zhang, 2005; Jiang, 2010), by investigating the additional roles of tensor couplings and Coulomb exchange. It builds on prior work showing that the density dependence of symmetry energy affects the neutron skin thickness and central depression of \(^{292}\)120 (Jiang, 2010), and that isoscalar-isovector coupling (Piekarewicz, 2002) is crucial for acceptable nuclear matter predictions (Dutra, 2014). The findings enable a more nuanced understanding of how these terms influence bulk properties of nuclei from light to super-heavy masses, while confirming that shell closure predictions for \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{292}\)120 remain robust.
