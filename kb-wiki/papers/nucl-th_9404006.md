# Full determination of transition matrix elements in the $\vec{d}(\vec{p},pp)n$ reaction

## Key claim

In the $d(p,pp)n$ reaction with a colinear geometry and the two outgoing protons detected in a singlet state, the number of independent T-matrix elements reduces to exactly two, which can be fully determined from spin correlation experiments. The matrix elements are strongly dependent on the deuteron helicity amplitudes, which are expected to vanish for $1.4fm^{-1}<p<1.7fm^{-1}$, though the precise location of these nodes depends on the NN interaction used and rescattering terms may become competitive in this momentum region.

## Method

The authors analyze the spin structure of the $\vec{d}(\vec{p},(pp)_\circ)n$ reaction in a colinear geometry (di-proton detected at $0^\circ$ or $180^\circ$) where the two outgoing protons are in a spin-singlet state, reducing the 24 independent transition matrix elements to two: $T_+$ and $T_-$. Using the impulse approximation, they express these T-matrix elements in terms of half-off-shell NN T-matrix elements and deuteron helicity amplitudes $\phi_\pm^d(p)$, and derive relations between the spin correlation observables ($\sigma_{unpol}$, $T_{20}$, $T_{1010}$, $T_{111-1}$, $T_{112-1}$) and the two complex amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The node in the deuteron helicity amplitude $\phi_-^d(\vec{p})$ occurs for $1.4fm^{-1}<p<1.7fm^{-1}$, with the solution strongly dependent on the NN interaction used to generate the deuteron wave function.
- The helicity amplitude $\phi_+^d(\vec{p})$ is expected to vanish at a considerably higher momentum, near $5fm^{-1}$.
- $T_{20}$ is predicted to reach its minimum value $-\sqrt{2}$ for an incoming kinetic energy close to 410 MeV in the laboratory frame, corresponding to a recoil neutron momentum of around $270 MeV/c$.
- The unpolarized cross section is given by $\sigma_0=2(|T_-|^2+|T_+|^2)$.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of deuteron breakup at intermediate and high energy (Author abbreviations from 1983-1992) that revealed strong sensitivity to the high-momentum behavior of the deuteron and the NN interaction. The authors note that the impulse approximation has been successfully applied to $d(p,2p)n$ reactions at intermediate energies for small recoil neutron momentum ($q_n < 200 MeV/c$) by several groups (Buras et al. 1985, Pudliner et al. 1988, Morlet et al. 1989, Kox et al. 1991, Carey et al. 1991, Perdrisat et al. 1990). The predicted minimum in $T_{20}$ is analogous to predictions for the $\vec{d}(e,e'p)n$ reaction (Brodsky et al. 1992, Frankfurt et al. 1983), where $T_{20}(0^\circ)$ also reaches $-\sqrt{2}$ when $\phi_-^d(\vec{p})=0$, neglecting electron-neutron scattering amplitude, final state interactions, and other lower order effects. The results enable direct experimental determination of the two independent T-matrix elements from measurements of the unpolarized cross section and spin correlation observables, providing new information on NN T-matrix elements and the deuteron wave function at high momentum.
