# Real and virtual $N\bar{N}$ pair production near the threshold

> Nucleon-antinucleon optical potential, which explains the experimental data for the processes $e^{+}e^{-}\rightarrow p\bar{p}$ and $e^{+}e^{-}\rightarrow\mbox{pions}$ near the threshold of $p\bar{p}$ pair production, is suggested. To obtain this potential we have used the available experimental data for $p\bar{p}$ scattering, $p\bar{p}$ pair production in $e^{+}e^{-}$ annihilation, and the ratio of electromagnetic form factors of a proton in the timelike region. It turns out that final-state interaction via the optical potential allows one to reproduce the available experimental data with good accuracy. Our results for the cross sections of $e^{+}e^{-}\to6π$ process near the threshold of $p\bar{p}$ pair production are in agreement with the recent experiments.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new optical potential for nucleon-antinucleon interaction in the \(J=1\) partial waves that simultaneously describes \(p\bar{p}\) scattering data, the cross sections of \(e^{+}e^{-}\to p\bar{p}\) and \(e^{+}e^{-}\to n\bar{n}\), the ratio of proton electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region, and the dip in the \(e^{+}e^{-}\to 6\pi\) cross section near the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold; the potential is constrained by the requirement that the inelastic cross section from virtual \(N\bar{N}\) pairs does not overestimate the meson production data, which rules out the Paris and Nijmegen models for these channels.

## Method

The authors construct an optical potential for the coupled \(^{3}S_{1}\) and \(^{3}D_{1}\) partial waves (total angular momentum \(J=1\)) as a sum of a short-range complex square well and a long-range one-pion-exchange tail, with separate isoscalar and isovector components. They fit the 20 free parameters (well depths, imaginary parts, and radii for each of the six potential components, plus two normalization constants \(G_{S}^{I}\)) to 35 data points: the cross sections of \(e^{+}e^{-}\to p\bar{p}\) and \(e^{+}e^{-}\to n\bar{n}\) from BABAR, CMD-3, and SND; the ratio \(|G_{E}^{p}/G_{M}^{p}|\) from BABAR; and the \(J=1\) partial-wave contributions to elastic, charge-exchange, and total \(p\bar{p}\) scattering cross sections derived from the Nijmegen partial-wave S-matrix. The total cross section for meson production via virtual \(N\bar{N}\) pairs is then computed using the Green's function of the Schrödinger equation with this optical potential.

## Key numbers

*   The fit yields \(\chi^{2}_{\mathrm{min}}/N_{\mathrm{df}} = 29/15\) for all 35 data points; excluding two less accurate \(n\bar{n}\) points gives \(\chi^{2}_{\mathrm{min}}/N_{\mathrm{df}} = 16/13\).
*   The isoscalar short-range central potential parameters are \(U_{S0} = -433 \pm 3\) MeV (real part) and \(W_{S0} = 224 \pm 10\) MeV (imaginary part), with radius \(a_{S0} = 0.564 \pm 0.002\) fm.
*   The isoscalar normalization constant is \(G_{S}^{0} = 0.179 \pm 0.006\); the isovector constant is \(G_{S}^{1} = 0.044 + 0.29i \pm 0.014\).
*   A quasi-bound state is found in the \(I=0\) channel at energy \(E_{B} = (10 - i\,32)\) MeV; with the imaginary part of the potential turned off, this state moves to \(E_{B} = -21\) MeV.
*   The best description of the \(e^{+}e^{-}\to 6\pi\) cross section is obtained when the \(6\pi\) contribution to the inelastic \(I=1\) cross section is 56%.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Dmitriev et al., 2007; Dmitriev et al., 2014) that expressed near-threshold \(N\bar{N}\) production cross sections in terms of radial wave functions at the origin. It addresses the failure of the Paris optical potential (El-Bennich et al., 2009) to describe meson production via virtual \(N\bar{N}\) annihilation due to its overly large imaginary central potential, and the difficulty of applying the Nijmegen model (Zhou et al., 2012) to the Green's function calculation because of its complicated matching condition. The model enables predictions for the energy dependence of \(e^{+}e^{-}\) annihilation into mesons near the \(p\bar{p}\) threshold and identifies the need for more precise experimental data on exclusive meson channels to further constrain the optical potential.
