# Collectivity of the electromagnetic transitions in near-threshold resonances

> Mixing of the shell model (SM) eigenstates due to the coupling via the common decay channel leads in many cases to the formation of a collective eigenstate which carries many features of the nearby decay channel. This generic mechanism in open quantum systems explains the phenomenological Ikeda diagram and generalizes it for various clusters/correlations in the vicinity of the respective cluster decay thresholds. The near-threshold collectivization of the SM states may also influence their electromagnetic decays. We discuss this phenomenon on the example of B(EL) decays of near-threshold 2+ states in 14C.

## Key claim

The mixing of shell model eigenstates via coupling to a common decay channel can produce a collective near-threshold state whose electromagnetic transition strengths are dramatically enhanced, but this enhancement is contingent on the continuum-coupling strength being near a specific value (here, \(V_0 = -523.3\) MeV fm\(^3\)) where exceptional points of the effective Hamiltonian lie close to the real axis.

## Method

The authors use the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC), a real-energy continuum shell model, to describe \(^{14}\)C. The Hilbert space is split into a subspace of 0 particles in the scattering continuum (\({\cal Q}_0\)) and a subspace of 1 particle in the continuum (\({\cal Q}_1\)). An energy-dependent effective Hamiltonian in \({\cal Q}_0\) includes the standard shell-model Hamiltonian plus a continuum-coupling term \(W_{{\cal Q}_0{\cal Q}_0}(E)\) that mixes SM eigenstates via 9 reaction channels (one elastic and eight inelastic channels built on excited states of \(^{13}\)C). The continuum-coupling strength \(V_0\) is varied to study its effect on electromagnetic transitions and quadrupole moments of the first three \(2^+\) states.

## Key numbers

- The near-threshold \(2^+_2\) state in \(^{14}\)C lies at 8.318 MeV excitation energy, 142 keV above the one-neutron emission threshold, with a total width of 3.4 keV.
- At the continuum-coupling strength \(V_0 = -523.3\) MeV fm\(^3\) (which reproduces the experimental \(B(E2; 2^+_1 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\)), the \(B(E1; 2^+_2 \rightarrow 1^-_1)\) probability is enhanced by a factor of \(\sim 60\) relative to the shell-model value.
- At the same \(V_0\), the \(B(E2; 2^+_2 \rightarrow 0^+_1)\) probability is enhanced by a factor of \(\sim 340\) relative to the shell-model value.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used: radius \(R_0 = 1.27 A^{1/3}\) fm, diffuseness \(a = 0.67\) fm, spin-orbit strength \(V_{\rm SO} = 6.34\) MeV; neutron separation energy \(S_n = 8.176\) MeV (for \(p_{1/2}\)), proton separation energy \(S_p = 20.831\) MeV (for \(p_{3/2}\)).
- Three exceptional-point trajectories cross the real-\(V_0\) axis in a narrow energy range around \(E \sim 3.5\) MeV above the lowest one-neutron emission threshold.

## Context

This work extends the generic mechanism of near-threshold collectivization and clustering previously studied with SMEC (Okołowicz 2003, Bennaceur 2000, Rotureau 2005) from structural properties to electromagnetic decays. It generalizes the phenomenological Ikeda diagram (Ikeda 1968) by showing that continuum-induced mixing can also strongly modify \(\gamma\)-transition rates and quadrupole moments, not just cluster configurations. The results enable future experimental studies of \(\gamma\)-decay selection rules in the continuum and systematic mapping of near-threshold collectivity across the nuclear chart.
