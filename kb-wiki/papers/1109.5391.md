# Chiral condensate and Mott-Anderson freeze-out

> We present the idea of a Mott-Anderson freeze-out that suggests a key role of the localization of the hadron wave functions when traversing the hadronization transition. The extension of hadron wave functions in dense matter is governed by the behavior of the chiral quark condensate such that its melting at finite temperatures and chemical potentials entails an increase of the size of hadrons and thus their geometrical strong interaction cross sections. It is demonstrated within a schematic resonance gas model, that a kinetic freeze-out condition reveals a correlation with the reduction of the chiral condensate in the phase diagram up to 50 % of its vacuum value. Generalizing the description of the chiral condensate by taking into account a full hadron resonance gas such correlation gets distorted. We discuss, that this may be due to our approximations in calculating the chiral condensate which disregard both, in-medium effects on hadron masses and hadron-hadron interactions. The latter, in particular due to quark exchange reactions, could lead to a delocalization of the hadron wave functions in accordance with the picture of a Mott-Anderson transition.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions is governed by a Mott-Anderson type delocalization of hadron wave functions driven by the melting of the chiral condensate, but this correlation is demonstrated only within a schematic resonance gas model and gets distorted when a full hadron resonance gas is used, likely due to neglected in-medium effects on hadron masses and hadron-hadron interactions.

## Method

The authors model freeze-out by equating the expansion time of the hadronic fireball with the collision time derived from geometrical hadron-hadron cross sections that scale with the square of hadron radii. Hadron radii are linked to the chiral condensate via the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation, so that condensate melting increases radii and cross sections. The chiral condensate is computed first in a schematic pion-nucleon gas with variable degrees of freedom, then in a full hadron resonance gas using a Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model extended with mesonic and baryonic contributions, but without self-consistent in-medium mass modifications.

## Key numbers

* The kinetic freeze-out condition yields freeze-out times of 3–5 fm/c in the energy range between RHIC and GSI (SIS).
* The best description of phenomenological freeze-out points in the schematic model is obtained with pion degeneracy \(d_\pi = 8\) and nucleon degeneracy \(d_N = 20\).
* The freeze-out curve in the schematic model corresponds to a reduction of the chiral condensate to 50% of its vacuum value.
* At freeze-out, the hadron-hadron cross section is roughly 3–4 times its vacuum value.
* The pion-nucleon sigma-term used is \(\Sigma_{\pi N} = 45\) MeV.
* Vacuum radii used: \(r_\pi = 0.59\) fm, \(r_N = 0.74\) fm, with a nucleon hard core radius \(r_0 = 0.45\) fm.
* The PNJL model parameters include a constituent quark mass cutoff \(\Lambda = 602.3\) MeV, current quark mass \(m_0 = 5.5\) MeV, and Polyakov-loop transition temperature \(T_0 = 187\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the idea of linking chemical freeze-out to the chiral condensate, previously introduced by the same authors (Blaschke 2011), and contrasts with purely geometrical percolation approaches (Magas 2003) and softest-point hydrodynamic mechanisms (Toneev 2000). The proposed Mott-Anderson freeze-out mechanism aims to unify geometrical, hydrodynamic, and kinetic aspects of freeze-out through the medium dependence of the chiral condensate. The failure of the full resonance gas treatment to reproduce the expected correlation points to the need for self-consistent in-medium hadron masses and inclusion of quark-exchange interactions, which could be addressed via Beth-Uhlenbeck type generalizations of the thermodynamic potential (Hufner 1994, Zhuang 1994).
