# Nuclear Broadening Effects on Hard Prompt Photons at Relativistic Energies

> We calculate prompt photon production in high-energy nuclear collisions. We focus on the broadening of the intrinsic transverse momenta of the partons in the initial state from nuclear effects, and their influence on the prompt photon p_t distribution. Comparing to WA98 data from Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy we find evidence for the presence of nuclear broadening at high p_t in this hard process. Below p_t=2.7 GeV the photon distribution is due to small momentum transfer processes. At RHIC energy, the effect of intrinsic transverse momentum on the spectrum of prompt photons is less prominent. The region p_t=3-4 GeV would be the most promising for studying the nuclear broadening effects at that energy. Below p_t=2-3 GeV the contribution from large momentum transfers flattens out, and we expect that region to be dominated by soft contributions.

## Key claim

The paper finds evidence for nuclear broadening of parton intrinsic transverse momentum in hard prompt photon production at SPS energies, but only for photon transverse momenta above approximately 2.7 GeV; below that threshold, the photon distribution is dominated by small momentum transfer processes that cannot be described by perturbative QCD with a minimum momentum transfer cut of 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors compute prompt photon production in p+p and A+A collisions using perturbative QCD with a Gaussian parametrization of parton intrinsic transverse momentum, employing CTEQ4L parton distribution functions and a minimum momentum transfer cut of 1 GeV. They compare calculations with various values of the average intrinsic transverse momentum squared to WA98 data for central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=17.4A GeV and to E704 p+p data at sqrt(s)=19.4 GeV, and also make predictions for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200A GeV at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- For p+p collisions at sqrt(s)=19.4 GeV, the best fit to E704 data requires intrinsic transverse momentum squared of 1.3-1.5 GeV^2 with K=2 and Q^2=4p_t^2
- For central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=17.4A GeV, nuclear broadening of Delta k_t^2 = 0.5-1.0 GeV^2 is needed to describe WA98 data above p_t=2.7 GeV
- The geometrical factor converting p+p cross section to photon multiplicity for the 10% most central Pb+Pb collisions is 24.3/mb
- For Au+Au at sqrt(s)=200A GeV with 10% centrality, the geometrical factor is 23.1/mb
- At RHIC energy, intrinsic transverse momentum can increase prompt photon multiplicity by up to a factor of 3 in the p_t=3-4 GeV region

## Context

This work extends the analysis of nuclear broadening effects previously applied to pion production in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energy by Wang (1998) and Gyulassy and Levai (1998). The authors compare their results to WA98 data and to fits of nuclear broadening from Drell-Yan and J/psi production in p+A collisions by McGaughey, Moss, and Peng (1999). The paper enables future experimental studies of nuclear broadening at RHIC using prompt photons, which are cleaner probes than hadrons because they do not suffer from final-state rescattering.
