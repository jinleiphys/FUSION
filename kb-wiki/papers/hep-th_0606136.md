# Jet Quenching Parameter in Medium with Chemical Potential from AdS/CFT

> We calculate the jet quenching parameter in medium with chemical potential from AdS/CFT correspondence. Our result is summarized in a plot. Moreover, we extract the explicit form of the jet quenching parameter of medium with small chemical potential for phases of dual SYM corresponding to large and small black holes. For the former phase, the jet quenching is increased as the charge density increases, however, for the latter it is the opposite though the background is thermodynamically unstable.

## Key claim
The jet quenching parameter in a strongly coupled plasma with nonzero chemical potential (R-charge density) is calculated via AdS/CFT, and the result shows that the effect of charge depends on the black hole size: for the thermodynamically stable large black hole phase (small chemical potential) the quenching parameter increases with charge density, while for the unstable small black hole phase it decreases.

## Method
The authors use the AdS/CFT correspondence to compute the thermal expectation value of a lightlike Wilson loop in the fundamental representation of N=4 super-Yang-Mills (SYM) theory, dual to a single R-charged black hole in five-dimensional gauged supergravity. The on-shell Nambu-Goto action for a string worldsheet ending on the AdS boundary is evaluated, and the jet quenching parameter is extracted from the dipole approximation relation \(\langle W^A(\mathcal{C}) \rangle = \exp(-\frac{1}{4} \hat{q} L^- L^2)\). The calculation follows the method of Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006) for the zero-charge case, extended to nonzero chemical potential.

## Key numbers
- The jet quenching parameter for the charged medium is given by \(\hat{q}_{YM}(\kappa,T_\kappa,\lambda) = \frac{\pi^2}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{\lambda} \left(\frac{2\sqrt{1+\kappa}}{2+\kappa}\right)^3 T_\kappa^3 Q(\kappa)\), where \(\kappa = q/r_+^2\) characterizes the charge-to-entropy density ratio, \(\lambda\) is the 't Hooft coupling, and \(Q(\kappa)\) is a monotonically increasing function defined by an integral.
- For small chemical potential (\(\kappa \ll 1\)), the ratio of jet quenching parameters at the same temperature is \(\hat{q}_{YM}(\kappa,T_\kappa=T_0)/\hat{q}_{YM}^{(0)}(T_0) = 1 + c_1 \kappa + O(\kappa^2)\) with \(c_1 \simeq 0.5596\).
- For large \(\kappa\) (small black hole, \(\kappa \gg 1\)), the jet quenching parameter behaves as \(\hat{q}_{YM}(\kappa,T_\kappa) \simeq \frac{8\pi^2 d_1}{\sqrt{2}} \sqrt{\lambda} T_\kappa^3 \kappa^{-1/3}\) with \(d_1 \sim 0.7275\).
- The background is thermodynamically stable only for \(\kappa < 2\).
- Example numerical values for \(\hat{q}_{YM}\) in GeV\(^2\)/fm with \(\lambda = 6\pi\): at \(T=300\) MeV, \(\hat{q} = 3.17\) (\(\kappa=0\)), 3.97 (\(\kappa=1\)), 2.46 (\(\kappa=10\)); at \(T=400\) MeV, \(\hat{q} = 7.51\) (\(\kappa=0\)), 9.42 (\(\kappa=1\)), 5.83 (\(\kappa=10\)); at \(T=500\) MeV, \(\hat{q} = 14.7\) (\(\kappa=0\)), 18.4 (\(\kappa=1\)), 11.4 (\(\kappa=10\)).

## Context
This work extends the holographic calculation of the jet quenching parameter by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006) from the AdS-Schwarzschild background (zero chemical potential) to the R-charged black hole background, which introduces a chemical potential dual to R-charge density in N=4 SYM. The results are qualitatively similar to those from drag force calculations by Herzog et al. (2006) and Caceres et al., in that the jet quenching reaches a maximum at \(\kappa \sim \mathcal{O}(1)\), but differ quantitatively in the detailed \(\kappa\) dependence. The authors note that the positive coefficient \(c_1\) for small \(\kappa\) is consistent with the expectation that charge density enhances jet quenching, which may help reconcile the theoretical value (which was smaller than experimental estimates in the zero-charge case) with data from RHIC. The work enables future comparison with experimental jet quenching data and suggests that the meta-stable phase for \(\kappa > 2\) might mimic the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions.
