# Studying the Impact of Virtuality-Dependent Nucleon Structure Modification on Spectator-Tagged Deep Inelastic Scattering

> Measurements of deep inelastic scattering from nuclei have revealed that the partonic structure of bound nucleons differs from that of free nucleons. One hypothesis is that this structure modification primarily occurs in highly virtual nucleons participating in short-range correlations, although distinguishing this from other hypotheses is difficult with inclusive measurements alone. Spectator-tagged deep inelastic scattering, on the other hand, may offer a way to specifically probe the partonic structure of highly-virtual nucleons by detecting the correlated emission of a spectator nucleon. Here, we present a method for calculating a "spectator-tagged" structure function for a nucleus by combining Generalized Contact Formalism's description of short-range correlations with light-cone convolution formalism to determine the impact of nucleon motion on the structure function. We apply this method to calculate predictions for helium-4, and find that differences in the virtuality-dependence of nucleon structure modification can lead to large measurable changes in the tagged structure function. The recent CLAS12 Short-Range Correlations Experiment, which collected electron scattering data on helium-4 and other nuclear targets, may be able to constrain this virtuality-dependence and help test whether correlations are the origin of the modification of bound nucleon structure.

# Studying the Impact of Virtuality-Dependent Nucleon Structure Modification on Spectator-Tagged Deep Inelastic Scattering

## Key claim

Spectator-tagged deep inelastic scattering on helium-4, combined with the Generalized Contact Formalism and light-cone convolution, can produce large measurable differences in the tagged structure function under different assumptions for virtuality-dependent nucleon structure modification; however, the method is only reliable for high spectator light-cone momentum fractions (above approximately α₂ = 1.3) where scale separation between correlated pair momentum and Fermi momentum holds.

## Method

The authors combine the Generalized Contact Formalism (GCF) description of short-range correlations with light-cone convolution formalism to calculate a spectator-neutron-tagged F₂ structure function for helium-4. They model the two-nucleon light-cone decay function using GCF with the AV18 potential for relative motion distributions and a Gaussian center-of-mass distribution with width σ = 100 MeV/c, then integrate over the struck nucleon's light-cone momentum and transverse momentum using Monte Carlo integration. Five different models for the off-shell nucleon modification function f_off(𝑥̃) are tested: no modification, constant (K = 0.96), linear (A = 0.31, B = 0.98), the Kulagin-Petti (KP) parameterization, and the CTEQ-Jefferson Lab (CJ) parameterization.

## Key numbers

- The EMC Effect in the heaviest nuclei saturates at approximately a 15% reduction in F₂ at a momentum fraction of approximately 0.7
- The center-of-mass momentum distribution width σ_CM is approximately 100–150 MeV/c; the authors use σ = 100 MeV/c based on the measurement in LabHallA (2014)
- The constant modification model uses K = 0.96 from the global fit analysis of Segarra (2020)
- The linear modification model uses A = 0.31 and B = 0.98 from the same global fit analysis
- The KP model parameters are C = 8.1, x₀ = 0.448, x₁ = 0.05
- The CJ model parameters are C = -3.6735, x₀ = 0.057717, x₁ = 0.36419
- Calculations are evaluated at Q² = 2 GeV²/c² and |p₂⊥| = 0, typical for CLAS12 coverage with a 6 GeV beam
- At α₂ = 1.6 and x_B = 0.7, the ratio of modified to unmodified tagged structure functions ranges from below 0.4 to above 1 across the different models
- At x_B = 0.3, different models predict 20–40% variation in the tagged structure function depending on α₂
- The CLAS12-SRC Experiment collected 3.9 × 10⁹ triggers on helium-4 over 10 days with a 6 GeV beam
- The BAND detector covers angles 155°–175° from the beamline and detects neutrons in the momentum range of approximately 200–700 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the convolution formalism developed by Segarra (2020) and the light-cone formulation of Generalized Contact Formalism described by Pybus (2020) to predict spectator-tagged structure functions for helium-4. It addresses the EMC-SRC hypothesis, which is supported by the correlation between EMC Effect magnitude and SRC pair prevalence across nuclei shown by Weinstein (2010), Hen (2012), and Arrington (2012), but which remains inconclusive due to the limitations of inclusive DIS measurements. The calculations are designed to be testable with data from the recently completed CLAS12 Short-Range Correlations Experiment (E12-17-006A), which collected data from November 2021 through February 2022 using the BAND detector for spectator neutron detection, and the methodology can be extended to other nuclei such as carbon-12 and calcium-40 for which GCF parameters are known.
