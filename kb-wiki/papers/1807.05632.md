# Hadronic observables in p+p and d+Au collisions at RHIC using CGC+PYTHIA

> The IP-Glasma model of CGC combined with the Lund model of PYTHIA provides a very successful description of hadron production from gluon dominated non-equilibrium matter in various small collision systems. This new CGC+PYTHIA framework, naturally reproduces several characteristic features of the hadronic observables such as the mass ordering of $v_{2}(p_{T})$ and $\langle p_{T}\rangle$, often ascribed to collectivity driven by hydrodynamics at the LHC. In this contribution we extend our work to provide a systematic comparison of particle spectra and multiplicity distributions in p+p and d+Au collisions at the RHIC.

## Key claim

The CGC+PYTHIA framework, which combines the IP-Glasma model of the Color Glass Condensate with the Lund string fragmentation model of PYTHIA, reproduces hadronic observables in p+p and d+Au collisions at RHIC without requiring strong final-state interactions or hydrodynamic evolution, though the results carry systematic uncertainties from parameter variations.

## Method

The authors computed event-by-event gluon distributions from classical Yang-Mills equations using the IP-Glasma model on a transverse lattice (size N=400, spacing a=0.04 fm, infrared regulator mass m=0.2 GeV). They sampled the resulting gluons to construct PYTHIA strings and fed them into the Lund string fragmentation routine in PYTHIA version 8.235, with the fragmentation function parameters a and b varied within their allowed ranges (0 to 2.0 for a, 0.2 to 2.0 for b) to estimate systematic uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Beam rapidity at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: y_beam = 5.36
- Ratio of saturation scale to color charge fluctuation width: Q_s/(g^2 mu) = 0.7
- Width of saturation scale fluctuations: sigma(log(Q_s)) = 0.5
- Evolution time for Yang-Mills phase varied in range: tau = 0.4 to 0.6 fm
- k_perp^max varied in range: 8 to 15 GeV
- Inelastic cross section for p+p collisions: sigma^pp_inel = 42 mb
- Total geometric cross section for d+Au collisions: sigma^d+Au_geo = 2180 mb (from MC-Glauber model)

## Context

This work extends the CGC+PYTHIA framework previously developed by Schenke et al. (2016) for LHC energies to RHIC energies, providing the first systematic comparison of particle spectra and multiplicity distributions in p+p and d+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV. The framework successfully describes observations such as multiplicity distributions and identified particle spectra that are often attributed to hydrodynamic collectivity, and it enables future studies of more complex observables from the RHIC small system scan, including the hierarchy of v_n(p_T) in p/d/He-Au collisions observed by Aidala et al. (2018) and the R_p/d/He-Au measurements by Sakaguchi et al. (2016).
