# Proton number fluctuations in partial chemical equilibrium

> We calculate volume-independent ratios of cumulants of the net-proton number distribution up to sixth order in a fireball that cools down after the chemical freeze-out. A hadron resonance gas model is used together with the assumption of partial chemical equilibrium, which fixes the number of observed stable hadrons after the chemical freeze-out. It is shown that due to only weak departure from the statistical Boltzmann distribution, also the volume-independent ratios of higher-order cumulants of the net-proton number show only weak dependence on the temperature. This observation supports the possibility to measure non-critical cumulants at chemical freeze-out even after subsequent cooling in the hadronic phase. Cumulants of the net-baryon number behave similarly, while those for the kaon number vary more strongly with the temperature. Our results are relevant for the current fluctuation studies of the RHIC-BES runs.

## Key claim

The volume-independent ratios of higher-order cumulants of the net-proton number distribution show only weak dependence on temperature when a fireball cools after chemical freeze-out under partial chemical equilibrium, because the dominant Boltzmann contribution makes all cumulants nearly constant when the mean is fixed.

## Method

The authors used a hadron resonance gas model with partial chemical equilibrium, where the effective numbers of stable hadrons are fixed at their chemical freeze-out values while resonances remain equilibrated with their decay daughters. They calculated cumulants of the net-proton number distribution up to sixth order from a generating function that accounts for both direct proton production and resonance decays, using chemical freeze-out parameters extracted by the STAR collaboration for central Au+Au collisions at nine beam energies from 7.7 GeV to 200 GeV. The chemical potentials for stable hadrons were evolved from chemical freeze-out down to lower temperatures by imposing conservation of the ratio of effective hadron number to entropy under isentropic expansion.

## Key numbers

- For net-proton number at the lowest collision energy (7.7 GeV), the ratio $\kappa^H\sigma^4 = \cum{(\delN_{p-\bar p})}{6}/\cum{(\delN_{p-\bar p})}{2}$ decreases by about 4% when cooling from chemical freeze-out temperature 144.3 MeV down to 70 MeV.
- For net-proton number at chemical freeze-out, the decrease of ratios with lowering collision energy reaches almost 10% for $\kappa^H\sigma^4$ when going from 200 GeV to 7.7 GeV.
- Including cooling and PCE down to kinetic freeze-out further lowers $\kappa^H\sigma^4$ by about 1% relative to the chemical freeze-out value.
- For the $(K^+-K^-)$ number distribution, the change of cumulant ratios due to decreasing temperature is not bigger than 5% even in the most extreme case of $S^H\sigma^5/M$.
- The chemical freeze-out temperature ranges from 144.3 MeV at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7$ GeV to 164.3 MeV at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV, with corresponding baryochemical potentials from 398.2 MeV down to 28.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas baseline calculations for fluctuation observables used in the search for the QCD critical point, following earlier studies by Nahrgang (2014) and Bzdak (2012-2018). It addresses the suggestion by Sochorova (2018) that different cumulants may not correspond to a unique temperature due to fast expansion, by showing that cumulant ratios calculated at chemical freeze-out remain valid after subsequent cooling under partial chemical equilibrium. The results provide a non-critical baseline for interpreting the STAR collaboration's Beam Energy Scan data, particularly the dramatic rise of the fourth-order cumulant at 7.7 GeV reported by Abdallah (2021).
