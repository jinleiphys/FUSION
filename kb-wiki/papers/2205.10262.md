# Response of a baryon-charged medium to energetic partons

> We explore the response to energetic partons of a baryon-charged medium produced in low energy heavy-ion collisions in which the partonic energy loss rate is expected to depend on both temperature and baryon chemical potential. The energy and momentum deposited by the partons are described by dynamical sources added to hydrodynamic equations of motion. We study the distortions of various hydrodynamic quantities, especially the energy and baryon densities, induced by energetic partons plowing through the medium. By studying the contribution from this medium response to the emission spectra of several identified hadron species we identify qualitative differences between the jet-induced modifications for mesons and net protons.

## Key claim

The paper explores the hydrodynamic response of a baryon-charged medium to energetic partons in low-energy heavy-ion collisions, but remains exploratory due to the lack of a fully calibrated 3-dimensional hydrodynamic model; the analysis is therefore based on a highly simplified background medium profile assuming an ideal (non-dissipative) fluid and neglecting baryon diffusion effects.

## Method

The authors use ideal Gubser flow as a semi-analytic background for a baryon-charged medium evolving according to conservation laws for energy-momentum and net baryon charge, with a conformal equation of state for massless quarks and gluons. Energy and momentum deposited by energetic partons are added as dynamical source terms to the hydrodynamic equations, using an energy loss rate constructed from holographic calculations of the jet quenching parameter that depends on both temperature and baryon chemical potential. The medium response is computed by numerically solving the sourced hydrodynamic equations with the BEShydro code, and the resulting distortions to final-state hadron spectra are obtained via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription.

## Key numbers

- Initial central temperature: T = 370 MeV, with initial time τ₀ = 1 fm/c
- Normalized baryon chemical potential: α = μ/T = 0.2 for the background medium
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 148 MeV
- Gubser scale parameter: q⁻¹ = 4.3 fm; integration constant C ≈ 3.24 fm⁻¹
- Initial fireball radius above freeze-out temperature: r ≈ 7.7 fm; fireball center freeze-out time: τ_f ≈ 4.3 fm/c
- Smearing width for parton source: σ = 0.2 fm
- Energy loss rate relation: ehat = qhat/(8T), with qhat from holographic model giving qhat ≈ 27.22 T³ for light quarks at (T, μ) = (398, 0) MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of jet-medium interaction at high collision energies (where the medium is baryon-neutral) to lower energies where the medium carries large net baryon density, as studied in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC. It builds on prior hydrodynamic response studies by Yan et al. (2017) and uses holographic calculations of the jet quenching parameter by Rougemont et al. (2015) that show enhancement near the phase transition. The results enable qualitative theoretical predictions for jet-induced modifications to identified hadron spectra, particularly differences between mesons and net protons, to aid analysis of STAR Collaboration measurements at BES energies.
