# The fate of pion condensation in quark matter: from the chiral limit to the physical pion mass

> We study aspects of the pion condensation in two-flavor neutral quark matter using the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model of QCD at finite density. We investigate the role of electric charge neutrality, and explicit symmetry breaking via quark mass, both of which control the onset of the charged pion $(π^c)$ condensation. We show that the equality between the electric chemical potential and the in-medium pion mass, $μ_{e}=M_{π^-}$, as a threshold, persists even for a composite pion system in the medium, provided the transition to the pion condensed phase is of the second order. Moreover, we find that the pion condensate in neutral quark matter is extremely fragile to the symmetry breaking effect via a current quark mass $m$, and is ruled out for $m$ larger than the order of 10 keV.

## Key claim
The paper shows that charged pion condensation in neutral two-flavor quark matter is ruled out for current quark masses larger than about 10 keV, even though the threshold condition μₑ = M_{π⁻} (the equality of the electric chemical potential and the in-medium pion mass) remains valid for second-order transitions in a composite pion model.

## Method
The authors use the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model of QCD at finite density and zero temperature, treating pions as bound states of quarks rather than elementary fields. They impose electric charge neutrality and compute the in-medium pion masses via the random phase approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, then determine the phase boundaries by minimizing the thermodynamic potential with respect to the chiral condensate σ and the charged pion condensate N.

## Key numbers
- The critical current quark mass above which charged pion condensation is impossible in neutral matter is of order 10 keV, corresponding to a vacuum pion mass m_π^c ∼ 9 MeV.
- At a toy current quark mass m = 10 keV, the onset of pion condensation occurs at μ = μ_{c1} ≈ 324 MeV, with μ_e ≈ 10 MeV; the condensate vanishes at μ = μ_{c2} ≈ 330 MeV.
- The vacuum parameters used are Λ = 651 MeV, G = 2.12/Λ², reproducing f_π = 92 MeV, ⟨ūu⟩ = −(250 MeV)³, and m_π = 139 MeV at the physical quark mass m = 5.5 MeV.
- At the physical point (m = 5.5 MeV), the neutrality line in the (μ, μ_e) plane never enters the region where the charged pion condensate is nonzero.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of S-wave pion condensation in the NJL model by Ebert (2005) and Andersen (2007), which found a window for a "gapless pion condensate" on the finite μ axis. The authors clarify that this window exists only extremely close to the chiral limit, and that explicit symmetry breaking via a physical quark mass eliminates it entirely. The result contradicts the possibility of charged pion condensation in neutron star matter, consistent with earlier nuclear-physics arguments based on the repulsive Tomozawa-Weinberg interaction (Muto, 2003). The analysis also connects to the BEC-BCS crossover problem at finite isospin density studied by Son (2000) and He (2005).
