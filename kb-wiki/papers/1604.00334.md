# Closed expression for the pair vibrational correlation energy of a uniform distribution of single-nucleon levels

> A closed expression is derived for the pair vibrational correlation energy generated in the random phase approximation by the isovector pairing force in the case when Kramers and charge degenerate single-nucleon levels are uniformly distributed in an interval. The expression is used to analyze the spectral density of pair vibrational frequencies relative to that of two-quasinucleon energies. Applications to the analysis of the symmetry energy of the isovector pairing model and to a Strutinskij renormalization of this model are discussed.

## Key claim

A closed expression is derived for the pair vibrational correlation energy generated by the isovector pairing force in the random phase approximation, under the simplifying assumption that the three components of the force act on selections of single-nucleon levels that are symmetric about the respective chemical potentials, which allows the finite picket-fence spectrum to be replaced by a continuous spectrum in an interval.

## Method

The author replaces the finite equidistant (picket-fence) single-nucleon spectrum with a continuous uniform distribution of levels in an interval, then evaluates the RPA correlation energy integrals analytically. The derivation uses the separable isovector pairing force in the Hartree-Bogolyubov plus RPA framework, following the formalism of Neergård (2009), and obtains a closed form for the RPA energy expressed in terms of the gap parameter Δ and a dimensionless reciprocal coupling constant a = 1/(gG).

## Key numbers

- The function I(a,0) for the two-neutron, two-proton, or neutron-proton (N=Z) case equals -1 at a=0, as given by I(0,0) = -1.
- For a six-level picket-fence spectrum populated by 12 nucleons with gG = 0.8 and T=0, the exact energy is -11.59, the HB+RPA energy is -11.59, and the continuous limit gives -11.62 (all in units of the single-nucleon level spacing 1/g).
- The derivative ∂I(a,l)/∂l evaluated at l=0⁺ equals 1, giving ∂E_RPA,np/∂δλ = 1/2 at δλ = 0⁺.
- The BCS energy in the continuous limit is E_BCS,τ = -(1/2)ΩΔ e^{-a}.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Strutinskij (1967), who derived a closed expression for the average BCS energy from a continuous spectrum, to the RPA correlation energy of the isovector pairing model. The results were used in preliminary form in Bentley, Frauendorf, and Neergård (2014) to provide smooth counterterms for a Strutinskij renormalization of the isovector pairing model. The closed expression enables analysis of the spectral density of pair vibrational frequencies and the contribution of non-collective pair vibrational modes to the symmetry energy, which influences the shape of the Wigner cusp in nuclear mass chains.
