# Nonspherical Pauli-forbidden states in deformed halo nuclei: Impact on the ${}^7\mathrm{Be}+p$ resonant states in the particle rotor model

> Background: An important aspect of reducing nuclear many-body problems to few-body models is the presence of Pauli forbidden (PF) states, which are excluded in fully antisymmetrized calculations. Insufficient treatments of PF states in deformed halo nuclei underscore the need for model refinement. Purpose: We propose a new method utilizing Nilsson states as PF states in the orthogonality condition model, and investigate the impact of PF states on the properties of resonant states. Method: We investigate the scattering states of ${}^8\mathrm{B}$ within the Particle Rotor Model (PRM) framework based on a deformed ${}^7\mathrm{Be}$ core and $p$ two-body model. We compare several methods for eliminating PF states and test them with the experimental data. Results: Our model successfully reproduces the experimental excitation function for elastic scattering cross section by properly eliminating PF states. The same calculation predicts the presence of a low-energy bump in the inelastic scattering excitation function, although its position is overestimated by about 1 MeV compared to experimental data. Conclusion: This study extends the applicability of the PRM, offering a comprehensive approach for exploring structures and reactions of loosely bound nuclei like ${}^8$B. Future integration with the continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method promises to further advance the research.

## Key claim

The paper proposes using Nilsson states as Pauli-forbidden (PF) states within the orthogonality condition model (OCM) for deformed halo nuclei, and demonstrates that this method successfully reproduces experimental elastic scattering data for the \({}^7\mathrm{Be}+p\) system, though the predicted low-energy bump in the inelastic excitation function overestimates the experimental position by about 1 MeV.

## Method

The authors model \({}^8\mathrm{B}\) as a deformed \({}^7\mathrm{Be}\) core plus a proton within the particle rotor model (PRM) framework. They compare three methods for eliminating Pauli-forbidden states: the standard PRM (std-PRM) which removes PF states after solving the Schrödinger equation, a spherical PF model (sph-PF) using spherical PF states in the OCM, and a new deformed PF model (def-PF) using Nilsson states as PF states in the OCM. The coupled-channel equations are solved with an \(R\)-matrix code, and the potential depth \(V_\mathrm{def}^0\) is adjusted for each \(J^\pi\) to reproduce experimental bound and resonant energies or ab initio NCSM/RGM phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The \({}^7\mathrm{Be}\) core deformation parameter \(\beta_2\) is set to 0.586.
- The ground state of \({}^8\mathrm{B}\) (\(J^\pi=2^+\)) has energy \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{gs} = -0.137\) MeV; the \(1^+\) resonant state is at \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{res} = 0.63\) MeV; the \(3^+\) resonant state is at \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{res} = 2.18\) MeV.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters are radius \(R_0 = 2.391\) fm and diffuseness \(a_0 = 0.535\) fm; the spin-orbit depth is \(V_{LS}^0 = -7\) MeV.
- For the \(0^+\) state, the def-PF model yields a resonant energy \(\varepsilon(0_1^+) \approx 1.1\) MeV, compared to the NCSM/RGM value of \(\varepsilon(0_1^+) \approx 2.8\) MeV.
- The potential depths \(V_\mathrm{def}^0\) for the def-PF model range from 48.00 MeV (\(0^+\), \(1^-\), \(2^-\)) to 53.00 MeV (\(2^-\)), with values for positive-parity states between 48.70 MeV (\(1^+\)) and 50.41 MeV (\(2^+\)).
- In the std-PRM for \(J^\pi=3^+\), an additional resonance emerges at \(\varepsilon \approx 1.6\) MeV, identified as a PF state that cannot be eliminated.

## Context

This work extends the particle rotor model (PRM) treatment of deformed halo nuclei, previously applied to \({}^31\mathrm{Ne}\) by Uraki and collaborators (2011, 2012), to the \({}^8\mathrm{B}\) system where PF states appear as resonances rather than bound states. The new method using Nilsson states as PF states overcomes limitations of the standard PRM approach, which fails for \(J^\pi=3^+\) where a PF state becomes unbound. The results are compared with ab initio NCSM/RGM calculations by Kravvaris and collaborators (2023) and with experimental elastic scattering data from Pan and collaborators (2019). The approach enables future integration with the continuum discretized coupled channels (CDCC) method for reaction calculations.
