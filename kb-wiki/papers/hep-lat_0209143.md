# Phase diagram of the three-dimensional NJL model

> With the exception of confinement the three-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL_3) model incorporates many of the essential properties of QCD. We discuss the critical properties of the model at nonzero temperature T and/or nonzero chemical potential $μ$. We show that the universality class of the thermal transition is that of the d=2 classical spin model with the same symmetry. We provide evidence for the existence of a tricritical point in the ($μ$,temperature) plane. We also discuss numerical results by Hands et al. which showed that the system is critical for $μ>μ_c$ and the diquark condensate is zero.

## Key claim

The three-dimensional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL₃) model exhibits a tricritical point in the (μ,T) plane, but only within a narrow region defined by T/T_c ≤ 0.23 and μ/μ_c ≥ 0.97, with the universality class of the thermal transition being that of the d=2 classical spin model with the same symmetry rather than mean field behavior.

## Method

The author performed lattice simulations of the Z₂-symmetric NJL₃ model using the Hybrid Monte Carlo method with N_f=12 flavors for the finite-temperature study and N_f=4 flavors for the nonzero chemical potential study. Finite size scaling (FSS) analysis was applied to extract critical exponents from simulations with temporal lattice size L_t=6 and spatial sizes ranging from L_s=18 to 50. The order parameter was the expectation value of the auxiliary sigma field (Σ ≡ ⟨σ⟩), and the fermion number density η(μ) was measured as a function of chemical potential at various temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Critical exponents from FSS: ν = 1.00(3), β_m/ν = 0.12(6), γ/ν = 1.66(9), consistent with 2d Ising model (ν=1, β_m/ν=0.125, γ/ν=1.75) rather than mean field (ν=0.5, β_m/ν=1, γ/ν=2)
- At leading order in 1/N_f, the critical temperature is T_c = m_f/(2 ln 2)
- The tricritical point lies on the phase boundary section defined by T/T_c ≤ 0.23 and μ/μ_c ≥ 0.97
- The critical coupling for d=3 in the large-N_f limit is g_c²/a ≈ 1.0 with lattice regularization
- For the diquark condensate study, the critical exponent δ falls in the range 3-5 for the μ values studied

## Context

This work extends the large-N_f analytical predictions of Klimenko (1990s) and the numerical studies of Hands et al. (1995) on the NJL₃ model, which serves as a tractable laboratory for QCD thermodynamics without the sign problem. The results contradict the mean field prediction of a first-order transition at T=0 and continuous transition for T>0 from Klimenko, showing instead that the second-order nature of the T≠0, μ=0 transition remains stable down to low T and large μ. The study enables future investigations of the phase diagram at even lower temperatures and higher chemical potentials, and provides a benchmark for comparing with results in NJL₄ where a nonzero diquark condensate has been observed by Walters (recent).
