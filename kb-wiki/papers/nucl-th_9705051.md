# Hadron Formation Time and Dilepton Mass Spectra in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We point out that formation time of pions produced in heavy ion collisions modifies the mass spectrum of dileptons produced via pipi -> ee annihilation. Increasing formation time enhances the production of dileptons with lower masses. The effect offers an explanation of a part of the enhanced production of dileptons below the rho-meson mass as observed by the CERES and HELIOS Collaborations at the CERN SPS.

# Hadron Formation Time and Dilepton Mass Spectra in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim

The formation time of pions produced in heavy ion collisions modifies the dilepton mass spectrum from π⁺π⁻ → e⁺e⁻ annihilation, with increasing formation time enhancing the production of lower-mass dileptons; this effect can explain *part* of the enhanced dilepton production below the ρ-meson mass observed by CERES and HELIOS, but the authors caution that their comparison is only qualitative and that a quantitative analysis requires a more detailed Monte Carlo code including ρ-mesons and other resonances.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo multiple scattering model, inspired by Humanic (1993), in which all secondary hadrons are treated as pions. The initial state of pion momenta and positions from nucleon-nucleon sub-collisions was generated using the Závada (1996) model. The rescattering program tracks each pion in short time steps, applying isospin-averaged ππ cross sections with s- and p-wave phase shifts, and introduces a formation time τ_f (Lorentz-dilated in the pion rest frame) during which a newly created pion cannot interact. The dilepton mass spectrum is then computed from the mass spectrum of ππ collisions using the ratio of the π⁺π⁻ → e⁺e⁻ cross section to the total ππ cross section.

## Key numbers

- Rate of collisions per pion varies from 3.7 for τ_f = 0.2 fm to 1.0 for τ_f = 1.0 fm in the simulation for Pb+Pb at 160 GeV/nucleon with impact parameter b = 7 fm.
- The rescattering time interval t_i for a ππ scattering event is taken as approximately 1 fm/c, corresponding roughly to ħ/Γ_ρ.
- The dilepton yield was rescaled to a 100 MeV bin size, charged multiplicity dN_ch/dη = 300, and yield per unit of rapidity.
- A rough qualitative agreement with CERES data could be reached for formation times τ_f ≈ 0.5 fm.
- The contribution around m_ee ≈ 500–600 MeV/c² may still be missing from the calculation.

## Context

This work extends the line of research on dilepton production in heavy ion collisions, particularly the excess observed by CERES (1995, 1996) and HELIOS (1993, 1995) collaborations at CERN SPS. The dominant explanation at the time was the Brown-Rho (1991) conjecture of decreasing ρ-meson mass with increasing density and temperature, which when included in transport codes by Li, Ko, and Brown (1995) gave qualitative agreement with data. The authors introduce the formation time concept, previously used by Stodolsky (1976) and in analyses of proton-nucleus data by others (1980s), as an additional mechanism that softens the dilepton spectrum. The paper suggests that formation time effects should be studied in more detailed calculations such as those by other groups (1996, 1997) to see if they can bring models closer to data, and notes that a similar excess shape has been observed by the DLS collaboration (1996).
