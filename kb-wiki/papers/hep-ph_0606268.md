# Once more about the K\bar K molecule approach to the light scalars

> We show that the recent paper \cite{kalash}, claiming that the radiative decays ϕ\to a_0(980)γand ϕ\to f_0(980)γshould be of the same order of magnitude regardless of whether the a_0(980) and f_0(980) are compact four-quark states or extended K\bar K molecule states, is misleading.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the claim by Kalashnikova (2005) that radiative decays φ→a₀(980)γ and φ→f₀(980)γ should be of the same order of magnitude for both compact four-quark states and extended K̄K molecule states is misleading, because the nonrelativistic kaon description used in that work is applied beyond its region of applicability.

## Method
The authors recalculate the φ→K⁺K⁻→γS amplitude (where S = a₀(980) or f₀(980)) using the same diagrams as Kalashnikova (2005), introducing a cutoff k₀ on kaon momentum to examine which momenta dominate the real part of the loop integral. They evaluate the auxiliary integral (a−b)ReI(a,b;Γ;k₀) for three values of the potential range parameter β (0.2, 0.3, and 0.8 GeV) and compare the momentum distribution of the full integral with the distribution obtained after integrating the (d)-diagram contribution by parts, which Kalashnikova (2005) called a "trick."

## Key numbers
- The limit values of (a−b)ReI(a,b;Γ) are: 0.16 for β = 0.8 GeV, 0.116 for β = 0.3 GeV, and 0.079 for β = 0.2 GeV.
- The contribution of nonrelativistic kaons (k₀ < 0.3 GeV) to ReI(a,b;Γ) is small in all cases.
- Ultrarelativistic kaons (k₀ > 2 GeV) determine the real part of the φ→K⁺K⁻→γS amplitude in the typical case of β = 0.8 GeV.
- The scalar meson mass m_S = 980 MeV and kaon mass m_K = 495 MeV are used, following Kalashnikova (2005).

## Context
This work extends the debate on the internal structure of the light scalar mesons a₀(980) and f₀(980), specifically challenging the K̄K molecule interpretation proposed by Kalashnikova (2005). The authors argue that the real part of the K⁺K⁻ loop is dominated by high kaon virtualities, pointing instead to a compact four-quark nature as previously argued by Achasov (2001, 2003). The analysis enables future work to distinguish between molecular and four-quark interpretations by properly accounting for relativistic kaon contributions in loop calculations.
