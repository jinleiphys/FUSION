# Nonsymmetrized Hyperspherical Harmonics approach to A=6 system

> The Hyperspherical Harmonics basis, without a previous symmetrization step, is used to calculate binding energies of the nuclear A=6 systems using a version of the Volkov potential acting only on s-wave. The aim of this work is to illustrate the use of the nonsymmetrized basis to deal with permutational-symmetry-breaking term in the Hamiltonian, in the present case the Coulomb interaction.

## Key claim

The nonsymmetrized Hyperspherical Harmonics basis can handle the permutational-symmetry-breaking Coulomb interaction in A=6 systems, but the method is demonstrated only for a simplified Volkov potential acting exclusively in s-wave, not for realistic nuclear forces.

## Method

The authors constructed a nonsymmetrized Hyperspherical Harmonics basis for six-body systems, expressing the Hamiltonian as an algebraic combination of sparse matrices. They used Jacobi coordinates adapted to each particle pair, transformed the basis between different pair coordinate systems via sparse matrix products, and diagonalized the Hamiltonian iteratively using a symmetry-adapted Lanczos algorithm. The calculation was performed for the Volkov potential (a sum of two Gaussians) acting only in s-wave, with Coulomb interaction added for two protons (modeling ⁶He) and three protons (modeling ⁶Li).

## Key numbers

- Volkov potential parameters: \(V_R = 144.86\) MeV, \(R_1 = 0.82\) fm, \(V_A = -83.34\) MeV, \(R_2 = 1.6\) fm; \(\hbar^2/m = 41.47\) MeV·fm²
- Binding energy without Coulomb (⁶He/⁶Li ground state, \(K_{\text{max}} = 22\)): 33.814 MeV
- Binding energy with two-proton Coulomb (⁶He model, \(K_{\text{max}} = 22\)): 33.016 MeV
- Binding energy with three-proton Coulomb (⁶Li model, \(K_{\text{max}} = 22\)): 32.087 MeV
- Maximum basis size used: \(K_{\text{max}} = 22\) with 11 Laguerre polynomials, corresponding to 3,527,160 basis elements
- Number of basis states at \(K_{\text{max}} = 2\): 15; at \(K_{\text{max}} = 22\): 3,527,160

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous nonsymmetrized HH approach (Gattobigio et al., 2009-2010) from smaller systems to A=6, where the HH basis degeneracy had previously prevented systematic use with realistic potentials. The method enables treatment of Coulomb symmetry breaking in light nuclei, splitting the \(S_6\) irreducible representation [4 2] into sub-representations under \(S_2 \otimes S_4\) (for ⁶He) and \(S_3 \otimes S_3\) (for ⁶Li), with only one physical state in each case. The next step would be to apply this technique to realistic potentials with higher partial waves.
