# Approximations of potentials through the truncation of their inverses

> The inverse of an $\infty \times \infty$ symmetric band matrix can be constructed in terms of a matrix continued fraction. For Hamiltonians with Coulomb plus polynomial potentials, this results in an exact and analytic Green's operator which, even in finite-dimensional representation, exhibits the exact spectrum. In this work we propose a finite dimensional representation for the potential operator such that it retains some information about the whole Hilbert-space representation. The potential should be represented in a larger basis, then the matrix should be inverted, then truncated to the desired size, and finally inverted again. This procedure results in a superb low-rank representation of the potential operator. The method is illustrated with a typical nucleon-nucleon potential.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a finite-dimensional representation of a short-range potential operator can be made to retain information from the full Hilbert space by first representing the potential in a larger basis, inverting the matrix, truncating to the desired size, and then inverting again; this procedure yields a low-rank representation that converges much faster than the naive truncation of the potential matrix.

## Method

The authors represent the short-range potential in a Coulomb-Sturmian basis of size \(N'\), numerically invert that matrix, truncate it to size \(N \times N\) (with \(N \le N'\)), and then invert the truncated matrix again to obtain the effective potential matrix \(\underline{v}^{N,N'}\). This effective potential is then used in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation alongside an exact and analytic representation of the Coulomb Green's operator \((\underline{g}_l^C)^{-1}\), which is constructed via a matrix continued fraction. The method is tested on the Malfliet-Tjon nucleon-nucleon potential for both bound-state (deuteron) and scattering calculations.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy converges to \(-2.23068774\) MeV (at \(N=20\) with \(N'=N+7\)), achieving four-digit accuracy already at \(N=5\) (\(-2.22996195\) MeV) and eight-to-nine-digit accuracy at \(N=20\).
- \(p-p\) scattering phase shift at \(E = 0.1\) MeV converges to \(-0.119161880\) rad (at \(N=20\) with \(N'=N+7\)).
- \(p-p\) scattering phase shift at \(E = 1.0\) MeV converges to \(-0.701504090\) rad (at \(N=20\) with \(N'=N+7\)).
- \(p-p\) scattering phase shift at \(E = 100\) MeV converges to \(0.407499381\) rad (at \(N=20\) with \(N'=N+7\)).
- The optimal overshoot in basis size is \(N' = N+7\); beyond that, no further improvement is observed.
- The Malfliet-Tjon potential parameters used: \(v_1 = 1438.720\) MeV, \(\beta_1 = 3.11\) fm\(^{-1}\), \(v_2 = -626.885\) MeV, \(\beta_2 = 1.55\) fm\(^{-1}\), with \(\hbar^2/m = 41.47\) MeV/amu, reduced mass \(\mu = 1/2\) amu, and Coulomb parameter \(Z = e^2 = 1.44\) MeV fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior method for constructing exact Green's operators for Hamiltonians with Coulomb plus polynomial potentials (Kelbert et al., 2007; Konya, 1997; Papp and Demir, 2006) to general short-range potentials. It improves upon earlier finite-basis approximations for potentials, specifically the Lanczos-type sigma-filtering approach (Revai, 1985; Gyarmati, 1979) and the double-basis method (PhysRevC.36.1275, 1987; PhysRevC.63.057001, 2001), by achieving faster convergence and higher accuracy. The technique enables efficient solution of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for nuclear systems with both Coulomb and short-range interactions, including Faddeev equations, using very small basis sizes.
