# Thermal effects on nuclear matter properties

> A quantitative description of the properties of hot nuclear matter will be needed for the interpretation of the available and forthcoming astrophysical data, providing information on the post merger phase of a neutron star coalescence. We have employed a recently developed theoretical model, based on a phenomenological nuclear Hamiltonian including two- and three-nucleon potentials, to study the temperature dependence of average and single-particle properties of nuclear matter relevant to astrophysical applications. The possibility to represent the results of microscopic calculations using simple and yet physically motivated parametrisations of thermal effects, suitable for use in numerical simulations of astrophysical processes, is also discussed.

## Key claim

The authors show that a parametrisation of thermal effects based on the Sommerfeld expansion (Raithel et al., 2019) reproduces the pressure of hot nuclear matter from a fully microscopic Nuclear Many-Body Theory calculation to within about 6% for densities above nuclear saturation, but the internal energy per baryon shows larger discrepancies (up to ~16% at 50 MeV), particularly at densities around 1.5 times saturation density.

## Method

The authors employed a phenomenological nuclear Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne v6' (AV6P) nucleon-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX (UIX) three-nucleon potential. Using correlated basis functions (CBF) and cluster expansion techniques, they derived a density-dependent effective interaction suitable for first-order perturbative calculations of hot nuclear matter properties at temperatures up to 50 MeV, with thermodynamic consistency enforced through a proper definition of the grand canonical potential.

## Key numbers

- Proton fraction in beta-stable npeμ matter at T=50 MeV shows a minimum at low density, deviating from the monotonic behaviour seen at T=0; at T=50 MeV and low density (ρ ≲ ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³) thermal contributions to chemical potentials scale as δμ_α ∝ T²/ρ_α^(2/3)
- At baryon density ρ = 0.32 fm⁻³ (2ρ₀), the difference between proton spectra at k=0 for T=0 and T=50 MeV reaches ~35.8 MeV for protons and ~17.5 MeV for neutrons
- Neutron effective masses show thermal modifications limited to ~5% over the full temperature (0-50 MeV) and density range considered; proton effective masses at T=50 MeV show ~25% deviation at ρ = ρ₀ and still ≥10% at ρ = 4ρ₀
- The quadratic approximation for proton spectra using zero-temperature effective mass shows ~25% discrepancy at k = k_Fp for T=50 MeV
- The Frozen Correlations Approximation yields proton fractions within ~3% of exact results for temperatures up to 50 MeV over the considered density range
- The Raithel et al. (2019) parametrisation of internal energy per baryon shows maximum relative error of ~16% at T=50 MeV (at ρ ~ 1.5ρ₀) and ~11% at T=30 MeV (at ρ ~ ρ₀)
- The pressure from the Raithel et al. (2019) parametrisation shows relative difference never exceeding ~6% for ρ > ρ₀

## Context

This work extends the microscopic calculations of Benhar (2022) to provide a detailed analysis of thermal effects on single-particle properties (energy spectra, effective masses) and composition of charge-neutral beta-stable matter relevant to neutron star mergers. The authors test the accuracy of the parametrisation proposed by Raithel et al. (2019), which uses Sommerfeld expansion and effective masses from Relativistic Mean Field models, against a Nuclear Many-Body Theory calculation based on the AV6P+UIX Hamiltonian, finding good agreement for pressure but notable discrepancies for internal energy at higher temperatures. This enables assessment of whether simplified thermal treatments (such as the hybrid-EOS approach or the Frozen Correlations Approximation used by Figura et al., 2020, 2021) are adequate for simulations of binary neutron star mergers and proto-neutron star evolution.
