# Geometry of random interactions

> It is argued that spectral features of quantal systems with random interactions can be given a geometric interpretation. This conjecture is investigated in the context of two simple models: a system of randomly interacting d bosons and one of randomly interacting fermions in a j=7/2 shell. In both examples the probability for a given state to become the ground state is shown to be related to a geometric property of a polygon or polyhedron which is entirely determined by particle number, shell size, and symmetry character of the states. Extensions to more general situations are discussed.

# Geometry of random interactions

## Key claim

The probability for a specific quantum state to become the ground state in a system with random two-body interactions can be computed from purely geometric properties of a polygon or polyhedron, provided the Hamiltonian is diagonal in a basis of states with definite seniority and angular momentum. This geometric interpretation is exact for the diagonal case but the authors conjecture it may extend to more general situations.

## Method

The authors consider systems of interacting bosons or fermions where the Hamiltonian matrix is diagonal in the chosen basis, so that eigenenergies are linear combinations of two-body matrix elements G_L with coefficients b^L determined by coefficients of fractional parentage. They represent each state as a point in a vector space spanned by differences of the G_L, then construct the convex polytope whose vertices correspond to states that can become the ground state. For two-dimensional problems (m=2) the ground-state probability at a vertex is related to the interior angle at that vertex; for three-dimensional problems (m=3) it is related to the sum of face angles at that vertex. The authors verify these geometric formulas against numerical Monte Carlo calculations with 20000 random interaction samples.

## Key numbers

- For a system of n=5 d bosons, the state J(ν)=2(5) has analytic ground-state probability 36.19%, compared to 36.59% from Gaussian random sampling
- For n=6 d bosons, the state J(ν)=0(0) has analytic probability 22.32%, numerical 22.13%
- For n=10 d bosons, the state J(ν)=0(0) has analytic probability 20.87%, numerical 20.77%
- For n=18 d bosons, the state J(ν)=0(0) has analytic probability 19.89%, numerical 19.87%
- For four j=7/2 fermions, the state J(ν)=0(0) has analytic probability 18.33%, numerical 18.38%, integral calculation 18.19%
- For four j=7/2 fermions, the state J(ν)=8(2) has analytic probability 24.16%, numerical 23.83%, integral calculation 24.15%
- For four j=7/2 fermions, the states J(ν)=4(2) and 5(4) have zero probability of being ground state in all three methods

## Context

This work extends earlier nuclear shell model studies by Johnson et al. (1998) and Bijker et al. (1999) and interacting boson model studies by Bijker et al. (2000) and Kusnezov (2000), which found a statistical preference for J=0+ ground states with random interactions. The authors provide a geometric foundation for the approximate integral method of Zhao and Arima (2001), showing that procedure is equivalent to projecting the polyhedron onto axes defined by two-body matrix elements. The results enable computation of ground-state probabilities from purely geometric quantities (angles, exterior angles, Gaussian curvature) without numerical diagonalization, and suggest that other spectral correlations may also have geometric interpretations.
