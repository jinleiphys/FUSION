# Mott effect at the chiral phase transition and anomalous J/Psi suppression

> We investigate the in-medium modification of the charmonium break-up processes due to the Mott effect for light (pi) and open-charm (D, D*) mesons at the chiral/deconfinement phase transition. A model calculation for the process J/Psi + pi -> D + \bar D* + h.c. is presented which demonstrates that the Mott effect for the D-mesons leads to a threshold effect in the thermal averaged break-up cross section. This effect is suggested as an explanation of the phenomenon of anomalous J/Psi suppression in the CERN NA50 experiment.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the Mott effect for open-charm mesons (D, D*) at the chiral/deconfinement phase transition leads to a threshold effect in the thermal averaged J/Psi break-up cross section, which could explain the anomalous J/Psi suppression observed in the CERN NA50 experiment. The authors hedge that this is a model calculation demonstrating the effect, not a definitive proof, and that a detailed comparison with NA50 data requires a full heavy-ion collision model.

## Method
The authors calculate the in-medium J/Psi break-up cross section for the process J/Psi + pi -> D + anti-D* + h.c. using a Born collision approximation with thermal Green functions. They model the spectral functions of pions and D-mesons as temperature-dependent Breit-Wigner forms, with widths that turn on above the Mott temperatures (T_Mott_D* = 110 MeV, T_Mott_D = 140 MeV, T_Mott_pi = 150 MeV). The vacuum cross section is taken from a fit to the Barnes-Swanson quark exchange model, and the in-medium cross section is obtained by convoluting this with the spectral functions of the final-state D-mesons.

## Key numbers
- Mott temperatures: T_Mott_D* = 110 MeV, T_Mott_D = 140 MeV, T_Mott_pi = 150 MeV
- Vacuum cross section fit parameters: sigma_0 = 7.5 x 10^9 mb, lambda = 0.9 GeV
- D-meson masses used: M_D* = 2.01 GeV, M_D = 1.87 GeV, giving vacuum threshold s_0 = 15.05 GeV^2
- The thermal averaged break-up cross section is negligible below T_Mott_D* = 110 MeV but becomes comparable to the nuclear absorption cross section of about 3 mb above T_crit ≈ 150 MeV
- The D*-meson at T = 140 MeV has a half width of about 80 MeV

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of J/Psi suppression as a quark-gluon plasma signal (Matsui and Satz, 1986) and kinetic break-up processes by mesons (Vogt, 1988; Mueller and Qiu, 1985). It specifically builds on the Mott effect for light mesons (Blaschke et al., 1993) and generalizes it to heavy mesons (Grigorian and Kalinovsky, 1999). The result provides an input for Glauber-type models (Blaschke, 2000; Wong, 1998; Capella, 2000; Blaizot, 2000; Hufner, 2000) and parton cascade models (Bass, 1999) to compare with NA50 data.
