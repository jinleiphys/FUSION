# Current algebra for a generalized two-site Bose-Hubbard model

> We present a current algebra for a generalized two-site Bose-Hubbard model and use it to get the quantum dynamics of the currents. For different choices of the Hamiltonian parameters we get different currents dynamics. We generalize the Heisenberg equation of motion to write the n-th time derivative of any operator.

## Key claim

The paper derives a current algebra for a generalized two-site Bose-Hubbard model and uses it to obtain the quantum dynamics of the currents, but the dynamics are only solved analytically for the special case where the interaction parameters α and β are set to zero, leaving the general case as a set of coupled nonlinear equations that require further approximation.

## Method

The author writes a generalized two-site Bose-Hubbard Hamiltonian with on-well energies, interaction strengths K_ij, tunnelling amplitudes Ω_ij, and external potentials μ_i. He defines three current operators (tunnelling current Ĵ, imbalance current Î, and coherent correlation tunnelling current T̂) and shows they satisfy an SU(2) algebra. Using a generalization of the Heisenberg equation of motion to write the n-th time derivative of any operator, he computes the second time derivatives of the currents and obtains coupled differential equations for their quantum dynamics.

## Key numbers

- For the special case α=β=0, the current Ĵ oscillates as a simple harmonic oscillator with natural angular frequency ω = √(ω_γ² + 4ω_Ω²) and period T = 2π/√(ω_γ² + 4ω_Ω²).
- Using experimental parameters from Oberthaler (2005) with total particle number N = 1150 and oscillation period T = 40.1 ms, the author obtains angular frequencies ω_Ω = 78.3 rad·Hz and ω_γ = 15 rad·Hz.
- For the same experimental comparison, the trap frequencies are ω_x ≈ 2π × 78.3 rad·Hz, ω_y ≈ 2π × 0.843 × 78.3 rad·Hz, and ω_z ≈ 2π × 1.150 × 78.3 rad·Hz for the tunnelling amplitude Ω.
- The barrier height is V_b ≈ 2πℏ × 3.36 ω_Ω ≈ 2πℏ × 17.53 ω_γ.
- In the limit α=β=γ=0, the currents Î and Ĵ become independent simple harmonic oscillators with ω_Î = ω_Ĵ = 2ω_Ω and period T = π/ω_Ω.

## Context

This work extends the current algebra approach previously applied to the canonical Josephson Hamiltonian (Leggett, 2001) and described in Santos Filho (earlier work) to a generalized two-site Bose-Hubbard model that includes on-well energies and allows different interaction parameters between the two condensates. The model can describe tunnelling between condensates of different atomic species or internal states, and the results enable comparison with experimental observations of Josephson oscillations in Bose-Einstein condensates such as those by Oberthaler (2005). The generalization of the Heisenberg equation to n-th time derivatives provides a formal tool for computing higher-order dynamics of any operator in the Heisenberg picture.
