# Parton Distribution Function with Non-perturbative Renormalization from Lattice QCD

> We present lattice results for the isovector unpolarized parton distribution with nonperturbative RI/MOM-scheme renormalization on the lattice. In the framework of large-momentum effective field theory (LaMET), the full Bjorken-$x$ dependence of a momentum-dependent quasi-distribution is calculated on the lattice and matched to the ordinary lightcone parton distribution at one-loop order, with power corrections included. The important step of RI/MOM renormalization that connects the lattice and continuum matrix elements is detailed in this paper. A few consequences of the results are also addressed here.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD calculation of the isovector unpolarized parton distribution function (PDF) that includes nonperturbative RI/MOM-scheme renormalization, but the final PDF shows that the sea flavor asymmetry is hardly visible, mainly because the rapid increase of the renormalization factor with distance amplifies the statistical errors.

## Method

The authors compute the bare quasi-PDF matrix elements for the isovector unpolarized case using clover valence fermions on 2+1+1 flavor HISQ ensembles with lattice spacing 0.12 fm, then renormalize these matrix elements nonperturbatively in the RI/MOM scheme using momentum-source quark propagators. The renormalized quasi-PDF is Fourier transformed from coordinate space to momentum fraction space, corrected for nucleon mass effects to all orders in M_N^2/P_z^2, and matched to the MS-bar PDF using the one-loop matching kernel calculated in continuum perturbation theory with dimensional regularization.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing a = 0.12 fm, box size L ≈ 3 fm, pion mass m_π ≈ 310 MeV, on a 24^3 × 64 lattice
* Nucleon momenta used: P_z = 0.86 GeV (2π/L) and 1.29 GeV (3π/L), with the smallest momentum 0.43 GeV (1π/L) dropped as less well-behaved
* RI/MOM renormalization scale μ_R^2 = 5.74 GeV^2 for the primary calculation, with additional checks at other scales
* The mixing coefficient between O_γ_z and O_I is about an order of magnitude smaller than the renormalization factor in the large-z region; ignoring mixing changes h_R by ~8% at z=6 and ~15% at z=12, smaller than statistical uncertainties
* Final PDF presented at renormalization scale μ^2 = 5.76 GeV^2 in the MS-bar scheme

## Context

This work extends the LaMET approach for direct PDF computation from lattice QCD, following the proposal of Ji (2013) and earlier lattice calculations by Lin et al. (2014), Alexandrou et al. (2015), and Chen et al. (2016). The key advance is the inclusion of nonperturbative RI/MOM renormalization, which was previously missing and addresses the mismatch between the UV transverse-momentum cutoff scheme used in one-loop matching and the lattice regularization. This enables a more systematic connection between lattice matrix elements and continuum PDFs, and the procedure can be applied to future calculations with larger momenta, finer lattices, and physical pion masses to reduce the remaining systematic uncertainties.
