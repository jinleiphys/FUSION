# The Axial Current in Electromagnetic Interaction

> We discussed the possibility that the charged axial currents of matter fields could be non-conserved in electromagnetic interaction at $O(e) $ order. It means that chiral symmetry is broken explicitly by electromagnetic interaction. This explicit symmetry breaking of chiral symmetry is shown to lead the mass differences between the charged and neutral particles of matter fields.

# The Axial Current in Electromagnetic Interaction

## Key claim
The charged axial currents of matter fields are non-conserved in electromagnetic interaction at O(e) order, meaning chiral symmetry is broken explicitly by electromagnetic interaction. This explicit symmetry breaking is shown to lead to mass differences between charged and neutral particles, but the authors note that the resulting mass relation deviates by about 7% from measured values, indicating that quark-level effects beyond the model are needed.

## Method
The authors start from the linear sigma model Lagrangian for the π-N system and introduce electromagnetic interaction via U(1) gauge-invariant covariant derivatives for both the nucleon and meson sectors. Using the Gell-Mann–Levy method with local chiral transformations, they derive the axial current and its divergence up to O(e) order, showing that the electromagnetic interaction breaks axial current conservation. They then relate this explicit symmetry breaking to mass differences by comparing the chiral symmetry breaking parameter ζ before and after including electromagnetic effects.

## Key numbers
- The mass relation derived is (m_{π^0} / m_{π^±})^2 = M_P / M_N, where M_N and M_P are neutron and proton masses, and m_{π^0} and m_{π^±} are neutral and charged pion masses.
- This relation deviates by about 7% when using measured values.
- The explicit symmetry breaking by electromagnetic interaction occurs at O(e) order, where e is the electromagnetic coupling constant.
- The pion mass from explicit symmetry breaking is given by m_π^2 f_π = (1/2)(m_u + m_d)⟨q̄q⟩, where ⟨q̄q⟩ is the vacuum expectation value of quark pairs.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of chiral symmetry breaking beyond the standard partial conservation of the axial current (PCAC) framework, which attributes explicit symmetry breaking only to finite quark masses. The authors contrast their O(e) effect with the Adler-Bell-Jackiw anomaly (Adler 1969, Bell and Jackiw 1969), which breaks axial current conservation at O(e^2) order. The results are applied to the quenching problem of the induced pseudo-scalar coupling constant in radiative muon capture (Cheon and Cheoun 1998). The paper enables further investigation of electromagnetic contributions to hadron mass differences at the quark level.
