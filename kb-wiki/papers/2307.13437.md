# Multi-step particle emission probabilities in superheavy nuclei at moderate excitation energies

> The probabilities of $xn$-, $pxn$-, and $αxn$-evaporation channels in excited superheavy nuclei were evaluated using the Monte Carlo method. The calculations utilized microscopically determined nuclear level densities and were compared with results obtained from the phenomenological Jackson formula. Effective temperatures derived from the microscopic approach were incorporated into the Jackson formula for different evaporation channels at low and moderate excitation energies. Additionally, an analytical formula was introduced to estimate the average kinetic energy of emitted particles in multi-step processes.

## Key claim

The Jackson analytic formula for multi-step particle emission probabilities, when used with an effective temperature of \(T = T_{CN}/\sqrt{2}\) for neutron channels and \(T = T_{CN}/\sqrt{2.25}\) for charged-particle channels, reproduces microscopic Monte Carlo calculations for superheavy nuclei at excitation energies up to 100 MeV, but the agreement degrades for channels with many emitted particles due to cumulative temperature effects.

## Method

The authors used a Monte Carlo method to simulate multi-step particle evaporation from excited superheavy nuclei, sampling particle kinetic energies from a distribution that includes microscopically calculated nuclear level densities based on a superfluidity model. Single-particle energies and decay thresholds were obtained from a deformed Woods-Saxon potential with Yukawa-plus-exponential macroscopic energy. The resulting probabilities for \(xn\)-, \(pxn\)-, and \(\alpha xn\)-evaporation channels were compared with the phenomenological Jackson formula, using effective temperatures derived from the microscopic calculations.

## Key numbers

- For \(^{288}\)Fl, the Jackson formula with \(C=2\) (i.e., \(T = T_{CN}/\sqrt{2}\)) gives results close to microscopic calculations for \(xn\)-evaporation channels at excitation energies of 10–100 MeV.
- For \(^{296}\)Lv \(\alpha xn\)-channels and \(^{291}\)Mc \(pxn\)-channels, the Jackson formula with \(C=2.25\) (i.e., \(T = T_{CN}/\sqrt{2.25}\)) reproduces microscopic calculations.
- The average kinetic energy of neutrons in the \(5n\)-evaporation channel of \(^{296}\)Lv is described by \(\bar{\varepsilon}_{i}=2T_{i}\) at moderate excitation energies, but the formula \(\bar{\varepsilon}_{i}(xn,U)=2T_{i}\left(1-e^{-\frac{U-B_{xn}}{x}}\right)\) provides good agreement with Monte Carlo calculations over the entire energy range.

## Context

This work extends the use of the Jackson formula (Jackson, 1956) for evaporation probabilities in superheavy nuclei by incorporating microscopically determined nuclear level densities, improving upon earlier phenomenological treatments that assumed a constant effective temperature. The results enable more accurate predictions of survival probabilities and production cross sections for superheavy nuclei synthesized in fusion-evaporation reactions, particularly at moderate excitation energies (\(E_{CN} \ge 50\) MeV) relevant to the Factory of Superheavy Nuclei at Dubna. The study also provides an analytical expression for average kinetic energies of emitted particles that improves on the simple Maxwellian distribution at low excitation energies.
