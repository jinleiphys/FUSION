# A systematic study of exact solutions in second-order conformal hydrodynamics

> In this paper we present the details of our previous work on exact solutions in second-order conformal hydrodynamics together with a number of new solutions found by mapping Minkowski space onto various curved spacetimes such as anti-de Sitter space and hyperbolic space. We analytically show how the solutions of ideal hydrodynamics are modified by the second-order effects including vorticity. We also find novel boost-invariant exact solutions which exist only in second-order hydrodynamics and have an unusual dependence on the proper time.

## Key claim

The paper presents exact analytical solutions to second-order conformal relativistic hydrodynamics, showing that the second-order equations simplify dramatically when the flow velocity has vanishing shear tensor (σ^{μν}=0) and expansion rate (ϑ=0) in a Weyl-transformed frame, allowing solutions that are valid for generic values of the transport coefficients rather than only in perturbative limits.

## Method

The authors exploit conformal symmetry to map Minkowski space onto curved spacetimes (anti-de Sitter space, de Sitter space, hyperbolic space, Einstein static universe) via Weyl transformations, then identify static or comoving solutions in the transformed coordinates. They solve the coupled system of energy-momentum conservation and the second-order Israel-Stewart-type equation for the shear-stress tensor π^{μν} (from Denicol et al. 2012), treating π^{μν} as an independent variable rather than approximating it by the Navier-Stokes term −2ησ^{μν}. The second-order equation simplifies because many terms vanish when the flow in the Weyl frame has σ^{μν}=0 and ϑ=0.

## Key numbers

* Bjorken flow energy density: ε ∝ 1/τ^{4/3} for conformal equation of state p = ε/3.
* Hubble flow (3D spherical expansion) energy density: ε ∝ 1/τ_r^4 in conformal theories, where τ_r = √(t²−r²).
* Gubser flow energy density: ε ∝ (1/τ^{4/3}) × 1/(L⁴ + 2(τ²+x_⊥²)L² + (τ²−x_⊥²)²)^{4/3}, with L the de Sitter radius.
* Rotating Hubble flow energy density: ε ∝ 1/(τ_r² − ω²x_⊥²)² = 1/(t²−r²−ω²x_⊥²)².
* Rotating conformal soliton flow: ε ∝ 1/((L²+(t+r)²)(L²+(t−r)²) − 4ω²L²x_⊥²)², with rotation parameter 0 ≤ ω ≤ 1.
* Conformal soliton flow energy density: ε ∝ (cos ξ + cos σ)⁴ ∝ 1/((L²+(r+t)²)²(L²+(r−t)²)²).

## Context

This work extends the exact solution program of Gubser (2010) for Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics and the semi-analytical Israel-Stewart solutions of Marrochio et al. (2013) by constructing fully analytical second-order solutions that include all terms in the conformal second-order equation of Denicol et al. (2012). The solutions enable testing of numerical hydrodynamic codes and provide physical insight into how second-order effects (including vorticity) modify ideal flows, particularly in the boost-invariant Bjorken-like flows where the shear stress tensor deviates from the Navier-Stokes approximation.
