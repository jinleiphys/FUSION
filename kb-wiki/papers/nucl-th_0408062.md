# Proton-neutron alignment in the yrast states of $^{66}$Ge and $^{68}$Ge

> The $^{66}$Ge and $^{68}$Ge nuclei are studied by means of the shell model with the extended $P+QQ$ Hamiltonian, which succeeds in reproducing experimentally observed energy levels, moments of inertia and other properties. The investigation using the reliable wave-functions predicts T=0, J=9 one-proton-one-neutron ($1p1n$) alignment in the $g_{9/2}$ orbit, at high spins ($14_1^+$, $16_1^+$ and $18_1^+$) in these $N \approx Z$ even-even nuclei. It is shown that a series of the even-$J$ positive-parity yrast states (observed up to $26_1^+$ for $^{68}$Ge) consists of the ground-state band and successive three bands with different types of particle alignments (two-neutron, $1p1n$, two-proton-two-neutron) in the $g_{9/2}$ orbit.

## Key claim

The shell model with the extended $P+QQ$ Hamiltonian predicts a $T=0$, $J=9$ one-proton-one-neutron ($1p1n$) alignment in the $g_{9/2}$ orbit at high-spin yrast states ($14_1^+$, $16_1^+$, $18_1^+$) of the $N \approx Z$ even-even nuclei $^{66}$Ge and $^{68}$Ge, a phenomenon previously discussed only in odd-odd nuclei. The authors qualify that this prediction depends on the condition that the high-spin $g_{9/2}$ orbit is far from the Fermi level and has opposite parity to the $pf$ shell, and that the $T=0$, $J=9$ $pn$ interaction must be stronger than the $T=1$, $J=8$ interaction.

## Method

Large-scale shell model calculations were performed for $^{66}$Ge and $^{68}$Ge using the extended $P+QQ$ Hamiltonian in a restricted configuration space ($p_{3/2}$, $f_{5/2}$, $p_{1/2}$, $g_{9/2}$) with single-particle energies $\varepsilon_{p3/2} = 0.00$, $\varepsilon_{f5/2} = 0.77$, $\varepsilon_{p1/2} = 1.11$, $\varepsilon_{g9/2} = 2.50$ MeV and force strengths $g_0 = 0.262$, $g_2 = 0.0$, $\chi_2 = 0.238$, $\chi_3 = 0.047$ MeV. The wave-functions were analyzed by calculating expectation values of proton and neutron numbers in the $g_{9/2}$ orbit, as well as spin ($J_{g9/2}$) and isospin ($T_{g9/2}$) of nucleons in that orbit, to identify the nature of particle alignments.

## Key numbers

- Single-particle energies used: $\varepsilon_{p3/2} = 0.00$, $\varepsilon_{f5/2} = 0.77$, $\varepsilon_{p1/2} = 1.11$, $\varepsilon_{g9/2} = 2.50$ MeV
- Force strengths: $g_0 = 0.262$, $g_2 = 0.0$, $\chi_2 = 0.238$, $\chi_3 = 0.047$ MeV
- In $^{66}$Ge, the $14_1^+$, $16_1^+$ and $18_1^+$ states show nearly integral numbers $\langle n^\pi _{g9/2} \rangle \approx \langle n^\nu _{g9/2} \rangle \approx 1$, indicating $1p1n$ alignment
- In $^{66}$Ge, the $8_2^+$ state shows $\langle n^\nu _{g9/2} \rangle \approx 2$, indicating $2n$ alignment with $J=8$, $T=1$
- In $^{66}$Ge, the $18_2^+$ state shows $J_{g9/2} \approx 16$ and $T_{g9/2} \approx 0$, indicating $2p2n$ alignment
- Yrast states observed up to $26_1^+$ for $^{68}$Ge

## Context

This work extends the shell model with extended $P+QQ$ interaction previously applied to $^{64}$Ge by Kaneko et al. (previous paper) and provides a detailed analysis of band structure in $^{66}$Ge and $^{68}$Ge, building on experimental data from Stefanova et al. and Ward et al. The results contradict the total Routhian surface (TRS) calculations by Stefanova et al., which suggested simultaneous alignment of two protons and two neutrons at $14_1^+$, and disagree with the particle-rotor model by Lima et al. and the VAMPIR calculation by Petrovici et al. regarding the nature of the backbending in $^{68}$Ge. The findings enable future exploration of $T=0$ $1p1n$ alignment in other $N \approx Z$ even-even nuclei near the Ge isotopes, and provide a benchmark for shell model calculations in heavier $N \approx Z$ nuclei.
