# Deutron Break-Up: Theoretical Description of Polarization Observables in Stapp formalism

> The vector and tensor analyzing powers as well as the polarization of the outgoing proton are calculated for the exclusive deuteron break-up reaction $\vec{d}p \to \vec{p}pn$ at a deuteron beam energy of 2 GeV. Two component covariant formalism of Stapp has been used to have the completely Lorentz invariant model. In addition to the Impulse Approximation the nucleon-nucleon double-scattering and delta-excitation mechanism have been added coherently. Good agreement with the precise data obtained at Saclay is achieved.

## Key claim

The paper claims that good agreement with precise Saclay data for the exclusive deuteron break-up reaction at 2 GeV is achieved when the Impulse Approximation is supplemented coherently with nucleon-nucleon double-scattering and delta-excitation mechanisms, but this agreement depends on the specific choice of the Fermi momentum in the double-scattering loop integral (the "optimal" momentum rather than the maximal virtual mass momentum).

## Method

The author uses the two-component covariant Stapp formalism (M-functions) to construct a completely Lorentz-invariant model of the exclusive reaction \(\vec{d}p \rightarrow \vec{p}pn\) at a deuteron beam energy of 2 GeV. The amplitude includes three coherently added mechanisms: the Impulse Approximation (one-nucleon exchange), nucleon-nucleon double-scattering (with the loop integral approximated by taking NN amplitudes outside the integral at a fixed Fermi momentum), and delta-excitation via virtual pion exchange (using \(\pi d \rightarrow\) NN amplitudes from Laget's one-loop box diagrams with N\(\Delta\) intermediate states). The NN amplitudes are taken from the phase shift analysis of Arndt et al. (1988), and the deuteron wave function uses Paris or Bonn potential parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron beam energy: 2 GeV (fixed throughout the calculation)
- The Impulse Approximation is valid only when the internal momentum of the deuteron does not exceed approximately 200 MeV/c
- The \(\pi\)NN coupling constant used: \(g_\pi \simeq 13.6\)
- The pion form factor cut-off: \(\Lambda = 1.0\) GeV
- The normalization constant for the deuteron S-wave: \(N_S^2 \simeq 0.16\) GeV
- The off-shell form factor for the double-scattering is applied up to 200 MeV; above this energy it is replaced by unity

## Context

This work extends the multiple-scattering approach initiated by Chew and Goldberger (1952) and the double-scattering calculations of Everett (1962), who used spin-dependent NN amplitudes from phase shift analysis. It also builds on the delta-isobar calculations of Yano (1985), who added the delta contribution incoherently, whereas this paper adds it coherently to allow interference with other amplitudes. The model enables a consistent theoretical description of the precise polarization data from Saclay (Ero1994, Exp145) and provides a framework for testing deuteron wave functions at high internal momenta beyond the 200 MeV/c limit where the Impulse Approximation alone fails.
