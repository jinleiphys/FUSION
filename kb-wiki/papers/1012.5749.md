# A unique decay process: beta delayed emission of a proton and a neutron by the $^{11}$Li halo nucleus

> The neutron-rich $^{11}$Li halo nucleus is unique among nuclei with known separation energies by its ability to emit a proton and a neutron in a $β$ decay process. The branching ratio towards this rare decay mode is evaluated within a three-body model for the initial bound state and with Coulomb three-body final scattering states. The branching ratio should be comprised between two extreme cases, i.e.\ a lower bound $6 \times 10^{-12}$ obtained with a pure Coulomb wave and an upper bound $5 \times 10^{-10}$ obtained with a plane wave. A simple model with modified Coulomb waves provides plausible values between between $0.8 \times 10^{-10}$ and $2.2 \times 10^{-10}$ with most probable total energies of the proton and neutron between 0.15 and 0.3 MeV.

## Key claim

The branching ratio for the unique beta-delayed proton-neutron emission by the halo nucleus 11Li is evaluated to be between 6 × 10⁻¹² and 5 × 10⁻¹⁰, with plausible values between 0.8 × 10⁻¹⁰ and 2.2 × 10⁻¹⁰, but the calculation relies on simplified three-body Coulomb wave approximations rather than full three-body continuum wave functions due to computational limitations.

## Method

The authors describe 11Li as a three-body system of ⁹Li plus two neutrons in a hyperspherical harmonics expansion, using the Minnesota nucleon-nucleon interaction and a downscaled ⁹Li-neutron interaction. For the final ⁹Li+n+p continuum states, they approximate the three-body scattering wave functions with Coulomb waves, including a shifted Coulomb wave model with shifts of 10 and 15 fm to simulate the attractive nuclear interaction. The decay probability per time unit is computed from Fermi and Gamow-Teller matrix elements integrated over the final-state phase space, and the branching ratio is obtained using the 11Li half-life of 8.75 ms.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy of ¹¹Li: S₂ₙ = 378 ± 5 keV
- Q value for beta-delayed np emission: Q ≈ 0.404 MeV
- Wave number of emitted nucleons: k < 0.14 fm⁻¹
- Lower bound branching ratio (pure Coulomb wave): 6 × 10⁻¹²
- Upper bound branching ratio (plane wave): 5 × 10⁻¹⁰
- Plausible branching ratio range (shifted Coulomb waves): 0.8 × 10⁻¹⁰ to 2.2 × 10⁻¹⁰
- Most probable total energies of proton and neutron: between 0.15 and 0.3 MeV
- Decay probability per time unit for shifted Coulomb waves: 0.6 × 10⁻⁸ s⁻¹ to 1.8 × 10⁻⁸ s⁻¹
- Effective charge for K=0 Coulomb wave: Z₀₀,₀₀⁰⁺ ≈ 4.83

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body model studies of beta-delayed deuteron emission from ¹¹Li (Baye, Tursunov, and Descouvemont, 2006; Tursunov, Baye, and Descouvemont, 2006) to the even more exotic case of simultaneous proton and neutron emission. The process is unique because ¹¹Li is the only nucleus with known two-neutron separation energy low enough (S₂ₙ < 0.782 MeV) to allow this decay. The predicted branching ratio is orders of magnitude smaller than the observed beta-delayed deuteron emission branching ratio of (1.3 ± 0.13) × 10⁻⁴ (Raabe et al., 2008) and the triton emission branching ratio of (0.93 ± 0.08) × 10⁻⁴ (Matea et al., 2009), meaning that experimental observation would require very high radioactive beam intensities and long measurement times.
