# Fluctuations of collective coordinates and convexity theorems for energy surfaces

> Constrained energy minimizations of a many-body Hamiltonian return energy landscapes e(b) where b=<B> representes the average value(s) of one (or several) collective operator(s), B, in an "optimized" trial state Phi_b, and e = <H> is the average value of the Hamiltonian in this state Phi_b. It is natural to consider the uncertainty, Delta e, given that Phi_b usually belongs to a restricted set of trial states. However, we demonstrate that the uncertainty, Delta b, must also be considered, acknowledging corrections to theoretical models. We also find a link between fluctuations of collective coordinates and convexity properties of energy surfaces.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that constrained energy minimizations of a many-body Hamiltonian, which produce energy surfaces \(e(b)\) as functions of collective coordinate expectation values \(b = \langle B \rangle\), must also account for the quantum uncertainty \(\Delta b\) in the collective coordinate itself; this uncertainty is linked to the convexity properties of the energy surface, and strict minimization can only produce convex \(e(b)\) unless fluctuations are explicitly considered.

## Method

The authors prove general theorems showing that exact ground states of a constrained Hamiltonian \(H - \lambda B\) yield a convex energy surface \(e(b)\) via Legendre transform, and that the same convexity holds for Hartree-Fock and Hartree-Bogoliubov approximations when strict minimization is performed. They then illustrate the consequences using analytical and numerical models: a toy model with random \(4\times4\) matrices, a harmonic oscillator shell model for \(A\) fermions, and a solvable Hartree model of \(N\) identical fermions in a harmonic trap with a local interaction, where the collective operator is the mass-weighted sum of squared radii.

## Key numbers

* For the \(4\times4\) matrix toy model, the ground-state branch of \(e(b)\) is convex with \(b\) ranging from \(b_- = -4.83\) to \(b_+ = 9.60\), and the unconstrained ground state occurs at \(b = -2.69\) with energy \(e = -9.71\).
* In the harmonic oscillator shell model with \(A = (n+1)(n+2)(n+3)/6\) fermions filling shells up to index \(n\), the average squared radius is \(\langle B \rangle = A^{-1} (n+1)(n+2)^2 (n+3)/8\).
* In the Hartree model with \(N=2\) fermions and a bimodal interaction, the uncertainty \(\Delta b\) as a function of \(\langle B \rangle\) is shown to be large enough to vitiate the meaning of the collective coordinate (Fig. 8, no explicit numerical values given for the uncertainty).

## Context

This work extends earlier discussions of constrained Hartree-Fock calculations and corrections to energy surfaces, notably by Giraud, LeTourneux, and Wong (1970s), and addresses issues raised in high-energy \((e,e'p)\) and \((p,2p)\) reactions by Ryckebusch (2011). The paper clarifies that strict minimization in a convex domain of trial states forces convexity of \(e(b)\), contradicting the concave branches often obtained in mean-field methods using quadratic cost functions (e.g., by Frisk, Quentin, Vautherin, and others). It enables a more careful assessment of collective models by highlighting that fluctuations in the collective coordinate must be kept small for the energy surface to be physically meaningful.
