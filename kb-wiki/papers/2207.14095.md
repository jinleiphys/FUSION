# New developments in relativistic hydrodynamics

> We review recent progress in relativistic hydrodynamics, discussing causal and stable first-order hydrodynamics, known as BDNK theories, hydrodynamic attractors, as well as hydrodynamics near the chiral critical point and spin hydrodynamics.

## Key claim

This review paper surveys recent progress in relativistic hydrodynamics, but its central claim is that hydrodynamics can be extended beyond its traditional near-equilibrium, long-wavelength regime to describe far-from-equilibrium systems, chiral symmetry breaking, and spin dynamics, with the caveat that many of these extensions remain under active development and are not yet fully validated against experimental data.

## Method

The paper reviews four distinct research directions: (1) BDNK first-order hydrodynamics, which achieves causality and stability by choosing specific frame and matching conditions for the energy-momentum tensor decomposition; (2) hydrodynamic attractors studied via the Boltzmann equation in the relaxation-time approximation for boost-invariant systems, comparing moment expansions, Chapman-Enskog expansions, and anisotropic hydrodynamics; (3) hydrodynamics near the chiral critical point, including hydro+ and superfluid-like treatments of Goldstone modes; and (4) spin hydrodynamics derived from kinetic theory with spin degrees of freedom and from linear response theory in torsionful backgrounds.

## Key numbers

- The Knudsen number Kn ≡ l_mfp / l_hydro, where l_mfp is the mean free path and l_hydro is a scale characterizing inverse gradients of hydrodynamic fields, defines the regime of validity of standard hydrodynamics.
- For spin hydrodynamics, the spin relaxation rate Γ_s is much smaller than the relaxation rate Γ of other nonhydrodynamic modes in the regime T ≪ m, with Γ_s ∼ (T/m) Γ ≪ Γ, where m is the mass and T is the temperature.
- In hydro+ theory, the original formulation is valid up to k ∼ ξ^{-2} (with k the wave number and ξ the correlation length), while an extension reaches k ∼ ξ^{-1}, i.e., closer to the chiral critical point where the correlation length is larger.
- For nonconformal boost-invariant systems, no early-time attractor exists for the bulk viscous pressure Π nor for the shear stress π, but the longitudinal pressure P_L = P_0 + Π − π has an attractive fixed point at P_L = 0.

## Context

This review extends the long tradition of relativistic hydrodynamics applied to quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions, as established by Heinz (2013) and Gale (2013). It builds on the BDNK formulation of causal first-order hydrodynamics developed by Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, and Kovtun (2017-2020), and on the hydro+ framework for critical fluctuations introduced by Stephanov (2017). The work enables future quantitative comparisons of spin hydrodynamics with Lambda hyperon polarization data from the STAR Collaboration (2017, 2019), and provides a framework for incorporating chiral symmetry breaking into hydrodynamic simulations of the QCD phase diagram.
