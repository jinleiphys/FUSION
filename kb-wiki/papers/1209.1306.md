# Chiral multicritical points driven by isospin density in the Ginzburg-Landau approach

> We study how a chiral tricritical point (TCP) on QCD phase diagram is affected by the imbalance of up and down quark densities (isospin density), using the generalized Ginzburg-Landau (GL) approach. The resulting phase diagram near TCP shows a rich fine structure which includes inhomogeneities of both the chiral and the charged pion condensations. It turns out that the TCP splits into multicritical points.

# Chiral multicritical points driven by isospin density in the Ginzburg-Landau approach

## Key claim

The chiral tricritical point (TCP) in the QCD phase diagram splits into four distinct multicritical points when isospin density (imbalance of up and down quark densities) is introduced, but this result is obtained within a generalized Ginzburg-Landau approach in the chiral limit (zero current quark mass) and restricted to one-dimensional spatial modulations.

## Method

The authors constructed a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy functional expanded to sixth order in the chiral 4-vector order parameters (sigma and pion fields) and their spatial derivatives, adding isospin-dependent terms up to quartic order in the charged pion condensates. They related the GL parameters to quark loop integrals expanded to second order in the isospin chemical potential μ_I, then determined the phase diagram by minimizing the potential for homogeneous condensates and for one-dimensional solitonic structures using Jacobi elliptic functions.

## Key numbers

- The TCP shifts from the origin to (α₂ = 0, α₄ = −μ_I²) in the GL parameter space, labeled TCP′ (Lifshitz tricritical point)
- A bicritical point P appears at (α₂ = 0, α₄ = 0)
- A Lifshitz bicritical point Q appears at (α₂ = 3μ_I⁴/32, α₄ = −3μ_I²/2)
- A critical (end) point R appears at (α₂ = 0.21μ_I⁴, α₄ = −2.22μ_I²)
- The second order critical line connecting TCP′ to Q is characterized by α₂ = 3(α₄ + μ_I²)²/8
- The second order critical line linking TCP′ to R is given by α₂ = 5(α₄ + μ_I²)²/36
- As α₄ → −∞, the first order critical line between HCC and HPC phases asymptotically approaches α₂ → 3μ_I²α₄/4 + μ_I⁴/24 + O(μ_I⁶)

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of the chiral TCP in isospin-symmetric 2-flavor quark matter, previously studied by Nickel (2009) and Abuki (2011), who showed the TCP is replaced by a Lifshitz point when inhomogeneous chiral condensates are considered. The authors incorporate isospin asymmetry, building on model-based work by Klein (2003) and the observation by Son (2000) that charged pion condensation occurs at large isospin density. The results enable mapping of the GL parameter space onto the low-temperature, intermediate-density region of the (T, μ) QCD phase diagram, and suggest the inhomogeneous charged pion condensate in quark matter may smoothly connect to classical pion condensates in nuclear matter as discussed by Migdal (1990).
