# Dilepton production with the SMASH model

> In this work the SMASH model is presented ("Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-Interacting Hadrons"), a next-generation hadronic transport approach, which is designed to describe the non-equilibrium evolution of hadronic matter in heavy-ion collisions. We discuss first dilepton spectra obtained with SMASH in the few-GeV energy range of GSI/FAIR, where the dynamics of hadronic matter is dominated by the production and decay of various resonance states. In particular we show how electromagnetic transition form factors can emerge in a transport picture under the hypothesis of vector-meson dominance.

## Key claim

The SMASH hadronic transport model can reproduce the electromagnetic transition form factor of the ω meson by assuming the ω→π⁰e⁺e⁻ decay proceeds via a two-step ω→πρ→πe⁺e⁻ process under vector-meson dominance, but only if the ω→πρ branching ratio is reduced from 89% to 57% to avoid overshooting the NA60 data.

## Method

The SMASH model solves the Boltzmann equation for a hadron resonance gas, treating particle interactions via resonance excitation and decay. For dilepton production, the ω Dalitz decay ω→π⁰e⁺e⁻ is modeled as a two-step chain ω→πρ→πe⁺e⁻ using a Breit-Wigner spectral function for the ρ meson, a phase-space factor, and a Blatt-Weisskopf barrier factor with angular momentum L=1 and cutoff parameter R=1 fm. Baryonic Dalitz decays (N*→e⁺e⁻N) are also treated as two-step processes via intermediate ρ or ω mesons, with branching ratios taken from the PDG database and from the Bonn-Gatchina analysis.

## Key numbers

- ω meson pole mass: 782 MeV
- Maximum dilepton mass for ω→π⁰e⁺e⁻: m_ω − m_π ≈ 644 MeV
- Best-fit pole approximation parameter for NA60 data: Λ = 668 MeV
- Reduced ω→πρ branching ratio: 57% (scaled down from 89% to match photon-point normalization)
- Blatt-Weisskopf cutoff parameter: R = 1 fm
- Kinetic energy for pp collisions simulated: E_kin = 3.5 GeV

## Context

This work presents first dilepton spectra from the SMASH transport model, extending the hadronic transport approach used in GiBUU (Buss 2011) and earlier work on N* and Δ* electromagnetic decays (Weil 2012, Weil 2014). The ω Dalitz decay treatment is compared to NA60 data (Arnaldi 2009) and to the effective-Lagrangian approach of Terschlüsen (2013). The baryonic ω coupling effects are compared using PDG branching ratios (Agashe 2014) versus the Bonn-Gatchina analysis (Denisenko 2016). The results enable future comparisons with HADES data and further investigation of ω meson production mechanisms in heavy-ion collisions at GSI/FAIR energies.
