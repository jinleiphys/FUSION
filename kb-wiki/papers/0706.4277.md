# On the Wilsonian renormalization group equation for nuclear current operators

> We present the solution to the recently derived Wilsonian renormalization group (RG) equation for nuclear current operators. In order to eliminate the present ambiguity in the RG equation itself, we introduce a new condition specifying the cutoff independence of the five point Green function corresponding to the two-body propagator with current operator insertion. The resulting effective current operator is then shown to obey a modified Ward-Takahashi identity which differs from the usual one, but that nevertheless leads to current conservation.

## Key claim

The paper resolves an ambiguity in the previously derived Wilsonian renormalization group (RG) equation for nuclear current operators by introducing a new condition based on the cutoff independence of the fully off-shell five-point Green function, rather than the on-shell matrix element used by Nakamura and Ando (2006); this new condition makes the RG equation both a necessary and sufficient condition for cutoff independence, whereas the earlier derivation only provided a sufficient condition.

## Method

The authors derive the RG equation for the two-body effective current operator \(\Gamma_\Lambda^\mu\) from the requirement that the five-point Green function \(\eta G \eta \Gamma_\Lambda^\mu \eta G \eta\) be independent of the cutoff \(\Lambda\), where \(\eta\) projects onto states with relative momentum below \(\Lambda\) and \(G\) is the two-body Green function. They then solve this RG equation explicitly, obtaining a solution that differs from the one given by Nakamura and Ando (2006), and verify it both by direct substitution into the RG equation and by direct derivation from the definition of the reduced-space current operator. Finally, they examine current conservation for the resulting effective current operator and derive a modified Ward-Takahashi identity that it satisfies.

## Key numbers

* The solution for the effective current operator \(\Gamma_\Lambda^\mu\) is given by \(\Gamma_\Lambda^\mu = (1 - V_{\bar\Lambda} G_0 \lambda)^{-1} \Gamma_{\bar\Lambda}^\mu (1 - \lambda G_0 V_{\bar\Lambda})^{-1}\), where \(\bar\Lambda > \Lambda\) is the starting cutoff, \(V_{\bar\Lambda}\) is the two-body interaction at cutoff \(\bar\Lambda\), and \(\lambda\) projects onto momenta in the interval \(\Lambda < k < \bar\Lambda\).
* The RG equation for the current operator is \(\partial \Gamma_\Lambda^\mu / \partial \Lambda = V_\Lambda G_0 (\partial \lambda / \partial \Lambda) \Gamma_\Lambda^\mu + \Gamma_\Lambda^\mu (\partial \lambda / \partial \Lambda) G_0 V_\Lambda\), where \(V_\Lambda\) is the effective potential at cutoff \(\Lambda\) and \(G_0 = (E - H_0)^{-1}\) is the free two-body propagator.
* The modified Ward-Takahashi identity for the effective current operator is \(q_\mu \eta \Gamma_\Lambda^\mu \eta = \eta \{G_0^{-1} - V_\Lambda\} \eta \Gamma_0^0 (1 + \lambda G_0 V_\Lambda) \eta - \eta (1 + V_\Lambda G_0 \lambda) \Gamma_0^0 \eta \{G_0^{-1} - V_\Lambda\} \eta\), where \(\Gamma_0^0\) is the zeroth component of the current operator for two non-interacting particles.

## Context

This work extends the Wilsonian RG approach for nuclear effective field theory, previously applied to two-body potentials by Birse (1998) and others, to the case of current operators. It resolves an ambiguity in the RG equation for current operators first derived by Nakamura and Ando (2006), whose solution the authors show differs from the correct one. The unambiguous solution and the modified Ward-Takahashi identity enable consistent construction of gauge-invariant effective current operators in reduced momentum space, which is necessary for practical calculations in nuclear EFT with a finite cutoff.
