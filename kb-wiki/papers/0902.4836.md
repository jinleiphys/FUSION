# Glueballs and statistical mechanics of the gluon plasma

> We study a pure gluon plasma in the context of quasiparticle models, where the plasma is considered as an ideal gas of massive bosons. In order to reproduce SU(3) gauge field lattice data within such a framework, we review briefly the necessity to use a temperature-dependent gluon mass which accounts for color interactions between the gluons near $T_{\text{c}}$ and agrees with perturbative QCD at large temperatures. Consequently, we discuss the thermodynamics of systems with temperature-dependent Hamiltonians and clarify the situation about the possible solutions proposed in the literature to treat consistently those systems. We then focus our attention to two possible formulations which are thermodynamically consistent and we extract the gluon mass from the equation of state obtained in SU(3) lattice QCD. We find that the thermal gluon mass is similar in both statistical formalisms. Finally, an interpretation of the gluon plasma as an ideal gas made of glueballs and gluons is also presented. The glueball mass is consistently computed within a relativistic formalism using a potential obtained from lattice QCD. We find that the gluon plasma might be a glueball-rich medium for $T\lesssim 1.13 T_{\text{c}}$ and suggest that glueballs could be detected in future experiments dedicated to the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a pure gluon plasma can be described as an ideal gas of massive bosons, but only if the gluon mass is temperature-dependent; the authors further propose that the plasma may be a glueball-rich medium for temperatures below approximately 1.13 times the critical temperature, suggesting glueballs could be detected in future quark-gluon plasma experiments.

## Method

The authors first review the failure of constant-mass and momentum-cutoff models to reproduce SU(3) lattice QCD equation-of-state data. They then develop a general statistical-mechanics formalism for systems with temperature-dependent Hamiltonians, deriving a differential equation linking the Lagrange multiplier β to temperature T. Finally, they extract the temperature-dependent gluon mass from lattice data using two thermodynamically consistent formulations and compute glueball masses using a spinless Salpeter equation with a lattice-derived potential.

## Key numbers

- The energy density apparently saturates at about 4.712 for T/Tc ≲ 5, roughly 10% below the Stefan-Boltzmann constant of 5.264.
- The maximum of the interaction measure is located at T/Tc ≈ 1.1 with a value of about 2.6.
- The decay of the interaction measure is approximately 3.344 (T/Tc)^(-2).
- The gluon plasma might be a glueball-rich medium for T ≲ 1.13 Tc.
- The ratio of pressure p/p^SB grows from about 0.85 around Tc to a value compatible with 1 at T/Tc = 3 × 10^7.

## Context

This work extends quasiparticle models of the gluon plasma previously developed by Gorenstein and Yang (1995), Peshier et al. (1996), and Bannur (2007), among others, which introduced temperature-dependent gluon masses to reproduce lattice QCD data from Boyd et al. (1995). The paper clarifies the debate over thermodynamic consistency in such models by deriving a unified formalism from first principles. It enables future experimental searches for glueballs in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC, and provides a framework for incorporating bound-state effects into quasiparticle descriptions of the quark-gluon plasma.
