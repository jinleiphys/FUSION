# Recent developments in neutron-proton scattering with Lattice Effective Field Theory

> In this contribution, we show some recent progress in the study of neutron-proton scattering with Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT). We present preliminary studies of both, the uncertainties in the $np$ phase shifts extracted with NLEFT, and the lattice spacing dependence in the transfer matrix formalism. Such investigations have not been performed before in the literature, and will be relevant for Monte Carlo simulations of nuclear structure with NLEFT.

## Key claim

The paper presents preliminary studies of both the uncertainties in neutron-proton phase shifts extracted with Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT) and the lattice spacing dependence in the transfer matrix formalism, noting that such systematic investigations have not been performed before in the NLEFT literature.

## Method

The authors placed the neutron-proton system in a box of size L^3 with spatial lattice spacing a = 1.97 fm and temporal spacing a_t = 1.32 fm, using the transfer matrix formalism. They performed least squares fits to the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (NPWA) phase shifts and the deuteron binding energy to determine the low-energy constants (LECs) at leading order (LO) and next-to-leading order (NLO), considering different ranges of center-of-mass momenta from p_CM^max = 30 MeV up to 100 MeV. Phase shifts were extracted from discrete energy levels computed on the lattice using the spherical wall method.

## Key numbers

- Spatial lattice spacing: a = 1.97 fm; temporal spacing: a_t = 1.32 fm
- LO fit range: p_CM^max from 30 MeV to 60 MeV for S-waves only
- NLO fit range: p_CM^max from 70 MeV to 100 MeV for all channels including P-waves
- LO scattering length in 1S0 channel: a_{1S0} = -23.31 fm; NLO: -23.79 fm; experimental: -23.76 fm
- LO scattering length in 3S1 channel: a_{3S1} = 5.26 fm; NLO: 5.23 fm; experimental: 5.42 fm
- LO deuteron binding energy: -2.223544 MeV; NLO: -2.224574 MeV; experimental: -2.224575(9) MeV
- LO 1P1 scattering volume: a_{1P1} = 3.79 fm^3; NLO: 2.89 fm^3; phenomenological: 2.81 fm^3
- LO 3P0 scattering volume: a_{3P0} = -3.14 fm^3; NLO: -2.78 fm^3; phenomenological: -2.56 fm^3
- Maximum three-momentum cutoff from spatial discretization: p_max = pi / a

## Context

This work extends the NLEFT formalism previously developed in pioneering calculations by Borasoy et al. (2006) and applied to many-nucleon systems such as the Hoyle state by Epelbaum et al. (2011) and alpha-alpha scattering by Elhatisari et al. (2015). The new systematic uncertainty analysis and lattice spacing dependence study in the transfer matrix formalism, which had not been performed before, will be relevant for future Monte Carlo simulations of nuclear structure with NLEFT and for extending calculations to higher energies and higher partial waves up to N^3LO.
