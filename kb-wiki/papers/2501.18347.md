# Description of di-hadron saturation signals within a universal nuclear parton distribution function approach

> Di-hadron and di-jet correlation measurements in proton-nucleus ($p$+A) and electron--nucleus collisions are widely motivated as sensitive probes of novel, non-linear QCD saturation dynamics in hadrons, which are particularly accessible in the dense nuclear environment at low values of Bjorken-$x$ ($x_\mathrm{A})$. Current measurements at RHIC and the LHC observe a significant suppression in the per-trigger yield at forward rapidities compared to that in proton-proton collisions, nominally consistent with the "mono-jet" production expected in a saturation scenario. However, the width of the azimuthal correlation remains unmodified, in contradiction to the qualitative expectations from this physics picture. I investigate whether the construction of these observables leaves them sensitive to effects from simple nuclear shadowing as captured by, for example, universal nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) analyses. I find that modern nPDF sets, informed by recent precision measurements sensitive to the shadowing of low-$x_\mathrm{A}$ gluon densities in LHC and other data, can describe all or the majority of the di-hadron/jet suppression effects in $p$+A data at both RHIC and the LHC, while giving a natural explanation for why the azimuthal correlation width is unmodified. Notably, this is achieved via a $(x_\mathrm{A},Q^2)$-differential suppression of overall cross-sections only, without requiring additional physics dynamics which alter the inter-event correlations.

# Description of di-hadron saturation signals within a universal nuclear parton distribution function approach

## Key claim
Modern nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) sets, informed by recent precision LHC measurements of low-x_A gluon shadowing, can describe all or the majority of the di-hadron and di-jet suppression effects observed in p+A data at both RHIC and the LHC, while naturally explaining why the azimuthal correlation width remains unmodified, and this is achieved purely via (x_A, Q^2)-differential suppression of overall cross-sections without requiring additional physics dynamics that alter inter-event correlations.

## Method
The author used Monte Carlo event generators (Pythia 8.307 for LHC di-jet simulations and Pythia 6.428 for RHIC di-hadron simulations) matched to the specific kinematics of ATLAS forward di-jet measurements at 5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions and STAR forward di-hadron measurements at 200 GeV p+Au and p+Al collisions. Event-level reweighting was applied using the modification factors from three modern nPDF sets (EPPS21, nCTEQ15, and nNNPDF 3.0) to compare the per-trigger yields and azimuthal correlation widths between p+A and p+p collisions.

## Key numbers
- ATLAS forward di-jet per-trigger yield suppression in p+Pb vs p+p: approximately 15% in data; EPPS21 gives approximately 7% suppression, with data and nPDF uncertainties making all but one kinematic bin compatible
- EPPS21 suppression of inclusive trigger jet yield N_1: approximately 0.89; di-jet coincidence yield N_12: approximately 0.84; resulting per-trigger yield ratio: approximately 0.94
- STAR forward di-hadron correlated yield suppression in p+Au vs p+p: 20-40% depending on kinematic selections; EPPS21 describes approximately half the suppression at lowest p_T^assoc (1-1.5 GeV) and full suppression at higher p_T^assoc selections
- Azimuthal correlation width ratio W_12(p+Pb)/W_12(p+p) for all nPDF sets: very nearly unity, within data uncertainties
- nCTEQ15 shows a modest 5% increase in correlation width for RHIC kinematics, still within data uncertainties

## Context
This work extends the long-standing debate over whether di-hadron and di-jet correlation measurements in p+A collisions can serve as definitive probes of non-linear QCD saturation dynamics (as predicted by the Color Glass Condensate framework of McLerran (1993), Gelis (2010), and others). Early measurements by STAR (2006) and PHENIX (2011) at RHIC showed suppression but no broadening, which contradicted saturation expectations. Previous investigations by Albacete (2010) and Chiu (2011) considered whether contemporary nPDF sets could describe such signatures. The present study uses updated nPDF sets (EPPS21 from Eskola (2021), nCTEQ15 from Kusina (2020), nNNPDF 3.0 from Abdul Khalek (2022)) that incorporate recent LHC precision data, finding that nPDF effects alone can account for most or all of the observed suppression. This finding suggests that di-hadron and di-jet observables defined as per-trigger yields may not provide a robust way to identify saturation phenomena, and motivates the search for multiple corroborating observables and the identification of the saturation scale Q_s at which nPDF universality assumptions break down.
