# Generic strong coupling behavior of Cooper pairs in the surface of superfluid nuclei

> With realistic HFB calculations, using the D1S Gogny force, we reveal a generic behavior of concentration of small sized Cooper pairs (2-3 fm) in the surface of superfluid nuclei. This study confirms and extends previous results given in the literature that use more schematic approaches.

## Key claim
Using realistic HFB calculations with the finite-range D1S Gogny force, the authors demonstrate that the strong concentration of small Cooper pairs (2-3 fm) in the nuclear surface is a generic feature of superfluid nuclei, confirming and extending earlier results obtained with more schematic approaches. The hedge is that the precise location of Cooper pairs depends on shell structure, but the probability distribution \(P(R,r)\), which enters observables, universally shows surface concentration.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the D1S Gogny force with a basis of 15 harmonic oscillator shells. They computed the Cooper-pair probability \(P(R,r) = R^2 r^2 |\kappa(R,r)|^2\) in center-of-mass and relative coordinates, as well as the coherence length \(\xi(R)\), for a range of superfluid nuclei from light (²²O) to heavy (²¹²Pb). They also developed a semiclassical Thomas-Fermi model with a fitted gap function \(\Delta(E) = 4/[1+\exp((E-\mu)/20)]\) MeV to reproduce the HFB-D1S results for ¹²⁰Sn and analyzed parity-projected contributions to understand the role of parity mixing.

## Key numbers
- Cooper pair size (diameter) of 2–3 fm in the nuclear surface, observed across all studied nuclei (²²O, ⁶⁰Ca, ⁶⁰Ni, ¹⁰⁴Sn, ¹²⁰Sn, ²¹²Pb).
- Coherence length \(\xi(R)\) shows pronounced minima at \(\xi \sim 2\)–3 fm for \(R\) at the surface radius.
- The semiclassical model uses a fitted gap function \(\Delta(E) = 4/[1+\exp((E-\mu)/20)]\) (all numbers in MeV) for ¹²⁰Sn.
- The rms diameter of the neutron-neutron virtual S-state in vacuum is 12 fm (cited from Hagino), providing a reference for the strong binding effect.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of single Cooper pair spatial correlations in ¹⁸O (Ibarra et al.), ²⁰⁶Pb and ²¹⁰Pb (Catara et al., Ferreira et al.), and the halo nucleus ¹¹Li (Bertsch, Hagino), as well as the condensate study in ¹¹⁴Sn (Tischler et al.). It confirms and generalizes the findings of Matsuo et al. using a zero-range pairing force and Barranco et al. using the Gogny interaction for neutron-star crusts. The results contradict the older belief (Bohr and Mottelson) that the coherence length of nuclear pairs is of the same order or larger than the nuclear diameter, and instead reveal a much more diverse local picture. This work enables improved interpretation of two-neutron transfer reactions and suggests that nuclear isovector pairing lies in the transition region from BEC to BCS.
