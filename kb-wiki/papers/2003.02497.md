# Neutron-neutron scattering length from $π^+$ photoproduction on the deuteron

> We discuss the possibility of extracting the neutron-neutron scattering length $a_{nn}$ and effective range $r_{nn}$ from cross section data ($d^2σ/dM_{nn}/dΩ_π$), as a function of the $nn$ invariant mass $M_{nn}$, for $π^+$ photoproduction on the deuteron ($γd\to π^+nn$). The analysis is based on a $γd\to π^+nn$ reaction model in which realistic elementary amplitudes for $γp\to π^+n$, $NN\to NN$, and $πN\to πN$ are built in. We show that $M_{nn}$ dependence (lineshape) of a ratio $R_{\rm th}$, $d^2σ/dM_{nn}/dΩ_π$ normalized by $dσ/dΩ_π$ for $γp\toπ^+ n$ and the nucleon momentum distribution inside the deuteron, at the kinematics with $θ_π=0^\circ$ and $E_γ\sim 250$ MeV is particularly useful for extracting $a_{nn}$ and $r_{nn}$ from the corresponding data $R_{\rm exp}$. It is found that $R_{\rm exp}$ with 2% error, resolved into the $M_{nn}$ bin width of 0.04 MeV (corresponding to the $p_π$ bin width of 0.05 MeV$/c$), can determine $a_{nn}$ and $r_{nn}$ with uncertainties of $\pm 0.21$ fm and $\pm 0.06$ fm, respectively, for the case of $a_{nn}=-18.9$ fm and $r_{nn}=2.75$ fm. The requirement of such narrow bin widths indicates that the momenta of the incident photon and the emitted $π^+$ have to be measured with high resolutions. This can be achieved by utilizing virtual photons of very small $Q^2$ from electron scattering at Mainz MAMI facility. The proposed method for determining $a_{nn}$ and $r_{nn}$ from $γd\to π^+ nn$ has a great experimental advantage over the previous one utilizing $π^- d\toγnn$ for being free from the formidable task of controlling the neutron detection efficiency and its uncertainty.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the neutron-neutron scattering length $a_{nn}$ and effective range $r_{nn}$ can be extracted from the $M_{nn}$ lineshape of a ratio $R_{\rm th}$ (the $\gamma d\to\pi^+nn$ cross section normalized by the $\gamma p\to\pi^+n$ cross section and deuteron momentum distribution) at $\theta_\pi=0^\circ$ and $E_\gamma\sim 250$ MeV, provided the data have 2% error and an $M_{nn}$ bin width of 0.04 MeV. This method avoids the systematic uncertainties from neutron detection efficiency that plague the traditional $\pi^- d\to\gamma nn$ approach.

## Method

The authors constructed a $\gamma d\to\pi^+nn$ reaction model using dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) amplitudes for $\gamma p\to\pi^+n$, $\pi N\to\pi N$, and $NN\to NN$ processes, including impulse, $NN$ rescattering, and $\pi N$ rescattering diagrams. They computed the $nn$ invariant mass distribution $d^2\sigma/dM_{nn}/d\Omega_\pi$ and the ratio $R_{\rm th}$ at $E_\gamma=200$--300 MeV and $\theta_\pi=0^\circ$--$45^\circ$, then performed Monte Carlo simulations to estimate the precision with which $a_{nn}$ and $r_{nn}$ could be determined from hypothetical data with varying bin widths and statistical errors.

## Key numbers

* For $a_{nn} = -18.9$ fm and $r_{nn} = 2.75$ fm, $R_{\rm exp}$ data with 2% error and $M_{nn}$ bin width of 0.04 MeV (corresponding to $p_\pi$ bin width of 0.05 MeV/$c$) can determine $a_{nn}$ with uncertainty $\pm 0.21$ fm and $r_{nn}$ with uncertainty $\pm 0.06$ fm.
* The $NN$ off-shell uncertainty on $R_{\rm th}$ is less than 1.5% for $M_{nn}-2m_n \le 10$ MeV and less than 1% for $M_{nn}-2m_n \le 0.5$ MeV.
* The quasi-free peak in the impulse contribution appears at $M_{nn}-2m_n \sim 1.3$ MeV at $E_\gamma = 250$ MeV.
* The subthreshold contribution to the $\gamma d\to\pi^+nn$ cross section is $\sim 2$% and becomes negligible for $E_\gamma \gtap 250$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by Lensky et al. (using chiral perturbation theory near threshold) to higher photon energies (200--300 MeV) where pion detection is experimentally feasible, and provides a controlled theoretical framework for extracting $a_{nn}$ and $r_{nn}$. The method offers an alternative to the traditional $\pi^- d\to\gamma nn$ approach (Chen et al., Howell et al.) which requires detecting low-energy neutrons with poorly known detection efficiencies around 2.4 MeV. The proposed technique uses virtual photons at the Mainz MAMI facility and avoids neutron detection entirely, enabling a cross-check of the $a_{nn}$ value from $nd$ breakup experiments (Huhn et al., Gonz et al., Wits et al.) that may suffer from three-body force uncertainties.
