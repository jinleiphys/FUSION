# On the participant-spectator matter and thermalization of neutron-rich systems in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the participant-spectator matter at the energy of vanishing flow for neutron-rich systems. Our study reveals similar behaviour of articipant-spectator for neutron-rich systems as for stable systems and also points towards nearly mass independence behaviour of participant-spectator matter for neutron-rich systems at the energy of vanishing flow. We also study the thermalization reached in the reactions of neutron-rich systems.

## Key claim

The participant-spectator matter at the energy of vanishing flow (EVF) for neutron-rich systems (N/Z = 1.6 and 2.0) behaves similarly to that of stable systems (N/Z = 1.0), and exhibits a nearly mass-independent behavior across the studied system masses, with only a very slight increase in spectator matter as N/Z increases.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of Ca+Ca, Ni+Ni, Zr+Zr, Sn+Sn, and Xe+Xe series with N/Z ratios of 1.0, 1.6, and 2.0 at impact parameter b/b_max = 0.2-0.4 at the incident energy equal to the balance energy for each system. They employed a soft equation of state with an isospin- and energy-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section reduced by 20% (σ = 0.8 σ_nn^free). Participant matter was defined as all nucleons that experienced at least one collision, while the remaining nucleons were labeled as spectator matter; the time evolution of these quantities was tracked until transverse in-plane flow saturated.

## Key numbers

- Balance energies (in MeV) for the simulated systems: 105 (40Ca+40Ca), 85 (52Ca+52Ca), 73 (60Ca+60Ca); 98 (58Ni+58Ni), 82 (72Ni+72Ni), 72 (84Ni+84Ni); 86 (81Zr+81Zr), 74 (104Zr+104Zr), 67 (120Zr+120Zr); 82 (100Sn+100Sn), 72 (129Sn+129Sn), 64 (150Sn+150Sn); 76 (110Xe+110Xe), 68 (140Xe+140Xe), 61 (162Xe+162Xe).
- Power-law exponent τ for spectator matter as a function of system mass A: -0.12 ± 0.04 (N/Z = 1.0), -0.14 ± 0.06 (N/Z = 1.6), -0.03 ± 0.06 (N/Z = 2.0).
- Power-law exponent τ for participant matter as a function of system mass A: 0.02 ± 0.01 (N/Z = 1.0), 0.02 ± 0.01 (N/Z = 1.6), 0.01 ± 0.01 (N/Z = 2.0).
- Power-law exponent τ for the anisotropy ratio as a function of system mass: 0.07 ± 0.03 (N/Z = 1.0), 0.04 ± 0.008 (N/Z = 1.6), 0.07 ± 0.009 (N/Z = 2.0).
- Saturation time for transverse in-plane flow: about 100 fm/c for lighter colliding nuclei and about 150 fm/c for heavier colliding nuclei.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Sood and Puri (2011) on participant-spectator matter for stable systems at the energy of vanishing flow to neutron-rich systems with N/Z up to 2.0. The results confirm that the nearly mass-independent behavior of participant-spectator matter at balance energy, previously observed for stable systems, also holds for neutron-rich systems, and that the anisotropy ratio shows a similar mass independence. This enables the use of participant-spectator matter as a barometer for studying balance energy in reactions with radioactive ion beams, and provides a baseline for future investigations of isospin effects in heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies.
