# Ginzburg-Landau free energy of crystalline color superconductors: A matrix formalism from solid-state physics

> The Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy of crystalline color superconductors is important for understanding the nature of the phase transition to the normal quark matter and predicting the preferred crystal structure. So far the GL free energy at zero temperature has only been evaluated up to the sixth order in the condensate. To give quantitative reliable predictions we need to evaluate the higher-order terms. In this work, we present a new derivation of the GL free energy by using the discrete Bloch representation of the fermion field. This derivation introduces a simple matrix formalism without any momentum constraint, which may enable us to calculate the GL free energy to arbitrary order by using a computer.

## Key claim

The authors present a new derivation of the Ginzburg-Landau free energy for crystalline color superconductors using the discrete Bloch representation, which introduces a simple matrix formalism without momentum constraints; this may enable calculation to arbitrary order in the condensate using a computer, but the paper does not actually compute any higher-order coefficients beyond those already known up to sixth order.

## Method

The authors reformulate the mean-field free energy of a crystalline color superconductor by expanding the fermion field in Bloch functions rather than plane waves, exploiting the periodicity of the order parameter. This transforms the Hamiltonian into a block-diagonal matrix in the space of reciprocal lattice vectors G, indexed by lattice momentum k within the Brillouin zone. The Ginzburg-Landau expansion is then performed by treating the off-diagonal pairing term as a perturbation, yielding expressions for the GL coefficients as traces over the G-space that involve only matrix multiplication of the structure factor matrices f_G and free propagators, without the momentum-conservation constraints required in the continuous momentum representation.

## Key numbers

- The second-order GL coefficient α is universal for all crystal structures and given analytically by α(δμ,q) = -1 + (δμ/(2q)) ln((q+δμ)/(q-δμ)) - (1/2) ln[Δ₀²/(4(q²-δμ²))], where Δ₀ is the pairing gap at vanishing mismatch.
- Near the conventional second-order phase transition point δμ = δμ₂ with optimal pair momentum q = 1.1997δμ, the coefficient α ≃ (δμ-δμ₂)/δμ₂.
- For the BCC structure (P=6), the GL free energy up to sixth order predicts a strong first-order phase transition at δμ_* ≃ 3.6Δ₀ with gap Δ ≃ 0.8Δ₀.
- For the BCC structure, the quasiclassical approach of Combescot and Mora predicted the upper critical field δμ_* is only 3.7% higher than δμ₂ with Δ ≃ 0.1Δ₀.
- For the FCC structure, the quasiclassical approach found its upper critical field is only 1.6% higher than δμ₂.

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau approach to crystalline color superconductors originally developed by Bowers and Rajagopal (2002), who evaluated GL coefficients up to sixth order for 23 crystal structures and found that the BCC structure (P=6) was favored among those with γ>0. The new matrix formalism addresses the limitation that higher-order terms (beyond sixth order) are needed for quantitative predictions, as demonstrated by the contradiction between the GL prediction of a strong first-order BCC-normal transition and the weak first-order transition found by Combescot and Mora (2004, 2005) using Eilenberger's quasiclassical equation, and by Cao (2015) using direct Hamiltonian diagonalization in Bloch space. The formalism enables future calculation of GL coefficients to arbitrary order, which could determine whether the GL series converges and allow treatment of strong first-order transitions within the GL approach.
