# Neutrino-induced single-pion production: Kinematics and Cross Section

> In the energy range of present and future accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments, single-pion production (SPP) is one of the main contributions to the neutrino-nucleus scattering cross section. For these neutrino energies, ranging from several hundreds of MeV to a few GeV, the SPP on the nucleus is usually described by the reaction in which the incoming lepton couples to one bound nucleon in the nucleus, producing a pion and the knock-out nucleon, along with the residual system and the scattered lepton in the final state. Here, the kinematics and cross section formula for this process are discussed, although the formalism can be applied to other $2\rightarrow4$ processes.

## Key claim

The paper derives the full kinematic and cross-section formalism for neutrino-induced single-pion production (SPP) as a \(2\rightarrow4\) process on a nucleus, but notes that current event generators typically treat it as a \(2\rightarrow3\) process on a free nucleon and then add nuclear effects, which may miss the full spectrum of independent variables.

## Method

The author counts the independent variables for the \(2\rightarrow4\) process (neutrino + nucleus → lepton + pion + nucleon + residual system), starting from 24 variables and reducing to 9 after applying energy-momentum conservation, mass-shell constraints, and fixing incoming kinematics. The cross section is derived from the transition amplitude, first for an off-shell nucleon, then incorporating the residual nucleus via a density-of-states function \(\rho(E_m)\) that accounts for the missing energy \(E_m\). Two integration schemes are presented: one neglecting nuclear recoil and one including it with a recoil factor \(f_{rec}\).

## Key numbers

- The number of independent variables needed to describe the \(2\rightarrow4\) SPP process is 9 (or 8 after removing one azimuthal angle).
- The cross section formula with nuclear recoil includes a recoil factor \(f_{rec} = \left|1 + \frac{E_N}{E_B}\left(1 +\frac{\np_N\cdot(\nk_\pi-\nq)}{p_N^2}\right) \right|\); in the limit \(E_N/E_B \ll 1\), \(f_{rec} \rightarrow 1\) and the no-recoil expression is recovered.
- For some kinematics, the equation for the outgoing nucleon momentum \(p_N\) yields two positive physical solutions, which must be added incoherently if the nucleon is not detected.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of neutrino-nucleus scattering to properly treat SPP as a full \(2\rightarrow4\) reaction, in contrast to the simplified \(2\rightarrow3\) (free nucleon) approach used in current event generators. It builds on prior work by Udias (1993) for center-of-mass corrections and by Gonzalez-Jimenez (2017, 2018) for the leptonic and hadronic tensors. The formalism enables more accurate modeling of SPP in the energy range of accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments (hundreds of MeV to a few GeV), and can be applied to other \(2\rightarrow4\) processes.
