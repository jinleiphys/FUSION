# Extended applicability domain of viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics in (2+1)-D Bjorken flow with transverse expansion

> We perform (2+1)-D simulations of viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics (VAH) under boost-invariant and conformal conditions. Comparing both VAH and traditional viscous hydrodynamics with kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation as the underlying microscopic theory, we show that VAH provides a superior description of the evolution across a wide range of opacity, effectively extending the applicability of hydrodynamic modeling. Our results demonstrate VAH's potential for describing collective flow in small systems where traditional hydrodynamics faces challenges.

## Key claim

Viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics (VAH) provides a superior description of the (2+1)-D Bjorken flow evolution compared to traditional viscous hydrodynamics across a wide range of opacity, effectively extending the applicability of hydrodynamic modeling, though at very high specific shear viscosities (4π η/s ≥ 10) discrepancies emerge in transverse dynamics, with VAH underestimating transverse flow velocity and overestimating momentum anisotropy.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-D simulations of viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics (VAH) under boost-invariant and conformal conditions, comparing the evolution with both traditional viscous hydrodynamics (using the vHLLE code) and kinetic theory in the relaxation-time approximation (RTA) as the underlying microscopic theory. The initial energy density profile was fixed as an average of Pb+Pb collisions in the 30-40% centrality class from a saturation-model based initial state generator, and the opacity parameter γ̂ was varied by changing the specific shear viscosity η/s. The evolution of transverse energy, inverse Reynolds number, average transverse flow velocity, and momentum anisotropy εp were tracked and compared across all three models.

## Key numbers

- For 4π η/s = 0.2, the VAH evolution curve for transverse energy lies almost on top of those from RTA kinetic theory
- For 4π η/s ≤ 5, VAH exhibits excellent agreement with RTA across all observables
- At 4π η/s = 10, VAH maintains 5% accuracy in the final-state value of momentum anisotropy εp
- At 4π η/s = 20, VAH deviation from RTA exceeds 15% for momentum anisotropy εp
- For γ̂ ≳ 2, VAH, scaled VAH, scaled vHLLE and RTA almost perfectly overlap for transverse energy dE⊥/dη
- 4π η/s = 10 corresponds to an opacity of γ̂ ≈ 1

## Context

This work extends previous comparisons between anisotropic hydrodynamics and kinetic theory that were limited to (0+1)-D Bjorken flow or other analytic fluid velocity profiles (Strickland 2015, Chen 2024). It builds on detailed comparisons of (2+1)-D evolution in kinetic theory and hydrodynamics by Ambrus (2022, 2023, 2024) and Werthmann (2023), which showed that in dilute systems transverse expansion develops before hydrodynamization. The results demonstrate VAH's potential for describing collective flow in small systems such as p+p and p+Pb collisions, where traditional hydrodynamics faces challenges, and enable future applications of VAH to systems with lower opacity than typical heavy-ion collisions.
