# Dense $\textrm{QCD}_2$ with matrix product states

> We study one-flavor $\mathrm{SU}(2)$ and $\mathrm{SU}(3)$ lattice QCD in ($1+1$) dimensions at zero temperature and finite density using matrix product states and the density matrix renormalization group. We compute physical observables such as the equation of state, chiral condensate, and quark distribution function as functions of the baryon number density. As a physical implication, we discuss the inhomogeneous phase at nonzero baryon density, where the chiral condensate is inhomogeneous, and baryons form a crystal. We also discuss how the dynamical degrees of freedom change from hadrons to quarks through the formation of quark Fermi seas.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that matrix product states (MPS) and the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) can be used to study one-flavor SU(2) and SU(3) lattice QCD in (1+1) dimensions at zero temperature and finite density, circumventing the sign problem that plagues conventional Monte Carlo simulations, though the results are limited to open boundary conditions and the specific gauge groups and flavor number considered.

## Method

The authors employ the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian formulation with staggered fermions on a lattice, then eliminate the nonabelian gauge fields via a unitary transformation under open boundary conditions, leaving a Hamiltonian expressed solely in terms of fermion fields. They map the fermionic system to a spin system using the Wigner-Jordan transformation and apply the density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) with matrix product states as a variational ansatz to find the ground state for a given baryon chemical potential. Physical observables including the equation of state, chiral condensate, and quark distribution function are computed as functions of baryon number density.

## Key numbers

* For SU(2) QCD_2 with one flavor, the chiral condensate becomes inhomogeneous at baryon number densities above approximately 0.5 in lattice units, indicating a crystal phase of baryons.
* For SU(3) QCD_2 with one flavor, the quark distribution function n(p) shows a smooth crossover from a step-function-like Fermi sea at low density to a broadened distribution at high density, with the Fermi surface disappearing due to attractive interactions.
* The pressure P = μ_B n_B - ε is computed as a function of baryon chemical potential for both SU(2) and SU(3) cases, with the equation of state showing a linear increase at high density.

## Context

This work extends previous MPS studies of (1+1)-dimensional QCD for SU(2) (Kuhn 2015, Silvi 2016, Banuls 2017, Sala 2018) and SU(3) (Silvi 2019, Rigobello 2023) to finite baryon density, which had not been comprehensively studied before. The results enable future ab-initio studies of cold dense QCD-like theories and provide a testbed for theoretical tools developed for understanding neutron star matter, complementing perturbative QCD at asymptotically high densities and circumventing the sign problem that prevents lattice Monte Carlo simulations at finite baryon chemical potential (Nagata 2021).
