# Gibbs-Tolman approach to the curved interface effects in asymmetric nuclei

> We redefine the surface tension coefficient and the symmetry energy for an asymmetric nuclear Fermi-liquid drop with a finite diffuse layer. Considering two-component charged Fermi-liquid drop and following Gibbs-Tolman concept, we introduce the equimolar radius $R_{e}$ of sharp surface droplet at which the surface tension is applied and the radius of tension surface $R_{s}$ (Laplace radius) which provides the minimum of the surface tension coefficient $σ$. We have shown that the nuclear Tolman length $ξ$ is negative and the modulus of $ξ$ growth quadratically with asymmetry parameter $X=(N-Z)/(N+Z)$.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the nuclear Tolman length ξ is negative and its magnitude grows quadratically with the asymmetry parameter X = (N−Z)/(N+Z), meaning curvature effects in the surface tension are enhanced in neutron-rich nuclei; this result depends on the presence of a finite diffuse surface layer, not merely on curvature itself.

## Method

The authors apply the Gibbs-Tolman concept of dividing surfaces to a two-component charged Fermi-liquid drop, introducing both an equimolar radius Re (physical size of a sharp-surface droplet) and a Laplace radius Rs (where surface tension is minimized). They perform direct variational calculations within the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation (ETFA) using Skyrme effective interactions (SkM, SkM*, SLy230b, T6) to obtain nucleon density profiles, total energies, and chemical potentials for nuclei with various mass numbers and asymmetry parameters. From these, they extract the surface tension coefficient σ(Re) as a function of curvature 2/Re and asymmetry X, and determine the Tolman length ξ from the slope of σ versus 2/Re.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric nuclear matter (X=0), the Tolman length ξ₀ ranges from −0.3565 fm (SkM) to −0.3703 fm (SkM*) across the Skyrme forces studied.
- The quadratic coefficient ξ₋ in ξ(X) = ξ₀ + ξ₋ X² ranges from −5.373 fm (SkM) to −2.944 fm (T6).
- The planar surface tension σ₀ (X=0) ranges from 0.9176 MeV·fm⁻² (SkM) to 1.021 MeV·fm⁻² (T6).
- The isovector surface tension coefficient σ₋ ranges from −3.131 MeV·fm⁻² (SLy230b) to −2.413 MeV·fm⁻² (T6).
- The volume symmetry energy bV for SkM force is 26.5 MeV; the surface-to-volume ratio |bS/bV| ranges from 1.17 (T6) to 1.47 (SkM*).
- The equimolar radius Re for A=208 varies with X, and neglecting Coulomb changes Re by less than 0.5%.

## Context

This work extends the Gibbs-Tolman thermodynamic approach, originally developed for classical liquids (Gibbs 1878, Tolman 1949), to asymmetric nuclear droplets with diffuse surfaces. It builds on prior ETFA calculations with Skyrme forces by Kolomietz and Sanzhur (2008, 2010, 2012) and relates the curvature correction to the nuclear matter equation of state parameters such as incompressibility K∞ and symmetry energy. The results enable improved treatment of surface and symmetry energies in the leptodermous A⁻¹/³ expansion for finite nuclei, which is relevant for modeling nuclear multifragmentation and cluster formation in heavy-ion collisions where small, neutron-rich fragments appear.
