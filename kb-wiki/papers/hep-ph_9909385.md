# N->Delta Quadrupole Transition in the Constituent Quark Model

> Information on the intrinsic deformation of the proton can be obtained by studying the electromagnetic p-->Delta+ quadrupole transition. Recent experiments have shown that the electric quadrupole (E2) strength in gamma p --> Delta+ is about 10 times larger than predicted by the simple quark model using only one-body currents. Our analysis provides evidence for the dominance of exchange currents in the N-->Delta quadrupole transition and identifies the physical mechanism leading to the observed E2 strength.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed electric quadrupole (E2) strength in the gamma p -> Delta+ transition, which is about 10 times larger than predicted by simple quark models using only one-body currents, is dominated by two-body exchange currents (gluon and pion exchange) rather than by D-wave admixtures in the nucleon and Delta wave functions; the key hedge is that this conclusion depends on using Siegert's theorem to relate the transverse electric and longitudinal Coulomb multipoles, and on the inclusion of two-body charge operators.

## Method

The authors employ a constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator wave functions that include S- and D-state admixtures, using one-gluon exchange and one-pion exchange potentials between quarks. They calculate the transverse electric quadrupole form factor G_E2(0) for the gamma p -> Delta+ transition using three approaches: (i) the one-body spatial current operator J_[1], (ii) the one-body charge operator rho_[1] combined with Siegert's theorem, and (iii) the full current including two-body exchange currents J_[2] required by current conservation. The two-body charge operator rho_[2] is then evaluated to obtain the double spin flip contribution to the Coulomb quadrupole amplitude.

## Key numbers

* The experimental E2 strength is G^{exp}_{E2} = 0.133(20) (dimensionless), about 10 times larger than early quark model predictions of G_{E2} = 0.003-0.01 using only one-body currents.
* The D-wave probabilities in the nucleon and Delta wave functions lie between 0.2% and 0.4% (Refs. Isg82, Ger82), with one calculation (Dre84) obtaining ~1%.
* The full calculation including both D-waves and exchange currents yields G_{E2}(0) = 0.017 (from rho_[1]) + 0.107 (from rho_[2]) = 0.124, compared to experimental values between 0.133(20) and 0.107(17).
* The parameter-independent prediction for the transition quadrupole moment is Q^{theory}_{p -> Delta+} = -0.083 fm^2, in agreement with Q^{MAMI}_{p -> Delta+} = -0.086(13) fm^2 and Q^{LEGS}_{p -> Delta+} = -0.105(16) fm^2.
* The exchange current contribution to the Delta quadrupole moment is Q^{exc}_{Delta} = r_n^2 e_Delta = -0.119 fm^2 e_Delta, using the measured neutron charge radius.
* The intrinsic quadrupole moment of the nucleon extracted from the collective model is Q_0 = 0.565 fm^2, giving a deformation parameter delta ~ 0.57 and axis ratio a/b ~ 1.79.

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark model calculations by Isgur (1982), Gershtein (1982), and Drechsel (1984) that found large discrepancies between predicted and measured E2 strength, and resolves the violation of the continuity equation noted by Drechsel (1984) and Borkowski (1987). The paper establishes that two-body exchange currents, rather than D-wave admixtures, dominate the N->Delta quadrupole transition, and derives a parameter-independent relation between the neutron charge radius and the transition quadrupole moment (Eq. 13). This enables future calculations of radiative E2 decays of all decuplet baryons (Wagenbrunn, 1998) and provides a framework for understanding intrinsic nucleon deformation in terms of nonvalence quark degrees of freedom.
