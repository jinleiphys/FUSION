# Implications of the isobar run results for chiral magnetic effect in heavy ion collisions

> Chiral magnetic effect (CME) is a macroscopic transport phenomenon induced by quantum anomaly in the presence of chiral imbalance and an external magnetic field. Relativistic heavy ion collisions provide the unique opportunity to look for CME in a non-Abelian plasma, where the chiral imbalance is created by topological transitions similar to those occurring in the Early Universe. The isobar run at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider was proposed as a way to separate the possible CME signal driven by magnetic field from the background. The first blind analysis results from this important experiment have been recently released by the STAR Collaboration. Under the pre-defined assumption of identical background in RuRu and ZrZr, the results are inconsistent with the presence of CME, as well as with all existing theoretical models (whether including CME or not). However the observed difference of backgrounds must be taken into account before any physical conclusion is drawn. In this paper, we show that once the observed difference in hadron multiplicity and collective flow are quantitatively taken into account, the STAR results could be consistent with a finite CME signal contribution of about $(6.8\pm2.6)\%$.

## Key claim

Once the observed differences in hadron multiplicity and collective flow between RuRu and ZrZr collisions are quantitatively accounted for, the STAR isobar run results could be consistent with a finite chiral magnetic effect (CME) signal contribution of about (6.8 ± 2.6)%, contrary to the initial blind analysis conclusion that assumed identical backgrounds.

## Method

The authors used the event-by-event anomalous-viscous fluid dynamics (EBE-AVFD) framework to simulate RuRu and ZrZr collisions, incorporating realistic nuclear structure inputs for the nucleon distributions. They compared the simulated multiplicity distributions and elliptic flow with STAR data, then used the simulations to establish baseline ratios for the scaled correlation observables N_ch × Δγ/v₂ and N_ch × Δδ, accounting for the observed differences in multiplicity and radial flow between the two isobar systems.

## Key numbers

- The CME signal fraction extracted from the Δγ̄ correlator for (20-50)% centrality is f_s = (6.8 ± 2.6)%.
- The measured ratio of scaled Δγ/v₂ between RuRu and ZrZr is R_γ^STAR = 1.011, compared to a baseline of R_γ^baseline = 1.
- The measured ratio of scaled N_ch × Δδ between RuRu and ZrZr is R_δ^STAR = 1.028, compared to a baseline of R_δ^baseline = 1.033.
- The overall measured ratio of Δγ̄ between RuRu and ZrZr is R = 0.9641 ± 0.0037.
- The background ratio parameter λ_b, dominated by multiplicity difference, is estimated as λ_b = +0.0508.
- The signal ratio parameter λ_s, determined from magnetic field simulations, is λ_s = +(0.15 ± 0.05).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical interpretation of the STAR Collaboration's isobar run results (STAR, 2021), which initially found the data inconsistent with CME under the assumption of identical backgrounds in RuRu and ZrZr collisions. The analysis builds on the EBE-AVFD framework developed by Shi et al. (2017-2021) and addresses the puzzle that the measured isobar ratios were less than 1, which no existing theory model predicted. The results enable future post-blinding re-analysis of STAR data and suggest that measurements using the participant-plane/spectator-plane contrast method and event-shape engineering could further verify the CME signal fraction.
