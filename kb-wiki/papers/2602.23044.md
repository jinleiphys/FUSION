# Form factors of the $ρ$ meson from effective field theory and the lattice

> The calculation of resonance form factors in effective field theory as well as on the lattice is a highly challenging task. In a recent paper, we proposed a novel method based on the introduction of a background field and the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to address the problem, and applied it to a toy model. In the present work we use this method for the electromagnetic form factors of the $ρ$-meson. By matching the results to Chiral Perturbation Theory, we provide a first, crude estimate of all three form factors of the $ρ$-meson within the effective field theory. Contact contributions to these form factors turn out to be substantial. A procedure for lattice calculations is outlined, paving the way for an ab initio approach to the problem.

## Key claim

The paper provides a first, crude estimate of all three electromagnetic form factors of the ρ-meson within non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) by matching to Chiral Perturbation Theory, but the authors emphasize that contact contributions to these form factors are substantial and that the results are only a lowest-order estimate, with the lattice procedure outlined but not yet executed.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic effective field theory (NREFT) framework to describe π⁺π⁰ scattering and introduce electromagnetic interactions by gauging the pion Lagrangian, treating the photon to lowest order. They separate the ρ-meson form factor into two contributions: a triangle diagram where the photon couples to the charged pion (expressed in terms of known ππ phase shifts and the pion form factor), and a contact term from local five-point vertices with couplings g₁, g₂, g₃ that are matched to Chiral Perturbation Theory. The finite-volume energy spectrum in the presence of a background field is derived via a modified Lüscher equation, providing the basis for future lattice extractions of the infinite-volume form factors.

## Key numbers

- The ρ-meson appears in the channel with total isospin I=1, where S- and D-waves do not contribute (assuming equal pion masses and neglecting isospin breaking).
- The Z-factor for the ρ-meson resonance is given by Z = 8q_R² / [1 + 2(-q_R²)^(1/2) b], where b is the derivative of q₀ cot δ₁(q₀) evaluated at q₀² = q_R².
- The P-wave scattering length is negative, so σ₁ = -1 in the NREFT Lagrangian.
- The coefficient c^(ℓ) for ℓ=1 (P-wave) is c^(1) = 1/3.

## Context

This work extends the background field method and Feynman-Hellmann theorem approach previously tested on a toy model by Lozano et al. (2022) to the physical case of the ρ-meson. It addresses the long-standing challenge of calculating resonance form factors on the lattice, where previous calculations were largely limited to large quark masses that rendered resonances stable (QCDSF 2008, Alexandrou 2008, Stokes 2019, Hall 2016). The method builds on the Lüscher formalism (Lüscher 1990) and prior work on finite-volume corrections for unstable particles (Hoja 2010, Bernard 2012, Baroni 2018, Briceno 2019, Briceno 2020), and enables future ab initio lattice calculations of resonance electromagnetic form factors.
