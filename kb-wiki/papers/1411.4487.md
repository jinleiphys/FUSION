# Angular dihadron correlations as interplay of elliptic and triangular flows

> The hybrid model HYDJET++, which combines soft and hard components, is employed for the analysis of dihadron angular correlations measured in PbPb collisions at center-of-mass energy sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. The model allows for study both individual contributions and mutual influence of lower flow harmonics, v_2 and v_3, on higher harmonics and dihadron angular correlations. It is shown that the typical structure called ridge in dihadron angular correlations in a broad pseudorapidity range could appear just as interplay of v_2 and v_3. Central, semi-central and semi-peripheral collisions were investigated. Comparison of model results with the experimental data on dihadron angular correlations is presented for different centralities and transverse momenta p_T.

## Key claim

The ridge structure in dihadron angular correlations over a broad pseudorapidity range can appear simply as an interplay of elliptic flow (v2) and triangular flow (v3), without requiring higher-order initial-state fluctuations; this description works well for mid-central collisions where lower-order collective flow dominates over higher harmonics.

## Method

The HYDJET++ hybrid model, which combines a soft thermal component from parametrized hydrodynamics with a hard component from PYTHIA and PYQUEN for jet production and quenching, was used to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV. The elliptic and triangular flow harmonics were introduced at the thermal freeze-out stage via spatial modulation of the freeze-out hypersurface and additional modulation of the flow velocity profile, allowing each harmonic to be switched on or off independently. Two-particle correlation functions C(Delta-eta, Delta-phi) were computed for central, semi-central, and semi-peripheral collisions and compared with experimental data from CMS and ALICE.

## Key numbers

* For centralities up to 35%, the difference between HYDJET++ and CMS correlation peak magnitudes is less than 3%; it increases to 12% in peripheral collisions with centrality 50-60%.
* At transverse momenta pT < 3.5 GeV/c, the Vn coefficients factorize into products of single-particle flow coefficients vn, with collective flow as the dominant correlation source.
* At pT > 3.5 GeV/c, non-flow contributions from the jet component dominate and become negative for odd vn{2PC} coefficients.
* The pentagonal flow v5{2PC} approximately follows the scaling v5 proportional to v2 times v3 at pT <= 2.5 GeV/c across all centralities studied.

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Alver and collaborators (2008) that triangular flow from initial-state fluctuations could explain the ridge structure, and builds on experimental observations of the ridge in Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC by ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS. The HYDJET++ model enables a controlled study by switching off genuine higher-order initial fluctuations, showing that v2 and v3 alone can reproduce the ridge and double-hump structure in mid-central collisions. This enables future work to isolate the role of initial-state fluctuations versus final-state flow interplay in both heavy-ion and small-system collisions (p+Pb and high-multiplicity pp).
