# Entanglement in a holographic Schwinger pair with confinement

> We revisit the entanglement of a Schwinger pair created by external fields of arbitrary strength, using a holographic dual description of QCD. When external fields are strong in comparison to the string tension, the entanglement is geometrically tied to the Einstein-Rosen (ER) bridge in the bulk, and disappears when the pair production is not exponentially suppressed at the boundary. For moderate external fields, the entanglement is shown to follow from the geometrical interplay between the position of the ER bridge and the confining wall in the bulk. We clarify the physical nature of quantum entanglement in pair production, and connect it to the entropy of entanglement between the left- and right-moving fermions. In particular, we clarify the effect of real radiation off the produced particles on quantum entanglement of the pair.

## Key claim

The entanglement entropy of a Schwinger pair in a confining holographic QCD background depends on the interplay between the Einstein-Rosen (ER) bridge and the confining wall, and is reduced by real radiation emitted by the produced particles; the net entanglement entropy for strong fields in the non-confining case remains constant at \(\sqrt{\lambda}/3\) regardless of field strength, contrary to the field-dependent result from the tunneling contribution alone.

## Method

The authors use a bottom-up holographic model of QCD with a hard wall at \(z = z_H\) in AdS\(_5\) to implement confinement, and describe the Schwinger pair production via a string worldsheet connecting the quark and antiquark on the boundary. For weak fields, they employ Mikhailov's light-like surface for uniformly accelerated quarks; for strong fields, they use a worldsheet instanton solution describing a circular Wilson loop. The entanglement entropy is extracted from the free energy via the thermodynamic relation \(S_{EE} = -\partial F / \partial T\), where the Unruh temperature \(T_U = a/(2\pi)\) is identified from the accelerated motion, and the contributions from causal (virtual) and non-causal (real radiation) parts of the worldsheet are separated.

## Key numbers

* Entanglement entropy in non-confining AdS\(_5\) from causal contribution only: \(S_{EE} = \sqrt{\lambda}\) (weak field limit).
* Net entanglement entropy in non-confining AdS\(_5\) including real radiation: \(S_{EE} = \sqrt{\lambda}/3\) (for \(d=4\) bulk dimensions).
* Entanglement entropy in confining walled AdS\(_5\) for weak fields: \(S^{(2)}_{EE} = \sqrt{\lambda} / (2 (a z_H)^2)\), which can be rewritten as \(S^{(2)}_{EE} = \pi \sigma_T M^2 / E^2\).
* Critical electric field for pair production: \(E_c\) defined by \(z_M a_c = z_M E_c / M = 1\).
* Lower bound on electric field in confining case from QCD phenomenology: \(E_{\text{min}} / E_c > 2\sqrt{\lambda} / \pi^2 \approx 4\sqrt{3} / \pi^2 \approx 0.7\), using \(\lambda \approx 12\), \(M/m_\rho \sim 1/2\), and \(\alpha' m_\rho^2 = 1/2\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic treatment of Schwinger pair production from conformal AdS (Jensen 2013, Hubeny 2014, Semenoff 2011) to confining backgrounds with arbitrary field strength, building on the initial study of the holographic Schwinger process in a confining background by Kim (2008). It clarifies the discrepancy between the entanglement entropy results of Jensen (2013) (\(S_{EE} = \sqrt{\lambda}/3\)) and Hubeny (2014) (\(S_{EE} = \sqrt{\lambda}\)) by attributing the difference to the inclusion of real radiation. The results enable phenomenological connections to the Lund model of string fragmentation and to experimental measurements of entanglement in high-energy hadron collisions, as discussed by Kharzeev (2005) and Muller (2022).
