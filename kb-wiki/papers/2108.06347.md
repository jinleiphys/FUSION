# Dijet impact factor in DIS at next-to-leading order in the Color Glass Condensate

> We compute the next-to-leading order impact factor for inclusive dijet production in deeply inelastic electron-nucleus scattering at small $x_{\rm Bj}$. Our computation, performed in the framework of the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory, includes all real and virtual contributions in the gluon shock wave background of all-twist lightlike Wilson line correlators. We demonstrate explicitly that the rapidity evolution of these correlators, to leading logarithmic accuracy, is described by the JIMWLK Hamiltonian. When combined with the next-to-leading order JIMWLK Hamiltonian, our results for the impact factor improve the accuracy of the inclusive dijet cross section to $\mathcal{O}(α_s^2 \ln(x_f/x_{\rm Bj}))$, where $x_f$ is a rapidity factorization scale. These results are an essential ingredient in assessing the discovery potential of inclusive dijets to uncover the physics of gluon saturation at the Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The authors compute the next-to-leading order (NLO) impact factor for inclusive dijet production in deeply inelastic scattering at small Bjorken x, demonstrating that when combined with the NLO JIMWLK Hamiltonian, the inclusive dijet cross-section accuracy improves to O(α_s² ln(x_f/x_Bj)), where x_f is a rapidity factorization scale; this represents a conditional improvement that depends on the separation of rapidity scales.

## Method

The computation is performed within the Color Glass Condensate effective field theory using covariant perturbation theory with momentum-space Feynman rules, where multiple scatterings off the classical gluon shock wave background are resummed into lightlike Wilson line correlators. All real and virtual gluon emission diagrams at NLO are computed, including self-energy and vertex corrections, with the dressed quark and gluon propagators expressed in terms of fundamental and adjoint Wilson lines respectively. The authors demonstrate that all soft, collinear, and ultraviolet divergences cancel at one-loop order, and extract the slow-gluon divergence to show that the rapidity evolution of the correlators is described by the JIMWLK Hamiltonian to leading logarithmic accuracy.

## Key numbers

- The NLO impact factor improves the inclusive dijet cross-section accuracy to O(α_s² ln(x_f/x_Bj)), where x_f is a rapidity factorization scale
- For longitudinally polarized virtual photons, the leading order perturbative factor contains K_0(\(\bar{Q} r_{xy}\)) with \(\bar{Q}^2 = z_q z_{\bar{q}} Q^2\)
- For transversely polarized photons, the leading order perturbative factor contains \(\bar{Q} K_1(\bar{Q} r_{xy})\) with the same \(\bar{Q}^2 = z_q z_{\bar{q}} Q^2\)
- The leading order differential cross-section for longitudinally polarized photons contains the factor \(8 z_q^3 z_{\bar{q}}^3 Q^2 K_0(\bar{Q} r_{xy}) K_0(\bar{Q} r_{x'y'})\)
- The leading order differential cross-section for transversely polarized photons contains the factor \(2 z_q z_{\bar{q}} [z_q^2 + z_{\bar{q}}^2] (\mathbf{r}_{xy} \cdot \mathbf{r}_{x'y'} / (r_{xy} r_{x'y'})) \bar{Q}^2 K_1(\bar{Q} r_{xy}) K_1(\bar{Q} r_{x'y'})\)

## Context

This work extends the leading order computation of inclusive dijet production in DIS by Dominguez (2011) to next-to-leading order within the CGC framework, filling a notable gap in existing NLO impact factor computations for this process. The results are an essential ingredient for assessing the discovery potential of inclusive dijets to uncover gluon saturation physics at the future Electron-Ion Collider, and enable novel studies of the Weizsacker-Williams gluon distribution and the quadrupole correlator of Wilson lines at small x. The computation follows the strategy developed for inclusive photon+dijet production by Roy (2019) but yields significantly more tractable analytic results due to the simpler process considered.
