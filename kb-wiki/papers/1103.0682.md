# Effective pseudopotential for energy density functionals with higher order derivatives

> We derive a zero-range pseudopotential that includes all possible terms up to sixth order in derivatives. Within the Hartree-Fock approximation, it gives the average energy that corresponds to a quasi-local nuclear Energy Density Functional (EDF) built of derivatives of the one-body density matrix up to sixth order. The direct reference of the EDF to the pseudopotential acts as a constraint that divides the number of independent coupling constants of the EDF by two. This allows, e.g., for expressing the isovector part of the functional in terms of the isoscalar part, or vice versa. We also derive the analogous set of constraints for the coupling constants of the EDF that is restricted by spherical, space-inversion, and time-reversal symmetries.

## Key claim

The paper derives a zero-range pseudopotential that includes all possible terms up to sixth order in derivatives, and shows that within the Hartree-Fock approximation the direct reference of the resulting quasi-local Energy Density Functional (EDF) to the pseudopotential acts as a constraint that divides the number of independent coupling constants of the EDF by two, allowing for expressing the isovector part of the functional in terms of the isoscalar part, or vice versa.

## Method

The authors constructed a zero-range pseudopotential containing all possible derivative terms up to sixth order and computed the average energy in the Hartree-Fock approximation to obtain a quasi-local nuclear EDF built from derivatives of the one-body density matrix up to sixth order. They then derived the linear relationships between the EDF coupling constants and the pseudopotential parameters, and provided the analogous set of constraints for the coupling constants restricted by spherical, space-inversion, and time-reversal symmetries. The results were made available in TeX, Mathematica, and Fortran formats as supplemental material.

## Key numbers

* 90 fourth-order (isoscalar and isovector) EDF coupling constants are expressed by 15 fourth-order pseudopotential parameters.
* 258 sixth-order EDF coupling constants are expressed by 26 sixth-order pseudopotential parameters.
* The fourth-order pseudopotential parameter set includes 15 parameters: C_{00,00}^{40}, C_{00,20}^{40}, C_{00,22}^{42}, C_{11,00}^{31}, C_{11,11}^{31}, C_{11,20}^{31}, C_{11,22}^{31}, C_{11,22}^{33}, C_{20,00}^{20}, C_{20,20}^{20}, C_{20,22}^{22}, C_{22,00}^{22}, C_{22,11}^{22}, C_{22,20}^{22}, C_{22,22}^{22}.
* The sixth-order pseudopotential parameter set includes 26 parameters: C_{00,00}^{60}, C_{00,20}^{60}, C_{00,22}^{62}, C_{11,00}^{51}, C_{11,11}^{51}, C_{11,20}^{51}, C_{11,22}^{51}, C_{11,22}^{53}, C_{20,00}^{40}, C_{20,20}^{40}, C_{20,22}^{42}, C_{22,00}^{42}, C_{22,11}^{42}, C_{22,20}^{42}, C_{22,22}^{40}, C_{22,22}^{42}, C_{22,22}^{44}, C_{31,00}^{31}, C_{31,11}^{31}, C_{31,20}^{31}, C_{31,22}^{31}, C_{31,22}^{33}, C_{33,00}^{33}, C_{33,11}^{33}, C_{33,20}^{33}, C_{33,22}^{33}.
* For the spherical symmetry-restricted case, 13 fourth-order pseudopotential parameters and 22 sixth-order pseudopotential parameters are used.

## Context

This work extends the program of constructing nuclear energy density functionals from a pseudopotential approach, which provides a systematic way to constrain the functional form and reduce the number of independent coupling constants. The derived constraints divide the number of independent coupling constants by two compared to a purely phenomenological EDF, enabling connections between isoscalar and isovector parts of the functional. The results enable future fitting of the pseudopotential parameters to nuclear properties, and the provided supplemental material in multiple formats facilitates implementation in nuclear structure calculations.
