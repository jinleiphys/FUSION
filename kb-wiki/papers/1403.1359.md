# Symmetry energy effects on isovector properties of neutron rich nuclei with a Thomas-Fermi approach

> We employ a variational method, in the framework of the Thomas-Fermi approximation, to study the effect of the symmetry energy on the neutron skin thickness and the symmetry energy coefficients of various neutron rich nuclei. We concentrate our interest on $^{208}$Pb, $^{124}$Sn, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{48}$Ca, although the method can be applied in the totality of medium and heavy neutron rich nuclei. Our approach has the advantage that the isospin asymmetry function $α(r)$, which is the key quantity to calculate isovector properties of various nuclei, is directly related with the symmetry energy as a consequence of the variational principle. Moreover, the Coulomb interaction is included in a self-consistent way and its effects can be separated easily from the nucleon-nucleon interaction. We confirm, both qualitatively and quantitatively, the strong dependence of the symmetry energy on the various isovector properties for the relevant nuclei, using possible constraints between the slope and the value of the symmetry energy at the saturation density.

## Key claim

The authors confirm a strong dependence of neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy coefficients on the symmetry energy slope parameter L, but note that their variational approach does not impose additional constraints to reproduce experimental binding energies or charge radii, meaning the results represent a systematic study over a large range of L values rather than a precise fit to data.

## Method

The authors employ a variational method within the Thomas-Fermi approximation, using a Fermi-type trial function for the total nuclear density and solving the Euler-Lagrange equations to obtain the isospin asymmetry function α(r) directly from the symmetry energy. The symmetry energy is parameterized as S(ρ)=J(ρ/ρ₀)^γ, with the slope L=3γJ, and the Coulomb interaction is included self-consistently. The total energy is minimized to compute neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy coefficients for ²⁰⁸Pb, ¹²⁴Sn, ⁹⁰Zr, and ⁴⁸Ca.

## Key numbers

- For ²⁰⁸Pb with J=30 MeV and L ranging from 10 to 100 MeV, the neutron skin thickness R_skin and asymmetry coefficient a_A are computed (Table I values not fully shown in truncated text).
- An approximate linear relation from Centelles et al. (2009) is cited: R_skin (fm) = 0.101 + 0.00147 L (MeV) for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The PREX experiment determined R_skin = 0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18} fm for ²⁰⁸Pb.
- The set J=34 MeV and L=65 MeV reproduces empirical a_A values for all four nuclei studied.
- The inequality 4.13 ≤ ΔL/ΔJ ≤ 5.18 holds approximately, meaning a 1 MeV change in J produces a 4.13 to 5.18 MeV change in L.
- For J and L in the region 34_{-0.2}^{+0.2} MeV and 65_{-1}^{+1} MeV the accuracy is high.
- The neutron skin dependence on asymmetry parameter I=(N-Z)/A follows R_skin = a + b I with a in [-0.02, 0.045] and b in [1.31, 1.45], compared to the experimental relation R_skin = (1.01±0.15)I + (-0.04±0.03) from Trzcinska et al. (2001).

## Context

This work extends the Thomas-Fermi approach of Brueckner et al. (1968, 1969, 1971) to systematically study symmetry energy effects on isovector properties, using a parameterization that relates the slope L and value J of the symmetry energy. The results are compared to the linear correlation between R_skin and L established by Centelles et al. (2009) using non-relativistic and relativistic models, and to constraints from heavy-ion collisions and nuclear structure observables reviewed by Horowitz et al. (2014). The approach enables future constraints on L and J from upcoming PREX-II and CREX experiments, which promise neutron skin measurements with ±0.06 fm accuracy for ²⁰⁸Pb and ±0.02 fm for ⁴⁸Ca.
