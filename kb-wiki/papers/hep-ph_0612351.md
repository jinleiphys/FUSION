# Generalized parton distributions of hadrons with composite constituents

> A method is described for calculating the Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) of spin 1/2 hadrons made of composite constituents, in an Impulse Approximation framework. GPDs are obtained from the convolution between the light cone non-diagonal momentum distribution of the hadron and the GPD of the constituent. DIS structure functions and electromagnetic form factors are consistently recovered with the proposed formalism. Results are presented for the nucleon and for the $^3$He nucleus. For a nucleon assumed to be made of composite constituent quarks, the proposed scheme permits to study the so-called Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage (ERBL) region, difficult to access in model calculations. Results are presented for both helicity-independent and helicity-dependent GPDs. For $^3$He, the calculation has been performed by evaluating a non-diagonal spectral function within the AV18 interaction. It turns out that a measurement of GPDs for $^3$He could shed new light on the short-range nuclear structure at the quark level.

## Key claim

A method is described for calculating Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) of spin-1/2 hadrons made of composite constituents within an Impulse Approximation framework, where the formalism is valid for small values of $\xi^2$ and $\Delta^2 \ll Q^2, M^2$; the approach permits access to the Efremov-Radyushkin-Brodsky-Lepage (ERBL) region for the nucleon and reveals that nuclear effects in $^3$He depend on the choice of the NN potential, unlike in the forward case.

## Method

The author calculates GPDs via a convolution between the light-cone non-diagonal momentum distribution of the hadron and the GPD of its constituent, using an Impulse Approximation expression valid for small $\xi^2$ and $\Delta^2 \ll Q^2, M^2$. For the nucleon, the constituent quark spectral function is approximated by a momentum distribution from the Isgur and Karl model, convoluted with constituent quark GPDs modeled using the double distribution representation and a phenomenological constituent quark form factor at the hadronic scale $\mu_0^2 = 0.34$ GeV$^2$. For $^3$He, a non-diagonal spectral function is evaluated using realistic wave functions from the AV18 interaction including Coulomb repulsion between protons, with the nucleon GPD input modeled via the double distribution representation.

## Key numbers

- The hadronic scale used for the nucleon calculation is $\mu_0^2 = 0.34$ GeV$^2$.
- For $^3$He, the removal energy $E_{min} = |E_{^3He}| - |E_{^2H}| = 5.5$ MeV.
- Nuclear effects in $^3$He for $x_3 \leq 0.7$ are as large as 15% at most, shown for $\Delta^2 = -0.25$ GeV$^2$ and $\xi_3 = 3\xi$ values of 0, 0.1, and 0.2.
- The $x$ integral of the nuclear GPD for $^3$He gives a good description of form factor data in the region $-\Delta^2 \leq 0.25$ GeV$^2$.
- Results for the nucleon are shown at $\Delta^2 = -0.15$ GeV$^2$ for the allowed values of $\xi$.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous calculations for the nucleon (Scopetta, 2003, 2004) and for $^3$He (Scopetta, 2005) to new kinematical regions, including higher momentum transfer values. The approach contrasts with another model that adds a meson cloud to a light-front quark model scenario (Boffi et al., 2003; Pasquini and Boffi, 2006) to access the ERBL region. The results enable feasibility studies for Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering experiments on $^3$He, which could serve as an effective polarized free neutron target (Friar et al., 1990) and allow investigation of short-range nuclear structure at the quark level, as well as the quark orbital angular momentum contribution to the free neutron spin.
