# Investigating in-medium properties of the $ω$ meson via the $ω\rightarrowπ^0γ$ decay

> We investigate the feasibility of studying in-medium properties of the $ω$ meson in photoproduction experiments via the decay $ω\rightarrowπ^0γ$. We use the GiBUU transport model to compare different methods of obtaining in-medium information, such as the invariant mass spectrum, transparency ratio, excitation function and momentum spectrum. We show that the final-state interaction of the pion poses a major obstacle for the interpretation of the invariant mass spectrum. The other three observables turn out to be fairly independent of final-state interactions and thus can give access to the $ω$'s in-medium properties.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the hadronic final-state interaction of the pion from the ω→π⁰γ decay poses a major obstacle for interpreting the invariant mass spectrum, making this channel unsuitable for in-medium studies; however, the transparency ratio, excitation function, and momentum spectrum are fairly independent of final-state interactions and can give access to the ω meson's in-medium properties.

## Method

The authors used the GiBUU hadronic transport model to simulate ω meson photoproduction on nuclei (C and Nb) over the photon energy range 1.2–2.2 GeV, with a 1/Eγ Bremsstrahlung weighting. They implemented two in-medium modifications, a density-dependent mass shift (α=0.16) and a collisional broadening (Γ₀=150 MeV), and compared four observables: the π⁰γ invariant mass spectrum, the transparency ratio, the excitation function, and the ω momentum spectrum. The pion final-state interaction was treated via elastic and inelastic πN scattering channels within the transport model.

## Key numbers

- The ω meson vacuum mass is m₀=782 MeV, and nuclear saturation density is ρ₀=0.168 fm⁻³.
- The mass-shift parameter α=0.16 corresponds to a 16% mass shift at ρ=ρ₀.
- The collisional width parameter Γ₀=150 MeV was chosen to match CBELSA/TAPS transparency-ratio data with a cross-section modification factor K=2.
- The ω meson vacuum lifetime is τ≈23.2 fm/c.
- The π⁰γ branching ratio is 8.3%, roughly three orders of magnitude above the dilepton channel.
- The free ω photoproduction threshold on a proton is Eγ=1.108 GeV.
- The CBELSA/TAPS detector mass resolution is 19 MeV (Gaussian folding).
- In the momentum spectrum, scenarios with mass shift show an average momentum shift of about 25–50 MeV relative to the vacuum case.
- About 30% of all ω→π⁰γ decays occur in the medium (at densities above 0.1ρ₀).

## Context

This work extends earlier transport studies by Muehlich et al. (2006) and Messchendorp et al. (2001) on ω in-medium effects, and directly addresses the CBELSA/TAPS experimental program (Trnka 2005, Kotulla 2008, Nanova 2010). The paper contradicts the earlier assumption that the π⁰γ invariant mass spectrum can reliably reveal an in-medium mass shift, showing instead that pion FSI cuts away most in-medium decays. It proposes the momentum spectrum as a new method to detect a mass shift, and notes that preliminary data (Metag 2011) seem to disfavor mass-shift scenarios. The results enable future experiments to focus on the excitation function and momentum spectrum rather than the invariant mass lineshape for studying ω in-medium mass modifications.
