# Tensor Coupling and Vector Mesons in Dense Nuclear Matter

> The effects of magnetic interaction between vector mesons and nucleons on the propagation (mass and width) of the $ρ$-meson in particular moving through very dense nuclear matter is studied and the modifications, qualitative and quantitative, due to the relevant collective modes (zero-sound and plasma frequencies) of the medium discussed. It is shown that the $ρ$-mesons produced in high-energy nuclear collisions will be longitudinally polarized in the region of sufficiently dense nuclear matter, in the presence of such an interaction.

# Tensor Coupling and Vector Mesons in Dense Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The magnetic (tensor) coupling between vector mesons and nucleons causes the transverse mode of the ρ-meson to become unstable in sufficiently dense nuclear matter, leading to the prediction that ρ-mesons produced in high-energy nuclear collisions will be entirely longitudinally polarized in that regime. This conclusion depends on the long-wavelength (collective mode) approximation where the meson wavelength exceeds the interparticle spacing, and on the use of a mean-field description valid at very high densities.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium polarization tensor Πμν for vector mesons (ρ and ω) from a Lagrangian that includes both vector and tensor (magnetic) coupling terms, using the relativistic generalization of the Matsubara-Galitskii-Migdal many-body formalism as developed by Chin (1977). The density-dependent part of the polarization is evaluated in the long-wavelength limit (q << kF) by integrating over the Fermi sphere, and the Dyson equation is solved in the random phase approximation (RPA) to obtain the dielectric functions and dispersion relations for longitudinal and transverse collective modes.

## Key numbers

- The plasma frequency Ω is given by Ω² = (1/3)(g²_V/π²)(k³_F/ε_F), where g_V is the vector coupling constant, k_F is the Fermi momentum, and ε_F is the Fermi energy.
- In the static limit (|q| → 0), the longitudinal mesonic branch has q²_0L = (m² + Ω²) / (1 + Ω²κ²/4M²), where κ is the tensor coupling parameter and M is the nucleon mass.
- In the static limit, the transverse mesonic branch has q²_0T = (m² + Ω²) / (1 - Ω²κ²/8M²).
- For the zero-sound branch (longitudinal, low-lying), the dispersion relation in the limit |q| → 0 gives q²_0 = Ω²|q|² / (Ω² + m² + (1 + Ω²κ²/2M²)|q|²).

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretic treatment of vector mesons in dense nuclear matter by Chin (1977), who considered only vector coupling, to include the tensor (magnetic) coupling between vector mesons and nucleons. The tensor coupling is known from the Bonn group's nucleon-nucleon interaction fits and from vector meson dominance of nucleon form factors to be more important for the ρ meson than for the ω meson due to the larger isovector magnetic moment. The results show that the transverse and longitudinal modes split even in the static limit, unlike in Chin's pure-vector case, and that the transverse mode becomes unstable at high density, implying that ρ-mesons produced in such environments will be longitudinally polarized, a prediction testable at facilities like CEBAF.
