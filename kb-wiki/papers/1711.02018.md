# Measurements of spin alignment of vector mesons and global polarization of hyperons with ALICE at the LHC

> We present the measurements related to global polarization of $Λ$ hyperons and spin alignment of $\mathrm{K^{*0}}~$ vector mesons at mid-rapidity for Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}~$= 2.76 TeV using the ALICE detector at the LHC. The global polarization measurements are carried out with respect to the first order event plane while the spin alignment measurements are carried out with respect to the production plane. No global polarization signal for $Λ$ is observed for 5-15\% and 15-50\% central Pb-Pb collisions. The spin density matrix element $ρ_{00}$ is found to have values slightly below 1/3 at low transverse momentum ($p_{\mathrm{T}}$) for $\mathrm{K^{*0}}~$ mesons, while it is consistent with 1/3 (no spin alignment) at higher $p_{\mathrm{T}}$. No spin alignment is observed for $\mathrm{K^{*0}}~$ in pp collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 13 TeV and for the spin zero hadron $\mathrm{K^{0}_{S}}~$ in 20-40\% Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}~$ = 2.76 TeV.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ for K*⁰ vector mesons in Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV is slightly below 1/3 at low transverse momentum (below 1.8 GeV/c), but the deviation is modest (about 2.5 standard deviations for the lowest pT bin) and consistent with no spin alignment at higher pT; no global polarization of Λ hyperons is observed within the statistical and systematic uncertainties.

## Method

The global polarization of Λ hyperons was measured via the angular distribution of their decay daughters (p and π) with respect to the first-order event plane reconstructed using zero degree calorimeters, using 49 million minimum-bias Pb-Pb events at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. The spin alignment of K*⁰ vector mesons was measured via the angular distribution of their decay daughters (K and π) with respect to the production plane, using 14 million minimum-bias Pb-Pb events at √sNN = 2.76 TeV and 43 million minimum-bias pp events at √s = 13 TeV; yields were extracted from invariant mass distributions after mixed-event background subtraction and corrected for efficiency and acceptance using Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key numbers

- Λ global polarization in 5-15% central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: PH(Λ) = -0.01 ± 0.13 (stat) ± 0.04 (syst), consistent with zero
- Λ global polarization in 15-50% central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV: PH(Λ) = -0.08 ± 0.10 (stat) ± 0.04 (syst), consistent with zero
- Antihyperon global polarization in 5-15% central Pb-Pb collisions: PH(Λ̅) = -0.09 ± 0.13 (stat) ± 0.08 (syst)
- Antihyperon global polarization in 15-50% central Pb-Pb collisions: PH(Λ̅) = 0.05 ± 0.10 (stat) ± 0.03 (syst)
- ρ₀₀ for K*⁰ in 10-50% central Pb-Pb collisions at 0.4 ≤ pT < 1.2 GeV/c is about 2.5 standard deviations below 1/3
- ρ₀₀ for K*⁰ in 10-50% central Pb-Pb collisions at 1.2 ≤ pT < 1.8 GeV/c is about 1.4 standard deviations below 1/3
- ρ₀₀ for K*⁰ in pp collisions at √s = 13 TeV is consistent with 1/3 (no spin alignment) across the full pT range studied
- ρ₀₀ for K⁰_S (spin zero) in 20-40% central Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV is consistent with 1/3 across the full pT range studied

## Context

This work presents the first measurements at LHC energies of spin alignment for vector mesons (K*⁰) and global polarization of Λ hyperons in Pb-Pb collisions, extending studies previously performed at RHIC by the STAR collaboration (STAR 2017). The results are consistent with theoretical expectations of large magnetic fields and angular momentum in the initial stages of heavy-ion collisions (Kharzeev 2007, Becattini 2007), though no significant polarization signal is observed at LHC energies. The analysis enables future higher-precision studies with the Pb-Pb data set at √sNN = 5.02 TeV and ongoing investigations of centrality dependence and comparisons between production-plane and reaction-plane measurements.
