# Proposal for exotic-hadron search by fragmentation functions

> It is proposed that fragmentation functions should be used to identify exotic hadrons. As an example, fragmentation functions of the scalar meson f_0(980) are investigated. It is pointed out that the second moments and functional forms of the u- and s-quark fragmentation functions can distinguish the tetraquark structure from $q\bar q$. By the global analysis of f_0 (980) production data in electron-positron annihilation, its fragmentation functions and their uncertainties are determined. It is found that the current available data are not sufficient to determine its internal structure, while precise data in future should be able to identify exotic quark configurations.

## Key claim

Fragmentation functions of the scalar meson f_0(980) can distinguish between a tetraquark and a qqbar internal structure through the second moments and functional forms of the u- and s-quark fragmentation functions, but the currently available e+e- annihilation data are not precise enough to determine which configuration is correct.

## Method

The authors performed a global next-to-leading-order chi-squared analysis of the 23 available data points for f_0(980) production in e+e- annihilation, parametrizing the u-, s-, g-, c-, and b-quark fragmentation functions at an initial scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2 with a standard functional form N_i z^alpha_i (1-z)^beta_i. Uncertainties were estimated using the Hessian method with a confidence level corresponding to one sigma. Three parameters (beta_g=1, alpha_u=10, alpha_s=10) were fixed due to the limited data, leaving twelve free parameters; the minimum chi-squared per degree of freedom was 0.907.

## Key numbers

- The second moment ratio M_u/M_s = 0.43, with an uncertainty of +/- 6.73, making it impossible to distinguish between configurations at the 50% level.
- Individual second moments: M_u = 0.0012 +/- 0.0107, M_s = 0.0027 +/- 0.0183, M_g = 0.0090 +/- 0.0046 at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- The experimental two-photon decay width of f_0(980) reported by the Belle collaboration is 0.205 (+0.095/-0.083 stat, +0.147/-0.117 syst) keV, compared to model calculations of 1.3-1.8 keV for the nonstrange qqbar picture.
- The strong decay width of f_0(980) into pi pi is experimentally 40-100 MeV, while theoretical calculations for an ordinary nonstrange qqbar configuration give 500-1000 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using fragmentation functions to probe hadron structure, building on the global analysis framework of Hirai et al. (2007) for pion, kaon, and proton fragmentation functions. It addresses the long-standing puzzle of the f_0(980) internal structure, which has been studied through phi radiative decays at VEPP-2M and DAPhiNE, two-photon processes by the Belle collaboration, and theoretical proposals using elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions. The paper enables future experimental programs at Belle and other facilities to determine exotic hadron configurations by measuring flavor-tagged fragmentation functions, particularly through semi-inclusive lepton-proton scattering to separate up- and strange-quark contributions.
