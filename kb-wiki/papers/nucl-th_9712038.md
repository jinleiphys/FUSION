# Translationally-invariant coupled-cluster method for finite systems

> The translational invariant formulation of the coupled-cluster method is presented here at the complete SUB(2) level for a system of nucleons treated as bosons. The correlation amplitudes are solution of a non-linear coupled system of equations. These equations have been solved for light and medium systems, considering the central but still semi-realistic nucleon-nucleon S3 interaction.

# Translationally-invariant coupled-cluster method for finite systems

## Key claim

The translationally-invariant coupled-cluster method at the SUB(2) level (TICC2) is formulated for bosonic nucleon systems, but the non-linear terms beyond the linearized TICI2 approximation become increasingly important as the number of particles grows, contributing up to 963.24 MeV for N=40; however, the method produces numerous spurious solutions that must be discriminated, and the TICC2 results remain significantly less bound than Jastrow variational lower bounds, indicating the need for three-body and higher-order correlations.

## Method

The authors construct a translationally-invariant cluster operator S^(1,2) as an admixture of one-body and two-body excitations on a harmonic-oscillator reference state, using Brody-Moshinsky brackets to ensure the center-of-mass remains in its ground state. The wave function is written as the normal-ordered exponential :exp(S^(1,2)): acting on the reference state, and the Schrödinger equation is projected onto 0p-0h, 1p-1h, and 2p-2h states to obtain a non-linear (quartic) system of coupled equations for the correlation amplitudes S(n). The equations are solved sequentially by increasing the maximum number of oscillator quanta N_max up to 30, using a quenching factor method to connect the linear TICI2 solutions to the full TICC2 solutions, and spurious solutions are identified by comparing TICC2 energies with variational Monte Carlo estimates.

## Key numbers

- For N=4 bosons with the S3 interaction, the TICC2 ground-state energy is -25.55 ± 0.05 MeV from variational Monte Carlo, in good agreement with the normal TICC2 value.
- For N=16 bosons, the TICC2 energy gain over TICI2 is not explicitly stated as a single number, but the variational Monte Carlo value for the physical TICC2 solution is -1207 ± 16 MeV, while the spurious solution gives -661 ± 171 MeV with very large variance.
- The energy gain from TICI2 to TICC2 ranges from 0.18 MeV for N=4 to 963.24 MeV for N=40.
- Convergence of the solutions is achieved at N_max = 30 oscillator quanta.

## Context

This work extends the translationally-invariant coupled-cluster method previously developed for ⁴He by Bishop et al. (1990, 1992) to larger bosonic systems, and generalizes the linearized TICI2 approach applied to 1p-shell nuclei up to ¹⁶O by Guardiola et al. (1996). The results are compared with Jastrow variational Monte Carlo calculations and with a hybrid TICI2+hyperradial approach that includes hyperspherical harmonics correlations. The paper identifies the need for SUB(3) and SUB(4) truncations to approach Jastrow-quality results, and suggests developing integro-differential equations for the two-body correlation function in coordinate representation as an alternative to solving the large system of algebraic equations.
