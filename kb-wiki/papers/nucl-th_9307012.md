# Effects of meson-exchange currents on the $(\protect\vec{e},e'p)$ structure functions

> The response functions for the unpolarized $(e,e'p)$ and polarized $(\vec{e},e'p)$ reaction are calculated for medium-heavy nuclei under quasifree conditions. The formalism presented here incorporates two-body currents related to meson-exchange and the $Δ(1232)$ excitation. The final-state interaction of the outgoing nucleon with the residual nucleus is handled in a HF-RPA formalism. The sensitivity of the results to the two-body currents is discussed for the five structure functions in quasielastic $(\vec{e},e'p)$ scattering off the target nuclei $^{16}O$ and $^{40}Ca$. A selective sensitivity to the two-body currents is obtained in the longitudinal-transverse interference term $W_{LT}$ where two-body currents can explain part of the discrepancy between the impulse-approximation calculations and the data.

# Effects of meson-exchange currents on the $(\vec{e},e'p)$ structure functions

## Key claim
Two-body currents from meson-exchange and intermediate $\Delta(1232)$ excitation can partially explain the discrepancy between impulse-approximation (DWIA) calculations and experimental data for the longitudinal-transverse interference structure function $W_{LT}$, but only for specific spin-orbit partner states (the $1p_{3/2}$ in $^{16}O$ and $1d_{5/2}$ in $^{40}Ca$), while the $1p_{1/2}$ and $1d_{3/2}$ states show less sensitivity.

## Method
The authors calculated the five nuclear structure functions for quasielastic $(e,e'p)$ and $(\vec{e},e'p)$ reactions on $^{16}O$ and $^{40}Ca$ using a non-relativistic formalism that includes one-body currents (convection and magnetization) plus two-body currents from seagull, pion-in-flight, and $\Delta(1232)$ excitation diagrams. Final-state interactions were treated within a Hartree-Fock continuum RPA formalism using a Skyrme-type effective interaction (SKE2), with bound and continuum states taken as eigenstates of the HF mean-field potential.

## Key numbers
- Kinematics for $^{16}O$: $\omega = 96$ MeV, $q = 460$ MeV (perpendicular kinematics)
- Kinematics for $^{40}Ca$: $\omega = 116$ MeV, $q = 446$ MeV (perpendicular kinematics)
- Spectroscopic factors used for $^{16}O$: $S(1p_{1/2}) = 0.60$, $S(1p_{3/2}) = 0.51$
- Spectroscopic factors used for $^{40}Ca$: $S(1d_{3/2}) = 0.48$, $S(1d_{5/2}) = 0.33$
- Pion cut-off mass adopted in calculations: $\Lambda_{\pi} = 800$ MeV, with uncertainty estimated at 20% of the total two-body contribution (range tested: 650 MeV to 1200 MeV)

## Context
This work extends the distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) calculations of Van Orden et al. and the non-relativistic DWIA model of the Pavia group (refs. rep, jan2, bern) by adding two-body meson-exchange currents. Previous $(e,e'p)$ separation measurements on $^{16}O$ at Saclay (ref. saclay) and on both $^{16}O$ and $^{40}Ca$ at NIKHEF-K (refs. chiara, wies, kramer) showed that DWIA could not simultaneously describe the total cross section and the $W_{LT}$ structure function, especially for spin-orbit partner states. The results enable future polarized $(\vec{e},e'p)$ experiments at MIT-Bates (ref. pap) to extract the fifth structure function $W_{LT}'$, which vanishes identically in plane wave impulse approximation and is completely determined by final-state interactions and two-body currents.
