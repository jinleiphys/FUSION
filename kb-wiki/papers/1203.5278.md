# QCD and QED dynamics of the EMC effect

> Applying exact QCD sum rules for the baryon charge and energy-momentum we demonstrate that if nucleons are the only degrees of freedom of nuclear wave function, the structure function of a nucleus would be the additive sum of the nucleon distributions at the same Bjorken x = AQ^2/2(p_Aq)< 0.5 up to very small Fermi motion corrections if x>0.05. Thus the difference of the EMC ratio from one reveals the presence of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in nuclei. Using exact QCD sum rules we show that the ratio R_A(x_p,Q^2) used in experimental studies, where x_p = Q^2/2q_0 m_p deviates from one even if a nucleus consists of nucleons with small momenta only. Use of the Bjorken x leads to additional decrease of R_A(x,Q^2) as compared to the x_p plots. Coherent contribution of equivalent photons into photon component of parton wave function of a nucleus unambiguously follows from Lorentz transformation of the rest frame nucleus Coulomb field. For A~200 photons carry ~0.0065 fraction of the light momentum of nucleus almost compensates the difference between data analysis in terms of Bjorken x and x_p. Different role of higher twist effects for Q^2 probed at electron and muon beams is emphasized. Direct observations of large and predominantly nucleonic short-range correlations in nuclei pose a serious challenge for most of the models of the EMC effect for x>0.6. The data are consistent with a scenario in which the hadronic EMC effect reflects fluctuations of inter nucleon interaction due to fluctuations of color distribution in the interacting nucleons. The dynamic realization of this scenario is the model in which the 3q (3qg) configurations with x > 0.5 parton have a weaker interaction with nearby nucleons, leading to suppression of such configurations giving a right magnitude of the EMC effect. The directions for the future studies and challenging questions are outlined.

## Key claim

Applying exact QCD sum rules for baryon charge and energy-momentum, the authors demonstrate that if nucleons were the only degrees of freedom in the nuclear wave function, the nuclear structure function would be the additive sum of nucleon distributions at the same Bjorken x up to very small Fermi motion corrections for x > 0.05; thus the deviation of the EMC ratio from one reveals the presence of non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in nuclei, though the use of the experimental variable x_p instead of Bjorken x introduces an artificial deviation even in the purely nucleonic case.

## Method

The authors apply exact QCD sum rules for baryon charge and energy-momentum conservation to derive the nuclear parton distribution functions (pdfs) in the impulse approximation, using a Taylor expansion of the convolution formula in powers of (alpha-1) to account for nucleon Fermi motion. They separately evaluate the coherent contribution of equivalent photons to the nuclear wave function via the Fermi-Weizsacker-Williams approximation, calculating the light-cone momentum fraction carried by photons from the Lorentz transformation of the rest-frame nuclear Coulomb field. The analysis compares the standard nuclear model (nucleons only) against experimental EMC ratios, correcting for the difference between the Bjorken variable x and the experimentally used x_p variable.

## Key numbers

* For A ~ 200, equivalent photons carry approximately 0.0065 fraction of the light-cone momentum of the nucleus, which almost compensates the difference between data analysis in terms of Bjorken x and x_p.
* The correction to the EMC ratio from using x_p instead of Bjorken x leads to about a 20% enhancement of the EMC effect for light nuclei (A <= 12) at x ~ 0.5, with a smaller correction for heavy nuclei.
* For realistic nuclear wave functions with A >= 40, the fraction of light-cone momentum carried by non-nucleonic degrees of freedom (eta_A) is approximately 2%, corresponding to R_A(0.6) ~ 0.94 in JLab/SLAC kinematics (n ~ 2), about half the observed effect.
* The Fermi motion contribution to R_A is zero at x = 2/(n+1), gives a small negative contribution for x < 2/(n+1), and leads to rapid growth of R_A for x > 2/(n+1), where n is the exponent in F_{2N}(x) proportional to (1-x)^n (n=2 at low Q^2 ~ 5 GeV^2, n=3 at high Q^2 ~ 40 GeV^2).

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework of the EMC effect first observed by the European Muon Collaboration (EMC) in 1983, building on exact QCD sum rules developed by Frankfurt and Strikman (1981, 1988) and correcting a sign error in Frankfurt (2010). The work addresses the challenge posed by recent high-precision measurements at TJNAF (Seely, 2009) and direct observations of short-range correlations (SRC) at BNL and TJNAF, which constrain many proposed models of the EMC effect for x >= 0.6. The analysis enables future global fits of nuclear pdfs that properly account for both the QCD variable x and the QED contribution of equivalent photons, and has implications for understanding superdense nuclear matter in neutron stars.
