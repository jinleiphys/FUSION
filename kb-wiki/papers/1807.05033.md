# Nonequilibrium quark production in the expanding QCD plasma

> We present real-time lattice simulation results for nonequilibrium quark production from an over-occupied gluon plasma in longitudinally expanding geometry. The quark number density per unit transverse area and rapidity shows almost linear growth in time, and its growth rate appears to be consistent with a simple kinetic theory estimate involving only two-to-two scattering processes in small-angle approximation. We also find that quarks produced at early times satisfy a nonequilibrium scaling law.

## Key claim

The authors present real-time lattice simulation results for nonequilibrium quark production from an over-occupied gluon plasma in longitudinally expanding geometry, finding that the quark number density per unit transverse area and rapidity shows almost linear growth in time. The growth rate appears consistent with a simple kinetic theory estimate involving only two-to-two scattering processes in small-angle approximation, despite the nonperturbatively large gluon occupation number of order \(1/g^2\) that would naively invalidate such a kinetic description.

## Method

The authors solved classical Yang-Mills equations for over-occupied non-Abelian gauge fields coupled to the Dirac equation for dynamical quark mode functions, using lattice-discretized versions in proper time \(\tau\) and rapidity \(\eta\) coordinates. They extracted momentum distribution functions for quarks and gluons by projecting the fields onto free modes, starting from an initial gluon distribution characterized by the saturation scale \(Q_s\) and anisotropy parameter \(\xi_0\), with vacuum initial conditions for quarks. Simulations were performed with \(N_c=2\) and \(N_f=1\), with coupling \(g=10^{-2}\), \(Q_s \tau_0 =100\), and \(\xi_0 =2\).

## Key numbers

- The total quark number density per unit transverse area and unit rapidity exhibits rapid increase at early times \(Q_s \tau \lesssim 101\), interpreted as nonperturbative particle production from the initial quench.
- At later times \(Q_s \tau \gtrsim 130\), the total quark number density increases almost linearly in time.
- The width of the longitudinal momentum distribution becomes almost constant for \(Q_s \tau \geq 150\), in contrast to free-streaming where the width would shrink as \(p_z \sim 1/\tau\).
- For quark masses \(m/Q_s = 0.01\) and \(0.1\), the transverse spectra are almost degenerate; for \(Q_s \simeq 1\) GeV, this indicates comparable production rates for the three light quark flavors in QCD.
- The transverse spectrum as a function of transverse mass \(m_T = \sqrt{p_\perp^2 + m^2}\) shows all data points for different masses lying on top of each other for \(m_T \gtrsim Q_s\), consistent with an exponential function \(A \exp(-\sqrt{p_\perp^2 + m^2}/Q_s)\) with fit value \(A=1.15\) at \(Q_s \tau =110\).

## Context

This work extends real-time lattice simulations of pre-equilibrium dynamics in heavy-ion collisions, which previously focused on pure gluonic systems (Berges 2013), to include dynamical quarks. The results enable first-principles-based understanding of quark production in the early stage of heavy-ion collisions, which is crucial for electromagnetic probes and for investigations of the chiral magnetic effect (Kharzeev 2015) as well as chemical equilibration between gluons and light quarks (Gelis 2005). The observation that kinetic theory estimates reproduce the lattice results despite nonperturbatively large occupation numbers suggests that effective kinetic descriptions may be more robust than perturbative power counting would indicate.
