# Spin-orbit splittings of neutron states in $N = 20$ isotones from covariant density functionals and their extensions

> Spin-orbit splitting is an essential ingredient for our understanding of the shell structure in nuclei. One of the most important advantages of relativistic mean-field (RMF) models in nuclear physics is the fact that the large spin-orbit (SO) potential emerges automatically from the inclusion of Lorentz-scalar and -vector potentials in the Dirac equation. It is therefore of great importance to compare the results of such models with experimental data. We investigate the size of $2p$ and $1f$ splittings for the isotone chain $^{40}$Ca, $^{38}$Ar, $^{36}$S, and $^{34}$Si in the framework of various relativistic and nonrelativistic density functionals. They are compared with the results of nonrelativistic models and with recent experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors investigate whether the different treatments of the spin-orbit force in relativistic and nonrelativistic mean-field models produce significantly different results for the $2p$ and $1f$ neutron spin-orbit splittings in the $N=20$ isotone chain, finding that the relativistic models generally predict larger splittings than nonrelativistic models, with the inclusion of particle-vibration coupling reducing the splittings toward experimental values.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent relativistic Hartree (RH) calculations using several nonlinear and density-dependent covariant density functionals (NL3, NL3*, FSUGold, DD-ME2, DD-ME$\delta$, PC-F1, DD-PC1) for the $N=20$ isotones $^{40}$Ca, $^{38}$Ar, $^{36}$S, and $^{34}$Si. They extended the calculations beyond the mean-field level by including pairing correlations within the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) scheme, incorporating tensor forces via relativistic Hartree-Fock (RHF) theory with pion exchange, and including particle-vibration coupling (PVC) beyond mean-field.

## Key numbers

- The $2p_{3/2}-2p_{1/2}$ splitting in $^{34}$Si from the NL3 functional is approximately 3.5 MeV, compared to the experimental value of about 2.5 MeV from Burgunder (2014)
- The $1f_{7/2}-1f_{5/2}$ splitting in $^{40}$Ca from NL3 is approximately 6.5 MeV, compared to the experimental value of about 5.5 MeV from Uozumi (1994)
- The inclusion of particle-vibration coupling reduces the $2p$ splitting in $^{34}$Si by approximately 1.0 MeV, bringing it closer to the experimental value
- The ratio $W_1/W_2$ for relativistic models is approximately $1 + 2(C_\rho + C_\delta)/(C_\omega + C_\sigma)$, where the isovector coupling constants reach only 10%-20% of the isoscalar values, while for standard Skyrme functionals this ratio equals 2

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic study by Grasso (2015) that analyzed $2p$ and $1f$ neutron spin-orbit splittings in $N=20$ isotones using Skyrme and Gogny functionals, and it responds to experimental measurements by Burgunder (2014) and Mutschler (2016) on the bubble nucleus $^{34}$Si. The results enable systematic comparison between relativistic and nonrelativistic density functionals for spin-orbit splittings in neutron-rich nuclei, and demonstrate that particle-vibration coupling is essential for quantitative agreement with experiment.
