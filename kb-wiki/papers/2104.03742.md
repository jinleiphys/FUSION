# The signature of charge dependent directed flow observables by electromagnetic fields in heavy ion collisions

> We discuss the generation of the directed flow $v_1(p_T,y_z)$ induced by the electromagnetic field as a function of $p_T$ and $y_z$. Despite the complex dynamics of charged particles due to strong interactions generating several anisotropies in the azimuthal angle, it is possible at $p_T > m$ to directly correlate the splitting in $v_1$ of heavy quarks with different charges to some main features of the magnetic field, and in particular its values at formation and freeze-out time. We further found that the slope of the splitting $dΔv_1/dy_z|_{y_z=0}$ of positively and negatively charged particles at high $p_T$ can be formulated as $dΔv_1/dy_z|_{y_z=0}=-α\frac{\partial \ln f}{\partial p_T}+\frac{2α-β}{p_T}$, where the constants $α$ and $β$ are constrained by the $y$ component of magnetic fields and the sign of $α$ is simply determined by the difference $Δ[tB_y(t)]$ in the center of colliding systems at the formation time of particles and at the time when particles leave the effective range of electromagnetic fields or freeze out. The formula is derived from general considerations and is confirmed by several related numerical simulations; it supplies a useful guide to quantify the effect of different magnetic field configurations and provides an evidence of why the measurement of $Δv_1$ of charm, bottom and leptons from $Z^0$ decay and their correlations are a powerful probe of the initial e.m. fields in ultra-relativistic collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the charge-dependent splitting of directed flow \(v_1\) for high-\(p_T\) heavy quarks and leptons can be directly linked to the time evolution of the magnetic field \(B_y\) at the center of the collision, specifically through the difference \(\Delta[tB_y(t)]\) between particle formation and freeze-out times, but this relation holds only under the approximation that spatial gradients of the electromagnetic fields are not too large within the inner fireball and that the particle mass is much smaller than its transverse momentum (\(p_T \gg m\)).

## Method

The authors derived an analytical formula for the slope of the directed flow splitting \(d\Delta v_1/dy_z|_{y_z=0}\) by expanding the phase-space distribution function under the influence of electromagnetic fields via the Lorentz force, assuming small perturbations and boost-invariant spectra. They then related the coefficients \(a_0\) and \(c_1\) in the Fourier decomposition of momentum shifts to the time-integrated magnetic field \(B_y\) and electric field \(E_x\) at the collision center, using Faraday's law to eliminate \(E_x\) and reduce the expression to a function of \(tB_y(t)\) only. Numerical simulations for charm and bottom quarks in 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb collisions at impact parameter \(b=7.5\) fm were performed using relativistic Langevin dynamics with a quasi-particle model for the QGP medium, and the analytical scaling was fitted to the numerical results at high \(p_T\).

## Key numbers

* The slope \(d\Delta v_1/dy_z|_{y_z=0}\) for charm quarks at \(p_T > 3\) GeV/\(c\) is fitted by \(\alpha = -3.6\) MeV and \(\beta = 2.5\) MeV, with \(\alpha\) determined by \(\tau_1 g(\tau_1,0) \approx 2.5\) MeV and \(\tau_0 g(\tau_0,0) = 11.5\) MeV (where \(g = -B_y\)), yielding \(K \approx 0.6\).
* For charm quarks, the quantity \(-\partial \ln f_c / \partial p_T\) reaches a maximum of 0.8 GeV\(^{-1}\) at \(p_T = 3.5\) GeV/\(c\); for bottom quarks, it reaches 0.4 GeV\(^{-1}\) at about 6 GeV/\(c\).
* The electrical conductivity values used in the simulations are \(\sigma_{el} = 0.0115\), 0.023, and 0.046 fm\(^{-1}\), with the latter two within the bound of lattice QCD results.
* The formation time of charm quarks is \(\tau_0 = 0.1\) fm/\(c\), and the QGP formation time is about 0.3 fm/\(c\).
* The impact parameter for the Pb+Pb collisions is \(b = 7.5\) fm at a center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the numerical studies of charge-dependent flow observables induced by electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions, such as those by Gursoy (2014) and Das (2016), by providing a general analytical formula that captures the essential physics without requiring detailed knowledge of the spatial and temporal configurations of the fields. The derived scaling for \(d\Delta v_1/dy_z\) in terms of the slope of the particle spectrum and the difference in \(tB_y\) at formation and freeze-out times enables a direct probe of the initial electromagnetic fields, and it can be applied to high-\(p_T\) heavy quarks, energetic jets, and leptons from \(Z^0\) decay. The results also explain why increasing electrical conductivity can reduce the magnitude of the splitting, contrary to naive expectations, and they provide a framework to interpret experimental measurements such as those from ALICE (2019).
