# Bayesian Inference of Heavy-Quark Dissipation and Jet Transport Parameters from D-Meson observables in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC energies

> We perform the first simultaneous Bayesian inference of the temperature-dependent heavy-quark spatial diffusion coefficient $2πT\mathcal{D}_s$ and the scaled jet transport coefficient $\hat{q}/T^3$ in the quark-gluon plasma, utilizing $D$-meson nuclear modification factor $R_\text{AA}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ data from Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 5.02\ \text{TeV}$. The analysis employs a unified improved Langevin transport model that incorporates both collisional and radiative energy loss, followed by coalescence plus fragmentation hadronization. The posterior distributions of the parameters of $\hat{q}/T^3$ and those of $2πT\mathcal{D}_s$ are well constrained, and compared with the results of theoretical models or other experimental data extraction, respectively. The $30-50\%$ centrality data provide significantly stronger constraints than the $0-10\%$ data. The extracted ratio $\hat{q}/κ$ between the quark jet transport and heavy-quark diffusion coefficients exhibits a non-monotonic temperature dependence, deviating from the value $2$ estimated from the definition, with a value interval spanning 0.25--0.8 corresponding to the mean values of the inferred parameters. This work establishes a data-driven quantitative relationship between these two fundamental transport properties in the same observables, offering crucial insight into their interplay in the strongly coupled medium.

## Key claim

The paper performs the first simultaneous Bayesian inference of the heavy-quark spatial diffusion coefficient and the jet transport coefficient from D-meson observables, but the extracted ratio between these two coefficients deviates substantially from the theoretical estimate of 2, exhibiting a non-monotonic temperature dependence with values spanning 0.25 to 0.8 for the posterior mean.

## Method

The authors employed a unified improved Langevin transport model (SHELL model) incorporating both collisional and radiative energy loss for charm quarks propagating through a quark-gluon plasma described by (3+1)-dimensional CLVisc hydrodynamics. Hadronization was modeled via a coalescence plus fragmentation mechanism, and a hierarchical Bayesian inference framework (implemented using PyMC) with Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling was used to extract posterior distributions of four free parameters governing the temperature dependence of the spatial diffusion coefficient and the scaled jet transport coefficient from D-meson experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The posterior mean of the scaled jet transport coefficient at the critical temperature, $\hat{q}_c/T_c^3$, was 0.47, compared to 0.45 from a global fit to light-hadron data by Xie (2024).
- The posterior mean of the scaled jet transport coefficient at the initial temperature, $\hat{q}_0/T_0^3$, was 0.48, while the corresponding value from light-hadron fits was 0.
- The ratio $\hat{q}/\kappa$ from the combined analysis varied between approximately 0.25 and 0.8 across the studied temperature range, with the 95% credibility interval spanning approximately 0.1 to 1.25.
- The initial temperatures used were 0.505 GeV for 0-10% centrality and 0.445 GeV for 30-50% centrality in Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_\text{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV.
- The critical temperature $T_c$ was taken as 0.165 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Bayesian extractions of heavy-quark diffusion coefficients (such as the Duke analysis by Xu (2017) and lattice QCD calculations by Altenkort (2023)) and jet transport coefficients from light-hadron observables (such as the JET Collaboration (2013), JETSCAPE (2021), and Xie (2024)). It enables a data-driven quantitative comparison between these two fundamental transport properties within a unified framework, offering insight into the interplay of collisional and radiative energy loss mechanisms in the quark-gluon plasma.
