# The outer crust of non-accreting cold neutron stars

> The properties of the outer crust of non-accreting cold neutron stars are studied by using modern nuclear data and theoretical mass tables updating in particular the classic work of Baym, Pethick and Sutherland. Experimental data from the atomic mass table from Audi, Wapstra, and Thibault of 2003 is used and a thorough comparison of many modern theoretical nuclear models, relativistic and non-relativistic ones, is performed for the first time. In addition, the influences of pairing and deformation are investigated. State-of-the-art theoretical nuclear mass tables are compared in order to check their differences concerning the neutron dripline, magic neutron numbers, the equation of state, and the sequence of neutron-rich nuclei up to the dripline in the outer crust of non-accreting cold neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper updates the classic work of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) on the outer crust of non-accreting cold neutron stars by using modern experimental and theoretical nuclear mass tables, finding that the inclusion of deformations in nuclear models is crucial for determining the composition of the outer crust, while the details of the approximation method (ETFSI, BCS, or HFB) are not important as long as parameters are fitted to a similar set of observables.

## Method

The authors apply the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) model, which treats pressure as the independent variable and minimizes the baryon chemical potential by varying mass number A and charge number Z at fixed pressure, using the binding energies from various nuclear mass tables as input. They compare experimental data from the Audi-Wapstra-Thibault atomic mass table of 2003 with multiple theoretical models including the finite range droplet model (FRDM), nine non-relativistic Skyrme parameterizations (from BRUSLIB and Dobaczewski et al.), and five relativistic mean-field models (NL3, NL-Z2, TMA, PCF1, and a chiral effective model), investigating the effects of pairing and deformation on the neutron dripline, equation of state, and sequence of equilibrium nuclei.

## Key numbers

- The outer crust begins at mass density approximately 10^4 g/cm^3 and ends at the neutron drip density of 4.3 × 10^11 g/cm^3
- The inner crust ends at approximately 2 × 10^14 g/cm^3 where nuclei no longer exist
- The starting pressure for calculations is 9.744 × 10^18 dynes/cm^2, corresponding to mass density approximately 1.044 × 10^4 g/cm^3
- The FRDM mass table lists 8979 nuclei ranging from 16O to 339136
- The BSk2 and BSk8 mass tables contain 9200 nuclei up to Z=120, fitted to binding energies of 2149 nuclei
- The NL3 mass table (spherical) contains 1315 even-even nuclei up to Z=98
- The TMA mass table contains nuclei from neutron to proton dripline up to Z=100
- The chiral model mass table extends from Z=16 to Z=100
- The adiabatic index asymptotically approaches the relativistic limit of Γ = 4/3 at high mass density
- The sequence of nuclei begins with 56Fe, continues through 62Ni, 64Ni, 66Ni, then 86Kr and 84Se before becoming model-dependent above approximately 10^10 g/cm^3

## Context

This work extends the classic study of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) which used nuclear data from the droplet model of Myers and Swiatecki from the 1960s, and updates subsequent work by Haensel, Zdunik, and Dobaczewski (1989) who used a Skyrme HFB calculation in spherical approximation, and Haensel and Pichon (1994) who used experimental data from the 1992 atomic mass table. The results enable future experimental verification of the predicted neutron-rich isotope sequences at facilities including FAIR at GSI, TRIUMF's ISAC-II, and the Rare Isotope Accelerator project, and provide improved input for low mass neutron star models, neutron star mergers, and core-collapse supernova simulations.
