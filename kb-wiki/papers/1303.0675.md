# Characterizing quark gluon plasma by thermal photons and lepton pairs

> The photon spectra measured by the ALICE collaboration in Pb+Pb collisions at Large Hadron Collider (LHC) energies has been analyzed with a view of extracting the properties of thermal system formed in these collisions. The results of the analysis are compared with the previously studied spectra measured at Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) and Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) energies. The thermal dilepton spectra from the Pb+Pb collision at LHC energy has been predicted for the initial conditions constrained by the thermal photon spectra at the same collision conditions. The slope of the photon has been used to estimate the rise in the effective degeneracy with the charged particle multiplicity of the system. The slopes of the lepton pair spectra for different invariant mass windows have been used to conjecture the average radial flow velocity of the high temperature phase.

## Key claim

The slope of thermal photon spectra can be used to estimate the rise in effective statistical degeneracy with charged particle multiplicity, and the slopes of lepton pair spectra for different invariant mass windows can be used to extract the average radial flow velocity of the high-temperature phase, but the analysis assumes that contributions from jet-thermal parton interactions and induced emissions by hard partons are negligible for the transverse momentum range considered.

## Method

The authors analyzed ALICE direct photon spectra from Pb+Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (0-40% centrality) using relativistic hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and cylindrical symmetry, with initial conditions constrained by hadronic multiplicity. Thermal photon and dilepton emission rates were computed using the complete O(ααs) calculation for QGP (Arnold et al.) and hadronic reactions with dipole form factors for the hadronic phase, with the space-time evolution modeled using lattice QCD equation of state for QGP and hadronic resonance gas EoS for the hadronic phase, including chemical non-equilibrium effects via chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature Ti = 553 MeV for Pb+Pb at √sNN = 2.76 TeV (0-40% centrality) with thermalization time τi = 0.1 fm
- Hadronic multiplicity dN/dy = 1212 for the same collision conditions
- Average transverse momentum ⟨pT⟩ = 245 MeV for (1/Npart)(dNch/dη) = 1.214; 265 MeV for 1.77; 300 MeV for 3.47
- Entropy density at LHC increases by 96% compared to RHIC, and by 46% at RHIC compared to SPS
- Normalized quantity ρeffav/⟨pT⟩3 increases by 15% from SPS to RHIC and 35% from RHIC to LHC
- Average radial flow velocity ⟨vr⟩ ∼ 0.065 for M = 2 GeV (QGP phase), ⟨vr⟩ ∼ 0.24 for M ∼ 1.6 GeV, ⟨vr⟩ ∼ 0.37 for M ∼ 1.3 GeV, and ⟨vr⟩ ∼ 0.46 for the hadronic phase near the ρ peak
- Kinetic freeze-out temperature Tf = 100 MeV; critical temperature Tc = 175 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of thermal photon spectra from SPS and RHIC energies (previously studied by various authors using different models) to LHC energies using ALICE data, and provides predictions for thermal dilepton spectra at LHC. The approach builds on prior work by Rapp (review), Alam et al. (reviews), and the hydrodynamic framework of Bjorken (1983) and Hwa and collaborators, using the complete photon emission rate calculation by Arnold et al. and the hadronic reaction rates from Turbide et al. The results enable extraction of the effective degeneracy increase across collision energies and estimation of radial flow in the QGP phase at LHC conditions, with the dilepton predictions serving as benchmarks for future experimental measurements.
