# Novel type of color solitons with topological charge not coinciding with baryon number and extended chiral group E$χ$

> We consider extended chiral group $Eχ$ of gauge plus chiral transformations of the quark path integral in the background gauge and present novel type of color solitons described by the effective action resulting from bosonization. O(3) solitons are formed by a chiral field including parameters with diquark quantum numbers within symplectic subgroup of $Eχ$, as well as within complete $Eχ$; isolated solitons (i.e. solitons in gluonic vacuum) can be stable due to proper asymptotics induced by background vacuum, their topological charge starts from $\pm \frac 43$ and does not coincide with the baryon number, as if notions of left anf right were dependent also on non-abelian directions in such a SU(2) group, where the baryon number defines a diagonal generator.

# Novel type of color solitons with topological charge not coinciding with baryon number and extended chiral group Eχ

## Key claim

The authors claim that classically stable, finite-mass topological solitons exist in the extended chiral group Eχ for three colors and two flavors, with topological charge starting from ±4/3 that does not coincide with the baryon number; this result depends on the presence of a chromomagnetic vacuum background field with positive gluon condensate and on the restriction of the soliton subgroup to O(3) within the larger symplectic or complete Eχ group.

## Method

The authors start from the extended chiral group Eχ of gauge plus chiral transformations of the quark path integral in a background gauge field, then perform bosonization to obtain an effective chiral action for 2N internal degrees of freedom. They construct chiral fields U = exp Θ for the symplectic subgroup Sp(2N) and for the complete Eχ group, restricting to O(3) subgroups in the case of three colors and two flavors, and evaluate the topological charge as a winding number in the baryon SU(2) space. The soliton mass is computed from the static effective Lagrangian averaged over directions of the background vacuum field, with the shape function asymptotics determined by the kinetic term and the gluon condensate.

## Key numbers

- Topological charge for solitons in the symplectic subgroup and complete Eχ group starts from |4/3| (no units, dimensionless topological charge).
- For three colors and N_F flavors, the topological charge in the left-right subgroup starts from (2/3)N_F.
- The shape function F(R) at large R decays as F → (f₀R)^(-3/2) exp(-(π/3)√(C_g/2) R²), where C_g is the gluon condensate.
- The kinetic term averaged over background vacuum directions contains terms proportional to (2/R² + (1/9)π² C_g R²) for sin²F and (4/R² + (1/9)π² C_g R²) for (cos F - 1)².

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of isolated color solitons stabilized by chromomagnetic vacuum fields (Novozhilov and Novozhilov, 2001) and builds on the Faddeev-Niemi color solitons in the gauge sector (Faddeev and Niemi, 1997; Battye and Sutcliffe, 1998) and the Skyrmion model for constituent quarks (Kaplan, 1990). The novel solitons exist within the extended chiral group Eχ previously introduced by Novozhilov, Pronko and Vassilevich (1995), and their topological charge not coinciding with baryon number may be relevant for discussions of baryon asymmetry and baryon number nonconservation. The next step would be to include additional gauge degrees ξ when describing vacuum by a set of condensates.
