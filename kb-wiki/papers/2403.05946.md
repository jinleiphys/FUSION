# Shear viscosity and electric conductivity of quark matter at finite temperature and chemical potential with QCD phase transitions

> In the Beam Energy Scan phase II (BES-II) experiments at RHIC STAR, the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced with changing collision energies may probe different regions of the QCD phase diagram. Correspondingly, studying the transport coefficients of quark matter in these regions will contribute to extracting the QCD phase structure through hydrodynamic approaches. We investigate the shear viscosity and electric conductivity within the framework of kinetic theory with the relaxation time approximation, in particular their behaviors near the Mott and first-order phase transitions with a spinodal structure as well as along the isentropic trajectories. To derived the scattering cross-section under different conditions, the temperature and chemical potential dependent masses of quarks, antiquarks and exchanged mesons are calculated in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu--Jona Lasinio (PNJL) model. The numerical results indicate that, at small chemical potential, the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio ($η/s$) has a minimum near the Mott phase transition and increases rapidly in the lower-temperature side of the chiral crossover phase transition. At large chemical potential (high baryon density), $η/s$ in the QGP phase is dominated by temperature, and the value of $η/s$ is greatly enhanced at low temperatures. At intermediate temperature and chemical potential near the QCD phase transition, the behavior of $η/s$ is influenced by the competition between temperature, density, and QCD phase transition. The electirc conductivity ($σ/T$) roughly exhibits similar characteristics to $η/s$ in the QCD phase diagram, whereas the dimensionless ratio of $η/s$ to $σ/T$ decreases monotonically with growing temperature, approaching a constant in the high-temperature limit.

## Key claim

The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s) and electric conductivity (σ/T) of quark matter exhibit distinct behaviors near the Mott phase transition, chiral crossover, and first-order phase transition with spinodal structure, with η/s showing a minimum near the Mott transition at small chemical potential and being greatly enhanced at low temperatures at large chemical potential, while the ratio of η/s to σ/T decreases monotonically with temperature and approaches a constant in the high-temperature limit.

## Method

The authors calculate shear viscosity and electric conductivity within kinetic theory using the relaxation time approximation, based on 2→2 elastic scatterings between u, d, s quarks and their antiparticles. The temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent masses of quarks, antiquarks, and exchanged mesons (pseudoscalar and scalar mesons) are computed in the 2+1 flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu–Jona Lasinio (PNJL) model. The cross sections and flavor-dependent relaxation times are derived from these medium-dependent masses, and the transport coefficients are evaluated across the QCD phase diagram including the Mott dissociation, chiral crossover, critical end point, and first-order phase transition with spinodal lines.

## Key numbers

* The critical end point (CEP) is located at T_C = 131 MeV and μ_C = 873 MeV in the T-μ_B plane, corresponding to ρ_C = 1.94 ρ_0 in the T-ρ_B plane.
* The difference between the pion/kaon Mott phase transition temperature and the chiral crossover temperature is less than 10 MeV in the whole crossover region.
* The Polyakov-loop effective potential parameters include T_0 = 210 MeV, with a_0 = 3.51, a_1 = -2.47, a_3 = 15.2, and b_3 = -1.75.
* The PNJL model parameters are Λ = 602.3 MeV, GΛ^2 = 1.835, KΛ^5 = 12.36, m_u,d = 5.5 MeV, and m_s = 140.7 MeV, fitted to f_π = 92.4 MeV, M_π = 135.0 MeV, m_K = 497.7 MeV, and m_η = 957.8 MeV.
* The electric conductivity is computed with e^2 = 4π/137 and quark charges q_i = +2/3 (u), -1/3 (d), -1/3 (s).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of shear viscosity in hot and dense quark matter (Marty 2013, Soloveva 2021, Deb 2016, Deng 2023, McLaughlin 2022) by specifically examining behavior near the Mott phase transition and the first-order chiral phase transition with spinodal structure, as well as along isentropic trajectories. The results are relevant for interpreting the Beam Energy Scan phase II (BES-II) experiments at RHIC STAR, where varying collision energies probe different regions of the QCD phase diagram, and the calculated transport coefficients can be used in future hydrodynamic simulations of QGP evolution with phase transitions.
