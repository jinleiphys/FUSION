# Systematics of radial and angular-momentum Regge trajectories of light non-strange q\bar{q}-states

> We reanalyze the radial (n) and angular-momentum (J) Regge trajectories for all light-quark states with baryon number zero listed in the 2011 edition of the Particle Data Tables. The parameters of the trajectories are obtained with linear regression, with weight of each resonance inversely proportional to its half-width squared, $(Γ/2)^2$. That way we are side-stepping possible channel-dependent and model-dependent extractions of the resonance parameters and are able to undertake an error analysis. The method complies to the fact that the pole position of the resonance is typically shifted from channel-dependent extractions by $\simΓ/2$. This is also a feature of the large-$N_c$ limit of QCD, where the masses change by $ Γ/2$ when evolving from $N_c=3$ to $N_c=\infty$. Our value for the slope of the radial Regge trajectories is $a=1.35(4) GeV^2$. We discuss the fundamental issue whether the masses of the light-quark non-strange states fit into a universal pattern $M_{nJ}^2 = a(n+J) +b$, as suggested by Afonin, and also predicted by some holographic models. Our joint linear-regression analysis in the $(n,J,M^2)$ Regge planes indicates, at a statistically significant level of 4.5 standard deviations, that the slopes of the radial Regge trajectories are larger from the angular-momentum slopes. Thus no strict universality of slopes occurs in the light non-strange meson spectra.

## Key claim

The authors find that, at a statistically significant level of 4.5 standard deviations, the slopes of radial Regge trajectories are larger than the slopes of angular-momentum trajectories for light non-strange mesons, contradicting the universal pattern \(M_{nJ}^2 = a(n+J) + b\) suggested by Afonin and predicted by some holographic models.

## Method

The authors performed linear regression fits to radial (n) and angular-momentum (J) Regge trajectories for all light-quark non-strange meson states listed in the 2011 Particle Data Group tables. Each resonance was weighted inversely proportional to the square of its half-width \((\Gamma/2)^2\), implementing the half-width rule that the pole position uncertainty is approximately \(\Gamma/2\). The analysis used the chi-squared function \(\chi^2 = \sum_n \left( (M_n^2 - M_{n,\text{exp}}^2) / (\Gamma_n M_n) \right)^2\) and compared slopes from separate radial and angular-momentum fits against a joint fit assuming universality.

## Key numbers

- Slope of radial Regge trajectories: \(a = 1.35(4)~\text{GeV}^2\)
- Radial slope for the \(a_1(1^{++})\) first trajectory: \(\mu^2 = 1.36(49)~\text{GeV}^2\) with \(\chi^2/\text{DOF} = 0.12\)
- Radial slope for the \(\eta(00^{-+})_{q\bar{q}}\) trajectory: \(\mu^2 = 1.33(11)~\text{GeV}^2\) with \(\chi^2/\text{DOF} = 0.26\)
- Radial slope for the \(\rho_1(11^{--})\) first trajectory: \(\mu^2 = 1.43(13)~\text{GeV}^2\) with \(\chi^2/\text{DOF} = 0.09\)
- Radial slope for the \(\pi(10^{-+})\) trajectory: \(\mu^2 = 1.27(27)~\text{GeV}^2\) with \(\chi^2/\text{DOF} = 0.16\)
- Radial slope for the \(f_0(00^{++})_{q\bar{q}}\) trajectory: \(\mu^2 = 1.31(12)~\text{GeV}^2\) with \(\chi^2/\text{DOF} = 0.11\)
- Radial slope for the \(\omega(01^{--})\) main trajectory: \(\mu^2 = 1.50(12)~\text{GeV}^2\) with \(\chi^2/\text{DOF} = 0.32\)
- Statistical significance of slope difference between radial and angular-momentum trajectories: 4.5 standard deviations

## Context

This work extends the analysis of radial Regge trajectories by Anisovich, Anisovich, and Sarantsev (2000), who found a slope of \(\mu^2 = 1.25(15)~\text{GeV}^2\) using older PDG data, and tests the universal pattern proposed by Afonin (2006). The authors use the half-width rule previously applied to scalar and pseudoscalar mesons by Ruiz Arriola and Arriola (2010, 2011) to incorporate resonance width uncertainties systematically. The results contradict predictions from soft-wall holographic models by Karch and Katz (2006) and their light-cone relatives by de Teramond and Brodsky (2008), which predict universal slopes for radial and angular-momentum trajectories.
