# Hartree-Fock Approximation for the $Ab initio$ No-Core Shell Model

> The spherical Hartree-Fock approximation is applied to the $abinitio$ no-core shell model, with a realistic effective nucleon-nucleon interaction in order to investigate the range of its utility. Hartree-Fock results for binding energies, one-body density distributions and occupation probabilities are compared with results from exact diagonalization in similar model spaces. We show that this mean field approximation, especially with second order corrections, is able to provide some useful approximatons for $^4He$ and $^{16}O$. We also explore the physical insights provided by the Hartree-Fock results for single-particle properties such as spin-orbit splittings. We find single particle state ordering consistent with the phenomenological shell model.

## Key claim

The spherical Hartree-Fock (SHF) approximation, especially with second-order perturbative corrections, can provide useful approximations for ground-state properties of light nuclei like $^4He$ and $^{16}O$ when using realistic effective nucleon-nucleon interactions from the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM), but the authors caution that the size of second-order corrections must be carefully evaluated as a gauge of the mean-field method's utility.

## Method

The authors applied spherical Hartree-Fock using the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon interaction (1996 for $^4He$, 2000 for $^{16}O$) with Coulomb interaction, employing a two-body effective interaction derived via the Lee-Suzuki similarity transformation method within the no-core shell model framework. They solved the SHF equations in model spaces of 4, 5, and 6 major harmonic-oscillator shells, evaluated second-order perturbative corrections to binding energies, occupation probabilities, and root-mean-square radii, and compared results against exact NCSM diagonalizations in $N_{max}=10$ ($^4He$) and $N_{max}=6$ ($^{16}O$) model spaces with $\hbar\Omega=22$ MeV and $\hbar\Omega=15$ MeV respectively.

## Key numbers

- For $^4He$: SHF ground-state binding energy is 13.76 MeV less bound than NCSM; SHF(2) reduces this difference to 2.9 MeV; the second-order correction is 15.3% of the total SHF interaction energy (-70.946 MeV)
- For $^4He$: SHF predicts $0P_{3/2}-0S_{1/2}$ particle-hole excited state at approximately 34.6/2 = 17.3 MeV, compared to experimental 21.84 MeV
- For $^{16}O$: SHF ground-state energy ranges from 7 to 25 MeV above NCSM depending on model space; SHF(2) differences range from 6 to 32 MeV; second-order corrections are [6.5%, 10.1%, 6.9%] of total SHF interaction energy [-481.01, -492.37, -554.61] MeV in [4, 5, 6] shell model spaces respectively
- For $^{16}O$: neutron $0D_{5/2} - 0P_{1/2}$ splitting from SHF is (23.6, 22.4, 24.7)/2 = (11.8, 11.2, 12.4) MeV in 4, 5, 6 shell results, compared to experimental 11.52 MeV from $^{17}O$ and $^{15}O$ binding energy differences
- For $^{16}O$: proton $0D_{5/2} - 0P_{1/2}$ splitting is (23.3, 22.4, 24.5)/2 = (11.7, 11.2, 12.3) MeV in 4, 5, 6 shell results

## Context

This work extends the application of Hartree-Fock methods to effective Hamiltonians derived from realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions within the ab initio no-core shell model framework, building on prior NCSM calculations for nuclei up to A=12 by Zheng (1995), Navratil (1996), and others. The results are compared with the work of Coraggio (2003) who used a $V_{low-k}$ potential and found larger second-order corrections (17% of SHF interaction energy) for $^{16}O$, and with the historical "Coester line" problem where mean-field methods underbind nuclei by 1-3 MeV per nucleon. The authors note that properly constructed Hamiltonians with bare NN and NNN interactions could enable mean-field methods to extend ab initio calculations to heavier nuclei, including those requiring delta excitations for many-body forces.
