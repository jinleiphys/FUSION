# Asymptotic Freedom and Vacuum Polarization Determine the Astrophysical End State of Relativistic Gravitational Collapse: Quark--Gluon Plasma Star Instead of Black Hole

> A general relativistic model of an astrophysical hypermassive extremely magnetized ultra-compact self-bound quark--gluon plasma object that is supported against its ultimate gravitational implosion by the simultaneous action of the vacuum polarization driven by nonlinear electrodynamics (NLED: light-by-light scattering) and the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) asymptotic freedom, is presented. These QCD stars can be the final figures of the equilibrium of collapsing stellar cores. Post-supernova fallback material pushes the nascent remnant beyond its stability to collapse into a hybrid hypermassive neutron star (HHMNS). Hypercritical accretion can unbind the whole HHMNS's baryons to spontaneously break away color confinement, powering a first-order hadron-to-quark phase transition to a sea of ever-freer quarks and gluons. This core is hydro-stabilized by the steady, endlessly compression-admitting asymptotic freedom state, possibly via gluon-mediated enduring exchange of color charge among bound states. The nonlinear TOV equation indicates the occurrence of hypermassive QGP/QCD stars with a wide mass spectrum ($0\lesssim$ M$^{\rm{QGP}}_{\rm{Star}}\lesssim$\,7\,M$_\odot$ and beyond), for star radii ($0\lesssim R^{\rm{QGP}}_{\rm{Star}}\lesssim 24$\,km and beyond) with B-fields ($10^{14} \leq$ B$^{\rm{QGP}}_{\rm{Star}} \leq 10^{16}$\,G and beyond). Such QCD stars can emulate what the true black holes are supposed to gravitationally do in most astrophysical settings. This color quark star could be found through a search for its eternal ``yo-yo'' state gravitational-wave emission, or via lensing phenomena like gravitational rainbows, as in this scenario it is expected that the light deflection angle, directly influenced by the larger effective mass/radius and magnetic field of the deflecting object, increases as the incidence angle decreases for impact parameter lower values.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a hypermassive, extremely magnetized quark-gluon plasma star can exist as the final equilibrium state of relativistic gravitational collapse, avoiding black hole formation, provided that both nonlinear electrodynamics vacuum polarization (light-by-light scattering) and QCD asymptotic freedom simultaneously act to hydro-stabilize the core; the authors hedge that this object "can emulate what the true black holes are supposed to gravitationally do in most astrophysical settings" rather than claiming direct observational confirmation.

## Method

The authors construct a general relativistic model by deriving a nonlinear Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation that incorporates both Born-Infeld nonlinear electrodynamics (NLED) and QCD asymptotic freedom effects into the stellar structure equations. They solve this modified TOV equation for a spherically symmetric, self-bound quark-gluon plasma object, imposing the surface zero-pressure condition and metric continuity at the boundary to match the Schwarzschild metric via Birkhoff's theorem. The model assumes that hypercritical accretion onto a hybrid hypermassive neutron star drives a first-order hadron-to-quark phase transition, with the resulting quark core stabilized by the compression-admitting nature of asymptotic freedom.

## Key numbers

- Mass range: \(0 \lesssim M^{\rm{QGP}}_{\rm{Star}} \lesssim 7\,M_\odot\) (and beyond)
- Radius range: \(0 \lesssim R^{\rm{QGP}}_{\rm{Star}} \lesssim 24\) km (and beyond)
- Magnetic field range: \(10^{14} \leq B^{\rm{QGP}}_{\rm{Star}} \leq 10^{16}\) G (and beyond)
- Surface gravitational redshift: \(z_{\rm Grav} \gtrsim 10^8\), making the object appear dark
- The highest magnetic field referenced from ATLAS/LHC data: \(B_c \simeq 90\,{\rm GeV}^2 \simeq 3.28 \times 10^{22}\) G for Born-Infeld NLED

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of ultra-compact objects beyond the standard neutron star and black hole paradigms, directly engaging with Kerr's (2023) questioning of black hole singularities and the Penrose-Hawking singularity theorems. The model builds upon experimental results from ALICE/LHC on quark-gluon plasma and ATLAS/LHC on light-by-light scattering (2017), as well as prior theoretical work on color-superconducting quark cores by Fraga, Palhares, and Restrepo (2023) and Sotani and Tatsumi (2015). The paper enables future searches for gravitational-wave "yo-yo" state emission and gravitational lensing phenomena (gravitational rainbows) as observational signatures distinguishing QGP stars from black holes.
