# Astrophysical S factor and rate of $^{7}{\rm Be}(p, γ)^{8}{\rm B}$ direct capture reaction in a potential model

> The astrophysical $^7{\rm Be}(p, γ)^8{\rm B}$ direct capture process is studied in the framework of a two-body single-channel model with potentials of the Gaussian form. A modified potential is constructed to reproduce the new experimental value of the $S$-wave scattering length and the known astrophysical $S$ factor at the Gamow energy, extracted from the solar neutrino flux. The resulting potential is consistent with the theory developed by Baye [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 62} (2000) 065803] according to which the $S$-wave scattering length and the astrophysical $S$ factor at zero energy divided by the square of ANC are related. The obtained results for the astrophysical $S$ factor at intermediate energies are in good agreement with the two data sets of Hammache {\it et al.} [Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 86}, 3985 (2001); {\it ibid.} {\bf 80}, 928 (1998)]. Linear extrapolation to zero energy yields $ S_{17}(0) \approx (20.5 \pm 0.5) \, \rm eV \, b $, consistent with the Solar Fusion II estimate. The calculated reaction rates are substantially lower than the results of the NACRE II collaboration.

# Astrophysical S factor and rate of 7Be(p,γ)8B direct capture reaction in a potential model

## Key claim

The modified Gaussian potential reproduces the new experimental S-wave scattering length (a01 = 17.34 fm) and the astrophysical S factor at the Gamow energy extracted from solar neutrino flux, yielding S17(0) ≈ (20.5 ± 0.5) eV b via linear extrapolation; however, the calculated reaction rates are substantially lower than the NACRE II collaboration results, indicating that the new scattering length data significantly alter the low-energy capture prediction.

## Method

The authors solved the two-body Schrodinger equation with Gaussian-form central potentials for p+7Be scattering states (including 3S1, 3P0, 3P1, 3P2, 3D1, 3D2, 3D3, 3F3 partial waves) and the bound 3P2 ground state of 8B, using the Numerov algorithm. They fitted the 3S1 potential depth and width to reproduce the experimental scattering length a01 = 17.34 fm from Pan (2019), and adjusted the 3P2 potential to match the astrophysical S factor at the Gamow energy S17(19+6-5 keV) = (19.0 ± 1.8) eV b from Takei (2018), while also tuning the 3D2 potential to describe the resonance region around 3 MeV. The electric dipole (E1) and magnetic dipole (M1) transition matrix elements were computed in the long-wavelength approximation to obtain cross sections, astrophysical S factors, and reaction rates.

## Key numbers

- S-wave scattering length in the spin=1 channel: a01 = 17.34 fm (reproduced by modified potential VM), compared to the original potential VD which gave a01 = -0.26 fm
- Astrophysical S factor at the Gamow energy: S17(19+6-5 keV) = (19.0 ± 1.8) eV b, used as a fitting constraint
- Extrapolated zero-energy astrophysical S factor: S17(0) ≈ (20.5 ± 0.5) eV b, consistent with the Solar Fusion II estimate of (20.8 ± 0.7_expt ± 1.4_theor) eV b
- Asymptotic normalization coefficient (ANC) for the bound 3P2 state: C2 = 0.538 fm-1 for the modified potential VM, compared to 0.496 fm-1 for the original potential VD
- Binding energy of the 8B(2+,1) ground state: Eb = 0.1375 MeV
- Partial astrophysical S factor at 0.6 keV for the 3S1(VM) → 3P2(VM) transition: Ss(0.6 keV)/C2 = 34.57 eV b fm, consistent with the Baye (2000) relation of 34.74 eV b fm
- Reaction rate at T9 = 0.015 (solar temperature): NA(σv) = 3.10 × 10-12 cm3 mol-1 s-1 for VM, compared to 2.97 × 10-11 cm3 mol-1 s-1 for VD (note: the text shows a possible typo in the VD value at this temperature)

## Context

This work extends the single-channel potential model of Dubovichenko (2019) by incorporating the new precise S-wave scattering length measurements from Pan (2019) and the Gamow-energy S factor from Takei (2018) extracted from solar neutrino flux data. The modified potential is consistent with the theoretical framework of Baye (2000) which relates the S-wave scattering length to the astrophysical S factor at zero energy divided by the square of the ANC. The results agree with the experimental data sets of Hammache (1998, 2001) at intermediate energies, but the calculated reaction rates are substantially lower than those of the NACRE II collaboration (Xu 2013) and Du (2015), suggesting that updated scattering length data may require revisions to standard reaction rate compilations used in solar neutrino modeling.
