# Excited-state uncertainties in lattice-QCD calculations of hadron masses and scattering phase shifts

> Lattice QCD has historically produced energy results interpretable as either estimates relying on implicit assumptions about asymptotic behavior or one-sided upper bounds. New Lanczos methods providing two-sided bounds with less-restrictive assumptions are introduced and quantified in a high-statistics calculation with unphysical quark masses. Two-sided bounds without spectral assumptions provide sub-percent constraints on the nucleon mass. Other bounds, which assume all states in a given energy window are resolved, provide meaningful two-sided constraints on nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts.

## Key claim

Lanczos-based two-sided bounds can provide sub-percent constraints on hadron masses without spectral assumptions, but for nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts, only gap bounds that assume all states below a specified energy threshold are resolved yield phenomenologically useful constraints at current statistical precision.

## Method

The authors perform a high-statistics lattice QCD calculation on a single ensemble of gauge field configurations at unphysical quark masses corresponding to m_pi ~ 800 MeV, using 16,368 configurations and 2.12 x 10^7 sparsened quark propagators. They apply scalar and block Lanczos methods to symmetric correlator matrices for the nucleon and for two-nucleon systems in the I=0 and I=1 channels, constructing residual bounds (requiring only Hermiticity) and gap bounds (requiring a no-missing-states assumption) on energy eigenvalues. The gap bounds are combined with variational bounds and propagated through the Luscher quantization condition to constrain scattering phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass: median Lanczos energy estimator E0 = 1.20349 in lattice units, with statistical uncertainty 0.00018 (0.02%), gap bound width 0.00027 (0.03%), and residual bound width 0.0078 (0.6%)
- I=1 two-nucleon (1D scalar Lanczos): E0 = 2.40311, statistical uncertainty 0.00043, gap bound width 0.0029, residual bound width 0.013
- I=0 two-nucleon (1D scalar Lanczos): E0 = 2.40207, statistical uncertainty 0.00041, gap bound width 0.0026, residual bound width 0.012
- I=1 two-nucleon (3D block Lanczos): E0 = 2.40252, statistical uncertainty 0.00041, gap bound width 0.0021, residual bound width 0.017
- I=0 two-nucleon (10D block Lanczos): E0 = 2.40094, statistical uncertainty 0.00041, gap bound width 0.0025, residual bound width 0.026
- Scattering lengths in both S-wave channels are unnaturally small with |a^{-1}| less than m_pi/10 at 68% confidence

## Context

This work extends the Lanczos methodology for lattice QCD correlator analysis introduced by Wagman (2024) and developed in subsequent studies, providing the first high-statistics demonstration on one- and two-nucleon systems. The residual bounds improve upon traditional variational bounds from Wilson (1981) and Luscher (1990) by providing two-sided constraints without spectral assumptions, while the gap bounds offer a more rigorous alternative to N-state-saturation assumptions that have been shown to fail in multi-hadron systems by Amarasinghe (2021) and in the companion paper. The results enable future work toward spectral-assumption-free determinations of hadronic interactions, though at present the gap bounds under no-missing-states assumptions are required for phenomenologically useful scattering phase shift constraints.
