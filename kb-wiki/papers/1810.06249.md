# Recent theoretical results for electromagnetically induced ultraperipheral reactions of heavy ions

> We briefly review our works on ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. We discuss both $╬│╬│$ and rescattering of hadronic photon fluctuation induced by one nucleus in the collision partner. Production of one and two leptonic and pionic and $p \bar p$ pairs is discussed as an example of photon-photon processes. The production of single vector mesons ($Žü^0$ or $J/Žł$) is an example of the second category. The double-scattering mechanisms of two $Žü^0$ meson production is discussed in addition.

## Key claim

The paper reviews a range of ultraperipheral heavy-ion collision processes, showing that while the equivalent photon approximation provides a good description of dielectron production without free parameters, the agreement for dimuon production is much worse, and the double-scattering mechanism for double rho0 production is insufficient to explain STAR data, leaving the role of possible higher-mass rho resonances as an open question.

## Method

The author calculates cross sections for photon-photon (╬│╬│) and photon-hadronic fluctuation processes in ultraperipheral collisions (UPC) using the equivalent photon approximation in impact-parameter space, with the condition that the impact parameter b > RA + RB to ensure no nuclear overlap. Double-scattering mechanisms for Žü0Žü0 pair production and two lepton pairs are also computed, and the calculations are compared with experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, and STAR.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Szczurek and collaborators, 2013-2018) on UPC processes at RHIC and the LHC, including comparisons with ALICE data for dielectrons (ALICE, 2014) and dipions (ALICE, 2016), ATLAS data for dimuons (ATLAS, 2016), and STAR data for four-pion production (STAR, 2009). The results enable future experimental tests of double-scattering mechanisms in four-lepton production at the LHC and motivate further theoretical studies of possible coherent Žü' or Žü'' meson contributions to explain the discrepancy with STAR four-pion data.
