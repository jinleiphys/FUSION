# Recent theoretical results for electromagnetically induced ultraperipheral reactions of heavy ions

> We briefly review our works on ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions. We discuss both $γγ$ and rescattering of hadronic photon fluctuation induced by one nucleus in the collision partner. Production of one and two leptonic and pionic and $p \bar p$ pairs is discussed as an example of photon-photon processes. The production of single vector mesons ($ρ^0$ or $J/ψ$) is an example of the second category. The double-scattering mechanisms of two $ρ^0$ meson production is discussed in addition.

## Key claim

The paper reviews a range of ultraperipheral heavy-ion collision processes, showing that while the equivalent photon approximation provides a good description of dielectron production without free parameters, the agreement for dimuon production is much worse, and the double-scattering mechanism for double rho0 production is insufficient to explain STAR data, leaving the role of possible higher-mass rho resonances as an open question.

## Method

The author calculates cross sections for photon-photon (γγ) and photon-hadronic fluctuation processes in ultraperipheral collisions (UPC) using the equivalent photon approximation in impact-parameter space, with the condition that the impact parameter b > RA + RB to ensure no nuclear overlap. For charged pion pairs, a multicomponent model is used to describe elementary γγ → ππ cross sections, and for coherent ρ0 production, a mechanism involving photon fluctuation into a virtual ρ0 meson with multiple rescattering in the collision partner is employed. Double-scattering mechanisms for ρ0ρ0 pair production and two lepton pairs are also computed, and the calculations are compared with experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, and STAR.

## Key numbers

- Dielectron invariant mass for PbPb → PbPb e+e−: good agreement with ALICE experimental data without free parameters (no explicit numerical cross section given in text).
- Dimuon production in Pb+Pb → Pb+Pb+μ++μ−: agreement with ATLAS data is "much worst" compared to dielectron case (no explicit numerical discrepancy given).
- Cross section for PbPb → PbPb p pbar at √sNN = 5.02 TeV: 100 μb for ALICE cuts (pt,p > 0.2 GeV, |yp| < 0.9); 160 μb for ATLAS cuts (pt,p > 0.5 GeV, |yp| < 2.5); 500 μb for CMS cuts (pt,p > 0.2 GeV, |yp| < 2.5); 104 μb for LHCb cuts (pt,p > 0.2 GeV, 2 < yp < 4.5).
- Predicted event counts for four-muon production at √sNN = 5.02 TeV with Lint = 1 nb−1: 815 events for |yi| < 2.5, pt > 0.5 GeV; 53 events for |yi| < 2.5, pt > 1.0 GeV; 31 events for |yi| < 0.9, pt > 0.5 GeV; 2 events for |yi| < 0.9, pt > 1.0 GeV; less than 1 event for |yi| < 2.4, pt > 4.0 GeV.
- Predicted event counts for four-electron production at √sNN = 5.5 TeV with Lint = 1 nb−1: 235 events for |yi| < 2.5, pt > 0.5 GeV; 10 events for |yi| < 2.5, pt > 1.0 GeV; 649 events for |yi| < 1.0, pt > 0.2 GeV; 1 event for |yi| < 1.0, pt > 1.0 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies (Szczurek and collaborators, 2013-2018) on UPC processes at RHIC and the LHC, including comparisons with ALICE data for dielectrons (ALICE, 2014) and dipions (ALICE, 2016), ATLAS data for dimuons (ATLAS, 2016), and STAR data for four-pion production (STAR, 2009). The results enable future experimental tests of double-scattering mechanisms in four-lepton production at the LHC and motivate further theoretical studies of possible coherent ρ' or ρ'' meson contributions to explain the discrepancy with STAR four-pion data.
