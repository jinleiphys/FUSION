# Improved Empirical Hamiltonians for Neutron-rich Nuclei in 132Sn region

> Using recent experimental information for 132Sn region, an empirical Hamiltonian is obtained by some modifications of a Hamiltonian (CW5082) originally derived from the 208Pb region. Shell model calculations with the new Hamiltonian shows remarkable improvement in the predictive power when compared with the available experimental results. It overcomes many limitations of the CW5082 Hamiltonian in this region, specially for N>=84 isotones. The calculated level spectra and B(E2) values with the new Hamiltonian, also compare well with the available results calculated with the CD-Bonn and SKX Hamiltonians, reflecting consistency in the wave function structure at least in the low-lying regions. Interesting behaviour of effective charges is revealed in this region. It is shown that a drastic reduction of proton effective charge is necessary for obtaining B(E2) values for the N=84 isotones. Neutron effective charge is found to be in the range (0.62 - 0.72)e. We predict the spectroscopic properties of 135,136Sn not yet known experimentally. Further improvement of the modified Hamiltonian is also initiated.

# Improved Empirical Hamiltonians for Neutron-rich Nuclei in 132Sn Region

## Key claim

The authors claim that modifying only 22 of 2101 two-body matrix elements (tbmes) of the existing CW5082 Hamiltonian, guided by recent experimental data for N=84 isotones, produces a new Hamiltonian (SMN5082) that yields remarkably improved agreement with experimental level spectra and B(E2) values for neutron-rich nuclei in the 132Sn region, though a systematic over-binding of approximately 0.1-0.6 MeV per valence neutron pair persists for N≥84 nuclei.

## Method

The authors start from the CW5082 Hamiltonian, originally derived by A^{-1/3} scaling from the 208Pb region, and replace all single-particle energies with experimentally determined values. They modify six neutron-neutron diagonal tbmes (multiplied by 0.48 to reproduce the 134Sn binding energy of -6.365 MeV), three ν2f_{7/2}^2 tbmes using 134Sn excited states, one ν1h_{9/2}2f_{7/2} tbme, and twelve proton-neutron tbmes using 134Sb binding energy and excited levels, producing the SMN5082 Hamiltonian. A further variant (SMPN5082) additionally modifies four proton-proton tbmes using 134Te data. Shell model calculations are performed with the OXBASH code using a 132Sn inert core with five proton and six neutron valence orbitals.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 134Sn: -6.365 MeV (used to set the 0.48 scaling factor for neutron-neutron tbmes)
- Excited states in 134Sn used for tbme modification: 725.6 keV (2+), 1073.4 keV (4+), 1247.4 keV (6+), and 2508.9 keV (8+)
- Binding energy of 134Sb: -12.952 MeV; excited levels used include 13.0 keV (1-), 330.7 keV (2-), 383.5 keV (3-), 554.8 keV (4-), 283.0 keV (7-), 1073 keV (8-), 2434 keV (10+), 2126 keV (9+), 4094 keV (10-), 4425 keV (11-), and 4517 keV (12-)
- Binding energy of 134Te: -20.56 MeV; excited levels used: 1279 keV (2+), 1576 keV (4+), 1692 keV (6+)
- Neutron effective charge range: (0.62 - 0.72)e
- B(E2) values for 134Sn with e_n^{eff}=0.72e: 73.2 e^2fm^4 (2+→0+), 73.8 e^2fm^4 (4+→2+), 36.5 e^2fm^4 (6+→4+), 4.8 e^2fm^4 (8+→6+); the 6+→4+ value of 36.5 matches the experimental 36±7 e^2fm^4
- For 134Te, proton effective charge of 1.34±0.01 reproduces the 6+→4+ B(E2), while 1.54±0.10 reproduces the 0+→2+ B(E2)
- For N=84 isotones, the best single set of effective charges is e_p^{eff}=1.0 and e_n^{eff}=0.62
- Predicted 2+ energy in 136Sn: 578 keV (compared to approximately 600 keV from systematics)
- Predicted B(E2) for 6+→4+ transition in 136Sn: 12.9 e^2fm^4 with e_n^{eff}=0.62

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Sarkar and Saha Sarkar (2001) which found that the CW5082 and KH5082 Hamiltonians worked reasonably for N=82-83 isotones but poorly for N≥84 isotones in the 132Sn region. The authors modify the CW5082 Hamiltonian originally developed by Chou and Warburton (1993) from the 208Pb region, incorporating recent precise binding energy measurements by Fogelberg et al. (1999) that changed the 0- state binding energy in 134Sb by about 200 keV. The results are compared with calculations using CD-Bonn and SKX Hamiltonians, showing consistency in wave function structure for low-lying states. The paper resolves the missing 12+ isomer issue in 136Te discussed by Korgul et al. (2001) and provides predictions for 135Sn and 136Sn that can guide future experiments and r-process nucleosynthesis calculations.
