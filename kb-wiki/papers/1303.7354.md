# Spectator induced electromagnetic effect on directed flow in heavy ion collisions

> We estimate the electromagnetic effect of the spectator charge on azimuthal anisotropies observed in heavy ion collisions. For peripheral Pb+Pb reactions at the top energy of the CERN Super Proton Synchrotron, $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV, we predict this effect to bring very large distortions to the observed directed flow, $v_1$, of positive and negative pions emitted close to beam rapidity. The overall magnitude of this effect is comparable to values of $v_1$ reported by the WA98 experiment. We argue that also at lower rapidities, the spectator induced electromagnetic effect may result in the splitting of values of $v_1$ observed for positive and negative pions. Such a splitting is visible in the data reported by the STAR Collaboration from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan. Both effects are sensitive to the space-time scenario assumed for pion emission. Therefore, they bring new information on the collision dynamics.

## Key claim

The spectator-induced electromagnetic interaction can produce directed flow values for charged pions that are comparable in magnitude to the experimentally measured $v_1$, meaning that a large part of the observed directed flow may be of electromagnetic rather than strong-interaction origin, with the effect depending sensitively on the assumed pion emission time $t_E$.

## Method

The authors model a peripheral Pb+Pb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV with 60 participating nucleons, representing the two spectator systems as homogeneously charged Lorentz-contracted spheres. Charged pions are emitted from a single space-time point with initial spectra taken from p+p data and full azimuthal symmetry, then numerically traced through the combined electromagnetic fields of both spectators using the classical relativistic Lorentz force equation until they reach 10,000 fm from the interaction point.

## Key numbers

- For peripheral Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3$ GeV, the spectator-induced electromagnetic effect produces $v_1$ values exceeding 0.2 (20%) for negative pions close to beam rapidity.
- At $y/y_\mathrm{beam}=0.5$, absolute $v_1$ values change from about 2.4% at $t_E=0$ fm/c down to about 0.5% at $t_E=1$ fm/c.
- At the lowest considered transverse momentum $p_T=75$ MeV/c, absolute $v_1$ values of about 0.6 are attained for positive pions close to beam rapidity.
- The geometrical impact parameter for 60 participating nucleons is $b_{geom}=10.61$ fm, with spectator charge $Q=70$ elementary units and effective impact parameter $b=12.13$ fm after center-of-gravity displacement of $\Delta b=0.76$ fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of spectator-induced electromagnetic effects on charged pion ratios (Rybicki and Szczurek, 2012) to the directed flow observable, and provides a possible electromagnetic explanation for the $v_1$ values reported by the WA98 experiment at SPS energies. The predicted charge splitting of $v_1$ for positive versus negative pions is argued to be visible in preliminary STAR data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan (Pandit, 2012), and the sensitivity to emission time offers a new probe of the space-time dynamics of pion production that complements existing hydrodynamical approaches (Bozek, 2012).
