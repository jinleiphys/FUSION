# Charmonia and Bottomonia in a Magnetic Field

> We study the effect of a static homogeneous external magnetic field on charmonium and bottomonium states. In an external magnetic field, quarkonium states do not have a conserved center-of-mass momentum. Instead there is a new conserved quantity called the pseudomomentum which takes into account the Lorentz force on the particles in the system. When written in terms of the pseudomomentum, the internal and center-of-mass motions do not decouple and, as a result, the properties of quarkonia depend on the states' center-of-mass momentum. We analyze the behavior of heavy particle-antiparticle pairs subject to an external magnetic field assuming a three dimensional harmonic potential and Cornell potential plus spin-spin interaction. In the case of the Cornell potential, we also take into account the mixing of the eta_c and J/psi states and eta_b and Upsilon states due to the background magnetic field. We then numerically calculate the dependence of the masses and mixing fractions on the magnitude of the background magnetic field and center-of-mass momentum of the state.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in a static homogeneous external magnetic field, the properties of charmonium and bottomonium states depend on the center-of-mass pseudomomentum because the internal and center-of-mass motions do not decouple, contrary to the zero-field case where center-of-mass momentum is conserved and can be separated.

## Method

The authors derived the relative Hamiltonian for a particle-antiparticle pair in a constant magnetic field using the conserved pseudomomentum operator, then solved the resulting Schrodinger equation numerically for the Cornell potential plus a spin-spin interaction. They also obtained analytic solutions for the case of a harmonic interaction potential as a check on their numerical methods. The mixing of spin-singlet and spin-triplet states (eta_c with J/psi and eta_b with Upsilon) due to the magnetic field was treated by diagonalizing a two-state effective Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- For charmonium, the 1s spin-singlet/triplet splitting in vacuum is approximately Delta E = 113 MeV
- For bottomonium, the 1s spin-singlet/triplet splitting in vacuum is approximately Delta E = 63 MeV
- Magnetic fields expected at RHIC are B ~ m_pi^2 ~ 10^18 Gauss
- Magnetic fields expected at LHC are B ~ 15 m_pi^2 ~ 1.5 x 10^19 Gauss
- Magnetar magnetic fields are on the order of 10^18 to 10^19 Gauss
- For the spin-spin potential V_s(r) = gamma * exp(-beta * r), the fitted parameters for charmonia are gamma = 0.825 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum mechanical bound states in magnetic fields, which dates back to Lamb's work on the motional Stark effect (PhysRev.85.259, 1952) and Herold's treatment of two-particle systems (Herold, 1981). The paper contributes to ongoing discussions of strong magnetic field effects on QCD bound states, including work by Marasinghe (2011), Tuchin (2011), Yang (2011), Simonov (2012, 2013), Andreichikov (2013), and Machado (2013). The results enable phenomenological predictions for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where the suppression of Upsilon decays to lepton pairs and the appearance of eta_b decays to lepton pairs could serve as experimental signatures of strong magnetic fields.
