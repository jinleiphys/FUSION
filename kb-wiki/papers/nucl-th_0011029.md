# Dynamical interpretation of chemical freeze-out in heavy ion collisions

> It is demonstrated that there exists a direct correlation between chemical freeze-out point and the softest point of the equation of state where the pressure divided by the energy density, $p(ε)/ε$, has a minimum. A dynamical model is given as an example where the passage of the softest point coincides with the condition for chemical freeze-out, namely an average energy per hadron $\approx$ 1 GeV. The sensitivity of the result to the equation of state used is discussed.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates a direct correlation between the chemical freeze-out point in heavy ion collisions and the softest point of the equation of state (where the pressure-to-energy-density ratio p(ε)/ε has a minimum), but this correlation is shown specifically for the Mixed Phase model and is sensitive to the choice of equation of state used.

## Method
The authors used the Mixed Phase (MP) model, which assumes quarks and gluons can coexist homogeneously with hadrons, with a density-dependent mean-field potential for quarks and gluons of the form U(ρ)=A/ρ^γ. They simulated central Au+Au collisions using initial conditions from the quark-gluon string model, followed by isentropic expansion within a scaled hydrodynamic prescription, and compared the resulting dynamical trajectories in the temperature-chemical potential plane to the chemical freeze-out curve defined by an average energy per hadron of approximately 1 GeV.

## Key numbers
- The softest point for zero baryon density is at energy density ε_SP ≈ 0.45 GeV/fm^3, consistent with lattice QCD estimates.
- The deconfinement temperature at zero baryon density is T_dec = 153 MeV in the MP model.
- The admixture of unbound quarks at the softest point amounts to about 13% at beam energy E_lab = 150 AGeV and 8% at E_lab = 10 AGeV.
- The softest point correlates with an average energy per quark of ε/ρ ≈ 350 MeV, close to the constituent quark mass.
- The softest point is gradually washed out at baryon densities n_B ≥ 0.4 n_0, where n_0 is normal nuclear density.

## Context
This work extends the observation by Cleymans and Redlich (1998, 1999) that chemical freeze-out occurs when the average energy per hadron is approximately 1 GeV, by providing a dynamical interpretation linking this condition to the softest point of the equation of state. The MP model results differ from the two-phase bag model approach of Heiselberg and Hjorth-Jensen (1994), which has a first-order phase transition with the softest point at ε_SP > 1 GeV/fm^3 independent of baryon density, and from the interacting hadron gas model which has no softest point. The findings enable further study of how the equation of state affects the expansion dynamics and chemical equilibration in heavy ion collisions across a range of beam energies from SIS to SPS.
