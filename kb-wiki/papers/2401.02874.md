# Chiral condensate and the equation of state at nonzero baryon density from the hadron resonance gas model with a repulsive mean field

> We study the QCD equation of state and the chiral condensate using the hadron resonance gas model with repulsive mean-field interactions. We find that the repulsive interactions improve the agreement with the lattice results on the derivatives of the pressure with respect to the baryon chemical potential up to eighth order. From the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate we estimate the crossover temperature as a function of baryon chemical potential, $T_{pc}(μ_B)$. We find that the chiral crossover line starts to deviate significantly from the chemical freeze-out line already for $μ_B>400$ MeV. Furthermore, we find that the chiral pseudocritical line can be parametrized as $T_{pc}(μ_B)/T_{pc}(0)=1-κ_2 (μ_B/T_{pc} (0))^2-κ_4 (μ_B/T_{pc} (0))^4$ with $κ_2=0.0150(2)$ and $κ_4=3.1(6) \times 10^{-5}$, which are in agreement with lattice QCD results for small values of $μ_B$. For the first time we find a tiny but non-zero value of $κ_4$ in our study.

## Key claim

The authors find that including repulsive mean-field interactions in the hadron resonance gas model improves agreement with lattice QCD results for higher-order baryon number fluctuations, and they estimate the chiral crossover temperature as a function of baryon chemical potential, finding that the chiral pseudocritical line deviates significantly from the chemical freeze-out line for baryon chemical potentials above 400 MeV.

## Method

The authors used the hadron resonance gas model with a repulsive mean-field interaction proportional to baryon density, with the mean-field coefficient K=33 GeV⁻² determined by fitting to lattice QCD results for second- and fourth-order baryon number fluctuations. They calculated the renormalized chiral condensate as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential using quark mass derivatives of hadron masses, and defined the chiral crossover temperature as the temperature where the condensate drops to half its vacuum value. The study included hadron states from the Particle Data Group up to 3 GeV mass plus quark model predicted states, with the Boltzmann approximation for baryons and antibaryons at zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Mean-field coefficient K = 33 GeV⁻² (equivalent to 450 MeV fm³ in earlier works)
- κ₂ = 0.0150(2) for the chiral pseudocritical line parametrization T_pc(μ_B)/T_pc(0) = 1 - κ₂(μ_B/T_pc(0))² - κ₄(μ_B/T_pc(0))⁴
- κ₄ = 3.1(6) × 10⁻⁵ for the same parametrization
- T_pc(μ_B=0) = 161.5(1.6) MeV for the repulsive QMHRG model with K=33 GeV⁻²
- T_pc(μ_B=0) = 161.2(1.7) MeV for the ideal (noninteracting) QMHRG model
- Maximum net-baryon density of 1.53(13)n₀ reached at μ_B=700 MeV for K=0 and at μ_B=750 MeV for K=33 GeV⁻², where n₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ is nuclear saturation density
- The chiral crossover temperature is more than 8 MeV larger than the freeze-out temperature at μ_B=400 MeV, and more than 20 MeV larger at μ_B=750 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of the chiral condensate in the ideal hadron resonance gas model (Biswas 2022) by including repulsive mean-field interactions among baryons, following the approach of Huovinen (2017, 2018) and Pal (2020, 2021). The results agree with lattice QCD curvature coefficients from the HotQCD Collaboration (2018) and Borsanyi (2020) for small baryon chemical potentials, and provide the first nonzero estimate of the quartic curvature coefficient κ₄ from a hadron resonance gas model. The finding that the chiral crossover line deviates from the chemical freeze-out line for μ_B>400 MeV has implications for interpreting heavy-ion collision data from the beam energy scan program at RHIC and experiments at GSI, suggesting significant nonequilibrium chemical evolution in the hadronic phase at these energies.
