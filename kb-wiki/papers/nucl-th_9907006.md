# Functional approach to the non-mesonic decay of Lambda-hypernuclei

> We present an evaluation of the non-mesonic decay widths for Lambda-hypernuclei (Lambda N --> NN, Lambda NN --> NNN) within the framework of the polarization propagator method. The full Lambda self-energy is evaluated microscopically in nuclear matter by using the functional approach, which supplies a theoretically well grounded approximation scheme for the classification of the relevant diagrams, according to the prescriptions of the bosonic loop expansion. We employ average Fermi momenta, suitably adapted to different mass number regions (medium-light, medium and heavy hypernuclei). Moreover, we study the dependence of the decay rates on the NN and Lambda-N short range correlations. With a proper choice of the parameters which control these correlations in the new approximation scheme, it is possible to reproduce the experimental decay widths for A > 10 hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by using the functional approach and the bosonic loop expansion to microscopically calculate the two-particle–two-hole (2p-2h) polarization propagator, and by properly choosing the short-range correlation parameters (the Landau parameters \(g'\) and \(g'_{\Lambda}\)), it is possible to reproduce the experimental non-mesonic decay widths for hypernuclei with mass number \(A \gtrsim 10\), but the calculation is performed in nuclear matter with average Fermi momenta rather than in finite nuclei via the local density approximation.

## Method

The authors evaluated the \(\Lambda\) self-energy in nuclear matter using the polarization propagator method, where the decay width is obtained from \(\Gamma_{\Lambda} = -2\,\text{Im}\,\Sigma_{\Lambda}\). They employed a functional approach and the semiclassical (bosonic loop) expansion to classify and compute the relevant Feynman diagrams, including ring-dressed meson propagators and one-boson-loop corrections (exchange, self-energy, and correlation diagrams) for the 2p-2h polarization propagator. The calculation used average Fermi momenta adapted to three mass regions (medium-light, medium, and heavy hypernuclei) and treated the NN and \(\Lambda\)N short-range correlations via Landau parameters \(g'\) and \(g'_{\Lambda}\), with the exchange of \(\pi\), \(\rho\), \(\omega\), and \(\sigma\) mesons included.

## Key numbers

- The free \(\Lambda\) decay yields a nucleon momentum of about \(100\) MeV, while the non-mesonic process \(\Lambda N \rightarrow NN\) produces final nucleons with momenta of about \(420\) MeV.
- The ratio of non-mesonic widths between heavy and light hypernuclei is \(\Gamma_{NM}(^{208}_{\Lambda}\text{Pb})/\Gamma_{NM}(^{12}_{\Lambda}\text{C}) \simeq 1.4\), where \(\Gamma_{NM} = \Gamma_1 + \Gamma_2\).
- The cut-off for the \(\pi\Lambda N\) form factor is \(\Lambda_{\pi} = 1.3\) GeV, and for the \(\rho NN\) form factor it is \(\Lambda_{\rho} = 2.5\) GeV.
- The weak coupling constants are fixed from free \(\Lambda\) decay: \(G \simeq 2.211 \times 10^{-7}/m_{\pi}^2\), \(A = 1.06\), and \(B = -7.10\).

## Context

This work extends the polarization propagator method previously applied by Alberico et al. (1999) to hypernuclear decay, where the 2p-2h polarization propagator was treated phenomenologically using data on pionic atoms. Here, the authors provide a microscopic derivation of the 2p-2h contributions using the functional approach and bosonic loop expansion, which offers a theoretically consistent scheme for classifying diagrams beyond the RPA and Hartree-Fock approximations. The results enable a parameter-free description of the non-mesonic decay for medium to heavy hypernuclei and can be used to test the role of short-range correlations and meson-exchange currents in weak hypernuclear processes.
