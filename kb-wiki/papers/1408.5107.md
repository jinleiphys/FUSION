# Propagation of Fluctuations in Au+Au Collisions at FAIR energy

> Event by event fluctuations of particle multiplicities and their ratios are considered to be sensitive probes to the exotic phenomena in high energy heavy ion collisions like phase transtion or the occurence of critical point. These phenomena might take place at different time after the collision based on fulfilling the required conditions at a particular time. Fluctuations are therefore expected to show non-monotonic behaviour at the of time of occurence of these phenomena. Experimentally, fluctuations are measured at freezeout. In this work, using the hybrid version of the UrQMD event generator, we have investigated the propagation of fluctuations of particle multiplicities, their ratios and the ratio of total positive and negative charges in AuAu collisions at E_{lab} < 90 AGeV. Two commonly used experimental measures i.e., {σ^2}/mean and {ν_{dyn}} have been used in the analysis in a given acceptance. The hybrid model, i.e., UrQMD with hydrodynamic evolution has been used to study the effect of hydrodynamic evolution on these conventional fluctuation measures. It is observed that the fluctuations as measured by {σ^2}/mean and {ν_{dyn}} gets reduced considerably at freezeout. The dominat structures present at the initial stage of the evolution get smoothen out. However, the energy dependence of the fluctuations remain preserved till the freezeout. The hydrodynamic evolution of the model with chiral equation of state shows considerably higher fluctuation at lower collision energy as compared to pure hadronic transport version or the hybrid version with hadronic equation of state. The time evolution of the higher order moments of net-proton distributios for particles in a specified coverage showed similar behaviour.

## Key claim

The paper claims that event-by-event fluctuations of particle multiplicities and their ratios, as measured by σ²/mean and ν_dyn, are significantly reduced at freezeout compared to their initial values, but the energy dependence of these fluctuations is preserved throughout the evolution. The hybrid UrQMD model with a chiral equation of state shows considerably higher fluctuations at lower collision energies compared to the pure hadronic transport version or the hybrid version with a hadronic equation of state.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD 3.3 event generator in both pure hadronic transport mode and hybrid mode (with 3+1 dimensional hydrodynamic evolution) to simulate Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 10 AGeV to 90 AGeV. They analyzed particles within pseudorapidity |η| < 1 and studied the time evolution of fluctuations from t = 1 fm/c to t = 10 fm/c using σ²/mean for multiplicities and ν_dyn for ratios (K/π and Q⁺/Q⁻). For net-proton distributions, they extracted higher-order moments (Sσ and Kσ²) within the kinematic acceptance of |η| < 0.5 and pT between 0.4 and 0.8 GeV/c, matching STAR experimental cuts.

## Key numbers

- Net-baryon density reaches values up to 10 times nuclear matter density during the overlap of nuclei at FAIR energies.
- The hydrodynamic evolution start times are 5.56, 3.94, 3.22, 2.79, 2.28, and 1.86 fm/c for beam energies of 10, 20, 30, 40, 60, and 90 AGeV, respectively.
- The threshold for stopping hydrodynamic evolution is an energy density of approximately 730 MeV/fm³ above the ground state.
- ν_dyn for K/π is positive at all energies studied, with higher values at lower beam energy, showing a decreasing trend with increasing Elab.
- ν_dyn for Q⁺/Q⁻ becomes negative after hydrodynamic evolution, indicating increased correlation between opposite charges, consistent with experimental results at RHIC.
- The chiral equation of state produces enhanced ν_dyn values at lower beam energy compared to the hadronic equation of state or pure transport.

## Context

This work extends studies of fluctuation propagation in heavy-ion collisions, following earlier investigations by Shk (2013) on point-like disturbance propagation through hydrodynamic media. The authors compare their results to experimental measurements from the NA49 collaboration (using Φq) and the STAR collaboration (using ν±,dyn), as well as to the RHIC beam energy scan results for net-proton higher-order moments. The study enables predictions for the FAIR energy range (Elab < 90 AGeV) where high net-baryon density matter is produced, and demonstrates that the chiral equation of state produces distinguishable fluctuation signatures at lower collision energies that could be tested experimentally.
