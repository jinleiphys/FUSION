# Implications of correlations and fluctuations in small systems

> The small collision systems like p+p and p+A collisions have shown new features like A+A collisions in the relativistic regime. These new aspects in small systems which have altered our research and understanding on the two-particle correlation measurements have been provided. Additionally, a critical observation of the fluctuation measurements provides new ways to infer such novel happening in the small collision systems. The ongoing and future endeavors towards the new measurements are also discussed.

## Key claim

Small collision systems (p+p and p+A) exhibit QGP-like collective features such as long-range azimuthal correlations and anisotropic flow, challenging the long-held assumption that such phenomena are exclusive to large A+A systems; however, the paper emphasizes that a definitive conclusion on whether a thermalized QGP is formed in small systems remains elusive, as hydrodynamic models cannot yet describe all multi-particle cumulant measurements and key QGP signatures like jet quenching have not been observed in these systems.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental results on two-particle angular correlations (Δη-Δφ correlation functions) and event-by-event fluctuations of flow harmonics and mean transverse momentum in p+p, p+A, and A+A collisions at RHIC and LHC energies. It compares measurements of anisotropic flow coefficients v_n obtained from k-particle correlations, symmetric cumulants SC(m,n), and the Pearson correlation coefficient ρ(v_n{2}²,[p_T]) across collision systems, and evaluates model comparisons with PYTHIA 8, AMPT, HIJING, and IP-Glasma+MUSIC+UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Long-range near-side ridge structure observed in p+p collisions at √s = 7 TeV by CMS for 2.0 < |Δη| < 4.8, in high-multiplicity events with N ≈ 90 or higher, most prominent in intermediate p_T range 1 < p_T < 3 GeV/c
- In p+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, long-range correlations studied for 2.0 < |Δη| < 4.0 by CMS showed qualitatively comparable near-side structure with larger correlation strength than in p+p
- Balance function narrowing observed in p+p, p+Pb, and Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 7, 5.02, and 2.76 TeV respectively, for 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c in higher-multiplicity events
- In Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV, v_n{2}(p_T^a)/v_n[2](p_T^a) and r_2 show deviations from unity, most visible in most central collisions
- Anisotropic flow coefficients show ordering v_2 > v_3 > v_4 in small systems (p+p and p+Pb), similar to large collision systems
- Modified Pearson correlation coefficient ρ(v_n{2}²,[p_T]) in p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV is negative and approximately independent of N_ch
- In peripheral Pb+Pb collisions (⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ < 100), √C_m/M(p_T)_m dependence on ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ is very close to that in p+p collisions; for ⟨dN_ch/dη⟩ > 500, a strong decrease is observed
- CMB fluctuations are on the order of 10⁻⁴ with respect to the thermal distribution

## Context

This review extends the work of CMS (2010) on the ridge in p+p collisions and CMS (2012) on p+Pb correlations, which first demonstrated QGP-like features in small systems. It builds on ALICE (2015) balance function studies and ALICE (2019) multi-particle cumulant measurements that showed v_2 > v_3 > v_4 ordering in small systems. The paper contrasts with the traditional view that p+p and p+A collisions serve only as baseline systems for A+A collisions, and it highlights open questions regarding whether observed flow signals have a hydrodynamic origin as argued by Weller (2017) or whether hydrodynamic simulations remain inadequate as noted by Zhou (2020). The upcoming LHC Run-3 and HL-LHC data are expected to provide higher-multiplicity p+p collisions to help resolve these questions.
