# Hard Exclusive Scattering in QCD

> We review the theory of hard exclusive scattering in Quantum Chromodynamics. After recalling the classical counting rules which describe the leading scale dependence of form factors and elastic reactions at fixed angle, the pedagogical example of the pion form factor is developped in some detail in order to show explicitely what factorization means in the QCD framework. The inclusion of transverse degrees of freedom leads to the discussion of Sudakov effects which are crucial for protecting the calculation from dangerous infrared regions. The picture generalizes to many hard reactions; a strategy to extract distribution amplitudes from future data is sketched. We discuss also the particular case of hadron-hadron collisions where the independent scattering mechanism dominates asymptotically and where a different factorization formula applies. We briefly present the concepts of color transparency and nuclear filtering and critically discuss the few present data on this subject.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theory of hard exclusive scattering in QCD, arguing that while the leading-twist factorization formalism predicts power-law scaling of form factors and elastic cross sections, the independent scattering mechanism (Landshoff process) asymptotically dominates in hadron-hadron collisions with a different, slower power-law falloff, and the interplay between these mechanisms remains unsettled.

## Method

The authors develop the QCD factorization formalism for hard exclusive processes using the pedagogical example of the pion electromagnetic form factor, deriving the leading-order hard scattering amplitude from Feynman diagrams in the collinear approximation. They then incorporate radiative corrections through the renormalization group evolution of the pion distribution amplitude, resumming large logarithms via the integro-differential evolution equation with the characteristic kernel \(V_{q\bar{q}\rightarrow q\bar{q}}\). The analysis extends to the independent scattering mechanism in hadron-hadron collisions, where the power counting is performed by counting delta functions of large momenta and transverse momentum constraints from hadronic wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Pion form factor scaling: \(F_{\pi}(Q^2) \propto 1/Q^2\) asymptotically
- Nucleon form factor scaling: \(F_N(Q^2) \propto 1/Q^4\) for helicity-conserving processes
- Compton scattering differential cross section scaling: \(d\sigma/dt \sim (1/s^6) f(t/s)\) for valence state transitions
- Independent scattering for meson-meson scattering: \(d\sigma/dt \propto \langle b^2 \rangle s^{-5}\)
- Independent scattering for proton-proton scattering: \(d\sigma/dt \propto (\langle b^2 \rangle)^2 s^{-8}\)
- Pion decay constant: \(f_{\pi} = 133\) MeV
- Evolution exponent for the first non-asymptotic term in the distribution amplitude: \(A_2/\beta_1 = -0.62\)
- Asymptotic pion distribution amplitude: \(\varphi(x,Q\rightarrow\infty) \sim 6f_{\pi}x(1-x)\)

## Context

This review extends the pre-QCD counting rules of Brodsky and Farrar (1973) by providing the full QCD factorization framework, including the evolution of distribution amplitudes and Sudakov effects. The work discusses the Landshoff independent scattering mechanism (Landshoff, 1974) which was shown by Botts and Sterman (1989) to be a distinct integration region within QCD rather than a separate process. The paper enables future experimental extraction of distribution amplitudes from exclusive scattering data, noting that non-perturbative methods such as lattice calculations and QCD sum rules remain too primitive to be trusted, though the Chernyak and Zhitnitsky (1984) distribution amplitude provides useful rate estimates.
