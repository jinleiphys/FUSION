# Antimatter production in proton-proton and heavy-ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energies

> One of the striking features of particle production at high beam energies is the near equal abundance of matter and antimatter in the central rapidity region. In this paper we study how this symmetry is reached as the beam energy is increased. In particular, we quantify explicitly the energy dependence of the approach to matter/antimatter symmetry in proton-proton and in heavy-ion collisions. Expectations are presented also for the production of more complex forms of antimatter like antihypernuclei.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies how the approach to matter/antimatter symmetry in the central rapidity region depends on collision energy, showing that the antiproton-to-proton ratio increases toward unity as beam energy increases, with the ratio being systematically larger in proton-proton collisions than in heavy-ion collisions at the same center-of-mass energy due to a lower baryo-chemical potential in pp collisions.

## Method

The authors used the statistical thermal model within the grand-canonical ensemble for heavy-ion collisions and a canonical formulation for strangeness conservation in pp collisions. They fitted the energy dependence of the baryo-chemical potential μ_B in pp collisions to measured antiproton/proton ratios using the THERMUS code, while adopting the same temperature parametrization as for heavy-ion collisions. They then compared statistical thermal model predictions with coalescence model calculations for (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypernuclei ratios using data from the STAR Collaboration at RHIC.

## Key numbers

- The parametrization for μ_B in pp collisions gives d_pp = 0.4 GeV and e_pp = 0.1599 GeV^{-1} in the formula μ_B = d_pp/(1 + e_pp √s_NN)
- For heavy-ion collisions, the freeze-out temperature parametrization uses a = 0.166 ± 0.002 GeV, b = 0.139 ± 0.016 GeV^{-1}, c = 0.053 ± 0.021 GeV^{-3}, d = 1.308 ± 0.028 GeV, and e = 0.273 ± 0.008 GeV^{-1}
- At LHC energies (√s_NN = 7 TeV), the chemical potential is smaller than 1 MeV, making antimatter/matter ratios close to unity
- The statistical thermal model predicts that the anti-³He/³He ratio equals exp[-6 μ_B/T], approximately (pbar/p)³

## Context

This work extends the statistical thermal model analysis of Andronic et al. (2010) on light nuclei and hypernuclei production in heavy-ion collisions by providing a systematic comparison between pp and heavy-ion systems. The authors derive the first parametrization of μ_B energy dependence in pp collisions, enabling predictions for antimatter production at LHC energies. The results enable future experimental tests at LHC to distinguish between thermal and coalescence production mechanisms for (anti)nuclei and (anti)hypernuclei.
