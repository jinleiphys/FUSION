# Majoron emitting neutrinoless double beta decay in the electroweak chiral gauge extensions

> Fundamental mechanisms for Majoron emitting neutrinoless double beta decay in SU(3)_C x G_W x U(1) models, for electroweak flavor chiral extensions, G_W = SU(3)_L and SU(4)_L are pointed out. Both kinds of known Majoron emitting processes, charged Majoron emitting where the massless Nambu-Goldstone boson itself carries lepton charge, $L=-2$, and the ordinary Majoron emitting where the boson has a small mass are found possible. PACS numbers: 11.15.Ex, 12.60.Fr, 14.80.Cp

# Majoron emitting neutrinoless double beta decay in the electroweak chiral gauge extensions

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both charged Majoron and ordinary Majoron emitting neutrinoless double beta decay processes are possible in SU(3)_C x G_W x U(1) models with G_W = SU(3)_L or SU(4)_L, but the specific mechanism realized depends on whether the vacuum expectation value of the scalar field sigma_1^0 is zero (allowing charged Majoron emission with conserved leptobarion number) or nonzero (allowing ordinary Majoron emission with spontaneously broken leptobarion symmetry).

## Method

The authors construct the Yukawa interactions for leptons with scalar sextets in SU(3)_L and scalar decuplets in SU(4)_L gauge extensions, identifying the specific couplings that give rise to Majoron emission vertices. They derive the decay amplitudes for ordinary Majoron and charged Majoron processes, compare them with the amplitude for neutrinoless double beta decay, and use experimental half-life limits from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment on germanium-76 together with nuclear matrix elements from the literature to extract bounds on the effective Majoron-neutrino coupling strength.

## Key numbers

- Experimental half-life limit for neutrinoless double beta decay of germanium-76: t_{1/2} > 5.6 x 10^24 yr (90% C.L.) from Particle Data Group (1996)
- Improved half-life limit for the same decay: t_{1/2} > 7.4 x 10^24 yr (90% C.L.) from the Heidelberg-Moscow experiment (1997)
- Bound on effective Majoron-neutrino coupling for charged Majoron emission: g_eff < 0.18 (90% C.L.), using experimental limit T_{1/2} > 5.85 x 10^21 yr from Heidelberg-Moscow experiment and nuclear matrix elements from Hirsch et al. (1996)
- Bound on effective Majoron-neutrino coupling for ordinary Majoron emission: g_eff < 2.3 x 10^{-4}, using experimental limit T_{1/2} > 7.91 x 10^21 yr from Heidelberg-Moscow experiment and nuclear matrix elements from Hirsch et al. (1996)
- Upper bound on the vacuum expectation value of sigma_1^0 from the rho mass: <sigma_1^0> < 10 MeV
- Upper bound on the mass of the new heavy neutral gauge boson Z': M_{Z'} < 2.2 TeV from matching gauge couplings at the electroweak scale, with absolute upper limit M_{Z'} < 3.2 TeV from sin^2 theta_W < 1/4
- Constraint on electroweak mixing angle: sin^2 theta_W < 1/4, with Landau pole at sin^2 theta_W = 1/4

## Context

This work extends the analysis of neutrinoless double beta decay mechanisms in G_0 models previously studied by Pleitez and Tonasse (1993), who noted contributions that do not depend explicitly on neutrino mass. The paper contrasts its results with the Gelmini-Roncadelli model (1981), which has been ruled out because its vacuum expectation values contribute to the Z boson mass and allow Z decay into Majorons, conflicting with measured Z widths. In the G_0 models presented here, only a Fermi-scale vacuum expectation value contributes to the Z mass, avoiding this conflict. The bounds derived serve to constrain the G_0 models, and the authors note that the new gauge bosons are guaranteed to be below a few TeV, making these models testable at next-generation colliders.
