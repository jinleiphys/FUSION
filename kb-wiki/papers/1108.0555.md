# Multistep shell model description of spin-aligned neutron-proton pair coupling: The formalism

> The multistep shell model was extended recently to incorporate both neutron and proton degrees of freedom and applied to study the structure of $N=Z$ systems with four, six and eight particles [arXiv:1108.0269]. In this work we give a brief introduction to the formalism thus developed. A more detailed explanation with applications will be updated later.

## Key claim

The multistep shell model (MSM) has been extended to incorporate both neutron and proton degrees of freedom, enabling the description of spin-aligned neutron-proton pair coupling in N=Z systems; this paper presents only the formalism, with detailed applications promised for a later update.

## Method

The authors construct many-body states for N=Z systems by solving the shell-model equation in successive steps: first building two-particle states (neutron-proton, proton-proton, and neutron-neutron pairs) via TDA equations, then using these as building blocks for four-particle states (two neutrons plus two protons) in a non-orthogonal overcomplete basis that includes both like-particle and np-pair couplings. The formalism is extended to six-particle states (three neutrons and three protons) by coupling two-particle and four-particle states, and to eight-particle states (four neutrons and four protons) by coupling two four-particle states. Overlap matrices and TDA equations are derived for each step, with transformation rules between different coupling schemes.

## Key numbers

* The formalism is designed for N=Z systems with 4, 6, and 8 particles (i.e., 2, 3, and 4 np pairs) outside a closed shell.
* The two-body TDA equations include energy denominators of the form (W - epsilon_p1 - epsilon_p2) for like-particle pairs and (W - epsilon_p1 - epsilon_n2) for np pairs.
* The four-particle wave function expansion includes terms summed over alpha_2 beta_2 and gamma_2 <= gamma_2' (Equation for P^dag(gamma_4)).
* The six-particle wave function uses the partition of two- times four-particles: P^dag(gamma_6) = sum_{gamma_2 gamma_4} X(gamma_2 gamma_4; gamma_6) P^dag(gamma_2) P^dag(gamma_4).
* The eight-particle wave function uses the partition of four- times four-particles: P^dag(gamma_8) = sum_{alpha_4 <= beta_4} X(alpha_4 beta_4; gamma_8) P^dag(alpha_4) P^dag(beta_4).

## Context

This work extends the multistep shell model method originally developed by Liotta (1981) to include both neutron and proton degrees of freedom, enabling the study of spin-aligned np pair coupling in N=Z nuclei. The formalism is applied in a companion paper (Xu, Qi, Liotta, and Wyss, 2011, arXiv:1108.0269) to study the structure of N=Z systems with four, six, and eight particles. This approach enables systematic calculations of heavier N=Z nuclei beyond the reach of conventional shell-model diagonalization, and can be extended to systems with more pairs in successive steps.
