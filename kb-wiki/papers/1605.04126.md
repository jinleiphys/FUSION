# Thermalization and hydrodynamization in the color-flux-tube model

> The study of transverse-momentum spectra of quarks and gluons produced by the color electric flux tube decaying through the Schwinger tunneling mechanism is reviewed. The hints for a fast hydrodynamization in the ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are found.

# Thermalization and hydrodynamization in the color-flux-tube model

## Key claim

Fast thermalization of the quark-gluon plasma produced from color electric flux tubes is achieved only when the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio equals the AdS/CFT lower bound of 1/(4π); however, for ratios up to three times that bound, the system still undergoes fast hydrodynamization, meaning dissipative viscous hydrodynamics describes the deviations from equilibrium well even if full thermal equilibrium is not reached within 1 fm.

## Method

The authors model the early stage of ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions using a color electric flux tube that decays into quarks, antiquarks, and gluons via the Schwinger tunneling mechanism, with the Abelian dominance approximation. The phase-space distributions of the produced partons are governed by kinetic Boltzmann-Vlasov-type equations with collisional kernels treated in the relaxation time approximation (RTA), where the relaxation time is given by the Anderson-Witting formula τ_eq = 5 η̄ / T. Under the assumption of boost-invariant and transversely homogeneous (Bjorken) symmetry, the solutions are obtained analytically, and the transverse-momentum spectra are computed using the Cooper-Frye formula.

## Key numbers

- For shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η̄ = η̄_AdS = 1/(4π), the inverse logarithmic slopes λ of the parton transverse-momentum spectra agree with the equilibrium slopes λ_eq within a short proper time τ ≲ 1 fm, indicating fast thermalization.
- For η̄ = 3 η̄_AdS, the deviations of the slopes λ from λ_eq at medium and large transverse momentum are well described by the viscous fluid dynamics slopes λ_visc, indicating fast hydrodynamization for η̄ ≲ 3 η̄_AdS.
- The relaxation time is expressed as τ_eq = 5 η̄ / T, where η̄ is the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio.
- The number of flavors used in the calculation is N_f = 2.

## Context

This work extends the color-flux-tube model introduced by Ryblewski (2013) and the detailed analysis of transverse-momentum spectra by Ryblewski (2015). It confirms the fast hydrodynamization of the quark-gluon plasma first proposed by Heller (2011). The results show that while full thermalization requires the maximal coupling corresponding to the AdS/CFT lower bound of Kovtun, Son, and Starinets (2004), the system can still be described by viscous hydrodynamics for shear viscosities up to three times that bound, which enables the use of dissipative fluid dynamics for modeling the early stages of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and the LHC.
