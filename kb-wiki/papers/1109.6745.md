# Electromagnetic Excitation of Nucleon Resonances

> Recent progress on the extraction of electromagnetic properties of nucleon resonance excitation through pion photo- and electroproduction is reviewed. Cross section data measured at MAMI, ELSA, and CEBAF are analyzed and compared to the analysis of other groups. On this basis, we derive longitudinal and transverse transition form factors for most of the four-star nucleon resonances. Furthermore, we discuss how the transition form factors can be used to obtain empirical transverse charge densities. Contour plots of the thus derived densities are shown for the Delta, Roper, S11, and D13 nucleon resonances.

## Key claim
The paper reviews the extraction of electromagnetic properties of nucleon resonances from pion photo- and electroproduction data, but the authors emphasize that the resonance-background separation required to obtain pure resonance couplings is inherently model-dependent, and that the quoted fit errors from global analyses (such as MAID2007) reflect only statistical uncertainty, while model uncertainty can be larger by an order of magnitude.

## Method
The authors used the unitary isobar model MAID (Mainz unitary isobar model) to analyze cross section data from pion photo- and electroproduction experiments at MAMI, ELSA, and CEBAF. The model separates resonance and background contributions using a Breit-Wigner formalism with unitarized background amplitudes, and fits the resulting helicity amplitudes \(A_{1/2}\), \(A_{3/2}\), and \(S_{1/2}\) to the world database of approximately 20,000 data points. For resonances above the \(\Delta(1232)\), the \(Q^2\) dependence of the transition form factors was parameterized as \(\bar{\mathcal A}_{\alpha}(Q^2) = \bar{\mathcal A}_{\alpha}(0) (1 + a_1 Q^2 + a_2 Q^4 + a_3 Q^6 + a_4 Q^8) e^{-b_1 Q^2}\).

## Key numbers
- For the \(\Delta(1232)\) resonance at \(W_{\rm res}=1232\) MeV: magnetic transition moment \(\mu_{N\Delta} = (3.46 \pm 0.03)\, \mu_N\); electric quadrupole moment \(Q_{N\Delta} = -(0.0846 \pm 0.0033)\, \text{fm}^2\); \(R_{EM} = -(2.5 \pm 0.1_{\text{stat.}} \pm 0.2_{\text{syst.}})\,\%\).
- Pole position of the \(\Delta(1232)\): \(M_p = (1212 \pm 1)\,\text{MeV}\), \(\Gamma_p = (99 \pm 2)\,\text{MeV}\).
- Proton helicity amplitude \(A_{1/2}\) for \(P_{33}(1232)\) from MAID2007: \(-140 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); for \(D_{13}(1520)\): \(A_{1/2} = -27 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\), \(A_{3/2} = 161 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\).
- Proton helicity amplitude \(A_{1/2}\) for \(F_{15}(1680)\) from MAID2007: \(-25 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); \(A_{3/2} = 134 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\).
- Neutron helicity amplitude \(A_{1/2}\) for \(D_{13}(1520)\) from MAID2007: \(-77 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\); \(A_{3/2} = -154 \times 10^{-3}\,\text{GeV}^{-1/2}\).

## Context
This work extends the earlier MAID analyses (MAID1998, MAID2007) by incorporating new electroproduction data, particularly the 2008 \(\pi^+\) data of Park et al. (2008), leading to updated transition form factors for six proton resonances including \(P_{11}(1440)\), \(D_{13}(1520)\), and \(F_{15}(1680)\). The results are compared with analyses from the GWU/SAID group (Arndt et al., 2006), the Bonn-Gatchina group (Anisovich et al., 2009), and the Giessen group (Penner and Shklyar, 2002; Shklyar et al., 2006). The extracted form factors enable mapping of transverse charge densities for nucleon resonances (as shown for \(\Delta\), Roper, \(S_{11}\), and \(D_{13}\)) and provide input for lattice QCD calculations and dispersive sum rule evaluations.
