# Dynamical Electromagnetic fields and Dynamical Electromagnetic Anomaly in heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies

> Electromagnetic field produced in non-central heavy ion collisions play a crucial role in phenomena such as chiral anomalous effects, directed flow of mesons and splitting of spin polarization of $Λ/\barΛ$. A precise description of these fields is essential for quantitatively studying these effects. We investigate the space-time evolution of the electromagnetic fields by numerically solving Maxwell's equations using the results from the UrQMD model, rather than relying on an ansatz. We present the space-averaged dynamic electromagnetic fields, weighted by energy density, in the central region of heavy-ion collisions. These measurements can serve as a barometer for assessing the effects induced by magnetic fields. Comparing the fields at geometric center of the collisions, the space-averaged dynamical fields weighted by the energy density are smaller at the early stage but damp much slower at the later stage. We discuss the impact of these space averaged dynamical magnetic fields on the spin polarization and spin alignment in heavy ion collisions. Additionally, we explore the opportunity to study non-perturbative regime of Quantum Electrodynamics (QED) by presenting the simulation results for space averaged dynamical electric field at intermediate collision energies. Finally, the space-averaged dynamical electromagnetic anomaly $\boldsymbol{\textbf{E}}$$\cdot\boldsymbol{\textbf{B}}$ weighted by energy density is also calculated and compared with experimentally measured slope parameter $r$.

## Key claim

The space-averaged dynamical electromagnetic fields weighted by energy density are smaller at the early stage but damp much slower at the later stage compared to fields evaluated at the geometric center of the collision, and this approach can serve as a barometer for assessing magnetic-field-induced effects in heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (Ultra Relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model to simulate the full collision process and calculate the four-current density from charged hadrons. They then numerically solved Maxwell's equations using Yee's algorithm (Finite-Difference Time Domain method) with finite electric conductivity σ = 5.8 MeV and chiral magnetic conductivity σχ = 1.5 MeV, decomposing fields into external and internal components. The space-averaged dynamical fields were obtained by weighting the field values in each grid cell by the local energy density, considering only particles within momentum rapidity range -0.5 ≤ Y ≤ 0.5.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy range studied: 3.5 GeV ≤ √sNN ≤ 27 GeV for Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b = 9 fm
- Peak space-averaged dynamical electric field strength ranges from 20 MeV to 45 MeV for collision energies between 3.5 GeV and 11.5 GeV at b = 0 fm
- The peak electric field strength follows the fit: max⟨Edyn⟩E = 12.997 × (√sNN/1 GeV)^(1/2)
- The effective lifetime T of the electric field follows the fit: T = 20.7671 × (√sNN/1 GeV)^(-1) + 65.8689 × (√sNN/1 GeV)^(-2)
- Space-averaged dynamical magnetic field at initial and intermediate times has magnitude of order 10^(-2) to 10^(-1) mπ^2, and at late times (t > 7 fm/c) of order 10^(-3) to 10^(-2) mπ^2
- Grid parameters: spatial volume -15 fm ≤ x,y ≤ 15 fm with dx = dy = 0.5 fm, -15 fm ≤ z ≤ 15 fm with dz = 0.1 fm, time step dt = 0.05 fm/c

## Context

This work extends previous studies of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions that used simpler approaches such as the vacuum scenario or Monte Carlo Glauber models (Bzdak 2011, Bloczynski 2012, Siddique 2022). By using the UrQMD transport model instead of an ansatz, the authors provide a more realistic description of the space-time evolution of electromagnetic fields. The results enable quantitative studies of chiral anomalous effects, spin polarization splitting of Λ/Λ̄, and exploration of the non-perturbative regime of Quantum Electrodynamics at intermediate collision energies, consistent with recent STAR data (STAR 2023) and prior work by Li (2023) and Peng (2023).
