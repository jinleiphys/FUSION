# Erratum: Bremsstrahlung from an Equilibrating Quark-Gluon Plasma [Phys. Rev. C62, 014902 (2000)]

> The implications of a numerical error in the photon production rate on the direct photon spectrum of a chemically non-equilibrated quark-gluon plasma are discussed.

# Erratum: Bremsstrahlung from an Equilibrating Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The corrected bremsstrahlung and annihilation-with-scattering photon production rates, previously overestimated by a factor of 4, reduce the total direct photon yield from a quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC by a factor of 2-3, but the qualitative conclusion that the equilibrium and chemically non-equilibrating spectra are similar remains unchanged.

## Method

The authors corrected a numerical error in the bremsstrahlung and annihilation-with-scattering photon production rates originally taken from Aurenche (1998), which had been overestimated by a factor of 4. They recomputed the direct photon spectra for both fully equilibrated and chemically non-equilibrated quark-gluon plasma scenarios at RHIC and LHC energies using the corrected values of the functions J_T and J_L, and compared the contributions from one-loop processes (Compton scattering and annihilation) versus two-loop processes (bremsstrahlung and annihilation with scattering).

## Key numbers

- The bremsstrahlung and annihilation-with-scattering rates were overestimated by a factor of 4 in the original paper by Mustafa and Thoma (2000).
- Corrected values: J_T = 1.11, J_L = -1.07 for two quark flavors (N_f = 2); J_T = 1.20, J_L = -1.13 for three quark flavors (N_f = 3).
- Total photon yield from the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC is reduced by a factor of 2-3 compared to the original paper.
- At RHIC, the non-equilibrium spectrum is smaller by a factor of 5 at p_T = 1 GeV, but larger by a factor of 2 at 5 GeV compared to the equilibrated case.
- At LHC, the non-equilibrium spectrum is suppressed by a factor of about 3 at all momenta compared to the equilibrated case.
- Thermal emission in chemical non-equilibrium exceeds the prompt photon yield for p_T < 3.5 GeV at both RHIC and LHC.

## Context

This erratum corrects a numerical error in the original work by Mustafa and Thoma (2000), which had used overestimated bremsstrahlung and annihilation-with-scattering rates from Aurenche (1998). The correction was identified by Steffen and Thoma (2001). The new results reverse the earlier finding about which process dominates in the equilibrating case: the one-loop contribution (Compton scattering and annihilation) is now the largest for all momenta and initial conditions (SSPC, HIJING-I, HIJING-II) in the chemically non-equilibrated scenario, whereas in equilibrium the annihilation-with-scattering contribution dominates. The corrected spectra enable more reliable comparisons between thermal photon yields and prompt photon backgrounds at RHIC and LHC energies.
