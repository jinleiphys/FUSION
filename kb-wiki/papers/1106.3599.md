# P_11 Resonance Extracted from pi-N Data and Its Stability

> We study the stability of resonance poles in pi-N P_11 partial wave, particularly the Roper resonance, by varying parameters significantly within the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels model, keeping a good fit to the empirical amplitude. We find that two Roper poles are stable against the variation. However, for higher energies, the number of poles can change depending on how the parameters are fitted within error bars. We also developed a model with a bare nucleon which forms the physical nucleon by being dressed by the meson-cloud. We still find a good stability of the Roper poles.

## Key claim

Within the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels model, two Roper resonance poles near the pi-Delta threshold are stable under large parameter variations that still fit the empirical amplitude, but at higher energies the number of poles can change depending on how parameters are fitted within error bars.

## Method

The author varied parameters of the meson-baryon interactions and bare N* states within the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels (EBAC-DCC) model, which includes pi N, eta N, and pi pi N channels (the latter with pi Delta, rho N, and sigma N components), while maintaining a good fit to the SAID single-energy solution (SAID-SES). A separate model with a bare nucleon state was also developed, where the physical nucleon is formed by dressing with the meson cloud, and the nucleon pole condition was enforced. Resonance pole positions were extracted using analytic continuation methods.

## Key numbers

* Two Roper poles near the pi-Delta threshold from the 2N*-3p fit: (1368, 82) MeV and (1375, 110) MeV, with chi-squared per data point of 3.28.
* Two Roper poles near the pi-Delta threshold from the 2N*-4p fit: (1372, 80) MeV and (1385, 114) MeV, with chi-squared per data point of 3.36.
* An additional pole at (1636, 67) MeV appeared in the 2N*-4p fit but not in the 2N*-3p fit.
* Two Roper poles from the bare nucleon model (1N0 1N*-3p): (1363, 81) MeV and (1377, 128) MeV, with chi-squared per data point of 2.51.
* For comparison, the JLMS model gave Roper poles at (1357, 76) MeV and (1364, 105) MeV, with chi-squared per data point of 3.55.

## Context

This work extends the EBAC dynamical coupled-channels analysis of the JLMS model (JLMS, 2007) and the earlier pole extraction (Suzuki et al., 2010) by testing the stability of the two-pole structure of the Roper resonance under significant parameter variation. The finding that two Roper poles are stable but higher-energy poles are sensitive to fitting choices indicates that more accurate data above W ~ 1.6 GeV are needed for reliable extraction of higher resonances. The bare nucleon model follows the approach of Afnan and Pearce (Afnan, 1999) and shows that the two Roper poles are insensitive to the analytic structure below the pi N threshold.
