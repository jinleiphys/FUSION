# Why the high lying glueball does not mix with the neighbouring $f_0$

> Chiral symmetry restoration in high-lying hadron spectra implies that hadrons which belong to different irreducible representations of the parity-chiral group cannot mix. This explains why the $f_0(2102 \pm 13)$, which was suggested to be a glueball, and hence must belong to the scalar (0,0) representation of the chiral group, cannot mix with the neighbouring $f_0(2040 \pm 38)$, which was interpreted as a $ n\bar n$ state, and that belongs to the $(1/2,1/2)$ representation of the chiral group. If confirmed, then we have an access to a "true" glueball of QCD.

# Why the high lying glueball does not mix with the neighbouring $f_0$

## Key claim

If chiral symmetry is approximately restored in high-lying hadron spectra, then the index of the irreducible representation of the parity-chiral group becomes a good quantum number, which forbids mixing between states belonging to different representations even when they are close in energy and share all other quantum numbers. This explains why the candidate glueball $f_0(2102 \pm 13)$, belonging to the scalar (0,0) representation, does not mix strongly with the neighbouring $f_0(2040 \pm 38)$, a $n\bar n$ state in the (1/2,1/2) representation, though the author notes that chiral restoration is not exact at masses around 2 GeV so a small amount of mixing is still possible.

## Method

The author applies the theoretical framework of chiral symmetry restoration of the second kind to the scalar meson spectrum, using the argument that at large space-like momenta the two-point correlation functions of chirally-connected currents become equal, implying that the spectral densities at large $s$ must also become equal. The known masses of high-lying $f_0$ and $\pi$ mesons from partial wave analyses of $p\bar p$ annihilation at LEAR are compared to identify chiral partner pairs, and the representation assignments of the glueball (0,0) versus $n\bar n$ states (1/2,1/2) are used to predict the absence of mixing.

## Key numbers

- $f_0(2102 \pm 13)$ MeV, width $211 \pm 29$ MeV: candidate glueball state observed in $p\bar p$ annihilation at LEAR, decays more strongly to $\eta\eta$ than to $\pi\pi$
- $f_0(2040 \pm 38)$ MeV, width $405 \pm 40$ MeV: neighbouring $n\bar n$ state interpreted as chiral partner of $\pi(2070 \pm 35)$ MeV
- $f_0(1770 \pm 12)$ MeV and $\pi(1801 \pm 13)$ MeV form a chiral partner pair
- $f_0(2337 \pm 14)$ MeV and $\pi(2360 \pm 25)$ MeV form a chiral partner pair
- $f_0(1370^{+130}_{-170})$ and $\pi(1300 \pm 100)$ MeV form the lowest chiral partner pair

## Context

This work extends the chiral symmetry restoration framework previously developed by Glozman (2000) and Cohen and Glozman (2002) for baryons and mesons, applying it specifically to the long-standing puzzle of the $0^{++}$ $f_0$ resonances where more states exist than the quark model can accommodate. The paper directly addresses the objection by Wilczek (2002) that glueball and quark-antiquark states cannot be distinguished and must inevitably mix. If confirmed by detailed decay mode studies, this would provide access to a "true" glueball of QCD and simultaneously serve as independent confirmation of chiral symmetry restoration in the hadron spectrum.
