# Impact of two-body currents on magnetic dipole moments of nuclei

> We investigate the effects of two-body currents on magnetic dipole moments of medium-mass and heavy nuclei using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group with chiral effective field theory interactions and currents. Focusing on near doubly magic nuclei from oxygen to bismuth, we have found that the leading two-body currents globally improve the agreement with experimental magnetic moments. Moreover, our results show the importance of multi-shell effects for $^{41}$Ca, which suggest that the $Z=N=20$ gap in $^{40}$Ca is not as robust as in $^{48}$Ca. The increasing contribution of two-body currents in heavier systems is explained by the operator structure of the center-of-mass dependent Sachs term.

## Key claim

The leading two-body currents from chiral effective field theory globally improve the agreement between ab initio valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group calculations and experimental magnetic dipole moments for near doubly magic nuclei from oxygen to bismuth, but the improvement is not universal: a small deterioration occurs in 41Ca due to multi-shell effects from the weaker closed-shell nature of the 40Ca core, and the 2BC contribution is small in 207Tl.

## Method

The authors employed the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) at the normal-ordered two-body level [VS-IMSRG(2)] to compute magnetic dipole moments, starting from chiral effective field theory nucleon-nucleon plus three-nucleon interactions (the 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction). The one-body and leading two-body magnetic dipole operators (the intrinsic and Sachs terms from pion-exchange currents) were evolved consistently with the Hamiltonian using the same unitary transformation. Calculations were performed for near doubly magic nuclei from A=17 to A=209, with additional multi-shell valence-space calculations for calcium isotopes and isotopic chains for indium.

## Key numbers

- For 207Tl and 209Bi, ground-state energies are -1660 ± 19 MeV and -1671 ± 19 MeV, respectively, after model-space extrapolation with a 3% error from the VS-IMSRG(2) approximation.
- The ratio |⟨μ_Sachs⟩ / ⟨μ_intr⟩| for the ground state is about 0.1 for 3H, while it is 10 for 131In, showing the growing dominance of the Sachs term in heavier systems.
- For 41Sc, the multi-shell calculation changes the magnetic moment only by 6% compared to the single-shell result.
- The 2BC contribution is decreased by less than 10% in 37Ca through momentum-space regulators.
- For indium isotopes, the interaction dependence between the 1.8/2.0 (EM) and ΔN2LO_GO(394) interactions is about a few percent, significantly smaller than the 2BC contribution.

## Context

This work extends ab initio calculations of electromagnetic observables with two-body currents from light nuclei (where quantum Monte Carlo and no-core shell model calculations have shown their significance, e.g., Piarulli (2013), Pastore (2013), Bacca (2014)) to medium-mass and heavy nuclei up to bismuth. It directly addresses the inability of previous VS-IMSRG calculations using only one-body electromagnetic operators to reproduce the magnetic moments of indium isotopes measured by Vernon (2022), and provides a critical test of electroweak operators for applications to fundamental symmetry tests with rare decays as noted by Brodeur (2023). The results enable future exploration of other electromagnetic properties of nuclei with full uncertainty quantification and inclusion of higher-order two-body currents.
