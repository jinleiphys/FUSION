# Can the hyperfine mass splitting formula in heavy quarkonia be applied to the $B_c$ system?

> The mass relation ${M_{0^{+}}+3M_{1^{+\prime}}+5M_{2^{+}}= 9M_{1^{+}}}$ miraculously holds for the $P$-wave charmonium $(c\bar{c})$ and bottomonium $(b\bar{b})$ systems with soaring precision. The origin of such relation can be addressed from Quark Models, and have been confirmed experimentally in a limited number of cases. In this connection, we propose $M_{0^{+}}+5M_{2^{+}}=3(M_{1^{+\prime}}+M_{1^{+}})$ as an extension to the $P$-wave $B_{c}$ case. In order to test its applicability, we employ a variety of Quark Model predictions for the $B_c$ mass spectrum. Our numerical analysis confirms such formula is accurate up to very small deviations.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the hyperfine mass splitting formula for heavy quarkonia can be extended to the unequal-flavor \(B_c\) system as \(M_{0^{+}}+5M_{2^{+}}=3(M_{1^{+\prime}}+M_{1^{+}})\), and they claim this relation holds with deviations below \(0.2\%\) based on Quark Model predictions, though the derivation relies on approximations including the equal spacing rule and neglecting mixing effects.

## Method

The authors derived a mass relation for \(P\)-wave \(B_c\) mesons by adapting the known hyperfine splitting formula from charmonium and bottomonium systems, assuming the equal spacing rule \((M_{c\bar{c}} + M_{b\bar{b}})/2 = M_{c\bar{b}}\) and approximating the spin-singlet and spin-triplet \(P\)-wave masses as equal. They then tested the relation by collecting predicted masses for the \(0^{+}\), \(1^{+\prime}\), \(1^{+}\), and \(2^{+}\) states from seven different Quark Model calculations and computing the deviation ratio \(r = (M_{0^{+}} + 5M_{2^{+}})/(3(M_{1^{+\prime}} + M_{1^{+}}))\).

## Key numbers

* For all seven Quark Model calculations surveyed, the relative deviation \(|\delta_r|\) from the proposed mass relation is less than \(2 \times 10^{-3}\) (below \(0.2\%\)).
* Using masses from Li (2019), the ratio \(r \simeq 1.001\), corresponding to a discrepancy of order 50 MeV between the left-hand and right-hand sides of the relation.
* For the empirical charmonium system, the deviation is \(\delta_r = 4.12 \times 10^{-3}\); for bottomonium, \(\delta_r = 7.2 \times 10^{-4}\).
* The experimental ratio \(\langle M(^3P)\rangle/M(^1P) \approx 1.00002\) for \(1P\) charmonium, and approximately \(0.99994\) and \(0.99996\) for \(1P\) and \(2P\) bottomonium respectively.
* The ratio \(\frac{M_{\eta_{c}}+M_{\eta_{b}}}{2M_{0^{-}}^{c\bar{b}}} \simeq 0.99\) from experimental data supports the equal spacing rule approximation.

## Context

This work extends the known hyperfine mass splitting relation for charmonium and bottomonium systems, previously studied in Quark Models by Eichten (1978, 1979) and confirmed experimentally for a limited number of cases, to the \(B_c\) meson system which lies in an intermediate mass regime between the two. The proposed relation could guide future experimental searches for \(B_c\) excited states at CERN (CMS and LHCb collaborations) and provide a test for QCD-connected studies and Lattice QCD simulations, as well as potentially inform understanding of exotic \(B_c\)-like structures.
