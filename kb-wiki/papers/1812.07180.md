# Nucleon microscopy in proton-nucleus scattering via analysis of bremsstrahlung emission

> We investigate an idea, how to use analysis of the bremsstrahlung photons to study the internal structure of proton under nuclear reaction with nucleus. A new model is constructed to describe bremsstrahlung emission of photons which accompanies the scattering of protons off nuclei. Our bremsstrahlung formalism uses many-nucleon basis that allows to analyze coherent and incoherent bremsstrahlung emissions. As scattered proton can be under the influence of strong forces and produces the largest bremsstrahlung contribution to full spectrum, we focus on accurate determination of its quantum evolution concerning nucleus basing on quantum mechanics and scattering theory. For such a motivation, we at first time generalize Pauli equation with interacting potential describing evolution of fermion inside strong field, with including the electromagnetic form-factors of nucleon basing on DIS theory. Anomalous magnetic momenta of nucleons reinforce our motivation to develop such a formalism, starting from low energy. The full bremsstrahlung spectrum in our model (after renormalization) is dependent on form-factors of the scattered proton. For calculations, we choose the scattering of $p + ^{197}{\rm Au}$ at proton beam energy of 190~MeV, where experimental bremsstrahlung data were obtained with high accuracy. We show that the full bremsstrahlung spectrum is sensitive to the form-factors of the scattered proton. In the limit without such form-factors, we reconstruct our previous result (where internal structure of the scattered proton was not studied).

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the full bremsstrahlung spectrum in proton-nucleus scattering, after renormalization, is sensitive to the electromagnetic form-factors of the scattered proton, enabling a new method to probe the internal structure of the proton via nuclear reactions rather than traditional lepton-nucleon deep inelastic scattering.

## Method

The authors generalize the Pauli equation for a fermion in a strong field by incorporating the electromagnetic form-factors (Dirac form-factor F1 and Pauli form-factor F2) from deep inelastic scattering theory into the Dirac equation describing the scattered proton. They then apply this formalism to calculate the bremsstrahlung spectrum for p + 197Au scattering at a proton beam energy of 190 MeV, comparing the results with and without the inclusion of the nucleon form-factors.

## Key numbers

*   The proton beam energy used for the calculation is 190 MeV for scattering on 197Au.
*   The Dirac form-factor at zero momentum transfer is F1^p(0) = 1, and the Pauli form-factor at zero momentum transfer is F2^p(0) = 1.793 for the proton.
*   The Pauli form-factor at zero momentum transfer for the neutron is F2^n(0) = -1.913.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous bremsstrahlung formalism for proton-nucleus scattering (Maydanyuk 2012, Maydanyuk and Zhang 2015), which did not include the internal structure of the scattered proton. It also connects with the many-nucleon bremsstrahlung developments of other researchers (Liu 1981, Baye 1985, Dohet-Eraly 2011). The approach enables future experimental studies using bremsstrahlung photons to extract information about nucleon structure in nuclear reactions at intermediate energies.
