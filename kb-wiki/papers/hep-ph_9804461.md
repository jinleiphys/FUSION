# Baryon Magnetic Moments and Quark Orbital Motion in the Chiral Quark Model

> Using the unified scheme for describing both quark spin and orbital angular momenta in the chiral quark model developed in the previous work, the magnetic moments of octet and decuplet baryons are calculated. The numerical result shows that the overall agreement with data is improved by including the orbital contributions.

## Key claim

Including quark orbital angular momentum contributions in the chiral quark model improves the overall agreement between calculated and measured baryon magnetic moments, but the improvement is modest and the orbital contribution is only one of several effects (such as gluon mixing) that can modify the predictions.

## Method

The author calculates baryon magnetic moments using a unified scheme that treats both quark spin and orbital angular momenta in the symmetry-breaking chiral quark model, where chiral fluctuations (q → q' + Goldstone boson) induce quark spin flips that generate orbital angular momentum. The magnetic moment is expressed as the sum over quark flavors of the quark magnetic moment multiplied by the sum of the quark spin content and the difference between quark and antiquark orbital angular momenta. Two models are considered: Model I with a static SU(3)_f ⊗ SU(2)_s valence quark structure, and Model II with a hybrid quark-gluon mixing configuration where the proton wave function includes a (3q+G) component.

## Context

This work extends the chiral quark model approach previously developed by the author (Song, 1998) to include orbital angular momentum contributions to baryon magnetic moments, building on earlier work by Sehgal (1974), Karl (1992), and Bartelski and Rodenberg (1990) who related quark spin fractions to baryon magnetic moments. The results improve upon the naive SU(6) constituent quark model (NQM) predictions and are comparable to calculations by Weber and Bodoor (1997) and Linde, Ohlsson, and Snellman (1998) that did not include orbital contributions. The work enables further tests of orbital angular momentum effects in baryon structure, particularly through more precise measurements of decuplet baryon magnetic moments where the predictions with and without orbital contributions differ only slightly.
