# Quark-meson diquark model and color superconductivity in dense quark matter

> We consider the two- and three-flavor QMD models as renormalizable low-energy models for QCD at finite quark chemical potentials with quarks, mesons, and diquarks as effective degrees of freedom. Using the on-shell scheme the parameters in the scalar sector can be fixed and expressed in terms of observed meson masses and decay constants. The remaining parameters can be varied. In the QMD models, all the symmetries are global, including the $SU(N_c)$ symmetry. The breaking of the global symmetries gives rise to a number of Goldstone bosons depending on the symmetry-breaking pattern, i.e. whether the system is in the 2SC phase or the color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase. This is in contrast to perturbative QCD, where some of the gauge bosons become massive via the Higgs mechanism. We classify the Goldstone bosons and show that their type and number are in accordance with general counting rules. The thermodynamic potential $Ω$ is calculated in the mean-field approximation, where we include quark loops, while mesons and diquarks are treated at tree level. As important applications, we study the properties of the pion-condensed phase at finite isospin chemical potential, and the 2SC and CFL phases at finite baryon chemical potential. We present a few numerical results focusing on the speed of sound, gaps, and condensates. It is shown that the BCS gaps approaches a constant for large isospin and baryon chemical potentials and that the speed of sound approaches the conformal value from above in the same limit.

## Key claim

The paper presents the quark-meson diquark (QMD) model as a renormalizable low-energy effective theory for QCD at finite chemical potential, but with the crucial caveat that all symmetries, including SU(N_c) color, are treated as global rather than gauged, meaning the Goldstone bosons from symmetry breaking appear instead of massive gauge bosons as in perturbative QCD.

## Method

The authors construct two- and three-flavor QMD models with quarks, mesons, and diquarks as effective degrees of freedom, fixing parameters in the scalar sector via the on-shell renormalization scheme using observed meson masses and decay constants. The thermodynamic potential Omega is computed in the mean-field approximation, including quark loops while treating mesons and diquarks at tree level. The Goldstone boson spectrum is classified for both the two-flavor color-superconducting (2SC) phase and the three-flavor color-flavor-locked (CFL) phase, and the model is applied to study pion-condensed matter at finite isospin chemical potential as well as 2SC and CFL phases at finite baryon chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson model framework to include diquark degrees of freedom, building on earlier effective model approaches to color superconductivity. The classification of Goldstone bosons in the 2SC and CFL phases with global symmetries contrasts with the Higgs mechanism in perturbative QCD treatments. The results enable future quantitative comparisons between the QMD model predictions and lattice QCD results for dense matter, as well as providing a renormalizable framework for studying the equation of state of neutron star matter.
