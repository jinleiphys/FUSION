# Production of complex particles in low energy spallation and in fragmentation reactions by in-medium random clusterization

> Rules for in-medium complex particle production in nuclear reactions are proposed. These rules have been implemented in two models to simulate nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions around the Fermi energy. Our work emphasizes the effect of randomness in cluster formation, the importance of the nucleonic Fermi motion as well as the role of conservation laws. The concepts of total available phase-space and explored phase-space under constraint imposed by the reaction are clarified. The compatibility of experimental observations with a random clusterization is illustrated in a schematic scenario of a proton-nucleus collision. The role of randomness under constraint is also illustrated in the nucleus-nucleus case.

# Production of complex particles in low energy spallation and in fragmentation reactions by in-medium random clusterization

## Key claim

The authors propose that complex particle production in nucleon-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus reactions around the Fermi energy can be described by a random sampling of nucleons from the target, subject only to energy conservation and the emission barrier, and that this "complete randomness hypothesis" is compatible with experimental data even though the physical origin of the randomness (phase transition, turbulence, quantum decoherence, etc.) remains unspecified.

## Method

The authors define two rules: (1) cluster formation by random selection of nucleons from a Thomas-Fermi ground-state distribution of the target, defining the "total accessible phase-space"; (2) cluster emission subject to two constraints, a lower kinetic energy limit from the Coulomb-plus-nuclear barrier and an upper limit from the total energy available in the reaction, defining the "explored phase-space." These rules are implemented in two Monte-Carlo event generators: n-IPSE for nucleon-induced reactions and HIPSE for heavy-ion reactions, which add in-medium nucleon-nucleon collisions, impact-parameter geometry, one-body dissipation, a coalescence algorithm for multi-cluster formation, final-state interaction including possible re-fusion, and an after-burner stage for de-excitation of excited fragments.

## Key numbers

- For a proton-induced reaction on a heavy target at beam energy E_B = 39 MeV, the calculated alpha-particle kinetic energy spectrum (open squares) is compared successfully with experimental data (filled circles) from Ber73.
- The n-IPSE model reproduces kinetic energy differential cross-sections for proton, deuteron, triton, and alpha particles for beam energies from 37 MeV to 135 MeV in both proton and neutron induced reactions on medium and heavy nuclei.
- For the Xe+Sn system, HIPSE calculations are compared with INDRA data at three beam energies: E_B = 25, 50, and 80 MeV/A, showing agreement for distributions of relative velocity and relative angle between the three largest fragments taken two-by-two.
- A single set of parameters adjusted on Xe+Sn reactions was used for Ni+Ni and Au+Au cases, giving reasonable agreement with data.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering random-sampling approach of Goldhaber (1974) for cluster formation to include emission constraints and multi-cluster final states, and generalizes it from fragmentation to spallation reactions. The n-IPSE and HIPSE models are compared against the GNASH model (Young 1992) for nucleon-induced reactions and against INDRA data (Hudan 2003) for nucleus-nucleus reactions. The results enable event generators that reproduce not only average properties but also fluctuations and internal correlations in multifragmentation data, while leaving open the question of the physical mechanism underlying the observed randomness.
