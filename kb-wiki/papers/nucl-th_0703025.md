# Energy Thresholds of Stability of Three-Particle Systems

> We have studied the general properties of the energy thresholds of stability for a three-particle system with short-range interaction. A wide region of the interaction constants and various ratios of the masses of particles are considered. The specific effects characteristic of the near-threshold stationary energy levels of three particles are revealed. The asymptotic estimates are obtained for the thresholds at some limiting cases, and the high-precision variational calculations of the thresholds for various values of the interaction constants and the masses of particles are carried out.

# Energy Thresholds of Stability of Three-Particle Systems

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the stability thresholds for three-particle systems with short-range interactions exhibit a universal diagrammatic structure on the plane of interaction constants, but this structure is conditional on the specific form of the pairwise potentials and the mass ratios; the claimed universality holds for nonsingular short-range potentials of general form, yet the detailed asymptotic formulas and the existence of effects such as the "tube" and "wedge" depend on the particular potential shapes and mass values.

## Method

The authors study a system of three particles (two identical, one possibly different) with pairwise short-range interactions described by Gaussian and exponential potentials, using the Galerkin variational method with optimized Gaussian bases to perform high-precision calculations of energy thresholds. They analyze asymptotic limits analytically by expanding the Hamiltonian near equilibrium configurations in Jacobi coordinates, reducing the problem to effective oscillatory Hamiltonians for the strong-coupling regimes. The thresholds are mapped as curves on the plane of dimensionless interaction constants \(g\) (for the identical pair) and \(\lambda\) (for the two different pairs), separating regions where the \(n\)-th three-particle bound state exists below the lowest two-particle threshold from regions where it does not.

## Key numbers

- Critical constants for two-particle \(s\)-states with Gaussian potential (unit radius, unit masses): \(g_{s,\mathrm{cr}}^{(0)} = 2.684005\), \(g_{s,\mathrm{cr}}^{(1)} = 17.79570\), \(g_{s,\mathrm{cr}}^{(2)} = 45.57348\), \(g_{s,\mathrm{cr}}^{(3)} = 85.96340\)
- For the ground-state threshold asymptote in region I (Gaussian potentials, unit radii): \(g = (27/16)(|\lambda| + C_{0,0}\sqrt{2|\lambda|} + \ldots)\), where \(C_{0,0} \to 0.06/\sqrt{m}\) as \(m \to 0\), reaches minimum \(C_{0,0} \approx -1.39\) at \(m \approx 0.057\), and approaches \(C_{0,0} \approx -0.55\) as \(m \to \infty\)
- Equilibrium distance between identical particles in region I: \(r_{\min}^2 = 4\ln(3/2) \approx 1.62\) (for Gaussian potentials with unit radii)
- Ratio of adjacent Efimov level energies for three identical particles: \(\Lambda_0 = e^{2\pi/s_0} = 515.035\ldots\) with the Danilov-Minlos-Faddeev-Efimov constant \(s_0 = 1.00623\); the ratio of system sizes for adjacent Efimov levels is \(\sqrt{\Lambda_0} \approx 22.69\)
- For region II asymptote as \(g \to -\infty\): \(\lambda_{\mathrm{cr}}^{(n)} \approx -((m+2)/(8m)) g_{s,\mathrm{cr}}^{(n)} (1 + 1/(8\sqrt{|g|}))\)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of three-particle quantum systems with short-range interactions, building on the foundational Efimov effect (Efimov, 1970) and the Thomas theorem (Thomas, 1935). The paper provides a comprehensive diagrammatic classification of stability thresholds across eight distinct regions of the interaction-constant plane, unifying known phenomena such as the Efimov infinite level series, the Thomas effect for the ground state at \(g=0\), and the newly identified "tube" effect where bound states persist under arbitrarily strong repulsion between identical particles. The results enable systematic predictions for three-nucleon systems, molecular trimers, and hypothetical systems like trineutrons, and provide a framework for interpreting near-threshold energy levels in ultracold atomic gases where Feshbach resonances allow tuning of two-body interactions.
