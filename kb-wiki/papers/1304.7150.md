# Quark scattering off quarks and hadrons

> The in-medium elastic scattering $qq\to qq, q\bar{q}\to q\bar{q}$ and $\bar{q}\bar{q}\to \bar{q}\bar{q}$ is calculated within the two-flavor Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. The integral and differential quark-quark scattering, its energy and temperature dependence are considered and their flavor dependence is emphasized. The comparison with results of other approaches is presented. The consideration is implemented to the case of quark-pion scattering characterizing the interaction between quarks and hadrons in a kinetic multiphase treatment, and the first estimate of the quark-pion cross sections is given. A possible application of the obtained results to heavy ion collisions is shortly discussed.

## Key claim

The authors provide the first estimate of quark-pion elastic scattering cross sections within the two-flavor Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model, but note that the center-of-mass energy is restricted by the scale \(\sqrt{s} \approx 1.5\) GeV and the PNJL description is reliable only up to approximately \(2.5 T_c\) (about 0.525 GeV for their parameters).

## Method

The authors calculate in-medium elastic scattering cross sections for \(qq \to qq\), \(q\bar{q} \to q\bar{q}\), and \(\bar{q}\bar{q} \to \bar{q}\bar{q}\) using the two-flavor PNJL model with scalar and pseudoscalar interactions, where meson propagators are treated in the pole approximation within the random-phase approximation. They then extend the formalism to quark-pion scattering by constructing amplitudes from \(s\)-channel and \(u\)-channel quark exchange diagrams and a \(t\)-channel sigma-meson exchange diagram, incorporating color and flavor factors for each reaction channel. The cross sections include medium effects through modified Fermi functions for quarks and Bose-Einstein factors for pions.

## Key numbers

- The model parameters are: current quark mass \(m_0 = 5.5\) MeV, cutoff \(\Lambda = 0.639\) GeV, coupling constant \(G = 5.227\) GeV\(^{-2}\), and Polyakov-loop parameter \(T_0 = 0.19\) GeV.
- The chiral transition temperature at vanishing chemical potential is \(T_c = 0.210\) GeV.
- The Mott temperature, where pions dissociate into their constituents, is \(T_{\rm Mott} = 0.231\) GeV.
- The center-of-mass energy is restricted by \(\sqrt{s} \approx 2\sqrt{\Lambda^2 + m_q^2} \approx 1.5\) GeV.
- For quark-quark scattering, \(\sigma_{el}(uu \to uu) \approx 2.5\ \sigma_{el}(ud \to ud)\) at comparable energies and temperatures.
- The elastic cross section for \(uu \to uu\) shows a maximum near \(\sqrt{s} \sim 1\) GeV at temperature \(T \sim 0.1\) GeV.
- For \(u\bar{u} \to u\bar{u}\) scattering, the cross section exhibits a resonance-dominated maximum near the sigma-meson mass, with a threshold divergence at the Mott temperature.

## Context

This work extends previous NJL model calculations of quark scattering by Zhuang et al. (1995) and Rehberg et al. (1996) by incorporating the Polyakov loop to simulate confinement effects, and by providing the first estimate of quark-pion cross sections needed for kinetic multiphase treatments of heavy-ion collisions. The results enable the inclusion of in-medium quark-hadron interactions in transport models such as AMPT, PHSD, and BAMPS, which currently rely on perturbative QCD cross sections or simpler approximations. The comparison shows that the PNJL results differ significantly from pQCD predictions, particularly at forward angles where pQCD diverges for vanishing quark masses.
