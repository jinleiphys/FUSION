# A relativistic mean field study of multi-strange system

> We study the binding energies, radii, single-particle energies, spin-orbit potential and density profile for multi-strange hypernuclei in the range of light mass to superheavy region within the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory. The stability of multi-strange hypernuclei as a function of introduced hyperons ($Λ$ and $Σ$) is investigated. The neutron, lambda and sigma mean potentials are presented for light to superheavy hypernuclei. The inclusion of hyperons affects the nucleon, lambda and sigma spin-orbit potentials significantly. The bubble structure of nuclei and corresponding hypernuclei is studied. The nucleon and lambda halo structure are also investigated. A large class of bound multi-strange systems formed from the combination of nucleons and hyperons (n, p, $Λ$, $Σ^+$ and n, p, $Λ$, $Σ^-$) is suggested in the region of superheavy hypernuclei which might be stable against the strong decay. These multi-strange systems might be produced in heavy-ion reactions.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a large class of bound multi-strange systems composed of nucleons and hyperons (n, p, Λ, Σ⁺ and n, p, Λ, Σ⁻) exists in the superheavy region and might be stable against strong decay, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific choice of hyperon-meson coupling constants (adopted from Mares (1994) and Schaffner (1994)) and the use of the NL3* nucleon parameter set within the relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism including hyperon-hyperon (YY) interaction via σ* and φ mesons.

## Method
The authors performed relativistic mean field (RMF) calculations using the NL3* parameter set for nucleons, with hyperon-meson coupling constants taken from the naive quark model and from Mares (1994) and Schaffner (1994). They introduced σ* and φ meson fields to incorporate hyperon-hyperon (YY) interaction, and solved the Dirac equations for nucleons, Λ hyperons, and Σ hyperons self-consistently, using constant-gap BCS pairing and a center-of-mass correction of E_cm = -(3/4)41A^{-1/3}. Binding energies, radii, single-particle energies, spin-orbit potentials, and density profiles were computed for multi-strange systems from light to superheavy mass regions by progressively replacing neutrons with Λ, Σ⁺, or Σ⁻ hyperons.

## Key numbers
- For single-Λ hypernuclei, the s-state Λ binding energy B_Λ^s ranges from -14.052 MeV (¹⁶_ΛN) to -27.215 MeV (³⁰⁴_Λ120), with experimental comparisons given for several cases (e.g., ¹⁶_ΛO: calculated -14.055 MeV vs. experimental 12.5±0.35 MeV).
- For single-Σ⁺ hypernuclei, the s-state Σ⁺ binding energy B_Σ⁺^s ranges from -13.631 MeV (¹⁶_Σ⁺N) to -34.004 MeV (²⁹⁸_Σ⁺114).
- For single-Σ⁻ hypernuclei, the s-state Σ⁻ binding energy B_Σ⁻^s ranges from -14.551 MeV (¹⁶_Σ⁻N) to -49.699 MeV (²⁹²_Σ⁻120).
- The depletion fraction (D.F.) for ¹⁶O is 15.11%, which drops to 4.81% for ¹⁶_1ΛO and to 0.0% for ¹⁶_5ΛO, ¹⁶_6ΛO, and ¹⁶_7ΛO.
- The charge radius r_ch for ²⁰⁸_ΛPb is 5.490 fm, and for ²⁰⁸_Σ⁺Pb is 5.494 fm, while for ²⁰⁸_Σ⁻Pb it is 5.475 fm.

## Context
This work extends earlier RMF studies of multi-lambda hypernuclei by Rufa et al. (1990), which did not include YY interaction and were limited to medium-heavy spherical nuclei. It also builds on the investigations of Schaffner et al. (1993, 1994) who studied multi-strange systems with Λ, Ξ⁰, Ξ⁻ baryons. The present paper adds Σ⁺ and Σ⁻ hyperons alongside Λ, covers the full periodic table from light to superheavy nuclei, and examines bubble structure and halo formation. The results enable future experimental searches for bound multi-strange systems in heavy-ion reactions and provide input for modeling the equation of state of neutron star matter with strangeness.
