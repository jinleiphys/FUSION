# Fine-tuning of the $\bar{K}NN$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ quasi-bound state calculations

> Fine-tuning of the binding energies and widths of the quasi-bound states in three-body systems consisting of antikaon(s) and nucleon(s) was performed. Dynamically exact three-body Faddeev-type AGS equations with three coupled particle channels were solved for the description of the $\bar{K}NN$ and $\bar{K} \bar{K} N$ systems in different spin states. New models of the antikaon-nucleon and pion-nucleon interactions were constructed, and together with our best versions for the remaining potentials were used as input. The characteristics of the quasi-bound $K^- pp$ state calculated with our new one-pole $\bar{K}N - πΣ- πΛ$ potential reproduces the experimental data from the E15 J-PARC experiment.

## Key claim

The paper claims that by fine-tuning antikaon-nucleon, hyperon-nucleon, and pion-nucleon interaction models within a dynamically exact three-body Faddeev-type AGS framework with three coupled particle channels, the calculated binding energy of the $K^- pp$ quasi-bound state can be brought into agreement with the E15 J-PARC experimental data, but the calculated width remains much smaller than the experimental value.

## Method

The authors solved the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) form of the Faddeev equations in momentum representation for the $\bar{K}NN$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ three-body systems, using separable $s$-wave potentials for all two-body interactions. For the $\bar{K}NN$ system, the equations included five three-body channels: $\bar{K}NN$, $\pi\Sigma N$ (with two permutations), and $\pi\Lambda N$ (with two permutations); for the $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ system, the channels were $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$, $\bar{K}\pi\Sigma$ (two permutations), and $\bar{K}\pi\Lambda$ (two permutations). New one-pole and two-pole phenomenological antikaon-nucleon potentials with coupled $\bar{K}N$, $\pi\Sigma$, and $\pi\Lambda$ channels were constructed, along with a refitted chirally motivated potential, and combined with a spin-dependent hyperon-nucleon potential and a new pion-nucleon potential fitted to $s$-wave phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The measured binding energy of the $\bar{K}NN$ quasi-bound state from the E15 J-PARC experiment is $B_{\bar{K}NN} \approx 40$ MeV, while the experimental width is $\Gamma_{\bar{K}NN} \approx 100$ MeV.
- The $1s$ level shift of kaonic hydrogen given by the new one-pole A version potential is $\Delta E_{1s} = -314.1$ eV, compared to the experimental value $-283 \pm 36 \pm 6$ eV from the SIDDHARTA collaboration.
- The $1s$ level width of kaonic hydrogen given by the same potential is $\Gamma_{1s} = 626.9$ eV, compared to the experimental value $541 \pm 89 \pm 22$ eV.
- The threshold branching ratio $\gamma$ for $K^- p \to \pi^+ \Sigma^-$ relative to $K^- p \to \pi^- \Sigma^+$ is $2.36 \pm 0.04$ experimentally; all new potentials reproduce $\gamma = 2.36$.
- The $\Sigma N$ scattering length in the $I=1/2, S=0$ state from the spin-dependent hyperon-nucleon potential is $a = -1.40 + i\,0.00$ fm, compared to $-1.03 + i\,0.00$ fm from an "advanced" potential.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous studies of the $K^- pp$ system (Shevchenko, 2023) by directly including the $\pi\Lambda N$ channel in the three-body AGS equations, which was previously omitted. It also follows up on a recent study (Shevchenko, 2024) that examined the dependence of three-body results on hyperon-nucleon and pion-nucleon interactions. The new antikaon-nucleon potentials with three coupled channels are fitted to the same experimental data as earlier models (Shevchenko, 2012) but with additional constraints on the isospin-1 strength constants to test a hypothesis from the E15 collaboration about attractive $I=1$ interactions. The results enable future calculations of the $\bar{K}\bar{K}N$ system with improved input and provide a benchmark for comparing theoretical widths to the much larger experimental value from J-PARC.
