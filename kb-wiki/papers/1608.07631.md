# Geometric symmetries in light nuclei

> The algebraic cluster model is is applied to study cluster states in the nuclei 12C and 16O. The observed level sequences can be understood in terms of the underlying discrete symmetry that characterizes the geometrical configuration of the alpha-particles, i.e. an equilateral triangle for 12C, and a regular tetrahedron for 16O. The structure of rotational bands provides a fingerprint of the underlying geometrical configuration of alpha-particles.

# Geometric symmetries in light nuclei

## Key claim
The algebraic cluster model (ACM) can describe cluster states in 12C and 16O as rotations and vibrations of an equilateral triangle and a regular tetrahedron of alpha particles, respectively, but the Hoyle state (0+2) in 12C cannot be interpreted as a simple vibrational excitation of a rigid triangular configuration and instead corresponds to a more floppy configuration with large rotation-vibration couplings.

## Method
The ACM uses spectrum-generating algebras U(7) for three-body clusters (12C) and U(10) for four-body clusters (16O), with Hamiltonians invariant under the permutation group Sn for identical alpha particles. Approximate solutions for the rotation-vibration spectrum were obtained for equilibrium shapes corresponding to alpha particles at the vertices of an equilateral triangle (point group D3h) for 12C and a regular tetrahedron (point group Td) for 16O. Electromagnetic transition form factors were calculated from a product of a spherical Bessel function and an exponential factor from a Gaussian charge distribution, with parameters determined from the elastic form factor minimum and charge radius.

## Key numbers
- 12C: B(E2; 2+1 → 0+1) = 8.4 e2fm4 (theoretical) versus 7.6 ± 0.4 e2fm4 (experimental)
- 12C: B(E3; 3-1 → 0+1) = 44 e2fm6 (theoretical) versus 103 ± 17 e2fm6 (experimental)
- 12C: M(E0; 0+2 → 0+1) = 0.4 fm2 (theoretical) versus 5.5 ± 0.2 fm2 (experimental)
- 16O: B(E3; 3-1 → 0+1) = 215 e2fm6 (theoretical) versus 205 ± 10 e2fm6 (experimental)
- 16O: B(E4; 4+1 → 0+1) = 425 e2fm8 (theoretical) versus 378 ± 133 e2fm8 (experimental)
- 16O: M(E0; 0+2 → 0+1) = 0.54 fm2 (theoretical) versus 3.55 ± 0.21 fm2 (experimental)

## Context
This work extends the ACM framework previously applied to two-body clusters (U(4) vibron model) to three-body (U(7)) and four-body (U(10)) alpha-cluster nuclei, building on prior studies by Marin (2014) and others. The results contradict simple shell model predictions by showing large B(EL) values indicating collectivity, and the large E0 deviation for the Hoyle state suggests it is not a rigid triangular configuration, unlike the ground state band. The measurement of a possible 3- Hoyle state in 12C is identified as crucial to determine whether the Hoyle band geometry is linear, bent, or triangular, which would distinguish between competing interpretations from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD), fermionic molecular dynamics (FMD), and BEC-like cluster models.
