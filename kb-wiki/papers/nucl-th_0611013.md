# Four-nucleon scattering: a new numerical approach

> The AGS equations are solved for $n{}^3\mathrm{H}$ and $p{}^3\mathrm{He}$ scattering including the Coulomb interaction. Comparison with previous work confirms the accuracy of the calculation and helps clarify a number of issues related to the $n{}^3\mathrm{H}$ total cross section at the peak of the resonance region, as well as an $A_y$ deficiency in $p{}^3\mathrm{He}$. Calculations are fully converged in terms of $NN$ partial waves and involve no uncontrolled approximations.

## Key claim

The authors solve the symmetrized Alt, Grassberger, and Sandhas (AGS) equations for four-nucleon scattering (n-³H and p-³He) including the Coulomb interaction, achieving fully converged calculations in terms of NN partial waves with no uncontrolled approximations; however, the resulting total cross sections for n-³H in the resonance region still fail to reproduce experimental data, and the calculated analyzing power A_y for p-³He shows a large discrepancy with data.

## Method

The symmetrized AGS equations for four identical nucleons are solved after partial-wave decomposition, yielding a three-variable integral equation that is discretized on a finite mesh with no further approximations. For p-³He scattering, the Coulomb interaction between protons is included via a screening and renormalization procedure using the two-potential formula, with convergence verified as a function of the screening radius R. Calculations are performed for several realistic NN potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, AV8', INOY04) and compared with previous benchmark results.

## Key numbers

- ⁴He binding energy with AV8' potential (no Coulomb): 25.91 MeV for I ≤ 6, consistent with literature range 25.90–25.94 MeV
- ⁴He binding energy with AV18 potential: 24.24 MeV for I ≤ 6, consistent with literature range 24.22–24.25 MeV
- ⁴He binding energy with CD Bonn potential: 26.11 MeV for I ≤ 6, consistent with literature range 26.13–26.16 MeV
- ⁴He binding energy with INOY04 potential: 29.11 MeV for I ≤ 6, matching literature value 29.11 MeV
- n-³H total cross section at E_n = 3.5 MeV with AV18: 2.329 b (this work) versus 2.24 b (ref. [6]) and 2.51 b (ref. [6])
- n-³H total cross section at E_n = 3.5 MeV with CD Bonn: 2.282 b (this work)
- p-³He observables at E_p = 4 MeV converge for screening radius R = 12 fm
- p-³He differential cross section and spin observables calculated at E_p = 5.54 MeV

## Context

This work extends the method previously developed for pd elastic scattering and breakup (Deltuva, 2005) to the four-nucleon system, enabling the first numerically converged solution of the AGS equations for n-³H and p-³He scattering with realistic NN forces and Coulomb. The results confirm earlier calculations by the Grenoble and Pisa groups (compiled in Lazauskas, 2005) while clarifying that the n-³H total cross section at the resonance peak remains underpredicted by both AV18 and CD Bonn potentials. The large discrepancy in p-³He analyzing power A_y points to a four-nucleon analog of the A_y puzzle in Nd scattering, while the reasonable agreement for spin correlation coefficients C_xx and C_yy suggests the deficiency is specific to certain observables.
