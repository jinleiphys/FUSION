# Regge phenomenology in $π^0$ and $η$ photoproduction

> The $γN \to π^0 N$ and $γN \to ηN$ reactions at photon beam energies above 4 GeV are investigated within Regge models. The models include $t$-channel exchanges of vector ($ρ$ and $ω$) and axial vector ($b_1$ and $h_1$) mesons. Moreover, Regge cuts of $ρ{\mathbb P}$, $ρf_2$, $ω{\mathbb P}$, and $ωf_2$ are investigated. A good description of differential cross sections and polarization observables at photon beam energies from 4 to 15 GeV can be achieved.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that Regge models incorporating both vector (ρ, ω) and axial vector (b₁, h₁) meson exchanges, together with Regge cuts from ρℙ, ρf₂, ωℙ, and ωf₂ rescattering, can achieve a good description of differential cross sections and polarization observables for π⁰ and η photoproduction at photon beam energies from 4 to 15 GeV, though the quality of description depends on which polarization data sets are included in the fit.

## Method
The authors constructed Regge models for γN → π⁰N and γN → ηN reactions by replacing single meson propagators with Regge trajectory propagators for vector mesons (ρ, ω) and axial vector mesons (b₁, h₁), and adding Regge cut contributions from two-Reggeon exchanges (ρℙ, ρf₂, ωℙ, ωf₂). They performed fits to available experimental data for differential cross sections and polarization observables (Σ, T, P, Rₙₚ) at photon beam energies from 4 to 15 GeV, comparing the Regge cut approach with the JPAC model that uses fixed-t dispersion relations.

## Key numbers
- Photon beam energy range: 4 to 15 GeV for the data included in the fits
- For π⁰ photoproduction, the best Regge cut fit (Solution I) achieved reduced χ² = 1.46 using all available data
- For η photoproduction, the best Regge cut fit (Solution I) achieved reduced χ² = 1.25
- The Regge trajectory parameters used: ρ trajectory α(t) = 0.477 + 0.885t, ω trajectory α(t) = 0.434 + 0.923t, b₁/h₁ trajectory α(t) = -0.013 + 0.664t
- The radiative widths used for electromagnetic couplings: Γ(ρ → π⁰γ) = 91.0 keV, Γ(ω → π⁰γ) = 703.0 keV, Γ(ρ → ηγ) = 50.6 keV, Γ(ω → ηγ) = 3.9 keV
- For π⁰ photoproduction, the fit excluding old polarization data (Solution III) achieved reduced χ² = 0.92

## Context
This work extends the Regge phenomenology of meson photoproduction above the resonance region (W ≈ 3 GeV), building on earlier Regge cut approaches by Landshoff (1972), Goldstein (1973), and Barker (1978), as well as the more recent JPAC model by Nys (2017). The study is motivated by new data from the GlueX experiment at JLab, which provides photon beam asymmetry measurements at Eγ = 8.7 GeV, and aims to establish a reliable description of high-energy non-resonant background amplitudes that is essential for baryon resonance analyses at lower energies, particularly for η, η', and K photoproduction.
