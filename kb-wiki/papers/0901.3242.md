# The possibility of quasi-bound state formation of $η$-meson with helium isotopes

> The necessary conditions of quasi-bound state formation of $η$-meson with isotopes $^{3}$He, $^{4}$He have been found within the framework of optical potential model. These conditions have been compared with the findings about helium nucleus densities and with the available information about $η$N-scattering length. Thus, we have conclude that within the framework of discussed model $η-^{3}$He quasi-bound state formation is not possible, but $η-^{4}$He quasi-bound state formation is possible with the great probability.

## Key claim

Within the optical potential model, quasi-bound state formation of an eta-meson with helium-4 is possible with high probability, but formation with helium-3 is impossible for any diffuseness of the nuclear density distribution, given the known eta-nucleon scattering lengths and root-mean-square radii.

## Method

The authors constructed an optical potential for eta-nucleus interaction using a Woods-Saxon (Fermi) density distribution for the helium isotopes, with the root-mean-square radii fixed to known values and the diffuseness parameter left free. They solved the Schrodinger equation with this complex potential to find the boundaries in the complex plane of the eta-nucleon scattering length where a quasi-bound state (with binding energy approximately zero) can exist, and compared these boundaries with published values of the eta-nucleon scattering length.

## Key numbers

- For eta-4He quasi-bound state formation, the condition requires |Re(a0)| >= 0.60 fm, where a0 is the eta-nucleon scattering length.
- The root-mean-square radius used for 3He is 1.9 fm; for 4He it is 1.6 fm.
- For 3He, the half-density radius Rc ranges from 1.571 fm (a/Rc = 0.25) to 2.210 fm (a/Rc = 0.10), with central nucleon density rho0 from 0.114 to 0.060 nucleon/fm^3.
- For 4He, the half-density radius Rc ranges from 1.331 fm (a/Rc = 0.25) to 2.021 fm (a/Rc = 0.05), with central nucleon density rho0 from 0.250 to 0.113 nucleon/fm^3.

## Context

This work extends prior optical model studies of eta-nucleus quasi-bound states by Haider and collaborators, and by other groups, specifically addressing the helium isotopes. The conclusion that eta-3He does not form a bound state agrees with the many-particle calculation of Fix and collaborators but contradicts the findings of Haider and of Kettle, who used different approximations. The result that eta-4He can form a bound state agrees with Haider, though the present work finds it requires larger real parts of the eta-nucleon scattering length. The paper enables future experimental searches for eta-4He bound states near threshold, noting that features seen in d+d -> 4He+eta and d+p -> 3He+eta reactions may indicate such states.
