# Nucleon-nucleon correlations and the single-particle strength in atomic nuclei

> We propose a phenomenological approach to examine the role of short- and long-range nucleon-nucleon correlations in the quenching of single-particle strength in atomic nuclei and their evolution in asymmetric nuclei and neutron matter. These correlations are thought to be the reason for the quenching of spectroscopic factors observed in $\rm (e,e'p)$, $\rm (p,2p)$ and transfer reactions. We show that the recently observed increase of the high-momentum component of the protons in neutron-rich nuclei is consistent with the reduced proton spectroscopic factors. Our approach connects recent results on short-range correlations from high-energy electron scattering experiments with the quenching of spectroscopic factors and addresses for the first time quantitatively this intriguing question in nuclear physics, in particular regarding its isospin dependence. We also speculate about the nature of a {\sl quasi-proton} (nuclear polaron) in neutron matter and its kinetic energy, an important quantity for the properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a phenomenological model that connects the recently observed increase of the high-momentum component of protons in neutron-rich nuclei (from Jefferson Lab) with the reduced proton spectroscopic factors, showing that the two are consistent. The authors explicitly state that their approach "addresses for the first time quantitatively this intriguing question in nuclear physics, in particular regarding its isospin dependence," but also note that the available data are "somewhat limited" and that the model is "rather speculative at this stage."

## Method

The authors construct a wavefunction for a "dressed" nucleon (quasi-particle) as a linear combination of a pure single-particle (SP) configuration plus particle-vibration coupling (PVC), pairing correlations (PC), and short-range correlation (SRC) components, assuming orthogonality between these states. They extract the SRC component's isospin dependence from the linear slopes of Jefferson Lab data on high-momentum fractions (SL^p_SRC = 2.8 ± 0.7 and SL^n_SRC = 0.3 ± 0.2) and fit the total quenching R = 1 - (R_PVC + R_PC + R_SRC) to spectroscopic factors from A(e,e'p) experiments on nuclei from 7Li to 208Pb, using Monte-Carlo resampling to extract errors and correlations.

## Key numbers

* SRC contribution at N=Z: γ = 22% ± 8% from the fit to (e,e'p) data
* LRC contribution (PVC + PC) at N=Z: δ = 14% ± 10% from the same fit
* PVC contribution for doubly magic nuclei: α = 10% ± 2% from the fit with SRC fixed to 22% and PC fixed to 3%
* PVC contribution for inclusive (p,2p) data on oxygen isotopes: α = 4% ± 2%
* Slope of proton high-momentum fraction vs. (N-Z)/A: SL^p_SRC = 2.8 ± 0.7
* Slope of neutron high-momentum fraction vs. (N-Z)/A: SL^n_SRC = 0.3 ± 0.2
* Predicted proton quenching factor in neutron matter (A → ∞, (N-Z)/A → 1): R_nM^p ≈ 0.16
* Predicted average kinetic energy of a quasi-proton in neutron matter: approximately 3 times that of a proton in a Fermi Gas
* Predicted average kinetic energy of a neutron in neutron matter: approximately 1.4 times that of a neutron in a Fermi Gas

## Context

This work extends the line of research connecting short-range correlations (SRC) observed in high-energy electron scattering at Jefferson Lab (Duer et al., Nature 2018) with the long-standing puzzle of spectroscopic factor quenching seen in (e,e'p) reactions (Lapikas, 1993; Kramer et al., 2001) and (p,2p) reactions (Devins et al.). The model provides a quantitative framework that reconciles the approximately 20% SRC contribution inferred from inclusive scaling (Hen et al., 2016; Fomin et al., 2012) with the total quenching of about 60-70% observed in spectroscopic factors, and enables predictions for the behavior of a "quasi-proton" (nuclear polaron) in neutron matter relevant to neutron star properties.
