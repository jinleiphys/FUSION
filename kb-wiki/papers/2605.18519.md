# Quark and gluon tomography of the helium-4 nucleus

> QCD collinear factorization allows coherent hard exclusive reactions to reveal the quark-gluon structure of light nuclei, enabling their 3D tomography. We study elastic form factors and deeply virtual Compton scattering on a helium-4 target, achieving theoretical precision unprecedented even in proton studies. Constraining generalized parton distributions at next-to-leading order in $α_s$, incorporating kinematic twist corrections, and using full evolution equations, we provide the first tomography of a light nucleus, revealing distinct transverse spatial distributions of quarks and gluons.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first quark and gluon tomography of a light nucleus (helium-4) by constraining generalized parton distributions at next-to-leading order in the strong coupling constant, including kinematic twist corrections and full evolution equations, but the authors note that genuine higher-twist contributions involving additional nonperturbative functions are left for future work.

## Method

The authors model helium-4 generalized parton distributions using double distributions with a Radon transform, where the t-dependent parton distribution functions are constructed from nNNPDF30 nuclear parton distributions and a novel ansatz for the t-dependence of valence quarks that captures the first two diffractive minima of the elastic form factor. The model parameters are fixed by fitting to elastic form factor data from multiple experiments and to CLAS beam-spin asymmetry data for deeply virtual Compton scattering, using helicity amplitudes evaluated at next-to-leading order in alpha_S with kinematic twist-3 and twist-4 corrections. The constrained generalized parton distributions are then Fourier-transformed to obtain transverse spatial distributions of quarks and gluons.

## Key numbers

- The fit to CLAS beam-spin asymmetry data achieves chi^2/N_pts = 1.00 at NLO/HT precision, compared to 1.31 at LO/LT and 1.28 at NLO/LT
- Mean squared transverse radius for valence quarks: <b_T^2>_qval = 1.943(28) fm^2
- Mean squared transverse radius for sea quarks: <b_T^2>_qsea = 3.51(63) fm^2
- Mean squared transverse radius for gluons: <b_T^2>_g = 3.50(62) fm^2
- Derived electric charge radius of helium-4: 1.71(21) fm, compared to the literature value of 1.6785(21) fm from Mohr (2024)
- The tomographic plots are not populated above x = 1/4 due to the nuclear parton distribution definitions

## Context

This work extends the technique of hadron tomography, previously applied to the proton by Burkardt (2002) and others, to a light nucleus for the first time. It builds on prior measurements of coherent beam-spin asymmetries for helium-4 by CLAS (2017) and elastic form factor data from multiple experiments including Jefferson Lab Hall A (2013). The results enable future studies of nuclear modifications of partonic distributions and provide a framework for analyzing exclusive scattering off other light nuclei, though the authors note that challenging the assumption of identical transverse profiles for sea quarks and gluons would require more DVCS data at lower x_A and t values.
