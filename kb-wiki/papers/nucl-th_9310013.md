# Convergence properties of the effective interaction

> The convergence properties of two perturbative schemes to sum the so-called folded diagrams are critically reviewed, with an emphasis on the intruder state problem. The methods we study are the approaches of Kuo and co-workers and Lee and Suzuki. The suitability of the two schemes for shell-model calculations are discussed.

# Convergence properties of the effective interaction

## Key claim

The convergence properties of the folded-diagram (FD) method of Kuo and co-workers and the Lee-Suzuki (LS) iterative scheme differ fundamentally only when an exact $\hat{Q}$-box can be defined; with a low-order approximate $\hat{Q}$-box, as used in realistic nuclear structure calculations, both methods yield nearly identical results and the LS method cannot reproduce intruder states even with an appropriately chosen starting energy.

## Method

The authors studied the convergence of two perturbative schemes for summing folded diagrams, the FD method of Kuo and co-workers and the LS method, first using an exactly solvable $2\times 2$ model where the Hamiltonian depends linearly on a strength parameter $z$, allowing definition of an exact $\hat{Q}$-box. They then applied both methods to the realistic nucleus $^{18}\text{O}$ using a model space of two nucleons in the $sd$-shell above a $^{16}\text{O}$ core, evaluating the $\hat{Q}$-box through third order in the $G$-matrix derived from the Bonn B potential with a harmonic oscillator basis (oscillator parameter $1.72$ fm). The eigenvalues were studied as functions of the starting energy $\omega$, and the $P$-space overlaps were extracted from the slopes of the graphical solutions.

## Key numbers

- For the $2\times 2$ model with parameters $\epsilon_P=0$, $\epsilon_Q=4$, $X_P=-X_Q=3$, $X=0.2$: at $z=1$, the $\Phi_P$ mixing of the lowest eigenvalue is $1\%$; for $z\leq 2/3$, the $\Phi_P$ component exceeds $90\%$.
- For $^{18}\text{O}$ $0^+$ states: the $P$-space overlap near the graphical solutions is approximately $0.93$–$0.95$ for the $0_1^+$ state and $0.98$–$0.99$ for the $0_2^+$ state, evaluated in the starting energy range $-20$ to $-4$ MeV.
- The $0_1^+$ state derivative becomes positive at $-28$ MeV ($dE/d\omega = 0.285$).
- With a fifth-order $\hat{Q}$-box in the $2\times 2$ model, the FD method becomes unstable at $z=1$; with coupling $X=0.01$, this divergence appears first with a tenth-order $\hat{Q}$-box.
- For the LS method in the $2\times 2$ model, a $\hat{Q}$-box of fiftieth order is needed to approach the lowest eigenvalue; further increase to approximately $150$th order causes divergence.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Brillouin-Wigner and Rayleigh-Schrödinger perturbation theory by Ellis and Osnes (1977) using the same $2\times 2$ model, and critically examines the iterative summation schemes of Kuo and co-workers (1990) and Lee and Suzuki (1980). The paper demonstrates that in realistic calculations with a low-order $\hat{Q}$-box, the LS method cannot selectively reproduce intruder states as its convergence criterion would suggest, and recommends the FD method for shell-model calculations because it converges to states with the largest model-space overlap, a property essential for applications to nuclei with more than two valence particles.
