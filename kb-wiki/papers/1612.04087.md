# Calculations of kaonic nuclei based on chiral meson-baryon coupled channel interaction models

> We present our latest calculations of $K^-$-nuclear quasi-bound states using a self-consistent scheme for constructing $K^-$-nuclear potentials from various subthreshold chirally inspired $\bar{K}N$ scattering amplitudes. We consider in-medium versions of the scattering amplitudes taking into account Pauli blocking in the intermediate states. The resulting $K^-$ binding energies as well as the widths exhibit the same A dependence, however, the binding energies strongly depend on the model used.

# Calculations of kaonic nuclei based on chiral meson-baryon coupled channel interaction models

## Key claim

The binding energies and widths of K−-nuclear quasi-bound states exhibit a similar dependence on mass number A across all considered chiral interaction models, but the actual binding energies vary strongly depending on which model is used. The Bonn models B2 and B4 failed to produce any bound states because their K−N amplitudes are weakly attractive or repulsive below threshold.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation for K−-nuclear quasi-bound states using a self-consistent scheme in which the K− self-energy was constructed in a tρ form from subthreshold chirally inspired K̅N scattering amplitudes. In-medium versions of the scattering amplitudes were obtained by applying the multiple scattering approach (WRW) and by considering Pauli blocking in intermediate states, with the available energy √s in the laboratory frame accounting for nucleon binding energy, kaon binding energy, and nucleon kinetic energy from the Fermi gas model. Calculations were performed for six different coupled-channel interaction models: Prague (P NLO), Kyoto-Munich (KM NLO), Murcia (M1 and M2), and Bonn (B2 and B4).

## Key numbers

- K− binding energies and widths were calculated for 1s states across the periodic table as a function of mass number A; the KM NLO model predicted widths twice as large as the P NLO and M1 models.
- In ⁴⁰Ca, the K− 1s state had the smallest K−N → πY conversion width in the P NLO and KM NLO models due to energy shift toward the πΣ threshold, while in the M1 model the widths followed the opposite trend.
- Core polarization from the K− was estimated from prior work to add up to 5 MeV to K− binding energies.
- Annihilation of K− on two nucleons was noted to increase the width by almost 50 MeV, making even 1s state widths comparable with binding energies.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent calculations of kaonic nuclei by comparing predictions from six different chiral meson-baryon coupled-channel interaction models (Prague, Kyoto-Munich, Murcia, and Bonn) that all reproduce low-energy K−N observables including the SIDDHARTA measurement of the 1s level shift and width in kaonic hydrogen. The results show that while the A-dependence of binding energies is robust across models, the absolute values are model-dependent, and the Bonn models fail to bind K− at all. The authors note that core polarization (adding up to 5 MeV) and two-nucleon absorption (adding nearly 50 MeV to widths) were not included, based on earlier work by Gal and Mareš, and they intend to incorporate these effects in future calculations.
