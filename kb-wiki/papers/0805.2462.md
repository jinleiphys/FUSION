# Nuclear Force from Monte Carlo Simulations of Lattice Quantum Chromodynamics

> The nuclear force acting between protons and neutrons is studied in the Monte Carlo simulations of the fundamental theory of the strong interaction, the quantum chromodynamics defined on the hypercubic space-time lattice. After a brief summary of the empirical nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials which can fit the NN scattering experiments in high precision, we outline the basic formulation to derive the potential between the extended objects such as the nucleons composed of quarks. The equal-time Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is a key ingredient for defining the NN potential on the lattice. We show the results of the numerical simulations on a $32^4$ lattice with the lattice spacing $a \simeq 0.137 $fm (lattice volume (4.4 fm)$^4$) in the quenched approximation. The calculation was carried out using the massively parallel computer Blue Gene/L at KEK. We found that the calculated NN potential at low energy has basic features expected from the empirical NN potentials; attraction at long and medium distances and the repulsive core at short distance. Various future directions along this line of research are also summarized.

## Key claim

The authors derive the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential from first-principles lattice QCD simulations, finding that the calculated potential reproduces the qualitative features of empirical potentials (attraction at long and medium distances, repulsive core at short distance), but only in the quenched approximation and at unphysically heavy quark masses, with quantitative agreement requiring future full QCD simulations at lighter quark masses.

## Method

The authors compute the equal-time Bethe-Salpeter (BS) amplitude for the proton-neutron system from four-point correlators on a \(32^4\) lattice with lattice spacing \(a \simeq 0.137\) fm in the quenched approximation. They define an effective central potential \(V_{\rm C}(r) = E_q + (1/m_{\rm N}) \nabla^2 \psi(r) / \psi(r)\) from the BS wave function \(\psi(r)\), using the nearest-neighbor discretized Laplacian and extracting the asymptotic momentum \(q\) by fitting the wave function to the Green's function in a finite periodic box. Simulations are performed at three values of the hopping parameter \(\kappa\) (corresponding to pion masses 732, 529, and 380 MeV) using the IBM Blue Gene/L at KEK.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing: \(a \simeq 0.137\) fm, determined from the \(\rho\) meson mass in the chiral limit, on a \(32^4\) lattice with physical volume \((4.4\ {\rm fm})^4\).
* Pion masses: \(m_\pi = 732.1(4)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1640\), \(529.0(4)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1665\), and \(379.7(9)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1678\).
* Nucleon masses: \(m_{\rm N} = 1558.4(63)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1640\), \(1333.8(82)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1665\), and \(1196.6(32)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1678\).
* Scattering lengths from Luscher's formula: \(a_0(^1S_0) = 0.115(26)\) fm and \(a_0(^3S_1) = 0.140(31)\) fm at \(m_\pi=732\) MeV; \(a_0(^1S_0) = 0.126(25)\) fm and \(a_0(^3S_1) = 0.140(31)\) fm at \(m_\pi=529\) MeV; \(a_0(^1S_0) = 0.159(66)\) fm and \(a_0(^3S_1) = 0.252(104)\) fm at \(m_\pi=380\) MeV.
* Non-relativistic energies: \(E_q(^1S_0) = -0.400(83)\) MeV and \(E_q(^3S_1) = -0.480(97)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1640\); \(E_q(^1S_0) = -0.509(94)\) MeV and \(E_q(^3S_1) = -0.560(114)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1665\); \(E_q(^1S_0) = -0.675(264)\) MeV and \(E_q(^3S_1) = -0.968(374)\) MeV at \(\kappa=0.1678\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Luscher (1986) for extracting scattering phase shifts from finite-volume lattice simulations to directly define a coordinate-space NN potential from the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude. It provides the first lattice QCD derivation of the NN potential with repulsive core and medium-range attraction, complementing phenomenological potentials such as the CD-Bonn potential, Argonne \(v_{18}\) potential (Wiringa, 1994), and Nijmegen potentials (Stoks, 1994). The method enables future full QCD simulations at physical quark masses, studies of tensor and spin-orbit forces, and extensions to three-nucleon forces and hyperon-nucleon interactions (e.g., \(\Xi\)N and \(\Lambda\)N potentials by Nemura et al., 2007-2008).
