# Spinning protons and gluons in the $η'$

> The proton spin puzzle has inspired a vast programme of experiments and theoretical work challenging our understanding of QCD and its role in the structure of hadrons.The proton's internal spin structure is connected to chiral symmetry and, through gluon degrees of freedom in the flavour singlet channel, to the physics of the $η'$ meson. Why do quarks contribute just about one third of the proton's spin? Why are $η'$ mesons and their interactions so sensitive to gluonic degrees of freedom? We review the status of these topics and some key observables for forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The proton spin puzzle, why quarks contribute only about one third of the proton's spin, can be resolved within present experimental and theoretical errors by combining the colour hyperfine one-gluon-exchange potential with pion cloud effects and modest polarized gluon contributions, provided that SU(3) flavour symmetry breaking in the octet axial charge is taken into account.

## Method

The paper reviews the status of polarized deep inelastic scattering experiments, where the first moment of the spin structure function \(g_1^p(x,Q^2)\) is extracted using next-to-leading order QCD evolution to a common \(Q^2\), and combined with the isovector axial charge from neutron beta decay and the octet axial charge from hyperon beta decays (with or without SU(3) symmetry assumptions). The flavour-singlet axial charge \(g_A^{(0)}\) is then related to the quark and gluon spin contributions via the QCD axial anomaly, and the results are compared with model calculations including the Cloudy Bag model, lattice QCD, and global fits to spin-dependent parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- Quarks contribute approximately 30% of the proton's spin, deduced from the flavour-singlet axial charge \(g_A^{(0)}|_{\rm pDIS, Q^2=3 {\rm GeV}^2} = 0.32 \pm 0.02 ({\rm stat.}) \pm 0.04 ({\rm syst.}) \pm 0.05 ({\rm evol.})\) measured by COMPASS.
- The isovector axial charge from neutron beta decay is \(g_A^{(3)} = 1.275 \pm 0.001\).
- The octet axial charge extracted assuming good SU(3) symmetry is \(g_A^{(8)} = 0.58 \pm 0.03\); with pion cloud corrections in the Cloudy Bag model this becomes \(g_A^{(8)} = 0.46 \pm 0.05\).
- The strange quark polarization extracted from the COMPASS data is \(\Delta s_{Q^2 \rightarrow \infty} = -0.09 \pm 0.02\) using the SU(3) octet value, or \(\Delta s \sim -0.04 \pm 0.03\) using the Cloudy Bag octet value.
- The Bjorken sum rule extracted from COMPASS gives \(1.29 \pm 0.05 \pm 0.10\), consistent with the neutron beta decay value.
- Polarized gluon polarization from NLO global fits gives \(\int_{0.05}^1 dx \Delta g(x) \approx 0.2 \pm 0.05\) and \(|\int_{0.001}^1 dx \Delta g(x)| \lesssim 0.8\) at \(Q^2=10\) GeV\(^2\).
- Lattice QCD gives \(g_A^{(0)} \approx 0.40 \pm 0.04\) with \(\Delta s\) close to -0.04.
- The \(\eta'\) mass shift in nuclear matter at density \(\rho_0\) measured by ELSA is \(V(\rho=\rho_0) = -40 \pm 6 \pm 15\) MeV with width \(W(\rho=\rho_0) = -13 \pm 3 \pm 3\) MeV.
- The proton GDH sum rule integral is \(+209 \pm 13\) \(\mu\)b, in agreement with the theoretical prediction of \(+205\) \(\mu\)b from the anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa=1.79\).

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Fritzsch (1975) on the role of gluonic degrees of freedom in the flavour-singlet channel, connecting the proton spin puzzle to the physics of the \(\eta'\) meson through the QCD axial anomaly and gluon topology. It builds on the operator product expansion analysis of polarized deep inelastic scattering by Altarelli (1988), Efremov (1988), and Carlitz (1988), and on the Witten-Veneziano mass formula for the \(\eta\) and \(\eta'\) mesons. The results enable future experimental tests at the Electron Ion Collider (EIC), which will extend measurements to Bjorken \(x\) down to \(10^{-4}\), and motivate searches for \(\eta'\) bound states in nuclei, which would represent a new form of matter bound by the strong interaction alone.
