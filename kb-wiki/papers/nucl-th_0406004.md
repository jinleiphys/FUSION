# Ultra-cold Neutron Production in Anti-ferromagnetic Oxygen Solid

> Spin waves, or magnons, in the anti-ferromagnetic $α$ phase of solid oxygen provide a novel mechanism for ultra-cold neutron (UCN) production. Magnons dominate the energy exchange mechanisms for cold neutrons and UCN in solid $α$-oxygen, much in the same way as do phonons in solid deuterium superthermal UCN sources. We present calculations of UCN production and upscattering rates in S-O$_2$. The results indicate that S-O$_2$ is potentially a much more efficient UCN source material than solid deuterium.

# Ultra-cold Neutron Production in Anti-ferromagnetic Oxygen Solid

## Key claim
Spin waves (magnons) in the anti-ferromagnetic α phase of solid oxygen provide a novel mechanism for ultra-cold neutron (UCN) production that is potentially much more efficient than solid deuterium, though the paper presents calculations rather than experimental measurements.

## Method
The authors calculated UCN production and upscattering rates in solid α-oxygen (S-O₂) by modeling magnon energy exchange mechanisms for cold neutrons and UCN. The calculations treat magnons as the dominant energy-exchange quasiparticles, analogous to phonons in solid deuterium superthermal UCN sources.

## Context
This work extends the superthermal UCN source concept, previously applied to solid deuterium (where phonons dominate energy exchange), to a new material system. The anti-ferromagnetic α phase of solid oxygen introduces magnons as the energy-exchange mechanism. The paper enables future experimental investigation of solid oxygen as a UCN source material, potentially offering higher efficiency than existing solid deuterium sources.
