# Fission process of low excited nuclei with Langevin approach

> Fragment mass distributions from the fission of U and Pu isotopes at low excitation energies are studied using a dynamical model based on the fluctuation-dissipation theorem formulated as Langevin equations. The present calculations reproduced the overall trend of the asymmetric mass distribution without parameter adjustment for the first time using the Langevin approach. The Langevin trajectories show a complicated time evolution on the potential surface, which causes the time delay of fission, showing that dynamical treatment is vital. It was found that the shell effect of the potential energy landscape has a dominant role in determining the mass distribution, although it is rather insensitive to the strength of dissipation. Nevertheless, it is essential to include the effect of dissipation, since it has a crucial role in giving "fluctuation" to Langevin trajectories as well as for explaining the multiplicities of pre-scission neutrons as the excitation energy increases. Therefore, the present approach can serve as a basis for more refined analysis.

## Key claim

For the first time, a Langevin-based dynamical model reproduces the overall trend of asymmetric fragment mass distributions for thermal-neutron-induced fission of U and Pu isotopes without parameter adjustment, but only when the full shell correction energy (Φ(T)=1) is used to simulate the low excitation energy introduced by thermal neutrons.

## Method

The authors solved three-dimensional Langevin equations (with collective coordinates z₀, δ, and α) based on the fluctuation-dissipation theorem, using a two-center shell model to compute the shell correction energy at each deformation. The potential energy combined a finite-range liquid-drop model with a temperature-dependent shell correction (Strutinski method), and the friction tensor was taken from the wall-and-window one-body dissipation model. Trajectories started at the second minimum of the potential energy surface (z=0.65, δ=0.2, α=0.0) at E*=20 MeV, and 10,000 trajectories were run per nucleus to build the mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- Fragment mass distributions for ²³⁶U, ²³⁴U, and ²⁴⁰Pu at excitation energy E*=20 MeV were calculated and compared to experimental data from JENDL Fission Yield Data File.
- For ²³⁶U at E*=20 MeV, the average total kinetic energy ⟨TKE⟩ = 171.8 MeV, consistent with experimental data from Vand (1973).
- The shell damping energy was tested at values Ed = 16.3, 20, 30, and 40 MeV; the gross features of the mass distribution did not vary significantly across these values.
- Friction strength was varied by factors of 0.1, 0.5, 1, 2, and 5; the mass distribution was "rather insensitive" to the strength of friction at this low excitation energy.
- Almost all fission events occurred dynamically until t = 1×10⁻¹⁸ s, after which the number of fission events became saturated.

## Context

This work extends earlier Langevin calculations (Wada 1993, Abe 1996, Frobrich 1998, and others) which had only been applied to high-excitation-energy fission and produced symmetric mass distributions. It directly addresses the long-standing puzzle of asymmetric mass splitting in U and Pu fission, which the liquid-drop model alone could not explain. The approach enables future refined analyses including independent fragment deformations, neutron emission from fragments, and microscopic transport coefficients, and provides a basis for simulating fission of minor actinides where experimental data are scarce. A related random-walk method (Randrup 2011) also reproduced experimental mass yields but cannot discuss the fission time scale, which the present dynamical treatment can.
