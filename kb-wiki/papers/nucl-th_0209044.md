# Hyperon ordering in neutron star matter

> We explore the possible formation of ordered phases in neutron star matter. In the framework of a quantum hadrodynamics model where neutrons, protons and Lambda hyperons interact via the exchange of mesons, we compare the energy of the usually assumed uniform, liquid phase, to that of a configuration in which di-lambda pairs immersed in an uniform nucleon fluid are localized on the nodes of a regular lattice. The confining potential is calculated self-consistently as resulting from the combined action of the nucleon fluid and the other hyperons, under the condition of beta equilibrium. We are able to obtain stable ordered phases for some reasonable sets of values of the model parameters. This could have important consequences on the structure and cooling of neutron stars.

## Key claim
The paper claims that an ordered phase of hyperons (di-lambda pairs localized on a cubic lattice) can be energetically more favorable than the uniform liquid phase in neutron star matter, but only for some reasonable sets of model parameters and above a threshold density. The hedge is that the result depends more strongly on the largely unknown in-medium hyperon-hyperon potential than on the properties of the nucleon background.

## Method
The authors used a quantum hadrodynamics model within the Density Dependent Hadron Field Theory (DDRH) framework, where neutrons, protons, and Lambda hyperons interact via sigma, omega, delta, and rho meson exchange with density-dependent coupling constants. They compared the energy density of the uniform liquid phase (with beta equilibrium among baryons and leptons) to a crystallized phase where di-lambda pairs with antiparallel spins are localized in gaussian clouds on a cubic lattice, with the confining potential calculated self-consistently from the combined action of the nucleon fluid and other hyperons. The equations for chemical equilibrium, effective masses, and the confining potential were solved simultaneously to determine the thermodynamical state.

## Key numbers
- The threshold for hyperon production above which the ordered phase becomes energetically more favorable is \( n_{\rm th} = 1.95 \, n_{\rm sat} \) (where \( n_{\rm sat} \) is saturation density).
- The hyperon density is related to the lattice parameter \( a \) by \( n_\Lambda = 2 / a^3 \).
- The gaussian distribution for hyperon density is given by \( n(r) = 2 \left( \frac{M_{\Lambda}\nu_{0}}{\pi }\right)^{3/2}e^{- M_{\Lambda} \nu_{0} r^{2} } \), with width \( \Gamma = 1/\sqrt{M_\Lambda \nu_0} \).
- The energy gain of the ordered phase over the liquid phase is smaller than that obtained for parameter set C in PerezGarcia (2002), though the shape of the curve is similar.

## Context
This work extends the simplified model of PerezGarcia (2002) by using a more realistic description of nuclear interaction based on the DDRH approach developed by the Giessen group (Hofmann 2000, Keil 2000, Hofmann 2001), which reproduces correct values of incompressibility modulus and asymmetry energy. The findings confirm that ordered phases can be energetically favorable, but the result depends strongly on the poorly known hyperon-hyperon potential. The authors note that future work should include additional hyperon species (Sigma, Xi) and meson exchanges (sigma*, phi, kaons, eta) as considered by Schaffner (1994), and should release the Sommerfeld approximation to account for nucleon redistribution via RPA-type screening correlations.
