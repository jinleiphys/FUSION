# Effects of phonon-phonon coupling on low-lying states in neutron-rich Sn isotopes

> Starting from an effective Skyrme interaction we present a method to take into account the coupling between one- and two-phonon terms in the wave functions of excited states. The approach is a development of a finite rank separable approximation for the quasiparticle RPA calculations proposed in our previous work. The influence of the phonon-phonon coupling on energies and transition probabilities for the low-lying quadrupole and octupole states in the neutron-rich Sn isotopes is studied.

# Effects of phonon-phonon coupling on low-lying states in neutron-rich Sn isotopes

## Key claim

The coupling between one- and two-phonon configurations, treated within a finite-rank separable approximation to the Skyrme interaction, systematically lowers the energies and reduces the transition probabilities of the first 2⁺ and 3⁻ states in neutron-rich Sn isotopes, but the effect is weaker for the doubly magic ¹³²Sn than for its neighbors.

## Method

The authors start from the Skyrme SLy4 interaction and perform Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations to obtain the single-particle spectrum, which is discretized on a harmonic oscillator basis. The particle-hole residual interaction is approximated by its Landau-Migdal form (keeping only l=0 terms) and then expressed as a rank-N separable interaction using a Gauss integration formula. Quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) phonons are constructed, and the Hamiltonian is rewritten in terms of phonon and quasiparticle operators. The wave functions of excited states are expanded to include both one-phonon and two-phonon configurations, and the resulting secular equation is solved to obtain energies and amplitudes.

- For ¹³²Sn: QRPA energy of 2⁺₁ = 1.57 MeV; 2PH = 1.49 MeV; experimental = 1.44 MeV.
- For ¹³⁴Sn: QRPA energy of 2⁺₁ = 1.32 MeV; 2PH = 1.17 MeV; experimental = 1.13 MeV.
- For ¹²⁴Sn: QRPA B(E2↑) = 0.146 e²b²; 2PH = 0.140 e²b²; experimental = 0.166 e²b².
- For ¹³²Sn: QRPA B(E2↑) = 0.107 e²b²; 2PH = 0.103 e²b²; experimental = 0.114 e²b².
- For ¹³⁴Sn: QRPA B(E2↑) = 0.036 e²b²; 2PH = 0.034 e²b²; experimental = 0.034 e²b².
- For ¹²⁴Sn: QRPA energy of 3⁻₁ = 2.83 MeV; 2PH = 2.56 MeV; experimental = 2.60 MeV.
- For ¹³²Sn: QRPA energy of 3⁻₁ = 3.24 MeV; 2PH = 2.95 MeV; experimental = 2.91 MeV.
- For ¹³⁴Sn: QRPA energy of 3⁻₁ = 2.93 MeV; 2PH = 2.64 MeV; experimental = 2.68 MeV.
- The rank of the separable approximation is N=45 for multipolarities λ ≤ 3 in nuclei with A ≤ 208.
- The cut-off in the space of two-phonon configurations is 21 MeV.
- The BCS subspace includes all subshells lying below 5 MeV.
- The single-particle spectra are cut off at 100 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the finite-rank separable approximation for QRPA calculations with Skyrme interactions, previously developed by the same group (Severyukhin et al., 2002), to include phonon-phonon coupling. It builds on the quasiparticle-phonon model (QPM) formalism of Soloviev but replaces phenomenological parameters with self-consistent Skyrme forces. The results are compared with experimental data from Hribar et al. and Raman et al., and with earlier QRPA calculations using Skyrme forces (Colo et al., 2003) and Gogny forces (Giam et al., 2003). The method enables structure calculations in very large configuration spaces without diagonalizing matrices whose dimensions grow with the configuration space size, and it can be applied to nuclei far from stability.
