# Rescattering and finite formation time effects in inclusive and exclusive electro-disintegration of nuclei within a relativistic approach: 1. The deuteron

> The rescattering contribution to the inclusive and exclusive deuteron electro-disintegration at the values of the Bjorken scaling variable $x=1$, as well as in the so called {\it cumulative} region ($x>1$) is calculated within a relativistic approach based on the Feynman diagram formalism taking into account colour transparency effects by the inclusion of the {\it finite formation time} (FFT) of the ejected nucleon via the introduction of the dependence of the scattering amplitude of the ejectile upon its virtuality. In the cumulative region the FFT effects which result from the real part of the ejectile propagator are taken into account. It is found that the relative weight of the rescattering steadily grows with $x$ becoming of the order of unity at $x>1.4÷1.5$. At such values of $x$ the finite formation time effects become fairly visible, which may serve for their study at relatively small value of the four-momentum transfer$Q^2$. The relativistic rescattering contribution is compared with the Glauber rescattering, which is shown to be not valid in the cumulative region starting from $x>1.4$.

## Key claim

The rescattering contribution to deuteron electro-disintegration becomes comparable to the impulse approximation (order of unity) at Bjorken scaling variable values x > 1.4-1.5, and at such x values finite formation time (FFT) effects become clearly visible even at relatively small four-momentum transfer Q^2, contrary to the usual expectation that such effects would be negligible in the cumulative region.

## Method

The authors calculated the rescattering contribution to inclusive and exclusive deuteron electro-disintegration using a relativistic Feynman diagram approach with spinless nucleons and an effective electromagnetic form-factor. They introduced finite formation time effects by modifying the ejectile propagator through subtraction of an excited state term with mass m* = 1.8 GeV, and compared their relativistic results with the standard Glauber rescattering approximation.

## Key numbers

- The relative weight of rescattering becomes of order unity at x > 1.4-1.5
- Finite formation time effects become fairly visible at x values above 1.4-1.5
- The Glauber rescattering approximation is shown to be not valid in the cumulative region starting from x > 1.4
- The excited state mass used for the FFT subtraction term was m* = 1.8 GeV
- The rescattering contribution reaches 50% of the total at x = 1.5
- FFT effects are very small at x < 1.3-1.4 but become pronounced at larger x

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous approach (Braun, Ciofi degli Atti, Kaptari, 2003) for treating final state interactions via Feynman diagrams with virtuality-dependent scattering amplitudes from x = 1 into the cumulative region x > 1. It contradicts the expectation from Kopeliovich (1990) that color transparency effects should be much smaller in the cumulative region than at x = 1, showing instead that FFT effects from the real part of the ejectile propagator become significant at x > 1.4. The approach enables experimental study of color transparency effects at relatively low Q^2 by measuring rescattering in the cumulative region, where the Glauber approximation fails.
