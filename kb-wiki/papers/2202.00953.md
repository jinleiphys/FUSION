# Quantum Skyrmion crystals and the symmetry energy of dense matter

> The canonical quantization method for collective coordinates in crystalline configurations of the generalized Skyrme model is applied in order to find the quantum ground state of Skyrmion crystals and study the quantum corrections to the binding energy resulting from the isospin degrees of freedom. This leads to a consistent description of asymmetric nuclear matter within the Skyrme framework and allows us to compute the symmetry energy of the Skyrmionic crystal as a function of the baryon density, and to compare with recent observational constraints.

## Key claim

The paper claims that semiclassical quantization of isospin degrees of freedom in Skyrmion crystals, combined with electric charge neutrality enforced by a lepton background, yields a consistent description of asymmetric nuclear matter and allows computation of the symmetry energy as a function of baryon density; however, the quantum ground state of the crystal is not simply the isospin-zero state, but rather the \(i_3 = -2\) state within the \(i=2\) multiplet, because electrical neutrality forces a nonzero isospin projection.

## Method

The authors start from the generalized Skyrme model Lagrangian with four terms (kinetic, Skyrme, sextic, and pion mass) and construct periodic classical Skyrmion crystal solutions for the FCC half-skyrmion phase, using a unit cell of size \(2L\) with baryon number \(B_{\text{cell}} = 4\). They then perform canonical quantization of the global isospin zero modes by imposing Finkelstein-Rubinstein constraints from the crystal's point-group symmetries (\(A_2\) and \(C_3\)), finding the allowed quantum isospin states for each unit cell. The quantum ground state is selected by requiring electric charge neutrality of the crystal, which introduces a neutralizing charged lepton background and imposes \(\beta\)-equilibrium conditions, leading to an isospin-asymmetric ground state and a density-dependent symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

*   Pion mass fixed to \(m_\pi = 140\ \text{MeV}\) throughout the paper.
*   Standard Skyrme model parameters: \(f_\pi = 129\ \text{MeV}\), \(e = 5.45\) (from fitting the \(B=1\) solution to proton and \(\Delta\) masses in Adkins (1983)).
*   The unit cell of the FCC half-skyrmion crystal has baryon number \(B_{\text{cell}} = 4\) and size \(2L\).
*   The FCC to BCC phase transition in the generalized model occurs at approximately 4–5 times nuclear saturation density.
*   The symmetry energy at saturation is cited as \(S_0 \sim 30\ \text{MeV}\) from nuclear experiments (Fiorella Fantina 2018).

## Context

This work extends the classical treatment of Skyrmion crystals (Adam 2021) by adding semiclassical quantization of isospin, which was previously only applied to finite nuclei. It enables the computation of the symmetry energy and equation of state for neutron-rich matter (including \(npe\mu\) matter) within the Skyrme framework, allowing direct comparison with astrophysical constraints from neutron star observations (Miller 2021, Riley 2021, Abbott 2017, Abbott 2020) and combined nuclear-astrophysical analyses (Landry 2021, Tang 2021, de Tovar 2021, Gil 2021, Li 2021). The approach resolves the issue that classical Skyrmion crystals describe only symmetric nuclear matter, whereas neutron stars require asymmetric matter with a small proton fraction.
