# Unified framework for generalized and transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions within a 3Q light-cone picture of the nucleon

> We present a systematic study of generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs). By taking specific limits or projections, these GTMDs yield various transverse-momentum dependent and generalized parton distributions, thus providing a unified framework to simultaneously model different observables. We present such simultaneous modeling by considering a light-cone wave function overlap representation of the GTMDs. We construct the different quark-quark correlation functions from the 3-quark Fock components within both the light-front constituent quark model as well as within the chiral quark-soliton model. We provide a comparison with available data and make predictions for different observables.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) provide a unified framework for modeling different parton distributions, but the actual calculations are restricted to the three-quark (3Q) Fock sector and the region ξ ≤ x ≤ 1, with the authors explicitly postponing inclusion of higher-Fock space components to future work.

## Method

The authors derive an overlap representation of GTMDs using light-cone wave functions (LCWFs) for the nucleon's three-quark Fock component, then construct the quark-quark correlation functions within both the light-front constituent quark model (LCCQM) and the chiral quark-soliton model (χQSM). By taking specific limits, the forward limit Δ=0 for transverse-momentum dependent distributions (TMDs) and integration over transverse momentum for generalized parton distributions (GPDs), they extract the various parton distributions from the same GTMD formalism. The LCWFs in both models share a similar structure with a spin-flavor part separated from a momentum part, connected by a generalized Melosh rotation relating light-cone helicity to canonical spin.

## Key numbers

- For up quarks in a proton, the spin-flavor factors are N_p^u = 2 and P_p^u = 4/3; for down quarks, N_p^d = 1 and P_p^d = -1/3
- Lattice results cited for the average dipole deformation of the k⊥ density: ⟨k_x^u(g_{1T})⟩ = 67(5) MeV for up quarks and ⟨k_x^d(g_{1T})⟩ = -30(5) MeV for down quarks in a transversely polarized nucleon
- Lattice results for transversely polarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon: ⟨k_x^u(h_{1L}^⊥)⟩ = -60(5) MeV for up quarks and ⟨k_x^d(h_{1L}^⊥)⟩ = 16(5) MeV for down quarks

## Context

This work extends the classification of GTMDs from Meissner (2009) by providing a concrete model framework for simultaneous calculation of TMDs, GPDs, PDFs, and form factors, which had previously been studied separately in different models. The formalism enables future inclusion of higher-Fock components and gluon degrees of freedom, and the authors note that the quark-model relations they derive (such as g_{1T} + h_{1L}^⊥ = 0) are supported by recent lattice calculations from Musch (2010) and Hagler (2009), though these relations break down under QCD evolution and in models with gauge-field degrees of freedom.
