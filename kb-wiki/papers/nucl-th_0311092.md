# Sequence of Potentials Lying Between the U(5) and X(5) Symmetries

> Starting from the original collective Hamiltonian of Bohr and separating the beta and gamma variables as in the X(5) model of Iachello, an exactly soluble model corresponding to a harmonic oscillator potential in the beta-variable (to be called X(5)-$β^2$) is constructed. Furthermore, it is proved that the potentials of the form $β^{2n}$ (with n being integer) provide a ``bridge'' between this new X(5)-$β^2$ model (occuring for n=1) and the X(5) model (corresponding to an infinite well potential in the beta-variable, materialized for n going to infinity. Parameter-free (up to overall scale factors) predictions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates are given for the potentials $β^2$, $β^4$, $β^6$, $β^8$, corresponding to E(4)/E(2) ratios of 2.646, 2.769, 2.824, and 2.852 respectively, compared to the E(4)/E(2) ratios of 2.000 for U(5) and 2.904 for X(5). Hints about nuclei showing this behaviour, as well as about potentials ``bridging'' the X(5) symmetry with SU(3) are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper constructs an exactly soluble model using a harmonic oscillator potential in the beta variable (X(5)-beta^2) and proves that potentials of the form beta^{2n} provide a bridge between this new model (n=1) and the X(5) model (n going to infinity), with the caveat that the X(5) infinite well is only materialized in the limit n to infinity, though n=4 is already quite close.

## Method

Starting from the Bohr collective Hamiltonian, the authors separate the beta and gamma variables as in the X(5) model of Iachello. They solve the resulting differential equation exactly for a harmonic oscillator potential in beta (X(5)-beta^2) and numerically for beta^4, beta^6, and beta^8 potentials using matrix representation of position and momentum operators and direct integration methods. Parameter-free predictions for spectra and B(E2) transition rates are computed for each potential.

## Key numbers

- E(4)/E(2) ratio for X(5)-beta^2: 2.646; for beta^4: 2.769; for beta^6: 2.824; for beta^8: 2.852; compared to 2.000 for U(5) and 2.904 for X(5)
- R_2 ratio (position of s=2 bandhead) for X(5)-beta^2: 3.562; for X(5): 5.649
- R_4 ratio (E(4)/E(2)) within ground state band for X(5)-beta^2: 2.646; for X(5): 2.904
- R_6 ratio for X(5)-beta^2: 4.507
- B(E2: 4_1+ to 2_1+) / B(E2: 2_1+ to 0_1+) ratio for X(5)-beta^2: 1.7790; for X(5): 1.5989
- R'_s ratio for X(5)-beta^2: 2.646 (independent of s); limiting value of R'_s for X(5) as s to infinity: 2.646

## Context

This work extends the X(5) model of Iachello (2001) and the E(5) model of Iachello (2000), which provide parameter-independent predictions for nuclear spectra and B(E2) transition rates related to phase transitions from U(5) (vibrational) to SU(3) (prolate deformed) and O(6) (gamma-unstable) nuclei respectively. The sequence of potentials enables systematic study of nuclei lying between the U(5) vibrational symmetry and the X(5) critical point, with preliminary candidates identified including Nd-148 for X(5)-beta^2, Er-158 for X(5)-beta^6, and Yb-160 for X(5)-beta^4. The authors suggest future work should find sequences interpolating between SU(3) and X(5) and between O(6) and E(5), proposing Davidson-like potentials as candidates.
