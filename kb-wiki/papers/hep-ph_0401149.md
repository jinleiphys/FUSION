# Effective Lagrangians for QCD: Deconfinement and Chiral Symmetry Restoration

> Effective Lagrangians for Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) especially suited for understanding deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration at nonzero temperature and matter density are reviewed. These effective theories allow one to study generic properties of phase transitions using non-order parameter fields without loosing the information encoded in the true order parameter. {}For the pure gauge theory we demonstrate that, near the deconfining phase transition, the center group symmetry is naturally linked to the conformal anomaly. Another relevant outcome is that when the theory contains also quarks we can explain the intertwining of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement for QCD with matter fields either in the fundamental or in the adjoint representation of the gauge group. As a test of our general approach we show our results to be applicable also at non zero baryon chemical potential. We also predict new testable substructures to be present in the phase diagram of quarks in the adjoint representation of the gauge group. Here we provide some new insights on the large $N$ limit of gauge theories by investigating the hadronic world. We propose that the world of infinite $N$ should already be well described when N=6 for QCD with two and three light flavors. Finally, we suggest possible future applications of our results for heavy ions collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the intertwining of chiral symmetry restoration and deconfinement in QCD can be explained by a trilinear interaction term between the singlet field and the order parameter, which had been neglected in previous literature; this term differentiates between fermions in the fundamental versus adjoint representation of the gauge group.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians constrained by symmetries including the center group symmetry, trace anomaly, and chiral symmetry, then integrate in heavy non-order parameter fields (glueballs and scalar mesons) alongside the Polyakov loop. They analyze the resulting potentials using mean-field theory and one-loop resummation techniques, and compare the predicted behavior of order parameters and screening masses to lattice data for pure gauge theory and QCD with quarks in fundamental and adjoint representations.

## Key numbers

* For two colors, the critical temperature is given by \(T_c = T_* + \frac{b_1}{e\alpha}\frac{\Lambda^4}{T_c^3}\), where below \(T_c\) the Polyakov loop expectation value is zero and the glueball field expectation value is \(\langle H\rangle = \Lambda^4/e\).
* For two colors near \(T_c\), the Polyakov loop scales as \(\langle \ell\rangle \propto \Delta T / T_c\) and the glueball field as \(\langle H\rangle = (\Lambda^4/e) \exp[-2 b_1 \langle \ell \rangle^2]\).
* For quarks in the adjoint representation, the critical temperature for chiral symmetry restoration is \(T_{\rm chiral} \simeq 8 T_{\rm deconf}\).
* The critical number of colors for which the large-\(N\) limit is already well described is \(N=6\) for QCD with two and three light flavors.

## Context

This work extends effective Lagrangian approaches to deconfinement that previously used either glueball theories (Agasian, Brown) or Polyakov loop models (Pisarski, Dumitru) separately, by marrying them into a unified framework. It also addresses the lattice puzzle from Karsch (2001) and Karsch (1998) regarding why fundamental-representation quarks show coincident chiral and deconfining transitions while adjoint-representation quarks show separated transitions. The results enable future applications to heavy-ion collision phenomenology and to the phase diagram of QCD at non-zero baryon chemical potential.
