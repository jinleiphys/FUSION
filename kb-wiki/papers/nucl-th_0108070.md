# Probing the effectiveness: chiral perturbation theory calculations of low-energy electromagnetic reactions on deuterium

> I summarize three recent calculations of electromagnetic reactions on deuterium in chiral perturbation theory. All of these calculations were carried out to O(Q^4), i.e. next-to-next-to-leading order. The reactions discussed here are: elastic electron-deuteron scattering, Compton scattering on deuterium, and the photoproduction of neutral pions from deuterium at threshold.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory calculations of low-energy electromagnetic reactions on deuterium carried out to O(Q^4) (next-to-next-to-leading order) can systematically describe elastic electron-deuteron scattering, Compton scattering on deuterium, and neutral pion photoproduction from deuterium at threshold, but the agreement with experimental data depends on the specific reaction and energy regime, with some results showing fortuitous agreement at lower orders and others requiring further theoretical development.

## Method

The author summarizes three calculations using chiral perturbation theory (χPT) to O(Q^4), where the small parameter Q represents nucleon or probe momenta, pion mass, or the scale of chiral symmetry breaking divided by Λ_χSB. For each reaction, the transition operator is expanded in powers of Q, and deuteron wave functions are constructed from χPT-derived NN potentials at leading order (one-pion exchange), NLO (including two-pion exchange from L_πN^(1)), and NNLO (including two-pion exchange from L_πN^(2)). The calculations incorporate one-body and two-body contributions, with nucleon structure included either via χPT predictions or by factorizing experimentally-measured single-nucleon form factors.

## Key numbers

- Proton polarizabilities from χPT at LO: α_p = 12.2 × 10^(-4) fm^3, β_p = 1.2 × 10^(-4) fm^3
- Experimental proton polarizabilities: α_p + β_p = 13.23 ± 0.86^(+0.20)_(-0.49) × 10^(-4) fm^3, α_p - β_p = 10.11 ± 1.74^(+1.22)_(-0.86) × 10^(-4) fm^3
- Single-scattering contribution to the deuteron electric dipole amplitude for γ d → π^0 d: E_d^(ss) = (0.36 ± 0.05) × 10^(-3)/m_π+
- Total O(Q^4) prediction for the deuteron electric dipole amplitude: E_d = (-1.8 ± 0.2) × 10^(-3)/m_π+
- Two-nucleon contributions to E_d at O(Q^3): -1.90 × 10^(-3)/m_π+; at O(Q^4): -0.25 × 10^(-3)/m_π+ (using AV18 wave function)
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q_d is underpredicted by about 4% at O(Q^4)
- χPT describes deuteron charge form factor G_C well below q ~ 700 MeV

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral perturbation theory, originally developed for single-nucleon systems by Bernard et al. (1995), to few-body nuclear reactions by following Weinberg's (1990, 1991, 1992) proposal to power-count the NN potential rather than the amplitude. The calculations build on prior χPT work for NN phase shifts and deuteron properties by Ordonez et al. (1996), Kaiser et al. (1997), Epelbaum et al. (1999), and Rentmeester et al. (1999). For Compton scattering, the results are similar to potential-model calculations by Wilhelm et al. (1995), Levchuk and L'vov (1998), and Karakowski and Miller (1999), but the χPT approach is the only one that does not employ the polarizability approximation for the γN amplitude. The neutral pion photoproduction prediction differs markedly from conventional models and was tested experimentally at Saskatoon (Bergstrom et al., 1998), showing χPT to be clearly superior to models. This work enables systematic, model-independent calculations of low-energy electromagnetic reactions on light nuclei and provides a framework for extracting neutron polarizabilities from deuteron Compton scattering.
