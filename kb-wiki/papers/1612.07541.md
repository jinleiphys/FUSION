# Magnetic-Field Induced Critical Endpoint

> The phase diagram of strong interaction matter is analyzed utilizing the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. Special emphasis is placed on its dependence on an external magnetic field and isospin chemical potential. Using flavor mixing induced by instanton effects the influence of isospin breaking due to the magnetic field and the isospin chemical potential is compared. It is found that at low temperatures and large quark chemical potential the magnetic field, depending on its strength, induces a new critical endpoint or a triple point.

## Key claim
Using the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with flavor mixing from instanton effects, an external magnetic field can induce either a new critical endpoint or a triple point in the QCD phase diagram at low temperatures and large quark chemical potential, depending on the field strength. This result is obtained for the case of completely decoupled quark flavors (parameter c=0) and is sensitive to the strength of the 't Hooft determinant interaction.

## Method
The two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a 't Hooft determinant term is analyzed in mean-field approximation. The effective potential is derived as a function of temperature, quark chemical potential, isospin chemical potential, and external magnetic field, with the instanton interaction strength controlled by a parameter c (c=0 for decoupled flavors, c=1/2 for the original NJL model). The chiral phase structure is mapped by minimizing the effective potential to find the up- and down-quark condensates and their corresponding constituent quark masses.

## Key numbers
- For the parameter choice m_u = m_d = 6 MeV, cutoff Λ = 590 MeV, and G_0 Λ^2 = 2.435, the model yields f_π = 92.6 MeV, m_π = 140.2 MeV, and ⟨ūu⟩ = -(241.5 MeV)^3.
- At B=0 and μ_I = 60 MeV, the separation of the first-order transitions for up and down quarks at T=0 is exactly μ_I = 60 MeV.
- For |eB| = 20 m_π^2 (with m_π^2 ≈ 10^18 Gauss) and μ_I = 0, the chiral critical temperature at vanishing quark chemical potential increases for both flavors (magnetic catalysis), more strongly for the up quark.
- The new phase C_0 (lowest Landau level filled) appears at |eB| = 10 m_π^2 and μ_I = 0; increasing the field to |eB| = 10.5 m_π^2 turns the first-order transition bordering the restored phase into a crossover above T ≈ 15 MeV, creating a new critical endpoint.
- A similar new phase for the up-quark condensate appears at |eB| ≈ 5 m_π^2.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of isospin breaking in the NJL model by Frank (2003) and Boomsma (2010) to finite temperature, comparing the effects of magnetic field and isospin chemical potential. The magnetic-field-induced critical endpoint is found to be stable against inclusion of vector interactions, as noted by Denke (2013). The results contrast with lattice QCD findings of inverse magnetic catalysis by Bali (2011) and Ilgenfritz (2013), which the author notes are not captured by this simplified model. The work enables further study of the rich phase structure at low temperatures and large quark chemical potential, particularly the interplay between multiple Landau-level phases and flavor mixing.
