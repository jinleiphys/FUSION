# Ground state angular momenta of random nuclei

> We investigate the phenomenom of emerging regular spectral features from random interactions. In particular, we address the dominance of L=0 ground states in the context of the vibron model and the interacting boson model. A mean-field analysis links different regions of the parameter space with definite geometric shapes. The results that are, partly, obtained in closed analytic form, provide a clear and transparent interpretation of the distribution of ground state angular momenta as observed before in numerical studies.

## Key claim

The dominance of L=0 ground states in random-interaction boson models arises not from angular momentum algebra or many-body dynamics, but from the occurrence of definite geometric phases (spherical and deformed shapes) that emerge from the mean-field analysis of the Hamiltonian parameter space.

## Method

The authors performed a mean-field analysis of the vibron model and the interacting boson model (IBM) with random one- and two-body interactions, where all Hamiltonian parameters were drawn independently from Gaussian distributions with zero mean. For the vibron model, the potential energy surface was expressed analytically as a function of a deformation angle alpha, allowing closed-form calculation of the probability distribution of equilibrium shapes. For the IBM, the energy surface was evaluated numerically for each Hamiltonian, and the ground state angular momentum was determined from the rotational structure of the condensate using the Thouless-Valatin formula for moments of inertia.

## Key numbers

- Spherical shape probability in vibron model: 39.6% (P1 = 0.396), obtained analytically from bivariate normal distribution
- Deformed shape probability in vibron model: 21.6% (P2 = 0.216)
- p-boson condensate probability in vibron model: 38.8% (P3 = 0.388)
- Total L=0 ground state percentage in vibron model: 70.8% for even N, 52.9% for odd N
- Spherical shape probability in IBM: 39.4%
- Deformed shape probability in IBM: 36.8%
- d-boson condensate probability in IBM: 23.8%
- Total L=0 ground state percentage in IBM: 76.6% for N=6k, 63.3% for N=6k+1 or 6k+5, 67.5% for N=6k+2 or 6k+4, 72.4% for N=6k+3

## Context

This work extends earlier numerical studies by Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998) who first observed L=0 ground state dominance in shell model calculations with random interactions, and by Bijker and Frank (2000) who found similar results in the IBM. The mean-field analysis provides a geometric interpretation that links the parameter space to spherical, deformed, and boson-condensate shapes, explaining the oscillations of L=0 and L=2 percentages with boson number N. The results suggest that the phenomenon is more general than previously thought and does not depend on the details of angular momentum coupling, though the authors note that the full implications for nuclear shell model calculations remain to be clarified.
