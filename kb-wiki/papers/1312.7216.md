# Nuclear vorticity in isoscalar E1 modes: Skyrme-RPA analysis

> Two basic concepts of nuclear vorticity, hydrodynamical (HD) and Rawenthall-Wambach (RW), are critically inspected. As a test case, we consider the interplay of irrotational and vortical motion in isoscalar electric dipole E1(T=0) modes in $^{208}$Pb, namely the toroidal and compression modes. The modes are described in a self-consistent random-phase-approximation (RPA) with the Skyrme force SLy6. They are examined in terms of strength functions, transition densities, current fields, and formfactors. It is shown that the RW conception (suggesting the upper component of the nuclear current as the vorticity indicator) is not robust. The HD vorticity is not easily applicable either because the definition of a velocity field is too involved in nuclear systems. Instead, the vorticity is better characterized by the toroidal strength which closely corresponds to HD treatment and is approximately decoupled from the continuity equation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that neither the hydrodynamical (HD) definition of vorticity (curl of velocity) nor the Rawenthall-Wambach (RW) prescription (using the upper current component \(j_+\) as the vorticity indicator) is robust for nuclear systems; instead, the toroidal strength is proposed as the most appropriate practical measure of nuclear vorticity, though it is acknowledged as "not perfect."

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Skyrme random-phase-approximation (RPA) calculations for the doubly-magic nucleus \(^{208}\)Pb using the Skyrme force SLy6, without any separable approximation and with a large configuration space including particle-hole states up to \(\sim 35\) MeV and fluid dynamical basis modes up to 200 MeV. They analyzed the isoscalar electric dipole E1(T=0) toroidal mode (TM) and compression mode (CM) by computing strength functions, transition densities, current and velocity fields, curls, divergences, and formfactors, using integral and average variables weighted by dipole probe operators to avoid sign ambiguities from individual RPA states.

## Key numbers

* The calculated toroidal mode (TM) strength is peaked at 7-8 MeV in \(^{208}\)Pb.
* The calculated compression mode (CM) strength is peaked at \(\sim 25\) MeV in \(^{208}\)Pb.
* The experimental \((\alpha,\alpha')\) data for the E1(T=0) resonance give maxima at 12.7 and 23.0 MeV, which the authors suggest may correspond to CM structures at 13-15 and 25 MeV in their calculations.
* The RW and HD strengths both show strong peaks at 7-8 MeV (the TM energy), but the RW strength has additional maxima at the GDR (10-15 MeV) and CM (25 MeV) regions.
* The RPA calculations used a 1D spherical coordinate-space grid with mesh size 0.3 fm and a calculational box of 21 fm.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of toroidal and compression modes in the E1(T=0) channel, including the authors' own earlier separable RPA (SRPA) analysis (Kvasil et al., 2011) which found that the sum of TM and CM operators gives the RW operator. The paper critically examines the RW vorticity concept proposed by Raventhall and Wambach (1987) and the HD vorticity definition from Landau and Lifshitz (1987), showing that neither is satisfactory for nuclear systems. The results enable a more reliable characterization of nuclear vorticity in future studies of low-energy E1 strength, particularly in the pygmy dipole resonance region, which has implications for understanding nuclear symmetry energy and neutron skin properties relevant to astrophysics.
