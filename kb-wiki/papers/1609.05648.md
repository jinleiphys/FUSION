# Nuclear structure calculations for two-neutrino double-beta decay

> We study the two-neutrino double-beta decay in 76Ge, 116Cd, 128Te, 130Te, and 150Nd, as well as the two Gamow-Teller branches that connect the double-beta decay partners with the states in the intermediate nuclei. We use a theoretical microscopic approach based on a deformed selfconsistent mean field with Skyrme interactions including pairing and spin-isospin residual forces, which are treated in a proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation. We compare our results for Gamow-Teller strength distributions with experimental information obtained from charge-exchange reactions. We also compare our results for the two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements with those extracted from the measured half-lives. Both single-state and low-lying-state dominance hypotheses are analyzed theoretically and experimentally making use of recent data from charge-exchange reactions and beta decay of the intermediate nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a deformed selfconsistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) approach can simultaneously reproduce experimental Gamow-Teller strength distributions and two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements for five candidate nuclei, though the agreement depends on tuning the particle-particle coupling constant and the choice of energy denominator, with the single-state dominance hypothesis not strongly supported by the calculations.

## Method

The authors performed deformed selfconsistent Hartree-Fock calculations with the SLy4 Skyrme interaction, including BCS pairing with fixed gap parameters from experimental mass differences, and then solved proton-neutron QRPA equations with separable spin-isospin residual interactions (repulsive particle-hole and attractive particle-particle channels) to obtain Gamow-Teller strength distributions and two-neutrino double-beta decay nuclear matrix elements for 76Ge, 116Cd, 128Te, 130Te, and 150Nd. The theoretical results were compared with experimental Gamow-Teller strengths from charge-exchange reactions and with nuclear matrix elements extracted from measured half-lives using both bare (gA=1.273) and quenched (gA=1) axial-vector coupling constants.

## Context

This work extends previous deformed QRPA calculations by Sarriguren et al. (2012, 2013, 2015) and Alvarez et al. (2004) to a systematic study of five double-beta decay candidates, comparing with recent high-resolution charge-exchange reaction data from Thies et al. (2012), Grewe et al. (2008), Puppe et al. (2012), Sasano et al. (2012), and Guess et al. (2011). The results enable testing the single-state dominance hypothesis (Abad et al. 1984) and the low-lying-state dominance hypothesis against experimental data, and provide a benchmark for deformed QRPA calculations that can be applied to neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements for planned experiments such as SNO+, SuperNEMO, and DCBA.
