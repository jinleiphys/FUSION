# Towards a consistent description of in-medium parton branching

> Ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are a window of opportunity to study QCD matter under extreme conditions of temperature and density, such as the quark-gluon plasma. Among the several possibilities, the study of jet quenching - generic name given to in-medium energy loss modifications of the parton branching - is a powerful tool to assess the properties of this new state of matter. The description of the parton shower is very well understood in vacuum (controlled reference) and medium-induced modifications of this process can be experimentally accessed through jet measurements. Current experimental data, however, cannot be entirely described only with energy loss phenomena. Transverse momentum broadening and decoherence effects, both theoretically established by now, and their interplay are essential to build a consistent picture of the medium-modifications of the parton branching and to achieve a correct description of the current experimental data. In this write-up, we will present the latest developments that address such unified description.

## Key claim

Current jet quenching Monte Carlos based on energy loss alone fail to describe experimental jet fragmentation functions, where the jet core remains unmodified while outer layers are strongly affected; a unified description requires the interplay of transverse momentum broadening and decoherence effects, which the authors show for the first time can be combined within a path-integral approach for a finite medium.

## Method

The authors employ a path-integral approach to compute the in-medium gluon bremsstrahlung spectrum, treating the medium as a collection of static scattering centers with frozen color fields and assuming multiple soft scatterings. They factorize the double differential radiation spectrum into three regions corresponding to independent broadening of the initial particle, coherent propagation during the gluon formation time, and a decoherence-corrected independent broadening of the final particles, evaluated in the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Key numbers

- The decoherence parameter \(\Delta_{med} \approx 1 - \exp\left\{-\frac{1}{12} \hat{q} \theta_{q\bar{q}} L^3\right\}\) controls the anti-angular ordering contribution in the QCD antenna setup, where \(\hat{q}\) is the transport coefficient, \(\theta_{q\bar{q}}\) the angle between quark-antiquark legs, and \(L\) the medium length.
- The decoherence parameter \(\Delta_{coh}\) contains an additional exponentially suppressed contribution where the two particles propagate coherently as in vacuum, expressed as \(\Delta_{coh} = 1 + N_c \frac{\hat{q}}{2}\int_{x_{2+}}^{L_+} d\tau (\mathbf{r_3}(\tau) - \mathbf{\bar{r}_3}(\tau)) \cdot (\mathbf{w_3}(\tau) - \mathbf{\bar{w}_3}(\tau)) \times \exp\left\{- \frac{\hat{q}}{2} \int_{x_{2+}}^{\tau} d\xi (\mathbf{r_3} - \mathbf{w_3})\cdot (\mathbf{\bar{w}_3} - \mathbf{\bar{r}_3})\right\}\).

## Context

This work extends prior path-integral approaches to jet quenching that treated energy loss (Apolinario 2012, Ovanesyan 2011, D'Eramo 2010), momentum broadening (Blaizot 2012), and decoherence (MehtarTani 2010) separately, often in different kinematic limits. By showing the interplay between coherent and incoherent propagation regimes when transverse Brownian motion is included, it provides a unified framework that can improve jet quenching Monte Carlos (Armesto 2009, Zapp 2012) to describe intra-jet modifications observed by ATLAS (Aad 2014) and CMS (2014).
