# Lattice QCD Method To Study Hadron Mass Is Not Correct

> Since the numerical path integration in the lattice QCD involves quark and gluon fields (not hadron fields) the lattice QCD cannot calculate any hadronic observable. Because of this reason the hadronic properties are extracted in the lattice QCD method by inserting complete set of hadron states $\sum_n |n><n|=1$ in between the partonic operators by assuming $H_{\rm QCD}|n>=E_n|n>$ where $E_n$ is the energy of the hadron. However, in this paper we find $H_{\rm QCD}|n>\neq E_n|n>$ because the QCD hamiltonian $H_{QCD}$ is unphysical but the $E_n$ and $|n>$ of the hadron are physical. We show that this is consistent with $E_{\rm QCD}(t)=<n|H_{QCD}|n>\neq E_n$ due to non-zero energy flux $E_{\rm flux}(t)$ in QCD because of confinement involving non-perturbative QCD. Hence we find that the lattice QCD method to study hadron mass is not correct.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the standard lattice QCD method for extracting hadron masses is fundamentally incorrect because the QCD Hamiltonian \(H_{QCD}\) is unphysical (built from quark and gluon fields that are not directly observed) while the hadron energy eigenstates \(|n\rangle\) and eigenvalues \(E_n\) are physical, so the assumed eigenvalue equation \(H_{QCD}|n\rangle = E_n|n\rangle\) does not hold; instead, the paper asserts \(H_{QCD}|n\rangle \neq E_n|n\rangle\) due to a non-zero energy flux arising from confinement.

## Method

The author argues by examining the standard lattice QCD procedure for extracting hadron masses from two-point and three-point correlation functions, where a complete set of hadron states is inserted between partonic operators. The paper contends that the QCD Hamiltonian \(H_{QCD}\) operating on a physical hadron state \(|n\rangle\) cannot yield the physical energy eigenvalue \(E_n\) because the energy of the quark-gluon system inside the hadron, \(E_{QCD}(t) = \langle n|H_{QCD}|n\rangle\), is time-dependent due to a non-vanishing energy flux \(E_{\text{flux}}(t)\) caused by confinement, whereas the hadron energy \(E_n\) is constant. The argument is presented through formal manipulation of the correlation function equations, referencing the author's prior work (Nayak, no year given) on energy flux in QCD.

## Key numbers

* The paper states that for pion mass extraction, the standard lattice QCD result is \(\sum_r \langle\Omega|\hat{\mathcal{O}}(r,t)\hat{\mathcal{O}}(0)|\Omega\rangle|_{t\rightarrow \infty} = \langle\Omega|\hat{\mathcal{O}}|\pi\rangle\langle\pi|\hat{\mathcal{O}}|\Omega\rangle e^{-tM_\pi}\), with \(\langle\Omega|\hat{\mathcal{O}}|\pi\rangle = f_\pi M_\pi\) where \(f_\pi\) is the pion decay constant; the paper claims this result is incorrect.
* No other numerical results are presented; the paper is a formal critique without new computed numbers.

## Context

This paper challenges the foundational assumption of lattice QCD hadron spectroscopy, specifically the use of the eigenvalue equation \(H_{QCD}|n\rangle = E_n|n\rangle\) which underlies all lattice extractions of hadron masses and matrix elements (as summarized in the Particle Data Group review). The argument extends the author's prior work (Nayak, no year given) on non-conservation of QCD energy due to confinement. If correct, this would invalidate decades of lattice QCD results for hadron masses, form factors, and structure functions, and would require a fundamentally new non-perturbative method to connect QCD to hadronic observables.
