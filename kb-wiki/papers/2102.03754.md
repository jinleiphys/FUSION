# Relativistic corrections to decays of heavy baryons in the quark model

> We investigate relativistic corrections of order $1/m^{2}$, where $m$ is the constituent quark mass, to heavy baryon decays by emitting one pseudoscalar meson in the quark model. This work is motivated by shortcomings in the previous studies in the nonrelativistic quark model for decays of the Roper-like states such as $Λ_c(2765)$. We find that the relativistic corrections due to the internal motion of quarks are essential ingredients in improving their decay properties such that the decay widths are significantly increased. In addition, such corrections can explain a phenomenological suppression of the quark axial-vector coupling constant $g_A^q$ for the $Σ_c(2455)$ and $Σ_c(2520)$ decays.

## Key claim

Relativistic corrections of order \(1/m^2\) (where \(m\) is the constituent quark mass), computed via the Foldy-Wouthuysen-Tani transformation, significantly increase the predicted decay widths of Roper-like heavy baryons such as \(\Lambda_c(2765)\) and \(\Lambda_b(6072)\), bringing them into better agreement with experiment, and naturally explain the phenomenological suppression of the quark axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A^q\) for \(\Sigma_c(2455)\) and \(\Sigma_c(2520)\) decays without introducing an ad hoc suppression factor.

## Method

The authors used the constituent quark model with harmonic oscillator wave functions and an axial-vector coupling between pseudoscalar mesons (pion or kaon) and light quarks. They applied the Foldy-Wouthuysen-Tani transformation to the interaction Lagrangian to derive relativistic corrections up to order \(1/m^2\), which include a term proportional to the square of the internal quark momentum \(\boldsymbol{p}_i^2\). Decay widths were computed for charmed and bottom baryons in various multiplets (ground states, negative parity states, and Roper-like states) and compared to nonrelativistic results and experimental data.

## Key numbers

* \(\Sigma_c(2455)^{++} \to \Lambda_c\pi\): nonrelativistic width 4.27–4.34 MeV; with relativistic corrections 0.36–1.95 MeV; experimental width \(1.84 \pm 0.04\) MeV (Belle).
* \(\Sigma_c(2520)^{++} \to \Lambda_c\pi\): nonrelativistic width 29.8–31.4 MeV; with relativistic corrections 2.70–14.1 MeV; experimental width \(14.77 \pm 0.25\) MeV (Belle).
* \(\Lambda_c(2765)^+ \to \Sigma_c(2455)\pi + \Sigma_c(2520)\pi\): nonrelativistic sum 1.38–4.70 MeV; with relativistic corrections 10.8–49.0 MeV; experimental width \(73 \pm 5\) MeV (Belle).
* \(\Lambda_b(6072)^0 \to \Sigma_b\pi + \Sigma_b^{*}\pi\): nonrelativistic sum 1.80–5.17 MeV; with relativistic corrections 12.8–52.3 MeV; experimental width \(72 \pm 11\) MeV (LHCb).
* \(\Xi_c(2970)^+\) (Roper-like) sum of decay channels: nonrelativistic 0.55–2.17 MeV; with relativistic corrections 4.07–18.9 MeV; experimental width \(28.1 \pm 2.4\) MeV (Belle).
* \(\Sigma_b(5810)^{+} \to \Lambda_b\pi\): nonrelativistic 11.9–12.3 MeV; with relativistic corrections 0.62–5.11 MeV; experimental width \(4.83 \pm 0.31\) MeV (LHCb).
* \(\Sigma_b(5830)^{+} \to \Lambda_b\pi\): nonrelativistic 20.4–21.4 MeV; with relativistic corrections 1.08–8.80 MeV; experimental width \(9.34 \pm 0.47\) MeV (LHCb).
* Constituent quark masses used: \(m_{u(d)} = 0.35 \pm 0.05\) GeV, \(M_c = 1.5 \pm 0.1\) GeV, \(M_b = 5.0 \pm 0.1\) GeV; spring constant \(k = 0.03 \pm 0.01\) GeV\(^3\); pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV; kaon decay constant \(f_K = 111\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the nonrelativistic quark model studies of heavy baryon decays by Nagahiro et al. (2016) and Arifi et al. (2020), which predicted decay widths for Roper-like states (e.g., \(\Lambda_c(2765)\)) that were too narrow by an order of magnitude. The relativistic corrections resolve this long-standing discrepancy without invoking nonresonant processes or \(f_0(500)\) contributions, which were shown to be insignificant in prior work by Arifi et al. (2020). The method follows the Foldy-Wouthuysen-Tani transformation used by Kubota and Ohta (1976) for nucleon photoexcitation amplitudes. The results enable consistent use of \(g_A^q = 1\) (supporting Weinberg's mended symmetry argument from 1990) and predict that the \(\rho\)-mode Roper-like states (with masses \(\sim 850\) MeV above ground state) are too broad to be observed, explaining their absence in experiment.
