# Structure of residual interaction in spherical nuclei

> The effect of residual interaction between nucleons (quasiparticles) on shell oscillations of the masses of spherical nuclei is considered. The singularity of the ground state energy of the system in the vicinity of nucleon magic numbers is analyzed for various types of the dependence of residual interaction on orbital momentum of the quasiparticle. It is shown that only the perturbation band width of the Fermi distribution due to residual interaction which is proportional to the square of the angular momentum vector is consistent with the character of the magic cusps. The coupling constants between the quasiparticles are determined on the basis of the available data. The constant decreases rapidly with increase of nuclear radius. Possible consequences pertaining to the energy spectrum of infinite nuclear matter are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that only a residual interaction whose smearing of the Fermi distribution has a width proportional to the square of the orbital angular momentum (i.e., \(k=2\) in their power-law model) is consistent with the experimentally observed character of magic cusps in nuclear masses; other dependencies, such as a temperature-type smearing independent of angular momentum, would either eliminate the singularities entirely or produce singularities stronger than the observed finite jumps in the first derivative.

## Method

The authors analyze the oscillating part of the ground-state energy of spherical nuclei by treating the quasiparticle distribution function \(w(\rho,\tilde{l})\) as a primary concept, rather than specifying an explicit interaction Hamiltonian. They first study a non-analytic model derived from a pairing-type Hamiltonian (Racah-Mottelson formula) that yields a linear interpolation of occupation numbers between zero and unity, with a smearing width proportional to \(\tilde{\beta}^{k-1}\) (where \(\tilde{\beta} \approx \tilde{l}/\rho_f\)). By evaluating the Fourier integral for the oscillating energy component and examining the convergence of the resulting trigonometric series, they determine that only \(k=2\) produces the correct finite-jump singularity. They then confirm this result with an analytic distribution function of Fermi-Dirac form whose width depends quadratically on \(\tilde{\beta}\), and extract coupling constants from experimental mass data for 52 magic nuclei.

## Key numbers

* For the analytic distribution function (33), the magic jump in nucleon binding energy is \(\Delta\eps = \bar{\eps} (d\rho_f^2/dN) F_1(g)\), where \(F_1(g)\) has a maximum of \(F_{1,\text{max}} = 1.20\) at \(g = 0.55\), giving a minimum \(\omega_{\text{min}} = -0.17\).
* For the non-analytic model (17) with \(k=2\), the coupling constant functions are \(f_1(g) = (1/(2g))\ln|(1+g)/(1-g)|\) and \(f_2(g) = \pi/(2g)\theta(g-1)\).
* The characteristic smearing width in the energy scale is \(\overline{\delta\eps} = (\hbar^2/R^2)(\rho_f/m^*)(g/8)\), with \(R = 1.2 \times 10^{-13} A^{1/3}\) cm; for neutron magic nuclei at \(N=28\), \(\overline{\delta\eps} = 5.2\) MeV, and at \(N=50\), \(\overline{\delta\eps} = 3.8\) MeV (proton case).
* For the neutron magic nucleus at \(N=28\), \(\sqrt{\omega} = 2.4\), \(g' = 2.7\), and \(g = 3.3\); for \(N=50\), \(\sqrt{\omega} = 1.8\), \(g' = 2.2\), and \(g = 2.6\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous macroscopic theory of shell oscillations (Kamchatnov and Nosov, 1972) by incorporating residual interaction effects that smear the Fermi distribution, which was previously treated as ideally sharp. It contradicts the possibility of a temperature-type smearing (independent of orbital angular momentum) or any smearing with a power-law exponent \(k < 2\), as these would either eliminate the magic singularities or produce singularities inconsistent with experiment. The results enable a systematic determination of residual interaction coupling constants from experimental mass data and show that the interaction strength decreases rapidly with increasing nuclear radius, with implications for the energy spectrum of infinite nuclear matter.
