# Interpretation of recent JLab results on quasi elastic (e,e'p) reactions off few-nucleon systems

> Recent JLab experimental data on quasi elastic 3He(e,e'p)2H(pn) and 4He(e,e'p)3H processes are interpreted using an approach based upon realistic wave functions and Glauber multiple scattering theory within a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA). The results of a non factorized calculation of the left-right asymmetry A_{TL} of the process 3He(e,e'p)2H, obtained using the full covariant form of the electromagnetic operator, are also presented.

## Key claim

The authors interpret recent JLab quasi-elastic (e,e'p) data on few-nucleon systems using realistic wave functions and a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) for final state interactions, finding that while factorized calculations reproduce the three-body breakup and the right wing of the two-body breakup well, the non-factorized calculation of the left-right asymmetry \(A_{TL}\) agrees with data only up to missing momentum \(p_m \simeq 600\) MeV/c, with a strong disagreement at higher momenta that remains unexplained.

## Method

The authors computed differential cross sections for \(^3He(e,e'p)^2H(pn)\) and \(^4He(e,e'p)^3H\) using realistic wave functions and a generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) to treat final state interactions (FSI), with the nuclear spectral function distorted by FSI calculated from experimentally determined nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes. For the asymmetry \(A_{TL}\) in \(^3He(e,e'p)^2H\), they performed a non-factorized calculation in momentum space using the full covariant form of the electromagnetic operator, without any non-relativistic reduction.

## Key numbers

- For \(^3He(e,e'p)^2H\), the cross section exhibits different slopes corresponding to PWIA and single- and double-rescattering contributions, with experimental data from JLab (He3data).
- For \(^4He(e,e'p)^3H\), the reduced cross section \(n_D(\mathbf{p}_m)\) shows satisfactory agreement with preliminary JLab experimental data (He4data).
- The asymmetry \(A_{TL}\) for \(^3He(e,e'p)^2H\) is reproduced by the non-factorized calculation up to \(p_m \simeq 600\) MeV/c, but fails at higher momenta; the factorized calculation cannot describe the data at \(p_m \geq 200\) MeV/c.

## Context

This work extends previous GEA calculations (GEA1, GEA2) for few-nucleon systems to interpret recent JLab data on \(^3He\) and \(^4He\) quasi-elastic reactions, showing that factorized calculations work well for breakup channels but that non-factorized effects are essential for spin observables like \(A_{TL}\). The results enable further development of non-factorized approaches for \(^4He(e,e'p)^3H\) (nonfact2) and highlight the need to resolve the persistent disagreement between theory and experiment at high missing momenta, a problem common to many calculations.
