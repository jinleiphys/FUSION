# Fusion dynamics around the Coulomb barrier

> We perform exact coupled-channels calculations, taking into account properly the effects of Coulomb coupling and the finite excitation energy of collective excitations in the colliding nuclei, for three Fm formation reactions, $^{37}$Cl + $^{209}$Bi, $^{45}$Sc + $^{197}$Au, and $^{59}$Co + $^{181}$Ta. For the $^{37}$Cl + $^{209}$Bi and $^{45}$Sc + $^{197}$Au reactions, those calculations well reproduce the experimental total fission cross sections, and a part of the extra-push phenomena can be explained in terms of the Coulomb excitations to multi-phonon states. On the other hand, for the heaviest system, the deep-inelastic collisions become much more significant, and the fission cross sections are strongly overestimated. We also discuss the surprisingly large surface diffuseness parameters required to fit recent high-precision fusion data for medium-heavy systems, in connection with the fusion supression observed in massive systems.

## Key claim

Exact coupled-channels calculations that properly include Coulomb coupling and finite excitation energy can reproduce experimental fission cross sections for the two lighter Fm formation reactions, but for the heaviest system ($^{59}$Co + $^{181}$Ta) the calculations strongly overestimate fission cross sections because deep-inelastic collisions become much more significant.

## Method

The authors performed exact coupled-channels calculations using an extended version of the computer code CCFULL, incorporating the effect of finite ground state spin within the isocentrifugal approximation. For each of the three Fm formation reactions, they included specific vibrational and rotational states in both projectile and target nuclei, with deformation parameters estimated from experimental B(E2) and B(E3) values. The calculations were compared to experimental total fission cross sections obtained by selecting fission events in time-of-flight versus energy-loss plots.

## Key numbers

- Charge products $Z_PZ_T$ for the three systems: 1411 ($^{37}$Cl + $^{209}$Bi), 1659 ($^{45}$Sc + $^{197}$Au), and 1971 ($^{59}$Co + $^{181}$Ta)
- For the $^{16}$O + $^{208}$Pb fusion reaction, a Woods-Saxon potential with surface diffuseness $a = 0.65$ fm significantly overestimates fusion cross sections at energies well above the barrier, while a potential with $a = 1.16$ fm fits the data well
- The suppression factor $S$ (ratio of experimental fusion cross sections to potential model predictions with $a \approx 0.63$ fm) is shown for systems with $Z_1Z_2$ ranging from about 400 to 2000, with two points at $Z_1Z_2 = 1400$ from $^{58}$Ni + $^{112,124}$Sn reactions
- Excitation energies for states included in the $^{37}$Cl + $^{209}$Bi calculation: 5/2$^+$ (3.09 MeV), 7/2$^-$ (3.1 MeV), and 9/2$^-$ (4.01 MeV) in $^{37}$Cl
- Rotational parameters for $^{197}$Au: $\beta_2 = -0.13$ and $\beta_4 = -0.03$; for $^{181}$Ta: $\beta_2 = 0.354$ and $\beta_4 = -0.05$

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channels method, previously successful for medium-heavy systems as reviewed by Dasgupta et al. (1998) and Hagino et al. (1999), to massive systems approaching the superheavy element region. The paper shows that Coulomb excitation provides an important mechanism for fusion inhibition in transitional systems, complementing earlier work by Reisdorf (1994) on deep-inelastic collisions and by Zagrebaev et al. (2002) on angular momentum truncation. The results enable future quantum mechanical treatments of the competition between fusion and deep-inelastic collisions, which remains an open problem, and suggest that the anomalously large surface diffuseness parameters (0.8-1.4 fm) required to fit recent high-precision fusion data for medium-heavy systems, as reported by Newton et al. (2001) and Hinde et al. (2001), may be partially explained by competition between fusion and deep-inelastic processes.
