# Relation between the critical spin and angular velocity of a nucleus immediately after backbending

> In nonspherical nuclei at $J = J_c + 0$ the relationship between the angular momentum and angular velocity immediately after backbending is the same as in the limiting case $J - J_c\to\infty$. This indicates that there is a unique type of cancellation of the deviations from a rigid-body moment of inertia in the upper phase $J>J_c$. An integral relationship is found which expresses this cancellation quantitatively. This formula permits $J_c$ to be calculated for the rotational bands of the even-even nuclei studied and the results are in agreement with those obtained by other methods of locating the Curie point. For the ground state band of W$^{170}$ the cancellation of the reciprocals of the true and rigid-body moments of inertia can be verified directly. The condition for the stability of the rotation of a nonspherical nucleus is analyzed in the Appendix in close connection with the problem of a reasonable definition of the concept of a variable moment of inertia.

## Key claim

The relationship between angular momentum and angular velocity immediately after backbending (at \(J = J_c + 0\)) is the same as in the asymptotic limit \(J - J_c \to \infty\), indicating a cancellation of deviations from rigid-body moment of inertia in the upper phase; this cancellation is expressed quantitatively by an integral relation that permits calculation of the critical spin \(J_c\) from observed angular velocities.

## Method

The authors derive an integral relation \(\int_{J_c}^\infty (1/I_0 - 1/I) dJ = 0\) (or equivalently \(\int_{J_c}^\infty (1 - I_0/I) dJ = 0\)) by integrating the inverse moment of inertia from the critical spin to infinity and comparing with the rigid-body value. They then use the relation \(\hbar J_c = I_0 \Omega_{nc}\) (where \(\Omega_{nc}\) is the angular velocity immediately after the backbending discontinuity) to compute theoretical critical spins for 28 rotational bands of even-even nuclei, using a rigid-body moment of inertia formula \(\hbar^2/I_0 = 85900 / A^{5/3}\) keV with nuclear radius \(r_0 = 1.1 \times 10^{-13}\) cm. The results are compared with experimental critical spins and extrapolated values from the square-root law of the lower phase.

## Key numbers

- For W\(^{170}\), the rotational velocity \(\hbar\Omega_{nc} = 216\) keV; for the ground state band of Ba\(^{126}\), \(\hbar\Omega_{nc} = 340\) keV.
- The rigid-body moment of inertia parameter \(\hbar^2/I_0 = 85900 / A^{5/3}\) keV, using \(r_0 = 1.1 \times 10^{-13}\) cm.
- For W\(^{170}\), the ratio \(j/I_0 = 1.88\); for Dy\(^{158}\), \(j/I_0 = 6.76\).
- For W\(^{170}\), the moment of inertia approaches the rigid-body value with an accuracy of about 4% after passing through the minimum.
- For Er\(^{156}\), after the first phase transition the moment of inertia reaches \(I = 1.38 I_0\) at \(J = 22\).
- Theoretical critical spins \(J_c^{theor}\) range from \(\leq 9.5\) (Ce\(^{134}\)) to \(\leq 18.6\) (Yb\(^{170}\), Hf\(^{170}\)) across the 28 bands studied.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier theory (Nosov and Kamchatnov, 1979) that treats backbending as a macroscopic quantum phenomenon with a square-root law for the static quadrupole moment near the Curie point. The integral relation provides a new method for locating the critical spin \(J_c\) that agrees with experimental values and extrapolation methods from the lower phase. The results enable future verification of the "area theorem" for nuclei with larger \(j/I_0\) ratios (such as Dy\(^{158}\), where measurements up to \(J \sim 40\) are suggested) and may help clarify the nature of second backbending regions observed in nuclei near magic numbers like Er\(^{156}\) and Er\(^{158}\).
