# Spin projection in the shell model Monte Carlo method and the spin distribution of nuclear level densities

> We introduce spin projection methods in the shell model Monte Carlo approach and apply them to calculate the spin distribution of level densities for iron-region nuclei using the complete $(pf+g_{9/2})$-shell. We compare the calculated distributions with the spin-cutoff model and extract an energy-dependent moment of inertia. For even-even nuclei and at low excitation energies, we observe a significant suppression of the moment of inertia and odd-even staggering in the spin dependence of level densities.

## Key claim

The paper introduces spin projection methods within the shell model Monte Carlo (SMMC) approach to calculate the spin distribution of nuclear level densities, and finds that for even-even nuclei at low excitation energies the moment of inertia is significantly suppressed and exhibits odd-even staggering, which the abstract describes as observed but does not qualify as being limited to the even-even case and to energies below approximately 8 MeV.

## Method

The authors implement a projection onto the spin component \(J_z = M\) using a discrete Fourier transform of the trace of the imaginary-time propagator rotated by \(e^{i\varphi_k \hat J_z}\), and then obtain the fixed-total-spin \(J\) partition function as the difference between \(M=J\) and \(M=J+1\) projected partitions. They apply this method within the SMMC framework to compute spin-projected thermal energies and level densities for iron-region nuclei in the complete \((pf+g_{9/2})\)-shell, using the Hamiltonian of Ref. [na97]. The spin-cutoff parameter \(\sigma^2\) is extracted by fitting the SMMC spin distributions to the spin-cutoff model, and the moment of inertia \(I\) is derived from \(\sigma^2 = I T / \hbar^2\).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{56}\)Fe, \(^{55}\)Fe, and \(^{60}\)Co, SMMC spin distributions \(Z_J/Z\) are calculated for inverse temperatures \(\beta\) in the range from 0 to \(\sim 2\) MeV\(^{-1}\).
- For \(^{56}\)Fe, odd-even spin staggering in \(Z_J/Z\) is observed for temperatures \(T \lesssim 1.5\) MeV.
- The spin-cutoff parameter \(\sigma^2\) is extracted from fits to \(\rho_J/\rho\) at fixed excitation energies; for \(^{56}\)Fe, deviations from the spin-cutoff model are seen below \(E_x \sim 8\) MeV.
- The moment of inertia \(I/\hbar^2\) is compared to the rigid-body value \(0.0137 A^{5/3}\) MeV\(^{-1}\) (with \(A=56\) for iron-region nuclei); for \(^{56}\)Fe at low excitations, \(I\) is close to half the rigid-body value.
- The \(J=0\) level density for \(^{56}\)Fe is enhanced for \(E_x \lesssim 8\) MeV relative to the rigid-body spin-cutoff prediction.

## Context

This work extends the SMMC approach for total level densities (Alhassid 1999, Nakada 1997) to include spin resolution, enabling microscopic calculation of spin distributions that were previously only modeled by the spin-cutoff model (Ericson 1960). The extracted energy-dependent moment of inertia reveals signatures of the pairing phase transition, previously observed in the heat capacity (Liu 2001, Alhassid 2003), and the odd-even staggering is explained by a simple pairing model with number parity projection (Alhassid 2005). The method enables calculation of spin-dependent level densities for mid-mass nuclei where conventional diagonalization is intractable, and can be applied to improve statistical reaction rate calculations for astrophysics.
