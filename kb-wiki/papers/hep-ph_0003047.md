# Phi-measure of pions in high energy heavy-ion collisions

> Event-by-Event fluctuations for high energy heavy-ion collisions are investigated. The $Φ$-measures are calculated for pions. It is found that multiparticle symmetrization correlation has great influence on the value of $Φ$ if the phase space density is high. Previous derivation of $Φ$-measure has neglected multiparticle quantum correlation. So the explanation of $Φ$-measure as a measure of dynamical correlation is reasonable only when multiparticles quantum correlation effects are smaller. We argue that the $Φ$-measure might be applicable at the SPS energy where the pion phase space density is much less than one.

# Phi-measure of pions in high energy heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The Phi-measure, previously interpreted as a probe of dynamical correlations in heavy-ion collisions, is significantly influenced by multiparticle Bose-Einstein symmetrization correlations when the pion phase space density is high; the measure can only be reliably interpreted as a dynamical signal when these quantum correlations are small, which the author argues is the case at SPS energies where the pion phase space density is much less than one.

## Method

The author derives the relationship between the Phi-measure and particle inclusive distributions using N-body distribution functions, then calculates Phi_2(p_t) for pions by modeling the pion source as a Gaussian in both coordinate and momentum space with source radius R(N) and temperature parameter Delta(N). The calculation incorporates multiparticle Bose-Einstein symmetrization effects through a permutation-sum formulation of the N-pion distribution function, using a Poisson multiplicity distribution and evaluating the single- and two-particle inclusive distributions to compute the Phi-measure.

## Key numbers

- For a source with R = 3 fm and Delta = 0.2 GeV, the calculated Phi_2(p_t) for mean multiplicity <N> ~ 100 is much larger than the experimental value of 4.6 +/- 1.5 MeV from NA49 for Pb+Pb at SPS.
- For a larger volume (R = 5.3 fm, Delta = 0.2 GeV) or higher temperature (R = 3 fm, Delta = 0.355 GeV), Phi ~ 7 MeV for <N> ~ 100, consistent with the two-particle correlation result of 5 +/- 1.5 MeV for Pb+Pb at SPS.
- Using the method of Mrowczynski (1999) with volume V = L^3 = (16)^3 fm^3 and temperature T = 150 MeV, the Phi value is around 6 MeV, consistent with the author's results and data.
- The pion phase space density at SPS energy is reported as 0.2, based on calculations by Ferenc (1999) and others.

## Context

This work extends the derivation of the Phi-measure by Belkacem et al. (1999) and Bialas and Koch (1999) by explicitly including multiparticle Bose-Einstein symmetrization effects that were neglected in those earlier formulations. It directly addresses the claim by Gazdzicki (1995) that the Phi-measure for AA collisions should equal that for pp collisions in the absence of new dynamics, showing that quantum correlations can produce differences. The calculation improves upon Mrowczynski (1999), who assumed a Bose-Einstein spectrum form corresponding to infinite source volume, by using a finite-size Gaussian source that allows study of finite-volume effects. The results suggest that at SPS energies, where the pion phase space density is low (0.2), multiparticle symmetrization effects are not significant, but at RHIC energies with higher phase space density, the Phi-measure interpretation requires caution.
