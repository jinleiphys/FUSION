# K^+ production in baryon-baryon and heavy-ion collisions

> Kaon production cross sections in nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-delta and delta-delta interactions are studied in a boson exchange model. For the latter two interactions, the exchanged pion can be on-mass shell, only contributions due to a virtual pion are included via the Peierls method by taking into account the finite delta width. With these cross sections and also those for pion-baryon interactions, subthreshold kaon production from heavy ion collisions is studied in the relativistic transport model.

# K⁺ production in baryon-baryon and heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The authors evaluate kaon production cross sections in nucleon-delta and delta-delta interactions using the Peierls method to regularize the on-shell pion singularity by including the finite delta width, and find that the latest KaoS data on the K⁺/π⁺ ratio in Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV are consistent with a weak repulsive kaon potential, but only if the scalar and vector potentials do not fully cancel.

## Method

Kaon production cross sections in nucleon-nucleon, nucleon-delta, and delta-delta interactions were calculated using a boson exchange model with pion and kaon exchange. For nucleon-delta and delta-delta interactions, the exchanged pion can become on-mass shell, so the Peierls method was applied by replacing the delta mass with a complex mass using its finite width, keeping only the real part of the modified pion propagator to obtain the off-shell contribution. These elementary cross sections, together with parameterized pion-baryon cross sections, were then used in a relativistic transport model to simulate subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key numbers

- The kaon production cross sections for nucleon-delta and delta-delta channels were parameterized as σ = a(√s − √s₀)² / [b + (√s − √s₀)ˣ] mb, with fitted parameters a, b, and x listed for six channels.
- The vector repulsion parameter b_K = 3/(8f_π²) ≈ 0.333 GeV fm³.
- The scalar attraction parameter a_K was treated as free; taking a_K ≈ 0.22 GeV² fm³ gives a kaon repulsive potential of about 20 MeV at normal nuclear matter density.
- For central Au+Au collisions at 1 AGeV, the kaon yield from pion-baryon collisions accounts for more than 60% of the total kaon yield.
- The π⁺/A_part ratio stays almost constant at 0.03 across the entire impact parameter range.
- The K⁺ yield normalized by A_part increases more than a factor of two from peripheral to central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous boson-exchange study of phi meson production in baryon-baryon collisions (Chung et al., 1997) to kaon production, and improves upon earlier parameterizations by Randrup and Ko (1980) and Schürmann and Zwermann (1987), which were shown to overestimate near-threshold cross sections. The results enable more reliable extraction of kaon in-medium properties from subthreshold heavy-ion collision data, particularly the K⁺/π⁺ ratio measured by the KaoS Collaboration (1994, 1996), and complement the transport model calculations of Cassing et al. (1997).
