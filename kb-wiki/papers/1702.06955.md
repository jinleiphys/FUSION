# Fermions in worldline holography

> We analyse the worldline holographic framework for fermions. Worldline holography is based on the observation that in the worldline approach to quantum field theory, sources of a quantum field theory over Mink$_4$ naturally form a field theory over AdS$_5$ to all orders in the elementary fields and in the sources. Schwinger's proper time of the worldline formalism automatically appears with the physical four spacetime dimensions in an AdS$_5$ geometry. The worldline holographic effective action in general and the proper-time profiles of the sources in particular solve a renormalisation group equation. By taking into account sources up to spin one, we reconstruct seminal holographic models. Considering spin two confirms AdS$_5$ as consistent background.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the worldline holographic framework, previously developed for scalar fields, extends consistently to fermionic elementary matter without overturning the formalism, and that considering sources up to spin one allows reconstruction of seminal holographic models while spin-two sources confirm AdS5 as a consistent background.

## Method

The authors start from the generating functional for fermionic quantum field theory and use the worldline formalism to express the fermion determinant as a proper-time integral, which naturally takes the form of an action over AdS5 with Schwinger's proper time as the fifth dimension. They then impose regulator independence as a Wilson-Polchinsky renormalisation condition, which requires completing the four-dimensional field theory to a five-dimensional one with full AdS5 isometries, and evaluate the resulting action on its saddle point with boundary conditions that identify the four-dimensional sources as the UV values of five-dimensional fields.

## Key numbers

- The squared AdS curvature radius is computed as natural = (5-1)(5-2)/12 = 1 from the leading terms of the effective action (negative cosmological constant and Einstein-Hilbert term).
- For the scalar field mass term in the five-dimensional action, the coefficient # = 3/2 is required to obtain power-law solutions T^alpha where alpha = 1 +/- 1/2, matching the holographic dictionary prediction for the fifth-dimensional scalar mass.
- The confining Gaussian model yields a warp factor exp(-cT + O(ln T)) after carrying out the path integral, where the product over n=1 to infinity of [1 + c^2 T^2/(4(2pi)^2 n^2)]^{-4} evaluates to [sinh(cT/4)/(cT/4)]^{-4}.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous worldline holography framework (Dietrich 2013, 2014, 2015, 2016) from scalar elementary matter to fermions, which constitute the matter content of the Standard Model. The resulting five-dimensional action for (pseudo)scalar and (axial)vector sources closely resembles the soft-wall AdS/QCD model (Karch 2006) and reproduces the holographic dictionary (Gubser 1998, Witten 1998) for the scalar mass. The self-consistency check showing AdS5 as a saddle-point solution of the Einstein equations derived from the effective action confirms the geometric background assumed in bottom-up holographic approaches (Erlich 2005, Karch 2006).
