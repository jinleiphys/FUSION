# Microscopic Calculation of Fusion: Light to Heavy Systems

> The density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) theory is a fully microscopic approach for calculating heavy-ion interaction potentials and fusion cross sections below and above the fusion barrier. We discuss recent applications of DC-TDHF method to fusion of light and heavy neutron-rich systems.

## Key claim

The density-constrained time-dependent Hartree-Fock (DC-TDHF) method provides a fully microscopic approach for calculating heavy-ion interaction potentials and fusion cross sections, but the authors note that at higher energies (2-3 times the barrier height) the TDHF results yield higher fusion cross sections than experimental data because many breakup channels are not naturally available in TDHF, and a significant portion of the collective kinetic energy is not equilibrated.

## Method

The DC-TDHF method extracts ion-ion interaction potentials directly from the TDHF time-evolution by using the instantaneous TDHF density to perform a static Hartree-Fock minimization while constraining neutron and proton densities to match the instantaneous TDHF densities. The resulting density-constrained energy, minus the binding energies of the two separate nuclei, defines the ion-ion potential V(R) as a function of separation distance R(t). An effective mass M(R) is then computed from energy conservation using the collective velocity obtained from the TDHF evolution, and fusion cross sections are calculated from these potentials.

## Key numbers

- For the $^{12}$C+$^{16}$O system, the DC-TDHF potential barrier has a peak energy of 7.77 MeV.
- For the $^{12}$C+$^{24}$O system, the barrier occurs at a slightly larger R value with a barrier peak of 6.64 MeV.
- At E_c.m. = 110 MeV, the experimental cross section for $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca is approximately 6 mb, compared to 0.8 mb for the more neutron-rich system $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca.
- The DC-TDHF potentials for $^{132}$Sn+$^{40}$Ca and $^{132}$Sn+$^{48}$Ca were calculated at the same center-of-mass energy E_TDHF = 120 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DC-TDHF method previously developed by Umar and Oberacker (2006) and applies it to a wide range of systems from light O+O and C+O reactions relevant to neutron star crust composition and heating, to heavy neutron-rich systems like $^{132}$Sn+$^{40,48}$Ca. The method addresses longstanding difficulties in reproducing sub-barrier fusion cross sections, such as for the $^{40}$Ca+$^{48}$Ca system which historically was not well reproduced by standard models, and enables predictions for neutron-rich systems relevant to superheavy element formation and nuclei far from stability.
