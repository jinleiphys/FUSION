# Nonequilibrium relaxation in neutral BCS superconductors: Ginzburg-Landau approach with Landau damping in real time

> We present a field-theoretical method to obtain consistently the equations of motion for small amplitude fluctuations of the order parameter directly in real time for a homogeneous, neutral BCS superconductor. This method allows to study the nonequilibrium relaxation of the order parameter as an initial value problem. We obtain the Ward identities and the effective actions for small phase the amplitude fluctuations to one-loop order. Focusing on the long-wavelength, low-frequency limit near the critical point, we obtain the time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau effective action to one-loop order, which is nonlocal as a consequence of Landau damping. The nonequilibrium relaxation of the phase and amplitude fluctuations is studied directly in real time. The long-wavelength phase fluctuation (Bogoliubov-Anderson-Goldstone mode) is overdamped by Landau damping and the relaxation time scale diverges at the critical point, revealing critical slowing down.

## Key claim

The authors derive a time-dependent Ginzburg-Landau effective action for neutral BCS superconductors near the critical temperature that is inherently nonlocal due to Landau damping, and they show that long-wavelength phase fluctuations (Bogoliubov-Anderson-Goldstone mode) are overdamped rather than propagating, with the relaxation time diverging at the critical point, contradicting the quasiparticle picture that holds well below $T_c$.

## Method

The authors implement the Schwinger-Keldysh nonequilibrium field theory combined with a "tadpole method" to obtain retarded equations of motion for small amplitude fluctuations of the order parameter directly in real time, formulated as an initial value problem. They compute the one-loop retarded self-energies and effective action for phase and amplitude fluctuations in the Nambu-Gor'kov formalism, then focus on the Ginzburg-Landau regime ($|\Delta_0(T)| \ll T$) near the critical temperature to study the long-wavelength, low-frequency dynamics. The real-time relaxation is analyzed by preparing an initial perturbation via an external source that is switched off at $t=0$, then evolving the system in the absence of sources.

## Key numbers

- The Ginzburg-Landau regime of validity is defined by $|\Delta_0(T)| \ll T_c$, where $\Delta_0(T)$ is the finite-temperature gap (order parameter).
- The study focuses on temperatures near $T_c$, in contrast to prior work by Aitchison et al. (1995, 1997) which considered the range $0 < T < 0.6\,T_c$.
- The relaxation time scale for long-wavelength phase fluctuations diverges at the critical point, exhibiting critical slowing down.
- Far from the Ginzburg-Landau regime at low temperatures, the spectral densities for both phase and amplitude fluctuations feature narrow quasiparticle peaks, and long-wavelength phase fluctuations are weakly underdamped by Landau damping.

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical treatment of dynamical fluctuations in neutral BCS superconductors by focusing on the critical region near $T_c$, whereas prior studies by Aitchison et al. (1995, 1997) examined the regime $0<T<0.6\,T_c$ where quasiparticle poles approximate the dynamics. The results contradict the quasiparticle description near $T_c$ and instead reveal overdamped relaxation governed by Landau damping, which is relevant for understanding neutral Fermi superfluids in ultracold alkali atoms (e.g., ${}^{40}\mathrm{K}$ and ${}^6\mathrm{Li}$) and for neutron superfluidity in nuclear matter and neutron stars. The method enables future real-time studies of nonequilibrium relaxation in charged superconductors and the Carlson-Goldman mode near $T_c$.
