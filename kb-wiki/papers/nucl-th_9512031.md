# An Effective Lagrangian Approach to Nuclei

> The construction of a general effective lagrangian consistent with the symmetries of QCD and intended for applications to finite-density systems is discussed. The low-energy structure of the composite nucleon is described within the theory from vector-meson dominance. Results are given for finite nuclei and nuclear matter at one-loop order. All of the coupling constants, appropriately defined according to naive dimensonal analysis, are found to be natural.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a general effective Lagrangian, consistent with QCD symmetries and incorporating vector-meson dominance to describe the composite nucleon, can successfully describe finite nuclei and nuclear matter at one-loop order, with all coupling constants found to be natural according to naive dimensional analysis (NDA). The hedge is that the one-loop calculation is only a first investigation, and a systematic calculation to quartic order would require at least two-loop contributions.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral effective Lagrangian for hadrons, including pions, nucleons, rho and omega mesons, and a light scalar field, using CCWZ's nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry and general gauge invariance. They apply naive dimensional analysis (NDA) to truncate the Lagrangian and assume naturalness (dimensionless coefficients of order unity), then perform a one-loop calculation to fit parameters to binding energies per nucleon, RMS charge radii, spin-orbit splittings, and diffraction minimum sharp radii in O-16, Sr-88, and Pb-208, and predict nuclear matter saturation properties.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy per nucleon for O-16: 7.98 MeV (experiment: 7.98 MeV)
- Binding energy per nucleon for Pb-208: 7.89 MeV (experiment: 7.87 MeV)
- RMS charge radius for O-16: 2.70 fm (experiment: 2.74 fm)
- RMS charge radius for Pb-208: 5.49 fm (experiment: 5.50 fm)
- Diffraction minimum sharp radius for O-16: 2.78 fm (experiment: 2.76 fm)
- Diffraction minimum sharp radius for Pb-208: 6.79 fm (experiment: 6.78 fm)
- Neutron spin-orbit splitting for O-16: 5.8 MeV (experiment: 6.2 MeV)
- Proton spin-orbit splitting for O-16: 5.8 MeV (experiment: 6.3 MeV)
- Neutron spin-orbit splitting for Pb-208: 0.77 MeV (experiment: 0.90 MeV)
- Proton spin-orbit splitting for Pb-208: 1.58 MeV (experiment: 1.33 MeV)
- Predicted nuclear matter saturation binding energy per nucleon: 16.1 MeV at Fermi momentum 1.31 fm^-1
- Predicted nuclear matter compressibility: 214 MeV
- Predicted nuclear matter symmetry energy: 37.5 MeV
- Predicted nuclear matter effective mass M*/M: 0.622
- Scalar and vector mean fields at saturation: g_s phi_0 / M, g_omega V_0 / M approximately 0.3 to 0.4
- Fitted coupling constants: g_s/4pi = 0.78, g_omega/4pi = 0.90, g_rho/4pi = 0.55

## Context

This work extends the quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) mean-field approach to nuclei by treating it as a modern effective field theory, incorporating chiral symmetry and vector-meson dominance to describe the composite nucleon structure. It builds on the success of the point-coupling model of Nikolaus et al. and the CCWZ formalism for chiral symmetry, and it enables systematic improvements through two-loop calculations and the inclusion of explicit Delta degrees of freedom.
