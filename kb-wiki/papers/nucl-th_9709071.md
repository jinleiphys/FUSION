# Elastic Proton-Deuteron Backward Scattering: Relativistic Effects and Polarization Observables

> The elastic proton-deuteron backward reaction is analyzed within a covariant approach based on the Bethe-Salpeter equation with realistic meson-exchange interaction. Lorentz boost and other relativistic effects in the cross section and spin correlation observables, like tensor analyzing power and polarization transfer etc., are investigated in explicit form. Results of numerical calculations for a complete set of polarization observables are presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that relativistic effects, particularly Lorentz boost corrections and contributions from negative-energy P-wave components of the deuteron wave function, significantly modify the cross section and polarization observables in elastic proton-deuteron backward scattering, with the caveat that these corrections are computed within the one-nucleon exchange mechanism and the Bethe-Salpeter formalism, keeping only leading-order terms in the expansion.

## Method

The authors analyze elastic proton-deuteron backward scattering using a covariant Bethe-Salpeter approach with a realistic one-boson exchange interaction. They separate the contributions of positive-energy waves (identified with the non-relativistic limit) from Lorentz boost effects and negative-energy P-wave contributions, computing the latter in leading order via Taylor expansion. The Bethe-Salpeter vertex function is expressed in terms of eight invariant scalar functions, and the amplitude is reduced to four independent helicity amplitudes in the collinear kinematics.

## Key numbers

- Lorentz boost effects contribute up to 30-40% to the non-relativistic cross section at moderate values of the laboratory momentum \(P_{lab}\)
- The Taylor expansion in \(P_{lab}^2/2m^2\) is justified since \(P_{lab}^2/2m^2 < 1\) in the whole range of initial energy \(\sqrt{s}\)
- The cross section in the positive-energy BS approach is proportional to \(P_{lab}^4\) times a series expansion: \(1 + P_{lab}^2/(2m^2) + 29P_{lab}^4/(16m^4) + 83P_{lab}^6/(32m^6) + \cdots\)
- The Lorentz boost factor \({\cal L}(P_{lab})\) is given by \(1 + P_{lab}^2/(4m^2) + 7P_{lab}^4/(8m^4) + \cdots\)
- Differences between the deuteron rest frame and three-body center-of-mass frame definitions of the internal momentum already occur at intermediate energies \(P_{lab} \sim 0.2\) to 0.3 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends earlier non-relativistic impulse approximation studies of elastic proton-deuteron backward scattering by Kislind, Keister and Tjon, Wilkin, and Nakamura, which showed that the cross section cannot be satisfactorily described without additional mechanisms such as meson-exchange triangle diagrams. The paper provides a detailed covariant investigation of relativistic corrections that was lacking in previous Bethe-Salpeter calculations, enabling future combined analysis of polarization data from elastic scattering, break-up, and quasi-elastic processes to constrain the reaction mechanism and the role of non-nucleon degrees of freedom. The results are presented in a form that allows direct comparison with the non-relativistic formulae of Rekalo and Ladygin.
