# Green's function method for strength function in three-body continuum

> Practical methods to compute dipole strengths for a three-body system by using a discretized continuum are analyzed. New techniques involving Green's function are developed, either by correcting the tail of the approximate wave function in a direct calculation of the strength function or by using a solution of a driven Schroedinger equation in a summed expression of the strength. They are compared with the complex scaling method and the Lorentz integral transform, also making use of a discretized continuum. Numerical tests are performed with a hyperscalar three-body potential in the hyperspherical-harmonics formalism. They show that the Lorentz integral transform method is less practical than the other methods because of a difficult inverse transform. These other methods provide in general comparable accuracies.

## Key claim

The paper proposes new Green's function techniques for computing dipole strength functions in three-body continuum systems, claiming that these methods provide comparable accuracy to the complex scaling method while being more practical than the Lorentz integral transform method, which suffers from a difficult inverse transform step.

## Method

The authors develop two Green's function approaches within the hyperspherical-harmonics formalism: one corrects the tail of approximate wave functions obtained from continuum-discretized states (CDA) using a Green's function to extract phase shifts and strength functions, and the other solves a driven Schroedinger equation with outgoing-wave boundary conditions (DEM). These methods are tested against numerically exact solutions obtained via the Numerov method for a hyperscalar three-body potential, and compared with the complex scaling method (CSM) and Lorentz integral transform (LIT) method.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energies for the four potential sets: Set 1 at -17.6 MeV, Set 2 at -8.95 MeV, Set 3 at -3.49 MeV, Set 4 at -38.4 MeV
- Root mean square hyperradii of ground states: Set 1 at 1.39 fm, Set 2 at 1.57 fm, Set 3 at 1.84 fm, Set 4 at 0.938 fm
- Set 1 potential produces a sharp resonance around E = 2.5 MeV for the P-wave (K=1) channel
- The basis expansion used up to 31 terms in a geometric progression with parameters b0 = 0.1 fm and p = 1.3 to assure convergence
- The electric dipole strength function s(E) is reported in units of fm^2 MeV^{-1}

## Context

This work extends the Green's function technique previously applied to phase-shift calculations of scattering between nuclei (Suzuki, 2008) to the three-body continuum problem. It provides a systematic comparison of four methods (CDA, DEM, CSM, and LIT) for computing dipole strengths, using a hyperscalar potential that reduces the three-body problem to a solvable single-channel case. The results enable future applications to realistic two-neutron halo nuclei such as 6He, where breakup cross sections and electric dipole strengths have been studied by Baye (2005), Myo (2007), and Egami (2004).
