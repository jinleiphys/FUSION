# Exploring universality of transversity in p-p collisions

> The transversity distribution was recently extracted from deep-inelastic scattering processes producing hadron pairs in the final state. Together with a specific chiral-odd di-hadron fragmentation function, it is involved in the elementary mechanism that generates a transverse-spin asymmetry in the azimuthal distribution of the detected hadron pairs. The same elementary mechanism was predicted to generate an analogous asymmetry when the hadron pairs are produced in proton-proton collisions with one transversely polarized proton. Recently, the STAR Collaboration has observed this asymmetry. We analyze the impact of these data on our knowledge of transversity.

# Exploring universality of transversity in p-p collisions

## Key claim
The same chiral-odd di-hadron fragmentation function \(H_1^{\sphericalangle}\) and transversity distribution \(h_1\) that generate transverse-spin asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and \(e^+e^-\) annihilations also produce the analogous asymmetry observed by the STAR Collaboration in proton-proton collisions, confirming the universality of this mechanism, but the agreement is less satisfactory for the pseudorapidity and transverse-momentum dependence, possibly due to missing higher-order QCD corrections and poorly constrained gluon fragmentation.

## Method
The transversity distribution \(h_1\) was extracted from a collinear-factorization fit to HERMES and COMPASS SIDIS data for \((\pi^+\pi^-)\) pair production, using the di-hadron fragmentation function \(H_1^{\sphericalangle}\) previously parametrized from BELLE \(e^+e^-\) data. The extracted \(h_1\) and \(H_1^{\sphericalangle}\) were then inserted into the leading-order factorization formula for the transverse spin asymmetry \(A_{pp}\) in \(p p^\uparrow \to (\pi^+\pi^-) X\) at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV, and the theoretical predictions were compared to STAR data. The impact of STAR data was quantified using a replica reweighting technique based on the \(\chi^2\) of each replica on the STAR data set.

## Key numbers
- The transversity extraction used a functional form with parameters \(A_q, B_q, C_q, D_q\) at starting scale \(Q_0^2 = 1\) GeV\(^2\), with the low-\(x\) behavior governed by an \(x^{1/2}\) term imposed for integrability.
- The STAR data were collected at center-of-mass energy \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV for \(p + p^\uparrow \to (\pi^+\pi^-) + X\).
- After reweighting replicas on STAR data, the Shannon entropy indicated that only 7% of the replicas are statistically relevant.
- The normalized \(\chi^2\) distribution of replicas on STAR data peaked slightly below 2 after reweighting.

## Context
This work extends the collinear-factorization extraction of transversity from di-hadron SIDIS (Bacchetta et al., 2011; Radici et al., 2015) to hadronic collisions, testing the universality of the \(h_1 H_1^{\sphericalangle}\) mechanism predicted by Bacchetta et al. (2004). It contrasts with the TMD-based Collins-effect extraction of transversity (Anselmino et al., 2013), which cannot be extended to proton-proton collisions due to TMD factorization breaking. The results enable a future global fit of all hard processes involving transversity and di-hadron production, and the reweighting analysis demonstrates that STAR data can strongly select among replicas from SIDIS fits, providing new constraints on the nucleon tensor charge relevant for beyond-Standard-Model searches.
