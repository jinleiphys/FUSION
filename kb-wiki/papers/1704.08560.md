# Optimizing phonon space in the phonon-coupling model

> We present a new scheme to select the most relevant phonons in the phonon-coupling model, named here time-blocking approximation (TBA). The new criterion, based on the phonon-nucleon coupling strengths rather than on $B(EL)$ values, is more selective and thus produces much smaller phonon spaces in TBA. This is beneficial in two respects: first, it curbs down the computational cost, and second, it reduces the danger of double counting in the expansion basis of TBA. We use here TBA in a form where the coupling strength is regularized to keep the given Hartree-Fock ground state stable. The scheme is implemented in an RPA and TBA code based on the Skyrme energy functional. We first explore carefully the cutoff dependence with the new criterion and can work out a natural (optimal) cutoff parameter. Then we use the freshly developed and tested scheme to a survey of giant resonances and low-lying collective states in six doubly magic nuclei looking also on the dependence of the results when varying the Skyrme parametrization.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a new criterion for selecting phonons in the time-blocking approximation (TBA) based on the dimensionless phonon-nucleon coupling strength \(v_n = \langle V \rangle_n / \omega_n\) rather than on \(B(EL)\) values, which is more selective and produces much smaller phonon spaces while maintaining comparable accuracy for giant resonance energies and widths.

## Method

The authors implement the new \(V/E\)-criterion within a fully self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA) and TBA framework based on the Skyrme energy density functional, using a continuum-response formalism with a discrete basis that properly accounts for the nucleon continuum. They systematically compare the new criterion with the previous \(B\)-criterion (based on \(B(EL)\) values) by computing giant resonances and low-lying collective states in six doubly magic nuclei (\(^{16}\)O, \(^{40,48}\)Ca, \(^{56}\)Ni, \(^{132}\)Sn, \(^{208}\)Pb) using three different Skyrme parametrizations (SV-bas, SV-mas07, SV-m64k6). The phonon space is restricted by a cutoff parameter \(v_{\text{min}}\) (or \(b_{\text{cut}}\) for the \(B\)-criterion), and the dependence of results on this cutoff is explored to identify an optimal value.

## Key numbers

*   For \(^{16}\)O with SV-m64k6, the \(V/E\)-criterion achieves the same giant resonance peak energies as the \(B\)-criterion (when scaling \(b_{\text{cut}}\) by factor 1/5) but with substantially fewer phonons (upper panel of Fig. 2 shows the number of phonon states as a function of cutoff).
*   The maximal single-particle energy \(\varepsilon^\text{sp}_\text{max}\) was set to 500 MeV for \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40,48}\)Ca, \(^{56}\)Ni and 100 MeV for \(^{132}\)Sn and \(^{208}\)Pb.
*   The maximal phonon energy is \(E^\text{phon}_\text{max} = 40\) MeV, and the maximal angular momentum of quasiparticle-phonon configurations is \(L^{1p1h\otimes\text{phon}}_\text{max} = 27\).
*   A smearing parameter \(\Delta = 400\) keV was used in all RPA and TBA calculations for the strength function \(S(E)\).

## Context

This work extends the quasiparticle time-blocking approximation (TBA) formalism developed by Tselyaev (2007) and applied in self-consistent Skyrme calculations by Tselyaev (2016). The new selection criterion improves upon earlier methods such as the \(B(EL)\)-based criterion used by Colo (1994), Kamerdzhiev (2004), and Litvinova (2007), and the \(\xi^2_n\) criterion of Bertulani (1999). The approach enables more efficient TBA calculations for giant resonances and low-lying collective states across a wide mass range, and the systematic variation of Skyrme parametrizations (from the survey by Kluepfel (2009)) allows discrimination of robust features from model-dependent ones.
