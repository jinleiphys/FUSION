# Configuration mixing effects on neutrinoless $ββ$-decay nuclear matrix elements

> Mixing and coexistence of intrinsic nuclear shapes play an important role to determine the low-energy structure of heavy nuclei, and are expected to affect nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) of neutrinoless double beta ($0νββ$) decay. This problem is addressed in the interacting boson model with configuration mixing that is formulated by using the nuclear energy density functional theory. It is shown that significant amounts of mixing of normal and deformed intruder configurations are present in the ground and excited $0^+$ states in the even-even nuclei that are parent or daughter nuclei of the $0νββ$ decay. An illustrative application to the $0νββ$ decays of $^{76}$Ge, $^{96}$Zr, $^{100}$Mo, $^{116}$Cd, and $^{150}$Nd shows that the inclusion of the configuration mixing reduces the NMEs for most of the $0^+_1$ $\to$ $0^+_1$ $0νββ$ decays.

## Key claim

The inclusion of configuration mixing (mixing of normal and intruder deformed shapes) in the interacting boson model generally reduces the nuclear matrix elements (NMEs) for the $0^+_1 \to 0^+_1$ neutrinoless double beta decay, but the effect is not universal and depends on the specific nucleus and the mean-field input used.

## Method

The author uses the interacting boson model with configuration mixing (IBMCM), where the Hamiltonian is built from constrained self-consistent mean-field (SCMF) calculations of potential energy surfaces using both relativistic (RHB with DD-PC1 interaction) and nonrelativistic (HFB with Gogny D1M interaction) energy density functionals. The IBMCM Hamiltonian parameters are determined by mapping the SCMF potential energy surfaces onto the boson energy surfaces, and the NMEs are computed within the closure approximation using transition operators extended to allow normal-to-normal, intruder-to-intruder, and normal-to-intruder transitions.

## Key numbers

- $^{76}$Ge $\to$ $^{76}$Se: RHB-IBMCM gives $\mzn(0^+_1\to0^+_1) = 1.48$ (reduced from 2.81 without configuration mixing); HFB-IBMCM gives 3.10 (reduced from 4.05)
- $^{96}$Zr $\to$ $^{96}$Mo: RHB-IBMCM gives $\mzn(0^+_1\to0^+_1) = 3.65$ (essentially unchanged from 3.66); HFB-IBMCM gives 4.58 (increased from 4.12)
- $^{100}$Mo $\to$ $^{100}$Ru: RHB-IBMCM gives $\mzn(0^+_1\to0^+_1) = 2.80$ (reduced from 3.04); HFB-IBMCM gives 3.95 (essentially unchanged from 3.92)
- $^{116}$Cd $\to$ $^{116}$Sn: RHB-IBMCM gives $\mzn(0^+_1\to0^+_1) = 3.28$ (reduced from 3.86); HFB-IBMCM gives 3.85 (reduced from 3.96)
- $^{150}$Nd $\to$ $^{150}$Sm: RHB-IBMCM gives $\mzn(0^+_1\to0^+_1) = 3.18$ (reduced from 4.93); HFB-IBMCM gives 3.06 (reduced from 4.76)
- For $^{76}$Ge $\to$ $^{76}$Se, the RHB-IBMCM $\mzn(0^+_1\to0^+_1) = 1.48$ is within the error bar of the IMSRG value $2.60^{+128}_{-136}$ from Belley (2024)
- The $^{150}$Nd $\to$ $^{150}$Sm NME is reduced by approximately 35% due to configuration mixing

## Context

This work extends the author's previous IBM-2 calculations for neutrinoless double beta decay (Nomura 2025) by explicitly including intruder configurations through configuration mixing, which were absent in earlier IBM studies by Barea (2009, 2013, 2015) and Deppisch (2020). The results show that shape coexistence and mixing, known from the work of Heyde (2011) and Garrett (2022), significantly affect NME predictions and bring some IBM values closer to those from the quasiparticle random-phase approximation (QRPA) and nuclear shell model (NSM). The approach enables future systematic studies of configuration mixing effects across the nuclear chart and can be extended to include higher-order intruder configurations.
