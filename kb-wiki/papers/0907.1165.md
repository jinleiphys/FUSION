# Density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy: a microscopic perspective

> We perform a systematic analysis of the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy within the microscopic Brueckner--Hartree--Fock (BHF) approach using the realistic Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential plus a phenomenological three body force of Urbana type. Our results are compared thoroughly to those arising from several Skyrme and relativistic effective models. The values of the parameters characterizing the BHF equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter fall within the trends predicted by those models and are compatible with recent constraints coming from heavy ion collisions, giant monopole resonances or isobaric analog states. In particular we find a value of the slope parameter $L=66.5$ MeV, compatible with recent experimental constraints from isospin diffusion, $L=88 \pm 25$ MeV. The correlation between the neutron skin thickness of neutron-rich isotopes and the slope, $L$, and curvature, $K_{sym}$, parameters of the symmetry energy is studied. Our BHF results are in very good agreement with the correlations already predicted by other authors using non-relativistic and relativistic effective models. The correlations of these two parameters and the neutron skin thickness with the transition density from non-uniform to $β$-stable matter in neutron stars are also analyzed. Our results confirm that there is an inverse correlation between the neutron skin thickness and the transition density.

## Key claim

The microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculation using the Argonne V18 potential plus a three-body force yields a slope parameter of the symmetry energy L = 66.5 MeV, which is compatible with the experimental constraint from isospin diffusion (L = 88 ± 25 MeV) but lies at the lower end of that range; the predicted value of Kτ = -334.7 MeV is far from the experimental lower bound of -450 MeV, though the authors caution that the experimental Kτ may be influenced by surface properties of finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter using the realistic Argonne V18 nucleon-nucleon potential supplemented with a phenomenological three-body force of Urbana type, reduced to a two-body density-dependent force. The Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved self-consistently with the continuous prescription for the single-particle potential, and the energy per particle was computed for various isospin asymmetries. The resulting equation of state was expanded around saturation density to extract bulk parameters of the symmetry energy (E_sym, L, K_sym, Q_sym), and these were compared to predictions from 27 Skyrme forces and several relativistic effective models (NL3, TM1, GM1, GM3, FSU, TW, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, DDHδ, QMC).

## Key numbers

- Slope parameter of the symmetry energy from BHF: L = 66.5 MeV, compared to experimental constraint from isospin diffusion: L = 88 ± 25 MeV.
- Isospin-dependent incompressibility parameter from BHF: Kτ = -334.7 MeV, compared to experimental constraint from giant monopole resonance: Kτ = -550 ± 100 MeV.
- Transition density from non-uniform to β-stable matter in neutron stars, estimated using the experimental constraint on L: between 0.063 fm⁻³ and 0.083 fm⁻³.
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter from BHF: not given as a single number in the text, but the TBF parameters were fixed to reproduce the saturation point; the text notes that without TBF the saturation density can be off by 40%.
- Energy per particle of symmetric matter at saturation (E₀) and incompressibility (K₀) are listed in Table I (not fully reproduced in the truncated text), but the text states K₀ = 240 ± 20 MeV is supported by experimental data.

## Context

This work extends microscopic BHF calculations of asymmetric nuclear matter (Bombaci, 1991; Zuo, 1999) to a systematic comparison with phenomenological Skyrme forces (Lyon group: SLy4, SLy10, SLy230a; SkI family; SIII, SV) and relativistic models (NL3, TM1, GM1, GM3, FSU, TW, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, DDHδ, QMC). It confirms the linear correlation between the neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb and the slope parameter L previously found by Brown and Typel (2000, 2001) and by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001), and the inverse correlation between neutron skin thickness and the transition density in neutron stars first pointed out by Horowitz and Piekarewicz (2001). The results enable more reliable predictions for neutron star crust properties and provide microscopic validation of correlations that were previously established only with effective models.
