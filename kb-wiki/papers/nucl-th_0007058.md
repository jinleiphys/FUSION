# Lattice QCD Calculations of Hadron Structure: Constituent Quarks and Chiral Symmetry

> New data from parity-violating experiments on the deuteron now allow isolation of the strange-quark contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment, G_M^s(0), without the uncertainty surrounding the anapole moment of the nucleon. Still, best estimates place G_M^s(0) > 0. It is illustrated how this experimental result challenges the very cornerstone of the constituent quark model. The chiral physics giving rise to G_M^s(0) \sim 0 is illustrated.

## Key claim

The experimental indication that the strange-quark contribution to the nucleon magnetic moment, \(G_M^s(0)\), is positive challenges the fundamental constituent quark model assumption that quark magnetic moments are intrinsic properties independent of the quark's environment; however, when lattice QCD data for valence quark contributions are extrapolated using the correct chiral behavior, the resulting ratio \(u_n / u_{\Xi^0}\) becomes consistent with a positive \(G_M^s(0)\), whereas naive linear extrapolation or the constituent quark model would yield a negative value.

## Method

The authors use charge-symmetry equalities derived from QCD three-point correlation functions to express \(G_M^s\) in terms of experimentally measured baryon magnetic moments and ratios of valence quark contributions (e.g., \(u_n / u_{\Xi^0}\)). They then extrapolate existing lattice QCD data for valence quark magnetic moments in the neutron, \(\Xi^0\), proton, and \(\Sigma^+\) to the physical pion mass using a Padé approximant that incorporates the correct leading non-analytic (LNA) chiral behavior from chiral perturbation theory, with the LNA coefficients for the valence sector taken from Table 1.

## Key numbers

- The earlier experimental result for \(G_M^s(0.1\ {\rm GeV}^2)\) was \(+0.61 \pm 0.17 \pm 0.21\ \mu_N\) (SAMPLE collaboration).
- Using the quark model assumptions \(u_n/u_{\Xi^0}=1\) and \({}^\ell R_d^s=0.65\) in Eq. (great) gives \(G_M^s = -0.81\ \mu_N\).
- The linear extrapolation of lattice data gives \(u_n / u_{\Xi^0} = 0.72 \pm 0.46\) at \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV; the chiral extrapolation gives \(u_n / u_{\Xi^0} = 1.51 \pm 0.37\).
- The ratio \(u_p / u_{\Sigma^+}\) is stable: \(1.14 \pm 0.08\) (linear) vs. \(1.14 \pm 0.11\) (chiral) at \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV.
- Using \({}^\ell R_d^s = 0.55\) and the chiral extrapolation, the authors obtain \(G_M^s = -0.16 \pm 0.18\ \mu_N\), which permits a small positive value within error.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of strange-quark contributions to the nucleon magnetic moment beyond earlier SAMPLE results that were complicated by the nucleon anapole moment; new parity-violating deuteron data (Pitt, 2000) allow isolation of \(G_M^s(0)\) without that uncertainty. The authors build on their prior chiral extrapolation method for nucleon magnetic moments (Leinweber 1998, Thomas 1999) and apply it to valence quark contributions, showing that the correct chiral behavior resolves the apparent contradiction between the constituent quark model and the experimental indication of \(G_M^s(0) > 0\). This enables future lattice QCD calculations at lighter quark masses to directly test the environment sensitivity of quark magnetic moments.
