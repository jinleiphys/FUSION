# Jost Function for Coupled Partial Waves

> An exact method for direct calculation of the Jost functions and Jost solutions for non-central potentials which couple partial waves of different angular momenta is presented. A combination of the variable-constant method with the complex coordinate rotation is used to replace the matrix Schrödinger equation by an equivalent system of linear first--order differential equations. Solving these equations numerically, the Jost functions can be obtained to any desired accuracy for all complex momenta of physical interest, including the spectral points corresponding to bound and resonant states. The effectiveness of the method is demonstrated using the Reid soft-core and Moscow nucleon-nucleon potentials which involve tensor forces.

# Jost Function for Coupled Partial Waves

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact method for direct calculation of the Jost function matrix for non-central potentials that couple partial waves of different angular momenta, claiming that the Jost function can be obtained to any desired accuracy for all complex momenta of physical interest, but only for potentials that satisfy the conditions that the potential matrix elements are less singular at the origin than the centrifugal term and vanish at large distances faster than the Coulomb potential.

## Method

The authors combine the variable-constant method with complex coordinate rotation to transform the matrix Schrödinger equation into an equivalent system of linear first-order differential equations for the matrices F^(±)(k,r). These equations are solved numerically starting from series expansions near the origin (obtained via an iterative procedure) and integrating outward; at large distances the matrices become r-independent and yield the Jost matrices as their limits. The method is demonstrated using the Reid soft-core and Moscow nucleon-nucleon potentials, which involve tensor forces that couple partial waves.

## Key numbers

- The triangle condition |J-s| ≤ ℓ ≤ |J+s| defines the maximal number of coupled partial waves for systems where non-central forces arise from nonzero spins.
- The complex rotation parameter θ satisfies 0 ≤ |θ| < π/2, with the rotation r = x exp(iθ).
- The Riccati-Hankel functions behave asymptotically as h_ℓ^(±)(kr) → ∓i exp[±i(kr - ℓπ/2)] as r → ∞.
- The boundary condition at the origin is given by lim_{r→0} Φ_{[ℓ][ℓ']}(k,r)/j_{ℓ'}(kr) = δ_{[ℓ][ℓ']}, where j_ℓ is the Riccati-Bessel function.

## Context

This work extends the Jost function formalism from central potentials (where partial waves are uncoupled) to non-central potentials that couple different angular momenta, generalizing the classical treatment in Newton's book (1966) which was the only prior work considering the coupled partial wave Jost matrix. The only previous calculation of the Jost matrix was done by Sasakawa (date not specified in text). The method enables direct calculation of bound states, scattering states, and resonance states (including very narrow and very broad resonances) for non-central potentials, building on earlier work by the authors for central potentials with and without Coulomb tails (Rakityansky et al., 1996) and on the complex coordinate rotation method used in variational approaches.
