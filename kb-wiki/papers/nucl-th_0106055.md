# Do Hadronic Charge Exchange Reactions Measure Electroweak L = 1 Strength?

> An eikonal model has been used to assess the relationship between calculated strengths for first forbidden beta decay and calculated cross sections for (p,n) charge exchange reactions. It is found that these are proportional for strong transitions, suggesting that hadronic charge exchange reactions may be useful in determining the spin-dipole matrix elements for astrophysically interesting leptonic transitions.

## Key claim

The paper finds an approximate proportionality between calculated cross sections for (p,n) charge exchange reactions and calculated first-forbidden beta decay strengths for strong spin-dipole transitions, accurate to within 10-15%, but this proportionality is established only for transitions with strength \(B_J > 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^2\) and may fail for weaker transitions.

## Method

The authors used an eikonal model with a sensitivity function approach, originally developed for Gamow-Teller transitions by Osterfeld et al., extended to describe \(L=1\) (spin-dipole) transitions. They calculated transition form-factors and spin-dipole strengths \(B_J\) from shell-model wave functions in a \(3\hbar\omega\) model space using the WBN residual interaction, then computed (p,n) cross sections including direct and exchange amplitudes with central and tensor components of the nucleon-nucleon \(t\)-matrix from Franey and Love (1985). The method was applied to the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}(p,n)^{12}\mathrm{N}\) reaction at 135 MeV proton energy, comparing with experimental data from Alford et al. (1996).

## Key numbers

- The proportionality between cross section at maximum and spin-dipole strength is accurate to within 10-15% for states with strength \(B_J > 0.1\ \mathrm{fm}^2\).
- For the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}(p,n)^{12}\mathrm{N}\) reaction at 135 MeV, the \(1^-\) state at excitation energy \(E_x = 1.8\ \mathrm{MeV}\) and the \(2^-\) state at \(E_x = 4.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\) were analyzed.
- The eikonal calculations with the fixed \(Q\) approximation for exchange are within 20% of DWBA calculations with exact exchange.
- The distortion factor \(D(r)\) at the center of the \(^{12}\mathrm{C}\) nucleus is approximately \(D(0) \approx 0.5\).
- The peak cross section occurs at a scattering angle of \(\theta = 4.3^\circ\).

## Context

This work extends the established proportionality between forward-angle (p,n) cross sections and Gamow-Teller (\(L=0\)) strength, confirmed by Daito et al., Goodman et al., Taddeucci et al., Xu et al., Fujiwara et al., and Nakayama et al., to the spin-dipole (\(L=1\)) channel. It addresses the open question of whether hadronic charge exchange reactions can reliably determine leptonic matrix elements for first-forbidden beta decay, which are important for astrophysical processes such as neutrino-nucleus interactions in supernovae and r-process nucleosynthesis. The authors note that future work must examine transition densities for heavier nuclei and the behavior of sensitivity functions in those systems to confirm the proportionality holds more generally.
