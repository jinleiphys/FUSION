# Anisotropy of the semi-classical gluon field of a large nucleus at high energy

> The McLerran-Venugopalan model describes a highly boosted hadron/nucleus as a sheet of random color charges which source soft classical Weizsäcker-Williams gluon fields. We show that due to fluctuations, individual configurations are azimuthally anisotropic. We present initial evidence that impact parameter dependent small-x JIMWLK resummation preserves such anisotropies over several units of rapidity. Finally, we compute the first four azimuthal Fourier amplitudes of the S-matrix of a fundamental dipole in such background fields.

## Key claim

Individual configurations of the semi-classical Weizsäcker-Williams gluon field of a large nucleus at high energy are azimuthally anisotropic due to fluctuations of the random color charge sources, and these anisotropies are preserved over several units of rapidity by impact-parameter-dependent JIMWLK resummation, contrary to mean-field evolution which would wash them out.

## Method

The authors generate random color charge configurations on a lattice (1024 sites per transverse direction, 100 longitudinal sites) according to the McLerran-Venugopalan model with a quadratic effective action, then compute the non-Abelian Weizsäcker-Williams fields in covariant gauge and the eikonal Wilson lines for a fundamental dipole. They extract the first four azimuthal Fourier amplitudes of the dipole S-matrix (both the C-even real part and C-odd imaginary part) for each configuration, then average over the ensemble of about 10^4 configurations, defining A_n = (π/2)|A'_n| to avoid cancellation of the random global phase. They also perform JIMWLK evolution with fixed coupling α_s = 0.14 to study the rapidity dependence of these amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The saturation scale is defined by ⟨S_ρ⟩(r = √2/Q_s) = exp(-1/2), giving Q_s ≈ 0.7125 g^2 μ.
- The quadrupole amplitude ⟨A_2⟩ reaches ≥ 20% at r ≲ 1/Q_s.
- The variance √⟨(δA_2)^2⟩ is comparable to ⟨A_2⟩, indicating large configuration-to-configuration fluctuations.
- ⟨A_1⟩ ∼ r at small r ≪ 1/Q_s, while ⟨A_3⟩ also scales linearly with r but with a smaller slope.
- At large r ≫ 1/Q_s, all harmonic amplitudes approach a common non-zero function when evaluated at fixed impact parameter, but are strongly suppressed when averaged over an area πr^2.

## Context

This work extends the conjecture by Kovner and Lublinsky (2014) that individual configurations of light-cone electric fields should be anisotropic, and provides the first direct lattice calculation of these anisotropies for a large nucleus. It also connects to the linearly polarized gluon distribution h_1^{⊥g} at small x, finding agreement with the result of TMD2 (2014) for small dipoles but differences at large r. The results enable quantitative understanding of azimuthal v_n harmonics in pp and pA collisions, with ⟨A_2⟩ ∼ 15-20% matching the v_2 observed in p+Pb collisions at the LHC.
