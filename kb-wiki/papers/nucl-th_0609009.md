# Microscopic Description of Nuclear Wobbling Motion -- Rotation of traxially deformed nuclei --

> The nuclear wobbling motion in the Lu region is studied by the microscopic cranked mean-field plus RPA method. The Woods-Saxon potential is used as a mean-field with a new parameterization which gives reliable description of rapidly rotating nuclei. The prescription of symmetry-preserving residual interaction makes the calculation of the RPA step parameter-free, and we find the wobbling-like RPA solution if the triaxial deformation of the mean-field is suitably chosen. It is shown that the calculated out-of-band $B(E2)$ of the wobbling-like solution depends on the triaxial deformation in the same way as in the macroscopic rotor model, and can be used to probe the triaxiality of the nuclear mean-field.

## Key claim

The microscopic cranked mean-field plus RPA method using the Woods-Saxon potential produces a wobbling-like RPA solution for triaxially deformed nuclei in the Lu region, but only if the triaxial deformation of the mean-field is suitably chosen; the calculated excitation energy is smaller than the measured one by about 100–150 keV, suggesting missing coupling effects to the odd quasiproton.

## Method

The authors used the cranked mean-field method with a Woods-Saxon potential (new parameterization by Ramon Wyss) to describe the horizontal rotational bands, and the random phase approximation (RPA) to describe the vertical phonon-like excitation. The residual interaction was constructed to restore rotational symmetry broken by the mean-field, making the RPA step parameter-free. The Marshalek theory was applied to transform from the uniformly rotating frame to the principal axis frame, allowing extraction of microscopic moments of inertia and comparison with the macroscopic rotor model.

## Key numbers

- The calculated excitation energy of the one-phonon wobbling band in 163Lu is smaller than experimental data by about 100–150 keV, and vanishes at about ω_rot = 0.52 MeV.
- The out-of-band B(E2) values are more than 50 Weisskopf units, indicating rotational rather than vibrational character (typical collective vibrations in deformed nuclei are about 10 Weisskopf units at most).
- The measured in-band B(E2) values in TSD bands are typically about 500–700 Weisskopf units, and out-of-band B(E2) values are about 100 units or more.
- The particle-rotor model estimate for the energy shift from coupling to the odd particle is Δω_wob = j/𝒥_x ≈ 93 keV (with j = 13/2 and 𝒥_x ≈ 70 ℏ²MeV⁻¹).
- The mean-field deformation parameters used were β₂ = 0.42, γ = 12°, β₄ = 0.034, taken as average values from Nilsson Strutinsky calculations.
- The calculated out-of-band over in-band B(E2) ratio at ω_rot ≈ 0.3 MeV is about 60% of the measured value.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic RPA calculations using the Nilsson potential (MSM02, MSM04) by employing the Woods-Saxon potential to avoid the spurious velocity dependence from the l²-term that caused problems with large moments of inertia. The results confirm the existence of the wobbling collective mode predicted by the macroscopic rotor model (BM75) and previously observed experimentally in 163Lu (Od01, Jen02). The study enables future quantitative understanding of triaxial deformation in the Lu region, particularly the apparent discrepancy between the γ ≈ 12° from Nilsson Strutinsky calculations and the γ ≈ 20° suggested by B(E2) data, and motivates explicit particle-vibration coupling treatments.
