# Two-photon exchange and elastic scattering of longitudinally polarized electron on polarized deuteron

> Structure functions and polarization observables in elastic scattering of longitudinally polarized electron on polarized deuteron are considered within approximation of one-photon + two-photon exchange. It is shown that contribution of two-photon exchange in the generalized structure function A is of order of few percent, while in the generalized structure function B it is of order of 10--20 %. We have found that components T_{20} and T_{21} of tensor analyzing power are mainly determined by one-photon exchange, but T_{22} is mainly determined by interference between one-photon exchange and two-photon exchange. We have also considered polarization observables T_{11}, C_{21} and C_{22} which are proportional to imaginary part of the reaction amplitude and vanish in the framework of one-photon exchange.

## Key claim
Two-photon exchange (TPE) contributions to elastic electron-deuteron scattering are shown to be of order few percent in the generalized structure function A, but 10–20% in the generalized structure function B at Q² > 2.5 GeV²; the tensor analyzing power T₂₂ is dominated by OPE–TPE interference, making it a promising observable for experimental study of TPE, while T₁₁, C₂₁, and C₂₂ vanish in one-photon exchange and are nonzero only due to TPE.

## Method
The authors calculate the scattering amplitude for longitudinally polarized electrons on polarized deuterons in the Breit frame, including both one-photon exchange (OPE) and two-photon exchange (TPE) diagrams. TPE amplitudes are computed using a semi-relativistic approach with deuteron wave functions from the CD-Bonn and Paris NN-potentials, while the deuteron electromagnetic form factors G_C, G_Q, and G_M are taken from a parametrization by KS with parameters from a fit by Ball. The generalized structure functions A and B and all polarization observables (T₂₀, T₂₁, T₂₂, T₁₁, C₁₀, C₁₁, C₂₁, C₂₂) are expressed in terms of generalized form factors and evaluated numerically.

## Key numbers
- TPE contribution to generalized structure function A: order of few percent (at θ_lab = 70°)
- TPE contribution to generalized structure function B: 10–20% at Q² > 2.5 GeV² (at θ_lab = 180°)
- TPE correction to T₂₁ at Q² = 3 GeV²: up to 20%
- T₂₂ is dominated by OPE–TPE interference at Q² > 0.5 GeV²
- T₁₁, C₂₁, and C₂₂ are nonzero only due to TPE (vanish in OPE approximation)
- C₁₀ and C₁₁ show TPE changes of not more than few percent

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of TPE in elastic electron-deuteron scattering, including calculations of type-II diagrams with a Gaussian wave function by Lev (year not given), type-I diagrams in an effective Lagrangian approach by Beijing (year not given) and Dong (2010), and combined type-I+type-II calculations in a semi-relativistic framework by KKD (year not given). The present paper provides a systematic calculation of all polarization observables in the OPE+TPE approximation, showing that T₂₂ is a particularly sensitive probe of TPE effects. The results enable experimental searches for TPE in ed-scattering using polarization measurements, especially for T₂₂ at Q² above 0.5 GeV².
