# Effect of proton-proton Coulomb repulsion on soft dipole excitations of light proton-rich nuclei

> We perform three-body model calculations for soft dipole excitations of the proton-rich Borromean nucleus $^{17}$Ne. To this end, we assume that $^{17}$Ne takes the $^{15}$O+p+p structure, in which the two valence protons are excited from the $0^+$ ground state configuration to $1^-$ continuum states. We employ a density-dependent contact force for the nuclear part of the pairing interaction, and discretize the continuum states with the box boundary condition. We show by explicitly including the Coulomb interaction between the valence protons that the Coulomb repulsion does not significantly alter the E1 strength distribution. We point out that the effect of the Coulomb repulsion in fact can be well simulated by renormalizing the nuclear pairing interaction.

## Key claim

The Coulomb repulsion between the two valence protons in the Borromean nucleus 17Ne does not significantly alter the E1 strength distribution for soft dipole excitations, and its effect can be well simulated by renormalizing the nuclear pairing interaction (e.g., using the proton-proton scattering length instead of the neutron-neutron scattering length).

## Method

The authors performed three-body model calculations for 17Ne assuming a 15O+p+p structure, solving the three-body Hamiltonian by expanding the wave function on an uncorrelated basis with single-particle angular momentum up to l=5. They employed a density-dependent contact force for the nuclear part of the pairing interaction, discretized continuum states with a box boundary condition at Rbox=30 fm, and explicitly included the Coulomb interaction between the valence protons to compare E1 strength distributions under different treatments of the Coulomb repulsion.

## Key numbers

- The two-proton separation energy of 17Ne used to adjust parameters: S2p = 0.944 MeV
- The sum-rule values for the core 15O (AC=15, ZC=8): S0,SR = 1.49 e2fm2 and S1,SR = 5.69 e2fm2MeV
- With full Coulomb treatment (Nucl.+Coul.): S0 = 1.206 e2fm2, S1 = 11.02 e2fm2MeV, centroid energy Ecent = 9.140 MeV
- With Coulomb switched off (Nucl. only): S0 = 1.206 e2fm2, S1 = 10.45 e2fm2MeV, centroid energy Ecent = 8.666 MeV, shift delta Ecent = -0.47 MeV
- With renormalized nuclear pairing (Ren. Nucl.): S0 = 1.205 e2fm2, S1 = 10.86 e2fm2MeV, centroid energy Ecent = 9.017 MeV, shift delta Ecent = -0.12 MeV
- The renormalization required about 10.0% reduction of the strength of the pairing interaction

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous three-body model analysis of ground state properties of 17Ne (Oishi, Hagino, Sagawa 2010) to excited states, and is consistent with Hartree-Fock+RPA calculations for medium-heavy nuclei by Sarchi et al. (2006) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations for N=20,28,50,82,126 isotones by Nakada and Yamagami (2011). The results enable practical use of renormalized pairing interactions for understanding proton-rich nuclei structure, and open the question of how this renormalization works for two-proton emission correlations as discussed by Bertulani, Hussein, and Verde (2008).
