# Spin dependent Momentum Distributions in Deformed Nuclei

> We study the properties of the spin dependent one body density in momentum space for odd--A polarized deformed nuclei within the mean field approximation. We derive analytic expressions connecting intrinsic and laboratory momentum distributions. The latter are related to observable transition densities in {\bf p}--space that can be probed in one nucleon knock--out reactions from polarized targets. It is shown that most of the information contained in the intrinsic spin dependent momentum distribution is lost when the nucleus is not polarized. Results are presented and discussed for two prolate nuclei, $^{21}$Ne and $^{25}$Mg, and for one oblate nucleus, $^{37}$Ar. The effects of deformation are highlighted by comparison to the case of odd--A nuclei in the spherical model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the intrinsic spin-dependent momentum distribution in odd-A deformed nuclei contains rich structure from the odd nucleon, but most of this information is lost when the nucleus is not polarized; only by polarizing the target and measuring vector momentum distributions can one access the full intrinsic spin-dependent information.

## Method

The authors perform density-dependent Hartree-Fock (DDHF) calculations for axially symmetric deformed nuclei, using the SKA effective interaction and the McMaster version of the deformed Hartree-Fock code. They derive analytic expressions connecting the intrinsic scalar and vector momentum distributions (defined in the body-fixed frame) to the laboratory-frame momentum distributions for a fully polarized nucleus, using the Bohr-Mottelson factorization approximation and rotation matrices. The intrinsic momentum distributions are computed from the single-particle wave functions in momentum space, obtained by Fourier transforming the coordinate-space Hartree-Fock solutions expanded in a cylindrical harmonic oscillator basis.

## Key numbers

* For 21Ne, the experimental quadrupole moment is 51.4 ± 3.8 fm², corresponding to a deformation parameter βʳ = 0.45 ± 0.03.
* For 20Ne, the experimental quadrupole moment is 58.5 ± 2.6 fm², corresponding to βʳ = 0.51 ± 0.02.
* For 36Ar, the experimental quadrupole moment is -38.5 ± 21 fm², corresponding to βʳ = -0.15 ± 0.08.
* The deformation parameter in momentum space βᵖ is close to zero for both even-even and odd-A nuclei, in contrast to the large βʳ values.
* For 37Ar and 21Ne, the only non-zero geometrical coefficient G(λ;JK) for λ > 0 is G(λ=2) = 1/5 and -1/5, respectively.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of momentum distributions in even-even deformed nuclei by Caballero, Moya de Guerra, and collaborators (1990, 1991), which investigated deformation effects, pairing, and short-range correlations. It also builds on studies of quasi-elastic electron scattering from polarized deformed nuclei (Caballero et al., 1994). The paper provides the theoretical framework needed to interpret one-nucleon knockout reactions from polarized deformed targets, enabling experimental access to spin-dependent momentum distributions that are otherwise lost in unpolarized measurements. The results are compared to the spherical shell model limit and to Nilsson model calculations with and without major-shell admixtures.
