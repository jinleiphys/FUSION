# VNI-3.1: MC-simulation program to study high-energy particle collisions in QCD by space-time evolution of parton-cascades and parton-hadron conversion

> VNI is a general-purpose Monte-Carlo event-generator, which includes the simulation of lepton-lepton, lepton-hadron, lepton-nucleus, hadron-hadron, hadron-nucleus, and nucleus-nucleus collisions. On the basis of renormalization-group improved parton description and quantum-kinetic theory, it uses the real-time evolution of parton cascades in conjunction with a self-consistent hadronization scheme that is governed by the dynamics itself. The causal evolution from a specific initial state (determined by the colliding beam particles) is followed by the time-development of the phase-space densities of partons, pre-hadronic parton clusters, and final-state hadrons, in position-space, momentum-space and color-space. The parton-evolution is described in terms of a space-time generalization of the familiar momentum-space description of multipl (semi) hard interactions in QCD, involving 2 -> 2 parton collisions, 2 -> 1 parton fusion processes, and 1 -> 2 radiation processes. The formation of color-singlet pre-hadronic clusters and their decays into hadrons, on the other hand, is treated by using a spatial criterion motivated by confinement and a non-perturbative model for hadronization. This article gives a brief review of the physics underlying VNI, which is followed by a detailed description of the program itself. The latter program description emphasizes easy-to-use pragmatism and explains how to use the program (including a simple example), annotates input and control parameters, and discusses output data provided by it.

## Key claim

VNI provides a Monte Carlo simulation of high-energy particle collisions that includes space-time evolution of parton cascades and a self-consistent hadronization scheme governed by the dynamics itself, but the approach is valid only when the typical momentum scale associated with parton interactions is larger than the inverse confinement length scale of approximately 1 fm, separating perturbative and non-perturbative domains.

## Method

The program solves coupled quantum-kinetic transport equations (in space-time) and renormalization-group evolution equations (in momentum space) for the phase-space densities of partons, pre-hadronic clusters, and hadrons in 7-dimensional phase-space d³r d³k dE. The simulation proceeds through three stages: construction of the initial state from beam and target particles using measured structure functions and form factors; evolution of parton cascades including 2→2 scatterings, 1→2 emissions, and 2→1 fusions; and hadronization via parton-coalescence into color-neutral clusters followed by isotropic two-body decay into hadrons. The time evolution is followed in small steps of Δt = O(10⁻³ fm) from initial contact until stable final-state particles remain.

## Key numbers

- Memory required: 2000 kwords (32-bit words)
- Program size: 25760 lines of main program, plus 49 and 244 lines for two example programs
- CPU time examples (Δt = 35 fm, 133-166 MHz processor): e⁺e⁻ at √s = 100 GeV: 10000 events/hour; p+p̅ at √s = 200 GeV: 5000 events/hour; p+Au at √s = 200 GeV/nucleon: 100 events/hour; Au+Au at √s = 200 GeV/nucleon: 1 event/hour
- Restriction: numerical inaccuracies may accumulate for hadronic collisions at √s ≫ 10 TeV and nuclear collisions at √s ≫ 5 TeV/nucleon

## Context

VNI occupies a position between two classes of Monte Carlo event generators: it extends the perturbative QCD parton-shower techniques used in programs like PYTHIA/JETSET, HERWIG, and ARIADNE by adding space-time variables, while applying these techniques to finite-density systems like heavy-ion collisions, which generators such as FRITIOF, VENUS, RQMD, and HIJING treat only at the hadronic or string level. The approach enables study of high-density QCD regimes relevant to HERA, RHIC, and LHC experiments, particularly the saturation of proton structure functions at small Bjorken-x and the formation of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions.
