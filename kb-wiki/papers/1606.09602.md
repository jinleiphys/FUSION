# Baryons as relativistic three-quark bound states

> We review the spectrum and electromagnetic properties of baryons described as relativistic three-quark bound states within QCD. The composite nature of baryons results in a rich excitation spectrum, whilst leading to highly non-trivial structural properties explored by the coupling to external (electromagnetic and other) currents. Both present many unsolved problems despite decades of experimental and theoretical research. We discuss the progress in these fields from a theoretical perspective, focusing on nonperturbative QCD as encoded in the functional approach via Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. We give a systematic overview as to how results are obtained in this framework and explain technical connections to lattice QCD. We also discuss the mutual relations to the quark model, which still serves as a reference to distinguish 'expected' from 'unexpected' physics. We confront recent results on the spectrum of non-strange and strange baryons, their form factors and the issues of two-photon processes and Compton scattering determined in the Dyson-Schwinger framework with those of lattice QCD and the available experimental data. The general aim is to identify the underlying physical mechanisms behind the plethora of observable phenomena in terms of the underlying quark and gluon degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

This review argues that functional methods (Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter/Faddeev equations) provide a well-founded continuum approach to baryon structure that can bridge the gap between QCD and baryon phenomenology, but the authors acknowledge that these calculations remain restricted by truncation assumptions which must be evaluated systematically, and that a complete understanding of baryons as relativistic three-quark bound states is still far from achieved.

## Method

The authors review the spectrum and electromagnetic properties of baryons using the functional approach of QCD, specifically Dyson-Schwinger equations for quark and gluon propagators combined with Bethe-Salpeter and Faddeev equations for three-quark bound states. They systematically explain how baryon masses are extracted from these equations, discuss the truncation schemes applied, and compare results with lattice QCD calculations and experimental data. The review also covers the formalism for calculating baryon form factors and two-photon processes such as Compton scattering within this framework.

## Key numbers

- The Delta(1232) resonance is about 300 MeV heavier than the nucleon, with a width of about 120 MeV
- The electromagnetic decay channel Delta to N gamma contributes less than 1% to the total decay width
- The quark-model prediction for the gamma N to Delta transition magnetic moment relates it to the proton magnetic moment via mu(gamma p to Delta) = 2*sqrt(2)*mu_p/3, but underestimates the experimental value by about 30%
- There are currently 13 four-star nucleon and Delta resonances below 2 GeV quoted by the Particle Data Group
- The helicity amplitude S_{1/2} for the N(1535) shows an unusually slow falloff with Q^2, with the corresponding Pauli form factor F_2(Q^2) consistent with zero above Q^2 ~ 2 GeV^2

## Context

This review extends the long tradition of quark model studies of baryons (Capstick 2000, Klempt 2009) by providing a QCD-based functional approach that can address the shortcomings of constituent quark models, particularly dynamical chiral symmetry breaking and relativistic dynamics. It complements lattice QCD simulations (which are mostly restricted to ground states and static quantities at the physical point) by offering a continuum method capable of exploring the qualitative physical mechanisms behind baryon phenomenology. The work enables future systematic comparisons between functional methods, lattice QCD, and experimental data from facilities such as Jefferson Lab, ELSA, MAMI, and Spring-8, particularly for excited states and form factors at medium to large momentum transfer.
