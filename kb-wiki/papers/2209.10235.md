# Chiral spin symmetry and hot/dense QCD

> Above the chiral symmetry restoration crossover around T_{ch} ~ 155 MeV a new regime arises in QCD, a stringy fluid, which is characterized by an approximate chiral spin symmetry of the thermal partition function. This symmetry is not a symmetry of the Dirac Lagrangian and is a symmetry of the electric part of the QCD Lagrangian. In this regime the medium consists of the chirally symmetric and approximately chiral spin symmetric hadrons that are made of the chirally symmetric quarks connected into the color singlet compounds by a confining chromoelectric field. This regime is evidenced by the approximate chiral spin symmetry of the spatial and temporal correlators and by the breakdown of the thermal perturbation theory at the crossover between the partonic (the quark-gluon plasma) and stringy fluid regimes at ~ 3 T_{ch}. The chiral spin symmetry smoothly disappears above ~ 3T_{ch} which means that the chromoelectric confining interaction gets screened. A direct evidence that the stringy fluid medium consists of densely packed hadrons is the pion spectral function that shows a distinct pion state and its first radial excitation above T_{ch}. Another direct evidence of the hadron degrees of freedom in the stringy fluid is the bottomonium spectrum with the 1S,2S,3S and 1P,2P radial and orbital excitations that become broad with temperature. The hadrons between T_{ch} and ~ 3 T_{ch} in the stringy fluid interact strongly which makes the stringy fluid more a liquid rather than a gas. We discuss how this chiral spin symmetric regime extends into the finite chemical potentials domain and present a qualitative sketch of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

Above the chiral symmetry restoration crossover at T_ch ~ 155 MeV, QCD enters a "stringy fluid" regime characterized by an approximate chiral spin symmetry of the thermal partition function, which is not a symmetry of the Dirac Lagrangian but rather a symmetry of the electric part of the QCD Lagrangian; this regime persists up to approximately 3 T_ch, where the chiral spin symmetry smoothly disappears as the chromoelectric confining interaction gets screened.

## Method

The paper reviews lattice QCD results on spatial and temporal correlators that demonstrate approximate chiral spin symmetry above T_ch, analyzes the renormalized Polyakov loop evolution and the color-singlet part of the Polyakov loop correlator to argue against deconfinement at the chiral crossover, and examines the pion spectral function and bottomonium spectrum extracted from lattice correlators to identify hadron-like degrees of freedom in the stringy fluid regime. The author also discusses the symmetry classification of the QCD Lagrangian, splitting the interaction into electric and magnetic parts in a given reference frame, and uses the SU(2)_CS and SU(2N_F) groups to distinguish between chromoelectric and chromomagnetic interactions.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry restoration crossover occurs at a pseudocritical temperature T_ch ~ 155 MeV, with the quark condensate decreasing from its zero-temperature value to practically zero between 120 MeV and 180 MeV.
- The stringy fluid regime extends from T_ch up to approximately 3 T_ch, where the approximate chiral spin symmetry smoothly disappears.
- The renormalized Polyakov loop evolves from 0 to 1 in a broad temperature interval up to T ~ 1 GeV, with its inflection point around 300 MeV, not near T_ch ~ 155 MeV.
- The eta/s ratio within the fireball at RHIC and LHC temperatures is of order 0.2, only slightly above the limiting value 1/(4 pi).
- The color-singlet part of the Polyakov loop correlator shows no Debye screening at T = 141 MeV and T = 166 MeV, with the extracted potential being the same for temperatures below and above T_ch.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier predictions (Glozman, 2013, 2015) that QCD above the chiral crossover should remain in a confining regime with hadron-like degrees of freedom, evidenced by chiral spin symmetry. It contradicts the common assumption of a simultaneous deconfinement and chiral restoration crossover around 155 MeV, citing the renormalized Polyakov loop results from the literature and the Bielefeld lattice group's conclusion that no evidence of deconfinement exists near the chiral pseudocritical temperature. The paper enables a new interpretation of the QCD phase diagram with three distinct regimes: spontaneously broken chiral symmetry below T_ch, a stringy fluid with approximate chiral spin symmetry between T_ch and ~3 T_ch, and a partonic regime at higher temperatures, and suggests this chiral spin symmetric band extends into the finite chemical potential domain.
