# Nonlocal energy density functionals for pairing and beyond-mean-field calculations

> We propose to use two-body regularized finite-range pseudopotential to generate nuclear energy density functional (EDF) in both particle-hole and particle-particle channels, which makes it free from self-interaction and self-pairing, and also free from singularities when used beyond mean field. We derive a sequence of pseudopotentials regularized up to next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), which fairly well describe infinite-nuclear-matter properties and finite open-shell paired and/or deformed nuclei. Since pure two-body pseudopotentials cannot generate sufficiently large effective mass, the obtained solutions constitute a preliminary step towards future implementations, which will include, e.g., EDF terms generated by three-body pseudopotentials.

## Key claim

The paper proposes using two-body regularized finite-range pseudopotentials to generate nuclear energy density functionals (EDFs) that are free from self-interaction, self-pairing, and singularities in beyond-mean-field calculations; however, because pure two-body pseudopotentials cannot generate a sufficiently large effective mass, the results constitute only a preliminary step toward future implementations that will include three-body pseudopotential terms.

## Method

The authors derived a sequence of regularized finite-range pseudopotentials up to next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO), using a Gaussian form factor as the regulator. They implemented the mean-field equations in a new spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov solver (FINRES4) and in the deformed code HFOOD, then adjusted the coupling constants by minimizing a penalty function that included constraints from infinite nuclear matter properties, binding energies and proton radii of doubly magic and semi-magic nuclei, average pairing gaps, and an empirical constraint to avoid finite-size isovector instabilities. A zero-range Skyrme-type term with fixed strength t0 = 1000 MeV fm3 was added to decorrelate the particle-hole and particle-particle channels and prevent pairing fields from being too strongly peaked at the nuclear surface.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of regularized pseudopotentials (Raimondi 2014, Bennaceur 2014) and addresses the long-standing problem of density-dependent terms in Skyrme and Gogny functionals that cause non-analytic behavior in beyond-mean-field calculations, compromise symmetry restoration (Anguiano 2001, Dobaczewski 2007), and introduce self-interaction in particle-vibration coupling (Tarpanov 2014). The approach contrasts with earlier attempts such as the SLyMR family (Sadoudi 2013) and the VLyB functional (Davesne 2015), which could not simultaneously reproduce bulk and pairing properties of finite nuclei while ensuring infinite-matter stability. The results enable future development of EDFs that include three-body pseudopotential terms to achieve realistic effective masses and fully predictive descriptions of nuclear structure.
