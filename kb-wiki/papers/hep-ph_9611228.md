# Formation of DCCs with the linear sigma model

> A simple approximate treatment of statistical equilibrium is developed within the linear sigma sigma model by means of the Hartree factorization technique, providing a simple means for sampling initial configurations of the chiral field. These are then subjected to pseudo-expansions and their non-equilibrium relaxation towards the normal vacuum is studied.

## Key claim

The formation of disoriented chiral condensates (DCCs) depends sensitively on the cooling rate of the expanding chiral matter; isotropic (three-dimensional) expansions can produce significant amplification of soft pion modes, while purely longitudinal expansions are insufficient to cause a quench when the initial fluctuations are of thermal magnitude.

## Method

The author developed a Hartree-factorized mean-field treatment of the linear sigma model to describe statistical equilibrium of macroscopically uniform chiral matter, then sampled thermal field configurations from the resulting statistical weight. These configurations were subjected to pseudo-expansions in D=1, 2, or 3 dimensions by augmenting the equation of motion with a Rayleigh dissipation term proportional to D/t, and the non-equilibrium relaxation toward the normal vacuum was studied by tracking the order parameter and field fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92 MeV; pion mass: m_pi = 138 MeV; sigma mass: m_sigma = 600 MeV; these fix the model parameters lambda = 20.14, v = 86.71 MeV, and H = (120.55 MeV)^3.
- The critical temperature for the effective masses is T_c = sqrt(2)*v, approximately 220 MeV; the most rapid change in the order parameter occurs at T approx 220 MeV.
- For isotropic (D=3) pseudo-expansion starting from T_0 = 400 MeV, the maximum amplification coefficient G^pi_{k=0} = 2.49, with a correlation length (FWHM) of 2.1 fm.
- For transverse (D=2) expansion at T_0 = 400 MeV, G^pi_{k=0} = 1.67 with correlation length 1.3 fm; for longitudinal (D=1) expansion at the same temperature, G^pi_{k=0} = 0.00 with correlation length 0.6 fm.
- The highest amplification coefficient for D=3 occurs at T_0 = 500 MeV: G^pi_{k=0} = 2.61 with correlation length 1.6 fm.
- For D=3 at T_0 = 200 MeV, the amplification is only 0.11 with correlation length 2.0 fm; at T_0 = 240 MeV, G^pi_{k=0} = 1.19 with correlation length 2.7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the Hartree-factorization approach developed in Randrup (1996, LBL-38125) and applies the pseudo-expansion method from Randrup (1996, Phys. Rev. Lett. 77, 1226) to study DCC formation. The results corroborate the conclusion of Gavin and Muller (1994) that isotropic expansion leads to amplification, and are consistent with the findings of Huang and Wang (1994) and Kluger et al. (1995) that effectively one-dimensional expansions are insufficient to cause a quench. The analysis enables future work using realistic initial conditions derived from early partonic evolution to assess DCC formation prospects at RHIC.
