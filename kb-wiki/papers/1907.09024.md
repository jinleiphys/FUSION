# Polarisabilities of the nucleon in baryon chiral perturbation theory and beyond

> We review the recent baryon chiral perturbation theory results for the nucleon polarisabilities that describe the different regimes of nucleon Compton scattering --- real, virtual, and doubly virtual. We stress the importance of the empirical verification of the theory in the context of the calculation of the inelastic nucleon structure corrections, such as the two-photon exchange contributions. We also discuss the recently obtained constraints that relate the different regimes of nucleon Compton scattering and can provide additional information on the nucleon structure.

# Polarisabilities of the nucleon in baryon chiral perturbation theory and beyond

## Key claim
Baryon chiral perturbation theory (BχPT) provides a unified framework for describing nucleon polarisabilities across real, virtual, and doubly virtual Compton scattering regimes, but the agreement with data is not uniform: while real Compton scattering is described well, there remain tensions in the magnetic dipole polarisability β_{M1} between χPT and dispersion relation extractions, and a significant discrepancy exists between different BχPT calculations for the spin polarisability δ_{LT} in the doubly virtual case.

## Method
The authors perform covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations including pion, nucleon, and Δ(1232) isobar degrees of freedom, using the δ-counting expansion where the nucleon-Δ mass difference is treated as an intermediate scale. The calculation includes πN loops at next-to-leading order (NLO) and πΔ loops plus the Δ pole graph at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), with no free parameters at NNLO. For virtual Compton scattering (VCS) and doubly virtual Compton scattering (VVCS), the photon-nucleon and photon-nucleon-Δ vertices acquire form factors dependent on photon virtuality.

## Key numbers
- Proton static scalar polarisabilities from NNLO BχPT: α_{E1} = 11.2 ± 0.7 × 10^{-4} fm³, β_{M1} = 3.9 ± 0.7 × 10^{-4} fm³ (compared to PDG values of 11.2 ± 0.4 and 2.5 ± 0.4 × 10^{-4} fm³ respectively)
- Proton static spin polarisabilities from NNLO BχPT: γ_{E1E1} = -3.3 ± 0.8 × 10^{-4} fm⁴, γ_{M1M1} = 2.9 ± 1.5 × 10^{-4} fm⁴, γ_{E1M2} = 0.2 ± 0.2 × 10^{-4} fm⁴, γ_{M1E2} = 1.1 ± 0.3 × 10^{-4} fm⁴ (compared to MAMI 2015 extraction of -3.5 ± 1.2, 3.16 ± 0.85, -0.7 ± 1.2, and 1.99 ± 0.29 × 10^{-4} fm⁴ respectively)
- Polarisability contribution to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift: -8.2^{+1.2}_{-2.5} μeV at NLO (πN loops only), shifting to -7.3^{+1.5}_{-2.7} μeV when the Δ pole contribution is added
- Proton quadrupole and dispersive polarisabilities from NNLO BχPT: α_{E2} = 17.3 ± 3.9 × 10^{-4} fm⁵, β_{M2} = 15.5 ± 3.5 × 10^{-4} fm⁵, α_{E1ν} = -1.3 ± 1.0 × 10^{-4} fm⁵, β_{M1ν} = 7.1 ± 2.5 × 10^{-4} fm⁵

## Context
This review extends the authors' previous BχPT calculations of nucleon Compton scattering across multiple regimes: real Compton scattering (Lensky et al., 2008-2015), virtual Compton scattering (Lensky, 2016), and doubly virtual Compton scattering (Lensky, 2014). The work contrasts with heavy-baryon χPT calculations (Griesshammer et al., 2012; McGovern et al., 2012) and dispersion relation approaches (Drechsel et al., 2002) that tend to obtain smaller values of β_{M1}. The results enable reliable calculation of two-photon exchange corrections to the muonic hydrogen Lamb shift, and the new analyticity constraints relating polarisabilities across regimes (Pascalutsa, 2014; Lensky et al., 2017) provide model-independent sum rules that can be tested with upcoming data from MAMI, JLab, and HIGS.
