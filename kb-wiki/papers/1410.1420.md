# Stability of the pion and the pattern of chiral symmetry breaking

> We investigate the pressure of the pion, which should be equal to zero to ensure the stability of the pion, within the framework of the chiral quark model beyond the chiral limit. The pressure of the pion turns out to vanish nontrivially by the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation within the present framework. It implies that the stability of the pion might be deeply rooted in spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking. We also discuss physical quantities relevant to the energy-momentum tensor operator.

# Stability of the pion and the pattern of chiral symmetry breaking

## Key claim
The pressure of the pion vanishes beyond the chiral limit due to the Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner (GOR) relation, implying that pion stability is deeply rooted in spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking and the pattern of explicit chiral symmetry breaking. The hedge is that this result is derived within the specific framework of the chiral quark model and is shown to hold nontrivially only when the GOR relation is satisfied.

## Method
The authors compute the energy-momentum tensor form factors (EMTFFs) of the pion within the SU(2) chiral quark model, using a nonlinear realization of the pion field and including explicit chiral symmetry breaking via a finite current quark mass. The pressure is extracted from the diagonal spatial components of the EMT matrix element at zero momentum transfer, and the calculation is regularized using proper-time regularization with a UV cutoff. The resulting expression for the pressure is shown to reduce to the GOR relation, which forces it to vanish.

## Key numbers
- Current quark mass: m = 16 MeV; UV cutoff: Lambda = 652 MeV (fixed using experimental m_pi = 139.57 MeV and f_pi = 93 MeV)
- Dynamical quark mass: M = 350 MeV
- Quark condensate: <psi-bar psi> = -(220)^3 MeV^3
- Gravitational low-energy constants from the chiral quark model: L_11 = 1.6 x 10^{-3}, L_12 = -3.2 x 10^{-3}, L_13 = 0.84 x 10^{-3}
- Transverse charge radius of the EMTFFs: sqrt(<b^2>_{20}) = 0.270 fm in the chiral limit and 0.264 fm with m_pi = 140 MeV
- Transverse charge radius of the pion electromagnetic form factor: sqrt(<b^2>_{10}) = 0.535 fm (phenomenological value 0.53 fm)

## Context
This work extends previous studies of pion EMTFFs from the chiral limit to the explicitly broken chiral symmetry case within the chiral quark model. The authors compare their results for the gravitational low-energy constants L_11, L_12, and L_13 with those from chiral perturbation theory (Donoghue 1991), finding good agreement. The calculation enables further study of the transverse charge density of the pion and shows that the stability condition is tied to the GOR relation, which is unique to the pion as a Goldstone boson.
