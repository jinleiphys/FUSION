# Initial Baryon Stopping and Angular Momentum in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Noncentral heavy-ion collisions create fireballs with large initial orbital angular momentum that is expected to induce strong vorticity in the hot bulk fluid and generate global spin polarization of the produced particles. As the collision beam energy $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ decreases to approach the two-nucleon-mass threshold, this initial angular momentum approaches zero. One may thus expect that the observed global spin polarization should reach a maximum and then drop to zero as increased stopping competes with decreased initial momentum. Recent experimental measurements, however, appear to show a continual rise of hyperon polarization even down to $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} =$ 2.42 GeV, suggesting a peak very near threshold which is difficult to interpret and calls for a better understanding of angular momentum initial conditions, especially at low energy. Here, we develop a new Glauber-based initial state model ("Glauber+") to investigate the initial distribution of angular momentum with respect to rapidity as well as the dependence of this distribution on initial baryon stopping across a wide range of collisional beam energy. We estimate that the angular momentum per produced final charged particle at mid-rapidity peaks around 5 GeV, which may present a potential challenge to an interpretation of the spin polarization measurements near threshold as being a consequence of the initial angular momentum of the colliding system.

## Key claim

The authors develop a "Glauber+" model to show that the initial angular momentum per produced final charged particle at mid-rapidity peaks around 5 GeV collision energy, which may challenge the interpretation that the observed continued rise of hyperon polarization down to 2.42 GeV is a direct consequence of the initial angular momentum of the colliding system.

## Method

The authors extend the optical Glauber model by introducing a coefficient of restitution parameter e and a fluctuation width σ to describe how participant nucleons lose rapidity after initial collisions, distributing net baryon number and angular momentum across rapidity. They calibrate the model parameters by comparing the predicted net baryon rapidity density at mid-rapidity with experimental net-proton measurements from STAR at multiple beam energies, using a baryon correction factor to convert net-proton to net-baryon numbers. The calibrated model then computes the rapidity distributions of angular momentum for Au-Au collisions across a beam energy range of 2 to 200 GeV and a broad range of impact parameters.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber model framework to include baryon stopping effects, building on ideas from Shen and Alzhrani (2020) regarding local conservation laws for 3D initial conditions and from Shen and Schenke (2022) on using logit-normal distributions for rapidity loss fluctuations. The model addresses the puzzle raised by recent STAR and HADES measurements showing a continual rise of hyperon polarization down to √s_NN = 2.42 GeV, which contradicts the expectation that polarization should peak at some energy and then drop toward threshold. The results enable future studies to better constrain the relationship between initial angular momentum conditions and observed spin polarization signals across a broad range of beam energies.
