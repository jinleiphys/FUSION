# On dipole compression modes in nuclei

> Isoscalar dipole strength distributions in spherical medium- and heavy-mass nuclei are calculated within random phase approximation (RPA) or quasiparticle RPA. Different Skyrme-type interactions corresponding to incompressibilities in the range 200 - 250 MeV are used. The results are discussed in comparison with existing data on isoscalar giant dipole resonances. Two main issues are raised, firstly the calculated giant resonance energies are somewhat higher than the observed ones, and secondly a sizable fraction of strength is predicted below 20 MeV which needs to be experimentally confirmed.

# On dipole compression modes in nuclei

## Key claim
The authors report that self-consistent RPA and QRPA calculations of the isoscalar giant dipole resonance (ISGDR) in medium- and heavy-mass nuclei systematically overestimate the observed centroid energies, with the discrepancy being much larger than for other giant resonances, while also predicting a sizable fraction of strength below 20 MeV that has not been confirmed experimentally.

## Method
The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus Random Phase Approximation (RPA) calculations for the doubly magic nucleus 208Pb, and Hartree-Fock-BCS (HF-BCS) plus quasiparticle RPA (QRPA) for the non-double-magic nuclei 90Zr, 116Sn, and 144Sm. Five different Skyrme-type interactions (SkP, SGII, SKM*, SLy4, SkI2) spanning nuclear incompressibilities from 200 to 250 MeV were used, and a projection procedure was applied to remove spurious center-of-mass strength from the ISGDR operator.

## Key numbers
- Experimental centroid energies E0 = m1/m0 for the ISGDR are: 26.3(4) MeV in 90Zr, 24.3(3) MeV in 116Sn, 23.0(3) MeV in 144Sm, and 20.3(2) MeV in 208Pb.
- The calculated ISGDR strength in the low-energy region (below 20 MeV) contains about 20% of the ISGDR energy-weighted sum rule.
- The Skyrme forces used span a range of nuclear incompressibility K∞ from 200 to 250 MeV.
- The predicted ISGDR peak and centroid energies lie in the region of 110A^{-1/3} MeV.
- Coupling to 2p-2h excitations produces a downward shift of less than 1 MeV in the centroid energy.

## Context
This work extends earlier RPA calculations using the finite-range Gogny interaction by Gogn (year not specified) which found ISGDR energies around 26 MeV, and relativistic RPA calculations by Giai (year not specified) which found energies around 25 MeV for 208Pb and 144Sm. The results contradict the experimental data from Clark (year not specified) obtained from 240 MeV inelastic alpha-scattering at Texas A&M University, which reported lower centroid energies. The paper identifies two open questions: the systematic overestimation of ISGDR energies compared to experiment, and the need for experimental confirmation of the predicted low-energy strength below 20 MeV.
