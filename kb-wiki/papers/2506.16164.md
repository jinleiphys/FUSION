# The Carrollian Kaleidoscope

> The Carroll group arises in the vanishing speed of light limit of the Poincaré group and was initially discarded as just a mathematical curiosity. However, recent developments have proved otherwise. Carroll and conformal Carroll symmetries are now ubiquitous, appearing in diverse physical phenomena starting from condensed matter physics to quantum gravity. This review aims to provide the reader a gateway into this fast-developing field. After an introduction and setting the stage with basics of the symmetry in question, we detail the construction of Carrollian and Carrollian Conformal field theories (CCFT). We then focus on applications. By far the most popular of these applications is in the context of the construction of holography in asymptotically flat spacetimes (AFS) in terms of a co-dimension one dual CCFT. We review the early work on AFS$_3$ /CCFT$_2$ before delving into an in-depth analysis for the construction of the dual to 4D AFS. Two other important sets of applications are in hydrodynamics and in condensed matter physics, which we discuss in detail. Carroll hydrodynamics is introduced as the $c\to 0$ limit of relativistic hydrodynamics first and then reconstructed from a symmetry based approach. Relations to ultrarelativistic flows and connections to the quark-gluon plasma are discussed with concrete examples of the Bjorken and Gubser flow models. In condensed matter applications, we cover connections to fractons, flat bands, and phase separation in Luttinger liquid models. To conclude, we give very brief outlines of other topics of interest including string theory and black hole horizons.

## Key claim

Carrollian symmetries, obtained from the vanishing speed of light limit of the Poincare group, are not merely a mathematical curiosity but are now understood to be ubiquitous in physics, appearing on generic null surfaces such as black hole event horizons and asymptotic null infinity, and providing a framework for flat space holography, hydrodynamics, and condensed matter systems; the review emphasizes that the effective speed of light that goes to zero can be played by different physical quantities depending on the context (e.g., distance from a horizon, string tension, inverse AdS radius, or Fermi velocity).

## Method

The paper is a comprehensive review that first develops the algebraic and geometric foundations of Carroll and conformal Carroll symmetries through the Inonu-Wigner contraction of the Poincare algebra in the c→0 limit, including the construction of Carrollian field theories for spins 0, 1/2, and 1 and Carrollian conformal field theories (CCFTs) in two and higher dimensions. It then applies these formalisms to three major areas: holography in asymptotically flat spacetimes (AFS), where it reviews the AFS3/CCFT2 correspondence and the construction of duals to 4D AFS; Carroll hydrodynamics derived as the c→0 limit of relativistic hydrodynamics and from symmetry principles, with applications to Bjorken and Gubser flow models relevant to quark-gluon plasma; and condensed matter physics, covering connections to fractons, flat bands, and phase separation in Luttinger liquid models.

## Key numbers

* The Carroll group arises from the Poincare group via the limit c→0, contrasted with the Galilean group which arises from c→∞.
* The conformal Carroll algebra in D dimensions is isomorphic to the Bondi-Metzner-Sachs (BMS) algebra in D+1 dimensions for the specific ratio N = λ1/λ2 = -2, where space and time scale identically under dilatation.
* The Schwarzschild black hole event horizon at r=2M provides an explicit example where the effective speed of light on codimension-one surfaces goes to zero as r→2M.
* The paper reviews the AFS3/CCFT2 correspondence and extends the analysis to the construction of a dual CCFT for 4D asymptotically flat spacetimes.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on Carroll symmetries by Levy-Leblond (1965) and Sen Gupta (1966), which had been largely dormant for over four decades, and builds upon the resurgence initiated by Bagchi (2010) and Duval (2014) connecting Carroll symmetries to flat space holography. The paper synthesizes recent developments showing that Carroll structures arise generically on null surfaces, enabling connections between apparently disparate fields: it relates the AFS/CCFT holographic correspondence to the Bondi-Metzner-Sachs (BMS) algebra from Bondi (1962) and Sachs (1962), connects Carroll hydrodynamics to ultrarelativistic flows and quark-gluon plasma models, and links Carroll symmetries to condensed matter phenomena including fractons (Bidussi 2021) and flat bands (Bagchi 2022). The review enables future work by providing a unified framework for understanding null surface physics across black hole horizons, flat space holography, and condensed matter systems.
