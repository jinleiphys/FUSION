# Unraveling Gluon Jet Quenching through $J/ψ$ Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Jet quenching has long been regarded as one of the key signatures for the formation of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions. Despite significant efforts, the separate identification of quark and gluon jet quenching has remained as a challenge. Here we show that $J/ψ$ in high transverse momentum ($p_\text{T}$) region provides a uniquely sensitive probe of in-medium gluon energy loss since its production at high $p_\text{T}$ is particularly dominated by gluon fragmentation. Such gluon-dominance is first demonstrated for the baseline of proton-proton collisions within the framework of leading power NRQCD factorization formalism. We then use the linear Boltzmann transport model combined with hydrodynamics for the simulation of jet-medium interaction in nucleus-nucleus collisions. The satisfactory description of experimental data on both nuclear modification factor $R_{\text{AA}}$ and elliptic flow $v_2$ reveals, for the first time, that the gluon jet quenching is the driving force for high $p_\text{T}$ $J/ψ$ suppression. This novel finding is further confirmed by the data-driven Bayesian analyses of relevant experimental measurements, from which we also obtain the first quantitative extraction of the gluon energy loss distribution in the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim

The paper claims that high-transverse-momentum ($p_\text{T}$) $J/\psi$ production in heavy-ion collisions is a uniquely sensitive probe of gluon jet quenching, because gluon fragmentation dominates $J/\psi$ production at high $p_\text{T}$ (above 10 GeV). The hedge is that this conclusion is demonstrated within the specific framework of leading-power NRQCD factorization combined with the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model, and is further confirmed by Bayesian analysis of experimental data.

## Method

The authors first validated the proton-proton baseline for $J/\psi$ production using a gluon-fragmentation-improved PYTHIA (GFIP) event generator within the leading-power NRQCD factorization formalism. They then simulated jet-medium interactions in nucleus-nucleus collisions using the linear Boltzmann transport (LBT) model combined with 3+1D CLVisc hydrodynamics, which includes both elastic and inelastic (higher-twist induced gluon radiation) processes for charm quarks and gluons scattering with medium constituents. Finally, they performed a data-driven Bayesian analysis to extract flavor-dependent parton energy loss distributions from experimental $R_{\text{AA}}$ data.

## Key numbers

- Gluon fragmentation contributes more than 85% of high-$p_\text{T}$ $J/\psi$ production in proton-proton collisions over a broad $p_\text{T}$ range, while charm-quark fragmentation contributes less than 15%.
- The minimum $p_\text{T}$ for gluon fragmentation to occur outside the QGP medium is estimated as $p_{\text{T,min}} \sim 2 m_c^2 L \sim 50$ GeV for a typical path length $L \sim 3$ fm.
- The strong coupling constant in the LBT model was set to $\alpha_s = 0.18$, with no additional free parameters.
- From Bayesian analysis of 0-10% central Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, the extracted parameters for gluon energy loss are: $\alpha = 5.25 \pm 1.09$, $\beta = 0.7 \pm 0.07$, $\gamma = 0.37 \pm 0.03$; for charm quark energy loss: $\alpha = 6.33 \pm 2.06$, $\beta = 0.53 \pm 0.19$, $\gamma = 0.36 \pm 0.09$.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to separately identify quark and gluon jet quenching, which had remained a challenge despite attempts using multivariate jet substructure analysis (Chien 2018), jet charge (Chen 2019, CMS 2020), and electroweak boson tagged jets (Zhang 2018, CMS 2017). The finding that high-$p_\text{T}$ $J/\psi$ suppression is dominated by gluon energy loss contradicts the naive expectation that $J/\psi$ suppression is driven by its constituent charm quarks. This result enables future quantitative extraction of gluon energy loss distributions and tests of the color-charge dependence of jet-medium interactions, and can be combined with open heavy flavor and light hadron measurements to further constrain parton-type dependence.
