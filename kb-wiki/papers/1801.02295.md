# Interatomic interaction effects on second-order momentum correlations and Hong-Ou-Mandel interference of double-well-trapped ultracold fermionic atoms

> Identification and understanding of the evolution of interference patterns in two-particle momentum correlations as a function of the strength of interatomic interactions are important in explorations of the nature of quantum states of trapped particles. Together with the analysis of two-particle spatial correlations, they offer the prospect of uncovering fundamental symmetries and structure of correlated many-body states, as well as opening vistas into potential control and utilization of correlated quantum states as quantum information resources. With the use of the second-order density matrix constructed via exact diagonalization of the microscopic Hamiltonian, and an analytic Hubbard-type model, we explore here the systematic evolution of characteristic interference patterns in the two-body momentum and spatial correlation maps of two entangled ultracold fermionic atoms in a double well, for the entire attractive- and repulsive-interaction range. We uncover statistics-governed bunching and antibunching, as well as interaction-dependent interference patterns, in the ground and excited states, and interpret our results in light of the Hong-Ou-Mandel interference physics, widely exploited in photon indistinguishability testing and quantum information science.

# Interatomic interaction effects on second-order momentum correlations and Hong-Ou-Mandel interference of double-well-trapped ultracold fermionic atoms

## Key claim

The evolution of two-particle momentum correlation patterns from a plaid pattern to striped interference fringes depends systematically on the interatomic interaction strength across the entire attractive and repulsive range, but the specific number of visible fringes or humps is also determined by the confining potential steepness (harmonic frequency), with a narrower trap producing more individual features than a wider one at the same interaction strength.

## Method

The authors used exact configuration-interaction (CI) diagonalization of the microscopic two-dimensional Hamiltonian for two ultracold fermionic atoms in a double-well trap, computing the second-order density matrix and its Fourier transform to obtain two-body momentum and spatial correlation maps. They supplemented these numerical results with an analytic two-site Hubbard-type model that incorporates spatial degrees of freedom via displaced Gaussian orbitals, allowing derivation of closed-form expressions for the momentum correlations as functions of the Hubbard parameter U/t. The Hubbard model parameters (on-site repulsion U and hopping t) were extracted from the microscopic CI calculations for each confining potential.

## Key numbers

- Interwell distance: d = 2 μm throughout all calculations
- Two confining potentials used: ℏω_x = 8 kHz, ℏω_y = 800 kHz and ℏω_x = 15 kHz, ℏω_y = 1500 kHz, both with aspect ratio ω_x/ω_y = 1/100
- Gaussian interaction width: σ = 0.01 μm, yielding σ/l_0 ~ 0.03 for ℏω = 8 kHz and σ/l_0 ~ 0.022 for ℏω = 15 kHz
- Mass of ^6Li used: M_{^6Li} = 10964.90 m_e
- For the non-interacting case (g ~ 0, U/t ~ 0), the ground-state momentum map shows four visible humps for ℏω = 8 kHz confinement and nine visible humps for ℏω = 15 kHz confinement
- At strong repulsion (U/t = 20), the ground-state momentum map shows three visible fringes for ℏω = 8 kHz and five visible fringes for ℏω = 15 kHz
- The barrier parameter ε_b was fixed at 0.5 throughout

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of two-particle momentum correlations beyond the non-interacting or weakly-interacting regimes previously studied in double-well systems, such as the experiments by Bergschneider et al. (2017) and Schmied et al. (2017). The authors connect their results to the Hong-Ou-Mandel interference physics originally demonstrated for photons by Hong, Ou, and Mandel (1987), and subsequently extended to electrons by Liu et al. (1998) and Bocquillon et al. (2013), and to bosonic atoms by Kaufman et al. (2014) and Aspect et al. (2015). The analytic Hubbard model with spatial degrees of freedom enables interpretation of the full interaction range from the non-interacting regime (with substantial double occupancy) to the Mott insulating regime at large U/t, and the results agree with preparatory experimental measurements by Bergschneider et al. (2017).
