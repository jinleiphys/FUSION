# Probing non-perturbative QCD through hadronic matrix elements extracted from exclusive hard processes

> QCD is the theory of strong interactions and non-perturbative methods have been developed to address the confinement property of QCD. Many experimental measurements probe the confining dynamics, and it is well-known that hard scattering processes allow the extraction of non perturbative hadronic matrix elements. To study exclusive hard processes, such as electromagnetic form factors and reactions like gamma* N -> gamma N', gamma* N -> pi N', gamma* gamma -> pi pi, antiproton proton ->gamma* pi in particular kinematics (named as generalized Bjorken regime), one introduces specific non-perturbative objects, namely generalized parton distributions (GPDs), distribution amplitudes (DA) and transition distribution amplitudes (TDA), which are Fourier transformed non-diagonal matrix elements of non-local operators on the light-cone. We review here a selected sample of exclusive amplitudes in which the quark and gluon content of hadrons is probed, and emphasize that much remains to be done to successfully compute their non-perturbative parts. We present some difficulties with respect to the application of the much publicized AdS-QCD approach to the calculation of these partonic quantities.

## Key claim

Exclusive hard processes allow the extraction of non-perturbative hadronic matrix elements such as generalized parton distributions (GPDs), distribution amplitudes (DAs), and transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs), but much remains to be done to successfully compute their non-perturbative parts, and the much-publicized AdS-QCD approach faces specific difficulties in recovering the partonic picture.

## Method

The paper reviews a selected sample of exclusive amplitudes, including electromagnetic form factors and reactions such as γ* N → γ N', γ* N → π N', γ* γ → π π, and antiproton-proton → γ* π, in the generalized Bjorken regime. For each process, the authors describe how specific non-perturbative objects (GPDs, DAs, and TDAs) are defined as Fourier-transformed non-diagonal matrix elements of non-local quark and gluon operators on the light-cone. They then examine the status of theoretical calculations for these objects, including QCD sum rules, lattice QCD, and the AdS/QCD correspondence, highlighting discrepancies and open problems.

## Key numbers

- First moments of the proton distribution amplitude estimated from QCD sum rules: (0.56, 0.19, 0.23); from lattice QCD: (0.40, 0.30, 0.30).
- Normalization constant f_H for the hybrid meson (J^PC = 1^{-+}) from QCD sum rules: f_H ≈ 50 MeV.
- Product of the quark condensate and the magnetic susceptibility of the QCD vacuum, χ ⟨q̄ q⟩, estimated via QCD sum rules: ≈ 50 MeV.
- Asymptotic form of the leading twist longitudinally polarized hybrid meson DA: φ₁^H(u) = 30 u (1-u)(1-2u).
- Asymptotic form of the photon distribution amplitude: φ_γ^{as}(z) = 6 z (1-z).

## Context

This review extends the long-standing program of extracting non-perturbative QCD quantities from hard exclusive processes, building on pioneering work on distribution amplitudes (DA) and generalized parton distributions (GPDs). It highlights discrepancies between QCD sum rule and lattice results for the proton DA, as noted in the comparison by Lenz (2009), and discusses the surprising BABAR data on the π⁰γ transition form factor. The paper critically examines the AdS/QCD approach, following the strategy of Polchinski and Strassler (2002), and finds that recovering the partonic picture, including the Callan-Gross relation, requires fine-tuning an infinite number of parameters, making the approach non-predictive. The work enables future experimental extraction of meson DAs and proton-to-pion TDAs from upcoming medium-energy experiments.
