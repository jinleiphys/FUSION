# Centrality dependence of strangeness and (anti)hyperon production at BNL RHIC

> We evaluate strangeness produced in Au--Au interactions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV, as function of reaction participant number $A$, and obtain the relative strange quark content at hadronization. Strange baryon and antibaryon rapidity density yields are studied, relative to, and as function of, participant number, and produced hadron yields.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the centrality dependence of strange baryon and antibaryon yields in Au–Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV can distinguish between chemical non-equilibrium and chemical equilibrium hadronization models, with the chemical non-equilibrium scenario providing a better description of the available data, particularly for the most central collisions.

## Method

The authors used the Statistical Hadronization Model (SHM) to analyze particle yields at central rapidity as a function of participant number A, fitting to PHENIX and STAR experimental data for pions, kaons, protons, antiprotons, K*(892), and φ mesons. They compared three scenarios: chemical non-equilibrium (γsH ≠ 1, γqH ≠ 1), chemical semi-equilibrium (γqH = 1, γsH ≠ 1), and full chemical equilibrium (γsH = γqH = 1), and used the resulting parameters to predict the centrality-dependent yields of Λ, Ξ, Ω and their antiparticles.

## Key numbers

- For the most central collisions (A ≈ 350), the strangeness-to-entropy ratio was found at s/S = 0.028 ± 0.002 in the chemical non-equilibrium model
- The strangeness occupancy γsH reached 2.4 for the most central collisions in the chemical non-equilibrium scenario
- For very peripheral collisions, the strangeness-to-entropy ratio was s/S ≈ 0.019
- The strangeness density at hadronization increased from ds/dV = 0.03/fm³ for peripheral collisions to approximately five times that value for central collisions in the chemical non-equilibrium model
- The yields of dΞ⁻/dy and dΞ̄⁺/dy for the chemical non-equilibrium case are 80% above those expected in the chemical equilibrium approach
- The pp reference yields used for normalization were: d(Λ+Λ̄)/dy = 0.066 ± 0.006, d(Ξ⁻+Ξ̄⁺)/dy = 0.0036 ± 0.0012, Λ̄/Λ = 0.88 ± 0.09, and Ξ̄⁺/Ξ⁻ = 0.90 ± 0.09

## Context

This work extends the SHM analysis of Rafelski (2004) for RHIC √sNN = 200 GeV Au–Au reactions by presenting the hadronization conditions for strangeness for the first time and using them to predict centrality-dependent yields of strange and multi-strange baryons and antibaryons. The results enable experimental tests that can distinguish between chemical equilibrium and non-equilibrium hadronization scenarios, particularly through the centrality dependence of hyperon yields normalized by pion yields or by participant number A, which the authors propose as a more discriminating test than simply measuring γsH and γqH.
