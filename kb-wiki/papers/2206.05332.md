# Impact of nuclear deformation on collective flow observables in relativistic U+U collisions

> A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model is used to investigate the efficacy of several flow observables to constrain the initial-state deformation of the Uranium nuclei in U$+$U collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{\textit{s}_{NN}}$ = 193 GeV. The multiparticle azimuthal cumulant method is used to investigate the sensitivity of (I) a set of quantities that are sensitive to both initial- and final-state effects as well as (II) a set of dimensionless quantities that are more sensitive to initial-state effects to the Uranium nuclei quadrupole shape deformation. We find that the combined use of the flow harmonics, flow fluctuations and correlations, linear and non-linear flow correlations to the quadrangular flow harmonic, and the correlations between elliptic flow and the mean-transverse momentum could serve to constrain the nuclear deformation of the Uranium nuclei. Therefore, a comprehensive set of measurements of such observables can provide a quantifying tool for the quadrupole shape deformation via data-model comparisons.

## Key claim

The combined use of several flow observables, including flow harmonics, flow fluctuations and correlations, linear and non-linear flow correlations to the quadrangular flow harmonic, and correlations between elliptic flow and mean-transverse momentum, could serve to constrain the quadrupole shape deformation of Uranium nuclei in U+U collisions, but the AMPT model calculations show poor quantitative agreement with experimental measurements, indicating that data-model comparisons are needed for quantification.

## Method

The study uses the AMPT model with string melting option to simulate U+U collisions at nucleon-nucleon center-of-mass energy 193 GeV, with the nucleon density distribution parametrized as a deformed Woods-Saxon profile. The multiparticle azimuthal cumulant method with two-subevent technique (pseudorapidity gap |Δη| > 0.7) is used to compute flow harmonics v_n{k}, normalized symmetric cumulants NSC(2,3), linear and non-linear contributions to v_4, the coupling constant χ_{4,22}, the symmetry plane correlation ρ_{4,22}, and the correlation coefficient ρ(v_2^2, ⟨p_T⟩) for charged hadrons with 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c and |η| < 1.0.

## Key numbers

- Collision energy: √s_NN = 193 GeV for U+U collisions
- Four AMPT parameter sets used: Set-1 (β_2 = 0.00, β_4 = 0.000), Set-2 (β_2 = 0.00, β_4 = 0.200), Set-3 (β_2 = 0.28, β_4 = 0.093), Set-4 (β_2 = 0.40, β_4 = 0.093)
- Transverse momentum range: 0.2 < p_T < 2.0 GeV/c
- Pseudorapidity acceptance: |η| < 1.0
- Fixed AMPT parameters: α_s = 0.47, μ = 3.41 fm^{-1}, Woods-Saxon skin depth a = 0.44
- v_2{2} shows sizable increase with β_2, while v_2{4} and v_2{6} show less sensitivity; v_2{k} shows no sensitivity to β_4
- The ratio v_2{4}/v_2{2} decreases with increasing β_2, indicating increased elliptic flow fluctuations

## Context

This work extends prior investigations of nuclear deformation effects in heavy-ion collisions, including studies comparing Au+Au and U+U collisions (Adamczyk 2015) and Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions (ALICE 2022, ALICE 2018). The study builds on findings that elliptic and triangular flow coefficients are linearly related to initial-state eccentricities (Song 2010, Niemi 2012, Gardim 2014), and that ρ(v_2^2, ⟨p_T⟩) shows sensitivity to nuclear deformation (Giacalone 2019, Bozek 2016, Schenke 2020). The results enable future data-model comparisons using comprehensive sets of flow observables to quantitatively constrain the quadrupole deformation of Uranium nuclei, though the AMPT model shows poor quantitative agreement with experimental data from STAR (2019, 2022).
