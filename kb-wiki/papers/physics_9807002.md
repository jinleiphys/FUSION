# Super Shell Structure of the Magnetic Susceptibility

> The magnetic susceptibility of electrons confined to a spherical cavity or a circular billiard shows slow oscillations as a function of the number of electrons, which are a new manifestation of the Super Shell Structure found in the free energy of metal clusters. The relationship of the oscillations of the two different quantities is analyzed by means of semiclassical calculations, which are in quantitative agreement with quantal results.

## Key claim

The magnetic susceptibility of electrons confined to a spherical cavity or circular billiard exhibits slow oscillations (Super Shell Structure) as a function of electron number, which should survive ensemble averaging over size and shape fluctuations that wash out the basic shell oscillations, provided the canonical ensemble is used and the temperature is in an intermediate range.

## Method

The authors used Strutinsky's shell correction method to numerically separate the shell contribution to the magnetic susceptibility from quantal single-particle levels in a spherical cavity and a circular billiard. They also applied semiclassical Periodic Orbit Theory (POT) to express the shell contributions as sums over classical periodic orbits, primarily the triangle and square orbits, and compared the semiclassical results with the quantal calculations. The canonical ensemble was enforced by solving for the chemical potential at fixed particle number, rather than using the grand canonical approximation.

## Key numbers

- For a spherical cavity with parameters appropriate for sodium (Fermi energy 3.24 eV, temperature 170 K = 0.005 epsilon_F), the shell contribution to the susceptibility oscillates with an amplitude of several hundred Landau units (LU), where 1 LU = 0.2715 M N r_S^2.
- Averaging over a Gaussian distribution in N^{1/3} of width Delta N^{1/3} = 0.75 damps out the basic shell oscillations but leaves the Super Shell Structure visible.
- For a circular quantum dot with radius 500 nm, effective mass 0.067 M_e, and temperature 0.13 K, the susceptibility shows a distinct Super Shell Structure; averaging over N^{1/2} with a Gaussian width Delta N^{1/2} = 1.6 (10% spread in N for N=1000) destroys only the basic shell structure.
- For the spherical cavity at T = 0.05 epsilon_F, the amplitude of basic shell oscillations remains below 5 LU for N < 100.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Super Shell Structure, previously found in the free energy and level density of metal clusters by Balian and Bloch (1972) and Nishioka, Hansen, and Mottelson (1990), to the magnetic susceptibility. The authors show that the susceptibility's Super Shell Structure arises from the same interference between triangular and square periodic orbits that produces the beat pattern in the free energy, but with different weighting of longer orbits. The results enable experimental detection of Super Shell Structure in ensembles of ballistic quantum dots (as studied by Levy et al., 1993; Ullmo, Richter, and Jalabert, 1995) and metal clusters, where only a moderate mass selection (about 30% resolution) would be needed rather than the high resolution required to resolve individual shell oscillations.
