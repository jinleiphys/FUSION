# Astrophysical constraints on the cold equation of state of the strongly interacting matter

> At present, the only experimental access to the properties of cold, dense strongly interacting matter is provided by astrophysical observations. Neutron stars are the only known systems in the Universe that reach densities several times higher than normal nuclear density at nearly zero temperature, making them unique laboratories for studying dense matter. Since most neutron star observables are sensitive to the equation of state (EOS), observational data place stringent constraints on the EOS of strongly interacting matter. In this work, we investigate constraints arising from perturbative QCD calculations at asymptotically high densities ($ρ\approx 40 ρ_0$), the mass of the heaviest observed neutron star (a black widow pulsar), NICER mass-radius measurements, and the tidal deformability inferred from the binary neutron star merger GW170817. We parametrize the EOS and allow its parameters to vary freely, using observational data to constrain the admissible parameter space. We find that neutron star observations significantly restrict the EOS of dense strongly interacting matter. While NICER has already provided measurements for five pulsars, the associated uncertainties remain relatively large. Within our modeling framework, we find that the existence of very massive neutron stars and constraints on the tidal deformability provide the most restrictive constraints on the EOS.

## Key claim
Neutron star observations significantly restrict the equation of state of cold dense strongly interacting matter, but the most restrictive constraints come from the existence of very massive neutron stars and tidal deformability measurements, while NICER mass-radius measurements have relatively large uncertainties that limit their constraining power.

## Method
The authors construct a unified equation of state by polynomially interpolating between a hadronic relativistic mean-field model (SFHo) at low densities and a quark-meson model based on U(3)xU(3) chiral symmetry at high densities, with a crust equation of state attached below approximately 0.5 nuclear saturation density. They perform Bayesian inference over three free parameters (vector coupling g_V, central transition density, and transition width) using approximately 10,000 sampled equations of state, incorporating constraints from perturbative QCD at asymptotically high densities, the minimum maximum neutron star mass, NICER mass-radius measurements for five pulsars, tidal deformability from GW170817, and optional constraints from HESS J1731-347 and GW190814.

## Key numbers
- Perturbative QCD reference point: pressure p = 3823 MeV/fm^3 at chemical potential mu = 2.6 GeV and density rho = 6.47 1/fm^3 (approximately 40 times nuclear saturation density rho_0)
- Minimum maximum neutron star mass adopted: M_TOV^min = 2.22 solar masses at 1-sigma confidence level, based on the black widow pulsar PSR J0952-0607
- NICER results for PSR J0030+0451: mass 1.4 (+0.13/-0.12) solar masses, equatorial radius 11.71 (+0.88/-0.83) km
- NICER results for PSR J0740+6620: mass 2.0073 +/- 0.069 solar masses, radius 12.49 (+1.28/-0.88) km
- NICER results for PSR J0614-3329: mass 1.44 (+0.06/-0.07) solar masses, equatorial radius 10.29 (+1.01/-0.86) km
- NICER results for PSR J1231-1411: mass 1.04 (+0.05/-0.03) solar masses, equatorial radius 13.5 (+0.3/-0.5) km
- NICER results for PSR J0437-4715: mass 1.418 +/- 0.037 solar masses, equatorial radius 11.36 (+0.95/-0.63) km
- Tidal deformability upper bound from GW170817: Lambda_tilde < 720 (conservative, EOS-agnostic analysis)
- More stringent tidal deformability bounds: 70 < Lambda_1.4 < 580 and 9.1 km < R_1.4 < 12.8 km at 90% confidence level
- Most probable parameter ranges: central transition density 4.6 to 5.0 rho_0, transition width 2.8 to 3.0 rho_0, vector coupling g_V between 4 and 6
- From 9261 initial equations of state, only 438 survive after minimum mass and pQCD constraints, and only 133 after adding the Lambda_tilde < 720 constraint

## Context
This work extends the approach of Takatsy (2023) by incorporating the most recent NICER measurements for five pulsars and updated constraints from the black widow pulsar analysis by Romani (2022). The polynomial interpolation method between hadronic and quark matter equations of state follows earlier work by Abgaryan (2018), Baym (2019), and Masuda (2012), while the pQCD consistency condition implements the framework of Komoltsev (2021) and Gorda (2022). The results enable future inclusion of strangeness degrees of freedom and comparison with upcoming measurements from NICA/MPD and FAIR/CBM facilities.
