# Proton-$\rm ^3He$ elastic scattering at intermediate energies

> We present a precise measurement of the cross section, proton and $\rm ^3He$ analyzing powers, and spin correlation coefficient $C_{y,y}$ for $p$-$\rm ^3He$ elastic scattering near 65 MeV, and a comparison with rigorous four-nucleon scattering calculations based on realistic nuclear potentials and a model with $Δ$-isobar excitation. Clear discrepancies are seen in some of the measured observables in the regime around the cross section minimum. Theoretical predictions using scaling relations between the calculated cross section and the $\rm ^3 He$ binding energy are not successful in reproducing the data. Large sensitivity to the $NN$ potentials and rather small $Δ$-isobar effects in the calculated cross section are noticed as different features from those in the deuteron-proton elastic scattering. The results obtained above indicate that $p$-$\rm ^3He$ scattering at intermediate energies is an excellent tool to explore nuclear interactions not accessible by three-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

The paper presents precise measurements of cross section and spin observables for proton-³He elastic scattering near 65-70 MeV, but the theoretical calculations omit the Coulomb force, so the predictions actually refer to the mirror reaction n-³H rather than the measured p-³He system.

## Method

The experiment measured the differential cross section dσ/dΩ and proton analyzing power Ay at 65 MeV using a polarized proton beam from the AVF cyclotron at RCNP, Osaka University, with scattered protons detected by NaI(Tl) scintillator counter telescopes. The ³He analyzing power A0y was measured at 70 MeV using a polarized ³He target based on spin-exchange optical pumping at CYRIC, Tohoku University, and the spin correlation coefficient Cy,y was measured at 65 MeV using both polarized beam and polarized target. The data were compared with rigorous four-nucleon scattering calculations solving the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations using various realistic NN potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, INOY04, chiral N⁴LO potentials) and a CD Bonn+Δ model with Δ-isobar excitation.

## Key numbers

- Cross section measured at 65 MeV over θ_cm = 26.9° to 170.1°, with statistical error better than ±2% and systematic uncertainty of 3%
- Proton analyzing power Ay measured at 65 MeV with statistical error of 0.02 or less and systematic uncertainty of 0.02
- ³He analyzing power A0y measured at 70 MeV over θ_cm = 46.6° to 141.4° with statistical error of 0.02 or less and systematic uncertainty of 0.02 or less
- Spin correlation coefficient Cy,y measured at 65 MeV at three angles (θ_cm = 46.6°, 89.0°, 133.2°) with statistical error of 0.03-0.06
- Beam polarization was typically 53% for the cross section measurement and 50% for spin-up and 20% for spin-down for the Cy,y measurement
- Target polarization was 40% with uncertainty of 2% for the polarized ³He target measurements
- Gradient of the correlation line between calculated cross section and ³He binding energy is approximately 0.3/MeV for p-³He scattering, compared to approximately 0.1/MeV for d-p scattering

## Context

This work extends the sparse data set for p-³He elastic scattering above 50 MeV, where previously only cross section data (Votta, Goldstein, Wesick, Langevin-Joliot) and proton analyzing power data (Wesick) existed, with few data for ³He analyzing power and spin correlation coefficients (Shimizu). The results show clear discrepancies between data and NN-potential-only calculations at the cross section minimum, similar to but larger than those seen in nucleon-deuteron elastic scattering (Sakamoto 1996), where inclusion of three-nucleon forces successfully resolved the discrepancies (Witała 1998, Sakai 2000). The larger NN potential dependence found in p-³He compared to d-p scattering suggests this system can probe nuclear interactions, including three-nucleon forces in the total isospin T=3/2 channel, that are not accessible in three-nucleon scattering.
