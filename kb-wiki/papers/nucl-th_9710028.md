# Signature and Angular Momentum in 3d-Cranked HFB states

> In terms of the exaxt angular momentum projection, properties of the three dimensional cranked HFB (3d-CHFB) states are analyzed quantitatively in the context of the relation between the signature of an intrinsic symmetry and the parity of angular momentum, (-1)^I. We found that the tilted states have favorable features to describe states involved with high-K quantum number and/or odd total angular momentum. This implies that 3d-CHFB can describe properly the backbending phenomena like a "t-band and g-band" crossing, which is suggested in N=106 isotopes.

## Key claim

The three-dimensional cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (3d-CHFB) method, particularly its tilted-axis rotating (TAR) states, contains significantly more odd-\(I\) and high-\(K\) angular momentum components than principal-axis rotating (PAR) states, making TAR states more suitable for describing backbending phenomena involving g-t band crossing, though the signature is only an effective quantum number and not exact enough to distinguish even from odd \(I\) without angular momentum projection.

## Method

The authors performed exact angular momentum projection onto 3d-CHFB intrinsic states generated by a variational calculation with a generalized BCS wave function, including constraints on all three components of the total angular momentum and boost operators to fix the principal axes of quadrupole deformation. They computed probability distributions \(W^I\) (total angular momentum content) and \(w_K^I\) (K-quantum number content) for PAR states, TAR states, and signature-projected TAR states, using 117936 integration points in Euler angle space. The overlap kernels were evaluated using the Onishi-Horibata formula, and the multiplicity \(m(I)\) was calculated from the eigenvalues of the norm matrix \(N^I_{KK'}\).

## Key numbers

- For a PAR state with \(\langle \hat{J}_x \rangle = 13\hbar\), even-\(I\) components occupy \(95\%\) of the wave function.
- For a TAR state with tilting angle \(\theta = 6^\circ\) and total angular momentum \(|\langle \mathbf{J} \rangle| = 13\hbar\), even-\(I\) components are reduced to \(83\%\).
- The sum \(\sum_I W^I\) up to \(I=26\) is 0.96 for \(\langle \hat{J}_x \rangle = 6\hbar\) and 0.86 for \(\langle \hat{J}_x \rangle = 13\hbar\) in PAR states.
- For the signature-projected TAR state with (+)-signature at \(\theta = 6^\circ\), even-\(I\) components occupy \(89\%\).
- For the signature-projected TAR state with (−)-signature at \(\theta = 6^\circ\), even-\(I\) and odd-\(I\) components are mixed evenly at \(50\%\) even-\(I\).
- For \(I=11\): \(W^I = 0.0153\) (TAR), 0.0108 ((+)-signature), 0.0418 ((−)-signature); for \(I=12\): \(W^I = 0.0888\) (TAR), 0.0960 ((+)-signature), 0.0470 ((−)-signature).
- For \(I=13\): \(W^I = 0.0181\) (TAR), 0.0125 ((+)-signature), 0.0511 ((−)-signature); for \(I=14\): \(W^I = 0.0889\) (TAR), 0.0945 ((+)-signature), 0.0555 ((−)-signature).

## Context

This work extends the angular momentum projection studies of Hara, Hayashi and Ring (1982) on PAR-CHFB states to the 3d-CHFB framework, which includes both PAR and TAR states. The analysis is motivated by experimental observations in \(N=106\) isotones (\(^{180}\)W and \(^{182}\)Os) by Walker et al. (1993) suggesting a high-\(K\) band (\(K^\pi = 8^+\)) interacting with the ground-state band and s-band, possibly as a "t-band" from Fermi alignment as proposed by Frauendorf (1981). The results enable quantitative assessment of whether 3d-CHFB states are adequate for describing g-t band crossing and signature splitting phenomena in yrare states, with the conclusion that angular momentum projection is necessary for such studies.
