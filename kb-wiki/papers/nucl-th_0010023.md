# Phase transitions in high energy heavy ion collisions within fluid dynamics

> Recent advances in Fluid Dynamical modeling of heavy ion collisions are presented, with particular attention to mesoscopic systems, QGP formation in the pre FD regime and QGP hadronization coinciding with the final freeze-out.

## Key claim

Fluid dynamics can describe phase transitions in heavy ion collisions even when the system is not in full phase equilibrium, provided local kinetic equilibrium is maintained within each phase; however, the applicability of fluid dynamics is limited when the system freezes out before kinetic or phase equilibrium is established, requiring a non-equilibrium post-freeze-out momentum distribution.

## Method

The authors develop a multi-module model coupling a coherent Yang-Mills field theory for the pre-equilibrium stage to fluid dynamics for the equilibrated QGP phase, and then to a kinetic freeze-out description. For the initial stage, they solve conservation equations for baryon current and energy-momentum in light-cone variables, treating nucleus-nucleus collisions as streak-by-streak interactions with an effective abelian field and string tension parameterization. For freeze-out, they use a kinetic model that yields a non-equilibrium momentum distribution satisfying exact conservation laws at the freeze-out surface, replacing the earlier cut-Jüttner distribution.

## Key numbers

- String tension values are 5–12 GeV/fm for beam energy ε₀ = 100 GeV per nucleon (Au+Au collisions).
- The latent energy density in non-hadronized strings is on average 8 GeV/fm³.
- The dimensionless parameter A in the string tension parameterization is around 0.045–0.055, checked to work in the energy range ε₀ = 10–3000 GeV per nucleon.
- For Au+Au collisions at ε₀ = 100 GeV/nucleon and impact parameter b = 0.5(R₁+R₂), the final QGP volume forms a tilted disk at approximately 45° to the beam axis.

## Context

This work extends earlier fluid dynamical models of heavy ion collisions by addressing the pre-equilibrium stage using a coherent Yang-Mills approach based on Glendenning and Csernai (1986), and improves freeze-out descriptions beyond the Cooper-Frye (1975) formalism by incorporating exact conservation laws and non-equilibrium distributions as initiated by Bugaev (1996). The model predicts a "third flow component" or "antiflow" in semi-central collisions, which may explain observed elliptic flow patterns, and enables future coupling of hadronic string models to fluid dynamics for more realistic freeze-out simulations.
