# The contribution of medium-modified color flow to jet quenching

> Multiple interactions between parton showers and the surrounding QCD matter are expected to underlie the strong medium-modifications of jet observables in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC and at the LHC. Here, we note that such jet-medium interactions alter generically and characteristically the color correlations in the parton shower. We characterize these effects in a color-differential calculation of the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum to first and second order in opacity. By interfacing simple branching histories of medium-modified color flow with the Lund hadronization model, we analyze how the medium modification of color correlations can affect the distribution of hadronic fragments in jets. Importantly, we observe that jet-medium interactions give rise to the medium-induced color decoherence of gluons from the parton shower. Since hadronization respects color flow and since each color singlet in a parton shower is hadronized separately, this medium-induced color decoherence leaves characteristic signatures in the jet fragmentation pattern. In particular, it can contribute to the quenching of leading hadron spectra. Moreover, it can increase strongly the yield of soft hadronic fragments from a jet, while the distribution of more energetic hadrons follows naturally the shape of a vacuum-like fragmentation pattern of lower total energy.

## Key claim

The paper argues that jet-medium interactions in heavy-ion collisions generically alter the color correlations within a parton shower, leading to medium-induced color decoherence of gluons from the projectile. This color decoherence, rather than purely kinematic energy loss, can leave characteristic signatures in jet fragmentation patterns, including enhanced soft hadron yields and a vacuum-like shape for the harder fragments, but the effect is calculated only to first and second order in opacity and is demonstrated using simple branching histories interfaced with the Lund hadronization model.

## Method

The authors performed a color-differential calculation of the medium-induced gluon radiation spectrum to first and second order in opacity, using the BDMPS-Z framework with the Gyulassy-Wang model of static scattering centers. They identified distinct color channels (e.g., Final State Radiation and Initial State Radiation for quarks; six channels for gluons) and computed their relative weights in the large-Nc limit. These color-differential spectra were then interfaced with the Lund string fragmentation model to analyze how medium-modified color flow affects the distribution of hadronic fragments.

## Key numbers

* The color-inclusive Gunion-Bertsch spectrum for a quark projectile is recovered as \(k^+ \frac{dN_g}{d\mathbf{k} dk^+} = C_A \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi^2} \left\langle \frac{\mathbf{q}^2}{\mathbf{k}^2 (\mathbf{k} - \mathbf{q})^2} \right\rangle\), where \(C_A\) is the universal color factor irrespective of projectile type.
* For a quark projectile at \(N=1\) opacity, the two color channels (labeled \(aa_1\) and \(a_1a\)) contribute equally in the soft limit (\(x_g \ll 1\)), each with weight \(\frac{N_c}{2} \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi^2} \left\langle \left[ \mathbf{K}_0 - \mathbf{K}_1 \right]^2 \right\rangle\).
* For a gluon projectile at \(N=1\) opacity, four of the six color channels (FSR/ISR on q or \(\bar{q}\) leg) each contribute \(\frac{N_c}{4} \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi^2} \left\langle \frac{\mathbf{q}^2}{\mathbf{k}^2 (\mathbf{k} - \mathbf{q})^2} \right\rangle\), while the two mixed channels (RqS\(\bar{q}\) and R\(\bar{q}\)Sq) are suppressed by a factor \(O(x_g^2)\) in the soft limit.
* The vacuum radiation spectrum for a quark is \(k^+ \frac{d\sigma^{\rm vac}}{dk^+ d\mathbf{k}} = d\sigma^{\rm hard} C_R \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi^2} \mathbf{K}_0^2\), with \(\mathbf{K}_0^2 = \mathbf{k}^2 / (\mathbf{k}^2 + x_g^2 M^2)^2\) showing the dead-cone effect for massive quarks.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z parton energy loss framework (Baier, Dokshitzer, Mueller, Peigne, Schiff, 1996; Zakharov, 1997) by calculating the color-differential radiation spectrum, going beyond the standard color-inclusive approach. It builds on earlier heuristic models of medium-modified hadronization (Sapeta, 2007; Aurenche, 2011) and Monte Carlo studies (Zapp, 2008) by providing a QCD-based calculation of how color flow is altered. The results enable future studies of jet fragmentation patterns as probes of medium properties, particularly the possibility that medium-induced color decoherence could explain enhanced soft hadron yields and the quenching of leading hadron spectra without requiring large energy loss.
