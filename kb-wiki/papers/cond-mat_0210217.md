# Atomic-Molecular Condensates with Large Positive Scattering Length

> We show that in the limit of large and positive atom--atom scattering length the properties of an atomic--molecular Bose--Einstein Condensate (amBEC) are determined by an universal energy density functional (EDF). We find that the optimal conditions for the formation of a stable amBEC are in the regime where there are no shallow trimers and the atom--dimer scattering length is negative and comparable in magnitude with the atom--atom scattering length. At temperatures lower than $T_c$ the chemical potentials for the atoms and molecules can be specified independently. Besides three--body recombinations processes into dimers of large size, inelastic processes involving the formation of deeply bound small size molecular states are possible. These inelastic processes do not lead to an efficient heating of the amBEC and can be used for its mostly non--destructive monitoring.

## Key claim

In the limit of large and positive atom-atom scattering length (a >> r0) and low density (na^3 << 1), the properties of an atomic-molecular Bose-Einstein Condensate (amBEC) are determined by a universal energy density functional (EDF) that depends only on the scattering length a and a three-body parameter a3', but the optimal conditions for a stable amBEC require the atom-dimer scattering length to be negative and comparable in magnitude to a, with no shallow trimers present.

## Method

The authors derived an effective Hamiltonian (Ham) that explicitly includes both atoms and dimers as separate fields, with coupling constants λaa, λam, and λmm determined by matching to the full microscopic Hamiltonian (Ha) via solutions of the Lippmann-Schwinger, Fadeev, and Yakubovski equations for two-, three-, and four-particle scattering. The leading-order energy density functional was then obtained from a mean-field approximation of Ham, which is valid in the regime ka << 1 where perturbation theory for Ha would fail. The atom-dimer scattering length λam was expressed in terms of the Efimov parameter s0 ≈ 1.00624 and the three-body scale a3'.

## Key numbers

- The dimer binding energy is ε2 = -ħ²/ma², with the dimer radius approximately a.
- The universal constant s0 ≈ 1.00624, with c1 ≈ 1.46 and c2 ≈ 2.15 in the expression for the atom-dimer scattering length.
- The three-body recombination rate for A+A+A → A+A2 is proportional to ħ a⁴ n_a² / m.
- The critical temperature Tc is proportional to (na³)^(2/3) ħ²/ma², which is parametrically much smaller than the dimer binding energy |ε2| = ħ²/ma².
- The trimer ground state energy near threshold is ε3 ≈ ε2 - 3ħ²/4ma_am².

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Efimov physics (Efimov 1970, 1971) and Feshbach resonance manipulation (Feshbach 1958) to describe mixed atomic-molecular condensates in the universal regime of large positive scattering length. It contrasts with earlier work on Bosenova (experimental) and previous theoretical treatments of amBEC (amBEC_t, eddy, heizen) by identifying a regime where the EDF is universal and the chemical potentials for atoms and molecules can be specified independently at low temperatures. The results enable the design of experiments to create stable amBECs and suggest that inelastic processes producing fast, weakly-interacting ejectiles could be used for non-destructive monitoring, analogous to neutrino detection in solar physics.
