# Gamow-Teller strength distribution in proton-rich nucleus $^{57}$Zn and its implications in astrophysics

> Gamow-Teller (GT) transitions play a preeminent role in the collapse of stellar core in the stages leading to a Type-II supernova. The B(GT) strength distributions for ground and excited states of $^{57}$Zn are calculated in the domain of proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-QRPA) theory. No experimental insertions were made (as usually made in other pn-QRPA calculations of B(GT) strength function) to check the performance of the model for proton-rich nuclei. The calculated ground-state B(GT) strength distribution is in good agreement with measurements and shows differences with the earlier reported shell model calculation. The pn-QRPA model reproduced the measured low-lying strength for $^{57}$Zn better in comparison to the KB3G interaction used in the large-scale shell model calculation. The stellar weak rates are sensitive to the location and structure of these low-lying states in daughter $^{57}$Cu. The primary mechanism for producing such nuclei is the rp-process and is believed to be important in the dynamics of the collapsing supermassive stars. Small changes in the binding and excitation energies can lead to significant modifications of the predictions for the synthesis of proton rich isotopes. The $β^{+}$-decay and electron capture (EC) rates on $^{57}$Zn are compared to the seminal work of Fuller, Fowler and Newman (FFN). The pn-QRPA calculated $β^{+}$-decay rates are generally in good agreement with the FFN calculation. However at high stellar temperatures the calculated $β^{+}$-decay rates are almost half of FFN rates. On the other hand, for rp-process conditions, the calculated electron capture ($β^{+}$-decay) rates are bigger than FFN rates by more than a factor 2 (1.5) and may have interesting astrophysical consequences.

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA model reproduces the measured low-lying Gamow-Teller strength for $^{57}$Zn better than the KB3G interaction used in large-scale shell model calculations, but the total summed B(GT) strength within the Q-window is larger than both the experimental extraction and the shell model prediction, which affects stellar weak rates at high densities.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-QRPA) theory with a model space of seven major shells ($7\hbar\omega$) to calculate the Gamow-Teller strength distributions from the ground state and 259 excited states of $^{57}$Zn, covering excitation energies up to around 30 MeV. No experimental insertions were made in the calculation. The resulting B(GT) strength functions were used to compute $\beta^{+}$-decay and electron capture rates on a fine grid of stellar temperatures and densities, incorporating a quenching factor of 0.8 for the pf-shell.

## Key numbers

- Summed B(GT) of 1.86 from pn-QRPA within the excitation energy interval 0–7 MeV in daughter $^{57}$Cu, compared to 1.25 from experiment (Jokinen 2002) and 1.68 from the shell model with KB3G interaction.
- Within the full Q-window (Q-value 14.51 MeV for $^{57}$Zn), pn-QRPA extracted a total GT strength of 15.23, while the KB3G shell model calculation gave 8.63.
- Calculated half-life for $\beta^{+}$-decay on $^{57}$Zn at T$_{9}$ = 0.01 and $\rho Y_{e}$ = 10 gcm$^{-3}$ is 39.6 ms, in excellent agreement with the measured value of 40 ms (Vieira 1976).
- At high stellar temperatures (T$_{9}$ = 30), the pn-QRPA $\beta^{+}$-decay rates are almost half of the FFN (Fuller, Fowler, Newman) rates.
- For typical peak rp-process conditions (T$_{9}$ ~ 3, $\rho$ ~ 10$^{7}$ gcm$^{-3}$), the pn-QRPA calculated $\beta^{+}$-decay rates are bigger than FFN rates by a factor of 1.5, and electron capture rates are bigger by a factor of 2.
- At $\rho Y_{e}$ = 10$^{11}$ gcm$^{-3}$, the calculated electron capture rates are bigger than the competing $\beta^{+}$-decay rates by more than four orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the pn-QRPA calculations of weak interaction rates to the proton-rich nucleus $^{57}$Zn, which is important for the rp-process in supermassive stars and x-ray bursts. The results are compared to the seminal weak rate calculations of Fuller, Fowler, and Newman (FFN, 1982) and to experimental data from Jokinen (2002). The pn-QRPA model reproduces the low-lying GT strength better than the large-scale shell model calculation using the KB3G interaction (Poves 2001), and the calculated weak rates show significant differences from FFN rates under rp-process conditions, which may affect core-collapse supernova simulations and nucleosynthesis predictions for proton-rich isotopes.
