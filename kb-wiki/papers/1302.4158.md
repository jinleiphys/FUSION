# Radiative capture reactions in lattice effective field theory

> We outline a general method for computing nuclear capture reactions on the lattice. The method consists of two major parts. In this study we detail the second part which consists of calculating an effective two-body capture reaction on the lattice at finite volume. We solve this problem by calculating the two-point Green's function using an infrared regulator and the capture amplitude to a two-body bound state. We demonstrate the details of this method by calculating on the lattice the leading M1 contribution to the radiative neutron capture on proton at low energies using pionless effective field theory. We find good agreement with exact continuum results.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general method for computing radiative capture reactions on the lattice, but the demonstration is limited to the second part of the method: calculating an effective two-body capture reaction at finite volume using an infrared regulator and extrapolation to zero regulator strength. The authors show that this approach reproduces continuum results for the leading M1 contribution to neutron-proton capture to within 2% for magnitude and 4% for phase angle.

## Method

The authors separate the calculation into two parts: first, projection Monte Carlo determines a multi-channel adiabatic lattice Hamiltonian for the participating nuclei; second, this adiabatic Hamiltonian is used to calculate the capture reaction via a two-point retarded Green's function. For the demonstration, they use pionless effective field theory at leading order on a cubic lattice with nearest-neighbor hopping and single-site contact interactions, tuning the triplet-channel coupling to produce a deuteron bound state at -2.225 MeV and using Lüscher's finite volume method to set the singlet-channel scattering length to -23.71 fm. They introduce an infrared regulator parameter epsilon (or delta = epsilon M p^2) to exponentially suppress finite-volume errors, then extrapolate linearly to the epsilon = 0 limit.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy: -2.225 MeV (used to tune triplet-channel coupling)
- Neutron-proton scattering length in spin-singlet channel: a = -23.71 fm (tuned via Lüscher's method)
- Deuteron binding momentum: gamma = 45.7 MeV
- Isovector nucleon magnetic moment: kappa_1 = 2.35
- Nucleon mass: M = 939 MeV
- Lattice spacing: b = 1/100 MeV^{-1} for scattering momenta p < 40 MeV, b = 1/200 MeV^{-1} for p > 40 MeV
- Box sizes up to L = 240b
- Extrapolated lattice results agree with continuum magnitude |M| to within 2% and phase angle phi to within 4%

## Context

This work extends lattice effective field theory methods from bound-state calculations to radiative capture reactions, building on prior lattice EFT work reviewed in Lee (2008) and the wavefunction projection technique developed in Epelbaum et al. (2012). The method is demonstrated on the well-studied p(n,gamma)d reaction previously calculated in continuum pionless EFT by Chen and Rupak (1999) and Rupak (1999). The approach enables future ab initio lattice calculations of photonuclear reactions for halo nuclei such as 14C(n,gamma)15C and more complex systems like alpha capture on carbon-12 or oxygen-16 in helium burning.
