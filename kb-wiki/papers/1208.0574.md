# Exploring the different phase diagrams of Strong Interactions

> In-medium field-theory is applied to different effective models and QCD to describe mass and isospin effects, finite volume corrections and magnetic fields in the phase diagram of Strong Interactions, keeping close contact with experiments and lattice results. Findings range from a technical nonperturbative solution of the hot and dense regime of a general massive Yukawa Theory and the computation of the cold and dense chiral surface tension -- a key quantity for supernovae explosions and compact star structure -- to the proposal of a novel signature for the QCD critical endpoint in heavy-ion collisions based on finite-size scaling. The behavior of the deconfining critical temperature as a function of the pion mass and the isospin chemical potential is also addressed in an effective model and the description obtained is in agreement with lattice simulations, in contrast to what is found in (Polyakov-extended) chiral models. We also discuss the thermodynamics of QCD in the presence of a (Abelian) magnetic field to two-loop order and a Functional-Renormalization-Group analysis of relativistic Bose-Einstein condensation of pions in isospin-dense media.

## Key claim

The paper argues that finite-size effects in heavy-ion collisions (HICs) produce pseudocritical behavior that is significantly shifted from the genuine critical endpoint (CEP) in the thermodynamic limit, meaning that experimental signatures of the CEP will probe shifted and smoothened quantities rather than the true critical singularity.

## Method

The author employs the linear sigma model coupled to two-flavor quarks (Nf=2) as an effective chiral model, replacing continuum momentum integrals with discrete sums over momentum states to incorporate finite volume effects for system sizes between 2 fm and 10 fm. The pseudocritical phase diagram is computed for both periodic boundary conditions (PBC) and antiperiodic boundary conditions (APC), and the resulting shifts in transition lines, critical endpoint location, crossover temperature, and isentropic trajectories are compared to the thermodynamic limit.

## Key numbers

- The typical critical temperature for deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration from lattice QCD is Tc ~ 150 MeV, giving a critical size scale Lc ~ 1.3 fm
- For PBC at L=2 fm, the crossover transition temperature at μ=0 increases by up to ~80% relative to the thermodynamic limit
- For PBC, the pseudocritical endpoint chemical potential μ_CEP is about 30% larger than in the thermodynamic limit
- Typical linear sizes in HICs are L ≲ 10-15 fm (ion diameter), corresponding to energies L^{-1} ≳ 13.1-19.7 MeV
- The primordial universe during the QCD transition had size L_univ ~ 10^18 fm, essentially in the thermodynamic limit

## Context

This work extends finite-size scaling (FSS) methods from lattice QCD and condensed matter physics (Goldenfeld, Binder and Landau) to the phenomenology of heavy-ion collisions, addressing a gap where most effective model and lattice predictions are made in the thermodynamic limit. It builds on prior studies of finite-volume effects in chiral perturbation theory (Damgaard 2008), the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and color superconductivity (Yamamoto 2009), while contradicting the implicit assumption that finite-size corrections are negligible for HIC observables. The results enable a proposed alternative signature for the CEP based on finite-size scaling analysis of HIC data from different centrality classes, as previously suggested by Palhares (2009) and further developed for RHIC applications.
