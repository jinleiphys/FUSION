# Hartree-Fock emulators for nuclei: Application to charge radii of $^{48,52}$Ca

> Understanding the emergence of complex structures of nuclei from chiral effective field theory (EFT) is a central challenge. The large number of low-energy couplings (LECs) in the EFT expansion and the significant cost of $\textit{ab initio}$ many-body calculations render large-scale sensitivity studies of many-body observables computationally prohibitive, necessitating the use of emulators as low-cost surrogates. In this work, we study a Hartree-Fock emulator based on eigenvector continuation to investigate trends in nuclear charge radii of neutron-rich calcium isotopes. We systematically vary the five LECs entering the leading three-nucleon (3N) interactions, and demonstrate the precision of the emulator through cross-validation over a wide parameter space. Our findings indicate that large charge radius increase from $^{48}$Ca to $^{52}$Ca is likely not explained by variations of the leading 3N couplings. This suggests that other effects, such as sensitivities to chiral two-nucleon interactions or neglected many-body effects, e.g., associated with nuclear collectivity, play an important role.

## Key claim

The large measured increase in charge radius from ⁴⁸Ca to ⁵²Ca (0.530 fm²) is likely not explained by variations of the five leading three-nucleon (3N) low-energy couplings (LECs) within chiral effective field theory, at least at the Hartree-Fock level; the authors suggest that sensitivities to chiral two-nucleon interactions or neglected many-body effects such as nuclear collectivity must play an important role.

## Method

The authors construct an eigenvector continuation (EC) emulator using Hartree-Fock (HF) training vectors (Slater determinants) to approximate the full many-body solution at low computational cost. They systematically vary the five 3N LECs (c₁, c₃, c₄, c_D, c_E) over wide ranges using latin hypercube sampling, evaluate the Hamiltonian and operator kernels via transition density matrices and the Thouless theorem, and cross-validate the emulated ground-state energies and charge radii against explicit HF calculations. For the most promising LEC solutions, in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) calculations are also performed.

## Key numbers

- The experimental charge radius increase from ⁴⁸Ca to ⁵²Ca is δ⟨R_ch²⟩⁴⁸·⁵² = 0.530(5) fm² (COLLAPS collaboration, Ruiz16Calcium).
- The maximal value of δ⟨R_ch²⟩⁴⁸·⁵² observed across all 3N LEC variations in the emulator sample set is about 0.32 fm², well below the experimental value.
- The emulator cross-validation shows maximum relative errors of about 1% for ground-state energies and 2% for charge radii over the full LEC sample set; when restricting to interactions satisfying energy and radius constraints, errors do not exceed a few per mille.
- The HF calculations use a harmonic oscillator basis with e_max = 12 (13 major shells), oscillator frequency ℏω = 16 MeV, and 3N truncation E_3max = 16.
- The 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction uses SRG evolution to λ_SRG = 1.8 fm⁻¹ with 3N regulator cutoff Λ_3N = 2.0 fm⁻¹; the nominal LEC values are c₁ = -0.81 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -3.2 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 5.4 GeV⁻¹, c_D = 1.264, c_E = -0.12.
- The LEC variation intervals are: c₃ ∈ [-6.2, -0.2] GeV⁻¹, c₄ ∈ [0.4, 10.4] GeV⁻¹, c_D ∈ [-5, 10], c_E ∈ [-2, 2], with c₁ fixed at -0.81 GeV⁻¹.
- The training set contains N_t = 100 points; the regularized EC subspace dimension is reduced to approximately 60-80 after singular value cutoff s_min = 10⁻⁶.
- For the SRG-evolved N³LO EMN 450 interaction, 37% of emulated interactions meet the energy and radius constraints; for the unevolved case, only about 1% meet the constraints.

## Context

This work extends the application of eigenvector continuation emulators (Frame 2018, Duguet 2025) to Hartree-Fock-level studies of nuclear charge radii, following the approach of Sun (2025) for multiscale sensitivity studies. It addresses the long-standing puzzle that ab initio calculations using various chiral NN+3N interactions fail to reproduce the large charge radius increase from ⁴⁸Ca to ⁵²Ca measured by the COLLAPS collaboration (Ruiz16Calcium). The results indicate that varying only the leading 3N couplings cannot resolve this discrepancy, pointing toward the need to explore sensitivities to chiral two-nucleon interactions or many-body correlation effects beyond the HF level, such as those associated with nuclear collectivity.
