# Developing predictions for pion fragmentation functions

> Exploiting crossing symmetry, the hadron scale pion valence quark distribution function is used to predict the kindred elementary valence quark fragmentation function (FF). This function defines the kernel of a quark jet fragmentation equation, which is solved to obtain the full pion FFs. After evolution to a scale typical of FF fits to data, the results for quark FFs are seen to compare favourably with such fits. However, the gluon FF is markedly different. Notably, although FF evolution equations do not themselves guarantee momentum conservation, inclusion of a gluon FF which, for four quark flavours, distributes roughly 11% of the total light-front momentum fraction, is sufficient to restore momentum conservation under evolution. Overall, significant uncertainty is attached to FFs determined via fits to data; hence, the features of the predictions described herein could potentially provide useful guidance for future such studies.

## Key claim

Using crossing symmetry (the Drell-Levy-Yan relation) and a hadron-scale pion valence quark distribution function from continuum Schwinger function methods, the authors predict the full pion fragmentation functions; however, the gluon fragmentation function is markedly different from existing fits, and the evolution equations for fragmentation functions do not themselves guarantee momentum conservation, requiring a gluon fragmentation function that distributes roughly 11% of the total light-front momentum fraction to restore it.

## Method

The authors first use the Drell-Levy-Yan relation to convert a realistic hadron-scale pion valence quark distribution function into an elementary valence quark fragmentation function. This elementary function defines the kernel of a Volterra integral equation (the quark jet fragmentation equation), which is solved to obtain the full singlet and nonsinglet pion fragmentation functions at the hadron scale. The results are then evolved to a scale of 1 GeV using an all-orders evolution scheme, with a gluon fragmentation function introduced at the hadron scale to ensure momentum conservation under evolution.

## Key numbers

- The hadron scale is predicted to be ζ_H = 0.331(2) GeV, deriving from the behaviour of the process-independent charge.
- For four quark flavours, the gluon fragmentation function must distribute roughly 11% of the total light-front momentum fraction at the hadron scale to restore momentum conservation under evolution.
- After evolution to ζ_1 = 1 GeV, the singlet quark fragmentation function carries a momentum fraction of 0.78, the gluon fragmentation function carries 0.11, the s + sbar fragmentation function carries 0.095, and the c + cbar fragmentation function carries 0.011.
- The contact interaction input yields a gluon momentum fraction of 0.11 at the hadron scale and 0.11 after evolution to 1 GeV, with the singlet quark fraction dropping from 0.89 to 0.78.
- The HKNS fits yield ⟨z⟩ for the sum of u and ubar fragmentation functions at 1 GeV of 0.80(9) and for the gluon fragmentation function of 0.115(111).

## Context

This work extends the all-orders evolution scheme developed by Yin (2023) and the continuum Schwinger function method calculations of pion valence quark distributions by Cui (2020) to the prediction of fragmentation functions. It provides the first realistic theoretical predictions for pion fragmentation functions from first-principles-inspired methods, which can guide future global fits such as those by Hirai (2007) and Bertone (2017), which currently have large uncertainties, particularly for the gluon fragmentation function. The approach enables predictions for other hadron systems and reveals that the seeds of confinement expressed in hadronisation are already present in the wave functions of the hadrons involved.
