# Azimuthal jet flavor tomography with CUJET2.0 of nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC

> A perturbative QCD based jet tomographic Monte Carlo model, CUJET2.0, is presented to predict jet quenching observables in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC/BNL and LHC/CERN energies. This model generalizes the DGLV theory of flavor dependent radiative energy loss by including multi-scale running strong coupling effects. It generalizes CUJET1.0 by computing jet path integrations though more realistic 2+1D transverse and longitudinally expanding viscous hydrodynamical fields contrained by fits to low $p_T$ flow data. The CUJET2.0 output depends on three control parameters, $(α_{max},f_E,f_M)$, corresponding to an assumed upper bound on the vacuum running coupling in the infrared and two chromo-electric and magnetic QGP screening mass scales $(f_E μ(T), f_M μ(T))$ where $μ(T)$ is the 1-loop Debye mass. We compare numerical results as a function of $α_{max}$ for pure and deformed HTL dynamically enhanced scattering cases corresponding to $(f_E=1,2, f_M=0)$ to data of the nuclear modification factor, $R^f_{AA}(p_T,ϕ; \sqrt{s}, b)$ for jet fragment flavors $f=π,D, B, e$ at $\sqrt{s}=0.2-2.76$ ATeV c.m. energies per nucleon pair and with impact parameter $b=2.4, 7.5$ fm. A $χ^2$ analysis is presented and shows that $R^π_{AA}$ data from RHIC and LHC are consistent with CUJET2.0 at the $χ^2/d.o.f< 2$ level for $α_{max}=0.23-0.30$. The corresponding $\hat{q}(E_{jet}, T)/T^3$ effective jet transport coefficient field of this model is computed to facilitate comparison to other jet tomographic models in the literature. The predicted elliptic asymmetry, $v_2(p_T;\sqrt{s},b)$ is, however, found to significantly underestimated relative to RHIC and LHC data. We find the $χ^2_{v_2}$ analysis shows that $v_2$ is very sensitive to allowing even as little as 10\% variations of the path averaged $α_{max}$ along in and out of reaction plane paths.

## Key claim
The CUJET2.0 model, which includes multi-scale running strong coupling effects and 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic backgrounds, can reproduce the pion nuclear modification factor R_AA data from RHIC and LHC with chi-squared per degree of freedom below 2 for alpha_max between 0.23 and 0.30, but significantly underestimates the elliptic flow v_2 data at both colliders.

## Method
The authors extended the DGLV opacity series formalism for flavor-dependent radiative energy loss by implementing multi-scale running of the strong coupling constant alpha_s with an infrared cutoff alpha_max, and replaced the static Glauber plus Bjorken expansion background of CUJET1.0 with 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic temperature fields constrained by low p_T flow data. The model computes jet path integrations through these evolving media, with three control parameters (alpha_max, f_E, f_M) governing the running coupling upper bound and the chromo-electric and magnetic screening mass scales, and compares predictions to nuclear modification factor R_AA and elliptic asymmetry v_2 data for pion, D meson, B meson, and non-photonic electron flavors.

## Key numbers
- The chi-squared analysis shows R^pi_AA data from RHIC and LHC are consistent with CUJET2.0 at the chi^2/d.o.f. < 2 level for alpha_max = 0.23-0.30.
- The model was compared to data at sqrt(s) = 0.2-2.76 ATeV center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair and impact parameters b = 2.4 and 7.5 fm.
- To compensate 50% decreased running scales Q_i, alpha_max needed to be 25% lower; to compensate 25% increased running scales Q_i, alpha_max needed to be 50% higher.
- The fixed coupling case shows energy loss index a ~ 1/3-1/4, while the all-running coupling case shows a(E) approximately 0.
- The Woods-Saxon parameters used for Au (RHIC) are R = 6.37 fm, a = 0.535 fm, sigma_in = 42 mb; for Pb (LHC) are R = 6.48 fm, a = 0.535 fm, sigma_in = 63 mb.

## Context
This work extends the CUJET1.0 model (Buzzatti, 2012) which solved the "heavy quark puzzle" by including dynamical magnetic scattering effects, but failed to reproduce the "surprising transparency" of QGP at LHC energies (Horowitz, 2011) due to fixed coupling assumptions. CUJET2.0 generalizes the DGLV theory (Djordjevic, 2003) and WHDG model by incorporating multi-scale running coupling and realistic 2+1D viscous hydrodynamic fields from the JET Collaboration depository, enabling systematic comparison of azimuthally averaged R_AA and its elliptic moment v_2. The model enables future tests of event-by-event fluctuating hydro grids and provides a framework for comparing pQCD-based jet tomography with holographic approaches.
