# Effect of quark-mass variation on big bang nucleosynthesis

> We calculate the effect of variation in the light-current quark mass, $m_q$, on standard big bang nucleosynthesis. A change in $m_q$ at during the era of nucleosynthesis affects nuclear reaction rates, and hence primordial abundances, via changes the binding energies of light nuclei. It is found that a relative variation of $δm_q/m_q = 0.016 \pm 0.005$ provides better agreement between observed primordial abundances and those predicted by theory. This is largely due to resolution of the existing discrepancies for 7Li. However this method ignores possible changes in the position of resonances in nuclear reactions. The predicted 7Li abundance has a strong dependence on the cross-section of the resonant reactions 3He(d,p)4He and t(d,n)4He. We show that changes in $m_q$ at the time of BBN could shift the position of these resonances away from the Gamow window and lead to an increased production of 7Li, exacerbating the lithium problem.

## Key claim

A relative variation in the light-current quark mass of δm_q/m_q = 0.016 ± 0.005 during the big bang nucleosynthesis era improves agreement between predicted and observed primordial abundances, primarily by resolving the 7Li discrepancy. However, this conclusion is threatened when shifts in the positions of resonances in the reactions 3He(d,p)4He and t(d,n)4He are taken into account, as such shifts could increase 7Li production and exacerbate the lithium problem.

## Method

The authors used a modified Kawano BBN code with updated reaction rates from the NACRE collaboration and cross-section fits from Cyburt (2004), including the recently measured 3He(α,γ)7Be reaction. They calculated the dependence of nuclear binding energies on the light-quark mass using K factors from Flambaum (2007, 2009) based on AV18+UIX nuclear Hamiltonians and Dyson-Schwinger equation calculations, then propagated these changes through the full BBN reaction network to determine primordial abundances. For the resonant reactions, they estimated the shift in resonance positions by assuming the resonant states of 5Li and 5He vary similarly to their ground states, and also considered an averaged sensitivity value.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit quark mass variation: δm_q/m_q = 0.016 ± 0.005 (weighted mean from 4He, D, and 7Li data)
- Individual constraints: δm_q/m_q = -0.002 ± 0.037 from 4He; 0.012 ± 0.011 from D; 0.018 ± 0.006 from 7Li
- Linear approximation gives δm_q/m_q = 0.014 ± 0.002 (compared to the nonlinear result of 0.016 ± 0.005)
- Resonance energies: E_r = 0.183 MeV for 3He(d,p)4He; E_r = 0.0482 MeV for t(d,n)4He
- Resonance widths: Γ_r = 0.256 MeV for reaction 1; Γ_r = 0.0806 MeV for reaction 2
- Gamow window at BBN temperatures: E_0 = 0.180 MeV (kT ≈ 0.07 MeV), remarkably close to the 0.183 MeV resonance
- Observed abundances used: D/H = (2.84 ± 0.26)×10^(-5); 4He Y_p = 0.249 ± 0.009; 7Li/H = (1.5 ± 0.5)×10^(-10)
- Theoretical BBN predictions (from code): Y_p = 0.2486; D/H = 2.53×10^(-5); 7Li/H = 5.05×10^(-10)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Dmitriev (2004) on deuterium binding energy variation and Dent (2007) on linear response of BBN to multiple parameter variations, as well as Flambaum (2007) who found δm_q/m_q = 0.013 ± 0.002 could resolve the 7Li discrepancy. The key advance is including nonlinear dependence on m_q and, crucially, the effect of resonance position shifts in the 3He(d,p)4He and t(d,n)4He reactions, which were previously ignored. The paper shows that these resonance effects could destroy the agreement obtained from binding energy variations alone, and calls for detailed nuclear model calculations of the sensitivity of the 5Li* and 5He* resonances to fundamental constants.
