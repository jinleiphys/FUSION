# Two bosons in a narrowly resonant trap

> It is rare for an $S$-wave resonance to remain narrow while approaching threshold as a result of the interaction parameters being fine tuned. Such an interaction is, however, realized by pion-charmed baryon system $πΣ_c$, which has a narrow resonance $Λ_c^+(2595)$ located a few MeVs above its threshold. We study the consequence of narrowly resonant inter-species interaction in a three-body system composed of two identical light bosons and an extremely heavy particle. We concern ourselves in the present paper with resonances of the three-body system, as opposed to bound states. The focus is on the technical aspects of constructing the integral equation for three-body amplitudes and of analytically continuing the integral equation to locate the "trimer" resonance pole in the unphysical sheet of the center-of-mass energy plane.

## Key claim

The paper develops a technique to analytically continue the three-body integral equation into the unphysical sheet of the center-of-mass energy plane to locate a "trimer" resonance pole, for a system of two identical light bosons and an extremely heavy particle where the inter-species interaction produces a narrow, near-threshold S-wave resonance. The authors emphasize that the focus is on the technical aspects of constructing and continuing the integral equation, rather than on bound states.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory (EFT) with contact interactions, using an auxiliary dimer field to represent the resonant two-body interaction between the light boson and heavy particle. They derive an integral equation for the three-body amplitude in the limit of vanishing light-to-heavy mass ratio, assuming negligible boson-boson interactions. The integral equation is then analytically continued into the complex center-of-mass energy plane by deforming the integration contour to avoid singularities, enabling extraction of the trimer resonance pole position.

## Key numbers

- The charmed baryon $\Lambda_c^+(2595)$ is located about 2 MeV above the $\pi \Sigma_c$ threshold, with pion mass 138 MeV and $\Sigma_c$ mass 2455 MeV.
- The trimer resonance pole trajectory is computed for $N_f = 3$ as $|r_0|/a_0$ varies from -4.1 to -0.65, with the unit of energy being $(2mr_0^2)^{-1}$.
- The real part of the trimer pole moves below the boson-dimer threshold for $|r_0|/a_0 \gtrapprox -2.55$.
- The ultraviolet cutoff used in numerical calculations is $\Lambda |r_0| = 100$, and varying it to 20 produced no visible change in the amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the study of near-threshold S-wave resonances, motivated by the physical example of $\pi \Sigma_c$ system and the $\Lambda_c^+(2595)$ resonance. The authors build on the EFT framework for few-body dynamics developed by Kaplan (1996), Bedaque (1998), and others, and the results are consistent with findings by Helfrich (2010) that the Efimov effect becomes less visible as the light-to-heavy mass ratio decreases. The technique enables prediction of three-body hadronic states such as $\pi \pi \Sigma_c$, and the convergence of the integral equation suggests that no three-body force is required at leading order, unlike in the three-boson system with divergent scattering length studied by Bedaque (2000) and Platter (2006).
