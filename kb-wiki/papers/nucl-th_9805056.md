# The astrophysical reaction 8Li(n,gamma)9Li from measurements by reverse kinematics

> We study the breakup of 9Li projectiles in high energy (28.5 MeV/u) collisions with heavy nuclear targets (208Pb). The wave functions are calculated using a single-particle model for 9Li, and a simple optical potential model for the scattering part. A good agreement with measured data is obtained with insignificant E2 contribution.

## Key claim

The Coulomb dissociation of 9Li projectiles at 28.5 MeV/u on 208Pb targets can be used to extract the 8Li(n,γ)9Li radiative capture cross section, with E2 contributions being negligible for the specific kinematical conditions of the MSU experiment, contrary to concerns that E2 excitations might complicate the extraction.

## Method

The author used a single-particle model for 9Li, describing its ground state as a p3/2 proton coupled to the 2+ ground state of the 8Li core, and solved the Schrödinger equation with a Woods-Saxon nuclear potential including spin-orbit coupling. The continuum wavefunctions for s, p, d, and f states were calculated with the same potential parameters, and the direct capture cross sections were computed using first-order perturbation theory with electric multipole transition matrix elements. The Coulomb breakup cross section on 208Pb was calculated using an eikonal approximation with an M3Y-based optical potential, and the results were factorized into virtual photon numbers and real-photon breakup cross sections to relate to the radiative capture reaction.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy of 9Li: E0 = -4.05 MeV
- Potential parameters: a = 0.52 fm, r0 = 1.25 fm, R = 2.5 fm, Fs.o. = 0.351 fm, V0 = -45.3 MeV
- At 28.5 MeV/nucleon: σNN = 20 fm² and α = 0.87 for the nucleon-nucleon interaction
- The E2 contribution to the radiative capture cross section is about 7 orders of magnitude smaller than the E1 contribution
- The E2 contribution to the Coulomb breakup cross section is 3 orders of magnitude smaller than the E1 contribution
- The hard-sphere model yields a cross section within a factor of 2 of the E1 direct capture results, with approximately the same energy dependence
- Scattering length for the n+8Li system at low energies: as = 2.03 fm

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb dissociation method developed by Baur, Bertulani, and Rebel (1986) and Bertulani and Baur (1988) to the specific case of 9Li breakup, addressing questions raised by the MSU experiment of Zecher et al. (1998) about the role of E2 excitations in extracting the 8Li(n,γ)9Li reaction rate. The result enables more reliable extraction of this astrophysical reaction cross section from Coulomb breakup measurements, which is relevant for understanding inhomogeneous Big Bang nucleosynthesis and the formation of 9Li.
