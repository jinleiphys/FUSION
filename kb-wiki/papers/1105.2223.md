# A covariant model for the gamma N -> N(1535) transition at high momentum transfer

> A relativistic constituent quark model is applied to the gamma N -> N(1535) transition. The N(1535) wave function is determined by extending the covariant spectator quark model, previously developed for the nucleon, to the S11 resonance. The model allows us to calculate the valence quark contributions to the gamma N -> N(1535) transition form factors. Because of the nucleon and N(1535) structure the model is valid only for Q^2> 2.3 GeV^2. The results are compared with the experimental data for the electromagnetic form factors F1* and F2* and the helicity amplitudes A_1/2 and S_1/2, at high Q^2.

# Wiki Page: Covariant Model for the γN → N(1535) Transition at High Momentum Transfer

## Key claim

The covariant spectator quark model, previously applied to the nucleon and other resonances, can predict the valence quark contributions to the γN → N(1535) transition form factors without any new adjustable parameters, but only in the region Q² > 2.3 GeV² where the model's approximations (pointlike diquark, no spin-3/2 core mixture, and near-orthogonality of initial and final states) are justified.

## Method

The authors extended the covariant spectator quark model, which represents baryons as quark-diquark systems, to the N(1535) resonance by constructing its wave function from mixed-symmetry ρ-type and λ-type spin-orbital states with negative parity (L=1, P-wave excitation). They assumed a pointlike diquark, represented the N(1535) exclusively by the spin-1/2 core component (no mixture with the N(1650) resonance), and used the same scalar momentum distribution for the nucleon and N(1535) wave functions. The transition current was calculated in the relativistic impulse approximation using a constituent quark current parametrized by vector meson dominance, with all parameters fixed from previous nucleon calculations.

## Key numbers

- Model validity threshold: Q² > 2.3 GeV², derived from the condition Q² ≫ |q|₀² where |q|₀ = (M_S² − M²)/(2M_S) ≈ 0.48 GeV is the photon three-momentum at Q²=0
- The overlap integral I₀(Q²) is proportional to |q|, the photon three-momentum in the N(1535) rest frame, which vanishes when M_S = M
- The model describes F₁* data well for Q² > 1.5 GeV², within the regime of application Q² > 2.3 GeV²
- For F₂*, the model predicts positive values while CLAS data shows values very close to zero for Q² > 2 GeV², indicating a failure of the valence-only description for this form factor
- Dalton et al. (2009) data for A₁/₂ at Q² > 5.4 GeV² was determined under the assumption S₁/₂ = 0

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model framework previously applied to the nucleon (Ramalho and Pena, earlier work), the Roper resonance, the Δ(1232), and the Δ(1600) to the S₁₁(1535) resonance. The model's predictions are compared with CLAS data from Aznauryan (2009) and MAID analysis, as well as earlier data from DESY (Brasse, 1984) and Jefferson Lab (Armstrong, 1999; Thompson, 2001; Denizli, 2007; Dalton, 2009). The failure to describe F₂* suggests that meson cloud effects, which are not included in the model, may be significant even at high Q², a finding at odds with what was observed for the nucleon and Roper systems. The work enables future extensions to include spin-3/2 core contributions and diquark internal P-state structure, which could resolve the discrepancy.
