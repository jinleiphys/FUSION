# Dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism and chirality production in parallel electromagnetic field

> We study particle and chirality production from the vacuum in the presence of a slow strong parallel electromagnetic field superimposed by a fast weak perturbative electromagnetic field. We derive an analytical formula for the particle and chirality production number based on the perturbation theory in the Furry picture. With the formula, we analytically discuss the interplay and dynamical assistance between the Schwinger mechanism and one-photon pair production and clarify effects of a parallel slow strong magnetic field. We also show that the dynamical assistance can significantly enhance chirality production, and that a sizable amount of chirality can be produced even for massive particles. Phenomenological applications including heavy-ion collisions and intense laser experiments are also discussed.

## Key claim

The dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism can significantly enhance chirality production even for massive particles, but only when the perturbative field frequency is optimally tuned; the enhancement occurs through the interplay between the Schwinger mechanism and one-photon pair production in the presence of a parallel slow strong magnetic field, which has been previously unexplored for non-vanishing Lorentz invariant G.

## Method

The authors extended the perturbation theory in the Furry picture, previously developed for purely electric fields, to include a parallel magnetic component. They derived an analytical formula for particle and chirality production numbers by evaluating the in-in vacuum expectation value of the chirality operator and the number operator, using mode functions that are exact solutions of the Dirac equation in a constant parallel electromagnetic field with a superimposed time-dependent perturbative electric field.

## Key numbers

- The Schwinger mechanism production number for a constant electric field is given by N = VT × (eE)²/(4π³) exp(-π m²/(eE)), where V is spatial volume and T is the time interval.
- Adding a parallel magnetic field enhances the production number by the factor π(eB/eE) coth[π(eB/eE)] > 1.
- The critical electric field strength for manifest Schwinger mechanism is eE_cr = m_e² ~ √(10²⁹ W/cm²).
- Current laser facilities reach eE ~ √(10²² W/cm²), while upcoming facilities like ELI and XCELS are expected to reach eE ~ √(10²⁴⁻²⁵ W/cm²), still several orders below critical.
- The production number distribution per mode is proportional to exp[-π m_⊥²/(eE)], where m_⊥ = √(m² + eB(2n+1-s)) is the transverse mass with Landau level n and spin s.

## Context

This work extends the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism, previously studied for purely electric fields by Schutzhold (2008), Dunne (2009), and others using worldline instanton and Furry picture methods, to include parallel magnetic fields. It also generalizes the work of Copinger (2016) who studied the dynamically assisted Schwinger mechanism with parallel fields only for slowly varying perturbations using worldline instantons. The results enable phenomenological applications to chirality production in heavy-ion collisions (where glasma fields produce chirality via the chiral anomaly) and to designing optimal field configurations for upcoming intense laser experiments at ELI and XCELS.
