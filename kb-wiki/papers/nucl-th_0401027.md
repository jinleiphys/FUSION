# Gamma-radiation as a Signature of Ultra Peripheral Ion Collisions at LHC energies

> We study the peripheral ion collisions at LHC energies in which a nucleus is excited to the discrete state and then emits $γ$-rays. Large nuclear Lorenz factor allows to observe the high energy photons up to a few ten GeV and in the region of angles of a few hundred micro-radians around the beam direction. These photons can be used for tagging the events with particle production in the central rapidity region in the ultra-peripheral collisions. For that it is necessary to have an electromagnetic detector in front of the zero degree calorimeter in the LHC experiments.

## Key claim

The authors propose that gamma rays emitted by a nucleus excited to a discrete nuclear state in ultra-peripheral ion collisions at LHC energies can serve as a tag for events with particle production in the central rapidity region, but only for systems with a sufficiently large cross-section for the produced system \(X_f\), because the cross-section with nuclear excitation is three orders of magnitude smaller than the elastic process without excitation.

## Method

The authors use the equivalent photon approximation to calculate the cross-section for the process \(A_1 + A_2 \to A_1^* (\lambda^P, E_0) + A_2 + X_f\), where one nucleus is excited to a discrete nuclear level and then decays by emitting a gamma ray. They introduce inelastic nuclear form factors for the transitions \(0^+ \to 2^+\) in \(^{16}\text{O}\) (6.92 MeV) and \(0^+ \to 3^-\) in \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) (2.615 MeV), using parameters from electron scattering data, and compute the two-photon luminosity and final cross-section for \(\eta_c\) production via \(\gamma\gamma\) fusion.

## Key numbers

*   For \(^{16}\text{O}^* (2^+, 6.92\ \text{MeV})\) at LHC energies, the photon energy in the ZDC region is \(19 \div 48\ \text{GeV}\).
*   For \(^{208}\text{Pb}^* (3^-, 2.615\ \text{MeV})\) at LHC energies, the photon energy in the ZDC region is \(7 \div 14\ \text{GeV}\).
*   The cross-section for \(\eta_c\) production with \(^{16}\text{O}^*\) excitation is \(0.201\ \text{nb}\), yielding about 2810 events per \(10^6\) seconds at a luminosity of \(1.4 \cdot 10^{31}\ \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\).
*   The cross-section for \(\eta_c\) production with \(^{208}\text{Pb}^*\) excitation is \(129\ \text{nb}\), yielding about 53 events per \(10^6\) seconds at a luminosity of \(4.2 \cdot 10^{26}\ \text{cm}^{-2}\text{s}^{-1}\).
*   The reduced transition probability for the \(2^+\) level in \(^{16}\text{O}\) is \(B(E_02) = (36.1 \pm 3.4) e^2\ \text{fm}^4\).
*   The reduced transition probability for the \(3^-\) level in \(^{208}\text{Pb}\) is \(B(E_03) = (6.12 \cdot 10^5 \pm 2.2\%) e^2\ \text{fm}^6\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Korotkikh) of nuclear excitation by electron/positron pairs to the case of \(\gamma\gamma\) fusion producing a system \(X_f\). It builds on the formalism of equivalent photon spectra for peripheral collisions reviewed by Baur et al. (1998) and Baur et al. (1999), who suggested measuring intact nuclei after the interaction. The proposed method enables a new trigger for ultra-peripheral collisions at LHC experiments like CMS and ALICE, using a photon signal in an electromagnetic detector in front of the zero degree calorimeter and a veto on neutrons to select events where the nucleus remains intact.
