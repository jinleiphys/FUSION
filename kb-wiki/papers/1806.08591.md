# Lattice calculations for two-component fermion systems with unequal masses: one dimension

> We consider systems of two-component fermions with unequal masses and interacting via a short-range attractive potential. We discuss the case where the two-component fermions form a shallow dimer with large scattering length. The three-fermion and four-fermion systems with such properties are universal and charazteried by the two-fermion scattering length $a_{\text{ff}}$ and the ratio of the mass of spin-$\uparrow$ fermion to the mass of spin-$\downarrow$ fermion, $m_{\uparrow}/m_{\downarrow}$. In this study using lattice effective field theory we analyze fermion-dimer and dimer-dimer systems, and calculate the universal fermion-dimer and dimer-dimer scattering lengths for various values of the mass ratio $m_{\uparrow}/m_{\downarrow}$. We find that these universal scattering lengths increase logarithmically with the mass ratio $m_{\uparrow}/m_{\downarrow}$.

## Key claim
Using lattice effective field theory in one spatial dimension, the universal fermion-dimer and dimer-dimer scattering lengths for two-component fermions with unequal masses increase logarithmically with the mass ratio \(m_{\uparrow}/m_{\downarrow}\), with the caveat that the results are obtained in the universal limit of large fermion-fermion scattering length and after continuum limit extrapolations to remove lattice discretization errors.

## Method
The author used a non-relativistic lattice Hamiltonian with \(\mathcal{O}(a^{4})\)-improved action for two-component fermions on a periodic lattice, tuning the zero-range interaction strength \(C_0\) to produce a shallow dimer with large scattering length. Low-energy spectra for fermion-dimer (two spin-up, one spin-down) and dimer-dimer (two spin-up, two spin-down) systems were computed using the Lanczos eigenvector method at various lattice sizes. Scattering phase shifts were extracted via the Luscher method for one dimension, accounting for topological phase corrections and lattice-determined dimer effective masses, and the scattering lengths were obtained from truncated effective range expansions followed by continuum limit extrapolations.

## Key numbers
- The two-fermion scattering length \(a_{\rm ff}\) was varied from 1.4 fm to 10 fm for continuum limit extrapolations.
- For the fermion-dimer system, the universal scattering length ratio \(a_{\rm fd}/a_{\rm ff}\) approaches 1 in the limit \(m_{\downarrow} \to \infty\).
- For the dimer-dimer system, the universal scattering length ratio \(a_{\rm dd}/a_{\rm ff}\) increases with the mass ratio \(m_{\uparrow}/m_{\downarrow}\).
- The lattice calculations used masses \(m_{\uparrow} = 1\) GeV and varied \(m_{\downarrow}\) to explore mass ratios from near 1 to the limits \(m_{\uparrow} \to \infty\) and \(m_{\downarrow} \to \infty\).

## Context
This work extends lattice effective field theory studies of universal few-body systems (Elhatisari 2016) to the case of unequal fermion masses in one dimension, where the mass ratio becomes a new control parameter. The results are benchmarked against Born-Oppenheimer calculations in the heavy-mass limit and against exact Bethe Ansatz solutions for the one-dimensional integrable system. The computed universal scattering lengths for fermion-dimer and dimer-dimer systems provide input for understanding the dynamics of many-body systems of two-component fermions with mass imbalance, relevant to ultracold atomic gases and nuclear physics.
