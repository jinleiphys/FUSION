# Production of $Œõ_c^+$ hypernuclei in antiproton - nucleus collisions

> We investigate the production of charm-baryon hypernucleus $^{16}_{Œõ_c^+}$O in the antiproton - $^{16}$O collisions within a fully covariant model that is based on an effective Lagrangian approach. The explicit ${\bar Œõ}_c^ - Œõ_c^+$ production vertex is described by the $t$-channel $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ meson-exchanges in the initial collision of the incident antiproton with one of the protons of the target nucleus. The $Œõ_c^+$ bound state spinors as well as the self-energies of the exchanged mesons employed in our calculations are derived from the quark-meson coupling model. The parameters of various vertices are taken to be the same as those used in our previous study of the elementary ${\bar p} + p \to {\bar Œõ}_c^- + Œõ_c^+$ reaction. We find that for antiproton beam momenta of interest to the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment, the 0$^\circ$ differential cross sections for the formation of $^{16}_{Œõ_c^+}$O hypernuclear states with simple particle-hole configurations, have magnitudes in the range of a few $Œº$b/sr.

## Key claim

The authors present the first calculation of cross sections for the production of a charm-baryon hypernucleus ($^{16}_{\Lambda_c^+}$O) in antiproton-nucleus collisions, finding that for antiproton beam momenta of interest to the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment (8-15 GeV/c), the 0$^\circ$ differential cross sections for simple particle-hole configurations have magnitudes in the range of a few $\mu$b/sr, though the results carry an uncertainty of up to a factor of 2 due to the poorly constrained antiproton-nucleon total cross section used in the distortion treatment.

## Method

The reaction ${\bar p} + ^{16}$O $\to {\bar \Lambda}_c^- + ^{16}_{\Lambda_c^+}$O was described within a fully covariant effective Lagrangian model, where ${\bar \Lambda}_c^- \Lambda_c^+$ production proceeds via $t$-channel exchanges of $D^0$ and $D^{*0}$ mesons in the initial collision of the antiproton with a bound proton in the target nucleus. The $\Lambda_c^+$ bound state spinors and the self-energies of the exchanged mesons were derived from the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, with the same vertex coupling constants and monopole form factors (cutoff 3.0 GeV) used in a prior study of the elementary ${\bar p} + p \to {\bar \Lambda}_c^- + \Lambda_c^+$ reaction. Initial- and final-state interactions were approximately accounted for using an eikonal-approximation based procedure.

## Key numbers

* Threshold momentum for $^{16}_{\Lambda_c^+}$O production in ${\bar p}$ + $^{16}$O is about 3.953 GeV/c, compared to 10.162 GeV/c for the elementary ${\bar p} + p \to {\bar \Lambda}_c^- + \Lambda_c^+$ reaction.
* Binding energies predicted by the QMC model for $\Lambda_c^+$ states in $^{16}_{\Lambda_c^+}$O: 12.78 MeV (0$s_{1/2}$), 7.17 MeV (0$p_{1/2}$), and 7.20 MeV (0$p_{3/2}$).
* For ${\bar p}$ beam momenta between 8-15 GeV/c, the 0$^\circ$ differential cross sections for the ($0p_{1/2}^{-p},0s_{1/2}^{\Lambda_c^+}$) configuration range from 1.5-3.8 $\mu$b/sr (0$^-$ state) and 5.0-11.0 $\mu$b/sr (1$^-$ state).
* For the ($0p_{1/2}^{-p},0p_{3/2}^{\Lambda_c^+}$) configuration at the same beam momenta, cross sections range from 0.9-2.8 $\mu$b/sr (1$^+$ state) and 1.6-6.0 $\mu$b/sr (2$^+$ state).
* Using $\sigma_{{\bar p}N}$ = 50 mb instead of 75 mb increases cross sections by almost a factor of 2 at 15 GeV/c; using 81 mb decreases them by about 10%.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of $\Lambda_c^+$ hypernuclei by Tsushima et al. (2003-2004) within the QMC model, which predicted binding energies and spectra for several charm-hypernuclei but did not calculate production cross sections. It also builds on the authors' earlier effective Lagrangian study of the elementary ${\bar p} + p \to {\bar \Lambda}_c^- + \Lambda_c^+$ reaction (Shyam, 2014) and on similar covariant treatments of hypernuclear production in antiproton-nucleus collisions. The results suggest that $\Lambda_c^+$ hypernuclear production experiments may be feasible even at the beginning stage of the FAIR project, and the calculated cross sections provide a basis for planning measurements at the ${\bar P}ANDA$ experiment that could constrain the $\Lambda_c^+ - N$ interaction.
