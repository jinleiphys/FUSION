# Probing the deuteron structure at small N-N distances by cumulative pion production

> The fragmentation of deuterons into pions emitted forward in the kinematic region forbidden for free nucleon-nucleon collisions is analyzed. It is shown that the inclusion of the non-nucleonic degrees of freedom in a deuteron results in a satisfactory description of the data for the inclusive pion spectrum and improves the description of the data about $T_{20}$. According to the data, $T_{20}$ has very small positive values, less than 0.2, which contradicts the theoretical calculations ignoring these degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

Including non-nucleonic degrees of freedom (such as six-quark states) in the deuteron wave function allows a satisfactory description of the inclusive pion spectrum from deuteron fragmentation and improves the description of the tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) at cumulative variable \(x_{\mathcal C} \ge 1\), although the inclusion is approximate and the best description of \(T_{20}\) requires a specific mixing parameter \(a = 2.3\).

## Method

The authors analyzed the inclusive reaction \(\overrightarrow{D} + p \to \pi(0^\circ) + X\) at initial energies of a few GeV within a relativistic impulse approximation using the Bethe-Salpeter formalism. They modified the deuteron wave function by adding a non-nucleonic component with probability \(\alpha_{2(3q)}\) (a state of two colorless three-quark systems) and introduced a mixing parameter \(a\) to construct new \(\mathcal{S}\) and \(\mathcal{D}\) waves, following the approach of Lykasov (1993) and Efremov (1988). The inclusive pion spectrum and tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) were then calculated and compared to experimental data from Afanasev (1998) and Baldin (1985).

## Key numbers

- The probability of the non-nucleonic component in the deuteron, \(\alpha_{2(3q)}\), is taken as \(0.02\) to \(0.04\) (2% to 4%).
- The best description of the tensor analyzing power \(T_{20}\) data is obtained for the mixing parameter \(a = 2.3\).
- The experimental data show that \(T_{20}\) has very small positive values, less than \(0.2\), at \(x_{\mathcal C} \ge 1\).
- The calculation is performed for a proton beam momentum of \(P_p = 9\) GeV/\(c\) and a deuteron beam momentum of \(P_D = 8.9\) GeV/\(c\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of deuteron fragmentation into protons by Lykasov (1993), where non-nucleonic degrees of freedom were needed to describe the proton spectrum at \(x \ge 1.7\), to the case of pion production. It contradicts earlier calculations by Illarionov (2002) that used only nucleonic degrees of freedom and could not describe the \(T_{20}\) data for pions at any \(x_{\mathcal C} \ge 1\). The results enable further tests of the deuteron structure at small internucleon distances (below 1 fm) and suggest that polarization observables like \(T_{20}\) provide independent information beyond the inclusive spectrum.
