# Single-Spin Asymmetries in Semi-inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering and Drell-Yan Processes

> We examine in detail the diagrammatic mechanisms which provide the change of sign between the single transverse spin asymmetries measured in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and in the Drell-Yan process (DY). This asymmetry is known to arise due to the transverse spin dependence of the target proton combined with a T-odd complex phase. Using the discrete symmetry properties of transverse spinors, we show that the required complex phase originates in the denominators of rescattering diagrams and their respective cuts. For simplicity, we work in a model where the proton consists of a valence quark and a scalar diquark. We then show that the phases generated in SIDIS and in DY originate from distinctly different cuts in the amplitudes, which at first appears to obscure the relationship between the single-spin asymmetries in the two processes. Nevertheless, further analysis demonstrates that the contributions of these cuts are identical in the leading-twist Bjorken kinematics considered, resulting in the standard sign-flip relation between the Sivers functions in SIDIS and DY. Physically, this fundamental, but yet untested, prediction occurs because the Sivers effect in the Drell-Yan reaction is modified by the initial-state "lensing" interactions of the annihilating antiquark, in contrast to the final-state lensing which produces the Sivers effect in deep inelastic scattering.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the sign-flip relation between the Sivers functions in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and Drell-Yan (DY) processes is preserved despite the fact that the complex phases in the two processes originate from distinctly different cuts in the Feynman diagrams, not from the same propagator being put on mass shell as previously assumed.

## Method

The authors employ a model where the proton consists of a valence quark and a scalar color-charged diquark, using Yukawa-type coupling. They calculate the single transverse spin asymmetry at leading order in perturbation theory by evaluating the interference between tree-level and one-loop amplitudes for the processes gamma* + p(up arrow) -> q + X (representing SIDIS) and anti-q + p(up arrow) -> gamma* + X (representing DY). Using discrete symmetry properties of transverse spinors, they show that the required complex phase originates in the denominators of rescattering diagrams and their respective cuts, and they perform explicit Feynman diagram calculations in the limit of high energy s and high photon virtuality Q^2.

## Key numbers

- The analysis works in the kinematic limit where s, Q^2, q_T^2 >> M^2, lambda^2, r_T^2, k_T^2 (the proton mass M, diquark mass lambda, and transverse momenta are all small compared to the hard scales)
- The photon virtuality is defined as q^2 = -Q^2, and in the Drell-Yan-West frame Q^2 = transverse-q^2
- The asymmetry A_N is defined as (dsigma(up arrow)(q_perp) - dsigma(down arrow)(q_perp)) / (2 dsigma_unp)

## Context

This work extends the earlier model calculations by Brodsky et al. (2002) for SIDIS and provides the first detailed diagrammatic demonstration of the sign reversal mechanism for DY, which was previously assumed to arise from the same propagator cuts as in SIDIS. The paper clarifies the connection between the time-reversal argument of Collins (2002) and the diagrammatic conditions for generating single-spin asymmetries, and it enables future model calculations for Sivers functions by establishing the correct cut structure in both processes.
