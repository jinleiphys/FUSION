# Is early thermalization really needed in A+A collisions?

> In this note we review our ideas, first published in year 2006, and corresponding results, including the new ones, which show that whereas the assumption of (partial) thermalization in relativistic A+A collisions is really crucial to explain soft physics observables, the hypotheses of {\it early} thermalization at times less than 1 fm/c is not necessary. The reason for the later conclusion is that the initial transverse flow in thermal matter as well as its anisotropy, leading to asymmetry of the transverse momentum spectra, could be developed at pre-thermal, either partonic or classical field - Glasma, stage with even more efficiency than in the case of very early perfect hydrodynamics. Such radial and elliptic flows develop no matter whether a pressure already established. The general reason for them is an essential finiteness of the system in transverse direction.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while (partial) thermalization is essential for explaining soft physics observables in relativistic A+A collisions, the hypothesis of *early* thermalization at times less than 1 fm/c is not necessary, because initial transverse flow and its anisotropy can be developed at the pre-thermal stage with even more efficiency than in very early perfect hydrodynamics.

## Method

The authors compare the development of transverse collective velocities in four different scenarios: non-relativistic free gas evolution, partonic free streaming from Color Glass Condensate (CGC) initial conditions, classical gluonic field (Glasma) expansion, and hydrodynamic expansion of a perfect fluid with a hard equation of state (p = ε/3). For the partonic and field cases, they evolve the system from an initial proper time τ₀ = 0.3 fm/c to a supposed thermalization time τᵢ = 1 fm/c, then compare the resulting flows with those from hydrodynamics started at the same initial time and energy density profile.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time for gluon formation: τ₀ ≈ 3/Λₛ, with Λₛ = 2 GeV corresponding to τ₀ = 0.3 fm/c
- For central collisions (b=0): initial energy density at τ₀ = 0.3 fm/c and r=0 is ε₀ = 67 GeV/fm³, with Gaussian width R = 5.33 fm
- For non-central collisions with impact parameter b = 6.3 fm: Rₓ = 3.7 fm, R_y = 4.6 fm
- Effective temperature parameter for gluon distribution: T_eff = 0.465 Λₛ; effective mass parameter: m_eff = 0.0358 Λₛ
- Thermalization time estimates from theory: typically 2-3 fm/c, with most optimistic estimates giving 1-1.5 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal from 2006 (Sinyukov et al., 2006) that transverse collective velocities can develop at the pre-thermal stage. It contradicts the prevailing interpretation of results from hydrodynamic models (Landau, 1953; STAR collaboration, 2000s) and elliptic flow analyses (elliptic, 2000s) which suggested that thermalization must occur at times below 1 fm/c. The paper also addresses the RHIC HBT puzzle discussed by Borysova (2000s), showing that the required initial transverse velocities at τᵢ = 1 fm/c can arise from pre-thermal dynamics rather than early thermalization. This enables a resolution of the discrepancy between hydrodynamic model requirements and theoretical estimates of thermalization times (early, 2000s), and provides a framework for understanding flow development at LHC energies where the discrepancy could be even larger.
