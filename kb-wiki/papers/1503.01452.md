# The Structure of the Nucleon: Elastic Electromagnetic Form Factors

> Precise proton and neutron form factor measurements at Jefferson Lab, using spin observables, have recently made a significant contribution to the unraveling of the internal structure of the nucleon. Accurate experimental measurements of the nucleon form factors are a test-bed for understanding how the nucleon's static properties and dynamical behavior emerge from QCD, the theory of the strong interactions between quarks. There has been enormous theoretical progress, since the publication of the Jefferson Lab proton form factor ratio data, aiming at reevaluating the picture of the nucleon. We will review the experimental and theoretical developments in this field and discuss the outlook for the future.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how Jefferson Lab double-polarization measurements of the proton form factor ratio $\mu_p G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}$ showed a dramatic linear decrease with $Q^2$ (dropping to 0.35 at $Q^2 = 5.6$ GeV$^2$), contradicting the earlier Rosenbluth-separation cross-section results which had suggested $\mu_p G_{Ep}/G_{Mp} \approx 1$ for $Q^2 \lesssim 6$ GeV$^2$; the review notes that two-photon exchange contributions may partially resolve this discrepancy but that direct measurements show such effects affect the polarization ratio at less than the percent level.

## Method

The paper reviews elastic electron-nucleon scattering experiments using both the Rosenbluth (longitudinal-transverse separation) technique on cross-section data and double-polarization methods (recoil polarization and beam-target asymmetry) at Jefferson Lab, MIT-Bates, MAMI, and NIKHEF. For the proton, polarization transfer experiments measured the ratio $G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}$ directly from the ratio of transverse to longitudinal recoil polarization components $P_t/P_\ell$, eliminating dependence on beam polarization and polarimeter analyzing power. For the neutron, target asymmetry measurements using polarized $^2$H or $^3$He targets extracted $G_{En}/G_{Mn}$ from the beam-target asymmetry $A_{perp}$.

## Key numbers

- $\mu_p G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}$ decreased approximately linearly with $Q^2$ for $Q^2 > 0.5$ GeV$^2$, dropping to 0.35 at $Q^2 = 5.6$ GeV$^2$ (the highest $Q^2$ investigated at the time of the initial JLab experiments)
- The proton charge radius from CODATA (from $ep$ elastic scattering) is 0.8775 (51) fm, while the muonic hydrogen value is 0.84087 (39) fm, a difference of seven standard deviations
- A search for normal polarization component at Orsay found 0.040 $\pm$ 0.027 at $Q^2 = 0.61$ GeV$^2$; the transverse component was 0.000 $\pm$ 0.028
- A single-spin experiment at SLAC (1970) with polarized proton target produced asymmetries of order 1 to 2% in the $Q^2$ range 0.38 to 0.98 GeV$^2$
- The dipole form factor parameterization is $G_D = (1 + Q^2/0.71)^{-2}$ (with $Q^2$ in GeV$^2$)
- The first recoil polarization measurement of $G_{En}$ by Madey et al. (1994) was at $Q^2 = 0.255$ GeV$^2$ at MIT-BATES
- The first double polarization measurement of $G_{Ep}/G_{Mp}$ at MIT-BATES (1994-1995) covered $Q^2$ values of 0.38 to 0.50 GeV$^2$

## Context

This review extends the experimental and theoretical work following the Jefferson Lab proton form factor ratio data published around 2000 (Jones, Punjabi, Gayou, Puckett), which contradicted the earlier Rosenbluth-separation results from Hand (1963), Janssens, Price, Litt, Berger, Bartel, Bork, Simon, Walker, Andivahis, Christy, and Qattan. The review covers the subsequent theoretical progress including vector meson dominance, dispersion analysis, constituent quark models, Dyson-Schwinger equations, lattice QCD, and generalized parton distribution models. It enables future experiments at Jefferson Lab that will measure proton and neutron form factors to $Q^2 = 10$ GeV$^2$ or greater, and contributes to resolving the proton radius puzzle between the CODATA value (Mohr, 2012) and the muonic hydrogen value (Pohl, 2013).
