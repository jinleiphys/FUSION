# Critical Exponents of the Statistical Multifragmentation Model

> For the statistical multifragmentation model the critical indices $α^\prime, β, γ^\prime, δ$ are calculated as functions of the Fisher parameter $τ$. It is found that these indices have different values than in Fisher's droplet model. Some peculiarities of the scaling relations are discussed. The basic model predicts for the index $τ$ a narrow range of values, $1.799< τ< 1.846$, which is consistent with two experiments on nuclear multifragmentation.

## Key claim

The critical exponents of the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) differ from those of Fisher's droplet model (FDM), meaning the two models belong to different universality classes; the SMM predicts a narrow range for the Fisher parameter tau (1.799 < tau < 1.846) that is consistent with two experiments on nuclear multifragmentation, but only for the specific parameterization where the surface tension exponent zeta = 5/4 and the surface area exponent sigma = 2/3.

## Method

The authors analytically solved a simplified version of the SMM in the thermodynamic limit using the grand canonical ensemble, where nucleon eigenvolume is included via a free volume V_f = V - bA and the free surface tension obeys a(epsilon) = a_0 epsilon^zeta for small reduced temperature epsilon = (T_c - T)/T_c. They derived the critical exponents alpha', beta, gamma', and delta as functions of the Fisher parameter tau by analyzing the asymptotic behavior of series expansions for the pressure, density, and compressibility near the critical point on the coexistence curve (nu = 0). The exponents were then compared to those of the FDM and to experimental data from the E900 and EOS multifragmentation experiments.

## Key numbers

- For the SMM with zeta = 5/4 and sigma = 2/3: beta = (15/8)(2 - tau), gamma' = (15/4)(tau - 3/2), delta = (tau - 1)/(2 - tau), and alpha'_s = 1/8 for tau < 5/3 or alpha'_s = (15/8)tau - 3 for tau >= 5/3.
- The predicted range for the Fisher parameter tau from real gas critical exponents (beta = 0.32-0.39, gamma' = 1.3-1.4, delta = 4-5) is 1.799 < tau < 1.846.
- For Ising-like systems within renormalized phi^4 theory, the estimated tau = 1.828 +/- 0.001.
- The E900 experiment (ISiS collaboration) found min(tau_eff) approximately 1.9 from pi- + Au multifragmentation.
- The EOS collaboration found min(tau_eff) in the range 1.8-1.9 from Au + C multifragmentation data.

## Context

This work extends the exact analytical solution of a simplified SMM found by Bugaev (2000, 2001) and contrasts it with Fisher's droplet model (Fisher, 1967), showing the SMM belongs to a different universality class due to nonzero nucleon volume and non-negative surface energy. The results contradict numerical studies by Elze (2000) for version V2 of the SMM, which the authors argue are incorrect, and show that version V3 of that study has no critical point at all for non-vanishing surface energy. The derived scaling relations and tau estimates enable experimental tests of whether the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition critical point has been reached, with the E900 (Beaulieu, 1999) and EOS (2000) multifragmentation data both showing tau values consistent with the SMM predictions rather than the original FDM.
