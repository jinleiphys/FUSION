# The $D^* D^* \bar{D}$ and $D^* D^* \bar{D}^*$ Three-Body Systems

> The hidden charm $X(3872)$ resonance is usually thought to be a $D^{*0} \bar{D}^0$ meson-antimeson molecule with quantum numbers $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$. If this is the case, there is the possibility that there might be three body bound states with two charmed mesons and a charmed antimeson. Here we argue that the theoretical existence of this type of three body molecules is expected from heavy quark spin symmetry. If applied to the two body sector, this symmetry implies that the interaction of the $D^{*0} \bar{D}^{*0}$ meson-antimeson pair in the $J^{PC} = 2^{++}$ channel is the same as in the $J^{PC} = 1^{++}$ $D^{*0} \bar{D}^0$ case. From this we can infer that the $J^P = 3^{-}$ $D^{*0} D^{*0} \bar{D}^{*0}$ molecule will be able to display the Efimov effect if the scattering length of the $2^{++}$ channel is close enough to the unitary limit. Heavy quark spin symmetry also indicates that the $J^P = 2^{-}$ $D^{*0} D^{*0} \bar{D}^0$ molecule is analogous to the $J^P = 3^{-}$ $D^{*0} D^{*0} \bar{D}^{*0}$ one. That is, it can also have a geometric spectrum. If we consider these triply heavy trimers in the isospin symmetric limit, the Efimov effect disappears and we can in principle predict the fundamental state of the $2^-$ $D^*D^*\bar{D}$ and $3^-$ $D^* D^* \bar{D}^*$ systems. The same applies to the $B^*B^* \bar{B}^*$ system: if the $Z_b(10650)$ is an isovector $B^* \bar{B}^*$ molecule then the $0^-$ isodoublet and the $1^-$, $2^-$ isoquartet $B^* B^* \bar{B}^*$ trimers might bind, but do not display Efimov physics. Finally from heavy flavour symmetry it can be argued that scattering in the $B D$ two-body system might be resonant. This would in turn imply the possibility of Efimov physics in the $B B D$ three body system.

## Key claim

The paper argues that if the X(3872) is a D*0 D̅0 molecule, heavy quark spin symmetry implies that the D*0 D̅*0 interaction in the JPC = 2++ channel is equally strong, which could enable the Efimov effect in the J P = 3− D*0 D*0 D̅*0 three-body system, but only if the scattering length of the 2++ channel is close enough to the unitary limit; in the isospin symmetric limit the Efimov effect disappears, allowing predictive calculations without a three-body force.

## Method

The author constructs Faddeev equations for the D* D* D̅ and D* D* D̅* three-body systems, assuming that charmed meson-meson pairs (D D, D D*, D* D*) do not interact and that only the meson-antimeson channels (D D̅* and D* D̅*) are resonant. Heavy quark spin symmetry is used to relate the 2++ D* D̅* interaction to the known 1++ D D̅* interaction of the X(3872). The Faddeev equations are solved in the unitary limit to test for the Efimov effect, and numerically with a Gaussian regulator (cutoff Λ = 0.5–1.0 GeV) to predict binding energies in the isospin symmetric limit.

## Key numbers

- For the neutral D*0 D*0 D̅0 and D*0 D*0 D̅*0 systems in the unitary limit, the discrete scaling factor for momenta is μ0 = eπ/s0 ≈ 1986.1, corresponding to a binding energy scaling factor μ0² ≈ 3.9447 × 10⁶.
- The critical coupling for the Efimov effect is λc = 3√3/(2π) ≈ 0.826993; the neutral systems have λ = 1 (above threshold), while the isospin symmetric limit gives λ = 3/4 (below threshold).
- For the 3− D* D* D̅* trimer in the isospin symmetric limit, the three-body binding energy below the dimer-particle threshold is B3 = 3+2−2 MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and B3 = 3+4−3 MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV).
- For the 2− D* D* D̅ trimer, the three-body binding energy is B3 = 1.9+0−1.4 MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and B3 = 1.3+0−1.4 MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV).
- The predicted 2++ D* D̅* partner (X(4012)) has binding energy B2 = 5+3−3 MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and B2 = 6+8−5 MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV).
- For the B* B* B̅* system assuming the Zb(10650) is a bound state with B2 = 2 ± 2 MeV, the trimer binding energy is B3 = 1.1+1.3−1.2 MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) and B3 = 1.0+1.1−1.0 MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV).
- The 2− D* D* D̅ bound state becomes unbound relative to the D X2 threshold when B2(X2) ≥ 16.6 MeV (Λ = 0.5 GeV) or ≥ 12.5 MeV (Λ = 1.0 GeV).

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis by Canham, Hammer, and Springer (2009), who considered D0 X and D*0 X scattering and concluded that the system does not fulfill conditions for the Efimov effect. The present paper updates that conclusion by incorporating heavy quark spin symmetry, which adds the resonant 2++ D* D̅* channel and enables the Efimov effect in the neutral D*0 D*0 D̅0 and D*0 D*0 D̅*0 systems. The results enable predictions for triply heavy trimers in the charm and bottom sectors, contingent on the molecular nature of the X(3872) and Zb(10650) resonances, and suggest that the B B D system may be a candidate for observable Efimov physics due to mass imbalance.
