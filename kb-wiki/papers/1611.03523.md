# Scale-setting, flavour dependence and chiral symmetry restoration

> We determine the flavour dependence of the renormalisation-group-invariant running interaction through judicious use of both unquenched Dyson-Schwinger equation and lattice results for QCD's gauge-sector two-point functions. An important step is the introduction of a physical scale setting procedure that enables a realistic expression of the effect of different numbers of active quark flavours on the interaction. Using this running interaction in concert with a well constrained class of dressed--gluon-quark vertices, we estimate the critical number of active lighter-quarks above which dynamical chiral symmetry breaking becomes impossible: $n_f^{\rm cr}\approx 9$; and hence in whose neighbourhood QCD is plausibly a conformal theory.

# Scale-setting, flavour dependence and chiral symmetry restoration

## Key claim

The authors determine that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking becomes impossible when the number of active lighter-quark flavours exceeds approximately 9, but this result depends critically on a newly introduced physical scale-setting procedure for unquenched lattice QCD data and on the inclusion of massless-ghost loop enhancement; without ghost-loop enhancement the critical number drops to approximately 5.

## Method

The authors combine unquenched Dyson-Schwinger equation analyses with lattice QCD results for the gluon propagator and ghost dressing function at n_f = (2,0) and n_f = (2,1,1) to construct the renormalisation-group-invariant running interaction kernel. They introduce a rescaling factor s_a = 1.06 to correct the lattice spacing for n_f = (2,0) simulations, enforcing the condition that the infrared value of the RGI function d̂(k²) must be equal for theories that differ only by heavy-quark content. Using this flavour-dependent interaction together with a class of realistic dressed-gluon-quark vertices constrained by three physical conditions, they solve the chiral-limit gap equation and extrapolate the chiral order parameter to determine the critical number of flavours.

## Key numbers

- Critical number of active lighter-quarks for DCSB: n_f^{cr} = 9.1 ± 0.3 (with ghost-loop enhancement included)
- Critical number without ghost-loop enhancement: n_f^{cr} = 5.4 ± 0.3
- Rescaling factor for n_f = (2,0) lattice spacing: s_a = 1.06
- Gluon mass scale: m_g ≈ 0.5 GeV
- Renormalisation point used: ζ_4 = 3.61 GeV
- Taylor coupling values at ζ_4: α = 0.33 for n_f = (2,0) and α = 0.37 for n_f = (2,1,1); after rescaling, n_f = (2,0) value becomes α = 0.35
- Ghost-loop enhancement ratio: l_w/m_g² = 1.71 GeV⁻² for both n_f = (2,0) and n_f = (2,1,1)
- Infrared value of RGI function: d̂(0) = 14.38 GeV⁻² for both n_f = (2,0) and n_f = (2,1,1)
- FLAG estimate of Λ_MSbar for n_f = 0: 260 ± 7 MeV; for n_f = (2,0): 330^{+21}_{-54} MeV; for n_f = (2,1,1): 294 ± 11 MeV
- PDG-like Λ_MSbar values: n_f = 0: 388 MeV; n_f = (2,0): 364 MeV; n_f = (2,1,1): 296 ± 10 MeV

## Context

This work extends the unification of top-down and bottom-up approaches to QCD's gauge sector achieved by Binosi, Chang, Papavassiliou, and Roberts (2014), who showed that the quark-antiquark scattering kernel can be determined directly from gauge-sector dynamics. The authors address the previously paradoxical finding that naively comparing quenched and unquenched lattice results suggested stronger DCSB with active quarks than without. The critical flavour number n_f^{cr} ≈ 9 agrees with independent lattice QCD analyses by Appelquist (2009), Hayakawa (2010), Cheng (2013), and Aoki (2013), who found values between n_f = 8 and n_f = 10 using completely different methods based on chiral extrapolations of lattice configurations. This agreement is meaningful because the continuum DSE approach and lattice simulations face different systematic uncertainties, particularly regarding scale setting. The results enable predictions for the flavour dependence of hadron observables and inform model-building for new physics based on non-Abelian gauge theories near the conformal window.
