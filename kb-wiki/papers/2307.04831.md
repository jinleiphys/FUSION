# Single-Inclusive Particle Production from $pA$ Collision at Next-to-Leading Order

> We present the first fully consistent NLO calculation of the single-inclusive forward hadron production in proton-nucleus (pA) collisions under the color glass condensate (CGC) framework. In the dilute-dense limit, the NLO cross-section can be written as a convolution of the NLO impact factor, NLO parton distribution function (PDF), NLO fragmentation function (FF) and dipole-target scattering amplitude which satisfies the NLO small-$x$ Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution. We demonstrate that, without the NLO corrections to the impact factor, we obtain a significant Cronin peak when the dipole amplitude satisfies the NLO BK equation. This would contradict the recent LHCb results. However, the Cronin peak becomes suppressed when the NLO correction to the impact factor is included. This is the main result of this work. The dependence on resummation schemes for the NLO BK evolution will also be discussed.

## Key claim

The first fully consistent next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of single-inclusive forward hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions under the color glass condensate (CGC) framework shows that the Cronin peak, which appears when only the dipole amplitude satisfies NLO Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) evolution, becomes suppressed when NLO corrections to the impact factor are included; this suppression is necessary to avoid contradiction with recent LHCb results.

## Method

The NLO cross-section is computed as a convolution of the NLO impact factor, NLO parton distribution functions (PDFs), NLO fragmentation functions (FFs), and the dipole-target scattering amplitude evolved with NLO small-x BK evolution. The dipole amplitude for proton-proton collisions is taken from Beuf (2020) using the MV-gamma model fitted to HERA structure function data, while for proton-nucleus collisions an optical Glauber model is employed with the Woods-Saxon nuclear density distribution. The calculation uses MSTW NLO PDFs and DSS NLO FFs, with the unsubtracted scheme for rapidity divergence subtraction and momentum-space running coupling for the impact factor.

## Key numbers

* The calculation is performed at LHCb kinematics with center-of-mass energy sqrt(s) = 8.16 TeV and rapidity y = 3.
* The factorization scale is varied such that mu = 2p_perp, 4p_perp, 8p_perp to construct error bands.
* The initial condition for the dipole amplitude uses the MV-gamma model with QCD scale Lambda = 0.241 GeV and initial x_0 = 0.01.
* With LO impact factor but NLO PDF, FF, and dipole, a Cronin peak appears around p_perp approximately 4-5 GeV.
* The nuclear modification factor R_pA is defined at impact parameter b_perp = 0, comparing pPb to pp yields normalized by the number of binary collisions N_bin at b_perp = 0.

## Context

This work extends previous CGC calculations of forward hadron production (Dumitru 2002, Dumitru 2005, Chirilli 2011, Chirilli 2012) by including for the first time NLO corrections to the impact factor consistently with NLO BK evolution of the dipole amplitude, NLO PDFs, and NLO FFs. Prior calculations (Stasto 2013, Watanabe 2015, Shi 2021) did not include NLO corrections to the BK evolution. The results are compared to LHCb forward pPb pion production data (LHCb 2022), and the suppression of the Cronin peak at full NLO resolves a tension with those measurements. The work enables future comparisons with upcoming forward scattering measurements from ALICE (2023) and will be extended to include the target momentum fraction BK (TBK) resummation scheme (Ducloue 2019).
