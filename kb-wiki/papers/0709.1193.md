# Hard exclusive processes with photons

> Virtual photons have proven to be very efficient probes of the hadronic structure, mostly through deep inelastic scattering and related processes. The advent of high luminosity lepton beams has allowed to enlarge the studied processes to hard exclusive reactions, such as deeply virtual Compton scattering and the electroproduction of mesons. We discuss theoretical progress which has lately been quite remarkable in this domain and first much encouraging experimental data.

# Hard exclusive processes with photons

## Key claim
Virtual photons in hard exclusive reactions, such as deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) and meson electroproduction, provide access to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), but the factorization of these amplitudes requires careful treatment of QED gauge invariance, particularly when real photons appear in the final state or when backward kinematics are considered.

## Method
The authors review the theoretical framework of hard exclusive processes involving virtual photons, focusing on the factorization of amplitudes into perturbatively calculable hard-scattering kernels and non-perturbative matrix elements such as GPDs, GDAs, and transition distribution amplitudes (TDAs). They discuss the definitions of these objects as Fourier transforms of light-cone operator matrix elements, the kinematic conditions for factorization (e.g., the generalized Bjorken limit), and the specific challenges posed by QED gauge invariance, including the need to include twist-3 quark-gluon correlators and to separate structure-dependent contributions from inner bremsstrahlung in processes with real photons.

## Key numbers
- The skewedness parameter ξ is related to the virtuality Q² and the squared center-of-mass energy W² by ξ ≈ Q²/(2W² − Q²) in the generalized Bjorken limit.
- The spatial resolution of transverse imaging of quarks and gluons in hadrons is of order 1/Q, determined by the virtuality of the incoming photon.
- For the γ*γ → ρρ process, the ratio dσ(γ*γ → ρ⁰ρ⁰)/dσ(γ*γ → ρ⁺ρ⁻) is proposed as a specific signature for isotensor tetraquark states, with a Q² dependence characteristic of twist-4 contributions.

## Context
This review extends the theoretical work on GPDs introduced by Mueller et al. (1994) and on GDAs introduced by Diehl et al. (1998), and it discusses the application of these concepts to processes studied at HERMES, COMPASS, and JLab. The paper also addresses the factorization of amplitudes for nucleon-antinucleon annihilation into a lepton pair and a meson, as well as backward electroproduction of mesons, which involve TDAs. The authors highlight the potential for hadron tomography via Fourier transforms of GPDs and TDAs, and they discuss the prospects for discovering exotic hybrid mesons and tetraquark states at current and future facilities.
