# Ground State Wave Functions in the Hyperspherical Formalism for Nuclei with A > 4

> The general formulation of a technically advantageous method to find the ground state solution of the Schrodinger equation in configuration space for systems with a number of particles A greater than 4 is presented. The wave function is expanded in pair correlated hyperspherical harmonics beyond the lowest order approximation and then calculated in the Faddeev approach. A recent efficient recursive method to construct antisymmetric A-particle hyperspherical harmonics is used. The accuracy is tested for the bound state energies of nuclei with A = 6,8,12. The high quality of the obtained results becomes evident from a comparison with other approaches.

## Key claim

The paper presents a general formulation of a method combining hyperspherical harmonics, pair correlation, and the Faddeev approach to calculate ground state wave functions for nuclei with A > 4, claiming that to the extent convergence in the expansions is reached the result can be considered an "exact" ground state solution of the many-body Schrodinger equation.

## Method

The authors expand the wave function in pair-correlated hyperspherical harmonics beyond the lowest order approximation, using a Faddeev approach where the total wave function is written as a sum over pair amplitudes. They construct antisymmetric A-particle hyperspherical harmonics using a recursive method that introduces the orthogonal group of kinematic rotations O_{A-1} as an intermediate subgroup, reducing the problem to a hyperradial equation involving at most four-body integrals. The hyperradial equation is solved by expanding the hyperradial functions in Laguerre polynomials.

## Key numbers

* Binding energies calculated for nuclei with A = 6, 8, and 12 (no specific numerical values given in the truncated text)
* The method reduces the 3(A-1)-dimensional Schrodinger equation to a 3x4 dimensional integro-differential equation
* The hyperradial basis functions use Laguerre polynomials L_{n_rho}^a with parameter b and exponent a - (3A-4)/2
* The kinematic rotation angle gamma = arcsin(sqrt((A-2)/(2(A-1)))) in the eta_N, eta_{N-1} plane

## Context

This work extends the Integro-Differential Equation Approach (IDEA) of Brizzi (1996), which used the HH expansion at first order, by including higher order HH functions. It also builds on the stochastic variational method (SVM) of Varga (1995, 1996) and the Green Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) approach of Pieper, Carlson, Pandharipande, and Wiringa (1998), which gave the most accurate results for A <= 9 but cannot generate wave functions. The method enables calculation of wave functions for nuclei with A > 4, which is not possible with GFMC, and can be extended to solve the Schrodinger-like equation with source needed for Lorentz integral transforms of response functions.
