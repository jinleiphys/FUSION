# Self-consistent Green's functions calculation of the nucleon mean-free path

> The extension of Green's functions techniques to the complex energy plane provides access to fully dressed quasi-particle properties from a microscopic perspective. Using self-consistent ladder self-energies, we find both spectra and lifetimes of such quasi-particles in nuclear matter. With a consistent choice of the group velocity, the nucleon mean-free path can be computed. Our results indicate that, for energies above 50 MeV at densities close to saturation, a nucleon has a mean-free path of 4 to 5 femtometers.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, for energies above 50 MeV at densities close to saturation, a nucleon has a mean-free path of 4 to 5 femtometers, but this result is conditional on using a fully dressed quasiparticle pole obtained from self-consistent Green's functions extended to the complex energy plane, rather than relying on simpler approximations that work well only above the Fermi surface.

## Method

The authors extend self-consistent Green's functions (SCGF) techniques to the complex energy plane, using ladder self-energies computed with CD-Bonn and Argonne v18 nucleon-nucleon potentials, with three-body forces included effectively via an average over a correlated third nucleon. They solve the Dyson equation for the analytically continued propagator in the complex plane to find the fully dressed quasiparticle pole, which yields the quasiparticle spectrum and inverse lifetime. The nucleon mean-free path is then computed as the ratio of the group velocity (derived from the fully dressed spectrum) to the inverse lifetime.

## Key numbers

* At saturation density (0.16 fm^-3) and energies above 50 MeV, the nucleon mean-free path is 4 to 5 femtometers.
* At the Fermi surface (k = k_F) and zero temperature, the quasiparticle width is zero, consistent with Fermi liquid theory.
* The theoretical uncertainty from different nucleon-nucleon interactions and three-body forces amounts to less than 1 fm at energies above 50 MeV.
* Calculations were performed at a density of 0.16 fm^-3 and temperatures of 0 MeV and 5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous Green's functions calculations of nuclear matter properties (Frick 2005, Soma 2008) by moving into the complex energy plane to obtain fully dressed quasiparticle properties without ad hoc nonlocality corrections. It provides a microscopic validation of earlier approximations used by Negele (1981), Fantoni (1981a), and Mahaux (1983), which relied on the k-mass approximation, and shows that those approximations are well justified above the Fermi surface. The results enable future systematic studies of the density, temperature, and isospin asymmetry dependence of the nucleon mean-free path, which is essential input for transport simulations and Glauber theory calculations.
