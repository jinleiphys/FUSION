# Nuclear Energy Density Functional for KIDS

> The density functional theory (DFT) is based on the existence and uniqueness of a universal functional $E[ρ]$, which determines the dependence of the total energy on single-particle density distributions. However, DFT says nothing about the form of the functional. Our strategy is to first look at what we know, from independent considerations, about the analytical density dependence of the energy of nuclear matter and then, for practical applications, to obtain an appropriate density-dependent effective interaction by reverse engineering. In a previous work on homogeneous matter, we identified the most essential terms to include in our "KIDS" functional, named after the early-stage participating institutes. We now present first results for finite nuclei, namely the energies and radii of $^{16,28}$O, $^{40,60}$Ca.

# Nuclear Energy Density Functional for KIDS

## Key claim
The KIDS (Korea–IBS–Daegu–Sungkyunkwan) functional, previously developed for homogeneous nuclear matter, can be extended to finite nuclei by reverse-engineering a Skyrme-type effective interaction, providing a "proof of principle" that the approach reproduces ground-state energies and radii of spin-saturated nuclei, though the spin degree of freedom and certain parameters remain unconstrained at this exploratory stage.

## Method
The authors start from a power expansion of the energy per particle in nuclear matter in terms of the Fermi momentum \(k_F\) (or \(\rho^{1/3}\)), with coefficients \(c_i\) determined from fits to pseudodata for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) and pure neutron matter (PNM) from Akmal, Pandharipande, and Ravenhall (1998). They then reverse-engineer a Skyrme-type effective interaction with three density-dependent terms (\(\rho^{1/3}\), \(\rho^{2/3}\), \(\rho\)) and momentum-dependent terms, whose parameters are derived from the \(c_i\) coefficients. The unknown splitting parameter \(k\) between momentum-dependent and density-dependent contributions to the \(c_2\rho^{5/3}\) term is varied, and a Skyrme-Hartree-Fock code is used to compute energies and charge radii of the spin-saturated nuclei \(^{16}\)O, \(^{28}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ca, and \(^{60}\)Ca.

## Key numbers
- Saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter: \(\varrho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\)
- Energy per particle at saturation: \(\EPA(\varrho_0) = -16\) MeV
- Incompressibility: \(K_{\infty} = 240\) MeV
- SNM coefficients: \(c_0(0) = -664.516\) fm\(^3\)MeV, \(c_1(0) = 763.545\) fm\(^4\)MeV, \(c_2(0) = 40.133\) fm\(^5\)MeV
- Skyrme parameters (for \(k\) as a free parameter): \(t_0 = -1772.04\) fm\(^3\)MeV, \(t_1 = 2492.11 \times k\) fm\(^5\)MeV, \(t_2 = -1459.77 \times k\) fm\(^5\)MeV, \(t_3 = 12216.71\) fm\(^4\)MeV, \(t_3' = 642.13 \times (1-k)\) fm\(^5\)MeV, \(t_3'' = 0.00\) fm\(^6\)MeV
- A value of \(k \approx 0.1\) (corresponding to an almost bare-nucleon effective mass) reproduces all finite-nuclei properties well.

## Context
This work extends the KIDS functional approach from homogeneous nuclear matter (previously presented by the same group in Papakonstantinou et al. (2016)) to finite nuclei, providing a first test of the reverse-engineering strategy. It contrasts with traditional nuclear DFT approaches that begin by assuming a specific form for the effective interaction and then derive the energy functional. The results enable future refinements including determination of spin dependence via pseudodata for polarized matter, inclusion of a spin-orbit term, fine-tuning against finite-nuclei data, and studies of excitations within the random-phase approximation (RPA).
