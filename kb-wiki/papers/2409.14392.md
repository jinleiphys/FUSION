# Finding Interaction Mechanism between Exotic Molecule and Conventional Hadron

> An intriguing and important question arises: how do hadronic molecules interact with conventional hadrons? In this work, we propose a novel mechanism to address this issue, focusing on the interactions between hidden-charm molecular pentaquarks ($P_c$) and nucleons ($N$). By applying this mechanism, we derive the corresponding interaction potentials, enabling the prediction of the bound state properties. Our results indicate the possible existence of $P_cN$ bound states-an entirely new form of matter, ripe for exploration in the era of high-precision hadron spectroscopy. Notably, this mechanism extends beyond the $P_cN$ systems and can be applied to any hadronic molecules interacting with conventional hadrons, offering the potential for discovering a variety of novel bound states.

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel mechanism for hadronic molecules interacting with conventional hadrons, where the interaction occurs through the constituent hadrons of the molecule exchanging light mesons with the conventional hadron, but they note that the binding momentum of the $P_c$ states (approximately 10 MeV) means the condition $\sqrt{2\mu E_B}\ll m_\pi$ is not strictly satisfied, introducing uncertainties in the coupling constants.

## Method

The authors treat the $P_c$ states as $\Sigma_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ molecular states and derive the $P_cN$ interaction by computing triangle loop diagrams where the constituent $\Sigma_c$ and $\bar{D}^{(*)}$ hadrons exchange $\pi$, $\rho$, $\omega$, and $\sigma$ mesons with the nucleon, using the one-boson-exchange (OBE) mechanism within the Fixed Center Approximation (FCA) to the Faddeev equations. The effective Lagrangians for the $P_c\Sigma_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ vertices are constructed with coupling constants determined from the binding energies of the $P_c$ states, and the resulting scattering amplitudes are reduced to non-relativistic potentials via the Breit approximation. The potentials are then Fourier-transformed to coordinate space with monopole-type form factors to account for hadron structure and off-shell effects.

## Key numbers

- The $P_c(4312)$, $P_c(4440)$, and $P_c(4457)$ states are treated as $\Sigma_c\bar{D}^{(*)}$ molecular pentaquarks, with binding energies $E_B\sim 10$ MeV
- Coupling constants used include: $l_S = 6.20$, $g_1=0.94$, $\beta_Sf_V = 12.00$, $\lambda_Sf_V = 19.20\;\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$ for $\Sigma_c$ interactions; $g_S = 0.76$, $g=0.59$, $\beta f_V = -5.25$, $\lambda f_V = -3.27\;\mathrm{GeV}^{-1}$ for $\bar{D}^{(*)}$ interactions; $g^2_{\sigma NN}/4\pi = 5.69$, $g^2_{\pi NN}/4\pi = 13.60$, $g^2_{\rho NN}/4\pi = 0.84$, $f_{\rho NN}/g_{\rho NN} = 6.10$ for nucleon interactions
- The form factor parameter $\tilde{\Lambda}=m_D+\alpha\Lambda_{\rm QCD}$ with $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}=0.22\;\mathrm{GeV}$ and $\alpha$ taken as 1.0
- The cutoff parameter $\Lambda$ in the monopole-type form factor is approximately 1 GeV, following studies of the deuteron

## Context

This work extends the OBE framework, previously applied to conventional hadron-hadron interactions (Machleidt, 1987; Tornqvist, 1993), to the novel problem of hadronic molecule-conventional hadron interactions. The authors note that the $\bar{D}N$ interaction has been studied by Hofmann (2005), Gamermann (2010), Haidenbauer (2007), and others with varying conclusions about bound states, while the $\Sigma_cN$ interaction predictions differ between the OBE approach (Liu, 2011) and chiral effective field theory (Haidenbauer, 2017). The mechanism enables predictions of $P_cN$ bound states as a new form of matter, and the authors state it is applicable to any hadronic molecule interacting with conventional hadrons, potentially leading to discovery of novel bound states in the era of high-precision hadron spectroscopy at facilities like LHCb.
