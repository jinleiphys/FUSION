# Model independent study of the Dirac structure of the nucleon-nucleon interaction

> Relativistic and non-relativistic modern nucleon-nucleon potentials are mapped on a relativistic operator basis using projection techniques. This allows to compare the various potentials at the level of covariant amplitudes were a remarkable agreement is found. In nuclear matter large scalar and vector mean fields of several hundred MeV magnitude are generated at tree level. This is found to be a model independent feature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the generation of large scalar and vector mean fields (several hundred MeV) in nuclear matter is a model-independent feature of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, not an artifact of any particular relativistic model, because it appears at tree level from all modern NN potentials when projected onto a covariant operator basis.

## Method

The authors map relativistic one-boson-exchange potentials (Bonn A, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I and II) and non-relativistic potentials (Argonne AV18, EFT Idaho N3LO, and Vlow-k) onto a set of five Lorentz-invariant Fermi covariants (scalar, vector, tensor, axial-vector, pseudoscalar) using standard projection techniques that transform from a partial-wave basis to plane-wave helicity states and then to the covariant basis. They then compute the relativistic self-energy in nuclear matter at tree level in Hartree-Fock approximation by integrating the covariant amplitudes over the Fermi sea.

## Key numbers

- At nuclear saturation density (rho0 ≈ 0.16 fm^-3), empirical relativistic mean field fits give scalar field Σs ≈ -350 MeV and vector field Σo ≈ +300 MeV.
- At Fermi momentum kF = 1.35 fm^-1, all potentials yield scalar fields Σs ≈ -(450 to 400) MeV and vector fields Σo ≈ +(350 to 400) MeV at tree level.
- The ratio of Fermi momentum to nucleon mass is kF/M ≈ 0.25, implying nucleons move at about 1/4 the speed of light.
- The single-particle potential from the cancellation of scalar and vector fields is U_s.p. ≈ -50 MeV.
- Higher-order Brueckner ladder correlations change the size of the fields by less than 20%.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of relativistic OBEPs (Bonn, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen) with non-relativistic potentials (AV18, EFT Idaho, Vlow-k) to the level of covariant amplitudes, finding remarkable agreement despite their different theoretical foundations. The results connect to QCD sum rules (Cohen 1991, Drukarev 1991) and relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations (ter Haar 1987, Gross 1999, van Dalen 2004), which all yield scalar/vector fields of the same sign and magnitude. The paper enables future work to use these covariant amplitudes as input for self-consistent nuclear matter calculations and to test whether the model-independent Dirac structure persists in the in-medium T-matrix.
