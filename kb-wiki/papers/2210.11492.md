# Emergent higher-form symmetry in Higgs phases with superfluidity

> We address emergent higher-form symmetry in Higgs phases with superfluidity. The emergent symmetry appears if a matter field is invariant under a transformation of a common subgroup of gauge and global $\mathrm{U}(1)$ symmetries. We explicitly construct the symmetry generator that is topological and gauge invariant in a low-energy effective theory. Such emergent symmetry is helpful to distinguish the Higgs and other phases with superfluidity, such as phases of QCD at finite density. We also discuss the possibility of phase transition between these phases.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in Higgs phases with superfluidity, an emergent higher-form symmetry (specifically a \(\mathbb{Z}_K\) \((d-1)\)-form symmetry) appears when a matter field is invariant under a common subgroup of the gauge and global \(\mathrm{U}(1)\) symmetries; this symmetry is robust against explicit breaking of flavor symmetry, unlike the \(\mathbb{Z}_3\) valued holonomy discussed in prior work.

## Method

The authors construct topological and gauge-invariant symmetry generators in a low-energy effective theory for a general gauge group \(G_\mathrm{C}\) and global \(\mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{Q}\) symmetry, where a common \(\mathbb{Z}_K\) subgroup exists. They analyze the symmetry-breaking pattern in the Higgs phase, using coset variables \((\xi_\mathrm{C}, \xi_q)\) to build a \(\mathrm{U}(1)\) \((d-1)\)-form generator from \(\xi_q^K\) and a \(\mathbb{Z}_K\) \((d-1)\)-form generator from a flat connection \(a = i \xi_\mathrm{C} d \xi_\mathrm{C}^\dagger\). The formalism is applied to two explicit examples: a \(\mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{C} \times \mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{Q}\) model in \((2+1)\) dimensions and three-flavor QCD at high density (color-flavor locked phase) in \((3+1)\) dimensions.

## Key numbers

* For the \(\mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{C} \times \mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{Q}\) model in \((2+1)\) dimensions, the common subgroup is \(\mathbb{Z}_K\) with \(K=2\); the emergent \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) one-form symmetry generator \(W(n,C)\) takes the value \(-1\) when a vortex defect has winding \(\int (\dd\varphi_+ - \dd\varphi_-)/2 \in (2\mathbb{Z}+1)\pi\).
* For three-flavor QCD in the color-flavor locked phase, the common subgroup is \(\mathbb{Z}_K\) with \(K=3\); the emergent \(\mathbb{Z}_3\) two-form symmetry generator \(W(\rho,C)\) takes the value \(\exp(i 2\pi n m_\rho / 3)\), where \(m_\rho\) is an integer depending on the representation of \(\mathrm{SU}(3)\).
* The \(\mathrm{U}(1)\) \((d-1)\)-form generator in the Higgs phase is constructed using \(K \int_C d\varphi_q\), where \(K=2\) for the \(\mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{C} \times \mathrm{U}(1)_\mathrm{Q}\) model and \(K=3\) for three-flavor QCD.

## Context

This work extends the characterization of Higgs phases using nonlocal order parameters, building on the 't Hooft loop for QED (Tong, Gukov 2013) and the holonomy approach for QCD at finite density (Cherman 2018, Hirono 2018, Cherman 2020). The emergent higher-form symmetry provides a robust distinction between Higgs and confinement phases with superfluidity, even when explicit flavor symmetry breaking destabilizes the \(\mathbb{Z}_3\) valued holonomy discussed by Hirono (2018). The results enable future nonperturbative studies of phase transitions between confinement and Higgs phases, using a gauge-invariant order parameter \(\Delta\) proposed in Eq. (13).
