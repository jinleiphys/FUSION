# Spin interaction of non-relativistic neutrons with an ultrashort laser pulse

> The non-relativistic Pauli equation is used to study the interaction of slow neutrons with a short magnetic pulse. In the extreme limit, the pulse is acting on the magnetic moment of the neutron only at one instant of time. We obtain the scattering amplitude by deriving the junction conditions for the Pauli wave function across the pulse. Explicit expressions are given for a beam of polarized plane wave neutrons subjected to a pulse of spatially constant magnetic field strength. Assuming that the magnetic field is generated by an ultrashort laser pulse, we provide crude numerical estimates.

## Key claim

The authors model an ultrashort laser pulse as a Dirac-delta magnetic pulse acting on the neutron's magnetic moment at a single instant, and derive exact junction conditions for the Pauli wave function; the numerical estimates are explicitly described as "crude" and the effect is only "in principle measurable" under idealized conditions.

## Method

The non-relativistic Pauli equation is solved for a magnetic field proportional to a Dirac delta in time, using Colombeau theory of generalized functions to handle the ill-defined product of distributions. The matching condition across the pulse is derived by imposing conservation of probability density, yielding a unitary transformation of the spinor at the pulse location. For a spatially constant magnetic field, the solution reduces to a spin rotation of an incident plane wave neutron beam, with the momentum unchanged.

## Key numbers

- Neutron magnetic moment: μ = 0.96623645(24) × 10⁻²⁶ J T⁻¹
- Laser pulse duration: τ ≈ 20 fs
- Peak laser intensity: I ≈ 5.5 × 10²² W/cm² (J-KAREN-P laser at KPSI, Japan)
- Peak magnetic field strength: B ≈ 1.5 × 10⁶ T (from Poynting vector estimate)
- Pulse energy: E = 5 J, cross-sectional area A = 2 (μm)², duration τ = 20 fs
- Characteristic dimensionless parameter: (μτ/ℏ)B ≈ 0.3
- Spin-flip probability: |⟨χ↓|χ₊⟩|² ≈ 0.09
- Final spin expectation values: ⟨S_z⟩ ≈ 0.83 (ℏ/2), ⟨S_y⟩ ≈ −0.56 (ℏ/2)

## Context

This work extends the Colombeau-based junction-condition approach previously applied to the Schrödinger equation by Baumann (1997) and Baumann and Aichelburg (2018) to the Pauli equation for neutral spin-1/2 particles. It provides an analytically tractable model for neutron spin manipulation by ultrashort laser pulses, complementing experimental efforts to generate neutron beams via laser-matter interaction (GU, 2022). The authors note that a full relativistic treatment using the Dirac equation is needed to account for electric field effects in the neutron's rest frame, which would introduce corrections of order (v/c).
