# Geometry of Configuration Mixing in Bose-Fermi Systems

> A geometric interpretation for an algebraic interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing is presented. The formalism is based on an extended Bose-Fermi matrix coherent states and is applied to gain insight on intertwined quantum shape-phase transitions and shape coexistence in odd-mass Nb nuclei.

# Geometry of Configuration Mixing in Bose-Fermi Systems

## Key claim
The paper presents a geometric interpretation for the interacting boson-fermion model with configuration mixing (IBFM-CM) using extended matrix coherent states, but the formalism is specifically applied to and validated only for odd-mass Nb isotopes with a single fermion in a $j=9/2$ orbital, where the classical analysis captures essential features of the quantum analysis of shape evolution and coexistence.

## Method
The authors construct an enlarged $(2j+1)\times(2j+1)$ potential matrix from the IBFM-CM Hamiltonian by taking expectation values in product states of bosonic projective coherent states (parameterized by quadrupole shape variables $\beta,\gamma$) and fermionic basis states $\ket{j,m}$. For axial shapes ($\gamma=0$), the matrix block-diagonalizes into $2\times2$ matrices $M_K(\beta)$ for each $K$ quantum number, whose diagonalization yields eigen-potentials $E_{\pm,K}(\beta)$ and mixing amplitudes that describe the geometry of normal and intruder configurations coupled to the odd fermion.

## Key numbers
- For $^{93}$Nb, all $K$-surfaces $E_{-,K}(\beta)$ have a minimum at $\beta=0$, reflecting a spherical core shape weakly coupled to the $j$-fermion.
- For $^{103}$Nb, the intruder $B$ configuration equilibrium deformations reach $\beta_{\text{eq}}(E_{B,K})\approx 0.9\!-\!1.2$ (except for $K=9/2$), while the normal $A$ configuration remains spherical with $\beta_{\text{eq}}(E_{A,K}) < 0.2$ along the entire Nb chain.
- The mixing parameter $\omega$ ranges from 0.1 MeV ($^{93,95}$Nb) to 0.02 MeV ($^{97-103}$Nb), and the energy offset $\Delta_B$ ranges from 1.84 MeV ($^{97}$Nb) to 0.8 MeV ($^{101,103}$Nb).
- The boson number $N$ in the normal $A$ configuration ranges from 1 ($^{93}$Nb) to 6 ($^{103}$Nb), with the intruder $B$ configuration having $N+2$ bosons.

## Context
This work extends the geometric formalism previously developed for the single-configuration interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) by Leviatan (1988) and Alonso (1992) to include configuration mixing, analogous to how the IBM-CM formalism (Vargas, 2004) extended the IBM for even-even nuclei. The application to odd-mass Nb isotopes builds on recent quantum IBFM-CM studies by Gavrielov and Leviatan (2022, 2023) that revealed intertwined Type I and Type II quantum shape-phase transitions in these nuclei and their Zr cores. The formalism enables classical analysis of shape coexistence and configuration mixing in Bose-Fermi systems, providing geometric insight into the quantum results and opening opportunities for studying general algebraic coexistence models with mixed statistics.
