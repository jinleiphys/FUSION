# Using TDHF to study quasifission dynamics

> We show that the microscopic TDHF approach provides an important tool to shed some light on the nuclear dynamics leading to the formation of superheavy elements. In particular, we discuss studying quasifission dynamics and calculating ingredients for compound nucleus formation probability calculations.

## Key claim
The paper argues that the microscopic TDHF approach, particularly the DC-TDHF method, can provide quantitative insight into quasifission dynamics and compound nucleus formation, but the results are limited to the dominant reaction channel due to the single-Slater-determinant approximation.

## Method
The authors performed 3D TDHF calculations on a Cartesian grid without symmetry restrictions for central and non-central collisions of \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{249}\)Bk and \(^{50}\)Ti+\(^{249}\)Bk. They used the density-constrained TDHF (DC-TDHF) method to compute nucleus-nucleus potential barriers, contact times, fragment masses and charges, and excitation energies of individual fragments. They also extracted the time-dependent moment-of-inertia tensor from TDHF densities to calculate effective moments of inertia for quasifission.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{249}\)Bk: tip orientation barrier \(E_B\)(tip)=191.22 MeV; side orientation barrier \(E_B\)(side)=204.36 MeV.
- For \(^{50}\)Ti+\(^{249}\)Bk: tip orientation barrier \(E_B\)(tip)=211.2 MeV; side orientation barrier \(E_B\)(side)=224.6 MeV.
- For \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{249}\)Bk side orientation at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=210\) MeV, contact times reach up to 22 zs; fusion (contact time exceeding 35 zs) occurs above 211 MeV.
- For \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{249}\)Bk side orientation, quasifission is limited to \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=\) 209–211 MeV; for tip orientation, quasifission spans \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=\) 191–230 MeV.
- For \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{249}\)Bk side orientation quasifission: heavy fragment excitation energy 55–60 MeV, light fragment 30–45 MeV; tip orientation: heavy fragment ~50 MeV, light fragment ~30 MeV.
- For \(^{50}\)Ti+\(^{249}\)Bk tip orientation quasifission: heavy fragment \(E^{*}_H=\) 57–69 MeV, light fragment \(E^{*}_L=\) 27–41 MeV; side orientation quasifission: \(E^{*}_H\simeq36\) MeV, \(E^{*}_L\simeq13\) MeV.
- For \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{249}\)Bk non-central collisions at \(E_{\mathrm{c.m.}}=218\) MeV, the moment-of-inertia ratio at final touching configuration ranges from 1.4 to 1.8.

## Context
This work extends earlier DC-TDHF studies of capture cross sections for superheavy element \(Z=112\) (Umar 2010) and quasifission in \(^{40,48}\)Ca+\(^{238}\)U (Wakhle 2014, Oberacker 2014). It directly addresses the experimental puzzle of why \(^{48}\)Ca beams are uniquely effective for superheavy element synthesis, compared to \(^{50}\)Ti beams, by comparing the \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{249}\)Bk and \(^{50}\)Ti+\(^{249}\)Bk reactions. The results enable future extensions to the Transition State Model (TSM) by providing dynamically calculated moments of inertia, which are currently treated with an arbitrary ratio of \(\Im_0/\Im_{\mathrm{eff}}=1.5\) (Yanez 2013).
