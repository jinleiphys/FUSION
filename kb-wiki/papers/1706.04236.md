# Small-$x$ Asymptotics of the Gluon Helicity Distribution

> We determine the small-$x$ asymptotics of the gluon helicity distribution in a proton at leading order in perturbative QCD at large $N_c$. To achieve this, we begin by evaluating the dipole gluon helicity TMD at small $x$. In the process we obtain an interesting new result: in contrast to the unpolarized dipole gluon TMD case, the operator governing the small-$x$ behavior of the dipole gluon helicity TMD is different from the operator corresponding to the polarized dipole scattering amplitude (used in our previous work to determine the small-$x$ asymptotics of the quark helicity distribution). We then construct and solve novel small-$x$ large-$N_c$ evolution equations for the operator related to the dipole gluon helicity TMD. Our main result is the small-$x$ asymptotics for the gluon helicity distribution: $ΔG \sim \left( \tfrac{1}{x} \right)^{α_h^G}$ with $α_h^G = \tfrac{13}{4 \sqrt{3}} \, \sqrt{\tfrac{α_s \, N_c}{2 π}} \approx 1.88 \, \sqrt{\tfrac{α_s \, N_c}{2 π}}$. We note that the power $α_h^G$ is approximately 20$\%$ lower than the corresponding power $α_h^q$ for the small-$x$ asymptotics of the quark helicity distribution defined by $Δq \sim \left( \tfrac{1}{x} \right)^{α_h^q}$ with $α_h^q = \tfrac{4}{\sqrt{3}} \, \sqrt{\tfrac{α_s \, N_c}{2 π}} \approx 2.31 \, \sqrt{\tfrac{α_s \, N_c}{2 π}}$ found in our earlier work.

## Key claim

The small-x asymptotics of the gluon helicity distribution is determined at leading order in perturbative QCD at large Nc, yielding a power-law behavior ΔG ~ (1/x)^{α_h^G} with α_h^G = (13/(4√3)) √(α_s Nc/(2π)) ≈ 1.88 √(α_s Nc/(2π)), which is approximately 20% lower than the corresponding quark helicity intercept α_h^q ≈ 2.31 √(α_s Nc/(2π)) found in the authors' earlier work.

## Method

The authors first evaluated the dipole gluon helicity transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) parton distribution at small x, expressing it in terms of light-cone Wilson lines with an insertion of the sub-eikonal longitudinal spin-dependent gluon field of the target. They then constructed and solved novel small-x large-Nc evolution equations for the operator related to the dipole gluon helicity TMD, which mix this "gluon helicity operator" with the "quark helicity operator" given by the polarized dipole amplitude from their previous work (Kovchegov, Pitonyak, Sievert 2015, 2016, 2017). The evolution equations resum double logarithms of energy, powers of α_s ln^2(1/x), in contrast to the leading-logarithmic resummation of α_s ln(1/x) in the unpolarized Balitsky-Fadin-Kuraev-Lipatov and Balitsky-Kovchegov/Jalilian-Marian-Iancu-McLerran-Weigert-Leonidov-Kovner equations.

## Key numbers

* The gluon helicity intercept: α_h^G = 13/(4√3) √(α_s Nc/(2π)) ≈ 1.88 √(α_s Nc/(2π))
* The quark helicity intercept (from earlier work): α_h^q = 4/√3 √(α_s Nc/(2π)) ≈ 2.31 √(α_s Nc/(2π))
* The gluon helicity intercept is approximately 20% lower than the quark helicity intercept
* The flavor-singlet quark helicity intercept α_h^q was about 30% smaller than that found by Bartels, Ermolaev and Ryskin (1996)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous derivation of small-x helicity evolution equations for the quark helicity distribution (Kovchegov, Pitonyak, Sievert 2015, 2016, 2017) to the gluon helicity distribution. The authors note that the operator governing the small-x behavior of the dipole gluon helicity TMD is different from the operator corresponding to the polarized dipole scattering amplitude used for the quark helicity distribution, in contrast to the unpolarized case where the dipole gluon TMD relates directly to the forward scattering amplitude for a qqbar dipole. The results enable extrapolation of helicity PDFs to the experimentally inaccessible small-x region, which is important for resolving the proton spin puzzle and for predictions at future facilities such as the Electron-Ion Collider.
