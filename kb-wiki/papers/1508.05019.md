# Neutron star structure from QCD

> In this review article, we argue that our current understanding of the thermodynamic properties of cold QCD matter, originating from first principles calculations at high and low densities, can be used to efficiently constrain the macroscopic properties of neutron stars. In particular, we demonstrate that combining state-of-the-art results from Chiral Effective Theory and perturbative QCD with the current bounds on neutron star masses, the Equation of State of neutron star matter can be obtained to an accuracy better than 30% at all densities.

## Key claim

Combining state-of-the-art results from Chiral Effective Theory (CET) and perturbative QCD (pQCD) with current bounds on neutron star masses constrains the Equation of State of neutron star matter to an accuracy better than 30% at all densities, provided the relative errors of the two approaches are taken as ±24% and the EoS must satisfy thermodynamic stability, subluminality, and support a two solar mass star.

## Method

The authors review the current status of perturbative QCD calculations of the Equation of State of cold quark matter, including a three-loop (order g⁴) result at zero temperature with nonzero quark masses from Kurkela (2009). They then describe an interpolation procedure from Kurkela (2014) that uses polytropes to parameterize the EoS in the density region between the CET and pQCD regimes, applying the criteria of thermodynamic stability, subluminality, and the ability to support a two solar mass star to constrain the allowed EoS band.

## Key numbers

- The relative errors of the CET and pQCD approaches used for switching to interpolating polytropes are ±24%.
- The combined constraints yield an EoS accuracy better than 30% at all densities.
- The pQCD pressure at zero temperature is computed to order g⁴, with results shown for baryon chemical potentials μ_B of order 2.5–3 GeV where the error bar decreases rapidly.
- A pocket formula for the pQCD pressure in beta equilibrium is given: P_QCD(μ_B,X) = P_SB(μ_B) (c₁ - a(X)/((μ_B/GeV) - b(X))), with c₁ = 0.9008, d₁ = 0.5034, d₂ = 1.452, ν₁ = 0.3553, ν₂ = 0.9101, valid for μ_B < 2 GeV, P(μ_B) > 0, and X ∈ [1,4].
- The high-temperature EoS of QGP is known up to O(g⁶ ln g) at high temperatures and at most moderate chemical potentials μ_B ≤ 10T.

## Context

This review extends the approach of Kurkela (2014), which used interpolating polytropes between CET and pQCD to constrain the neutron star matter EoS, and builds on perturbative calculations by Kurkela (2009) at zero temperature and by Kajantie (2002) and Vuorinen (2003) at high temperature. The work enables future efforts to reduce the current uncertainty in the EoS of neutron star matter, with the most crucial step being the determination of the next orders in the weak coupling expansion (order g⁶ ln g and the full O(g⁶) four-loop result).
