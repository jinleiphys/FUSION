# An overview of symmetric nuclear matter properties from chiral interactions up to fourth order of the chiral expansion

> We present and discuss predictions for a cross section of bulk and single-particle properties in symmetric nuclear matter based on recent high-quality nucleon-nucleon potentials at N3LO and including all subleading three-nucleon forces. We begin with the equation of state and its saturation properties and proceed to the single-nucleon potential. We also explore short-range correlations as seen through the defect function. The various predictions which we present have a common foundation in an internally consistent ab initio approach.

## Key claim

Chiral effective field theory (EFT) predictions for symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) at N³LO, including all subleading three-nucleon forces (3NFs), produce an equation of state (EoS) with realistic saturation properties, but only when using a softer cutoff (450 MeV) and specific choices of the low-energy constants (LECs) c_D and c_E; the best saturating behavior is obtained with set (c) of these LECs.

## Method

The authors computed the EoS of SNM using the nonperturbative particle-particle ladder approximation (Brueckner G-matrix) with chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potentials at orders from LO to N³LO, employing a cutoff Λ = 450 MeV. Three-nucleon forces at N²LO and N³LO were incorporated as density-dependent effective two-nucleon interactions in the partial wave formalism, with LECs taken from Drischler et al. (2019). The single-particle spectrum was computed self-consistently, and short-range correlations were analyzed through the momentum-space defect function and the wound integral.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density at N³LO: ρ₀ = (0.161 ± 0.015) fm⁻³
- Saturation energy at N³LO: e(ρ₀) = (-14.98 ± 0.85) MeV
- Incompressibility at N³LO: K₀ = (216 ± 33) MeV
- At k_F = 1.3 fm⁻¹, the wound integral from all partial waves with N³LO+3NF is 0.151, compared to 0.085 with N³LO 2NF only
- At k_F = 1.4 fm⁻¹, the wound integral from all partial waves with N³LO+3NF is 0.1599, compared to 0.073 with N³LO 2NF only
- The wound integral from all partial waves at k_F = 1.3 fm⁻¹ is 0.085 for N³LO(450), 0.114 for CD-Bonn, and 0.157 for AV18 (2NFs only)

## Context

This work extends order-by-order chiral EFT calculations of nuclear matter, building on the high-quality NN potentials of Entem, Machleidt, and Nosyk (2017) and the neutron matter study of Sammarruca and Millerson (2021). It provides consistent N³LO predictions for SNM that agree within uncertainties with the many-body calculations of Drischler et al. (2019) using the same 2NF. The results enable future work on neutron skins, neutron stars, and pairing correlations in nuclear and neutron matter.
