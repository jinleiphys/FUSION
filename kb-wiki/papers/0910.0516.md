# $n\bar{n}$ transitions in medium

> The models of $n\bar{n}$ transitions in the medium based on unitary $S$-matrix are considered. The time-dependence and corrections to the models are studied. The lower limits on the free-space $n\bar{n}$ oscillation time are obtained as well.

# n-nbar transitions in medium

## Key claim

The paper argues that the standard optical-potential model for neutron-antineutron (n-nbar) transitions in nuclear matter gives the wrong dependence on the antineutron annihilation width, and that two alternative unitary S-matrix models (labeled "a" and "b") yield fundamentally different results for the transition probability and the resulting lower limit on the free-space n-nbar oscillation time, with the author expressing caution about both models due to unresolved theoretical issues.

## Method

The author constructs two field-theoretical models for n-nbar transitions in medium followed by antineutron annihilation, both based on a Hermitian Hamiltonian and unitary S-matrix. Model "a" uses a bare antineutron propagator with zero self-energy, while model "b" uses a dressed propagator with a scalar field V representing the antineutron self-energy. The process amplitude is calculated in lowest order in the n-nbar conversion Hamiltonian, and the time-dependence of the transition probability is derived for each model, with corrections from additional baryon-number-violating diagrams also evaluated.

## Key numbers

- For model "b", the ratio of transition probabilities to the standard optical-potential result is r = W_b / W_stan = 25, using Gamma = 100 MeV and Sigma = Re U_nbar - U_n = 10 MeV.
- The lower limit on the free-space n-nbar oscillation time from model "b" is tau^b_min = (3.5 - 7.5) x 10^8 s.
- For model "a", the transition probability follows W_a(t) ~ epsilon_nbar^2 t^2 for Gamma t >> 1, giving a lower limit tau^a_min = 10^16 yr.
- The contribution of diagram 3a to model "b" is suppressed by W_3a / W_b = V^2 / m_Phi^2 << 1.
- For model "a", the ratio of diagram 3a contribution to the main process is W_3a(t) / W_a(t) = Gamma / (m_pi^2 t), which is negligible when m_pi^2 t / Gamma >> 1 and Gamma t >> 1, with Gamma ~ 100 MeV and observation time T_0 = 1.3 yr in proton-decay type experiments.
- The free-space n-nbar transition operator comes from Higgs boson exchange with mass m_H > 10^5 GeV, and medium corrections to the vertex are less than 0.25 based on nuclear beta decay renormalization effects.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous diagrammatic approach (Nazaruk 2008) and addresses criticisms from other researchers (2009) who showed that earlier models using propagators instead of wave functions for the neutron line were unsuitable, leading to divergent results when the neutron binding energy goes to zero. The paper contrasts its unitary S-matrix models with the standard optical-potential calculations used in prior n-nbar transition studies (Dover, Gal, and Richard 1983; Kondratyuk 1982; Kopeliovich 1983), which the author argues give incorrect Gamma-dependence for the total process probability. The results enable improved experimental constraints on free-space n-nbar oscillation time, with the two models providing lower bounds differing by many orders of magnitude, and the author calls for further investigations to resolve the theoretical ambiguity.
