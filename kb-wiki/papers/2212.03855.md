# Detectability of Sub-Solar Mass Neutron Stars Through a Template Bank Search

> We study the detectability of gravitational-wave signals from sub-solar mass binary neutron star systems by the current generation of ground-based gravitational-wave detectors. We find that finite size effects from large tidal deformabilities of the neutron stars and lower merger frequencies can significantly impact the sensitivity of the detectors to these sources. By simulating a matched-filter based search using injected binary neutron star signals with tidal deformabilities derived from physically motivated equations of state, we calculate the reduction in sensitivity of the detectors. We conclude that the loss in sensitive volume can be as high as $78.4 \%$ for an equal mass binary system of chirp mass $0.17 \, \textrm{M}_{\odot}$, in a search conducted using binary black hole template banks. We use this loss in sensitive volume, in combination with the results from the search for sub-solar mass binaries conducted on data collected by the LIGO-Virgo observatories during their first three observing runs, to obtain a conservative upper limit on the merger rate of sub-solar mass binary neutron stars. Since the discovery of a low-mass neutron star would provide new insight into formation mechanisms of neutron stars and further constrain the equation of state of dense nuclear matter, our result merits a dedicated search for sub-solar mass binary neutron star signals.

## Key claim

Finite-size effects from large tidal deformabilities and lower merger frequencies of sub-solar mass neutron stars can cause a loss in sensitive volume of up to 78.4% for an equal-mass binary system of chirp mass 0.17 M⊙ when searched using binary black hole template banks, motivating a dedicated search for sub-solar mass binary neutron star signals.

## Method

The authors simulated a matched-filter search using the PyCBC toolkit, injecting binary neutron star signals with tidal deformabilities derived from three equations of state (APR, SLy4, BSk21) into the Advanced LIGO zero-detuned high power noise curve. They constructed a template bank of 1.7 × 10^6 templates spanning component masses 0.2–1.0 M⊙ and computed fitting factors to quantify the mismatch between binary neutron star signals and binary black hole templates. The merger frequencies were estimated from quasi-circular equilibrium simulations using the LORENE library to determine Roche lobe overflow frequencies.

## Key numbers

- Loss in sensitive volume can be as high as 78.4% for an equal-mass binary system of chirp mass 0.17 M⊙ when searched using binary black hole template banks
- Template bank contained 1.7 × 10^6 templates spanning the mass range m1, m2 ∈ [0.2, 1.0] M⊙
- For the APR equation of state terminated at Roche lobe overflow, 63.06% of injected signals had fitting factors below the minimal match of 0.95
- For the BSk21 equation of state terminated at ISCO, 87.12% of injected signals had fitting factors below the minimal match of 0.95
- Minimum fitting factor for APR equation of state with Roche lobe overflow termination was 0.6024
- Upper limit on merger rate of sub-solar mass binary neutron stars derived as [830–210,000] Gpc⁻³ yr⁻¹ for the chirp mass range (0.17, 0.87) M⊙

## Context

This work extends the search for sub-solar mass compact binaries previously conducted by LIGO and Virgo (Nitz 2022), which assumed zero tidal deformability and used binary black hole templates. The authors show that matter effects from large tidal deformabilities and lower merger frequencies significantly reduce detection efficiency, building on earlier work by Cullen (2017) on matter effects in binary neutron star searches. The results enable future dedicated searches using template banks that incorporate tidal deformabilities and physical merger frequencies, which would be more sensitive to sub-solar mass binary neutron star signals than existing binary black hole searches.
