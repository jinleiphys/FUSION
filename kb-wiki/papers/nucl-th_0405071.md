# Many-body systems in the presence of the random interaction and the $J$ pairing interaction

> In this talk I shall discuss some regularities of many-body systems in the presence of random interactions and regularities of a single-$j$ shell for the $J$ pairing interaction which works only when two particles are coupled to spin $J$. I shall first explain an empirical rule to predict the spin $I$ ground state probability. Then I shall present some interesting results of a single-$j$ shell under the $J$ pairing interaction. Last I shall discuss some preliminary results of binding energies in the presence of random two-body interactions.

# Many-body systems in the presence of the random interaction and the $J$ pairing interaction

## Key claim

The empirical rule for predicting ground-state spin probabilities, setting one two-body matrix element to -1 and all others to zero, then counting how often each spin gives the lowest eigenvalue, yields good agreement with full random-interaction calculations for fermions in a single-$j$ shell, but the rule is approximate and the agreement is not exact (e.g., predicted 60% vs. calculated 66.4% for spin 0 with four fermions in $j=9/2$).

## Method

For a single-$j$ shell with $j=9/2$, the Hamiltonian is defined as $H = \sum_J G_J A^{J\dagger} \cdot A^J$, where $G_J$ are the two-body matrix elements. The empirical rule sets each $G_J$ to -1 in turn (with all others zero) and records which total spin $I$ gives the lowest eigenvalue; the predicted probability $P(I)$ is the fraction of such calculations where spin $I$ wins. For the $J$ pairing interaction (where $G_{J'} = -\delta_{JJ'}$), the three-fermion system is exactly solvable with a single non-zero eigenvalue given by a closed-form expression involving a six-$j$ symbol. For four or more fermions under the $J_{\text{max}}$ pairing interaction, eigenvalues are classified as integer (asymptotically 0, -1, -2) or non-integer, and the non-integer eigenvalues are shown to correspond to a three-particle cluster plus a spectator particle.

## Key numbers

- For four fermions ($n=4$) in a single-$j$ shell with $j=9/2$, the empirical rule predicts $P(0)=60\%$, $P(4)=20\%$, $P(I_{\text{max}})=20\%$; random-interaction calculations give $P(0)=66.4\%$, $P(4)=11.8\%$, $P(I_{\text{max}})=17.9\%$.
- For five fermions ($n=5$) in the same shell, the empirical rule predicts $P(j)=40\%$, $P(3/2)=20\%$, $P(5/2)=20\%$, $P(I_{\text{max}})=20\%$; random-interaction calculations give $P(j)=33.9\%$, $P(3/2)=20.5\%$, $P(5/2)=15.5\%$, $P(I_{\text{max}})=18.4\%$.
- The predicted $P(I_{\text{max}})$ for a single-$j$ shell is $1/N = 1/(j+1/2)$, independent of particle number $n$; for $j=9/2$, $N=5$ and $P(I_{\text{max}})=20\%$.
- For three fermions ($n=3$) with the $J$ pairing interaction, the non-zero eigenvalue is given by $E^{(3)}_{I^{(3)}, J(j)} = -1 - 2(2j+1) \left\{ \begin{array}{ccc} J & j & I^{(3)} \\ J & j & j \end{array} \right\}$.
- For four fermions under the $J_{\text{max}}$ pairing interaction, many eigenvalues are asymptotically integers (0, -1, and -2); the non-integer eigenvalues of $n=4$ are very close to those of $n=3$, with overlaps of 1.0 within high precision.

## Context

This work extends the discovery of spin-0 ground-state dominance in random interactions by Johnson, Bertsch, Dean, and Talmi (1998) and the subsequent review by Zhao (2003). The empirical rule was proposed by Arima and collaborators (2003) as a simple predictive tool. The exact solvability of three fermions for any $J$ pairing interaction was shown in Arima and Zhao (2004), and the cluster structure for $J_{\text{max}}$ pairing was studied by Ginocchio (2004). The parity distribution results and the Garvey-Kelson relation tests are preliminary; the latter respond to the claim by Bohigas and Leboeuf (2004) that chaotic components impose a lower limit on the accuracy of theoretical binding-energy predictions. The work enables further exploration of broken symmetries in $J_{\text{max}}$ pairing and deeper understanding of ground-state spin and parity regularities in random-interaction ensembles.
