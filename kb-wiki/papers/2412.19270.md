# Investigating nuclear density profiles to reveal particle-hole configurations in the island of inversion

> Background: In the mass regions with an abnormal shell structure, the so-called ``island of inversion," the spin-parity of odd-mass nuclei provides quantitative insights into the shell evolution. However, the experimental determination of the spin-parity is often challenging, leaving it undetermined in many nuclei. Purpose: We discuss how the shell structure affects the density profiles of nuclei in the island of inversion and investigate whether these can be probed from the total reaction and elastic scattering cross sections. Method: The antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) is employed to generate various particle-hole configurations and predict the energy levels of these nuclei. The obtained density distributions are used as inputs to the Glauber model, which is employed to calculate the total reaction and elastic scattering cross sections for revealing their relationship to the particle-hole configurations. Results: In addition to the well-known correlation between nuclear deformation and radius, we show the correlations between the particle-hole configurations and both central density and diffuseness. We show that different particle-hole configurations are well reflected in the total reaction and elastic scattering cross sections. Conclusion: The total reaction and elastic scattering cross sections are useful probes to identify the spin-parity of nuclei when different particle-hole configurations coexist.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that total reaction and elastic scattering cross sections can serve as probes to identify the spin-parity of nuclei in the island of inversion when different particle-hole configurations coexist, but the method's effectiveness depends on the specific nucleus and may be limited when cross section differences are small.

## Method

The authors employed antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective interaction to generate various particle-hole configurations and predict energy levels for nuclei in the island of inversion. The resulting density distributions were used as inputs to the Glauber model to calculate total reaction cross sections on a carbon target and elastic scattering cross sections for proton scattering, allowing correlation between particle-hole configurations and observable cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For $^{31}$Mg: the $1/2^{+}$ (2p3h) ground state has a matter radius of 3.29 fm, diffuseness parameter 0.63 fm, and total reaction cross section 1349 mb at 240 MeV/nucleon on a carbon target; the measured value is 1329(11) mb
- For $^{31}$Mg: the $3/2_{2}^{+}$ (0p1h) state has diffuseness 0.53 fm and total reaction cross section 1280 mb; the $3/2_{1}^{-}$ (3p4h) state has diffuseness 0.66 fm and cross section 1373 mb
- For $^{31}$Mg: the first diffraction peak height of the $3/2_{1}^{-}$ (3p4h) state is 30 mb/sr at 800 MeV/nucleon, approximately 60% smaller than the $3/2_{2}^{+}$ (0p1h) state
- For $^{29}$Ne: the $3/2_{2}^{-}$ (3p4h) state has total reaction cross section 1348 mb at 240 MeV/nucleon, matching the measured interaction cross section of 1344(13) mb
- For $^{33}$Mg: the $3/2_{1}^{-}$ (3p2h) state has total reaction cross section 1393 mb at 240 MeV/nucleon, closest to the measured value of 1399(12) mb; the $3/2_{1}^{+}$ (4p3h) state gives 1423 mb and the $3/2_{2}^{+}$ (2p1h) state gives 1365 mb
- For $^{35}$Mg: the $5/2_{1}^{-}$ (5p2h) state has total reaction cross section 1444 mb at 240 MeV/nucleon, matching the measured value of 1443(12) mb; the first diffraction peak heights for different states differ by only 2.7 mb/sr

## Context

This work extends the approach of using nuclear density profiles and reaction cross sections to probe nuclear structure in the island of inversion, building on prior studies that established correlations between deformation and radius (Takechi 2014, Horiuchi 2012) and between diffuseness and particle-hole configurations in Ne and Mg isotopic chains (V.C. 2021). The method enables identification of spin-parity assignments for nuclei where experimental determination remains challenging, such as $^{29}$Ne, $^{33}$Mg, and $^{35}$Mg, though the authors note that for $^{35}$Mg the cross section differences are too small for definitive assignment. The work uses AMD calculations consistent with earlier results by Kimura (2007) and compares with shell model calculations using SDPF-M and SDPF-U-SI interactions.
