# The hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars

> We study the hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of neutron stars (NS). For the hadronic sector, we use a microscopic equation of state (EOS) involving nucleons and hyperons derived within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach. For the quark sector, we employ the MIT bag model, as well as the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) and the Color Dielectric (CD) models, and find that the NS maximum masses lie in the interval between 1.5 and 1.8 solar masses.

# The hadron-quark phase transition in neutron stars

## Key claim

The maximum mass of neutron stars containing a hadron-quark phase transition lies between 1.5 and 1.97 solar masses, depending on which quark matter model is used, but the inclusion of color superconductivity in the NJL model reduces the maximum mass to 1.77 solar masses, keeping it well below two solar masses.

## Method

The hadronic equation of state was computed using the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with nucleons and hyperons, including three-body forces. For the quark sector, the MIT bag model (with both constant and density-dependent bag constants), the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, and the Color Dielectric model were employed, and the hadron-quark phase transition was treated via the Maxwell construction. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were then solved to obtain neutron star mass-radius relations and maximum masses.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses lie between 1.5 and 1.97 solar masses across all quark models considered
- With color superconductivity included in the NJL model, the maximum mass decreases to 1.77 solar masses
- Hyperon onset densities are about 2 to 3 times normal nuclear matter density for the Sigma- and Lambda hyperons
- Phase transition density in the extended MIT bag model is approximately 0.6 fm^{-3}
- Phase transition density in the Color Dielectric model is approximately 0.05 fm^{-3}
- Neutron stars built with purely nucleonic plus hyperonic EOS have maximum mass less than 1.3 solar masses, below the observational limit of 1.44 solar masses

## Context

This work extends earlier Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations by Burgio and collaborators (2003) that included hyperons and found maximum masses below the observational limit, by now adding quark matter degrees of freedom. The study compares three quark models (MIT bag, NJL, and Color Dielectric) and finds that the observational detection of a neutron star with mass above 1.8 solar masses would challenge current theoretical modeling of high-density nuclear matter or the assumptions about the hadron-quark phase transition. The results enable future work to constrain quark matter models using neutron star mass measurements.
