# Nuclear correlation functions in lattice QCD

> We consider the problem of calculating the large number of Wick contractions necessary to compute states with the quantum numbers of many baryons in lattice QCD. We consider a constructive approach and a determinant-based approach and show that these methods allow the required contractions to be performed in computationally manageable amount of time for certain choices of interpolating operators. Examples of correlation functions computed using these techniques are shown for the quantum numbers of the light nuclei, He, Be, C, O and Si.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that lattice QCD correlation functions for nuclei up to atomic number A=28 can be computed with computationally feasible Wick contractions, but only when using specially constructed interpolating fields with simple spatial wave-functions; the extracted energies for systems with A<20 are consistent with A non-interacting nucleons but with large uncertainties, and no clean extraction of ground state binding energies is achieved.

## Method

The authors construct nuclear interpolating fields using a hadronic basis of octet baryons combined via Clebsch-Gordan coefficients, then translate these to quark-level interpolating fields. They implement two contraction methods: a quark-hadron approach using baryon building blocks that scales factorially with quark number, and a determinant-based approach that replaces Wick contractions with determinants of quark propagator matrices, scaling polynomially as n_u^3 n_d^3 n_s^3. The determinant method is applied to compute two-point correlation functions on a 32^3 x 48 lattice with a ~ 0.145 fm using tadpole-improved gauge and clover fermion actions with three flavours of quarks at the physical strange quark mass.

## Key numbers

- Lattice spacing a ~ 0.145 fm, lattice dimensions L^3 x T = 32^3 x 48, corresponding to physical volume (4.6 fm)^3 x 7.0 fm
- A 4He two-point correlation function can be computed in ~0.8 seconds per time slice on a single core of a Dual Core AMD Opteron 285 processor
- Calculations performed on an ensemble of O(250) gauge configurations
- Baryon number density for 28Si is 0.3 fm^{-3}
- Correlation functions computed for nuclei with atomic numbers A=4, 8, 12, 16, and 28

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD study of multi-baryon systems beyond the first calculations by Beane (2009), Yamazaki (2009), and others, addressing the factorial growth of Wick contractions that previously limited such calculations. The determinant-based approach enables computations for larger nuclei than the quark-hadron contraction method used in the NPLQCD collaboration's hypernuclei study (2012). The results demonstrate computational feasibility but show that significant advances in statistical precision, volume, and interpolating operator quality are needed to extract binding energies and excitation spectra of these systems.
