# A microscopic NN to NN*(1440) potential

> By means of a NN to NN*(1440) transition potential derived in a parameter-free way from a quark-model based NN potential, we determine simultaneously the $πNN^*(1440)$ and $σNN^*(1440)$ coupling constants. We also present a study of the target Roper excitation diagram contributing to the $p(d,d')$ reaction.

# A microscopic NN to NN*(1440) potential

## Key claim

The authors derive a parameter-free NN to NN*(1440) transition potential from a quark-model based NN potential and use it to extract the πNN*(1440) and σNN*(1440) coupling constants, but the sign of these coupling constants is ambiguous because it depends on the arbitrarily chosen relative sign between the N and N*(1440) wave functions; only absolute values and ratios between the two coupling constants are well-defined predictions.

## Method

The NN → NN*(1440) transition potential is obtained by sandwiching the quark-quark potential (containing confinement, one-gluon-exchange, and pion and sigma exchange terms) between NN and NN*(1440) states written in terms of quarks, for all quark pairs belonging to different baryons. The asymptotic behavior (R ≥ 4 fm) of the resulting potential is fitted to baryonic one-pion-exchange and one-sigma-exchange parametrizations to extract the meson-NN* coupling constants. For the p(d,d') reaction study, the scalar part of the transition potential is isolated by evaluating the potential in different spin-isospin channels and solving a system of equations to extract the V0 component.

## Key numbers

- |gπNN*(1440) / gπNN| = 0.25 (ratio of coupling constants, extracted from asymptotic fit)
- |gσNN*(1440) / gσNN| = 0.47 (ratio of coupling constants, extracted from asymptotic fit)
- gπNN*(1440) / √(4π) = -0.94 (absolute value only, sign ambiguous)
- gπNN*(1440) / gπNN = 0.53 × (gσNN*(1440) / gσNN) (ratio of the two ratios, a definitive model prediction)
- Λ = 4.2 fm⁻¹ (cut-off parameter used in the quark model)
- [Λ²/(Λ² - mπ²)] = 1.03 (vertex form factor factor for pion, close to 1)
- f²πNΔ / 4π = 0.22 (quark model value for the πNΔ coupling constant, compared to baryonic value of 0.35)

## Context

This work extends the same quark-model approach previously used for NN scattering and transition potentials involving the Δ resonance (Juliá-Díaz et al., 2003) to the Roper resonance N*(1440). The extracted σNN*(1440) coupling constant ratio of 0.47 agrees well with the experimental value of 0.48 obtained from p(α,α') reactions in the 10-15 GeV region (Ref. [11] in the paper). The p(d,d') reaction study tests the scalar short-range part of the interaction, revealing that quark-model results differ qualitatively from baryonic calculations (Hirenzaki et al., Ref. [17]) due to contributions from quark antisymmetry and dynamics; the results enable future full quark-model calculations of both the Δ and Roper contributions to these reactions without the uncertainties from baryonic form factors.
