# Isospin-breaking effects in the three-pion contribution to hadronic vacuum polarization

> Isospin-breaking (IB) effects are required for an evaluation of hadronic vacuum polarization at subpercent precision. While the dominant contributions arise from the $e^+e^-\toπ^+π^-$ channel, also IB in the subleading channels can become relevant for a detailed understanding, e.g., of the comparison to lattice QCD. Here, we provide such an analysis for $e^+e^-\to 3π$ by extending our dispersive description of the process, including estimates of final-state radiation (FSR) and $ρ$-$ω$ mixing. In particular, we develop a formalism to capture the leading infrared-enhanced effects in terms of a correction factor $η_{3π}$ that generalizes the analog treatment of virtual and final-state photons in the $2π$ case. The global fit to the $e^+e^-\to 3π$ data base, subject to constraints from analyticity, unitarity, and the chiral anomaly, gives $a_μ^{3π}|_{\leq 1.8\,\text{GeV}}=45.91(53)\times 10^{-10}$ for the total $3π$ contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, of which $a_μ^\text{FSR}[3π]=0.51(1)\times 10^{-10}$ and $a_μ^{ρ\text{-}ω}[3π]=-2.68(70)\times 10^{-10}$ can be ascribed to IB. We argue that the resulting cancellation with $ρ$-$ω$ mixing in $e^+e^-\to 2π$ can be understood from a narrow-resonance picture, and provide updated values for the vacuum-polarization-subtracted vector-meson parameters $M_ω=782.70(3)\,\text{MeV}$, $M_ϕ=1019.21(2)\,\text{MeV}$, $Γ_ω=8.71(3)\,\text{MeV}$, and $Γ_ϕ=4.27(1)\,\text{MeV}$.

## Key claim

The paper provides the first comprehensive analysis of isospin-breaking (IB) effects in the $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ channel for hadronic vacuum polarization, finding that the dominant IB contributions come from final-state radiation and $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing, with the latter partially canceling against the analogous effect in the $2\pi$ channel. The authors caution that the $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing contribution is sensitive to the assumed line shape off the resonance and could potentially be misidentified as non-resonant background.

## Method

The authors extend a dispersive Khuri-Treiman representation of the $\gamma^*\to 3\pi$ amplitude to include isospin-breaking effects, developing a formalism for infrared-enhanced electromagnetic corrections via a correction factor $\eta_{3\pi}(s)$ that generalizes the treatment used for the $2\pi$ channel. For $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing, they employ a coupled-channel formalism that reproduces the vacuum polarization function and predicts the line shape in $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ consistently with the $2\pi$ channel, using a dispersively improved variant of a Breit-Wigner ansatz. Global fits to the $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ data base from SND and CMD-2 are performed subject to constraints from analyticity, unitarity, and the chiral anomaly.

## Key numbers

- Total $3\pi$ contribution to $a_\mu$ up to $1.8$ GeV: $a_\mu^{3\pi}|_{\leq 1.8\,\text{GeV}} = 45.91(53) \times 10^{-10}$
- FSR contribution to $a_\mu$ from $3\pi$: $a_\mu^\text{FSR}[3\pi] = 0.51(1) \times 10^{-10}$
- $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing contribution to $a_\mu$ from $3\pi$: $a_\mu^{\rho\text{-}\omega}[3\pi] = -2.68(70) \times 10^{-10}$
- Updated vacuum-polarization-subtracted $\omega$ mass: $M_\omega = 782.70(3)$ MeV
- Updated vacuum-polarization-subtracted $\phi$ mass: $M_\phi = 1019.21(2)$ MeV
- Updated vacuum-polarization-subtracted $\omega$ width: $\Gamma_\omega = 8.71(3)$ MeV
- Updated vacuum-polarization-subtracted $\phi$ width: $\Gamma_\phi = 4.27(1)$ MeV
- Real part of $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing parameter from global fit: $\Re\epsilon_{\omega} = 1.48(28) \times 10^{-3}$ (for the preferred fit with $p_\text{conf}=2$, $n_\text{conf}=0$)

## Context

This work extends the dispersive description of $e^+e^-\to 3\pi$ from Hoferichter et al. (2019) by incorporating isospin-breaking effects needed for subpercent precision in hadronic vacuum polarization, complementing similar IB analyses in the $2\pi$ channel by Colangelo et al. (2022) and the $\bar K K$ channel by Stamen et al. (2022). The results enable more precise comparisons between data-driven HVP evaluations and lattice QCD calculations such as those from Borsanyi et al. (2020), and support efforts to understand tensions between $e^+e^-$ data and lattice results, particularly in the intermediate window observable.
