# Radial sensitivity of kaonic atoms and strongly bound $\bar K$ states

> The strength of the low energy K^- nucleus real potential has recently received renewed attention in view of experimental evidence for the possible existence of strongly bound K^- states. Previous fits to kaonic atom data led to either 'shallow' or to 'deep' potentials, where only the former are in agreement with chiral approaches but only the latter can produce strongly bound states. Here we explore the uncertainties of the K^- nucleus optical potentials, obtained from fits to kaonic atom data, using the functional derivatives of the best-fit chi^2 values with respect to the potential. We find that only the deep type of potential provides information which is applicable to the K^- interaction in the nuclear interior.

## Key claim

The deep type of K⁻-nucleus optical potential (which yields excellent fits to kaonic atom data) is reliably determined to depths of 150–180 MeV at regions of almost full nuclear matter density, whereas the shallow type of potential is only well-determined at the nuclear surface and cannot be extrapolated to the nuclear interior.

## Method

The authors computed functional derivatives of the best-fit χ² values with respect to the K⁻-nucleus optical potential, using a Gaussian smeared δ-function to introduce local perturbations at radial positions defined in units of diffuseness relative to the charge radius (r = R_c + η a_c). They applied this method to two types of global potentials fitted to 65 kaonic atom data points from ⁶Li to U: a shallow tρ potential and a deep t(ρ) potential (the "F" potential of Friedman and Gal (2006)), and extrapolated the functional derivatives to the limit of zero perturbation range.

## Key numbers

- The shallow tρ potential yields a total χ² of 130 for 65 data points; the deep t(ρ) "F" potential yields χ² = 84 for the same 65 data points.
- For the deep "F" potential, the sensitive radial region extends from η = −3.5 to η = 4 (where r = R_c + η a_c); for the shallow tρ potential, the sensitive region is from η = −1.5 to η = 6.
- η = −2.2 corresponds to 90% of the central charge density; η = 2.2 corresponds to 10% of that density.
- The deep potentials are determined reliably to depths of 150–180 MeV at regions of almost full nuclear matter density.
- For Ni, the sensitivity of the "F" potential extends to depths of 180 MeV at radii where the density is essentially the full nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the "notch test" approach of Batty, Friedman, and Gal (1997) to a mathematically well-defined functional-derivative limit, addressing the long-standing ambiguity between shallow and deep K⁻-nucleus potentials. The deep potentials are consistent with experimental reports of strongly bound K⁻ states with binding energies B_K ∼ 100–200 MeV (Shevchenko et al. 2004; Shevchenko et al. 2005; Kishimoto et al. 2005; Agnello et al. 2005), but the discrepancy with shallow chiral-motivated potentials (Ramos and Oset 2000; Cieplý et al. 2001) remains an open problem. The results enable reliable extrapolation of the deep potentials to the nuclear interior for predicting strongly bound antikaon states, though the authors caution that this does not imply such states necessarily exist.
