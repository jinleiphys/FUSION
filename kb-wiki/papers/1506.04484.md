# Study of weakly-bound odd-A nuclei with quasiparticle blocking

> The coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with quasiparticle blocking has been applied to study the odd-A weakly bound nuclei $^{17,19}$B and $^{37}$Mg, in which halo structures have been reported in experiments. The Skyrme nuclear forces SLy4 and UNEDF1 have been adopted in our calculations. The results with and without blocking have been compared to demonstrate the emergence of deformed halo structures due to blocking effects. In our calculations, $^{19}$B and $^{37}$Mg have remarkable features of deformed halos.

## Key claim

The coordinate-space Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach with quasiparticle blocking predicts that the odd-A weakly bound nuclei \(^{19}\)B and \(^{37}\)Mg exhibit remarkable deformed halo structures, but only when the blocking effect is included; without blocking, these halo features are absent or less pronounced. The claim is conditional on the choice of Skyrme force (SLy4 or UNEDF1) and on the specific blocked quasiparticle state (proton \(1p_{1/2}\) for boron isotopes, neutron \(\Omega^{\pi}=1/2^{-}\) for \(^{37}\)Mg).

## Method

The authors solved the Skyrme-HFB equation in a deformed coordinate space using the HFB-AX solver with B-spline techniques on a two-dimensional axial-symmetric lattice (box size 27 fm, maximum mesh spacing 0.6 fm, B-spline order 12). For odd-A nuclei, the quasiparticle blocking method was implemented self-consistently by modifying the particle density, pairing density, and particle number equation to exclude the blocked state \(\mu\). Two Skyrme forces (SLy4 and UNEDF1) were used for the particle-hole channel, and a density-dependent surface pairing interaction (strength \(V_0=500\) MeV for SLy4, 460 MeV for UNEDF1) was employed for the particle-particle channel.

## Key numbers

- \(^{17}\)B: total quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.30\) with blocking using SLy4 force; without blocking, \(\beta_2 = 0.001\).
- \(^{19}\)B: two-neutron separation energy \(S_{2n} = 0.23\) MeV with blocking using SLy4 force (experimental value \(0.14 \pm 0.39\) MeV).
- \(^{37}\)Mg: one-neutron separation energy \(S_n = 0.43\) MeV with blocking using UNEDF1 force (experimental value \(0.22^{+0.12}_{-0.09}\) MeV).
- \(^{37}\)Mg: neutron r.m.s. radius \(r_n = 4.07\) fm with blocking using UNEDF1 force; without blocking, \(r_n = 3.70\) fm.
- \(^{17}\)B: calculated mass r.m.s. radius \(r_m = 3.06\) fm with blocking using SLy4 force (experimental value \(3.00 \pm 0.06\) fm).
- \(^{19}\)B: experimental mass r.m.s. radius \(3.11 \pm 0.13\) fm, smaller than all theoretical results in this work.
- Pairing gaps: proton pairing gap \(\Delta_p\) drops to \(\sim 10^{-5}\)–\(10^{-6}\) MeV with blocking for all three nuclei, compared to values of 0.01–2.03 MeV without blocking.

## Context

This work extends previous coordinate-space HFB studies of deformed halos in even-even nuclei (Pei 2013, Pei 2014) to odd-A systems, which are experimentally more common (e.g., \(^{11}\)Be, \(^{31}\)Ne, \(^{37}\)Mg) but computationally more challenging due to the need for quasiparticle blocking. The results are compared to experimental data on \(^{17}\)B (Suzuki 1999, Estrade 2014), \(^{19}\)B (Suzuki 1999, Gaudefroy 2012), and \(^{37}\)Mg (Mg37 experiment), and to earlier relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov calculations without blocking (Lalazissis 2004). The finding of a deformed halo in \(^{37}\)Mg with a large \(p\)-wave component supports the experimental interpretation and suggests that blocking-induced polarization can drive deformation in weakly bound nuclei. The observation of oscillatory pairing densities in \(^{37}\)Mg is noted as analogous to the Larkin-Ovchinnikov phase in spin-polarized cold Fermi gases (Pei 2010).
