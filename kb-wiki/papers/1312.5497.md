# The initial stages of heavy-ion collisions in the Color Glass Condensate framework

> In this short review, we present the description of the early stages of a heavy ion collision at high energy in the Color Glass Condensate framework.

## Key claim

The Color Glass Condensate framework provides a weak-coupling description of the early stages of heavy-ion collisions at high energy, justified when the saturation momentum \(Q_s\) is much larger than the QCD non-perturbative scale \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\), but the leading-order predictions for longitudinal pressure remain much smaller than transverse pressure, casting doubt on the applicability of hydrodynamics unless next-to-leading order corrections and resummation of instabilities alter this conclusion.

## Method

The paper reviews the application of the Color Glass Condensate effective theory to heavy-ion collisions, where fast partons are treated as classical color sources and slow gluons as quantum fields. At leading order, observables are computed from classical solutions of the Yang-Mills equations with retarded boundary conditions. At next-to-leading order, large logarithmic corrections from soft gluon radiation are resummed via the JIMWLK equation into separate probability distributions for each nucleus, while exponentially growing mode functions from classical instabilities (related to the Weibel instability) are resummed by promoting linearized fluctuations to Gaussian-distributed initial conditions for the classical fields, evaluated numerically on a lattice.

## Key numbers

* For lead nuclei (\(A\sim 200\)) at LHC energy (\(x\sim 10^{-4}\)), the saturation momentum squared \(Q_s^2\) is in the range \(2-4\ {\rm GeV}^2\), which is sufficiently above \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD}\approx 200\ {\rm MeV}\) to justify a weak coupling expansion.
* The numerical simulations were performed on a \(64\times 64\times 128\) lattice with longitudinal lattice spacing \(a_\eta=1/64\) and transverse lattice spacing \(Q_s a_\perp =1\).
* The resummation of instabilities uses mode functions computed on a proper time surface at \(Q_s\tau\ll 1\), with the variance of Gaussian fluctuations given by the 1-loop calculation.

## Context

This review extends the McLerran-Venugopalan model (McLerran and Venugopalan, 1994) and the JIMWLK renormalization group approach (Balitsky, 1996; Jalilian-Marian et al., 1997-1999; Iancu et al., 2001) for high-energy nuclear collisions. It addresses the problem that leading-order classical Yang-Mills calculations (Krasnitz et al., 1998-2003; Lappi, 2003) yield negligible longitudinal pressure, and the resummation of instabilities (related to the Weibel instability studied by Mrowczynski, 1993-1997; Romatschke and Strickland, 2003-2004; Arnold et al., 2003-2005) is needed to determine whether the system can generate sufficient longitudinal pressure for hydrodynamics to become applicable.
