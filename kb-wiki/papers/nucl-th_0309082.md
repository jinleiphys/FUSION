# Instability of nuclear wobbling motion and tilted axis rotation

> We study a possible correspondence between the softening of the wobbling mode and the "phase transition" of the one-dimensionally rotating mean field to a three-dimensionally rotating one by comparing the properties of the wobbling mode obtained by the one-dimensional cranking model + random phase approximation with the total routhian surface obtained by the three-dimensional tilted-axis cranking model. The potential surface for the observed wobbling mode excited on the triaxial superdeformed states in $^{163}$Lu is also analyzed.

## Key claim

The softening of the nuclear wobbling mode as triaxiality changes corresponds to a "phase transition" from one-dimensional principal-axis rotation to three-dimensional tilted-axis rotation, demonstrated by comparing the one-dimensional cranking model plus random phase approximation (RPA) with the three-dimensional tilted-axis cranking model. The authors show this correspondence explicitly for an oblate configuration in \(^{146}\)Gd and argue that experimental data on triaxial superdeformed bands in \(^{163}\)Lu show signatures of this softening.

## Method

The authors performed two complementary microscopic calculations using a Nilsson-plus-BCS mean field with doubly-stretched quadrupole-quadrupole residual interactions. First, they applied the one-dimensional cranking model (rotation about the x-axis) plus RPA to compute the wobbling mode excitation energy, wobbling angles, and moments of inertia as functions of the triaxiality parameter \(\gamma\). Second, they used the three-dimensional tilted-axis cranking (TAC) model to compute total routhian surfaces as functions of the polar and azimuthal tilting angles \((\theta, \varphi)\) of the rotational frequency vector \(\mathbf{\Omega}\), searching for stationary solutions where \(\langle\mathbf{J}\rangle \parallel \mathbf{\Omega}\).

## Key numbers

- In \(^{146}\)Gd for the \([(\nu h_{9/2},f_{7/2})^2(\pi h_{11/2})^2]_{16^+}\) configuration at \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{rot} = 0.25\) MeV, \(\epsilon_2 = 0.19\), \(\gamma = 60^\circ\), \(\Delta_n = 0.8\) MeV, \(\Delta_p = 0.6\) MeV: the wobbling excitation energy in the rotating frame is \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{wob} = 0.198\) MeV, giving a laboratory-frame energy of 0.198 MeV + 0.25 MeV = 0.448 MeV.
- The wobbling mode becomes unstable (excitation energy goes to zero) at \(\gamma = 32^\circ\) in \(^{146}\)Gd, where a tilted-axis minimum appears in the energy surface.
- For the one-phonon wobbling state in \(^{163}\)Lu (triaxial superdeformed, \(\epsilon_2 = 0.43\), \(\gamma = 20^\circ\), \(\Delta_n = \Delta_p = 0.3\) MeV, \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{rot} = 0.5\) MeV): \(\hbar\omega_\mathrm{wob} = 0.185\) MeV, \(\theta_\mathrm{wob} = 14.2^\circ\), \(\varphi_\mathrm{wob} = 7.6^\circ\).
- The experimental two-phonon wobbling excitation energy in \(^{163}\)Lu satisfies \(\Delta E'_\mathrm{2-phonon} < 2 \times \Delta E'_\mathrm{1-phonon}\), indicating softening of the energy surface.

## Context

This work extends the microscopic RPA studies of wobbling motion by Shimizu and Matsuyanagi (Er isotopes) and Matsuzaki (\(^{182}\)Os) to explicitly connect wobbling-mode softening with the emergence of three-dimensional rotation, a possibility first raised theoretically by one of the authors (Matsuzaki) in a prior Rapid Communication. The calculations are compared to the experimental triaxial superdeformed wobbling bands in \(^{163}\)Lu discovered by Ødegård et al. (2001) and the two-phonon wobbling excitations observed by Jensen et al. (2002). The results enable future application of more sophisticated many-body methods such as the selfconsistent collective coordinate (SCC) method to understand two-phonon wobbling states.
