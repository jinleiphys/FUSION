# Repulsive force by vector mesons and quark-hadron phase transition

> Using a phenomenological model with vector-type interactions, we discuss a role of repulsive force in the quark-hadron phase transition at high density. For realization of the quark phase at high density, strong vector coupling is needed in the hadron phase, while it is forbidden in the quark phase. For the quark-phase, a NJL-type model with a multi-quark interaction is investigated. We show that, in this model, the restoration of chiral symmetry decreases effective vector coupling and the quark phase is realized at high density, even if we have strong vector coupling at low density. In this model, the strong coupling in the hadron phase is induced by the chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a strong repulsive (vector) interaction is necessary in the hadron phase to allow a quark phase to be realized at high density, but such a strong vector coupling must be forbidden in the quark phase. The author shows that in an NJL-type model with a multi-quark interaction, the restoration of chiral symmetry at high density naturally reduces the effective vector coupling in the quark phase, even if it is strong at low density, thereby enabling the quark-hadron phase transition.

## Method

The author uses a two-phase description, comparing the hadron phase described by the Walecka model (with σ and ω mesons) and the quark phase described by a two-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a vector interaction. To determine which phase is realized, the pressures of both phases are compared at zero temperature using the mean-field approximation. A multi-quark interaction term is added to the NJL Lagrangian to make the effective vector coupling density-dependent, decreasing as the chiral condensate (σ̃) melts.

## Key numbers

* The baryonic chemical potential at normal nuclear density is μ₀ = 923 MeV, derived from the nucleon mass M minus the binding energy a₁.
* In a free gas model, the ratio of quark-phase pressure to hadron-phase pressure is 1/27 in the high-density limit, due to the relation μ = 3μ_Q.
* The hadron phase pressure P_H = 0 at normal density, requiring the quark-phase pressure P_Q(T=0, μ=μ₀) to be negative (i.e., a positive bag constant B) for the hadron phase to be stable at normal density.
* The effective vector coupling in the quark phase is given by G_v^* = G_v + G_sv σ̃², which decreases as the chiral condensate σ̃ decreases with density.

## Context

This work extends the standard two-phase description of the quark-hadron transition, which often uses a bag model, by explicitly incorporating vector repulsion in both phases. The author notes that in a free gas model, the hadron phase always wins at high density (Kouno, 2006). The paper builds on the Walecka model for nuclear matter (Walecka, 1974) and the NJL model with vector interactions (Kunihiro, 1991). The key result, that chiral symmetry breaking can induce strong vector coupling in the hadron phase, suggests a mechanism for reconciling the need for repulsion in hadronic matter with its suppression in quark matter, enabling future studies of the phase diagram at high baryon density.
