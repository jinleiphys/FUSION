# Anomalous dimensions determine the power counting -- Wilsonian RG analysis of nuclear EFT --

> The Legendre flow equation, a version of exact Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) equation, is employed to consider the power counting issues in Nuclear Effective Field Theory. A WRG approach is an ideal framework because it is nonperturbative and does not require any prescribed power counting rule. The power counting is determined systematically from the scaling dimensions of the operators at the nontrivial fixed point. The phase structure is emphasized and the inverse of the scattering length, which is identified as a relevant coupling, is shown to play a role of the order parameter. The relations to the work done by Birse, McGovern, and Richardson and to the Kaplan-Savage-Wise scheme are explained.

## Key claim

The paper argues that power counting in nuclear effective field theory (NEFT) should be determined by the quantum scaling dimensions (canonical plus anomalous dimensions) of operators at a fixed point, not by the fixed-point value of the coupling itself; the inverse scattering length is identified as a relevant coupling that serves as the order parameter distinguishing two phases in the RG flow.

## Method

The authors employ the Legendre flow equation, an exact Wilsonian renormalization group (WRG) equation, to analyze the pionless NEFT for the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)–\(^3D_1\) two-nucleon channels. They truncate the space of operators to those with canonical dimension up to eight, substitute an ansatz for the effective average action into the flow equation, and derive first-order differential RG equations for the coupling constants. The RG flows are studied near both the trivial and nontrivial fixed points, and the scaling dimensions of operators are extracted from the linearized RG equations.

## Key numbers

* The inverse scattering lengths are \(1/a^{({}^1S_0)} \sim -8\) MeV and \(1/a^{({}^3S_1)} \sim 40\) MeV, both much smaller than the pionless NEFT cutoff \(\Lambda_0 \sim m_\pi \sim 140\) MeV, indicating fine-tuning.
* The deuteron binding energy is 2.2 MeV, characterizing a shallow bound state.
* The RG analysis is carried out to order \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\) in the derivative expansion for both the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)–\(^3D_1\) channels.
* The physical cutoff for pionful NEFT is estimated as \(\Lambda_0 \sim 500\) MeV, larger than the pionless case.

## Context

This work extends the Wilsonian RG approach of Birse, McGovern, and Richardson (1998), who analyzed nonrelativistic two-body scattering using an RG equation for an effective potential, by placing the analysis in a fully field-theoretical framework using the Legendre flow equation. It also clarifies the relation to the Kaplan-Savage-Wise (KSW) power counting scheme, which is based on RG behavior at a nontrivial fixed point corresponding to infinite scattering length. The paper enables a systematic determination of power counting for nonperturbative, fine-tuned systems without requiring a prescribed counting rule, and it provides a foundation for extending the analysis to pionful NEFT and higher partial waves.
