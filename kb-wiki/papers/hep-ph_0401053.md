# Transverse quark motion inside charmonia in diffractive photo- and electroproductions

> We reexamine the Fermi motion effects on the diffractive photo- and electroproductions of heavy vector-mesons, J/psi and psi', off a nucleon in the leading ln(Q^2/Lambda_QCD^2) approximation (LLA) of QCD. We take into account all the Fermi motion corrections arising from the relative motion of quarks inside the charmonium, which is treated as a nonrelativistic bound state of c and \bar{c}. Our key ingredients are the correct spin structure for the ^3S_1 c\bar{c} bound state, the off-shellness in the c\bar{c} \to J/psi (psi') hadronization vertex, and the modification of the gluon's longitudinal momentum fraction probed by the process, due to the relative motion between c and \bar{c}. We demonstrate that these three contributions produce the new Fermi motion effects in the LLA diffractive amplitude in QCD. It is found that our new effects moderate the strong suppression of the diffractive J/psi (psi') production cross sections, which was reported in the previous works on the Fermi motion effects. We emphasize the role of the transverse quark motion for the heavy meson production, and also discuss the strong helicity dependence of the Fermi motion effects and its implication in the longitudinal to transverse production ratio, sigma_L/sigma_T.

## Key claim

The authors reexamine Fermi motion corrections in diffractive photo- and electroproduction of J/psi and psi' mesons, finding that three previously neglected contributions (correct spin structure for the 3S1 c-cbar bound state, off-shellness in the hadronization vertex, and modification of the gluon's longitudinal momentum fraction due to quark relative motion) moderate the strong suppression of cross sections reported in earlier works, rather than enhancing it.

## Method

The authors compute the forward diffractive amplitude for gamma(*) + N -> V + N (V = J/psi, psi') in the leading logarithmic approximation (LLA) of QCD, using the factorization approach with light-cone wave functions. They construct the vector-meson wave function from a nonrelativistic Schrodinger equation with a realistic quark potential, incorporating the correct spin structure via a projector onto the large components of the Dirac spinors, and include off-shell effects in the c-cbar to V vertex as well as the dependence of the gluon momentum fraction on the transverse and longitudinal relative motion of the quarks.

## Key numbers

- The average velocity squared of the charm quark in the charmonium rest frame is v^2 approximately 0.2 to 0.3, based on quark potential model calculations and lattice QCD simulations.
- Frankfurt-Koepf-Strikman (FKS) predicted suppression of the J/psi photoproduction cross section by a factor less than or equal to 0.1 (and less than or equal to 0.3 for electroproduction) due to Fermi motion effects.
- Ryskin-Roberts-Martin-Levin (RRML) gave a weaker suppression factor of 0.4 to 0.6 for J/psi photoproduction.
- The authors' results are qualitatively similar to RRML (1996) but show new features in the longitudinal-to-transverse cross section ratio sigma_L/sigma_T.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of Fermi motion effects in diffractive charmonium production by Frankfurt-Koepf-Strikman (FKS, 1998), Ryskin-Roberts-Martin-Levin (RRML, 1996), and an earlier work by P. Hoyer (PH). The authors identify three contributions missing from any single previous treatment: the correct spin projector for the 3S1 state, off-shellness of the hadronization vertex, and modification of the gluon momentum fraction. Their results enable more precise comparison with HERA data and clarify the role of transverse quark motion, which is important for Q^2 less than or comparable to m_c^2, in contrast to light meson production where longitudinal motion dominates.
