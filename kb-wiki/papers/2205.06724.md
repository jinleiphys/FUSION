# Spatial entanglement in two dimensional QCD: Renyi and Ryu-Takayanagi entropies

> We derive a general formula for the replica partition function in the vacuum state, for a large class of interacting theories with fermions, with or without gauge fields, using the equal-time formulation on the light front. The result is used to analyze the spatial entanglement of interacting Dirac fermions in two-dimensional QCD. A particular attention is paid to the issues of infrared cut-off dependence and gauge invariance. The Renyi entropy for a single interval, is given by the rainbow dressed quark propagator to order ${\cal O}(N_c)$. The contributions to order ${\cal O}(1)$, are shown to follow from the off-diagonal and off mass-shell mesonic T-matrix, with no contribution to the central charge. The construction is then extended to mesonic states on the light front, and shown to probe the moments of the partonic PDFs for large light-front separations. In the vacuum and for small and large intervals, the spatial entanglement entropy following from the Renyi entropy, is shown to be in agreement with the Ryu-Takayanagi geometrical entropy, using a soft-wall AdS$_3$ model of two-dimensional QCD.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general equal-time formula for the replica partition function in the vacuum state for interacting fermionic theories, and uses it to show that the spatial entanglement entropy in two-dimensional QCD, for both small and large intervals, agrees with the Ryu-Takayanagi geometrical entropy from a soft-wall AdS₃ model, but only in the regular cutoff gauge and at leading order in the large Nc expansion.

## Method

The authors construct the replica partition function in real-time Minkowski space by duplicating spacetime n times and gluing the copies together with twisted fermionic boundary conditions across a spatial cut, deriving an equal-time representation as an expectation value of exponential charge operators in a tensor product of n identical vacua. They then evaluate this partition function in two-dimensional QCD using light-front perturbation theory for small intervals and large Nc planar diagram resummation for arbitrary interval sizes, with the gauge fixed in the regular cutoff prescription (A=0 gauge). The resulting Renyi entropy is compared to the Ryu-Takayanagi entropy computed from a soft-wall holographic dual of two-dimensional QCD.

## Key numbers

* The central charge for massless free fermions in two dimensions is Nc/3, obtained from the vacuum polarization diagram as S(L) = (Nc/3) ln(L/a).
* The Renyi entropy for a single interval at order O(Nc) is given by S_n = [(n+1)Nc/(6n)] ∫₀¹ dx [K₀(ma/√(x(1-x))) - K₀(mL/√(x(1-x)))], where K₀ is the modified Bessel function, m is the fermion mass, a is the UV cutoff, and L is the interval length.
* For massive fermions at large L, the central charge decays exponentially as Nc e^{-2mL}, and the Renyi entropy approaches the constant UV contribution [(n+1)Nc/(6n)] ln(1/(ma)) plus O(e^{-2mL}) corrections.
* At small intervals, the entanglement entropy is S(L) = (Nc/3) ln(L/a) + O(g²L²), where g is the gauge coupling.

## Context

This work extends the replica construction for entanglement entropy from free fermion theories (Casini 2005) to interacting gauge theories with fermions, specifically two-dimensional QCD, which is solvable in the large Nc limit (t Hooft 1974, Bars 1976). The paper shows that the gauge dependence of the entanglement entropy in gauge theories can be controlled by appropriate gauge fixing, and that the leading results for small and large intervals are gauge independent and match holographic predictions from a soft-wall AdS₃ model. This enables future comparisons between entanglement entropy computed in lattice QCD simulations and holographic dual descriptions, and suggests that moments of partonic PDFs can be extracted from Renyi entropy for space-like intervals in fast-moving hadrons.
