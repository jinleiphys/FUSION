# Re-examining the premise of isobaric collisions and a novel method to measure the chiral magnetic effect

> In this proceeding we will show that the expectations of the isobaric $^{96}_{44}\mathrm{Ru}+^{96}_{44}\mathrm{Ru}$ and $^{96}_{40}\mathrm{Zr}+^{96}_{40}\mathrm{Zr}$ collisions on chiral magnetic effect (CME) search may not hold as originally anticipated due to large uncertainties in the isobaric nuclear structures. We demonstrate this using Woods-Saxon densities and the proton and neutron densities calculated by the density functional theory. Furthermore, a novel method is proposed to gauge background and possible CME contributions in the same system, intrinsically better than the isobaric collisions of two different systems. We illustrate the method with Monte Carlo Glauber and AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) simulations.

## Key claim

The original expectation that isobaric collisions of Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr would provide a clean comparison for chiral magnetic effect (CME) searches, with nearly identical backgrounds and different magnetic fields, may not hold due to large uncertainties in the nuclear structures of these two isotopes; the authors demonstrate that realistic density profiles from density functional theory introduce significant differences in both elliptic flow and magnetic field between the two systems, undermining the original premise.

## Method

The authors calculated proton and neutron density distributions for Ru and Zr nuclei using density functional theory (DFT) with the SLy4 mean field including pairing correlations (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approach), with theoretical uncertainties estimated using SLy5 and SkM* functionals. These densities were implemented in Monte Carlo Glauber simulations to compute eccentricities and magnetic fields, and in AMPT (A Multi-Phase Transport) model with string melting to simulate elliptic flow. A novel method was then proposed comparing measurements of the three-point correlator with respect to the reaction plane versus the participant plane within the same collision system.

## Key numbers

- The relative difference in eccentricity with respect to the participant plane between RuRu and ZrZr collisions can be as large as approximately 3%, using DFT densities
- The relative difference in magnetic field squared with respect to the reaction plane is significantly smaller for DFT densities than for Woods-Saxon densities, except at large impact parameter
- The atomic numbers of Ru (Z=44) and Zr (Z=40) differ by 10%, which was the basis for expecting different magnetic fields
- A finite proton radius of 0.88 fm was used in magnetic field calculations to avoid singularity at zero relative distance

## Context

This work extends the isobaric collision proposal by Voloshin (2010) for CME searches, which assumed nearly identical nuclear shapes for Ru and Zr based on Woods-Saxon density profiles as used in earlier calculations by Deng (2016). The authors show that realistic nuclear structure calculations introduce significant systematic uncertainties that weaken the power of the isobaric approach. Their proposed novel method, using comparative measurements within the same collision system rather than between two different systems, has already been applied to experimental data by the STAR collaboration and promises to decisively answer the question of CME existence with improved statistics.
