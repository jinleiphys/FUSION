# QCD Scales in Finite Nuclei

> The role of QCD scales and chiral symmetry in finite nuclei is examined. The Dirac-Hartree mean-field coupling constants of Nikolaus, Hoch, and Madland (NHM) are scaled in accordance with the QCD-based prescription of Manohar and Georgi. Whereas the nine empirically-based coupling constants of NHM span thirteen orders of magnitude, the scaled coupling constants are almost all {\it natural}, being dimensionless numbers of order one. We speculate that this result provides good evidence that QCD and chiral symmetry apply to finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The nine empirically-based coupling constants of the Dirac-Hartree mean-field model of Nikolaus, Hoch, and Madland (NHM), which span thirteen orders of magnitude in their dimensional form, become almost all "natural" (dimensionless numbers of order one) when scaled according to the QCD-based prescription of Manohar and Georgi, providing evidence that QCD and chiral symmetry apply to finite nuclei, though the authors hedge that the NHM Lagrangian was not constructed with chiral symmetry in mind and is an incomplete mix of three different orders in the expansion.

## Method

The authors took the nine coupling constants from the zero-range Dirac-Hartree mean-field Lagrangian of NHM, which had been fitted to ground-state observables of fifty-seven nuclei, and scaled them using the Manohar-Georgi prescription for a generic chiral Lagrangian component. The scaling expressed each coupling constant in terms of the pion decay constant (92.5 MeV), the pion mass (139.6 MeV), and the QCD scale Λ ≈ 1 GeV, yielding dimensionless coefficients c_{lmn} that were then compared to the naturalness criterion of being of order one.

## Key numbers

- The nine dimensional coupling constants of NHM span more than thirteen orders of magnitude.
- Six of the nine scaled dimensionless coupling constants are natural (order one).
- The pion decay constant used in the scaling is 92.5 MeV; the pion mass used is 139.6 MeV; the QCD scale Λ is taken as 1 GeV.
- The unnatural coefficients are the very small α_{TS} and the large γ_{S} and γ_{V}; however, the sum of γ_{S} and γ_{V} appears to be natural.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory framework of Weinberg (1990) and the scaling prescription of Manohar and Georgi (1984) from few-nucleon systems to finite nuclei, building on the earlier work of Lynn (1993) on nuclear chiral liquids. The NHM model itself was originally motivated by empirical improvements to the Walecka scalar-vector model of Serot and Walecka (1979) and Horowitz and Serot (1981), not by chiral symmetry. The result enables a systematic study of QCD scales and chiral symmetry in finite nuclei using mean-field models, suggesting that naturalness may hold even in incomplete effective Lagrangians.
