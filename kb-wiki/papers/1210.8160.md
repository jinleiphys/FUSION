# Deconfinement to Quark Matter in Magnetars

> We model magnetars as hybrid stars, which have a core of quark matter surrounded by hadronic matter. For this purpose, we use an extended version of the SU(3) non-linear realization of the sigma model in which the degrees of freedom change naturally from hadrons to quarks as the temperature/density increases. The presence of a variable magnetic field allows us to study in detail the influence of Landau quantization and the anomalous magnetic moment on the particle population of the star, more precisely on particles with different spin projections. This allows us to calculate the polarization of the system throughout different phases of the star, hadronic, quark and also a mixed phase.

# Deconfinement to Quark Matter in Magnetars

## Key claim
Magnetars can be modeled as hybrid stars containing a quark matter core surrounded by hadronic matter, with a variable magnetic field that depends on baryon chemical potential rather than density, preventing discontinuities at the phase transition. The total spin polarization of the system increases with magnetic field and chemical potential until quarks appear, after which it decreases because the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) for quarks is not included in the calculations.

## Method
The authors use an extended SU(3) non-linear realization of the sigma model that naturally transitions from hadronic to quark degrees of freedom as density increases, with mesons treated as classical fields in the mean-field approximation. A variable magnetic field in the z-direction is introduced with an explicit dependence on baryon chemical potential, following a functional form that reproduces effective magnetic field curves from prior work. The model assumes a small surface tension between phases, allowing global charge neutrality and a mixed phase, and includes Landau quantization and AMM for baryons (but not quarks) to compute particle populations, spin polarization, and magnetization.

## Key numbers
- Surface magnetic field value: \( B_{surf} = 69.25 \, \text{MeV}^2 = 10^{15} \, \text{G} \) (when \( \mu_B = 938 \, \text{MeV} \))
- Central magnetic field studied: \( B_c = 5 \times 10^5 \, \text{MeV}^2 = 7.22 \times 10^{18} \, \text{G} \)
- Conversion factor: \( 1 \, \text{MeV}^2 = 1.44 \times 10^{13} \, \text{G} \)
- Parameters for effective magnetic field function: \( a = 2.5 \), \( b = -4.08 \times 10^{-4} \), with \( \mu_B \) in MeV
- Only about 70% of the central magnetic field \( B_c \) can be reached inside stars in practice
- Compact star temperatures are of order 1 MeV, allowing zero-temperature approximation

## Context
This work extends prior studies of magnetars as hybrid stars using the SU(3) non-linear sigma model, which has been successful in reproducing nuclear matter properties (Papazoglou 1998), heavy ion collision data (Steinheimer 2009), and compact star properties (Dexheimer 2008, Negreiros 2010, Dexheimer 2011). The variable magnetic field approach builds on earlier work by Bandyopadhyay (1997), Mao (2001), and Rabhi (2009) that used density-dependent magnetic fields, while avoiding discontinuities at phase transitions. The results enable future studies of magnetic field effects on compact star observables such as mass-radius relations and cooling, and connect to lattice QCD constraints on the phase diagram at finite temperature and chemical potential.
