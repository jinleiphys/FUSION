# Constraints for proton structure fluctuations from exclusive scattering

> We constrain the average density profile of the proton and the amount of event-by-event fluctuations by simultaneously calculating the coherent and incoherent exclusive diffractive vector meson production cross section in deep inelastic scattering. Working within the Color Glass Condensate picture, we find that the gluonic density of the proton must have large geometric fluctuations in order to describe the experimentally measured large incoherent cross section.

## Key claim

The gluonic density of the proton must have large geometric fluctuations (beyond color charge fluctuations alone) in order to describe the experimentally measured large incoherent diffractive cross section, as shown by simultaneously calculating coherent and incoherent exclusive J/psi production within the Color Glass Condensate framework.

## Method

The authors computed coherent and incoherent diffractive J/psi vector meson production cross sections in deep inelastic scattering using the Color Glass Condensate effective theory, employing both the IPsat model (with a Gaussian proton profile and constituent quark positions) and the IP-Glasma framework (which includes local color charge fluctuations via Wilson lines). Geometric fluctuations were introduced by modeling the proton as three constituent quarks (hot spots) whose positions were sampled from a Gaussian distribution of width B_qc, with each constituent quark having a Gaussian density profile of width B_q. The dipole-proton scattering amplitude was then averaged at the amplitude level for coherent diffraction and at the cross section level for incoherent diffraction.

## Key numbers

- Coherent and incoherent cross sections were calculated at average center-of-mass energy ⟨W⟩ = 75 GeV, corresponding to x_pom ~ 10^-3
- Good agreement with H1 data was obtained with geometric fluctuation parameters B_qc = 3.3 GeV^-2 and B_q = 0.7 GeV^-2 in the IPsat model
- In the IP-Glasma model, geometric fluctuations with B_q = 3.0 GeV^-2 and B_qc = 0.3 GeV^-2 were needed to describe the data
- The IPsat model without geometric fluctuations (smooth proton) significantly underestimates the incoherent cross section
- Additional saturation scale fluctuations (with Gaussian width sigma ≈ 0.5 for ln Q_s^2/⟨Q_s^2⟩) mainly affect the incoherent cross section at the smallest |t|, while geometric fluctuations dominate at larger momentum transfer

## Context

This work extends the Color Glass Condensate description of exclusive diffractive vector meson production, which had previously successfully described coherent diffraction at HERA (Kowalski 2006) and inclusive DIS (Albacete 2010, Lappi 2013, Rezaeian 2012), by now constraining event-by-event fluctuations of the proton density. The results enable more accurate initial state modeling for hydrodynamical calculations of proton-nucleus collisions, where collective phenomena have been observed (Dusling 2015). The approach builds on the Good-Walker picture of diffractive scattering (Good 1960) and the Miettinen-Pumplin formalism (Miettinen 1978) relating incoherent diffraction to fluctuations of the scattering amplitude.
