# Analytical mass formula and nuclear surface properties in the ETF approximation

> The problem of the determination of the nuclear surface and surface symmetry energy is addressed in the framework of the Extended Thomas Fermi (ETF) approximation using Skyrme functionals. We propose an analytical model for the density profiles with variationally determined diffuseness parameters. For the case of symmetric nuclei, the resulting ETF functional can be exactly integrated, leading to an analytical formula expressing the surface energy as a function of the couplings of the energy functional. The importance of non-local terms is stressed, which cannot be simply deduced from the local part of the functional. In the case of asymmetric nuclei, we propose an approximate expression for the diffuseness and the surface energy. These quantities are analytically related to the parameters of the energy functional. In particular, the influence of the different equation of state parameters can be explicitly quantified. Detailed analyses of the different energy components (local/non-local, isoscalar/isovector, surface/curvature and higher order) are also performed. Our analytical solution of the ETF integral improves over previous models and leads to a precision better than 200 keV per nucleon in the determination of the nuclear binding energy for dripline nuclei.

## Key claim
The authors derive an analytical mass formula for nuclear binding energies within the Extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) approximation using Skyrme functionals, achieving a precision better than 200 keV per nucleon for dripline nuclei, but only when both local and non-local terms in the energy functional are separately calculated and summed.

## Method
The ETF energy functional was expanded to second order in ℏ², and a Fermi-function density profile with variationally determined diffuseness was assumed. For symmetric nuclei, the ETF integrals were analytically integrated to express surface, curvature, and higher-order energy components as functions of Skyrme parameters. For asymmetric nuclei, two approximations were proposed: one neglecting the neutron skin, and another assuming the surface symmetry energy density is strongly peaked at the nuclear surface.

## Key numbers
- Precision better than 200 keV per nucleon in the determination of nuclear binding energy for dripline nuclei.
- The slab diffuseness for symmetric nuclei with the SLy4 interaction is approximately 0.45 fm when only the surface term is considered, shifting to about 0.4 fm when curvature and mass-independent terms are included.
- The approximation neglecting the neutron skin leads to an overestimation of Hartree-Fock energies of medium-heavy and heavy nuclei of no more than 200-400 keV/nucleon even for the most extreme dripline nuclei.
- A truncation at i_max=7 in the Taylor expansion of the effective mass inverse produces an error of about 1% at the highest possible density ρ_sat=0.16 fm⁻³ for the SLy4 interaction.

## Context
This work extends the ETF-based analytical approach to nuclear masses, improving over previous models such as those by Krivine (1985) and the ETFSI parametrizations, which required numerical fitting of mass formula coefficients to density functional calculations. The analytical link between Skyrme parameters and mass formula coefficients enables direct correlation between functional form and physical properties of the effective interaction, avoiding the need to refit when functional constraints are updated. The results enable systematic decomposition of nuclear binding into bulk, surface, curvature, and higher-order components, and can be applied to study nuclear configurations in dilute stellar matter and to optimize Skyrme functionals for improved predictive power.
