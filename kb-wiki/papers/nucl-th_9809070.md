# Bose-Einstein Correlations and the Equation of State of Nuclear Matter

> Within a relativistic hydrodynamic framework, we use four different equations of state of nuclear matter to compare to experimental spectra from CERN/SPS experiments NA44 and NA49. Freeze-out hypersurfaces and Bose-Einstein correlation functions for identical pion pairs are discussed. We find that two-pion Bose-Einstein interferometry measures the relationship between the temperature and the energy density in the equation of state during the late hadronic stage of the fireball expansion. Little sensitivity of the light-hadron data to a quark-gluon plasma phase-transition is seen.

## Key claim

Two-pion Bose-Einstein interferometry measures the relationship between temperature and energy density in the equation of state during the late hadronic stage of fireball expansion, but the authors find that Bose-Einstein correlations used by themselves show little sensitivity to the presence or absence of a quark-gluon plasma phase transition.

## Method

The authors used the relativistic hydrodynamic simulation code HYLANDER-C with four different equations of state (EOS-I, EOS-II, EOS-III, and EOS-Ib) and Cooper-Frye freeze-out to model 158 A GeV Pb+Pb collisions. They compared the resulting single-particle momentum distributions and Bose-Einstein correlation functions for identical pion pairs to experimental data from the CERN/SPS NA44 and NA49 Collaborations, using the Bertsch-Pratt parametrization to extract correlation function widths.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for EOS-I: T_c = 200 MeV with critical energy density epsilon_c = 3.2 GeV/fm^3
- Critical temperature for EOS-II: T_c = 160 MeV with critical energy density epsilon_c approximately 1.5 GeV/fm^3
- Freeze-out energy densities: epsilon_f = 0.292 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-I), 0.126 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-II), 0.130 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-III), and 0.127 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-Ib and EOS-I with T_f = 116 MeV)
- Fireball lifetimes: 13.1 fm/c (EOS-I, T_f = 139 MeV), 20.2 fm/c (EOS-I, T_f = 116 MeV), 20.5 fm/c (EOS-Ib, T_f = 139 MeV)
- Maximum transverse velocity at freeze-out: 0.46 c (EOS-I, T_f = 139 MeV), 0.61 c (EOS-I, T_f = 116 MeV), 0.58 c (EOS-Ib, T_f = 139 MeV)
- Maximum initial energy density: 15.3 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-I, T_f = 139 MeV), 16.8 GeV/fm^3 (EOS-I, T_f = 116 MeV and EOS-Ib)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Rischke et al. (1996) that addressed the role of the equation of state in intensity interferometry, and builds on the authors' own prior hydrodynamic calculations (Schlei et al., 1998). The finding that Bose-Einstein correlations are insensitive to the quark-gluon plasma phase transition structure means that future work must combine BEC measurements with other observables, such as single-particle momentum spectra, to constrain the equation of state, rather than relying on BEC alone as a QGP signature.
