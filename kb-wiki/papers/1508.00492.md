# Coincidence charged-current neutrino-induced deuteron disintegration

> Deuteron disintegration by charged-current neutrino (CC$ν$) scattering offers the possibility to determine the energy of the incident neutrino by measuring in coincidence two of the three resulting particles: a charged lepton (usually a muon) and two protons, where we show that this channel can be isolated from all other, for instance, from those with a pion in the final state. We discuss the kinematics of the process for several detection scenarios, both in terms of kinematic variables that are natural from a theoretical point of view and others that are better matched to experimental situations. The deuteron structure is obtained from a relativistic model (involving an approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation) as an extension of a previous, well-tested model used in deuteron electrodisintegration. We provide inclusive and coincidence (semi-inclusive) cross sections for a variety of kinematic conditions, using the plane-wave impulse approximation, introducing final-state hadronic exchange terms (plane-wave Born approximation) and final-state hadronic interactions (distorted-wave Born approximation).

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that charged-current neutrino-induced deuteron disintegration (νμ + ²H → μ⁻ + p + p) can be used to determine the incident neutrino energy by measuring only a subset of final-state particles, provided that events with pion production can be kinematically isolated; the authors show that the "no-pion" cross section can be separated from pion-producing channels through kinematic cuts, making the semi-inclusive cross section effectively exclusive.

## Method

The authors compute inclusive and coincidence (semi-inclusive) cross sections for charged-current neutrino-deuteron disintegration using a relativistic deuteron model based on an approximation to the Bethe-Salpeter equation, extending a previous model used for deuteron electrodisintegration. They employ the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA), add final-state hadronic exchange terms to obtain the plane-wave Born approximation (PWBA), and include final-state hadronic interactions to reach the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA). The weak charged-current operator includes vector-isovector form factors from the GKex05 parameterization and axial-vector form factors with a dipole parameterization using MA = 1.03 GeV, while no two-body meson-exchange currents are included.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron mass: Md = 1875.61 MeV
- Axial-vector form factor dipole mass: MA = 1.03 GeV
- Axial coupling constant: GA(0) = 1.2695
- Example kinematic reconstruction: for muon momentum k' = 1 GeV, scattering angle θ = 60°, emitted proton momentum p1 = 0.5 GeV, and proton emission angle θ1 = 20°, the intersection of energy and momentum conservation curves yields incident neutrino momentum k = 1.45 GeV and undetected proton momentum p2 = 0.83 GeV
- Estimated ratio of meson-exchange current contributions to impulse approximation for ²H: approximately 5–7 × 10⁻⁴, based on characteristic momentum of about 55 MeV for deuterium compared to 228 MeV for ¹²C

## Context

This work extends the relativistic deuteron model previously applied to deuteron electrodisintegration by Van Orden and collaborators (2008, 2009, 2014) to the charged-current neutrino channel, providing a framework for neutrino energy determination that is not possible with complex nuclei such as carbon or oxygen due to unknown residual system masses. The paper builds on the semi-inclusive formalism of Donnelly et al. and the scaling analyses of Day (1990) and Donnelly (1998, 1999), and enables future determination of the nucleon axial-vector form factor GA(1) once deuteron and pp system modeling uncertainties are evaluated, as well as potential neutrino flux determination using the calculated cross sections.
