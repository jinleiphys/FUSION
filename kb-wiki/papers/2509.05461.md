# Bayesian Analysis of the $^{86}$Sr$(α, α)$ Reaction to Constrain the $^{86}$Sr$(α, n)$ Cross Section at Astrophysical Energies

> The Alpha Optical Model Potential (\aomp \!) is a phenomenological approach used to describe elastic scattering where multiple reaction channels are open. It is one of the most critical inputs for the calculation of thermonuclear reaction rates in explosive stellar environments, but uncertainties within the $α$-OMP lead to imprecise predictions hindering comparisons between calculations and observations. In order to improve the precision of the $α$-OMP, additional nuclear physics data are required. In this paper, a measurement of the $^{86}$Sr($α$, $α$) elastic scattering cross section at multiple energies is reported. A local optical potential is constructed via a fully Bayesian analysis of the elastic scattering data. The resulting uncertainties on the low energy cross sections relevant to nuclear astrophysics are then calculated and shown to be on the order of $50 \%$.

## Key claim

The paper reports that a single, modestly precise elastic scattering experiment, analyzed with a fully Bayesian approach, can constrain the low-energy $^{86}$Sr$(α, n)$ cross section to uncertainties on the order of 50%, but this result is conditional on using a single optical model formalism and focusing on a single energy point (6.2 MeV) as a stand-in for the full reaction rate uncertainty.

## Method

Elastic scattering angular distributions for $^{86}$Sr$(α, α)$ were measured at three beam energies (approximately 12, 18, and 21 MeV) using silicon surface barrier detectors at Triangle Universities Nuclear Laboratory. A local alpha optical model potential with six parameters (real and imaginary Woods-Saxon volume terms with linear or Fermi energy dependence) was constructed via Bayesian inference, and the resulting posterior parameter distributions were propagated through Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations using the TALYS 2.0 code with the back-shifted Fermi gas level density to predict the $^{86}$Sr$(α, n)$ cross section at a single astrophysically relevant energy.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies for the $^{86}$Sr$(α, α)$ experiment were determined to be 12077 ± 6 (stat.) ± 40 (syst.) keV, 18101 ± 9 (stat.) ± 60 (syst.) keV, and 20530 ± 10 (stat.) ± 70 (syst.) keV.
- The SrO layer on the target had an areal density of 24.6(25) μg/cm², deposited onto a carbon foil of 40(4) μg/cm² thickness.
- The resulting uncertainties on the low-energy $^{86}$Sr$(α, n)$ cross section relevant to nuclear astrophysics were shown to be on the order of 50%.
- Predictions from eight alpha optical model potentials included in TALYS for the $^{86}$Sr$(α, n)$ cross section at $E_α$ = 6.2 MeV range from 0.096 μb to 5.403 μb, a variation of two orders of magnitude.
- The McFadden and Satchler optical model predicted 0.642 μb, while the Atomki-V2 potential predicted 0.237 μb at the same energy.

## Context

This work extends efforts to constrain alpha optical model potentials for astrophysical $(\alpha, n)$ reactions, which are critical for understanding weak r-process nucleosynthesis in neutrino-driven winds from core-collapse supernovae. Previous studies by Mohr (1997) on $^{144}$Sm and by Fulop (2001) on $^{94}$Mo found that elastic scattering data alone failed to predict $(\alpha, \gamma)$ and $(\alpha, n)$ cross sections accurately, while Palumbo (2012) found mixed success for a larger set of isotopes. Unlike those earlier works, this paper provides a fully Bayesian uncertainty quantification for the optical model parameters, enabling confidence intervals for the predicted $(\alpha, n)$ cross section and illustrating both the limitations of elastic scattering data and the potential for combining scattering with direct cross section measurements to improve predictive power.
