# Charge radii in covariant density functional theory: a global view

> A systematic global investigation of differential charge radii has been performed within the CDFT framework for the first time. Theoretical results obtained with conventional covariant energy density functionals and separable pairing interaction are compared with experimental differential charge radii in the regions of the nuclear chart in which available experimental data crosses neutron shell closures at N = 28, 50, 82 and 126. The analysis of absolute differential radii of different isotopic chains and their relative properties indicate clearly that such properties are reasonably well described in model calculations in the cases when the mean-field approximation is justified. However, while the observed clusterization of differential charge radii of different isotopic chains is well described above the N=50 and N=126 shell closures, it is more difficult to reproduce it above the N=28 and N=82 shell closures because of possible deficiencies in underlying single-particle structure. The impact of the latter has been evaluated for spherical shapes and it was shown that the relative energies of the single-particle states and the patterns of their occupation with increasing neutron number have an appreciable impact on the evolution of the differential charge radii. It is shown that the kinks in the charge radii at neutron shell closures are due to the underlying single-particle structure and due to weakening or collapse of pairing at these closures. It is usually assumed that pairing is a dominant contributor to odd-even staggering (OES) in charge radii. Our analysis paints a more complicated picture. It suggests a new mechanism in which the fragmentation of the single-particle content of the ground state in odd-mass nuclei due to particle-vibration coupling provides a significant contribution to OES in charge radii.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first systematic global investigation of differential charge radii within covariant density functional theory (CDFT), showing that conventional covariant energy density functionals can reasonably describe these radii where the mean-field approximation is justified, but that deficiencies in underlying single-particle structure limit predictive power above the N=28 and N=82 shell closures, and that a new mechanism involving fragmentation of single-particle content in odd-mass nuclei due to particle-vibration coupling contributes significantly to odd-even staggering in charge radii.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) calculations using five covariant energy density functionals (NL3*, DD-PC1, DD-ME2, DD-MEδ, and PC-PK1) with a separable pairing interaction of finite range. They computed differential mean-square charge radii relative to reference nuclei and three-point indicators for odd-even staggering, comparing results to experimental data across isotopic chains crossing neutron shell closures at N=28, 50, 82, and 126, using both spherical and axially deformed codes.

## Key numbers

- The rms deviation of calculated absolute charge radii from experimental values is at the level of approximately 0.03 fm, corresponding to an average precision of 0.625% in prediction of charge radii
- For the Pb isotopes, the differential charge radii δ⟨r²⟩^{N,126} values at the extremes range from -0.58 fm² to -0.48 fm² (without pairing) and from -0.51 fm² to -0.49 fm² (with pairing) for the lower end, and from 0.86 fm² to 1.12 fm² (without pairing) and from 0.78 fm² to 0.92 fm² (with pairing) for the upper end
- The experimental charge radius of ²⁰⁸Pb is 5.5012 ± 0.0013 fm; calculated values range from 5.509 fm (DD-MEδ and NL3*) to 5.519 fm (PC-PK1)
- The neutron skin thickness of ²⁰⁸Pb from the PREX-II experiment is 0.283 ± 0.071 fm, while non-PREX experiments give approximately 0.19 fm; calculated values range from 0.186 fm (DD-MEδ) to 0.288 fm (NL3*)
- The neutron 2g₉/₂ orbital has a single-particle rms neutron radius approximately 0.6 fm larger than the 1i₁₁/₂ orbital in relativistic DFT

## Context

This work extends previous CDFT studies of charge radii that were limited to specific isotopic chains, such as the first successful description of the kink in lead isotopes by Sharma et al. (1993) and recent work on Pb and Hg isotopes by the same group (2021). It contrasts with non-relativistic approaches: conventional Skyrme functionals failed to reproduce the kink in Pb isotopes (Tajima et al., 1993), while Fayans functionals with gradient terms have been more successful (Fayans et al., 1994-2000; Reinhard and Nazarewicz, 2017) but lack clear microscopic justification. The paper identifies deficiencies in single-particle structure above N=28 and N=82 shell closures and proposes a new mechanism for odd-even staggering via particle-vibration coupling, enabling future studies of charge radii in regions where beyond-mean-field correlations are expected to be important.
