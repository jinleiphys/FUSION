# Low-energy isovector and isoscalar dipole response in neutron-rich nuclei

> The self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA), based on the framework of relativistic energy density functionals, is employed in the study of isovector and isoscalar dipole response in $^{68}$Ni, $^{132}$Sn, and $^{208}$Pb. The evolution of pygmy dipole states (PDS) in the region of low excitation energies is analyzed as a function of the density-dependence of the symmetry energy for a set of relativistic effective interactions. The occurrence of PDS is predicted in the response to both the isovector and isoscalar dipole operators, and its strength is enhanced with the increase of the symmetry energy at saturation and the slope of the symmetry energy. In both channels the PDS exhausts a relatively small fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule but a much larger percentage of the inverse energy-weighted sum rule. For the isovector dipole operator the reduced transition probability $B(E1)$ of the PDS is generally small because of pronounced cancellation of neutron and proton partial contributions. The isoscalar reduced transition amplitude is predominantly determined by neutron particle-hole configurations, most of which add coherently, and this results in a collective response of the PDS to the isoscalar dipole operator.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that pygmy dipole states (PDS) appear in both the isovector and isoscalar dipole response of neutron-rich nuclei, with the isoscalar channel showing a much more collective and pronounced PDS than the isovector channel, but this result is conditional on the specific set of relativistic effective interactions used and the density dependence of the symmetry energy.

## Method

The authors employed the fully self-consistent relativistic random phase approximation (RRPA) based on relativistic energy density functionals with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings. They calculated the isovector and isoscalar dipole strength distributions for the neutron-rich nuclei 68Ni, 132Sn, and 208Pb using a set of five effective interactions with varying symmetry energy at saturation (a4 from 30 to 38 MeV) and corresponding slope parameters L, plus the DD-ME2 interaction, and analyzed the moments of the strength distributions and partial transition amplitudes of particle-hole configurations.

## Key numbers

- The dipole polarizability of 208Pb calculated with the DD-ME2 interaction is 20.8 fm3, in agreement with the experimental value of (20.1 ± 0.6) fm3 (Tam. 2011)
- For 68Ni, the percentage of the energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) exhausted by the PDS in the isovector channel increases linearly with the slope parameter L, with the experimental constraint from Wie. 2009 and Car. 2010 shown as a shaded area
- In the isoscalar channel for 68Ni, the PDS exhausts more than 10% of the EWSR and more than 50% of the m-1 moment for all values of L
- For 132Sn, the main pygmy state appears at 7.81 MeV excitation energy for the interaction with a4 = 32 MeV and L = 46.5 MeV
- The neutron-skin thickness in 132Sn increases from rn - rp = 0.19 fm for a4 = 30 MeV, L = 30 MeV to rn - rp = 0.36 fm for a4 = 38 MeV, L = 110.8 MeV (from Kli. 2007)
- The isovector reduced transition amplitude of the PDS in 132Sn is characterized by destructive interference, with the total neutron amplitude (0.38 fm) and proton amplitude (0.64 fm) for a4 = 32 MeV partially canceling
- The isoscalar reduced transition amplitude of the PDS in 132Sn shows coherence, with total neutron amplitude approximately 84 fm3 and proton amplitude approximately 17 fm3 for a4 = 32 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Roca-Maza et al. (2011) on the isospin character and collectivity of low-energy dipole response, but uses a more systematic set of relativistic effective interactions rather than the three Skyrme parameter sets (SGII, SLy5, SkI3) employed in that study. It also complements the work of Piekarewicz (2011) who analyzed the PDS distribution only for 68Ni and only in the isovector channel. The results enable future studies to use the isoscalar dipole response as a more sensitive probe of the density dependence of the symmetry energy and neutron-skin thickness, and provide constraints on the slope parameter L consistent with the value L = 64.8 ± 15.7 MeV deduced by Carbone et al. (2010).
