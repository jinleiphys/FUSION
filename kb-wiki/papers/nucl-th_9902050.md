# On the Simulation of Extended TDHF Theory

> A novel method is presented for implementation of the extended mean-field theory incorporating two-body collisions. At a given time, stochastic imaginary time propagation of occupied states are used to generate a convenient basis. The quantal collision terms, including memory effects, is then computed by a backward mean-field propagation of these single-particle states. The method is illustrated in an exactly solvable model. Whereas the usual TDHF fails to reproduce the long time evolution, a good agreement is found between the extended TDHF and the exact solution.

# On the Simulation of Extended TDHF Theory

## Key claim

The paper presents a practical numerical method for implementing extended Time-Dependent Hartree-Fock (ETDHF) theory with quantal collision terms including memory effects, and demonstrates in a one-dimensional two-fermion model that ETDHF reproduces the exact single-particle dynamics at long times where standard TDHF fails. The method relies on generating relevant unoccupied states via stochastic imaginary time propagation, but the authors caution that the approach may depend on the structure of the residual interaction and should be tested carefully before application to nuclear collisions around Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors implement ETDHF by iteratively evolving the single-particle density matrix in the natural representation, using a backward mean-field propagation of single-particle states to compute the quantal collision term with memory effects over a time interval larger than the mean-field time step but smaller than the correlation time. At each time step, relevant unoccupied states are generated by repeated application of a stochastic imaginary time propagator on the occupied states, followed by orthonormalization via a Schmidt procedure; these states are then evolved backward in time using the mean-field propagator to evaluate the collision integral. The method is tested on a one-dimensional system of two identical fermions in an anharmonic potential interacting via a short-range Gaussian force, comparing ETDHF results against exact Liouville von Neumann evolution of the two-particle density matrix.

## Key numbers

- The anharmonic potential parameters are: k = -0.04 MeV/fm^2, k' = 0.08 MeV/fm^4, with x0 = 9.3 fm.
- The two-body interaction is a Gaussian with v0 = -4 MeV and sigma = 2 fm.
- The system is initially prepared at temperature T = 5 MeV.
- The numerical time step for mean-field evolution is 0.5 fm/c, while the collision term is evaluated over intervals of Delta t = 6 fm/c.
- Two iterations of the imaginary time propagator were sufficient to generate the relevant unoccupied states.
- The ETDHF calculation requires approximately 20-100 times more computation time than the corresponding TDHF simulation.

## Context

This work extends the formal development of ETDHF theory (Ayi80, Goek, Bal86) by providing a practical numerical implementation that overcomes the major difficulty of specifying relevant unoccupied states, which had previously limited applications to simplified models (Toh85, Tohayama, Giant). The method enables quantal transport calculations that can test the validity of semi-classical approximations like the Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) model (Ber88), which has been widely used for nuclear collisions at intermediate energies. The authors note that a first step toward realistic applications has been taken in a recent study of collective vibrations at finite temperature in the small-amplitude limit of ETDHF (d2), and work on full ETDHF simulations for large-amplitude collective motion is in progress.
