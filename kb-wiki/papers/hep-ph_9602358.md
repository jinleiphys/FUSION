# A Dynamical Model of Color Confinement

> A dynamical model of confinement based on a transport theoretical description of the Friedberg-Lee model is extended to explicit color degrees of freedom. The string tension is reproduced by an adiabatic string formation from the nucleon ground state. Color isovector oscillation modes of a $q\bar{q}$-system are investigated for a wide range of relative $q\bar{q}$-momenta and the dynamical impact of color confinement on the quark motion is shown.

# A Dynamical Model of Color Confinement

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that color confinement is maintained dynamically in a transport model based on the Friedberg-Lee Lagrangian, even for arbitrary relative momenta of the quarks, but only within the abelian U(1) subspace of SU(3)-color and under the assumption that the nonabelian effects are fully absorbed into the color dielectric function.

## Method

The authors derive semiclassical Vlasov transport equations for color singlet and color octet distribution functions from the Friedberg-Lee Lagrangian, then restrict to a U(1) subspace where only the eighth color component is nonzero. They solve the coupled system of the Dirac equation (via transport), the Klein-Gordon equation for the sigma field, and the Maxwell equation for the colorelectric field, using a Thomas-Fermi ground state and a test-particle method for time evolution. The strong coupling constant is fixed by requiring that the colorelectric energy from an adiabatic separation of quark and antiquark distributions reproduces the string tension.

## Key numbers

- The string tension is reproduced at approximately τ ≈ 1 GeV/fm, requiring a strong coupling constant of α_s ≈ 2.
- Initial momenta p_z for the excited color isovector oscillation modes range from p_z = μ = 350 MeV up to p_z = 30μ.
- Quark separation velocities tested range from v_z = 0.1c to v_z = 0.5c to verify velocity independence of the colorelectric field.
- Excitation energies studied are typically of order a few GeV, with lower excitations around 300 MeV showing damping effects.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous transport theory based on the Friedberg-Lee model (Kaeding et al. 1993, Vetter et al. 1995) by adding explicit color degrees of freedom, whereas those earlier studies neglected color entirely. The approach is conceptually close to the transport theory of Zhang and Wilets based on the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model, but that work did not include confinement or perform dynamical simulations. The model enables future studies of color gluon fields in nucleon-nucleon collisions, dynamical treatment of hadronization in quark-gluon plasma scenarios, and implementation of q-qbar production via the Schwinger mechanism.
