# Numerically Fitting The Electron Fermi Energy and The Electron Fraction in A Neutron Star

> Based on the basic definition of Fermi energy of degenerate and relativistic electrons, we obtain a special solution to electron Fermi energy, $E_{\rm F}(e)$, and express $E_{\rm F}(e)$ as a function of electron fraction, $Y_{e}$, and matter density, $ρ$. Several useful analytical formulae for $Y_{e}$ and $ρ$ within classical models and the work of Dutra et al. 2014 (Type-2) in relativistic mean field theory are obtained using numerically fitting. When describing the mean-field Lagrangian, density, we adopt the TMA parameter set, which is remarkably consistent with with the updated astrophysical observations of neutron stars. Due to the importance of the density dependence of the symmetry energy, $S$, in nuclear astrophysics, a brief discussion on the symmetry parameters $S_v$ and $L$ (the slope of $S$) is presented. Combining these fit formulae with boundary conditions for different density regions, we can evaluate the value of $E_{\rm F}(e)$ in any given matter density, and obtain a schematic diagram of $E_{\rm F}(e)$ as a continuous function of $ρ$. Compared with previous study on the electron Fermi energy in other models, our methods of calculating $E_{\rm F}(e)$ are more simple and convenient, and can be universally suitable for the relativistic electron regions in the circumstances of common neutron stars. We have deduced a general expression of $E_{\rm F}(e)$ and $n_{e}$, which could be used to indirectly test whether one EoS of a NS is correct in our future studies on neutron star matter properties. Since URCA reactions are expected in the center of a massive star due to high-value electron Fermi energy and electron fraction, this study could be useful in the future studies on the NS thermal evolution.

# Numerically Fitting The Electron Fermi Energy and The Electron Fraction in A Neutron Star

## Key claim
The authors derive a special solution for electron Fermi energy as a function of electron fraction and matter density, and obtain analytical fitting formulae that are simpler and more convenient than previous model-dependent approaches, but these formulae are only approximately suitable for relativistic electron regions above 8.6 × 10⁶ g cm⁻³ in common neutron stars, and the fits rely on specific boundary conditions and model assumptions (BBP, BPS, NV, and an ideal mixed model) that may not hold at the highest densities.

## Method
The authors start from the basic definition of Fermi energy for degenerate relativistic electrons and derive a general expression linking electron Fermi energy E_F(e) to electron fraction Y_e and matter density ρ. They then obtain a special solution for E_F(e) under the assumption of an ideal n-p-e system in β-equilibrium in the outer core, where neutrons and protons are non-relativistic. Using numerically fitting, they derive analytical formulae for Y_e as a function of ρ in different density regions (outer crust, inner crust, outer core, inner core) based on classical models (BPS, BBP, NV) and the TMA parameter set from relativistic mean field theory, and combine these with the special solution to produce continuous schematic diagrams of E_F(e) versus ρ.

## Key numbers
- The electron Fermi energy general formula: E_F(e) = [1 + 1.018 × 10⁻⁴ (ρ Y_e)^(2/3)]^(1/2) × 0.511 MeV, approximately suitable for ρ ≥ 8.6 × 10⁶ g cm⁻³
- The special solution in the outer core: E_F(e) = 60 (ρ/ρ₀)^(2/3) MeV, where ρ₀ = 2.8 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³
- Neutron drip density ρ_d = 4.3 × 10¹¹ g cm⁻³, with Y_e ≈ 0.3051 and E_F(e) ≈ 26.2 MeV at this density
- Crust-core transition density ρ_t = 1.32 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³ (from NV model), with Y_e ≈ 0.0026 and E_F(e) ≈ 36.3 MeV
- Threshold density for muon appearance ρ_μ = 8.21 × 10¹⁴ g cm⁻³, corresponding to x_e = 206.8, x_p = 0.1126, x_n = 0.4986
- In the outer crust, Y_e values range from 0.4643 (for ⁵⁶Fe at 8.1 × 10⁶ g cm⁻³) to 0.3051 (for ¹¹⁸Kr at 4.3 × 10¹¹ g cm⁻³)
- In the inner crust lower density region (4.3 × 10¹¹ to 6.25 × 10¹² g cm⁻³), the fitted formula is Y_e = 0.0164 − 214.87 e^(−Log₁₀ρ) + 3.67 × 10⁹ e^(−2 Log₁₀ρ)
- In the outer core, Y_e ≈ 0.005647 × (ρ/ρ₀) in the range 0.5ρ₀ to 2.5ρ₀
- In the inner core (8.2 × 10¹⁴ to 2.0 × 10¹⁵ g cm⁻³), the fitted formula is Y_e ≈ −0.0115 + 7.402 × 10⁻⁹ e^(Log₁₀ρ)
- The magnitude of relative difference |(E_F'(e) − E_F(e))/E_F(e)| between the special solution and BBP model values is ∼ 10⁻³

## Context
This work extends the approach of Shapiro and Teukolsky (1983) for the outer core and applies numerical fitting to classical neutron star matter models including Baym, Pethick and Sutherland (1971) for the outer crust, Baym, Bethe and Pethick (1971) for the inner crust, and Negele and Vautherin (1973) for the crust-core transition. The authors also reference the relativistic mean field work of Dutra et al. (2014) and adopt the TMA parameter set. The resulting analytical formulae for electron Fermi energy and electron fraction as continuous functions of density enable simpler evaluation of these quantities across different density regions of a neutron star, which can be used to test equations of state and study thermal evolution via URCA reactions.
