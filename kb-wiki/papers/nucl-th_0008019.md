# Semiempirical Shell Model Masses with Magic Number Z=126 for Translead Elements with N smaller or equal to 126

> A semiempirical shell model mass equation based on magic number Z=126 and applicable to translead elements with N smaller or equal to 126 is presented. For alpha decay energies the equation is shown to have a high predictive power and an rms deviation from the data of about 100 keV. The rms deviations for masses and other mass differences are between about 200 and 300 keV.

# Semiempirical Shell Model Masses with Magic Number Z=126 for Translead Elements with N ≤ 126

## Key claim
The paper presents a semiempirical shell model mass equation (SSME) based on magic number Z=126 rather than Z=114, which achieves high predictive power for alpha decay energies (rms deviation ~100 keV) in translead nuclei with N ≤ 126, but the authors caution that this does not constitute proof of superior magicity of Z=126 compared to other proposed magic numbers, as no comparative studies of this kind were made.

## Method
The authors used a least-squares adjustment of a shell model mass equation (comprising pairing, deformation, and Coulomb energy terms) to 29 masses and 62 Q_alpha values from pre-1973 data, with Z=126 as the upper proton boundary in the shell region where N ≤ 126 and Z ≥ 82. They then tested the predictive power against 121 new masses and 3 new Q_alpha values that became available after the original adjustment, and subsequently performed a re-adjustment of only four parameters (alpha, epsilon, Theta, kappa) using all 150 known masses while keeping the other seven coefficients fixed.

## Key numbers
- rms deviation for Q_alpha values: about 100 keV (both old and new data)
- rms deviations for masses and other mass differences: between about 200 and 300 keV
- For the re-adjusted parameters using all 150 masses: average mass deviation 2 keV, rms mass deviation 246 keV
- The original adjustment used 29 masses and 62 Q_alpha values; the test set included 121 new masses not used in the original adjustment
- For Delta M and Q_beta- after re-adjustment, the rms deviations are respectively 1.5 and 1.1 times larger than for the original nuclei

## Context
This work extends the earlier SSME of Liran and Zeldes (1976) which assumed Z=114 as the next spherical proton magic number after lead, but which became unsuitable for extrapolation beyond Hs (Z=108). The authors build on phenomenological studies by Zamfir (1995) and Zeldes (1998) indicating Z=126 as the next magic number, and on their own recent communication (Liran, Marinov, Zeldes) showing predictive power in the heavier region B (126 ≤ N ≤ 184). The proposed mass equation enables improved predictions for masses and particularly Q_alpha values in translead nuclei with N ≤ 126, pending a future full adjustment covering both regions A and B.
