# Exploring the DGLAP resummation in the JIMWLK Hamiltonian

> We explore the recently derived equation that resums DGLAP corrections to the JIMWLK Hamiltonian in the simplified setting of the SU(2) gauge theory. We solve the equation numerically for the scattering matrix of a dressed gluon for a particular initial condition, that corresponds to a dipole initial state. As expected, the $S$-matrix of a single dressed gluon state ceases to be unitary if evolved to significant $\ln Q^2/Q_s^2$. Our numerical results indicate an interesting universal (independent of the coupling constant) pattern for this deviation from unitarity.

## Key claim

The paper numerically solves the recently derived equation that resums DGLAP corrections to the JIMWLK Hamiltonian, but only in the simplified setting of SU(2) gauge theory and under a dilute approximation that assumes the scattering matrix remains close to unity. The authors find that the S-matrix of a single dressed gluon state ceases to be unitary when evolved to significant \(\ln Q^2/Q_P^2\), and report a universal (coupling-constant-independent) pattern for this deviation from unitarity.

## Method

The authors decompose the SU(2) dressed-gluon S-matrix \(\mathbb{S}_Q\) into scalar (A), vector (\(\lambda^a\)), and symmetric traceless tensor (\(B^{ab}\)) components, then write the DGLAP-type evolution equation for each component. They work in the dilute limit where \(\lambda \sim \alpha_s\) and \(\Delta, B \propto \lambda^2\), which decouples the equation for \(\lambda\) and turns the equations for \(\Delta\) and \(B\) into linear inhomogeneous forms. The equations are solved numerically in momentum space for an initial condition corresponding to a single dipole target, with the evolution run from the target inverse correlation scale \(Q_T\) down to the projectile scale \(Q_P\).

## Key numbers

* The evolution of \(\lambda_Q\) for \(\alpha_s=0.1\) shows at most \(5-8\%\) deviation from the initial condition down to \(Q = Q_T/10\).
* Deviations of \(\Delta_Q\) from initial conditions remain under \(10\%\) unless one goes to extremely small values of \(Q\).
* The asymptotic behavior of \(\lambda_Q^c(\mathbf{p})/\lambda_{Q_T}^c(\mathbf{p})\) for \(Q\to 0\) or \(p\to\infty\) is \((Q_T/Q)^{\alpha_s \beta_0^g / 2\pi}\).
* The homogeneous part of \(\Delta_Q\) decays as \((Q_T/Q)^{\alpha_s \beta_0^g / 2\pi}\) for \(Q\to 0\) or \(p\to\infty\), and as \((Q/Q_T)^{\alpha_s \beta_0^g / 2\pi}\) for \(p\to 0\).
* The homogeneous part of \(B_Q\) decays as \((Q_T/Q)^{\alpha_s \beta_0^g / 2\pi}\) for \(Q\to 0\) or \(p\to\infty\), and as \((Q_T/Q)^{\alpha_s \beta_0^g / \pi}\) for \(p\to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the analytic results of Kovner (2023) by going beyond the leading-order dilute approximation to include second-order corrections that allow probing deviations from unitarity. The DGLAP resummation addressed here is part of a broader effort to connect saturation physics at high energy with intermediate-x DGLAP evolution, motivated by the need for controlled predictions at the upcoming Electron Ion Collider (EIC). The paper specifically studies the SU(2) case as a simplified setting, leaving the full SU(3) problem for future work.
