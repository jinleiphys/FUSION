# Phi meson mass and decay width in nuclear matter

> The $ϕ$ meson spectrum, which in vacuum is dominated by its coupling to the $\bar{K} K$ system, is modified in nuclear matter. Following a model based on chiral SU(3) dynamics we calculate the $ϕ$ meson selfenergy in nuclear matter considering the $K$ and $\bar{K}$ in-medium properties. For the latter we use the results of previous calculations which account for $S-$ and $P-$wave kaon-nucleon interactions based on the lowest order meson-baryon chiral effective Lagrangian, and this leads to a dressing of the kaon propagators in the medium. In addition, a set of vertex corrections is evaluated to fulfill gauge invariance, which involves contact couplings of the $ϕ$ meson to $S-$wave and $P-$wave kaon-baryon vertices. Within this scheme the mass shift and decay width of the $ϕ$ meson in nuclear matter are studied.

# Phi meson mass and decay width in nuclear matter

## Key claim

The phi meson mass shift in cold symmetric nuclear matter is small (approximately 8 MeV downward at normal nuclear density), while the decay width increases by nearly an order of magnitude to about 30 MeV at normal nuclear density, but the phi meson remains narrow enough to be observed as an isolated resonance in dilepton or kaon-antikaon spectra.

## Method

The authors calculate the phi meson selfenergy in nuclear matter by dressing kaon propagators with S-wave and P-wave selfenergies derived from chiral SU(3) dynamics. The S-wave antikaon selfenergy comes from a selfconsistent coupled-channel unitary calculation of the antikaon-nucleon amplitude including Pauli blocking, pion selfenergy, and mean-field baryon potentials. The P-wave antikaon selfenergy accounts for hyperon-hole excitations (Lambda, Sigma, Sigma*(1385)). Vertex corrections are evaluated to satisfy gauge invariance, involving contact couplings of the phi meson to S-wave and P-wave kaon-baryon vertices, and the free selfenergy is subtracted from the in-medium result to obtain a finite subtracted selfenergy.

## Key numbers

- The phi meson coupling constant to kaons is fixed as g_phi = 4.57 by matching the calculated free width to the experimental value of Gamma(phi -> K+ K-).
- The phi meson mass shift at normal nuclear density (rho_0) is approximately 8 MeV downward.
- The phi meson decay width reaches around 30 MeV at the vacuum phi mass for normal nuclear density.
- The P-wave kaon selfenergy uses a cutoff parameter Lambda = 1.05 GeV for the static dipolar form factors, with a scaling constant C = 1.04 determined by on-shell matching.
- The relevant momenta running in the kaon loops for a phi meson at rest are around 300 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Oset (2001) by calculating both the real and imaginary parts of the phi selfenergy (whereas Oset (2001) only obtained the imaginary part for the width), and improves the treatment of relativistic recoil corrections in the P-wave kaon selfenergy. The results are consistent with Klingl (1998) in finding a small mass shift and a significantly increased width, though the width obtained here (30 MeV) is smaller than the 45 MeV reported by Klingl (1998). The calculation enables interpretation of experiments measuring dilepton or kaon-antikaon spectra from phi meson decays in nuclear reactions, such as proton-nucleus collisions studied by Ozawa (2001), and provides a baseline for distinguishing medium effects from final-state distortions.
