# NLO corrections to timelike and spacelike DVCS

> Generalized Parton Distributions (GPDs) offer a new way to access the quark and gluon nucleon structure. We advocate the need to supplement the experimental study of deeply virtual Compton scattering by its crossed version, timelike Compton scattering. We review recent progress in this domain, emphasizing the need to include NLO corrections to any phenomenological program to extract GPDs from experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper argues that experimental studies of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) must be supplemented by its crossed version, timelike Compton scattering (TCS), and that next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections in alpha_s are essential for any phenomenological program to extract generalized parton distributions (GPDs) from experimental data, because the NLO coefficient functions differ significantly between the two processes.

## Method
The authors computed NLO corrections to the coefficient functions for both timelike and spacelike deeply virtual Compton scattering, using the handbag factorization mechanism. They derived the quark and gluon coefficient functions including the collinear logarithms and evolution kernels, and compared the real and imaginary parts of the NLO-to-Born ratios in the ERBL and DGLAP regions for both processes. They also evaluated the Compton form factors for DVCS using the G-K model for GPDs.

## Key numbers
- For TCS at LHC with nominal luminosity, the anticipated rate is of the order of 10^5 events per year.
- The differential cross sections were computed for t = -0.2 GeV^2, Q'^2 = 5 GeV^2, integrated over theta = [pi/4, 3pi/4], for gamma-N c.m. energy squared s = 10^7 GeV^2 and s = 10^5 GeV^2.
- The NLO calculations used alpha_s = 0.25 and factorization scale mu_F^2 = |Q^2|/2.
- The DVCS Compton form factor was evaluated at LO and NLO for mu_F^2 = Q^2 = 4 GeV^2 as a function of xi.
- The gluon coefficient function ratio between TCS and DVCS in the DGLAP region was plotted for eta = 0.05.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework for hard exclusive reactions beyond leading order, building on the handbag mechanism for DVCS and TCS described by the authors in prior work (Szymanowski et al., 2008). It complements experimental programs at JLab@12 GeV, COMPASS, RHIC, and the LHC, and anticipates future measurements at a forthcoming electron-ion collider (discussed in Szymanowski et al., 2011). The calculations enable more precise extraction of universal GPDs by accounting for the different NLO behavior in timelike versus spacelike kinematics, which is necessary to avoid systematic errors in phenomenological analyses.
