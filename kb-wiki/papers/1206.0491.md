# Relation of isospin-symmetry breaking correction for superallowed beta decay to energy of charge-exchange giant monopole resonance

> After application of an analytical transformation, a new exact representation for the nuclear isospin-symmetry breaking correction $δ_C$ to superallowed beta decay is obtained. The correction is shown to be essentially the reciprocal of the square of an energy parameter $Ω_M$ which characterizes the charge-exchange monopole strength distribution. The proportionality coefficient in this relation is determined by basic properties of the ground state of the even-even parent nucleus, and should be reliably calculable in any realistic nuclear model. Therefore, the single parameter $Ω_M$ contains all the information about the properties of excited $0^+$ states needed to describe $δ_C$. This parameter can possibly be determined experimentally by charge-exchange reactions. Basic quantities of interest are calculated within the isospin-consistent continuum random phase approximation, and the values of $δ_C$ are compared with the corresponding results from other approaches.

## Key claim

The isospin-symmetry breaking correction δ_C for superallowed beta decay can be expressed exactly as essentially the reciprocal of the square of an energy parameter Ω_M that characterizes the charge-exchange monopole strength distribution, provided the proportionality coefficient (determined by ground-state properties of the even-even parent nucleus) is reliably calculable in any realistic nuclear model.

## Method

The author derives an exact analytical transformation of the standard expression for δ_C, relating it to energy-weighted Coulomb sum rules and the commutator of charge-exchange operators. The key energy parameter Ω_M is defined via the ratio of non-energy-weighted to inverse-energy-weighted Coulomb sum rules for the charge-exchange monopole strength. Numerical calculations are performed for four superallowed decays (¹⁰C→¹⁰B, ³⁸K→³⁸Ar, ⁶⁶As→⁶⁶Ge, ⁷⁰Br→⁷⁰Se) using an isospin-consistent continuum random phase approximation (continuum-RPA) with Landau-Migdal zero-range forces and a phenomenological mean field.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁰C→¹⁰B: δ_C = 0.147% (RPA) and 0.142% (from Eq. δ_C111); Ω_M = 12.13 MeV
- For ³⁸K→³⁸Ar: δ_C = 0.434% (RPA) and 0.436% (from Eq. δ_C111); Ω_M = 45.38 MeV
- For ⁶⁶As→⁶⁶Ge: δ_C = 0.992% (RPA) and 1.007% (from Eq. δ_C111); Ω_M = 61.90 MeV
- For ⁷⁰Br→⁷⁰Se: δ_C = 0.992% (RPA) and 0.993% (from Eq. δ_C111); Ω_M = 66.66 MeV
- The calculated IAS excitation energies ω_A are: -1.66 MeV (¹⁰C), 5.57 MeV (³⁸K), 8.97 MeV (⁶⁶As), 9.49 MeV (⁷⁰Br), compared to experimental values -1.397 MeV, 5.533 MeV, 9.609 MeV, and 10.109 MeV respectively

## Context

This work extends the theoretical understanding of isospin-symmetry breaking in superallowed beta decay, which is critical for testing CKM matrix unitarity (Hardy and Towner 2009 found |V_ud|²+|V_us|²+|V_ub|² = 0.99990 ± 0.00060). The new representation directly connects δ_C to the experimentally accessible charge-exchange giant monopole resonance, previously studied in pion charge-exchange reactions (Auerbach 1972, 1983) and more recently in (³He,t) and (t,³He) reactions (Zegers 2000, Miki 2011). The calculated δ_C values are close to those of Liang et al. (2009) from relativistic Hartree+RPA and systematically smaller than those of Towner and Hardy (2008) from shell-model calculations, with the difference attributed to the collectivity of the isovector monopole resonance captured in the continuum-RPA but missing in mean-field approaches.
