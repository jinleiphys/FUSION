# On the Approach Towards Equilibrium Through Momentum-Dependent Relaxation:Insights from Evolution of the Moments in Kinetic Theory

> We investigate the impact of momentum-dependent relaxation time approximation in the Boltzmann equation within the Bjorken flow framework by analyzing the moments of the single-particle distribution function. The moment equations, which form an infinite hierarchy, provide important insights about the system dynamics and the approach towards equilibrium for systems far from equilibrium. The momentum-dependent collision kernel couples moments through both the energy exponents and the angular dependence via various-order Legendre polynomials, resulting in an intricate system of infinitely coupled equations. A naive truncation of the coupled equations results in diverging moments at late times. We outline strategies for solving the coupled system, including a novel approach for managing the divergences and non-integer moments. We show a significant influence of momentum dependent relaxation time on the time evolution of the moments, particularly for higher-order moments and system with smaller shear viscosity over entropy density, emphasizing the importance of incorporating such dependence for a more accurate description of the system dynamics with low shear viscosity such as the quark-gluon-plasma produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating a momentum-dependent relaxation time in the Boltzmann equation (within the Bjorken flow framework) significantly alters the time evolution of moments of the single-particle distribution function, particularly for higher-order moments and systems with smaller shear viscosity over entropy density; however, a naive truncation of the infinite hierarchy of coupled moment equations leads to diverging moments at late times, requiring novel strategies for managing these divergences and non-integer moments.

## Method

The authors derived moment evolution equations from the relativistic Boltzmann equation using a momentum-dependent relaxation time approximation (MDRTA) with a collision kernel that conserves particle number and energy-momentum irrespective of the momentum dependence. They considered a conformal, boost-invariant system under Bjorken flow, defined moments as phase-space integrals weighted by powers of particle energy and Legendre polynomials, and solved the resulting infinite hierarchy of coupled differential equations by truncating at (n_min, n_max) = (0,5) and (l_min, l_max) = (0,100) using the RK4 algorithm. The momentum-dependent relaxation time was parameterized as τ_R(p) = τ_R^0(T) p^Λ, where Λ is a positive exponent, and the system was initialized at temperature T(τ_0) = 1 GeV with zero chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature T(τ_0) = 1 GeV and initial chemical potential μ(τ_0) = 0 for the conformal system with particle mass m = 0.
* Two values of initial shear viscosity to entropy density ratio were used: η_0/s_0 = 0.2 and η_0/s_0 = 0.02.
* Initial scaled time τ_0/τ_R(τ_0) was chosen from the set {0.10, 1.0} to match the Λ = 0 case for comparison.
* The moment hierarchy was truncated at (n_min, n_max) = (0,5) and (l_min, l_max) = (0,100).
* Two values of the momentum-dependence exponent were compared: Λ = 0 and Λ = 0.125.

## Context

This work extends the method-of-moments approach previously applied to the Bjorken flow with momentum-independent relaxation time approximation (AW-RTA) by Blaizot (2017), de Brito (2024), and others, where only the angular index l couples in the moment hierarchy. The inclusion of momentum-dependent relaxation time introduces coupling through the energy exponent n as well, creating an infinite ladder of coupling towards lower n moments. This enables a more accurate description of systems with low shear viscosity such as the quark-gluon plasma produced in high-energy heavy-ion collisions, where microscopic momentum transfer is significant, and builds upon recent hydrodynamic studies using MDRTA by Teaney (2013), Rocha (2021), and Mitra (2020).
