# Determination of the 3He+alpha\to 7Be asymp. normalization coefficients (nucl. vertex constants) and their application for extrapolation of the 3He(alpha,gamma)7Be astroph. S-factors to the solar energy region

> A new analysis of the precise experimental astrophysical $S$-factors for the direct capture $^3He(α,γ)^7{\rm {Be}}$ reaction [B.S. Nara Singh et al., Phys.Rev.Lett. {\bf 93} (2004) 262503; D. Bemmerer et al., Phys.Rev.Lett. {\bf 97} (2006) 122502; F.Confortola et al., Phys.Rev. {\bf C 75} (2007) 065803 and T.A.D.Brown et al., Phys.Rev. {\bf C 76} (2007) 055801] populating to the ground and first excited states of $^7{\rm Be}$ is carried out based on the modified two - body potential approach in which the direct astrophysical $S$-factor, $S_{34}(E)$, is expressed in terms of the asymptotic normalization constants for $^3{\rm {He}}+α\to ^7{\rm {Be}}$ and two additional conditions are involved to verify the peripheral character of the reaction under consideration. The Woods--Saxon potential form is used for the bound ($α+^3{\rm {He}}$)- state and the $^3{\rm {He}}α$- scattering wave functions. New estimates are obtained for the "indirectly measured", values of the asymptotic normalization constants (the nuclear vertex constants) for $^3{\rm {He}}+α\to^7{\rm {Be}}(g.s.)$ and $^3{\rm {He}}+α\to^7{\rm {Be}}(0.429 MeV)$ as well as the astrophysical $S$-factors $S_{34}(E)$ at E$\le$ 90 keV, including $E$=0. The values of asymptotic normalization constants have been used for getting information about the $α$-particle spectroscopic factors for the mirror ($^7Li^7{\rm {Be}}$)-pair.

## Key claim
The authors extract "indirectly measured" asymptotic normalization coefficients (ANCs) for the virtual decays \(^3\text{He}+\alpha \to {}^7\text{Be}\)(ground state) and \(^3\text{He}+\alpha \to {}^7\text{Be}\)(0.429 MeV) directly from precise experimental astrophysical \(S\)-factors, under the condition that the reaction is peripheral and that the extracted ANCs are independent of the geometric parameters of the Woods-Saxon potential within physically acceptable ranges.

## Method
The authors use a modified two-body potential approach in which the direct astrophysical \(S\)-factor \(S_{34}(E)\) is expressed in terms of the ANC for \(^3\text{He}+\alpha \to {}^7\text{Be}\). They verify the peripheral character of the reaction by checking two conditions: that the reduced single-particle \(S\)-factor \({\cal R}_{l_fj_f}(E,C^{(sp)}_{l_fj_f})\) is independent of the single-particle ANC \(C^{(sp)}_{l_fj_f}\) for fixed energy, and that the extracted squared ANC \(C_{l_fj_f}^2\) is constant across energies. Woods-Saxon potentials with geometric parameters varied in the ranges \(r_o = 1.62\)–\(1.98\) fm and \(a = 0.63\)–\(0.77\) fm are used for both bound and scattering states.

## Key numbers
- Weighted mean squared ANC for \(^3\text{He}+\alpha \to {}^7\text{Be}\)(g.s.): \(C_{1,3/2}^2\) values obtained from all experimental data sets agree within about 12%.
- Weighted mean squared ANC for \(^3\text{He}+\alpha \to {}^7\text{Be}\)(0.429 MeV): \(C_{1,1/2}^2\) values obtained from all experimental data sets agree within about 12%.
- The ratio \(\lambda_C = (C_{1,1/2}/C_{1,3/2})^2\) is determined as 0.666 from three data points at \(E = 92.9\), 105.6, and 147.7 keV.
- The contribution of the nuclear interior (\(r < r_N\)) to the calculated astrophysical \(S\)-factors does not exceed about 15.5%.
- The uncertainty in the extracted \(C_{l_fj_f}^2\) values is up to \(\pm 5.0\%\) when geometric parameters \(r_o\) and \(a\) are varied within the stated ranges.

## Context
This work extends the asymptotic method previously applied by Igamov (1997) to the \(^3\text{He}(\alpha,\gamma)^7\text{Be}\) reaction, but uses more precise experimental data from Nara Singh (2004), Bemmerer (2006), Confortola (2007), and Brown (2007) rather than the older data from Osborne (1982) that had considerable spread. The extracted ANCs enable extrapolation of the astrophysical \(S\)-factor \(S_{34}(E)\) to solar energies (\(E \le 90\) keV, including \(E=0\)) and provide information about \(\alpha\)-particle spectroscopic factors for the mirror pair \(^7\text{Li}\)–\(^7\text{Be}\). The approach reduces ambiguities inherent in standard two-body potential model calculations that depend on the choice of geometric parameters and spectroscopic factors.
