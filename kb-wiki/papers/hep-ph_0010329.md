# Rho meson properties in nuclear matter from QCD sum rules

> We study the properties of rho mesons in nuclear matter by means of QCD sum rules at finite density. For increased sensitivity, we subtract out the vacuum contributions. With the spectral function as estimated in the literature, these subtracted sum rules are found to be not well satisfied. We suppose that Landau singularities from higher resonance states in the nearby region in this channel are the cause for this failure.

## Key claim

The subtracted QCD sum rules for the rho meson in nuclear matter, which subtract out vacuum contributions for increased sensitivity, are not well satisfied when using the spectral function as estimated in the literature; the author suggests that Landau singularities from higher resonance states in the nearby region are the cause for this failure, meaning the method cannot give reliable information about the density dependence of the rho meson mass and width.

## Method

The author computed QCD sum rules at finite nuclear density and zero temperature by equating the operator product expansion (including operators up to dimension four) with the spectral representation for the two invariant amplitudes \(T_l\) and \(T_t\) of the vector current correlator. Vacuum contributions were subtracted out, and the Borel transform was applied to both sides of the sum rules. The mass and width of the rho meson were expanded linearly in the nucleon number density \(\bar n\) up to first order, and the coefficients \(a\) and \(b\) (for the mass and width, respectively) were plotted as functions of the Borel parameter \(M\).

## Key numbers

- The nuclear saturation density is \(\bar{n}_s = (110\ \text{MeV})^3\).
- The sigma term used is \(\sigma \simeq 45\ \text{MeV}\), defined as \(\sigma = \langle p | \hat{m} (\bar{u} u + \bar{d} d) | p \rangle / (2 m_N)\).
- The parton distribution function values used are \(A^q = 0.62\) and \(A^g = 0.35\).
- The analysis considers the Borel parameter region \(M^2 > 1\ \text{GeV}^2\) where higher dimensional operator contributions are expected to be relatively small.

## Context

This work extends the QCD sum rule approach to finite density, following earlier vacuum studies by Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and medium extensions by various authors. It directly addresses conflicting results in the literature: most previous studies (e.g., Hatsuda and Lee 1992, Asakawa and Ko 1993) found a drop of the rho meson mass of about 10-20% at nuclear saturation density, while others (e.g., Klingl, Kaiser, and Weise 1997) observed no significant shift. The failure of the subtracted sum rules here suggests that future work must account for contributions from higher resonance states such as \(N \bar{N}^\star\) to properly model the hadronic spectral function.
