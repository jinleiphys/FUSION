# Angular and energy dependence of $(e,e^{\prime})$ cross sections for orbital 1$^+$ excitations

> The main features of the $(e,e^{\prime})$ cross sections of low-lying orbital excitations with $K^π = 1^+$ in heavy deformed nuclei are studied in RPA on the example of $^{156}$Gd. The dependence of the DWBA E2 and M1 cross sections on the scattering angle $0^{\circ} < θ< 180 ^{\circ}$ and incident electron energy $E_i < 210$ MeV is analyzed in PWBA. The cross section is larger for M1 than for E2 transitions at any angle if $E_i < 30$ MeV. The longitudinal (Coulomb) C2 excitation dominates the E2 response for $5^{\circ} < θ< 170 ^{\circ}$. Only transverse M1 and E2 excitations compete for $θ> 175 ^{\circ}$ and the former one is dominant for $q < 1.2$ fm$^{-1}$. The M1 response is almost purely orbital up to $q = 1.4$ fm$^{-1}$ even in backward scattering. Qualitative PWBA estimates based on the $q$-dependence of the form factors alone are not able to predict some important features of the $(e,e^{\prime})$ cross sections stemming from the strong magnetic and orbital character of the studied 1$^+$ excitations. The expectation for M1 over E2 dominance in backward scattering should not be extended to higher momentum transfers and incident energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the common PWBA expectation for M1 dominance over E2 in backward electron scattering should not be extended to higher momentum transfers and incident energies, because the nuclear dynamics of orbital 1⁺ excitations in heavy deformed nuclei produce important deviations from these qualitative kinematic estimates.

## Method

The authors performed quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations for the low-lying K^π = 1⁺ excitation in ¹⁵⁶Gd using a deformed Woods-Saxon mean field with pairing and a separable residual interaction. They computed DWBA and PWBA (e,e') cross sections from realistic RPA transition densities for M1 and E2 transitions, analyzing their dependence on scattering angle (0° to 180°) and incident electron energy (10 to 210 MeV).

## Key numbers

- The strongest theoretical M1 transition in ¹⁵⁶Gd has excitation energy E_x = 2.90 MeV with B(M1) = 1.24 μ_N² (experimental: 3.07 MeV, B(M1) = 1.30 ± 0.20 μ_N²)
- The accompanying E2 transition has theoretical B(E2) = 42 e²fm⁴ (experimental: 40 ± 6 e²fm⁴)
- The M1 cross section is larger than the E2 cross section at any angle for incident energies E_i < 30 MeV
- The M1 response is almost purely orbital up to momentum transfer q = 1.4 fm⁻¹ even in backward scattering
- The M1 cross section dominates over the C2 cross section only for q < 0.17 fm⁻¹ (E_i < 30 MeV)
- The orbital-to-spin ratio of the M1 transition matrix elements is R_o.s. = 7.8 for this excitation

## Context

This work extends the study of low-lying orbital M1 excitations in heavy deformed nuclei, which were first observed experimentally at Darmstadt (Bohle et al., 1984) and reviewed by Richter (1990s). The authors had previously found (Nojarov et al., 1994; Dingfelder et al., 1995) that E2 contributions contaminate M1 cross sections measured at θ = 165° in heavy deformed nuclei, contrary to the common expectation from PWBA that magnetic excitations dominate backward scattering (Barber, 1962; Barber, 1963; Fagg, 1975). The results enable more accurate interpretation of existing (e,e') data on orbital M1 excitations and provide guidance for future experiments at different angles and energies beyond the standard 165° measurement.
