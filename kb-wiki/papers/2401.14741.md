# Asymmetric collisions in MadGraph5_aMC@NLO

> We will gain unprecedented, high-accuracy insights into the internal structure of the atomic nucleus thanks to lepton-hadron collision studies in the coming years at the Electron-Ion-Collider (EIC) in the United States. A good control of radiative corrections is necessary for the EIC to be fully exploited and to extract valuable information from various measurements. We present our extension of photoproduction at fixed order in MadGraph5_aMC@NLO, a widely used framework for (next-to-)leading order calculations at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). It applies to electron-hadron collisions, in which the quasi-real photon comes from an electron as well as to proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus collisions.

## Key claim

The authors present an extension of MadGraph5_aMC@NLO to handle direct photoproduction in electron-hadron collisions and asymmetric hadron-hadron collisions, validated at next-to-leading order; the extension currently covers only the direct photoproduction channel, not the resolved channel, though the framework can in principle accommodate both.

## Method

The authors modified the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO algorithm to allow simultaneous calls to two distinct LHAPDF sets for asymmetric hadron-hadron collisions, and replaced one PDF with the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) photon flux for electron-hadron photoproduction. They introduced separate control variables for the maximal photon virtuality Q²_max and the factorization scale, then validated the implementation by comparing LO and NLO predictions for bottom and charm quark photoproduction against the existing codes HELAC-Onia and FMNR. Finally, they produced NLO predictions for b-quark photoproduction at four future electron-proton facilities (EIcC, EIC, LHeC, FCC-eh) with scale and PDF uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- LO bottom quark photoproduction at √s = 319 GeV: agreement within about 5% between MG5, HELAC-Onia, and FMNR for the P_T(b) distribution, using CTEQ6M PDFs with μ_F = μ_R = m_b = 4.75 GeV.
- NLO bottom quark photoproduction at √s = 319 GeV: agreement up to ~O(1%) between MG5 and FMNR for P_T(b) > 1 GeV, with m_b varied in [4.5, 5.0] GeV and μ_R,F = μ_0 = ½ √(m_b² + ⟨P_T(b)⟩²) varied by factors 0.5 and 2.0.
- Kinematical cuts applied for NLO validation: |η_{b, b̅}| ≤ 2 and 16 GeV < W_γp < 207 GeV.
- Predicted CM energies for future ep facilities: EIcC at 16.7 GeV, EIC at 45-140 GeV, LHeC at 1.2 TeV, FCC-eh at 3.4 TeV.
- Integrated luminosities for future facilities: EIcC 50 fb⁻¹, EIC 10-100 fb⁻¹, LHeC 100 fb⁻¹, FCC-eh 100 fb⁻¹.
- For predictions, μ_F = μ_R = 10 GeV with scale uncertainties spanning 0.5 μ_0 to 2 μ_0, using CT18NLO PDF set.

## Context

This work extends the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework, previously restricted to symmetric hadron-hadron collisions, to asymmetric collisions including electron-hadron photoproduction relevant for the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). It builds on prior photoproduction tools such as HELAC-Onia (Shao, 2015), Pythia (Helenius, 2017), and FMNR (Frixione, 1993), which are limited to LO or are not automated, and complements recent NLO developments for SHERPA (Hoeche, 2023; Meinzinger, 2023). The extension enables NLO predictions for heavy-quark photoproduction at future facilities (EIcC, EIC, LHeC, FCC-eh) and can be expanded to resolved photoproduction, top quark and Higgs boson photoproduction, and inclusive ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC.
