# Comparative Study of Langevin and Random Walk Models for Nuclear Fission in the Overdamped Regime

> We present a comparative study of Langevin dynamics and a Metropolis random walk model applied to thermal neutron-induced fission of $^{229}$Th, $^{235}$U, $^{239}$Pu, $^{245}$Cm, $^{249}$Cf, and $^{255}$Fm. Both methods are implemented within an identical four-dimensional Fourier-over-Spheroid framework, using potential energy surfaces derived from the macroscopic-microscopic model. We show that the Metropolis walk corresponds to the overdamped limit of the Langevin equations and confirm this correspondence numerically by Langevin calculations performed in the strongly damped regime and with quantum corrections to the random force switched off. Under these conditions, the two approaches produce essentially identical mass distributions for the lighter actinides. Systematic deviations develop for the heavier actinides, where the Langevin dynamics yields a non-negligible symmetric fission component absent in the random walk results. We trace this difference to the kinematic structure of the Metropolis sampling and to the residual inertial dynamics retained in the Langevin framework. A parallel comparison of Langevin calculations with and without the quantum-corrected effective temperature $T^*$ isolates the contribution of zero-point fluctuations and suggests that their standard phenomenological treatment may overestimate their impact in certain cases. Both approaches qualitatively reproduce the asymmetric peak positions and their systematic evolution across the actinide chain, while a common quantitative limitation -- the narrowness of the predicted distributions -- points to the role of higher-dimensional deformation modes not included in the present parametrization.

## Key claim

The Metropolis random walk model corresponds to the overdamped limit of the Langevin equations, and the two approaches produce essentially identical mass distributions for lighter actinides when Langevin calculations are performed in the strongly damped regime with quantum corrections to the random force switched off; however, systematic deviations develop for heavier actinides, where Langevin dynamics yields a non-negligible symmetric fission component absent in the random walk results.

## Method

Both methods were implemented within an identical four-dimensional Fourier-over-Spheroid (FoS) framework using potential energy surfaces derived from the macroscopic-microscopic model with the Lublin-Strasbourg Drop formula and Yukawa-folded single-particle potential. The Langevin dynamics simulated coupled stochastic differential equations for three deformation coordinates (c, a3, a4) and their conjugate momenta, with friction estimated using the one-body Wall model with a phenomenological temperature-dependent reduction, while the Metropolis random walk evolved as a sequence of probabilistic steps between neighboring grid points governed by the density of intrinsic nuclear states. Both approaches initiated trajectories near the outer saddle point for thermal neutron-induced fission of six actinide targets (229Th, 235U, 239Pu, 245Cm, 249Cf, and 255Fm) and used a critical neck radius of 1.2 fm to define scission.

## Key numbers

- For the six studied systems (229Th, 235U, 239Pu, 245Cm, 249Cf, and 255Fm), an ensemble of 10^5 independent Langevin trajectories was generated for each fissioning system.
- The critical neck radius defining scission was set to 1.2 fm, corresponding to a4 ≈ 0.72 in the FoS parametrization.
- The phenomenological friction tensor uses a residual friction parameter ε = 0.05 at T → 0 and approaches 2/3 of the Wall model value at high temperatures, with a transition function centered at T = 1.3 MeV with width 0.2 MeV.
- The quantum-corrected effective temperature T* uses a zero-point energy E0 ≈ 1 MeV, such that T* → E0 as T → 0 and T* → T as T → ∞.
- The level density parameter in the random walk model was set to a constant a0 = A/8.5 MeV⁻¹.
- The temperature dependence of microscopic corrections uses a Fermi function with parameters T = 1.5 MeV and width 0.3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Langevin dynamics studies of Pomorski (2024, 2023) using the Fourier-over-Spheroid parametrization by providing a direct comparison with the Metropolis random walk methodology of Randrup (2011, 2013). The finding that both approaches qualitatively reproduce asymmetric peak positions and their systematic evolution across the actinide chain, while sharing a common quantitative limitation of narrow predicted distributions, points to the role of higher-dimensional deformation modes not included in the present parametrization and motivates future work incorporating additional multipole deformations such as a5 and a6.
