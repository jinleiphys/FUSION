# Shell Effects on Rotational Damping in Superdeformed Nuclei

> Damping of rotational motion in superdeformed Hg and Dy-region nuclei is studied by means of cranked shell model diagonalization. It is shown that a shell oscillation in single-particle alignments affects significantly properties of rotational damping. Onset properties of damping and damping width for Hg are quite different from those for Dy-region superdeformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in superdeformed \(^{192}\)Hg, the rotational damping width is remarkably small (\(\Gamma_{\rm rot} \sim 20\text{--}30\) keV) and the number of rotational bands is unusually large (\(N_{\rm band} \sim 150\)), due to a shell effect in single-particle alignments rather than simply the level density effect from shell gaps.

## Method

The authors performed a fully microscopic cranked shell model diagonalization for superdeformed states in \(^{192}\)Hg, constructing multi-particle multi-hole basis states from diabatic single-particle routhian orbits of the cranked Nilsson Hamiltonian. They included a delta residual two-body interaction and diagonalized the Hamiltonian matrix for each spin and parity, then calculated E2 transition strengths between eigenstates to characterize rotational damping properties. The results were compared with superdeformed \(^{143}\)Eu, \(^{152}\)Dy, and normally deformed \(^{168}\)Yb using the same interaction parameters.

## Key numbers

- Rotational damping width \(\Gamma_{\rm rot}^{(2)}\) for \(^{192}\)Hg is \(25\text{--}30\) keV, smaller by more than a factor of 4 than in \(^{143}\)Eu (\(70\text{--}140\) keV) and a factor of 2 smaller than in \(^{152}\)Dy (\(30\text{--}70\) keV)
- Number of superdeformed rotational bands in \(^{192}\)Hg amounts up to about 150 at a given rotational frequency, compared to about 30 in normally deformed nuclei
- Onset energy of rotational damping in \(^{192}\)Hg varies from 1.8 to 1.3 MeV with increasing spin, smaller than in \(A\sim 150\) superdeformed nuclei (\(2\text{--}3\) MeV) but larger than in \(^{168}\)Yb
- The dispersion of rotational frequency \(4\Delta\omega_{\rm direct}\) for \(^{192}\)Hg is less than 50 keV at spins \(I \lesssim 40\hbar\), about four times smaller than in \(^{143}\)Eu and \(^{168}\)Yb
- Equilibrium deformation parameters for superdeformed \(^{192}\)Hg are \((\epsilon_2,\epsilon_4) = (0.451, 0.0115)\)

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of rotational damping developed by Lauritzen et al. (1986) and the cranked shell model approach of Aberg (1992) and Matsuo (1997), applying it specifically to superdeformed nuclei in the \(A\sim 190\) region. It contrasts with previous calculations for \(A\sim 150\) superdeformed nuclei (Yoshida, 1997) and normally deformed rare-earth nuclei, revealing that the single-particle alignment structure produces a shell oscillation that dramatically reduces the rotational damping width in \(^{192}\)Hg. The results provide predictions for future fluctuation analysis method (FAM) experiments on superdeformed \(^{192}\)Hg, which has already been studied experimentally by Lauritsen (1992) but without direct extraction of damping parameters.
