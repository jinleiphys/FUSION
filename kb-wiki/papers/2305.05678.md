# What if $ϕ^4$ theory in 4 dimensions is non-trivial in the continuum?

> Traditionally, scalar $ϕ^4$ theory in four dimensions is thought to be quantum trivial in the continuum. This tradition is apparently well grounded both in physics arguments and mathematical proofs. Digging into the proofs one finds that they do not actually cover all physically meaningful situations, in particular the case of multi-component fields and non-polynomial action. In this work, I study multi-component scalar field theories in four dimensions in the continuum and show that they do evade the apparently foregone conclusion of triviality. Instead, one finds a non-trivial interacting theory that has two phases, bound states and non-trivial scattering amplitudes in the limit of many components. This has potentially broad implications, both for the foundations of quantum field theory as well as for the experimentally accessible Higgs sector of the Standard Model.

# What if φ⁴ theory in 4 dimensions is non-trivial in the continuum?

## Key claim

Multi-component scalar φ⁴ theory in four dimensions can evade quantum triviality in the continuum if one allows a negative bare coupling constant and takes the large-N limit, contrary to the traditional consensus that scalar φ⁴ theory is necessarily trivial. The mathematical proofs of triviality do not cover the cases of multi-component fields with N>2 or non-polynomial actions, leaving open the possibility of a non-trivial interacting continuum theory.

## Method

The author studies the O(N) scalar field theory with quartic interaction in the large-N limit, using a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to introduce an auxiliary field and solving the path integral by steepest descent. Renormalization is performed non-perturbatively using a UV cutoff, with renormalization conditions valid at any coupling strength. The connected amputated four-point function is computed at next-to-leading order in the 1/N expansion using the R2 resummation scheme. For the single-component case, the author performs direct numerical integration of the discretized path integral on small lattices (Nσ=2,3, Nτ=1) using a complex contour deformation (cone contour) to handle negative bare coupling.

## Key numbers

- The renormalized running coupling in the continuum limit is λ_R(μ=Q) = (2π)²/ln(Λ_LP²/Q²), where Λ_LP is the Landau pole scale
- The β-function is β = λ_R²(μ)/(2π)², positive for all real-valued couplings
- The s-channel cross-section shows a bound state at energy E ≃ 1.84 M, where M = Λ_LP is the scalar mass scale
- The bound state mass prediction for the Standard Model Higgs sector is M ≃ 1.84 m_H ≃ 230 GeV
- Lattice results for N=1 show Δ ln Z/(V T³) approaching the Stefan-Boltzmann constant for a single bosonic degree of freedom as λ₀ → 0⁻

## Context

This work directly challenges the long-standing consensus on quantum triviality of scalar φ⁴ theory, which dates back to Wilson's renormalization group arguments (Wilson, 1973) and is supported by mathematical proofs for N≤2 (Frohlich, 1982; Aizenman, 2019). The author extends earlier large-N calculations (Moshe, 2003; Romatschke, 2022) and builds on PT-symmetric quantum mechanics (Bender and Böttcher, 1998; Dorey, 2001) to justify negative bare couplings. The results agree with an earlier calculation by Abbott (1975) and suggest that the Standard Model Higgs sector (N=4) may not be covered by triviality proofs, potentially enabling a new class of continuum quantum field theories beyond the effective field theory paradigm.
