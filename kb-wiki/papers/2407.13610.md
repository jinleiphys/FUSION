# Dimuon and ditau production in photon-photon collisions at next-to-leading order in QED

> Next-to-leading-order (NLO) quantum electrodynamics (QED) corrections to the production of muon and tau pairs in photon-photon collisions, $ﾎｳﾎｳ¥toﾎｼ^{+}ﾎｼ^{-},ﾏЭ{+}ﾏЭ{-}$, are calculated in the equivalent photon approximation. We mostly consider $ﾎｳﾎｳ$ processes in ultraperipheral collisions of hadrons at the LHC, but the $ﾎｳﾎｳ¥toﾏЭ{+}ﾏЭ{-}$ process in $¥mathrm{e}^+¥mathrm{e}^-$ collisions at LEP is also discussed. The NLO terms are found to modify the total fiducial cross sections by up to 5%, increasing the tails of the dilepton acoplanarity and transverse momentum distributions, and depleting by up to 15% the yields at high masses, with respect to the leading-order predictions including the very small virtuality of the colliding photons. At the LHC, the calculations obtained with the charge form factor for protons and lead ions including the NLO QED corrections improve the data--theory agreement for all measured differential distributions, and prove an indispensable ingredient for the extraction of precision quantities in photon-photon processes, such as the anomalous magnetic moment of the tau lepton.

## Key claim

The NLO QED corrections modify the total fiducial cross sections for dimuon and ditau production in photon-photon collisions by up to 5%, increase the tails of the acoplanarity and transverse momentum distributions, and deplete the yields at high masses by up to 15% relative to leading-order predictions that include the very small virtuality of the colliding photons; these corrections are an indispensable ingredient for extracting precision quantities such as the anomalous magnetic moment of the tau lepton.

## Method

The matrix elements for the NLO QED corrections to $¥gamma¥gamma¥to¥mu^{+}¥mu^{-},¥tau^{+}¥tau^{-}$ were computed within the MadGraph5_aMC@NLO framework extended with automated NLO electroweak corrections, using the $¥alpha(0)$ renormalization scheme with lepton masses kept nonzero. The equivalent photon approximation (EPA) was employed with the gamma-UPC code (v1.6) to provide the photon fluxes and hadron survival probabilities for ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC, using the charge form factor (ChFF) for protons and lead ions as the default choice. The initial photon virtualities were incorporated through a $k_¥perp$ smearing procedure applied to the Les Houches event files, and the NLO corrections were combined with this smearing in an ad-hoc manner with uncertainties of order 25% in the overlapping region.

## Key numbers

- NLO QED corrections modify total fiducial cross sections by up to 5% for $¥gamma¥gamma¥to¥mu^{+}¥mu^{-},¥tau^{+}¥tau^{-}$ in ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC.
- The yields at high masses are depleted by up to 15% relative to leading-order predictions including the very small photon virtuality.
- The maximum photon virtuality for coherent emission is $Q^2¥lesssim 0.08$ GeV$^2$ for protons ($R_¥mathrm{p}¥approx 0.7$ fm) and $Q^2¥lesssim 8¥times10^{-4}$ GeV$^2$ for Pb nuclei ($R_¥mathrm{Pb}¥approx 7$ fm).
- The lepton pair transverse momentum reaches up to $¥pT^{¥lele}¥approx 0.4$ GeV for p-p UPCs and 0.2 GeV for Pb-Pb UPCs due to the nonzero photon virtualities.
- The muon and tau lepton masses used are $m_¥mu=0.10566$ GeV and $m_¥tau=1.77686$ GeV, with $¥alpha=1/137.036$.
- The ChFF flux cross sections are larger than EDFF flux cross sections by about 10-20% for p-p and p-Pb UPCs and 20-40% for Pb-Pb UPCs.

## Context

This work extends the NLO QED corrections previously calculated for $¥gamma¥gamma¥to t¥bar{t}$ and $¥gamma¥gamma¥to¥gamma¥gamma$ processes using the gamma-UPC code (Shao 2022, AH 2023) to the dimuon and ditau channels, which serve as standard candles for photon-photon fusion and as probes of the tau lepton anomalous magnetic moment. The calculations improve data-theory agreement for all measured differential distributions at the LHC, addressing the increasing deviations observed in the latest Pb-Pb UPC results from the CMS and ATLAS collaborations when compared to leading-order QED Monte Carlo event generators such as Starlight (Klein 2016) and Superchic (Harland-Lang 2020). The results enable more precise extractions of the tau anomalous magnetic moment $a_¥tau$, for which the current best measurement of $a_¥tau = 0.0009_{-0.0031}^{+0.0032}$ from CMS (2024) approaches the sensitivity needed to probe the Schwinger term $a_¥tau^¥text{NLO} = ¥alpha/(2¥pi) ¥approx 0.00116$.
