# Nucleon resonances $N(1875)$ and $N(2100)$ as strange partners of LHCb pentaquarks

> In this work, we investigate the possibility of interpreting two nucleon resonances, the $N(1875)$ and the $N(2100)$, as hadronic molecular states from the $Σ^*K$ and $ΣK^*$ interactions, respectively. With the help of effective Lagrangians in which coupling constants are determined by the SU(3) symmetry, the $Σ^*K$ and $ΣK^*$ interactions are described by the vector-meson and pseudoscalar-meson exchanges. With the one-boson-exchange potential obtained, bound states from the $Σ^*K$ and $ΣK^*$ interactions are searched for in a quasipotential Bethe-Saltpeter equation approach. A bound state with quantum number $I(J^P)=1/2(3/2^-)$ is produced from the $Σ^*K$ interaction, which can be identified as the $N(1875)$ listed in PDG. It can be seen as a strange partner of the LHCb pentaquark $P_c(4380)$ with the same quantum numbers in the molecular state picture. The $ΣK^*$ interaction also produces a bound state with quantum number $I(J^P)=1/2(3/2^-)$, which is related to experimentally observed $N(2100)$ in the $ϕ$ photoproduction. Our results suggest that the $N(2120)$ observed in the $KΛ(1520)$ photoproduction and the $N(2100)$ observed in the $ϕ$ photoproduction have different origins. The former is a conventional three-quark state while the latter is a $ΣK^*$ molecular state, which can be seen as a strange partner of the $P_c(4450)$ with different spin parity.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the nucleon resonances N(1875) and N(2100) can be interpreted as hadronic molecular states from the Sigma*K and Sigma K* interactions respectively, serving as strange partners of the LHCb pentaquarks Pc(4380) and Pc(4450); however, the identification depends on the choice of cutoff parameters in the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter approach, and the N(2120) observed in K-Lambda(1520) photoproduction is argued to have a different (conventional three-quark) origin than the N(2100) observed in phi photoproduction.

## Method

The Sigma*K and Sigma K* interactions were described using one-boson-exchange potentials with vector-meson and pseudoscalar-meson exchanges, where coupling constants were determined by SU(3) symmetry. Bound states were searched for by solving the quasipotential Bethe-Salpeter equation in partial-wave form with fixed spin-parity JP, using an exponential regularization with cutoff Lambda or a direct momentum cutoff pmax. The calculation was performed for one-channel Sigma*K and Sigma K* interactions separately, and then extended to a coupled-channel Sigma*K-Sigma K* calculation.

## Key numbers

- A bound state with I(JP) = 1/2(3/2-) from the Sigma*K interaction was found at energies around 1874-1880 MeV for cutoffs Lambda = 1.5-1.7 GeV, identified as the N(1875) listed in the PDG.
- A bound state with I(JP) = 1/2(3/2-) from the Sigma K* interaction was found at energies around 2076-2086 MeV for cutoffs Lambda = 0.8-1.2 GeV, related to the N(2100) observed in phi photoproduction.
- The N(2100) from phi photoproduction was reported in prior analysis to have a mass of 2.08 +/- 0.04 GeV and JP = 3/2-.
- The cutoff Lambda about 1.6 GeV for the Sigma*K interaction corresponded to a cutoff pmax about 1.2 GeV.
- For the Sigma K* interaction, additional bound states with quantum numbers 1/2(5/2+), 1/2(3/2+), 1/2(5/2-), 3/2(5/2+), and 3/2(1/2+) were found but were less stable with cutoff variation.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecular state interpretation previously applied to the LHCb hidden-charmed pentaquarks Pc(4380) and Pc(4450) from Sigma_c* D and Sigma_c D* interactions (He, 2015; He, 2016) into the light-quark sector. It contradicts the assumption that the N(2120) observed in K-Lambda(1520) photoproduction and the N(2100) observed in phi photoproduction have the same origin, arguing instead that the former is a conventional three-quark state as found in constituent quark model calculations (He, 2012; He, 2014) while the latter is a Sigma K* molecular state. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted isospin 3/2 bound states and further tests of the molecular picture for nucleon resonances near 2 GeV.
