# Kondo effect in charm/bottom nuclei

> We discuss the Kondo effect for isospin-exchange interaction between a $\bar{D}$, $B$ meson and a valence nucleon in charm/bottom atomic nuclei including the discrete energy-levels for valence nucleons. To investigate the binding energy by the Kondo effect, we introduce the mean-field approach for the bound state of the $\bar{D}$, $B$ meson in charm/bottom nuclei. Assuming a simple model, we examine the validity of the mean-field approximation by comparing the results with the exact solutions. We also estimate the effect of the quantum fluctuation beyond the mean-field approximation. We discuss the competition between the Kondo effect and the other correlations in valence nucleon, the isospin symmetry breaking and the nucleon pairings.

## Key claim

The Kondo effect can occur in charm/bottom nuclei via isospin-exchange interaction between a $\bar{D}$ or $B$ meson (isospin 1/2) and a valence nucleon, provided the valence nucleons occupy discrete energy levels in a finite nuclear system; the mean-field approximation provides a useful method for analyzing this effect, though its validity depends on comparison with exact solutions for simplified models.

## Method

The author introduces a Hamiltonian with an isospin-exchange (Kondo) interaction term between a $\bar{D}$ or $B$ meson impurity and valence nucleons occupying discrete single-particle states. Exact energy eigenvalues are obtained for the simplified case where all single-particle states have the same energy, using a variational wave function method and a pseudo-isospin SU(2) algebra. A mean-field approximation is then applied by introducing auxiliary fermion fields to represent the impurity isospin, and the results are compared with the exact solutions; quantum fluctuations beyond mean-field are estimated using the random-phase approximation (RPA).

## Key numbers

- Mass of the lightest charm meson ($\bar{D}$ meson): 1870 MeV, about twice the nucleon mass.
- Mass of the lightest bottom meson ($B$ meson): 5280 MeV, about 5.6 times the nucleon mass.
- For $n=1$ valence nucleon with $N$ single-particle states and coupling $g$, the exact energy eigenvalues are $\epsilon - \frac{3}{2}Ng$ (isospin $I=0$, degeneracy 1) and $\epsilon + \frac{1}{2}Ng$ (isospin $I=1$, degeneracy 1), plus $\epsilon$ with degeneracy $N-1$ for each isospin channel.
- For $n=2$ valence nucleons with $N=2$, the exact energies include $2\epsilon - \frac{3}{2}Ng$ (degeneracy 1) for $I=1/2$ and $2\epsilon + \frac{1}{2}Ng$ (degeneracy 1) for $I=3/2$.
- For $n=3$ valence nucleons with $N=2$, the exact energies include $3\epsilon - \frac{3}{2}Ng$ (degeneracy 1) for $I=0$ and $3\epsilon + \frac{1}{2}Ng$ (degeneracy 1) for $I=1$.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the Kondo effect from condensed matter physics to nuclear systems with heavy flavor, following earlier proposals for the Kondo effect in nuclear matter via isospin-exchange and color-exchange interactions by Yasui (2013) and Hattori (2015). The study specifically addresses finite-volume effects and discrete energy levels of valence nucleons in charm/bottom nuclei, in contrast to the continuous density treatments typical in condensed matter Kondo problems (Takano, 1966; Yoshimori, 1970). The results enable future investigation of competition between the Kondo effect and other nuclear correlations such as isospin symmetry breaking and nucleon pairings.
