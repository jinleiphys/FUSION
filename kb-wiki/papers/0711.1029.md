# Four-quark Bound States in Chiral SU(3) Quark Model

> The possibility of $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ heavy-light four-quark bound states has been analyzed by means of the chiral SU(3) quark model, where $Q$ is the heavy quark ($c$ or $b$) and $q$ is the light quark ($u$, $d$ or $s$). We obtain a bound state for the $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ configuration with quantum number $J^{P}=1^{+},I=0$ and for the $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ ($J^{P}=1^{+},I=0$) configuration which is not bound but slightly above the $D^{*}D^{*}$ threshold (n is $u$ or $d$ quark). Meanwhile, we also conclude that a weakly bound state in $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ system can also be found without considering the chiral quark interactions between the two light quarks, yet its binding energy is weaker than that with the chiral quark interactions.

# Four-quark Bound States in Chiral SU(3) Quark Model

## Key claim

Using the chiral SU(3) quark model, the authors predict a bound four-quark state for the $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ configuration with quantum numbers $J^{P}=1^{+}, I=0$, while the $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ configuration with the same quantum numbers is not bound but lies slightly above the $D^{*}D^{*}$ threshold. A weakly bound state in the $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ system also exists without chiral quark interactions between the two light quarks, but its binding energy is weaker than when those interactions are included.

## Method

The authors solve the Schrödinger equation for $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ four-quark systems using a variational method with trial wave functions expressed as superpositions of harmonic oscillator basis states. The Hamiltonian includes confinement potentials, one-gluon-exchange (OGE) interactions, and for light quark pairs, chiral quark interactions (scalar and pseudoscalar meson exchange). Parameters for heavy-light and heavy-heavy quark pairs are fitted to reproduce experimental masses of $D$, $D^{*}$, $D_{s}$, $D_{s}^{*}$, $\eta_{c}$, $J/\psi$, $h_{c}(1p)$ and their bottom counterparts $B$, $B^{*}$, $B_{s}$, $B_{s}^{*}$, $\eta_{b}$, $\Upsilon(1s)$. The stability of each four-quark state is determined by comparing its total energy to the sum of the masses of the two lowest allowed two-meson decay channels.

## Key numbers

- $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ with $(J^{P};I)=(1^{+};0)$: total energy $E = 10576$ MeV, $\Delta E = -32$ MeV (bound below threshold)
- $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ with $(J^{P};I)=(1^{+};0)$: total energy $E = 4068$ MeV, $\Delta E = +50$ MeV (unbound, above $D^{*}D^{*}$ threshold)
- $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ with $(J^{P};I)=(1^{+};0)$ without chiral quark interactions: total energy $E = 10590$ MeV, $\Delta E = -18$ MeV (bound but weaker than with chiral interactions)
- $cc\bar{n}\bar{n}$ with $(J^{P};I)=(1^{+};0)$ without chiral quark interactions: total energy $E = 4078$ MeV, $\Delta E = +60$ MeV (unbound)
- $bb\bar{s}\bar{s}$ with $(J^{P};I)=(1^{+};0)$: total energy $E = 10945$ MeV, $\Delta E = +216$ MeV (unbound)
- $cc\bar{s}\bar{s}$ with $(J^{P};I)=(1^{+};0)$: total energy $E = 4462$ MeV, $\Delta E = +363$ MeV (unbound)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of heavy-light four-quark states using the MIT bag model by Carlson et al. (1998), which found a bound $bb\bar{u}\bar{d}$ state but no bound $cc\bar{u}\bar{d}$ state, and the pseudoscalar meson exchange approach by Pepin et al. (2001), which predicted binding for both bottom and charm sectors. The present chiral SU(3) quark model calculation supports the Carlson et al. conclusion for the bottom sector while disagreeing with the Pepin et al. result for charm. The finding that a bound $bb\bar{n}\bar{n}$ state persists even without chiral quark interactions, though with weaker binding, clarifies the role of different interaction mechanisms. The results enable future experimental searches for tetraquark states in the bottomonium sector and motivate further theoretical studies of the $QQ\bar{q}\bar{q}$ system with more sophisticated treatments of the light quark dynamics.
