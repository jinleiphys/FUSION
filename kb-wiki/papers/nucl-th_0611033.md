# Extraction and Interpretation of gamma N --> Delta Form Factors within a Dynamical Model

> Within the dynamical model of Refs. [Phys. Rev. C54, 2660 (1996); C63, 055201 (2001)], we perform an analysis of recent data of pion electroproduction reactions at energies near the Delta(1232) resonance. We discuss possible interpretations of the extracted bare and dressed gamma N --> Delta form factors in terms of relativistic constituent quark models and Lattice QCD calculations. Possible future developments are discussed.

## Key claim

The bare $\gamma N \rightarrow \Delta$ form factors extracted from pion electroproduction data using the dynamical Sato-Lee (SL) model can be interpreted in terms of constituent quark degrees of freedom, but only if one assumes that meson cloud effects account for approximately 20% of the nucleon magnetic moment; the extracted bare E2 transition form factor $G_E(0)$ cannot be understood within the non-relativistic constituent quark model.

## Method

The authors refined the Sato-Lee dynamical model (SL2) by reweighting the fit to $\pi N$ scattering phase shifts in the $P_{13}$ partial wave, then used this model to analyze pion electroproduction data at energies near the $\Delta(1232)$ resonance. They performed $\chi^2$ fits to differential cross section data at individual $Q^2$ points (0.06, 0.127, 0.2, 0.9, 1.45 (GeV/c)$^2$) by adjusting the bare $\gamma N \rightarrow \Delta$ form factors $G_M(Q^2)$, $G_E(Q^2)$, and $G_C(Q^2)$, abandoning the previous parameterization that assumed identical $Q^2$ dependence for all three couplings.

## Key numbers

- Bare magnetic dipole form factor at $Q^2=0$: $G_M(0) = 1.85$ (from the SL model fit to photoproduction data)
- Bare electric quadrupole form factor at $Q^2=0$: $G_E(0) = 0.0251$ (SL2 model) or $0.025$ (SL model)
- Bare $G_E(0) = -0.025$ (sign convention used in the text for the E2 transition)
- Dressed $\Delta$ width from the SL model: $\Gamma^{SL}=93$ MeV, compared to the experimental value $\Gamma^{exp}_\Delta = 115$ MeV
- Bare $\Delta$ mass: $m_\Delta = 1296.9$ MeV (SL2 model) or $1299.07$ MeV (SL model)
- $\pi N$ scattering fit parameters include $g_{\rho NN}g_{\rho\pi\pi}=37.133$, $\kappa_\rho=2.8496$, $\Lambda_{\pi NN}=3.6177$ (GeV/c), $\Lambda_{\rho}=7.4639$ (GeV/c), $\Lambda_{\pi N\Delta}=3.2132$ (GeV/c) for the SL2 model
- Data analyzed cover $Q^2 < 2.00$ (GeV/c)$^2$ from JLab, MIT-Bates, and Mainz experiments

## Context

This work extends the dynamical model of Sato and Lee (1996, 2001) by incorporating recent pion electroproduction data from JLab (Joo 2002, Smith 2006), MIT-Bates (Bates collaboration), and Mainz (Leukel 2001, Stave 2006, Sparveris 2006). The extracted bare form factors are compared with relativistic constituent quark model calculations by Capstick and Keister (front form) and Julia-Diaz and Riska (instant form), as well as a Lattice QCD calculation by Alexandrou. The results enable future comparisons with hadron structure calculations that include meson cloud effects, and the authors note that simultaneous fits to $\pi N$ and electroproduction data including polarization measurements (Kelly 2005) would be a worthwhile next step.
