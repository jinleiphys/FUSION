# The pressure distribution and shear forces inside the proton

> The distributions of pressure and shear forces inside the proton are investigated using lattice Quantum Chromodynamics (LQCD) calculations of the energy momentum tensor, allowing the first model-independent determination of these fundamental aspects of proton structure. This is achieved by combining recent LQCD results for the gluon contributions to the energy momentum tensor with earlier calculations of the quark contributions. The utility of LQCD calculations in exploring, and supplementing, the assumptions in a recent extraction of the pressure distribution in the proton from deeply virtual Compton scattering experiments is also discussed. Based on this study, the target kinematics for experiments aiming to determine the pressure and shear distributions with greater precision at Thomas Jefferson National Accelerator Facility and a future Electron Ion Collider are investigated.

# The pressure distribution and shear forces inside the proton

## Key claim
The paper presents the first model-independent determination of the pressure and shear distributions inside the proton using lattice QCD calculations of the energy momentum tensor, but the calculations are performed at heavier-than-physical quark masses corresponding to a pion mass roughly three times the physical value, and at a single lattice spacing and volume.

## Method
The authors combined recent lattice QCD results for the gluon contributions to the energy momentum tensor with earlier calculations of the quark contributions to determine the D-term form factors, from which the pressure and shear distributions are constructed via Fourier transforms. The D-term form factors were parametrized using both tripole forms and modified z-expansions to interpolate and extrapolate the discrete lattice data over a finite range of momentum transfer. The quark calculations used masses corresponding to m_pi ~ 0.5 GeV, while the gluon calculations used masses corresponding to m_pi ~ 0.45 GeV, with both sets of calculations neglecting disconnected contractions and quark-gluon operator mixing.

## Key numbers
- The gluon D-term form factor at the scale mu=2 GeV in the MSbar scheme is approximately twice as large as the isoscalar quark D-term form factor at similar quark masses.
- The mechanical radius of the proton is found to be 0.51(2) fm^2 using the modified z-expansion and 0.57(1) fm^2 using the tripole ansatz.
- The connected isoscalar quark GFF determined from LQCD is approximately 1.7 times smaller in magnitude than the BEG experimental extraction.
- For a determination of the pressure distribution distinct from zero at 2 standard deviations at the maximum of the first peak, experimental data must extend to at least |t| ~ 1.0 GeV^2.
- The LQCD calculations show that the quark and gluon D-term GFFs have significant support up to |t| ~ 2 GeV^2.

## Context
This work extends the first experimental extraction of the proton pressure distribution from deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) by Burkert, Elhouadrhiri and Girod (2018) (referred to as BEG), which relied on assumptions about gluon contributions that the LQCD calculations now test. The results provide support for the BEG assumption that isovector quark contributions vanish (D_u = D_d), but find mild tension with the BEG assumption that D_g = D_q, and demonstrate that the tripole form assumed in BEG introduces significant model-dependence. The study enables future experimental goals by identifying that CLAS12 at JLab and a future Electron Ion Collider must extend kinematic coverage to at least |t| ~ 1 GeV^2 for a model-independent pressure extraction, and that flavor separation through deeply-virtual meson production and DVCS on deuterons will be necessary.
