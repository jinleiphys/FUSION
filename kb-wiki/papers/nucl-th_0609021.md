# Universality of the saturation scale and the initial eccentricity in heavy ion collisions

> Recent estimates that Color Glass Condensate initial conditions may generate a larger initial eccentricity for noncentral relativistic heavy ion collisions (relative to the initial eccentricity assumed in earlier hydrodynamic calculations) have raised the possibility of a higher bound on the viscosity of the Quark Gluon Plasma. We show that this large initial eccentricity results in part from a definition of the saturation scale as proportional to the number of nucleons participating in the collision. A saturation scale proportional to the nuclear thickness function (and therefore independent of the probe) leads to a smaller eccentricity, albeit still larger than the value used in hydrodynamic models. Our results suggest that the early elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions (unlike multiplicity distributions) is sensitive to the universality of the saturation scale in high energy QCD.

# Universality of the saturation scale and the initial eccentricity in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The initial eccentricity in noncentral heavy ion collisions computed from Color Glass Condensate initial conditions is sensitive to whether the saturation scale is defined as proportional to the nuclear thickness function (universal) or to the number of participating nucleons (non-universal); the universal definition yields a smaller eccentricity than the participant-based definition, though both are larger than the values traditionally used in hydrodynamic models.

## Method

The authors compare two prescriptions for the transverse coordinate dependence of the saturation scale Qs: a universal form where Qs^2 is proportional to the nuclear thickness function T_A of each nucleus independently, and a non-universal form where Qs^2 is proportional to the number of participating nucleons N_part. They compute the initial eccentricity using the Classical Yang-Mills (CYM) numerical solution of the Glasma fields and compare with results from the k_T-factorized KLN approach, analyzing the parametric behavior of gluon multiplicity and transverse energy in regions where the two saturation scales differ significantly.

## Key numbers

- The CYM eccentricity is computed at tau = 0.25 fm after the collision.
- The CYM calculation uses g^2 mu = 1.6 GeV, corresponding to an estimated gluon multiplicity of approximately 1000 in central Au-Au collisions at RHIC.
- The infrared cutoff m = 0.5 GeV is used to regulate Coulomb tails of the gluon field; changing m by a factor of 10 changes the best estimate for g^2 mu by about 20 percent.
- The universal Qs^2 ~ T_A prescription reproduces the observed centrality dependence of the charged particle multiplicity per participant pair measured by PHOBOS.

## Context

This work extends earlier CGC calculations of initial conditions for heavy ion collisions, particularly the CYM numerical studies by Krasnitz et al. (2002) and Lappi (2003-2006) and the KLN approach of Kharzeev et al. (2001-2004). It directly addresses the recent estimates by Hirano (2005) and Drescher (2006) that CGC initial conditions generate larger eccentricities than traditional hydrodynamic assumptions, which had raised the possibility of a higher bound on the viscosity of the Quark Gluon Plasma. The results suggest that early elliptic flow is sensitive to the universality of the saturation scale, unlike multiplicity distributions, and that a dynamical understanding of how eccentricity decreases from the initial Glasma stage to the thermalized stage is needed before firm viscosity bounds can be established.
