# NJL-Chiral Soliton and the Nucleon Equation of State at supra-saturation density: Impact of Chiral Symmetry Restoration

> It has been conjectured that, at sufficiently high baryon densities, the equation of state (EoS) of bulk nuclear matter can be identified with that of the nucleon core. In this work, we illustrate how the energy density and pressure distributions inside individual nucleons can be utilized to construct the EoS of supra-dense matter. In our framework, nucleons arise as topological solitons stabilized by vector mesons, which are dynamically generated through the path integral bosonization of an underlying Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The restoration of chiral symmetry is implemented dynamically via a self-consistent, density-dependent scalar field, which modifies the (isovector) and (isoscalar) channels of the soliton. We analyze the resulting changes in soliton properties for different NJL parameter sets and demonstrate that the progressive restoration of chiral symmetry leads to a stiffening of the soliton-based EoS, making it compatible with existing neutron star EoSs. An EoS constructed from the solutions of the energy-density and pressure profiles at the center of the nucleon is also explored.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the equation of state (EoS) of supra-dense nuclear matter can be constructed from the energy density and pressure distributions inside individual nucleons, where nucleons are described as topological solitons stabilized by vector mesons within a bosonized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The key advance over prior work (Fukushima 2020) is that chiral symmetry restoration is implemented dynamically via a self-consistent, density-dependent scalar field, rather than through a qualitative rescaling argument, and this progressive restoration leads to a stiffening of the soliton-based EoS that makes it compatible with existing neutron star EoS constraints.

## Method

The authors construct nucleons as topological solitons (Skyrmions) from a bosonized NJL model that includes pion, rho meson, and omega meson fields, using a hedgehog ansatz for the pion field and Wu-Yang-'t Hooft-Polyakov ansatz for the rho meson. The coupled second-order differential equations for the meson profiles (chiral angle F(r), rho profile G(r), and omega profile ω(r)) are solved numerically with boundary conditions F(0)=π, F(∞)=0, G(0)=-2, G(∞)=0, ω'(0)=0, ω(∞)=0, and the soliton energy density and pressure are computed from the solutions. In-medium effects are incorporated through a density-dependent scalar field S that modifies all meson parameters (pion decay constant, pion mass, vector meson mass, and coupling constants) according to NJL-model relations, with the scalar field value determined self-consistently from the nuclear density.

## Key numbers

- The vacuum constituent quark mass M₀ is approximately 350 MeV, obtained as the solution of the gap equation.
- Nuclear saturation density ρ_sat is approximately 0.16 fm⁻³.
- The r.m.s. charge radius of the soliton may increase by up to 17% near nuclear saturation density (Christov 1990).
- A phase transition from the chirally broken Goldstone phase to the chiral restored phase is predicted near a baryon density of approximately 3 ρ_sat (Christov 1990).
- An equilibrium between nuclear and quark phases could exist around approximately 6 ρ_sat, with a complete quark plasma phase expected at approximately 12 ρ_sat (Birse 1988).
- The hard core overlap regime, where the nucleon EoS applies, occurs near approximately 8 ρ_sat (Fukushima 2020).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Fukushima (2020), who derived a dense quark matter EoS from the nucleon core described as a skyrmion of the pion field under vacuum conditions, but only qualitatively accounted for chiral symmetry restoration via rescaling with the in-medium pion decay constant. The present study provides a self-consistent treatment of chiral symmetry restoration through a density-dependent scalar field within the NJL bosonization framework developed by Chanfray and collaborators (Chanfray 2010, Chanfray 2025), enabling a systematic connection between NJL model parameters and both vacuum and in-medium nucleon properties. This framework enables future work to incorporate explicit confining mechanisms (such as string tension) and to study the transition from hadronic to quark matter in neutron star interiors, building on earlier studies of in-medium soliton modifications by Christov (1990), Alkofer (1991), and Amore (2000).
