# Shock Waves in Relativistic Anisotropic Hydrodynamics

> Shock wave solutions in anisotropic relativistic hydrodynamics are considered. A new phenomenon of anisotropy-related angular deflection of the incident flow by the shock wave front is described. Patterns of velocity and momentum transformation by the shock wave front are described.

## Key claim

Shock wave solutions in relativistic anisotropic hydrodynamics exhibit a new phenomenon: the incident flow is deflected by the shock wave front at an angle that depends on the anisotropy parameter, and for sufficiently large anisotropies the relative velocity drop can change sign, turning a rarefaction shock wave into a compression shock wave that accelerates the flow.

## Method

The authors derive matching conditions for the energy-momentum tensor across a discontinuity surface in anisotropic relativistic hydrodynamics, using the kinetic-theory-based ansatz of Martinez and Strickland (2010) with a one-dimensional anisotropy parameter ξ. They solve the resulting system of equations analytically for transverse and longitudinal normal shock waves, and numerically for shock waves incident at an arbitrary polar angle, obtaining the upstream and downstream flow velocities and the deflection angle as functions of the anisotropy parameter ξ and the pressure ratio σ = P'iso/Piso.

## Key numbers

- For a transverse normal shock wave, the product of upstream and downstream velocities equals the transverse speed of sound squared: ρ⊥(ξ) = R⊥/(3R), which grows from 1/√3 at ξ=0 to 1/√2 as ξ→∞.
- For a longitudinal normal shock wave, the product of upstream and downstream velocities equals the longitudinal speed of sound squared: ρ∥(ξ) = R∥/(3R), which decays from 1/√3 at ξ=0 to 0 as ξ→∞.
- The relative velocity drop for a transverse shock wave at ξ→∞ approaches δ⊥ → -(σ-1)/(2σ+1).
- The relative velocity drop for a longitudinal shock wave at ξ→∞ approaches δ∥ → -(σ-1)/σ.
- The gap between transverse and longitudinal relative velocity drops at ξ→∞ approaches (σ-1)²/[σ(2σ+1)].
- The critical incidence angle separating different velocity transformation regimes is αcrit ∼ π/4.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of sound propagation and Mach cone formation in anisotropic relativistic hydrodynamics by Kirakosyan et al. (2018) to the case of shock waves. It generalizes the classic treatment of relativistic shock waves from Landau and Lifshitz (2013), Israel (1960), and Mitchell (2014) to anisotropic media relevant for the glasma phase in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The results enable rethinking of transverse shock wave generation in the primordial turbulent gluon/minijet medium studied by Gyulassy et al. (1996) in terms of anisotropic hydrodynamics, and provide a foundation for studying shock wave effects on transverse momentum and rapidity spectra in heavy ion collisions.
