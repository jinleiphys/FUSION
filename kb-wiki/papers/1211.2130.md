# EPS09s and EKS98s: Impact parameter dependent nPDF sets

> In our recent study we have determined two new spatially dependent nuclear PDF (nPDF) sets, EPS09s and EKS98s. With these, the hard-process cross-sections can be calculated in different centrality classes consistently with the globally analyzed nPDFs for the first time. The sets were determined by exploiting the $A$-systematics of the globally fitted nPDF sets, EPS09 and EKS98. For the spatial dependence of the nPDFs we used a power series ansatz in the nuclear thickness function $T_A$. In this flash talk we introduce the framework, and present our NLO EPS09s-based predictions for the nuclear modification factor in four centrality classes for inclusive neutral pion production in p+Pb collisions at the LHC and for inclusive prompt photon production in d+Au collisions at RHIC at midrapidity.

## Key claim
The paper presents two new spatially dependent nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) sets, EPS09s and EKS98s, which for the first time allow hard-process cross-sections to be calculated in different centrality classes consistently with globally analyzed nPDFs. The spatial dependence is derived from the A-systematics of the earlier global fits, not from direct centrality data, so the results are predictions contingent on the assumed power-series ansatz in the nuclear thickness function.

## Method
The authors introduce a spatially dependent nuclear modification factor r_i^A(x,Q^2,s) and require its spatial average over the nuclear thickness function T_A(s) to equal the globally fitted modification R_i^A(x,Q^2) from EPS09 (Eskola 2009) or EKS98 (Eskola 1998). They parametrize r_i^A as a power series in T_A(s) with A-independent coefficients, then determine those coefficients by minimizing a chi-squared function that reproduces the A-dependence of the global fits across all parton flavors and an (x,Q^2) grid. The procedure is applied to EKS98 LO, EPS09 LO, and EPS09 NLO plus 30 error sets, yielding the new sets EPS09s and EKS98s.

## Key numbers
- The power series in the nuclear thickness function uses n=4 terms, which is sufficient to reproduce the A-systematics accurately for all sets considered.
- Predictions are shown for neutral pion production in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.0 TeV and y=0 at the LHC, for four centrality classes: 0-20%, 20-40%, 40-60%, and 60-80%.
- Predictions are shown for prompt photon production in d+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV and y=0 at RHIC, for the same four centrality classes.
- The EPS09 initial scale for the gluon modification plots is Q^2=1.69 GeV^2.
- For prompt photons at p_T ~ 5 GeV/c, antishadowing compensates for isospin suppression; at p_T ~ 15-20 GeV/c, isospin suppression dominates and centrality dependence is minimal.

## Context
This work extends the global nPDF analyses of Eskola (2009) for EPS09 and Eskola (1998) for EKS98, which previously only provided spatially averaged modifications. The new spatially dependent sets enable consistent calculations of nuclear modification factors in centrality classes, as demonstrated by comparisons with PHENIX data for pi0 production in d+Au collisions (Adler 2006) and with ALICE data for charged hadron production in p+Pb collisions (ALICE 2012). The framework enables future studies of centrality-dependent hard processes at RHIC and the LHC, and the sets are publicly available.
