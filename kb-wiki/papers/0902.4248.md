# Eta-Mesic Nucleus: A New Form of Nuclear Matter

> Formation of eta-mesic nucleus, a bound state of an eta meson in a nucleus, is reviewed in this paper. Three different theoretical approaches are used to calculate the binding energies and widths of such nuclei. The effect of eta-mesic nucleus in pion double-charge-exchange reaction is discussed. Experimental efforts by different groups to detect the nucleus are also discussed. The ramifications of the theoretical and experimental studies of the bound state of eta in a nucleus are pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper reviews the theoretical prediction and experimental search for eta-mesic nuclei, a bound state of an eta meson in a nucleus, but notes that the attraction is not strong enough to bind an eta to a single nucleon and that bound-state formation is only possible in finite nuclei with mass number greater than 10.

## Method

Three theoretical approaches were used to calculate binding energies and widths of eta-mesic nuclei by solving a momentum-space relativistic three-dimensional integral equation for the eta-nucleus system: a full off-shell covariant optical potential, a factorization approximation with an energy shift parameter, and an on-shell optical potential using the s-wave eta-nucleon scattering length. The calculations used eta-nucleon interaction parameters from a coupled-channel analysis of pion-nucleon reactions, and nuclear wave functions derived from experimental form factors.

## Key numbers

- For the full off-shell calculation, the 1s binding energy and half-width for eta in 12C is -(1.19 + 3.67i) MeV; for 208Pb, the 1s state is -(18.46 + 10.11i) MeV
- The s-wave eta-nucleon scattering length used is a_etaN = (0.28 + 0.19i) fm, corresponding to an attractive interaction
- The eta-nucleon interaction coupling constant g_etaNN* is 0.77
- For the factorization approximation with energy shift Delta = 30 MeV, the 1s binding energy and half-width for 12C is -(1.10 + 4.10i) MeV; for 208Pb it is -(18.96 + 10.22i) MeV
- For the on-shell optical potential with a_etaN = (0.28 + 0.19i) fm, the 1s binding energy and half-width for 12C is -(2.91 + 10.22i) MeV; with a_etaN = (0.51 + 0.21i) fm it is -(19.61 + 14.20i) MeV
- The calculated widths of eta-mesic nuclei vary from 10 to 13 MeV in lighter nuclei and are between 15 and 20 MeV for heavier ones
- About 95% of the width is due to decay with emission of one pion and one nucleon; the remaining 5% is due to emission of two pions
- No bound state solutions were found for nuclei with mass number A less than or equal to 10
- The binding energy of eta in 15O increases with the coupling constant g_etaNN* for values up to 0.90, then decreases and ceases to exist when it becomes greater than 1.0

## Context

This work extends the authors' original 1986 prediction of eta-mesic nuclei (Haider and Liu, 1986) and builds on the coupled-channel analysis of Bhalerao and Liu that found attractive near-threshold eta-nucleon interaction. The paper reviews experimental efforts including those at Brookhaven National Laboratory (1987), LAMPF by Johnson et al., and more recent COSY-GEM Collaboration experiments that claim detection of eta bound in 25Mg. The existence of eta-mesic nuclei with excitation energies of about 540 MeV (compared to about 200 MeV for hypernuclei) enables study of nuclear structure far from equilibrium and can yield information on the eta-NN* coupling constant involving bound nucleons.
