# Anomaly in conformal quantum mechanics: From molecular physics to black holes

> A number of physical systems exhibit a particular form of asymptotic conformal invariance: within a particular range of distances, they are characterized by a long-range conformal interaction (inverse square potential), the absence of dimensional scales, and an SO(2,1) symmetry algebra. Examples from molecular physics to black holes are provided and discussed within a unified treatment. When such systems are physically realized in the appropriate strong-coupling regime,the occurrence of quantum symmetry breaking is possible. This anomaly is revealed by the failure of the symmetry generators to close the algebra in a manner shown to be independent of the renormalization procedure.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that a conformal anomaly, a quantum breaking of classical SO(2,1) conformal symmetry, occurs in systems described by an inverse-square potential in the strong-coupling regime, and this anomaly is independent of the specific regularization or renormalization procedure used to handle the short-distance singularity.

## Method
The authors analyze the Hamiltonian H = p²/(2m) − g/r² in d spatial dimensions, focusing on the strong-coupling regime where the dimensionless coupling λ = 2mg/ħ² exceeds the critical value λ^(*) = (l+ν)² (with ν = (d−2)/2). They regularize the singular potential by introducing a generic real-space core interaction for r < a, then compute the expectation value of the dilation operator commutator [D,H] using the regularized wave functions. The anomaly is extracted from the time derivative of the dilation expectation value ⟨D⟩, and its value is shown to be independent of the details of the ultraviolet regulator.

## Key numbers
- The critical dimensionless coupling for the isotropic inverse-square potential is λ^(*) = (l+ν)², where ν = (d−2)/2 and l is the angular momentum quantum number.
- For dipole-bound anions, the critical dipole moment is p^(*) ≈ 1.625 D (debye), corresponding to a dimensionless coupling λ^(*) ≈ 1.279.
- For the Efimov effect in three-dimensional space (d=3) with identical bosons, the effective conformal parameter is Θ_eff ≈ 1.006, obtained from the transcendental equation 8 sinh(πΘ_eff/6) = √3 Θ_eff cosh(πΘ_eff/2).
- The near-horizon black hole analysis yields an effective conformal parameter Θ_eff = ω/|f'(r₊)| for the nonextremal Reissner-Nordström geometry, where ω is the frequency and f'(r₊) is the derivative of the metric function at the outer horizon.

## Context
This work unifies several previously separate physical systems, dipole-bound anions (molecular physics), the Efimov effect (three-body nuclear physics), and near-horizon black hole physics, under the common framework of conformal quantum mechanics with an inverse-square potential. It extends the earlier recognition of conformal anomalies in two-dimensional contact interactions (Jackiw, 1991) to higher-dimensional systems with long-range conformal interactions, and provides a systematic regularization and renormalization treatment that shows the anomaly is independent of ultraviolet details. The results enable a unified understanding of bound-state formation via renormalization in these diverse systems and provide analytical predictions for critical coupling values and bound-state energies.
