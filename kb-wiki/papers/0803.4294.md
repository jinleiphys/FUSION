# Glauber Monte-Carlo predictions for LHC

> In the framework of various Glauber-like models we compute several correlation observables in nuclear collisions at the SPS, RHIC, and LHC energies. We analyze fluctuations of the eccentricity of the fireball created in the collision, in particular the {variable-axes} harmonic moments $ε^\ast$, as well as the fluctuations of multiplicity of charged particles. We find moderate model dependence of the scaled standard deviation $σ(ε^\ast)/ε^\ast$ on the choice of the particular Glauber model. For all considered models the values of $σ(ε^\ast)/ε^\ast$ range from $\sim$~0.5 for central collisions to $\sim$~0.3-0.4 for peripheral collisions. The results are confronted to the recent measurement of the elliptic-flow fluctuations at RHIC. We also find that the dependence of multiplicity fluctuations on the centrality of the collision is too weak to explain the measurements at the SPS energies. The magnitude of the Glauber multiplicity fluctuations increases by about 20% from the RHIC to LHC energies.

## Key claim

The Glauber Monte-Carlo models predict that the scaled standard deviation of eccentricity fluctuations, \(\sigma(\varepsilon^\ast)/\varepsilon^\ast\), ranges from about 0.5 for central collisions to about 0.3–0.4 for peripheral collisions, with only moderate model dependence; however, the Glauber-model predictions for multiplicity fluctuations are too weak to explain the non-monotonic centrality dependence observed by NA49 at SPS energies, indicating that dynamical effects beyond the initial-state geometry are required.

## Method

The authors used the GLISSANDO computer program to simulate four variants of Glauber-like Monte-Carlo models: the standard wounded-nucleon model, a mixed model with an admixture of binary collisions, a hot-spot model with rare semi-hard collisions, and a hot-spot+\(\Gamma\) model that superimposes a gamma distribution over the source weights. They computed event-by-event fluctuations of the variable-axes eccentricity \(\varepsilon^\ast\) and of charged-particle multiplicity for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV), Au+Au at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV), and Pb+Pb at LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.5\) TeV), using wounding cross sections of 32 mb, 42 mb, and 65 mb respectively.

## Key numbers

* Scaled standard deviation of eccentricity fluctuations \(\sigma(\varepsilon^\ast)/\varepsilon^\ast\) ranges from \(\sim 0.5\) for central collisions to \(\sim 0.3\)–\(0.4\) for peripheral collisions, across all considered Glauber models.
* For central collisions at impact parameter \(b=0\), the central-limit-theorem result gives \(\sigma(v_2^\ast)/v_2^\ast \simeq \sigma(\varepsilon^\ast)/\varepsilon^\ast = \sqrt{4/\pi-1} \simeq 0.52\).
* The mixing parameter \(\alpha\) for the mixed model is 0.145 at RHIC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV), 0.12 at SPS (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=17.3\) GeV), and an educated guess of 0.2 for LHC (\(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.5\) TeV).
* The hot-spot model uses a semi-hard cross section \(\sigma_{\rm hot-spot} = 0.5\) mb for all energies.
* The magnitude of Glauber multiplicity fluctuations increases by about 20% from RHIC to LHC energies, due to the larger wounding cross section at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber-model analyses of Broniowski et al. (2007) and Broniowski et al. (2007) to LHC energies, and confronts the predictions with elliptic-flow fluctuation measurements from PHOBOS (Alver et al., 2006; Alver et al., 2007) and STAR (Sorensen et al., 2006) at RHIC, as well as multiplicity fluctuation data from NA49 (Alt et al., 2006) at SPS. The results show that initial-geometry fluctuations from Glauber models are sufficient to explain the observed elliptic-flow fluctuations, but cannot account for the non-monotonic centrality dependence of multiplicity fluctuations, which requires dynamical effects such as those discussed by Baym and collaborators (1995) and Rybczynski and collaborators (2004). The predictions provide a baseline for future LHC measurements to discriminate between different initial-state models.
