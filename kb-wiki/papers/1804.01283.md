# Fine structure of the cross sections of $e^{+}e^{-}$ annihilation near the thresholds of $p\bar{p}$ and $n\bar{n}$ production

> The energy dependence of the cross sections of $p\bar p$, $n\bar n$, and meson production in $e^+e^-$ annihilation in the vicinity of the $p\bar p$ and $n\bar n$ thresholds is studied. The proton-neutron mass difference and the $p\bar p$ Coulomb interaction are taken into account. The values of the cross sections are very sensitive to the parameters of the optical potential. It is shown that the commonly accepted factorization approach for the account of the Coulomb interaction does not work well enough in the vicinity of the threshold due to the finite size of the optical potential well.

## Key claim
The commonly accepted factorization approach using the Sommerfeld-Gamow-Sakharov factor to account for the Coulomb interaction in \(e^{+}e^{-}\to p\bar{p}\) does not work well enough near threshold due to the finite size of the optical potential well, and the cross sections are very sensitive to the parameters of the optical potential.

## Method
The authors solve the coupled-channels radial Schrodinger equation for the \(^{3}S_{1}-^{3}D_{1}\) states of the \(p\bar{p}\) and \(n\bar{n}\) system, using a matrix optical potential that includes the strong interaction (with real and imaginary parts from potential wells and pion exchange), the Coulomb potential for \(p\bar{p}\), and the proton-neutron mass difference \(\Delta = m_n - m_p\). The wave functions at the origin are used to construct the Sachs form factors and cross sections for elastic \(p\bar{p}\) and \(n\bar{n}\) production, as well as the inelastic (meson production) and total cross sections via the Green's function formalism. The optical potential parameters are refitted to experimental data on nucleon-antinucleon scattering, \(e^{+}e^{-}\) annihilation cross sections, the proton electromagnetic form factor ratio, and \(J/\psi\) decay spectra.

## Key numbers
- The influence of the Coulomb interaction on the \(e^{+}e^{-}\to p\bar{p}\) cross section is noticeable only in the energy region of about 2 MeV above the threshold.
- The very narrow region below the \(p\bar{p}\) production threshold where the energy dependence is not smooth due to Coulomb bound states is approximately \(-15\ \text{keV} < E < 0\).
- The fitted short-range potential parameters include: \(U_S^0 = -458_{-12}^{+10}\ \text{MeV}\), \(W_S^0 = 247 \pm 5\ \text{MeV}\), \(a_S^0 = 0.531_{-0.006}^{+0.007}\ \text{fm}\); \(U_D^0 = -184_{-20}^{+17}\ \text{MeV}\), \(W_D^0 = 82_{-7}^{+13}\ \text{MeV}\), \(a_D^0 = 1.17_{-0.03}^{+0.02}\ \text{fm}\); \(U_T^0 = -43_{-3}^{+4}\ \text{MeV}\), \(W_T^0 = -31_{-6}^{+2}\ \text{MeV}\), \(a_T^0 = 0.74 \pm 0.03\ \text{fm}\); \(U_S^1 = 1.9 \pm 0.6\ \text{MeV}\), \(W_S^1 = -8.9_{-0.5}^{+0.8}\ \text{MeV}\), \(a_S^1 = 1.88 \pm 0.02\ \text{fm}\); \(U_D^1 = 991_{-15}^{+13}\ \text{MeV}\), \(W_D^1 = 5_{-20}^{+14}\ \text{MeV}\), \(a_D^1 = 0.479 \pm 0.003\ \text{fm}\); \(U_T^1 = -4.5_{-0.1}^{+0.2}\ \text{MeV}\), \(W_T^1 = 1.7_{-0.1}^{+0.2}\ \text{MeV}\), \(a_T^1 = 2.22 \pm 0.03\ \text{fm}\).
- The fitted production amplitudes are: \(g_p = 0.338 \pm 0.004\) and \(g_n = -0.15 - 0.33i \pm 0.01\).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous calculations (Dmitriev et al., 2007; Dmitriev et al., 2016) that neglected the Coulomb interaction and proton-neutron mass difference, by incorporating these isospin-violating effects. It addresses the need for detailed theoretical predictions in the energy region between the \(p\bar{p}\) and \(n\bar{n}\) thresholds, where the CMD-3 detector at VEPP-2000 is collecting data (Sol, 2017). The finding that the factorization approach fails due to the finite size of the optical potential well is consistent with a similar conclusion by Voloshin (2018) regarding meson yield ratios in \(\psi(3770)\) and \(\Upsilon(4S)\) decays. The results enable refinement of optical potential parameters through comparison with high-resolution experimental data near threshold.
