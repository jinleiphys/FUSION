# QCD Hidden-Color Hexa-diquark in the Central Core of Nuclei

> Hidden-color configurations are a key prediction of QCD with important physical consequences. In this work we examine a QCD color-singlet configuration in nuclei formed by combining six scalar $[u d]$ diquarks in a strongly bound $\rm SU(3)_C$ channel. The resulting hexadiquark state is a charge-2, spin-0, baryon number-4, isospin-0, color-singlet state. It contributes to alpha clustering in light nuclei and to the additional binding energy not saturated by ordinary nuclear forces in \he as well as the alpha-nuclei sequence of interest for nuclear astrophysics. We show that the strongly bound combination of six scalar isospin-0 $[ud]$ diquarks within the nuclear wave function - relative to free nucleons - provides a natural explanation of the EMC effect measured by the CLAS collaboration's comparison of nuclear parton distribution function ratios for a large range of nuclei. These experiments confirmed that the EMC effect; i.e., the distortion of quark distributions within nuclei, is dominantly identified with the dynamics of neutron-proton (``isophobic'') short-range correlations within the nuclear wave function rather than proton-proton or neutron-neutron correlations.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a hidden-color hexadiquark state, a strongly bound QCD color-singlet configuration of six scalar [ud] diquarks with the same quantum numbers as an alpha particle, exists within nuclei with A ≥ 4, and that this state provides a natural explanation for the EMC effect's observed isospin dependence, specifically the dominance of neutron-proton short-range correlations over proton-proton or neutron-neutron correlations.

## Method

The authors construct the hexadiquark wave function in a three-step SU(3)_C group-theoretic procedure: first, two quarks form a scalar [ud] diquark in the antisymmetric color **3̅_C** representation; second, two such diquarks combine into a duo-diquark in the symmetric **6̅_C** representation; third, three duo-diquarks bind into a color-singlet hexadiquark. They then argue that this hidden-color Fock state mixes with the conventional four-nucleon bound state in the QCD eigenstate of ⁴He and heavier alpha-clustered nuclei, and that deep inelastic scattering on quarks within the hexadiquark's diquarks produces the observed isophobic pattern of the EMC effect.

## Key numbers

- The hexadiquark has baryon number B=4, charge Q=2, spin J^P=0^+, and isospin I=0, matching the quantum numbers of ⁴He.
- The color factor for the hexadiquark formation channel **6̅_C** ⊗ **6̅_C** ⊗ **6̅_C** → **1** is C_F = -5, compared with C_F = -2 for the nucleon in its color-singlet representation.
- The color factor for two diquarks forming a duo-diquark in the **6̅_C** channel is C_F = +1/3 (short-range repulsion), while for two duo-diquarks in the **6̅_C** ⊗ **6̅_C** → **6_C** channel it is C_F = -5/3 (short-range attraction).
- The binding energy of ⁴He is approximately 7 MeV per nucleon, and the maximum contribution of the hexadiquark to this binding approaches the full 7 MeV per nucleon.
- Neutron-proton correlated pairs are reported to be as much as 20 times as prevalent as proton-proton and neutron-neutron pairs, based on prior work (Subedi 2008).

## Context

This work extends the hidden-color concept first applied to the deuteron (1983) and develops a QCD-based mechanism for the EMC effect that contrasts with mean-field nuclear explanations. It builds on the CLAS collaboration's finding (Schmookler 2019) that the EMC effect is dominantly associated with neutron-proton short-range correlations, and on prior observations of isophobic SRCs from Brookhaven AGS data (Piasetzky 2006). The hexadiquark model offers a quark-level explanation for alpha clustering in light nuclei and for the additional binding energy in the alpha-nuclei sequence (⁴He, ⁸Be, ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ...) beyond what standard nuclear forces saturate, and it predicts that the MARATHON experiment at Jefferson Lab on A=3 nuclei should not exhibit strong isophobic SRCs since the hexadiquark cannot form there.
