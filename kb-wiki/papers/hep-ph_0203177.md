# Transport coefficients, spectral functions and the lattice

> Transport coefficients are determined by the slope of spectral functions of composite operators at zero frequency. We study the spectral function relevant for the shear viscosity for arbitrary frequencies in weakly-coupled scalar and nonabelian gauge theories at high temperature and compute the corresponding correlator in euclidean time. We discuss whether nonperturbative values of transport coefficients can be extracted from euclidean lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that extracting transport coefficients such as shear viscosity from Euclidean lattice simulations is possible in principle, but the commonly used ansatz for the spectral function is inadequate; the authors propose a better ansatz based on a one-loop calculation that includes both high-frequency decay processes and low-frequency pinch-singularity contributions.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop spectral function for the shear viscosity in weakly-coupled scalar φ⁴ theory and SU(Nc) nonabelian gauge theory at high temperature, using Kubo formulas and the relation between spectral functions and Euclidean correlators via an integral equation with a thermal kernel. For the low-frequency region where pinch singularities appear, they replace on-shell one-particle spectral functions with Breit-Wigner forms containing a finite thermal width, and they evaluate the resulting integrals analytically and numerically. In the gauge theory case, they incorporate hard thermal loop (HTL) resummation for the gluon propagators and separate contributions from quasiparticle poles and Landau damping.

## Key numbers

- In scalar theory, the one-loop shear viscosity from the low-frequency region is η₁₋ₗₒₒₚ = (2ζ(5)/π²) T⁴/γ, where γ = λ²T/(1536π) is the damping rate parameter.
- The high-frequency contribution to the spectral function in scalar theory is ρ_{ππ}(ω) = θ(ω-2m) (ω²-4m²)^{5/2}/(48πω) [n(ω/2)+1/2], with threshold at ω = 2m.
- The Euclidean correlator at the central point τT = 1/2 receives equal contributions from low- and high-frequency regions: G^{E,high}_{ππ}(1/2T) = (4π²/45)T⁵ [1 - (25/(8π²)) m²/T² + ...] and G^{E,low}_{ππ}(1/2T) ≈ (4π²/45)T⁵.
- In the gauge theory, the pole-pole contribution for transverse gluons at large ω is ρ^{pp}_{ππ}(ω) = (d_A/(4π)) ω⁴ [n(ω/2)+1/2] for ω ≫ ω_pl, where d_A = N_c²-1.
- The pole-cut contribution for soft frequencies ω ∼ gT scales as ρ^{pc}_{ππ}(ω ∼ gT) ∼ g²T⁴, while for hard frequencies ω ∼ T it scales as g²T⁴ ln(1/g).

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculation of transport coefficients using Kubo formulas, following the diagrammatic analysis of Jeon (1995) for scalar theory and the kinetic theory approach of Arnold, Moore, and Yaffe (2000) for gauge theories. The authors directly address the lattice extraction method proposed by Karsch (1986) and later pursued by Nakamura (1996, 1997), showing that the ansatz used in those papers is inadequate because it does not capture the constant τ-independent contribution from low-frequency pinch singularities. The results enable improved ansatze for extracting shear viscosity from Euclidean lattice data using the Maximum Entropy Method or fitting procedures.
