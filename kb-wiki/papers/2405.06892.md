# Hadronic energy-momentum tensor revisited

> Everything gravitates and they do so through the energy-momentum tensor (EMT). However, there is no consensus on how to define the EMT of a hadron, e.g. the proton, the fundamental building blocks of the visible world. In this work, we show that the hadronic EMT can be cast into the form of relativistic continua with spin. The fluid-like form allows a clear identification of the proper hadronic energy density, pressure and shear. A spin tensor contribution is also identified without the reference to the total angular momentum operator, in alignment with the recent development in relativistic spin hydrodynamics. The hadronic EMT is related to the quantum expectation value of the EMT operator through the convolution with the hadronic wavepacket. To factorize out the hadronic EMT, different multipole expansion schemes are explored. We show that the popular Breit-frame densities and light-front densities emerge as the monopole momentum densities from different factorization schemes.

# Hadronic energy-momentum tensor revisited

## Key claim

The hadronic energy-momentum tensor can be cast into the form of relativistic continua with spin, allowing unambiguous identification of proper hadronic energy density, pressure, shear, and a spin tensor contribution; however, this identification requires a multipole expansion to factorize the hadronic EMT from the quantum expectation value, and the popular Breit-frame and light-front densities emerge only as the monopole momentum densities from different factorization schemes, not as unique physical densities.

## Method

The authors start from the quantum expectation value of the Belinfante-Rosenfeld EMT operator for a hadronic eigenstate of the QCD Hamiltonian, expressed as a convolution with the hadronic wavepacket. Using the covariant decomposition of hadronic matrix elements in terms of gravitational form factors A(q²), J(q²), and D(q²), they derive explicit expressions for the hadronic energy density, pressure, spin tensor, and shear tensor in a fluid-like form. To extract these intrinsic densities from the wavepacket-convolved expectation value, they perform multipole expansions in the convective derivative, recovering the Breit-frame densities as the monopole term in the rest-frame expansion and the light-front densities as the monopole term in the infinite-momentum-frame expansion.

## Key numbers

- The proton Compton wavelength λ_Comp = M_p⁻¹ ≈ 0.2 fm is comparable to the proton matter radius r_p ≈ 0.5 fm, motivating the wave-like treatment of the proton under sub-femtometer probes.
- The forward limits of the gravitational form factors are A(0) = 1 and J(0) = 1/2, normalizing the energy density integral to the hadron mass M and the pressure integral to zero.
- The 4-momentum transfer squared in the Breit-frame expansion scales as q_0 ~ O(l_had²/l_Ψ²) and q² = -q_vec² + O(l_had²/l_Ψ²), where l_had = max{r_p, λ_Comp} and l_Ψ = λ_deBrog.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate on defining the hadronic EMT, addressing issues raised by Jaffe (2020) regarding the nonrelativistic validity of Breit-frame definitions and by Lorcé (2020) and Freese (2021-2023) on light-front and transverse phase-space approaches. The authors provide a covariant framework that unifies the Breit-frame densities of Polyakov (2018) and the light-front densities of Miller (2007-2018) and Freese (2021-2023) as monopole moment densities from different multipole expansion schemes. The spin tensor contribution aligns with recent developments in relativistic spin hydrodynamics by Fukushima (2020) and others, and the formalism enables application to hadrons of different spin, including pions where J(q²) = 0.
