# Coulomb displacement energy and the low-energy astrophysical S factor for the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction

> The relationship between the Coulomb displacement energy for the A=8, J=2+, T=1 state and the low-energy astrophysical S factor for the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction is discussed. The displacement energy is interpreted in a particle-hole model. The dependence of the particle displacement energy on the potential well geometry is investigated and is used to relate the particle displacement energy to the rms radius and the asymptotic normalization of the valence proton wave function in B8. The asymptotic normalization is used to calculate the astrophysical S factor for the Be7(p,gamma)B8 reaction. The relationship to the Li7(n,gamma) reaction, the B8 quadrupole moment, radial density, and break-up momentum distribution are also discussed.

# Coulomb Displacement Energy and the Low-Energy Astrophysical S Factor for the \(^{7}\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{8}\)B Reaction

## Key claim

The astrophysical S factor \(S_{17}(20\ \text{keV})\) for the \(^{7}\)Be(p,\(\gamma\))\(^{8}\)B reaction can be constrained to \(26.5 \pm 2.0\) eV-barns by using the Coulomb displacement energy of the A=8, J=2\(^{+}\), T=1 state to determine the potential well geometry, but this result depends on the assumption that the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly correction is about 10% of the direct Coulomb shift and that the shell-model correction \(\Delta_{sm}\) is 64 keV.

## Method

The authors used a particle-hole model to relate the A=8, T=1 Coulomb displacement energy to the sum of the A=7 and A=9 displacement energies, with a shell-model correction calculated using the Ormand-Brown Coulomb plus charge-asymmetric interaction in a full 0p shell-model basis. They then calculated the direct Coulomb shift \(\Delta_o\) for a Woods-Saxon potential with varying radius \(R\) and diffuseness \(a\), fixing the potential depth to reproduce the 138 keV proton separation energy in \(^{8}\)B, and applied corrections for exchange, spin-orbit, vacuum polarization, proton-neutron mass difference, and the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly. The resulting asymptotic normalization of the valence proton wave function was used to compute \(S_{17}\) via the relationship between the density at 10 fm and the Whittaker function normalization.

## Key numbers

- The experimental A=8, T=1 displacement energy is \(\Delta_8 = 3.540\) MeV, compared to the sum \(\Delta_7 + \Delta_9 = 3.496\) MeV from the A=7 and A=9 mirror pairs.
- The empirical direct Coulomb shift for the valence particle is \(\Delta_o(\text{empirical}) = 1.780\) MeV.
- The valence proton rms radius is constrained to the range 4.5 to 4.7 fm for the empirical \(\Delta_o\).
- The valence proton density at \(r = 10\) fm is \(\rho(10\ \text{fm}) = (7.7 \pm 0.4) \times 10^{-6}\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- The total spectroscopic factor is \(S = 1.15 \pm 0.05\).
- The resulting astrophysical S factor is \(S_{17}(20\ \text{keV}) = 26.5 \pm 2.0\) eV-barns.
- The thermal \(^{7}\)Li(n,\(\gamma\))\(^{8}\)Li cross section calculated with the same potentials gives 78–83 mb, overestimating the experimental value of \(45.4 \pm 3.0\) mb.
- The calculated \(^{8}\)B quadrupole moment is 8.2 \(e\) fm\(^2\), compared to the experimental value of \(6.83 \pm 0.21\) \(e\) fm\(^2\).
- The valence proton rms radius is 4.60 fm, the core proton rms radius is 2.39 fm, and the neutron rms radius is 2.21 fm.
- The calculated charge rms radius is 3.05 fm and the matter rms radius is 2.71 fm.

## Context

This work extends the use of Coulomb displacement energy systematics, previously applied to heavier nuclei (A=17, A=41) in the context of the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, to constrain the potential geometry for the loosely bound valence proton in \(^{8}\)B. The result \(S_{17} = 26.5 \pm 2.0\) eV-barns agrees with the higher experimental values from Parker (1966) and Kavanagh et al. (1969) but is above the weighted average of all data (22.2 eV-barns) used in standard solar models. The paper also addresses the discrepancy in the \(^{7}\)Li(n,\(\gamma\)) cross section raised by Barker (1980), arguing that the required potential modifications are inconsistent with the Coulomb displacement energy constraint, and suggests that off-shell effects in the scattering wave function may resolve the neutron capture discrepancy without affecting the proton capture calculation.
