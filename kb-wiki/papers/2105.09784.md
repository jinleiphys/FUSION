# Parity Partner Bands in $^{163}$Lu: A novel approach for describing the negative parity states from a triaxial super-deformed band

> The wobbling spectrum of $^{163}$Lu is described through a novel approach, starting from a triaxial rotor model within a semi-classical picture, and obtaining a new set of equations for all four rotational bands that have wobbling character. Redefining the band structure in the present model is done by adopting the concepts of Signature Partner Bands and Parity Partner Bands. Indeed, describing a wobbling spectrum in an even-odd nucleus through signature and parity quantum numbers is an inedited interpretation of the triaxial super-deformed bands.

## Key claim

The four triaxial super-deformed bands (TSD1–TSD4) in \(^{163}\)Lu can all be described by coupling a single positive-parity \(i_{13/2}\) proton to a triaxial rotor core, provided the core is assigned positive parity for TSD1–3 and negative parity for TSD4, with an ad-hoc mean-field correction of 0.6 MeV to lift the parity degeneracy that would otherwise arise.

## Method

A particle-triaxial-rotor Hamiltonian is treated semi-classically via a time-dependent variational principle, using a trial function that mixes components of definite K. The classical equations of motion are linearized around the energy minimum to obtain two wobbling frequencies \(\Omega_1^I\) and \(\Omega_2^I\). The four bands are assigned phonon numbers, signature, and parity as shown in Table I, and their energies are computed from the minimal classical energy plus a phonon term, with separate single-particle energy shifts for the signature-unfavored band (TSD2) and the negative-parity band (TSD4).

## Key numbers

* Root-mean-square deviation of the fit to all four bands simultaneously: ≈ 79 keV, compared to ≈ 240 keV in the prior approach of Raduta (2020).
* Moments of inertia (MoI) from the fit: \(\mathcal{I}_1 = 72\ \hbar^2/\text{MeV}\), \(\mathcal{I}_2 = 15\ \hbar^2/\text{MeV}\), \(\mathcal{I}_3 = 7\ \hbar^2/\text{MeV}\).
* Triaxiality parameter: \(\gamma = 22^\circ\); potential strength: \(V = 2.1\ \text{MeV}\).
* Single-particle energy shifts: \(\epsilon_{j,1} - \epsilon_j = 0.3\ \text{MeV}\) (signature splitting), \(\epsilon_{j,2} - \epsilon_j = 0.6\ \text{MeV}\) (negative parity).
* Total signature splitting for TSD2: \(E(27/2) - E(25/2) = 492\ \text{keV}\) at the band head; \(E(91/2) - E(89/2) = 936\ \text{keV}\) at the terminus.
* Energy difference between TSD4 and TSD2 states of the same angular momentum: ≈ 300 keV.

## Context

This work extends the particle-triaxial-rotor description of wobbling in \(^{163}\)Lu previously developed by Raduta (2020), which required separate parameter sets for the negative-parity band TSD4 and achieved an r.m.s. of 240 keV. By reinterpreting TSD4 as a parity partner of TSD2, arising from a core of negative parity with \(\mathbf{R}=1,3,5,\dots\), the present model uses a single set of MoI for all four bands and halves the r.m.s. deviation. The parity-partner concept follows the definitions of Chasman (1970) and Raduta (1990s). The results enable a unified description of signature and parity partner bands in triaxial super-deformed nuclei and suggest that the same formalism can be applied to other wobblers such as \(^{161,165,167}\)Lu, \(^{135}\)Pr, and \(^{167}\)Ta.
