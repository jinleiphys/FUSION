# Chiral symmetry breaking and electromagnetic structure of the nucleon

> The electromagnetic form factors are the most fundamental quantities to describe the internal structure of the nucleon and are related to the charge radii of the baryons. We have calculated the charge radii of octet baryons in the framework of chiral constituent quark model with the inclusion of the spin-spin generated configuration mixing. This model is quite successful in predicting the low energy hadron matrix elements. The results obtained in the case of charge radii are also comparable to the latest experimental studies and show improvement over some theoretical interpretations.

## Key claim

The chiral constituent quark model, when augmented with spin-spin generated configuration mixing, yields charge radii for the octet baryons that are comparable to the latest experimental studies and show improvement over some theoretical interpretations, though the results remain model-dependent and rely on the specific parameterization of one-, two-, and three-quark contributions.

## Method

The authors calculated the charge radii of the octet baryons by evaluating matrix elements of a charge-radius operator expressed as a sum of one-, two-, and three-quark contributions between spin-flavor wave functions. The operator was simplified using the expectation value of the spin operator \(2 J \cdot \sum_i e_i \sigma_i\) between baryon states, reducing the calculation to the flavor and spin structure of each baryon. The framework employed was the chiral constituent quark model with the inclusion of spin-spin generated configuration mixing, which extends the naive quark model by incorporating the effects of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking.

## Key numbers

- Proton charge radius: \(r_p = 0.877 \pm 0.007\) fm (corresponding to \(r^2_p = 0.779 \pm 0.025\) fm\(^2\)), from Rosenfelder (2000).
- Neutron charge radius squared: \(r^2_n = -0.1161 \pm 0.0022\) fm\(^2\), from the Particle Data Group.
- \(\Sigma^-\) charge radius squared: \(r^2_{\Sigma^-} = 0.61 \pm 0.21\) fm\(^2\), from the SELEX Collaboration.
- Dirac form factor normalizations: \(F^p_1(0) = 1\), \(F^p_2(0) = \kappa_p = 1.793\), \(F^n_1(0) = 0\), \(F^n_2(0) = \kappa_n = -1.913\).

## Context

This work extends the naive quark model (NQM) calculations of baryon charge radii, which the authors note are in disagreement with experimental data, by incorporating chiral symmetry breaking effects through the chiral constituent quark model. The results are compared with experimental measurements from MAMI, JLAB, and SELEX Collaborations, as well as with the general parameterization method of Morpurgo (1992). The study enables further investigation of charge radii for other long-lived strange baryons such as \(\Lambda\), \(\Sigma\), and \(\Xi\), which the authors suggest may be measured in the near future.
