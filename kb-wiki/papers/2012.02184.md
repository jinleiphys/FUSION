# Constraining the initial stages of ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> It is frequently supposed that quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions undergoes free streaming at early times. We examine this issue based on the assumption that a universal attractor dominates the dynamics already at the earliest stages, which offers a way to connect the initial state with the start of the hydrodynamic expansion in an approximate but conceptually transparent fashion. We demonstrate that the centrality dependence of the measured particle multiplicities can be used to quantitatively constrain the pressure anisotropy and find that it strongly depends on the model of the initial energy deposition. As an illustration, we compare three initial state models and show that they predict rather different early-time values of the pressure anisotropy. This strongly suggests that assuming free streaming prior to hydrodynamization is not necessarily compatible with a generic initial state model and that features of the pre-hydrodynamic flow need to be matched with the model of the initial state.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the prehydrodynamic evolution of quark-gluon plasma is not independent of the chosen initial state model; assuming free streaming prior to hydrodynamization is not necessarily compatible with a generic initial state model, and the appropriate early-time attractor depends on the specific model of initial energy deposition.

## Method

The authors assume a universal attractor governs the early-time dynamics of boost-invariant systems, parameterizing the early-time energy density as ε ∼ τ^{-β} where β characterizes the attractor. They use the centrality dependence of measured charged particle multiplicities in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV to constrain β for three different initial state models (Model I based on nuclear thickness functions, the Trento p=-1 model, and a product-of-thicknesses model), fitting the ratio of multiplicities at different centralities to ALICE data.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit values of β for the three initial state models: β^{(I)} = 1.12, β^{(II)} = 1.96, β^{(III)} = 0.44, with statistical errors not exceeding 0.02
- The β values differ by a factor of almost 4.5 across the three models
- For free streaming, the expected behavior is ε ∼ 1/τ, corresponding to β = 1; none of the fitted β values are close to this, with the closest case (β = 1.12) giving a pressure anisotropy about 30% lower than free streaming
- The analysis uses ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV with total inelastic cross-section σ = 797 fm²

## Context

This work extends the concept of far-from-equilibrium attractors identified in hydrodynamic models (Heller 2015, Aniceto 2015), strongly-coupled N=4 SYM via AdS/CFT (Romatschke 2017, Spalinski 2017), and kinetic theory (Strickland 2017, Kurkela 2019) to phenomenological heavy-ion collisions. It contradicts the common assumption of free streaming prior to hydrodynamization used in many studies (Giacalone 2019, JETSCAPE 2020) and suggests that Bayesian analyses scanning over initial state models (Bernhard 2016, Nijs 2020) should not assume free-streaming prehydrodynamic evolution for all models. The method enables constraining the early-time behavior of the energy-momentum tensor from basic observables, with potential for more sophisticated constraints when symmetry requirements are relaxed.
