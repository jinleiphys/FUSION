# In-medium Y(1S,2S,3S) suppression in Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV

> We present model calculations for the in-medium suppression of the Y(1S,2S,3S) states in sqrt(s_NN)=5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider in comparison with recent CMS data for all three spin-triplet s-wave states. The model parameters initial central temperature, and formation times for the Y(nS) states are determined in simultaneous chi^2 minimizations with respect to the data, such that the sequential centrality- and transverse-momentum-dependent suppression of the observed states is reproduced.

## Key claim
The model reproduces the sequential centrality- and transverse-momentum-dependent suppression of Y(1S,2S,3S) states in 5.02 TeV Pb-Pb collisions, but only if the in-medium formation times are proportional to the inverse masses of the states rather than inversely proportional to their binding energies, contrary to common expectation.

## Method
The authors solve the radial stationary Schrodinger equation with a complex, temperature-dependent potential to obtain wavefunctions and binding energies for six bottomonia states. They compute dissociation widths from collisional damping and gluodissociation, fold these with a hydrodynamic background evolution including transverse expansion, and apply a relativistic Doppler correction for the finite transverse momentum of the bottomonia. The initial central temperature T0 and formation times for the three Y(nS) states are determined via simultaneous chi-squared minimization against CMS centrality- and pT-dependent suppression data.

## Key numbers
- Initial central temperature T0 = 535.9 MeV from chi-squared minimization (chi-squared/ndf = 45.6/32 = 1.43)
- Average Y(1S) formation time = 0.34 fm, Y(2S) = 0.32 fm, Y(3S) = 0.31 fm
- Y(1S) vacuum formation time from e+e- annihilation: 0.32 fm (Kharzeev and Tuchin, 1999)
- Gluon thermalization timescale: 0.1 fm
- String tension: sigma = 0.192 GeV^2
- QCD scale Lambda_QCD = 276.3 MeV (one-loop running with matched charm and bottom masses)

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier model (Wolschin and collaborators, 2017, 2020) for Y suppression in heavy-ion collisions, which previously used a single formation time of 0.4 fm for all states and predicted T0 = 513 MeV from entropy scaling. The new simultaneous fit to CMS data (2024) yields a higher T0 = 536 MeV and state-dependent formation times. The finding that excited states have shorter formation times than the ground state contradicts the common assumption of formation times being inversely proportional to binding energies (Kohler, 2015; Chen and collaborators, 2024), but has a precedent in muonic atom formation (Leon and Watanabe, 1974). The results enable future chi-squared minimizations at RHIC energies when more precise Au-Au data become available.
