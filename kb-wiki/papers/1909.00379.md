# Color transparency in $\bar p d \to π^- π^0 p$ reaction

> We consider exclusive two-pion production in antiproton-deuteron interactions at the beam momenta around 10 GeV/c in the kinematics with large momentum transfer in the underlying hard process $\bar p n \to π^- π^0$. The calculations are performed taking into account the antiproton and pion soft rescattering on the spectator proton in the framework of the generalized eikonal approximation. We focus on the color transparency effect that is modeled by introducing the dependence of rescattering amplitudes on the relative position of the struck and spectator nucleons along the momentum of a fast particle. As a consequence of the interplay between the impulse approximation and rescattering amplitudes the nuclear transparency ratio reveals a pretty complicated behaviour as a function of the transverse momentum of the spectator proton and the relative azimuthal angle between the $π^-$-meson and the proton. Color transparency significantly suppresses rescattering amplitudes which leads to substantial modifications of the nuclear transparency ratio moving it closer to the value obtained in the impulse approximation. By performing the Monte-Carlo analysis we determine that this effect can be studied at PANDA with a reasonable statistics.

## Key claim
Color transparency (CT) effects in the exclusive reaction $\bar p d \to \pi^- \pi^0 p$ at beam momenta around 10 GeV/c significantly suppress soft rescattering amplitudes, moving the nuclear transparency ratio closer to the impulse approximation value, but the effect is strongly dependent on the transverse momentum of the spectator proton and the relative azimuthal angle between the $\pi^-$ meson and the proton.

## Method
The authors model the reaction using the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA), including a single-step impulse approximation (IA) amplitude and three two-step amplitudes with elastic rescattering of the incoming antiproton and each outgoing pion on the spectator proton. Color transparency is incorporated via the quantum diffusion model (QDM), which introduces a dependence of the rescattering amplitudes on the relative longitudinal position of the struck and spectator nucleons, using an effective cross section that grows linearly with distance up to the coherence length.

## Key numbers
- Beam momenta studied: 5, 10, and 15 GeV/c.
- Coherence length at momenta around 10 GeV/c is approximately 4-6 fm.
- The average transverse momentum of a parton in the scattered hadron, $\sqrt{\langle k_{ht}^2\rangle}$, is set to 0.35 GeV/c.
- The mass denominator for the coherence length, $\Delta M^2$, is varied between 0.7 and 1.1 GeV$^2$.
- The mean square charge radius of the pion, $\langle r_\pi^2\rangle$, is $0.439 \pm 0.008$ fm$^2$.
- The four-differential cross section for $\beta=1.5$ (corresponding to $\Theta_{c.m.}=60\degree$) is larger by an order of magnitude compared to the case of $\beta=1$ ($\Theta_{c.m.}=90\degree$).

## Context
This work extends the study of color transparency to antiproton-induced reactions, specifically the two-pion production channel, which is predicted to show a more pronounced CT signal than baryon production due to the higher probability of a $q \bar q$-pair forming a point-like configuration. It builds on prior GEA studies of the $d(p,2p)n$ reaction by Frankfurt et al. (1996) and aims to provide a test case for the PANDA experiment at FAIR, where the interplay between hard and soft processes can be studied at relatively low beam momenta where the coherence length is comparable to nuclear radii.
