# Holographic heavy-baryons in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with the D0-D4 background

> We extend the holographic analysis of light-baryon spectrum in \cite{key-50} to the case involving the heavy flavors. With the construction of the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model in the D0-D4 background, we use the mechanism proposed in \cite{key-59,key-60,key-61} by including two light and one heavy flavor branes, to describe the heavy-light baryons as heavy mesons bound to a flavor instanton. The background geometry of this model corresponds to an excited state in the dual field theory with nonzero glue condensate $\left\langle \mathrm{Tr}\mathcal{F}\wedge\mathcal{F}\right\rangle =8π^{2}N_{c}\tildeκ$, or equivalently a $θ$ angle, which is proportional to the number density of the D0-brane charge. At strongly coupled limit, this model shows that the heavy meson is always bound in the form of the zero mode of the flavor instanton in the fundamental representation. We systematically study the quantization for the effective Lagrangian of heavy-light baryons by employing the soliton picture, and derive the mass spectrum of heavy-light baryons in the situation with single- and double-heavy baryon. We find the difference in the mass spectrum becomes smaller if the density of D0-brane charge increases and the constraint of stable states of the heavy-light baryons is $1<b<3$. It indicates that baryon can not stably exist for sufficiently large density of D0 charge which is in agreement with the conclusions in the previous study of this model.

## Key claim

This work extends the holographic analysis of light-baryon spectra in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model with D0-D4 background to include heavy flavors, finding that the mass spectrum difference between single- and double-heavy baryons decreases as the D0-brane charge density increases, with stable heavy-light baryons existing only in the parameter range \(1 < b < 3\).

## Method

The authors construct a holographic model using the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model in a D0-D4 brane background, incorporating two light flavor D8/anti-D8 brane pairs and one heavy flavor D8/anti-D8 brane pair separated from the light branes. Heavy-light baryons are described as heavy mesons bound to a flavor instanton configuration on the light flavor branes, with the heavy meson fields treated as zero modes in the double limit of large 't Hooft coupling \(\lambda\) and large heavy meson mass \(m_H\). The effective Lagrangian is quantized using the soliton picture to derive the mass spectrum for single- and double-heavy baryons.

## Key numbers

* The constraint for stable heavy-light baryon states is \(1 < b < 3\), where \(b \equiv H_0(U_{KK})\) is related to the D0-brane charge density.
* The glue condensate is given by \(\langle \mathrm{Tr}\mathcal{F}\wedge\mathcal{F}\rangle = 8\pi^2 N_c \tilde{\kappa}\), where \(\tilde{\kappa} = N_0/(N_c V_4)\) is proportional to the D0-brane number density.
* The heavy meson mass parameter is defined as \(m_H = \frac{1}{\sqrt{6}}\frac{N_f+1}{N_f} v b^{1/4}\), where \(v\) is the vacuum expectation value from the moduli solution.
* The four-dimensional Yang-Mills coupling is \(g_{YM}^2 = 4\pi^2 g_s l_s / \beta\), with the KK mass scale \(M_{KK} = 2\pi/\beta\) and \(\beta = 4\pi U_{KK}^{-1/2} R^{3/2} b^{1/2}/3\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic analysis of light-baryon spectra previously studied in the D0-D4/D8 system by Li (2016) and others to include heavy flavors, following the mechanism proposed by Bigazzi et al. (2014) and others for incorporating heavy quarks via separated flavor branes. The model enables the holographic study of heavy-light baryon spectra with a nonzero \(\theta\) angle (proportional to D0-brane charge density), and the finding that baryons cannot stably exist for sufficiently large D0 charge density agrees with previous conclusions from the same model. This approach develops the bound state method from the Skyrme model holographically by including \(\theta\) dependence.
