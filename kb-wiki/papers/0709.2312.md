# Asymptotic estimation of some multiple integrals and the electromagnetic deuteron form factors at high momentum transfer

> A theorem about asymptotic estimation of multiple integral of a special type is proved for the case when the integrand peaks at the integration domain bound, but not at a point of extremum. Using this theorem the asymptotic expansion of the electromagnetic deuteron form factors at high momentum transfers is obtained in the framework of two-nucleon model in both nonrelativistic and relativistic impulse approximations. It is found that relativistic effects slow down the decrease of deuteron form factors and result in agreement between the relativistic asymptotics and experimental data at high momentum transfers.

## Key claim

The paper proves a theorem for the asymptotic estimation of multiple integrals where the integrand peaks at the integration domain boundary (not at an extremum), and uses it to derive the first rigorous asymptotic expansions of deuteron electromagnetic form factors at high momentum transfer, finding that relativistic effects slow the decrease of the form factors and bring the asymptotics into agreement with experimental data.

## Method

The authors first prove a general theorem for the asymptotic expansion of n-tuple integrals of Laplace type when the maximum of the integrand lies on the boundary of the integration domain and the point is not an extremum. They then apply this theorem to the double integrals representing the deuteron charge, quadrupole, and magnetic form factors in both the nonrelativistic impulse approximation and a relativistic impulse approximation formulated in the instant form of Poincare-invariant quantum mechanics (PIQM). The asymptotic expansions are obtained for deuteron wave functions expressed as a discrete superposition of Yukawa-type terms, and the leading asymptotic terms are written explicitly in terms of the wave function parameters.

## Key numbers

- The nonrelativistic charge form factor asymptotics scale as \(G_C^{NR} \sim 1/Q^8\) with a multiplicative constant involving \(\left[\sum_j C_j m_j^2\right]^2\) and the nucleon electric form factors.
- The nonrelativistic quadrupole form factor asymptotics scale as \(G_Q^{NR} \sim 1/Q^{12}\) with a multiplicative constant involving \(\left[\sum_j C_j m_j^2\right]\left[\sum_j D_j m_j^4\right]\).
- The nonrelativistic magnetic form factor asymptotics scale as \(G_M^{NR} \sim 1/Q^8\) with a multiplicative constant involving \(\left[\sum_j C_j m_j^2\right]^2\) and the nucleon magnetic form factors.
- The relativistic charge and magnetic form factors are related to the nonrelativistic ones by \(G_{C,M}^R(Q^2) \sim (Q^3 / (2^{7/2} M^3)) \, G_{C,M}^{NR}(Q^2)\).
- The relativistic quadrupole form factor is related to the nonrelativistic one by \(G_Q^R(Q^2) \sim (Q^4 / (2^{11/2} M^4)) \, G_Q^{NR}(Q^2)\).
- The exponential factor in the nonrelativistic case is \(\exp(-r_0^2 Q^2 / 16)\), while in the relativistic case it is \(\exp\left(-\frac{r_0^2}{4}(M\sqrt{Q^2+4M^2}-2M^2)\right)\), which scales as \(\exp(-\text{const} \cdot Q)\) at large \(Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of the instant form of Poincare-invariant quantum mechanics (Krutov et al., 2002-2007) and provides the first rigorous asymptotic expansions for deuteron form factors, including multiplicative preasymptotic constants, rather than just power-law exponents as in earlier studies. The results enable quantitative comparison with experimental data from JLab elastic electron-deuteron scattering experiments at \(Q^2 \simeq 10\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) (Arrington et al., 2005) and help determine the limits of applicability of the two-nucleon model versus quark approaches to the deuteron. The theorem itself is a general mathematical tool applicable to any multiple integral of the special type where the integrand peaks at the boundary.
