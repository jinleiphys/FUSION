# Analysis of rho-omega interference in the pion form-factor

> The formalism underlying the analysis of e+e- -> pi+pi- in the rho-omega interference region is carefully revisited. We show that the standard neglect of the pure I=0 omega, omega_I, `direct'' coupling to pi-pi is not valid, and extract those combinations of the direct coupling and ρ-omega mixing allowed by experiment. The latter is shown to be only very weakly constrained by experiment, and we conclude that data from the e+e- -> pi+pi- interference region cannot be used to fix the value of rho-omega mixing in a model-independent way unless the errors on the experimental phase can be significantly reduced. Certain other modifications of the usual formalism necessitated by the unavoidable momentum-dependence of rho-omega mixing are also discussed.

# Analysis of rho-omega interference in the pion form-factor

## Key claim
The standard neglect of the pure I=0 omega "direct" coupling to ππ is not valid, and data from the e+e- → π+π- interference region cannot be used to fix the value of ρ-ω mixing in a model-independent way unless the errors on the experimental phase can be significantly reduced. The extracted mixing amplitude is only very weakly constrained by experiment, with the true uncertainty far larger than the ±600 MeV² usually quoted.

## Method
The authors revisit the formalism for e+e- → π+π- in the ρ-ω interference region, working to first order in isospin breaking. They derive the relation between the isospin-pure (ρ_I, ω_I) and physical (ρ, ω) bases, accounting for the momentum-dependence of the off-diagonal self-energy Π_ρω(q²). Using the S-matrix formalism with complex pole positions, they extract the combinations of the direct ω_I → ππ coupling (parameterized by G = g_ω_Iππ / g_ρ_Iππ) and the ρ-ω mixing amplitude (Π̃_ρω) that are allowed by experimental data from the Orsay phase analysis.

## Key numbers
- The complex parameter z, which governs the cancellation between direct and mixing contributions, is z = 0.9324 + 0.3511 i from the S-matrix fit of Bernicha (1994), and z = 1.023 + 0.2038 i from the conventional fit of Benayoun (1994).
- The experimental ω exchange amplitude has magnitude A = -0.0109 ± 0.0011 and phase φ = (116.7 ± 5.8)°.
- The ratio of electromagnetic couplings |r_ex| = 0.30 ± 0.01, derived from the ω → e+e- and ρ → e+e- decay widths.
- Fixing A at its central value, the experimental phase alone restricts Π̃_ρω(m_ρ²) to the range (-1090 MeV², -5980 MeV²); including the G constraint narrows this to (-2290 MeV², -6180 MeV²). Including the experimental error on A extends the phase constraint range to (-840 MeV², -6240 MeV²).
- For comparison, artificially setting G = 0 gives Π̃_ρω(m_ρ²) = -3960 MeV².
- The ρ pole position from the S-matrix fit is m̂_ρ = 757.00 ± 0.59 MeV, Γ_ρ = 143.41 ± 1.27 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Renard (1973) and the standard analyses of the Orsay group, showing that the conventional neglect of the direct ω_I → ππ coupling is invalid due to the significant imaginary part of the parameter z. The authors demonstrate that the extracted ρ-ω mixing cannot be reliably used in few-body isospin-breaking calculations as done by H. B. O'Connell (1995) and others, unless the experimental phase uncertainty is reduced. The paper also generalizes the treatment of momentum-dependent mixing, building on earlier work by K. Maltman (1995) and showing that the q²-dependence of Π_ρω is unavoidable. The results enable future experimental improvements in phase determination to simultaneously extract both the mixing amplitude and the isospin-breaking ratio G, and provide non-trivial constraints for models of vector meson mixing such as that of K. Mitchell and P. Tandy (1995).
