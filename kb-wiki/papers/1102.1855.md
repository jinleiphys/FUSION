# Heavy lepton pair production in nucleus-nucleus collisions at LHC energy - a case study

> We present a study of $τ^{+}τ^{-}$ lepton pair production in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.5 TeV. The larger $τ^{\pm}$ mass ($\sim$ 1.77 GeV) compared to $e^{\pm}$ and $μ^{\pm}$ leads to considerably small hadronic contribution to the $τ^{+}τ^{-}$ pair invariant mass ($M$) distribution relative to the production from thermal partonic sources. The quark-anti-quark annihilation processes via intermediary virtual photon, Z and Higgs bosons have been considered for the production of $ τ^{+}τ^{-}$. We observe that the contribution from Drell-Yan process dominates over thermal yield for $τ^{+}τ^{-}$ pair mass from 4 to 20 GeV at the LHC energy. We also present the ratio of $τ$ lepton pair yields for nucleus-nucleus collisions relative to yields from p+p collisions scaled by number of binary collisions at LHC energies as a function $τ$ pair mass. The ratio is found to be significantly above unity for the mass range 4 to 6 GeV. This indicates the possibility of detecting $τ^{+}τ^{-}$ pair from quark gluon plasma (QGP) in the mass window $4\leq M$(GeV)$\leq 6$.

## Key claim

The ratio of tau lepton pair yields in Pb+Pb collisions to binary-collision-scaled p+p yields is significantly above unity for the invariant mass range 4 to 6 GeV, indicating the possibility of detecting tau pairs from quark gluon plasma in that mass window, provided the Drell-Yan background can be subtracted using p+p measurements.

## Method

The authors computed tau+tau- pair production from quark-antiquark annihilation via virtual photon, Z boson, and Higgs boson mediators, using leading-order matrix elements with standard model parameters (m_Z = 91 GeV, m_H = 120 GeV, m_tau = 1.78 GeV). The Drell-Yan contribution was obtained by folding partonic cross sections with CTEQ5M parton distribution functions and scaling by the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions (N_coll = 1369) from a Glauber model at impact parameter b = 3.6 fm for 0-50% centrality. The thermal contribution from quark-gluon plasma was calculated by convolving the same elementary cross sections with thermal quark distributions and integrating over a hydrodynamic space-time evolution with initial temperature T_i = 700 MeV at proper time tau_i = 0.08 fm/c, using lattice QCD equation of state from the MILC collaboration and crossover temperature T_c = 175 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature of the thermal system: T_i = 700 MeV, at proper time tau_i = 0.08 fm/c, for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV.
* Number of binary collisions: N_coll = 1369, for impact parameter b = 3.6 fm corresponding to 0-50% centrality at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV.
* Drell-Yan contribution dominates over thermal yield for tau+tau- pair invariant mass from 4 to 20 GeV at LHC energy.
* Ratio of Pb+Pb yields to binary-collision-scaled p+p yields is approximately 2 at M = 4 GeV and decreases toward unity above M = 6 GeV.
* Tau lepton mass: approximately 1.77 GeV; Z boson mass: 91 GeV; Higgs boson mass: 120 GeV; weak mixing angle sin^2(theta_w) = 0.234.

## Context

This work extends previous dilepton studies at SPS (CERES, NA60) and RHIC (PHENIX) energies, which focused on e+e- and mu+mu- pairs, to the third-generation tau lepton at LHC energies. The key advantage over lighter leptons is that the tau mass (1.77 GeV) places the tau pair mass distribution above the rho, omega, and phi meson resonances that contaminate the low-mass e+e- and mu+mu- spectra. The results suggest that the mass window 4-6 GeV is optimal for extracting thermal QGP signals via tau pairs, and the ratio method using binary-collision-scaled p+p data (as proposed by the authors) provides a way to isolate the thermal contribution from the dominant Drell-Yan background.
