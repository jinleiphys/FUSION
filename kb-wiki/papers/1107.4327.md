# Event-by-event mean p_T fluctuations in pp and Pb-Pb collisions measured by the ALICE experiment at the LHC

> Non-statistical event-by-event fluctuations of the mean transverse momentum of charged particles in pp and Pb-Pb collisions are studied using the ALICE experiment at the LHC. Little collision energy dependence is observed in pp. The data indicate a common scaling behaviour with event multiplicity from pp to semi-central Pb-Pb collisions. In central Pb-Pb, the results deviate from this trend, exhibiting a significant reduction of the fluctuation strength. The results are compared with measurements in Au-Au collisions at lower energies and with Monte Carlo simulations.

## Key claim
Non-statistical event-by-event mean transverse momentum fluctuations in pp collisions show little collision energy dependence, and a common scaling with event multiplicity extends from pp to semi-central Pb-Pb collisions; however, central Pb-Pb collisions exhibit a significant additional reduction of fluctuation strength beyond this common trend.

## Method
The analysis used charged particle tracks reconstructed with the ALICE Time Projection Chamber (TPC) and Inner Tracking System (ITS) in the kinematic range 0.15 < pT < 2 GeV/c and |η| < 0.8. The dynamical component of event-by-event mean pT fluctuations was quantified using the two-particle correlator Cm, defined as the mean covariance of all particle pairs within an event relative to the inclusive mean pT of the multiplicity class. Results were obtained for pp collisions at √s = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV, and for Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV in 5% centrality intervals.

## Key numbers
- In pp collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV, the relative fluctuations √Cm / ⟨pT⟩m as a function of accepted multiplicity Nacc follow a power law with exponent b = -0.385 ± 0.003.
- The HIJING event generator yields a power law exponent b = -0.508 ± 0.004 for Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV, consistent with scaling by 1/√Nacc.
- The pp data at √s = 0.9, 2.76, and 7 TeV show universal behavior except at small multiplicities where fluctuations increase slightly with beam energy.
- In central Pb-Pb collisions, the fluctuation strength deviates significantly from the pp baseline, showing a reduction beyond the common scaling observed up to Nacc ≈ 600.

## Context
This work extends measurements of mean pT fluctuations from RHIC Au-Au collisions at √sNN = 0.2 TeV by STAR (2005) to LHC energies, comparing pp and Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV. The results show that the pp baseline scaling also describes peripheral Au-Au data at RHIC, but the deviation in central collisions is more pronounced at LHC energies. The findings enable further studies into mechanisms such as thermalization, collectivity, or critical behavior near a phase boundary that could cause the non-trivial reduction of fluctuations in central heavy-ion collisions.
