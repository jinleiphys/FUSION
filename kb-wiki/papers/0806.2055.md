# Resonance Recombination Model and Quark Distribution Functions in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We investigate the consequences of space-momentum correlations in quark phase-space distributions for coalescence processes at the hadronization transition. Thus far it has been proved difficult to reconcile such correlations with the empirically observed constituent quark number scaling (CQNS) at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). To address this problem we combine our earlier developed quark recombination model with quark phase-space distributions computed from relativistic Langevin simulations in an expanding Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). Hadronization is based on resonance formation within a Boltzmann equation which recovers thermal equilibrium and obeys energy conservation in the quark-coalescence process, while the fireball background is adjusted to hydrodynamic simulations of semi-central Au-Au collisions at RHIC. To facilitate the applicability of the Langevin process, we focus on strange and charm quarks. Their interactions in the QGP are modeled using leading-order perturbative QCD augmented by effective Lagrangians with resonances which smoothly merge into hadronic states formed at T_c. The interaction strength is adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation value for the quark-elliptic flow, v_{2,q}^{sat}~7-8%. The resulting phi and J/ψelliptic flow recover CQNS over a large range in transverse momentum (p_T) within a few percent. As a function of transverse kinetic energy, both the quark spectra from the Langevin simulations and the meson spectra generated via resonance recombination recover CQNS from zero to at least 3 GeV.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that space-momentum correlations in quark phase-space distributions, generated by relativistic Langevin simulations of an expanding quark-gluon plasma, can be reconciled with constituent quark number scaling (CQNS) of meson elliptic flow to within a few percent, provided that the hadronization process obeys energy conservation via resonance formation in a Boltzmann equation.

## Method

The authors combine relativistic Langevin simulations for strange and charm quarks in an expanding thermal QGP background with a resonance recombination model based on a Boltzmann equation for meson formation via $q+\bar{q} \to M$ scattering. The quark interactions in the QGP are modeled using leading-order perturbative QCD augmented by effective Lagrangians with resonances that smoothly merge into hadronic states at $T_c$, with interaction strengths adjusted to reproduce the empirical saturation value of quark elliptic flow. The resulting quark phase-space distributions at hadronization are then used as input for the Boltzmann-based hadronization formalism evaluated in the stationary equilibrium limit.

## Key numbers

- Quark elliptic flow saturation value: $v_{2,q}^{\mathrm{sat}} \simeq 7$-$8\%$, used to adjust interaction strength
- $\phi$ meson resonance parameters: mass $m_{\phi}=1.02\ \mathrm{GeV}$, width $\Gamma_{\phi}=50\ \mathrm{MeV}$, with $s$-quark mass $m_s=0.45\ \mathrm{GeV}$
- $J/\psi$ meson resonance parameters: mass $3.1\ \mathrm{GeV}$, width $\Gamma_{J/\psi}=0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}$, with $c$-quark mass $m_c=1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}$
- Resonance width for $s$-quark interactions: $\Gamma_{s\bar q} \simeq 0.3\ \mathrm{GeV}$; for $c$-quark interactions: $\Gamma_{c\bar q} \simeq 0.6\ \mathrm{GeV}$
- Fireball volume for semicentral Au-Au collisions: $V_{\mathrm{FB}} \simeq 1200\ \mathrm{fm}^3$ at $T_c=180\ \mathrm{MeV}$
- Average radial surface expansion velocity at hadronization: $\langle \beta_0 \rangle \simeq 0.48c$
- CQNS recovered within a few percent for $\phi$ and $J/\psi$ elliptic flow over a large range in $p_T$, and from zero to at least $3\ \mathrm{GeV}$ in transverse kinetic energy
- $c$-quark $v_2$ deviates from strange-quark $v_2$ by up to $\sim 20\%$ at low $p_t$

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier resonance recombination model (Ravagli, van Hees, Rapp 2007) by replacing blast-wave parameterizations with microscopically computed quark phase-space distributions from Langevin simulations. It addresses a long-standing difficulty identified in prior work by Pratt (2004), Molnar (2004), and Greco (2005), who found that incorporating space-momentum correlations into quark coalescence models tended to spoil the empirically observed constituent quark number scaling. The approach enables a more controlled extension of coalescence mechanisms to low $p_T$ and makes contact with hydrodynamic descriptions of bulk matter at RHIC, while also linking the strongly interacting nature of the sQGP to quark coalescence from a collective partonic source.
