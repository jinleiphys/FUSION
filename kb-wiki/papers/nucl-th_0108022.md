# Microscopic description of $η$-photoproduction on light nuclei

> A microscopic four-body description of near-threshold coherent photoproduction of the $η$ meson on the (3N)-nuclei is given. The photoproduction cross-section is calculated using the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian. The results indicate that the final state interaction of the $η$ meson with the residual nucleus plays an important role in the photoproduction process. Sensitivity of the results to the choice of the $ηN$ T-matrix is investigated. The importance of obeying the condition of $ηN$ unitarity is demonstrated.

# Microscopic description of η-photoproduction on light nuclei

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the final state interaction of the η meson with the residual nucleus plays an important role in near-threshold coherent photoproduction on ³H and ³He targets, and that obeying the two-body unitarity condition for the ηN T-matrix is essential. However, the results are conditional on the choice of the ηN T-matrix: two versions that reproduce the same ηN scattering length and S₁₁ resonance pole give significantly different cross-sections, while two versions that both obey unitarity give nearly identical results despite having different scattering lengths.

## Method
The authors employ a microscopic four-body description using the Finite Rank Approximation (FRA) of the nuclear Hamiltonian, which neglects virtual excitations of the nucleus during its interaction with the η meson. The photon is introduced by treating ηN and γN states as two channels of the same system, with the T-operator replaced by a 2×2 matrix. Three different separable versions of the ηN T-matrix t^{ηη} are constructed, all with the same form factor g(k) = (k² + α²)⁻¹ (α = 3.316 fm⁻¹) but different propagators τ(z): a Breit-Wigner form (version I), a unitarity-preserving form from an energy-dependent potential (version II), and a unitarity-constrained Breit-Wigner form (version III). The nuclear wave function is obtained via the Integro-Differential Equation Approach (IDEA) with the Malfliet–Tjon potential.

## Key numbers
- The ηN scattering length for versions I and II: a_{ηN} = (0.55 + i 0.30) fm
- The ηN scattering length for version III: a_{ηN} = (0.76 + i 0.61) fm
- The S₁₁ resonance pole position: E₀ = 1535 MeV − (m_N + m_η), Γ = 150 MeV
- The form factor parameter: α = 3.316 fm⁻¹
- The ratio of neutron to proton photoproduction T-matrices: A = −0.84 ± 0.15, from multipole analysis of Muk (year not given in text)
- The off-shell extension parameter κ is set equal to α

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous treatment of coherent η-photoproduction on the deuteron using exact AGS equations (Belyaev et al., 2001) to the three-nucleon systems ³H and ³He. It contrasts with the first-order optical potential approach of Tiator (year not given in text), which underestimates the cross-section near threshold because it is inadequate near resonances. The results enable future experimental determination of the neutron-to-proton photoproduction ratio A by showing that tritium is a more sensitive target than ³He for this purpose.
