# Isobar configurations: $螖N$ correlations versus the independent particle model

> We present a comparative analysis of two models for the $A( {纬,\,蟺N})B$ reaction, which take into account the isobar configurations in the ground state of the nuclei: the $螖N$ correlation model and the quasifree pion photoproduction model. The considered models differ in their descriptions of the nucleus states. The $螖N$ correlation model takes into account the dynamic correlations of the nucleon and isobar formed in the virtual transition $NN \to 螖N$, and in the quasifree pion photoproduction model, isobars and nucleons in the nucleus are considered as independent constituents. The predictions of the models are considered for two reactions ${}^{16}$O$( {纬,\,蟺^{ +} p} ){}^{15}$C and ${}^{16}$O$( {纬,\,蟺^{ -} p} ){}^{15}$O. It is shown, that the two models predict the differential cross section significantly differing both in absolute values, and in the shape of the angular dependence. We compare the results of the $螖N$ correlation model for the $( {纬,\,蟺N} )$ and $( {纬,\,蟺NN})$ reactions with the ${}^{16}$O$( {纬,\, 蟺^{ -} p})$ reaction data measured at BNL. Our results give support to the $螖N$ correlation model.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the $\Delta N$ correlation model, which accounts for dynamic correlations between nucleons and isobars formed in virtual $NN \to \Delta N$ transitions, provides a significantly different description of the $A(\gamma,\pi N)B$ reaction compared to the quasifree pion photoproduction model (which treats isobars and nucleons as independent constituents), and that comparison with experimental data supports the $\Delta N$ correlation model over the independent particle approach.

## Method

The authors performed a comparative analysis of two theoretical models for the $^{16}\text{O}(\gamma,\pi^+p)^{15}\text{C}$ and $^{16}\text{O}(\gamma,\pi^-p)^{15}\text{O}$ reactions: the $\Delta N$ correlation model, which includes dynamic correlations from the $NN \to \Delta N$ transition and uses a wave function expansion in states of total angular momentum $J$, isospin $T$, orbital angular momentum $l$, and total spin $s$, and the quasifree pion photoproduction model, which treats isobars and nucleons as independent constituents within an independent particle model. The squared modulus of the direct reaction amplitude was computed using one-body density matrices incorporating isobar configurations, with the $\gamma\Delta \to N\pi$ transition operator expanded in spin and isospin structures and the $\gamma N \to N'\pi$ operator taken from the non-relativistic Blomqvist-Laget photoproduction operator. Calculations were performed in the plane-wave approximation to isolate polarization effects from $\Delta N$ correlations, and the $\Delta N$ correlation model results were compared with ${}^{16}\text{O}(\gamma,\pi^-p)$ reaction data measured at the LEGS facility of Brookhaven National Laboratory.

## Key numbers

- The $\Delta N$ correlation model and quasifree model predictions for the $^{16}\text{O}(\gamma,\pi^+p)^{15}\text{C}$ reaction differential cross section differ by up to $\sim 80\%$ outside the scope of coplanarity of particle momenta.
- For the $^{16}\text{O}$ nucleus, the factor $N_C^{\Delta N}$ in the $\Delta N$ correlation model equals 0.97.
- The coefficients $N_C^{qf}$ and $N_C^{\Delta N}$ differ by a value $N_\Delta/A$, which does not exceed $\sim 0.02$ for $p$-shell nuclei.
- The differential cross section of $^{16}\text{O}(\gamma,\pi^+p)^{15}\text{C}$ was calculated at photon energy $E_\gamma = 450$ MeV, with pion momentum in the c.m. of the pion-nucleon pair perpendicular to the photon momentum, polar angle $\theta_B = 90^\circ$, and azimuth angle $\varphi_\pi = 90^\circ$, with residual nucleus momentum $p_B = 370$ MeV/$c$ for angular dependence calculations.
- The $\Delta N$ system from the $NN \rightarrow \Delta N$ transition was assumed to be in a state with quantum numbers $J = 0$, $T = 1$, and $l = s = 2$.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previously proposed $\Delta N$ correlation model for the $A(\gamma,\pi N)B$ reaction (Glavanakov and Tabachenko, 2014) and provides a direct comparison with the quasifree pion photoproduction model used in earlier studies of $( \pi^+, \pi^- p)$ reactions (cited as references 5,6) and $(p, p' \pi^+ p)$ reactions (reference 7). The analysis enables interpretation of exclusive $(\gamma,\pi N)$ reaction data at high momenta of the residual nucleus, where isobar configuration contributions are expected to be significant, and follows the approach used for $(\gamma,\pi NN)$ reactions where $\Delta N$ correlations served as a basis similar to how short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations were used for $(e, e' NN)$ reactions (reference 12).
