# The nuclear binding and the EMC effect in the deuteron

> Influence of the nuclear binding in the deuteron deep inelastic structure function $F_2(x,Q^2)$ is investigated. The description is based on the Bethe-Salpeter formalism in the ladder approximation and the operator product expansion within an effective meson-nucleon theory. It is shown that the binding in a fully relativistic treatment can be simply parametrised and its origin can be understood in terms of conventional nuclear degrees of freedom. A numerical estimate of the EMC effect in the deuteron is given.

## Key claim

The nuclear binding effect in the deuteron deep inelastic structure function can be parametrized simply and understood in terms of conventional nuclear degrees of freedom, but the magnitude of the EMC effect in the deuteron is predicted to be about 2% deviation from unity at intermediate x, which contradicts extrapolations from heavy nuclei data that suggest a 4% effect.

## Method

The authors use the Bethe-Salpeter formalism in the ladder approximation combined with the operator product expansion within an effective meson-nucleon theory to compute the deuteron structure function F2(x,Q^2). They calculate the moments of the deuteron structure function by evaluating matrix elements of twist-two operators over the deuteron ground state, then reconstruct the structure function via Mellin transform. The nucleon momentum distribution is obtained from the Bethe-Salpeter vertex function using a separable interaction approximation, and the pion exchange current contribution is estimated from the one-pion-exchange kernel.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon contribution to the deuteron momentum from the relativistic impulse approximation gives ⟨z⟩_RIA = 1.0153, which breaks the energy-momentum sum rule
- The interaction term reduces the nucleon momentum fraction by μ2(int) = -0.0188, yielding a final value ⟨z⟩ = 0.9965
- The fraction of deuteron momentum carried by exchange mesons is Δ = 0.0035
- The mean number of excess pions is ⟨N_π⟩ ≈ 0.015
- The ratio R(x,Q^2) = F2^D(x,Q^2)/F2^N(x,Q^2) shows a maximum at x=0 given by R(0) = 1 + ⟨N_π⟩ F2^π(0)/F2^N(0)
- The theoretical prediction shows about 2% deviation from unity at intermediate x, versus approximately 4% from extrapolations of heavy nuclei data
- Calculations are performed at Q^2 = 25 GeV^2

## Context

This work extends the method introduced in Refs. (New, 1995) and (PhysRev, 1995) for investigating deep inelastic scattering on light nuclei, applying it specifically to the deuteron. The approach differentiates pure binding effects from off-shell effects, which have unlike origins. The results contradict the suggestion by Frankfurt (1995) that the A-dependence of F2^A(x) should be determined by average nuclear density, which would imply a larger EMC effect in the deuteron than the authors find. The work enables future studies of nuclear binding effects in heavier nuclei using the same relativistic formalism.
