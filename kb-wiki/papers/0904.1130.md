# Non-Linear derivative interactions in relativistic hadrodynamics

> The Lagrangian density of Relativistic Hadrodynamics (RHD) is extended by introducing non-linear derivative (NLD) interactions of the nucleon with the meson fields. As a consequence, the nucleon selfenergy becomes both momentum and density dependent. With a single cut-off parameter, which regulates the NLD Lagrangian, our approach is compatible with results from microscopic nuclear matter calculations as well as with Dirac phenomenology. It also reproduces the correct behavior of the real part of the proton-nucleus potential both at low and at high proton energies.

## Key claim

The authors extend Relativistic Hadrodynamics (RHD) by introducing non-linear derivative (NLD) interactions of the nucleon with meson fields, which makes the nucleon selfenergy both momentum and density dependent. The model achieves compatibility with microscopic nuclear matter calculations and Dirac phenomenology using a single cut-off parameter, though the abstract's claim of reproducing the correct behavior of the proton-nucleus potential applies specifically to the real part and is demonstrated only for the isoscalar sector.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian density where the interaction terms contain an exponential operator of the nucleon field derivatives, regulated by a cut-off parameter Λ. They derive the generalized Euler-Lagrange equations and Noether theorem for Lagrangians with higher-order derivatives, then apply the formalism to infinite nuclear matter in the mean-field approximation. The coupled equations for the meson fields and the Dirac equation are solved self-consistently as functions of Fermi momentum and nucleon momentum.

## Key numbers

- Cut-off parameter Λ = 0.770 GeV is chosen for quantitative results
- At baryon density ρ_B = 2ρ_sat, nucleons contribute about 92% and mesons 8% to the baryon density
- The proton-nucleus optical potential levels off at proton energies of about 700-800 MeV (cited from prior work by Hama et al.)
- The Dirac-Brueckner approach reproduces the optical potential for energies up to approximately 350 MeV (cited from prior work)

## Context

This work extends the linear derivative coupling approach of Zimanyi and Moszkowski (1989) and Typel (2005) by including all higher-order derivative terms through an exponential resummation, rather than truncating at linear order. The NLD model provides a covariant framework that generates both density and momentum dependence of nucleon selfenergies with a single parameter, in contrast to approaches requiring separate density-dependent couplings (Toki et al., Sugahara et al.) or explicit Fock terms (Cassing et al.). The model enables practical applications to nuclear matter and heavy-ion collisions where the full Dirac-Brueckner approach is too complex, particularly at high energies and densities.
