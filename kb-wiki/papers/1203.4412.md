# Amplitude analysis of gamma n --> pi- p data above 1 GeV

> We report a new extraction of nucleon resonance couplings using pi- photoproduction cross sections on the neutron. The world database for the process gamma n --> pi- p above 1 GeV has quadrupled with the addition of new differential cross sections from the CEBAF Large Acceptance Spectrometer (CLAS) at Jefferson Lab in Hall B. Differential cross sections from CLAS have been improved with a new final-state interaction determination using a diagramatic technique taking into account the NN and piN final-state interaction amplitudes. Resonance couplings have been extracted and compared to previous determinations. With the addition of these new cross sections, significant changes are seen in the high-energy behavior of the SAID cross sections and amplitudes.

## Key claim

The addition of 855 new differential cross sections from CLAS for the reaction γ n → π⁻ p above 1 GeV has quadrupled the world database for this process, but the extracted neutron-target resonance couplings remain model-dependent, as shown by the fact that two different fit forms (GB12 and GZ12) give significantly different values for some states, such as the N(1650)1/2⁻ and N(1720)3/2⁺, and even opposite signs for the imaginary parts of corresponding multipoles.

## Method

The authors extracted γ n → π⁻ p differential cross sections from CLAS data on the reaction γ d → π⁻ p p using a diagrammatic final-state interaction (FSI) technique that includes NN and πN FSI amplitudes, accounting for the experimental cut that rejects protons with momenta below 200 MeV/c. These new cross sections were incorporated into the SAID partial-wave analysis (PWA) database and fit using two different parameterizations: the GB12 solution (multi-channel K-matrix with a phenomenological term) and the GZ12 solution (unified Chew-Mandelstam parameterization including πN, πΔ, ρN, and ηN channels). Resonance couplings were extracted and compared to previous SN11 determinations and PDG averages.

## Key numbers

- The CLAS data set added 855 differential cross sections for γ n → π⁻ p between photon energies of 1.05 and 3.5 GeV, quadrupling the world database above 1 GeV.
- The overall systematic uncertainty of the CLAS data varies from 5.8% at the lowest photon energy to 9.4% at the highest photon energy.
- The FSI correction factor R is less than 1, with the effect (1-R) less than 10%, and the contribution of FSI calculations to overall systematics is estimated at 2% below 1800 MeV, 3% above 1800 MeV, and 5% above 2700 MeV.
- The total pion photoproduction database below Eγ = 2.7 GeV consists of 26179 data points, fit with χ² = 54832 (GB12) and 50998 (GZ12).
- The 626 new CLAS γ n → π⁻ p data points up to Eγ = 2.7 GeV contribute χ² = 1580 (GB12) and 1190 (GZ12), compared to a starting χ² = 45636 using the previous SN11 solution.
- For the N(1535)1/2⁻ state, the GB12 fit gives A₁/₂ = −58 ± 6 (in units of 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²), while the GZ12 fit gives −85 ± 15.
- For the N(1650)1/2⁻ state, the GB12 fit gives A₁/₂ = −40 ± 10, while the GZ12 fit gives a value about 50% lower in magnitude.
- For the N(1440)1/2⁺ state, the GB12 fit gives A₁/₂ = 48 ± 4, compared to the SN11 value of 45 ± 15 and PDG average of 40 ± 10.

## Context

This work extends the SAID partial-wave analysis of single-pion photoproduction, previously applied to proton-target data and to neutron-target data with the SN11 solution (Strakovsky et al., 2011), by incorporating the new CLAS γ n → π⁻ p cross sections. The new data require large adjustments to the high-energy behavior of the SAID cross sections and amplitudes, and the two different fit forms (GB12 and GZ12) reveal significant model dependence in the extracted neutron resonance couplings, particularly for the N(1650)1/2⁻ and N(1720)3/2⁺ states. Future improvements await new beam-asymmetry measurements from CLAS for γ n → π⁻ p at energies from 910 to 2400 MeV, which should further constrain the neutron multipoles.
