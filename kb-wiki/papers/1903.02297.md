# In-medium heavy quarkonium from lattice NRQCD

> We present the final results from a multi-year study of the in-medium spectral properties of heavy quarkonium bound states on the lattice. In this work we combine high statistics $N_f=2+1$ ensembles from the HotQCD collaboration with the effective theory NRQCD and improved Bayesian spectral reconstruction methods. We corroborate with high precision the hierarchical in-medium modification of quarkonium states with respect to their vacuum binding energy and provide updated values on melting temperatures. In particular we are able to understand previous disagreements between different Bayesian methods as resulting from underestimated systematic uncertainties. The main quantitative result is a robust determination of the in-medium mass shifts of quarkonium ground states, which we find are negative, consistent with the behavior observed in strongly coupled pNRQCD potential based computations.

# In-medium heavy quarkonium from lattice NRQCD

## Key claim
The in-medium mass shifts of heavy quarkonium ground states are negative (i.e., masses decrease in the medium), consistent with strongly coupled pNRQCD potential-based computations, but only after correctly accounting for systematic artifacts from truncating the Euclidean time extent of the correlators; previous studies that reported positive shifts used an incorrect baseline.

## Method
The authors combined high-statistics \(N_f=2+1\) lattice QCD ensembles from the HotQCD collaboration with non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) for heavy quarks, using a Lagrangian up to \(\mathcal{O}(v^4)\). They computed Euclidean correlation functions for bottomonium and charmonium in multiple quantum-number channels, then performed Bayesian spectral reconstructions using three methods, Maximum Entropy Method (MEM), the standard BR method, and a novel smooth variant of the BR method that penalizes arc length, to extract in-medium spectral functions and bound-state properties.

## Key numbers
- Pion mass of the ensembles: \(m_\pi = 161\) MeV
- Temperature range explored: \(T \in [140, 407]\) MeV for bottomonium; \(T \in [140, 251]\) MeV for charmonium
- Vacuum binding energies: \(\Upsilon\) \(E^{T=0}_{\rm bind} = 1.1\) GeV; \(\chi_{b1}\) \(E^{T=0}_{\rm bind} \approx 640\) MeV; \(J/\Psi\) \(E^{T=0}_{\rm bind} \approx 640\) MeV; \(\chi_{c1}\) \(E^{T=0}_{\rm bind} \approx 200\) MeV
- Correlator ratio deviation at highest temperature: \(\Upsilon\) at \(T=407\) MeV shows \(1.75\%\) deviation; \(\chi_{b1}\) at \(T=407\) MeV shows \(6.5\%\) deviation; \(J/\Psi\) at \(T=251\) MeV shows \(5\%\) deviation; \(\chi_{c1}\) at \(T=251\) MeV shows \(13\%\) deviation
- Melting temperature window for \(\chi_{b1}\): \(T_{\rm melt} \in [185, 223]\) MeV
- Melting temperature window for \(J/\Psi\): \(T_{\rm melt} \in [200, 210]\) MeV
- Melting temperature for \(\chi_{c1}\): \(T_{\rm melt} \lessapprox 185\) MeV
- Systematic uncertainty band from \(T=0\) mass splittings: at most \(35\) MeV

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous lattice NRQCD study (Kim 2014) by including charm quarks, significantly higher statistics, and a larger temperature range. It directly addresses and resolves previous disagreements between different Bayesian reconstruction methods (MEM vs. BR) by identifying them as arising from underestimated systematic uncertainties. The results corroborate the hierarchical in-medium modification pattern predicted by potential-based pNRQCD computations (Burnier 2015, Burnier 2016) and provide a robust baseline for interpreting heavy quarkonium suppression in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The study also highlights that improved spectral reconstructions will require anisotropic lattices rather than simply finer isotropic lattice spacings.
