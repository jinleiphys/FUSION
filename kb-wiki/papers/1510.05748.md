# Three-gluon correlator contribution to Sivers asymmetry in SIDIS

> We study the Sivers asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering: the three-gluon correlation function contribution. At the cross section level, we establish the matching between the twist-3 collinear factorization approach and the transverse momentum dependent factorization formalism at the moderate transverse momentum region. The so-called coefficient functions when one expands the quark Sivers function in terms of three-gluon correlation functions are derived, which are essential components in the usual transverse momentum dependent evolution formalism. Finally we calculate the transverse-momentum-weighted spin-dependent differential cross section at the next-to-leading order, from which we identify the off-diagonal/three-gluon correlation contribution to the QCD collinear evolution of the twist-3 Qiu-Sterman function.

# Three-gluon correlator contribution to Sivers asymmetry in SIDIS

## Key claim
The matching between the twist-3 collinear factorization approach and the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization formalism for the three-gluon correlation contribution to the Sivers asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) is established at the cross section level, but only in the moderate transverse momentum region where Lambda_QCD is much less than p_h_perp and p_h_perp is much less than Q.

## Method
The author computed the three-gluon correlation function contribution to the spin-dependent differential cross section for light hadron production in SIDIS at order O(alpha_em alpha_s), using the metric contraction of the hadronic tensor. The quark Sivers function was expanded in terms of the symmetric (O) and antisymmetric (N) three-gluon correlation functions in the perturbative region k_perp much greater than Lambda_QCD, and the resulting expression was matched to the TMD factorization formalism in b-space. Next-to-leading order perturbative QCD corrections to the transverse-momentum-weighted spin-dependent cross section were calculated in n=4-2epsilon dimensions to regularize collinear divergences, with the divergent parts absorbed into the renormalized Qiu-Sterman function via MS-bar regularization.

## Key numbers
- The matching condition holds for moderate transverse momentum satisfying Lambda_QCD << p_h_perp << Q, where Q is the virtuality of the exchanged photon.
- The gluon-to-quark splitting kernel used is P_{q<-g}(hat x) = T_R [hat x^2 + (1-hat x)^2] with T_R = 1/2.
- The coefficient functions for the off-diagonal contribution are: C_{q<-g,1}(hat x) = (alpha_s/4pi)[P_{q<-g}(hat x) ln(c^2/(b^2 mu^2)) + hat x(1-hat x)] and C_{q<-g,2}(hat x) = (alpha_s/4pi)[P_{q<-g}(hat x) ln(c^2/(b^2 mu^2)) - (1/2)(1-6hat x+6hat x^2)], where c = 2e^{-gamma_E}.
- The evolution equation for the Qiu-Sterman function from the off-diagonal contribution is: partial/partial ln mu_f^2 T_{q,F}(x_B, x_B, mu_f^2) = (alpha_s/2pi) integral_{x_B}^1 dx/x^2 P_{q<-g}(hat x) (1/2)[O(x,x,mu_f^2) + O(x,0,mu_f^2) + N(x,x,mu_f^2) - N(x,0,mu_f^2)].

## Context
This work extends the matching between twist-3 collinear factorization and TMD factorization previously established for the quark-gluon correlation (Qiu-Sterman) function in Drell-Yan by Ji, Ma, and Yuan (2006) and in SIDIS by Bacchetta, Boer, Diehl, and Mulders (2008) to the three-gluon correlation function channel. The derived coefficient functions are essential components for the TMD evolution formalism of the Sivers function developed by Kang, Aybat, Echevarria, and others (2011-2014). The evolution kernel for the Qiu-Sterman function from three-gluon correlations agrees with earlier findings by Kang, Braun, and Ma (2008-2012). This enables full NLO phenomenological studies of the Sivers asymmetry in SIDIS and tests of the predicted sign change of the Sivers function between SIDIS and Drell-Yan processes.
