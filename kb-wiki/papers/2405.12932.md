# Small cross section of the synthesis of darmstadtium in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{232}$Th reaction

> The smallness of the cross section of evaporation residues formed in the hot fusion reaction $^{48}$Ca+$^{232}$Th is analyzed by the dinuclear system model (DNS). The capture probability has been calculated by solving the dynamical equations of motion for the relative distance between the centers-of-mass of the DNS nuclei. Fusion of nuclei is considered as evolution of the DNS to a stable compound nucleus. The fusion probability has a bell-like shape and quasifission is one of reasons causing smallness of the yield of the evaporation residues products. Another reason is the decrease of the fission barrier for the isotopes $^{275-285}$Dm related with the shell effects in the neutron structure. The agreement of the theoretical results obtained for the yield of the evaporation residues with the experimental data measured in the Factory of superheavy elements of Joint Institute for Nuclear Research is well.

## Key claim

The smallness of the evaporation residue cross section in the hot fusion reaction $^{48}$Ca+$^{232}$Th is caused by two factors: the dominance of quasifission over complete fusion, and the decrease of the fission barrier for the darmstadtium isotopes $^{275-285}$Dm due to shell effects in the neutron structure, with the neutron number of $^{280}$Ds (N=170) lying between the magic numbers 162 and 184.

## Method

The authors used the dinuclear system model (DNS) to calculate capture, fusion, and evaporation residue cross sections. Capture probabilities were obtained by solving dynamical equations of motion for the relative distance between the centers of mass of the DNS nuclei, including friction coefficients and the nucleus-nucleus potential calculated by the double folding procedure. Fusion was treated as evolution of the DNS to a stable compound nucleus, with the fusion probability calculated from the branching ratio of level densities over the intrinsic fusion barrier, quasifission barrier, and symmetry barrier. The survival probability of the heated and rotating compound nucleus against fission was calculated using the statistical model implemented in KEWPIE2.

## Key numbers

- Maximum experimental cross section for the 4n channel: 0.7$^{+1.1}_{-0.5}$ pb at the synthesis of darmstadtium in the $^{48}$Ca+$^{232}$Th reaction (Oganessian 2023)
- Maximum experimental cross section for the 5n channel: 0.34$^{+0.59}_{-0.16}$ pb (Oganessian 2024)
- Experimental cross section for the cold fusion reaction $^{64}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb: 15$^{+9}_{-6}$ pb (Hofmann 1998)
- Coulomb barrier for the $^{48}$Ca+$^{232}$Th reaction: $V_B$ = 184 MeV for collisions with orientation angle $\alpha_T=30^\circ$ and 198 MeV for $\alpha_T=90^\circ$
- Excitation energy of the compound nucleus at the maximum of the 4n channel: $E_{\rm CN}^*$ = 40 MeV, corresponding to $E_{\rm lab}$ = 237.5 MeV
- Fusion probability for $^{48}$Ca+$^{232}$Th: $P_{\rm CN} = 8.3 \times 10^{-3}$
- Fusion probability for $^{64}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb: $P_{\rm CN} = 10^{-5}$ (Giardina 2000)
- Fission barrier for $^{272}$Ds: 5.62 MeV; for $^{280}$Ds: 3.29 MeV (Kowal 2010)

## Context

This work extends the DNS model analysis of superheavy element synthesis to the $^{48}$Ca+$^{232}$Th reaction, which produces darmstadtium isotopes with neutron numbers between the magic shells. The results are compared with the cold fusion reaction $^{64}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb studied by Hofmann (1998) and Giardina (2000), where the compound nucleus $^{272}$Ds has a magic neutron number N=162 and a correspondingly higher fission barrier. The calculations agree well with experimental data from the Factory of Superheavy Elements at JINR (Oganessian 2023, 2024). The work enables better understanding of the optimal conditions for synthesizing new superheavy elements by identifying the roles of quasifission dominance and shell-effect-driven fission barrier suppression.
