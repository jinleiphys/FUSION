# Hollowness in pp scattering at the LHC

> We examine how the effect of hollowness in pp scattering at the LHC (minimum of the inelasticity profile at zero impact parameter) depends on modeling of the phase of the elastic scattering amplitude as a function of the momentum transfer. We study the cases of the constant phase, the Bailly, and the so called standard parameterizations. It is found that the 2D hollowness holds in the first two cases, whereas the 3D hollowness is a robust effect, holding for all explored cases.

# Hollowness in pp scattering at the LHC

## Key claim
The 2D hollowness effect (minimum of the inelasticity profile at zero impact parameter) in pp scattering at LHC energies depends on the modeling of the phase of the elastic scattering amplitude as a function of momentum transfer, holding for constant and Bailly parameterizations but not for the standard parameterization; however, the 3D hollowness is a robust effect that holds for all explored phase models.

## Method
The authors parametrize separately the absolute value and the phase of the strong elastic pp scattering amplitude, fitting the absolute value to LHC differential elastic cross section data at √s = 7 TeV using a form from Fagundes (2013). They then compute the inelasticity profile in impact-parameter space via Fourier-Bessel transform and the eikonal approximation, testing three phase parameterizations: constant (ρ₀ = 0.14), Bailly et al. (1987) with t_d = 0.52 GeV², and a standard parametrization with t₀ = 0.5 GeV² and τ = 0.1 GeV².

## Key numbers
- ρ(7 TeV, 0) = 0.145(100) from TOTEM (2013) for the ratio of real to imaginary parts of the elastic amplitude at t = 0
- t_d = 0.52 GeV² for the position of the diffractive minimum in the Bailly parametrization
- t₀ = 0.5 GeV² and τ = 0.1 GeV² for the standard parametrization parameters
- ρ₀ = 0.14 used for the constant phase model

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Ruiz Arriola, 2016) and talks (Arriola, 2016; Broniowski, 2017) by examining the sensitivity of the hollowness effect to the phase modeling, which was not treated with sufficient attention in prior studies by Alkin (2014), Dremin (2014-2017), Anisovich (2014), Albacete (2016), and Troshin (2016-2017). The results enable future experimental determination of the 2D hollowness through refined Coulomb separation analyses, while establishing the 3D hollowness as a robust quantum-mechanical feature of the scattering mechanism.
