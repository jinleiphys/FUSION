# Charge symmetry violation: a NNLO study of partonic observables

> Charge and isospin symmetry violations to valence and sea distribution functions in the nucleon are evaluated (at low resolution scale) by means of a meson cloud model and light-cone quark wave functions. Their perturbative evolution are implemented at Next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using an original evolution code in order to include the perturbatively generated strange - anti-strange asymmetry typical of the three loop evolution expansion. Charge symmetry violating QED effects are also added and the distributions, evolved at the experimental scale, are compared with available information. The role of non-perturbative effects is emphasized in the interpretation of the, so called, NuTeV anomaly and new experiments at very-high energy.

## Key claim

Charge symmetry violation (CSV) effects from quark mass differences and QED radiation, evaluated within a unified meson-cloud and light-front quark model and evolved at NNLO, can reduce the NuTeV anomaly to less than 1 sigma, but the result depends critically on the poorly known strange-antistrange asymmetry and on the inclusion of NNLO evolution.

## Method

The author constructs parton distribution functions at a low hadronic scale using a relativistic light-front constituent quark model with an hypercentral potential, surrounded by a meson cloud (including pion, kaon, and Lambda fluctuations) to incorporate non-perturbative sea quarks. These distributions are evolved to experimental scales using an original NNLO QCD evolution code that includes the perturbatively generated strange-antistrange asymmetry from three-loop evolution, and QED splitting contributions are added via leading-order photon radiation evolution equations.

## Key numbers

- Second moments of valence CSV from lattice QCD (Horsley et al., 2011): ⟨x δu_V⟩ = -0.0023(6) and ⟨x δd_V⟩ = +0.0020(3) at Q² = 4 GeV²
- Starting scale for scenario A (no strange sea): Q₀² = 0.149 GeV², with ⟨x[u_V + d_V]⟩ = 0.890 and ⟨x[2ū + 2d̅]⟩ = 0.110
- Starting scale for scenario B (with strange sea): Q₀² = 0.161 GeV², with ⟨x[u_V + d_V]⟩ = 0.849 and ⟨x[2ū + 2d̅ + s + s̅]⟩ = 0.151
- Strange asymmetry at Q₀² in scenario B: ⟨x(s - s̅)⟩ = -0.0057
- Strange asymmetry at Q² = 4 GeV²: ⟨x(s - s̅)⟩ = -0.0039 (scenario B) and -0.0015 (scenario A, vanishing strange sea at Q₀²)
- Valence CSV moments from mass difference at Q₀²: ⟨x δu_V|_mass⟩ = -0.0018, ⟨x δd_V|_mass⟩ = 0.0044
- Total CSV correction to the Paschos-Wolfenstein ratio: ΔR^CSV = -0.0018 ± 0.0001 (from the functional analysis)
- Corrected weak mixing angle: sin²θ_W = 0.2246 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0020 (syst)

## Context

This work extends earlier CSV studies by Sather (1992) and Rodionov et al. (1994) using MIT bag models, and the phenomenological global fit by Martin et al. (MRST, 2004), by providing a unified theoretical framework that simultaneously treats mass-difference and QED CSV effects. It also addresses the NuTeV anomaly, where the measured sin²θ_W = 0.2277 ± 0.0013 (stat) ± 0.0009 (syst) deviates from the world fit value of 0.2227 ± 0.0004. The results are compared with the reassessment by Bentz et al. (2010) and enable predictions for future CSV measurements at a possible LHeC electron-proton collider as proposed by Hobbs et al. (2011).
