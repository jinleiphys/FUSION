# Effective Lagrangian Approach to pion photoproduction from the nucleon

> We present a pion photoproduction model on the free nucleon based on an Effective Lagrangian Approach (ELA) which includes the nucleon resonances ($Δ(1232)$, N(1440), N(1520), N(1535), $Δ(1620)$, N(1650), and $Δ(1700)$), in addition to Born and vector meson exchange terms. The model incorporates a new theoretical treatment of spin-3/2 resonances, first introduced by Pascalutsa, avoiding pathologies present in previous models. Other main features of the model are chiral symmetry, gauge invariance, and crossing symmetry. We use the model combined with modern optimization techniques to assess the parameters of the nucleon resonances on the basis of world data on electromagnetic multipoles. We present results for electromagnetic multipoles, differential cross sections, asymmetries, and total cross sections for all one pion photoproduction processes on free nucleons. We find overall agreement with data from threshold up to 1 GeV in laboratory frame.

## Key claim

The authors present a pion photoproduction model based on an Effective Lagrangian Approach that achieves overall agreement with world data from threshold up to 1 GeV in laboratory frame, but the model's success is conditional on the specific choice of gauge-invariant couplings for spin-3/2 resonances (first introduced by Pascalutsa) which avoid pathologies present in previous off-shell extension frameworks.

## Method

The model constructs fully relativistic, chiral symmetric, gauge invariant, and crossing symmetric invariant amplitudes from effective Lagrangians for Born terms, vector meson exchange (rho and omega), and seven nucleon resonances (Delta(1232), N(1440), N(1520), N(1535), Delta(1620), N(1650), and Delta(1700)). Spin-3/2 resonances are treated using gauge-invariant couplings that satisfy the condition p_mu O^{mu...}=0, replacing the traditional off-shell extension approach of Nath, Etemadi, and Kimel. The model parameters are determined by fitting to world data on electromagnetic multipoles using modern optimization techniques.

## Key numbers

- Pseudovector coupling constant: f^2_{pi N}/4 pi = 0.0749
- Leptonic decay constant of the pion: f_pi = 92.3 MeV
- Nucleon form factors at photon point: F_1^S = F_1^V = 1, F_2^S = kappa^p + kappa^n = -0.12, F_2^V = kappa^p - kappa^n = 3.70
- Energy range of the model: from threshold up to 1 GeV in laboratory frame
- Seven nucleon resonances included: Delta(1232), N(1440), N(1520), N(1535), Delta(1620), N(1650), and Delta(1700)

## Context

This work extends the Effective Lagrangian Approach of Moya de Guerra (2000) by implementing the gauge-invariant spin-3/2 treatment developed by Pascalutsa (1998), which resolves pathologies such as Johnson-Sudarshan and Velo-Zwanziger problems present in the traditional off-shell extension framework of Nath, Etemadi, and Kimel (1971). The model enables future studies of resonances in nuclear medium, pion electroproduction, and exclusive X(gamma,N pi)Y processes, and is intended as a first step toward understanding the role of pions and resonances in more complicated processes.
