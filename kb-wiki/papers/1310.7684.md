# One-dimensional $α$ condensation of $α$-linear-chain states in ${^{12}{\rm C}}$ and ${^{16}{\rm O}}$

> We present a new picture that the $α$-linear-chain structure for ${^{12}{\rm C}}$ and ${^{16}{\rm O}}$ has one-dimensional $α$ condensate character. The wave functions of linear-chain states which are described by superposing a large number of Brink wave functions have extremely large overlaps of nearly $100\%$ with single Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave functions, which were proposed to describe the $α$ condensed "gas-like" states. Although this new picture is different from the conventional idea of the spatial localization of $α$ clusters, the density distributions are shown to have localized $α$-clusters which is due to the inter-$α$ Pauli repulsion.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the ideal straight α-linear-chain states in ¹²C and ¹⁶O have a one-dimensional α condensate character, meaning the α clusters occupy an identical lowest orbit of a prolate-deformed mean-field potential in a non-localized manner, rather than being spatially localized as traditionally assumed. This claim is hedged by the fact that the study restricts the α-particle motion to a pure one-dimensional line along the z-axis, and the authors note that real linear-chain states may bend.

## Method

The authors performed generator coordinate method (GCM) calculations by superposing a large number (100 for ¹²C, 300 for ¹⁶O) of Brink wave functions with randomly generated inter-cluster distances, restricting the α particles to lie on a straight line (Rix = Riy = 0). They then computed squared overlaps between these converged Brink-GCM wave functions and single Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave functions, which describe α condensate states, using the Volkov No.2 force with Majorana parameters M=0.59 for ¹²C and M=0.63 for ¹⁶O.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C, the maximum squared overlap between the Brink-GCM wave function and a single THSR wave function for the 0⁺ state is 0.987, at parameters (βx=βy=0.1 fm, βz=5.1 fm), with a THSR energy of -76.6 MeV.
- For ¹⁶O, the maximum squared overlap for the 0⁺ state is 0.944, at parameters (βx=βy=0.1 fm, βz=8.2 fm), with a THSR energy of -89.8 MeV.
- For ¹²C, the maximum squared overlaps for the 2⁺ and 4⁺ states are 0.989 and 0.981, respectively.
- For ¹⁶O, the maximum squared overlaps for the 2⁺ and 4⁺ states are 0.942 and 0.931, respectively.
- The maximum squared overlaps between the GCM wave functions and single Brink wave functions are only 78% for the 0⁺ state of ¹²C and 48% for the 0⁺ state of ¹⁶O.
- The 0⁺ state energy from the Brink-GCM calculation for ¹²C is -76.8 MeV, which is 7.2 MeV above the 3α threshold energy of -83.97 MeV.
- The 0⁺ state energy from the Brink-GCM calculation for ¹⁶O is -90.1 MeV, above the 4α threshold energy of -111.96 MeV.
- The size parameter of the α particle is b=1.376 fm, giving a minimum α particle energy of -28.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the non-localized clustering picture previously applied to the Hoyle state in ¹²C (Funaki, 2003) and to the α+¹⁶O cluster structure in ²⁰Ne (Zhou, 2013; Zhou, 2014) to the α-linear-chain states, which had been the classic example of localized clustering since Morinaga (1956). The results contradict the traditional understanding that linear-chain states require spatially localized α clusters, and instead show that the one-dimensional α condensate character is more essential. This opens the possibility of reinterpreting other cluster systems through the non-localized clustering picture, and the authors note they are investigating momentum distributions to clarify the magnitude of the Pauli effect in these states.
