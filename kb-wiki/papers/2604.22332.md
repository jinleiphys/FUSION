# Confronting Color Glass Condensate at next-to-leading order with HERA data

> We perform a global analysis of HERA total inclusive cross section and charm quark production data to extract the non-perturbative initial condition for the next-to-leading order Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation. We extend our previous analyses to full next-to-leading order + next-to-leading logarithm (NLO+NLL) accuracy by combining the NLO DIS impact factors with the NLO BK equation that includes the resummation of large double and single logarithms. The developed Bayesian setup extracts the posterior that describes the distribution of the parameters that best fit the data. The posterior distribution allows for estimates of the theoretical uncertainty of the dipole amplitude and, hence, offers a streamlined method for propagating uncertainties in the BK initial condition in NLO CGC calculations.

## Key claim

This work extracts the non-perturbative initial condition for the next-to-leading order Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) equation by performing a global fit to HERA inclusive and charm production data, achieving for the first time full NLO+NLL accuracy by combining NLO DIS impact factors with the NLO BK equation that includes resummation of both double and single transverse logarithms. The Bayesian framework provides posterior distributions that enable uncertainty propagation in NLO Color Glass Condensate calculations, though the fit shows some tension with charm production data (chi-squared per degree of freedom of order 2).

## Method

The authors compute the total DIS cross section at NLO accuracy in the dipole picture, including virtual loop corrections to the gamma-to-quark-antiquark splitting and contributions from the quark-antiquark-gluon Fock state, using the unsubtracted scheme. The Bjorken-x dependence of the dipole-target scattering amplitude is obtained by solving the NLO BK equation with resummation of large double and single transverse logarithms, using the Balitsky prescription for the running coupling evaluated at the smallest dipole size. A Bayesian inference framework combining a Gaussian process emulator trained on 500-750 design points with Markov Chain Monte Carlo sampling extracts the posterior distribution of the five model parameters (initial saturation scale squared, anomalous dimension, running coupling scale parameter, proton transverse area, and charm quark mass) by fitting to HERA reduced cross section and charm production data with x <= 0.01 and Q^2 between 2 and 45 GeV^2.

## Key numbers

- Initial saturation scale squared Q_s,0^2 = 0.040 GeV^2 (MAP value for MV^gamma initial condition with Balitsky+smallest dipole running coupling)
- Anomalous dimension gamma = 1.22 (MAP value for MV^gamma initial condition with Balitsky+smallest dipole running coupling)
- Proton transverse area sigma_0/2 = 20.3 mb (MAP value for MV^gamma initial condition with Balitsky+smallest dipole running coupling)
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.27 GeV (MAP value for MV^gamma initial condition with Balitsky+smallest dipole running coupling)
- Running coupling scale parameter log10(C^2) = 0.75 (MAP value for MV^gamma initial condition with Balitsky+smallest dipole running coupling)
- Saturation scale at Y = ln(1/0.01) is Q_s^2 = 0.04 GeV^2 for the MV model initial condition, smaller than the leading order fit by a factor of 7
- NLO result for F_2 is roughly 50% larger than leading logarithmic accuracy at Q^2 = 4.5 GeV^2 and 100% larger at Q^2 = 45 GeV^2
- Proton transverse area in NLO fit is approximately 75% larger than the corresponding leading order fit of Casuga (2023)

## Context

This work extends previous NLO analyses by Casuga (2025) and Hanninen (2022) by using the full NLO BK equation with resummation of both double and single transverse logarithms, rather than the kinematically-constrained BK equation approximation used in earlier work. The Bayesian framework developed in Casuga (2023, 2025) enables systematic uncertainty quantification of the non-perturbative initial condition, which can be propagated to other observables calculated within the CGC framework at NLO accuracy. The results enable more precise tests of the saturation picture at the upcoming Electron-Ion Collider, though the tension in charm production data and the need for large anomalous dimension (gamma > 1) suggest that further theoretical improvements may be needed.
