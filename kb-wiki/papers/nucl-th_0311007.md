# Hadron deformation from Lattice QCD

> We address the issue of hadron deformation within the framework of lattice QCD. For hadrons with spin greater than 1/2 the deformation can be determined by evaluating the charge and matter distributions. Deviation of the nucleon shape from spherical symmetry is determined by evaluating the quadrupole strength in the transition $γN \to Δ(1232)$ both in the quenched and in the unquenched theory.

# Hadron deformation from Lattice QCD

## Key claim

Lattice QCD calculations show that the charge density distribution of the rho meson exhibits a non-zero quadrupole deformation while the matter density distribution does not, suggesting that hadron charge deformation is a relativistic effect rather than a genuine deformation of the hadron's spatial wave function. For the nucleon, deformation is probed indirectly via the electric quadrupole amplitude in the gamma N to Delta(1232) transition, which is found to be non-zero at a level that excludes zero for the first time in a lattice QCD calculation.

## Method

The authors compute two-current correlators on 16^3 x 32 and 32^3 x 64 lattices to extract charge and matter density distributions for the pion, rho, nucleon, and Delta, using both quenched configurations at beta=6.0 and unquenched SESAM configurations at beta=5.6 with two dynamical quark flavors. For the gamma N to Delta transition, they evaluate three-point functions using sequential propagators with both fixed-operator and fixed-source methods, extracting the Sachs form factors G_M1, G_E2, and G_C2 from appropriate combinations of the Delta spin index, current direction, and projection matrices in the Delta rest frame.

## Key numbers

- Rho meson charge deformation parameter delta = 0.03 +/- 0.01 in the chiral limit, defined as delta = (3/4) * <3z^2 - r^2> / <r^2>
- Quenched results at Q^2 = 0.64 GeV^2: G_M1 = 1.72(6) e/2m_N, G_E2 = 0.099(19) e/2m_N, R_EM = -5.1(1.1)%
- Quenched results at Q^2 = 0.13 GeV^2: G_M1 = 2.51(6) e/2m_N, G_E2 = 0.104(12) e/2m_N, R_EM = -4.5(1.4)%
- Unquenched results at Q^2 = 0.53 GeV^2: G_M1 = 1.30(4) e/2m_N, G_E2 = 0.050(31) e/2m_N, R_EM = -2.8(1.6)%
- Unquenching decreases G_M1 and G_E2 but leaves R_EM in the range -(2-4)% for the SESAM quark masses studied
- Finite volume corrections on the 16^3 x 32 lattice are (10-15)% compared to infinite volume, while on the 32^3 x 64 lattice the correction is negligible
- For baryons, the charge radius is approximately 20% larger than the matter radius

## Context

This work extends earlier constituent quark model studies of hadron deformation by Isgur and collaborators, cloudy bag model calculations by Eisenberg, and soliton model approaches, providing a model-independent lattice QCD determination. The finding that charge deformation in the rho is relativistic in origin challenges the interpretation of deformation in phenomenological models such as those invoking two-body current contributions from Buchmann. The gamma N to Delta transition form factor results enable direct comparison with experimental measurements from Bates and Jefferson Lab, and the authors plan to repeat the calculation with lighter pions to investigate pion cloud contributions to the transition form factors.
