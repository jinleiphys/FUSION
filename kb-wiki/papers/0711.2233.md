# Parity-violating two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon interaction

> We calculate in chiral perturbation theory the parity-violating two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials at leading one-loop order. At a distance of $r= m_π^{-1} \simeq 1.4 $fm they amount to about $\pm 16%$ of the parity-violating $1π$-exchange potential. We evaluate also the parity-violating effects arising from $2π$-exchange with excitation of virtual $Δ(1232)$-isobars. These come out to be relatively small in comparison to those from diagrams with only nucleon intermediate states. The reason for this opposite behavior to the parity-conserving case is the blocking of the dominant isoscalar central channel by CP-invariance. Furthermore, we calculate the T-matrix related to the iteration of the parity-violating $1π$-exchange with the parity-conserving one. The analytical results presented in this work can be easily implemented into calculations of parity-violating nuclear observables.

## Key claim

The parity-violating two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential at leading one-loop order amounts to about ±16% of the parity-violating one-pion exchange potential at a distance of 1.4 fm, and the contribution from diagrams with virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitation is relatively small compared to those with only nucleon intermediate states, contrary to the parity-conserving case.

## Method

The author calculates parity-violating two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potentials using chiral perturbation theory at leading one-loop order, computing spectral functions via the Cutkosky cutting rule and reconstructing the T-matrix through once-subtracted dispersion relations. The parity-violating effects from two-pion exchange with virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitation are also evaluated, using the empirically satisfied relation g_{πNΔ} = 3 g_A M_N / (√2 f_π) for the πNΔ coupling constant. Additionally, the T-matrix related to the iteration of parity-violating one-pion exchange with parity-conserving one-pion exchange is calculated.

## Key numbers

- At r = m_π^{-1} ≈ 1.4 fm, the parity-violating two-pion exchange potentials amount to about ±16% of the parity-violating one-pion exchange potential.
- At r = 1.4 fm, the ratio U^{(2π)}/U^{(1π)} = -0.159 and W^{(2π)}/U^{(1π)} = 0.177.
- At r = 1.4 fm, the Δ-induced ratios are U^{(Δ)}/U^{(1π)} = -0.055 and W^{(Δ)}/U^{(1π)} = 0.095.
- The Δ-nucleon mass splitting used is Δ = 293 MeV.
- The nucleon axial vector coupling constant g_A ≈ 1.3, the pion decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV, and the nucleon mass M_N = 939 MeV.
- The parity-violating πNN coupling constant h_π has an experimental upper bound |h_π| < 1.43 × 10^{-7}.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory reformulation of nuclear parity violation (described in prior work by the same community, e.g., the papers cited as pveft and liu) by calculating the long-range tail of the parity-violating two-pion exchange interaction. The results are quantitatively consistent with the findings of Hyun and collaborators, who quantified the combined effect of cut-off regularized two-pion exchange, short-distance counterterms, and a new electromagnetic operator on the photon asymmetry A_γ in n p → d γ as a minus 10-20% correction to the dominant one-pion exchange contribution. The analytical results can be implemented into calculations of parity-violating nuclear observables, particularly for improving the determination of the weak πNN coupling constant h_π from measurements of the photon asymmetry in radiative neutron capture.
