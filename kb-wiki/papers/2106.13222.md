# Short-distance constraints for the longitudinal component of the hadronic light-by-light amplitude: an update

> We reassess the impact of short-distance constraints for the longitudinal component of the hadronic light-by-light amplitude on the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon, $a_μ=(g-2)_μ/2$, by comparing different solutions that have recently appeared in the literature. In particular, we analyze the relevance of the exact axial anomaly and its impact on $a_μ$ and conclude that it remains rather limited. We show that all recently proposed solutions agree well within uncertainties on the numerical estimate of the impact of short-distance constraints on $a_μ$, despite differences in the concrete implementation. We also take into account the recently calculated perturbative corrections to the massless quark loop to update our estimate and outline the path towards future improvements.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that despite significant differences in the implementation of short-distance constraints (SDCs) for the longitudinal component of the hadronic light-by-light amplitude, all recently proposed solutions agree well within uncertainties on the numerical impact on the muon anomalous magnetic moment, and that the exact axial anomaly has only a rather limited numerical relevance for \(a_\mu\).

## Method

The authors compare three different approaches to satisfying short-distance constraints for the longitudinal hadronic light-by-light amplitude: the Melnikov-Vainshtein (MV) model, holographic QCD (hQCD) models (specifically the HW2 model), and a Regge model of excited pseudoscalars. They analyze the function \(G(q_1^2,q_2^2,q_3^2)\) and the longitudinal VVA correlator \(w_L(q^2)\), examining how each model implements the operator product expansion (OPE) constraints and the axial anomaly. The numerical comparison is performed for the isovector component, and the authors also incorporate recently calculated perturbative and non-perturbative corrections to the OPE.

## Key numbers

* The MV model for the longitudinal VVA correlator gives \(w_L^\text{MV}(q^2) = 2 N_C / (q^2 - M_\pi^2)\), with \(N_C = 3\).
* The asymptotic behavior of the pion transition form factor is \(\Fpigg(\hat{q}^2,\hat{q}^2) = -2 F_\pi / (3 \hat{q}^2) + \Order(\hat{q}^{-3})\), where \(F_\pi\) is the pion decay constant.
* The chiral limit OPE result for the longitudinal amplitude is \(\bar{\Pi}_1(\hat{q}^2,\hat{q}^2,q_3^2)|_{m_q=0} = -1/(6\pi^2) \cdot 1/(\hat{q}^2 q_3^2) + \Order(\hat{q}^{-3})\).
* The singly-virtual pion TFF asymptotic limit is \(\Fpig(\hat{q}^2) = -2 F_\pi / \hat{q}^2 + \Order(\hat{q}^{-3})\).
* The HW2 model parameter \(z_0 = (\sqrt{2} \pi F_\pi)^{-1}\).
* The pion decay constant normalization gives \(F_{\pi\gamma\gamma} := \Fpig(0) = 1/(4\pi^2 F_\pi)\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of short-distance constraints for hadronic light-by-light scattering that began with Melnikov and Vainshtein (2003), who first derived SDC1 and pointed out the role of the axial anomaly. The paper reconciles apparent disagreements between the MV model, the holographic QCD models of Leutgeb and Rebhan (2019) and Cappiello et al. (2019), and the Regge model of excited pseudoscalars by Colangelo et al. (2019). The findings confirm and extend earlier studies of relevant momentum regions by Bijnens et al. (2007), and the updated estimate incorporates perturbative corrections to the massless quark loop recently calculated by Bijnens et al. (2021). The work enables future improvements by outlining a path toward incorporating axial-vector contributions into the dispersive formalism for hadronic light-by-light scattering.
