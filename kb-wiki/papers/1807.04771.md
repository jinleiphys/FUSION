# Measurements of directed and elliptic flow for $D^{0}$ and $\overline{D^{0}}$ mesons using the STAR detector at RHIC

> We report on the first evidence for a non-zero rapidity-odd directed flow ($v_{1}$) for $D^{0}$ and $\overline{D^{0}}$ mesons in 10-80\% centrality Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200~GeV measured with the STAR detector at RHIC. The slope of the $v_1$ rapidity dependence ($dv_{1}/dy$) averaged over $D^{0}$ and $\overline{D^{0}}$ mesons is -0.081 $\pm$ 0.021 $\pm$ 0.017, while that of charged kaons is -0.0030 $\pm$ 0.0001 $\pm$ 0.0002, suggesting significantly larger slope for the $D^{0}$ mesons. Models indicate that the large $dv_{1}/dy$ of $D^{0}$ mesons is sensitive to the initially tilted source. We also present a new measurement of the $D^{0}$ meson elliptic flow ($v_2$) as a function of transverse momentum ($p_{T}$) in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 200~GeV with an improved precision with respect to the previously published results.The $D^{0}$ $v_{2}$ results are compared to those of light-flavor hadrons to test the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling. Both the $v_{1}$ and $v_{2}$ results are compared to recent hydrodynamic and transport model calculations.

# Measurements of directed and elliptic flow for \(D^{0}\) and \(\overline{D^{0}}\) mesons using the STAR detector at RHIC

## Key claim

First evidence for a non-zero rapidity-odd directed flow (\(v_1\)) for \(D^{0}\) and \(\overline{D^{0}}\) mesons in 10-80% centrality Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV, with the slope \(dv_1/dy\) averaged over both meson species being -0.081 ± 0.021 ± 0.017, corresponding to a 3\(\sigma\) significance; however, the current precision does not permit firm conclusions about the difference and ordering between \(D^{0}\) and \(\overline{D^{0}}\) mesons that would be sensitive to the initial electromagnetic field.

## Method

\(D^{0}\) and \(\overline{D^{0}}\) mesons were reconstructed via their hadronic decay channels \(D^{0} \rightarrow K^{-}\pi^{+}\) and \(\overline{D^{0}} \rightarrow K^{+}\pi^{-}\) (branching ratio 3.89%) using the STAR Time Projection Chamber and Heavy Flavor Tracker in Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV. The first-order event plane was reconstructed using the Zero-Degree Calorimeter Shower Maximum Detectors, while the second-order event plane used TPC tracks with a pseudorapidity gap of \(\Delta\eta > 0.05\) relative to the reconstructed \(D^{0}\) to suppress non-flow effects. The \(v_1\) and \(v_2\) coefficients were calculated using the event-plane method, with yields weighted by the inverse of reconstruction efficiency times acceptance, and corrected for event-plane resolution.

## Key numbers

- Slope \(dv_1/dy\) averaged over \(D^{0}\) and \(\overline{D^{0}}\) mesons: -0.081 ± 0.021 (stat.) ± 0.017 (syst.) in 10-80% centrality Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV with \(p_T > 1.5\) GeV/\(c\)
- Slope \(dv_1/dy\) for charged kaons: -0.0030 ± 0.0001 (stat.) ± 0.0002 (syst.) under the same conditions, giving a \(D^{0}\) slope about 20 times larger (2.9\(\sigma\) significance)
- Slope \(dv_1/dy\) for \(D^{0}\) mesons separately: -0.102 ± 0.030 (stat.) ± 0.021 (syst.)
- Slope \(dv_1/dy\) for \(\overline{D^{0}}\) mesons separately: -0.061 ± 0.030 (stat.) ± 0.023 (syst.)
- The combined 2014 and 2016 datasets provided about a 30% improvement in statistical precision for \(v_2\) measurements compared to previously published results using 2014 data alone

## Context

This work extends previous STAR measurements of \(D^{0}\) elliptic flow from 2014 data (STAR Collaboration, 2017) by combining with 2016 data for improved precision, and presents the first directed flow measurement for charm mesons at RHIC. The results test predictions from a hydrodynamic model incorporating Langevin dynamics for heavy quarks with an initially tilted source (Chattopadhyay and Bozek, 2018), which predicts larger \(v_1\) for heavy flavors compared to light ones, and models of electromagnetic-field-induced \(v_1\) splitting between charm and anti-charm quarks (Das and Greco, 2018; Gursoy, 2014). The \(v_2\) results enable improved tests of number-of-constituent-quark scaling for charm quarks and comparisons with multiple transport and hydrodynamic models (SUBATECH, TAMU, Duke, 3D viscous hydro, LBT, PHSD, Catania), with the TAMU model requiring charm quark diffusion to describe the data.
