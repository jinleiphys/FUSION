# A particle-number-conserving solution to the generalized pairing problem

> An exact, number-conserving solution to the generalized, orbit-dependent pairing problem is derived by introducing an infinite-dimensional algebra. A method for obtaining eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the corresponding Hamiltonian is also given. The relevance of the orbit-dependent pairing solution is demonstrated by comparing predictions of the model with shell-model calculations.

# Particle-number-conserving solution to the generalized pairing problem

## Key claim
The paper derives an exact, particle-number-conserving solution to the generalized pairing problem, but only for the case of a **separable** orbit-dependent pairing interaction (where the pair-pair interaction strength factorizes as \(c_{jj'} = c_j^* c_{j'}\)), not for the fully general orbit-dependent pairing Hamiltonian. The authors argue that this separability assumption yields good approximations to the more general theory, as demonstrated by comparisons with shell-model calculations.

## Method
The authors introduce an infinite-dimensional algebra generated by operators \(S^\mu_m\) (\(\mu = 0, +, -; m = 0, \pm 1, \pm 2, \ldots\)) that satisfy commutation relations similar to an affine Lie algebra without central extension. Using this algebra, they derive a system of coupled algebraic equations (Eqs. 19-20 for nonzero eigenvalues, Eqs. 23-24 for zero eigenvalues) that simultaneously determine the eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the separable pairing Hamiltonian for any number of pairs. The eigenvectors are expressed as products of generalized pair creation operators \(S^+_{x_i}\) acting on the pairing vacuum.

## Key numbers
- For the \(ds\)-shell test (\(0d_{5/2}\), \(0d_{3/2}\), and \(1s_{1/2}\) orbitals), the generalized pairing coefficients were: \(c_{d_{5/2}} = 0.570243\), \(c_{d_{3/2}} = 0.626089\), and \(c_{s_{1/2}} = 0.531823\).
- The interaction strength was set to \(|G| = 3.12778\) to reproduce the two-particle ground-state energy.
- Wave functions obtained with the method have better than 94% overlap with shell-model states (96% for the ground state).
- The quasi-spin approximation (QSA) gave ground-state energies of -6.389, -10.684, and -12.788 for \(N = 1, 2, 3\) pairs, respectively (units not explicitly stated but consistent with the shell-model Hamiltonian scale).

## Context
This work extends the quasi-spin formalism (Racah, 1940s) and the generalized pairing concept of Talmi (1970s) to provide an exact number-conserving solution, addressing limitations of BCS methods noted by Kerman and Lawson (1970s) for small nucleon numbers where particle-number nonconservation introduces spurious states. The method improves upon earlier number-conserving approaches for well-deformed nuclei (which limited each orbital to a single pair) and complements approximate Lipkin-Nogami projection techniques (Pradhan et al., 1973; Nogami, 1964). The solution enables exact treatment of pairing correlations in pair-rich systems where full shell-model diagonalization is computationally prohibitive, and can be applied to any number of pairs in any model space.
