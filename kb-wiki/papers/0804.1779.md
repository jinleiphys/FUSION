# Octet baryon magnetic moments from QCD sum rules

> A comprehensive study is made for the magnetic moments of octet baryons in the method of QCD sum rules. A complete set of QCD sum rules is derived using the external field method and generalized interpolating fields. For each member, three sum rules are constructed from three independent tensor structures. They are analyzed in conjunction with the corresponding mass sum rules. The performance of each of the sum rules is examined using the criteria of OPE convergence and ground-state dominance, along with the role of the transitions in intermediate states. Individual contributions from the u, d and s quarks are isolated and their implications in the underlying dynamics are explored. Valid sum rules are identified and their predictions are obtained. The results are compared with experiment and previous calculations.

## Key claim

The paper claims to provide a comprehensive, independent calculation of octet baryon magnetic moments using QCD sum rules with generalized interpolating fields, but the results depend on the choice of mixing parameter β and the specific sum rule (tensor structure) used, with only "valid sum rules" identified and used for predictions.

## Method

The authors use the external field method in QCD sum rules, introducing a constant background electromagnetic field and computing the linear response of the two-point correlation function. They employ generalized interpolating fields with a mixing parameter β for each octet baryon, derive three independent sum rules from three tensor structures (WE₁, WO₁, WO₂), and perform a Monte-Carlo analysis to extract magnetic moments while accounting for transition contributions between ground and excited states.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment: μ_p = 2.79 μ_N (experimental value, used for comparison)
- Neutron magnetic moment: μ_n = -1.91 μ_N (experimental value, used for comparison)
- The strange quark mass is taken as m_s ≠ 0 while m_u = m_d = 0
- The quark condensate parameter a = -(2π)² ⟨ūu⟩
- The gluon condensate parameter b = ⟨g_c² G²⟩
- The mixed condensate parameter ⟨ūg_cσ·G u⟩ = -m₀² ⟨ūu⟩
- Quark charge factors: e_u = 2/3, e_d = -1/3, e_s = -1/3
- The renormalization scale μ = 500 MeV
- The vacuum susceptibility χ has dimension GeV⁻², while κ and ξ are dimensionless

## Context

This work extends the earlier QCD sum rule calculations of octet baryon magnetic moments by Balitsky (1983), Ioffe (1984), Chiu (1986), Pasupathy (1986), and Wilson (1987), which were done more than 20 years prior. The authors improve upon these by using generalized interpolating fields with optimal mixing, deriving sum rules at all three tensor structures (not just one), performing Monte-Carlo analysis, and properly treating transition terms in intermediate states. The results enable future studies of individual quark contributions to baryon magnetic moments and their implications for quark-gluon dynamics.
