# Systematic Power Counting in Cutoff Effective Field Theories for Nucleon-Nucleon Interactions and the Equivalence With PDS

> An analytic expression for the ${}^1S_0$ phase shifts in nucleon-nucleon scattering is derived in the context of the Schrödinger equation in configuration space with a short distance cutoff and with a consistent power counting scheme including pionic effects. The scheme treats the pion mass and the inverse scattering length over the intrinsic short distance scale as small parameters. Working at next-to-leading order in this scheme, we show that the expression obtained is identical to one obtained using the recently introduced PDS approach which is based on dimensional regularization with a novel subtraction scheme. This strongly supports the conjecture that the schemes are equivalent provided one works to the same order in the power counting.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a cutoff effective field theory (EFT) for nucleon-nucleon interactions, implemented in configuration space with a short-distance cutoff and a systematic power counting scheme, yields an expression for the ${}^1S_0$ phase shift at next-to-leading order that is identical to the result from the PDS (pole divergent subtraction) scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), provided both treatments implement the same power counting rules and work to the same order.

## Method

The authors implement a cutoff in configuration space by introducing a separation length scale $R$, parameterizing short-distance physics for $r < R$ and calculating long-distance effects for $r > R$ using the Schrödinger equation with the one-pion exchange potential. They expand the wave function in a Born-like series, impose boundary conditions at $R$, and derive an expression for $k \cot(\delta)$ at next-to-leading order (order $Q^2$), treating $m_\pi$, $1/a$, and $k$ as all of order $Q$ and $m_\pi R$ as a small parameter of order $Q/\Lambda$. They then compare this result term-by-term with the PDS result from Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), matching the coefficients that encode short-distance physics.

## Key numbers

* The ${}^1S_0$ scattering length is approximately 23 fm, and the ${}^3S_1$ scattering length is approximately 6 fm, compared to the hadronic scale $m_\rho^{-1} \sim 0.25$ fm.
* The pion decay constant used is $f_\pi \approx 93$ MeV, which is $1/\sqrt{2}$ times the $f_\pi$ used in Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998).
* The inverse scattering length $1/a$ is of order 10 MeV in the ${}^1S_0$ channel, setting the scale at which a purely perturbative treatment breaks down.

## Context

This work extends the cutoff EFT approach to nucleon-nucleon interactions, previously developed by Ordonez and van Kolck (1992-1996), Park, Min, and Rho (1995), and Cohen, Friar, Miller, and van Kolck (1996), by demonstrating that a systematic power counting scheme can be implemented straightforwardly. It directly addresses the "unnatural scattering length" problem identified by Weinberg (1990-1992) and shows equivalence with the PDS scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), thereby validating both formalisms. This enables future work to use either approach for systematic calculations of nuclear observables with controlled error estimates.
