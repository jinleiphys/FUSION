# Test of a modified BCS theory performance in the Picket Fence Model

> Analyses of a modified BCS (MBCS) theory performance at finite temperatures in the Picket Fence Model (PFM) for light and heavy systems is presented. Both symmetric, $Ω=N$ ($N$ particles on $Ω$ twice-degenerate levels), and asymmetric, $Ω\neq N$, versions of the PFM are considered. The quantities known exactly from particle-hole symmetry of the $Ω=N$ PFM are calculated. Starting from very low temperatures, these quantities are found in dramatic deviation from the exact values in MBCS results. Consequences of the MBCS prediction that heating generates a thermal constituent of the pairing gap, are discussed. Thermodynamical inconsistency of the MBCS is also addressed.

# Test of a modified BCS theory performance in the Picket Fence Model

## Key claim

The modified BCS (MBCS) theory produces a pairing gap that appears visually reasonable in some temperature interval [0, T_M], but analysis of other quantities (quasiparticle energies, occupation probabilities, Fermi surface energy, and entropy) shows that the MBCS predictions deviate dramatically from exact results starting from very low temperatures, making the theory unreliable even in the temperature range where the gap looks acceptable.

## Method

The authors test the MBCS theory in the Picket Fence Model (PFM), a pairing Hamiltonian applied to N fermions on Ω twice-degenerate equidistant levels, considering both symmetric (Ω = N) and asymmetric (Ω ≠ N) systems with N ranging from 6 to 100. They compare MBCS predictions for quantities known exactly from particle-hole symmetry in the Ω = N PFM, including quasiparticle energies, occupation probabilities, Fermi surface energy, and thermal occupation numbers, and also examine the thermodynamic consistency of the MBCS by comparing entropy calculated from its thermodynamic definition versus the single-particle entropy formula.

## Key numbers

- The MBCS pairing gap for N = 14 particles with Ω = N + 1 levels remains small and almost constant up to T_M = 7.2 MeV, but adding one more extra level (k = 2) reduces T_M to 2.15 MeV.
- In the conventional PFM with N = Ω = 10, the MBCS predicts a phase transition at T ≈ 1.75 MeV, characterized by a sharp increase in the pairing gap, a sharp decrease in system energy, and a discontinuity in specific heat.
- The ratios of quasiparticle-number fluctuations δN_i/δN_{-i} and occupation number ratios n_i/n_{-i} deviate from the exact value of 1 by a few thousand percent near the conventional BCS critical temperature T_c.
- For T > 100 keV in the N = Ω = 10 system, the exact thermodynamic entropy, BCS thermodynamic entropy, BCS single-particle entropy, and MBCS single-particle entropy are all approximately equal, while the MBCS thermodynamic entropy is much smaller (S_th^MBCS << S_sp^MBCS).
- In calculations for ¹²⁰Sn, the MBCS thermodynamic entropy and single-particle entropy differ by two orders of magnitude.

## Context

This work extends the critique of the MBCS theory previously raised in Ponomarev and Vdovin (2005) and Ponomarev and Vdovin (2006), and directly contradicts the claims of Dang and Arima (2003a) and Dang (2007) that the MBCS is applicable in an extended temperature range when Ω = N + 1. The authors show that this single exceptional case (Ω = N + 1) is not representative, as all other asymmetric PFM configurations produce unphysical phase transitions at much lower temperatures. The paper demonstrates that the MBCS violates fundamental particle-hole symmetry of the conventional PFM and is thermodynamically inconsistent, which prevents its reliable use for nuclear pairing at finite temperatures.
