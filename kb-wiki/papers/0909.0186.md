# Antiproton-nucleus electromagnetic annihilation as a way to access the proton timelike form factors

> Contrary to the reaction pbar + p --> e+ e- with a high momentum incident antiproton on a free target proton at rest, in which the invariant mass M of the (e+ e-) pair is necessarily much larger than the (pbar p) mass, in the reaction pbar + d --> n e+ e- the value of M can take values near or below the (pbar p) mass. In the antiproton-deuteron electromagnetic annihilation, this allows to access the proton electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region of q^2 near the (pbar p) threshold. We estimate the cross section dsigma(pbar +d --> e+ e- n)/dM for an antiproton beam momentum of 1.5 GeV/c. We find that near the (pbar p) threshold this cross section is about 1 pb/MeV. The case of heavy nuclei target is also discussed. Elements of experimental feasibility are presented for the process pbar + d --> n e+ e- in the context of the Panda project.

## Key claim

The reaction \(\bar{p} + d \to e^+ e^- + n\) can access the proton electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region of \(q^2\) near or below the \(\bar{p}p\) threshold, even with a fast incident antiproton beam, because the off-shellness of the bound proton in the deuteron allows the invariant mass of the \(e^+ e^-\) pair to take values near \(2m\) that are kinematically forbidden for a free target proton at rest.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section \(d\sigma_{\bar{p}d \to e^+ e^- n}/d\mathcal{M}\) in the impulse approximation, using the deuteron wave function from Carbonell (1995) and treating the proton as pointlike (form factors set to 1) to isolate the nuclear effect. The cross section is expressed as a product of the free \(\bar{p}p \to e^+ e^-\) annihilation cross section and a distribution \(\eta(\mathcal{M})\) that accounts for the momentum distribution of the bound proton in the deuteron. For heavier nuclei, the deuteron momentum distribution was replaced by nuclear momentum distributions from Antonov (2004, 2006), multiplied by the number of protons \(Z\), and an estimate of antiproton absorption in the nucleus was applied using the Glauber approach.

## Key numbers

* For an antiproton beam momentum of 1.5 GeV/c on a deuteron target, the differential cross section at the \(\bar{p}p\) threshold (\(\mathcal{M} = 2m = 1880\) MeV) is approximately 1 pb/MeV.
* The cross section integrated over a 100 MeV bin centered on the threshold (1830–1930 MeV) is about 100 pb.
* The total cross section integrated over all \(\mathcal{M}\) for the deuteron target, with pointlike nucleons, is 43 nb.
* For heavier nuclei at \(\mathcal{M} = 1880\) MeV, the differential cross section scales as approximately \(6.5 \, Z\) pb/MeV, yielding about 39 pb for \(^{12}\)C, 0.17 nb for \(^{56}\)Fe, and 0.5 nb for \(^{197}\)Au over a 1 MeV interval, before accounting for antiproton absorption (which reduces these by a factor of about 2).
* When an effective proton form factor from Aubert (2005) is included, the total cross section on deuteron is about 1 nb, comparable to the free \(\bar{p}p \to e^+ e^-\) cross section at \(\mathcal{M} = 2257\) MeV.

## Context

This work revives an idea explored in the 1980s by Dalkarov (1980) to use a nuclear target to access the under-threshold timelike form factor region, which is kinematically forbidden in free \(\bar{p}p\) annihilation. It extends theoretical studies of off-shell nucleon form factors by Naus (1987) and Tiemeijer (1990), and it is motivated by the future PANDA experiment at the FAIR facility at GSI. The calculation enables experimental searches for predicted structures in the proton form factor near the \(\bar{p}p\) threshold, such as those from quasinuclear vector mesons discussed by Shapiro (1978) and Dalkarov (1989), and it provides a baseline cross section estimate for designing measurements of the reaction \(\bar{p} + d \to n e^+ e^-\).
