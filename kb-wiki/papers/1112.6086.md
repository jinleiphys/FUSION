# Theta vacuum and entanglement interaction in the three-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model

> We investigate theta-vacuum effects on the QCD phase diagram for the realistic 2+1 flavor system, using the three-flavor Polyakov-extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and the entanglement PNJL model as an extension of the PNJL model. The theta-vacuum effects make the chiral transition sharper. For large theta-vacuum angle the chiral transition becomes first order even if the quark number chemical potential is zero, when the entanglement coupling between the chiral condensate and the Polyakov loop is taken into account. We finally propose a way of circumventing the sign problem on lattice QCD with finite theta.

## Key claim

The theta-vacuum effects make the chiral transition sharper, and for large theta-vacuum angle the chiral transition becomes first order even at zero quark number chemical potential, but only when the entanglement coupling between the chiral condensate and the Polyakov loop is taken into account in the EPNJL model.

## Method

The authors used the three-flavor Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and its extension, the entanglement PNJL (EPNJL) model, to study the QCD phase diagram in the presence of a theta vacuum. They performed mean-field calculations of the thermodynamic potential as a function of temperature T, quark chemical potential mu, and theta angle, solving stationary conditions for the chiral condensates sigma_f, pseudoscalar condensates eta_f, and the Polyakov loop Phi.

## Key numbers

- The pseudocritical temperature of the chiral transition at theta=0 and mu=0 is 200 MeV in the PNJL model and 162 MeV in the EPNJL model (both crossover)
- The critical temperature of parity restoration at theta=pi and mu=0 is 194 MeV (second order) in the PNJL model and 158 MeV (first order) in the EPNJL model
- At theta=pi and mu=300 MeV, the critical temperature of parity restoration is 110 MeV in the PNJL model and 99 MeV in the EPNJL model (both first order)
- The NJL model parameters used: m_l=5.5 MeV, m_s=140.7 MeV, Lambda=602.3 MeV, G_S Lambda^2=1.835, G_D Lambda^5=12.36
- The Polyakov potential parameters: a_0=3.51, a_1=-2.47, a_2=15.2, b_3=-1.75, with T_0 rescaled to 195 MeV (PNJL) and 150 MeV (EPNJL)

## Context

This work extends previous two-flavor PNJL and EPNJL studies by Kouno and Sakai (2010-2011) to the realistic 2+1 flavor case, and complements similar analyses using the NJL model by Chatterjee (2011). The results enable predictions for the QCD phase diagram at finite theta, relevant for understanding CP violation in heavy-ion collisions and the early universe, and the authors propose a transformation of the quark fields to minimize the sign problem in lattice QCD calculations with finite theta.
