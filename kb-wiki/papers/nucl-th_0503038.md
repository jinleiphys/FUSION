# Probing the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections with radioactive beams

> Within a transport model we search for potential probes of the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross sections. Traditional measures of the nuclear stopping power are found sensitive to the magnitude but they are ambiguous for determining the isospin dependence of the in-medium NN cross sections. It is shown that isospin tracers, such as the neutron/proton ratio of free nucleons, at backward rapidities/angles in nuclear reactions induced by radioactive beams in inverse kinematics is a sensitive probe of the isospin dependence of the in-medium NN cross sections. At forward rapidities/angles, on the other hand, they are more sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. Measurements of the rapidity/angular dependence of the isospin transport in nuclear reactions will enable a better understanding of the isospin dependence of in-medium nuclear effective interactions.

# Probing the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections with radioactive beams

## Key claim
Within a transport model, the neutron/proton ratio of free nucleons at backward rapidities/angles in nuclear reactions induced by radioactive beams in inverse kinematics is a sensitive probe of the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections, while at forward rapidities/angles this ratio is more sensitive to the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The authors caution that traditional measures of nuclear stopping power, such as the quadruple moment, are sensitive only to the magnitude of the in-medium cross sections and are ambiguous for determining the isospin dependence.

## Method
The authors used an isospin-dependent but momentum-independent transport model (IBUU) to simulate head-on and finite-impact-parameter collisions of a neutron-rich projectile (100Zn) on a symmetric target (40Ca) at beam energies of 100, 200, and 300 MeV/A. They varied the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections by changing the ratio σnp/σpp while keeping angular distributions fixed, and compared results using free-space cross sections, equalized cross sections, and reduced cross sections. The symmetry energy was parameterized as Esym(ρ) = Esym(ρ0)·(ρ/ρ0)^γ with γ = 1 and γ = 2, using Esym(ρ0) = 30 MeV.

## Key numbers
- The free-space σnp/σpp ratio changes from about 2.7 at Elab = 50 MeV to 1.7 at Elab = 300 MeV.
- The symmetry energy parameterization used Esym(ρ0) = 30 MeV with γ = 1 and γ = 2, based on fitting variational many-body calculations by Akmal et al. (1997) who obtained Esym(ρ0) = 32 MeV and γ = 0.6.
- The quadruple moment Qzz was found insensitive to the σnp/σpp ratio when the total number of NN collisions was kept approximately the same, up to about Ebeam/A = 220 MeV.
- At backward angles (cosθ ≤ -0.25) in head-on collisions at 200 MeV/A, the free neutron/proton ratio was significantly higher than the target neutron/proton ratio of 1.0, with the effect most pronounced at low nucleon kinetic energies (below about 100 MeV).
- At forward angles (cosθ > 0.5), the free neutron/proton ratio showed stronger sensitivity to the symmetry energy parameter γ than to the in-medium cross sections.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of nuclear stopping power by Liu et al. (using IQMD model) which proposed the quadruple moment as a probe of the isospin dependence of in-medium NN cross sections; the present authors find that conclusion premature. The paper addresses a gap in experimental knowledge: while strong evidence for reduced in-medium NN cross sections exists from heavy-ion collisions (e.g., studies by Gelbke, Xu, and Danielewicz), all prior analyses assumed an overall reduction of all NN scattering channels without extracting isospin dependence. The results enable future experiments with radioactive beams to simultaneously constrain both the magnitude and isospin dependence of in-medium NN cross sections (via backward-angle neutron/proton ratios) and the density dependence of the symmetry energy (via forward-angle ratios). The authors note that a momentum-dependent transport model with consistent in-medium cross sections and mean field is in progress.
