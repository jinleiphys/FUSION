# Energy Dependence of Breakup Cross Sections of Halo Nucleus 8B and Effective Interactions

> We study the energy dependence of the cross sections for nucleon removal of 8B projectiles. It is shown that the Glauber model calculations with nucleon-nucleon t-matrix reproduce well the energy dependence of the breakup cross sections of 8B. A DWBA model for the breakup cross section is also proposed and results are compared with those of the Glauber model. We show that to obtain an agreement between the DWBA calculations, the Glauber formalism, and the experimental data, it is necessary to modify the energy behavior of the effective interaction. In particular, the breakup potential has a quite different energy dependence than the strong absorption potential.

## Key claim

The Glauber model with nucleon-nucleon t-matrix reproduces the energy dependence of 8B breakup cross sections, but a DWBA model requires a modified effective interaction whose energy dependence differs from that of the strong absorption potential to achieve agreement with both the Glauber formalism and experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculated proton removal cross sections for 8B projectiles on carbon targets using both a Glauber model (with eikonal phases derived from nucleon-nucleon t-matrix and folding integrals over nuclear densities) and a DWBA model for stripping reactions. The DWBA transition matrix was expressed in terms of a "transition" potential U_ex, and the cross section was computed using eikonal distorted waves with optical potentials constructed from the M3Y effective nucleon-nucleon interaction. The energy dependence of the effective interaction was then adjusted to match experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The binding energy of the valence proton in 8B is ε = 0.138 MeV, reproduced by a Woods-Saxon+spin-orbit potential with parameters V0 = -44.66 MeV, a = 0.56 fm, r0 = 1.25 fm, R = 2.391 fm, and Fso = 0.351 fm.
- The M3Y interaction parameters are A = 7999 MeV, B = -2134 MeV, C = -276 MeV fm³, β1 = 4 fm⁻¹, and β2 = 2.5 fm⁻¹.
- The 7Be core density uses parameters a = 1.77 fm and c = 0.327 fm.
- The volume integral of the M3Y interaction is t0 = 421 MeV.
- The imaginary part of the optical potential is parameterized as Im U = λ U_M3Y with λ = 0.6-0.8.
- The modified effective interaction that reproduces experimental data follows t(E,s) ∝ E⁻⁰·²⁵ t(s) for E < 200 MeV, and U_ex ∝ constant · t(s) for E ≥ 200 MeV.

## Context

This work extends Glauber model calculations for halo nuclei, which had been successfully applied to stable nuclei since Glauber (1958), and compares them with a DWBA formalism. The study was motivated by experimental data from Bla97 and a model by Hansen (1996) that forced nucleon removal cross sections to have the same energy dependence as total reaction cross sections. The finding that the breakup potential has a different energy dependence than the absorption potential has implications for understanding halo nuclei structure and for constructing optical potentials from effective interactions, suggesting further studies with other halo nuclei are needed.
