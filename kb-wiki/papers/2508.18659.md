# Finite density nuclear matter and neutron stars in hard-wall AdS/QCD model

> We investigate properties of nuclear matter, equation of state (EOS) of neutron stars and its mass-radius relation in a hard-wall AdS/QCD model by regarding baryons as solitonic configurations in gauge fields. Compared with previous approaches, we employ a different homogeneous approximation that takes into account the equations of motion for the pure gauge fields. By choosing appropriate parameters, we realize a chiral phase transition within the baryonic phase, where the chiral condensate decreases with the baryon chemical potential, until it reaches zero -- chiral symmetry is restored. In addition, independent of the existence of chiral phase transition, we also find that the speed of sound converges to the conformal limit at the density relevant to cores of massive stars but the trace of energy-momentum tensor does not vanish which indicates the pseudoconformal structure and intrinsic manifestation of scale symmetry in compact star matter. Through calculations, we obtain an equation of state that is more tightly constrained than previous works, and the resulting mass-radius relation of neutron stars is consistent with current observations.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that, by employing a different homogeneous approximation that includes the equations of motion for pure gauge fields, a chiral phase transition can occur within the baryonic phase at zero temperature, decoupled from the onset of baryons, and the resulting equation of state is more tightly constrained than in previous hard-wall AdS/QCD approaches.

## Method

The authors model baryons as solitonic configurations in flavor gauge fields within a hard-wall AdS/QCD framework, using a homogeneous approximation that assumes static, spatially homogeneous gauge and scalar fields depending only on the holographic coordinate z. They solve the equations of motion for the scalar field VEV, the gauge field associated with baryon chemical potential, and the topological field φ, subject to boundary conditions at the UV and IR cutoffs, and minimize the grand potential to determine the relationship between chemical potential and baryon density. Three parameter sets are studied: one without the scalar field, and two sets (A and B) with different values of the IR boundary potential parameters m₁ and m₂, fitted to reproduce the chiral condensate and the parameter D (related to the ratio of chemical potential to density at saturation).

## Key numbers

- Saturation density ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ is used as the empirical reference value for normal nuclear matter density.
- In set B, the chiral phase transition (second-order) occurs at μ ≈ 1535 MeV, where ω_ir reaches zero and chiral symmetry is restored.
- The speed of sound converges to the conformal limit (v_s² = 1/3) for all three parameter sets at densities ≈ 6 ρ₀, relevant to cores of massive stars.
- In set A, the chiral condensate is already zero at nuclear saturation density ρ₀; in set B, there is a discontinuous jump in chiral condensate at μ = 922 MeV (the onset of baryons), followed by a gradual decrease to zero at μ = 1535 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous hard-wall AdS/QCD studies of nuclear matter (Bartolini 2022) by using a different homogeneous approximation from Elliot-Ripley (2016) that includes the equations of motion for pure gauge fields, which were previously neglected. The results enable a more constrained equation of state for neutron star matter that is consistent with current observational mass-radius constraints, and reveal a pseudoconformal structure (speed of sound approaching the conformal limit while the trace of the energy-momentum tensor remains non-zero) that manifests scale symmetry in compact star matter, as discussed in prior work by Ma (2018).
