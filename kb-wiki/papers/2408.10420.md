# $K$-isomeric states in the isotopic and isotonic chains of $^{178}$Hf

> We study the evolution of $K^π=6^{+}$ and $8^{-}$ two-quasiparticle (q.p.) configurations in the isotopic and isotonic chains of even-even deformed nuclei around $^{178}$Hf and their ability to describe series of observed $K$-isomer excitations within the framework of a Skyrme Hartree--Fock--BCS (SHFBCS) approach using SIII interaction and seniority pairing strengths with self-consistent blocking. We apply the approach along the prescription in [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 105}, 044329 (2022)] used to describe $K$-isomers in the actinide and transfermium mass regions. The calculations allow us to identify the regions where proton or neutron configurations or their mixture may be responsible for the $K$-isomer formation. The obtained results provide a detailed test for the Skyrme SIII interaction used and outline the limits of applicability of the overall SHFBCS approach in the regions of well deformed nuclei. The study suggests that similar systematic analysis can be implemented in the heavier mass regions whenever enough data are available.

## Key claim

The Skyrme SIII Hartree-Fock-BCS approach with self-consistent blocking can reproduce the systematic behavior of K-isomer energies in the isotopic and isotonic chains around 178Hf, but its applicability is limited to the best rotators (R42 around 3.28-3.31) and requires configuration mixing between neutron and proton two-quasiparticle states to explain the observed isomer energies in several nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed Skyrme Hartree-Fock-BCS (SHFBCS) calculations with the SIII interaction and seniority pairing strengths (Gn=16 MeV, Gp=15 MeV) using self-consistent blocking of the single-particle orbitals entering the excited two-quasiparticle configuration. They calculated Kπ=6+ and 8- excitation energies as the difference between total HFBCS energies of the blocked 2qp configuration and the ground state for even Hf isotopes from A=168 to A=186 and for N=106 even isotones from 170Gd to 182Os, using an axially-deformed harmonic-oscillator basis truncated at N0+1=15 major shells with optimized basis parameters b=0.480 and q=1.185.

## Key numbers

- For the Kπ=6+ isomer in 176Hf, the two-neutron (5/2-[512],7/2-[514]) configuration gives Eth=1.061 MeV, underestimating the experimental value of 1.333 MeV by 272 keV
- For the Kπ=6+ isomer in 178Hf, the two-neutron configuration gives Eth=1.913 MeV, overestimating the experimental value of 1.554 MeV by 359 keV
- For the Kπ=8- isomer in 176Hf, the two-proton (7/2+[404],9/2-[514]) configuration gives Eth=1.598 MeV, overestimating the experimental value of 1.559 MeV by about 40 keV
- For the Kπ=8- isomer in 178Hf, the two-neutron (7/2-[514],9/2+[624]) configuration gives Eth=1.136 MeV, underestimating the experimental value of 1.147 MeV by about 10 keV
- For the Kπ=8- isomer in 180Hf, the two-proton configuration gives Eth=1.179 MeV, overestimating the experimental value of 1.142 MeV by about 40 keV
- For the Kπ=8- isomer in 176Yb (N=106 isotone), the two-neutron configuration gives Eth=1.028 MeV, underestimating the experimental value of 1.050 MeV by 22 keV
- The experimental R42 ratios for the best rotators range from 3.28 to 3.31 (176Hf: 3.284, 178Hf: 3.291, 180Hf: 3.307)

## Context

This work extends the SHFBCS approach previously applied to actinide and transfermium nuclei (Minkov et al., Phys. Rev. C 105, 044329, 2022) to the rare-earth and trans-lanthanide region around 178Hf, providing the first systematic test of the Skyrme SIII interaction on long isotopic and isotonic chains of K-isomers. The results are compared with covariant density functional theory calculations using DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 functionals (Karakat et al., 2020) which studied the same Kπ=6+ and 8- isomeric energies in the Hafnium isotopic and N=104,106 isotonic chains. The study enables future systematic analysis of K-isomers in heavier mass regions and identifies the limits of applicability of the SHFBCS approach for well-deformed nuclei.
