# Triaxial nuclear models and the outer crust of nonaccreting cold neutron stars

> The properties and composition of the outer crust of nonaccreting cold neutron stars are studied by applying the model of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (BPS) and taking into account for the first time triaxial deformations of nuclei. Two theoretical nuclear models, Hartree-Fock plus pairing in the BCS approximation (HF-BCS) with Skyrme SLy6 parametrization and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) with Gogny D1S force, are used to calculate the nuclear masses. The two theoretical calculations are compared concerning their neutron drip line, binding energies, magic neutron numbers, and the sequence of nuclei in the outer crust of nonaccreting cold neutron stars, with special emphasis on the effect of triaxial deformations. The BPS model is extended by the higher-order corrections for the atomic binding, screening, exchange and zero-point energies. The influence of the higher-order corrections on the sequence of the outer crust is investigated.

# Triaxial nuclear models and the outer crust of nonaccreting cold neutron stars

## Key claim
For the first time, triaxial deformations of nuclei are included in calculations of the outer crust composition of nonaccreting cold neutron stars, but the effect is too weak to significantly alter the sequence of ground-state nuclei compared to spherical or axially deformed models. The sequence remains robustly determined by magic neutron numbers (N=50 and 82) across most state-of-the-art nuclear models.

## Method
The authors apply the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) model extended with higher-order corrections (atomic binding, screening, exchange, and zero-point energies) to compute the ground-state composition of the outer crust. Nuclear masses for neutron-rich nuclei with proton numbers Z=20 to 50 are calculated using two triaxial mean-field models: Hartree-Fock plus BCS pairing (HF-BCS) with the Skyrme SLy6 parametrization, and Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) with the Gogny D1S force. The neutron drip line and crust sequences from both models are compared with each other and with prior results from the finite-range droplet model (FRDM) and other theoretical approaches.

## Key numbers
- The neutron drip density is approximately 4.3 × 10¹¹ g/cm³, where free neutrons begin to appear and the inner crust begins.
- For Germanium isotopes, Gogny HFB predicts the drip-line nucleus at ¹⁰²Ge, while SLy6 HF-BCS and FRDM predict ¹⁰⁶Ge as the last bound nucleus.
- The last nucleus in the sequence with experimentally determined mass is ⁸⁰Zn, common to both models.
- For the HF-BCS model at densities above 4.9 × 10¹⁰ g/cm³, the nucleus ⁷⁸Ni (with neutron magic number 50) appears in the sequence.
- The higher-order corrections affect the sequence only at high densities above 1.4 × 10¹¹ g/cm³.
- The higher-order corrections lower the total pressure by up to approximately 3% at lower densities; above 10⁶ g/cm³ their contribution drops below 10⁻³.
- The sum of higher-order corrections reaches a fraction of 10⁻⁶ to 10⁻⁵ of the total energy density at densities above 10⁹ g/cm³.

## Context
This work extends the BPS model (Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland, 1971) and the subsequent updates by Haensel, Zdunik, and Dobaczewski (1989), Haensel and Pichon (1994), and Ruster, Hempel, and Schaffner-Bielich (2006) by incorporating triaxial deformations for the first time. The results enable comparison with future experimental measurements of neutron-rich nuclei at facilities such as ISAC-II and FAIR at GSI Darmstadt. The finding that triaxial deformations do not significantly alter the crust composition confirms the robustness of earlier predictions based on spherical or axially deformed models, while the Gogny HFB calculation shows distinctive deviations (e.g., appearance of ¹³⁰Cd) that warrant further investigation with improved basis sets or parametrizations.
