# Novel Neutron Star Structures with Nucleon Mass Radius

> In order to reveal the difference between the latest neutron star observation experiment GW170817 and the existing theory, we mainly consider the effect of the nucleon radius on the neutron star from the existing theory. We believe that the effect of nucleon radius in neutron star is not negligible, and the mass radius of nucleon should be used instead of the charge radius. The nucleon mass radius is set as $r_m = 0.55\pm0.09$ fm from the new measurements. It is considered as an input to the "Excluded Volume Effects" model in the equation of state of nuclear matter. We propose a novel neutron star mass-radius relation by using proton mass radius is consistent with the observation GW170817.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the nucleon mass radius (0.55 ± 0.09 fm), rather than the charge radius, should be used as the finite-size correction in the equation of state for neutron star matter, and that this correction yields a mass-radius relation consistent with the GW170817 observation. The claim is conditional on using the "Excluded Volume Effects" model with the NL3 parameter set and the specific mass radius value extracted from vector meson photoproduction data.

## Method

The authors start from the Relativistic Mean Field (RMF) theory with the NL3 parameter set to describe nucleon-nucleon interactions via scalar (σ) and vector (ω, ρ) meson exchange. They incorporate the nucleon mass radius (r_m = 0.55 ± 0.09 fm) as an input to the "Excluded Volume Effects" (EVE) model, which reduces the effective volume available to nucleons from V to V' = V - N V_n, where V_n = 4π r_m^3/3. The modified baryon density ρ = ρ'/(1 + V_n ρ') is then used to compute the equation of state, including β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions with lepton contributions. Finally, the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations are solved to obtain the mass-radius relation, using the BPS and BBP equations of state for the neutron star crust.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon mass radius: r_m = 0.55 ± 0.09 fm, extracted from vector meson photoproduction data near threshold
- Proton charge radius: r_ch = 0.8409 ± 0.0004 fm (from Particle Data Group 2020)
- Neutron magnetic radius: r_mag = 0.864 ± 0.008 fm (from Particle Data Group 2020)
- GW170817 measurement: R_{1.4M_⊙} = 11.0_{-0.6}^{+0.9} km (90% credible interval) for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star
- Critical density for nuclear matter: ρ_c = 0.09371 fm^{-3} (from Baym 1971)
- NL3 parameter set values: m = 939 MeV, m_σ = 508.194 MeV, m_ω = 782.501 MeV, m_ρ = 763 MeV; coupling constants g_σ = 10.217 MeV, g_ω = 12.868 MeV, g_ρ = 4.474 MeV, g_2 = -10.431 fm^{-1}, g_3 = -28.885 MeV

## Context

This work extends the line of research using the Excluded Volume Effects model in neutron star structure, previously applied by Mart (2013) and Suparti (2017) with charge radii, and by Zhu (2018) who argued that nucleon radius effects are small. The authors contradict Zhu (2018) by arguing that the mass radius (not charge radius) is the relevant scale in dense matter where strong interactions dominate. The results are consistent with the GW170817 observation by Capano (2019) and the pulsar mass constraint from Antoniadis (2013). The authors note that future data from the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) and the Electron-ion Collider in China (EicC) could reduce the uncertainty in the nucleon mass radius measurement.
