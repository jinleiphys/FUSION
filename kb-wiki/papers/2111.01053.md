# Complementarity between neutrinoless double beta decay and collider searches for heavy neutrinos in composite-fermion models

> Composite-fermion models predict excited quarks and leptons with mass scales which can potentially be observed at high-energy colliders like the LHC; the most recent exclusion limits from the CMS and ATLAS Collaborations corner excited-fermion masses and the compositeness scale to the multi-TeV range. At the same time, hypothetical composite Majorana neutrinos would lead to observable effects in neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$) experiments. In this work, we show that the current composite-neutrino exclusion limit $M_N>4.6$ TeV, as extracted from direct searches at the LHC, can indeed be further improved to $M_N>8.8$ TeV by including the bound on the nuclear transition $^{136}$Xe $\to$ $^{136}$Ba $+2e^-$. Looking ahead, the forthcoming HL-LHC will allow probing a larger portion of the parameter-space, nevertheless, it will still benefit from the complementary limit provided by $0 νββ$ future detectors to explore composite-neutrino masses up to $12.6$ TeV.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the current LHC exclusion limit on composite Majorana neutrino masses (M_N > 4.6 TeV) can be improved to M_N > 8.8 TeV by incorporating bounds from neutrinoless double beta decay in 136Xe, but this complementarity depends on the specific nuclear matrix element values used and assumes the contact interaction mechanism dominates over gauge interactions.

## Method

The authors calculated the contribution of four-fermion contact interactions to neutrinoless double beta decay mediated by heavy composite Majorana neutrinos, using the closure approximation for nuclear matrix elements and non-relativistic nucleon currents. They derived an analytical expression for the 0νββ half-life from contact interactions and compared the resulting exclusion limits in the (M_N, Λ) parameter space with existing CMS Run-2 data at 13 TeV and projected HL-LHC sensitivities at 14 TeV with 3 ab^-1 integrated luminosity.

## Key numbers

- Current CMS exclusion limit on composite neutrino mass: M_N > 4.6 TeV (from 2.3 fb^-1 at sqrt(s)=13 TeV)
- Improved limit including 0νββ bound from 136Xe: M_N > 8.8 TeV (using maximum NME value from QRPA model)
- Lower bound from 0νββ alone ranges from M_N > 7.3 TeV (minimum NME, IBM model) to M_N > 8.8 TeV (maximum NME, QRPA model)
- Current experimental half-life limit for 136Xe: T_1/2 > 1.07 × 10^26 yr (90% C.L.)
- Current experimental half-life limit for 76Ge: T_1/2 > 1.8 × 10^26 yr (90% C.L.)
- Future HL-LHC projection: excludes M_N up to 8.0 TeV at Λ=M_N
- Future 0νββ projection (T_1/2 > 10^28 yr): excludes M_N up to 12.6 TeV at Λ=M_N
- Nuclear matrix element range for 136Xe: 72.6 (IBM model) to 186 (QRPA model)
- Average excitation energy Δ ≈ 10 MeV used in closure approximation

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of 0νββ mediated by composite neutrinos via gauge interactions (Panella 1995, 1997, 1998) by computing the dominant contact interaction contribution. It complements direct LHC searches for excited fermions by CMS (Sirunyan 2017) and ATLAS, showing that low-energy nuclear decay experiments can probe composite neutrino masses beyond collider reach, particularly in the high-mass region. The results enable future comparisons between next-generation 0νββ experiments (such as nEXO targeting T_1/2 > 10^28 yr) and HL-LHC projections, demonstrating continued complementarity for exploring composite-fermion models up to M_N ≈ 12.6 TeV.
