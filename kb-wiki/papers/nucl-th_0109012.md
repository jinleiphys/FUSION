# Meson Dynamics and the resulting "3-Nucleon-Force" diagrams: Results from a simplified test case

> A simplified 1D (one-dimensional) model for a generalized 3N system is considered, as a testing ground for the explicit treatment of the meson dynamics in this system. We focus attention on the irreducible diagrams generated by the pion dynamics in the 3N system, and in particular to a new type of three-nucleon force discussed recently in the literature, and generated by the one-pion-exchange mechanism in presence of a nucleon-nucleon correlation. It is found that these new terms in the simplified model have an approximately 30% effect compared to the standard three-nucleon force terms in a `Triton' binding energy calculation. It is suggested that this effect should also not be ignored in realistic calculations.

## Key claim

In a simplified one-dimensional model of the three-nucleon system, a new type of three-nucleon force diagram generated by one-pion-exchange in the presence of a nucleon-nucleon correlation produces an approximately 30% effect on the model `triton' binding energy compared to standard three-nucleon force terms, suggesting this effect should not be ignored in realistic calculations.

## Method

The authors constructed a one-dimensional, spinless scattering model with a Malfliet-Tjon type potential whose strength parameters were chosen to reproduce the deuteron binding energy. They used the Unitary Pole Approximation for t-matrices and a sturmian procedure to find binding energies, then calculated the `triton' binding energy first without correction terms, then including a Fujita-Miyazawa type correction term (in static approximation), and finally including both that term and a new topological correction term describing intermediate pion propagation under a correlated two-nucleon cluster.

## Key numbers

- `Deuteron' binding energy: not explicitly given in the text (only stated that strength parameters were chosen to reproduce it).
- `Triton' binding energy without correction terms: -7.28 MeV.
- `Triton' binding energy including only the Fujita-Miyazawa type correction term (with adjustable parameter c_πN = 0.01): -8.17 MeV.
- `Triton' binding energy including both types of correction terms (with c_πN = 0.01): -8.48 MeV.
- The effect of the new correction terms relative to the Fujita-Miyazawa type terms is approximately 30%.

## Context

This work extends the rigorous four-body theory of Grassberger-Sandhas (1967) and Yakubovsky (1967) and the subsequent method for describing the coupled NNN-πNNN system developed by Canton (1998). The authors' approximation scheme (Canton, Melde, Svenne, 2001) produced correction terms to the standard Faddeev-Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas three-nucleon equation that can be interpreted as three-nucleon force diagrams. The simplified model enables investigation of these effects before moving to realistic calculations, and the authors note that Canton and Schadow (2001) have confirmed in a more realistic study that a new three-nucleon force term of tensor structure generated by these corrections could help solve the A_y-puzzle in nucleon-deuteron scattering.
