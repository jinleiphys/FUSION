# Symmetry Restoration in Mixed-Spin Paired Heavy Nuclei

> The nature of the nuclear pairing condensates in heavy nuclei, specifically neutron-proton (spin-triplet), versus identical-particle (spin-singlet) pairing has been an active area of research for quite some time. In this work, we probe three candidates that should display spin-triplet, spin-singlet, and mixed-spin pairing. Using theoretical approaches such as the gradient method and symmetry restoration techniques, we find the ground state of these nuclei in Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory and compute ground-state to ground-state pair-transfer amplitudes to neighbouring isotopes while simultaneously projecting to specific particle number and nuclear spin values. We identify specific reactions for future experimental research that could shed light on spin-triplet and mixed-spin pairing.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the nature of the ground-state pairing condensates in heavy nuclei (spin-triplet, spin-singlet, or mixed-spin) survives after symmetry restoration (particle-number and angular-momentum projection), but the authors caution that the results are at the mean-field level and should be understood as qualitative rather than quantitative representations of the physical ground state.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using a Wood-Saxon one-body potential and a contact two-body interaction with strengths of 300 MeV (spin-singlet) and 450 MeV (spin-triplet). They employed the gradient method for energy minimization and then applied projection after variation (PAV) to restore particle number and nuclear spin symmetries, using Pfaffian-based overlap formulas free of sign ambiguities. Pair-transfer amplitudes for ground-state to ground-state transitions were computed by explicitly constructing creation operators with specific quantum numbers and evaluating overlaps between different HFB wave functions.

## Key numbers

* Correlation energy for unconstrained ground state of ${}^{132}_{66}$Dy (spin-triplet candidate): 11.315 MeV.
* Correlation energy for unconstrained ground state of ${}^{132}_{64}$Gd (mixed-spin candidate): 7.478 MeV.
* Correlation energy for unconstrained ground state of ${}^{132}_{60}$Nd (spin-singlet candidate): 8.037 MeV.
* For ${}^{132}_{66}$Dy, removing spin-singlet pairing leaves correlation energy at 11.315 MeV (nearly degenerate with unconstrained case), while removing spin-triplet pairing reduces it to 4.853 MeV.
* For ${}^{132}_{60}$Nd, removing spin-triplet pairing leaves correlation energy at 8.035 MeV (nearly degenerate with unconstrained case), while removing spin-singlet pairing reduces it to 1.630 MeV.
* For ${}^{132}_{64}$Gd, removing spin-singlet pairing gives correlation energy 6.299 MeV and removing spin-triplet pairing gives 4.630 MeV (both similar to the unconstrained value of 7.478 MeV).
* Approximately 90 percent of the ${}^{132}_{60}$Nd wave function has nuclear spin $I_0 = 0$ after particle-number projection to the target values.

## Context

This work extends earlier HFB studies by Gezerlis (2011) and Bulthuis (2016) that identified candidate nuclei for spin-triplet and mixed-spin pairing in the $A \geq 130$ region near the proton dripline. It goes beyond those studies by applying symmetry restoration (particle-number and angular-momentum projection) to confirm that the pairing character survives projection, and by computing pair-transfer amplitudes to identify specific reactions for future experimental investigation. The paper also builds on the gradient method of Robledo (2011) and the ambiguity-free Pfaffian overlap formulas of Bertsch (2012).
