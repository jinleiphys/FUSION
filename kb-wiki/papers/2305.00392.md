# Bayesian Inference of Supernova Neutrino Spectra with Multiple Detectors

> We implement the Bayesian inference to retrieve energy spectra of all neutrinos from a galactic core-collapse supernova (CCSN). To achieve high statistics and full sensitivity to all flavours of neutrinos, we adopt a combination of several reaction channels from different large-scale neutrino observatories, namely inverse beta decay on proton and elastic scattering on electron from Hyper-Kamiokande (Hyper-K), charged current absorption on Argon from Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) and coherent elastic scattering on Lead from RES-NOVA. Assuming no neutrino oscillation or specific oscillation models, we obtain mock data for each channel through Poisson processes with the predictions, for a typical source distance of 10 kpc in our Galaxy, and then evaluate the probability distributions for all spectral parameters of theoretical neutrino spectrum model with Bayes' theorem. Although the results for either the electron-neutrinos or electron-antineutrinos reserve relatively large uncertainties (according to the neutrino mass hierarchy), a precision of a few percent (i.e., $\pm 1 \% \sim \pm 4 \%$ at a credible interval of $2 σ$) is achieved for primary spectral parameters (e.g., mean energy and total emitted energy) of other neutrino species. Moreover, the correlation coefficients between different parameters are computed as well and interesting patterns are found. Especially, the mixing-induced correlations are sensitive to the neutrino mass hierarchy, which potentially makes it a brand new probe to determine the neutrino mass hierarchy in the detection of galactic supernova neutrinos. Finally, we discuss the origin of such correlation patterns and perspectives for further improvement on our results.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that Bayesian inference can retrieve energy spectra of all neutrino flavors from a galactic core-collapse supernova using a combination of multiple detector channels, achieving a precision of a few percent for primary spectral parameters of heavy-flavor neutrinos, but with the caveat that results for electron-neutrinos and electron-antineutrinos retain relatively large uncertainties that depend on the neutrino mass hierarchy.

## Method

The authors generate mock data for a supernova at 10 kpc using Poisson processes based on predicted event rates from three detectors: inverse beta decay on proton and elastic scattering on electron from Hyper-Kamiokande, charged current absorption on argon from DUNE, and coherent elastic scattering on lead from RES-NOVA. They apply the adiabatic Mikheyev-Smirnov-Wolfenstein (MSW) model for neutrino oscillations, considering both normal mass ordering (NMO) and inverted mass ordering (IMO), and then evaluate posterior probability distributions for the spectral parameters (mean energy, total emitted energy, and pinching parameter) of the Garching formula using Bayes' theorem.

## Key numbers

- Source distance assumed: 10 kpc (typical galactic distance)
- Precision achieved for primary spectral parameters of heavy-flavor neutrinos: ±1% to ±4% at a 2σ credible interval
- Fiducial mass for Hyper-K: 220 kton (one tank)
- Fiducial mass for DUNE: 40 kton
- Detector mass for RES-NOVA (RN-3 phase): 465 ton
- RES-NOVA energy threshold: 1 keV for nuclear recoil energy
- RES-NOVA energy resolution: 0.2 keV for nuclear recoil energy
- Input spectral parameters from simulation: ν_e mean energy 14.1 MeV, total energy 7.70×10^52 erg; ν̄_e mean energy 16.3 MeV, total energy 6.44×10^52 erg; ν_x mean energy 17.2 MeV, total energy 5.88×10^52 erg
- Mixing angle used: sin²θ₁₂ = 0.307 ± 0.013

## Context

This work extends previous statistical approaches to supernova neutrino spectrum reconstruction (Laha 2013, Nikrant 2017, GalloRosso 2017, 2020) by combining three complementary detectors (Hyper-K, DUNE, and RES-NOVA) to achieve sensitivity to all neutrino flavors simultaneously. The Bayesian method builds on earlier applications to SN 1987A data (Loredo 2001) and contrasts with unfolding methods (Li 2017, 2019; Nagakura 2020) that do not rely on analytical spectral formulas but suffer from ill-posed inversion problems. The finding that mixing-induced correlations between spectral parameters are sensitive to the neutrino mass hierarchy potentially provides a new probe for determining the mass ordering in future galactic supernova neutrino detection.
