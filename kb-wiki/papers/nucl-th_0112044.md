# Thermal description of particle production in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The grand-canonical version of the thermal model is used to analyze the ratios of particle abundances measured in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. Exactly the same model is applied to study the heavy-ion reactions at BNL AGS, CERN SPS, and BNL RHIC. A very good description is achieved for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS, and for Au+Au collisions at RHIC. In these two cases the value of the temperature characterizing the chemical freeze-out is practically same: T=168 MeV at SPS and T=165 MeV at RHIC. On the other hand, the particle ratios measured in the collisions of lighter nuclei are described only in the qualitative way. We discuss also the effect of the possible in-medium modifications of hadron masses and widths on the thermal fits. For Pb+Pb collisions at SPS and Au+Au collisions at RHIC we find that the chi^2 fits favor slightly a moderate (20%) decrease of the masses. In-medium modifications of the widths have little effect on the fits, unless they are increased by a factor larger than 2. We study in detail the thermodynamic conditions characterizing the chemical freeze-out. In particular, we find that the average baryon energy is 1.6 GeV and the average meson energy is 0.9 GeV. This difference reflects a different behavior of the mass spectra of mesons and baryons.

## Key claim

The grand-canonical thermal model provides a very good description of particle abundance ratios for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS and Au+Au collisions at BNL RHIC, yielding nearly identical chemical freeze-out temperatures of 168 MeV and 165 MeV respectively; however, for collisions of lighter nuclei the model succeeds only qualitatively, not quantitatively.

## Method

The author applied a single grand-canonical thermal model, assuming an ideal hadron gas in full thermal and chemical equilibrium at freeze-out, to analyze measured particle abundance ratios from BNL AGS, CERN SPS, and BNL RHIC heavy-ion reactions. The model included 164 baryonic states and 110 mesonic states (all light-flavor hadrons containing u, d, and s quarks), with full treatment of strong resonance decays using branching ratios from the Review of Particle Physics, and considered three levels of weak-decay feedback. The fits determined the temperature T and baryon chemical potential μ_B as free parameters, with strangeness neutrality and the charge-to-baryon ratio of the colliding nuclei imposed as constraints, and the volume cancelling out in all ratios.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature for Pb+Pb at SPS: T_chem = 168 ± 3 MeV
- Chemical freeze-out temperature for Au+Au at RHIC: T_chem = 165 ± 7 MeV
- Average baryon energy at freeze-out: 1.6 GeV
- Average meson energy at freeze-out: 0.9 GeV
- χ² fits favor a moderate ~20% decrease of hadron masses for Pb+Pb at SPS and Au+Au at RHIC
- In-medium modifications of hadron widths have little effect unless increased by a factor larger than 2
- Baryon Hagedorn temperature: T_H^B = 186 MeV
- Meson Hagedorn temperature: T_H^M = 311 MeV

## Context

This work extends the thermal model analyses previously performed by several groups (such as those cited as PBM-AGS, CleyEllSa, RafLetTou, and BecCleyKSR1) by applying a single consistent formulation across multiple collision systems and energies, from AGS through SPS to RHIC. The finding that chemical freeze-out temperatures at SPS and RHIC are nearly identical (168 vs 165 MeV) while the baryon chemical potential drops significantly constrains models of particle production and the QCD phase diagram. The study of in-medium mass modifications connects to predictions from effective QCD theories and the Brown-Rho scaling hypothesis, and the results enable further combined analyses with hydrodynamic models to describe transverse-momentum spectra, elliptic flow, and HBT radii, as demonstrated in the work of Florkowski, Broniowski, and Michalec (2001) on p⊥ spectra.
