# Exploration of relativistic symmetry by the similarity renormalization group

> The similarity renormalization group is used to transform Dirac Hamiltonian into a diagonal form, which the upper (lower) diagonal element becomes an operator describing Dirac (anti-)particle. The eigenvalues of the operator are verfied to be in good agreement with that of the original Hamiltonian. Furthermore, the pseudospin symmetry is investigated. It is shown that the pseudospin splittings appearing in the nonrelativistic limit are reduced by the contributions from these terms relating the spin-orbit interactions, added by those relating the dynamical terms, and the quality of pseudospin symmetry origins mainly from the competition of the dynamical effects and the spin-orbit interactions. The spin symmetry of antiparticle spectrum is well reproduced in the present calculations.

## Key claim

The similarity renormalization group (SRG) can transform the spherical Dirac Hamiltonian into a diagonal form whose eigenvalues agree with the exact relativistic results, and the pseudospin symmetry (PSS) is shown to originate mainly from the competition between spin-orbit interactions and dynamical effects, rather than being a non-relativistic symmetry.

## Method

The author applies Wegner's SRG formulation to the spherical Dirac Hamiltonian, using a generator that commutes with the beta matrix to drive the Hamiltonian toward diagonal form. The flow equations are solved perturbatively in 1/M, yielding a diagonal operator for Dirac particles (H1) and antiparticles (H2) expanded up to order 1/M^3. The energy spectra of H1 and H2 are then calculated in a harmonic oscillator basis using a Woods-Saxon parameterization fitted to relativistic mean-field calculations for 208Pb, and the contribution of each operator component to the pseudospin energy splitting is evaluated.

## Key numbers

- For the 2d5/2 and 1g7/2 pseudospin doublet, the total pseudospin splitting Δε = 3.946 MeV (sum of all eight operator components), compared to the non-relativistic limit contribution from O1 alone of 4.777 MeV.
- For the 2f7/2 and 1h9/2 pseudospin doublet, the total pseudospin splitting Δε = 3.671 MeV, compared to the non-relativistic limit contribution from O1 alone of 5.732 MeV.
- The spin-orbit-related terms O3 and O6 contribute negative values to Δε: for 2d5/2/1g7/2, O3 gives -1.230 MeV and O6 gives -0.650 MeV; for 2f7/2/1h9/2, O3 gives -1.815 MeV and O6 gives -0.884 MeV.
- The dynamical terms O2 and O5 contribute positive values to Δε: for 2d5/2/1g7/2, O2 gives 0.765 MeV and O5 gives 0.383 MeV; for 2f7/2/1h9/2, O2 gives 0.445 MeV and O5 gives 0.303 MeV.
- The contributions of O4, O7, and O8 to Δε are all below 0.06 MeV in magnitude for both doublets.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic interpretation of pseudospin symmetry first identified by Ginocchio (1997) and the dynamical character studies by Alberto et al. (2001), Lisboa et al. (2010), and Marco et al. (2001, 2008). By using the SRG to produce a Hermitian, singularity-free diagonal operator, the author avoids the coupling between energy and operator that plagued earlier Schrodinger-like equation approaches, enabling a clean decomposition of the pseudospin splitting into individual operator contributions. The formalism enables future systematic studies of PSS breaking mechanisms in realistic nuclear potentials and can be extended to deformed systems.
