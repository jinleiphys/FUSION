# Compact star constraints on the high-density EoS

> A new scheme for testing the nuclear matter (NM) equation of state (EoS) at high densities using constraints from compact star (CS) phenomenology is applied to neutron stars with a core of deconfined quark matter (QM). An acceptable EoS shall not to be in conflict with the mass measurement of 2.1 +/- 0.2 M_sun (1 sigma level) for PSR J0751+1807 and the mass-radius relation deduced from the thermal emission of RX J1856-3754. Further constraints for the state of matter in CS interiors come from temperature-age data for young, nearby objects. The CS cooling theory shall agree not only with these data, but also with the mass distribution inferred via population synthesis models as well as with LogN-LogS data. The scheme is applied to a set of hybrid EsoS with a phase transition to stiff, color superconducting QM which fulfills all above constraints and is constrained otherwise from NM saturation properties and flow data of heavy-ion collisions. We extrapolate our description to low temperatures and draw conclusions for the QCD phase diagram to be explored in heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

A hybrid equation of state (EoS) with a phase transition to stiff, color superconducting quark matter can simultaneously satisfy all current compact star constraints (mass, mass-radius, cooling, LogN-LogS) and heavy-ion collision flow constraints, whereas no purely nuclear EoS tested in prior work meets all these constraints.

## Method

The authors apply a testing scheme from Klahn (2006) that confronts the EoS with multiple astrophysical and experimental constraints: the mass measurement of PSR J0751+1807, the mass-radius relation from RX J1856-3754, temperature-age data for young nearby compact stars, population synthesis models for the mass distribution, and LogN-LogS data from the ROSAT all-sky survey, plus flow data from heavy-ion collisions. They construct a hybrid EoS combining a Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) nuclear matter description (Bonn A potential) with a three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) type quark matter model including scalar diquark and vector meson mean fields, and compute compact star configurations and cooling curves.

## Key numbers

- Mass measurement for PSR J0751+1807: 2.1 ± 0.2 solar masses (1 sigma level), giving a lower limit for the maximum mass of approximately 1.9 solar masses
- Lower limit on radius from RX J1856-3754 thermal emission: R > 12 km
- Nuclear DBHF EoS compressibility: K = 230 MeV
- Direct Urca threshold proton fraction: x_DU = 0.11 (rising to 0.14 with muons)
- Maximum mass for hybrid stars with vector coupling η_V = 0.5: approximately 2.1 solar masses
- Critical mass for onset of deconfinement (quark matter core): M_crit = 1.22 solar masses
- Upper mass limit for Vela description: not exceeding 1.45 solar masses
- Temperature-age avoidance region: log T > 6.3-6.4 K for ages 10^3 to 10^4.5 years
- Nuclear matter description unreliable above T ~ 45 MeV

## Context

This work extends the testing scheme of Klahn (2006) which found that no purely nuclear EoS meets all compact star constraints simultaneously, whereas every individual constraint could be satisfied by some EoS. The hybrid EoS approach builds on the "masquerade" concept of Alford (2004) where a phase transition with small latent heat produces hybrid stars that resemble neutron stars. The results enable predictions for the QCD phase diagram at low temperatures and finite densities relevant for future heavy-ion collision experiments at FAIR Darmstadt, suggesting the deconfinement transition may be almost a crossover with negligible hydrodynamical signatures.
