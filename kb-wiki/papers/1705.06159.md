# Fluctuations in quantum mechanics and field theories from a new version of semiclassical theory. II

> This is the second paper on semiclassical approach based on the density matrix given by the Euclidean time path integral with fixed coinciding endpoints. The classical path, interpolating between this point and the classical vacuum, called "flucton", plus systematic one- and two-loop corrections, has been calculated in the first paper \cite{Escobar-Ruiz:2016aqv} for double-well potential and now extended for a number of quantum-mechanical problems (anharmonic oscillator, sine-Gordon potential). The method is based on systematic expansion in Feynman diagrams and thus can be extended to QFTs. We show that the loop expansion in QM reminds the leading log-approximations in QFT. In this sequel we present complete set of results obtained using this method in unified way. Alternatively, starting from the Schrödinger equation we derive a {\it generalized} Bloch equation which semiclassical-like, iterative solution generates the loop expansion. We re-derive two loop expansions for all three above potentials and now extend it to three loops, which has not yet been done via Feynman diagrams. All results for both methods are fully consistent with each other. Asymmetric (tilted) double-well potential (non-degenerate minima) is also studied using the second method.

## Key claim

The paper extends a semiclassical "flucton" approach, based on the Euclidean-time path integral with fixed coinciding endpoints, from the double-well potential to the anharmonic oscillator and sine-Gordon potential, showing that the loop expansion in quantum mechanics resembles the leading-log approximation in quantum field theory. The authors claim that the method can be systematically extended to quantum field theories, but they hedge that the three-loop results for the sine-Gordon potential rely on an assumption that transcendental terms cancel (as they do at two loops) and on numerical fitting, rather than on fully analytic calculation.

## Method

The authors compute the diagonal density matrix \(P(x_0,\beta)\) via a Euclidean path integral with fixed coinciding endpoints, expanding around the classical "flucton" path (a zero-energy trajectory from \(x_0\) to the classical vacuum and back). They evaluate one-loop corrections by relating the fluctuation determinant to the Green function via the Brown–Creamer method, and two-loop corrections via explicit Feynman diagrams (three diagrams: one one-dimensional integral and two two-dimensional integrals) in the flucton background. Separately, they derive a generalized Bloch equation (a Riccati-type equation) from the Schrödinger equation for the logarithmic derivative of the wavefunction, whose iterative solution reproduces the loop expansion and is extended to three loops for all three potentials.

## Key numbers

* Anharmonic oscillator (AHO): two-loop correction \(B_1^{(AHO)} = \frac{(1-X)(5+16X+25X^2+17X^3)}{12 X^3 (1+X)}\) where \(X = \sqrt{1+g^2 x^2}\); the classical flucton action is \(S_E = \frac{2}{3}\frac{(1+g^2 x_0^2)^{3/2}-1}{g^2}\).
* Double-well potential (DWP): two-loop correction \(B_1^{(DWP)} = -X \frac{4+3X}{(1+X)^2}\) where \(X = g x\); the classical flucton action is \(S_E = x_0^2 (1 + \frac{2}{3} g x_0)\).
* Sine-Gordon potential (SGP): two-loop correction \(B_1^{(SGP)} = -\frac{g^2}{16} \tan^2(X)\) where \(X = g x/4\); the classical flucton action is \(S_E = \frac{16}{g^2} \sin^2(g x_0/4)\).
* Determinant for AHO: \(\log \text{Det}(O_{\text{flucton}}) = 2 \log[X (1+X)]\).
* Determinant for DWP: \(\frac{\partial \log \text{Det}(O_{\text{flucton}})}{\partial X} = \frac{4}{1+X}\).
* Determinant for SGP: \(\frac{\partial \log \text{Det}(O_{\text{flucton}})}{\partial X} = 4 \tan X\).

## Context

This work extends the flucton-based semiclassical method introduced in the authors' previous paper (Escobar-Ruiz, Shuryak, Turbiner 2016) for the double-well potential to the anharmonic oscillator and sine-Gordon potential, and adds three-loop results via the generalized Bloch equation. The approach contrasts with standard WKB methods, which are difficult to extend beyond one dimension or beyond first corrections; the path-integral formulation with Feynman diagrams is intended to be generalizable to quantum field theories. The results enable systematic higher-order semiclassical calculations for multi-dimensional systems and field theories, with the observation that the classical flucton action always equals the zero-energy WKB action \(\int p\,dq\).
