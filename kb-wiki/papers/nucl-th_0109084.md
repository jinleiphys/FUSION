# Parity-Violating Interaction Effects I: the Longitudinal Asymmetry in pp Elastic Scattering

> The proton-proton parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry is calculated in the lab-energy range 0--350 MeV, using a number of different, latest-generation strong-interaction potentials--Argonne V18, Bonn-2000, and Nijmegen-I--in combination with a weak-interaction potential consisting of rho- and omega-meson exchanges--the model known as DDH. The complete scattering problem in the presence of parity-conserving, including Coulomb, and parity-violating potentials is solved in both configuration- and momentum-space. The predicted parity-violating asymmetries are found to be only weakly dependent upon the input strong-interaction potential adopted in the calculation. Values for the rho- and omega-meson weak coupling constants $h^{pp}_ρ$ and $h^{pp}_ω$ are determined by reproducing the measured asymmetries at 13.6 MeV, 45 MeV, and 221 MeV.

## Key claim

The parity-violating longitudinal asymmetry in proton-proton elastic scattering is calculated across 0–350 MeV and found to be only weakly dependent on which modern strong-interaction potential (Argonne V18, Bonn-2000, or Nijmegen-I) is used, provided the DDH weak-interaction model with rho- and omega-meson exchanges is adopted; the weak coupling constants \(h^{pp}_\rho\) and \(h^{pp}_\omega\) are then determined by fitting to measured asymmetries at three specific energies.

## Method

The complete scattering problem was solved in both configuration-space and momentum-space, including Coulomb, parity-conserving strong potentials (Argonne V18, Nijmegen-I, CD-Bonn), and a parity-violating weak potential based on rho- and omega-meson exchanges (the DDH model). For the local potentials (AV18, Nijmegen-I), coupled-channel Schrodinger equations were solved directly; for the non-local CD-Bonn potential, a momentum-space approach with a finite-range separation at 20 fm was used. The S-matrix was diagonalized to extract phase shifts and mixing angles, and the longitudinal asymmetry was computed from the spin-dependent scattering amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Measured asymmetry at 13.6 MeV: \((-0.95 \pm 0.15) \times 10^{-7}\) (Eversheim 1991)
- Measured asymmetry at 45 MeV: \((-1.50 \pm 0.23) \times 10^{-7}\) (Kistryn 1987)
- Measured asymmetry at 221 MeV: \((+0.84 \pm 0.29) \times 10^{-7}\) (Berdoz 2001)
- Older measurements at 15 MeV: \((-1.7 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-7}\) (Nagle 1979) and at 45 MeV: \((-2.3 \pm 0.9) \times 10^{-7}\) (Balzer 1984)
- Measurement at 800 MeV: \((+2.4 \pm 1.1) \times 10^{-7}\) (Yuan 1986)
- The AV18 and Nijmegen-I potentials were fitted to the 1992 Nijmegen database of 1787 pp data; the CD-Bonn potential was fitted to the 1999 database of 2932 pp data with chi-squared per datum of 1.01

## Context

This work sharpens and updates the earlier study by Driscoll and Miller (1989), who used a distorted-wave Born-approximation approach with older strong-interaction potentials and the same DDH weak model. By solving the full scattering problem with modern potentials (Argonne V18, Nijmegen-I, CD-Bonn) and including Coulomb effects exactly, the authors demonstrate that the predicted asymmetry is robust against changes in the strong potential. The extracted weak coupling constants enable more reliable predictions for ongoing and planned experiments on parity violation in np radiative capture and deuteron electro-disintegration, and help disentangle weak interaction effects from nucleon strange form factor extractions in SAMPLE-type measurements.
