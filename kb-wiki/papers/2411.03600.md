# Triple-$α$ reaction rates below $T_9=3$ by a non-adiabatic three-body model

> The triple-$α$ reaction from the ternary continuum states at off-resonant energies, $α+α+α\rightarrow^{12}$C, remains an open question. This direct process is scrutinized using a non-adiabatic Faddeev hyperspherical harmonics $R$-matrix expansion method, and the derived reaction rates are discussed. After reviewing the model, the resultant photo-disintegration of $^{12}$C($2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+$) is shown to be much smaller than the values predicted by the adiabatic models for $0.15 \le E \le 0.35$ MeV. Despite the large difference, the derived reaction rates are illustrated to be concordant with the current evaluated rates for $0.08 \le T_9 \le 3$. The difference below $E=0.20$ MeV can be seen in the rates for $T_9 \le 0.07$. In comparison with the calculations, the rates are found to be reduced by a factor of 10$^{-4}$ at $T_9=0.05$, because of an accurate description for $^8$Be break-up. Uncertainties of the rates are also estimated by examining the sensitivity to the 3$α$ potentials. By introducing three-body $S$-factors and a resonant term, the present rates are expressed in an analytic form, and they are provided in a tabular form for astrophysical applications. To update the evaluated rates, non-resonant sequential process between $α+^8$Be could be removed. The astrophysical impact is not expected to be large, although the rates are reduced around $T_9=0.05$.

# Triple-α reaction rates below T₉ = 3 by a non-adiabatic three-body model

## Key claim

The direct triple-α reaction rates derived from a non-adiabatic Faddeev hyperspherical harmonics R-matrix (HHR*) method are concordant with current evaluated rates for 0.08 ≤ T₉ ≤ 3, despite the calculated photo-disintegration cross sections of ¹²C(2⁺₁ → 0⁺) being much smaller than adiabatic model predictions for 0.15 ≤ E ≤ 0.35 MeV. The rates are reduced by a factor of 10⁻⁴ at T₉ = 0.05 due to an accurate description of ⁸Be break-up, but the astrophysical impact is not expected to be large.

## Method

The author solves the three-body Schrödinger equation for the α+α+α system using a non-adiabatic Faddeev approach with hyperspherical harmonics expansion and R-matrix theory (HHR*). The coupled-channel equations are solved by matrix diagonalization with harmonic oscillator basis functions in hyperspherical coordinates, using hyperangular momenta up to K=26 for 0⁺ and K=12 for 2⁺, with internal angular momenta up to lₓ₃=12 and lᵧ₃=12. The R-matrix radius is set to a_c = 100 fm, and the calculated 2⁺₁ wavefunction is normalized by matching the calculated γ width of 0⁺₂ to the experimental value of 3.9 ± 0.39 meV.

## Key numbers

- Photo-disintegration cross sections of ¹²C(2⁺₁ → 0⁺) are much smaller than adiabatic model predictions for the energy range 0.15 ≤ E ≤ 0.35 MeV
- Derived reaction rates are concordant with current evaluated rates for 0.08 ≤ T₉ ≤ 3
- Rates are reduced by a factor of 10⁻⁴ at T₉ = 0.05 compared to previous calculations
- The difference in rates appears for T₉ ≤ 0.07, corresponding to energies below E = 0.20 MeV
- The harmonic oscillator range parameter ν = 0.23 fm⁻¹ with n_ρ = 160 basis functions is used for efficient expansion
- The R-matrix radius a_c = 100 fm is used (a_c = 50 fm would lead to similar results)
- The 0⁺₂ state in ¹²C has energy E = 0.379 MeV above the 3α threshold

## Context

This work extends the Faddeev hyperspherical harmonics R-matrix (HHR) approach of Nguyen et al. (2012, 2013) and applies it to the direct triple-α process, which remains an open question at off-resonant energies. The non-adiabatic method contrasts with the adiabatic Coulomb modified Faddeev (CMF) method of Ishikawa (2013, 2014, 2016) and the adiabatic channel function (ACF) expansion method of Suno (2016), which assume constrained internal motion of the α-particles. The results enable updated triple-α reaction rates for astrophysical applications at low temperatures (T₉ < 0.1), relevant to novae, x-ray bursts, and type-Ia supernovae, and are provided in analytic form and tabular format for use in stellar evolution codes such as REACLIB.
