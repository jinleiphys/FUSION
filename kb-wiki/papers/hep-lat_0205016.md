# The QCD phase diagram for small densities from imaginary chemical potential

> We present results on the QCD phase diagram for mu_B <= pi T. Our simulations are performed with an imaginary chemical potential mu_I for which the fermion determinant is positive. On an 8^3 x 4 lattice with 2 flavors of staggered quarks, we map out the phase diagram and identify the pseudo-critical temperature T_c(mu_I). For mu_I/T <= pi/3, this is an analytic function, whose Taylor expansion is found to converge rapidly, with truncation errors far smaller than statistical ones. The truncated series may then be continued to real mu, yielding the corresponding phase diagram for mu_B <~ 500 MeV. This approach provides control over systematics and avoids reweighting. We compare it with other recent work.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that the QCD phase diagram for real baryon chemical potentials up to about 500 MeV can be reliably obtained by simulating with an imaginary chemical potential (where the fermion determinant is positive) and analytically continuing the resulting critical line, provided the Taylor expansion of the critical coupling converges rapidly and truncation errors are smaller than statistical uncertainties.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of staggered quarks on an 8^3 x 4 lattice at imaginary chemical potential mu_I, where the fermion determinant is positive and standard Monte Carlo sampling works. They measured susceptibilities of the plaquette, chiral condensate, and Polyakov loop to locate the pseudo-critical coupling beta_c(mu_I) for six values of a mu_I, then fitted the results to a Taylor series in (a mu_I)^2 and analytically continued to real chemical potential by flipping the sign of the expansion terms.

## Key numbers
- The accessible range of real baryon chemical potential is mu_B less than about 500 MeV, corresponding to the imaginary chemical potential limit mu_I/T <= pi/3.
- On the 8^3 x 4 lattice with bare quark mass am = 0.025, the critical coupling at zero chemical potential is beta_c(0) = 5.2865(18) from plaquette susceptibilities.
- The coefficient of the leading quadratic term in the Taylor expansion of beta_c is c_1 = 0.596(40) for fits to plaquette susceptibility data.
- The Z(3) transition occurs at the predicted critical value a mu_I^c = pi/12 = 0.262, with the critical coupling beta_c = 5.321(7) determined from cumulant crossing on 6^3 x 4 and 8^3 x 4 lattices.
- The cumulant ratio at the Z(3) critical point is 1.8(2), consistent with the 3d Ising universality class value 1.604(1).

## Context
This work extends the approach of using imaginary chemical potential previously applied to screening masses in dimensionally reduced QCD (Hart, Laine, Philipsen 2000, 2001) to the deconfinement critical line itself. It provides a controllable alternative to reweighting methods such as those of Fodor and Katz (2002) and the Taylor expansion approach of Gavai and Gupta (2002), which lack systematic error control. The method enables future finite-volume scaling studies to determine the order of the transition and locate the critical endpoint expected on general theoretical grounds (Karsch 1994).
