# Strong versus weak wave-turbulence in relativistic field theory

> Nonthermal scaling phenomena can exhibit a characteristic dependence on the dimensionality d of space. For d=3 and 4 we simulate a relativistic scalar field theory on a lattice and compute turbulent scaling exponents. We recover Kolmogorov or weak wave-turbulence in the perturbative high-momentum regime, where it exhibits the scaling exponent kappa_w = d - 3/2. In the nonperturbative infrared regime, we find a different scaling exponent kappa_s = 4 (5) for d=3 (4), which is in agreement with the recently predicted anomalously large values kappa_s = d + 1 of strong turbulence. We show how the latter can be seen to characterize stationary transport of a conserved effective particle number.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nonperturbative infrared scaling in relativistic scalar field theory exhibits a strong-turbulence exponent \(\kappa_s = d + 1\) (assuming dynamic exponent \(z=1\) and vanishing anomalous dimension \(\eta=0\)), which is associated with stationary transport of a conserved effective particle number, in contrast to the perturbative weak wave-turbulence exponent \(\kappa_w = d - 3/2\) observed at high momenta.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice simulations of a relativistic \(O(N)\)-symmetric scalar field theory in \(d=3\) and \(d=4\) spatial dimensions, using a leap-frog discretization on cubic lattices. Initial conditions were chosen to trigger parametric resonance instability, with a nonzero initial field expectation value \(\langle \varphi_1(t=0) \rangle = \phi_0\) and small-amplitude white noise on nonzero momentum modes. The momentum-dependent particle number spectrum was extracted from spatial Fourier transforms of the fields and averaged over noise realizations.

## Key numbers

* For \(d=3\): the infrared strong-turbulence scaling exponent \(\kappa_s \simeq 4\), in agreement with the predicted \(\kappa_s = d + 1 = 4\).
* For \(d=3\): the high-momentum weak wave-turbulence scaling exponent \(\kappa_w \simeq 1.5\), in agreement with the predicted \(\kappa_w = d - 3/2 = 1.5\).
* For \(d=4\): the infrared strong-turbulence scaling exponent \(\kappa_s \simeq 5\), in agreement with the predicted \(\kappa_s = d + 1 = 5\).
* For \(d=4\): the high-momentum weak wave-turbulence scaling exponent \(\kappa_w \simeq 2.5\), in agreement with the predicted \(\kappa_w = d - 3/2 = 2.5\).
* Simulations used lattice sizes of \(192^3\) for \(d=3\) and unspecified sizes for \(d=4\), with coupling \(\lambda = 24\) and \(m^2 = 0\) for the \(d=3\) case, and varied couplings for \(d=4\).
* The number of field components was varied between \(N=4\) and \(N=10\), with no observed dependence of the scaling exponents on \(N\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier discovery of nonperturbative infrared scaling in relativistic scalar field theory by Berges (2008) and the analytic prediction of a characteristic dependence on spatial dimensionality by Berges (2008). It provides numerical confirmation of the predicted strong-turbulence exponent \(\kappa_s = d + 1\) for \(d=3\) and \(d=4\), and shows that this scaling is associated with a conserved effective particle number. The results enable further classification of universality classes for nonthermal scaling phenomena in relativistic field theories, with implications for early universe reheating dynamics, supernova physics, and ultra-cold quantum gas experiments.
