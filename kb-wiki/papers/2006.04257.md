# The remnant of GW170817: a trapped neutron star with a hypermassive incompressible superfluid core

> Our bimetric spacetime model of glitching pulsars is applied to the remnant of GW170817. Accordingly, pulsars are born with embryonic incompressible superconducting gluon-quark superfluid cores (SuSu-matter) that are embedded in Minkowski spacetime, whereas the ambient compressible and dissipative media (CDM) are imbedded in curved spacetime. As pulsars cool down, the equilibrium between both spacetime is altered, thereby triggering the well-observed glitch phenomena. Based thereon and assuming all neutron stars (NSs) to be born with the same initial mass of $M_{NS}(t=0) \approx 1.25\,\mathcal{M}_{\odot},$ we argue that the remnant of GW170817 should be a relatively faint NS with a hypermassive central core made of SuSu-matter. The effective mass and radius of the remnant are predicted to be $[2.8 \mathcal{M}_{\odot} < \mathcal{M}_{rem} \le 3.351 \mathcal{M}_{\odot}]$ and $R_{rem}=10.764$ km, whereas the mass of the enclosed SuSu-core is $\mathcal{M}_{core}=1.7 \mathcal{M}_{\odot}.$ Here, about $1/2~ \mathcal{M}_{core}$ is an energy enhancement triggered by the phase transition of the gluon-quark-plasma from the microscopic into macroscopic scale. The current compactness of the remnant is $α_c = 0.918,$ but predicted to increase as the CDM and cools down, rendering the remnant an invisible dark energy object, and therefore to an excellent black hole candidate.

## Key claim

The remnant of GW170817 is predicted to be a relatively faint neutron star with a hypermassive central core made of incompressible superconducting gluon-quark superfluid (SuSu-matter), rather than a classical neutron star or a stellar black hole, under the assumption that all neutron stars are born with the same initial mass of approximately 1.25 solar masses.

## Method

The authors applied their bimetric spacetime model of glitching pulsars, previously used to study the Crab and Vela pulsars, to the GW170817 remnant. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov (TOV) equation using a polytropic equation of state with parameters fitted to the "alf4" and "sly" equations of state, employing explicit adaptive mesh refinement with approximately 10^5 grid points and an aspect ratio of 10^5 to capture strong gradients near the surface.

## Key numbers

- Remnant total mass: between 2.8 solar masses and 3.351 solar masses
- Remnant radius: 10.764 km
- Mass of the enclosed SuSu-core: 1.7 solar masses
- About half of the core mass (0.85 solar masses) is an energy enhancement from the phase transition of gluon-quark plasma from microscopic to macroscopic scale
- Current compactness of the remnant: alpha_c = 0.918
- Gravitational redshift at the surface: Z approximately 3, roughly four times larger than that of a massive neutron star with 2.075 solar masses
- Initial mass of newly born pulsars assumed: 1.25 solar masses
- Maximum mass achievable with standard equations of state (alf4 and sly): 2.075 solar masses
- Central density at which fluid becomes purely incompressible: rho_max = 6 times nuclear number density n_0

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous bimetric spacetime model of glitching pulsars (Hujeirat and Samtaney, 2019, 2020), which was applied to explain the glitch phenomena in the Crab and Vela pulsars. The model contradicts standard neutron star equations of state that cannot stably model interiors more massive than 2.075 solar masses (as shown with the alf4 equation of state from Aford (2005) and the sly equation of state from Douchin (2001)). The prediction enables future searches for dark energy objects that would appear as excellent black hole candidates, and suggests that the remnant's surface emission would be approximately 5 to 7 times fainter than that of normal neutron stars due to gravitational redshift.
