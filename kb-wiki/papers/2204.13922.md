# Nucleon and $О”$ resonances in $Оіn \to K^+ОЈ^-$ photoproduction

> The most recent data on beam asymmetries, $ОЈ$, and beam-target asymmetries, $E$, from the CLAS Collaboration, together with the previous data on differential cross sections and beam asymmetries from the CLAS and LEPS Collaborations, for the $Оіn \to K^+ОЈ^-$ reaction are studied based on an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree-level Born approximation. The $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast(892)$ exchanges, the $u$-channel $ОЈ$ exchange, the interaction current, and the exchanges of $N$, $О”$, and their excited states in the $s$ channel are considered in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the available experimental data. The reaction mechanisms of $Оіn \to K^+ОЈ^-$ are analyzed, and the associated resonances' parameters are extracted. The numerical results show that the $О”$ exchange and the $N(1710)1/2^+$, $N(1880)1/2^+$, $N(1900)3/2^+$, and $О”(1920)3/2^+$ resonance exchanges in the $s$-channel dominate the $Оіn \to K^+ОЈ^-$ reaction in the lower energy region, and the $t$-channel $K^\ast(892)$ exchange plays a crucial role at forward angles in the higher energy region.

## Method
The authors constructed reaction amplitudes using a tree-level Born approximation with effective Lagrangians, including t-channel K and K*(892) exchanges, u-channel ОЈ exchange, an interaction current, and s-channel exchanges of N, О”, and their excited states. They fitted 38 parameters for resonances and background contributions to 780 data points for Оіn вҶ’ KвҒәОЈвҒ» from threshold up to EОі = 3550 MeV, using the MINUIT minimization routine. The resonance masses and widths were varied within ranges advocated by RPP, and the products of electromagnetic and hadronic coupling constants were determined from the fit.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical analyses of KвҒәОЈвҒ» photoproduction by Vancraeyveld (2009), Mart (2019), Clymton (2021), and Luthfiyah (2021), which used Regge-plus-resonance or isobar models but only analyzed data published before 2010. The inclusion of the new CLAS beam-asymmetry and beam-target-asymmetry data provides additional constraints on the reaction dynamics. The results enable future coupled-channels analyses that properly account for unitarity and analyticity, which the authors note is beyond the scope of this work.
