# Nucleon and $Δ$ resonances in $γn \to K^+Σ^-$ photoproduction

> The most recent data on beam asymmetries, $Σ$, and beam-target asymmetries, $E$, from the CLAS Collaboration, together with the previous data on differential cross sections and beam asymmetries from the CLAS and LEPS Collaborations, for the $γn \to K^+Σ^-$ reaction are studied based on an effective Lagrangian approach in the tree-level Born approximation. The $t$-channel $K$ and $K^\ast(892)$ exchanges, the $u$-channel $Σ$ exchange, the interaction current, and the exchanges of $N$, $Δ$, and their excited states in the $s$ channel are considered in constructing the reaction amplitudes to describe the available experimental data. The reaction mechanisms of $γn \to K^+Σ^-$ are analyzed, and the associated resonances' parameters are extracted. The numerical results show that the $Δ$ exchange and the $N(1710)1/2^+$, $N(1880)1/2^+$, $N(1900)3/2^+$, and $Δ(1920)3/2^+$ resonance exchanges in the $s$-channel dominate the $γn \to K^+Σ^-$ reaction in the lower energy region, and the $t$-channel $K^\ast(892)$ exchange plays a crucial role at forward angles in the higher energy region.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the recently released CLAS beam-asymmetry (Σ) and beam-target-asymmetry (E) data for γn → K⁺Σ⁻ can be well described by an effective Lagrangian model that includes s-channel exchanges of the N(1710)1/2⁺, N(1880)1/2⁺, N(1900)3/2⁺, and Δ(1920)3/2⁺ resonances plus Δ exchange, but only after adding the N(1710)1/2⁺ resonance (which is marked as "seen" in its KΣ branching ratio) to the seven resonances that have considerable KΣ branching ratios according to the Review of Particle Physics (RPP).

## Method
The authors constructed reaction amplitudes using a tree-level Born approximation with effective Lagrangians, including t-channel K and K*(892) exchanges, u-channel Σ exchange, an interaction current, and s-channel exchanges of N, Δ, and their excited states. They fitted 38 parameters for resonances and background contributions to 780 data points for γn → K⁺Σ⁻ from threshold up to Eγ = 3550 MeV, using the MINUIT minimization routine. The resonance masses and widths were varied within ranges advocated by RPP, and the products of electromagnetic and hadronic coupling constants were determined from the fit.

## Key numbers
- The fit yielded χ²/N = 3.11 for 780 data points.
- The N(1710)1/2⁺ resonance was fitted with mass 1692 ± 1 MeV and width 161 ± 1 MeV, with extracted branching ratios β(Nγ) = 0.01% and β(KΣ) = 0.008%.
- The N(1880)1/2⁺ resonance was fitted with mass 1893 ± 1 MeV and width 400 ± 2 MeV, with β(Nγ) = 0.05% and β(KΣ) = 18.531%.
- The N(1900)3/2⁺ resonance was fitted with mass 1942 ± 1 MeV and width 122 ± 2 MeV, with β(Nγ) = 0.04% and β(KΣ) = 6.999%.
- The Δ(1920)3/2⁺ resonance was fitted with mass 1870 ± 1 MeV and width 360 ± 1 MeV, with β(Nγ) = 0.2% and β(KΣ) = 3.019%.
- The Δ(1950)7/2⁺ resonance was fitted with mass 1915 ± 1 MeV and width 235 ± 5 MeV, with β(Nγ) = 0.001% and β(KΣ) = 0.300%.
- The cutoff parameter for K*(K) exchange was fitted as 584 ± 1 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical analyses of K⁺Σ⁻ photoproduction by Vancraeyveld (2009), Mart (2019), Clymton (2021), and Luthfiyah (2021), which used Regge-plus-resonance or isobar models but only analyzed data published before 2010. The inclusion of the new CLAS beam-asymmetry and beam-target-asymmetry data provides additional constraints on the reaction dynamics. The results enable future coupled-channels analyses that properly account for unitarity and analyticity, which the authors note is beyond the scope of this work.
