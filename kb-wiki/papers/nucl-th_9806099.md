# Soft Neutron Production in DIS: a Window to the Final State Interactions

> Recently E-665 reported the first measurement of soft ($E_n \le 10 $MeV) neutron production in deep inelastic scattering (DIS) off nuclei. We report the first theoretical analysis of the data. We find that the observed cross section can be quantitatively explained as due to the final state interactions (FSI) of low energy nucleons ($E_N \sim 200-400 MeV$) produced in the elementary $μN$ interactions. We argue that the data indicate strong a suppression of the FSI's of fast partons (hadrons) in DIS at high energies, and that studies of the soft neutron production would provide a new sensitive probe of the dynamics of FSI's in DIS both at fixed target energies and at the HERA $eA$ collider.

## Key claim

The observed soft neutron multiplicity in DIS off lead can be quantitatively explained as a *lower limit* from final state interactions of low-energy nucleons (200-400 MeV) produced in the elementary muon-nucleon interaction, leaving little room for additional contributions from rescattering of fast partons or hadrons.

## Method

The authors modeled soft neutron production in DIS by first removing a nucleon from a random point in the lead nucleus with probability proportional to nuclear density, then assigning it a kinetic energy based on the measured spectrum of recoil nucleons in the target fragmentation region (Eq. 3). The subsequent interactions of this nucleon with the residual nucleus were simulated using a Monte Carlo cascade code that includes nucleon knock-out, pion production and absorption, preevaporation, and evaporation of neutrons and charged particles, tested against existing proton-nucleus data at intermediate energies.

## Key numbers

- Average soft neutron multiplicity for lead target: $\left<N_n(Pb)(E_n \le 10 \text{ MeV})\right> = 5 \pm 1$ (E-665 data)
- Calculated lower limit for lead: $\left<N_n(Pb)(E_n \le 10 \text{ MeV})\right>_{\text{lower limit}} = 6 \pm 1.5$
- Typical kinetic energies of nucleons produced in elementary DIS: 200-400 MeV, with average $\left<W_n\right> \approx 300$ MeV
- Nuclear shadowing correction for lead at average $x \approx 0.015$: $\eta \equiv \sigma_{shad}/\sigma_{imp.appr.} \approx 5\%$, leading to a multiplicity increase factor of $(1-\eta)^{-1} \approx 1.05$
- For proton-lead collisions at $E_p \sim 10$ GeV, neutron multiplicity is about a factor of 5-6 larger than for muon-lead DIS
- Per-wounded-nucleon neutron yield is still about a factor of two larger for proton projectiles than for muon projectiles
- An effective cross section $\sigma_{eff} = 10$ (20) mb for leading quark interactions would yield estimated multiplicities of $8.75 \pm 1.75$ ($12.5 \pm 2.5$), well above the experimental value

## Context

This work provides the first theoretical analysis of the E-665 measurement of soft neutron production in DIS (E-665 collaboration, 1997), extending earlier studies of final state interactions in DIS that focused on leading hadrons in the current fragmentation region (E-665, Busza, HERA). The results contradict the expectation that secondary interactions of slow nucleons would be the dominant source of soft neutrons, and instead suggest strong suppression of FSI of fast partons at high energies. The analysis enables future studies of FSI at the HERA eA collider using forward neutron calorimeters, and calls for systematic experimental studies at HERMES, COMPASS, and TJNAF to investigate the energy dependence of soft neutron production and correlations with leading hadron spectra.
