# Reaction-in-Flight Neutrons as a Signature for Shell Mixing in NIF capsules

> We present analytic calculations and results from computational simulations showing that reaction-in-flight (RIF) neutrons act as a robust indicator for mixing of the ablator shell material into the fuel in DT capsules designed for the National Ignition Facility. The sensitivity of RIF neutrons to hydrodynamical mixing arises through the dependence of RIF production on charged-particle stopping lengths in the mixture of DT fuel and ablator material. Since the stopping power in the plasma is a sensitive function of the electron temperature and density, it is also sensitive to mix. RIF production scales approximately inversely with the degree of mixing taking place, and the ratio of RIF to downscattered neutrons provides a measure of the mix fraction and/or the mixing length. For sufficiently high-yield capsules, where spatially resolved RIF images may be possible, neutron imaging could be used to map RIF images into detailed mix images.

# Reaction-in-Flight Neutrons as a Signature for Shell Mixing in NIF Capsules

## Key claim

Reaction-in-flight (RIF) neutrons provide a robust indicator for mixing of ablator shell material into DT fuel in NIF capsules, with RIF production scaling approximately inversely with the degree of mixing; however, this sensitivity depends on the charged-particle stopping length being short compared to the system size, and the quantitative relationship requires a transfer function that depends on the effective charge of the mix material and the electron temperature.

## Method

The authors performed analytic calculations of RIF production rates using a simplified model that expresses the total number of RIF neutrons in terms of knock-on production, charged-particle stopping lengths, and plasma properties. They then ran one-dimensional radiation-hydrodynamic-burn simulations incorporating the Scannapieco and Cheng mix model, which uses a single parameter α to control the dynamic mixing length, with charged-particle transport treated using an effective diffusion model.

## Key numbers

- RIF neutrons are expected to make up to about 1.5% of the total neutron production in high-yield NIF capsule implosions
- The RIF neutron spectrum extends up to about 17.6 MeV when alpha-induced RIF production is included
- The mixing parameter α was varied between 0 and 0.16 in the simulations, with Omega experiments typically requiring α ~ 0.06 to reproduce observed yields
- RIF neutrons were defined as all neutrons above 15 MeV; downscattered neutrons were defined as those in the energy range 10-12 MeV
- The unmixed shell material temperature was assumed to be 300 eV in the analytic model calculations

## Context

This work extends prior studies of knock-on production and charged-particle stopping in DT plasmas by Radha and Petrasso (various years), and builds on the Scannapieco and Cheng mix model for hydrodynamic mixing. The paper enables the possibility of using spatially resolved RIF neutron images to map mix fraction distributions in high-yield NIF capsules, assuming sufficient yields for a Uses of Ignition program. The approach contrasts with other neutron spectrum components that simply scale with total yield, offering a mix-specific diagnostic that could complement radiochemical techniques and neutron time-of-flight measurements.
