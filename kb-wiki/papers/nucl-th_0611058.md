# Matter induced charge symmetry breaking and pion form factor in nuclear medium

> Medium modification of pion form factor has been evaluated in asymmetric nuclear matter. It is shown that both the shape and the pole position of the pion form factor in dense asymmetric nuclear matter is different from its vacuum counterpart with $Žü$-$Žē$ mixing. This is due to the density and asymmetry dependent $Žü$-$Žē$ mixing which could even dominate over its vacuum counterpart in matter. Effect of the in-medium pion factor on experimental observables {\it e.g.}, invariant mass distribution of lepton pairs has been demonstrated.

# Matter induced charge symmetry breaking and pion form factor in nuclear medium

## Key claim

The pion form factor in dense asymmetric nuclear matter is modified not only by the usual vacuum $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing from the neutron-proton mass difference, but also by a density- and asymmetry-dependent mixing that can dominate over the vacuum contribution; this effect is substantial in the present relativistic mean-field model with tensor interactions, contrary to earlier QCD sum-rule studies (Zschocke, 2004) which found no significant effect on $F_\pi$.

## Method

The authors calculate the $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing amplitude in asymmetric nuclear matter using the Walecka relativistic mean-field model, evaluating both the Dirac-sea (vacuum) part and the density-dependent (Fermi-sphere) part of the nucleon-loop polarization functions. The in-medium pion form factor is then derived from the mixed $\rho$-$\omega$ propagator via vector-meson dominance, keeping the mixing angle arbitrary (not assuming the small-angle approximation). The resulting form factor is used to compute the $\pi^+\pi^- \to e^+e^-$ cross section and dilepton production rates at finite temperature.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing amplitude at the $\omega$ pole: $\sim -3447$ MeV$^2$ (with free nucleon masses), consistent with Gardner (1998).
- In-medium vacuum mixing amplitude $\Pi_{\rho\omega}^F(m_\omega^2)$: $-3716$ MeV$^2$ at $\rho_0$ and $-4675$ MeV$^2$ at $2\rho_0$ in symmetric nuclear matter.
- Coupling constants used: $g_\omega = 10.1$, $\kappa_\omega = 0$; $g_\rho = 2.63$, $\kappa_\rho = 6.0$; $g_{\rho\pi\pi}^2/4\pi \sim 2.9$, $g_\rho^2/4\pi \sim 2.0$.
- Ratio $g_\omega/g_\rho = 3.5 \pm 0.18$ from leptonic decay widths.
- The $\rho$-$\pi$-$\pi$ coupling can increase by $\sim 30\%$ near the $\rho$-pole at nuclear saturation density for finite $\Delta$ width, or up to double in zero-width approximation.
- Results shown for asymmetry parameters $\alpha = 0.3$ and $\alpha = 0.7$, at densities $\rho_0$ and $2\rho_0$, for three-momenta $|q| = 200$ MeV and $|q| = 600$ MeV.
- Dilepton production rates calculated at temperature $T = 100$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of $\rho$-$\omega$ mixing in matter by Dutt-Mazumder (1996) and Broniowski and Florkowski (1998), who used hadronic models, and by Dutt-Mazumder (2001) and Zschocke (2004) using QCD sum rules. Unlike the QCD sum-rule result of Zschocke (2004), the present relativistic mean-field calculation finds substantial medium effects on the pion form factor, attributed to tensor interactions. The formalism is developed for arbitrarily large mixing angles, relevant for highly asymmetric matter such as in $Pb$-like nuclei. The results are directly applicable to dilepton production in compressed baryonic matter at GSI energies, enabling experimental probes of in-medium vector-meson properties and charge symmetry violation.
