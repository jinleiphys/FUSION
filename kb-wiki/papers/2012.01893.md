# Multiple Dirac eikonal scattering of polarized intermediate-energy protons by nuclei

> An improved model of the multiple Dirac eikonal scattering of proton on nucleons of the target nucleus is considered. In this model, the amplitudes of elastic $p-A$ scattering are found on the basis of the Watson series of multiple scattering by means of the eikonal expansion of the Dirac propagator for the free proton motion between scattering events on nucleons, and the nucleus structure is described in the relativistic mean field model. The calculations have been performed for the complete set of observables for the elastic $p+^{40}$Ca and $p+^{208}$Pb scattering at 800 MeV. The effects of allowing for distinction between the relativistic scalar and vector nucleon densities on the description of the $p-A$ scattering observables have been studied, as well as the results of calculations using nucleon densities obtained in different modern variants of the relativistic mean field model have been compared.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved relativistic formulation of the multiple Dirac eikonal scattering (MDES) model for elastic proton-nucleus scattering, which for the first time incorporates the distinction between relativistic scalar and vector nucleon densities from the relativistic mean field (RMF) model, with the aim of improving the quantitative description of spin observables that the traditional nonrelativistic multiple diffraction scattering theory (MDST) fails to reproduce accurately.

## Method

The authors construct the elastic p-A scattering amplitude using the Watson series of multiple scattering, employing an eikonal expansion of the Dirac propagator for the free proton motion between successive scatterings on target nucleons. The target nucleus structure is described using relativistic mean field (RMF) models, and the elementary proton-nucleon amplitudes are taken from phase analysis solutions, restricted to central and spin-orbit terms. The calculations are performed for elastic p+⁴⁰Ca and p+²⁰⁸Pb scattering at 800 MeV, comparing results obtained with and without the scalar-vector density distinction, and using nucleon densities from different RMF model variants.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: 800 MeV for both p+⁴⁰Ca and p+²⁰⁸Pb scattering.
- The difference between scalar density ρ_Sj and vector density ρ_Vj is within 10% in the inner region of nuclei and practically vanishes in the surface region.
- The Sommerfeld parameter ξ = Ze²/ħv is used in the Coulomb phase formulas.
- The anomalous magnetic moment of the proton μ_a = 1.79 is used in the electromagnetic spin-orbit parameter κ_pp.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier MDES model (Pilipenko, 2015) which used only baryon densities and discarded scalar-vector density differences, and it builds upon the traditional MDST approach of Glauber (1959) and subsequent refinements by Wall (1974), Bleuler (1978), and others. The model serves as a relativistic complement to the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) approach of McNeil (1983), Ray (1985), and Tjon (1985) in the energy region above 500 MeV where the Glauber approximation is typically applied, and it enables more accurate analysis of complete sets of p-A scattering observables (differential cross section, analyzing power, and spin rotation function) for both stable and potentially unstable nuclei.
