# Recent astrophysical and accelerator based results on the Hadronic Equation of State

> In astrophysics as well as in hadron physics progress has recently been made on the determination of the hadronic equation of state (EOS) of compressed matter. The results are contradictory, however. Simulations of heavy ion reactions are now sufficiently robust to predict the stiffness of the (EOS) from (i) the energy dependence of the ratio of $K^+$ from Au+Au and C+C collisions and (ii) the centrality dependence of the $K^+$ multiplicities. The data are best described with a compressibility coefficient at normal nuclear matter density $κ$ around 200 MeV, a value which is usually called ``soft'' The recent observation of a neutron star with a mass of twice the solar mass is only compatible with theoretical predictions if the EOS is stiff. We review the present situation.

## Key claim

The hadronic equation of state (EOS) extracted from subthreshold K+ production in heavy-ion collisions around 1 A GeV is soft, with compressibility kappa around 200 MeV or less, but this result appears to contradict the recent astrophysical observation of a neutron star with twice the solar mass, which requires a stiff EOS.

## Method

The authors employed the Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) transport approach, which calculates the time evolution of nucleons described by Gaussian wave functions using a variational principle that yields classical Hamilton equations with the expectation value of the real part of the Brueckner G-matrix as the two-body potential. The five-parameter potential was fixed by the binding energy at saturation density, the compressibility kappa, and the optical potential from pA reactions, then used to simulate K+ production in Au+Au and C+C collisions. Two observables were compared with experimental data from the KaoS collaboration: (i) the energy dependence of the ratio of K+ multiplicities per mass number from Au+Au and C+C collisions, and (ii) the centrality dependence of K+ multiplicities parameterized as M_K+ = A_part^alpha.

## Key numbers

- Compressibility coefficient kappa around 200 MeV (soft EOS) best describes the K+ data, compared to kappa = 380 MeV for a hard EOS.
- The binding energy per nucleon at equilibrium density rho_0 is E/A(rho=rho_0, T=0) = -16 MeV, known from the Weizsaecker mass formula.
- The effective energy threshold for K+ production in the NN center-of-mass system is 671 MeV.
- The kaon mass in medium is modified as m^K(rho) = m^K_0 (1 - 0.075 rho/rho_0).
- The measured centrality scaling exponent for Au+Au at 1.5 A GeV from the KaoS collaboration is alpha = 1.34 +/- 0.16, compatible only with a soft EOS.
- The compressibility extracted from giant monopole resonances is around kappa = 240 MeV.
- A neutron star with mass of two solar masses has been observed, which is only compatible with a stiff EOS.

## Context

This work extends previous efforts to determine the nuclear equation of state from heavy-ion collisions, specifically the approach using subthreshold K+ production first proposed by Aichelin and Ko (1985). The results contradict earlier conclusions from in-plane flow analyses by Danielewicz, Lacey, and Lynch (2002) that favored a hard EOS, though those conclusions were later shown to be premature due to quantitative disagreements among transport theories as demonstrated by Andronic et al. (2005). The soft EOS result from heavy-ion data also conflicts with the astrophysical requirement from the two-solar-mass neutron star observation, which demands a stiff EOS; this tension motivates further work to reconcile laboratory and astrophysical constraints on the hadronic equation of state.
