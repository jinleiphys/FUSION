# Toroidal, compression, and vortical dipole strengths in 124Sn

> The toroidal, compression and vortical dipole strength functions in semi-magic $^{124}$Sn (and partly in doubly-magic $^{100,132}$Sn) are analyzed within the random-phase-approximation method with the SkT6, SkI3, SLy6, SV-bas, and SkM* Skyrme forces. The isoscalar (T=0), isovector (T=1), and electromagnetic ('elm') channels are considered. Both convection $j_c$ and magnetization $j_m$ nuclear currents are taken into account. The calculations basically confirm the previous results obtained for $^{208}$Pb with the force SLy6. In particular, it is shown that the vortical and toroidal strengths are dominated by $j_c$ in T=0 channel and by $j_m$ in T=1 and 'elm' channels. The compression strength is always determined by $j_c$. It is also shown that the 'elm' strength (relevant for (e,e') reaction) is very similar to T=1 one. The toroidal mode resides in the region of the pygmy resonance. So, perhaps, this region embraces both irrotational (pygmy) and vortical (toroidal) flows.

## Key claim

The toroidal dipole mode resides in the same energy region (6-10 MeV) as the pygmy resonance, suggesting that this region may host both irrotational (pygmy) and vortical (toroidal) flows, though the authors hedge that this is a possibility ("perhaps") rather than a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) calculations using five Skyrme forces (SkT6, SV-bas, SkM*, SLy6, SkI3) with different isoscalar effective masses. They computed toroidal, compression, and vortical dipole strength functions for the semi-magic nucleus 124Sn and partly for the doubly-magic isotopes 100Sn and 132Sn, considering isoscalar (T=0), isovector (T=1), and electromagnetic ('elm') channels with both convection and magnetization nuclear currents.

## Key numbers

- The toroidal and vortical strengths in the T=0 channel are dominated by the convection current j_c, while in T=1 and 'elm' channels they are dominated by the magnetization current j_m
- The compression mode has zero contribution from the magnetization current j_m and is fully determined by the convection current j_c
- The pygmy resonance region 6-10 MeV embraces noticeable fractions of the toroidal and vortical strengths, especially in the T=0 channel
- For the force SLy6, the toroidal and vortical strengths are peaked in the 6-10 MeV region
- The compression mode low-energy branch at 6-10 MeV grows considerably from 100Sn to 132Sn, indicating strong dominance of neutron oscillations
- The toroidal and vortical strengths show weak dependence on the neutron skin from 100Sn to 132Sn, suggesting the vortical motion takes place mainly in the N=Z core
- The best agreement with experimental photoabsorption data in 124Sn was obtained with the SLy6 force

## Context

This work extends the previous SRPA study of toroidal, compression, and vortical dipole strengths in 208Pb by Kvasil et al. (2011) using the SLy6 force to the semi-magic nucleus 124Sn with a wider set of Skyrme parameterizations. The results confirm the earlier findings and establish that the electromagnetic ('elm') channel relevant for (e,e') reactions is very similar to the T=1 channel, meaning the E1(T=1) toroidal mode represents an unusual case of electric collective motion driven by the magnetization current. The study enables future experimental investigation of these modes via (e,e') reactions and clarifies the relationship between the pygmy resonance and toroidal/compression modes in neutron-rich nuclei.
