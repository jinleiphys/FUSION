# Systematic shell-model study of $^{98-130}$Cd isotopes and $8^+$ isomeric states

> We present systematic shell-model studies of even-even $^{98-130}$Cd isotopes using a realistic effective shell-model interaction derived from the G-matrix approach with an inert core $^{88}$Sr. Our calculated low-lying excited energy spectra and electromagnetic properties are compared with the experimental data. On the basis of recently available experimental data, we predict spins and parities corresponding to unconfirmed states. We also discuss the properties of $8^+$ isomeric states in $^{98-104,130}$Cd isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper presents systematic shell-model calculations for even-even $^{98-130}$Cd isotopes using a G-matrix effective interaction with an inert $^{88}$Sr core, claiming good agreement with experimental data for low-lying states but noting that calculated energy spectra deviate from experiment starting from $^{110}$Cd and become compressed up to $^{120}$Cd, with the authors attributing this to limitations of the realistic interaction and missing degrees of freedom in the valence space.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the KSHELL code with a model space comprising $1p_{1/2}$ and $0g_{9/2}$ proton orbitals and $1d_{5/2}$, $2s_{1/2}$, $1d_{3/2}$, $0g_{7/2}$, and $0h_{11/2}$ neutron orbitals above an inert $^{88}$Sr core. The effective interaction was derived from a G-matrix approach based on a charge-symmetry breaking nucleon-nucleon potential with monopole modifications, previously used in Boelaert et al. (2010). The largest M-scheme dimension was 0.96 × 10^9 for $^{114}$Cd.

## Key numbers

- The largest M-scheme dimension in the calculations is 0.96 × 10^9 for $^{114}$Cd.
- For $^{102}$Cd, the calculated half-life of the $4^+_1$ state is 1.44 ps, compared to the experimental upper limit of less than 5.6 ps.
- For $^{104}$Cd, the calculated half-life of the $4^+_1$ state is 2.37 ps, compared to the experimental upper limit of less than 4.2 ps.
- For $^{106}$Cd, the calculated $R_{4/2}$ ratio is 2.393, compared to the experimental value of 2.360.
- For $^{126}$Cd, the calculated $7^-$ state differs from the tentative experimental level by 15 keV.
- For $^{98}$Cd, the calculated $B(E2; 4^+_1 \rightarrow 6^+_1)$ value is 0.018 $e^2$b$^2$, matching the experimental value of 0.018(3) $e^2$b$^2$.
- For $^{102}$Cd, the calculated $B(E2; 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ value is 0.274 $e^2$b$^2$, compared to the experimental value of 0.257(23) $e^2$b$^2$.
- For $^{106}$Cd, the calculated $B(E2; 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ value is 0.444 $e^2$b$^2$, compared to the experimental value of 0.407(12) $e^2$b$^2$.
- For $^{108}$Cd, the calculated $B(E2; 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ value is 0.487 $e^2$b$^2$, compared to the experimental value of 0.419(14) $e^2$b$^2$.
- For $^{110}$Cd, the calculated $B(E2; 0^+_1 \rightarrow 2^+_1)$ value is 0.547 $e^2$b$^2$, compared to the experimental value of 0.426(21) $e^2$b$^2$.

## Context

This work extends previous shell-model studies of Cd isotopes by Schmidt et al. (1999) for $^{98-108}$Cd and Boelaert et al. (2010) for $^{98-106}$Cd, covering the full neutron range from N=50 to N=82 for the first time with a consistent interaction. The calculations enable comparison with recent experimental data from TITAN at TRIUMF and GRIFFIN, and provide predictions for unconfirmed spin-parity assignments and $8^+$ isomeric states in $^{98-104,130}$Cd that can guide future experiments.
