# Are the Dressed Gluon and Ghost Propagators in the Landau Gauge presently determined in the confinement regime of QCD?

> The Gluon and Ghost propagators in Landau gauge QCD are investigated using the Schwinger-Dyson equation approach. Working in Euclidean spacetime, we solve for these propagators using a selection of vertex inputs, initially for the ghost equation alone and then for both propagators simultaneously. The results are shown to be highly sensitive to the choices of vertices. We favor the infrared finite ghost solution from studying the ghost equation alone where we argue for a specific unique solution. In order to solve this simultaneously with the gluon using a dressed-one-loop truncation, we find that a non-trivial full ghost-gluon vertex is required in the vanishing gluon momentum limit. The self-consistent solutions we obtain correspond to having a mass-like term in the gluon propagator dressing, in agreement with similar studies supporting the long held proposal of Cornwall.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the infrared behavior of the gluon and ghost propagators in Landau gauge QCD is not yet definitively determined, because the solutions are highly sensitive to arbitrary choices of the ghost-gluon and triple-gluon vertices. They favor the infrared finite ghost solution obtained by renormalizing at a perturbative momentum point, which excludes the singular ghost solution without fine-tuning.

## Method

The authors solve the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the gluon and ghost propagators in Euclidean Landau gauge, using a dressed-one-loop truncation that neglects two-loop diagrams. They first solve the ghost equation alone with a fixed model gluon input, then solve both propagators simultaneously using various vertex ansätze, including the bare vertex, a transverse vertex, and a vertex modified to satisfy Taylor's theorem. The solutions are obtained numerically with a momentum subtraction renormalization scheme.

## Key numbers

* The model gluon dressing function uses parameters $\mu^{2}=10^{4}$, $m^{2}=0.1$, $g^{2}(\mu^{2})/4\pi=0.12$ and $\rho=1$ to produce a gluon propagator dressing that behaves as $p^{2}$ in the infrared.
* For the ghost equation solved with a fixed gluon input, ghost dressings with infrared values in the range $2<\Gh(0)<\infty$ are negligibly different in the ultraviolet region.
* Using the vertex of Eq. (12) (modified to satisfy Taylor's theorem) produces corrections of up to 20% over the bare vertex result for the ghost dressing function.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate between two classes of solutions for the Landau gauge propagators: the singular ghost solution with linked infrared power laws (Alkofer 2003, Fischer 2008) and the finite ghost solution (Boucaud 2005, Aguilar 2009, Rodriguez-Quintero 2010). The authors argue that only the finite ghost solution is compatible with a perturbative renormalization scheme without fine-tuning, and that lattice QCD results (Bogolubsky 2009, Cucchieri 2010) currently favor a finite ghost dressing. The results are intended to inform future Bethe-Salpeter and Faddeev equation calculations of hadron masses and form factors.
