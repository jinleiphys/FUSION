# Deuterium-tritium fusion process in strong laser fields: Semiclassical simulation

> In this paper, we investigate the deuterium-tritium (DT) fusion process in the presence of strong laser fields with a semiclassical (SC) method. In this model, two nuclei with a given incident kinetic energy that closely approach each other are simulated by tracing the classical Newtonian trajectories in the combined Coulomb repulsive potentials and laser fields. At the nearest position or classical turning point, quantum tunneling through the Coulomb barrier emerges, and its penetrability is estimated with the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin formula. Nuclear fusion occurs after the tunneling, and the total fusion cross section takes the Gamow form. We find that the tunneling penetrability can be enhanced dramatically because the nuclei can closely approach each other due to the quiver motion of the charged nuclei driven by the intense laser fields. We then calculate the DT fusion section for a wide range of laser parameters according to various incident nuclei kinetic energies and obtain the phase diagrams for the enhanced DT fusion. We compare our SC results with the quantum results of the Kramers-Henneberger approximation and the Volkov state approximation.

## Key claim

The semiclassical model predicts that the DT fusion cross section can be enhanced dramatically by strong laser fields, but this enhancement is strongly frequency-dependent and only becomes significant above a critical laser intensity that depends on both the field frequency and the incident kinetic energy.

## Method

The authors simulate the relative motion of deuterium and tritium nuclei by solving classical Newtonian equations in the combined Coulomb repulsive potential and a linearly polarized laser field, using a 4-5th Runge-Kutta algorithm. At the classical turning point, quantum tunneling penetrability through the Coulomb barrier is calculated with the Wentzel-Kramers-Brillouin (WKB) formula, and the total fusion cross section is obtained from the Gamow formula using an astrophysical S-factor fitted to experimental data. The initial state of the incident nucleus is represented by a Gaussian wave packet, and more than 10^5 classical trajectories are sampled via Monte Carlo to ensure numerical convergence.

## Key numbers

- The Coulomb barrier peak energy for DT fusion is V_b = 370 keV, and the contact radius is r_n ≈ 3.89 fm.
- For incident energy ε = 1 keV and laser frequency ω = 10 eV, the penetrability enhancement is over eight orders of magnitude for η = 0.002 and over twelve orders of magnitude for η = 0.01 (where η = v_quiver/c).
- For ω = 1 eV, the fusion cross section enhancement is above approximately six orders of magnitude at I = 10^22 W/cm^2.
- For ω = 1 keV, the enhancement is less than one order of magnitude at I = 10^26 W/cm^2.
- The Schwinger limit for laser intensity is 4.6 × 10^29 W/cm^2, and the QED limit for laser frequency is 0.51 MeV.
- For incident energy ε = 1 keV, the effective enhancement region is bounded approximately by η > 0.0002; for ε = 10 keV, the region shrinks and is bounded by η > 0.001 and ω > 10 eV.

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies on laser-assisted nuclear processes, including Floquet scattering theory (Floquet, year not given), the Kramers-Henneberger (KH) approximation by Lv (year not given), and the Volkov state approximation (VSA) by Wang (year not given). The semiclassical approach bridges the high-frequency regime where KH theory applies and the low-frequency regime where VSA applies, and it shows that VSA overestimates the cross section by up to five orders of magnitude at high intensities. The results provide phase diagrams for laser parameters that could be tested at future ultra-intense laser facilities such as the Extreme Light Infrastructure (ELI).
