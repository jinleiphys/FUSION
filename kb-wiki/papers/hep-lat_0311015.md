# The Study of Chiral Restoration using the Quark-Gluon Mixed Condensate g<\bar{q} σG q> in Lattice QCD at Finite Temperature

> The quark-gluon mixed condensate g<\bar{q} σG q> is studied using SU(3)c lattice QCD with the Kogut-Susskind fermion at the quenched level. Using the lattices as β= 6.0 with 16^3 * N_t (N_t=16,12,10,8,6,4), β= 6.1 with 20^3 * N_t (N_t=20,12,10,8,6) and β= 6.2 with 24^3 * N_t (N_t=24,16,12,10,8) in high statistics of 100-1000 gauge configurations, we perform accurate measurement of the thermal effects on g<\bar{q} σG q> as well as <\bar{q}q> in the chiral limit. We find that the thermal effects on both the condensates are very weak except for the vicinity of T_c, while both the condensates suddenly vanish around T_c \simeq 280 MeV, which indicates strong chiral restoration near T_c. We also find that the ratio m_0^2 = g<\bar{q} σG q>/ <\bar{q}q> is almost independent of the temperature even in the very vicinity of T_c, which means that these two different condensates obey the same critical behavior. This nontrivial similarity between them would impose constraints on the chiral structure of the QCD vacuum near T_c.

## Key claim

The quark-gluon mixed condensate and the quark condensate both vanish suddenly near the critical temperature T_c ≈ 280 MeV, indicating strong chiral restoration, but the ratio m_0^2 = g⟨q̄σGq⟩/⟨q̄q⟩ remains almost independent of temperature even very close to T_c, meaning the two condensates obey the same critical behavior.

## Method

The authors performed quenched SU(3)_c lattice QCD simulations using the Kogut-Susskind fermion with the standard Wilson action at β = 6.0, 6.1, and 6.2 on lattices of varying temporal extent (N_t = 4–24) to achieve different temperatures. They measured the quark-gluon mixed condensate g⟨q̄σGq⟩ and the quark condensate ⟨q̄q⟩ at current-quark masses m = 21, 36, 52 MeV, then extrapolated to the chiral limit using a linear fit. The clover-type definition for the gluon field strength was used to eliminate O(a) discretization error, and the condensate ratios between finite and zero temperature were taken to cancel renormalization constants.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c ≈ 280 MeV at the quenched level, determined from the sudden disappearance of both condensates.
- Lattice spacings: a ≈ 0.10 fm at β = 6.0, a ≈ 0.09 fm at β = 6.1, a ≈ 0.07 fm at β = 6.2, set to reproduce the string tension σ = 0.89 GeV/fm.
- Statistical errors on condensates are typically less than 5%; finite volume artifact is estimated at about 1%.
- 100–1000 gauge configurations were used per lattice, with 1000 configurations for the two lattices nearest T_c (20^3 × 8 at β = 6.1 and 24^3 × 10 at β = 6.2).
- Thermal effects on both condensates are very weak below T ≈ 0.9 T_c.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations of the quark-gluon mixed condensate at zero temperature by Doi et al. (2003) and by other groups using Domain-Wall fermions, and provides the first lattice study of the thermal behavior of the mixed condensate at finite temperature. The finding that m_0^2 is temperature-independent near T_c constrains models of the QCD vacuum and chiral symmetry restoration, and the authors note that a full QCD (unquenched) lattice calculation is in progress to include dynamical quark effects.
