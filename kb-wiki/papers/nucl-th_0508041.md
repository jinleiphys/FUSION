# Isospin relations for four nucleons in a single j shell

> We had previously used techniques involving isospin to count the number of states for three identical fermions in a single j shell with total angular momentum I=j. We generalize this to all I, but the main thrust of this work is to consider now a 4-fermion system. As before, one evaluates the eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian \sum_{i<j}[a + bt(i)t(j)] both from an isospin point of view and an angular momentum point of view. In the 4-particle case, we get a more limited result than in the 3-particle case, namely the number of T=0 states minus twice the number of T=2 states, all of a given angular momentum I.

# Isospin relations for four nucleons in a single j shell

## Key claim
For a system of four nucleons (two protons and two neutrons) in a single j shell, the number of T=0 states minus twice the number of T=2 states at a given angular momentum I can be expressed in terms of a sum over even angular momenta J_P and J_N involving a 9j symbol. This is a more limited result than the three-particle case, where the full number of T=3/2 states could be obtained.

## Method
The authors consider a Hamiltonian of the form V = Σ_{i<j}[a + b t(i)·t(j)] acting on a four-nucleon system with two protons and two neutrons in a single j shell. They evaluate the expectation value of this Hamiltonian both from an isospin perspective (using the known eigenvalue formula in terms of total isospin T) and from an angular momentum perspective (using a basis where two protons are coupled to J_P and two neutrons to J_N, then coupled to total I). By equating the traces from both approaches, they derive a relation connecting the number of states of each isospin to a sum over 9j symbols.

## Key numbers
- For A=4 valence nucleons, the isospin eigenvalue of the Hamiltonian is ⟨V⟩ = 6a − 3b/2 + bT(T+1)/2.
- The angular momentum expression for the matrix element is ⟨[J_P J_N]^I | V | [J_P J_N]^I⟩ = 6a − b/2 [1 + 4(2J_P+1)(2J_N+1) times a 9j symbol].
- The derived relation is: X(I) = N(0) − 2N(2), where X(I) = 2 Σ_{J_P,J_N even} (2J_P+1)(2J_N+1) times the 9j symbol with rows j,j,J_P; j,j,J_N; J_P,J_N,I.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous isospin-counting technique for three identical fermions in a single j shell (Zamick and Escuderos, 2005) to the four-particle case. It builds on the earlier work of Ginocchio and Haxton (1993) on counting states of given angular momentum for identical fermions, and parallels recent results by Zhao and Arima (2005) who obtained expressions for X(I) for four identical nucleons using a J-pairing Hamiltonian. The result enables future work to determine the number of T=0 and T=2 states separately for four nucleons in a single j shell, though the present relation only gives their weighted difference.
