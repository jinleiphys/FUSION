# Dynamic Spin Response for Heisenberg Ladders

> We employ the recently proposed plaquette basis to investigate static and dynamic properties of isotropic 2-leg Heisenberg spin ladders. Simple non-interacting multi-plaquette states provide a remarkably accurate picture of the energy/site and dynamic spin response of these systems. Insights afforded by this simple picture suggest a very efficient truncation scheme for more precise calculations. When the small truncation errors are accounted for using recently developed Contractor Renormalization techniques, very accurate results requiring a small fraction of the computational effort of exact calculations are obtained. These methods allow us to determine the energy/site, gap, and spin response of 2x16 ladders. The former two values are in good agreement with density matrix renormalization group results. The spin response calculations show that nearly all the strength is concentrated in the lowest triplet level and that coherent many-body effects enhance the response/site by nearly a factor of 1.6 over that found for 2x2 systems.

## Key claim

The plaquette basis reveals that the low-energy physics of isotropic 2-leg Heisenberg spin ladders is dominated by a small number of "frozen" configurations where the diagonal spins of each plaquette are coupled to triplets, and that a simple non-interacting multi-plaquette picture already captures the essential features of the dynamic spin response, with coherent many-body effects enhancing the response per site by nearly a factor of 1.6 over the single-plaquette value.

## Method

The authors decompose the 2-leg ladder into 2x2 plaquettes and construct a basis where the intra-plaquette Hamiltonian is diagonal. They truncate the basis to retain only the low-energy "frozen" triplet-triplet configurations and apply the Contractor Renormalization (CORE) method up to range-3 to correct for truncation errors in both the Hamiltonian and the spin transition operator. They compute the dynamic spin response S(q,ω) at momentum transfer q=(π,π) for 2xL ladders with L=6, 8, and 16 using Lanczos techniques in the truncated frozen basis.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy per site for 2x8 ladder: CORE range-3 value -0.55711 (0.020% discrepancy from exact -0.55722)
- Ground state energy per site for 2x16 ladder: CORE range-3 value -0.56742 (0.037% discrepancy from DMRG -0.56763)
- Spin gap (E1-E0) for 2x8 ladder: 0.62835 (0.423% discrepancy from exact 0.63101)
- Spin gap (E1-E0) for 2x16 ladder: 0.54441 (1.782% discrepancy from DMRG 0.55411)
- Integrated spin response to lowest triplet level S(qππ,Δ)/site for 2x8 ladder: 0.99920 (0.014% discrepancy from exact 0.99934)
- Integrated spin response to lowest triplet level S(qππ,Δ)/site for 2x16 ladder: 1.08785 (no exact or DMRG comparison available)
- Single-plaquette response per site: 2/3
- Extrapolated thermodynamic limit response per site: between 1.10 and 1.12, corresponding to enhancement factor of approximately 1.6 over single-plaquette value
- Non-interacting multi-plaquette estimate of energy/site: -0.5 (compared to DMRG bulk value -0.57804)
- Second-order perturbation theory improves plaquette basis energy/site to -0.55816 (3.4% discrepancy)

## Context

This work extends the plaquette basis approach introduced by Piekarewicz and Shepard (1996) to larger systems and dynamical quantities, building on prior numerical studies using direct diagonalization, Lanczos techniques, quantum Monte Carlo (Barnes et al., 1993), and density matrix renormalization group (White et al., 1994). The results enable comparison with future inelastic neutron scattering measurements on single-crystal ladder compounds such as vanadyl pyrophosphate, and demonstrate that the CORE method with the truncated plaquette basis can achieve high accuracy for 2x16 ladders with computational effort comparable to exact diagonalization of 2x8 systems.
