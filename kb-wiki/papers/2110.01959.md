# Calculations for nuclear matter and finite nuclei within and beyond energy--density--functional theories through interactions guided by effective field theory

> We propose a novel idea to construct an effective interaction under energy-density-functional (EDF) theories which is adaptive to the enlargement of the model space. Guided by effective field theory principles, iterations of interactions as well as enlargements of the model space through particle-hole excitations are carried out for infinite nuclear matter and selected closed-shell nuclei ($^4$He, $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca, $^{56}$Ni and $^{100}$Sn) up to next-to-leading order. Our approach provides a new way for handling the nuclear matter and finite nuclei within the same scheme, with advantages from both EDF and ab initio approaches.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a novel framework that combines energy-density-functional (EDF) and effective field theory (EFT) ideas to construct an effective interaction that is adaptive to the enlargement of the model space, allowing for order-by-order improvements for both infinite nuclear matter and finite nuclei. The key hedge is that the leading-order (LO) calculations systematically overbind nuclei, and only by including specific next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections (prescription ii) with Skyrme-type \(t_1, t_2\) terms can reasonable agreement with experimental binding energies be achieved, with errors growing for heavier nuclei beyond \(^{40}\)Ca.

## Method

The authors start with a LO interaction based on a \(t_0-t_3\) Skyrme-type model, evaluated in a shell-model harmonic oscillator basis up to the highest occupied shell. At NLO, they include once-iterated diagrams (particle-hole excitations) using the LO interaction, renormalized by matching to symmetric matter and neutron matter equations of state (EoS) from the SLy5 functional. They extract an effective Fermi momentum \(k_A\) for each finite nucleus by comparing ratios of interaction terms to those in nuclear matter, then compute NLO ground-state energies for five closed-shell nuclei (\(^4\)He, \(^{16}\)O, \(^{40}\)Ca, \(^{56}\)Ni, \(^{100}\)Sn) using two prescriptions: (i) iterating only the \(t_0\) part with a contact counterterm, and (ii) adding Skyrme \(t_1, t_2\) terms as counterterms.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies at LO for \(^4\)He, \(^{16}\)O, and \(^{40}\)Ca are systematically overbound relative to experimental data, even at the empirical \(\hbar\omega \approx 45A^{-1/3} - 25A^{-2/3}\) (e.g., \(\hbar\omega \approx 13\) MeV for \(^{16}\)O).
- Extracted effective Fermi momenta \(k_A\) for \(^{16}\)O range from 1.08 fm\(^{-1}\) at \(\hbar\omega = 11\) MeV to 1.38 fm\(^{-1}\) at \(\hbar\omega = 18\) MeV; for \(^{40}\)Ca, \(k_A\) ranges from 1.25 fm\(^{-1}\) at \(\hbar\omega = 11\) MeV to 1.58 fm\(^{-1}\) at \(\hbar\omega = 18\) MeV.
- With NLO prescription (ii) fitted only to EoS, relative errors in binding energies are \(\leq 8\%\) for nuclei up to \(A=40\), but grow to \(\sim 15\%\) for \(^{56}\)Ni and \(^{100}\)Sn.
- With a global fit (NLO (ii)\(_{\text{fit all}}\)) to EoS and all five nuclei, all binding energies are reproduced within \(3\%\), allowing the symmetric matter EoS to be \(\leq 2\%\) more attractive around saturation than the SkP EoS.

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to bridge EDF and EFT ideas, such as those by Yang et al. (2016, 2017) and Burrello et al. (2020) for infinite matter, and by Brenna et al. (2014) for \(^{16}\)O binding energy with second-order Dyson diagrams. It addresses the longstanding problem that EDF interactions are fitted at the mean-field level and cannot be consistently improved order by order, while ab initio methods face model-space convergence issues. The approach enables a unified treatment of nuclear matter and finite nuclei, potentially applicable across the nuclear chart, and opens the door to higher-order (NNLO) calculations and a full EFT power-counting analysis.
