# Modeling Ultra-High-Energy Cosmic Rays propagation using the input from Configuration Interaction Shell Model

> The dipole response of a nuclear system, characterized by its photon strength function (PSF), is a key ingredient of many applications of nuclear structure, ranging from nuclear reactor design and nuclear waste transmutation to astrophysical models of nucleosynthesis and stellar evolution. While the majority of those applications require the knowledge of PSF of mid-mass and heavy nuclei, there is now renewed interest in $E1$ strength distributions of light nuclei in the framework of the PANDORA project, which aims at an understanding of the mass distribution of ultrahigh-energy cosmic radiation (UHECR).UHECR is of extragalactic origin and its interaction along the travel path is dominated by photoabsorption of cosmic background radiation boosted to the Giant Dipole Resonance (GDR) energy region in the center-of-mass system. Thus, systematic knowledge of the photoabsorption cross sections in light nuclei and of their subsequent particle decay is required. The purpose of this work is to enhance the database of available theoretical evaluations of PSF of light nuclei that are necessary in the studies of UHECR propagation. We employ the Configuration Interaction Shell Model (CI-SM) approach to provide predictions of $E1$ dipole response for $p$ and $sd$-shell nuclei, with mass number $A$ between 7 and 40. Theoretical predictions are compared to available data and to existing predictions from phenomenological and microscopic models. Finally, the impact of using of CI-SM PSF on the predicted propagation of a $^{40}$Ca UHECR source is studied.

## Key claim

The Configuration Interaction Shell Model (CI-SM) provides predictions of electric dipole photon strength functions for light nuclei (mass number A between 7 and 40) that exhibit stronger variation and fragmentation than linear-response and phenomenological models, and when used in ultra-high-energy cosmic ray propagation simulations, these CI-SM predictions yield outcomes compatible with the RQFAMz approach but at stronger variance with D1M+QRPA predictions.

## Method

The authors employed the Configuration Interaction Shell Model (CI-SM) to calculate E1 dipole response for p-shell and sd-shell nuclei with mass numbers A between 7 and 40, using the WBP effective Hamiltonian for p-shell nuclei and the PSDPF interaction for sd-shell nuclei. The E1 strength distributions were obtained through the Lanczos strength-function method with 300 iterations using the m-scheme shell-model code ANTOINE, and the resulting B(E1) distributions were folded with a generalized Lorentzian to account for coupling to the continuum. The dipole operator was renormalized using effective charges determined from the Thomas-Reich-Kuhn sum rule with an enhancement factor of kappa = 0.14 for sd-shell nuclei and kappa = 0 for p-shell nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Mass range of studied nuclei: A between 7 and 40 (p-shell and sd-shell nuclei)
- Folding width parameter Gamma = 1 MeV for sd-shell nuclei, except for Ca-40 where Gamma = 3.5 MeV; for p-shell nuclei, Gamma = 3.5 MeV in all cases
- Thomas-Reich-Kuhn sum rule value: S_TRK = 14.8 NZ/A e^2 fm^2 MeV
- Enhancement factor kappa = 0.14 for sd-shell nuclei and kappa = 0 for p-shell nuclei
- Energy cutoff for sum rule calculations: E_gamma_max = 50 MeV
- Effective charges for sd-shell nuclei: (e_eff^n, e_eff^p) = (-0.8Z/A, 0.8N/A), corresponding to an overall strength reduction factor Q^2 = 0.64
- CI-SM centroid predictions show root-mean-square deviation of 0.85 MeV across 26 cases compared to experimental data
- CI-SM width predictions show root-mean-square deviation of 0.50 MeV compared to experimental data
- D1M+QRPA root-mean-square deviation: 1.71 MeV on centroids, 0.49 MeV on widths
- RQFAMz root-mean-square deviation: 0.89 MeV on centroids, 0.48 MeV on widths
- CI-SM mean error on centroids: -0.36 MeV; D1M+QRPA: -1.47 MeV; RQFAMz: 0.54 MeV
- CI-SM mean error on widths: 0.27 MeV; D1M+QRPA: 0.13 MeV; RQFAMz: -0.33 MeV

## Context

This work extends the CI-SM investigation of E1 photon strength functions previously presented by Le Noan et al. (2025) for sd-shell nuclei, now covering all long-lived p-shell and sd-shell nuclei with mass numbers between 7 and 40. The study is part of the PANDORA project (Tamii et al., 2022), which aims to improve understanding of ultra-high-energy cosmic ray propagation through better characterization of photo-nuclear reactions in light nuclei. The CI-SM results are compared against the D1M+QRPA approach (Goriely et al., 2018), the RQFAMz approach (Gonzalez-Miret et al., 2025), and the phenomenological SMLO model (Plujko et al., 2018; Goriely et al., 2019). The work enables future extension to pf-shell nuclei to complete the CI-SM systematics of dipole photon strength functions needed for ultra-high-energy cosmic ray propagation modeling.
