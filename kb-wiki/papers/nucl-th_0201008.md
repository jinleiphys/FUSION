# Phenomenological Description of the Yrast Lines

> Analysis of the collective structure of 0^{+} states in terms of phonon tations for great amount of nuclei is presented .It is shown that the rotational bands built on these states strongly depend on their structure. The spectrum of the bands is obtained by the application of the Nuclear Surface Oscillation /NSO/ model and assumption of "Kassini form" for the nuclear shape. The yrast lines energies can be successfully described by the crossing of the different rotational "beta - bands", if the collective structure of these band heads is properly taken into account.

## Key claim

The yrast line energies of deformed even-even nuclei can be successfully described by the crossing of rotational beta-bands built on excited \(0^{+}\) states, provided the collective structure of those band heads (quantified by a phonon number \(n\)) is properly taken into account; the description uses only two fitting parameters and a simple rigid rotator model, but shows small deviations at angular momenta where back-bending may occur.

## Method

The author first fits the experimental energies of \(K^{\pi}=0^{+}\) states in even-even nuclei to a phenomenological formula \(E_n = An - Bn^2\), interpreting the integer \(n\) as the number of monopole phonons. A collective Hamiltonian expressed in terms of Holstein-Primakoff bosons is shown to reproduce this spectrum. The moment of inertia for rotational bands built on each \(0^{+}\) band head is then calculated using a rigid rotator formula and a Kassini (Cassini oval) shape for the nuclear surface, with the parameter \(a\) of the shape made dependent on the phonon number \(n\) via the Nuclear Surface Oscillation model. The ground-state band fixes the parameter \(a\), and one excited band fixes the ratio \(E_0/C_0\); all other bands are then predicted without further adjustment.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{174}\)Hf, the rigid moment of inertia for the Kassini form at the limiting case is \(\mathcal{F}(c,c) = 13489.5\ \text{MeV fm}^2\).
* For \(^{168}\)Yb, the fitted model parameters are \(a = 3.65\), \(R_{ms} = 5.31\) fm, \(\beta_K = 0.31\), and \(E_0/C_0 = 0.015\); the HFB reference values are \(R_{ms}^{(HFB)} = 5.298\) fm and \(\beta^{(HFB)} = 0.317\).
* For \(^{174}\)Hf, the fitted model parameters are \(a = 3.50\), \(R_{ms} = 5.39\) fm, \(\beta_K = 0.27\), and \(E_0/C_0 = 0.013\); the HFB reference values are \(R_{ms}^{(HFB)} = 5.365\) fm and \(\beta^{(HFB)} = 0.317\).
* Fitted parameters \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) for the collective Hamiltonian are tabulated for eight nuclei; for example, \(^{114}\)Cd: \(\alpha = 1.19593\), \(\beta = 0.28923\); \(^{194}\)Pt: \(\alpha = 0.763518\), \(\beta = 0.072\).

## Context

This work extends the Nuclear Surface Oscillation model (NSO) and the use of the Kassini form for nuclear shapes to describe rotational bands built on excited \(0^{+}\) states, going beyond earlier treatments that ignored band-head structure. The calculated mean square radii and deformation parameters \(\beta_K\) are compared with results from the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach (reference [5] in the paper), showing good agreement. The approach enables a phenomenological description of yrast lines via band crossings and provides a physical interpretation for the parameters used in variable-moment-of-inertia models, while leaving open the detailed explanation of back-bending and phase transitions at high angular momentum.
