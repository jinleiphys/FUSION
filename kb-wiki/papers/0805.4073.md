# Nuclear deformation and neutrinoless double-$β$ decay of $^{94,96}$Zr, $^{98,100}$Mo, $^{104}$Ru, $^{110}$Pd, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd nuclei in mass mechanism

> The $(β^{-}β^{-})_{0ν}$ decay of $^{94,96}$Zr, $^{98,100}$Mo, $^{104}$Ru, $^{110}$Pd, $^{128,130}$Te and $^{150}$Nd isotopes for the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ transition is studied in the Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework. In our earlier work, the reliability of HFB intrinsic wave functions participating in the $β^{-}β^{-}$ decay of the above mentioned nuclei has been established by obtaining an overall agreement between the theoretically calculated spectroscopic properties, namely yrast spectra, reduced $B(E2$:$0^{+}\to 2^{+})$ transition probabilities, quadrupole moments $Q(2^{+})$, gyromagnetic factors $g(2^{+})$ as well as half-lives $T_{1/2}^{2ν}$ for the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ transition and the available experimental data. In the present work, we study the $(β^{-}β^{-})_{0ν}$ decay for the $0^{+}\to 0^{+}$ transition in the mass mechanism and extract limits on effective mass of light as well as heavy neutrinos from the observed half-lives $T_{1/2}^{0ν}(0^{+}\to 0^{+})$ using nuclear transition matrix elements calculated with the same set of wave functions. Further, the effect of deformation on the nuclear transition matrix elements required to study the $(β^{-}β^{-})_{0ν}$ decay in the mass mechanism is investigated. It is noticed that the deformation effect on nuclear transition matrix elements is of approximately same magnitude in $(β^{-}β^{-})_{2ν}$ and $(β^{-}β^{-})_{0ν}$ decay.

## Key claim

The Projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (PHFB) framework, using the same set of intrinsic wave functions previously validated against spectroscopic properties and two-neutrino double-beta decay half-lives, yields nuclear transition matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay in the mass mechanism that show deformation effects of approximately the same magnitude as in the two-neutrino mode, but the extracted limits on effective neutrino masses depend on the specific experimental half-life limits available for each isotope and on the treatment of short-range correlations.

## Method

The authors employed the PHFB model with a PPQQ (pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole) effective two-body interaction to generate intrinsic wave functions for the initial and final nuclei, then projected states of good angular momentum. Nuclear transition matrix elements for the Fermi (M_F, M_Fh) and Gamow-Teller (M_GT, M_GTh) channels were calculated in the closure approximation for both light and heavy neutrino exchange, using Jastrow-type short-range correlations and finite nucleon size corrections with a dipole form factor (Lambda = 850 MeV). Limits on the effective light neutrino mass <m_nu> and heavy neutrino mass <M_N> were extracted from experimental half-life limits using phase space factors evaluated for g_A = 1.254.

## Key numbers

- For light neutrinos with point nucleons plus short-range correlations, the NTMEs M_F and M_GT are reduced by 17-22% and 11.6-14.4% respectively; with finite nucleon size the reduction is 20.8-26.5%.
- For heavy neutrinos, M_Fh and M_GTh are reduced by 34.5-39.3% and 65.4-69.5% with inclusion of finite size and finite size plus short-range correlations.
- The spreads in NTMEs M^(0nu) for light neutrinos across different models are: 6.7 (96Zr), 127.2 (100Mo), 5.4 (128Te), 2.8 (130Te), and 3.9 (150Nd); excluding one FQRPA result the spreads become 2.4, 4.2, 2.9, 2.5, and 3.9 respectively.
- Experimental half-life limits used: T_{1/2}^{0nu} > 1.9 x 10^19 yr (94Zr), > 1.0 x 10^21 yr (96Zr), > 1.0 x 10^14 yr (98Mo), > 4.6 x 10^23 yr (100Mo), > 6.0 x 10^17 yr (110Pd), > 1.1 x 10^23 yr (128Te), > 3.0 x 10^24 yr (130Te).
- The experimental deformation parameter beta_2 in the mass region 94 <= A <= 150 ranges from 0.081 +/- 0.016 (96Zr) to 0.2848 +/- 0.0021 (150Nd).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous PHFB studies of two-neutrino double-beta decay in 94,96Zr, 98,100Mo, 104Ru, 110Pd, 128,130Te, and 150Nd (Chandra 2005, Singh 2007) to the neutrinoless mode, where the same wave functions had already been validated against yrast spectra, B(E2) values, quadrupole moments, and g-factors. The results are compared with shell model calculations by Caurier (2007), QRPA/RQRPA calculations by Rodin (2007) and Simkovic (2008), and pseudo-SU(3) calculations by Hirsch (1995). The convergence of NTMEs across different models to within a factor smaller than 4 is noted as encouraging, while the role of deformation and the ongoing debate about tensorial terms and short-range correlation treatments are identified as areas requiring further study.
