# Inhomogeneous condensates in dilute nuclear matter and BCS-BEC crossovers

> We report on recent progress in understanding pairing phenomena in low-density nuclear matter at small and moderate isospin asymmetry. A rich phase diagram has been found comprising various superfluid phases that include a homogeneous and phase-separated BEC phase of deuterons at low density and a homogeneous BCS phase, an inhomogeneous LOFF phase, and a phase-separated BCS phase at higher densities. The transition from the BEC phases to the BCS phases is characterized in terms of the evolution, from strong to weak coupling, of the condensate wavefunction and the second moment of its density distribution in $r$-space. We briefly discuss approaches to higher-order clustering in low-density nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper reports that the phase diagram of dilute nuclear matter at small and moderate isospin asymmetry includes not only homogeneous BCS and BEC phases but also an inhomogeneous LOFF phase and phase-separated BCS and BEC phases, with the BCS-BEC crossover characterized by the sign change of the average chemical potential and the evolution of the condensate wavefunction's spatial extent.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled gap equation and density equations for neutron-proton pairing in the \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channel, allowing for a finite center-of-mass momentum of Cooper pairs (LOFF phase) and for phase separation into superfluid and normal domains. The gap equation was solved with an angle-averaged approximation for the gap in the denominator, using a realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction potential and accounting for the chemical potential mismatch \(\delta\mu = (\mu_n - \mu_p)/2\) induced by isospin asymmetry. The BCS-BEC crossover was identified by the sign of the average chemical potential \(\bar\mu\) and by computing the coherence length \(\xi = \sqrt{\langle r^2 \rangle}\) from the condensate wavefunction.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of symmetrical nuclear matter: \(\rho_0 = 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) g cm\(^{-3}\).
- Tetra-critical point at \(\log_{10}(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.22\) and \(T = 2.85\) MeV for isospin asymmetry \(\alpha_4 = 0.255\).
- LOFF phase disappears at \(\log_{10}(\rho/\rho_0) = -0.65\) and \(T = 0\) for \(\alpha = 0.62\).
- Zero-density binding energy of the alpha particle: \(E^0_\alpha = -28.3\) MeV.
- Density at which the alpha particle bound state dissolves (binding energy crosses the continuum \(4E_F\)): \(\rho \sim 0.03\) fm\(^{-3}\).
- Fitting formula for the Pauli blocking shift of the alpha particle binding energy at zero temperature and asymmetry: \(\Delta E_4^{\rm Pauli}(\rho) = 4515.9\, \rho - 100935\, \rho^2 + 1202538\, \rho^3\) (energy in MeV, density in fm\(^{-3}\)).

## Context

This work extends the Nozieres-Schmitt-Rink theory of BCS-BEC crossover (Nozieres and Schmitt-Rink, 1985) to isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, building on earlier studies of pairing in low-density nuclear matter (Alm et al., 1993; Baldo et al., 1995; Sedrakian et al., 2005) and the LOFF phase in nuclear systems (Sedrakian et al., 2000; Muther et al., 2002). The phase diagram unifies previous separate treatments of unconventional pairing and BCS-BEC crossover (Stein et al., 2012) and provides a framework for including higher-order clustering (deuterons, tritons, \(^3\)He, alpha particles) via in-medium Schrodinger equations, with direct relevance for supernova and neutron star matter.
