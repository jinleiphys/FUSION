# Phase diagram and thermal properties of strong-interaction matter

> We introduce a novel procedure for computing the (mu,T)-dependent pressure in continuum QCD; and therefrom obtain a complex phase diagram and predictions for thermal properties of the system, providing the in-medium behaviour of the trace anomaly, speed of sound, latent heat and heat capacity.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a novel continuum method for computing the (mu,T)-dependent pressure in QCD using Dyson-Schwinger equations, but the results are limited to the dressed-quark contribution only, omitting the gluon contribution to the total pressure.

## Method

The authors solve the quark gap equation in rainbow-ladder truncation at finite temperature and chemical potential, using a gluon propagator model that incorporates a screening mass and temperature-dependent infrared strength. The dressed-quark pressure is computed via the stationary phase approximation, with ultraviolet divergences removed by subtracting the vacuum result using a spectral representation that separates the divergent and physical pieces.

## Key numbers

- Critical endpoint for chiral symmetry restoration: (mu_E^chi = 0.111 GeV, T_E^chi = 0.128 GeV), with ratio mu_E^chi/T_E^chi = 0.87
- Critical endpoint for deconfinement: (mu_E^varsigma = 0.185 GeV, T_E^varsigma = 0.106 GeV), with ratio mu_E^varsigma/T_E^varsigma = 1.75
- mu=0 pseudo-critical temperature for chiral transition: T_c0^P = 0.15 GeV
- Onset of qualitative changes in pressure and trace anomaly occurs at mu_p = 0.106 GeV
- Critical exponent epsilon = 0.67 +/- 0.02 for heat capacity and quark number susceptibility near the CEP
- Dressed-quark thermal mass m_T = 0.8 T at T = 2 T_c

## Context

This work extends earlier DSE studies of the QCD phase diagram (Qin 2010, Gao 2014) by using an improved kernel that agrees with one-loop QCD renormalisation group running, while remaining consistent with lattice QCD results for the trace anomaly and speed of sound (Borsanyi 2012, Bazavov 2014). The method enables computation of thermal properties at nonzero chemical potential without the sign problem that limits lattice QCD, and provides a framework for future continuum studies with more sophisticated symmetry-preserving kernels.
