# Is the Sullivan Process Compatible with QCD Chiral Dynamics?

> We calculate the leading non-analytic quark-mass dependence in the moments of isovector quark distributions using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. The results differ from those obtained from the Sullivan process in which hard scattering occurs through the virtual pion cloud of the nucleon. Our results provide useful guidance for improving upon the meson-cloud model and can be used to constrain the extrapolations of the existing lattice QCD results to the physical quark masses.

## Key claim

The leading non-analytic quark-mass dependence of isovector quark-distribution moments calculated in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory differs from the results obtained via the Sullivan process (pion-cloud model), specifically in the coefficient multiplying the chiral logarithm; the two approaches agree only in the limit g_A = 1, but disagree in general, particularly in the large-N_c limit where g_A scales as N_c.

## Method

The authors used heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to compute the leading non-analytic (chiral logarithm) quark-mass dependence of the moments of isovector (u minus d) spin-averaged, helicity, and transversity quark distributions in the nucleon. They constructed chiral expansions of the relevant twist-two quark operators in terms of hadronic (pion and nucleon) operators, then calculated contributions from nucleon wave-function renormalization, virtual-pion cloud diagrams, and pion tadpole diagrams. The results were compared with those from the linear sigma model used in the traditional Sullivan process, and the discrepancy was traced to differences between linear and non-linear formulations of chiral expansion.

## Key numbers

- The leading non-analytic correction for the isovector spin-averaged moment ⟨x^n⟩_{u-d} has the form 1 − [(3g_A² + 1) m_π² / (4π f_π)²] ln(m_π²/μ²), where f_π = 93 MeV and μ is the renormalization scale (taken as 4π f_π ∼ 1 GeV).
- The leading non-analytic correction for the isovector helicity moment ⟨x^{n-1}⟩_{Δu−Δd} has the form 1 − [(2g_A² + 1) m_π² / (4π f_π)²] ln(m_π²/μ²).
- The leading non-analytic correction for the isovector transversity moment ⟨x^{n-1}⟩_{δu−δd} has the form 1 − [(4g_A² + 1) m_π² / {2(4π f_π)²}] ln(m_π²/μ²).
- The Sullivan process result for ⟨x^n⟩_{u-d} gave a prefactor of 4g_A² instead of (3g_A² + 1); the two results agree only when g_A = 1.

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory analysis of parton distributions initiated by Thomas, Melnitchouk, and collaborators (2000), who first calculated the leading non-analytic quark-mass dependence using the Sullivan process in the linear sigma model. The results show that the traditional meson-cloud model (Sullivan process) must be modified by adding higher-dimensional operators to the linear sigma model Lagrangian to achieve consistency with the non-linear chiral dynamics of QCD. The calculated chiral logarithms provide constraints for extrapolating lattice QCD results (such as those by Detmold, Melnitchouk, Negele, Renner, and Thomas, 2001, and Dolgov et al., 2001) to physical quark masses, and offer guidance for improving meson-cloud models.
