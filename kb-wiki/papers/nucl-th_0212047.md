# Precise Study of Three-Nucleon Systems within a Representation without Explicit Use of the Isospin Formalism

> A representation without explicit use of the isospin formalism is developed for the precise study of few-nucleon systems, and the advantages of the proposed approach are demonstrated. Using the example of three-nucleon systems with central exchange NN-interaction potentials of the general type, the complete equivalence is shown for the both approaches with and without the isospin formalism. The new systems of equations contain a less number of components as compared to the commonly used ones and are more suitable for precise study of few-nucleon systems, in particular, within variational approaches. Optimal variational schemes are developed with Gaussian basis, and the binding energies, r.m.s. radii, density distributions, formfactors, and pair correlation functions are calculated with a high and controlled precision for 3H and 3He nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a representation of three-nucleon systems without explicit use of the isospin formalism is completely equivalent to the traditional isospin formalism for central exchange NN-interaction potentials, while reducing the number of coupled spatial equations from six to two for the doublet spin state (S=1/2) of 3He and 3H.

## Method

The authors reformulated the Schrodinger equation for three-nucleon bound states (3H and 3He) by introducing a unitary transformation that relates the six spatial components of the wave function in the isospin formalism to a new set of components, of which only two are independent. They then solved the resulting reduced system of two equations using an optimal variational method with a Gaussian basis, calculating binding energies, r.m.s. radii, density distributions, formfactors, and pair correlation functions for the Afnan-Tang (ATS3) and Minnesota potentials.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of 3H with the AT-(S3)+ potential: given with accuracy of one unit in the last digit (exact results are slightly greater, within not more than 1 KeV)
- Binding energy of 3He with the AT-(S3)+ potential: given with accuracy of one unit in the last digit (exact results are slightly greater, within not more than 1 KeV)
- Binding energy of 3H with the Minnesota potential: given with accuracy of one unit in the last digit (exact results are slightly greater, within not more than 1 KeV)
- Binding energy of 3He with the Minnesota potential: given with accuracy of one unit in the last digit (exact results are slightly greater, within not more than 1 KeV)
- Variational basis size: about 60-100 Gaussian basis functions for both the symmetric and antisymmetric components
- Optimal ratio of basis functions: about three times more Gaussian components for the symmetric function Phi_1 than for the antisymmetric function Phi_2
- Coulomb parameter used: e^2 = 1.44 MeV*fm
- Conversion factor used: hbar^2/M = 41.47 MeV*fm^2
- Experimental formfactor zero-crossing for 3He: q^2_min,exp = 11.6 fm^-2

## Context

This work extends the variational approaches of earlier studies (references 1-5 in the paper) for few-nucleon systems by providing a more efficient formulation that reduces the number of coupled equations. The authors show their numerical results are of higher precision than the Varga-Suzuki results (2000) despite using fewer Gaussian basis functions. The approach enables precise fitting of nucleon-nucleon potentials universal for all light nuclei and opens the possibility for accurate calculations of four-nucleon systems (two equations for S=0), five-nucleon systems (five equations for S=1/2), and six-nucleon systems (five equations for both 6He and 6Li).
