# Unveiling the Structure of Hidden-Bottom Strange Pentaquarks via Magnetic Moments

> Motivated by the discovery of hidden-charm strange pentaquarks, we conduct a systematic study of the magnetic moments of the hidden-bottom strange pentaquarks in molecular picture. We calculate magnetic moments of hidden-bottom strange pentaquarks with strangeness-1 and 2. Magnetic moment gives valuable information about the inner structure and shape of the hadron. The obtained results may be helpful to determine the inner structure of these new yet hypothetical states.

# Unveiling the Structure of Hidden-Bottom Strange Pentaquarks via Magnetic Moments

## Key claim
The magnetic moments of hidden-bottom strange pentaquark states, calculated in the molecular picture, differ between the $8_{1f}$ and $8_{2f}$ flavor representations, and these differences could serve to identify the internal quantum numbers and spin configurations of these hypothetical states, provided they are observed experimentally.

## Method
The authors adopt a molecular picture where hidden-bottom strange pentaquarks are composed of a singly-bottom baryon ($b q_1 q_2$) and a bottom-light meson ($\bar{b} q_3$), with the baryon and meson treated as hadronic molecules. They construct flavor wave functions under $SU(3)$ symmetry for strangeness-1 ($P_{bs}$) and strangeness-2 ($P_{bss}$) states in both $8_{1f}$ and $8_{2f}$ representations, then compute magnetic moments using constituent quark masses ($m_u = 0.338$ GeV, $m_d = 0.350$ GeV, $m_s = 0.500$ GeV, $m_b = 4.67$ GeV) via the operator $\hat{\mu} = \sum_i (Q_i / 2 m_i) \hat{\sigma}_i$, summing contributions from baryon and meson components with appropriate spin couplings.

## Key numbers
- For $P_{bs}^1$ ($I=1$, $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$) with $\frac{1}{2}^+ \otimes 0^-$: magnetic moment $1.939\ \mu_N$ ($8_{1f}$) and $-0.067\ \mu_N$ ($8_{2f}$)
- For $P_{bs}^1$ ($I=1$, $J^P=\frac{3}{2}^-$) with $\frac{1}{2}^+ \otimes 1^-$: magnetic moment $2.206\ \mu_N$ ($8_{1f}$) and $1.850\ \mu_N$ ($8_{2f}$)
- For $P_{bs}^4$ ($I=0$, $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$) with $\frac{1}{2}^+ \otimes 0^-$: magnetic moment $-0.076\ \mu_N$ ($8_{1f}$) and $-0.067\ \mu_N$ ($8_{2f}$)
- For $P_{bss}^1$ ($I=\frac{1}{2}$, $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$) with $\frac{1}{2}^+ \otimes 0^-$: magnetic moment $-0.261\ \mu_N$ ($8_{1f}$) and $-0.067\ \mu_N$ ($8_{2f}$)
- All $J^P=\frac{1}{2}^-$ states with $\frac{1}{2}^+ \otimes 0^-$ in the $8_{2f}$ representation yield the same magnetic moment: $\mu = -0.067\ \mu_N$
- Octet baryon magnetic moments are reproduced within ~0.1–0.2 $\mu_N$ of experimental values (e.g., proton: $2.764\ \mu_N$ calculated vs $2.793\ \mu_N$ experimental; $\Lambda$: $-0.625\ \mu_N$ calculated vs $-0.613 \pm 0.006\ \mu_N$ experimental)

## Context
This work extends the molecular picture previously applied to hidden-charm strange pentaquarks $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4338)$ and $P_{\psi s}^{\Lambda}(4459)$, observed by LHCb (2019, 2021, 2022), to their hidden-bottom analogues. The authors note that electromagnetic properties of the charm-sector states have been studied by Li (2021), Gao (2021), Ozdem (2021, 2022, 2023, 2024), Wang (2022), Zhang (2023), and Li (2024), and that the present calculations provide predictions for future experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb. The results enable discrimination between $8_{1f}$ and $8_{2f}$ flavor assignments and between different spin-parity configurations, and the authors encourage comparison with three-point QCD sum rule, light-cone QCD sum rule, or lattice QCD studies.
