# The study of multifragmentation around transition energy in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions

> Fragmentation of light charged particles is studied for various systems at different incident energies between 50 and 1000 MeV/nucleon. We analyze fragment production at incident energies above, below and at transition energies using the isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics(IQMD) model. The trends observed for the fragment production and rapidity distributions depend upon the incident energy, size of the fragments, composite mass of the reacting system as well as on the impact parameter of the reaction. The free nucleons and light charged particles show continous homogeneous changes irrespective of the transition energies indicating that there is no relation between the transition energy and production of the free as well as light charged particles.

## Key claim

The study finds no particular structure or non-trivial behavior in multifragmentation at the transition energy of elliptical flow; the production of free nucleons and light charged particles shows a continuous homogeneous change across incident energies, regardless of whether the energy is below, at, or above the transition energy.

## Method

The authors used the isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate collisions of symmetric systems (Ca+Ca, Xe+Xe, Au+Au) at incident energies between 50 and 1000 MeV/nucleon. The phase space generated by the model was analyzed using the minimum spanning tree (MST) method at 200 fm/c, with a binding distance of 4 fm between nucleons. A hard equation of state was employed along with isospin dependent free nucleon-nucleon cross-sections.

## Key numbers

- Transition energies for free nucleons: 325 MeV/nucleon for Ca+Ca, 150 MeV/nucleon for Xe+Xe, and 140 MeV/nucleon for Au+Au.
- Transition energies for light charged particles: 210 MeV/nucleon for Ca+Ca, 140 MeV/nucleon for Xe+Xe, and 135 MeV/nucleon for Au+Au.
- Power law exponent tau for free nucleon multiplicity as a function of system mass: 0.97 below transition energy, 0.88 at transition energy, and 1.02 above transition energy.
- Power law exponent tau for light charged particle multiplicity: 0.81 below transition energy, 0.74 at transition energy, and 0.84 above transition energy.
- Incident energies used for comparison: 100 MeV/nucleon (below transition) and 400 MeV/nucleon (above transition).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Puri and coworkers (2000s) who investigated fragmentation at balance energy and found no particular pattern there. The present study examines whether a similar lack of structure holds at the transition energy of elliptical flow, which marks the switch from in-plane to out-of-plane emission. The results indicate that the behavior of light charged particles, nuclear stopping, and elliptical flow show a resemblance that the authors suggest could be important for heavy-ion physics, while free nucleons and light charged particles show no relation to the transition energy.
