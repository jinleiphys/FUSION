# Broken Symmetries from Minimally Doubled Fermions

> Novel chirally symmetric fermion actions containing the minimum amount of fermion doubling have been recently proposed in the literature. We study the symmetries and renormalization of these actions and find that in each case, discrete symmetries, such as parity and time-reversal, are explicitly broken. Consequently, when the gauge interactions are included, these theories radiatively generate relevant and marginal operators. Thus the restoration of these symmetries and the approach to the continuum limit require the fine-tuning of several parameters. With some assumptions, we show that this behavior is unavoidable for actions displaying minimal fermion doubling.

## Key claim

Minimally doubled fermion actions (Borici-Creutz, Wilczek, Karsten) necessarily break discrete symmetries such as parity and time-reversal, which causes relevant and marginal operators to be radiatively generated when gauge interactions are included; consequently, the continuum limit requires fine-tuning of several parameters, and under general assumptions this behavior is unavoidable for any chirally symmetric action with minimal fermion doubling.

## Method

The authors analyze the symmetries of the Borici-Creutz and Wilczek lattice actions by rewriting them in momentum space to expose their flavor structure, then construct the Symanzik effective theory valid at distances larger than the lattice spacing but smaller than the confinement radius. They identify the seven symmetries of the Borici-Creutz action (gauge invariance, discrete translation invariance, baryon number, U(1)_L ⊗ U(1)_R chiral symmetry, combined CPT invariance, combined IC invariance, and S_4 permutation symmetry) and use these to enumerate all relevant (dimension-three) and marginal (dimension-four) operators that appear in the effective Lagrangian. A perturbative calculation of a tadpole self-energy diagram demonstrates that an insertion of an O(a) dimension-five operator generates a relevant operator proportional to α_s / a.

## Key numbers

- The Borici-Creutz action has exactly two poles of the propagator corresponding to massless fermions, at p^(1)_μ = (p̃, p̃, p̃, p̃) and p^(2)_μ = -p^(1)_μ, where cos p̃ = C, with 0 < C < 1.
- For the Borici-Creutz action, the choice B = 1, C = 1/√2 gives the Borici action; the choice B = 3, C = 3/√10 gives orthogonal axes as shown by Creutz (2007).
- The Wilczek action has two poles at k_μ^(1) = (0,0,0,0) and k_μ^(2) = (0,0,0,π), with parameter λ > 1/2.
- The tadpole self-energy diagram generates a contribution ∼ α_s / a to a relevant operator of the form O_3 = ∑_μ Q̄ (i ζ_μ ⊗ τ^3) Q.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of minimally doubled fermion actions originally proposed by Karsten (1981), Wilczek (1987), Creutz (2007), and Borici (2007), which were suggested as computationally cheaper alternatives to chirally symmetric actions that bypass the Nielsen-Ninomiya no-go theorem. The authors show that these actions break discrete symmetries, leading to fine-tuning requirements that hinder practical calculations, in contrast to domain wall or overlap fermion approaches. The paper enables future work to search for non-standard symmetries that might emerge at particular parameter values and potentially eliminate the relevant operators, or to develop alternative minimal doubling constructions that preserve PT symmetry.
