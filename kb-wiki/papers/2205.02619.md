# Phase shifts of the light pseudoscalar meson and heavy meson scattering in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory

> We calculate the complete $T$ matrices of the elastic light pseudoscalar meson and heavy meson scattering to the third order in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory. We determine the low-energy constants by fitting the phase shifts and scattering lengths from lattice QCD simulations simultaneously and predict the phase shifts at the physical meson masses. The phase shifts in the $Dπ(I=1/2)$, $DK(I=0)$, $D\bar{K}(I=0)$, $D_s\bar{K}$, $Dη$ and $D_sη$ $S$ waves are so strong that bound states or resonances may be generated dynamically in all these channels. The $DK(I=0)$ channel corresponds to the well-known exotic state $D_{s0}^{*}(2317)$. The $DK(I=0)$ channel corresponds to the well-known exotic state $D_{s0}^{*}(2317)$. The coupled-channel $Dπ$, $Dη$ and $D_s\bar{K}$ scattering corresponds to $D_{0}^{*}(2400)$. The coupled-channel $Dπ$, $Dη$ and $D_s\bar{K}$ scattering corresponds to $D_{0}^{*}(2400)$. We also predict the scattering lengths and scattering volumes and observe good convergence in the scattering volumes. Our calculations provide a possibility to accurately investigate the exotic state in the light pseudoscalar meson and heavy meson interactions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate complete T matrices for elastic light pseudoscalar meson and heavy meson scattering up to third order in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory (HMChPT), but the phase shifts are computed perturbatively without iterating the T matrix to infinite order, meaning the bound states or resonances that may be generated dynamically (such as D_{s0}*(2317) and D_{0}*(2400)) are not directly produced in the calculation itself.

## Method

The authors constructed the effective chiral Lagrangian for heavy mesons (D, D_s) and light pseudoscalar mesons (pi, K, eta) in HMChPT up to O(p^3), including tree-level and one-loop diagrams. They determined the low-energy constants (LECs) c_0 through c_5 and kappa_1 through kappa_3 by simultaneously fitting S-wave and P-wave phase shifts and scattering lengths from multiple lattice QCD simulations at nonphysical meson masses. Using the fitted LECs, they then predicted the phase shifts at physical meson masses for eleven scattering channels.

## Key numbers

- The fitted LECs include: c_0 = -0.77 +/- 0.39 GeV^{-1}, c_1 = -0.64 +/- 0.35 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = -5.04 +/- 1.83 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = 5.47 +/- 1.57 GeV^{-1}, c_4 = 8.99 +/- 0.93 GeV^{-1}, c_5 = -3.08 +/- 0.70 GeV^{-1}, kappa_1 = 0.21 +/- 0.04 GeV^{-2}, kappa_2 = 7.81 +/- 3.88 GeV^{-2}, kappa_3 = -1.87 +/- 1.47 GeV^{-2}, with chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.19.
- The linear combination c_2 + c_4 = 0.89 +/- 0.02 GeV^{-1} and c_3 + c_5 = -0.32 +/- 0.04 GeV^{-1} were obtained from an alternative fit.
- The scattering length for the DK(I=0) channel from lattice QCD is -1.33(20) fm from Mohler (2013), used to determine the coupling constant g_R^2 = 0.81 +/- 0.04 for D_{s0}*(2317).
- Physical meson masses used for predictions: m_pi = 139.57 MeV, m_K = 493.68 MeV, f_pi = 92.07 MeV, f_K = 110.03 MeV, M_D = 1869.66 MeV, M_Ds = 1968.35 MeV from PDG (2020).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous calculations of scattering lengths up to O(p^4) in HMChPT (Liu 2009, Huang 2022) by computing the complete T matrices and phase shifts to O(p^3). The study is motivated by the need to extrapolate lattice QCD results for meson-meson scattering from nonphysical to physical meson masses, particularly for channels involving the exotic states D_{s0}*(2317) and D_{0}*(2400). The results enable future nonperturbative calculations that could dynamically generate these bound states and resonances, and provide predictions that may be tested by LHCb or Belle II measurements of phase shifts in channels such as D^{-}K^{+}.
