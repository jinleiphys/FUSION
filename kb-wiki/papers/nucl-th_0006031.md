# Determination of the Delta++ magnetic dipole moment

> We study the elastic and radiative pi+ p scattering within a full dynamical model which incorporates the finite width effects of the Delta++. The scattering amplitudes are invariant under contact transformations of the spin 3/2 field and gauge-invariance is fulfilled for the radiative case. The pole parameters of the Delta++ obtained from the elastic cross section are m_Delta = 1211.2 \pm 0.4 MeV and Gamma_Delta = 88.2 \pm 0.4 MeV. From a fit to the most sensitive observables in radiative pi+ p scattering, we obtain mu_Delta = 6.14 \pm 0.51 e/2m_p for the magnetic dipole moment of the Delta++.

## Key claim

The magnetic dipole moment of the Delta++ resonance is determined from a full dynamical model that incorporates finite width effects and is free of ambiguities from contact transformations, yielding a value of 6.14 ± 0.51 e/2m_p, which is compatible with SU(6) quark model predictions but larger than some soft-photon approximation results.

## Method

The authors constructed a dynamical model for elastic and radiative pi+ p scattering that includes intermediate states with nucleons, Delta++, Delta0, rho, and sigma resonances, using effective Lagrangian densities. Finite width effects of the Delta++ were incorporated via the complex mass scheme, replacing m_Delta^2 with m_Delta^2 - i m_Delta Gamma_Delta in the propagator, which preserves gauge invariance. The mass, width, and coupling constants were fitted to elastic pi+ p total cross section data for incident pion kinetic energies from 75 to 300 MeV, then the Delta++ magnetic dipole moment was the only adjustable parameter in fits to radiative pi+ p scattering data from the UCLA experiment.

## Key numbers

- Delta++ pole mass from elastic scattering: m_Delta = 1211.2 ± 0.4 MeV
- Delta++ width from elastic scattering: Gamma_Delta = 88.2 ± 0.4 MeV
- Delta N pi coupling constant from full fit: f_Delta N pi^2/4pi = 0.317 ± 0.003
- Weighted average magnetic dipole moment of Delta++: mu_Delta = 6.14 ± 0.51 e/2m_p
- Individual kappa_Delta fits for T_lab = 269 MeV: 3.27 ± 0.76 (G7 geometry), 3.01 ± 0.67 (G4 geometry), 2.74 ± 0.87 (G1 geometry)
- Individual kappa_Delta fits for T_lab = 298 MeV: 3.10 ± 0.87 (G7 geometry), 2.90 ± 0.75 (G4 geometry), 2.61 ± 1.00 (G1 geometry)

## Context

This work extends previous approaches to resonance description based on gauge invariance and analyticity, similar to methods used for the W± gauge boson by the authors of FLS and for the rho± meson by BLS. The extracted pole parameters are consistent with the model-independent S-matrix analysis of Bernicha (1997), who obtained M = 1212.20 ± 0.23 MeV and Gamma = 97.06 ± 0.35 MeV. The result is compatible with the SU(6) quark model prediction of 5.58 e/2m_p and the quark model calculation of Fra (1998) giving 6.17 e/2m_p, but larger than the soft-photon approximation result of Lin (1995) which gave 3.7 to 4.9 e/2m_p. This is the first determination of the Delta++ MDM from a full dynamical model that consistently incorporates finite width effects and is free of contact transformation ambiguities.
