# Bohr Hamiltonian with an energy dependent $γ$-unstable Coulomb-like potential

> An exact analytical solution for the Bohr Hamiltonian with an energy dependent Coulomb-like $γ$-unstable potential is presented. Due to the linear energy dependence of the potential's coupling constant, the corresponding spectrum in the asymptotic limit of the slope parameter resembles the spectral structure of the spherical vibrator, however with a different state degeneracy. The parameter free energy spectrum as well as the transition rates for this case are given in closed form and duly compared with those of the harmonic $U(5)$ dynamical symmetry. The model wave functions are found to exhibit properties that can be associated to shape coexistence. A possible experimental realization of the model is found in few medium nuclei with a very low second $0^{+}$ state known to exhibit competing prolate, oblate and spherical shapes.

# Bohr Hamiltonian with an energy dependent γ-unstable Coulomb-like potential

## Key claim

The paper presents an exact analytical solution for the Bohr Hamiltonian with a linearly energy-dependent Coulomb-like γ-unstable potential, showing that in the asymptotic limit of the slope parameter the spectrum resembles the spherical vibrator (U(5)) energy sequence but with a different state degeneracy indexed by N = n + τ rather than N = 2n + τ. The model's wave functions exhibit properties associated with shape coexistence, and the parameter-free asymptotic limit (AEDC model) is proposed as a description for nuclei with very low second 0⁺ states.

## Method

The Bohr Hamiltonian is separated into β and γ-angular parts under the γ-unstable condition, with the β-potential taken as a Coulomb-like form v(β) = −A/β where the coupling constant depends linearly on energy as A(ε) = 1 + aε. The resulting Schrödinger equation is solved exactly using Whittaker functions, yielding energy eigenvalues and wave functions expressed in terms of associated Laguerre polynomials. E2 transition probabilities are computed using SO(5) Clebsch-Gordan coefficients and β-integrals with a modified integration measure that accounts for the energy-dependent potential.

## Key numbers

- The energy spectrum in the asymptotic limit (a → ∞) is given by ε_{nτ}^{(asymp)} = −1/a + (2/a^{3/2})(N + 2), where N = n + τ
- The ratio R_{4/2} of excitation energies for the first two excited states reaches the value 2 in the asymptotic regime
- To achieve an accuracy of 10⁻³ for the first 3 excited states, the slope parameter a must be of order 10⁶
- The relative separation between ground state and first β-excited 0⁺ state deformations is (⟨β'⟩₁₀ − ⟨β'⟩₀₀)/⟨β'⟩₀₀ = 0.5 in the AEDC model, compared to 0.3 in U(5)
- Experimental comparisons are made for ⁷²Se, ⁷⁴Se, ⁷²Kr, ⁹⁸Mo, and ¹⁰⁰Mo; for example, the theoretical 4₀,₂ → 2₀,₁ transition rate is 300 (normalized to B(E2;2₁⁺→0₁⁺)=100), compared to experimental values of 232(27) for ⁷²Se and 336(92) for ⁷²Kr

## Context

This work extends the formalism of energy-dependent (non-local) potentials in the Bohr Hamiltonian, previously applied to the five-dimensional harmonic oscillator (the Stiffening Spherical Vibrator or SSV model) by the same author (Budaca, 2016), to a Coulomb-like β-potential. The asymptotic limit (AEDC) provides a new parameter-free collective solution comparable to the U(5) spherical vibrator model (Bohr and Mottelson) and the critical point solutions E(5) and X(5) (Iachello). The model is applied to nuclei known for shape coexistence (⁷²Se, ⁷⁴Se, ⁷²Kr, ⁹⁸Mo, ¹⁰⁰Mo), where the low-lying 0⁺ states and the β-density probability distributions show features consistent with shape coexistence as described by microscopic calculations (Hilaire, Bender, Hinohara). The work enables a fully collective description of shape coexistence phenomena without resorting to deformed mean-field or shell-model calculations.
