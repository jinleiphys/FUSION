# RPA approach to rotational symmetry restoration in a three-level Lipkin model

> We study an extended Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model that permits a transition to a deformed phase with a broken continuous symmetry. Unlike simpler models, one sees a persistent zero-frequency Goldstone mode past the transition point into the deformed phase. We found that the RPA formula for the correlation energy provides a useful correction to the Hartree-Fock energy when the number of particle N satisfies N > 3, and becomes accurate for large N. We conclude that the RPA correlation energy formula offers a promising way to improve the Hartree-Fock energy in a systematic theory of nuclear binding energies.

## Key claim

The RPA correlation energy formula provides a useful correction to the Hartree-Fock energy for the three-level Lipkin model when the number of particles N satisfies N > 3, and becomes accurate for large N, but for N = 2 the exact correlation energy is several times the RPA value and such near-magic nuclei would require a more elaborate treatment.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-level Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model with N identical fermions in three single-particle levels, each N-fold degenerate, using parameters that mimic quadrupole motions in nuclei. They solved the model in the Hartree-Fock approximation by minimizing the energy surface with respect to a variational parameter alpha, then applied the Random Phase Approximation to compute the correlation energy using the formula E_corr = 1/2 (sum_i hbar omega_i - Tr(A)). The exact ground state energy was obtained by numerically diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a basis of symmetric states.

## Key numbers

* The phase transition from spherical to deformed phase occurs at chi = V(N-1)/epsilon = 1, where chi is the dimensionless coupling parameter.
* In the deformed phase (chi > 1), the HF ground state energy is E_HF = (N epsilon/4)(2 - chi - 1/chi).
* For N = 20, the RPA correction significantly improves the HF energy across the entire range of chi shown, working well even near the critical point chi = 1.
* The RPA correction is within a factor of 2 of the exact correlation energy for N >= 4 at chi = 5.0.
* For N = 2 at chi = 5.0, the exact correlation energy is several times the RPA value.

## Context

This work extends the three-level Lipkin model studies by Brito et al. (1995), whose parameters did not leave a continuous symmetry to be broken as is the case for the transition between spherical and deformed nuclei. The authors use parameters that mimic quadrupole motions in nuclei, allowing study of the correlation associated with rotational motion. The paper argues that the RPA correlation formula can provide a better method than the projection after variation approach of Ring and Schuck (1980) for correcting broken symmetry in HF theory, and suggests that treating correlation energies systematically by RPA could lead to a better microscopic global theory of nuclear binding energies, improving on the liquid drop model with perturbative shell effects by Moller et al. (1995) which fits binding energies with an RMS deviation of 0.67 MeV.
