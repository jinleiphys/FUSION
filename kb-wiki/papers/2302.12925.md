# Finite volume effects in the extended linear sigma model via low momentum cutoff

> Contrary to field theoretical calculations in the thermodynamic limit where the volume is assumed to be infinitely large, the heavy-ion collisions always carry the effects of finite size. A sufficiently small system size is expected to affect the thermodynamic quantities and the phase diagram of the strongly interacting matter. To study these effects one can take into account the finite spatial extent of the system within the framework of an effective model too, via the restriction of the momentum integrals using discretization or in a simplified case using a low momentum cutoff. We investigated the effects of the finite volume in a vector meson extended Polyakov quark-meson model and found a remarkable change in the thermodynamics and the phase transition, especially in the location of the critical endpoint.

## Key claim

The finite volume effects in the vector meson extended Polyakov quark-meson model, implemented via a low momentum cutoff approximation, produce a remarkable change in thermodynamics and the phase transition, especially in the location of the critical endpoint; however, the authors note that below a certain system size the validity of thermodynamic models becomes questionable.

## Method

The authors used the mean-field grand potential of the extended linear sigma model (ELSM) with four full meson nonets and 2+1 flavor constituent quarks. They implemented finite volume effects by introducing a low momentum cutoff \(\lambda = \pi/L\) in the fermionic one-loop momentum integration, rather than full discretization, based on the observation that zero momentum modes are most relevant for criticality. The field equations were solved by minimizing the grand potential in the scalar-isoscalar meson condensates and Polyakov loop variables.

## Key numbers

- At zero temperature and zero chemical potential, the vacuum value of the non-strange meson condensate decreases with decreasing system size \(L\).
- The critical endpoint (CEP) disappears around \(L = 2.5\) fm, after which the criticality of the system is washed out.
- The transition temperature at \(\mu_q = 0\) decreases with decreasing volume, while the transition becomes smoother.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of the ELSM thermodynamics and phase diagram by Kovacs (2016, 2021) to include finite volume effects relevant for heavy-ion collision fireballs. The low momentum cutoff approximation follows a similar approach used in the hadron resonance gas model by Karsch (2015), which showed good agreement with direct finite volume calculations. The results enable future comparisons with full momentum discretization methods, as used by Palhares (2009), Tripolt (2013), and Bernhardt (2021), and further studies of baryon fluctuations near the critical endpoint.
