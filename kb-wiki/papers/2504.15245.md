# Probing the octupole deformation of $^{238}$U in high-energy nuclear collisions

> Some atomic nuclei exhibit ``pear" shapes arising from octupole deformation ($β_3$), though direct experimental evidences for such exotic shapes remains scarce. Low-energy model studies suggest $^{238}$U may have a modest octupole deformation arising from collective vibrational degrees of freedom, in addition to a large prolate shape. We investigated the impact of this modest octupole shape on observables involving triangular flow ($v_3$) in high-energy nuclear collisions. Using a hydrodynamic framework, we show $v_3$ and its correlation with mean transverse momentum, $\langle v_3^2 δp_{\rm{T}} \rangle$, exhibit strong sensitivity to $β_3$. We found that $\langle v_3^2\rangle$ follows a linear increase with $β_3^2$, while the characteristic anticorrelation in $\langle v_3^2 δp_{\rm{T}} \rangle$ shows a pronounced $β_3$-dependent suppression. Our findings demonstrate that the collective-flow-assisted nuclear imaging method in high-energy nuclear collisions, when compared with experimental data, can provide unique quantitative constraints on higher-order deformations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that octupole deformation (\(\beta_3\)) in \(^{238}\)U can be probed via triangular flow (\(v_3\)) and its correlation with mean transverse momentum in high-energy nuclear collisions, but the claim is conditional: the sensitivity requires that \(^{238}\)U has a large quadrupole deformation (\(\beta_2\)) to amplify the octupole signal, and the extracted \(\beta_3\) may reflect either static deformation or vibrational collectivity, which low-energy experiments cannot distinguish.

## Method

The authors used the IP-Glasma initial-state model coupled with (2+1)D relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (MUSIC) and hadronic transport (UrQMD) to simulate \(^{238}\)U+\(^{238}\)U collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 193\) GeV and \(^{197}\)Au+\(^{197}\)Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV. They varied \(\beta_{3,\rm{U}}\) from 0 to 0.20 while fixing \(\beta_{2,\rm{U}} = 0.28\) and \(\gamma_{\rm{U}} = 0^\circ\), and also tested \(\beta_{4,\rm{U}} = 0\) and 0.09. Ratios of \(\langle v_3^2 \rangle\) and \(\langle v_3^2 \delta p_{\rm{T}} \rangle\) between the two collision systems were constructed to cancel final-state effects and isolate deformation signals.

## Key numbers

* For \(\beta_{3,\rm{U}} = 0.078\)–0.10 (from nuclear structure predictions), the ratio \(R_{v_3^2} = \langle v_3^2 \rangle_{\rm{U+U}} / \langle v_3^2 \rangle_{\rm{Au+Au}}\) in 0–2% ultra-central collisions is predicted to be 1.05–1.12.
* \(\langle v_3^2 \rangle\) follows a linear increase with \(\beta_3^2\) in 0–2% centrality; the ratio \(R_{v_3^2}\) exceeds unity for \(\beta_{3,\rm{U}} \gtrsim 0.05\).
* For \(\beta_{3,\rm{U}} > 0.15\), \(\langle v_3^2 \delta p_{\rm{T}} \rangle\) becomes negative, producing a strong suppression in the ratio \(R_{v_3^2 \delta p_{\rm{T}}}\).
* The ratio \(R_{v_3^2 \delta p_{\rm{T}}}\) in ultra-central collisions shows a near-linear dependence on \(\beta_{2,\rm{U}} \beta_{3,\rm{U}}^2\), with values in the range 0.5–1 for \(\beta_{3,\rm{U}} \lesssim 0.1\) and \(\beta_{4,\rm{U}} \approx 0.05\)–0.1.
* \(\beta_{4,\rm{U}} = 0.09\) alone produces a negative contribution to \(R_{v_3^2 \delta p_{\rm{T}}}\) comparable to \(\beta_{3,\rm{U}} = 0.10\) alone.

## Context

This work extends the flow-assisted nuclear imaging method previously applied to quadrupole and triaxial deformations in \(^{238}\)U by the STAR experiment (STAR 2024) and to hexadecapole deformation in model studies (Ryssens 2023, Xu 2024, Wang 2024). It provides the first quantitative hydrodynamic predictions for octupole deformation signatures in \(^{238}\)U+\(^{238}\)U collisions, enabling future experimental constraints from the STAR Collaboration. The results also connect to low-energy nuclear structure predictions for octupole collectivity in \(^{238}\)U (Agbemava 2016, McGowan 1994, Kibedi 2002) and open the question of whether the extracted \(\beta_3\) reflects static deformation or a vibrational mode.
