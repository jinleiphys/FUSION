# New Forms of QCD Matter Discovered at RHIC

> We discuss two special limiting forms of QCD matter which may be produced at RHIC. We conclude from the available empirical evidence that an equilibrated, but strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma has been made in such collisions. We also discuss the growing body of evidence that its source is a Color Glass Condensate.

## Key claim

Based on the available empirical evidence from RHIC, an equilibrated but strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma (sQGP) has been produced, and there is growing evidence that its source is a Color Glass Condensate (CGC); however, the QGP hypothesis is on firmer theoretical ground than the CGC hypothesis, which is newer and "somewhat more tenuous."

## Method

The authors review published experimental data from the four RHIC experiments (BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR) on Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 AGeV, together with lower-energy data from the SPS/CERN, to assess evidence for two limiting forms of QCD matter. They examine collective flow observables (particularly elliptic flow v2) as a test of thermalization and the QCD equation of state, and they analyze particle multiplicities and transverse energy measurements to estimate initial energy densities via Bjorken scaling and one-dimensional hydrodynamic expansion.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for the QGP transition is T_c ~ 160 MeV, with the energy density changing by about an order of magnitude over a narrow temperature range Delta T ~ 10-20 MeV.
- The initial energy density achieved at RHIC is bounded between 2-3 GeV/fm^3 (lower bound) and 20-30 GeV/fm^3 (upper bound), compared to nuclear matter density of about 0.15 GeV/fm^3.
- The proper formation time for the saturated color glass at RHIC is tau_0 ~ 1/Q_s ~ 0.2 fm/c, comparable to the crossing time of Lorentz-contracted nuclei.
- The particle multiplicity as a function of center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair has been measured at RHIC and lower AGS and SPS energies, shown in Fig. 6 of the paper.

## Context

This paper extends the theoretical framework developed over 30 years from the Bevalac, SIS, AGS, and SPS experiments, building on the foundational work of Collins and Perry (1975) on deconfined QCD matter and Bjorken (1983) on relativistic hydrodynamic evolution. The authors argue that RHIC data now allow tests of QCD predictions in a regime where uncertainties from the poorly understood hadronic resonance phase are minimized, in contrast to lower-energy collisions where effective hadronic models (QHD, Chiral Perturbation Theory) are required. The work enables future systematic studies of sQGP and CGC properties at RHIC and the LHC, and motivates searches for the QCD critical point at finite baryon density.
