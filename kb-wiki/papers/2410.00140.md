# Probing fermionic asymmetric dark matter cores using global neutron star properties

> It is possible for asymmetric dark matter (ADM) to accumulate in neutron star interiors and affect their global properties. Considering the effects of this accumulation, neutron star mass-radius measurements can deliver new insights into the cold dense matter equation of state (EoS). In this paper, we employ Bayesian parameter estimation using real and synthetic neutron star mass-radius data to infer constraints on the combined baryonic matter and fermionic ADM EoS, where the fermionic ADM forms a core in the neutron star interior. Using currently available mass-radius data, we find that the lower bound of the ratio between ADM effective self-repulsion strength ($g_χ/m_ϕ$) and particle mass ($m_χ$) can be constrained at the 68\% (95\%) credible level to $10^{-6.59}$ ($10^{-7.77}$). We also find that, if neutron star mass-radius measurement uncertainties are reduced to the 2\% level, the constraints on the lower bound of the ratio of $g_χ/m_ϕ$ to $m_χ$ can be improved to $10^{-6.49}$ and $10^{-7.68}$ at the 68\% and 95\% credible levels, respectively. However, all other combinations, of $m_χ$, $g_χ$, and the ADM mass-fraction, $F_χ$, (i.e., the ratio of the gravitational ADM mass to the gravitational mass of the neutron star) are unconstrained. Furthermore, in the pressure-energy density and mass-radius planes, the inferences which include the possibility of fermionic ADM cores are nearly identical with the inferences that neglect fermionic ADM for $F_χ\leq 1.7\%$ and neutron star mass-radius uncertainties $\geq 2\%$. Therefore, we find that neutron star mass-radius measurements can constrain the ratio of $g_χ/m_ϕ$ to $m_χ$ and that neutron stars with ADM are indistinguishable from purely baryonic stars. This implies that neutron stars with ADM are equally as consistent with the available mass-radius data as neutron stars without ADM.

## Key claim

Using current NICER mass-radius data, the lower bound of the ratio between fermionic asymmetric dark matter effective self-repulsion strength and particle mass can be constrained, but all other combinations of dark matter particle mass, self-repulsion strength, and mass-fraction remain unconstrained; furthermore, neutron stars with fermionic ADM cores are indistinguishable from purely baryonic stars for ADM mass-fractions up to 1.7% and current measurement uncertainties.

## Method

The authors employed Bayesian parameter estimation using the two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to model neutron stars containing both baryonic matter and a fermionic ADM core described by the Nelson (2018) model. The baryonic matter equation of state was modeled using a parametrized piecewise polytropic model connected to chiral effective field theory calculations at low densities, while the fermionic ADM equation of state included repulsive self-interactions mediated by a vector gauge boson. The analysis used both real NICER mass-radius data from PSR J0740+6620 and PSR J0030+0451, and synthetic data representing six future STROBE-X sources with 2% measurement uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Lower bound of the ratio between ADM effective self-repulsion strength (g_chi/m_phi) and particle mass (m_chi) constrained to 10^{-6.59} at 68% credible level and 10^{-7.77} at 95% credible level using current NICER data
- With 2% measurement uncertainties (Future-X scenario), constraints improve to 10^{-6.49} at 68% credible level and 10^{-7.68} at 95% credible level
- ADM mass-fraction threshold for indistinguishability from purely baryonic stars: F_chi less than or equal to 1.7% for neutron star mass-radius uncertainties greater than or equal to 2%
- Dark matter particle mass range considered: MeV-GeV scale; vector gauge boson mass scale: eV-MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Bayesian analysis of bosonic ADM cores (Rutherford 2023) to fermionic ADM, which has additional support against gravity through Fermi degeneracy pressure and allows zero self-repulsion. It differs from prior studies by Miao (2022), who used fixed baryonic EoSs and found fermionic ADM particle masses near 0.6 GeV for cores, and Guha (2024), who used 10 baryonic EoS models with hard cut-offs from NICER and LIGO/VIRGO data. The results enable future work to determine whether improved mass-radius measurements from missions like STROBE-X, eXTP, or Athena can break degeneracies between ADM parameters and distinguish ADM-admixed from purely baryonic neutron stars.
