# Production of fully-heavy tetraquark states through the double parton scattering mechanism in $pp$ and $pA$ collisions

> The production of fully-heavy tetraquark states in proton-proton ($pp$) and proton-nucleus ($pA$) collisions at the center-of-mass energies of the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and at the Future Circular Collider (FCC) is investigated considering that these states are produced through the double parton scattering mechanism. We estimate the cross sections for the $T_{4c}$, $T_{4b}$ and $T_{2b2c}$ states and present predictions for $pp$, $pCa$ and $pPb$ collisions considering the rapidity ranges covered by central and forward detectors. We demonstrate that the cross sections for $pA$ collisions are enhanced in comparison to the $pp$ predictions scaled by the atomic number. Moreover, our results indicate that a search of these exotic states is, in principle, feasible in the future runs of the LHC and FCC.

## Key claim

The cross sections for fully-heavy tetraquark production via double parton scattering in proton-nucleus collisions are enhanced by a factor larger than the simple atomic-number scaling expected from a superposition of independent proton-nucleon collisions, making a future experimental search feasible at the LHC and FCC.

## Method

The authors model the production of fully-heavy tetraquark states ($T_{4c}$, $T_{4b}$, $T_{2b2c}$) as arising from two independent gluon-gluon scatterings in a single hadron-hadron collision, each producing a heavy quark pair. The transition from the $Q_i\bar{Q}_iQ_j\bar{Q}_j$ system to the color-neutral tetraquark is described by the Color Evaporation Model with a nonperturbative factor $F_{\cal{T}}$, fixed by normalizing to the $X(3872)$ cross section measured by CMS. For proton-nucleus collisions, two contributions are summed: one where both gluons from the nucleus come from the same nucleon (scaling as $A$ times the $pp$ cross section) and one where they come from different nucleons (involving the nuclear thickness function). Calculations use the CT14 gluon distribution, $m_c = 1.5$ GeV, $m_b = 4.5$ GeV, and $\sigma_{eff,pp} = 15$ mb.

## Key numbers

- $T_{4c}$ production cross section in $pp$ collisions at LHC ($\sqrt{s}=14.0$ TeV): 6.05 nb (central detector, $-2.5 \le Y \le +2.5$) and 1.86 nb (forward detector, $+2.0 \le Y \le +4.5$).
- $T_{4c}$ production cross section in $pPb$ collisions at LHC ($\sqrt{s}=8.1$ TeV): 3816.44 nb (central) and 1171.86 nb (forward).
- $T_{4b}$ production cross section in $pp$ collisions at LHC: 0.00033 nb (central) and 0.000078 nb (forward).
- $T_{2b2c}$ production cross section in $pp$ collisions at LHC: 0.021 nb (central) and 0.0055 nb (forward).
- $T_{4c}$ production cross section in $pp$ collisions at FCC ($\sqrt{s}=100.0$ TeV): 57.27 nb (central) and 21.74 nb (forward).
- $pCa$ ($A=40$) cross sections for $T_{4c}$ are enhanced by a factor $\approx 85$ relative to $pp$ predictions; $pPb$ ($A=208$) cross sections are enhanced by a factor $\approx 630$ (approximately $3A$).
- The nonperturbative factor $F_{\cal{T}} = 0.00119$ is used for all tetraquark states.

## Context

This work updates the model of Carvalho (2015) for $T_{4c}$ production by using a mass of 6.9 GeV (consistent with the LHCb di-$J/\psi$ resonance) instead of 5.4 GeV, and adopts the CT14 gluon parametrization. It extends the double parton scattering mechanism to $T_{4b}$ and $T_{2b2c}$ states, and presents the first predictions for fully-heavy tetraquark production in proton-nucleus collisions, motivated by LHCb results showing enhancement of double parton scattering in $pA$ collisions as predicted by earlier theoretical work (e.g., treleanistrik, dps_pa, Cazaroto (2016)). The results suggest that DPS contributions are at least comparable to single parton scattering contributions, making these predictions a lower bound for total cross sections.
