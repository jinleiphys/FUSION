# Quark-nucleon dynamics and Deep Virtual Compton Scattering

> We consider deeply virtual Compton scattering and deep inelastic scattering in presence of Regge exchanges that are part of the non-perturbative quark-nucleon amplitude. In particular we discuss contribution from the Pomeron exchange and demonstrate how it leads to Regge scaling of the Compton amplitude. Comparison with HERA data is given.

## Key claim

The paper argues that when Regge exchanges (specifically the Pomeron) are incorporated into the quark-nucleon amplitude within the handbag approximation, the deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) amplitude breaks collinear factorization and Bjorken scaling, instead exhibiting Regge-type scaling; this breakdown is presented as not unexpected because the collinear approximation assumes the parton-nucleon amplitude is a soft function of the invariant parton-nucleon energy, which fails when the amplitude has a Regge-type dependence.

## Method

The authors construct a model where an infinite class of perturbative gluon ladder diagrams is absorbed into an effective quark-nucleon amplitude with Regge asymptotics, then compute the DVCS amplitude using the handbag diagram. They decompose the quark-nucleon scattering amplitude into a basis of six independent Dirac-Lorentz structures, impose Regge asymptotics on the spectral functions, and evaluate the Compton amplitude both in the collinear approximation and beyond it using Feynman parameter integration. The resulting DVCS cross section is fitted to HERA data from H1 and ZEUS using a parametrization motivated by the Regge scaling derived from the model.

## Key numbers

- Combined fit to H1 and ZEUS data: sigma_0 = 28 +/- 4 nb, Q_0 = 1.51 +/- 0.05 GeV, alpha-1 = 0.43 +/- 0.03, with chi^2/d.o.f. = 2.01
- Independent fit to H1 data alone: sigma_0 = 17 +/- 3 nb, Q_0 = 1.83 +/- 0.1 GeV, alpha-1 = 0.34 +/- 0.05, with chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.2
- Independent fit to ZEUS data alone: sigma_0 = 41 +/- 7 nb, Q_0 = 1.49 +/- 0.06 GeV, alpha-1 = 0.34 +/- 0.03, with chi^2/d.o.f. = 1.1
- The leading Pomeron contribution to the DVCS amplitude scales as T_DVCS ~ (1/Q^2) * (Q^2/x_B)^(alpha_P) ~ W^(2*alpha_P)/Q^2 in the limit Q^2/mu^2 >> 1
- The experimental DVCS cross section follows sigma_DVCS = sigma_0 * (W^2)^(2*alpha-2) * (Q^2)^delta with delta approximately -1.5

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous model (Gorchtein and Szczepaniak, 2009; 2010) that incorporated diffractive phenomena at the parton-nucleon level, building on earlier studies of Regge phenomena in DIS by Landshoff (1970) and Brodsky (1973). The paper contrasts its Regge-based approach with GPD-based models that include Regge-like background (Polyakov and Guzy, 2002; Lech, 2003) and with color dipole models (Donnachie and Dosch, 2000; Jenkovszky, 2000). The result that DVCS data can be described without GPDs raises questions about the model independence of GPD extraction from HERA data, and the derived parametrization matches the form previously proposed by Donnachie and Dosch (2000).
