# Dead cone due to parton virtuality

> A general expression for the dead cone of gluons radiated by virtual partons has been derived. The conventional dead cone for massive on-shell quarks and the dead cone for the massless virtual partons have been obtained by using different limits of the general expression. Radiative suppression due to the virtuality of initial parton jets in Heavy-Ion Collisions (HIC) has been discussed. It is observed that the suppression caused by the high virtuality is overwhelmingly large as compared to that on account of conventional dead-cone of heavy quarks. The dead cone due to virtuality may play a crucial role in explaining the observed similar suppression patterns of light and heavy quarks jets in heavy ion collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC).

## Key claim

The authors derive a general expression for the dead cone of gluons radiated by virtual partons, showing that the radiative suppression due to high virtuality is "overwhelmingly large" compared to the conventional dead cone from quark mass. This virtuality-induced dead cone may explain the observed similar suppression patterns of light and heavy quark jets at RHIC, a result that is conditional on the partons being off-shell with sufficiently high virtuality.

## Method

The authors start from tree-level Feynman diagrams for gluon radiation in the process e+e- → Q Qbar g, assuming low virtuality (V² = q² - m_Q²) for external quarks so that Dirac's equation remains approximately valid. They isolate the soft part of the amplitude, square it, and sum over spins to obtain a radiation factor R. Working in the equal-beta frame where the quark and antiquark have equal velocities back-to-back, they derive a general expression for the gluon emission spectrum F as a function of virtuality V, parton energy E, and emission angle θ. They then explore the limits V=0 (conventional dead cone for massive on-shell quarks) and β=1 (light quarks) to recover known results.

## Key numbers

* For heavy quarks with E = 1 GeV, the normalized dead cone factor F_RHθ(E,θ) = F(E=1 GeV,θ)/F(E=1 GeV,θ=0) shows suppression at small θ similar to the conventional dead cone for massive quarks.
* For light partons (β=1) with E = 1 GeV, the normalized factor F_RLθ shows a sin⁻²θ behavior, giving a minimum at θ=π/2 rather than a maximum.
* At E = 20 GeV, the normalized F for both light and heavy quarks shows similar suppression patterns as a function of θ, indicating virtuality-induced suppression dominates over mass effects.
* The general expression for the spectrum is F = 4β² (V⁴/(ω²E²) + 4V²/(ωE) + 4sin²θ) / (V⁴/(ω²E²) + 4V²/(ωE) + 4(1-β²cos²θ))².

## Context

This work extends the conventional dead cone formalism of Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001) for massive on-shell quarks to include the effect of parton virtuality, following the suggestion by Kopeliovich (2004) that virtuality from initial hard collisions creates its own dead cone. The authors also reference the work of Roy (2007) concluding that radiative energy loss reduction for heavy quarks is due to the dead cone effect. The result enables a unified explanation for the similar suppression of light and heavy hadrons observed by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations at RHIC, which has been a puzzle because conventional dead cone theory predicts less suppression for heavy quarks.
