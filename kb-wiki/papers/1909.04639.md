# Universality driven analytic structure of the QCD crossover: radius of convergence in the baryon chemical potential

> Recent lattice QCD calculations strongly indicate that the chiral crossover of QCD at zero baryon chemical potential μ_B is a remnant of the second-order chiral phase transition. Universal properties of this second-order phase transition can be mapped to QCD temperature T and μ_B using non-universal parameters determined by lattice QCD recently. Motivated by these results, first, we discuss the analytic structure of the partition function in the QCD crossover regime - the so-called Yang-Lee edge singularity - solely based on universal properties. Next, utilizing the lattice QCD results for non-universal parameters we map this singularity to the real T and complex μ_B plane, leading to the determination of the radius of convergence is in μ_B in the QCD crossover regime. These universality- and QCD-based results provide tight constraints on the range of validity of the lattice QCD calculations at μ_B. Implication of this result on the location of the conjectured QCD critical point is discussed.

## Key claim

The radius of convergence of the QCD partition function in the baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\) is determined by mapping the universal Yang-Lee edge singularity of the O(4) scaling function to the real temperature and complex \(\mu_B\) plane, using non-universal parameters from lattice QCD. The result provides a lower bound of \(|\mu_B| \gtrsim 400\) MeV for the radius of convergence, implying that the conjectured QCD critical point, if it exists, is most likely located at \(\mu_B \gtrsim 400\) MeV.

## Method

The authors use the universal scaling function \(\g(z)\) of the three-dimensional O(4) universality class, which describes the chiral condensate in the scaling regime. They map the scaling variable \(z\) to QCD variables (temperature \(T\), baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\), and light quark mass \(m_l\)) using non-universal parameters from lattice QCD: the chiral transition temperature \(T_c^0\), the curvature \(\kappa^B_2\) of the pseudo-critical line, and the scale factor \(z_0\). The location of the Yang-Lee edge singularity \(z_c\) in the complex \(z\)-plane is taken from Functional Renormalization Group calculations, and this singularity is translated to the complex \(\mu_B\) plane to compute the radius of convergence \(R_{\rm conv}\) as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

* The chiral transition temperature for massless u/d quarks is \(T_c^0 = 132^{+3}_{-6}\) MeV (Ding 2019).
* The pseudo-critical temperature at zero chemical potential is \(T_{pc} = 156.5 \pm 1.5\) MeV (Bazavov 2018).
* The curvature of the pseudo-critical line is \(\kappa_2^B = 0.012(2)\) (Bazavov 2018).
* The magnitude of the Yang-Lee edge singularity for O(4) is \(|z_c| \approx 1.665\) from Functional Renormalization Group calculations (Connelly 2020).
* The O(4) critical exponents are \(\beta = 0.380\) and \(\delta = 4.824\) (Engels 2003).
* The scale factor is estimated to be \(z_0 \simeq 1-2\) (HotQCD collaboration, private communication).
* The radius of convergence is larger than \(|\mu_B| \gtrsim 400\) MeV for temperatures in the vicinity of the QCD chiral crossover.

## Context

This work extends the discussion of the analytic structure of the QCD crossover and its connection to the radius of convergence previously explored by Stephanov (2006) and Almasi (2019), who provided mean-field (random matrix) model-based estimates. The authors use recent lattice QCD results for non-universal parameters (Bazavov 2018, Ding 2019) and the Functional Renormalization Group determination of the Yang-Lee edge singularity location (Connelly 2020) to produce a universality-based estimate. This result provides a tight constraint on the range of validity of lattice QCD calculations at non-zero \(\mu_B\) (Taylor expansions and analytic continuation from imaginary \(\mu_B\)) and suggests that the QCD critical point, if it exists, is likely at \(\mu_B \gtrsim 400\) MeV.
