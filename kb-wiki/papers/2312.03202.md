# Numerical Convergence of Electromagnetic Responses with the Finite-Amplitude Method

> The response of a nucleus to an electromagnetic probe is a key quantity to simulate photabsorption or photodeexcitation processes. For large calculations at the scale of the entire mass table, this response can be estimated by linear response theory. Thanks to the introduction of the finite-amplitude method (FAM), calculations are computationally efficient. In this paper, we investigate in more details the convergence of FAM calculations of the response function as a function of the parameters controlling the numerical implementation of the theory. We show that the response is much less sensitive to the details of the single-particle basis than, e.g., Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations.

## Key claim
The electromagnetic response function computed with the finite-amplitude method (FAM) is much less sensitive to the details of the harmonic oscillator basis (number of shells, oscillator length, basis deformation) than the underlying Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) ground-state energy, and is also insensitive to the quasiparticle cutoff when implemented consistently at both HFB and FAM levels; only the smearing width Γ has a visible impact on the results.

## Method
The authors performed FAM calculations of the isoscalar electric quadrupole (E2, K=0) response in ²⁴⁰Pu using the SLy4 Skyrme functional with a zero-range density-dependent pairing force, with HFB ground states computed using the code HFBTHO in axial symmetry. They systematically varied the harmonic oscillator basis parameters (number of shells N_sh, oscillator length b₀, basis deformation β₂), the quasiparticle cutoff energy E_cut (with refitting of pairing strengths to maintain constant pairing gaps in ²³²Th), and the smearing width Γ, then compared the resulting response functions and HFB energies.

## Key numbers
- The height of the second peak at ω ≈ 10 MeV varies by less than 15% when changing the number of shells N_sh in the basis.
- The variation of the HFB energy with oscillator length b₀ does not exceed 700 keV at N_sh = 20 shells.
- The default smearing width used throughout the study (unless explicitly specified) is Γ = 1.0 MeV.
- Pairing strengths used: V₀^(n) = -219.973 MeV and V₀^(p) = -285.524 MeV for neutrons and protons respectively.

## Context
This work extends earlier FAM studies by Nakatsukasa (2007) and Avogadro (2011) that established the method's computational efficiency, and by Stoitsov (2011) and Oishi (2016) which presented results for only two basis sizes. The paper systematically quantifies numerical uncertainties that had not been fully discussed in prior applications, enabling future large-scale calculations of electromagnetic responses across the entire nuclear mass table. The authors note that the smearing width Γ has by far the largest impact on results, which connects to phenomenological Lorentzian fits of photoabsorption cross sections as in Bohr (1998) and the survey of experimental Γ variation with mass number by Goriely (2019).
