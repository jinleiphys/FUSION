# On the Stability of $Λ(1405)$ Matter

> A hypothesis of absolutely stable strange hadronic matter composed of $Λ(1405)$ baryons, here denoted $Λ^*$, is tested within many-body calculations performed using the Relativistic Mean-Field approach. In our calculations, we employed the $Λ^*Λ^*$ interaction compatible with the $Λ^*Λ^*$ binding energy $B_{Λ^*Λ^*}=40$~MeV given by the phenomenological energy-independent $\bar{K}N$ interaction model by Yamazaki and Akaishi (YA). We found that the binding energy per $Λ^*$, as well as the central density in $Λ^*$ many-body systems saturates for mass number $A\geq120$, leaving $Λ^*$ aggregates highly unstable against strong interaction decay. Moreover, we confronted the YA interaction model with kaonic atom data and found that it fails to reproduce the $K^-$ single-nucleon absorption fractions at rest from bubble chamber experiments.

## Key claim

The hypothesis that aggregates of Λ(1405) baryons (Λ*) could form absolutely stable strange hadronic matter is tested and found to be false: although binding energy per Λ* and central density saturate for mass numbers A ≥ 120, the resulting Λ* aggregates remain highly unstable against strong interaction decay, and the underlying Yamazaki-Akaishi (YA) interaction model fails to reproduce kaonic atom data and K⁻ single-nucleon absorption fractions.

## Method

The authors performed Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) calculations of many-body systems composed solely of Λ(1405) baryons, using meson-exchange Λ*Λ* potentials (Dover-Gal and Machleidt forms) fitted to reproduce the Λ*Λ* binding energy of 40 MeV predicted by the YA model. They solved the coupled Klein-Gordon equations for σ and ω meson fields and the Dirac equation for Λ* self-consistently, with coupling constants scaled to match the two-body binding energy. Additionally, they confronted the YA interaction model with kaonic atom data by constructing optical potentials and calculating χ² fits, and computed K⁻ single-nucleon absorption fractions for comparison with bubble chamber experiments.

## Key numbers

- Λ*Λ* binding energy B_Λ*Λ* = 40 MeV, derived from the YA model as B(KKNN)_I=0 − 2B(KN)_I=0 = 93 MeV − 2×27 MeV ≈ 40 MeV.
- The YA-based optical potential yields χ²(65) = 150 for 65 kaonic atom data points, worse than chiral models (e.g., B4: χ²=105, KM: χ²=123).
- Binding energy per Λ* saturates for mass number A ≥ 120, reaching tens of MeV depending on the potential; far below ≈ 290 MeV needed to reduce the Λ(1405) mass below the Λ(1116) mass.
- Central density in Λ* systems saturates at 0.25–0.35 fm⁻³, about twice nuclear matter density.
- For a system of 168 Λ* baryons, the 1s single-particle energy ε_Λ* = −202.6 MeV and conversion width Γ_Λ*Λ* = 99.9 MeV (using HS model with α_σ = 1.0913).

## Context

This work tests the hypothesis of Akaishi and Yamazaki (2017) that Λ* matter becomes absolutely stable for A ≥ 8, which was based on few-body calculations with purely attractive Λ*Λ* interactions that necessarily lead to collapse. The authors extend the analysis to many-body systems using the Relativistic Mean-Field approach, which naturally incorporates saturation through Lorentz covariance, and find no stability. They also confront the YA model with kaonic atom data and bubble chamber experiments, showing it fails tests that chiral models (e.g., Kyoto-Munich, Prague) pass. This rules out the YA model as a viable basis for stable Λ* matter and suggests that future work should use chiral interaction models with energy-dependent amplitudes.
