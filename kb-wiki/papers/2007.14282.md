# Variational Monte Carlo calculations of $\mathbf{A\leq 4}$ nuclei with an artificial neural-network correlator ansatz

> The complexity of many-body quantum wave functions is a central aspect of several fields of physics and chemistry where non-perturbative interactions are prominent. Artificial neural networks (ANNs) have proven to be a flexible tool to approximate quantum many-body states in condensed matter and chemistry problems. In this work we introduce a neural-network quantum state ansatz to model the ground-state wave function of light nuclei, and approximately solve the nuclear many-body Schrödinger equation. Using efficient stochastic sampling and optimization schemes, our approach extends pioneering applications of ANNs in the field, which present exponentially-scaling algorithmic complexity. We compute the binding energies and point-nucleon densities of $A\leq 4$ nuclei as emerging from a leading-order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian. We successfully benchmark the ANN wave function against more conventional parametrizations based on two- and three-body Jastrow functions, and virtually-exact Green's function Monte Carlo results.

# Variational Monte Carlo calculations of A ≤ 4 nuclei with an artificial neural-network correlator ansatz

## Key claim

The artificial neural-network (ANN) correlator ansatz reproduces ground-state energies of A ≤ 4 nuclei to within about 0.1 MeV of virtually-exact Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) results for the deuteron and triton, and about 0.3-0.6 MeV for ⁴He, when using a leading-order pionless effective field theory Hamiltonian; the remaining discrepancy is attributed to spin-dependent correlations not fully captured by the ANN ansatz, rather than a fundamental limitation of the neural-network representation.

## Method

The authors construct a variational wave function using a Deep Sets architecture to enforce permutation invariance, with separate neural networks for a spin-independent correlator U and a spin-isospin dependent correlator V applied to a mean-field Slater determinant. They minimize the variational energy using the stochastic-reconfiguration (natural gradient) algorithm with Monte Carlo sampling, computing ground-state energies and point-nucleon densities for ²H, ³H, and ⁴He with a leading-order pionless EFT Hamiltonian at two cutoff values (Λ = 4 fm⁻¹ and Λ = 6 fm⁻¹). The ANN results are benchmarked against conventional variational Monte Carlo with two- and three-body Jastrow functions (VMC-JS) and against virtually-exact GFMC calculations.

## Key numbers

- ²H ground-state energy at Λ = 4 fm⁻¹: VMC-ANN = −2.224(1) MeV, VMC-JS = −2.223(1) MeV, GFMC = −2.224(1) MeV
- ²H ground-state energy at Λ = 6 fm⁻¹: VMC-ANN = −2.224(4) MeV, VMC-JS = −2.220(1) MeV, GFMC = −2.225(1) MeV
- ³H ground-state energy at Λ = 4 fm⁻¹: VMC-ANN = −8.26(1) MeV, VMC-JS = −7.80(1) MeV, GFMC = −8.38(2) MeV
- ³H ground-state energy at Λ = 6 fm⁻¹: VMC-ANN = −8.27(1) MeV, VMC-JS = −7.74(1) MeV, GFMC = −8.38(2) MeV
- ⁴He ground-state energy at Λ = 4 fm⁻¹: VMC-ANN = −23.30(2) MeV, VMC-JS = −22.54(1) MeV, GFMC = −23.62(3) MeV
- ⁴He ground-state energy at Λ = 6 fm⁻¹: VMC-ANN = −24.47(3) MeV, VMC-JS = −23.44(2) MeV, GFMC = −25.06(3) MeV
- ANN improvement over Jastrow for ³H: approximately 0.5 MeV more bound at both cutoffs
- ANN improvement over Jastrow for ⁴He: approximately 0.8 MeV (Λ = 4 fm⁻¹) and 1.0 MeV (Λ = 6 fm⁻¹) more bound
- ANN contains 13058 trainable parameters total

## Context

This work extends pioneering ANN applications to nuclear physics, which previously demonstrated a proof-of-principle for the deuteron (Keeble 2019) but with exponentially-scaling algorithmic complexity. The authors build on neural-network correlator approaches developed for condensed matter (Nomura 2017, Ferrari 2019) and chemistry (Hermann 2019), adapting them to include spin-isospin dependent correlations for nuclear systems. The results enable future application of ANN-based variational Monte Carlo to medium-mass nuclei, where conventional Jastrow-based methods face exponential scaling with nucleon number; the authors note that remaining discrepancies with GFMC could be resolved by introducing spin-dependent backflow correlations as in recent chemistry applications (Pfau 2019, Hermann 2019, Choo 2019).
