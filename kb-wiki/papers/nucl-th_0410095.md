# Renormalization of the Sigma-Omega model within the framework of U(1) gauge symmetry

> It is shown that the Sigma-Omega model which is widely used in the study of nuclear relativistic many-body problem can exactly be treated as an Abelian massive gauge field theory. The quantization of this theory can perfectly be performed by means of the general methods described in the quantum gauge field theory. Especially, the local U(1) gauge symmetry of the theory leads to a series of Ward-Takahashi identities satisfied by Green's functions and proper vertices. These identities form an uniquely correct basis for the renormalization of the theory. The renormalization is carried out in the mass-dependent momentum space subtraction scheme and by the renormalization group approach. With the aid of the renormalization boundary conditions, the solutions to the renormalization group equations are given in definite expressions without any ambiguity and renormalized S-matrix elememts are exactly formulated in forms as given in a series of tree diagrams provided that the physical parameters are replaced by the running ones. As an illustration of the renormalization procedure, the one-loop renormalization is concretely carried out and the results are given in rigorous forms which are suitable in the whole energy region. The effect of the one-loop renormalization is examined by the two-nucleon elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Sigma-Omega (Walecka) model, despite the mass term of the omega meson, possesses a local U(1) gauge symmetry when the Lorentz condition is treated as a primary constraint on the vector field, and that this gauge symmetry provides the correct basis for renormalization via Ward-Takahashi identities, contrary to previous treatments that considered the model gauge-non-invariant.

## Method

The authors first demonstrate that the omega meson field must be treated as a constrained system, with the Lorentz condition imposed from the outset, and show the Lagrangian expressed in terms of the transverse vector potential is gauge-invariant. They then quantize the model in both Hamiltonian and Lagrangian path-integral formalisms, obtaining an effective action with gauge-fixing and ghost terms that exhibits BRST symmetry. Renormalization is performed using mass-dependent momentum space subtraction combined with renormalization group equations, with Ward-Takahashi identities derived from the U(1) gauge symmetry providing the basis for the renormalization procedure. One-loop renormalization is carried out explicitly, and the effect is examined through two-nucleon elastic scattering.

## Key numbers

* The omega meson mass \(m_\omega\) and sigma meson mass \(m_\sigma\) appear as parameters in the Lagrangian, with the condition \(m_\omega \neq 0\) ensuring the Lorentz condition \(\partial^\mu A_\mu = 0\) follows from the constraint equations.
* The gauge parameter \(\alpha\) appears in the effective Lagrangian, with the ghost particle acquiring a spurious mass \(\nu = \sqrt{\alpha} m_\omega\) for \(\alpha \neq 0\).
* The one-loop renormalization results are given in "rigorous forms which are suitable in the whole energy region" and are examined via the differential cross section of two-nucleon elastic scattering in the approximation of order \(g^2\).

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group approach previously applied by the authors to QED and QCD (Su, 2004) to the Sigma-Omega model of quantum hadrodynamics (QHD). It directly addresses difficulties noted in prior renormalization attempts, such as the finding by Chin (1977) that two-loop contributions altered nuclear bound state descriptions qualitatively, leading to the conclusion that "the loop expansion does not provide a reliable approximation scheme in renormalizable QHD" (Serot, 1992). The paper enables a systematic renormalization procedure for the Sigma-Omega model based on gauge symmetry principles, responding to the call to "develop and apply systematic and consistent 'power counting' schemes" and to "study renormalization group methods that could determine the analytic structure of the ground-state energy functional" (Serot, 1992).
