# Over-populated gauge fields on the lattice

> We study nonequilibrium dynamics of SU(2) pure gauge theory starting from initial over-population, where intense classical gauge fields are characterized by a single momentum scale Q_s. Classical-statistical lattice simulations indicate a quick evolution towards an approximate scaling behavior with exponent 3/2 at intermediate times. Remarkably, the value for the scaling exponent may be understood as arising from the leading O(g^2) contribution in the presence of a time-dependent background field. The phenomenon is associated to weak wave turbulence describing an energy cascade towards higher momenta. This particular aspect is very similar to what is observed for scalar theories, where an effective cubic interaction arises because of the presence of a time-dependent Bose condensate.

# Over-populated gauge fields on the lattice

## Key claim

Classical-statistical lattice simulations of SU(2) pure gauge theory starting from initial over-population show an approximate scaling behavior with exponent 3/2 at intermediate times, which can be understood as arising from the leading O(g²) contribution in the presence of a time-dependent background field. This scaling is not a true steady state but a quasi-stationary evolution that eventually deviates from the power law, with the system slowly evolving toward lower exponents.

## Method

The authors performed classical-statistical lattice gauge theory simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills theory using the Wilsonian lattice action in Minkowski space-time, with initial Gaussian conditions characterized by a single momentum scale Q_s and non-perturbatively large occupancy n_p(Q_s) ~ 1/g². The dynamics was solved in temporal axial gauge, then configurations were transformed to Coulomb gauge via overrelaxation, and occupation number distributions were extracted from equal-time correlation functions of the gauge fields and electric fields. The scaling exponent was determined by fitting a power-law dependence to the occupation number distribution over a range of momenta, with error bars estimated by varying the fitting region.

## Key numbers

- The occupation number distribution exhibits a power-law behavior ~ |p|^{-3/2} for momenta |p| ≲ 2 Q_s at intermediate times, with the exponent reaching 3/2 after a quick initial evolution.
- The quasi-stationary evolution around exponent 3/2 persists for a time duration of hundreds in units of Q_s (e.g., t Q_s = 315 and t Q_s = 730 shown).
- Simulations were performed on spatial lattices with N³ = 128³ to 256³ sites.
- The dispersion deviates from the free massless dispersion ω_p = |p| only for momenta below p² ≲ 0.1 Q_s², with decreasing amplitude as time grows.
- The scaling analysis yields κ = 3/2 from the condition Δ = -1, derived from the stationarity condition with a time-dependent background field.

## Context

This work extends the study of nonequilibrium dynamics from over-populated initial conditions, previously demonstrated for scalar quantum fields by Berges (2012) where Bose condensation and dual cascades were observed. The authors compare their results to scalar theories where an effective cubic interaction arises from a time-dependent Bose condensate (Micha and Tkachev, 2004; Berges, 2008). The finding that Yang-Mills theory exhibits the same turbulence exponent as scalars is non-trivial because gauge theories have elastic and inelastic processes of parametrically the same order, unlike scalar theories where inelastic processes are higher order. This work enables further investigation of whether a transient condensate develops in gauge theories during heavy-ion collisions, and motivates studies including longitudinal expansion for sufficiently high Q_s.
