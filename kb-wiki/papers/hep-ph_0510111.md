# Soliton Picture for Pentaquarks

> In this talk I report on a thorough comparison between the bound state and rigid rotator approaches to generate baryon states with non--zero strangeness in chiral soliton models. This comparison shows that the scattering amplitude in the bound state approach contains contributions generated by the exchange of the rigid pentaquark excitation, and that the two approaches are consistent with each other in the large $N_C$ limit. The comparison paves the way to unambiguously compute the width of the $Θ^+$ pentaquark in chiral soliton models.

## Key claim

The bound state and rigid rotator approaches to chiral soliton models are consistent in the large \(N_C\) limit and both predict pentaquark states, but the rigid rotator approach must be generalized to a rotation-vibration approach with constrained fluctuations to unambiguously extract the resonance contribution and compute the \(\Theta^+\) width.

## Method

The author compared the bound state approach (BSA), which treats kaon modes as small amplitude fluctuations in harmonic approximation, with the rigid rotator approach (RRA), which treats kaon modes as collective excitations of the classical soliton to all orders. To enable a proper comparison, the RRA was generalized to a rotation-vibration approach (RVA) that imposes constraints to exclude zero-modes from the fluctuations, and the resulting Yukawa couplings between collective excitations and fluctuations were derived. The phase shifts and mass spectra from the BSA and RVA were computed in the Skyrme model with Wess-Zumino and flavor symmetry breaking terms, and the \(R\)-matrix formalism was applied to extract the resonance phase shift and decay width.

## Key numbers

- The mass difference between the \(\Theta^+\) pentaquark and the nucleon in the flavor symmetric case increases from \(\omega_0\) to \((N_C+3)/4\Theta_K\) for \(N_C=3\) when subleading effects are included.
- Assuming the observed resonance is the \(\Theta^+(1540)\), a width of roughly \(40\ \mathrm{MeV}\) is read off from the model prediction for \(N_C=3\).
- The \(\Delta\)-nucleon mass difference is \({\cal O}(1/N_C)\) even with flavor symmetry breaking included, in contrast to claims in Pr03.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing comparison between the bound state approach (Weigel, 1998) and the rigid rotator approach (Yabu, 1988) for generating baryon states with strangeness in chiral soliton models. It directly contradicts the argument of It04 that pentaquarks are an artifact of the rigid rotator approach, showing instead that both approaches yield identical results in the large \(N_C\) limit. The study enables the unambiguous computation of the \(\Theta^+\) width, correcting earlier approaches (Di97, El04) that incorrectly assumed \(SU(3)\) relations among hadronic decays and fitted coefficients from multiple decay channels.
