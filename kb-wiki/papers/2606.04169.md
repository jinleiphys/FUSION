# TMD factorization in diffractive heavy-quark production in photon-nucleus collisions

> Using the Colour Glass Condensate effective theory, we study the diffractive production of a massive quark-antiquark pair accompanied by a gluon in coherent photon-nucleus collisions at high energy. This partonic configuration provides the leading twist contribution to the cross section in the correlation limit where two of the partons are hard and nearly back to back in the transverse plane, while the third one is semi-hard, with a transverse momentum of the order of the nuclear saturation momentum. We consider two scenarios: (i) a hard quark-antiquark pair together with a semi-hard gluon; in this case we demonstrate transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization with a mass-dependent ''hard'' factor and the standard expression for the gluon diffractive TMD, and (ii) a hard antiquark-gluon pair and a semi-hard quark; in this case we find TMD factorization with a mass-independent ''hard'' factor and a mass-dependent quark diffractive TMD, which represents a new result. We show that increasing the quark mass reduces (or even washes out) the effects of gluon saturation on the quark diffractive TMD. In particular, it leads to the suppression of the Cronin peak that we observe in the massless limit. Our results are the basis for future phenomenological studies of quarkonium and open charm production in the saturation regime in ultraperipheral collisions at the Large Hadron Collider, and in deep inelastic scattering at the Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization holds for diffractive heavy-quark production in photon-nucleus collisions, but only in the correlation limit where two partons are hard and nearly back-to-back while the third is semi-hard with transverse momentum of order the saturation scale; the quark mass reduces or washes out gluon saturation effects on the quark diffractive TMD, suppressing the Cronin peak seen in the massless limit.

## Method

The authors use the Colour Glass Condensate (CGC) effective theory and Light Cone Perturbation Theory (LCPT) to compute the light cone wavefunction for a virtual photon splitting into a massive quark-antiquark-gluon system, then calculate the scattering matrix element including Wilson lines representing interaction with the nuclear target. They perform a power expansion in the ratio of the semi-hard scale (the saturation momentum Q_s) to the hard scale M_h, retaining only the leading-twist contribution, and change longitudinal variables from the projectile frame to the target frame to extract the diffractive TMD distributions.

## Key numbers

* The hard scale is defined as M_h^2 = Q^2 + M_{q\bar q}^2, where M_{q\bar q}^2 is approximately (P_perp^2 + m^2)/(z_1 z_2); the condition for hardness is M_h^2 >> Q_s^2(x_P, A)
* The semi-hard gluon carries longitudinal momentum fraction z_3 of order z_3 ~ Q_s^2 / M_h^2 << 1 and transverse momentum k_{3_perp} ~ Q_s
* The transverse momentum imbalance K_perp of the hard q\bar q pair satisfies K_perp ~ k_{3_perp} ~ Q_s, while the relative transverse momentum P_perp satisfies P_perp >> Q_s in the correlation limit
* The diffractive mass M_{qqg}^2 of the three-parton final state is given by M_{qqg}^2 = M_{qq}^2 + K_perp^2 + k_{3_perp}^2/z_3
* The beta variable is beta = Q^2 / (Q^2 + M_{qqg}^2), and Bjorken-x satisfies x_Bj = x_P * beta

## Context

This work extends previous CGC-based TMD factorization results for massless partons in diffractive dijet production (Iancu 2021, Iancu 2022, Hauksson 2024) to the case of massive quarks, which can hadronize into quarkonium or open charm. The results provide the theoretical foundation for future phenomenological studies of quarkonium and open charm production in the saturation regime at the LHC (ultraperipheral collisions) and at the Electron-Ion Collider, complementing earlier work on inclusive heavy flavor photoproduction (Gimeno-Estivill 2025) and collinear factorization approaches (Cacciari 2025).
