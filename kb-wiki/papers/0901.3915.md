# The three dimensional calculations of NN bound and scattering states with chiral potential up to N3LO

> The recently developed chiral nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) potential by Epelbaum \emph{et al.} has been employed to study the two-nucleon bound and scattering states. Chiral $NN$ potential up to next-to-next-to-next-to leading order (N$^3$LO) is used to calculated the np differential cross section and deuteron binding energy in a realistic three dimensional approach. The obtained results based on this helicity representation are compared to the standard partial wave (PW) results. This comparison shows that the 3D approach provides the same accuracy in the description of $NN$ observables and the results are in close agreement with available experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a three-dimensional (3D) helicity representation of the chiral nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential up to N3LO yields np differential cross sections and deuteron binding energies with accuracy equivalent to the standard partial wave (PW) approach, while avoiding the energy-dependent truncation of angular momentum channels inherent in PW methods.

## Method

The chiral NN potential of Epelbaum et al. was rewritten in an operator form compatible with a 3D helicity basis, using six spin operators (Ω_i) and scalar functions of the initial and final relative momenta magnitudes and the angle between them. The low-energy coefficients (LECs) for contact terms, originally fitted to Nijmegen phase shifts in 14 PW channels up to J=3, were converted to 3D matrix elements by summing over those channels. The resulting coupled integral equations in two variables (momentum magnitude p and angle θ) were solved for np scattering and the deuteron bound state using five different cutoff combinations (Λ₁, Λ₂) in MeV/c: (450,500), (600,600), (550,600), (450,700), and (600,700).

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding energy for cutoff (450,500): -2.216 MeV in 3D, compared to -2.215 MeV in PW (Young) and the experimental value -2.224575(9) MeV.
- Deuteron binding energy for cutoff (450,700): -2.219 MeV in 3D, compared to -2.218 MeV in PW (Young).
- Deuteron binding energy for cutoff (600,700): -2.222 MeV in 3D, compared to -2.220 MeV in PW (Young).
- The PW results of Epelbaum et al. for deuteron binding energy range from -2.216 to -2.223 MeV across cutoffs.
- np differential cross sections were calculated at laboratory energies 50, 96, 143, and 200 MeV; 3D and PW results agree closely at all energies and match experimental data, particularly at backward angles.

## Context

This work extends the 3D approach previously applied by Fachruddin et al. (2000, 2001) to realistic potentials (Bonn-B and AV18) by incorporating the chiral NN potential of Epelbaum et al. (2005), which is rooted in chiral perturbation theory and QCD symmetries. The 3D method avoids the energy-dependent truncation of partial waves needed in standard PW calculations, making it more efficient for higher-energy few-body problems; the authors note that the number of coupled integral equations in 3D is fixed and does not grow with energy. This enables future calculations of 3N bound states (³H, ³He), Nd capture, and 3N photodisintegration using chiral potentials, and the eventual inclusion of three-nucleon chiral forces.
