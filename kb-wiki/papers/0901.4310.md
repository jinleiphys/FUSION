# Valence quark contribution for the gamma N -> Delta quadrupole transition extracted from lattice QCD

> Starting with a spectator quark model developed for the nucleon (N) and the Delta in the physical pion mass region, we extend the predictions of the reaction gamma N -> Delta to the lattice QCD regime. The quark model includes S and D waves in the quark-diquark wavefunctions. Within this framework it is the D-wave part in the Delta wavefunction that generates nonzero valence contributions for the quadrupole form factors of the transition. Those contributions are however insufficient to explain the physical data, since the pion cloud contributions dominate. To separate the two effects we apply the model to the lattice regime in a region where the pion cloud effects are negligible, and adjust the D-state parameters directly to the lattice data. This process allows us to obtain a better determination of the D-state contributions. Finally, by adding a simple parametrization of the pion cloud we establish the connection between the experimental data and the lattice data.

# Valence quark contribution for the gamma N -> Delta quadrupole transition extracted from lattice QCD

## Key claim

The valence quark D-state contributions to the quadrupole form factors of the gamma N -> Delta transition, while necessary, are insufficient to explain physical data because pion cloud contributions dominate; however, by fitting the D-state parameters directly to quenched lattice QCD data at pion masses above 400 MeV (where pion cloud effects are negligible), the valence contribution can be cleanly separated and then combined with a pion cloud parametrization to describe experimental data.

## Method

The authors used a covariant spectator quark model with S and D waves in the quark-diquark wavefunctions for the nucleon and Delta, extending it to the lattice QCD regime by introducing the dependence of hadronic masses (nucleon, Delta, rho) on the lattice pion mass. They fit five D-state parameters (three range parameters and two admixture coefficients for the D3 and D1 states) to quenched lattice QCD data from Alexandrou (2008) at three pion masses (563, 490, and 411 MeV), then extrapolated the resulting valence contribution to the physical point and added a pion cloud contribution derived from large-Nc relations connecting the quadrupole form factors to the neutron electric form factor.

## Key numbers

- The fit to quenched lattice data yielded D-state range parameters alpha_i = 0.338-0.351, with the S-state range parameter fixed at alpha_1 = 0.3366 from prior work
- The best fit corresponds to an admixture of 0.72% for both D3 and D1 states in the Delta wavefunction, compared to 4.36% for the D1 component from the physical data fit in prior work
- The total chi-squared for the quenched fit was 0.842 across all three pion masses (563, 490, and 411 MeV)
- The diquark mass used was m_s = 0.87 m_N = 817 MeV
- The physical pion mass is m_pi = 138 MeV, while lattice data were taken at m_pi = 563, 490, and 411 MeV

## Context

This work extends the covariant spectator quark model previously developed for the nucleon (Ramalho and Pena, 2008) and the gamma N -> Delta transition (Ramalho and Pena, 2008) from the physical pion mass region to the lattice QCD regime. It addresses the long-standing problem that pure valence quark models cannot explain the quadrupole form factor data at the physical point, where pion cloud effects dominate (Pascalutsa, 2006; Drechsel, 2007). The approach enables a cleaner determination of D-state contributions than was possible in prior work (Ramalho and Pena, 2008) where valence and pion cloud effects were entangled, and establishes a connection between lattice QCD simulations and experimental data that can be refined with more precise lattice data and improved pion cloud estimates.
