# Hyperfine Populations Prior to Muon Capture

> It is shown that the 1S level hyperfine populations prior to muon capture will be statistical when either target or beam are unpolarised independent of the atomic level at which the hyperfine interaction becomes appreciable. This assertion holds in the absence of magnetic transitions during the cascade and is true because of minimal polarisation after atomic capture and selective feeding during the cascade.

# Hyperfine Populations Prior to Muon Capture

## Key claim

The 1S level hyperfine populations prior to muon capture will be statistical (i.e., proportional to 2f+1) when either the target nucleus or the muon beam is unpolarised, regardless of the atomic level at which the hyperfine interaction becomes appreciable. This assertion holds only in the absence of magnetic transitions during the atomic cascade.

## Method

The author describes the population of atomic states using statistical tensors B_k(j), following the formalism of Nagamine and Yamazaki (1974) and Kuno, Nagamine and Yamazaki (1987). Starting from the assumption of isotropic atomic capture with minimal polarisation, the author derives the evolution of statistical tensors through electric multipole transitions during the electromagnetic cascade, showing that only rank-0 and rank-1 tensors survive due to selective feeding (Δk=0 selection rule). The hyperfine populations at the 1S level are then parameterised as B_0(1,0,1/2,f) = γ + δ P_μ P_i, where P_μ is the muon polarisation and P_i is the nuclear vector polarisation.

## Key numbers

- A deviation of 2.5% from statisticity in the hyperfine populations would change the measured capture rate by 1% for the proposed ³He measurement.
- The proposed experiment at PSI aims to measure the statistical capture rate by ³He to a precision of 1%.
- The parameter γ for the 1S hyperfine populations is given by γ = (2f+1) / [2(2i+1)], where i is the nuclear spin and f is the hyperfine state.

## Context

This work extends the earlier result of Mukhopadhyay (1977), who showed that hyperfine populations are statistical when the hyperfine interaction acts only at the 1S state. The question arose because measurements of residual muon polarisations in light nuclei by Favart et al. (1970) showed that the hyperfine interaction must be active at atomic states higher than n=1. The result is directly relevant to the experimental proposal by Deutsch to measure the muon capture rate by ³He at PSI, as it establishes that the hyperfine populations are indeed statistical under the stated conditions, enabling precise interpretation of the capture rate measurement.
