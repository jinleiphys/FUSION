# Single Cooper pair transfer in stable and in exotic nuclei

> We discuss in this paper both the past and the present of two-nucleon transfer reactions, as well as try to foresee the near future of such studies in nuclei, induced by light as well as by heavy ion reactions.

# Single Cooper pair transfer in stable and in exotic nuclei

## Key claim
The paper argues that the exchange of collective vibrations (phonons) between the two halo neutrons in $^{11}$Li provides the essential pairing mechanism that binds the neutron Cooper pair to the $^9$Li core, and that this phonon-mediated pairing can be directly observed through two-neutron transfer reactions, specifically the $^1$H($^{11}$Li,$^9$Li)$^3$H reaction. The claim is conditional on the fact that the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction (Argonne potential) alone is insufficient to bind the two halo neutrons, requiring the induced interaction from phonon exchange to produce a bound state.

## Method
The authors performed Nuclear Field Theory (NFT) calculations combining a mean-field description with random phase approximation (RPA) vibrational states to compute the single-particle spectrum of $^{10}$Li and the two-neutron ground state of $^{11}$Li. They then calculated differential cross sections for the $^{112}$Sn($p,t$)$^{110}$Sn reaction at 26 MeV and the $^1$H($^{11}$Li,$^9$Li)$^3$H reaction, including successive, simultaneous, non-orthogonality, breakup, and inelastic contributions using second-order perturbation theory within the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA) framework.

## Context
This work extends the study of two-nucleon transfer reactions from stable nuclei to exotic halo nuclei, specifically addressing the role of pairing correlations in $^{11}$Li. The authors build on prior shell model calculations for $^{110}$Sn by Guazzoni (2006) using a $v_{low-k}$ potential derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential (Machleidt 2001). For the halo nucleus $^{11}$Li, the work contrasts with three-body cluster model calculations (Esbensen 1997) that treat the $^9$Li core as inert, by including dynamical renormalization effects from coupling single-particle motion to quadrupole vibrations of the core. The results enable direct experimental tests of phonon-mediated pairing through two-neutron transfer reactions, as demonstrated by the TRIUMF experiment (Tanihata 2008), and suggest that similar studies at higher incident energies could further challenge the theoretical framework.
