# Neutrino effects in two-body electron-capture measurements at GSI

> Oscillatory behavior of electron capture rates in the two-body decay of hydrogen-like ions into recoil ions plus undetected neutrinos, with a period of approximately 7 s, was reported in storage ring single-ion experiments at the GSI Laboratory, Darmstadt. Ivanov and Kienle [PRL 103, 062502 (2009)] have relegated this period to neutrino masses through neutrino mixing in the final state. New arguments are given here against this interpretation, while suggesting that these `GSI Oscillations' may be related to neutrino spin precession in the static magnetic field of the storage ring. This scenario requires a Dirac neutrino magnetic moment six times smaller than the Borexino solar neutrino upper limit 0.54 x 10E(-10) of the Bohr magneton [PRL 101, 091302 (2008)], and its consequences are explored.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the oscillatory behavior of electron-capture decay rates observed at GSI (the "GSI Oscillations") cannot be explained by neutrino mass mixing, because when no neutrino is detected the total decay rate sums incoherently over neutrino mass eigenstates and any interference terms cancel. Instead, the author proposes that the oscillations may arise from interference between decay amplitudes with and without neutrino spin precession in the static magnetic field of the storage ring, requiring a Dirac neutrino magnetic moment six times smaller than the Borexino upper limit.

## Method

The author first demonstrates analytically, using time-dependent perturbation theory and the unitarity of the neutrino mixing matrix, that summing over all undetected neutrino flavors eliminates interference terms between mass-eigenstate amplitudes, yielding a time-independent decay rate. The author then constructs a schematic model where the storage ring's static magnetic field (covering approximately 35% of the circumference) induces spin precession of emitted Dirac neutrinos, creating interference between decay amplitudes from regions with and without magnetic field. The resulting time-modulated rate is derived and compared with the GSI experimental parameters.

## Key numbers

- The GSI experiment reported oscillatory modulation with angular frequency ω_EC^lab ≈ 0.89 s^(-1), corresponding to period T_EC^lab ≈ 7.1 s in the laboratory system, and amplitude a_EC ≈ 0.2
- The rest-frame energy equivalent is ħω_EC ≈ 0.84 × 10^(-15) eV
- For the neutrino mass-mixing interpretation, the correct energy scale is ħΩ_νe = Δ(m_ν c^2)^2 / (2E_ν) ≈ 0.95 × 10^(-11) eV, using Δ(m_ν c^2)^2 = (0.76 ± 0.02) × 10^(-4) eV^2 and E_ν ≈ 4 MeV
- This yields an oscillation period T ~ 4.4 × 10^(-4) s, over four orders of magnitude shorter than the reported 7 s period
- The static magnetic field in the ESR is B = 1.19 T for ^140Pr
- The required Dirac neutrino magnetic moment to match the GSI oscillations is μ_ν ~ 0.9 × 10^(-11) μ_B, six times smaller than the Borexino upper limit of 0.54 × 10^(-10) μ_B
- The magnetic field covers approximately 35% of the storage ring circumference
- The experimental time resolution is approximately 0.5 s

## Context

This work extends the debate over the "GSI Oscillations" reported by Litvinov et al. (2008), where hydrogen-like ^140Pr and ^142Pm ions in the ESR storage ring showed time-modulated electron-capture decay rates. The paper directly contradicts the neutrino mass-mixing interpretation proposed by Ivanov and Kienle (2009) and earlier works by Kienle and collaborators, showing that their energy scale calculation was incorrect by over four orders of magnitude. The author instead proposes a magnetic spin-precession mechanism that could be tested by varying the magnetic field B or the ion velocity β. The work also notes that preliminary data on three-body β^+ decay of ^142Pm show no time modulation, with amplitude a_β+ < 0.03(3), consistent with the proposed mechanism being unique to two-body decays.
