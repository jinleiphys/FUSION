# Non-boost-invariant motion of dissipative and highly anisotropic fluid

> The recently formulated framework of anisotropic and dissipative hydrodynamics (ADHYDRO) is used to describe non-boost-invariant motion of the fluid created at the early stages of heavy-ion collisions. Very strong initial asymmetries of pressure are reduced by the entropy production processes. By the appropriate choice of the form of the entropy source we can reproduce realistic scenarios for the isotropization expected in heavy-ion collisions. Our previous results are generalized by including the realistic equation of state as the limit of the isotropization processes.

## Key claim

The ADHYDRO framework can describe non-boost-invariant longitudinal expansion of highly anisotropic fluid created in heavy-ion collisions, with the isotropization time controlled by the entropy source term; by choosing the entropy source appropriately, isotropization times of about 1 fm can be achieved, consistent with the common expectation that perfect-fluid hydrodynamics may be applied at such times.

## Method

The authors solve the energy-momentum conservation and entropy growth equations for a fluid with an energy-momentum tensor that allows different transverse and longitudinal pressures, parameterized by an anisotropy parameter x. They consider one-dimensional non-boost-invariant motion along the beam axis, using a parameterization of the fluid four-velocity and the longitudinal direction four-vector in terms of proper time τ and space-time rapidity η. The entropy source Σ is taken as a function of x and a timescale parameter τ_eq, and the equations are solved numerically with initial conditions at τ_0 = 0.2 fm, including a Gaussian-like entropy profile in rapidity and an initial anisotropy x_0 = 100.

## Key numbers

- Initial proper time τ_0 = 0.2 fm
- Initial energy density ε_0 = 100 GeV/fm³
- Initial anisotropy parameter x_0 = 100
- Timescale parameter τ_eq = 0.25 fm
- For τ > 2 fm the system is practically isotropic
- Isotropization at about 1 fm requires τ_eq ≤ 0.1 fm
- Entropy increases by about 70% due to dissipative effects in the early stage
- Parameters for the initial entropy profile: Δη = 1, δη = 1.3
- Degeneracy factor g_0 = 16 (gluons)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous boost-invariant ADHYDRO framework (Florkowski and Ryblewski, 2010) to non-boost-invariant motion, generalizing results by including a realistic QCD equation of state as the isotropization limit. It contrasts with studies by Satarov et al. (2006) and Bozek (2007) that focused on equation-of-state and shear viscosity effects, respectively, by concentrating on highly asymmetric early stages. The approach enables a unified description of multiple stages of heavy-ion collisions: an early anisotropic phase (Glasma, color-flux tubes), transition to isotropic phase with QCD equation of state, perfect-fluid stage (sQGP), and phase transition to hadron gas.
