# Towards a unified treatment of $ΔS=0$ parity violation in low-energy nuclear processes

> We revisit the unified treatment of low-energy hadronic parity violation espoused by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein to the end of an ab initio treatment of parity violation in low-energy nuclear processes within the Standard Model. We use our improved effective Hamiltonian and precise non-perturbative assessments of the quark charges of the nucleon within lattice QCD to make new assessments of the parity-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants. Comparing with recent, precise measurements of hadronic parity violation in few-body nuclear reactions, we find improved agreement with these experimental results, though some tensions remain. We thus note the broader problem of comparing low-energy constants from nuclear and few-nucleon systems, considering, too, unresolved theoretical issues in connecting an ab initio, effective Hamiltonian approach to chiral effective theories. We note how future experiments and lattice QCD studies could sharpen the emerging picture, promoting the study of hadronic parity violation as a laboratory for testing ``end-to-end'' theoretical descriptions of weak processes in hadrons and nuclei at low energies.

## Key claim

The authors revisit the unified treatment of low-energy hadronic parity violation by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (DDH) using an improved effective Hamiltonian with complete renormalization group evolution in leading-order QCD and precise lattice QCD assessments of nucleon quark charges, finding improved but not perfect agreement with recent experimental results from the NPDGamma and n3He collaborations, with some tensions remaining.

## Method

The authors compute the parity-violating meson-nucleon coupling constants at a renormalization scale of 2 GeV by matching quark-level matrix elements of their effective Hamiltonian to hadron-level matrix elements of the DDH phenomenological Hamiltonian, employing the factorization approximation and using lattice QCD results for the quark flavor charges of the nucleon. They evolve the Wilson coefficients from the W mass scale to 2 GeV using leading-order QCD renormalization group equations with matching across heavy-flavor thresholds, treating all three isosectors (I=0, I=1, I=2) in a unified manner.

- The ratio \( m_\pi^2/(m_u + m_d) = 1941 (32)\,\rm MeV \) using renormalization-group-invariant quark masses from \( N_f=2+1 \) lattice QCD simulations

- The axial charges of the nucleon from \( N_f=2+1+1 \) lattice QCD simulations are \( g_A^u = 0.777 (25)(30) \), \( g_A^d = -0.438 (18) (30) \), and \( g_A^s = -0.053 (8) \)

## Context

This work extends the original unified treatment of hadronic parity violation by Desplanques, Donoghue, and Holstein (1979) by incorporating a complete leading-order QCD renormalization group evolution of the effective Hamiltonian and modern lattice QCD results for nucleon quark charges, replacing the poorly controlled flavor-symmetry-based estimates used previously. The results are compared with precise experimental measurements from the NPDGamma collaboration (2018) measuring the parity-violating asymmetry in \( \vec n + p \to d + \gamma \) and the n3He collaboration (2020) measuring \( \vec n + {}^3{\rm He} \to t + p \), finding improved agreement though some tensions remain. The work enables future comparisons between ab initio effective Hamiltonian approaches and chiral effective field theory treatments, and the authors note that future experiments and lattice QCD studies could sharpen the emerging picture of hadronic parity violation as a test of end-to-end theoretical descriptions of weak processes in hadrons and nuclei.
