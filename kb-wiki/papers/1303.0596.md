# Limits on Lepton Flavor Violation from μ-e-conversion

> We revisit the status of Lepton Flavor Violating (LFV) μe qq contact interactions from the view point of μ- e -conversion in nuclei. We consider their contribution to this LFV process via the two mechanisms on the hadronic level: direct nucleon and meson exchange ones. In the former case the quarks are embedded directly into the nucleons while in the latter in mesons which then interact with nucleons in a nucleus. We revise and in some cases reevaluate the hadronic parameters relevant for both mechanisms and calculate the contribution of the above mentioned contact interactions in coherent μ- e -conversion in various nuclei. Then we update the existing upper bounds and also derive new ones for the scales of the μe qq contact interactions from the experimental limits on the capture rates of μ- e -conversion. We compare these limits with the ones derived in the literature from other LFV processes and comment on the prospects of LHC searches related to the contact μe qq interactions.

## Key claim

The paper derives updated and new upper limits on the scales of lepton flavor violating (LFV) $\mu e qq$ contact interactions from experimental bounds on $\mu-e$ conversion in nuclei, but these limits depend on the assumed hadronization mechanism (direct nucleon mechanism or meson exchange mechanism) and on the specific quark flavor, with the strongest limits reaching $\Lambda_{\mu e} \sim 10^4$ TeV for isoscalar vector couplings.

## Method

The authors consider two hadronization mechanisms for converting quark-level LFV contact interactions into nucleon-level operators: direct nucleon mechanism (DNM), where quark currents are embedded directly into nucleon currents, and meson exchange mechanism (MEM), where quark currents first couple to vector and scalar mesons which then interact with nucleons. They evaluate nucleon form factors for light and heavy quarks, including newly calculated scalar form factors for charm and top quarks, and compute the contribution of all possible $\mu e qq$ contact terms to coherent $\mu-e$ conversion in various nuclei. Using experimental limits from SINDRUM II on $\mu-e$ conversion rates in $^{48}$Ti, $^{197}$Au, and $^{208}$Pb, they extract upper bounds on the dimensionless LFV parameters $\eta$ and corresponding mass scales $\Lambda_{\mu e}$ for each quark flavor and operator type.

## Key numbers

- Upper limit on $\mu-e$ conversion rate in $^{197}$Au: $R_{\mu e}^{Au} \leq 4.3 \times 10^{-12}$ (SINDRUM II, Bertl 2006)
- Upper limit on $\mu-e$ conversion rate in $^{48}$Ti: $R_{\mu e}^{Ti} \leq 7.0 \times 10^{-13}$ (SINDRUM II)
- Upper limit on $\mu-e$ conversion rate in $^{208}$Pb: $R_{\mu e}^{Pb} \leq 4.6 \times 10^{-11}$ (SINDRUM II, Honecker 1996)
- For isoscalar vector couplings $\eta^{(0)}_{hV}$: DNM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(0) hV} \geq 4.7 \times 10^{3}$ TeV, MEM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(0) hV} \geq 7.2 \times 10^{3}$ TeV
- For isovector vector couplings $\eta^{(3)}_{hV}$: DNM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(3) hV} \geq 10^{3}$ TeV, MEM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(3) hV} \geq 900$ TeV
- For strange quark vector couplings $\eta^{(s)}_{hV}$: MEM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(s) hV} \geq 770$ TeV (using $g_{\phi NN} = -0.24$) to $5.4 \times 10^{3}$ TeV (using $g_{\phi NN} = -18.3$)
- For charm quark vector couplings $\eta^{(c)}_{hV}$: MEM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(c) hV} \geq 54$ TeV
- For bottom quark vector couplings $\eta^{(b)}_{hV}$: MEM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(b) hV} \geq 3$ TeV
- For isoscalar scalar couplings $\eta^{(0)}_{rS}$: DNM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(0) rS} \geq 6.8 \times 10^{3}$ TeV (conservative form factors), MEM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(0) rS} \geq 5.8 \times 10^{3}$ TeV
- For top quark scalar couplings $\eta^{(t)}_{rS}$: DNM gives $\Lambda_{\mu e}^{(t) rS} \geq 90$ TeV
- Planned sensitivity of PRISM/PRIME experiment: $R_{\mu e}^{Ti} \sim 10^{-18}$ (Witte 2012)

## Context

This work extends previous analyses by Faessler et al. (2004, 2005) by including all possible contact terms contributing to $\mu-e$ conversion, evaluating nucleon form factors for heavy quark currents (charm, bottom, top), and incorporating contributions from heavy vector mesons $J/\Psi$ and $\Upsilon$ that are inaccessible in the direct nucleon mechanism. The derived limits complement those from other LFV processes studied in the literature and provide constraints on new physics scales that can be compared with LHC search prospects for contact $\mu e qq$ interactions. The results enable future experiments like Mu2e (planned sensitivity $7 \times 10^{-17}$), COMET ($10^{-16}$), and PRISM/PRIME ($10^{-18}$) to probe LFV mass scales up to $10^{4}$ TeV or higher.
