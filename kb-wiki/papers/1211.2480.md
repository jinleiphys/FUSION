# Channel coupling effect and important role of imaginary part of coupling potential for high-energy heavy-ion scatterings

> The recent works by the present authors and their collaborator predicted that the real part of heavy-ion optical potentials changes its character from attraction to repulsion around the incident energy per nucleon $E =$ 200 -- 300 MeV/u on the basis of the complex $G$-matrix interaction and the double-folding model (DFM) and revealed that the three-body force plays an important role there. In the present paper, we have analyzed the energy dependence of the coupling effect with the Microscopic Coupled Channel (MCC) method and its relation to the elastic and inelastic-scattering angular distributions in detail in the case of the $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C system in the energy range of $E =$ 100 -- 400 MeV/u. The large channel coupling effect is clearly seen in the elastic cross section although the incident energies are enough high. The dynamical polarization potential is derived to investigate the channel coupling effect. Moreover, we analyze the effect of imaginary part of the coupling potential on elastic and inelastic cross sections.

## Key claim

The channel coupling effect remains sizable in elastic scattering of the $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C system even at incident energies as high as 400 MeV/u, and the sign and strength of the resulting dynamical polarization potential (DPP) are governed by the relative sign and magnitude of the real and imaginary parts of the microscopic coupling potential, which change dramatically across the attractive-to-repulsive transition region around 200–300 MeV/u.

## Method

The authors performed microscopic coupled-channel (MCC) calculations for the $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C system at incident energies per nucleon of 100, 200, 300, and 400 MeV, using the complex $G$-matrix interaction CEG07b (which includes three-body force effects) in the double-folding model. The diagonal and coupling potentials were constructed by folding the complex effective nucleon-nucleon interaction with the nucleon densities of the colliding nuclei, including single and mutual excitations of $^{12}$C to the $2_{1}^{+}$, $0_{2}^{+}$, $3_{1}^{-}$, and $2_{2}^{+}$ states. The dynamical polarization potential (DPP) was extracted from the full-CC solutions to isolate the channel-coupling effect on the elastic channel.

## Key numbers

- Incident energies per nucleon studied: $E/A =$ 100, 200, 300, and 400 MeV for the $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C system.
- The real part of the diagonal folding potential changes sign (from attractive to repulsive) between $E/A =$ 200 and 300 MeV.
- The real part of the coupling potential between the elastic channel and the $2_{1}^{+}$ single-excitation channel also changes sign between $E/A =$ 200 and 300 MeV.
- The grazing angular momentum values used for DPP extraction: $J_{\rm gr} =$ 52 at 100 MeV/u, 74 at 200 MeV/u, 90 at 300 MeV/u, and 104 at 400 MeV/u.
- The grazing impact parameter was taken as $b_{\rm gr} \simeq 4$ fm for the $^{12}$C + $^{12}$C system.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Furumoto et al., 2009–2012) that predicted an attractive-to-repulsive transition in the real part of heavy-ion optical potentials around 200–300 MeV/u using the complex $G$-matrix interaction CEG07, and revealed the importance of the three-body force. It applies the same microscopic interaction to coupled-channel calculations for the first time at these energies, going beyond earlier MCC studies that used only real effective interactions (e.g., M3Y or DDM3Y) and required phenomenological imaginary parts. The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted transition (Guo et al., 2012) and provide a framework for analyzing channel-coupling effects in high-energy heavy-ion reactions without free parameters (except the imaginary renormalization factor $N_W$, set to unity here).
