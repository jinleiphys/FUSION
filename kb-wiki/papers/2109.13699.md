# Optical potential parameters of light nuclear fusion based on precise Coulomb wave functions

> Based on precise Coulomb wave functions (CWFs), we attempt to calculate the fusion cross sections of light nuclei in a complex spherical square-well nuclear potential (i.e., optical potential model). Comparing with experimental benchmark cross section data, we can calibrate optical potential parameters associated with D+D, D+T, D+3He, p+D, p+6Li and p+7Li fusion reactions. Surprisingly, we find that our calculated optical potential parameters are quite different from those of many previous results (e.g., Phys. Rev. C. 61 (2000) 024610, Nucl. Phys. A 986 (2019) 98, etc.), in which approximate Coulomb wave functions (ACWFs) with only retaining the leading terms are exploited for the continuity conditions at the radius of nuclear potential. Furthermore, with the obtained optical potential parameters, we compare the fusion cross sections and astrophysical S-factors with that formulated from ACWFs approach, and also find apparent deviations especially for the fusion reactions with resonance peaks such as D+T and D+3He fusion reactions. We then calculate the phase diagrams of the fusion cross sections with respect to the optical potential parameters and demonstrate several narrow shape resonance belts. It implies that a small deviation of ACWFs from the exact CWFs at nuclear radius might lead to fall off the resonance regimes and therefore causes the big difference on the optical parameters as well as the cross sections.

## Key claim

Using precise Coulomb wave functions (CWFs) rather than the approximate Coulomb wave functions (ACWFs) that retain only leading terms, the authors find that calibrated optical potential parameters for light nuclear fusion reactions differ substantially from previous results; a small deviation of ACWFs from exact CWFs at the nuclear radius can cause the fitted parameters to fall off narrow shape resonance belts, leading to large differences in both the optical parameters and the calculated cross sections.

## Method

The authors model light nuclear fusion using a complex spherical square-well nuclear potential (optical model) with a long-range Coulomb repulsive potential. They solve the Schrodinger equation for S-wave scattering, using exact regular and irregular Coulomb wave functions (CWFs) to impose continuity conditions at the nuclear radius \(R_N\). The three optical potential parameters (real part \(V_r\), imaginary part \(V_i\), and radius parameter \(r_0\)) are calibrated by fitting the calculated fusion cross sections to experimental benchmark data for six reactions (D+D, D+T, D+\(^3\)He, p+D, p+\(^6\)Li, p+\(^7\)Li) using a nonlinear least-square method.

## Key numbers

* For D+D fusion: \(V_r = -60.00\) MeV, \(V_i = -186.1\) keV, \(r_0 = 1.074\) fm (relative errors vs. ACWFs: -19.13%, 41.48%, -158.9% respectively).
* For D+T fusion: \(V_r = -30.00\) MeV, \(V_i = -49.64\) keV, \(r_0 = 1.338\) fm (relative errors vs. ACWFs: 35.63%, 120.0%, 41.03%).
* For D+\(^3\)He fusion: \(V_r = -16.74\) MeV, \(V_i = -43.34\) keV, \(r_0 = 2.935\) fm (relative errors vs. ACWFs: -29.15%, 497.6%, 13.49%).
* For p+D fusion: \(V_r = -55.0\) MeV, \(V_i = -0.00326\) keV, \(r_0 = 1.420\) fm (relative error for \(V_i\): -621%).
* For p+\(^6\)Li fusion: \(V_r = -17.05\) MeV, \(V_i = -2661\) keV, \(r_0 = 1.433\) fm (relative errors: 147.8%, 181.8%, -17.66%).
* For p+\(^7\)Li fusion: \(V_r = -90.0\) MeV, \(V_i = -300.0\) keV, \(r_0 = 1.270\) fm (relative errors: -5.56%, 31.67%, 4.724%).
* For p+D at \(E_{\rm lab}=10^3\) keV: cross section \(\sigma = 3.2 \times 10^{-7}\) barn with CWFs vs. \(1.9 \times 10^{-6}\) barn with ACWFs.

## Context

This work extends the optical model approach of Li et al. (2000) and subsequent studies (e.g., Singh 2019) that used approximate Coulomb wave functions (ACWFs) retaining only leading terms for the continuity conditions at the nuclear radius. The authors show that using exact CWFs yields significantly different optical parameters, especially for reactions with resonance peaks (D+T, D+\(^3\)He), and that ACWFs become invalid at the nuclear radius for these systems. The phase diagrams of fusion cross sections versus optical parameters reveal narrow shape resonance belts, explaining why small wave function inaccuracies cause large parameter shifts. This enables more reliable extraction of optical potential parameters and suggests that future work should extend the analysis to higher partial waves (\(l>0\)), more realistic Woods-Saxon potentials, and spin-dependent statistical factors.
