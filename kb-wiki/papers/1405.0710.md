# Centrality dependence of hadronization and chemical freeze-out conditions in heavy ion collisions at \sqrt s_{NN} = 2.76 TeV

> We present an analysis of hadronic multiplicities measured in Pb-Pb collisions at \sqrt s_{NN} = 2.76 TeV as a function of the collision centrality within the statistical hadronization model. Evidence is found of a dependence of the chemical freeze-out temperature as a function of centrality, with a slow rise from central to peripheral collisions, which we interpret as an effect of post-hadronization inelastic scatterings. Using correction factors calculated by means of a simulation based on the UrQMD model, we are able to obtain a significant improvement in the statitical model fit quality and to reconstruct the primordial chemical equilibrium configuration. This is characterized by a nearly constant temperature of about 164 MeV which we interpret as the actual hadronization temperature.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out temperature extracted from standard statistical hadronization model (SHM) fits to ALICE Pb-Pb data at √sNN = 2.76 TeV shows a mild rise from central to peripheral collisions, but after correcting for post-hadronization inelastic scatterings using UrQMD transport simulations, the primordial hadronization temperature is reconstructed as nearly constant at about 164 MeV, interpreted as the actual QCD hadronization temperature.

## Method

The authors analyzed midrapidity hadronic multiplicities measured by ALICE in Pb-Pb collisions at √sNN = 2.76 TeV across nine centrality bins, fitting them first with the standard SHM (with temperature T, baryochemical potential μB=0, and optionally γS as free parameters). They then calculated modification factors Fi = NiFO/NiCE for each hadron species using the hybrid UrQMD model, which simulates hadronic afterburning following a hydrodynamical stage with Cooper-Frye freeze-out; these factors quantify the deviation from primordial chemical equilibrium due to inelastic rescattering. The measured yields were corrected by these factors and refitted to reconstruct the primordial chemical equilibrium configuration.

## Key numbers

- The primordial hadronization temperature reconstructed after afterburning corrections is 164 ± 3 MeV (quoted in abstract and text as 164±3 MeV).
- For the most central (0-5%) collisions, the plain SHM fit with γS=1 gives T = 157.2 ± 4.1 MeV with χ²/dof = 18.52/8; after including UrQMD modification factors, T = 167.3 ± 3.8 MeV with χ²/dof = 10.95/8.
- For the most peripheral (60-70%) collisions, the plain SHM fit with γS=1 gives T = 153.3 ± 5.2 MeV with χ²/dof = 14.36/8; after afterburning corrections, T = 157.8 ± 5.2 MeV with χ²/dof = 12.39/8.
- The UrQMD Cooper-Frye stage yields an average temperature of 158.2 ± 2.2 MeV when fitted with the SHM (μB=0 fixed, χ²/dof = 2.52/8).
- The measured antiproton-to-pion ratio in central Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV is 0.045 ± 0.005; the corresponding pp ratio at 7 TeV is not given for comparison at the same energy.
- The measured Ξ+/π- ratio in central Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV is (4.7 ± 0.5)×10⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of central collisions at various energies (Becattini et al., 2013) to study centrality dependence at fixed LHC energy, testing the hypothesis that afterburning effects diminish toward peripheral collisions due to reduced multiplicity. It addresses the "proton anomaly" observed by ALICE (Milano, 2012) and earlier by NA49 at SPS, where measured p/π and pbar yields fall below SHM predictions. The results confirm expectations from hydrodynamical investigations (Heinz and Kestin, 2008) that chemical freeze-out temperature should rise from central to peripheral collisions if afterburning is active, and enable extraction of a universal hadronization temperature consistent with lattice QCD pseudo-critical temperature (Karsch, 2002; Fodor and Katz, 2004; D'Elia, 2010).
