# Pseudoscalar-Meson Contributions to g-2 via Schwinger's Sum Rule

> The Schwinger sum rule is presented as a new promising tool to study the hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment. In particular, we show preliminary results for the light-by-light scattering contribution of pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key claim

The Schwinger sum rule provides a new dispersive, data-driven framework for calculating hadronic contributions to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, including the hadronic light-by-light contribution, but the required doubly-polarized photoabsorption cross section data are not yet available, so the authors present preliminary model-based results for the pseudoscalar-meson production channel only.

## Method

The authors evaluate the contribution of pseudoscalar-meson photoproduction channels (gamma mu -> mu pi0, mu eta, mu eta') to the muon g-2 using the Schwinger sum rule. They compute the interference cross section between the tree-level QED Compton diagrams and the Primakoff diagram for pseudoscalar production, using transition form factors for the meson-photon couplings and dispersion relations for the meson-lepton couplings, with input from experimentally known decay widths. The resulting doubly-polarized photoabsorption cross section sigma_LT is integrated over energy from threshold to obtain the contribution to a_mu.

## Key numbers

* Experimental muon AMM: a_mu^exp = [11 659 209.1 +/- 6.3] x 10^{-10}
* Standard Model prediction: a_mu^th = [11 659 178.3 +/- 4.3] x 10^{-10}
* Leading-order hadronic vacuum polarization: a_mu^LO HVP = [689.46 +/- 3.25] x 10^{-10}
* Hadronic light-by-light contribution: a_mu^HLbL = [10.34 +/- 2.88] x 10^{-10}
* Pseudoscalar-pole contributions from literature: 8.3 +/- 1.2 x 10^{-10} (Knecht and Nyffeler) and 11.4 +/- 1.0 x 10^{-10} (Melnikov and Vainshtein)
* Most recent pi0-pole contribution: a_mu^{pi0-pole} = 6.26^{+0.30}_{-0.25} x 10^{-10} (Hoferichter et al.)
* Pseudoscalar-meson production channel contributions via Schwinger sum rule: pi0: 16.6^{+4.1}_{-3.7} x 10^{-10}; eta: 8.5^{+2.1}_{-2.9} x 10^{-10}; eta': 1.0 +/- 0.3 x 10^{-10}; total: 26.1^{+4.6}_{-4.7} x 10^{-10}
* Decay widths used: Gamma(pi0 -> e+ e-) = 0.499 +/- 0.028 micro-eV; Gamma(eta -> mu+ mu-) = 7.6 +/- 1.1 meV

## Context

This work extends the Schwinger sum rule approach proposed by Hagelstein and Pascalutsa (2017) to treat hadronic contributions to g-2 in a unified dispersive framework, analogous to the well-known dispersive formula for hadronic vacuum polarization. The results for pseudoscalar-meson production are a factor 2-3 larger than conventional pseudoscalar-pole calculations by Knecht and Nyffeler (2001) and Melnikov and Vainshtein (2003), but the authors note this is only one of four photoabsorption channels contributing at O(alpha^3), and inclusion of the remaining channels may restore agreement. The approach enables a future data-driven determination of hadronic light-by-light contributions once doubly-polarized photoabsorption cross section measurements become available.
