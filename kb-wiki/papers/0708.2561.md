# Mean free paths and in-medium scattering cross sections of energetic nucleons in neutron-rich nucleonic matter within the relativistic impulse approximation

> The mean free paths and in-medium scattering cross sections of energetic nucleons in neutron-rich nucleonic matter are investigated using the nucleon optical potential obtained within the relativistic impulse approximation with the empirical nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes and the nuclear densities obtained in the relativistic mean field model. It is found that the isospin-splitting of nucleon mean free paths, sensitive to the imaginary part of the symmetry potential, changes its sign at certain high kinetic energy. The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections are analytically and numerically demonstrated to be essentially independent of the isospin asymmetry of the medium and increase linearly with density in the high energy region where the relativistic impulse approximation is applicable.

# Mean free paths and in-medium scattering cross sections of energetic nucleons in neutron-rich nucleonic matter within the relativistic impulse approximation

## Key claim
The isospin-splitting of nucleon mean free paths changes sign at a certain high kinetic energy (around 630 MeV), and the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections are essentially independent of the isospin asymmetry of the medium and increase linearly with density in the high energy region where the relativistic impulse approximation is applicable, but these results are limited to energies roughly above 400 MeV where Pauli blocking becomes irrelevant.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic impulse approximation (RIA) to construct the nucleon optical potential in asymmetric nuclear matter, combining empirical free-space Lorentz-invariant nucleon-nucleon scattering amplitudes with nuclear scalar and vector densities obtained from the relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the SL1 parameter set. From the resulting Schrödinger equivalent potentials (SEPs), they calculated nucleon mean free paths via the dispersion relation and then extracted in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections by inverting the relation between mean free path, density, and cross section.

## Key numbers
- The imaginary part of the symmetry potential changes sign at a kinetic energy of approximately 630 MeV.
- The isospin-splitting between neutron and proton mean free paths changes sign at approximately 630 MeV.
- At kinetic energies of 700 MeV and above, the nucleon mean free path becomes insensitive to the isospin asymmetry of the medium.
- The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections at a density of 0.1 times the saturation density (0.1 ρ₀) are very close to the free-space nucleon-nucleon scattering data.
- The ratio of the neutron-proton cross section to the neutron-neutron/proton-proton cross section becomes smaller than 1 above approximately 580 MeV in free space, and the in-medium calculations show a similar trend with the crossover occurring around 600 MeV depending on density.
- The in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections increase linearly with density in the high-energy region where the RIA is valid.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections that were mostly limited to symmetric nuclear matter at low and intermediate energies, such as those using relativistic and non-relativistic Brueckner theory (e.g., Li and Machleidt, 1993; Fuchs et al., 2001) and the Green function approach (e.g., Mao et al., 1994). The authors note that most existing microscopic calculations were devoted to lower energies, leaving the high-energy region largely unknown, and that the RIA approach is complementary to those efforts. The results are relevant for Glauber model analyses of total reaction cross sections used to extract neutron skin sizes and nucleon density profiles of neutron-rich nuclei, as well as for transport model inputs in heavy-ion reactions where the isospin dependence of in-medium cross sections affects the extraction of the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
