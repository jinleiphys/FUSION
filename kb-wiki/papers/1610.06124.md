# Holographic black hole engineering at finite baryon chemical potential

> This is a contribution for the Proceedings of the Conference Hot Quarks 2016, held at South Padre Island, Texas, USA, 12-17 September 2016. I briefly review some thermodynamic and baryon transport results obtained from a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton holographic model engineered to describe the physics of the quark-gluon plasma at finite temperature and baryon density. The results for the equation of state, baryon susceptibilities, and the curvature of the crossover band are in quantitative agreement with the corresponding lattice QCD results with $2+1$ flavors and physical quark masses. Baryon diffusion is predicted to be suppressed by increasing the baryon chemical potential.

# Holographic black hole engineering at finite baryon chemical potential

## Key claim
A bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton holographic model quantitatively reproduces lattice QCD results for the equation of state, baryon susceptibilities, and crossover curvature at finite temperature and baryon chemical potential, while predicting that baryon diffusion is suppressed as baryon chemical potential increases, consistent with the eventual existence of a critical point above 400 MeV.

## Method
The author reviews a bottom-up Einstein-Maxwell-Dilaton (EMD) holographic model constructed to describe the quark-gluon plasma at finite temperature and baryon density. Thermodynamic quantities (equation of state, baryon susceptibilities, crossover curvature) were computed and compared to lattice QCD data with 2+1 flavors and physical quark masses. Transport coefficients (baryon DC conductivity, baryon diffusion constant) were calculated as functions of temperature and baryon chemical potential in the same holographic framework.

## Key numbers
- Crossover curvature in the EMD model: κ_EMD ≈ 0.013, in quantitative agreement with lattice results κ_latt(I) = 0.0135(20) from Bonati (2015) and κ_latt(II) = 0.0149(21) from Bellwied (2015)
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s ≈ 0.095 from hydrodynamic simulations of the QGP, close to the holographic bound η/s = 1/4π
- Critical point in the EMD model is expected for baryon chemical potential μ_B > 400 MeV

## Context
This work extends the non-conformal bottom-up EMD approach originally proposed by Gubser (2008) and DeWolfe (2010) to match lattice QCD thermodynamics at zero chemical potential. The model was further refined by Finazzo (2014), Rougemont (2015), and collaborators to compute transport coefficients in the (T, μ_B) plane. The results enable predictions for baryon transport properties that are difficult to access via first-principle lattice QCD calculations, particularly the suppression of baryon diffusion at high baryon density and the search for a critical point in the QCD phase diagram.
