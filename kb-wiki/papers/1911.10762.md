# Basis Light-Front Quantization for a Chiral Nucleon-Pion Lagrangian

> We present the first application of the Basis Light-Front Quantization method to a simple chiral model of the nucleon-pion system as a relativistic bound state for the physical proton. The light-front mass-squared matrix of the nucleon-pion system is obtained within a truncated basis. The mass and the corresponding light-front wave function (LFWF) of the proton are computed by numerical diagonalization of the resulting mass-squared matrix. With the boost invariant LFWF, we calculate the probability density distribution of the pion's longitudinal momentum fraction and the Dirac form factor of the proton.

## Key claim

This paper presents the first non-perturbative application of the Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) method to a simple chiral model of the nucleon-pion system, treating the physical proton as a relativistic bound state; however, the calculation is limited to terms up to order \(g_A/F\) in the Lagrangian (single-pion emission/absorption only), with higher-order terms such as two-pion processes deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral nucleon-pion Lagrangian density, perform a field redefinition to obtain a transformed interaction, and then derive the light-front Hamiltonian density via Legendre transformation, retaining only one-pion emission/absorption terms. They construct a multi-particle basis using discretized plane waves in the longitudinal direction and two-dimensional harmonic oscillator (2DHO) states in the transverse direction, with truncations on the total longitudinal momentum (\(K_{\text{max}}\)) and the total transverse excitation quanta (\(N_{\text{max}}\)). The mass-squared matrix of the nucleon-pion system is obtained within a truncated Fock space consisting of the nucleon sector and the nucleon-plus-one-pion sector, and the proton mass and light-front wave function are computed by numerical diagonalization, with a Lipkin-Lawson term added to remove spurious center-of-mass excitations.

## Key numbers

* Proton mass computed from the chiral model with \(M_N = 939\) MeV, \(M_\pi = 137\) MeV, \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV, and \(g_A = 1.25\) (tree-level axial-vector coupling constant).
* Pion decay constant set to 93 MeV; pion mass taken as 137 MeV.
* Basis truncation parameters: \(K_{\text{max}}\) (longitudinal momentum cutoff) and \(N_{\text{max}}\) (transverse excitation quanta cutoff) are used to control the basis size, with continuum limits approached as \(K_{\text{max}} \to \infty\) and \(N_{\text{max}} \to \infty\).
* Ultraviolet cutoff in transverse momentum approximated as \(p^\perp_{\text{max}} \approx b \sqrt{2 N_{\text{max}}}\) and infrared cutoff as \(p^\perp_{\text{min}} \approx b / \sqrt{2 N_{\text{max}}}\), where \(b\) is the 2DHO basis strength.

## Context

This work extends the earlier perturbative treatment of the chiral nucleon-pion model by Miller (1997, 2000) to a non-perturbative solution via the BLFQ method, which has previously been applied to quantum electrodynamics (electron anomalous magnetic moment, positronium spectrum) and heavy quarkonium systems. The results enable future inclusion of higher-order terms (e.g., two-pion processes and Weinberg-Tomozawa contact terms) and extension to more realistic models of the proton, including form factor calculations that can be compared with experimental data at higher momentum transfers.
