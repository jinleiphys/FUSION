# Correlation effects on the weak response of nuclear matter

> The consistent description of the nuclear response at low and high momentum transfer requires a unified dynamical model, suitable to account for both short- and long-range correlation effects. We report the results of a study of the charged current weak response of symmetric nuclear matter, carried out using an effective interaction obtained from a realistic model of the nucleon-nucleon force within the formalism of correlated basis functions. Our approach allows for a clear identification of the kinematical regions in which different interaction effects dominate.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a unified description of the weak response of nuclear matter at both low and high momentum transfer can be achieved using an effective interaction derived from a realistic nucleon-nucleon force within the correlated basis functions formalism, but the authors note that the results should be seen as a step towards a unified description rather than a final model.

## Method

The authors computed the charged current weak response of symmetric nuclear matter using an effective interaction obtained from a truncated Argonne v18 potential (v'8) within the correlated basis functions formalism, with three-nucleon forces included through a density-dependent modification. They calculated transition matrix elements to correlated one particle-one hole states using a cluster expansion, and included long-range correlation effects through the Tamm Dancoff approximation, diagonalizing matrices of approximately 3000 particle-hole states for each spin-isospin channel.

## Key numbers

- The quenching of the Fermi transition matrix element due to short-range correlations is approximately 30 percent, as shown at momentum transfer |q| = 0.3 fm^-1
- The response was calculated at momentum transfers |q| = 0.3, 1.5, 1.8, 2.4, and 3.0 fm^-1, covering three kinematical regions: |q| < k_F, k_F < |q| < 2k_F, and |q| > 2k_F
- The basis size for particle-hole states was N_h = 3040 at |q| = 0.3 fm^-1, 2637 at |q| = 1.8 fm^-1, and 2777 at |q| = 3.0 fm^-1
- Long-range correlations dominate at |q| less than or approximately 0.5 fm^-1, while short-range correlations dominate at |q| greater than or approximately 2.0 fm^-1
- A collective mode in the S=1, T=0 channel is visible at |q| less than or approximately 1.2 fm^-1 but disappears if exchange contributions to the effective interaction matrix element are neglected

## Context

This work extends the correlated basis functions approach of Shannon et al. (2006) by using an improved effective interaction that includes three-nucleon forces as developed by Valli et al. (2008). The results enable a consistent treatment of both short-range correlations (which quench the response) and long-range correlations (which produce collective modes), with applications to neutrino scattering experiments in the energy range 0.5-3 GeV relevant to K2K and MiniBOONE, as well as low-energy neutrino interactions important for supernova evolution and neutron star cooling.
