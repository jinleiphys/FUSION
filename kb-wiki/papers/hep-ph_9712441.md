# The ratio of p and n yields in NC neutrino(antineutrino)-nucleus scattering and strange form factors of the nucleon

> We calculate the ratio of proton and neutron yields in NC induced neutrino(antineutrino)-nucleus inelastic scattering at neutrino energies of about 1 GeV. We show that this ratio depends very weakly on the nuclear models employed and that in the neutrino and antineutrino cases the ratios have different sensitivity to the axial and vector strange form factors; moreover, the ratio of antineutrino--nucleus cross sections turns out to be rather sensitive to the electric strange form factor. We demonstrate that measurements of these ratios will allow to get information on the strange form factors of the nucleon in the region Q > 0.4 GeV^2.

# The ratio of p and n yields in NC neutrino(antineutrino)-nucleus scattering and strange form factors of the nucleon

## Key claim

The ratio of proton to neutron yields in neutral-current neutrino-nucleus and antineutrino-nucleus inelastic scattering at incident energies of about 1 GeV depends very weakly on nuclear model details, and the two ratios have different sensitivities to the axial, magnetic, and electric strange form factors of the nucleon, potentially allowing a combined determination of all three strange form factors in the region Q^2 >= 0.4 GeV^2.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section ratio R^{nu(nubar)}_{p/n} for the reactions nu_mu (or antinu_mu) + 12C -> nu_mu (or antinu_mu) + p + (A-1,Z-1) and the corresponding neutron knockout channel, at incident neutrino energy E_nu = 1 GeV. They employed plane wave impulse approximation (PWIA) within two nuclear models (relativistic Fermi gas and relativistic shell model) and also included distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) with a relativistic optical potential for final-state interactions, using standard dipole parameterizations for the strange form factors with cutoff masses of the non-strange vector and axial form factors.

## Key numbers

- The ratio R^nu_{p/n} without strangeness is approximately 0.73 for the integrated cross section over the interval 100 MeV <= T_N <= 400 MeV.
- The axial strange constant from deep inelastic scattering is g_A^s = -0.10 +/- 0.03 (Ellis analysis).
- The strange magnetic form factor measured at Bates (SAMPLE) at Q^2 = 0.1 GeV^2 is G_M^s = 0.23 +/- 0.37 +/- 0.15 +/- 0.19, corresponding to mu_s = 0.30 +/- 0.48 +/- 0.20 +/- 0.25 when extrapolated to Q^2 = 0 using dipole form factors.
- Different parameterizations of the Sachs electromagnetic form factors affect the ratios by no more than 1-2%.
- For neutrinos, the sensitivity to the axial cutoff M_A is less than 3%; for antineutrinos, a 6-7% variation in M_A can induce an effect as large as 7-8% in R^{nubar}_{p/n}.
- The electric strange form factor effect on R^nu_{p/n} does not exceed 25% of the correction from the axial strange form factor, while it can be about 50% of the correction from a strange magnetic moment mu_s = -0.3.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Garvey et al. (Krewald, Garvey) who first proposed the p/n yield ratio as a probe of strange form factors at lower neutrino energies (E_nu ~ 200 MeV), and by the same authors (Alberico et al., 1996, 1997) who showed that at those lower energies final-state interactions introduce significant theoretical uncertainties. The paper also builds on the SAMPLE experiment at Bates (1997) which measured the strange magnetic form factor via parity-violating electron-proton scattering, and on the analysis of polarized structure function data from CERN and SLAC that determined the axial strange constant. The results suggest that combined measurements of the neutrino and antineutrino ratios at facilities such as Brookhaven, KEK, Protvino, or the proposed BOONE experiment at Fermilab could disentangle all three strange form factors, complementing parity-violating electron scattering which has difficulty accessing the electric strange form factor.
