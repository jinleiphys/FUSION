# The Gluon Propagator without lattice Gribov copies

> We study the gluon propagator in quenched lattice QCD using the Laplacian gauge which is free of lattice Gribov copies. We compare our results with those obtained in the Landau gauge on the lattice, as well as with various approximate solutions of the Dyson Schwinger equations. We find a finite value $\sim (445 \rm{MeV})^{-2}$ for the renormalized zero-momentum propagator (taking our renormalization point at 1.943 GeV), and a pole mass $\sim 640 \pm 140$ MeV.

## Key claim

The gluon propagator in quenched lattice QCD, when computed using the Laplacian gauge (which is free of lattice Gribov copies), yields a finite zero-momentum propagator and supports the existence of a pole mass, in contrast to the Landau gauge where the pole extraction is unstable and may give a complex pole.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations on lattices of sizes 8^4, 16^4, and 16^3 x 32 at beta values of 5.8, 6.0, 6.2, and 6.5. They fixed the gauge using the Laplacian gauge, which employs the lowest-lying eigenvectors of the covariant Laplacian to define a unique gauge without Gribov copies, and extracted the transverse gluon propagator D(q^2) from the gauge-fixed configurations. They compared their results with Landau gauge data from the Gauge Connection database and fitted various analytic models, including Cornwall's dynamically massive gluon model and the phenomenological model A of Leinweber et al.

## Key numbers

* Renormalized zero-momentum propagator value: (445 MeV)^{-2}, corresponding to 5.020(16) GeV^{-2}, at renormalization point mu = 1.943 GeV (beta=6.0)
* Pole mass estimate: 640 +/- 140 MeV, with an interval [500, 785] MeV from various extrapolations
* Ratio of lattice spacings a_{beta=6.0}/a_{beta=5.8} = 0.71 +/- 0.02, in agreement with the value 0.72(4) from static potential analysis
* Ratio of wavefunction renormalization constants Z_{beta=6.0}/Z_{beta=5.8} = 1.07 +/- 0.075
* Renormalization factor Z_3(mu,a) at beta=6.0: approximately 2.312

## Context

This work extends lattice studies of the gluon propagator by addressing the problem of Gribov copies that plague the standard Landau gauge fixing procedure used in prior lattice calculations such as those by Leinweber et al. (1999) and Marenzoni et al. The Laplacian gauge, proposed by Vink and Wiese (1992), provides an unambiguous gauge condition. The results contradict the infrared enhancement prediction of Mandelstam (1979) and the vanishing infrared behavior argued by Gribov (1978) and Zwanziger, instead favoring a dynamically generated gluon mass as in Cornwall's model (1982). This work enables future studies of the gluon propagator at finite temperature and larger volumes to consolidate the pole mass determination.
