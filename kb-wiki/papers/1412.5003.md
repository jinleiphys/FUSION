# Heavy Quark Potential at Finite Temperature in a Dual Gravity Closer to Large N QCD

> In gauge-gravity duality, the heavy quark potential at finite temperature is usually calculated with the pure AdS background, which does not capture the renormalization group (RG) running in the gauge theory part. In addition, the potential does not contain any confining term in the deconfined phase. Following the Klebanov-Strassler geometry, we employ a geometry, which captures the RG flow similar to QCD, to obtain the heavy quark potential by analytically continuing the string configurations into the complex plane. In addition to the attractive terms, the obtained potential has confining terms both at $T = 0$ and $T \neq 0 $, compared to the calculations usually done in the literature, where only the Coulomb-like term is present in the deconfined phase. The potential also develops an (negative) imaginary part above a critical separation, $r_c (= 0.53 z_h )$. Moreover, our potential exhibits a behavior different from the usual Debye screening obtained from perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The authors claim that by using a Klebanov-Strassler-inspired geometry (OKS-BH) that captures renormalization group running similar to QCD, rather than pure AdS background, the heavy quark potential at finite temperature acquires confining terms both at zero and nonzero temperature, plus a negative imaginary part above a critical separation, unlike the purely Coulomb-like term found in standard AdS/CFT calculations.

## Method

The authors construct the Nambu-Goto action for an open string in the OKS-BH geometry, which includes a warp factor with logarithmic corrections and a black hole factor \(g(z)=1-z^4/z_h^4\) where \(z_h=1/(\pi T)\). They extremize the string worldsheet attached to a static quark-antiquark pair at the boundary, expand the action in the limit of small coefficients \(A_i, B_i\) (which are of order \(g_s N_f\)), and analytically continue the string configurations into the complex plane to obtain both real and imaginary parts of the potential. The potential is derived in two asymptotic limits: \(z_{\rm max} \ll z_h\) (small separation) and \(z_{\rm max} \gg z_h\) (large separation), and then interpolated.

## Key numbers

* Critical separation for the onset of an imaginary part: \(r_c = 0.53\, z_h\), where \(z_h = 1/(\pi T)\) is the black hole horizon.
* For small separation (\(rT \ll 1\)), the real part of the potential takes the form \(Re[V] \approx -5/(9\pi r) + [9(A_2+B_2)/(20\pi)] r + \mathcal{O}(r^3)\), resembling a Cornell potential at \(T=0\).
* For large separation (\(rT \gg 1\)), the leading real part scales as \(Re[V] \propto \pi^3 T^2 \hat{r}^2\) (where \(\hat{r}=rT\)) and the leading imaginary part scales as \(Im[V] \propto -\pi^3 T^2 \hat{r}^2\).
* The screening length \(r_0\) is given by \(r_0 = [40(c_1^2+c_2^2)/(9 c_1 \pi^4 T^4)]^{1/3}\), where \(c_1\) and \(c_2\) are constants defined in terms of \(A_2\), \(B_2\), and \(z_h\).

## Context

This work extends the standard AdS/CFT calculation of the heavy quark potential at finite temperature, originally done for \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM by Maldacena (1998) and by Brandhuber (1998) and Rey (1998) using pure AdS or AdS-black-hole backgrounds, which lack confining terms and RG running. The authors follow the Klebanov-Strassler geometry (2000) and the Ouyang-Klebanov-Strassler-Black-Hole (OKS-BH) geometry developed by Mia et al. (2010, 2011), which captures logarithmic running of the coupling. The complex string configuration method follows Yavier (2008), who found a critical separation of \(0.87 z_h\) in pure AdS. The results enable a strong-coupling description of heavy quarkonium dissociation that includes both screening and thermal width effects, going beyond the perturbative Debye screening picture.
