# Photon bremsstrahlung and diffusive broadening of a hard jet

> The photon bremsstrahlung rate from a quark jet produced in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus is studied in the collinear limit. The leading medium-enhanced higher twist corrections which describe the multiple scattering of the jet in the nucleus are re-summed to all orders of twist. The propagation of the jet in the absence of further radiative energy loss is shown to be governed by a transverse momentum diffusion equation. We compute the final photon spectrum in the limit of soft photons, taking into account the leading and next-to-leading terms in the photon momentum fraction y. In this limit, the photon spectrum in a physical gauge is shown to arise from two interfering sources: one where the initial hard scattering produces an off-shell quark which immediately radiates the photon and then undergoes subsequent soft re-scattering; alternatively the quark is produced on-shell and propagates through the medium until it is driven off-shell by re-scattering and radiates the photon. Our result has a simple formal structure as a product of the photon splitting function, the quark transverse momentum distribution coming from a diffusion equation and a dimensionless factor which encodes the effect of the interferences encountered by the propagating quark over the length of the medium. The destructive nature of such interferences in the small y limit are responsible for the origin of the Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal (LPM) effect. Along the way we also discuss possible implications for quark jets in hot nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The paper derives an all-twist resummed expression for the photon bremsstrahlung spectrum from a hard quark jet undergoing multiple scattering in a dense medium, showing that in the soft photon limit the spectrum factorizes into a product of the photon splitting function, a transverse momentum distribution from a diffusion equation, and a dimensionless interference factor; the authors explicitly note that the result is derived in the collinear limit and for soft photons (taking only leading and next-to-leading terms in the photon momentum fraction y), and that the medium is assumed to have a short distance color correlation length rather than being a thermalized plasma.

## Method

The authors calculate the semi-inclusive hadronic tensor for deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) off a large nucleus in the Breit frame, using light-cone gauge (A⁻=0) and the collinear limit. They isolate length-enhanced higher twist contributions from multiple gluon exchanges between the struck quark and the nuclear medium, resumming these to all orders of twist by converting the twist expansion into a transverse momentum gradient expansion. The resulting expression for the photon spectrum is obtained by summing over all possible photon emission points along the quark's trajectory, with the quark propagation in the absence of radiative energy loss governed by a transverse momentum diffusion equation.

## Key numbers

- The photon momentum fraction is defined as y = l⁻/q⁻, where l⁻ is the photon's forward momentum and q⁻ is the quark's forward momentum
- The Bjorken variable x_B = Q²/(2p⁺q⁻), with Q² being the virtuality of the incoming photon
- The formation time of the radiated photon is τ_f = 1/(p⁺x_L), where x_L = l_⊥²/[2p⁺q⁻y(1-y)]
- The leading twist photon spectrum contains the quark-to-photon splitting function P_{q→qγ}(y) = (2-2y+y²)/y
- The final hadronic tensor at leading twist is proportional to 1/l_⊥² times the splitting function

## Context

This work extends the higher twist (HT) approach to jet modification, previously applied to transverse momentum broadening in Majumder (2007), by incorporating photon bremsstrahlung from a multiply scattering jet. The authors position their formalism as complementary to the AMY (Arnold-Moore-Yaffe), ASW (Armesto-Salgado-Wiedemann), and GLV (Gyulassy-Levai-Vitev) approaches to radiative energy loss, noting that unlike AMY which assumes a thermalized plasma at asymptotically high temperature, their HT scheme makes no such approximation about the medium. The results are directly relevant to photon-hadron correlation measurements at RHIC and provide a framework for calculating hard photon production from both cold nuclear matter and hot partonic media, with the Landau-Pomeranchuck-Migdal (LPM) effect arising naturally from destructive interferences in the small y limit.
