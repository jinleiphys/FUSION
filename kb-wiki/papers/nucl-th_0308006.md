# Pentaquark baryon production at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider

> Production of pentaquark $Θ^+$ baryons in central relativistic heavy ion collisions is studied in a kinetic model. Assuming that a quark-gluon plasma is produced in the collisions, we first determine the number of $Θ^+$ produced from the quark-gluon plasma using a parton coalescence model, and then take into consideration its production and absorption in subsequent hadronic matter via the reactions $KN\leftrightarrowΘ$, $KN\leftrightarrowπΘ$, and $\piN\leftrightarrow\bar KΘ$. We find that although the final $Θ^+$ number is affected by hadronic interactions, it remains sensitive to the initial number of $Θ^+$ produced from the quark-gluon plasma, particularly in the case of a small $Θ^+$ width as imposed by the $K^+N$ and $K^+d$ scattering data. Because of small baryon chemical potential in the hot dense matter produced in these collisions, the number of produced anti-$Θ$ is only slightly smaller than that of $Θ^+$.

## Key claim

The final number of $\Theta^+$ pentaquark baryons produced in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC is sensitive to the initial number produced from the quark-gluon plasma via parton coalescence, particularly if the $\Theta^+$ width is small (5 MeV or less) as suggested by $K^+N$ and $K^+d$ scattering data; if the width is larger (e.g., 60 MeV), hadronic interactions can erase this sensitivity.

## Method

The authors model central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV using a boost-invariant Bjorken picture with accelerated transverse expansion, starting from a quark-gluon plasma phase at critical temperature $T_C=175$ MeV. They compute the initial $\Theta^+$ yield from the quark-gluon plasma using a parton coalescence model with a Gaussian Wigner distribution for the five-quark ($uudd\bar{s}$) wave function, then evolve the $\Theta^+$ abundance through the mixed phase and hadronic matter using a kinetic equation that includes production and absorption via the reactions $KN\leftrightarrow\Theta$, $KN\leftrightarrow\pi\Theta$, and $\pi N\leftrightarrow\bar K\Theta$, with cross sections determined from a Breit-Wigner resonance formula and nucleon-pole diagrams.

## Key numbers

- Initial $\Theta^+$ yield from quark-gluon plasma coalescence: 0.19 per unit rapidity in central Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV.
- Final $\Theta^+$ abundance after hadronic evolution with $\Gamma_\Theta=20$ MeV: 0.46 per unit rapidity (starting from 0.19 initial); 0.41 for zero initial; 0.62 for 0.94 initial.
- With $\Gamma_\Theta=5$ MeV or 1 MeV, hadronic effects become insignificant and final $\Theta^+$ number is essentially determined by the quark-gluon plasma contribution.
- Thermal averaged cross sections at $T=125$–$175$ MeV: $\langle \sigma_{KN\rightarrow\Theta}v\rangle \approx 1$ mb, $\langle \sigma_{KN\rightarrow\pi\Theta}v\rangle \approx 0.3$ mb, $\langle \sigma_{\pi N\rightarrow\bar K\Theta}v\rangle = 1$–$3$ $\mu$b; absorption cross sections: $\langle \sigma_{\pi\Theta\rightarrow KN}v\rangle \approx 6$ mb, $\langle \sigma_{\bar K\Theta\rightarrow\pi N}v\rangle \approx 0.6$ mb.
- $\Theta^+$ root-mean-square radius estimated as 0.9 fm, based on a width parameter $\sigma=0.86$ fm fit to the proton charge radius.
- $\bar\Theta^-$ to $\Theta^+$ ratio predicted to be $(0.89)^4 \approx 0.63$, based on light antiquark to quark ratio 0.89 and antistrange to strange ratio 1.

## Context

This work extends the statistical model estimate of Randrup (2003), which predicted about one $\Theta^+$ per unit rapidity in chemical equilibrium, and the more recent analysis of Letessier (2003), which gave a factor of two larger than the coalescence result. The coalescence model used here had previously been applied successfully to describe yields and transverse momentum spectra of normal hadrons at RHIC by Greco, Hwa, and Fries (2003). The study enables experimental searches for $\Theta^+$ and $\bar\Theta^-$ at RHIC, where the small baryon chemical potential allows production of both particles and antiparticles, unlike photo- or kaon-nucleus reactions.
