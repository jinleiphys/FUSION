# Pion-nucleon scattering and isospin violation

> I discuss low-energy pion-nucleon scattering in the framework of chiral perturbation theory. I argue that using this theoretical method one is able to handle the in some cases tremendous experimental accuracy (for the low partial waves). I then show how strong and electromagnetic isospin violation can be treated simultaneously. Some first results for neutral pion scattering and the $σ$-term are given.

## Key claim
Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) can simultaneously treat strong and electromagnetic isospin violation in low-energy pion-nucleon scattering, and first results indicate that isospin-breaking effects in neutral pion scattering off protons can be as large as the isospin-conserving contributions, though these results are only indicative until a fourth-order calculation is completed.

## Method
The author constructs the most general effective chiral Lagrangian for pions, nucleons, and virtual photons up to third chiral order, with the electric charge counted as a small momentum. Low-energy constants (LECs) are fitted to partial-wave data from three different solutions (KA85 and VPI SP98) for pion lab momenta between 40 and 100 MeV, allowing predictions for S-wave scattering lengths and the sigma-term. Strong isospin violation enters via the quark mass difference \(m_u - m_d\), while electromagnetic effects are treated through explicit photon loops and contact terms.

## Key numbers
- Current algebra prediction for the isovector S-wave scattering length: \(\tilde{a}^- = 8.96\) (in units of \(10^{-2}/M_\pi\))
- CHPT prediction range for the isovector S-wave scattering length: \(8.3 \le a^- \le 9.3\) (in units of \(10^{-2}/M_\pi\))
- CHPT prediction range for the isoscalar S-wave scattering length: \(-1.0 \le a^+ \le 0.6\) (in units of \(10^{-2}/M_\pi\))
- Panofsky ratio: \(P = 1.543 \pm 0.008\), leading to \(a^- = 8.5 \pm 0.2\) from dispersion theory
- Neutral pion-proton scattering length decomposition: \(a(\pi^0 p) = (-0.48 - 0.11 - 0.29)\) (units of \(10^{-2}/M_\pi\)), where the three terms are isospin-conserving strong, isospin-violating strong, and electromagnetic contributions
- Proton sigma-term: \(\sigma_p(0) = 47.2\ \mathrm{MeV} - 3.9\ \mathrm{MeV} = 43.3\ \mathrm{MeV}\), an 8% reduction from isospin violation
- Sigma-term difference: \(\sigma_p(2M_{\pi^+}^2) - \sigma_p(0) = 7.5\ \mathrm{MeV}\), differing from the isospin-limit value of 7.9 MeV by 5%

## Context
This work extends the chiral perturbation theory treatment of pion-nucleon scattering by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1995) to include isospin violation, confirming Weinberg's (1977) prediction of large isospin violation in neutral pion scattering. It provides a consistent framework that avoids the mismatch between electromagnetic and strong treatments found in phenomenological analyses by Gibbs (1995) and Matsinos (1997). The results enable future extraction of the quark mass difference \(m_u - m_d\) from precise data on pionic atoms and pion photoproduction, and motivate a complete fourth-order calculation currently in progress.
