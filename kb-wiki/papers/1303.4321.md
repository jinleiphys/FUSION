# MCNP6 Simulation of Quasi-Monoenergetic 7Li(p,n) Neutron Sources below 150 MeV

> The applicability of MCNP6 to simulate quasi-monoenergetic neutron sources from interactions of proton beams with energies below 150 MeV on thick 7Li targets have been studied. Neutron spectra at zero degrees from a 2-mm 7Li layer backed by a 12-mm carbon beam stopper in an Al flange bombarded with protons of 20, 25, 30, 35, and 40 MeV have been calculated with MCNP6 using the recent Los Alamos data library as well as using the Bertini+Dresner and CEM03.03 event generators. A comparison with the experimental neutron spectra shows that the event generators do not do well in describing such reactions, while MCNP6 using the LANL data library simulates production of neutrons from p + 7Li in good agreement with the measured data.

## Key claim

MCNP6 using the Los Alamos data library simulates neutron production from p + 7Li in good agreement with measured data for proton energies from 20 to 40 MeV, while the Bertini+Dresner and CEM03.03 event generators fail to reproduce the measured spectra, especially the quasi-monoenergetic neutron peak, even at the highest tested energy of 40 MeV.

## Method

Neutron spectra at zero degrees from a 2-mm thick 7Li layer backed by a 12-mm carbon beam stopper in an aluminum flange were calculated with MCNP6 for proton beams of 20, 25, 30, 35, and 40 MeV. The calculations used the Los Alamos evaluated data library for p+7Li, as well as the Bertini+Dresner and CEM03.03 event generators, and were compared to experimental data from Uwamino et al. (1997).

## Key numbers

- Neutron spectra were calculated for proton beam energies of 20, 25, 30, 35, and 40 MeV incident on a 2-mm thick 7Li target.
- The target assembly included a 12-mm carbon beam stopper and an aluminum flange.
- The quasi-monoenergetic neutron peak was not reproduced well by the event generators even at 40 MeV, the highest energy tested.

## Context

This work extends prior evaluations of the 7Li(p,n) reaction by Mashnik and collaborators (2001) that produced an ENDF-formatted data library for incident protons up to 150 MeV, and by Simakov et al. (2011) who used the library in MCNPX simulations for activation studies. The present study validates the use of the Los Alamos data library with the newer MCNP6 code and demonstrates that event generators are inadequate for simulating these quasi-monoenergetic neutron sources at low proton energies. This enables reliable simulation of neutron production for applications such as activation cross-section measurements and neutron source design.
