# Ridges and Soft Jet Components in Untriggered Di-hadron Correlations in Pb+Pb Collisions at 2.76 TeV

> We study untriggered di-hadron correlations in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, based on an event-by-event simulation of a hydrodynamic expansion starting from flux tube initial conditions. The correlation function shows interesting structures as a function of the pseudorapidity difference $Δη$ and the azimuthal angle difference $Δϕ$, in particular comparing different centralities. We can clearly identify a peak-like nearside structure associated with very low momentum components of jets for peripheral collisions, which disappears towards central collisions. On the other hand, a very broad ridge structure from asymmetric flow seen at central collisions, gets smaller and finally disappears towards peripheral collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that untriggered di-hadron correlations in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV reveal three distinct structures, elliptic flow, a broad ridge from asymmetric flow, and a nearside peak from low-momentum jet components, but the ridge and peak are strongly centrality-dependent, with the ridge dominating central collisions and the jet peak dominating peripheral collisions, and both disappearing at opposite extremes.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event simulations using a hydrodynamic expansion starting from flux tube initial conditions (EPOS model), with an equation of state compatible with lattice gauge results and a hadronic cascade after hadronization. They computed the two-dimensional di-hadron correlation function R(Δη, Δφ) for different centrality classes, both with and without Bose-Einstein statistics, and compared the elliptic flow component to v₂ measurements at η=0.

## Key numbers

- The ridge structure is most prominent for the highest centrality class (5-10%), where it is defined as the difference R(Δη, Δφ) - R(Δη, π) cos(2Δφ).
- The jet peak is clearly identified in the most peripheral centrality class (80-90%).
- The radial flow velocity reaches up to a certain percentage of c at τ = 4.6 fm/c (shown in figure but exact percentage not given in text).
- The energy loss formula for high transverse momentum string segments is ΔE ∝ ε^(3/8) √E dL, and for low momentum segments ΔE ∝ ρ dL.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of di-hadron correlations in Au+Au collisions at 200 GeV (Ridge1, Ridge2, Ridge3) and proton-proton reactions at 7 TeV (Ridge4), and uses the EPOS flux tube approach compatible with the color glass condensate picture (CGC1). The results enable a unified understanding of how initial density fluctuations, elliptic flow, and low-momentum jet components contribute to the correlation function across different centralities, and provide a link to the crucial question of separating bulk matter from low-momentum pieces of jets in heavy-ion collisions.
