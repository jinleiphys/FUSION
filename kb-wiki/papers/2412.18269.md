# Global analysis of the non-uniformity of nucleon density distributions

> Background: Saturation of nuclear density is a fundamental property of atomic nuclei but in reality, the nuclear internal density distribution is not uniform, e.g., some nuclei are known to have the so-called bubble structure, in which the central density is depressed. Purpose: We aim to unveil the emergent mechanism of the non-uniformity of the nucleon density distributions for whole nuclear mass regions, not only for a typical bubble structure. Method: We systematically investigate the nucleon density distributions using the Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer calculation represented in the three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate space. The ground states of 1,389 even-even nuclei are generated. To quantify the nonuniformity of these density distributions, a ``generalized bubble parameter" is introduced. Results: We find that the bubble structure appears around the magic numbers, which correspond to the regions where the s orbit appears near the Fermi surface. The nuclear deformation and pairing correlations strongly affect the occupation probability, but the robust bubble structure of a medium mass nucleus, $^{100}$Sn, is found. We confirm that the Coulomb force enhances the bubble degree in the superheavy region. The nuclear non-uniformity is further generalized by the ``multi-layered" bubble structure, which exhibits some density depression in the internal regions of the density distributions. Conclusion: The non-uniformity of the internal density distribution occurs due to the deficiency of the specific single-particle orbits: the nodal $s$, $p$, and $d$ orbits. This is certainly reflected in the density distribution near the nuclear surface, which can be deduced from proton-elastic scattering.

## Key claim

The non-uniformity of nucleon density distributions, including both the ordinary bubble structure (central depression) and the newly identified "multi-layered" bubble structure, arises from the deficiency of specific single-particle orbits (nodal s, p, and d orbits) near the Fermi surface, with the bubble structure appearing around magic numbers where the s orbit is near the Fermi surface rather than being a universal property of all nuclei.

## Method

The authors performed systematic Skyrme Hartree-Fock plus Bardeen-Cooper-Schrieffer (HF+BCS) calculations in three-dimensional Cartesian coordinate space for 1,389 even-even nuclei with Z=6-118. They introduced a "generalized bubble parameter" G(r_min) to quantify density non-uniformity, defined as the relative difference between the maximum density and the density at a local minimum, and classified bubble multiplicity M_B to identify multi-layered bubble structures.

## Key numbers

- 1,389 even-even nuclei were calculated with Z=6-118
- Mesh sizes: Δx=0.8 fm with R_cal=12 fm for Z=6-18; Δx=1.0 fm with R_cal=15 fm for Z=20-80; Δx=1.0 fm with R_cal=20 fm for Z=82-118
- For 132Sn, the proton bubble parameter G^p_B=0.21 with ϱ^p_max at r=3.8 fm; the multi-layered bubble parameters were G^p_MLB=0.031 at r_min^p=2.8 fm and G^n_MLB=0.044 at r_min^n=1.6 fm
- For 346Og (Z=118), varying Coulomb strength by ±10% changed G^p_B by +0.064 (+35%) and -0.032 (-18%); for 270Hs (Z=108), G^p_B varied by about ±20% for the same ±10% Coulomb variation
- The maximum amplitude of G^m_B appears at 100Sn
- For 346Og, G^p_MLB changed by +0.085 (+44%) and -0.038 (-19%) for Coulomb strength varied by +10% and -10%, respectively

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bubble nuclei from specific cases (e.g., 34Si studied by Mutschler (2017), 206Pb and 205Tl by Euteneuer (1976)) to a global systematic survey across the entire nuclear chart. The authors build on the concept that bubble structure arises from s-orbit deficiency, as discussed by Davies (1972), Campi (1973), and more recent work by Saxena (2019). The introduction of the "multi-layered" bubble structure generalizes the bubble concept beyond central depression, and the correlation between bubble parameters and nuclear surface diffuseness (established in PhysRevC.97.054607 and PhysRevC.102.034619) enables experimental extraction of bubble signatures from proton-elastic scattering measurements.
