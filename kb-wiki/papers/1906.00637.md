# EoS from terrestrial experiments: static and dynamic polarizations of nuclear density

> We critically examine nuclear matter and neutron matter equation of state (EoS) parameters by using best available terrestrial experimental results. The nuclear incompression modulus $K_{\infty}$ is re-examined in comparisons with RPA results of modern relativistic and non-relativistic EDF and up-to-date experimental data of isoscalar giant monopole resonance energy of $^{208}$Pb. The symmetry energy expansion coefficients $J$, $L$ and $K_{sym}$ are examined by recent FRDM mass model and the neutron skin of $^{48}$Ca extracted from $(p,p')$ experiments.

## Key claim

The paper extracts nuclear matter equation-of-state parameters from terrestrial experiments, but the authors explicitly note that the extracted incompressibility value of $K_{\infty}=225\pm20$ MeV carries uncertainty from both the empirical determination of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) energy and from the theoretical models used in the microscopic calculations, and that the mass-number dependence of excitation energies is not perfectly regular.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus random phase approximation (HF+RPA) calculations with multiple Skyrme, Gogny, and relativistic mean field (RMF) effective interactions to correlate the nuclear matter incompressibility $K_{\infty}$ with the calculated ISGMR centroid energy in $^{208}$Pb. For the symmetry energy, they optimized the finite-range droplet mass model (FRDM) against experimental binding energies of several thousand nuclei to extract $J$ and $L$, and used the neutron skin of $^{48}$Ca measured from $(p,p')$ experiments to extract $L$ and $K_{sym}$ via correlations with Skyrme and RMF energy density functionals.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter incompressibility: $K_{\infty}=225\pm20$ MeV, extracted from ISGMR data of $^{208}$Pb including superfluid Sn and Cd isotopes.
- From FRDM mass model optimization: $J=32.3\pm0.5$ MeV and $L=53.5\pm15$ MeV.
- From $(p,p')$ neutron skin of $^{48}$Ca: $\Delta r_{np}=0.168^{+0.025}_{-0.028}$ fm.
- From Skyrme EDF correlation with $^{48}$Ca neutron skin: $L=42\pm15$ MeV and $K_{sym}=-120\pm40$ MeV.
- Experimental ISGMR centroid energy in $^{208}$Pb: $13.6\pm0.1$ MeV from $(d,d')$ experiments, with other measurements ranging from $13.5\pm0.3$ MeV (Orsay) to $13.96\pm0.2$ MeV (Texas A and M).

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent HF+RPA approach of Colo, Khan, and others to determine the nuclear incompressibility, and uses the FRDM mass model of Moller (2016) to constrain symmetry energy parameters. The neutron skin measurement of $^{48}$Ca from Zenihiro provides a new benchmark for extracting $L$ and $K_{sym}$, complementing earlier studies on $^{208}$Pb using proton elastic scattering, coherent pion-photoproduction, antiprotonic atom X-rays, and electric dipole polarizability. The results enable improved modeling of neutron star structure and are consistent with the metamodeling analysis of the equation of state with Skyrme EDF by Margueron et al. (2018).
