# The Study of Magnetically Deformed Atoms in the Outer Crust of Neutron Stars in Presence of Strong Quantizing Magnetic Field

> We have studied the various properties of magnetically deformed atoms, replaced by deformed Wigner-Seitz cells, at the outer crust region of strongly magnetized neutron stars (magnetars) using a relativistic version of Thomas-Fermi model in cylindrical coordinates.

# The Study of Magnetically Deformed Atoms in the Outer Crust of Neutron Stars in Presence of Strong Quantizing Magnetic Field

## Key claim
Magnetically deformed atoms in the outer crust of magnetars, modeled as cylindrical Wigner-Seitz cells, exhibit significant changes in electron density, kinetic energy, and cell dimensions only when the magnetic field exceeds approximately \(10^{16}\) G, with the approximation that electrons occupy only the zeroth Landau level (\(\nu_{max}=0\)) and electron rest mass is neglected to obtain an analytical solution for the Poisson equation.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic Thomas-Fermi model in cylindrical coordinates to study non-uniform electron gas within deformed Wigner-Seitz cells, assuming azimuthal symmetry and a constant external magnetic field along the Z-direction. They solve the Poisson equation analytically by approximating the electron Fermi momentum as \(p_F \sim \mu_e + e\phi\) (setting \(\nu_{max}=0\) and \(m_e=0\)), obtaining solutions in terms of Bessel functions and exponential functions. The electron number density, kinetic energy density, electron-nucleus interaction energy, and kinetic pressure are then computed using the more exact expression for Fermi momentum that includes finite Landau level occupation.

## Key numbers
- Outer crust density of metallic iron: \(\sim 4.3 \times 10^{11}\) gm/cc
- Normal nuclear density \(\rho_0\): \(\sim 2.8 \times 10^{14}\) gm/cc
- Outer crust width: \(\sim 0.3\) km
- Nuclear radius parameter: \(r_0 = 1.12\) fm
- The separation constant \(\xi\) is approximately 100 MeV for low and moderate magnetic fields, increasing sharply beyond \(10^{17}\) G
- The normalization constant \(C\) saturates to \(\sim 100\) MeV at high magnetic fields
- The longitudinal cell dimension \(z_{max}\) saturates to \(\sim 10\) fm at extremely high magnetic fields
- The transverse cell dimension \(r_{max}\) tends to zero for extremely large magnetic field strengths
- Beyond \(500 \times B_c^{(e)}\), the maximum Landau quantum number \(\nu_{max}\) becomes identically zero throughout the Wigner-Seitz cell
- The first zero of the Bessel function \(J_0(\xi r_n)\) occurs at \(2.40482\)

## Context
This work extends the study of strongly magnetized neutron star crusts (magnetars) beyond the conventional neutron star scenario, where surface magnetic fields are \(\sim 10^{15}\) G and interior fields may reach \(\sim 10^{18}\) G as suggested by the Virial theorem. The authors build on prior work by Chakrabarty (2009) and others on dense matter in strong magnetic fields, and cite the classic Wigner and Bardeen approach for approximate analytical treatments. The results enable future studies of more realistic prolate spheroidal deformation of atoms, evaluation of electron-electron direct and exchange interaction energies using Monte Carlo methods, and investigation of cigar-shaped atoms in metallic crystals under ultra-strong magnetic fields.
