# C and P violations in the PNJL model

> Using the PNJL model, we investigate C and P violations when theta =pi and Theta =mu/(iT)=π/3, where T, theta and mu are the temperature, the parameter of theta-vacuum and the quark number chemical potential, respectively. It is shown that the C violation and the P restoration happen almost simultaneously at theta=pi and Theta =pi/3, if the deconfinement and the chiral symmetry restoration happen almost simultaneously at theta =Theta =0.

## Key claim

Using the PNJL and EPNJL models, the authors show that C violation and P restoration happen almost simultaneously at \(\theta = \pi\) and \(\Theta = \mu/(iT) = \pi/3\), *if* the deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration transitions happen almost simultaneously at \(\theta = \Theta = 0\).

## Method

The authors use the PNJL (Polyakov–Nambu–Jona-Lasinio) model and its extension, the EPNJL (entanglement PNJL) model, to compute the temperature dependence of the chiral condensate \(\sigma\), the Polyakov loop \(\Phi\), the \(\eta\) condensate (a parity-odd quantity), and the phase \(\psi\) of the modified Polyakov loop \(\Psi = \Phi e^{i\Theta}\) at \(\theta = \pi\) and \(\Theta = \pi/3\). Transition temperatures are defined by the peaks of the susceptibilities of \(\Phi\) and \(\sigma\). The parameter \(c\) is set to 0.2, following the definition in Boer and Boomsma (2009).

## Key numbers

- In the original PNJL model at \(\theta = \Theta = 0\), the chiral restoration temperature \(T_\chi\) is larger than the deconfinement temperature \(T_{\rm d}\) by \(\Delta T_{\rm \chi d} \equiv T_\chi - T_{\rm d} \sim 40\) MeV.
- In the original PNJL model at \(\theta = \pi\) and \(\Theta = \pi/3\), the P restoration temperature \(T_{\rm P}\) is larger than the C violation temperature \(T_{\rm C}\) by \(\Delta T_{\rm PC} \equiv T_{\rm P} - T_{\rm C} \sim 80\) MeV.
- In the EPNJL model at \(\theta = \Theta = 0\), \(\Delta T_{\rm \chi d} \equiv T_\chi - T_{\rm d} \sim 0\) MeV.
- In the EPNJL model at \(\theta = \pi\) and \(\Theta = \pi/3\), \(\Delta T_{\rm PC} \equiv T_{\rm P} - T_{\rm C} \sim 8\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of C violation at \(\Theta = \pi/3\) (related to the Roberge-Weiss transition, first studied by Roberge and Weiss (1986)) and P violation at \(\theta = \pi\) (the Dashen phenomenon, analyzed in the NJL model by Boer and Boomsma (2009)) to the PNJL and EPNJL frameworks. The results suggest a strong correlation between the C violation/P restoration at finite imaginary chemical potential and \(\theta\)-vacuum and the deconfinement/chiral restoration at zero chemical potential and \(\theta = 0\), enabling future studies of the relation between these transitions in QCD.
