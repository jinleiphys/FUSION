# Transverse momentum moments

> We establish robust relations between Transverse Momentum Dependent distributions (TMDs) and collinear distributions. We define weighted integrals of TMDs that we call Transverse Momentum Moments (TMMs) and prove that TMMs are equal to collinear distributions evaluated in some minimal subtraction scheme. The conversion to the modified minimal subtraction ($\bar{MS}$) scheme, can be done by a calculable factor, which we derive up to three loops for some cases. We discuss in detail the zeroth, the first, and the second TMMs and provide phenomenological results for them based on the current extractions of TMDs. The results of this paper open new avenues for theoretical and phenomenological investigation of the three-dimensional and collinear hadron structures.

# Transverse momentum moments

## Key claim
The paper establishes that weighted integrals of Transverse Momentum Dependent distributions (TMDs) with an upper cutoff, called Transverse Momentum Moments (TMMs), are exactly equal to collinear parton distribution functions evaluated in a specific minimal subtraction scheme (the TMD scheme), provided the perturbative scale is high enough to neglect power-suppressed terms of order μ⁻². The conversion between the TMD scheme and the standard MS-bar scheme requires a calculable factor derived up to three loops for the zeroth moment.

## Method
The authors define TMMs as integrals of TMDs over transverse momentum with an upper cutoff μ, using either the ζ-prescription (optimal TMDs at vanishing Collins-Soper kernel) or the equal-scale prescription (all scales set to μ). They prove equivalence to collinear matrix elements by showing that the Collins-Soper kernel dependence cancels in these specific scale choices, and derive the matching coefficients between the TMD scheme and the MS-bar scheme using operator product expansion techniques. For the zeroth moment (n=0), they compute the matching factor up to N³LO; for the first moment (n=1), they relate the Sivers TMD to the Qiu-Sterman function; for the second moment (n=2), they develop a subtraction procedure for power divergences in the unpolarized case.

## Key numbers
- The matching coefficient between the TMD scheme and MS-bar scheme for the zeroth TMM is derived up to three loops (N³LO) in the strong coupling αₛ.
- The first TMM of the Sivers function is computed using the extraction of the Sivers function at N³LO from Bury (2021), yielding the Qiu-Sterman function and average transverse momentum shifts of quarks in a transversely polarized proton.
- The second TMM for unpolarized TMDs is validated phenomenologically using the ART23 extraction from Moos (2023), which achieves approximate N⁴LL (next-to-next-to-next-to-next-to-leading logarithm) accuracy.
- Power-suppressed corrections to the TMM-collinear equivalence are of order μ⁻², where μ is the cutoff scale.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework relating three-dimensional TMDs to one-dimensional collinear PDFs, building on the operator product expansion approach of Ebert (2020, 2022) and the ζ-prescription formalism of Scimemi (2018). It provides a rigorous phenomenological bridge that earlier model-based approaches (e.g., Gamberg 2022, Cammarota 2020) lacked, and enables direct comparison between TMD extractions (such as ART23 from Moos 2023) and collinear PDF analyses. The results open avenues for using TMMs as benchmarks for lattice QCD calculations and for systematic inclusion of TMD information in global collinear PDF fits.
