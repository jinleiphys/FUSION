# Reply to the comment of P. Lipavsky

> The critics of P. Lipavsky on the derivation of conservation laws including gradient corrections is refuted since his counterexample is based on a mathematical error. Instead, the derived conservation laws for density, momentum and energy remain valid even without using the objected relation. Only for the entropy local terms are missing which vanish under integration.

## Key claim
The conservation laws for density, momentum, and energy derived in Morawetz (2017) remain valid even without using the objected mathematical identity (46), but local terms for entropy are missing that vanish under time integration.

## Method
The author refutes Lipavsky's criticism by showing that the counterexample contains a mathematical error: assuming δ(x)f(x)=0 implies f(x)=0 for Dirac-δ functions. The derivation of the key relation (46) is repeated, showing it holds under integration over the corresponding variable. For density, momentum, and energy, the author demonstrates that the same results are obtained without using relation (46) by employing transformation B (interchange of final and initial coordinates) and conservation laws contained in the δ-function.

## Key numbers
- The additional term K = (ξ₁+ξ₂)/4 D[∂_t Δ_t(E) - ∂_t Δ_t(E')] is shown to be zero for density (ξ=1), momentum (ξ=k), and energy (ξ=ε)
- For entropy, the additional term leads to a gain term and a symmetrized collision term ∼ N, both fitting the H-theorem proof in Morawetz (2017)

## Context
This reply addresses the comment by Lipavsky (2017) concerning missing power feed from time-dependent external potentials and an allegedly wrongly used mathematical identity in Morawetz (2017). The work extends the derivation of conservation laws including gradient corrections for binary-correlated terms that survive under integration. The author notes that comparison with papers expressing binary correlated entropy in terms of pair-correlation functions has not yet been worked out and requires translation into phase-shift expressions.
