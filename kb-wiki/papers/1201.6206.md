# QCD critical point in the strong coupling lattice QCD and during black hole formation

> We discuss the QCD phase diagram from two different point of view. We first investigate the phase diagram structure in the strong coupling lattice QCD with Polyakov loop effects, and show that the the chiral and Z_{N_c} deconfinement transition boundaries deviate at finite mu as suggested from large N_c arguments. Next we discuss the possibility to probe the QCD critical point during prompt black hole formation processes. The thermodynamical evolution during the black hole formation would result in quark matter formation, and the critical point in isospin asymmetric matter may be swept. (T,mu_B) region probed in heavy-ion collisions and the black hole formation processes covers most of the critical point locations predicted in recent lattice Monte-Carlo simulations and chiral effective models.

# QCD critical point in the strong coupling lattice QCD and during black hole formation

## Key claim
The chiral and Z_{N_c} deconfinement transition boundaries deviate at finite baryon chemical potential in strong coupling lattice QCD with Polyakov loop effects, supporting the existence of quarkyonic-like matter; additionally, the QCD critical point in isospin asymmetric matter may be swept during prompt black hole formation processes, but this sweep depends on whether the critical point lies in the low-temperature, high-chemical-potential region as predicted by chiral effective models rather than in the low-chemical-potential region suggested by some lattice Monte Carlo simulations.

## Method
The authors performed a mean-field analysis of strong coupling lattice QCD (SC-LQCD) including next-to-leading order (NLO, O(1/g^2)) fermionic and leading order pure gluonic contributions, with Polyakov loop effects incorporated (P-SC-LQCD). They also computed the thermodynamic potential of the Polyakov loop extended quark meson (PQM) model in the mean field approximation, including vector meson interactions, to map the first-order phase boundary and critical point location in (T, mu_B, delta_mu) space. The BH formation profile was obtained from neutrino-radiation hydrodynamical simulations of a 40 solar mass star collapse.

## Key numbers
- Critical point in P-SC-LQCD at beta=4 in the chiral limit: (mu_CP, T_CP) = (0.58, 0.19) in lattice units.
- The Z_{N_c}-induced crossover temperature T_Q ~ 0.52 in lattice units at beta=4, nearly independent of chemical potential and quark mass.
- BH formation reaches T ~ 90 MeV and baryon density rho_B ~ 4 rho_0 in the collapse of a 40 M_sun star.
- Baryon chemical potential mu_B reaches approximately 1000, 1100, and 1300 MeV at t = 0.5, 1.0, and 1.344 sec after bounce, respectively.
- Isospin chemical potential delta_mu = (50-130) MeV in the inner region of the proto-neutron star.
- In the PQM model, the critical temperature T_CP reaches zero at delta_mu_c ~ (50-80) MeV.
- The vector coupling shifts mu_c upward by about 10-15 MeV at g_v/g = 0.2.

## Context
This work extends the strong coupling lattice QCD framework of Langelage and others by incorporating Polyakov loop effects (P-SC-LQCD) to study the separation of chiral and deconfinement transitions at finite density, building on the quarkyonic matter concept from McLerran and Pisarski (2007). The black hole formation analysis extends the hydrodynamical simulations of Sumiyoshi and collaborators (2007) by comparing the thermodynamic trajectory with critical point predictions from lattice Monte Carlo studies (LR04, LT04, LC11) and chiral effective models including NJL, P-NJL, P-NJL with 8-quark interactions, and PQM models. The results suggest that black hole formation could probe critical points in the high-mu_B, low-T region inaccessible to heavy-ion collisions, but the comparison relies on hadronic equations of state and should be verified with combined quark-hadron EOS.
