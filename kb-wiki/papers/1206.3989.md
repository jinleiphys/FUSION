# Effect of Light Fermions on the Confinement Transition in QCD-like Theories

> Dependence of the confinement transition parameters on the fermion content provides information on the mechanism of confinement. Recent progress in lattice gauge theories has allowed to study it for light flavor number $N_f\sim O(10)$ and found this transition to shift toward significantly stronger coupling. We propose an explanation for that: light fermions can occupy the chromo-magnetic monopoles, via zero modes, making them "distinguishable" and unsuitable for Bose-Einstein Condensation. Such dilution of unoccuplied monopoles is compensated by stronger coupling that makes them lighter and more numerous. We also suggest that flavor-carrying quark-monopole objects account for the density beyond quark Fermi sphere seen in cold dense phase of $N_c=2$ lattice QCD.

## Key claim

Light fermions occupy zero modes on chromo-magnetic monopoles, making those monopoles "distinguishable" and thus unsuitable for Bose-Einstein Condensation (BEC); the resulting dilution of unoccupied monopoles must be compensated by stronger coupling, which shifts the confinement transition to significantly stronger coupling as the number of light fermion flavors increases.

## Method

The authors model the confinement transition as BEC of chromo-magnetic monopoles and apply the Feynman criterion for BEC onset, which depends on the exchange action between neighboring bosons. They incorporate the effect of light fermions by reducing the effective density of "pure" (unoccupied) monopoles by a factor \((1+f)^{2 N_f N_M}\), where \(f\) is a zero-mode fugacity and \(N_M\) is the number of zero modes per flavor. Using magnetic scaling relations (\(m^*/T \sim 1/g\), \(n^{1/3}/T \sim g^2\)), they derive an expression for the critical lattice coupling \(\beta_c\) as a function of \(N_f\) and fit it to lattice data from Miura et al. (2011).

## Key numbers

* Critical lattice coupling \(\beta_c\) shifts from pure gauge (\(N_f=0\)) to \(N_f=12\) by a substantial factor, with data from Miura et al. (2011) shown as blue boxes in Fig. 1.
* Fit of the model \(\beta_c(N_f) = \beta_0 (1+f)^{-8 N_f N_M/15}\) to the Miura et al. (2011) data yields optimal zero-mode fugacity \(f \approx 0.154\).
* The suppression factor \(f \approx 0.154\) is close to the estimate \(e^{-\Delta M/T} \sim 0.135\) from the mass difference \(\Delta M \sim 2 T_c\) between electric and magnetic particles near \(T_c\).
* For \(N_c=2\) theory with two adjoint fermions, the infrared fixed point coupling is \(1/g^2_* = 0.20(4)(3)\), about a factor 4 stronger than the critical coupling for deconfinement in the \(N_c=2\) pure gauge theory.
* Estimated contribution to the quark number susceptibility from monopole-quark states: \(\chi^{m-q}_2 \approx (n/T^3) \cdot 2f/(1+f)^{N_f N_M} \sim 0.4 - 0.8\), using total monopole density near \(T_c\) of \(n/T^3 \sim 2 - 4\) and \(N_f N_M = 3\).

## Context

This work extends the dual superconductor picture of confinement ('t Hooft, Mandelstam) and the BEC-of-monopoles scenario developed by Liao and Shuryak (2006) and Cristoforetti and Shuryak (2009). It explains the systematic shift of the critical coupling with flavor number observed in lattice simulations by Miura et al. (2011) for \(N_f=0\) to \(N_f=12\), and by other groups for \(N_f=2,3,8\). The mechanism predicts an approximate "\(N_f N_M\) scaling" that can be tested with different fermion representations, and suggests that monopole-quark states account for the excess quark density beyond the Fermi sphere seen in cold dense \(N_c=2\) lattice QCD by Hands et al. (2011).
