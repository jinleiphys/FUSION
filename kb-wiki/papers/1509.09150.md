# Study of the $f_2(1270)$ and $a_2(1320)$ resonances in $╬│^*(Q^2)╬│$ collisions

> We discuss studies of the $Q^2$ dependence of the $f_2(1270)$ and $a_2 (1320)$ production cross sections in $╬│^*(Q^2)╬│$ collisions at current and coming into operation colliders with a high luminosity. Changing the dominant helicity amplitude occurs in the reactions $╬│^*(Q ^2)╬│\to f_2(1270)$ and $╬│^*(Q^2) ╬│\to a_2(1320)$ with increasing $Q^2$. This is caused by the coming of the QCD asymptotics. It is shown that the transition to the asymptotic behavior of QCD in the amplitudes $╬│^*(Q^2) ╬│\to f_2(1270),a_2(1320)$ is provided by the compensation of the contributions of ground vector states $Žü$ and $Žē$ in $Q^2$-channel with the contributions of their radial excitations.

## Key claim

The transition to QCD asymptotic behavior in the amplitudes for $\gamma^*(Q^2)\gamma \to f_2(1270)$ and $\gamma^*(Q^2)\gamma \to a_2(1320)$ is provided by the compensation of the contributions of ground vector states $\rho$ and $\omega$ in the $Q^2$-channel with the contributions of their radial excitations, but the paper notes that deviations from this picture are possible in principle since the tensor meson production can be described by three independent invariant amplitudes.

## Method

The authors used a generalized vector meson dominance model (GVDM) to calculate the $Q^2$ dependence of the helicity amplitudes $V^{(\lambda)}_{\lambda_1,\lambda_2}(T; s, Q^2)$ for the $\gamma^*\gamma \to T$ vertices, where $T = f_2(1270)$ or $a_2(1320)$. They employed an effective gauge-invariant Lagrangian that describes tensor meson production by two photons with opposite helicities only, and constructed a simple form factor $F_T(Q^2) = 1/[(1+Q^2/m^2_\rho)(1+Q^2/m^2_{\rho'})]$ with $m_\rho = 0.775$ GeV and $m_{\rho'} = 1.465$ GeV to model the transition to asymptotic QCD behavior.

## Key numbers

- The $f_2(1270)$ and $a_2(1320)$ production in $\gamma\gamma$ collisions with helicity $\lambda = \pm2$ is more than 95% of the total cross section.
- The two-photon width of the $f_2(1270)$ is approximately 3 keV, while the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ have $\gamma\gamma$ widths of approximately 0.29 keV and 0.3 keV respectively.
- The GVDM calculation uses $m_\rho = 0.775$ GeV and $m_{\rho'} = 1.465$ GeV for the form factor.
- For $Q^2 \gtrsim 10$ GeV$^2$, the vertex function with helicity $\lambda = 0$ becomes the dominant one and close to its asymptotic value.
- The analysis covers the range $0 < Q^2 < 40$ GeV$^2$, with the asymptotic regime for $\pi^0$, $\eta$, $\eta'$ apparently occurring near 40 GeV$^2$.
- The Belle data on $\gamma^*(Q^2)\gamma \to f_2(1270)$ for $Q^2$ up to 30 GeV$^2$ are compared with the theoretical curves and show satisfactory agreement.

## Context

This work extends the study of two-photon physics from real photons to virtual photons, building on the experimental results from the Belle Collaboration on $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^+\pi^-$ (Mo 2007), $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^0\pi^0$ (Ue 2008), and $\gamma\gamma \to \pi^0\eta$ (Ue 2009) that established the small two-photon widths of the $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ resonances. The paper provides a framework for analyzing upcoming high-luminosity data from the SuperKEKB factory (planned luminosity $8\times10^{35}$ cm$^{-2}\cdot$c$^{-1}$) and enables experimental tests of QCD asymptotics in tensor meson production by measuring the transition from $\sin^4\theta$ angular distributions at $Q^2=0$ to $\frac{2}{3}(3\cos^2\theta-1)^2$ distributions at large $Q^2$.
