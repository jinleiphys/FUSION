# Finite temperature effects in antiferromagnetism of nuclear matter

> The influence of the finite temperature on the antiferromagnetic (AFM) spin ordering in symmetric nuclear matter with the effective Gogny interaction is studied within the framework of a Fermi liquid formalism. It is shown that the AFM spin polarization parameter of partially polarized nuclear matter for low enough temperatures increases with temperature. The entropy of the AFM spin state for some temperature range is larger than the entropy of the normal state. Nerveless, the free energy of the AFM spin state is always less than the free energy of the normal state and, hence, the AFM spin polarized state is preferable for all temperatures below the critical temperature.

## Key claim

The antiferromagnetic (AFM) spin polarization parameter in symmetric nuclear matter can increase with temperature at low enough temperatures, contrary to the expectation that thermal fluctuations destroy spin ordering; this behavior occurs only for partially polarized states at zero temperature, and the AFM spin state remains thermodynamically preferable over the normal state for all temperatures below the critical temperature.

## Method

The author solved self-consistent Fermi liquid equations for symmetric nuclear matter using the effective Gogny interaction D1S, expanding the normal distribution function and single-particle energy in Pauli matrices in spin and isospin space. The four components of the single-particle energy were determined from the normal Fermi liquid amplitudes, and the chemical potentials, ferromagnetic order parameter, and antiferromagnetic order parameter were found jointly with the normalization conditions. The free energy and entropy of the AFM spin state were then compared to those of the normal state as functions of temperature at fixed densities.

## Key numbers

- The AFM spin state in symmetric nuclear matter with the D1S Gogny interaction appears at a density of approximately 3.8 times the nuclear matter saturation density (3.8 rho_0, where rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}) at zero temperature.
- The nuclear matter saturation density is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
- At zero temperature, the Fermi surface radii k_1 and k_2 satisfy the relations (1/(3 pi^2))(k_2^3 - k_1^3) = Delta_rho_updownarrow and (1/(3 pi^2))(k_1^3 + k_2^3) = rho.

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature study of AFM spin ordering in symmetric nuclear matter with the Gogny interaction by Isayev (2004), which found that the AFM spin state appears at approximately 3.8 rho_0. It contrasts with earlier studies using Skyrme forces (e.g., Vidaurre et al. 1984, Bonche and Vautherin 1981) that predicted ferromagnetic transitions at densities of 2-4 rho_0, and with Isayev (2003) which found that ferromagnetic ordering is thermodynamically preferable to AFM ordering for Skyrme interactions. The anomalous entropy behavior observed here is analogous to that found in superfluid asymmetric nuclear matter by Sedrakian and Lombardo (2000). The results cannot be directly extrapolated to proto-neutron stars because only symmetric nuclear matter was considered; for strongly isospin-asymmetric systems the D1P parametrization of the Gogny force would be more appropriate.
