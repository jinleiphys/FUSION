# Alpha-Alpha scattering, chiral symmetry and 8Be lifetime

> Alpha-alpha scattering is discussed in terms of a chiral two pion exchange potential which turns out to be attractive and singular at the origin, hence demanding renormalization. When 8Be is treated as a resonance state a model independent correlation between the Q-factor and lifetime 1/Gamma for the decay into two alpha particles arises. For a wide range of parameters compatible with potential model analyses of low energy pi-alpha scattering it is found Gamma = 4.4(4)eV} in fairly good agreement with the experimental value Gamma = 5.57 (25) eV. The remaining discrepancy as well as the phase shift up to ELAB=15MeV could be accommodated by the leading nuclear peripheral contributions due to the 3H+p and 3He+n continuum.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a chiral two-pion exchange (TPE) potential, which is attractive and singular at the origin, can provide the bulk of the 8Be lifetime, but that leading nuclear structure effects from the 3H+p and 3He+n continuum are needed to fully reproduce the experimental width of 5.57(25) eV and the phase shifts up to 15 MeV.

## Method

The author constructs an effective field theory for alpha-alpha scattering using a heavy-alpha field and chiral symmetry, deriving the two-pion exchange potential from a Lagrangean with three dimensionless couplings (g0, g1, g2) estimated from low-energy pion-alpha scattering. The singular TPE potential is renormalized by imposing a real, energy-independent short-distance boundary condition, and the 8Be resonance is treated both via the Breit-Wigner approximation and as a pole of the S-matrix in the complex energy plane. Leading nuclear corrections from triangle diagrams involving the 3H+p and 3He+n continuum are added to improve agreement with data.

## Key numbers

- Experimental 8Be resonance Q-factor: Q = 91.84(4) KeV
- Experimental Breit-Wigner width: Gamma_BW = 5.57(25) eV
- Calculated Breit-Wigner width from TPE potential: Gamma = 4.3(3) eV
- Calculated S-matrix pole width from TPE potential: Gamma_pole = 3.4(2) eV
- Experimental scattering length: alpha_0 = -1630(150) fm
- Theoretical scattering length from TPE: alpha_0_th = -1210(70) fm
- Experimental effective range: r_0 = 1.08(1) fm
- Theoretical effective range from TPE: r_0_th = 1.03(1) fm
- Bohr radius: a_B = 3.63 fm
- Alpha-particle matter radius: r_alpha_em = 1.668(5) fm
- Couplings from pion-alpha scattering: g_0 = -82(11), g_1 = -5.3(3), g_2 = 77(12)

## Context

This work extends the application of chiral effective field theory to nuclear systems beyond the nucleon-nucleon case, specifically to alpha-alpha scattering and the 8Be resonance. It builds on prior potential model analyses of low-energy pion-alpha scattering (Khankhasaev 1989) and earlier phenomenological treatments of alpha-alpha interactions such as the square well with hard core (Kermode 1965), Woods-Saxon (Kukulin 1971), and Gaussian potentials. The approach enables a model-independent correlation between the Q-factor and lifetime of 8Be, and suggests that further work on pion-alpha and nucleon-alpha scattering is needed to determine the couplings more precisely and resolve the remaining discrepancy with experiment.
