# Nucleon-nucleon interaction: Central potential and pion production

> We show that the tail of the chiral two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential is proportional to the $πN$ scalar form factor and discuss how it can be translated into effective scalar meson interactions. We then construct a kernel for the process $NN\toπNN$, due to the exchange of two pions, which may be used in either three body forces or pion production in NN scattering. Our final expression involves a partial cancellation among three terms, due to chiral symmetry, but the net result is still important. We also find that, at large internucleon distances, the kernel has the same spatial dependence as the central NN potential and we produce expressions relating these processes directly.

## Key claim

The tail of the chiral two-pion exchange nucleon-nucleon potential is proportional to the πN scalar form factor, and the corresponding two-pion-exchange kernel for the process NN→πNN involves a partial cancellation among three large terms due to chiral symmetry, but the net result remains important.

## Method

The authors derived the central two-pion exchange potential (TPEP) from the isoscalar NN amplitude, expressing its long-range part in terms of the πN scalar form factor σ(t). They then constructed a covariant kernel for NN→πNN by evaluating the leading contributions from πN→πN and πN→ππN subamplitudes, using chiral symmetry and including pion-pole, contact, and z-graph diagrams. The kernel was reduced to a non-relativistic form and related directly to the central potential at large internucleon distances.

## Key numbers

- The scalar form factor σ(t) is related to the πN σ-term by σ(0)=σ_N, with its value at the Cheng-Dashen point t=2μ² extractable from experiment.
- Effective scalar meson parameters derived from input data: g_s values range from 4.36 to 9.09, and m_s values range from 393 MeV to 564 MeV (Table 1, using inputs from H83, GSS, GLS, Kauf+99, Pav+99).
- Using α₀₀⁺ = 3.68 (H83), the non-relativistic kernel is t₁ = i (g_A/f_π) (t^S/2m) { -1.18 σ·(p'−p) + 0.18 σ·q }^(1).
- Using α₀₀⁺ = 6.74 (Kauf+99), the kernel is t₁ = i (g_A/f_π) (t^S/2m) { -0.87 σ·(p'−p) - 0.13 σ·q }^(1).

## Context

This work extends the chiral two-pion exchange framework for the NN potential developed by Ordonez, Ray, and van Kolck (1994) and by Robilotta and collaborators, and it builds on the three-body potential of Coon, Peña, and Riska (1995) which used effective scalar meson exchange. The derived kernel can be applied to both three-body forces and threshold pion production in NN scattering, addressing the long-standing underprediction of cross sections seen in earlier Koltun-Reitan (1966) and heavy-baryon ChPT calculations by van Kolck (1996) and others. The direct relation between the kernel and the central potential enables consistent use with any realistic NN potential.
