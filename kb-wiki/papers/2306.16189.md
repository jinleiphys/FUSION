# Intruder configurations in $^{29}$Ne at the transition into the island of inversion: Detailed structure study of $^{28}$Ne

> Detailed $γ$-ray spectroscopy of the exotic neon isotope $^{28}$Ne has been performed for the first time using the one-neutron removal reaction from $^{29}$Ne on a liquid hydrogen target at 240~MeV/nucleon. Based on an analysis of parallel momentum distributions, a level scheme with spin-parity assignments has been constructed for $^{28}$Ne and the negative-parity states are identified for the first time. The measured partial cross sections and momentum distributions reveal a significant intruder $p$-wave strength providing evidence of the breakdown of the $N=20$ and $N=28$ shell gaps. Only a weak, possible $f$-wave strength was observed to bound final states. Large-scale shell-model calculations with different effective interactions do not reproduce the large $p$-wave and small $f$-wave strength observed experimentally, indicating an ongoing challenge for a complete theoretical description of the transition into the island of inversion along the Ne isotopic chain.

## Key claim

The one-neutron removal reaction from 29Ne on a proton target reveals a significant intruder p-wave strength in 28Ne, providing evidence for the breakdown of both the N=20 and N=28 shell gaps, but large-scale shell-model calculations with different effective interactions fail to reproduce the experimentally observed large p-wave and small f-wave strength, indicating an ongoing challenge for theoretical description of the transition into the island of inversion along the Ne isotopic chain.

## Method

In-beam gamma-ray spectroscopy of 28Ne was performed using the one-neutron removal reaction from a 29Ne beam at 240 MeV/nucleon on a liquid hydrogen target surrounded by the DALI2 NaI(Tl) array for gamma-ray detection and the MINOS time projection chamber for reaction vertex reconstruction. The reaction residues were identified using the SAMURAI spectrometer, and parallel momentum distributions of the 28Ne residues were analyzed in coincidence with gamma-ray transitions to assign spin-parities to the observed states. The measured partial cross sections were compared to distorted wave impulse approximation (DWIA) and eikonal model calculations using spectroscopic factors from shell-model calculations with SDPF-M, SDPF-U-MIX, and EEdf1 interactions.

## Key numbers

* Inclusive cross section for one-neutron removal from 29Ne to all bound states of 28Ne: 28(1) mb
* Ground-state cross section: 8.4(8) mb, assigned to 2p3/2 neutron removal
* 2+1 state at 1323 keV: cross section 3.6(3) mb, with a fit allowing 71 +/- 15% p-wave and 29 +/- 15% f-wave contributions (p-wave 2.6(6) mb, f-wave 1.0(5) mb)
* Summed experimental 2p3/2 spectroscopic factors: 1.82(7) using DWIA single-particle cross sections, 1.16(5) using eikonal single-particle cross sections
* Excited states identified at 2441 keV (2.9(1) mb), 2923 keV (7.7(2) mb), 3055 keV (0.1(1) mb), 3401 keV (1.4(1) mb), 3825 keV (1.3(1) mb), and 4006 keV (2.5(1) mb)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of intruder configurations in neutron-rich Ne isotopes, particularly following the identification of pf-shell intruder strength in 28Ne by Terry (2008) and the dominant p-wave ground-state configuration in 29Ne reported by Kobayashi (2016). The results contradict shell-model predictions from SDPF-M, SDPF-U-MIX, and EEdf1 interactions, which all underestimate the observed p-wave strength and overestimate f-wave contributions to bound states. The findings enable quantitative tests of theoretical descriptions of the island of inversion and provide data for comparing proton-induced nucleon removal reactions with the more extensively studied reactions on carbon targets.
