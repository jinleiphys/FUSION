# The nucleon spin and momentum decomposition using lattice QCD simulations

> We determine within lattice QCD, the nucleon spin carried by valence and sea quarks, and gluons. The calculation is performed using an ensemble of gauge configurations with two degenerate light quarks with mass fixed to approximately reproduce the physical pion mass. We find that the total angular momentum carried by the quarks in the nucleon is $J_{u+d+s}{=}0.408(61)_{\rm stat.}(48)_{\rm syst.}$ and the gluon contribution is $J_g {=}0.133(11)_{\rm stat.}(14)_{\rm syst.}$ giving a total of $J_N{=}0.54(6)_{\rm stat.}(5)_{\rm syst.}$ consistent with the spin sum. For the quark intrinsic spin contribution we obtain $\frac{1}{2}ΔΣ_{u+d+s}{=}0.201(17)_{\rm stat.}(5)_{\rm syst.}$. All quantities are given in the $\overline{\textrm{MS}}$ scheme at 2~GeV. The quark and gluon momentum fractions are also computed and add up to $\langle x\rangle_{u+d+s}+\langle x\rangle_g{=}0.804(121)_{\rm stat.}(95)_{\rm syst.}+0.267(12)_{\rm stat.}(10)_{\rm syst.}{=}1.07(12)_{\rm stat.}(10)_{\rm syst.}$ satisfying the momentum sum.

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD simulations with two degenerate light quarks at approximately the physical pion mass, the authors find that the total nucleon spin sum of quarks and gluons is consistent with 1/2, resolving the long-standing proton spin puzzle, but the results are obtained from a single ensemble and thus cannot directly assess finite volume and lattice spacing effects.

## Method

The authors compute nucleon matrix elements of the axial-vector operator (for quark intrinsic spin) and the one-derivative vector operator (for quark total angular momentum and momentum fraction) using three-point correlation functions on a 48^3×96 lattice with lattice spacing a=0.0938(3) fm. Disconnected quark loop contributions are evaluated using stochastic techniques with exact deflation for light quarks and the truncated solver method for strange and charm quarks, while gluon contributions are computed from the gluon operator with stout smearing. Renormalization is performed non-perturbatively in the RI'-MOM scheme for quark operators and perturbatively at one-loop for the gluon operator, with excited state contamination assessed via plateau, summation, and two-state fit methods.

- Quark total angular momentum: J_{u+d+s} = 0.408(61)_stat.(48)_syst.

- Isovector momentum fraction: ⟨x⟩_{u-d} = 0.194(9)(11)
- Isovector generalized form factor: B_{20}^{u-d}(0) = 0.313(19)
- All quantities in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon structure by providing the first complete decomposition of nucleon spin and momentum at the physical pion mass, including both valence and sea quark contributions as well as gluons. The results are consistent with phenomenological analyses from COMPASS (Adolph 2015) and global fits (Alekhin 2017), and agree with earlier lattice calculations of the gluon helicity by Yang (2016) using a different approach. The computation enables future studies with multiple ensembles to assess systematic uncertainties from finite volume, lattice spacing, and strange quark quenching effects.
