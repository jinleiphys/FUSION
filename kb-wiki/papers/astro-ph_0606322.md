# Rotochemical Heating of Neutron Stars: Rigorous Formalism with Electrostatic Potential Perturbations

> The electrostatic potential that keeps approximate charge neutrality in neutron star matter is self-consistently introduced into the formalism for rotochemical heating presented in a previous paper by Fernandez and Reisenegger. Although the new formalism is more rigorous, we show that its observable consequences are indistinguishable from those of the previous one, leaving the conclusions of the previous paper unchanged.

# Rotochemical Heating of Neutron Stars: Rigorous Formalism with Electrostatic Potential Perturbations

## Key claim

The electrostatic potential perturbations that maintain charge neutrality in neutron star matter can be self-consistently incorporated into the rotochemical heating formalism, but the observable consequences are indistinguishable from the earlier, less rigorous formalism of Fernandez and Reisenegger (2005). The quasi-steady state reached by old neutron stars is strictly unaffected, and the time-dependent evolution at earlier times shows only quantitatively negligible changes.

## Method

The authors introduce the electrostatic potential perturbation δψ into the chemical potential relations of the rotochemical heating formalism, modifying the coefficients that connect particle number perturbations δN_i to chemical imbalance variables η^∞_{npl}. They derive new expressions for the matrix elements B_{ij} that relate δN_i to δμ^∞_j, incorporating charge neutrality constraints, and recalculate the Z-coefficients (Z_{np}, Z_{npe}, Z_{npμ}) that determine the evolution equations. The formalism is applied to neutron star models using the A18+δυ+UIX* equation of state, comparing time-dependent thermal evolution for stars of different masses undergoing magnetic dipole spin-down.

## Key numbers

- The electrostatic potential scale is |ψ| ~ μ_e/e ~ 10^8 V, set by the electron chemical potential μ_e ≈ 10^2 MeV at typical interior densities n_e ~ 10^37 cm^{-3}
- The excess charge density required to produce the electrostatic potential is ~ μ_e/(eR)^2 ~ 10^2 cm^{-3}, compared to the charged species density ~ 10^37 cm^{-3}
- The fractional changes in the coefficients Z_{npe} and Z_{npμ} between the old and new formalisms reach up to 10-20% at the largest stellar masses
- The gravitational potential perturbation δΦ from chemical imbalance is about 50 times smaller than the intrinsic chemical potential perturbation term, based on a 1.6 M_⊙ model with 1% excess electron and proton density

## Context

This work extends the rotochemical heating formalism developed by Fernandez and Reisenegger (2005, FR05), which was the first to go beyond order-of-magnitude estimates and calculate thermal evolution of neutron stars with full general relativistic structure and realistic equations of state. The correction addresses a formal incompleteness in FR05 that ignored electrostatic potential perturbations, following a suggestion by Michael Gusakov. The result confirms that the earlier conclusions about rotochemical heating in millisecond pulsars like PSR J0437-4720 (Kargaltsev 2004) remain valid, and that the quasi-steady state prediction of Reisenegger (1995) is robust. The corrected formalism enables more accurate calculations of the approach to quasi-steady state and the evolution of younger neutron stars with stronger magnetic fields.
