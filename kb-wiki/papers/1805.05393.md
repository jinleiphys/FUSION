# Effects of thermonuclear X-ray bursts on non-burst emissions in the soft state of 4U 1728--34

> It has recently been shown that the persistent emission of a neutron star low-mass X-ray binary (LMXB) evolves during a thermonuclear (type-I) X-ray burst. The reason of this evolution, however, is not securely known. This uncertainty can introduce significant systematics in the neutron star radius measurement using burst spectra, particularly if an unknown but significant fraction of the burst emission, which is reprocessed, contributes to the changes in the persistent emission during the burst. Here, by analyzing individual burst data of AstroSat/LAXPC from the neutron star LMXB 4U 1728--34 in the soft state, we show that the burst emission is not significantly reprocessed by a corona covering the neutron star. Rather, our analysis suggests that the burst emission enhances the accretion disk emission, possibly by increasing the accretion rate via disk. This enhanced disk emission, which is Comptonized by a corona covering the disk, can explain an increased persistent emission observed during the burst. This finding provides an understanding of persistent emission components, and their interaction with the thermonuclear burst emission. Furthermore, since burst photons are not significantly reprocessed, non-burst and burst emissions can be reliably separated, which is required to reduce systematic uncertainties in the stellar radius measurement.

## Key claim

The paper shows that during thermonuclear X-ray bursts from 4U 1728–34 in the soft state, the burst emission is not significantly reprocessed by a corona covering the neutron star; instead, the burst emission likely enhances the accretion disk emission by increasing the accretion rate via the disk, and this enhanced disk emission, Comptonized by a corona covering the disk, explains the increased persistent emission observed during the burst.

## Method

The authors analyzed individual burst data from AstroSat/LAXPC for five thermonuclear bursts detected in 61.05 ks of exposure, using spectral fitting with the Comptonization model nthcomp in XSPEC. They compared the conventional method (using preburst spectrum as background) with the "f_a method" (scaling the preburst model with a free factor f_a), and then tested two scenarios: one where a Comptonizing layer covers the neutron star and reprocesses burst photons, and another where a Comptonizing layer covers the disk and reprocesses disk emission. The fits were evaluated using chi-squared per degree of freedom, with 90% parameter errors estimated via MCMC.

## Key numbers

- Five thermonuclear bursts were detected in 61.05 ks of useful LAXPC exposure from 4U 1728–34.
- The preburst spectrum was fit with nthcomp using a fixed hydrogen column density of 2.3 × 10^22 cm^-2.
- For the reprocessing of burst emission scenario, chi-squared/dof values were typically ~100 (unacceptable fits), with values ranging from ~3 to 800 across all time bins and bursts.
- For the disk-corona scenario, chi-squared/dof values were much smaller and fits were acceptable for burst-1 and the other four bursts.
- The burst blackbody normalization was usually lower for the f_a method than for the conventional method, indicating systematic error in the conventional approach.

## Context

This work extends the findings of Worpel et al. (2013, 2015) who showed that persistent flux changes during bursts, and contrasts with the spreading layer model proposed by Kajava et al. (2017a) which implies significant reprocessing of burst photons. The paper also relates to studies of hard X-ray deficits during bursts in the hard state by Ji et al. (2013, 2014a, 2014b, 2015) and Kajava et al. (2017b), noting that such deficits are not found in the soft state. The conclusion that burst photons are not significantly reprocessed enables more reliable separation of burst and non-burst emissions, which is required to reduce systematic uncertainties in neutron star radius measurements using burst spectra.
