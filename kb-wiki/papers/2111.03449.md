# Nuclear effects on the $ρ$ meson produced in the inclusive photonuclear reaction

> The hadronic properties of the $ρ$ meson produced in the inclusive photonuclear reaction have been investigated. The elementary reaction occurring in the nucleus is assumed as $γN \to ρ^0 N$. The $ρ$ meson, while propagating through the nucleus, interacts with the nuclear particles, and therefore, the properties of the $ρ$ meson can be modified because of this interaction. Being a short-lived particle, the $ρ$ meson decays to various elementary particles, such as, $e^+e^-$, $π^+π^-$, .... etc. The $e^+e^-$ invariant mass, i.e., the $ρ$ meson mass, distribution spectra have been calculated to extract the information about the parameters, viz., mass and width, of the $ρ$ meson in the nucleus. The calculated results have been compared with the data reported from Jefferson Laboratory.

## Key claim

The calculated differential cross section for the rho meson mass distribution in inclusive photonuclear reactions reproduces the CLAS data only when the rho meson pole mass is reduced from 775.26 MeV to 750.26 MeV (about 3 percent), consistent with the mass-shift parameter reported by the CLAS collaboration; however, the calculation shows no mass-shift in the spectrum due to the rho meson optical potential, only a broadening of the width.

## Method

The author calculates the differential cross section for the dielectron invariant mass distribution from the decay of the rho meson produced in the inclusive photonuclear reaction A(gamma, rho -> e+e-)X. The rho meson propagation through the nucleus is described by a scalar propagator that includes an optical potential, which is constructed by folding the rho-nucleon scattering amplitude with the nuclear density distribution. The elementary gamma N -> rho N production amplitude is connected to the rho N scattering amplitude via the vector meson dominance model, and the Fermi motion of nucleons is incorporated using the nuclear spectral function. The tagged photon beam profile is simulated by weighting six energy bins with relative weights from 13.7 percent to 10.9 percent for photon energies from 1.0 to 3.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- The rho meson pole mass in free space is taken as 775.26 MeV; the calculation uses a reduced value of 750.26 MeV (about 3 percent reduction) to match CLAS data.
- The calculated width of the rho meson mass distribution spectrum Gamma*_rho for C-12 is 163.5 MeV, for Fe-56 is 172.1 MeV, and for Pb-208 is 197.5 MeV.
- The enhancement in the width Delta Gamma_rho due to the rho meson optical potential is 19.6 MeV for C-12, 29.4 MeV for Fe-56, and 54.8 MeV for Pb-208.
- The tagged photon beam weights are 13.7 percent at 1.0 GeV, 23.5 percent at 1.5 GeV, 19.3 percent at 2.0 GeV, 20.1 percent at 2.5 GeV, 12.6 percent at 3.0 GeV, and 10.9 percent at 3.5 GeV.
- The rho meson momentum is restricted to 0.8-3.0 GeV/c, matching the CLAS measurement conditions.

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier study of coherent rho meson photoproduction (Das, 2015) to the inclusive reaction where the final nuclear state is not identified, enabling direct comparison with CLAS data (Nass, 2019). The calculation supports the CLAS finding of enhanced width without mass-shift for the rho meson in nuclei, in contrast to the mass-shift observed for narrow resonances like omega and phi mesons in earlier KEK-PS experiments (Naruki, 2006), which the CLAS collaboration (Wood, 2010) argued suffered from background subtraction issues. The results also contrast with the CB-ELSA collaboration (Nanova, 2012; Thiel, 2013) which found no mass-shift for the omega meson but reported width enhancement.
