# Three- and four-body kaonic nuclear states: Binding energies and widths

> Using separable potentials for $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ interaction, we investigated four-body kaonic nuclear systems such as $K^{-}ppn$ and $K^{-}K^{-}pp$, with the Faddeev AGS method in the momentum representation. The Faddeev calculations are based on the quasi-particle method and the method of the energy dependent pole expansion was used to obtain the separable representation for the integral kernels in the three- and four-body equations. Different types of $\bar{K}N-πΣ$ potentials based on phenomenological and chiral SU(3) approach are used and it was shown that the kaonic nuclear systems under consideration are tightly bound.

## Key claim

Using separable potentials for the $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction and the energy-dependent pole expansion (EDPE) method within the Faddeev AGS framework, the authors find that the four-body kaonic nuclear systems $K^{-}ppn$ and $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ are tightly bound, but the binding energies and widths depend significantly on whether a one-pole or two-pole model of the $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance is used, and on whether the $\bar{K}N$ potential is phenomenological or chiral.

## Method

The authors solved the four-body Faddeev AGS equations in momentum space using the quasi-particle method, with the energy-dependent pole expansion (EDPE) method used to obtain separable representations of the integral kernels for the three- and four-body subsystems. They employed three different separable potentials for the $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction: two phenomenological SIDDHARTA-based potentials (one-pole and two-pole versions) and one chiral SU(3) energy-dependent potential, along with the PEST separable approximation of the Paris nucleon-nucleon potential and a Yamaguchi-type $\bar{K}\bar{K}$ potential. The coupled-channel $\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma$ interaction was approximated by constructing an exact optical $\bar{K}N(-\pi\Sigma)$ potential, and the pole positions were extracted by solving inhomogeneous Faddeev equations at complex energies without integration in the complex momentum plane.

## Key numbers

- $K^{-}ppn$ binding energy with $V^{1,SIDD}_{\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma}$: $73.5$ MeV, width $22.0$ MeV
- $K^{-}ppn$ binding energy with $V^{2,SIDD}_{\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma}$: $58.5$ MeV, width $27.0$ MeV
- $K^{-}ppn$ binding energy with $V^{chiral}_{\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma-\pi\Lambda}$: $41.4$ MeV, width $31.5$ MeV
- $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ binding energy with $V^{1,SIDD}_{\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma}$: $99.2$ MeV, width $11.4$ MeV
- $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ binding energy with $V^{2,SIDD}_{\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma}$: $89.0$ MeV, width $11.4$ MeV
- $K^{-}K^{-}pp$ binding energy with $V^{chiral}_{\bar{K}N-\pi\Sigma-\pi\Lambda}$: $60.9$ MeV, width $65.0$ MeV
- $\bar{K}N$ first pole positions: $1428.6-i46.5$ MeV (SIDD-1), $1419.6-i56.0$ MeV (SIDD-2), $1420.6-i20.3$ MeV (chiral)
- $\bar{K}N$ second pole positions: $1380.1-i104.5$ MeV (SIDD-2), $1343.0-i72.5$ MeV (chiral)
- $K^{-}pp$ pole positions from direct search: $2326.0-i34.2$ MeV (SIDD-1), $2325.0-i24.1$ MeV (SIDD-2), $2346.5-i22.0$ MeV (chiral)
- $N_r = 15$ terms in the separable expansion provided satisfactory accuracy for convergence

## Context

This work extends previous Faddeev calculations of four-body kaonic systems by Shevchenko (2012) and Khosravi (2015) that used the Hilbert-Schmidt pole expansion (HSE) method, finding that the EDPE method yields deeper binding energies than HSE for the same potentials. The results are compared with Faddeev-Yakubowsky calculations by Maeda (2013) and variational calculations by Barnea, Gal, and Liverts (2012) and by Kohno, Tanaka, and Taniya (2014) using Akaishi-Yamazaki (AY) and Hyodo-Weise (HW) potentials. The authors note that the optical potential approximation is more accurate for one-pole than two-pole models, and that fully coupled-channel calculations of $\bar{K}NNN-\pi\Sigma NN$ and $\bar{K}\bar{K}NN-\pi\pi\Sigma\Sigma$ systems would be needed for a definitive judgment. The study also investigates whether the $K^{-}pp$ system exhibits a double-pole structure similar to $\Lambda(1405)$, finding that the second pole cannot be seen in the three-body scattering amplitudes for all models of $\bar{K}N$ interaction.
