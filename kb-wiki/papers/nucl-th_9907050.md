# Exact Treatment of the Pauli Exclusion Operator in Nuclear Matter Calculation

> Exact expressions of the Pauli exclusion operator Q in the nuclear matter calculation are presented in detail. Exact formulae are also given for the calculations of the single-particle-potential energy and the binding energy per nucleon with the exact Q operator. Numerical calculations of the G matrix in the lowest-order Brueckner theory are carried out to check the reliability of the standard angle-average approximation for the Q operator by employing the Bonn B and C NN potentials. It is observed that the exact treatment of the operator Q brings about non-negligible and attractive contributions to the binding energy.

# Exact Treatment of the Pauli Exclusion Operator in Nuclear Matter Calculation

## Key claim

The exact treatment of the Pauli exclusion operator Q in nuclear matter calculations yields non-negligible attractive contributions to the binding energy per nucleon at all densities studied, contrary to a recent study by Schiller, Müther and Czerski (1999) which found repulsive contributions at higher densities and attractive contributions only at low densities.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for the Pauli exclusion operator Q in angular-momentum-coupled states, including its dependence on the center-of-mass momentum direction. They solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the G matrix in the lowest-order Brueckner theory using both the exact Q operator and the standard angle-average approximation, employing the Bonn B and C NN potentials with partial waves up to J=6 treated rigorously and higher partial waves up to J=18 in the Born approximation.

## Key numbers

- For the Bonn B potential at k_F = 1.4 fm^{-1}: exact binding energy = -12.98 MeV, angle-average = -12.82 MeV, difference = -0.16 MeV (attractive)
- For the Bonn B potential at k_F = 1.8 fm^{-1}: exact binding energy = -13.61 MeV, angle-average = -13.32 MeV, difference = -0.29 MeV (attractive)
- For the Bonn C potential at k_F = 1.4 fm^{-1}: exact binding energy = -11.78 MeV, angle-average = -11.62 MeV, difference = -0.16 MeV (attractive)
- For the Bonn C potential at k_F = 1.8 fm^{-1}: exact binding energy = -10.53 MeV, angle-average = -10.24 MeV, difference = -0.29 MeV (attractive)
- The lJ-nondiagonal terms contributed at most 0.4% of the total potential energy
- The single-particle potential difference changed sign at approximately k_lambda = k_F / 2

## Context

This work extends the standard angle-average approximation for the Pauli operator Q introduced by Brueckner and Gammel (1958) and used in many nuclear matter calculations. The authors provide analytic formulae that avoid numerical integration of step functions, enabling exact treatment. The results contradict a very recent study by Schiller, Müther and Czerski (1999), who found that exact treatment gives repulsive contributions at higher densities and attractive contributions only at low densities, leading to a shift in the saturation point. The present work finds attractive contributions at all densities with negligible change in saturation density, suggesting the need for further investigation of the discrepancy.
