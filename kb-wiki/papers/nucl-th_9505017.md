# Chiral Lagrangian Approach to Exchange Vector Currents in Nuclei

> Exchange vector currents are calculated up to one-loop order (corresponding to next-to-next-to-leading order) in chiral perturbation theory. As an illustration of the power of the approach, we apply the formalism to the classic nuclear process $n+p\rightarrow d +γ$ at thermal energy. The exchange current correction comes out to be $(4.5 \pm 0.3)$ \% in amplitude giving a predicted cross section $σ= (334\pm 3)\ {\mbox mb}$ in excellent agreement with the experimental value $(334.2\pm 0.5)\ {\mbox mb}$. Together with the axial charge transitions computed previously, this result provides a strong support for the power of chiral Lagrangians in nuclear physics. As a by-product of our results, we suggest an open problem in the application of chiral Lagrangian approach to nuclear processes that has to do with giving a physical meaning to the short-range correlations that play an important role in nuclei.

## Key claim

Exchange vector currents calculated in chiral perturbation theory to next-to-next-to-leading order (one-loop order) give a predicted cross section for thermal neutron capture \(n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma\) that agrees with experiment, but the approach relies on filtering out short-range correlations below a hard-core radius \(r_c\), and the result must not depend sensitively on the precise value of \(r_c\) within the range \(\Lambda_\chi^{-1} \lesssim r_c \lesssim (2m_\pi)^{-1}\).

## Method

The authors computed exchange vector currents in nuclei using chiral perturbation theory in the heavy-fermion formalism, working to one-loop order (next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion). They applied the resulting two-body current operators to the thermal neutron capture process \(n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma\), evaluating the matrix elements with realistic nuclear wave functions that incorporate short-range correlations. The calculation included all diagrams at the appropriate chiral order, with ultraviolet divergences handled by dimensional regularization and counterterms fixed from experiment.

## Key numbers

- Exchange current correction to the amplitude for \(n+p\rightarrow d+\gamma\) at thermal energy: \((4.5 \pm 0.3)\%\).
- Predicted cross section: \(\sigma = (334 \pm 3)\ \text{mb}\).
- Experimental cross section: \((334.2 \pm 0.5)\ \text{mb}\).
- Chiral symmetry scale \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 4\pi f_\pi \sim 1\ \text{GeV}\).
- Pion decay constant \(f_\pi \simeq 93\ \text{MeV}\), pion mass \(m_\pi \simeq 139\ \text{MeV}\), nucleon mass \(m_N \simeq 939\ \text{MeV}\), axial coupling constant \(g_A \simeq 1.25\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach previously applied by the same authors (Park, Min, Rho, 1995) to axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei, and confirms the earlier phenomenological calculation by Riska and Brown (1972) who explained the cross section using one-pion-exchange, omega, and Delta resonance diagrams. The results support the "chiral filter" conjecture of Kubodera, Delorme, and Rho (1978) and demonstrate that chiral Lagrangians can systematically describe nuclear electroweak currents, while also raising an open problem about giving physical meaning to short-range correlations that cannot be accessed by chiral perturbation theory.
