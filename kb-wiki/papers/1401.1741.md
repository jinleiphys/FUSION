# A New Topological "Twist" to BR Scaling

> When vector mesons are considered on the same footing as pions as suggested by hidden local symmetry, the property of the nuclear tensor forces is strongly controlled by the behavior of the vector mesons in dense medium. This led to BR scaling in 1991. When baryons as skyrmions are put on crystal, there can be a phase transition from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density above that of normal nuclear matter. This topology change can induce fundamental changes to the parameters in hidden local symmetric Lagrangian, hence BR scaling, and brings a drastic modification to the structure of nuclear forces, in particulrar, the tensor forces. This can have far-reaching consequences on the EoS of compact-star matter and the structure of neutron-rich nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density above normal nuclear matter (estimated near 2n₀) can fundamentally alter the parameters of hidden local symmetric Lagrangian and thus modify the original BR scaling, particularly affecting nuclear tensor forces and the symmetry energy in dense matter, but the exact location of this transition density and its quantitative consequences remain uncertain and require further theoretical development.

## Method

The author uses skyrmion crystal lattice methods to model baryonic matter at high density, identifying a topological phase transition from skyrmions to half-skyrmions. The topological effects are then translated into scaling parameters of the hidden local symmetric (HLS) Lagrangian with baryons, specifically modifying the ρ meson mass and coupling constants. The tensor forces from pion and ρ meson exchange are computed using the BHLS (baryon HLS) formalism, and the symmetry energy is estimated via a closure approximation relating it to the tensor force strength.

## Key numbers

- The transition density from skyrmions to half-skyrmions is estimated to be in the vicinity of ∼2n₀, where n₀ ≃ 0.16 fm⁻³ is normal nuclear matter density
- In the half-skyrmion phase, the pion decay constant fπ*/fπ stabilizes at ≳ 0.6, rather than continuing to decrease
- A K⁻ meson propagating in skyrmion medium undergoes a mass drop of order 60 MeV at the transition density n₁/₂
- The symmetry energy Esym shows a cusp at n₁/₂ ∼ 2n₀, decreasing from n₀ to n₁/₂ then increasing above n₁/₂
- Using the modified scaling, the maximum neutron star mass is found to be ∼2.4 M⊙, with radius ∼11 km and maximum central density ∼5.5n₀
- The average excitation energy mediated by tensor forces used in the closure formula is Ē ≈ 200 MeV

## Context

This work extends the original BR scaling proposed by Brown and Rho (1991), which described how vector meson masses scale in dense nuclear matter based on hidden local symmetry. The paper introduces a topological phase transition from skyrmions to half-skyrmions, building on earlier work by Klebanov (1985) on skyrmion crystals and by Park et al. on half-skyrmion phases. The approach contrasts with standard chiral perturbation theory (sChPT) which integrates out vector mesons, and with relativistic mean field models that do not incorporate topology changes. The results enable predictions for the equation of state of compact-star matter and the structure of neutron-rich nuclei, and suggest a possible connection between half-skyrmion matter and strongly-coupled quark matter via a Cheshire Cat mechanism.
