# Cluster structure in stable and unstable nuclei

> Cluster structure in stable and unstable nuclei has been studied. We report recent developments of theoretical studies on cluster aspect, which is essential for structure study of light unstable nuclei. We discuss negative parity bands in even-even Be and Ne isotopes and show the importance of cluster aspect. Three-body cluster structure and cluster crystallization are also introduced. It was found that the coexistence of cluster and mean-field aspect brings a variety of structures to unstable nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper argues that cluster structure is an essential feature of both stable and unstable light nuclei, and that the coexistence of cluster and mean-field aspects brings a variety of structures to unstable nuclei; however, the abstract's claim that "cluster aspect is essential" is hedged by the paper's focus on specific cases (Be, Ne, C isotopes) and by the statement that the assumption of clusters is not necessarily valid for systematic study, requiring methods like AMD that treat all single nucleons independently.

## Method

The authors review theoretical developments in cluster models for light nuclei, including the molecular orbital method (MO), generator coordinate method (GCM), stochastic variational method (SVM), and antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). They apply AMD calculations with the MV1 effective force to study negative parity rotational bands in even-even Be and Ne isotopes, and discuss GCM calculations for $^{22}$Ne and AMD+GCM calculations for $^{30}$Ne. For C isotopes, they review molecular orbital model calculations of $^{14}$C with $3\alpha$ core and excess neutrons.

## Key numbers

- In $^{12}$Be, the $B(E1;0^+_1\rightarrow 1^-_2)$ transition from the $K^\pi=0^-$ band is 1.0 e$^2$fm$^4$, while $B(E1;0^+_1\rightarrow 1^-_1)$ from the $K^\pi=1^-$ band is 0.2 e$^2$fm$^4$.
- In $^{22}$Ne, the $^{18}$O+$\alpha$ threshold energy is 9.7 MeV excitation energy, and the $\alpha$-cluster states in negative parity bands start from the $1^-$ state at 12 MeV excitation energy.
- In $^{30}$Ne, the AMD+GCM calculations predict the $0^+_3$ band (a $4p$-$4h$ state) at excitation energy $E_x(0^+_3)=4$ MeV, and the band-head $1^-$ state of the $K^\pi=1^-$ band is predicted to be less than 3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the known cluster structure in stable nuclei like $^8$Be, $^{12}$C, and $^{20}$Ne into unstable neutron-rich isotopes, building on prior molecular orbital models by von Oertzen (1997) and AMD calculations by Kanada-En'yo (1998). It enables further study of cluster crystallization, a concept introduced by Itagaki (2004) for $^{14}$C, and suggests that valence neutrons in molecular orbitals can stabilize and rigidify cluster configurations in neutron-rich nuclei, opening the way to systematic studies of cluster states across a wider mass region.
