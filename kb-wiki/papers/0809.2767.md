# New supersymmetric quartet of nuclei: 192Os, 193Os, 193Ir, 194Ir

> We present evidence of the existence of a new supersymmetric quartet of nuclei in the A=190 mass region. The analysis is based on new experimental information on the odd-odd nucleus 194Ir from transfer and capture reactions. The new data allow the identification of a new supersymmetric quartet, consisting of the 192,193Os and 193,194Ir nuclei. We make explicit predictions fo r193Os, and suggest that its spectroscopic properties be measured in dedicated experiments. Finally, we study correlations between different transfer reactions.

## Key claim

The paper presents evidence for a new supersymmetric quartet of nuclei (192Os, 193Os, 193Ir, 194Ir) in the A=190 mass region, based on new experimental data from transfer and neutron capture reactions on the odd-odd nucleus 194Ir; the claim is hedged by the statement that the identification depends on the assumption that the odd-odd nucleus 194Ir provides another example of the U(6/12)ν ⊗ U(6/4)π supersymmetry, and that the predictions for 193Os require dedicated experimental verification.

## Method

The authors analyzed new experimental data from polarized (d,α) transfer reactions and neutron capture reactions on 194Ir to establish spin and parity assignments of its energy levels. They then applied the energy formula of the U(6/12)ν ⊗ U(6/4)π dynamical supersymmetry, fitting the parameters A, B, B', C, D, and E simultaneously to the excitation energies of the four nuclei in the proposed quartet. Using the same parameter set determined from 194Ir, they predicted the spectrum of 193Os and studied correlations between one-neutron transfer reactions (stripping and pick-up) for the Pt and Os isotopes.

## Key numbers

- The energy formula parameters for the supersymmetric quartet (all in keV): A = 26.3, B = 8.7, B' = -33.6, C = 35.1, D = 6.3, E = 4.5.
- For the stripping reaction 194Pt → 195Pt (N=5), the predicted intensity ratio Rj = 29.3 for excitation of the (1,0), L=2 doublet relative to the ground state band, compared to an experimental value of 19.0 for j=5/2.
- For the stripping reaction 192Os → 193Os (N=6), the predicted intensity ratio Rj = 37.8.
- For the pick-up reaction 195Pt → 194Pt (Nπ=1, Nν=4), the predicted intensity ratio Rj = 1.96.
- For the pick-up reaction 193Os → 192Os (Nπ=2, Nν=4), the predicted intensity ratio Rj = 3.24.

## Context

This work extends the concept of nuclear supersymmetric quartets, previously best exemplified by the 194,195Pt and 195,196Au nuclei studied by Metz (1999), Groeger (2000), Wirth (2003), and Barea (2005, 2006), to a new mass region with different boson numbers (Nν=5, Nπ=3). The new data on 194Ir from Balodis (2007) provided crucial information that allowed the identification of this second quartet. The paper enables future experimental studies of 193Os, particularly through (d,p) stripping and (p,d) pick-up reactions, to test the parameter-independent predictions of nuclear supersymmetry and establish the existence of the quartet definitively.
