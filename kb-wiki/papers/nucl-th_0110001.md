# Analysis of Pion Electroproduction Data

> A fit to the existing pion electroproduction data is presented. This work builds upon our previous analyses of pion photoproduction and elastic pion-nucleon scattering over the Delta resonance region. We comment on the extraction of E_1+ ^3/2 / M_1+ ^3/2 (E2/M1) and S_1+ ^3/2 / M_1+ ^3/2 (S2/M1) ratios, and note that the E2/M1 ratio approaches, and possibly crosses, zero below a Q2 of 5 (GeV/c}^2.

# Analysis of Pion Electroproduction Data

## Key claim

The E2/M1 ratio approaches, and possibly crosses, zero below a Q² of 5 (GeV/c)², but the authors note that while some fits find a cross-over to positive values, others do not, and their own single-Q² fits have such large uncertainties that they overlap with results that find no cross-over.

## Method

The authors performed energy- and Q²-dependent fits over the full kinematic range of existing pion electroproduction data, using a direct modification of their photoproduction formalism with correct threshold behavior and Watson's theorem built in. Multipoles were parameterized with Born terms plus phenomenological structure functions, with the photoproduction analysis anchoring the fit at Q² = 0. They also performed single-Q² fits to data clustered around particular Q² values to look for trends or problems in the global fit, and included preliminary CLAS data in most fits.

## Key numbers

- The existing database contains approximately 10,000 data points, with preliminary CLAS data expected to increase this to approximately 30,000 points
- Most of the database is limited to Q² values below about 1 (GeV/c)²
- The Q²-dependent fit over the full range (0.0–5.0 (GeV/c)²) including CLAS data gave χ²/data = 53828/33209
- The Q²-dependent fit over the full range excluding CLAS data gave χ²/data = 18713/10713
- Single-Q² fits were performed in bins such as 0.2–0.4 (GeV/c)² (χ²/data = 10808/6733), 0.4–0.6 (GeV/c)² (χ²/data = 17163/11020), and 3.8–4.2 (GeV/c)² (χ²/data = 831/697)
- Preliminary CLAS data are concentrated below 2 (GeV/c)²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of pion photoproduction (Arndt, Strakovsky, and Workman, 1996) and elastic pion-nucleon scattering over the Delta resonance region. The paper compares its results for E2/M1 and S2/M1 ratios to recent determinations from Frolov et al. (1999), Pospischil et al. (2001), Kalleicher et al. (1997), the MAID fits of Kamalov et al. (2001), and the dynamical model of Sato and Lee (2001). The authors note that the database is very sparse above Q² of about 1 (GeV/c)² and that improvement will require more complete coverage of measurements above 1–2 (GeV/c)².
