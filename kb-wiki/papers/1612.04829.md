# Dynamics, nucleosynthesis, and kilonova signature of black hole - neutron star merger ejecta

> We investigate the ejecta from black hole - neutron star mergers by modeling the formation and interaction of mass ejected in a tidal tail and a disk wind. The outflows are neutron-rich, giving rise to optical/infrared emission powered by the radioactive decay of $r$-process elements (a kilonova). Here we perform an end-to-end study of this phenomenon, where we start from the output of a fully-relativistic merger simulation, calculate the post-merger hydrodynamical evolution of the ejecta and disk winds including neutrino physics, determine the final nucleosynthetic yields using post-processing nuclear reaction network calculations, and compute the kilonova emission with a radiative transfer code. We study the effects of the tail-to-disk mass ratio by scaling the tail density. A larger initial tail mass results in fallback matter becoming mixed into the disk and ejected in the subsequent disk wind. Relative to the case of a disk without dynamical ejecta, the combined outflow has lower mean electron fraction, faster speed, larger total mass, and larger absolute mass free of high-opacity Lanthanides or Actinides. In most cases, the nucleosynthetic yield is dominated by the heavy $r$-process contribution from the unbound part of the tidal tail. A Solar-like abundance distribution can however be obtained when the total mass of the dynamical ejecta is comparable to the mass of the disk outflows. The kilonova has a characteristic duration of 1 week and a luminosity of ~$10^{41}$ erg/s, with orientation effects leading to variations of a factor ~2 in brightness. At early times (< 1 day) the emission includes an optical component from the (hot) Lanthanide-rich material, but the spectrum evolves quickly to the infrared thereafter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the combined outflow from a black hole-neutron star merger (tidal tail plus disk wind) has a lower mean electron fraction, faster speed, larger total mass, and larger absolute mass free of high-opacity Lanthanides or Actinides relative to a disk without dynamical ejecta, but a Solar-like abundance distribution can only be obtained when the total mass of the dynamical ejecta is comparable to the mass of the disk outflows.

## Method

The authors start from the output of a fully relativistic general-relativistic merger simulation of a 7 solar mass black hole with a 1.4 solar mass neutron star (case M14-7-S8 from Foucart et al. 2014), axisymmetrize the matter distribution, and evolve the post-merger hydrodynamics of the ejecta and disk winds for about 10 seconds using the FLASH3 code with an alpha viscosity prescription and neutrino leakage. They then post-process 10,000 tracer particle trajectories with the nuclear reaction network SkyNet to compute nucleosynthetic yields, and calculate kilonova emission with the Monte Carlo radiative transfer code SEDONA, varying the tail-to-disk mass ratio by scaling the initial dynamical ejecta density by factors of 1/10, 1/3, and 3 relative to the baseline.

## Key numbers

- Baseline model (F0): black hole mass 8.071 solar masses, spin 0.861, disk mass 0.06 solar masses, gravitationally bound dynamical ejecta mass 0.038 solar masses, unbound dynamical ejecta mass 0.075 solar masses
- Disk-only model (Fdisk): 8% of initial disk mass ejected as wind, with mass-weighted mean electron fraction 0.35 and mean radial velocity 0.039c
- Combined model F0: 15% total ejected fraction, mean radial velocity 0.044c, mean electron fraction 0.24, mass ejected with Ye > 0.25 equal to 0.009 solar masses
- Model Ft0.1 (dynamical ejecta scaled by 1/10): 11% total ejected fraction, mean electron fraction 0.30, mass ejected with Ye > 0.25 equal to 0.006 solar masses
- Model Ft3.0 (dynamical ejecta scaled by 3): 15% total ejected fraction, mean electron fraction 0.20, mass ejected with Ye > 0.25 equal to 0.008 solar masses
- Kilonova characteristic duration: 1 week, luminosity approximately 10^41 erg/s, with orientation effects leading to variations of a factor of about 2 in brightness
- At early times (less than 1 day), the emission includes an optical component from Lanthanide-rich material, evolving quickly to the infrared thereafter

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Fernandez et al. (2015) which used Newtonian merger initial conditions and found no significant changes in disk outflow properties when dynamical ejecta was present, by instead using fully relativistic initial conditions from Foucart et al. (2015) and exploring a range of dynamical ejecta-to-disk mass ratios. The results enable more accurate predictions for kilonova signatures from black hole-neutron star mergers, which will be essential for interpreting electromagnetic counterparts to gravitational wave detections by Advanced LIGO, and demonstrate that the relative masses of tidal tail and disk outflow critically determine whether the nucleosynthetic yield can match the Solar System r-process abundance distribution.
