# Dynamical dipole $γ$ radiation in heavy-ion collisions on the basis of the quantum molecular dynamics model

> Dynamical dipole $γ$-ray emission in heavy-ion collisions is explored in the framework of the quantum molecular dynamics model. The studies are focused on systems of $^{40}$Ca bombarding $^{48}$Ca and its isotopes at different incident energies and impact parameters. Yields of $γ$ rays are calculated and the centroid energy and dynamical dipole emission width of the $γ$ spectra are extracted to investigate the properties of $γ$ emission. In addition, sensitivities of dynamical dipole $γ$-ray emission to the isospin and the symmetry energy coefficient of the equation of state are studied. The results show that detailed study of dynamical dipole $γ$ radiation can provide information on the equation of state and the symmetry energy around the normal nuclear density.

## Key claim

Dynamical dipole gamma-ray emission in heavy-ion collisions, simulated with the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model, can serve as a probe for the equation of state and the symmetry energy around normal nuclear density, but the model's energy non-conservation means the results are qualitative rather than quantitative for giant dipole resonance studies.

## Method

The authors used the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IDQMD) model to simulate collisions of Ca-40 projectiles with Ca-48, Ca-52, and Ca-40 targets at beam energies of 10, 15, and 20 MeV/nucleon and impact parameters from 1 to 5 fm. They calculated the time evolution of the giant dipole moment in coordinate and momentum space, then performed Fourier transforms to obtain gamma-ray emission probabilities. The model incorporated Skyrme potentials (both soft and stiff parameter sets), Yukawa, Coulomb, and symmetry potentials, with the symmetry energy coefficient varied to study its effect.

## Key numbers

- For the Ca-40 + Ca-48 system at 10 MeV/nucleon and b = 1 fm, the giant dipole moment attenuates rapidly after t = 400 fm/c.
- The N/Z asymmetry system Ca-40 + Ca-52 (N/Z = 1.3) shows an enhanced gamma yield of about 35% relative to the symmetric system Ca-40 + Ca-40 (N/Z = 1.0).
- The N/Z asymmetry system Ca-40 + Ca-48 (N/Z = 1.2) shows an enhanced gamma yield of about 20% relative to the symmetric system Ca-40 + Ca-40 (N/Z = 1.0).
- The saturation nuclear density used in the model is rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3.
- Soft EOS parameters: alpha = -356 MeV, beta = 303 MeV, gamma = 7/6.
- Stiff EOS parameters: alpha = -124 MeV, beta = 70.5 MeV, gamma = 2.
- The Yukawa potential parameters: V_y = 0.0024 GeV, m = 0.83, Gaussian width L = 2.0 fm^2.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of dynamical dipole emission using time-dependent Hartree-Fock (Simenel 2001), Boltzmann-Nordheim-Vlasov (Baran 2009), and constrained molecular dynamics (Papa 2005) models by applying the quantum molecular dynamics model for the first time to giant dipole resonance gamma radiation. The results confirm earlier findings that pre-equilibrium GDR emission is sensitive to the N/Z asymmetry of the entrance channel and the symmetry energy, as discussed by Pappalardo (2003) and others. The study enables future experimental investigations of the equation of state and symmetry energy near normal nuclear density using dynamical dipole gamma-ray measurements in low-energy heavy-ion collisions.
