# Effective Field Theory for Bulk Properties of Nuclei

> Recent progress in Lorentz-covariant quantum field theories of the nuclear many-body problem ({\em quantum hadrodynamics}, or QHD) is discussed. The importance of modern perspectives in effective field theory and density functional theory for understanding the successes of QHD is emphasized. The inclusion of hadronic electromagnetic structure and of nonanalytic terms in the energy functional is also considered.

## Key claim

Lorentz-covariant effective field theories (quantum hadrodynamics, or QHD) can provide a realistic description of bulk nuclear properties and the spin-orbit force throughout the Periodic Table when applied within the framework of modern effective field theory (EFT) and density functional theory (DFT), but this success relies on the presence of large scalar and vector mean fields that are small compared to the nucleon mass (ratios of roughly 1/4 to 1/3 at normal densities), which serve as expansion parameters justifying truncation of the effective lagrangian.

## Method

The paper reviews the construction of an effective hadronic lagrangian with nonlinear chiral symmetry, including valence nucleons, pions, and low-lying non-Goldstone bosons, expanded in powers of fields and derivatives using Georgi's naive dimensional analysis. Results for finite nuclei and nuclear matter are calculated at one-baryon-loop order, leading to a factorized parametrization of the ground-state energy functional, with parameters determined from fits to nuclear properties. The paper also discusses an alternative DFT approach where the energy functional depends on valence-nucleon wave functions and classical meson fields, with an expansion in classical meson fields and their derivatives to approximate the exchange-correlation functional.

## Key numbers

- The ratios of meson mean fields in nuclei to the nucleon mass are roughly 1/4 to 1/3 at nuclear densities of interest.
- Roughly five isoscalar non-gradient parameters, one gradient parameter, and one isovector parameter can be determined from bulk and single-particle nuclear data.
- Only one isovector parameter in covariant mean-field density functionals is determined by conventional fits to nuclear properties.

## Context

This work extends the program of quantum hadrodynamics (QHD) initiated by Serot and Walecka (1986, 1992, 1997) and others, incorporating modern perspectives from effective field theory (Weinberg, 1967; Georgi, 1993) and density functional theory (Kohn and Sham, 1965; Dreizler and Gross, 1990). The paper reconciles the successful relativistic mean-field picture with chiral symmetry by showing that the large scalar field originates from correlated two-pion exchange (Durso et al.; Lin and collaborators, 1989, 1990), and it enables systematic inclusion of exchange, correlation, and vacuum effects beyond the one-loop level, as well as studies of electroweak interactions with nuclei and the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy relevant for neutron star structure.
