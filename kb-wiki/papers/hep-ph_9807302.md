# A renormalisation-group treatment of two-body scattering

> Nonrelativistic two-body scattering by a short-ranged potential is studied using the renormalisation group. Two fixed points are identified: a trivial one and one describing systems with a bound state at zero energy. The eigenvalues of the linearised renormalisation group are used to assign a systematic power-counting to terms in the potential near each of these fixed points. The expansion around the nontrivial fixed point is shown to be equivalent to the effective-range expansion.

# A renormalisation-group treatment of two-body scattering

## Key claim
The renormalisation group analysis reveals two fixed points for nonrelativistic two-body scattering by short-ranged potentials: a trivial fixed point describing weak scattering, and a nontrivial fixed point describing systems with a bound state at zero energy. The expansion around the nontrivial fixed point is shown to be equivalent to the effective-range expansion, but only for potentials that lie exactly on the "critical surface" where the scattering length is infinite; for finite but large scattering lengths the equivalence holds only in a limited cut-off regime.

## Method
The authors study s-wave two-body scattering using Wilson's continuous renormalisation group, imposing a sharp momentum cut-off Λ on virtual states in the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the reactance matrix K. They derive an RG equation for the rescaled potential by demanding that the off-shell K-matrix be independent of Λ, then identify fixed points and linearise the RG equation to find eigenfunctions with definite scaling dimensions. The analysis is performed for a potential consisting only of contact interactions, expanded in powers of momenta and energy.

## Key numbers
- The trivial fixed point has RG eigenvalues ν = l + m + n + 1, where l, m, n are non-negative even integers, all positive (stable fixed point).
- The nontrivial fixed point has allowed RG eigenvalues ν = -1, 1, 3, ... for energy-dependent perturbations, with one negative eigenvalue (unstable fixed point).
- Momentum-dependent perturbations around the nontrivial fixed point have RG eigenvalues ν = 2, 4, 6, ... for the leading terms.
- The fixed-point potential at zero energy is inversely proportional to Λ: V₀(p=0,Λ) = -2π²/(MΛ).
- The coefficients relating potential parameters to scattering observables are: Ĉ₋₁ = -π/(2Λ₀ a) and Ĉ₁ = πΛ₀ rₑ/4, where a is the scattering length and rₑ is the effective range.

## Context
This work extends the effective field theory approach to nuclear forces initiated by Weinberg (1990) and addresses difficulties with unnaturally large scattering lengths noted in that work. The RG analysis provides a systematic framework that reconciles Weinberg's power counting (for the trivial fixed point) with the alternative counting proposed by Kaplan, Savage and Wise (1998) using the PDS scheme, and by van Kolck (1997, 1998) using subtractive renormalisation. The equivalence demonstrated between the nontrivial fixed point and the effective-range expansion (Bethe, 1949; Blatt and Weisskopf, 1952) provides a foundation for extending these methods to systems with long-ranged pion-exchange forces and three-body systems.
