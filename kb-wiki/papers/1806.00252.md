# Phase transition dynamics for hot nuclei

> An abnormal production of events with almost equal-sized fragments was theoretically proposed as a signature of spinodal instabilities responsible for nuclear multifragmentation in the Fermi energy domain. On the other hand finite size effects are predicted to strongly reduce this abnormal production. High statistics quasifusion hot nuclei produced in central collisions between Xe and Sn isotopes at 32 and 45 AMeV incident energies have been used to definitively establish, through the experimental measurement of charge correlations, the presence of spinodal instabilities. N/Z influence was also studied.

## Key claim

Using high-statistics data from central Xe+Sn collisions at 32 and 45 AMeV, the authors definitively establish the presence of spinodal instabilities as the mechanism driving nuclear multifragmentation, via a fossil signature of equal-sized fragments observed in charge correlations. The signal is reduced for neutron-rich systems and at higher incident energy, consistent with theoretical predictions of increased instability growth times.

## Method

Quasifusion hot nuclei were produced in central collisions of 124,136Xe + 112,124Sn at 32 and 45 AMeV, measured with the INDRA 4π multidetector at GANIL. Events with at least 80% of the total charge detected and a flow angle greater than 60° were selected to isolate compact single-source events. Higher-order charge correlation functions (CFs) were constructed using the intrinsic probability method to compare the experimental yield of events with given mean charge (⟨Z⟩) and variance (σ_Z) to an uncorrelated reference, searching for an excess of events with nearly equal-sized fragments (low σ_Z).

## Key numbers

- Cross-sections for selected quasifusion events: ≈ 40 mb at 32 AMeV and ≈ 25 mb at 45 AMeV; estimated total cross-sections ≈ 250 mb at 32 AMeV and ≈ 180 mb at 45 AMeV.
- Correlation function peaks at σ_Z < 2 for 32 AMeV 124Xe+112Sn: CF values of 1.268 (M×⟨Z⟩ range 60-66) and 1.498 (M×⟨Z⟩ range 66-72), with confidence levels of 6.09 and 7.29 sigma units, respectively.
- For 32 AMeV 136Xe+124Sn: CF values of 1.143 (M×⟨Z⟩ 60-66, 2.98σ) and 1.402 (M×⟨Z⟩ 66-72, 6.47σ).
- For 45 AMeV 124Xe+112Sn: CF values of 1.075 (M×⟨Z⟩ 54-60, 2.29σ) and 1.257 (M×⟨Z⟩ 60-66, 0.82σ).
- For 45 AMeV 136Xe+124Sn: CF value of 1.502 (M×⟨Z⟩ 66-72, 2.08σ).
- Extra event percentages (events with σ_Z < 2 beyond uncorrelated expectation): 0.068 ± 0.004% (32 AMeV 124Xe+112Sn), 0.064 ± 0.004% (32 AMeV 136Xe+124Sn), 0.025 ± 0.003% (45 AMeV 124Xe+112Sn), and 0.0065 ± 0.0017% (45 AMeV 136Xe+124Sn).
- The most unstable spinodal modes correspond to wavelengths λ ≈ 10 fm and characteristic times ≈ 30-50 fm/c; primitive fragment sizes ≈ λ/2 ≈ 5 fm correspond to Z ≈ 8-10.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of phase transition signatures in hot nuclei, including negative heat capacity observations for 32-50 AMeV Xe+Sn central collisions (Borderie et al., 2002) and bimodality in Au+Au collisions (Bonnet et al., 2009). It resolves a long-standing question about the dynamics of fragment formation, confirming the spinodal fragmentation scenario predicted by stochastic mean-field approaches over molecular dynamics models (QMD, AMD) that produce fragments without unstable conditions. The results enable future studies of isospin effects on nuclear phase transitions and the interplay between spinodal instabilities, cavitation, and nucleation in multifragmentation.
