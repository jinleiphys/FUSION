# Higher-shell corrections for systems with one and two valence nucleons: A spin-orbit and tensor interaction analysis

> It is shown that when higher-shell admixtures are included for systems with two valence particles or holes, there are effects which are quite different from those for one-valence-nucleon systems. For example, for nuclei with one valence particle or hole, there is no first-order correction for the magnetic dipole moment or the Gamow-Teller transition amplitude. However for nuclei with two valence particles or holes, one can get substantial corrections. The effects of the tensor and spin-orbit interactions in core renormalization are emphasized. We find that in 6Li, the spin-orbit interaction causes the quadrupole moment of the J=1+ ground state to be positive, but the tensor interaction causes it to be negative. The G-matrices derived from realistic interactions are employed in these calculations.

# Higher-shell corrections for systems with one and two valence nucleons: A spin-orbit and tensor interaction analysis

## Key claim

While first-order perturbation corrections to magnetic dipole moments and Gamow-Teller transition amplitudes vanish for one-valence-nucleon systems, substantial corrections arise for two-valence-nucleon systems due to renormalization of the effective interaction between the valence particles. The tensor and spin-orbit components of the nucleon-nucleon interaction have opposing effects on the quadrupole moment of the $J^\pi=1^+$ ground state of $^6$Li, with the spin-orbit interaction driving it positive and the tensor interaction driving it negative.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model diagonalizations using the OXBASH code in a $(0+2)\hbar\omega$ model space, where either two core nucleons are excited through one major shell or one core nucleon is excited through two major shells. Brueckner G-matrices derived from the Nijmegen potential (NijmII) were used, with comparisons to Hamada-Johnson, Reid-soft-core, and Reid93 potentials on a selected basis. The single-particle energies were not treated as parameters but were implicitly generated from the kinetic energy and G-matrix elements, and the starting energy in the G-matrix calculation included an adjustable parameter $\Delta$ to yield reasonable nuclear binding.

## Key numbers

- For $^{17}$O, the calculated polarization charge for the $d_{5/2}$-neutron is $\epsilon_\nu = 0.123$, much smaller than the phenomenological value of 0.5.
- For a valence proton in $A=17$, the polarization charge is $\epsilon_\pi = 0.044$, compared to the phenomenological value of 0.2.
- The $B$(GT) value for $^{14}$C $\rightarrow$ $^{14}$N changes from 2.627 in the $0\hbar\omega$ space to 1.305 in the $(0+2)\hbar\omega$ space.
- For $^{15}$O, $B$(GT) changes only from 0.333 to 0.326 when going from $0\hbar\omega$ to $(0+2)\hbar\omega$ space.
- For $^{17}$O, $B$(GT) changes from 1.400 to 1.381 between the two spaces.
- The experimental magnetic dipole moment of the $^6$Li $1^+$ ground state is $0.822\ \mu_N$; the $0\hbar\omega$ calculation gives $0.866\ \mu_N$ and the $(0+2)\hbar\omega$ calculation gives $0.849\ \mu_N$.
- The experimental quadrupole moment of $^6$Li is $-0.082\ e\ \text{fm}^2$; the $0\hbar\omega$ calculation gives $-0.237\ e\ \text{fm}^2$ and the $(0+2)\hbar\omega$ calculation gives $-0.163\ e\ \text{fm}^2$.
- With the schematic interaction, turning on only the spin-orbit interaction ($x=1$, $y=0$) yields $Q = 0.121\ e\ \text{fm}^2$; turning on only the tensor interaction ($x=0$, $y=1$) yields $Q = -0.608\ e\ \text{fm}^2$.
- The calculated $1/2^-_1$--$3/2^-_1$ splitting in $^5$He is 3.633 MeV; in $^{15}$O the $3/2^-_1$--$1/2^-_1$ splitting is 5.479 MeV; the $3/2^+_1$--$5/2^+_1$ splitting in $^{17}$O is 6.260 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model calculations by van Hees, Wolters, and others who included higher-shell admixtures using phenomenological interactions, but here the focus is on testing G-matrices from realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials. The results agree qualitatively with higher-order perturbation theory calculations by Skouras and Muther (1992) who also obtained small polarization charges. The finding that the tensor interaction weakens effectively in the valence space due to higher-shell admixtures connects to the classic observation by Inglis that a tensor interaction is needed to obtain a vanishing Gamow-Teller matrix element for the $^{14}$C beta decay. The work enables systematic studies of how higher-shell effects modify nuclear properties in a nonuniform way as nucleons are added, and points toward the need for excitations beyond $2\hbar\omega$ to reproduce empirical polarization charges.
