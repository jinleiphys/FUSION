# Low-energy bound states, resonances, and scattering of light ions

> We describe bound states, resonances and elastic scattering of light ions using a $δ$-shell potential. Focusing on low-energy data such as energies of bound states and resonances, charge radii, asymptotic normalization coefficients, effective-range parameters, and phase shifts, we adjust the two parameters of the potential to some of these observables and make predictions for the nuclear systems $d+α$, $\mbox{$^3$He}+α$, $\mbox{$^3$He}+α$, $α+α$, and $p+\mbox{$^{16}$O}$. We identify relevant momentum scales for Coulomb halo nuclei and propose how to apply systematic corrections to the potentials. This allows us to quantify statistical and systematic uncertainties. We present a constructive criticism of Coulomb halo effective field theory and compute the unknown charge radius of $^{17}$F.

## Key claim

The paper argues that finite-range potentials (specifically a δ-shell potential) are necessary for accurately describing low-energy bound states, resonances, and scattering of light ions with strong Coulomb interactions, because zero-range potentials fail when the Coulomb momentum is large compared to the bound-state or resonance momentum; the authors show that for systems where \(2\sqrt{2k_c D} \gg 1\), observables become exponentially enhanced and model-dependent, contradicting the assumptions of Coulomb halo effective field theory at leading order.

## Method

The authors employed a δ-shell potential \(V(r) = \frac{\hbar^2 \lambda_0}{m} \delta(r-R)\) plus the Coulomb potential, solving analytically for bound states, resonances, and scattering phase shifts using Coulomb wave functions. They adjusted the two parameters (strength \(\lambda_0\) and range \(R\)) to reproduce low-energy observables such as resonance energies, widths, bound-state energies, and charge radii for the systems \(d+\alpha\), \(^3\)He+\(\alpha\), \(\alpha+\alpha\), and \(p+^{16}\)O. Systematic uncertainties were estimated by introducing a momentum-dependent coupling \(\lambda(k) = \lambda_0 + \tilde{g}_2 k^4\) and varying the coefficient within natural expectations based on the breakdown scale.

## Key numbers

- For \(\alpha+\alpha\) scattering: the resonance energy is \(E \approx 92\) keV and the width is \(\Gamma \approx 6\) eV; the δ-shell potential yields a scattering length \(a_0 \approx -2482\) fm, an effective range \(r_0 \approx 1.2\) fm, and a resonance width \(\Gamma \approx 7.5\) eV from simple estimates.
- For the \(p+^{16}\)O system (bound \(J^\pi = 1/2^+\) state): the asymptotic normalization coefficient is estimated as \(C_0 \approx 80\) fm\(^{-1/2}\) from the simple formula in Table I.
- The effective range for systems with strong Coulomb interactions satisfies \(r_0 - 1/(3k_c) = -\pi D e^{-4\sqrt{2k_c D}}\), with \(1/(3k_c)\) being the causality limit; for \(\alpha+\alpha\), \(k_c \approx 0.28\) fm\(^{-1}\) gives \(r_0 \approx 1.2\) fm.
- The breakdown momentum for the \(\alpha+\alpha\) system is \(\pi/D \approx 0.95\) fm\(^{-1}\), corresponding to a center-of-mass energy \(E_b \approx 9.4\) MeV; the model momentum scale is \(\Lambda_m \approx 0.4\) fm\(^{-1}\) corresponding to \(E_m \approx 1.7\) MeV.
- The δ-shell potential parameters for \(^8\)Be are \(\lambda_0 = -0.81\) fm\(^{-1}\) and \(R = 3.54\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the effective range expansion and δ-shell potential formalism of Mur (1993) and Kok (1982) to multiple light-ion systems, providing a constructive criticism of Coulomb halo effective field theory as applied to \(^8\)Be by Higa (2008) and to \(^{17}\)F by Ryberg (2014). The authors show that zero-range potentials are incompatible with finite-size ions when \(k_c D \gg 1\), and they propose systematic corrections via a Coulomb-corrected derivative expansion. The prediction of the unknown charge radius of \(^{17}\)F enables experimental tests by Garcia Ruiz (2016), and the model-independent relations derived (such as Eq. (new) relating scattering length to resonance width) provide tools for estimating hard-to-measure quantities in Coulomb halo nuclei.
