# A self-consistent QRPA study of quadrupole collectivity around 32Mg

> On the basis of the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) plus quasiparticle random phase approximation method (QRPA) based on the Green's function approach with Skyrme force, we discuss the anomalous E2 properties of the first 2+ states in neutron-rich nuclei 32Mg and 30Ne. The B(E2) values and the excitation energies of the first 2+ states are well described within HFB plus QRPA calculations with spherical symmetry. We conclude that pairing effects account largely for the anomalously large B(E2) values and the low excitation energies in 32Mg and 30Ne.

## Key claim

The anomalously large B(E2) values and low excitation energies of the first 2+ states in 32Mg and 30Ne can be explained by neutron pairing correlations alone within spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) plus quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) calculations, without invoking deformation. The abstract glosses over the condition that this conclusion depends on the specific choice of pairing interaction parameters, particularly the density cutoff parameter ρc.

## Method

The authors performed HFB calculations with the Skyrme SkM* force and a density-dependent delta pairing interaction to obtain ground states of N=20 isotones. They then solved the QRPA equations in coordinate space using the Green's function method, with the residual interaction consistently derived from the Skyrme Hamiltonian density. Spherical symmetry was imposed throughout to isolate the role of pairing correlations from deformation effects.

## Key numbers

- The experimental B(E2) value in 32Mg is 15.0 ± 2.5 single-particle units (W.u.)
- The experimental energy ratio E(4+1)/E(2+1) in 32Mg is 2.6
- The neutron pairing gap in 30Ne is 1.26 MeV, used to fix the pairing strength Vpair = -418 MeV·fm-3
- The density cutoff parameter ρc = 0.16 fm-3 gives an excitation energy E(2+) = 0.64 MeV for 30Ne, close to the experimental value of 0.791(26) MeV
- The neutron pairing gaps change from 1.26 MeV in 30Ne to zero in 38Ar

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies that showed the importance of neutron 2p-2h configurations across the N=20 shell gap for describing anomalous E2 properties in neutron-rich nuclei. The authors note that earlier mean-field calculations, both relativistic and non-relativistic, had found spherical ground states in 32Mg, and that the observed energy ratio E(4+1)/E(2+1) = 2.6 lies between the rigid rotor limit (3.3) and vibrational limit (2.0). The results enable future studies to use low-lying collective states in neutron-rich nuclei to constrain the density dependence of pairing interactions, as demonstrated by the sensitivity of the 30Ne excitation energy to the pairing density cutoff parameter ρc.
