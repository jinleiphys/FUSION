# Renormalization group invariance in pionless effective field theory for the NN system

> We consider the NN interaction in pionless effective field theory (EFT) up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) and use a recursive subtractive renormalization scheme to describe NN scattering in the 1S0 channel. We fix the strengths of the contact interactions at a reference scale, chosen to be the one that provides the best fit for the phase-shifts, and then slide the renormalization scale by evolving the driving terms of the subtracted Lippmann-Schwinger equation through a non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik equation. The results show that such a systematic renormalization scheme with multiple subtractions is fully renormalization group invariant.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the subtracted kernel method (SKM) with multiple subtractions provides a fully renormalization group invariant framework for NN interactions in pionless effective field theory, with the driving terms evolving through a non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik equation to maintain invariance of the K-matrix under changes of the renormalization scale.

## Method

The authors apply the SKM approach to NN scattering in the 1S0 channel using pionless EFT potentials up to NNLO, which consist of Dirac-delta plus derivative contact interactions. They fix the strengths of contact interactions at a reference scale by matching the effective range expansion parameters, then evolve the driving terms of the subtracted Lippmann-Schwinger equation through a non-relativistic Callan-Symanzik equation to slide the renormalization scale. The procedure is implemented recursively, with the number of subtractions increasing at each order (one subtraction for LO, three for NLO, four for NNLO).

## Key numbers

- Scattering length: a = -23.7 fm, used to fix contact interaction strengths at reference scale mu = 0.6 fm^-1
- Effective range: r_e = 2.7 fm, used as matching parameter for the effective range expansion
- Curvature: v_2 = 0.48 fm^3, used as matching parameter for the effective range expansion to order k^4
- Reference scale for NRCS evolution: mu_bar = 0.76 fm^-1, evolved to final scale mu = 0.63 fm^-1
- Relative differences between phase-shifts from initial and NRCS evolved driving terms: smaller than 10^-13 due to numerical errors

## Context

This work extends the subtracted kernel method previously developed by the authors and collaborators (Timoteo et al., 2009; 2010) for renormalizing NN interactions, providing an alternative to the standard cutoff regularization approach used in Weinberg's chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum, 2006; Lepage, 1997). The SKM approach enables analytic treatment for pionless EFT and can be extended to chiral EFT in future work, where the procedure must be performed numerically due to the inclusion of pion-exchange interactions alongside contact terms.
