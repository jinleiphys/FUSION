# Model-independent effects of Delta excitation in nucleon polarizabilities

> Model-independent effects of $Δ$(1232) excitation on nucleon polarizabilities are computed in a Lorentz-invariant fashion. We find a large effect of relative order $(M_Δ- M)/M$ in some of the spin polarizabilities, with the backward spin polarizability receiving the largest contribution. Similar subleading effects are found to be important in the fourth-order spin-independent polarizabilities $α_{Eν}$, $\al_{E2}$, $\be_{Mν}$, and $\be_{M 2}$. Combining our results with those for the model-independent effects of pion loops we obtain predictions for spin and fourth-order polarizabilities which compare favorably with the results of a recent dispersion-relation analysis of data.

## Key claim

The paper computes the complete, model-independent effect of Delta(1232) excitation on nucleon polarizabilities using a manifestly covariant formalism, finding that subleading contributions suppressed by only one power of the Delta-nucleon mass splitting (Delta/M) can be as large as or larger than the leading contributions, especially for the backward spin polarizability gamma_pi.

## Method

The authors compute the s- and u-channel Delta-exchange graphs for Compton scattering using a gauge-invariant and spin-3/2 gauge-invariant gamma N Delta Lagrangian. They expand the resulting amplitudes in powers of photon energy to extract the polarizabilities, then perform a systematic expansion in Delta/M_+ (where M_+ = (M + M_Delta)/2) to separate leading-order (1/Delta^2 for spin polarizabilities) and next-to-leading-order (1/Delta) contributions. They combine these Delta-excitation results with previously computed model-independent pion-loop contributions from chiral perturbation theory and compare the sums to dispersion-relation analyses.

## Key numbers

- Delta = M_Delta - M = 293 MeV (the Delta-nucleon mass splitting)
- The subleading-to-leading ratio for the backward spin polarizability gamma_pi from Delta excitation is: gamma_pi^{NLO} / gamma_pi^{LO} = 8 * Delta * M_+ / M^2 ≈ 2
- For the proton backward spin polarizability, the sum of all model-independent pieces gives gamma_pi^(p) = -34 × 10^(-4) fm^4
- The leading Delta-excitation contribution to the magnetic polarizability is: beta = (e^2/4pi) * (g_M^2) / (2 M_+^2 Delta) = O(1/Delta)
- The leading pion-loop contribution to alpha and beta is: (alpha, beta) = (e^2/4pi) * (g_A/(4pi f_pi))^2 * (1/m_pi) * (5pi/6, pi/12)
- The leading pion-loop contribution to the fourth-order polarizabilities scales as: O(1/m_pi^3) with coefficients (3/4, 7/6, 7, -3) for (alpha_{Enu}, beta_{Mnu}, alpha_{E2}, beta_{M2})

## Context

This work extends the leading-order Delta-excitation calculations of Hemmert et al. (1997, 1998) for alpha, beta, and gamma_1-gamma_4, and of Holstein et al. (2000) for fourth-order polarizabilities, by computing the complete covariant result including subleading 1/Delta contributions. It combines these with the model-independent pion-loop contributions from Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991), Gellas et al. (2000), and McGovern et al. (2000) to obtain predictions that compare favorably with dispersion-relation analyses. The results enable more reliable extraction of nucleon polarizabilities from Compton scattering data and clarify which pieces of these quantities are truly model-independent predictions of chiral effective field theory with an explicit Delta resonance.
