# Improved background subtraction and a fresh look at jet sub-structure in JEWEL

> Interactions of hard partons in the Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP) created with relativistic heavy ion collisions lead to characteristic modifications of the internal structure of reconstructed jets. A large part of the observed jet sub-structure modifications stem from the QGP's response to energy and momentum deposited by hard partons. Good control over medium response in theoretical calculations is thus instrumental to a quantitative understanding of medium modified (quenched) jets in heavy ion collisions. We present an improved way of handling the medium response in the jet quenching model JEWEL and present results for a variety of jet sub-structure observables. The new recoil handling is more versatile and robust than the old scheme, giving a better control over many observables and, in particular, greatly improves the description of the jet mass.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved background subtraction method for the JEWEL event generator that resolves the problem of unphysical negative squared jet masses produced by the previous four-momentum subtraction scheme, while the abstract's claim of "greatly improves the description of the jet mass" is conditional on using event-wise constituent subtraction that preserves particle flavour information.

## Method

The authors implement a constituent subtraction procedure where thermal momenta (medium response particles) are subtracted from final state particles using a distance-ordered pairing algorithm, with transverse momentum and mass subtracted independently between each particle-thermal pair. The subtraction can be performed either on reconstructed jets (jet-wise) or on the entire event before jet reconstruction (event-wise), with the latter allowing preservation of particle flavour information for observables such as charged jet mass. The method is compared to the previous four-momentum subtraction approach in JEWEL 2.3.0 using di-jet samples in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV and 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Negative squared jet masses occur for roughly half of jets with four-momentum subtraction, as shown in the signed mass distribution for R=0.4 anti-kT jets with pT(jet) > 100 GeV and |eta(jet)| < 2.5 at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- The constituent subtraction uses a distance cut-off of 0.5 in Delta R_ik for the pairing of final state particles with thermal momenta
- The QGP medium model uses initial temperature T_i = 485 MeV and proper time tau_i = 0.6 fm for sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, and T_i = 590 MeV with tau_i = 0.4 fm for sqrt(s_NN) = 5.0 TeV
- Jet-hadron correlation results are shown for R=0.3 anti-kT jets with pT(jet) > 100 GeV and 0.3 < |eta(jet)| < 2 at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, including charged particles with pT > 1 GeV
- Groomed jet mass results are shown for R=0.4 anti-kT jets with 160 GeV < pT(jet) < 180 GeV and |eta(jet)| < 1.6, using SoftDrop parameters (z_cut = 0.1, beta = 0) and (z_cut = 0.5, beta = 1.5)

## Context

This work extends the JEWEL jet quenching model development from KunnawalkamElayavalli (2017), which introduced the original four-momentum and grid subtraction methods that showed limitations for jet mass observables. The new constituent subtraction method enables reliable calculation of jet mass and charged jet observables that were problematic with the previous approach, and the authors demonstrate improved agreement with ALICE charged jet mass measurements (Acharya 2017) and CMS jet-hadron correlation measurements (CMS 2013, CMS 2018). The method enables future comparisons of JEWEL predictions with experimental data for observables that depend on full four-momentum information and particle identification.
