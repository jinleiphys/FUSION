# Reply to the comments by I. Angeli and J. Csikai on: 'Cross sections of neutron-induced reactions'

## Key claim

The authors acknowledge that two references (Angeli 1970 and Angeli 1974) were omitted from their original paper, but assert that these omissions do not affect the results and conclusions of the original manuscript (Mukhopadhyay 2010). The authors also defend their use of the term "nuclear Ramsauer model" and clarify that the channel radius, not the potential radius, is energy dependent.

## Method

The authors derive expressions for scattering, reaction, and total neutron cross sections from partial wave analysis, assuming the phase shift is independent of the orbital angular momentum quantum number l and summing partial waves up to kR, where k is the wave number and R is the channel radius. They compare their derivation of the average chord length (4R/3) with Peterson's (1962) optical analogy, arguing that Peterson's treatment of the neutron as a photon leads to physically unacceptable results such as a refractive index less than vacuum and a critical angle for transmission.

## Key numbers

- The parameter γ used for fine-tuning the radius dependence is 0.00793, compared to the leading exponent of 1/3.
- The average chord length inside the nucleus is 4R/3, where R is the nuclear radius.
- Peterson's (1962) result for the average chord length asymptotically approaches 4R/3 at energies higher than the magnitude V of the real part of the nuclear potential.

## Context

This reply addresses comments by Angeli and Csikai regarding omissions of references (Angeli 1970, Angeli 1974) and the proper naming of the model used in Mukhopadhyay (2010). The work extends the semiclassical optical model approach (Angeli 1974) and diffraction effect terminology (Lane 1953), while correcting Peterson's (1962) optical analogy derivation. The clarification of the energy-dependent channel radius versus the constant potential radius enables more accurate parameterization of neutron-induced reaction cross sections.
