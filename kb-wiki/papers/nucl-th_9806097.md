# 3P_2-3F_2 pairing in neutron matter with modern nucleon-nucleon potentials

> We present results for the $^3P_2 - ^3F_2$ pairing gap in neutron matter with several realistic nucleon-nucleon potentials, in particular with recent, phase-shift equivalent potentials. We find that their predictions for the gap cannot be trusted at densities above $ρ\approx 1.7ρ_0$, where $ρ_0$ is the saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter. In order to make predictions above that density, potential models which fit the nucleon-nucleon phase shifts up to about 1 GeV are required.

## Key claim

The predictions of modern phase-shift-equivalent nucleon-nucleon potentials for the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ pairing gap in neutron matter cannot be trusted at densities above approximately 1.7 times the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter ($\rho \approx 1.7\rho_0$), because above that density the potentials must extrapolate the nucleon-nucleon phase shifts beyond their fitted range (below 350 MeV lab energy), and their off-shell behaviors diverge.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled BCS gap equations for the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ channel in neutron matter using the bare nucleon-nucleon interaction as the pairing kernel, with single-particle energies computed both in free space and in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approximation with continuous choice. They employed three independent numerical methods (separable expansion, constant-gap eigenvalue iteration, and direct iteration) to ensure accuracy, and compared results from four modern phase-shift-equivalent potentials (Argonne $V_{18}$, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II) as well as three older potentials (Paris, Argonne $V_{14}$, Bonn B).

## Key numbers

* The $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ pairing gap predictions become unreliable above a density of $\rho \approx 1.7\rho_0$, where $\rho_0$ is the saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter.
* A lab energy of 350 MeV corresponds roughly to a Fermi momentum $k_F = 2.0\;\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$, which is the upper limit of the fitted range of the modern potentials.
* The $^1S_0$ neutron pairing gap shows a peak value of about 3 MeV at a Fermi momentum close to $k_F \approx 0.8\;\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ (cited from prior work).
* The modern potentials (Argonne $V_{18}$, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II) have $\chi^2/\mathrm{datum}$ values of 1.09, 1.03, and approximately 1.0 (for the Nijmegen potentials), respectively, when fitted to scattering data below 350 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ pairing gap by Amundsen and Ostgaard (1985), Baldo et al. (1992), Takatsuka and Tamagaki (1993), Elgaroy et al. (1996), and Khodel et al. (1997), which used older potentials that were not phase-shift equivalent and had higher $\chi^2/\mathrm{datum}$ values (around 2-3). The paper demonstrates that even the new generation of high-precision potentials (Argonne $V_{18}$, CD-Bonn, Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II) give divergent predictions for the gap above $k_F \approx 2.0\;\mathrm{fm}^{-1}$ due to uncontrolled extrapolations of the phase shifts. The results imply that reliable calculations of the $^3P_2$-$^3F_2$ gap at neutron-star core densities require potential models that fit nucleon-nucleon phase shifts up to about 1 GeV, and that medium polarization effects (neglected here) must eventually be included.
