# Cooperative damping mechanism of the resonance in the nuclear photoabsorption

> We propose a resonance damping mechanism to explain the disappearance of the peaks around the position of the resonances higher than the $Δ$ resonance in the nuclear photoabsorption. This phenomenon is understood by taking into account the cooperative effect of the collision broadening of $Δ$ and $N^{*}$, the pion distortion and the interference in the two-pion photoproduction processes in the nuclear medium.

# Cooperative damping mechanism of the resonance in the nuclear photoabsorption

## Key claim
The disappearance of excitation peaks around the $D_{13}(1520)$ resonance in nuclear photoabsorption is explained by a cooperative damping mechanism combining collision broadening of $\Delta$ and $N^{*}$, pion distortion, and interference effects in two-pion photoproduction, without requiring the extremely large resonance widths that earlier simplified models had assumed.

## Method
The authors calculate nuclear photoabsorption cross sections using a Fermi gas model for the nucleus, with three absorption components: quasi-free one-pion photoproduction, quasi-free two-pion photoproduction, and genuine many-body absorption processes. For the two-pion photoproduction, they employ a separable potential model treating $N^{*}$, $\Delta$, and $\rho$ meson resonances, where the $T$-matrix includes $\Delta$ Kroll-Ruderman, $\Delta$ pion-pole, $\rho$ Kroll-Ruderman, and $N^{*}$ terms. Medium modifications are introduced via mass shifts, collision widths (spreading potentials) for $\Delta$ and $N^{*}$, and a pion optical potential for distortion, with the target nucleon momentum approximated as zero in the $T$-matrix elements.

## Key numbers
- $\delta M_{\Delta} = 10$ MeV (mass shift of $\Delta$ in nuclear medium)
- $\Gamma_{\Delta sp} = 80$ MeV (collision width of $\Delta$)
- $\delta M_{N^{*}} = -20$ MeV (mass shift of $N^{*}$)
- $\Gamma_{N^{*} sp} = 80$ MeV (collision width of $N^{*}$, same as $\Delta$)
- Pauli blocking effect is very small ($\leq 5\%$) above 500 MeV photon energy
- The $D_{13}$ resonance has a $\pi N$ branching fraction of 50-60% and a photocoupling at least twice larger than other relevant resonances ($P_{11}(1440)$, $S_{11}(1535)$, $P_{33}(1600)$)

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts by other authors (Refs. [phe1,phe2] in the paper) that assumed collision broadening as the dominant damping mechanism but required very large resonance widths that were not justified by estimates from resonance-nucleon interactions (Ref. [phe3]). The authors build on the $\Delta$-hole model studies of Hirata and Weise for pion-nucleus scattering, and on the two-pion photoproduction models of Tejedor and Oset, and Murphy and Laget. The paper enables a more complete understanding of nuclear photoabsorption in the $D_{13}$ resonance region (500-850 MeV) without invoking unrealistically large collision widths, and the authors state that inclusion of Fermi motion in future calculations would likely lead to even smaller collision widths than the 80 MeV they obtained.
