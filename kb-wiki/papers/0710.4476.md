# Dynamical freeze-out condition in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions

> We determine the decoupling surfaces for the hydrodynamic description of heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC by comparing the local hydrodynamic expansion rate with the microscopic pion-pion scattering rate. The pion $p_T$ spectra for nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC are computed by applying the Cooper-Frye procedure on the dynamical-decoupling surfaces, and compared with those obtained from the constant-temperature freeze-out surfaces. Comparison with RHIC data shows that the system indeed decouples when the expansion rate becomes comparable with the pion scattering rate. The dynamical decoupling based on the rates comparison also suggests that the effective decoupling temperature in central heavy ion collisions remains practically unchanged from RHIC to LHC.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a dynamical freeze-out condition, where the local hydrodynamic expansion rate is compared to the microscopic pion-pion scattering rate, yields decoupling surfaces that reproduce RHIC data when the proportionality constant between the two rates is of order unity (c ~ 1-1.5); this condition then predicts that the effective decoupling temperature in central heavy ion collisions remains practically unchanged from RHIC to LHC, contrary to the naive expectation that stronger flow at LHC would raise the decoupling temperature.

## Method

The authors used boost-invariant ideal hydrodynamics with transverse expansion for azimuthally symmetric (nearly central) collisions, with initial conditions from a pQCD plus saturation model (EKRT). They computed the local expansion rate θ = ∂μuμ and the local pion-pion scattering rate Γ = nπ(T) σππ(T), using a parametrization of the pion cross section that includes the rho resonance peak. The decoupling surface was defined by the condition θ = c Γ, with the constant c fixed by comparison to RHIC data, and the Cooper-Frye procedure was then applied to compute pion pT spectra at both RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature fixed to Tc = 165 MeV at μB = 0.
- For the pion cross section parametrization: σ0 = 0.60 fm², C = 0.78 fm², T0 = 105 MeV, Δ = 170 MeV, valid in the temperature range T < 165 MeV.
- At RHIC (√sNN = 200 GeV Au+Au), constant-temperature freeze-out at Tdec = 150 MeV (BC initial profile) or Tdec = 140 MeV (WN initial profile) reproduces data; Tdec = 120 MeV clearly overshoots the data.
- The dynamical condition with c = 1-1.5 gives a good description of RHIC data; c ≃ 1 gives perhaps the best description for BC initial conditions.
- For LHC (√sNN = 5.5 TeV Pb+Pb), the effective decoupling temperature is predicted to be in the range Tdec = 140-150 MeV.
- The dynamical decoupling curves at the edge of the matter start at Tc = 165 MeV and drop to values of order 100-120 MeV at the center for c ≳ 1.

## Context

This work extends the idea of comparing expansion and scattering rates for freeze-out, first discussed by Dumitru (1999) and Hung (1997), and applies it to predict LHC spectra from RHIC-constrained dynamics. It builds on the authors' previous hydrodynamic studies (Eskola 2005) that used constant-temperature freeze-out, and shows that the dynamical condition supports the earlier assumption that Tdec is similar at RHIC and LHC. The results enable more reliable predictions for pion spectra at LHC and suggest that the effective freeze-out temperature is insensitive to the initial transverse profile (binary collisions vs. wounded nucleons) once total entropy is fixed and RHIC data are used to constrain the decoupling dynamics.
