# Abelian anomaly and neutral pion production

> We show that in fully-self-consistent treatments of the pion; namely, its static properties and elastic and transition form factors, the asymptotic limit of the product Q^2 G_{γ* γπ^0}(Q^2), determined a priori by the interaction employed, is not exceeded at any finite value of spacelike momentum transfer. Furthermore, in such a treatment of a vector-vector contact-interaction one obtains a γ* γ-> π^0 transition form factor that disagrees markedly with all available data. We explain that the contact interaction produces a pion distribution amplitude which is flat and nonvanishing at the endpoints. This amplitude characterises a pointlike pion bound-state. Such a state has the hardest possible form factors; i.e., form factors which become constant at large momentum transfers and hence are in striking disagreement with completed experiments. On the other hand, interactions with QCD-like behaviour produce soft pions, a valence-quark distribution amplitude that vanishes as ~(1-x)^2 for x~1, and results that agree with the bulk of existing data. Our analysis supports a view that the large-Q^2 data obtained by the BaBar Collaboration is not an accurate measure of the γ* γ-> π^0 form factor.

## Key claim

In fully self-consistent treatments of the pion, the asymptotic limit of \(Q^2 G_{\gamma^*\gamma\pi^0}(Q^2)\) is determined a priori by the interaction employed and is never exceeded at any finite spacelike momentum transfer; the analysis supports the view that the large-\(Q^2\) BaBar data are not an accurate measure of the form factor.

## Method

The authors use Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) in rainbow-ladder truncation to compute the \(\gamma^*\gamma \to \pi^0\) transition form factor for two interactions: a QCD-like massless vector-boson exchange and a momentum-independent vector-vector contact interaction. For the contact interaction, a symmetry-preserving regularization with infrared and ultraviolet cutoffs is applied, and the dressed-quark-photon vertex is solved consistently to satisfy the vector Ward-Takahashi identity. The pion's Bethe-Salpeter amplitude is obtained from the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation, and the transition form factor is evaluated from the triangle-diagram expression.

## Key numbers

* The contact interaction yields a constant asymptotic limit for \(G(Q^2,-(m_\pi^2+Q^2)/2,0)\) as \(Q^2\to\infty\), in contrast to the QCD expectation \(\lim_{Q^2\to\infty} Q^2 G(Q^2,-\frac{1}{2}Q^2,0) = 4\pi^2 f_\pi^2\) from Lepage and Brodsky (1980).
* For the doubly-off-shell process \(\gamma^*\gamma^* \to \pi^0\), the QCD-based DSE result gives \(\lim_{Q^2\to\infty} Q^2 G(Q^2,-Q^2-m_\pi^2/2,Q^2) = \frac{2}{3} \, 4\pi^2 f_\pi^2\).
* The neutral-pion interaction radius from the contact interaction is \(r_{\pi^0}^\ast = 0.30\) fm with a bare vertex and \(r_{\pi^0}^{\ast\rho} = 0.45\) fm with a dressed vertex.
* The anomalous Ward-Takahashi identity requires \(G_E(0,0,0) = 1/2\); the contact interaction with the chosen regularization gives \(f_\pi G_E(0,0,0) = 0.36\) unless the regularization is removed.

## Context

This work extends DSE studies of the pion (Maris and Tandy, 1999; Maris and Roberts, 2002) to compare QCD-like interactions with a contact interaction, showing that the latter produces a pointlike pion with form factors that become constant at large momentum transfers. The results contradict the large-\(Q^2\) BaBar data (Aubert, 2009) while agreeing with earlier experiments (Behrend, 1991; Gronberg, 1998). The analysis enables future work to distinguish between soft and pointlike pion descriptions by testing the asymptotic power-law behavior of transition form factors.
