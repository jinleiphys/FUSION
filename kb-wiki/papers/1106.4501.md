# From Fixed Points to the Fifth Dimension

> 4D Lorentzian conformal field theory (CFT) is mapped into 5D anti-de Sitter spacetime (AdS), from the viewpoint of "geometrizing" conformal current algebra. A large-N expansion of the CFT is shown to lead to (infinitely many) weakly coupled AdS particles, in one-to-one correspondence with minimal-color-singlet CFT primary operators. If all but a finite number of "protected" primary operators have very large scaling dimensions, it is shown that there exists a low-AdS-curvature effective field theory regime for the corresponding finite set of AdS particles. Effective 5D gauge theory and General Relativity on AdS are derived in this way from the most robust examples of protected CFT primaries, Noether currents of global symmetries and the energy-momentum tensor. Witten's prescription for computing CFT local operator correlators within the AdS dual is derived. The main new contribution is the derivation of 5D locality of AdS couplings. This is accomplished by studying a confining IR-deformation of the CFT in the large-N "planar" approximation, where the discrete spectrum and existence of an S-matrix allow the constraints of unitarity and crossing symmetry to be solved (in standard fashion) by a tree-level expansion in terms of 4D local "glueball" couplings. When the deformation is carefully removed, this 4D locality (with plausible technical assumptions specifying its precise nature) combines with the restored conformal symmetry to yield 5D AdS locality. The sense in which AdS/CFT duality illustrates the possibility of emergent relativity, and the special role of strong coupling, are briefly discussed. Care is taken to conclude each step with well-defined mathematical expressions and convergent integrals.

## Key claim

The paper derives 5D AdS locality from 4D CFT data by studying a confining IR-deformation of the CFT in the large-N planar approximation, then carefully removing the deformation while combining the resulting 4D locality with restored conformal symmetry, under plausible technical assumptions about the polynomial nature of interaction vertices.

## Method

The author maps 4D Lorentzian CFT into 5D anti-de Sitter spacetime by geometrizing conformal current algebra, introducing a fifth coordinate w > 0 and defining a 5D scalar field φ(x,w) = w^Δ O(x) to make dilatations act purely on coordinates. A large-N expansion of the CFT is shown to produce weakly coupled AdS particles in one-to-one correspondence with minimal-color-singlet CFT primary operators. The derivation of 5D locality proceeds by deforming the CFT to a confining theory with an S-matrix, where unitarity and crossing symmetry constraints are solved by tree-level expansions in terms of 4D local glueball couplings, then removing the deformation while restoring conformal symmetry.

## Key numbers

* The AdS_5 metric is given by ds²_AdS = (R_AdS² / w²) (η_μν dx^μ dx^ν - dw²), where R_AdS is a constant radius of curvature (set to 1 in the paper) and w > 0
* For Lorentz-scalar primary operators, the unitarity bound from spectral decomposition analysis gives Δ ≥ 1, derived from the IR divergence of the two-point function integral ∼ ∫ dm² m^{2Δ-4}
* The conformal algebra is isomorphic to SO(4,2), with J_μν generating the SO(3,1) subgroup and J_μ4 = ½(K_μ - P_μ), J_μ5 = ½(K_μ + P_μ), J_54 = S

## Context

This work extends the foundational AdS/CFT correspondence conjectured by Maldacena (1997) and developed by Witten (1998) and Gubser, Klebanov, and Polyakov (1998), providing a derivation of 5D locality that earlier approaches had left as a central challenge. The paper builds on insights from Heemskerk, Penedones, Polchinski, and Sully (2009) regarding the importance of a large gap in CFT scaling dimensions for a general relativistic effective field theory regime on the AdS side. The derivation enables a general AdS/CFT grammar that does not rely on supersymmetry, string theory, or D-branes, potentially applicable to a wider class of strongly-coupled CFTs and their holographic duals.
