# Finite size effects on kaonic pasta structures

> Non-uniform structures of mixed phases at the first-order phase transition to charged kaon condensation are studied using a density functional theory within the relativistic mean field model. Including electric field effects and applying the Gibbs conditions in a proper way, we numerically determine density profiles of nucleons, electrons and condensed kaons. Importance of charge screening effects is elucidated and thereby we show that the Maxwell construction is effectively justified. Surface effect is also studied to figure out its effect on the density profiles.

## Key claim

The paper shows that when electric field effects and charge screening are properly included in a density functional theory calculation, the Maxwell construction is effectively justified for the first-order phase transition to charged kaon condensation, contradicting earlier claims that a broad mixed-phase region (kaonic pasta) would exist. The structured mixed phase appears only in a limited density range, and its existence depends on the interplay between long-range Coulomb screening and short-range surface effects.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean field (RMF) model within a density functional theory framework, solving coupled equations of motion for nucleons, electrons, kaons, and meson mean fields (sigma, omega, rho) together with the Poisson equation for the Coulomb potential. They divide space into Wigner-Seitz cells of different geometries (sphere, cylinder, slab) and minimize the energy per nucleon to determine the preferred structure shape and size at each baryon density. The short-range meson fields are treated with their gradient terms, while the long-range Coulomb and kaon fields are solved self-consistently to capture screening effects.

## Key numbers

- The kaonic pasta structures appear for baryon densities between 0.41 fm^-3 and 0.74 fm^-3 for a kaon optical potential U_K = -130 MeV.
- For a shallower potential U_K = -120 MeV, the density range narrows to between 0.49 fm^-3 and 0.71 fm^-3.
- The critical density for uniform kaon condensation (single phase) is approximately 0.46 fm^-3.
- The total Debye screening length at rho_B = 0.55 fm^-3 is approximately 3 fm, while the typical Coulomb potential length scale is about 5 fm.
- The nuclear saturation density used is rho_0 = 0.153 fm^-3, with a minimum energy per nucleon of -16.3 MeV, incompressibility K(rho_0) = 240 MeV, and effective nucleon mass m_N*(rho_0) = 0.78 m_N.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of charge screening effects in hadron-quark deconfinement transitions (Voskresensky et al., 2004; Maruyama et al., 2005) and nuclear pasta at sub-nuclear densities (Maruyama et al., 2005) to the case of s-wave kaon condensation. It contradicts earlier claims by Glendenning (1992) and others (GS99, CG00, CGS00) that a broad mixed-phase region with various pasta geometries would occur, showing instead that screening effects largely suppress the mixed phase and justify the Maxwell construction. The results enable more realistic equations of state for neutron star matter that include kaon condensation, with implications for neutron star cooling and supernova collapse scenarios.
