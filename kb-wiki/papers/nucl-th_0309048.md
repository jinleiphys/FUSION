# Neutron Moderation in the Oklo Natural Reactor and the Time Variation of alpha

> In the analysis of the Oklo (gabon) natural reactor to test for a possible time variation of the fine structure constant alpha, a Maxwell-Boltzmann low energy neutron spectrum was assumed. We present here an analysis where a more realistic spectrum is employed and show that the most recent isotopic analysis of samples implies a non-zero change in alpha, over the last two billion years since the reactor was operating, of Δα/α\geq 4.5\times 10^{-8} (6σconfidence). Issues regarding the interpretation of the shifts of the low energy neutron resonances are discussed.

# Neutron Moderation in the Oklo Natural Reactor and the Time Variation of α

## Key claim
When a more realistic neutron spectrum (deviating from the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution due to the presence of a 1/v absorber like uranium) is employed instead of the previously assumed Maxwell-Boltzmann spectrum, the most recent isotopic analysis of Oklo samples implies a non-zero decrease in the fine-structure constant α over the last two billion years, with Δα/α ≥ 4.5×10⁻⁸ at 6σ confidence, though the authors note their results are probably accurate to within 20% and a full MCNP model would be useful.

## Method
The authors modeled the Oklo natural reactor as a homogeneous infinite reactor and estimated the hydrogen-to-uranium atomic density ratio (f_H ≈ 3) and impurity absorption parameter (β ≈ 2 b) required for criticality. They then calculated the neutron flux spectrum including the suppression of low-energy neutrons by 1/v absorption, parameterized by Δ = 2AΣ_a(v_T)/Σ_s, and performed numerical integrations of the effective cross sections for ¹⁴⁹Sm and ¹⁵⁵Gd as functions of resonance energy shift and moderator temperature, replacing the Maxwell-Boltzmann spectrum with spectra derived from Monte Carlo calculations following the procedure in Lamarsh (1966).

## Key numbers
- The effective cross section for ¹⁴⁹Sm derived from Oklo isotopic analysis: ⟨σ⟩₁₄₉ = 91 ± 6 kb
- The derived shift in the ¹⁴⁹Sm resonance energy at 600 K moderator temperature: ΔE_r = [-45 (+7/-15)] × 10⁻³ eV
- The implied change in the fine-structure constant: (α_now − α_past)/α = -[45 (+15/-7)] × 10⁻⁹, non-zero at more than 6σ confidence
- The 95% confidence upper limit on the integrated rate of change: |α̇/α| < 3.8 × 10⁻¹⁷/yr
- For a pure 1/E spectrum (Δ → ∞), two solutions exist for ¹⁴⁹Sm: ΔE_r = (-135 ± 5) × 10⁻³ eV or (-58 ± 5) × 10⁻³ eV
- For ¹⁵⁵Gd, the Oklo data imply ⟨σ⟩₁₅₅ ≈ 28 ± 10 kb, giving ΔE_r = (-90 ± 20) × 10⁻³ eV

## Context
This work extends the earlier analyses by Shlyakhter (1976) and the more detailed studies by Damour and Dyson (1996) and Fujii et al. (2000) of the Oklo natural reactor as a test for time variation of α. The key advance is that the authors show the assumption of a Maxwell-Boltzmann neutron spectrum, which was noted as a potential issue in a footnote by Damour and Dyson (1996) but not considered important, becomes critical at the high precision of the Fujii et al. (2000) results. The finding of a non-zero variation is of opposite sign and an order of magnitude smaller than a recent astrophysical determination by Webb et al. (2001), and the authors note it has been suggested that the time variation of α may not be monotonic. The results enable future work using a full MCNP model of the reactor and could be interpreted in terms of m_q/Λ_QCD, for which the sensitivity is about two orders of magnitude higher.
