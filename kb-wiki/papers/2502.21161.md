# Charge symmetry breaking in hypernuclei within RMF model

> We study the charge symmetry breaking (CSB) effect in the binding energy of mirror hypernuclei in the mass region $A=7\sim 48$ in relativistic mean field (RMF) models introducing $NN$ and $ΛN$ interactions. The phenomenological $ΛN$ CSB interaction is introduced and the strength parameter is fitted to reproduce the experimental binding energy difference between the mirror hypernuclei $^{12}_Λ$B and $^{12}_Λ$C. This model is applied to calculate the CSB energy anomaly in mirror hypernuclei with the mass $A=7\sim48$. The model is further applied to predict the binding energy difference of mirror hypernuclei of $A$=40 with the isospin $T=1/2$, $3/2$ and $5/2$ nuclei together with various hyper Ca isotopes and their mirror hypernuclei. Finally the binding energy systematics of $A=$48 hypernuclei are predicted with/without the CSB effect by the PK1 and TM2 energy density functionals (EDFs).

## Key claim

The paper introduces a phenomenological ΛN charge symmetry breaking (CSB) interaction within a relativistic mean field (RMF) framework, fitted to reproduce the centroid value of the binding energy difference between the mirror hypernuclei ¹²_ΛB and ¹²_ΛC (ΔB_Λ = 0.229 MeV), and then applies this interaction to predict CSB effects in heavier mirror hypernuclei up to A=48, noting that the error bar for ¹²_ΛC remains large.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with PK1 and TM2 energy density functionals for the NN interaction, and include ΛN couplings to σ and ω mesons plus an ωΛΛ tensor term to reproduce the small Λ spin-orbit splitting. A phenomenological ΛN CSB interaction of the form V_CSB^ΛN = (1/2) V_0^ΛN Σ_k ψ̄_Λ γ_μ ψ_Λ ψ̄_k γ^μ τ_3 ψ_k is introduced, with the strength parameter V_0^ΛN fitted to the experimental ΔB_Λ of the A=12 mirror pair ¹²_ΛB and ¹²_ΛC; the Dirac and Klein-Gordon equations are solved iteratively in coordinate space with a box size of 20 fm and step size 0.05 fm.

## Key numbers

- The ΛN CSB strength parameter V_0^ΛN is determined as -33.05 MeV fm³ for PK1 EDF and -38.00 MeV fm³ for TM2 EDF, with upper limits of -63.15 MeV fm³ and -72.00 MeV fm³ and lower limits of -2.85 MeV fm³ and -3.70 MeV fm³, respectively.
- The experimental ΔB_Λ for A=12 mirror hypernuclei (¹²_ΛB minus ¹²_ΛC) is 0.229 MeV, based on B_Λ(¹²_ΛB) = 11.529 ± 0.025 MeV and B_Λ(¹²_ΛC) = 11.30 ± 0.19 MeV.
- For A=7 mirror hypernuclei (⁷_ΛHe, ⁷_ΛBe), the calculated ΔB_Λ is about 0.37 MeV with PK1 EDF, compared to the experimental value of 0.39 MeV.
- For A=40 mirror hypernuclei (⁴⁰_ΛK, ⁴⁰_ΛCa), the calculated ΔB_Λ with CSB is 0.089 ± 0.059 MeV; for A=48 mirror hypernuclei (⁴⁸_ΛCa, ⁴⁸_ΛCo), the calculated ΔB_Λ with CSB is 0.378 ± 0.205 MeV.
- The calculated B_Λ for ⁴⁸_ΛK is 21.08 MeV with CSB and 20.68 MeV without CSB using PK1 EDF.
- The spin-spin CSB contribution to ΔB_Λ is 0.0102 MeV for A=40 and -0.0145 MeV for A=48, negligibly small compared to the total ΔB_Λ values.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of charge symmetry breaking in hypernuclei, which were historically focused on the A=4 systems ⁴_ΛH and ⁴_ΛHe (Dalitz and Von Hippel, 1960s; Nogga, Haidenbauer, and Hiyama, 2000s) and later on p-shell hypernuclei such as A=7 and A=10 (Hiyama, 2009). The paper builds on the phenomenological odd-state CSB interaction introduced by Hiyama (2009) to reproduce both s-shell and p-shell data, and applies it within a relativistic mean field framework to predict CSB effects in heavier systems up to A=48. The results enable future experimental tests at JLab and J-PARC, where production of ⁴⁰_ΛK and ⁴⁸_ΛK via (e, e'K⁺) reactions on ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca targets is planned, and where the J-PARC E94 experiment aims to improve the binding energy measurement of ¹⁰_ΛB.
