# Differential Equation Model for the Ground to First 2+ State Excitation Energy E2 of Even-Even Nuclei

> We propose here a new model termed as the Differential Equation Model for the ground to first 2+ state excitation energy E2 of a given even-even nucleus, according to which the energy E2 is expressed in terms of its derivatives with respect to the neutron and proton numbers. This is based on a similar derivative equation satisfied by its complementary physical quantity namely the Reduced Electric Quadrupole Transition Probability B(E2) in a recently developed model. Although the proposed differential equation for E2 has been perceived on the basis of its close similarity to B(E2), its theoretical foundation otherwise has been clearly demonstrated. We further exploit the very definitions of the derivatives occurring in the differential equation in the model to obtain two different recursion relations for E2, connecting in each case three neighboring even-even nuclei from lower to higher mass numbers and vice-verse. We demonstrate their numerical validity using the available data throughout the nuclear chart and also explore their possible utility in predicting the unknown E2 values.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a Differential Equation Model (DEM) for the first excited state energy E2 of even-even nuclei, where E2 is expressed in terms of its partial derivatives with respect to neutron and proton numbers. The model is derived by analogy with a similar differential equation for B(E2) and is theoretically justified only under the approximations that E2 can be separated into independent functions of N and Z and that these functions are locally linear, meaning the model is not expected to hold strictly at magic numbers or regions of rapidly changing deformation.

## Method

The authors postulate a differential equation relating E2 to its partial derivatives with respect to neutron number N and proton number Z, analogous to a previously developed equation for B(E2). Using forward and backward finite-difference definitions for the derivatives, they derive two recursion relations (E2-F and E2-B) that each connect three neighboring even-even nuclei. They test these recursion relations against the experimental data set of Raman et al. throughout the mass range A=10 to 240, computing percentage errors, and also compare DEM predictions with newer data from Pritychenko et al. for isotopes of Cr, Fe, Ni, and Zn.

## Key numbers

- For the forward recursion relation (E2-F), 284 out of 417 cases lie within plus or minus 25 percent error.
- For the backward recursion relation (E2-B), 278 out of 416 cases lie within plus or minus 25 percent error.
- The experimental E2 values tested range from 0.043 MeV (for Cf-250 and Cm-246) to 2.701 MeV (for Ni-56).
- Predictions are compared with data for isotopes of Z=24, 26, 28, and 30, with DEM values ranging from 0.548 MeV (for Ni-74) to 2.078 MeV (for Ni-54).
- The mass range tested spans A=10 to 240.

## Context

This work extends the Differential Equation Model previously developed by the same authors for B(E2) to the complementary quantity E2, the first 2+ state excitation energy. It builds on earlier difference-equation approaches by Ross and Bhaduri and by Patnaik et al., and is conceptually related to the Infinite Nuclear Matter (INM) model used for nuclear mass predictions. The recursion relations enable prediction of unknown E2 values throughout the nuclear chart, with the main limitation being reduced accuracy near magic numbers (20, 50, 82, 126) and semi-magic numbers (28, 40).
