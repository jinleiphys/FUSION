# Instantaneous Shape Sampling - a model for the $γ$-absorption cross section of transitional nuclei

> The influence of the quadrupole shape fluctuations on the dipole vibrations in transitional nuclei is investigated in the framework of the Instantaneous Shape Sampling Model, which combines the Interacting Boson Model for the slow collective quadrupole motion with the Random Phase Approximation for the rapid dipole vibrations. Coupling to the complex background configurations is taken into account by folding the results with a Lorentzian with an energy dependent width. The low-energy energy portion of the $γ$- absorption cross section, which is important for photo-nuclear processes, is studied for the isotopic series of Kr, Xe, Ba, and Sm. The experimental cross sections are well reproduced. The low-energy cross section is determined by the Landau fragmentation of the dipole strength and its redistribution caused by the shape fluctuations. Collisional damping only wipes out fluctuations of the absorption cross section, generating the smooth energy dependence observed in experiment. In the case of semi-magic nuclei, shallow pygmy resonances are found in agreement with experiment.

## Key claim

The Instantaneous Shape Sampling (ISS) model reproduces experimental gamma-absorption cross sections for transitional nuclei by combining the Interacting Boson Model for slow quadrupole shape fluctuations with the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation for fast dipole vibrations, but only after folding with an energy-dependent collisional damping width that smooths out the fluctuations generated by the shape sampling.

## Method

The authors first used the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) to generate a set of instantaneous deformation parameters (beta_n, gamma_n) and their probabilities P(beta_n, gamma_n) for the ground state of each nucleus. They then performed separate Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA) calculations for each instantaneous shape using a triaxial Woods-Saxon potential with monopole pairing, computing both E1 and M1 dipole absorption cross sections. The total cross section was obtained as the incoherent sum of the instantaneous cross sections weighted by their probabilities, and finally folded with a Breit-Wigner function using an energy-dependent width Gamma(E) = alpha E^2 to account for collisional damping.

## Key numbers

- The energy-dependent width used for collisional damping is Gamma(E) = 0.0111 * E^2, corresponding to Gamma = 2.5 MeV at E = 15 MeV
- The isovector strength constant in the QRPA residual interaction uses the self-consistent form with hbar*omega_0 = 41 * A^(-1/3) MeV and an empirical factor eta approximately 3 fm^(-2)
- The M1 contribution to the total dipole cross section can reach up to 20 percent of the corresponding E1 cross section in the low-energy region
- The M1 strength parameter kappa_s^(t=1) was set to -1 MeV/hbar^2, the same value as in the authors' previous study of Mo isotopes
- The isoscalar coupling constants for removing spurious motion were set to kappa_(t=0) = 1000 MeV fm^(-2) for E1 and kappa_j^(t=0) = 1000 MeV/hbar^2 for M1

## Context

This work extends the ISS approach previously applied to Mo, Zr, and Sr isotopes (Zhang 2009) and to La-139 (Makinaga 2010), now providing systematic calculations for the isotopic series of Kr, Xe, Ba, and Sm. The model addresses the long-standing challenge of describing dipole strength functions in transitional nuclei that undergo large-amplitude shape fluctuations, where traditional approaches using static deformation parameters (such as the two-Lorentzian model used in Hauser-Feshbach codes by Rauscher and Thielemann 2000) or spherical QRPA with phenomenological deformation splitting (Goriely et al., Ripley et al.) are insufficient. The ISS approach enables microscopic predictions of gamma-absorption cross sections in the low-energy region around the particle-emission threshold, which is critical for nuclear astrophysics and nuclear technology applications involving unstable nuclei where experimental measurements are not possible.
