# Nuclear chiral rotation induced by superfluidity

> The microscopic understanding on the influence of the pairing correlations or the superfluidity on the nuclear chiral rotation has been a longstanding and challenging problem. Based on the three-dimensional cranking covariant density functional theory, a shell-model-like approach with exact particle number conservation is implemented to take into account the pairing correlations and applied for the chiral doublet bands in Nd135. The data available are well reproduced. It is found that the superfluidity can reduce the critical frequency and make the chiral rotation easier. The mechanism is that the particle/hole alignments along the short/long axis are reduced by the pairing correlations, resulting in the enhanced preference of the collective rotation along the intermediate axis, and inducing the early appearance of the chiral rotation.

## Key claim

Pairing correlations (superfluidity) can reduce the critical frequency for nuclear chiral rotation and make chiral rotation easier, contrary to earlier microscopic density functional calculations that neglected pairing. The mechanism is that particle/hole alignments along the short/long axes are reduced by pairing, enhancing the preference for collective rotation along the intermediate axis.

## Method

The authors implemented a shell-model-like approach (SLAP) with exact particle number conservation within the three-dimensional cranking covariant density functional theory (3D cranking CDFT) to incorporate pairing correlations. They applied this framework to the chiral doublet bands built on the configuration πh²₁₁/₂ ⊗ νh⁻¹₁₁/₂ in ¹³⁵Nd, using the point-coupling density functional PC-PK1 for the particle-hole channel and a monopole pairing interaction for the particle-particle channel. The many-body Hamiltonian was diagonalized in a truncated many-particle configuration space, and the resulting occupation probabilities were used to self-consistently update the nucleon densities and currents.

## Key numbers

- The effective neutron and proton pairing strengths (Gₙ, Gₚ) are (0.55 MeV, 0.60 MeV), determined by reproducing experimental odd-even mass differences around ¹³⁵Nd.
- Without pairing, the azimuth angle φ remains zero up to ħω = 0.55 MeV, indicating no chiral rotation in that range.
- When the proton pairing strength is enhanced by 150%, the azimuth angle φ becomes nonzero at ħω = 0.47 MeV, indicating the occurrence of chiral rotation.
- When the neutron pairing strength is enhanced by 100%, the azimuth angle φ becomes nonzero at ħω = 0.53 MeV.
- With pairing strengths (Gₙ, Gₚ), the moments of inertia are Jₛ = 17.2 ħ²/MeV, Jᵢ = 36.1 ħ²/MeV, and Jₗ = 18.7 ħ²/MeV.
- For (Gₙ, 2Gₚ), Jₛ is suppressed by 33% relative to (Gₙ, Gₚ); for (2Gₙ, Gₚ), Jₗ is suppressed by 20%.
- The influence of pairing strengths on deformation parameters is within Δβ = 0.04 and Δγ = 6°.

## Context

This work addresses a longstanding problem in nuclear chirality: the microscopic understanding of pairing correlations on chiral rotation, which had been neglected in previous self-consistent density functional calculations such as those by Olbratowski (2004) and Zhao (2017). The study extends the 3D cranking CDFT framework by adding exact particle-number-conserving pairing via SLAP, building on prior work by Zeng (1983, 1994) and Meng (2006). The results enable future microscopic studies of chiral rotation in open-shell nuclei where pairing is significant, and provide a mechanism for the earlier onset of chiral rotation than predicted by calculations without pairing.
