# Generating spin polarization from vorticity through nonlocal collisions

> We derive the collision term in the Boltzmann equation using the equation of motion for the Wigner function of massive spin-1/2 particles. To next-to-lowest order in $\hbar$ it contains a nonlocal contribution, which is responsible for the conversion of orbital into spin angular momentum. In a proper choice of pseudo-gauge the antisymmetric part of the energy-momentum tensor arises solely from this nonlocal contribution. We show that the collision term vanishes only in global equilibrium and that the spin potential is then equal to the thermal vorticity. In the nonrelativistic limit, the equations of motion for the energy-momentum and spin tensors reduce to the well-known form for hydrodynamics for micropolar fluids.

## Key claim

The paper derives a nonlocal collision term in the Boltzmann equation for massive spin-1/2 particles, showing that this term is responsible for converting orbital angular momentum into spin angular momentum. The collision term vanishes only in global equilibrium, where the spin potential equals the thermal vorticity, but the authors note that away from global equilibrium nonlocal collisions always imply dissipative dynamics.

## Method

The authors start from the Dirac equation with a general interaction term and derive the transport equation for the Wigner function of massive spin-1/2 particles. They perform an expansion in powers of ℏ (a gradient expansion) and compute the collision term up to first order in ℏ, separating local and nonlocal contributions. The nonlocal collision term is derived by accounting for finite impact parameters in binary scattering processes, leading to position shifts in the distribution functions that are of first order in ℏ.

## Key numbers

- The position shift for a particle with momentum p and spin ms is given by Δ^μ = −(ℏ/2m(p·t̂+m)) ε^{μναβ} p_ν t̂_α ms_β, where t̂^μ = (1,0) is the time-like unit vector in the frame where p^μ is measured.
- The integration measure over spin variables is ∫ dS(p) ≡ (1/κ(p)) ∫ d⁴ms δ(ms·ms+3) δ(p·ms), with κ(p) ≡ √3 π/√p².
- The equilibrium distribution function has the form f_eq(x,p,ms) = 1/(2πℏ)³ exp[−β(x)·p + (ℏ/4) Ω_{μν}(x) Σ_ms^{μν}].

## Context

This work extends the kinetic theory approach to spin hydrodynamics, building on the nonrelativistic treatment of Hess (1966) who first pointed out that local collision terms cannot describe polarization through rotation. The paper provides a systematic derivation from quantum field theory that confirms the condition Ω_{μν} = ϖ_{μν} (spin potential equals thermal vorticity) in global equilibrium, previously known from statistical quantum field theory (Becattini, 2012) and for massless particles (Chen, 2015). The results enable the future derivation of relativistic dissipative spin hydrodynamics using the method of moments (Denicol, 2012).
