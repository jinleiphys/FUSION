# Effect of Nuclear Deformation on Electron Capture Cross-section on Chromium Isotopes

> The electron capture plays significant role in the pre supernova and supernova evolutions of massive stars which in turn are of great importance in synthesizing heavy elements beyond iron. In this paper we study the effect of nuclear deformation on the computed electron capture cross section on selected even even chromium isotopes (464850Cr). The nuclear deformation parameters were computed using two different theoretical models: Interacting Boson Model (IBM 1) and Macroscopic (Yukawa plus exponential) microscopic (Folded Yukawa) model (Mac mic model). A third value of deformation parameter was adopted from experimental data. We chose the pn QRPA model to perform our calculations. The predictive power of the chosen model was first tested by calculating Gamow Teller (GT) strength distributions of selected fp shell nuclei where measured GT data was available. The calculated GT strength distributions were well fragmented over the energy range 0 12 MeV and were noted to be in decent agreement with experimental data. The total GT strength was found to increase (decrease) with decrease (increase) in the value of deformation parameter for the three chromium isotopes. The computed GT strength distributions satisfied the model independent Ikeda sum rule. The ECC were calculated as a function of the deformation parameter at core temperature 1.0 MeV. Our results show that the calculated ECC increased with increasing value of nuclear deformation.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for the three even-even chromium isotopes studied (⁴⁶Cr, ⁴⁸Cr, ⁵⁰Cr), the computed electron capture cross-section (ECC) increases with increasing value of nuclear deformation parameter, but the authors explicitly state they are not in a position to generalize this finding and that further investigation is in progress.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a Nilsson deformed basis to calculate Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions and electron capture cross-sections for ⁴⁶Cr, ⁴⁸Cr, and ⁵⁰Cr. Three different nuclear deformation parameters (β₂) were employed: one calculated using the Interacting Boson Model (IBM-1), one using the Macroscopic (Yukawa-plus-exponential) – microscopic (Folded-Yukawa) model (Mac-mic model), and one adopted from experimental B(E2)↑ data from Raman (2001). The model was first validated by comparing calculated GT strength distributions for ⁴²Ti, ⁴⁶Cr, ⁵⁰Fe, and ⁵⁴Ni against measured data and shell model calculations using KB3G and GXPF1a interactions.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴²Ti, measured GT strength from β-decay experiment was available up to excitation energy E_ex = 1.888 MeV in the daughter nucleus; charge exchange reaction data (⁴²Ca(³He, t)) was measured up to E_ex = 3.688 MeV.
- For ⁴⁶Cr, measured GT strength from β-decay experiment was available up to E_ex = 3.867 MeV; from ⁴⁶Ti(³He, t) charge exchange reaction up to E_ex = 5.717 MeV.
- For ⁵⁰Fe, measured GT strength from β-decay experiment was available up to E_ex = 4.315 MeV; from charge exchange reaction up to E_ex = 5.545 MeV.
- For ⁵⁴Ni, measured GT strength from β-decay experiment was available up to E_ex = 5.202 MeV; from ⁵⁴Fe(³He, t) charge exchange reaction up to E_ex = 5.917 MeV.
- The oscillator constant for both neutrons and protons was taken as ħω = 41A^(1/3); pairing gaps were taken as Δ_p = Δ_n = 12/√A MeV.
- The ratio g_A/g_V was taken as -1.254 (Hardy, 2009); the constant D was taken as 6143 s (Nakamura, 2010).
- As core temperature increased from 0.5 MeV to 1.0 MeV, the computed ECC increased on average by two orders of magnitude; further increase from 1.0 MeV to 1.5 MeV gave only marginal increase (less than a factor of 2).
- ECC was calculated at stellar temperatures of 0.5, 1.0, and 1.5 MeV; the core temperature for deformation dependence study was fixed at 1.0 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of electron capture cross-sections on fp-shell nuclei by Nabi (2019) to specifically investigate the role of nuclear deformation on chromium isotopes. The study validates the pn-QRPA model against experimental GT strength data for iron-regime nuclei and against shell model calculations using KB3G and GXPF1a interactions (Kumar, 2016). The results enable future investigation of deformation effects on electron capture for a broader set of nuclei, including oblate deformations, which is stated as work in progress.
