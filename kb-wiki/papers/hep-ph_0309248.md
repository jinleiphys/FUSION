# Chiral dynamics with strange quarks

> In the first part of the talk, I review what we know (or rather do not know) about the structure of the QCD vacuum in the presence of strange quarks. Chiral perturbation theory allows to study reactions of pions and kaons and to further sharpen our understanding of symmetry violation in QCD. I review recent progress on the description of pion-kaon scattering, in particular concerning isospin violation and the extraction of threshold and resonance parameters from Roy-Steiner equations. In the third part, it is shown how a unitary extension of chiral perturbation theory leads to novel insight into the structure of the Lambda(1405).

## Key claim

The talk reviews evidence that the three-flavor quark condensate may be strongly suppressed relative to the two-flavor condensate, with a central value indicating a 54% suppression, but the uncertainties are large enough to allow marginal consistency with the standard scenario where the condensate is not strongly suppressed.

## Method

The author reviews chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) calculations for pion-kaon scattering, including isospin-violating corrections to threshold parameters, and compares them to results from a new dispersive analysis using Roy-Steiner equations. For the Lambda(1405) resonance, a non-perturbative coupled-channel approach matched to CHPT is used to dynamically generate the resonance, and the pole trajectories are studied as a function of an SU(3) symmetry-breaking parameter.

## Key numbers

- The three-flavor condensate relative to the two-flavor condensate: Sigma(3) = Sigma(2) * [1 - 0.54 +/- 0.27], indicating a central suppression of 54% with large uncertainty
- CHPT predictions for S-wave pi-K scattering lengths (in units of M_pi^-1): a_0^{1/2} = 0.18 +/- 0.03, a_0^{3/2} = -0.05 +/- 0.02
- Dispersive analysis results for S-wave pi-K scattering lengths: a_0^{1/2} = 0.22 +/- 0.02, a_0^{3/2} = -0.045 +/- 0.008
- Mass of the spin-1 K* meson from P-wave isospin 1/2 phase crossing: 905 +/- 3 MeV, compared to the PDG value of 891.7 +/- 0.3 MeV
- Isospin-violating corrections for pi-K atoms: epsilon = 1.3 +/- 1.2%, epsilon' = 1.1 +/- 3.2%
- Theoretical charge radius of Sigma-: 0.67 +/- 0.03 fm^2, compared to SELEX experimental result: 0.61 +/- 0.12 +/- 0.09 fm^2
- In the SU(3) limit, the singlet pole appears at 1390 MeV and the octet pole at 1426 MeV, which combine to form the Lambda(1405)

## Context

This talk extends the long-standing work on chiral perturbation theory with strange quarks, building on earlier CHPT calculations by Bernard, Kaiser, and Meissner (1991) for pion-kaon scattering and on the dispersive analysis by Buttiker, Descotes-Genon, and Moussallam. The two-pole nature of the Lambda(1405) was earlier found in the cloudy bag model by Fink et al. (1990) and is here confirmed in a chiral coupled-channel framework, enabling experimental tests through photoproduction reactions at facilities such as SPRING-8, JLab, and ELSA.
