# Dynamic scaling of order parameter fluctuations in model B

> We describe numerical simulations of the stochastic diffusion equation with a conserved charge. We focus on the dynamics in the vicinity of a critical point in the Ising universality class. The model we consider is expected to describe the critical dynamics near a possible QCD critical point if the coupling of the order parameter to the momentum density of the fluid can be neglected. The simulations are performed on a spatial lattice, and the time evolution is performed using a Metropolis algorithm. We determine the dynamical critical exponent $z\simeq 3.972(2)$, which agrees with predictions of the epsilon expansion. We also study non-equilibrium sweeps of the reduced temperature and observe approximate Kibble-Zurek scaling.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Metropolis algorithm can simulate stochastic diffusion (model B) dynamics for a conserved order parameter near a critical point in the Ising universality class, but only if the coupling of the order parameter to the momentum density of the fluid can be neglected; the actual QCD critical point is expected to be governed by model H, not model B.

## Method

The authors discretize the Ising Hamiltonian on a three-dimensional cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions and evolve the field using a Metropolis algorithm that updates fluxes between adjacent lattice sites to exactly conserve the total charge. For model B, they implement a fourfold checkerboard pattern that updates pairs of cells by applying a stochastic flux at their interface, with the trial update accepted according to the Metropolis probability. They measure the dynamic density-density correlation function at the critical point and extract the dynamical critical exponent from finite-size scaling, and also study non-equilibrium sweeps of the reduced temperature by linearly varying the mass parameter.

## Key numbers

- Dynamical critical exponent: z = 3.972(2), extracted from finite-size scaling of the relaxation time at the critical point for lattice sizes L = 8, 12, 16, 24
- Critical mass parameter: m_c^2 = -2.28587(7) for lambda = 4, determined from Binder cumulants in model A and carried over to model B
- Correlation length exponent from model B fits: nu = 0.542, with m_c^2 = -2.267 (consistent with but less precise than the conformal bootstrap value nu = 0.6299(5))
- Relaxation time at criticality for the slowest mode: tau_R approximately 2 x 10^(-3) L^z
- Kibble-Zurek scaling exponent p from correlation function collapse: p = 0.08-0.16, compared to the theoretical prediction p = nu/(z*nu+1) approximately 0.16

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier study of model A dynamics (Schaefer 2022) to the case of a conserved order parameter (model B), which is relevant for understanding critical fluctuations near a possible QCD critical point in heavy ion collisions. The results agree with the dynamic renormalization group prediction z = 4 - eta from Hohenberg and Halperin (1977) and with conformal bootstrap determinations of eta by El-Showk (2014). The demonstration of approximate Kibble-Zurek scaling supports the use of this framework to estimate critical fluctuation magnitudes in far-from-equilibrium systems, and the long-term goal is to generalize the method to model H, which couples the order parameter to fluid momentum density as required for the QCD critical point.
