# Chiral spin symmetry and hot QCD

> In this talk we overview main results indicating existence in QCD of three qualitatively different regimes connected by smooth crossovers upon heating: a hadron gas, a stringy fluid and a quark-gluon plasma. In the combined large N_c and chiral limit these regimes likely become distinct phases separated by phase transitions: a chiral restoration phase transition around T_{ch} ~ 130 MeV and a deconfinement phase transition around T_d ~ 300 MeV. It should be an important task to verify this issue on the lattice. We will introduce a chiral spin symmetry, which is a symmetry of the electric part of electrodynamics and of QCD with light quarks. It is realized approximately in QCD above the chiral restoration crossover and disappears in the QGP regime. The center symmetry of the pure glue action and the chiral spin symmetry of the electric part of the QCD Lagrangian with light quarks are complementary to distinguish the confining regime and its disappearance. We also address other lattice evidences for stringy fluid: hadron resonances extracted from the lattice correlators; breakdown of the thermal perturbation theory at T < ~ 600 MeV and fluctuations of conserved charges that point out the N_c scaling above T ~ 155 MeV.

# Chiral spin symmetry and hot QCD

## Key claim

QCD exhibits three qualitatively different regimes connected by smooth crossovers upon heating, hadron gas, stringy fluid, and quark-gluon plasma, and in the combined large N_c and chiral limit these likely become distinct phases separated by phase transitions at T_ch ~ 130 MeV and T_d ~ 300 MeV, but this remains to be verified on the lattice.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD results for spatial and temporal correlators of all J=0,1 isovector bilinears at multiple temperatures, using N_F=2 QCD with a chirally symmetric Dirac operator and 2+1+1 QCD. Screening masses from pseudoscalar and vector channels are analyzed across temperatures from ~155 MeV to 160 GeV, and fluctuations of conserved quark numbers are compared to the hadron resonance gas model. The Polyakov loop temperature evolution is examined to track deconfinement.

## Key numbers

- Chiral restoration crossover temperature: T_ch ~ 155 MeV (physical quark masses) and T_ch ~ 130 MeV (large N_c, chiral limit)
- Deconfinement phase transition temperature: T_d ~ 300 MeV (large N_c, chiral limit); deconfinement crossover around T_d ~ 300 MeV (N_c=3)
- Chiral spin symmetry persists up to T ~ 500 MeV, then smoothly disappears
- Thermal perturbation theory breaks down below T < 500–600 MeV
- Screening masses at very high T (1–160 GeV) are parameterized by perturbative expansion with coefficients p_2, p_3, p_4, s_4 fixed by EQCD calculations
- Polyakov loop reaches ~0.5 at T ~ 400–500 MeV
- Fluctuations of conserved charges deviate from hadron resonance gas model above T ~ 155 MeV
- N_c scaling: hadron gas quantities scale as N_c^0, stringy fluid as N_c^1, QGP as N_c^2

## Context

This work extends the author's prior introduction of chiral spin symmetry (Glozman, 2010, 2011) and its review (Glozman, 2019) to the hot QCD regime. It synthesizes lattice results from multiple groups: screening mass studies by Dalla Brida et al. (2022) and Bazavov et al. (2019), spectral function reconstructions by L. Ya. Glozman and collaborators (2022, 2023), and Polyakov loop calculations by Petreczky and Schadler (2015). The proposed three-regime picture contradicts the simple two-phase (hadron gas vs. QGP) paradigm and suggests that the intermediate "stringy fluid" regime, where chiral symmetry is restored but confinement persists, can be tested via large N_c lattice simulations of the chiral restoration temperature.
