# Meson-meson scattering lengths at maximum isospin from lattice QCD

> We summarize our lattice QCD determinations of the pion-pion, pion-kaon and kaon-kaon s-wave scattering lengths at maximal isospin with a particular focus on the extrapolation to the physical point and the usage of next-to-leading order chiral perturbation theory to do so. We employ data at three values of the lattice spacing and pion masses ranging from around 230 MeV to around 450 MeV, applying Luescher's finite volume method to compute the scattering lengths. We find that leading order chiral perturbation theory is surprisingly close to our data even in the kaon-kaon case for our entire range of pion masses.

## Key claim

Leading-order chiral perturbation theory (LO χPT) is surprisingly close to the lattice QCD data for all three meson-meson systems (ππ, πK, KK) at maximal isospin across the entire range of pion masses studied (230–450 MeV), with deviations not exceeding a few percent even at the heaviest pion masses.

## Method

The authors employ lattice QCD with Nf=2+1+1 twisted mass fermions on gauge ensembles from the European Twisted Mass Collaboration at three lattice spacings and pion masses from 230 to 450 MeV. They apply Lüscher's finite volume method to extract s-wave scattering lengths for ππ (I=2), πK (I=3/2), and KK (I=1) systems, then extrapolate to the physical point and continuum limit using next-to-leading order (NLO) chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

- ππ scattering length: Mπ a0 = -0.0442(2)_stat(+4/-0)_sys (dimensionless, at physical point in continuum limit)
- KK scattering length: MK a0 = -0.385(16)_stat(+0/-12)_ms(+0/-5)_ZP(4)_rf (dimensionless, at physical point in continuum limit)
- πK scattering length: μπK a0 = -0.05937(82)_stat(139)_fr(64)_tp(9)_χPT (dimensionless, at physical point in continuum limit)
- Pion masses used: 230 MeV to 450 MeV (range across ensembles)
- Three lattice spacings employed (no explicit numerical values given in text)

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD determinations of meson-meson scattering lengths by CP-PACS (2004), NPLQCD (2006, 2008), ETM (2013), Yagi et al. (2011), Fu (2013), and PACS-CS (2013) for ππ; NPLQCD (2007) and PACS-CS (2013) for KK; and Lang et al. (2012), Janowski et al. (2014), Fu (2011), Sasaki et al. (2013), and Beane et al. (2006) for πK. It is the first study to employ multiple lattice spacings and a wide range of pion masses simultaneously for all three systems, enabling controlled continuum and chiral extrapolations. The results enable future global fits combining all three systems with meson masses and decay constants, and would benefit from configurations directly at the physical point.
