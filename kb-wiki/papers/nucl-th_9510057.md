# Quasifree Pion Electroproduction from Nuclei in the $Δ$ Region

> We present calculations of the reaction $A(e,e^\prime πN)B$ in the distorted wave impulse approximation. The reaction allows for the study of the production process in the nuclear medium without being obscured by the details of nuclear transition densities. First, a pion electroproduction operator suitable for nuclear calculations is obtained by extending the Blomqvist-Laget photoproduction operator to the virtual photon case. The operator is gauge invariant, unitary, reference frame independent, and describes the existing data reasonably well. Then it is applied in nuclei to predict nuclear cross sections under a variety of kinematic arrangements. Issues such as the effects of gauge-fixing, the interference of the $Δ$ resonance with the background, sensitivities to the quadrupole component of the $Δ$ excitation and to the electromagnetic form factors, the role of final-state interactions, are studied in detail. Methods on how to experimentally separate the various pieces in the coincidence cross section are suggested. Finally, the model is compared to a recent SLAC experiment.

## Key claim

The paper presents a theoretical framework for analyzing exclusive, quasifree pion electroproduction from complex nuclei in the Δ(1232) resonance region, but the authors stress that the results are predictions rather than fits, as no parameters were readjusted to match data, all parameters were already fixed by the Blomqvist-Laget photoproduction operator.

## Method

The authors extended the Blomqvist-Laget photoproduction operator to the virtual photon case by introducing electromagnetic form factors at vertices, including terms proportional to ε·k that vanish in photoproduction, and adding the longitudinal quadrupole C2 term. They then applied this operator within a Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) framework, using harmonic oscillator wave-functions for bound nucleons, a non-relativistic reduction of the Clark global optical model for outgoing nucleons, and the SMC optical model for outgoing pions. Gauge invariance was restored using an ad hoc method that defines a new conserved current J′^μ = J^μ − k^μ(k_νJ^ν)/k^2, which does not change the interaction in the Lorentz gauge.

## Key numbers

- The kinematic singularity in the c.m. frame occurs when ω^c vanishes; for W = 1230 MeV this happens at approximately −k² = 0.63 (GeV/c)².
- The missing momentum (momentum transfer to the recoiling nucleus) is considered small when less than 300 MeV/c, where the impulse approximation is expected to hold.
- The virtual photon equivalent energy is defined as K = (W² − m²)/(2m), where m is the nucleon mass.
- The invariant mass threshold for pion electroproduction is W ≥ m + m_π ≈ 1080 MeV.
- The kinematic singularity in the c.m. frame can occur for 4-momentum transfer starting at −k² = m²_π + 2m m_π ≈ 0.24 (GeV/c)².
- Separated cross sections are shown at k² = −1 (GeV/c)², where agreement with data is described as excellent.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DWIA approach for photoproduction (Li et al.) to the electroproduction case A(e,e′πN)B, which had no prior theoretical treatment in the Δ region according to the authors. The reaction enables study of Δ excitation and propagation in the nuclear medium while reducing sensitivity to nuclear transition densities compared to traditional A(γ,π)B reactions (as discussed in the book by the authors). The framework is designed for comparison with the first such experiment at SLAC and anticipates future data from high duty cycle accelerators including LEGS at Brookhaven, Bates, NIKHEF, MAMI, and CEBAF.
