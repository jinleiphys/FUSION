# Quantum Symmetry Restoration and Emergent Effective Deformation in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Classically deformed nuclear geometries are commonly employed in standard descriptions of relativistic collisions between two even-even nuclei, despite the fact that their exact ground states are rotationally invariant $0^+$ states. In this paper, we formulate the collision geometry directly from the eikonal scattering matrix based on a nonorthogonal Generator Coordinate Method construction of rotationally invariant ground states. In the optical limit, using a localized transported-density approximation for the collision-channel one-body response, rotational overlap localization generates an effective one-body density associated with the scattering process. Within this approximation, using the Gaussian Overlap Approximation and its heat-kernel representation, we show that rotational symmetry restoration acts as a geometric low-pass filter which exponentially suppresses effective deformation modes. The classical rigid-rotor limit is recovered for large intrinsic angular momentum fluctuations. We establish a microscopic framework connecting rotational symmetry restoration, collective overlap localization, and the effective deformation geometries of nuclei in high energy collisions.

# Quantum Symmetry Restoration and Emergent Effective Deformation in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim

Rotational symmetry restoration in even-even nuclei acts as a geometric low-pass filter that exponentially suppresses effective deformation modes in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, with the suppression factor governed by the intrinsic angular momentum fluctuation $\langle\hat{J}_y^2\rangle$ rather than the intrinsic deformation alone; the classical rigid-rotor limit is recovered only for large $\langle\hat{J}_y^2\rangle$, meaning that standard phenomenological Glauber models that assume classically deformed nuclear geometries are only valid in this semiclassical limit.

## Method

The authors formulate the collision geometry by evaluating the eikonal scattering matrix on a nonorthogonal Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) rotational manifold, constructing rotationally invariant $0^+$ ground states for even-even nuclei. They employ a localized transported-density approximation for the collision-channel one-body response, then use the Gaussian Overlap Approximation (GOA) with its heat-kernel representation on the rotational manifold to analytically evaluate the orientation integrals. The effective one-body density is expanded in spherical harmonics, yielding a closed-form expression for the effective deformation parameters $\beta_l^{\rm eff}$ in terms of the intrinsic deformation parameters $\beta_l^{\rm intr}$ and the angular momentum fluctuation $\langle\hat{J}_y^2\rangle$.

## Key numbers

- The effective deformation parameters are given by $\beta_l^{\rm eff} = \beta_l^{\rm intr} \exp\left[ -l(l+1) / (2\langle\hat{J}_y^2\rangle) \right]$, where the exponential suppression factor depends on the multipole order $l$ and the intrinsic angular momentum fluctuation $\langle\hat{J}_y^2\rangle$.
- For a test case using the geometric overlap kernel $D(\theta) = \cos^8\theta$ (motivated by Elliott's SU(3) model for $^{20}$Ne), the exact suppression factors are $R_2^{\rm exact} = 0.727$, $R_4^{\rm exact} = 0.336$, and $R_6^{\rm exact} = 0.090$; the heat-kernel approximation yields $R_2^{\rm HK} = 0.687$, $R_4^{\rm HK} = 0.287$, and $R_6^{\rm HK} = 0.072$.
- The GOA heat-kernel representation maps the localization width to $\tau = 1/(2\langle\hat{J}_y^2\rangle)$, connecting the quantum overlap kernel to a rotational diffusion parameter in collective space.

## Context

This work addresses the conceptual inconsistency between the exact rotationally invariant $0^+$ ground states of even-even nuclei and the classically deformed nuclear geometries used in standard Monte Carlo Glauber models (Glauber, 1959; Miller et al., 2007) and energy-deposition methods such as trento (Moreland, 2014). It extends a recent study by Ke (2025) that incorporated quantum orientation superposition into the Glauber model for light deformed nuclei like $^{20}$Ne, showing rotational coherence reduces observed eccentricity. The framework establishes a microscopic connection between rotational symmetry restoration (Ring and Schuck; Bohr, 1951; Hill, 1952; Elliott, 1958), collective overlap localization, and effective deformation geometries, enabling future development of next-generation event generators that sample from the correlated hierarchy $\rho_{\rm eff}^{[n]}$ rather than relying on independent-particle factorization.
