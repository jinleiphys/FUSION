# Charge-Symmetry-Breaking Three-Nucleon Forces

> Leading-order three-nucleon forces that violate isospin symmetry are calculated in Chiral Perturbation Theory. The effect of the charge-symmetry-breaking three-nucleon force is investigated in the trinucleon systems using Faddeev calculations. We find that the contribution of this force to the 3He - 3H binding-energy difference is approximately 5 keV.

# Charge-Symmetry-Breaking Three-Nucleon Forces

## Key claim
The leading-order charge-symmetry-breaking three-nucleon force contributes approximately 5 keV to the ³He–³H binding-energy difference, but this value depends on the unknown quark-mass portion of the nucleon-mass difference δM_N^qm, taken here as −2.5 MeV for illustration. The actual contribution scales linearly with δM_N^qm, which is not yet determined model-independently.

## Method
The authors derived the leading isospin-violating three-nucleon force in Chiral Perturbation Theory (χPT) without an explicit Δ-isobar, using a field redefinition that eliminates the nucleon-mass difference from the free Lagrangian. They implemented this force in Faddeev calculations for the trinucleon systems, regulating the Yukawa function consistently with the Tucson-Melbourne (TM′) force, and used perturbation theory to compute the ³He–³H binding-energy difference.

## Key numbers
- The CSB three-nucleon force contributes 5 keV to the ³He–³H binding-energy difference (using δM_N^qm = −2.5 MeV).
- The total theoretical sum of all CSB mechanisms is 760(22) keV, compared to the experimental value of 764 keV.
- The Coulomb potential contributes 648 keV, the Breit interaction 28 keV, the kinetic-energy difference 14 keV, and the two-body CSB forces 65(22) keV.
- The naive dimensional analysis estimate for the CSB three-nucleon force is 50 keV, a factor of about 3 larger than the calculated value.
- The scattering-length difference a_nn − a_pp is −1.5(5) fm, attributed to CSB two-body forces.

## Context
This work extends the authors’ prior χPT calculations of isospin-violating two-nucleon forces (Friar et al., 2003; 2004) to three-nucleon forces for the first time. It completes the χPT treatment of isospin violation through the first three orders. The result is consistent with CSB extractions for A=6–10 systems and does not upset the agreement between theory and experiment for the trinucleon binding-energy difference. The calculation enables future determinations of δM_N^qm from pion-production experiments or lattice QCD, and provides a framework for including sub-leading electromagnetic three-nucleon forces.
