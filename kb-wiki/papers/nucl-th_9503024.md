# Quark Matter 95: Concluding Remarks

> 14 pages, latex, 9 uuencoded figures, revised text

## Key claim

The paper summarizes the state of the field at the Quark Matter 95 conference, arguing that global characteristics of high-energy nuclear collisions (such as entropy production) are under control and consistent with existing models, but that detailed probes of baryon stopping, collective flow, strangeness, and rare probes remain unresolved and may require new physics beyond conventional hadronic transport.

## Method

The author reviews experimental results presented at the Quark Matter 95 conference, comparing them to predictions from several event generators (HIJING, VENUS, RQMD, FRITIOF, ARC) and to lattice QCD calculations. The analysis covers data from the AGS (Au+Au at 11 AGeV), SPS (Pb+Pb at 160 AGeV, S+S at 200 AGeV), and Bevalac/SIS energies, focusing on charged particle multiplicities, transverse energy, collective flow, strangeness production, dilepton spectra, J/psi suppression, and open charm production.

## Key numbers

- Pb+Pb reactions at 160 AGeV produce approximately 1600 (pi, p, K) particles per event, close (20%) to model expectations.
- The compressibility K = 210 MeV from the Urbana UV14+UVII force is consistent with nuclear breathing modes and reproduces collective flow data from 0.2 to 1.2 AGeV.
- Baryon densities up to 10 times nuclear saturation density (10 rho_0) are estimated to be generated at AGS energies.
- The K/pi ratio in A+Au at 11 AGeV shows a power dependence of K/pi ~ A_proj^0.13 from A_proj = 1 to 197.
- The NA45/CERES collaboration reports an excess of dilepton pairs in S+Au in the mass region 2 m_pi < m < 1.5 GeV by a factor of 5 +/- 0.7 +/- 2 compared to p+A extrapolations.
- Lattice QCD results for N_f=2 show the transition temperature T_c near 150 MeV, with perturbative Stefan-Boltzmann behavior approximately holding above T > 2 T_c ~ 300 MeV.

## Context

This concluding talk reviews the first Pb+Pb data at the CERN/SPS (160 AGeV) and Au+Au data at the BNL/AGS (11 AGeV), extending the work presented at Quark Matter 93. The paper contrasts the success of hadronic transport models (such as HIJING, VENUS, RQMD) in describing global observables with the unresolved questions about baryon stopping, the nature of the QCD phase transition, and the interpretation of strangeness enhancement and J/psi suppression. The results set the stage for future experiments at RHIC (expected by 1999) and LHC, and highlight the need for improved theoretical understanding of nuclear structure functions, color rope effects, and the equation of state at high baryon density.
