# Fate of charmed mesons near chiral symmetry restoration in hot matter

> Chiral thermodynamics of charmed mesons is formulated at finite temperature within a $2+1+1$-flavored effective Lagrangian incorporating heavy quark symmetry. The charmed-meson mean fields act as an extra source which breaks the chiral symmetry explicitly. This leads to effective interactions between the light and heavy-light mesons, which intrinsically depend on temperature. Effective masses of the scalar and pseudoscalar charmed-mesons tend to approach each other as increasing temperature, so that the splitting between the chiral partners is reduced. These chiral splittings are shown to be less sensitive to the light-quark flavors, attributed to the underlying heavy quark symmetry. Consequently, chiral symmetry restoration is more manifest for the strange charmed-mesons than for the strange light mesons. The effective masses are also compared with the results in the one-loop chiral perturbation theory. A substantial difference is found at a relatively low temperature, $T \sim f_π$.

## Key claim

The chiral mass splittings between scalar and pseudoscalar charmed mesons are essentially insensitive to light-quark flavors (non-strange vs. strange), a consequence of heavy quark symmetry; this "blindness" contrasts with the behavior of light mesons and holds despite non-negligible explicit chiral SU(3) breaking.

## Method

The author formulates a chiral mean-field theory for light and heavy-light mesons at finite temperature using a 2+1+1-flavored effective Lagrangian that incorporates heavy quark symmetry. The charmed-meson mean fields act as an extra source of explicit chiral symmetry breaking, leading to temperature-dependent effective interactions between light and heavy-light mesons. To avoid an unrealistic strong mixing between the light-flavor and charmed meson sectors, the author introduces temperature-dependent couplings (particularly \(g_\pi^s(T)\) and \(k_s(T)\)) that are constrained by lattice QCD chiral condensate profiles, and solves the gap equations for the mesonic mean fields self-consistently.

## Key numbers

* The pseudo-critical temperature of chiral symmetry restoration is fixed to \(T_{\rm pc} = 154\) MeV, extracted from lattice QCD chiral susceptibility.
* At \(T_{\rm pc}\), the chiral mass splittings for both non-strange and strange charmed mesons are \(\delta M_D(T_{\rm pc}) \sim \delta M_{D_s}(T_{\rm pc}) \sim 200\) MeV.
* The non-strange scalar charmed meson mass drops by \(\sim 200\) MeV and the strange scalar meson mass by \(\sim 100\) MeV as temperature increases toward \(T_{\rm pc}\).
* The vacuum parameters include: \(m_c = 1.27\) GeV, \(M_D(0^-) = 1.868\) GeV, \(M_{D_s}(0^-) = 1.969\) GeV, \(M_{D_s}(0^+) = 2.318\) GeV, \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, \(f_K = 113\) MeV.
* The model predicts \(M_D(0^+)/M_D^{\rm PDG}(0^+) = 0.96\) relative to the PDG value \(2318 \pm 29\) MeV.
* The coupling constant \(g_\pi^q = 3.78\) is consistent with the PDG value \(g_\pi^{\rm PDG} = 3.6\) (ratio 0.95).
* A substantial difference between the mean-field and one-loop chiral perturbation theory results appears at a relatively low temperature \(T \sim f_\pi \sim 0.67 m_\pi\).

## Context

This work extends chiral effective theory treatments of charmed mesons (e.g., the chiral SU(4) approach of Su4 (2004) and the heavy-quark-symmetry-based framework of NRZ and BH) by providing a self-consistent mean-field calculation at finite temperature that avoids the unrealistic mixing seen in earlier models. The key result, that chiral mass splittings in the heavy-light sector are flavor-blind due to heavy quark symmetry, contrasts with the SU(4) model prediction where \(\delta M_D\) is much smaller than \(\delta M_{D_s}\). The paper enables future studies of charmed meson dissociation in hot matter and applications to heavy-ion collision observables such as the nuclear modification factor and elliptic flow, as well as reexamination of charmonium suppression scenarios (RBC).
