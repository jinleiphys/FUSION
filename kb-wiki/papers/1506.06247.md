# Baryon mass splittings and strong CP violation in SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We study $SU(3)$ flavor-breaking corrections to the relation between the octet baryon masses and the nucleon-meson CP-violating interactions induced by the QCD $\barθ$ term. We work within the framework of $SU(3)$ chiral perturbation theory and work through next-to-next-to-leading order in the $SU(3)$ chiral expansion, which is $\mathcal{O}(m_q^2)$. At lowest order, the CP-odd couplings induced by the QCD $\barθ$ term are determined by mass splittings of the baryon octet, the classic result of Crewther et al. We show that for each isospin-invariant CP-violating nucleon-meson interaction there exists one relation which is respected by loop corrections up to the order we work, while other leading-order relations are violated. With these relations we extract a precise value of the pion-nucleon coupling $\bar g_0$ by using recent lattice QCD evaluations of the proton-neutron mass splitting. In addition, we derive semi-precise values for CP-violating coupling constants between heavier mesons and nucleons with $\sim 30\%$ uncertainty and discuss their phenomenological impact on electric dipole moments of nucleons and nuclei.

## Key claim
The authors show that for each isospin-invariant CP-violating nucleon-meson interaction induced by the QCD \(\bar\theta\) term, there exists exactly one relation to baryon mass splittings that is preserved by loop corrections up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO), while other leading-order relations are violated; this allows a precise extraction of the isoscalar coupling \(\bar g_0\) from the proton-neutron mass splitting, but no robust relation exists for the isovector coupling \(\bar g_1\).

## Method
The authors work within the framework of \(SU(3)\) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, computing octet baryon masses and CP-odd meson-nucleon couplings induced by the QCD \(\bar\theta\) term through next-to-next-to-leading order in the chiral expansion, which is \(\mathcal{O}(m_q^2)\). They include one-loop diagrams with octet and decuplet intermediate states, tree-level contributions from the \(\mathcal{O}(q^4)\) Lagrangian, and identify which leading-order relations between mass splittings and CP-violating couplings are preserved by higher-order corrections.

## Key numbers
- The isoscalar CP-violating pion-nucleon coupling \(\bar g_0\) is extracted using the relation to the proton-neutron mass splitting \(\delta m_N\), with lattice QCD evaluations of \(\delta m_N\) allowing a determination with \(10\%\) accuracy when considering only lattice uncertainties.
- The leading-order relation between \(\bar g_0\) and the mass difference of the \(\Xi\) and \(\Sigma\) baryons gives a value that differs by about \(50\%\) from the value obtained using the proton-neutron mass splitting, indicating large \(SU(3)\) flavor-breaking corrections.
- Semi-precise values for CP-violating coupling constants between heavier mesons and nucleons are derived with \(\sim 30\%\) uncertainty.
- The current experimental limit on the neutron electric dipole moment is \(|d_n| < 2.9 \times 10^{-13} \, e\) fm, bounding \(\bar\theta < 10^{-10}\).
- The quark mass parameters used are \(\bar{m} = 3.42 \pm 0.09\) MeV, \(m_s/\bar{m} = 27.46 \pm 0.44\), and \(\varepsilon = 0.37 \pm 0.03\) from the FLAG lattice average.

## Context
This work extends the classic result of Crewther et al. (1979), which related CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings to baryon mass splittings at leading order in chiral perturbation theory. The authors quantify the \(SU(3)\) flavor-breaking corrections that cause the leading-order relations to differ by about \(50\%\), and identify which relations remain robust through N\(^2\)LO. The results enable more precise extractions of CP-violating couplings from lattice QCD calculations of baryon masses, which are needed to interpret electric dipole moment measurements of nucleons and nuclei in terms of the QCD \(\bar\theta\) term and to constrain new sources of CP violation.
