# Nuclear Matter and Nuclear Dynamics

> Highlights on the recent research activity, carried out by the Italian Community involved in the "Nuclear Matter and Nuclear Dynamics" field, will be presented.

## Key claim

This paper reviews recent research by the Italian nuclear physics community on nuclear matter and nuclear dynamics, but the abstract's claim of presenting "highlights" glosses over the fact that the review primarily summarizes existing work rather than presenting new original results, and that the density dependence of the symmetry energy remains largely unconstrained despite the studies discussed.

## Method

The paper reviews microscopic many-body calculations using Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone (BBG) theory with two-body forces (CD Bonn, Argonne v18) and three-body forces (phenomenological Urbana model and meson-exchange approaches) to compute the nuclear equation of state. Transport simulations based on the Boltzmann-Langevin equation are employed to model heavy ion collisions from low to relativistic energies, testing two representative parametrizations of the symmetry energy (Asysoft and Asystiff) against observables such as prompt dipole gamma-ray emission and isospin transport in dissipative collisions.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy at sub-saturation density approximately described by E_sym/A = 31.3 (rho/rho_0)^0.6, where rho_0 is saturation density
- Two representative parametrizations used: Asysoft with C(rho)/rho_0 = 482 - 1638 rho (MeV fm^3) and Asystiff with constant C = 32 MeV or linearly increasing C(rho)
- Isoscalar compressibility fixed around 220 MeV for both parametrizations
- Prompt dipole gamma emission studied for reactions at 10 AMeV with impact parameter b = 4 fm
- Imbalance ratio analysis performed for Sn+Sn collisions at 35 and 50 AMeV

## Context

This review extends the line of work on constraining the nuclear equation of state, particularly the symmetry energy, which was previously studied by Cons (year not given) for symmetric matter up to five times saturation density. The paper discusses how microscopic many-body calculations by Baldo (2008) and Lombardo (2008) show that three-body forces reduce differences between EOS predictions from different two-body interactions, but the high-density behavior remains uncertain. The work enables future experimental studies with radioactive beams at facilities like SPIRAL2 to better constrain the symmetry energy through observables such as prompt dipole radiation and isospin diffusion, building on earlier transport code developments by Chomaz (year not given) and Baran (year not given).
