# Improved Honeycomb and Hyperhoneycomb Lattice Hamiltonians for Quantum Simulations of Non-Abelian Gauge Theories

> Improved Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonians for quantum simulations of non-Abelian Yang-Mills gauge theories are developed for honeycomb (2+1D) and hyperhoneycomb (3+1D) spatial tessellations. This is motivated by the desire to identify lattices for quantum simulations that involve only 3-link vertices among the gauge field group spaces in order to reduce the complexity in applications of the plaquette operator. For the honeycomb lattice, we derive a classically ${\cal O}(b^2)$-improved Hamiltonian, with $b$ being the lattice spacing. Tadpole improvement via the mean-field value of the plaquette operator is used to provide the corresponding quantum improvements. We have identified the (non-chiral) hyperhoneycomb as a candidate spatial tessellation for 3+1D quantum simulations of gauge theories, and determined the associated ${\cal O}(b)$-improved Hamiltonian.

## Key claim

The authors derive classically improved Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonians for non-Abelian Yang-Mills gauge theories on honeycomb (2+1D) and hyperhoneycomb (3+1D) spatial lattices, achieving O(b²) improvement for the honeycomb case and O(b) improvement for the hyperhoneycomb case, where b is the lattice spacing. The improvement relies on gauge-fixing and specific combinations of loop operators, with the hyperhoneycomb identified as a non-chiral alternative to triamond lattices that avoids complications from chirality in higher-order improvement.

## Method

The authors perform Symanzik improvement by expanding plaquette operators in powers of the lattice spacing b using a specific gauge-fixing condition (A_y=0 in 2+1D; A_z=0, A_x=0 at z=0, A_y=0 at x=z=0 in 3+1D) and combining different loop sizes to cancel leading lattice-spacing artifacts. For the honeycomb lattice, they combine 6-link and 10-link loops to remove O(b²) errors in the magnetic Hamiltonian, and apply non-local smearing of the electric field operator with n=3 links. For the hyperhoneycomb lattice, they combine 10-link and 12-link loops to achieve O(b) improvement. Tadpole improvement is incorporated via mean-field renormalization of the link operators using the expectation value of the smallest plaquette.

## Key numbers

- Honeycomb lattice cell area: S_hex = 3√3 b²/2
- The classically improved magnetic Hamiltonian for honeycomb combines 6-link and 10-link loops with coefficients 14/9 and -5/36 respectively, yielding O(b⁷) accuracy
- Electric field improvement for honeycomb uses coefficients 53/54 for the local term and 1/54 for the non-local term with n=3
- Hyperhoneycomb cell volume: V_HHC = 9b³
- The 10-link loop contribution gives Tr[G_xy² + 6G_xz² + 6G_yz²] while the 12-link loop gives Tr[G_xy²]; their combination with coefficient 5/4 for the 12-link term yields the correct magnetic Hamiltonian with O(b⁶) accuracy
- Tadpole improvement factor for honeycomb: u_hex⁶ = 1 + (1/(2N_c))⟨ψ|P_6 + P_6†|ψ⟩
- Tadpole improvement factor for hyperhoneycomb: u_HHC¹⁰ = 1 + (1/(2N_c))⟨ψ|P_10 + P_10†|ψ⟩

## Context

This work extends the Symanzik improvement program, previously applied to cubic lattices by Moore (1996), Luo (1998), and Carlsson (2001, 2003), to honeycomb and hyperhoneycomb spatial tessellations for quantum simulation of non-Abelian gauge theories. It builds on the honeycomb lattice formulation introduced by Muller (2023) and Turro (2024, 2025), and the triamond lattice proposal by Kavaki and Lewis (2024, 2025), identifying the hyperhoneycomb as a non-chiral alternative that simplifies higher-order improvement. The work enables more efficient quantum simulations of 2+1D and 3+1D Yang-Mills theories with reduced lattice-spacing artifacts, and strengthens connections between lattice gauge theory and quantum spin liquid models such as Kitaev's honeycomb model (2005) and its 3D extensions.
