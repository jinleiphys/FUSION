# String dynamics and metastability of fully-heavy tetraquarks

## Key claim

Full-charm $(cc\bar c\bar c)$ and full-beauty $(bb\bar b\bar b)$ tetraquarks are found to be unbound in a rigorous four-body treatment of current quark models, meaning their stability would require more subtle effects beyond the simple constituent quark picture. The $(bc\bar b\bar c)$ configuration might be more favorable, but only if the naive color-additive model of confinement is replaced by a string-inspired interaction.

## Method

The authors solve the four-body problem for fully-heavy tetraquarks using hyperspherical harmonic expansions and correlated Gaussian wave functions within a non-relativistic constituent quark model. They compare the color-additive chromoelectric potential (Eq. 1) with string-inspired confinement models including the double Y-shape ("butterfly") interaction (Eq. 5) and the flip-flop interaction (Eq. 6), and also examine the chromomagnetic interaction (Eq. 7) as a separate contribution.

## Key numbers

- $(cc\bar c\bar c)$ and $(bb\bar b\bar b)$ are found to be **unbound** (no binding energy given; the paper states they lie above their dissociation threshold)
- For a purely linear string potential with unit strength and unit quark mass, the equal-mass tetraquark $(QQ'\bar Q\bar Q')$ has energy $\simeq 4.639$, compared to a threshold of $2\,[Q\bar Q] = 4.676$ (twice the negative of the first zero of the Airy function)
- From this binding, the stability domain for $(Q_1Q_2\bar Q_1\bar Q_2)$ extends to at least $1/1.72 < M_1/M_2 < 1.72$ for a purely linear interaction
- The $(bb\bar u\bar d)$ system is predicted to achieve stability with current quark models (no numerical binding energy given in the text)

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of tetraquark stability including Zouzou et al. (1986) on $(QQ\bar q\bar q)$ as a function of mass ratio, and Vijande et al. (2007) on string-inspired confinement for tetraquarks. It contradicts the claim by Lloyd and Vary (2003) of a stable $(cc\bar c\bar c)$, which the authors note was received with skepticism. The paper enables future investigations of fully-heavy pentaquarks and hexaquarks, and clarifies that the diquark-antidiquark approximation used by Karliner and Rosner (2016) and Bai et al. (2016) may artificially lower the chromoelectric contribution. The metastability of $(bc\bar b\bar c)$ below the $(b\bar c)+(c\bar b)$ threshold but above the $(b\bar b)+(c\bar c)$ threshold is analogous to the hydrogen-antihydrogen molecule problem studied by Froelich et al. (2004).
