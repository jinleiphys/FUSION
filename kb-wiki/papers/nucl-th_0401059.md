# Addendum: Attenuation of the intensity within a superdeformed band

> We investigate a random matrix model [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 65} 024302 (2002] for the decay-out of a superdeformed band as a function of the parameters: $Γ^\downarrow/Γ_S$, $Γ_N/D$, $Γ_S/D$ and $Δ/D$. Here $Γ^\downarrow$ is the spreading width for the mixing of an SD state $|0>$ with a normally deformed (ND) doorway state $|d>$, $Γ_S$ and $Γ_N$ are the electromagnetic widths of the the SD and ND states respectively, $D$ is the mean level spacing of the compound ND states and $Δ$ is the energy difference between $|0>$ and $|d>$. The maximum possible effect of an order-chaos transition is inferred from analytical and numerical calculations of the decay intensity in the limiting cases for which the ND states obey Poisson and GOE statistics. Our results show that the sharp attenuation of the decay intensity cannot be explained solely by an order-chaos transition.

## Key claim

The sharp attenuation of decay intensity within a superdeformed band cannot be explained solely by an order-chaos transition in the normally deformed states; the spin dependence of the deformation barrier (which determines the spreading width ratio Gamma_down/Gamma_S) is of more importance, even when the energy difference between the superdeformed state and the normally deformed doorway state is nonzero.

## Method

The authors calculated the average intraband decay intensity I_av for a superdeformed band using a random matrix model with a strong doorway assumption (model B from Sargeant (2002)). They evaluated I_av in the limiting cases where the normally deformed states obey Poisson statistics (regular, lambda=0) and Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE) statistics (chaotic, lambda=1), using Breit-Wigner and box function representations for the strength function in the Poisson limit. The decay intensity was studied as a function of four dimensionless parameters: Gamma_down/Gamma_S, Gamma_N/D, Gamma_S/D, and Delta/D, where Delta is the energy difference between the SD state and the ND doorway state.

## Key numbers

- For Hg-194-1 at spin 12 hbar: D = 16.3 eV, Gamma_N = 4.8 x 10^{-3} eV, Gamma_S = 97 x 10^{-6} eV (these values from Krucken (2001) are used throughout the calculations)
- The GOE limit yields I_av^{GOE} = (1 + Gamma_down/Gamma_S)^{-1}, valid when Gamma_S + Gamma_down is much less than L
- Krucken (2001) obtained a spreading width Gamma_down = 0.025 eV (Gamma_down/Gamma_S = 258) from an experimental total intraband decay intensity of 0.58 at spin 12 hbar, giving I_av^{GOE} = 1/259
- When Gamma_down/Gamma_S = 0.01, an order-chaos transition reduces I_av from 1 to 0.99 at most (for Delta much greater than 0)

## Context

This addendum responds to criticism by Aberg (2003) of the authors' earlier work (Sargeant (2002)), which had assumed zero energy difference between the SD state and the ND doorway state. The present study extends that work by allowing nonzero energy difference Delta and calculating the maximum possible effect of an order-chaos transition. The results reinforce the authors' earlier conclusion that the decay-out is mostly due to the spin dependence of the barrier, contrary to Aberg's suggestion (1999) that chaos-assisted tunneling could explain the sharp attenuation. The work enables more careful extraction of the spreading width from experimental data, though the sensitivity to the unknown parameter Delta complicates such extractions.
