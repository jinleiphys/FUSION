# Tests on the molecular structure of $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$ from $ψ(nS)$ and $Υ(nS)$ decays

> Based on previous studies that support the vector-vector molecular structure of the $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, $\bar{K}^{*\,0}_2(1430)$, $f_0(1370)$ and $f_0(1710)$ resonances, we make predictions for $ψ(2S)$ decay into $ω(ϕ) f_2(1270)$, $ω(ϕ) f'_2(1525)$, $K^{*0}(892) \bar{K}^{*\,0}_2(1430)$ and radiative decay of $Υ(1S),Υ(2S), ψ(2S)$ into $γf_2(1270)$, $γf'_2(1525)$, $γf_0(1370)$, $γf_0(1710)$. Agreement with experimental data is found for three available ratios, without using free parameters, and predictions are done for other cases.

## Key claim

The paper extends tests of the vector-vector molecular structure of the tensor resonances $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, and $K^*_2(1430)$ from $J/\psi$ decays to $\psi(2S)$ and $\Upsilon(nS)$ decays, assuming that the SU(3) singlet nature and the OZI-violation parameter $\nu$ are the same for all heavy quarkonium states. The agreement with three available experimental ratios is good within large uncertainties, but the authors note that some ratios differ by up to a factor of 40, so the test is not yet decisive.

## Method

The authors use the formalism previously developed for $J/\psi$ decays into a spectator vector meson ($\phi$, $\omega$, or $K^*$) plus a pair of interacting vector mesons that rescatter to form a tensor resonance. The transition amplitude for each decay channel is given by $t = \sum_j W_j G_j g_j$, where $W_j$ are SU(3) weights, $G_j$ are loop functions for the intermediate vector-vector states, and $g_j$ are the couplings of the resonance to those states. For radiative decays, the photon is assumed to be radiated from the initial $c\bar{c}$ or $b\bar{b}$ state (not from the final vector mesons), and the same formalism is applied with different weights $\widetilde{w}_j$. The only change from $J/\psi$ to $\psi(2S)$ or $\Upsilon(nS)$ is the phase-space factor from the different parent mass in the width formula $\Gamma = (1/8\pi)(1/M^2)|t|^2 q$.

## Key numbers

- For $\psi(2S)$ decays: $\widetilde{R_4} = \Gamma_{\psi(2S)\to K^{*0} \bar{K}^{*0}_2(1430)} / \Gamma_{\psi(2S)\to\omega f_2(1270)}$: theory = $1.41^{+0.82}_{-0.53}$, experiment = $0.86^{+0.47}_{-0.32}$ (from PDG, BES 2004, BES 1998).
- For $\psi(2S)$ decays: $\widetilde{R_1}\cdot\widetilde{R_3} = \Gamma_{\psi(2S)\to\omega f_2(1270)} / \Gamma_{\psi(2S)\to\phi f'_2(1525)}$: theory = $2.64^{+3.1}_{-1.4}$, experiment = $5.0^{+4.3}_{-2.0}$.
- For $\Upsilon(1S)$ radiative decays: $\widetilde{R_T} = \Gamma_{\Upsilon(1S)\to\gamma f_2(1270)} / \Gamma_{\Upsilon(1S)\to\gamma f'_2(1525)}$: molecular picture = $1.84 \pm 0.92$, data = $2.66^{+1.13}_{-0.70}$ (from CLEO).
- For $J/\psi$ radiative decays: $R_T = \Gamma_{J/\psi\to\gamma f_2(1270)} / \Gamma_{J/\psi\to\gamma f'_2(1525)}$: molecular picture = $2 \pm 1$, data = $3.18^{+0.58}_{-0.64}$.
- For $\Upsilon(1S)$ radiative decays: $\widetilde{R_S} = \Gamma_{\Upsilon(1S)\to\gamma f_0(1370)} / \Gamma_{\Upsilon(1S)\to\gamma f_0(1710)}$: prediction = $1.05 \pm 0.26$ (no data available).

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of $f_2(1270)$, $f'_2(1525)$, and $K^*_2(1430)$ as dynamically generated from vector-vector interactions, previously tested in $J/\psi$ decays by Dai and Oset (2012) and in radiative $J/\psi$ decays by Hanhart, Krewald, and Meissner (2010). The predictions for $\psi(2S)$ and $\Upsilon(nS)$ decays provide new tests that can discriminate between the molecular picture and a simple quark model, which gives different predictions for the ratio $R_S/R_T$ (about half the quark model value). Future measurements of the predicted ratios for scalar channels and for $\psi(2S)$ and $\Upsilon(2S)$ radiative decays will further constrain the nature of these resonances.
