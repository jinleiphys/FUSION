# Strange hadron production in pp, pPb and PbPb collisions at LHC energies

> We present a systematic analysis of transverse momentum $(p_{T})$ spectra of the strange hadrons in different multiplicity events produced in pp collision at $\sqrt{s}$ = 7 TeV, pPb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV and PbPb collision at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 2.76 TeV. Both the single and differential freeze out scenarios of strange hadrons $K^0_s$, $Λ$ and $Ξ^-$ are considered while fitting using a Tsallis distribution which is modified to include transverse flow. The $p_{T}$ distributions of these hadrons in different systems are characterized in terms of the parameters namely, Tsallis temperature $(T)$, power $(n)$ and average transverse flow velocity $(β)$. It is found that for all the systems, transverse flow increases as we move from lower to higher multiplicity events. In the case of the differential freeze-out scenario, the degree of thermalization remains similar for events of different multiplicity classes in all the three systems. The Tsallis temperature increases with the mass of the hadrons and also increases with the event multiplicity in pp and pPb system but shows little variation with the multiplicity in PbPb system. In the case of the single freeze-out scenario, the difference between small systems (pp, pPb) and PbPb system becomes more evident. The high multiplicity PbPb events show higher degree of thermalization as compared to the events of pp and pPb systems. The trend of variation of the temperature in PbPb system with event multiplicity is opposite to what is found in the pp and pPb systems.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified Tsallis distribution with transverse flow can characterize strange hadron production across pp, pPb, and PbPb collisions, but the behavior of the extracted parameters (temperature, power, flow velocity) with event multiplicity differs between the small systems (pp, pPb) and the large PbPb system, particularly in the single freeze-out scenario where the temperature trend in PbPb is opposite to that in pp and pPb.

## Method

The authors fitted the transverse momentum spectra of K⁰_s, Λ, and Ξ⁻ hadrons measured by the CMS experiment using a Tsallis distribution modified to include an average transverse flow velocity β. They performed two types of fits: individual fits to each hadron species (differential freeze-out scenario) and simultaneous combined fits to all three hadrons (single freeze-out scenario), extracting the Tsallis temperature T, power parameter n, and average transverse flow velocity β for each multiplicity class in pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV, pPb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, and PbPb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV.

## Key numbers

- pp collisions at √s = 7 TeV: six multiplicity classes with average track multiplicities ⟨N_trk⟩ = 14, 51, 79, 111, 134, and 161; highest β values in differential freeze-out range 0.4-0.5 for all hadrons
- pPb collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV: eight multiplicity classes with ⟨N_trk⟩ = 21, 57, 89, 125, 159, 195, 236, and 280; highest β values 0.4-0.5 for K⁰_s and Λ, and 0.75-0.85 for Ξ⁻ in differential freeze-out
- PbPb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV: eight multiplicity classes with ⟨N_trk⟩ = 21, 58, 92, 130, 168, 210, 253, and 299; highest β values 0.3-0.4 for K⁰_s and Λ, and 0.75-0.85 for Ξ⁻ in differential freeze-out
- In single freeze-out scenario for pp: n decreases to ∼5 for high multiplicity classes; β reaches up to 0.5 in the three highest multiplicity bins
- In single freeze-out scenario for pPb: n decreases to ∼6 for high multiplicity classes; β saturates at ∼0.5 for largest multiplicity bins
- In single freeze-out scenario for PbPb: n has higher value ∼12 compared to smaller systems; β increases up to 0.2 for the highest multiplicity class
- χ²/NDF values for individual fits range from 0.013 to 0.439 for pp, 0.015 to 0.111 for pPb, and 0.023 to 0.507 for PbPb
- χ²/NDF values for combined fits range from 0.261 to 1.989 for pp, 0.202 to 1.559 for pPb, and 0.421 to 1.954 for PbPb

## Context

This work extends previous Tsallis distribution analyses of hadron spectra in heavy ion collisions, such as those by Khandai (2013) and Sett (2015), by applying the modified Tsallis function with transverse flow to strange hadrons across three different collision systems at LHC energies. The study builds on the observation of collectivity in small systems by Ghosh (2014) and differential freeze-out scenarios suggested by Lao (2015) and Wei (2016). The results enable systematic comparison of thermalization and collective flow between small (pp, pPb) and large (PbPb) systems, showing that the single freeze-out scenario reveals qualitative differences in temperature and thermalization behavior between system sizes.
