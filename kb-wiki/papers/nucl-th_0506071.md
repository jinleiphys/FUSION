# Strange-quark contribution to the ratio of neutral- to charged-current cross sections in neutrino-nucleus scattering

> A formalism based on a relativistic plane wave impulse approximation is developed to investigate the strange-quark content ($g_{A}^{s}$) of the axial-vector form factor of the nucleon via neutrino-nucleus scattering. Nuclear structure effects are incorporated via an accurately calibrated relativistic mean-field model. The ratio of neutral- to charged-current cross sections is used to examine the sensitivity of this observable to $g_{A}^{s}$. For values of the incident neutrino energy in the range proposed by the FINeSSE collaboration and by adopting a value of $g_{A}^{s}=-0.19$, a 30% enhancement in the ratio is observed relative to the $g_{A}^{s}=0$ result.

## Key claim

The ratio of neutral- to charged-current cross sections in neutrino-nucleus scattering on carbon shows a 30% enhancement when the strange-quark contribution to the axial-vector form factor is set to \(g_{A}^{s} = -0.19\) relative to the \(g_{A}^{s} = 0\) result, but only for incident neutrino energies in the range proposed by the FINeSSE collaboration (500 and 1000 MeV) and under the assumption that final-state interactions between the outgoing proton and the residual nucleus can be neglected.

## Method

The authors developed a relativistic plane wave impulse approximation formalism for neutrino-nucleus scattering, incorporating nuclear structure effects via an accurately calibrated relativistic mean-field model (NL3 parameter set) for the target nucleus \({}^{12}\)C. They computed the differential cross section for both neutral-current (\(\nu + X \rightarrow \nu + p + X\)) and charged-current (\(\nu + n \rightarrow \mu^{-} + p\)) reactions, then formed the ratio \(R(NC/CC)\) of these cross sections. The strange-quark contribution was introduced through the axial-vector form factor \(G_A(Q^2)\) at \(Q^2 = 0\), parameterized as \(g_A^s\), and the sensitivity of the ratio to this parameter was examined.

## Key numbers

- For incident neutrino energy \(E_k = 500\) MeV and \(g_A^s = -0.19\), a 30% enhancement in the ratio \(R(NC/CC)\) is observed relative to the \(g_A^s = 0\) result.
- The threshold laboratory energy for the elementary process \(\nu + n \rightarrow \mu^{-} + p\) is approximately 112 MeV.
- For \({}^{12}\)C, the NL3 parameter set predicts binding energies \(E_B(1s^{1/2}) \approx 53\) MeV and \(E_B(1p^{3/2}) \approx 19\) MeV.
- For \(E_k = 200\) MeV, the cross section for knockout of the \(1s^{1/2}\) neutron shows a sharp cut-off at outgoing proton kinetic energy \(T_{p'} \approx 40\) MeV.
- The Cabbibo angle cosine is \(\cos\theta_C = 0.974\).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of van der Ventel and Piekarewicz (2004) for neutral-current neutrino-nucleus scattering to the charged-current case, incorporating a finite muon mass. It builds on the proposal by Garvey and collaborators (1992, 1993) to extract the strange-quark contribution to the nucleon spin from cross-section ratios, and addresses nuclear-structure corrections that were important in experiment E374 at Brookhaven National Laboratory. The results enable the FINeSSE collaboration to interpret their planned measurements of the NC/CC ratio on carbon targets, with the finding that final-state interactions between the outgoing proton and the residual nucleus appear to factor out from the ratio, as suggested by Meucci (2004) and Martinez-Pinedo (2005).
