# Dissociation of heavy quarkonia in an anisotropic hot QCD medium in a Quasi-Particle Model

> The present article is the follow up work of, Phys.\ Rev.\ D {\bf 94}, 094006 (2016), where we have extended the study of quarkonia dissociation in (momentum) anisotropic hot QCD medium. As evident by the experimentally observed collective flow at RHIC and LHC, the momentum anisotropy is present at almost all the stages after the collision and therefore, it is important to include its effects in the analysis. Employing the in-medium (corrected) potential while considering the anisotropy (both oblate and prolate cases) in the medium, the thermal widths and the binding energies of the heavy quarkonia states (s-wave charmonia and s-wave bottomonia specifically, for radial quantum numbers n = 1 and 2) have been determined. The hot QCD medium effects have been included employing a quasi-particle description. The presence of anisotropy has modified the potential and then the thermal widths and the binding energies of these states in a significant manner. The results show a quite visible shift in the values of dissociation temperatures as compared to the isotropic case. Further, the hot QCD medium interaction effects suppress the dissociation temperature as compared to the case where we consider the medium as a non-interacting ultra-relativistic gas of quarks (anti-quarks) and gluons.

## Key claim

The inclusion of momentum anisotropy (both oblate and prolate) in the hot QCD medium, modeled via a quasi-particle description, significantly shifts the dissociation temperatures of heavy quarkonia compared to the isotropic case, with the hot QCD medium interaction effects further suppressing these temperatures relative to a non-interacting ultra-relativistic gas.

## Method

The authors employed the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) to incorporate hot QCD medium effects, introducing momentum anisotropy at the level of the distribution function by stretching (prolate, ξ = -0.3) and squeezing (oblate, ξ = 0.3) it in one direction. They calculated the real and imaginary parts of the in-medium Cornell potential using the dielectric permittivity derived from the gluon self-energy in the static limit, then determined the thermal width and binding energy for s-wave charmonia and bottomonia states (n = 1 and 2). Dissociation temperatures were obtained from the criterion that the thermal width equals twice the real part of the binding energy.

## Key numbers

- For the leading order (non-interacting) case, the dissociation temperature of Υ (1s state of b-bbar) is 2.861 Tc (prolate, ξ = -0.3), 2.964 Tc (isotropic, ξ = 0.0), and 3.062 Tc (oblate, ξ = 0.3).
- For the leading order case, the dissociation temperature of J/ψ (1s state of c-cbar) is 1.487 Tc (prolate), 1.520 Tc (isotropic), and 1.551 Tc (oblate).
- For the leading order case, the dissociation temperature of Υ' (2s state of b-bbar) is 1.447 Tc (prolate), 1.478 Tc (isotropic), and 1.508 Tc (oblate).
- Using 3-loop HTL perturbative results, the dissociation temperature of Υ is 2.427 Tc (prolate), 2.540 Tc (isotropic), and 2.639 Tc (oblate).
- Using 3-loop HTL perturbative results, the dissociation temperature of J/ψ is 1.054 Tc (prolate), 1.119 Tc (isotropic), and 1.172 Tc (oblate).
- Using 3-loop HTL perturbative results, the dissociation temperature of Υ' is 1.008 Tc (prolate), 1.067 Tc (isotropic), and 1.118 Tc (oblate).
- Using (2+1)-flavor lattice results, the dissociation temperature of Υ is 2.427 Tc (prolate), 2.540 Tc (isotropic), and 2.639 Tc (oblate).
- Using (2+1)-flavor lattice results, the dissociation temperature of J/ψ is 1.054 Tc (prolate), 1.119 Tc (isotropic), and 1.172 Tc (oblate).
- Using (2+1)-flavor lattice results, the dissociation temperature of Υ' is 1.008 Tc (prolate), 1.067 Tc (isotropic), and 1.118 Tc (oblate).
- The critical temperature is fixed at Tc = 0.17 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of quarkonia dissociation in an isotropic hot QCD medium (Agotiya et al., Phys. Rev. D 94, 094006, 2016) by incorporating momentum anisotropy, motivated by experimental observations of collective flow at RHIC and LHC. The analysis employs the EQPM framework (Chandra and collaborators) to include hot QCD medium effects, using equations of state from 3-loop HTL perturbative calculations (Haque et al.) and (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations (Bazabov et al., Fodor et al., 2014). The results enable more realistic predictions for sequential quarkonia suppression patterns in heavy-ion collisions, accounting for the anisotropic nature of the QGP at all stages after collision.
