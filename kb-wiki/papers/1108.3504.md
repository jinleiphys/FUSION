# Spatial entanglement and massive neutrino oscillations produced by orbital electron capture decay

> The two-particle wave function of neutrino and recoil nucleus is found as a solution of an initial value problem in the far zone for a time longer than the electron capture decay lifetime of a hydrogenlike ion. The neutrino-recoil entanglement arising in such a process is a consequence of the momentum conservation and is closely related to the wave packet structure of the state. Because of neutrino mixing, the joint wave packet involves the coherent superposition of the neutrino mass eigenstate packets. This is the new physical realization of the Einstein-Podolsky-Rosen thought experiment, which has no analogue in quantum optics and quantum informatics. A class of possible experiments for the registration of a neutrino and a recoil nucleus is proposed. It is shown that due to spatial correlations neutrino oscillations can be observed in the coincidence experiment with the recoil.

## Key claim

The two-particle wave function of the neutrino and recoil nucleus, derived as a solution of an initial value problem in the far zone for times longer than the electron capture decay lifetime, shows that the three massive neutrino eigenstates have equal energies and different momenta, resolving the long-standing "same energy or same momentum" debate. This spatial entanglement, which has no analogue in quantum optics, allows neutrino oscillations to be observed in coincidence experiments with the recoil nucleus.

## Method

The author applies the Weisskopf-Wigner theory of spontaneous emission to treat orbital electron capture decay of a hydrogen-like ion confined in a trap, using time-dependent perturbation theory to solve the Schrodinger equation with a Hamiltonian that includes weak interaction, hyperfine interaction, and neutrino mixing. The two-particle wave function is found analytically in the coordinate representation by using the far zone approximation and expanding around the decay energy Q_EC, then evaluating integrals by the residue method to obtain a product of center-of-mass and relative motion wave functions.

## Key numbers

- Decay energy Q_EC is the fixed kinetic energy of the coherent superposition of neutrino mass eigenstates, with small parameters alpha = Q_EC/M and delta_i = m_i/Q_EC
- The decay rate Gamma (one half of the Gamow-Teller transition rate) is given by Gamma = [(G_F V_ud)^2 / (2 pi (2F+1))] [<f_1s> g_A M(aI,bI') Q_EC]^2
- For a nucleus with mass number A ~ 100 and initial atomic wave packet size d ~ 10^-6 cm, the center-of-mass spreading time t_CM = M d^2 ~ 10^-6 s
- The relative motion wave packet spreading time t_i = M D_i^2 ~ 10^19 yr for Gamma ~ 1 s^-1 and A ~ 100
- The coherence length L_coh = L_osc Q_EC / Gamma ~ 10^21 L_osc, where L_osc is the oscillation length
- The oscillation phase is phi_ij = (Delta m^2_ij / (2 Q_EC)) (L_nu + L_n), where L_nu and L_n are distances from source to neutrino and recoil detectors respectively

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Fedorov et al. (2005, 2007) for two-particle decay of composite objects to the specific case of orbital electron capture decay and neutrino production, addressing unresolved issues in the theory of neutrino oscillations such as the equal energy versus equal momentum debate raised by Akhmedov (2007). The paper proposes coincidence experiments first considered by Dolgov et al. (2005) for observing neutrino oscillations through spatial correlations, and enables a new class of experiments that could be of interest for the next generation of neutrino detectors, building on the GSI experiment by Litvinov et al. (2008, 2010) with hydrogen-like ions.
