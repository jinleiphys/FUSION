# Super-allowed alpha-decay above doubly-magic 100Sn and properties of 104Te = 100Sn otimes alpha

> Alpha-decay half-lives for 104,105,106Te and 108,109,110Xe close above the doubly-magic 100Sn are calculated from systematic double-folding potentials. The derived alpha preformation factors are compared to results for 212,213,214Po and 216,217,218Rn above the doubly-magic 208Pb. Alpha-decay energies of Q_alpha = 5.42 \pm 0.07 MeV and 4.65 \pm 0.15 MeV are predicted for 104Te and 108Xe; the corresponding half-lives are T_{1/2} approx 5 ns for 104Te and of the order of 60 μs for 108Xe. Additionally, the properties of rotational bands in 104Te are analyzed, and the first excited 2+ state in 104Te is predicted at E_x = 650 \pm 40 keV; it decays preferentially by gamma emission with a reduced transition strength of 10 Weisskopf units to the ground state of 104Te and with a minor branch by alpha emission to the ground state of 100Sn.

## Key claim

Alpha-decay half-lives for nuclei just above doubly-magic 100Sn are calculated using systematic double-folding potentials, and the derived preformation factors confirm "super-allowed" alpha-decay in this region, with the caveat that the predicted half-life for 104Te has an uncertainty of about a factor of three due to the exponential dependence on the decay energy and the assumed preformation factor.

## Method

The author calculated alpha-decay widths using a semi-classical approximation with double-folding nuclear potentials, where the potential strength parameter was adjusted to reproduce the alpha-particle binding energy in each alpha-emitter. The Wildermuth condition was used to determine the number of nodes of the bound-state wave function, and preformation factors were extracted by comparing calculated half-lives (assuming P=1) to experimental half-lives. For 104Te and 108Xe, the decay energies were predicted by extrapolating the volume integral of the folding potential from neighboring nuclei, and the preformation factors were assumed based on the systematic trend observed for the other nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Predicted alpha-decay energy for 104Te: Q_alpha = 5.42 ± 0.07 MeV; corresponding half-life T_{1/2} ≈ 5 ns with an uncertainty of about a factor of three.
- Predicted alpha-decay energy for 108Xe: Q_alpha = 4.65 ± 0.15 MeV; corresponding half-life of the order of 60 μs.
- First excited 2+ state in 104Te predicted at E_x = 650 ± 40 keV; it decays preferentially by gamma emission with a reduced transition strength of 10 Weisskopf units to the ground state of 104Te.
- Preformation factor for 106Te: P = 14.4^{+3.0}_{-4.8} % for the decay 106Te → 102Sn with Q_alpha = 4.290 MeV and experimental half-life (6.0^{+3.0}_{-1.0}) × 10^{-5} s.
- Preformation factor for 105Te: P = 4.95 ± 0.56 % for the decay 105Te → 101Sn with Q_alpha = 4.889 MeV and experimental half-life (6.2 ± 0.7) × 10^{-7} s.
- Preformation factor for 212Po: P = 2.96 ± 0.02 % for the decay 212Po → 208Pb with Q_alpha = 8.954 MeV and experimental half-life (2.99 ± 0.02) × 10^{-7} s.
- Preformation factor for 214Po: P = 5.06 ± 0.06 % for the decay 214Po → 210Pb with Q_alpha = 7.834 MeV and experimental half-life (1.64 ± 0.02) × 10^{-4} s.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of super-allowed alpha-decay near 100Sn, following the experimental detection of alpha-decay of 105Te by Seweryniak et al. (2006) and the decay chain 109Xe → 105Te → 101Sn by Liddick et al. (2006). The results are compared to the analogous decays above doubly-magic 208Pb (212,213,214Po and 216,217,218Rn), and the preformation factors for Te isotopes are found to be approximately three times larger than those for Po isotopes, confirming the super-allowed nature. The predictions for 104Te and 108Xe enable future experimental searches for these nuclei, and the finding that gamma-decay dominates over alpha-decay for excited states in 104Te is important for designing production reactions.
