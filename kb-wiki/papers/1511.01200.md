# Compositeness of baryonic resonances: Applications to the Delta(1232), N(1535), and N(1650) resonances

> We present a formulation of the compositeness for baryonic resonances in order to discuss the meson-baryon molecular structure inside the resonances. For this purpose, we derive a relation between the residue of the scattering amplitude at the resonance pole position and the two-body wave function of the resonance in a sophisticated way, and we define the compositeness as the norm of the two-body wave functions. As applications, we investigate the compositeness of the $Δ(1232)$, $N (1535)$, and $N (1650)$ resonances from precise $πN$ scattering amplitudes in a unitarized chiral framework with the interaction up to the next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory. The $πN$ compositeness for the $Δ(1232)$ resonance is evaluated in the $πN$ single-channel scattering, and we find that the $πN$ component inside $Δ(1232)$ in the present framework is nonnegligible, which supports the previous work. On the other hand, the compositeness for the $N (1535)$ and $N (1650)$ resonances is evaluated in a coupled-channels approach, resulting that the $πN$, $ηN$, $K Λ$ and $K Σ$ components are negligible for these resonances.

## Key claim

The paper provides a formal definition of compositeness for baryonic resonances as the norm of the two-body wave function extracted from the residue of the scattering amplitude at the resonance pole, and applies this to the Delta(1232), N(1535), and N(1650) resonances using a unitarized chiral framework. The authors find that while the pi N component inside the Delta(1232) is nonnegligible, the pi N, eta N, K Lambda, and K Sigma components are negligible for the N(1535) and N(1650) resonances, contradicting expectations from earlier chiral unitary studies that suggested these resonances might be meson-baryon molecular states.

## Method

The authors derive a relation between the residue of the two-body scattering amplitude at the resonance pole position and the two-body wave function, defining compositeness as the norm of the two-body wave functions. They then fit model parameters to partial wave analysis solutions for pi N scattering amplitudes using a unitarized chiral framework with interactions up to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, evaluating the Delta(1232) in a single-channel pi N scattering and the N(1535) and N(1650) in a pi N-eta N-K Lambda-K Sigma coupled-channels approach without introducing explicit bare states.

## Context

This work extends the compositeness formalism previously applied to the Lambda(1405) by Sekihara (2014) and to scalar mesons f0(980) and a0(980) by Sekihara (2014), and builds on the chiral unitary approach developed by Kaiser (1995), Nieves (2001), and others. The results for N(1535) and N(1650) contradict the suggestion from Garcia-Recio (2003) that these resonances, together with Lambda(1405) and Xi(1690), form degenerate octets as dynamically generated meson-baryon molecular states in a flavor SU(3) symmetric world. The findings enable further investigation into the internal structure of baryonic resonances and the role of missing channels beyond the pseudoscalar meson-baryon dynamics considered.
