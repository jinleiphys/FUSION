# Parity-violating longitudinal response

> The longitudinal quasielastic parity-violating electron scattering response is explored within the context of a model that builds antisymmetrized RPA-HF correlations on a relativistic Fermi gas basis. The large sensitivity to nuclear dynamics of this observable, found in previous studies where only pionic correlations were included, is shown to survive in the present model where the effects from pion, rho, sigma and omega exchange in a version of the Bonn potential are incorporated. Through an intricate diagrammatic cancellation/filtration mechanism the longitudinal parity-violating response turns out to be close to the one obtained in first-order perturbation theory with the pion alone. Finally, in accord with our previous work, the parity-violating response is seen to display appreciable sensitivity to the electric strangeness content of the nucleon, especially at high momentum transfer.

# Parity-violating longitudinal response

## Key claim
The large sensitivity of the parity-violating longitudinal response to nuclear dynamics, previously found in a model with only pionic correlations, survives when a more realistic interaction (the Bonn potential including π, ρ, σ, and ω exchange) is treated in a fully antisymmetrized RPA-HF framework; however, through a cancellation/filtration mechanism the response ends up close to that obtained in first-order perturbation theory with the pion alone.

## Method
The authors compute the quasielastic parity-violating longitudinal response \(R^L_{AV}\) within a relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model incorporating Hartree-Fock (HF) based fully antisymmetrized RPA correlations. The nucleon-nucleon interaction is taken from the Bonn potential, which includes exchange of π, ρ, σ, and ω mesons. The response is evaluated as a function of energy transfer ω at fixed momentum transfers q = 300, 500, 800, and 1000 MeV/c, using Fermi momenta k_F = 200 MeV/c (corresponding to \(^{12}\)C in the correlated model) and k_F = 225 MeV/c for comparison.

## Key numbers
- At q = 500 MeV/c, the parity-violating longitudinal response shows a pronounced oscillatory behavior in frequency, becoming strongly negative at low frequencies and strongly positive at large frequencies.
- The asymmetry difference \(\Delta\mathcal{A}(q,\theta)\) (defined as the difference between integrals of the asymmetry below and above the quasielastic peak) is an order of magnitude larger for the pionic-correlated RFG than for the pure RFG, for momentum transfers up to about 500 MeV/c in forward-angle scattering.
- The scale of the asymmetry is set by \(\mathcal{A}_0 = G|Q^2|/(2\pi\alpha\sqrt{2}) \approx 3.1 \times 10^{-4}\tau\), where \(\tau = |Q^2|/(4m_N^2)\).
- The free RFG \(R^L_{AV}\) is very small and negative at q = 300 MeV/c, then remains very small but positive up to about 2 GeV/c.
- The HF field alone leaves \(R^L_{AV}\) almost identical to the free RFG case, except for a shift in the response region.
- At q = 500 MeV/c, the ring diagrams involving σ and ω mesons produce a huge impact on \(R^L_{AV}\), which is then almost completely canceled by the pion exchange contribution interfering with σ and ω.
- The resulting \(R^L_{AV}\) in the full HF-RPA model with the Bonn potential is close to the result obtained in first-order perturbation theory with the pion alone.
- For the electric strangeness form factor, three parameter choices are considered: \(\rho_s = 0\) (no strangeness); \(\rho_s = -3, \lambda_E^{(s)} = 5.6\); and \(\rho_s = -3, \lambda_E^{(s)} = 0\).
- The parity-violating longitudinal response with strangeness included is significantly different from the no-strangeness case at high momentum transfer, where the strangeness form factor (suppressed at low \(|Q^2|\) by a factor of \(\tau\)) becomes active.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by the same group (Donnelly et al. 1992; Alberico et al. 1993; Barbaro et al. 1994) that first explored the unusual sensitivity of the parity-violating longitudinal response using a pionic-correlated Fermi gas model. The present calculation incorporates a more realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction (the Bonn potential of Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster 1987) and a more sophisticated many-body framework (antisymmetrized RPA-HF rather than first-order perturbation theory). The results confirm that \(R^L_{AV}\) remains a powerful probe of isospin-correlation effects in nuclei and, at high momentum transfer, may provide an alternative way to study the electric strangeness content of the nucleon. The work enables future experimental programs using parity-violating electron scattering to extract nuclear structure information and nucleon strangeness form factors.
