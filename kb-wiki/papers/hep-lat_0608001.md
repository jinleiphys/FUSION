# Chiral corrections to the Roper mass

> We analyze the quark mass dependence of the Roper mass to one-loop order in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory. The loop integrals are evaluated using infrared regularization which preserves chiral symmetry and establishes a chiral counting scheme. The derived chiral expansion of the Roper mass may prove useful for chiral extrapolations of lattice data. For couplings of natural size the quark mass dependence of the Roper mass is similar to the one of the nucleon.

## Key claim
The quark mass dependence of the Roper mass, calculated to one-loop order in relativistic baryon chiral perturbation theory using infrared regularization, is similar to that of the nucleon for couplings of natural size. The one-loop formula cannot be trusted for pion masses much beyond 350 MeV.

## Method
The authors constructed an SU(2) chiral effective Lagrangian for nucleon and Roper fields, including pion-Roper and pion-nucleon-Roper couplings up to fourth chiral order. They evaluated one-loop self-energy diagrams with intermediate pion-nucleon and pion-Roper pairs using an extension of infrared regularization that handles two heavy mass scales (M_N and M_R) and preserves chiral power counting, isolating the infrared-singular parts of the loop integrals.

## Key numbers
- The Roper mass in the chiral limit is taken as M_R = 1.4 GeV; the nucleon mass in the chiral limit is M_N = 0.885 GeV (from Bernard et al. 2003).
- The pion-nucleon-Roper coupling g_NR is determined from Roper decay branching ratios to be 0.3–0.4; the pion-Roper coupling g_R is set to 1.0.
- The low-energy constant c_1* is bounded by |c_1*| ≲ 1 GeV^{-1}, with a natural value around -0.5 GeV^{-1}; c_2* and c_3* are bounded conservatively by ±1 GeV^{-1}.
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is f_π = 87 MeV (from Colangelo et al. 2003).
- The leading non-analytic contribution from the pion-Roper loop scales as m_π^3, with coefficient -3 g_R^2/(32 π f_π^2).

## Context
This work extends the infrared regularization method of Becher and Leutwyler (1999) to systems with two heavy mass scales, enabling chiral extrapolation functions for lattice QCD studies of the Roper resonance. It contrasts with simple linear extrapolations in m_π^2 used by Burch et al. (2006) and addresses the need for improved chiral functions highlighted by lattice results from Mathur et al. (2003) and Guadagnoli et al. (2004), which disagree on whether the Roper and S_11(1535) states cross near the chiral limit. The formalism can be applied to other resonances such as the S_11(1535), though an SU(3) calculation would be needed there due to the important ηN decay channel.
