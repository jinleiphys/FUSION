# The impact of the medium and the jet-medium coupling on jet measurements at RHIC and LHC

> We compare a perturbative QCD-based jet-energy loss model to the measured data of the pion nuclear modification factor and the high-pT elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC energies. This jet-energy loss model (BBMG) is currently coupled to state-of-the-art hydrodynamic descriptions. We report on a model extension to medium backgrounds generated by the parton cascade BAMPS. In addition, we study the impact of realistic medium transverse flow fields and a jet-medium coupling which includes the effects of the jet energy, the temperature of the bulk medium, and non-equilibrium effects close to the phase transition. By contrasting the two different background models, we point out that the description of the high-pT elliptic flow for a non-fluctuating medium requires to include such a jet-medium coupling and the transverse flow fields. While the results for both medium backgrounds show a remarkable similarity, there is an impact of the background medium and the background flow on the high-pT elliptic flow.

# The impact of the medium and the jet-medium coupling on jet measurements at RHIC and LHC

## Key claim

The simultaneous description of the pion nuclear modification factor and high-pT elliptic flow for non-fluctuating initial conditions requires both realistic transverse flow fields and a jet-medium coupling that depends on jet energy, medium temperature, and non-equilibrium effects near the phase transition; the background medium itself also significantly influences the high-pT elliptic flow, even though results from hydrodynamic and parton-cascade backgrounds show remarkable similarity.

## Method

The authors compare the perturbative QCD-based BBMG jet-energy loss model, using the energy-loss formula dE/dτ = -κ E^0 τ^1 e^{3/4} ζ_q Γ_f, to pion R_AA and high-pT v_2 data at RHIC and LHC. They couple this model to two different medium backgrounds: the viscous hydrodynamic code VISH2+1 and the parton cascade BAMPS, and contrast a constant jet-medium coupling κ=κ(√s_NN) with the CUJET3.0-inspired coupling κ=κ(E^2,T) that includes running coupling, Polyakov-loop suppression, and near-critical magnetic monopole enhancement.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition temperature used is T_c ~ 160 MeV.
- The initial time for the BAMPS medium is t_0 = 0.3 fm at RHIC and t_0 = 0.2 fm at LHC energies.
- The high-pT elliptic flow below pT ~ 20 GeV is about a factor of two below measured data for models without the full jet-medium coupling and flow fields.
- The running coupling is parameterized as α_S(E^2) = 1/[c + 9/4π log(E^2/T_c^2)] with c = 1.05.
- The Polyakov-loop fit function is L(T) = [1/2 + 1/2 Tanh[7.69(T - 0.0726)]]^10, with N_f = 3.
- The magnetic screening function is f_M(T) = 0.3 μ(T)/(T √(1+N_f/6)).

## Context

This work extends the BBMG model (Betz 2014, Betz 2012) and the CUJET3.0 framework (Xu 2014) by contrasting hydrodynamic (VISH2+1, Heinz and Shen) and parton-cascade (BAMPS, Uphoff 2014) medium backgrounds. It addresses the "high-pT v_2 problem" identified in earlier studies (Betz 2014, Xu, Molnar), where theoretical models underpredicted elliptic flow by a factor of two. The results enable future studies of event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions (Betz 2016) and demonstrate that the choice of background medium matters for jet observables beyond the nuclear modification factor.
