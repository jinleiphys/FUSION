# Quasinormal modes of nonthermal fixed points

> Quasinormal modes play a prominent role in relaxation of diverse physical systems to equilibria, ranging from astrophysical black holes to tiny droplets of quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC accelerators. We propose that a novel kind of quasinormal modes govern the direct approach to self-similar time evolution of nonthermal fixed points, whose relevance ranges from high energy physics to cold atom gases. We utilize black hole perturbation theory techniques to compute the spectrum of these far from equilibrium quasinormal modes for a kinetic theory with a Focker-Planck collision kernel in isotropic and homogeneous states. Our conclusion is that quasinormal modes of nonthermal fixed points give rise to a tower of progressively more decaying power-law contributions. A byproduct of our analysis is a precise determination and improved understanding of the distribution function characterizing nonthermal fixed points.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a novel kind of quasinormal modes (QNMs) govern the direct approach to self-similar time evolution of nonthermal fixed points (NTFPs), with the caveat that this is demonstrated explicitly only for a kinetic theory with a Fokker-Planck collision kernel in isotropic and homogeneous states, and the general expectation is that the approach to a NTFP occurs as a power law with possible slow oscillations logarithmic in time.

## Method

The authors linearize the Boltzmann equation around a self-similar NTFP scaling solution, using a separation of variables ansatz to derive an eigenvalue problem for perturbations of the distribution function. They solve this eigenvalue problem numerically using a Chebyshev-Gauss-Lobatto pseudospectral method on a compactified momentum grid, first determining the NTFP scaling function self-consistently and then computing the QNM spectrum for the Fokker-Planck collision kernel relevant for small-angle elastic scattering of gluons at very weak coupling.

## Key numbers

- The direct energy cascade NTFP for the Fokker-Planck kernel has exponents (alpha, beta) = (-4/7, -1/7) with sigma = 4 from energy conservation.
- The scaling function g_s behaves as proportional to 1/p for p << 1 and proportional to p * exp(-p^2/2) for p >> 1 on the full momentum grid without cut-offs.
- The predicted t_* mode has eigenvalue Omega = -7i (from Omega = i/beta with beta = -1/7).
- Numerically computed QNM frequencies include Omega approximately equal to -4.29i, -6.49i, and -8.59i, which are robust to changes in the UV cut-off.
- The zero-mode (Omega = 0) corresponds to a shift in the reference time t_ref and is removed by imposing the boundary condition that perturbations carry no energy density (delta epsilon = 0).

## Context

This work extends the concept of quasinormal modes, previously used to describe relaxation to thermal equilibrium in black hole physics and holography (Horowitz 1999, Kovtun 2005, Berti 2009), to the far-from-equilibrium setting of nonthermal fixed points studied theoretically (Berges 2008, Kurkela 2015) and observed experimentally in cold atom gases (Prufer 2018, Erne 2018, Glidden 2020). The paper provides a bridge between NTFP dynamics and black hole perturbation theory techniques, enabling future studies of the attractive nature of NTFPs using methods from holography and the possibility of direct experimental observation of these QNMs in cold atomic gas systems.
