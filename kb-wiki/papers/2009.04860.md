# Exclusive $J/ψ$ photoproduction off deuteron in d+Au ultra-peripheral collisions at STAR

> Gluon density and its distributions inside nuclei and the parton modification of bounded nucleons inside a nucleus, are some of the main standing problems in nuclear and particle physics. In recent years, ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) of heavy ions have provided a new way of probing the gluon density, which is based on coherent diffractive vector-meson productions, e.g., $J/ψ$ meson. For heavy ions, e.g., Pb, the gluon density is found to be significantly suppressed through the UPC $J/ψ$ measurement, suggesting a strong gluon shadowing effect in heavy nuclei. In this analysis, we aim to look at a unique set of data taken by the STAR experiment, where $J/ψ$ mesons are photoproduced off the deuteron target with no other particle produced, except for the deuteron or its breakup products. The Zero Degree Calorimeter response with respect to the deuteron dissociation by detecting a beam-rapidity neutron is also investigated and provides additional information about the underlying physics process. The cross section of $J/ψ$ photoproduction in the photon-deuteron system is measured at the photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy $W\sim25~\rm{GeV}$, as well as the momentum transfer $t$ dependence cross section, $dσ/dt$. Data suggests a wider gluon density distribution than the Hulthen charge density distribution in deuteron.

# Exclusive \(J/\psi\) photoproduction off deuteron in d+Au ultra-peripheral collisions at STAR

## Key claim
The gluon density distribution in the deuteron, extracted from coherent \(J/\psi\) photoproduction, is found to be wider than the Hulthen charge density distribution, based on a template fit that assumes the coherent diffractive component is an exponential function with the slope as a free parameter and fixes the shapes of incoherent and nucleon dissociation contributions using H1 data.

## Method
The STAR experiment measured exclusive \(J/\psi\) photoproduction in deuteron-gold ultra-peripheral collisions, selecting events with a \(J/\psi\) candidate reconstructed via the di-electron channel at mid-rapidity and no other activity in the main detector. The differential cross section \(d\sigma/dt\) was obtained as a function of the \(J/\psi\) transverse momentum squared, corrected by the transversely polarized photon flux calculated from the STARlight event generator. A template fit was performed using the H1 data from electron-proton scattering to separate the coherent diffractive, incoherent diffractive, and nucleon dissociation components, with the coherent slope as a free fit parameter.

## Key numbers
- The extracted slope of the coherent diffractive component is \(-8.5 \pm 1.2\ \text{(stat.)} \pm 1.5\ \text{(sys.)}\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\).
- The measurement was performed at photon-nucleon center-of-mass energy \(W \sim 25\ \text{GeV}\).
- The online trigger required Barrel Electromagnetic Calorimeter response above roughly 0.5 GeV energy threshold for a back-to-back pair of towers.

## Context
This work extends the study of gluon density modifications from heavy nuclei (e.g., Pb, where strong gluon shadowing was observed at LHC by Khachatryan (2016)) to the simplest two-body nuclear system, the deuteron. It provides the first measurement of gluon imaging for a deuteron target, complementing the EMC effect studies by Aubert (1983) and short-range correlation investigations by Higinbotham (2010). The result enables future gluon imaging of bounded nucleons with and without short-range correlations at the Electron-Ion Collider, as proposed by Tu (2020).
