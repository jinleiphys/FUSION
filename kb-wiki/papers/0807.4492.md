# Shape/Phase Transitions and Critical Point Symmetries in Atomic Nuclei

> Shape/phase transitions in atomic nuclei have first been discovered in the framework of the Interacting Boson Approximation (IBA) model. Critical point symmetries appropriate for nuclei at the transition points have been introduced as special solutions of the Bohr Hamiltonian, stirring the introduction of additional new solutions describing wide ranges of nuclei. A short review of these recent developments will be attempted.

## Key claim

Shape/phase transitions in atomic nuclei are quantum phase transitions (not thermodynamic ones), and critical point symmetries such as E(5) and X(5) provide parameter-free descriptions of nuclei at the transition points, but only for specific legs of the Interacting Boson Model symmetry triangle: E(5) corresponds to the second-order transition between U(5) and O(6), while X(5) corresponds to the first-order transition between U(5) and SU(3).

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of shape/phase transitions in atomic nuclei using the Interacting Boson Model (IBM) and the Bohr Hamiltonian. It examines the classical limit of the IBM Hamiltonian via coherent state methods to derive energy functionals E(β,γ) and locate phase transition points. For critical point symmetries, the Bohr Hamiltonian is solved with specific potentials (infinite square well, harmonic oscillator, Davidson potential) to obtain energy spectra and B(E2) transition rates, with the E(5) and X(5) solutions corresponding to Bessel equation eigenfunctions.

## Key numbers

- The R₄/₂ = E(4₁⁺)/E(2₁⁺) ratio for E(5) is 2.199
- The R₄/₂ ratio for X(5) is 2.904
- The R₄/₂ ratio for X(3) is 2.44
- For the U(5)-SU(3) leg with χ=-1.32 and N_B=10, ζ* = 0.507 and ζ_crit = 0.511
- For the U(5)-O(6) leg with χ=0 and N_B=10, ζ_crit = 0.556
- The E(5)-β⁴, E(5)-β⁶, and E(5)-β⁸ solutions give R₄/₂ = 2.093, 2.135, and 2.157 respectively
- The X(5)-β⁴, X(5)-β⁶, and X(5)-β⁸ solutions give R₄/₂ = 2.769, 2.824, and 2.852 respectively
- The X(5)-β² solution gives R₄/₂ = 2.646

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on shape phase transitions in the Interacting Boson Model first studied 25 years prior by Deans and collaborators, and the critical point symmetries E(5) and X(5) introduced by Iachello (2000-2001). It enables systematic classification of nuclei near phase transition points and provides a framework for interpreting experimental data on candidate nuclei such as ¹³⁴Ba, ¹⁰²Pd (for E(5)) and ¹⁵²Sm, ¹⁵⁰Nd (for X(5)). The review also discusses extensions to odd nuclei (E(5/4), E(5/12)), triaxial nuclei (Z(5)), and the prolate-to-oblate transition, as well as connections to relativistic mean field calculations by Fossion and collaborators.
