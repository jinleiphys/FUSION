# Exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair: A new class of observables to probe GPDs

> We discuss the exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair with large $ p_{T} $ as a channel to probe generalised parton distributions (GPDs). Like other $ 2\to3 $ exclusive processes, this channel allows us to better study the $ x $-dependence of GPDs, in contrast to $ 2\to2 $ processes such as Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) which give only "moment-type" information. Moreover, it also gives the possibility to access, at leading twist, the chiral-odd GPDs, which are still completely unknown experimentally. In the first part of these proceedings, we present the computation of the amplitude at leading order and leading twist, for charged pions, and rho mesons of any charge and polarisation. These processes are sensitive to quark GPDs only. We also discuss the possibility of measuring such processes at various experiments, namely JLab, COMPASS, future EIC and LHC in ultra-peripheral collisions. In particular, in collider experiments, the dependence of GPDs at small skewness $ (<10^{-3}) $ can be extracted. For the second part, we report on our recent work on the exclusive photoproduction of $ π^{0}γ$ pair, which, in addition to a quark GPD channel, also has a contribution from gluon GPDs. In the latter case, we demonstrate that a collinear factorisation of the process fails, due to the presence of a Glauber pinch. Our findings also imply that other similar processes, like $ π^{0} N \to γγN $ also suffer from the same issue. On the other hand, we stress that the processes discussed earlier, which are sensitive to the quark GPD channels only, are safe from these factorisation breaking effects.

# Exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair: A new class of observables to probe GPDs

## Key claim

Exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair with large transverse momentum can probe generalized parton distributions (GPDs) with enhanced x-dependence compared to 2-to-2 processes like DVCS, and can access chiral-odd GPDs at leading twist for the first time; however, for channels involving gluon GPD contributions (such as pi0-gamma pair production), collinear factorization fails due to a Glauber pinch, while quark-only channels remain safe from this breakdown.

## Method

The authors computed the amplitude at leading order and leading twist for exclusive photoproduction of a photon-meson pair (charged pions and rho mesons of any charge and polarization) using collinear factorization, convolving coefficient functions with GPDs parameterized via double distributions and meson distribution amplitudes (asymptotic and holographic forms). For the pi0-gamma channel, they performed a direct leading-order calculation and a two-loop analysis of momentum regions to identify the Glauber pinch responsible for factorization breaking. Kinematic cuts of -u', -t' > 1 GeV^2 and -t < 0.5 GeV^2 were imposed to ensure large transverse momentum and avoid resonances.

## Key numbers

- Expected number of events at JLab (integrated luminosity 864 fb^-1): 1.3-2.4 x 10^5 for rho0_L, 2.1-4.2 x 10^4 for rho0_T, 0.3-1.8 x 10^5 for pi+
- Expected number of events at future EIC (integrated luminosity 10 fb^-1): 1.3-2.4 x 10^4 for rho0_L, 1.2-2.4 x 10^3 for rho0_T, 0.2-1.3 x 10^4 for pi+
- Expected number of events at LHC in ultraperipheral collisions (integrated luminosity 1200 nb^-1): 0.9-1.6 x 10^4 for rho0_L, 0.8-1.7 x 10^3 for rho0_T, 1.6-9.3 x 10^3 for pi+
- For small skewness studies (S_gammaN > 300 GeV^2, corresponding to xi approximately 5 x 10^-5 to 5 x 10^-3): at EIC, 0.6-1.2 x 10^3 events for rho0_L and 1.4-5.0 x 10^2 for pi+; at LHC UPCs, 4.1-8.1 x 10^2 for rho0_L and 1.0-3.4 x 10^2 for pi+
- Linear polarization asymmetry (LPA) reaches up to 60% for both pi+ and rho+_L cases
- The divergent integral in the gluon GPD channel for pi0-gamma production involves propagator poles with opposite i-epsilon prescriptions at x approaching xi and z-bar approaching 0

## Context

This work extends the program of using 2-to-3 exclusive processes to probe GPDs, following earlier proposals for two-meson photoproduction by El Beiyad et al. (2010) and photon-rho_T pair photoproduction by Boussarie et al. (2016). It builds on the authors' previous calculations for photon-meson pair production (Duplancic et al., 2018, 2022, 2023) and the general factorization proofs for 2-to-3 exclusive processes by Qiu et al. (2022). The finding of factorization breaking for the gluon GPD channel in pi0-gamma production, explained in Nabeebaccus et al. (2023), is analogous to Glauber pinch issues in double diffractive processes like pp to pp-gamma-pi0, and also affects the crossed process pi0 N to gamma-gamma N discussed by Qiu et al. (2022). The results enable experimental extraction of chiral-odd GPDs at leading twist for the first time, and provide a pathway to study the x-dependence of GPDs at small skewness (below 10^-3) at collider experiments.
