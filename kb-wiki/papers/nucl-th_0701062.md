# Looking for bimodal distributions in multi-fragmentation reactions

> The presence of a phase transition in a finite system can be deduced, together with its order, from the shape of the distribution of the order parameter. This issue has been extensively studied in multifragmentation experiments, with results that do not appear fully consistent. In this paper we discuss the effect of the statistical ensemble or sorting conditions on the shape of fragment distributions, and propose a new method, which can be easily implemented experimentally, to discriminate between different fragmentation scenarii. This method, based on a reweighting of the measured distribution to account for the experimental constraints linked to the energy deposit, is tested on different simple models, and appears to provide a powerful discrimination.

## Key claim

The presence of a first-order phase transition in nuclear multifragmentation can be inferred from the shape of the order parameter distribution only if the data are sorted in a canonical ensemble; experimental sorting conditions (centrality bins, energy constraints) can mask or destroy bimodality, and a reweighting method that accounts for the measured energy distribution can recover the underlying entropy concavity and discriminate between first-order and second-order transitions.

## Method

The author analyzes the effect of statistical ensemble (canonical vs. microcanonical vs. gaussian) on the distribution of the largest fragment size in multifragmentation, using the lattice gas model (first-order transition) and the bond percolation model (second-order transition) for N=216 particles. A reweighting procedure is proposed: each event is weighted by the inverse of the measured energy distribution, so that the resulting weighted distribution reflects the density of states \(W(E,Z)/W(E)\) rather than the experimental energy bias. The concavity of the reweighted distribution is then examined via its first and second derivatives to identify the convex intruder characteristic of a first-order transition.

## Key numbers

- Lattice gas model: canonical distributions at temperatures \(T = 3.72, 3.74, 3.76, 3.78, 3.80\) MeV close to the transition temperature (closest neighbors coupling \(\epsilon = 5.5\) MeV); microcanonical distributions at energies \(E/N = 0.75, 1.25, 1.75, 2.25, 2.75\) MeV spanning the coexistence region.
- Bond percolation model: distributions for bond-breaking probability \(p\) from \(p = 0.16\) to \(p = 0.56\) including the critical value \(p_c\).
- Model parameters for the bimodal case: liquid phase \(E_l = 0.2 E_{tot}\), \(Z_l = 0.7 Z_{tot}\), \(\sigma_{El} = 0.12 E_{tot}\), \(\sigma_{Zl} = 0.15 Z_{tot}\); gas phase \(E_g = 0.5 E_{tot}\), \(Z_g = 0.18 Z_{tot}\), \(\sigma_{Eg} = 0.05 E_{tot}\), \(\sigma_{Zg} = 0.1 Z_{tot}\); correlation coefficient \(\rho_l = \rho_g\) varied from \(-0.15\) to \(-0.85\).
- Model parameters for the monomodal case: \(E_{\beta} = 0.35 E_{tot}\), \(Z_{\beta} = 0.45 Z_{tot}\), \(\sigma_E = 0.1 E_{tot}\), \(\sigma_Z = 0.15 Z_{tot}\), \(\rho = -0.6\); experimental energy variance \(\sigma_{exp} = \sigma_E / 2\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of bimodal distributions in multifragmentation by the INDRA collaboration (Lopez, Pichon, Borderie, Bonnet, Lautesse, Frankland, 2000s), who found that heaviest fragment distributions are never bimodal in central collisions but show bimodality in peripheral collisions when sorted by the asymmetry \(Z_{asy}\). The paper addresses the inconsistency between these results by showing that the sorting conditions (centrality bins) effectively impose a gaussian ensemble rather than a canonical one, which can suppress bimodality even when a first-order transition exists. The proposed reweighting method enables future experimental analyses to recover the entropy concavity and distinguish between a first-order liquid-gas transition and a critical (second-order) phenomenon, as modeled by the lattice gas model (Chomaz, 2000s) and percolation (Stauffer, 1980s), respectively.
