# Large Transverse Momentum in Semi-Inclusive Deeply Inelastic Scattering Beyond Lowest Order

> Motivated by recently observed tension between $O\left(α_s^2\right)$ calculations of very large transverse momentum dependence in both semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan scattering, we repeat the details of the calculation through $O\left(α_s^2\right)$ transversely differential cross section. The results confirm earlier calculations, and provide further support to the observation that tension exists with current parton distribution and fragmentation functions.

## Key claim

The paper confirms earlier $O(\alpha_s^2)$ calculations of the transversely differential cross section in semi-inclusive deeply inelastic scattering (SIDIS), and provides further support that tension exists between these calculations and current parton distribution and fragmentation functions when the transverse momentum is of order $Q$ (the photon virtuality).

## Method

The authors repeated the $O(\alpha_s^2)$ calculation of the large transverse momentum SIDIS cross section using a slightly different formal framework from earlier work, employing qgraf for Feynman graph generation, FORM for spinor and color traces, and FeynArts/FeynCalc for renormalization counter terms. They classified all partonic Feynman graphs needed at $O(\alpha_s^2)$ into $2\to2$ and $2\to3$ scattering processes, performed phase space integrals in dimensional regularization with space-time dimension $n=4-2\epsilon$, and applied collinear factorization to remove singularities through subtraction terms.

## Key numbers

* The $O(\alpha_s^2)$ correction gives an order of magnitude increase over the leading order, yet this is still not sufficient to achieve reasonable agreement with data for $Q$ in the region of one to several GeV, transverse momentum of order $Q$, and for moderate Bjorken-$x$.
* The calculation was performed for $Q$ values in the range of one to several GeV, with transverse momentum $\Tsc{q}{} \sim Q$, using standard collinear factorization expected to be valid in this region.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Gonzalez-Hernandez (2018) which first highlighted the challenge of finding agreement between $O(\alpha_s^2)$ calculations and existing SIDIS data at very large transverse momentum. The paper also connects to similar observations for Drell-Yan scattering pointed out by Bacchetta (2019), indicating a general tension between transverse momentum dependent cross sections at $\Tsc{q}{} \sim Q$ and collinear factorization. The results enable future work on re-tuning collinear parton distribution and fragmentation functions to transversely differential SIDIS cross sections, and provide a numerical implementation relevant to current experimental programs at COMPASS and Jefferson Lab 12 GeV.
