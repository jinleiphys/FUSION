# Microscopic investigation of magnetic and antimagnetic rotational motion in atomic nuclei

> In the present work, we have generalized the projected shell model (PSM) approach to include the quasiparticle excitations from two major oscillator shells, and have also extended the basis space to five-quasiparticle configurations for odd-mass nuclei. The magnetic and antimagnetic rotational structures observed in odd-neutron Pd- and Cd-isotopes have been investigated as a first major application of the new development. It is shown that PSM approach provides a reasonable description of the observed properties of magnetic and antimagnetic rotational bands.

## Key claim

The authors claim that their newly extended projected shell model (PSM), which now includes quasiparticle excitations from two major oscillator shells and up to five-quasiparticle configurations for odd-mass nuclei, provides a "reasonable description" of the observed properties of magnetic and antimagnetic rotational bands in odd-neutron Pd and Cd isotopes, though the description is not exact and relies on input deformation parameters from other sources.

## Method

The authors generalized the projected shell model (PSM) by expanding the basis space to include quasiparticle excitations from two major oscillator shells (e.g., N=4 and N=5 for neutrons in the A~110 region) and by extending the basis to five-quasiparticle configurations for odd-mass nuclei. They performed angular-momentum projection and configuration mixing using a Hamiltonian with quadrupole-quadrupole and pairing interactions, with deformation parameters taken from prior literature. They also employed a semiclassical particle-rotor model (SCM) as an alternative approach, where the total energy is expressed as a sum of core rotational energy and effective interactions between shears blades, with the total angular momentum obtained by minimizing energy with respect to the shears angle.

## Key numbers

*   For the first AMR band identified in $^{109}$Cd, the ratio of the moment of inertia to B(E2) is about 165 $\hbar^2$ MeV$^{-1}$ (eb)$^{-2}$.
*   The pairing interaction parameters used in the PSM are $G_1 = 22.68$ and $G_2 = 16.22$ (units implicit from the parametrization $G_M = [G_1 \mp G_2 (N-Z)/A] (1/A)$).
*   In the SCM for the A~100 mass region, the effective interaction strengths are typically $V_{\pi\nu} \approx 1.2$ MeV and $V_{\pi\pi} \approx 0.2$ MeV.
*   Axial quadrupole deformations ($\epsilon$) used in the PSM range from 0.128 (for $^{101}$Pd) to 0.230 (for $^{111}$Pd), and from -0.110 (for $^{107}$Cd) to 0.150 (for $^{103}$Cd), as listed in Table 1.

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model approach, which previously restricted valence particles to one major oscillator shell (KY95, SUN19941, PALIT2001141, SINGH201641), to enable microscopic investigation of magnetic and antimagnetic rotational bands in the A~110 region. The study builds on prior experimental identification of AMR bands in $^{109}$Cd (chiara20, 109cd), $^{101}$Pd (101pdsuga, 101pdsuga2, 101pd), $^{103}$Pd (103pdamr), $^{105}$Cd (105cdamr), and $^{107}$Cd (107cdamr), and on theoretical approaches including the cranked shell model (CSM) (csm, FRau97), semiclassical model (SCM) (Macchiavelli, macch1, 144dy), and relativistic density functional theory (RDFT) (dft1, dft2). The new PSM development enables future calculations of electromagnetic transition probabilities with well-defined angular-momentum wavefunctions, in contrast to the cranking approximation used in other models.
