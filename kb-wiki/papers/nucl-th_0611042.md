# QCD susceptibilities and nuclear matter saturation in a chiral theory: inclusion of pion loops

> We derive the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter in a relativistic theory with $σ$ and $ω$ exchange. We take a chiral version of this model which insures all the chiral constraints. Going beyond the mean field approach we introduce the effects of the pion loops. For the parameters of the model, in order to fix those linked to pion exchange, we exploit the most recent information on the short-range part of the spin-isospin interaction. For those linked to the scalar meson exchange we make use of an analysis of lattice results on the nucleon mass evolution with the quark mass. With these inputs we are able reach a correct description of the saturation properties. From the equation of state of symmetric nuclear matter we alsoderive the density dependence of the quark condensate and of the QCD susceptibilities.

## Key claim

The authors show that including pion loops (Fock term and correlation energy) on top of a chiral relativistic mean-field model with scalar and vector meson exchange, and using lattice-QCD constraints on the nucleon mass evolution with quark mass, can reproduce nuclear matter saturation properties while also yielding the density dependence of the quark condensate and QCD susceptibilities. The hedge is that the pion loop contribution is treated non-relativistically and assumed to be of vector nature when added to the energy density, an approximation that has small numerical impact except for the free-nucleon part.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral version of the sigma-omega model with a chiral-invariant scalar field s and a nucleon scalar susceptibility term. They compute the mean-field energy density for symmetric nuclear matter, then add the pion loop energy (Fock term plus RPA ring correlation energy) from the pion-nucleon p-wave interaction, including short-range Landau-Migdal parameters (g'_NN = 0.7, g'_NΔ = 0.3, g'_ΔΔ = 0.5) and rho meson exchange. The pion loop energy is evaluated using a charging formula and Wick rotation, with the Lindhard functions for particle-hole and Delta-hole excitations calculated non-relativistically. The quark condensate and scalar susceptibility are then derived via the Feynman-Hellman theorem from the grand potential.

## Key numbers

- Pion cloud contribution to the free nucleon sigma term: σ_N^(π) = 21.5 MeV
- Corresponding pion cloud self-energy: Σ_π = -420 MeV
- Dipole form factor cutoff for πNN vertex: Λ = 0.98 GeV
- Landau-Migdal parameters used: g'_NN = 0.7, g'_NΔ = 0.3, g'_ΔΔ = 0.5
- Ratio of πNΔ to πNN coupling constants: R_NΔ = √(72/25)
- C_ρ = 2 (strong rho coupling)
- Leading-order in-medium quark condensate result: ⟨q̄q⟩ = ⟨q̄q⟩_vac (1 - σ_N ρ / (f_π² m_π²)), with σ_N = σ_N^(π) + σ_N^(σ) and σ_N^(σ) = f_π g_S m_π² / m_σ²

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Hartree-approximation study (Chanfray and Ericson, 2005) by incorporating pion loops, which were omitted in that earlier treatment. It builds on the chiral sigma-omega model framework proposed by Chanfray, Ericson, and Guichon (2002) and addresses the known problem that attractive tadpole diagrams in chiral theories destroy saturation (Kerman and Miller, 1974; Brown and Thouless, 2001). The approach has similarities to the relativistic density functional of Finelli, Kaiser, Vretenar, and Weise (2006) but differs in the treatment of the scalar field and pion loops. The results enable a unified description of nuclear saturation, the density evolution of the quark condensate, and the QCD scalar susceptibility, with the pionic contribution to the scalar susceptibility linked to the same medium effects that reshape the low-invariant-mass region in two-pion production experiments (Bonutti et al., 1996; Starostin et al., 2000; Mühlbauer et al., 2002).
