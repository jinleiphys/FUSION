# Impact parameter dependence of the effect of background field on coupling constant in heavy ion collisions

> We investigated the impact parameter dependence of the background field's effect on the coupling constant of the $γγ\rightarrow l^{+} l^{-} γ$ process in heavy-ion collisions. The peripheral electric fields of heavy ions collide, and after photon annihilation into lepton pairs, the subsequent emission of radiation photons will be affected by the background field. Numerical estimates indicate that the dependence of this effect on the impact parameter is significant during this process.

## Key claim

The background field's effect on the electromagnetic coupling constant in the γγ → l⁺l⁻γ process depends significantly on the impact parameter, meaning that inconsistent selection of ultra-peripheral collision (UPC) cases can lead to substantial errors in both theoretical calculations and experimental analyses.

## Method

The author derived impact parameter-dependent cross sections for the γγ → l⁺l⁻γ process in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating a background photon distribution function f(k) that modifies the effective coupling constant α_eff(k) = α_e f(k). The nuclear charge form factor was parameterized using the STARlight MC generator form, and the impact parameter dependence was introduced following the formalism developed in the 1990s (impact1, impact2). Numerical estimates were performed for three different UPC cases (UPC, centrality 60%-99.9%, and tagged UPC) within the kinematic ranges of RHIC and LHC.

## Key numbers

- For Au-Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the cross section as a function of final photon energy |k| was evaluated with dilepton rapidity in [-1, 0] and p_{1⊥}, p_{2⊥} ∈ [0.2, 0.8] GeV, showing the UPC case yields the largest cross section and the tagged UPC case the smallest.
- For Ru-Ru collisions at √s = 200 GeV, the cross section is smaller than for Au-Au under the same center-of-mass energy, with the same integration intervals for rapidity and transverse momentum.
- For Pb-Pb collisions at √s = 5.02 TeV, the cross section magnitudes are closely aligned with Au-Au results due to the similarity in atomic numbers (Au: 79, Pb: 82), with the same kinematic cuts applied.
- The tagged UPC cross section uses a neutron emission probability parameterized as P(b⊥) = 5.45 × 10⁻⁵ × Z³(A-Z)/(A²/³ b⊥²) × exp[-5.45 × 10⁻⁵ × Z³(A-Z)/(A²/³ b⊥²)].

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Li, 2024) on electromagnetic coupling constant corrections due to background photon fields in heavy-ion ultra-peripheral collisions, which had integrated out the impact parameter. The method for impact parameter-dependent cross sections was developed in the 1990s (impact1, impact2) and differs significantly from the equivalent photon approximation, particularly in explaining the transverse momentum spectrum of lepton pairs at low transverse momentum (lowTMD1, lowTMD2). The results enable more accurate comparisons with experimental data from RHIC (STAR) and LHC (ATLAS) by accounting for how different methods of extracting UPC events introduce potential errors since impact parameters are not directly observable.
