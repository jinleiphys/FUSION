# Elliptic and triangular flow anisotropy in deuteron-gold collisions at RHIC and proton-lead collisions at the LHC

> We study elliptic and triangular flow in the collisions of deuteron-gold nuclei at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV at RHIC and of proton-lead nuclei at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 5.02$ TeV at the LHC, utilizing (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the dynamic evolution of the fireball and a Glauber-based Monte-Carlo energy deposition model for simulating the fluctuating initial conditions. Sizable values of elliptic and triangular flow are obtained for both colliding systems, and the results are consistent with PHENIX, ALICE, ATLAS and CMS measurements. For these studied centralities, we find that the elliptic flow in proton-lead collisions is smaller than deuteron-gold collisions, while the triangular flows are comparable in both colliding systems. Our results indicate that the observed collective anisotropic flow in deuteron-gold and proton-lead collisions may be obtained from relativistic hydrodynamic evolution of the fireball with initial state fluctuations.

## Key claim

The observed collective anisotropic flow in deuteron-gold and proton-lead collisions may be obtained from relativistic hydrodynamic evolution of the fireball with initial state fluctuations, using ideal hydrodynamics without shear viscosity.

## Method

The authors used a Monte-Carlo Glauber-based energy deposition model to simulate fluctuating initial conditions, including both multiplicity fluctuations and initial flow fluctuations, for deuteron-gold collisions at 200 GeV and proton-lead collisions at 5.02 TeV. The pre-equilibrium stage was treated with a free-streaming approximation, followed by (3+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamics for the fireball evolution, with a sudden thermalization time of 0.7 fm/c. The final anisotropic flow coefficients were calculated using the two-particle correlation method, and results were multiplied by a factor 2/√π to account for flow fluctuations from initial geometry fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron-gold collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV; inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section taken as 42 mb
- Proton-lead collisions at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV; inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross section taken as 67.7 mb
- Thermalization time t0 = 0.7 fm/c for both colliding systems
- Final charged hadron multiplicities for |η| < 2.5: about 90 for 0-20% d-Au collisions at RHIC, about 150 for 0-2% p-Pb collisions at the LHC
- For d-Au collisions: negative binomial distribution parameters μ = 2.35, k = 1.9; transverse momentum distribution parameters b = 1.18, c = 4.09
- For p-Pb collisions: negative binomial distribution parameters μ = 5.36, k = 0.9; transverse momentum distribution parameters b = 1.12, c = 3.08
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Au nucleus: R = 6.38 fm, d = 0.535 fm; for Pb nucleus: R = 6.62 fm, d = 0.546 fm
- Hulthen wave function parameters for deuteron: A = 0.228 fm⁻¹, B = 1.18 fm⁻¹

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier Glauber-based initial condition model from heavy-ion collisions (Qin 2010) to the much smaller d-Au and p-Pb collision systems. The results are consistent with measurements from PHENIX, ALICE, ATLAS and CMS Collaborations, and show that elliptic flow in p-Pb collisions is smaller than in d-Au collisions while triangular flows are comparable, mirroring the initial geometry eccentricities. The study enables further investigations comparing different initial condition models, the role of shear viscosity, and complete event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations to better understand the origin of collective flow in small collision systems.
