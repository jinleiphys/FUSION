# Hadron wave functions and the issue of nucleon deformation

> Using gauge invariant hadronic two- and three- density correlators we extract information on the spatial distributions of quarks in hadrons, and on hadron shape and multipole moments within quenched lattice QCD. Combined with the calculation of N to Delta transition amplitudes the issue of nucleon deformation can be addressed.

## Key claim

Using gauge-invariant density correlators in quenched lattice QCD, the authors extract hadron spatial distributions and find that the rho meson is deformed, while the nucleon and Delta baryon show no statistically significant deformation; the claim of nucleon deformation is addressed indirectly via the N to Delta transition amplitude ratio \(R_{EM}\), which is found to be non-zero and consistent with experimental measurements.

## Method

The authors compute gauge-invariant two- and three-density correlators for mesons and baryons in quenched lattice QCD on a \(16^3 \times 32\) lattice at \(\beta = 6.0\) with four values of the hopping parameter \(\kappa\). For the rho meson, they decompose the density-density correlator into \(L=0\) and \(L=2\) angular momentum components to quantify deformation. They also calculate three-point correlation functions for the N to \(\Delta\) electromagnetic transition and extract the ratio \(R_{EM}\) of electric quadrupole to magnetic dipole amplitudes using the ratio method of Leinweber (1995).

## Key numbers

- Quark masses studied: \(m_q \sim 300\), 170, 130, and 85 MeV, corresponding to pion-to-rho mass ratios of 0.88, 0.84, 0.78, and 0.70 respectively.
- Inverse lattice spacing set from string tension: \(a^{-1} \sim 1.94\) GeV.
- Rho meson deformation from angular decomposition: \(\delta \equiv (3/4)(\langle 3z^2 - r^2 \rangle / \langle r^2 \rangle) \sim 0.01\) with about 80% error; direct quadrupole moment determination gives \(\delta = 0.03 \pm 0.01\).
- \(R_{EM}\) in unquenched theory (SESAM configurations) at \(\mathbf{q}^2 = 0.54\) GeV\(^2\) in the chiral limit: \((-3.7 \pm 0.5)\%\).
- \(R_{EM}\) in quenched theory on a \(32^3 \times 64\) lattice at \(\beta = 6.0\) and \(\mathbf{q}^2 = 0.15\) GeV\(^2\): \((-3.0 \pm 0.3)\%\).

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD approach of Gupta (1995) for hadron wave functions using gauge-invariant density correlators, avoiding the gauge-fixing issues of Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes. The calculation of \(R_{EM}\) for the N to \(\Delta\) transition directly addresses the issue of nucleon deformation under experimental investigation at facilities such as Bates and Jefferson Lab. The results enable future studies of finite volume and lattice spacing effects on \(R_{EM}\), and provide lattice QCD input for comparing with quark model predictions where deformation arises from the color-magnetic tensor force, and with cloudy baryon models where it arises from meson exchange currents.
