# Free Energy of a Hot Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Thermal (Th-)QCD properties appear to disagree with lattice (L-)QCD results, which suggests that Th-QCD does not have a valid perturbative expansion. However, we reproduce L-QCD using the first order $α_s$ corrections in Th-QCD.

## Key claim

The paper claims that first-order \(\alpha_s\) corrections in thermal QCD (Th-QCD) can reproduce lattice QCD (L-QCD) results for the free energy of a hot quark-gluon plasma, contradicting earlier claims that Th-QCD lacks a valid perturbative expansion. This agreement holds only when using a numerically computed running coupling \(\alpha_s(2\pi T)\) rather than the approximate two-loop coupling used in prior work.

## Method

The authors computed the pressure \(P/T^4 = -\mathcal{F}/VT^4\) at first order in \(\alpha_s\) using a running coupling obtained by integrating the renormalization group equation with the two-loop \(\beta\)-function, evaluated at scale \(\mu = 2\pi T\). They compared this to L-QCD results for 2, 2+1, and 3 dynamical quark flavors, adding a vacuum energy term \(B = 0.19\ \text{GeV/fm}^3\) to the free energy to influence the slope near \(T_c\). For the 2+1 flavor case, they used a renormalized strange quark mass \(m_s/T = 1.7\), which reduces the strange quark number by 50%.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature is assumed to be \(T_c \simeq 150\)–\(160\ \text{MeV}\).
- The vacuum energy density used is \(B = 0.19\ \text{GeV/fm}^3\).
- For the 2+1 flavor case, the renormalized strange quark mass is set to \(m_s/T = 1.7\), leading to a 50% reduction in strange quark number.
- The first-order \(\alpha_s\) corrections accurately describe L-QCD free energy for temperatures \(T \ge 2.5 T_c\).

## Context

This work extends the thermal QCD approach of Andersen, Braaten, and Strickland (1999), who used an approximate two-loop coupling and found disagreement with pure-glue L-QCD results from Boyd et al. (1996). The present paper shows that using a more accurate running coupling resolves this discrepancy, enabling a semi-perturbative description of the quark-gluon plasma free energy that matches lattice results with dynamical quarks from Karsch, Laermann, and Peikert (2000).
