# Precision Model-Independent Bounds from Global Analysis of $b \to c \ell Î½$ Form Factors

> We present a model-independent global analysis of hadronic form factors for the semileptonic decays $b\rightarrow c\ellÎ½$ that exploits lattice-QCD data, dispersion relations, and heavy-quark symmetries. The analysis yields predictions for the relevant form factors, within quantifiable bounds. These form factors are used to compute the semileptonic ratios $R(H_c)$ and various decay-product polarizations. In particular, we find $R(D_s^*)=0.20(3)$ and $R(J/Ïˆ)=0.25(3)$, predictions that can be compared to results of upcoming LHCb measurements. In developing this treatment, we obtain leading-order NRQCD results for the nonzero-recoil relations between the $B_c^+ \rightarrow \{J/Ïˆ, Î·_c \}$ form factors.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent global analysis of hadronic form factors for semileptonic \(b \to c \ell \nu\) decays that combines lattice-QCD data, dispersion relations, and heavy-quark symmetries to produce predictions within quantifiable bounds, but the authors note that some ad hoc judgment was required in assigning systematic errors for lattice calculations with incomplete error treatments, making the error bounds larger than they would otherwise be.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous BGL (Boyd-Grinstein-Lebed) parametrization fit to lattice-QCD data for seven exclusive hadronic \(b \to c\) processes: \(B \to \{D, D^*\}\), \(B_s \to \{D_s, D_s^*\}\), \(B_c \to \{J/\psi, \eta_c\}\), and \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\). They used dispersion relations and unitarity constraints from two-point Green's functions computed to two-loop perturbative QCD order, and employed heavy-quark symmetry relations (Isgur-Wise functions for heavy-light systems and leading-order NRQCD trace formalism for heavy-heavy systems) to relate form factors where lattice data was sparse. Synthetic data points were generated from the heavy-quark symmetry relations with conservative systematic error allowances (20% for heavy-light violations, 50% for heavy-heavy violations) to supplement the lattice input.

## Key numbers

* \(R(D_s^*) = 0.20(3)\), prediction for the semileptonic ratio of \(B_s \to D_s^* \ell \nu\) decays
* \(R(J/\psi) = 0.25(3)\), prediction for the semileptonic ratio of \(B_c \to J/\psi \ell \nu\) decays
* For \(B \to D\) form factors: \(f_0\) coefficient 7.2(1.0), \(f_+\) coefficient 0.25(4) (from the BGL expansion parameters table)
* For \(B \to D^*\) form factors: \(g\) coefficient 0.67(17), \(f\) coefficient 0.42(3), \(\mathcal{F}_1\) coefficient 0.07(4), \(\mathcal{F}_2\) coefficient 5.4(1.0)
* For \(B_s \to D_s\) form factors: \(f_0\) coefficient 5.23(8), \(f_+\) coefficient 0.179(5)
* For \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c\) form factors: \(H_V\) coefficient 0.256(9), \(F_1\) coefficient 0.85(4), \(F_0\) coefficient 5.14(18), \(H_A\) coefficient 0.0613(18), \(G_1\) coefficient 0.356(12), \(G_0\) coefficient 5.23(18)
* For \(B_c \to \eta_c\) form factors: \(f_0\) coefficient 6.1(6), \(f_+\) coefficient 0.18(3)
* For \(B_c \to J/\psi\) form factors: \(g\) coefficient 0.47(9), \(f\) coefficient 0.34(5), \(\mathcal{F}_1\) coefficient 0.058(10), \(\mathcal{F}_2\) coefficient 4(10)

## Context

This work extends the BGL parametrization approach (Boyd, Grinstein, and Lebed, 1997) from single-exclusive-process fits to a global simultaneous analysis of multiple \(b \to c\) semileptonic channels, building on earlier lattice-QCD calculations by Na et al. (2015), the Lattice collaboration (2015), McLean et al. (2019), Detmold et al. (2015), and Colquhoun et al. (2016). The predictions for \(R(D_s^*)\) and \(R(J/\psi)\) are designed to be compared with upcoming LHCb Run III measurements, and the framework enables future improvements as lattice-QCD calculations with more complete error treatments become available.
