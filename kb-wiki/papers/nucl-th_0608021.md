# Forward-Backward Charge Fluctuations at RHIC Energies

> We use the ultra-relativistic quantum molecular dynamic UrQMD version 2.2 to study forward backward fluctuations and compare our results with the published data by the PHOBOS.

## Key claim
The authors use the UrQMD hadronic transport model (version 2.2) to provide a baseline estimate for forward-backward charge fluctuations at RHIC, finding that the model can reproduce peripheral Au+Au data but overestimates the experimental results for central collisions, indicating that the cluster structure in UrQMD survives because hadronic rescattering is not strong enough to destroy such clusters.

## Method
The authors analyzed 5x10^5 pp and minimum bias Au+Au events at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV using UrQMD version 2.2. They computed the asymmetry variable C=(N_F-N_B)/sqrt(N_F+N_B) and its variance sigma^2_C as a function of pseudorapidity eta and window width Delta_eta, comparing results with PHOBOS data and HIJING calculations for 0-20% central and 40-60% peripheral collisions.

## Key numbers
- For pp collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, sigma^2_C is approximately 1 at eta=0.25 and increases to 1.1 at eta=2.75 (Delta_eta=0.5)
- For pp collisions, sigma^2_C reaches approximately 1.6 at eta=2 with Delta_eta=3, close to the resonance gas value K_eff~1.5 from Stephanov (1999)
- For Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, sigma^2_C is approximately 1 at eta=0.25 for both 0-20% central and 40-60% peripheral collisions (Delta_eta=0.5)
- For Au+Au collisions, sigma^2_C approaches 1.6 for 0-20% central and 1.8 for 40-60% peripheral collisions at larger eta
- For Au+Au collisions at eta=2, sigma^2_C increases up to 2.2 for central and 2.8 for peripheral collisions as Delta_eta increases
- The effective cluster multiplicity K_eff from PHOBOS data was found to be approximately 2.7 for peripheral collisions and approximately 2.2 for central collisions (Abdel-Aziz and Bleicher, 2006)

## Context
This work extends the analysis of forward-backward charge fluctuations previously performed by the PHOBOS collaboration (Back, 2006) and the UA5 experiment (Alpgard, 1983) for pp collisions. The study provides a hadronic transport baseline using UrQMD to compare with the experimental data and with HIJING calculations, enabling future extraction of the effective cluster multiplicity K_eff to test for cluster survival or melting in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC energies.
