# Comment on: Non-perturbative finite T broadening of the rho meson and dilepton emission in heavy ion-collisions (by J. Ruppert and T. Renk Phys. Rev. C71 (2005) 064903; nucl-th/0412047

> In this comment we point out several problems concerning kinematical singularities which are encountered in the calculation of the dilepton rates in {Ruppert:2004yg}. We also comment on the method introduced in {vanHees:2000bp} and further used in refs. {Riek:2004kx,Renk:2006dt,Renk:2006ax,Ruppert:2004yg}

## Key claim

The projection method used by Ruppert and Renk (2005) to restore four-transversality of the rho-meson spectral function introduces kinematical singularities on the light cone, producing a spurious and unphysical zero-mass mode of the rho-meson that artificially broadens the resonance; this artifact, not genuine non-perturbative physics, is the source of the claimed broadening.

## Method

The authors reanalyzed the self-consistent Dyson-resummation scheme of Ruppert and Renk, which uses a Phi-derivable approximation to compute rho-meson self-energies from interactions with pions. They identified that the naive projection method employed to enforce four-transversality of the polarization tensor introduces a divergence proportional to \(q^\mu q^\nu / (q^2)^2\) on the light cone, leading to a fictitious massless mode. They also examined an alternative projection method by van Hees and Knoll (2000), which constructs the full tensor from spatial components \(\Pi^{ik}\) to avoid this singularity, but noted residual ambiguities near zero energy.

## Key numbers

- At \(T = 160\) MeV and momentum \(q = 400\) MeV, the time-time component of the rho-meson polarization tensor shows a divergence on the light cone after the Ruppert-Renk projection (Fig. 1).
- The energy-weighted integral strength of the fictitious light-cone mode (obtained from the residue) is "about identical to the resonance strength integrated across \(m_\rho\)" (no explicit numerical value given for \(m_\rho\)).
- The spurious peak in the time-time component \(A^{00}(q)\) from the Ruppert-Renk method (Fig. 2) is orders of magnitude larger than the corresponding component from the van Hees-Knoll method (Fig. 3), as noted by the difference in ordinate scales.

## Context

This comment directly challenges the conclusions of Ruppert and Renk (2005), who claimed a strong broadening of the rho-meson from pion interactions alone, which contradicts expectations from low-energy QCD and the Goldstone-boson nature of the pion (Chiral, year not given). The authors also critique the use of the van Hees and Knoll (2000) projection method in subsequent studies by Renk (2006), Riek (2004), and Ruppert (2004), noting that it too has uncontrolled behavior near zero energy. The work calls for a reinvestigation of the scheme to comply with sum-rule constraints (Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn, f-sum rule, Weinberg's sum rule) before quantitative comparison with NA60 dimuon data.
