# $U_A(1)$ symmetry restoration at high baryon density

> We study the relation between chiral and $U_A(1)$ symmetries in the quark-meson model. Although quarks and mesons are described in mean field approximation, the topological susceptibility characterizing the $U_A(1)$ breaking comprises two components: one controlled by the condensate and the other by the meson fluctuation. The $U_A(1)$ restoration is governed by the competition of these components. In a hot medium, the condensates melt. However, the fluctuation is enhanced. Therefore, the $U_A(1)$ symmetry cannot be solely restored via the temperature effect. Nevertheless, the baryon density reduces the condensates and fluctuation, and thereby, the $U_A(1)$ symmetry can only be restored in a dense or dense and hot medium. The strange condensate plays a weak role in the susceptibility, and the chiral and $U_A(1)$ symmetry restorations occur almost at the same critical point.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to the temperature-only scenario where $U_A(1)$ symmetry remains broken even above the chiral critical temperature, the $U_A(1)$ symmetry can be fully restored only in a dense or dense and hot medium, because baryon density reduces both the chiral condensates and the meson fluctuations that contribute to the topological susceptibility.

## Method

The authors employ the (2+1)-flavor quark-meson model in mean field approximation, separating the topological susceptibility into a condensate-controlled part and a meson-fluctuation part. They compute the condensates, meson masses, and the various loop integrals contributing to the susceptibility as functions of baryon chemical potential and temperature, using model parameters fixed by vacuum meson properties and decay constants.

## Key numbers

- The critical baryon chemical potential for chiral phase transition at zero temperature is $\mu_B^c = 0.91$ GeV.
- The vacuum topological susceptibility obtained from the model parameters is $\chi = (191.033\ \text{MeV})^4$, in agreement with the lattice result $\chi = (191 \pm 5\ \text{MeV})^4$ from Del Debbio (2004).
- The quark-meson coupling constant is $g = 6.4$, yielding a non-strange constituent quark mass $m_q = 300$ MeV and a strange quark mass $m_s = 433$ MeV.
- The anomaly parameter controlling $U_A(1)$ breaking is $c = 4807.24$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous model calculations of $U_A(1)$ symmetry at finite temperature in NJL-type models (Fukushima 2001, Costa 2002-2005) and quark-meson models (Jiang 2015) by explicitly including baryon density effects. It addresses the limitation of lattice QCD at large baryon chemical potential due to the sign problem (Barbour 1997), and provides a framework for understanding $U_A(1)$ restoration in heavy-ion collision experiments that create high baryon density (An 2021).
