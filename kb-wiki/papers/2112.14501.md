# Exact expressions for the number of levels in single-\textit{j} orbits for three, four and five fermions

> We propose closed-form expressions of the distributions of magnetic quantum number $M$ and total angular momentum $J$ for three and four fermions in single-$j$ orbits. The latter formulas consist of polynomials with coefficients satisfying congruence properties. Such results, derived using doubly-recursive relations over $j$ and the number of fermions, enable us to deduce explicit expressions for the total number of levels in the case of three-, four- and five-fermion systems. We present applications of these formulas, such as sum rules for six-$j$ and nine-$j$ symbols, obtained from the connection with fractional-parentage coefficients, an alternative proof of the Ginocchio-Haxton relation or cancellation properties of the number of levels with a given angular momentum.

## Key claim

The authors derive closed-form polynomial expressions for the number of states with a given magnetic quantum number \(P(M)\) and total angular momentum \(Q(J)\) for three and four identical fermions in a single-\(j\) shell, and for the total number of levels for five fermions, using doubly-recursive relations over \(j\) and particle number; these formulas are exact but piecewise, depending on congruence classes of \(j\) modulo small integers.

## Method

The authors start from Talmi's recurrence relation for \(P(M;j,N)\) and apply it iteratively to obtain explicit formulas for \(P(M;j,3)\) and \(P(M;j,4)\) by splitting the analysis into cases where \(M\) is greater than or less than \(j\) (for three fermions) or \(2j\) (for four fermions). They then use the relation \(Q(J)=P(J)-P(J+1)\) to derive \(Q(J)\) for three and four fermions, and sum over \(J\) to obtain the total number of levels \(Q_{\text{tot}}\) for three, four, and five fermions. The derivations involve evaluating sums of floor functions and distinguishing six congruence classes of \(j\) modulo 3 or 6.

## Key numbers

* For three fermions, the total number of levels is \(Q_{\text{tot}}(j^3) = \frac12\left(j^2 - \frac14\right)\) (Eq. labeled qtotj3).
* For three fermions, \(Q(j;j,3) = \lfloor (2j+1)/6 \rfloor\) and \(Q(j+1;j,3) = \lfloor (2j-1)/6 \rfloor\) (from text after Eq. QjpqjN3).
* For four fermions, the expression for \(P(2j+2q;j,4)\) is given by \(\frac{X^3}{18} - \frac{X}{6} \pm \frac19\) depending on \(j-q+3/2 \bmod 3\), where \(X = j - q - 1/2\) (Eq. P2jpj4e).
* For four fermions, \(P(2j-2q;j,4) = \frac{(j+q-1/2)^3}{18} - \frac{(j+q-1/2)}{6} - H(q)f(q) + \theta(j+q)\), with \(f(q)\) given piecewise as \(\frac29 p^3 - \frac{p^2}{6} - \frac{p}{6}\) plus constants for \(p=3m, 3m+1, 3m+2\) (Eqs. hypP2jm2q and valfp).
* For five fermions, the total number of levels \(Q_{\text{tot}}(j^5)\) is derived (no explicit numeric formula shown in the truncated text, but the paper claims it as a new result).

## Context

This work extends earlier empirical formulas by Zhao and Arima (2003, 2005) and the recursion relation of Talmi (2005) for the number of levels in single-\(j\) configurations. It provides explicit closed-form expressions where previously only recursive or empirical results existed, and it enables sum rules for six-\(j\) and nine-\(j\) symbols via connection with fractional-parentage coefficients. The results also yield an alternative proof of the Ginocchio-Haxton relation and cancellation properties of \(Q(J)\).
