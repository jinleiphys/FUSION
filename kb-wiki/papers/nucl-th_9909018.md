# Boson spectra and correlations for thermal locally equilibrium systems

> The single- and multi- particle inclusive spectra for strongly inhomogeneous thermal boson systems are studied using the method of statistical operator. The thermal Wick's theorem is generalized and the analytical solution of the problem for an boost-invariant expanding boson gas is found. The results demonstrate the effects of inhomogeneity for such a system: the spectra and correlations for particles with wave-lengths larger than the system's homogeneity lengths change essentially as compared with the results based on the local Bose-Einstein thermal distributions. The effects noticeable grow for overpopulated media, where the chemical potential associated with violation of chemical equilibrium is large enough.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for strongly inhomogeneous thermal boson systems, the standard local Bose-Einstein distribution and the usual two-particle correlation formula (based on the Wigner function) break down when particle wavelengths are comparable to or larger than the system's homogeneity lengths; the author derives corrected spectra and correlation functions using a generalized thermal Wick's theorem, with the effects growing noticeably for overpopulated media where the chemical potential associated with violation of chemical equilibrium is large.

## Method

The author uses the method of the quasi-equilibrium statistical operator (Zubarev 1974, Smolyansky 1980, Panferov 1988) to construct a locally equilibrium density matrix for a free scalar Klein-Gordon field on a hypersurface, imposing commutation conditions on the energy-momentum tensor. The operator averages are obtained by solving integro-differential equations for the creation/annihilation operators under the boost-invariant hydrodynamic expansion (Bjorken 1983), and the thermal Wick's theorem is generalized to express multi-operator averages as sums of products of two-operator averages including non-zero terms like ⟨a⁺a⁺⟩ and ⟨aa⟩.

## Key numbers

* The condition for validity of the local Bose-Einstein approximation is λ_p << λ_hydr, where λ_hydr ∝ τ_f (the proper time of hydrodynamic expansion); for pions and kaons mτ_f ≥ 1, while for chiral quarks and gluons mτ << 1.
* Typical effective sizes of systems formed in A+A collisions at SPS CERN are R ≃ 3 to 7 fm from pion interferometry.
* The longitudinal interferometry size for hydrodynamically expanding systems is approximately R_L ∝ λ_hydr ≃ τ_f in the central rapidity region.
* At the initial stage of thermalization, the typical hydrodynamic length is λ_hydr ∝ τ_0 ≃ 1 fm.
* The condition for neglecting interaction energy and momentum is nα/T << 1, where α is the coupling constant with dimension of energy times volume.
* For the boost-invariant expansion, the hypersurface is defined by τ = const with τ² = t² - x_L², and the 4-velocity is u_0 = t/τ, u_L = x_L/τ, u_T = 0.

## Context

This work extends the statistical operator method of Zubarev (1974) and the Gaudin (1960) approach for globally equilibrium systems to locally equilibrium inhomogeneous boson systems, addressing a gap in the standard hydrodynamic description (DeGroot 1980) where the condition λ_p << λ_hydr fails. The results enable calculation of single- and multi-particle inclusive spectra for strongly inhomogeneous systems such as those formed in ultra-relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions at SPS, RHIC, and LHC, and provide a foundation for correcting the pion interferometry analysis (Kopylov 1974, Pratt 1986, Kolehmainen 1986, Hama 1988, Makhlin and Sinyukov 1988, Weiner 1991) when homogeneity lengths are small.
