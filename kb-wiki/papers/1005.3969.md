# Probing pre-formed alpha particles in the ground state of nuclei

> In this Letter, we report on alpha particle emission through the nuclear break-up in the reaction 40Ca on a 40Ca target at 50A MeV. It is observed that, similarly to nucleons, alpha particles can be emitted to the continuum with very specific angular distribution during the reaction. The alpha particle properties can be understood as resulting from an alpha cluster in the daughter nucleus that is perturbed by the short range nuclear attraction of the collision partner and emitted. A time-dependent theory that describe the alpha particle wave-function evolution is able to reproduce qualitatively the observed angular distribution. This mechanism offers new possibilities to study alpha particle properties in the nuclear medium.

## Key claim
The paper reports that alpha particles can be emitted from the ground state of 40Ca via a sudden nuclear break-up mechanism (the "Towing mode"), and that the observed angular distributions can be qualitatively reproduced by a time-dependent Schrodinger equation calculation assuming a pre-formed alpha cluster wave-function in the ground state. The claim is hedged by noting that the model is schematic, no adjustment was made to the nuclear potential, and the energy resolution (1.15 MeV) prevented precise separation of final states.

## Method
The experiment used a 50A MeV 40Ca beam on a natural Ca target at GANIL, detecting ejectiles in the SPEG spectrometer in coincidence with light charged particles in the INDRA 4π array. The missing energy of the 36Ar daughter nucleus was reconstructed after alpha emission, and angular distributions were extracted for three low-lying states of 36Ar (ground state, first 2+ state, and a state around 4.4 MeV). A time-dependent Schrodinger equation (TDSE) calculation was performed on a 3D mesh, modeling the alpha particle wave-function initially in 6s, 5d, or 4g states of 40Ca, with the projectile following a Coulomb trajectory.

## Key numbers
- Beam energy: 50A MeV for 40Ca on 40Ca target.
- Excitation energy resolution: 350 keV (Δp/p = 9 × 10⁻⁵).
- Alpha particle energy threshold: 4 MeV (software threshold).
- Alpha particle energy resolution: 1.1 MeV; missing energy resolution: 1.15 MeV.
- Target thickness: 0.2 mg/cm² self-supported natural Ca.
- TDSE mesh: 259 × 259 × 199 points with r-step 0.2 fm and time step 1.22 fm/c.
- Impact parameter range for TDSE: 9.5 to 11.5 fm.
- Binding energies of alpha states in 40Ca: 7.0 MeV (6s), 6.4 MeV (5d), 5.5 MeV (4g).
- Potential parameters: V₀ = -175.7 MeV, a₀ = 0.73 fm, α = 0.3, R₀ = 4.33 fm.
- Angular range for INDRA: 14° to 176°; SPEG: 0.5° to 5°.

## Context
This work extends the "Towing mode" mechanism previously observed for nucleon emission (Scarpaci 1998, Lacroix 1999) to alpha particles, providing evidence for pre-formed alpha clusters in the ground state of 40Ca. It complements studies of alpha clustering in N=Z nuclei (Von Oertzen 2006) and AMD calculations of the α+36Ar configuration in 40Ca (Taniguchi 2007), while contrasting with the Ikeda picture (Ikeda 1972) where clusters appear primarily in excited states near decay thresholds. The results open the possibility of extracting spectroscopic information about pre-formed alpha particle wave-functions in ground states, analogous to what was done for nucleons in 11Be and 6He (Lim 2007, Assie 2008, 2009).
