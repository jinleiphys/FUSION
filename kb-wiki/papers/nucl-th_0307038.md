# Valence Quark Distribution in A=3 Nuclei

> We calculate the quark distribution function for 3He/3H in a relativistic quark model of nuclear structure which adequately reproduces the nucleon approximation, nuclear binding energies, and nuclear sizes for small nuclei. The results show a clear distortion from the quark distribution function for individual nucleons (EMC effect) arising dominantly from a combination of recoil and quark tunneling effects. Antisymmetrization (Pauli) effects are found to be small due to limited spatial overlaps. We compare our predictions with a published parameterization of the nuclear valence quark distributions and find significant agreement.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the EMC effect in A=3 nuclei arises dominantly from a combination of recoil (Fermi motion) and quark tunneling effects, with antisymmetrization (Pauli) effects found to be small due to limited spatial overlaps between quarks from different quasi-nucleons; the calculated valence quark distributions show significant agreement with a published phenomenological parameterization, though the model overpredicts the ratio at large Bjorken-x.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic quark model of A=3 nuclei using four-component Dirac quark wavefunctions in a confining potential composed of three individual linearly confining wells arranged in an equilateral triangle, with the well separation d and a quark delocalization parameter epsilon determined variationally by minimizing the total energy. The valence quark distributions are calculated at a low momentum scale (below 1 GeV^2) by evaluating light-cone matrix elements in Peierls-Yoccoz projected momentum eigenstates, with the nuclear wavefunction including full antisymmetrization over all nine quarks and rotational projection onto zero orbital angular momentum. The calculation incorporates recoil effects through a modified spectator overlap function G(k,epsilon) and includes tunneling terms to order epsilon*Delta(d) and exchange terms to all orders in Delta(d), where Delta(d) is the overlap of quark wavefunctions from different wells.

## Key numbers

- Variational parameter epsilon = 0.104 for 3He/3H (with d = 1.80 fm)
- Variational well separation d = 1.80 fm for 3He/3H
- Binding energy of (4 +/- 2) MeV for 3He/3H
- RMS matter size slightly smaller than 1.34 fm (the 4He value)
- Confining potential slope k = 0.9 GeV/fm with r0 = 0.57 fm
- Overlap Delta(d) ~ 0.1, providing a two order of magnitude suppression of Pauli exchange effects
- Quark tunneling parameter epsilon = 0.104 at the variational minimum

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier relativistic quark model calculations for A=4 nuclei (Benesh, Goldman, Stephenson, 2000) to the A=3 system, providing a dynamical quark-level description of the EMC effect first observed by the EMC collaboration (1983). The model contrasts with heuristic approaches such as nucleon swelling, binding energy rescaling, and meson convolution models (Close, 1988) by providing a parameter-free calculation from quark degrees of freedom. The finding that Pauli effects are negligible differs from the work of Hood (1998) but agrees with other authors (OTHRS, 2000), and the model naturally avoids the excess antiquark amplitude predicted by pion excess models (which is inconsistent with Drell-Yan measurements from E772). The results enable future inclusion of collective oscillations of well centers, self-consistent confining potentials, and extension to larger A nuclei.
