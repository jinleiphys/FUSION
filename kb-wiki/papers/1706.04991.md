# Quantifying the sensitivity of Big Bang Nucleosynthesis to isospin breaking with input from lattice QCD

> We perform the first quantitative study of the sensitivity of Big Bang Nucleosynthesis to variations in isospin breaking with precise input from lattice QCD calculations. The predicted light nuclear abundances are most sensitive to the neutron-proton mass splitting as both the initial relative abundance of neutrons to protons and the $n \rightleftharpoons p$ weak reaction rates are very sensitive to this quantity. Lattice QCD has been used to determine this mass splitting to greater than 5-sigma, including contributions from both the down-quark up-quark mass splitting, $2δ= m_d-m_u$ and from electromagnetic coupling of the quarks to the photons with a strength governed by the fine structure constant, $α_{fs}$. At leading order in isospin breaking, the contribution of $δ$ and $α_{fs}$ to $M_n-M_p$ and the nuclear reaction rates can be varied independently. We use this knowledge and input from lattice QCD to quantitatively study variations of the predicted light nuclear abundances as $δ$ and $α_{fs}$ are varied. The change in the D and ${}^4$He abundances individually allow for potentially large simultaneous variations in $δ$ and $α_{fs}$ while maintaining consistency with the observed abundances, however the combined comparison restricts variations in these sources of isospin breaking to less than $\lesssim1.25\%$ at the 3-sigma confidence level. This sensitivity can be used to place tight constraints on prospective beyond the Standard Model theories that would modify these isospin breaking effects in the primordial Universe.

## Key claim
The combined comparison of deuterium and helium-4 abundances restricts variations in the two Standard Model sources of isospin breaking (the down-up quark mass splitting and the fine structure constant) to less than approximately 1.25% at the 3-sigma confidence level, even though each abundance individually would allow much larger correlated variations.

## Method
The authors modified a publicly available Big Bang Nucleosynthesis code (based on Kawano's work) to independently vary the contributions of the down-up quark mass splitting parameter δ and the fine structure constant α_fs to the neutron-proton mass splitting, using lattice QCD input to parameterize these contributions. They computed the resulting primordial abundances of hydrogen, deuterium, and helium-4 for a grid of (δ, α_fs) values, then compared the predicted D and ⁴He abundances to observed values to derive joint constraints.

## Key numbers
- The neutron-proton mass splitting from lattice QCD: ΔM_{n-p}^δ = 2.39(12) MeV (QCD contribution from quark mass splitting)
- The electromagnetic contribution to the neutron-proton mass splitting: ΔM_{n-p}^γ = -1.10(12) MeV (derived from experimental splitting minus lattice QCD value)
- Combined 3-sigma constraint: variations in δ and α_fs are restricted to ≤ 1.25% when varied in a correlated manner
- Independent variation constraint at 3-sigma: variations of δ and α_fs individually are restricted to ≤ 0.8%
- The values of δ and α_fs needed to reduce ⁷Li/H to the observed upper limit of (1.6 ± 0.3) × 10^{-10} lie in a region of greater than 5-sigma discrepancy from the combined D and ⁴He constraints

## Context
This work provides the first quantitative study of BBN sensitivity to simultaneous variations of both Standard Model sources of isospin breaking, using precise lattice QCD input. Previous studies considered variations of the fine structure constant alone (Kolb 1985, Campbell 1994, Bergstrom 1999, Nollett 2002) or the average light quark mass (Bedaque 2010, Epelbaum 2012, Berengut 2013), but did not separately treat the down-up quark mass splitting. The results enable constraints on beyond-Standard-Model theories that would modify isospin breaking effects in the primordial Universe, and rule out primordial variations in δ and α_fs as a resolution to the ⁷Li puzzle.
