# Effects of Minijets on Hadronic Spectra and Azimuthal Harmonics in Au-Au Collisions at 200 GeV

> The production of hadrons in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC in the low transverse-momentum ($p_T$) region is investigated in the recombination model with emphasis on the effects of minijets on the azimuthal anisotropy. Since the study is mainly on the hadronization of partons at late time, the fluid picture is not used to trace the evolution of the system. The inclusive distributions at low $p_T$ are determined as the recombination products of thermal partons. The $p_T$ dependencies of both pion and proton have a common exponential factor apart from other dissimilar kinematic and resonance factors, because they are inherited from the same pool of thermal partons. Instead of the usual description based on hydrodynamics, the azimuthal anisotropy of the produced hadrons is explained as the consequence of the effects of minijets, either indirectly through the recombination of enhanced thermal partons in the vicinity of the trajectories of the semihard partons, or directly through thermal-shower recombination. Although our investigation is focussed on the single-particle distribution at midrapidity, we give reasons why a component in that distribution can be identified with the ridge, which together with the second harmonic $v_2$ is due to the semihard partons created near the medium surface that lead to calculable anisotropy in $ϕ$. It is shown that the higher azimuthal harmonics, $v_n$, can also be well reproduced without reference to flow. The $p_T$ and centrality dependencies of the higher harmonics are prescribed by the interplay between TT and TS recombination components. The implication of the success of this drastic departure from the conventional approach is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the azimuthal anisotropy (including the ridge and all harmonic coefficients \(v_n\)) in Au-Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV can be explained by the effects of minijets from semihard partons via the recombination model, without invoking hydrodynamical flow or early thermalization. The hedge is that this alternative approach applies only to the low transverse-momentum region (\(p_T < 2\) GeV/c) and relies on the assumption that local equilibration of the soft sector is achieved only at late time just before hadronization.

## Method

The authors use the recombination model to compute single-particle distributions of pions and protons at midrapidity, separating the production into three components: an azimuthally isotropic Base from thermal parton recombination (TT for pions, TTT for protons), a Ridge component from enhanced thermal partons near semihard parton trajectories, and a Minijet component from thermal-shower (TS) recombination. The azimuthal dependence of the Ridge is derived from a geometric function \(S(\phi,b)\) that relates the spatial elliptical overlap region to the momentum anisotropy via the segment length on the initial ellipse through which semihard partons can contribute. The harmonic coefficients \(v_n\) are then calculated from the Fourier decomposition of the total \(\phi\)-dependent distribution, with the \(p_T\) dependence governed by the interplay between the Base and Ridge thermal components characterized by inverse slopes \(T_0\) and \(T\).

## Key numbers

*   Inverse slope \(T = 0.283\) GeV for the common exponential factor \(\exp(-p_T/T)\) in both pion and proton spectra at 20-30% centrality, determined from a fit to proton data.
*   Inverse slope for the Base component \(T_0 = 0.245\) GeV, used to fit \(v_2^\pi\) at 0-5% centrality.
*   Inverse slope for the Ridge component \(T_R = 0.32\) GeV, giving a difference \(T_R - T = 47\) MeV, which the authors note is close to the 45 MeV difference reported by Putschke between triggered ridge and inclusive distributions.
*   Width of the Gaussian correlation in \(\phi\) between a semihard parton and a ridge hadron: \(\sigma = 0.33\), used to define the angular range in the geometric function \(S(\phi,b)\).

## Context

This work extends the recombination model of Hwa and Yang (2003) and earlier studies by Hwa, Chiu, and Zhu (2008-2010) to the low-\(p_T\) region (\(p_T < 2\) GeV/c) at RHIC, where hydrodynamical models (e.g., those by Heinz and Kolb) have been the conventional approach. The paper directly contradicts the hydrodynamical interpretation of elliptic flow and higher harmonics as arising from anisotropic pressure gradients and early thermalization, instead attributing them to minijet effects from semihard partons created near the medium surface. It enables a non-flow, non-hydrodynamical framework for understanding azimuthal harmonics and the ridge phenomenon, and suggests that the success of this alternative approach may cast doubt on the necessity of the assumptions made in conventional hydrodynamical descriptions.
