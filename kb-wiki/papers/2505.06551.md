# On the validity of the complex Langevin method near the deconfining phase transition in QCD at finite density

> In our previous paper [JHEP 10 (2020) 144], we found that the complex Langevin (CL) method works for QCD at finite density on the $16^3 \times 32$ lattice in the low-temperature high-density regime within the range $μ/ T = 1.6 - 9.6$ with $μ$ and $T$ being the quark chemical potential and the temperature, which enabled us to see a clear trend towards the formation of the Fermi sphere. Here we investigate the validity of the CL method on the $24^3 \times 12$ lattice in the deconfined phase near the deconfinement phase transition. As before, we use four-flavor staggered fermions and judge the validity using the criterion based on the probability distribution of the drift term. The spatial extent is $L = (1.3 - 2.7 {\rm ~fm} )> Λ_{\rm LQCD}^{-1} \sim 1{\rm ~fm}$, in contrast to our previous study with $L < Λ_{\rm LQCD}^{-1}$. We find that the CL method works in a broad region up to $μ/ T = 4.8$, while it starts to fail as we approach the phase boundary due to the singular drift problem, which can be understood qualitatively by extending the Banks-Casher relation to the case at finite density.

## Key claim
The complex Langevin (CL) method works in a broad region of the deconfined phase up to μ/T = 4.8 on a 24³ × 12 lattice, but fails as one approaches the phase boundary due to the singular drift problem caused by near-zero eigenvalues of the Dirac operator, which can be understood qualitatively by extending the Banks-Casher relation to finite density.

## Method
The authors performed complex Langevin simulations of QCD with four-flavor staggered fermions on a 24³ × 12 lattice at β = 5.2–5.5 and μ/T = 0.6–4.8 with bare quark mass m = 0.01. They judged the validity of the CL method using the criterion based on the probability distribution of the drift term, fitting the tail of the fermionic drift histogram to both a power law and an exponential function to distinguish between valid and invalid regimes.

## Key numbers
- The CL method works up to μ/T = 4.8 in the deconfined phase, with the spatial extent L = 1.3–2.7 fm > Λ_LQCD⁻¹ ∼ 1 fm.
- At μ/T = 0.6, the CL method is valid at β = 5.3, 5.4, and 5.5 but not at β = 5.2, where the fermionic drift histogram shows a power-law tail with exponent p = 7.4 ± 0.3.
- The lattice spacing ranges from a = 0.112 fm at β = 5.2 to a = 0.053 fm at β = 5.5, corresponding to temperatures T = 146–308 MeV.
- The physical pion mass is approximately 519 MeV at β = 5.2 and 742 MeV at β = 5.4.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Ito et al., 2020) which found the CL method works in the low-temperature high-density regime on a 16³ × 32 lattice with L < Λ_LQCD⁻¹, where they observed a clear trend towards Fermi sphere formation. The present study investigates the deconfined phase near the phase transition, motivated by earlier work by Fodor et al. (2015) who compared CL results with reweighting on a 16³ × 8 lattice and found instability near β ∼ 5.15. The results enable future CL simulations to explore the color superconducting phase predicted by Schwinger-Dyson equation studies (Yokota et al., 2023), with ongoing work by Miura et al. (2025) to confirm this prediction.
