# Oscillating Color Transparency in $πA\to πp (A-1)$ and $γA\to πN (A-1)$

> The energy dependence of $90^o$ $cm$ fixed angle scattering of $πp \to π' p'$ and $γp\to π^+ n$ at large momentum transfer are found to be well described in terms of interfering short and long distance amplitudes with dynamical phases induced by Sudakov effects. We calculate the color transparency ratio for the corresponding processes in nuclear environments $πA\to π'p(A-1)$ and $γA\to πN (A-1)$ taking nuclear filtering into account. A prediction that the transparency ratio for these reactions will oscillate with energy provides an important test of the Sudakov phase shift and nuclear filtering hypothesis which is testable in upcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the color transparency ratio for pion-induced and photon-induced nuclear reactions will oscillate with energy, with the oscillations being 180 degrees out of phase with oscillations observed in the corresponding free-space cross sections, providing a test of the Sudakov phase shift and nuclear filtering hypothesis that is testable in upcoming experiments at CEBAF and BNL.

## Method

The authors fit free-space fixed-angle 90-degree center-of-mass scattering data for pi p -> pi' p' and gamma p -> pi+ n using a two-component model that includes interfering short-distance and long-distance amplitudes with dynamical phases from Sudakov effects. They then calculate the color transparency ratio for nuclear targets by incorporating nuclear filtering, where long-distance amplitudes are attenuated by standard hadronic cross sections (sigma_pi p = 26 mb, sigma_pp = 40 mb) and short-distance amplitudes are attenuated by a model cross section scaling as k/(x1 x2 Q^2), with short-range nuclear correlations included.

## Key numbers

- For pi p -> pi' p' free-space fit: best fit parameters A0 = -0.638, A1 = 5.1, c1 = 25.6, d1 = 5.13, A2 = -0.065, c2 = -26.3, d2 = -1.16 with chi^2/dof = 1.97; the short-distance model s^-8 fit gives chi^2/dof = 99
- For gamma p -> pi+ n free-space fit: best fit parameters A0 = 0.90, A1 = 2.65/s, c1 = 64.5, A2 = 8.01/s, c2 = -126.4 with chi^2/dof = 0.69; the short-distance s^-7 model gives chi^2/dof = 2.9
- Absorptive cross sections used: sigma_pi p = 26 mb, sigma_pp = 40 mb; short-distance cross section model sigma_S = k(1.6 mb)(GeV^2/Q^2) with k varied between 5 and 10
- Transparency ratio calculations shown for nuclear targets with A = 12, 56, and 197

## Context

This work extends the oscillatory color transparency phenomenon previously observed in proton-proton reactions at BNL (Carroll et al., 1988) to pion-initiated and photon-initiated processes, showing that oscillations are a generic prediction of perturbative QCD rather than unique to pp reactions. The calculations use the nuclear filtering framework developed by Ralston and Pire (1990) and incorporate Sudakov phase effects from Botts and Sterman (1989). The predictions are testable in experiments currently underway at CEBAF (Gao and Holt, Jefferson Lab experiment E94-104) and would distinguish the pQCD-based approach from hadronic-basis models (Farrar et al., 1990; Jennings and Miller, 1993) which predict monotonic or linearly increasing transparency ratios.
