# High-energy neutrino deep inelastic scattering cross sections

> We present a state-of-the-art prediction for cross sections of neutrino deep inelastic scattering (DIS) from nucleon at high neutrino energies, $E_ν$, from 100 GeV to 1000 EeV ($10^{12}$ GeV). Our calculations are based on the latest CT18 NNLO parton distribution functions (PDFs) and their associated uncertainties. In order to make predictions for the highest energies, we extrapolate the PDFs to small $x$ according to several procedures and assumptions, thus affecting the uncertainties at ultra-high $E_ν$; we quantify the uncertainties corresponding to these choices. Similarly, we quantify the uncertainties introduced by the nuclear corrections which are required to evaluate neutrino-nuclear cross sections for neutrino telescopes. These results can be applied to currently-running astrophysical neutrino observatories, such as IceCube and KM3NeT, as well as various future experiments which have been proposed.

## Key claim

The paper presents state-of-the-art predictions for neutrino deep inelastic scattering cross sections from 100 GeV to 1000 EeV, but the uncertainties at ultra-high energies depend on how the parton distribution functions are extrapolated to very small Bjorken x, which is not directly constrained by experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculate charged-current and neutral-current neutrino-nucleon deep inelastic scattering cross sections using the CT18 next-to-next-to-leading order parton distribution functions as a baseline, with hard coefficient functions determined in the S-ACOT-chi heavy-quark scheme. They extrapolate the PDFs to small x using several procedures (including evolution and freeze-in methods) and quantify the resulting uncertainties, while also incorporating nuclear corrections from the EPPS21 and nCTEQ15WZ nuclear PDF sets to evaluate cross sections on nuclear targets relevant for neutrino telescopes.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino energy range covered: 100 GeV to 1000 EeV (10^12 GeV)
- The backward evolution region Q in [1, 1.3] GeV contributes at most approximately 0.8% (1.2%) to the (anti)neutrino cross sections, peaking at E_nu = 10^3 GeV
- The extrapolation region x less than 10^-9 contributes at most 3% for E_nu = 10^12 GeV
- The variation at E_nu = 100 GeV from backward evolution can be negligible for charged-current DIS, while 1% to 3% for neutral current
- The N3LO' corrections give a small decrease at low energy and a slightly larger increase at high energy, with the difference much smaller than the NNLO/NLO difference

## Context

This work extends the CSMS calculation (Cooper-Sarkar 2011), which is frequently used in IceCube publications, by using the more recent CT18 NNLO PDFs and exploring approximate N3LO corrections. It also compares with other calculations such as GQRS (Gandhi 1998), CTW (Connolly 2011), and BGR (Bertone 2018, Garcia 2020). The results enable improved modeling of neutrino detection and propagation through Earth for current observatories like IceCube and KM3NeT, as well as future facilities such as IceCube-Gen2, GRAND, and POEMMA.
