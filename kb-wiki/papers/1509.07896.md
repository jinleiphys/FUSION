# Asymmetry energy of nuclear matter: Temperature and density dependence, and validity of semi-empirical formula

> In this work, we have done a completely microscopic calculation using a many-body variational method based on the cluster expansion of energy to compute the asymmetry energy of nuclear matter. In our calculations, we have employed the $AV_{18}$ nuclear potential. We have also investigated the temperature and density dependence of asymmetry energy. Our results show that the asymmetry energy of nuclear matter depends on both density and temperature. We have also studied the effects of different terms in the asymmetry energy of nuclear matter. These investigations indicate that at different densities and temperatures, the contribution of parabolic term is very substantial with respect to the other terms. Therefore, we can conclude that the parabolic approximation is a relatively good estimation, and our calculated binding energy of asymmetric nuclear matter is in a relatively good agreement with that of semi-empirical mass formula. However, for the accurate calculations, it is better to consider the effects of other terms.

## Key claim

The parabolic approximation (semi-empirical mass formula) for the asymmetry energy of nuclear matter is a relatively good estimation, but for accurate calculations the effects of non-square terms (first, third, and fourth powers of the asymmetry parameter) should be considered, especially at high densities and low values of the asymmetry parameter.

## Method

The authors performed a fully microscopic variational many-body calculation using the cluster expansion of energy, employing the AV18 nucleon-nucleon potential. They computed the binding energy of asymmetric nuclear matter as a function of the asymmetry parameter (ξ = (N-Z)/(N+Z)) at various densities and temperatures. The results were fitted to a polynomial up to the fourth power of ξ, and the coefficients were compared to the semi-empirical mass formula which contains only a quadratic term in ξ.

## Key numbers

- At T = 0 MeV and density ρ = 0.3 fm⁻³, the quadratic coefficient a₂ = 37.37 MeV, while the quartic coefficient a₄ = 3.02 MeV (ratio a₄/a₂ ~ 0.08).
- At T = 0 MeV and density ρ = 0.05 fm⁻³, the ratio |a₁/a₂| is of order 10⁻³ (a₁ = -0.05167 MeV, a₂ = 12.01 MeV).
- At T = 0 MeV and density ρ = 0.5 fm⁻³, the ratio |a₁/a₂| is of order 10⁻¹ (a₁ = -0.6323 MeV, a₂ = 50.63 MeV).
- The highest order of magnitude of the ratio |a₃/a₂| reaches about 10⁻² across all densities and temperatures.
- The magnitude of |a₄/a₂| is of order 10⁻¹ across all densities and temperatures.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous variational calculations on nucleonic matter (Bordhar et al., multiple years) by explicitly computing the asymmetry energy with the AV18 potential at finite temperature. It tests the validity of the parabolic approximation commonly used in semi-empirical mass formulas, which is important for studies of neutron star structure, heavy ion collisions, and supernova dynamics. The results enable more accurate modeling of asymmetric nuclear matter by quantifying when non-parabolic terms become significant, particularly at high densities and low asymmetry parameters.
