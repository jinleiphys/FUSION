# Exact Relativistic Ideal Hydrodynamical Solutions in (1+3)D with Longitudinal and Transverse Flows

> A new method for solving relativistic ideal hydrodynamics in (1+3)D is developed. Longitudinal and transverse radial flows are explicitly embedded and the hydrodynamic equations are reduced to a single equation for the transverse velocity field only, which is much more tractable. As an application we use the method to find analytically all possible solutions with power dependence on proper time and transverse radius. Possible application to the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions and possible generalizations of the method are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors develop a new method to reduce the (1+3)D relativistic ideal hydrodynamics equations to a single constraint equation for the transverse velocity field only, under the condition that longitudinal boost-invariant flow and transverse radial flow are embedded. The method is demonstrated by finding all possible solutions with power-law dependence on proper time and transverse radius, but the authors note that the approach is limited to cases where the flow field ansatz has vanishing azimuthal and longitudinal rapidity components.

## Method

The authors embed the Hwa-Bjorken boost-invariant longitudinal flow (setting u^η=0) and isotropic transverse radial flow (setting u^φ=0) into the flow velocity field in curved coordinates (τ,η,ρ,φ). Using a linear equation of state p=ν(ε+p) with speed of sound c_s=√(ν/(1-ν)), they express the stress tensor components and substitute them into the covariant hydrodynamics equations. The η and φ components of the equations force the pressure to depend only on τ and ρ, while the τ and ρ components are reduced to a single second-order partial differential equation for the transverse velocity field v̄_ρ(τ,ρ) through consistency conditions on the combined field variable K.

## Key numbers

* Speed of sound constraint: c_s ≤ 1 requires 0 < ν ≤ 1/2
* Solution-I (1-D Bjorken): A=0 with 0<ν≤1/2, pressure p = constant/τ^{1/(1-ν)}
* Solution-II (3-D Hubble): A=1, B=-1, C=1 with 0<ν≤1/2, pressure p = constant/(τ^2-ρ^2)^{3/[2(1-ν)]}
* Solution-III: A=1, B=1, C=-1 with 0<ν≤1/2, pressure p = constant/[(τρ)^{1/(1-ν)} (ρ^2-τ^2)^{(1-3ν)/(2ν-2ν^2)}]
* Solution-IV: A=1, B=1/3, C=-1/3 with ν=1/4, pressure p = constant × (ρ^{2/3}-τ^{2/3})^{2/3}/(ρτ)^{4/3}
* Solution-V: A=-1, B=-1, C=1 with ν=1/2, pressure p = constant × (τ^2-ρ^2)

## Context

This work extends the known Hwa-Bjorken (1+1)D solution (Hwa 1974, Bjorken 1983) and the (1+3)D Hubble-type solution to a broader class of analytical solutions with both longitudinal and transverse radial flows. The method enables systematic classification of all power-law solutions for the transverse velocity field, which the authors note has implications for constraining flow profile ansatze in blast-wave models used at RHIC. The authors suggest extensions to include nontrivial longitudinal embeddings from more recent (1+1)D solutions (Nagy 2007, Bialas 2007, Pratt 2008, Wong 2008) and to treat elliptic flow via linearized perturbations or hyperbolic coordinate embeddings.
