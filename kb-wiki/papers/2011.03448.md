# Partial decay widths of $ϕ(2170)$ to kaonic resonances

> In this work we study the strong decays of $ϕ(2170)$ to final states involving the kaonic resonances $K(1460)$, $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$, on which experimental data have recently been extracted by the BESIII Collaboration. The formalism developed here is based on interpreting $ϕ(2170)$ and $K(1460)$ as states arising from three-hadron dynamics, which is inspired by our earlier works. For $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$ we investigate different descriptions, such as a mixture of states belonging to the nonet of axial resonances, or the former one as a state originating from the vector-pseudoscalar dynamics. The ratios among the partial widths of $K^+(1460)K^-$, $K^+_1(1400)K^-$ and $K^+_1(1270)K^-$ obtained are compatible with the experimental results, reinforcing the three-body nature of $ϕ(2170)$. Within our formalism, we can also explain the suppressed decay of $ϕ(2170)$ to $K^*(892) \bar K^*(892)$, as found by the BESIII Collaboration. Furthermore, our results can be useful in clarifying the properties of $K(1460)$, $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$ when higher statistics data would be available.

## Key claim

The paper shows that interpreting $\phi(2170)$ as a three-body resonance arising from $\phi K\bar K$ dynamics (specifically as a $\phi f_0(980)$ molecular state) yields partial decay width ratios to $K^+(1460)K^-$, $K^+_1(1400)K^-$, and $K^+_1(1270)K^-$ that are compatible with BESIII experimental results, while simultaneously explaining the suppressed decay to $K^*(892)\bar K^*(892)$, but the results depend on which model is used for the $K_1$ resonances, with different approaches giving significantly different coupling values.

## Method

The authors calculate the partial decay widths of $\phi(2170)$ to $K^+(1460)K^-$, $K^+_1(1400)K^-$, and $K^+_1(1270)K^-$ using a triangular loop diagram involving virtual $\phi$, $f_0(980)$, and $K^\pm$ mesons, with $\phi(2170)$ treated as a $\phi f_0(980)$ molecular state and $K(1460)$ as a $K f_0(980)$ state. For $K_1(1270)$ and $K_1(1400)$, three different models are employed: a mixing scheme of axial nonet states (Model B), a molecular description where $K_1(1270)$ arises from vector-pseudoscalar dynamics (Model A), and a phenomenological approach using experimental data (Model C). The coupling constants for each vertex are determined from previous three-body calculations and chiral unitary approaches, and the loop integrals are evaluated using Passarino-Veltman reduction.

## Key numbers

- The coupling of $\phi(2170)$ to $\phi f_0(980)$ is $|g_{\phi_R\to \phi f_0}| = 3123 \pm 561$ MeV, obtained by varying the partial decay width $\Gamma_{\phi_R\to \phi f_0}$ in the range 30–50 MeV.
- The coupling of $K(1460)$ to $K^+ f_0(980)$ is $|g_{K^+_R\to K^+ f_0}| = 4858 \pm 1337$ MeV, obtained by varying the $K(1460)$ width in the range 50–110 MeV.
- For the mixing model (Model B), the $K_1(1270)\to\phi K$ coupling averages $1104\pm171$ MeV and the $K_1(1400)\to\phi K$ coupling averages $3533\pm21$ MeV, using mixing angles of $29^\circ$, $47^\circ$, and $62^\circ$.
- For the molecular model (Model A), the two poles of $K_1(1270)$ have couplings to $\phi K$ of $g^{(1)}=2096-i1208$ MeV (pole at $1195-i123$ MeV) and $g^{(2)}=1166-i774$ MeV (pole at $1284-i73$ MeV).
- For the phenomenological model (Model C), three solutions for $|g_{K^+_1(1270)\to \phi K^+}|$ are found: $3967\pm419$ MeV (Solution $\mathbb{S}_1$), $12577\pm763$ MeV (Solution $\mathbb{S}_2$), and $19841\pm1177$ MeV (Solution $\mathbb{S}_3$); the $K_1(1400)$ coupling is $8480\pm1333$ MeV.
- The $f_0(980)\to K\bar K$ coupling is $g_{f_0\to (K \bar K)_0}=3895+i1328$ MeV from the chiral unitary approach.

## Context

This work extends the three-body interpretation of $\phi(2170)$ developed by Martinez Torres et al. (2008) and the three-kaon description of $K(1460)$ by Torres et al. (2011), applying them to explain the decay patterns recently measured by the BESIII Collaboration (2020). The results contradict predictions from conventional $s\bar s$ quarkonium models (Barnes 2002; Ding 2007) and hybrid $s\bar s g$ descriptions (Ding 2006), which would predict larger decay widths to $K^*(892)\bar K^*(892)$ and $K^*(1410)\bar K$ than observed. The work enables future experimental studies with higher statistics to clarify the nature of $K(1460)$, $K_1(1270)$, and $K_1(1400)$, and suggests that the three-body molecular interpretation of $\phi(2170)$ provides a natural explanation for the suppressed $K^*(892)\bar K^*(892)$ decay channel.
