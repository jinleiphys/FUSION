# Nonuniqueness of Green's functions at special points

> We investigate a new property of retarded Green's functions using AdS/CFT. The Green's functions are not unique at special points in complex momentum space. This arises because there is no unique incoming mode at the horizon and is similar to the "pole-skipping" phenomenon in holographic chaos. Our examples include the bulk scalar field, the bulk Maxwell vector and scalar modes, and the shear mode of gravitational perturbations. In these examples, the special points are always located at $ω_\star = -i(2πT)$ with appropriate values of complex wave number.

## Key claim

Retarded Green's functions in AdS/CFT are not uniquely defined at special points in complex momentum space because the incoming-wave boundary condition at the black hole horizon becomes ambiguous, but these special points lie in the lower-half frequency plane (ω⋆ = −i(2πT)) and therefore do not indicate chaotic behavior, unlike the pole-skipping phenomenon in holographic chaos.

## Method

The authors analyze bulk field equations near the black hole horizon in incoming Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates for Schwarzschild-AdS backgrounds. They identify special points where the horizon changes from a regular singularity to a regular point, eliminating the unique distinction between incoming and outgoing modes. They examine the scalar field, Maxwell vector and scalar modes, and the shear mode of gravitational perturbations, expanding the field equations around each special point to show that the incoming-wave boundary condition depends on the slope δq/δω.

## Key numbers

- Special points always occur at ω⋆ = −i(2πT) for all examples considered
- Massive scalar (SAdS_{p+2}): ω⋆ = −i, q⋆² = −[2p(p+1)+4m²]/(p+1)²
- Maxwell vector (SAdS_{p+2}): ω⋆ = −i, q⋆² = −2(p−2)/(p+1)
- Maxwell scalar (SAdS_{p+2}): ω⋆ = −i, q⋆² = +2(p−2)/(p+1)
- Gravitational shear (SAdS₄): ω⋆ = −i, q⋆² = +4/3
- Gravitational sound (SAdS₄): ω⋆ = +i, q⋆² = −4/3
- For p=1 massive scalar at the special point, the Green's function depends on δq/δω as G_O^⋆ = −(Δ₊−Δ₋)(1+δq/δω)/(1−δq/δω)
- For p=2 Maxwell vector mode, the product of longitudinal and transverse Green's functions satisfies Π_T^⋆ Π_L^⋆ = (2πT)²

## Context

This work extends the pole-skipping phenomenon previously studied for energy-density correlators by Grozdanov (2017) and Blake (2018) to a broader class of Green's functions including scalar fields, Maxwell fields, and gravitational shear modes. Unlike the sound mode special point studied by Natsuume (2019), which lies in the upper-half frequency plane and relates to the Lyapunov exponent and butterfly velocity, the special points found here lie in the lower-half plane and are not necessarily connected to hydrodynamic poles. The results enable systematic classification of special points across different perturbation types and background geometries, including asymptotically flat black branes and Lifshitz black holes.
