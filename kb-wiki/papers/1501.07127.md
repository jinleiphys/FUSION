# The Tsallis Distribution at Large Transverse Momenta

> Fits to the transverse momentum distributions of charged particles produced in p - p collisions at LHC energies based on the Tsallis distribution have been shown to work over 14 orders of magnitude. Two versions of the Tsallis distribution are compared and discussed, the thermodynamically consistent version leads to a temperature of T = 74 +/- 13 MeV at the highest beam energy, a result which is in agreement with previous analyses done with lower transverse momentum data.

## Key claim

The Tsallis distribution in a thermodynamically consistent form fits charged particle transverse momentum spectra from proton-proton collisions at LHC energies over 14 orders of magnitude, up to pT values of 200 GeV/c, using the same parameters as those obtained at low pT; the abstract's claim of a temperature of T = 74 +/- 13 MeV at the highest beam energy is a rounded value, as the actual fits yield T = 75.00 +/- 3.21 MeV for ATLAS at 7 TeV and T = 73.00 +/- 1.42 MeV for CMS at 7 TeV.

## Method

The authors fit the charged particle transverse momentum spectra measured by the ATLAS and CMS collaborations at sqrt(s) = 0.9 and 7 TeV using a thermodynamically consistent form of the Tsallis distribution, which satisfies standard thermodynamic relations. The fit function sums three Tsallis distributions for pions, kaons, and protons, with relative weights determined by degeneracy factors, and uses the square root of the sum of squares of statistical and systematic errors. The fit parameters q, T, and a radius parameter R (derived from volume V) are extracted, and the charged particle multiplicity dN/dy at mid-rapidity is calculated from the fitted parameters.

## Key numbers

- For CMS data at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the fit yields chi-squared/NDF = 0.52/24, with pT values up to 200 GeV/c.
- For ATLAS data at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the temperature parameter T = 75.00 +/- 3.21 MeV, with q = 1.150 +/- 0.002 and R = 5.05 +/- 0.07 fm.
- For CMS data at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV, the temperature parameter T = 73.00 +/- 1.42 MeV, with q = 1.153 +/- 0.002 and R = 5.04 +/- 0.27 fm.
- For ATLAS data at sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV, the temperature parameter T = 74.21 +/- 3.55 MeV, with q = 1.129 +/- 0.005 and R = 4.62 +/- 0.29 fm.
- For CMS data at sqrt(s) = 0.9 TeV, the temperature parameter T = 76.00 +/- 0.17 MeV, with q = 1.129 +/- 0.003 and R = 4.32 +/- 0.29 fm.
- The total charged particle multiplicity dN/dy at y=0 from the Tsallis fit for ATLAS at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV is 6.36, compared to the measured dNch/deta at eta=0 of 6.25 +/- 0.30.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses using the Tsallis distribution at lower transverse momenta, as done by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2012) and others, by showing that the same distribution fits data up to 200 GeV/c without needing a separate hard-scattering component. The results confirm the quality of fits reported in earlier studies but obtain different parameter values due to using a thermodynamically consistent version of the Tsallis distribution. The paper enables further investigation of the log-periodic oscillations observed in the ratio of data to fit, which were first noticed by Wilk and Wlodarczyk (2012) for CMS data and are now also seen in ATLAS data.
