# Nonextensive critical effects in the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model

> The critical phenomena in strongly interaction matter are generally investigated using the mean-field model and are characterized by well defined critical exponents. However, such models provide only average properties of the corresponding order parameters and neglect altogether their possible fluctuations. Also the possible long range effect are neglected in the mean field approach. Here we investigate the critical behavior in the nonextensive version of the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model (NJL). It allows to account for such effects in a phenomenological way by means of a single parameter $q$, the nonextensivity parameter. In particular, we show how the nonextensive statistics influence the region of the critical temperature and chemical potential in the NJL mean field approach.

## Key claim

The nonextensive (Tsallis) version of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model, parameterized by a single nonextensivity parameter \(q\), modifies the critical behavior near the critical end point in a way that depends on whether \(q<1\) (correlations) or \(q>1\) (fluctuations), but the model does not differentiate between the particular dynamical mechanisms responsible for departure from the Boltzmann-Gibbs picture.

## Method

The authors construct a \(q\)-NJL model by replacing the standard Fermi-Dirac distributions in the SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio gap equations with \(q\)-deformed distributions \(\tilde{e}_q(x) = [1 + |(q-1)x|]^{x/|(q-1)x|}\), using the Tsallis formalism. They compute the thermodynamic potential, pressure, energy density, and quark condensates self-consistently for different values of \(q\) near the critical temperature and chemical potential, following the bosonization procedure with four-quark interactions only.

## Key numbers

- For \(q = 1\) (standard BG case), the critical compression is \(\rho/\rho_0 = 1.67\) with chemical potential \(\mu = 318.5\) MeV.
- For \(q = 1.01\), the chemical potential at the critical point shifts to \(\mu = 321\) MeV; for \(q = 1.02\), \(\mu = 326.1\) MeV.
- For \(q = 0.99\), the chemical potential shifts to \(\mu = 313\) MeV; for \(q = 0.98\), \(\mu = 307.7\) MeV.
- For \(q < 1\) (\(q = 0.98\)), the critical compression shifts to \(\rho/\rho_0 = 1.72\).
- Preliminary results for the critical exponent \(\epsilon\) describing baryon number susceptibility: \(\epsilon \sim 0.6\) for \(q = 1.02\) and \(\epsilon \sim 0.8\) for \(q = 0.98\), compared to the mean-field value \(\epsilon = 2/3\).

## Context

This work extends the standard mean-field NJL model (as described in CRS) by incorporating nonextensive statistics to phenomenologically account for fluctuations and long-range correlations neglected in the Boltzmann-Gibbs approach. It builds on prior applications of Tsallis statistics to nuclear matter, such as the \(q\)-Walecka model of Pereira, Silva, and Almeida (2009), and the \(q\)-NJL model introduced earlier by the same authors (Rozynek and Wilk, 2009). The results enable fine-tuning of baryon number fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, potentially allowing extraction of the characteristic \(q\) value from experimental charge fluctuation data, and motivate comparison with lattice QCD results from different universality classes.
