# Complete NLO BFKL impact factors for quarkonium hadroproduction in NRQCD: the case of ${}^1S_0^{[1]}$, ${}^1S_0^{[8]}$, and ${}^3S_1^{[8]}$ states

> We present the first complete next-to-leading-order calculation of the impact factors for hadroproduction of the ${}^1S_0^{[1]}$, ${}^1S_0^{[8]}$, and ${}^3S_1^{[8]}$ NRQCD states within the BFKL formalism. We complete the recent virtual-correction computation presented in JHEP 12 (2024) 129 by that of the real-emission contributions. We observe the cancellation of the soft divergences between these real- and virtual-emission contributions and we note that the surviving collinear singularities are compatible with factorisation up to one loop for a novel class of processes where BFKL resummation can be applied. Our work indeed represents the first complete NLO quarkonium impact factor in the BFKL framework and paves the way to first next-to-leading-logarithmic-precision studies for hadroproduction of forward-backward quarkonium associated production at hadron colliders.

## Key claim

The authors present the first complete next-to-leading-order (NLO) calculation of the BFKL impact factors for hadroproduction of three specific NRQCD states (¹S₀[¹], ¹S₀[⁸], and ³S₁[⁸]), completing the virtual-correction computation from JHEP 12 (2024) 129 by adding the real-emission contributions and verifying cancellation of soft divergences, with the caveat that the surviving collinear singularities are compatible with factorisation up to one loop for a novel class of processes where BFKL resummation can be applied.

## Method

The authors computed the NLO corrections to the 2→1 impact factors for the three S-wave NRQCD states within the BFKL formalism, using the Gauge-Invariant Effective Field Theory for Multi-Regge processes in QCD. They added real-emission contributions to the previously computed one-loop virtual corrections, employed a subtractive approach to isolate infrared, collinear, and rapidity divergences, and verified that soft divergences cancel between real and virtual contributions while collinear singularities are absorbed via standard DGLAP factorisation. The final NLO impact factors are expressed as the sum of a subtracted real-emission part and an analytic part containing virtual corrections and distributions.

## Key numbers

- The LO impact factor for ¹S₀[¹,⁸] states is proportional to α_s²(μ_R) × F₀/(8π²) × M²√(N_c²−1)/(M²+p_T²)², with F₀(¹S₀[¹])=64π³/(N_c²(N_c²−1)) and F₀(¹S₀[⁸])=64π³(N_c²−4)/(N_c(N_c²−1)²)
- The LO impact factor for ³S₁[⁸] state is proportional to α_s²(μ_R) × F₀/(8π²) × p_T²√(N_c²−1)/(M²+p_T²)², with F₀(³S₁[⁸])=128π³/(N_c²−1)²
- The finite part of the one-loop virtual correction for ¹S₀[¹] is F^(virt.)_{¹S₀[¹]}(τ) = −(10/9)n_F + C_F×C[gR→¹S₀[¹],C_F] + C_A×C[gR→¹S₀[¹],C_A]
- The finite part of the one-loop virtual correction for ¹S₀[⁸] is F^(virt.)_{¹S₀[⁸]}(τ) = −(10/9)n_F + C_F×C[gR→¹S₀[⁸],C_F] + C_A×C[gR→¹S₀[⁸],C_A]

## Context

This work extends the virtual-correction computation of Nefedov (2024) for quarkonium impact factors in the BFKL framework by adding the real-emission contributions, completing the first full NLO quarkonium impact factor in BFKL. It enables next-to-leading-logarithmic-precision studies for forward-backward quarkonium associated production at hadron colliders, analogous to Mueller-Navelet dijet studies by Colferai (2010), Caporale (2012), and Ducloue (2013-2015), but without the restriction to the fragmentation approximation used in existing NLL cross sections by Celiberto (2022-2025). The results also provide the missing pieces to upgrade existing LL quarkonium BFKL computations for quarkonium+jet by Boussarie (2017) and quarkonium pairs by He (di-Jpsi) to NLL precision.
