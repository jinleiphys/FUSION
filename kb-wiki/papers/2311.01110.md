# Electromagnetic moments of the antimony isotopes $^{112-133}$Sb

> Nuclear moments of the antimony isotopes $^{113-133}$Sb are measured by collinear laser spectroscopy and used to benchmark phenomenological shell-model and \textit{ab initio} calculations in the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG). The shell-model calculations reproduce the electromagnetic moments over all Sb isotopes when suitable effective $g$-factors and charges are employed. Good agreement is achieved by VS-IMSRG for magnetic moments on the neutron-deficient side for both odd-even and odd-odd Sb isotopes while its results deviate from experiment on the neutron-rich side. When the same effective $g$-factors are used, VS-IMSRG agrees with experiment nearly as well as the shell model. Hence, the wave functions are very similar in both approaches and missing contributions to the M1 operator are identified as the cause of the discrepancy of VS-IMSRG with experiment. Electric quadrupole moments remain more challenging for VS-IMSRG.

## Key claim

The valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) reproduces magnetic moments of neutron-deficient antimony isotopes well but deviates on the neutron-rich side; when the same effective g-factors used in the shell model are artificially employed in VS-IMSRG, the agreement becomes nearly as good as the shell model, indicating that the nuclear wave functions are very similar in both approaches and that missing contributions to the M1 operator, likely meson-exchange currents, are the cause of the discrepancy.

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at ISOLDE-CERN on the atomic transition \(5s^25p^3\ ^4\text{S}_{3/2} \rightarrow 5s^25p^26s\ ^4\text{P}_{3/2}\) at 217 nm to measure hyperfine spectra of \(^{113-133}\)Sb isotopes, including long-lived isomeric states. Hyperfine parameters \(A\) and \(B\) were extracted by fitting the spectra with the SATLAS package, and magnetic dipole moments and electric quadrupole moments were derived using \(^{123}\)Sb as a reference isotope, with the differential hyperfine anomaly calculated from the Breit-Rosenthal and Bohr-Weisskopf effects. The experimental results were compared to large-scale shell-model calculations using the SNBG3 and \(V_{MU}\) interactions and to ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations based on the 1.8/2.0 (EM) nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interaction.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic moment of \(^{113}\)Sb (\(5/2^+\)): \(3.402(7)\ \mu_N\)
- Magnetic moment of \(^{133}\)Sb (\(7/2^+\)): \(3.077(4)\ \mu_N\)
- Magnetic moment of \(^{126}\)Sb (\(8^-\)): \(1.121(3)\ \mu_N\)
- Electric quadrupole moment of \(^{113}\)Sb (\(5/2^+\)): \(-0.568(12)\ \text{b}\)
- Electric quadrupole moment of \(^{133}\)Sb (\(7/2^+\)): \(-0.304(7)\ \text{b}\)
- Electric quadrupole moment of \(^{130}\)Sb (\(8^-\)): \(-0.033(2)\ \text{b}\)
- The remaining difference between VS-IMSRG calculations and experiment for magnetic moments on the neutron-rich side is approximately \(0.5\ \mu_N\), consistent with the meson-exchange current contribution of \(0.52\ \mu_N\) calculated by Stone et al. (1997) for \(^{133}\)Sb
- Effective g-factors used in the shell model: \(g_l^\pi = 1.11\), \(g_l^\nu = -0.02\), \(g_{s,\text{eff}} = 0.6 g_s\)
- Effective charges used in the shell model: \(e_\pi = 1.6\), \(e_\nu = +1.05\)

## Context

This work extends collinear laser spectroscopy measurements of antimony isotopes beyond the earlier study by Lechner (2021) and provides the first comprehensive comparison of electromagnetic moments across the full \(^{112-133}\)Sb isotopic chain between experiment, phenomenological shell-model calculations (using interactions from Utsuno (2012, 2014) and Honma (2008)), and ab initio VS-IMSRG calculations. The finding that the wave functions from both theoretical approaches are very similar, with the discrepancy arising from the M1 operator rather than the nuclear interaction, motivates future inclusion of meson-exchange currents in ab initio calculations for heavier systems, which is work in progress.
