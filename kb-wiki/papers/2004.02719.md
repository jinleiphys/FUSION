# Fluctuation dynamics near the QCD critical point

> The evolution of non-hydrodynamic slow processes near the QCD critical point is explored with the novel Hydro+ framework, which extends the conventional hydrodynamic description by coupling it to additional explicitly evolving slow modes describing long wavelength fluctuations. Their slow relaxation is controlled by critical behavior of the correlation length and is independent from gradients of matter density and pressure that control the evolution of the hydrodynamic quantities. In this exploratory study we follow the evolution of the slow modes on top of a simplified QCD matter background, allowing us to clearly distinguish, and study both separately and in combination, the main effects controlling the dynamics of critical slow modes. In particular, we show how the evolution of the slow modes depend on their wave number, the expansion of and advection by the fluid background, and the behavior of the correlation length. Non-equilibrium contributions from the slow modes to bulk matter properties that affect the bulk dynamics (entropy, pressure, temperature and chemical potential) are discussed and found to be small.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the non-equilibrium evolution of critical slow modes near the QCD critical point can be studied analytically using a simplified Gubser flow background, but this approach cannot account for the back-reaction of fluctuations on the bulk flow because doing so would destroy the symmetry enabling the analytic treatment; the authors expect these back-reaction effects to be very small (never larger than ~10^{-4} times the background hydrodynamic fields) based on their findings and those of Rajagopal (2019).

## Method

The authors employ the Hydro+ framework, which extends conventional hydrodynamics by coupling it to additional explicitly evolving slow modes describing long-wavelength fluctuations of the entropy per baryon at fixed pressure, δ(s/n). They replace a full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulation with an analytically solvable model using ideal Gubser flow as the background for the non-equilibrium fluctuation dynamics, allowing them to isolate and study separately the effects of expansion, advection, and critical correlations. The slow-mode evolution follows relaxation equations where the relaxation rate Γ_Q depends on the wave number Q and the correlation length ξ, with the equilibrium value φ̄_Q given by an Ornstein-Zernike form φ̄_Q = (c_p/n²)(ξ/ξ₀)² / (1 + (Qξ)²).

## Key numbers

- The correlation length ξ diverges near the critical point, with the heat capacity at constant pressure c_p ∝ ξ², while the thermal conductivity λ_T ∝ ξ, giving a relaxation rate Γ_ξ ∝ ξ^{-4} for modes with Q ∼ ξ^{-1} (critical exponent z = 4 in model B).
- The back-reaction effects of non-equilibrium fluctuations on bulk hydrodynamic fields are found to be never larger than ∼10^{-4} times the background hydrodynamic fields.
- The QCD critical point, if it exists, is likely located at a baryon chemical potential ≳ 400 MeV, based on prior work by Bazavov (2017), Mukherjee (2019), and Giordano (2019).
- The relaxation rate in the hydrodynamic regime is Γ_Q = 2(λ_T/c_p) Q², with the factor 2 arising from the relaxation of a two-point function.

## Context

This work extends the Hydro+ framework introduced by Stephanov (2017) and follows the first application by Rajagopal (2019), which studied a simpler limit with the critical point near zero baryon chemical potential. Unlike Rajagopal (2019), this paper considers the more generic situation of a critical point at large baryon chemical potential, requiring simultaneous evolution of net baryon density and baryon diffusion current. The authors use model B for the dynamical critical exponent (following Akamatsu (2018)) rather than model A used by Rajagopal (2019) or model H used by Stephanov (2017), and they note that a full (3+1)-dimensional study with back-reaction using their BES+ code is left for future work.
