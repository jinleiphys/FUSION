# Two-Body Random Ensembles: From Nuclear Spectra to Random Polynomials

## Key claim

The two-body random ensemble (TBRE) for a many-body bosonic theory can be mapped exactly to a problem of random polynomials on the unit interval, providing analytic expressions for ground-state properties, energy gaps, and level densities. The predominance of 0+ ground states arises not from the width of level densities or Hilbert-space dimensionality (as previously argued for other TBREs), but from the tendency of interactions to place extreme spin states at the ends of the spectra.

## Method

The author maps the U(4) Vibron model (a bosonic theory with 0+ and 1- bosons used for diatomic molecules) onto a tridiagonal Lanczos representation, where the Hamiltonian matrix elements become functions of the continuous variables z = n_p/N and j = J/N in the large-N limit. The random Gaussian coefficients of the Hamiltonian are grouped into a vector x = (c_0, c_2, u_0, u_1, v_0, epsilon_s, epsilon_p), and the lowest eigenvalue for each spin J is expressed as the infimum of a quadratic random polynomial f_j(z) = a z^2 + b z + d_j over z in [j,1]. The distribution of the polynomial coefficients is computed analytically from the Gaussian measure on the Hamiltonian parameters.

## Key numbers

- Frequency of 0+ ground states: approximately 70% for even N (70.5%, 71.7%, 72.3%, 72.5% for N=8, 16, 32, 64 respectively); analytic estimate gives roughly 69.5%
- For the case with only off-diagonal interactions (beta=0), the ratio E_calc^j / E_min^j for J=0,1,2 is 1.06, 1.05, 1.03 for N=20 and 1.01, 1.01, 1.01 for N=80
- Width of the 0+ density of states: <E^2>_{J=0} = 0.784
- Width of the maximum-spin density of states: <E^2>_{J=N} = 1.137
- Numerical constants for the lowest eigenvalue distribution at N=64: R^2 = 1.07, A_1 = 0.663, A_2 = 0.632, A_3 = 0.423
- The distribution of minima P(z_0) yields 22% of minima in the interior (0,1), 39% at z=0, and 39% at z=1 (split between J=0 and J=N)

## Context

This work extends the study of two-body random ensembles for bosonic systems, following numerical studies of the U(6) TBRE by Bijker and Frank (2000) and earlier work on fermionic TBREs by Johnson, Bertsch, Dean, and Talmi (1999). The author provides the first analytic understanding of ground-state properties in TBREs, contradicting earlier conjectures that Hilbert-space dimensionality or level-density widths determine the 0+ ground-state predominance. The results enable analytic predictions for distributions of lowest eigenvalues, energy gaps, and level densities that can be compared directly to nuclear spectroscopic properties obtained in the interacting boson model (IBM), and suggest that the Lanczos approach could generalize the connection between TBREs and random polynomials to other bosonic or fermionic theories.
