# Symmetry energy in neutron star matter

> We investigate the structure attained by neutron star matter with proton to neutron ratios ranging from x=0.1 to 0.5, densities in the range of 0.02 fm-3 to 0.085 fm-3, and temperatures T<4 MeV. In particular we study the pasta shapes and the phase changes previously observed in nuclear matter, as well as the behavior of the symmetry energy. We corroborate the existence of homogeneous to non-homogeneous phase transitions, and the different values of the symmetry energy at different densities and temperatures.

# Symmetry energy in neutron star matter

## Key claim

The authors use classical molecular dynamics simulations to show that neutron star matter exhibits pasta structures and phase transitions similar to those previously observed in nuclear matter, but the introduction of the screened Coulomb potential from the electron gas smooths out these transitions and eliminates the saturation density, making the system unbound for most densities and temperatures studied.

## Method

The authors performed classical molecular dynamics (CMD) simulations using the New Medium nucleon-nucleon potentials (an improved version of the Pandharipande Medium potentials) with a Thomas-Fermi screened Coulomb potential to mimic the electron gas. Systems of 4000 nucleons were simulated in a cubic cell with periodic boundary conditions at proton fractions x = 0.1 to 0.5, densities from 0.02 fm^-3 to 0.085 fm^-3, and temperatures from 0.2 MeV to 4 MeV, using a Nose-Hoover thermostat. The resulting structures were characterized using Minkowski functionals (volume, surface area, Euler characteristic, and integral mean curvature) and radial distribution functions, while the symmetry energy was extracted by fitting the internal energy as a function of density and isospin asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Systems of A = 4000 nucleons were simulated in a cubic cell under periodic boundary conditions
- Densities were varied between 0.02 fm^-3 and 0.085 fm^-3
- Temperatures ranged from T = 4 MeV down to 0.2 MeV with Delta T < 0.1%
- Proton fractions studied: x = 0.1, 0.2, 0.3, 0.4, and 0.5
- The screening length for the Thomas-Fermi Coulomb potential was lambda = 20 fm
- Cubic voxels of d = 2.35 fm were used for Minkowski functional calculations
- The caloric curve shows a change in slope at T approximately 1.5 MeV and a sharper change at T approximately 0.5 MeV
- For symmetric neutron star matter (x = 0.5), the system is bound only for T less than or equal to 1.0 MeV and rho less than or equal to 0.05 fm^-3
- At rho = 0.04 fm^-3, the energy minimum shifts from x = 0.4 at T = 0.2 MeV to x = 0.3 at T = 2.0 MeV
- At rho = 0.085 fm^-3, the energy minimum remains at x = 0.3 at all temperatures studied

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of nuclear matter (Dorso 2018) to neutron star matter by including the screened Coulomb potential from the electron gas. The study corroborates the existence of homogeneous to non-homogeneous phase transitions and pasta structures previously observed in nuclear matter by Dorso (2014), but finds that the Coulomb potential significantly modifies the energy-density relationship and eliminates the saturation density. The results are relevant for understanding neutrino cooling in neutron stars and heavy element synthesis in neutron star mergers, as the symmetry energy and pasta structures affect the equation of state and neutrino opacity.
