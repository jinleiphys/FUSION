# Bayesian evidence for two peaks in the sound speed in cold dense QCD

> I show that in addition to the well-known peak inside massive neutron stars, the sound speed in cold dense QCD matter likely exhibits another peak above neutron star densities before it asymptotes to $c_s=\sqrt{C_s}=\sqrt{1/3}$. Based on the framework reported in arxiv:2408.16738, this approach does not rely on any assumption about the ultra-dense matter not realized in nature. Current multimessenger observation of neutron stars favors the two-peak scenario with a Bayes factor $5.1_{-0.7}^{+0.9}$, where the uncertainties are systematics due to models of neutron star inner cores. This evidence grows to $27_{-8}^{+11}$ if the $2.6M_\odot$ component of GW190814 is a neutron star. The trough in $C_s$ separating the two peaks is inferred to be below $0.05^{+0.04}_{-0.02}$ (at the $50\%$ level) if $2.6M_\odot$ neutron stars exist. The second peak above $1/3$ beyond baryon chemical potential $μ_B=1.6-1.9$ GeV most likely signals non-perturbative effects in cold quark matter, for instance color superconductivity.

## Key claim

The paper claims that cold dense QCD matter likely exhibits two peaks in the speed of sound squared \(C_s\), not just the well-known peak inside neutron stars, with the second peak occurring above neutron star densities before the asymptotic conformal value \(C_s = 1/3\) is reached. The claim is hedged by the condition that the evidence depends on the pQCD renormalization scale and on whether the \(2.6 M_\odot\) component of GW190814 is a neutron star, with Bayes factors ranging from marginal to very strong depending on these assumptions.

## Method

The author derives sufficient conditions for two peaks in \(C_s\) based on thermodynamic concordance between the neutron star equation of state (EOS) at TOV densities and perturbative QCD at high densities, using the mean value bound and maximally soft EOS constructions from Zhou (2024). Bayesian analyses are performed with agnostic parameterizations of neutron star inner cores above \(2n_0\), using chiral effective field theory (Weinberg 1968, Kaplan 1996) up to \(2n_0\) as prior knowledge, and imposing constraints from pulsar mass measurements, NICER radius observations, and pQCD calculations at \(\mu_B = 2.4\) GeV. The pQCD renormalization scale \(X = \bar{\Lambda}/(\mu_B/3)\) is marginalized over uniform and Gaussian priors.

## Key numbers

- Bayes factor for the two-peak scenario using current multimessenger data: \(5.1_{-0.7}^{+0.9}\) with pQCD renormalization scale \(X \sim \mathcal{N}(2.5, 0.5)\)
- Bayes factor grows to \(27_{-8}^{+11}\) if the \(2.6 M_\odot\) component of GW190814 is a neutron star (same \(X\) prior)
- The trough in \(C_s\) separating the two peaks is inferred to be below \(0.05^{+0.04}_{-0.02}\) at the \(50\%\) level if \(2.6 M_\odot\) neutron stars exist
- The second peak occurs beyond baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B = 1.6-1.9\) GeV
- Onset density of the second peak when \(M_\text{TOV} \gtrsim 2.6 M_\odot\): \(n_B \in [4.5_{-0.3}^{+0.5}, 24^{+3}_{-6}] n_0\) or \(\mu_B \in [1.8^{+0.1}_{-0.2}, 1.9^{+0.2}_{-0.1}]\) GeV
- Onset density when \(M_\text{TOV} \lesssim 2.2 M_\odot\): \(n_B \in [5.8_{-0.9}^{+1.0}, 26^{+4}_{-9}] n_0\) or \(\mu_B \in [1.7^{+0.2}_{-0.2}, 2.0^{+0.3}_{-0.3}]\) GeV

## Context

This work extends the framework of Zhou (2024) for thermodynamic concordance between neutron star and perturbative QCD densities, and builds on earlier demonstrations that two-solar-mass pulsars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) require a peak in \(C_s\) above \(1/3\) (Bedaque 2014, Tews 2018, Drischler 2020). The results contradict the simplest picture of a single peak followed by a monotonic approach to conformality, and instead favor non-perturbative effects in cold quark matter such as color superconductivity (Alford 1997, Rajagopal 2000). The analysis enables future inference of the magnitude and location of sound speed extrema in dense QCD matter.
