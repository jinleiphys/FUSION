# Neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements in light nuclei

> We present the first ab initio calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements in $A=6$-$12$ nuclei using Variational Monte Carlo wave functions obtained from the Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon potential and Illinois-7 three-nucleon interaction. We study both light Majorana neutrino exchange and potentials arising from a large class of multi-TeV mechanisms of lepton number violation. Our results provide benchmarks to be used in testing many-body methods that can be extended to the heavy nuclei of experimental interest. In light nuclei we have also studied the impact of two-body short range correlations and the use of different forms for the transition operators, such as those corresponding to different orders in chiral effective theory.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first ab initio calculations of neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements in light nuclei (A=6-12), using Variational Monte Carlo wave functions, but the authors explicitly note that these transitions are not directly relevant from an experimental point of view; instead, the results serve as benchmarks for testing many-body methods that can be extended to heavier nuclei of experimental interest.

## Method

The authors used Variational Monte Carlo (VMC) wave functions constructed from the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and Illinois-7 three-nucleon interaction to compute neutrinoless double beta decay matrix elements for transitions in A=6-12 nuclei. They studied both light Majorana neutrino exchange and short-range potentials arising from dimension-seven and dimension-nine operators of lepton number violation, and examined the impact of two-body short range correlations and different forms for the transition operators corresponding to different orders in chiral effective theory.

## Key numbers

- Axial coupling constant used: g_A = 1.2723(23) (from Olive 2016)
- Vector and axial dipole masses: Lambda_V = 850 MeV, Lambda_A = 1040 MeV
- Anomalous nucleon isovector magnetic moment: kappa_1 = 3.7
- Nuclear radius definition: R_A = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm
- Low-energy constants at mu = 3 GeV in MSbar scheme: g_{27x1} = 0.37 +/- 0.08, g_{8x8} = -(3.1 +/- 1.3) GeV^2, g^{mix}_{8x8} = -(13 +/- 4) GeV^2, g_{6x6bar} = (3.2 +/- 0.7) GeV^2, g^{mix}_{6x6bar} = -(1.1 +/- 0.3) GeV^2
- Current experimental limits: T_{1/2} > 5.3 x 10^25 y for Ge-76 (Agostini 2017) and T_{1/2} > 1.07 x 10^26 y for Xe-136 (KamLAND-Zen 2016)
- GFMC propagation improves VMC results by less than 3% for A <= 10 nuclei (Pastore 2012, Pastore 2017)
- Variation of matrix elements with regulator function F(r) for R_L in {0.6, 0.8} fm: less than 17% for A=8-12 decays

## Context

This work extends the ab initio Variational Monte Carlo and Green's Function Monte Carlo methods previously applied to single beta decays in A=6-10 nuclei (Pastore 2017) to the case of neutrinoless double beta decay. The calculations provide benchmarks for testing many-body methods that can be extended to heavy nuclei of experimental interest such as Ge-76 and Xe-136, where current many-body approaches lead to estimates differing by a factor of two to three (Engel 2016). The results also enable systematic study of the impact of two-body correlations and different operator forms, and can be compared with results for A=48-136 nuclei (Hyvarinen 2015) that also use the measured value of g_A.
