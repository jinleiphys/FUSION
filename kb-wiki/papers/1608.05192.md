# Maximal isospin few-body systems of nucleons and $Ξ$ hyperons

> By using local central Yukawa-type interactions that reproduce the low-energy parameters of the latest updates of the Nijmegen ESC08c potentials we show that the $NΞ$, $NNΞ$, $NΞΞ$ and $NNΞΞ$ systems with maximal isospin are bound. Since in these states the strong decay $NΞ\toΛΛ$ is forbidden by isospin conservation, these strange few-body systems will be stable under the strong interaction. These results may suggest that other states with different number of $N$'s and $Ξ$'s in the maximal isospin channel could also be bound.

# Maximal isospin few-body systems of nucleons and Ξ hyperons

## Key claim
Using local central Yukawa-type interactions that reproduce the low-energy parameters of the latest Nijmegen ESC08c potentials, the authors show that the NΞ, NNΞ, NΞΞ, and NNΞΞ systems with maximal isospin are bound. However, the parametrization does not explicitly include coupling to higher mass channels (ΛΣ and ΣΣ), so the authors acknowledge they may lose some binding and do not expect their results to overestimate the binding energies.

## Method
The authors constructed local central Yukawa-type potentials (attractive plus repulsive terms) for the NN, NΞ, and ΞΞ S-wave interactions, fitting the low-energy scattering parameters (scattering length and effective range) from the latest updates of the Nijmegen ESC08c potentials for strangeness -2 and -4 sectors. They solved the Faddeev equations for the three-body systems NNΞ and NΞΞ using a formalism for three fermions with two identical particles, and used a variational method with generalized Gaussians for the four-body NNΞΞ system.

## Key numbers
- NΞ (maximal isospin, (I)J^P = (1)1^+): binding energy 1.67 MeV
- NNΞ (maximal isospin, (I)J^P = (3/2)1/2^+): binding energy 3.00 MeV
- NΞΞ (maximal isospin, (I)J^P = (3/2)1/2^+): binding energy 4.52 MeV
- NNΞΞ (maximal isospin, (I)J^P = (2)0^+): binding energy 7.4 MeV
- The NΞ ^3S_1 interaction has a bound state (the D*) with binding energy 1.67 MeV; the NΞ ^1S_0 interaction is mainly repulsive
- The ΞΞ ^1S_0 scattering length is -7.25 fm with effective range 2.00 fm (attractive but unbound)
- The NΞ ^3S_1 scattering length is 4.911 fm with effective range 0.527 fm
- The NΞ ^1S_0 scattering length is 0.579 fm with effective range -2.521 fm

## Context
This work extends studies of Ξ hypernuclei, motivated by the KISO event (Nakazawa 2015) which measured a Ξ^- binding energy of 4.38 ± 0.25 MeV in ^14N, and by recent updates of the Nijmegen ESC08c potentials (Nagels 2015, Rijken 2016) and lattice QCD results (Sasaki 2015) suggesting attractive ΞN interactions. The authors note that other models, such as the chiral constituent quark model (Carames 2012), also predict bound ΞN states, while some earlier Nijmegen models and other approaches (Fujiwara 2007, Haidenbauer 2016) do not. The results suggest that larger maximal-isospin systems with more nucleons and Ξ hyperons could also be bound, complementing existing Ξ hypernuclei studies (Yamamoto 2010, Hiyama 2008).
