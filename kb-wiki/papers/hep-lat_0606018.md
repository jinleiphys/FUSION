# Robustness of baryon-strangeness correlation and related ratios of susceptibilities

> Using quenched lattice QCD simulations we investigate the continuum limit of baryon-strangeness correlation and other related conserved charge-flavour correlations for temperatures T_c<T\le2T_c. By working with lattices having large temporal extents (N_τ=12, 10, 8, 4) we find that these quantities are almost independent of the lattice spacing, i.e, robust. We also find that these quantities have very mild dependence on the sea quark mass and acquire values which are very close to their respective ideal gas limits. Our results also confirm robustness of the Wroblewski parameter.

# Robustness of baryon-strangeness correlation and related ratios of susceptibilities

## Key claim
Using quenched lattice QCD simulations with large temporal extents (Nτ = 12, 10, 8, 4), the ratios of off-diagonal to diagonal quark number susceptibilities, including the baryon-strangeness correlation CBS and the Wroblewski parameter λs, are found to be almost independent of lattice spacing (within ~5%) for temperatures Tc < T ≤ 2Tc, and acquire values very close to their respective ideal gas limits, supporting a quasi-particle picture of the quark-gluon plasma rather than a bound-state model.

## Method
The author performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using the standard Wilson gauge action and unimproved staggered fermions on lattices with temporal extents Nτ = 4, 8, 10, and 12, for temperatures 1.1Tc ≤ T ≤ 2Tc at zero chemical potential. Flavor-diagonal and flavor-off-diagonal quark number susceptibilities were computed using stochastic estimators for the traces, with light valence quark mass mq/Tc = 0.1 for u and d flavors and mq/Tc = 1 for the s flavor. Continuum extrapolations were performed by fitting the diagonal susceptibilities linearly in 1/Nτ2 and the off-diagonal susceptibilities to a constant, given their negligible lattice-spacing dependence.

## Key numbers
- Continuum-extrapolated diagonal susceptibility χu/T2 = 0.79(1) at 1.1Tc, rising to 0.86(2) at 2.0Tc
- Continuum-extrapolated diagonal susceptibility χs/T2 = 0.33(1) at 1.1Tc, rising to 0.70(2) at 2.0Tc
- Off-diagonal susceptibility χud/T2 = -4(4) × 10−3 at 1.1Tc, consistent with zero within errors at all temperatures
- Off-diagonal susceptibility χus/T2 = -6(4) × 10−3 at 1.1Tc, consistent with zero within errors at all temperatures
- The Wroblewski parameter λs = χs/χu remains constant within ~5% across all lattice spacings for all temperatures in 1 < T/Tc ≤ 2
- The ratios CBS, CQS, CBU, and CQU are independent of lattice spacing within ~5% errors and are compatible with their respective ideal gas values (CBS = CQS = 1, CBU = 1, CQU = 2) for the entire temperature range

## Context
This work extends the lattice QCD studies of Gavai (2005), which used dynamical simulations on small Nτ = 4 lattices, by performing a systematic continuum extrapolation using quenched simulations with larger temporal extents. The results confirm that the ratios CBS, CQS, and the Wroblewski parameter have very mild dependence on both lattice spacing and sea quark mass, supporting the quasi-particle picture of QGP over the bound-state model of Shuryak and Zahed (2004). The findings contradict predictions from that model, which gave CBS ≈ 0.62 at 1.5Tc and much smaller values near Tc, while the lattice results remain close to the ideal gas value of 1. This enables reliable comparison with experimental data from RHIC, where λs ≈ 0.43 extracted from hadron gas fireball models is consistent with the lattice results near Tc.
