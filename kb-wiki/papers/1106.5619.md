# On kinematical constraints in fermion-antifermion systems

> We consider the scattering of fermions off antifermions with spin 1/2 and 3/2. Starting from helicity partial-wave scattering amplitudes we derive transformations that eliminate all kinematical constraints. Such amplitudes are expected to satisfy partial-wave dispersion relations and therefore provide a suitable basis for data analysis and the construction of effective field theories. Our derivation relies on a decomposition of the various scattering amplitudes into suitable sets of invariant functions.

## Key claim

The paper derives transformations that eliminate all kinematical constraints from helicity partial-wave scattering amplitudes for fermion-antifermion systems with spin 1/2 and 3/2, under the condition that parity is conserved and the derivation relies on a decomposition of scattering amplitudes into suitable sets of invariant functions that are free of kinematical singularities.

## Method

The authors decompose on-shell scattering amplitudes for all two-body reactions involving spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 fermions and antifermions into bases of invariant functions, using helicity partial-wave amplitudes in the Jacob and Wick formalism. They then apply nonunitary transformation matrices that map the initial and final helicity states to new covariant states, eliminating kinematical constraints at thresholds and pseudo-thresholds. The mapping procedure uses exclusively on-shell matrix elements, avoiding any model-dependent off-shell dependence.

## Key numbers

- The number of independent helicity amplitudes for a given reaction is given by (1/2) x (2 S1 + 1) x (2 S2 + 1) x (2 S1_bar + 1) x (2 S2_bar + 1), where S1, S2, S1_bar, S2_bar are the spins of initial and final particles.
- The spin-1/2 to spin-1/2 scattering amplitude is decomposed into 8 scalar functions F_i^+ and F_i^- (i = 1 to 4).
- Reactions involving a single spin-3/2 particle require 16 scalar functions F_i^+ and F_i^- (i = 1 to 8).
- Reactions involving two spin-3/2 particles require 32 scalar functions F_i^+ and F_i^- (i = 1 to 16).
- The scattering of two spin-3/2 particles requires 64 scalar functions F_i^+ and F_i^- (i = 1 to 64).

## Context

This work extends the technique previously applied to two-body scattering systems with photons, pions, and nucleons (Chew 1957, Ball 1961, Barut 1963, Hara 1964, Cohen-Tannoudji 1968) and to pseudoscalar-vector scattering (Lutz 2003). It provides kinematically unconstrained amplitudes for systems involving spin-1/2 and spin-3/2 fermions, which had not been established before. The results are expected to be relevant for partial-wave analysis and effective field theory approaches, particularly for the PANDA experiment at FAIR where protons and antiprotons may be annihilated into systems of spin-3/2 states.
