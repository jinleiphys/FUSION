# Dilepton Production at Fermilab and RHIC

> Some recent results from several fixed-target dimuon production experiments at Fermilab are presented. In particular, we discuss the use of Drell-Yan data to determine the flavor structure of the nucleon sea, as well as to deduce the energy-loss of partons traversing nuclear medium. Future dilepton experiments at RHIC could shed more light on the flavor asymmetry and possible charge-symmetry-violation of the nucleon sea. Clear evidence for scaling violation in the Drell-Yan process could also be revealed at RHIC.

# Dilepton Production at Fermilab and RHIC

## Key claim

Fixed-target dimuon experiments at Fermilab have used Drell-Yan data to determine the flavor structure of the nucleon sea and to deduce parton energy loss in nuclear matter, but clear evidence for scaling violation in the Drell-Yan process has not yet been established; future dilepton experiments at RHIC could provide unambiguous confirmation of scaling violation and disentangle flavor asymmetry from possible charge-symmetry-violation effects.

## Method

The authors present results from a series of fixed-target dimuon production experiments at Fermilab (E772, E789, E866) using 800 GeV proton beams on hydrogen, deuterium, and nuclear targets, measuring Drell-Yan dimuon yields and heavy quarkonium (J/ψ, ψ′, Υ) production cross sections. The nuclear dependence is parameterized as A^α, and the ratio of Drell-Yan cross sections from hydrogen and deuterium targets is used to extract the x-dependence of the \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) ratio. The authors also discuss prospects for W boson production asymmetry measurements at RHIC as a charge-symmetry-independent probe of the sea quark asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- Gottfried integral measured by NMC: \(I_G = 0.235 \pm 0.026\), significantly below the Gottfried Sum Rule value of 1/3
- E866 data show \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) increases linearly with \(x\) for \(x < 0.15\), then decreases rapidly toward unity beyond \(x_2 = 0.2\)
- NA51 result for \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) shown as an open box in Figure 2 (specific numerical value not given in text)
- E772 found nuclear dependence for J/ψ and ψ′ approximately as \(A^{0.92}\), and for Υ states approximately as \(A^{0.96}\)
- Mean total energy loss for p+W Drell-Yan process deduced from Eq. (2) and Figure 7: \(\Delta E \approx 0.6\) GeV
- E772 data consistent with \(\Delta E = 2.0 \pm 1.7\) GeV for p+W from the x_F dependence analysis
- p_t broadening for J/ψ is larger than for Drell-Yan by approximately a factor of 5
- Drell-Yan cross section at \(\sqrt{s} = 500\) GeV predicted to drop by a factor of two compared to \(\sqrt{s} = 38.9\) GeV due to scaling violation

## Context

This work reviews results from the Fermilab fixed-target program (E772, E789, E866) that extended earlier measurements by NA3 (Badier et al., 1984), E605 (Moreno et al., 1991), and NA51 (Baldit et al., 1994). The E866 data on \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\) asymmetry directly impacted the global fits of Martin et al. (1998) (MRST parton distributions). The analysis of nuclear effects builds on the Cronin effect (Cronin et al., 1975) and tests the energy-loss formalism of Baier et al. (1997). The proposed W boson asymmetry measurements at RHIC would provide a charge-symmetry-independent determination of \(\bar{d}/\bar{u}\), complementing the Drell-Yan approach that requires the assumption of charge symmetry.
