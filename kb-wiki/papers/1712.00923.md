# Temperature dependence of transport coefficients of QCD in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

> Using our developed new relativistic viscous hydrodynamics code, we investigate the temperature dependence of shear and bulk viscosities from comparison with the ALICE data: single particle spectra and collective flows of Pb+Pb $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. We find that from the comprehensive analyses of centrality dependence of single particle spectra and collective flows we can extract detailed information on the quark-gluon plasma bulk property, without the information being smeared by the final state interactions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that comprehensive analyses of centrality dependence of single particle spectra and collective flows can extract detailed information on the quark-gluon plasma bulk property, without the information being smeared by final state interactions, but this conclusion is conditional on using their specific relativistic viscous hydrodynamics code with the TRENTo initial condition and a particular parametrization of temperature-dependent transport coefficients.

## Method

The authors developed a new relativistic viscous hydrodynamics code in Milne coordinates using a Riemann solver with the two shock approximation and the piecewise exact solution method. They used the TRENTo initial condition model with parameters fixed by pseudorapidity distributions of charged hadrons at central collisions, a realistic parametrized equation of state based on continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results combined with a hadron resonance gas model, and the UrQMD model for final state interactions after hydrodynamic expansion terminates at switching temperature T_SW = 150 MeV. They compared their calculations with ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, testing constant shear viscosity values, finite bulk viscosity parametrized as zeta/s = b * (eta/s) * (1/3 - c_s^2)^2, and temperature-dependent shear viscosity parametrized with parameters c_1 and c_2 around T_c = 167 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature T_c = 167 MeV used as the reference temperature in the shear viscosity parametrization
- Switching temperature T_SW = 150 MeV for transition from hydrodynamic expansion to UrQMD model
- Initial time tau_0 = 0.6 fm for the hydrodynamic evolution
- Spatial grid sizes Delta x = Delta y = 0.2 fm, Delta eta_s = 0.3, and time step Delta tau = 0.5 * Delta x fm
- 2000 minimum bias events prepared using TRENTo for the analysis
- Parameter sets tested: (d) eta/s = 0.17, b = 40; (f) (eta/s)_min = 0.08, c_1 = 10 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 0.7 GeV^{-1}, b = 40; (g) (eta/s)_min = 0.08, c_1 = 0, c_2 = 0.7 GeV^{-1}, b = 40; (h) (eta/s)_min = 0.08, c_1 = 10 GeV^{-1}, c_2 = 0, b = 40
- Normalization N values ranged from 88 to 116 and sigma_0 values were 2.7 or 2.9 depending on the parameter set

## Context

This work extends phenomenological analyses of transport coefficients in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on prior studies of temperature-dependent shear viscosity by Niemi (2011), Molnar (2014), and Niemi (2015), and on investigations of bulk viscosity effects by Bozek (2009), Noronha-Hostler (2013), and Rose (2014). The authors use the TRENTo initial condition model developed by Moreland (2014) and Ke (2016), and their code is based on a Riemann solver approach previously applied by Takamoto (2011) and Okamoto (2016). The results enable more precise constraints on the temperature dependence of both shear and bulk viscosities of QCD matter through comparison with LHC data, and the finding that bulk viscosity enhances the profile function around the critical temperature may affect calculations of photon and lepton-pair production in the medium.
