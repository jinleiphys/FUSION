# The complex heavy-quark potential with the Gribov-Zwanziger action

> Gribov-Zwanziger prescription in Yang-Mills theory improves the infrared dynamics. In this work, we study the static potential of a heavy quark-antiquark pair with the HTL resummed perturbation method within the Gribov-Zwanziger approach at finite temperature. The real and imaginary parts of the heavy quark complex potential are obtained from the one-loop effective static gluon propagator. The one-loop effective gluon propagator is obtained by calculating the one-loop gluon self-energies containing the quark, gluon, and ghost loop. The gluon and ghost loops are modified in the presence of the Gribov parameter. We also calculate the decay width from the imaginary part of the potential. We also discuss the medium effect of heavy quark potential with the localized action via auxiliary fields.

## Key claim

The Gribov-Zwanziger (GZ) action, which improves the infrared behavior of Yang-Mills theory, leads to a larger Debye mass and a larger imaginary part of the heavy quark-antiquark potential compared to standard Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) perturbation theory, implying stronger screening and a larger thermal decay width for quarkonium states at finite temperature.

## Method

The authors compute the one-loop gluon self-energy in the Landau gauge at finite temperature, including contributions from the gluon loop, ghost loop, and quark loop, where the gluon and ghost loops are modified by the Gribov parameter. The real and imaginary parts of the static heavy quark-antiquark potential are obtained from the Fourier transform of the effective static gluon propagator, which is constructed from the Dyson-Schwinger equation using the Gribov-modified tree-level propagator and the one-loop self-energy. The decay width for quarkonium states is then calculated by integrating the imaginary part of the potential weighted by the Coulombic ground-state wave function.

## Key numbers

- The Debye mass in the Gribov plasma is larger than the perturbative HTL value at a given temperature; the perturbative Debye mass squared is given by \((m_D^{T})^2 = (N_c + N_f/2) g^2 T^2 / 3\).
- The real part of the potential at \(T = 0.2\) GeV is less negative for the Gribov case than for the HTL case at fixed distance \(r\), indicating stronger screening.
- The magnitude of the imaginary part of the potential at \(T = 0.2\) GeV is larger for the Gribov case than for the perturbative case.
- The decay width for \(J/\psi\) (charmonium, \(m_c = 1.275\) GeV) and \(\Upsilon\) (bottomonium, \(m_b = 4.66\) GeV) is larger in the Gribov plasma than in the usual HTL case; the thermal width for \(\Upsilon\) is smaller than that for \(J/\psi\).

## Context

This work extends the zero-temperature calculation of the heavy quark-antiquark potential within the Gribov-Zwanziger framework (Gracey 2009, Golterman 2012) to finite temperatures, using the HTL resummation method of Laine (2006) which first showed the potential is complex. It contrasts with standard perturbative QCD calculations by incorporating non-perturbative infrared effects via the Gribov parameter, and it complements lattice QCD studies (Rothkopf 2011, Bala 2019) and effective field theory approaches (Brambilla 2008, 2013) that also find a finite imaginary part. The results enable more realistic modeling of quarkonium suppression in the quark-gluon plasma by providing a potential that includes both screening and Landau damping from a non-perturbative starting point.
