# Elliptic flow at large transverse momenta from quark coalescence

> We show that hadronization via quark coalescence enhances hadron elliptic flow at large pT relative to that of partons at the same transverse momentum. Therefore, compared to earlier results based on covariant parton transport theory, more moderate initial parton densities dN/dη(b=0) ~ 1500-3000 can explain the differential elliptic flow v_2(pT) data for Au+Au reactions at s^1/2=130 and 200 AGeV from RHIC. In addition, v2(pT) could saturate at about 50% higher values for baryons than for mesons. If strange quarks have weaker flow than light quarks, hadron v_2 at high pT decreases with relative strangeness content.

# Elliptic flow at large transverse momenta from quark coalescence

## Key claim

Hadronization via quark coalescence enhances hadron elliptic flow at high $p_\perp$ relative to parton flow at the same transverse momentum, which means that initial parton densities of only $dN/d\eta(b=0) \sim 1500-3000$ can explain the RHIC $v_2(p_\perp)$ data, a factor of 3-6 smaller than required by earlier parton transport models that assumed independent fragmentation or parton-hadron duality.

## Method

The authors apply a quark coalescence formalism where meson spectra are proportional to the square of the quark spectrum at half the hadron momentum, and baryon spectra to the cube of the quark spectrum at one-third the hadron momentum. They derive analytic expressions for elliptic flow $v_2$ of mesons and baryons in terms of the underlying parton $v_2$, assuming $v_2 \ll 1$, and compare the resulting amplification to the transport opacity parameterization from earlier parton transport calculations. The analysis uses the parameterization $v_2(p_\perp,\chi) = v_2^{max}(\chi) \tanh[p_\perp / p_0(\chi)]$ from Ref. v2 (2002) to relate parton elliptic flow to the transport opacity $\chi \equiv \sigma dN/d\eta / (940\text{ mb})$.

## Key numbers

- Initial parton densities of $dN/d\eta(b=0) \sim 1500-3000$ can explain RHIC $v_2$ data, compared to $dN_g/d\eta(b=0) \sim 1000$ from the EKRT perturbative estimate
- Transport opacity required is $\sigma dN/d\eta(b=0) \sim 5000-10000$ mb after accounting for non-flow effects, down from $\sim 45000$ mb in earlier parton transport fits
- Baryon elliptic flow at high $p_\perp$ saturates at about 50% higher values than meson elliptic flow, $v_2^B \approx 1.5 v_2^M$
- Saturation of elliptic flow sets in at $p_\perp > 2-4$ GeV for hadrons, with baryon saturation occurring at 50% higher $p_\perp$ than meson saturation
- The increase of saturation $v_2^{max}$ with opacity scales as $\chi^{0.61}$, so a 2-3 times smaller parton $v_2$ corresponds to 3-6 times smaller opacities

## Context

This work extends the covariant parton transport theory of Ref. v2 (2002), which required an order-of-magnitude enhancement of parton opacities to match RHIC data (the "opacity puzzle"), by introducing quark coalescence as an alternative hadronization mechanism. The coalescence approach was previously applied in the ALCOR and MICOR models for particle abundances and spectra, and was suggested by Voloshin (2003) as an explanation for anomalous meson/baryon ratios at RHIC. The results differ from those of Lin and Ko (2002), who considered coalescence independent of relative quark momenta and found no amplification of hadron flow. The predictions for flow ordering by strangeness content ($v_2^p > v_2^\Lambda > v_2^\Xi > v_2^\Omega$ and $v_2^\pi > v_2^K > v_2^\phi$) can be tested in current and future heavy-ion experiments and, if confirmed, may indicate the formation of deconfined nuclear matter at RHIC.
