# Relativistic Mean-Field Models with Scaled Hadron Masses and Couplings: Hyperons and Maximum Neutron Star Mass

> An equation of state of cold nuclear matter with an arbitrary isotopic composition is studied within a relativistic mean-field approach with hadron masses and coupling constants depending self-consistently on the scalar mean-field. All hadron masses decrease universally with the scalar field growth, whereas meson-nucleon coupling constants can vary differently. More specifically we focus on two modifications of the KVOR model studied previously. One extension of the model (KVORcut) demonstrates that the equation of state stiffens if the increase of the scalar-field magnitude with the density is bounded from above at some value for baryon densities above the saturation nuclear density. This can be realized if the nucleon vector-meson coupling constant changes rapidly as a function of the scalar field slightly above the desired value. The other version of the model (MKVOR) utilizes a smaller value of the nucleon effective mass at the nuclear saturation density and a saturation of the scalar field in the isospin asymmetric matter induced by a strong variation of the nucleon isovector-meson coupling constant as function of the scalar field. A possibility of hyperonization of the matter in neutron star interiors is incorporated. Our equations of state fulfill majority of known empirical constraints including the pressure-density constraint from heavy-ion collisions, direct Urca constraint, gravitational-baryon mass constraint for the pulsar J0737-3039B, and the constraint on the maximum mass of the neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper constructs relativistic mean-field (RMF) models with self-consistent scaling of hadron masses and coupling constants that can simultaneously satisfy the 2 solar mass neutron star constraint and the direct Urca cooling constraint, but only if the scalar-field growth is artificially bounded (KVORcut) or if the isospin-asymmetric matter induces a saturation of the scalar field via a strong variation of the isovector-meson coupling (MKVOR).

## Method

The authors start from the KVOR model of Kolomeitsev et al. (2004) and introduce two modifications: KVORcut, where the scalar-field magnitude is capped above saturation density by a rapid change of the nucleon vector-meson coupling constant; and MKVOR, which uses a smaller nucleon effective mass at saturation and a strong variation of the isovector-meson coupling constant to saturate the scalar field in asymmetric matter. Hyperon degrees of freedom are included using SU(6) symmetry relations for vector couplings, with scalar couplings fixed by hyperon binding energies in nuclear matter. The equation of state is solved self-consistently from the energy density minimization with respect to the scalar field variable \(f\).

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass \(M_{\rm max} > 2.0 M_\odot\) is achieved in both KVORcut and MKVOR models, satisfying the constraints from PSR J1614-2230 (\(1.97 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\)) and PSR J0348+0432 (\(2.01 \pm 0.04 M_\odot\)).
- The direct Urca threshold mass \(M^{(n)}_{\rm DU}\) is larger than \(1.5 M_\odot\) in the MKVOR model, satisfying the "strong" DU constraint; in KVORcut it exceeds \(1.35 M_\odot\).
- Hyperon binding energies used: \(\mathcal{E}_{\rm bind}^{\Lambda}(n_0) = -28\) MeV, \(\mathcal{E}_{\rm bind}^{\Sigma}(n_0) = +30\) MeV, \(\mathcal{E}_{\rm bind}^{\Xi}(n_0) = -15\) MeV.
- Nuclear saturation density fixed at \(n_0 \simeq 0.16 \pm 0.015\) fm\(^{-3}\) and energy per nucleon \(\mathcal{E}_0 \simeq -15.6 \pm 0.6\) MeV.
- Incompressibility \(K\) varied in the range 250–315 MeV based on Stone (2014).
- Symmetry energy slope parameter \(L = 55 \pm 25\) MeV from Centelles et al. (2008) and \(L = 106 \pm 46\) MeV from Cozma et al. (2013) are both considered as constraints.

## Context

This work extends the scaled hadron mass-coupling (SHMC) approach of Kolomeitsev et al. (2004) and the KVOR/KVR models tested by Klahn et al. (2006) against updated constraints. It addresses the "hyperon puzzle", the tension between the observed 2 \(M_\odot\) neutron stars and the softening of the equation of state when hyperons appear, by demonstrating that the MKVOR model can reach \(M_{\rm max} > 2 M_\odot\) even with hyperons, while keeping the direct Urca threshold above \(1.5 M_\odot\). This contrasts with earlier RMF models (e.g., Glendenning, Sedrakian 2007) that typically predicted maximum masses below \(1.6 M_\odot\) with hyperons. The results enable future work on finite-temperature extensions for heavy-ion collisions and more detailed neutron star cooling simulations.
