# A description of odd mass Xe and Te isotopes in the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model

> Recent interest in spectroscopic factors for single-neutron transfer in low-spin states of the even-odd Xenon $^{125,127,129.131}$Xe and even-odd Tellurium, $^{123,125,127,129,131}$Te isotopes stimulated us to study these isotopes within the frame work of the Interacting Boson-Fermion Model. The fermion that is coupled to the system of bosons is taken to be in the positive parity $3s_{1/2}$, $2d_{3/2}$, $2d_{5/2}$, $1g_{7/2}$ and in the negative $1h_{11/2}$ single-particle orbits, the complete 50-82 major shell. The calculated energies of low-spin energy levels of the odd isotopes are found to agree well with the experimental data. Also B(E2), B(M1) values and spectroscopic factors for single-neutron transfer are calculated and compared with experimental data.

## Key claim

The Interacting Boson-Fermion Model (IBFM), using a single set of interaction strengths for both positive and negative parity states across multiple isotopes, can reproduce experimental energy levels, B(E2) and B(M1) values, and spectroscopic factors for odd-mass Xe and Te isotopes, provided that quasi-particle energies and occupation probabilities are treated as adjustable parameters rather than derived from BCS calculations.

## Method

The authors coupled a single neutron in the complete 50-82 major shell orbitals (3s1/2, 2d3/2, 2d5/2, 1g7/2, and 1h11/2) to even-even IBM cores described by parameters from Pascu (2010). They diagonalized the IBFM Hamiltonian using the ODDA code, adjusting the boson-fermion interaction strengths (monopole A0, quadrupole Gamma0, exchange Lambda0) and the quasi-particle energies and occupation probabilities to fit excitation energies, while also comparing calculated B(E2), B(M1) values, and spectroscopic factors to experimental data.

## Key numbers

*   For all Xe and Te isotopes studied, the boson-fermion interaction strengths were fixed at Lambda0 = 1.4 MeV, Gamma0 = 0.25 MeV, and A0 = -0.15 MeV.
*   For 123Te, the calculated B(E2) for the 5/2_1+ to 3/2_1+ transition is 75.9 Wu, compared to experimental values of 10. Wu and 11. +/- 2 Wu from two references.
*   For 125Te, the calculated B(E2) for the 5/2_2+ to 3/2_1+ transition is 37.55 Wu, compared to experimental values of 11. and 51. +/- 3 Wu.
*   For 125Xe, the calculated B(E2) for the 3/2_1+ to 1/2_1+ transition is 14. Wu, compared to experimental values of 100 +/- 80 Wu and 130. Wu.
*   For 127Xe, the calculated B(E2) for the 3/2_1+ to 1/2_1+ transition is 14.62 Wu, compared to an experimental value of 17 +/- 6 Wu.
*   For 129Xe, the calculated B(E2) for the 3/2_2+ to 3/2_1+ transition is 19. Wu, compared to experimental values ranging from 3.5 to 21.7 +/- 0.9 Wu across five references.
*   For 131Xe, the calculated B(E2) for the 5/2_1+ to 3/2_1+ transition is 25. Wu, compared to experimental values ranging from 1.1 to 27.5 +/- 0.9 Wu.
*   For 123Te, the calculated spectroscopic factor for the h11/2 stripping reaction is 0.41, compared to an experimental (d,p) value of 0.30.
*   For 125Te, the calculated spectroscopic factor for the s1/2 stripping reaction is 0.45, compared to an experimental (d,p) value of 0.42.
*   For 127Te, the calculated spectroscopic factor for the d3/2 stripping reaction is 0.43, compared to an experimental (d,p) value of 0.24.
*   For 129Te, the calculated spectroscopic factor for the d3/2 stripping reaction is 0.38, compared to an experimental (d,p) value of 0.33.
*   For 131Te, the calculated spectroscopic factor for the d3/2 stripping reaction is 0.39, compared to an experimental (d,p) value of 0.5.

## Context

This work extends the IBFM calculations of Yosoi (2008), which only addressed positive parity states in two Xe isotopes, to a comprehensive study of both positive and negative parity states in nine odd-mass Xe and Te isotopes. It also builds on the IBM description of even-even cores in this mass region by Pascu (2010). The unified treatment of both parities with consistent interaction strengths and the inclusion of spectroscopic factors as a sensitive test of wave functions enables more reliable predictions for nuclear structure in the A=130 region, which is relevant for double-beta decay studies.
