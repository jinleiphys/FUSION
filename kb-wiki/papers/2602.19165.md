# Two nearby states in the $X(3872)$ region: Resolving the radiative-decay ratio tension with $η_{c2}$

> Recently, LHCb reported the radiative-decay ratio ${\cal R}^{ψγ}\equiv {\cal B}[X(3872)\to ψ'γ]/{\cal B}[X(3872)\to J/ψγ]=1.67\pm 0.25$ extracted from $B^+\to K^+(J/ψγ, ψ'γ)$. This result differs markedly ($\sim4.6σ$) from the BESIII value obtained from $e^+e^-\to γ(J/ψγ, ψ'γ)$, ${\cal R}^{ψγ}=-0.04\pm 0.28$. Such a significant tension suggests that more than one state in the $X(3872)$ region contributes to the processes. We therefore propose a two-state scenario: a shallow $D^{*0}\bar{D}^0$ bound state with $J^{PC}=1^{++}$ and a $2^{-+}$ charmonium candidate, $η_{c2}$, slightly above the $D^{*0}\bar{D}^0$ threshold. We show that this hypothesis consistently describes these ratios along with other branching fractions and lineshapes across multiple processes. By contrast, fits without the $η_{c2}$ component fail to reproduce the radiative-ratio data. We also predict helicity-angle distributions that motivates the future experiments to test the two-state hypothesis and search for the so-far missing $η_{c2}$.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the tension between LHCb and BESIII measurements of the radiative-decay ratio \( \mathcal{R}^{\psi\gamma} \) (differing by approximately 4.6 sigma) is resolved not by a single \( X(3872) \) state but by two distinct nearby states: a shallow \( D^{*0}\bar{D}^0 \) bound state with \( J^{PC}=1^{++} \) (the established \( X(3872) \)) and a \( 2^{-+} \) charmonium candidate, \( \eta_{c2} \), lying slightly above the \( D^{*0}\bar{D}^0 \) threshold.

## Method

The author constructs a coupled-channel model with separable s-wave potentials for the \( \{D^{*0}\bar{D}^0\} \), \( \{D^{*+}D^-\} \), \( J/\psi\omega \), \( J/\psi\rho \), \( J/\psi\gamma \), and \( \psi'\gamma \) channels, generating the \( X(3872) \) as a pole of the \( J^{PC}=1^{++} \) system. The \( \eta_{c2} \) and \( \chi_{c0}(3915) \) contributions are added as explicit Breit-Wigner excitation mechanisms, and the model is fitted to invariant mass distributions and branching-fraction ratios from \( B^+ \to K^+ f \) and \( e^+e^- \to \gamma f \) data.

## Key numbers

* The LHCb radiative-decay ratio \( \mathcal{R}_{B}^{\psi\gamma} = 1.67 \pm 0.25 \) differs from the BESIII value \( \mathcal{R}_{e^+e^-}^{\psi\gamma} = -0.04 \pm 0.28 \) at the 4.6 sigma level.
* The extracted pole position for \( X(3872) \) is \( 3871.4 - 0.022i \) MeV, slightly below the \( D^{*0}\bar{D}^0 \) threshold (3871.7 MeV).
* The extracted pole position for \( \eta_{c2} \) is \( 3874. - 0.25i \) MeV, slightly above the \( D^{*0}\bar{D}^0 \) threshold.
* The default model (A) yields \( \mathcal{R}_{B}^{\psi\gamma} = 1.6 \) and \( \mathcal{R}_{e^+e^-}^{\psi\gamma} = 0.26 \), giving a ratio of approximately 6.4 between the two; the \( \eta_{c2} \)-less model yields \( \mathcal{R}_{B}^{\psi\gamma} \sim \mathcal{R}_{e^+e^-}^{\psi\gamma} = 0.53 \).
* The \( \eta_{c2} \) non-open-charm partial width is taken as \( \Gamma^{\rm nOC}_{\eta_{c2}} = 0.5 \) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing puzzle of the \( X(3872) \) state, first discovered by Belle (2003), which has been interpreted both as a hadronic molecule (Swanson 2004, Braaten and Kusunoki 2004) and as a compact charmonium state (Suzuki 2005, Kalashnikova 2005). The two-state hypothesis resolves the radiative-decay ratio tension between LHCb and BESIII, which a single-state description cannot accommodate. The predictions for helicity-angle distributions in \( B^+ \to (\psi\gamma)K^+ \) and \( e^+e^- \to (\psi\gamma)\gamma \) provide a direct experimental test for the existence of the missing \( \eta_{c2} \) charmonium state, which is expected from quark models (Godfrey and Isgur 1985, Barnes and Godfrey 2004) and lattice QCD but has not yet been observed.
