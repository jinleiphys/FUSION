# Pentaquark hadrons from lattice QCD

> We study spin 1/2 isoscalar and isovector candidates in both parity channels for the recently discovered Θ^+(1540) pentaquark particle in quenched lattice QCD. Our analysis takes into account all possible uncertainties, such as statistical, finite size and quenching errors when performing the chiral and continuum extrapolations and we have indications that our signal is separated from scattering states. The lowest mass that we find in the I^P=0^- channel is in complete agreement with the experimental value of the Θ^+ mass. On the other hand, the lowest mass state in the opposite parity I^P=0^+ channel is much higher. Our findings suggests that the parity of the Θ^+ is negative.

## Key claim

The authors find that the lowest mass state in the \(I^P=0^-\) channel agrees with the experimental \(\Theta^+(1540)\) mass, while the \(I^P=0^+\) state is much heavier, suggesting negative parity for the \(\Theta^+\). This conclusion is hedged by the use of the quenched approximation and the need to separate the signal from the nearby \(N+K\) S-wave scattering state.

## Method

The authors performed quenched lattice QCD simulations using the Wilson gauge action and Wilson fermion action at three lattice spacings (a = 0.17, 0.12, and 0.09 fm) with spatial box sizes around 2 fm. They constructed spin-1/2 interpolating operators for both isoscalar (I=0) and isovector (I=1) channels, computed correlators in both parity channels, and performed chiral extrapolations from four light quark masses per lattice spacing followed by a linear continuum extrapolation. The strange quark mass was fixed using two schemes: (A) reproducing the physical kaon mass in the chiral limit, and (B) keeping the nucleon-to-kaon mass ratio constant.

## Key numbers

* The mass ratio \(m_{0^-}/(m_K+m_N) = 1.073(34)\) on the finest lattice (a = 0.093 fm, \(\beta=6.0\), \(20^3\times 36\)).
* The continuum-extrapolated mass of the \(I^P=0^-\) state is \(m_{0^-} = 1539 \pm 50\) MeV, in agreement with the experimental \(\Theta^+(1540)\) mass.
* The \(I^P=1^-\) state is about 15% heavier than the \(\Theta^+\) mass, but still within one standard deviation.
* The \(I^P=0^+\) parity partner is almost two times heavier than the \(\Theta^+\) mass, lying several standard deviations above the experimental value.
* From a \(2\times 2\) correlation matrix analysis on the largest lattice at the largest quark mass (300 configurations), the smallest energy state was \(E_0/(m_N+m_K)=0.994(18)\) (interpreted as the S-wave scattering state) and the next state was \(E_1/(m_N+m_K)=1.074(20)\) (the \(\Theta^+\) candidate).

## Context

This work extends the application of lattice QCD to the study of exotic pentaquark hadrons following the experimental discovery of the \(\Theta^+(1540)\) by Nakano et al. (2003) and Barmin et al. (2003). The results contradict predictions from chiral soliton models and correlated quark models (Jaffe, 2003) that suggested positive parity, instead supporting negative parity as predicted by uncorrelated quark models. The analysis is consistent with the independent lattice study of Sasaki (2003), who also separated the \(K+N\) S-wave scattering state. The work enables future unquenched calculations and systematic studies of different interpolating operators to probe the \(\Theta^+\) wave function structure, as well as searches for additional exotic pentaquark states with different quantum numbers.
