# Extended-soft-core Baryon-Baryon Model ESC08 I. Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering

> The Nijmegen extended-sft-core ESC08c model for the baryon-baryon (BB) interactions of the SU(3) flavor-octet of baryons ($N, Λ, Σ$, and $Ξ$) is presented. In this first of a series of papers, the NN results are reported in detail. In the spirit of the Yukawa-approach to the nuclear force problem, the interactions are studied from the meson-exchange picture viewpoint, using generalized soft-core Yukawa-functions. These interactions are supplemented with (i) multiple-gluon-exchange, and (ii) structural effects due to the quark-core of the baryons. The extended-soft-core (ESC) meson-exchange interactions consist of local- and non-local-potentials due to ((i) One-boson-exchanges (OBE, which are the members of nonets of pseudoscalar , vector, scalar, and axial-vector mesons, (ii) diffractive (i.e. multiple-gluon) exchanges, (iii) two pseudoscalar exchange (PS-PS), and (iv) meson-pair-exchange (MPE). The OBE- and MPE-vertices are regulated by gaussian form factors producing potentials with a soft behavior near the origin. The assignment of the cut-off masses for the BBM-vertices is dependent on the SU(3)-classification of the exchanged mesons for OBE, and a similar scheme for MPE. The simultaneous fit of the ESC-models to the NN- and YN-scattering data achieved excellent results for the NN, YN, and favorable properties for the $ΛΛ$ and $ΞN$ systems. In the case of ESC08c, the version of this paper, the results are: (i) For the selected 4313 pp and np scattering data ($ 0 \leq T_{lab} \leq 350$ MeV), the model achieved $χ^2/N_{data} = 1.08$. (ii) The deuteron binding energy and all NN low energy parameters are fitted very nicely. (iii) The YN-data are described also very well with $χ^/N_{data} = 1.09$. (iv) The model predicts a bound $ΞN(^3S_1,I=1)$ states with binding energy 1.56 MeV.

## Key claim

The ESC08c model achieves a simultaneous fit to nucleon-nucleon (NN) and hyperon-nucleon (YN) scattering data with a single set of parameters, yielding a chi-squared per data point of 1.08 for NN data and 1.09 for YN data, but this quality depends on including new short-range ingredients (odderon exchange and quark-core effects) that were absent in the predecessor ESC04 model.

## Method

The authors construct a baryon-baryon potential from meson-exchange diagrams (one-boson-exchange, two-pseudoscalar exchange, and meson-pair-exchange) supplemented by diffractive multiple-gluon exchange (pomeron and odderon) and phenomenological quark-core repulsions. All vertices use gaussian form factors with SU(3)-dependent cut-off masses, and coupling constants are kept SU(3)-symmetric. The model is fitted simultaneously to 4313 pp and np scattering data points (0 to 350 MeV laboratory energy) together with YN scattering data and hypernuclear constraints, solving the Thompson equation in momentum space.

## Key numbers

- For 4313 pp and np scattering data (0 ≤ T_lab ≤ 350 MeV), the model achieves χ²/N_data = 1.08
- For YN scattering data, the model achieves χ²/N_data = 1.09
- The model predicts a bound ΞN(³S₁, I=1) state with binding energy 1.56 MeV
- The η-η' mixing angle is taken as θ_P = -13°, the φ-ω mixing angle as θ_V = 38.70°, and the f₁-f₁' mixing angle as θ_A = 50.0°

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen soft-core approach from the earlier ESC04 model (Rijken 2004) by adding odderon exchange (odd-gluon number exchange) and explicit quark-core Pauli-blocking repulsions, which were needed to improve short-range behavior in channels such as Σ⁺p(I=3/2,³S₁) and ΣN(I=1/2,¹S₀). The model enables parameter-free predictions for strangeness S=-2, -3, -4 baryon-baryon channels relevant to hypernuclear physics and neutron-star matter, and will be followed by companion papers on YN (paper II) and YY (papers III and IV) results.
