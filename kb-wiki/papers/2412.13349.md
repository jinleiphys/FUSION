# Neutrino nucleus Quasi-Elastic and resonant Neutral Current scatterings with Non-Standard Interactions

> As well known, the cross sections of the resonance and Quasi-Elastic (QE) scattering off nucleons depend on quantities known as form factors that describe the nucleon structure. There are alternative approaches to determine the values of these non-perturbative quantities, some of them relying on the Neutral Current (NC) scattering of neutrinos off nucleons. In the presence of NC Non-Standard Interactions (NSI), such derivations must be revisited. The aim of the present paper is to discuss how information on NSI can be extracted by combining alternative approaches for deriving the form factors. We discuss how the KamLAND atmospheric neutrino data with $E_ν<{\rm GeV}$ (used to determine the axial strange form factor $g_A^s$) can already constrain the axial NSI of $ν_τ$ with nucleons. We also argue that if the precision measurement of $ν_μ$ NC QE scattering establishes unexpectedly large vector strange form factor ({\it e.g.,} $F_1^s(Q^2)\sim 0.01$), it will be an indication for nonzero NSI coupling with $u$ and $d$ quarks ($ε_{μμ}^{Au/d}\sim 0.01$). We study the QE and resonance scattering cross sections of $ν_τ$ and $ν_e$ off Argon and show that if their axial NSI is of the order of (but of course below) the present bounds, the deviation of QE cross sections from the SM prediction will be sizable and distinct from the uncertainties induced by the form factors.

## Key claim
The paper argues that by combining multiple independent approaches for determining nucleon form factors (lattice QCD, parity-violating electron scattering, beta decay, and neutrino scattering), degeneracies between non-standard interaction (NSI) parameters and form factor uncertainties can be resolved, but notes that a specific degenerate solution exists where the hadronic current transforms to its opposite, making the SM and NSI cases indistinguishable for certain parameter choices.

## Method
The authors compute the hadronic current for neutral-current quasi-elastic (QE) and resonance scattering in the presence of both vector and axial NSI, expressing the cross sections in terms of modified form factors that absorb the NSI contributions. They then review existing constraints from oscillation experiments, deep inelastic scattering (CHARM, NuTeV), coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (CEνNS), and the SNO experiment, and identify which combinations of NSI parameters remain poorly constrained. Finally, they study the QE and resonance cross sections for ν_τ and ν_e scattering off argon, incorporating nuclear effects, and compare the deviations induced by axial NSI to those from form factor uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- KamLAND atmospheric neutrino data with E_ν < 1 GeV yields g_A^s = -0.14^{+0.25}_{-0.25} in the SM; this measurement can already constrain axial NSI of ν_τ with nucleons.
- Lattice QCD predicts g_A^s = -0.044 ± 0.008 and g_A^{(8)} = 0.53 ± 0.022 with M_A^{(8)} = 1.154(101) GeV.
- The axial mass from CC interactions is M_A = 0.999 ± 0.011 GeV.
- Bounds from NuTeV give ε^{Au/d}_{μμ} < 0.01 and ε^{Au/d}_{μτ} < 0.005.
- SNO bounds on axial isovector combinations: -0.13 < ε_{ee}^{Au} - ε_{ee}^{Ad} < 0.19 and -0.15 < ε_{ττ}^{Au} - ε_{ττ}^{Ad} < 0.2.
- NOνA data constrains -0.41 < ε^{An}_{ττ} = ε^{Ap}_{ττ} < 0.41 and -0.40 < ε^{An}_{eτ} = ε^{Ap}_{eτ} < 0.40.
- If precision ν_μ NC QE scattering finds F_1^s(Q^2) ∼ 0.01, this would indicate ε_{μμ}^{Au/d} ∼ 0.01.
- The weak axial coupling from beta decay is |g_A| = 1.27641(55).

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous analysis of NC NSI in deep inelastic scattering at DUNE (Abbaslu 2023) and a toy model with ε^{Au}_{ττ} = ε^{Ad}_{ττ} ∼ 1 (Abbaslu 2024). It builds on prior studies of vector NSI effects on QE scattering by Papoulias (2016) and the impact of μμ NSI on QE cross sections by Ilma (2024). The paper identifies that the KamLAND atmospheric neutrino determination of g_A^s (KamLAND 2022) can already constrain axial NSI, and that future measurements at DUNE, NOνA, T2K, and T2HK can probe axial NSI couplings that are currently weakly bounded, particularly for ν_τ and ν_e flavors. The analysis enables disentangling NSI from form factor uncertainties by comparing results across different neutrino flavors and experimental approaches.
