# Pedestal and Peak Structure in Jet Correlation

> We study the characteristics of correlation between particles in jets produced in heavy-ion collisions. In the framework of parton recombination we calculate the $η$ and $ϕ$ distributions of a pion associated with a trigger particle. The origin of the pedestal in $Δη$ is related to the longitudinal expansion of the thermal partons that are enhanced by the energy loss of hard partons traversing the bulk medium. The peaks in $Δη$ and $Δϕ$ are related to the same angular spread of the shower partons in a jet cone. No artificial short- or long-range correlations are put in by hand. A large part of the correlation between hadrons in jets is due to the correlation among the shower partons arising from momentum conservation. Recombination between thermal and shower partons dominates the correlation characterisitics in the intermediate $p_T$ region.

## Key claim

The pedestal in Δη arises from the longitudinal expansion of thermal partons that are locally enhanced by the energy loss of hard partons traversing the medium, while the peaks in Δη and Δϕ both originate from the same angular spread of shower partons within a jet cone, with no artificial short- or long-range correlations introduced by hand.

## Method

The authors generalized the one-dimensional parton recombination model to three dimensions to calculate the η and ϕ distributions of a pion associated with a trigger particle in heavy-ion collisions. They decomposed the two-particle distribution into thermal-thermal (TT), thermal-shower (TS), and shower-shower (SS) components, with the shower parton distribution including a Gaussian angular spread G(ψ, x) whose width depends on the momentum fraction x. The pedestal was modeled by subtracting the un-enhanced thermal background (T₀) from the enhanced thermal distribution (T) in the TT component, while the peak was computed from the TS and SS components using the angular-dependent shower parton distributions.

## Key numbers

- ΔT = 15 MeV: the increase in the inverse slope of enhanced thermal partons relative to the undisturbed medium (T₀ = 0.317 GeV/c), fitted to reproduce the pedestal height of approximately 0.05 in the Δη distribution.
- σ₀ = 0.22: the adjustable parameter controlling the half-width of the Gaussian angular spread of shower partons, fitted to reproduce the peak width in Δη.
- σ_φ = 1.2: the half-width of the Gaussian H(Δφ) describing the Δφ dependence of the pedestal, chosen so that the pedestal vanishes at |Δφ| = 1.
- Trigger window: 4 < p_trig < 6 GeV/c; associated particle window: 2 < p_assoc < 4 GeV/c; pseudorapidity range: |η| < 0.7.

## Context

This work extends the parton recombination model previously applied to single-particle distributions in Au+Au collisions (Hwa and Yang, 2004) and d+Au collisions (Hwa and Yang, 2004), as well as to dihadron correlations (Hwa and Yang, 2004; Hwa and Tan, 2005). It provides the first theoretical interpretation of the pedestal and peak structure observed by the STAR collaboration (Adams et al., 2004) in terms of the interplay between thermal and shower partons. The results enable quantitative modeling of jet-medium interactions and the role of energy loss in modifying the thermal environment around hard partons.
