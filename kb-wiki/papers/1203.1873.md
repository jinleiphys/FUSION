# Non-yrast nuclear spectra in a model of coherent quadrupole-octupole motion

> A model assuming coherent quadrupole-octupole vibrations and rotations is applied to describe non-yrast energy sequences with alternating parity in several even-even nuclei from different regions, namely $^{152,154}$Sm, $^{154,156,158}$Gd, $^{236}$U and $^{100}$Mo. Within the model scheme the yrast alternating-parity band is composed by the members of the ground-state band and the lowest negative-parity levels with odd angular momenta. The non-yrast alternating-parity sequences unite levels of $β$-bands with higher negative-parity levels. The model description reproduces the structure of the considered alternating-parity spectra together with the observed B(E1), B(E2) and B(E3) transition probabilities within and between the different level-sequences. B(E1) and B(E3) reduced probabilities for transitions connecting states with opposite parity in the non-yrast alternating-parity bands are predicted. The implemented study outlines the limits of the considered band-coupling scheme and provides estimations about the collective energy potential which governs the quadrupole-octupole properties of the considered nuclei.

## Key claim

The model of coherent quadrupole-octupole motion (CQOM) can describe non-yrast alternating-parity bands in even-even nuclei by connecting excited β-bands with higher negative-parity sequences, but the authors acknowledge that this band-coupling scheme does not exhaust the entire collective spectrum and is limited to axial quadrupole and octupole degrees of freedom, excluding γ-vibrations.

## Method

The CQOM model was extended by assuming that the radial quantum number n=0 corresponds to the yrast alternating-parity band (ground-state band plus lowest negative-parity levels), while n=1,2,... correspond to non-yrast structures where β-bands are coupled to higher negative-parity sequences with odd angular momenta. The model Hamiltonian was solved analytically in ellipsoidal coordinates, yielding energy eigenvalues E_{n,k}(I) = ħω[2n+1+√(k²+bX(I))] with X(I)=[d₀+I(I+1)]/2, and the angular quantum numbers k were allowed to vary (k=1,3,5,... for positive parity; k=2,4,6,... for negative parity) rather than being fixed as in the original yrast treatment. Transition probabilities B(E1), B(E2), and B(E3) were calculated using generalized angular operators to account for multiple maxima in the deformation density distributions.

## Key numbers

- Nuclei studied: ¹⁵²Sm, ¹⁵⁴Sm, ¹⁵⁴Gd, ¹⁵⁶Gd, ¹⁵⁸Gd, ²³⁶U, and ¹⁰⁰Mo
- The charge factor M₁ used fixed average values J=35 MeV and Q=45 MeV for all considered nuclei
- The parameter p must satisfy p > 1/√2 ≈ 0.7071, with q expressed as q = p/√(2p²-1)
- The angular quantum numbers k were varied in the range 1 ≤ k ≤ 20 for each nucleus
- The nuclear radius parameter r₀ ≈ 1.2 fm was used in the charge factors M₂ and M₃

## Context

This work extends the CQOM model previously applied to yrast alternating-parity bands in ¹⁵⁰Nd, ¹⁵²Sm, ¹⁵⁴Gd, and ¹⁵⁶Dy (Minkov et al., 2006) to non-yrast regions, addressing a question about the model's validity at higher energies. The approach differs from the Extended Coherent States Model (Raduta et al., 2006) which considered coupling of β and γ bands with opposite-parity bands, as the present work assumes a persistent role of quadrupole-octupole motion in forming non-yrast structures. The results enable experimental searches for predicted B(E1) and B(E3) transitions in non-yrast alternating-parity bands and provide a framework for classifying highly excited states whose interpretation in experimental databases is ambiguous.
