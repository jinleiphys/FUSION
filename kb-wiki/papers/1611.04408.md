# Cross section line shape of $e^+e^-\toχ_{c0}ω$ around the $Y(4260)$ mass region

> We demonstrate that the recent measurement of the cross section line shapes of $e^+e^-\to χ_{c0}ω$ can be naturally explained by the molecular picture for $Y(4260)$ where the $Y(4260)$ is treated as a hadronic molecule dominated by $\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.$ This result is consistent with properties extracted for $Y(4260)$ as the $\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.$ molecular state in other reactions such as $e^+e^-\to J/ψππ$, $h_cππ$, $\bar D D^*π+c.c.$, and $γX(3872)$.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the recent BESIII measurement of the cross section line shape for \(e^+e^-\to\chi_{c0}\omega\) can be naturally explained by treating the \(Y(4260)\) as a hadronic molecule dominated by \(\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.\), with the caveat that a single dimensionless overall constant \(\mathcal C\) (fitted to 0.09–0.11) is needed to absorb the combined uncertainties of the known coupling constants.

## Method

The authors compute the \(e^+e^-\to\chi_{c0}\omega\) cross section via a triangle-loop diagram where the \(Y(4260)\) molecular state first couples to \(\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.\), which then rescatters into \(\chi_{c0}\omega\) through charmed-meson exchanges. The dressed \(Y(4260)\) propagator and couplings are taken from previous fits to \(e^+e^-\to J/\psi\pi\pi\) and \(h_c\pi\pi\) data (Cleven et al., 2013), while the \(\omega\)-meson coupling to charmed mesons is determined via vector meson dominance and the \(\chi_{c0}\) coupling from radiative charmonium decays (Cao et al., 2016). Only one free parameter \(\mathcal C\) is fitted to the BESIII data.

## Key numbers

- Renormalized mass of \(Y(4260)\): \(m_Y = 4.217 \pm 0.002\) GeV, from fits to \(e^+e^-\to J/\psi\pi\pi\) and \(h_c\pi\pi\) data (Cleven et al., 2013).
- Non-\(\bar{D}D_1\) width: \(\Gamma^{\text{non}-\bar{D}D_1} = 0.056 \pm 0.003\) GeV, from the same fits.
- Effective coupling \(y_\text{eff} = 3.94 \pm 0.04\) GeV\(^{-1/2}\) and contact coupling \(g_c = 29.50 \pm 0.47\) GeV\(^{-2}\), from the same fits.
- Reduced \(\chi^2\) for the fit to all BESIII data: \(\chi^2/N_{d.o.f} = 3\); for the fit to only the first 7 data points: \(\chi^2/N_{d.o.f} = 13/7 \sim 2\).
- Partial width \(\bar{\Gamma}(Y(4260)\to\chi_{c0}\omega) = 1.4 \pm 0.3\) MeV (with finite-width correction), compared to \(1.2 \pm 0.2\) MeV without correction.
- Total width of \(Y(4260)\) in the molecular picture: \(\Gamma_Y = 0.075\) GeV (73.9 \(\pm\) 4.5 MeV from earlier work), smaller than the Breit-Wigner width of about 120 MeV.
- Peak cross section for \(e^+e^-\to\chi_{c0}\omega\) is about 50 pb, of the same order as the \(J/\psi\pi\pi\) channel but an order of magnitude smaller than the \(D\bar{D}^*\pi+c.c.\) channel.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of \(Y(4260)\) as a \(\bar{D}D_1(2420)+c.c.\) bound state, previously applied to \(e^+e^-\to J/\psi\pi\pi\), \(h_c\pi\pi\), and \(D\bar{D}^*\pi+c.c.\) by Wang et al. (2013), Guo et al. (2013), Cleven et al. (2013), and Qin et al. (2016), to the newly measured \(\chi_{c0}\omega\) channel. It contrasts with alternative explanations: Li and Voloshin (2014) attribute the structure to the \(Y(4160)\) resonance; Faccini et al. (2014) use a tetraquark model; and Dai et al. (2012) propose a \(\chi_{c0}\omega\) molecule. The paper enables future experimental discrimination by predicting a leptonic decay width of about 500 eV for the \(\bar{D}D_1\) molecule, versus about 23 eV for the \(\chi_{c0}\omega\) molecule and less than 40 eV for a hybrid charmonium from lattice QCD (Chen et al., 2016).
