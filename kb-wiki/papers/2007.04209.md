# Pion interferometry at 200 GeV using anisotropic hydrodynamics

> In this paper, we continue our phenomenological studies of heavy-ion collisions using 3+1d anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro). In previous works, we compared quasiparticle aHydro (aHydroQP) with ALICE 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb and RHIC 200 GeV Au-Au collision results. At both energies, the agreement was quite good between aHydroQP and the experimental data for many observables. In this work, we present comparisons of the Hanbury Brown--Twiss (HBT) radii and their ratios determined using pi+ pi+ pairs produced in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions. We first present comparisons with STAR results for the HBT radii and their ratios. We then present comparisons with PHENIX results for the HBT radii and their ratios. In both cases, we find reasonable agreement between aHydroQP predictions and available experimental results for the ratios of HBT radii. At the level of the radii themselves, in some cases quantitative differences on the order of 10-20% remain, which deserve further study.

## Key claim

The quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) model yields reasonable agreement with experimental data for the ratios of HBT radii in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions, but quantitative differences of 10-20% remain for the radii themselves, particularly for the longitudinal radius \(R_{\rm long}\).

## Method

The authors used a previously calibrated 3+1d quasiparticle anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydroQP) code with smooth Glauber initial conditions, momentum-space isotropic initial conditions, and a constant shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (\(\eta/s\)). They computed the HBT correlation function for \(\pi^+ \pi^+\) pairs using the 3-D Bertsch-Pratt parameterization and compared the resulting radii (\(R_{\rm out}\), \(R_{\rm side}\), \(R_{\rm long}\)) and their ratios to data from the STAR and PHENIX collaborations across multiple centrality classes as a function of pair mean transverse momentum \(k_T\).

## Key numbers

* The model parameters used were \(\tau_0 = 0.25\) fm/c, \(T_0 = 455\) MeV, \(\eta/s = 0.179\), and \(T_{\rm FO} = 130\) MeV, fixed from fits to pion, kaon, and proton spectra in 0-5% and 30-40% centrality classes.
* For comparisons with STAR data, the \(p_T\) cuts used were \(0.15 < p_T < 0.8\) GeV.
* For comparisons with PHENIX data, the \(k_T\) cuts used were \(0.2 < k_T < 2.0\) GeV.
* In comparisons with PHENIX data, the model overestimates \(R_{\rm out}\) by approximately 30% at large \(k_T \sim 0.8\) GeV and approximately 20% at low \(k_T \sim 0.3\) GeV.
* The model overestimates \(R_{\rm long}\) by on the order of 10-20% compared to both STAR and PHENIX data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phenomenological comparisons of aHydroQP with ALICE 2.76 TeV Pb-Pb and RHIC 200 GeV Au-Au collision data (Alqahtani et al., 2017; Almaalol et al., 2018) by now focusing on HBT radii. The results are similar in quality to prior dissipative hydrodynamics studies (Broniowski et al., 2008; Kisiel et al., 2008; Pratt et al., 2008; Bozek et al., 2010; Karpenko et al., 2012; Bozek et al., 2014), but cover a larger range of centrality classes and compare with both STAR and PHENIX data. The discrepancies suggest that the assumed strong initial boost-invariant Bjorken flow and smooth Glauber initial conditions may be too simplistic, motivating future work with fluctuating initial conditions and modified longitudinal flow profiles.
