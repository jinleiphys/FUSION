# Towards a Stability Analysis of Inhomogeneous Phases in QCD

> The possible occurrence of crystalline or inhomogeneous phases in the QCD phase diagram at large chemical potential has been under investigation for over thirty years. Such phases are present in models of QCD such as the Gross-Neveu model in 1+1 dimensions, Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) and quark meson models. Yet, no unambiguous confirmation exists from actual QCD. In this work, we propose a new approach for a stability analysis that is based on the two-particle irreducible effective action and compatible with full QCD calculations within the framework of functional methods. As a first test, we reproduce a known NJL model result within this framework. We then discuss the additional difficulties which arise in QCD due to the non-locality of the quark self-energy and suggest a method to overcome them. As a proof of principle and as an illustration of the analysis, we consider the Wigner-Weyl solution of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) within a simple truncation of QCD in the chiral limit and analyse its stability against homogeneous chiral-symmetry breaking fluctuations. For temperatures above and below the tricritical point we find that the boundary of the instability region coincides well with the second-order phase boundary or the left spinodal, respectively, obtained from the direct solutions of the DSEs. Finally, we outline how this method can be generalized to study inhomogeneous fluctuations.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new stability analysis method based on the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action that is compatible with full QCD calculations, but they only demonstrate it for homogeneous chiral-symmetry breaking fluctuations in a simple truncation of QCD, not yet for the inhomogeneous fluctuations that are the ultimate target.

## Method

The authors expand the 2PI effective action around a homogeneous equilibrium solution of the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE), treating small fluctuations of the propagator or self-energy. For the NJL model test, they expand in fluctuations of the self-energy rather than the propagator to exploit the locality of the self-energy. For QCD, they apply the method to the Wigner-Weyl solution of the quark DSE within a simple truncation in the chiral limit and analyze its stability against homogeneous chiral-symmetry breaking fluctuations, comparing the instability boundary to the phase boundary from direct DSE solutions.

## Context

This work extends stability analyses previously applied to NJL, Gross-Neveu, and Quark-Meson models (Pannullo 2022, Buballa 2018, Koenigstein 2021) to a framework compatible with full QCD. It builds on earlier attempts to find inhomogeneous solutions in QCD using DSEs (Muller 2013) and functional renormalization group studies that found a "moat regime" (Fu 2019). The method enables future systematic searches for inhomogeneous phases in QCD without relying on cutoff-dependent non-renormalizable models, addressing doubts raised by Buballa (2020) and Pannullo (2022) about whether inhomogeneous phases in NJL-type models are cutoff artefacts.
