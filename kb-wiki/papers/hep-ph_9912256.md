# Multiquark picture for Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1620)

> 5 pages, 3 ps files. Talk given at 11th International Light-Cone School and Workshop : New Directions in Quantum Chromodynamics, and 12th Nuclear Physics Summer School and Symposium (NuSS'99), Seoul, Korea, 26 May - 26 Jun, 1999

# Multiquark picture for Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1620)

## Key claim

The masses of Lambda(1405) and Sigma(1620) can be predicted from QCD sum rules using multiquark (pi-Sigma, anti-K N, pi-Lambda) interpolating fields, but the extracted masses depend on the chosen continuum threshold and the method of identifying the resonance mass from the Borel-mass dependence of the coupling strength, rather than from a direct plateau in the mass itself.

## Method

The author constructs QCD sum rules for I=0 and I=1 multiquark interpolating fields corresponding to the pi-Sigma, anti-K N, and pi-Lambda decay channels. The operator product expansion (OPE) is computed for the Pi_1 (unit) structure, including quark condensates up to dimension 6 while neglecting gluon condensates, and the phenomenological side is parameterized with a single pole plus continuum. The mass is determined not from a Borel plateau but by finding the point where the coupling strength lambda^2 reaches a maximum as a function of Borel mass, using a threshold s_0 fixed by requiring the "unbound" diagram limit to reproduce the sum of the constituent hadron masses.

## Key numbers

- m(pi-Sigma) = 1.424 GeV for I=0 at s_0 = 3.082 GeV^2, identified as the Lambda(1405) mass
- m(anti-K N) = 1.589 GeV for I=0 at s_0 = 3.852 GeV^2, identified as the Lambda(1600) mass
- For I=1 states at s_0 = 3.852 GeV^2: m(anti-K N) = 1.589 GeV, m(pi-Sigma) = 1.606 GeV, m(pi-Lambda) = 1.581 GeV
- Input parameters: quark condensate q = -(0.230 GeV)^3, strange quark condensate s = 0.8 q, strange quark mass m_s = 0.150 GeV

## Context

This work extends earlier QCD sum rule studies of Lambda(1405) using three-quark interpolating fields (Leinweber 1990; Kim and Lee 1997) and five-quark operators (Liu 1984) by instead using multiquark fields that explicitly couple to the pi-Sigma and anti-K N decay channels. The approach enables a systematic treatment of both I=0 and I=1 multiquark states, predicting masses for Lambda(1405), Lambda(1600), and Sigma(1620) within a unified framework. The author notes that isospin symmetry breaking effects and instanton corrections could improve the agreement with experimental values from the Particle Data Group (1998).
