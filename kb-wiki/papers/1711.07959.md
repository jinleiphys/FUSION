# Three-fluid Hydrodynamics-based Event Simulator Extended by UrQMD final State interactions (THESEUS) for FAIR-NICA-SPS-BES/RHIC energies

> We present a new event generator based on the three-fluid hydrodynamics (3FH) approach, followed by a particlization at the hydrodynamic decoupling surface and a subsequent UrQMD afterburner stage based on the microscopic UrQMD transport model that accounts for hadronic final state interactions. First results for Au+Au collisions are presented. The following topics are addressed: the directed flow, transverse-mass spectra, as well as rapidity distributions of protons, pions and kaons for two model equations of state, one with a first-order phase transition, the other with a crossover transition. Preliminary results on the femtoscopy are also discussed. We analyze the accuracy of reproduction of the 3FH results by the new event generator and the effect of the subsequent UrQMD afterburner stage.

## Key claim

The THESEUS event generator successfully reproduces the results of the underlying three-fluid hydrodynamics (3FH) model for bulk observables, but the hadronic afterburner stage qualitatively changes the directed flow of pions at lower collision energies (from flow to antiflow) while leaving proton flow and the baryon stopping signal for a first-order phase transition largely unaffected.

## Method

The simulation proceeds in two sequential steps: first, a numerical solution of the three-fluid hydrodynamics (3FH) is computed, treating projectile, target, and fireball fluids with coupled continuity and energy-momentum equations. Second, at the hydrodynamic decoupling surface (energy density threshold 0.4 GeV/fm^3), a Monte Carlo procedure samples hadrons from a grand canonical ensemble for each fluid droplet, and the UrQMD transport model simulates hadronic final state interactions including resonance decays, string excitations, and elastic scatterings.

## Key numbers

- Freeze-out energy density threshold: 0.4 GeV/fm^3
- Initial baryon density for fluid evolution: 0.15 fm^-3
- Initial energy density: 0.14 GeV/fm^3
- Transverse momentum spectra reproduced up to p_T = 2.2 GeV (limited by event statistics)
- Directed flow results presented at E_lab = 8 A GeV and 30 A GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Femtoscopy radii at sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 GeV for central Au+Au collisions
- Reliable curvature C_y determination requires 10^4 events for central/semi-central collisions and 10^5 events for peripheral collisions
- Energy scan range: sqrt(s_NN) = 4-11 GeV

## Context

This work extends the three-fluid hydrodynamics model of Ivanov (2005) by adding a particlization stage and the UrQMD afterburner, solving the previous lack of event-by-event particle output and hadronic final state interactions. The approach enables direct comparison with experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan, SPS program, and future FAIR and NICA experiments, and allows investigation of the "wiggle" in the midrapidity curvature of proton rapidity distributions proposed by Ivanov (2015) as a signal for first-order phase transitions. The pion shadowing effect was first demonstrated by Bass (1993) and is now shown to produce antiflow at lower energies, improving agreement with STAR data from Adamczyk (2014).
