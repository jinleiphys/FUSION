# Two-Baryon Systems with Twisted Boundary Conditions

> I derive the most general quantization condition for energy eigenvalues of two interacting baryons in a finite cubic volume when arbitrary twisted boundary conditions are imposed on their finite-volume wavefunctions. These quantization conditions are used, along with experimentally known scattering parameters of two-nucleon systems in the coupled 3S1-3D1 channels, to demonstrate the expected effect of a selection of twisted boundary conditions on the spectrum of the deuteron. It is shown that an order of magnitude reduction in the finite-volume corrections to the deuteron binding energy arise in moderate volumes with a proper choice of boundary conditions on the proton and the neutron, or by averaging the result of periodic and anti-periodic boundary conditions. These observations mean that a sub-percent accuracy can be achieved in the determination of the deuteron binding energy at (spatial) volumes as small as ~(9[fm])^3 in upcoming lattice QCD calculations of this nucleus with physical light-quark masses. The results reviewed in this talk are presented in details in Ref. [1].

# Two-Baryon Systems with Twisted Boundary Conditions

## Key claim
By imposing twisted boundary conditions (TBCs) on the proton and neutron individually, or by averaging results from periodic and anti-periodic boundary conditions, the finite-volume corrections to the deuteron binding energy can be reduced by an order of magnitude at moderate volumes, enabling sub-percent accuracy at spatial volumes as small as ~(9 fm)^3 in upcoming lattice QCD calculations with physical light-quark masses.

## Method
The author derives the most general quantization condition for two interacting spin-1/2 baryons in a finite cubic volume with arbitrary twisted boundary conditions, starting from the two-nucleon quantization condition of Briceno et al. (2013) and accounting for relativistic kinematics. The condition is expressed as a determinant involving the infinite-volume scattering amplitude and a finite-volume matrix whose elements depend on the twist angles through modified momentum modes. For the deuteron, the infinite-dimensional quantization condition is truncated to channels with orbital angular momentum L ≤ 2, and experimentally known phase shifts and mixing parameters extrapolated to negative energies are used to predict the finite-volume spectrum.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron infinite-volume binding energy: B_d^∞ = 2.224644(34) MeV
- At volumes as small as ~(9 fm)^3, the finite-volume energies from i-PBCs (twist angles φ = (π/2, π/2, π/2) with φ^p = -φ^n) are already very close to the infinite-volume value
- With i-PBCs, the leading volume corrections scale as ~e^{-2κL}/L, where κ is the deuteron binding momentum
- With averaging of periodic and anti-periodic boundary conditions, deviations scale as ~e^{-√2 κL}/L
- For periodic boundary conditions at ~(9 fm)^3, the deuteron in a finite volume is either unbound or twice as bound compared to infinite volume

## Context
This work extends the Luscher quantization condition framework (Luscher, 1986, 1990) to two-baryon systems with twisted boundary conditions, building on earlier work by Bedaque (2004) on S-wave two-nucleon systems with TBCs. The approach is complementary to the boosting method of Davoudi (2011) and Briceno (2013) for reducing finite-volume effects. The results enable lattice QCD calculations of the deuteron binding energy at physical quark masses without requiring prohibitively large volumes, and the quantization condition can also be used to constrain scattering parameters by providing additional kinematic points. The author notes that partial twisting (imposing TBCs only on valence quarks) is equivalent to full twisting in two-nucleon systems up to exponential corrections already neglected in the formalism, following the argument of Bedaque (2004).
