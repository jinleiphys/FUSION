# The role of $f_0(1710)$ in the $ϕω$ threshold peak of $J/Ψ\to γϕω$

> We study the process $J/Ψ\to γϕω$, measured by the BES experiment, where a neat peak close to the $ϕω$ threshold is observed and is associated to a scalar meson resonance around 1800 MeV. We make the observation that a scalar resonance coupling to $ϕω$ unavoidably couples strongly to $K \bar K$, but no trace of a peak is seen in the $K \bar K$ spectrum of the $J/Ψ\to γK \bar K$ at this energy. This serves us to rule out the interpretation of the observed peak as a signal of a new resonance. After this is done, a thorough study is performed on the production of a pair of vector mesons and how its interaction leads necessarily to a peak in the $J/Ψ\to γϕω$ reaction close to the $ϕω$ threshold, due to the dynamical generation of the $f_0(1710)$ resonance by the vector-vector interaction. We then show that both the shape obtained for the $ϕω$ mass distribution, as well as the strength are naturally reproduced by this mechanism. The work also explains why the $ϕω$ peak is observed in the BES experiment and not in other reactions, like $B^\pm\to K^\pm ϕω$ of Belle.

## Key claim

The peak observed near the ϕω threshold in J/Ψ → γϕω is not evidence of a new resonance around 1800 MeV, but is instead an unavoidable consequence of the dynamically generated f0(1710) resonance coupling to ϕω, combined with the absence of tree-level ϕω production in this reaction.

## Method

The authors use a coupled-channel unitary approach with dynamics from local hidden gauge Lagrangians to describe vector-vector interactions, which dynamically generates the f0(1710) resonance. They compute the J/Ψ → γϕω amplitude by summing over primary vector-vector production channels (ρρ, K*K̅*, ωω, ϕϕ) with SU(3) singlet weights, then rescattering through the f0(1710) to the ϕω final state, using the couplings and loop functions from Geng and Oset (2009). The ϕω invariant mass distribution is obtained from this amplitude including a small constant background, and the ratio of the integrated ϕω production to J/Ψ → γf0(1710) is compared with experimental branching fractions.

## Key numbers

- The f0(1710) resonance mass is taken as 1720 ± 6 MeV and width 135 ± 8 MeV from the PDG.
- The couplings of f0(1710) to vector channels (in MeV): ρρ = −1030 + i1086, K*K̅* = 7124 + i96, ωω = −1763 + i108, ϕϕ = −2493 − i204, ϕω = 3010 − i210.
- The ratio RΓ = Γ(J/Ψ → γϕω) / Γ(J/Ψ → γf0(1710)) is calculated as 0.15+0.07−0.04, compared with the experimental value 0.14+0.12−0.07 extracted from BES data.
- The experimental product branching fraction B(J/Ψ → γR) × B(R → ϕω) = (2.00 ± 0.08+1.38−1.00) × 10−4 from the BES experiment.
- The branching fraction B(J/Ψ → γf0(1710) → γKK̅) = (8.5+1.2−0.9) × 10−4 from the PDG.

## Context

This work extends the vector-vector interaction studies of Geng and Oset (2009) and the J/Ψ radiative decay formalism of Geng et al. (2010), which dynamically generate the f0(1710) resonance. It contradicts the interpretation of the BES peak as a new resonance (as claimed in the BES experiments) and also rules out the f0(1790) as an explanation, since f0(1790) has suppressed KK̅ decays while a ϕω-coupled resonance would have strong KK̅ decays. The work explains why the peak appears in BES but not in Belle's B± → K±ϕω reaction, due to the absence of tree-level ϕω production in J/Ψ decay versus its presence in weak decays. It enables a consistent interpretation of the f0(1710) as a dynamically generated vector-meson molecule without invoking new exotic states.
