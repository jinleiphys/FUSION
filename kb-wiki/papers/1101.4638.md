# Hadron spectra and elliptic flow for 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions from viscous hydrodynamics coupled to a Boltzmann cascade

> It is shown that the recently developed hybrid code VISHNU, which couples a relativistic viscous fluid dynamical description of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) with a microscopic Boltzmann cascade for the late hadronic rescattering stage, yields an excellent description of charged and identified hadron spectra and elliptic flow measured in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions at the Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC). Using initial conditions that incorporate event-by-event fluctuations in the initial shape and orientation of the collision fireball and values eta/s for the specific shear viscosity of the quark-gluon plasma that were recently extracted from the measured centrality dependence of the eccentricity-scaled, p_T-integrated charged hadron elliptic flow, we obtain universally good agreement between theory and experiment for the p_T-spectra and differential elliptic flow v_2(p_T) for both pions and protons at all collision centralities.

## Key claim

The hybrid code VISHNU, coupling viscous hydrodynamics for the quark-gluon plasma with a Boltzmann cascade for hadronic rescattering, yields excellent agreement with measured hadron spectra and elliptic flow in 200 A GeV Au+Au collisions, but only when using specific shear viscosity values (η/s)QGP of 0.08 for MC-Glauber initial conditions or 0.16 for MC-KLN initial conditions, with the uncertainty in initial fireball eccentricity completely dominating the error range in the phenomenological extraction of (η/s)QGP.

## Method

The authors used the VISHNU hybrid code, which describes the quark-gluon plasma stage with relativistic viscous hydrodynamics (assuming constant η/s in the temperature range Tc < T < 2Tc) and switches to the UrQMD microscopic Boltzmann cascade at a switching temperature Tsw = 165 MeV. Initial entropy density profiles were generated from Monte Carlo Glauber (MC-Glauber) and Monte Carlo fKLN (MC-KLN) models, incorporating event-by-event fluctuations in fireball shape and orientation by recentering and rotating each event before averaging. The initial entropy density was normalized to reproduce the measured charged hadron rapidity density in most central collisions, with the centrality dependence taken directly from each model.

## Key numbers

- Switching temperature from hydrodynamics to UrQMD cascade: Tsw = 165 MeV
- For MC-Glauber initial conditions, preferred (η/s)QGP = 0.08; for MC-KLN initial conditions, preferred (η/s)QGP = 0.16
- Parameter pairs (η/s, cτ0) used: (0, 0.4 fm), (0.08, 0.6 fm), (0.16, 0.9 fm), and (0.24, 1.2 fm)
- MC-Glauber and MC-KLN models give initial eccentricities that differ by up to 25% depending on centrality
- In the 0-5% centrality bin, STAR and PHENIX v2 data differ by 30%
- For MC-Glauber model, a two-component entropy production model with hard fraction δ = 0.14 was used
- Theoretical proton spectra are uniformly about 50% larger than PHENIX data but agree with their slope

## Context

This paper extends the work of Song et al. (2010) which extracted (η/s)QGP by comparing eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow data with VISHNU calculations, and provides the detailed documentation of the spectra and differential elliptic flow comparisons announced in that earlier work. The hybrid approach builds on earlier viscous hydrodynamic work by Romatschke (2007) and the UrQMD cascade model by Bass et al. (1998). The results enable future improvements in the accuracy of (η/s)QGP extraction, but the authors emphasize that such improvements cannot be achieved without better experimental and/or theoretical control over the initial fireball eccentricity, and they recommend against extracting (η/s)QGP directly from pT-differential elliptic flow due to interdependencies with hadron spectra and δf corrections.
