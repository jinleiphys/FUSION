# Role of chiral symmetry in the nucleon excitation spectrum

> The origin of the low-lying nature of the $N$*(1440), or Roper resonance, has been the subject of significant interest for many years, including several investigations using lattice QCD. The majority of lattice studies have not observed a low-lying excited state energy level in the region of the Roper resonance. However, it has been claimed that chiral symmetry could play an important role in our understanding of this resonance. The purpose of this study is to systematically examine the role of chiral symmetry in the low-lying nucleon spectrum by directly comparing the clover and overlap fermion actions. To ensure any differences in results are attributable to the choice of fermion action, simulations are performed on the same set of gauge field configurations at matched pion masses. Correlation matrix techniques are employed to determine the excitation energy of the first positive-parity excited state for each action. The clover and overlap actions show a remarkable level of agreement. We do not find any evidence that fermion action chiral symmetry plays a significant role in understanding the Roper resonance on the lattice.

## Key claim

The authors find no evidence that the chiral symmetry properties of the fermion action play a significant role in determining the low-lying nucleon excitation spectrum on the lattice; the clover (nonchiral) and overlap (chirally symmetric) actions produce statistically equivalent results for the ground and first positive-parity excited state masses when simulations are performed on identical gauge configurations at matched pion masses.

## Method

The study compares the nonperturbatively improved clover fermion action (which explicitly breaks chiral symmetry) with the overlap fermion action (which satisfies the Ginsparg-Wilson relation and thus an exact lattice-deformed chiral symmetry). Both simulations are performed on the same set of 100 PACS-CS 2+1-flavor gauge field configurations at a lattice spacing of a = 0.0961 fm, with three matched valence pion masses. A 4×4 correlation matrix is constructed from Gaussian-smeared interpolating fields (16, 35, 100, and 200 sweeps) and analyzed using standard variational correlation matrix techniques to extract the ground and first positive-parity excited state masses for each action.

## Context

This work directly addresses the claim by the χQCD Collaboration (Liu et al., 2014, 2016) that the use of overlap fermions with exact chiral symmetry is essential for observing a low-lying Roper-like excited state on the lattice. The results contradict that claim by showing that clover and overlap actions produce statistically identical spectra when compared on the same gauge configurations with matched pion masses and identical analysis techniques. The study extends previous lattice investigations of the nucleon excitation spectrum (Mahbub et al., 2010, 2013; Kiratidis et al., 2015, 2016; Alexandrou et al., 2014; Edwards et al., 2011, 2012) and suggests that the origin of the Roper resonance discrepancy lies elsewhere, motivating further work with multiparticle interpolating fields and the Luscher formalism to connect finite-volume lattice results to infinite-volume resonance properties.
