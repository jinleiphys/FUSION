# A New Approach To Relativistic Gaussian Basis Functions: Theory And Applications

> We present a new hybrid method to solve the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Roothan equations where the one- and two-electron radial integrals are evaluated numerically by defining the basis functions on a grid. This procedure reduces the computational costs in the evaluation of two-electron radial integrals. The orbitals generated by this method are employed to compute the ionization potentials, excitation energies and oscillator strengths of alkali-metal atoms and elements of group IIIA through second order many-body perturbation theor and other correlated theories.

## Key claim

The authors present a hybrid method for solving relativistic Hartree-Fock-Roothan equations that combines finite basis set expansion with numerical integration of radial integrals, claiming this reduces computational cost and improves accuracy for heavy atoms, but the actual validation is limited to second-order many-body perturbation theory (MBPT) results for alkali-metal and group IIIA elements, with the caveat that single-reference MBPT is inadequate for group IIIA due to quasi-degeneracy effects.

## Method

The large and small components of Dirac spinors are expanded in Gaussian-type orbitals (GTOs) with geometric exponents (α₀=0.0052, β=2.75), where the small component is generated by imposing kinetic balance. Unlike conventional analytical approaches, all one- and two-electron radial integrals are evaluated numerically on a grid, reducing the two-electron integral evaluation from N(N+1)/2 operations to N_c operations (where N is basis size and N_c is number of occupied orbitals). The resulting orbitals are used in second-order MBPT calculations of ionization potentials, excitation energies, and oscillator strengths.

## Key numbers

- Basis sizes range from 20s15p15d15f (Lithium) to 28s24p20d16f10g (Francium)
- Koopmans' theorem ionization potential accuracy for s₁/₂ states degrades from 1% for Lithium 2s₁/₂ to 12% for Francium 7s₁/₂
- Second-order MBPT ionization potentials for Cesium are comparable to CCSD results of Eliav et al.
- For alkali-metal atoms, second-order MBPT estimates s₁/₂→p₁/₂ transition energies more accurately than s₁/₂→p₃/₂ transitions
- Second-order MBPT provides less accurate ionization potentials for group IIIA elements compared to alkali-metal atoms

## Context

This work extends the finite basis set expansion (FBSE) method for relativistic Dirac-Fock calculations, which was previously developed using Slater-type orbitals by Grant et al. and Gaussian-type orbitals by Ishikawa et al., Matsuoka et al., and Clementi et al. The hybrid numerical-analytical approach reduces computational scaling compared to conventional FBSE methods. The computed results are compared with B-spline basis calculations by Johnson et al. and with coupled cluster calculations by Eliav et al., showing competitive accuracy for heavy alkalis. The authors identify that single-reference MBPT fails for group IIIA elements due to quasi-degeneracy between ns²np and np³ configurations, requiring multi-reference MBPT treatment, which they indicate is under development.
