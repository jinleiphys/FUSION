# Symmetry Constraints on Pion Valence Structure

> The profile of the pion valence quark distribution function (DF) remains controversial. Working from the concepts of QCD effective charges and generalised parton distributions, we show that since the pion elastic electromagnetic form factor is well approximated by a monopole, then, at large light-front momentum fraction, the pion valence quark DF is a convex function described by a large-$x$ power law that is practically consistent with expectations based on quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper shows that if the pion elastic electromagnetic form factor is well approximated by a monopole, then at large light-front momentum fraction the pion valence quark distribution function is a convex function described by a large-x power law that is practically consistent with quantum chromodynamics expectations, but the authors note that the monopole approximation receives small modifications at large Q² and consequently on the endpoint domains x ≈ 0,1.

## Method

The authors use the all-orders (AO) evolution scheme based on a QCD effective charge to define a hadron scale ζ_H where all pion properties are carried by valence degrees of freedom. They combine the overlap representation of generalised parton distributions (GPDs) with the monopole approximation to the pion form factor F_π(Q²) = 1/(1+Q²/m_M²) with m_M ≈ 0.73 GeV, and exploit G-parity symmetry and GPD symmetry properties to derive a master equation that determines the pion valence quark GPD and distribution function without dynamical input.

## Key numbers

- The monopole mass parameter m_M ≈ 0.73 GeV approximates the pion elastic electromagnetic form factor on 0 < Q²/GeV² ≤ 2.45
- At the hadron scale, the valence quark distribution function endpoint behaviour is u_π(x) ≈ 3.163 (1-x) ln(1/(1-x)) + ... for x ≈ 1
- The pion valence quark distribution amplitude has ⟨ξ²⟩_φ_π = 0.246, compared to ⟨ξ²⟩_φ_asy = 0.2 for the asymptotic DA
- At ζ_5 = 5.2 GeV, the symmetry-based result yields large-x power law β = 1.99 ± 0.06 with coefficient c = 1.88 ∓ 0.09
- The CSM prediction at ζ_5 yields β = 2.79 ± 0.08 with coefficient c = 7.54 ∓ 0.07
- The valence quark momentum fraction at ζ_5 is ⟨x⟩_u_π = 0.21(1)

## Context

This work extends the all-orders evolution approach previously applied to pion and kaon DFs by Cui et al. (2020), proton and hyperon DFs by Chang et al. (2022) and Lu et al. (2022), and gravitational form factors by Yao et al. (2024). It provides a model-independent, parameter-free prediction that confirms continuum Schwinger function method results from Ding et al. (2019) and Cui et al. (2020), and favours the analysis of E615 Drell-Yan data by Aicher et al. (2010) and Chang et al. (2014) that includes soft gluon resummation. The results motivate future experiments by the AMBER Collaboration and potential measurements at Jefferson Laboratory or an electron ion collider.
