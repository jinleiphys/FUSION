# Superscaling in Nuclei: A Search for Scaling Function Beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model

> We construct a scaling function $f(ψ^{\prime})$ for inclusive electron scattering from nuclei within the Coherent Density Fluctuation Model (CDFM). The latter is a natural extension to finite nuclei of the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model within which the scaling variable $ψ^{\prime}$ was introduced by Donnelly and collaborators. The calculations show that the high-momentum components of the nucleon momentum distribution in the CDFM and their similarity for different nuclei lead to quantitative description of the superscaling in nuclei. The results are in good agreement with the experimental data for different transfer momenta showing superscaling for negative values of $ψ^{\prime}$, including those smaller than -1.

# Superscaling in Nuclei: A Search for Scaling Function Beyond the Relativistic Fermi Gas Model

## Key claim
The Coherent Density Fluctuation Model (CDFM) can quantitatively describe superscaling in inclusive electron scattering for negative values of the scaling variable ψ′ down to ψ′ < -1, a region where the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model predicts zero scaling function. The agreement depends on the similarity of high-momentum tails of nucleon momentum distributions across different nuclei, which in the CDFM arises from short-range and tensor correlations.

## Method
The authors construct the scaling function f(ψ′) as a weighted superposition of RFG scaling functions for pieces of nuclear matter with different densities, using the CDFM weight function |F(x)|² derived from experimental charge density distributions via Eq. (23). The CDFM Fermi momentum k_F for each nucleus is calculated consistently from Eq. (31) rather than treated as a fitting parameter. The scaling function is evaluated for transfer momenta q = 500, 1000, and 1650 MeV/c for nuclei from ⁴He to ¹⁹⁷Au.

## Key numbers
- CDFM Fermi momenta: k_F = 1.201 fm⁻¹ for ⁴He, 1.200 fm⁻¹ for ¹²C, 1.267 fm⁻¹ for ²⁷Al, 1.270 fm⁻¹ for ⁵⁶Fe, and 1.335 fm⁻¹ for ¹⁹⁷Au (Table 1).
- Density parameters for ⁴He: half-radius R = 1.710 fm, diffuseness b = 0.290 fm; for ¹²C: R = 2.470 fm, b = 0.420 fm (Table 1).
- For ¹⁹⁷Au, the standard diffuseness b = 0.449 fm gave poor agreement; using an effective b = 1.0 fm restored agreement with superscaling data.
- The CDFM scaling function reproduces experimental data at q = 1000 MeV/c for ⁴He, ¹²C, ²⁷Al, and ¹⁹⁷Au (with b = 1.0 fm) for ψ′ from 0 down to ψ′ < -1.
- At q = 1650 MeV/c, CDFM results for ⁴He and ¹⁹⁷Au agree with data; at q = 500 MeV/c, results for ¹²C agree with data in the interval 500-600 MeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the superscaling concept introduced by Donnelly and collaborators (1999) beyond the RFG model, which fails for ψ′ ≤ -1. The CDFM, developed by Antonov and collaborators (1979, 1985, 1988, 1993), provides a natural extension to finite nuclei by incorporating realistic density distributions. The results confirm that the similarity of high-momentum components of nucleon momentum distributions across nuclei, known from studies of short-range and tensor correlations (Antonov 1988, 1993), is the physical origin of superscaling. This enables future work to define the CDFM weight function directly from phenomenological or theoretical momentum distributions rather than from charge densities.
