# Incorporating Brueckner-Hartree-Fock correlations in the Density Matrix Expansion approach

> Recently, a microscopically motivated nuclear energy density functional was derived by applying the density matrix expansion to the Hartree-Fock (HF) energy obtained from long-range chiral effective field theory two- and three-nucleon interactions. However, the HF approach cannot account for all many-body correlations. One class of correlations is included by Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) theory, which gives an improved definition of the one-body HF potential by replacing the interaction by a reaction matrix $G$. In this paper, we find that the difference between the $G$-matrix and the nucleon-nucleon potential $V_{\mathrm{NN}}$ can be well accounted for by a truncated series of contact terms. This is consistent with renormalization group decoupling generating a series of counterterms as short-distance physics is integrated out. The coefficients $C_{n}$ of the power series expansion $\sum C_{n}q^{n}$ for the counterterms are examined for two potentials at different renormalization group resolutions and at a range of densities. The success of this expansion for $G-V_{\mathrm{NN}}$ means we can apply the density matrix expansion at the HF level with low-momentum interactions and density-dependent zero-range interactions to model BHF correlations.

## Key claim

The difference between the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) reaction matrix \(G\) and the Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG)-evolved nucleon-nucleon potential \(V_{\mathrm{SRG}}\) can be accurately represented by a truncated series of contact counterterms, enabling the modeling of BHF correlations at the Hartree-Fock level using low-momentum interactions plus density-dependent zero-range interactions.

## Method

The authors solved the Bethe-Goldstone equation self-consistently to obtain the \(G\)-matrix for nuclear matter at various Fermi momenta, using the AV18 and N3LO nucleon-nucleon potentials evolved via the SRG to different flow parameters (\(\lambda = 10, 3.0, 2.0, 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\)). They then performed a chi-squared fitting procedure to determine the coefficients \(C_n\) of a power series expansion \(\sum C_n q^n\) that minimizes the difference between the \(G\)-matrix and the sum \(V_{\mathrm{SRG}} + V_{\mathrm{CT}}\) for each partial-wave channel, with a momentum cutoff \(\Lambda\) set to the Fermi momentum \(k_{\mathrm{F}}\).

## Key numbers

* The \(G\)-matrix was evaluated at saturation density with \(k_{\mathrm{F}} = 1.3\) fm\(^{-1}\) for correlation plots comparing AV18 and N3LO potentials.
* SRG evolution to \(\lambda = 1.5\) fm\(^{-1}\) caused the counterterm coefficients (particularly \(C_0\) in the \(^1\)S\(_0\) channel) to decay to zero rapidly with Fermi momentum, compared to non-zero values at \(\lambda = 10\) fm\(^{-1}\).
* Coefficients beyond \(C_4\) were found to be one order smaller than \(C_4\) and could be ignored; \(C_6\) was typically negligible.
* The density-dependent Skyrme parameters \(t_0(\rho)\) and \(x_0(\rho)\) were constructed from the leading \(C_0\) terms in the \(^1\)S\(_0\) and \(^3\)S\(_1\) channels at \(\lambda = 3.0\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the program of constructing microscopically motivated nuclear energy density functionals via the density matrix expansion (DME), previously applied at the Hartree-Fock level by Dyhdalo et al. (2017) using chiral potentials. It directly tests the interpretation that refitting Skyrme parameters to data approximates the short-range part of the \(G\)-matrix, as suggested by Gebremariam et al. (2010, 2011) and implemented by Bogner et al. (2011). The results enable the incorporation of BHF ladder correlations into DME-based functionals without solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation, by using SRG-evolved potentials supplemented by density-dependent zero-range interactions.
