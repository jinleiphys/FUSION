# Dilepton-tagged jets in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions: A case study

> We study the A+B -> l+ l- + jet +X process in nucleus-nucleus collisions at relativistic energies. The dilepton as well as the jet will pass through the matter produced in such collisions. The recoiling dilepton will carry information about the kinematical features of the jet, and will thus prove to be a very effective tool in isolating in-medium effects such as energy-loss and fragmentation function modifications. We estimate the contributions due to correlated charm and bottom decay and we identify a window where they are small as compared to pairs from the NLO Drell-Yan process.

## Key claim

Dilepton-tagged jets from the NLO Drell-Yan process can provide a clean measurement of the energy of the progenitor parton in nucleus-nucleus collisions, but only for large dilepton transverse momenta where the background from correlated charm and bottom decays becomes small relative to the NLO Drell-Yan signal.

## Method

The authors calculate the cross sections for the NLO Drell-Yan processes (Compton scattering qg → qγ* and annihilation qq̅ → gγ*) and for heavy-quark pair production at NLO, using CTEQ4M parton distribution functions with factorization and renormalization scales set to μF = μR = M² + qT². The correlated semi-leptonic decays of charm and bottom mesons are estimated by fragmenting heavy quarks to D and B mesons and modeling their decays with branching ratios of 12% and 10% respectively, with results verified against PYTHIA and the parametrizations of Gavin et al. (1997). The cross sections are scaled by the nuclear thickness function TAB(b=0) to obtain rates for central A+B collisions at RHIC (√s = 200 GeV) and LHC (√s = 5500 GeV).

## Key numbers

- Total cross sections for charm production in pp collisions: LO = 59.3 μb, NLO = 145.9 μb at √s = 200 GeV; LO = 1054 μb, NLO = 3362 μb at √s = 5500 GeV
- Total cross sections for bottom production in pp collisions: LO = 0.84 μb, NLO = 1.67 μb at √s = 200 GeV; LO = 79.7 μb, NLO = 174.4 μb at √s = 5500 GeV
- The rapidity correlation between dilepton and recoiling jet has a width of about 2 units of rapidity
- The intrinsic transverse momentum spread of partons is approximately 400 MeV (Gaussian width)
- Typical calorimeter geometries have π⁰-photon shower merging limits as low as 20 GeV/c
- Anticipated total sampled event sizes of approximately 10¹⁰ events at RHIC

## Context

This work extends the concept of photon-tagged jet measurements proposed by Wang, Huang, and Sarcevic (1996) to dilepton tagging, addressing the severe background from π⁰ decay photons that limits real-photon tagging at low transverse momenta. The authors identify that the correlated charm and bottom decay background, which dominates thermal dilepton studies as shown by Gavin et al. (1997), becomes negligible at the large transverse momenta relevant for jet-quenching studies. The method enables precise determination of parton energy loss in quark-gluon plasma, complementing W/Z-tagged jet studies proposed for the CMS experiment and addressing the varying predictions for dE/dx dependence on parton energy from theoretical work in the preceding decade.
