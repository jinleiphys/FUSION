# Shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Kr isotopes: Constraints on the single-particle spectrum of self-consistent mean-field models from collective excitations

> We discuss shape coexistence in the neutron-deficient Kr72-Kr78 isotopes in the framework of configuration mixing calculations of particle-number and angular-momentum projected axial mean-field states obtained from self-consistent calculations with the Skyrme interaction SLy6 and a density-dependent pairing interaction. While our calculation reproduces qualitatively and quantitatively many of the global features of these nuclei, such as coexistence of prolate and oblate shapes, their strong mixing at low angular momentum, and the deformation of collective bands, the ordering of our calculated low-lying levels is at variance with experiment. We analyse the role of the single-particle spectrum of the underlying mean-field for the spectrum of collective excitations.

## Key claim

The Skyrme SLy6-based configuration mixing calculation reproduces many global features of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Kr isotopes (such as prolate/oblate coexistence and strong mixing at low angular momentum), but the ordering of the calculated low-lying levels is at variance with experiment, indicating that the single-particle spectrum of the underlying mean-field model imposes constraints on the collective excitation spectrum that are not fully consistent with data.

## Method

The authors performed configuration mixing calculations of particle-number and angular-momentum projected axial mean-field states obtained from self-consistent Hartree-Fock+BCS calculations with the Skyrme interaction SLy6 and a density-dependent zero-range pairing interaction. The mean-field states were generated on a three-dimensional mesh in coordinate space with a constraint on the axial quadrupole moment, then projected onto good particle number and angular momentum, and finally mixed using the generator coordinate method (GCM) by solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation. The same effective interaction was used for all steps, with the pairing strength set to -1000 MeV fm³ for both protons and neutrons.

## Context

This work extends previous self-consistent mean-field studies of shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Kr isotopes using Skyrme (Bonche 1985) and Gogny (Giraud 1989) interactions, as well as relativistic Lagrangians (Lalazissis 1995). It also builds on the same angular-momentum projected GCM method previously applied to shape coexistence in neutron-deficient Pb isotopes (Duguet 2003, Bender 2003). The results enable direct comparison with experimental data from Coulomb excitation experiments (Becker 2006, Clement 2005, Görgen 2005) and provide constraints for improving the single-particle spectra of self-consistent mean-field models, particularly the relative positions of the p₃/₂, p₁/₂, f₅/₂, and g₉/₂ levels.
