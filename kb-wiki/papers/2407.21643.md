# Weak rates in strongly coupled cold quark matter

> The rates of flavor-changing weak processes are crucial in determining the conditions of beta equilibrium in neutron stars and mergers, influencing the damping of oscillations, the stability of rotating pulsars, and the emission of gravitational waves. We derive a formula for these rates at nonzero temperature, to leading order in the Fermi coupling and exact in the QCD coupling. Utilizing a simple phenomenological holographic model dual to QCD, we study massless unpaired quark matter at high densities. We numerically compute the rate for small deviations from beta equilibrium and derive an analytic approximation for small temperatures. Our findings reveal that, compared to the perturbative result, the rate is suppressed by logarithmic factors of the temperature.

## Key claim

The authors derive a non-perturbative formula for flavor-changing weak reaction rates in strongly coupled quark matter, exact in the QCD coupling to leading order in the Fermi coupling, and find that at strong coupling the rate is suppressed by logarithmic factors of the temperature compared to the perturbative result, though they caution that the holographic model used is a simplified phenomenological approximation and not a first-principles derivation from QCD.

## Method

The authors derive Ward identities for flavor currents in the presence of the Fermi interaction, expressing the weak rates in terms of thermal correlators of flavor currents that factorize into products of two-point functions at leading order in the Fermi coupling and at large N_c. They then compute these flavor current correlators using a simple bottom-up holographic model dual to QCD (the Erlich-Katz-Son-Stephanov model) for massless unpaired quark matter at high density and nonzero temperature, extracting the spectral functions needed for the rate formula.

## Key numbers

- The weak rate coefficient for the process u + e -> d + nu is given by lambda_{ud}^e = 4 G_F^2 cos^2(theta_C) gamma_{ue -> d nu}, where G_F is Fermi's constant and theta_C is the Cabibbo angle
- The rate for the process u + e -> s + nu is given by lambda_{us}^e = 4 G_F^2 sin^2(theta_C) gamma_{ue -> s nu}
- The rate for the process ud -> su is given by lambda_{ds} = 4 G_F^2 sin^2(theta_C) cos^2(theta_C) gamma_{ud -> su}
- The thermal distribution factor appearing in the rate formula is n_{f;ab}(k_0) = 1/(exp(beta(k_0 + mu_{f^a} - mu_{f^b})) - 1), where beta = 1/T

## Context

This work extends the perturbative calculations of weak rates by Heiselberg (1986, 1992) and Madsen (1993), which used tree-level scattering matrix elements, to a non-perturbative setting using holographic methods. The authors aim to improve estimates of bulk viscosity in neutron star matter, which was previously computed holographically by Cruz Rojas (2024) but relied on perturbative weak rates. The results enable future computation of weak rates and bulk viscosity in more refined holographic models such as the D3-D7 model, the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, and the V-QCD model, which could serve as additional constraints on neutron star models.
