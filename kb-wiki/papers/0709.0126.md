# Rapidity-dependent chemical potentials in a statistical approach

> We present a single-freeze-out model with thermal and geometric parameters dependent on the position within the fireball and use it to describe the rapidity and transverse-momentum spectra of pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons measured at RHIC at 200 GeV} by BRAHMS. THERMINATOR is used to perform the necessary simulation, which includes all resonance decays. The result of the fit to the data is the expected growth of the baryon and strange chemical potentials with the spatial rapidityα_\parallel. The value of the baryon chemical potential at α_\parallel ~ 3 is about 200 MeV, i.e. lies in the range of the highest SPS energies. The chosen geometry of the fireball has a decreasing transverse size as the magnitude of α_\parallel is increased, which also corresponds to decreasing transverse flow. The strange chemical potential obtained from the fit to the K+/K- ratio is such that the local strangeness density in the fireball is compatible with zero. The resulting rapidity spectra of net protons are described qualitatively within the statistical approach. As a result of our study, the knowledge of the ``topography'' of the fireball is acquired, allowing for other analyses and predictions.

# Rapidicity-dependent chemical potentials in a statistical approach

## Key claim

The baryon and strange chemical potentials grow with spatial rapidity α∥, reaching values at α∥ ~ 3 comparable to those at the highest SPS energies (√sNN = 17 GeV), but the temperature is held fixed at 165 MeV throughout the fireball because the universal freeze-out curve gives a practically constant T in the rapidity range studied (|y| ≤ 3.3).

## Method

The authors extend the single-freeze-out model of Broniowski (2001) to boost-non-invariant systems by allowing the freeze-out hypersurface, collective flow, and thermal parameters to depend on spatial rapidity α∥. The fireball transverse size decreases with |α∥| according to a Gaussian parameterization controlled by Δ, and the chemical potentials μB, μS, and μI3 are parameterized as μi(α∥) = μi(0)[1 + Ai α∥^2.4]. The THERMINATOR event generator performs the full simulation including all resonance decays, and the model parameters are fitted to BRAHMS data at √sNN = 200 GeV for pions, kaons, protons, and antiprotons.

## Key numbers

- Baryon chemical potential at α∥ ~ 3: about 200 MeV, comparable to the highest SPS energy fit (μB ≃ 230 MeV at √sNN = 17 GeV)
- Baryon chemical potential at mid-rapidity: μB(0) = 19 MeV, lower than earlier mid-rapidity fits (26 MeV) from Broniowski (2001) and Braun-Munzinger (2001)
- Strange chemical potential at mid-rapidity: μS(0) = 4.8 MeV, growing to 50 MeV at α∥ = 3
- Isospin chemical potential at mid-rapidity: μI3(0) = -1 MeV
- Temperature fixed at T = 165 MeV throughout
- Geometric parameters: τ = 9.74 fm, ρmax(z=0) = 7.74 fm, Δ = 3.33
- Ratio μB/μS varies weakly with rapidity, from ~4 at mid-rapidity to ~3.5 at larger rapidities

## Context

This work extends the boost-invariant single-freeze-out model of Broniowski (2001, 2002) to describe rapidity-dependent observables at RHIC, where previous 4π studies at lower energies (SIS, AGS) and mid-rapidity analyses at RHIC had not addressed the spatial variation of thermal parameters. The results enable predictions for net-proton spectra and other observables at forward rapidity, and the authors note that the naive extraction of chemical potentials from particle ratios (p/p̄, K+/K−) works surprisingly well at RHIC energies but should not work at lower SPS energies. The finding that local strangeness density is compatible with zero at all α∥ is obtained independently from fitting the chemical potentials to data, without imposing that condition.
