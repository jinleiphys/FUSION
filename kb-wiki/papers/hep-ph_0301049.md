# Light meson resonances from unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory

> We report on our recent progress in the generation of resonant behavior in unitarized meson-meson scattering amplitudes obtained from Chiral Perturbation Theory. These amplitudes provide simultaneously a remarkable description of the resonance region up to 1.2 GeV as well as the low energy region, since they respect the chiral symmetry expansion. By studying the position of the poles in these amplitudes it is possible to determine the mass and width of the associated resonances, as well as to get a hint on possible classification schemes, that could be of interest for the spectroscopy of the scalar sector.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the resonant behavior observed in meson-meson scattering up to 1.2 GeV can be generated from unitarized one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory amplitudes without explicitly including any resonances, but the scalar pole positions (particularly for the broad sigma and kappa states) carry large uncertainties and depend on the choice of decay constants used in the amplitude normalization.

## Method

The authors unitarized the complete set of one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory meson-meson scattering amplitudes using the coupled-channel Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM), which enforces exact partial wave unitarity while preserving the chiral expansion at low energies. They performed fits to the available meson-meson scattering data using MINUIT, adding systematic errors of 1%, 3%, or 5% to account for unquantified experimental uncertainties, and then analytically continued the resulting amplitudes to the second Riemann sheet to extract pole positions.

## Key numbers

- The IAM fit describes meson-meson scattering data up to 1.2 GeV, with threshold parameters such as the S-wave pi-pi scattering length a_{0,0} = 0.231^{+0.003}_{-0.006} (experiment: 0.26 ± 0.05) and the P-wave pi-pi scattering length a_{1,1} = 0.0377 ± 0.0007 (experiment: 0.038 ± 0.002)
- The rho(770) pole from the IAM II fit: sqrt(s_pole) = 754 ± 18 - i (74 ± 10) MeV, compared to the PDG mass of 771 ± 0.7 MeV and width 149 ± 0.9 MeV
- The sigma (f0(600)) pole from IAM II: sqrt(s_pole) = 440 ± 8 - i (212 ± 15) MeV, consistent with the PDG range of (400-1200) - i (300-500) MeV
- The K*(892) pole from IAM II: sqrt(s_pole) = 889 ± 13 - i (24 ± 4) MeV, compared to PDG mass 891.66 ± 0.26 MeV and width 50.8 ± 0.9 MeV
- The f0(980) pole from IAM II: sqrt(s_pole) = 973 - i 11 MeV, with large asymmetric errors (+39/-127 for real part, +189/-11 for imaginary part)
- The chiral parameters L_1 through L_8 from the IAM fits are compatible with previous determinations; for example L_3 = (-2.79 ± 0.14) × 10^{-3} in IAM I compared to the ChPT value of (-3.5 ± 1.1) × 10^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the earlier approximate coupled-channel IAM calculations of Oller and Oset (1997), which used only leading-order ChPT plus dominant s-channel loops with a cutoff regularization, by incorporating the complete one-loop ChPT amplitudes with proper dimensional regularization and renormalization. The full one-loop calculation allows comparison of the fitted chiral parameters L_i with those from other low-energy processes such as K_{l4} decays (Bijnens, 2001), demonstrating compatibility. The results enable future studies of the nature of scalar mesons and their classification, particularly regarding whether states like the sigma and kappa are genuine q\bar{q} resonances or have other origins such as meson-meson molecules or glueballs.
