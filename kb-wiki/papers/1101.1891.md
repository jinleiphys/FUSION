# Resonant three-body physics in two spatial dimensions

> We discuss the three-body properties of identical bosons exhibiting large scattering length in two spatial dimensions. Within an effective field theory for resonant interactions, we calculate the leading non-universal corrections from the two-body effective range to bound-state and scattering observables. In particular, we compute the three-body binding energies, the boson-dimer scattering properties, and the three-body recombination rate for finite energies. We find significant effective range effects in the vicinity of the unitary limit. The implications of this result for future experiments are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The leading non-universal corrections from the two-body effective range to three-body observables in two spatial dimensions are large, with significant effects appearing already for small values of the dimensionless effective range parameter \( |r^2 \kappa^2| \sim 10^{-4} \) to \( 10^{-3} \), contrary to the expectation that the unitary limit would be approached quickly.

## Method

The authors use an effective field theory for identical bosons with large scattering length in two spatial dimensions, including a dynamical dimer field to incorporate effective range effects. They solve the integral equation for the boson-dimer scattering amplitude in the S-wave channel, projecting onto bound states and scattering observables. For positive effective range, they employ a wave number cutoff to respect the Wigner causality bound and vary the cutoff to estimate theoretical uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Universal three-body binding energies in the unitary limit: \( E_3^{(1)} = 1.2704091(1) \, E_2 \) and \( E_3^{(0)} = 16.522688(1) \, E_2 \), where \( E_2 \) is the dimer binding energy.
- For \( r^2 \kappa^2 = -0.01 \), the three-body binding energies shift to \( E_3^{(1)} = 1.145(1) \, E_2 \) and \( E_3^{(0)} = 10.578(1) \, E_2 \), representing approximately 10% and 30% shifts respectively.
- Linear coefficients for the effective range correction to binding energies: \( E_3^{(0)}/E_2 = 16.522688(1) + 28000(5000) \, r^2 \kappa^2 \) and \( E_3^{(1)}/E_2 = 1.2704091(1) + 540(80) \, r^2 \kappa^2 \) for negative effective range.
- Universal atom-dimer scattering length: \( \kappa a_{AD} = 2.614(1) \), corresponding to \( a_{AD} = 2.328(1) \, a \) and \( \ln(a_{AD}/a) = 0.845(1) \).
- For \( r^2 \kappa^2 = -0.01 \), the atom-dimer scattering length shifts to \( \kappa a_{AD} = 3.540(1) \); for \( r^2 \kappa^2 = 0.01 \), it shifts to \( \kappa a_{AD} = 2.713(1) \).
- The universal atom-dimer effective range is \( (\kappa r_{AD})^2 = 4.0(2) \); for \( r^2 \kappa^2 = -0.01 \) it becomes \( 7.4(2) \), and for \( r^2 \kappa^2 = 0.01 \) it becomes \( 2.9(2) \).

## Context

This work extends the universal three-body calculations in two dimensions by Bruch and Tjon (1973) and Son (2005) to include the leading non-universal corrections from the two-body effective range. The results show that effective range effects are much larger than previously expected, consistent with the Monte Carlo calculations of Blume (2005) for Lennard-Jones and helium potentials. The calculation enables experimental searches for three-body states in two-dimensional Bose gases through zero-energy scattering resonances, similar to Efimov states in three dimensions, and provides a basis for interpreting future experiments on lower-dimensional ultracold gases.
