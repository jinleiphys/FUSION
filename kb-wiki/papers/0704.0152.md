# Kinetic equation for finite systems of fermions with pairing

> The solutions of the Wigner-transformed time-dependent Hartree--Fock--Bogoliubov equations are studied in the constant-$Δ$ approximation. This approximation is known to violate particle-number conservation. As a consequence, the density fluctuation and the longitudinal response function given by this approximation contain spurious contributions. A simple prescription for restoring both local and global particle-number conservation is proposed. Explicit expressions for the eigenfrequencies of the correlated systems and for the density response function are derived and it is shown that the semiclassical analogous of the quantum single--particle spectrum has an excitation gap of $2Δ$, in agreement with the quantum result. The collective response is studied for a simplified form of the residual interaction.

## Key claim

The constant-Δ approximation to the Wigner-transformed time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations violates both local and global particle-number conservation, but a simple prescription based on the continuity equation can eliminate the spurious contributions to the density fluctuation and longitudinal response function, yielding a semiclassical excitation spectrum with a gap of 2Δ that agrees with the quantum result.

## Method

The authors start from the TDHFB equations for the Wigner transforms of the normal density matrix ρ and pair correlation function κ, reformulated in terms of even and odd components of ρ. They linearize these equations around equilibrium in the constant-Δ approximation, solve them using action-angle variables, and derive expressions for the phase-space density fluctuations and eigenfrequencies. For one-dimensional and spherical systems, they identify spurious contributions to the density response that violate the continuity equation, and propose replacing the density fluctuation calculated directly from the even part of δf with one obtained from the continuity equation using the odd part (transverse response). The collective response is then studied for a separable multipole-multipole residual interaction.

## Key numbers

- The minimum value of the frequency Ω(ε) = 2E(ε)/ħ is 2Δ/ħ, giving an excitation gap of 2Δ in the correlated system.
- The pairing gap is taken as Δ ≈ 1 MeV.
- For the spherical cavity calculations, the radius is R = 1.2 A^(1/3) fm with A = 208, and the Fermi energy is ε_F ≈ 33.33 MeV.
- The small parameter ε used to smooth the response function is 0.1 MeV.
- The collective response uses interaction strengths κ_2 = -1 × 10^(-3) MeV/fm^4 for quadrupole and κ_3 = -2 × 10^(-5) MeV/fm^6 for octupole channels.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical Vlasov equation approach of Brink, Dellafiore, and Matera (2000) to systems with pairing correlations, building on the TDHFB equations derived by Urban and Schuck (2006) for trapped fermion droplets. The constant-Δ approximation follows earlier work by Di Toro and Kolomietz (1993) in nuclear physics. The paper enables the study of pairing effects on giant resonances in heavy nuclei using a semiclassical framework, showing that for quadrupole and octupole channels in A=208 nuclei the effects are very small, with the main difference being a 2Δ gap in the low-energy strength function.
