# Microscopic calculation of the inclusive electron scattering structure function in O-16

> We calculate the charge form factor and the longitudinal structure function for $^{16}$O and compare with the available experimental data, up to a momentum transfer of 4 fm$^{-1}$. The ground state correlations are generated using the coupled cluster [exp(S}] method, together with the realistic v-18 NN interaction and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction. Center-of-mass corrections are dealt with by adding a center-of-mass Hamiltonian to the usual internal Hamiltonian, and by means of a many-body expansion for the computation of the observables measured in the center-of-mass system.

## Key claim

The coupled cluster [exp(S)] method, using the Argonne v18 NN interaction and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, can produce a charge form factor for O-16 in reasonable agreement with experimental data up to 4 fm^-1, but only after including center-of-mass corrections, meson-exchange current contributions, and a Distorted Wave Born Approximation treatment of Coulomb distortions.

## Method

The authors solve for the correlated ground state of O-16 using the coupled cluster expansion method, where the ground-state wave function is generated by applying an exponential cluster correlation operator to a shell-model Slater determinant reference state. The Hamiltonian includes the Argonne v18 two-nucleon potential and the Urbana IX three-nucleon interaction, with the three-body force treated via a density-dependent approach. Center-of-mass corrections are handled by adding a center-of-mass Hamiltonian to the internal Hamiltonian and using a many-body expansion for computing observables in the center-of-mass frame, with calculations carried out in a 50 hbar-omega configuration space.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of O-16: 7.54 MeV/nucleon (calculated without the center-of-mass Hamiltonian)
- Momentum transfer range for comparison with experimental data: up to 4 fm^-1
- The Argonne v18 potential fits pp and nn scattering data up to 350 MeV with chi-squared per datum near one
- Meson-exchange charge density contributions become measurable only for q > 2 fm^-1

## Context

This work extends the coupled cluster method, originally developed by the Bochum group (Kummel et al., 1978), to a medium-mass nucleus (A=16) beyond the A <= 7 systems accessible to Green's function Monte Carlo (Pudliner et al., 1997) and the A=3,4 systems treated by Fadeev and Correlated Hyperspherical Harmonics methods. The calculation enables future microscopic studies of other closed-shell nuclei in the p- and sd-shells using realistic interactions, and provides a benchmark for comparing with Variational Monte Carlo results (Pieper et al., 1992) which had previously been the only successful approach for medium nuclei.
