# Accessing the gluonic structure of light nuclei at the Electron Ion Collider

> We show how exclusive vector meson production off light ions can be used to probe the spatial distribution of small-$x$ gluons in the deuteron and $^3$He wave functions. In particular, we demonstrate how short range repulsive nucleon-nucleon interactions affect the predicted coherent $J/Ψ$ production spectra. Fluctuations of the nucleon substructure are shown to have a significant effect on the incoherent cross section above $|t|\gtrsim 0.2\,\mathrm{GeV}^2$. By explicitly performing the JIMWLK evolution, we predict the $x$-dependence of coherent and incoherent cross sections in the EIC energy range. Besides the increase of the average size of the nucleus with decreasing $x$, both the growth of the nucleons and subnucleonic hot spots are visible in the cross sections. The decreasing length scale of color charge fluctuations with decreasing $x$ is also present, but may not be observable for $|t|<1\,\mathrm{GeV}^2$, if subnucleonic spatial fluctuations are present.

# Accessing the gluonic structure of light nuclei at the Electron Ion Collider

## Key claim

Exclusive J/Ψ vector meson production off deuteron and ³He targets at the Electron Ion Collider can probe the spatial distribution of small-x gluons in light nuclei, but the decreasing length scale of color charge fluctuations with decreasing x may not be observable for |t| < 1 GeV² if subnucleonic spatial fluctuations are present.

## Method

The authors compute coherent and incoherent J/Ψ production cross sections within the dipole picture, using both the IPsat parametrization and the Color Glass Condensate framework with explicit JIMWLK evolution equations. Nucleon positions in deuteron and ³He are sampled from realistic wave functions (Argonne v18 potential and Hulthen parametrization for deuteron; AV18+UIX interaction for ³He), and nucleon substructure fluctuations are included via a hot spot model with log-normal saturation scale fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron root mean square size: 3.93 fm (Argonne potential) and 4.07 fm (Hulthen wave function)
- Hulthen wave function parameters: a = 0.228 fm⁻¹, b = 1.18 fm⁻¹
- Incoherent cross section shows significant effect from nucleon substructure fluctuations above |t| ≳ 0.2 GeV²
- Coherent cross section slope at t=0: B_D ≈ 28 GeV⁻² (Argonne) and B_D ≈ 29 GeV⁻² (Hulthen)
- JIMWLK evolution performed with infrared regulator m = 0.2 GeV and fixed coupling α_s = 0.21
- Lattice spacing a = 0.01 fm with lattice size L = 13 fm (deuteron) and L = 10 fm (helium)
- Initial x₀ = 0.01 for JIMWLK evolution
- Hot spot parameters for IPsat: B_qc = 4.5 GeV⁻², B_q = 1.0 GeV⁻², σ = 0.65
- Hot spot parameters for CGC: B_qc = 4.0 GeV⁻², B_q = 0.3 GeV⁻², σ = 0.5
- Incoherent cross section difference between Argonne and Hulthen wave functions at |t| ~ 0.1 GeV² is ≈ 5%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of proton shape fluctuations in exclusive vector meson production (Mäntysaari and Schenke, 2016) and heavy nuclei (Mäntysaari and Schenke, 2017) to light nuclei. It provides predictions for the future Electron Ion Collider, where center-of-mass energies up to √s_NN = 140√(Z/A) GeV allow reaching x values down to 10⁻⁴–10⁻³ in J/Ψ photoproduction. The calculations can be used to constrain models of deuteron-gold and helium-gold collisions at RHIC, and to disentangle initial state geometry effects from other sources of collectivity observed in proton-lead collisions at the LHC.
