# Scalar Mesons and Axial-vector Mesons Via Dyson-Schwinger Equation and Bethe-Salpeter Equation Approach

> In this work, I studied the spectrum of scalar mesons and axial-vector mesons via Dyson-Schwinger equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach in the rainbow-ladder approximation. An interaction model with a repulsive term added to the one used for the pseudoscalar and vector mesons is proposed. My results are consistent with the experiment data and other model results, which shows that this interaction model is effective for all the heavy, heavy-light, and light scalar and axial-vector mesons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a modified interaction model, which adds a repulsive term to the rainbow-ladder Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equation framework, can systematically reproduce the masses of scalar and axial-vector mesons across heavy, heavy-light, and light sectors, but the model's success is conditional on fitting three free parameters to three specific meson masses (scalar charmonium, scalar bottomium, and scalar D meson), and the systematic errors for light mesons are significantly larger than for heavy mesons.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states in the rainbow-ladder approximation, using a Gaussian interaction model for the effective quark-gluon vertex. They modify the infrared part of the effective interaction by adding a repulsive term \(\Delta_{fg}\) that depends on the quark mass functions at zero momentum and the interaction widths, with three parameters (\(\delta\), \(\alpha\), \(\beta\)) fitted to the masses of the scalar charmonium (\(0^{++}\)), scalar bottomium (\(0^{++}\)), and scalar D meson (\(0^{+}\)). For meson masses beyond the direct calculable contour border in complex momentum space, an extrapolation scheme using a rational function fit is employed.

## Key numbers

*   Scalar charmonium (\(0^{++}\)) mass: 3.415(0)(0) GeV (used as input fit value, experiment: 3.415 GeV).
*   Scalar bottomium (\(0^{++}\)) mass: 9.859(0)(0) GeV (used as input fit value, experiment: 9.859 GeV).
*   Scalar D meson (\(0^{+}\)) mass: 2.333(6)(0) GeV (used as input fit value, experiment: 2.333 GeV).
*   Axial-vector charmonium (\(1^{+-}\)) mass: 3.502(4)(10) GeV (output, experiment: 3.525 GeV, deviation about 20 MeV).
*   Axial-vector bottomium (\(1^{++}\)) mass: 9.897(1)(2) GeV (output, experiment: 9.893 GeV, deviation within 10 MeV).
*   Scalar \(B_c\) meson (\(0^{+}\)) mass: 6.700(1)(4) GeV (output, lattice QCD: 6.712 GeV, deviation within 20 MeV).
*   Light scalar \(n\bar{n}\) (\(0^{++}\)) mass: 1.098(6)(8) GeV (output, no pure \(q\bar{q}\) experiment value cited).
*   Light axial-vector \(n\bar{n}\) (\(1^{+-}\)) mass: 1.220(10)(40) GeV (output, experiment: 1.230 GeV).
*   Mass splitting for light \(n\bar{s}\) axial-vector mesons: \(M^{\text{DSE}}_{n\bar{s}}(1^{+}_2) - M^{\text{DSE}}_{n\bar{s}}(1^{+}_1) \approx 70\) MeV (output, compared to experiment splitting of about 150 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equation approach, previously successful for pseudoscalar and vector mesons (Maris 1997, Maris 1998, Maris 1999, Qin 2011) and extended to heavy and heavy-light mesons (Fischer 2015, Hilger 2015, Chen 2019), to the scalar and axial-vector channels. It addresses the known problem that rainbow-ladder models systematically underestimate the masses of orbital and radial excited states (Qin 2011, Krassnigg 2011, Fischer 2015, Hilger 2015, Chen 2017, Chen 2020, Chang 2020) by introducing a phenomenological repulsive term to mimic missing higher-order corrections to the quark-gluon vertex (Bender 1996). The results enable predictions for unmeasured meson masses (e.g., scalar and axial-vector \(B_c\) mesons) and suggest that the scalar and axial-vector \(D_s\) mesons may not be pure \(q\bar{q}'\) states (Chen 2004, Faessler 2007, Faessler 2007a) and that the experimentally measured \(K(1)(1270)\) may consist of two poles (Geng 2007, Meissner 2020, Wang 2020).
