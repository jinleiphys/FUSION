# Production of Kaon and $Λ$ in nucleus-nucleus collisions at ultra-relativistic energy from a blast wave model

> The particle production of Kaon and $Λ$ are studied in nucleus-nucleus collisions at relativistic energy based on a chemical equilibrium blast-wave model. The transverse momentum spectra of Kaon and $Λ$ at the kinetic freeze-out stage from our model are in good agreement with the experimental results. The kinetic freeze-out parameters of temperature ($T_{kin}$) and radial flow parameter $ρ_{0}$ are presented for the FOPI, RHIC and LHC energies. And the resonance decay effect is also discussed. The systematic study for beam energy dependence of the strangeness particle production will help us to better understand the properties of the matter created in heavy-ion collisions at the kinetic freeze-out stage.

## Key claim

The blast-wave model with chemical equilibrium can reproduce the transverse momentum spectra of Kaon and Lambda across a wide range of collision energies (from 2.6 GeV to 2.76 TeV), but the agreement depends on using kinetic freeze-out parameters (temperature and radial flow) that are extracted from experimental data rather than predicted from first principles.

## Method

The authors employed a blast-wave model assuming local thermal equilibrium at kinetic freeze-out, combined with a chemical equilibrium model to determine particle yields. They fitted the transverse momentum spectra of Kaons and Lambdas at mid-rapidity for central nucleus-nucleus collisions at FOPI (Ni+Ni at 2.6 GeV), RHIC (Au+Au at 62.4 and 200 GeV), and LHC (Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV) energies. The resonance decay contributions from heavier hadrons (such as K*(892), phi(1020), Sigma, and Xi) were included to estimate the fraction of Kaons and Lambdas originating from resonance decays.

## Key numbers

- At FOPI energy (Ni+Ni at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.6 GeV): kinetic freeze-out temperature T_kin = 30 MeV, radial flow parameter rho_0 = 0.6, chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch = 49 MeV, baryon chemical potential mu_B = 815 MeV
- At RHIC 62.4 GeV (Au+Au): T_kin = 105 MeV, rho_0 = 0.7, T_ch = 156 MeV, mu_B = 70 MeV
- At RHIC 200 GeV (Au+Au): T_kin = 91 MeV, rho_0 = 0.8, T_ch = 156 MeV, mu_B = 20 MeV
- At LHC 2.76 TeV (Pb+Pb): T_kin = 99 MeV, rho_0 = 1.0, T_ch = 160 MeV, mu_B = 10 MeV
- Percentage of Kaons from resonance decay: 0.2% at 2.6 GeV, 44.4% at 62 GeV, 44.6% at 200 GeV, 46.9% at 2000 GeV
- Percentage of Lambdas from resonance decay: 19.5% at 2.6 GeV, 73.8% at 62 GeV, 74.1% at 200 GeV, 74.9% at 2000 GeV

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave model analysis previously applied to pions, kaons, and protons (Song and collaborators, 2013) to strangeness hadrons (Kaon and Lambda) across a wider energy range. The results are consistent with kinetic freeze-out parameters extracted by the RHIC-STAR collaboration (2013) and with chemical freeze-out parameters from thermal model analyses (Andronic et al., 2006; Cleymans et al., 2006). The systematic energy dependence of strangeness production provides a reference for future experiments at the Lanzhou Cooling Storage Ring facility and the High Intensity Accelerator Facility, and supports the beam energy scan program at RHIC.
