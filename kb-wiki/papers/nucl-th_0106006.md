# An isobar model for eta photo- and electroproduction on the nucleon

> An isobar model containing Born terms, vector meson exchange and nucleon resonances is used to analyze recent eta photoproduction data for cross section and beam asymmetry, as well as JLab electroproduction data. Good overall description is achieved up to Q^2 = 4.0 (GeV/c)^2. Besides the dominant S11(1535) resonance, we show that the second S11 resonance, S11(1650), is also necessary to be included in order to extract S11(1535) resonance parameters properly. In addition, the beam asymmetry data allow us to extract very small (<0.1%) N* --> eta N decay branching ratios of D13(1520) and F15(1680) resonances because of the overwhelming s-wave dominance. The model is implemented as a part of the MAID program.

# An isobar model for eta photo- and electroproduction on the nucleon

## Key claim
The isobar model achieves a good overall description of eta photoproduction and electroproduction data up to Q² = 4.0 (GeV/c)², but the authors note that the second S11 resonance, S11(1650), must be included alongside the dominant S11(1535) to extract correct S11(1535) resonance parameters; a single-resonance fit yields significantly different decay width and photon coupling values.

## Method
The authors constructed an isobar model containing Born terms, vector meson exchange, and contributions from eight nucleon resonances (S11(1535), D13(1520), S11(1650), D15(1675), F15(1680), D13(1700), P11(1710), and P13(1720)). Resonance multipoles were parameterized with a Breit-Wigner energy dependence, and the model was fitted to recent eta photoproduction data (total and differential cross sections from TAPS and GRAAL, beam asymmetries from GRAAL) and JLab electroproduction data. For electroproduction, all parameters from the photoproduction fit were fixed except the Q² dependence of the helicity amplitudes.

## Key numbers
- The model describes data up to Q² = 4.0 (GeV/c)² for electroproduction.
- In the single S11 resonance fit, the decay width was 159 MeV versus 191 MeV in the full fit; the photon coupling was 103 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² versus 118 × 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/² in the full fit.
- The extracted branching ratio for D13(1520) → ηN decay is 0.06%.
- The extracted branching ratio for F15(1680) → ηN decay is 0.06%.
- The beam asymmetry data allow extraction of N* → ηN decay branching ratios smaller than 0.1% for D13(1520) and F15(1680).

## Context
This work extends the unitary isobar model (UIM) developed by Drechsel et al. (1998) for pion photo- and electroproduction, adapting it to eta production where unitarization is not feasible due to the lack of experimental eta-nucleon scattering data. The model is implemented as part of the MAID program. The analysis uses recent precise data from TAPS (MAMI/Mainz) by Krusche et al. (1995), GRAAL by Renard et al. (2000) and Ajaka et al. (1998), and JLab electroproduction data by Armstrong et al. (1998) and Thompson et al. (2000). The model enables extraction of resonance parameters for S11(1535) and very small branching ratios for D13(1520) and F15(1680) that would be inaccessible without the s-wave dominance and beam asymmetry measurements.
