# Non-conformal attractor in boost-invariant plasmas

> We study the dissipative evolution of (0+1)-dimensionally expanding media with Bjorken symmetry using the Boltzmann equation for massive particles in relaxation-time approximation. Breaking conformal symmetry by a mass induces a non-zero bulk viscous pressure in the medium. It is shown that even a small mass (in units of the local temperature) drastically modifies the well-known attractor for the shear Reynolds number previously observed in massless systems. For generic nonzero particle mass, neither the shear nor the bulk viscous pressure relax quickly to a non-equilibrium attractor; they approach the hydrodynamic limit only late, at small values of the inverse Reynolds numbers. Only the longitudinal pressure, which is a combination of thermal, shear and bulk viscous pressures, continues to show early approach to a far-off-equilibrium attractor, driven by the rapid longitudinal expansion at early times. Second-order dissipative hydrodynamics based on a gradient expansion around locally isotropic thermal equilibrium fails to reproduce this attractor.

## Key claim

Even a small non-zero particle mass (relative to the local temperature) destroys the well-known early-time attractor for the shear Reynolds number that exists in massless conformal systems; only the longitudinal pressure, a combination of thermal, shear, and bulk viscous pressures, retains a universal early-time attractor driven by rapid longitudinal expansion, and second-order dissipative hydrodynamics fails to reproduce this attractor.

## Method

The authors solved the (0+1)-dimensional relativistic Boltzmann equation in relaxation-time approximation (RTA) for massive particles undergoing Bjorken expansion, using an iterative Landau matching procedure to determine the temperature evolution and numerical quadrature to obtain the distribution function and energy-momentum tensor. They compared these exact kinetic theory solutions with second-order Chapman-Enskog hydrodynamic equations for the same system, using transport coefficients derived from the RTA Boltzmann equation, and studied the evolution of shear and bulk viscous stresses as functions of scaled proper time for various initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions set at proper time τ₀ = 0.1 fm/c with initial temperature T₀ = 500 MeV; particle mass m = 50 MeV
- Relaxation time parametrized as τ_R = 5C/T, with coupling constants C = 3/(4π) (strongly coupled) and C = 10/(4π) (weakly coupled)
- For small initial m/T₀ ≈ 0.1, the upper bound on normalized bulk stress generated by the initial distribution is approximately Π_max ≈ z₀²/6 where z₀ = m/T₀
- In the free-streaming limit, second-order non-conformal hydrodynamics yields three fixed points for normalized stresses: {Π*, π*} = {0, 1.214}, {0, -2.64}, and {-3.56, -1.56}
- Shear stress trajectories with different initial bulk viscous pressures collapse on the late-time Navier-Stokes attractor only at scaled time τ̄ ≡ τ/τ_R ≳ 3
- All kinetic theory solutions for longitudinal pressure join a universal attractor at early times τ̄ ≲ 0.5, starting from P_L/P ≈ 0 at τ̄₀ → 0

## Context

This work extends studies of attractor solutions in conformal Bjorken systems (Heller 2011, Romatschke 2017, Strickland 2017, Kurkela 2018) to non-conformal systems with massive particles, where bulk viscosity couples to shear viscosity. It was motivated by an ongoing investigation of attractor solutions to second-order hydrodynamic equations by Denicol (2012) and Jaiswal (2014). The results show that the early-time attractor structure is fundamentally different from conformal systems, and that second-order hydrodynamics fails to describe the early-time behavior; a modified version of anisotropic hydrodynamics (to be reported in a companion paper) avoids this failure for Bjorken flow.
