# Anomalous Transport Processes in Anisotropically Expanding Quark-Gluon Plasmas

> We derive an expression for the anomalous viscosity in an anisotropically expanding quark-gluon-plasma, which arises from interactions of thermal partons with dynamically generated color fields. The anomalous viscosity dominates over the collisional viscosity for large velocity gradients or weak coupling. This effect may provide an explanation for the apparent ``nearly perfect'' liquidity of the matter produced in nuclear collisions at RHIC without the assumption that it is a strongly coupled state.

## Key claim

The paper derives an expression for the anomalous shear viscosity in an anisotropically expanding quark-gluon plasma, which arises from interactions of thermal partons with dynamically generated color fields from Weibel-type instabilities, and claims this anomalous viscosity can dominate over collisional viscosity for large velocity gradients or weak coupling, potentially explaining the small viscosity observed at RHIC without requiring the plasma to be a strongly coupled state.

## Method

The authors derive a diffusive Vlasov-Boltzmann equation for the ensemble-averaged parton distribution function, incorporating the effects of turbulent color fields through a diffusion term obtained by linear response theory. They assume the color fields are turbulent with Gaussian correlation functions and factorize the ensemble average of field-force terms, then solve for the color octet distribution function and substitute it back into the transport equation to obtain a diffusion operator. The anomalous shear viscosity is extracted by expanding the distribution function around equilibrium using the Chapman-Enskog formalism and comparing the resulting stress-energy tensor with the Navier-Stokes form.

## Key numbers

* The collisional shear viscosity over entropy density ratio is given by \(\eta_{\rm C}/s = d_f / [g^4 \ln(\sqrt{4\pi}/g)]\) with \(d_f \approx 5\) for three light quark flavors (Equation 2).
* The anomalous shear viscosity over entropy density ratio is given by \(\eta_{\rm A}/s = (1/g^{3/2}) [ (N_c^2-1)T\tau / (10 b_0 N_c) ]^{1/2}\), where \(N_c\) is the number of colors, \(b_0\) is an unknown numerical coefficient, and \(\tau\) is a characteristic time (Equation 1).
* The transport cross section for perturbative QCD is \(\sigma_{\rm tr} \approx (5g^4 / 4\bar{p}^2) \ln(\sqrt{4\pi}/g)\) (Equation 5).
* The memory time for the turbulent field correlations is defined as \(\tau_{\rm m}^{\rm el/mag} = \frac{1}{2} \int_{-\infty}^{\infty} dt' \, \Phi^{\rm (el/mag)}_\tau(|t-t'|) \, \tilde\Phi^{\rm (el/mag)}_\sigma(|{\mathbf v}(t-t')|)\) (Equation 13).
* The wave vector domain of unstable modes satisfies \(k^2 \leq \xi m_{\rm D}^2\), where \(m_{\rm D} \sim gT\) is the Debye screening mass and \(\xi\) is the anisotropy parameter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Letter (Asakawa, Bass, Muller 2006) by providing detailed derivations of the anomalous viscosity arising from color field instabilities in expanding quark-gluon plasmas. It builds on the theory of Weibel-type instabilities in QCD plasmas (Mrowczynski 1988, 1993; Romatschke 2003) and the formalism of particle propagation in turbulent plasmas originally developed by Dupree (1966, 1968) for electromagnetic plasmas, later applied to transport coefficients by Abe and Niu (1980). The paper offers an alternative to the strongly coupled plasma interpretation (Gyulassy 2004; Shuryak 2004) for explaining the small viscosity observed at RHIC, and it enables future calculations of anomalous transport coefficients in anisotropic QCD plasmas without assuming strong coupling.
