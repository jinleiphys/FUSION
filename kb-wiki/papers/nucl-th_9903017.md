# Moments of event observable distributions and many-body correlations

> We investigate event-by-event fluctuations for ensembles with non-fixed multiplicity. Moments of event observable distributions, like total energy distribution, total transverse momentum distribution, etc, are shown to be related to the multi-body correlations present in the system. For classical systems, these moments reduce in the absence of any correlations to the moments of particle inclusive momentum distribution. As a consequence, a zero value for the recently introduced $Φ$-variable is shown to indicate the vanishing of two-body correlations from one part, and of correlations between multiplicity and momentum distributions from the other part. It is often misunderstood as a measure of the degree of equilibration in the system.

## Key claim

A zero value of the recently introduced Phi-variable indicates the absence of two-body correlations and of correlations between multiplicity and momentum distributions, but it is often misunderstood as a measure of the degree of equilibration in the system; the paper shows that a zero value for Phi does not necessarily imply complete thermal or chemical equilibration.

## Method

The authors derive general expressions for the moments of event observable distributions (such as total energy or total transverse momentum) in terms of s-body distribution functions for systems with non-fixed multiplicity. They then show how these moments reduce in the absence of any correlations for classical systems, where the s-body distribution functions factorize into products of one-body distribution functions. The analysis is extended to show that the quantities (⟨Z^k⟩/⟨N⟩ - y̅^k) for k>1 provide a test for the presence of many-body correlations and non-statistical fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The average value of Z is always zero (⟨Z⟩ ≡ 0) with the choice y(p) = x(p) - x̅, even in the presence of strong correlations.
- In the absence of correlations for classical systems, ⟨Z²⟩ = ⟨N⟩ ∫ dp y²(p) f^{incl}(p) and ⟨Z³⟩ = ⟨N⟩ ∫ dp y³(p) f^{incl}(p).
- A non-zero value of (⟨Z²⟩/⟨N⟩ - y̅²) indicates the presence of two-body correlations and possible correlations between particle multiplicity and momentum distributions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for event-by-event fluctuation analysis in relativistic heavy-ion collisions, building on the Phi-variable introduced by Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (1990s). The paper clarifies that the Phi-variable tests for correlations rather than equilibration, contradicting earlier interpretations by Gazdzicki (1995) and Bleicher (1999). The method applies to all p-p, p-A, or A-A collisions and can be generalized to hadronic-type distributions, enabling more precise interpretation of experimental data from large acceptance detectors such as the NA49 collaboration's Pb-Pb collisions at 160 AGeV.
