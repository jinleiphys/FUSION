# Analysis of Pion Photoproduction Data

> A partial-wave analysis of single-pion photoproduction data has been completed. This study extends from threshold to 2 GeV in the laboratory photon energy, focusing mainly on the influence of new measurements and model-dependence in the choice of parameterization employed above the two-pion threshold. Results are used to evaluate sum rules and estimate resonance photo-decay amplitudes. These are compared to values obtained in the MAID analysis.

## Key claim

A new partial-wave analysis (SM02) of single-pion photoproduction data from threshold to 2 GeV in laboratory photon energy yields a significantly improved fit compared to the previous SM95 solution, but the authors acknowledge that the choice of parameterization above the two-pion threshold introduces model-dependence, and some polarization quantities remain difficult to describe.

## Method

The authors performed an energy-dependent partial-wave analysis of the combined π⁰p, π⁺n, π⁻p, and π⁰n databases up to 2 GeV, using a parametrization where multipoles were expressed in terms of Born terms, phenomenological polynomials, and the elastic πN T-matrix. A new additive term proportional to (Im TπN − TπN²) replaced the previous multiplicative overall phase to improve fits to recent Σ beam-asymmetry measurements. Both energy-dependent and single-energy solutions were obtained, and resonance photo-decay amplitudes were extracted by fitting multipoles to a Breit-Wigner plus background form.

## Key numbers

- The energy-dependent solution SM02 had a χ² of 35296 for 17571 data points (π⁰p, π⁺n, π⁻p, and π⁰n) to 2 GeV, with an overall χ²/data significantly lower than the previous published result (χ²/data = 2.4) from Arndt (1996).
- The total database increased by 30% since the publication of Arndt (1996) and is about 40% larger than the set available for the analysis of Li (1993).
- Since 1995, 87% of all new π⁰p data and 21% of all new π⁺n data have been produced at Mainz using the MAMI facility.
- For the Δ(1232) resonance, MAID2001 gives photo-decay amplitudes A₁/₂ = −133 ± 4 and A₃/₂ = −252 ± 6 (in units of 10⁻³ GeV⁻¹/²).
- The GDH sum rule predicts −205 μb for the proton and −233 μb for the neutron.
- For the proton, the Baldin sum rule gives (14.0 ± 0.3) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from Levchuk and L'vov, (13.8 ± 0.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from an independent Mainz determination, and (13.25 ± 0.86⁺⁰.²³₋₀.₅₈) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ from the LEGS group.
- The forward spin polarizability γ₀ for a proton target from the LEGS analysis is (−1.55 ± 0.15 ± 0.003) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁴.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous partial-wave analyses of pion photoproduction (Arndt 1996, Arndt 1990, Li 1993) and complements their studies of pion-nucleon elastic scattering and pion-electroproduction fits. The new solution (SM02) is compared to the MAID analysis, with significant differences found in several multipoles and resonance couplings, particularly for states with small πN branching fractions. The results enable evaluation of the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn sum rule, the Baldin sum rule, and the forward spin polarizability for single-pion contributions, showing convergence by 2 GeV and good agreement with Mainz data below 450 MeV.
