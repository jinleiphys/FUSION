# Spin-dependent observables and the D_2 parameter in breakup of deuteron and 3He

> We analyze the momentum distributions of constituents in 3He, as well as the spin-dependent observables for (3He,d), (3He,p), and (d,p) breakup reactions. Special attention is paid to the region of small relative momenta of the helium-3 and the deuteron constituents, where a single parameter, D_2, has determining role for the spin-dependent observables. We extract also this parameter for the deuteron, basing on existing data for the tensor analyzing power of this reaction.

## Key claim

The spin-dependent observables for breakup reactions of \(^3\)He and the deuteron at small internal momenta are completely determined by a single parameter, \(D_2\), but the analysis relies on the spectator model and neglects Coulomb effects and final-state interactions, which may cause deviations at very low momenta (below about 50 MeV/\(c\)).

## Method

The authors used a parametrization of the fully antisymmetric three-nucleon wave function for \(^3\)He, based on the Paris and CD-Bonn potentials, restricted to five partial waves in the channel spin coupling scheme. Within the spectator model and a plane-wave Born approximation, they calculated the tensor analyzing powers and polarization transfer coefficients for \((^3\mathrm{He},d)\), \((^3\mathrm{He},p)\), and \((d,p)\) breakup reactions, and extracted the \(D_2\) parameter for the deuteron by fitting existing \(T_{20}\) data from Saclay and Dubna.

## Key numbers

*   \(D_2\) parameter for \(^3\)He: \(-0.2387\) fm\(^2\) (Paris potential), \(-0.2487\) fm\(^2\) (CD-Bonn potential), compared to the experimental value \(-0.259 \pm 0.014\) fm\(^2\) from Sen.
*   \(D_2\) parameter for the deuteron: \(2D^d_2 = +(23.70 \pm 0.33)\) (GeV/\(c\))\(^{-2}\) extracted from a fit to \(T_{20}\) data for \(p(d,p)X\) at \(k \leq 0.15\) GeV/\(c\) (\(\chi^2/\mathrm{DoF} = 19.7/12\)).
*   Effective number of deuterons in \(^3\)He: \(\mathcal{N}_d = 1.39\) (Paris) and 1.36 (CD-Bonn), compared to 1.38 from variational calculations with Argonne and Urbana potentials.
*   Probabilities of the \(D\)-wave in the \(d+p\) configuration of \(^3\)He: 1.53% (Paris) and 1.43% (CD-Bonn).
*   At small \(q\), the tensor polarization \(\rho_{20} \approx -2 q^2 D_2\) and the polarization transfer \(\kappa_d \to 2/3\) as \(q \to 0\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Kobushkin and Strokovsky (2012) on momentum distributions and spin-dependent observables in \(^3\)He breakup, using the wave function parametrization of Baru. It provides a new method to extract the \(D_2\) parameter for both \(^3\)He and the deuteron from intermediate-energy breakup reactions, complementary to low-energy rearrangement reactions studied by Knutson and Sen. The results enable the use of \((d,p)\) breakup for polarimetry of deuteron beams and highlight the need for consistent angular momentum summation conventions in three-nucleon calculations.
