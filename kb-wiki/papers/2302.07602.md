# Hidden Euclidean dynamical symmetry in the U(n+1) vibron model

> Based on the boson realization of the Euclidean algebras, it is found that the E($n$) dynamical symmetry (DS) may emerge at the critical point of the U($n$)-SO($n+1$) quantum phase transition. To justify this finding, we provide a detailed analysis of the critical dynamics in the U($n+1$) vibron model in both quantal and classical ways. It is further shown that the low-lying structure of $^{82}$Kr may serve as an excellent empirical realization of the E(5) DS in experiments.

## Key claim

The Euclidean dynamical symmetry E($n$) can emerge at the critical point of the U($n$)-SO($n+1$) quantum phase transition in the U($n+1$) vibron model, but only under the approximate condition that the expectation value $\langle \hat{n}_{b^l}/N\rangle$ is close to zero; this condition is exactly satisfied in the classical (large-$N$) limit and becomes a reasonable approximation for finite boson number $N$.

## Method

The authors constructed a boson realization of the $n$-dimensional Euclidean algebra E($n$) with $n=2l+1$ using spin-$l$ boson operators. They analyzed a schematic Hamiltonian that interpolates between the U($n$) and SO($n+1$) dynamical symmetry limits of the U($n+1$) vibron model, with a control parameter $\eta$. By computing commutation relations between the E($n$) generators and the Hamiltonian, and by performing both quantal diagonalization and classical mean-field analysis, they identified the parameter point $\eta=1/2$ as the critical point where the E($n$) dynamical symmetry emerges under the condition $\langle \hat{n}_{b^l}/N\rangle \rightarrow 0$.

## Key numbers

- The critical point of the U($n$)-SO($n+1$) quantum phase transition is located at $\eta_c = 1/2$.
- For the U(4) and U(6) models with total boson number $N=100$, the order parameter $\rho(\eta)=\langle\hat{n}_{b^l}/N\rangle$ remains near zero for $\eta\in[0, 1/2]$ and rapidly increases for $\eta>1/2$.
- In the classical limit, the ground state deformation $\bar{\beta}_e$ is zero for $0\leq\eta\leq 1/2$ and equals $\sqrt{2(\eta-1/2)/\eta}$ for $\eta>1/2$, with a critical exponent $u=1/2$.
- For $^{82}$Kr, the E(5) Hamiltonian was solved with truncation $m=200$, $\alpha=100.2$ keV, $b=10$ keV, and $c=15$ keV; the $3_1^+$ state excitation energy is overestimated by about 0.5 MeV.
- All $B(E2)$ results are normalized to $B(E2;2_1^+\rightarrow0_1^+)=100$ (arbitrary units).

## Context

This work generalizes the algebraic realization of the E(5) critical point symmetry previously developed by Zhang et al. (2014) and Zhang (2014) to arbitrary odd dimension $n=2l+1$, extending the group contraction analysis for $n=5$ discussed by Bonatsos et al. (2011) and Meyer (1979). The study provides a theoretical foundation for the critical point symmetry role of E($n$) in second-order quantum phase transitions, and demonstrates that $^{82}$Kr, recently suggested as an E(5) candidate by Rajbanshi et al. (2021), serves as an empirical realization of the E(5) dynamical symmetry. The results enable systematic algebraic descriptions of critical-point dynamics in $2^l$-pole deformed systems described by the U($n+1$) vibron model, including the U(6) interacting boson model for nuclei and the U(4) vibron model for molecules.
