# Modeling nuclear weak-interaction processes with relativistic energy density functionals

> Relativistic energy density functionals have become a standard framework for nuclear structure studies of ground-state properties and collective excitations over the entire nuclide chart. We review recent developments in modeling nuclear weak-interaction processes: charge-exchange excitations and the role of isoscalar proton-neutron pairing, charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions relevant for supernova evolution and neutrino detectors, and calculation of beta-decay rates for r-process nucleosynthesis.

# Modeling nuclear weak-interaction processes with relativistic energy density functionals

## Key claim

The relativistic nuclear energy density functional (RNEDF) framework can provide a self-consistent description of weak-interaction processes across the nuclide chart, but the quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant \(g_A\) (from 1.262 to 1.0, a factor of 0.8) is required to reproduce measured Gamow-Teller transition strengths, and the isoscalar pairing strength \(V_0\) must be adjusted locally or with a smooth mass dependence rather than taken as a single global value.

## Method

The authors employ the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with the DD-ME2 functional for ground-state calculations and the relativistic quasiparticle random-phase approximation (RQRPA) for charge-exchange excitations, using the same effective interaction consistently in both steps. For open-shell nuclei, pairing correlations are described by the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S in the isovector channel, while an isoscalar spin-triplet pairing interaction with a Gaussian form is introduced in the particle-particle channel of the RQRPA. Neutrino-nucleus cross sections are computed using the weak-interaction Hamiltonian in current-current form, including multipole operators up to \(J^\pi = 5^\pm\), and are averaged over Fermi-Dirac neutrino spectra with temperatures \(T = 2-10\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- The flux-averaged cross section for \(^{56}\text{Fe}(\nu_e, e^-)^{56}\text{Co}\) from the RNEDF (DD-ME2) calculation is \(263 \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\), compared to the experimental KARMEN value of \((256 \pm 108 \pm 43) \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\) and a theoretical average of \((258 \pm 57) \times 10^{-42} \text{ cm}^2\).
- For \(^{56}\text{Fe}\), the RQRPA calculation with \(g_A = 1\) (quenching factor 0.8 relative to the free-nucleon value \(g_A = 1.262\)) yields B(GT\(^-\)) within experimental uncertainties.
- In \(^{42}\text{Ca}\), with \(V_0 = 300\) MeV the low-lying GT state exhausts 59% and the high-lying state 33% of the GT sum rule \(3(N-Z) = 6\), compared to 34% and 53% respectively for \(V_0 = 0\) MeV.
- For \(^{18}\text{O}\), increasing the isoscalar pairing strength from \(V_0 = 0\) to \(V_0 = 300\) MeV shifts the main GT\(^-\) peak from 10.8 MeV to 7.5 MeV.
- The main GT\(^-\) peak in \(^{22}\text{O}\) is shifted to lower energy by 5.4 MeV relative to \(^{18}\text{O}\).
- For the \(^{56}\text{Fe}(\nu_e, e^-)^{56}\text{Co}\) reaction at \(E_{\nu_e} = 40\) MeV, the dominant contribution comes from \(J^\pi = 1^+\) transitions, while at 80 MeV higher multipoles contribute significantly.
- The RHB+RQRPA cross sections for neutron-rich nuclei (\(N/Z > 1.5\)) are enhanced up to an order of magnitude compared to stable nuclei, and are systematically larger than ETFSI+CQRPA results by factors of approximately 4-6.

## Context

This review extends the RNEDF framework previously applied to ground-state properties and collective excitations (Paar et al., 2007; Vretenar et al., 2012) into the domain of weak-interaction processes, building on earlier work on beta-decay of r-process nuclei (Nikšić et al., 2005; Marketin et al., 2007) and charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions (Paar et al., 2008, 2011, 2013). The results are compared with shell model calculations using the GXPF1J Hamiltonian (Honma et al., 2002) and with alternative approaches such as the ETFSI+CQRPA framework (Borzov, 2000) and the hybrid model of Kolbe et al. (2001). The work enables systematic large-scale calculations of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for target nuclei from oxygen to lead (\(Z = 8-82\), \(N = 8-182\)) that can be used in supernova simulations and r-process nucleosynthesis models, as well as for interpreting neutrino detector responses.
