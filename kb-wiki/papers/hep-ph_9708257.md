# Pade Improvement of the Free Energy in High Temperature QCD

> Pade approximants (PA's) are constructed from the perturbative coefficients of the free energy through O(g^5) in hot QCD. Pade summation is shown to reduce the renormalization-scale dependence substantially even at temperature (T) as low as 250 MeV. Also, PA's predict that the free energy does not deviate more than 10 % from the Stefan-Boltzmann limit for T > 250 MeV.

## Key claim

Padé approximants (PAs) constructed from the perturbative coefficients of the free energy through O(g^5) in hot QCD reduce the renormalization-scale dependence substantially even at temperatures as low as 250 MeV, and predict that the free energy does not deviate more than 10% from the Stefan-Boltzmann (SB) limit for T > 250 MeV, but this small deviation does not necessarily imply the system can be approximated by a non-interacting gas of quarks and gluons.

## Method

The author constructed naive Padé approximants (NPAs) and constrained Padé approximants (CPAs) from the perturbative coefficients of the QCD free energy through O(g^5) in the MS-bar scheme for massless quarks with n_f = 4 flavors and vanishing chemical potential. The running coupling alpha_s was computed numerically using the two-loop beta-function with an initial condition alpha_s(mu = 5 GeV) = 0.21, and the renormalization scale was set to mu = 2 pi T based on the lowest non-zero Matsubara frequency for gluons. The author tested all possible PAs satisfying N+M <= 5 for NPAs and N+M <= 6 for CPAs, selecting those that gave results qualitatively consistent with each other and showed reduced scale dependence.

## Key numbers

- At T = 250 MeV (1.7 times the critical temperature T_c ~ 150 MeV), the original perturbative series shows a sizable mu dependence and becomes unstable (negative pressure) for low mu; after Padé summation, the renormalization-scale dependence is reduced substantially.
- The fastest convergence criterion (FAC) gives an unphysically large value mu = 37.7 pi T for n_f = 4.
- The BLM criterion suggests mu = (0.95 to 4.4) pi T but gives a series reliable only for T > 2 GeV.
- After Padé summation, the free energy does not deviate more than 10% from the SB limit even at T = 250 MeV, in contrast to the 60% deviation of the original perturbative series at that temperature.
- The [4/1]-NPA and [1/4]-NPA develop poles in the denominator at alpha_s ~ 0.04 and ~ 0.06, respectively; [3/3]-CPA has poles at alpha_s ~ 0.12 and ~ 0.3.
- For [3/2]-CPA and [2/3]-CPA, the predicted O(g^6) coefficients are f_6 = f_4 f_5 / f_3 and f_6 = f_2^3 + f_3^2 + (f_4 - f_2^2)/f_3, respectively.

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between lattice QCD simulations (which show the energy density, pressure, and entropy density approach the SB limit quickly above T_c ~ 150 MeV) and the naive perturbative expansion (which works only for T > 2 GeV due to asymptotic nature of the series). The author extends the successful application of Padé summation to perturbative QCD series in high-energy processes (as done by Ellis and Karliner (1993) and Gardi (1997)) to the finite-temperature free energy, where odd powers of g and logarithmic terms in g appear in the coefficients. The results enable estimation of the O(g^6) coefficient and suggest that similar analyses with finite chemical potential and finite quark masses, more relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collision data, remain as future problems.
