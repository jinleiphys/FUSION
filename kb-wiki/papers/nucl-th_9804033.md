# Electric polarizability of nuclei and a longitudinal sum rule

> Recently, a longitudinal sum rule for the electric polarizability of nuclei was used to revise a relativistic correction in a dipole sum rule for the polarizability (nucl-th/9802011). This revision is shown to be wrong because of neglecting an asymptotic contribution in the underlying dispersion relation. The status and correct use of the longitudinal sum rule is clarified.

# Electric polarizability of nuclei and a longitudinal sum rule

## Key claim

The longitudinal sum rule for the electric polarizability, when used in its unsubtracted form as in Bernabeu et al. (1998), is invalid for nuclei because it neglects an asymptotic contribution from the underlying dispersion relation; when the correct subtracted form is used, the longitudinal sum rule agrees with the standard dipole sum rule and contains no potential-dependent correction.

## Method

The paper analyzes the 00-component of the forward Compton tensor \(T_{00}(\omega, q^2)\) and compares two sum rules derived from it: the dipole sum rule for the electric polarizability \(\bar\alpha\) and the longitudinal sum rule. The author expands the matrix elements in powers of \(v/c\) for a weakly-relativistic nuclear system, evaluates the asymptotic contribution to the dispersion relation at high \(\omega\), and shows that the potential-dependent terms found by Bernabeu et al. (1998) are exactly cancelled by the asymptotic term \(c^2 A_2\) when the subtracted form of the longitudinal sum rule is used.

## Key numbers

- The recoil correction to the electric polarizability is \(\alpha_{\rm rec} = Z^2 e^2 r_E^2 / (3Mc^2)\), where \(M\) is the nuclear mass, \(Ze\) the charge, and \(r_E\) the electric radius.
- The unsubtracted longitudinal sum rule gives a positive right-hand side and is violated whenever \(\bar\alpha < 0\), as occurs for pions in the linear \(\sigma\)-model: \(\bar\alpha_{\pi^\pm} = 4\bar\alpha_{\pi^0} \simeq -2 \times 10^{-4}\,{\rm fm}^3\).
- The asymptotic coefficient \(A_2\) is given by \(A_2 = -\frac{1}{4c^4} \langle 0 | [[C_2,V],C_2] | 0 \rangle + \frac{1}{3c^4} \langle 0 | [[C_1,V],C_3] | 0 \rangle\), where \(V\) is the binding potential and \(C_k\) are charge-moment operators.
- The final expression for the polarizability from the corrected longitudinal sum rule is \(\bar\alpha = I_0 + Z^2 e^2 r_E^2 / (3Mc^2)\), matching the dipole sum rule.

## Context

This work corrects a claim by Bernabeu et al. (1998) that an additional relativistic correction driven by non-local parts of the nuclear binding potential appears in the electric polarizability. The paper extends the earlier dipole sum rule derivation by Petrun'kin (1961) and the longitudinal sum rule formalism of Sucher (1972) and Bernabeu and Tarrach (1975). It clarifies that the unsubtracted dispersion relation for \(\omega^{-2} T_{00}\) is generally invalid due to Regge behavior (\(T_{00} \sim \omega^{\alpha_R(0)+2}\) with \(\alpha_R(0) \simeq 1\)), and that a subtraction is required. The result enables consistent use of the longitudinal sum rule for nuclear polarizability calculations without spurious potential-dependent artifacts.
