# Collisional and thermal dissociation of $J/ψ$ and $Υ$ states at the LHC

> We present new results for the suppression of high transverse momentum charmonium [$J/ψ, ψ(2S)$] and bottomonium [$Υ(1S),Υ(2S),Υ(3S)$] states in Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider. Our theoretical formalism combines the collisional dissociation of quarkonia, as they propagate in the quark-gluon plasma, with the thermal wavefunction effects due to the screening of the $Q\bar{Q}$ attractive potential in the medium. We find that a good description of the relative suppression of the ground and higher excited quarkonium states, transverse momentum and centrality distributions is achieved, when comparison to measurements at a center-of-mass energy of 2.76 TeV is performed. Theoretical predictions for the highest Pb+Pb center-of-mass energy of 5.02 TeV at the LHC, where new experimental results are being finalized, are also presented.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining collisional dissociation of quarkonia propagating through the quark-gluon plasma with thermal wavefunction effects from screening of the QCD potential provides a good description of the relative suppression of ground and excited quarkonium states, transverse momentum, and centrality distributions at LHC energies, but the agreement depends on varying the formation time between 1.0 and 1.5 fm and the broadening parameter between 1 and 2.

## Method

The authors solve rate equations for quarkonium production and dissociation in a 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic medium (iEBE-VISHNU package), using non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) to obtain baseline nucleon-nucleon cross sections and feed-down contributions. The dissociation rate is computed from the survival probability of quarkonium states, which depends on the overlap between the initial proto-quarkonium wavefunction (broadened by collisional interactions with medium partons) and the thermal wavefunction obtained from solving the Schrodinger equation with a finite-temperature potential derived from lattice QCD internal energies. The formalism is applied to Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and predictions are made for 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers

- Binding energies at zero temperature: J/psi 0.700 GeV, psi(2S) 0.086 GeV, Upsilon(1S) 1.122 GeV, Upsilon(2S) 0.578 GeV, Upsilon(3S) 0.214 GeV
- At T = 192 MeV, psi(2S), Upsilon(3S), and chi_b(3P) cease to exist as bound states
- RMS radii at zero temperature: J/psi 2.24 GeV^-1, Upsilon(1S) 1.23 GeV^-1, Upsilon(3S) 3.89 GeV^-1
- Coupling constant g = 1.85 fixed; broadening parameter xi varied between 1 and 2
- Formation time varied between 1.0 fm and 1.5 fm
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.34 GeV, bottom quark mass m_b = 4.5 GeV
- Cornell potential parameters: xi = 0.385, sigma = 0.224 GeV^2
- Debye screening scale mu_D^2 = g^2 T^2 (1 + N_f/6) with N_f = 2 active light quark flavors

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous rate-equation approach (Sharma 2012) by incorporating a 2+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamic medium instead of a simplified 1+1 dimensional Bjorken expansion, and by using lattice-motivated thermal potentials (Mocsy 2007, Petreczky 2010, Bazavov 2012) to compute thermal wavefunction effects. The formalism differs from approaches based on the imaginary part of the finite-temperature potential (Laine 2006, Brambilla 2013) and from open quantum system methods (Akamatsu 2011, Blaizot 2015, Brambilla 2016) by focusing on high transverse momentum quarkonia and relating the decay rate to transverse momentum broadening from multiple scattering. The results enable predictions for the highest LHC Pb+Pb energy of 5.02 TeV, where experimental results were being finalized at the time of publication.
