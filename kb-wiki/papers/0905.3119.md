# Heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory approach to thermal neutron capture on ${}^{3}{He}$

> The cross section for radiative thermal neutron capture on ${}^{3}He$ ($\He3 +n \to \He4 +γ$; known as the $hen$ reaction) is calculated based on heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. The relevant M1 operators are derived up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N${}^3$LO). The initial and final nuclear wave functions are obtained from the rigorous Faddeev-Yakubovski equations for five sets of realistic nuclear interactions. Up to N${}^3$LO, the M1 operators contain two low-energy constants, which appear as the coefficients of non-derivative two-nucleon contact terms. After determining these two constants using the experimental values of the magnetic moments of the triton and ${}^3 He$, we carry out a parameter-free calculation of the $hen$ cross section. The results are in good agreement with the data.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the thermal neutron capture cross section on \(^{3}\text{He}\) using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory up to N\(^3\)LO, finding good agreement with experimental data after fixing two low-energy constants from the magnetic moments of \(^{3}\text{H}\) and \(^{3}\text{He}\); however, the result is conditional on the choice of nuclear potential and cutoff parameter, with the best estimates showing a residual model dependence of about 15–20%.

## Method

The M1 transition operators were derived up to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^3\)LO) in heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory, including one-body, one-pion-exchange, two-pion-exchange, and contact-term contributions. The initial \(n\)-\(^{3}\text{He}\) scattering state and final \(^{4}\text{He}\) bound state were obtained by solving the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space for five realistic nuclear interactions (AV18, I-N3LO, INOY, AV18+UIX, I-N3LO+UIX*). The two low-energy constants \(g_{4s}\) and \(g_{4v}\) appearing in the contact-term operators were fixed for each potential and each cutoff value (\(\Lambda = 500, 700, 900\) MeV) by reproducing the experimental magnetic moments of the triton and \(^{3}\text{He}\).

## Key numbers

- Thermal neutron capture cross section on \(^{3}\text{He}\): \(\sigma = (49.4 \pm 8.5)\ \mu\text{b}\) for the AV18+UIX potential, and \(\sigma = (44.4 \pm 6.7)\ \mu\text{b}\) for the I-N3LO+UIX* potential.
- Experimental cross section values cited: \(\sigma_{\text{exp}} = (54 \pm 6)\ \mu\text{b}\) (Wolfs) and \(\sigma_{\text{exp}} = (55 \pm 3)\ \mu\text{b}\) (Wervelman).
- Photon momentum \(q = 20.578\ \text{MeV}\) used in the cross section formula.
- Thermal neutron velocity \(v_{\text{rel}} = 2200\ \text{m/s}\).
- Cutoff parameters \(\Lambda = 500, 700, 900\ \text{MeV}\) were tested; inclusion of contact terms reduced the \(\Lambda\)-dependence by a factor of \(\sim 5\).

## Context

This work extends the "more-effective effective field theory" (MEEFT) approach previously applied to the \(hep\) process (Park et al., 2005) to the analogous \(hen\) reaction, providing a parameter-free calculation with fully realistic four-body wave functions. Prior calculations by Carlson et al. (1990) and Schiavilla et al. (1992) using the standard nuclear physics approach gave cross sections of 112 \(\mu\)b and 86 \(\mu\)b, respectively, while earlier shell-model results ranged from 14 to 125 \(\mu\)b (Towner and Khanna, 1981; Wervelman et al., 1990). The good agreement with experiment supports the reliability of the MEEFT framework for the \(hep\) solar neutrino process, whose cross section cannot be measured directly.
