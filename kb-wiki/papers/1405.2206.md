# The equation of state of QCD at finite chemical potential

> We obtain the baryon number density, n, and the excess contribution to the pressure, Delta P, at finite chemical potential, mu_B, and temperature, T, by resumming the Taylor series expansion in a lattice computation with lattice spacing of 1/(4T) and two flavours of quarks at three different quark masses. The method proceeds by giving a critical mu_B and limits on the critical exponent, and permits reliable estimations of the errors in resummed quantities. We find that n and Delta P are insensitive to the quark mass. We also report the bulk isothermal compressibility, kappa, over a range of T and mu_B.

# The equation of state of QCD at finite chemical potential

## Key claim

The authors claim that the baryon number density and excess pressure at finite chemical potential can be reliably obtained by resumming the Taylor series expansion in lattice QCD, but only within a limited range of chemical potential and temperature; the method assumes a critical chemical potential and limits on the critical exponent, and the errors grow uncontrollably near the critical point due to critical slowing down, requiring substantially larger statistics to reach higher chemical potentials.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations on 4×16³ lattices with two flavours of degenerate dynamical quarks at three different quark masses corresponding to mπ/mρ ratios of approximately 0.58, 0.31, and 0.25. They computed the quark number susceptibilities (QNS) up to sixth order using the Taylor expansion method, then applied a DLOG Padé approximant to resum the series for the baryon number susceptibility, from which the baryon number density and excess pressure were obtained by successive integration. The analysis used bootstrap procedures for error estimation and examined the radius of convergence of the series to locate the critical end point.

## Key numbers

- The critical coupling for set A was βc = 5.3720 ± 0.0005, in agreement with an older MILC result of βc = 5.375 ± 0.020 on 4×8³ lattices.
- For set B, the critical end point was found at Tᴱ/Tc = 0.95 ± 0.01 and μBᴱ/Tᴱ = 1.5⁺⁰·⁵₋₀·₂.
- For set A, the critical end point was found at Tᴱ/Tc = 0.94 ± 0.02 and μBᴱ/Tᴱ = 1.5⁺⁰·²₋₀·₁.
- For set C, the critical end point was found at Tᴱ/Tc = 0.96 ± 0.01 and μBᴱ/Tᴱ = 1.4⁺⁰·⁴₋₀·₂.
- The peak in the sixth order QNS χB⁶ at Tc was stable when using Nv = 2000 source vectors, compared to the 50 sources sufficient for second order QNS.
- With Nv = 2000, m₁ had 50% errors at μB/T = 0.66; with Nv = 3000, at μB/T = 0.73; the authors estimate Nv > 10000 would be needed to reach μB/T = 1.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice studies of the QCD equation of state at finite chemical potential, particularly the Taylor expansion approach of Gupta et al. (2014) and the MILC collaboration's earlier measurements. The authors compare their results to the scaling conjecture of Friman et al. (2004) which relates the chiral critical behaviour to the QCD critical point, but find that their data do not support a power-law dependence of the fourth order QNS on quark mass, nor the predicted zero in χB⁶ at Tc. The method enables the evaluation of the equation of state over a larger part of the QCD phase diagram relevant to heavy-ion collision experiments, and provides estimates of the isothermal compressibility, but the authors note that substantially larger statistics will be needed to control errors at higher chemical potentials.
