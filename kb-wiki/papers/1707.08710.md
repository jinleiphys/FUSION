# Electron and Nucleon Localization Functions of Oganesson: Approaching the Thomas-Fermi Limit

> Fermion localization functions are used to discuss electronic and nucleonic shell structure effects in the superheavy element oganesson, the heaviest element discovered to date. Spin-orbit splitting in the $7p$ electronic shell becomes so large ($\sim$ 10 eV) that Og is expected to show uniform-gas-like behavior in the valence region with a rather large dipole polarizability compared to the lighter rare gas elements. The nucleon localization in Og is also predicted to undergo a transition to the Thomas-Fermi gas behavior in the valence region. This effect, particularly strong for neutrons, is due to the high density of single-particle orbitals.

## Key claim

Oganesson is predicted to exhibit uniform-gas-like behavior in both its electronic and nucleonic valence regions, approaching the Thomas-Fermi limit, due to strong spin-orbit splitting of the 7p electronic shell and high density of single-particle orbitals in the nuclear case; this transition is particularly strong for neutrons.

## Method

The authors computed electron localization functions (ELFs) for Xe, Rn, and Og using nonrelativistic, scalar-relativistic, and 4-component Dirac-Hartree-Fock methods with the DIRAC15 code, including the Gaunt term and using uncontracted relativistic quadruple-zeta basis sets. For nucleon localization functions (NLFs), they performed nuclear density functional theory calculations with the Skyrme energy density functionals UNEDF1 and SV-min, constrained to spherical geometry, for the doubly magic nuclei 132Sn, 302Og, and the theoretical superheavy nucleus 472164. Static dipole polarizabilities were computed at the CCSD(T) Coupled-Cluster level using the finite-field method.

## Key numbers

- Spin-orbit splitting of the 7p electronic shell in Og: 9.920 eV at Dirac-Breit-Hartree-Fock level and 10.125 eV at Fock-Space Coupled-Cluster level
- Ionization potentials of Og: 8.842 eV for 7p3/2 and 18.967 eV for 7p1/2 from Fock-Space Coupled-Cluster calculations
- Static dipole polarizability of Og: 57.98 a.u. (relativistic), compared to 45.30 a.u. (nonrelativistic) and 43.78 a.u. (scalar-relativistic)
- Static dipole polarizability of Xe: 27.815 a.u.; of Rn: 33.18 a.u.
- Radii of 7p orbitals in Og: 2.796 a.u. for 7p3/2 and 2.039 a.u. for 7p1/2 (difference of 0.75 a.u.)
- Radii of 5p orbitals in Xe: 2.239 a.u. for 5p3/2 and 2.141 a.u. for 5p1/2
- Electron binding energy increase due to relativistic effects in Og: 227 keV (compared to 40 keV for Pb)
- Estimated alpha-decay half-life of 294Og: 0.89 (+1.07/-0.31) ms
- Positive electron affinity of Og: 0.064 eV, including quantum electrodynamic correction of 0.006 eV

## Context

This work extends the application of fermion localization functions, originally developed for electronic systems by Becke and Edgecombe (1990) and later introduced to nuclear physics by Reinhard et al. (2011), to superheavy elements for the first time. The predicted transition to Thomas-Fermi gas behavior in Og's valence region contradicts the expected shell-structure-dominated behavior of lighter rare gas elements like Xe and Rn, and enables quantitative predictions of Og's chemical properties, including enhanced van-der-Waals interactions due to its large dipole polarizability. The nuclear calculations build on prior work on superheavy nuclei by Cwiok et al. (1996), Bender et al. (1999, 2001), and others, and support the prediction that 294Og is a semi-bubble nucleus with central depression of proton density (Schuetrumpf et al., 2017).
