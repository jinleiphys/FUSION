# The analysing powers in proton-deuteron elastic scattering

> It is shown that the ratio of the deuteron and proton analysing powers in proton-deuteron elastic scattering at small angles is sensitive to subtle effects in a theoretical description. These include the transverse spin-spin term in the elementary nucleon-nucleon amplitudes and double-scattering corrections. On the other hand there is far less sensitivity to the spin-orbit amplitude and to binding or other kinematic effects associated with the use of the deuteron, as either target or projectile. The available data are in agreement with the results of a refined Glauber theory model.

## Key claim

The ratio of the deuteron to proton analysing powers in proton-deuteron elastic scattering at small angles is sensitive to subtle effects such as the transverse spin-spin term in the elementary nucleon-nucleon amplitudes and double-scattering corrections, but shows far less sensitivity to the spin-orbit amplitude or binding effects; the available data are consistent with a refined Glauber theory model, though the ratio deviates from the simple single-scattering expectation of 2/3.

## Method

The authors evaluated the ratio R = A_y^d / A_y^p of the deuteron vector analysing power to the proton analysing power in proton-deuteron elastic scattering, using experimental data from multiple sources at beam energies of 135, 200, 250, and 800 MeV. They compared these data to predictions from a refined Glauber eikonal model that retains the full spin dependence of nucleon-nucleon amplitudes, including both single-scattering and double-scattering contributions, with input from a partial wave analysis of NN amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- At 250 MeV per nucleon, two data points gave R = 0.65 ± 0.03 at q = 1.45 fm⁻¹ and R = 0.66 ± 0.01 at q = 1.59 fm⁻¹, compatible with 2/3 within statistical errors plus 3% systematic uncertainty.
- At q = 0, the refined Glauber model predicts deviations from 2/3 expressed as 100(R − 2/3): at 135 MeV, single-scattering (SS) gives −1.09 and full model (SS+DS) gives −1.24; at 200 MeV, SS gives −0.82 and full gives −0.73; at 800 MeV, SS gives −2.75 and full gives −2.00.
- At 800 MeV, using the earlier A_y^p data would reduce the systematic discrepancy between data and the full model from about 7% to about 4%.
- The influence of nucleon-nucleon spin-spin amplitudes on R is typically at the 2-3% level.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Iro et al. (1983), who noted that the proton analysing power at small angles is dominated by interference of spin-independent and spin-orbit NN amplitudes, and who suggested a simple model yielding R = 2/3. The present study uses data from COSY-ANKE (Barsov et al., 2018), IUCF (Przewoski et al., 2006), and other experiments (Hajdas et al., 1987; Arvieux et al., 1988; Ghazikhanian et al., 1991) to test the refined Glauber model of Platonova and Kukulin (2010). The results enable future precise measurements of R to constrain the NN transverse spin-spin amplitude in the forward direction, independent of extraction from total cross sections and dispersion relations.
