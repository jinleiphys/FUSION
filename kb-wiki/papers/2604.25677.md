# Coherent deeply virtual Compton scattering on helium-4 beyond leading power

> Coherent hard exclusive reactions on light nuclei provide access to their quark and gluon structure and enable three-dimensional tomography of these complex systems. We study deeply virtual Compton scattering on a helium-4 target, including both kinematic twist-3 and twist-4 corrections, as well as next-to-leading-order corrections to the twist-2 amplitude in the strong coupling $α_s$. We show that these contributions are crucial for achieving a precise description of the data and, as a result, obtain the first tomographic image of the helium-4 nucleus at the quark-gluon level.

## Key claim
The authors show that including kinematic twist-3 and twist-4 corrections together with next-to-leading-order (NLO) corrections in the strong coupling constant is crucial for a precise description of deeply virtual Compton scattering (DVCS) on helium-4, and that these corrections enable the first tomographic image of the helium-4 nucleus at the quark-gluon level, but only within the kinematic-twist sector (genuine higher-twist contributions from matrix elements of higher-twist operators are excluded).

## Method
The authors compute the DVCS amplitude on a spinless helium-4 target using collinear factorization, expressing the Compton tensor in terms of photon-helicity-dependent amplitudes that convolve perturbative coefficient functions with generalized parton distributions (GPDs). They include kinematic twist-3 and twist-4 corrections to the coefficient functions, as well as NLO corrections in the strong coupling constant to the leading-twist (twist-2) amplitude, and also compute the Bethe-Heitler background amplitudes using Kleiss-Stirling spinor techniques. The total cross section is obtained by summing the DVCS and Bethe-Heitler amplitudes, and the GPDs for helium-4 are modeled treating the nuclear state as a QCD object without assuming a nucleon decomposition.

## Key numbers
- The hard scale of the process is given by $\scale^2 = Q^2 + t$, where $Q^2$ is the photon virtuality and $t$ is the Mandelstam variable.
- The nuclear Bjorken variable is defined as $x_A = Q^2/(2pq) \approx x_B/A$, where $x_B$ is the proton Bjorken variable and $A$ is the mass number (4 for helium-4).
- The skewness variable is given by $\xi = (Q^2+t)/(2Q^2/x_A - Q^2 + t)$, which at leading twist approximates to $\xi \approx x_A/(2-x_A) \approx x_B/(2A - x_B)$.
- The transverse momentum squared is $\bp_\perp^2 = M^2 - (t/4)(1 - 1/\xi^2)$, where $M$ is the nucleus mass.
- The higher-twist contributions proportional to $\bp_\perp$ vanish as $t \to t_0$, with $2\xi^2\bp_\perp^2 = (1-\xi^2)(t-t_0)/2$.

## Context
This work extends the study of coherent hard exclusive reactions on light nuclei, which was recognized by Berger (2001), Kirchner (2003), Guzey (2003), Cano (2003), and Scopetta (2004) as key to understanding quark and gluon structure beyond the nucleon. It builds on previous DVCS calculations for spinless targets by Braun (2012, 2014) and Martinez-Fernandez (2025), and incorporates NLO corrections to the leading-twist amplitude as highlighted by Pire (2011). The approach differs from that of Fucini (2018), who convoluted the nucleus wave function with nucleon GPDs; instead, the authors treat the nuclear state as a QCD object without prejudice on its nucleon decomposition. This work enables tomographic imaging of helium-4 at the quark-gluon level and allows future comparisons between different modeling approaches using existing JLab measurements from 2017 and 2021.
