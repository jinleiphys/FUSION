# Comment on the article ``UCN anomalous losses and the UCN capture cross section on material defects'' by A. Serebrov et al

> We present correct solution of the problem about a scattering of the neutron on a point-like defect existing in a medium and show that this mechanism cannot explain anomalous losses of UCN in storage bottles.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the correct solution to the quantum mechanical scattering problem for a neutron on a point-like defect in a medium shows no enhancement of the capture cross section for subbarrier ultracold neutrons, contrary to the claim by Serebrov et al. that such enhancement could explain anomalous UCN losses in storage bottles.

## Method

The authors solve the scattering problem using the distorted wave Born approximation (DWBA), where the medium is described by a complex Fermi potential and the defect by a delta-function potential. They calculate the capture cross section as proportional to the squared modulus of the initial neutron wave function at the defect position, using the exact solutions of the Schrödinger equation for the medium potential as distorted waves.

## Key numbers

- For subbarrier neutrons with energy \(E_0 < U\), the capture cross section is \(\sigma_{\text{capt}}|_{E_0<U} = \frac{4k^2}{(k+\ae'')^2+\ae'^2} e^{-2\ae' z_0} \sigma_{\text{capt}}^0\), which approximates to \(\frac{4E}{U} e^{-2\ae' z_0} \sigma_{\text{capt}}^0\).
- The proportionality factor takes a maximal value of 4 at \(E_0 = U\) and \(z_0 = 0\), arising from coherent summation of incident and reflected neutron waves.
- For realistic distances \(z_0 > 0\) between the defect and the medium surface, the factor \(|\Psi^{(1)+}_\mathbf{k}(\mathbf{r}_0)|^2\) produces suppression, not enhancement.

## Context

This comment directly refutes the mechanism proposed by Serebrov et al. (2005) for anomalous UCN losses, which was based on an incorrect solution to the scattering problem. The authors show that a repulsive external interaction (positive Fermi potential) cannot produce enhancement, drawing analogy to Coulomb effects in nuclear reactions as discussed by Wigner (1948), where attractive interactions enhance cross sections and repulsive ones suppress them. The correct DWBA treatment rules out defect scattering as an explanation for UCN losses, redirecting the search for the loss mechanism to other physical processes.
