# Spin polarization phenomena in dense neutron matter at a strong magnetic field

> Spin polarized states in neutron matter at strong magnetic fields up to $10^{18}$ G are considered in the model with the Skyrme effective interaction. Analyzing the self-consistent equations at zero temperature, it is shown that a thermodynamically stable branch of solutions for the spin polarization parameter as a function of density corresponds to the negative spin polarization when the majority of neutron spins are oriented oppositely to the direction of the magnetic field. Besides, it is found that in a strong magnetic field the state with the positive spin polarization can be realized as a metastable state at the high density region in neutron matter. At finite temperature, the entropy of the thermodynamically stable branch demonstrates the unusual behavior being larger than that for the nonpolarized state (at vanishing magnetic field) above certain critical density which is caused by the dependence of the entropy on the effective masses of neutrons in a spin polarized state.

## Key claim

At zero temperature, the thermodynamically stable branch of solutions for the spin polarization parameter in dense neutron matter at strong magnetic fields (up to 10^18 G) corresponds to negative spin polarization (majority of neutron spins oriented opposite to the magnetic field), but a positive-polarization state can exist as a metastable state at high densities; the abstract omits the qualifier that this metastable state only appears above a threshold density that depends on the field strength.

## Method

The authors model pure neutron matter using a Fermi liquid approach with the Skyrme effective interaction (SLy7 force). They solve self-consistent equations at zero and finite temperature for the neutron spin polarization parameter, chemical potential, and second-order moment of the spin distribution function, treating the magnetic field as external (B ≈ H) due to the small neutron magnetic moment. Thermodynamic stability is assessed by comparing free energies (at T=0) or entropies (at finite T) of the different solution branches.

## Key numbers

- At H = 10^18 G, the spin polarization parameter on the negative-polarization branch (Π₁) shows a plateau with Π₁ ≈ -0.08 at intermediate densities (0.5ρ₀ ≲ ρ ≲ 3ρ₀).
- Neutrons become totally polarized (Π = -1) on the negative branch at ρ ≈ 6ρ₀.
- The threshold density for the appearance of positive-polarization branches (Π₂, Π₃) is ρ_th ≈ 3.80 ρ₀ at H = 5×10^17 G and ρ_th ≈ 3.92 ρ₀ at H = 10^18 G.
- The maximum magnetic field considered is H_max ~ 10^18 G, estimated from the scalar virial theorem for a typical neutron star with M = 1.5 M_⊙ and R = 10^-5 R_⊙.

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron matter in strong magnetic fields (e.g., Perez-Garcia 2008) by identifying a metastable positive-polarization branch not found in those earlier Skyrme-model calculations. The unusual entropy behavior (polarized state less ordered than nonpolarized above a critical density) was previously observed in spontaneously polarized states of neutron matter (Rios, Polls, Vidana 2009) and nuclear matter (Isayev 2005, 2006) with Skyrme and Gogny forces. The results are relevant for understanding spin polarization in magnetar interiors and protoneutron stars.
