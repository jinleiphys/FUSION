# Bound States and Threshold Resonances in Quantum Wires with Circular Bends

> We study the solutions to the wave equation in a two-dimensional tube of unit width comprised of two straight regions connected by a region of constant curvature. We introduce a numerical method which permits high accuracy at high curvature. We determine the bound state energies as well as the transmission and reflection matrices, ${\cal T}$ and ${\cal R}$ and focus on the nature of the resonances which occur in the vicinity of channel thresholds. We explore the dependence of these solutions on the curvature of the tube and angle of the bend and discuss several limiting cases where our numerical results confirm analytic predictions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a two-dimensional quantum wire with a circular bend always supports at least one bound state below the continuum threshold, and that additional bound states appear as the bend angle increases, with the ground state binding energy approaching a finite limit determined by the first zero of the Bessel function \(J_0\) as the angle goes to infinity.

## Method

The authors solve the wave equation in a two-dimensional tube of unit width consisting of two straight segments connected by a circular arc of constant curvature \(\kappa\). They expand the wavefunction inside the curved region in terms of Bessel functions and match it to exterior solutions in the straight regions using continuity conditions. To overcome convergence problems at high curvature (\(\kappa > 0.6\)), they develop a novel numerical method that retains both rising and falling exponential solutions in closed channels and minimizes the coefficients of the rising exponentials, allowing the number of interior and exterior channel spaces to differ (\(M > L\)).

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy for \(\kappa = 1\) as \(\theta \rightarrow \infty\) approaches \((2.405)^2\), the square of the first zero of \(J_0\).
- For \(\kappa = 0.8\) and \(\theta = \pi\), the ground state eigenvalue \(\bar{k}_1\) converges to approximately \(5.4635 \times 10^{-5}\) (in units where \(\hbar^2/2m = 1\)) when using \(L = 3\) interior and \(M = 30\) exterior channels.
- The angle at which the first excited bound state appears approaches \(2\pi\) as \(\kappa\) approaches zero.
- The elastic transmission amplitude \(\T_{11}\) drops to zero at the energy of the quasibound state just below the second channel threshold at \(E = 4\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends the general proof by Goldstone and Jaffe (1992) that a bent tube always has a bound state below the continuum threshold, providing explicit numerical solutions for the specific case of a circular bend. It confirms and extends earlier numerical studies by Lent (1990) on \(\theta = \pi/2\) bends and by Sols and Macucci (1992) who found resonances near propagation thresholds and multiple bound states at large angles. The method developed here enables accurate calculations at high curvatures where traditional matching techniques fail, and the results provide a benchmark for understanding threshold resonances and bound state systematics in quantum waveguide geometries.
