# NN,NΔCouplings and the Quark Model

> We examine mass-corrected SU(6) symmetry predictions in the quark model relating vector, axial-vector and strong NN and NΔcouplings, and demonstrate that the experimental NΔvalue is significantly higher than predicted in each case. Nevertheless the Goldberger-Treiman relation is satisfied in both sectors. Possible origins of the discrepancy of the quark model predictions with experiment are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that mass-corrected SU(6) quark model predictions for vector, axial-vector, and strong NΔ couplings are systematically 25–35% lower than experimental values, yet the Goldberger-Treiman relation remains satisfied in both the NN and NΔ sectors despite these discrepancies.

## Method

The authors used the wavepacket formalism to connect coordinate-space constituent quark model wavefunctions with momentum-space form factors for NN and NΔ transitions. They equated quark model matrix elements (using S-wave quark wavefunctions with upper and lower components) to wavepacket expressions for vector form factors (f₁, f₂, c₁, c₂, c₃), axial form factors (g₁, d₁, d₂, d₄), and strong coupling constants (gπNN, gπNΔ), then scaled the NΔ results from the timelike point q₀² = (MΔ − M)² = 0.086 GeV² to q² = 0 using empirical parametrizations. Experimental values were extracted from photoproduction data (for vector NΔ couplings), neutrino scattering experiments (for axial NΔ couplings), and πN phase shift analyses (for strong couplings).

## Key numbers

- Experimental vector NΔ form factor c₁(0) = 7.21 ± 0.78, compared to quark model prediction c₁(0) ≈ 4.8 (more than 30% larger)
- Experimental axial NΔ form factor d₁(0) = 2.4 ± 0.25 (from Kitagaki et al. data), compared to quark model prediction d₁QM(0) = 1.5 ± 0.05 (more than 35% larger)
- Experimental strong coupling ratio gπNΔ/gπNN = 2.21 ± 0.08, compared to mass-corrected SU(6) prediction of approximately 1.6 (25% larger)
- Experimental gπNN = 13.05 ± 0.31 and gπNΔ = 28.6 ± 0.3
- Kitagaki et al. measured ratio R = 0.50 ± 0.05 at q² = 0.1 GeV² for νd → μ⁻Δ⁺⁺n relative to νd → μ⁻pp
- Experimental d₁(0) = 2.0 ± 0.4 from Barish et al. (consistent with Kitagaki result within uncertainties)

## Context

This work extends the constituent quark model framework of SU(6) symmetry, previously applied to NN couplings, to the NΔ sector with explicit mass corrections. The systematic underestimation of NΔ couplings (vector, axial, and strong) contradicts the simple picture where nucleon and Δ(1232) share identical internal quark dynamics with only color-hyperfine mass splitting. The results enable future work to explore origins of the discrepancy, such as gluonic hyperfine effects (which the authors note are unable to account for the excess M1 strength), and confirm that the generalized Goldberger-Treiman relation (connecting d₁ and gπNΔ) remains valid despite the quark model failures.
