# Nucleon-nucleon potential from instanton holonomies

> We derive the nucleon-nucleon interaction from the Skyrme model using the instanton and product approximations to skyrmion dynamics. In doing so, we also calculate the classical potential and metric for skyrmion dynamics in each of the approximations. This is the first time they have been compared in detail and the results show major disagreements between the approximations. We derive the eight low energy nucleon-nucleon interaction potentials and compare them with the Paris model. For the instanton approximation we find strong negative isoscalar and isovector spin-orbit potentials, matching phenomenological models and our geometric intuition. Results for the other potentials are mixed, in part due to the zero pion mass limit used in this approximation.

## Key claim

The nucleon-nucleon interaction potentials derived from the Skyrme model using the instanton approximation show strong negative isoscalar and isovector spin-orbit potentials matching phenomenological models, but results for the other potentials are mixed, in part due to the zero pion mass limit used in this approximation.

## Method

The authors calculated the classical potential and metric for two-skyrmion dynamics using the dipole, product, and instanton approximations, then quantised the system using second-order perturbation theory to extract the eight low-energy nucleon-nucleon interaction potentials. The instanton approximation was implemented using the ADHM construction to solve the parallel transport equation by multiplying projection operators, allowing efficient computation across the seven-dimensional configuration space. The Fourier coefficients of the metric and potential were computed numerically on a grid of 61 radial points and 32 points on the 3-sphere, with three grid resolutions used for extrapolation.

## Key numbers

- The moment of inertia of a single skyrmion is Λ = 106.83 (in Skyrme units)
- The mass of a single skyrmion is M = 145.85 (in Skyrme units)
- The constant C in the asymptotic tail of the 1-skyrmion profile function is 2.1596 (for m=0)
- The axial 2-skyrmion occurs at separation r = 1.731 (in Skyrme units)
- The radial range sampled was r ∈ [1.731, 7.731] with lattice spacing 0.1 (in Skyrme units)
- The dipole approximation yields leading-order Fourier coefficients scaling as ρ/r² for A, ρ/r for B¹², ρ/r³ for C, and ρ/r³ for D

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of the nucleon-nucleon interaction in the Skyrme model, which had generally found the spin-orbit force with the wrong sign when using the product approximation and first-order perturbation theory (Nyman 1986, Otofuji 1987, Kaelbermann 1995, Abada 1996). The authors had previously attempted to correct this using the dipole approximation and second-order perturbation theory (Halcrow 2020) but found an error in that calculation. The instanton approximation, originally proposed by Atiyah and Manton (1989), is the only known approximation that properly accounts for the geometry of the 2-skyrmion space. This work enables future researchers to calculate nucleon-nucleon interactions using different versions of the Skyrme model by providing data files of the NN potentials in terms of the classical metric and potential.
