# A Comparative Interpretation of the Thermodynamic Functions of a Relativistic Bound State problem proposed with an Attractive or a Repulsive Surface Effect

> Generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential (GSWSP) energy well has a significant importance not only in nuclear physics but also in atomic and molecular physics. A GSWSP energy well takes the surface effects (describing e.g. repulsive interaction at the nucleus edge in nuclear physics) into account in addition to the volume effect. These effects can be, in general, both repulsive or attractive. In this paper, the recently obtained bound state solution of the Klein-Gordon equation with vector and scalar GSWSP energy in the spin symmetry limit is used to calculate a neutral pion's energy spectra in attractive and repulsive cases via various potential parameters. Then, the spectra are employed to find the thermodynamic functions such as Helmholtz free energy, entropy, internal energy, and specific heat. These functions in the attractive and repulsive cases are compared comprehensively. Finally, the role of the shape parameters on the thermodynamic functions in repulsive and attractive cases, respectively, is analyzed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the thermodynamic functions (Helmholtz free energy, entropy, internal energy, specific heat) of a neutral pion confined in a generalized symmetric Woods-Saxon potential (GSWSP) well differ significantly depending on whether the surface effect is repulsive or attractive, and that these functions are sensitive to the shape parameters (slope parameter \(a\) and effective length \(L\)), but only when the depth parameter \(V_0\) is sufficiently large (equal to the rest mass energy \(m_0c^2\)) to allow a full range of bound states.

## Method

The authors solved the Klein-Gordon equation with vector and scalar GSWSP in the spin symmetry limit to obtain bound-state energy spectra for a neutral pion (rest mass 134.976 MeV) under both repulsive (\(W_0 = +2m_0c^2\)) and attractive (\(W_0 = -2m_0c^2\)) surface effects, using the Newton-Raphson method on transcendental equations derived in prior work (Lutfuoglu, 2018). They then used these discrete energy spectra to compute the canonical partition function and derived thermodynamic functions (Helmholtz free energy, internal energy, entropy, specific heat) as functions of temperature, comparing results across variations of the slope parameter \(a\) (1.0, 1.5, 2.0 fm\(^{-1}\)) and effective length \(L\) (6, 7, 8 fm) for two depth values \(V_0 = m_0c^2/2\) and \(V_0 = m_0c^2\).

## Key numbers

- Neutral pion rest mass used: 134.976 MeV.
- For repulsive case with \(V_0 = m_0c^2/2\), \(W_0 = 2m_0c^2\), \(L = 6\) fm, \(a = 1.0\) fm\(^{-1}\): ground state energy \(E_0 = 30.7239\) MeV; first excited \(E_1 = 82.4986\) MeV; second excited \(E_2 = 131.9274\) MeV.
- For repulsive case with \(V_0 = m_0c^2\), \(W_0 = 2m_0c^2\), \(L = 6\) fm, \(a = 1.0\) fm\(^{-1}\): ground state \(E_0 = -75.9057\) MeV; \(E_1 = -10.7144\) MeV; \(E_2 = 46.3186\) MeV; \(E_3 = 97.0587\) MeV.
- For attractive case with \(V_0 = m_0c^2/2\), \(W_0 = -2m_0c^2\), \(L = 6\) fm, \(a = 1.0\) fm\(^{-1}\): no \(E_0\) or \(E_1\) states exist; first state is \(E_2 = 34.8076\) MeV; \(E_3 = 68.0564\) MeV; \(E_4 = 98.1005\) MeV; \(E_5 = 123.2749\) MeV.
- For attractive case with \(V_0 = m_0c^2\), \(W_0 = -2m_0c^2\), \(L = 6\) fm, \(a = 1.0\) fm\(^{-1}\): ground state \(E_0 = -107.4134\) MeV; \(E_1 = -72.0512\) MeV; \(E_2 = -30.0914\) MeV; \(E_3 = 10.4123\) MeV; \(E_4 = 47.5805\) MeV; \(E_5 = 81.0955\) MeV; \(E_6 = 110.2205\) MeV; \(E_7 = 131.7276\) MeV.
- Barrier height for repulsive case (when \(|W_0| > V_0\)) given by \(\frac{(V_0 - W_0)^2}{4W_0}\); for \(V_0 = m_0c^2/2\), \(W_0 = 2m_0c^2\), barrier height is 37.962 MeV; for \(V_0 = m_0c^2\), barrier height is 16.872 MeV.
- Pocket depth for attractive case (when \(|W_0| > V_0\)) given by same formula; for \(V_0 = m_0c^2/2\), \(W_0 = -2m_0c^2\), minimum pocket depth is -105.4500 MeV; for \(V_0 = m_0c^2\), maximum well depth is -151.848 MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Lutfuoglu and collaborators (Lutfuoglu et al., 2016, 2017, 2018) that examined thermodynamic functions of nucleons and alpha particles in GSWSP wells with only repulsive surface effects, and by Lutfuoglu (2018) who solved the Klein-Gordon bound state for GSWSP. It also connects to the broader literature on thermodynamic functions for Woods-Saxon-type potentials (Ikhdair et al., 2013; Onate et al., 2015; Ikot et al., 2016) and to the equivalence between the improved Rosen-Morse potential and GSWSP established by Zhang et al. (2012), suggesting applicability to diatomic molecular thermodynamics. The paper fills a gap by systematically comparing attractive versus repulsive surface effects on thermodynamic functions, and by analyzing the role of shape parameters \(a\) and \(L\) in both regimes.
