# Theory for Quartet Condensation in Fermi Systems with Applications to Nuclei and Nuclear Matter

> The theory of quartet condensation is further developed. The onset of quartetting in homgeneous fermionic matter is studied with the help of an in-medium modified four fermion equation. It is found that at very low density quartetting wins over pairing. At zero temperature, in analogy to pairing, a set of equations for the quartet order parameter is given. Contrary to pairing, quartetting only exists for strong coupling and breaks down for weak coupling. Reasons for this finding are detailed. In an application to nuclear matter, the critical temperature for alpha particle condensation can reach values up to around 8 MeV. The disappearance of alpha particles with increasing density, i.e. the Mott transition, is investigated. In finite nuclei the Hoyle state, that is the second 0+ state of 12C is identified as an 'alpha-particle condensate' state. It is conjectured that such states also exist in heavier n-alpha nuclei, like 16O, 20Ne, etc. The sixth 0+ state in 16O is proposed as an analogue to the Hoyle state. The Gross-Pitaevski equation is employed to make an estimate of the maximum number of alpha particles a condensate state can contain. Possible quartet condensation in other systems is discussed briefly.

## Key claim

Quartet condensation in Fermi systems exists only in the strong-coupling (BEC) regime with negative chemical potential, unlike pairing which also exists in the weak-coupling (BCS) regime; the authors show that for positive chemical potential the three-hole level density passes through zero, preventing the formation of quartet correlations around the Fermi energy.

## Method

The authors develop a theory for quartet condensation analogous to BCS theory for pairing, introducing a quartet condensate wavefunction and a killing operator that superposes two fermion pair operators. They derive a bosonic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equation for fermion pairs and an effective single-particle equation with a three-body gap field, then apply an in-medium modified four-nucleon equation (Thouless criterion) to study the onset of quartetting in homogeneous symmetric nuclear matter. The in-medium binding energies of light clusters (deuteron, triton/helium-3, alpha particle) are computed using perturbation theory and Faddeev-Yakubovski techniques with Pauli blocking.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature for alpha particle condensation in nuclear matter can reach values up to around 8 MeV.
- The saturation density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter is rho_0 approximately 0.16 nucleons per fm^3.
- The binding energy of an alpha-like cluster with zero center-of-mass momentum vanishes at around rho_0/10.
- The alpha particle has a binding energy per nucleon of approximately 7 MeV.
- The rms radius of the 0+ ground state of 8Be is about 3.7 fm, compared to the nuclear systematics value of 2.44 fm from R = r_0 A^(1/3).
- The ground state of 12C has a radius of 2.4 fm.

## Context

This work extends the theory of quartet condensation previously developed for nuclear systems, building on the Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach of Jemai and collaborators. The authors identify the Hoyle state (second 0+ state of 12C) as an alpha-particle condensate state and propose the sixth 0+ state in 16O as an analogue. The study enables further investigation of alpha condensate states in heavier n-alpha nuclei such as 16O and 20Ne, and opens the possibility of searching for quartet condensation in other Fermi systems with four different fermion species, such as cold atoms in four magnetic substates as suggested by Salomon and collaborators.
