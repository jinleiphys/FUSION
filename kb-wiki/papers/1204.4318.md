# Nuclear thermodynamics and the in-medium chiral condensate

> The temperature dependence of the chiral condensate in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter at varying baryon density is investigated using thermal in-medium chiral effective field theory. This framework provides a realistic approach to the thermodynamics of the correlated nuclear many-body system and permits calculating systematically the pion-mass dependence of the free energy per particle. One- and two-pion exchange processes, $Δ(1232)$-isobar excitations, Pauli blocking corrections and three-body correlations are treated up to and including three loops in the expansion of the free energy density. It is found that nuclear matter remains in the Nambu-Goldstone phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry in the temperature range $T\lesssim 100\,$MeV and at baryon densities at least up to about twice the density of normal nuclear matter, $2ρ_0 \simeq 0.3\, $fm$^{-3}$. Effects of the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition on the chiral condensate at low temperatures are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper finds that nuclear matter remains in the Nambu-Goldstone phase with spontaneously broken chiral symmetry for temperatures up to about 100 MeV and baryon densities at least up to twice normal nuclear matter density (2ρ₀ ≈ 0.3 fm⁻³), with no indication of a first-order chiral phase transition in this range. This result is conditional on the inclusion of Δ(1232)-isobar excitations and Pauli blocking effects, which are crucial for stabilizing the condensate at higher densities.

## Method

The authors use thermal in-medium chiral effective field theory to compute the free energy density of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter up to three-loop order, including one- and two-pion exchange processes, Δ(1232)-isobar excitations, Pauli blocking corrections, and three-body correlations. They then apply the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to relate the derivative of the free energy density with respect to the squared pion mass to the in-medium chiral condensate ⟨q̄q⟩(ρ,T). The calculation also incorporates the effects of thermal pions and the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition at low temperatures.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density: ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Twice normal nuclear matter density: 2ρ₀ ≈ 0.3 fm⁻³
- Temperature range investigated: T ≲ 100 MeV
- Nucleon sigma term used: σ_N = 45 MeV (central value, with empirical range σ_N = (45 ± 8) MeV at physical pion mass m_π = 135 MeV)
- Critical temperature for the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition: T_c ≈ 15 MeV
- Effective nucleon sigma term from the modified low-density theorem: σ̃_N ≈ 36 MeV
- Binding energy per particle of saturated nuclear matter: Ē₀ ≈ -16 MeV
- Chiral limit parameters used: f_π = 86.5 MeV, g_A = 1.224, c₁ = -0.93 GeV⁻¹, M_N = 882 MeV

## Context

This work extends a previous chiral effective field theory calculation by the same group (Fiorilla, Kaiser, Weise, 2011) of the density-dependent in-medium condensate at zero temperature to finite temperatures. It contrasts with earlier studies using QCD sum rules (Drukarev, 1980s) or boson exchange models (Li and Ko, 1994; Brown and Weise, 1996) for the density dependence at T=0, and with schematic Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) approaches (Hatsuda and Kunihiro, 1980s; Klevansky, Lemmer, and Weise, 1990s) that included temperature effects but did not properly account for nucleon correlations. The results set nuclear physics constraints for the QCD equation of state relevant to relativistic heavy-ion collisions and are consistent with a recent investigation of chemical freeze-out in heavy-ion collisions (Fiorilla and Weise, 2012).
