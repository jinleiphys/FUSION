# Eta, eta-prime photoproduction and electroproduction off nucleons

> The photo- and electroproduction of eta, eta-prime mesons on nucleons are investigated within a relativistic chiral unitary approach based on coupled channels. The s-wave potentials for electroproduction and meson-baryon scattering are derived from a chiral effective Lagrangian which includes the eta-prime as an explicit degree of freedom and incorporates important features of the underlying QCD Lagrangian such as the axial U(1) anomaly. The effective potentials are iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation and cross sections for eta, eta-prime photo- and electroproduction from nucleons are obtained. The results for the eta-prime photoproduction cross section on protons reproduce the appearance of an S_{11} resonance around 1.9 GeV observed at ELSA. The inclusion of electromagnetic form factors increases the predicted eta electroproduction cross sections on the proton, providing a qualitative explanation for the hard form factor of the photocoupling amplitude observed at CLAS.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic chiral unitary approach, which includes the eta-prime as an explicit degree of freedom and incorporates the axial U(1) anomaly, can reproduce the S11 resonance around 1.9 GeV observed in eta-prime photoproduction at ELSA and provide a qualitative explanation for the hard form factor of the photocoupling amplitude seen at CLAS, but only when restricted to s-waves and near-threshold energies.

## Method

The authors derived s-wave potentials for electroproduction and meson-baryon scattering from a U(3) chiral effective Lagrangian that includes the eta-prime explicitly. These potentials were iterated in a Bethe-Salpeter equation within a coupled-channel formalism (five channels for isospin 1/2: pi N, eta N, K Lambda, K Sigma, eta-prime N; two channels for isospin 3/2: pi N, K Sigma) to obtain cross sections for eta and eta-prime photo- and electroproduction off nucleons.

## Key numbers

* The S11 resonance in eta-prime photoproduction on protons appears around 1.9 GeV, consistent with ELSA observations.
* The pion decay constant used is f_pi = 92.4 MeV.
* The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Lambda_chi = 4 pi F_pi = 1.2 GeV.
* The eta-prime mass is 958 MeV.
* The axial vector couplings extracted from semileptonic hyperon decays are D = 0.80 +/- 0.01 and F = 0.46 +/- 0.01.
* The low-energy constants from baryon mass differences are b_D = 0.066 GeV^{-1} and b_F = -0.213 GeV^{-1}.
* The pion-nucleon sigma-term is sigma_{pi N} = 45 +/- 8 MeV (or alternatively 60 +/- 7 MeV from other authors).
* The decuplet coupling constant |C| = 1.2-1.8 from decays T -> B pi.
* The eta electroproduction data from CLAS covers Q^2 between 0.375 and 1.375 GeV^2.
* A possible onset of scaling in e + p -> e + p + eta is reported at Q^2 = 3.6 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the coupled-channel chiral unitary approach previously applied to the Lambda(1405) by Kaiser, Siegel, and Weise (1995) and to the S11(1535) by Kaiser, Waas, and Weise (1997), by including the eta-prime as an explicit degree of freedom and incorporating the axial U(1) anomaly. It contrasts with the effective Lagrangian approach of Zhao, Mukherjee, and Bennhold (2001) which used pseudoscalar eta-prime coupling and found dominance of the D13(2080), and with the quark model of Li (1997) which emphasized off-shell effects of the S11(1535). The results enable predictions for eta-prime electroproduction that will be testable at Jefferson Lab, and provide a framework for understanding gluonic effects and the axial U(1) anomaly in low-energy hadron physics.
