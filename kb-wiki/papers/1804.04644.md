# Partonic quasidistributions in two-dimensional QCD

> As a sequel of our preceding work [1] we carry out a comprehensive comparative study between the quasi parton distribution functions (PDFs), distribution amplitudes (DAs) and their light-cone counterparts for various flavor-neutral mesons, in the context of the 't Hooft model, that is, the two-dimensional QCD in the large $N$ limit. In contrast to the original derivation via diagrammatic techniques exemplified by Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, here we employ the Hamiltonian operator approach to reconstruct the celebrated 't Hooft equation in light-front quantization, and Bars-Green equations in equal-time quantization. The novelty of our derivation is to employ the soft momentum cutoff as the IR regulator. As a virtue of this operator approach, the functional form of the quasi distributions can be transparently built out of the Bars-Green wave functions and the Bogoliubov angle with the aid of bosonization technique. Equipped with various bound-state wave functions numerically inferred in [1], we then investigate how rapidly the quasi distributions approach their light-cone counterparts with the increasing meson momentum. We observe that, light mesons' quasi distributions approach the light-cone distributions in a slower pace than the heavy quarkonia. Curiously, lattice simulations of quasi distributions in four-dimensional QCD also discover this feature. Furthermore, we also compute the partonic light-cone PDF and quasi-PDF to one-loop order in perturbation theory, again employing the momentum cutoff as the IR regulator. We explicitly verify one of the backbones underlying the large momentum effective field theory (LaMET), namely, both quasi-PDFs and light-cone PDFs in ${\rm QCD}_2$ indeed possess the same IR behavior at leading order in $1/P^z$

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in the 't Hooft model (two-dimensional QCD in the large N limit), quasi parton distribution functions and light-cone parton distribution functions share identical infrared behavior at leading order in 1/P^z, but light mesons' quasi distributions approach their light-cone counterparts more slowly under increasing meson momentum than heavy quarkonia do.

## Method

The authors employed a Hamiltonian operator approach with a soft momentum cutoff as the infrared regulator to derive the 't Hooft equation in light-front quantization and the Bars-Green equations in equal-time quantization. They constructed quasi distributions from Bars-Green wave functions and the Bogoliubov angle using bosonization techniques, then numerically solved for bound-state wave functions of various flavor-neutral mesons to compare quasi and light-cone distributions at different meson momenta. Additionally, they computed light-cone PDFs and quasi-PDFs to one-loop order in perturbation theory using the same momentum cutoff regularization.

## Key numbers

- The 't Hooft coupling constant is defined as λ ≡ g_s^2 N / (4π), fixed in the large N limit
- The soft momentum cutoff ρ → 0^+ is introduced as an infrared regulator in the Green functions G_ρ^(1) and G_ρ^(2)
- The dimensionless momentum ratio x for light-cone wave functions is restricted to the interval [0,1], while for Bars-Green wave functions x is unbounded (−∞ < x < ∞)
- In the infinite momentum frame (P → ∞), the positive-energy Bars-Green wave function φ_+^n(xP,P) asymptotically approaches the 't Hooft wave function φ^n(x), while the negative-energy component φ_-^n(xP,P) vanishes

## Context

This work extends the authors' preceding numerical study (Jia 2017) of Bars-Green equations in the 't Hooft model by constructing quasi distributions from those wave functions. It provides a nonperturbative test of the Large Momentum Effective Field Theory (LaMET) framework introduced by Ji (2013, 2014), explicitly verifying that quasi-PDFs and light-cone PDFs share identical IR behavior in QCD_2. The results enable future benchmarking of different approaches to extracting parton distributions from lattice QCD, including the quasi distribution approach, pseudo-PDF method (Radyushkin 2017), and lattice cross section approach (Ma 2017).
