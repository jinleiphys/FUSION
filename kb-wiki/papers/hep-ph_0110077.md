# Thermal fluctuations in the interacting pion gas

> We derive the two-particle fluctuation correlator in a thermal gas of pi-mesons to the lowest order in an interaction due to a resonance exchange. A diagrammatic technique is used. We discuss how this result can be applied to event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, in particular, to search for the critical point of QCD. As a practical example, we determine the shape of the rapidity correlator.

## Key claim

The paper derives the leading-order correction to the two-pion fluctuation correlator in a thermal gas due to sigma-meson exchange, but the result is strictly rigorous only in the limit of small coupling constant; the author conjectures that using an effective (rather than bare) interaction and effective self-energies may make the leading-order calculation sufficient in practice for hot QCD at freezeout temperatures around 120 MeV.

## Method

The author uses a diagrammatic technique in the mixed time-momentum representation to compute the free energy of an interacting pion gas to order G², where the interaction is given by a σππ coupling. By introducing fictitious chemical potentials for each momentum mode and differentiating the resulting free energy, the two-particle fluctuation correlator is extracted from the diagrams. Self-energy-type corrections proportional to δ_{pk} are discarded under the assumption that they have been absorbed into renormalized single-particle energies.

## Key numbers

- Temperature T = 120 MeV is used as the characteristic freezeout temperature for high-energy heavy-ion collisions.
- The coupling-to-mass ratio G/˜m = 4.5 is used in the example calculation of the rapidity correlator (motivated by prior work Stephanov (1999) and Berdnikov (2000)).
- The rapidity correlator C(y_p - y_k) vanishes exponentially with increasing |y_p - y_k| on the scale of 1-2 units of rapidity.
- The centrality dependence of the correlator from the t-channel exchange term is predicted to be linear in dN/dy.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of pion fluctuations in a thermal gas beyond the free Bose gas result (Landau) and beyond the t-channel sigma exchange contribution previously calculated by Stephanov (1999) using a different method. The diagrammatic approach allows inclusion of s- and u-channel exchange terms, which are subleading when the sigma mass approaches zero near the QCD critical point. The results enable quantitative predictions for event-by-event fluctuation measures in heavy-ion collisions, such as the rapidity correlator, and can be generalized to other resonance exchanges like the rho meson, with applications to charge fluctuation studies (Jeon and Koch (2000), charge, balance, Heiselberg and Jackson (2000), Gazdzicki, and others).
