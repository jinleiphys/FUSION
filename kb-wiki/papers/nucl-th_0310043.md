# Zemach Moments for Hydrogen and Deuterium

> We determine the Zemach moments of hydrogen and deuterium for the first time using only the world data on elastic electron-proton and electron-deuteron scattering. Such moments are required for the calculation of the nuclear corrections to the hyperfine structure of these hydrogenic atoms. We compare the resulting HFS predictions to the available high-precision data and provide an estimate of the size of the nuclear polarization corrections necessary to produce agreement between experimental HFS and theoretical calculations.

# Zemach Moments for Hydrogen and Deuterium

## Key claim

The Zemach moments for hydrogen and deuterium are determined for the first time using only world data on elastic electron-proton and electron-deuteron scattering, yielding values that are more precise than previous model-dependent estimates; however, the resulting hyperfine splitting predictions still require nuclear polarization corrections of about 3.2(5) ppm for hydrogen to match experiment, indicating that either the polarization or recoil corrections may be more uncertain than currently believed.

## Method

The authors analyzed world data on elastic electron-proton scattering up to a maximum momentum transfer of 4 fm^{-1} using a 5-parameter continued fraction expansion for both the charge and magnetic form factors, and electron-deuteron data up to 8 fm^{-1} using a 10-parameter SOG parameterization for the electric monopole, magnetic dipole, and electric quadrupole form factors. Charge and magnetic form factors were fit simultaneously to the cross section data, with Coulomb distortion of electron waves included, and the error matrix from the fit was used to propagate statistical and systematic uncertainties into the Zemach moment integral.

## Key numbers

- Proton Zemach moment: 1.086 ± 0.012 fm
- Deuteron Zemach moment: 2.593 ± 0.016 fm
- Proton Zemach correction to hyperfine splitting: −41.0(5) ppm relative to the Fermi splitting
- Deuteron Zemach correction to hyperfine splitting: −98.0(6) ppm relative to the Fermi splitting
- Experimental 1S hyperfine splitting in hydrogen: 1,420,405.751,768(1) kHz
- Experimental 1S hyperfine splitting in deuterium: 327,384.352,522(2) kHz
- Residue after subtracting QED and Zemach corrections for hydrogen: 3.2(5) ppm (without polarization)
- Leading-order recoil contribution for hydrogen: 5.22 ppm

## Context

This work extends the comprehensive analysis of electron-proton scattering data by Sick (2003) and Sick (1996b) to the calculation of Zemach moments, which are required for nuclear corrections to hyperfine structure. Previous calculations of the Zemach moment relied on simple dipole form factor parameterizations or the Hoehler 8.2 fit, giving values around 1.0 fm for the proton, while the present result of 1.086 fm is significantly larger. The results enable more precise tests of nuclear polarization calculations, such as those by Faustov (2002) and Milshtein (1996), and provide a benchmark for potential model calculations of the deuteron, as demonstrated by comparison with the AV18 potential model which gave 2.656 fm.
