# Solar neutrinos: global analysis and implications for SNO

> We present a global analysis of all the available solar neutrino data treating consistently the 8B and hep neutrino fluxes as free parameters. The analysis reveals at 99.7% C.L. eight currently-allowed discrete regions in two-neutrino oscillation space, five regions corresponding to active neutrinos and three corresponding to sterile neutrinos. Most of the allowed solutions are robust with respect to changes in the analysis procedure, but the traditional vacuum solution is fragile. The globally-permitted range of the 8B neutrino flux, 0.45 to 1.95 in units of the BP2000 flux, is comparable to the 3 sigma range allowed by the standard solar model. We discuss the implications for SNO of a low mass, Delta m^2 ~ 6 times 10^{-12} eV^2, vacuum oscillation solution, previously found by Raghavan, and by Krastev and Petcov, but absent in recent analyses that included Super-Kamiokande data. For the SNO experiment, we present refined predictions for the charged-current rate and the ratio of the neutral-current rate to charged-current rate. The predicted charged-current rate can be clearly distinguished from the no-oscillation rate only for the LMA solution. The predicted ratio of the neutral-current rate to charged-current rate is distinguishable from the no-oscillation ratio for the LMA, SMA, LOW, and VAC solutions for active neutrinos.

## Key claim

The global analysis reveals eight discrete allowed regions in two-neutrino oscillation space at 99.7% C.L. (five for active neutrinos, three for sterile neutrinos), but only when the 8B and hep neutrino fluxes are treated as free parameters rather than constrained by standard solar model predictions; the traditional vacuum solution is fragile and disappears when Super-Kamiokande rate data are included.

## Method

The authors performed a global chi-squared fit of all available solar neutrino data (Homestake chlorine, SAGE, GALLEX+GNO total rates, and Super-Kamiokande day and night electron recoil energy spectra) on a 201x500 lattice over Delta m^2 from 10^-12 to 10^-3 eV^2 and tan^2 theta from 10^-4 to 10^1, treating the 8B and hep neutrino fluxes as free parameters consistently in both rate and spectrum fits. Survival probabilities were calculated numerically using the BP2000 solar model electron density profile, with a hybrid algorithm for different E/Delta m^2 regimes and including Earth regeneration effects for the relevant parameter range.

## Key numbers

- Best-fit LMA solution: Delta m^2 = 4.2 x 10^-5 eV^2, tan^2 theta = 2.6 x 10^-1, chi^2_min = 29.0 (75% g.o.f.)
- Best-fit SMA solution: Delta m^2 = 5.2 x 10^-6 eV^2, tan^2 theta = 5.5 x 10^-4, chi^2_min = 31.1 (66% g.o.f.)
- Best-fit LOW solution: Delta m^2 = 7.6 x 10^-8 eV^2, tan^2 theta = 7.2 x 10^-1, chi^2_min = 36.0 (42% g.o.f.)
- Best-fit Just So^2 solution: Delta m^2 = 5.5 x 10^-12 eV^2, tan^2 theta = 1.0 x 10^0, chi^2_min = 36.1 (42% g.o.f.)
- Globally-permitted range of 8B neutrino flux: 0.45 to 1.95 in units of the BP2000 flux (compared to the 3-sigma SSM range of 0.52 to 1.6)
- Super-Kamiokande measured 8B flux: (2.40 +/- 0.03 +0.08/-0.07) x 10^6 cm^-2 s^-1
- Just So^2 solution predicts Super-Kamiokande rate of 0.461 of standard model rate (measured: 0.475 +/- 0.016)

## Context

This work extends the global analysis approach of Bahcall, Krastev, and Smirnov (1998) and Fogli, Lisi, and Montanino (1998) by treating the 8B and hep fluxes as free parameters consistently across all data, rather than constraining them with standard solar model uncertainties. The analysis reveals that the Just So^2 solution, previously found by Raghavan (1995) and by Krastev and Petcov (1996) but absent in recent analyses that included Super-Kamiokande data, reappears when the 8B flux is allowed to vary freely. The results provide refined predictions for the SNO experiment's charged-current rate and neutral-current to charged-current ratio, enabling discrimination among oscillation solutions once SNO data become available.
