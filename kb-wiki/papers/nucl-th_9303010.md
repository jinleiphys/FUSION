# Ghost Poles in the Nucleon Propagator: Vertex Corrections and Form Factors

> Vertex corrections are taken into account in the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator in a relativistic field theory of fermions and mesons. The usual Hartree-Fock approximation for the nucleon propagator is known to produce the appearance of complex (ghost) poles which violate basic theorems of quantum field theory. In a theory with vector mesons there are vertex corrections that produce a strongly damped vertex function in the ultraviolet. One set of such corrections is known as the Sudakov form factor in quantum electrodynamics. When the Sudakov form factor generated by massive neutral vector mesons is included in the Hartree-Fock approximation to the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator, the ghost poles disappear and consistency with basic requirements of quantum field theory is recovered.

## Key claim

When the Sudakov form factor generated by massive neutral vector mesons is included in the Hartree-Fock approximation to the Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator, the ghost poles disappear and consistency with basic requirements of quantum field theory is recovered; however, the negative spectral function regions associated with negative metric states persist even after the complex poles are eliminated.

## Method

The authors solved the renormalized Schwinger-Dyson equation for the nucleon propagator in a relativistic field theory of nucleons, pions, and omega mesons, using the Hartree-Fock approximation modified by vertex corrections. They replaced the bare vertices with Sudakov form factors (derived from summing leading-log vertex corrections from vector meson dressing) and with phenomenological monopole form factors, then solved the coupled integral equations iteratively for the spectral function A_R(kappa) and the renormalized inverse propagator.

## Key numbers

* For the pion-only case with Sudakov form factor, ghost poles disappear for cutoff values below Lambda_crit approximately 0.9 M (where M is the nucleon mass), and the renormalization constant Z_2 equals 0.16.
* For the omega-meson-only case with Sudakov form factor, ghost poles disappear for Lambda_crit approximately 0.55 M.
* In the Hartree-Fock approximation without vertex corrections, the pion-only case produced complex poles at z = (0.73 plus or minus 1.25 i) M with residues -0.75 plus or minus 0.32 i.
* In the Hartree-Fock approximation without vertex corrections, the omega-only case produced complex poles at z = (5.67 plus or minus 11.76 i) M with residues -1.0412 plus or minus 0.22 i.
* In the Hartree-Fock approximation without vertex corrections, the combined pion and omega case produced complex poles at z = (1.05 minus or plus 1.26 i) M with residues -0.77 plus or minus 0.20 i.

## Context

This work extends the earlier analysis of Brown, Puff, and Wilets (1970) who first identified ghost poles in the nucleon propagator within the Hartree-Fock approximation. It follows the approach of Allendes and Serot (1992) who included the Sudakov form factor in the polarization loop correction to the vector meson propagator and found that ghosts were eliminated there. The results enable consistent calculation of vacuum corrections to mean field approximations in relativistic nuclear field theories, though the authors note that the off-shell behavior of vertex functions at low momentum transfer requires further investigation.
