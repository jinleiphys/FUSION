# Is $K_{1}/K^{*}$ enhancement in heavy ion collisions a signature of chiral symmetry restoration?

> We extend the recent study of $K_{1}/K^{*}$ enhancement as a signature of chiral symmetry restoration in heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) via the kinetic approach to include the effects due to non-unity hadron fugacities during the evolution of produced hadronic matter and the temperature-dependent $K_1$ mass. Although the effect of non-unity fugacity only slightly reduces the $K_1/K^*$ enhancement due to chiral symmetry restoration, the inclusion of the temperature-dependent $K_1$ mass leads to a substantial reduction in the $K_1/K^*$ enhancement. However, the final $K_1/K^*$ ratio in peripheral collisions still shows a more than factor of two enhancement compared to the case without chiral symmetry restoration and thus remains a good signature for chiral symmetry restoration in the hot dense matter produced in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

# Is $K_{1}/K^{*}$ enhancement in heavy ion collisions a signature of chiral symmetry restoration?

## Key claim
The $K_{1}/K^{*}$ yield ratio in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies shows a factor of 2.4 enhancement compared to the case without chiral symmetry restoration, even after including non-unity hadron fugacities and a temperature-dependent $K_{1}$ mass that substantially reduce the enhancement found in prior work. The enhancement remains a good signature for chiral symmetry restoration, but the effect is weaker and more centrality-dependent than previously claimed.

## Method
The authors solve coupled kinetic equations for the time evolution of $K_{1}$ and $K^{*}$ numbers during the hadronic stage of Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV, using a schematic hydrodynamic model for the temperature evolution. They incorporate temperature-dependent $K_{1}$ mass from QCD sum rules (Lee 2023) and non-unity pion, kaon, and nucleon fugacities determined by requiring constant effective particle numbers and constant entropy per particle during hadronic expansion. The kinetic equations include reactions $K_{1}\pi \leftrightarrow K\pi$, $K_{1}\pi \leftrightarrow K^{*}\rho$, $K_{1}\rho \leftrightarrow K^{*}\pi$, $K_{1}\rho \leftrightarrow K\rho$, $K_{1} \leftrightarrow K^{*}\pi$, $K_{1} \leftrightarrow K\rho$, $K^{*}\pi \leftrightarrow K\rho$, $K^{*}\rho \leftrightarrow K\pi$, and $K^{*} \leftrightarrow K\pi$, with thermal-averaged cross sections calculated using the massive Yang-Mills approach.

## Key numbers
- The $K_{1}/K^{*}$ ratio in peripheral collisions (70-80% centrality) is 0.357, compared to 0.08 in central collisions (0-5% centrality) and 0.158 in mid-central collisions (40-50% centrality), including both non-unity fugacities and temperature-dependent $K_{1}$ mass.
- The final $K_{1}/K^{*}$ ratio in peripheral collisions shows a factor of 2.4 enhancement compared to the case without chiral symmetry restoration.
- The $K_{1}$ mass at the critical temperature $T_{C}=156$ MeV is about 1.1 GeV, rather than the $K^{*}$ free-space mass of 0.892 GeV assumed in prior work (Sung 2021).
- The statistical model prediction for $K_{1}/K^{*}$ is about 0.14, independent of collision centrality.
- In the limit of fast chemical equilibration (very large $\rho$ and $K_{1}$ widths), the $K_{1}/K^{*}$ ratio approaches 0.182 for peripheral collisions, 0.084 for mid-central collisions, and 0.053 for central collisions.
- The constant entropy per particle during hadronic evolution has a value of 6.1.
- Initial volumes for the three centralities are 6,076 fm$^{3}$ (0-5%), 938 fm$^{3}$ (40-50%), and 135 fm$^{3}$ (70-80%).
- Kinetic freeze-out temperatures are 90 MeV (0-5%), 108 MeV (40-50%), and 147 MeV (70-80%).

## Context
This work extends the kinetic approach of Sung (2021), which found a factor of 3 enhancement in mid-central collisions and a factor of 6 enhancement in peripheral collisions, by including two previously neglected effects: non-unity hadron fugacities (motivated by AMPT model studies of Xu 2017) and a temperature-dependent $K_{1}$ mass from QCD sum rules (Lee 2023). The temperature-dependent quark condensate is taken from Weise (2012). The results show that the enhancement is substantially reduced but remains robust in peripheral collisions, confirming the $K_{1}/K^{*}$ ratio as a signature for chiral symmetry restoration. The study enables more realistic comparisons with future experimental measurements from the ALICE Collaboration at the LHC.
