# Nambu-Jona-Lasinio Model at the Next-to-Leading Order in 1/N

> We derived and solved the compositeness condition in the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model at the next-to-leading order in 1/N, and obtained the expressions for the effective coupling constants in terms of the compositeness scale. In the NJL model with a scalar composite, the next to leading contributions are too large for N=3. In the induced gauge theory with abelian gauge symmetry, the correction term is reasonably suppressed, while, in the SU(N_c) gauge theory with N_f flavors of fermions, the correction is suppressed only for N_f>11N_c/2, complementarily to asymptotic freedom.

## Key claim

The next-to-leading order (NLO) corrections in the 1/N expansion of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model are too large for N=3, making the scalar composite model unstable, but in induced gauge theories the NLO corrections are naturally suppressed by a small factor (epsilon in the abelian case) or by the condition N_f > 11N_c/2 (in the non-abelian case), which is complementary to asymptotic freedom.

## Method

The author derived the compositeness condition (Z_phi = Z_lambda = 0 for the scalar case, Z_3 = 0 for the gauge case) at next-to-leading order in 1/N by calculating the boson self-energy and four-boson vertex diagrams (Figs. 1-4) in the equivalent renormalized Yukawa or gauge theory, then solving the resulting equations iteratively for the effective coupling constants in terms of the compositeness scale (cutoff or epsilon in dimensional regularization).

## Key numbers

- For the scalar NJL model at NLO: g_r^2 = (1/NI)[1 - 1/N + O(1/N^2)], lambda_r = (1/NI)[1 - 10/N + O(1/N^2)], where I = 1/(16 pi^2 epsilon) in dimensional regularization
- The ratio M_H/m_f = 2[1 - 9/(2N) + O(1/N^2)], which for N=3 gives a correction too large to trust the expansion
- For abelian induced gauge theory: e_r^2 = (12 pi^2 epsilon/N)[1 - 9 epsilon/(4N)], with epsilon estimated as 6x10^{-3} for QCD with 3 generations, making the NLO correction only 0.1% of the leading term
- For non-abelian SU(N_c) gauge theory: g_r^2 = (3/(2N_f I))[1 + 11N_c/(2N_f) + O(1/N_f^2)], valid only when N_f > 11N_c/2

## Context

This work extends the compositeness condition approach (Z_3=0) originally developed for the NJL model to next-to-leading order in 1/N, building on earlier leading-order results by Eguchi and Sugawara (1974) and the auxiliary field method. The analysis shows that while the scalar composite NJL model fails for N=3 (the physically relevant case), the induced gauge theory approach works when the gauge theory is not asymptotically free, complementing the known result that asymptotically free gauge theories cannot have composite gauge bosons of this type. The methods enable systematic study of composite dynamics for hadrons and possible composite gauge bosons.
