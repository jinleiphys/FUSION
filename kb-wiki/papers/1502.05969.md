# An effective model for the QCD phase transitions at finite baryon density

> We introduce an effective quark-meson-nucleon model for the QCD phase transitions at finite baryon density. The nucleon and the quark degrees of freedom are described within a unified framework of a chiral linear sigma model. The deconfinement transition is modeled through a simple modification of the distribution functions of nucleons and quarks, where an additional auxiliary field, the bag field, is introduced. The bag field plays a key role in converting between the nucleon and the quark degrees of freedom. The model predicts that the chiral and the deconfinement phase transitions are always separated. Depending on the model parameters, the chiral transition occurs in the baryon density range of $(1.5-15.5)n_0$, while the deconfinement transition occurs above $5 n_0$, where $n_0$ is the saturation density.

## Key claim

The model predicts that the chiral and deconfinement phase transitions at zero temperature are always separated, with the chiral transition occurring at lower baryon density than the deconfinement transition, contrary to the approximate coincidence seen at finite temperature in lattice QCD.

## Method

The authors construct a unified chiral linear sigma model that includes both nucleon and quark degrees of freedom, using a parity doublet model for nucleons and a quark-meson model for quarks. Statistical confinement is implemented by modifying the Fermi-Dirac distribution functions: quarks are restricted to momenta above a dynamical "bag field" \(b\), while nucleons are restricted to momenta below \(\alpha b\), with the bag field generated by a phenomenological vacuum potential whose parameters are fit to the QCD vacuum energy and the pseudo-critical temperature from lattice simulations.

## Key numbers

- The chiral transition occurs in the baryon density range of \((1.5-15.5)n_0\), where \(n_0\) is the saturation density.
- The deconfinement transition occurs above \(5 n_0\).
- The bag field vacuum expectation value is \(b_0 = 570.3\) MeV for \(\epsilon_{\rm vac} = (250 \, {\rm MeV})^4\) and \(\kappa_b = 155\) MeV.
- The onset of quarks in the bQM model at \(T=0\) is at \(\mu_q \simeq 450\) MeV, compared to \(\mu_q \simeq 250\) MeV in the QM model without statistical confinement.
- The pseudo-critical temperature for the chiral transition is fixed to \(T_c = 170\) MeV, matching lattice QCD results for \(N_f=2\).
- The chiral transition at \(\alpha b_0 = 300\) MeV is first order and occurs at \(\mu_B = 1135\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the parity doublet model of Zschiesche (2006) and Sasaki (2010) by adding quark degrees of freedom and a statistical confinement mechanism inspired by the Polyakov loop approach of Fukushima (2003) and Ratti (2005), but adapted to finite baryon density where the Polyakov loop is problematic. The model enables future studies of the QCD phase diagram at high density relevant for compact stars and heavy-ion collisions, and contrasts with approaches using the excluded volume method of Steinheimer (2011) or the spectral function method of Yamazaki (2012).
