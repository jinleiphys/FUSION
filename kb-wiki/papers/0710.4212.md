# $πN\to ηN$ process in a $χ$QM approach

> A chiral quark model approach is used to investigate the $π^{-}p \to ηn$ process at low energies. The roles of the most relevant nucleon resonances in $n\leq 2$ shells are briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The chiral quark model approach, extended from meson photoproduction to the πN → ηN process, reproduces low-energy cross-section data, but the P11(1710) resonance requires a reversed sign for its coupling from the beginning, which may indicate a breakdown of the non-relativistic constituent quark model or unconventional internal configurations.

## Method

The authors use a chiral quark model effective Lagrangian with vector and axial currents to compute s-channel, u-channel, and t-channel (a0-exchange) amplitudes for π−p → ηn. The amplitudes are evaluated in a harmonic oscillator basis, including nucleon pole terms, degenerate u-channel contributions, and resonant contributions from n=1 and n=2 shell nucleon resonances with Breit-Wigner masses and widths from the Particle Data Group.

## Key numbers

- gπNN = 13.48 and gηNN = 0.81 are adopted for meson-nucleon-nucleon couplings.
- Differential cross-sections are calculated at pion incident momenta Pπlab = 0.718, 0.850, and 1.005 GeV, corresponding to total centre-of-mass energies W = 1.507, 1.576, and 1.674 GeV, respectively.
- The S11(1535) plays a crucial role: constructive at the lowest energies and destructive at the highest energy (W = 1.674 GeV).
- The S11(1650) has a smaller, destructive effect.
- The D13(1520) contribution at W = 1.576 GeV is needed to produce the right curvature.
- The P11(1710) produces significant effects at the highest energy, despite the overall n=2 shell contribution being rather small.

## Context

This work extends a unified chiral quark model approach previously applied to γp → ηp, K+Λ, and γN → πN reactions (Li; Saghai) to the πN → ηN process. It responds to high-precision data from the BNL Crystal Ball Collaboration (Prakhov et al., 2005) and complements coupled-channel formalisms such as the K-matrix approach (Geisen), meson-exchange model (Juelich), chiral model (Valencia), T-matrix (Zagreb), and dynamical formalism (EBAC). The finding of a required sign reversal for the P11(1710) opens questions about the validity of the non-relativistic constituent quark model for that resonance and motivates further investigation into its internal structure.
