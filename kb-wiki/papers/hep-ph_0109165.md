# Instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation: improved analytical solution

> Studying the Bethe-Salpeter formalism for interactions instantaneous in the rest frame of the bound states described, we show that, for bound-state constituents of arbitrary masses, the mass of the ground state of a given spin may be calculated almost entirely analytically with high accuracy, without the (numerical) diagonalization of the matrix representation obtained by expansion of the solutions over a suitable set of basis states.

## Key claim

For ground-state masses of fermion-antifermion bound states described by the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation with a pure time-component Lorentz-vector kernel, the mass eigenvalue can be obtained with a few percent accuracy using a fully analytical variational approximation, without any numerical matrix diagonalization.

## Method

The authors reduce the coupled instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equations for spin-0 (¹S₀) bound states of equal-mass fermions to a single eigenvalue equation for M². They then apply a variational approach using exponential trial wavefunctions ϕ(r) = 2 μ^(3/2) exp(-μ r) with a single variational parameter μ, and derive an analytic closed-form expression for M² in terms of expectation values of the interaction potential. For a linear confining potential V(r) = λ r, they compute the required expectation values analytically and minimize M² with respect to μ.

## Key numbers

- For m = 0.1 GeV and λ = 0.2 GeV², the variational ground-state mass is M = 1.703 GeV, which is 2.5% larger than the "exact" value M = 1.661 GeV from a 25×25 matrix diagonalization (Lucha 2001).
- For m = 300 MeV and λ = 0.29 GeV², the present work gives M² = 4.568 GeV², with a relative error of 2.4% in M compared to the value M² = 4.357 GeV² from Parramore (1995).
- For m = 500 MeV and λ = 0.29 GeV², M² = 5.478 GeV² (present work) versus M² = 5.255 GeV² (Parramore 1995), relative error 2.1% in M.
- For m = 900 MeV and λ = 0.29 GeV², M² = 8.515 GeV² (present work) versus M² = 8.248 GeV² (Parramore 1995), relative error 1.6% in M.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analytical treatment of the instantaneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (Lucha 2001) from the massless constituent case to the case of arbitrary constituent masses. Earlier work (Lucha 2001) had fixed the variational parameter μ equal to the constituent mass m, which gave slow convergence for small masses; the present approach treats μ as a free variational parameter, yielding accurate ground-state masses without matrix diagonalization. This enables rapid initial estimates for fitting procedures in relativistic quark models of hadrons, where linear confining potentials are commonly used (Lucha 1991, Lucha 1992).
