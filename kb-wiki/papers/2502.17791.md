# Dynamical evolution of critical fluctuations with second-order baryon diffusion coupled to chiral condensate

> We develop a dynamical model to describe critical fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, incorporating the baryon diffusion current and chiral condensate as dynamical degrees of freedom, to address their nontrivial scale separation. The model couples fluctuations of the chiral condensate $σ$ with baryon density fluctuations $n$ and the diffusion current $ν$ based on a second-order diffusion equation with a finite relaxation time of the baryon diffusion $τ_\mathrm{R}$. We analyze the spacetime evolution and these correlation functions of the fluctuations in one-dimensionally expanding background. We confirm that an appropriate relaxation time $τ_\mathrm{R}$ ensures causality. We show that propagating waves with finite $τ_\mathrm{R}$ split into two modes at the critical temperature due to a rapid change of kinetic coefficients. In the correlation functions, we find that dynamical $σ$ blurs the structure and peak around the critical temperature. With finite $τ_\mathrm{R}$, the effect of the critical fluctuations persists longer into the later stages of the evolution. These findings suggest importance of dynamical effects of the chiral condensate and baryon diffusion current in identifying critical-point signals in heavy-ion collisions, where the scale separation is nontrivial.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in heavy-ion collisions, where scale separation is insufficient, the chiral condensate and baryon diffusion current must be treated as dynamical degrees of freedom rather than fast modes that can be integrated out, and that a finite relaxation time of baryon diffusion ensures causality while causing critical fluctuations to persist longer into later stages of evolution.

## Method

The authors formulate a coupled system of stochastic differential equations for baryon density fluctuations n, chiral condensate fluctuations σ, and the baryon diffusion current ν, based on a second-order Müller-Israel-Stewart-type diffusion equation with finite relaxation time τ_R. They analyze the spacetime evolution and two-point correlation functions of these fluctuations in a 1+1 dimensionally expanding Bjorken background, comparing results across four systems: n–σ–ν, n–ν, n–σ, and n alone.

## Key numbers

- The relaxation time of baryon diffusion τ_R is typically of order τ_R ∼ η/(e+P) ∼ 1/T ∼ 1 fm in heavy-ion collisions
- The system lifetime and size are 1–10 fm, comparable to τ_R
- At large wave number |k|, propagating modes appear with wave velocity v² = (λ̃B + λC)/τ_R
- The diffusion constant D = λΔ/A vanishes at the critical point where Δ = 0

## Context

This work extends prior studies of critical dynamics based on model B (Sakaida 2017, Nahrgang 2017, Bluhm 2019) that considered only baryon density fluctuations, and model A studies of chiral condensate dynamics (Nahrgang 2011, Mukherjee 2015). It also builds on the n–σ system analysis by Son (2004) and Fujii (2004) by adding the baryon diffusion current as a dynamical degree. The results enable future implementation of critical fluctuations in realistic 3+1D relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan, NA61/SHINE, and HADES.
