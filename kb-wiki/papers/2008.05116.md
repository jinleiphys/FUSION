# Ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions as a source of heavy quarkonia

> Heavy quarkonium production in ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions (UPC) is described within the QCD dipole formalism. Realistic quarkonium wave functions in the $Q\bar Q$ rest frame are calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation with a subsequent Lorentz boost to high energy. We rely on several realistic $Q\bar Q$ potentials, which allow to describe well the quarkonium masses and decay widths, as well as data on diffractive electroproduction of quarkonia on protons. Nuclear effects are calculated with the phenomenological dipole cross sections fitted to DIS data. The higher twist quark shadowing related to the lowest $Q\bar Q$ Fock component of the photon, as well as the leading twist gluon shadowing, related to higher components containing gluons, are included. The results for coherent and incoherent photoproduction of charmonia and bottomonia in UPC of heavy nuclei are in good accord with available data from the LHC. They can also be verified in future experiments at electron-ion colliders.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that heavy quarkonium photoproduction in ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions can be described within the QCD dipole formalism with realistic wave functions and nuclear effects, achieving good agreement with LHC data, but only when including both higher-twist quark shadowing from the lowest \(Q\bar Q\) Fock component and leading-twist gluon shadowing from higher Fock components containing gluons, with the latter requiring a Green function formalism rather than the frozen eikonal approximation even at LHC energies.

## Method

The authors calculate quarkonium wave functions by solving the Schrödinger equation in the \(Q\bar Q\) rest frame using power-like (POW) and Buchmüller-Tye (BT) potentials, then Lorentz-boost them to high energy. Nuclear cross sections for coherent and incoherent photoproduction are computed within the light-front dipole approach, incorporating corrections for finite coherence length via a Green function formalism and including gluon shadowing from the \(|Q\bar Q G\rangle\) Fock state. The dipole cross sections are taken from three parametrizations (KST, GBW, BGBK) fitted to deep inelastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Coherent \(\Jpsi\) photoproduction cross sections in Pb-Pb UPC are suppressed by finite coherence length effects for center-of-mass energies \(s \lesssim 10^3\,\text{GeV}^2\)
- Coherent \(\Upsilon\) photoproduction cross sections are affected by finite coherence length for \(s \lesssim 10^4\,\text{GeV}^2\)
- Incoherent cross sections for \(\Jpsi(1S)\) are enhanced by approximately \(20\%-30\%\) due to finite coherence length effects at lower energies
- Incoherent cross sections for \(\Upsilon(1S)\) are enhanced by approximately \(7\%-12\%\) due to finite coherence length effects
- The parameter \(\beta\) controlling the node position in the \(2S\) wave function is \(\beta = 0.908\) for \(\psi^{\prime}\) and \(\beta = 0.963\) for \(\Upsilon^{\prime}\)
- The oscillatory frequency used in the harmonic oscillator approximation is \(\omega \approx 0.3\,\text{GeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the color dipole approach previously applied to charmonium photoproduction off nucleons (Hufner 2000, Krelina 2018, Cepila 2019) and nuclei (Ivanov 2002, Nemchik 2002) by incorporating for the first time rigorous path-integral treatment of finite coherence length effects and gluon shadowing within the Green function formalism, rather than relying on the vector dominance model (VDM) corrections used in earlier work (Hufner 1996, Ivanov 2002). The results enable predictions for future electron-ion collider experiments and provide a framework that avoids the unjustified photon-like \(V \to Q\bar Q\) transition model used in some prior approaches.
