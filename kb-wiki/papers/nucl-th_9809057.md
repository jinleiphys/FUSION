# Pion and Kaon dissociation in hot quark medium

> Pion and kaon dissociation in a medium of hot quark matter is studied in the Nambu Jona-Lasinio model. The decay width of pion and kaon are found to be large but finite at temperatures much higher than the so called critical temperature of chiral or deconfinement transition, kaon decay width being larger. Consequently, pions and even kaons (with a lower density compared to pions) should coexist with quarks and gluons at such high temperatures. The implication of the above result in the study of Quark-Gluon plasma is discussed.

# Pion and Kaon dissociation in hot quark medium

## Key claim
Pion and kaon decay widths are large but finite at temperatures much higher than the critical temperature for chiral or deconfinement transition, with kaon decay width larger than pion decay width; however, the exact temperature at which the decay width goes to zero depends on the value of the quark mass considered, and the model does not incorporate confinement.

## Method
The authors used the Nambu Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in flavour SU(3) with a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t-Hooft (KMT) term to calculate quark condensates and constituent quark masses at finite temperature via gap equations. Meson masses for pions and kaons were obtained from the dispersion relation using the one-loop polarization function, and decay widths were evaluated from the imaginary part of the polarization function using empirical meson-quark couplings.

## Key numbers
- Constituent u/d quark mass drops from 135 MeV to the current quark mass value just after a temperature of 200 MeV.
- At around 450 MeV temperature, pion mass reaches 900 MeV and kaon mass reaches 100 MeV.
- At 500 MeV temperature, kaon decay width is around 3 GeV and pion decay width is around 1.4 GeV.
- Decay width goes to zero at around T = 0.2 GeV.
- Parameters used: cutoff Lambda = 631.4 MeV, g_s Lambda^2 = 3.67, g_D Lambda^5 = -9.29, current masses m_u,d = 5.5 MeV and m_s = 135.7 MeV.
- Empirical meson-quark couplings: G_pi q = 3.5 and G_K q = 3.6.

## Context
This work extends earlier lattice results showing that non-perturbative hadron-like excitations could survive at temperatures far above the chiral phase transition temperature, as reported by Shuryak (1994) and others. It also builds on the three-flavour NJL model formulation by Hatsuda et al. (1994) and Bernard et al. (1988). The results contradict the conclusion of Boyd et al. (1996) that free quarks exist at high temperatures, and instead support the persistence of mesonic modes. The work enables dynamical studies of hadronisation in quark-gluon plasma evolution, suggesting that pions and kaons become stable and dominant only below approximately 200 MeV temperature.
