# Influence of Coulomb distortion on polarization observables in elastic electromagnetic lepton hadron scattering at low energies

> The formal expression for the most general polarization observable in elastic electromagnetic lepton hadron scattering at low energies is derived for the nonrelativistic regime. For the explicit evaluation the influence of Coulomb distortion on various polarization observables is calculated in a distorted wave Born approximation. Besides the hyperfine interaction also the spin-orbit interactions of lepton and hadron are included. For like charges the Coulomb repulsion reduces strongly the size of polarization observables compared to the plane wave Born approximation whereas for opposite charges the Coulomb attraction leads to a substantial increase of these observables for hadron lab kinetic energies below about 20 keV.

## Key claim

The paper derives the most general expression for polarization observables in elastic electromagnetic lepton-hadron scattering at low energies using a nonrelativistic distorted wave Born approximation, and finds that Coulomb distortion significantly alters polarization observables compared to the plane wave Born approximation: for like charges (e.g., electron-proton) Coulomb repulsion strongly reduces the size of polarization observables, while for opposite charges (e.g., positron-antiproton) Coulomb attraction leads to a substantial increase for hadron lab kinetic energies below about 20 keV.

## Method

The author derived the general quadruple polarization cross section for elastic scattering of spin-1/2 hadrons and leptons in the center-of-mass system, including Coulomb, lepton spin-orbit, hadron spin-orbit, and hyperfine (spin-spin) interactions in the T-matrix. The T-matrix elements were evaluated both in plane wave approximation (one-photon exchange) and in distorted wave Born approximation using nonrelativistic Coulomb scattering wave functions, with numerical evaluation performed via partial wave expansion (up to l_max=4000 for spin-orbit terms) and checked against an integral representation method.

## Key numbers

- For like charges, Coulomb repulsion reduces polarization observables compared to plane wave approximation; for opposite charges, Coulomb attraction increases them for hadron lab kinetic energies below about 20 keV
- The hadronic spin-orbit strength parameter is approximately three orders of magnitude smaller than the leptonic one, with ratio c_h^LS / c_l^LS ≈ 3 × 10^{-3}
- Partial wave expansion required l_max = 2000 for hyperfine amplitude convergence and l_max = 4000 for spin-orbit interaction convergence
- The structure function S_2^+ dominates the lepton-hadron polarization transfer, with additional contributions from S_2^- (quadratic in hyperfine amplitude) and L_2^l (hyperfine-leptonic spin-orbit interference) being "quite small if not negligible" compared to S_2^+

## Context

This work extends the author's previous study (Arenhövel 2007) on polarization transfer in elastic electromagnetic scattering, which had been criticized by Milstein et al. (2008) for numerical accuracy problems and for using an observable that cannot contribute to net hadron polarization in a storage ring. The present paper corrects those issues and provides the complete formalism for all polarization observables including spin-flip transitions, which are necessary for polarizing hadrons via scattering on polarized leptons in a storage ring as originally proposed by H.O. Meyer (1994). The results enable quantitative evaluation of hadron polarization schemes at low energies where Coulomb distortion effects are significant.
