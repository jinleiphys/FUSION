# High baryon and energy densities achievable in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=$ 39 GeV

> Baryon and energy densities, which are reached in central Au+Au collisions at collision energy of $\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 39$ GeV, are estimated within the model of three-fluid dynamics. It is shown that the initial thermalized mean proper baryon and energy densities in a sizable central region approximately are $n_B/n_0 \approx$ 10 and $\varepsilon\approx$ 40 GeV/fm$^3$, respectively. The study indicates that the deconfinement transition at the stage of interpenetration of colliding nuclei makes the system quite opaque. The final fragmentation regions in these collisions are formed not only by primordial fragmentation fireballs, i.e. the baryon-rich matter passed through the interaction region (containing approximately 30\% of the total baryon charge), but also by the baryon-rich regions of the central fireball pushed out to peripheral rapidities by the subsequent almost one-dimensional expansion of the central fireball along the beam direction.

## Key claim

The authors claim that in central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV, the initial thermalized mean proper baryon and energy densities in a sizable central region reach approximately nB/n0 ≈ 10 and ε ≈ 40 GeV/fm³, but this result is conditional on the use of deconfinement equations of state (first-order phase transition or crossover) with friction forces fitted to reproduce midrapidity observables; using purely hadronic friction yields very high transparency and fails to reproduce experimental data.

## Method

The authors employed the three-fluid dynamics (3FD) model, which treats the projectile and target baryon-rich matter as two interpenetrating fluids plus a third fluid for newly produced particles (fireball fluid), all governed by hydrodynamic equations coupled by friction terms. Simulations were performed for central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV using two equations of state involving deconfinement transitions (first-order phase transition and smooth crossover), with friction forces in the hadronic phase taken from Satarov (1990) and in the quark-gluon phase fitted phenomenologically to reproduce baryon stopping and other observables at high collision energies.

## Key numbers

- Mean proper baryon density nB/n0 ≈ 10 (in units of normal nuclear density n0 = 0.15 fm⁻³) in the central region at the initial thermalized stage (t ≈ 1 fm/c) for central Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 39 GeV
- Mean proper energy density ε ≈ 40 GeV/fm³ in the same central region at the same time
- Approximately 70% of the total baryon charge is stopped in the central region, while approximately 30% is located in the primordial fragmentation fireballs at the initial stage
- The fluid unification measure (indicating mutual stopping of projectile and target fluids) is less than 0.02 at t ≥ 1 fm/c
- The baryon-fireball relative velocity v_fB ≤ 0.1 at t ≥ 1 fm/c
- The evolution time of the fragmentation regions in the center-of-mass frame lasts approximately 40 fm/c

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical considerations of baryonic fireballs by Anishetty et al. (1980), Csernai (1984), Gyulassy (1986), Frankfurt (2002), and Mishustin (2001), and updates the analysis using the three-fluid dynamics model that has successfully reproduced midrapidity observables at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (Ivanov 2013, 2014, 2016). The results contrast with the McLerran-Venugopalan model predictions for top RHIC energy (Li 2016), which found high baryon densities in fragmentation regions rather than the central fireball; the present study shows that at 39 GeV the central fireball contains most of the baryon charge, and this dense matter is subsequently pushed to peripheral rapidities by one-dimensional expansion. The findings enable predictions for the fragmentation region properties at the AFTER@LHC fixed-target experiment (Brodsky 2012), which would operate at √sNN = 72 GeV, slightly above the energy studied here.
