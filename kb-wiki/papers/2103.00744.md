# Topology change, emergent symmetries and compact star matter

> Topology effects have being extensively studied and confirmed in strongly correlated condensed matter physics. In the large color number limit of QCD, baryons can be regarded as topological objects -- skyrmions -- and the baryonic matter can be regarded as a skyrmion matter. We review in this paper the generalized effective field theory for dense compact-star matter constructed with the robust inputs obtained from the skyrmion approach to dense nuclear matter, relying to possible ``emergent" scale and local flavor symmetries at high density. All nuclear matter properties from the saturation density $n_0$ up to several times $n_0$ can be fairly well described. A uniquely novel -- and unorthdox -- feature of this theory is the precocious appearance of the pseudo-conformal sound velocity $v^2_{s}/c^2 \approx 1/3$, with the non-vanishing trace of the energy momentum tensor of the system. The topology change encoded in the density scaling of low energy constants is interpreted as the quark-hadron continuity in the sense of Cheshire Cat Principle (CCP) at density $\gsim 2n_0$ in accessing massive compact stars. We confront the approach with the data from GW170817 and GW190425.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at a density \(n_{1/2} \gsim 2n_0\) is a robust feature of dense nuclear matter, and that this change, encoded in the density scaling of low-energy constants, leads to a pseudo-conformal sound velocity \(v_s^2/c^2 \approx 1/3\) with non-vanishing trace of the energy-momentum tensor, a result that contradicts the standard scenario of deconfined quarks in compact-star cores.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized nuclear effective field theory (G\(n\)EFT) that includes nucleons, pions, the lowest-lying vector mesons \(\rho\) and \(\omega\), and the scalar meson \(f_0(500)\) as explicit degrees of freedom. The vector mesons are introduced via hidden local symmetry (HLS) and the scalar meson via hidden scale symmetry (genuine dilaton). The robust predictions of the skyrmion crystal approach, specifically the topology change at \(n_{1/2}\) and the resulting density dependence of hadron properties, are incorporated as intrinsic density dependence (IDD) in the Lagrangian parameters, with different scaling laws below (R-I) and above (R-II) the topology change density. The equation of state is then computed using a \(V_{lowk}\) renormalization group approach to construct the pseudo-conformal model (PCM).

## Key numbers

* Saturation density \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is used as the reference density.
* The topology change density is constrained to \(2.0 n_0 \lsim n_{1/2} \lsim 4.0 n_0\) by astrophysical observations.
* In the half-skyrmion phase, the pion decay constant scales as \(f_\pi^{\ast 2}/f_\pi^2 \simeq 1/3\).
* The sound velocity approaches the pseudo-conformal limit \(v_s^2/c^2 \approx 1/3\) at densities relevant to compact stars.
* The vector manifestation (VM) fixed-point density is estimated as \(n_{\rm VM} \gsim 25 n_0\).
* The scaling parameter in R-I is \(c_I \approx 0.13\)–\(0.20\) in \(\Phi_I = 1/(1 + c_I n/n_0)\).
* Predicted nuclear matter properties at saturation: binding energy \(16.7\) MeV, symmetry energy \(E_{sym}(n_0) = 30.2\) MeV, incompressibility \(K_0 = 250.0\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion crystal approach to dense nuclear matter (Klebanov 1985, Park and Vento) and the Brown-Rho scaling (Brown 1991) by incorporating topology change as a key input. It contrasts with standard chiral effective field theory (\(s\chi\)EFT), which is believed to break down at densities above \(\sim n_0\), and with the conventional scenario of deconfined quarks in compact-star cores (Tews 2018). The pseudo-conformal model (PCM) enables predictions for the equation of state up to \(\sim 10 n_0\) that can be tested against gravitational-wave data from GW170817 and GW190425, and it suggests that the core of massive compact stars consists of confined quasi-fermions with fractional baryon charge rather than deconfined quarks.
