# Soft gluons are heavy and rowdy

> We study dynamical mass generation in pure Yang-Mills theory and report on a recently developed ansatz that exactly solves the tower of Dyson-Schwinger equations in Landau gauge at low Euclidean momentum, featuring enhanced gluon-gluon vertices, a finite ghost-gluon vertex in agreement with an old argument of Taylor, and an IR suppressed gluon propagator. This ansatz reinforces arguments in favor of the concept of a gluon mass gap at low momentum (although the minimum of the gluon's dispersion relation is not at zero momentum). As an application, we have computed the spectrum of oddballs, three-gluon glueballs with negative parity and C-parity. The three body problem is variationally solved employing the color density-density interaction of Coulomb gauge QCD with a static Cornell potential. Like their even glueball counterparts, oddballs fall on Regge trajectories with similar slope to the pomeron. However their intercept at t=0 is smaller than the omega Regge trajectory and therefore the odderon may only be visible in experimental searches (for example at BNL) with higher -t than conducted to date at DESY.

## Key claim

The paper presents an ansatz that exactly solves the tower of Dyson-Schwinger equations in Landau gauge at low Euclidean momentum, yielding infrared-suppressed gluon propagators and enhanced gluon-gluon vertices; however, the authors note that the minimum of the gluon's dispersion relation is not at zero momentum, qualifying the concept of a gluon mass gap.

## Method

The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equations for Yang-Mills theory in Landau gauge by proposing a self-consistent ansatz for the infrared behavior of Green's functions with m gluon legs and 2n ghost legs, showing that the dressing scales as (p^2)^((n-m)κ) when all momenta tend to zero simultaneously. They then apply this to compute the oddball (three-gluon glueball) spectrum by variationally solving the three-body problem using a Coulomb gauge Hamiltonian with a static Cornell potential and a Bogoliubov-Valatin canonical transformation to dress the gluon quasiparticles.

## Key numbers

- The infrared exponent κ lies in the range (0.5, 0.6), with a specific calculation yielding 0.596.
- The three-gluon and four-gluon vertices are enhanced by dressings of (p^2)^(-3κ) and (p^2)^(-4κ) respectively.
- The odderon Regge trajectory has a slope of 0.25 in the authors' preferred model H_eff.
- The odderon and omega Regge trajectories intersect at t = -2.3 GeV^2, where J is below -1.
- The authors recommend searches for the odderon at -t greater than 0.5 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger approach to Yang-Mills theory developed by Alkofer, Fischer, and collaborators (2000-2005), which provided analytical and numerical solutions for infrared ghost and gluon propagators, and builds on the earlier conjecture of a gluon mass gap by Cornwall (1981). The oddball spectrum calculation complements lattice studies by Morningstar and Peardon (1999) and Meyer (2003), and the Regge trajectory analysis supports the pomeron-glueball conjecture of Simonov and Kaidalov (1999), while disagreeing with earlier assumptions that the odderon trajectory passes through the 1^-- state. The results enable future experimental searches at BNL at higher -t than those conducted at DESY.
