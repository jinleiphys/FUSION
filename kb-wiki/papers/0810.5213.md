# Nuclear Scissors with Pairing and Continuity Equation

> The coupled dynamics of the isovector and isoscalar giant quadrupole resonances and low lying modes (including scissors) are studied with the help of the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method generalized to take into account pair correlations. Equations of motion for collective variables are derived on the basis of the Time Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations in the harmonic oscillator model with quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) residual interaction and a Gaussian pairing force. Special care is taken of the continuity equation.

## Key claim

The paper extends the Wigner Function Moments (WFM) method to include pair correlations while preserving the continuity equation, which was violated in an earlier attempt by Malov (2005). The authors show that neglecting the variation of the pairing gap during vibrations leads to an unphysical term in the equation for the coordinate moment, and they propose an approximation based on the dominance of phase oscillations over magnitude oscillations of the pairing gap to restore the continuity equation.

## Method

The authors start from the Time Dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (TDHFB) equations in the harmonic oscillator model with quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) residual interaction and a Gaussian pairing force. They apply the Wigner transformation and then take phase-space moments with weight functions xz, p_x p_z, and angular-momentum-like operators to derive equations of motion for collective variables describing K^π = 1^+ modes. After linearization, they introduce the approximation that δκ_+ (magnitude oscillations of the anomalous density) is much smaller than δκ_- (phase oscillations), which allows them to reduce the system to six equations per isospin that can be solved analytically.

## Key numbers

* The isovector giant quadrupole resonance (IVGQR) energy E_+ and the scissors mode energy E_- are given by E^2_± = D_ω ± sqrt(D_ω^2 - 8Δ'Δ̃'ℏ²ω²ψ - 4(ℏω̄)^4δ²(1-α)(1-ξ)(1-αξ) + χ₀Δ̃'k₀'ℏ⁴/m²), where D_ω = 2Δ'Δ̃' + ℏ²ω̄²[(1+δ/3)(2-α-ξ) - (2/3)δαξ].
* For a spherical nucleus (δ=0), the low-lying mode energy is estimated at about 2.88 MeV, compared to ~2.2 MeV from QRPA calculations by Matsuo (2005) for ¹⁵⁸Sn.
* The energy-weighted sum rule for B(M1) transitions is E_sc B(M1)_sc + E_iv B(M1)_iv = (1-α)(1-ξ) (mω̄²/2π) Q₀₀ᵖ δ² μ_N², which coincides exactly with the sum rule calculated without pairing by Balbutsev and Schuck (2004).
* The energy-weighted sum rule for B(E2) transitions is E_ISL B(E2)_ISL + E_is B(E2)_is + E_sc B(E2)_sc + E_iv B(E2)_iv = e² (ℏ²/m) (5/2π) Q₀₀ᵖ (1+δ/3), also identical to the no-pairing result.

## Context

This work extends the WFM method previously applied without pairing by Balbutsev and Schuck (2004) to include pair correlations, and corrects an earlier attempt by Malov (2005) that violated the continuity equation by neglecting the variation of the pairing gap during vibrations. The improved treatment enables analytical expressions for energies and transition probabilities of the scissors mode and isovector giant quadrupole resonance in deformed nuclei with pairing, and the authors note that a full numerical analysis of the complete eight-equation system is postponed to a future publication.
