# Production of true para-muonium in linearly polarized photon fusions

> True muonium (TM) -- the bound state of $μ^+μ^-$ -- has not been discovered yet. It was demonstrated that searching for TM via $γγ$ fusions in heavy ion collisions is feasible due to the enhancement of the atom number. We study the production of the true para-muonium (pTM) in the collisions of linearly polarized photons in the experiments of heavy-ion collisions, calculate the production rate as well as the transverse spectrum of pTM, and explore the discovery potential in nuclear experiments. Our results show that there is a significant correlation between the linearly polarized photon distribution and the transverse momentum distribution of pTM. The optimal kinematic region of the generated pTM is identified, which can provide a theoretical guide to the detection of pTM in experiments.

# Production of true para-muonium in linearly polarized photon fusions

## Key claim

The production rate and transverse momentum distribution of true para-muonium (pTM) in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions are significantly modified by linearly polarized photons in the small transverse momentum region, with the differential cross section vanishing as transverse momentum approaches zero when photons are fully polarized; however, the total cross section and average transverse momentum are unaffected by photon polarization.

## Method

The authors calculate the transverse-momentum-dependent differential cross section for pTM production via gamma-gamma fusion in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions using the equivalent photon approximation (Weizsacker-Williams method) with transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization. The photon correlation matrix is decomposed into unpolarized and linearly polarized photon distribution functions, and the partonic scattering amplitude for gamma-gamma to pTM is computed using the nonrelativistic QED bound-state formalism with the radial wavefunction at the origin. Numerical results are obtained for Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies and Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies using Woods-Saxon and STARlight form factors.

## Key numbers

- Total cross section for pTM in Au-Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: 0.20 microbarns
- Total cross section for pTM in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s)=2.76 TeV: 1.20 microbarns
- Total cross section for pTM in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV: 1.59 microbarns
- Total cross section for para-positronium in Au-Au at sqrt(s)=200 GeV: 1.36 x 10^5 microbarns
- Total cross section for true para-tauonium in Pb-Pb at sqrt(s)=5.02 TeV: 1.08 x 10^{-3} microbarns
- Average transverse momentum of pTM at RHIC energy sqrt(s)=200 GeV: approximately 1.4 MeV
- The differential cross section for fully linearly polarized photons decreases to zero when q_T approaches 0, and reaches maximum around several MeV at RHIC
- Rapidity range for pTM: |y| less than about 5 at RHIC, |y| less than about 8 at LHC

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of true muonium production in heavy-ion collisions by Ginzburg (1998) and others, which computed total cross sections but did not explore the transverse momentum distribution or the effect of linearly polarized photons. The authors build on the TMD factorization formalism developed for quarkonium production by Boer (2012) and Ma (2012), and on the observation by Li (2019) that linearly polarized photon distributions can be extracted from azimuthal asymmetries in lepton pair production. The results provide theoretical guidance for detecting pTM at RHIC and LHC experiments, though the authors note that detecting pTM with transverse momenta around several MeV presents a challenge at the LHC where the minimum measured transverse momentum is approximately 1 GeV.
