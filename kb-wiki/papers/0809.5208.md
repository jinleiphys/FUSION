# Nonthermal fixed points and the functional renormalization group

> Nonthermal fixed points represent basic properties of quantum field theories, in addition to vacuum or thermal equilibrium fixed points. The functional renormalization group on a closed real-time path provides a common framework for their description. For the example of an O(N) symmetric scalar theory it reveals a hierarchy of fixed point solutions, with increasing complexity from vacuum and thermal equilibrium to nonequilibrium.

## Key claim

Nonthermal fixed points exist in quantum field theories as a distinct class of infrared fixed points alongside vacuum and thermal equilibrium fixed points, and the functional renormalization group on a closed real-time path provides a common framework for their description. For an O(N) symmetric scalar theory, the nonthermal fixed point exhibits a scaling relation κ = –η + z + d that differs from the equilibrium relations because the fluctuation-dissipation relation is absent out of equilibrium, allowing the dimensionality of space d to enter.

## Method

The authors employ the functional renormalization group (FRG) for the average action on the Schwinger-Keldysh closed time path to study nonthermal fixed points in an O(N) symmetric scalar quantum field theory. They derive an exact flow equation for the scale-dependent effective action using retarded/advanced cutoff functions R_k^R and R_k^A while setting the statistical cutoff functions R_k^F and R_k^F̃ to zero, which implements an infrared cutoff on fluctuations. The fixed point analysis is performed using a resummed large-N expansion to next-to-leading order (NLO) to evaluate the scaling form of correlation functions from the flow equations.

## Key numbers

- For the nonthermal fixed point scaling relation: κ = –η + z + d, where d is the dimensionality of space, in contrast to thermal equilibrium where κ = –η + z and vacuum where κ = –η.
- For three spatial dimensions (d=3), the nonthermal scaling solution exhibits strongly enhanced infrared fluctuations ∼ p^−4 compared to a thermal distribution at low momenta ∼ p^−1.
- The scaling form of the statistical two-point function is F(p^0, p) = s^(2+κ) F(s^z p^0, s p) and the spectral function is ρ(p^0, p) = s^(2−η) ρ(s^z p^0, s p), where z is the dynamical scaling exponent, η is the anomalous dimension, and κ is the occupation number exponent.

## Context

This work extends the concept of nonthermal fixed points introduced by Berges (2008) for relativistic self-interacting scalar field theories, which demonstrated that far-from-equilibrium long-distance properties are characterized by universality. The functional renormalization group approach provides a complementary method to the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action techniques used in that earlier work, and builds on the successful application of FRG to critical phenomena in thermal equilibrium as reviewed by Berges and Pawlowski (2005). The results enable the study of far-from-equilibrium critical phenomena relevant to early universe dynamics after inflation and thermalization in heavy-ion collisions, where nonthermal fixed points may prevent or substantially delay thermalization.
