# Energy dependence of barKN interactions and resonance pole of strange dibaryons

> We study the resonance energy of the strange dibaryons using two models with the energy-independent and energy-dependent potentials for the s-wave barKN interaction, both of which are derived by certain reductions from the leading order term of the effective chiral Lagrangian. These potential models produce rather different off-shell behaviors of the two-body barKN - piSigma amplitudes in I=0 channel, i.e., the model with energy-independent (energy-dependent) potential predicts one (two) resonance pole in the Lambda(1405) region, while they describe the available data equally well. We find that the energy-independent potential model predicts one resonance pole of the strange dibaryons, whereas the energy-dependent potential model predicts two resonance poles: one is the shallow quasi-bound state of the barKNN, and another is the resonance of the piYN with large width. An investigation of the binding energy of the strange dibaryons will make a significant contribution to clarify resonance structure of s-wave barKN - piSigma around the Lambda(1405) region.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the choice between an energy-independent and an energy-dependent potential for the s-wave $\bar{K}N$ interaction, both derived from the same leading-order chiral Lagrangian term, produces qualitatively different predictions for the number and nature of resonance poles in the strange dibaryon ($\bar{K}NN - \pi YN$) system, even though both models describe the available two-body data equally well.

## Method

The authors constructed two coupled-channel potential models for the $I=0$ $\bar{K}N - \pi\Sigma$ subsystem, one with an energy-independent potential (E-indep) and one with an energy-dependent potential (E-dep), both derived from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term of the chiral effective Lagrangian. They solved the Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the two-body amplitudes, regularizing loop integrals with dipole form factors and fitting cutoff parameters to the $\pi\Sigma$ invariant mass distribution from $K^-p \rightarrow \pi\pi\pi\Sigma$ data. They then solved the coupled-channel Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations for the three-body $\bar{K}NN - \pi YN$ system in the $J^\pi=0^-$, $I=1/2$ channel, using non-relativistic kinematics to avoid problems with energy-dependent potentials.

## Key numbers

* E-indep model: one $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance pole at $(1405 - 1411) - i(14 - 20)$ MeV on the $\bar{K}N$ physical and $\pi\Sigma$ unphysical sheet.
* E-indep model: one virtual state at $(1296 - 1306) - i0$ MeV on the same sheet.
* E-dep model: two $\Lambda(1405)$ resonance poles at $(1417 - 1423) - i(16 - 21)$ MeV and $(1335 - 1341) - i(65 - 79)$ MeV.
* E-indep model for strange dibaryon: one resonance pole at binding energy $B = 44 - 58$ MeV and half-width $\Gamma/2 = 17 - 20$ MeV.
* E-dep model for strange dibaryon: Pole I at $B = 9 - 16$ MeV, $\Gamma/2 = 17 - 23$ MeV (shallow quasi-bound $\bar{K}NN$ state).
* E-dep model for strange dibaryon: Pole II at $B = 67 - 89$ MeV, $\Gamma/2 = 122 - 160$ MeV (broad $\pi YN$ resonance).
* Cutoff parameters for both models: $\Lambda_{\bar{K}N} = 975 - 1000$ MeV, $\Lambda_{\pi\Sigma} = 675 - 725$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior Faddeev calculations (Ikeda and Sato, 2007, 2008) and those of Shevchenko et al., which found that off-shell behavior of the $\bar{K}N - \pi\Sigma$ amplitudes strongly affects the predicted strange dibaryon energy. The study contrasts with the chiral unitary approach (Oller and Meissner, 1998; Jido et al., 2003) which predicts two $\Lambda(1405)$ poles, and shows that the energy-dependent potential model yields a shallow $\bar{K}NN$ bound state similar to that found by Dote et al. using variational methods. The results indicate that experimental determination of the strange dibaryon binding energy could discriminate between the two theoretical pictures of the $\Lambda(1405)$ structure.
