# Hydrodynamic manifestations of gravitational chiral anomaly

> The conservation of an axial current modified by the gravitational chiral anomaly implies the universal transport phenomenon (Kinematical Vortical Effect) dependent solely on medium vorticity and acceleration but not dependent explicitly on its temperature and density. This general analysis is verified for the case of massless fermions with spin 1/2.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the gravitational chiral anomaly produces a universal transport phenomenon called the Kinematical Vortical Effect (KVE), where the axial current depends only on fluid vorticity and acceleration, not explicitly on temperature or density. This relation holds for dissipation-free fluids and massless constituents with arbitrary spin, and is verified explicitly for massless spin-1/2 fermions.

## Method

The authors construct a hydrodynamic gradient expansion up to third order in derivatives in the presence of an external gravitational field, imposing global thermodynamic equilibrium via the Killing equation for the inverse temperature vector. They decompose the axial current into all possible pseudovector terms at third order, substitute this expansion into the gravitational chiral anomaly equation, and solve the resulting system of equations to relate the transport coefficients to the anomaly factor. The general result is then verified by direct comparison with known transport coefficients for the Dirac field computed using the Zubarev density operator.

## Key numbers

* The gravitational chiral anomaly factor for the Dirac field is \( \mathscr{N} = 1/(384 \pi^2) \), and the relation \( (\lambda_1 - \lambda_2)/32 = \mathscr{N} \) is verified by \( (-1/(24 \pi^2) + 1/(8 \pi^2))/32 = 1/(384 \pi^2) \)
* For the Dirac field, the axial current in flat space-time is \( j_{\mu}^{A} = (T^2/6 + \mu^2/(2 \pi^2) - \omega^2/(24 \pi^2) - a^2/(8 \pi^2)) \omega_{\mu} \)
* The anomalous part of the current in the limit \( |\omega| \ll |a| \) gives \( j_{\mu (\text{anom})}^A \simeq 64 \pi^2 \mathscr{N} \, T_U^2 \omega_{\mu} \), where \( T_U \simeq |a|/(2\pi) \) is the Unruh temperature

## Context

This work extends the framework relating chiral anomalies to transport phenomena, previously developed for the chiral magnetic effect and chiral vortical effect (Son 2009, Kharzeev 2022), to the gravitational chiral anomaly. It generalizes the approach of Yang (2022) and Son (2009) from gauge anomalies to gravitational fields, and resolves an ambiguity in the \( \omega^3 \) term noted by Stone (2018). The results provide a new test of the equivalence between statistical and gravitational approaches (Luttinger 1964, Verlinde 2010) and suggest that the KVE could enable experimental searches for gravitational anomaly effects in heavy-ion collisions without requiring curved space-time.
