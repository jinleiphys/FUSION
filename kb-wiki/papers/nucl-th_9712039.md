# On the binding energy of double Λhypernuclei in the relativistic mean field theory

> We calculate the binding energy of two $Λ$ hyperons bound to a nuclear core within the relativistic mean field theory. The starting point is a two-body relativistic equation of the Breit type suggested by the RMFT, and corrected for the two-particle interaction. We evaluate the 2 $Λ$ correlation energy and estimate the contribution of the $σ^*$ and $Φ$ mesons, acting solely between hyperons, to the bond energy $Δ{B_{ΛΛ}}$ of $^6_{ΛΛ}He$, $^{10}_{ΛΛ}Be$ and $^{13}_{ΛΛ}B$. Predictions of the $Δ{B_{ΛΛ}}$ A dependence are made for heavier $Λ$-hypernuclei.

## Key claim

The relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, even when including two-Λ correlation energy from σ and ω exchange, cannot by itself account for the empirical bond energy ΔBΛΛ of about 4.5 MeV in light double Λ hypernuclei; at least half of this energy must come from additional strange meson exchanges (σ* and Φ) acting solely between hyperons.

## Method

The authors reduced a relativistic two-body Dirac equation of the Breit type to a Schrödinger-like equation by approximating the Λ single-particle potential as a harmonic oscillator fitted to experimental single-Λ binding energies. They solved the separated center-of-mass and relative-motion equations to obtain the two-Λ correlation energy from σ and ω exchange, then added σ* and Φ meson exchanges between the two Λ hyperons. The σ*Λ coupling constant was treated as a free parameter and fitted to reproduce the empirical ΔBΛΛ values of the three observed double Λ hypernuclei.

## Key numbers

- The empirical ΛΛ bond energy ΔBΛΛ for light hypernuclei is approximately 4–5 MeV.
- For the HS RMF parametrization with αω = 2/3, the correlation energy from σ and ω exchange contributes about 0.5–1.0 MeV to ΔBΛΛ, leaving at least half of the empirical value to be explained by σ* and Φ meson exchanges.
- Fitting the σ*Λ coupling to experimental data yields ασ* around 0.71 for αω = 1/3 and around 0.79 for αω = 2/3, using the HS parametrization.
- The σ* and Φ meson masses adopted are mσ* = 975.0 MeV and mΦ = 1020.0 MeV, with the ΦΛ coupling taken from SU(6) relations as gΦΛ/gωN = −√2/3.
- The predicted ΔBΛΛ decreases with increasing mass number A for heavier double Λ hypernuclei.

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF calculations of double Λ hypernuclei by Schaffner et al. (1994), who introduced σ* and Φ mesons to strengthen ΛΛ binding but obtained ΔBΛΛ(⁶ΛΛHe) of only about 3 MeV. The present study adds the previously neglected two-Λ correlation energy from σ and ω exchange, showing that even with this contribution the RMF cannot reproduce the empirical 4–5 MeV bond energy without the extra strange mesons. The results enable predictions of ΔBΛΛ for heavier systems and constrain the Λ couplings to σ* and Φ mesons, which is relevant for extending RMF calculations to multi-Λ hypernuclei.
