# Gapless Color Superconductivity

> We present the dispersion relations for quasiparticle excitations about the color-flavor locked ground state of QCD at high baryon density. In the presence of condensates which pair light and strange quarks there need not be an energy gap in the quasiparticle spectrum. This raises the possibility of gapless color superconductivity, with a Meissner effect but no minimum excitation energy. Analysis within a toy model suggests that gapless color superconductivity may occur only as a metastable phase.

# Gapless Color Superconductivity

## Key claim

In the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of QCD at high baryon density, pairing between light quarks and heavier strange quarks can produce a condensate without an energy gap in the quasiparticle spectrum, but analysis within a toy model suggests that gapless color superconductivity may occur only as a metastable phase rather than the thermodynamic ground state.

## Method

The authors derive quasiparticle dispersion relations from the poles of the full quark propagator, which includes a proper self-energy matrix containing both diquark condensates (pairing fields) and the strange quark constituent mass. They use a four-fermion interaction abstracted from single-gluon exchange and solve the coupled mean-field Schwinger-Dyson equations for the CFL condensates, focusing on the 2x2 block structure describing pairing between massless u-quarks and massive s-quarks.

## Key numbers

- The condition for gapless superconductivity to arise is that the condensate ⟨us⟩ is less than approximately M_s²/4μ, where M_s is the effective strange quark mass and μ is the quark number chemical potential.
- At the unlocking transition with μ=525 MeV and M_s=350 MeV, the condensate ⟨us⟩=85 MeV yields a true gap of 15 MeV, which is small compared to the condensate.
- Gapless superconductivity sets in for ⟨us⟩ ≤ M_s²/3.8μ, which occurs in the metastable phase below the unlocking transition.
- In the metastable CFL phase at μ=490 MeV with M_s=350 MeV, the condensate ⟨us⟩=45 MeV produces gapless dispersion relations with zero minimum excitation energy.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of color superconductivity in QCD at high baryon density, building on the CFL phase identified by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) and the treatment of strange quark mass effects by Alford, Berges, and Rajagopal (1999). The authors note that the CFL phase breaks chiral symmetry and has the same symmetries as sufficiently dense hypernuclear matter, suggesting possible quark-hadron continuity as proposed by Schäfer and Wilczek (1999). The finding that gaps depend sensitively on fermion masses as well as condensates makes it difficult to infer hadronic-phase physics from quark-phase calculations. The authors draw an analogy to pairing between spin-up and spin-down electrons in ordinary superconductors placed in a magnetic field, where gapless superconductivity is precluded by a first-order transition except as a metastable phase, and note that paramagnetic impurities can stabilize gapless superconductivity in that context.
