# Blast-wave model description of the Hanbury-Brown--Twiss radii in pp collisions at LHC energies

> The blast wave model is applied to the recent data on HBT radii in pp collisions, measured by the ALICE collaboration. A reasonable description of data is obtained for a rather low temperature of the kinetic freeze-out, T ~ 100 MeV, and the transverse profile corresponding to the emission from a shell of a fairly small width 2 d ~ 1.5 fm. The size and the life-time of the produced system are determined for various multiplicities of the produced particles.

## Key claim

The blast-wave model can describe the Hanbury-Brown--Twiss (HBT) radii measured by ALICE in pp collisions at 7 TeV, but only if the transverse emission profile is taken as a thin shell (width about 1.5 fm) rather than a Gaussian, and the kinetic freeze-out temperature is low (below 120 MeV). The model reproduces the small ratio R_out/R_side, which is the "HBT puzzle" in heavy-ion physics.

## Method

The authors used a boost-invariant, azimuthally symmetric blast-wave model with Hubble-like transverse expansion and a Boltzmann emission function. The source function was constructed from the Cooper-Frye formula with a freeze-out hypersurface at constant proper time tau_f. The transverse density profile was taken as a Gaussian shell f(r) ~ exp[-(r-R)^2/delta^2], with radius R and half-width delta. HBT radii R_long, R_side, and R_out were computed from the logarithmic derivative of the correlation function at q=0, and fitted to ALICE data for 8 multiplicity classes and 6 transverse momentum bins, with the average transverse momentum constrained by CMS data.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature T = 100 MeV (acceptable only below 120 MeV)
- Shell half-width 2*delta = 1.5 fm (delta = 0.75 fm, constant across multiplicities)
- Shell radius R ranges from 1.15 fm (multiplicity class 1-11, <N_c>=6.3) to 2.91 fm (class 52-151, <N_c>=59.9)
- Freeze-out proper time tau_f ranges from 1.90 fm to 3.09 fm across the same multiplicity classes
- Average transverse momentum of pions follows <P_T> ≈ [400 + 2.5(N_c - 10)] MeV for N_c between 10 and 50
- Effective freeze-out volume V ranges from 46.2 fm^3 (lowest multiplicity) to 183.2 fm^3 (highest multiplicity)
- The pion packing radius r_pi (radius of sphere of volume V/N_c) decreases from 1.21 fm to 0.90 fm with increasing multiplicity

## Context

This work extends the blast-wave model, originally developed for heavy-ion collisions (Siemens 1978, Schnedermann 1993, Retiere 2003), to pp collisions at LHC energies, following earlier applications to high-multiplicity pp events (Ghosh 2014). The shell-like transverse profile found here is qualitatively similar to that obtained by Csorgo in analysis of NA22 data on 250 GeV pion and kaon collisions with fixed proton targets. The results support the idea that a thermalised, collectively expanding medium may form even in small systems like pp collisions, and provide predictions for correlation functions at large q that can be tested against future ALICE and CMS data.
