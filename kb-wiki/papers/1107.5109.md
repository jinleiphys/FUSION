# The first candidate for chiral nuclei in the $A\sim80$ mass region: $^{80}$Br

> Excited states of $^{80}$Br have been investigated via the $^{76}$Ge($^{11}$B, $־±$3n) and $^{76}$Ge($^{7}$Li, 3n) reactions and a new $־”I$ = 1 band has been identified which resides $\sim$ 400 keV above the yrast band. Based on the experimental results and their comparison with the triaxial particle rotor model calculated ones, a chiral character of the two bands within the $ֿ€g_{9/2}\otimes ־½g_{9/2}$ configuration is proposed, which provides the first evidence for chirality in the $A\sim80$ region.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the two positive-parity bands observed in \(^{80}\)Br are candidates for chiral doublet bands, providing the first evidence for chirality in the \(A\sim80\) mass region. However, the authors qualify this claim by noting that the coupling pattern of angular momenta departs from ideal chiral geometry and that the doublet bands correspond to a chiral vibration pattern rather than static chirality.

## Method

High-spin states of \(^{80}\)Br were populated via the fusion-evaporation reactions \(^{76}\)Ge(\(^{11}\)B, \(\alpha\)3n) at 54 MeV and \(^{76}\)Ge(\(^{7}\)Li, 3n). Gamma-rays were detected using the AFRODITE array of nine Compton-suppressed clover detectors at iThemba LABS, and additional high-fold coincidence data were collected with six EUROBALL CLUSTER detectors. The experimental level scheme and transition multipolarities were compared with triaxial particle rotor model (PRM) calculations using deformation parameters from configuration-fixed constrained triaxial relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations.

## Key numbers

- The new \(\Delta I = 1\) band (Band 2) resides approximately 400 keV above the yrast band (Band 1) within the observed spin interval.
- The yrast band was extended up to \(I^\pi = (16)^+\); Band 2 was established up to spin \((13)^+\).
- Self-consistent deformation parameters from RMF calculations: \(\beta_2 = 0.346\) and \(\gamma = 24.59^\circ\) for the \(\pi g_{9/2} \otimes \nu g_{9/2}\) configuration in \(^{80}\)Br.
- Using the triaxial deformation \(\gamma = 24.59^\circ\) and the lifetime data from Ray (2000), a quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2 = 0.321\) was determined for the yrast \(10^+\) state.
- Pairing gaps used in PRM calculations: \(\Delta_p = 1.295\) MeV and \(\Delta_n = 1.287\) MeV.
- The moment of inertia in PRM calculations: \(\Im = 12\ \hbar^2/\)MeV.
- The experimental energy staggering parameter \(S(I)\) yields an almost constant value of approximately 25 keV/\(\hbar\).

## Context

This work extends the search for nuclear chirality from the well-studied \(A\sim130\) and \(A\sim100\) mass regions (e.g., Starosta 2001, Koike 2001, Vaman 2004, Joshi 2004) into the \(A\sim80\) region, following the theoretical prediction by Dimitrov (2000) that chiral doublet bands may form in this mass region with the \(\pi g_{9/2} \otimes \nu g_{9/2}\) configuration. The results enable further experimental and theoretical studies to explore nuclear chirality and search for possible static chiral patterns in the \(A\sim80\) region, with the authors noting that the chiral structure in \(^{80}\)Br is not very stable and exhibits mainly chiral vibration characteristics.
