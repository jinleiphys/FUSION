# The quark-photon vertex and meson electromagnetic form factors

> The ladder Bethe-Salpeter solution for the dressed photon-quark vertex is used to study the low-momentum behavior of the pion electromagnetic and the $γ^\star π^0 γ$ transition form factors. With model parameters previously fixed by light meson masses and decay constants, the low-momentum slope of both form factors is in excellent agreement with the data. In comparison, the often-used Ball-Chiu Ansatz for the vertex is found to be deficient; less than half of the obtained $r_π^2$ is generated by that Ansatz while the remainder of the charge radius could be attributed to the tail of the $ρ$ resonance.

# The quark-photon vertex and meson electromagnetic form factors

## Key claim
The ladder Bethe-Salpeter solution for the dressed quark-photon vertex, with model parameters previously fixed by light meson masses and decay constants, yields pion electromagnetic and transition form factors in excellent agreement with low-momentum data; however, the commonly used Ball-Chiu Ansatz generates less than half of the obtained pion charge radius, with the remainder attributable to the tail of the rho resonance.

## Method
The authors solve the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the dressed quark-photon vertex under a ladder truncation consistent with the rainbow truncation used for the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation, preserving both the vector and axial-vector Ward-Takahashi identities. They then compute the pion electromagnetic form factor and the gamma* pi0 gamma transition form factor in the impulse approximation using triangle diagrams that incorporate the dressed quark propagator, the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and the dressed quark-photon vertex from the Dyson-Schwinger equation solution. Results are compared with those obtained using the bare vertex and the Ball-Chiu Ansatz.

## Key numbers
- Pion charge radius from the Dyson-Schwinger equation vertex solution: r_pi^2 = 0.45 fm^2, compared to the experimental value of 0.44 fm^2.
- Pion charge radius from the Ball-Chiu Ansatz: r_pi^2 = 0.18 fm^2, less than half the experimental value.
- Transition form factor interaction radius from the Dyson-Schwinger equation vertex solution: r^2 = 0.40 fm^2, compared to the experimental value of approximately 0.42 fm^2.
- Transition form factor interaction radius from the Ball-Chiu Ansatz: r^2 = 0.13 fm^2.
- Vector meson dominance prediction for the pion charge radius: 6 g_{rho pi pi} / (m_rho^2 g_rho) ~ 0.48 fm^2.
- At least 40% of r_pi^2 arises from non-resonant photon coupling to the quark substructure of the pion.

## Context
This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach to meson properties developed by Maris and Tandy (1999) and Maris and Roberts (1997), which successfully described light meson masses and decay constants. It directly addresses the long-standing question of how much of the low-momentum pion form factor can be described by the rho resonance mechanism of vector meson dominance (VMD). The results show that the Ball-Chiu Ansatz (Ball and Chiu, 1980), which satisfies the Ward-Takahashi identity but lacks vector meson poles, is deficient for low-momentum form factors; the full Dyson-Schwinger equation solution, which includes both resonant and non-resonant contributions, is necessary to reproduce experimental data. This enables future calculations of electromagnetic form factors for other mesons and baryons within the same framework.
