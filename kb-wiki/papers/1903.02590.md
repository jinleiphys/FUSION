# Universal behavior of $p$-wave proton-proton fusion near threshold

> We calculate the $p$-wave contribution to the proton-proton fusion $S$ factor and its energy derivative in pionless effective field theory (EFT) up to next-to-leading order. The leading contributions are given by a recoil piece from the Gamow-Teller and Fermi operators, and from relativistic $1/m$ suppressed weak interaction operators. We obtain the value of $(2.5\pm0.3 )\times 10^{-28}~\mathrm{MeV\ fm^2}$ for the $S$ factor and $(2.2\pm0.2) \times 10^{-26}~\mathrm{fm^2}$ for its energy derivative at threshold. These are smaller than the results of a prior study that employed chiral EFT by several orders of magnitude. We conclude that, contrary to what has been previously reported, the $p$-wave contribution does not need to be considered in a high-precision determination of the $S$ factor at astrophysical energies. Combined with the chiral EFT calculation of Acharya {\it et al.} [Phys. Lett. B \bf{760}, 584 (2016)] for the $s$-wave channel, this gives a total threshold $S$ factor of $S(0) = (4.047^{+0.024}_{-0.032}) \times 10^{-23}~{\rm MeV~fm}^2$.

## Key claim

The p-wave contribution to the proton-proton fusion S factor is several orders of magnitude smaller than previously claimed by Marcucci et al. (2013), and contrary to that prior report, it does not need to be included in a high-precision determination of the S factor at astrophysical energies. The authors qualify that their result applies specifically within pionless effective field theory up to next-to-leading order, and that the p-wave contribution remains negligible even when combined with the s-wave channel.

## Method

The authors calculated the p-wave contribution to proton-proton fusion using pionless effective field theory (EFT), which treats nucleons as non-relativistic particles interacting through short-range forces without explicit pion degrees of freedom. They included the leading contributions from a recoil piece arising from the Gamow-Teller and Fermi operators, and from relativistic 1/m suppressed weak interaction operators, with the Coulomb interaction between protons treated non-perturbatively. The cross section was evaluated by numerical integration of analytic expressions derived from coordinate-space Coulomb wave functions, and a polynomial fit was used to extrapolate the S factor and its energy derivative to zero energy.

## Key numbers

- S factor at threshold: S_1(0) = (2.47 ± 0.25 ± 0.01) × 10^{-28} MeV fm^2, where the first error is EFT uncertainty and the second is numerical fit error
- Energy derivative at threshold: S'_1(0) = (2.16 ± 0.22 ± 0.01) × 10^{-26} fm^2
- Combined with the s-wave calculation of Acharya et al. (2016), the total threshold S factor is S(0) = (4.047^{+0.024}_{-0.032}) × 10^{-23} MeV fm^2
- The p-wave result is smaller than the value of 2.0 × 10^{-25} MeV fm^2 claimed by Marcucci et al. (2013) by several orders of magnitude

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT treatment of proton-proton fusion previously applied to the s-wave channel by Kong and collaborators, and directly contradicts the p-wave results of Marcucci et al. (2013), who used chiral EFT and claimed the p-wave contribution was comparable to the next-to-next-to-leading s-wave contribution. The analytic results provide a benchmark for numerical chiral EFT calculations and demonstrate that the p-wave contribution is negligible for high-precision solar modeling, while also showing that the total S factor from Acharya et al. (2016) remains unmodified upon inclusion of the p-wave contribution calculated here.
