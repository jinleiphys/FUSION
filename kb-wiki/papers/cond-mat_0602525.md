# Superfluid phases of the three-species fermion gas

> We discuss the zero temperature phase diagram of a dilute gas with three fermionic species. We make use of solvable limits to conjecture the behavior of the system in the "unitary" regions. The physics of the Thomas-Efimov effect plays a role in these considerations. We find a rich phase diagram with superfluid, gapless superfluid and inhomogeneous phases with different symmetry breaking patterns. We then discuss one particular possible experimental implementation in a system of ^6Li atoms and the possible phases arising in this system as an external magnetic field is varied across three overlaping Feshbach resonances. We also suggest how to experimentally distinguish the different phases.

## Key claim
The zero-temperature phase diagram of a dilute three-species fermion gas is conjectured by interpolating between solvable limits where the diluteness parameters \(n|a|^3\) and \(n|\bar a|^3\) are much smaller than 1; the analysis is restricted to the regime where the range of interatomic forces \(r_0\) is much smaller than both the scattering lengths and the interparticle spacing, and the three-body parameter is kept fixed.

## Method
The authors analyze the ground state of a three-species fermion gas with equal densities using a Hamiltonian with contact interactions, where the couplings are determined by the s-wave scattering lengths \(a_{ij}\) and a three-body force. They consider the idealized case \(a_{12}=a_{23}=a\) and \(a_{31}=\bar a\), and map the phase diagram by solving the system in the dilute BEC limit (positive scattering lengths, \(na^3, n\bar a^3 \ll 1\)) using a simple energy functional of bound molecules and unbound atoms, and in the BCS limit (negative scattering lengths, \(n|a|^3, n|\bar a|^3 \ll 1\)) using mean-field BCS theory. The stability against atom-molecule phase separation is assessed using a criterion involving the atom-dimer scattering length, which can diverge due to the Efimov effect.

## Key numbers
- The Efimov scaling factor is \(e^{\pi/s_0} \approx 22.7\), where \(s_0 \approx 1.006\) is a numerical constant.
- For the \(^6\)Li system, three Feshbach resonances are predicted between atoms in the lowest three hyperfine states in the magnetic field range \(60\ \text{mT} < \mathbf{B} < 100\ \text{mT}\), occurring at \(\mathbf{B} \approx 69\ \text{mT}\) (for the "13" pair), \(\mathbf{B} \approx 81.1\ \text{mT}\) (for the "12" pair), and \(\mathbf{B} \approx 83.4\ \text{mT}\) (for the "23" pair).
- The dimer-dimer scattering length for "13" dimers is approximately \(a_{13,13} \approx 0.6 \bar a\).
- The atom-dimer scattering length \(a_{13,2}\) for \(\bar a < a\) is given by \(\bar a (A + B \tan(s_0 \log(\bar a / r_0) + \Phi))\), with numerical constants \(A\), \(B\), and \(s_0 \approx 1.006\).

## Context
This work extends the well-studied two-species fermion gas (BEC-BCS crossover) to three species, introducing competition between different pairing channels and the role of the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970) and the Thomas effect (Thomas 1935). The analysis builds on the solvable dilute limits used for two-component gases and incorporates three-body correlations via a contact three-body force (Bedaque, Hammer, and van Kolck). The conjectured phase diagram includes superfluid, gapless superfluid, and inhomogeneous phases, and the authors suggest experimental realization in \(^6\)Li using overlapping Feshbach resonances predicted by Grimm. The work enables future experimental searches for the Efimov effect via atom-molecule phase separation and vortex-based phase discrimination.
