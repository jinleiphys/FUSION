# Quasi-elastic scattering and fusion with a modified Woods-Saxon potential

> The elastic and large-angle quasi-elastic scattering reactions were studied with the same nucleus-nucleus potential proposed for describing fusion reactions. The elastic scattering angle distributions of some reactions are reasonably well reproduced by the proposed Woods-Saxon potential with fixed parameters at energies much higher than the Coulomb barrier. With an empirical barrier distribution based on the modified Woods-Saxon potential and taking into account the influence of nucleons transfer, the calculated quasi-elastic scattering cross sections of a series of reactions are in good agreement with the experimental data.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a single modified Woods-Saxon potential (MWS), with fixed parameters, can simultaneously describe both elastic scattering at energies much higher than the Coulomb barrier and, when combined with an empirical barrier distribution and a correction for nucleon transfer, large-angle quasi-elastic scattering and fusion cross sections for a series of reactions. The hedge is that the MWS potential alone fails to reproduce fusion cross sections at above-barrier energies, requiring the additional empirical barrier distribution to account for coupling to internal degrees of freedom.

## Method
The authors used a modified Woods-Saxon potential with five parameters (depth, radius, diffuseness, and isospin-dependent terms) derived from the Skyrme energy-density functional and extended Thomas-Fermi approach. Elastic scattering angular distributions were calculated by solving the Schrödinger equation with the optical model using the MWS potential as both the real and imaginary parts. For quasi-elastic scattering and fusion, an empirical barrier distribution (a weighted sum of two Gaussian functions based on the MWS barrier height) was introduced, and a correction term approximating the one-neutron transfer probability was added to the quasi-elastic scattering formula.

## Key numbers
- MWS potential parameters: $r_0 = 1.27$ fm, $c = -1.37$ fm, $u_0 = -44.16$ MeV, $\kappa = -0.40$, $a = 0.75$ fm.
- For $^{16}$O+$^{208}$Pb: barrier height $B_0 = 78.72$ MeV; most probable barrier height $B_{\text{m.p.}} = 74.43$ MeV.
- For $^{16}$O+$^{232}$Th: measured transfer probabilities (including 1p, 1p1n, 1α, 2p, 2p1n channels) were compared with the Gaussian function $D_2$ from the empirical barrier distribution at above-barrier energies.
- Quasi-elastic scattering and fusion cross sections were calculated for six reactions: $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm, $^{16}$O+$^{154}$Sm, $^{16}$O+$^{92}$Zr, $^{16}$O+$^{186}$W, $^{32}$S+$^{208}$Pb, and $^{16}$O+$^{116}$Sn, and four additional reactions: $^{12}$C+$^{142}$Nd, $^{16}$O+$^{232}$Th, $^{16}$O+$^{64}$Zn, and $^{32}$S+$^{110}$Pd.

## Context
This work extends the modified Woods-Saxon potential model previously proposed by Wang (2008) and Liu (2006) for fusion reactions to also describe elastic and quasi-elastic scattering. It addresses a discrepancy noted by Mukherjee (2007) and Zamrun and Hagino (2008), who found that a standard Woods-Saxon potential could not simultaneously fit fusion and elastic scattering data for reactions like $^{12}$C+$^{208}$Pb and $^{16}$O+$^{144}$Sm. The approach enables a unified description of scattering and fusion for a wide range of heavy-ion reactions, potentially improving predictions for unmeasured systems and for the synthesis of super-heavy nuclei.
