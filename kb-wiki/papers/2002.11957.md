# New developments in lattice QCD on equilibrium physics and phase diagram

> I review recent new lattice QCD results on a few selected topics which are relevant to the heavy ion physics community. Special emphasis is put on the QCD phase diagram towards the chiral limit and at nonzero baryon density as well as the fate of quarkonia and heavy quark drag coefficients.

## Key claim

The paper reviews recent lattice QCD results to constrain the QCD phase diagram, arguing that the chiral phase transition temperature in the chiral limit (132+3-6 MeV) provides an upper bound for the temperature of the QCD critical end point (CEP), which is estimated to be below 135-140 MeV with a baryon chemical potential above 300 MeV, though these bounds depend on the assumption that the chiral transition is second-order O(4) and that the tricritical point lies between the CEP and the zero-density transition.

## Method

The author surveys lattice QCD simulations using Taylor expansion and imaginary chemical potential methods to circumvent the sign problem at nonzero baryon density. Chiral susceptibilities are computed for various pion masses (55-160 MeV) and extrapolated to the chiral and continuum limits using O(4) scaling relations. Higher-order baryon number susceptibilities (up to 8th order) are calculated via Taylor expansion of the pressure, and quarkonium spectral functions are extracted from NRQCD correlation functions on anisotropic lattices with m_pi ≈ 161 MeV and from continuum-extrapolated quenched QCD correlators fitted with pQCD-motivated ansatze.

## Key numbers

- Chiral phase transition temperature in the chiral limit: T_c^0 = 132+3-6 MeV (Ding 2019)
- Chiral crossover temperature at physical quark masses and zero baryon density: 156.5 ± 1.5 MeV (HotQCD) or 158.0 ± 0.6 MeV (Wuppertal-Budapest)
- Curvature parameter κ2 for the chiral crossover line: range 0.008 to 0.0171, with κ4 consistent with zero
- Best estimate for CEP location: T_c^CEP < 135-140 MeV, μ_B^CEP > 300 MeV
- Lowest critical pion mass for the first-order chiral transition region: about 55 MeV (Bazavov 2017)
- At μ_B=0 and T=150 MeV: skewness and kurtosis ratios R_31^B ≈ R_42^B = 0.84(2); hyper-skewness and hyper-kurtosis ratios R_51^B ≈ R_62^B = -0.1(3) (up to leading order in μ_B)
- Bottomonium thermal width ordering: Γ_Υ(1S) < Γ_χb0(1P) < Γ_Υ(2S) < Γ_χb0(2P) < Γ_Υ(3S)
- No J/ψ peak needed in spectral function fits for T ≥ 1.1 T_c; Υ resonance peak persists up to 1.5 T_c

## Context

This review extends lattice QCD studies of the Columbia plot (Endrodi 2007, Bazavov 2017) and chiral transition scaling (Fukaya 2017, Suzuki 2020) to constrain the CEP location, building on earlier work by Hatta (2002) and Halasz (1998) relating the tricritical point and CEP temperatures. The results on baryon number fluctuations provide a thermal equilibrium baseline for comparison with STAR collaboration measurements (Adam 2020), while the quarkonium studies (Larsen 2019, Lorenz 2020) extend NRQCD and quenched QCD approaches to sequential dissociation and heavy quark transport, enabling future comparisons with pQCD and phenomenological models of heavy-ion collisions.
