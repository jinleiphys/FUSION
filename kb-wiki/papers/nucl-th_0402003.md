# Induced Nucleon Polarization and Meson-Exchange Currents in (e,e'p) Reactions

> Nucleon recoil polarization observables in $(e,e'\vec{p})$ reactions are investigated using a semi-relativistic distorted-wave model which includes one- and two-body currents with relativistic corrections. Results for the induced polarization asymmetry are shown for closed-shell nuclei and a comparison with available experimental data for $^{12}$C is provided. A careful analysis of meson exchange currents shows that they may affect significantly the induced polarization for high missing momentum.

## Key claim

Meson exchange currents (MEC) may affect the induced polarization asymmetry significantly for high missing momentum (above 200 MeV/c), but are negligible for low missing momentum (below 200 MeV/c), justifying the impulse approximation in the low-p regime.

## Method

The authors use a semi-relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (DWIA) model that includes one-body (OB) and two-body meson exchange currents (MEC) with relativistic corrections expanded to first order in missing momentum over nucleon mass. The final proton state is described by solving a Schrodinger equation with a non-relativistic optical potential but using relativistic energy-momentum relation, and the induced polarization components (P_l, P_t, P_n) are computed for proton knockout from closed-shell nuclei (16O and 12C) at various kinematics.

## Key numbers

- For 16O at q=460 MeV/c, omega=100 MeV, theta_e=30 deg: MEC contributions to P_n are negligible for p<200 MeV/c but modify results significantly for p>200 MeV/c, especially for phi=0 deg
- For 16O at q=1 GeV/c, omega=450 MeV, theta_e=30 deg: MEC contributions are negligible for p<=200 MeV/c but become important for p>300 MeV/c, particularly for P_n at phi=180 deg and P_t at phi=90 deg
- For 12C at q~760 MeV/c, omega~290 MeV (|Q^2|=0.5 (GeV/c)^2): MEC contributions to P_n are negligible for p<200 MeV/c where experimental data are located, with significant effects only for p>300 MeV/c
- The experimental data point for 12C at p~40 MeV/c shows extreme sensitivity to kinematics: a slight change of (q,omega) can shift the allowed region by more than 25 MeV/c

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous semi-relativistic model (Kazemi Tabatabaei 2003) from transferred polarization asymmetries (requiring polarized electrons) to induced polarization asymmetries from unpolarized electrons, which are zero in plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and thus sensitive to final state interactions. The results are compared with experimental data for 12C from Woo (1998) and with prior calculations by the Gent group (Ryc99) and Pavia group (Boffi 1990), finding agreement on small MEC effects at low missing momentum but discrepancies due to different FSI models. The work enables future experimental exploration of MEC effects at high missing momentum, where both dynamical relativity (Udias 2000, Cris03) and two-body currents are expected to contribute.
