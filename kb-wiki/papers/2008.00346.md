# Flavor-dependent U(3) Nambu Jona Lasinio coupling constant

> A non-perturbative one gluon exchange quark-antiquark interaction is considered to compute flavor dependent U(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type interaction of the form $G_{ij, Γ} (\barψ λ_i Γψ) ( \barψ λ_j Γψ)$ for $i,j=0...8$ and $Γ=I, i γ_5$ from one loop polarization process with non degenerate u-d-s quark effective masses. The resulting NJL-type coupling constants in all channels are resolved in the long-wavelength limit and numerical results are presented for different choices of an effective gluon propagator. Leading deviations with respect to a flavor symmetric coupling constant are found to be of the order of $(M_{f_2}^*-M_{f_1}^*)^n/(M_{f_2}^*+M_{f_1}^*)^n$, for $n=1,2$, where $M_{f_i}^*$ are the effective masses of quarks $f_1,f_2=u, d$ and $s$. The scalar channel coupling constants $G_{ij, s}$ can be considerably smaller than pseudoscalar ones. The effect of the flavor-dependence of coupling constants for the masses of pions and kaons may be nearly of the same order of magnitude as the effect of the u,d and s quark mass non-degeneracy. The effect of these coupling constants is also verified for some of the light scalar mesons masses, usually described by quark-antiquark states, and for some observables of the pseudoscalar mesons.

## Key claim

The paper derives flavor-dependent U(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL)-type coupling constants from a non-perturbative one-gluon exchange interaction, finding that leading deviations from a flavor-symmetric coupling constant scale as $(M_{f_2}^*-M_{f_1}^*)^n/(M_{f_2}^*+M_{f_1}^*)^n$ for $n=1,2$, where $M_{f_i}^*$ are effective quark masses; the effect of this flavor-dependence on pion and kaon masses may be nearly the same order of magnitude as the effect of the u,d,s quark mass non-degeneracy itself.

## Method

The authors start from a low-energy quark effective action with a non-perturbative one-gluon exchange interaction, perform a Fierz transformation to isolate color-singlet scalar and pseudoscalar channels, and split the quark field into background (constituent) and sea components using a background field method. They then compute the one-loop polarization process with non-degenerate u,d,s effective quark masses, perform a large quark mass expansion with a zero-order derivative expansion in the long-wavelength limit, and resolve the resulting NJL-type coupling constants $G_{ij,\Gamma}$ for all flavor channels $i,j=0...8$ and $\Gamma=I, i\gamma_5$ using three different effective gluon propagators (one from Schwinger-Dyson equations and two confining parameterizations).

- For parameter set $X_{20}$ with gluon propagator $D_{I,2}$: $G_{11}^n = 10.00$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{44}^n = 9.77$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{88}^n = 7.61$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{00}^n = 8.60$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{08}^n = 1.80$ GeV$^{-2}$
- For parameter set $X_{20}$ with gluon propagator $D_{II,5}$: $G_{11}^n = 10.00$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{44}^n = 9.82$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{88}^n = 8.13$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{00}^n = 8.90$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{08}^n = 1.41$ GeV$^{-2}$
- For parameter set $Y_{14}$ with gluon propagator $D_{I,2}$ in the scalar channel: $G_{11,s}^n = 0.31$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{88,s}^n = -0.98$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{00,s}^n = -0.47$ GeV$^{-2}$, $G_{08,s}^n = 0.96$ GeV$^{-2}$

- The difference $G_{33} - G_{11} \propto ((M_d^* - M_u^*)/(M_d^* + M_u^*))^2 \sim 10^{-4}$

## Context

This work extends previous calculations for u-d-s or u-d degenerate quarks by Braghin (2014, 2016) to the full U(3) flavor case with non-degenerate effective masses, deriving flavor-dependent NJL coupling constants from a non-perturbative gluon exchange framework. The results are relevant for fine-tuning descriptions of light pseudoscalar mesons (pions, kaons) and scalar mesons ($a_0$, $K^*$ or $\kappa$), addressing the known problem in standard NJL models of the inverted mass behavior of $a_0$ and $K^*$ noted by Hiller et al. (2007). The flavor-dependent coupling constants enable further investigation of $\eta-\eta'-\pi_0$ mixings and may provide insights into the structure of light scalar mesons, which have been debated as possible tetraquarks, glueballs, or meson molecules by Pelaez (2016) and others.
