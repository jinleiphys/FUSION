# Impact of ground-state correlations on the multipole response of nuclei: Ab initio calculations of moment operators

> We develop a framework that allows to calculate integrated properties of the nuclear response from first principles. Using the ab initio in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG), we calculate the expectation values of moment operators that are linked to the multipole response of nuclei. This approach is applied to the isoscalar mono- and quadrupole as well as the isovector dipole response of closed-shell nuclei from $^4$He to $^{78}$Ni for different chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions. We find that the inclusion of many-body correlations in the nuclear ground state significantly impacts the multipole response when going from the random-phase approximation to the IMSRG level. Our IMSRG calculations lead to an improved description of experimental data in $^{16}$O and $^{40}$Ca, including a good reproduction of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn enhancement factor. These findings highlight the utility of the moment method as a benchmark for other ab initio approaches that describe nuclear response functions through the explicit treatment of excited states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including many-body correlations in the nuclear ground state via the in-medium similarity renormalization group (IMSRG) significantly impacts the calculated multipole response compared to random-phase approximation (RPA) results, but this improvement is conditional on the specific chiral interaction used and is most pronounced for the isovector dipole response.

## Method

The authors compute the zeroth and first moments of the strength function for isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole operators using ground-state expectation values of moment operators derived from nested commutators. They perform calculations at the Hartree-Fock (HF) level and the IMSRG(2) level for closed-shell nuclei from ⁴He to ⁷⁸Ni, using four different chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions expanded in a spherical harmonic-oscillator basis with up to 13 major shells.

## Key numbers

- For the isovector dipole m₁ moment, model-space convergence from e_max=10 to e_max=12 shows variations smaller than 0.2% at both HF and IMSRG(2) levels for all nuclei studied
- For the isovector dipole m₀ moment, the relative difference between e_max=10 and e_max=12 results is smaller than 1.3%
- The average relative spread (standard deviation) of m₁ results across different interactions drops from 6% at HF/RPA to 2% at IMSRG(2) for all multipolarities
- For the isovector dipole centroid energy Ē = m₁/m₀, the largest convergence error is 1.3% in ⁵²Ca
- For the isovector dipole m₁ in ⁷⁸Ni, the relative standard deviation across calculations at ħω=12, 16, and 20 MeV is 0.23% at HF and 0.17% at IMSRG level
- The authors estimate a comprehensive 2% error for m₀ and 0.3% error for m₁ from the finite basis space

## Context

This work extends the moment method previously applied within the phenomenological shell model (Johnson 2015, Lu and Johnson 2018, Johnson 2020, Xie 2025) and the projected generator coordinate method (Porro 2024) to ab initio IMSRG calculations. The approach enables benchmarking of other ab initio methods that describe nuclear response functions through explicit treatment of excited states, such as the Lorentz integral transform method with coupled-cluster (Bacca 2013, 2014; Miorelli 2016; Acharya 2024), self-consistent Green's function method (Raimondi 2018), and equation-of-motion IMSRG (Parzuchowski 2016, 2017). The results highlight the importance of consistently treating correlations in both ground and excited states, as emphasized by Carter (2022) and Beaujeault-Taudiere (2022).
