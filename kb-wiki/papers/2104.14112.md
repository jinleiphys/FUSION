# Thermomagnetic modification of the anomalous magnetic moment of quarks using the NJL model

> The effective photon-quark-antiquark ($γq \overline{q}$) vertex function is evaluated at finite temperature in the presence of an arbitrary external magnetic field using the two-flavor gauged Nambu--Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean field approximation. The lowest order diagram contributing to the magnetic form factor and the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of the quarks is calculated at finite temperature and external magnetic field using the imaginary time formalism of finite temperature field theory and the Schwinger proper time formalism. The Schwinger propagator including all the Landau levels with non-zero AMM of the dressed quarks is considered while calculating the loop diagram. Using sharp as well as smooth three momentum cutoff, we regularize the UV divergences arising from the vertex function and the parameters of our model are chosen to reproduce the well known phenomenological quantities at zero temperature and zero magnetic field, such as pion-decay constant ($f_π$), vacuum quark condensate, vacuum pion mass ($m_π$) as well as the magnetic moments of proton and neutron. We then study the temperature and magnetic field dependence of the AMM and constituent mass of the quark. We found that, the AMM as well as the constituent quark mass are large at the chiral symmetry broken phase in the low temperature region. Around the pseudo-chiral phase transition they decrease rapidly and at high temperatures both of them approach vanishingly small values in the symmetry restored phase.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the anomalous magnetic moment (AMM) of up and down quarks from the photon-quark-antiquark vertex function using the two-flavor gauged Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, finding that both the AMM and constituent quark mass are large in the chiral symmetry broken phase at low temperature, decrease rapidly around the pseudo-chiral phase transition, and approach vanishingly small values at high temperatures in the symmetry restored phase; the calculation explicitly reproduces the experimental magnetic moments of the proton and neutron at zero temperature and zero magnetic field.

## Method

The effective photon-quark-antiquark vertex function was evaluated at finite temperature and arbitrary external magnetic field using the two-flavor gauged NJL model in the mean field approximation. The lowest order diagram contributing to the magnetic form factor was calculated using the imaginary time formalism of finite temperature field theory and the Schwinger proper time formalism, incorporating the full Schwinger propagator including all Landau levels with non-zero AMM of the dressed quarks. The UV divergences from the vertex function were regularized using both sharp and smooth three-momentum cutoffs, and the model parameters were fixed to reproduce the pion-decay constant, vacuum quark condensate, vacuum pion mass, and the magnetic moments of the proton and neutron. The AMM and constituent quark mass were then obtained by solving a set of three coupled non-linear gap equations simultaneously.

## Key numbers

* At zero temperature and zero magnetic field, the constituent quark mass is M = 343.8 MeV, with up quark AMM kappa_u = 0.02399 GeV^{-1} and down quark AMM kappa_d = 0.09595 GeV^{-1}.
* The model parameters are: sharp cutoff Lambda_sharp = 623.95 MeV, smooth cutoff Lambda_smooth = 568.69 MeV, coupling constant G = 5.844 GeV^{-2}, and current quark mass m = 5.6 MeV.
* The vacuum quark condensate per flavor is -(243.5)^3 MeV^3, the pion decay constant is f_pi = 93 MeV, and the pion mass is m_pi = 138 MeV.
* The magnetic moments of the nucleons reproduced at zero temperature and zero magnetic field are mu_proton = 2.7928 mu_N and mu_neutron = -1.9130 mu_N, where mu_N is the nuclear magneton and the nucleon mass M_N = 938 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies using the NJL model with non-zero AMM of quarks (Chaudhuri et al., Sadooghi et al.) that examined modifications to thermodynamical quantities, meson masses, and dilepton production rates in magnetic fields. The calculation explicitly derives the AMM from the vertex function rather than using constituent quark model values extracted from nucleon magnetic moments as done in earlier works (Halzen and Martin, 1984; Bicudo et al., 1998). This enables self-consistent studies of the thermomagnetic behavior of quark matter and its phase structure, relevant for understanding the quark-gluon plasma created in heavy-ion collisions and the interior of magnetars.
