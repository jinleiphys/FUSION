# Extended WKB method, resonances and supersymmetric radial barriers

> Semiclassical approximations are implemented in the calculation of position and width of low energy resonances for radial barriers. The numerical integrations are delimited by t/T<<8, with t the period of a classical particle in the barrier trap and T the resonance lifetime. These energies are used in the construction of `haired' short range potentials as the supersymmetric partners of a given radial barrier. The new potentials could be useful in the study of the transient phenomena which give rise to the Moshinsky's diffraction in time.

## Key claim

The paper presents an extended WKB method that can calculate the position and width of low-energy shape resonances for radial barriers, with the condition that the numerical integrations are limited by the inequality $\tau/\tau_{\rm life}\ll 8$, where $\tau$ is the period of a classical particle in the barrier trap and $\tau_{\rm life}$ is the resonance lifetime.

## Method

The authors extend the standard WKB method to complex-valued energies $\epsilon = E - i\Gamma/2$ by imposing the Siegert outgoing-wave condition and requiring regularity at the origin. They derive a quantization condition for the resonance position $E$ and an analytical expression for the resonance width $\Gamma = 2e^{-2\Omega(a,b)}/\tau$, where $\Omega(a,b)$ is the WKB tunneling integral between the classical turning points $a$ and $b$, and $\tau$ is the classical oscillation period. The method is tested on the BBJS potential $V(r)=V_0 r^2 e^{-\lambda r}$ and compared with results from Bain et al. (1974) and Korsch et al. (1982).

## Key numbers

* For the BBJS potential with $\lambda=1$ and $V_0=15$ ($V_{\rm max}=8.12012$), the single resonance from the extended WKB method is $\epsilon_0 = 7.01129 - i\,0.03717$, compared to the BBJS value $6.8722 - i\,0.025549$ and the KLM value $6.994 - i\,0.02787$.
* For $V_0=350$ ($V_{\rm max}=189.46939$), the five resonances are: $\epsilon_0 = 46.4430 - i\,4.23210\times10^{-36}$, $\epsilon_1 = 95.9768 - i\,2.91608\times10^{-21}$, $\epsilon_2 = 135.8367 - i\,1.23305\times10^{-11}$, $\epsilon_3 = 167.0870 - i\,5.98751\times10^{-5}$, $\epsilon_4 = 188.5395 - i\,0.71602$.
* For the anharmonic spherical oscillator $V(r)=\frac12 kr^2 - gr^3$ with $k=800$, $g=400$ ($V_{\rm max}=59.25925$), the single low-energy resonance is $\epsilon = 47.0105 - i\,0.861937$.
* The potential $V(r)=V_0 r^2 e^{-r}$ admits a single resonance when $8.44539 \leq V_0 < 45.9804$, as determined by the condition $V_0(n) = \beta_0 (n+3/4)^2$ with $\beta_0 \approx 15.014$.

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical WKB approach to resonance calculations for radial barriers, building on the complex-scaling method of Bain et al. (1974) and the Milne differential equation approach of Korsch et al. (1982). The derived resonance energies are used to construct complex Darboux (supersymmetric) transformations, producing "haired" short-range potentials that are real-valued after a second transformation. These new potentials could enable studies of transient scattering phenomena related to Moshinsky's diffraction in time, connecting supersymmetric quantum mechanics to time-delay problems.
