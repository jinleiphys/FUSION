# In-Medium Modifications of Hadron Masses and Chemical Freeze-Out in Ultra-Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> We confront the hypothesis of chemical freeze-out in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions with the hypothesis of large modifications of hadron masses in nuclear medium. We find that the thermal-model predictions for the ratios of particle multiplicities are sensitive to the values of in-medium hadronic masses. In particular, the $π^+/p$ ratio decreases by 35% when the masses of all hadrons (except for pseudo-Goldstone bosons) are scaled down by 30%.

## Key claim

The thermal-model predictions for particle multiplicity ratios in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions are sensitive to in-medium modifications of hadron masses; specifically, the π⁺/p ratio decreases by 35% when the masses of all hadrons (except pseudo-Goldstone bosons) are scaled down by 30%, but the authors caution that the numerical success of the simple thermal model may be misleading and that a more elaborate treatment could still allow substantial mass modifications.

## Method

The authors used the standard ideal-gas equilibrium expression to calculate particle densities at chemical freeze-out, taking the thermodynamic parameters (T_chem = 168 MeV, μ_B = 266 MeV, μ_S = 71 MeV, μ_I = -5 MeV) from previous thermal-model fits to Pb+Pb collisions at CERN/SPS energies. They rescaled the masses of all meson and baryon resonances (excluding pions, kaons, and the eta) by universal parameters x_M and x_B, while keeping branching ratios at vacuum values, and computed the resulting π⁺/p ratio for various scaling scenarios.

## Key numbers

- T_chem = 168 MeV, μ_B = 266 MeV, μ_S = 71 MeV, μ_I = -5 MeV for Pb+Pb collisions at CERN/SPS energies (from prior thermal-model fits)
- π⁺/p ratio decreases by 35% when all hadron masses (except pseudo-Goldstone bosons) are scaled down by 30% (x_M = x_B = 0.7)
- Reduction of masses by 20% changes the π⁺/p ratio by at least 30% in the case where x_M = x_B
- With constant meson masses (x_M = 1), a 20% decrease in baryon masses reduces the π⁺/p ratio by 50%
- With constant baryon masses (x_B = 1), a 20% decrease in meson masses enhances the π⁺/p ratio by 60%
- The scaling parameters x_M and x_B were restricted to the range 0.6 < x < 1.1

## Context

This work extends the thermal-model analysis of chemical freeze-out in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, previously applied by Braun-Munzinger and Stachel (1999) and others, by incorporating in-medium mass modifications predicted by Brown and Rho (1991) and other theoretical calculations. The results suggest that the successful thermal fits leave little room for mass modifications larger than 10-20%, but the authors note that this conclusion is preliminary given the incomplete understanding of hadron production mechanisms, and they call for incorporating mass scaling into other existing approaches to heavy-ion collisions.
