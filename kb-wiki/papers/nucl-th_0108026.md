# Singlet and Triplet Differential Cross Sections for pp-> pp pi^0

> The singlet and triplet differential cross sections for pp-> pp pi^0 have been estimated for the first time at 325, 350, 375 and 400 MeV using the results of the recent experimental measurements [Phys. Rev. C 63, 064002 (2001)] of Meyer et al.

# Singlet and Triplet Differential Cross Sections for pp → pp π⁰

## Key claim

The singlet and triplet differential cross sections for pp → pp π⁰ have been estimated for the first time at 325, 350, 375 and 400 MeV, using the experimental measurements of Meyer et al. (2001) and an irreducible tensor formalism that is not limited by the number of final partial waves; the estimates rely on the assumption that the coefficients from Meyer et al. (2001) are adequate to deduce the one-dimensional differential cross sections, but additional experimental data on the H₃ coefficients would be needed for double-differential estimates.

## Method

The authors employed an irreducible tensor approach to pion production in NN collisions, expressing the differential cross section in terms of irreducible tensor amplitudes M^λ_μ(s_f, s_i) of rank λ = |s_i − s_f| to (s_i + s_f). They related the bilinear combinations of these amplitudes to the experimentally determined coefficients E, F_k, H_k^{ij}, I, K from Meyer et al. (2001), then extracted the singlet and triplet components of the differential cross section by evaluating the channel-spin decomposition of the initial spin density matrix. The one-dimensional differential cross sections were obtained by integrating over the appropriate kinematic variables, using 16 partial wave amplitudes (11 leading to s_f = 1 and 5 leading to s_f = 0).

- At 325 MeV: α₁ = 0.166 ± 0.082, α₂ = 2.996 ± 0.349, F₁ = 0.168 ± 0.021 (dimensionless coefficients from Table 1)
- At 350 MeV: α₁ = 0.361 ± 0.087, α₂ = 2.218 ± 0.364, F₁ = 0.265 ± 0.022
- At 375 MeV: α₁ = 0.665 ± 0.034, α₂ = 1.622 ± 0.142, F₁ = 0.262 ± 0.007
- At 400 MeV: α₁ = 0.717 ± 0.054, α₂ = 1.378 ± 0.233, F₁ = 0.297 ± 0.013

## Context

This work extends the analysis of polarization observables for pp → pp π⁰ reported by Meyer et al. (2001) by providing the first decomposition of differential cross sections into singlet and triplet components, which previous analyses by Bilenky and Ryndin had only addressed at the total cross section level. The irreducible tensor formalism developed earlier by the authors (Ramachandran, prior work) enables this decomposition without being limited by the number of final partial waves, unlike partial-wave analyses that had considered transitions to Ss, Ps, Pp, Sd, and Ds states. The results enable future experimental measurements to directly determine the double-differential singlet and triplet cross sections, for which the authors note that additional data on the H₃ coefficients from Meyer et al. (2001) would be required.
