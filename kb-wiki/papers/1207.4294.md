# Perturbative versus non-perturbative aspects of jet quenching: in-medium breaking of color coherence

> The quenching of jets (and high-pT particle spectra) observed in heavy-ion collisions is interpreted as due to the energy lost by hard partons crossing the Quark Gluon Plasma. Here we review recent efforts to include in its modeling important qualitative features of QCD, like the correlations in multiple gluon emissions and the color-flow pattern in parton branchings. In particular, the modification of color connections among the partons of a shower developing in the presence of a medium is a generic occurrence accompanying parton energy-loss. We show how this effect can leave its fingerprints at the hadronization stage, leading by itself to a softening of hadron spectra and to an enhanced production of soft particles in jet-fragmentation.

## Key claim

The modification of color connections among partons in a shower developing inside a Quark Gluon Plasma is a generic occurrence that accompanies parton energy loss, and this color decoherence can by itself lead to a softening of hadron spectra and enhanced soft particle production in jet fragmentation, independent of when hadronization occurs.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical framework of jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, focusing on how medium-induced gluon radiation modifies the angular ordering and color-flow pattern of QCD showers. It analyzes the radiation spectrum of a color-singlet q-anti-q antenna in the presence of a medium, and examines color-differential calculations of gluon radiation where the color flow determines whether emitted gluons remain color-connected to the leading fragment (Final State Radiation channel) or become decohered (Initial State Radiation channel). The Lund string fragmentation model implemented in PYTHIA 6.4 is applied to partonic configurations arising from medium interactions to compute the resulting hadron distributions.

## Key numbers

- The medium-induced radiation parameter Delta_med ranges from 0 (no medium effect) to 1 (complete decoherence of the q-anti-q pair), where at Delta_med = 1 the q and anti-q radiate as two uncorrelated color charges
- In the ISR channel example, the radiated gluon carries transverse momentum k_T = 0.1 p_T relative to the leading quark and is emitted at relative angle phi = 0.1
- The ratio of 6-th moments of the ISR and FSR contributions to fragmentation functions is plotted as a function of the leading quark transverse momentum p_T, providing an estimate of hadron suppression purely from modified color connections
- CMS used a p_T-cut of 4 GeV in their jet fragmentation analysis and found no modification of the hard part of the fragmentation function relative to pp collisions

## Context

This work extends the standard factorized approach to jet quenching (where medium-modified fragmentation functions arise from vacuum fragmentation functions acting on partons that have suffered energy loss according to a probability density P(Delta E)) by incorporating color correlations that were previously ignored. It builds on the angular ordering of vacuum QCD radiation (Angord) and the string effect observed at LEP (Delphi), and connects to recent calculations of QCD antenna radiation in a medium (Yac1, Yac2). The analysis suggests that accounting for modified color connections would allow experimental data to be accommodated with milder values of transport parameters like the widely used parameter hat-q, and raises the question of whether similar color-decoherence effects could be observed in proton-nucleus collisions, as was seen at Fermilab (E557).
