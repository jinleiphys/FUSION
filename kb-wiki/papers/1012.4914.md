# The renormalisation group and nuclear forces

> I give an outline of recent applications of the renormalisation group to effective theories of nuclear forces, focussing on the use of a Wilsonian approach to analyse systems of two or three nonrelativistic particles.

# The renormalisation group and nuclear forces

## Key claim

The renormalisation group (RG) provides a rigorous tool to analyse scale dependences in effective field theories of nuclear forces, but the power counting appropriate for strongly interacting systems near the unitary limit differs substantially from naive dimensional analysis because two-body interactions can become relevant and other operators are strongly promoted. The paper argues that a Wilsonian RG approach can resolve long-standing debates over how to renormalise nonperturbative systems consistently and which pieces of the interaction should be iterated to all orders.

## Method

The author applies a Wilsonian RG approach to construct functional RG equations for systems of two or three nonrelativistic particles, using a momentum-space cutoff regulator and demanding that the off-shell K-matrix be independent of the cutoff. The RG equations are solved exactly for simple cases to identify fixed-point solutions, and perturbations around these fixed points are classified using linearised RG equations to determine the anomalous dimensions of operators and construct a power-counting scheme. The analysis covers short-range contact interactions, long-range forces including Coulomb and one-pion exchange, and three-body systems with particle-exchange forces.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon scattering lengths are of order 5–20 fm, much larger than the range of the interactions.
- The inverse Bohr radius for two protons is κ = α M_N / 2 ≃ 3.4 MeV.
- The momentum scale associated with one-pion exchange is λ_{πNN} = 16π F_π^2 / (g_A^2 M_N) ≃ 290 MeV.
- For three bosons or three distinct fermions near the unitary limit, the attractive 1/R^2 potential leads to an imaginary exponent s_0 ≃ 1.006 as R → 0, producing the Efimov effect.
- In spin-triplet waves with tensor one-pion exchange, the leading contact interaction is of order Q^{-1/2} in waves with L = 1 or 2.
- The three-body operator coupling the ^1S_0 and ^3S_1 NN channels is promoted to order Q^{5/4} by nonperturbative treatment of two-body forces.

## Context

This review extends the work of Weinberg (1990, 1991) on applying effective field theory to nuclear forces, and addresses debates over power counting reviewed by Beane et al. (2001), Bedaque and van Kolck (2002), and Epelbaum et al. (2009). The RG approach developed in Manchester (Birse et al., 1999; Barford and Birse, 2003, 2005) provides a systematic framework that explains the Phillips line correlation between triton binding energy and nd scattering length (Phillips, 1968, 1977) and the Tjon line correlation between ^4He and triton binding energies (Tjon, 1975). The results enable consistent construction of effective current operators and three- and more-body forces, with applications to halo nuclei, heavy-quark meson interactions, and ultracold atomic systems.
