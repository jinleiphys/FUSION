# Microscopic calculations of isospin mixing in N~Z nuclei and isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to the superallowed beta-decay

> Recently, we have applied for the first time the angular momentum and isospin projected nuclear density functional theory to calculate the isospin-symmetry breaking (ISB) corrections to the superallowed beta-decay. With the calculated set of the ISB corrections we found |V_{ud}|=0.97447(23) for the leading element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix. This is in nice agreement with both the recent result of Towner and Hardy [Phys. Rev. {\bf C77}, 025501 (2008)] and the central value deduced from the neutron decay. In this work we extend our calculations of the ISB corrections covering all superallowed transitions A,I^π=0^+,T=1,T_z \rightarrow A,I^π=0^+,T=1,T_z+1 with T_z =-1,0 and A ranging from 10 to 74.

## Key claim

The paper extends the authors' earlier angular-momentum and isospin projected nuclear density functional theory (DFT) calculations of isospin-symmetry-breaking (ISB) corrections to cover all superallowed beta-decay transitions with A from 10 to 74, but the confidence level of the model is lower than that of other models primarily due to a very small delta_C for a single transition in A=62, and the model uses an old and low-quality SV parametrization of the Skyrme force.

## Method

The authors applied angular-momentum and isospin projection on top of Skyrme-DFT, rediagonalizing the entire Hamiltonian including the Coulomb interaction in a good angular-momentum and good isospin basis projected from self-consistent Slater determinants. For even-even nuclei, the I=0, T≈1, T_z=±1 state was projected from the ground-state Slater determinant; for odd-odd N=Z nuclei, the I=0, T≈1, T_z=0 state was projected from an antialigned configuration (odd neutron and odd proton in time-reversed orbits). Calculations used N=10 and N=12 spherical harmonic oscillator shells for A≤34 and A≥38, respectively, with the SV Skyrme interaction augmented by a tensor term.

## Key numbers

- Calculated average nucleus-independent reduced lifetime for twelve high-precision superallowed beta-decays (excluding 38K→38Ar): overline{Ft}=3070.4(9) s
- Resulting CKM matrix element: |V_ud|=0.97447(23)
- CKM first-row unitarity sum: |V_ud|^2 + |V_us|^2 + |V_ub|^2 = 1.00031(61), implying unitarity fulfilled with 0.1% precision
- Calculated delta_C values for A=10: 0.559% (T_z=-1) and 0.497% (T_z=0)
- Calculated delta_C values for A=38: 8.315% (T_z=-1) and 9.826% (T_z=0)
- Calculated delta_C values for A=58: 5.828% (T_z=-1) and 4.235% (T_z=0)
- Calculated delta_C values for A=62: 1.739% (T_z=-1) and 0.854% (T_z=0)
- Calculated delta_C values for A=74: 1.768% (T_z=-1) and 1.956% (T_z=0)

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier angular-momentum and isospin projected DFT calculations (Rafalski et al. 2009, Satula et al. 2010-2011) to cover all superallowed transitions from A=10 to A=74, and compares results to the standard calculations of Towner and Hardy (2008). The approach avoids the artificial division into configuration-mixing and radial-mismatch corrections (delta_C1 and delta_C2) required in shell-model plus mean-field methods, but the SV parametrization used has poor spectroscopic quality and the model lacks pairing correlations. The results enable further tests of CVC hypothesis and CKM unitarity, but the delayed onset of large delta_C around A=62 (compared to A~62 in Towner and Hardy 2008) indicates that configuration mixing in this region is fragile in self-consistent calculations and requires further study.
