# Thermodynamics conditions of matter in neutron star mergers

> Matter in neutron star collisions can reach densities up to few times the nuclear saturation threshold and temperatures up to one hundred MeV. Understanding the structure and composition of such matter requires many-body nonperturbative calculations that are currently highly uncertain.Unique constraints on the neutron star matter are provided by gravitational-wave observations aided by numerical relativity simulations. In this work, we explore the thermodynamical conditions of matter and radiation along the merger dynamics. We consider 3 microphysical equation of state models and numerical relativity simulations including an approximate neutrino transport scheme. The neutron star cores collision and their multiple centrifugal bounces heat the initially cold matter to several tens of MeV. Streams of hot matter with initial densities $\sim1-2ρ_0$ move outwards and cool due to decompression and neutrino emission. The merger can result in a neutron star remnant with densities up to $3-5ρ_0$ and temperatures $\sim 50$~MeV. The highest temperatures are confined in an approximately spherical annulus at densities $\simρ_0$. Such temperatures favour positron-neutron capture at densities $\simρ_0$, thus leading to a neutrino emission dominated by electron antineutrinos. We study the impact of trapped neutrinos on the remnant matter's pressure, electron fraction and temperature and find that it has a negligible effect. Disks around neutron star or black hole remnant are neutron rich and not isentropic, but they differ in size, entropy and lepton fraction depending on the nature of the central object. In presence of a black hole, disks are smaller and mostly transparent to neutrinos; in presence of a massive neutron star, they are more massive, geometrically and optically thick.

## Key claim

The thermodynamical conditions of matter in neutron star mergers are explored using three microphysical equation of state models and numerical relativity simulations with an approximate neutrino transport scheme, finding that the highest temperatures (up to approximately 50 MeV) are confined to an approximately spherical annulus at densities near nuclear saturation density, and that trapped neutrinos have a negligible effect on the remnant matter's pressure, electron fraction, and temperature.

## Method

The authors performed general-relativistic hydrodynamical simulations of binary neutron star mergers using the WhiskyTHC code, employing three different nuclear equation of state models (DD2, LS220, and SFHo) with an approximate M0 neutrino transport scheme. They post-processed simulation results to compute the thermodynamical conditions of matter in the presence of a trapped neutrino gas in weak and thermal equilibrium, solving for equilibrium electron fraction and temperature while conserving lepton number and total internal energy.

## Key numbers

- Maximum temperatures reach approximately 70-100 MeV during the first core bounce, with peak temperatures occurring at typical densities near nuclear saturation density (rho_0 approximately 2.7 x 10^14 g/cm^3)
- The merger remnant can reach densities up to 3-5 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0) and temperatures of approximately 50 MeV
- Neutron star cores in the initial binaries have densities of 2-3 times rho_0, with individual gravitational masses of 1.35-1.40 solar masses
- The LS220 EOS has a symmetry energy slope L = 73.82 MeV, while DD2 has L = 55.04 MeV and SFHo has L = 47.10 MeV
- Cold, non-rotating maximum NS masses are 2.42 solar masses (DD2), 2.06 solar masses (LS220), and 2.06 solar masses (SFHo)
- The cold NS radius for 1.4 solar masses is 13.2 km (DD2), 12.7 km (LS220), and 11.9 km (SFHo)
- Neutrino trapping thresholds are set at rho_lim,e = 5 x 10^11 g/cm^3 for electron neutrinos and antineutrinos, and rho_lim,x = 5 x 10^12 g/cm^3 for heavy-lepton neutrinos

## Context

This work extends previous numerical relativity studies of binary neutron star mergers (Shibata 1999, Sekiguchi 2011, Bernuzzi 2015, Radice 2018) by providing a detailed quantitative mapping of the thermodynamical conditions across the merger dynamics. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino emission, nucleosynthesis yields, and kilonova light curves, and provide a framework for interpreting gravitational-wave observations from post-merger remnants. The finding that trapped neutrinos have negligible dynamical impact supports the use of simpler leakage schemes in future simulations.
