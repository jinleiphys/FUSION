# Scaling Function and Nucleon Momentum Distribution

> Scaling studies of inclusive quasielastic electron scattering reactions have been used in the past as a basic tool to obtain information on the nucleon momentum distribution in nuclei. However, the connection between the scaling function, extracted from the analysis of cross section data, and the spectral function only exists assuming very restricted approximations. We revisit the basic expressions involved in scaling studies and how they can be linked to the nucleon momentum distribution. In particular, the analysis applied in the past to the so-called scaling region, {\it i.e.,} negative values of the scaling variable $y$, is extended here to positive $y$, since a "universal" superscaling function has been extracted from the analysis of the separated longitudinal data. This leads to results that clearly differ from the ones based solely on the negative-$y$ scaling region, providing new information on how the energy and momentum are distributed in the spectral function.

## Key claim

The paper extends the traditional analysis of the scaling function from the negative-\(y\) region (below the quasielastic peak) to the positive-\(y\) region (above the peak) by using the "universal" longitudinal superscaling function extracted from separated longitudinal data, which is less contaminated by non-Impulse-Approximation effects than the transverse channel; this extension yields results for the nucleon momentum distribution that clearly differ from those based solely on the negative-\(y\) scaling region.

## Method

The authors revisit the Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) expressions linking the scaling function \(F(q,\omega)\) to the spectral function \(S(p,\mathcal{E})\) via integrals over missing momentum \(p\) and excitation energy \(\mathcal{E}\) within a kinematically allowed region \(\Sigma(q,\omega)\). They derive analytic derivatives \(\partial F/\partial y\) and \(\partial F/\partial q\) for both negative and positive scaling variable \(y\), separating the spectral function into a ground-state term \(n_0(p)\delta(\mathcal{E})\) and an excited-state term \(S_1(p,\mathcal{E})\). The analysis is applied to the Relativistic Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA) for \(^{12}\text{C}\) at various momentum transfers, and the results are compared with the experimental superscaling function.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{12}\text{C}\) with Fermi momentum \(k_F = 1.2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\), the RPWIA superscaling function \(f(\psi)\) increases with \(q\) at fixed negative \(\psi\) and at small positive \(\psi\) (e.g., \(y/k_F \sim 0.1\) GeV/c), while for larger positive \(y/k_F > 0.3\) the \(q\)-dependence becomes very weak.
* The crossover where \(\partial F/\partial q\) changes sign occurs near \(y/k_F \sim 0.364\) for \(q = 0.5\) GeV/c, \(y/k_F \sim 0.375\) for \(q = 1.0\) GeV/c, and \(y/k_F \sim 0.382\) for \(q = \infty\) (with \(k_F = 1.2\ \text{fm}^{-1}\)), corresponding to \(y \sim 0.1\) GeV/c.
* In the thermodynamic limit (\(M_B^0 \to \infty\)) and for large \(q\), the upper limit of missing momentum becomes \(Y \to 2q + y\), and the excitation energy limits simplify to \(\mathcal{E}^\pm \to \sqrt{(q+y)^2 + m_N^2} - \sqrt{(q \pm p)^2 + m_N^2}\).

## Context

This work extends the \(y\)-scaling formalism developed by Day et al. (1990) and Ciofi degli Atti et al. (1987–1999), which was previously restricted to negative \(y\) values below the quasielastic peak. By exploiting the longitudinal superscaling function extracted from separated \((e,e')\) data (Donnelly and Sick, 1999; Maieron, Donnelly, and Sick, 2002), the authors enable a new probe of the spectral function's energy and momentum content in the positive-\(y\) region, which is otherwise masked by scaling violations from meson-exchange currents and inelastic processes in the transverse channel. The results provide constraints for theoretical models of neutrino-nucleus cross sections relevant to oscillation experiments (Amaro et al., 2004).
