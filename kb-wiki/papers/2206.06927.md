# On Ambiguity of Definition of Shear and Spin-Hall Contributions to $Λ$ Polarization in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> Recently proposed thermal-shear and spin-Hall contributions to the particle polarization in heavy-ion collisions are discussed. Alternative definitions of the thermal-shear contribution, i.e. those of Becattini-Buzzegoli-Palermo on the one hand and Liu-Yin on the other, are very similar in the midrapidity region while quite different at forward-backward rapidities, which are measured in fixed-target experiments. It is shown that the thermal-shear contribution to the global polarization with momentum averaging extended to all momenta is very different within these alternative definitions. The spin-Hall contribution to the global polarization, defined similarly to the Liu-Yin shear one, is identically zero, if averaging runs over all momenta. Only application of restrictive momentum acceptance and the boost (to $Λ$ rest frame) correction result in nonzero global spin-Hall polarization. If the spin-Hall contribution were defined similarly to Becattini-Buzzegoli-Palermo shear one, the global spin-Hall polarization would be non-zero even without any acceptance and the boost correction.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the alternative definitions of the thermal-shear and spin-Hall contributions to Λ polarization, those of Becattini-Buzzegoli-Palermo (using n_β = t̂_β) versus Liu-Yin (using n_β = u_β), produce very different results for global polarization when averaged over all momenta, despite being similar in the midrapidity region.

## Method

The authors analytically integrated the thermal-vorticity, thermal-shear, and spin-Hall contributions to the mean spin vector over momentum space, without imposing experimental acceptance constraints, to derive the tensor structure of each contribution to the global polarization. They then compared the resulting expressions for the two choices of the normal four-vector n_β (the time direction in the center-of-mass frame versus the fluid four-velocity) and examined the role of the boost correction to the Λ rest frame.

## Key numbers

- The STAR Collaboration measured global Λ polarization corresponding to fluid vorticity of ω ≈ (9 ± 1) × 10^21 s^{-1}
- Non-central heavy-ion collisions at high energies are characterized by global angular momentum of order 10^3–10^5 ħ, depending on collision energy and centrality
- If n^μ = u^μ (Liu-Yin definition), the spin-Hall contribution to global polarization from S_ζ^μ is identically zero when averaging over all momenta
- If n^μ = t̂^μ (Becattini-Buzzegoli-Palermo definition), the global spin-Hall polarization would be non-zero even without any acceptance and the boost correction

## Context

This work extends the thermodynamic approach to Λ polarization in heavy-ion collisions, which was previously based primarily on thermal vorticity (Becattini et al., 2013–2016) and meson-field contributions (Csernai, 2018). The paper directly compares the field-theoretical approach of Becattini-Buzzegoli-Palermo (2021) with the quantum kinetic approach of Liu-Yin (2020–2021), showing that the choice of normal vector n_β leads to qualitatively different predictions for global polarization at forward-backward rapidities relevant to fixed-target experiments such as STAR (2021) and HADES (2021). The results enable experimental discrimination between these theoretical definitions through fixed-target polarization measurements.
