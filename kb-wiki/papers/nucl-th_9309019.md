# Width of the $Δ$ resonance in nuclei

> In this work we evaluate the imaginary part of the isobar $Δ$ self-energy $Σ_Δ$ from the two-body absorption process $Δ+N\rightarrow 2N$. This contribution is calculated using a recently developed non-relativistic scheme, which allows for an evaluation of the self-energy with a basis of single-particle states appropriate for both bound hole states and for particle states in the continuum. In order to test the medium dependence of the self-energy, we calculate the two-body absorption term $Σ_Δ^{A2}$ for several finite nuclei with $N=Z$, i.e.\ $^{16}$O, $^{40}$Ca and $^{100}$Sn. The resulting self-energy, which is energy dependent and non-local, is compared with a simple parameterization derived from nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The microscopic calculation of the two-body absorption contribution to the Delta self-energy in finite nuclei supports the nuclear-matter-based parameterization of Nieves et al. (1993), but only when comparing at a "typical" radius of 1.5 fm and an average density of 0.75 times nuclear matter saturation density; the finite-nucleus results also show a surface enhancement at lower energies (below 200 MeV) that the parameterization does not capture.

## Method

The authors calculated the imaginary part of the Delta self-energy from the two-body absorption process Delta + N -> 2N using a non-relativistic scheme with harmonic oscillator wave functions for bound hole states and plane waves for particle states in the continuum. They employed a transition potential with pi and rho meson exchange, regularized by monopole form factors with cutoff masses of 1.2 GeV and 1.3 GeV respectively, and used the Bonn B nucleon-nucleon potential to compute the G-matrix via the Bethe-Goldstone equation with a fixed starting energy of -10 MeV and Fermi momentum 1.4 fm^-1. The self-energy was evaluated for the finite nuclei 16O, 40Ca, and 100Sn, and a local approximation was extracted as a function of radial distance from the nuclear center.

## Key numbers

- The cutoff masses used for the transition potential are Lambda_pi = 1.2 GeV and Lambda_rho = 1.3 GeV.
- The coupling constants are g_piNN^2/4pi = 14.6 and g_rhoNN^2/4pi = 0.95, with f_rhoNN/g_rhoNN = 6.1.
- The oscillator parameters for the single-hole wave functions are 1.72 fm for 16O, 2.04 fm for 40Ca, and 2.20 fm for 100Sn.
- The Bethe-Goldstone equation was solved with starting energy Omega = -10 MeV and Fermi momentum k_F = 1.4 fm^-1.
- The nuclear matter parameterization of Nieves et al. (1993) was evaluated at density rho = 0.75 rho_0, the average density of nucleons in 100Sn.
- The local approximation of the self-energy was extracted at a typical radius of r = 1.5 fm for comparison with the parameterization.

## Context

This work extends the nuclear-matter calculations of Oset and Salcedo (1987) and the subsequent parameterization by Nieves et al. (1993) to finite nuclei, using a method developed by Bonatsos and Muther (1989) and Hjorth-Jensen et al. (1993) that handles both bound and continuum single-particle states. The results support the use of the nuclear-matter parameterization for heavier nuclei like 100Sn but indicate that for lighter nuclei such as 16O, and for energies below 200 MeV, a surface-enhanced contribution to the Delta self-energy may be important for accurate modeling of pion-nucleus scattering and absorption processes.
