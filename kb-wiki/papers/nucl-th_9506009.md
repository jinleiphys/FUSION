# Phasespace Correlations of Antideuterons in Heavy Ion Collisions

> In the framework of the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics approach ({\small RQMD}) we investigate antideuteron ($\overline{d}$) observables in Au+Au collisions at 10.7~AGeV. The impact parameter dependence of the formation ratios $\overline{d}/\overline{p}^2$ and ${d}/{p}^2$ is calculated. In central collisions, the antideuteron formation ratio is predicted to be two orders of magnitude lower than the deuteron formation ratio. The $\overline{d}$ yield in central Au+Au collisions is one order of magnitude lower than in Si+Al collisions. In semicentral collisions different configuration space distributions of $\overline{p}$'s and $\overline{d}$'s lead to a large ``squeeze--out'' effect for antideuterons, which is not predicted for the $\overline{p}$'s.

# Phasespace Correlations of Antideuterons in Heavy Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The antideuteron formation ratio in central Au+Au collisions at 10.7 AGeV is predicted to be two orders of magnitude lower than the deuteron formation ratio, and the absolute antideuteron yield actually decreases from approximately 10^-7 per Si+Al event to approximately 10^-8 per central Au+Au event. This strong suppression arises from antibaryon annihilation in the baryon-rich environment, which distorts the configuration space distribution of antinucleons and is not captured by simple momentum-space coalescence models.

## Method
The authors used the relativistic quantum molecular dynamics (RQMD 1.07 cascade mode) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at 10.7 AGeV, propagating all hadrons on classical trajectories with Hamilton constraint dynamics including secondary interactions and antiproton annihilation via the free p-anti-p cross section. Antideuteron formation was calculated after strong freeze-out by projecting the antinucleon pair phase space onto the antideuteron wave function using the Wigner-function method with a Hulthén parametrization, employing event mixing to improve statistics.

## Key numbers
- Antideuteron formation ratio in central Au+Au collisions is two orders of magnitude lower than the deuteron formation ratio.
- Absolute antideuteron yield decreases from approximately 10^-7 per Si+Al event to approximately 10^-8 per central Au+Au event.
- Up to 95% of initially produced antinucleons are reabsorbed in central collisions.
- The momentum coalescence model with a cutoff parameter of Delta p = 120 MeV shows little sensitivity to impact parameter, in contrast to the full calculation.
- The spatial antiproton density shows less than 30% decrease along the football-shaped region in the x-y plane.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of antideuteron formation in Si+Au collisions at the AGS (M. Bleicher 1993, A. Jahns 1994) by examining the larger Au+Au system and the impact parameter dependence of formation ratios. The results contradict simple momentum-space coalescence predictions and instead show that antibaryon annihilation in the baryon-rich environment produces a characteristic "squeeze-out" effect for antideuterons perpendicular to the reaction plane, which is not predicted for antiprotons. This enables experimental tests of annihilation assumptions and provides a probe of the spatial structure of the dense fireball that is inaccessible from antiproton or proton data alone.
