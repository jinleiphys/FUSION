# Elastic pion-nucleon scattering in chiral perturbation theory: Explicit $Δ$(1232) degrees of freedom

> For the first time, elastic pion-nucleon scattering is analyzed in the framework of chiral perturbation theory up to fourth order with explicit $Δ$ degrees of freedom. The analysis is performed within the heavy-baryon expansion as well as in a covariant approach based on an extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme. The renormalization of low-energy constants in both chiral approaches is discussed in detail and the explicit expressions to cancel both power-counting breaking as well as decoupling breaking terms are given. The low-energy constants from the $2π\bar{N}N$ interaction as well as additional constants from the $Δ$ sector are reliably constrained by fits to experimental data. The traditional $K$-matrix unitarization is employed in the near threshold region, whereas a complex mass approach is used to extend the applicability of the theory up to the $Δ$ pole region. Additionally, we estimate a theoretical error based on the truncation of the chiral series as employed in recent analyses of nuclear forces as well as pion-nucleon scattering. The obtained results provide a clear evidence that the explicit inclusion of $Δ$ degrees of freedom is fundamental to describe pion-nucleon physics at threshold. The resulting predictions for the subthreshold and threshold parameters as well as phase shifts are in excellent agreement with the ones determined by the recent Roy-Steiner analysis of pion-nucleon scattering.

## Key claim

For the first time, elastic pion-nucleon scattering is analyzed in chiral perturbation theory up to fourth order (order ε⁴) with explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom, using both heavy-baryon and covariant approaches. The analysis provides clear evidence that explicit Δ degrees of freedom are fundamental to describe pion-nucleon physics at threshold, but the low-energy constants extracted from fits to phase shifts turn out to be strongly correlated, making reliable determination problematic.

## Method

The pion-nucleon scattering amplitude was calculated in the small-scale expansion (counting the Δ-nucleon mass splitting as order ε) up to full one-loop order (ε⁴) using both heavy-baryon expansion and a covariant extended on-mass-shell renormalization scheme. The renormalization procedure absorbed power-counting breaking terms and decoupling breaking terms into low-energy constants, and the amplitudes were unitarized via K-matrix unitarization in the near-threshold region and a complex mass approach for the Δ resonance to extend applicability to the Δ pole region. Low-energy constants from the 2πNN interaction and the Δ sector were constrained by fits to experimental scattering data, with theoretical truncation errors estimated following the approach of Epelbaum (2014).

## Key numbers

- The expansion parameter ε is defined as {q/Λ_b, M_π/Λ_b, Δ/Λ_b} where Λ_b ∈ {Λ_χ, 4πF, m}
- The Δ mass is parametrized as m_Δ = m_Δ^Re − iΓ/2, where Γ ∼ O(ε³) receives its leading contribution at loop-level
- The K-matrix unitarization condition tan δ ≃ δ is a good approximation for |δ| ≪ π/6
- The complex mass approach uses the renormalization conditions Re Σ(m_Δ^Re) = 0, Im Σ(m_Δ^Re) = −Γ/2, and Σ′(m_Δ^Re) = 0

## Context

This work extends the Δ-less analysis of Siemens (2016) to include explicit Δ(1232) degrees of freedom at full one-loop order, building on earlier calculations at order ε³ by Fettes (2000) in the heavy-baryon approach and Yao (2016) in the covariant approach. The results enable reliable extraction of pion-nucleon low-energy constants that enter two-pion exchange contributions to two- and three-nucleon forces in chiral effective field theory, and the predictions for subthreshold and threshold parameters as well as phase shifts show excellent agreement with the Roy-Steiner analysis of Hoferichter (2015).
