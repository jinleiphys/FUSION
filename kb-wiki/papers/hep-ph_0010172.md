# Equation of state of resonance-rich matter in the central cell in heavy-ion collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=200 AGeV

> The equilibration of hot and dense nuclear matter produced in the central cell of central Au+Au collisions at RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ AGeV) energies is studied within a microscopic transport model. The pressure in the cell becomes isotropic at $t\approx 5$ fm/$c$ after beginning of the collision. Within the next 15 fm/$c$ the expansion of matter in the cell proceeds almost isentropically with the entropy per baryon ratio $S/A \approx 150$, and the equation of state in the $(P,ε)$ plane has a very simple form, $P=0.15ε$. Comparison with the statistical model of an ideal hadron gas indicates that the time $t \approx 20$ fm/c may be too short to reach the fully equilibrated state. Particularly, the creation of long-lived resonance-rich matter in the cell decelerates the relaxation to chemical equilibrium. This resonance-abundant state can be detected experimentally after the thermal freeze-out of particles.

## Key claim

The hot hadron-string matter produced in the central cell of central Au+Au collisions at RHIC reaches kinetic equilibrium by about 5 fm/c and expands nearly isentropically with entropy per baryon S/A ≈ 150, but full thermal and chemical equilibrium is not achieved even by the freeze-out time of about 20 fm/c due to the creation of long-lived resonance-rich matter that decelerates relaxation.

## Method

The authors used the Ultra-relativistic Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) transport model to simulate central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV and analyzed a cubic central cell of volume V = 125 fm³. They extracted energy density, baryon density, and strangeness density from the microscopic calculations and used these as input to a statistical model (SM) of an ideal hadron gas to determine temperature T, baryon chemical potential μB, and strangeness chemical potential μS, then compared the microscopic particle yields and energy spectra to the SM predictions.

## Key numbers

- Pressure becomes isotropic at t ≈ 5 fm/c after beginning of the collision
- Entropy per baryon ratio S/A ≈ 150 during the quasi-equilibrium expansion stage (range 135-180 from t=5 to t=18 fm/c)
- Equation of state in the (P,ε) plane: P = 0.15 ε at RHIC (same as at SPS, while at AGS it was P = 0.12 ε)
- Energy density in the cell decreases from 2330 MeV/fm³ at t=5 fm/c to 145 MeV/fm³ at t=18 fm/c
- Temperature from SM fit ranges from T = 201 MeV at t=5 fm/c to T = 140 MeV at t=18 fm/c
- Baryon chemical potential remains small, ranging from μB = 39.7 MeV to 43.4 MeV over the same interval
- Pion spectrum at t=5 fm/c shows two-component structure with T_low ≈ 114 MeV and T_high ≈ 158 MeV; at t=10 fm/c: T_low ≈ 100 MeV and T_high ≈ 150 MeV, while SM temperature at t=10 fm/c is 171 MeV
- Baryon resonances constitute approximately 70% of all baryons in the cell from t=5 to t=19 fm/c at RHIC
- More than 80% of baryon non-strange resonances at RHIC are Δ(1232) resonances

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Bravina et al. (1998, 1999) that examined equilibration in the central cell at AGS (10.7 AGeV) and SPS (160 AGeV) energies using the same UrQMD model and cell volume. The results contradict the expectation that resonance abundance should diminish with increasing collision energy; instead, baryon resonances remain at about 70% of all baryons at RHIC, higher than at SPS (decreasing from 70% to 35%) or AGS (60% to 25%). The predicted entropy per baryon ratio of 150-170 at RHIC can be compared to upcoming experimental data, and the finding that the system freezes before reaching full equilibrium complicates the standard thermal model extraction of chemical and thermal freeze-out parameters.
