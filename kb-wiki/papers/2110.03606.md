# Update on (2+1+1)-flavor QCD equation of state

> We report on preliminary results from the calculations of the QCD equation of state for 2+1+1 flavors using HISQ action. The calculations are performed on lattices with temporal extents $N_τ=6,~8,~10$, and $12$ and aspect ratio $N_σ/N_τ=4$. We find that there is a significant contribution to the pressure from charm quarks at temperatures $T> 300$ MeV.

# Update on (2+1+1)-flavor QCD equation of state

## Key claim
The authors report preliminary results showing a significant contribution from charm quarks to the pressure at temperatures above 300 MeV, but note that substantial increases in statistics on finer lattices (Nτ=10, 12) are needed before a robust continuum extrapolation can be accomplished.

## Method
The authors performed lattice QCD calculations of the trace anomaly using the integral method with the HISQ action for 2+1+1 flavors on Nτ=6, 8, 10, and 12 lattices with aspect ratio Nσ/Nτ=4. They subtracted vacuum contributions at zero temperature, determined lattice beta functions from fits to the r1 scale and quark masses using Allton-type ansatze, and obtained the pressure by integrating the trace anomaly from a low reference temperature using the hadron resonance gas model.

## Key numbers
- Temperature range covered: T ∈ [149, 967] MeV for Nτ=6 and T ∈ [136, 725] MeV for Nτ=8
- Pion mass along the line of constant physics: mπ ≈ 300 MeV in the continuum limit
- Charm quark mass (MS scheme, Nf=4): mc(mc, Nf=4) = 1.2735(35) GeV
- Crossover temperature reference: Tpc = 156.5(1.5) MeV
- Lattice spacing scale: r1 ≈ 0.3106 fm
- Finest lattice spacing reached: 0.034 fm, corresponding to 967 MeV for Nτ=6
- Statistics: most Nτ=6 ensembles reached 50 thousand molecular dynamics time units; most Nτ=8 ensembles reached 100 thousand time units

## Context
This work extends previous (2+1)-flavor QCD equation of state calculations by HotQCD (2014, 2017) and Borsanyi et al. (2013) to include dynamical charm quarks, addressing the need for an independent cross-check of the earlier (2+1+1)-flavor results by Borsanyi (2016). The inclusion of charm is motivated by future heavy-ion collision experiments at higher energies and early universe physics. The results enable future continuum extrapolations once finer lattice ensembles achieve sufficient statistics.
