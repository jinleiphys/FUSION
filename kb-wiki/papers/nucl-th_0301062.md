# The Equation of State for Cold and Dense Strongly Interacting Matter

> We discuss recent results for the equation of state for cold and dense strongly interacting matter. We consider the extreme cases of very high densities, where weak-coupling approaches may in principle give reasonable results, and very low densities, where we use the framework of heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory. We also speculate on the nature of the chiral transition and present possible astrophysical implications.

## Key claim

The paper discusses recent results for the equation of state of cold and dense strongly interacting matter, but the authors explicitly state that the most phenomenologically relevant region for compact stars lies between the high-density and low-density extremes, where both perturbative QCD and heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory fail completely.

## Method

The authors compare several approaches for the high-density regime: finite-density perturbative QCD, hard-thermal-loop (HDL) perturbative QCD, and quasiparticle model descriptions, all evaluated for massless quarks and gluons at zero temperature and nonzero quark chemical potential. For the low-density regime, they perform one-loop calculations within heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory to compute nucleon and pion self-energy corrections due to the medium, working to lowest order in nucleon density and using a non-relativistic approximation. They also use results from Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (1998) for pure neutron matter up to approximately twice nuclear saturation density.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of nuclear matter: n0 ~ 0.16 baryons/fm^3
- Energy per baryon for pure neutron matter up to ~2 n0: (E/A) - mN ≈ 15 MeV × (n/n0)
- Hadron pressure relative to free gas pressure: p_hadron/p_free ≈ 0.04 × (n/n0)^2
- Pion decay constant at finite density from chiral perturbation theory: fπ(pf)/fπ = 1 - (15/939) × (n/n0) + ...
- Input parameters from Particle Data Group: mN = 939 MeV, mπ = 135 MeV, fπ = 130 MeV, gπNN = 13.1 (gA = 1.81)
- Perturbative QCD approaches agree reasonably well for μ >> 1 GeV and point in the same direction even for μ ~ 1 GeV and smaller
- Matching between quark matter and hadron equations of state occurs somewhere between μ ≈ 313 MeV and μ ≈ 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of the QCD equation of state to both very high densities (using perturbative QCD techniques from Larry, Baluni, and Fraga, Pisarski and Schaffner-Bielich (2001)) and very low densities (using heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory). The authors compare their results with lattice QCD simulations at nonzero chemical potential by Fodor and Katz (2002) and Allton et al. (2002), and with the nuclear matter equation of state from Akmal, Pandharipande and Ravenhall (1998). The paper enables future work on hybrid neutron star phenomenology, particularly regarding the mass-radius diagram and the interpretation of the isolated neutron star candidate RX J1856.5-3754, and identifies the need to describe the intermediate density regime where neither perturbative nor chiral perturbation theory approaches work.
