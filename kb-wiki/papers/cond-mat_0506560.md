# Enhancement of pairing due to the presence of resonant cavities

> A correlated fermion system is considered surrounding a finite cavity with virtual levels. The pairing properties are calculated and the influence of the cavity is demonstrated. To this end the Gell-Mann and Goldberger formula is generalized to many-body systems. We find a possible enhancement of pairing temperature if the Fermi momentum times the cavity radius fulfills a certain resonance condition which suggests an experimental realization.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the critical temperature for fermion pairing can be enhanced by placing the correlated fermion system around a finite resonant cavity, provided the Fermi momentum times the cavity radius satisfies a specific resonance condition; this enhancement is conditional on the cavity amplitude factor \(A_{k_f R}\) having minima at certain values of \(k_f R\).

## Method

The authors generalize the Gell-Mann and Goldberger scattering formula to many-body systems using Green's functions in the ladder approximation, treating the cavity as an external potential and the fermion-fermion interaction as a separable s-wave potential. They solve the resulting \(T\)-matrix equation for the two-particle channel and apply the Thouless criterion to find the critical temperature for pairing, using an opaque-wall cavity model with a delta-shell potential.

## Key numbers

* The gap \(\Delta\) versus density is calculated at temperature \(T = 0.05 \beta^2 / 2m\) for a scattering length \(a_0 = -21.5 \hbar / \beta\), free binding energy \(E_b^0 = 0.005 \beta^2 / 2m\), and cavity radius \(R = 4 \hbar / \beta\).
* The critical temperature \(T_c\) is modified by the factor \(A_{k_f R}^4\) in the exponent: \(T_c = 1.13 \omega_c \exp(-2 / (N V_0 A_{k_f R}^4))\).
* Minima of the cavity amplitude \(A_x\) occur at \(2 x_n = n \pi - \arctan(2 x_n / \Omega)\), with maximum enhancement factors: for \(x_n = n \pi / 2 < \Omega\), \((1/A_x^4)_{\text{max}} \approx (2 \Omega / n \pi)^4\); for \(x_n = (2n-1) \pi / 4 > \Omega\), \((1/A_x^4)_{\text{max}} \approx 1 + 8 \Omega / ((2n-1)\pi)\).
* For a Fermi energy of about 1 eV and the first resonance condition \(k_f R = \pi \hbar / 2\), the required cavity radius is about 200 nm.

## Context

This work extends the Gell-Mann and Goldberger formula (1953) to many-body systems and builds on prior treatments of pairing in finite systems, such as the related problem of crystal fields by Fulde (1979) and pair-breaking mechanisms by Fulde and Zwicknagl (1989). It also connects to studies of Feshbach resonances in ultracold atomic gases, e.g., the observation of BCS superfluid states in \(^6\)Li and \(^{40}\)K clouds by Jochim et al. (2003) and Greiner et al. (2003), and superfluidity in resonantly interacting Fermi gases by Kinast et al. (2004). The results suggest an experimental realization using a resonant cavity to enhance the critical temperature, potentially enabling new studies of superconductivity in nanostructures.
