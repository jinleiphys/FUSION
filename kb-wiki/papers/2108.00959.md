# QED Mesons, the QED Neutron, and the Dark Matter

> Schwinger's boson solution for massless fermions in QED in 1+1D has been applied and generalized to quarks interacting in QED and QCD interactions, leading to stable and confined open-string QED and QCD boson excitations of the quark-QCD-QED system in 1+1D. Just as the open-string QCD excitations in 1+1D can be the idealization of QCD mesons with a flux tube in 3+1D, so the open-string QED excitations in 1+1D may likewise be the idealization of QED mesons with masses in the tens of MeV region, corresponding possibly to the anomalous X17 and E38 particles observed recently. A further search for bound states of quarks interacting in the QED interaction alone leads to the examination on the stability of the QED neutron, consisting of two $d$ quarks and one $u$ quark. Theoretically, the QED neutron has been found to be stable and estimated to have a mass of 44.5 MeV, whereas the analogous QED proton is unstable, leading to a long-lived QED neutron that may be a good candidate for the dark matter.

# QED Mesons, the QED Neutron, and the Dark Matter

## Key claim

The quark-QCD-QED system supports independent, stable collective QED excitations (QED mesons) in the color-singlet subspace, separate from QCD mesons in the color-octet subspace; the QED neutron (d-u-d) is theoretically stable at 44.5 MeV and may be a dark matter candidate, but only if the analogous QED proton is indeed unstable and provides no final state for weak decay.

## Method

The author generalizes Schwinger's exact 1+1D QED boson solution for massless fermions to the combined QED+QCD system by expanding quark currents and gauge fields into color-singlet (U(1)) and color-octet (SU(3)) subgroups, which decouple and yield independent Klein-Gordon equations with masses set by their respective coupling constants. For the QED neutron, a variational calculation is performed using three mass-shell constraint equations with an effective two-body QED interaction extracted from Schwinger's solution, employing a Gaussian trial wave function with the center-of-mass condition imposed. The QED proton stability is examined by the same variational method over a large range of parameters.

## Key numbers

- QED neutron mass minimum: 44.5 MeV, at variational parameters sigma_1 = 2.40 and sigma_2 = 1.05 (corresponding to spatial widths 19.9 fm and 8.71 fm respectively)
- X17 candidate QED meson: semi-empirical mass 17.9 ± 1.5 MeV, compared to experimental 16.94 ± 0.24 MeV
- E38 candidate QED meson: semi-empirical mass 36.4 ± 3.8 MeV, compared to experimental 37.38 ± 0.71 MeV
- QCD coupling constant used: alpha_QCD = 0.68 ± 0.08; flux tube radius R_T = 0.40 ± 0.04 fm
- QED coupling constant: alpha_QED = 1/137

## Context

This work extends the author's prior bosonization approach to QCD+QED in 1+1D (Wong 2010, 2020) and applies it to interpret the anomalous X17 particle reported by Krasznahorkay et al. (2021) and the E38 particle reported by Abraamyan et al. (2019) as QED mesons. It also builds on Schwinger's original solution (Schwinger 1962, 1963) and Gribov's demonstration of massless fermion confinement in QED (Gribov 1982, 1999). The predicted stable QED neutron, if confirmed, would provide a new dark matter candidate whose production could occur during the deconfinement-to-confinement phase transition in the early universe, in neutron-star mergers as discussed by Bauswein et al. (2019), or in neutron star cores as suggested by Annala et al. (2020).
