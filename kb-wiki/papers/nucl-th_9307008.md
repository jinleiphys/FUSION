# Pion-nucleus reactions in a microscopic transport model

> We analyse pion-nucleus reactions in a microscopic transport model of the BUU type, which propagates nucleons, pions, deltas and N(1440)-resonances explicitly in space and time. In particular we examine pion absorption and inelastic scattering cross sections for pion kinetic energies T(pi) =85-315MeV and various target masses. In general, the mass-dependence of the experimental data is well described for energies up to the delta-resonance (\approx 160 MeV) while the absorption cross sections are somewhat overestimated for the higher energies. In addition we study the possible dynamical effects of delta- and pion-potentials in the medium on various observables as well as alternative models for the in-medium delta-width.

## Key claim

The BUU transport model reproduces the mass-dependence of experimental pion absorption and inelastic scattering cross sections well for pion kinetic energies up to the delta resonance region (≈160 MeV), but systematically overestimates absorption cross sections at higher energies (Tπ = 315 MeV), especially for heavier nuclei.

## Method

The authors solve coupled Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport equations for nucleons, pions, Δ resonances, and N(1440) resonances using a test-particle method, propagating all particles explicitly in space and time. They implement resonance production and decay via Breit-Wigner forms, ΔN ↔ NN reactions using detailed balance with several prescriptions (including VerWest-Arndt parametrization and OBE model results), and include direct pion production/absorption channels via a one-boson-exchange model. Inclusive cross sections are obtained by impact-parameter integration over simulated pion-nucleus collisions.

## Key numbers

- Pion kinetic energy range studied: Tπ = 85–315 MeV
- Δ resonance peak mass: MΔ = 1232 MeV; width Γr = 110 MeV
- Maximum π⁺p → Δ⁺⁺ cross section: σ_max = 200 mb; maximum π⁻p cross section: 70 mb
- N(1440) resonance width: Γ = 200 MeV; maximum πN → N(1440) cross section: 30 mb
- Nucleon mean-field parameters: t₀ = −1177.8 MeV/fm³, t₃ = 1845.5 MeV/fm⁵, τ = 5/3, giving binding energy −16 MeV/nucleon at ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³ and incompressibility K = 308.4 MeV
- Naive detailed balance (eq. DB) underestimates absorption cross section by about 50% compared to data at Tπ = 165 MeV
- N(1440) contributes only about 10% difference to absorption at Tπ ≈ 300 MeV
- Direct pion production/absorption gives 10% enhancement for absorption on ¹²C at all energies

## Context

This work extends BUU transport model studies previously applied to heavy-ion collisions (Wol93, wol90, wol92) to explicit pion-nucleus reactions, testing the pion and resonance dynamics in a simpler environment. It compares with earlier INC calculations (cug88, fra82) that underestimated absorption by a factor of 2, and with the cascade model of Salcedo et al. (1988) which also overestimates absorption at higher energies for heavier nuclei. The results enable systematic testing of in-medium Δ and pion potentials and alternative Δ-width models, and clarify that total inclusive pion absorption in heavy nuclei is insensitive to true many-body effects because subsequent two-body steps with off-shell deltas can simulate them.
