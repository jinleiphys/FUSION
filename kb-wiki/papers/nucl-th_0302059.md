# Precise estimation of shell model energy by second order extrapolation method

> A second order extrapolation method is presented for shell model calculations, where shell model energies of truncated spaces are well described as a function of energy variance by quadratic curves and exact shell model energies can be obtained by the extrapolation. This new extrapolation can give more precise energy than those of first order extrapolation method. It is also clarified that first order extrapolation gives a lower limit of shell model energy. In addition to the energy, we derive the second order extrapolation formula for expectation values of other observables.

# Precise estimation of shell model energy by second order extrapolation method

## Key claim

The authors show that shell model energies computed in truncated Hilbert spaces follow a quadratic curve when plotted against energy variance, and that extrapolating this curve to zero variance yields exact energies more accurately than the previously used linear (first order) extrapolation. The first order extrapolation systematically underestimates the true energy, giving a lower limit, while the second order correction accounts for both the decreasing norm of the rest component and its changing structure.

## Method

The authors derive a second order extrapolation formula by expanding the energy difference δE between a truncated-space eigenvalue and the exact eigenvalue in powers of the modified energy variance ΔE. They fit quadratic curves to shell model energies computed in a series of truncated spaces (defined by limiting the number of nucleons allowed in higher-lying orbits) and extrapolate to ΔE = 0. The method is tested on exactly solvable nuclei (⁴⁸Cr, ⁴⁹Cr) and then applied to the large-scale case of ⁶⁴Zn, where exact results are available from prior work. A similar second order formula is derived for expectation values of other observables.

## Key numbers

- For ⁴⁸Cr ground state: extrapolated energies are -32.91 MeV (truncation scheme I) and -32.97 MeV (scheme II); exact energy is -32.95 MeV.
- For ⁴⁹Cr ground state: extrapolated energies are -35.59 MeV (scheme I) and -35.60 MeV (scheme II); exact energy is -35.59 MeV.
- For ⁶⁴Zn ground state: extrapolated energies are -106.38 MeV (scheme I) and -106.34 MeV (scheme II); exact energy is -106.3 MeV.
- For ⁶⁴Zn B(E2) sum rule: extrapolated values are 1.17 × 10³ e²fm⁴ (scheme I) and 1.18 × 10³ e²fm⁴ (scheme II); exact value is 1157 e²fm⁴.
- For ⁶⁴Zn Gamow-Teller sum rule (unquenched): extrapolated values are 5.3 (scheme I) and 5.5 (scheme II); exact value is 5.54.
- For ⁴⁸Cr, the moment ratio A is fitted as A = 0.037 + 0.0018 ΔE (scheme I) and A = 0.036 + 0.0010 ΔE (scheme II).

## Context

This work extends the first order extrapolation method previously applied to nuclear shell model calculations by Mizusaki and Imada (2002) and to correlated electron systems by Imada and Kashima (2000) and Sorella (2001). The improvement to second order addresses the systematic underestimation of energies found in the linear method. The technique enables more precise ground state energies and expectation values for nuclei in the fp shell (e.g., ⁶⁴Zn with a 501-million-dimensional M-scheme space) that are beyond the reach of direct Lanczos diagonalization. The use of two independent truncation schemes that converge to the same extrapolated value provides a practical cross-check of reliability.
