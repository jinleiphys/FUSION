# Gamma-Z box contributions to parity violating elastic e-p scattering

> Parity-violating (PV) elastic electron-proton scattering measures Q-weak for the proton, $Q_W^p$. To extract $Q_W^p$ from data, all radiative corrections must be well-known. Recently, disagreement on the gamma-Z box contribution to $Q_W^p$ has prompted the need for further analysis of this term. Here, we support one choice of a debated factor, go beyond the previously assumed equality of electromagnetic and gamma-Z structure functions, and find an analytic result for one of the gamma-Z box integrals. Our numerical evaluation of the gamma-Z box is in agreement within errors with previous reports, albeit somewhat larger in central value, and is within the uncertainty requirements of current experiments.

## Key claim

The gamma-Z box correction to the proton's weak charge is known well enough for current experiments (such as Q-weak at JLab), but more accurate determinations will be needed within several years for future higher-precision measurements. The authors support the factor "2" used by Sibirtsev et al. (2010) in a debated formula, and find that their own numerical evaluation is slightly larger in central value but compatible within uncertainty limits.

## Method

The authors calculate the real part of the gamma-Z box diagram at zero momentum transfer using a dispersive relation that connects the box evaluation to inelastic structure functions. They go beyond the previously assumed equality of electromagnetic and gamma-Z structure functions by modifying the Christy-Bosted fits (2007) in the resonance region (W < 2.5 GeV) using quark charge arguments, the Capella et al. (1994) fits in the high-energy low-Q² region, and CTEQ parton distributions (CT10.00, 2010) in the scaling region. They also derive an analytic result for one of the triple integrals, simplifying the numerical evaluation.

## Key numbers

- At the JLab Q-weak experiment energy (E_lab = 1.165 GeV): Re(□_γZ^V) = 0.0057 ± 0.0009
- This agrees within uncertainty with the Sibirtsev et al. (2010) result of 0.0047^{+0.0011}_{-0.0004}
- At 180 MeV (for a proposed MAMI experiment): Re(□_γZ^V) = 0.00125 ± 0.00018
- The average Q² within the integrals for incoming energies in the JLab range is about 0.4 GeV²
- The resonant contribution to F_{1,2}^{γZ} is about 9% smaller than to F_{1,2}^{γγ}
- The high Q² contribution from CTEQ at JLab energy gives 0.00019 of the total
- The longitudinal part of the structure functions contributes about 0.0007 to the result at JLab energy
- The Q-weak experiment aims to measure Q_W^p to about 4% combined statistical and systematic error, requiring absolute accuracy of about 0.0028
- The 180 MeV experiment goal of a part in a thousand measurement requires uncertainty of about 0.00006

## Context

This work addresses a disagreement between Gorchtein and Horowitz (2008) and Sibirtsev et al. (2010) regarding the gamma-Z box contribution to the proton weak charge Q_W^p. The authors corroborate the factor "2" used by Sibirtsev et al. and improve upon the approximation F_{1,2}^{γZ} = F_{1,2}^{γγ} used in both prior works. The result enables the extraction of Q_W^p from parity-violating elastic electron-proton scattering experiments, with the current uncertainty sufficient for the JLab Q-weak experiment but requiring further improvement for planned higher-precision measurements at MAMI.
