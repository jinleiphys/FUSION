# Vacuum energy in the effective field theory of general relativity II: Inclusion of fermions and a comment on the QCD contribution

> Recently in the framework of a two-loop order calculation for an effective field theory of scalar and vector fields interacting with the metric field we have shown that for the cosmological constant term which is fixed by the condition of vanishing vacuum energy the graviton remains massless and there exists a self-consistent effective field theory of general relativity defined on a flat Minkowski background. In the current paper we extend the two-loop analysis for an effective field theory of fermions interacting with the gravitational field and obtain an analogous result. We also address the issues of fine tuning of the strong interaction contribution to the vacuum energy and the compatibility of chiral symmetry in the light quark sector with the consistency of the effective field theory of general relativity in a flat Minkowski background.

## Key claim

By demanding that the vacuum expectation value of the full four-momentum of matter and gravitational fields vanishes, the cosmological constant term is uniquely fixed as a function of all other parameters of the theory, which also ensures the graviton remains massless; this condition resolves the apparent fine-tuning problem of the QCD contribution to the vacuum energy without contradicting any physically relevant symmetries of QCD.

## Method

The authors extended a previous two-loop order analysis of an effective field theory of scalar and vector fields interacting with the metric field to include fermions, using the Landau-Lifshitz definition of the energy-momentum pseudotensor and full four-momentum. They calculated one-loop and two-loop Feynman diagrams contributing to the vacuum expectation value of the energy-momentum pseudotensor times (-g) and to the vacuum expectation value of the graviton field, applying dimensional regularization and the program FeynCalc. They then examined the QCD contribution to the vacuum energy by constructing a low-energy effective field theory of pions interacting with gravity and analyzing the compatibility of chiral symmetry with the consistency condition of the effective field theory of general relativity.

## Context

This work extends the two-loop analysis of Gegelia (2019) from scalar and vector fields to fermions, confirming the consistency conditions first obtained by Burns (2014) for a self-consistent effective field theory of general relativity on a flat Minkowski background. The authors address the fine-tuning problem raised by Donoghue (2016) regarding the QCD contribution to the vacuum energy, showing that the cosmological constant, as a fixed function of the quark masses, exactly cancels the QCD contribution analytically rather than requiring numerical fine-tuning. This enables a self-consistent perturbative effective field theory of gravitation coupled to the Standard Model fields, with the cosmological constant determined by the condition of vanishing vacuum energy.
