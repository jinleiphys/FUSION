# Baryons in the plasma: in-medium effects and parity doubling

> We investigate the fate of baryons made out of u, d and s quarks in the hadronic gas and the quark-gluon plasma, using nonperturbative lattice simulations, employing the FASTSUM anisotropic Nf=2+1 ensembles. In the confined phase a strong temperature dependence is seen in the masses of the negative-parity groundstates, while the positive-parity groundstate masses are approximately temperature independent, within the error. At high temperature parity doubling emerges. A noticeable effect of the heavier s quark is seen. We give a simple description of the medium-dependent masses for the negative-parity states and speculate on the relevance for heavy-ion phenomenology via the hadron resonance gas.

# Baryons in the plasma: in-medium effects and parity doubling

## Key claim
Parity doubling emerges near the deconfinement transition primarily through a reduction of negative-parity baryon masses with temperature, while positive-parity groundstate masses remain approximately constant; this behavior is similar across all octet and decuplet channels studied, but the effect of the heavier strange quark is noticeable in that baryons with larger strangeness still show a deviation from full parity doubling at the highest temperatures studied.

## Method
The authors used nonperturbative lattice QCD simulations on the FASTSUM anisotropic Nf=2+1 ensembles with a fixed-scale approach, varying temperature by changing the number of time slices Nτ from 128 down to 16, corresponding to T/Tc from 0.24 to 1.90. They computed all octet (spin 1/2) and decuplet (spin 3/2) baryon correlators for both positive and negative parity, extracting groundstate masses in the confined phase via exponential fits, and constructed a ratio R of correlators as a quasi-order parameter for parity doubling in the deconfined phase.

## Key numbers
- Crossover temperature Tc = 185(4) MeV, determined via the inflection point of the renormalised Polyakov loop
- Pion mass mπ = 384(4) MeV (light quarks not at physical masses)
- Spatial lattice spacing as = 0.1227(8) fm with as/aτ = 3.5
- Positive-parity nucleon mass in vacuum m+ = 939 MeV; negative-parity nucleon mass m- = 1535 MeV
- Mass splittings at low temperature are around 500-600 MeV across channels
- Fit parameter γ ranges from 0.22 to 0.35 across the 8 channels studied
- Ratio m-(Tc)/m+(0) ranges from 0.85 to 1.1 across channels
- At T/Tc = 1.09, no clearly identifiable boundstates appear to be present

## Context
This work extends the few existing lattice studies of baryonic thermal screening (DeTar 1987, Pushkina 2004) and temporal masses (Datta 2012) by systematically studying all octet and decuplet baryons across the deconfinement transition. The results can be used to constrain effective parity-doublet models (Detar 1988) and recent work along those lines (Motohiro 2015, Mukherjee 2017, Sasaki 2017). The in-medium modification of the hadron resonance gas using temperature-dependent negative-parity masses shows improvement over the ideal HRG in the |S|=1,2,3 sectors when compared to lattice data from Alba (2017), suggesting relevance for heavy-ion phenomenology.
