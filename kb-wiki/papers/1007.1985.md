# Holographic chiral magnetic spiral

> We study the ground state of baryonic/axial matter at zero temperature chiral-symmetry broken phase under a large magnetic field, in the framework of holographic QCD by Sakai-Sugimoto. Our study is motivated by a recent proposal of chiral magnetic spiral phase that has been argued to be favored against previously studied phase of homogeneous distribution of axial/baryonic currents in terms of meson super-currents dictated by triangle anomalies in QCD. Our results provide an existence proof of chiral magnetic spiral in strong coupling regime via holography, at least for large axial chemical potentials, whereas we don't find the phenomenon in the case of purely baryonic chemical potential.

## Key claim

The authors provide an existence proof of the chiral magnetic spiral phase in the strong coupling regime via holography, at least for large axial chemical potentials, whereas they do not find the phenomenon in the case of purely baryonic chemical potential.

## Method

The authors study linearized stability of the homogeneous chiral spiral solution in the Sakai-Sugimoto holographic QCD model against small perturbations of transverse currents with finite momentum along the magnetic field direction. They work with a two-derivative truncation of the Dirac-Born-Infeld action for a single flavor, solving the linearized equations of motion for helicity ±1 transverse modes to identify complex frequencies that indicate instability toward chiral magnetic spiral formation.

## Key numbers

- The minimum eigenvalue of the Kaluza-Klein reduction operator for massive vector mesons is λ²_min = 0.669 in units of M_KK², corresponding to the rho meson mass square.
- The Chern-Simons coupling strength is given by the parameter Ṽ = N_c B / (8π² κ M_KK²), where κ = (π/4)(f_π/M_KK)².
- The phase boundary for instability is determined by the condition -(N_c/(16π²κ))²(C_A² - C_B²) + λ²_eff M_KK² < 0, where C_A and C_B are integration constants related to axial and baryonic chemical potentials respectively.

## Context

This work extends the holographic studies of magnetized matter in the Sakai-Sugimoto model by Thompson (2008) and Bergman (2008), which constructed homogeneous chiral spiral solutions with baryonic chemical potential. The paper tests the proposal by Basar, Dunne, and Kharzeev (2010) that chiral magnetic spirals should form in QCD with magnetic field, providing a strong-coupling check of their weak-coupling argument based on effective 1+1 dimensional reduction. The results enable future construction of the full nonlinear chiral magnetic spiral ground state and its phase diagram in the space of chemical potentials and magnetic field.
