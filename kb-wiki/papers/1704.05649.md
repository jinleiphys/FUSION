# Toroidal, compressive, and $E1$ properties of low-energy dipole modes in $^{10}$Be

> We studied dipole excitations in $^{10}$Be based on an extended version of the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics, which can describe 1p-1h excitations and large amplitude cluster modes. Toroidal and compressive dipole operators are found to be good proves to separate the low-energy and high-energy parts of the isoscalar dipole excitations, respectively. Two low-energy $1^-$ states, the toroidal dominant $1^-_1$ state at $E\sim 8$ MeV and the $E1$ dominant $1^-_2$ state at $E\sim 16$ MeV, were obtained. By analysis of transition current densities, the $1^-_1$ states is understood as a toroidal dipole mode with exotic toroidal neutron flow caused by rotation of a deformed $^6\textrm{He}$ cluster, whereas the $1^-_2$ state is regarded as a neutron-skin oscillation mode, which are characterized by surface neutron flow with inner isoscalar flow caused by the surface neutron oscillation against the $2α$ core.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two distinct low-energy dipole modes coexist in \(^{10}\)Be: a toroidal-dominant \(1^-_1\) state at \(E\sim 8\) MeV and an \(E1\)-dominant \(1^-_2\) state at \(E\sim 16\) MeV, with the toroidal mode arising from rotation of a deformed \(^6\)He cluster and the \(E1\) mode from a neutron-skin oscillation against the \(2\alpha\) core. The hedge is that these assignments rely on the specific choice of toroidal and compressive dipole operators as probes to separate low-energy and high-energy isoscalar dipole excitations, and the analysis is performed within the framework of an extended antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) model that includes both 1p-1h excitations and large-amplitude cluster modes.

## Method

The authors used a shifted-basis AMD (sAMD) combined with an \(\alpha\)-cluster generator coordinate method (\(\alpha\)GCM) to describe the ground state and \(1^-\) states of \(^{10}\)Be. They superposed AMD wave functions including 1p-1h excitations (by shifting Gaussian centroids of single-particle wave functions) and large-amplitude cluster configurations (by varying the \(^6\)He–\(\alpha\) inter-cluster distance), then performed angular-momentum and parity projections and diagonalized the Hamiltonian. Transition current densities for the \(0^+_1 \to 1^-_k\) excitations were computed from the parity-projected intrinsic wave functions to analyze the toroidal, compressive, and \(E1\) properties.

## Key numbers

* The \(1^-_1\) state is found at excitation energy \(E \sim 8\) MeV and is toroidal dominant; the \(1^-_2\) state is at \(E \sim 16\) MeV and is \(E1\) dominant.
* The \(E1\) strength for the \(1^-_2\) state exhausts 10% of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule.
* The low-energy CD (compressive dipole) strengths exhaust 5% of the ISD sum rule (Harakeh 1981).
* The ground-state \(^6\)He–\(\alpha\) inter-cluster distance is \(D_0 = 2.8\) fm; the \(1^-_1\) intrinsic state has a distance \(D = 3.9\) fm, and the \(1^-_2\) state has maximum overlap with the \(\alpha\)GCM basis at \(D = 3.8\) fm.
* The IVGDR shows a narrow peak at \(E \sim 25\) MeV (longitudinal mode) and a broad bump around \(E \sim 40\) MeV (transverse mode).
* The effective interaction (MV1 force with G3RS spin-orbit) gives saturation density \(\rho_s = 0.192\) fm\(^{-3}\), saturation energy \(E_s = -17.9\) MeV, incompressibility \(K = 245\) MeV, symmetry energy \(S = 37.6\) MeV, and slope parameter \(L = 47.7\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of low-energy dipole excitations in neutron-rich Be isotopes by Kanada-En'yo (2015) using the same sAMD+\(\alpha\)GCM framework, now focusing on toroidal and compressive properties. It contrasts with mean-field and RPA calculations for heavier nuclei such as \(^{208}\)Pb and \(^{132}\)Sn (Kvasil 2011, Repko 2012, Nesterenko 2016), which also found toroidal dominance in low-energy isoscalar dipole strengths, but here the toroidal mode is linked to a cluster rotation rather than a purely collective vortical flow. The results enable future studies of toroidal modes in light neutron-rich nuclei and suggest that the toroidal and compressive dipole operators are effective probes to separate low-energy cluster modes from high-energy giant resonances.
