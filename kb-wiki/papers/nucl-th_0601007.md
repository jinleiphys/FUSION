# A Novel Nuclear Model for Double Beta Decay

> The possibility of applying the Quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (QTDA) to describe the nuclear double beta decay is explored. Several serious inconveniences found in the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), such as: i) the extreme sensitivity of the $2νββ$ decay amplitudes ${\cal M}_{2ν}$ on the residual interaction in the particle-particle channel, ii) the ambiguity in treating the intermediate states, and iii) the need for performing a second charge-conserving QRPA to describe the $ββ$-decays to the excited final states, are not present in the QTDA. Also, the QTDA allows for explicit evaluation of energy distributions of the double-charge-exchange transition strengths and of their sum rules, and can be straightforwardly applied to single- and double-closed shell nuclei. As an example, the $^{48}$Ca$\go^{48}$Ti decay is discussed within the $1fp$-shell in the particle-hole limit of the QTDA. The general [$(1,1)$-Padé-approximant-like] behavior of the $2νββ$-decay amplitude in the plain QRPA as well as within its different variations is briefly reviewed.

## Key claim
The Quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (QTDA) can describe nuclear double beta decay without the extreme sensitivity of the $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay amplitudes ${\cal M}_{2\nu}$ to the residual interaction in the particle-particle channel, the ambiguity in treating intermediate states, and the need for a second charge-conserving QRPA for decays to excited final states, all of which are serious inconveniences found in the Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA).

## Method
The author formulates the QTDA for double beta decay by constructing the nuclear matrix element ${\cal M}_{2\nu}$ using a sum over intermediate virtual states $\ket{\lambda^+_\alpha}$ with Fermi and Gamow-Teller operators, analogous to the QRPA formulation but without the backward-going ground-state correlations that cause the QRPA collapse. The method is applied to the $^{48}$Ca $\rightarrow$ $^{48}$Ti $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay within the $1fp$-shell in the particle-hole limit of the QTDA, and the general $(1,1)$-Padé-approximant-like behavior of the $2\nu\beta\beta$-decay amplitude in the plain QRPA and its variations is briefly reviewed.

## Key numbers
- The $2\nu\beta\beta$ decay half-lives are on the order of $T_{2\nu} \sim (10^{20}-10^{24})$ yr, while $0\nu\beta\beta$ half-lives are $T_{0\nu} \sim (10^{13}-10^{15})$ yr, based on phase-space factors.
- The Ikeda sum rule (ISR) for single-charge-exchange transitions gives $S_\lambda^\beta = N-Z$ for both Fermi and Gamow-Teller transitions.
- The double-charge-exchange sum rule for Fermi transitions is $S^{\beta\beta}_F = 2(N-Z)(N-Z-1)$.
- The double-charge-exchange sum rule for Gamow-Teller transitions is $S^{\beta\beta}_{GT} = 2(N-Z)(N-Z-1+2S^{\beta^+}_1) - \frac{2}{3}C$, where $C$ is a relatively small quantity.
- In the two-vacua QRPA (TVQRPA), the Ikeda sum rule gives $\widetilde{S}^{\beta}_{GT} \cong N-Z-2$, and when gap equations are solved for the intermediate nucleus, it gives exactly $N-Z-2$.
- In the renormalized QRPA (RQRPA), the Ikeda sum rule is violated by about $20-30\%$.

## Context
This work extends the author's prior collaboration with Dubravko Tadić on double beta decay (Bar96, Bar97, Bar98, Bar99) and addresses the well-known collapse problem in the QRPA first identified by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986) and Civitarese, Faessler, and Tomoda (1987). The QTDA avoids the need for the ad hoc averaging procedures (Methods M1 and M2) used in standard QRPA calculations, and enables explicit evaluation of double-charge-exchange transition strength distributions and sum rules, which the QRPA cannot provide. This approach can be straightforwardly applied to single- and double-closed shell nuclei, and the formalism can be extended to the $0\nu\beta\beta$ decay mode.
