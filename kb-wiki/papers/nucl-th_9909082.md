# Current Conservation in the Covariant Quark-Diquark Model of the Nucleon

> The description of baryons as fully relativistic bound states of quark and glue reduces to an effective Bethe-Salpeter equation with quark-exchange interaction when irreducible 3-quark interactions are neglected and separable 2-quark (diquark) correlations are assumed. This covariant quark-diquark model of baryons is studied with the inclusion of the quark substructure of the diquark correlations. In order to maintain electromagnetic current conservation it is then necessary to go beyond the impulse approximation. A conserved current is obtained by including the coupling of the photon to the exchanged quark and direct ``seagull'' couplings to the diquark structure. Adopting a simple dynamical model of constituent quarks and exploring various parametrisations of scalar diquark correlations, the nucleon Bethe-Salpeter equation is solved and the proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors are calculated numerically. The resulting magnetic moments are still about 50% too small, the improvements necessary to remedy this are discussed. The results obtained in this framework provide an excellent description of the electric form factors (and charge radii) of the proton, up to a photon momentum transfer of 3.5GeV^2, and the neutron.

## Key claim

The covariant quark-diquark model of the nucleon, when extended to include the quark substructure of diquark correlations, requires going beyond the impulse approximation to maintain electromagnetic current conservation; the resulting magnetic moments are still about 50% too small, even after including the necessary photon couplings to the exchanged quark and direct "seagull" couplings to the diquark structure.

## Method

The authors solved the nucleon Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) for a bound state of a scalar diquark and a quark, using a quark-exchange interaction kernel with separable diquark correlations. They computed electromagnetic form factors by including three contributions: the impulse-approximation coupling of the photon to the quark, the coupling to the exchanged quark, and direct "seagull" couplings to the diquark structure, with the latter two required to satisfy the Ward-Takahashi identity. The calculations employed a simple dynamical model of constituent quarks with various parametrisations of scalar diquark amplitudes, and the transition from Minkowski to Euclidean metric required careful treatment of propagator pole singularities via residue terms.

## Key numbers

- Proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors were calculated up to a photon momentum transfer of 3.5 GeV²
- The resulting magnetic moments are about 50% too small compared to experimental values
- For the impulse-approximate contribution to the proton electric form factor, deviations between wave function and amplitude-plus-residue calculations were at most 4% for momentum transfers above 3 GeV², decreasing rapidly at lower Q²
- The diquark mass parameter used was m_s/M = 0.7, where M is the nucleon mass
- The momentum partitioning variable η = 0.4 was used for stable numerical results, with η = 1/3 producing insufficient Chebyshev expansion convergence

## Context

This work extends the covariant quark-diquark model of baryons by Oettel et al. (1998) and Helmbach et al. (1997), incorporating the quark substructure of diquark correlations and the necessary current-conserving couplings beyond the impulse approximation. The results provide an excellent description of the electric form factors and charge radii of the proton and neutron up to 3.5 GeV², but the remaining discrepancy in magnetic moments indicates the need for further improvements, such as including axial-vector diquark correlations or more sophisticated dynamical models.
