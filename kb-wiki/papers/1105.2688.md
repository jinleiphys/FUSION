# Consistent interactions for high-spin fermion fields

> We address the issue of consistent interactions for off-shell fermion fields of arbitrary spin. These interactions play a crucial role in the quantum hadrodynamical description of high-spin baryon resonances in hadronic processes. The Rarita-Schwinger description of high-spin fermion fields involves unphysical degrees of freedom, associated with their lower-spin content. These enter the interaction if not eliminated outright. The invariance condition of the interaction under the unconstrained Rarita-Schwinger gauge removes the lower-spin content of the fermion propagator and leads to a consistent description of the interaction. We develop the most general, consistent interaction structure for high-spin fermions. We find that the power of the momentum dependence of a consistent interaction rises with the spin of the fermion field. This leads to unphysical structures in the energy dependence of the computed cross sections when the short-distance physics is cut off with standard hadronic form factors. A novel, spin-dependent hadronic form factor is proposed that suppresses the unphysical artifacts.

## Key claim

The paper claims that consistent interactions for off-shell high-spin fermion fields (spin ≥ 3/2) require invariance under the unconstrained Rarita-Schwinger gauge, but that the resulting momentum dependence of the interaction rises with spin, producing unphysical artifacts in cross sections when standard hadronic form factors are used; a novel spin-dependent form factor is proposed to suppress these artifacts.

## Method

The authors extend Pascalutsa's gauge-invariant method for spin-3/2 fields to arbitrary half-integer spin by constructing gauge-invariant fields from products of spin-3/2 interaction operators. They derive the general form of the spin-(n+1/2) projection operator and propagator, then apply the formalism to construct consistent (φψψ*)-type and (Aμψψ*)-type couplings for high-spin baryon resonances in hadronic processes. The resulting interaction structures are tested in tree-level cross section calculations, where the authors identify problems arising from standard hadronic form factors and propose a multidipole-Gauss form factor as a remedy.

## Key numbers

- The Rarita-Schwinger description of a spin-s fermion with s = n + 1/2 has 4(n+3)!/(3!n!) degrees of freedom, whereas the required number is 4(n+1).
- The spin-5/2 propagator derived in Eq. (propagator-52-1) contains terms up to order 1/m^4, including a term (2/5m^4) p_μ p_ν p_λ p_ρ.
- The spin-5/2 projection operator coefficients are given by A_k^n = 1/[(-2)^k] × n!/[k!(n-2k)!] × (2n-2k-1)!!/(2n-1)!!.
- The spin-(n+1/2) projection operator coefficients are A_k^n = 1/[(-2)^k] × 1/[n! k! (n-2k)!] × (2n-2k+1)!!/(2n+1)!! and B_k^n = -1/[(-2)^k] × 1/[n! k! (n-2k-1)!] × (2n-2k-1)!!/(2n+1)!!.

## Context

This work extends Pascalutsa's (1998) consistent interaction theory for spin-3/2 fields to arbitrary half-integer spin, and generalizes the gauge-invariant field construction method. It addresses the long-standing Johnson-Sudarshan and Velo-Zwanziger problems for high-spin fields by providing a systematic framework for constructing consistent interactions. The paper enables future calculations of hadronic processes involving high-spin baryon resonances (such as Δ and N* resonances) with controlled treatment of unphysical lower-spin components, and identifies a previously unrecognized issue with standard hadronic form factors that the authors resolve with a new spin-dependent form factor.
