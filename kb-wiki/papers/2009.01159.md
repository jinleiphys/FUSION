# Role of continuum in nuclear direct reactions with one-neutron halo nuclei: a one-dimensional model

> We study the evolution of a single-particle wave function during the collision of a one dimensional potential well by another well, which can be regarded as a simple model for the problem of the scattering of a one-neutron halo nucleus by another nucleus. This constitutes an effective three-body problem, whose solution in three dimensions can be extremely complicated, particularly when breakup and rearrangement channels are to be considered. Our one-dimensional model provides the essential three-body nature of this problem, and allows for a much simpler application and assessment of different methods of solution. To simplify further the problem, we assume that the potential well representing the projectile moves according to a predetermined classical trajectory, although the internal motion of the "valence" particle is treated fully quantum-mechanically. This corresponds to a semiclassical approach of the scattering problem. Different approaches are investigated to understand the dynamics involving one-body halo-like systems: the "exact" time-dependent solution of the Schrödinger equation is compared to a numerical continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CC) calculation presenting various model cases including different reaction channels. This framework allows us to discuss the reaction mechanism and the role of continuum, whose inclusion in the CC calculation results to be crucial to reproduce the "exact" solution, even when the initial and final states are well bound. We also link each dynamical situation with analogous problem solved in a three dimensional (3D) CC framework, discussing the main challenges experienced in the usual 3D models.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in a one-dimensional semiclassical model of one-neutron halo nucleus scattering, the inclusion of continuum states in coupled-channels (CC) calculations is crucial to reproduce the exact time-dependent solution, even when the initial and final states are well bound and the breakup probability is small.

## Method

The authors model the collision of two one-dimensional Woods-Saxon potential wells, where one well (the projectile) moves along a classical trajectory while the other (the target) remains fixed. The valence neutron wavefunction is treated fully quantum-mechanically. The "exact" solution is obtained by numerically solving the time-dependent Schrödinger equation, and this is compared to continuum-discretized coupled-channels (CC) calculations using pseudostates obtained from an infinite square well basis (BOX method) to discretize the continuum. The CC calculations include bound states and discretized continuum pseudostates for both target and projectile wells, with probabilities evaluated in the unitary representation.

- Continuum pseudostates in CC$^{(4)}$ calculated in [-20, +20] fm range centered on each potential, with energy cutoff of 4 MeV (Case A)

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical time-dependent approach of Esbensen (1981) by including continuum states in the coupled-channels treatment, which was previously omitted. The one-dimensional model allows direct comparison between approximate CC methods and exact solutions, providing insight into limitations of three-dimensional CDCC calculations where continuum treatment is more challenging. The results confirm findings from three-dimensional studies (e.g., YOMM12, MCNT02, LCAGQG09, KAKR09) that continuum-continuum couplings are important for weakly-bound nuclei, and demonstrate that even when breakup is negligible, continuum inclusion can be essential for accurate reaction description.
