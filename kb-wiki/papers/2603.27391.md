# Probe charmonium-nucleon interactions in high energy proton-proton collisions

> We investigate charmonium production and the charmonium-nucleon correlation function in pp collisions using the EPOS4+CATS framework. For the first time, the emission source of charmonium-proton pairs is dynamically generated and found to be non-Gaussian. This enables a femtoscopic extraction of the charmonium-proton interaction directly from experimental correlation functions. Both ground and excited charmonium states are included. We found that the excited states induce sizable uncertainties even negative in the observed prompt $J/ψ$-proton correlation through feed-down effects, reflecting their stronger interactions.

## Key claim

The charmonium-proton emission source in pp collisions is dynamically generated for the first time and found to be non-Gaussian, enabling femtoscopic extraction of the charmonium-proton interaction; however, excited charmonium states induce sizable uncertainties, even negative contributions, in the observed prompt J/ψ-proton correlation through feed-down effects, reflecting their stronger interactions.

## Method

The authors combine the EPOS4 event generator (which provides the classical phase-space distribution of heavy quarks and protons) with the CATS framework to solve the Schrödinger equation for the scattering wave function, using charmonium-nucleon potentials from both the QCD multipole expansion (QCDME) and HAL QCD lattice calculations. The charmonium emission source is obtained by projecting the charm-anticharm pair density matrix onto charmonium states via a quantum density matrix formalism, with the relative distance between c and c̅ given by a Gaussian distribution of width σ_c̅c = 0.3–0.35 fm. The correlation function is then computed by convolving the dynamically generated source with the spin-averaged scattering wave functions, including feed-down contributions from χ_c and ψ(2S) with branching ratios of 30% and 61%, respectively.

## Key numbers

- The effective width parameter for the relative separation of the charm-anticharm pair is σ_c̅c = 0.3–0.35 fm, the only model parameter in charmonium production.
- The Gaussian source fit to the EPOS4 emission source gives r_0 = 1.006 fm, which yields the same rms radius as the dynamically generated source.
- Perturbative chromoelectric polarizabilities: α_J/ψ = 0.2 GeV⁻³, α_ψ(2S) = 12 GeV⁻³; nonperturbative values: α_J/ψ = 1.6 ± 0.8 GeV⁻³, α_χc = 10.37 GeV⁻³, α_ψ(2S) = 47.72 GeV⁻³.
- HAL QCD potential parameters for J/ψ-N: for spin-3/2 (spin-1/2) state, c₁ = -51 (-101) MeV, c₂ = -13 (-33) MeV, c₃ = -22 (-23) MeV, with ranges b₁ = 0.09 (0.13) fm, b₂ = 0.49 (0.44) fm, b₃ = 0.82 (0.83) fm.
- Only charmonium-proton pairs with relative momentum below 300 MeV are considered for femtoscopic correlations.
- Feed-down contribution to prompt J/ψ is about 40% in pp collisions.

## Context

This work extends femtoscopic studies of hadron-hadron interactions, previously applied to nucleon-hyperon and hyperon-hyperon systems by STAR and ALICE, and to heavy-light systems such as D-π, D-K, and D-p by ALICE, to the charmonium-nucleon system. It builds on pioneering femtoscopic studies of charmonium-nucleon correlations by Krein (2020, 2023) and Liu (2025) that used the Lednický-Lyuboshits model with Gaussian sources, and on lattice QCD potentials from the HAL QCD Collaboration (Lyu, 2024). The framework enables direct extraction of charmonium-nucleon interactions from experimental correlation functions at LHC Run 3 and future experiments, and provides a unified approach to probing quarkonium-hadron interactions with arbitrary hadrons.
