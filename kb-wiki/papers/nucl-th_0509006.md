# Extended quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model and the deconfinement phase transition

> An extended quark mass density- and temperature- dependent model which includes the couplings between quarks and the sigma-mesons, omega-mesons is suggested. The MIT bag boundary constrain has been given up and the interactions between quarks and mesons are extended to the whole free space. We show that the present model is successful to describe both the saturation properties and the deconfinement phase transition of nuclear matter. When the effective nucleon masses vanish and the bag radius tends to infinite, the quark deconfinement takes place. The corresponding QGP phase diagram is addressed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that an extended quark mass density- and temperature-dependent (EQMDTD) model, which includes couplings between quarks and sigma- and omega-mesons while discarding the MIT bag boundary condition, can simultaneously describe both the saturation properties of nuclear matter and the deconfinement phase transition within a single unified framework. The deconfinement transition occurs specifically when the effective nucleon mass vanishes and the bag radius tends to infinity, with the model's success contingent on introducing density-dependent quark-sigma couplings.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian density where u and d quarks with temperature- and density-dependent masses (following the QMDTD prescription) couple to scalar sigma and vector omega meson fields via mean-field approximation. The effective quark mass is modified by the sigma mean field, and three quarks form a nucleon "bag" whose radius is determined by minimizing the bag energy. The model parameters (g_sigma^q(0), g_sigma^q(1), g_sigma^q(2), g_omega^q) are fixed by requiring binding energy E = -15 MeV, effective nucleon mass ratio M_N*/M_N = 0.6 at saturation density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3, and deconfinement at rho_B = 10 rho_0 at zero temperature.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: E = -15 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass ratio at saturation: M_N*/M_N = 0.6
- Deconfinement transition at zero temperature occurs at baryon density: rho_B = 10 rho_0
- Critical temperature for deconfinement at zero baryon density: T_c = 170 MeV
- Bag constant at zero temperature: B_0 = 173 MeV fm^-3
- Omega meson mass: m_omega = 783 MeV
- Sigma meson mass: m_sigma = 509 MeV
- Fitted coupling constants: g_sigma^q(0) = 5.93 fm^-3, g_sigma^q(1) = -0.747 fm^-6, g_sigma^q(2) = 0.035 fm^-9, g_omega^q = 4.23

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QMDTD model (Zhang and Su, 2003) by introducing quark-meson couplings similar to the Quark-Meson Coupling (QMC) model of Guichon (1988), but replaces the permanent confinement of the MIT bag with a non-permanent mechanism based on the Friedberg-Lee soliton bag model. Unlike the QMC model where interactions are confined within the bag, the EQMDTD model spreads quark-meson interactions throughout free space. The model enables a unified description of both hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases, contrasting with typical approaches that use separate models for each phase and Gibbs conditions to determine the phase diagram (Lee and Heinz, 1993; Muller, 1997).
