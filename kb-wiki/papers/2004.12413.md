# Symmetry restoration and the gluon mass in the Landau gauge

> We investigate the generation of a gluon screening mass in Yang-Mills theory in the Landau gauge. We propose a gauge-fixing procedure where the Gribov ambiguity is overcome by summing over all Gribov copies with some weight function. This can be formulated in terms of a local field theory involving constrained, nonlinear sigma model fields. We show that a phenomenon of radiative symmetry restoration occurs in this theory, similar to what happens in the standard nonlinear sigma model in two dimensions. This results in a nonzero gluon screening mass, as seen in lattice simulations.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a nonzero gluon screening mass in Landau-gauge Yang-Mills theory can arise from the gauge-fixing procedure itself, specifically from how Gribov copies are averaged, rather than purely from infrared dynamics, but only when a radiative symmetry restoration occurs in an auxiliary nonlinear sigma model sector, which requires a nonzero value of the gauge-fixing parameter ζ.

## Method

The authors propose a gauge-fixing procedure that sums over Gribov copies with a weight function depending on two parameters β and ζ, formulated as a local field theory involving constrained nonlinear sigma model (NLσ) superfields. They integrate out the NLσ fluctuations exactly while treating the gluon, ghost, and Nakanishi-Lautrup fields at tree level, then study the gap equations for the average fields to determine whether the O(4) symmetry of the NLσ sector is broken or restored. The analysis is performed for SU(2) in dimensional regularization, with renormalization of the UV divergences.

## Key numbers

- The tree-level gluon square mass is nβ, which vanishes in the physically relevant limit n→0 in the broken-symmetry phase.
- In the radiatively restored symmetric phase, the gluon square mass is β (nonzero in the n→0 limit).
- The gap equation for the symmetric phase is: 8π²β_r/ḡ_r² = (χ̂_{r,sym}+ζ_r) ln(μ̄²/(χ̂_{r,sym}+ζ_r)) – χ̂_{r,sym} ln(μ̄²/χ̂_{r,sym}).
- A solution for χ̂_{r,sym} ≥ 0 exists only when 8π²β_r/ḡ_r² ≤ ζ_r ln(μ̄²/ζ_r) ≤ μ̄²/e.
- The broken-phase solution gives N̂_{r,brok}² = 1 – (ḡ_r²/(16π²))(ζ_r/β_r) ln(μ̄²/ζ_r).

## Context

This work extends the gauge-fixing proposal of Serreau (2012) by adding a nonzero parameter ζ that weights Gribov copies near the first Gribov horizon, relaxing the topological constraints of the earlier formulation. It provides an explicit example where a gluon mass term emerges from the treatment of Gribov copies, resembling the Curci-Ferrari model (Curci, 1976) which has been used to describe lattice results. The resulting gauge-fixed theory includes a ghost mass term with square mass ζ and massive fields determined self-consistently, offering a framework to compute propagators at one-loop order and compare with lattice simulations.
