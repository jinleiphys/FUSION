# Finite-volume spectrum of $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^+$ and $ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^+ŽĆ^+$ systems

> The ab-initio understanding of hadronic three-body systems above threshold, such as exotic resonances or the baryon spectrum, requires the mapping of the finite-volume eigenvalue spectrum, produced in lattice QCD calculations, to the infinite volume. We present the first application of such a formalism to a physical system in form of three interacting positively charged pions. The results for the ground state energies agree with the available lattice QCD results by the NPLQCD collaboration at unphysical pion masses. Extrapolations to physical pion masses are performed using input from effective field theory. The excited energy spectrum is predicted. This demonstrates the feasibility to determine three-body amplitudes above threshold from lattice QCD, including resonance properties of axial mesons, exotics, and excited baryons.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first application of a relativistic finite-volume formalism to a physical three-body system, demonstrating that three-body amplitudes above threshold can be extracted from lattice QCD. The authors hedge that the formalism is validated only for the ground state energies, which agree with existing lattice results at unphysical pion masses, while the excited spectrum is a prediction that awaits future lattice verification.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic three-body scattering amplitude that satisfies three-body unitarity, parametrized via an isobar model where the two-body sub-amplitude is the isospin I=2 ŽĆŌü║ŽĆŌü║ S-wave. The finite-volume quantization condition is derived by discretizing momenta in a cubic box of size L, and the determinant of a matrix involving the isobar propagator and the one-pion-exchange kernel is solved to find energy eigenvalues. The two-body input is fixed by fitting to experimental ŽĆŽĆ phase shifts using the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) with chiral perturbation theory at next-to-leading order, and the remaining three-body contact term C is fit to the NPLQCD ground-state energies.

## Context
This work extends the finite-volume formalism of Luscher (1986, 1990) from two-body to three-body systems, building on the formal developments of Mai (2017) and Doring (2018). It directly analyzes lattice QCD results from the NPLQCD collaboration (Beane 2007, Detmold 2008) for the ŽĆŌü║ŽĆŌü║ŽĆŌü║ system, which is the simplest three-hadron system free from coupled channels and spin complications. The demonstrated feasibility enables future extraction of three-body amplitudes for systems like the Roper resonance (N(1440)1/2Ōü║) and exotic mesons such as the ŽĆŌéü(1600), where lattice data already exist from Lang (2016) and others.
