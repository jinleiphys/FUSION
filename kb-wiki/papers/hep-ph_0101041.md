# Nuclear Effects in Prompt Photon Production at the Large Hadron Collider

> We present a detailed study of prompt photon production cross section in heavy-ion collisions in the central rapidity region at energy of $\sqrt{s}=5.5$ TeV, appropriate to LHC experiment. We include the next-to-leading order radiative corrections, $O(α_{em}α_s^2)$, nuclear shadowing and the parton energy loss effects. We find that the nuclear effects can reduce the invariant cross section for prompt photon production by an order of magnitude at $p_t=3$ GeV. We discuss theoretical uncertainties due to parton energy loss and nuclear shadowing parameters. We show that the K-factor, which signifies the importance of next-to-leading order corrections, is large and has a strong $p_t$ dependence.

## Key claim

Nuclear effects (shadowing and parton energy loss) can reduce the prompt photon production cross section in heavy-ion collisions at LHC energies by up to an order of magnitude at low transverse momentum, but the precise magnitude depends strongly on the choice of nuclear shadowing parameterization and energy loss model, which remain theoretically uncertain.

## Method

The authors calculate the inclusive prompt photon production cross section in Pb+Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy √s = 5.5 TeV using next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD at O(α_em α_s^2), including direct and bremsstrahlung contributions. Nuclear structure functions are modeled using two parameterizations (Benesh-Qiu-Vary and Eskola-Kajantie-Ruuskanen-Tuominen) to account for nuclear shadowing, and medium-induced parton energy loss is incorporated via a phenomenological modification of the photon fragmentation functions, considering both constant and energy-dependent energy loss per scattering.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear effects reduce the invariant cross section for prompt photon production by an order of magnitude at p_t = 3 GeV (90% reduction).
* At p_t = 20 GeV, nuclear effects reduce the cross section by approximately 50% for the energy-dependent energy loss scenario.
* The difference between the BQV and EKS98 nuclear shadowing parameterizations is about 4% at p_t = 3 GeV and about 20% at p_t = 20 GeV.
* Varying the renormalization, factorization, and fragmentation scales between 0.5 p_t and 2.0 p_t results in a theoretical uncertainty of about 30%.
* The K-factor (ratio of NLO to LO cross sections) is large and has a strong p_t dependence at √s = 5.5 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Jalilian-Marian et al., 2000) of prompt photon production in heavy-ion collisions by including next-to-leading order radiative corrections and a more detailed treatment of nuclear shadowing and parton energy loss. It builds on the NLO prompt photon formalism developed by Aurenche et al. and uses the energy loss model of Wang, Huang, and Sarcevic. The results highlight the need for a future eA collider (eRHIC) to reduce uncertainties in nuclear structure functions at small x and high Q^2, and they enable future calculations of the prompt photon to pion ratio to reduce theoretical uncertainties.
