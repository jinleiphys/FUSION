# Electromagnetic M1 transition strengths from inelastic proton scattering: The cases of 48Ca and 208Pb

> Inelastic proton scattering at energies of a few hundred MeV and extreme forward angles selectively excites the isovector spin-flip M1 (IVSM1) resonance. A method based on isospin symmetry is presented to extract its electromagnetic transition strength from the (p,p') cross sections. It is applied to 48Ca, a key case for an interpretation of the quenching phenomenon of the spin-isospin response, and leads to a M1 strength consistent with an older (e,e') experiment excluding the almost two times larger value from a recent (γ,n) experiment. Good agreement with electromagnetic probes is observed in 208Pb suggesting the possibility to extract systematic information on the IVSM1 resonance in heavy nuclei.

## Key claim

The authors present a method to extract electromagnetic M1 transition strengths from inelastic proton scattering data using isospin symmetry, which yields results consistent with electromagnetic probes in 208Pb and resolves a discrepancy in 48Ca by supporting the older (e,e') value of 3.9(3) μN² over the almost two times larger value from a recent (γ,n) experiment.

## Method

The method relates the cross section for isovector spin-flip M1 (IVSM1) excitation in (p,p') reactions at 295 MeV and extreme forward angles to the reduced transition strength B(M1στ) via a unit cross section concept analogous to that used for Gamow-Teller (GT) strength extraction from charge-exchange reactions. The unit cross section for (p,p') is assumed equal to that for (p,n) reactions based on isospin symmetry, and the electromagnetic B(M1) strength is derived from B(M1στ) by accounting for spin and orbital contributions, with corrections for isoscalar contributions and quenching factors. The method is applied to 48Ca and 208Pb using data from experiments at RCNP Osaka with the Grand Raiden spectrometer.

## Key numbers

- For 48Ca: the IVSM1 strength is concentrated in a single state at 10.23 MeV; the extracted B(M1) ranges from 3.85(32) to 4.63(38) μN² depending on the assumed isoscalar quenching, consistent with the (e,e') value of 3.9(3) μN² (Steffen et al., 1983) and excluding the (γ,n) value of 6.8(5) μN² (Tonchev et al., 2011)
- For 208Pb: the summed B(M1) strength from (p,p') data is 20.5(1.3) μN² for the spin-M1 resonance; the summed strength up to 8 MeV is 16.0(1.2) μN², compared to 14.8(+1.9/-1.5) μN² from electromagnetic probes (Laszewski et al., 1988; Kohler et al., 1987)
- The unit cross section parameterization for (p,n) at 297 MeV is σ̂_GT = 3.4(3) exp[-0.40(5)(A^(1/3) - 90^(1/3))] (Sasano et al., 2009)
- The 48Ca(p,p') experiment used a beam intensity of 4-10 nA, a 48Ca foil with 95.2% isotopic enrichment and 1.87 mg/cm² areal density, and achieved about 25 keV energy resolution

## Context

This work extends the isospin symmetry connection between M1 and GT modes, previously discussed by Osterfeld (1992) and Fujita et al. (2011), to extract B(M1) strengths from (p,p') reactions. It resolves a discrepancy in 48Ca where a recent (γ,n) measurement at HIγS (Tonchev et al., 2011) gave a value almost twice that from electron scattering (Steffen et al., 1983), and confirms the complete M1 strength distribution in 208Pb claimed by Laszewski et al. (1988). The method enables systematic studies of the IVSM1 resonance in heavy nuclei where electromagnetic probes are limited by the neutron threshold, with ongoing work including 90Zr.
