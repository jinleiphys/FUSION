# Weak Transitions in A=6 and 7 Nuclei

> The $^6$He beta decay and $^7$Be electron capture processes are studied using variational Monte Carlo wave functions, derived from a realistic Hamiltonian consisting of the Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions. The model for the nuclear weak axial current includes one- and two-body operators with the strength of the leading two-body term--associated with $Δ$-isobar excitation of the nucleon--adjusted to reproduce the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium $β$-decay. The measured half-life of $^6$. He is under-predicted by theory by $\simeq$ 8%, while that of $^7$Be for decay into the ground and first excited states of $^7$Li is over-predicted by $\simeq$ 9%. However, the experimentally known branching ratio for these latter processes is in good agreement with the calculated value. Two-body axial current contributions lead to a $\simeq$ 1.7% (4.4%) increase in the value of the Gamow-Teller matrix element of $^6$He ($^7$Be), obtained with one-body currents only, and slightly worsen (appreciably improve) the agreement between the calculated and measured half-life. Corrections due to retardation effects associated with the finite lepton momentum transfers involved in the decays, as well as contributions of suppressed transitions induced by the weak vector charge and axial current operators, have also been calculated and found to be negligible.

## Key claim

The measured half-life of $^6$He is under-predicted by theory by approximately 8%, while that of $^7$Be for decay into the ground and first excited states of $^7$Li is over-predicted by approximately 9%, with the experimentally known branching ratio for the latter processes in good agreement with the calculated value.

## Method

Variational Monte Carlo wave functions derived from the Argonne $v_{18}$ two-nucleon and Urbana-IX three-nucleon interactions were used to study $^6$He beta decay and $^7$Be electron capture. The nuclear weak axial current model included one- and two-body operators, with the strength of the leading two-body term associated with $\Delta$-isobar excitation adjusted to reproduce the Gamow-Teller matrix element in tritium $\beta$-decay. Reduced matrix elements of multipole operators were computed by Monte Carlo integration, and the total transition rates were obtained by integrating over outgoing lepton momenta with Coulomb distortion effects included approximately.

## Context

This work extends previous microscopic calculations of weak transitions in few-nucleon systems to the $A=6$ and $7$ nuclei, using realistic wave functions derived from the same Hamiltonian as earlier studies of tritium $\beta$-decay by Marcucci et al. (2001) and electromagnetic form factors by Wiringa et al. (1998). Prior shell-model calculations had typically failed to reproduce measured Gamow-Teller matrix elements without quenching the axial coupling constant $g_A$, while cluster-model approaches using Faddeev techniques by Parke et al. (1978) or the resonating-group method by Walliser et al. (1983) had tenuous connections to the underlying two- and three-nucleon dynamics. The present results enable improved understanding of whether quenching of $g_A$ reflects deficiencies in wave functions, the axial current operator model, or true in-medium modifications of the nucleon axial coupling.
