# Chiral fluid dynamics with explicit propagation of the Polyakov loop

> We present a fully dynamical model to study nonequilibrium effects in both the chiral and the deconfinement phase transition. The sigma field and the Polyakov loop as the corresponding order parameters are propagated by Langevin equations of motion. The locally thermalized background is provided by a fluid of quarks and antiquarks. Allowing for an exchange of energy and momentum through dissipative and stochastic processes we ensure that the total energy of the coupled system remains conserved. We study its relaxational dynamics in different quench scenarios and are able to observe critical slowing down as well as the enhancement of long wavelength modes at the critical point. During the fluid dynamical expansion of a hot plasma fireball typical nonequilibrium effects like supercooling and domain formation occur when the system evolves through the first order phase transition.

## Key claim
The authors present a fully dynamical model coupling the chiral sigma field and the Polyakov loop order parameter to an ideal fluid of quarks via Langevin equations, but the Polyakov loop dynamics is treated on a phenomenological basis because a consistent field-theoretical derivation is not yet possible, unlike for the sigma field.

## Method
The sigma field and Polyakov loop are propagated by separate Langevin equations of motion with damping and stochastic noise, coupled to a locally thermalized quark-antiquark fluid described by ideal relativistic fluid dynamics. Energy and momentum exchange between the fields and the fluid is ensured through dissipative and stochastic source terms, with the total energy of the coupled system conserved. The model uses a Polyakov loop extended quark-meson (PQM) Lagrangian with a polynomial Polyakov loop potential, and the equations are solved on a 3+1 dimensional grid using the SHASTA algorithm for the fluid and a staggered leap-frog algorithm for the fields.

## Key numbers
- For the first order transition scenario with coupling g=4.7, the transition temperature is T_c=172.9 MeV, with two degenerate minima at (sigma=9 MeV, ell=0.40) and (sigma=81 MeV, ell=0.22).
- For the critical point scenario with g=3.52, the transition temperature is T_c=180.5 MeV, with a single broad minimum at (sigma=49 MeV, ell=0.43).
- The Polyakov loop potential uses T_0=208 MeV for two flavors at zero baryochemical potential, reduced from the pure gauge value of 270 MeV.
- The damping coefficient for the sigma field is derived from the sigma to quark-antiquark decay process, with a value of eta_sigma=2.2/fm used below the phase transition for the sigma to pion-pion reaction.
- The Polyakov loop damping coefficient is chosen as eta_ell=5/fm, with the authors noting the results are not sensitive to this value.
- The grid spacing is Delta x=0.2 fm, with a fluid time step of Delta t=0.08 fm and a field time step of Delta t=0.02 fm.

## Context
This work extends the chiral fluid dynamics model developed by Nahrgang et al. (2011) and earlier by Mishustin et al. (1998) and Paech et al. (2003), which previously only included the chiral order parameter, by adding explicit propagation of the Polyakov loop as the deconfinement order parameter. The dynamical treatment of the Polyakov loop builds on ideas from Dumitru et al. (2001) and Fraga et al. (2007), who proposed Langevin dynamics for the deconfinement order parameter in pure gauge theory. The model enables future studies of nonequilibrium effects such as critical slowing down, supercooling, and domain formation in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC, SPS, FAIR, and NICA, providing a framework to connect dynamical phase transition phenomena with experimental observables.
