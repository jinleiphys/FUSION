# Neutral current (anti)neutrino scattering: relativistic mean field and superscaling predictions

> We evaluate the neutral current quasi-elastic neutrino cross section within two nuclear models: the SuSA model, based on the superscaling behavior of electron scattering data, and the RMF model, based on relativistic mean field theory. We also estimate the ratio $(νp \to νp)/(νN \to νN)$ and compare with the MiniBooNE experimental data, performing a fit of the parameters $M_A$ and $g_A^{(s)}$ within the two models. Finally, we present our predictions for antineutrino scattering.

## Key claim

The SuSA and RMF nuclear models, when using the standard axial mass \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV, underpredict the MiniBooNE NCQE neutrino cross section data; however, by treating the axial mass as an effective parameter to incorporate missing nuclear effects (such as multi-nucleon knockout), the models can restore agreement with the data, yielding best-fit values of \(M_A = 1.34 \pm 0.06\) GeV (RMF) and \(M_A = 1.42 \pm 0.06\) GeV (SuSA).

## Method

The authors computed the neutral-current quasi-elastic (NCQE) neutrino-nucleus cross section using two relativistic nuclear models: the SuSA model, which relies on the superscaling function extracted from electron scattering data, and the RMF model, which describes the process microscopically with relativistic scalar and vector potentials for both bound and final states. They performed a \(\chi^2\) fit of the axial mass \(M_A\) to the MiniBooNE NCQE cross section data on CH\(_2\), and then a separate \(\chi^2\) fit of the axial strangeness parameter \(g_A^{(s)}\) to the MiniBooNE ratio \((\nu p \to \nu p)/(\nu N \to \nu N)\). Predictions for antineutrino scattering were also presented using the fitted parameter sets.

## Key numbers

*   Best-fit axial mass for the RMF model: \(M_A = 1.34 \pm 0.06\) GeV, with \(\chi^2/DOF = 16.5/22\).
*   Best-fit axial mass for the SuSA model: \(M_A = 1.42 \pm 0.06\) GeV, with \(\chi^2/DOF = 4.7/22\).
*   For the standard axial mass \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV, the \(\chi^2/DOF\) values were 46.2/22 (RMF) and 45.3/22 (SuSA).
*   Best-fit axial strangeness for the RMF model: \(g_A^{(s)} = +0.04 \pm 0.28\), with \(\chi^2/DOF = 33.6/29\).
*   Best-fit axial strangeness for the SuSA model: \(g_A^{(s)} = -0.06 \pm 0.31\), with \(\chi^2/DOF = 31.3/29\).
*   The SuSA cross section is smaller than the RFG one by about 20%.

## Context

This work extends the application of the SuSA and RMF models, previously used for charged-current quasi-elastic (CCQE) scattering (Amaro 2004, Amaro 2011), to neutral-current quasi-elastic (NCQE) scattering, comparing with MiniBooNE data (Aguilar-Arevalo 2010). The results show that, unlike in the CCQE case where the two models give similar results, the NC data are more sensitive to the treatment of final-state interactions. The analysis enables future studies of the nucleon's axial strangeness content and provides tuned nuclear models for use in neutrino-oscillation experiments, with the caveat that meson-exchange currents and multi-nucleon effects were not included.
