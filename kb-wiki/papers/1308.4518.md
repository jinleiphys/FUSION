# Phase Structure of QCD Matter in a Chiral Effective Model with Quarks

> Using a unified hadron-quark effective model for the QCD equation of state, this paper studies the phase structure of strongly interacting matter in a wide range of temperature and baryonchemical potential. At small potentials the model yields a smooth cross-over to chirally restored matter with a transition temperature and curvature in line with recent lattice QCD estimates and thermal model fits of freeze-out curves. Trajectories of constant entropy per net baryon number show a clear dependence on the particle composition in the model and on repulsive vector field interactions. Although the model might feature a critical end-point at a rather high baryonchemical potential and low temperature, probing it in heavy-ion collisions might be highly challenging due to a large thermodynamic spread of matter in the collision fireball.

## Key claim

Using a unified hadron-quark effective model, the paper finds that while a critical end-point (CEP) may exist at high baryochemical potential and low temperature, probing it in heavy-ion collisions is highly challenging because initial-state fluctuations cause a large thermodynamic spread of fireball matter, meaning no single narrow isentropic adiabat corresponds to a given collision energy.

## Method

The authors construct a chiral SU(3)-flavor sigma-omega model for hadrons combined with a Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) type approach for quarks, treating all fields in mean-field approximation. They include all baryons from the octet and decuplet plus resonances up to 2.6 GeV, all meson resonance states, and the three lightest quark flavors (u, d, s). Thermodynamic quantities are derived from the grand canonical potential, and isentropic expansion paths (lines of constant entropy per net baryon number) are computed for different values of the resonance vector coupling parameter r_v and the quark vector coupling g_qω, then compared with dynamic expansion paths from ideal hydrodynamics and the UrQMD hybrid model.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature at zero baryochemical potential: T_0 = T_c(μ_B = 0) = 164 MeV
- Chiral transition curvature parametrization: T_c(μ_B) = T_0 [1 - 0.0193 (μ_B/T_0)^2]
- Explicit mass terms: δm_{u,d} = 6 MeV for quarks, δm_s = 105 MeV for strange quarks, δm_{n,p} = 150 MeV for nucleons
- Maximum net baryon density at freeze-out occurs near E_lab = 30 A GeV in fixed-target collisions
- For r_v = 0.4: first-order phase transition exists up to T ≈ 60 MeV, with an adjacent CEP
- For r_v = 0.9: no first-order phase transition or CEP exists
- Thermodynamic spread of fireball matter: at least 50 MeV in μ_q per single event

## Context

This work extends earlier chiral effective model studies (Rau 2012) by including quarks alongside hadrons in a unified framework, and contrasts with pure quark PNJL models (Fukushima 2003, Ratti 2006) and Polyakov-quark-meson models (Schaefer 2007, Herbst 2010) whose Polyakov loop potentials are fixed at vanishing μ_B. The results enable assessment of whether the critical end-point predicted by older lattice QCD estimates (Fodor 2004) can be probed at upcoming FAIR energies (5-40 GeV per nucleon), and highlight the difficulty of mapping final-state observables to specific points in the QCD phase diagram due to initial-state fluctuations seen in dynamic models (Bass 2012).
