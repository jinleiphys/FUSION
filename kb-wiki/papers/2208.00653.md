# Pion, kaon, and (anti-)proton production in U+U Collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 193 GeV measured with the STAR detector

> We present the first measurements of transverse momentum spectra of $π^{\pm}$, $K^{\pm}$, $p(\bar{p})$ at midrapidity ($|y| < 0.1$) in U+U collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 193 GeV with the STAR detector at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC). The centrality dependence of particle yields, average transverse momenta, particle ratios and kinetic freeze-out parameters are discussed. The results are compared with the published results from Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} =$ 200 GeV in STAR. The results are also compared to those from A Multi Phase Transport (AMPT) model.

## Key claim

The first measurements of pion, kaon, and (anti-)proton production in U+U collisions at √sNN = 193 GeV show that the average transverse momenta and particle yields are similar to those in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV for comparable numbers of participating nucleons, with the caveat that the U+U system allows access to higher ⟨Npart⟩ values (above 400) than previously achievable at RHIC.

## Method

Transverse momentum spectra of π±, K±, and p(p̅) were measured at midrapidity (|y| < 0.1) using the STAR Time Projection Chamber (TPC) for particle identification via ionization energy loss (dE/dx) up to pT of 0.8, 0.8, and 1.0 GeV/c respectively, with the Time Of Flight (TOF) detector extending identification to higher pT. Raw yields were extracted using the z-variable method for TPC data and m² distributions for TOF data, then corrected for tracking efficiency, acceptance, energy loss, TOF matching efficiency, pion feed-down from weak decays, and proton background from detector material interactions. The corrected spectra were fitted with Bose-Einstein, mT-exponential, and double exponential functions for pions, kaons, and protons respectively to extract dN/dy and ⟨pT⟩.

## Key numbers

- The average number of participating nucleons in the most central (0-5%) U+U collisions is ⟨Npart⟩ = 414.9 ± 6.0, exceeding the maximum ⟨Npart⟩ ≈ 350 in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV
- The ⟨pT⟩ for π⁺ in 0-5% central U+U collisions is 0.449 ± 0.039 GeV/c; for K⁺ it is 0.686 ± 0.066 GeV/c; for protons it is 1.004 ± 0.101 GeV/c
- The ⟨pT⟩ for π⁺ in 70-80% peripheral U+U collisions is 0.396 ± 0.031 GeV/c; for K⁺ it is 0.619 ± 0.054 GeV/c; for protons it is 0.721 ± 0.052 GeV/c
- The total systematic uncertainties on particle yields are 10% for pions, 11% for kaons, and 13% for (anti-)protons
- The pion feed-down fraction from weak decays and muon contamination has a maximum of 15% at pT = 0.2 GeV/c and vanishes above pT > 1.2 GeV/c
- The proton background fraction from detector material interactions is maximum for low pT (approximately 9% for mid-central collisions) and becomes negligible for pT > 1.2 GeV/c

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of identified particle production in heavy-ion collisions at RHIC by using deformed uranium nuclei (prolate shape) as a complement to the nearly spherical gold nuclei studied previously by STAR in Au+Au collisions at √sNN = 200 GeV (Abelev 2008, Adams 2003). The results enable comparisons with the AMPT model modified to incorporate uranium nuclear deformation (Haque 2011), and provide the highest ⟨Npart⟩ measurements at RHIC for testing the centrality dependence of radial flow and kinetic freeze-out conditions.
