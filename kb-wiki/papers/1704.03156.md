# Structural Properties of Finite and Infinite Nuclear Systems and Related Phenomena

> In the present thesis, we have carried a thorough investigation of nuclear structure properties. We start our investigation from the study of the magic property of nucleus in the super -heavy region. We know the magic combination of proton and neutron in the light and medium heavy region. But in the super-heavy region, it is still unclear. We applied SEI (simple effective interaction ) and RMF (relativistic mean field ) formalism with a different parameter sets to predict the magic combinations and it turned out Z=114, 120, 126 with N=184. We have also studied theisoscalar giant monopole resonance energy of nucleus of Z=114, 120,126, with scaling and constrained method using RETF formalism. Isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) is also known as the breathing mode. We have a developed a new constrained type calculation for the ISGMR and IVGDR. ## Key claim
The thesis claims that the self-interacting higher-order $\sigma$ and $\omega$ fields in the relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian are crucial for reproducing the correct depth of the nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential and for explaining the Coester band problem, but the abstract’s implication of a definitive prediction for super-heavy magic numbers is hedged: the predicted magic combinations (Z=114, 120, 126 with N=184) are stated as a result of applying SEI and RMF formalisms, not as a confirmed discovery.

## Method
The author applied the relativistic mean field (RMF) formalism with the NL3 parameter set, adding a non-linear self-coupling term for the $\omega$-meson ($c_3$) on top of the standard non-linear $\sigma$ terms. For finite nuclei, the binding energy, excitation energy, and incompressibility of $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb were calculated as a function of $c_3$ using a scaling method within the extended Thomas-Fermi (RETF) approximation. For infinite nuclear matter, the energy per particle and pressure density were computed as functions of baryon density for various $c_3$ values. A new perturbative constrained calculation for the excitation energy of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) and isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) was also developed, based on a Taylor series expansion of the energy functional around equilibrium.

## Key numbers
- The depth of the NN potential for the linear RMF (NL3) case is approximately 150 MeV, while the non-linear case gives a depth of approximately 50 MeV.
- The non-linear self-coupling of the $\omega$-meson gives an attractive component at a very short distance of about 0.2 fm.
- The nuclear matter compressibility modulus for the NL3 set is 271.76 MeV, compared to the empirical value of $K_\infty^{emp}=210\pm30$ MeV.
- The excitation energy ($E_x$) for $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb remains almost constant for a wide range of $c_3$ values (approximately -2 to +1), beyond which it increases drastically for positive values.
- The binding energy of $^{40}$Ca and $^{208}$Pb monotonically changes for all values of $c_3$, deviating from the data when $c_3$ is non-zero.

## Context
This work extends the study of nuclear structure and the NN interaction within the RMF framework, specifically investigating the effects of non-linear $\omega$-meson self-coupling (as in the FSU-Gold parameter set of Piekarewicz (2001)) on top of the standard NL3 force. The analysis of the ISGMR and IVGDR using a new perturbative constrained method builds on prior semi-classical RTF and RETF calculations by Patra (2002) and aims to provide a simplified way to estimate average excitation energies, complementing full relativistic RPA calculations by Ma (2001) and Wei (2013). The results on the equation of state are discussed in the context of neutron star properties, noting that the recent measurement by Demorest (2010) suggests the NL3 force needs a slightly softer EOS, while the G2 (E-RMF) model of Singh (2013) demands a slightly stiffer one.
