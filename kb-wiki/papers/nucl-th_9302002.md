# Test of Time Reversal Invariance in Proton Deuteron System

> Internal target experiments with high quality proton beams allow for a new class of experiments providing null tests of time reversal symmetry in forward scattering. This could yield more stringent limits on T-odd P-even observables. A excellent candidate for such experiments is the proton deuteron system. This system is analyzed in terms of effective T-violating P-conserving nucleon-nucleon interactions and bounds on coupling strengths that might be expected are given.

# Test of Time Reversal Invariance in Proton Deuteron System

## Key claim

A forward scattering experiment in the proton-deuteron system could provide a true null test of time reversal symmetry, potentially reaching an experimental accuracy of $|\expect{T-{\rm odd}}| < 10^{-6} |\expect{T-{\rm even}}|$, but the achievable bounds on T-odd P-even coupling strengths depend on the specific microscopic model assumed for the T-violating interaction and the energy regime chosen.

## Method

The paper analyzes the proton-deuteron forward scattering amplitude in terms of spin tensor expansions, identifying a T-odd P-even amplitude $\F_{12;2}^T$ that contributes to the total cross section when both projectile and target are polarized. The total spin correlation $\ST$ is evaluated using a lowest-order rescattering approximation for the three-nucleon system, with the T-odd nucleon-nucleon interaction parameterized either through constant coupling strengths (model I) or through specific meson-exchange potentials including vector ($\rho$) and axial vector ($a_1$) exchange (model II), using the Bonn potential deuteron wave function.

## Key numbers

- Experimental accuracy projected for forward scattering: $|\expect{T-{\rm odd}}| < 10^{-6} |\expect{T-{\rm even}}|$ (from Eversheim et al., 1991)
- Bound from model I on T-odd NN matrix elements: $|\expect{t^T_{NN}}| < 2 \times 10^{-4} |\expect{t^E_{NN}}|$ at $k_{cm} \simeq 0.3$ GeV/c with $\sigma^0_{tot} \simeq 50$ mb
- Bound on $\rho$-meson T-odd coupling from model II: $\phi_\rho = g^T_{\rho NN}/g_{\rho NN} < 10^{-3}$
- Bound on $a_1$-meson T-odd coupling from model II: $\phi_{a_1} = g^T_{ANN}/g_{ANN} < 2 \times 10^{-3}$
- Maxima of T-odd asymmetry in model II: $\ST\sigma^0_{tot} \simeq 0.04\phi_\rho$ mb for $\rho$-exchange and $\ST\sigma^0_{tot} \simeq 0.02\phi_{a_1}$ mb for $a_1$-exchange at $k_{cm} \simeq 0.3$ GeV
- Previous bound from $^{57}$Fe $\gamma$-decay: $G^T \simeq (0.7 \pm 1.6 \pm 0.5) \times 10^{-5}$ (Beyer, 1989)
- Previous bound from detailed balance experiments: $G^T < 2.6 \times 10^{-4}$ (Harvey et al.)
- Bound on T-odd P-even NN amplitude from electric dipole moment analysis (Herczeg): $|f^T| < 2 \times 10^{-5}$; corresponding meson-nucleon coupling: $|g^T_{MNN}/g^E_{MNN}| = \phi < 10^{-4}$
- Bound from Khriplovich (1991) renormalized to axial vector mass scale: $|\expect{t_{NN}^T}| < 2 \times 10^{-4} |\expect{t_{NN}^E}|$
- Bound from combined T-odd P-odd and P-odd amplitudes: $|\expect{t_{NN}^{TP} t_{NN}^P}| \leq 10^{-17} \sigma^0_{tot}$

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Conzett (1992) for a null test of time reversal symmetry in forward scattering, applying it specifically to the proton-deuteron system. It contrasts with experiments on complex nuclei (such as $^{57}$Fe $\gamma$-decay by Cheung et al., 1977, and detailed balance experiments by Blanke et al., 1983) where enhancement factors from nuclear structure may occur but are absent in light systems. The analysis enables direct extraction of bounds on T-odd P-even nucleon-nucleon coupling constants from a relatively simple three-body system, complementing electric dipole moment measurements (Altarev et al., 1981; Ramsey, 1977) which primarily constrain T-odd P-odd interactions.
