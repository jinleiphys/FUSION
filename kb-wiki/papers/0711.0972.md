# Proton Electromagnetic Form Factor Ratios at Low Q^2

> We study the ratio $R\equivμG_E(Q^2)/G_M(Q^2)$ of the proton at very small values of $Q^2$. Radii commonly associated with these form factors are not moments of charge or magnetization densities. We show that the form factor $F_2$ is correctly interpretable as the two-dimensional Fourier transformation of a magnetization density. A relationship between the measurable ratio and moments of true charge and magnetization densities is derived. We find that existing measurements show that the magnetization density extends further than the charge density, in contrast with expectations based on the measured reduction of $R$ as $Q^2$ increases.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the magnetization density of the proton extends further than its charge density, which is the opposite of what one might expect from the observed decrease of the ratio μG_E/G_M with increasing Q^2. This conclusion relies on interpreting F_2 as a two-dimensional Fourier transform of a true magnetization density, and on a derived relationship that includes a relativistic correction (the Foldy term) that changes the sign of the difference between effective radii compared to the difference of true moments.

## Method

The authors derive a formal relationship between the Sachs form factor ratio at low Q^2 and the second moments of the true charge density (from F_1) and true magnetization density (from F_2), using light-cone formalism and generalized parton distributions (GPDs). They then perform a linear fit to the world dataset of high-precision polarization transfer experiments for μG_E/G_M at low Q^2, using a fixed value of the effective magnetic radius R_M* from Volotka (2006), to extract the difference ⟨b²⟩_M − ⟨b²⟩_Ch.

## Key numbers

- ⟨b²⟩_M − ⟨b²⟩_Ch = 0.10960 ± 0.00678 fm² (from the linear fit to polarization transfer data)
- The relativistic Foldy correction term μ/M² ≈ 0.1235 fm²
- The difference of effective square radii (2/3)(μ/κ)(R_M*² − R_E*²) = −0.0139 ± 0.00678 fm², which has opposite sign to the true moment difference
- R_M* = 0.778(29) fm (taken from Volotka (2006) as a fixed input)
- The anomalous magnetic moment κ = 1.79 and the total magnetic moment μ = 2.79

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Burkardt (2000, 2003) on impact-parameter GPDs and the interpretation of F_1 as a two-dimensional Fourier transform of a charge density (Soper, 1977; Diehl, 2002). It provides a model-independent derivation showing that F_2 is the two-dimensional Fourier transform of a magnetization density, contradicting the textbook interpretation of Sachs form factor radii as moments of three-dimensional densities (Ernst, Sachs, and Wali, 1960). The result enables future low-Q² polarization transfer or cross-section measurements to distinguish between diverse model calculations (e.g., Miller, 2002; Belushkin, 2006; Arrington, 2004) and to better determine the difference of true charge and magnetization radii.
