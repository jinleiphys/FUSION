# Simple Solution to the Strangeness Horn Description Puzzle

> We propose to use the thermal model with the multi-component hard-core radii to describe the hadron yield ratios from the low AGS to the highest RHIC energies. It is demonstrated that the variation of the hard-core radii of pions and kaons enable us to drastically improve the fit quality of the measured mid-rapidity data and for the first time to completely describe the Strangeness Horn behavior as the function of the energy of collision without spoiling the fit quality of other ratios. The best global fit is found for the vanishing hard-core radius of pions and for the hard-core radius of kaons being equal to 0.35 fm, whereas the hard-core radius of all other mesons is fixed to 0.3 fm and that one of baryons is fixed to 0.5 fm. It is argued that the multi-component hadron resonance gas model opens us a principal possibility to determine the second virial coefficients of hadron-hadron interaction.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the long-standing "Strangeness Horn" puzzle (the non-monotonic behavior of the \(K^+/\pi^+\) ratio as a function of collision energy) can be fully described for the first time by a thermal hadron resonance gas model, but only if the hard-core radii of pions and kaons are treated as independent, adjustable parameters rather than fixed to a common value.

## Method

The authors used a multi-component hadron resonance gas model with a Boltzmann gas of \(N\) hadron species, where the second virial coefficients \(b_{ij}\) are defined via hard-core radii as \(b_{ij} \equiv \frac{2\pi}{3}(R_i+R_j)^3\). They fixed the hard-core radius of baryons to \(R_b = 0.5\) fm and that of all other mesons to \(R_m = 0.3\) fm, then treated the pion radius \(R_\pi\) and kaon radius \(R_K\) as free fitting parameters. The model was fitted to mid-rapidity hadron yield ratios from AGS, SPS (NA49 data), and RHIC (STAR data) energies using a \(\chi^2/\text{dof}\) criterion, with temperature \(T\), baryonic chemical potential \(\mu_B\), and isospin chemical potential \(\mu_{I3}\) as free parameters per energy.

## Key numbers

* The best global fit achieved \(\chi^2/\text{dof} \simeq 1.018\) for collision energies \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.7, 3.3, 3.8, 4.3, 4.9, 6.3, 7.6, 8.8, 12, 17, 130, 200\) GeV.
* The optimal hard-core radii were \(R_\pi = 0\) fm (vanishing) and \(R_K = 0.35\) fm.
* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17.3\) GeV, the fit gave \(\chi^2/\text{dof} \simeq 1.57\), compared to \(\chi^2/\text{dof} \simeq 2.78\) from the one-component model of Andronic (2005).
* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 4.9\) GeV, the best-fit parameters were \(T \simeq 131\) MeV, \(\mu_B \simeq 539\) MeV, \(\mu_{I3} \simeq -16\) MeV.
* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 17\) GeV, the best-fit parameters were \(T \simeq 147.6\) MeV, \(\mu_B \simeq 218\) MeV, \(\mu_{I3} \simeq -2.1\) MeV.
* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 130\) GeV, the best-fit parameters were \(T \simeq 163.1\) MeV, \(\mu_B \simeq 27.3\) MeV.
* At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the best-fit parameters were \(T \simeq 162.2\) MeV, \(\mu_B \simeq 17.6\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadron resonance gas model of Andronic (2005) and the multi-component van der Waals approach of Bugaev and Oliinychenko (2012) by introducing independent hard-core radii for pions and kaons. It directly addresses the failure of previous thermal models (including those using a single hard-core radius or the Hagedorn mass spectrum approach of the Strangeness Horn sigma meson paper) to describe the \(K^+/\pi^+\) and \(\Lambda/\pi^-\) ratios simultaneously. The results enable a systematic extraction of second virial coefficients for hadron-hadron interactions and motivate improved experimental data at energies up to \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} \simeq 20\) GeV, which could be provided by facilities such as NICA and FAIR.
