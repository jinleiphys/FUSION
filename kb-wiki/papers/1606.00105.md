# Quantum corrections to the Relativistic mean-field theory

> In this paper, we compare the RMF theory and the model of deformed oscillator shells (DOS) in description of the quantum properties of the bound states of the spherically symmetric light nuclei. We obtain an explicit analytical relation between differential equations for the RMF theory and DOS model, which determine wave functions for nucleons. On such a basis we perform analysis of correspondence of quantum properties of nuclei. We find: (1) Potential $V_{RMF}$ of the RMF theory for nucleons has the wave functions $f$ and $g$ with joint part $h$ coincident exactly with the nucleon wave function of DOS model with potential $V_{\rm shell}$. But, a difference between $V_{RMF}$ and $V_{\rm shell}$ is essential for any nucleus. (2) The nucleon wave functions and densities obtained by the DOS and RMF theories are essentially different. The nucleon densities of the RMF theory contradict to knowledge about distribution of the proton and neutron densities inside the nuclei obtained from experimental data. This indicates that $g$ and $f$ have no sense of the wave functions of quantum physics. But, $h$ provides proper description of quantum properties of nucleons inside the nucleus. (3) We calculate meson function $w^{0}$ and potential $V_{w}$ in RMF theory based on the found nucleon density. (4) $f$ and $g$ are not solutions of Dirac equation with $V_{w}$. If the meson theory describes quantum properties of nucleus well, then a difference between $V_{w}$ and $V_{RMF}$ should be as small as possible. We introduce new quantum corrections characterizing difference between these potentials. We find that (a) The function $w^{0}$ should be reinforced strongly, (b) The corrections are necessary to describe the quantum properties of the nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the Relativistic Mean-Field (RMF) theory and the deformed oscillator shells (DOS) model can be linked analytically, but the nucleon wave functions and densities from RMF theory contradict experimental knowledge of proton and neutron distributions, indicating that the RMF wave function components \(g\) and \(f\) lack physical meaning as quantum wave functions; only the joint part \(h\) provides a proper quantum description, and new quantum corrections are necessary to reconcile the meson potential with the RMF potential.

## Method

The authors derived an explicit analytical relation between the differential equations of the RMF theory and the DOS model for spherically symmetric light nuclei, focusing on the ground state of \(^{4}\text{He}\). They transformed the Dirac equation for nucleons into a Schrodinger-type equation for a new wave function \(h(r)\) and used a series expansion approach to determine the RMF potential \(V_{RMF}\) from the shell-model potential \(\bar{V}\). They then calculated the meson function \(w^{0}\) and potential \(V_w\) from the nucleon density and introduced quantum corrections \(\Delta V(r)\) to quantify the difference between \(V_w\) and \(V_{RMF}\).

## Key numbers

- For \(^{4}\text{He}\) in the ground state, the shell model wave function \(h_1(r)\) has a maximum of \(8.31769\ \text{MeV}^{1/2}\) at \(r_{\text{max}} = 1.2024\ \text{fm}\).
- For \(^{4}\text{He}\) in the ground state, the RMF radial wave function \(g(r)\) has a maximum of \(r_{\text{max}} g(r_{\text{max}}) = 162.29505\ \text{MeV}^{1/2}\) at \(r_{\text{max}} = 4.64930\ \text{fm}\).
- For \(^{4}\text{He}\) in the ground state, the RMF radial wave function \(f(r)\) has a maximum of \(r_{\text{max}} f(r_{\text{max}}) = 8.73757 \times 10^{8}\ \text{MeV}^{1/2}\) at \(r_{\text{max}} = 4.32866\ \text{fm}\).
- The RMF nucleon density \(\rho_v(r)\) has its maximum at \(3.96794\ \text{fm}\).
- The intersection point of potentials \(V_1(r)\) and \(v_w(r)\) is at \(r_0 = \sqrt{3} a_r\), and for \(^{4}\text{He}\) the quantum correction factor is \(g_{\text{quant, w}} = 2.3864127 \times 10^{6}\).

## Context

This work extends the comparison between the relativistic mean-field theory (developed by Walecka, Ring, and others from the 1970s onward) and the microscopic deformed oscillator shells model (part of the resonating group method by Wheeler and developed by Steshenko and others since the 1970s). The paper contradicts the assumption that RMF wave functions \(g\) and \(f\) provide a valid quantum description of nucleons inside the nucleus, based on experimental data on nucleon densities from electron scattering and muon atoms. It enables future refinement of RMF theory by introducing quantum corrections to the meson potential, aiming to align it with the shell-model description of light nuclei.
