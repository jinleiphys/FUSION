# Full pf shell study of A = 47 and A = 49 nuclei

> Complete diagonalizations in the pf major shell, lead to very good agreement with the experimental data (level schemes, transitions rates, and static moments) for the A=47 and A=49 isotopes of Ca, Sc, Ti, V, Cr, and Mn. Gamow-Teller and M1 strength functions are calculated. The necessary monopole modifications to the realistic interactions are shown to be critically tested by the spectroscopic factors for one particle transfer from 48Ca, reproduced in detail by the calculations. The collective behaviour of 47Ti, and of the mirror pairs 47V-47Cr and 49Cr-49Mn is found to follow at low spins the particle plus rotor model. It is then analysed in terms of the approximate quasi-SU(3) symmetry, for which some new results are given.

## Key claim

Complete diagonalizations in the full pf shell produce very good agreement with experimental data for A=47 and A=49 nuclei, but the agreement is systematically less satisfactory for Ca isotopes, suggesting that a full understanding of these nuclei requires closer examination of intruder excitations from the 40Ca core.

## Method

The authors performed exact shell-model diagonalizations in the full pf major shell using the KB3 interaction, which is a realistic interaction with minimal monopole modifications to correct bad saturation properties. They used the Lanczos algorithm implemented in the code ANTOINE to calculate level schemes, electromagnetic transitions and moments, Gamow-Teller and M1 strength functions, and spectroscopic factors for one-particle transfer from 48Ca. The collective behavior was analyzed in terms of the particle-plus-rotor model and the approximate quasi-SU(3) symmetry.

## Key numbers

- Gap in 48Ca: KB3 gives 5.22 MeV (monopole estimate) and 5.17 MeV (exact calculation), compared to experimental value of 4.81 MeV
- Gap in 56Ni: KB3 gives 8.57 MeV (monopole estimate) and 7.90 MeV (t=4 truncation), compared to experimental value of 6.30 MeV
- KB3 overbinds all A=48 nuclei by approximately 780 keV
- For 47Ti, a scissors-like excitation is predicted with a 7/2- state at 2.34 MeV with total B(M1)=0.776 and a 3/2- state at 2.37 MeV with total B(M1)=0.370
- The ratio B_l/B_s for these scissors candidates is 1.0 and 0.7 using bare gyromagnetic factors
- For 49Sc, the unperturbed single-particle excitation energies from the monopole Hamiltonian are: epsilon(p3/2)=4.58 MeV, epsilon(p1/2)=5.99 MeV, epsilon(f5/2)=5.66 MeV
- The monopole modifications to KB involved: V_fr^T centroids shifted by 300 keV (KB'), V_ff^0 shifted by -350 keV and V_ff^1 shifted by -110 keV (KB1), plus minor non-monopole changes of -300 keV for J=1,3 and -200 keV for J=2,1 (KB3)

## Context

This work extends the full pf-shell study of A=48 nuclei (Martinez-Pinedo et al., 1996, referred to as I) to the neighboring odd-A nuclei A=47 and A=49. It builds on the earlier Kuo-Brown interaction (KB) and its modified version KB3 developed by Pasquini, Zuker, Poves, and collaborators. The paper demonstrates that the monopole modifications necessary to cure the realistic interaction can be critically tested by spectroscopic factors for one-particle transfer from 48Ca. The collective behavior of 47Ti and the mirror pairs 47V-47Cr and 49Cr-49Mn is analyzed in terms of quasi-SU(3) symmetry, for which new results are presented. The work enables future studies of nuclei beyond A=60, though the authors warn that KB3 should not be relied upon above approximately A=60 due to poor single-hole properties around 79Zr, as pointed out by Brown and Ormand.
