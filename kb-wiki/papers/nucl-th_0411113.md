# Parity and spin of the $ﾎ録+$ pentaquark in the $NN\to Yﾎ録+$ reaction at the threshold

> Spin observables of a binary reaction $1+2\to 3+4$ are discussed at the threshold in general form using P-parity and angular momentum conservation. General formulae for polarization transfer, spin-spin correlation parameters in the initial and final states and induced tensor polarization are derived for arbitrary spins of participating particles. This formalism is worked out in detail for the $NN\to Yﾎ録+$ reaction. Assuming that the spin of the pentaquark $ﾎ録+$ takes the values 1/2, 3/2 and 5/2, whereas the spin of the hyperon $Y$ equals 1/2, explicite formulae are obtained for the observables in terms of few non-vanishing at the threshold spin amplitudes separately for the spin-singlet and spin-triplet initial NN states. In case of all particles in the $NN\to Yﾎ録+$ reaction have the spin-1/2 a full spin-structure for totally polarized cross sections is derived. Some of the obtained spin observables strongly depend on the intrinsic P-parity of the $ﾎ録+$ and the total isospin of the reaction. Therefore, a measurement of these observables allows one to determine the P-parity of the pentaquark $ﾎ録+$ in a model independent way for any spin of the $ﾎ録+$.

## Key claim

The intrinsic P-parity of the pentaquark $\Theta^+$ can be determined in a model-independent way by measuring spin observables (spin-spin correlation parameters $C_{i,j}$, spin-transfer coefficients $K_i^j$, or induced tensor polarization) in the $NN\to Y\Theta^+$ reaction near threshold, for any assumed spin of the $\Theta^+$ (1/2, 3/2, or 5/2), provided the total isospin of the reaction is fixed and no isospin mixing occurs.

## Method

The author derived general formulas for spin observables of a binary reaction $1+2\to 3+4$ at threshold using partial-wave expansion, P-parity conservation, and angular momentum conservation, expressed in terms of a few non-vanishing spin amplitudes $a_J^{LS}$. These formulas were then applied specifically to the $NN\to Y\Theta^+$ reaction, with the hyperon $Y$ having spin 1/2 and the $\Theta^+$ having spin 1/2, 3/2, or 5/2, separately for spin-singlet and spin-triplet initial NN states. A non-standard method was also developed to derive elegant formulas for the initial spin-spin correlation parameters.

## Key numbers

- For $j_\Theta = 1/2$, $\pi = -1$, $T=1$: $C_{y,y} = |a_0^1|^2 / (|a_0^1|^2 + 3|a_1^1|^2)$
- For $j_\Theta = 1/2$, $\pi = +1$, $T=0$: $C_{y,y} = |a_1^0 - \sqrt{2}a_1^2|^2 / (3|a_1^0|^2 + 3|a_1^2|^2)$
- For $j_\Theta = 3/2$, $\pi = -1$, $T=1$: $C_{y,y} = |\sqrt{2}a_2^1 - \sqrt{3}a_2^3|^2 / (3|a_1^1|^2 + 5|a_2^1|^2 + 5|a_2^3|^2)$
- For $j_\Theta = 3/2$, $\pi = +1$, $T=0$: $C_{y,y} = |a_1^0 - \sqrt{2}a_1^2|^2 / (3|a_1^0|^2 + 3|a_1^2|^2 + 5|a_2^2|^2)$
- For $j_\Theta = 5/2$, $\pi = -1$, $T=1$: $C_{y,y} = |\sqrt{2}a_2^1 - \sqrt{3}a_2^3|^2 / (5|a_2^1|^2 + 5|a_2^3|^2 + 7|a_3^3|^2)$
- For $j_\Theta = 5/2$, $\pi = +1$, $T=0$: $C_{y,y} = |\sqrt{3}a_3^2 - 2a_3^4|^2 / (5|a_2^2|^2 + 7|a_3^2|^2 + 7|a_3^4|^2)$
- For the spin-singlet initial state ($S=0$): $C_{x,x} = C_{y,y} = C_{z,z} = -1$ and all polarization transfer coefficients $K_i^j = 0$
- For the spin-triplet initial state ($S=1$): $C_{z,z} = 1 - 2C_{y,y}$

## Context

This work extends previous model-independent methods for determining the $\Theta^+$ parity (Thomas et al. 2004, Hanhart et al. 2004, Uzikov 2004, Rekalo and Gakh 2004) from the spin-1/2 assumption to arbitrary spins of the $\Theta^+$ (1/2, 3/2, 5/2). The formalism generalizes the approach of Bilenky (1964) for spin-tensor operators to any binary reaction near threshold. The results enable experimental determination of the $\Theta^+$ parity through double-spin measurements (spin-spin correlations or polarization transfer) or single-spin measurements of tensor polarization induced by a polarized beam, without relying on specific production models.
