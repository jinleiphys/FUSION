# Constituent Quarks and Gluons, Polyakov loop and the Hadron Resonance Gas Model

> Based on first principle QCD arguments, it has been argued in arXiv:1204.2424[hep-ph] that the vacuum expectation value of the Polyakov loop can be represented in the hadron resonance gas model. We study this within the Polyakov-constituent quark model by implementing the quantum and local nature of the Polyakov loop hep-ph/0412308, hep-ph/0607338. The existence of exotic states in the spectrum is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Polyakov loop expectation value can be represented within the Hadron Resonance Gas Model (HRGM), but only when the quantum and local nature of the Polyakov loop is implemented beyond the mean-field approximation, and this representation necessarily includes contributions from exotic multiparton states such as quark-gluon hybrids.

## Method

The authors study the Polyakov-constituent quark model by treating the Polyakov loop as a quantum and local degree of freedom, integrating over the SU(3) gauge group at each spatial point. They decompose space into independent confinement domains of volume V_sigma = 8 pi T^3 / sigma^3, where sigma is the string tension, and perform a series expansion of the quark and gluon actions to identify hadronic states as color-singlet clusters of constituent quarks, antiquarks, and gluons with Boltzmann factors e^{-nM/T}.

## Key numbers

* The confinement domain volume is V_sigma = 8 pi T^3 / sigma^3, where sigma is the string tension.
* Quark Boltzmann factors behave as e^{-M_q/T} for single quarks, mesonic contributions as e^{-2M_q/T}, and baryonic contributions as e^{-3M_q/T} (for N_c = 3).
* The partition function expansion includes terms up to order lambda^4, with degeneracy factors such as 1/2 for [q\bar{q}] mesons, 1/6 g_f(g_f+1)(g_f+2) for baryons [q^3] where g_f = 2 N_f, and 2 g_f^2 for [q\bar{q}g] hybrids.
* The Polyakov loop expectation value at leading order is lambda \bar{Q}_1, corresponding to a heavy quark source screened by one antiquark [h\bar{q}].

## Context

This work extends the hadronic representation of the Polyakov loop proposed by Megias (2012) by implementing the quantum and local features of the Polyakov loop developed in earlier work by Megias, Ruiz Arriola, and Salcedo (2004, 2006). The model provides a diagnostic tool for lattice QCD computations of the Polyakov loop at low temperature, where recent lattice data from Borsanyi (2010) and Bazavov (2013) suggest that conventional hadrons alone are insufficient, potentially signaling the existence of exotic multiparton states such as hybrids, tetraquarks, and pentaquarks.
