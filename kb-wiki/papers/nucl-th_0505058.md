# Determination of π^0 meson quadrupole polarizabilities from the process γγ-> π^0π^0

> A fit of the experimental data to the total cross section of the process γγ->π^0π^0 in the energy region from 270 to 2250 MeV has been carried out using dispersion relations for the invariant amplitudes where the quadrupole polarizabilities are free parameters. As a result the sum and difference of the electric and magnetic quadrupole polarizabilities of the π^0 meson have been found for the first time: (\alpha2+\beta2)_π^0=(-0.181+-0.004)*10^{-4}fm^5, (\alpha2-\beta2)_pi^0=(39.70+-0.02)*10^{-4}fm^5. In addition, dispersion sum rules have been constructed for this sum and difference, respectively. The values of (\alpha2-\beta2)_π^0 and (\alpha2+\beta2)_π^0 extracted from the experimental data on the process γγ->π^0π^0 are in good agreement with the result of calculations in the framework of these dispersion sum rules.

## Key claim

The sum and difference of the electric and magnetic quadrupole polarizabilities of the π⁰ meson have been determined for the first time from a fit to the total cross section of γγ → π⁰π⁰, using dispersion relations where these polarizabilities are free parameters; the extracted values agree with the authors' newly constructed dispersion sum rules.

## Method

The authors performed a fit of experimental data for the total cross section of γγ → π⁰π⁰ in the energy region from 270 to 2250 MeV using dispersion relations at fixed t with one subtraction for the invariant helicity amplitudes M++ and M+−. The subtraction functions were determined by dispersion relations in the cross channels with two subtractions, where the subtraction constants were expressed through the dipole and quadrupole polarizabilities of the π⁰ meson. The fit treated the sum (α₂+β₂) and difference (α₂−β₂) of the quadrupole polarizabilities as free parameters, along with the effective radius and two-photon decay width of the f₂(1270) meson.

## Key numbers

*   Sum of quadrupole polarizabilities: (α₂+β₂)_π⁰ = (−0.181 ± 0.004) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵.
*   Difference of quadrupole polarizabilities: (α₂−β₂)_π⁰ = (39.70 ± 0.02) × 10⁻⁴ fm⁵.
*   Effective radius of the f₂(1270) meson: r_f = 0.96 ± 0.01 fm.
*   Two-photon decay width of the f₂(1270) meson: Γ_{f₂→γγ} = 3.05 ± 0.11 keV.
*   The fit covered the energy region √t = 270 to 2250 MeV, using data from Marsiske et al. and Bienlein et al.

## Context

This work extends prior analyses of the γγ → π⁰π⁰ process that determined the dipole polarizabilities of the π⁰ meson (Fil'kov and Kashevarov, 2005; Kaloshin and Serebryakov, 1995) by including quadrupole polarizabilities as free parameters, which resolved a discrepancy between model predictions and experimental data at energies above 1700 MeV. The quadrupole polarizabilities were also investigated using dispersion sum rules, with results consistent with the experimental extraction. The study enables future tests of chiral perturbation theory predictions for higher-order pion polarizabilities and provides constraints for models of meson structure.
