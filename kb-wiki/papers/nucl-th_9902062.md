# Hadronic freeze-out following a first order hadronization phase transition in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We analyze the hadronic freeze-out in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC in a transport approach which combines hydrodynamics for the early, dense, deconfined stage of the reaction with a microscopic non-equilibrium model for the later hadronic stage at which the hydrodynamic equilibrium assumptions are not valid. With this ansatz we are able to self-consistently calculate the freeze-out of the system and determine space-time hypersurfaces for individual hadron species. The space-time domains of the freeze-out for several hadron species are found to be actually four-dimensional, and differ drastically for the individual hadrons species. Freeze-out radii distributions are similar in width for most hadron species, even though the Omega-baryon is found to be emitted rather close to the phase boundary and shows the smallest freeze-out radii and times among all baryon species. The total lifetime of the system does not change by more than 10% when going from SPS to RHIC energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that hadronic freeze-out in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC occurs in a four-dimensional space-time region rather than on a three-dimensional hypersurface, and that the freeze-out characteristics differ drastically between individual hadron species, with the total system lifetime changing by no more than 10% when going from SPS to RHIC energies.

## Method

The authors combine boost-invariant hydrodynamics with a first-order phase transition from a quark-gluon plasma to a hadronic fluid, using the MIT bag model equation of state above T_C=160 MeV and an ideal hadron gas below T_C. The hadron spectra from the Cooper-Frye formula on the hadronization hypersurface are used as initial conditions for the microscopic non-equilibrium transport model UrQMD, which handles the later hadronic stage including resonance decays, scattering, and detailed balance. The freeze-out space-time points are determined self-consistently by the interplay between the local expansion scalar, elementary cross sections, and the dynamically changing equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Initial average energy density: 20 GeV/fm^3; initial average baryon density: 2.3 times nuclear matter density
- Bag constant: B = 380 MeV/fm^3
- Initial transverse energy at midrapidity: dE_T/dy = 1.3 TeV; decreases to 716 GeV on the hadronization hypersurface and to 714 GeV at kinetic freeze-out
- Average freeze-out radii: pions 9.5 fm, kaons 10.2 fm, protons 11.3 fm, Lambda/Sigma hyperons 11.6 fm, cascades 14.2 fm, Omega baryons 7.3 fm
- Average freeze-out times: pions 23.1 fm/c, protons 25.8 fm/c (with hadronic reinteractions); pions 17.2 fm/c, protons 11.3 fm/c (without hadronic reinteractions)
- Thickness of hadronic phase: pions 1.7 fm, kaons 3.1 fm, protons/lambda/sigma/cascades 5.8 fm, Omega 2.6 fm
- Duration of hadronic reinteraction phase: pions 5.9 fm/c, kaons 8.0 fm/c, protons 14.5 fm/c, hyperons 15.4 fm/c, Omega 8.0 fm/c
- Hadron multiplicity changes due to rescattering: pions +9.3%, kaons -5%, hyperons +12%, protons -21%, antiprotons -31%, antihyperons +11%
- Chemical freeze-out time: approximately 25 fm/c (upper estimate)
- Kinetic freeze-out time: approximately 35 fm/c

## Context

This work extends prior hydrodynamic approaches by Rischke (1996), Dumitru (1999), and Hung (1998) by replacing the ad-hoc freeze-out hypersurface with a self-consistent calculation using microscopic transport. It contradicts the assumption of a unique freeze-out hypersurface for all hadrons used in earlier hydrodynamic models, and finds results similar to other microscopic transport models by Grassi (1997) and Pratt (1998) at lower AGS and SPS energies. The approach enables future studies of how the first-order phase transition affects freeze-out observables, particularly HBT parameters, and allows comparison with parton cascade model results by Geiger (1996) and Bass (1999).
