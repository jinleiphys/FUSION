# Duality in Inclusive Semileptonic Heavy Quark Decay

> I identify a source of Lambda_{QCD}/m_Q corrections to the assumption of quark-hadron duality in the application of heavy quark methods to inclusive heavy quark semileptonic decays Q->q l nu_l. These corrections could substantially affect the accuracy of such methods in practical applications and in particular compromise their utility for the extraction of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa matrix element V_{cb}.

# Duality in Inclusive Semileptonic Heavy Quark Decay

## Key claim

The paper identifies a source of $\Lambda_{QCD}/m_Q$ corrections to quark-hadron duality in inclusive heavy quark semileptonic decays, which could substantially affect the accuracy of heavy quark effective theory (HQET) methods for extracting $V_{cb}$, despite the operator product expansion (OPE) suggesting leading corrections of order $\Lambda_{QCD}^2/m_Q^2$. The duality-violating effects arise from hadronic thresholds that delay the compensation between elastic and inelastic channels, and these effects may be larger than previously assumed.

## Method

The author uses a simplified scalar field model of $b \rightarrow c \phi$ decay to compare the inclusive quark-level decay rate with the sum over exclusive hadronic channels, employing harmonic oscillator wavefunctions from the ISGW quark model to compute transition form factors. The analysis examines the ratio $R \equiv \sum_n \Gamma(B \rightarrow D^{(n)} \phi) / \Gamma(b \rightarrow c \phi)$ as a function of the scaled energy release $T^*$, truncating the sum over resonances to study the approach to duality. The author also constructs models incorporating hybrid mesons and nonresonant states with a high-mass tail characterized by a constituent quark size scale $1/r_q \sim 2$ GeV.

## Key numbers

- About 65% of the $X_c$ spectrum in $\bar B \rightarrow X_c \ell \bar \nu_\ell$ is due to the narrow ground states $D$ and $D^*$; the $D_2^*(2460)$ and $D_1(2420)$ account for perhaps another 5% of the rate.
- The gap $\Delta \equiv m_{D^{**}} - m_D \simeq 500$ MeV covers more than one third of the Dalitz plot for $b \rightarrow c$ decays, where $(w-1)_{max} \simeq 0.6$.
- The baseline values for the model are $\epsilon = 1/6$ and $T^*_{max} = 6$, corresponding to $(m_b - m_c)/\Delta \sim 6$ and $\rho^2 \sim 1$.
- The constituent quark size scale is estimated as $1/r_q \sim 2$ GeV, based on spectroscopic arguments.
- The hybrid mass spectrum begins about 1.5 GeV above the ground state, providing a minimum value for $\bar t^*_{hybrid}$.
- Model calculations show duality-violation of the order of 10% could easily arise in $b \rightarrow c$ decays from the delayed onset of resonances with scale $\Delta \simeq 500$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the ongoing debate about quark-hadron duality in inclusive heavy quark decays, building on the foundational work by Shifman and Voloshin (1987-1988) on the small velocity limit and the Bjorken sum rule (Bjorken, 1990). The paper challenges the prevailing view from Bigi, Uraltsev, Shifman, Vainshtein, and others (1992-1997) that OPE-based inclusive calculations are accurate up to $\Lambda_{QCD}^2/m_Q^2$ corrections, arguing that hadronic threshold effects introduce $\Lambda_{QCD}/m_Q$ corrections that must be better understood before inclusive methods can be applied with confidence to extract $V_{cb}$. The analysis also connects to the long-standing $\bar B$ semileptonic branching ratio puzzle and suggests that similar duality violations would affect Cabibbo-forbidden charm decays, where the OPE predicts identical rates for $D^0$ and $D^+$ that simple isospin arguments show cannot hold.
