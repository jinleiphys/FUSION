# Heavy-flavor dynamics in nucleus-nucleus collisions: from RHIC to LHC

> The stochastic dynamics of c and b quarks in the fireball created in nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC and LHC is studied employing a relativistic Langevin equation, based on a picture of multiple uncorrelated random collisions with the medium. Heavy-quark transport coefficients are evaluated within a pQCD approach, with a proper HTL resummation of medium effects for soft scatterings. The Langevin equation is embedded in a multi-step setup developed to study heavy-flavor observables in pp and AA collisions, starting from a NLO pQCD calculation of initial heavy-quark yields, complemented in the nuclear case by shadowing corrections, k_T-broadening and nuclear geometry effects. Then, only for AA collisions, the Langevin equation is solved numerically in a background medium described by relativistic hydrodynamics. Finally, the propagated heavy quarks are made hadronize and decay into electrons. Results for the nuclear modification factor R_AA of heavy-flavor hadrons and electrons from their semi-leptonic decays are provided, both for RHIC and LHC beam energies.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a weak-coupling pQCD-based Langevin model, including both hard and soft (HTL-resummed) scatterings, can reproduce the nuclear modification factor R_AA of heavy-flavor electrons measured at RHIC for pT above about 3 GeV/c, but the model underestimates the elliptic flow v2 data, suggesting that additional mechanisms (such as coalescence hadronization) are needed at lower pT.

## Method

The authors simulate heavy-quark (c and b) propagation in the quark-gluon plasma using a relativistic Langevin equation with transport coefficients kappa_T and kappa_L computed from pQCD with a separation of hard and soft scatterings via an intermediate cutoff, where soft collisions are treated with Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) resummation. Initial heavy-quark yields are generated at NLO pQCD using POWHEG with CTEQ6M PDFs, including EPS09 nuclear shadowing, kT-broadening, and nuclear geometry for AA collisions; the Langevin evolution is then solved numerically in a background medium described by viscous relativistic hydrodynamics, and final heavy quarks are fragmented using Peterson functions and decayed into electrons via PYTHIA.

## Key numbers

- RHIC Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: R_AA of heavy-flavor electrons in minimum-bias events (0-92% inelastic cross section) agrees with PHENIX data for pT >= 3 GeV/c
- RHIC centrality dependence: R_AA integrated over electron yields above given pT is reproduced as a function of N_part except for the most-peripheral centrality bin
- LHC Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: predictions for R_AA of heavy-flavor electrons and D, B mesons for 0-10% most-central events show stronger suppression than at RHIC
- For LHC, the HTL2 calculation (mu = |t|) yields weaker quenching for pT > 3 GeV/c compared to HTL1 (mu proportional to T)
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: epsilon = 0.04 for c quarks, epsilon = 0.005 for b quarks
- Langevin simulation starts at proper time tau_0 = 1 fm/c for the HTL1 calculation shown

## Context

This work extends the Langevin-based approach to heavy-quark energy loss previously developed by the same group (Monteno et al., 2009; 2011), providing a full multi-step setup from initial production to final decay electrons. It addresses the difficulty faced by models based solely on medium-induced gluon radiation (DjoGyu, ASW) in reproducing RHIC heavy-flavor electron suppression, and supports the importance of collisional energy loss as emphasized by other groups (vHGR, bamps). The results provide a weak-coupling benchmark for comparison with first LHC Pb-Pb data from ALICE and CMS, and the authors note that incorporating coalescence hadronization should improve the description of both v2 and R_AA at pT below 3 GeV/c.
