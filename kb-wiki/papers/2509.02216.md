# Softening holographic nuclear matter

> Baryons in the holographic Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model are described by instanton solutions on the flavor branes. A commonly used approximation for dense baryonic matter replaces the many-instanton solution by a simpler, spatially homogeneous, ansatz, which requires a discontinuity in the holographic direction of the non-abelian gauge field in order to account for topological baryon number. We point out that the simplest configuration with a single jump - often used in previous studies - results in matter at saturation density that is much stiffer than real-world nuclear matter. This is improved, although not completely remedied, by adding a second jump. We present a systematic discussion of all possible configurations up to four jumps, dynamically computing locations of and behavior at the discontinuities. We find solutions that continuously connect to those based on pointlike baryons, thus, for the first time, establishing a concrete link between the instantonic and homogeneous pictures. This is supported by translating the multi-jump profiles of the gauge field into gauge invariant multi-layer charge distributions. The most important of our novel configurations has a block-like structure in the bulk, becomes pointlike at low density and/or large coupling, and is energetically preferred over all previously studied configurations. Therefore, our work lays the ground for improved predictions from holography for dense nuclear matter in neutron stars.

## Key claim
The commonly used single-jump homogeneous ansatz for dense baryonic matter in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model produces matter at saturation density that is much stiffer than real-world nuclear matter; this is improved, but not completely remedied, by adding a second jump. The authors systematically find new multi-jump configurations that, for the first time, establish a concrete link between the instantonic and homogeneous pictures, with one novel block-like configuration being energetically preferred over all previously studied ones.

## Method
The authors work within the homogeneous ansatz for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, using the Yang-Mills approximation for the SU(2) gauge theory on the flavor branes with a Chern-Simons term. They systematically classify and solve for all possible stationary configurations of the non-abelian gauge field \(h(u)\) with up to four discontinuities in the holographic direction, dynamically computing the locations of and behavior at the discontinuities by minimizing the thermodynamic potential. The resulting multi-jump profiles are translated into gauge-invariant multi-layer charge distributions to connect to the instantonic picture.

## Key numbers
- The 1-jump phase yields an incompressibility \(K_0 \approx 3000\) MeV, which is much larger than the real-world value of approximately 240 MeV.
- The 2-jump \(D_L\) phase yields an incompressibility \(K_0 \approx 1000\) MeV, an improvement over the 1-jump phase but still significantly stiffer than real-world nuclear matter.
- The novel 3-jump \(D_{0L}\) configuration (with a jump at \(z=0\) and one \(L\)-type jump on each half) and the 4-jump \(D_{RL}\) configuration (with one \(R\)-type and one \(L\)-type jump on each half) are found to be energetically preferred over all previously studied configurations.
- The dimensionless baryon density is given by \(\bar{n}_B = \frac{3\lambda_0}{4}\left[\sum_{k=1}^d (h_{k-}^3-h_{k+}^3)-h_c^3\right]\), where \(h_c\) is the value at the tip and \(h_{k\pm}\) are the values at the discontinuities.

## Context
This work extends the homogeneous approach to dense baryonic matter in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, pioneered by Rozali (2007) and pursued by Li (2015), Bartolini (2022), and CruzRojas (2023), by systematically including configurations with up to four jumps in the non-abelian gauge field. It directly addresses the stiffness problem of the single-jump phase and provides a concrete link to pointlike baryon descriptions (Bergman, 2007) and multi-layer instantonic configurations (Kaplunovsky, 2012; Preis, 2016). The results lay the groundwork for improved holographic predictions for dense nuclear matter in neutron stars, enabling a more realistic comparison with phenomenological models and perturbative QCD.
