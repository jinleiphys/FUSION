# Probing nuclear symmetry energy with the sub-threshold pion production

> Within the framework of semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model, we investigated the effects of symmetry energy on the sub-threshold pion using the isospin MDI interaction with the stiff and soft symmetry energies in the central collision of $^{48}$Ca + $^{48}$Ca at the incident beam energies of 100, 150, 200, 250 and 300 MeV/nucleon, respectively. We find that the ratio of $π^{-}/π^{+}$ of sub-threshold charged pion production is greatly sensitive to the symmetry energy, particularly around 100 MeV/nucleon energies. Large sensitivity of sub-threshold charged pion production to nuclear symmetry energy may reduce uncertainties of probing nuclear symmetry energy via heavy-ion collision.

## Key claim

The ratio of sub-threshold charged pion production (\(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\)) in central \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{48}\)Ca collisions is particularly sensitive to the nuclear symmetry energy at lower incident beam energies, with sensitivity reaching about 100% at 100 MeV/nucleon; however, the authors note that this qualitative trend (soft symmetry energy giving larger \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratios) is inconsistent with the results of Feng et al. using the ImIQMD model, which found the opposite dependence.

## Method

The authors used the semiclassical isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU04) transport model, including nucleons, \(\Delta\) and \(N^{*}\) resonances, and pions with isospin-dependent dynamics. They simulated central collisions of \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{48}\)Ca at incident beam energies of 100, 150, 200, 250, and 300 MeV/nucleon, employing the momentum-dependent single nucleon potential (MDI) with two parameter choices (\(x=1\) for soft symmetry energy and \(x=-1\) for stiff symmetry energy). The in-medium elastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections were reduced from free-space values by a factor of \((\mu_{NN}^{*}/\mu_{NN})^{2}\), while free inelastic cross sections were used for \(NN \rightarrow NR\) channels.

## Key numbers

- The maximal baryon density reached in the central reaction \(^{48}\)Ca+\(^{48}\)Ca is about 1.5 times saturation density at 100 MeV/nucleon and about 2 times saturation density at 300 MeV/nucleon.
- At 150 MeV/nucleon, the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio is sensitive to the symmetry energy for pion kinetic energies of 30–120 MeV, with a sensitivity of about 80%.
- At 100 MeV/nucleon, the sensitivity of the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio to the symmetry energy reaches a maximum of about 100%.
- The symmetry energy at normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_{0}=0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is about 32 MeV, and the incompressibility of symmetric nuclear matter is set to 211 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion production as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy (Yong 2006, Xiao 2009, Feng) by focusing on sub-threshold pion production (below the free-space threshold energy) rather than above-threshold production. The authors find that the sensitivity of the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio to the symmetry energy increases with decreasing beam energy, which they attribute to the stronger mean-field effect at lower energies. However, they note a qualitative inconsistency with the results of Feng et al. using the ImIQMD model, where the excitation function of the \(\pi^{-}/\pi^{+}\) ratio increased with the stiffness of the symmetry energy, opposite to the trend found here. This discrepancy requires further clarification and may motivate future experimental measurements at facilities such as NSCL, GSI, RIKEN, and CSR in Lanzhou.
