# Kaon condensation in skyrmion matter and compact stars

> We address the possibility of the appearance of a charged kaon condensate in neutron star cores described within a generalized Skyrme model. Our treatment of strange degrees of freedom is based on the Bound State Approach by Callan and Klebanov, which allows to obtain an in-medium effective potential for the $s$-wave kaon condensate. We predict the onset of kaon condensation at a certain threshold density - whose value depends on the parameters of the model, and ranges between 1.5 and 2.5 times saturation density -, and obtain both the particle fractions and equation of state for dense matter in the kaon condensed phase. Finally, we discuss the effect of such condensates on the mass-radius curves and other observable properties of neutron stars with kaon condensed cores.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that charged kaon condensation in neutron star cores occurs at a threshold density between 1.5 and 2.5 times nuclear saturation density, but this range is explicitly parameter-dependent and the actual onset density depends on the choice of coupling constants in the generalized Skyrme model.

## Method

The authors use a generalized Skyrme model with four terms (quadratic, quartic, sextic, and pion mass) to describe dense nuclear matter as a periodic skyrmion crystal with face-centered cubic symmetry. Strange degrees of freedom are incorporated via the Callan-Klebanov Bound State Approach, treating kaon fluctuations on top of an SU(2) skyrmion background, and a mean-field approximation is applied to obtain an in-medium effective potential for the s-wave kaon condensate. The system of equations for charge-neutral beta-equilibrated matter (including neutrons, protons, electrons, muons, and kaons) is solved to find particle fractions and the equation of state, which is then used in the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations to compute neutron star mass-radius curves.

## Key numbers

- Threshold density for kaon condensation: between 1.5 and 2.5 times nuclear saturation density (parameter-dependent)
- Pion mass: 140 MeV (fixed physical value)
- Symmetry energy at saturation density: approximately 30 MeV (quoted from literature, not computed in this paper)
- Baryon number per unit cell: 4 (for the FCC crystal with side length 2L)
- Half-skyrmion baryon content: 1/2 per simple cubic sub-cell

## Context

This work extends previous applications of the Skyrme model to neutron stars (Adam 2020, Adam 2021, Adam 2022) by incorporating strange degrees of freedom via kaon condensation, which had been studied in other approaches such as those by Kaplan (1987) and Glendenning (1997). The Bound State Approach for kaons in the Skyrme model was originally developed by Callan and Klebanov (1985) and Klebanov (1990) for hyperons, and is here applied for the first time to crystalline skyrmion matter. The results enable predictions for how kaon condensates affect neutron star observables such as mass-radius relations, and can be compared with constraints from NICER and gravitational-wave observations.
