# Chiral Lagrangians at finite density

> The effective SU(2) chiral Lagrangian with external sources is given in the presence of non-vanishing nucleon densities by calculating the in-medium contributions of the chiral pion-nucleon Lagrangian. As a by product, a relativistic quantum field theory for Fermi many-particle systems at zero temperature is directly derived from relativistic quantum field theory with functional methods.

# Chiral Lagrangians at finite density

## Key claim
The paper derives the effective SU(2) chiral Lagrangian with external sources in the presence of non-vanishing nucleon densities by calculating in-medium contributions from the chiral pion-nucleon Lagrangian, but the derivation relies on the adiabatic hypothesis to determine the interacting ground state from the free case by turning on interactions adiabatically, and the formalism is restricted to zero temperature.

## Method
The author starts from the transition amplitude between two ground states representing symmetric Fermi seas of free protons and neutrons, then uses functional methods to evaluate the generating functional in the presence of external vector, axial, scalar, and pseudoscalar sources. The in-medium contributions are obtained by expanding the logarithm of an operator defined in the subspace of Fermi sea states, leading to a double expansion: one in chiral powers of momenta and quark masses, and another in the number of insertions of on-shell fermions from the Fermi sea. The resulting Feynman rules treat vacuum and in-medium contributions separately, with Fermi-sea insertions represented by integrals over momenta up to the Fermi momentum.

## Key numbers
- Fermi momentum: \( k_F = (3\pi^2 \rho/2)^{1/3} \) for symmetric nuclear matter, where \( \rho \) is the total nuclear density.
- For asymmetric nuclear matter: proton Fermi momentum \( k_F^{(p)} = (3\pi^2 \rho_p)^{1/3} \) and neutron Fermi momentum \( k_F^{(n)} = (3\pi^2 \rho_n)^{1/3} \).
- At nuclear saturation density, \( k_F \simeq 2 M_\pi \) where \( M_\pi \) is the pion mass, giving \( k_F \) a chiral counting of \( \mathcal{O}(p) \).
- The chiral scale is \( \Lambda_\chi \approx M_\rho \), the rho meson mass.

## Context
This work extends chiral perturbation theory, originally developed for vacuum QCD by Weinberg and Gasser and Leutwyler, to finite nucleon density at zero temperature, following earlier work on finite temperature at zero density by Gasser, Leutwyler, and collaborators. The formalism avoids the use of chemical potentials and the nontrivial zero-temperature limit associated with anomalous diagrams in finite-temperature field theory as discussed by Luttinger and by Negele. The paper provides a framework for evaluating in-medium QCD Green functions and enables systematic calculations of low-energy QCD quantities in nuclear matter, with applications presented in a companion paper (Oller, 2001). The approach also addresses the need to incorporate local multi-nucleon vertices to reabsorb ultraviolet divergences from pion loops, and notes the challenge of large S-wave scattering lengths in nucleon-nucleon scattering, which introduces an extra scale of about 10 MeV already in vacuum.
