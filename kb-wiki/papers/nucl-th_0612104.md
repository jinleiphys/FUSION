# Two-photon exchange at low Q^2

> We study two-photon exchange for elastic electron-proton scattering at low Q^2. Compact approximate formulae for the amplitudes are obtained. Numerical calculations are done for Q^2 < 0.1 GeV^2 with several realistic form factor parameterizations, yielding similar results. They indicate that the corrections to magnetic form factor can visibly affect cross-section and proton radii. For low-Q^2 electron-neutron scattering two-photon exchange corrections are shown to be negligibly small.

## Key claim

The paper derives compact approximate analytic formulas for two-photon exchange (TPE) amplitudes in elastic electron-proton scattering at low momentum transfer, showing that magnetic TPE corrections are large enough to visibly affect the cross-section and proton radii extraction, contrary to earlier numerical claims that TPE increases the charge radius.

## Method

The authors reduce the elastic TPE amplitude, previously expressed as a twofold integral over proton form factors (FFs), to closed analytic forms by expanding in the low-\(Q^2\) limit (\(Q^2 < 0.1\) GeV\(^2\)) and neglecting subleading terms of order \(\sqrt{-t}\) relative to the leading contributions. They integrate over the kinematic variable \(\beta\) analytically, leaving one-dimensional integrals over \(\alpha\) that depend on the Dirac and Pauli FFs \(F_1\) and \(F_2\). Numerical evaluations are performed using dipole FFs and checked against alternative FF parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- The calculation is valid for \(Q^2 < 0.1\) GeV\(^2\), specifically for \(-t/4m^2 \gg 1\), \(\sqrt{-t}/2M \ll 1\), and \((t/t_0)^2 \ll 1\) where \(t_0 \approx 0.3\) GeV\(^2\) for the proton.
- At \(t=0\), the TPE amplitudes reduce to point-like results: \(\delta \tilde G_M = \mu \alpha \pi (\xi+1/2)/(\xi+1)\), \(\delta {\cal G}_M = \mu \alpha \pi (\xi+1/2)/(\xi^2+1)\), and \(\delta {\cal G}_E = \alpha \pi (1/2)/(\xi+1)\), with \(\mu = 2.79\).
- For electron-neutron scattering, TPE amplitudes are suppressed by a factor of \(\alpha t\), making them negligibly small at low \(Q^2\).
- Previous work by Rosen and Sick claimed TPE increases the charge radius by 0.008–0.013 fm and 0.0015 fm, respectively; the present results suggest TPE should instead lessen the proton rms radii.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Borisyuk and Kobushkin (2006) for elastic TPE contributions at low energies, providing the first closed analytic expressions for the TPE amplitudes \(\delta {\cal G}_E\), \(\delta {\cal G}_M\), and \(\delta \tilde G_M\) in the low-\(Q^2\) regime. It contradicts the numerical findings of Rosen and Sick regarding the sign of the TPE correction to the proton charge radius, and it enables more precise extraction of proton rms radii by accounting for magnetic TPE corrections that were previously neglected. The results also show that TPE corrections to electron-neutron scattering are negligible, simplifying future neutron form factor analyses.
