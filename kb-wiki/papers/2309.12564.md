# Generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method for induced fission dynamics

> The generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method (TD-GCM) is extended to include pairing correlations. The correlated GCM nuclear wave function is expressed in terms of time-dependent generator states and weight functions. The particle-hole channel of the effective interaction is determined by a Hamiltonian derived from an energy density functional, while pairing is treated dynamically in the standard BCS approximation with time-dependent pairing tensor and single-particle occupation probabilities. With the inclusion of pairing correlations, various time-dependent phenomena in open-shell nuclei can be described more realistically. The model is applied to the description of saddle-to-scission dynamics of induced fission. The generalized TDGCM charge yields and total kinetic energy distribution for the fission of 240Pu, are compared to those obtained using the standard time-dependent density functional theory (TD-DFT) approach, and with available data.

## Key claim
The generalized time-dependent generator coordinate method (TD-GCM) is extended to include dynamical pairing correlations via the BCS approximation, enabling a more realistic description of saddle-to-scission fission dynamics. The authors show that quantum fluctuations from the coherent superposition of TD-DFT trajectories can induce scission even when the single TD-DFT trajectory gets trapped in local minima and does not lead to scission.

## Method
The correlated GCM nuclear wave function is expressed as a superposition of time-dependent generator states and weight functions, where the generator states are obtained from deformation-constrained self-consistent mean-field equations and evolved using time-dependent density functional theory (TD-DFT) with the point-coupling relativistic energy density functional PC-PK1. Pairing is treated dynamically in the standard BCS approximation with time-dependent pairing tensor and single-particle occupation probabilities, using a monopole pairing interaction. The time-dependent kernels (overlap, Hamiltonian, and mean-field) are calculated using Pfaffian algorithms, and the collective wave function is evolved via the Hill-Wheeler equation.

## Key numbers
- Mesh spacing of the lattice: 1.0 fm for all directions
- Box size: Lx × Ly × Lz = 20 × 20 × 60 fm³
- Time step for evolution: 6.67 × 10⁻⁴ zs
- Pairing strength parameters: -0.135 MeV for neutrons, -0.230 MeV for protons, determined by empirical pairing gaps of ²⁴⁰Pu using the three-point odd-even mass formula
- Initial box size for deformation-constrained calculations: Lx × Ly × Lz = 20 × 20 × 50 fm³
- Scission occurs after approximately 1200 fm/c for the trajectory starting at (β₂₀, β₃₀) = (2.30, 1.13)
- The initial TD-GCM wave packet reaches the iso-energy contour after 30 zs

## Context
This work extends the generalized TD-GCM framework previously developed by Li et al. (2023) to include pairing correlations, and applies it to induced fission of ²⁴⁰Pu. It addresses limitations of the standard time-dependent GCM+DFT approach, which could not reproduce both fission yields and kinetic energy distributions at a quantitative level because quantum fluctuations were included in GCM but not in DFT trajectories. The approach contrasts with standard TD-DFT (which models classical evolution of independent nucleons and cannot account for quantum fluctuations) and with the adiabatic TD-GCM (which only considers collective degrees of freedom and cannot describe dissipative dynamics beyond the saddle point). This work enables fully quantum mechanical description of fission dynamics that includes both one-body dissipation and quantum fluctuations.
