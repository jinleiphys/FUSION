# A determination of the fragmentation functions of pions, kaons, and protons with faithful uncertainties

> We present NNFF1.0, a new determination of the fragmentation functions (FFs) of charged pions, charged kaons, and protons/antiprotons from an analysis of single-inclusive hadron production data in electron-positron annihilation. This determination, performed at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD, is based on the NNPDF methodology, a fitting framework designed to provide a statistically sound representation of FF uncertainties and to minimise any procedural bias. We discuss novel aspects of the methodology used in this analysis, namely an optimised parametrisation of FFs and a more efficient $χ^2$ minimisation strategy, and validate the FF fitting procedure by means of closure tests. We then present the NNFF1.0 sets, and discuss their fit quality, their perturbative convergence, and their stability upon variations of the kinematic cuts and the fitted dataset. We find that the systematic inclusion of higher-order QCD corrections significantly improves the description of the data, especially in the small-$z$ region. We compare the NNFF1.0 sets to other recent sets of FFs, finding in general a reasonable agreement, but also important differences. Together with existing sets of unpolarised and polarised parton distribution functions (PDFs), FFs and PDFs are now available from a common fitting framework for the first time.

## Key claim
The NNFF1.0 determination provides fragmentation functions (FFs) for charged pions, charged kaons, and protons/antiprotons with statistically faithful uncertainties, but the analysis is limited to single-inclusive hadron production data in electron-positron annihilation (SIA) and therefore carries little information on flavour separation, is scarcely sensitive to the gluon FF, and is completely blind to the separation between quark and antiquark FFs.

## Method
The analysis uses the NNPDF methodology, extended to FFs for the first time, to fit SIA data from experiments including BELLE, BABAR, TASSO, TPC, TOPAZ, ALEPH, DELPHI, OPAL, and SLD at leading, next-to-leading, and next-to-next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD. Methodological improvements include removing the preprocessing function from the neural network parametrization and using the CMA-ES family of algorithms for more efficient chi-squared minimization. The fitting framework was validated by closure tests.

## Key numbers
- The dataset includes 428 data points for charged pions, 385 for charged kaons, and 360 for protons/antiprotons after kinematic cuts.
- The bulk of the dataset consists of LEP and SLD measurements at sqrt(s) = 91.20 GeV and B-factory measurements at sqrt(s) approximately 10.5 GeV, with relative uncertainties at the level of a few percent.
- The kinematic coverage in z is limited roughly to the region 0.006 less than or similar to z less than or similar to 0.95.
- The default kinematic cut is z_min = 0.02, and the FF sets are tabulated in the range [10^{-2}:1] in z and [1:10000] GeV in Q.
- The initial scale for parametrization is Q_0 = 5 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the NNPDF fitting framework, previously used for unpolarised and polarised parton distribution functions (PDFs), to fragmentation functions for the first time, making FFs and PDFs available from a common fitting framework. The NNFF1.0 sets are compared to recent DEHSS (de Florian et al., 2014, 2017) and JAM (Sato et al., 2016) FFs for charged pions and kaons at NLO, finding general fair agreement with some noticeable differences mostly for the gluon FFs and their uncertainties. Future updates will include SIDIS data from COMPASS and HERMES and pp collision data from LHC and RHIC to improve flavour separation and gluon sensitivity.
