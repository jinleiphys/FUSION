# A Multi-Phase Transport model for nuclear collisions at RHIC

> To study heavy ion collisions at energies available from the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, we have developed a multi-phase transport model that includes both initial partonic and final hadronic interactions. Specifically, the parton cascade model ZPC, which uses as input the parton distribution from the HIJING model, is extended to include the quark-gluon to hadronic matter transition and also final-state hadronic interactions based on the ART model. Predictions of the model for central Au on Au collisions at RHIC are reported.

## Key claim

The AMPT model predicts that both partonic and hadronic evolution contribute appreciably to longitudinal collective flow in central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but the specific numerical results (e.g., transverse energy reduction, baryon rapidity shifts) depend on the chosen fragmentation scheme and the inclusion of final-state interactions, which the abstract does not quantify.

## Method

The model combines the HIJING event generator for initial parton distributions, the ZPC parton cascade for gluon-gluon elastic scatterings (with a constant screening mass of μ=3 fm⁻¹), a modified HIJING fragmentation scheme that includes diquark-antidiquark pair formation (with 80% BM\bar{B} probability), and the ART hadronic transport model extended to include nucleon-antinucleon annihilation, inelastic kaon/antikaon interactions, and neutral kaon production. Parton formation times are determined from a Lorentzian distribution with half-width t_f = E/m_T², and hadronization occurs after an additional proper time of about 1.2 fm.

## Key numbers

- Transverse energy rapidity density dE_T/dy at central rapidity: about 900 GeV in default HIJING, decreasing by about 100 GeV with modified fragmentation, further reduced by about 15 GeV after parton cascade, and by about 50 GeV after hadronic evolution.
- Net baryon rapidity distribution peak: AMPT gives peak value of 80 at y ~ 3.9; default HIJING gives peak value of 85 at y ~ 4.5.
- Net baryon number at central rapidity: about 12 in AMPT, similar to default HIJING.
- Antiproton survival: about 50% of antiprotons survive absorption in hadronic matter, giving about 10 antiprotons at central rapidities.
- \bar{p}/p ratio at central rapidity: about 60%, compared to 10% in Pb+Pb collisions at 158 GeV/nucleon from SPS.
- Comparison with Fritiof1.7: net baryon peak height about 80 in AMPT versus about 100 in Fritiof; Fritiof gives almost zero net baryon number at central rapidity.
- Comparison with Venus4.02: net baryon distribution peaks at y ~ 2.8 in Venus; π⁺ rapidity distribution peaks at central rapidity with height about 350 in AMPT and Fritiof, versus about 1300 in Venus.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING model (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) and the ZPC parton cascade (Zhang, 1998) by adding a hadronization transition and final-state hadronic interactions via the ART model (Li and Ko, 1995), creating a unified multi-phase transport framework. It enables quantitative predictions for RHIC observables such as transverse energy, baryon stopping, and antiproton yields, and provides a baseline for testing against upcoming RHIC data, while also motivating future studies of parton inelastic scatterings and alternative hadronization schemes.
