# Constituent quark number scaling from strange hadron spectra in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 13 TeV

> We show that the data of $p_{T}$ spectra of $Ω^{-}$ and $ϕ$ at midrapidity in inelastic events in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 13 TeV exhibit a constituent quark number scaling property, which is a clear signal of quark combination mechanism at hadronization. We use a quark combination model under equal velocity combination approximation to systematically study the production of identified hadrons in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$= 13 TeV. The midrapidity data of $p_{T}$ spectra of proton, $Λ$, $Ξ^{-}$, $Ω^{-}$, $ϕ$ and $K^{*}$ in inelastic events are simultaneously well fitted by the model. The data of multiplicity dependency of yields of these hadrons are also well understood. The strong $p_{T}$ dependence for data of $p/ϕ$ ratio is well explained by the model, which further suggests that the production of two hadrons with similar masses is determined by their quark contents at hadronization. $p_{T}$ spectra of strange hadrons at midrapidity in different multiplicity classes in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 13 TeV are predicted to further test the model in the future. The midrapidity $p_{T}$ spectra of soft ($p_T<2$ GeV/c) strange quark and up/down quark at hadronization in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 13 TeV are extracted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the $p_T$ spectra of $\Omega^{-}$ and $\phi$ in inelastic $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}=$ 13 TeV exhibit a constituent quark number scaling property, which is a clear signal of quark combination hadronization. However, this scaling holds well only in high multiplicity classes and in inelastic events at 13 TeV, while it is broken to a certain extent in low multiplicity classes and in inelastic events at 7 TeV.

## Method

The authors apply a quark combination model under the equal velocity combination approximation, where constituent quarks and antiquarks at hadronization combine with equal velocity to form baryons and mesons. The model uses the $p_T$ spectra of up/down and strange quarks extracted from the scaling property of $\Omega^{-}$ and $\phi$ data, then calculates the $p_T$ spectra of proton, $\Lambda$, $\Xi^{-}$, $\Omega^{-}$, $\phi$, and $K^{*0}$ at midrapidity in inelastic events and different multiplicity classes. The model also incorporates quark number fluctuations and threshold effects for strange hadron production when the average number of strange quarks is small.

## Key numbers

- The extracted strange quark $p_T$ distribution parameters: $a_s = 0.15$ GeV/c, $b_s = 0.649$, $n_s = 4.14$, $M_s = 0.5$ GeV, $c_s = 0.346$
- The extracted up/down quark $p_T$ distribution parameters: $a_u = 0.15$ GeV/c, $b_u = 0.355$, $n_u = 3.46$, $M_u = 0.33$ GeV, $c_u = 0.358$
- Quark numbers at hadronization in inelastic events: $\langle N_u \rangle = 2.8$ and $\langle N_s \rangle = 0.86$ for $|y| < 0.5$
- The fraction of baryons relative to mesons is taken as $N_B/N_M \approx 0.085$ at vanishing net-quarks
- Model parameters: $R_{V/P} = 0.45$ (vector to pseudoscalar meson ratio) and $R_{D/O} = 0.5$ (decuplet to octet baryon ratio)
- The suppression factor for strange quark distribution at $N_s \geq 3$ is $\gamma_s = 0.8$
- Constituent quark masses: $m_s = 500$ MeV, $m_u = m_d = 330$ MeV
- The scaling coefficient $\kappa_{\phi,\Omega}$ in inelastic events at 13 TeV is 1.5

## Context

This work extends the quark combination mechanism previously observed in $p$-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$ TeV by Song (2017) and in $pp$ collisions at 7 TeV by Gou (2017) to the higher energy of 13 TeV. The authors show that the quark number scaling property is better fulfilled at 13 TeV than at 7 TeV, indicating stronger evidence for quark combination hadronization at higher collision energies. The extracted quark $p_T$ spectra deviate from Boltzmann distribution at low $p_T$, suggesting thermalization may not be reached in the small partonic system created in $pp$ collisions. The model successfully explains the decreasing $p/\phi$ ratio with $p_T$ in $pp$ collisions, in contrast to the flat ratio observed in Pb-Pb collisions by Abelev (2014), and predicts $p_T$ spectra of strange hadrons in different multiplicity classes for future experimental tests.
