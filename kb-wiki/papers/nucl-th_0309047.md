# Can quark effects be observed in intermediate heavy ion collisions?

> In recent years a tentative description of the short-range part of hadron interactions with constituent quark interchange has been developed providing an alternative approach to meson physics. Quark interchange plays a role, for example, in the nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) phase-shifts and cross-section. In heavy ion collision simulations at intermediate energies one of the main features is the $NN$ cross-section in the collisional term, where in most cases it is an input adjusted to the free space value. In this paper we introduce the quark degrees of freedom to the $NN$ cross-section in the Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) model and explore the possibility that these effects appear in the observables at lower energies.

## Key claim

The paper explores whether quark substructure effects, introduced via a quark-interchange contribution to the nucleon-nucleon (NN) cross-section, can produce observable changes in heavy ion collision simulations at intermediate energies, but only in a qualitative, parameter-dependent manner using a simplified Gaussian wave-function and a single free parameter (the nucleon rms radius lambda).

## Method

The authors incorporate quark degrees of freedom into the NN cross-section within the Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (VUU) model using the Fock-Tani formalism, which transforms composite baryon operators into ideal fermion operators. The quark-quark interaction is taken as the spin-spin hyperfine component of one-gluon exchange, and the NN cross-section is evaluated analytically using Gaussian wave-functions for the nucleon. The resulting cross-section, which depends on the nucleon rms radius lambda, is then used in VUU simulations of Nb+Nb collisions at 1050 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter b=3 fm, using a hard equation of state (compression modulus k=380 MeV).

## Key numbers

* The nuclear ground state density is taken as rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.
* The Delta-nucleon mass splitting is set to 0.3 GeV.
* Four values of the nucleon rms radius lambda are used: 0.2, 0.3, 0.4, and 0.5 fm, corresponding to alpha_s/m_q^2 ratios of 0.57, 1.97, 4.69, and 9.08 GeV^{-2} respectively.
* The simulation is for Niobium-Niobium (Nb+Nb) collisions at E_lab = 1050 MeV/nucleon with impact parameter b = 3 fm.
* The hard equation of state uses parameters alpha = -124 MeV, beta = 70.5 MeV, gamma = 2, with compression modulus k = 380 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Fock-Tani formalism for baryon-baryon interactions, previously applied to NN phase-shifts and KN systems by Barnes and Swanson, Silvestre-Brac, and Hadjimichef, Haidenbauer and Krein, into the domain of heavy ion collision simulations. It contrasts with standard VUU approaches that use free-space NN cross-sections as input, and with studies of in-medium cross-section reduction from collective flow analyses. The paper enables future work incorporating density-dependent effective parameters lambda* and m_q* for nucleons in medium, and extension to higher-energy models like UrQMD.
