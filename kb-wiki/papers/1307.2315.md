# Shell and isospin effects in nuclear charge radii

> The shell effect and isospin effect in nuclear charge radii are systematically investigated and a four-parameter formula is proposed for the description of the root-mean-square (rms) charge radii by combining the shell corrections and deformations of nuclei obtained from the Weizsäcker-Skyrme mass model. The rms deviation with respect to the 885 measured charge radii falls to 0.022 fm. The proposed formula is also applied for the study of the charge radii of super-heavy nuclei and nuclear symmetry energy. The linear relationship between the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy and the rms charge radius difference of 30S - 30Si mirror pair is clearly observed. The estimated slope parameter is about $L=54 \pm 19$ MeV from the coefficient of the isospin term in the proposed charge radius formula.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a four-parameter formula for nuclear charge radii that reproduces 885 measured values with an rms deviation of 0.022 fm, but the key hedge is that the formula depends on shell corrections and deformations from the Weizsacker-Skyrme mass model, which itself has an rms deviation of 441 keV for 2149 known masses; the claimed linear relationship between the slope parameter L and the charge radius difference of the 30S-30Si mirror pair is observed with a Pearson correlation coefficient of 0.95, but only for this specific pair and not for other mirror pairs like 34S-34Ar or 18O-18Ne.

## Method

The authors combined the Weizsacker-Skyrme (WS*) mass model's microscopic shell corrections and quadrupole (beta2) and hexadecapole (beta4) deformations with a phenomenological formula for the nuclear charge radius R_c = r0 A^(1/3) + r1 A^(-2/3) + rs I(1-I) + rd DeltaE/A, where DeltaE is the shell correction and I=(N-Z)/A is the isospin asymmetry. They fitted the four parameters to 885 measured rms charge radii for nuclei with A>=16 from the Ang13 compilation, minimizing the rms deviation between experimental data and model calculations. They then used the Skyrme Hartree-Fock model with 62 different Skyrme forces to calculate the charge radius difference between the mirror pair 30S-30Si and correlated it with the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key numbers

- Rms deviation of the proposed formula with respect to 885 measured charge radii: 0.022 fm
- Rms deviation for 343 even-even nuclei: 0.016 fm
- Parameters of the charge radius formula: r0 = 1.2260(9) fm, r1 = 2.86(9) fm, rs = -1.09(3) fm, rd = 0.99(17) MeV^(-1) fm
- Rms deviation of the HFB21 model for 782 measured charge radii: 0.027 fm; for the 885 radii in this work: 0.026 fm
- Rms deviation of the RMF model with force NL3 for 343 even-even nuclei: 0.026 fm
- Estimated slope parameter L from the isospin term coefficient: 54 +/- 19 MeV
- Predicted charge radii for super-heavy nuclei: 286114 = 6.17 fm, 290116 = 6.19 fm, compared to experimental extractions of 6.24 +/- 0.14 fm and 6.13 +/- 0.16 fm respectively
- Pearson correlation coefficient between L and Delta r_ch for the 30S-30Si mirror pair with 62 Skyrme forces: 0.95
- Pearson correlation coefficients for other mirror pairs: 34S-34Ar = 0.58, 18O-18Ne = 0.69
- Estimated Delta r_ch for 30S-30Si: 0.113 +/- 0.003 fm

## Context

This work extends phenomenological charge radius formulas by incorporating microscopic shell corrections from the Weizsacker-Skyrse mass model (Wang 2010), improving upon earlier approaches such as the HFB21 model (which achieved 0.027 fm rms deviation) and the RMF model with force NL3 (0.026 fm for even-even nuclei). The paper specifically addresses the inability of previous models to reproduce the parabolic charge radii trend in Ca isotopes due to shell closures at N=20 and N=28, as noted by Ganioglu (2004). The proposed formula enables predictions for super-heavy nuclei and establishes a linear relationship between the slope parameter L of the nuclear symmetry energy and charge radius differences of mirror nuclei, offering an alternative to neutron skin thickness measurements of 208Pb for constraining L, with the extracted value of L=54 +/- 19 MeV consistent with results from Chen (2011) and Xu (2010).
