# Wronskian differential formula for confluent supersymmetric quantum mechanics

> A Wronskian differential formula, useful for applying the confluent second-order SUSY transformations to arbitrary potentials, will be obtained. This expression involves a parametric derivative with respect to the factorization energy which, in many cases, is simpler for calculations than the previously found integral equation. This alternative mechanism shall be applied to the free particle and the single-gap Lame potential.

## Key claim

The paper derives a differential formula for the Wronskian in confluent second-order supersymmetric quantum mechanics that avoids evaluating the integrals required by the previously known integral equation, but the new formula introduces a free constant \(D\) that must be chosen to avoid singularities in the transformed potential.

## Method

The authors start from the confluent case (\(c=0\)) of second-order SUSY QM, where two factorization energies converge to a single value \(\epsilon_1\). They show that the second seed solution \(u_2\) in the Jordan chain \((H-\epsilon_1)u_2 = u_1\) can be expressed as \(u_2 = C u_1 + D u_1^\perp + \partial u_1/\partial\epsilon_1\), where \(u_1^\perp\) is the orthogonal function satisfying \(W(u_1,u_1^\perp)=1\). The Wronskian \(W(u_1,u_2)\) then becomes \(D + W(u_1,\partial u_1/\partial\epsilon_1)\), leading to a new potential formula \(\widetilde{V}=V-2\{\ln[D + W(u_1,\partial u_1/\partial\epsilon_1)]\}^{\prime\prime}\) that uses a parametric derivative instead of an integral. This differential formula is applied to the free particle and the single-gap Lame potential.

## Key numbers

* Free particle: For \(u_1 = e^{\kappa_1 x}\) with \(\epsilon_1 = -\kappa_1^2\) and \(\kappa_1 > 0\), the confluent second-order SUSY partner potential is \(\widetilde{V} = -2\kappa_1^2 \text{sech}^2[\kappa_1(x-x_0)]\), which is the Poschl-Teller potential with one bound state at energy \(E_0 = \epsilon_1 = -\kappa_1^2\).
* Single-gap Lame potential with \(n=1\): The spectrum is \(\text{Sp}(H) = [m,1] \cup [1+m,\infty)\), with energy gaps \((-\infty,m)\) and \((1,1+m)\). The factorization energy \(\epsilon_1\) is related to the displacement \(\delta\) by \(\epsilon_1 = \frac{2}{3}(m+1) - \wp(\delta)\).
* For the Lame potential example with \(m=0.5\), \(\epsilon_1=0.1\), \(x_0=0\), and \(D=-45\), a bound state is created inside the infinite energy gap.
* For the Lame potential example with \(m=0.1\), \(\epsilon_1=1.05\), \(x_0=0\), and \(D=20\), a bound state is created inside the finite energy gap \((1,1+m)\).

## Context

This work extends the confluent second-order SUSY QM formalism previously developed by Fernandez and collaborators (2003, 2005), which used an integral equation for the Wronskian that was difficult to evaluate for complicated seed solutions. The differential formula generalizes earlier treatments by Matveev (1992) and Stahlhofen (1995) that considered only the \(D=0\) case for the free particle, and it introduces the constant \(D\) as a free parameter to generate families of non-singular potentials. The method enables the creation of bound states inside energy gaps of periodic potentials like the Lame potential, producing localized impurity states that could serve as intermediate states for transitions between energy bands in solid-state physics models.
