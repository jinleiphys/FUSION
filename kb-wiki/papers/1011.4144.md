# Spatially Modulated Phase in Holographic Quark-Gluon Plasma

> We present a string theory construction of a gravity dual of a spatially modulated phase. In our earlier work, we showed that the Chern-Simons term in the 5-dimensional Maxwell theory destabilizes the Reissner-Nordstrom black holes in anti-de Sitter space if the Chern-Simons coupling is sufficiently high. In this paper, we show that a similar instability is realized on the worldvolume of 8-branes in the Sakai-Sugimoto model in the quark-gluon plasma phase. We also construct and analyze a non-linear solution describing the end-point of the transition. Our result suggests a new spatially modulated phase in quark-gluon plasma when the baryon density is above 0.8 N_f fm^{-3} at temperature 150 MeV.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the Chern-Simons coupling on the worldvolume of 8-branes in the Sakai-Sugimoto model is 3 times the critical value required for instability, and that a spatially modulated phase in quark-gluon plasma appears when the baryon density exceeds approximately 0.8 N_f fm^{-3} at a temperature of 150 MeV, but only in the deconfined, chirally symmetric phase where the 8-brane configuration is smooth.

## Method

The authors study the Dirac-Born-Infeld (DBI) action with a Chern-Simons term on the worldvolume of D8 branes in the Sakai-Sugimoto model, in the deconfinement phase where the thermal circle is contractible. They first analyze linearized fluctuations around a homogeneous background with finite baryon density, solving the resulting second-order ordinary differential equation numerically to find the critical density where tachyonic modes appear. They then construct non-linear solutions using an ansatz with spatially modulated gauge fields and solve the full equations numerically to identify the end-point of the phase transition and compute the free energy.

## Key numbers

- The Chern-Simons coupling on the worldvolume theory is α = 3/4, which is above the limiting value of 1/4 required for instability.
- The critical quark density at α = 3/4 is numerically evaluated as ρ̃_crit = 3.714 u_T^(5/2), where u_T is related to the temperature.
- The critical baryon density expressed in QCD quantities is ρ_crit/N_c ≈ 0.8 N_f fm^{-3} at T = 150 MeV, using M_KK = 949 MeV and λ = 16.6.
- At the critical density, the instability begins at momentum k = 2.39 u_T^(1/2), which in original coordinates is k/R ≈ 10 T; for T = 150 MeV this gives momentum about 1.5 GeV and wavelength 0.8 fm.
- For a sample case with ρ̃ = 5 u_T^(5/2), the minimum free energy occurs at k = 2.35 u_T^(1/2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier finding (Ooguri and Park, 2010) that the Chern-Simons term in 5-dimensional Maxwell theory destabilizes Reissner-Nordstrom black holes in anti-de Sitter space, by realizing a similar instability in the Sakai-Sugimoto model for QCD. It builds on the Sakai-Sugimoto model (Sakai and Sugimoto, 2005) and prior studies of finite baryon density solutions in that model (Sakai, Aharony, Bergman). The result suggests a new spatially modulated phase in quark-gluon plasma, complementing a phenomenological holographic model of a baryon density wave by Domokos (2008) and earlier instability studies by Chuang (2009) that did not consider the chirally symmetric phase.
