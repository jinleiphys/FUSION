# The deformation of the interacting nucleon in the Skyrme model

> Changes in the nucleon shape are investigated by letting the nucleon deform under the strong interactions with another nucleon. The parameters of the axial deformations are obtained by minimizing the static energy of the two nucleon system at each internucleon distance $ R $. It is shown that the intrinsic quadrupole moment of the interacting proton, $Q_{p}$, is about $0.02 fm^2$ at distances near $R \sim 1.25 $ fm.

## Key claim

The nucleon can change its shape under strong interactions with another nucleon, becoming prolate at intermediate separations and oblate at short distances, but the intrinsic quadrupole moment of the interacting proton reaches only about 0.02 fm^2 near R ~ 1.25 fm, indicating the deformation is very small.

## Method

The authors used the Skyrme model supplemented by a dilaton scalar field to satisfy the QCD trace anomaly constraint, with both skyrmions allowed to deform into ellipsoidal shapes described by axial deformation parameters delta_x and delta_z. The deformation parameters were obtained by minimizing the total static energy of the two-nucleon system at each internucleon distance R using the product ansatz for the chiral field and dilaton field. The resulting deformed configurations were then used to compute the intrinsic quadrupole moments of the baryon charge distribution.

## Key numbers

- Intrinsic quadrupole moment of the interacting proton Q_p is about 0.02 fm^2 at distances near R ~ 1.25 fm
- Maximum value of Q_p = 0.016 fm^2 at R ~ 1.5 fm
- Deformation effects contribute about -2 MeV to the central scalar-isoscalar part of the nucleon-nucleon potential at the minimum point R ~ 1.25 fm
- Nucleon static properties in the dilaton case: M_N = 1054 MeV, g_A = 0.65, isoscalar RMS radius = 0.38 fm, isovector RMS radius = 0.66 fm
- Parameters used: F_pi = 186 MeV, e = 2 pi, m_pi = 139 MeV, gluon condensate = (283 MeV)^4, scalar meson mass m_sigma = 610 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of nucleon swelling in the skyrmion model by Kalbermann et al. (1994) and investigations of deformed skyrmion states by Hajduk and Schwesinger (1986). It builds on the authors' previous work (Musakhanov and Rahimov, 1995) that used the Andrianov-Novozhilov model with a dilaton field to address the missing central attraction in NN interactions. The results enable future experimental tests through high-energy electron scattering on nuclei to detect the predicted small deviations from spherical nucleon shape.
