# Electric conductivity of hot and dense nuclear matter

> Transport coefficients play an important role in characterising hot and dense nuclear matter, such as that created in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions (URHIC). In the present work we calculate the electric conductivity of hot and dense hadronic matter by extracting it from the electromagnetic spectral function, through its zero energy limit at vanishing 3-momentum. We utilise the vector dominance model (VDM), in which the photon couples to hadronic currents predominantly through the $ρ$ meson. Therefore, we use hadronic many-body theory to calculate the $ρ$-meson's self-energy in hot and dense hadronic matter, by dressing its pion cloud with $π$-$ρ$, $π$-$σ$, $π$-$K$, N-hole, and $Δ$-hole loops. We then introduce vertex corrections to maintain gauge invariance. Finally, we analyze the low-energy transport peak as a function of temperature and baryon chemical potential, and extract the conductivity along a proposed phase transition line.

## Key claim

The electric conductivity of hot and dense hadronic matter is extracted from the electromagnetic spectral function's low-energy transport peak, which is generated by the absorption of a virtual rho meson by a thermal pion; the conductivity decreases along the phase transition line when pion-nucleon scattering is included, contrary to the rising trend when only pion-pion and pion-kaon interactions are considered.

## Method

The authors calculate the rho meson self-energy in hot and dense hadronic matter using hadronic many-body theory, dressing its pion cloud with pi-rho, pi-sigma, pi-K, nucleon-hole, and delta-hole loops within the vector dominance model. They introduce effective vertex corrections to maintain gauge invariance (the Ward identities) for the pi-pi and pi-K interactions, and adopt existing vertex corrections for the pi-N interaction. The electromagnetic spectral function is computed at vanishing three-momentum, and the electric conductivity is extracted from its zero-energy limit along a proposed phase transition line estimated from chemical freeze-out data.

## Key numbers

* At T=150 MeV and mu_B=450 MeV, the vertex corrections increase the height of the transport peak by approximately 15%.
* At T=150 MeV and mu_B=450 MeV, including pi-N scattering reduces the transport peak height by more than a factor of two compared to the case without pi-N scattering.
* The pi-K scattering model is fitted to isospin-1/2 P-wave pi-K scattering phase shifts, yielding g_K=4.97, bare K* mass m_K*^(0)=976 MeV, kaon mass m_K=494 MeV, and cutoff Lambda_K=630 MeV.
* The vacuum rho parameters are g_rho=5.9, bare rho mass m_rho^(0)=853 MeV, and cutoff Lambda_0=1 GeV, obtained from fits to P-wave pi-pi phase shifts and the pion electromagnetic form factor.

## Context

This work extends the calculation of Atchison (2023), which computed the conductivity of hot pion matter, by incorporating pion interactions with nucleons and kaons in hot and dense hadronic matter. The pi-N scattering contributions follow the approach of Urban (1998, 1999), and the pi-K scattering is modeled through a K* resonance. The results are relevant for interpreting low-mass dilepton measurements at facilities such as HADES, STAR, and ALICE, where a broadening transport peak may produce observable enhancements in the dilepton spectrum as suggested by Rapp (2024).
