# Particlization of an interacting hadron resonance gas with global conservation laws for event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions

> We revisit the problem of particlization of a QCD fluid into hadrons and resonances at the end of the fluid dynamical stage in relativistic heavy-ion collisions in a context of fluctuation measurements. The existing methods sample an ideal hadron resonance gas, therefore, they do not capture the non-Poissonian nature of the grand-canonical fluctuations, expected due to QCD dynamics such as the chiral transition or QCD critical point. We address the issue by partitioning the particlization hypersurface into locally grand-canonical fireballs populating the space-time rapidity axis that are constrained by global conservation laws. The procedure allows to quantify the effect of global conservation laws, volume fluctuations, thermal smearing and resonance decays on fluctuation measurements in various rapidity acceptances, and can be used in fluid dynamical simulations of heavy-ion collisions. As a first application, we study event-by-event fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC using an excluded volume hadron resonance gas model matched to lattice QCD susceptibilities, with a focus on (pseudo)rapidity acceptance dependence of net baryon, net proton, and net charge cumulants. We point out large differences between net proton and net baryon cumulant ratios that make direct comparisons between the two unjustified. We observe that the existing experimental data on net-charge fluctuations at the LHC shows a strong suppression relative to a hadronic description.

## Key claim

The authors propose a new particlization routine for fluid dynamical simulations that can capture non-Poissonian fluctuations from interacting hadron resonance gas models, but the method relies on the assumption that each rapidity slice is large enough to be in the thermodynamic limit (volume much larger than the correlation length cubed), which may not hold for all systems.

## Method

The authors partition the particlization hypersurface into slices along the space-time rapidity axis, treat each slice as a locally grand-canonical fireball, and then enforce exact global conservation of baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness via a Kronecker delta projection that breaks the factorization of multiplicity distributions across slices. They implement rejection sampling for an excluded volume hadron resonance gas (EV-HRG) model, using a Poisson envelope distribution centered at the thermodynamic mean particle number, and test the sampling procedure against analytic calculations for a single-component EV model.

## Key numbers

- The EV-HRG model uses a baryonic excluded volume parameter b = 1 fm^3 at T = 160 MeV and mu_B = 0, giving a dimensionless parameter kappa = 0.03.
- Cumulant ratios up to sixth order approach within a few percent of the thermodynamic limit for reduced volume V_tilde >= 20 (where V_tilde = V/b).
- The typical chemical freeze-out temperatures at LHC are T_ch ~ 155-160 MeV, close to the lattice QCD pseudo-critical temperature T_pc ~ 155-160 MeV at mu_B = 0.
- The hyperkurtosis chi_6^B/chi_2^B turns negative around T_pc in lattice QCD predictions.

## Context

This work extends the subensemble acceptance method (SAM) from Vovchenko (2020) by incorporating thermal smearing and resonance decays, moving from configuration-space analysis to momentum-space observables. It addresses the limitation of existing particlization routines (e.g., those by Kisiel (2005), Shen (2014), Karpenko (2015)) that sample only ideal hadron resonance gas with Poisson distributions, thus missing non-Poissonian fluctuations from QCD dynamics such as the chiral transition or critical point. The method enables quantitative comparison of fluctuation measurements from LHC and RHIC experiments with interacting HRG models matched to lattice QCD susceptibilities.
