# Nucleation versus Spinodal decomposition in a first order quark hadron phase transition

> We investigate the scenario of homogeneous nucleation for a first order quark-hadron phase transition in a rapidly expanding background of quark gluon plasma. Using an improved preexponential factor for homogeneous nucleation rate, we solve a set of coupled equations to study the hadronization and the hydrodynamical evolution of the matter. It is found that significant supercooling is possible before hadronization begins. This study also suggests that spinodal decomposition competes with nucleation and may provide an alternative mechanism for phase conversion particularly if the transition is strong enough and the medium is nonviscous. For weak enough transition, the phase conversion may still proceed via homogeneous nucleation.

## Key claim

The paper shows that for a first-order quark-hadron phase transition in a rapidly expanding quark-gluon plasma, significant supercooling is possible before hadronization begins, and spinodal decomposition may compete with homogeneous nucleation as a phase conversion mechanism specifically when the transition is strong enough and the medium is nonviscous; for weak transitions or dissipative plasmas, homogeneous nucleation remains the dominant mechanism.

## Method

The authors solved a set of coupled equations combining the homogeneous nucleation rate (using an improved dynamical prefactor that includes both viscous and nonviscous components) with energy-momentum conserving hydrodynamic equations for a spherically expanding system. They used a phenomenological effective potential based on the bag equation of state, with parameters determined by surface tension and correlation length, to model the first-order phase transition and compute the nucleation barrier. The hadron fraction was calculated using a kinetic equation that accounts for bubble nucleation, growth, and the available space for new bubbles.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature fixed at T_C = 160 MeV, giving B^(1/4) = 236 MeV
- For surface tension sigma = 10 MeV/fm^2 and correlation length xi = 0.7 fm, the order parameter at T_C is phi_h = 91 MeV (comparable to the pion decay constant)
- For sigma = 30 MeV/fm^2 and xi = 0.7 fm, the barrier height at T_C is V_b(T_C) = 3 sigma / (16 xi) = 3 * 30 / (16 * 0.7) MeV/fm^3
- The spinodal temperature T_S is given by T_S = [B / (B + 27 V_b(T_C))]^(1/4) * T_C
- Assuming initial temperature T_i = 320 MeV and formation time tau_i = 1 fm/c, Bjorken scaling predicts tau_C = 8 fm/c for (1+1) dimensional expansion
- For a nonviscous plasma with tau_C = 8 fm/c, the system supercools to a minimum temperature T_m before reheating, with the amount of supercooling increasing for larger sigma
- Viscosity coefficients considered: eta_q = 5 T^3 and eta_q = 14.4 T^3 (in units where T is in MeV)

## Context

This work extends the homogeneous nucleation approach of Csernai and Kapusta (1992) and Ruggeri and Friedman (1996) by using an improved dynamical prefactor that combines both viscous and nonviscous contributions. It also builds on earlier studies of subcritical hadron bubbles by the same authors (Shukla and Mohanty, 2000) and addresses the competition between nucleation and spinodal decomposition previously suggested by Dumitru and collaborators for rapidly expanding systems. The results enable future studies to determine which hadronization mechanism dominates under specific conditions of transition strength, viscosity, and expansion rate relevant to heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.
