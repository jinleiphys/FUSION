# Constraints on the muon fraction and density profile in neutron stars

> Muons in neutron stars (NSs) play especially important roles in addressing several interesting new physics questions associated with detecting as well as understanding interactions and astrophysical effects of muonphilic dark matter particles. The key model inputs for studying the latter are the total muon mass $M_μ$, the muon mass fraction $M_μ/M_{\rm NS}$ over the NS mass $M_{\rm NS}$ and the muon radial density profile $ρ_μ(r)$ in NSs of varying masses. We investigate these quantities within a minimum model for the core of NSs consisting of neutrons, protons, electrons, and muons using an explicitly isospin-dependent parametric Equation of State (EOS) constrained by available nuclear laboratory experiments and the latest astrophysical observations of NS masses, radii and tidal deformabilities. We found that the absolutely maximum muon mass $M_μ$ and its mass fraction $M_μ/M_{\rm NS}$ in the most massive NSs allowed by causality are about 0.025 $M_\odot$ and 1.1\%, respectively. For the most massive NS of mass 2.14 $M_\odot$ observed so far, they reduce to about 0.020 $M_\odot$ and 0.9\%, respectively. We also study respective effects of individual parameters describing the EOS of high-density neutron-rich nucleonic matter on the muon contents in NSs with varying masses. We found that the most important but uncertain nuclear physics ingredient for determining the muon contents in NSs is the high-density nuclear symmetry energy.

## Key claim

The maximum muon mass and muon mass fraction in neutron stars are constrained to about 0.025 solar masses and 1.1%, respectively, but only for the most massive neutron stars allowed by causality; for the most massive neutron star observed so far (2.14 solar masses), these values reduce to about 0.020 solar masses and 0.9%, respectively.

## Method

The authors use a minimum npeμ model (neutrons, protons, electrons, muons) for the neutron star core with an explicitly isospin-dependent parametric Equation of State (EOS) parameterized up to third order in density. They constrain the high-density EOS parameter space (K_sym, J_sym, J_0) using nuclear laboratory experiments and astrophysical observations including neutron star masses, radii, tidal deformabilities from GW170817, and NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451, then solve the inverse-structure problem to determine muon contents.

## Key numbers

- Maximum muon mass M_μ in the most massive neutron stars allowed by causality: about 0.025 M_⊙
- Maximum muon mass fraction M_μ/M_NS in the most massive neutron stars allowed by causality: about 1.1%
- For the most massive observed neutron star of mass 2.14 M_⊙: M_μ ≈ 0.020 M_⊙ and M_μ/M_NS ≈ 0.9%
- Canonical neutron star (1.4 M_⊙) radius R_1.4 varies between about 11.5 km and 13 km when high-density EOS parameters are varied
- Nuclear symmetry energy parameters at saturation: E_sym(ρ_0) = 31.7 ± 3.2 MeV, L ≈ 58.7 ± 28.1 MeV
- High-density EOS parameter ranges: -400 ≤ K_sym ≤ 100 MeV, -200 ≤ J_sym ≤ 800 MeV, -800 ≤ J_0 ≤ 400 MeV
- NICER results for PSR J0030+0451: M = 1.34^{+0.16}_{-0.15} M_⊙, R = 12.71^{+1.19}_{-1.14} km (Riley et al. 2019)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Zhang et al. (2018, 2019a, 2019b) on constraining the high-density EOS of neutron-rich matter, now specifically focusing on muon contents as inputs for muonphilic dark matter studies. The results provide constrained model inputs for studies of muonphilic dark matter capture in neutron stars, as discussed in reviews by Garani et al. (2019) and Dror et al. (2019), and enable more realistic calculations of dark matter heating and merger effects. The authors note that hyperons and baryon resonances (e.g., Σ^- and Δ^-(1232)) could reduce muon fractions, so these results represent optimistic estimates within the minimum npeμ model.
