# Quantised orbital alignment and Identical High-K Bands in A=180 region

> The origin of the identical gamma-ray energies (IB) observed in the K=33/2$^+$ and K=16$^+$ bands in $^{177}$Lu and $^{178}$Hf respectively is investigated using deformed Hartree-Fock and Angular Momentum Projection technique. We find that quantised orbital (rather than spin) alignment of protons in the m=1/2$^+$ inert orbit leads to identical energy spectra. A K=16$^+$ band with identical spectrum is predicted in $^{176}$Yb at 4.48 Mev of excitation.

# Quantised orbital alignment and Identical High-K Bands in A=180 region

## Key claim
The identical gamma-ray energies observed in the K=33/2⁺ band in ¹⁷⁷Lu and the K=16⁺ band in ¹⁷⁸Hf arise from quantised orbital alignment (not spin alignment) of protons occupying the m=1/2⁺ inert orbit, with the relative orbital alignment between corresponding states being exactly ½ħ and the relative spin alignment negligible.

## Method
Deformed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations were performed for valence nucleons outside a ¹³²Sn core using a surface-delta interaction (strength 0.3 MeV for pp, nn, and pn interactions) with spherical single-particle energies specified for protons and neutrons. Angular momentum projection (AMP) was then applied to the intrinsic HF configurations to obtain physical states, and expectation values of the operators J, L_z, and S_z were evaluated for the projected states to extract orbital and spin alignments from individual proton orbits.

## Key numbers
- The K=16⁺ band in ¹⁷⁸Hf is observed at 2.447 MeV excitation; the K=33/2⁺ band in ¹⁷⁷Lu is observed at 2.771 MeV excitation; calculated values are 2.255 MeV and 3.252 MeV respectively.
- The inert m=1/2⁺ proton orbit has a quadrupole deformation of -0.009 b² (negligible), while the deformed m=1/2⁺ proton orbit has a quadrupole moment of 1.341 b².
- Theoretical γ-ray transition energies for the identical bands agree with experiment within 10-20 keV; after band mixing, the 17→16 transition in ¹⁷⁸Hf (K=16⁺) is 419 keV (PHF), 357 keV (experiment); the 35/2→33/2 transition in ¹⁷⁷Lu (K=33/2⁺) is 443 keV (PHF), 357 keV (experiment).
- A K=16⁺ band with identical spectrum is predicted in ¹⁷⁶Yb at 4.48 MeV excitation, with calculated γ-ray energies (e.g., 580 keV for 17→16, 613 keV for 18→17) nearly identical to those in ¹⁷⁸Hf.
- The relative orbital alignment between corresponding states in ¹⁷⁷Lu and ¹⁷⁸Hf is L_diff = ½ħ; the relative spin alignment S_diff is almost zero.

## Context
This work extends the study of identical bands (IB), first observed in superdeformed bands of ¹⁵²Dy and ¹⁵¹Tb by Stephens et al. (1990), to high-K isomeric states in the A=180 region following the experimental identification by Dracoulis et al. (year not given in text) of IB in ¹⁷⁷Lu and ¹⁷⁸Hf. Prior models invoked quantised spin alignment (Stephens et al.), pseudo-spin symmetry, or supersymmetry, but the authors find that for these high-K bands the mechanism is quantised orbital alignment rather than spin alignment. The prediction of an identical K=16⁺ band in ¹⁷⁶Yb provides a testable case to confirm the inert-orbit mechanism, and the work suggests that ¹⁷⁶Yb acts as a core for the high-K isomers in ¹⁷⁷Lu and ¹⁷⁸Hf.
