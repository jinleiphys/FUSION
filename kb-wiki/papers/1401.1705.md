# On the Momentum Dependence of the Flavor Structure of the Nucleon Sea

> Difference between the $\bar u$ and $\bar d$ sea quark distributions in the proton was first observed in the violation of the Gottfried sum rule in deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) experiments. The parton momentum fraction $x$ dependence of this difference has been measured over the region $0.02 < x < 0.35$ from Drell-Yan and semi-inclusive DIS experiments. The Drell-Yan data suggested a possible sign-change for $\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)$ near $x \sim 0.3$, which has not yet been explained by existing theoretical models. We present an independent evidence for the $\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)$ sign-change at $x \sim 0.3$ from an analysis of the DIS data. We further discuss the $x$-dependence of $\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)$ in the context of meson cloud model and the lattice QCD formulation.

## Key claim

The paper presents an independent evidence from NMC deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) data that the difference \(\bar d(x) - \bar u(x)\) changes sign near \(x \sim 0.3\), consistent with earlier Drell-Yan data from Fermilab E866; this sign-change cannot be explained by any existing theoretical model of the nucleon sea.

## Method

The authors use the leading-order QCD factorization relation \(\bar d(x) - \bar u(x) = \frac{1}{2}[u_v(x)-d_v(x)] - \frac{3}{2x}[F_2^p(x)-F_2^n(x)]\) to extract \(\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)\) from NMC measurements of \(F_2^p(x)-F_2^n(x)\) and the valence quark difference \(u_v(x)-d_v(x)\) taken from recent PDF parametrizations (JR14 and CT10). They then compare these extracted values at \(Q^2 = 4\) GeV\(^2\) and at higher \(Q^2\) bins (34, 45, 63, 95 GeV\(^2\)) with the E866 Drell-Yan results at \(Q^2 = 54\) GeV\(^2\). The analysis uses the NMC ratio \(R(x)=F_2^d(x)/F_2^p(x)\) and the CJ12mid parametrization for the deuteron correction \(r_N^d(x)\).

## Key numbers

* The NMC measurement of the Gottfried sum rule gave \(I_G = 0.235 \pm 0.026\), implying \(\int_0^1 [\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)]dx = 0.148 \pm 0.039\).
* The E866 Drell-Yan data at \(Q^2 = 54\) GeV\(^2\) show \(\bar d/\bar u\) falls below unity at \(x=0.315\), suggesting a sign-change of \(\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)\) near \(x \sim 0.3\).
* From NMC data at the four highest \(Q^2\) bins (mean values \(\sim 34, 45, 63, 95\) GeV\(^2\)), the extracted \(\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)\) at \(x>0.3\) has mean values of \(-0.009 \pm 0.006\), \(-0.012 \pm 0.006\), \(-0.016 \pm 0.008\), and \(-0.001 \pm 0.008\), respectively.
* The perturbative QCD corrections to the leading-order relation are estimated to be small, on the order of 0.2% at \(Q=10\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of the flavor asymmetry of the nucleon sea, first observed via the Gottfried sum rule violation by the NMC Collaboration (1991) and later measured in Drell-Yan experiments by NA51 and Fermilab E866. The paper provides an independent confirmation from DIS data that \(\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)\) changes sign at \(x \sim 0.3\), a feature that existing theoretical models (meson cloud model of Thomas (1983), Henley and Miller (1990), Kumano (1991); chiral quark model of Eichten et al. (1992); intrinsic sea model of Chang and Peng (2011); chiral-quark soliton model of Pobylitsa et al. (1999); statistical model of Soffer et al.) all fail to predict. The result motivates future Drell-Yan experiments (Fermilab E906, J-PARC) and lattice QCD calculations to directly compute \(\bar d(x)-\bar u(x)\) via the method of Liu and Dong (1994) or the Lorentz-boost approach of Ji (2013) and Lin et al. (2014).
