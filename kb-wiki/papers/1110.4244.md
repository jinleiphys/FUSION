# Efficient method to perform quantum number projection and configuration mixing for most general mean-field states

> Combining several techniques, we propose an efficient and numerically reliable method to perform the quantum number projection and configuration mixing for most general mean-field states, i.e., the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) type product states without symmetry restrictions. As for example of calculations, we show the results of the simultaneous parity, number and angular-momentum projection from HFB type states generated from the cranked Woods-Saxon mean-field with a very large basis that is composed of Nmax=20 spherical harmonic oscillator shells.

## Key claim

The authors propose an efficient and numerically reliable method for quantum number projection and configuration mixing from general Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) type product states without symmetry restrictions, where the key innovation is that the effective model space can be truncated to a much smaller dimension (typically 100-250 states) than the full basis (over 3000 states) by using Thouless amplitudes rather than the conventional (U,V) amplitudes, provided the pairing correlations are not too strong.

## Method

The method employs the Thouless representation of HFB states with respect to a canonical-like basis obtained by diagonalizing the density matrix, where the Thouless amplitude Z is confined to a small P-space of orbits with occupation probabilities above a threshold (typically 10⁻⁴-10⁻⁵). The norm overlap and all basic contractions (transition density matrix and transition pairing tensors) required for the generalized Wick theorem are then calculated entirely within this truncated P-space, even though the symmetry transformation (rotation, gauge angle, or parity) mixes the P-space with the complementary Q-space. The sign of the norm overlap is fixed by computing a pfaffian rather than a square root of a determinant.

## Key numbers

* The spherical harmonic oscillator basis used in example calculations has Nmax=20 shells, giving a full basis dimension M > 3000.
* The effective P-space dimension Lp is approximately 100-250 for either neutrons or protons, about one order of magnitude smaller than M.
* The truncation threshold epsilon is typically chosen as 10⁻⁴-10⁻⁵, which is sufficient for final results (e.g., energy spectra) to remain unchanged.
* For heavy stable nuclei, the typical number of spherical oscillator shells needed is Nosc ≈ 12-14, but up to Nosc ≈ 20 may be required for weakly bound orbits.

## Context

This work extends the angular momentum projection framework developed by Hara and collaborators (1979-1982) and the variation-after-projection calculations by Schmid and collaborators (1987, 2004), which were restricted to small model spaces (2-3 harmonic oscillator shells) or axial symmetry. The method enables simultaneous parity, number, and angular momentum projection from fully symmetry-breaking HFB states (no axial symmetry, no time-reversal invariance, no parity conservation) in large model spaces, which is essential for modern energy density functional calculations with Skyrme, Gogny, or relativistic mean-field approaches as pursued by Bender and Heenen (2008), Yao and collaborators (2009-2010), and Rodriguez and Egido (2010). The truncation technique using Thouless amplitudes was previously noted in appendices of Bonche, Flocard, and Heenen (1990) and later by Yao and collaborators (2009).
