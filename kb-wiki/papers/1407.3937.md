# Bootstrapping the statistical uncertainties of NN scattering data

> We use the Monte Carlo bootstrap as a method to simulate pp and np scattering data below pion production threshold from an initial set of over 6700 experimental mutually $3σ$ consistent data. We compare the results of the bootstrap, with 1020 statistically generated samples of the full database, with the standard covariance matrix method of error propagation. No significant differences in scattering observables and phase shifts are found. This suggests alternative strategies for propagating errors of nuclear forces in nuclear structure calculations.

## Key claim

The Monte Carlo bootstrap method for simulating pp and np scattering data yields no significant differences in scattering observables and phase shifts compared to the standard covariance matrix method of error propagation, suggesting that the simpler covariance method is a fairly good approximation despite the bootstrap method being more reliable in principle.

## Method

The authors applied the Monte Carlo bootstrap technique to an initial set of over 6700 experimental pp and np scattering data below pion production threshold that had been mutually filtered to 3-sigma consistency. They generated 1020 statistically independent synthetic databases by adding normally distributed random fluctuations to each experimental data point, then performed a least-squares fit to each synthetic database using a delta-shell potential with one pion exchange (DS-OPE) to obtain 1020 sets of potential parameters. The resulting parameter distributions and propagated uncertainties in phase shifts were compared with those obtained from the standard covariance matrix method and from a Monte Carlo sampling of the covariance matrix.

## Key numbers

- Over 6700 experimental data points (6713 from the database presented in Perez (2013)) were used as the initial set, with chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.04
- 1020 statistically generated samples of the full database were produced for the bootstrap analysis
- The delta-shell potential used a grid spacing of Delta r = 0.6 fm for the short-range component
- The long-range OPE component used a fixed pion-nucleon coupling constant f^2 = 0.075

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous development of phenomenological NN potentials (Perez 2013) that accurately describe over 6700 scattering data points, where the covariance matrix method was used to propagate errors. It also builds on normality testing of residuals that the authors had applied to their DS-OPE, DS-chiTPE, and Gaussian OPE potentials (Perez 2014). The bootstrap method had been successfully exploited in pi-pi scattering error analyses (Nieves 1999) but had never been implemented within the context of the NN force. This work enables alternative strategies for propagating errors of nuclear forces in nuclear structure calculations, particularly when the covariance matrix is not directly available or when parameter distributions show significant asymmetry.
