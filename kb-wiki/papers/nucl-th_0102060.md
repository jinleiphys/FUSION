# Validity of the one-body current for the calculation of form factors in the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics

> Form factors are calculated in the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics for the lowest energy states of a system made of two scalar particles interacting via the exchange of a massless boson. They are compared to the exact results obtained by using solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter equation which are well known in this case (Wick-Cutkosky model). Deficiencies of the point-form approach together with the single-particle current are emphasised. They point to the contribution of two-body currents which are required in any case to fulfil current conservation.

## Key claim

The point-form approach using only the single-particle (impulse approximation) current fails to reproduce exact Bethe-Salpeter form factors for a two-scalar system, with discrepancies that grow with momentum transfer and coupling strength; the failure is linked to violation of current conservation and the need for two-body currents, which are more essential in the point form than in other relativistic approaches.

## Method

The authors compare form factors calculated in the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics (using wave functions from a Coulomb-like potential) to exact results from the Wick-Cutkosky model, which solves the Bethe-Salpeter equation in ladder approximation for two distinguishable scalar particles exchanging a massless scalar boson. Elastic form factors for the ground state and inelastic transition form factors to the first radially excited state are computed for three coupling strengths (α = 1.0, 3.0, and 2π) and momentum transfers Q² up to 10 times the constituent mass squared, m². The point-form results are also compared to non-relativistic calculations.

## Key numbers

- For elastic form factor at α = 1 and Q²/m² = 10.0: Bethe-Salpeter gives 0.0137, non-relativistic gives 0.0135, point-form gives 0.0097.
- For elastic form factor at α = 3 and Q²/m² = 10.0: Bethe-Salpeter gives 0.139, non-relativistic gives 0.145, point-form gives 0.056.
- For elastic form factor at α = 2π (total mass zero) and Q²/m² = 10.0: Bethe-Salpeter gives 0.339, non-relativistic gives 0.378, point-form (E = 1.90 m) gives 0.124 × 10⁻⁶.
- For inelastic transition at α = 3 and Q²/m² = 0.01: Bethe-Salpeter F₁ = -0.0032, non-relativistic F₁ = -0.0032, point-form F₁ = -0.0101 (violates current conservation as Q² → 0).
- The point-form elastic form factor drops roughly like 1/Q⁶ instead of the correct 1/Q⁴.

## Context

This work extends the test of relativistic approaches to form factors previously performed by Karmanov and Smirnov (2000) for the light-front approach using the same Wick-Cutkosky model. The authors note that non-relativistic calculations agree well with exact Bethe-Salpeter results up to Q² ≈ 100 m², while the point-form approach, recently applied to deuteron form factors by Klink (1998) and nucleon form factors by Wagenbrunn (2000), shows large discrepancies that worsen with binding strength. The results suggest that the apparent success of point-form calculations for nucleon form factors may be due to neglecting significant two-body current contributions, leaving room for vector-meson dominance and other relativistic effects.
