# Universalities of Triplet Pairing in Neutron Matter

> The fundamental structure of the full set of solutions of the BCS $^3 P_2$ pairing problem in neutron matter is established. The relations between different spin-angle components in these solutions are shown to be practically independent of density, temperature, and the specific form of the pairing interaction. The spectrum of pairing energies is found to be highly degenerate.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that the structure of the full set of solutions of the BCS \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing problem in neutron matter is universal, meaning the relations between different spin-angle components are practically independent of density, temperature, and the specific form of the pairing interaction, but this universality holds only when mixing of different \(L,J\) channels (specifically the \(^3\)P\(_2\)–\(^3\)F\(_2\) coupling) is neglected.

## Method

The authors generalized a separation method previously developed for S-wave pairing (Khodel, Khodel, and Clark, 1998) to decompose the gap equation into a linear integral equation for a shape factor \(\chi_{LJ}(p)\) and a nonlinear equation for the structure coefficients \(D^M_{LJ}\). They then specialized to the \(L=S=1\), \(J=2\) channel and analytically solved the resulting system of five coupled equations for the real and imaginary parts of the gap components, using a rotation technique to reduce the structure function to one-dimensional form.

* The relative error from replacing \(E(p,{\bf n})\) by \(|\xi(p)|\) and \(\tanh(E/2T)\) by 1 in integrals containing the remainder \(W_{LJ}\) is of order \(D^2({\bf p})/\epsilon_F^2 \sim 10^{-6}\).
* The splitting between the upper and lower groups of states at zero temperature is given by \(\ln(\Delta_{\rm u}^2 / \Delta_{\rm l}^2) = 2\pi/(9\sqrt{3}) + 2/3 - \ln 3 \simeq -0.028\).
* The \(^3\)P\(_2\) channel is most favored at Fermi wave numbers \(k_F = p_F/\hbar \sim 2\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the analytical understanding of triplet pairing beyond the Ginzburg-Landau approach near the critical temperature (Mermin, 1974) and the numerical calculations reviewed by Takatsuka and Tamagaki (1971). The results are directly relevant to neutron star physics, where \(^3\)P\(_2\) pairing affects neutrino cooling processes (Page, 1995) and vortex structure (Sauls, 1989; Lamb, 1991). The analysis enables future characterization of solutions in superfluid \(^3\)He and potentially in superdeformed bands in atomic nuclei.
