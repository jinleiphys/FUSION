# Flow and equation of state in heavy-ion collisions

> The status of flow in heavy-ion collisions and of inference of hadronic-matter properties is reviewed.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that collective flow observables in heavy-ion collisions can be used to infer properties of the hadronic equation of state (EOS), but the separation of EOS effects from other unknowns (such as momentum dependence of mean fields and in-medium cross sections) remains challenging and requires careful transport-model comparisons.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental data on radial, sideward, and elliptic flow across beam energies from below 100 MeV/nucleon to above 100 GeV/nucleon, and compares them to transport-model simulations based on the Boltzmann equation formulated within relativistic Landau theory. The model includes nucleons, pions, delta and N* resonances, with single-particle energies and EOS specified by parametrizing volume energy density in terms of phase-space distribution functions, using both scalar and vector density parametrizations for momentum-independent and momentum-dependent fields.

## Key numbers

* Transverse collective velocity in heavy systems saturates at AGS energies (around 10 GeV/nucleon), as shown in the excitation function of transverse temperature and velocity at midrapidity.
* In central Au + Au collisions at 10.7 GeV/nucleon, the measured transverse-momentum distribution of protons at midrapidity has an effective temperature of 215 MeV; when rotated to a rapidity distribution, it is twice as narrow as the measured proton rapidity distribution, indicating incomplete stopping.
* The sideward flow excitation function is maximal between 0.1 and 10 GeV/nucleon in central collisions of heavy nuclei.
* The elliptic flow excitation function shows squeeze-out (negative v2) at moderate energies becoming positive at high energies, with an upward movement of v2 between 2 and 4 GeV/nucleon beam energy in Au + Au collisions.
* In the transport model, calculations use incompressibility values of K = 210 and 380 MeV for soft and stiff EOS, respectively, with effective mass at normal density for momentum-dependent fields taken as mN* = 0.65 mN.
* The out-of-plane to in-plane ratio RN for protons from 400 MeV/nucleon Bi + Bi reactions shows evidence for momentum dependence of the mean field up to 1 GeV/nucleon.

## Context

This review extends earlier work on collective flow in heavy-ion collisions, referencing specific experimental results from the FOPI Collaboration (Pog95, Rei97), NA44 Collaboration (Bea97), E802 Collaboration (Ahl98), and KaoS Collaboration (Bri96). The paper discusses how flow observables can probe the EOS, building on prior theoretical work by Ollitrault (1992) on elliptic flow and by Jan92 and Dan92 on the timing of sideward versus radial flow production. The results enable future searches for a phase transition to quark-gluon plasma at baryon densities above 4 times normal nuclear density, particularly by correlating elliptic flow with sideward flow and transverse-momentum distributions at AGS energies.
