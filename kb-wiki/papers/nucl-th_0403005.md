# Stopping and Isospin Equilibration in Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the density behaviour of the symmetry energy with respect to isospin equilibration in the combined systems $Ru(Zr)+Zr(Ru)$ at relativistic energies of 0.4 and $1.528 AGeV$. The study is performed within a relativistic framework and the contribution of the iso-vector, scalar $δ$ field to the symmetry energy and the isospin dynamics is particularly explored. We find that the isospin mixing depends on the symmetry energy and a stiff behaviour leads to more transparency. The results are also nicely sensitive to the "fine structure" of the symmetry energy, i.e. to the covariant properties of the isovector meson fields. The isospin tracing appears much less dependent on the in-medium neutron-proton cross-sections ($σ_{np}$) and this makes such observable very peculiar for the study of the isovector part of the nuclear equation of state. Within such a framework, comparisons with experiments support the introduction of the $δ$ meson in the description of the iso-vector equation of state.

## Key claim

The isospin mixing (transparency) in mixed Ru+Zr collisions depends on the symmetry energy, with a stiffer behavior leading to more transparency; however, the observed effect is moderate at 0.4 AGeV and larger at 1.528 AGeV, and comparisons with experimental data favor the inclusion of the isovector scalar delta meson, though more experimental information is needed for a definitive conclusion.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic transport simulations using the Relativistic Landau Vlasov (RLV) method for the Vlasov part and a Monte-Carlo procedure for the collision term, including inelastic processes involving nucleon resonances. They simulated central collisions of Ru+Zr and Zr+Ru at 0.4 and 1.528 AGeV beam energies, using two relativistic mean-field models: NLρ (including only the ρ meson) and NLρδ (including both ρ and δ mesons). The imbalance ratio R(y⁰) = N^{RuZr}(y⁰)/N^{ZrRu}(y⁰) was computed as a function of normalized rapidity for protons, neutrons, tritons, helium-3, and charged pions, with centrality selection via the ERAT observable and coalescence for fragment production.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies: 0.4 AGeV and 1.528 AGeV for Ru(Zr)+Zr(Ru) collisions
- N/Z ratios: Ru (N/Z=1.18) and Zr (N/Z=1.4)
- In the full transparency limit, R(p) = Z^{Zr}/Z^{Ru} = 40/44 = 0.91 for protons and R(n) = N^{Zr}/N^{Ru} = 56/52 = 1.077 for neutrons at target rapidity
- Coalescence radii: <R>_c = 4 fm in coordinate space and <R>_p = 1.3 fm^{-1} in momentum space
- Central collisions selected with impact parameter b ≤ 2 fm
- Compression phase duration: 15 < t < 30 fm/c
- Maximum compression density at 0.4 AGeV: ρ_B < 2·ρ_sat

## Context

This work extends previous studies of isospin flows (Gaitanos et al., 2004) and particle production (Bao et al., 2003) by examining the correlation between stopping and isospin equilibration using data from the FOPI collaboration (FOPI, 2003; FOPI, 2004). The results show that the imbalance ratio is sensitive to the microscopic Lorentz structure of the symmetry energy and is much less dependent on in-medium neutron-proton cross sections, making it a clean probe of the isovector equation of state. The findings enable future experimental measurements of the triton-to-helium-3 imbalance ratio and the charged pion ratio as definitive tests of the high-density symmetry energy and its fine structure.
