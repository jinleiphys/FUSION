# Low energy analysis of $πN$ scattering and the pion-nucleon sigma term with covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory

> The pion-nucleon sigma term ($σ_{πN}$) is an observable of fundamental importance because embodies information about the internal scalar structure of the nucleon. Nowadays this quantity has triggered renewed interest because it is a key input for a reliable estimation of the dark matter-nucleon spin independent elastic scattering cross section. In this proceeding we present how this quantity can be reliably extracted by employing only experimental information with the use covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory. We also contrast our extraction with updated phenomenology related to $σ_{πN}$ and show how this phenomenology favours a relatively large value of $σ_{πN}$. Finally, we extract a value of $σ_{πN}=59(7)$ MeV from modern partial wave analyses data.

## Key claim
The authors extract the pion-nucleon sigma term as σπN = 59(7) MeV from modern partial wave analyses, claiming this relatively large value is favored by updated phenomenology, but the extraction depends on the specific choice of partial wave analysis and the inclusion of the Δ(1232) resonance as an explicit degree of freedom in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Method
The authors calculated the πN scattering amplitude up to O(p³) in covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory using the extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) renormalization scheme, including the Δ(1232) resonance as an explicit dynamical degree of freedom. They fitted the low-energy constants (LECs) of the chiral Lagrangian to phase shifts from three different partial wave analyses: Karlsruhe (KA85), George Washington University (WI08), and Matsinos (EM06). The sigma term was then extracted from the fitted LEC c₁ using the relation σπN = -4c₁Mπ² plus loop corrections.

## Key numbers
- σπN = 59(7) MeV extracted from modern PWAs (WI08 and EM06), with the error accounting for uncertainty in the LEC c₁ and the first higher-order correction not included in the O(p³) calculation
- σπN = 43(5) MeV extracted from the KA85 PWA using Δ-ChPT
- σπN = 59(4) MeV from WI08 Δ-ChPT and σπN = 59(2) MeV from EM06 Δ-ChPT
- The Δ(1232) Breit-Wigner width ΓΔ = 118(2) MeV from the PDG, which relates to the NΔ axial coupling hA = 2.90(2)
- The scalar-isoscalar scattering length a₀₊⁺ = -1.0(9) × 10⁻³ Mπ⁻¹ from π-atoms data, compared to values from KA85 Δ-ChPT (-11(10)), WI08 Δ-ChPT (-1.2(3.3)), and EM06 Δ-ChPT (2.3(2.0))
- The Σ-term values from Δ-ChPT: 45(7) MeV (KA85), 64(6) MeV (WI08), 64(1) MeV (EM06)

## Context
This work extends previous chiral perturbation theory analyses of πN scattering that suffered from poor convergence, such as those by Fettes et al. (2000) and Becher and Leutwyler (2001), by including the Δ(1232) resonance explicitly and using the EOMS covariant scheme to preserve analytic properties. The authors resolve the longstanding discrepancy between the Karlsruhe PWA value of σπN ≈ 45(8) MeV and the George Washington University PWA value of σπN ≈ 64(7) MeV by showing that modern data favor the larger value. The result enables more reliable estimations of the dark matter-nucleon spin-independent elastic scattering cross section for direct detection experiments, and is consistent with a negligible strange quark contribution to the nucleon as shown in related work by the same authors.
