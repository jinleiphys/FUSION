# Emergence of low-energy monopole strength in the neutron-rich calcium isotopes

> The isoscalar monopole response of neutron-rich nuclei is sensitive to both the incompressibility coefficient of symmetric nuclear matter and the density dependence of the symmetry energy. The main goal of this paper is to explore the emergence, evolution, and origin of low energy monopole strength along the even-even calcium isotopes: from 40Ca to 60Ca. The distribution of isoscalar monopole strength is computed in a relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) using three effective interactions that have been calibrated to the properties of finite nuclei and neutron stars. A non-spectral approach is adopted that allows for an exact treatment of the continuum without any reliance on discretization. For the stable calcium isotopes, no evidence of low-energy monopole strength is observed, even as the 1f7/2 neutron orbital is being filled and the neutron-skin thickness progressively grows. Further, in contrast to experimental findings, a mild softening of the monopole response with increasing mass number is predicted. Beyond 48Ca, a significant amount of low-energy monopole strength emerges as soon as the weak-binding neutron orbitals (2p and 1f5/2) become populated. The emergence and evolution of low-energy strength is identified with transitions from these weakly-bound states into the continuum. Moreover, given that models with a soft symmetry energy tend to reach the neutron-drip line earlier than their stiffer counterparts, we identify an inverse correlation between the neutron-skin thickness and the inverse energy weighted sum.

## Key claim
The paper predicts that for stable calcium isotopes (40Ca to 48Ca), no low-energy monopole strength emerges even as the 1f7/2 neutron orbital fills and neutron-skin thickness grows, but beyond 48Ca a significant amount of low-energy monopole strength appears as soon as the weakly-bound 2p and 1f5/2 neutron orbitals become populated, with transitions from these states into the continuum driving the effect.

## Method
The distribution of isoscalar monopole strength was computed using a relativistic random phase approximation (RPA) with three effective interactions (NL3, FSUGold, FSUGarnet) calibrated to finite nuclei and neutron star properties. A non-spectral approach was adopted that treats the continuum exactly without discretization, solving an inhomogeneous differential equation for the Green's function in the mean-field approximation. The response was computed in the long wavelength limit for all even-even calcium isotopes from 40Ca to 60Ca.

## Key numbers
- For 40Ca, the FSUGarnet model predicts a centroid energy (m1/m0) of 20.81 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 19.2 ± 0.4 MeV
- For 48Ca, the FSUGarnet model predicts a centroid energy (m1/m0) of 20.22 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 19.88 +0.14/-0.18 MeV
- The neutron-skin thickness for 48Ca predicted by FSUGarnet is 0.167 fm; for 60Ca it is 0.488 fm
- The incompressibility coefficient K0 for symmetric nuclear matter is 229.5 MeV for FSUGarnet, 230.0 MeV for FSUGold, and 271.5 MeV for NL3
- The symmetry energy slope L is 51.0 MeV for FSUGarnet, 60.5 MeV for FSUGold, and 118.2 MeV for NL3
- For 60Ca, the incompressibility coefficient of asymmetric matter (K60) is 202.1 MeV for FSUGarnet, 199.3 MeV for FSUGold, and 193.8 MeV for NL3

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the giant monopole resonance in calcium isotopes, particularly addressing the experimental puzzle from Youngblood (2001) and Lui (2011) that the GMR energy in 40Ca appears lower than in 48Ca, contrary to theoretical expectations. The paper also builds on the measurement of isoscalar monopole strength in 68Ni by Vandebrouck (2014), where a controversy arose about the interpretation of low-energy structure due to discretized continuum treatments by Capelli (2009) and Khan (2011). The findings enable future measurements along unstable neutron-rich calcium isotopes to probe the role of the continuum in developing a soft monopole mode and to constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy.
