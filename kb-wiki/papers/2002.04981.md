# Light-front quantum mechanics and quantum field theory

## Key claim

Light-front quantum theories have the smallest number of Poincaré generators that require interactions (only three dynamical generators), but the light-front hyperplane is not a suitable initial value surface because it has light-like tangent vectors, and the self-adjointness of the light-front Hamiltonian is required to resolve ambiguities associated with this bad initial value surface.

## Method

The paper reviews the mathematical structure of light-front dynamics as one of Dirac's three forms of relativistic dynamics, focusing on the seven-parameter kinematic subgroup that leaves the light-front hyperplane invariant. It derives the transformation properties of single-particle basis states, discusses the construction of many-body bases using light-front boost-invariant internal variables, and examines how interactions are added to the non-interacting light-front Hamiltonian while preserving kinematic symmetries and rotational covariance.

## Key numbers

- The light-front kinematic subgroup is a seven-parameter subgroup of the Poincaré group, compared to six-parameter subgroups for the Lorentz group and three-dimensional Euclidean group (instant-form and point-form dynamics).
- The light-front dispersion relation is given by \( P^- = (\mathbf{P}_{\perp}^2 + M^2)/P^+ \).
- The light-front field commutation relation on the light front is \( [\phi (\tilde{\mathbf{x}},x^+=0), \phi (\tilde{\mathbf{y}},y^+=0)] = i (\pi/4) \delta (\mathbf{x}_{\perp} -\mathbf{y}_{\perp})\epsilon (x^-- y^-) \).

## Context

This contribution extends the foundational work of Wigner (1939) on unitary ray representations of the Poincaré group and Dirac (1949) on the three forms of relativistic dynamics. It discusses how light-front representations relate to instant-form and point-form dynamics through scattering-equivalent unitary transformations, and addresses open questions about zero-mode contributions at \( P^+ = 0 \) in light-front field theory, particularly in relation to the triviality of the Fock vacuum and the need to handle singularities at \( p^+ = 0 \) that are linked to ultraviolet singularities.
