# Neutrino-nucleus cross sections for W-boson and trident production

> The physics of neutrino-nucleus cross sections is a critical probe of the Standard Model and beyond. A precise understanding is also needed to accurately deduce astrophysical neutrino spectra. At energies above $\sim 5$ GeV, the cross section is dominated by deep inelastic scattering, mediated by weak bosons. In addition, there are subdominant processes where the hadronic coupling is through virtual photons, $γ^\ast$: (on-shell) $W$-boson production (e.g., where the underlying interaction is $ν_\ell + γ^\ast \rightarrow \ell^- + W^+$) and trident production (e.g., where it is $ν+ γ^\ast \rightarrow ν+ \ell_1^- + \ell_2^+$). These processes become increasingly relevant at TeV--PeV energies. We undertake the first systematic approach to these processes (and those with hadronic couplings through virtual $W$ and $Z$ bosons), treating them together, avoiding common approximations, considering all neutrino flavors and final states, and covering the energy range $10\,$--$10^8$ GeV. In particular, we present the first complete calculation of $W$-boson production and the first calculation of trident production at TeV--PeV energies. When we use the same assumptions as in prior work, we recover all of their major results. In a companion paper, we show that these processes should be taken into account for IceCube-Gen2.

## Key claim

The authors present the first complete calculation of neutrino-nucleus cross sections for W-boson production and the first calculation of trident production at TeV–PeV energies, covering the energy range 10–10⁸ GeV; they claim that previous calculations using the equivalent photon approximation (EPA) overestimate cross sections by more than 200% in some cases, particularly for electron final states.

## Method

The authors calculate neutrino-nucleus cross sections for W-boson and trident production using a unified framework that avoids the equivalent photon approximation, treating the hadronic coupling through virtual photons with full Standard Model matrix elements rather than the four-Fermi theory. They compute cross sections in three scattering regimes, coherent (σ ∝ Z²), diffractive (σ ∝ Z), and inelastic (σ ∝ Z), including nuclear effects such as Pauli blocking, and consider all neutrino flavors and final states across the energy range 10–10⁸ GeV.

## Key numbers

- The W-boson production cross section for νₑ → e⁻W⁺ on ¹⁶O reaches approximately 10% of the charged-current deep inelastic scattering cross section at energies around 10⁵–10⁷ GeV, based on prior claims that the authors re-evaluate.
- For neutrino-real photon scattering, the W-boson production cross section becomes constant above s_νγ > 10⁶ GeV², with σ_νγ ≃ 2√2 α G_F ≃ 10⁻³⁴ cm⁻².
- For trident production on real photons, the CC and CC+NC channels show a sharp increase above s_νγ = m_W² ≃ 6.5 × 10³ GeV² due to the W-boson resonance, with the cross section becoming constant above 10⁶ GeV² at σ ≃ 2√2 α G_F × Γ_{W→ν_ℓ ℓ⁻}/Γ_W ≃ 10⁻³⁵ cm⁻².
- IceCube has detected 60 starting events with reconstructed energies above 60 TeV over 7.5 years, giving a nominal precision scale of 13%.
- The claimed theoretical precision from parton-distribution functions at 10⁷ GeV is approximately 2% (CooperSarkar 2011) or approximately 1.5% (Connolly 2011).

## Context

This work extends prior calculations by Seckel (1997) and Alikhanov (2014, 2015) for W-boson production, and by Altmannshofer et al. (2014), Magill and Plestid (2016), Ge et al. (2017), Ballett et al. (2018), and Altmannshofer et al. (2019) for trident production, correcting their use of the equivalent photon approximation and four-Fermi theory approximations. The results enable more accurate predictions for neutrino cross sections at IceCube-Gen2 energies, as detailed in a companion paper, allowing improved tests of astrophysical neutrino spectra, flavor ratios, and beyond-Standard-Model physics.
