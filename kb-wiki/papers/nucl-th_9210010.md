# Saturation in the Nuclear Matter Problem

> Once density-dependent meson masses are introduced into the nuclear many-body problem, conventional mechanisms for saturation no longer operate. We suggest that a loop correction, essentially the introduction of the axial vector coupling $g_A(ρ,k)$ as function of density $ρ$ and momentum $k$, can bring about saturation, and present schematic calculations to illustrate this. We find that a very small density-dependence in $g_A$ gives rise to a very large saturating effect on nuclear matter. In fact, this new saturation mechanism turns out to be more powerful than any of the conventional mechanisms.

# Saturation in the Nuclear Matter Problem

## Key claim

The authors suggest that a loop correction introducing the axial vector coupling \(g_A(\rho,k)\) as a function of density \(\rho\) and momentum \(k\) can produce saturation in nuclear matter, but only after a near cancellation between repulsive and attractive effects reduces the effective density-dependence by roughly an order of magnitude compared to earlier estimates. This new saturation mechanism is claimed to be more powerful than any conventional mechanism, including the Pauli effect.

## Method

The authors start from the Bonn A potential and employ the relativistic density-dependent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock formalism. They introduce a density- and momentum-dependent axial vector coupling \(g_A(\rho,k)\) that multiplies both the pion-nucleon and scalar-nucleon coupling constants, with the effective density dependence parameterized as \((g_A)_{\text{eff}} = 1/(1 + b_{\text{eff}} \rho/\rho_0)\). The calculations compare cases where nucleon effective mass and meson masses (except the pion mass, held fixed) scale together, with and without this loop correction.

## Key numbers

- The vector-meson mass scaling is given by \(m_V(\rho)/m_V(0) \approx 1 - C \rho/\rho_0\) with \(C \approx 0.18\), from QCD sum rule calculations by Hatsuda et al. (1992).
- The nucleon effective mass from the SkM\(^\star\) potential is \(m^*_N/m_N = 1/(1 + 0.25 \rho/\rho_0)\), giving \(m^*_N/m_N = 0.8\) at \(\rho = \rho_0\).
- The effective density-dependence parameter used in the loop correction is \(b_{\text{eff}} = 0.035\) for Case 1 and \(b_{\text{eff}} = 0.045\) for Case 2, about an order of magnitude smaller than the \(b \approx (g_0')_{N\Delta}\) from earlier estimates.
- The saturation energy in both cases is \(-15.3\) MeV per nucleon, with saturation Fermi momenta \(k_F = 1.35\) fm\(^{-1}\) for Case 1 and \(k_F = 1.30\) fm\(^{-1}\) for Case 2.
- For comparison, relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations without this mechanism yield \(k_F = 1.40\) fm\(^{-1}\) and an energy of \(-15.6\) MeV per nucleon (Brown and Machleidt, 1990).

## Context

This work addresses the failure of conventional saturation mechanisms once density-dependent meson masses are introduced, following the scaling proposal of Brown and Rho (1991). The authors extend earlier work by Brown et al. (1990) that forced saturation in an ad hoc fashion using two-loop corrections, and instead propose a physically motivated loop correction based on the density dependence of \(g_A\) derived by Rho (1974) and Ohta and Wahamatsu (1975). The results support the connection between Walecka mean-field theory and QCD sum rule calculations (Cohen, Furnstahl, and Griegel, 1991; Drukarev and Levin, 1990, 1992), and suggest that future QCD sum rule calculations of nuclear binding energy must include the momentum-dependent \(g_A(\rho,k)\) to achieve saturation.
