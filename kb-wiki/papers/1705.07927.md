# Modelling double charge exchange response function for tetraneutron system

> This work is an attempt to model the $4n$ response function of a recent RIKEN experimental study of the double charge exchange $^4$He($^8$He,$^8$Be)$^4$n reaction in order to put in evidence an eventual enhancement mechanism of the zero energy cross section, including a near-threshold resonance. This resonance can indeed be reproduced only by adding to the standard nuclear Hamiltonian an unphysically large T=3/2 attractive 3n-force which destroys the neighboring nuclear chart. No other mechanisms like cusps or related structures were found.

## Key claim

The paper models the tetraneutron (4n) response function for the double charge exchange reaction at RIKEN and finds that a near-threshold resonance can only be reproduced by adding an unphysically large T=3/2 attractive three-nucleon force, which would destroy the neighboring nuclear chart; no other mechanisms like cusps or related structures were found to enhance the zero-energy cross section.

## Method

The authors factorized the double charge exchange transition amplitude into a fast part (carrying most of the 186 MeV/u kinetic energy) and a slow part involving the charge exchange between the initial 4He and final 4n states. They computed the response function using the complex scaling method applied to the four-body Schrodinger equation, solving both Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations in configuration space and a variational Gaussian expansion method. The nuclear Hamiltonian consisted of the Argonne AV8' two-neutron interaction plus three-nucleon forces in both T=1/2 and T=3/2 total isospin channels, with the T=3/2 attractive strength treated as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- The experimental resonance parameters reported by Kisamori et al. were E_R = 0.86 +/- 0.65 +/- 1.25 MeV and Gamma < 2.5 MeV.
- The critical W_1(T=3/2) strength value at which the 4n system is bound by E = -1.07 MeV was found to be W^0_1(T=3/2) = -36 MeV.
- The range of W_1 values compatible with the experimental findings was W_1 in [-36, -30] MeV.
- For W_1(T=3/2) = -30 MeV, the tetraneutron resonance parameters were E_R = 2.8 MeV and Gamma = 0.7 MeV.
- The T=1/2 three-nucleon force strength is W_1(T=1/2) = -2.04 MeV, a factor of about 15 smaller than the T=3/2 values required for a 4n resonance.
- For W_1(T=3/2) = -20 MeV, neighboring nuclei 4H, 4Li and 4He (T=1) become resonant, and the n-3H elastic cross section would strongly disagree with experimental data.
- The ratio of three-nucleon to two-nucleon potential energy contributions for the 4n system in the resonance region was 43.8% (at W_1 = -30 MeV) compared to 2.59% for 4He with physical T=1/2 strength.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Lazauskas et al., 2016) which showed the difficulty of accommodating a near-threshold 4n resonance without disturbing the nuclear chart. The paper contradicts claims of a resonant tetraneutron state from the RIKEN experiment (Kisamori et al., 2016) and from recent calculations by Pieper (2003), Andrei (2016), and Gandolfi (2016), attributing the difference to approximate methods used by those authors for dealing with the 4n continuum. The results agree with a recent work by Marek (2016) using No-Core Gamow Shell Model. The paper enables future experimental clarification through improved-statistics repetitions of the RIKEN experiment and new experiments at J-PARC on 4He(pi-,pi+)4n.
