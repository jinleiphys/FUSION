# Isovector EMC effect from global QCD analysis with MARATHON data

> We report the results of a Monte Carlo global QCD analysis of unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs), including for the first time constraints from ratios of $^3$He to $^3$H structure functions recently obtained by the MARATHON experiment at Jefferson Lab. Our simultaneous analysis of nucleon PDFs and nuclear effects in $A=2$ and $A=3$ nuclei reveals the first indication for an isovector nuclear EMC effect in light nuclei. We find that while the MARATHON data yield relatively weak constraints on the $F_2^n/F_2^p$ neutron to proton structure function ratio and on the $d/u$ PDF ratio, they suggest an enhanced nuclear effect on the $d$-quark PDF in the bound proton, questioning the assumptions commonly made in nuclear PDF analyses.

## Key claim

The first global QCD analysis that simultaneously extracts nucleon parton distribution functions (PDFs) and nuclear effects in A ≤ 3 nuclei, including MARATHON data, reveals an isovector component to the nuclear EMC effect in light nuclei. The MARATHON data primarily constrain nuclear off-shell corrections rather than the d/u PDF ratio, and the analysis does not assume the super-ratio R = R(³He)/R(³H) crosses unity at x = 0.31 as was done in the original MARATHON extraction.

## Method

The JAM Monte Carlo global QCD framework was used to perform a simultaneous fit of unpolarized PDFs and nuclear effects in A = 2 and A = 3 nuclei, including for the first time MARATHON ³He/³H structure function ratios and Jefferson Lab E03-103 ³He/D data. Nuclear effects were modeled within the weak binding approximation at the quark level, parameterizing off-shell corrections δu and δd separately to allow isospin-dependent nuclear effects, with the off-shell smearing functions computed from nuclear wave functions (Paris deuteron and KPSV ³He spectral functions). The analysis included DIS data from fixed target experiments (BCDMS, NMC, SLAC, Jefferson Lab) and HERA, Drell-Yan data from E866 and E906, W/Z boson data from Tevatron and LHC, and jet production data.

## Key numbers

- Overall χ² per datum of 1.11 for 4466 data points
- MARATHON ³He/³H data: χ²/N_dat = 0.63 for 22 points, fitted normalization 1.007(6)
- MARATHON D/p data: χ²/N_dat = 0.95 for 7 points, fitted normalization 1.019(4)
- JLab E03-103 ³He/D data: χ²/N_dat = 0.25 for 16 points, fitted normalization 1.006(10)
- Super-ratio R reaches mean value of 0.96 at x = 0.825, with uncertainties from ±0.4% at low x to ±3.5% at highest x
- Without MARATHON data, uncertainties on R vary between 1.3% and 6.5%
- Average off-shell smearing functions: ⟨f_p/D^(off)⟩ ≈ -4.3%, ⟨f_p/³He^(off)⟩ ≈ -6.8%, ⟨f_p/³H^(off)⟩ ≈ -9.5%
- When off-shell corrections are switched off, χ²/N_dat for MARATHON ³He/³H increases to 1.29

## Context

This work extends previous global QCD analyses of deuteron DIS data within the weak binding approximation (Accardi 2016, Alekhin 2017) by including A = 3 nuclei and allowing flavor-dependent off-shell corrections, whereas the Kulagin-Petti model (Kulagin 2004) assumed equal proton and neutron off-shell modifications. The analysis contradicts the KP model prediction that the super-ratio R rises to 1.01 at x = 0.825 and finds that the KP model uncertainties are significantly underestimated. The finding of an isovector EMC effect challenges standard nuclear PDF analyses (Kovarik 2015, Eskola 2016, NNPDF 2019, Walt 2019) which assume u^(p/A) = d^(n/A), and may impact nuclear PDF fits, heavy-ion collision simulations, and neutrino-nucleus interaction calculations.
