# Dilepton Production and Chiral Symmetry

> We discuss how dilepton production is related to chiral symmetry and its restoration. We then analyse presently available data by the CERES collaboration in this context. We find that the present data do not support any conclusions concerning the restoration of chiral symmetry. We finally provide a prediction for the dilepton spectrum for the just completed low energy (40 GeV) run at the SPS.

# Dilepton Production and Chiral Symmetry

## Key claim
The present CERES dilepton data do not support any conclusions concerning the restoration of chiral symmetry, because the contribution from a1-Dalitz decay (the key indicator of vector-axial-vector mixing) is at best a 2% effect and thus indistinguishable within experimental error bars.

## Method
The authors performed a transport calculation using the URQMD model to generate dilepton spectra for Pb+Au collisions at 160 GeV/A and Pb+Pb collisions at 40 GeV/A. They included contributions from a1-Dalitz decay and N(1520) Dalitz decay, calculating the N(1520) width using both relativistic and nonrelativistic parameterizations based on an analysis of pion photoproduction data from Feuster (1998). The transport results were compared with the preliminary CERES data from 1996.

## Key numbers
- The a1-Dalitz contribution is at best a 2% contribution to the total dilepton spectrum at 160 GeV/A.
- The N(1520) Dalitz decay contribution is about a factor of 4 below the contribution of the omega-Dalitz decay at 160 GeV/A.
- The N(1520) contribution was estimated by Song (1998) to be at most 50% of the omega-Dalitz decay in the mass range of 400-500 MeV.
- The prediction for the 40 GeV/A run assumes a mass resolution of 1% to account for the improved CERES spectrometer resolution.
- The prediction for 40 GeV/A uses central events with N_charge = 220.

## Context
This work extends the transport model calculations of Song (1998) by including the N(1520) resonance contribution and providing predictions for the lower energy SPS run at 40 GeV/A. The results contradict the expectation that dilepton measurements at SPS energies could provide clear evidence for chiral symmetry restoration, and agree with the findings of Haglin (1999) and Gale (1999) that the a1-Dalitz rate is considerably smaller than the pion annihilation rate. The paper enables future experimental tests at 40 GeV/A where the improved mass resolution should make the omega peak clearly visible, providing a strong constraint on model calculations.
