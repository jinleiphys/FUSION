# Hard Pion Chiral Perturbation Theory: What is it and is it relevant for $η^\prime$ decays?

> In this talk I give a short introduction to hard pion Chiral Perturbation Theory and an overview of the available applications $K\toππ$, $B,D\to D,π,K,η$ semileptonic decays and $χ_{c0,2}\toππ,KK$. It is pointed out that the reults for the semileptonic decays obey the LEET relation between $f_+$ and $f_-$.

# Hard Pion Chiral Perturbation Theory: What is it and is it relevant for η′ decays?

## Key claim
Hard Pion Chiral Perturbation Theory (HPChPT) can provide predictions from chiral symmetry for processes where not all pions are soft, by expanding the dependence on hard momenta and absorbing it into low-energy constants (LECs), but the method is not applicable to η′ decays.

## Method
The method identifies soft lines in a Feynman diagram, cuts them, and treats the remaining hard part as analytic in the soft momenta, describable by an effective Lagrangian with coupling constants dependent on the external hard momenta. The non-analytic dependence on light quark masses is then reproduced by loops in that effective Lagrangian, following the same logic used in baryon ChPT and heavy-meson ChPT where a large mass scale is separated from soft momenta.

## Context
This work extends the heavy-particle ChPT techniques developed for baryons (Weinberg, 1979; Gasser and Leutwyler, 1984) and vector mesons (Bijnens, Gosdzinsky, and Talavera) to processes with multiple hard scales. The method was tested against a two-loop calculation by Bijnens and Talavera (2002) and applied to K→ππ decays (Bijnens and Celis, 2011), semileptonic B,D→D,π,K,η decays (Bijnens and Jemos, 2011-2012), and charmonium decays (Bijnens and Jemos, 2012). The results for semileptonic decays obey the LEET relation between f₊ and f₋, and the chiral logarithm corrections improve agreement with CLEO data on D→π and D→K form factors. The paper notes that no η′ decays are amenable to the present method.
