# Do final state interactions obscure short range correlation effects in quasielastic $A(e,e'p)$ scattering?

> Are short range correlations in the ground state of the target nucleus (initial state correlations ISC)observable inexperiments on quasielastic $A(e,e'p)$ scattering at large missing momentum $p_{m}$? Will the missing momentum spectrum observed at CEBAF be overwhelmed by final state interactions of the struck proton? Taking the $^{4}He$ nucleus with a realistic model wave function for a testing ground, we present a full calculation of the missing momentum distribution in inclusive $^{4}He(e,e'p)$ scattering. We find a complex interplay and strong quantum-mechanical interference of FSI and ISC contributions to scattering at large $p_{m}$, with drastic change of the interference pattern from the (anti)parallel to transverse kinematics. We show that in all the kinematical conditions, for missing momenta $p_{m}\gsim 1$\,fm$^{-1}$, quasielastic scattering is dominated by FSI effects and the sensitivity to details of the nuclear ground state is lost. The origin of the FSI dominance is well understood and can be traced back to the anisotropic behaviour of FSI which is long ranged in the longitudinal direction and short ranged in the transverse direction in the opposite to the short ranged ground state correlations.

## Key claim

The paper argues that for missing momenta \(p_{m} \gsim 1\) fm\(^{-1}\), final state interactions (FSI) dominate quasielastic \(A(e,e'p)\) scattering and completely obscure the sensitivity to short range correlations in the nuclear ground state, contrary to the hope that such correlations could be cleanly extracted from CEBAF data.

## Method

The authors performed a full calculation of the missing momentum distribution \(W(\vec{p}_{m})\) in inclusive \(^{4}\text{He}(e,e'p)\) scattering using a realistic model wave function consisting of a harmonic oscillator mean field with a Jastrow correlation factor to represent initial state correlations (ISC). Final state interactions were described using Glauber multiple scattering theory, and the expansion of the combined FSI and Jastrow operators was evaluated semi-analytically to all orders in the pair correlation function and the Glauber profile function.

## Key numbers

* For missing momenta \(p_{m} \gsim 1\) fm\(^{-1}\), quasielastic scattering is dominated by FSI effects and sensitivity to details of the nuclear ground state is lost.
* The correlation radius used in the model is \(r_{c} \approx 0.5\)–\(0.6\) fm; the oscillator radius \(R_{0} = 1.29\) fm reproduces the experimental charge radius of \(^{4}\text{He}\) for a hard core correlation with \(C_{0}=1\) and \(r_{c}=0.5\) fm.
* The normalization of the FSI-induced contribution to the missing momentum distribution is larger than the corresponding ISC contribution by a factor \(\sim R_{0}/r_{c} \cdot 1/C_{0} \gg 1\), based on the estimate \(\sigma_{tot} \sim 4\pi b_{0}^{2}\) with \(b_{0} \approx 0.5\) fm and \(\sigma_{tot} \approx 40\) mb at \(T_{kin} \sim 1\) GeV.
* The large-\(p_{m}\) tail of the single-particle momentum distribution (SPMD) from ISC alone scales as \(\propto (r_{c}/R_{0})^{6} \exp(-r_{c}^{2} p_{m}^{2})\), and higher-order correlation terms renormalize the lowest-order quasi-deuteron contribution by about 40%.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of FSI effects in quasielastic scattering, including the authors' own classification in Helium4 (1995) and analyses of the deuteron by Deuter (1995) and Tensor (1995), which found strong FSI even in dilute nuclei. It contradicts the simple expectation that large-\(p_{m}\) spectra directly reflect ground state short range correlations, as proposed in Gottfried's classic work (1960s) and pursued in the quasi-deuteron approximation. The results imply that model-independent extraction of the single-particle momentum distribution from \(A(e,e'p)\) data is hardly possible, and they provide guidance for interpreting upcoming CEBAF experiments and for \(y\)-scaling analyses.
