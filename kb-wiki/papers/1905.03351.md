# Where we stand on structure dependence of ISGMR in the Zr-Mo region: Implications on K_\infty

> Isoscalar giant resonances, being the archetypal forms of collective nuclear behavior, have been studied extensively for decades with the goal of constraining bulk nuclear properties of the equation of state, as well as for modeling dynamical behaviors within stellar environments. An important such mode is the isoscalar electric giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) that can be understood as a radially symmetric density vibration within the saturated nuclear volume. The field has a few key open questions, which have been proposed and remain unresolved. One of the more provocative questions is the extra high-energy strength in the $A\approx 90$ region, which manifested in large percentages of the $E0$ sum rule in $^{92}$Zr and $^{92}$Mo above the main ISGMR peak. The purpose of this article is to introduce these questions within the context of experimental investigations into the phenomena in the zirconium and molybdenum isotopic chains, and to address, via a discussion of previously published and preliminary results, the implications of recent experimental efforts on extraction of the nuclear incompressibility from this data.

## Key claim

The paper disputes previously reported claims of anomalously large nuclear incompressibility in the A=92 isobars (92Zr and 92Mo) by presenting independent experimental results that show no extra high-energy ISGMR strength above 20 MeV, and that the ISGMR peak energies and extracted incompressibilities are consistent across the Zr-Mo isotopic chain within uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed inelastic alpha-particle scattering experiments at 386 MeV beam energy using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at RCNP, Osaka University, on isotopically enriched targets of 90,92Zr and 92,94,96Mo. Multipole-decomposition analysis (MDA) was carried out within the Distorted-Wave Born Approximation (DWBA) framework using a hybrid single-folding optical model, with instrumental background subtracted via the vertical-focusing technique of the spectrometer. The ISGMR strength distributions were fitted with Lorentzian functions, and moment ratios (constrained, centroid, and scaling) were calculated to extract finite nuclear incompressibilities.

## Key numbers

- ISGMR peak energy for 90Zr: 16.8 ± 0.2 MeV with 84 ± 2% EWSR exhausted
- ISGMR peak energy for 92Zr: 16.4 ± 0.1 MeV with 91 ± 2% EWSR exhausted
- ISGMR peak energy for 92Mo: 16.5 ± 0.1 MeV with 73 ± 2% EWSR exhausted
- ISGMR peak energy for 94Mo: 16.4 ± 0.2 MeV (high peak) with 86 ± 3% EWSR
- ISGMR peak energy for 96Mo: 16.4 ± 0.2 MeV (high peak) with 89 ± 3% EWSR
- Scaling-model moment ratio sqrt(m3/m1) for 90Zr: 18.9 ± 0.2 MeV; for 92Zr: 18.7 ± 0.1 MeV; for 92Mo: 18.6 ± 0.1 MeV; for 94Mo: 18.5 ± 0.5 MeV; for 96Mo: 18.4 ± 0.4 MeV
- Beam energy: E_alpha = 386 MeV
- Target areal densities: approximately 5 mg/cm^2 with isotopic purity approximately 95%

## Context

This work directly contradicts the findings of the Texas A&M group (Youngblood et al., 2013; Krishichayan et al., 2015; Youngblood et al., 2015; Button et al., 2017), who reported anomalously large K_A values for 92Zr and 92Mo due to extra high-energy E0 strength above the main ISGMR peak. The present results are consistent with the earlier measurements of 90Zr by Krishichayan et al. (2015) but find no evidence for the reported structure dependence. The paper enables a more reliable extraction of the nuclear matter incompressibility K_infinity from this mass region, as the ISGMR response in 90-96Zr and 92-96Mo now appears to follow the expected A^{-1/3} scaling with no anomalous shell effects.
