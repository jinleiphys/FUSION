# Non-Abelian Half-Quantum Vortices in ${}^{3}P_{2}$ Topological Superfluids

> ${}^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids realized in neutron stars are the largest topological quantum matters in our Universe. We establish the existence and stability of non-Abelian half-quantum vortices (HQVs) in ${}^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids with strong magnetic fields. Using a self-consistent microscopic approach, we find that a singly quantized vortex is energetically destabilized into a pair of two non-Abelian HQVs owing to the strongly spin-orbit-coupled pairing. We find a topologically protected Majorana fermion on each HQV, thereby providing two-fold non-Abelian anyons characterized by both Majorana fermions and a non-Abelian first homotopy group.

## Key claim

The paper establishes the existence and stability of non-Abelian half-quantum vortices (HQVs) in ${}^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids under strong magnetic fields, where a singly quantized vortex is energetically destabilized into a pair of two non-Abelian HQVs. The hedge is that this result holds specifically for the $D_{4}$-BN (dihedral-four biaxial nematic) phase, which is the ground state only in the presence of strong magnetic fields relevant to magnetars.

## Method

The authors use a self-consistent microscopic approach based on quasiclassical Eilenberger theory, solving the Eilenberger equation for the quasiclassical propagator $\check{g}(\bm{k}_{\F},\bm{R};\ii\omega_{n})$ together with a gap equation for the ${}^{3}P_{2}$ order parameter $\mathcal{A}_{\mu\nu}$, a $3\times3$ traceless symmetric tensor. They assume uniformity along the $z$-direction and determine the spatial profile of the order parameter in the $(x,y)$ plane at temperature $T = 0.4 T_{\mathrm{c}}$ and Zeeman field $V_{\mathrm{Z}} = 0.5 T_{\mathrm{c}}$. The interaction energy between HQVs is computed from the Luttinger-Ward energy functional, and the Bogoliubov-de Gennes (BdG) equation is solved to identify Majorana zero modes.

## Key numbers

- Temperature: $T = 0.4 T_{\mathrm{c}}$ and Zeeman field: $V_{\mathrm{Z}} = 0.5 T_{\mathrm{c}}$, where $T_{\mathrm{c}}$ is the critical temperature.
- Intervortex distance for the bound pair: $d_{\mathrm{v}} \simeq 10.7 \xi_{0}$, where $\xi_{0} = v_{\F}/(2\pi T_{\mathrm{c}})$ is the coherence length.
- Bulk gap: $\omega_{\mathrm{g}}/T_{\mathrm{c}} \sim 2.0$.
- Level spacing of vortex bound states: on the order of $\omega^{2}_{\mathrm{g}}/\varepsilon_{\mathrm{F}} \sim 0.255 T_{\mathrm{c}}$.
- Fermi momentum parameter: $k_{\F}\xi_{0} = 5$.

## Context

This work extends prior Ginzburg-Landau studies of ${}^{3}P_{2}$ superfluids in neutron stars (Richardson 1972, Sauls 1978, Masuda 2015, Yasui 2018) that predicted non-Abelian HQVs, by providing the first microscopic confirmation of their stability and the existence of Majorana fermions on each HQV. It contrasts with stabilization mechanisms in superfluid ${}^{3}$He-A phase (PhysRevLett.55.1184, 1985) and polar phases (Nagamura 2018, Tange 2020), which rely on Fermi liquid corrections or extrinsic impurity effects, whereas the present mechanism arises intrinsically from the strongly spin-orbit-coupled pairing. The findings enable new directions in topological quantum computation and neutron star physics, particularly regarding the pulsar glitch phenomenon (Marmorini 2020).
