# An $N/D$ study of the $S_{11}$ channel $πN$ scattering amplitude

> Extensive dynamical $N/D$ calculations are made in the study of $S_{11}$ channel low energy $π$N scatterings, based on various phenomenological model inputs of left cuts at tree level. The subtleties of the singular behavior of the partial wave amplitude at the origin of the complex $s$ plane are carefully analysed. Furthermore, { it is found that the dispersion representation for the phase shift, $δ$, has to be modified in the case of $π$N scatterings. An additional contribution from the dispersion integral exists, which is, however, almost exactly cancelled the contribution from two virtual poles located near the end points of the segment cut induced by $u$ channel nucleon exchanges.} Relying very little on the details of the dynamical inputs, the subthreshold resonance $N^*(890)$ survives.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a subthreshold resonance \(N^*(890)\) survives in the \(S_{11}\) channel of \(\pi N\) scattering, based on \(N/D\) calculations that rely very little on the details of the dynamical inputs. However, the dispersion representation for the phase shift must be modified for \(\pi N\) scatterings, and an additional contribution from the dispersion integral is almost exactly cancelled by contributions from two virtual poles located near the end points of the segment cut induced by \(u\) channel nucleon exchanges.

## Method

The authors performed extensive dynamical \(N/D\) calculations for the \(S_{11}\) channel low energy \(\pi N\) scattering amplitude, using various phenomenological model inputs for the left cuts at tree level. They solved the coupled \(N/D\) integral equations numerically, using the inverse matrix method, with the left-hand cut input extracted from tree-level chiral perturbation theory (\(\chi\)PT) amplitudes at \(\mathcal{O}(p^2)\) and \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\). The analytical continuation to the second Riemann sheet was performed using \(D^{\mathrm{II}}(s)=D(s)+2i\rho N(s)\) to search for poles.

## Key numbers

*   Subthreshold pole location from a toy model with a single pole input at \(s_1=0\): \(\sqrt{s_{pole}} = 0.810 - 0.125i\) GeV.
*   Subthreshold pole location from a toy model with a single pole input at \(s_1=-m_N^2\): \(\sqrt{s_{pole}} = 0.788 - 0.185i\) GeV.
*   The \(\mathcal{O}(p^1)\) \(N/D\) unitarization (with no free parameters) yields a pole location \(\sqrt{s}=1.08 - i0.23\) GeV, with a phase shift larger than data by roughly 5 degrees at \(\sqrt{s}=1.16\) GeV.
*   The subthreshold resonance \(N^*(890)\) from prior work has a pole mass \(\sqrt{s}=(0.895 \pm 0.081) - (0.164 \pm 0.023)i\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends prior studies by Wang et al. (2017, 2018) that suggested the existence of a subthreshold \(1/2^-\) nucleon resonance \(N^*(890)\) using the production representation (PKU representation) and \(K\)-matrix fits. The present paper provides further evidence by directly finding a pole in the \(S\) matrix element calculated via the \(N/D\) method, which is argued to be more reliable than previous unitarization techniques. The analysis enables a more robust confirmation of the \(N^*(890)\) resonance, and it clarifies subtle issues in the dispersion representation for unequal mass scatterings, such as the modification of the phase shift representation and the handling of singularities at \(s=0\).
