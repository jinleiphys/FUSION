# CPT, CP, and C transformations of fermions, and their consequences, in theories with B-L violation

> We consider the transformation properties of fermions under the discrete symmetries CPT, CP, and C in the presence of B-L violation. We thus generalize the analysis of the known properties of Majorana neutrinos, probed via neutrinoless double beta decay, to include the case of Dirac fermions with B-L violation, which can be probed via neutron-antineutron oscillations. We show that the resulting CPT phase has implications for the interplay of neutron-antineutron oscillations with external fields and sources and consider the differences in the Majorana dynamics of neutrinos and neutrons in the context of theories with self-conjugate isospin I=0 and I=1/2 fields.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes the known discrete-symmetry phase restrictions for Majorana neutrinos to Dirac fermions in theories with B-L violation, showing that the CPT phase constraint \((\eta_c \eta_p \eta_t)^2 = -1\) is necessary to preserve the CPT theorem and that certain Lorentz-invariant but CPT-odd operators vanish due to fermion anticommutation relations, resolving a conflict in the literature regarding neutron-antineutron oscillations in magnetic fields.

## Method

The authors construct Majorana fields from linear combinations of Dirac fields and apply the discrete symmetry transformations C, P, T, CP, and CPT to derive phase restrictions on the transformation parameters \(\eta_c\), \(\eta_p\), and \(\eta_t\). They then enumerate all lowest-mass-dimension B-L violating bilinear operators involving Dirac fields (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, tensor, and axial-tensor) and compute their transformation properties under CPT and CP, evaluating which operators vanish when fermion anticommutation relations are applied. The analysis is extended to bound and composite particles using the M.I.T. bag model for the neutron case.

## Key numbers

- The current empirical limit on the free \(n\)-\(\bar n\) oscillation time gives a Majorana mass \(\delta m = (\tau_{n \bar n})^{-1} \le 6 \times 10^{-29}\) MeV at 90% C.L. (BaldoCeolin 1994).
- The phase constraint \(\eta_p^2 = -1\) is derived, implying \(\mathbf{P}^2 \psi(x) \mathbf{P}^{-2} = -\psi(x)\).
- The combination \(\eta_c \eta_t\) must be real, while \(\eta_c \eta_p\) remains unconstrained.
- The CPT phase factor satisfies \((\eta_c \eta_p \eta_t)^2 = -1\), which flips the sign of each operator's CPT eigenvalue.

## Context

This work extends the seminal phase-constraint analyses of Kayser and Goldhaber (1983) and Kayser (1984) for Majorana neutrinos, as well as earlier work by Carruthers (1971) and Feinberg and Weinberg (1959) on self-conjugate multiplets, to Dirac fermions with B-L violation. The results resolve a conflict between Gardner (2014) and Berezhiani (2015) regarding whether transverse magnetic fields can enable neutron-antineutron oscillations, showing that the correct CPT phase eliminates spin-flip transitions and restores rotational invariance. The analysis enables future experimental searches for neutron-antineutron oscillations to properly account for discrete symmetry constraints, particularly in the design of experiments using external fields or sources.
