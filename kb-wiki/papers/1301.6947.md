# Calculation of parity non-conserving optical rotation in iodine at 1315 nm

> We examine the feasibility of a parity non-conserving (PNC) optical rotation experiment for the $^2$P$_{3/2}\rightarrow ^2$P$_{1/2}$ transition of atomic iodine at 1315 nm. The calculated $E1_{\rm PNC}$ to $M1$ amplitude ratio is $R=0.80(16)\times 10^{-8}$. We show that very large PNC rotations (greater than 10 $μ$rad) are obtained for iodine-atom column densities of $\sim 10^{22}$ cm$^{-2}$, which can be produced by increasing the effective interaction pathlength by a factor of $\sim 10^4$ with a high-finesse optical cavity. The simulated signals indicate that measurement of the nuclear anapole moment is feasible, and that a 1% PNC precision measurement should resolve the inconsistency between previous measurements in Cs and Tl.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a parity non-conserving (PNC) optical rotation experiment on the 1315 nm transition of atomic iodine is feasible, but only if a high-finesse optical cavity is used to increase the effective interaction pathlength by a factor of about 10^4, because the calculated E1_PNC to M1 amplitude ratio for iodine (R = 0.80(16) × 10^-8) is about an order of magnitude smaller than that for Tl, Bi, and Pb.

## Method

The authors performed configuration interaction (CI) calculations for seven valence electrons of neutral iodine in the V^N approximation, treating the 5s, 5p_1/2, and 5p_3/2 states as valence states. They used the B-spline technique to construct single-electron orbitals and applied the random-phase approximation (RPA) to account for external field effects, with summation over intermediate many-electron states via the Dalgarno-Lewis technique. The PNC optical rotation signal was then simulated for various column densities and Lorentzian broadening widths, using the calculated E1_PNC and M1 amplitudes to predict the rotation angle and hyperfine resolution.

## Key numbers

- The calculated E1_PNC to M1 amplitude ratio is R = 0.80(16) × 10^-8.
- The reduced matrix element for the E1_PNC transition amplitude between the ^2P^o_3/2 and ^2P^o_1/2 states of ^127I is 3.35(67) × 10^-11 i e a_B.
- The M1 amplitude between the ^2P^o_3/2 and ^2P^o_1/2 states is 0.00420 e a_B.
- PNC optical rotations greater than 10 μrad are obtained for iodine-atom column densities of about 10^22 cm^-2.
- The iodine-atom column density of 10^22 cm^-2 is produced by increasing the effective interaction pathlength by a factor of about 10^4 with a high-finesse optical cavity.
- The recombination rate for I(^2P_3/2) atoms is k_r = 3.1(3) × 10^-30 cm^2 molecule^-2 s^-1 at 308 K.
- The absorption cross section of I_2 at 308 K peaks at σ = 2.8 × 10^-18 cm^2 for λ ≈ 530 nm.
- The collision-broadened Lorentz widths for the M1 transition are 4.3 MHz/Torr for I collision partners and 10.3 MHz/Torr for I_2 collision partners at room temperature.
- The predicted values for the nuclear spin-dependent parameter κ in ^127I are 0.13(20) from Tl and -0.38(6) from Cs, using the single-valence-proton model.

## Context

This work extends the cavity-enhanced optical rotation technique previously proposed by Bougas et al. (2012) for Hg and Xe to a new candidate, atomic iodine, which has an odd-proton nucleus (^127I). The paper aims to help resolve the inconsistency between the anapole moment measurements in Cs (Wood et al., 1997) and Tl (Vetter et al., 1995), as well as discrepancies with theoretical nuclear calculations reviewed by Ginges and Flambaum (2004), Bouchiat and Piketty (1991), and Haxton and Wieman (2001). A successful measurement on iodine would enable a test of the nuclear anapole moment for an odd-proton nucleus, complementing ongoing PNC experiments on Yb and Dy at Berkeley, Rb and Fr at TRIUMF, Ra^+ at KVI Groningen, and Ba^+ at the University of Washington.
