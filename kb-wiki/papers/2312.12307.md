# Quarkonium transport in weakly and strongly coupled plasmas

> We report on progress in the nonperturbative understanding of quarkonium dynamics inside a thermal plasma. The time evolution of small-size quarkonium is governed by two-point correlation functions of chromoelectric fields dressed with an adjoint Wilson line, known in this context as generalized gluon distributions (GGDs). The GGDs have been calculated in both weakly and strongly coupled plasmas by using perturbative and holographic methods. Strikingly, the results of our calculations for a strongly coupled plasma indicate that the quarkonium dissociation and recombination rates vanish in the transport descriptions that assume quarkonium undergoes Markovian dynamics. However, this does not imply that the dynamics is trivial. As a starting point to explore the phenomenological consequences of the result at strong coupling, we show a calculation of the $Υ(1S)$ formation probability in time-dependent perturbation theory. This is a first step towards the development of a transport formalism that includes non-Markovian effects, which, depending on how close the as of yet undetermined nonperturbative QCD result of the GGDs is to the strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ SYM result, could very well dominate over the Markovian ones in quark-gluon plasma produced at RHIC and the LHC.

## Key claim

The authors show that in a strongly coupled plasma (modeled by \(\mathcal{N}=4\) supersymmetric Yang-Mills theory), the quarkonium dissociation and recombination rates vanish under the Markovian approximations used in standard transport formalisms, but this does not mean the dynamics is trivial, non-Markovian effects can still produce nonzero formation probabilities, as demonstrated for the \(\Upsilon(1S)\) state.

## Method

The authors compute generalized gluon distributions (GGDs), two-point correlation functions of chromoelectric fields dressed with an adjoint Wilson line, using perturbative QCD for weakly coupled plasmas and holographic AdS/CFT methods for strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM theory. They then derive a non-Markovian expression for the singlet formation probability from an initial octet state, without assuming the quantum Brownian motion or quantum optical limits, and evaluate it numerically for the \(\Upsilon(1S)\) using a Karsch-Mehr-Satz potential and a Bjorken flow temperature profile.

## Key numbers

* The strongly coupled \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM time-ordered GGD is given by \([g_{\rm adj}^{{\mathcal{T}}}](\omega) = \frac{ (\pi T)^3 \sqrt{\lambda} \, T_F}{{ 4}\pi} \left( \frac{-i}{F^-_{|\omega|}(0)} \frac{\partial^3 F^-_{|\omega|}}{\partial \xi^3}(0) \right)\), with \(\lambda = g^2 N_c\) and \(\Omega = \omega/(\pi T)\).
* For a flowing medium, the effective temperature experienced by quarkonium is modified as \(T \to T_{\rm eff} = \sqrt{\gamma} \, T\).
* The \(\Upsilon(1S)\) formation probability calculation uses a Bjorken flow temperature profile \(T(\tau) = (\tau_f/\tau)^{1/3} T_f\) with \(T_f = 155 \, {\rm MeV}\), \(\tau_i = 0.6 \, {\rm fm/c}\), and \(\tau_f = 10 \, {\rm fm/c}\).
* The coupling constant for the numerical illustration is set to \(\lambda = 13\), corresponding to \(g \approx 2.1\).

## Context

This work extends the open quantum system and pNRQCD framework for quarkonium dynamics developed by Brambilla (2016, 2017) and Yao (2018), which assumed Markovian dynamics in the quantum Brownian motion and quantum optical limits respectively. The authors show that those limits give vanishing rates for strongly coupled plasmas, contradicting the physical expectation of bound-state formation and dissociation. This result enables the development of a new transport formalism that includes non-Markovian effects, which could dominate over Markovian ones in quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and the LHC, depending on how close the true nonperturbative QCD result for the GGDs is to the \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM result.
