# Core destruction in knockout reactions

> A model is presented to calculate projectile core destruction in knockout reactions. It incorporates physics arguments similar to the formulation of the state of the art theory to calculate stripping and diffraction dissociation cross sections in heavy ion collisions with bombarding energies around 100 MeV/nucleon and larger. It is shown that secondary collisions between the incoming and struck nucleons and the projectile core decrease the core survival probability by as much as 9.5\%. However, no clear evidence is found for reduction of the cross section with increasing binding energy of the removed nucleon.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model for projectile core destruction in knockout reactions that predicts a reduction of core survival probability by up to 9.5%, but finds no clear evidence that this reduction correlates with the binding energy of the removed nucleon, contrary to recent suggestions that such an effect could explain the spectroscopic factor "quenching puzzle."

## Method

The author modifies the standard eikonal formalism for stripping cross sections by introducing an additional factor (1 - ⟨|S_nc|²⟩) that accounts for the probability that a nucleon from the first nucleon-nucleon collision undergoes a secondary scattering with the projectile core, potentially destroying it. The secondary scattering probability is computed by averaging the nucleon-core S-matrix over scattering angles weighted by the free nucleon-nucleon elastic differential cross section, using parametrizations of experimental pp and np data from the EXFOR library. The calculations employ ab initio No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) wavefunctions for the single-particle states.

## Key numbers

- Core destruction reduces the stripping cross section by as much as 9.5% (for ¹²C(¹²C,¹¹B) at 250 MeV/nucleon, from 64.68 mb to 58.70 mb)
- For ¹²C(⁸B,⁷Be) at 285 MeV/nucleon (proton separation energy S_p = 0.137 MeV), the stripping cross section decreases from 42.49 mb to 39.02 mb, a change of -8.17%
- For ¹²C(¹⁰C,⁹C) at 120 MeV/nucleon (neutron separation energy S_n = 21.28 MeV), the stripping cross section decreases from 44.57 mb to 40.50 mb, a change of -9.31%
- For ⁹Be(¹⁰Be,⁹Li) at 80 MeV/nucleon (S_n = 19.64 MeV), the stripping cross section decreases from 35.33 mb to 32.47 mb, a change of -8.09%
- For ¹²C(¹⁶O,¹⁵N) at 2100 MeV/nucleon (S_n = 22.04 MeV), the stripping cross section decreases from 46.90 mb to 42.48 mb, a change of -9.42%

## Context

This work extends the standard eikonal formalism for knockout reactions developed by Hussein (1985), Bertulani (1992, 2004, 2006), and Hencken (1996) by adding a core destruction mechanism. It directly addresses recent experimental suggestions by Panin (2019) and Wamers (2023) that core destruction might be important, and contrasts with the model of Gomez-Ramos (2023) which found a strong binding-energy dependence of core destruction effects using a non-local density formalism and compound nucleus formation. The results enable future revisions of spectroscopic factors extracted from knockout experiments, though the author argues that the "quenching puzzle" may be better resolved by using ab initio wavefunctions as shown in PhysRevC.104.L061602 and PhysRevC.105.024613 rather than through core destruction effects.
