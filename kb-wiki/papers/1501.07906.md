# Glueball Decay Rates in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto Model

> We revisit and extend previous calculations of glueball decay rates in the Sakai-Sugimoto model, a holographic top-down approach for QCD with chiral quarks based on D8 probe branes in Witten's holographic model of nonsupersymmetric Yang-Mills theory. The rates for decays into two pions, two vector mesons, four pions, and the strongly suppressed decay into four pi0 are worked out quantitatively, using a range of the 't Hooft coupling which closely reproduces the decay rate of rho and omega mesons and also leads to a gluon condensate consistent with QCD sum rule calculations. The lowest holographic glueball, which arises from a rather exotic polarization of gravitons in the supergravity background, turns out to have a significantly lower mass and larger width than the two widely discussed glueball candidates f0(1500) and f0(1710). The lowest nonexotic and predominantly dilatonic scalar mode, which has a mass of 1487 MeV in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, instead provides a narrow glueball state, and we conjecture that only this nonexotic mode should be identified with a scalar glueball component of f0(1500) or f0(1710). Moreover the decay pattern of the tensor glueball is determined, which is found to have a comparatively broad total width when its mass is adjusted to around or above 2 GeV.

## Key claim

The Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model predicts two distinct scalar glueball modes, and only the predominantly dilatonic mode (mass 1487 MeV) should be identified with a scalar glueball component of the experimental candidates f0(1500) or f0(1710), while the lowest holographic glueball mode (mass 855 MeV) arises from an exotic polarization and appears ill-suited to model the lowest glueball of QCD as found in lattice simulations.

## Method

The authors calculate glueball decay rates by deriving effective interaction Lagrangians from the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, a holographic top-down approach based on D8 probe branes in Witten's model of nonsupersymmetric Yang-Mills theory. They compute the normalization factors for the glueball modes from 11-dimensional supergravity, then couple these modes to meson fields from the D8-brane Dirac-Born-Infeld action to obtain decay rates into two pions, two vector mesons, four pions, and four pi0 mesons. The 't Hooft coupling is chosen to reproduce the decay rate of rho and omega mesons and to yield a gluon condensate consistent with QCD sum rule calculations.

## Key numbers

- Lowest exotic scalar glueball mass: 855 MeV (from M_G = sqrt(7.30834/9) * M_KK with M_KK = 949 MeV)
- Predominantly dilatonic scalar glueball mass: 1487 MeV (from M_D = sqrt(22.0966/9) * M_KK)
- Tensor glueball mass: 1487 MeV (degenerate with dilatonic scalar, from M_T = sqrt(22.0966/9) * M_KK)
- Lowest pseudoscalar glueball mass: 1789 MeV (from M_P = sqrt(31.9853/9) * M_KK)
- Excited exotic scalar glueball mass: 2168 MeV (from M_E* = sqrt(46.9855/9) * M_KK)
- Excited dilatonic/tensor glueball mass: 2358 MeV (from M_D* = M_T* = sqrt(55.5833/9) * M_KK)
- Kaluza-Klein mass scale M_KK = 949 MeV, fixed by matching the rho meson mass to 776 MeV
- Pion decay constant f_pi^2 = (1/(54 pi^4)) * lambda * N_c * M_KK^2
- Normalization factor for exotic scalar: [H_G(R)]^{-1} = 0.0069183 * lambda^{1/2} * N_c * M_KK
- Normalization factor for dilatonic scalar: [H_D(R)]^{-1} = 0.033588 * lambda^{1/2} * N_c * M_KK
- Normalization factor for tensor: [H_T(R)]^{-1} = 0.013712 * lambda^{1/2} * N_c * M_KK

## Context

This work extends the earlier holographic glueball decay calculations of Hashimoto, Tan, and Terashima (2007) by including important corrections and by considering the predominantly dilatonic scalar mode and the tensor glueball in addition to the lowest exotic scalar mode. The results contradict the identification of the lowest holographic glueball with the f0(1500) or f0(1710) candidates, instead suggesting the dilatonic mode as a more plausible glueball component. The paper enables quantitative comparisons between holographic predictions and experimental glueball candidates, and provides a framework for future studies of glueball-meson interactions in the Witten-Sakai-Sugimoto model, including the possibility of extrapolating to decays into massive pseudo-Goldstone bosons.
