# Higher order anisotropies in hydrodynamics

> In the last years it has been revealed that if measuring relative to higher order event planes $Ψ_n$, higher order flow coefficients $v_n$ for $n>2$ can be measured. It also turned out that Bose-Einstein (HBT) correlation radii also show 3rd order oscillations if measured versus the third order event plane $Ψ_3$. In this paper we investigate how these observables can be described via analytic hydro solutions and hydro parameterizations. We also investigate the time evolution of asymmetry coefficients and the mixing of velocity field asymmetries and density asymmetries.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that exact analytic solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics can be extended to include higher-order azimuthal asymmetries (multipole symmetries) beyond the usual spherical, axial, or ellipsoidal geometries, and that these solutions yield higher-order flow coefficients compatible with experimental data; however, the analytic solutions assume that the anisotropies do not change over time due to the lack of pressure gradients and the Hubble-flow, so the time evolution of asymmetries must be studied separately via numerical methods.

## Method

The authors constructed a generalized scale variable \(s = \sum_N \frac{r^N}{R^N}(1 + \epsilon_N \cos(N(\phi - \psi_N))) + \frac{z^N}{R^N}\) that allows arbitrary-shaped initial distributions in a 1+3D relativistic hydrodynamic solution with a 3D Hubble-flow velocity profile. They calculated hadronic observables from this analytic solution and fitted them to PHENIX data on higher-order harmonics in 200 GeV Au+Au collisions. Separately, they performed numerical simulations using a multi-stage predictor-corrector finite volume scheme (GFORCE flux) to study the time evolution of asymmetry coefficients under the influence of pressure gradients, viscosity, and varying speed of sound, and they developed a multipole version of the Buda-Lund model to investigate the mixing of velocity-field and density-field anisotropies.

## Key numbers

- Fit parameters to PHENIX 200 GeV Au+Au data included \(\epsilon_N\) for \(N=2,3,4\), with \(T_0\) and \(\tau_0\) fixed from spectra and HBT comparisons of a similar model.
- In the numerical viscosity study, a viscosity of \(\mu = 10\ \mathrm{MeV}\cdot\mathrm{fm}\) was used, which made the disappearance of asymmetries slower compared to the viscosity-free case.
- The speed of sound parameter \(\kappa = c_s^{-2}\) was varied; increasing \(\kappa\) (decreasing speed of sound) made the disappearance of asymmetries slower in both nonrelativistic and relativistic calculations.

## Context

This work extends the first 1+3D relativistic solution with ellipsoidal geometry from Csorgo (2003) to include higher-order multipole symmetries, building on the analytic framework of Csanad (2014). It provides a tool for describing event-by-event fluctuating initial conditions in heavy-ion collisions, complementing numerical calculations such as those by Chatterjee (2014) and Yan (2015). The results enable future studies of how spatial and momentum-space anisotropies separately contribute to measured flow coefficients, and the numerical framework allows investigation of viscosity and speed-of-sound effects on the time evolution of asymmetries.
