# Dynamical generation of hadronic resonances in effective models with derivative interactions

> Light scalar mesons can be understood as dynamically generated resonances. They arise as 'companion poles' in the propagators of quark-antiquark seed states when accounting for hadronic loop contributions to the self-energies of the latter. Such a mechanism may explain the overpopulation in the scalar sector - there exist more resonances with total spin $J=0$ than can be described within a quark model. Along this line, we study an effective Lagrangian approach where the isovector state $a_{0}(1450)$ couples via both non-derivative and derivative interactions to pseudoscalar mesons. It is demonstrated that the propagator has two poles: a companion pole corresponding to $a_{0}(980)$ and a pole of the seed state $a_{0}(1450)$. The positions of these poles are in quantitative agreement with experimental data. Besides that, we investigate similar models for the isodoublet state $K_{0}^{\ast}(1430)$ by performing a fit to $πK$ phase shift data in the $I=1/2,$ $J=0$ channel. We show that, in order to fit the data accurately, a companion pole for the $K_{0}^{\ast}(800)$, that is, the light $κ$, is required. A large-$N_{c}$ study confirms that both resonances below $1$ GeV are predominantly four-quark states, while the heavy states are quarkonia.

## Key claim

Light scalar mesons such as the \(a_0(980)\) and \(K_0^*(800)\) can be dynamically generated as "companion poles" in the propagators of heavier quark-antiquark seed states (\(a_0(1450)\) and \(K_0^*(1430)\) respectively) when hadronic loop contributions to the self-energies are included, but only if both derivative and non-derivative interaction terms are present in the effective Lagrangian.

## Method

The author constructed effective Lagrangian models for the isovector \(a_0(1450)\) and the isodoublet \(K_0^*(1430)\) coupling to pseudoscalar mesons via both non-derivative and derivative interactions. For the isovector sector, the model parameters were constrained by requiring two poles in the propagator (the seed state and a companion pole) and reproducing the experimental branching ratios of \(a_0(1450)\). For the isodoublet sector, a fit to experimental \(\pi K\) phase shift data in the \(I=1/2, J=0\) channel was performed using four variants of the model (with and without derivative couplings, and with a modified form factor). A large-\(N_c\) study was conducted for both sectors to distinguish four-quark from quark-antiquark nature of the resonances.

## Key numbers

* The heavy isovector state \(a_0(1450)\) has a mass of \(1474 \pm 19\) MeV according to the Particle Data Group (PDG).
* The heavy isodoublet state \(K_0^*(1430)\) has a mass of \(1425 \pm 50\) MeV according to the PDG.
* The light isodoublet state \(K_0^*(800)\) (the \(\kappa\)) has a mass below 1 GeV; the fit to \(\pi K\) phase shift data extracted its pole position with smaller errors than previous PDG results.
* The light isovector state \(a_0(980)\) has a mass below 1 GeV; the large-\(N_c\) study showed its pole approached the real axis for decreasing couplings and disappeared at a critical value, while the \(a_0(1450)\) pole became that of a stable particle.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Tornqvist and Roos (1990s) and Boglione and Pennington (2000s) on dynamical generation of scalar mesons, clarifying that Boglione and Pennington's model did not actually produce a pole for \(a_0(1450)\) at the correct mass. The results support the interpretation that the light scalar mesons below 1 GeV (\(a_0(980)\) and \(K_0^*(800)\)) are predominantly four-quark states generated by hadronic loops, while the heavier states (\(a_0(1450)\) and \(K_0^*(1430)\)) are ordinary quarkonia. The next step would be to test this mechanism for the isoscalar (\(I=0\)) sector and to fix the eleven free parameters of the extended Linear Sigma Model (eLSM) through a simultaneous fit to a larger set of experimental data.
