# Reply to the comment on 'Validity of certain soft photon amplitudes'

> We respond to the accompanying Comment on our paper, 'Validity of certain soft photon amplitudes'. While we hope the discussion here clarifies the issues, we have found nothing which leads to a change in the original conclusions of our paper.

# Reply to the comment on 'Validity of certain soft photon amplitudes'

## Key claim

The authors maintain that none of the objections raised by Liou et al. in their Comment require any change to the original conclusions of Welsh and Fearing (1999), because the alleged issues either rely on irrelevant O(k) terms that are beyond the domain of validity of a soft photon approximation, or have been superseded by subsequent properly symmetrized amplitudes.

## Method

The authors analyze two objections from Liou et al. regarding generalized soft photon approximations (SPA) for processes such as proton-proton bremsstrahlung. First, they address the symmetrization of amplitudes for identical particles, showing that the ad hoc Δᵢ terms added to enforce gauge invariance can be chosen in infinitely many ways, all valid through O(k⁰). Second, they examine the claimed error size from using an unsymmetrized TuTts amplitude, demonstrating that the difference between properly symmetrized and unsymmetrized amplitudes can be either O(k/k) or O(k) depending on how the symmetrized elastic amplitude is defined, and that this ambiguity is irrelevant for a true SPA.

## Key numbers

- The generalized SPA's differ from each other only by terms of O(k), as stated in the original paper.
- The difference between the properly symmetrized TuTts amplitude (Eq. 27) and the unsymmetrized prescription (Eq. 28) is O(k/k) when using one definition of the symmetrized elastic amplitude, but O(k) when using an alternative definition symmetric in u ↔ t.
- The O(k²/k) structures at O(k) that Liou et al. object to are explicitly acknowledged to be irrelevant for a SPA valid only through O(k⁰).

## Context

This reply defends the conclusions of Welsh and Fearing (1999) against the Comment by Liou et al. (2000). The original paper was motivated in part by Liou et al. (1995) which applied generalized SPA's (TuTts and TsTts) to proton-proton bremsstrahlung. The discussion clarifies that while Liou et al. (1998) later derived a properly symmetrized amplitude matching one of the examples given in the original paper, the fundamental ambiguities in generalized SPA's remain: for small photon momentum k all SPA's agree, but for the larger k relevant to modern proton-proton bremsstrahlung measurements, model-dependent choices are required that have nothing to do with SPA per se.
