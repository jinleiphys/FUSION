# Femtoscopy in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions and its Relation to Bulk Properties of QCD Matter

> Using a viscous hydrodynamic model coupled to a hadronic cascade code, numerous features of the dynamics and equilibrium properties are explored for their impact on femtoscopic measurements. The equation of state, viscous parameters and initial conditions are investigated. We find that femtoscopy is affected by numerous model features at the 10% level, and that by including features and adjusting unknown parameters, one can explain experimental source size measurements to better than 10%.

## Key claim
Using a viscous hydrodynamic model coupled to a hadronic cascade code, the authors find that femtoscopic source sizes are affected by numerous model features at the 10% level, and that by including these features and adjusting unknown parameters, one can explain experimental source size measurements to better than 10%.

## Method
A relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model based on a modified Israel-Stewart formalism was coupled to a hadronic cascade code to simulate the evolution of heavy ion collisions under assumptions of azimuthal symmetry and boost invariance. The hydrodynamic module evolved the system until the energy density fell below a breakup density of 400 MeV/fm³, at which point particles were generated and passed to the cascade module, which simulated hadronic interactions and decays using a fixed cross section of 15 mb along with resonant absorptions and decays. Two-pion correlation functions were generated from the output phase-space points and fitted to Gaussian source parameters \(R_{\rm out}\), \(R_{\rm side}\), and \(R_{\rm long}\) as functions of transverse momentum \(k_t\).

## Key numbers
- Experimental source size measurements from the STAR collaboration at RHIC were reproduced to better than 10% by the model.
- The initial density profile radius can vary by approximately 10% depending on the theoretical picture (e.g., color-glass-condensate-inspired versus wounded nucleon models).
- Longitudinal acceleration is known to affect femtoscopic results at the 5-10% level.
- The hydrodynamic code conserved entropy to better than 0.2% when run in the ideal limit.
- The breakup density for transitioning from hydrodynamics to the cascade was set to 400 MeV/fm³.
- The fixed cross section used in the cascade was 15 mb, with an energy-averaged cross-section for the hadronic gas relaxation time of 25 mb.
- All collisions before 25 fm/c were simulated in the cascade.
- For generating correlation functions, particles with transverse momenta within 5 MeV/c of the pair momentum were selected.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of femtoscopy in relativistic heavy ion collisions by using a full viscous hydrodynamic model coupled to a cascade code, rather than simpler blast-wave models or ideal hydrodynamics. The authors compare their results to experimental data from the STAR collaboration at RHIC, and note that earlier blast-wave analyses (Kisiel 2008, Retiere 2003) suggested negative \(x-t\) correlations and shorter emission durations, whereas this model finds positive \(x-t\) correlations and longer emission durations. The study enables future quantitative extraction of the equation of state and transport coefficients of QCD matter by demonstrating that femtoscopic observables are sensitive to the equation of state, shear and bulk viscosities, and initial conditions at the 10% level.
