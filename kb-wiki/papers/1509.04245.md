# Proton-lambda correlation functions at the LHC with account for residual correlations

> The theoretical analysis of $\bar{p}-Î›\oplus p-\barÎ›$ correlation function in 10% most central Au+Au collisions at RHIC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV shows that the contribution of residual correlations is the necessary factor to obtain a satisfactory description of the experimental data. A neglecting of the residual correlation effect, leads to unrealistically low source radius, about 2 times smaller than the corresponding value for $p-Î›\oplus \bar{p}-\barÎ›$ case, when one fits the experimental correlation function within Lednicky-Lyuboshitz analytical model. Recently an approach accounting effectively for residual correlations for the baryon-antibaryon correlation function was proposed, and a good RHIC data description was reached with the source radius extracted from the hydrokinetic model (HKM). The $\bar{p}-Î›$ scattering length, as well as the parameters characterizing the residual correlation effect --- annihilation dip amplitude and its inverse width --- were extracted from the corresponding fit. In this paper we use these extracted values and simulated in HKM source functions for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV to predict the corresponding $pÎ›$ and $p\barÎ›$ correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the $p\Lambda$ and $\bar{p}\Lambda$ correlation functions in 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV will be slightly more narrow than those at RHIC, with the LHC source radius $r_0=3.76$ fm being about 1.15 times larger than the corresponding RHIC radius, provided that residual correlations are accounted for using parameters previously extracted from RHIC data.

## Method

The authors use the Lednick\'y-Lyuboshitz analytical model to compute baryon-baryon correlation functions, and a modified formula that includes a Gaussian residual correlation term for baryon-antibaryon functions. Source radii are extracted from Gaussian fits to angle-averaged source functions generated by the hydrokinetic model (HKM) for 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV. For the $\bar{p}\Lambda$ case, the scattering length parameters ($\Re f_0$, $\Im f_0$) and the residual correlation parameters ($\beta$, $R$) are taken from previous fits to RHIC data, with $R$ scaled proportionally to the source radius ratio.

## Key numbers

- Source radius for both $p\Lambda$ and $\bar{p}\Lambda$ at LHC: $r_0 = 3.76$ fm, about 1.15 times larger than the RHIC value.
- Real part of $\bar{p}\Lambda$ scattering length: $\Re f_0 = 0.14 \pm 0.66$ fm (taken from RHIC fit).
- Imaginary part of $\bar{p}\Lambda$ scattering length: $\Im f_0 = 1.53 \pm 1.31$ fm (taken from RHIC fit).
- Residual correlation strength parameter: $\beta = 0.034 \pm 0.005$ (taken from RHIC fit).
- Residual correlation inverse width at LHC: $R_{\mathrm{LHC}} = 0.55 \pm 0.06$ fm, scaled from $R_{\mathrm{RHIC}} = 0.48 \pm 0.05$ fm by factor $r_0^{\mathrm{LHC}}/r_0^{\mathrm{RHIC}} \approx 1.15$.
- LHC $p\Lambda$ correlation function intercept is about 1.7, and the Gaussian width differs from RHIC by a factor of approximately 1.13.

## Context

This work extends the method developed by Shapoval et al. (2015) for describing $\bar{p}\Lambda$ correlation functions at RHIC, where residual correlations were shown to be necessary to avoid unrealistically small source radii. The predictions are based on HKM simulations that successfully describe bulk observables at both RHIC and LHC, as previously shown by the same group. The results provide testable predictions for the ALICE experiment at LHC and demonstrate how residual correlation parameters extracted at RHIC can be scaled to LHC energies, enabling future extraction of $\bar{p}\Lambda$ scattering parameters from LHC data.
