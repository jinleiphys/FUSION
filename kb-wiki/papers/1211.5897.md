# Jet Quenching in Heavy-Ion Collisions - The Transition Era from RHIC to LHC

> A status report on the jet quenching physics in heavy-ion collisions is given as it appears after more than 10 years of collecting and analysing data at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) and ~1.5 years of physics at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC). The (theoretical) predictions and expectations before the start of the LHC program are contrasted with the most recent experimental results, focussing on the nuclear modification factor R_{AA}, the elliptic flow v_2 of high-p_T particles, and on the problem of initial conditions.

# Jet Quenching in Heavy-Ion Collisions - The Transition Era from RHIC to LHC

## Key claim
While early LHC data show that the nuclear modification factor R_AA increases with p_T as originally predicted by pQCD, the magnitude of jet quenching at LHC for p_T > 10 GeV is surprisingly similar to RHIC, contradicting density-dependent energy loss models that predicted stronger suppression at LHC energies.

## Method
The paper reviews and contrasts theoretical predictions for jet quenching made before the LHC heavy-ion program with first experimental results from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS collaborations. It focuses on three observables: the nuclear modification factor R_AA, the elliptic flow v_2 of high-p_T particles, and the role of initial conditions modeled by either the Glauber model or the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework. A generic analytic absorption model (dE/dx = -kappa E^a x^z T^c) is introduced to interpolate between weakly-coupled pQCD and strongly-coupled AdS/CFT energy loss prescriptions.

## Key numbers
- Center-of-mass energy per nucleon increases by a factor of ten from RHIC (200 GeV) to LHC (2.76 TeV)
- Particle multiplicity at LHC rises by a factor of approximately 2.2 compared to RHIC, corresponding to a 30% increase in temperature
- At RHIC, hadron suppression of R_AA(p_T) ~ 0.2 is observed, staying nearly flat up to p_T = 20 GeV
- Photon R_AA(p_T > 4 GeV) ~ 1 at RHIC, confirming direct photons do not interact with the medium
- At LHC, R_AA(p_T) has a minimum at p_T ~ 6-7 GeV and increases with p_T
- RHIC and LHC results for R_AA(p_T) are in remarkable agreement for p_T > 10 GeV
- For the falling-string AdS/CFT scenario, the effective reduced jet-medium coupling would require a reduction of lambda_LHC by a factor of approximately 2-4 relative to RHIC
- The intermediate p_T band where consistent description of R_AA and v_2 is lacking spans 2 GeV < p_T < 10 GeV

## Context
This status report reviews the transition from over 10 years of RHIC data collection to approximately 1.5 years of LHC physics, contrasting pre-LHC predictions with first experimental results. It extends the discussion of jet quenching from the RHIC white papers (BRAHMS, PHENIX, PHOBOS, STAR) and earlier theoretical work by Gyulassy (1990), Wang (1991), and Baier (1996) on parton energy loss. The paper identifies that pQCD-based models (GLV, ASW, PQM, YaJEM) underpredict the measured LHC R_AA data, while a moderate reduction of the pQCD coupling due to slow running above the deconfinement temperature may better describe the data. The work enables future tests of jet energy loss models with upcoming p+Pb runs at LHC scheduled for 2012 and with data at p_T > 60 GeV.
