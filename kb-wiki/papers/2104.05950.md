# Heavy Magnetic Neutron Stars

> We systematically study the properties of pure nucleonic and hyperonic magnetic stars using a density-dependent relativistic mean field (DD-RMF) equations of state. We explore several parameter sets and hyperon coupling schemes within the DD-RMF formalism. We focus on sets that are in better agreement with nuclear and other astrophysical data, while generating heavy neutron stars. Magnetic field effects are included in the matter equation of state and in general relativity solutions, which in addition fulfill Maxwell's equations. We find that pure nucleonic matter, even without magnetic field effects, generates neutron stars that satisfy the potential GW190814 mass constraint; however, this is not the case for hyperonic matter, which instead only satisfies the more conservative 2.1 M$_{\odot}$ constraint. In the presence of strong but still somehow realistic internal magnetic fields $\approx10^{17}$ G, the stellar charged particle population re-leptonizes and de-hyperonizes. As a consequence, magnetic fields stiffen hyperonic equations of state and generate more massive neutron stars, which can satisfy the possible GW190814 mass constraint but present a large deformation with respect to spherical symmetry.

## Key claim

Pure nucleonic matter can produce neutron stars satisfying the GW190814 mass constraint (2.50–2.67 M☉) even without magnetic fields, but hyperonic matter alone only reaches the 2.1 M☉ constraint; however, strong internal magnetic fields (≈10¹⁷ G) re-leptonize and de-hyperonize the stellar charged particle population, stiffening hyperonic equations of state and enabling hyperonic stars to also satisfy the GW190814 mass constraint, albeit with large deformation from spherical symmetry.

## Method

The authors employed density-dependent relativistic mean-field (DD-RMF) equations of state using four parameter sets (DD-LZ1, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, DD-MEX) and several hyperon coupling schemes based on SU(6) symmetry and hyperon optical potentials. Magnetic field effects were included in both the matter equation of state and general relativity solutions that also fulfill Maxwell’s equations, with stellar configurations computed using the LORENE library to solve the coupled Einstein-Maxwell field equations for stable magnetic star configurations.

## Key numbers

- Pure nucleonic matter without magnetic fields generates neutron stars satisfying the GW190814 mass constraint of 2.50–2.67 M☉
- Hyperonic matter without magnetic fields only satisfies the 2.1 M☉ constraint
- Internal magnetic fields of ≈10¹⁷ G are considered “strong but still somehow realistic”
- Canonical mass (1.4 M☉) neutron star radius constraints from NICER: R = 13.02⁺¹·²⁴₋₁·₀₆ km for M = 1.44⁺⁰·¹⁵₋₀·₁₄ M☉ (Miller 2019) or R = 12.71⁺¹·¹⁴₋₁·₁₉ km for M = 1.34⁺⁰·¹⁵₋₀·₁₆ M☉ (Riley 2019)
- Upper limit on NS radius for 1.4 M☉: R₁·₄ ≤ 13.76 km (PhysRevLett.120.172702)
- Symmetry energy parameter J for the parameter sets is compatible with J = 31.6 ± 2.66 MeV
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L satisfies L = 59.57 ± 10.06 MeV
- Incompressibility K₀ values: DD-LZ1 = 231.237 MeV, DD-ME1 = 243.881 MeV, DD-ME2 = 251.306 MeV, DD-MEX = 267.059 MeV (accepted range K₀ = 240 ± 20 MeV, except DD-MEX slightly higher)
- Saturation density ρ₀: 0.158 fm⁻³ for DD-LZ1, 0.152 fm⁻³ for DD-ME1, DD-ME2, and DD-MEX
- Binding energy E/A at saturation: −16.126 MeV (DD-LZ1), −16.668 MeV (DD-ME1), −16.233 MeV (DD-ME2), −16.140 MeV (DD-MEX)
- Nucleon effective mass M*/M at saturation: 0.558 (DD-LZ1), 0.578 (DD-ME1), 0.572 (DD-ME2), 0.556 (DD-MEX)

## Context

This work extends studies of neutron star structure with exotic degrees of freedom, particularly hyperonic matter, which has been known to soften the equation of state and reduce maximum masses by about 0.5 M☉ (Glendenning 1982, Chatterjee 2016). The paper directly addresses the GW190814 event (Abbott 2020) and the question of whether a 2.50–2.67 M☉ secondary object could be a neutron star containing hyperons, contradicting claims by Li (2020) and Sedrakian (2020) that such a massive object would rule out exotic degrees of freedom. The work enables future investigation of how strong magnetic fields can reconcile hyperonic matter with high-mass neutron star observations, and it builds on prior magnetic neutron star studies by Dexheimer (2011), Pili (2017), and Gomes (2019) that demonstrated deviations from spherical symmetry in strongly magnetized stars.
