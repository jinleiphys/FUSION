# Resonance Region Structure Functions and Parity Violating Deep Inelastic Scattering

> The primary motive of parity violating deep inelastic scattering experiments has been to test the standard model, particularly the axial couplings to the quarks, in the scaling region. The measurements can also test for the validity of models for the off-diagonal structure functions $F_{1,2,3}^{γZ}(x,Q^2)$ in the resonance region. The off-diagonal structure functions are important for the accurate calculation of the $γZ$-box correction to the weak charge of the proton. Currently, with no data to determine $F_{1,2,3}^{γZ}(x,Q^2)$ directly, models are constructed by modifying existing fits to electromagnetic data. We present the asymmetry value for deuteron and proton target predicted by several different $F_{1,2,3}^{γZ}(x,Q^2)$ models, and demonstrate that there are notable disagreements.

# Resonance Region Structure Functions and Parity Violating Deep Inelastic Scattering

## Key claim

Parity violating deep inelastic scattering (PVDIS) in the resonance region can test models of the off-diagonal structure functions F1,2,3^{γZ}(x,Q^2) that are needed for the γZ-box correction to the weak charge of the proton, but the predicted asymmetries from different models disagree by several tens of percent in some kinematic regions, meaning current data cannot distinguish which model is correct.

## Method

The authors computed the PVDIS asymmetry for proton and deuteron targets at Q^2 = 1.1 GeV^2 and 1.9 GeV^2 using four different models for the γZ structure functions in the resonance region: a constituent quark model (CQM) that modifies Christy-Bosted electromagnetic fits using helicity amplitudes with Z-boson couplings; two models from Gorchtein et al. (2011) that use Q^2-independent corrective ratios based on photoproduction data with color dipole or generalized vector meson backgrounds; and a model using MAID fits to resonance electroproduction helicity amplitudes with full Q^2-dependent corrective ratios. The F3^{γZ} contributions were estimated from constituent quark model resonance amplitudes and scaling-region limits for the background, and were found to be at most 3% of the total asymmetry.

## Key numbers

- The PVDIS asymmetry was computed at Q^2 = 1.1 GeV^2 and Q^2 = 1.9 GeV^2 for both proton and deuteron targets, corresponding to x ≈ 0.3 at the 6 GeV PVDIS experiment kinematic points.
- The F3^{γZ} contribution to the asymmetry is at most 3% of the total for the kinematics considered.
- For the proton background uncertainty estimates, the ratio F1,2^{γZ}/F1,2^{γγ} ranges between 1 + Q_W^{p,LO} (SU(3) limit) and 2/3 + Q_W^{p,LO} (valence SU(6) limit), with the central curve taken as the mean.
- For the deuteron background, the valence-only limit gives F3^{γZ}/F1^{γγ} = 18/5, while the low-x limit gives approximately zero; the central value was taken as 9/5 with 100% uncertainty bounds.
- The Roper resonance transverse helicity amplitude in the MAID model changes sign at about 2/3 GeV^2 for the proton, causing a dip in the predicted asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of γZ box corrections to the Qweak experiment (Gorchtein 2008, Rislow 2010, Gorchtein 2011) by showing that PVDIS in the resonance region can directly test the models used for those corrections. The models compared include the constituent quark model of Rislow (2010), the Gorchtein-Horowitz-Ramsey-Musolf models (2011) based on color dipole fits (Cvetic 2001) and generalized vector meson dominance (Alwall 2004), and the MAID fits to resonance helicity amplitudes (Tiator 2011). The results show that these models predict distinctly different asymmetries, meaning PVDIS data can discriminate between them and thereby improve the accuracy of γZ box corrections to elastic parity-violating electron-proton scattering.
