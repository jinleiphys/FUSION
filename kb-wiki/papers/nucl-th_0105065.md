# Spin-vibrational 1+ states in 140Ce

> The properties of collective I^$Π$=1+ states in double even semimagic nucleus 140Ce are investigated in random phase approximation(RPA). The calculation of the B(M1) strength, elastic gamma-scattering cross section $σ_(gamma)(ω)$ and gamma-decay width $Σ_(gamma)(ω_i)$ show that in 140Ce the isovector spin-spin interactions concenrate the main strength of M1 transition at energy 7.89 MeV where the isovector spin-flip magnetic resonance may appear. The predisctions are consistent with the observed M1 strength in 140Ce which is seen to be concentrated within a range of about 0.8 MeV centered at an excitation of 7.95 MeV.

## Key claim

The random phase approximation (RPA) calculation predicts that the isovector spin-spin interaction concentrates the main M1 transition strength in 140Ce at 7.89 MeV, where an isovector spin-flip magnetic resonance may appear, and this prediction is consistent with the observed M1 strength concentrated within a range of about 0.8 MeV centered at an excitation of 7.95 MeV.

## Method

The authors performed RPA calculations for the double even semimagic nucleus 140Ce to investigate collective I^Π=1+ states. They computed the B(M1) strength distribution, the elastic gamma-scattering cross section σ_γ(ω), and the gamma-decay width Σ_γ(ω_i) to characterize the M1 response.

## Key numbers

- The main M1 transition strength is predicted at an energy of 7.89 MeV in 140Ce.
- The experimentally observed M1 strength is concentrated within a range of about 0.8 MeV centered at an excitation energy of 7.95 MeV.

## Context

This work extends RPA studies of spin-vibrational 1+ states in semimagic nuclei, specifically addressing the isovector spin-flip magnetic resonance in 140Ce. The predictions are compared with experimental observations of M1 strength in this nucleus. The results enable further investigation of the role of isovector spin-spin interactions in concentrating M1 strength in heavy nuclei.
