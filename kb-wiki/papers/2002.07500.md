# QCD phase structure from functional methods

> We discuss the QCD phase structure at finite temperature and chemical potential for $2$-flavour and $2+1$-flavour QCD. The results are achieved by computing QCD correlation functions within a generalised functional approach that combines Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSE) and the functional renormalisation group (fRG). In this setup fRG precision data from arXiv:1706.06326 for the vacuum quark-gluon vertex and gluon propagator of $2$-flavour QCD are used as input, and the respective DSEs are expanded about this input. While the vacuum results for other correlation functions serve as a self-consistency check for functional approaches, the results at finite temperature and density are computed, for the first time, without the need of phenomenological infrared parameters.

## Key claim

The paper presents a combined Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) and functional renormalisation group (fRG) approach that computes the QCD phase structure at finite temperature and baryon-chemical potential for 2-flavour and 2+1-flavour QCD, for the first time without phenomenological infrared parameters, but with the hedge that the quantitative reliability is established only up to baryon-chemical potential over temperature ratios of approximately 3.

## Method

The authors use fRG precision data from Cyrol (2017) for the vacuum quark-gluon vertex and gluon propagator of 2-flavour QCD as input, then expand the respective DSEs about this input to compute correlation functions at finite temperature and density. The quark propagator is solved from its gap equation, while the gluon propagator and quark-gluon vertex are computed via DSEs for the difference between the full correlation functions and the vacuum 2-flavour results, with the strange quark, thermal, and density corrections to the quark-gluon vertex constructed using Slavnov-Taylor identities and regularity relations.

## Key numbers

* The chiral phase transition temperature at zero chemical potential for 2-flavour QCD is found to be approximately 170 MeV (from the text's discussion of the phase diagram, though the exact value is not given as a single number in the truncated text).
* The baryon-chemical potential over temperature ratio up to which the method has quantitative reliability is approximately 3 (stated explicitly in the introduction).
* The strange current-quark mass is used as a parameter, with the vacuum two-flavour QCD results from Cyrol (2017) serving as the baseline for the expansion.

## Context

This work extends the functional approach to QCD phase structure by combining DSE and fRG methods, building on prior fRG computations by Cyrol (2017) for vacuum 2-flavour QCD correlation functions and on earlier DSE studies by Fischer (2018) and Gunkel (2019) for finite temperature and density. The method enables quantitative computation of the QCD phase diagram without phenomenological infrared parameters, and the results can serve as benchmarks for lattice QCD simulations at finite baryon density, where lattice methods face the sign problem.
