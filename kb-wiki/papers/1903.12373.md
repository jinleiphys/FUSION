# Shape and shell-structure of lighter (N<90) neutron-rich nuclei based on phenomenological Woods-Saxon potential

> By using a phenomenologically successful Woods-Saxon potential, I study the shape and shell structure of (A) neutron drip line nuclei with 10 \leq N \leq 60, (B) neutron-rich nuclei related to the r- process with 40 \leq N \leq 90, and (C) one-particle spectra in the potential provided by the nucleus 70Fe as a representative of so-called N=40 "island of inversion (IoI)" nuclei. First, the shell structure that is unique in very weakly-bound neutrons is systematically studied, and the approximate neutron number of odd-N nuclei at which spherical (or deformed) halos can be found is pinned down. Second, the difference of the shell structure in r-process nuclei from that in stable nuclei is examined. Third, the similarity and the difference between the shell structure of N=20 IoI nuclei and that of N=40 IoI nuclei are analyzed. As a result of it, it is concluded that in Fe and Cr isotopes the deformation called "N=40 IoI" continuing up to N=50 is unlikely.

## Key claim

Using a phenomenologically successful Woods-Saxon potential, the author concludes that in Fe and Cr isotopes the deformation called "N=40 island of inversion (IoI)" continuing up to N=50 is unlikely, contrary to what might be expected from analogy with the N=20 IoI region.

## Method

The author employs a spherically-symmetric Woods-Saxon potential with a neutron-excess term proportional to (N−Z)/A, with parameters taken from Bohr and Mottelson (1969). For nuclei where spherical shell structure suggests possible deformation, the study extends to axially symmetric quadrupole-deformed potentials, solving coupled differential equations for the radial wave-functions in coordinate space with correct asymptotic behavior at infinity. The shell structure is examined separately for three categories: neutron drip line nuclei with N/Z=2 (10<N<60), neutron-rich r-process nuclei with N/Z=1.7 (40<N<90), and the specific case of ⁷⁰Fe as a representative N=40 IoI nucleus.

## Key numbers

- Woods-Saxon potential depth for neutrons: V_WS = −51 + 33(N−Z)/A MeV
- For N/Z = 2 (drip line nuclei), V_WS = −40 MeV
- For N/Z = 1.7 (r-process nuclei), V_WS = −42.4 MeV
- In ⁷⁰Fe spherical potential: neutron 1g₉/₂ to 2d₅/₂ energy gap = 2.62 MeV; neutron 1g₉/₂ to 2p₁/₂ gap = 3.15 MeV
- In ⁷⁰Fe spherical potential: proton 2d₅/₂ to 1g₉/₂ energy difference = 5.37 MeV; proton 1g₉/₂ to 2p₁/₂ difference = 1.57 MeV
- In ³⁴Mg₂₂ (N=20 IoI region): spherical energy gap at N=20 = 4.51 MeV; 2p₃/₂ to 1f₇/₂ energy difference = 0.391 MeV
- Diffuseness parameter a = 0.67 fm; radius parameter r₀ = 1.27 fm

## Context

This work extends the author's previous successful applications of the Woods-Saxon potential to light neutron drip line nuclei (Hamamoto 2007, 2010, 2016) and the deformed rare-earth region studied by Bohr and Mottelson (1975). The systematic comparison between N=20 and N=40 island of inversion regions addresses recent experimental studies of Cr and Fe isotopes (GB15, CS15, GS16). The results suggest that while N=20 IoI nuclei can be deformed up to N=28 due to near-degeneracy of the 2p₃/₂ and 1f₇/₂ shells, the N=40 IoI in Fe and Cr isotopes likely extends only to N≈46, not continuously to N=50, because the 2.62 MeV gap between 1g₉/₂ and 2d₅/₂ prevents the Fermi energy from dropping below the spherical value at N=50.
