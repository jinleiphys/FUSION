# Quantum microscopic approach to low-energy heavy ion collisions

> The Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory is applied to the study of heavy ion collisions at energies around the Coulomb barrier. The competition between fusion and nucleon transfer mechanisms is investigated. For intermediate mass systems such as 16O+208Pb, proton transfer favors fusion by reducing the Coulomb repulsion. A comparison with sub-barrier transfer experimental data shows that pairing correlations are playing an important role in enhancing proton pair transfer. For heavier and more symmetric systems, a fusion hindrance is observed due to the dominance of the quasi-fission process. Typical quasi-fission time of few zeptoseconds are obtained. Actinide collisions are also investigated both within the TDHF approach and with the Ballian-Vénéroni prescription for fluctuation and correlation of one-body observables. The possible formation of new heavy neutron-rich nuclei in actinide collisions is discussed.

# Quantum microscopic approach to low-energy heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory, despite omitting pairing correlations and two-body collisions, reproduces key features of near-barrier heavy-ion reactions including fusion thresholds, nucleon transfer probabilities, and quasi-fission dynamics; however, comparison with experimental data on sub-barrier proton transfer in 16O+208Pb shows that pairing correlations are essential for correctly describing the enhancement of two-proton transfer relative to one-proton transfer, and these correlations are not yet included in the TDHF calculations presented.

## Method

The authors performed 3-dimensional TDHF calculations using the Skyrme SLy4d energy density functional with the tdhf3d code, treating the many-body wave function as an antisymmetrized independent particle state at all times. Initial ground states were obtained from static Hartree-Fock calculations, and collisions were simulated by applying Galilean boosts to nuclei initially separated by 30-50 fm, assuming Rutherford trajectories before the initial time. For transfer probabilities, a particle-number projection technique was applied to the outgoing fragments, and for fluctuation and correlation estimates in actinide collisions, the Balian-Veneroni prescription was used.

## Key numbers

- Fusion barrier for 16O+208Pb from TDHF: V_B^TDHF = 74.445 ± 0.005 MeV, approximately 1.5 MeV lower than the HF-frozen approximation barrier of V_B^frozen ≈ 76.0 MeV at R_B^frozen ≈ 11.8 fm
- Experimental fusion barrier centroid for 16O+208Pb: V_B^exp ≈ 74.5 MeV
- For 90Zr+124Sn (Z1Z2 = 2000), the proximity model predicts a barrier of ≈215 MeV, while TDHF requires an additional ≈25 MeV above this to achieve fusion, compared to the extra-push model prediction of E^X-push ≈ 14.8 MeV
- Quasi-fission contact times in 90Zr+124Sn at E_cm = 235 MeV are approximately 5 zeptoseconds
- In 238U+238U central collisions at E_cm = 900 MeV, tip-side orientation transfers on average ≈6 protons and ≈11 neutrons, forming a 255Cf primary fragment
- In 232Th+250Cf collisions at E_cm = 916 MeV, a 265Lr fragment is formed in the exit channel via inverse quasi-fission

## Context

This work extends the TDHF approach to heavy-ion collisions around the Coulomb barrier, building on early TDHF calculations by Bonche (1978) and more recent 3D implementations by Kim (1997) and others. The study of fusion hindrance in heavy systems (Z1Z2 ≥ 1600) connects to the extra-push model of Swiatecki (1982) and the proximity model of Blocki (1977), while the actinide collision studies relate to predictions by Zagrebaev (2006, 2012) and Karpov (2010) for producing neutron-rich heavy nuclei via multi-nucleon transfer. The comparison with experimental transfer data from Evers (2011) demonstrates the need for future time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations including pairing, which have recently become feasible in 3D codes by Avez (2008), Assie (2009), and Ebata (2010).
