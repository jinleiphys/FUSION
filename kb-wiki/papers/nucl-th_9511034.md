# Quantum Solitons Lead to Yukawa Coupling

> Recently, it was shown that zero modes in semiclassical soliton models do not lead to Yukawa couplings. We show that taking into account the contributions of the quantum soliton into the renormalization scheme, which cannot be done in semiclassical treatments, leads to a Yukawa coupling. A similar analysis should be possible for the Skyrmion, renewing the hope, that this model will lead to a correct description of hadron physics.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including the quantum soliton's contributions in the renormalization scheme, something impossible in semiclassical treatments, produces a Yukawa coupling between mesons and baryons, whereas prior semiclassical analyses had shown that zero modes do not lead to such couplings.

## Method

The author constructs a soliton vertex operator for the sine-Gordon model, creates a one-soliton vacuum state, and then defines new annihilation operators (analogous to quasiparticles in BCS theory) that annihilate this vacuum. The Hamiltonian is rewritten in terms of these operators, and a cutoff Λ is introduced to match the quantum soliton vacuum energy to the classically renormalized soliton mass, effectively integrating out energetic mesons.

## Key numbers

- The soliton mass M is set equal to the sum ∑_n^Λ ω_n |c_n|^2, where Λ is the cutoff and c_n are constants derived from the transformation to the soliton vacuum.
- The Yukawa coupling matrix element is of order √M: ⟨σ(x)|H|γ⁺_k, σ(x)⟩ ∼ √M.
- The topological charge of the state created by the soliton operator σ(x) is 1: ⟨vac|σ⁺(x) Q_t σ(x)|vac⟩ = 1.
- The field expectation value in the soliton vacuum is of order β⁻¹, where β is the parameter relating to the soliton mass and meson coupling.

## Context

This work extends the sine-Gordon soliton analysis of Mandelstam (1975), who integrated out mesons completely to obtain a massive Thirring model, by instead performing a partial integration of energetic mesons. It directly contradicts the conclusion of the semiclassical analysis by Yoneya (1994) and others, who argued that physical asymptotic states yield no Born terms and thus no Yukawa coupling. The method is claimed to be applicable to the Skyrme model, potentially renewing its viability for describing hadron physics.
