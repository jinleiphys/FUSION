# Anticharmed strange pentaquarks from the one-boson-exchange model

> Inspired by the LHCb's discovery of hidden-charm pentaquarks, the anticharmed strange pentaquarks $P_{\bar c s}$ in a favor of the hadronic molecule picture are investigated from the one-boson-exchange model. Similar to the hidden-charm pentaquarks, three molecular bound states, one with spin parity $J^P=1/2^-$ below the $\bar DΣ$ threshold and another two with $J^P=\{1/2^-,3/2^-\}$ below the $\bar D^*Σ$ threshold, are predicted at $3-3.2$ GeV mass region. The mass ordering of the later two states can be interchanged by different reductions of the $δ(r)$ term. Furthermore, resonances associated with these three bound states are examined by considering $ D_s^-N-\bar DΛ-\bar D Σ-\bar D^*Λ-\bar D^*Σ$ coupled-channel dynamics, and decay widths of them are predicted. Our study indicates that the $ D_s^- p$ invariant mass spectrum in the $\bar B_s^0\to \bar n D_s^- p$ decay is an appropriate place to detect the $P_{\bar c s}$ pentaquarks.

## Key claim

The paper predicts three anticharmed strange pentaquark states as hadronic molecules, but the mass ordering of the two states below the \(\bar D^*\Sigma\) threshold can be interchanged by different treatments of the short-range \(\delta(\bm r)\) term, indicating that the precise spin-parity assignment depends on how short-range physics is modeled.

## Method

The authors use the one-boson-exchange (OBE) model with effective Lagrangians incorporating heavy quark spin symmetry and SU(3) flavor symmetry to derive potentials for the \(\bar D^{(*)}\Sigma\) systems. They solve the Schrödinger equation for single-channel bound states and then examine resonances through coupled-channel dynamics involving \(D_s^-N\), \(\bar D\Lambda\), \(\bar D\Sigma\), \(\bar D^*\Lambda\), and \(\bar D^*\Sigma\), including \(S\)-\(D\) wave mixing effects and a reduction parameter \(a\) to adjust the short-range \(\delta(\bm r)\) contribution.

## Key numbers

- Three molecular bound states are predicted in the \(3-3.2\) GeV mass region: one with \(J^P=1/2^-\) below the \(\bar D\Sigma\) threshold, and two with \(J^P=\{1/2^-,3/2^-\}\) below the \(\bar D^*\Sigma\) threshold.
- The scalar meson coupling is taken as \(g_S = g_\pi/(2\sqrt{6})\) with \(g_\pi = 3.73\); the pseudoscalar coupling is \(g = -0.59\) extracted from \(D^*\to D\pi\) decay widths with pion decay constant \(f_\pi = 132\) MeV.
- Vector meson couplings are \(g_V = 5.9\), \(\beta = 0.9\), and \(\lambda = 0.56\) GeV\(^{-1}\); baryon-scalar couplings are \(g_{NN\sigma}=8.465\), \(g_{\Lambda\Lambda\sigma}=7.579\), and \(g_{\Sigma\Sigma\sigma}=10.85\).
- The \(\sigma\) meson mass is taken as 600 MeV; isospin-averaged masses for exchanged mesons are \(m_\pi=137.2\), \(m_\eta=547.9\), \(m_\rho=775.3\), \(m_\omega=782.7\), \(m_{\bar K}=493.7\), and \(m_{\bar K^*}=891.7\) in MeV.

## Context

This work extends the hadronic molecule approach previously applied to the hidden-charm pentaquarks \(P_c(4312)\), \(P_c(4440)\), and \(P_c(4457)\) discovered by LHCb, which were reproduced as \(\bar D\Sigma_c\) and \(\bar D^*\Sigma_c\) bound states in studies by He (2019), Chen (2019), Liu (2019), and Du (2021). The authors draw an analogy between the \(\bar D^{(*)}\Sigma_c\) and \(\bar D^{(*)}\Sigma\) systems, replacing the charm quark in \(\Sigma_c\) with a strange quark. The study enables experimental searches for anticharmed strange pentaquarks in the \(D_s^- p\) invariant mass spectrum from \(\bar B_s^0\to \bar n D_s^- p\) decay, building on earlier proposals of \(D_s^-N\) bound states by Lipkin (1987) and Gignoux (1987) and coupled-channel studies by Hofmann (2005).
