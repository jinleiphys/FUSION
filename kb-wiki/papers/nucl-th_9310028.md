# An antiproton-proton partial wave analysis

> Invited talk presented by the first author at the XIVth European Conference of Few-Body Problems in Physics, Amsterdam, August 23-28, 1993

## Key claim

The authors present the first energy-dependent partial-wave analysis (PWA) of antiproton-proton scattering data below 925 MeV/c, achieving a statistically acceptable fit (\(\chi^2/N_{\rm df}=1.09\)) but noting that the database below 400 MeV/c remains insufficient and that systematic errors on extracted parameters are hard to estimate.

## Method

The Nijmegen group extended their established PWA methods for nucleon-nucleon scattering to the \(\overline{p}p\) system, using the relativistic Schrödinger equation with a meson-exchange potential (including one-pion exchange and heavy-meson exchanges from the 1978 Nijmegen \(NN\) potential) for the long-range interaction region \(r > b = 1.3\) fm. The short-range interaction, including annihilation, was parametrized via a complex \(P\)-matrix boundary condition at \(r = b\), and the full database of 3646 \(\overline{p}p\) data points between 119 and 923 MeV/c was fitted with 30 boundary-condition parameters.

## Key numbers

- Database: 3646 \(\overline{p}p\) data points in the momentum interval 119–923 MeV/c.
- Final fit: \(\chi^2 = 3801.0\) with 3503 degrees of freedom, giving \(\chi^2/N_{\rm df} = 1.09\) (3.5 standard deviations from the expectation value of \(\chi^2 = 3503 \pm 84\)).
- Charged-pion–nucleon coupling constant: \(f^2_c = 0.0732(11)\) at the pion pole, determined from the full 1993 database (superseding the 1991 preliminary value \(f^2_c = 0.0751(17)\)).
- Relativistic correction to the Coulomb potential (\(\alpha'\) instead of \(\alpha\)): drop of \(\Delta\chi^2 = 30\) (5.5 standard deviations).
- Inclusion of magnetic-moment interaction: drop of \(\Delta\chi^2 = 14\) (3.7 standard deviations).
- Use of correct pion masses instead of an average mass of 138 MeV: a 3 standard-deviation effect.
- Boundary radius: \(b = 1.3\) fm, indicating the range of the annihilation process.

## Context

This work extends the Nijmegen group's established PWA methods for \(pp\) and \(np\) scattering (Bergervoet et al. 1990, Kloet et al. 1993) to the \(\overline{p}p\) system, made possible by high-quality data from the LEAR facility at CERN (collaborations PS172, PS173, PS198, PS199). The resulting phase-shift parameters provide a meeting ground between theory and experiment, enabling improvements to \(\overline{N}N\) interaction models (Cottingham et al. 1982, Timmermans et al. 1984) and serving as input for studies of protonium and specific annihilation processes. The analysis also demonstrates that quantitative information, such as the charged-pion coupling constant, can now be extracted from the \(\overline{p}p\) system, and it establishes a convention for extracting phase-shift and inelasticity parameters from the non-unitary \(S\) matrix.
