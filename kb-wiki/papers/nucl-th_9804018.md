# K^+ versus Λflow in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> We study $K^+$ and $Λ$ flow in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies of about 2A GeV. We present our results in both the `traditional' (i.e., in terms of the average transverse momentum in the reaction plane) as well as `modern' (i.e., in terms of coefficients of the Fourier analysis of azimuthal distributions) methods of flow analysis. We find significant differences between the $K^+$ and the $Λ$ flow: while the $Λ$ flow is basically similar to that of nucleons, the $K^+$ flow almost disappears. This difference is attributed chiefly to their different mean field potentials in dense matter. The comparisons with the experimental data, as well as theoretical results from independent calculations, indicate clearly the pivotal roles of both $K^+$ and $Λ$ medium effects. We emphasize that similar experimental data from independent collaborations are essential for the eventual verification of these medium effects.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed differences between \(K^+\) and \(\Lambda\) collective flow in heavy-ion collisions at about 2A GeV are chiefly due to their different mean-field potentials in dense matter, but this conclusion depends on the inclusion of both rescattering and mean-field propagation in the transport model, and the authors emphasize that similar experimental data from independent collaborations are essential for eventual verification of these medium effects.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic transport model based on an effective chiral Lagrangian, incorporating nucleons, delta resonances, and pions, with kaons and lambdas produced via pion-baryon and baryon-baryon collisions. They simulated Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93A GeV, Ru+Ru collisions at 1.69A GeV, and Au+Au collisions at 2A GeV, analyzing flow via both the traditional average transverse momentum \(\langle p_x \rangle\) versus rapidity and the modern Fourier coefficient \(v_1\) method. They compared scenarios without final-state interactions, with only rescattering, and with both rescattering and propagation in mean-field potentials, using a kaon scalar parameter \(a_K \approx 0.22\) GeV\(^2\)fm\(^3\) and a \(\Lambda\) potential set to 2/3 of the nucleon potential.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon flow parameter in central Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93A GeV: about 150 MeV/c.
- Primordial \(K^+\) flow parameter (no final-state interactions): 20 MeV/c; primordial \(\Lambda\) flow parameter: 40 MeV/c.
- After rescattering only: \(K^+\) flow parameter increases to 40 MeV/c; \(\Lambda\) flow parameter increases to 75 MeV/c.
- After rescattering and mean-field potentials: \(K^+\) flow parameter becomes about -15 MeV/c (antiflow); \(\Lambda\) flow parameter increases to about 130 MeV/c.
- In semi-central Ru+Ru collisions at 1.69A GeV with transverse momentum cut \(p_t/m > 0.5\), the kaon flow parameter is about -20 MeV/c; in central collisions it is about 10 MeV/c.
- In Au+Au collisions at 2A GeV for impact parameter \(b < 6\) fm, the proton first Fourier moment \(v_1\) reaches about 0.3 near projectile/target rapidity.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Li et al. (1995) on kaon flow as a probe of the kaon potential and by Li and Ko (1996) on lambda flow, using a relativistic transport model with an improved effective chiral Lagrangian from Furstahl, Tang, and Serot. The results are compared with experimental data from the FOPI collaboration for Ni+Ni collisions and with independent calculations using Hadron-String Dynamics (Bratkovskaya, 1997), Quantum-Molecular Dynamics (Fachruddin, 1997), and Relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (Li, 1998). The findings support the scenario that \(K^+\) feels a weak repulsive potential while \(\Lambda\) feels an attractive potential in dense matter, and the predictions for Ru+Ru and Au+Au collisions are intended to guide ongoing experiments by the FOPI and E895 collaborations.
