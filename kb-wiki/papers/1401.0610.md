# Studying the baryon properties through chiral soliton model at finite temperature and denstity

> We have studied the chiral soliton model in a thermal vacuum. The soliton equations are solved at finite temperature and density. The temperature or density dependent soliton solutions are presented. The physical properties of baryons are derived from the soliton solutions at finite temperature and density. The temperature or density dependent variation of the baryon properties are discussed.

## Key claim

The baryon mass, charge radius, magnetic moment, and axial-to-vector coupling ratio all increase with temperature or chemical potential, contrary to the decreasing baryon mass found in some prior work (ref. [23]) because the present calculation includes the meson interaction energy in the baryon mass formula.

## Method

The authors solve the coupled nonlinear radial differential equations of the chiral soliton (linear sigma) model for quark fields u(r), v(r) and meson fields sigma(r), pi(r) in the mean-field approximation with the hedgehog ansatz. Finite temperature and density effects are introduced by replacing the classical meson potential U(sigma, pi) with a thermal effective potential V_eff(T, mu) derived from the one-loop thermodynamic potential of the quark medium, and the equations are solved numerically using a modified COLSYS package.

## Key numbers

- Baryon mass in vacuum: M_B = 1140 MeV (at T=0, mu=0)
- Root mean square charge radius in vacuum: r_B = 0.717 fm
- Magnetic moment in vacuum: mu_B = 0.195 e fm
- Axial-to-vector coupling ratio in vacuum: g_A/g_V = 1.162
- At T=160 MeV, mu=0: M_B = 2096 MeV, r_B = 0.797 fm, mu_B = 0.222 e fm, g_A/g_V = 1.193
- At mu=400 MeV, T=50 MeV: M_B = 1268 MeV, r_B = 0.743 fm, mu_B = 0.204 e fm, g_A/g_V = 1.189
- Soliton amplitudes decrease by 1-2% when T increases from 0 to 100 MeV, and by 6-9% when T increases from 100 to 160 MeV (at mu=0)
- Soliton amplitudes decrease by 2-5% when mu increases from 0 to 400 MeV (at T=50 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the chiral soliton model, previously solved in vacuum by Birse and Banerjee (1984) and others, to finite temperature and density using standard finite-temperature field theory. It differs from the approach of ref. [23] (which found decreasing baryon mass with temperature) by including the meson interaction energy in the baryon mass calculation, and agrees with the results of ref. [22] which also included that term. The results enable future study of soliton behavior near the chiral phase transition.
