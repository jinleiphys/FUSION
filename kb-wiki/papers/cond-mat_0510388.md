# Pairing Correlations in Finite Systems: From the weak to the strong fluctuations regime

> The Particle Number Projected Generator Coordinate Method is formulated for the pairing Hamiltonian in a detailed way in the projection after variation and the variation after projection methods. The dependence of the wave functions on the generator coordinate is analyzed performing numerical applications for the most relevant collective coordinates. The calculations reproduce the exact solution in the weak, crossover and strong pairing regimes. The physical insight of the Ansatz and its numerical simplicity make this theory an excellent tool to study pairing correlations in complex situations and/or involved Hamiltonians.

# Pairing Correlations in Finite Systems: From the weak to the strong fluctuations regime

## Key claim

The Particle Number Projected Generator Coordinate Method (GCM), when formulated with variation after projection (VAP), reproduces the exact Richardson solution for the pairing Hamiltonian across weak, crossover, and strong pairing regimes, but only when the generator coordinate is chosen appropriately; the simpler projection after variation (PAV) approaches already predict a smooth crossover absent in PBCS, though the GCMVAP results coincide with exact results.

## Method

The authors formulate the Generator Coordinate Method using particle-number-projected BCS wave functions as generator states, solving the Hill-Wheeler equation to obtain a superposition of these states. They compare three generator coordinates (the trial pairing strength G_trial, the chemical potential μ, and the particle number fluctuation ΔN²) in both projection-after-variation (GCMPAV) and variation-after-projection (GCMVAP) frameworks, and test the method against the exact Richardson solution for the pairing Hamiltonian at half filling with equally spaced single-particle levels ε_k = kd.

## Key numbers

- The BCS coupling constant λ for Al is taken as 0.224, with G = λ d where d is the level spacing.
- For N=20 particles, the condensation energy (in units of the bulk gap Δ̃) from the G_trial coordinate PAV is -1.7716, from μ PAV is -0.7864, from ΔN² PAV is -1.1438, from VAP is -2.0625, and the exact value is -2.2026.
- For N=400 particles, the condensation energy from G_trial PAV is -3.4192, from μ PAV is -3.4625, from ΔN² PAV is -3.5564, from VAP is -3.6551, and the exact value is -4.8891.
- The crossover between weak and strong pairing regimes is characterized by d/Δ̃ = 2 sinh(1/λ)/N, with the bulk gap Δ̃.
- For convergence of the Hill-Wheeler equation, 80 mesh points are sufficient for all coordinates, and a tolerance of 10⁻¹⁰ for the norm eigenvalues corresponds to approximately 15 linearly independent states for the μ and ΔN² coordinates.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous GCM approach to pairing correlations in superconducting grains (Fernandez and Egido, 2003) by providing a detailed derivation and systematic comparison of generator coordinates. It addresses the failure of BCS theory to describe the crossover from few-electron to bulk regimes, where the strong phase transition predicted by BCS is absent in exact solutions (Richardson, 1964) and in more advanced theories. The method enables the study of more sophisticated pairing Hamiltonians for which no exact solution exists, with preliminary studies on the most general pairing Hamiltonians (Dukelsky et al., 2001) showing results of the same quality.
