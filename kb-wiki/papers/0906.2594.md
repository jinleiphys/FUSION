# Odd-even mass difference and isospin dependent pairing interaction

> The neutron and proton odd-even mass differences are studied with Hartree-Fock+BCS (HFBCS) calculations with Skyrme interactions and an isospin dependent contact pairing interaction, which is recently derived from a microscopic nucleon-nucleon interaction. To this end, we perform HFBCS calculations for even and odd semi-magic Tin and Lead isotopes together with even and odd Z isotones with $N$= 50 and 82. The filling approximation is applied to the last unoccupied particle in odd nuclei. Comparisons with the experimental data show a clear manifestation of the isospin dependent pairing correlations in both proton and neutron pairing gaps.

# Odd-even mass difference and isospin dependent pairing interaction

## Key claim
The isospin dependent (IS+IV) contact pairing interaction, derived from microscopic nucleon-nucleon calculations, reproduces the experimental odd-even mass staggering (OES) for both neutron and proton pairing gaps in semi-magic nuclei significantly better than the isoscalar (IS) surface-peaked pairing interaction, but only when the filling approximation is applied to odd nuclei and the comparison is restricted to sub-closed shell spherical configurations.

## Method
Hartree-Fock+BCS (HFBCS) calculations were performed using the EV8 code with Skyrme interactions (SkP and SLy4) and a contact pairing interaction whose density-dependent term includes an isospin dependence parameterized by the asymmetry parameter β = (ρ_n − ρ_p)/ρ. The odd-even mass staggering was computed using the 3-point formula Δ^(3) centered at the odd nucleus, with odd-A nuclei treated via the filling approximation (setting v²_i = u²_i = 1/2 for the blocked orbital) starting from the HF+BCS solutions of neighboring even-even nuclei, within a 10 MeV energy window around the Fermi level.

## Key numbers
- Pairing strength V₀ = 824 MeV for the isospin dependent (g_τ) interaction, adjusted to best fit odd-even staggering of nuclear masses.
- Pairing strength V₀ = 1400 MeV for the isoscalar surface-peaked (g_s) interaction, adjusted to a best global fit of nuclear masses.
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³ used in the density-dependent function.
- Experimental Δ_n^(3) for Sn isotopes is approximately constant around 1.2 MeV until N=80, decreasing below 1 MeV above N=82.
- Experimental Δ_n^(3) for Pb isotopes is about 1.4 MeV in neutron-deficient isotopes, decreasing to 0.7 MeV in neutron-rich isotopes.
- For Pb isotopes, the phenomenological formula c/A^α gives 0.93 MeV (N=99) and 0.90 MeV (N=121), while experimental values are 1.23 MeV and 0.73 MeV respectively.
- For N=50 isotones, the phenomenological formula gives proton OES of 1.10 MeV (Z=31) and 1.04 MeV (Z=47), while experimental values are 0.74 MeV and 1.14 MeV respectively.
- HF+BCS iteration accuracy is about 100 keV or less in 500 iterations.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of odd-even staggering by Duguet et al. (2001), Margueron et al. (2007), and Yamagami et al. (2008) by directly comparing calculated OES from binding energies rather than inferring it from average HFB gap parameters, which can differ substantially. The isospin dependent pairing interaction was previously derived from microscopic nucleon-nucleon calculations of nuclear matter pairing gaps by Margueron et al. (2007). The results confirm that isospin dependent pairing correlations are clearly manifested in both proton and neutron pairing gaps, and the authors plan a more comprehensive study of OES across the entire mass region as future work.
