# Coherent photoproduction of heavy quarkonia on nuclei

> The differential cross section of coherent photo-production of heavy quarkonia on nuclear targets is calculated within the QCD color dipole formalism. The higher-twist nuclear shadowing corresponding to the $\bar QQ$ Fock component of the photon, is calculated including the correlation between dipole orientation $\vec r$ and impact parameter of a collision $\vec b$, which is related to the transverse momentum transfer via Fourier transform. We also included the leading twist gluon shadowing corresponding to higher Fock components of the photon containing gluons, which have specifically short coherence time, especially for multi-gluon components, even at very high energies. The contribution of such fluctuating gluonic dipole is calculated employing the path-integral technique. Our results are in good agreement with recent ALICE data on charmonium production in ultra-peripheral nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the differential cross section of coherent heavy quarkonium photoproduction on nuclei can be accurately calculated within the QCD color dipole formalism by including both the correlation between dipole orientation and impact parameter (higher-twist nuclear shadowing) and the leading-twist gluon shadowing from higher Fock components, with the latter requiring a single-gluon approximation because multi-gluon Fock states have coherence lengths too short to contribute at available energies.

## Method

The authors calculated the differential cross section for coherent photoproduction of heavy quarkonia on nuclear targets using the QCD color dipole formalism, starting from the dipole-proton amplitude that includes the correlation between dipole orientation vector r and impact parameter b. For nuclear targets, they employed an eikonal form for the dipole-nucleus amplitude and separately computed the leading-twist gluon shadowing contribution from the |QQg⟩ Fock component using a path-integral Green function technique, while demonstrating that multi-gluon Fock states have coherence lengths reduced by a factor proportional to the number of gluons and can be neglected. The quarkonium wave functions were generated using the Buchmuller-Tye potential with Lorentz boosting to the light-front frame including Melosh spin rotation corrections.

## Key numbers

- The GBW saturation model parameters used: σ₀ = 23.03 mb, R₀(x) = 0.4 fm × (x/x₀)^0.144 with x₀ = 3.04 × 10⁻⁴
- The BGBK model parameters: σ₀ = 23 mb, A_g = 1.2, λ_g = 0.28, μ₀² = 0.52 GeV², C = 0.26
- The effective gluon mass m_g ≈ 0.7 GeV, which makes the gluonic coherence length nearly an order of magnitude shorter than for QQ fluctuations
- At x = 10⁻³, the mean coherence length for single-gluon fluctuations is reduced by factor ⟨P_g⟩ = 0.13 relative to the maximal possible coherence length 1/(x m_N)
- The D-wave component in the J/ψ wave function squared from realistic potentials is 0.05%, compared to 5-6% from the photon-like vertex model; for Υ(1S) the D-wave component is 0.004%

## Context

This work extends the color dipole formalism for coherent photoproduction of heavy quarkonia on nuclei, building on prior calculations by Kopeliovich (1991), Hufner (2000), and Ivanov (2002). The inclusion of the r-b correlation effect, previously studied for proton targets by Kopeliovich (2021), is applied here to nuclear targets where the effect is diluted except at the nuclear periphery. The gluon shadowing calculation using the path-integral technique follows the approach developed by Kopeliovich (1999) for deep inelastic scattering and applied to vector meson electroproduction by Ivanov (2002) and Nemchik (2002). The results are compared with ALICE data from ultra-peripheral collisions at the LHC, and the single-gluon approximation is justified by showing that multi-gluon Fock states have coherence lengths too short to contribute, which contradicts the applicability of the Balitsky-Kovchegov equation to nuclear targets at available energies as argued by Kopeliovich (2000).
