# One-loop matching of $CP$-odd four-quark operators to the gradient-flow scheme

> The translation of experimental limits on the neutron electric dipole moment into constraints on heavy $CP$-violating physics beyond the Standard Model requires knowledge about non-perturbative matrix elements of effective operators, which ideally should be computed in lattice QCD. However, this necessitates a matching calculation as an interface to the effective field theory framework, which is based on dimensional regularization and renormalization by minimal subtraction. We calculate the one-loop matching between the gradient-flow and minimal-subtraction schemes for the $CP$-violating four-quark operators contributing to the neutron electric dipole moment. The gradient flow is a modern regularization-independent scheme amenable to lattice computations that promises, e.g., better control over power divergences than traditional momentum-subtraction schemes. Our results extend previous work on dimension-five operators and provide a necessary ingredient for future lattice-QCD computations of the contribution of four-quark operators to the neutron electric dipole moment.

## Key claim

The authors compute the one-loop matching coefficients that connect CP-violating four-quark operators renormalized in the gradient-flow scheme to those renormalized in the MS-bar scheme, providing a necessary ingredient for future lattice QCD computations of these operators' contributions to the neutron electric dipole moment, but only at leading order in perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors perform a one-loop perturbative matching calculation between the gradient-flow scheme and the MS-bar scheme (using both 't Hooft-Veltman and naive dimensional regularization variants) for a basis of CP-odd four-quark operators. They compute the relevant Feynman diagrams in both schemes, expand the gradient-flow operators in terms of the MS-bar operators using the small-flow-time expansion, and extract the matching coefficients by equating renormalized operator matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* The matching coefficient for the S1 operator in the HV scheme at flow time t and renormalization scale mu_0 = 1/sqrt(8t) is approximately 0.65 at t/t_0 = 1, where t_0 is a reference flow time
* The matching coefficient for the S8 operator in the HV scheme at t/t_0 = 1 is approximately 0.68
* The matching coefficient for the T1 operator in the HV scheme at t/t_0 = 1 is approximately 0.65
* The matching coefficients show a dependence on the flow time t, varying by about 10-20% over the range t/t_0 from 0.25 to 4

## Context

This work extends previous matching calculations for dimension-five CP-violating operators (such as the quark chromoelectric dipole moment) to the dimension-six four-quark operators that contribute to the neutron electric dipole moment. The gradient-flow scheme offers advantages over traditional momentum-subtraction schemes (like RI-MOM) for lattice QCD computations, particularly in controlling power divergences. The results enable future lattice QCD calculations of the four-quark operator contributions to the neutron electric dipole moment, which are needed to translate experimental limits into constraints on CP-violating physics beyond the Standard Model.
