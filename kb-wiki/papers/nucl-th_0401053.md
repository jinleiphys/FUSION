# Local density and the RPA corrections in charge current quasielastic neutrino on Oxygen, Argon and Iron scattering

> Numerical computations of cross sections for quasielastic charge current scattering of neutrino on Oxygen, Argon and Iron in Local Density Approximation (LDA) are presented. We consider processes for a few GeV neutrino energy. We include corrections from nucleon re-interaction in nucleus described by relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RPA). We adopt the relativistic Fermi gas model of nucleus with and without taking into account the effective mass of nucleons.

# Local density and the RPA corrections in charge current quasielastic neutrino on Oxygen, Argon and Iron scattering

## Key claim
The local density effect in quasielastic neutrino-nucleus cross sections can be replaced by calculations using equivalent average parameters (average Fermi momentum and effective mass), making numerical computations faster, with differences between local and constant density approaches on the order of 0 to 1.5% regardless of how sophisticated the nuclear model is.

## Method
The author computes differential and total cross sections for charge current quasielastic neutrino scattering on Oxygen-16, Argon-40, and Iron-56 at 1 GeV neutrino energy using the Local Density Approximation (LDA), where the cross section per nucleon is integrated over the nuclear charge density profile. The relativistic Fermi gas model is used as the starting point, with corrections from nucleon re-interaction described by relativistic Random Phase Approximation (RPA) including pion and rho meson contributions, with a Landau-Migdal parameter g' = 0.7, and calculations are performed both with and without the effective mass of nucleons determined from the self-consistency equation of relativistic Mean Field Theory.

## Key numbers
- Average Fermi momenta: Oxygen 199.21 MeV, Argon 216.88 MeV, Iron 216.91 MeV
- Average effective masses: Oxygen 690.78 MeV, Argon 631.37 MeV, Iron 634.84 MeV
- Ratio of total cross sections (local density to constant density) for free Fermi gas at 1 GeV: Oxygen 0.997, Argon 0.994, Iron 0.994
- Ratio of total cross sections (local density to constant density) for Fermi gas with effective mass at 1 GeV: Oxygen 0.994, Argon 0.986, Iron 0.987
- Ratio of total cross sections (local density to constant density) for Fermi gas with RPA at 1 GeV: Oxygen 0.997, Argon 0.994, Iron 0.995
- Ratio of total cross sections (local density to constant density) for Fermi gas with RPA and effective mass at 1 GeV: Oxygen 0.993, Argon 0.989, Iron 0.990
- Application of effective mass lowers the peak of differential cross sections by about 30%
- Axial mass M_A = 1.03 GeV, vector mass squared M_V^2 = 0.71 GeV^2, axial coupling g_A = -1.26

## Context
This work extends the author's previous paper (Graczyk, 2003) where relativistic MFT and RPA corrections were developed analytically, by applying the Local Density Approximation to specific nuclear targets relevant to neutrino experiments: Oxygen (Super-Kamiokande), Argon (ICARUS), and Iron (MINOS). The approach builds on the relativistic Fermi gas model of Moniz (1969) and the relativistic RPA framework of Horowitz (1993), using experimental charge density distributions from DeVries (1987). The results enable faster numerical calculations for neutrino-nucleus cross sections by showing that average parameters can replace full LDA integration, which is important for oscillation analyses in long-baseline and atmospheric neutrino experiments.
