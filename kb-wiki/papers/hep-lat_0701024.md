# Phase diagram at finite temperature and quark density in the strong coupling region of lattice QCD for color SU(3)

> We study the phase diagram of quark matter at finite temperature (T) and chemical potential (mu) in the strong coupling region of lattice QCD for color SU(3). Baryon has effects to extend the hadron phase to a larger mu direction relative to Tc at low temperatures in the strong coupling limit. With the 1/g^2 corrections, Tc is found to decrease rapidly as g decreases, and the shape of the phase diagram becomes closer to that expected in the real world.

## Key claim

The authors claim that including baryon effects and finite coupling corrections (1/g^2) in the strong coupling limit of lattice QCD can bring the ratio R_muT = mu_c(T=0)/T_c(mu=0) from below 0.5 up to approximately 1.8 at g ~ 1, making the phase diagram qualitatively closer to that expected in the real world, though the results are not fully consistent with previous findings by Bilic (1995).

## Method

The authors derive an effective free energy for color SU(3) lattice QCD in the strong coupling limit by integrating spatial links and decomposing the six-fermion baryonic action into bilinear form using auxiliary fields (diquark field phi, auxiliary baryon field b, chiral condensate sigma, and an auxiliary field omega). They then incorporate 1/g^2 corrections from plaquette contributions using a mean-field bosonization scheme, obtaining modified quark mass and effective chemical potential, and compute the phase diagram at finite temperature T and chemical potential mu.

## Key numbers

- In the strong coupling limit, baryon effects extend the hadron phase in the larger mu direction relative to T_c by up to about 30%
- The ratio R_muT = mu_c(T=0)/T_c(mu=0) is less than 0.5 in all previous strong coupling limit works
- With 1/g^2 corrections, R_muT grows to around 1.8 at g ~ 1
- The decomposition parameter alpha = 0.2 approximately maximizes the ratio R_muT

## Context

This work extends the strong coupling limit approach to lattice QCD developed by Kawamoto (1981), Faldt and Petersson (1986), Bilic (1992), and Nishida (2004), which previously ignored baryon effects in finite temperature treatments through the 1/d expansion. The results are not fully consistent with previous findings by Bilic (1995), and the authors note that differences in the bosonization scheme and lattice anisotropy need further investigation. The work enables future simultaneous evaluation of both finite coupling corrections and baryon effects to better understand the actual QCD phase diagram.
