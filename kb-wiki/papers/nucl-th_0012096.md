# Nucleon Polarisabilities from Compton Scattering on the Deuteron

> An analytic calculation of the differential cross section for elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron at photon energies ωin the range of 25-50 MeV is presented to next-to-next-to-leading order, i.e. to an accuracy of \sim 3%. The calculation is model-independent and performed in the low energy nuclear Effective Field Theory without dynamical pions. The iso-scalar, scalar electric and magnetic nucleon polarisabilities α_0 and β_0 enter as free parameters with a theoretical uncertainty of about 20%. Using data at $ω_{Lab}=49 MeV$ we find $α_0=8.4\pm 3.0(exp)\pm 1.7(theor)$, $β_0=8.9\pm 3.9(exp)\pm 1.8(theor)$, each in units of $10^{-4} fm^3$. With the experimental constraint for the iso-scalar Baldin sum rule, $α_0=7.2\pm 2.1(exp)\pm 1.6(theor)$, $β_0=6.9\mp 2.1(exp)\mp 1.6(theor)$. A more accurate result can be achieved by: (i) better experimental data, and (ii) a higher order theoretical calculation including contributions from a couple of so far undetermined four-nucleon-two-photon operators.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent extraction of the iso-scalar scalar electric and magnetic nucleon polarisabilities from elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron, but the results carry a theoretical uncertainty of about 20% and are limited to photon energies around 49 MeV where the polarisability contributions become visible at next-to-next-to-leading order.

## Method

The calculation was performed in the low-energy nuclear Effective Field Theory without dynamical pions, where nucleons and photons are the only dynamical degrees of freedom and pions are integrated out into contact interactions. The differential cross section for elastic Compton scattering on the deuteron was computed analytically to next-to-next-to-leading order (N2LO) in the centre-of-mass frame for photon energies in regime II (omega ~ gamma ~ 45 MeV), where the binding momentum of the deuteron gamma = 45.7066 MeV sets the scale. The iso-scalar scalar electric and magnetic polarisabilities alpha_0 and beta_0 enter as free parameters at N2LO, and a chi-squared fit to experimental data at omega_Lab = 49 MeV was performed to extract their values.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron binding momentum: gamma = 45.7066 MeV
- Deuteron wave function renormalisation residue: Z_d = 1.690(3)
- Nucleon-nucleon scattering length in the 1S0 channel: a^(1S0) = -23.714 fm
- Iso-vector nuclear magnetic moment squared: kappa_1^2 = 5.536
- Without Baldin sum rule constraint: alpha_0 = 8.4 +/- 3.0 (exp) +/- 1.7 (theor), beta_0 = 8.9 +/- 3.9 (exp) +/- 1.8 (theor), each in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- With Baldin sum rule constraint: alpha_0 = 7.2 +/- 2.1 (exp) +/- 1.6 (theor), beta_0 = 6.9 -/+ 2.1 (exp) -/+ 1.6 (theor), each in units of 10^{-4} fm^3
- Theoretical accuracy of the calculation: ~3% (order (Q/Lambda)^3 less than or equal to 0.03)
- Photon energy used for data fit: omega_Lab = 49 MeV
- Photon energy range of the calculation: 25-50 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous Effective Field Theory calculations of Compton scattering on the deuteron at next-to-leading order by Chen, Griesshammer, Savage, and Springer (1999) and by Beane, Malheiro, McGovern, Phillips, and van Kolck (2000), which demonstrated consistency with chiral perturbation theory values for the iso-scalar polarisabilities. The calculation is unique in being model-independent and free from assumptions about the pion-nucleon interaction, the inter-nucleon potential, or the choice of regulator, unlike earlier extractions from neutron scattering off heavy nuclei by Koester et al. (1995) and by Schmiedmayer, Riehs, Harvey, and Hill (1991), which gave conflicting values. The results enable future improvements through better experimental data and higher-order theoretical calculations including four-nucleon-two-photon operators.
