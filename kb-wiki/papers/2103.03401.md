# Modified equilibrium distributions for Cooper--Frye particlization

> We introduce a positive definite single-particle distribution that is suitable for describing the transition from a macroscopic hydrodynamic to a microscopic kinetic description during the late stages of heavy-ion collisions in the presence of moderately large viscous corrections. The modified equilibrium distribution function can be constructed with hydrodynamic input from either relativistic viscous fluid dynamics or anisotropic fluid dynamics. We test the modified equilibrium distribution's hydrodynamic output for a stationary hadron resonance gas subject to either shear stress, bulk pressure, or baryon diffusion current at a given freeze-out temperature and baryon chemical potential. While it does not reproduce all components of the net baryon current and energy-momentum tensor exactly, it significantly improves upon the customary linearized approximations for the non-equilibrium correction $δf_n$ which typically lead to unphysical negative distribution functions at large particle momenta. A comparison of particle spectra and $p_T$-differential elliptic flow coefficients from the Cooper-Frye formula computed with the modified equilibrium distribution and with linearized $δf_n$ corrections is presented, for two different (2+1)-dimensional hypersurfaces corresponding to central and non-central Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim

The authors introduce a positive definite modified equilibrium distribution for Cooper-Frye particlization that avoids the unphysical negative distribution functions at high momentum that plague linearized δf corrections, but they acknowledge that this modified distribution does not exactly reproduce all components of the net baryon current and energy-momentum tensor, with discrepancies growing at moderately large viscous corrections.

## Method

The authors derive momentum-dependent perturbations to the temperature, chemical potential, and fluid velocity from the first-order RTA Chapman-Enskog expansion of the Boltzmann equation, then incorporate these perturbations into the exponential argument of the equilibrium distribution function. They further reformulate the momentum-dependent perturbations using a local momentum transformation (deformation matrix and shift vector) originally developed by Pratt and Torrieri (2010), extended here to include baryon diffusion. The resulting modified equilibrium distribution is tested on a stationary hadron resonance gas subject to shear stress, bulk pressure, or baryon diffusion, and then applied to compute particle spectra and elliptic flow from (2+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic hypersurfaces for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energies.

- The small-viscosity limits for validity are stated as |Π| << β_Π, sqrt(V_{B,μ} V_B^μ) << β_V, and sqrt(π_{μν} π^{μν}) << 2β_π.

## Context

This work extends the modified equilibrium distribution approach of Pratt and Torrieri (2010), which added dissipative perturbations to the temperature, chemical potential, and momentum variables in the Boltzmann factor, but which showed strong deviations for bulk viscous pressure. The authors improve upon that formulation by deriving the perturbations systematically from the RTA Chapman-Enskog expansion and by including baryon diffusion effects not considered in the original Pratt-Torrieri work. The modified distribution is also related to the Romatschke-Strickland anisotropic distribution (2003) used in anisotropic fluid dynamics, and the authors demonstrate that their method can be applied to that distribution as well. This work enables more reliable particlization in hybrid hydrodynamic-kinetic models of heavy-ion collisions, particularly in regions where bulk viscous corrections are large near the quark-hadron phase transition.
