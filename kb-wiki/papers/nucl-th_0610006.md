# Three-Body Elastic and Inelastic Scattering at Intermediate Energies

> The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering at arbitrary energies is formulated in momentum space and directly solved in terms of momentum vectors without employing a partial wave decomposition. For identical bosons this results in a three-dimensional integral equation in five variables, magnitudes of relative momenta and angles. The cross sections for both elastic and breakup processes in the intermediate energy range up to about 1 GeV are calculated based on a Malfliet-Tjon type potential, and the convergence of the multiple scattering series is investigated.

# Three-Body Elastic and Inelastic Scattering at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim
The Faddeev equation for three-body scattering can be solved directly in momentum space using vector variables without partial wave decomposition, enabling calculations of elastic and breakup cross sections at intermediate energies up to about 1 GeV. However, for specific exclusive breakup configurations such as the space star and coplanar star at 1 GeV, the multiple scattering series converges very slowly or oscillates, meaning the full Faddeev solution is required even at this high energy and low-order approximations are not reliable.

## Method
The authors formulated and solved the Faddeev equation for three identical bosons in momentum space using vector Jacobi momenta, resulting in a three-dimensional integral equation in five variables (magnitudes of relative momenta and angles). They employed a Malfliet-Tjon type potential (short-range repulsive and intermediate-range attractive Yukawa forces) with parameters adjusted to support a bound state at -2.23 MeV, and treated logarithmic singularities with a spline-based semi-analytic method. The scattering amplitude was then used to calculate elastic differential cross sections and both semi-exclusive and exclusive breakup cross sections, with convergence of the multiple scattering series examined by comparing the full solution to successive low-order contributions.

## Key numbers
- Bound state energy of the Malfliet-Tjon potential: \(E_d = -2.23\) MeV
- Elastic differential cross sections calculated at laboratory energies: 0.2 GeV, 0.5 GeV, 0.8 GeV, and 1.0 GeV
- Semi-exclusive cross section \(d\sigma(N,N')\) calculated at 1.0 GeV laboratory incident energy for emission angles 15° and 33°
- For exclusive breakup at \(E_{lab} = 1.0\) GeV, the energy of an outgoing particle in the star configuration is approximately \((\frac{2}{3} E_{lab} + E_d)/2 = 332\) MeV
- For exclusive breakup at \(E_{lab} = 1.0\) GeV, the quasi-free scattering peak occurs at approximately 168 MeV (one quarter of total energy)
- The maximum energy of an outgoing particle in exclusive breakup at 1.0 GeV is \(\frac{2}{3} E_{lab} + E_d \approx 664\) MeV
- Semi-exclusive cross section at \(E_{lab} = 495\) MeV and \(\theta_{lab} = 18^\circ\) calculated with first order term, comparing non-relativistic, relativistic kinematics, and full relativistic dynamics

## Context
This work extends the traditional partial-wave-based Faddeev approach to three-body scattering, which becomes computationally prohibitive at intermediate energies due to the proliferation of partial waves needed for convergence. The authors directly solve the Faddeev equation in vector variables, avoiding the partial wave decomposition used in prior low-energy three-nucleon calculations. The results enable systematic investigation of the multiple scattering series convergence across the full three-body phase space at energies up to 1 GeV, and provide a framework for incorporating relativistic effects via Poincaré invariant quantum mechanics, with first-order relativistic comparisons shown against data from Chen (year not specified in text). Further studies scanning the complete three-body phase space are underway to determine regions where low-order calculations may be valid, which is important for interpreting previous analyses of p(d,ppn) reactions that relied on low-order reaction mechanisms.
