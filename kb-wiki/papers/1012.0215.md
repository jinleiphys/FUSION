# Charge Symmetry Breaking in Parton Distribution Functions from Lattice QCD

> By determining the quark momentum fractions of the octet baryons from N_f=2+1 lattice simulations, we are able to predict the degree of charge symmetry violation in the parton distribution functions of the nucleon. This is of importance, not only as a probe of our understanding of the non-perturbative structure of the proton but also because such a violation constrains the accuracy of global fits to parton distribution functions and hence the accuracy with which, for example, cross sections at the LHC can be predicted. A violation of charge symmetry may also be critical in cases where symmetries are used to guide the search for physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD determination of charge symmetry violation (CSV) in the second moments of nucleon parton distribution functions, finding that the CSV momentum fractions are roughly equal in magnitude with opposite sign, but the results are based on a single lattice volume and lattice spacing and do not include electromagnetic effects.

## Method

The authors performed N_f=2+1 lattice QCD simulations on 24^3 x 48 volumes with lattice spacing a=0.083(3) fm, using Symanzik improved gluon action and nonperturbatively O(a) improved Wilson fermions. They computed the second moments (quark momentum fractions) of the octet baryons (proton, Sigma, Xi) by evaluating matrix elements of local twist-2 operators via two-point and three-point correlation functions at zero momentum. By varying the light and strange quark masses around the SU(3)_flavour symmetric point while keeping the singlet quark mass constant, they extracted the derivatives needed to determine CSV from the u-d mass difference using linear flavour expansions.

## Key numbers

- The CSV momentum fractions obtained are delta u = -0.0023(6) and delta d = 0.0020(3) at Q^2 approximately 4 GeV^2
- The ratio m_delta/m_qbar = 0.066(7) from chiral perturbation theory was used as input
- The isovector momentum fraction x_{u-d}^p = 0.158 at 4 GeV^2 was taken from experiment
- The slopes extracted from the lattice data are: delta u / x_{u-d}^p = (m_delta/m_qbar)(-0.221 +/- 0.054) and delta d / x_{u-d}^p = (m_delta/m_qbar)(0.195 +/- 0.025)
- Pion masses ranged from 334(12) MeV to 460(17) MeV across the five ensembles
- The ratio x_u^p / x_d^p was found to be approximately 2.3

## Context

This work provides the first nonperturbative QCD determination of CSV in PDFs, extending earlier phenomenological estimates from the MIT bag model by Sather (1991) and Rodionov et al. (1994), which predicted delta u = -0.0014 and delta d = 0.0015. The results are consistent with the best-fit values from the global analysis by Martin et al. (2003) (MRST), which found delta u = -delta d = -0.002^{+0.009}_{-0.006} at 90% CL, but with considerably smaller uncertainties. The paper enables improved constraints on CSV that are relevant for reducing the NuTeV anomaly in neutrino deep-inelastic scattering and for improving the accuracy of cross-section predictions at the LHC, though the authors note that future work must address finite-volume and discretisation effects and separately calculate CSV from electromagnetic effects.
