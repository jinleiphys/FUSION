# Neutron Stars in the Relativistic Hartree-Fock Theory and Hadron-Quark Phase Transition

> Based on the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock theory (DDRHF) for hadronic matter, the properties of neutron stars have been studied and compared with the results from the density-dependent relativistic mean field theory (DDRMF). Though similar equations of state are obtained, DDRHF calculations give larger fractions of proton, electron and muon at high baryon density for neutron star matter than the ones from DDRMF. The maximum masses of neutron stars lie between 2.3 M$_\odot$ and 2.5 M$_\odot$, and the corresponding radii between 11.7 km and 12.5 km. In addition, the phase transition from hadronic matter to quark matter in neutron stars is studied by using the MIT bag model for the quark phase. The transition is studied in both Gibbs and Maxwell constructions.

## Key claim

The density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory predicts neutron star maximum masses between 2.3 and 2.5 solar masses, consistent with observational constraints from PSR J0751+1807, but the larger proton fractions it yields at high baryon density violate the direct Urca cooling constraint for stars below 1.5 solar masses, which the authors note may be mitigated by uncertainties in cooling mechanisms or the appearance of a quark phase.

## Method

The authors computed the equation of state (EoS) of beta-stable neutron star matter using the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory with three parameter sets (PKO1, PKO2, PKO3) and compared results with four density-dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) interactions (TW99, DD-ME1, DD-ME2, PKDD). For the hadron-quark phase transition, they employed the MIT bag model with bag constants B = 90, 120, and 150 MeV/fm^3, treating the transition via both Maxwell and Gibbs (Glendenning) constructions.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star masses from DDRHF: between 2.3 M_sun and 2.5 M_sun
- Corresponding radii for maximum-mass configurations: between 11.7 km and 12.5 km
- For the PKO1 interaction, direct Urca processes occur at a fairly low mass of 1.2 M_sun
- For hybrid stars (hadronic + MIT bag model quark phase), the maximum mass never exceeds about 1.6 M_sun regardless of bag constant
- Bag constants used for the MIT model: B = 90, 120, 150 MeV/fm^3
- Strange quark mass used: m_s = 150 MeV
- Renormalization point for quark matter: sigma_ren = 313 MeV

## Context

This work extends the density-dependent relativistic Hartree-Fock (DDRHF) theory developed by the same group (DDRHF1, DDRHF2, DDRHF3) to neutron star structure, comparing with the density-dependent relativistic mean field (DDRMF) models of Typel and Wolter (1999), DD-ME1, DD-ME2, and PKDD. The hadron-quark phase transition analysis using the MIT bag model follows the approach of Glendenning (2001) and earlier quark matter studies. The results enable future work with more refined quark models, as the simple MIT bag model with constant B cannot reproduce recent observational constraints on neutron star masses and radii.
