# How Universal is the Coupling in the Sigma Model?

> We calculate pion-pion scattering lengths and sigma, rho and a1 decay widths from a gauged linear sigma model with two flavours and its globally invariant generalisation.

# How Universal is the Coupling in the Sigma Model?

## Key claim
The gauged linear sigma model with local chiral symmetry cannot simultaneously reproduce experimental pion-pion scattering lengths and the decay widths of the sigma, rho, and a1 mesons; promoting the local symmetry to a global one introduces separate coupling constants for scalar-vector and vector-vector interactions, which can fix the rho decay width but still leaves the sigma decay width too small unless additional scalar degrees of freedom are added.

## Method
The authors calculated tree-level pion-pion scattering amplitudes and decay widths from a U(2)R x U(2)L gauged linear sigma model containing scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, and axial-vector mesons. They then modified the Lagrangian by replacing the single gauge coupling with two independent couplings (g1 for scalar-vector interactions and g2 for vector-vector interactions) to implement global rather than local chiral symmetry, and added an extra scalar-pion interaction term to address the sigma decay width.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant: f_pi = 92.4 MeV
- For the locally symmetric model, the sigma mass required to match scattering lengths is m_sigma = 315-345 MeV, depending on the choice of the a1 mass and the parameter Z = m_a1 / m_rho
- The locally symmetric model gives Gamma_rho->pi pi = 86.5 MeV, compared to the experimental value of 149.4 +/- 1.0 MeV
- The locally symmetric model gives Gamma_sigma->pi pi < 107 MeV for m_sigma < 800 MeV, compared to the PDG range of 600-1200 MeV
- With global symmetry and g2 = 1.77, the rho decay width matches the PDG value of 149.4 MeV
- With global symmetry, Gamma_a1->rho pi = 516 MeV for m_a1 = 1097 MeV (KSFR rule) and approximately 1.3 GeV for m_a1 = 1230 MeV
- After adding the extra scalar S interpreted as f0(600), the best fit gives m_S = 608 MeV and Gamma_S->pi pi = 466 MeV, obtained for a0^0 = 0.206, a0^2 = -0.0295, and coupling g3 = 0.645

## Context
This work extends the gauged linear sigma model approach of Gasiorowicz and Geffen (1969) and Koch and Rischke (2000) by addressing the failure of local chiral symmetry to simultaneously describe scattering lengths and decay widths. The authors follow the suggestion of Urban, Wambach, and Brink (2002) to use global instead of local chiral symmetry, which introduces separate coupling constants. The paper enables future studies of vacuum phenomenology including the nature of scalar mesons (whether f0(600) and a0(980) are quarkonia or tetraquark states), extension to nonzero temperature for chiral symmetry restoration, and inclusion of nucleon fields with chiral partners.
