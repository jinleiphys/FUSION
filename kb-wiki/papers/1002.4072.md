# Energy scan by $ϕ$ mesons and threshold energy for the confinement-deconfinement phase transition

> We argue that the ratio of $ϕ$ mesons multiplicity over cube of the mean $p_T$ is proportional to the degeneracy of the medium produced in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The ratio extracted from the existing $ϕ$ meson data in the energy range $\sqrt{s}$=6.3-200 GeV, indicate that beyond a threshold energy $\sqrt{s}_{th}=15.74\pm 8.10$ GeV, the medium crosses over from a confined phase to a deconfined phase.

## Key claim

The ratio of phi meson multiplicity over the cube of the mean transverse momentum is argued to be proportional to the degeneracy of the medium produced in heavy ion collisions, and existing data in the energy range sqrt(s)=6.3-200 GeV indicate a threshold energy for the confinement-deconfinement phase transition of sqrt(s)_th=15.74±8.10 GeV, though the step function fit and a power law fit are statistically equivalent (both giving chi^2/N < 1), so the claim of a step-function behavior cannot be definitively established from chi^2 analysis alone.

## Method

The ratio R = ⟨N^φ⟩ / (0.5 N_part ΔY ⟨p_T^φ⟩^3) was constructed from published NA49 and STAR data for φ meson multiplicity and mean transverse momentum in Pb+Pb, Au+Au, and Cu+Cu collisions across sqrt(s)=6.3-200 GeV. The ratio was fitted with a step function (tanh form) to extract a threshold energy, and also with a power law for comparison. Using a previously published hydrodynamic model analysis (Roy 2009) that gave a logarithmic relation between spatially averaged initial temperature and collision energy, the energy dependence of the ratio was converted into temperature dependence and compared with lattice QCD results for s/T^3 from Cheng (2007).

## Key numbers

- Threshold energy for confinement-deconfinement phase transition: sqrt(s)_th = 15.74 ± 8.10 GeV, from step function fit to the ratio R (chi^2/N = 0.3)
- Width of the transition: Δsqrt(s)_th = 14.52 ± 14.93 GeV
- Step function fit parameter α = 0.017 ± 0.009
- Power law fit to the ratio R gave chi^2/N = 0.92
- Threshold temperature extracted from the ratio as a function of temperature: T_th = 202 ± 23 MeV, with ΔT_th = 33 ± 37 MeV
- Cross-over temperature from lattice simulation (Cheng 2007): T_co = 197 ± 3 MeV
- Logarithmic fit parameters for average initial temperature vs. collision energy: A = 0.126, B = 0.063 (from Roy 2009 hydrodynamic analysis)
- Uncertainty in φ multiplicity at low SPS energy: ~20-40%; at RHIC energy: ~10-20%
- Uncertainty in mean p_T across the full energy range: less than 10%

## Context

This work extends the search for a threshold energy for the confinement-deconfinement phase transition, which is one aim of the STAR energy scan programme at RHIC (Caines 2009, Odyniec 2008). The observable proposed here builds on the established use of strangeness enhancement as a QGP signature (Koch 1986) and on the unique properties of φ mesons as probes (Mohanty 2009). The ratio R is compared directly with lattice QCD results for s/T^3 from Cheng (2007), and the extracted threshold temperature (202 MeV) agrees closely with the lattice cross-over temperature (197 MeV). The work enables future STAR energy scan data at sqrt(s)=5, 7.7, 11.5, 17.3, 27, and 39 GeV to determine the threshold energy and transition width more accurately.
