# Probing Invisible Vector Meson Decays with NA64 and LDMX

> Electron beam fixed target experiments such as NA64 and LDMX use missing energy-momentum to detect the production of dark matter and other long-lived states. The most studied production mechanism is dark Bremsstrahlung through a vector mediator. In this work, we explore a complementary source of missing energy-momentum signals: Bremsstrahlung photons can convert to hard vector mesons in exclusive photoproduction processes, which then decay to dark matter or other invisible particles, such as neutrinos. We find that existing NA64 data can improve the leading constraints on invisible light vector meson decays, while a future run of LDMX could improve them by up to $5$ orders of magnitude. For the examples of a dark photon and a $U(1)_B$ gauge boson mediator, accounting for meson decays substantially enhances these experiments' sensitivity, especially to thermal relic dark matter of mass $m_χ\gtrsim 0.1\ \mathrm{GeV}$.

## Key claim
Existing NA64 data can improve leading constraints on invisible light vector meson decays, and a future run of LDMX could improve them by up to 5 orders of magnitude, but these improvements depend on the meson yield surviving detector veto cuts for recoiling nucleons from incoherent photoproduction, which requires detailed simulation.

## Method
The authors calculate the exclusive photoproduction yields of vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ, J/ψ) in electron beam fixed-target experiments by combining measured per-nucleon photoproduction cross sections with Glauber optical model calculations for coherent and incoherent production on nuclear targets. They then compute invisible branching ratios for these mesons in dark photon and U(1)_B gauge boson models, accounting for mixing effects and resonance overlap between the vector meson and the dark mediator. The expected number of missing-energy events is obtained by multiplying the meson yield by the invisible decay branching ratio, assuming zero background events for 90% C.L. limits.

## Key numbers
- NA64 (current): 2.8 × 10^11 electrons on target at 100 GeV beam energy, yielding 7 × 10^6 ρ mesons, 5 × 10^5 ω mesons, 5 × 10^5 φ mesons, and 6 × 10^3 J/ψ mesons
- NA64 (ultimate): 5 × 10^12 electrons on target, yielding 1.2 × 10^8 ρ, 9 × 10^6 ω, 8 × 10^6 φ, and 1.1 × 10^5 J/ψ mesons
- LDMX Phase I: 4 × 10^14 electrons at 4 GeV beam energy, yielding 1.1 × 10^9 ρ, 1.9 × 10^8 ω, and 1.1 × 10^7 φ mesons
- LDMX Phase II: 10^16 electrons at 8 GeV beam energy, yielding 3 × 10^10 ρ, 3 × 10^9 ω, and 5 × 10^8 φ mesons
- The probability p_V for a hard Bremsstrahlung photon to produce a vector meson via exclusive photoproduction is approximately 10^-5 × (X_0 / 12.86 g/cm^2) × (σ_0^V / 1 μb) for copper-like targets
- Existing experimental limits on invisible branching ratios include: ω < 7 × 10^-5 (BESIII 2018), φ < 1.7 × 10^-4 (BESIII 2018), J/ψ < 7 × 10^-4 (BaBar 2013), and π^0 < 4.4 × 10^-9 (NA62 2020)

## Context
This work extends the missing energy/momentum approach of NA64 (Gninenko 2014) and LDMX (2018, 2019) beyond dark Bremsstrahlung production to include exclusive vector meson photoproduction as a complementary signal source. It complements the work of Gninenko (2014) on invisible decays of light pseudoscalar mesons and the effective field theory studies of invisible meson decays by Darme (2020) and Bertuzzo (2020). The results enable electron beam experiments to probe dark matter couplings to quarks, complementing existing constraints from flavor factories (BESIII, BaBar) and beam dumps (LSND, E137, MiniBooNE, COHERENT), and are particularly sensitive to thermal relic dark matter with mass m_χ ≳ 0.1 GeV.
