# Study of 14.1 MeV Neutron Moderation in Beryllium

> This study investigates the moderation of 14.1 MeV neutrons in a natural beryllium moderator arranged in a spherical geometry. The neutron interactions and moderation efficiency were analyzed using Monte Carlo simulations with the GEANT4 toolkit. Various sphere radii were tested to determine the optimal moderator thickness for neutron thermalization.

## Key claim

The optimal thickness for a spherical natural beryllium moderator to thermalize 14.1 MeV neutrons lies between 20 cm and 30 cm radius, where both total neutron production and thermal neutron output are maximized; however, the abstract's claim of "optimal moderator thickness" is qualified by the finding that the thermal neutron yield peaks at 40 cm before decreasing at larger radii.

## Method

Monte Carlo simulations using the GEANT4 toolkit (version 10.2) with the High Precision physics list were performed for a point-like isotropic 14.1 MeV neutron source at the center of natural beryllium spheres. Thirteen sphere radii from 2.5 cm to 45.0 cm were tested, with 10^6 neutrons generated per configuration. The analysis tracked neutrons exiting the sphere, neutrons with energy below 0.025 eV exiting the sphere, the energy spectrum of exiting neutrons, and energy deposited within the sphere.

## Key numbers

* 10^6 neutrons of 14.1 MeV were generated from the center of each of thirteen beryllium spheres.
* Thirteen sphere radii were tested: 2.5 cm, 5.0 cm, 7.5 cm, 10.0 cm, 12.5 cm, 15.0 cm, 17.5 cm, 20.0 cm, 25.0 cm, 30.0 cm, 35.0 cm, 40.0 cm, and 45.0 cm.
* Thermal neutron yield (neutrons with energy less than or equal to 0.025 eV exiting the sphere) is negligible for moderator thicknesses below 10 cm.
* Thermal neutron yield peaks at 40 cm radius before decreasing at larger thicknesses.
* The optimal moderator thickness range is between 20 cm and 30 cm radius.

## Context

This work extends prior Monte Carlo studies of neutron moderation in beryllium by systematically testing thirteen sphere radii to identify the optimal configuration for 14.1 MeV neutron thermalization. The results enable design of beryllium moderators for fusion neutron sources, with the authors noting that neutrons with slightly higher energies than thermal could be further moderated in a second step when crossing a Lithium Deuteride fuel cell. The study uses the GEANT4 toolkit (version 10.2) with High Precision physics lists, building on the standard GEANT4 models described by the GEANT4 collaboration (2006) and the GEANT4 physics reference manual.
