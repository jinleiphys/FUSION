# Probability distribution for the relative velocity of colliding particles in a relativistic classical gas

> We find the probability density function $\mathcal{P}(V_{\texttt{r}})$ of the relativistic relative velocity for two colliding particles in a non-degenerate relativistic gas. The distribution reduces to Maxwell distribution for the relative velocity in the non-relativistic limit. We find an exact formula for the mean value $\langle V_{\texttt{r}}\rangle$. The mean velocity tends to the Maxwell's value in the non-relativistic limit and to the velocity of light in the ultra-relativistic limit. At a given temperature $T$, when at least for one of the two particles the ratio of the rest energy over the thermal energy $m c^2/k_B T$ is smaller than 40 the Maxwell distribution is inadequate.

# Probability distribution for the relative velocity of colliding particles in a relativistic classical gas

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact probability density function for the relativistic relative velocity of two colliding particles in a non-degenerate relativistic gas, showing that the non-relativistic Maxwell distribution for relative velocity becomes inadequate when at least one particle has a ratio of rest energy to thermal energy \(m c^2 / k_B T\) smaller than 40.

## Method

Starting from the Lorentz-invariant reaction rate expressed in terms of the Jüttner distribution (the relativistic generalization of the Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution), the author performs a change of variables from particle momenta and energies to the Mandelstam invariant \(s\) and then to the relativistic relative velocity \(V_{\texttt{r}}\). The probability density function \(\mathcal{P}_{\texttt{r}}(V_{\texttt{r}})\) is extracted from the normalized integral, and the mean relative velocity \(\langle V_{\texttt{r}} \rangle\) is computed analytically using integrals of modified Bessel functions of the second kind.

## Key numbers

- The Maxwell distribution for relative velocity is inadequate when \(m c^2 / k_B T < 40\) for at least one of the two colliding particles.
- In the non-relativistic limit (\(x \gg 1\)), the mean relative velocity for equal masses reduces to \(\langle V_{\texttt{r}} \rangle_d \sim 4\sqrt{1/(\pi x)}\) to lowest order, matching the Maxwell value.
- In the ultra-relativistic limit (\(x \ll 1\)), the mean relative velocity for equal masses approaches \(\langle V_{\texttt{r}} \rangle_d \sim 1 + \mathcal{O}(x^4)\), tending to the speed of light.
- The non-relativistic Maxwell mean value \(\langle v_r \rangle = 4\sqrt{1/(\pi x)}\) is a good approximation for \(x > 40\).
- At \(x \lesssim 0.7\), the mean relative velocity is already well approximated by the asymptotic value 1.

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic treatment of relative velocity distributions in gases, where the Maxwell distribution (Equation 3 in the paper) has been standard, to the relativistic regime using the Jüttner distribution (Jüttner, 1911; DeGroot, 1980; Cercignani, 1988; Csernai, 1994). The derivation follows the approach of Gondolo and Gelmini (1991) for changing variables to the Mandelstam invariant \(s\). The results enable correct calculation of thermally averaged cross sections in relativistic contexts, such as dark matter relic density calculations, where the Møller velocity has often been used in place of the true relativistic relative velocity.
