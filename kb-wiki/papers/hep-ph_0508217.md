# Fit to the Bjorken, Ellis-Jaffe and Gross-Llewellyn-Smith sum rules in a renormalon based approach

> We study the large order behaviour in perturbation theory of the Bjorken, Ellis-Jaffe and Gross-Llewellyn-Smith sum rules. In particular, we consider their first infrared renormalons, for which we obtain their analytic structure with logarithmic accuracy and also an approximate determination of their normalization constant. Estimates of higher order terms of the perturbative series are given. The Renormalon subtracted scheme is worked out for these observables and compared with experimental data. Overall, good agreement with experiment is found. This allows us to obtain {\hat a}_0 and some higher-twist non-perturbative constants from experiment: {\hat a}_0=0.141\pm 0.089; f_{3,RS}(1 GeV)=-0.124^{+0.137}_{-0.142} GeV^2.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Renormalon Subtracted (RS) scheme, previously successful in heavy quark physics, can be applied to the Bjorken, Ellis-Jaffe, and Gross-Llewellyn-Smith sum rules to improve the convergence of the perturbative series and extract non-perturbative higher-twist matrix elements, though the situation is less optimal than in heavy quark physics because the closest singularities lie at u=±1 rather than u=1/2.

## Method

The authors computed the analytic structure of the first infrared renormalon for the leading-twist Wilson coefficients of the Bjorken, Ellis-Jaffe, and Gross-Llewellyn-Smith sum rules with logarithmic accuracy, and determined the normalization constants using conformal mapping to avoid ultraviolet renormalon effects. They then defined the Renormalon Subtracted scheme by subtracting the renormalon contribution from the perturbative series and adding it to the twist-4 operators, and performed fits to experimental data from the CCFR collaboration and other sources to extract the non-perturbative constants.

## Key numbers

- The extracted singlet axial charge: $\hat{a}_0 = 0.141 \pm 0.089$
- The extracted twist-4 matrix element in the RS scheme: $f_{3,RS}(1\ \text{GeV}) = -0.124^{+0.137}_{-0.142}\ \text{GeV}^2$
- For $n_f=3$, the infrared renormalon normalization constants: $N_B = -0.414 \pm 0.079$, $N_{EJ} = -0.103 \pm 0.035$, $N_{GLS} = -0.393 \pm 0.070$
- The neutron beta-decay constant used: $|g_A/g_V| = 1.2695 \pm 0.0029$
- The hyperon decay constant used: $a_8 = 0.572 \pm 0.019$

## Context

This work extends the renormalon-based approach previously applied to heavy quark physics by Pineda (2001, 2002) and Bali (2003) to the case of light-fermion systems in deep inelastic scattering. It provides estimates of higher-order perturbative coefficients that improve upon large-$\beta_0$ estimates by Broadhurst (1993) and are consistent with results from Pade approximants by Ellis (1995), while enabling more reliable extraction of higher-twist non-perturbative matrix elements from experimental data.
