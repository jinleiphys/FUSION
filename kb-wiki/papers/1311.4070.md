# Shear viscosity due to the Landau damping from quark-pion interaction

> We have calculated the shear viscosity coefficient $η$ of the strongly interacting matter in the relaxation time approximation, where a quasi particle description of quarks with its dynamical mass is considered from NJL model. Due to the thermodynamic scattering of quarks with pseudo scalar type condensate (i.e. pion), a non zero Landau damping will be acquired by the propagating quarks. This Landau damping may be obtained from the Landau cut contribution of the in-medium self-energy of quark-pion loop, which is evaluated in the framework of real-time thermal field theory.

## Key claim
The shear viscosity coefficient η of strongly interacting matter is calculated using the relaxation time approximation, where the Landau damping of quarks arises from forward and inverse scattering with pions in the thermal bath. The calculation is conditional on the on-shell condition that the Landau damping Γ_Q and Γ_π receive non-zero values only in the temperature range where m_π > 2M_Q, requiring a folding procedure with the pion spectral function to obtain finite results at lower temperatures.

## Method
The shear viscosity is computed in the relaxation time approximation using the quark and pion contributions, where the Landau damping Γ_Q and Γ_π are obtained from the imaginary parts of the retarded quark and pion self-energies evaluated at their respective on-shell momenta. The self-energies are calculated from one-loop quark-pion and quark-antiquark diagrams using real-time thermal field theory, with the dynamical quark mass M_Q determined from the temperature-dependent gap equation of the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model. The pion spectral function due to the Q\bar{Q} decay width is used to fold the quark Landau damping, removing divergences in the temperature region where m_π < 2M_Q.

## Key numbers
- NJL model parameters: current quark mass m_Q = 0.0056 GeV, zero-temperature constituent quark mass M_Q = 0.4 GeV, three-momentum cutoff Λ = 0.588 GeV, corresponding critical temperature T_c = 0.222 GeV for μ = 0.
- The isospin factor for the quark-pion loop is I_F = 3.
- The pion component of η after folding gives a negligible contribution of approximately 10^{-5} GeV^3.
- Lattice QCD calculation by Meyer gives η ∼ 0.054–0.47 GeV^3 near T_c, which is higher than the NJL-based calculations.
- The collision time τ below T ∼ 160 MeV exceeds the typical survival time of strongly interacting matter in heavy ion collisions (∼ 30–50 fm).

## Context
This work extends the calculation of shear viscosity in strongly interacting matter using the NJL model, building on the relaxation time approximation approach of Sasaki and Redlich (2009) and the earlier NJL model estimation by Zhuang et al. (1995). The results are compared with hadronic domain calculations by Nicola (2005) and Weise (2004), as well as lattice QCD results by Meyer (2007). The approach enables a more detailed understanding of the temperature dependence of η near the chiral phase transition, where the rapid melting of the quark condensate influences the viscosity, though the low-temperature results show discrepancies with standard hadronic matter calculations.
