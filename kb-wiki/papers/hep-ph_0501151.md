# D-mesons in dense nuclear matter

> The D-meson properties in dense nuclear matter are studied. The D-meson spectral density is obtained within the framework of a coupled-channel self-consistent calculation assuming, as bare meson-baryon interaction, a separable potential. The $Λ_c(2593)$ resonance is generated dynamically in our coupled-channel model. The medium modifications of the D-meson properties due to Pauli blocking and the dressing of D-mesons, nucleons and pions are also studied. We conclude that the self-consistent coupled-channel process reduces the in-medium effects on the D-meson compared to previous literature which do not considered the coupled-channel structure

## Key claim

The self-consistent coupled-channel calculation reduces the in-medium effects on the D-meson compared to previous literature that did not consider the coupled-channel structure, resulting in a small attractive real part of the in-medium D-meson potential regardless of the medium properties of the intermediate states.

## Method

The authors performed a self-consistent coupled-channel calculation of the D-meson single-particle potential in infinite symmetric nuclear matter using a separable potential as the bare meson-baryon interaction. The G-matrix was solved including coupled channels (DN, πΛc, πΣc, ηΛc, ηΣc) with the Λc(2593) resonance generated dynamically, and the D-meson self-energy was obtained in the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock approach with self-consistency achieved for the on-shell quasiparticle potential. Medium modifications from Pauli blocking and the dressing of D-mesons, nucleons, and pions were incorporated.

## Key numbers

- The Λc(2593) resonance position is reproduced at 2593.9 ± 2 MeV with width Γ = 3.6^{+2.0}_{-1.3} MeV, for coupling constants and cutoffs ranging from 0.8 GeV to 1.4 GeV.
- When only the D-meson is dressed self-consistently at nuclear saturation density ρ₀, the D-meson potential at k_D=0 ranges from 8.6 MeV (Λ=0.8 GeV) to -11.2 MeV (Λ=1.4 GeV).
- For the full self-consistent calculation including dressing of nucleons and pions at ρ₀, the D-meson potential at k_D=0 ranges from 2.6 MeV (Λ=0.8 GeV) to -12.3 MeV (Λ=1.4 GeV).
- The quasiparticle peak in the spectral density at zero momentum moves slightly to lower energies as density increases, with the Λc resonance appearing as a second peak around 1.63-1.65 GeV.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of D-meson in-medium properties using QCD sum-rule approaches (Arata, year not specified) and the quark-meson coupling model (QMC, year not specified), which predicted a mass drop of 50-60 MeV at nuclear matter density. The results contradict those earlier predictions by showing much smaller in-medium effects when coupled-channel dynamics and self-consistent dressing are included. The findings enable more realistic predictions for open-charm enhancement in nucleus-nucleus collisions and J/Ψ suppression, and can be tested with the future PANDA experiment at GSI.
