# Simultaneous minijets and QGP evolution

> Minijets traversing through the QGP formed in heavy-ion collisions deposit significant amount of energy in the bulk medium. They also create gradients in temperatures which alter the flow profile and enhance entropy production. We study the effects of minijets in a simultaneous hydro + jet framework and find that inclusion of minijets requires recalibration of transport properties extracted from the model-to-data comparisons.

## Key claim

Including minijets in a simultaneous hydro + jet framework for heavy-ion collisions requires recalibration of transport properties extracted from model-to-data comparisons, specifically the shear viscosity to entropy density ratio and the soft initial state normalization, even though the final hadronic observables can be matched with appropriate tuning.

## Method

The authors simulate Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV using PYTHIA8 for hard processes and IP-Glasma for the soft bulk medium initialization, with the same binary collision positions used for both. The bulk medium is evolved with the 3+1D hydrodynamic model MUSIC, while minijets propagate through the medium using the strong-weak hybrid model where energy loss occurs in the strong coupling limit and splittings in the weak coupling limit. The energy lost by minijets is smeared by a Gaussian and fed into the medium as source terms in the energy-momentum conservation equations, with hadronization performed via Cooper-Frye prescription at 145 MeV for the soft sector and Lund String model in PYTHIA for hard partons, followed by decays and cascade in UrQMD.

## Key numbers

- Minimum momentum threshold p^J_min values studied: 4 GeV, 7 GeV, and 10 GeV, with a "No Jets" baseline
- For p^J_min = 4 GeV: s_factor = 0.45, eta/s = 0.02
- For p^J_min = 7 GeV: s_factor = 0.82, eta/s = 0.1
- For p^J_min = 10 GeV: s_factor = 0.9, eta/s = 0.125
- For No Jets: s_factor = 0.915, eta/s = 0.13
- Hadronization temperature: 145 MeV

## Context

This work extends the simultaneous hydro + jet framework from Pablos (2022) by demonstrating that minijet inclusion significantly alters the hydrodynamic evolution profile and requires recalibration of transport coefficients, even when final observables are matched. The study contradicts the common practice of neglecting minijet-medium interactions in standard model-to-data comparisons for heavy-ion collisions at LHC and RHIC, as described in Gale (2013). Future model-to-data comparisons will need to account for minijet effects when extracting QGP properties, and the authors identify the need for a gradual soft-hard scale separation model to describe intermediate momentum modes.
