# Strange and non-strange quark mass dependence of elastic light resonances from SU(3) Unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory to one loop

> We study the light quark mass dependence of the f_0(600), kappa(800), rho(770) and K*(892) resonance parameters generated from elastic meson-meson scattering using unitarized one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory. First, we show that it is possible to fit simultaneously all experimental scattering data up to 0.8-1 GeV together with lattice results on decay constants and scattering lengths up to a pion mass of 400 MeV, using chiral parameters compatible with existing determinations. Then, the strange and non-strange quark masses are varied from the chiral limit up to values of interest for lattice studies. In these amplitudes, the mass and width of the rho(770) and K*(892) present a similar and smooth quark mass dependence. In contrast, both scalars present a similar non-analyticity at high quark masses. Nevertheless, the f_0(600) dependence on the non-strange quark mass is stronger than for the kappa(800) and the vectors. We also confirm the lattice assumption of quark mass independence of the vector two-meson coupling that, in contrast, is violated for scalars. As a consequence, vector widths are very well approximated by the KSRF relation, and their masses are shown to scale like their corresponding meson decay constants.

## Key claim

The quark mass dependence of the f0(600), κ(800), ρ(770) and K*(892) resonance parameters can be described using unitarized one-loop Chiral Perturbation Theory, but the scalar resonances (f0(600) and κ(800)) exhibit a non-analytic behavior at high quark masses that is absent for the vector resonances, and the f0(600) shows a stronger dependence on the non-strange quark mass than the κ(800) and the vectors.

## Method

The authors used the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) and its modified version (mIAM) to unitarize one-loop SU(3) Chiral Perturbation Theory amplitudes for elastic ππ and Kπ scattering. They performed simultaneous fits to experimental scattering data up to 0.8-1 GeV and to lattice results on decay constants and scattering lengths for pion masses up to 400 MeV, obtaining two sets of low-energy constants (LECs) labeled Fit I and Fit II. The quark masses were then varied from the chiral limit up to values relevant for lattice studies, with the non-strange quark mass varied up to m̂/m̂phys ≤ 9 (corresponding to Mπ < 440 MeV) and the strange quark mass varied in the range 0.7 < ms/ms,phys < 1.3.

## Key numbers

- The non-strange quark mass was varied up to m̂/m̂phys ≤ 9, corresponding to Mπ < 440 MeV, keeping ms fixed at its physical value
- The strange quark mass was varied in the range 0.7 < ms/ms,phys < 1.3, corresponding to 400 MeV < MK < 585 MeV, keeping m̂ fixed
- The naive relations m̂/m̂phys ≃ Mπ²/Mπ,phys² and ms/ms,phys ≃ MK²/MK,phys² hold to within less than 10% error over the range of variation studied
- The chiral scale is 4πf0 ≃ 1.2 GeV
- The IAM and mIAM differ by less than 1% in the physical and resonance region

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Hanhart (2008) on the pion mass dependence of the f0(600) and ρ(770) resonances from a SU(2) formalism to a full SU(3) framework that includes the strange quark mass dependence. It enables lattice practitioners to extrapolate their results on the κ(800) resonance (which is more feasible for lattice calculations due to its nonzero isospin and strangeness, as noted by Nagata (2008)) to physical quark masses, and provides predictions for the behavior of all four light resonances under variations of both the strange and non-strange quark masses. The approach uses LECs compatible with previous determinations from Kℓ4 analyses by Amoros (2001) and dispersive πK analyses by Buettiker (2003).
