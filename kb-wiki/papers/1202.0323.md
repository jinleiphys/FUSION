# First extraction of Interference Fragmentation Functions from e+e- data

> We report on the first extraction of interference fragmentation functions from the semi-inclusive production of two hadron pairs in back-to-back jets in e+e- annihilation. A nonzero asymmetry in the correlation of azimuthal orientations of opposite π+π- pairs is related to the transverse polarization of fragmenting quarks through a significant polarized dihadron fragmentation function. Extraction of the latter requires the knowledge of its unpolarized counterpart, the probability density for a quark to fragment in a π+π- pair. Since data for the unpolarized cross section are missing, we extract the unpolarized dihadron fragmentation function from a Monte Carlo simulation of the cross section.

## Key claim

The authors report the first extraction of interference fragmentation functions (IFFs) from e+e- data, specifically from the azimuthal asymmetry in the correlation of opposite π+π- pairs measured by the Belle collaboration. However, because no experimental data exist for the unpolarized cross section, the unpolarized dihadron fragmentation function (DiFF) D1 is extracted from a Monte Carlo simulation (PYTHIA) rather than from real data, making the extraction of the polarized IFF H1^< (the interference fragmentation function) dependent on this simulated input.

## Method

The authors parametrize both the unpolarized DiFF D1^q and the polarized DiFF H1,sp^<q at a low hadronic scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2, using a form inspired by previous model calculations that includes contributions from continuum production and from ρ, ω, and K0S resonances. They evolve these DiFFs at leading order to the Belle scale Q^2 = 100 GeV^2 using the HOPPET code extended to include chiral-odd splitting functions. The unpolarized D1^q is fitted to the single-pion-pair distribution from a PYTHIA Monte Carlo simulation of e+e- annihilation at Belle kinematics, and the polarized H1,sp^<q is then extracted by fitting the Belle data on the Artru-Collins azimuthal asymmetry A, which depends on the ratio of polarized to unpolarized DiFFs.

## Key numbers

- The Monte Carlo simulation uses an integrated luminosity L_MC = 647.26 pb^-1, corresponding to 2.194 × 10^6 events and a total of 1.040 × 10^6 produced π+π- pairs.
- The invariant mass range for the π+π- pairs is 0.29 ≤ M_h ≤ 1.29 GeV, with each pair required to have fractional energy z ≥ 0.2.
- The condition γ_h ≡ 2M_h/(zQ) ≤ 1/2 is imposed to avoid large mass corrections, with Q^2 = 100 GeV^2 at the Belle scale.
- The global χ^2/dof for the fit of D1 to the Monte Carlo output is 1.62, with individual channel values: continuum 1.69, ρ 1.28, ω 1.68, and K0S 1.85.
- The ρ resonance parameters are fixed at m_ρ = 0.776 GeV and Γ_ρ = 0.150 GeV.
- The best-fit parameters for D1,ρ^u include N1^ρ = 0.209 ± 0.011, α1^ρ = 0.104 ± 0.025, α2^ρ = -1.2095 ± 0.0078, β1^ρ = 0.999 ± 0.013, and η1^ρ = 1.103 ± 0.057.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) developed by Bianconi et al. (1999), Bacchetta et al. (2002, 2006, 2008), and Boer et al. (2003), and provides the necessary nonperturbative input for extracting the transversity parton distribution h1^q from combined analyses of SIDIS and e+e- data, as previously attempted by Bacchetta et al. (2011). The extraction enables future global fits using real unpolarized cross section data when they become available, and it complements the Collins-effect-based transversity extraction by Anselmino et al. (2008).
