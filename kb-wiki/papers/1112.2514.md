# Hydrodynamics of a quark droplet

> We present a simple model of a multi-quark droplet evolution based on the hydrodynamical description. This model includes collective expansion of the droplet, effects of the vacuum pressure and surface tension. The hadron emission from the droplet is described following Weisskopf's statistical model. We have considered evolution of baryon-free droplets which have different initial temperatures and expansion rates. As a typical trend we observe an oscillating behavior of the droplet radius superimposed with a gradual shrinkage due to the hadron emission. The characteristic life time of droplets with radii 1.5-2 fm are about 9-16 fm/c.

## Key claim

The paper presents a simple hydro-kinetic model of a quark droplet evolution that includes collective expansion, vacuum pressure, and surface tension, but the authors explicitly note that the model assumes baryon-free droplets and uses a temperature-independent surface tension coefficient of 50 MeV/fm², which is chosen from a wide range of possible values (5-100 MeV/fm²) reported in other studies.

## Method

The authors derive averaged hydrodynamic equations for a spherically-symmetric quark-antiquark droplet using the MIT bag model equation of state with massless quarks, assuming a Hubble-like velocity profile. They solve the coupled equations for energy and entropy conservation, including a surface energy term with relativistic correction, and incorporate hadron emission from the droplet surface using Weisskopf's statistical model with emission rates calculated for mesons (π, K, ρ, ω), baryons (N, Δ), and hyperons (Λ, Σ). Numerical simulations using a Runge-Kutta scheme track the time evolution of droplet radius, surface velocity, and temperature for four different initial conditions.

## Key numbers

- Droplet lifetime: 9-16 fm/c for initial radii of 1.5-2 fm
- Bag constant: B = 200 MeV/fm³ (slightly lower than standard 235 MeV/fm³)
- Surface tension coefficient: σ = 50 MeV/fm² (chosen from literature range 5-100 MeV/fm²)
- Equilibrium radius of 2 fm requires initial temperature T₀ = 153.4 MeV with minimal droplet energy of 34.4 GeV
- Small-amplitude oscillation period: τ = 6.11 fm/c (analytical), matching numerical result of 6.19 fm/c for lowest energy case
- Droplet shrinkage rate: approximately 0.075 c (speed of light)
- Initial emission rates: approximately 3 pions, 1 kaon, and 1 heavier hadron per fm/c, carrying away 1 GeV, 0.8 GeV, and 0.8 GeV of energy per fm/c respectively
- Total emitted particles from droplet with 30 GeV initial thermal energy: about 30 pions, 10 kaons, and 7 heavier particles
- Maximum expansion radii: 2.32 fm (high initial temperature case) and 2.16 fm (non-zero initial speed case)
- Temperature range during evolution: approximately 140-175 MeV

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic models of quark droplets by Bir83 and Bar90 by including surface tension effects and using the entropy balance equation for temperature determination, rather than assuming constant temperature. The model is motivated by the scenario where rapidly-expanding quark-gluon plasma in relativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC splits into droplets, as studied in earlier work by Mis99b and Mis09. The calculated droplet lifetimes of 9-16 fm/c are comparable to hadronization times extracted from HBT measurements (about 10 fm/c at LHC and 7 fm/c at RHIC, as reported by Aam11), and the results enable future studies of the fragmentation and hadronization dynamics of quark-gluon plasma droplets.
