# The QGP Discovered at RHIC

> Three empirical lines of evidence from RHIC have converged and point to the discovery of a strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma. The evidence includes (1) bulk collective elliptic flow and (2) jet quenching and mono-jet production, observed in Au+Au collisions at 200 AGeV, and (3) a critical control experiment using D+Au at 200 AGeV.

## Key claim
The paper claims that three independent lines of evidence from RHIC (bulk elliptic flow, jet quenching, and the D+Au control experiment) are necessary and sufficient to establish the discovery of a strongly coupled Quark Gluon Plasma (QGP), but explicitly hedges that this discovery does not mean its physical properties are understood, only that a focused research direction has begun.

## Method
The author synthesizes published experimental data from the four RHIC experiments (STAR, PHENIX, PHOBOS, BRAHMS) and compares them to theoretical predictions from lattice QCD, perturbative QCD (pQCD) radiative energy loss models (GLV opacity formalism), and ideal hydrodynamic calculations. The analysis defines a symbolic equation, QGP = P_QCD + pQCD + dA, where P_QCD represents bulk thermodynamic evidence from collective flow, pQCD represents short-wavelength tomographic evidence from jet quenching, and dA represents the critical control experiment using deuteron-gold (D+Au) collisions to isolate initial-state effects from final-state QGP effects.

## Key numbers
- The transition temperature for the QGP is approximately T_c ~ 160 MeV, with a narrow transition width of ΔT_c/T_c ~ 0.1.
- The speed of sound squared, c_s^2, drops below 1/3 for temperatures T < 2T_c ≈ 300 MeV.
- The initial gluon rapidity density required to account for jet quenching is dN_g/dy ~ 1000 ± 200.
- The maximal initial energy density in central Au+Au collisions is estimated to be ε_0 ≈ 20 GeV/fm^3, which is about 100 times the nuclear energy density ε_A.
- The formation time of the initial out-of-equilibrium QGP is estimated as ℏ/p_0 ≈ 0.2 fm/c, with a local equilibrium time τ_eq < 0.6 fm/c.
- The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/σ) for the QGP is bounded by the uncertainty principle to be greater than 1/12, which is within 5% of the string theory bound of 1/4π.

## Context
This paper extends the long search for the QGP that began with theoretical predictions by Collins and Perry (1974) and experimental strategies proposed by T.D. Lee and by Greiner and collaborators (Hofmann, Stocker). It directly contrasts the near-perfect fluid behavior observed at RHIC with the dissipative, non-equilibrium collective flow seen at lower energies at the SPS (CERES data) and BEVALAC, where nuclear transport theory (BUU) by Danielewicz (2002) is required. The work enables the use of jet quenching as a tomographic tool to probe parton densities, and it motivates further theoretical exploration of the minimal viscosity bound, linking to string theory duality conjectures by Policastro (2002).
