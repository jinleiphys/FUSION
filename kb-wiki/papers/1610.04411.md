# Exotic Hadron Bound State Production at Hadronic Colliders

> The non-relativistic wave function framework is applied to study the production and decay of the exotic hadrons which can be effectively described as bound states of other hadrons. Employing the factorized formulation, we investigate the production of exotic hadrons in the multiproduction processes at high energy hadronic colliders with the help of event generators. This study provides crucial information for the measurements of the relevant exotic hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the production of exotic hadrons which can be described as bound states of other hadrons (hadronic molecules) can be studied using a non-relativistic wave function framework combined with event generators, but the validity of this factorized approach cannot be proven a priori and must be checked for each particle across different processes and energies.

## Method

The authors apply the non-relativistic wave function method to describe hadronic molecules as bound states of two ingredient hadrons, expanding the production amplitude around zero relative momentum between the ingredients. They use event generators (such as PYTHIA) to obtain the production cross section of the free ingredient hadron pairs, then extrapolate to the vanishing relative momentum case to obtain the bound state production cross section, which factorizes into the square of the wave function at the origin and the free pair production cross section.

## Key numbers

- Production ratio ρ of X(5568) yield to B_s^0 meson yield measured by D0: (9.1 ± 2.6 ± 1.6)% for 10 < p_T(B_s^0) < 15 GeV/c and (8.2 ± 2.7 ± 1.6)% for 15 < p_T(B_s^0) < 30 GeV/c, with average (8.6 ± 1.9 ± 1.4)%
- The analysis is performed for pp collisions at √s = 8 TeV at LHC
- The D0 measurement was at collision energy 1.96 TeV within phase space region 10 < p_T < 30 GeV/c and |η| < 3
- A small energy resolution of δE = 10 MeV is used for one extrapolation example

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic wave function framework previously applied to near-threshold enhancements in J/Ψ decays (such as X(ppbar) and X(pΛbar) production) to direct multiproduction at high energy hadronic colliders. It builds on prior studies of X(3872) production by Bignamini (2009), Artoisenet (2009), and others, and addresses the challenge that effective Lagrangians cannot be constructed for complex high-energy pp processes. The framework enables predictions for production rates and distributions of various hadronic molecules (listed in Table I including f0(980), a0(980), X(3872), Zc(3900), Pc(4380), X(5568), and Xc) at different collider energies, which can guide experimental searches and trigger settings at LHC detectors.
