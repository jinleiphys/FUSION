# Efimov effect in a $D$-dimensional Born-Oppenheimer approach

> We study a three-body system, formed by two identical heavy bosons and a light particle, in the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for an arbitrary dimension $D$. We restrict $D$ to the interval $2\,<\,D\,<\,4$, and derive the heavy-heavy $D$-dimensional effective potential proportional to $1/R^2$ ($R$ is the relative distance between the heavy particles), which is responsible for the Efimov effect. We found that the Efimov states disappear once the critical strength of the heavy-heavy effective potential $1/R^2$ approaches the limit $-(D-2)^2/4$. We obtained the scaling function for the $^{133}$Cs-$^{133}$Cs-$^6$Li system as the limit cycle of the correlation between the energies of two consecutive Efimov states as a function of $D$ and the heavy-light binding energy $E^{D}_2$. In addition, we found that the energy of the $(N+1)^{\rm th}$ excited state reaches the two-body continuum independently of the dimension $D$ when $\sqrt{E^{D}_2/E_3^{(N)}}=0.89$, where $E_3^{(N)}$ is the $N^{\rm th}$ excited three-body binding energy.

# Efimov effect in a D-dimensional Born-Oppenheimer approach

## Key claim

The Efimov effect exists for a heavy-heavy-light three-body system in spatial dimensions between 2 and 4, but the critical point where an excited three-body state reaches the two-body continuum occurs at a fixed ratio of binding energies that is independent of the dimension D, specifically when sqrt(E2^D / E3^(N)) = 0.89 for the 133Cs-133Cs-6Li system.

## Method

The authors employed the Born-Oppenheimer approximation to separate the three-body Schrodinger equation into a light-particle equation and a heavy-heavy equation in D spatial dimensions. They used a zero-range contact interaction for the heavy-light potential, eliminated the coupling strength in favor of the two-body binding energy, and solved the resulting transcendental equation analytically to obtain the effective heavy-heavy potential. The three-body energy spectrum was then computed by numerically solving the regularized D-dimensional radial Schrodinger equation for the heavy-heavy system.

## Key numbers

- For D=3, the effective potential strength at short distances is g(3) = 0.3216, reproducing prior results from Fonseca et al.
- The dimensional interval for the Efimov effect in the 133Cs-133Cs-6Li system (mass ratio mA = 6/133) is 2.21 < D < 3.90
- The critical ratio sqrt(E2^D / E3^(N)) = 0.89 marks where the (N+1)th excited state reaches the two-body continuum, independent of dimension D
- The Born-Oppenheimer results for the boundaries of Efimov existence differ from the exact calculation by less than 2%

## Context

This work extends the pioneering study by Fonseca, Redish and Shanley (1981) on the Born-Oppenheimer approximation for mass-asymmetric three-body systems from three spatial dimensions to arbitrary dimensions between 2 and 4. It also generalizes the authors' previous work (Rosa et al., 2017) on the Efimov effect in AAB systems by including finite two-body binding energies and calculating the full three-body spectrum. The results enable connections to experiments with ultracold atomic clouds in confining potentials that effectively change the spatial dimension, and provide a benchmark for future studies of dimensionality reduction in mass-asymmetric few-body systems beyond three bodies.
