# Investigating the effect of nuclear deformation on $β$-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei

> We examine the effect of nuclear deformation on the calculated $β$-decay half-lives of 55 neutron-rich nuclei. The deformation values were computed using DD-PC1 and DD-ME2 interactions in the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model. Yet another set of deformation was adopted from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM). The model-dependent deformation values were used as a free parameter in the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation model to calculate the $β$-decay properties under terrestrial and stellar conditions. The Gamow-Teller strength distributions, branching ratios, half-lives and stellar weak rates of selected nuclei were later investigated. It was concluded that the $β$-decay properties of neutron-rich nuclei changed with nuclear deformation. The FRDM computed deformation values provided the best predictions for calculated half-lives followed by the DD-ME2 functional. The terrestrial $β$-decay half-lives changed up to 3 orders of magnitude and the stellar $β$-rates within a factor of 2 as the neutron-rich nuclei switched their geometrical configurations. For magic number nucleus $^{138}$Sn, the stellar rates changed substantially by more than 1 order of magnitude as the nucleus transitioned away from the spherical shape. Our investigation might prove useful for a realistic modeling of nucleosynthesis calculation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear deformation significantly affects calculated β-decay half-lives of neutron-rich nuclei, with terrestrial half-lives changing by up to 3 orders of magnitude and stellar rates changing within a factor of 2 as nuclei switch geometrical configurations; however, these results are model-dependent, with the FRDM deformation values providing the best predictions compared to the DD-ME2 and DD-PC1 functionals from relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory.

## Method

The authors computed ground-state deformation parameters (β₂) for 55 neutron-rich nuclei (mass range 134 ≤ A ≤ 155) using the Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) model with two density-dependent interactions (DD-PC1 and DD-ME2), and adopted a third set from the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM). These three sets of deformation values were then used as input parameters in the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model to calculate Gamow-Teller strength distributions, branching ratios, terrestrial β-decay half-lives, and stellar β-decay rates at selected temperatures (3, 10, 30 GK) and densities (10³, 10⁷, 10¹¹ g/cm³).

## Key numbers

- Terrestrial β-decay half-lives changed by up to 3 orders of magnitude as neutron-rich nuclei switched their geometrical configurations
- Stellar β-rates changed within a factor of 2 as nuclei switched geometrical configurations
- For the magic number nucleus ¹³⁸Sn, stellar rates changed substantially by more than 1 order of magnitude as the nucleus transitioned away from spherical shape
- For ¹⁴³Xe, half-lives changed by more than 400%: calculated as 148.44 ms using DD-PC1 deformation versus 750.99 ms using FRDM deformation (measured half-life is 519 ms)
- ¹³⁸Sb half-life calculated as 2207.66 ms and ¹³⁹Sb as 119.19 ms using FRDM deformation
- Maximum Δβ₂ (difference between maximum and minimum computed absolute β₂ values) was 0.13
- Ground-state β₂ values for ¹³⁷-¹⁴⁴Te ranged from 0.115 to 0.195, for ¹⁴²-¹⁴⁸Xe from 0.181 to 0.233, and for ¹⁴⁸-¹⁵³Ba from 0.217 to 0.304

## Context

This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of β-decay properties for neutron-rich nuclei, including recent studies of Cerium isotopes (Nabi, 2022) and Rb-Sr-Y-Zr isotopes (Sheikh, 2022), as well as experimental measurements at RIKEN's RIBF facility (Wu, 2020) for nuclei in the same mass region. The investigation is motivated by the need for accurate theoretical predictions of β-decay half-lives for r-process nucleosynthesis modeling, particularly for neutron-rich nuclei near the neutron-drip line where experimental data are limited. The results suggest that incorporating deformation effects from FRDM or DD-ME2 functionals into pn-QRPA calculations could improve the realism of nucleosynthesis simulations, especially for nuclei where shape coexistence or transitions between spherical and deformed configurations occur.
