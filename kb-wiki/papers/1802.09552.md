# Pion and kaon valence-quark quasiparton distributions

> Algebraic Ansaetze for the Poincaré-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave functions of the pion and kaon are used to calculate their light-front wave functions (LFWFs), parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs), quasi-PDAs (qPDAs), valence parton distribution functions (PDFs), and quasi-PDFs (qPDFs). The LFWFs are broad, concave functions; and the scale of flavour-symmetry violation in the kaon is roughly 15%, being set by the ratio of emergent masses in the $s$-and $u$-quark sectors. qPDAs computed with longitudinal momentum $P_z =1.75\,$GeV provide a semiquantitatively accurate representation of the objective PDA; but even with $P_z=3\,$GeV, they cannot provide information about this amplitude's endpoint behaviour. On the valence-quark domain, similar outcomes characterise qPDFs. In this connection, however, the ratio of kaon-to-pion $u$-quark qPDFs is found to provide a good approximation to the true PDF ratio on $0.3\lesssim x \lesssim 0.8$, suggesting that with existing resources computations of ratios of quasi-parton-distributions can yield results that support empirical comparison.

## Key claim

Using algebraic Ansätze for Poincaré-covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave functions, the authors find that quasi-PDFs computed with longitudinal momentum up to 3 GeV cannot reproduce the endpoint behavior of true PDFs, but the ratio of kaon-to-pion u-quark quasi-PDFs provides a good approximation to the true PDF ratio on the valence-quark domain 0.3 ≲ x ≲ 0.8, suggesting that ratios of quasi-parton-distributions can support empirical comparison with existing computational resources.

## Method

The authors employ algebraic Ansätze for dressed-quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes of the pion and kaon, with parameters fitted to reproduce experimental leptonic decay constants and Mellin moments from modern continuum analyses. They compute light-front wave functions, parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs), quasi-PDAs (qPDAs), valence parton distribution functions (PDFs), and quasi-PDFs (qPDFs) using the same framework at an hadronic scale ζ_H ∼ 1 GeV, without perturbative QCD evolution. The quasi-distributions are obtained by replacing the light-like vector n with the spatial vector ñ = (0,0,1,0) in the standard PDF and PDA formulae, with an additional correction term S(x) required for the qPDFs to ensure momentum conservation.

## Key numbers

- Flavour-symmetry violation in the kaon is roughly 15%, set by the ratio of emergent masses M_s/M_u = 1.2, with M_u = 0.31 GeV
- For the pion qPDA at P_z = 1.75 GeV, the L₁-difference from the true PDA is 18% and the (2x-1)²-moment is 78% of the objective value; at P_z = 3.0 GeV, the L₁-difference is 10% and the moment is 85% of the objective value
- For the kaon qPDA at P_z = 1.75 GeV, the L₁-difference from the true PDA is 28% and the (2x-1)¹-moment is 75% of the objective value; at P_z = 3.0 GeV, the L₁-difference is 20% and the (2x-1)¹-moment is 82% of the objective value
- Reducing the L₁-difference between the kaon qPDA and true PDA to 10% would require P_z ≈ 20 GeV
- For the pion valence-quark qPDF, L₁-differences from the true PDF are: 19% at P_z = 1 GeV, 9% at P_z = 1.75 GeV, 5% at P_z = 2.4 GeV, and 4% at P_z = 3 GeV
- The pion's (2x-1)²-moment objective value is 0.25; the qPDA at P_z = 1 GeV gives only 33% of this value
- The kaon qPDA peaks at x = 0.44 (true PDA peak at x = 0.44), while the pion PDA is symmetric
- The pion and kaon LFWFs fall as 1/k⊥⁴ for k⊥² ≫ Λ_G²

## Context

This work extends continuum bound-state calculations of meson parton distributions using algebraic Ansätze, building on prior analyses by Chang (2013), Segovia (2013), Shi (2014, 2015), and Chen (2016) for pion and kaon PDAs and PDFs. It complements lattice QCD studies of quasi-distributions by Zhang (2017) and Chen (2017), and spectator model analyses by Gamberg (2015) and Bacchetta (2016) for the proton. The results enable assessment of how large P_z must be in lattice simulations to extract reliable information about true parton distributions, and suggest that ratios of quasi-distributions may provide useful empirical comparisons even at moderate P_z values accessible to current lattice resources (approximately 48 spatial sites with 0.06 fm spacing for P_z ≈ 3 GeV).
