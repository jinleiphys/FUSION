# Holographic Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy and the quantum Lyapunov spectrum

> In classical chaotic systems the entropy, averaged over initial phase space distributions, follows an universal behavior. While approaching thermal equilibrium it passes through a stage where it grows linearly, while the growth rate, the Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy, is given by the sum over all positive Lyapunov exponents. A natural question is whether a similar relation is valid for quantum systems. We argue that the Maldacena-Shenker-Stanford bound on quantum Lyapunov exponents $λ$ implies that the upper bound on the growth rate of the entropy, averaged over states in Hilbert space that evolve towards a thermal state with temperature $T$ and entropy $S_{eq}$, should be given by $S_{eq} πT =\sum_{λ>0}2 πT$. Strongly coupled, large $N$ theories with black hole duals should saturate the bound. By studying a large number of isotropization processes of random, spatially homogeneous, far from equilibrium initial states in large $N$, $\mathcal{N}=4$ Super Yang Mills theory at strong coupling and computing the ensemble averaged growth rate of the dual black hole's apparent horizon area, we find both an analogous behavior as in classical chaotic systems and numerical evidence that the conjectured bound on averaged entropy growth is saturated granted that the Lyapunov exponents are degenerate $λ= \pm 2 πT$. This fits to the behavior of classical systems with plus/minus symmetric Lyapunov spectra, a symmetry which implies the validity of Liouville's theorem.

## Key claim

The authors argue that the Maldacena-Shenker-Stanford bound on quantum Lyapunov exponents implies an upper bound on the growth rate of entropy averaged over states in Hilbert space, and provide numerical evidence that strongly coupled, large N theories with black hole duals saturate this bound, but only if the Lyapunov spectrum retains the plus/minus symmetry of classical chaotic systems with degenerate exponents λ = ±2πT.

## Method

The authors study isotropization processes of random, spatially homogeneous, far-from-equilibrium initial states in large N, N=4 Super Yang Mills theory at strong coupling using holography, solving the five-dimensional Einstein equations on null slices via spectral methods and fourth-order Runge Kutta. They compute the ensemble-averaged growth rate of the dual black hole's apparent horizon area across five different ensembles of initial anisotropy functions, ranging in size from several hundred thousand to 3 million simulations, and compare this growth rate to the sum over positive Lyapunov exponents expected from the number of degrees of freedom of the equilibrated black hole.

## Key numbers

- The number of degrees of freedom per unit volume is N_DOF/V = N^2 μ^3/(2π), where μ = πT.
- The Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy density is given by s_KS = c (N^2/2) π^3 T^4, where c = 1 if the Lyapunov spectrum is plus/minus symmetric with λ = ±2πT, and c = 2 if all Lyapunov exponents are positive and maximal.
- The equilibrium entropy density of the thermal state is S_eq, with the bound on entropy growth rate given by S_eq πT = Σ_{λ>0} 2πT.
- For ensemble (IV), the maximal deviation of the ensemble-averaged pressure components from the equilibrium value N^2 π^2 T^4/8 was 1.6%.

## Context

This work extends the classical relation between Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy and the sum of positive Lyapunov exponents, established for classical chaotic systems by Bar (year not specified), to quantum systems via holography. It builds on the Maldacena-Shenker-Stanford bound (2015) on quantum Lyapunov exponents and the observation by Han (year not specified) that black holes might be the fastest entropy generators. The numerical approach follows the holographic isotropization framework developed by Chesler and Yaffe (2009, 2010, 2014), and the results suggest that the quantum Lyapunov spectrum of large N SYM retains the plus/minus symmetry observed in classical Yang-Mills theory, as studied by Biro (1993) and Kunihiro (2008).
