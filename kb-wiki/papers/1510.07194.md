# Event-by-Event Study of Space-Time Dynamics in Flux-Tube Fragmentation

> In the semi-classical description of the flux-tube fragmentation process for hadron production and hadronization in high-energy $e^+e^-$ annihilations and $pp$ collisions, the rapidity-space-time ordering and the local conservation laws of charge, flavor, and momentum provide a set of powerful tools that may allow the reconstruction of the space-time dynamics of quarks and mesons in exclusive measurements of produced hadrons, on an event-by-event basis. We propose procedures to reconstruct the space-time dynamics from event-by-event exclusive hadron data to exhibit explicitly the ordered chain of hadrons produced in a flux tube fragmentation. As a supplementary tool, we infer the average space-time coordinates of the $q$-$\bar q$ pair production vertices from the $π^-$ rapidity distribution data obtained by the NA61/SHINE Collaboration in $pp$ collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 6.3 to 17.3 GeV.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the semi-classical description of flux-tube fragmentation, with its rapidity-space-time ordering and local conservation laws, allows reconstruction of the space-time dynamics of quarks and mesons from exclusive hadron measurements on an event-by-event basis, but the authors acknowledge that on an event-by-event basis there will be high degrees of fluctuations and uncertainties in the dynamical variables.

## Method

The authors derive differential equations relating the forward and backward light-cone coordinates (u,v) of q-anti-q pair-production vertices to the hadron rapidity distribution dN/dy, then solve these equations analytically for boost-invariant, plateau, and Gaussian rapidity distributions. They fit the NA61/SHINE pi-minus rapidity distribution data from pp collisions with single-Gaussian and double-Gaussian distributions, then use the semi-classical flux-tube fragmentation model to infer the average space-time coordinates of the pair-production vertices from the fitted parameters.

## Key numbers

* For pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 6.25 GeV (p_lab = 20 GeV/c): average number of pi-minus per collision = 1.04 +/- 0.05, Gaussian width sigma = 0.98 +/- 0.030
* For pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 17.27 GeV (p_lab = 158 GeV/c): average number of pi-minus per collision = 2.45 +/- 0.13, Gaussian width sigma = 1.43 +/- 0.065
* The transverse spectra of low-p_T pions are represented by dN/m_T dm_T proportional to exp(-m_T/T) with T ranging from 150 to 160 MeV
* String tension assumed: kappa = 1 GeV/fm
* For a boost-invariant distribution, the proper production time tau_0 = (m_T/kappa) * (dN/dy)_0

## Context

This work extends the semi-classical flux-tube fragmentation description developed by Artru (1974), Andersson (1979, 1983), and implemented in the Lund model via PYTHIA (Sjostrand, 2006). It provides a method to reconstruct space-time dynamics from exclusive hadron data, which could discriminate between different models of particle production in pp collisions, including the Lund model's two-colorless-flux-tube picture, the color-glass condensate/glasma flux tube model (McLerran, 1994; Kovchegov, 1995), and Landau hydrodynamics (Landau, 1953). The approach enables future event-by-event studies to identify and enumerate fragmenting flux tubes and test the many-body correlations predicted by the semi-classical description.
