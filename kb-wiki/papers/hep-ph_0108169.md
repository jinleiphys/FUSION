# Lepton pair production in a charged quark gluon plasma

> We investigate the effects of a charge asymmetry on the spectrum of dileptons radiating from a quark gluon plasma. We demonstrate the existence of a new set of processes in this regime. The dilepton production rate from the corresponding diagrams is shown to be as important as that obtained from the Born-term quark-antiquark annihilation.

## Key claim

The authors show that a finite charge density (nonzero chemical potential) in a quark-gluon plasma breaks Furry's theorem, enabling a new dilepton production channel, gluon-gluon fusion into electron-positron pairs, whose differential rate can be comparable to or larger than the standard Born-term quark-antiquark annihilation rate for intermediate invariant masses, though the effect is suppressed at asymptotically high temperatures.

## Method

The authors calculate the imaginary part of the three-loop photon self-energy at finite temperature and chemical potential, focusing on the cut corresponding to the process \(gg \rightarrow e^{+}e^{-}\). They evaluate the differential production rate for back-to-back dileptons (photon three-momentum \(\vec{p}=0\)) using the discontinuity of the self-energy, and compare it to the Born-term \(q\bar{q}\rightarrow e^{+}e^{-}\) rate for three massless quark flavours. The calculation is performed for two temperatures (400 MeV and 600 MeV) and two values of the quark chemical potential (\(\mu = 0.1T\) and \(\mu = 0.5T\)), using a running strong coupling \(\alpha_s(T)\). A Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) analysis is also carried out to examine the high-temperature limit.

## Key numbers

- Differential dilepton production rate from \(gg \rightarrow e^{+}e^{-}\) is calculated for invariant masses from 0.5 to 2.5 GeV at \(T = 400\) MeV and \(T = 600\) MeV.
- Two chemical potential values are used: \(\mu = 0.1T\) and \(\mu = 0.5T\).
- The strong coupling is given by \(\alpha_s(T) = 6\pi / [27 \ln(T / 50\ \text{MeV})]\).
- Initial plasma temperatures at RHIC and LHC are cited as predicted in the range 300–800 MeV.
- The HTL analysis shows the \(gg\gamma\) vertex has no term proportional to \((gT)^2\); the leading term is of order \(g^2 T\), whereas the \(q\bar{q}\gamma\) HTL vertex is of order \((gT)^2\).

## Context

This work extends the study of dilepton production from a quark-gluon plasma beyond the standard Born-term annihilation (Gale and Kapusta, 1991) by incorporating the effects of a finite charge density. It demonstrates the breakdown of Furry's theorem in a medium with nonzero chemical potential, a symmetry-breaking effect noted earlier by Chiu and Welton (1992). The new channel \(gg \rightarrow e^{+}e^{-}\) may be particularly relevant for the early, gluon-dominated stage of ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC, where gluon numbers are predicted to exceed chemical equilibrium values (Wang, 1996; Ruppert and others, 2000).
