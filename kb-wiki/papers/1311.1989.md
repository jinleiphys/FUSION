# Ab-initio self-consistent Gorkov-Green's function calculations of semi-magic nuclei - II. Numerical implementation at second order with a two-nucleon interaction

> The newly developed Gorkov-Green's function approach represents a promising path to the ab initio description of medium-mass open-shell nuclei. We discuss the implementation of the method at second order with a two-body interaction, with particular attention to the numerical solution of Gorkov's equation. Different sources of theoretical error and degrees of self-consistency are investigated. We show that Krylov projection techniques with a multi-pivot Lanczos algorithm efficiently handle the growth of poles in the one-body Green's function when Gorkov's equation is solved self-consistently. The end result is a tractable, accurate and gently scaling ab initio scheme applicable to full isotopic chains in the medium-mass region.

## Key claim

The Gorkov-Green's function method, implemented at second order with a two-nucleon interaction, provides a tractable and accurate ab initio scheme for medium-mass open-shell nuclei, provided that Krylov projection techniques are used to manage the rapid growth of poles in the one-body Green's function during self-consistent iterations; the partially self-consistent "sc0" scheme reproduces fully self-consistent solutions at the 1% level in small model spaces.

## Method

The authors solve Gorkov's equation in its energy-independent matrix form, where the self-energy is expanded to second order in the two-nucleon interaction, generating three-quasiparticle (3QP) configurations as intermediate states. To handle the explosive growth of the matrix dimension at each self-consistent iteration (scaling as N_b^{3^n}), they employ a multi-pivot Lanczos algorithm that projects the energy denominators onto a Krylov subspace of controlled size. The method is benchmarked against exact diagonalization in small model spaces, and convergence is studied as a function of the number of Lanczos iterations per pivot (N_ℓ) and the harmonic oscillator model-space size.

## Key numbers

- For typical large-scale calculations of mid-mass nuclei, a Krylov projection using N_ℓ ≈ 50 yields a sufficient degree of accuracy on the observables of interest.
- The partially self-consistent sc0 scheme reproduces fully self-consistent solutions in small model spaces at the 1% level.
- The dimension of the Gorkov matrix at first iteration scales as N_tot ≈ N_b^3/3, where N_b is the number of single-particle basis states.
- The number of three-quasiparticle poles at the first iteration is N_s ≈ N_b^3/6, growing to N_s ≈ N_b^9/216/6 at the second iteration.

## Context

This work extends the Gorkov-Green's function formalism introduced by Soma, Duguet, and Barbieri (2011) for ab initio description of open-shell nuclei, building on earlier Dyson SCGF calculations that were limited to doubly closed-shell nuclei (Barbieri, 2009; Cipollone, 2013). The Krylov projection technique follows the approach of Schirmer (1989) and earlier Lanczos-based methods used in nuclear structure (Van Neck, 1991; Muether, 1993), but generalizes them to handle off-diagonal self-energy components via multiple pivots. The results enable systematic calculations of complete isotopic chains in the medium-mass region and open the possibility to apply the method to heavier systems than ⁷⁴Ni, as well as to perform converged calculations with harder chiral interactions.
