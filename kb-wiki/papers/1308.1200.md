# Isoscalar giant monopole resonance in Sn isotopes using a quantum molecular dynamics model

> The isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in Sn isotopes and other nuclei is investigated in the framework of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model. The spectrum of GMR is calculated by taking the root-mean-square (RMS) radius of a nucleus as its monopole moment. The peak energy, the full width at half maximum (FWHM), and the strength of GMR extracted by a Gaussian fit to the spectrum have been studied. The GMR peak energies for Sn isotopes from the calculations using a mass-number dependent Gaussian wave-packet width $σ_r$ for nucleons are found to be overestimated and show a weak dependence on the mass number compared with the experimental data. However, it is found that experimental data of the GMR peak energies for $^{56}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, and $^{208}$Pb as well as Sn isotopes can be nicely reproduced after taking into account the isospin dependence in isotope chains in addition to the mass number dependence of $σ_r$ for nucleons in the IQMD model calculation.

# Isoscalar giant monopole resonance in Sn isotopes using a quantum molecular dynamics model

## Key claim
The isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model can reproduce experimental ISGMR peak energies for Sn isotopes, as well as for ⁵⁶Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb, only after introducing a Gaussian wave-packet width σᵣ that depends on both mass number and isospin asymmetry, rather than on mass number alone. Without this isospin dependence, the model overestimates peak energies by about 1 MeV and shows a weaker mass-number dependence than experimental data.

## Method
The authors used the IQMD model to simulate the collective oscillation of nuclei whose initial density distribution is not the ground state for the nuclear interaction used, taking the root-mean-square (RMS) radius as the monopole moment. The GMR spectrum was obtained by Fourier transformation of the second time derivative of the RMS radius, and the peak energy, full width at half maximum (FWHM), and strength were extracted via Gaussian fitting. The calculation used ²⁰⁸Pb as a target and the concerned nucleus as projectile, with the Coulomb interaction modifying the oscillation.

## Key numbers
- GMR peak energies for Sn isotopes calculated with mass-number-dependent σᵣ are overestimated by about 0.3–1 MeV compared to experimental data from Li et al. (2007).
- The new isospin-dependent σᵣ function is: σᵣ = −3A⁻¹/³ + 2.5[(N−Z)/A]² + 1.6×10⁻⁸ Z²A⁻⁴/³ + 2.01 fm.
- The soft equation of state with momentum-dependent interaction (MDI) gave the best agreement with experimental data for Sn isotopes.
- For ⁴⁰Ca, the model still overestimates the GMR peak energy compared to experimental data, attributed to shell structure and pairing correlations beyond the IQMD framework.

## Context
This work extends previous IQMD studies of giant dipole and pygmy dipole resonances in Ni isotopes (Tao et al., 2013) to the isoscalar giant monopole resonance. It addresses the "why is Tin so soft?" puzzle raised by Piekarewicz (2007), where models that reproduce GMR peak energies in ⁹⁰Zr, ¹⁴⁴Sm, and ²⁰⁸Pb overestimate those in Sn isotopes. The new isospin-dependent σᵣ function enables the IQMD model to simultaneously reproduce experimental data for Sn isotopes and for ⁵⁶Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb, improving upon earlier QMD treatments with constant or mass-number-dependent σᵣ. The approach may be tested on other observables in future IQMD calculations.
