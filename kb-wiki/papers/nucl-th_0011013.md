# Collective flow and multiparticle azimuthal correlations

> The measurement of azimuthal distributions in nucleus-nucleus collisions relies upon the assumption that azimuthal correlations between particles result solely from their correlation with the reaction plane (i.e. flow). We show that at SPS energies, the ansatz is no longer valid, and two-particle correlations due to momentum conservation, final state interactions or resonance decays become of the same order as those arising from flow. This leads us to introduce new methods to analyse collective flow, based on a cumulant expansion, which enable to extract smaller values than those accessible to the standard analysis.

# Collective flow and multiparticle azimuthal correlations

## Key claim
At SPS energies, the standard assumption that two-particle azimuthal correlations arise solely from collective flow is no longer valid; nonflow correlations from momentum conservation, final-state interactions, and resonance decays become comparable to flow-induced correlations. The authors introduce a cumulant expansion method that can extract smaller flow values than the standard two-particle analysis, down to limits set by higher-order particle multiplicities.

## Method
The authors derive a cumulant expansion of multiparticle azimuthal correlations to isolate collective flow from nonflow correlations. For integrated flow, they compute the fourth-order cumulant of the event flow vector Q_n, defined as ⟨⟨|Q_n|⁴⟩⟩ = ⟨|Q_n|⁴⟩ − 2⟨|Q_n|²⟩², which removes two-particle nonflow contributions and yields −⟨Q_n⟩⁴ plus a residual of order 1/M. For differential flow of a specific particle species (e.g., protons), they construct mixed cumulants such as ⟨⟨|Q_n|² Q_n^* e^{inψ}⟩⟩, which isolates the product v'_n v_n³. Acceptance corrections for non-isotropic detectors are implemented by redefining the cumulant expression to include additional moment terms.

## Key numbers
- At SPS energies with N ≈ 2500 emitted particles, the standard two-particle method is limited to v_n ≫ 2%, while the four-particle cumulant method allows measurements of v_n ≫ 0.3%.
- Published values at SPS for pions are v₁ ≈ v₂ ≈ 3% (NA49, 2000).
- The four-particle cumulant method measures integrated flow down to v_n ≫ 1/N^(3/4), compared to the standard limit v_n ≫ 1/N^(1/2).
- Direct k-particle correlations scale as O(1/N^(k−1)), with the four-particle cumulant residual of order 1/N³.

## Context
This work extends the standard flow analysis method of Poskanzer and Voloshin (1998), which relies on two-particle azimuthal correlations and the Fourier expansion of the one-particle distribution (Voloshin, 1996). The authors show that at SPS energies, nonflow correlations from momentum conservation, HBT quantum effects, and resonance decays significantly modify the NA49 values for pion and proton flow. The cumulant method enables reliable flow measurements at ultrarelativistic energies where the standard ansatz fails, and can be generalized to higher-order cumulants (e.g., six-particle correlations) for even smaller flow values.
