# Capture of alpha Particles by Isospin-Symmetric Nuclei

> The reaction rates for alpha capture processes on self-conjugate nuclei in the mass range A=20-40 have been investigated. The rates were calculated using the statistical model code NON-SMOKER taking into account isospin suppression rules. These theoretical predictions are compared with rates derived from the available experimental data about the alpha capture reactions taking also into account additional experimental information from different reaction channels populating the alpha unbound states of the self-conjugate compound nuclei.

# Capture of Alpha Particles by Isospin-Symmetric Nuclei

## Key claim
The statistical model code NON-SMOKER, when including explicit isospin suppression of E1 and M1 gamma transitions via a level-density-dependent suppression factor, predicts alpha capture rates on self-conjugate nuclei (A=20-40) that agree with empirically derived rates within a factor of about 3 for temperatures above 1 GK, but the agreement worsens at lower temperatures due to the breakdown of statistical model assumptions and deficiencies in the optical alpha-nucleus potential near the Coulomb barrier.

## Method
The authors used the Hauser-Feshbach statistical model code NON-SMOKER to calculate alpha capture cross sections and reaction rates on self-conjugate (N=Z) target nuclei from Mg to Mo (Z=12-42). Isospin suppression of E1 and M1 gamma transitions was implemented by setting the suppression factor f_iso proportional to the ratio of T=1 to T=0 level densities in the compound nucleus, replacing earlier phenomenological suppression factors of 5 or 2. The theoretical rates were compared to rates derived from experimental resonance parameters for reactions on 20Ne, 24Mg, 28Si, 32S, 36Ar, and 40Ca, where the experimental resonance set was extended using information from (p,alpha), (p,gamma), and alpha-transfer measurements on the same compound nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For 28Si(alpha,gamma)32S, the present NON-SMOKER rate is higher by a factor of 1.96 compared to previous Hauser-Feshbach calculations (Hol76, Woo78).
- For the temperature range T=1-5 GK, the ratio of theoretical to empirical rates for all reactions studied (except 32S and 36Ar) deviates by a factor of 3 or less.
- Above T=3.5 GK, the theoretical rates agree with empirical rates to within about 30%.
- For 20Ne(alpha,gamma)24Mg, the HF calculation agrees within a factor of about 2 with experimental values for T greater than or equal to 0.4 GK.
- The resonance strength for the 20Ne(alpha,gamma)24Mg resonance at E_r=0.7992 MeV (J^pi=0^+) is 2.86E-04 eV (calculated) versus 2.90E-04 eV (experimental).
- The resonance strength for the 24Mg(alpha,gamma)28Si resonance at E_r=1.311 MeV (J^pi=1^-) is 9.08E-03 eV (calculated) versus 1.10E-01 eV (experimental).

## Context
This work extends previous statistical model treatments of alpha capture on self-conjugate nuclei by replacing the phenomenological isospin suppression factors of 5 (for alpha capture) and 2 (for nucleon capture) used by Holmes (1976), Woosley (1978), Cowan (1991), and Schatz (1998) with a physically motivated, energy-dependent suppression factor based on level densities. The calculations are relevant for nucleosynthesis in Ne-burning and O-burning phases of massive stars, explosive burning in supernovae, and the production of 44Ti. The results enable more reliable reaction rate predictions for astrophysical networks, though the authors note that remaining discrepancies at low temperatures require improved optical alpha-nucleus potentials and better level density descriptions, particularly for 44Ti where the low level density limits the statistical model's applicability.
