# Effective Field Theory for Halo Nuclei: Shallow p-Wave States

> Halo nuclei are a promising new arena for studies based on effective field theory (EFT). We develop an EFT for shallow p-wave states and discuss the application to elastic n-alpha scattering. In contrast to the s-wave case, both the scattering length and effective range enter at leading order. We also discuss the prospects of using EFT in the description of other halos, such as the three-body halo nucleus 6He.

## Key claim

The paper develops an effective field theory (EFT) for shallow p-wave states in halo nuclei, showing that in contrast to the s-wave case, both the scattering length and effective range must enter at leading order for proper renormalization when a shallow p-wave resonance is present.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic EFT for two spinless fermions with p-wave interactions, introducing an auxiliary dimeron field to sum the leading-order contact interactions to all orders. They then extend this formalism to elastic neutron-alpha scattering by including nucleon spin and isospin, treating the 1+ (p3/2) partial wave nonperturbatively while treating the 0+ (s1/2) and 1- (p1/2) partial waves in perturbation theory. The low-energy constants are fixed by matching to the effective-range parameters extracted from the phase-shift analysis of Arndt, Long, and Roper (1973).

## Key numbers

- For the 1+ (p3/2) partial wave in n-alpha scattering: scattering length a = -62.951(3) fm^3, effective range r = -0.8819(11) fm^-1, shape parameter P = -3.002(62) fm
- For the 0+ (s1/2) partial wave: scattering length a = 2.4641(37) fm, effective range r = 1.385(41) fm
- For the 1- (p1/2) partial wave: scattering length a = -13.821(68) fm^3, effective range r = -0.419(16) fm^-1
- From the 1+ partial wave parameters, the low-energy scale Mlo is estimated at approximately 70 MeV and the high-energy scale Mhi at approximately 260 MeV
- The p3/2 partial wave displays a resonance at E approximately 1 MeV corresponding to a shallow virtual bound state

## Context

This work extends the pionless EFT approach previously applied to s-wave systems by van Kolck (1999) and Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998) to the case of shallow p-wave states, using n-alpha scattering as a test case relevant to the Borromean three-body halo nucleus 6He. The EFT framework enables systematic, model-independent calculations with controlled error estimates, in contrast to standard potential model calculations where errors can only be estimated by comparing different potentials. The authors note that the 1+ partial wave in n-alpha scattering has all the characteristics of a two-body halo nucleus, and the formalism developed here is intended for future application to the three-body halo 6He and the reaction p + 7Be to 8B + gamma.
