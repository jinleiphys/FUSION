# The Good, the Bad, and the Subtle: Relativistic mode sums for neutron-star tidal response

> Time-dependent tidal interactions during the late inspiral of binary neutron stars encode valuable information about neutron-star structure, but systematically extending the familiar Newtonian mode-sum picture into full general relativity is nontrivial. In this paper, we develop a practical relativistic implementation of mode-sum tidal response for non-rotating neutron stars in Regge-Wheeler gauge. Using near-zone boundary conditions, we systematically define the interior tidal field, the relativistic overlap integrals, and the corresponding mode amplitudes. The good is that the dominant f-mode contribution is remarkably robust, reproducing the direct matching calculation to within $\sim 3$\% across the equations of state we consider. The bad is that the operator governing mode inner product is not positive definite on the full Regge-Wheeler-gauge function space, so the relativistic mode sum truncated at ${\cal{O}}(ω^2)$ is not expected to strictly converge to the direct matching solution. The subtle is that the tidal field inside the star is not unique, although this ambiguity has only a limited impact on the dominant f-mode response for the classes of extensions studied here. Our results establish the practical utility of relativistic mode-sum approximations, while making clear that their predictive power comes from a controlled low-mode description, rather than from a formally convergent strong-field expansion.

## Key claim

The paper develops a practical relativistic mode-sum approximation for the tidal response of non-rotating neutron stars in Regge-Wheeler gauge, but finds that while the dominant f-mode contribution reproduces direct matching calculations to within approximately 3% across equations of state, the mode-sum truncated at O(ω²) is not expected to strictly converge because the operator governing mode inner product is not positive definite on the full function space.

## Method

The authors implement a relativistic mode-sum framework for non-rotating neutron stars in Regge-Wheeler gauge, using near-zone boundary conditions to define interior tidal fields, relativistic overlap integrals, and mode amplitudes. They solve the linearized Einstein equations inside the star using the Detweiler-Ipser formalism, reconstruct metric variables from the master function Z outside the star, and compute the dynamical tidal response function by matching interior and exterior solutions at the stellar surface. The mode-sum approximation is constructed by expanding the interior solution in eigenfunctions obtained from the homogeneous problem with near-zone boundary conditions, and the results are compared against direct numerical matching calculations.

## Key numbers

* The dominant f-mode contribution from the mode-sum approximation reproduces the direct matching calculation to within approximately 3% across the equations of state considered.
* The eigenfunctions and eigenfrequencies obtained with near-zone boundary conditions closely track the real part of the quasi-normal mode solutions for mode frequencies below the f-mode frequency.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Hegade K. R. et al. (2025) by providing concrete numerical implementation in Regge-Wheeler gauge, answering questions raised by Pitre et al. (2023) about the eigenfunction basis, definition of interior tidal field, and overlap integrals in full general relativity. The approach contrasts with phenomenological mode-sum models used in effective one-body waveform modeling (Hinderer 2016, Steinhoff 2016) and with quasi-normal mode approaches (Chakrabarti 2013, Saketh 2024), instead using matched-asymptotic expansions and post-Newtonian theory as in Poisson (2020). The results enable systematic relativistic mode-sum approximations for neutron-star tidal response while clarifying their formal limitations, particularly that predictive power comes from controlled low-mode descriptions rather than formally convergent strong-field expansions.
