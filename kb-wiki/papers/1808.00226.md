# $T_{bbb}$: a three $B-$meson bound state

> By solving exactly the Faddeev equations for the bound-state problem of three mesons, we demonstrate that current theoretical predictions pointing to the existence of a deeply-bound doubly bottom axial vector tetraquark lead to the existence of a unique bound state of three $B$ mesons. We find that the $BB^*B^*-B^*B^*B^*$ state with quantum numbers $(I)J^P=(1/2)2^-$, $T_{bbb}$, is about 90\,MeV below any possible three $B$-meson threshold for the reported binding of the doubly bottom axial vector tetraquark, $T_{bb}$.

# $T_{bbb}$: a three $B-$meson bound state

## Key claim

The existence of a deeply-bound doubly bottom axial vector tetraquark ($T_{bb}$) necessarily implies the existence of a unique three-body bound state of three $B$ mesons, $T_{bbb}$, provided the $T_{bb}$ binding energy exceeds approximately 50 MeV; below that threshold, the three-body system becomes unbound.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations exactly for the bound-state problem of three mesons, using as input the two-body $t-$matrices that generate the axial vector $bb\bar u \bar d$ tetraquark ($T_{bb}$) as a coupled-channel system of pseudoscalar-vector ($BB^*$) and vector-vector ($B^*B^*$) components. The three-body system considered is the $BB^*B^*-B^*B^*B^*$ channel with quantum numbers $(I)J^P=(1/2)2^-$, which is the only channel that contains the attractive two-body subsystems while avoiding the non-attractive $BB$ interaction.

## Key numbers

- The $T_{bbb}$ state lies about 90 MeV below the lowest three $B$-meson threshold when using the $T_{bb}$ binding energy from Fra17 (180 MeV).
- For $T_{bb}$ binding energies of 180, 144, 117, and 87 MeV, the corresponding $T_{bbb}$ binding energies are 90, 77, 57, and 43 MeV, respectively (all measured from the lowest strong decay threshold $m_B + 2 m_{B^*} - B(T_{bb})$).
- The $\Omega_{bbb}$ baryon mass is calculated as 14.84 GeV within the same scheme, placing the $\Omega_{bbb} - \bar p$ threshold at 15.78 GeV, above the $T_{bbb}$ state.
- If the $T_{bb}$ binding energy is reduced to 50 MeV, the three-body system would have only about 23 MeV binding, lying 19 MeV above the lowest $BBB$ threshold.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical consensus on the existence of a deeply-bound doubly bottom tetraquark $T_{bb}$ (predicted by Fra17, Kar17, Eic17, Bic16, Vij09, Ric18, Luo17, Duc13, Cza18, Ade82) to the three-body sector, demonstrating that such a two-body bound state naturally leads to a three-body bound state. The stability of this hexaquark state was anticipated in an exploratory study by Vij12 using a quark string model. The authors note that the same mechanism does not produce binding in the charm sector because the pseudoscalar-vector meson mass difference is 141 MeV in charm versus 45 MeV in bottom, making coupled-channel effects much weaker. The work enables experimental searches for manifestly exotic hadrons ($T_{bb}$ and $T_{bbb}$) that would be stable under strong and electromagnetic interactions.
