# Cross-correlators of conserved charges in QCD

> We present cross-correlators of QCD conserved charges at $μ_B=0$ from lattice simulations and perform a Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model analysis to break down the hadronic contributions to these correlators. We construct a suitable hadronic proxy for the ratio $-χ_{11}^{BS}/χ_2^S$ and discuss the dependence on the chemical potential and experimental cuts. We then perform a comparison to preliminary STAR results and comment on a possible direct comparison of lattice and experiment.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a hadronic proxy for the ratio \(-\chi_{11}^{BS}/\chi_2^S\) that reproduces the full lattice QCD result for temperatures around the QCD transition, but a direct comparison between lattice QCD and experimental measurements is premature because the same kinematic cuts must be applied to all hadronic species for the proxy to work.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations at \(\mu_B=0\) to compute the cross-correlator \(\chi_{11}^{BS}\) between baryon number and strangeness, then used a Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model to decompose the correlator into contributions from individual hadronic species and their decays. They separated hadrons into measured species (pions, kaons, protons, lambdas, xis, omegas) and non-measured species, and constructed a proxy \(\widetilde{C}^{\Lambda,\Lambda K}_{BS,SS} = \sigma_\Lambda^2/( \sigma_K^2 + \sigma_\Lambda^2)\) for the ratio \(-\chi_{11}^{BS}/\chi_2^S\). The proxy was tested at finite chemical potential along parametrized freeze-out lines and compared to preliminary STAR data with experimental kinematic cuts.

## Key numbers

- The continuum-extrapolated lattice result for \(\chi_{11}^{BS}\) is shown as a function of temperature at \(\mu_B=0\) (no single numerical value given for the correlator itself).
- The proxy \(\widetilde{C}^{\Lambda,\Lambda K}_{BS,SS}\) is compared to the full ratio \(-\chi_{11}^{BS}/\chi_2^S\) along freeze-out lines with \(T(\mu_B=0) = 145\) MeV and \(T(\mu_B=0) = 165\) MeV.
- Preliminary STAR data are compared to the proxy along the same freeze-out lines, with the higher freeze-out temperature of 165 MeV preferred.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD calculations of conserved charge fluctuations (Borsanyi 2011, Bazavov 2012) and connects them to experimental measurements of net-particle fluctuations and correlations (Adamczyk 2013, Adamczyk 2014, Adamczyk 2017, Nonaka 2019, Adam 2019). The HRG model analysis builds on the PDG2016+ hadronic list from Alba (2017) and decay lists from Alba (2017). The proxy enables a possible direct comparison between lattice QCD and experimental data from STAR, but requires consistent kinematic cuts across all hadronic species before such a comparison is reliable.
