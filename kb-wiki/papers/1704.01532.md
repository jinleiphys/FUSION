# Statistical moments in superposition models and strongly intensive measures

> First, we present a concise glossary of formulas for composition of standard, cumulant, factorial, and factorial cumulant moments in superposition (compound) models, where final particles are created via independent emission from a collection of sources. Explicit mathematical formulas for the composed moments are given to all orders. We discuss the composition laws for various types of moments via the generating-function methods and list the formulas for the unfolding of the unwanted fluctuations. Second, the technique is applied to the difference of the scaled multiplicities of two particle types. This allows us for a systematic derivation and a simple algebraic interpretation of the so-called strongly intensive fluctuation measures. With the help of the formalism we obtain several new strongly intensive measures involving higher-rank moments. The reviewed as well as the new results may be useful in investigations of mechanisms of particle production and event-by-event fluctuations in high-energy nuclear and hadronic collisions, and in particular in the search for signatures of the QCD phase transition at a finite baryon density.

## Key claim

The paper provides a systematic derivation of composition laws for statistical moments in superposition (compound) models, showing that strongly intensive fluctuation measures can be obtained from cumulants of the difference of scaled multiplicities of two particle types, with the key condition that the structural form of the moments is identical between the source-level and final-particle-level expressions, thereby canceling the dependence on the number of sources.

## Method

The authors derive composition laws for generating functions of standard, cumulant, factorial, and factorial cumulant moments in superposition models where final particles are produced via independent emission from a collection of sources. They apply generating-function methods and Faà di Bruno’s formula with Bell polynomials to obtain explicit formulas for composed moments to all orders, then extend the formalism to multivariate cases involving two particle types and use cumulants of the difference of scaled multiplicities to systematically derive strongly intensive measures.

## Key numbers

- The Bernoulli trial efficiency correction yields uniform scaling: \( f^N_m = p^m f^S_m \) and \( \kappa'^N_m = p^m \kappa'^S_m \), where \( p \) is the detection probability.
- The Poisson distribution overlaid on sources gives \( f^N_m = \beta^m \mu'^S_m \) and \( \kappa'^N_m = \beta^m \kappa^S_m \), with \( \beta \) denoting the mean of the Poisson.
- For the normal distribution with \( K^n(t) = \mu t + \sigma^2 t^2/2 \), the composition formulas involve Bessel numbers: \( P_n = \sum_{k=0}^{\lfloor n/2 \rfloor} B(n,n-k) R_1^{n-2k} R_2^k Q_{n-k} \), where \( B(n,k) = \frac{2^{k-n} n!}{(2k-n)! (n-k)!} \).
- The rank-2 strongly intensive relation: \( Q_1 \left( \frac{P_{20}}{P_{10}^2} - \frac{P_{02}}{P_{01}^2} \right) = \frac{R_{20}}{R_{10}^2} - \frac{R_{02}}{R_{01}^2} \).
- The rank-3 strongly intensive relation: \( Q_1^2 \left( \frac{P_{30}}{P_{10}^3} - \frac{3P_{21}}{P_{10}^2 P_{01}} + \frac{3P_{12}}{P_{10} P_{01}^2} - \frac{P_{03}}{P_{01}^3} \right) = \frac{R_{30}}{R_{10}^3} - \frac{3R_{21}}{R_{10}^2 R_{01}} + \frac{3R_{12}}{R_{10} R_{01}^2} - \frac{R_{03}}{R_{01}^3} \).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of strongly intensive fluctuation measures introduced by Gazdzicki (1992) and Gorenstein (2011) by providing a systematic generating-function derivation and generalizing to higher-rank moments. The results enable the unfolding of trivial volume fluctuations in event-by-event analyses of high-energy nuclear collisions, with direct applicability to the search for QCD critical point signatures pursued by the NA61 Collaboration (Gazdzicki, 2015) and to analyses of net-proton and net-charge cumulants from STAR (Aggarwal, 2010; Adamczyk, 2014).
