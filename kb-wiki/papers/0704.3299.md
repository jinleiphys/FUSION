# D_{s0}^+(2317) as an Iso-triplet Four-quark Meson and Production of Its Neutral and Doubly Charged Partners

> By studying the decays D_{s0}^+(2317) --> D_s^+ pi^0 and D_s^{*+} gamma, It is shown that its assignment to the I_3=0 component of iso-triplet scalar four-quark mesons is favored. Productions of its neutral and doubly charged partners are also studied. As the result, it is concluded that they could be observed in B decays. Their iso-singlet partner might have been observed in the radiative channels of B decays by the BELLE Collaboration.

# Wiki Page: D_{s0}^+(2317) as an Iso-triplet Four-quark Meson

## Key claim

Assigning the D_{s0}^+(2317) resonance to the I_3=0 component of an iso-triplet scalar four-quark meson (denoted \hat F_I^+) is favored by experimental decay constraints, while assignment to an iso-singlet state (conventional scalar D_{s0}^{*+}, four-quark \hat F_0^+, or DK molecule) is not favored. The claim depends on the measured upper limit on the radiative decay ratio R(D_{s0}^+(2317)) = Gamma(D_{s0}^+ -> D_s^{*+} gamma) / Gamma(D_{s0}^+ -> D_s^+ pi^0) < 0.059, which only the iso-triplet assignment satisfies.

## Method

The author computed decay widths for D_{s0}^+(2317) and related charm-strange scalar mesons using a hard-pion technique in the infinite momentum frame for strong decays and vector meson dominance (VMD) for radiative decays, with flavor SU_f(4) symmetry for spatial wavefunction overlaps and explicit color-spin overlap factors (|beta_0|^2 = 1/12 for pseudoscalar-pseudoscalar, |beta_1|^2 = 1/4 for vector-pseudoscalar). Isospin non-conserving decays were modeled via eta-pi^0 mixing with mixing parameter epsilon = 0.0105 +/- 0.0013. Production of the neutral and doubly charged partners in B decays was studied via quark-line diagrams within the minimal q\bar q-pair creation approximation, using the BSW effective weak Hamiltonian including non-factorizable terms.

## Key numbers

- Experimental constraint: R(D_{s0}^+(2317)) = Gamma(D_{s0}^+ -> D_s^{*+} gamma) / Gamma(D_{s0}^+ -> D_s^+ pi^0) < 0.059 (CLEO)
- Measured inverse ratio for D_s^{*+}: R(D_s^{*+})^{-1} = Gamma(D_s^{*+} -> D_s^+ pi^0) / Gamma(D_s^{*+} -> D_s^{*+} gamma) = 0.062 +/- 0.008 (PDG 2006)
- SU_f(4) symmetry estimate: Gamma(\hat F_I^+ -> D_s^+ pi^0)_{SU_f(4)} ~ 8 MeV; after 20-30% symmetry breaking correction: Gamma(\hat F_I^+ -> D_s^+ pi^0) ~ 3-5 MeV
- SU_f(4) estimate for radiative decay: Gamma(\hat F_I^+ -> D_s^{*+} gamma)_{SU_f(4)} ~ 45 keV (Table I)
- Ratio from SU_f(4) estimates: Gamma(\hat F_I^+ -> D_s^{*+} gamma) / Gamma(\hat F_I^+ -> D_s^+ pi^0) ~ 0.005, satisfying the experimental constraint
- SU_f(4) estimate for D_s^{*+} -> D_s^+ gamma: 0.8 keV (Table I)
- SU_f(4) estimate for \hat F_0^+ -> D_s^{*+} gamma: 4.7 keV (Table I)
- SU_f(4) estimate for D_{s0}^{*+} -> D_s^{*+} gamma: 35 keV (Table I)
- SU_f(4) estimate for D_s^{*+} -> D_s^+ pi^0: ~ 0.05 keV, giving R(D_s^{*+})^{-1} ~ 0.06 (consistent with measurement)
- SU_f(4) estimates for isospin non-conserving decays: Gamma(D_{s0}^{*+} -> D_s^+ pi^0)_{SU_f(4)} ~ Gamma(\hat F_0^+ -> D_s^+ pi^0)_{SU_f(4)} ~ 0.6 keV
- Resulting ratios: R(D_{s0}^{*+}) ~ 60, R(\hat F_0^+) ~ 7, R(\hat F_I^+) ~ 0.005
- Predicted branching fractions: B(B_u^+ -> D^- \hat F_I^{++}[D_s^+ pi^+]) ~ B(B_d^0 -> \bar D^0 \hat F_I^0[D_s^+ pi^-]) ~ 10^{-3}

## Context

This work extends the four-quark meson interpretation of light scalar mesons (a_0(980), f_0(980), K_0^*(800), f_0(600)) proposed by Jaffe (long ago) to the charm sector, specifically the D_{s0}^+(2317) observed by BABAR and CLEO in inclusive e+e- annihilation. It contradicts the DK molecular interpretation proposed by Barnes, Close, and Lipkin (2003), which was rejected by Mehen and Springer (2005) for predicting too large a radiative decay ratio. The analysis enables searches for the predicted neutral partner \hat F_I^0 (decaying to D_s^+ pi^-) and doubly charged partner \hat F_I^{++} (decaying to D_s^+ pi^+) in B decays, with estimated branching fractions of order 10^{-3}, and suggests that the iso-singlet partner \hat F_0^+ may have already been observed by BELLE in the radiative channel D_s^{*+} gamma.
