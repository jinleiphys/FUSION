# Microscopic description of Coulomb and nuclear excitation of multiphonon states in $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca collisions

> We calculate the inelastic scattering cross sections to populate one- and two-phonon states in heavy ion collisions with both Coulomb and nuclear excitations. Starting from a microscopic approach based on RPA, we go beyond it in order to treat anharmonicities and non-linear terms in the exciting field. These anharmonicities and non-linearities are shown to have important effects on the cross sections both in the low energy part of the spectrum and in the energy region of the Double Giant Quadrupole Resonance. By properly introducing an optical potential the inelastic cross section is calculated semiclassically by integrating the excitation probability over all impact parameters. A satisfactory agreement with the experimental results is obtained.

# Microscopic description of Coulomb and nuclear excitation of multiphonon states in $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca collisions

## Key claim

The authors show that including anharmonicities in the internal Hamiltonian and non-linear terms in the external excitation field produces a sizable increase in calculated inelastic cross sections compared to standard harmonic RPA calculations, and that this extended model yields satisfactory agreement with experimental data for the $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca reaction at 50 MeV/u, provided the theoretical spectrum is smoothed with a 5 MeV Lorentzian width.

## Method

The authors perform self-consistent Hartree-Fock plus RPA calculations with the Skyrme SGII interaction to obtain a basis of one-phonon states, then construct all possible two-phonon states and diagonalize an anharmonic bosonic Hamiltonian in this space. The excitation process is treated semiclassically by solving coupled-channel equations along classical trajectories, with form factors obtained by double-folding the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction and the Coulomb interaction with RPA transition densities. An optical potential fitted to experimental elastic scattering data is introduced to account for absorption from channels not explicitly included, with a procedure to avoid double-counting of the explicitly treated inelastic channels.

## Key numbers

- Reaction: $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca at 50 MeV/u
- The ratio between cross section in the Giant Resonance region and that in the two-phonon region varies from 3.7 (anharmonic and non-linear case) to 4.6 (harmonic and linear calculation)
- For single and double isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance only, those ratios increase to 6.5 and 9.6 respectively
- In the double giant quadrupole resonance energy region (28-38 MeV), non-linearities give an increase of about 40% with respect to a harmonic and linear calculation
- The experimental GR cross section extracted from the inclusive spectrum is between 113 and 223 mb/sr (depending on background hypothesis), corresponding to 40-80% of the quadrupole EWSR
- The theoretical inelastic cross section in the anharmonic and non-linear case adds up to 22 mb in the GR region
- The experimental and theoretical ratios of one-phonon to two-phonon cross sections (without background subtraction) are 2.4 and 2.3 respectively
- The imaginary part of the optical potential was scaled by a factor of 0.627 to fit experimental elastic cross section

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous microscopic model (Catara et al., 1998; Lanza et al., 1999; Lanza et al., 2000) by introducing an optical potential to handle absorption from unaccounted channels, removing the need for an arbitrary minimum impact parameter cutoff. The calculations are compared to experimental data from Scarpaci et al. (1993) for the $^{40}$Ca + $^{40}$Ca reaction at 50 MeV/u. The results improve upon earlier qualitative work by Chomaz et al. (1993) that used an optical potential. The authors note that a recent experiment using an improved apparatus (Franchoo et al., 2001) is expected to eliminate most experimental uncertainties and enable more reliable determination of theoretical parameters, particularly the optical potential.
