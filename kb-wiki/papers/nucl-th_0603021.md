# Density matrix renormalization group approach for many-body open quantum systems

> The density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) approach is extended to complex-symmetric density matrices characteristic of many-body open quantum systems. Within the continuum shell model, we investigate the interplay between many-body configuration interaction and coupling to open channels. It is shown that the DMRG technique applied to broad resonances in the unbound neutron-rich nucleus 7He provides a highly accurate treatment of the coupling to the non-resonant scattering continuum.

# Density matrix renormalization group approach for many-body open quantum systems

## Key claim
The density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) method can be extended to complex-symmetric density matrices for many-body open quantum systems, and when applied within the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) to broad resonances in the unbound neutron-rich nucleus ⁷He, it provides a highly accurate treatment of the coupling to the non-resonant scattering continuum, though the convergence shows a periodic "plateau-unstable" pattern rather than monotonic improvement.

## Method
The authors extended the DMRG approach to handle complex-symmetric reduced density matrices characteristic of open quantum systems described by the Gamow Shell Model (GSM). The configuration space was divided into two Fock subspaces: subspace A containing the resonant single-particle shells (0p₁/₂ and 0p₃/₂) and subspace B containing the discretized non-resonant continuum shells {p₁/₂}꜀ and {p₃/₂}꜀. In a warm-up phase, pairs of non-resonant continuum shells were successively added to subspace B, the Hamiltonian was diagonalized, and the reduced density matrix was computed and diagonalized to retain only the Nₒₚₜ eigenstates with eigenvalues of largest modulus; this was followed by a sweeping phase where the procedure was repeated in alternating directions until convergence.

## Key numbers
- The ground state (J^π = 3/2⁻) resonance in ⁷He has a width Γ = 0.14 MeV; the first excited state (J^π = 1/2⁻) has a width Γ = 3.37 MeV.
- The relative precision of the real part of the ground state energy in ⁷He reached approximately 10⁻⁶ for Nₒₚₜ = 24 and Nₛw = 4 sweeps (ΔE/E = 4.7 × 10⁻⁶).
- The relative precision improved from 9.4 × 10⁻⁴ (Nₒₚₜ = 20, Nₛw = 2) to 2.6 × 10⁻⁶ (Nₒₚₜ = 26, Nₛw = 4).
- The dimension of the GSM matrix in the J-coupling scheme varied from D = 6149 for Nₛₕ = 26 to D = 332,171 for Nₛₕ = 98, while the rank of the largest matrix diagonalized in GSM+DMRG changed only from d = 941 to d = 1001 over the same range.
- The single-particle basis used 2 resonant shells and 60 non-resonant continuum shells (30 in each of {p₁/₂}꜀ and {p₃/₂}꜀).

## Context
This work extends the DMRG method, previously applied to closed quantum systems with Hermitian density matrices (reviewed in Rev1, Rev2) and to finite Fermi systems such as molecules (pap2), superconducting grains (pap3), quantum dots (pap4), and atomic nuclei (pap1), to open quantum systems described by the Gamow Shell Model (GSM) developed by Mic02 and Bet02. The approach enables systematic high-precision studies of continuum coupling effects in multiparticle open quantum systems, including open microwave resonators, multi-electron open quantum dots, atomic nuclei, and atomic clusters near the particle drip line, where the explosive growth of the GSM configuration space had previously been the principal limitation.
