# Investigation of the two-quasiparticle bands in the doubly-odd nucleus $^{166}$Ta using a particle-number conserving cranked shell model

> The high-spin rotational properties of two-quasiparticle bands in the doubly-odd ${}^{166}$Ta are analyzed using the cranked shell model with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving method, in which the blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The experimental moments of inertia and alignments and their variations with the rotational frequency $\hbarω$ are reproduced very well by the particle-number conserving calculations, which provides a reliable support to the configuration assignments in previous works for these bands. The backbendings in these two-quasiparticle bands are analyzed by the calculated occupation probabilities and the contributions of each orbital to the total angular momentum alignments. The moments of inertia and alignments for the Gallagher-Moszkowski partners of these observed two-quasiparticle rotational bands are also predicted.

## Key claim

The particle-number conserving cranked shell model (PNC-CSM) reproduces the experimental moments of inertia and alignments of four two-quasiparticle bands in \({}^{166}\)Ta very well, but the sharp backbendings are not very well reproduced because the cranking model mixes bands with very different alignments, and the calculated second backbending frequency for band 2 (about 0.55 MeV) is a little larger than the data (about 0.46 MeV).

## Method

The cranked shell model Hamiltonian with monopole pairing is solved directly in a truncated Fock space (cranked many-particle configuration space) using a particle-number conserving method, which exactly conserves particle number and accounts for Pauli blocking effects. Deformation parameters \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.192\) and \(\varepsilon_4 = -0.0045\) are taken from an average of neighboring even-even Hf and W isotopes, with Nilsson parameters adjusted slightly for the neutron \(N=6\) shell and the proton \(\pi 1/2^-[541]\) orbital shifted upward by about 0.7 MeV. The effective pairing strengths are \(G_{\rm p} = 0.34\) MeV for protons and \(G_{\rm n} = 0.46\) MeV for neutrons, with a CMPC space dimension of about 1000 for both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters: \(\varepsilon_2 = 0.192\) and \(\varepsilon_4 = -0.0045\) for \({}^{166}\)Ta.
- Proton orbital \(\pi 1/2^-[541]\) shifted upward by about 0.7 MeV.
- Effective pairing strengths: \(G_{\rm p} = 0.34\) MeV for protons, \(G_{\rm n} = 0.46\) MeV for neutrons.
- \(\hbar\omega_{\rm 0p} = 7.160\) MeV for protons and \(\hbar\omega_{\rm 0n} = 7.760\) MeV for neutrons.
- First backbending frequency in band 1, band 3, and band 4: \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.30\) MeV.
- First backbending frequency in band 2: \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.20\) MeV.
- Second backbending frequency in band 2: experimental \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.46\) MeV; calculated \(\hbar\omega \sim 0.55\) MeV (overestimated by about 0.1 MeV).
- Harris parameters used for alignment extraction: \(J_0 = 20\ \hbar^2\)MeV\(^{-1}\) and \(J_1 = 40\ \hbar^4\)MeV\(^{-3}\).

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM approach, previously applied to even-even and odd-\(A\) nuclei (Zeng 1994, Liu 2004, Zhang 2009), to the doubly-odd nucleus \({}^{166}\)Ta, supporting the configuration assignments of Hartley (2010) for four two-quasiparticle bands. The analysis of occupation probabilities and orbital contributions to alignment clarifies the backbending mechanisms, and the predictions for Gallagher-Moszkowski partner bands provide guidance for future experiments.
