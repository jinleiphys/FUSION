# Polarization effects in $\bar N+N\to π+\ell^++\ell^-$ reaction. General analysis and numerical estimations

> A general formalism is developed to calculate the cross section and the polarization observables for the reaction $\bar N+N\to π+\ell^++\ell^-$. The matrix element and the observables are expressed in terms of six scalar amplitudes (complex functions of three kinematical variables) which determine the reaction dynamics. The numerical predictions are given in frame of a particular model in the kinematical range accessible in the PANDA experiment at FAIR.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general, model-independent formalism for calculating polarization observables in the reaction \(\bar N+N\to \pi+\ell^++\ell^-\), but the numerical predictions are given only within a particular Born-approximation model (the "scattering channel exchange" mechanism) and are restricted to the kinematical range accessible in the PANDA experiment at FAIR.

## Method

The authors express the matrix element for \(\bar p+p\to \pi^0+\gamma^*\to \pi^0+\ell^++\ell^-\) in terms of six complex scalar amplitudes \(f_i\) (functions of three kinematical variables) using an orthogonal basis built from the virtual photon and antiproton momenta. They then derive the hadronic tensor and the differential cross section, integrating over the lepton-pair phase space, and obtain expressions for single-spin asymmetries (proton or antiproton polarized normally to the reaction plane) and double-spin correlation coefficients \(C_{ij}\) in terms of these amplitudes. Numerical evaluations are performed using the Born-approximation amplitudes from a previous model (Gakh et al., 2009), which assumes nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region and a \(\pi NN\) vertex with coupling constant \(g\).

## Key numbers

* Unpolarized differential cross section expression contains factor \(\beta_e / \beta_N \cdot (1/q^2) \cdot (1 + 2m^2/q^2)\) where \(\beta_e = \sqrt{1-4m^2/q^2}\) and \(\beta_N = \sqrt{1-4M^2/s}\).
* Numerical results for polarization observables are shown at total energy squared \(s = 5.5\) GeV\(^2\) (corresponding to antiproton laboratory energy \(E^{Lab}_{\bar p} = 2\) GeV) and at \(s = 14.9\) GeV\(^2\) (\(E^{Lab}_{\bar p} = 7\) GeV).
* Three values of momentum transfer squared are used in the numerical plots: \(q^2 = 0.5\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), \(q^2 = 2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), and \(q^2 = 4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
* The single-spin asymmetries \(A_y\) and \(\bar A_y\) coincide in Born approximation because the term \(f_3 f_4^*\) is real; a difference between them would be an experimental signature of contributions beyond Born approximation.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Gakh et al. (2009) (which calculated the unpolarized cross section for moving antiproton) to include polarization observables for the reaction \(\bar p+p\to \pi^0+\ell^++\ell^-\). It is motivated by the planned PANDA experiment at FAIR and by the possibility of polarizing antiproton beams or using polarized proton targets (PAX collaboration). The reaction allows access to nucleon electromagnetic form factors in the time-like region below the \(\bar N N\) threshold (\(q^2 < 4M^2\)), which is inaccessible in \(e^+e^- \leftrightarrow N\bar N\) reactions. The formalism enables future extraction of the imaginary part of the complex form factors and can be applied to other reaction mechanisms, such as s-channel vector meson exchange (Bystritskiy et al., 2010).
