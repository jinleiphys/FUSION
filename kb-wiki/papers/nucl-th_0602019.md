# Scattering of Ultra Cold Neutrons on Nano-size Bubbles

> Inelastic scattering of ultra cold neutrons on bubbles with the size of nanometers is considered. It is shown that neutron-bubble cross section is large and sensitive to different vibration modes of bubbles. This process could be used for study of dynamics of nano-size bubbles and for new methods of ultra cold neutron production.

## Key claim
Inelastic scattering of ultra cold neutrons on nanometer-sized bubbles can have a large cross section sensitive to different vibration modes, but the feasibility of applications such as UCN production depends on whether appropriately sized bubbles with sufficient density can be created.

## Method
The author analytically solves the inelastic neutron scattering problem on a spherical bubble in liquid helium, modeling the bubble as a square-well Fermi potential and describing its shape deformations in terms of harmonic oscillators for the breathing (l=0) and quadrupole surface (l=2) modes. The cross section is derived using the Born approximation with plane waves for the neutron, integrating over the deformed bubble volume and the harmonic oscillator wavefunctions for the bubble states.

## Key numbers
- Neutron wavelength at kinetic energy 100 neV is 90.4 nm; at 1 neV it is 904 nm.
- Fermi potential for liquid 4He is 16 neV, for 3He is 28 neV, and for solid deuterium is 104 neV.
- For a bubble with radius 1.75 nm in liquid helium, the breathing mode frequency is hbar*omega_b = 3.8e-5 eV and the surface mode frequency is hbar*omega_s = 5e-5 eV.
- The elastic cross section for low-energy scattering (kR << 1) scales as sigma ~ (4/9) R^2 (K0 R)^4 ~ R^6.
- The scattering parameter kR ranges from about 0.07 to 10 for UCN on nano-size particles.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Nesvizhevsky and collaborators (2005) on UCN interactions with nano-particles, which proposed that coherent scattering on nano-size objects could explain UCN losses in traps and enable new UCN production methods. The author provides an analytically solvable model for bubbles in liquid helium, contrasting with the numerically intensive calculations required for solid nano-particles. The results enable future experimental studies of bubble dynamics in liquids and suggest a possible method for moderating thermal neutrons to ultra-cold energies using bubble gases, though the author notes that experimental feasibility remains to be determined.
