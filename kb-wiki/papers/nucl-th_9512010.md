# Symmetry Breaking for Rho Meson in Neutron Matter

> Qualitative changes in the collective excitation spectra of the $ρ$-meson triplet in neutron matter is studied, with particular emphasis on the breaking of the discrete $p\leftrightarrow n$ symmetry. The appearance of additional branches in the dispersion characteristics, the mass splitting among the charge states, the splitting between longitudinal and transverse modes of the $ρ^{\pm}$ mesons and the appearance of `island' modes (or loops) in the time-like region are some of the features that are exposed.

# Symmetry Breaking for Rho Meson in Neutron Matter

## Key claim
The paper shows that in pure neutron matter (the extreme limit of isospin asymmetry), the collective excitation spectra of the ρ-meson triplet exhibit qualitatively new features, including mass splitting among charge states, longitudinal-transverse mode splitting for ρ± even in the static limit, and the appearance of "island" modes, that are absent in symmetric nuclear matter, but these results are obtained without including Dirac sea contributions, which the authors note may affect quantitative estimates.

## Method
The authors calculate the in-medium ρ-meson propagator in neutron matter at zero temperature using a Lagrangian with both vector and tensor (magnetic) ρ-nucleon couplings, with coupling constants taken from Bonn group estimates (g_v²/4π = 0.92, κ = 6.1). They compute the density-dependent part of the polarization tensor from nucleon-hole excitations in the Fermi sea (excluding Dirac sea contributions), then solve the dielectric function eigenconditions ε_T²(q) ε_L(q) = 0 numerically to obtain dispersion curves for longitudinal and transverse collective modes of ρ⁰, ρ⁺, and ρ⁻.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constants used: g_v²/4π = 0.92, κ = 6.1 (from Bonn group estimates; VMD suggests κ = 3)
- The tensor coupling strength κ = 6.1 is used to emphasize the effects of magnetic interaction
- For ρ⁺ propagation, up to four longitudinal and three transverse modes appear in the region of stable collective oscillation
- The maximum momentum |q|_max for island modes approaches a saturation value with increasing density (shown in Fig. 4)
- Island modes exist even in the limit κ → 0 for both ρ⁺ and ρ⁻ propagation

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of in-medium ρ-meson mass shifts in symmetric nuclear matter by authors such as Asakawa and collaborators (1992) and Song (1993), which were confined to isospin-symmetric systems. The paper specifically contrasts with those by examining the extreme asymmetric case of pure neutron matter, relevant for heavy ion collisions and nuclear astrophysics. The results enable future studies of ρ-meson propagation at intermediate asymmetries and motivate inclusion of Dirac sea contributions and mixing effects for more reliable quantitative predictions.
