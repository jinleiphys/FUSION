# Study of entanglement in Ne, Mg, and Si isotopic chains

> In this article, we have presented the results for mode entanglement and its evolution in Ne, Mg, and Si isotopic chains. A detailed study has also been conducted on the role of nuclear forces with the assistance of the spin-tensor decomposition of two-body interaction into three components: central, spin-orbit, and tensor. This study can shed light on the high entanglement entropy observed for $N=Z$ nuclei and the importance of the tensor component on the proton-neutron entanglement entropy.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that proton-neutron entanglement entropy in Ne, Mg, and Si isotopic chains is maximized at the N=Z condition, and that the tensor component of the nuclear force plays a significant role in increasing entanglement entropy by attracting the proton and neutron d5/2 and d3/2 orbitals, while the spin-orbit component reduces entanglement by increasing the energy splitting between these orbitals.

## Method

The authors performed configuration-interaction shell model calculations using the BIGSTICK code with the USDB interaction for Ne (20-28), Mg (22-30), and Si (24-32) isotopic chains. They computed mode entanglement entropy via von Neumann entropy from occupation probabilities of single-neutron orbitals, and performed spin-tensor decomposition of the USDB interaction into central (k=0), spin-orbit (k=1), and tensor (k=2) components to isolate each contribution's effect on entanglement entropy and effective single-particle energies.

## Key numbers

- Single-orbital entropy for the d5/2 orbital peaks at N=12 in all Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes, where its occupation is close to half
- The s1/2 orbital shows maximum entropy in 24Ne, 28Mg, and 28Si
- The d3/2 orbital reaches highest entropy at N=18, again due to occupation near 0.5
- For Si isotopes, entanglement entropy reaches a maximum at 28Si (N=Z) for all three decomposed interaction components
- The central component yields the largest entanglement entropy across the Si isotopic chain; adding the spin-orbit term drops entropy significantly; further inclusion of the tensor component causes entropy to rise again
- For N=Z nuclei in the sd-shell, the central component exhibits odd-even staggering that disappears after adding the spin-orbit term
- At shell closures 28Si and 32S, inclusion of the tensor component significantly increases entanglement entropy

## Context

This work extends the study of quantum information measures in nuclear systems, following the mode entanglement formalism developed by Carl (2021) and Johnson (2025) for proton-neutron partitions. The spin-tensor decomposition approach follows the method of Smirnova (2012) for separating nuclear force components. The results explain the high entanglement entropy observed at N=Z nuclei and demonstrate how the tensor force, as described by Otsuka (2005), attracts the d5/2 and d3/2 orbitals to increase entanglement, enabling future studies of entanglement evolution across the nuclear chart using decomposed interactions.
