# Exploring Zeptosecond Quantum Equilibration Dynamics: From Deep-Inelastic to Fusion-Fission Outcomes in $^{58}$Ni+$^{60}$Ni Reactions

> Energy dissipative processes play a key role in how quantum many-body systems dynamically evolve towards equilibrium. In closed quantum systems, such processes are attributed to the transfer of energy from collective motion to single-particle degrees of freedom; however, the quantum many-body dynamics of this evolutionary process are poorly understood. To explore energy dissipative phenomena and equilibration dynamics in one such system, an experimental investigation of deep-inelastic and fusion-fission outcomes in the $^{58}$Ni+$^{60}$Ni reaction has been carried out. Experimental outcomes have been compared to theoretical predictions using Time Dependent Hartree Fock and Time Dependent Random Phase Approximation approaches, which respectively incorporate one-body energy dissipation and fluctuations. Excellent quantitative agreement has been found between experiment and calculations, indicating that microscopic models incorporating one-body dissipation and fluctuations provide a potential tool for exploring dissipation in low-energy heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

Excellent quantitative agreement between experiment and microscopic calculations (TDHF and TDRPA) demonstrates that models incorporating only one-body dissipation and fluctuations can reproduce energy dissipative outcomes in low-energy heavy ion collisions, but the paper hedges that this conclusion applies specifically to the $^{58}$Ni+$^{60}$Ni system and that the wide mass component could originate from both deep-inelastic and fusion-fission processes rather than being uniquely assigned.

## Method

The experiment used the CUBE two-body fission spectrometer at the ANU Heavy Ion Accelerator Facility to measure total kinetic energy (TKE), mass ratio, and scattering angle for binary outcomes of $^{58}$Ni+$^{60}$Ni collisions at 20 beam energies from approximately 194 to 270 MeV. Experimental results were compared to 3D Time-Dependent Hartree Fock (TDHF) calculations using the SLy4d Skyrme energy-density functional, which provide average fragment masses and TKE, and to Time-Dependent Random Phase Approximation (TDRPA) calculations, which provide mass fluctuations. The TDHF calculations were performed for a reaction timescale of 13 zeptoseconds, with impact parameters ranging from a minimum value (below which fusion was assumed) up to 10 fm.

## Key numbers

- The experimental fusion barrier $V_B$ = 96.87 MeV in the center-of-mass frame for $^{58}$Ni+$^{60}$Ni.
- For the reaction at $E/V_B$=1.14 and minimum impact parameter $b$ = 3.8 fm: experimental $\sigma_{MR, expt}= 0.027(9)$ and calculated $\sigma_{MR, calc}= 0.017$.
- For the reaction at $E/V_B$=1.27 and minimum impact parameter $b$ = 5.0 fm: experimental $\sigma_{MR, expt}= 0.025(9)$ and calculated $\sigma_{MR, calc}= 0.017$.
- The widest calculated mass distribution widths result from reactions with contact times greater than approximately 3 zeptoseconds.
- The calculated timescale for full mass equilibration in deep-inelastic outcomes is approximately 4 zeptoseconds, consistent with the 5-10 zeptosecond timescales observed for quasifission in heavier systems by Toke (1985) and du Rietz (2011, 2013).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of energy dissipation in heavy ion collisions by providing a direct comparison between experimental observables and microscopic TDHF/TDRPA calculations, building on the approach used by Simenel (2011) for $^{40}$Ca+$^{40}$Ca. The results enable future exploration of whether one-body dissipation mechanisms are sufficient to describe energy equilibration in other low-energy heavy ion systems, and suggest that mass equilibration can occur on zeptosecond timescales comparable to those observed for quasifission in heavier systems.
