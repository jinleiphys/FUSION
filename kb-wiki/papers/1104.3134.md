# Chiral effective field theories of the strong interactions

> Effective field theories of the strong interactions based on the approximate chiral symmetry of QCD provide a model-independent approach to low-energy hadron physics. We give a brief introduction to mesonic and baryonic chiral perturbation theory and discuss a number of applications. We also consider the effective field theory including vector and axial-vector mesons.

## Key claim

This paper provides a pedagogical introduction to chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) as the effective field theory of QCD at low energies, covering both mesonic and baryonic sectors; the authors note that while ChPT provides a systematic, model-independent framework, its predictions depend on low-energy constants (LECs) that must be determined from experiment, and the power counting that organizes the expansion assumes energies far below 1 GeV.

## Method

The authors construct the most general Lagrangian consistent with the approximate chiral symmetry of QCD, organizing terms by increasing numbers of derivatives and powers of quark masses according to Weinberg's power counting. For the mesonic sector, they use the Goldstone boson matrix U(x) and external fields to build the effective Lagrangian, then calculate observables such as pseudoscalar meson masses and pion electromagnetic polarizabilities at successive chiral orders. For the baryonic sector, they introduce nucleon fields with appropriate chiral transformation properties and discuss renormalization schemes including heavy-baryon ChPT, infrared regularization, and the extended on-mass-shell (EOMS) scheme to address the power-counting problem caused by the non-vanishing nucleon mass.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant in the chiral limit: physical value \(F_\pi = 92.42(26)\,\text{MeV}\)
- At leading order (O(q^2)), the quark mass ratio from meson masses: \(m_s/\hat m = 25.9\) from \(M_K/M_\pi\) and \(m_s/\hat m = 24.3\) from \(M_\eta/M_\pi\), using \(M_\pi=135\) MeV, \(M_K=496\) MeV, \(M_\eta=548\) MeV
- Charged pion polarizability at O(q^4): \(\alpha_{\pi^+} = (2.64 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\)
- Two-loop (O(q^6)) predictions for charged pion polarizabilities: \((\alpha + \beta)_{\pi^+} = 0.16 \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\), \((\alpha - \beta)_{\pi^+} = (5.7 \pm 1.0) \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\)
- Experimental result from MAMI for \((\alpha-\beta)_{\pi^+} = (11.6 \pm 1.5_{\text{stat}} \pm 3.0_{\text{syst}} \pm 0.5_{\text{mod}}) \times 10^{-4}\,\text{fm}^3\)
- Nucleon mass in the chiral limit from EOMS scheme: \(m = 882.8\,\text{MeV}\), with \(\Delta m = 55.5\,\text{MeV}\)

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984) who first constructed mesonic chiral perturbation theory, and Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1987) who extended it to baryons. The paper discusses the power-counting problem in baryon ChPT and compares solutions including heavy-baryon ChPT by Jenkins and Manohar (1990), infrared regularization by Becher and Leutwyler (1999), and the EOMS scheme by Fuchs, Gegelia, and Scherer (2003). The applications shown, such as pion polarizabilities and nucleon mass calculations, demonstrate the current status of ChPT and highlight remaining tensions with experimental data (e.g., the MAMI result for \((\alpha-\beta)_{\pi^+}\) deviates by 2 standard deviations from the ChPT prediction), pointing to the need for further theoretical and experimental work.
