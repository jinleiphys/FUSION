# $╬İ^{+}$ hypernuclei in relativistic mean field model

> We have investigated the properties of $╬İ^{+}$ in nuclei within the framework of relativistic mean field. The coupling constants are educed with quark meson coupling model. There is strong attractive interaction for $╬İ^{+}$-nucleus and $╬İ^{+}$ can be bind in nuclei. The depth of optical potential for $╬İ^{+}$ in nuclear matter is estimated.

## Key claim

Using a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with coupling constants derived from the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, the authors predict that the pentaquark $\Theta^{+}$ (assumed $J^{P}=1/2^{+}$, $I=0$) experiences a strong attractive interaction with nuclei and can form bound $\Theta^{+}$ hypernuclei, but the binding is highly sensitive to the $\sigma-\Theta^{+}$ coupling constant; if $g_{\sigma}^{\Theta}$ falls below approximately 11.725 (i.e., $13.925 \times 0.84$), no bound states exist in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$.

## Method

The authors employed the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with the NL-SH parameter set for the nucleonic sector, and derived the $\sigma-\Theta^{+}$ and $\omega-\Theta^{+}$ coupling constants using the quark meson coupling (QMC) model, assuming the meson fields couple only to the $u$ and $d$ quarks in the $\Theta^{+}$. For $\Theta^{+}$ with isospin $I=0$, the $\rho$ meson coupling vanishes, and the coupling constants are given by $g_{\sigma}^{\Theta} = \frac{4}{3} g_{\sigma}^{N} \Gamma_{\Theta/B}$ (with $\Gamma_{\Theta/B} \approx 1$) and $g_{\omega}^{\Theta} = \frac{4}{3} g_{\omega}^{N}$. They solved the RMF equations for $\Theta^{+}$ in various nuclei ($^{6}\mathrm{Li}$, $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, $^{16}\mathrm{O}$, $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$, $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$) and also estimated the $\Theta^{+}$ optical potential depth in nuclear matter.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the 1s1/2 state for $\Theta^{+}$ in $^{13}_{\Theta}\mathrm{C}$: 116.24 MeV (with $g_{\sigma}^{\Theta}=13.925$); this decreases to 2.01 MeV when $g_{\sigma}^{\Theta}=11.725$.
- Binding energy per baryon for $^{13}_{\Theta}\mathrm{C}$: 14.41 MeV, compared to 7.47 MeV for normal $^{12}\mathrm{C}$.
- Binding energy per baryon for $^{9}_{\Theta}\mathrm{Be}$: 11.43 MeV, compared to 5.42 MeV for normal $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$.
- Separation between the 1s1/2 and 1p3/2 states: about 60 MeV for $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, about 24 MeV for $^{6}\mathrm{Li}$, about 30 MeV for $^{16}\mathrm{O}$, about 18 MeV for $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$, and about 4 MeV for $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$.
- Spin-orbit splitting between 1p3/2 and 1p1/2: about 5 MeV for $^{6}\mathrm{Li}$ and $^{16}\mathrm{O}$, about 10 MeV for $^{12}\mathrm{C}$, about 2 MeV for $^{40}\mathrm{Ca}$, and no obvious splitting for $^{208}\mathrm{Pb}$.
- Estimated $\Theta^{+}$ optical potential depth in nuclear matter: $-81 \leq U \leq -46$ MeV for $\varrho_{0}=0.15\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ and $13.925 \leq g_{\sigma}^{\Theta} \leq 13.925 \times 0.93$; $-84 \leq U \leq -47$ MeV for $\varrho_{0}=0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$; $-92 \leq U \leq -52$ MeV for $\varrho_{0}=0.17\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}$.
- Shrinkage effect: r.m.s. charge radius $r_{ch}$ for $^{13}_{\Theta}\mathrm{C}$ is 2.32 fm, compared to 2.46 fm for normal $^{12}\mathrm{C}$; for $^{9}_{\Theta}\mathrm{Be}$, $r_{ch}=2.36$ fm versus 2.48 fm for $^{8}\mathrm{Be}$.

## Context

This work extends the RMF approach previously applied to $\Lambda$, $\Sigma$, and $\Xi$ hypernuclei (e.g., by the same group in earlier works) to the newly discovered $\Theta^{+}$ pentaquark, following suggestions by Cabrera et al. (2004) that $\Theta^{+}$ could be bound in nuclei due to in-medium pion renormalization. The authors compare their results to the simpler Schrodinger-equation-based estimates of Cabrera et al. (2004), finding agreement for the 1s binding energy range in $^{12}\mathrm{C}$ (34-87 MeV from that work versus 40-116 MeV here depending on coupling). The prediction of a shrinkage effect in light $\Theta^{+}$ hypernuclei mirrors similar findings in $\Lambda$ hypernuclei (e.g., for lithium hypernuclei), though no shrinkage is found for $^{7}_{\Theta}\mathrm{Li}$. The work enables future experimental searches for $\Theta^{+}$ hypernuclei by providing predicted binding energies and spin-orbit splittings that could be resolved given the narrow width of $\Theta^{+}$ (upper limits of 5-15 MeV from chiral soliton models and $K^{+}N$ scattering analyses).
