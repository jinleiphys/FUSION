# Description of heavy deformed nuclei within the pseudo-SU(3) shell model

> We present a review of the pseudo-SU(3) shell model and its application to heavy deformed nuclei. The model have been applied to describe the low energy spectra, B(E2) and B(M1) values. A systematic study of each part of the interaction within the Hamiltonian was carried out. The study leads us to a consistent method of choosing the parameters in the model. A systematic application of the model for a sequence of rare earth nuclei demonstrates that an overarching symmetry can be used to predict the onset of deformation as manifested through low-lying collective bands.The scheme utilizes an overarching sp(4,R) algebraic framework.

## Key claim

The pseudo-SU(3) shell model, using a Hamiltonian with quadrupole-quadrupole, pairing, single-particle, and rotor terms, can systematically reproduce low-energy spectra and band-head energies of heavy deformed rare-earth nuclei, provided the interaction strengths are fixed through a systematic study of each term's effect. The abstract's claim of "good agreement" is hedged by the paper's explicit demonstration that the rotor-term parameters (a, b, a_s, a_3) must be individually fitted per nucleus to achieve the reported accuracy.

## Method

The authors constructed a many-body basis using pseudo-SU(3) irreps for normal-parity valence protons and neutrons, with unique-parity spaces treated in a seniority-zero scheme. They diagonalized a Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole (Q·Q), proton and neutron single-particle energies, pairing interactions, and rotor terms (J², K²_J, C₂, C₃) for a sequence of even-even rare-earth nuclei. The strength of each interaction term was varied systematically to study its effect on energy levels and B(E2) transitions, leading to fixed values for pairing (G_π=21/A, G_ν=17/A) and quadrupole-quadrupole (χ=35A^{-5/3}), while rotor parameters were fitted per nucleus to match experimental band heads.

- For ¹⁵⁶Gd: valence protons n^N_π = 8 in pseudo-shell η̃_π = 3 (degeneracy Ω_π = 20), valence neutrons n^N_ν = 6 in η̃_ν = 4 (Ω_ν = 30); 32 possible proton SU(3) irreps and 48 neutron irreps, with the first 6 proton and 5 neutron irreps selected by largest C₂ value.
- For ¹⁵⁶Gd: oscillator constant ℏω₀ = 41 MeV × A^{-1/3} = 7.616 MeV; Nilsson parameters κ_π = 0.06370, κ_ν = 0.60, μ_π = 0.06370, μ_ν = 0.42.
- Fixed pairing strengths: G_π = 21/A, G_ν = 17/A; quadrupole-quadrupole strength χ = 35A^{-5/3}.
- For ¹⁶⁴Dy (F₀=0 multiplet): calculated E(2₁) = 0.073 MeV (experimental 0.073 MeV), E(0⁺_{K=0₂⁺}) = 1.67 MeV (exp. 1.66 MeV), E(2⁺_{K=2₁⁺}) = 0.76 MeV (exp. 0.76 MeV); rotor parameters a = -0.001, b = 0.04, a_s = 0.0008×10⁻⁴, a₃ = 0.65.
- For ¹⁵⁶Gd (F₀=1 multiplet): calculated E(2₁) = 0.094 MeV (exp. 0.089 MeV), E(0⁺_{K=0₂⁺}) = 1.06 MeV (exp. 1.05 MeV), E(2⁺_{K=2₁⁺}) = 1.14 MeV (exp. 1.15 MeV); rotor parameters a = 0.001, b = 0.10, a_s = 0.0014×10⁻⁴, a₃ = 1.36.
- For ¹⁷⁶Hf (F₀=0): calculated E(2₁) = 0.178 MeV (exp. 0.088 MeV), showing a discrepancy; E(0⁺_{K=0₂⁺}) = 1.15 MeV (exp. 1.15 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-SU(3) shell model developed by Draayer and Weeks (1984), who described ground and gamma bands using a single SU(3) irrep, and by Castanos, Draayer, and Leschber (1987), who expanded the model to multiple irreps for calculating B(E2) and B(M1) transitions. The paper systematically studies each Hamiltonian term's effect, building on earlier parameter choices by Bahri and Draayer (1994) and Popa et al. (2000), and applies the model to F-spin multiplets of an Sp(4,R) classification scheme from Drenska (1995). This enables a microscopic interpretation of the irregular behavior of first excited K=0₂⁺ and K=2⁺ band heads across rare-earth nuclei, and the authors indicate ongoing work to expand the study to other nucleus groups and to analyze transition probabilities.
