# Particle distribution and nuclear stopping in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV

> The transverse momentum distribution of produced charged particles is investigated for gold-gold collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. A simple parameterization is suggested for the particle distribution based on the nuclear stopping effect. The model can fit very well both the transverse momentum distributions at different pseudo-rapidities and the pseudo-rapidity distributions at different centralities. The ratio of rapidity distributions for peripheral and central collisions is calculated and compared with the data.

# Particle distribution and nuclear stopping in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=200 GeV

## Key claim
The transverse momentum and pseudo-rapidity distributions of charged particles in Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV can be described by a simple factorized parameterization with a Gaussian form in transverse rapidity and an exponential form in longitudinal momentum, provided an effective mass of 0.51 GeV is used for the $p_T$ distributions and the pion mass (0.139 GeV) for the $\eta$ distributions, with the longitudinal exponent $d$ fixed to 0.94 across all centralities.

## Method
The authors assumed a factorized invariant particle distribution $E d^3N/dp^3 = A \exp(-\xi^2/2b) H(p_L)$ where $\xi = \ln((m_T+p_T)/m)$ is the transverse rapidity and $H(p_L) \propto \exp(-c p_L^d)$ describes longitudinal momentum degradation. They fitted the four parameters $A$, $b$, $c$, and $d$ to BRAHMS data for charged particle $p_T$ distributions at $\eta=0$ and $\eta=2.2$ for four centrality bins, then separately fitted PHOBOS pseudo-rapidity distributions for six centrality bins using the same functional form with $d$ fixed to 0.94 and $m$ set to the pion mass.

## Key numbers
- Longitudinal exponent $d$ fixed to 0.94, very close to 1 as expected from p-A collisions (Hwa, 2002)
- For $p_T$ distributions (BRAHMS data, $p_T$ range 0.45 to 4.65 GeV/$c$): parameter $b$ ranges from 0.351 (0-10% centrality) to 0.340 (40-60% centrality); parameter $c$ ranges from 0.0878 (0-10%) to 0.0788 (40-60%)
- For $\eta$ distributions (PHOBOS data, $-5.4 \leq \eta \leq 5.4$): parameter $b$ is nearly constant at 0.540-0.542 across six centralities; parameter $c$ ranges from 0.21 (0-6%) to 0.154 (45-55%)
- Effective mass used for $p_T$ fits: 0.51 GeV (average of proton, kaon, and pion masses); mass used for $\eta$ fits: $m_\pi = 0.139$ GeV
- Beam rapidity $y_{\rm beam} = 5.36$ for $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV

## Context
This work extends the Gaussian transverse rapidity parameterization originally suggested for proton-proton collisions (Carruthers, 1973) and later applied to nucleus-nucleus collisions at RHIC (Nao, 2005), by adding a longitudinal momentum degradation term inspired by p-A studies (Hwa, 2002). The model provides a unified phenomenological description of both $p_T$ and $\eta$ distributions across centralities, enabling calculation of the ratio $R_{PC}$ of per-participant-pair yields between peripheral (35-40%) and central (0-6%) collisions. The approach contrasts with blast-wave models (Tomasik, 2005) which are valid only at low $p_T$ and cannot account for hard parton contributions.
