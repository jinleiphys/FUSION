# An analysis of constraints on relativistic species from primordial nucleosynthesis and the cosmic microwave background

> We present constraints on the number of relativistic species from a joint analysis of cosmic microwave background (CMB) fluctuations and light element abundances (helium and deuterium) compared to big bang nucleosynthesis (BBN) predictions. Our BBN calculations include updates of nuclear rates in light of recent experimental and theoretical information, with the most significant change occuring for the d(p,gamma)^3He cross section. We calculate a likelihood function for BBN theory and observations that accounts for both observational errors and nuclear rate uncertainties and can be easily embedded in cosmological parameter fitting. We then demonstrate that CMB and BBN are in good agreement, suggesting that the number of relativistic species did not change between the time of BBN and the time of recombination. The level of agreement between BBN and CMB, as well as the agreement with the standard model of particle physics, depends somewhat on systematic differences among determinations of the primordial helium abundance. We demonstrate that interesting constraints can be derived combining only CMB and D/H observations with BBN theory, suggesting that an improved D/H constraint would be an extremely valuable probe of cosmology.

## Key claim

The number of relativistic species (Neff) did not change between the time of big bang nucleosynthesis and the time of recombination, but the level of agreement between BBN and CMB constraints depends on systematic differences among determinations of the primordial helium abundance, and the agreement with the standard model of particle physics is conditional on which helium measurement is adopted.

## Method

The authors computed BBN light-nuclide yields using a modified Wagoner/Kawano code with Monte Carlo techniques to propagate nuclear cross section uncertainties, incorporating updated rates for p(n,gamma)d (using pionless effective field theory), d(d,n)3He and d(d,p)3H (using new data from Leonard et al. 2006), and d(p,gamma)3He (adopting an ab initio theoretical cross section from the Pisa group rather than empirical data). They then constructed a likelihood function for BBN theory and observations that accounts for both observational errors and nuclear rate uncertainties, and embedded it within the CosmoMC Markov chain Monte Carlo framework to jointly analyze CMB data from WMAP7 and the South Pole Telescope together with BBN predictions.

## Key numbers

- The updated d(p,gamma)3He cross section from ab initio theory reduces D/H by about 5.6% at the WMAP baryon density compared to previous calculations
- D/H changes by about 10% per unit Neff at the WMAP value of Omega_b h^2
- The difference between the formerly recommended neutron lifetime (885.7 +/- 0.8 s) and the Serebrov value (878.5 +/- 1 s) amounts to Delta Yp = 0.0015, equivalent to Delta Neff = 0.12
- Switching between empirical and ab initio d(p,gamma)3He rates corresponds roughly to Delta Neff = 0.42 in D/H
- The CMB-only constraint from WMAP7+SPT gives Neff = 3.85 +/- 0.62 when BBN theory relation between Yp and Neff is enforced, and Neff = 3.4 +/- 1.0 without a BBN prior on Yp
- The primordial helium abundance measurements considered are: Peimbert et al. (2007) Yp = 0.2477 +/- 0.0029; Izotov and Thuan (2010) Yp = 0.2565 +/- 0.0010 (stat) +/- 0.0050 (syst); Aver et al. (2011) Yp = 0.2573 +/- 0.0033
- The average deuterium abundance from eight extragalactic systems is log(D/H) = -4.556 +/- 0.034

## Context

This work extends previous BBN constraints on Neff from Steigman (2010), Hamann et al. (2010), and Mangano et al. (2011) by incorporating updated nuclear reaction rates, particularly the ab initio calculation for d(p,gamma)3He from Viviani et al. (2000) and Marcucci et al. (2005). It also builds on the CMB analysis of Keisler et al. (2011) by adding a full treatment of BBN nuclear rate uncertainties within a joint likelihood framework. The results enable improved constraints on the number of relativistic species by combining BBN and CMB data, and demonstrate that an improved D/H measurement would be a valuable probe of cosmology independent of uncertainties in the primordial helium abundance.
