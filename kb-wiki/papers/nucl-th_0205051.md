# Excluded Volume Effects in the Quark Meson Coupling Model

> Excluded volume effects are incorporated in the quark meson coupling model to take into account in a phenomenological way the hard core repulsion of the nuclear force. The formalism employed is thermodynamically consistent and does not violate causality. The effects of the excluded volume on in-medium nucleon properties and the nuclear matter equation of state are investigated as a function of the size of the hard core. It is found that in-medium nucleon properties are not altered significantly by the excluded volume, even for large hard core radii, and the equation of state becomes stiffer as the size of the hard core increases.

# Excluded Volume Effects in the Quark Meson Coupling Model

## Key claim
Incorporating excluded volume effects into the quark meson coupling (QMC) model produces an effective repulsion of at most 4 MeV at saturation density, and in-medium nucleon properties change by no more than 2%, even for hard core radii as large as 0.6 fm; the equation of state becomes stiffer as the hard core radius increases, but the formalism remains causal.

## Method
The authors apply a thermodynamically consistent excluded volume prescription, originally developed for ideal gases and extended to relativistic field theories, to the baryonic sector of the QMC model at zero temperature. The excluded volume is implemented by replacing the chemical potential μ with an effective chemical potential μ̃ = μ − v₀P, where v₀ is the hard-sphere volume and P the pressure, and then solving the coupled equations for the scalar mean field σ₀ and the vector mean field ω₀ self-consistently. Two versions of the model are considered: one with a fixed bag constant and one with a density-dependent bag constant following the Jin and Jennings (1996) ansatz.

## Key numbers
- Saturation binding energy E_B = −15.7 MeV at saturation density ρ₀ = 0.15 fm⁻³, used as the fitting condition for coupling constants.
- Hard core radii studied: r = 0.4 fm, 0.5 fm, and 0.6 fm; bag radii studied: R = 0.6 fm and 0.8 fm.
- The effective repulsion from excluded volume at saturation density is at most 4 MeV.
- In-medium nucleon properties (bag radius, nucleon mass, bag eigenvalue) change by at most 2% compared to the case without excluded volume.
- The incompressibility increases by at most 120 MeV due to the excluded volume.
- Meson masses used: m_σ = 550 MeV, m_ω = 783 MeV; free nucleon mass M = 939 MeV.
- The density-dependent bag constant uses g_σ^B = 2.8, following Lu et al. (1998).

## Context
This work extends the QMC model, originally proposed by Guichon (1988) and extensively developed by Saito and Thomas (1995), by incorporating short-range hard-core repulsion via an excluded volume prescription from Gorenstein et al. (1981) and Gorenstein et al. (1995). It addresses the problem of overlapping bags at high density, which was raised by the density-dependent bag constant ansatz of Jin and Jennings (1996) that leads to bag radius increases of 25% at saturation and up to 50% at three times saturation density. The results enable future extensions to finite temperatures and Lorentz-contracted bags, and point toward the need for explicit Bethe-Goldstone-type calculations of short-range correlations in the QMC framework.
