# Multiplicity Moments using Tsallis Statistics in High-Energy Hadron-Nucleus Interactions

> The study of higher-order moments of a distribution and its cumulants constitute a sensitive tool to investigate the correlations between the particle produced in high energy interactions. In our previous work we have used the Tsallis $q$ statistics, NBD, Gamma and shifted Gamma distributions to describe the multiplicity distributions in $π^-$ -nucleus and $p$ -nucleus fixed target interactions at various energies ranging from P$_{Lab}$ = 27 GeV to 800 GeV. In the present study we have extended our analysis by calculating the moments using the Tsallis model at these fixed target experiment data. By using the Tsallis model we have also calculated the average charged multiplicity and its dependence on energy. It is found that the average charged multiplicity and moments predicted by the Tsallis statistics are in much agreement with the experimental values and indicates the success of the Tsallis model on data from visual detectors. The study of moments also illustrates that KNO scaling hypothesis holds good at these energies.

## Key claim

The Tsallis q-statistics model successfully describes the multiplicity distributions and their higher-order moments for hadron-nucleus fixed target interactions at laboratory momenta from 27 GeV to 800 GeV, with the caveat that the model's predictions are in "much agreement" with experimental values rather than exact matches, and the analysis is limited to data from visual detectors (nuclear emulsions).

## Method

The authors used the Tsallis non-extensive statistical model, characterized by an entropic index q, to calculate probability distribution functions for charged particle multiplicities in pion-emulsion and proton-emulsion fixed target interactions. They computed the average charged multiplicity and the normalized moments C_alpha and factorial moments F_alpha up to fifth order from the Tsallis generating function, then compared these values with experimental data from nuclear emulsion detectors at laboratory momenta ranging from 27 GeV to 800 GeV. The power-law dependence of average multiplicity on energy was fitted for both data and model predictions.

## Key numbers

- For pion-emulsion interactions at P_Lab = 525 GeV: Tsallis model average multiplicity <N>_TS = 15.36 ± 0.80, compared to experimental <N> = 15.93 ± 0.22
- For proton-emulsion interactions at P_Lab = 800 GeV: Tsallis model average multiplicity <N>_TS = 19.59 ± 0.63, compared to experimental <N> = 20.02 ± 0.29
- Power law fit for pion-emulsion data: <N> = 2.826 (P_Lab)^0.226
- Power law fit for pion-emulsion Tsallis model: <N> = 2.708 (P_Lab)^0.274
- Power law fit for proton-emulsion data: <N> = 2.281 (P_Lab)^0.331
- Power law fit for proton-emulsion Tsallis model: <N> = 2.276 (P_Lab)^0.335
- The entropic index q was found to be greater than 1 in all cases and increased linearly with energy

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Sharma et al., 2017) which compared negative binomial, gamma, shifted gamma, and Tsallis distributions for hadron-nucleus data but found no clear-cut best choice. The present study focuses exclusively on the Tsallis model to analyze correlations via higher-order moments, confirming that KNO scaling holds at these lower energies (27-800 GeV). The success of the Tsallis model at both these fixed-target energies and at higher LHC energies suggests it is a preferable choice for studying particle production mechanisms, and the authors propose future application to heavy-ion data at center-of-mass energies above 13 TeV.
