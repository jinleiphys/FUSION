# Modeling quark-hadron duality in polarization observables

> We apply a model for the study of quark-hadron duality in inclusive electron scattering to the calculation of spin observables. The model is based on solving the Dirac equation numerically for a scalar confining linear potential and a vector color Coulomb potential. We qualitatively reproduce the features of quark-hadron duality for all potentials considered, and discuss the onset of scaling and duality for the responses, spin structure functions, and polarization asymmetries. Duality may be applied to gain access to kinematic regions which are hard to access in deep inelastic scattering, namely for $x_{Bj} \to 1$, and we discuss which observables are most suitable for this application of duality.

## Key claim

The authors qualitatively reproduce the features of quark-hadron duality for all potentials considered, but caution that their model results should not be compared quantitatively to inclusive electron scattering from a nucleon because the calculation most closely resembles scattering from a B-meson (with an infinitely heavy diquark), and the goal is only qualitative understanding of duality.

## Method

The authors model a constituent quark bound to an infinitely heavy diquark using the Dirac equation with a scalar linear confining potential (b = 0.18 GeV²) and, in some cases, a vector color Coulomb potential. They numerically solve for bound-bound transitions (excitation of the quark to higher bound states) and bound-free transitions (quark knocked into the continuum) for three-momentum transfers up to 12 GeV, then smear the delta-function response functions with a narrow Gaussian for visualization. The spin observables (responses R_T' and R_TL', spin structure functions g₁ and g₂, and polarization asymmetries A₁ and A₂) are extracted from the hadronic tensor components.

## Key numbers

- The scalar confining potential slope is b = 0.18 GeV²
- The constituent quark mass used is m = 258.46 MeV (also tested with m = 10 MeV with no qualitative changes)
- The static Coulomb vector potential uses α_s = 0.181
- The running Coulomb vector potential uses parameters including α_c = 0.118, α₁ = 0.239, α₂ = 0.271, γ₁ = 0.746 GeV, γ₂ = 5.40 GeV
- The bound-free response R_T' converges to its asymptotic value roughly at q = 40 GeV
- The bound-free response R_TL' scales much faster than R_T', with curves coinciding at lower q values
- The asymptotic polarization asymmetry A₁ for the bound-free transition is close to 1 (the pQCD prediction for x → 1)
- The asymptotic value of A₂ is zero

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous modeling efforts (Jeschonnek and Van Orden, earlier papers in the series) which started with an all-scalar model and gradually added proper spin. It contrasts with the non-relativistic constituent quark model approach of Close and Isgur (1990s) and the relativistic one-body equation approach of other groups. The paper aims to provide theoretical guidance for experimental applications of duality, particularly for extracting the neutron polarization asymmetry A₁ⁿ at high x_Bj → 1, which is difficult to access in deep inelastic scattering but could be measured in the resonance region using duality.
