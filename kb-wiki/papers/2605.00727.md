# Hyperon-nucleon interaction through the $K^-d\toπΛN$ reaction

> The hyperon-nucleon interaction is investigated through the final-state interaction in the $K^-d\toπ^-Λp$ reaction. We focus on the $ΛN$-$ΣN$ coupled-channel interaction, which produces characteristic structures around the $ΣN$ thresholds in the $Λp$ invariant mass spectrum. The spin-triplet $ΣN\toΛp$ conversion amplitude is constructed within the $K$-matrix formalism using scattering lengths in the isospin basis. We first examine the dependence of the conversion amplitude on the $ΣN$ scattering lengths and find that the threshold structure is particularly sensitive to the sign of the real part of the $I=1/2$ scattering length. We then calculate the $Λp$ invariant mass spectrum of the $K^-d\toπ^-Λp$ reaction, including the contributions from the background diagrams. The resulting spectra show characteristic structures around the $ΣN$ thresholds, whose shapes depend on the choice of the interaction parameters. These results suggest that the $Λp$ invariant mass spectrum can serve as a useful observable for constraining the $ΛN$-$ΣN$ coupled-channel interaction.

# Hyperon-nucleon interaction through the $K^-d\to\pi\Lambda N$ reaction

## Key claim
The $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum of the $K^-d\to\pi^-\Lambda p$ reaction can serve as a useful observable for constraining the $\Lambda N$--$\Sigma N$ coupled-channel interaction, but only if the sign of the real part of the $I=1/2$ $\Sigma N$ scattering length is correctly determined, as the threshold structure is particularly sensitive to this sign.

## Method
The spin-triplet $\Sigma N\to\Lambda p$ conversion amplitude was constructed within the $K$-matrix formalism using scattering lengths in the isospin basis, with the $\Lambda p$ scattering length fixed to an empirical value. The $\Lambda p$ invariant mass spectrum of the $K^-d\to\pi^-\Lambda p$ reaction was then calculated from the $\Sigma$-exchange diagrams, using the ANL-Osaka DCC model for the elementary $K^-N \to \pi^-Y$ amplitude and the CD-Bonn potential for the deuteron wave function, with background contributions included and kinematical cuts applied to suppress them.

## Key numbers
- $\Lambda p$ scattering length fixed to $a_{\Lambda p} = -1.56^{+0.19}_{-0.22}$ fm, taken from Budzanowski (2010)
- $\Sigma N$ scattering length ranges scanned: $\mathrm{Re}(a_{\Sigma N(I=1/2)}) \in [-4.0,-1.0]$ fm and $[1.0,4.0]$ fm, $\mathrm{Im}(a_{\Sigma N(I=1/2)}) \in [1.0,4.0]$ fm, and $a_{\Sigma N(I=3/2)} \in [-1.0,1.0]$ fm
- Three parameter sets used for spectrum calculation: Param. 1 (Rijken 1999) $a_{\Sigma N(I=1/2)} = 1.68-2.35i$ fm, $a_{\Sigma N(I=3/2)} = -0.25$ fm; Param. 2 (Haidenbauer 2005) $a_{\Sigma N(I=1/2)} = -3.83-3.01i$ fm, $a_{\Sigma N(I=3/2)} = 0.29$ fm; Param. 3 (Haidenbauer 2023) $a_{\Sigma N(I=1/2)} = 2.53-2.64i$ fm, $a_{\Sigma N(I=3/2)} = 0.41$ fm
- Initial kaon momentum: $1.0$ GeV/$c$
- Kinematical cuts applied: $\cos\theta_\pi > 0.9$ for pion angle, $p_n > 200$ MeV/$c$ for neutron momentum

## Context
This work extends previous studies by Iizawa (2022) and Yasunaga (2025) on isospin symmetry breaking in the $\Lambda N$ interaction via the $K^-d\to\pi\Lambda N$ reaction, now focusing specifically on the $\Lambda N$--$\Sigma N$ coupled-channel interaction and the cusp structure at the $\Sigma N$ threshold. The results are intended for comparison with the ongoing J-PARC E90 experiment (Ichikawa 2022), which investigates the $YN$ interaction through the same reaction. The approach enables future experimental constraints on the $\Lambda N$--$\Sigma N$ coupled-channel interaction parameters, particularly the sign of the real part of the $I=1/2$ scattering length, which has been predicted with different signs by different theoretical models such as Rijken (1999), Haidenbauer (2005), and Haidenbauer (2023).
