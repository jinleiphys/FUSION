# Many--Particle Correlations in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> Many--particle correlations due to Bose-Einstein interference are studied in ultrarelativistic heavy--ion collisions. We calculate the higher order correlation functions from the 2--particle correlation function by assuming that the source is emitting particles incoherently. In particular parametrizations of and relations between longitudinal, sidewards, outwards and invariant radii and corresponding momenta are discussed. The results are especially useful in low statistics measurements of higher order correlation functions. We evaluate the three--pion correlation function recently measured by NA44 and predict the 2--pion--2--kaon correlation function. Finally, many particle Coulomb corrections are discussed.

## Key claim

Higher-order correlation functions (three-pion, four-pion, two-pion-two-kaon) can be predicted parameter-free from the measured two-particle correlation function alone, under the assumption that the source emits particles incoherently; any experimental deviation from this prediction would signal new physics such as coherent emission or true higher-order correlations.

## Method

The authors derive N-body Bose-Einstein correlation functions from the symmetrized wavefunction for an incoherent source emitting plane waves, expressing them as sums of products of the Fourier transform F(q) of the source distribution S(x,K). They extract |F(q)| from three-parameter Gaussian fits to two-pion and two-kaon correlation data from the NA44 experiment (S+Pb and p+Pb at 200 AGeV), then compute the three-pion correlation function C3(Q3) by integrating over relative momenta with a delta function enforcing momentum conservation and the invariant momentum variable Q3. They also provide an expansion for slightly deformed sources and a generalization of the Baym-Braun-Munzinger Coulomb correction to N particles.

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic approach of Cramer (1994) for many-body Bose-Einstein correlations and applies it to NA44 data from Rama (1995) and Sanjeev (1995). It provides a parameter-free prediction of three-pion correlations that agrees with the NA44 measurement by Janus (1996), showing no need for additional three-body correlations beyond those from two-body data. The method enables future studies of four-pion correlations and two-pion-two-kaon correlations with larger data samples from Pb+Pb collisions at CERN SPS or RHIC/LHC, and includes a generalization of the two-particle Coulomb correction of Baym and Braun-Munzinger (1996) to N particles.
