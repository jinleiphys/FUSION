# Higher Twist Effects in Parton Fragmentation Functions

> We study twist expansions for parton fragmentation functions based on the definition of the twist as an invariant matrix element of a light-cone, bilocal operator. The results are then applied to a method which might be used to extract higher twist effects in the fragmentation sector using both $e^+ e^-$, and $e^- p$ collisions. We discuss how to apply the later measurements to experiments at the Jefferson National Acceleration Facility.

## Key claim

The paper argues that higher twist contributions to parton fragmentation functions can be systematically expanded using a twist analysis of light-cone bilocal operators, and that the sign of the coefficients in this expansion may produce duality phenomena in the fragmentation sector analogous to Bloom-Gilman duality in structure functions, but only if the coefficients are not positive definite.

## Method

The authors derive a twist expansion for the hadronic tensor in semi-inclusive \(e^+ e^- \rightarrow h+X\) by expressing the quark field bilocal operator in terms of light-cone coordinates and expanding in powers of \(\hat{\xi}^\mu\) (the deviation from exact light-like separation), with each power accompanied by gauge-covariant derivatives. They then extend the analysis to semi-inclusive \(e^- p \rightarrow h+X\) scattering, proposing a method to isolate higher twist fragmentation effects by choosing a sufficiently high \(Q_S^2\) in the structure sector (so that twist-two contributions dominate there) while varying \(Q_F^2\) in the fragmentation sector, and subtracting the calculated twist-two parton structure contributions from the measured cross section.

## Key numbers

- The mass scale \(M_0^2\) in the twist expansion for structure function moments is approximately \((400\text{--}500\ \text{MeV})^2\).
- For the Jlab experiment discussed, the beam energy is \(E_e \sim 5.5\ \text{GeV}\).
- Example calculations of pion production cross sections are presented at \(Q_S^2 = 10.0\ \text{GeV}^2\) with scattered quark energies \(E_{q'} = 5.0\ \text{GeV}\) and \(4.0\ \text{GeV}\).
- The twist expansion for the hadronic tensor is given as \(\hat{W} = \sum_n (M^2/Q^2)^n f_n\), where \(M\) is a generic QCD mass scale.

## Context

This work extends the operator product expansion analysis of Bloom-Gilman duality, originally applied to nucleon structure functions by de Rujula, Georgi, and Politzer (1977) and later quantified by Ji and Unrau, into the parton fragmentation sector. The paper provides a theoretical framework for interpreting the Jlab E00-108 experiment, which studies duality in semi-inclusive \(ep\) reactions. The results enable future experimental extraction of higher twist fragmentation contributions by comparing measurements at different \(Q^2\) values in \(e^+ e^-\) collisions, or by subtracting twist-two structure function contributions in semi-inclusive \(ep\) scattering at Jlab.
