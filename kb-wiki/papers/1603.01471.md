# Charmed partner of the exotic $X(5568)$ state and its properties

> The mass, decay constant and width of a hypothetical charmed partner $X_c$ of the newly observed exotic $X_b(5568)$ state are calculated using a technique of QCD sum rule method. The $X_c=[su][\overline{c}\overline{d}]$ state with $J^{P}=0^{+}$ is described employing two types of the diquark-antidiquark interpolating currents. The evaluation of the mass $m_{X_c}$ and decay constant $f_{X_c}$ is carried out utilizing the two-point sum rule method by including vacuum condensates up to eight dimensions. The widths of the decay channels $X_c \to D_s^{-} π^{+}$ and $X_c \to D^{0} K^{0}$ are also found. To this end, the strong couplings $g_{X_{c}D_{s}π}$ and $g_{X_{c}D K}$ are computed by means of QCD sum rules on the light-cone and soft-meson approximation.

## Key claim

The authors predict the mass, decay constant, and strong decay widths of a hypothetical charmed partner \(X_c\) of the observed \(X_b(5568)\) state, assuming it is a \(J^{PC}=0^{++}\) tetraquark with diquark-antidiquark structure \([su][\overline{c}\overline{d}]\). The predictions depend on the choice of interpolating current and are subject to uncertainties from the QCD sum rule method, with the mass results for the two currents being close but the total widths differing by about 10 MeV in central value.

## Method

The mass and decay constant of the \(X_c\) state were computed using two-point QCD sum rules, including vacuum condensates up to eight dimensions, for two types of diquark-antidiquark interpolating currents (\(J_1^{X_c}\) and \(J_2^{X_c}\)). The strong couplings \(g_{X_c D_s \pi}\) and \(g_{X_c D K}\) were then calculated using QCD sum rules on the light-cone in the soft-meson approximation, and the corresponding decay widths for \(X_c \to D_s^- \pi^+\) and \(X_c \to D^0 K^0\) were derived from these couplings.

## Key numbers

*   Mass of \(X_c\) using current \(J_1^{X_c}\): \(m_{X_c} = (2590 \pm 60)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
*   Mass of \(X_c\) using current \(J_2^{X_c}\): \(m_{X_c} = (2634 \pm 62)\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
*   Decay constant using current \(J_1^{X_c}\): \(f_{X_c} = (0.20 \pm 0.03) \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{GeV}^4\).
*   Decay constant using current \(J_2^{X_c}\): \(f_{X_c} = (0.11 \pm 0.02) \times 10^{-2}\ \mathrm{GeV}^4\).
*   Strong coupling for \(X_c \to D_s^- \pi^+\) (both currents): \(g_{X_c D_s \pi} = (0.51 \pm 0.10)\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) (for \(J_1\)) and \((0.51 \pm 0.11)\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) (for \(J_2\)).
*   Width of \(X_c \to D_s^- \pi^+\): \(\Gamma = (8.0 \pm 2.0)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (for \(J_1\)) and \((8.2 \pm 2.1)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (for \(J_2\)).
*   Strong coupling for \(X_c \to D^0 K^0\): \(g_{X_c D K} = (1.57 \pm 0.34)\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) (for \(J_1\)) and \((1.36 \pm 0.32)\ \mathrm{GeV}^{-1}\) (for \(J_2\)).
*   Width of \(X_c \to D^0 K^0\): \(\Gamma = (55.4 \pm 14.0)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (for \(J_1\)) and \((45.5 \pm 11.4)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (for \(J_2\)).
*   Total width of \(X_c\) (sum of both channels): \(\Gamma_{X_c}^1 \simeq (63.4 \pm 14.2)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (for \(J_1\)) and \(\Gamma_{X_c}^2 \simeq (53.7 \pm 11.6)\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (for \(J_2\)).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QCD sum rule analyses of the \(X_b(5568)\) state (Agaev, Azizi, Sundu, 2016) to its hypothetical charmed partner, \(X_c\). The predictions for the \(X_c\) mass are consistent with an earlier estimate by Chen et al. (2016) of \(m_{X_c} = (2.55 \pm 0.09)\ \mathrm{GeV}\). The results provide a theoretical target for experimental searches for a new four-quark exotic state with quark content \(c s u d\), which would be a charmed analogue of the \(X_b(5568)\) discovered by the D0 Collaboration.
