# Multiparticle correlations and momentum conservation in nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Particle correlations are very actively studied in heavy-ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies. Here, an attempt is made at determining a proper reference for such studies, by taking properly into account the multiparticle correlations induced by the conservation of total momentum in the collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the "minimally-biased background" for multiparticle correlation studies in heavy-ion collisions must include the correlations induced by total momentum conservation, and that using the marginal single-particle distribution rather than the conditional distribution after fixing a trigger particle leads to systematic biases that can mimic or hide genuine dynamical effects.

## Method

The author derives the multiparticle cumulants arising from the global momentum conservation constraint using generating functions of joint probability distributions and a saddle-point integration, assuming a large total number of final-state particles N. The calculation is performed for the general case of anisotropic transverse emission (elliptic flow) by diagonalizing the tensor of mean squared momenta along the principal axes, and the resulting expressions for the two- and three-particle scaled cumulants are given explicitly.

## Key numbers

- The M-particle cumulant from momentum conservation scales as 1/N^(M-1), where N is the total number of final-state particles.
- For Au-Au collisions at RHIC energies, the correction from momentum conservation might be at most 1 or 2% when choosing a high-pT trigger particle.
- The spurious structure from using an isotropic correction instead of the azimuthally-dependent one is of order less than 0.005 for typical RHIC values of N, mean pT^2, and v2.
- The mean second-harmonic transverse anisotropy is defined as v2_bar = mean(px^2 - py^2)/mean(px^2 + py^2), yielding values approximately 0.1 in mid-central Au-Au collisions at RHIC.
- Values of v2(pT) = 0.16 were reported for pT >= 2 GeV/c in minimum-bias Au-Au collisions at RHIC (Adams 2004).

## Context

This work extends earlier semi-quantitative treatments of momentum conservation effects by Foster (1973) and the quantitative two-particle calculations by Danielewicz (1987) and Borghini (2000) that assumed isotropic emission, generalizing them to arbitrary M-particle cumulants with anisotropic flow. The formalism enables precise determination of the momentum-conservation background for correlation studies at RHIC and LHC, which is necessary because the 1-2% correction is of the same magnitude as the dynamical effects measured in studies such as those by Adams (2005).
