# Charmonium spectrum with a Dirac potential model in the momentum space

> We study the charmonium spectrum using a complete one gluon exchange approach based on a phenomenological relativistic $q\bar{q}$ potential model with Dirac spinors in momentum space. We use phenomenological screening factors to include vacuum quantum effects. Our formulation does not rely on nonrelativistic approximations. We fit the lowest-lying charmonia (below the $D\bar{D}$ threshold) and predict the higher-lying resonances of the spectrum. In general, we reproduce the overall structure of the charmonium spectrum and, in particular, we can reasonably describe the $X(3872)$ resonance mass as (mostly) a $c\bar{c}$ state. The numerical values of the free parameters of the model are determined taking into account also the experimental uncertainties of the resonance energies. In this way, we are able to obtain the uncertainties of the theoretical resonance masses and the correlation among the free parameters of the model.

## Key claim

The authors claim that the X(3872) resonance mass can be reasonably described as (mostly) a conventional c-cbar state using a fully relativistic Dirac potential model in momentum space, without relying on nonrelativistic approximations, but only after including phenomenological screening factors to account for vacuum quantum effects.

## Method

The authors constructed a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation using positive-energy Dirac spinors for the quark and antiquark, with vector and scalar interaction terms in momentum space. They included phenomenological screening factors as momentum-dependent functions and solved the resulting integral eigenvalue equation variationally by diagonalizing a 10x10 Hamiltonian matrix built from harmonic oscillator basis functions. The model parameters were determined by fitting the lowest-lying charmonia below the D-Dbar threshold using a bootstrap resampling technique with 1000 samples to propagate experimental uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- Constituent charm quark mass: 1455 (+30, -26) MeV for potential I; 1364 (+46, -63) MeV for potential II
- Effective strong coupling constant: 0.63 (+0.08, -0.06) for potential I; 0.544 (+0.027, -0.060) for potential II
- Fitted eta_c (1^1S_0) mass: 2990 (+6, -12) MeV for potential I; 2981 (+8, -12) MeV for potential II; experimental value 2983.6 +/- 0.6 MeV
- Fitted J/psi (1^3S_1) mass: 3089.3 (+5.7, -6.5) MeV for potential I; 3101 (+13, -10) MeV for potential II; experimental value 3096.916 +/- 0.011 MeV
- Fitted chi_c0 (1^3P_0) mass: 3417.0 (+8.4, -5.9) MeV for potential I; 3416 (+11, -13) MeV for potential II; experimental value 3414.75 +/- 0.31 MeV
- Fitted psi' (2^3S_1) mass: 3674.9 (+5.4, -5.7) MeV for potential I; 3679.2 (+9.2, -9.4) MeV for potential II; experimental value 3686.109 (+0.012, -0.014) MeV
- Screening parameter p_s: 0.698 (+0.065, -0.067) GeV for potential I; 0.91 (+0.11, -0.06) GeV for potential II

## Context

This work extends the semirelativistic quark model approach of De Sanctis (2012) and Cao (2012) by avoiding the Fermi-Breit expansion in powers of p/m and instead working directly in momentum space with Dirac spinors. The inclusion of phenomenological screening factors follows the approach of Li and Gonzalez for incorporating vacuum quantum effects without the full complexity of unquenched quark models as developed by Santo and Ferretti. The bootstrap uncertainty analysis enables rigorous identification of which charmonium states require physics beyond the conventional c-cbar picture, and the method can be applied to other quarkonium systems.
