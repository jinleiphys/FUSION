# Basis Light-Front Quantization Approach to Positronium

> We present the first application of the recently developed Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) method to self-bound systems in quantum field theory, using the positronium system as a test case. Within the BLFQ framework, we develop a two-body effective interaction, operating only in the lowest Fock sector, that implements photon exchange, neglecting fermion self-energy effects. We then solve for the mass spectrum of this interaction at the unphysical coupling $α=0.3$. The resulting spectrum is in good agreement with the expected Bohr spectrum of non-relativistic quantum mechanics. We examine in detail the dependence of the results on the regulators of the theory.

## Key claim
The paper presents the first application of Basis Light-Front Quantization (BLFQ) to a self-bound system (positronium) and shows that, at an unphysical coupling α=0.3, the calculated mass spectrum agrees well with the non-relativistic Bohr spectrum, but only after introducing a counterterm to remove a logarithmic divergence in the ground state energy; the results are explicitly conditional on the use of a regulated effective interaction and a fictitious photon mass μ.

## Method
The authors truncate the Fock space to the lowest two sectors (|e⁺e⁻⟩ and |e⁺e⁻γ⟩) and derive a two-body effective interaction via the Bloch formalism, implementing one-photon exchange while neglecting fermion self-energy. They discretize longitudinal momentum using periodic boundary conditions (parameterized by K) and employ a 2D harmonic oscillator basis (truncated by N_max) for transverse degrees of freedom, then diagonalize the light-cone Hamiltonian matrix using ScaLAPACK. A counterterm is added to remove a small-x singularity from the instantaneous photon exchange, and a separate modification (the “regulated” interaction) is introduced to eliminate a logarithmic divergence traced to a Dirac-delta-like term in the effective potential.

## Key numbers
- Coupling constant used: α = 0.3 (unphysical, chosen to exaggerate binding and hyperfine splitting for numerical testing).
- Basis parameters: K = 19 and N_max = 19 for the representative spectrum; ground state convergence studied with K up to 95 and N_max up to 39.
- Optimal basis energy scale b = 0.4 m_f for ground state convergence; b = 0.1 m_f for the 2³P₂ state.
- Fictitious photon mass μ varied from 0.1 m_f down to 0.01 m_f; the 2³P₂ state becomes bound only for μ < 0.04 m_f.
- Non-relativistic QM predictions used for comparison: singlet mass M = 2 − α²/4 (1 + 63/48 α²), triplet mass M = 2 − α²/4 (1 − 1/48 α²), 2³P₂ mass M = 2 − α²/16 (1 + 43/960 α²), all in units of fermion mass m_f.

## Context
This work extends the BLFQ method, previously applied to single-electron QED problems (Zhao 2011, 2014) and electron GPDs (Chakrabarti 2014), to self-bound two-body systems for the first time. It directly builds on earlier Discretized Light-Cone Quantization (DLCQ) studies of positronium (Pauli and Wolz; Trittman and Pauli) and muonium, which identified the same logarithmic divergence and proposed similar counterterm prescriptions. The results enable future BLFQ applications to hadron physics (e.g., QCD bound states) and provide a benchmark for implementing Fock-sector-dependent renormalization schemes.
