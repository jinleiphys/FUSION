# Wilson fermions at fine lattice spacings: scale setting, pion form factors and (g-2)_mu

> We present an update on our on-going project to compute hadronic observables for Nf=2 flavours of O(a) improved Wilson fermions at small lattice spacings. The procedure to determine the lattice scale via the mass of the Omega baryon is described. Furthermore we present preliminary results for the pion form factor computed using twisted boundary conditions, and report on the implementation of a novel approach to determine the contribution of the hadronic vacuum polarisation to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon.

## Key claim

The authors present preliminary lattice QCD results for the pion electromagnetic form factor and the hadronic vacuum polarisation contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, using Nf=2 flavours of O(a) improved Wilson fermions at fine lattice spacings, but caution that the results are preliminary and based on ensembles where the topological charge autocorrelation problem is not observed, rather than on a full continuum extrapolation.

## Method

The lattice scale is set using the mass of the Omega baryon, determined from two-point correlation functions with Jacobi-smeared and HYP-smeared sources, with an interpolation in the valence strange quark mass to the physical kaon-to-vector-meson mass ratio. The pion electromagnetic form factor is computed using flavour-twisted boundary conditions to achieve fine momentum resolution near Q^2=0, with the form factor extracted from a ratio of correlators where the renormalisation factor of the local vector current cancels. The hadronic vacuum polarisation contribution to (g-2)_mu is computed via a convolution integral of the vacuum polarisation amplitude, using twisted boundary conditions on the connected (flavour-non-singlet) part of the correlator, with the disconnected contribution expected to be suppressed by chiral perturbation theory.

## Key numbers

* Lattice spacing at beta=5.5 determined from Omega baryon mass: a_Omega = 0.053(1) fm
* Lattice spacing at beta=5.3 from scaling relation: a_Omega = 0.069(2) fm
* Reference scale a_ref at beta=5.3: 0.0784(10) fm (from kaon mass at m_pi/m_K=0.85)
* Reference scale a_ref at beta=5.5: 0.0603(15) fm
* Pion masses on N3, N4, N5 ensembles after rescaling: 600, 510, and 410 MeV respectively
* Lattice sizes range from L=1.7 fm to L=3.4 fm, with pion masses from approximately 290 MeV to 600 MeV
* m_pi L values range from 4.2 to 9.0 across all ensembles

## Context

This work is part of the CLS (Coordinated Lattice Simulations) project, extending previous lattice calculations of hadronic observables using Nf=2 Wilson fermions to finer lattice spacings. The pion form factor results are compared with those from the UKQCD collaboration (Boyle et al., 2008) using Nf=2+1 domain wall quarks and from the ETM collaboration (Frezzotti et al., 2009). The approach for computing the hadronic vacuum polarisation contribution to (g-2)_mu follows the strategy outlined by Juttner and Della Morte (2009), building on earlier work by de Rafael (1994) and Blum (2003). The results enable future continuum extrapolations and investigations of hadronic form factors at large momentum transfers, as well as first-principles determinations of a_mu^had to complement the semi-phenomenological dispersion relation approach.
