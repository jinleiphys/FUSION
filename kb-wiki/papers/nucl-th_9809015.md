# Exchange currents in octet hyperon charge radii

> Octet hyperon charge radii are calculated in a chiral constituent quark model including electromagnetic exchange currents between quarks. In impulse approximation one observes a decrease of the hyperon charge radii with increasing strangeness. This effect is reduced by exchange currents. Due to exchange currents, the charge radius of the negatively charged hyperons are close to the proton charge radius.

## Key claim

Exchange currents reduce the impulse-approximation charge radii of charged hyperons by less than 10%, and weaken the SU(3) flavor symmetry breaking that otherwise causes a decrease of charge radii with increasing strangeness; the charge radii of negatively charged hyperons become comparable to the proton charge radius specifically because of pseudoscalar meson cloud contributions.

## Method

The authors calculate octet hyperon charge radii in a chiral constituent quark model using spherical (0s)^3 oscillator states with explicit flavor symmetry breaking (m_s ≠ m_u = m_d) and SU(6) spin-flavor baryon wave functions. The electromagnetic charge form factor is constructed from one-body (impulse approximation) and two-body (exchange current) charge densities via nonrelativistic reduction of Feynman diagrams, with constituent quarks modeled as extended objects via a monopole form factor of size r_γq^2 = (0.6 fm)^2 guided by vector meson dominance. The Hamiltonian includes quadratic confinement, one-gluon exchange in Fermi-Breit form, and pseudoscalar meson exchange (π, K, η) plus a σ-meson.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_N/3 = 313 MeV, with m_u/m_s = 0.55
- Harmonic oscillator parameter: b = 0.61 fm
- Electromagnetic constituent quark size: r_γq^2 = (0.6 fm)^2 = 0.36 fm^2
- Proton charge radius: r_p = 0.807 fm (with r_γq^2 = 0.36 fm^2); for r_γq^2 = 0.42 fm^2 gives r_p = 0.848 fm; for r_γq^2 = 0.49 fm^2 gives r_p = 0.889 fm
- Pseudoscalar meson exchange reduces charge radii of p, Σ^-, Ξ^- by -0.057 fm^2, -0.029 fm^2, and -0.022 fm^2 respectively
- Gluon plus scalar exchange current contributions sum to -0.027 fm^2, -0.020 fm^2, and -0.024 fm^2 for p, Σ^-, Ξ^-
- Spin-flavor matrix element of the pion pair charge operator for Σ^- is 9 times smaller than for the proton
- Experimental proton charge radius: 0.862(12) fm from elastic ep scattering (Simon 1980); 0.890(14) fm from atomic physics (Udem 1997); 0.847(9) fm from dispersion relation analysis (Mergell 1996)
- Neutron mean square charge radius: r_n^2 = -0.113(3) fm^2 (Kopecky 1995)

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model with exchange currents previously applied to octet baryon magnetic moments and magnetic radii (Wagner 1995) and to radiative hyperon decays (Wagner 1998) to the calculation of hyperon charge radii. The approach builds on the nucleon model of Buchmann (1991) where two-body currents explain the neutron's negative mean square charge radius. The results are compared with other theoretical approaches including the semi-bosonized SU(3) Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model (Kim 1996), three-flavor Skyrme models (Kunz 1990; Gobbi 1992; Schwesinger 1992), relativised quark models (Baran 1990; Warns 1991), and the strong-interaction/electromagnetic-radius relation (Povh 1990). The predictions are intended for experimental test at new facilities investigating hyperon charge radii (Heiselberg 1995).
