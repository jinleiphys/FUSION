# Neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung from nucleon-$α$ versus nucleon-nucleon scattering

> We study the impact of the nucleon-$α$ P-wave resonances on neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung. Because of the non-central spin-orbit interaction, these resonances lead to an enhanced contribution to the nucleon spin structure factor for temperatures $T \lesssim 4$ MeV. If the $α$-particle fraction is significant and the temperature is in this range, this contribution is competitive with neutron-neutron bremsstrahlung. This may be relevant for neutrino production in core-collapse supernovae or other dense astrophysical environments. Similar enhancements are expected for resonant non-central nucleon-nucleus interactions.

## Key claim

The nucleon-α P-wave resonances, arising from the non-central spin-orbit interaction, enhance the nucleon spin structure factor for neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung at temperatures T ≲ 4 MeV, making this contribution competitive with neutron-neutron bremsstrahlung if the α-particle fraction is significant.

## Method

The authors calculated the spin-summed matrix element for nα bremsstrahlung using the T-matrix formalism with nα phase shifts from fits to data and optical model calculations, then integrated over phase space to obtain the spin structure factor Ξ(ω) and energy-loss rates Qνν. They compared these results to nn bremsstrahlung calculated from NN phase shifts, using Boltzmann-averaged expressions valid for non-degenerate conditions. The calculations were performed for temperatures between 0.5 and 6 MeV, focusing on the P-wave resonance region near relative momentum p ∼ 35–50 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The nα P-wave resonance occurs at relative momentum p ∼ 35 MeV, corresponding to laboratory energy E ∼ 1 MeV
- For T = 0.5 MeV and T = 1 MeV, Ξ(ω) for nα dominates over nn by several orders of magnitude
- For T = 4 MeV, the nα response is larger than nn for ω/T < 2
- The nα energy-loss rate fit: Qννnα = 8.2·10¹⁴ T² / [(T+0.07)³ + 0.4³] × T⁵ × f_n × ρ_α,12/4 [erg/(s·g)]
- The nn energy-loss rate fit: Qννnn = (7.4·10¹² T + 8.4·10¹³) × T³/(T³+2.4³) × T⁵ × f_n × ρ_n,12 [erg/(s·g)]
- For T = 2 MeV, the ratio of energy-loss rates (with densities scaled out) is ∼ 10
- nα bremsstrahlung dominates over nn for T < 6 MeV when α-particle density is comparable to neutron density

## Context

This work extends the study of neutrino-pair bremsstrahlung beyond the well-studied nucleon-nucleon case (Friman and Maxwell 1979; Hannestad and Raffelt 1998; Bacca et al. 2012) to include contributions from α particles via resonant non-central interactions. The findings are relevant for neutrino production in core-collapse supernovae and proto-neutron star environments where α particles are abundant, and complement recent work by Bartl et al. (2014) on resonant enhancement in NN bremsstrahlung at low densities. The results enable improved modeling of neutrino spectra and fluxes from supernovae, and suggest similar enhancements may occur for other resonant non-central nucleon-nucleus interactions.
