# Anomalous Relativistic Tunneling and Exotic Point Interactions

> We examine one-dimensional quantum scattering of a Dirac particle off relativistic potential barriers. With proper considerations of Dirac sea, existence of anomalous tunneling at zero incident-energy is revealed for a particular type of relativistic potential having same magnitudes and opposite signs for scalar and vector components. It is also shown that this leads to an exotic short range limit of the potentials.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a Dirac particle scattering off a one-dimensional relativistic potential barrier where the scalar and vector components have equal magnitude but opposite signs (the "S = -V" condition), proper treatment of the Dirac sea reveals anomalous full transmission at zero incident energy, in contrast to the generic perfect reflection at zero energy for other potential configurations.

## Method

The authors solve the one-dimensional two-component Dirac equation for a step potential barrier and for a finite-width square barrier, using the Giachetti-Sorace factor to enforce proper boundary conditions that exclude wave function components from the occupied negative-energy Dirac sea. They then examine the low-energy limit of the transmission and reflection amplitudes, and take the zero-range limit (L → 0) of the finite barrier while keeping the volume integrals of the potentials constant, to derive effective point interactions.

## Key numbers

- For the generic case (s + v ≠ 0), as incident kinetic energy w → 0, the transmission amplitude T → 0 and reflection R → 1 (perfect reflection).
- For the special case s + v = 0, as w → 0, the transmission T → 1 and reflection R → 0 (anomalous full transmission at zero energy).
- In the zero-range limit with constant volume integrals v = v̄/L and s = s̄/L, the special case s̄ = -v̄ yields transmission T = 1/(1 + i v̄ K) and reflection R = -i v̄ K/(1 + i v̄ K), where K = √[w/(w+2m)].
- In the non-relativistic kinematics limit (K → k/(2m)), the s̄ = -v̄ case reproduces the "delta-prime" point interaction, while the s̄ = v̄ case reproduces the conventional delta-function point interaction.

## Context

This work extends the resolution of the Klein paradox by Klein (1929) and the resolution of Plesset's no-bound-state problem by Giachetti and Sorace (2007), showing that proper treatment of the Dirac sea has further subtle consequences for low-energy scattering. The "S = -V" condition is known in three dimensions as pseudospin symmetry, which plays an important role in nuclear level degeneracy (Hecht and Adler, 1969; Ginocchio, 1997). The results enable a smooth relativistic construction of the "delta-prime" point interaction, which in non-relativistic quantum mechanics requires singular procedures (Albeverio and Kurasov, 1994; Cheon and Shigehara, 1998) and causes discontinuity in the wave function itself rather than its derivative (Sebastian, 1986).
