# Beta decay and electron capture rates on manganese isotopes in astrophysical environments

> The isotopes of manganese in the mass range A equal to 53 to 63 are abundant in the core material of high mass stars and are believed to be of prime importance in the progression of the pre collapse phases. During these late evolutionary phases, nuclear processes associated with weak interactions, including decay and electron capture (EC) on these isotopes, significantly alter the Ye (lepton to baryon ratio) of the cores composition. The temporal change of this parameter is one of the basic elements to simulate a successful explosion. Hence, the decay and EC rates of manganese (Mn) nuclides may serve as an important input in the simulation codes of core collapse supernova. In this paper, we focus on the study of the weak decay characteristics of 55 63Mn nuclides. The strength distributions of Gamow Teller transitions in the directions of decay and EC for these isotopes were calculated employing the proton neutron quasi particle random phase approximation (pn QRPA) model. The decay half life values of Mn isotopes under terrestrial conditions were computed and compared with measured data and previous calculations. The pn QRPA estimated half lives are in good agreement with the experimental values. The and EC rates were later calculated covering a large range of stellar temperatures (001 30) 109 K and densities (10 1011) g/cm3.

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA model produces Gamow-Teller strength distributions that are more fragmented than those from the Large-Scale Shell Model (LSSM) and Independent Particle Model (IPM), leading to stellar electron capture rates that are larger by up to an order of magnitude and beta-decay rates that are smaller by up to an order of magnitude compared to those earlier calculations, but only when a quenching factor of 0.6 is applied to renormalize the calculated GT strengths.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasi-particle random-phase-approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a deformed Nilsson basis, BCS pairing correlations, and separable residual Gamow-Teller interactions to compute GT strength distributions for manganese isotopes with mass numbers 55 through 63. They calculated terrestrial beta-decay half-lives for 56Mn through 63Mn and then computed stellar beta-decay and electron capture rates over a temperature range of (0.01 to 30) x 10^9 K and density range of 10 to 10^11 g/cm^3, using a quenching factor of 0.6 for the GT strengths.

## Key numbers

- Terrestrial half-life of 56Mn: pn-QRPA gives 10636.32 s, compared to experimental value 9284.04 +/- 0.36 s
- Terrestrial half-life of 57Mn: pn-QRPA gives 86.86 s, compared to experimental value 85.40 +/- 1.80 s
- Terrestrial half-life of 58Mn: pn-QRPA gives 3.12 s, compared to experimental value 3.00 +/- 0.10 s
- Terrestrial half-life of 60Mn: pn-QRPA gives 0.29 s, compared to experimental value 0.28 +/- 0.02 s
- Terrestrial half-life of 62Mn: pn-QRPA gives 0.07 s, compared to experimental value 0.09 +/- 0.013 s
- At high density (rho Ye = 10^11 g/cm^3) and temperature T9 = 30, pn-QRPA EC rates are enhanced by a factor of 3-10 compared to IPM rates
- For 55Mn and 56Mn, pn-QRPA beta-decay rates are smaller than LSSM rates by up to 1 order of magnitude and smaller than IPM rates by up to 2 orders of magnitude
- The pn-QRPA model used force constants chi = 1.18/A^0.7 MeV and kappa = 1.53/A^0.7 MeV, with the ratio gA/gV = -1.2694

## Context

This work extends previous weak rate calculations for pf-shell nuclei by Fuller (1980-1985) using the IPM and by Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000-2003) using the LSSM, specifically for manganese isotopes identified as key electron capture and beta-decay nuclei in pre-supernova simulations by Aufderheide (1994) and Nabi (2021). The pn-QRPA approach avoids Brink's hypothesis used by LSSM and IPM by computing state-by-state GT transition strengths from each excited parent level, and the computed rates are intended as nuclear physics input for core-collapse supernova simulation codes.
