# Forward Rapidity Hadron Production in Deuteron Gold Collisions from Valence Quarks

> We consider hadron production in deuteron gold collisions at RHIC in the forward rapidity region. Treating the target nucleus as a Color Glass Condensate and the projectile deuteron as a dilute system of valence quarks, we obtain good agreement with the BRAHMS minimum bias data on charged hadron production in the forward rapidity ($y=3.2$) and low $p_t$ region. We provide predictions for neutral pion production in minimum bias deuteron gold collisions in the forward rapidity region, $y=3.8$, measured by the STAR collaboration at RHIC.

# Forward Rapidity Hadron Production in Deuteron Gold Collisions from Valence Quarks

## Key claim
Treating the target nucleus as a Color Glass Condensate and the projectile deuteron as a dilute system of valence quarks yields good agreement with BRAHMS minimum bias data on charged hadron production at forward rapidity (y=3.2) and low transverse momentum, but only for minimum bias events and only up to k_t ~ 2 GeV, where the formalism is expected to break down due to gluon radiation and the absence of the Cronin effect in the dipole parameterization used.

## Method
The author calculates the hadron production cross section by convolving a quark-nucleus scattering cross section (derived from scattering of a massless quark on a Color Glass Condensate target) with the LO GRV98 quark distribution function in the deuteron and the LO KKP quark-hadron fragmentation function. The dipole cross section is taken from the Iancu-Itakura-Munier parameterization fitted to HERA data on the proton structure function F2, with the nuclear saturation scale for minimum bias set to Q^2_s = 0.95 GeV^2 at x=0.01 and nuclear radius R_A = 1.1 A^{1/3} R_p.

## Key numbers
- Charged hadron production at y=3.2 in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV is normalized to data at k_t = 1.1 GeV using a K factor of 2.57.
- The nuclear saturation scale for minimum bias deuteron-gold collisions is Q^2_s = 0.95 GeV^2 at x=0.01.
- The saturation scale of the nucleus at y=3.2 is estimated to be Q_s^A ~ 1.5-1.8 GeV (minimum bias).
- The geometric scaling region extends up to Q_es ~ 2.5 GeV, corresponding to hadron k_t ~ z Q_es ~ 2 GeV.
- The deuteron wave function at y ~ 3.2 and p_t ~ 2 GeV probes x_q ~ 0.25 in the deuteron and x_g ~ 4 x 10^{-4} in the target nucleus.
- Isospin symmetry considerations can affect results by 15-20% in the kinematic region covered (k_t > 2 GeV).

## Context
This work provides the first quantitative analysis of forward rapidity hadron spectra at RHIC using the Color Glass Condensate formalism, extending prior qualitative predictions by Kharzeev, Kovchegov, and Tuchin (2003) and by Albacete, Armesto, Kovchegov, Salgado, and Wiedemann (2003) that forward rapidity suppression would be observed. The calculation uses the dipole parameterization of Iancu, Itakura, and Munier (2003) fitted to HERA data, and the nuclear saturation scale from Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2003). The results enable predictions for neutral pion production at y=3.8 to be tested by the STAR collaboration, and open the question of whether the formalism can describe the centrality dependence of the suppression factor measured by BRAHMS.
