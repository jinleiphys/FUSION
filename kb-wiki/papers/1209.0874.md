# Relativistic Chiral Mean Field Model for Finite Nuclei

> We present a relativistic chiral mean field (RCMF) model, which is a method for the proper treatment of pion-exchange interaction in the nuclear many-body problem. There the dominant term of the pionic correlation is expressed in two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) states with particle-holes having pionic quantum number, J^{pi}. The charge-and-parity-projected relativistic mean field (CPPRMF) model developed so far treats surface properties of pionic correlation in 2p-2h states with J^{pi} = 0^{-} (spherical ansatz). We extend the CPPRMF model by taking 2p-2h states with higher spin quantum numbers, J^{pi} = 1^{+}, 2^{-}, 3^{+}, ... to describe the full strength of the pionic correlation in the intermediate range (r > 0.5 fm). We apply the RCMF model to the ^{4}He nucleus as a pilot calculation for the study of medium and heavy nuclei. We study the behavior of energy convergence with the pionic quantum number, J^{pi}, and find convergence around J^{pi}_{max} = 6^{-}. We include further the effect of the short-range repulsion in terms of the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) for the central part of the pion-exchange interaction. The energy contribution of about 50% of the net two-body interaction comes from the tensor part and 20% comes from the spin-spin central part of the pion-exchange interaction.

## Key claim

The relativistic chiral mean field (RCMF) model extends the earlier charge-and-parity-projected relativistic mean field (CPPRMF) model by including two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) states with higher pionic quantum numbers up to J^pi_max = 6^-, which is necessary to capture the full strength of pionic correlations in the intermediate range (r > 0.5 fm) rather than only the surface properties described by the J^pi = 0^- spherical ansatz.

## Method

The authors start from the nonlinear realization of the chiral sigma model Lagrangian and construct a trial wave function consisting of a 0p-0h relativistic mean field ground state plus 2p-2h states with particle-hole pairs coupled to pionic quantum numbers J^pi = 0^-, 1^+, 2^-, 3^+, ..., 6^-. They perform variational minimization of the total energy with respect to both the amplitudes and the Gaussian range parameters of the particle states, and solve the meson field equations self-consistently. The pion-exchange interaction is treated in the direct (Hartree) channel with a monopole form factor for finite nucleon size, and short-range repulsion is included via the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) for the central part.

## Key numbers

- The total energy of ^4He converges as the maximum pionic quantum number approaches J^pi_max = 6^-, corresponding to single-particle orbital angular momentum l_p = 7.
- The Gaussian range parameter of the particle state that minimizes the total energy shifts from 0.83 fm with J^pi_max = 0^- to 0.75 fm with J^pi_max = 6^-.
- The energy minimum for the particle state Gaussian range occurs at approximately b = 0.7-0.8 fm, about half the dominant hole state range of 1.4 fm.
- About 50% of the net two-body interaction energy comes from the tensor part and 20% from the spin-spin central part of the pion-exchange interaction.
- The root-mean-square matter radius of ^4He used to fix parameters is R_rms = 1.488 fm.

## Context

This work extends the charge-and-parity-projected relativistic mean field (CPPRMF) model developed by the same group (Ogawa et al., earlier papers), which only included J^pi = 0^- pionic correlations and showed a rapid decrease of pionic energy per nucleon with mass number. The RCMF model addresses the inconsistency with chiral perturbation theory results for nuclear matter by Kaiser et al. and with variational Monte Carlo calculations of light nuclei by the Argonne-Illinois group (Pandharipande et al.), both of which indicate a volume effect from pion exchange. The method enables systematic study of medium and heavy nuclei with full treatment of pionic correlations beyond the spherical ansatz.
