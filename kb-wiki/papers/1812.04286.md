# Double parton scattering and the proton transverse structure at the LHC

> We consider double parton distribution functions (dPDFs), essential quantities in double parton scattering (DPS) studies, which encode novel non perturbative insight on the partonic proton structure. We develop the formalism to extract this information from dPDFs and present results by using constituent quark model calculations within the Light-Front approach, focusing on radiatively generated gluon dPDFs. Moreover, we generalize the relation between the mean transverse partonic distance between two active partons in a DPS process and the so called effective cross section to include partonic correlations and the so called 2v1 mechanism contribution. Finally we investigate the impact of relativistic effects on digluon distributions and study the structure of the corresponding longitudinal and transverse correlations.

## Key claim

The paper develops a formalism to extract information on the proton transverse structure from double parton distribution functions (dPDFs) and generalizes the relation between the mean transverse partonic distance and the effective cross section sigma_eff to include partonic correlations and the splitting 2v1 mechanism contribution, but the results rely on a pure radiative evolution scheme that neglects non-perturbative sea quark and gluon contributions at the hadronic scale.

## Method

The authors use constituent quark model calculations within the Light-Front approach, specifically the hypercentral quark model (HP) and the harmonic oscillator model (HO), to compute valence-valence dPDFs at the hadronic scale Q0^2 = 0.26 GeV^2, then radiatively generate gluon dPDFs via pQCD evolution at fixed transverse distance b_perp. They derive relations between sigma_eff and the mean squared transverse partonic distance using the effective form factor formalism, and extend these relations to include the splitting 2v1 mechanism and longitudinal correlations.

## Key numbers

- Hadronic scale set to Q0^2 = 0.26 GeV^2, tuned to reproduce known single parton scattering cross sections
- Mean intergluon distance sqrt(<b^2>) for HO model at Q^2 = m_H^2 ranges from 0.365 fm (x1=10^-2, x2=10^-2) to 0.393 fm (x1=10^-4, x2=10^-4)
- Mean intergluon distance sqrt(<b^2>) for HP model at Q^2 = m_H^2 ranges from 0.377 fm (x1=10^-2, x2=0.4) to 0.408 fm (x1=10^-4, x2=10^-2)
- Non-relativistic HO model gives sqrt(<b^2>) = 0.283 fm independent of x values
- Centre-of-mass energy set to sqrt(s) = 13 TeV for LHC kinematics
- J/Psi production gives access to fractional momenta in range 10^-6 less than x less than 10^-2
- Higgs production gives access to fractional momenta in range 10^-4 less than x less than 1
- Lower bound derived: <b^2> >= sigma_eff / (2 pi)
- Upper bound derived: 2 pi / sigma_eff <= 2 / <b^2>

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous investigation in Rinaldi and Ceccopieri (rapid) on the relationship between sigma_eff and mean interpartonic distance, and builds upon constituent quark model calculations of dPDFs from Mel_19, noiold, Mel_23, and noir. The formalism enables extraction of proton transverse structure information from experimental DPS measurements at the LHC, complementing information obtained from generalized parton distribution functions (GPDs) and transverse momentum dependent PDFs (TMDs), and provides a framework for interpreting future measurements of double J/Psi and double Higgs production.
