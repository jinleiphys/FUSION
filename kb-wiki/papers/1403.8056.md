# Breaking and restoration of rotational symmetry on the lattice for bound state multiplets

> We explore the breaking of rotational symmetry on the lattice for bound state energies and practical methods for suppressing this breaking. We demonstrate the general problems associated with lattice discretization errors and finite-volume errors using an $α$ cluster model for $^8$Be and $^{12}$C. We consider the two and three $α$-particle systems and focus on the lowest states with non-zero angular momentum which split into multiplets corresponding to different irreducible representations of the cubic group. We examine the dependence of such splittings on the lattice spacing and box size. We find that lattice spacing errors are closely related to the commensurability of the lattice with the intrinsic length scales of the system. We also show that rotational symmetry breaking effects can be significantly reduced by using improved lattice actions, and that the physical energy levels are accurately reproduced by the weighted average of a given spin multiplets.

## Key claim

Rotational symmetry breaking on the lattice for bound state multiplets can be significantly reduced by using improved lattice actions and by taking the weighted average of a given spin multiplet, but the effectiveness of lattice improvement depends on commensurability of the lattice spacing with the intrinsic length scales of the system, and for large lattice spacings the splitting may increase with higher derivative improvement index if the interaction itself is not also improved.

## Method

The authors solved the Schrodinger equation for two-alpha (8Be) and three-alpha (12C) systems on a cubic lattice with periodic boundary conditions, using an isotropic Ali-Bodmer-type potential with Coulomb repulsion and a three-body Gaussian interaction for 12C. They discretized spatial derivatives using finite difference formulas of order N (N=1 to 4) to control truncation errors, and studied the splitting of J=2 bound state multiplets into E and T2 irreducible representations of the cubic group as functions of lattice spacing a and box size L.

## Key numbers

- For 8Be with artificially strengthened potential, ground state E(0+) = -10.8 MeV and excited state E(2+) = -3.3 MeV relative to alpha-alpha threshold
- For 8Be at a=1.0 fm with N=4, a box size of L=10 fm gives excitation energy error less than 100 keV, while the energy splitting Delta E(2+) still exceeds 1 MeV
- For 8Be, the average distance between alpha particles in the 2+ states is d approximately 2.9 fm
- For 8Be at a=2.5 fm with N=4, the energy splitting reaches as large as 10 MeV, while the multiplet-averaged excitation gap error does not exceed 0.5 MeV
- For 12C, finite volume effects become negligible for L greater than or equal to 18 fm

## Context

This work extends the lattice improvement program of Symanzik (Weisz 1983, Weisz 1984, Luscher 1984) and the analysis of rotational symmetry breaking by Davoudi (2012) to bound state multiplets in nuclear cluster models. It demonstrates that multiplet-averaged excitation gaps, combined with improved dispersion relations, can reduce discretization errors even at large lattice spacing, which is relevant for ab initio lattice calculations of light nuclei using chiral effective field theory (Epelbaum 2010, Epelbaum 2011, Epelbaum 2013). The results enable practical strategies for controlling systematic errors in lattice simulations of nuclear bound states where the continuum limit cannot be taken.
