# Energy reconstruction in the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment

> The Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment aims at measuring fundamental physical parameters to high precision and exploring physics beyond the standard model. Nuclear targets introduce complications towards that aim. We investigate the uncertainties in the energy reconstruction, based on quasielastic scattering relations, due to nuclear effects. The reconstructed event distributions as a function of energy tend to be smeared out and shifted by several 100 MeV in their oscillatory structure if standard event selection is used. We show that a more restrictive experimental event selection offers the possibility to reach the accuracy needed for a determination of the mass ordering and the $CP$-violating phase. Quasielastic-based energy reconstruction could thus be a viable alternative to the calorimetric reconstruction also at higher energies.

# Energy Reconstruction in the Long-Baseline Neutrino Experiment

## Key claim
Quasielastic-based energy reconstruction using a 0-pion event sample yields errors of up to 500 MeV in neutrino event rates, insufficient for LBNE's required 100 MeV resolution; however, a more restrictive event selection (0 pions, exactly 1 proton, and any number of neutrons) reduces the shift to about 100 MeV, making quasielastic-based reconstruction a viable alternative to calorimetric reconstruction even at higher energies.

## Method
The authors used the GiBUU transport model to simulate neutrino-nucleus events on an argon target, treating both initial reaction mechanisms (true quasielastic scattering, 2p-2h excitations, resonance production, and deep inelastic scattering) and final state interactions. They generated full events distributed according to the LBNE flux (peak at 2.5 GeV, tail to 30 GeV) and reconstructed the neutrino energy from muon kinematics alone using the standard quasielastic formula with a binding energy of 0.03 GeV. Two event selections were compared: events with no pions in the final state, and events with no pions, exactly one proton, and any number of neutrons.

## Key numbers
- Required energy resolution for LBNE to resolve the region between first and second oscillation maxima: about 100 MeV
- For 0-pion events: reconstructed event distribution shifted by about 0.5 GeV (500 MeV) toward lower energies relative to true energy
- For 0-pion, 1-proton events: downward shift reduced to about 100 MeV
- Event rate for the restrictive 0-pion, 1-proton sample: about a factor of 3 lower than for the 0-pion sample
- At the peak of the 0-pion distribution: about 50% of events are true quasielastic, about 20% from Delta excitation, with the remainder from 2p-2h and deep inelastic scattering
- For the restrictive sample at the peak: about 80% originate in true quasielastic events
- Flux maximum at about 2.5 GeV with a high-energy tail up to 30 GeV

## Context
This work extends prior studies of quasielastic-based energy reconstruction from lower-energy experiments (MiniBooNE, T2K) to the higher-energy LBNE regime. Previous work by Leitner (2010) identified the "stuck-pion" problem, and studies by Lalakulich (2012), Martini (2012), and Nieves (2012) showed that 2p-2h excitations and other processes cause downward shifts in reconstructed energy. The authors' earlier work (Lalakulich 2012) demonstrated for a carbon target at lower energies that events with 0 pions and exactly 1 proton are dominated by true quasielastic scattering. The present results enable LBNE to consider quasielastic-based reconstruction as an alternative to the calorimetric method, provided the restrictive event selection is applied experimentally. The authors note that similar studies for the calorimetric method exist (Harris 2003, Kordosky 2007, Dytman 2008) but require more accurate event generators.
