# Dihadron Fragmentation Functions and Transversity

> We present preliminary results for an updated extraction of the transversity parton distribution based on the analysis of pion-pair production in deep-inelastic scattering off transversely polarized targets in collinear factorization. Data for proton and deuteron targets by HERMES and COMPASS allow for a flavor separation of the valence components of transversity, while di-hadron fragmentation functions are taken from the semi-inclusive production of two pion pairs in back-to-back jets in $e^+ e^-$ annihilation. The latter data from Belle have been reanalyzed using the replica method and a more realistic estimate of the uncertainties on the chiral-odd interference fragmentation function has been obtained. After encoding this piece of information into the deep-inelastic scattering cross section, the transversity has been re-extracted by using the most recent and more precise COMPASS data for proton target. This picture represents the current most realistic estimate of the uncertainties on our knowledge of transversity. The preliminary results indicate that the valence up component seems smaller and with a narrower error band than in previous extraction.

## Key claim

The updated extraction of the transversity parton distribution using dihadron fragmentation functions in collinear factorization yields a valence up component that is smaller and has a narrower error band than in previous extractions, but this result is preliminary and may alter the previous overall agreement with transversity extracted through the Collins effect.

## Method

The authors extracted the chiral-odd dihadron fragmentation function (DiFF) H1^sphericalangle from Belle e+e- annihilation data for two back-to-back pion pairs using the replica method with 100 replicas to obtain a more realistic uncertainty estimate. They then used this DiFF together with the most recent and more precise COMPASS data for unidentified hadron pairs on a proton target to re-extract the valence components of transversity from the single-spin asymmetry in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, employing a flexible functional form that satisfies the Soffer bound.

## Key numbers

- The fit of the unpolarized DiFF D1 to PYTHIA-generated data used 79 parameters and achieved an average chi-squared per degree of freedom of 1.62 at the starting scale Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- The Artru-Collins asymmetry data from Belle were organized in a (z, M_h) grid of 64 bins.
- The extraction of H1^sphericalangle used 9 parameters at Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2.
- The new transversity extraction covers the x interval 0.0064 ≤ x ≤ 0.28 where experimental data exist.
- The tensor charge for valence up quarks at Q^2 = 2.4 GeV^2 is δu_v = 0.44 ± 0.15.
- The tensor charge for valence down quarks at Q^2 = 2.4 GeV^2 is δd_v = -0.35 ± 0.45.

## Context

This work extends the previous collinear-factorization extraction of transversity by Bacchetta et al. (2012) by using the replica method for DiFF uncertainties and the most recent COMPASS proton data from Adolph et al. (2014). The results update the earlier analysis that had shown overall agreement with the transversity extracted through the Collins effect by Anselmino et al. (2013), and the new smaller up-quark transversity now poses a question about the real size of transversity. The analysis enables future improvements through neural-network-based error analysis and the need for unpolarized cross section data for hadron pair production.
