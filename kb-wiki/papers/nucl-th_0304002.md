# Alpha decay and proton-neutron correlations

> We study the influence of proton-neutron (p-n) correlations on alpha-decay width. It is shown from the analysis of alpha Q values that the p-n correlations increase the penetration of the alpha particle through the Coulomb barrier in the treatment following Gamow's formalism, and enlarges the total alpha-decay width significantly. In particular, the isoscalar p-n interactions play an essential role in enlarging the alpha-decay width. The so-called "alpha-condensate" in Z > 84 isotopes are related to the strong p-n correlations.

# Alpha decay and proton-neutron correlations

## Key claim

The paper shows that proton-neutron (p-n) correlations increase the alpha-decay width by raising the Q-value, which enhances the penetration probability through the Coulomb barrier; the isoscalar (T=0) p-n interactions are identified as the essential component, but the analysis is limited to the static contribution to the Q-value and does not treat the formation amplitude microscopically.

## Method

The authors decompose the experimental alpha Q-value into a p-n correlation component (Q_pn = 4δV^(2)), a pairing component (Q_pair from odd-even mass differences), and a separation-energy component (Q_S). They then compute the alpha-decay constant in the WKB approximation using experimental Q-values and compare it to the value obtained after removing Q_pn, assuming a formation amplitude of unity. The double difference of binding energies δV^(2) is used as the empirical measure of p-n correlations, and its behavior is examined for isotopes with Z=84–100 and N=110–157.

## Key numbers

- The double difference δV^(2) for isotopes with N>126 is about 1.33 MeV, roughly twice the value of about 0.78 MeV for lighter isotopes with N<126.
- The experimental formation amplitude g² for ²¹²Po is 0.020, consistent with the hybrid model of Varga et al. and the BCS approach of Delion et al.
- The Coulomb barrier height for ²¹²Po is about 25 MeV, while the Q-value reaches about 10 MeV above N=128.
- The channel radius used in the WKB calculation is r_c = 1.2A^(1/3) + 3.0 fm.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of p-n correlations via double differences of binding energies (Kaneko 2001, 2002; Brenner 1990; Zhang 1989) to the alpha-decay region above ²⁰⁸Pb. It contrasts with earlier studies (Delion 1997) that found only a small enhancement of the formation amplitude from p-n interactions, and instead shows that p-n correlations affect the penetration factor through the Q-value. The "alpha-condensate" concept of Gambhir (1993) is invoked to interpret the large δV^(2) peaks as evidence of strong alpha-like 2p-2n correlations. The results enable future work to incorporate dynamical p-n correlations beyond the static Q-value contribution and to improve the treatment of the formation amplitude, particularly for deformed nuclei.
