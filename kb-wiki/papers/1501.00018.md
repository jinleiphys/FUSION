# Quark-gluon plasma phenomenology from anisotropic lattice QCD

> The FASTSUM collaboration has been carrying out simulations of N_f=2+1 QCD at nonzero temperature in the fixed-scale approach using anisotropic lattices. Here we present the status of these studies, including recent results for electrical conductivity and charge diffusion, and heavy quarkonium (charm and beauty) physics.

## Key claim

The FASTSUM collaboration presents lattice QCD results for electrical conductivity, charge diffusion, and heavy quarkonium properties in a quark-gluon plasma, but the spectral reconstructions rely on Bayesian methods (maximum entropy method and a novel Bayesian approach) that show qualitative agreement for S-wave states while producing conflicting results for P-wave states in the beauty sector, indicating that the systematic uncertainties from the reconstruction method are not yet fully resolved.

## Method

The collaboration performed simulations of \(N_f=2+1\) QCD at nonzero temperature using a fixed-scale approach on anisotropic lattices with a smaller temporal than spatial spacing, employing improved Wilson-type fermions with a physical strange quark and \(m_\pi\approx400\) MeV. They computed Euclidean correlators on the lattice and extracted spectral functions via the maximum entropy method with Bryan's algorithm and a modified kernel, as well as a novel Bayesian reconstruction method developed by Burnier and Rothkopf (2013). For heavy quarkonium, they used non-relativistic QCD (NRQCD) for beauty quarks and a Schrodinger-equation-based potential extraction for charm quarks.

## Key numbers

- Second generation ensemble: anisotropy \(\xi = a_s/a_\tau = 3.5\), spatial lattice spacing \(a_s = 0.123\) fm, inverse temporal spacing \(a_\tau^{-1} = 5.63\) GeV, pion mass \(m_\pi \approx 400\) MeV, spatial lattice size \(L_s = 2.94\) fm (24 sites) or 3.94 fm (32 sites)
- First generation ensemble: anisotropy \(\xi = 6.0\), \(a_s = 0.162\) fm, \(a_\tau^{-1} = 7.35\) GeV, \(m_\pi/m_\rho = 0.54\), \(L_s = 1.94\) fm (12 sites)
- Pseudocritical temperature \(T_c\) determined from inflection point of renormalised Polyakov loop; temperatures studied range from \(T = 44\) MeV (\(T/T_c = 0.24\)) to \(T = 352\) MeV (\(T/T_c = 1.90\)) for generation 2
- Charmonium S-wave ground state survives up to \(T \approx 1.6 T_c\); P-wave ground state melts close to \(T_c\)
- Charge diffusion coefficient \(D_Q\) has a minimum near \(T_c\) with a value close to the AdS/CFT prediction \(D_Q = 1/(2\pi T)\)
- Beauty S-wave (Upsilon) ground state survives up to the highest temperatures studied (approximately \(T/T_c = 1.90\)); P-wave (chi_b) ground state shows conflicting survival depending on reconstruction method

## Context

This work extends the FASTSUM collaboration's previous studies on anisotropic lattices with \(N_f=2\) flavours (Morrin 2006, Oktay 2010) to \(N_f=2+1\) flavours with a physical strange quark, using parameters shared with the Hadron Spectrum Collaboration (Edwards 2008, Lin 2008). The results for charmonium spectral functions build on earlier quenched calculations (Asakawa 2003, Datta 2003, Ding 2012) and recent 2+1 flavour studies (Borsanyi 2014), while the beauty sector results using NRQCD extend the collaboration's first-generation work (Aarts 2010, 2011, 2012, 2013). The conflicting P-wave results between the maximum entropy method and the novel Bayesian method (Burnier and Rothkopf 2013) mirror similar discrepancies seen in studies using HotQCD ensembles (Kim 2014), and resolving these differences is necessary before quantitative predictions for Upsilon mass shifts and widths can be provided for heavy-ion collision phenomenology.
