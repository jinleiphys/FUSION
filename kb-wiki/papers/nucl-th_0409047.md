# Collective flow of open and hidden charm in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV

> We study the collective flow of open charm mesons and charmonia in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}$ = 200 GeV within the hadron-string-dynamics (HSD) transport approach. The detailed studies show that the coupling of $D,\bar{D}$ mesons to the light hadrons leads to comparable directed and elliptic flow as for the light mesons. This also holds approximately for $J/Ψ$ mesons since more than 50% of the final charmonia for central and mid-central collisions stem from $D+\bar{D}$ induced reactions in the transport calculations. The transverse momentum spectra of $D,\bar{D}$ mesons and $J/Ψ$'s are only very moderately changed by the (pre-)hadronic interactions in HSD which can be traced back to the collective flow generated by elastic interactions with the light hadrons.

## Key claim

The collective flow of open charm mesons and charmonia in Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV is comparable to that of light hadrons, but this result depends critically on the finding that more than 50% of final J/Ψ mesons in central and mid-central collisions originate from D + anti-D annihilation channels rather than primary nucleon-nucleon production, and the collective flow is generated by elastic interactions with light hadrons within the hadron-string-dynamics (HSD) transport approach.

## Method

The authors employed the HSD transport model, which includes formation and multiple rescattering of formed hadrons and unformed leading pre-hadrons, to simulate Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 GeV. Open charm mesons and charmonia were treated perturbatively, with initial production from scaled PYTHIA simulations and subsequent propagation through the full hadronic background, using parametrized cross sections for charm hadron interactions with baryons and mesons, including elastic scattering and charmonium production via D + anti-D annihilation channels with detailed balance.

## Key numbers

- More than 50% of final J/Ψ mesons in central and mid-central Au+Au collisions stem from D + anti-D induced reactions
- Approximately 17 D + anti-D pairs are produced in top central Au+Au collisions
- J/Ψ multiplicity in top central collisions is approximately 5 × 10⁻², lower than D + anti-D multiplicity by a factor of about 300
- J/Ψ suppression in very central collisions relative to very peripheral reactions is about a factor of 2.5
- D, anti-D mesons freeze out on average at 9-12 fm/c
- Elliptic flow v₂ of open charm mesons is ≤ 3% for impact parameter b = 7 fm
- Elliptic flow of charged hadrons reaches up to about 5% at midrapidity for b = 7 fm
- Cross sections for charmonium dissociation with baryons: σ_{c anti-c B} = 6 mb, σ_{J/Ψ B} = 4 mb, σ_{χ_c B} = 5 mb, σ_{Ψ' B} = 5 mb
- Elastic cross section for D, D* mesons with mesons and baryons taken as 10 mb

## Context

This work extends previous HSD transport studies by Bratkovskaya et al. (2003) and Kostyuk et al. (2004) on charmonium production and suppression, now focusing on collective flow properties. The results contradict expectations from earlier work by Cassing et al. (2001) that J/Ψ should decouple early due to low cross sections with light hadrons, and instead show that J/Ψ acquire collective flow through the delayed D + anti-D annihilation mechanism. The predictions provide a baseline for future RHIC experimental measurements; any deviations would indicate additional sources of pressure or strong interactions beyond the standard hadron/string picture, such as explicit partonic interactions in a colored medium.
