# Deep learning for nuclear masses in deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum

> Most nuclei are deformed, and these deformations play an important role in various nuclear and astrophysical phenomena. Microscopic nuclear mass models have been developed based on covariant density functional theory to explore exotic nuclear properties. Among these, we adopt mass models based on the relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory (RCHB) with spherical symmetry and the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with axial symmetry to study the effects of deformation on the abundances produced during the rapid neutron-capture process (r-process). Since the DRHBc mass table has so far been completed only for even-Z nuclei, we first investigate whether a Deep Neural Network (DNN) can be used to extend the DRHBc mass table by focusing on nuclear binding energies. To incorporate information about odd-odd and odd-even isotopes into the DNN, we also use binding energies from AME2020 as a training set, in addition to those from the DRHBc mass table for even-Z nuclei. After generating an improved mass table through the DNN study, we conduct a sensitivity analysis of r-process abundances to deformation or mass variations using the RCHB$^\star$ and DRHBc$^\star$ mass tables (where $\star$ indicates that the mass table is obtained from the DNN study). For the r-process sensitivity study, we consider magnetohydrodynamic jets and collapsar jets. Our findings indicate that r-process abundances are sensitive to nuclear deformation, particularly within the mass range of $A=80-120$.

## Key claim

The authors show that a Deep Neural Network (DNN) can extend the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) mass table to odd-odd and odd-even nuclei, and that the resulting mass differences between spherical (RCHB*) and deformed (DRHBc*) models produce up to two orders of magnitude variation in r-process yields in the mass range A=80-120, but only under specific astrophysical conditions (MHD jets) and not in collapsar jets where fission recycling dominates.

## Method

The authors trained a DNN with five inputs (proton number Z, neutron number N, pairing delta, and separate proton and neutron shell corrections P_n, P_p) using binding energies from the even-Z DRHBc mass table and AME2020 experimental data. They then generated complete mass tables (RCHB* and DRHBc*) and performed r-process nucleosynthesis calculations using the Libnucnet reaction network code with JINA-REACLIB reaction rates, comparing yields from magnetohydrodynamic (MHD) jet and collapsar jet astrophysical models with and without fission recycling.

## Key numbers

- RMS deviation between DRHBc* (five-input DNN) and AME2020: 0.842 MeV, compared to 1.433 MeV for the even-Z DRHBc mass table alone
- RMS deviation between DRHBc* and newly measured neutron-rich isotopes from Xian (2024): 0.725 MeV
- Original RCHB mass table RMS deviation from AME2020: 7.980 MeV
- Maximum mass difference between RCHB* and DRHBc*: 6.3 MeV for ^93As (deformed nucleus with beta_2 = 0.316 in FRDM(2012))
- Pairing strength in DRHBc: -325.0 MeV fm^3; in RCHB: -342.5 MeV fm^3
- Q-values for ^90As(n,gamma)^91As: 1.04 MeV (RCHB*) and 6.22 MeV (DRHBc*)
- Q-values for ^96As(n,gamma)^97As: 4.41 MeV (RCHB*) and -1.13 MeV (DRHBc*)

## Context

This work extends the DRHBc mass table program (DRHBc Mass Table Collaboration, 2020, 2022, 2024) which had previously completed masses only for even-Z nuclei, using a DNN approach similar to machine learning studies by Niu (2018), Mumpower (2023), and others. The sensitivity study builds on prior r-process mass sensitivity work by Mumpower (2015) using +/-0.5 MeV variations and Hao (2023) using +/-1 MeV variations, but here the mass differences between spherical and deformed models reach 5-6 MeV. The results enable future complete DRHBc mass table studies of the relationship between nuclear deformation and the solar r-process abundance peak near A=104.
