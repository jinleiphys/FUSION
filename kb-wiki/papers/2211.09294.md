# Exotic states with triple charm

> In this work we investigate the possibility of the formation of states from the dynamics involved in the $D^*D^*D^*$ system by considering that two $D^*$'s generate a $J^P=1^+$ bound state, with isospin 0, which has been predicted in an earlier theoretical work. We solve the Faddeev equations for this system within the fixed center approximation and find the existence of $J^P=0^-$, $1^-$ and $2^-$ states with charm $3$, isospin $1/2$, masses $\sim 6000$ MeV, which are manifestly exotic hadrons, i.e., with a multiquark inner structure.

# Exotic states with triple charm

## Key claim

The authors predict the existence of bound three-body states in the $D^*D^*D^*$ system with charm 3, isospin 1/2, and spin-parities $0^-$, $1^-$, and $2^-$, but only the $0^-$ state is considered likely to be observable experimentally because its signal in the three-body scattering matrix is about six times stronger than the other two states.

## Method

The authors solve the Faddeev equations within the fixed center approximation (FCA), treating the $D^*D^*D^*$ system as a $D^*$ meson scattering off a bound $D^*D^*$ cluster. The two-body $D^*D^*$ interaction is taken from earlier work (Dai et al., 2021) using an extension of the local hidden gauge approach with vector meson exchange, where the $D^*D^*$ system forms a $J^P=1^+$ bound state with isospin 0. The three-body $T$-matrix is computed for different cut-off values $q_\text{max}$ (450, 550, and 650 MeV) used to regularize the two-body loops, and the effect of the cluster width is also incorporated.

## Key numbers

* For $q_\text{max}=450$ MeV, the $J^P=0^-$ state has mass 6006.5 MeV and width 46.6 MeV, about 19 MeV below the three-body $D^*D^*D^*$ threshold.
* For $q_\text{max}=450$ MeV, the $J^P=1^-$ state has mass 6014.1 MeV and the $J^P=2^-$ state has mass 6015.4 MeV (widths not meaningfully determined due to weak signals).
* Varying $q_\text{max}$ from 450 to 650 MeV shifts the $J^P=0^-$ mass from 6006.5 MeV to 5914.5 MeV and increases its width from 46.6 MeV to 136.0 MeV.
* Including the cluster width ($\Gamma_B=29.54$ MeV for $q_\text{max}=450$ MeV) increases the $J^P=0^-$ width to 57.3 MeV at mass 6004.5 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the study of multi-charm exotic hadrons following the experimental discovery of the doubly-charmed $T_{cc}$ state by LHCb (2021). It builds on earlier predictions for $D^*D^*$ bound states (Molina et al., 2010; Dai et al., 2021) and on studies of multi-rho systems (Roca et al., 2010). The results are compared with those of Luo et al. (2021), who studied the $D^*D^*D^*$ system using a one-boson exchange model and the Gaussian expansion method; the present work finds qualitative agreement for $I=1/2$ states but obtains a more bound $0^-$ state relative to the $1^-$ and $2^-$ states, and does not find a $3^-$ state. The authors argue that the flavor conservation of strong interactions prevents these triple-charm states from decaying into systems with fewer mesons, potentially making them relatively narrow and experimentally accessible.
