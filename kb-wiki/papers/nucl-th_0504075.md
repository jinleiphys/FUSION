# Coordinates, modes and maps for the density functional

> Special bases of orthogonal polynomials are defined, that are suited to expansions of density and potential perturbations under strict particle number conservation. Particle-hole expansions of the density response to an arbitrary perturbation by an external field can be inverted to generate a mapping between density and potential. Information is obtained for derivatives of the Hohenberg-Kohn functional in density space. A truncation of such an information in subspaces spanned by a few modes is possible. Numerical examples illustrate these algorithms.

# Coordinates, modes and maps for the density functional

## Key claim

The paper defines special bases of orthogonal polynomials that allow expansions of density and potential perturbations under strict particle number conservation, enabling a constructive mapping between density and potential for the Hohenberg-Kohn functional. The authors show that a truncation of the functional information to subspaces spanned by a few modes is possible, but caution that this truncation is only valid for small perturbations in the linear response regime, with curvature effects appearing for finite perturbations.

## Method

The authors develop a formalism using orthogonal polynomial bases (constructed from Hermite polynomials modified to satisfy the constraint of zero integral) to expand both density perturbations and potential perturbations. They derive the functional derivatives δρ/δu and δF/δρ through particle-hole expansions, first for independent fermions (Section II), then within the Hartree-Fock approximation with two-body forces (Section III), and finally for the exact correlated case using Brillouin-Wigner perturbation theory (Section IV). Numerical tests are performed on one-dimensional toy models with Z=4 fermions in a harmonic oscillator potential, where the mapping between potential coordinates u_α and density coordinates ρ_α is computed and visualized.

## Key numbers

- Z=4 fermions are used in the numerical toy model for the harmonic oscillator Hamiltonian H_0 = Σ_i (p_i²/2 + r_i²/2)
- Polynomials Γ_α are generated up to degree 100 for the basis expansions
- The particle index I runs up to 100 and the α index runs up to 100 for precalculated matrix elements D
- A 15-parameter polynomial ansatz for F_app is fitted to 26 exact numerical values for the Z=4 system
- The fitted F_app has a minimum at the origin with value 8.0005176 (the true minimum of the functional)

## Context

This work extends the Hohenberg-Kohn (HK) density functional formalism (Hohenberg and Kohn, 1964) and its finite temperature generalization by Mermin (1965), which lack constructive algorithms after their existence theorems. The paper builds upon the Thomas-Fermi approach and related developments such as those by Bethe and Weizsacker (1935) and Kohn and Sham (1965), as well as applications of Skyrme forces to nuclear densities. The formalism enables testing of phenomenological functionals by calculating quantities such as ũ G D̃ from microscopic wave functions and energies for simple systems, and provides a framework for constructing approximate functionals through truncation to few density modes.
