# Nilsson parameters kappa and mu in the relativistic mean field models

> Nilsson parameters kappa and mu have been studied in the framework of the relativistic mean field (RMF) models. They are used to investigate the reason that the RMF models give a relatively well prediction of the spin-orbit splitting, but fail to reproduce the placement of the states with different orbital angular momenta. Instead of the relatively small effective mass M*, the independence of M* from the angular momentum l is found to be the reason.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the failure of relativistic mean field (RMF) models to correctly reproduce the relative placement of single-particle states with different orbital angular momenta is not due to the relatively small effective mass (M*/m ≈ 0.6) itself, but rather because the effective mass is independent of the orbital angular momentum l.

## Method

The authors computed single-particle spectra (SPS) for 208Pb, 132Sn, and 40Ca using four RMF parameter sets (NL-Z, NL-Z2, NL-VT1, and PC-F1) and compared them with experimental data from Isakov. They derived a non-relativistic limit of the RMF Hamiltonian to obtain effective central, spin-orbit, and orbit-orbit potentials in a Nilsson-like form, then extracted the Nilsson parameters κ and μ as functions of radial position. To test the role of effective mass, they generated additional parameter sets with M*/m values ranging from 0.67 to 0.80, both by varying only the scalar coupling constant and by refitting all coupling constants to nuclear matter properties.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb proton spin-orbit splittings, all parameter sets show only 15% deviation from experimental values, except for the 3p states of PC-F1.
- For 208Pb neutron spin-orbit splittings, only the 2f states deviate by less than 10% from experiment; NL-VT1 has four gaps with deviations under 20% but one gap (1i states) exceeding 20%.
- The relative position α_pf for 208Pb protons deviates by 60% from experiment; for neutrons, α_pf deviates by about 100%.
- For 132Sn, the relative position between 2d and 2f neutron levels (α_df^n) deviates by almost 40% from experimental data.
- For 40Ca, the relative positions between 1d and 1f levels for both neutron and proton (α_df) deviate by less than 20% from experiment.
- The RMF models have M*/m ≈ 0.6; the maximum value of Δ (sum of scalar and vector potentials) for 208Pb neutrons is more or less 400 MeV.
- When M*/m increases to 0.77, the RMF κ value at the Fermi surface coincides with the Nilsson model prediction.

## Context

This work extends earlier findings by Rutz (2000) that RMF models poorly reproduce the relative placement and ordering of single-particle states, while confirming that spin-orbit splittings are reasonably predicted. The authors connect their analysis to the pseudo-spin symmetry study by Chen (2003) which examined V_LS and V_LL from RMF models. The paper shows that, unlike the Nilsson model where κ and μ are position-independent constants, in RMF models both parameters depend on radial position, and that the spatial dependence of V_LS^eff is a genuine feature of self-consistent RMF models not present in non-relativistic Skyrme Hartree-Fock models. The results suggest that future calculations should include exchange effects and/or correlations beyond mean-field to address the l-independence of M*.
