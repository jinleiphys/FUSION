# The isovector effective charge and the staggering of the 2+ to 0+ transition probabilities in the Titanium isotopes

> In an effort to understand the magical status of N=32 and N=34 at the very neutron rich edge, experiments have been carried out in the Titanium isotopes up to A=56. The measured staggering of the B(E2)'s is not reproduced by the shell model calculations using the best effective interactions. We argue that this may be related to the choice of the isovector effective charge and to the value of the N=34 neutron gap.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the puzzling disagreement between shell-model calculations and experimental B(E2) values in Titanium isotopes can be resolved by using a nonzero isovector effective charge, specifically a larger neutron effective charge (qν = 0.8 e) and a reduced proton effective charge (qπ = 1.15 e), rather than the standard isoscalar-only choice (qπ = 1.5 e, qν = 0.5 e). This choice also sheds light on the debated N=34 sub-shell closure, as the two interactions (KB3G and GXPF1) give diverging predictions for ⁵⁶Ti depending on their N=34 gap.

## Method

Full pf-shell model calculations were performed for even Titanium isotopes from A=48 to A=56 using two effective interactions: KB3G and GXPF1 (and its variant GXPF1A). The B(E2) transition probabilities for the 2⁺ → 0⁺ transitions were computed with two different sets of effective charges: the standard set (qπ = 1.5 e, qν = 0.5 e, giving zero isovector charge) and a new set (qπ = 1.15 e, qν = 0.8 e, giving an isovector effective charge of +0.65 e). The results were compared to experimental data from Dinca, Janssens et al. (2004).

## Key numbers

- B(E2) values for ⁴⁸Ti, ⁵⁰Ti, ⁵²Ti, and ⁵⁴Ti calculated with KB3G and GXPF1 are identical regardless of which effective charge set is used.
- With the new effective charges (qπ = 1.15 e, qν = 0.8 e), the experimental trend and even the experimental values are much better reproduced across the isotope chain.
- The isovector effective charge of +0.65 e is taken from a recent experimental and shell-model analysis of the mirror pair ⁵¹Fe-⁵¹Mn by Rietz et al. (2004).
- The standard isoscalar effective charge in this region is +1.0 e, with the isovector component taken as zero in previous calculations.
- Dufour and Zuker obtained an isovector effective charge of +0.2 e, in contrast to the +0.65 e value used here.

## Context

This work extends the experimental study of Dinca, Janssens et al. (2004) on Titanium isotopes, which found that shell-model calculations with the GXPF1 and GXPF1A interactions could not reproduce the staggering of B(E2) values. The paper also builds on earlier work on the N=32 sub-shell closure in Calcium isotopes by Klotz et al. and on N=34 isotones by Liddick et al. The analysis resolves a controversy between the GXPF1 interaction (which predicts a large N=34 gap) and KB3G (which does not), showing that the choice of isovector effective charge is critical for interpreting the N=34 sub-shell closure. This enables future studies to use the new effective charges to probe shell structure in neutron-rich nuclei.
