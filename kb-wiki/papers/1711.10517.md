# Effective field theory for one-dimensional nonrelativistic particles with contact interaction

> We consider a field theory describing interacting nonrelativistic particles of two types, which map to each other under time reversal, with point-like interaction. We identify a new type of interaction which depends on the relative velocity between the particles. We compute the renormalization group running of the coupling constants and find a fixed point and a fixed line. We show that the scattering amplitudes can be expressed in terms of three parameters. The result matches with a quantum mechanical analysis and represents the most general point-like interaction consistent with unitarity and time reversal invariance.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a new type of Galilean-invariant, velocity-dependent contact interaction for one-dimensional nonrelativistic particles of two types that map to each other under time reversal, and shows that the most general point-like interaction consistent with unitarity and time reversal invariance can be expressed in terms of three parameters (two real numbers and one phase), rather than the single coupling constant of the familiar delta-function potential.

## Method

The authors construct a Lagrangian for two species of nonrelativistic particles in one spatial dimension containing three interaction terms: a standard density-density coupling (λ), a velocity-dependent coupling (a), and a derivative coupling (c). They compute the full two-body scattering amplitudes (transmission and reflection) by summing all bubble diagrams in the center-of-momentum frame, using a sharp momentum cutoff Λ for regularization. They then derive exact renormalization group beta functions for the dimensionless couplings by demanding cutoff independence of the scattering amplitudes, and solve the RG flow equations to find fixed points and the general form of the scattering amplitudes in terms of three integration constants (μ, α, γ).

## Key numbers

- The coupling λ has dimension 1 (momentum), a is dimensionless, and c has dimension -1.
- The one-loop bubble integral B₀ = 1/(2√E) converges, while B₂ = -iΛ/π + √E/2 diverges linearly with cutoff Λ.
- The beta functions are: β_λ̃ = -λ̃ + ã²/π, β_ã = ãc̃/π, β_c̃ = c̃²/π + c̃.
- The isolated nontrivial fixed point occurs at (λ̃, ã, c̃) = (0, 0, -π).
- The fixed line is given by (λ̃, ã, c̃) = (ã²/π, ã, 0).
- At the isolated fixed point, the reflection amplitude R = 1 and transmission T = 0 (total reflection with phase +1).
- On the fixed line, T = (1 + iα)/(1 - iα) and R = 0 (total transmission with phase shift δ = 2 arctan α).
- Without fine tuning, the infrared behavior gives T = 0, R = -1 (total reflection with phase -1, as for a hard wall).

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach to nonrelativistic systems, previously applied to nucleons (Weinberg 1990, Kaplan 1998) and fermions at unitarity (O'Hara 2002, Regal 2004, Zwierlein 2004), to the one-dimensional case. It builds on a recent suggestion by Geracie (2016) of a new Galilean-invariant velocity-dependent interaction, and provides an effective field theory interpretation of the extensive mathematical literature on self-adjoint extensions of the 1D Hamiltonian (Coutinho 1997, Albeverio 2004). The results enable future exploration of few-body and many-body aspects of one-dimensional systems with nonrelativistic conformal symmetry, which previously had only been found in a theory with four-particle contact interaction (Nishida 2009).
