# Thermal vorticity and spin polarization in heavy-ion collisions

> The hot and dense matter generated in heavy-ion collisions contains intricate vortical structure in which the local fluid vorticity can be very large. Such vorticity can polarize the spin of the produced particles. We study the event-by-event generation of the so-called thermal vorticity in Au + Au collisions at energy region $\sqrt{s}=7.7-200$ GeV and calculate its time evolution, spatial distribution, etc., in a multiphase transport (AMPT) model. We then compute the spin polarization of the $Λ$ and $\barΛ$ hyperons as a function of $\sqrt{s}$, transverse momentum $p_T$, rapidity, and azimuthal angle. Furthermore, we study the harmonic flow of the spin, in a manner analogous to the harmonic flow of the particle number. The measurement of the spin harmonic flow may provide a way to probe the vortical structure in heavy-ion collisions. We also discuss the spin polarization of $Ξ^0$ and $Ω^-$ hyperons which may provide further information about the spin polarization mechanism of hadrons.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the thermal vorticity generated in heavy-ion collisions can be studied event-by-event using the AMPT model, and that the resulting spin polarization of Λ and anti-Λ hyperons can be computed as a function of collision energy, transverse momentum, rapidity, and azimuthal angle. The authors propose that measuring "spin harmonic flow" coefficients (analogous to standard harmonic flow coefficients for particle number) may provide a way to probe the detailed vortical structure of the quark-gluon plasma, though they note a puzzling discrepancy between their theoretical results and experimental data for the azimuthal dependence of the polarization.

## Method
The authors used the string-melting version of the AMPT (A MultiPhase Transport) model to simulate Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV, tracking each parton's position and momentum during the system evolution. They computed the energy-momentum tensor using a smearing function method, defined the flow velocity as the eigenvector of this tensor (Landau-Lifshitz frame), extracted local temperature from energy density, and then calculated the thermal vorticity tensor and the resulting spin polarization of hyperons using the formula from Becattini et al. (2013) that relates spin to thermal vorticity at local thermal equilibrium.

## Key numbers
- The thermal vorticity component ⟨\bar{ϖ}_{zx}⟩ at mid-rapidity was computed for Au+Au collisions at √s = 19.6, 62.4, and 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 9 fm, showing decreasing vorticity with increasing collision energy.
- The global spin polarization P_y of Λ and anti-Λ hyperons was computed for the centrality range 20-50% and rapidity window |Y| < 1 across √s = 7.7 to 200 GeV, with results consistent with STAR experimental data except at 7.7 GeV.
- The spin polarization of Ξ^0 and Ω^- hyperons was computed for the same centrality and rapidity range, showing similar energy dependence to Λ and anti-Λ, with the ordering P(Λ) > P(Ξ^0) > P(Ω^-) attributed to mass ordering (m_Λ < m_Ξ^0 < m_Ω^-) and spin (Ω^- has spin 3/2 while Λ and Ξ^0 have spin 1/2).
- The first and second spin harmonic coefficients f_1 and f_2 were computed as functions of rapidity for √s from 19.6 to 200 GeV with impact parameter b = 9 fm, showing f_1 is odd in rapidity and f_2 is even in rapidity.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of vorticity in heavy-ion collisions, particularly the measurement of global Λ and anti-Λ spin polarization by STAR (2017, 2018) which revealed vorticity of order 10^21 s^{-1}. The authors build on the theoretical framework of Becattini et al. (2013, 2015, 2016) relating thermal vorticity to spin polarization, and on earlier AMPT simulations of kinematic vorticity by Deng et al. (2016). The paper introduces the concept of spin harmonic flow coefficients as a new observable to probe the spatial vortical structure of the quark-gluon plasma, analogous to how standard harmonic flow coefficients v_n probe the initial spatial anisotropy. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted smoke-loop type vortical structure at finite rapidity and the quadrupolar pattern in spin polarization, though the authors note a puzzling discrepancy between their theoretical azimuthal dependence of P_y and the experimental data from STAR (2018) that requires further investigation.
