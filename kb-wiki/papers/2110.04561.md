# Singly heavy baryons in nuclear matter from an SU(3) chiral soliton model

> We investigate how the masses of the singly heavy baryons undergo changes in nuclear matter, based on a medium-modified SU(3) chiral soliton model. Having explained the bulk properties of nuclear matter, we discuss the masses of the singly heavy baryons in nuclear matter. We generalize the vector-meson Lagrangian including the heavy-meson soliton interaction. The mass spectrum of the singly heavy baryon are obtained with the effects of explicit SU(3) symmetry breaking considered as a perturbation. The results show that the mass of the singly heavy baryon mass in nuclear medium is rather sensitive to the medium modifications of the heavy meson mass.

## Key claim

The mass shifts of singly heavy baryons in nuclear matter are highly sensitive to the medium modification of the heavy meson (D-meson) mass, but the sign and magnitude of the shift depend on an undetermined parameter \(C_4\) that governs whether the D-meson mass increases, stays constant, or decreases in the medium.

## Method

The authors generalize a previously developed medium-modified SU(3) chiral soliton model with vector-meson degrees of freedom to include heavy mesons via a bound-state approach, where a singly heavy baryon emerges as a bound state of a heavy meson and an SU(3) baryon soliton. The bulk properties of isospin-symmetric infinite nuclear matter (volume energy, compressibility, pressure) are fitted by introducing three density-dependent medium functions for the light meson sector, with parameters fixed to reproduce a volume energy of -16 MeV and compressibility \(K_0 = 220\) MeV at normal nuclear density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\). The heavy meson mass is parametrized linearly with density via a free parameter \(C_4\), and the mass spectrum of the singly heavy baryons (antitriplet and sextet representations) is computed with explicit SU(3) symmetry breaking treated as a perturbation.

## Context

This work extends the medium-modified SU(2) chiral soliton model developed by Jung et al. (2012) to the SU(3) flavor sector with heavy mesons, following the bound-state approach of Momen et al. (1993) for singly heavy baryons. It relates the in-medium mass shifts of singly charmed baryons directly to the poorly known medium modification of the D-meson mass, a connection that has not been explored in previous studies of heavy baryons in nuclear matter by Tsushima et al. (2002), Wang et al. (2011), or more recent works by Yasui et al. (2018, 2019) and Haidenbauer et al. (2020). The results provide a framework to guide future experiments on charmed baryons in nuclei, but the strong dependence on the unknown parameter \(C_4\) means that definitive predictions require independent constraints on the D-meson mass in nuclear matter.
