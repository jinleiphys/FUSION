# Do Minimal Parity Solutions to the Strong CP Problem Work?

> One class of solutions to the strong CP problem relies on generalized parity symmetries. A minimal model of this type, constructed by Babu and Mohapatra and based on a softly broken parity symmetry, has the remarkable property that effective QCD vacuum angle $\barθ$ vanishes up to one-loop order. We compute the leading two-loop contributions to $\barθ$ in this model and estimate subleading contributions. In contrast to previous estimates, we argue that $\bar θ$ is not suppressed by the weak scale, and we find contributions of order $10^{-3}$-$10^{-2}$ multiplying unknown mixing angles and phases. Thus the model does not generically address the strong CP problem, but it might be made consistent with $\barθ<10^{-10}$ in some corners of parameter space. For such non-generic parameters, $\barθ$ is still likely to be just below present bounds, and therefore provides the dominant source of hadronic EDMs. We discuss the resulting EDM phenomenology.

## Key claim

The authors compute the leading two-loop contributions to the QCD vacuum angle \(\bar\theta\) in the Babu-Mohapatra model of softly broken generalized parity and find corrections of order \(10^{-3}\)–\(10^{-2}\) multiplying unknown mixing angles and phases, contradicting earlier estimates that \(\bar\theta\) would be suppressed by the weak scale. The model does not generically solve the strong CP problem, but might be made consistent with \(\bar\theta<10^{-10}\) in some corners of parameter space.

## Method

The authors computed two-loop corrections to the effective \(6\times6\) Yukawa matrices in the symmetric phase (positive Higgs mass parameters) using FeynArts and an in-house Mathematica program, working in the isosinglet mass basis with diagonal, real isosinglet mass matrices. They expanded the loop functions in the seesaw limit where the weak scale \(v\) is much smaller than the mirror weak scale \(v'\) and the isosinglet masses \(|\mathcal{M}_{u,d}|\), and verified that the results are gauge-parameter independent and UV/IR finite at lowest nonvanishing order. The leading contributions arise from diagrams involving internal Higgs lines, while diagrams with gluons cancel between irreducible and reducible terms.

## Key numbers

- Two-loop corrections to \(\bar\theta\) are of order \(10^{-3}\)–\(10^{-2}\) multiplied by mixing angles and phases, not suppressed by the weak scale as previously claimed.
- For the case of nonhermitian isosinglet masses with a benchmark \(v'/M = 0.02\), the up-sector contribution is \(\argdet(\mtxY_u) \approx \sin(2(\varphi_u-\varphi_d)) \times [ -2.4\times10^{-3} (0.02/(v'/M)) (1-|U_{u3}|^2)(1-|U_{t3}|^2) - 1.0\times10^{-3} (0.02/(v'/M))^2 (1-|U_{t3}|^2)^2 ]\).
- For the same benchmark, the down-sector contribution is \(\argdet(\mtxY_d) \approx \sin(2(\varphi_u-\varphi_d)) \times 1.5\times10^{-2} (0.02/(v'/M)) (1-|U_{c3}|^2)(1-|U_{t3}|^2)\).
- In the restricted case where isosinglet masses are nearly hermitian (soft parity breaking only from Higgs mass splitting), the correction is much smaller: \(\argdet(\mtxY_u) \approx 9.9\times10^{-9}\,\text{Im}[U_{t3}^* U_{u3}] + 4.4\times10^{-9}\,\text{Im}[U_{t3}^* U_{c3}]\) for \(v'/M = 0.02\).
- The neutron EDM from a \(\bar\theta\) term is \(d_n = -(1.5 \pm 0.7)\times 10^{-3}\,\bar\theta\,e\,\text{fm}\) from lattice QCD with chiral perturbation theory fit.
- The CP-odd pion-nucleon couplings are \(\bar g_0 = -(14.7\pm2.3)\times 10^{-3}\,\bar\theta\) and \(\bar g_1 = (3.4\pm2.4)\times 10^{-3}\,\bar\theta\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the Babu-Mohapatra model (Babu and Mohapatra, 1989) by performing a complete two-loop calculation of \(\bar\theta\) corrections, finding they are much larger than the original estimate of \(\bar\theta \lesssim 10^{-12}\) based on qualitative analysis of a subset of diagrams and a decoupling argument. The results contradict the assumption that corrections decouple like \(v/v'\) and instead show threshold corrections do not decouple as new degrees of freedom are made heavy, consistent with general effective field theory expectations. The paper enables future work to search for viable parameter space where the model might still satisfy the \(\bar\theta<10^{-10}\) bound, and motivates hadronic EDM experiments since the natural prediction for \(\bar\theta\) in viable regions is just below present limits, with EDMs dominated by the \(\bar\theta\) term rather than higher-dimensional operators.
