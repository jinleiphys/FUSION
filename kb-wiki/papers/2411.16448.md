# Analytical Solution of the Nonlinear Relativistic Boltzmann Equation

> We provide an exact analytical solution to the nonlinear relativistic Boltzmann equation for a homogeneous, anisotropically scattering massless gas. Utilizing a BKW-like trial solution, we cast the Boltzmann equation into a set of nonlinear coupled equations for scalar moments, based on which the analytical solution is derived. One remarkable feature of our analytical solution lies in the nontrivial scattering angle dependence. We also show that this analytical solution admits a stable fixed point corresponding to the equilibrium solution as long as the parameters are physically feasible. Furthermore, a clear correspondence between our solution and the BKW solution pertaining to nonrelativistic Maxwell molecules is established, thereby clarifying the non-existence of a BKW-type solution in the relativistic domain for massive particles.

## Key claim

The paper provides an exact analytical solution to the nonlinear relativistic Boltzmann equation for a homogeneous, isotropically scattering massless gas, but only under the condition that the scattering cross section is independent of particle momentum and the gas is spatially homogeneous and momentum-isotropic. The solution is a BKW-like trial form that works specifically for massless particles, and the authors clarify that such a BKW-type solution does not exist for relativistic massive particles.

## Method

The authors propose a BKW-like trial solution for the one-particle distribution function of the form \(f(p^0,\tau)=e^{-p^0/\alpha(\tau)}(A(\tau)+B(\tau)p^0)\) and substitute it into the moment equations derived from the relativistic Boltzmann equation. They transform the collision integral using total and relative four-momentum variables, then compute the scalar moments analytically using known results from relativistic kinetic theory. The functions \(A(\tau)\) and \(B(\tau)\) are expressed in terms of \(\alpha(\tau)\) via conservation laws, and a first-order differential equation for \(\alpha(\tau)\) is obtained by considering the \(n=2\) moment, with the solution verified to be independent of the moment index \(n\).

## Key numbers

* The evolution of the parameter \(\alpha(\tau)\) is given by \(\alpha(\tau)=(\gamma-\frac{e_0}{3n_0})e^{\frac{1}{90}\tau(6\pi n_0\sigma(0,2)-15n_0)}+\frac{e_0}{3n_0}\), where \(\gamma=\alpha(0)\) is the initial condition, \(e_0\) and \(n_0\) are the conserved dimensionless energy and particle densities, and \(\sigma(0,2)\) is a Legendre-weighted moment of the angular scattering function.
* The physical solution requires the cross-section condition \(\sigma(0,2) < \frac{5}{2\pi}\) and the initial condition \(\frac{e_0}{4n_0} \leq \gamma \leq \frac{e_0}{3n_0}\).
* The fixed point of the solution is \(\alpha = \frac{e_0}{3n_0}\), which corresponds to the equilibrium solution when \(\frac{e_0}{3n_0}=1\).
* For the isotropic scattering limit, \(\sigma(0,2)=0\) and \(\alpha(\tau)=(\gamma-\frac{e_0}{3n_0})e^{-\frac{1}{6}\tau n_0}+\frac{e_0}{3n_0}\).

## Context

This work extends the first analytical solution of the nonlinear relativistic Boltzmann equation for a massless gas with hard sphere potential in an expanding system (Bazow 2015, Bazow 2016) by incorporating a nontrivial scattering angle dependence in the differential cross section. The solution establishes a clear correspondence with the nonrelativistic BKW solution for Maxwell molecules (Bobylev 1975, Krook and Wu 1976) and clarifies why no such BKW-type solution exists for relativistic massive particles. The results can serve as a benchmark for numerical algorithms solving the relativistic Boltzmann equation and hydrodynamic equations, and may provide insights into non-equilibrium phenomena such as equilibration and the emergence of hydrodynamic behavior in systems like quark-gluon plasma.
