# Interplay between Mach cone and radial expansion and its signal in $γ$-jet events

> We study the hydrodynamic response to jet quenching in expanding quark-gluon plasma (QGP) and its signal in the resulting particle distribution. The ideal hydrodynamic simulations of the $γ$-jet events in heavy-ion collisions are performed in a full (3 + 1)-dimensional setup. The jet-induced Mach cone and the radial expansion of the background mutually push and distort each other. As a result, the particle emission is suppressed in the direction in which the radial flow is pushed back by the Mach cone when the jet path is an off-central one. This is the direct signal of hydrodynamic response to the jetand, moreover, includes information about the jet path in the expanding QGP fluid.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the interplay between the jet-induced Mach cone and the radial expansion of the background QGP fluid produces a suppression of particle emission in a specific direction when the jet path is off-central, and that this suppression serves as a direct signal of the hydrodynamic response that encodes information about the jet path in the expanding medium. The authors note that this signal appears as a dip in the azimuthal angle distribution rather than the double-hump structure traditionally associated with Mach cones.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional ideal relativistic hydrodynamic simulations with source terms to model the energy and momentum deposition from a jet traversing the QGP fluid in gamma-jet events for central Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energy. They used the optical Glauber model for the initial entropy density profile at tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c, a lattice QCD equation of state, and a temperature-dependent energy loss formula with dE/dl|_0 = 15 GeV/fm at T_0 = 500 MeV. The final particle spectra were obtained via the Cooper-Frye freeze-out formula at T_f = 145 MeV, considering only charged pions directly emitted from the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- Initial hydrodynamic evolution start time: tau_0 = 0.6 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 145 MeV
- Reference temperature for energy loss: T_0 = 500 MeV
- Energy loss rate at reference temperature: dE/dl|_0 = 15 GeV/fm
- Jet trigger threshold in final state: p_T,jet > 80 GeV/c
- Photon trigger range: p_T,gamma = 100-120 GeV/c
- Charged pion transverse momentum range analyzed: 1 < p_T < 2 GeV/c
- Parameters for jet production rate: p_0 = 205 GeV/c, alpha = 6.43
- Parameters for initial entropy density profile: C = 19.8, alpha = 0.14
- Parameters for longitudinal profile: eta_flat = 3.8, sigma_eta = 3.2

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Mach cone formation in expanding QGP by Satarov (2005), Chaudhuri (2006, 2007), Li (2010), Betz (2010), Bouras (2014), and Tachibana (2014) by performing full (3+1)-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations of gamma-jet events. The study contradicts the earlier expectation that Mach cones would produce a double-hump structure in azimuthal correlations, which was previously explored by Stoecker (2004), Casalderrey-Solana (2004), and Ruppert (2005). The results enable future experimental searches for this dip signal in gamma-jet events at LHC, particularly using trigger bias on jet and photon transverse momenta to select specific jet path geometries, and provide a foundation for including viscosity effects and jet shower structure in future work.
