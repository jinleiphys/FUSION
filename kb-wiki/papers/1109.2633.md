# Towards a Holographic Model of the QCD Phase Diagram

> We describe the temperature-chemical potential phase diagrams of holographic models of a range of strongly coupled gauge theories that display chiral symmetry breaking/restoration transitions. The models are based on the D3/probe-D7 system but with a phenomenologically chosen running coupling/dilaton profile. We realize chiral phase transitions with either temperature or density that are first or second order by changing the dilaton profile. Although the models are only caricatures of QCD they show that holographic models can capture many aspects of the QCD phase diagram and hint at the dependence on the running coupling.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that by phenomenologically adjusting the running coupling/dilaton profile in a D3/probe-D7 holographic model, they can realize chiral phase transitions of either first or second order with temperature or density, but they explicitly note that their simple step-function ansatz cannot achieve a second-order transition with temperature, and that the models are "only caricatures of QCD."

## Method

The authors use the D3/probe-D7 brane system in AdS5 x S5 with a phenomenologically chosen dilaton profile that introduces a running gauge coupling via a hyperbolic tangent step function. They introduce temperature through an AdS-Schwarzschild black hole metric and chemical potential through a U(1) baryon number gauge field on the D7 brane, then compute the DBI action to find D7 brane embeddings (flat, Minkowski, and black hole embeddings) and determine the free energy to map out phase transitions in the temperature-chemical potential plane.

## Key numbers

- The dilaton profile parameter A (coupling increase across the step) has a critical value A_c = 2.1 for triggering chiral symmetry breaking when lambda = 1.7 and Gamma = 1
- The black hole horizon radius is given by r_H = pi R^2 T, relating temperature to the geometry
- The UV asymptotic of the embedding equation gives w_5 = m + c/rho^2 + ..., where m is proportional to quark mass (m_q = m/(2 pi alpha')) and c is proportional to the quark condensate
- The renormalized free energy requires subtracting (1/4)(Lambda_UV)^4 from both the free energy and grand potential integrals

## Context

This work extends previous holographic studies of the QCD phase diagram using the D3/D7 system with magnetic fields (Evans 2010) and axion/dilaton geometries (Gwak 2011), where the phase diagrams showed first-order transitions with temperature and second-order with density. The authors compare their results to the DeWolfe (2010) holographic model of confinement without quarks, and note that unlike QCD (which has a second-order transition with temperature and first-order with density), their simple step ansatz cannot reproduce the QCD-like second-order transition with temperature, though breaking the SO(6) symmetry of the dilaton profile may allow such behavior.
