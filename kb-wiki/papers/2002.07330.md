# Universality of semisuper-Efimov effect

> We study the semisuper-Efimov effect, which is found for four identical bosons with a resonant three-body interaction in 2D, in various systems. Based on solutions of bound-state and renormalization-group equations, we first demonstrate an emergence of the semisuper-Efimov effect in mass-imbalanced bosons in 2D. Compared with the Efimov and the super-Efimov effects, the mass ratio-dependent scaling parameter is unexpectedly found to take on a finite value even for extremely mass-imbalanced situations, where the mass ratio is 0 or $\infty$. By a renormalization-group analysis, we also show that a weak two-body interaction sustains the semisuper-Efimov effect. Finally, we liberate the universality of the semisuper-Efimov effect from 2D by showing that bosons with linear-dispersion relation support the semisuper-Efimov effect in 1D.

## Key claim

The semisuper-Efimov effect, previously found only for four identical bosons with a resonant three-body interaction in 2D, is shown to emerge also in mass-imbalanced boson systems in 2D and in one-dimensional bosons with a linear dispersion relation, with the mass-ratio-dependent scaling parameter remaining finite even in the extreme limits of mass ratio 0 or infinity.

## Method

The author solves four-body Bethe-Salpeter (bound-state) equations analytically under the leading-logarithmic approximation for two-component and three-component mass-imbalanced bosons in 2D, using a separable potential regulator. A renormalization-group analysis with an effective field theory containing auxiliary trimer fields is performed to verify the bound-state results and to study the stability of the semisuper-Efimov effect against weak two-body interactions. The analysis is extended to identical bosons with a linear dispersion relation in 1D by solving the three- and four-body T-matrix equations.

## Key numbers

- For two-component mass-imbalanced bosons in 2D, the binding energies satisfy: sqrt(ln(Lambda^2/E_{n+1})) - sqrt(ln(Lambda^2/E_n)) = pi * sqrt((alpha+1)(3alpha+1)/(8(2alpha+1)^2)), where alpha = m_A/m_B is the mass ratio.
- For three-component mass-imbalanced bosons in 2D, the binding energies satisfy: sqrt(ln(Lambda^2/E_{n+1})) - sqrt(ln(Lambda^2/E_n)) = pi * sqrt((alpha+1)(3alpha+1)/(4(2alpha+1)^2)).
- The scaling parameter for two-component bosons is (alpha+1)(3alpha+1)/(8(2alpha+1)^2), which remains finite as alpha approaches 0 or infinity.
- The infrared cutoff from two-body interactions gives the minimum binding energy as max{Lambda^2 * exp(-16pi/(alpha|g_A(0)|)), Lambda^2 * exp(-4pi(alpha+1)/(alpha|g_{AB}(0)|))}.

## Context

This work extends the semisuper-Efimov effect predicted by Nishida (2017) for four identical bosons in 2D to mass-imbalanced systems and to 1D with linear dispersion, demonstrating universality beyond the original setting. The mass-ratio dependence differs qualitatively from the Efimov effect (Efimov 1970) and the super-Efimov effect (Nishida 2013, Moroz 2014), where the scaling parameter diverges or vanishes in extreme mass-imbalanced limits. The results enable experimental searches for semisuper-Efimov tetramers in ultracold atomic systems with engineered three-body interactions and in condensed-matter systems with linear dispersion.
