# A study of the $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ resonances from CLAS data on $ep \rightarrow e'π^{+} π^{-} p'$

> The transition helicity amplitudes from the proton ground state to the $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ excited states ($γ_{v}pN^*$ electrocouplings) were determined from the analysis of nine independent one-fold differential $π^{+} π^{-} p$ electroproduction cross sections off a proton target, taken with CLAS at photon virtualities 0.25\enskip {\rm GeV$^{2}$} $<$ $Q^{2}$ $<$ 0.60 \enskip {\rm GeV$^{2}$}. The phenomenological reaction model was employed for separation of the resonant and non-resonant contributions to the final state. The $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ electrocouplings were obtained from the resonant amplitudes parametrized within the framework of a unitarized Breit-Wigner ansatz. They are consistent with results obtained in the previous CLAS analyses of the $π^+n$ and $π^0p$ channels. The successful description of a large body of data in dominant meson-electroproduction channels off protons with the same $γ_{v}pN^*$ electrocouplings offers clear evidence for the reliable extraction of these fundamental quantities from meson-electroproduction data. This analysis also led to the determination of the long-awaited hadronic branching ratios for the $D_{13}(1520)$ decay into $Δπ$ (24%-32%) and $Nρ$ (8%-17%).

## Key claim

The transition helicity amplitudes (electrocouplings) for the $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ resonances were extracted from CLAS data on $ep \rightarrow e'\pi^{+}\pi^{-}p'$ at photon virtualities $0.25 < Q^{2} < 0.60$ GeV$^{2}$, with the caveat that the separation of resonant and non-resonant contributions relies on a phenomenological reaction model (the JM model) and a unitarized Breit-Wigner parametrization, meaning the extracted values are model-dependent and their reliability is supported by consistency with previous CLAS analyses of the $\pi^{+}n$ and $\pi^{0}p$ channels.

## Method

The analysis used nine independent one-fold differential $\pi^{+}\pi^{-}p$ electroproduction cross sections measured with the CLAS detector at Jefferson Lab, covering the invariant mass range $1.31 < W < 1.56$ GeV and photon virtualities $0.25 < Q^{2} < 0.60$ GeV$^{2}$. The phenomenological JM reaction model was employed to separate resonant and non-resonant contributions, with resonant amplitudes parametrized using a unitarized Breit-Wigner ansatz that respects general unitarity conditions. The $\gamma_{v}pN^{*}$ electrocouplings for the $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ states were determined from a combined fit to all measured differential cross sections, and the hadronic decay widths for the $D_{13}(1520)$ into $\Delta\pi$ and $N\rho$ channels were extracted under the constraint of $Q^{2}$-independent hadronic decay amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- Photon virtuality range: $0.25$ GeV$^{2}$ $<$ $Q^{2}$ $<$ $0.60$ GeV$^{2}$ for the CLAS data analyzed.
- Invariant mass range: $1.31$ GeV $<$ $W$ $<$ $1.56$ GeV for the $\pi^{+}\pi^{-}p$ final state.
- Hadronic branching ratio for $D_{13}(1520)$ decay into $\Delta\pi$: $24\%$ to $32\%$.
- Hadronic branching ratio for $D_{13}(1520)$ decay into $N\rho$: $8\%$ to $17\%$.
- Total decay width of the $P_{11}(1440)$ resonance: approximately $300$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous CLAS analyses of $N\pi$ electroproduction channels ($\pi^{+}n$ and $\pi^{0}p$) by Aznauryan et al. (2009) to the independent $\pi^{+}\pi^{-}p$ channel, providing a cross-check of the extracted $\gamma_{v}pN^{*}$ electrocouplings for the $P_{11}(1440)$ and $D_{13}(1520)$ resonances. The results are consistent with those from the $N\pi$ channels, supporting the reliability of the extraction method. The analysis also provides the first determination of the $D_{13}(1520)$ hadronic branching ratios to $\Delta\pi$ and $N\rho$ from electroproduction data, which complements information from hadronic probes. The extracted electrocouplings at low $Q^{2}$ are relevant for studies of meson-baryon dressing contributions to resonance structure, as investigated by the EBAC dynamical coupled-channel approach (Lee et al., 2008-2011), and will help constrain the transition between meson-baryon cloud and quark core dominance in nucleon resonance structure.
