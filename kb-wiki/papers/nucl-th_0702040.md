# Eft for DFT

> These lectures give an overview of the ongoing application of effective field theory (EFT) and renormalization group (RG) concepts and methods to density functional theory (DFT), with special emphasis on the nuclear many-body problem.

## Key claim

The paper argues that effective field theory (EFT) and renormalization group (RG) methods can provide a systematic, controlled, and microscopic foundation for density functional theory (DFT) in nuclear physics, elevating phenomenological approaches like Skyrme Hartree-Fock to a full DFT treatment with reliable error estimates, though many of the topics covered are still in their infancy.

## Method

The author reviews the conceptual framework for applying EFT and RG to DFT for fermion many-body systems, using the nuclear many-body problem as the primary example. The approach involves using RG-evolved low-momentum potentials (V_low_k) to soften the nucleon-nucleon interaction, which tames the hard core and tensor force, and then constructing energy density functionals systematically from chiral EFT interactions including three-nucleon forces, with power counting determining which diagrams contribute and the truncation error.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation occurs at Fermi momentum k_F ~ 1.35 fm^{-1} and energy per particle E/A ~ -16 MeV (empirical values cited as reference points)
- For ^8Be, the full many-body wave function requires 17,920 complex functions in 21 dimensions (3A-3 = 21 spatial coordinates)
- A mesh spacing of 0.5 fm for a 10 fm nucleus would require 10^{27} grid points for the full wave function
- For nuclei, r_0 ~ 1.1 fm ~ 2.75c (where c is the hard core radius), implying nuclear matter is dilute and delicately bound
- For liquid ^3He, r_0 ~ 2.4 A ~ c
- Skyrme SkIII model parameters: t_0 = -1129, t_1 = 395, t_2 = -95, t_3 = 14000, W_0 = 120 (in units of MeV-fm^n)
- The Argonne v_18 potential in first-order Hartree-Fock is not even bound for nuclear matter
- For V_low_k, the in-medium T-matrix shows rapid convergence by 2nd order

## Context

This lecture paper extends the application of EFT and RG concepts, previously successful in particle physics and low-energy nuclear interactions, to the construction of nuclear energy density functionals. It contrasts the traditional approach (exemplified by the Argonne v_18 potential and phenomenological Skyrme HF with parameters fit to data) with a new EFT-based approach where resolution scale is a tool rather than fixed. The work builds on the V_low_k program (Bogner, Kuo, Schwenk, 2000s) and chiral EFT for nuclear forces, and aims to enable systematic, improvable DFT calculations for nuclei throughout the nuclear chart, including unstable nuclei where extrapolation with controlled theoretical errors is essential.
