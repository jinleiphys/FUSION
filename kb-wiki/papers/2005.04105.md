# Evolution of the dipole polarizability in the stable tin isotope chain

> The dipole polarizability of stable even-mass tin isotopes 112,114,116,118,120,124 was extracted from inelastic proton scattering experiments at 295 MeV under very forward angles performed at RCNP. Predictions from energy density functionals cannot account for the present data and the polarizability of 208Pb simultaneously. The evolution of the polarizabilities in neighboring isotopes indicates a kink at 120Sn while all model results show a nearly linear increase with mass number after inclusion of pairing corrections.

## Key claim

The dipole polarizability extracted from inelastic proton scattering on stable even-mass tin isotopes shows a kink at 120Sn, while all energy density functional (EDF) model results predict a nearly linear increase with mass number after including pairing corrections; furthermore, no single EDF parametrization can simultaneously reproduce the polarizability data for the tin isotopes and for 208Pb.

## Method

The dipole polarizability of 112,114,116,118,120,124Sn was extracted from inelastic proton scattering at 295 MeV and very forward angles (0°, 2.5°, 4.5°) using the Grand Raiden spectrometer at RCNP, Osaka University. A multipole decomposition analysis (MDA) of the measured cross sections was performed using theoretical angular distributions from the Quasiparticle Phonon Model (QPM) to separate E1, M1, and higher-multipole contributions. The resulting E1 Coulomb excitation cross sections were converted to photoabsorption cross sections via the virtual photon method in an eikonal approach, and the dipole polarizability was computed from the inverse-energy-weighted integral of the photoabsorption cross section.

## Key numbers

* Total dipole polarizability values (in fm³) for the six tin isotopes: 112Sn = 7.19(50), 114Sn = 7.29(58), 116Sn = 7.52(51), 118Sn = 7.91(87), 120Sn = 8.08(60), 124Sn = 7.99(56)
* The low-energy contribution (6 MeV to neutron threshold) varies from 13% (112Sn) to 8% (124Sn) of the total polarizability
* The high-energy contribution (above 20 MeV) from QPM calculations amounts to 9-10% in all isotopes
* The contribution from the region above 20 MeV was estimated at about 8% based on QRPA and QPM calculations, with an associated error of 10%
* The previous value for 120Sn of 8.59(37) fm³ (Has15) is larger than the present result due to averaging with photoabsorption data from Fultz (1969) and Leprêtre (1974)

## Context

This work extends previous measurements of dipole polarizability in 120Sn (Has15) and 208Pb (Tam11) to a full isotopic chain of stable tin nuclei, providing a systematic benchmark for EDF predictions. The results challenge current EDF parametrizations including SV-bas (Klu09a), KDE0-J33 (Agr05), DDMEa (Vre03), and DD-PCX (Yuk19), none of which can simultaneously describe the tin chain and 208Pb data. The findings enable improved constraints on the symmetry energy and its density dependence, and motivate future measurements on neutron-rich unstable tin isotopes using relativistic Coulomb breakup at FAIR (Aum20).
