# Equilibration in Heavy Ion Collisions at LHC and at RHIC

> We consider the evolution of a parton plasma created in Au+Au collisions at LHC and at RHIC energies. Using Boltzmann equation, relaxation time approximation and perturbative QCD, we show the physics of both thermal and chemical equilibration in a transparent manner. In particular, we show inelastic processes are, contrary to common assumption, more important than elastic processes, the state of equilibration of the system can compensate for some powers of $\a_s$ for the purpose of equilibration, the two-stage equilibration scenario is, barring any unknown non-perturbative effects, inevitable and is an intrinsic feature of perturbative QCD, and gluon multiplication is the leading process for entropy generation.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to common assumption, inelastic processes (especially gluon multiplication) are more important than elastic processes for both thermal and chemical equilibration of the parton plasma created in heavy ion collisions, and that complete equilibration cannot be attained before the phase transition at approximately 200 MeV.

## Method

The author uses the Boltzmann equation with Baym's form for one-dimensional longitudinal expansion, combined with the relaxation time approximation for the collision term. The collision times and equilibrium temperatures for gluons and quarks are determined separately from perturbative QCD matrix elements at tree level, including the leading inelastic processes (gg ↔ ggg and gg ↔ qq̅) and all binary elastic processes, with infrared regularization via medium effects and inclusion of the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect for soft gluon emissions. The evolution is solved numerically using initial conditions from HIJING for Au+Au collisions at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- The assumed phase transition temperature is T_c ~ 200 MeV, below which the evolution is stopped.
- Gluon multiplication (gg ↔ ggg) dominates over other processes initially, but is overtaken by gg ↔ qq̅ at around 2.0 fm/c at LHC and 4.0 fm/c at RHIC.
- For quarks, the gg ↔ qq̅ process remains dominant throughout the entire evolution.
- In the free-streaming limit (no interactions), the ratio p_L/p_T approaches 2 τ₀²/τ² → 0 and ε/3p_T approaches 2/3 as τ → ∞, which is clearly not observed in the interacting case.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that addressed either thermalization (Baym 1984, Gavin and collaborators, Shuryak, Heiselberg and Wang) or chemical equilibration (Biro and collaborators, Shuryak and Xiong) separately, but not simultaneously. It provides a simpler alternative to the parton cascade model (PCM) of Geiger, which also considered both equilibration processes simultaneously but required very involved computations. The paper enables a clearer understanding of the relative importance of inelastic versus elastic processes in equilibration, and suggests that the system should be treated as a fluid mixture of an approximately thermalized gluon plasma with a still far-from-equilibrated quark and antiquark plasma.
