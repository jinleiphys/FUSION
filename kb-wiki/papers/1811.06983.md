# Soft drop groomed jet angularities at the LHC

> Jet angularities are a class of jet substructure observables where a continuous parameter is introduced in order to interpolate between different classic observables such as the jet mass and jet broadening. We consider jet angularities measured on an inclusive jet sample at the LHC where the soft drop grooming procedure is applied in order to remove soft contaminations from the jets. The soft drop algorithm allows for a precise comparison between theory and data and could be used to extract the QCD strong coupling constant $α_s$ from jet substructure data in the future. We develop a framework to realize the resummation of all relevant large logarithms at the next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy. To demonstrate that the developed formalism is suitable for the extraction of $α_s$, we extend our calculations to next-to-next-to-leading logarithm (NNLL) for the jet mass case. Overall, we find good agreement between our NLL numerical results and Pythia simulations for LHC kinematics and we observe an improved agreement when the NNLL components are included. In addition, we expect that groomed jet angularities will be a useful handle for studying the modification of jets in heavy-ion collisions.

# Soft drop groomed jet angularities at the LHC

## Key claim
The authors develop a resummation framework for soft-drop-groomed jet angularities at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, and extend to next-to-next-to-leading logarithmic (NNLL) for the jet mass case with β=0, finding good agreement with Pythia simulations; however, the extraction of the QCD strong coupling constant α_s from these observables remains a future goal requiring further theoretical precision (matching to NNLO fixed-order calculations).

## Method
The cross section for inclusive jet production pp→jet+X is factorized using Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) in the small-jet-radius limit (R≪1). The semi-inclusive jet functions are refactorized into collinear, soft (groomed and ungroomed), and hard matching functions, each evaluated at its natural scale and evolved via renormalization group equations to resum large logarithms in the jet radius R, the angularity τ_a, and the soft drop grooming parameter z_cut. The groomed jet angularity τ_a is defined as (1/p_T) Σ_i p_Ti ΔR_{iJ}^{2-a}, where a is a continuous parameter interpolating between jet mass (a=0) and jet broadening (a=1). Non-perturbative effects are included via a shape function convolution.

## Key numbers
- Jet radius R = 0.8 used for numerical results at LHC kinematics (√s = 13 TeV)
- Transverse momentum cut p_T > 600 GeV and rapidity |η| < 1.5 for inclusive jet sample
- Soft drop grooming parameters: z_cut = 0.1 and β = 0, 1, 2
- Non-perturbative shape function parameter Ω_{a=0} = 1 GeV used for the NP model
- The transition point where grooming becomes irrelevant is at τ_a = z_cut R^{2-a}, independent of β
- The two-loop non-cusp anomalous dimension for the ungroomed soft function γ^{ungr,(1)}_{S_i} was computed and checked against Chien (2015) with full agreement
- The two-loop non-cusp anomalous dimension for the soft function S_i^{∉gr} is numerically identical to half the anomalous dimension of the global soft function in Frye (2016) up to O(10^{-5}) relative error

## Context
This work extends the groomed jet mass calculation of Kang (2018) to the more general class of jet angularities, interpolating between jet mass (a=0) and jet broadening (a=1). It builds on the soft drop declustering algorithm of Larkoski (2014) and the factorization framework for ungroomed jet angularities of Ellis (2010) and Kang (2018). The results enable future extractions of α_s from LHC jet substructure data, analogous to how event shape variables in e⁺e⁻ collisions have been used for this purpose (Abbate 2010, Hoang 2015). The authors also note that groomed jet angularities could serve as a tool for studying jet modification in heavy-ion collisions, where the related jet broadening (a=1) measured by ALICE (Acharya 2018) shows significant modification.
