# Perturbative renormalization of chiral nuclear forces at subleading order in 3S1-3D1 channel

> We investigate renormalization of chiral nuclear forces in the coupled channel of 3S1-3D1 of nucleon-nucleon scattering. The one-pion exchange potential is treated nonperturbatively at leading order while subleading potentials are perturbations. Very much like the uncoupled channel of 3P0 , the singular attraction of one-pion exchange gives rise to the so-called genuine exceptional cutoffs, where artificial correlations between subleading contact operators emerge and they result in ill-defined values of the low-energy constants. To address this issue we follow the solution proposed for 3P0 in Ref. [1] and apply it to 3S1-3D1 . The truncation uncertainty of an effective field theory allows certain degrees of freedom in choosing renormalization conditions, or fitting schemes of the low-energy constants. By exploiting this freedom near the exceptional cutoffs, we are able to remove the said correlations. A much mitigated cutoff variation of the phase shifts, which is acceptable to the power counting, is thus obtained.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that genuine exceptional cutoffs (GECs) in the coupled 3S1-3D1 channel, which cause artificial correlations between subleading contact operators and ill-defined low-energy constants, can be removed by exploiting the truncation uncertainty of effective field theory to slightly modify the renormalization conditions (fitting schemes) near the problematic cutoff values, yielding cutoff variations acceptable to the power counting.

## Method

The authors treat the one-pion exchange potential nonperturbatively at leading order (LO) by solving the Lippmann-Schwinger equation exactly, with the LO contact potential fitted to the empirical deuteron binding energy of 2.225 MeV. Subleading potentials at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO), including two-pion exchange and three contact operators (C^(2), D^(0), E^(0)), are treated perturbatively via distorted-wave expansion. The NNLO low-energy constants are determined by solving a linear system that matches the EFT to partial-wave analysis data for the 3S1 phase shift at momentum k1, the mixing angle at k2, and the deuteron binding energy. Near GECs, the fitting scheme is modified by adjusting the LO binding energy by +/-0.5 MeV or changing the fitting momenta (k1, k2) to shift the determinant of the linear system away from zero.

## Key numbers

* The empirical deuteron binding energy used for LO fitting: B_emp = 2.225 MeV
* Two LO coupling constant divergence points: Lambda_star = 1032 MeV and 2953 MeV
* GEC examples studied: Lambda_E = 1000 MeV and 1434 MeV
* Modified fitting near 1000 MeV uses B^(0) = B_emp +/- 0.5 MeV (Delta B = 0.5 MeV)
* Modified fitting near 1434 MeV uses k1 = 350.7 MeV and k2 = 300.2 MeV or 160.7 MeV
* NNLO truncation error estimated as delta_1^PWA (k/m_sigma)^3 ~ 4 degrees with m_sigma ~ 500 MeV
* Background cutoff variation of the 3S1 phase shift at k=250 MeV is about 3 degrees

## Context

This work extends the GEC solution previously developed for the uncoupled 3P0 channel by Peng, Long, and Xu (2024) to the more complicated coupled 3S1-3D1 channel. It revisits the renormalization problem originally tackled by Valderrama (2009), Pavon Valderrama (2011), and Long (2011), in light of the discovery of GECs by Gasparyan (2022). The paper enables practical determination of subleading low-energy constants in chiral effective field theory for nucleon-nucleon scattering while maintaining renormalization group invariance, and confirms that the OPE tensor force in higher partial waves (3P2-3F2 and beyond) is sufficiently perturbative to avoid this issue, as shown by Wu (2018).
