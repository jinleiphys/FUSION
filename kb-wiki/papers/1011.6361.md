# Directed flow at midrapidity in heavy-ion collisions

> It was recently shown that fluctuations in the initial geometry of a heavy ion collision generally result in a dipole asymmetry of the distribution of outgoing particles. This asymmetry, unlike the usual directed flow, is expected to be present at a wide range of rapidity -- including midrapidity. First evidence of this phenomenon can be seen in recent two-particle correlation data by STAR, providing the last element necessary to quantitatively describe long-range dihadron correlations. We extract differential directed flow from these data and propose a new direct measurement.

# Directed flow at midrapidity in heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Fluctuations in the initial geometry of a heavy-ion collision produce a dipole asymmetry that generates directed flow at midrapidity, unlike the usual directed flow which is odd in rapidity; this phenomenon provides the missing element needed to quantitatively describe long-range dihadron correlations, though the extracted values have sizable systematic errors from the momentum conservation estimate.

## Method

The authors extracted differential directed flow \(v_1\) from STAR two-particle correlation data by decomposing the measured first Fourier harmonic \(\langle\cos\Delta\phi\rangle\) into a contribution from directed flow and a contribution from global transverse momentum conservation. They estimated the momentum conservation term using measured pion and kaon multiplicities from BRAHMS (2004) and \(m_t\) spectra from PHENIX (2003), then subtracted it to isolate the product \(v_1^{(t)} v_1^{(a)}\). Assuming a value for the trigger particle \(v_1^{(t)}\) in the range 0.10–0.13, they extracted \(v_1\) as a function of transverse momentum for charged hadrons. They also proposed a new event-plane method using a \(p_t\)-dependent weight \(w(p_t) = p_t - \langle p_t^2\rangle/\langle p_t\rangle\) that eliminates bias from momentum conservation.

## Key numbers

- Directed flow changes sign near \(p_t \simeq 0.8\) GeV/c, consistent with the requirement that the sum of \(p_t v_1(p_t)\) over all particles vanishes.
- For trigger particles with \(p_t^{(t)} = 3\)–4 GeV and \(p_t^{(t)} = 4\)–6 GeV, assuming \(v_1^{(t)} = 0.10\)–0.13 gives consistent extracted \(v_1^{(a)}\) curves.
- The momentum conservation correlation is estimated as \(\langle\cos\Delta\phi\rangle_{\rm p_t\ cons.} \simeq (-0.00185 / ({\rm GeV}/c)^2) \, p_t^{(t)} p_t^{(a)}\).
- In the momentum range \(1.5 < p_t < 2\) GeV, hydrodynamics predicts \(v_1/\varepsilon_1 \simeq v_2/\varepsilon_2\); with \(v_2 \simeq 0.2\) and \(\varepsilon_1\) approximately 4 times smaller than \(\varepsilon_2\), one expects \(v_1 \simeq 0.05\), in agreement with the extracted value.
- The estimate for the denominator in the momentum conservation term is accurate to \(\sim \pm 20\%\), which can change the extracted \(v_1\) by as much as 50\%.
- Directed flow may reach values as large as 10\% at high \(p_t\) for mid-central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical prediction by Teaney and Yan (2010) that fluctuations in the initial geometry create a dipole asymmetry \(\varepsilon_1\) and corresponding directed flow, analogous to how triangular flow arises from initial-state fluctuations as shown by Alver and collaborators (2010). The analysis uses STAR correlation data from Agakishiev and collaborators (2010) and builds on the framework of Luzum (2010) showing that all long-range correlations originate from collective flow and momentum conservation. The proposed measurement method enables direct experimental study of this fluctuation-driven directed flow at RHIC and LHC, which will help constrain models of initial conditions and confirm hydrodynamic behavior of the collision system.
