# Kinetic and canonical momentum broadening in the Glasma

> We lay the foundations for a quantum formalism describing the real-time evolution of particles in the Glasma phase of a heavy-ion collision, focusing on the implications of gauge invariance in the definition of the momentum of a particle in a classical background field. We first establish the correspondence between the classical Wong's equations and the Heisenberg equations of motion for a particle in a classical non-Abelian background field. Using this correspondence, we obtain equations of motion for both the kinetic momentum -- the gauge invariant, physically measurable quantity -- and the canonical momentum, which is conjugate to the coordinates in the Hamiltonian. In particular, the kinetic momentum broadening receives non-trivial contributions from the transverse field components, even in the eikonal limit. Finally, we demonstrate that imposing a transverse Coulomb gauge condition at the initial time significantly reduces the accumulation of numerical errors, thereby providing an optimized framework for the forthcoming quantum implementation.

## Key claim
The paper establishes that in the Glasma phase of heavy-ion collisions, the gauge-invariant kinetic momentum broadening receives non-trivial contributions from transverse field components even in the eikonal limit, contrary to the common lore that only longitudinal gauge fields contribute at leading order in the high-energy approximation; this distinction between kinetic and canonical momentum has not been addressed in previous studies of parton propagation in the Glasma.

## Method
The authors first establish the correspondence between classical Wong's equations and the Heisenberg equations of motion for a quark in a classical non-Abelian background field, deriving equations for both kinetic and canonical momentum. They then numerically solve the classical Yang-Mills equations for boost-invariant Glasma fields on a transverse lattice using the leapfrog method, and propagate classical quarks through these fields using Wong's equations. Two alternative methods are presented for evaluating the decomposition of kinetic momentum broadening: one tracking the Lorentz force directly, and another decomposing kinetic momentum into canonical and gauge field contributions.

## Context
This work extends the classical transport equation approach to hard probes in the Glasma (Wong 1970; Ruggieri 2018; Carrington 2020; Ipp 2020; Avramescu 2023) by clarifying the distinction between kinetic and canonical momentum broadening, which previous studies using classical transport equations had not addressed. The paper lays the foundation for a quantum implementation using the light-front Hamiltonian formalism (Li 2020, 2021, 2023, 2025) that will enable first-principle studies of radiative processes in the Glasma, complementing recent work on jet quenching in Glasma-like media (Barata 2024) and pre-equilibrium effects on heavy quark and jet observables (Boguslavski 2023; Pandey 2023; Du 2023).
