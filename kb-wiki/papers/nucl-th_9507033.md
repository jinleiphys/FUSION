# Reheating after Supercooling in the Chiral Phase Transition

> The chirally symmetric quark-gluon plasma produced in energetic heavy-ion collisions is predicted to supercool at the late stages of its evolution. The thermal energy is then transformed into the potential energy associated with an energetically unfavorable field configuration. Since the system is in an unstable state it eventually rolls down to the true minimum of the effective chiral potential. When this motion is described in terms of the sigma-model, we find that the energy of the coherent $σ-$field is very efficiently converted into pionic excitations due to anharmonic oscillations around this minimum. The system is expected to partially thermalize before its disintegration.

## Key claim

If the quark-gluon plasma produced in heavy-ion collisions supercools significantly during a first-order QCD phase transition, the coherent sigma-field energy is efficiently converted into pionic excitations via anharmonic oscillations, leading to partial thermalization on a short time scale; however, the model makes no statement about an entropy increase, and the pionic excitations carry no entropy until decoherence occurs.

## Method

The authors model the chiral phase transition using the linear sigma model with parameters λ ≈ 20, v ≈ 90 MeV, and mπ ≈ 140 MeV. They treat the sigma and pion fields classically, assume the system starts in a supercooled chirally symmetric state with σ ≈ v and π ≈ 0, and derive equations of motion that reduce to Mathieu equations for the field modes. By analyzing the resonant solutions of these equations, they compute the characteristic growth times for unstable pion and sigma modes.

## Key numbers

- The sigma mass is mσ = √(2λ)v = 600 MeV.
- For maximal coupling parameters (qσ = 3, qπ = 2), the imaginary characteristic exponents are 0.35 for sigma modes and 0.17 for pion modes at A ≈ 4.
- The characteristic growth time for sigma modes is τσ = 0.9 fm/c; for pion modes it is τπ = 1.9 fm/c.
- If the potential energy density εpot = (λ/4)v⁴ is fully converted to thermal energy of a massless pion gas, the resulting temperature is T ≈ 135 MeV.
- The system may supercool to T ≈ 0.7Tc, corresponding to a reduction by a factor three in entropy density, according to prior nucleation model calculations.

## Context

This work extends the nucleation model of hadronization by Cse, CK, and Sve (1990s), which assumed thermal equilibrium and found slow conversion times (>10 fm/c) with significant supercooling. It is inspired by the reheating-after-inflation mechanism of Kofman, Linde, and Starobinsky (1994) and is closely related to the study of long-wavelength mode excitation during false vacuum decay by Rajagopal and Wilczek (1993). The mechanism provides a fast hadronization channel (≈2 fm/c) that may compete with or dominate over nucleation, and it suggests that the resulting broad pion spectrum could partially thermalize, potentially obscuring signals of Disoriented Chiral Condensates as studied by Rajagopal, Wilczek, Gavai, and Asakawa (1990s).
