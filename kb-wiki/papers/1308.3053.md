# Rotating hybrid compact stars

> Starting from equations of state of nucleonic and color-superconducting quark matter and assuming a first-order phase transition between these, we construct an equation of state of stellar matter, which contains three phases: a nucleonic phase, as well as two-flavor and three-flavor color-superconducting phases of quarks. Static sequences of the corresponding hybrid stars include massive members with masses of $\sim 2 M_{\odot}$ and radii in the range of $13< R < 16$ km. We investigate the integral parameters of rapidly rotating stars and obtain evolutionary sequences that correspond to constant rest-mass stars spinning down by electromagnetic and gravitational radiation. Physically new transitional sequences are revealed that are distinguished by a phase transition from nucleonic to color-superconducting matter for some configurations that are located between the static and Keplerian limits. The snapshots of internal structure of the star, displaying the growth or shrinkage of superconducting volume as the star's spin changes, are displayed for constant rest mass stars. We further obtain evolutionary sequences of rotating supramassive compact stars and construct pre-collapse models that can be used as initial data to simulate a collapse of color-superconducting hybrid stars to a black hole.

## Key claim

The paper constructs hybrid compact star models that include a first-order phase transition from nucleonic matter to color-superconducting quark matter (both two-flavor 2SC and three-flavor CFL phases), and identifies "transitional sequences" where a star that is purely nucleonic at the Keplerian (maximum rotation) limit develops a color-superconducting quark core as it spins down toward the static limit, contingent on the assumption of uniform rotation and a Maxwell construction for the phase transition.

## Method

The authors combine a relativistic density-functional nucleonic equation of state (DD-ME2 parameterization of Lalazissis et al. 2005) with a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for quark matter that includes vector interactions and color-superconducting pairing gaps, matching the two via a Maxwell construction at a transition density of 2.5 times nuclear saturation density. They solve Einstein's equations for axially symmetric rotating configurations using the public-domain RNS code, generating sequences of constant rest-mass stars that spin down from the Keplerian limit to the static limit, and also construct supramassive sequences that have no stable static counterpart.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of purely nucleonic static sequences: approximately 2.7 solar masses
- Static hybrid star masses reach approximately 2 solar masses, with radii in the range 13 to 16 km
- Transition density from nucleonic to quark matter fixed at 2.5 times nuclear saturation density (rho_0)
- Ratio of vector coupling to scalar coupling in quark matter fixed at G_V/G_S = 0.8
- Constant rest-mass evolutionary sequences computed with a mass step of 0.2 solar masses
- Reference value for onset of gravitational-wave instability: T/W approximately 0.08 for compressible models

## Context

This work extends prior studies of rotating hybrid stars with color-superconducting cores (e.g., Iida et al. 2008) by systematically exploring evolutionary sequences and identifying rotationally induced phase transitions, building on the framework of Cook et al. (1994) for supramassive configurations. It enables construction of pre-collapse models for simulating the collapse of color-superconducting hybrid stars to black holes, and provides a theoretical basis for interpreting pulsar observations such as the 2-solar-mass pulsar J1614-2230 (Demorest et al. 2010) as potentially having undergone a phase transition to superconducting quark matter during spin-down.
