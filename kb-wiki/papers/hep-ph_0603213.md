# Determination of polarized parton distribution functions with recent data on polarization asymmetries

> Global analysis has been performed within the next-to-leading order in Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) to determine polarized parton distributions with new experimental data in spin asymmetries. The new data set includes JLab, HERMES, and COMPASS measurements on spin asymmetry A_1 for the neutron and deuteron in lepton scattering. Our new analysis also utilizes the double-spin asymmetry for pi^0 production in polarized pp collisions, A_{LL}^{pi^0}, measured by the PHENIX collaboration. Because of these new data, uncertainties of the polarized PDFs are reduced. In particular, the JLab, HERMES, and COMPASS measurements are valuable for determining Delta d_v(x) at large x and Delta qbar(x) at x~0.1. The PHENIX pi^0 data significantly reduce the uncertainty of Delta g(x). Furthermore, we discuss a possible constraint on Delta g(x) at large x by using the HERMES data on g_1^d in comparison with the COMPASS ones at x~0.05.

## Key claim

The inclusion of PHENIX double-spin asymmetry data for pi0 production in polarized pp collisions significantly reduces the uncertainty of the polarized gluon distribution, but the resulting polarized PDFs remain consistent with A_LL^pi0 = 0 across the entire pT region, indicating a need for more precise data.

## Method

A next-to-leading-order (NLO) QCD global analysis was performed using the MSbar scheme, fitting polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) to spin asymmetry data from inclusive deep inelastic scattering (A_1) and from pi0 production in polarized pp collisions (A_LL^pi0). The polarized PDFs were parameterized at Q0^2 = 1 GeV^2 using a functional form based on unpolarized GRV98 PDFs, with four distributions determined: Delta u_v, Delta d_v, Delta qbar (flavor-symmetric antiquarks), and Delta g. The first moments of Delta u_v and Delta d_v were fixed to 0.926 and -0.341 respectively using semileptonic decay data of octet baryons, and the Hessian method was used for uncertainty estimation with Delta chi^2 = 12.65 for eleven free parameters.

## Key numbers

- First moment of Delta g at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2: 0.31 +/- 0.32 (Type 1, including PHENIX data), compared to 0.50 +/- 1.27 from the previous AAC03 analysis
- First moment of Delta qbar at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2: -0.05 +/- 0.01 (Type 1)
- First moment of Delta Sigma (quark spin content) at Q^2 = 1 GeV^2: 0.27 +/- 0.07 (Type 1)
- Total chi^2/d.o.f. = 0.904 for 421 data points (Type 1), including 413 DIS data points (chi^2 = 359.29) and 8 PHENIX pi0 data points (chi^2 = 11.18)
- PHENIX data scale uncertainty of 40% (dominated by beam polarization uncertainty) translates to approximately 20% uncertainty in Delta g(x) and its first moment

## Context

This work extends the previous AAC03 analysis (Hirai, Kumano, Saito, 2003) by incorporating new spin asymmetry data from JLab Hall A, HERMES, COMPASS, and PHENIX collaborations. The new JLab neutron data improve determination of Delta d_v(x) at large x (x > 0.2), while HERMES and COMPASS deuteron data reduce uncertainties in polarized antiquark distributions at x ~ 0.1. The inclusion of PHENIX pi0 data reduces the gluon first moment uncertainty from 1.27 to 0.32, enabling more precise tests of the proton spin sum rule. The results are compared with other contemporary polarized PDF sets including GRSV (Gluck, Reya, Stratmann, Vogelsang, 2001), BB (Blumlein and Bottcher, 2002), and LSS (Leader, Sidorov, Stamenov, 2006), showing agreement within uncertainties except for the gluon distribution.
