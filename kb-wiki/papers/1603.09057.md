# Multiplicity dependent and non-binomial efficiency corrections for particle number cumulants

> In this note we extend previous work on efficiency corrections for cumulant measurements [1,2]. We will discuss the limitations of the methods presented in these papers. Specifically we will consider multiplicity dependent efficiencies as well as a non-binomial efficiency distributions. We will discuss the most simple and straightforward methods to implement those corrections.

# Multiplicity dependent and non-binomial efficiency corrections for particle number cumulants

## Key claim

The standard binomial efficiency correction for cumulant measurements (Bzdak 2012, Bzdak 2013) fails when the detection efficiency depends on the number of produced particles or when the efficiency distribution is not binomial; even a very small multiplicity dependence of efficiency can produce substantial deviations in higher-order cumulant ratios, and the simplest unfolding method based on solving triangular equations recovers correct cumulants even when the unfolded multiplicity distribution is unphysical.

## Method

The authors model the observed particle distribution as a convolution of the true production distribution with an efficiency distribution B(n,N), then compute factorial moments and cumulants for cases where efficiency depends linearly on N and for non-binomial efficiency distributions (hypergeometric, beta-binomial, Gaussian). They test unfolding by inverting the triangular matrix equation p(n) = B(n,N) P(N) via backward substitution, using a Poisson input distribution with known cumulant ratios K_n/K_2 = 1, and compare results to the standard constant-efficiency correction formula F_i = f_i / epsilon^i.

## Key numbers

- For a Poisson input with <N> = 40 and epsilon_0 = 0.65, a linear multiplicity dependence epsilon(N) = epsilon_0 + epsilon'(N - <N>) with epsilon' = -4 x 10^-4 (corresponding to STAR at sqrt(s) = 7.7 GeV) produces a 30% correction in K_4/K_2 and much larger corrections in K_6/K_2 when corrected using constant efficiency.
- For the hypergeometric distribution with epsilon = 2/3 and alpha = 0.6, the ratio K_4/K_2 becomes 0.66 (compared to the true value of 1) when corrected using binomial formulas.
- For the beta-binomial distribution with epsilon = 0.7 and alpha = 30, K_4/K_2 becomes 0.82; for alpha = 60, K_4/K_2 becomes 1.45.
- For the Gaussian distribution with epsilon = 0.7 and alpha = 5.0, K_4/K_2 becomes 0.54; for alpha = 1.0, K_4/K_2 becomes 1.58.
- The determinant of the binomial matrix for M = 100 and epsilon = 0.7 is approximately 10^-782, making the matrix effectively singular.
- With 10^7 sampled events, the triangular solution method recovers K_4/K_2 centered at 1.0 for multiplicity-dependent efficiency with <N> = 40, epsilon_0 = 0.7, epsilon' = -0.0005, with statistical spread comparable to the constant-efficiency method.

## Context

This work extends the efficiency correction framework of Bzdak (2012) and Bzdak (2013), which assumed binomial efficiency distributions with constant efficiency, and the phase-space-dependent binomial corrections of Luo (2014) and Kitazawa (2016). It addresses the observation from STAR preliminary data (Luo 2015) that efficiency depends on total charged particle multiplicity, making the standard analytic corrections inadequate. The paper demonstrates that solving the triangular matrix equation for the full efficiency matrix yields correct cumulants, and recommends that experiments extract the full efficiency matrix (e.g., via GEANT simulations) rather than relying on binomial assumptions, while noting that more sophisticated unfolding methods from high-energy physics (DAgostini 1995, Schmitt 2012) have not yet been tested for higher-order cumulant extraction.
