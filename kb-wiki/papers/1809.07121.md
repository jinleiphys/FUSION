# On the width of the $Δ(1232)$ in $NΔ$ and $ΔΔ$ states

> Due to the finite kinetic energy in the intermediate $NΔ$ state the (internal) energy available for mesonic decay is decreased and consequently the effective $NΔ$ width is suppressed in $NN$ scattering. The same can happen also in $Δ$$Δ$ case. Also the $NΔ$ angular momentum suppresses the width as well, while the effect of the initial $NN$ angular momentum is more subtle. The state dependence affects e.g. pion production observables and can also be seen as the origin of T=1 "dibaryons".

# On the width of the Δ(1232) in NΔ and ΔΔ states

## Key claim
The effective width of the Δ(1232) resonance is significantly suppressed when it appears as an intermediate state in NN scattering, because the finite kinetic energy of the NΔ system reduces the internal energy available for pionic decay; this state-dependent suppression can explain the quantum numbers and masses of isovector "dibaryons" without requiring exotic quark configurations.

## Method
The effective decay width of NΔ configurations in NN scattering was calculated as an average over kinematically allowed momenta using Eq. (1), where the Fourier-transformed NΔ wave function is weighted by the free Δ → Nπ width after subtracting the relative kinetic energy from the total center-of-mass energy. For ΔΔ states, a double integral over the relative momentum and the pion momentum from one decaying Δ was performed using Eq. (2), with the effective width taken as the average of the two single-Δ widths. The half-width was used as an imaginary constant potential input in the NΔ-channel Schrödinger equation.

## Key numbers
- The width of the ⁵D₀(NΔ) state from ¹S₀(NN) is totally negligible below Elab ≈ 600 MeV and only about 15–20 MeV above 800 MeV.
- The centrifugal barrier at the most influential interaction range (≈ 1 fm) is ≈ 40 × L(L+1) MeV.
- The ΔΔ width in the ⁷S₃(ΔΔ) state (I=0) from both ³D₃(NN) and ³G₃(NN) is less than a single free Δ width and agrees with the experimental resonance d'(2380) reported in WASA@COSY experiments.
- The ΔΔ threshold cusp is at 2440 MeV, while the d'(2380) resonance lies at 2380 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the author's earlier calculations (Niskanen, 2018) on improved agreement with experiment in pp ↔ dπ⁺ reactions, and is consistent with results from Gal and Garcilazo (various years) on narrow dibaryon states. The state-dependent width suppression provides an alternative explanation for the narrowness of the d'(2380) resonance discovered in WASA@COSY experiments, challenging the interpretation that this resonance is a "smoking gun" for quark exotics in intermediate-energy NN scattering. The calculations could be extended to isospin 2 or 3 states not directly coupled to two-nucleon scattering.
