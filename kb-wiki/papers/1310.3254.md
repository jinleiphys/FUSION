# Neutrinoless double beta decay of 48Ca in the shell model: Closure versus nonclosure approximation

> Neutrinoless double-beta decay is a unique process that could reveal physics beyond the Standard Model. Essential ingredients in the analysis of neutrinoless double-beta rates are the associated nuclear matrix elements. Most of the approaches used to calculate these matrix elements rely on the closure approximation. Here we analyze the light neutrino-exchange matrix elements of 48Ca neutrinoless double-beta decay and test the closure approximation in a shell-model approach. We calculate the neutrinoless double-beta nuclear matrix elements for 48Ca using both the closure approximation and a nonclosure approach, and we estimate the uncertainties associated with the closure approximation. We demonstrate that the nonclosure approach has excellent convergence properties which allow us to avoid unmanageable computational cost. Combining the nonclosure and closure approaches we propose a new method of calculation for neutrinoless double-beta decay rates which can be applied to the neutrinoless double-beta decay rates of heavy nuclei, such as 76Ge or 82Se.

## Key claim

The closure approximation underestimates the neutrinoless double-beta decay nuclear matrix element for ⁴⁸Ca by about 10% compared to the full nonclosure calculation, with the difference arising primarily from transitions to 1⁺ states in the intermediate nucleus ⁴⁸Sc.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations in the full pf shell using the GXPF1A interaction, computing the light neutrino-exchange nuclear matrix elements for ⁴⁸Ca both with the closure approximation (using an average intermediate energy ⟨E⟩ = 7.72 MeV) and with a nonclosure approach that explicitly sums over intermediate states in ⁴⁸Sc. To manage the large number of intermediate states (about 100,000), they introduced a cutoff on excitation energy and demonstrated that including only about 100 states per angular momentum Jκ gives accuracy better than 1%. They also developed a mixed method that combines nonclosure for low-lying states with closure for higher excitations to accelerate convergence.

## Key numbers

- The total nuclear matrix element M⁰ν for ⁴⁸Ca is 0.729 in the closure approximation and 0.800 in the nonclosure approach, a difference of 10% (AV18 SRC parametrization, ⟨E⟩ = 7.72 MeV)
- The Gamow-Teller matrix element M⁰ν_GT is 0.676 (closure) versus 0.747 (nonclosure), an 11% difference
- The Fermi matrix element M⁰ν_F is -0.204 (closure) versus -0.208 (nonclosure), a 2% difference
- The Tensor matrix element M⁰ν_T is -0.077 (closure) versus -0.079 (nonclosure), a 3% difference
- Including only the first 100 intermediate states per spin Jκ yields accuracy better than 1% for the total NME
- Using the mixed method, only about 20 states per Jκ (up to 7 MeV excitation energy) are needed for 1% accuracy
- The nonclosure NME for ⁴⁸Ca with different SRC parametrizations: no SRC gives 0.839, Miller-Spencer gives 0.568, CD-Bonn gives 0.875, AV18 gives 0.800

## Context

This work extends earlier shell-model analyses of ⁴⁸Ca neutrinoless double-beta decay that were limited to the f7/2 orbital only (P. Vogel and collaborators, 1990) by using the full pf shell with the GXPF1A interaction, which includes the crucial f5/2 spin-orbit partner. The results confirm the QRPA-based finding of Šimkovic et al. (2011) that nonclosure NME are about 10% larger than closure NME, but provide the first shell-model decomposition of the NME by intermediate spin Jκ. The mixed method proposed here enables future shell-model calculations of nonclosure NME for heavier nuclei such as ⁷⁶Ge and ⁸²Se, which were previously computationally prohibitive.
