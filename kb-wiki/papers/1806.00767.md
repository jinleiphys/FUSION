# Microscopic description of proton-induced spallation reactions with the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) Model

> We studied the complete dynamics of the proton-induced spallation process with the microscopic framework of the Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) Model. We performed calculations of proton-induced spallation reactions on 181Ta, 208Pb, and 238U targets with the CoMD model and compared the results with a standard two-step approach based on an intranuclear cascade model (INC) followed by a statistical deexcitation model. The calculations were also compared with recent experimental data from the literature. Our calculations showed an overall satisfactory agreement with the experimental data and suggest further improvements in the models. We point out that this CoMD study represents the first complete dynamical description of spallation reactions with a microscopic N-body approach and may lead to advancements in the physics-based modelling of the spallation process.

## Key claim
The Constrained Molecular Dynamics (CoMD) model provides the first complete dynamical description of proton-induced spallation reactions using a microscopic N-body approach, showing overall satisfactory agreement with experimental data for fission fragments, though it currently cannot reproduce the heavy residue mass distribution in its present form.

## Method
The authors performed CoMD simulations of proton-induced spallation on 181Ta, 208Pb, and 238U targets at 200, 500, and 1000 MeV, collecting approximately 10,000 events per reaction. They compared these results with a standard two-step approach using the intranuclear cascade code ISABEL followed by the Statistical Multifragmentation Model (SMM) for deexcitation, and with experimental data from the CHARMS collaboration and other literature.

## Key numbers
- Mean excitation energies of primary residues from p+208Pb at 200, 500, and 1000 MeV were 70, 110, and 170 MeV, respectively, calculated with the INC model.
- Maximum impact parameters for CoMD simulations were 7.0 fm (p+Ta), 7.5 fm (p+Pb), and 8.0 fm (p+U).
- Each CoMD event was followed up to 15000 fm/c (5×10−20 s).
- For the p+U reaction at 1000 MeV, CoMD-calculated IMF cross sections were lower than INC/SMM calculations by a factor of approximately 5–10.

## Context
This work extends the application of the CoMD model, previously used for fission at low and intermediate energy by Vonta (2015), to the complete spallation process. It contrasts with traditional two-stage approaches like the INC code ISABEL followed by SMM, as used by Filges (2001) and others. The results enable future improvements in microscopic modeling of spallation, particularly by addressing the need for inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions in CoMD and improving the description of heavy residue distributions.
