# Nucleon scalar and tensor charges using lattice QCD simulations at the physical value of the pion mass

> We present results on the light, strange and charm nucleon scalar and tensor charges from lattice QCD, using simulations with $N_f=2$ flavors of twisted mass Clover-improved fermions with a physical value of the pion mass. Both connected and disconnected contributions are included, enabling us to extract the isoscalar, strange and charm charges for the first time directly at the physical point. Furthermore, the renormalization is computed non-perturbatively for both isovector and isoscalar quantities. We investigate excited state effects by analyzing several sink-source time separations and by employing a set of methods to probe ground state dominance. Our final results for the scalar charges are $g_S^u = 5.20(42)(15)(12)$, $g_S^d = 4.27(26)(15)(12)$, $g_S^s=0.33(7)(1)(4)$, $g_S^c=0.062(13)(3)(5)$ and for the tensor charges $g_T^u = 0.782(16)(2)(13)$, $g_T^d = -0.219(10)(2)(13)$, $g_T^s=-0.00319(69)(2)(22)$, $g_T^c=-0.00263(269)(2)(37)$ in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ scheme at 2~GeV. The first error is statistical, the second is the systematic error due to the renormalization and the third the systematic arising from possible contamination due to the excited states.

## Key claim
Using lattice QCD simulations with two flavors of twisted mass clover-improved fermions at the physical pion mass, the authors present the first direct extraction of isoscalar, strange, and charm nucleon scalar and tensor charges including both connected and disconnected contributions, with the caveat that cut-off and finite volume effects cannot be evaluated since only a single gauge ensemble is analyzed.

## Method
The authors computed nucleon two-point and three-point correlation functions on a 48^3 x 96 lattice with lattice spacing a=0.0938(2) fm and pion mass 0.1305(4) GeV. Connected three-point functions were evaluated using the sequential inversion through the sink method for five sink-source separations (t_s/a = 10,12,14,16,18) for scalar charges and three separations (t_s/a = 10,12,14) for tensor charges. Disconnected contributions for isoscalar, strange, and charm quarks were computed using stochastic techniques with the one-end trick and the Truncated Solver Method, with non-perturbative renormalization applied for both isovector and isoscalar quantities.

## Key numbers
- Scalar charges in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV: g_S^u = 5.20(42)(15)(12), g_S^d = 4.27(26)(15)(12), g_S^s = 0.33(7)(1)(4), g_S^c = 0.062(13)(3)(5)
- Tensor charges in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV: g_T^u = 0.782(16)(2)(13), g_T^d = -0.219(10)(2)(13), g_T^s = -0.00319(69)(2)(22), g_T^c = -0.00263(269)(2)(37)
- Lattice spacing a = 0.0938(2) fm determined from the nucleon mass using approximately 215,000 statistics
- Pion mass m_pi = 0.1305(4) GeV with m_pi L = 2.98
- Renormalized quark masses at 2 GeV in MS-bar: m_s^R = 108.6(2.2) MeV, m_c^R = 1392.6(23.5) MeV (statistical errors only)

## Context
This work extends previous lattice QCD calculations of nucleon charges by including disconnected contributions for isoscalar, strange, and charm quarks directly at the physical pion mass, building on earlier studies by Abdel-Rehim (2015), Bhattacharya (2016), and others that evaluated isovector charges or used heavier pion masses. The results provide crucial input for interpreting dark matter direct detection experiments, where the scalar matrix element brings the largest uncertainty on the nucleon dark matter cross section, and for constraining new physics beyond the Standard Model through neutron beta decay and electric dipole moment measurements. Future work should address cut-off and finite volume effects by analyzing additional gauge ensembles.
