# Can we extract the pion electromagnetic form factor from a t-channel diagram only?

> We show that we are able to extract the pion electromagnetic form factor by using the recent charged pion electroproduction data from JLab and a simple t-channel diagram. For this purpose we have performed the Q2-independent and dependent analyses. The result of the first analysis is in good agreement with those of previous works and fit the Maris and Tandy model as well as the monopole parameterization which describes a pion radius of 0.672 fm. The result of the second analysis corroborates the findings in the first analysis. Our findings therefore provide a direct proof that at the given kinematics the t-channel diagram really dominates the process. This could also set a new constraint to the phenomenological models that try to describe the process.

# Can we extract the pion electromagnetic form factor from a t-channel diagram only?

## Key claim
The authors show that the pion electromagnetic form factor can be extracted from charged pion electroproduction data using only a simple t-channel diagram, provided the kinematics are restricted to small Mandelstam variable t and moderate Q². The extracted values agree with previous works, but the method's validity depends on the hadronic form factor cut-off choice and is limited to data sets where the t-channel truly dominates.

## Method
The authors fit the modified longitudinal differential cross section (t - m_π²)² dσ_L/dt to JLab electroproduction data using a t-channel amplitude that includes the pion electromagnetic form factor F_π, a monopole hadronic form factor F_h(t) = (1 - t/Λ_h²)⁻¹, and a constant g to absorb neglected contributions. They perform two analyses: a Q²-independent analysis where F_π is extracted at fixed Q² values, and a Q²-dependent analysis where F_π is parameterized as a monopole form F_π(Q²) = (1 + Q²/Λ_π²)⁻¹ and all data are fitted simultaneously to extract the pion charge radius, hadronic cut-off, and pion-nucleon coupling constant.

## Key numbers
- Pion charge radius of 0.672 fm (PDG value) used as reference for monopole parameterization
- Extracted F_π at Q² = 0.60 GeV²: 0.470 ± 0.037 (Λ_h = 0.85 GeV) and 0.438 ± 0.066 (Λ_h = 1.30 GeV)
- Extracted F_π at Q² = 1.00 GeV²: 0.393 ± 0.044 (Λ_h = 0.85 GeV) and 0.341 ± 0.074 (Λ_h = 1.30 GeV)
- Extracted F_π at Q² = 2.45 GeV²: 0.209 ± 0.017 (Λ_h = 0.85 GeV) and 0.153 ± 0.042 (Λ_h = 1.30 GeV)
- From Q²-dependent fit to all 36 data points: pion charge radius ⟨r_π²⟩^{1/2} = 0.576 ± 0.020 fm, Λ_h = 0.850 ± 0.034 GeV, g_πNN²/(4π) = 14.500 ± 0.827, χ²/N = 1.118
- From Q²-dependent fit to 18 selected data points: pion charge radius ⟨r_π²⟩^{1/2} = 0.625 ± 0.272 fm, Λ_h = 1.300 ± 0.230 GeV, χ²/N = 0.263

## Context
This work extends the extraction of the pion electromagnetic form factor from JLab electroproduction data (Horn et al., 2006; Tadevosyan et al., 2007) by using a simpler t-channel-only method that avoids the full Regge model employed in those prior extractions. The approach is a la Chew-Low and provides the first direct test of the t-channel dominance assumption that previous works merely assumed. The results enable a new constraint on phenomenological models of pion electroproduction: at the kinematics studied, contributions from other channels must vanish or be minimal. The method is limited by the hadronic form factor cut-off uncertainty (ranging from 0.4 to 1.5 GeV in the literature) and by the need for more precise longitudinal cross section data at smaller |t|.
