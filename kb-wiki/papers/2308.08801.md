# Compositeness of near-threshold exotic hadrons with decay and coupled-channel effects

> The near-threshold exotic hadrons such as $T_{cc}$ and $X(3872)$ are naively considered as the hadronic molecular state from the viewpoint of the low-energy universality. However, it is also known that the elementary dominant state is not completely excluded as the internal structure of the near-threshold states. Furthermore, the dominance of molecules is expected to be modified by the decay or coupled channels. We discuss these features of the near-threshold bound states by calculating the compositeness with the effective field theory.

## Key claim

The paper quantitatively demonstrates that while near-threshold bound states are composite dominant (hadronic molecular) in most of the parameter space, an elementary dominant state can still be realized even for very weakly bound states, but only with a significant fine tuning of the model parameter.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory model with a single-channel scattering Hamiltonian containing a bare state field coupled to two scattering particles. They derive an exact expression for the compositeness X of the bound state and vary the bare state energy parameter nu_0 to map the model dependence. They then extend the analysis to the actual exotic hadrons T_cc and X(3872) by introducing a complex coupling constant to account for decay effects and coupling to an isospin partner channel above threshold.

## Key numbers

- For a weakly bound state with binding energy B = 0.01 E_typ (where E_typ is the typical energy scale), the compositeness X is greater than 0.5 for 88% of the nu_0 parameter region, while elementary dominant states (X less than 0.5) occur in the remaining 12% with fine tuning.
- For T_cc, the eigenenergy used is E = -0.36 - 0.024i MeV, with binding energy B = 0.36 MeV and decay width Gamma = 0.048 MeV; the threshold energy difference to the coupled channel is 1.41 MeV.
- For X(3872), the eigenenergy used is E = -0.04 - 0.595i MeV, with binding energy B = 0.04 MeV and decay width Gamma = 1.19 MeV; the threshold energy difference to the coupled channel is 8.23 MeV.
- The cutoff Lambda is set to m_pi = 140 MeV, assuming pion exchange as the long-range interaction between D mesons.

## Context

This work extends the low-energy universality framework of Braaten (2004) and Naidon (2016) and the compositeness formalism of Hyodo (2011) and Aceti (2012) to include decay and coupled-channel effects. It builds on the earlier demonstration by Hanhart (2014) that elementary dominant states are possible with fine tuning, and applies the method to the experimentally observed exotic hadrons T_cc (LHCb, 2021) and X(3872) (Belle, 2003). The results enable quantitative assessment of whether specific near-threshold states are molecular or elementary, accounting for the modifications introduced by finite decay widths and coupled channels.
