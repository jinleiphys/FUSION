# The Linear Correlation Coefficient vs. the Cross Term in Bose-Einstein Correlations

> We investigate the nature of the new cross term for Gaussian parameterizations of Bose-Einstein correlations of identical particles emitted from purely chaotic hadron sources formed by relativistic heavy ion collisions. We find that this additional parameter in the so-called Bertsch parameterization can be expressed in terms of a linear ``out-longitudinal'' correlation coefficient for emission of bosons and two already known ``radius'' parameters, $R_l$ and $R_o$. The linear correlation coefficient is of kinematical nature and can be used to determine the widths of longitudinal momentum distributions.

## Key claim

The cross-term radius parameter \(R_{ol}\) in the generalized Bertsch parameterization of Bose-Einstein correlations can be expressed as a linear "out-longitudinal" correlation coefficient \(\rho_{ol}\) multiplied by the two known radius parameters \(R_o\) and \(R_l\), where \(\rho_{ol}\) is a kinematical quantity bounded by \(|\rho_{ol}| \leq 1\) and can be used to determine the widths of longitudinal momentum distributions.

## Method

The authors analytically relate the cross-term \(R_{ol}^2\) in the Gaussian Bertsch parameterization to a linear correlation coefficient \(\rho_{ol}\) via \(R_{ol}^2 = -\rho_{ol} R_o R_l\). They then compute \(\rho_{ol}\) and other correlation coefficients from two sources: the microscopic transport model RQMD for 200 AGeV S+S central collisions, and the hydrodynamical code HYLANDER for 160 AGeV Pb+Pb central collisions, analyzing negative kaon emission as a function of pair rapidity.

## Key numbers

- For 200 AGeV S+S central collisions from RQMD, no correlation between \(q_l\) and \(q_o\) is seen in the mid-rapidity window, while sizable correlations are found in other rapidity windows.
- For 160 AGeV Pb+Pb collisions from HYLANDER at \(K_\perp = 1.5\) GeV/c, the linear correlation coefficient between emission time and transverse coordinate satisfies \(|\rho_{t, x}| \approx 0.85\) in the non-trivial region (NTR) of the freeze-out hypersurface.
- For the same system, the linear correlation coefficient between emission time and longitudinal coordinate satisfies \(|\rho_{t, z}| \approx 1.0\) in the simple wave region (SWR).
- The kaon-pair rapidity boundary between the NTR and SWR is \(|y_K| = 1.4\) for this calculation.
- The Full Widths at Half Maximum of the rapidity spectra of negative kaons nearly coincide with the positions of extrema of the corresponding \(\rho_{ol}\) values.

## Context

This work extends the generalized Bertsch parameterization introduced by Chapman, Scotto, and Heinz (1995), which showed that a purely chaotic hadron source requires an additional cross-term radius \(R_{ol}\) beyond the three standard radius parameters. The paper resolves the interpretive difficulty by showing that \(R_{ol}\) can be replaced by the kinematical correlation coefficient \(\rho_{ol}\), which is directly proportional to the correlation between transverse and longitudinal emission coordinates \(\rho_{x,z}\). This enables experimental determination of particle rapidity distributions and finite-size effects of fireballs in rapidity space, and the framework can be generalized to include further correlation coefficients \(\rho_{os}\) and \(\rho_{sl}\) for collisions without azimuthal symmetry.
