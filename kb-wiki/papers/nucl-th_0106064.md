# Restoration of broken symmetries in Self-Consistent RPA

> It is shown that the Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach allows in a very natural way to restore symmetries, spontaneously broken on the mean field level. This is achieved via the introduction of a second Lagrange multiplier which constrains the variance of the symmetry operator to zero. This important feature of SCRPA, here pointed out for the first time, is illustrated employing a simplified model of the nuclear superfluidity.

# Restoration of broken symmetries in Self-Consistent RPA

## Key claim

The Self-Consistent RPA (SCRPA) approach can restore symmetries spontaneously broken at the mean-field level by introducing a second Lagrange multiplier that constrains the variance of the symmetry operator to zero, but this is demonstrated only for a simplified seniority model of nuclear superfluidity, not for a general nuclear system.

## Method

The authors start from the seniority model Hamiltonian and add a constrained Hamiltonian with two Lagrange multipliers: one for the mean particle number and one for the mean squared particle number. They transform to Bogoliubov quasiparticles, derive the SCQRPA secular equations from the equation-of-motion approach, and obtain coupled mean-field equations for the one-body amplitudes (u, v) and the RPA amplitudes (x, y). The two number equations for N and N^2 are solved to determine the amplitudes, while the mean-field equations determine the Lagrange multipliers.

## Key numbers

- The second Lagrange multiplier is found to be μ₂ = G/4, independent of particle number N, which matches the exact value from the derivative of the exact ground state energy with respect to N².
- The chemical potential is μ₁ = -G/2 (Ω + 1), again matching the exact value.
- The RPA eigenvalue E = 0 is obtained, indicating a Goldstone mode (spurious mode) at zero energy, signifying exact particle number symmetry restoration.
- The exact ground state energy of the seniority model is E₀ = -G/2 (Ω + 1) N + G/4 N², which is recovered by the SCQRPA calculation.

## Context

This work extends the Self-Consistent RPA approach developed by Dukelsky and Schuck (1990s) and earlier by Hara, Rowe, and Zimmermann and Röpke (Cluster-Hartree-Fock method). It addresses a known difficulty: standard RPA restores broken symmetries via the Goldstone theorem, but going beyond standard RPA typically breaks conservation laws. The paper shows that SCRPA, being variational and non-perturbative, can maintain symmetry restoration through the second constraint, unlike earlier attempts by Lipkin and Nogami (1960s) to fix the second moment in HF/HFB approaches. This enables future application of the method to more realistic nuclear systems with deformation or superfluidity.
