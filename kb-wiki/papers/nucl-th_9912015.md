# Pion and Kaon Electromagnetic Form Factors in a $SU_{L}(3)\otimes SU_{R}(3)$ Effective Lagrangian

> A SU(2) effective lagrangian is extended to a $SU_{L}(3)\otimes SU_{R}(3)$ by including the vector and axial vector meson. With this effective lagrangian, electromagnetic form factors of charged pion and kaon are calculated. The pseudoscalar meson loops are taken into account. Good agreement with experimental data is obtained for those form factors. Decay widths of $ρ\to ππ$ and $ϕ\to K^{+}K^{-}$ are also calculated and shown to agree with experimental data very well.

# Pion and Kaon Electromagnetic Form Factors in a SU_L(3) ⊗ SU_R(3) Effective Lagrangian

## Key claim

The authors extend a previous SU(2) effective chiral Lagrangian to SU(3) by including vector and axial-vector mesons through non-linear realization of chiral symmetry, and claim that with only mass and kinetic terms for spin-1 mesons plus pseudoscalar meson loops, they reproduce experimental pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors and decay widths without needing higher-order terms from chiral perturbation theory. The agreement depends on introducing a scalar meson interaction term to generate strange/non-strange mass splittings and on fixing the parameters g = 5.36 and β = 0.32 from the ρ → ππ decay width and the renormalized γ-ρ coupling.

## Method

The authors construct an SU_L(3) ⊗ SU_R(3) effective Lagrangian with pseudoscalar mesons (π, K), vector mesons (ρ, ω, φ), and axial-vector mesons, using non-linear realization of chiral symmetry. They include a scalar meson interaction term L_S to generate mass splittings between strange and non-strange particles, remove unphysical mixings (π-A, V-γ, nonet mixing) through field redefinitions, and compute the pion and kaon electromagnetic form factors in both time-like and space-like regions by including ρ and φ meson self-energies from pseudoscalar meson loops, regularized with Pauli-Villars method (Λ₁ = 1 GeV). The ρ-ω mixing is also incorporated for the pion form factor, and ρ, ω, and φ contributions are included for the kaon form factor.

## Key numbers

- Physical ρ meson mass: m_ρP ≈ 771 MeV; bare mass: ṁ_ρ ≈ 808 MeV; ρ → ππ decay width: Γ_ρ→ππ ≈ 150 MeV
- Physical φ meson mass: m_φP ≈ 1019 MeV; bare mass: ṁ_φ ≈ 940 MeV; φ → K⁺K⁻ width: Γ_φ→K⁺K⁻ ≈ 2.32 MeV; φ → K_S⁰K_L⁰ width: Γ_φ→K_S⁰K_L⁰ ≈ 1.517 MeV
- Charged pion mean square radius: ⟨r_π²⟩ = 0.447 fm² (experimental: 0.44 ± 0.01 fm²)
- Charged kaon mean square radius: ⟨r_K±²⟩ = 0.332 fm² (experimental: 0.34 ± 0.05 fm²)
- Neutral kaon mean square radius: ⟨r_K⁰²⟩ = -0.0549 fm² (experimental: -0.054 ± 0.0026 fm²)
- ρ-ω mixing parameter: z_ρω = -4.52 × 10⁻³ GeV²; ω meson width: Γ_ω = 8.4 MeV
- Coupling constants: g = 5.36, β = Gr² = 0.32, g_ρππ = 6.037, Re[g_φ(q²)] ≈ 6.5

## Context

This work extends the SU(2) effective Lagrangian of Park, Han, Cheoun, and Lee (1996) to SU(3), providing an alternative to the hidden gauge symmetry approach of Bando, Kugo, and Yamawaki (1988) and the massive Yang-Mills approach reviewed by Meissner (1988). The authors show their Lagrangian reproduces the O(p⁴) coefficients of chiral perturbation theory (Ecker, Gasser, Pich, and de Rafael, 1989) when vector mesons are integrated out, and they incorporate the ρ-ω mixing treatment of Klingl, Kaiser, and Weise (1996). The results enable calculation of pseudoscalar meson electromagnetic form factors and radii within a unified effective field theory framework without explicit higher-order terms, and the logarithmic divergence of the pion radius in the chiral limit is shown to be consistent with chiral perturbation theory.
