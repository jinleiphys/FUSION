# Coherence phenomena between initial and final state radiation in a dense QCD medium

> In this work we investigate medium modifications to the interference pattern between initial and final state radiation. We compute single gluon production off a highly energetic parton that undergoes a hard scattering and subsequently crosses a dense QCD medium of finite size. We extend our previous studies obtained at first order in opacity by providing general results for multiple soft scatterings and their specific formulation within the harmonic oscillator approximation. We show that there is a gradual onset of decoherence between the initial and final state radiation due to multiple scatterings, that opens the phase space for large angle emissions. By examining the multiplicity of produced gluons, we observe a potentially large double logarithmic enhancement for dense media and small opening angles. This result points to a possible modification of the evolution equations due to a QCD medium of finite size. We briefly comment on the phenomenological consequences of this setup in high-energy nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that multiple soft scatterings in a dense QCD medium cause a gradual decoherence between initial and final state radiation, opening phase space for large-angle emissions; this decoherence is not captured by the first-order-in-opacity approximation used in the authors' prior work (Armesto et al., 2012).

## Method

The authors compute single gluon production off a highly energetic parton that undergoes a hard scattering and subsequently traverses a dense QCD medium of finite size, using a semiclassical approach based on linearized Classical Yang-Mills equations in light-cone gauge. They model the medium as a background gauge field with a Gaussian white noise distribution and evaluate the gluon spectrum within the harmonic oscillator approximation, which allows analytic treatment of multiple soft scatterings via path integrals. The total spectrum is decomposed into six contributions depending on whether the gluon is emitted before or after the hard scattering, and inside or outside the medium.

## Key numbers

* The medium transport coefficient \(\hat{q}\) appears in the harmonic oscillator frequency \(\Omega = \frac{1-i}{2}\sqrt{\hat{q}/k^+}\), where \(k^+\) is the gluon light-cone momentum.
* The dipole S-matrix in the harmonic oscillator approximation takes the form \(\S_{osc}(y^+,x^+;\r) = \exp\left(-\frac{1}{4}\hat{q}\r^2(y^+-x^+)\right)\).
* The path integral kernel in the harmonic oscillator approximation is \({\cal K}_{osc}(x^+,\x;y^+,\y|k^+) = \frac{A}{\pi i}\,\exp \left[ i\, AB (\x^2+\y^2)-2iA\,\x\cdot\y\right]\) with \(A = \frac{k^+\Omega}{2\sin(\Omega\Delta\tau)}\) and \(B = \cos(\Omega\Delta\tau)\), where \(\Delta\tau = x^+-y^+\).
* The medium has finite longitudinal size \(L^+\); the hard scattering occurs at \(x^+_0 = 0\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Armesto et al., 2012) from a dilute medium modeled by a single hard scattering to a dense medium with multiple soft scatterings, using the harmonic oscillator approximation common in jet quenching literature (Zakharov, 1996; Baier et al., 1998; Wiedemann, 2000). It builds on recent studies of color coherence in in-medium color antennas (Mehtar-Tani et al., 2010; Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2011) and suggests that the observed double logarithmic enhancement of gluon multiplicity for dense media and small opening angles may require modification of the usual perturbative evolution equations for jets in finite-size QCD media.
