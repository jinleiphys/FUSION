# Unpolarized GPDs at small $x$ and non-zero skewness

> We study the small-$x$ asymptotics of unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs). Unlike the previous works in the literature, we consider the case of non-zero (but small) skewness while allowing for non-linear contributions to the evolution equations. We show that unpolarized GPDs and GTMDs at small $x$ are related to the eikonal dipole amplitude $N$, whose small-$x$ evolution is given by the BK/JIMWLK evolution equations, and to the odderon amplitude $\cal O$, whose evolution is also known in the literature. We show that the effect of non-zero skewness $ξ\neq 0$ is to modify the value of the evolution parameter (rapidity) in the arguments for the dipole amplitudes $N$ and $\cal O$ from $Y = \ln (1/x)$ to $Y = \ln \min \left\{ 1/|x| , 1/|ξ| \right\}$.

## Key claim

The authors show that for unpolarized generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and generalized transverse momentum distributions (GTMDs) at small $x$ and small but non-zero skewness $\xi$, the effect of non-zero skewness is to modify the evolution parameter (rapidity) in the dipole amplitudes from $Y = \ln (1/x)$ to $Y = \ln \min \left\{ 1/|x| , 1/|\xi| \right\}$, with this prescription holding even when non-linear contributions to the evolution equations are included.

## Method

The authors relate unpolarized gluon and quark GPDs and GTMDs at small $x$ and small skewness to the eikonal dipole amplitude $N$ and the odderon amplitude $\mathcal{O}$ using the Light Cone Operator Treatment (LCOT) formalism. They then analyze the kinematics of ladder diagrams and general gluon cascades in light-cone perturbation theory for non-forward elastic scattering, imposing the lifetime ordering and minus-momentum ordering conditions that define the leading logarithmic approximation, to determine how the longitudinal momentum transfer (skewness) affects the rapidity cutoff in the evolution of the dipole amplitudes.

## Key numbers

* The gluon unpolarized GPD at small $x$ and $\xi$ is given by $H^g (|x| \ll 1, |\xi| \ll 1, t, Q^2) = - \frac{N_c}{2 \pi^2 \, \alpha_s} \, \int d^2 b_{\perp} \, e^{i \mathbf{\Delta} \cdot \mathbf{b}} \, \left[ \left( \nabla_{\mathbf{x}_1} \cdot \nabla_{\mathbf{x}_0} \right) N_{10} (Y) \right]_{x_{10}^2 = 1/Q^2}$, where $N_c$ is the number of colors and $\alpha_s$ is the QCD coupling.
* The evolution parameter (rapidity) for the dipole amplitudes is modified from $Y = \ln (1/x)$ to $Y = \ln \min \left\{ 1/|x| , 1/|\xi| \right\}$ when non-zero skewness is included.
* The leading logarithmic contribution from $n$ steps of evolution gives $\frac{1}{n!}\alpha_s^{n} \ln^{n} (z/z_{\min})$ with $z_{\min} = \max \left\{ \Lambda^2/s, |\zeta| \right\}$, where $\Lambda$ is an infrared scale characterizing the target and $\zeta = \Delta^- / 2 q^-$.

## Context

This work extends the small-$x$/saturation formalism for GPDs beyond the conventional approximation of zero skewness used in prior dipole model calculations (Kovchegov 1999, Kowalski 2006, Mantysaari 2016). It generalizes the non-forward small-$x$ evolution result derived by Bartels (1981) for the linear BFKL equation to the full non-linear evolution described by the Balitsky-Kovchegov (BK) and Jalilian-Marian-Iancu-McLerran-Weigert-Leodinov-Kovner (JIMWLK) equations. The results enable more rigorous treatment of skewness effects in exclusive processes like Deeply Virtual Compton Scattering (DVCS) and Deeply Virtual Meson Production (DVMP) at small $x$, relevant for the upcoming Electron Ion Collider.
