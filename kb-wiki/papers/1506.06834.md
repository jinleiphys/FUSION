# Effects of a $N^*$ resonance with hidden charm in the $π^- p \to D^- Σ_c^+$ reaction near threshold

> We study the effect of a hidden charm nuclear excited state $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ in the $π^- p \to D^- Σ_c^+$ reaction near threshold using an effective Lagrangian approach. We calculate the background contribution of the $t$ and $u$ channels by the $D^{*0}$ vector meson exchange and $Σ_c^{++}$ intermediate state, respectively. We show that the consideration of a $N^*_{c\bar{c}}$ resonance provides an enhancement of the total cross section close to the reaction threshold. We also evaluate the differential cross section for different energies and we study the angle dependence. It is expected that our model calculations will be tested in future experiments.

## Key claim

The hidden charm resonance \(N^*_{c\bar{c}}(4261)\) produces a clear enhancement in the total cross section of the \(\pi^- p \to D^- \Sigma_c^+\) reaction near threshold, but this signal is only visible when the resonance contribution is added to the background from \(t\)-channel \(D^{*0}\) exchange and \(u\)-channel \(\Sigma_c^{++}\) exchange; the background alone produces a much smaller cross section near threshold.

## Method

The authors used an effective Lagrangian approach to compute the tree-level Feynman diagrams for the \(\pi^- p \to D^- \Sigma_c^+\) reaction. The background was calculated from the \(t\)-channel exchange of a \(D^{*0}\) vector meson and the \(u\)-channel exchange of a \(\Sigma_c^{++}\) intermediate state, while the resonance contribution came from an \(s\)-channel \(N^*_{c\bar{c}}(4261)\) state. Coupling constants were fixed using partial decay widths from the literature (e.g., \(\Gamma(N^*_{c\bar{c}} \to \pi N) = 3.8\) MeV) and \(SU(4)\) symmetry relations, and form factors with a cutoff \(\Lambda = 2.5\) GeV were applied to all hadronic vertices.

## Key numbers

- Mass of the \(N^*_{c\bar{c}}(4261)\) resonance: \(M_{N^*} = 4261.87\) MeV.
- Partial decay width of \(N^*_{c\bar{c}} \to \pi N\): \(\Gamma = 3.8\) MeV, yielding coupling \(g_{\pi N N^*} = 0.1\).
- Coupling of \(N^*_{c\bar{c}}\) to \(\bar{D} \Sigma_c\): \(g_{N^* \Sigma_c \bar{D}} = 3.13\) (from Xiao (2013)).
- Coupling \(g_{D^*D\pi} = 14.1\), \(g_{\Sigma_c N D^*} = -7.8\), \(g_{D N \Sigma_c} = 2.69\), \(g_{\pi \Sigma_c \Sigma_c} = 10.76\).
- Cutoff parameter for all form factors: \(\Lambda = 2.5\) GeV.
- Total cross section shows a peak near threshold at \(W \approx 4.35\) GeV when the \(N^*_{c\bar{c}}\) resonance is included; the background alone (dotted line) is much smaller at that energy.
- Differential cross sections are shown at \(W = 4.35\) GeV, \(4.45\) GeV, and \(4.55\) GeV; the angular dependence is weak at threshold but becomes significant at higher energies.

## Context

This work extends the study of hidden charm baryon resonances, specifically the \(N^*_{c\bar{c}}(4261)\) predicted by Wu (2010) and Xiao (2013) as a \(\bar{D}\Sigma_c\) bound state, into the \(\pi^- p \to D^- \Sigma_c^+\) channel. It follows the effective Lagrangian approach used by Xie (2015) for \(\pi^- p \to D^- D^0 p\) and by Wu (2014) for \(\pi^- p \to K^0 \Lambda\). The results suggest that the \(\pi^- p \to D^- \Sigma_c^+\) reaction at J-PARC pion beam energies (above 20 GeV) could provide a clean signal for the \(N^*_{c\bar{c}}(4261)\) resonance, complementing earlier studies of \(\pi^- p \to \eta_c n\) by Wang (2015) and photo-production by Huang (2013), where the background was found to be problematic.
