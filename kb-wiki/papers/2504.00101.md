# Open quantum system approach to inclusive jet production in heavy-ion collisions

> We derive a factorization formula for inclusive jet production in heavy-ion collisions using the tools of Effective Field Theory (EFT). We show how physics at widely separated scales in this process can be systematically separated by matching to EFTs at successively lower virtualities. Owing to a strong scale separation, we recover a vacuum-like DGLAP evolution above the jet scale, while the additional low-energy scales induced by the medium effectively probe the internal structure of the jet. As a result, the cross section can be written as a series with an increasing number of subjets characterized by perturbative matching coefficients each of which is convolved with a {\it distinct} function. These functions encode broadening, medium-induced radiations as well as quantum interference such as the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect and color coherence dynamics to all orders in perturbation theory. As a first application of this EFT framework, we investigate the case of an unresolved jet and show how the cross section can be factorized and fully separate the jet dynamics from the universal physics of the medium. To compare to the existing literature, we explicitly compute the medium jet function at next-to-leading order in the coupling and leading order in medium opacity.

## Key claim

The paper derives a factorization formula for inclusive jet production in heavy-ion collisions using Effective Field Theory (EFT), showing that when the jet scale \(p_T R\) is much larger than the medium scale \(Q_{\rm med}\), the cross section can be written as a series with an increasing number of subjets, each convolved with a distinct function that encodes medium effects. The authors explicitly compute the medium jet function at next-to-leading order in the coupling and leading order in medium opacity only for the case of an unresolved jet where the medium cannot resolve the jet's internal color structure.

## Method

The authors employ Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) extended with Glauber modes to systematically separate physics at widely separated scales by matching to EFTs at successively lower virtualities. They identify relevant momentum modes (hard-collinear, collinear-soft, collinear, and soft) based on the hierarchy \(p_T \gg p_T R \gtrsim E_{\rm out} \gg Q_{\rm med} \gtrsim T \sim \Lambda_{\rm QCD}\) and use an open quantum system approach to treat the jet as interacting with the thermal medium environment. The factorization is derived by analyzing the Lund plane phase space and identifying which modes contribute at leading power in the expansion parameters \(\beta = E_{\rm out}/p_T\) and \(\delta = Q_{\rm med}/E_{\rm out} \sim R\).

## Key numbers

* The medium scale \(Q_{\rm med}^2 = \hat{q}L\) is estimated as \(Q_{\rm med} \sim 1-3\) GeV based on previous leading-order calculations and models fitted to data.
* The medium temperature is taken as \(T \sim 0.5-1\) GeV, which is achievable in current collider experiments.
* The jet transport parameter is estimated as \(\hat{q} \sim 1-2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm and medium length \(L \sim 2-5\) fm, giving \(Q_{\rm med} \sim 1-3\) GeV.
* The decoherence angle \(\theta_c\) is estimated to lie in the range \(\{0.02, 0.2\}\) for most phenomenological purposes.
* The nuclear modification factor is approximated as \(R_{AA} \sim (1 + E_{\rm out}/p_T)^{-n}\) with \(n \sim 5\), implying \(E_{\rm out} \lesssim p_T/n \ll p_T\).

## Context

This work extends the factorization framework for jet production in proton-proton collisions (Dasgupta 2014, Kang 2016, Dai 2016) to heavy-ion collisions by incorporating medium effects through an EFT approach. It builds on previous SCET applications to jet quenching (Idilbi 2008, D'Eramo 2010, Ovanesyan 2011) and on the non-linear DGLAP evolution approach for medium-modified jets (Mehtar-Tani 2017, 2021, 2024). The framework enables systematic, analytic calculations of inclusive jet observables in heavy-ion collisions and can be generalized to jet substructure observables and jet correlations.
