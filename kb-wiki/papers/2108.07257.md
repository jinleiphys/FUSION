# Resummation of the Sivers asymmetry in heavy flavor dijet production at the Electron-Ion Collider

> We review our transverse momentum dependent factorization and resummation formalism for heavy flavor dijet production at the EIC. In this formalism, we have calculated the heavy flavor mass corrections in the collinear-soft and jet functions, and in the resummed expression for the cross section. By establishing this formalism, we then study the effects of the mass corrections by providing predictions at the EIC for the massive case and for the case where the mass is neglected. We find that the heavy flavor mass effects can give sizable corrections to the predicted asymmetry.

## Key claim
The heavy flavor mass corrections to the Sivers asymmetry in dijet production at the EIC are sizable, but for charm dijets the mass corrections are comparable in size to the theoretical scale uncertainties, meaning a higher perturbative order is needed to resolve the mass effect; for bottom dijets the mass corrections exceed the theoretical uncertainties and could be important for future global analyses.

## Method
The authors established a transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) factorization and resummation formalism for heavy flavor dijet production in polarized proton-electron scattering at the EIC, working in the kinematic region where the dijet imbalance \(q_T \lesssim m_{\mathcal{Q}} \lesssim p_T R \ll p_T\). They calculated the one-loop anomalous dimensions for the global soft function, the heavy-flavor jet function, and the collinear-soft function including finite quark mass corrections, then solved the renormalization group evolution equations to obtain resummed expressions for both the unpolarized cross section and the spin-dependent cross section sensitive to the gluon Sivers function.

## Key numbers
- Charm dijet production: \(5\ \text{GeV} < p_T < 10\ \text{GeV}\), charm quark mass \(m_c = 1.5\ \text{GeV}\), jet radius \(R = 0.6\), rapidity \(|y_J| < 4.5\)
- Bottom dijet production: \(10\ \text{GeV} < p_T < 15\ \text{GeV}\), bottom quark mass \(m_b = 5\ \text{GeV}\), jet radius \(R = 0.6\), rapidity \(|y_J| < 4.5\)
- Electron beam energy 20 GeV, proton beam energy 250 GeV
- Projected Sivers asymmetry magnitude: roughly \(0.1\%\)
- For charm dijets, mass corrections are close in magnitude to the theoretical uncertainties from scale variations; for bottom dijets, mass corrections are larger than the theoretical uncertainties

## Context
This work extends the TMD factorization and resummation formalism previously applied to light-flavor dijet production at the EIC to the heavy flavor case, building on the PYTHIA reweighting analysis of Zheng et al. (2018) which identified dijet production as the cleanest probe of the gluon Sivers asymmetry. The results enable future global analyses of gluon TMD PDFs at the EIC using heavy flavor dijets, particularly for bottom quark production where mass corrections must be included. The formalism also provides a framework for studying mass effects in other TMD observables at the EIC.
