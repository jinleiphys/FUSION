# Trinucleon Photo Reactions with $Δ$-Isobar Excitation: Radiative Nucleon-Deuteron Capture and Two-Body Photo Disintegration of the Three-Nucleon Bound State

> Radiative nucleon-deuteron capture and photo disintegration of the three-nucleo n bound state with two-body final states are described. The description uses nucleon degrees of freedom extended to include the excitation of a single nucleon to a \diso. The baryonic interaction and the electromagnetic cu rrent couple nucleonic states and states with a \diso. Exact solutions of three-p article scattering equations are employed for the initial or final states of the reactions. The current has one-baryon and two-baryon contributions. The role of t he \diso in the description of the considered photo reactions is discussed and fo und to be moderate. The spin observables $A_{yy}$ and $T_{20}$ at $90^{\circ}$ la b scattering angle can be calculated model-independently from the $E1$ Siegert te rm in the long-wavelength limit.

## Key claim

The role of the Delta-isobar in radiative nucleon-deuteron capture and two-body photo disintegration of the three-nucleon bound state is found to be moderate; at lower energies the effect arises mainly from the shift in triton binding energy and the corresponding change in nucleonic components of the bound-state wave function, while at higher energies it comes dominantly from changes in the scattering wave function and in the electromagnetic current due to the Delta-isobar.

## Method

The authors employ a coupled-channel two-baryon potential between nucleons that allows excitation of a single nucleon to a Delta-isobar, using exact solutions of three-particle scattering equations for the initial or final states. The electromagnetic current includes one-baryon and two-baryon contributions with transitions to states containing a single Delta-isobar, and the hadronic interaction is treated via a separable expansion of the two-baryon transition matrix. Calculations are performed for triton (³H) rather than ³He, as the Coulomb interaction between two protons is not taken into account.

## Key numbers

- Reaction energies are below 50 MeV in the three-nucleon center-of-mass system.
- Below 30 MeV deuteron lab energy, the spin observables A_yy and T_20 at 90° lab scattering angle can be obtained model-independently from the E1 Siegert term in the long-wavelength limit.

## Context

This work extends the description of elastic and inelastic nucleon-deuteron scattering previously given by Nemoto, Chmielewski, and others (1998) to trinucleon photo reactions, aiming to find clear indications for the working of a three-nucleon force and corresponding two- and three-nucleon electromagnetic exchange currents. The calculation is more complete with respect to the employed current than the separable expansion work of Fonseca and Lehman (1993, 2000), and adds the Delta-isobar degree of freedom as a new theoretical dimension compared to the realistic descriptions of Anklin and Golak (2000). The results enable future comparisons with experimental data on ³He photo reactions and can help isolate Delta-isobar effects in three-nucleon dynamics.
