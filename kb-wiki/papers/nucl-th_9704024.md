# Coherent two pion photoproduction on 12C

> We develop the formalism for coherent two pion photoproduction in nuclei and perform actual calculations of cross sections for $π^-π^+$ and $π^0π^0$ photoproduction on $^{12}C$. We find that due to the isospin symmetry the cross section for $π^0π^0$ production is very small and has a maximum when the pions propagate together. However, the kinematical region where the energies and polar angles of the two $π^0$ mesons are equal and their relative azimuthal angle $ϕ=180^0$ is forbidden. Conversely in the $π^-π^+$ production the pions prefer to have a relative azimuthal angle 180$^0$ and the production of the pions propagating together is suppressed. The dominant one-body mechanism in both channels is related to the excitation of the $Δ$ isobar. Hence the reaction can serve as a source of information about $Δ$'s properties in nucleus. We have found that the reaction is sensitive to effects of the pion and $Δ$ renormalization in the nuclear medium, similar to those found in the coherent $(γ,π^0)$ reaction, but magnified because of the presence of the two pions.

# Coherent two pion photoproduction on 12C

## Key claim

The cross section for coherent $(\gamma,\pi^0\pi^0)$ on $^{12}C$ is very small and has a maximum when the two pions propagate together, but the kinematical region where the energies and polar angles of the two $\pi^0$ mesons are equal and their relative azimuthal angle $\phi=180^\circ$ is forbidden; conversely, in $(\gamma,\pi^-\pi^+)$ the pions prefer a relative azimuthal angle of $180^\circ$ and propagation together is suppressed. These features follow from isospin symmetry and Bose statistics rather than from the detailed reaction mechanism, so experimental deviations would indicate isospin violation.

## Method

The authors developed a formalism for coherent two-pion photoproduction on spin-zero nuclei using the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) and extended it to the Distorted Wave Impulse Approximation (DWIA) in momentum space to include final-state interactions of the pions with the residual nucleus. The elementary amplitude was taken from a previously developed model for $(\gamma,\pi\pi)$ on free nucleons, which includes $\Delta(1232)$, $N^*(1440)$, and $N^*(1520)$ resonances, with the dominant one-body mechanism being $\Delta$ isobar excitation via the Kroll-Ruderman term. Medium modifications of the $\Delta$ propagator were incorporated through Pauli blocking, $\Delta$ self-energy, and the Landau-Migdal force with $g_\Delta' = 0.55$.

## Key numbers

- The differential cross section for $(\gamma,\pi^-\pi^+)$ at $\phi=0$ (pions propagating together) is about 0.1 nb at $E_\gamma = 450$ MeV, $\theta_1=\theta_2=15^\circ$.
- The $(\gamma,\pi^0\pi^0)$ cross section in the region where pions propagate together is about three orders of magnitude smaller than the $(\gamma,\pi^-\pi^+)$ cross section at $\phi=180^\circ$.
- Born terms contribute only about 1-2% of the $(\gamma,\pi^-\pi^+)$ cross section over a large photon energy region.
- At $E_\gamma = 450$ MeV, DWIA results using different off-shell extrapolations (cut-off parameter $\Lambda = 500$, 1000, 1500 MeV) differ by about 50%.
- At $E_\gamma > 500$ MeV, final-state interactions reduce the total cross section by a factor of 4-5.
- At $350 < E_\gamma < 450$ MeV, final-state interactions can increase the cross section by up to a factor of two.
- The $\pi^0\pi^0$ channel total cross section is about 1000 times smaller than the $\pi^-\pi^+$ channel.
- The $\Delta$ resonance region corresponds to pion kinetic energy around $T_\pi = 180$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of coherent single-pion photoproduction (studied by Oset (1982), Koch (1984), and Carrasco (1993)) to the two-pion case, which had not been studied theoretically or experimentally before. The reaction is sensitive to pion and $\Delta$ renormalization in the nuclear medium, similar to effects found in coherent $(\gamma,\pi^0)$ reactions but magnified by the presence of two pions. The predictions are intended to guide forthcoming experiments at facilities like the Mainz Microtron and TJNAF, where $(\gamma,\pi\pi)$ reactions on the proton are already being investigated.
