# Is the Aharonov-Bohm phase shift for a non-closed path a measurable quantity ?

> There recently appear some interesting attempts to explain the AB-effect through the interaction between the charged particle and the solenoid current mediated by the exchange of a virtual photon. A vital assumption of this approach is that AB-phase shift is proportional to the change of the interaction energy between the charged particle and solenoid along the path of the moving charge. Accordingly, they insist that the AB-phase change along a path does not depend on the gauge choice so that the AB-phase shift for a non-closed path is in principle measurable. We however notice the existence of two fairly different discussions on the interaction energy between the solenoid and a charge particle, the one is due to Boyer and the other is due to Saldanha and others. In the present paper, based on a self-contained quantum mechanical treatment of the combined system of a solenoid, a charged particle, and the quantized electromagnetic fields, we show that both interaction energies of Boyer and of Saldanha are in fact gauge invariant at least for non-singular gauge transformations but they are destined to cancel each other. Our analysis rather shows that the origin of the AB-phase can be traced back to other part of our effective Hamiltonian. Furthermore, based on the path-integral formalism with our effective Lagrangian, we explicitly demonstrate that the AB-phase shift for a non-closed path is not a gauge-variant quantity, which means that it would not correspond to direct experimental observables.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Aharonov-Bohm phase shift for a non-closed path is a gauge-dependent quantity and therefore does not correspond to a direct experimental observable, contradicting recent claims by Boyer (1971), Saldanha (2021), and others that such a phase shift is gauge-invariant and measurable. The author shows that the interaction energies derived by Boyer and by Saldanha have opposite signs and cancel each other, so the AB-phase cannot be attributed to these interaction energies.

## Method

The author constructs a self-contained quantum mechanical treatment of the combined system of a solenoid, a nonrelativistic charged particle, and the quantized electromagnetic fields using two equivalent Hamiltonians (scheme A and scheme B) in the Coulomb gauge. By decomposing the total electromagnetic field into parts generated by the external solenoid current and by the moving charge, and using the nontrivial identity relating magnetic interaction energy to the coupling with the solenoid current, the author demonstrates that the two Hamiltonians coincide up to constant terms. The analysis is then extended using a path-integral formalism with an effective Lagrangian to examine the gauge dependence of the AB-phase shift for non-closed paths.

## Key numbers

- The interaction energy of Boyer is given as \(\Delta \varepsilon (\text{Boyer}) = + e \, \bm{v} \cdot \bm{A}^{(S)} (\bm{x}^\prime)\), while that of Saldanha is \(\Delta \varepsilon (\text{Saldanha}) = - e \, \bm{v} \cdot \bm{A}^{(S)} (\bm{x}^\prime)\), where \(\bm{A}^{(S)} (\bm{x}^\prime) = \frac{1}{4 \pi} \int d^3 x \, \frac{\bm{j} (\bm{x})}{|\bm{x}^\prime - \bm{x}|}\) is the vector potential from the solenoid current.
- Saldanha's predicted AB-phase shift for a non-closed path is \(\Delta \phi_{AB} = |e| \int_{\text{path a}} \bm{A}^{(S)} (\bm{x}^\prime) \cdot d \bm{x}^\prime - |e| \int_{\text{path b}} \bm{A}^{(S)} (\bm{x}^\prime) \cdot d \bm{x}^\prime = \theta \, \frac{|e| \Phi}{2 \pi}\), where \(\Phi\) is the magnetic flux and \(\theta\) is an angle defined in Saldanha (2021).

## Context

This work extends the long-standing debate on the Aharonov-Bohm effect, specifically addressing recent proposals by Boyer (1971), Vaidman (2012), Kang (2017), Marletto and Vedral (2020), and Saldanha (2021) who claim that the AB-phase shift for a non-closed path is gauge-invariant and measurable. The author's analysis contradicts these claims by showing that the interaction energies from Boyer and Saldanha cancel each other, and that the AB-phase for non-closed paths remains gauge-dependent. This enables a clearer understanding of the topological nature of the AB-effect and reinforces the standard view that only closed-path AB-phase shifts correspond to observables.
