# Effects of initial state fluctuations on the mean transverse momentum

> We show that in ideal hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions, initial state fluctuations result in an increase of the mean transverse momentum of outgoing hadrons, $\langle p_t\rangle$. Specifically, $\langle p_t\rangle$ is larger by a few percent if realistic fluctuations are implemented than with smooth initial conditions, the multiplicity and impact parameter being kept fixed. We show that result can be traced back to the fact that for a given total entropy, the initial energy contained in the fluid is larger if the density profile is bumpy. We discuss the implication of these results for the extraction of the equation of state of QCD from experimental data.

## Key claim

Initial state fluctuations in ideal hydrodynamic simulations of heavy-ion collisions increase the mean transverse momentum of outgoing hadrons by a few percent compared to smooth initial conditions, even when multiplicity and impact parameter are kept fixed. This increase is traced to the fact that for a given total entropy, the initial energy contained in the fluid is larger if the density profile is bumpy, due to the convexity of the equation of state.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic simulations using the MUSIC code with longitudinal boost invariance, starting at an initial time of 0.6 fm/c. They generated 850 events using the TRENTO parameterization of initial entropy density profiles tuned to LHC data, all with the same impact parameter (b = 2.5 fm) and the same total entropy, corresponding to the mean entropy of Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV in the 0-5% centrality window. The fluid was frozen out into hadrons at T_f = 156.5 MeV, resonance decays were implemented, and the mean transverse momentum was evaluated for charged particles after decays.

## Key numbers

- Mean transverse momentum is larger by 7% on average with fluctuating initial conditions compared to smooth initial conditions, for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV in the 0-5% centrality window
- The effective temperature T_eff is related to mean transverse momentum by the approximate relation <p_t> ≈ 3.03 T_eff for all events, including the smooth event
- The average fraction of energy remaining after longitudinal cooling is x ≈ 0.41
- The effective temperature obtained in prior work assuming smooth initial conditions is T_eff = 222 ± 9 MeV, which the authors find is robust with respect to inclusion of initial state fluctuations

## Context

This work extends the study of initial state fluctuations from anisotropic flow (Alver 2006, Takahashi 2009, Alver 2010) to their effect on mean transverse momentum, and builds on the tight correlation between <p_t> and energy per particle established with smooth initial conditions (Van Hove 1982, Gardim 2019). The results show that while the proportionality between T_eff and <p_t> is robust, the effect of fluctuations on the effective volume V_eff is potentially large, which has implications for extracting the QCD equation of state from experimental data. The authors note that their use of ideal hydrodynamics at early times likely overestimates the energy increase, and that a quantitative study requires including transport coefficients and a dynamical treatment of the thermalization phase (Kurkela 2018).
