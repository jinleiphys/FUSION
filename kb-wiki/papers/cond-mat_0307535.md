# No dinosaurs in microcanonical gravitation: No special "microcanonical phase transitions"

> It is shown that the recently introduced singularities of the microcanonical entropy like "microcanonical phase transitions", and exotic pattern of the microcanonical caloric curve T(E) like multi-valuednes or the appearance of "dinosaur's necks" are inconsistent with Boltzmann's fundamental definition of entropy S=ln[W(E)] for a system at equilibrium even for extremely large systems as astro-physical ones.

## Key claim
The paper argues that "microcanonical phase transitions" and exotic features such as multi-valued caloric curves or "dinosaur's necks" in the microcanonical temperature T(E) are inconsistent with Boltzmann's fundamental definition of entropy S = ln[W(E)] for any finite system at equilibrium, including large astrophysical ones.

## Method
The author examines the mathematical properties of the microcanonical entropy S(E) defined by Boltzmann's principle as the logarithm of the geometric area W(E) of the energy surface in 6N-dimensional phase space. By noting that W(E) is a (6N-1)-fold integral over a compact manifold, the author argues that S(E) and its derivative β(E) = T⁻¹(E) = dS/dE are necessarily single-valued, smooth, and multiply differentiable for any finite N, provided β > 0 (i.e., E is finite). The paper then contrasts this with the claims of Bouchet and Barré (2003) and Chavanis (2002) regarding singularities and hysteretic cycles.

## Key numbers
- No specific numerical results are presented; the paper is a theoretical critique without experimental or simulation numerics.

## Context
This work directly contradicts the claims of Bouchet and Barré (2003) regarding "microcanonical phase transitions" defined by singularities of the entropy or temperature, and the "hysteretic cyclings" and "dinosaur's necks" introduced by Chavanis (2002). The author argues that such features arise from considering only a subset of configurations (e.g., stars below hydrogen ignition density) rather than the full microcanonical ensemble, and notes that prior work by Gross (various years) already handled such constrained ensembles by adding an upper limit to local density, which still yields smooth, differentiable functions. The paper enables a clearer distinction between genuine equilibrium thermodynamics and meta-stable configurations that lie outside fundamental statistical mechanics.
