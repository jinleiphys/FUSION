# Excitations of the unstable nuclei ^{48}Ni and ^{49}Ni

> The isoscalar E1 and E2 resonances in the proton-rich nuclei ^{48,49}Ni and the {f_{7/2}3^-} multiplet in ^{49}Ni have been calculated taking into account the single-particle continuum exactly. The analogous calculations for the mirror nuclei ^{48}Ca and ^{49}Sc are presented. The models used are the continuum RPA for ^{48}Ni, ^{48}Ca and the Odd RPA for ^{49}Ni, ^{49}Sc, the latter has been developed recently and describes both single-particle and collective excitations of an odd nucleus on a common basis. In all four nuclei we obtained a distinct splitting of the isoscalar E1 resonance into 1 h-bar omega and 3 h-bar omega peaks at about 11 MeV and 30 MeV, respectively. The main part of the isoscalar E1 EWSR is exhausted by the 3 h-bar omega resonances. The 1 h-bar omega resonances exhaust about 35% of this EWSR in ^{48,49}Ni and about 22% in ^{48}Ca and ^{49}Sc. All seven {f_{7/2}3^-} multiplet members in ^{49}Ni are calculated to be in the (6-8) MeV energy region and have noticeable escape widths.

# Excitations of the unstable nuclei ⁴⁸Ni and ⁴⁹Ni

## Key claim

The isoscalar E1 resonance splits into two distinct components (at about 11 MeV and 30 MeV) in both the proton-rich nickel isotopes and their mirror nuclei, but the calculations cannot predict total widths because only Landau and escape damping mechanisms were included, not the spreading-width formation mechanism.

## Method

The authors used the continuum random phase approximation (continuum RPA) for the doubly-magic nuclei ⁴⁸Ni and ⁴⁸Ca, and the Odd RPA (ORPA) for the odd-mass nuclei ⁴⁹Ni and ⁴⁹Sc, both based on the theory of finite Fermi systems (TFFS) with a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential and Landau-Migdal particle-hole interaction. The single-particle continuum was treated exactly using a Green function method in coordinate representation, and the parameters of the Woods-Saxon potential were fitted to reproduce Hartree-Fock single-particle energies from the Skyrme SIII interaction, while the Landau-Migdal parameter f_ex was adjusted to reproduce the 3₁⁻ level energy in ⁴⁸Ca at 4.51 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The 1ħω isoscalar E1 resonance exhausts about 35% of the isoscalar E1 energy-weighted sum rule (EWSR) in ⁴⁸Ni and ⁴⁹Ni, and about 22% in ⁴⁸Ca and ⁴⁹Sc.
- The 3ħω isoscalar E1 resonance exhausts about 59% (⁴⁸Ni), 60% (⁴⁹Ni), 73% (⁴⁸Ca), and 73% (⁴⁹Sc) of the isoscalar E1 EWSR.
- The isoscalar E1 resonance peaks appear at about 11 MeV (1ħω) and about 30 MeV (3ħω) in all four nuclei.
- The isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (GQR) peak energies are 17.8 MeV (⁴⁸Ni), 17.6 MeV (⁴⁹Ni), 16.6 MeV (⁴⁸Ca), and 16.4 MeV (⁴⁹Sc), with widths at half maximum of 1.5 MeV, 1.8 MeV, 1.1 MeV, and 1.3 MeV respectively.
- The isoscalar GQR exhausts 94% (⁴⁸Ni), 94% (⁴⁹Ni), 91% (⁴⁸Ca), and 92% (⁴⁹Sc) of the isoscalar E2 EWSR in the 10-30 MeV interval.
- The 3₁⁻ level in ⁴⁸Ni has an escape width Γ↑ = 51 keV at an energy of 6.802 MeV.
- The seven members of the {f₇/₂ ⊗ 3⁻} multiplet in ⁴⁹Ni lie in the energy region 6-8 MeV, with escape widths ranging from 28 keV (13/2⁺) to 77 keV (9/2⁺).
- The B(E3) values for the multiplet members in ⁴⁹Ni range from 6.7 W.u. (13/2⁺) to 16.2 W.u. (9/2⁺), where 1 W.u. = 142.6 e²fm⁶.

## Context

This work extends the continuum RPA and ORPA methods previously developed by the same group (Kamerdzhiev, Tselyaev, and collaborators) for stable nuclei to the proton-rich drip-line nuclei ⁴⁸Ni and ⁴⁹Ni, which were discovered recently and have proton separation energy near zero. The calculations provide predictions for future experiments and enable comparison with self-consistent continuum RPA results for ⁴⁸Ca from Hamamoto, Sagawa, and Zhang (1997-1998), and with empirical formulas for giant resonance energies. The results for the {f₇/₂ ⊗ 3⁻} multiplet escape widths in ⁴⁹Ni are particularly valuable because measuring these widths may be experimentally easier than measuring the 3₁⁻ level in ⁴⁸Ni itself.
