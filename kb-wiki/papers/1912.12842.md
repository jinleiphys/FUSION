# A possible evidence of the gluon condensation effect in cosmic positron and gamma-ray spectra

## Key claim
The excess in the cosmic positron spectrum observed by AMS around 300–400 GeV may originate from the gluon condensation (GC) effect in Tycho's supernova remnant, based on a hadronic framework where gluon condensation in proton-proton collisions produces a characteristic broken power-law in both the gamma-ray and positron spectra. The claim is hedged by the statement that the GC mechanism is a "plausible but unproved assumption" and that other models (leptonic and hadronic without GC) can also describe Tycho's data.

## Method
The authors model secondary particle production from proton-proton collisions using a gluon condensation framework, where gluons condense at a critical momentum and produce a maximum number of pions that take up all available kinetic energy. They derive analytic expressions for the gamma-ray spectrum (Equation 2.11) and positron spectrum (Equation 2.13) that exhibit a broken power-law shape at the GC threshold energy. They then fit these GC-model predictions to the Fermi-LAT and VERITAS gamma-ray data for Tycho's supernova remnant and to the AMS positron flux data, using the same spectral indices for both fits.

## Key numbers
- The GC threshold energy is fixed at E_pi^GC = 400 GeV for both the gamma-ray and positron fits.
- The fit to Tycho's gamma-ray spectrum yields chi^2/d.o.f. = 3.24/6 = 0.54, with parameters beta_p^Tycho = 0.89, beta_gamma^Tycho = 2.14, and C_gamma^Tycho = 6 x 10^-10 GeV m^-2 s^-1.
- The fit to the AMS positron flux yields chi^2/d.o.f. = 19.65/44 = 0.45, with the same beta_p and beta_gamma values as the gamma-ray fit.
- The center-of-mass energy corresponding to the GC threshold for nucleus-nucleus collisions is estimated as sqrt(S^GC_A-A) = 5.5 TeV, derived from E_pi^GC = 400 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the gluon condensation model previously developed by Zhu et al. (2008, 2006, 2017) and applied to cosmic gamma-ray and electron-positron fluxes by Feng et al. (2018) and Zhu et al. (2018). It directly addresses the positron excess observed by AMS (Aguilar et al. 2019) and the broken power-law gamma-ray spectrum of Tycho's supernova remnant measured by VERITAS and Fermi-LAT (Archambault et al. 2017). The authors note that other models (leptonic and hadronic without GC) can also describe Tycho's data, as discussed by Bykov et al. (2018) and Cristofari et al. (2018), but claim that only the GC framework connects the two observations with consistent parameters. The work enables future searches for GC signals in heavy-ion collisions at the LHC, where the estimated threshold of 5.5 TeV is near the current Pb-Pb collision energy of 5.02 TeV.
