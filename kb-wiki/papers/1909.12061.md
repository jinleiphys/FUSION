# Exploring continuum structures in reactions with three-body nuclei

> The Transfer to the Continuum method has been applied to describe the $^{11}\text{Li}(p,pn)$ and $^{14}\text{Be}(p,pn)$ reactions in inverse kinematics, using structure overlaps computed within a full three-body model for the projectile. Calculations agree with the available experimental data on the unbound $^{10}$Li and $^{13}$Be nuclei.

# Exploring continuum structures in reactions with three-body nuclei

## Key claim

The Transfer to the Continuum (TC) method, using structure overlaps from a full three-body model for the projectile, reproduces experimental data on the unbound nuclei 10Li and 13Be without fitting procedures; however, for 13Be the agreement depends on including core excitations via an effective quadrupole deformation, and the low-lying peak is best described by a p-wave resonance rather than the s-wave resonance proposed in prior work.

## Method

The authors applied the Transfer to the Continuum (TC) framework to (p,pn) knockout reactions from two-neutron halo projectiles in inverse kinematics, where the residual core+n system is unbound. The transition amplitude was computed using a participant/spectator approximation, with structure information contained in overlap functions between the three-body projectile ground state and the two-body continuum states of the residual nucleus, obtained by solving the three-body problem within the hyperspherical framework using a pseudostate basis. For 14Be(p,pn)13Be, an effective quadrupole deformation was introduced in the core+n potential to allow couplings to the first 2+ excited state in 12Be.

## Key numbers

- 11Li(p,pn)10Li reaction measured at GSI at 280 MeV/u; the three-body model for 11Li produces a 3/2- ground state with 31% of p1/2 components.
- 14Be(p,pn)13Be reaction measured at RIKEN at 265 MeV/u; the adopted model for 13Be is dominated by a low-lying p-wave state at approximately 0.5 MeV, together with a 5/2+ (l=2) resonance around 2 MeV.
- For 14Be, the core-excited contribution in the ground-state wave function amounts to approximately 20% of the norm.
- The TC calculations for 13Be underestimate the strength above 2 MeV relative energy, attributed to missing components coupled with other excited states of the core.

## Context

This work extends the Transfer to the Continuum framework previously developed by Moro (2015) and Gomez-Ramos (2017) for three-body projectiles, applying it to 11Li(p,pn)10Li and 14Be(p,pn)13Be reactions measured at GSI and RIKEN facilities. The results for 10Li confirm the persistence of parity inversion for N=7 beyond the neutron dripline, consistent with prior analyses of (p,d) and (d,p) transfer data by Casal (2017) and Moro (2019). For 13Be, the findings contrast with the s-wave resonance interpretation proposed by Aksyutina (2013) based on a chi-square fitting procedure using Hansen's method (1996); the present approach avoids such fitting ambiguities by using a prior three-body structure model, and reinforces the need for consistent structure and reaction calculations in analyzing knockout experiments.
