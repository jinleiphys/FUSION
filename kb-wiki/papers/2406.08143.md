# Constraining the axial-vector X17 interpretation with ${}^{12}$C data

> Recent findings of an unexpected, narrow resonance in the $e^+e^-$ decay spectra of excited states of $^8$Be, $^4$He and $^{12}$C by the ATOMKI collaboration have received considerable experimental and theoretical attention, whereby a new, 17-MeV vector-like or axial-vector-like boson termed X17 was conjectured as an explanation of the anomaly. Further analysis of all existing constraints disfavors a vector X17 scenario. For a similar analysis of the axial-vector scenario, a calculation of the reduced matrix element of a spin-dipole operator between the excited nuclear state ${}^{12}$C(17.23) and the carbon ground state is required. In the present work, we compute the aforementioned reduced matrix element under the assumption that the state ${}^{12}$C(17.23) is well represented by the $2s_{1/2}1p^{-1}_{3/2}$ particle-hole shell-model excitation of the ground state, as supported by experimental data. Within such a framework, our results indicate that, like the vector scenario, the axial-vector interpretation of X17 shows strong tensions with the other existing constraints on the nucleon coupling of a conjectured X17.

# Constraining the axial-vector X17 interpretation with ${}^{12}$C data

## Key claim
Under the assumption that the ${}^{12}$C(17.23) state is well represented by a $2s_{1/2}1p^{-1}_{3/2}$ particle-hole shell-model excitation, the axial-vector interpretation of the X17 boson shows strong tensions with existing constraints on nucleon couplings, similar to the previously disfavored vector scenario. The authors note that at $\geq 2\sigma$ both theoretical and experimental uncertainty is sufficiently large that no clear conclusion can be drawn, but consistency at $1\sigma$ between different data sets would require nucleon couplings of order $10^{-2}$.

## Method
The authors computed the reduced matrix element of the spin-dipole operator between the ${}^{12}$C(17.23) state and the ${}^{12}$C ground state using a $2s_{1/2}1p^{-1}_{3/2}$ particle-hole shell-model excitation in a coupled harmonic-oscillator basis with oscillator parameter $a = 1.63$ fm. They derived the decay rates for both the axial-vector X17 decay and the electromagnetic E1 decay, then compared the ratio $\Gamma_X/\Gamma_\gamma$ to the ATOMKI experimental value of $3.6(3) \times 10^{-6}$, applying an empirical reduction factor of approximately 5.4 based on the overestimate of the electromagnetic decay width.

## Key numbers
- The electromagnetic decay width calculated in the 1p1h approximation is $\Gamma_\gamma \approx 251$ eV, compared to the experimental value $\Gamma_\gamma^\text{exp} = 44$ eV, requiring a reduction factor of approximately 5.4.
- The ATOMKI measured decay ratio is $(\Gamma_X/\Gamma_\gamma)_\text{ATOMKI} = 3.6(3) \times 10^{-6}$.
- The energy of the excited state above the ground state is $\Delta E = 17.23$ MeV.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter is $a = 1.63$ fm.
- The derived constraints on the nucleon coupling difference $|g^A_p - g^A_n|$ are: scenario 1 (with reduction factor) $(0.87-2.5) \times 10^{-2}$, scenario 2 (without reduction factor) $(0.37-1.1) \times 10^{-2}$, scenario 3 (using shell-model ratio) $(0.90-2.6) \times 10^{-2}$.

## Context
This work extends the comprehensive analyses of Barducci (2022) and Hostert (2023), which disfavored the vector X17 interpretation, by providing the missing nuclear matrix element for the ${}^{12}$C decay needed to test the axial-vector scenario. Prior shell-model calculations for the ${}^8$Be decay by Kozaczuk (2016) and the ${}^4$He decay by Barducci (2022) had been performed, but the ${}^{12}$C matrix element was previously unavailable. The calculation builds on the particle-hole description of ${}^{12}$C(17.23) established by Vinh-Mau and Brown (1962), Goswami (1963), and Gillet (1964), and later refined by Lewis, Walecka, and Donnelly (1970-1971). The results enable a combined analysis of all three ATOMKI anomalies (${}^8$Be, ${}^4$He, ${}^{12}$C) against preexisting constraints from $\pi^0 \to e^+ e^-$ (KTeV, 2006) and $\pi^+ \to e^+ \nu_e e^+ e^-$ (SINDRUM-I, 1989), and motivate more comprehensive shell-model calculations and ongoing verification experiments such as CCPAC and PADME.
