# Resonances of $A=4$ $T=1$ isospin triplet states within the \textit{ab initio} no-core Gamow shell model

> The $A=4$ nuclei, i.e., $^4$H, $^4$He and $^4$Li, establish an interesting isospin $T=1$ isobaric system. $^4$H and $^4$Li are unbound broad resonances, whereas $^4$He is deeply bound in its ground state but unbound in all its excited states. The present situation is that experiments so far have not given consistent data on the resonances. Few-body calculations have well studied the scatterings of the $4N$ systems. In the present work, we provide many-body calculations of the broad resonance structures, in an \textit{ab initio} framework with modern realistic interactions. It occurs that, indeed, $^4$H, $^4$Li and excited $^4$He are broad resonances, which is in accordance with experimental observations. The calculations also show that the first $1^-$ excited state almost degenerates with the $2^-$ ground state in the pair of mirror isobars of $^4$H and $^4$Li, which may suggest that the experimental data on energy and width are the mixture of the ground state and the first excited state. The $T = 1$ isospin triplet formed with an excited state of $^4$He and ground states of $^4$H and $^4$Li is studied, focusing on the effect of isospin symmetry breaking.

## Key claim

The paper provides many-body *ab initio* calculations of broad resonance structures in the A=4 T=1 isospin triplet (4H, 4He, 4Li) using the no-core Gamow shell model, showing that the first 1- excited state nearly degenerates with the 2- ground state in 4H and 4Li, which may suggest that experimental data on energy and width are actually a mixture of the ground state and first excited state.

## Method

The no-core Gamow shell model (NCGSM) was used with a Berggren basis comprising bound, resonance, and scattering single-particle states, producing a complex-symmetric Hamiltonian matrix whose eigenvalues give resonance energies (real part) and widths (imaginary part). The calculations employed several realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions (Argonne v18, CD-Bonn, chiral N3LO, and Daejeon16) softened via the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method, with natural orbitals generated from truncated-space calculations to accelerate convergence. For 4H, a harmonic oscillator basis was used for protons and Berggren basis for neutrons; the opposite was done for 4Li; for excited 4He, Berggren basis was used for both protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- 4H ground state energy: about 1.7 MeV above the 3H+n threshold, with width close to 0.9 MeV (using N3LO-SRG2.0, CD-Bonn-SRG2.0, and AV18-SRG2.0 interactions)
- 4H ground state energy with N3LO-SRG1.5 and Daejeon16: more bound by about 0.4 MeV, widths smaller by about 0.2 MeV compared to the SRG2.0 calculations
- Splitting between 4H 2- ground state and 1- first excited state: about 50 keV in NCGSM calculations
- 4Li ground state energy: about 2.7 MeV above the 3He+p threshold, with resonance width about 2 MeV (using AV18-SRG2.0, CD-Bonn-SRG2.0, and N3LO-SRG2.0)
- 4Li ground state energy with N3LO-SRG1.5 and Daejeon16: about 2.2 MeV, width about 1.8 MeV
- 4He 2- excited state: calculated at 22.03 MeV above the 4He ground state, with resonance width about 1.5 MeV
- Isospin expectation value for the 2- excited state of 4He: about 0.71, indicating large isospin mixing

## Context

This work extends previous few-body calculations of 4N scatterings using Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY), Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS), and hyperspherical harmonics (HH) methods, which studied scattering cross sections but did not directly calculate resonance energies and widths. The FY calculation by Lazauskas (2019) assessed the 4H resonance width using an indirect extrapolation method, while the present NCGSM approach provides direct diagonalization results without extrapolation. The calculations enable better interpretation of inconsistent experimental data on A=4 resonances and provide a framework for studying isospin symmetry breaking in light nuclear systems, with the 4He 2- excited state showing significant isospin mixing consistent with experimental observations from PhysRevLett.57.543 and PhysRevLett.72.3476.
