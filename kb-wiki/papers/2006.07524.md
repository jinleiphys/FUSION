# Least action and the maximum-coupling approximations in the theory of spontaneous fission

> We investigate the dynamics of spontaneous fission in a configuration-interaction (CI) approach. In that formalism the decay rate is governed by an effective interaction coupling the ground-state configuration and a fission doorway configuration, with the interaction strength determined by inverting a high-dimensioned CI Hamiltonian matrix that may have a block-tridiagonal structure. It is shown that the decay rate decreases exponentially with the number of blocks at a rate determined by the largest eigenvalue of a matrix in the block space for Hamiltonians with identical off-diagonal blocks. The theory is greatly simplified by approximations similar in spirit to the adiabatic and the least-action approximations in continuum representations. Here each block is replaced by a single matrix element. While the adiabatic reduction underestimates the coupling, a reduction based on a maximum-coupling approximation works well in a schematic CI model.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the adiabatic approximation, which is standard in spontaneous fission theory, significantly underestimates the effective coupling strength between the ground state and fission doorway state, but a maximum-coupling approximation (analogous to the least-action approach in WKB theory) reproduces the exact coupling to within 1% in a schematic configuration-interaction model.

## Method

The authors constructed a schematic configuration-interaction (CI) model with 6 doubly-degenerate orbitals and 6 paired particles (the (6,6) model), using a pairing-plus-quadrupole Hamiltonian. They reduced the full Hamiltonian matrix to an effective 2x2 matrix and computed the effective coupling strength \(v_{\rm eff}\) exactly, then compared it to values obtained from the adiabatic approximation (projecting each block onto its local ground state) and from a maximum-coupling approximation (increasing the pairing strength \(G\) to \(G_p\) to maximize \(v_{\rm eff}\)). They also analyzed the scaling of \(v_{\rm eff}\) with the number of interior blocks \(N_b\) using block Toeplitz matrix theory.

## Key numbers

* Exact effective coupling \(v_{\rm eff} = 0.0650\) (in units of \(\epsilon_0\)) for the (6,6) model with two interior blocks.
* Adiabatic approximation gives \(v_{\rm eff} = 0.0221\), a suppression factor of 0.34 relative to exact.
* Maximum-coupling approximation with \(G_p/G = 5.4\) gives \(v_{\rm eff} = 0.0653\), a ratio of 1.00 to exact.
* The asymptotic \(N_b\)-scaling factor \(C = 0.2386\) for the ratio \(v_{\rm eff}(N_b)/v_{\rm eff}(N_b-1)\) as \(N_b \to \infty\).
* The least-action approach gives an optimum \(G_p/G = 4.1\), close to the maximum-coupling value of 5.4.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous schematic CI model studies of induced fission (Bertsch 2020) and spontaneous fission (Hagino and Bertsch 2020), which showed that the adiabatic approximation underestimates decay widths. The maximum-coupling approximation is analogous to the least-action approach in WKB theory, which was pioneered by Moretto and Babinet (1974) and recently applied in realistic GCM calculations by Robledo et al. (2014, 2018). The paper enables future realistic CI calculations for spontaneous fission by providing a truncation scheme that accounts for non-adiabatic effects without requiring full diagonalization of large matrices.
