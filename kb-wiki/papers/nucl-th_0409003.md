# Studying Intermediate pT Hadron Production with Fluctuations

> Mechanisms for particle production at intermediate pT in nuclear collisions at RHIC are discussed, emphasizing the differences in associated jet-like correlations between color-neutral and colored production. An alternative production mechanism involving both recombination and fragmentation is suggested, which might simultaneously lead to an enhancement of baryons and to jet-like correlations. To gain more insight into the relative importance of different mechanisms a study of constrained distributions of associated multiplicity is proposed. In a simple model it is shown that these multiplicity distributions may change significantly, if the nature of the production mechanism fluctuates from event to event.

## Key claim

The paper argues that intermediate transverse momentum hadron production in nuclear collisions at RHIC may involve a mixture of color-neutral (recombination/coalescence) and colored (fragmentation) mechanisms, and that studying fluctuations in the multiplicity associated with a high-\(p_T\) trigger particle can discriminate between these mechanisms, even though the abstract does not specify that the proposed method works only if background correlations (e.g., from elliptic flow) are small or can be controlled.

## Method

The author constructs a simple mathematical model of constrained multiplicity distributions: for a fixed total multiplicity \(J\) in a \(180^\circ\) azimuthal window, the distribution of multiplicity \(I\) in a narrower \(60^\circ\) cone is computed. The model folds a Gaussian parameterization of the jet-associated multiplicity from p+p collisions (mean \(\mu^{pp}=1.18\), width \(\sigma^{pp}=0.55\)) with a random binomial background and a Gaussian background multiplicity distribution, allowing the fraction \(X\) of events containing a jet correlation to vary.

## Key numbers

* In p+p collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200\) GeV, the associated multiplicity in a narrow cone (\(|\Delta\phi|<30^\circ\)) around a trigger particle with \(p_T>4\) GeV/\(c\) (associated particles with \(p_T>2\) GeV/\(c\)) has mean \(\mu^{pp}=1.18\) and width \(\sigma^{pp}=0.55\), parameterized as a Gaussian.
* For a diquark recombination plus fragmentation mechanism, the baryon-to-meson ratio at \(p_T=3\) GeV/\(c\) and effective temperature \(T=350\) MeV reaches order 1 if the leading baryon carries an average momentum fraction \(\langle z\rangle>0.8\).
* In the model, for a fixed total multiplicity \(J=4\) and background mean \(\mu_{AA}=4.5\), the mean of the constrained distribution \(\langle I\rangle\) becomes independent of the jet fraction \(X\) for \(X>0.4\), while the probability \(P(I=0)\) shows strong relative variation as \(X\) decreases from 1.

## Context

This work extends discussions of intermediate-\(p_T\) hadron production at RHIC, where recombination/coalescence models (Fries et al., 2003; Greco et al., 2003) explain baryon enhancement but cannot account for jet-like correlations observed by STAR (2003) and PHENIX (2002). The paper proposes a hybrid diquark recombination plus fragmentation mechanism as an alternative, and suggests constrained multiplicity distributions as a new observable to disentangle color-neutral and colored production, noting that further study of background correlations (e.g., from elliptic flow) is needed.
