# Thermalization of charm quarks in infinite and finite QGP matter

> We study the thermalization process of charm quarks in hot and dense matter. The diffusion of heavy quarks is calculated via a Langevin equation, both for a static medium as well as a QGP medium generated by a (3+1)D hydrodynamic model. We define two criteria for the thermalization of the heavy quarks, and observe thermalization times that are longer than the lifetime of the QGP phase for reasonable values of the diffusion constant.

## Key claim

The paper finds that for diffusion coefficient values that best describe experimental data on charm quark elliptic flow and nuclear modification factor, charm quarks do not fully thermalize within the lifetime of the quark-gluon plasma phase, even though they exhibit strong collective behavior; full thermalization requires a diffusion coefficient roughly one-quarter of the currently favored value.

## Method

The authors simulate charm quark diffusion using a Langevin equation, first in an infinite static medium at fixed temperature and then in an expanding and cooling QGP medium generated by a (3+1)D relativistic ideal hydrodynamic model. They define two thermalization criteria: a "quasi-equilibrium" stage where the energy distribution becomes thermal but momentum distributions remain non-isotropic, and a "full equilibrium" stage where both energy and momentum distributions yield the same temperature as the medium. The temperature of the charm quark ensemble is extracted by fitting the energy distribution with an exponential and the momentum distributions with a shifted thermal form.

## Key numbers

- For a static medium at 300 MeV with charm quarks initialized at 5 GeV/c and diffusion coefficient D = 6/(2πT), full equilibrium is reached at approximately 25 fm/c.
- For a static medium at 160 MeV with charm quarks initialized at 5 GeV/c and D = 6/(2πT), quasi-equilibrium is reached at 35 fm/c and full equilibrium at 70 fm/c.
- For a static medium at 160 MeV with charm quarks initialized at 1 GeV/c and D = 6/(2πT), quasi-equilibrium is reached at 10 fm/c and full equilibrium at 20 fm/c.
- For a static medium at 300 MeV with D = 1.5/(2πT), quasi-equilibrium is reached at 10 fm/c and full equilibrium at 25 fm/c; for D = 12/(2πT), these times increase to 70 fm/c and 150 fm/c respectively.
- In the expanding QGP medium with D = 6/(2πT) and charm quarks initialized at 5 GeV/c in the longitudinal direction, quasi-equilibrium is reached at 8 fm/c but full equilibrium is never achieved before freeze-out.
- In the expanding QGP medium with D = 1.5/(2πT) and charm quarks initialized at 5 GeV/c randomly in the transverse plane, charm quarks appear very close to full equilibrium at freeze-out.

## Context

This work extends earlier estimates by Moore (2004) that charm quark thermalization time is of order 7 fm/c, and by van Hees (2004, 2005) who found that resonant heavy-light quark interactions could reduce thermalization times from over 30 fm/c to a few fm/c. The paper uses the same Langevin framework as Svetitsky (1987) and Moore (2004), and the hydrodynamic model of Nonaka (2006). The key finding contradicts the assumption that strong elliptic flow and small nuclear modification factor necessarily imply thermalization; instead, charm quarks can exhibit strong collective behavior while remaining off-equilibrium. The results enable future studies of memory effects, pre-equilibrium phase interactions, and the role of the Weibel instability in heavy quark thermalization.
