# Two-Step Electroweak Baryogenesis

> We analyze electroweak baryogenesis during a two-step electroweak symmetry breaking transition, wherein the baryon asymmetry is generated during the first step and preserved during the second. Focusing on the dynamics of CP-violation required for asymmetry generation, we discuss general considerations for successful two-step baryogenesis. Using a concrete model realization, we illustrate in detail the viability of this scenario and the implications for present and future electric dipole moment (EDM) searches. We find that CP-violation associated with a partially excluded sector may yield the observed baryon asymmetry while evading present and future EDM constraints.

## Key claim

The authors argue that electroweak baryogenesis can succeed during the first step of a two-step electroweak symmetry breaking transition, with the generated baryon asymmetry preserved through the second step, and that the CP-violating interactions required for this scenario may involve a partially secluded sector whose impacts on low-energy CP-violating observables are highly suppressed, potentially evading present and future electric dipole moment constraints.

## Method

The authors construct a concrete model extending the Standard Model with a real triplet (1,3,0), a real singlet, and a second Higgs doublet, imposing a Z2 symmetry to avoid tree-level flavor changing neutral currents. They analyze the finite-temperature effective potential and transport equations describing the dynamics of particle-antiparticle asymmetry generation during the first step of the two-step phase transition, where the triplet and singlet acquire vacuum expectation values while the Higgs doublets remain in the symmetric phase. The CP-violating asymmetries are generated entirely in the scalar sector through spacetime-dependent complex phases in the doublet mass-squared matrix arising from interactions with the expanding bubble walls, and are transferred to Standard Model left-handed fermions via Yukawa interactions.

## Key numbers

* The observed baryon-to-photon ratio is Y_B = (8.59 plus or minus 0.11) times 10 to the minus 11, obtained from Planck, WMAP, and large scale structure measurements.
* The electroweak symmetry breaking transition occurred roughly ten picoseconds after the Big Bang.
* The Higgs boson mass is too large to accommodate a first order electroweak phase transition in the Standard Model; the transition is of a cross-over type.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of two-step electroweak symmetry breaking induced by a real triplet (Patel, 2012) and general conditions for successful two-step phase transitions (Blinov, 2015). It differs qualitatively from earlier two-step baryogenesis scenarios such as Land (1992) using a Two Higgs Doublet Model, where baryon number violating processes are suppressed during the first step, and Jiang (2015) using a complex scalar singlet, where CP-violating asymmetries are induced during the second step. The scenario enables a new window for weak-scale baryogenesis that may be relatively immune to near-term experimental constraints on CP violation from electric dipole moment searches.
