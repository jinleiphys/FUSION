# A computationally tractable version of the collective model

> A computationally tractable version of the Bohr-Mottelson collective model is presented which makes it possible to diagonalize realistic collective models and obtain convergent results in relatively small appropriately chosen subspaces of the collective model Hilbert space. Special features of the proposed model is that it makes use of the beta wave functions given analytically by the softened-beta version of the Wilets-Jean model, proposed by Elliott et al., and a simple algorithm for computing SO(5) > SO(3) spherical harmonics. The latter has much in common with the methods of Chacon, Moshinsky, and Sharp but is conceptually and computationally simpler. Results are presented for collective models ranging from the sherical vibrator to the Wilets-Jean and axially symmetric rotor-vibrator models.

## Key claim

The paper presents a computationally tractable version of the Bohr-Mottelson collective model that achieves convergent diagonalization in relatively small, appropriately chosen subspaces of the collective model Hilbert space, provided one uses beta wave functions from the softened-beta Wilets-Jean model and a new simpler algorithm for SO(5) spherical harmonics.

## Method

The author constructs a basis for the collective model Hilbert space using SU(1,1) × SO(5) dynamical symmetry, where beta wave functions are given analytically by a five-dimensional Davidson model (harmonic oscillator plus a 1/β² term) following Elliott et al. (1986). SO(5) spherical harmonics are built from polynomials of the elementary v=1, L=2 quadrupole moment functions, then Gramm-Schmidt orthogonalized to obtain an orthonormal basis with good seniority. The method is applied to diagonalize Hamiltonians ranging from the spherical vibrator through the Wilets-Jean gamma-soft model to the axially symmetric rotor-vibrator model.

## Context

This work extends the Bohr-Mottelson collective model by providing a practical computational framework that bridges the analytically solvable limits (harmonic vibrator, Wilets-Jean gamma-soft model, axially symmetric rigid rotor) with intermediate regions. It builds on the earlier U(5) ⊃ SO(5) ⊃ SO(3) basis methods of Chacon, Moshinsky, and Sharp (1976) and the Frankfurt collective model approach of Hess et al., but offers a simpler construction that converges much faster for deformed nuclei. The method enables future microscopic interpretations by formulating the collective model in algebraic terms suitable for connection to shell model representations, following the symplectic model approach of Rosensteel and Rowe (1977).
