# Enhanced proton-boron nuclear fusion cross sections in intense high-frequency laser

> We investigate the proton-boron nuclear fusion cross sections under the influence of the intense linearly polarized monochromatic laser fields with high frequency. First, we rewrite the time-dependent Schrödinger equation using Kramers-Henneberger (KH) transformation which allows for shifting all time dependence of the problem into the potential function. Then, for the intense laser fields that satisfy the high frequency limit, the time-averaged scheme in the KH framework should be valid. We can use WKB approximation to evaluate Coulomb barrier penetrability and then calculate proton-boron nuclear fusion cross sections by a phenomenological Gamow form. We show that the corresponding Coulomb barrier penetrability increases significantly due to the depression of the time-averaged potential barrier. As a result, we find that proton-boron nuclear fusion cross sections can be enhanced effectively depending on a dimensionless quantity $n_{\mathrm{d}}$, which equals the ratio of the quiver oscillation amplitude to the geometrical touching radius of the proton and boron nucleus. For $n_{\mathrm{d}}=9$, we predict that the resonance peak of the fusion cross-section is enhanced by about $26$ times at the incident energy of $\varepsilon=148$ keV. And for another incident energy of $\varepsilon=586$ keV, the resonance peak of fusion cross-section is not only enhanced but also shifted to lower energy of $\varepsilon=392$ keV due to the mechanism of over-barrier fusion.

# Enhanced proton-boron nuclear fusion cross sections in intense high-frequency laser fields

## Key claim

The authors predict that proton-boron fusion cross sections can be enhanced by up to 26 times in intense high-frequency laser fields, but only when the dimensionless parameter n_d (ratio of quiver oscillation amplitude to nuclear touching radius) reaches 9, requiring laser intensities on the order of 10^27 W/cm^2, far beyond current XFEL capabilities.

## Method

The authors apply the Kramers-Henneberger (KH) transformation to the time-dependent Schrodinger equation for the p-11B system, shifting all time dependence into the potential. For high-frequency laser fields (photon energy above 10 keV), they use a time-averaged static potential in the KH framework, then compute Coulomb barrier penetrability via the WKB approximation. The fusion cross sections are obtained using a phenomenological Gamow form that multiplies the penetrability by the geometrical cross section (1/epsilon) and the astrophysical S factor from field-free fitting functions.

## Key numbers

- The geometrical touching radius of the proton and boron nucleus is r_n = 4.64 fm, calculated as 1.44 x (1 + 11^(1/3)) fm
- The height of the Coulomb barrier is V_0 = 1.55 MeV
- For n_d = 9 at incident energy epsilon = 148 keV, the resonance peak of the fusion cross section is enhanced to 25.7 barns, approximately 26 times the field-free value
- For n_d = 9 at incident energy epsilon = 586 keV, the fusion cross section is enhanced to 5.93 barns (about 5 times field-free), and a new resonance peak appears at epsilon = 392 keV with cross section 8.24 barns (about 19 times field-free)
- For laser frequency 10 keV, the required intensities are 5.00 x 10^26 W/cm^2 (n_d = 3), 2.00 x 10^27 W/cm^2 (n_d = 6), and 4.50 x 10^27 W/cm^2 (n_d = 9)
- The Coulomb interaction time duration ranges from 31.61 femtoseconds to 1 attosecond for incident energies from 1 keV to 1 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on deuteron-triton fusion enhancement in intense high-frequency laser fields (Lv 2019) to the aneutronic proton-boron reaction, which is environmentally cleaner than DT fusion but has much smaller cross sections. The approach uses the same KH transformation and time-averaged potential method as Queisser and Schutzhold (2019) for DT fusion in XFEL fields. The results suggest that laser-assisted p-11B fusion could achieve larger cross sections at lower relative energies, potentially reducing the Lawson criterion for fusion ignition, though the required laser intensities exceed current XFEL capabilities and the model assumes fully coherent, monochromatic fields.
