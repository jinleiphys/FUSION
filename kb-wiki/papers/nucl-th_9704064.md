# Structure analysis of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude at low energies

> We analyze virtual Compton scattering off the nucleon at low energies in a covariant, model-independent formalism. We define a set of invariant functions which, once the irregular nucleon pole terms have been subtracted in a gauge-invariant fashion, is free of poles and kinematical zeros. The covariant treatment naturally allows one to implement the constraints due to Lorentz and gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, and the discrete symmetries. In particular, when applied to the $ep\to e'p'γ$ reaction, charge-conjugation symmetry in combination with nucleon crossing generates four relations among the ten originally proposed generalized polarizabilities of the nucleon.

# Structure analysis of the virtual Compton scattering amplitude at low energies

## Key claim
The regular part of the virtual Compton scattering (VCS) amplitude can be written in terms of only six independent generalized polarizabilities instead of ten, provided one imposes charge-conjugation symmetry in combination with nucleon crossing under the same kinematical approximations as Guichon et al. (1995). If this symmetry is not assumed, none of the four derived relations between the polarizabilities holds, and the full set of ten is required.

## Method
The authors constructed a covariant, model-independent formalism for the VCS amplitude by defining invariant functions free of poles and kinematical zeros after subtracting the irregular nucleon pole terms in a gauge-invariant fashion. They expanded the residual (non-Born) amplitude to lowest order in the outgoing real photon energy ω' and compared two different low-energy expansions in the center-of-mass frame: one in terms of generalized polarizabilities following Guichon et al. (1995), and one in terms of the covariant invariant functions f_i. By imposing constraints from Lorentz invariance, gauge invariance, crossing symmetry, parity, time reversal, and charge conjugation combined with nucleon crossing, they derived four relations among the ten generalized polarizabilities.

## Key numbers
- The number of independent generalized polarizabilities is reduced from 10 to 6 when charge conjugation and nucleon crossing are applied.
- Three vector polarizabilities vanish at \(\bar{q} = 0\): \(P^{(01,01)1}(0) = P^{(11,11)1}(0) = P^{(11,00)1}(0) = 0\).
- A relation among four remaining vector polarizabilities at \(\bar{q} = 0\) is given by \(P^{(01,12)1}(0) + \sqrt{3} P^{(11,02)1}(0) - \sqrt{3} \hat{P}^{(01,1)1}(0) - 2 \sqrt{5} M \hat{P}^{(11,2)1}(0) = 0\).
- The structure functions \(P_{LT}\) and \(P_{LT}'\) satisfy \(P_{LT}(\bar{q}) + \frac{\bar{q}}{\omega_{0}} P_{LT}'(\bar{q}) = 0\) for unpolarized experiments.

## Context
This work extends the formalism of Tarrach (1975) for general Compton scattering with two virtual photons to the specific case of VCS with a real photon in the final state, and simplifies the analysis of Guichon et al. (1995) who originally proposed ten generalized polarizabilities. The authors previously showed (Drechsel et al., 1996) that only two of the three spin-independent generalized polarizabilities are independent; the present paper extends this result to the spin-dependent sector. The results provide constraints for testing model predictions (e.g., constituent quark models, effective Lagrangians, heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory) and for analyzing upcoming VCS experiments at MAMI, Jefferson Lab, and MIT-Bates.
