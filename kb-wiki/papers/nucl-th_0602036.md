# Pygmy dipole resonance as a constraint on the neutron skin of heavy nuclei

> The isotopic dependence of the isovector Pygmy dipole response in tin is studied within the framework of the relativistic random phase approximation. Regarded as an oscillation of the neutron skin against the isospin-symmetric core, the pygmy dipole resonance may place important constraints on the neutron skin of heavy nuclei and, as a result, on the equation of state of neutron-rich matter. The present study centers around two questions. First, is there a strong correlation between the development of a neutron skin and the emergence of low-energy isovector dipole strength? Second, could one use the recently measured Pygmy dipole resonance in 130Sn and 132Sn to discriminate among theoretical models? For the first question we found that while a strong correlation between the neutron skin and the Pygmy dipole resonance exists, a mild anti-correlation develops beyond 120Sn. The answer to the second question suggests that models with overly large neutron skins--and thus stiff symmetry energies--are in conflict with experiment.

## Key claim

The pygmy dipole resonance (PDR) in tin isotopes shows a strong correlation with neutron skin thickness for nuclei up to 120Sn, but a mild anti-correlation develops beyond that point; models with overly large neutron skins (and thus stiff symmetry energies) are in conflict with experimental data from 130Sn and 132Sn, though the paper notes that the experimental error bars are large.

## Method

The study used a relativistic mean-field (MF) plus random-phase-approximation (RPA) formalism to calculate the isovector dipole response of even-neutron tin isotopes from 100Sn to 132Sn. Two accurately-calibrated parameter sets (NL3 and FSUGold) were employed, along with two additional sets (FSUGold' and NL3') designed to further soften or stiffen the symmetry energy while preserving ground-state observables. The calculations neglected pairing correlations and coupling to more complex configurations, but the author argues these effects do not affect the main conclusions.

## Key numbers

- For 130Sn, the PDR centroid energy from experiment is 10.1(7) MeV, while FSUGold predicts 7.91 MeV and NL3 predicts 7.91 MeV
- For 132Sn, the experimental PDR centroid energy is 9.8(7) MeV, compared to FSUGold's 7.90 MeV and NL3's 7.90 MeV
- The neutron skin thickness (Rn-Rp) for 132Sn ranges from 0.189 fm (FSUGold') to 0.432 fm (NL3'), with FSUGold at 0.271 fm and NL3 at 0.346 fm
- The PDR-to-GDR energy-weighted sum rule ratio (m1) for 132Sn is 3.04% (FSUGold'), 4.89% (FSUGold), 6.44% (NL3), and 8.69% (NL3'), while experiment gives 0.03(2)%
- For 130Sn, the experimental GDR centroid energy is 15.9(5) MeV, with FSUGold predicting 15.78 MeV and NL3 predicting 15.13 MeV
- The charge radius of 208Pb is known to better than 0.001 fm, while the neutron radius uncertainty is about 0.2 fm

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of the pygmy dipole resonance dating back to Suzuki (1990) and Van Isacker (1992), and builds on sophisticated MF+RPA approaches developed by Hamamoto (1996, 1998) and Vretenar (2000, 2001). It provides the first direct comparison of relativistic MF+RPA calculations against the experimental data from Adrich (2005) on 130Sn and 132Sn. The results enable discrimination between accurately-calibrated models like NL3 (Lalazissis 1996) and FSUGold (Todd-Rutel 2005) that have different density dependences for the symmetry energy, and connect to the Parity Radius Experiment (PREX) at Jefferson Laboratory aiming to measure the neutron radius of 208Pb to within 0.05 fm.
