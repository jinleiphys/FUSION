# Microscopic Approach in Inelastic Heavy-Ions Scattering with Excitation of Nuclear Collective States

> In the density distribution of a deformed target-nucleus, the spherical $λ= 0$ and the deformed $λ= 2$ parts were considered. On this basis, the corresponding potential parts $U_0$ and $U^{(2)}_{int}$ of a double -folding microscopic nucleus-nucleus optical potential are obtained. Then, for these potentials and by using the coupled-channel technique (ECIS), the elastic and inelastic amplitudes are calculated for $^{17}O$ heavy ion scattering on $2^{+}$ collective excited state of various target nuclei. Besides, the same cross-sections are calculated in the frame of an adiabatic approach of the eikonal approximation, where the inelastic amplitude is the linear function of $U^{(2)}_{int}$. Both the obtained results are compared with the experimental data, and also discuss their efficiency in predicting the deformation parameters of nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a microscopic double-folding transition potential, derived from the deformed target-nucleus density, can be used to calculate inelastic heavy-ion scattering cross sections for excitation of collective 2+ states, but the extracted nuclear deformation parameters β^(n) differ by a factor of 1.5–2 between the coupled-channel (ECIS) and high-energy approximation (HEA) methods, indicating that the deformation parameter is not uniquely determined by the potential model.

## Method

The authors constructed a microscopic transition potential V_int from the deformed part of the target-nucleus density, using the double-folding model with the CDM3Y6 effective nucleon-nucleon interaction and including exchange terms. They then calculated inelastic scattering cross sections for ¹⁷O projectiles at 1435 MeV on ⁶⁰Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹²⁰Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb targets using two approaches: the coupled-channel method (ECIS code) which includes all orders of the transition potential, and the adiabatic eikonal approximation (HEA) which keeps only linear terms in the transition potential. The deformation parameters β^(n) were fitted to experimental inelastic scattering data, while the strength parameters N_r and N_im for the central optical potential were first fitted to elastic scattering data.

## Key numbers

- Projectile: ¹⁷O at laboratory energy E_lab = 1435 MeV on targets ⁶⁰Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, ¹²⁰Sn, and ²⁰⁸Pb
- Renormalizing coefficients for the real part N_r: 0.6 (⁶⁰Ni, ⁹⁰Zr), 0.5 (¹²⁰Sn, ²⁰⁸Pb)
- Renormalizing coefficients for the imaginary part N_im: 0.6 (⁶⁰Ni), 0.5 (⁹⁰Zr, ¹²⁰Sn), 0.8 (²⁰⁸Pb)
- Coulomb deformation parameters β^(c): 0.2067 (⁶⁰Ni), 0.091 (⁹⁰Zr), 0.1075 (¹²⁰Sn), 0.0544 (²⁰⁸Pb)
- Nuclear deformation parameters from ECIS β^(n): 0.2541 (⁶⁰Ni), 0.071 (⁹⁰Zr), 0.1063 (¹²⁰Sn), 0.078 (²⁰⁸Pb real part)
- For ²⁰⁸Pb in ECIS, the imaginary part required a separate deformation parameter β^(n)_im = 0.0222
- Nuclear deformation parameters from HEA β^(n): 0.4 (⁶⁰Ni), 0.14 (⁹⁰Zr), 0.25 (¹²⁰Sn), 0.12 (²⁰⁸Pb)
- Coulomb radius parameter used: R_c = 1.2 (A_1^(1/3) + A_2^(1/3)) fm

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of elastic scattering (Lukyanov et al., 2006) and inelastic scattering (Lukyanov et al., 2005) using the high-energy approximation, by now incorporating a fully microscopic double-folding transition potential rather than derivative-based semi-microscopic potentials. The results are compared with experimental data from Ref. [8] and with coupled-channel calculations using the ECIS code (Ref. [7]). The paper highlights the need for simultaneous fitting of strength and deformation parameters in both elastic and inelastic channels to obtain more reliable deformation information, and it opens the question of how to reconcile the factor-of-two discrepancy in β^(n) between the two theoretical methods.
