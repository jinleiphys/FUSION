# The algebraic cluster model. Three-body clusters

> A new method is introduced to study three-body clusters. Triangular configurations with ${\cal D}_{3h}$ point-group symmetry are analyzed. The spectrum, transition form factors and $B(Eλ)$ values of $^{12}$C are investigated. It is concluded that the low-lying spectrum of $^{12}$C can be described by three alpha-particles at the vertices of an equilateral triangle, but not as a rigid structure. Large rotation-vibration interactions, Coriolis forces and vibration-vibration interactions are needed. Other interpretations, such as the harmonic oscillator and a soft deformed oscillator with SO(6) hyperspherical symmetry, appear to be excluded by electron scattering data.

## Key claim

The low-lying spectrum of ¹²C can be described by three alpha-particles at the vertices of an equilateral triangle, but not as a rigid structure; large rotation-vibration interactions, Coriolis forces, and vibration-vibration interactions are required, and other interpretations such as the harmonic oscillator and a soft deformed oscillator with SO(6) hyperspherical symmetry appear to be excluded by electron scattering data.

## Method

The authors introduce an algebraic cluster model (ACM) based on bosonic quantization of the three-body problem using a U(7) algebra, where Jacobi coordinates and momenta are quantized with boson operators plus an additional scalar boson. They analyze triangular configurations with D₃h point-group symmetry by constructing a Hamiltonian with up to two-body terms invariant under the S₃ permutation group, and solve the eigenvalue problem by matrix diagonalization. The method is applied to compute the spectrum, transition form factors, and B(Eλ) values of ¹²C, comparing classical and quantum treatments including harmonic oscillator, deformed oscillator, and oblate symmetric top configurations.

## Key numbers

- For ¹²C, extracted parameters from form factors: α = 0.52 fm⁻² and β = 1.74 fm for the extended Gaussian charge distribution of alpha particles at triangle vertices.
- The charge radius for the oblate top, harmonic oscillator, and deformed oscillator is given by ⟨r²⟩¹/² = √(3/(2α) + β²).
- For point-like charges in the oblate top, B(E2) = (Ze)² × (5/(4π)) × (1/4) × β⁴, B(E3) = (Ze)² × (7/(4π)) × (5/8) × β⁶, and B(E4) = (Ze)² × (9/(4π)) × (9/64) × β⁸.
- For the extended Gaussian charge distribution, the coefficient in front of K² in the rotational energy reduces from -0.50 (point-like) to -0.30 for the extracted α and β values.

## Context

This work extends the algebraic method previously applied to two-body clusters with U(4) algebra (Iachello, prior work) to three-body clusters using U(7) algebra, building on the long history of the alpha-particle model in nuclear physics from Wheeler and the Japanese school (Ikeda, Fujiwara, Horiuchi). The approach enables a systematic study of soft, non-rigid cluster structures in light nuclei, with ¹²C as the first application, and provides a formalism that can be extended to other three-body cluster systems and to the study of clustering in nuclei far from stability.
