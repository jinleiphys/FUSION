# The Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov correction to the mean-field critical field transition to Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov states

> The Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) states, characterized by Cooper pairs condensed at finite-momentum are, at the same time, exotic and elusive. It is partially due to the fact that the FFLO states allow superconductivity to survive even in strong magnetic fields at the mean-field level. The effects of induced interactions at zero temperature are calculated in both clean and dirty cases, and it is found that the critical field at which the quantum phase transition to an FFLO state occurs at the mean-field level is strongly suppressed in imbalanced Fermi gases. This strongly shrinks the phase space region where the FFLO state is unstable and more exotic ground state is to be found. In the presence of high level impurities, this shrinkage may destroy the FFLO state completely.

## Key claim

The Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov (GMB) correction to the mean-field critical field for the transition to Fulde-Ferrell-Larkin-Ovchinnikov (FFLO) states is calculated for the first time, showing that induced interactions from particle-hole fluctuations strongly suppress the critical chemical potential imbalance \(h_s\) at which the FFLO phase appears, thereby shrinking the phase space region where the FFLO state is predicted at mean-field level; in the presence of high-level impurities, this shrinkage may destroy the FFLO state completely.

## Method

The authors calculate the static polarization function \(\chi_\text{ph}(q,h)\) for an imbalanced Fermi gas at zero temperature, obtaining the generalized Lindhard function \(L(x,y)\) for the spin-up and spin-down contributions separately. They then average the induced interaction over the Fermi sphere to obtain the s-wave part of the effective pairing interaction, and incorporate this GMB correction into the Thouless criterion for pairing instability. The critical field \(h_s\) for the FFLO transition is determined by solving the divergence condition of the T-matrix with the renormalized interaction, both in the clean limit and in the presence of nonmagnetic impurities via a lifetime broadening.

## Key numbers

* The mean-field FFLO transition occurs at \(h_s \simeq 0.75 \Delta_0\) without GMB corrections, with the Clogston-Chandrasekhar limit at \(h_c \approx 0.71 \Delta_0\), giving an FFLO window of \(0.71 < h/\Delta_0 < 0.75\).
* The GMB correction reduces the critical field to \(h_s/\Delta_0 = (h_s/\Delta_0)^\text{MF} e^{-\bar L(y_s)}\), where \(\bar L(0) = (1+2\ln 2)/3 = 0.795431454\) for the balanced case, decreasing to 0.69 as the imbalance parameter \(y = h/\mu\) increases from 0 to 1.
* In the balanced case, the zero-temperature BCS gap is modified as \(\Delta_0^\text{GMB} = \Delta_0 / (4e)^{1/3}\), where \((4e)^{1/3} \approx 2.2\).
* The critical wave-vector for the FFLO state is \(q_c \simeq 1.8 \Delta_0 / v_F\), corresponding to \(\bar q_c \simeq 1.2\) from the extremal condition \(2 \bar q_c = \ln |(1+\bar q_c)/(1-\bar q_c)|\).

## Context

This work extends the original Gor'kov and Melik-Barkhudarov (1961) calculation of induced interactions in balanced Fermi gases to the imbalanced case relevant for FFLO states, which were proposed by Fulde and Ferrell (1964) and Larkin and Ovchinnikov (1964). Previous investigations with nonzero Zeeman field by Yu (2010) and Resende focused on the tricritical point, while the present work specifically addresses the FFLO transition window. The results constrain the phase space where mean-field FFLO solutions exist, suggesting that regions previously predicted to host FFLO states may instead exhibit more conventional solutions such as polaronic normal phases or phase separation, as observed in experiments by Zwierlein and Salomon groups.
