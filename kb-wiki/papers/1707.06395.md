# String percolation threshold for elliptically bounded systems

> It has been shown that a hot and dense deconfined nuclear matter state produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions, can be quantitatively described by the String Percolation phenomenological model. The model address the phase transition in terms of the two-dimensional continuum percolation theory over strings, which are schematic representations of the fundamental interactions among the partons of the colliding nuclei in the initial state. In this work, we present an extension of the critical string density results including the eccentricity dependence on the initial state geometry focus on small string number with different density profile, small deviations from the different profile densities are found. The percolation threshold shows consistency with the thermodynamic limit for the uniform density profile with a large number of strings in the case of circular boundary system. A significant dependence on the eccentricity for a small number of strings compared to high occupancy systems is exhibited, the implications may become relevant in hadron-hadron or hadron-nucleus collision systems.

## Key claim
The percolation threshold for string percolation in elliptically bounded systems shows a significant dependence on eccentricity for small numbers of strings (N ~ 13), but becomes independent of eccentricity for highly populated systems approaching the thermodynamic limit; the abstract's claim of "small deviations from the different profile densities" applies only to the comparison between Uniform and Gaussian profiles for small N, where spatial distributions are similar.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations using a find-union algorithm to model two-dimensional continuum percolation of N penetrable discs (strings) of radius r0 = 0.25 fm, randomly placed within an elliptical boundary defined by major semi-axis a and minor semi-axis b. The filling factor η = r0²N/(ab) and eccentricity ε = √(1 - b²/a²) were varied for N = 13, 55, 96, and 500 strings, using both Uniform and three Gaussian density profiles (denoted 1s, 2^(1/2)s, and 2s). For each parameter set, 2×10⁵ realizations were performed, and the percolation probability P was determined by identifying spanning clusters that cross the minor semi-axis through peripheral strings.

## Key numbers
- For N = 500 with Uniform density profile and circular boundary (ε = 0), the percolation threshold approaches η_c ~ 1.13, consistent with the thermodynamic limit for continuum percolation.
- For the 2^(1/2)s Gaussian model with ε = 0.00, the percolation threshold η_c ~ 1.59 was reported (referencing Rodrigues (1998)).
- Simulations were performed for filling factors η from 0.10 to 1.8 with step increments Δη = 0.05.
- The percolation threshold shows strong eccentricity dependence for N = 13, particularly at eccentricities greater than 0.7, where the cross-over length approaches the string diameter.

## Context
This work extends the String Percolation Model (SPM) previously applied to circular boundaries by Amelin (1993), Braun (2001), and Satz (2002) to include elliptical boundaries and eccentricity dependence, motivated by the relevance of initial-state geometry in small collision systems like pp and p-Pb at LHC and RHIC energies. The study builds on the finding by Bautista (2015) that string densities in pp collisions can be comparable to those in AA collisions, and addresses the finite-size effects noted by Rodrigues (1998) for Gaussian density profiles. The results enable future corrections to SPM parameters for physical observables of Quark-Gluon Plasma formation in small collision systems, with implications for understanding elliptic flow and azimuthal asymmetry.
