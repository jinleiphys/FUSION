# Wavelets in Field Theory

> We advocate the use of Daubechies wavelets as a basis for treating a variety of problems in quantum field theory. This basis has both natural large volume and short distance cutoffs, has natural partitions of unity, and the basis functions are all related to the fixed point of a linear renormalization group equation.

## Key claim

Daubechies wavelets provide a basis for quantum field theory that has both natural large-volume and short-distance cutoffs, partitions of unity, and basis functions related to the fixed point of a linear renormalization group equation; however, the paper advocates specifically for the Daubechies K=3 wavelets because they have small support and one continuous derivative, which allows replacing finite-difference approximations of derivatives with actual derivatives.

## Method

The authors construct an orthonormal basis for L²(R) using Daubechies scaling functions and wavelets, generated from a scaling function that solves a linear renormalization group equation with discrete translations and dyadic scale transformations. They extend this to three spatial dimensions by taking products of one-dimensional basis functions, then expand free scalar field operators exactly as sums of these basis functions with time-dependent operator coefficients. The discrete field operators satisfy canonical equal-time commutation relations, and the authors show how to construct discrete creation and annihilation operators that annihilate the free-field vacuum.

## Key numbers

- Daubechies K=3 scaling coefficients (rounded to two decimal places): h₀ = -g₅ = 0.43, h₁ = g₄ = 1.11, h₂ = -g₃ = 0.66, h₃ = g₂ = -0.34, h₄ = -g₁ = -0.39, h₅ = g₀ = -0.66
- Only one of the six K=3 coefficients (h₁ = g₄) has magnitude larger than 1
- The scaling function s(x) and mother wavelet w(x) have support on the interval [0, 2K-1]; for K=3, this is [0, 5]
- Basis functions sⁿₖ(x) and wⁿₖ(x) have compact support on the interval [2⁻ᵏn, 2⁻ᵏ(n+2K-1)]
- The scaling equation eigenvalue for the scaling function at integer points is 1/√2; for the first derivative it is 1/2^(3/2)

## Context

This work extends earlier wavelet applications in field theory by Best (1994, 2000), Ismail (2003), and others, as well as more formal wavelet treatments by Federbush (1995), Battle (1999), and Altaisky (2007, 2010). The paper argues that many useful properties of Daubechies wavelets have not been exploited in field theory, particularly the ability to compute overlap integrals of products of basis functions and their derivatives exactly using only the renormalization group equation and normalization condition, without needing to evaluate the fractal basis functions pointwise. This enables exact expansions of fields with natural cutoffs, systematic study of truncation effects, and a framework for formulating renormalization group transformations.
