# Hydrodynamics and Flow

> In this lecture note, we present several topics on relativistic hydrodynamics and its application to relativistic heavy ion collisions. In the first part we give a brief introduction to relativistic hydrodynamics in the context of heavy ion collisions. In the second part we present the formalism and some fundamental aspects of relativistic ideal and viscous hydrodynamics. In the third part, we start with some basic checks of the fundamental observables followed by discussion of collective flow, in particular elliptic flow, which is one of the most exciting phenomenon in heavy ion collisions at relativistic energies. Next we discuss how to formulate the hydrodynamic model to describe dynamics of heavy ion collisions. Finally, we conclude the third part of the lecture note by showing some results from ideal hydrodynamic calculations and by comparing them with the experimental data.

## Key claim

This lecture note provides a pedagogical introduction to relativistic hydrodynamics and its application to heavy ion collisions, emphasizing that ideal hydrodynamic models show agreement with experimental data at RHIC, but the authors note that the most recent studies were not addressed as they would be presented at the Quark Matter 2008 conference.

## Method

The authors present the formalism of relativistic ideal and viscous hydrodynamics, starting from energy-momentum conservation and current conservation, then decomposing the energy-momentum tensor into ideal and dissipative parts. They derive the equations of motion for both first-order (Navier-Stokes) and second-order theories, including constitutive equations for shear stress tensor and bulk pressure, and apply these to Bjorken's boost-invariant scaling solution for one-dimensional expansion.

## Key numbers

- The system produced in heavy ion collisions dynamically evolves within time duration of order 10-100 fm/c
- For Bjorken's scaling solution, the expansion scalar θ = 1/τ, where τ is proper time
- From N=4 SUSY Yang-Mills theory: η/s = 1/4π and ζ/s = 0
- From lattice calculations of pure SU(3) gauge theory: η/s = O(0.1-1) for gluonic matter
- Bulk viscosity has a prominent peak around Tc from trace anomaly of QCD

## Context

This lecture note was prepared for the Quark Matter 2008 conference in Jaipur, India, and serves as a pedagogical introduction to the hydrodynamic description of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. It extends the foundational work of Bjorken (1982) on boost-invariant scaling solutions and builds upon the formalism developed by Eckart (1940), Landau and Lifshitz, and Israel (1976, 1979). The note enables students and young postdocs to understand the frontier of hydrodynamic modeling, including recent viscous fluid simulations by Muronga (2001-2006), Heinz (2005), Song (2007), Baier (2006), Romatschke (2007), and Dusling (2007).
