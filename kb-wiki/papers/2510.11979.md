# First simultaneous global QCD analysis of kaon and pion parton distributions with lattice QCD constraints

> We perform the first simultaneous global QCD analysis of pion and kaon parton distribution functions (PDFs), constrained by pion- and kaon-induced Drell-Yan (DY) and leading neutron electroproduction data, together with lattice QCD data on pion and kaon PDF moments. The analysis indicates a softer valence $\bar u$ distribution in the $K^-$ than in the $π^-$, and a significantly more peaked valence $s$-quark density in $K^-$ compared with the $\bar u$. The effective exponent governing the high-$x$ behavior of the PDF is found to be larger for $\bar u$ in the kaon, $β_{\bar u}^{K^-}\!= 1.6(2)$, than in the pion, $β_{\bar u}^{π^-}\!= 1.16(4)$, in the range $0.7 \leq x \leq 0.95$. From the gluon momentum fractions we find the pion's gluon content accounts for $\approx 1/3$ of the mass budget of the pion at $μ=2~{\rm GeV}$, but only $\approx 1/4$ for the kaon.

## Key claim
The first simultaneous global QCD analysis of pion and kaon parton distribution functions (PDFs) constrained by both experimental Drell-Yan and leading neutron data together with lattice QCD moments reveals that the valence strange quark distribution in the kaon is significantly harder and more peaked at high x than the valence anti-up distribution, and that the gluon momentum fraction is smaller in the kaon (≈1/4 of the mass budget) than in the pion (≈1/3) at μ=2 GeV.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian Monte Carlo analysis using next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL) QCD factorization for Drell-Yan cross sections, fitting pion-induced DY data from NA10 and E615 experiments, leading neutron electroproduction data from H1 and ZEUS at HERA, and kaon-induced DY ratio data from the NA3 experiment at CERN. Lattice QCD moments from the ETM Collaboration for n=1,2,3 of quark and gluon PDFs in both mesons were included as additional constraints, with PDFs parameterized at the input scale μ₀ = 1.28 GeV using a standard functional form with 25 free parameters total.

## Key numbers
- Effective high-x exponent for anti-up valence in kaon: β_ū^(K⁻) = 1.6(2) in the range 0.7 ≤ x ≤ 0.95
- Effective high-x exponent for anti-up valence in pion: β_ū^(π⁻) = 1.16(4) in the range 0.7 ≤ x ≤ 0.95
- Effective high-x exponent for strange valence in kaon: β_s^(K⁻) = 1.2(4) at 68% credible interval
- Gluon momentum fraction in pion at μ=2 GeV: ⟨x⟩_g^π = 0.439(28) from JAM analysis
- Gluon momentum fraction in kaon at μ=2 GeV: ⟨x⟩_g^K = 0.333(17) from JAM analysis
- Strange quark momentum fraction in kaon at μ=2 GeV: ⟨x⟩_s^K = 0.340(11) from JAM analysis
- Anti-up momentum fraction in kaon at μ=2 GeV: ⟨x⟩_u^K = 0.269(7) from JAM analysis
- χ²/N_dat = 0.80 for all experimental data combined
- χ²/N_dat = 0.65 for all lattice QCD data combined
- Luminosity threshold for 3σ discrimination of kaon valence PDFs at AMBER: ≈ 2 × 10⁻² fb⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the JAM collaboration's previous pion PDF analyses (Barry 2018, Barry 2021, Barry 2022, Good 2025) by performing the first simultaneous fit of pion and kaon PDFs, which is essential because the NA3 experiment (1980) measured only a ratio of kaon to pion cross sections. The analysis supersedes earlier kaon PDF extractions using constituent quark model relations (Gluck 1997) and statistical models (Bourrely 2023), and enables predictions for the upcoming AMBER experiment at CERN, which will measure kaon-induced Drell-Yan cross sections for the first time in nearly 50 years, as well as for future Jefferson Lab and Electron-Ion Collider measurements via the Sullivan process.
