# Superscaling in the resonance region for neutrino-nucleus scattering: The SuSAv2-DCC model

> In this work the SuSAv2 and dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) models have been combined and tested in the inelastic regime for electron and neutrino reactions on nuclei. The DCC model, an approach to study baryon resonances through electron and neutrino induced meson production reactions, has been implemented for the first time in the SuSAv2-inelastic model to analyze the resonance region. Within this framework, we also present a novel description about other inelasticities in the resonance region (SoftDIS). The outcomes of these approaches are firstly benchmarked against (e,e') data on 12C. The description is thus extended to the study of neutrino-nucleus inclusive cross sections on 12C and 40Ar and compared with data from the T2K, MicroBooNE, ArgoNEUT and MINERvA experiments, thus covering a wide kinematical range.

## Key claim

The SuSAv2-DCC model combines the superscaling nuclear framework with the dynamical coupled-channels (DCC) approach for pion production, providing a description of the resonance region for neutrino-nucleus scattering that is benchmarked against electron data before being extended to neutrinos; the model introduces a "SoftDIS" component to account for inelastic processes below 2.1 GeV invariant mass that are not captured by the DCC resonance description.

## Method

The authors implemented the DCC model for pion production in lepton-nucleon interactions within the SuSAv2-inelastic nuclear framework, which uses a mean-field scaling function to describe nuclear dynamics. The inelastic nuclear responses are computed as an integral over final hadronic states of the single-nucleon inelastic hadronic tensor times the inelastic scaling function, with integration limits set to W_X between m_N + m_pi and 2.1 GeV for the DCC resonance contribution. Above 2.1 GeV, inelastic structure functions from Bodek-Ritchie, Bosted-Christy, or parton distribution functions describe the deep-inelastic scattering regime, while the SoftDIS contribution is obtained by subtracting the RES-DCC prediction from the full inelastic prediction in the resonance region.

## Key numbers

* The DCC model has a range of validity within W_X < 2.1 GeV and Q^2 < 3.0 GeV^2
* The Bosted-Christy parametrization works for Q^2 < 8 GeV^2 and 1.1 < W_X/GeV < 3.1
* The parton distribution functions work for high Q^2 (0.8 < Q^2/GeV^2 < 10^6) and W greater than about 3 GeV
* The Bodek-Ritchie parametrization covers a Q^2 range from 0.1 to 30 GeV^2
* The lower limit for the DCC resonance integral is W_X^{min} = m_N + m_pi and the upper limit is W_X^{max} = 2.1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the SuSAv2-inelastic model previously developed by Gonzalez-Rosa et al. (2022), which described resonance production and deep inelastic contributions via extension of the SuSAv2 inelastic model from electron to neutrino reactions. The DCC model, developed by Nakamura et al. (2015) and tested against electron and neutrino scattering data, provides a more complete description of resonant and non-resonant regimes including channel coupling and interference effects. The combined SuSAv2-DCC approach enables improved predictions for neutrino-nucleus inclusive cross sections on 12C and 40Ar, which are relevant for experiments such as T2K, MicroBooNE, ArgoNEUT, and MINERvA, and will be essential for the next-generation DUNE experiment.
