# Solutions to Problems at Les Houches Summer School on EFT

> This work details worked solutions to the various problems set by the lecturers during the course of the Les Houches summer school 2017 on effective field theories in particle physics and cosmology.

## Key claim

This paper presents worked solutions to problems posed during the Les Houches summer school 2017 on effective field theories in particle physics and cosmology; the solutions are based on the final chapter of the school's book, and further exercises added after the school are not included.

## Method

The authors compiled and wrote up detailed solutions to problem sets assigned in lecture series by Neubert, Manohar, Pich, Burgess, Baldauf, and van Kolck, covering renormalization, operator classification, power counting, matching, field redefinitions, and applications of EFT to inflation, large-scale structure, and nuclear physics. The solutions are presented as a formal document with numbered exercises, each containing derivations, algebraic manipulations, and explicit calculations using standard quantum field theory techniques.

## Key numbers

* The superficial degree of divergence for 1PI QCD Feynman graphs is derived as \( D = 4 - N_g - \frac{3}{2}(N_q + N_c) \), where \(N_g\), \(N_q\), \(N_c\) are numbers of external gluons, quarks, and ghosts.
* The one-loop mass renormalization factor \(Z_m\) in QCD yields the divergent part \( I_{\bar q q,\text{div}} = \frac{4 m g_s^2 C_F}{(4\pi)^2} \frac{1}{\hat \epsilon} \), with \(C_F = 4/3\).
* The one-loop quark self-energy divergent part is \( I_{2,\text{div}} = \frac{g_s^2 C_F \slashed p}{(4\pi)^2} \frac{1}{\hat \epsilon} \).
* The Fierz identity for \(SU(N)\) generators normalized to \(\text{Tr}\, T^A T^B = \delta^{AB}/2\) is \( [T^A]^\alpha_{\ \beta} [T^A]^{\lambda}_{\ \sigma} = \frac12 \delta^\alpha_\sigma \delta^\lambda_\beta - \frac{1}{2N} \delta^\alpha_\beta \delta^\lambda_\sigma \).

## Context

This work extends the pedagogical material presented at the Les Houches school by providing explicit solutions to exercises from lectures by Neubert (2019), Manohar (2018), Pich (2018), Burgess (2017), Baldauf (lectures), and van Kolck (2019). It enables students and researchers to verify their understanding of EFT foundations, including renormalization group equations, power counting, and operator classification, and serves as a reference for self-study. The solutions cover introductory to intermediate topics in EFT, complementing the lecture notes and standard textbooks such as Manohar (1996, 2003) and references on SMEFT by Jenkins (2013, 2014) and Alonso (2014).
