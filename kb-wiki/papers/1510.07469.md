# Nuclear saturation in lowest-order Brueckner theory with two- and three-nucleon forces in view of chiral effective field theory

> The nuclear saturation mechanism is discussed in terms of two-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions in chiral effective field theory (Ch-EFT), using the framework of lowest-order Brueckner theory. After the Coester band, which is observed in calculating saturation points with various nucleon-nucleon (NN) forces, is revisited using modern NN potentials and their low-momentum equivalent interactions, detailed account of the saturation curve of the Ch-EFT interaction is presented. The three-nucleon force (3NF) is treated by reducing it to an effective two-body interaction by folding the third nucleon degrees of freedom. Uncertainties due to the choice of the 3NF low-energy constants $c_D$ and $c_E$ are discussed. The reduction of the cutoff-energy dependence of the NN potential is explained by demonstrating the effect of the 3NF in the $^1$S$_0$ and $^3$S$_1$ states.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that including three-nucleon forces (3NF) from chiral effective field theory in lowest-order Brueckner theory substantially reduces the cutoff-energy dependence of nuclear matter saturation curves and brings them closer to the empirical saturation point, but only when the 3NF low-energy constants satisfy the approximate relation \(c_D \approx 4c_E\) which causes the \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) term contributions to largely cancel.

## Method

The author performed lowest-order Brueckner theory (LOBT) calculations in symmetric nuclear matter using N\(^3\)LO chiral effective field theory two-nucleon (NN) interactions with cutoff energies of 450, 550, and 600 MeV. The N\(^2\)LO three-nucleon force was reduced to a density-dependent effective two-body interaction by folding the third nucleon degrees of freedom, then added to the bare NN interaction in the G-matrix equation with the continuous choice for intermediate spectra. The author also revisited the Coester band using modern NN potentials (AV18, NSC97, CD-Bonn, fss2) and low-momentum equivalent interactions with varying cutoff scales.

## Key numbers

- Saturation densities with 3NF included: \(\rho_0 = 0.157\) fm\(^{-3}\) for \(\Lambda=450\) MeV, 0.160 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(\Lambda=550\) MeV, and 0.162 fm\(^{-3}\) for \(\Lambda=600\) MeV
- Saturation energies with 3NF included: \(E(\rho_0)/A = -15.4\) MeV for \(\Lambda=450\) MeV, \(-14.1\) MeV for \(\Lambda=550\) MeV, and \(-13.2\) MeV for \(\Lambda=600\) MeV
- Incompressibilities with 3NF included: \(K = 211\) MeV for \(\Lambda=450\) MeV, 209 MeV for \(\Lambda=550\) MeV, and 194 MeV for \(\Lambda=600\) MeV
- Symmetry energies with 3NF included: \(S = 34\) MeV for \(\Lambda=450\) MeV, 30 MeV for \(\Lambda=550\) MeV, and 30 MeV for \(\Lambda=600\) MeV
- Slope parameters with 3NF included: \(L = 70\) MeV for \(\Lambda=450\) MeV, 57 MeV for \(\Lambda=550\) MeV, and 53 MeV for \(\Lambda=600\) MeV
- The bare S-D tensor interaction is enhanced by about 40% at normal density through the 3NF
- The relation \(c_D \approx 4c_E\) causes cancellation of the \(c_D\) and \(c_E\) term contributions to nuclear matter energies

## Context

This work extends the author's previous LOBT calculations (Kohno, 2012, 2013) and is similar to calculations by Sammarruca et al. (2012, 2015) and Carbone et al. (2013, 2014) who also investigated chiral EFT 3NF contributions in nuclear matter. The paper revisits the Coester band phenomenon first demonstrated by Coester et al. (1970), showing that modern NN potentials still miss the empirical saturation point. The use of low-momentum interactions follows the approach of Bogner et al. (2003), and the treatment of the 3NF as a density-dependent effective NN force builds on the work of Holt, Kaiser, and Weise (2010). The results enable more reliable predictions for nuclear structure calculations using chiral EFT interactions, particularly for finite nuclei where the reduced cutoff dependence is desirable.
