# Consequences of a covariant Description of Heavy Ion Reactions at intermediate Energies

> Heavy ion collisions at intermediate energies are studied by using a new RQMD code, which is a covariant generalization of the QMD approach. We show that this new implementation is able to produce the same results in the nonrelativistic limit (i.e. 50MeV/nucl.) as the non-covariant QMD. Such a comparison is not available in the literature. At higher energies (i.e. 1.5 GeV/nucl. and 2 GeV/nucl.) RQMD and QMD give different results in respect to the time evolution of the phase space, for example for the directed transverse flow. These differences show that consequences of a covariant description of heavy ion reactions within the framework of RQMD are existing even at intermediate energies.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a new covariant RQMD code reproduces non-covariant QMD results in the nonrelativistic limit (50 MeV/nucleon), but at higher intermediate energies (1.5 and 2 GeV/nucleon) the covariant description produces different results for the directed transverse flow, showing that relativistic effects matter even at intermediate energies where they are often neglected.

## Method

The authors implemented a new RQMD code within the UNISCO simulation package, using Constrained Hamilton Dynamics to achieve a manifestly covariant generalization of the standard QMD approach. They compared QMD and RQMD simulations of semicentral Ca+Ca and C+C collisions at 50 MeV/nucleon, 1.5 GeV/nucleon, and 2 GeV/nucleon, analyzing the time evolution of nucleon density, rapidity distributions, and directed transverse momentum. Additional Vlasov-mode (no collisions) and cascade-mode (collisions only) calculations were performed to separate the effects of mutual potential interactions and two-body collisions on the flow.

## Key numbers

- At 50 MeV/nucleon for semicentral C+C collisions (b=1.3 fm), QMD and RQMD show "excellent agreement" for nucleon density, rapidity distribution, and directed transverse flow; for Ca+Ca at the same energy, differences in directed flow are only a "few percent"
- At 1.5 GeV/nucleon for semicentral Ca+Ca collisions (b=2 fm), the directed transverse flow is decreased in RQMD compared to QMD for the soft equation of state but increased for the hard equation of state
- At 2 GeV/nucleon for the same system, the flow is enhanced in RQMD compared to QMD for both hard and soft equations of state
- At 2 GeV/nucleon, RQMD in the Vlasov limit creates a positive final flow while QMD in the Vlasov limit gives a negative flow for the same reaction
- The nuclei used in the simulations (12C, 28Si, 40Ca) are stable for at least 200 fm/c as measured by root mean square radius

## Context

This work extends the RQMD formalism originally developed by Sorge et al. (1989) for ultrarelativistic energies down to the intermediate energy range (100 MeV/nucleon to a few GeV/nucleon). It addresses a gap in the literature by providing the first direct comparison of covariant and non-covariant approaches at nonrelativistic energies as a benchmark, which was not done in earlier RQMD studies by Maruyama et al. (1991). The results enable future studies to reliably separate genuine relativistic effects from numerical artifacts when extracting the nuclear equation of state from heavy ion collision data.
