# First-Generation New Physics in Simplified Models: From Low-Energy Parity Violation to the LHC

> New-physics (NP) constraints on first-generation quark-lepton interactions are particularly interesting given the large number of complementary processes and observables that have been measured. Recently, first hints for such NP effects have been observed as an apparent deficit in first-row CKM unitarity, known as the Cabibbo angle anomaly, and the CMS excess in $q\bar q\to e^+e^-$. Since the same NP would inevitably enter in searches for low-energy parity violation, such as atomic parity violation, parity-violating electron scattering, and coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering, as well as electroweak precision observables, a combined analysis is required to assess the viability of potential NP interpretations. In this article we investigate the interplay between LHC searches, the Cabibbo angle anomaly, electroweak precision observables, and low-energy parity violation by studying all simplified models that give rise to tree-level effects related to interactions between first-generation quarks and leptons. Matching these models onto Standard Model effective field theory, we derive master formulae in terms of the respective Wilson coefficients, perform a complete phenomenological analysis of all available constraints, point out how parity violation can in the future be used to disentangle different NP scenarios, and project the constraints achievable with forthcoming experiments.

## Key claim

The paper systematically investigates all simplified models (leptoquarks, vector bosons, vector-like quarks, and vector-like leptons) that can produce tree-level modifications to first-generation quark-lepton interactions, finding that the Cabibbo angle anomaly and the CMS di-lepton excess can be simultaneously explained only by specific leptoquark representations, while other simplified models face strong constraints from low-energy parity violation and electroweak precision observables.

## Method

The authors match each simplified model onto Standard Model effective field theory (SMEFT) at tree level, deriving master formulae for the Wilson coefficients of dimension-6 four-fermion operators. They then compute the contributions to parity-violating electron scattering, atomic parity violation, coherent neutrino-nucleus scattering, electroweak precision observables, the Cabibbo angle anomaly, and LHC di-lepton searches, performing a combined phenomenological analysis of all available constraints.

## Key numbers

- Proton weak charge from Qweak experiment: Q_w^p = 0.0704(47), extracted from asymmetry A_e^p = -226.5(9.3) x 10^{-9} at <Q^2> = 0.0248 GeV^2 and <theta> = 7.90 degrees
- Weak charge of Cs-133: Q_w(Cs-133) = -72.94(43), compared to SM prediction of -73.24(5)
- PVDIS asymmetries: A_PVDIS^(1) = -91.10(3.11)(2.97) x 10^{-6} (SM: -87.7(7) x 10^{-6}) and A_PVDIS^(2) = -160.80(6.39)(3.12) x 10^{-6} (SM: -158.9(1.0) x 10^{-6})
- Future P2 experiment aims to measure proton weak charge with 1.83% relative precision at <Q^2> = 0.006 GeV^2 and <theta> = 35 degrees

## Context

This work extends the analysis of first-generation new physics by considering all four classes of simplified models that can modify vector and axial-vector quark-lepton interactions, building on earlier SMEFT analyses by de Blas et al. (2013) and Falkowski et al. (2017) of 4-fermion contact interactions, and the analysis of beta decays and LHC bounds by Cirigliano et al. (2012). The results enable future experimental programs such as the P2 experiment at MESA and the SoLID experiment at Jefferson Lab to distinguish between different new physics scenarios through improved parity-violation measurements.
