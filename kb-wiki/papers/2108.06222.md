# Mass spectra and decays of open-heavy tetraquark states

> Open-heavy tetraquark states, especially those contain four different quarks have drawn much attention in both theoretical and experimental fields. In the framework of the improved chromomagnetic interaction (ICMI) model, we complete a systematic study on the mass spectra and possible strong decay channels of the $S$-wave open-heavy tetraquark states, $qq\bar{q}\bar{Q}$ ($q=u,d,s$ and $Q=c,b$), with different quantum number $J^P=0^+$, $1^+$, and $2^+$. The parameters in the ICMI model are extracted from the conventional hadron spectra and used directly to predict the mass of tetraquark states. Several compact bound states and narrow resonances are found in both charm-strange and bottom-strange tetraquark sectors, most of them as a product of the strong coupling between the different channels. Our results show the recently discovered four different flavors tetraquark candidates $X_0(2900)$ is probably compact $ud\bar{s}\bar{c}$ state with quantum number $J^P=0^+$. The predictions about $X_0(2900)$ and its partners are expected to be better checked with other theories and future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the recently observed tetraquark candidate \(X_0(2900)\) is probably a compact \(ud\bar{s}\bar{c}\) state with quantum number \(J^P = 0^+\), based on calculations in the improved chromomagnetic interaction (ICMI) model; the claim is hedged by noting that the predictions should be better checked with other theories and future experiments.

## Method

The authors use the improved chromomagnetic interaction (ICMI) model, which includes both chromomagnetic and chromoelectric two-body interactions, to compute the mass spectra of \(S\)-wave open-heavy tetraquark states of the form \(qq\bar{q}\bar{Q}\) (with \(q = u,d,s\) and \(Q = c,b\)) for quantum numbers \(J^P = 0^+\), \(1^+\), and \(2^+\). Model parameters (effective masses \(m_{ij}\) and coupling strengths \(v_{ij}\)) are extracted by fitting the experimental masses of conventional mesons and baryons from the Particle Data Group, then used directly to predict tetraquark masses by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in a color-spin basis. The analysis considers both meson-meson and diquark-antidiquark color configurations and includes the mixing between different color-spin channels.

## Key numbers

- The \(X_0(2900)\) candidate has experimental mass \(M = 2.866 \pm 0.007 \pm 0.002\ \text{GeV}\) and width \(\Gamma = 57 \pm 12 \pm 4\ \text{MeV}\) from LHCb.
- The \(X_1(2900)\) candidate has experimental mass \(M = 2.904 \pm 0.005 \pm 0.001\ \text{GeV}\) and width \(\Gamma = 110 \pm 11 \pm 4\ \text{MeV}\) from LHCb.
- For the \(nn\bar{s}\bar{c}\) system with \(J^P = 0^+\), the predicted mass closest to \(X_0(2900)\) is \(2799.9\ \text{MeV}\) (one of four eigenstates).
- For the \(nn\bar{s}\bar{c}\) system with \(J^P = 1^+\), the predicted masses range from \(2417.9\ \text{MeV}\) to \(2975.3\ \text{MeV}\).
- For the \(sn\bar{s}\bar{c}\) system with \(J^P = 0^+\), the predicted masses are \(2412.4\ \text{MeV}\), \(2751.9\ \text{MeV}\), \(2962.9\ \text{MeV}\), and \(3138.5\ \text{MeV}\).
- The extracted parameter \(v_{n\bar{c}} = 6.591\ \text{MeV}\) and \(m_{n\bar{c}} = 1975.11\ \text{MeV}\) for meson-like pairs.

## Context

This work extends the improved chromomagnetic interaction model, previously applied to mesons and baryons by Weng et al. (2018) and others, to systematically study open-heavy tetraquarks with four different flavors. It directly addresses the controversy over the structure of the \(X_0(2900)\) and \(X_1(2900)\) states observed by LHCb (2020), which have been interpreted as molecular states by some groups (e.g., using QCD sum rules by Chen (2020) and one-boson-exchange models by Liu (2020)) and as compact tetraquarks by others (e.g., using chromomagnetic interaction models by He (2020)). The predictions for masses and decay channels of charm-strange and bottom-strange tetraquarks provide targets for future experimental searches and for comparison with other theoretical approaches such as lattice QCD or quark models.
