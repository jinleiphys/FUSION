# Self-consistent collective subspaces and diabatic/adiabatic motion in nuclei

## Key claim

The theory of adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion (ALACM) can successfully describe both anharmonic vibrations and shape coexistence in a fully microscopic pairing-plus-quadrupole model, but the method may fail for some states in the double-well (shape-coexistence) regime, which remains under investigation.

## Method

The authors apply the adiabatic large-amplitude collective motion (ALACM) formalism of Klein et al. (1991) to a Hamiltonian with O(4) dynamical symmetry, consisting of monopole pairing and quadrupole-quadrupole (QQ) interactions. They truncate the Hamiltonian to quadratic order in momenta, then solve iteratively for a single collective coordinate by requiring that the covariant RPA eigenvector satisfies a force condition at each point along the collective path. The method is tested against exact diagonalisation in a two-shell O(4) model with different quadrupole force strengths.

## Key numbers

- Two-shell calculation uses shells with \(\Omega=10\) each, quadrupole weights \(q_1=3, q_2=1\), and single-particle energies \(\epsilon_1=0, \epsilon_2=10\).
- Three quadrupole force strengths are studied: \(\chi=0.1\) (almost harmonic motion), \(\chi=0.3\) (flat potential at the turnover from single to double well), and \(\chi=0.4\) (shape coexistence with double-well structure).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of collective motion in a pairing model with level crossings (Nakatsukasa and Walet, 1998) to a fully microscopic Hamiltonian that can describe both vibrational and well-deformed nuclei. The model Hamiltonian was originally developed by PSS (1980) for \(K^\pi=0^+\) excitations in deformed nuclei and has been used to test other collective methods by Mat (1982, 1986) and SM (1988). The results enable future application to the proton-neutron O(4) model and to the realistic pairing-plus-quadrupole model.
