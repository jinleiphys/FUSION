# On the resolution of the sign of gluon polarization in the proton

> Recently the possible existence of negative gluon helicity, $Δg$, has been observed to be compatible with existing empirical constraints, including from jet production in polarized proton-proton collisions at RHIC, and lattice QCD data on polarized gluon Ioffe time distributions. We perform a new global analysis of polarized parton distributions in the proton with new constraints from the high-$x$ region of deep-inelastic scattering (DIS). A dramatic reduction in the quality of the fit for the negative $Δg$ replicas compared to those with positive $Δg$ suggest that the negative $Δg$ solution cannot simultaneously account for high-$x$ polarized DIS data along with lattice and polarized jet data.

## Key claim

The paper finds that negative gluon helicity solutions cannot be ruled out by Higgs production cross sections when lattice QCD constraints are included, but the combination of polarized jet data from RHIC, lattice QCD simulations, and fixed-target high-x deep-inelastic scattering data does strongly disfavor solutions with negative polarized glue.

## Method

The authors performed a global QCD analysis within the JAM framework using next-to-leading order fixed-order collinear factorization, fitting unpolarized and polarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions simultaneously to a large dataset. They considered three scenarios: a baseline fit from the JAM22 analysis, a fit adding lattice QCD pseudo-Ioffe time distributions, and a fit further adding 1370 high-x polarized DIS data points by lowering the W² cut from 10 GeV² to 4 GeV², including target mass corrections and higher-twist parametrizations.

## Key numbers

- The total dataset includes 8633 data points across all fits, with 2067 polarized data points and 5954 unpolarized data points
- For the negative Δg solution with high-x DIS included, the reduced χ² for LQCD data increases from 1.18 to 3.92, and for inclusive jets from 1.10 to 1.44
- For the positive Δg solution with high-x DIS included, the reduced χ² for LQCD data remains at 0.58 and for inclusive jets at 0.90
- The lattice QCD data consists of 48 data points from 1901 configurations with pion mass mπ = 358(3) MeV and lattice spacing a = 0.096(1) fm
- The Higgs production asymmetry ALL^H at √s = 510 GeV for mH = 125 GeV exceeds unity for most negative Δg replicas in the baseline fit, but falls below unity for the vast majority when LQCD data is added

## Context

This work extends the JAM22 analysis of helicity-dependent PDFs (Cocuzza 2022) and the combined analysis of spin-dependent PDFs with lattice QCD data by Karpie (2023), which found that negative Δg solutions could not be ruled out. It contradicts the claim by de Florian (2024) that negative Δg solutions could be ruled out by Higgs production positivity constraints, showing that LQCD constraints make those solutions consistent with Higgs observables. The analysis enables future studies of gluon polarization using dijet production at RHIC and polarized SIDIS at Jefferson Lab and the Electron-Ion Collider.
