# Dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics: a new way to derive the equations of motion from kinetic theory

> We re-derive the equations of motion of dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics from kinetic theory. In contrast to the derivation of Israel and Stewart, which considered the second moment of the Boltzmann equation to obtain equations of motion for the dissipative currents, we directly use the latter's definition. Although the equations of motion obtained via the two approaches are formally identical, the coefficients are different. We show that, for the one-dimensional scaling expansion, our method is in better agreement with the solution obtained from the Boltzmann equation.

## Key claim
The equations of motion for dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics derived directly from the definitions of the dissipative currents (rather than from the second moment of the Boltzmann equation as in Israel-Stewart theory) are formally identical in structure but have different transport coefficients, and for the one-dimensional scaling expansion they agree better with the numerical solution of the Boltzmann equation.

## Method
The authors start from the relativistic Boltzmann equation and define the dissipative currents (bulk viscous pressure Π, particle diffusion current n^μ, and shear stress tensor π^μν) in terms of the single-particle distribution function. They compute the comoving time derivatives of these currents directly from their definitions, then close the resulting equations using the 14-moment approximation for the distribution function. The resulting transport coefficients are compared to those obtained by Israel and Stewart, who instead used the second moment of the Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers
- For a massless Boltzmann gas in Bjorken scaling expansion, the shear transport coefficients from the new method are: β_π = 4P_0/5, τ_π^{-1} = (3/5)σP_0β_0, and λ ≡ (4/9)τ_{ππ} + δ_{ππ} = 124/63.
- For Israel-Stewart theory, the corresponding coefficients are: β_π = 2P_0/3, τ_π^{-1} = (5/9)σP_0β_0, and λ = 2.
- The shear viscosity from the new method, η = β_π τ_π, is related to the Israel-Stewart shear viscosity by η = (10/9)η_IS.
- The numerical comparison uses initial temperature T = 500 MeV and initial π = 0, with viscosity-to-entropy ratios η/s = 0.05 and η/s = 3.33.

## Context
This work extends the derivation of dissipative relativistic fluid dynamics from kinetic theory, which was previously developed by Israel and Stewart (1979) using the 14-moment approximation and the second moment of the Boltzmann equation. The new method resolves inconsistencies noted between Israel-Stewart theory and numerical solutions of the Boltzmann equation (Denicol et al. 2010; El et al. 2009), and produces transport coefficients consistent with quantum field theoretical calculations (Dusling et al. 2010). This enables more accurate fluid-dynamical modeling of relativistic heavy-ion collisions and astrophysical systems.
