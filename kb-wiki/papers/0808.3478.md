# Scaling laws and higher-order effects in Coulomb excitation of neutron halo nuclei

> Essential properties of halo nuclei can be described in terms of a few low-energy constants. For neutron halo nuclei, analytical results can be found for wave functions and electromagnetic transition matrix-elements in simple but well-adapted models. These wave functions can be used to study nuclear reactions; an especially simple and instructive example is Coulomb excitation. A systematic expansion in terms of small parameters can be given. We present scaling laws for excitation amplitudes and cross sections. The results can be used to analyze experiments like ${}^{11}$Be Coulomb excitation. They also serve as benchmark tests for more involved reaction theories.

## Key claim

The paper presents analytical scaling laws for Coulomb excitation amplitudes and cross sections of neutron halo nuclei, showing that higher-order electromagnetic effects reduce the first-order cross section by 6–11% for existing ${}^{11}$Be experiments, with the reduction depending only on binding energies and a core size parameter rather than detailed nuclear potential shapes.

## Method

The authors treat Coulomb excitation in the semiclassical straight-line sudden approximation, using analytical single-particle wave functions for $s$-wave ground states and $p$-wave excited states of neutron halo nuclei in the exterior region beyond the core radius $R$. They expand the excitation amplitude in powers of a dimensionless strength parameter $z = q_{\rm Coul}/(q_0+q_1)$ and derive closed-form expressions for the leading-order and next-to-leading-order contributions to the excitation probability and total cross section.

## Key numbers

- Neutron separation energies for ${}^{11}$Be: $E_0 = 504$ keV (ground state) and $E_1 = 184$ keV (excited $1/2^-$ state), giving excitation energy $\hbar\omega = 320$ keV
- Dimensionless scaling parameters for ${}^{11}$Be: $\gamma_0 = 0.4116$ and $\gamma_1 = 0.2487$ (using core radius $R = 2.78$ fm)
- Calculated $B(E1) = 0.193$ $e^2$fm$^2$ for the $1/2^+ \rightarrow 1/2^-$ transition in ${}^{11}$Be (compared to experimental $0.105(12)$ $e^2$fm$^2$ from GANIL data)
- Scattering length in the $l=1$, $j=1/2$ channel of ${}^{11}$Be: $457(67,-66)$ fm$^3$; effective-range parameter $r_1 = -0.547$ fm$^{-1}$
- Reduction factors $R(v)$ for cross sections: 0.8902 (GANIL at 38.6 MeV/nucleon), 0.9025 (GANIL at 43.0 MeV/nucleon), 0.9321 (MSU at 60.0 MeV/nucleon), 0.9366 (RIKEN at 64.0 MeV/nucleon)
- Maximum strength parameter $z_{\rm max}$ values: 0.6549 (GANIL 38.6 MeV), 0.6227 (GANIL 43.0 MeV), 0.5343 (MSU 60.0 MeV), 0.5189 (RIKEN 64.0 MeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of higher-order electromagnetic effects in ${}^{11}$Be Coulomb excitation (Typel and Baur, 1995) by using improved analytical halo wave functions that properly account for the angular momentum barrier in the $p$-wave excited state. The results provide benchmark tests for more sophisticated numerical approaches such as XCDCC (used in the analysis of recent ${}^{11}$Be experiments) and time-dependent calculations, and demonstrate that halo nuclei properties depend only on a few low-energy constants in accord with effective-range theory.
