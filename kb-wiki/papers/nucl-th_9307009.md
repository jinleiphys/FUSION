# Self-consistent solution of the Schwinger-Dyson equations for the nucleon and meson propagators

> The Schwinger-Dyson equations for the nucleon and meson propagators are solved self-consistently in an approximation that goes beyond the Hartree-Fock approximation. The traditional approach consists in solving the nucleon Schwinger-Dyson equation with bare meson propagators and bare meson-nucleon vertices; the corrections to the meson propagators are calculated using the bare nucleon propagator and bare nucleon-meson vertices. It is known that such an approximation scheme produces the appearance of ghost poles in the propagators. In this paper the coupled system of Schwinger-Dyson equations for the nucleon and the meson propagators are solved self-consistently including vertex corrections. The interplay of self-consistency and vertex corrections on the ghosts problem is investigated. It is found that the self-consistency does not affect significantly the spectral properties of the propagators. In particular, it does not affect the appearance of the ghost poles in the propagators.

## Key claim

The paper finds that solving the coupled Schwinger-Dyson equations for the nucleon, pion, and omega meson propagators self-consistently, including vertex corrections via form factors, does not significantly alter the spectral properties of the propagators compared to the non-self-consistent Hartree-Fock approach; in particular, self-consistency does not remove the ghost poles that appear in the propagators.

## Method

The authors solved the coupled system of Schwinger-Dyson equations for the nucleon, pion, and omega meson propagators in vacuum using an iterative procedure. The equations were truncated by postulating specific forms for the three-point meson-nucleon vertex functions, using either bare vertices or monopole form factors with an ultraviolet cutoff Lambda. The spectral functions for all three propagators were computed self-consistently by iterating between the nucleon self-energy and the meson self-energies until convergence.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_pi^2/(4 pi) = 14.6, g_omega^2/(4 pi) = 6.36
- Meson masses: m_pi = 0.144 M, m_omega = 0.833 M, where M is the nucleon mass
- For bare vertices, the nucleon ghost pole position is 1.06 +/- 1.25i M (self-consistent) versus 1.05 +/- 1.26i M (non-self-consistent), with residues -0.77 +/- 0.20i M in both cases
- For bare vertices, the pion ghost pole position is -1.04 M^2 (self-consistent) versus -1.44 M^2 (non-self-consistent), with residues -1.08 M^2 and -1.13 M^2 respectively
- For bare vertices, the omega ghost pole position is -3.50 M^2 (self-consistent) versus -5.68 M^2 (non-self-consistent), with residues -1.30 M^2 and -1.49 M^2 respectively
- With monopole form factors, ghost poles disappear for cutoff Lambda less than approximately 1.60 M (self-consistent), compared to approximately 1.75 M found in prior work without self-consistency

## Context

This work extends the investigation of ghost poles in relativistic meson-nucleon field theory, following earlier studies by Brown, Puff and Wilets (1970) who first identified complex conjugate poles in the nucleon propagator, and by Krein, Nielsen, Puff and Wilets (1993) who studied the effect of vertex form factors on ghost poles without full self-consistency. The paper also relates to work by Allendes and Serot (1992) who found that Sudakov form factors could eliminate ghost poles in the meson propagator. The results enable future studies of nucleon and meson properties in nuclear matter, where vacuum polarization effects have typically been neglected.
