# Solar reaction rates, non-extensivity and quantum uncertainty

> We show that in weakly non-ideal plasmas, like the solar interior, both non-extensivity and quantum uncertainty (a' la Galitskii and Yakimets) should be taken into account to derive equilibrium ion distribution functions and to estimate nuclear reaction rates and solar neutrino fluxes.

# Solar reaction rates, non-extensivity and quantum uncertainty

## Key claim

In weakly non-ideal plasmas such as the solar interior, both non-extensive (Tsallis) statistics and quantum uncertainty effects (a la Galitskii and Yakimets) must be included simultaneously to derive equilibrium ion distribution functions and estimate nuclear reaction rates; the non-extensive parameter q is not a free parameter but can be derived from the plasma parameter and the ion correlation function, and the combined effects can bring calculated solar neutrino fluxes into agreement with experimental measurements without requiring new physics beyond the plasma.

## Method

The authors derived an equilibrium ion distribution function from a Fokker-Planck equation that includes the average energy density of electric microfields, obtaining a Maxwellian distribution multiplied by a factor exp[-delta (epsilon/kT)^2] which approximates the Tsallis distribution for q near 1. They expressed the delta parameter (related to q as delta = (1-q)/2) in terms of the plasma parameter Gamma and the Ichimaru ion correlation parameter alpha, using the elastic diffusion cross section and the enforced Coulomb cross section from the Wigner-Seitz ion sphere model. They then computed nuclear reaction rates by combining the non-extensive distribution with the quantum uncertainty correction of Galitskii and Yakimets, which adds a power-law tail to the momentum distribution, and solved the time-dependent rate equations with the solar luminosity constraint to obtain steady-state densities and neutrino fluxes.

## Key numbers

- Plasma parameter in the solar core: Gamma = Z^2 e^2/(a_i kT) approximately 0.1
- Quantum uncertainty parameter: hbar nu/kT approximately 0.1 in the solar core
- Derived non-extensive parameter: q = 0.990 (delta = 0.005) for alpha = 0.55 and Gamma approximately 0.1
- Calculated Gallium neutrino flux: 81 SNU
- Calculated Chlorine neutrino flux: 2.8 SNU
- Ratio of B-8 neutrino flux to standard solar model: Phi(B)/Phi^M(B) approximately 1/2
- Ratio of Be-7 neutrino flux to standard solar model: Phi(Be^7)/Phi^M(Be^7) = 1/50
- Assumed He-3 density relative to standard solar model: n_3/n_3^M approximately 3 x 10^{-3}
- pp neutrino flux and luminosity: practically unchanged from standard solar model values

## Context

This work extends the approach of Savchenko (using Green's function technique for tunneling reaction probability) and the quantum uncertainty treatment of Galitskii and Yakimets (1964) for dense plasmas, as applied to the solar core by the same authors in prior work. It also builds on the non-extensive thermostatistics of Tsallis and the earlier ad hoc distribution postulated by Clayton (more than twenty years prior) for the solar neutrino problem. The results enable a consistent astrophysical solution to the solar neutrino problem that does not require neutrino oscillations or other particle physics beyond the standard model, though the authors note the evaluation of the ion correlation parameter alpha carries significant uncertainty.
