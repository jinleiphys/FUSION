# Formation of three-particle clusters in hetero-junctions and MOSFET structures

> A novel interaction mechanism in MOSFET structures and $GaAs/AlGaAs$ hetero-junctions between the zone electrons of the two-dimensional (2D) gas and the charged traps on the insulator side is considered. By applying a canonical transformation, off-diagonal terms in the Hamiltonian due to the trapped level subsystem are excluded. This yields an effective three-particle attractive interaction as well as a pairing interaction inside the 2D electronic band. A type of Bethe- Goldstone equation for three particles is studied to clarify the character of the binding and the energy of the three-particle bound states. The results are used to offer a possible explanation of the Metal-Insulator transition recently observed in MOSFET and hetero-junctions.

# Formation of three-particle clusters in hetero-junctions and MOSFET structures

## Key claim
The paper proposes that a trap-mediated three-particle attractive interaction, arising from the scattering of two-dimensional band electrons on charged trap centers in MOSFET and GaAs/AlGaAs hetero-junction structures, can lead to the formation of three-electron bound states. This mechanism offers a possible explanation for the Metal-Insulator transition observed at very low temperatures, but only under the condition that the trap level energy lies above the chemical potential of the band electrons and that the attractive potential exceeds a threshold value.

## Method
The authors model a system of two-dimensional band electrons interacting with dispersionless charged trap centers located on the insulator side of the interface. They apply a canonical (unitary) transformation to eliminate off-diagonal terms in the Hamiltonian that describe electron trapping and release processes, analogous to the treatment of electron-phonon interactions in superconductivity. This transformation generates an effective Hamiltonian containing both a two-particle attractive interaction and a three-particle attractive interaction. The three-particle bound state problem is then studied using a Bethe-Goldstone-type equation in Jacobi coordinates, assuming a simplified attractive interaction potential within a narrow energy window near the Fermi surface.

## Key numbers
- The density of trapped centers in MOSFET structures is of the order of $10^9$ cm$^{-2}$, which is considerably smaller than the typical carrier concentration of $\sim 2\times 10^{11}$ cm$^{-2}$.
- The charged donors are located within a region of at most $\sim 200$ Å from the oxide-silicon interface.
- The threshold condition for three-particle bound state formation is $V_0 \ge 1/(\ln{2} \, N^2 \hbar \omega_0)$, where $N$ is the density of electronic states at the Fermi surface and $\hbar \omega_0$ is the cut-off energy comparable to the trap level energy measured from the Fermi level.
- In the strong coupling regime, the bound state energy is $\epsilon = -\frac{3}{4} V_0 (\hbar \omega_0 N)^2$.
- Experimental data fitted with the model use electron densities ranging from $n=6.85$ to $7.85 \times 10^{10}$ cm$^{-2}$, with fitted critical temperatures $T_c$ ranging from 0.8 K to 3.9 K and randomness parameters $\lambda$ ranging from 0.004 to 0.86.
- The conductivity at the critical density $G_c$ is observed to be $\sim e^2/h$ in the strongly disordered case (Altshuler et al., 2001) and $\sim 120 e^2/h$ in the weakly disordered case (Pudalov, 1998).

## Context
This work extends the understanding of the Metal-Insulator transition in two-dimensional electron systems by proposing a three-particle clustering mechanism, in contrast to earlier explanations based on temperature-dependent trapped-electron concentration proposed by Altshuler and Maslov (1999), which the authors note could not reproduce quantitative experimental features. The paper also discusses an alternative interpretation of the MIT as resulting from possible superconducting ground state fluctuations (Nature, 1998), but argues that phase fluctuations suppress superconductivity in 2D. The model further suggests connections to the Fractional Quantum Hall effect, where the angular momentum $M=3$ of the triangular cluster configuration could provide a natural basis for the filling factor $\nu=1/3$ in Laughlin's theory. The results enable future experimental tests of the predicted temperature and density dependence of conductivity, particularly the logarithmic temperature dependence in the metallic phase observed by Pudalov (1998).
