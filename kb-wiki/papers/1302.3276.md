# Practical corollaries of transverse Ward-Green-Takahashi identities

> The gauge principle is fundamental in formulating the Standard Model. Fermion--gauge-boson couplings are the inescapable consequence and the primary determining factor for observable phenomena. Vertices describing such couplings are simple in perturbation theory and yet the existence of strong-interaction bound-states guarantees that many phenomena within the Model are nonperturbative. It is therefore crucial to understand how dynamics dresses the vertices and thereby fundamentally alters the appearance of fermion--gauge-boson interactions. We consider the coupling of a dressed-fermion to an Abelian gauge boson, and describe a unified treatment and solution of the familiar longitudinal Ward-Green-Takahashi identity and its less well known transverse counterparts. Novel consequences for the dressed-fermion--gauge-boson vertex are exposed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the transverse Ward-Green-Takahashi identities, together with the familiar longitudinal identity, form a complete set of twelve linearly independent equations that uniquely determine the dressed-fermion--gauge-boson vertex in an Abelian theory, provided one accepts that the nonlocal terms in the transverse identities can be parameterized by eight unknown scalar functions.

## Method

The authors write the dressed-fermion--gauge-boson vertex as a sum of four longitudinal and eight transverse tensor structures. They contract the transverse Ward-Green-Takahashi identity for the axial-vector vertex with two specific tensor projectors, thereby eliminating the coupling between vector and axial-vector channels and obtaining three matrix-valued equations (the longitudinal identity plus two transverse projections) that involve only the vector vertex. These equations constitute twelve coupled linear equations for the twelve unknown scalar coefficient functions, which are then solved algebraically in terms of the fermion propagator functions A(p^2) and B(p^2) and the eight unknown scalar functions X_i that encode the nonlocal contributions.

## Key numbers

- The minimal vertex Ansatz yields a renormalization-point-invariant anomalous magnetic moment kappa = 1.14 for a Euclidean constituent-quark mass M = 0.38 GeV obtained from the DCSB-improved gap equation kernel of Chang (2013).
- The same minimal Ansatz gives kappa = 1.6 when using the vertex Ansatz of Bashir (2011) for comparison.
- Averaged over the nonperturbative domain p^2 in [0,2] GeV^2, the anomalous magnetic moment distribution gives mean kappa = 0.49.
- The gluon mass function at zero momentum is m_G(0) approximately 0.5 GeV, which is greater than or approximately equal to the quark mass function M(0).

## Context

This work extends the longitudinal Ward-Green-Takahashi identity treatment of Ball (1980) by incorporating the transverse identities derived by Takahashi (1985) and He (2000, 2006, 2007). The solution confirms and places on firmer ground the vertex Ansatz proposed by Bashir (2011) based on multiplicative renormalisability and one-loop perturbation theory, while also providing a simpler minimal Ansatz. The results enable improved Poincare-covariant studies of hadron electromagnetic form factors as described by Cloet (2008) and Eichmann (2011), and can serve as a prototype for constructing electromagnetic currents in few-nucleon physics following Gross (1987).
