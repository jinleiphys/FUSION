# Neutrino induced pion production at MiniBooNE and K2K

> We investigate charged and neutral current neutrino induced incoherent pion production off nuclei at MiniBooNE and K2K energies within the GiBUU model. We assume impulse approximation and treat the nucleus as a local Fermi gas of nucleons bound in a mean-field potential. In-medium spectral functions are also taken into account. The outcome of the initial neutrino nucleon reaction undergoes complex hadronic final state interactions. We present results for neutral current pi^0 and charged current pi^+ production and compare to MiniBooNE and K2K data.

## Key claim

The GiBUU model, which includes a full treatment of hadronic final state interactions (FSI) via a coupled-channel transport approach, can reproduce the shape and normalization of MiniBooNE and K2K pion production data, but a fully consistent comparison is not yet possible because the experimental "data" have been corrected using model-dependent Monte Carlo generators with specific assumptions on the initial neutrino-nucleon cross section and the nuclear model.

## Method

The authors model neutrino-nucleus scattering as a two-step process within the GiBUU framework. In the initial state, neutrinos interact with nucleons bound in a local Fermi gas with a mean-field potential, including 12 N* and Delta resonances with invariant masses below 2 GeV and non-resonant background contributions, with axial couplings obtained from PCAC and fits to neutrino-nucleon data. In the final state, the outgoing particles are propagated through the nucleus using a semi-classical coupled-channel BUU transport model that includes elastic and inelastic collisions, resonance decays, pion absorption via the Delta resonance, charge exchange, and medium-modified spectral functions with collisional broadening.

## Key numbers

- MiniBooNE neutrino flux peaks at about 0.7 GeV neutrino energy; K2K flux peaks at about 1.2 GeV neutrino energy.
- For NC single-pi0 production on 12C averaged over the MiniBooNE flux, the pion absorption via the Delta resonance causes a reduction in the region around T_pi = 0.1-0.3 GeV.
- For the single-pi+/QE ratio on 12C, the full calculation after FSI (curve 1) agrees perfectly with MiniBooNE data for energies up to 1 GeV and remains within error bars above 1 GeV.
- The K2K data for the single-pi+/QE ratio show good agreement with the GiBUU calculation, with a slight underestimate at higher energies.
- Next-generation experiments such as T2K require the CC pi+ cross section to be known to 5% and the NC pi0 cross section to 10% for the resulting error on oscillation parameters to be comparable to statistical uncertainties.

## Context

This work extends the GiBUU model, previously tested against electron and photon scattering data (Krusche 2004, Leitner 2008), to neutrino-induced pion production on carbon and oxygen at MiniBooNE and K2K energies. It contrasts with the Rein-Sehgal model used in Monte Carlo event generators (Andreopoulos 2007) and with earlier approaches that either neglected FSI (Praet 2008) or used simplified treatments of pion absorption and charge exchange without proper pi-N-Delta dynamics (Paschos 2000, Ahmad 2006). The results enable future comparisons with acceptance-corrected cross sections from MiniBooNE and K2K, which are needed to test theoretical models without the model dependence introduced by current data extraction procedures.
