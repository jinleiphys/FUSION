# Astrophysical constraints on nuclear EOSs and coupling constants in RMF models

> Utilizing various astrophysical constraints on neutron star structures, we carry out a Bayesian analysis on the density-dependent behaviors of coupling constants in RMF models as well as the nuclear matter properties at supranuclear densities. The effective nucleon interactions in the isoscalar-scalar, isoscalar-vector, and isovector-vector channels are considered, where the corresponding coupling constants ($Оұ_S, Оұ_V, Оұ_{TV}$) are fixed by dividing entire density range into three regions with six independent parameters. In this work we focus on constraining the density-dependent point-coupling constants at supranuclear densities, while the coupling constants at subsaturation densities are derived from the covariant density functional DD-ME2. For those consistent with astrophysical observations, the coupling constants generally decrease with density and approach to small positive values at large enough densities, which qualitatively agrees with various RMF models. The posterior probability density functions and their correlations of the coupling constants and various nuclear matter properties are examined as well. At $1Пғ$ level, the constrained coupling constants at density $1.5n_0$ ($2.5n_0$) are $Оұ_S = 3.1^{+0.1}_{-0.05} (1.55^{+0.85}_{-0.2}) \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{MeV}^{-2}$, $Оұ_V = 2.3^{+0.1}_{-0.0} (1.3^{+0.55}_{-0.1}) \times 10^{-4} \mathrm{MeV}^{-2}$, and $Оұ_{TV} = 2.05^{+0}_{-0.4} (2.05^{+0}_{-0.5})\times 10^{-5} \mathrm{MeV}^{-2}$. At larger densities, we find the lower limit of $Оұ_{TV}$ is not well constrained, so that more extensive calculations with larger number of free parameters are necessary.

## Key claim
The density-dependent coupling constants in relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, when constrained by astrophysical neutron star observations, generally decrease with density and approach small positive values at large densities, qualitatively agreeing with various RMF models; however, the lower limit of the isovector-vector coupling constant Оұ_TV is not well constrained at larger densities, indicating that more extensive calculations with a larger number of free parameters are necessary.

## Method
The authors performed a Bayesian analysis using Markov-Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm to constrain six independent parameters governing the density-dependent point-coupling constants (Оұ_S, Оұ_V, Оұ_TV) in the isoscalar-scalar, isoscalar-vector, and isovector-vector channels at supranuclear densities. The density range was divided into three regions (0.1 fmвҒ»Ві to nвӮҖ, nвӮҖ to 2nвӮҖ, and above 2nвӮҖ), with coupling constants at subsaturation densities fixed by the covariant density functional DD-ME2 (Lalazissis 2005). The resulting equations of state were used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations, and the likelihood was constructed from neutron star radius data from GW170817, quiescent low-mass X-ray binaries, NICER observations of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, and HESS J1731-347, along with a maximum mass filter of 1.97 MвҠҷ and causality/thermodynamic stability conditions.

## Context
This work extends Bayesian analyses of neutron star equations of state (e.g., Xie 2019, Xie 2020) by specifically constraining the density-dependent behavior of point-coupling constants in RMF models, rather than only the EOS itself. The results are consistent with the covariant density functional DD-ME2 (Lalazissis 2005) at subsaturation densities and qualitatively agree with other RMF models. The finding that neutron stars can reach 2.5 MвҠҷ while meeting current observations is relevant for interpreting the secondary objects in GW190814 and GW200210 (LVC 2020, Zhu 2022) and the hypothesized lower mass gap 2.5-5 MвҠҷ (Yang 2020). The authors note that the central compact object in HESS J1731-347 (Doroshenko 2022) shows no overlap with their predicted mass-radius distributions, suggesting it might be a strange star or other exotic object.
