# Hyperons and quarks in proto-neutron stars

> In this work, we study matter in the cores of proto-neutron stars, focusing on the impact of their composition on the stellar structure. We begin by examining the effects of finite temperature (through a fixed entropy per baryon) and lepton fraction on purely nucleonic matter by making use of the DSH model . We then turn our attention to a relativistic mean-field model containing exotic degrees of freedom, the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, again, under the conditions of finite temperature and trapped neutrinos. In the latter, since both hyperons and quarks are found in the cores of large-mass stars, their interplay and the possibility of mixtures of phases is taken into account and analyzed. Finally, we discuss how stellar rotation can affect our results.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in proto-neutron stars, the interplay between hyperons and quarks depends sensitively on whether lepton fraction is conserved globally or locally, and that stellar rotation can determine whether a mixed phase of hadrons and quarks develops; however, the authors note that their treatment of phase coexistence with fixed entropy per baryon produces a small temperature discontinuity (of order 1 MeV) that technically violates thermal equilibrium, though they argue this does not affect practical results.

## Method

The authors use two models: the DSH phenomenological model for purely nucleonic matter (with 10 randomly selected parameterizations) and the Chiral Mean Field (CMF) model, a relativistic mean-field model based on chiral SU(3) symmetry that includes nucleons, hyperons, and quarks with a deconfinement order parameter. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for both cold neutron stars (beta-equilibrated, zero temperature) and proto-neutron stars (fixed lepton fraction Y_l = 0.4, fixed entropy per baryon S_B = 1 or 2), considering different treatments of charge neutrality and lepton fraction conservation (local vs. global) during phase transitions. They also compute rotating stellar configurations using the Glendenning (1993) formalism for the Kepler frequency.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass for NS with only hadrons (CMF): 2.07 M_sun, radius 11.94 km, central mu_B = 1599.0 MeV
- Maximum mass for NS with locally conserved charge (CMF): 1.97 M_sun, radius 12.95 km, central mu_B = 1345.0 MeV
- Maximum mass for NS with globally conserved charge (CMF): 1.93 M_sun, radius 13.07 km, central mu_B = 1319.0 MeV
- Maximum mass for PNS with only hadrons (CMF): 2.06 M_sun, radius 16.38 km, central mu_B = 1460.8 MeV
- Maximum mass for PNS with locally conserved charge and lepton fraction (CMF): 2.04 M_sun, radius 17.69 km, central tilde-mu = 1452.0 MeV
- Maximum mass for PNS with globally conserved lepton fraction (CMF): 2.03 M_sun, radius 18.04 km, central tilde-mu = 1326.0 MeV
- Minimum central mu_B for Lambda hyperons in NS: 1229.5 MeV; in PNS: 955.7 MeV
- Minimum central mu_B for quarks in NS LCN: 1345.5 MeV; in PNS LCN: 1090.5 MeV
- For rotating PNS with fixed baryon number, mixed phase appears only when rotational frequency drops below 89 Hz
- For rotating NS, Lambda hyperons appear below 903 Hz, mixed phase with quarks appears below 295 Hz
- Saturation density n_s = 0.16 +/- 0.01 fm^{-3}

## Context

This work extends prior studies of proto-neutron star matter with hyperons (Prakash 1997, Dexheimer 2008) and deconfinement phase transitions (Steiner 2000, Hempel 2013) by presenting the first results for mixtures of phases inside stars with global lepton conservation and fixed entropy per baryon in the CMF model. The DSH model is presented for the first time at fixed lepton fraction. The results enable future consistent simulations of neutron star evolution from core-collapse through the proto-neutron star stage to cold neutron stars using the same equation of state, which the authors identify as their goal. The paper also notes that a PNS with a mixed phase cannot have a cold neutron star counterpart at the same baryon number and would collapse to a black hole.
