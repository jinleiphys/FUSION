# Impact of low-energy nuclear excitations on neutrino-nucleus scattering at MiniBooNE and T2K kinematics

> [Background] Meticulous modeling of neutrino-nucleus interactions is essential to achieve the unprecedented precision goals of present and future accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments. [Purpose] Confront our calculations of charged-current quasielastic cross section with the measurements of MiniBooNE and T2K, and to quantitatively investigate the role of nuclear-structure effects, in particular, low-energy nuclear excitations in forward muon scattering. [Method] The model takes the mean-field (MF) approach as the starting point, and solves Hartree-Fock (HF) equations using a Skyrme (SkE2) nucleon-nucleon interaction. Long-range nuclear correlations are taken into account by means of the continuum random-phase approximation (CRPA) framework. [Results] We present our calculations on flux-folded double differential, and flux-unfolded total cross sections off $^{12}$C and compare them with MiniBooNE and (off-axis) T2K measurements. We discuss the importance of low-energy nuclear excitations for the forward bins. [Conclusions] The CRPA predictions describe the gross features of the measured cross sections. They underpredict the data (more in the neutrino than in the antineutrino case) because of the absence of processes beyond pure quasielastic scattering in our model. At very forward muon scattering, low-energy nuclear excitations ($ω< $ 50 MeV) account for nearly 50% of the flux-folded cross section.

## Key claim
The continuum random-phase approximation (CRPA) model, which includes long-range nuclear correlations, predicts that low-energy nuclear excitations (excitation energy ω < 50 MeV) account for nearly 50% of the flux-folded charged-current quasielastic cross section at very forward muon scattering angles, a feature absent in Fermi-gas based approaches. However, the model systematically underpredicts the measured cross sections because it does not include multi-nucleon knockout or single-pion production processes.

## Method
The authors solved Hartree-Fock (HF) equations using a Skyrme SkE2 nucleon-nucleon interaction to obtain mean-field single-nucleon wave functions for 12C. Long-range nuclear correlations were then incorporated via a continuum random-phase approximation (CRPA) based on a Green's function formalism, iterating the particle-hole polarization propagator with the same SkE2 interaction. Flux-folded double-differential and flux-unfolded total charged-current quasielastic cross sections were computed for both neutrinos and antineutrinos and compared with MiniBooNE and T2K (off-axis ND280) measurements.

## Key numbers
- Low-energy nuclear excitations (ω < 50 MeV) account for nearly 50% of the flux-folded cross section in the most forward muon scattering bin (0.94 < cosθμ < 1.0) for T2K kinematics.
- The CRPA cross sections underpredict MiniBooNE neutrino data more than antineutrino data; the antineutrino data are reproduced better by the model.
- The axial mass used in all calculations was MA = 1.03 GeV.
- Both MiniBooNE and T2K neutrino beams have average energies around 800 MeV; the T2K beam is more sharply peaked with fewer contributions beyond 1 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the CRPA formalism previously applied to electron scattering and supernova-energy neutrino scattering (Jachowicz et al., 2002; Pandey et al., 2014) to the kinematics of accelerator-based oscillation experiments. The results contrast with relativistic Fermi-gas based models used in Monte-Carlo generators, which cannot describe the low-energy collective excitations. The paper confirms the necessity of including multi-nucleon effects (meson-exchange currents, Δ-isobar currents) as suggested by Martini et al. (2009, 2010) and Nieves et al. (2011) to fully describe MiniBooNE data, while highlighting that forward-angle scattering requires careful treatment of nuclear structure beyond the Fermi-gas approach.
