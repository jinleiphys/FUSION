# The island of deformation and shape co-existence in neutron-deficient nuclei of the Pb region using relativistic mean field model

> We have investigated the ground-state structures of even-even neutron-deficient isotopes of Hg and Pb nuclei within the framework of a deformed relativistic mean field formalism for a number of commonly used parameter sets, namely NL1, NL3, NL-SH, NL-RA1 and TM1. The ground states of a bunch of Hg and Pb-isotopes towards the proton-dripline are found to be deformed for all the forces, with a constant pairing gap. The small differences in the ground- and the first-excited state binding energies predict a sea of low-lying excited states, and hence of shape co-existence, for both the Hg and Pb nuclei. In general, a discrepancy between the experimentally observed and theoretically predicted (sign of) quadrupole deformations is noticed in this mass region. The constrained potential energy surfaces and the single-particle energy spectra analyzed for some selected nuclei show that the known large shell gap for Pb nuclei at Z=82 is almost extinguished for its proton-rich isotopes.

## Key claim

The ground states of many neutron-deficient Hg and Pb isotopes near the proton dripline are deformed for all five relativistic mean field parameter sets tested (NL1, NL3, NL-SH, NL-RA1, and TM1), with a constant pairing gap; however, the sign of the predicted quadrupole deformation often disagrees with experimental data, and the results depend strongly on the choice of parameter set and pairing treatment.

## Method

The authors solved the deformed relativistic mean field (RMF) equations by expanding the Dirac spinors and meson fields in a deformed harmonic oscillator basis and iterating to self-consistency. Pairing correlations were added in the BCS formalism using constant pairing gap parameters taken from the Madland-Nix prescription (Moeller 1988). Constrained potential energy surfaces were calculated for selected nuclei by minimizing ⟨H − λQ⟩ to obtain binding energy as a function of quadrupole deformation β₂.

## Key numbers

- For ¹⁸⁰Hg with the NL1 parameter set and pairing, the superdeformed solution has binding energy BE = 1420.420 MeV (β₂ = 0.569) and the normal deformation solution has BE = 1422.405 MeV (β₂ = 0.323); without pairing, the normal solution has BE = 1420.304 MeV (β₂ = 0.328) and the superdeformed solution has BE = 1420.420 MeV (β₂ = 0.569).
- For ¹⁸⁴Hg, the maximum prolate quadrupole deformation is β₂ = 0.30–0.33 across the five parameter sets.
- For ¹⁹²Pb and ¹⁹⁴Pb with the NL3 set and no pairing, the predicted quadrupole deformations are β₂ = −0.172 and −0.166, respectively, in excellent agreement with experimental values of β₂ = −0.18, −0.175, and −0.17 (Dendooven 1989).
- The energy difference ΔE between ground and first excited states is within ∼1.5 MeV for most Hg and Pb isotopes without pairing, and within ∼2.7 MeV with pairing for the exceptions (¹⁸²–¹⁸⁸Hg and ¹⁹⁴–¹⁹⁸Pb).
- For ¹⁸⁰Hg and ¹⁸⁶Pb, the constrained potential energy surfaces show the two solutions have about the same energy difference of ∼3 MeV for all parameter sets.

## Context

This work extends earlier RMF calculations by Patra (1994) and Yoshida (1994) that used only the NL1 parameter set and found prolate ground states for neutron-deficient Hg and superdeformed ground states in ¹⁸⁰Hg, which had been criticized by Heyde (1996) as possibly force-dependent. The present study tests five parameter sets systematically and finds that shape coexistence in Hg and Pb is nearly independent of force parameters and pairing correlations, but the predicted sign of quadrupole deformation often disagrees with experimental data from Dracoulis (1988) and with the Woods-Saxon calculations of Bengtsson (1987). The results enable further work on the weakening of the Z=82 shell gap for neutron-deficient Pb isotopes, consistent with the mass measurements of Novikov (2002) and the triple shape coexistence observed in ¹⁸⁶Pb by Andreyev (2000).
