# Thermal effects on quark-gluon mixed condensate g<\bar{q} σG q> from lattice QCD

> We present the first study of the thermal effects on the quark-gluon mixed condensate g<\bar{q} σG q>, which is another chiral order parameter, in SU(3)c lattice QCD with the Kogut-Susskind fermion at the quenched level. Using the lattices at β=6.0, 6.1, 6.2 in high statistics, we calculate the mixed condensate as well as the quark condensate in the chiral limit for the temperature 0MeV <= T <= 500MeV. Except for the sharp decrease of both the condensates around T_c \simeq 280MeV, the thermal effects are found to be weak below T_c. We also find that the ratio m_0^2 = g<\bar{q} σG q>/ <\bar{q}q> is almost independent of the temperature even in the very vicinity of T_c. This result indicates nontrivial similarity in the chiral behaviors of the two different condensates.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first lattice QCD study of thermal effects on the quark-gluon mixed condensate \(g\langle\bar{q}\sigma_{\mu\nu}G_{\mu\nu}q\rangle\) and finds that, while both the mixed condensate and the ordinary quark condensate drop sharply near the critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 280\ \mathrm{MeV}\), their ratio \(m_0^2 = g\langle\bar{q}\sigma G q\rangle / \langle\bar{q}q\rangle\) remains almost independent of temperature even very close to \(T_c\). This result is presented as a nontrivial similarity in the chiral behavior of the two condensates, but the study is performed in quenched QCD (no dynamical quarks) and the authors note that a full QCD calculation is needed for deeper insight.

## Method

The authors performed SU(3)\(_c\) lattice QCD simulations with the Kogut-Susskind (KS) fermion at the quenched level, using the standard Wilson gauge action at \(\beta = 6.0, 6.1, 6.2\). They calculated the quark-gluon mixed condensate and the quark condensate on lattices with varying temporal extent \(N_t\) to cover temperatures from \(0\ \mathrm{MeV}\) up to about \(500\ \mathrm{MeV}\), and extracted the condensates in the chiral limit by linear extrapolation from three non-zero quark masses.

## Key numbers

* The critical temperature for the confinement/deconfinement phase transition is \(T_c \simeq 280\ \mathrm{MeV}\), obtained from the Polyakov-loop susceptibility with \(T_c / \sqrt{\sigma} \simeq 0.64\).
* The lattice spacings are \(a^{-1} = 1.9,\ 2.3,\ 2.7\ \mathrm{GeV}\) for \(\beta = 6.0,\ 6.1,\ 6.2\) respectively, using the string tension \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 427\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The statistical error on the condensates is typically 5-7% level for every temperature.
* The ratio \(m_0^2(T) / m_0^2(T=0)\) is found to be almost independent of temperature up to \(T_c\), with the electric and magnetic components of the mixed condensate showing identical temperature dependence.

## Context

This work extends earlier lattice calculations of the mixed condensate at zero temperature by the same group (Doi et al., 2003) and by another group using Domain-Wall fermions, and provides the first finite-temperature results for this quantity. The finding that the ratio \(m_0^2\) is temperature-independent suggests a universal chiral behavior among different chiral order parameters near \(T_c\), which the authors interpret via a "Swiss cheese picture" of coexisting phases or via the spectral density of the Dirac operator. The results are expected to provide input for QCD sum rule studies of hadron properties at finite temperature, relevant for heavy-ion collision experiments.
