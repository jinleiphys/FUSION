# A differential equation approach for examining the subtraction schemes

> We propose a natural differential equation with respect to mass(es) to analyze the scheme dependence problem. It is shown that the vertex functions subtracted at an arbitrary Euclidean momentum (MOM) do not satisfy such differential equations, as extra unphysical mass dependence is introduced which is shown to lead to the violation of the canonical form of the Slavnov-Taylor identities, a notorious fact with MOM schemes. By the way, the traditional advantage of MOM schemes in decoupling issue is shown to be lost in the context of Callan-Symanzik equations.

# A differential equation approach for examining the subtraction schemes

## Key claim

The paper proposes that renormalization schemes can be tested by whether vertex functions satisfy natural differential equations with respect to mass, and shows that MOM (momentum subtraction) schemes introduce extra unphysical mass dependence that violates the canonical form of Slavnov-Taylor identities. The author does not claim that MOM schemes cannot consistently renormalize quantum field theories with masses, but leaves the implications open.

## Method

The author formulates Feynman amplitudes as solutions to differential equations parametrized by masses, using the fact that differentiation with respect to mass lowers the divergence degree of superficially divergent diagrams. The analysis is illustrated with the one-loop photon vacuum polarization tensor in QED, comparing the behavior of mass-independent schemes (MIS) against MOM schemes. The author then examines the decoupling of heavy particles in the context of Callan-Symanzik equations for both scheme types.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop vacuum polarization in QED yields $\Pi(p^2,m;C) = \frac{e^2}{2\pi^2} \int_0^1 dx \, x(1-x) \ln \frac{m^2 - x(1-x)p^2}{C}$, where $C$ is an integration constant fixed by the subtraction scheme.
- In MOM schemes, the subtraction condition $\Pi(p^2,m;C_{Mom})|_{p^2=-\mu^2}=0$ forces $C_{Mom} = m^2 + x(1-x)\mu^2$, introducing an extra term $\delta(\mu^2,m) = -\frac{e^2}{\pi^2}\int_0^1 dx \frac{x(1-x)m}{m^2 + x(1-x)\mu^2}$ in the mass derivative equation.
- At lowest order, the beta function in both mass-independent and MOM schemes gives $\beta = \frac{2\alpha}{3\pi}$ for QED with one massive fermion.
- The heavy particle limit yields $\Delta\beta = -\frac{2\alpha}{3\pi}$ for the cancellation of heavy fermion contributions in both scheme types.

## Context

This work extends the differential form of Ward identities to a general differential equation analysis of renormalization schemes, building on the observation by Witten (1976) and Caswell and Kennedy (1980) that mass differentiation lowers divergence degree. The paper contradicts the conventional belief that all subtraction schemes are equivalent, and specifically addresses the known problems with on-shell schemes for unstable particle masses documented by Willenbrock and Valencia (1991), Sirlin (1991), and others. The analysis enables a reexamination of scheme dependence and shows that the traditional advantage of MOM schemes in decoupling, as argued by Appelquist and Carazzone (1975), is lost in the context of Callan-Symanzik equations, where mass-independent schemes achieve decoupling through the same mechanism.
