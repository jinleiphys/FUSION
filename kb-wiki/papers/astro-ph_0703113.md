# Hypernuclear Physics and Compact Stars

> Hypernuclear physics plays a decisive role for several features of compact star physics. I review the impact of hypernuclear potential depths, two-body hyperon-nucleon and hyperon three-body forces as well as hyperon-hyperon interactions on the maximum mass, the mass-radius relation, and cooling properties of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Hyperons are likely to appear in neutron star cores at around twice nuclear saturation density, but their presence softens the equation of state so severely that most modern many-body calculations produce maximum neutron star masses below the observational lower limit of about 1.6 solar masses for pulsar J0751+1807, implying that repulsive hyperon three-body forces or other missing physics must be invoked to reconcile hypernuclear data with pulsar observations.

## Method

The paper reviews relativistic mean-field (RMF) models, Brueckner–Hartree–Fock calculations, and nonrelativistic potential models that incorporate hyperon-nucleon (YN) and hyperon-hyperon (YY) interactions fitted to hypernuclear data. It compares the resulting equations of state, particle compositions, and cooling rates against observational constraints from pulsar masses, X-ray spectra, and heavy-ion kaon production data.

## Key numbers

- The best-determined pulsar mass is that of the Hulse–Taylor pulsar: M = (1.4411 ± 0.00035) solar masses.
- For pulsar J0751+1807, a mass range of M = 2.1 ± 0.2 solar masses (1σ) and M = 1.6–2.5 solar masses (2σ) has been inferred.
- The Λ hyperon potential depth at nuclear saturation density is U_Λ = −30 MeV.
- The Ξ hyperon potential depth is U_Ξ = −18 MeV (with relativistic corrections).
- In a free gas of hadrons, Σ− hyperons appear at 4n₀ and Λ hyperons at 8n₀; with interactions, hyperons appear around 2n₀.
- In a relativistic mean-field calculation using parameter set GM1, the Λ appears at 2.3n₀, the Ξ− at 2.7n₀, and the Ξ⁰ at 4.7n₀.
- The maximum mass for a neutron star with nucleons and leptons only is about 2.3 solar masses; including hyperons reduces it to about 1.7 solar masses.
- Brueckner–Hartree–Fock calculations with Nijmegen YN potentials give M_max = 1.47 solar masses without YY interactions and M_max = 1.34 solar masses with YY interactions; including three-body forces for nucleons gives M_max = 1.26 solar masses.
- The proton fraction must exceed n_p/n ≥ 1/9 ≈ 11% for the direct nucleon URCA process to start; this critical density is 1.5n₀ in relativistic calculations.
- A conservative lower limit on the radius of RXJ 1856.35-3754 is R_∞ = 16.5 km (d/117 pc).
- The binding energy of strange hadronic matter with Λs and Ξs reaches E/A = −12 MeV (assuming weak YY interaction).

## Context

This review extends the line of work initiated by Glendenning and Moszkowski (1991), who first consistently implemented relativistic hyperon potential depths in neutron star matter and found that hyperons reduce the maximum mass from about 2.3 to about 1.7 solar masses. It contrasts with earlier free-gas estimates by Ambartsumyan and Saakyan (1960) and with modern Brueckner–Hartree–Fock calculations by Baldo (1999) and Vidana (2000) that produce maximum masses below 1.4 solar masses. The paper enables future experimental programs at DAΦNE, Jefferson Lab, KEK, J-PARC, MAMI-C, and FAIR to provide the hypernuclear data needed to resolve the tension between soft equations of state favored by heavy-ion data and stiff equations of state required by pulsar mass measurements.
