# A study of elliptic flows in a quark combination model

> We carry out a detail study of elliptic flows in Au-Au collisions at 200 AGeV in a quark combination model. We find that elliptic flow data for a variety of hadrons can be well reproduced except pions if constituent quarks with equal parallel transverse momenta combine into initially produced hadrons. In a combination mechanism where initial hadrons are formed by quarks with unequal parallel transverse momenta, theoretical predictions agree with data for all available hadrons including pions. The mass hierarchy at low transverse momenta in elliptic flows can be understood in the same quark combination mechanism as in the mediate range of transverse momenta.

# A study of elliptic flows in a quark combination model

## Key claim

The quark combination model can reproduce elliptic flow data for all available hadrons including pions, but only if constituent quarks with unequal parallel transverse momenta are allowed to combine into initially produced hadrons; the equal transverse momentum combination scheme fails for pions even after including resonance decays and quark mass effects.

## Method

The authors extract quark elliptic flow functions $v_{2,q}(p_T)$ by fitting experimental hadron data, then use these to construct elliptic flows for all initially produced hadrons via quark combination rules based on QCD color requirements. Resonance decays are handled using the PYTHIA event generator to obtain final-state hadron elliptic flows. Three combination schemes are tested: equal $p_T$ combination with identical $v_{2,q}$ for all quarks, equal $p_T$ combination with different $v_{2,q}$ for strange versus light quarks, and unequal $p_T$ combination.

## Key numbers

- Quark elliptic flow input for the naive scheme: $v_{2,q}(p_T) = 0.256 p_T^3$ for $0 \leq p_T \leq 0.365$ GeV, and $v_{2,q}(p_T) = 0.078 \tanh[1.45(p_T - 0.25)]$ for $p_T > 0.365$ GeV.
- Quark elliptic flow input for the unequal $p_T$ scheme: $v_{2,q}(p_T) = 0.286 p_T^4$ for $0 \leq p_T \leq 0.525$ GeV, and $v_{2,q}(p_T) = 0.11 \tanh[1.6(p_T - 0.4)]$ for $p_T > 0.525$ GeV.
- Scaling parameters for strange and light quarks in the modified scheme: $\alpha_{u,d} = 0.94$, $\alpha_s = 1.12$, derived from constituent quark masses $m_{u,d} = 0.34$ GeV and $m_s = 0.5$ GeV.
- In central Au-Au collisions at 200 AGeV, the yield of charm hadrons is about 28 in the whole rapidity range, negligible compared to light quarks.

## Context

This work extends coalescence and recombination models (Molnar 2003, Hwa 2002, Fries 2003, Greco 2003) by focusing on the low $p_T$ region where the mass hierarchy of elliptic flows had not been fully reproduced. It contrasts with hydrodynamic models (Huovinen 2001) that explain mass hierarchy via radial flow but fail at mediate and high $p_T$, and with the multi-phase transport model (Lin 2004) that reproduced the mass hierarchy for pions, kaons, and protons. The results enable further study of whether the equal or unequal $p_T$ combination scheme is correct, which could be distinguished by more precise measurements of $\phi$ and $K^*$ meson elliptic flows.
