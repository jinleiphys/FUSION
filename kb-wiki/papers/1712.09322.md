# Effects of composite pions on the chiral condensate within the PNJL model at finite temperature

> We investigate the effect of composite pions on the behaviour of the chiral condensate at finite temperature within the Polyakov-loop improved NJL model. To this end we treat quark-antiquark correlations in the pion channel (bound states and scattering continuum) within a Beth-Uhlenbeck approach that uses medium-dependent phase shifts. A striking medium effect is the Mott transition which occurs when the binding energy vanishes and the discrete pion bound state merges the continuum. This transition is triggered by the lowering of the continuum edge due to the chiral restoration transition. This in turn also entails a modification of the Polyakov-loop so that the SU(3) center symmetry gets broken at finite temperature and dynamical quarks (and gluons) appear in the system, taking over the role of the dominant degrees of freedom from the pions. At low temperatures our model reproduces the chiral perturbation theory result for the chiral condensate while at high temperatures the PNJL model result is recovered. The new aspect of the current work is a consistent treatment of the chiral restoration transition region within the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach on the basis of mesonic phase shifts for the treatment of the correlations.

## Key claim
The paper claims that including composite pions via a Beth-Uhlenbeck approach with medium-dependent phase shifts allows the PNJL model to reproduce the chiral perturbation theory result for the chiral condensate at low temperatures while recovering the standard PNJL result at high temperatures, but this is achieved only with a schematic ansatz for the continuum phase shift that produces artefactual step-like behavior at the Mott temperature.

## Method
The authors decompose the thermodynamic potential into a quark-gluon sector treated in the mean-field approximation to the SU(2) PNJL model and a hadronic part described by a Mott-hadron resonance gas restricted to the pion channel. They introduce a schematic model for the quark-antiquark phase shift in the pion channel as a step-down function of energy representing an "anti-bound state" at the continuum threshold, which obeys the Levinson theorem. The chiral condensate is then computed from the derivative of the total thermodynamic potential with respect to the current quark mass, receiving contributions from both the PNJL mean-field and the pion correlations.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass in vacuum: m = 319 MeV, with current quark mass m0 = 5.5 MeV and cutoff Λ = 639 MeV
- Coupling constant: GΛ² = 2.13
- Vacuum pion properties reproduced: mπ = 140 MeV, fπ = 93 MeV
- Chiral condensate in vacuum: ⟨q̄q⟩ = -(244 MeV)³
- Polyakov-loop effective potential parameter T0 = 0.185 GeV
- Lattice QCD pseudocritical temperature cited: T = 154 ± 9 MeV (Bazavov 2011)
- Pion bound state merges continuum at momentum ~600 MeV (citing Kitazawa 2014)

## Context
This work extends the Beth-Uhlenbeck approach for hadronic correlations in quark matter developed by Hüfner (1994) and Zhuang (1994), and recently reformulated by Wergieluk (2012) and Blaschke (2013). It addresses the known problem that mean-field NJL models give incorrect temperature dependence of the chiral condensate melting compared to chiral perturbation theory, as discussed by Buballa (2003) and Oertel (2000). The approach enables a unified description of quark-gluon-hadron thermodynamics that could eventually be compared to lattice QCD results, particularly for the pseudocritical temperature of the chiral transition which PNJL mean-field models overestimate.
