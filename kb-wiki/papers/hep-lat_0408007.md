# Vector meson at non-zero baryon density and zero sound

> We present simulation results of the (2+1)d four-fermion model with a baryon chemical potential. We examine temporal correlation functions of the vector meson, and find evidence of phonon-like behavior characterised by a linear dispersion relation in the long wavelength limit. We also discuss the consistency of our numerical results with analytical solutions to the Boltzmann equation corresponding to zero sound. We argue that our results provide the first evidence for a collective excitation in a lattice simulation.

# Vector meson at non-zero baryon density and zero sound

## Key claim
The authors present lattice simulation evidence for a phonon-like collective excitation (zero sound) in the (2+1)d Gross-Neveu model at non-zero baryon chemical potential, but the measured speed ratio s = β₀/β_F^bare is less than one (0.896(8) to 0.959(2)), whereas analytical Fermi liquid theory predicts s > 1; the authors argue this discrepancy likely arises from finite temperature and finite volume effects suppressing Landau damping on the lattice.

## Method
The authors simulate the (2+1)d Gross-Neveu model with N_f=4 flavors of staggered fermions on L_s²×48 lattices with L_s=32 and 48, at chemical potentials μ ranging from 0.2 to 0.6 (in lattice units). They measure temporal correlation functions of the conserved vector current (point-split meson operators) for momenta k=(k,0) and extract dispersion relations ω(k) by fitting the correlator C_∥(π-k,t) to an exponential form with alternating and non-alternating components. The results are compared to analytical solutions of the Boltzmann equation from Fermi liquid theory at leading order in 1/N_f.

## Key numbers
- Critical chemical potential: μ_c ≃ 0.16 a⁻¹ for the chiral transition
- Fermion mass at μ=0: m_f ≃ 0.17 a⁻¹
- Analytical Fermi velocity (leading 1/N_f): β_F = 1 - 1/(16N_f) ≃ 0.984
- Analytical Fermi momentum ratio: k_F/(μβ_F) = 1 - 1/(8N_f) ≃ 0.969
- Speed ratio s = β₀/β_F^bare: s = 0.896(8) at μ=0.2, rising to s = 0.959(2) at μ=0.6
- Lattice sizes: L_s = 32 and 48, with L_t = 48 time slices
- Momentum range measured: k = 0, 2π/L_s, …, π/2

## Context
This work extends prior analytical studies of the (2+1)d Gross-Neveu model at finite baryon density by Hands and Strouthos (2003), who solved the self-consistent equation for zero sound in Fermi liquid theory to leading order in 1/N_f. The numerical results are compared to those analytical predictions and to the known behavior of zero sound in liquid ³He, where the sound speed increases as T→0. The authors argue this is the first evidence for a collective excitation (zero sound) identified in a lattice simulation, and the work enables future studies on larger volumes and finer lattices to disentangle finite-size, finite-temperature, and discretization effects.
