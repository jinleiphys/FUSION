# Impact of particle production mechanisms on pseudorapidity distribution and directed flow in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 19.6 GeV using AMPT model

> The STAR experiment at the top RHIC energy has observed that the directed flow ($v_1$) of inclusive light hadrons is independent of the collision system size at a given centrality~\cite{STAR:2008jgm}. However, recent STAR measurements indicate a system-size dependence in the $v_1(y)$-slope ($dv_{1}/dy$) of protons, antiprotons, and their differences ($Δdv_{1}/dy$) at a given centrality, suggesting a potential influence of baryon production and transport mechanisms~\cite{Taseer:SQM2024talk}. We have studied pseudorapidity ($dN/dy$) distributions and directed flow ($v_1$ and $dv_{1}/dy$) for pions, kaons, and protons in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 19.6$ GeV using the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model. Specifically, we investigated the influence of string junction parameters in AMPT via the PYTHIA/JETSET routines, focusing on the popcorn mechanism and string-splitting parameters, on $dN/dy$, $dv_{1}/dy$, and their charge-dependent splittings ($ΔdN/dy$ and $Δdv_{1}/dy$). We observe that string junction parameters can affect $dN/dy$, $dv_{1}/dy$, $ΔdN/dy$, and $Δdv_{1}/dy$ for $π$, K, and p, and influence their system-size dependence. The effect is most prominent on the $v_1$ of protons, non-trivial for kaons, while the pions $v_1$ remain largely unchanged. These findings provide insights into the interplay between particle production mechanisms, baryon transport, and directed flow in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The AMPT model shows that tuning string junction parameters (popcorn mechanism and Lund string-splitting parameter a) can affect the pseudorapidity distributions and directed flow of pions, kaons, and protons in Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at 19.6 GeV, with the effect most prominent on protons, non-trivial for kaons, while pions remain largely unchanged; however, the model underpredicts mid-rapidity yields for mid-central and central collisions compared to STAR data.

## Method

The authors used the default A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model with HIJING initial conditions, Zhang's Parton Cascade (ZPC) for parton scattering, Lund string fragmentation via PYTHIA/JETSET routines, and a hadron cascade (ART) for final-state interactions. They varied the popcorn mechanism parameters (MSTJ(12) and PARJ(5)) and the Lund fragmentation parameter a (PARJ(41)) across four parameter sets (Table I), simulating approximately 1 million events per configuration for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV over impact parameters 0.0-15.0 fm. The v1(y) was fitted with a linear function within -1.0 < y < 1.0 to extract the slope dv1/dy.

## Key numbers

- Pseudorapidity distributions (dN/dy) and directed flow slopes (dv1/dy) were studied at sqrt(s_NN) = 19.6 GeV for Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions
- The Lund fragmentation parameter a was varied from 0.55 (default) to 2.2, with b fixed at 0.15 GeV^-2
- The popcorn mechanism parameter PARJ(5) was set to 0 (no popcorn), 0.5, or 1.0 across different parameter sets
- For ultra-central collisions (b < 1 fm/c), pions and kaons show dv1/dy nearly zero, while protons and antiprotons exhibit a considerable non-zero magnitude
- In 10-40% centrality, Delta dv1/dy for protons is larger in Au+Au collisions compared to Cu+Cu collisions
- For kaons in central collisions, the magnitude of Delta dv1/dy in Cu+Cu is larger than in Au+Au, opposite to the proton ordering
- At small average number of participants (<N_part>), dv1/dy and Delta dv1/dy approximately scale with <N_part>, but this scaling breaks down at large <N_part>

## Context

This work extends recent STAR measurements (STAR 2023, Taseer SQM2024 talk) that showed a system-size dependence in the v1(y)-slope of protons, antiprotons, and their differences at a given centrality, contrasting earlier RHIC results (STAR 2008) where inclusive charged particle v1 appeared system-size independent. The study uses the AMPT model to explore whether particle production mechanisms (popcorn mechanism and string fragmentation) can explain these system-size dependencies, complementing hydrodynamic approaches (Parida 2023, 2025) that qualitatively reproduce baryon Delta v1 trends but fail to simultaneously describe all particle species. The findings enable future extensions to higher and lower beam energies, and highlight the need for advanced models combining transport mechanisms, mean-field effects, and electromagnetic field interactions to fully describe experimental observations.
