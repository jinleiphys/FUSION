# Medium Modification of γ-jets in High-energy Heavy-ion Collisions

> Medium modification of γ-tagged jets in high-energy heavy-ion collisions is investigated within a Linearized Boltzmann Transport model for jet propagation that includes both elastic parton scattering and induced gluon emission. Inclusion of recoiled medium partons in the reconstruction of partonic jets is found to significantly reduce the net jet energy loss. Experimental data on γ-jet asymmetry and survival rate in Pb + Pb collisions at \sqrt{s}=2.76 TeV can be reproduced. Medium modifications of reconstructed jet fragmentation function, transverse profile and energy flow outside the jet-cone are found to be sizable especially for γ-tagged jets with small values of x=p_T^{jet}/p_T^γ.

## Key claim

The paper explains two puzzling features of jet quenching in LHC Pb+Pb collisions, large dijet asymmetry without azimuthal angle broadening, and enhancement of the reconstructed jet fragmentation function at both small and large momentum fractions, by showing that rapid 3D expansion of the medium causes jet energy loss to grow approximately linearly with path length while transverse momentum broadening grows only logarithmically, and that leading partons dominate the reconstructed jet energy because softer partons are transported outside the jet cone.

## Method

The authors use a Linearized Boltzmann Transport (LBT) model that simulates jet shower partons and recoiled medium partons via elastic scattering and induced gluon emission, with the emission rate given by the high-twist approach. Initial γ-jet configurations are generated from HIJING for p+p collisions at √s=2.76 TeV with pTγ>60 GeV, and partons are transported through a (3+1)D ideal hydrodynamic medium. Jets are reconstructed using the anti-kt algorithm with cone size R=0.3, including negative partons to account for backreaction from the medium.

## Key numbers

- γ-tagged jets lose about 15% of their initial energy in central (0%–10%) Pb+Pb collisions at √s=2.76 TeV with cone size R=0.3
- The strong coupling constant αs=0.2 reproduces CMS γ-jet asymmetry data; αs=0.15–0.23 for CMS cuts and αs=0.2–0.27 for ATLAS cuts reproduce the averaged asymmetry and survival rate
- Jet energy loss saturates approximately 10 fm/c after an initial linear rise in the expanding medium
- The γ-jet asymmetry distribution dN/dx with x=pTjet/pTγ is calculated for pTγ>60 GeV, pTjet>30 GeV, |ηγ|<1.44, |ηjet|<1.6, Δφ>7π/8 (CMS cuts) and 60<pTγ<90 GeV, pTjet>25 GeV, |ηγ|<1.3, |ηjet|<2.1, Δφ>7π/8 (ATLAS cuts)

## Context

This work extends the LBT model of Li (2010) to γ-tagged jets and addresses puzzles in CMS (2012) and ATLAS (2012) data on jet modification that earlier parton energy loss models by Dai (2012) and Qin (2012) could not fully explain. The results enable extraction of the jet transport parameter q̂ from the proposed γ-tagged jet fragmentation function, and show that recoiled medium partons must be included for precision studies of jet modification.
