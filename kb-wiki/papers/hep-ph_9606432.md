# Redundance of $Δ$-isobar Parameters in Effective Field Theories

> It is shown that the off-shell parameters in the interaction Lagrangian of pions, nucleons, and $Δ$-isobars are redundant in the framework of effective field theories. Our results also suggest the necessity of including the $Δ$ as an explicit dynamical degree of freedom.

## Key claim

The off-shell parameters \(A\), \(Z\), \(Z_2\), and \(Z_3\) in the interaction Lagrangian of pions, nucleons, and \(\Delta\)-isobars are redundant in effective field theories because they can be absorbed into other parameters in the Lagrangian; however, the \(\Delta\) must be retained as an explicit dynamical degree of freedom because expanding the resulting nonlocal Lagrangian in derivatives violates naive dimensional analysis.

## Method

The authors start from a chiral Lagrangian for pions, nucleons, and \(\Delta\)-isobars that includes the off-shell parameters \(A\) (from the free \(\Delta\) Lagrangian) and \(Z\), \(Z_2\), \(Z_3\) (from the \(\pi N\Delta\) and \(\pi\Delta\Delta\) interactions). They perform a field redefinition to remove the \(A\) parameter, then functionally integrate out the \(\Delta\) field to obtain an equivalent \(\pi N\) Lagrangian. By expanding the resulting nonlocal term up to two derivatives on the pion field and using the nucleon equation of motion, they show that all \(Z\)-dependent terms can be absorbed into the coefficients of the existing \(\pi N\) Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

* The \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling is denoted \(h_{\rm A}\) and the \(\pi\Delta\Delta\) coupling \(\tilde{h}_{\rm A}\); no numerical values are given.
* The pion decay constant is \(f_\pi \approx 93\ \text{MeV}\) and the axial coupling is \(g_{\rm A} \approx 1.26\).
* The constant \(C\) that appears in the absorption of \(Z\)-dependent terms is \(C = \frac{2}{9} \frac{h_{\rm A}^2}{M_\Delta^2} \left[ (Z+1/2)^2 (2M_\Delta + M) - (Z+1/2) M_\Delta \right]\), where \(M\) is the nucleon mass and \(M_\Delta\) the \(\Delta\) mass.
* The authors note that expanding the nonlocal term in derivatives gives a suppression factor of \(1/(M_\Delta - M)\) rather than \(1/M\), which violates naive dimensional analysis.

## Context

This work extends the modern effective field theory (EFT) framework, specifically chiral perturbation theory (ChPT), to the treatment of \(\Delta\)-isobar off-shell parameters. It contradicts the claim of Nath et al. (1971) that the off-shell parameter \(Z\) has physical consequences, and it resolves the impasse raised by Banerjee and Milana (1995) regarding the need for off-shell parameters in heavy-baryon ChPT. The results enable systematic inclusion of \(1/M\) corrections involving the \(\Delta\) in heavy-baryon ChPT and in calculations for nuclear matter and finite nuclei.
