# High-precision Measurements of piP Elastic Differential Cross Sections in the Second Resonance Region

> Cross sections for pi+-p elastic scattering have been measured to high precision, for beam momenta between 800 and 1240 MeV/c, by the EPECUR Collaboration, using the ITEP proton synchrotron. The data precision allows comparisons of the existing partial-wave analyses (PWA) on a level not possible previously. These comparisons imply that updated PWA are required.

## Key claim
The EPECUR Collaboration measured π±p elastic differential cross sections with sufficient precision to distinguish between existing partial-wave analyses (PWA), but the data do not definitively confirm a narrow pentaquark-like structure; instead, they show that updated PWA fits are required, and that some older fits (KH80) perform better than others (KA84) when compared to the new data.

## Method
The experiment used a two-arm non-magnetic spectrometer at the ITEP proton synchrotron, with beam momenta from 800 to 1240 MeV/c. Beam particles were tagged by proportional chambers with 0.1% momentum precision, and scattered particles were detected by drift chambers with hexagonal structure. Elastic events were selected based on angular correlation of pion and proton tracks, requiring a common vertex inside the liquid hydrogen target and coplanarity; inelastic background was subtracted bin-by-bin, and differential cross sections were corrected for acceptance and chamber efficiency.

## Key numbers
- 2638 π⁺p and 4277 π⁻p data points were collected for beam momenta between 800 and 1240 MeV/c, covering center-of-mass energies from 1560 to 1800 MeV.
- Total systematic uncertainty was estimated as 2.6% (sum in quadrature of contributions: beam pollution 1%, luminosity normalization 2%, tracking efficiency and geometry 1%, Monte-Carlo acceptance 0.8%, analysis cuts 0.5%).
- Beam momentum was calibrated with 0.1% precision at three values: 1057, 1095, and 1297 MeV/c.
- The experiment accumulated 1.25×10⁹ triggers for π⁻p and 0.69×10⁹ triggers for π⁺p, yielding 2.24×10⁷ and 1.48×10⁷ elastic events respectively.
- Beam contamination: electrons/positrons fell from about 3% at 840 MeV/c to 1.5% at 1240 MeV/c; muons fell from 6% to 4% over the same range; residual proton contamination was less than 0.2%.

## Context
This work extends the search for narrow structures in πp scattering, motivated by the predicted pentaquark anti-decuplet near 1.7 GeV, and provides data that discriminate between the classic Karlsruhe-Helsinki (KH80 and KA84) and Carnegie-Mellon-Berkeley (CMB) partial-wave analyses, as well as the more recent GW INS DAC fit (WI08). The new data enable future multi-channel fits with analytically built-in thresholds for opening channels such as KΛ and KΣ, as planned using the Jülich model of pion-induced reactions.
