# Radial Overlap Correction to Superallowed $0^+ \to 0^+$ $β$-decay revisited

> Within the nuclear shell model, we investigate the correction $δ_{RO}$ to the Fermi matrix element due to a mismatch between proton and neutron single-particle radial wave functions. Eight superallowed $0^+ \to 0^+$ $β$ decays in the $sd$-shell, comprised of $^{22}$Mg, $^{26m}$Al, $^{26}$Si, $^{30}$S, $^{34}$Cl, $^{34}$Ar, $^{38m}$K and $^{38}$Ca are re-examined. The radial wave functions are obtained from a spherical Woods-Saxon potential whose parametrizations are optimized in a consistent adjustment of the depth and the length parameter to relevant experimental observables, such as nucleon separation energies and charge radii, respectively. The chosen fit strategy eliminates the strong dependence of the radial mismatch correction to a specific parametrization, except for calculations with an additional surface-peaked term. As an improvement, our model proposes a new way to calculate the charge radii, based on a parentage expansion which accounts for correlations beyond the extreme independent-particle model. Apart from the calculations with a surface-peak term and the cases where we used a different model space, the new sets of $δ_{RO}$ are in general agreement with the earlier result of Towner and Hardy [1]. Small differences of the corrected average $\overline{\mathcal{F}t}$ value are observed.

## Key claim

The radial overlap correction δ_RO to superallowed β-decay matrix elements is strongly dependent on the parametrization of the Woods-Saxon potential when using standard global parameter sets, but this dependence can be eliminated by consistently adjusting the potential depth and length parameter to reproduce experimental nucleon separation energies and charge radii, respectively, except for calculations that include an additional surface-peaked term.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear shell-model calculations in the full sd-shell using the NuShellX@MSU code with USD, USDA, and USDB effective interactions, obtaining single-particle radial wave functions from a spherical Woods-Saxon potential. The potential depth V_0 was adjusted separately for each valence orbital to reproduce experimental nucleon separation energies, while the radius parameter r_0 was fitted to reproduce experimental charge radii of the parent nuclei, with charge radii calculated using a parentage expansion that accounts for correlations beyond the independent-particle model. The radial overlap correction δ_RO was computed from the overlap integrals between proton and neutron radial wave functions using the Towner-Hardy formalism that neglects the interference term.

## Key numbers

- Eight superallowed 0+ → 0+ β decays in the sd-shell were re-examined: 22Mg, 26mAl, 26Si, 30S, 34Cl, 34Ar, 38mK, and 38Ca
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters r_0 = 1.26 fm and a_0 = a_s = 0.662 fm were used as starting values for both the BM_m and SWV parametrizations
- The SWV parametrization required r_0 values about 1.2% lower than those obtained with the BM_m parametrization after re-adjustment
- The proton charge distribution parameter a_p = 0.694 fm was used in the charge radius formula

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculations of Towner and Hardy (2002, 2008) by introducing a consistent optimization procedure for the Woods-Saxon potential parameters that eliminates the strong parametrization dependence previously observed. The study is limited to sd-shell nuclei where precise shell-model wave functions are available, and the authors plan to extend this method to heavier emitters using large-scale shell-model diagonalization. The results are relevant for testing the Conserved Vector Current hypothesis and extracting the |V_ud| element of the Cabibbo-Kobayashi-Maskawa quark-mixing matrix, where the current uncertainty is dominated by theoretical corrections for isospin-symmetry breaking.
