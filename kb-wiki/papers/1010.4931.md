# Role of nuclear radii in the exotic cluster-decay

> We have calculated the half-lives of $^{56}$Ni$^{\ast}$ within PCM for clusters $\ge ^{16}$O. The cluster decay half-lives studied with respect to radius formula due to Royer (R$_{Royer}$) shows that the half-lives lie within $\pm$7%, excluding Bass radius formula where these lie within $\pm$10%.

## Key claim

The half-lives of cluster decays from \(^{56}\text{Ni}^*\) for clusters \(\ge ^{16}\text{O}\) calculated with the Preformed Cluster Model (PCM) agree within \(\pm 7\%\) when using the Royer radius formula, but only within \(\pm 10\%\) when using the Bass radius formula, indicating that the choice of nuclear radius parametrization introduces systematic uncertainty at the level of a few percent.

## Method

The author used the Preformed Cluster Model (PCM), based on quantum mechanical fragmentation theory, to compute cluster decay half-lives of the compound nucleus \(^{56}\text{Ni}^*\). The nuclear potential was constructed using the proximity force theorem with seven different nuclear radius formulas (Prox77, AW, Prox00, Royer, Ngo, Bass, and CW). The decay constant \(\lambda\) was obtained as \(\lambda = P_0 \nu_0 P\), where \(P_0\) is the preformation probability, \(P\) the penetrability, and \(\nu_0\) the assault frequency, with the penetration path starting at \(R_a = R_{\text{min}}\) and ending at \(R_b\) where \(V(R_b) = Q_{\text{eff}}\).

## Key numbers

- Half-lives calculated with the Royer radius formula lie within \(\pm 7\%\) of a reference (presumably the Royer-based values themselves).
- Half-lives calculated with the Bass radius formula lie within \(\pm 10\%\) of the same reference.
- Calculations were performed for the compound nucleus \(^{56}\text{Ni}^*\) at an excitation energy \(E^*_{\text{CN}}\) (value not specified in the text) and for clusters with mass \(\ge ^{16}\text{O}\).
- The fragmentation potential and fractional mass distribution yields were computed at a temperature \(T = 3.0\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the application of the Preformed Cluster Model (PCM) by systematically comparing seven different nuclear radius parametrizations (Royer, Bass, Prox77, AW, Prox00, Ngo, CW) for cluster decay of \(^{56}\text{Ni}^*\). The results show that the Royer radius formula gives the smallest spread in half-lives, while the Bass formula gives the largest. This enables future PCM studies to choose a radius parametrization with quantified uncertainty and suggests that the Royer formula is preferable for cluster-decay calculations of medium-mass nuclei.
