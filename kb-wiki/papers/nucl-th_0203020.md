# Three-Nucleon Force Effects in Nucleon Induced Deuteron Breakup: Comparison to Data (II)

> Selected Nd breakup data over a wide energy range are compared to solutions of Faddeev equations based on modern high precision NN interactions alone and adding current three-nucleon force models. Unfortunately currently available data probe phase space regions for the final three nucleon momenta which are rather insensitive to 3NF effects as predicted by current models. Overall there is good to fair agreement between present day theory and experiment but also some cases exist with striking discrepancies. Regions in the phase space are suggested where large 3NF effects can be expected.

# Three-Nucleon Force Effects in Nucleon Induced Deuteron Breakup: Comparison to Data (II)

## Key claim

Selected Nd breakup data over a wide energy range (13, 65, and 200 MeV) show good to fair agreement with Faddeev solutions based on modern high-precision NN interactions alone and with added three-nucleon force (3NF) models, but the currently available data probe phase space regions that are largely insensitive to 3NF effects as predicted by current models; the paper identifies specific phase space regions where large 3NF effects are expected but have not yet been experimentally explored.

## Method

Fully converged solutions of the Faddeev equations were computed for nucleon-deuteron breakup at laboratory energies of 13, 65, and 200 MeV, using five modern high-precision NN potentials (AV18, CD Bonn, Nijm I, Nijm II, Nijm 93) alone and with the Tucson-Melbourne (TM) 3NF adjusted separately to the 3H binding energy for each NN force. Additional calculations were performed for the AV18 + Urbana IX and CD Bonn + TM' combinations. The theoretical predictions were compared to existing nd and pd breakup cross section and analyzing power data from multiple experimental groups.

## Key numbers

- At 13 MeV, the symmetric space star (SSS) configuration shows theoretical nd predictions underestimate nd data by about 20% and overestimate pd data by about 15%
- At 65 MeV, the quasi-free scattering (QFS) maximum is overestimated by theory by about 13%
- At 65 MeV, the SSS configuration shows pd data lie below theoretical predictions by about 7%
- At 13 MeV, the space star cross section is approximately 60% from the 3S1 NN force, 30% from the 1S0 force, and about 10% from P-wave forces
- At 65 MeV, 3NF effects of up to 100% and higher are predicted in certain geometries (theta1 ~ 20 deg, theta2 ~ 10 deg, phi12 between 0 and 60 deg) but these regions have not been measured
- At 200 MeV, the cross section bands are very narrow and overlapping, indicating practically no 3NF effects in the measured configurations

## Context

This paper (Part II) follows a systematic search for 3NF effects in the full phase space of Nd breakup (Kuros-Zolnierczuk et al., 2002, referred to as I), which identified sensitive regions for 3NF effects at each energy. The work extends earlier comparisons by Glockle et al. (1996) that used older NN potentials (Bonn B, AV14, Paris) or only one modern potential. The "space star anomaly" identified by Setze et al. (1996) remains unexplained. The paper calls for new breakup experiments guided by the theoretical predictions from Part I, particularly at higher energies in configurations where large 3NF effects are predicted, and notes that ongoing measurements at KVI Groningen (Kistryn et al., 2001) will cover a larger part of the phase space with higher accuracy.
