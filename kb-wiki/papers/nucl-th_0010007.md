# Production of isoscalar pion pairs in the pd -> 3He pi pi reaction near threshold

> The production near threshold of isoscalar pion pairs in the pd -> 3He(pi pi)^0 reaction is estimated in a two-step model which successfully describes the production of eta, omega and eta' mesons. A virtual pion beam, generated through an NN -> d pi reaction on one of the nucleons in the deuteron, produces a second pion via a pi N -> pi pi N reaction on the other nucleon. Using the same scale factor as for heavy meson production, the model reproduces the total pi^0 pi^0 production rate determined at an excess energy of 37 MeV. There are some indications in the data for a suppression of events with low pi pi masses, as in the pi^- p -> pi^0 pi^0 n reaction, and this is confirmed within the model. The model suggests that a significant fraction of the charged pion production in the p d -> 3He pi^+ pi^- reaction at Q=70 MeV might be associated with isoscalar pion pairs, though this does not explain the strong dependence observed on the pi^+ pi^- relative momentum angle.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that near-threshold isoscalar pion pair production in the pd → ³He ππ reaction can be described by a two-step sequential model involving virtual pion exchange, using the same normalization factor N=2.4 that successfully describes η, ω, and η' production, though the model cannot explain the strong angular dependence observed in charged pion production at Q=70 MeV.

## Method

The authors model the reaction as a two-step process where a virtual pion is first produced via an NN → dπ reaction on one nucleon in the deuteron, then this pion produces a second pion through a πN → ππN reaction on the other nucleon. The calculation uses the same formalism previously applied to heavy meson production (Faldt and Wilkin, 1997), incorporating nuclear form factors from deuteron and ³He wave functions, and parameterizes the πN → ππN amplitude using fits to the Valencia model predictions (Oset et al., 1987) for I=0 pion pairs.

## Key numbers

- Total π⁰π⁰ production rate at excess energy Q=37 MeV is reproduced using normalization factor N=2.4, the same factor used for η, ω, and η' production
- The I=0 ππ amplitude parameterization is given by |a(m_ππ,Q)|² = (1.092-0.0211Q+0.00015Q²) + (4.18+0.0075Q-0.00098Q²)x + (47.65-0.935Q+0.00743Q²)x² μb/MeV², where x=m_ππ/m_π-2, valid up to Q≈100 MeV
- The CELSIUS π⁰π⁰ data point at Q=37 MeV and the MOMO π⁺π⁻ data point at Q=70 MeV are compared with model predictions
- The IUCF π⁺π⁻ point at Q=0.67 MeV is noted as heavily influenced by Coulomb distortion

## Context

This work extends the two-step model previously applied to single meson production (η, ω, η', φ) by Faldt and Wilkin (1997) to the case of isoscalar pion pair production. The model addresses the absence of the ABC enhancement at low Q, which contrasts with the prominent ABC peaks observed at Q≈200-300 MeV by the ABC collaboration (1960) and Banaigs et al. (1973). The results enable future comparison with higher-statistics data from the Crystal Ball collaboration on π⁻p → π⁰π⁰n at 750 MeV/c, and suggest that further theoretical work is needed to incorporate p-wave contributions that could explain the angular dependence observed by the MOMO collaboration (1998).
