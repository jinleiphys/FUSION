# Initial conditions for hydrodynamics from weakly coupled pre-equilibrium evolution

> We use leading order effective kinetic theory to simulate the pre\nobreakdash-equilibrium evolution of transverse energy and flow perturbations in heavy-ion collisions. We provide a Green function which propagates the initial perturbations of the energy-momentum tensor to a time when hydrodynamics becomes applicable. With this map, the pre-thermal evolution from saturated nuclei to hydrodynamics can be modeled in the framework of weakly coupled QCD.

## Key claim

The paper provides a Green function that propagates initial perturbations of the energy-momentum tensor from the microscopic formation time to the onset of hydrodynamics, but this mapping is valid only for weakly coupled QCD at leading order in the coupling constant and for small transverse perturbations around a locally constant background.

## Method

The author uses leading-order effective kinetic theory of high-temperature QCD, solving the Boltzmann equation for a boost-invariant gluon distribution with elastic 2↔2 and inelastic 1↔2 collision processes. The distribution function is split into a translationally invariant background and a linear perturbation with a transverse wavenumber k⊥, and the system of coupled differential equations is solved for constant k⊥. Spatial Green functions are obtained by simulating the kinetic theory response to several wavenumber perturbations and then taking a Fourier-Hankel transform to coordinate space.

## Key numbers

- Equilibration time τ_init ∼ 1 fm/c, with microscopic formation time τ₀ ∼ 0.1 fm/c
- Causally connected region c(τ_init − τ₀) ∼ 1 fm, much smaller than transverse nuclear geometry R_Pb ∼ 5 fm but comparable to nucleon scale R_p ∼ 1 fm
- Coupling constant α_s ∼ 0.3 used for realistic values
- Energy response function E(r; τ, τ₀) shown at scaled time τ T/(η/s) = 10

## Context

This work extends the effective kinetic theory approach of Kurkela (2015) and Keegan (2016) to study transverse energy and flow perturbations during pre-equilibrium evolution in heavy-ion collisions. It provides a practical mapping from Color Glass Condensate-inspired initial conditions at the saturation scale to hydrodynamic initialization, which could reduce the dependence on hydro initialization time and better account for pre-equilibrium flow in models such as IP-Glasma. The approach is limited to weakly coupled QCD and small perturbations, and future work could extend it to larger gradients or include fermionic degrees of freedom.
