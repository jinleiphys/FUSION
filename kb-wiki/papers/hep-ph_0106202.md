# Heavy quark colorimetry of QCD matter

> We consider propagation of heavy quarks in QCD matter. Because of large quark mass, the radiative quark energy loss appears to be qualitatively different from that of light quarks at all energies of practical importance. Finite quark mass effects lead to an in-medium enhancement of the heavy-to-light D/πratio at moderately large (5--10 GeV) transverse momenta. For hot QCD matter a large enhancement is expected, whose magnitude and shape are exponentially sensitive to the density of colour charges in the medium.

## Key claim

The radiative energy loss of heavy quarks in QCD matter is qualitatively different from that of light quarks at all practically relevant energies, leading to an in-medium enhancement of the heavy-to-light \(D/\pi\) ratio at moderately large transverse momenta (5–10 GeV). The magnitude and shape of this enhancement are exponentially sensitive to the density of colour charges in the medium, but the quantitative prediction is semi-quantitative and depends on a simplified model of a static uniform medium with a fixed quark path length.

## Method

The authors compare the medium-induced gluon radiation spectra for heavy and light quarks by incorporating the "dead cone" suppression factor (which reduces radiation at angles smaller than \(M/E\)) into the standard radiative energy loss formalism of Baier–Dokshitzer–Mueller–Peigné–Schiff (BDMPS). They compute the quenching factor \(Q(p_\perp)\) for the transverse momentum spectrum by taking the Mellin transform of the integrated gluon multiplicity, using parameterizations of vacuum \(p_\perp\) distributions for pions and \(D\) mesons fitted to experimental data (RHIC hadroproduction data and E769 charm data). The ratio of quenching factors for heavy and light quarks is then evaluated for both cold nuclear matter and hot quark–gluon plasma.

## Key numbers

* Cold nuclear matter transport coefficient: \(\hat{q}_{\mathrm{cold}} \simeq 0.01\ \mathrm{GeV}^3 \simeq 8\ \rho\) (with \(\rho \simeq 0.16\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\)), consistent with the analysis of \(J/\psi\) transverse momentum distributions yielding \(\hat{q} = (9.4 \pm 0.7)\,\rho\).
* Hot medium transport coefficient: \(\hat{q}_{\mathrm{hot}} \simeq 0.2\ \mathrm{GeV}^3 \simeq 20\,\hat{q}_{\mathrm{cold}}\) (for \(T \sim 250\ \mathrm{MeV}\)).
* Characteristic heavy-quark energy scale for cold matter: \(E_{\mathrm{HQ}} \simeq 20\ \mathrm{GeV}\,(L/5\ \mathrm{fm})^{3/2}\) for charm quarks (\(M = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\)).
* Characteristic heavy-quark energy scale for hot matter: \(E_{\mathrm{HQ}} \simeq 92\ \mathrm{GeV}\,(L/5\ \mathrm{fm})^{3/2}\) for charm quarks.
* In cold nuclear matter (\(L = 5\ \mathrm{fm}\)), the heavy-to-light quenching ratio is about 1.15 (15% enhancement); for \(L = 2\ \mathrm{fm}\) it is about 1.01 (1% enhancement).
* In hot matter (\(L = 5\ \mathrm{fm}\)), the \(D/\pi\) ratio is enhanced by a factor of \(\sim 2\) at \(p_\perp \sim 5\)–\(10\ \mathrm{GeV}\).
* The vacuum \(p_\perp\) distribution for light hadrons uses parameters \(p_0 = 1.71\ \mathrm{GeV}\) and \(m = 12.42\); for \(D\) mesons the parameters are \(b = 1.4 \pm 0.3\), \(n = 10.0 \pm 1.2\), and \(m_c = 1.5\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS formalism for medium-induced gluon radiation (Baier–Dokshitzer–Mueller–Peigné–Schiff, 1997) to heavy quarks by incorporating the dead-cone effect, previously studied in the context of heavy quark fragmentation functions (Dokshitzer–Khoze–Trottsky, 1991; Nason–Webber, 1994) and multiplicity spectra (Dokshitzer–Kharzeev, 2001). It contradicts the expectation that heavy and light quark energy loss are similar at high energies, showing instead that finite mass effects persist at all practical energies. The predicted enhancement of the \(D/\pi\) ratio provides a new observable for probing the colour charge density of the quark–gluon plasma in heavy ion collisions, complementing earlier work on jet quenching (Baier–Dokshitzer–Mueller–Schiff, 2001) and the Cronin effect.
