# Theoretical mean field and experimental occupation probabilities in the double beta decay system 76Ge to 76Se

> Usual Woods-Saxon single particle levels with BCS pairing are not able to reproduce the experimental occupation probabilities of the proton and neutron levels 1p_{3/2}, 1p_{1/2}, 0f_{5/2}, 0g_{9/2} in the double-beta decay system 76Ge to 76Se. Shifting down the 0g_{9/2} level by hand can explain the data but it is not satisfactory. Here it is shown that a selfconsistent Hartree-Fock+BCS approach with experimental deformations for 76Ge and 76Se may decisively improve the agreement with the recent data on occupation probabilities by Schiffer et al. and Kay et al. Best agreement with available data on 76Ge and 76Se, as well as on neighbor isotopes, is obtained when the spin-orbit strength for neutrons is allowed to be larger than that for protons. The two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix element is also shown to agree with data.

# Theoretical mean field and experimental occupation probabilities in the double beta decay system 76Ge to 76Se

## Key claim
A selfconsistent Hartree-Fock+BCS approach with experimental deformations improves agreement with measured occupation probabilities in 76Ge and 76Se, but the best agreement is only obtained when the spin-orbit strength for neutrons is allowed to be larger than that for protons, specifically by increasing the neutron spin-orbit parameter W0 from 120 to 200 MeV fm^5 while keeping the proton value fixed.

## Method
The authors performed deformed Skyrme Hartree-Fock (HF) mean field calculations with BCS pairing, solving the BCS equations at each HF iteration to obtain single particle wave functions, energies, and occupation probabilities until convergence. They then solved the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pnQRPA) equations on each deformed ground state basis for 76Ge and 76Se to compute Gamow-Teller strength distributions and the two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix element. The Skyrme force parameters were varied, focusing on the spin-orbit strength W0, with experimental quadrupole deformations (beta=0.10 for 76Ge, beta=0.16 for 76Se) imposed during the HF+BCS procedure.

## Key numbers
- Experimental deformation parameter beta = 0.10 for 76Ge and beta = 0.16 for 76Se, with 25-30% uncertainty, from Coulomb excitation reorientation measurements (Rag89) and charge radii (Ang04)
- Best agreement with experimental occupation probabilities achieved with neutron spin-orbit strength W_nu = 200 MeV fm^5 and proton spin-orbit strength W_pi = 120 MeV fm^5 (the standard Sk3 value)
- Binding energy: experimental 661.6 MeV for 76Ge and 662.07 MeV for 76Se; Sk3 calculation underestimates by 0.5%, Sk3^nu calculation overestimates by 2%
- Charge radii: HF(Sk3)+BCS gives 4.12 fm for 76Ge and 4.17 fm for 76Se; Sk3^nu gives 4.09 fm for 76Ge and 4.14 fm for 76Se, agreeing better with experimental values of 4.08 fm for 76Ge and 4.14 fm for 76Se (Ang04)
- pnQRPA coupling constants: chi_ph = 0.25 MeV and kappa_pp = 0.08 MeV

## Context
This work extends previous mean field calculations using Woods-Saxon potentials with BCS pairing (Suh08, Sim09, Bar09) that could not reproduce experimental occupation probabilities measured by Schiffer et al. (2008) for neutrons and Kay et al. (2009) for protons in 76Ge and 76Se. The authors show that a deformed Skyrme HF+BCS approach with an enhanced neutron spin-orbit strength improves agreement beyond what was possible with spherical Woods-Saxon potentials or with standard Skyrme forces having equal spin-orbit strength for protons and neutrons. The improved single particle basis enables better calculations of Gamow-Teller strength distributions and two-neutrino double-beta decay matrix elements, and suggests that more flexible isospin-dependent spin-orbit terms should be incorporated into Skyrme energy density functionals.
