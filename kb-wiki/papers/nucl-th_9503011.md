# CHIRAL SYMMETRY CONSTRAINTS ON THE $K^+$ INTERACTION WITH THE NUCLEAR PION CLOUD

> The real part of the $K^{+}$ selfenergy for the interaction of the $K^{+}$ with the pion nuclear cloud is evaluated in lowest order of chiral perturbation theory and is found to be exactly zero in symmetric nuclear matter. This removes uncertainties in that quantity found in former phenomenological analyses and is supported by present experimental data on $K^{+}$ nucleus scattering.

## Key claim

The real part of the $K^{+}$ selfenergy from interaction with the nuclear pion cloud is exactly zero in symmetric nuclear matter when evaluated in lowest order of chiral perturbation theory, removing the off-shell uncertainties that plagued earlier phenomenological analyses by Brown et al. (1991) and Oset et al. (1993).

## Method

The authors evaluated the $K^{+}$ selfenergy in symmetric nuclear matter using the lowest-order chiral perturbation theory Lagrangian, including the $KK\pi\pi$ vertex, the $\pi\bar{N}N$ vertex, and the $\bar{N}NKK\pi$ contact term. They demonstrated that the diagrams contributing to the real part of the selfenergy from the $K^{+}\pi$ interaction cancel exactly with additional chiral terms that appear at the same order, for both nucleon-hole and $\Delta$-hole excitations, in symmetric nuclear matter with equal numbers of protons and neutrons.

## Key numbers

- The pion decay constant used is $f = f_{\pi} = 92.4$ MeV.
- The axial coupling constant is $D + F = g_A = 1.257 \pm 0.003$.
- The cancellation holds for $K^{+}$ momenta satisfying $k^2 = k'^2 = m_K^2$.
- The analysis applies to symmetric nuclear matter where the number of protons equals the number of neutrons.

## Context

This work resolves uncertainties in the real part of the $K^{+}$ selfenergy that were found in earlier phenomenological analyses by Brown et al. (1991) and Oset et al. (1993), where results depended strongly on the off-shell extrapolation of the $K^{+}\pi$ amplitude. The exact cancellation found here supports the empirical conclusion from Oset et al. (1993) that the data were best reproduced with $\text{Re}(\delta\Pi) \simeq 0$, and enables future systematic higher-order chiral perturbation theory calculations of both the real and imaginary parts of the $K^{+}$ selfenergy.
