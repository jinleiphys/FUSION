# Polarized Proton Nucleus Scattering

> We show that, to a very good approximation, the ratio of the spin-flip to the non-flip parts of the elastic proton-nucleus amplitude is the same as for proton-nucleon scattering at very high energy. The result is used to do a realistic calculation of the analyzing power A_N for pC scattering in the Coulomb-nuclear interference (CNI) region of momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The ratio of the spin-flip to non-flip parts of the elastic proton-nucleus amplitude is, to a very good approximation, the same as for proton-nucleon scattering at very high energy, provided the nuclear radius is much larger than the slopes of the proton-proton amplitudes (a condition that is not satisfied over much of the periodic table, requiring finite-size corrections).

## Method

The authors derive the spin-flip and non-flip eikonals for proton-nucleus scattering in the optical model for large nuclei, then prove a theorem showing that if the spin-flip parameter μ_P is independent of momentum transfer q, the relation F_s(q) = (μ_P / 2m_N) F_o(q) holds exactly. They test the robustness of this result by numerically evaluating the amplitude for He-4 and O-16 using harmonic-oscillator wave functions and exponential forms for the scattering amplitudes, varying the slope of the spin-flip amplitude. They also estimate corrections from inelastic shadowing using triple-Regge phenomenology and fan-diagram summation.

## Key numbers

- For He-4, the ratio of reduced nuclear spin-flip amplitude to non-flip amplitude at t=0 varies by less than about ±5% when the ratio of slopes R_s/R_o is varied over a large range.
- The forward single diffractive cross section ratio (dσ_sd^{PPR}/dt)/(dσ_el/dt) at t=0 is approximately 0.06.
- The inelastic correction from the PPR triple-Regge term grows proportionally to A^{1/3} and ranges from about 1% for carbon to about 2% for lead.
- For carbon, the nuclear radius squared R_A^2 is about 50 GeV^{-2}, while the non-flip slope 2B_o is about 20 GeV^{-2}.
- The calculation for pC scattering uses σ_tot = 39 mb at E_p = 22 GeV and the reduced slope B_NN = 5.0 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the earlier result of Bethe (1958) for low-energy potential scattering, who cited analogous results by Kohler and by Levintov, to high-energy proton-nucleus scattering using Glauber theory. The authors show that the relation between spin-flip and non-flip amplitudes is robust against finite-size corrections and inelastic shadowing, enabling the use of nuclear targets to measure the spin-flip part of the Pomeron amplitude. This enables a clean determination of the Pomeron spin properties from the E950 experiment at BNL, which can then be used for polarimetry of polarized proton beams at RHIC.
