# Isospin violating decay $D^*_s \to D_s π^0$ in chiral perturbation theory

> We systematically calculate the isospin violating decay, $D_s^*\to D_sπ^0$, with the heavy meson chiral perturbation theory up to $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ including the loop diagrams. The $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ tree level amplitudes contain four undetermined LECs. We use two strategies to estimate them. With the nonanalytic dominance approximation, we get $Γ[D_s^\ast\to D_sπ^0]=(3.38\pm0.12)$ eV. With the naturalness assumption, we give a possible range of the isospin violating decay width, $[1.11-6.88]$ eV. We find that the contribution of the $\mathcal{O}(p^3)$ corrections might be significant.

## Key claim

The isospin-violating decay width of \(D_s^* \to D_s \pi^0\) is calculated up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) in heavy meson chiral perturbation theory, but the result depends on four undetermined low-energy constants (LECs) at tree level; the paper provides two estimates, a central value under the nonanalytic dominance approximation and a range under the naturalness assumption, and notes that the \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) corrections may be significant.

## Method

The authors used heavy meson chiral perturbation theory, incorporating both heavy quark symmetry and chiral symmetry, to compute the decay amplitude for \(D_s^* \to D_s \pi^0\) up to \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\). They included the leading-order \(\mathcal{O}(p)\) tree diagram (via \(\eta\)-\(\pi\) mixing), all \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) one-loop diagrams with vertices from the leading-order Lagrangian, and \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) tree diagrams with unknown LECs. The loop integrals were renormalized using dimensional regularization in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme, and the unknown LECs were estimated via two strategies: nonanalytic dominance approximation and naturalness assumption.

## Key numbers

- \(\Gamma[D_s^* \to D_s \pi^0] = (3.38 \pm 0.12)\) eV under the nonanalytic dominance approximation (Strategy A).
- \(\Gamma[D_s^* \to D_s \pi^0]\) range \([1.11, 6.88]\) eV under the naturalness assumption (Strategy B, with parameter \(\alpha\) varied from \(-1\) to \(1\)).
- Leading-order \(\mathcal{O}(p)\) amplitude contribution: \(-46.90 \times 10^{-3} \text{GeV}^{-1}\) (in units of \(i\mathcal{M}/(q\cdot\varepsilon)\)).
- \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) loop contribution: \(-2.73 \times 10^{-3} \text{GeV}^{-1}\) (with \(\Delta \neq 0\)); \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) tree contribution: \(\pm 1.08 \times 10^{-3} \text{GeV}^{-1}\).
- Estimated total width of \(D_s^*\): \(\Gamma[D_s^*] = 58.26^{+10.37}_{-8.11}\) eV, derived from the branching fraction \((5.8 \pm 0.7)\%\) for \(D_s^* \to D_s \pi^0\).

## Context

This work extends earlier tree-level calculations of \(D_s^* \to D_s \pi^0\) by Cho (1994), Ivanov (1998), and Terasaki (2015) by including \(\mathcal{O}(p^3)\) loop diagrams and systematic treatment of next-to-leading-order tree contributions. The analytical expressions are intended to aid chiral extrapolations in lattice QCD simulations, and the results highlight the importance of heavy quark symmetry breaking effects in the charm sector, as noted in prior work by Wang (2019).
