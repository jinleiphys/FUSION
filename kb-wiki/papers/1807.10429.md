# Cross Sections for Inelastic 2-to-2 Meson-Meson Scattering in Hadronic Matter

> With quark-antiquark annihilation and creation in the first Born approximation, we study the reactions: $K \bar {K} \to K \bar {K}^\ast, ~K \bar{K} \to K^* \bar{K}, ~πK \to πK^\ast, ~πK \to ρK, ~ππ\to K \bar{K}^\ast, ~ππ\to K^\ast \bar{K}, ~ππ\to K^\ast \bar{K}^\ast, ~πρ\to K \bar{K}, ~πρ\to K^\ast \bar{K}^\ast, ~ρρ\to K^\ast \bar{K}^\ast, ~K \bar{K}^\ast \to ρρ$, and $K^* \bar{K} \to ρρ$. Unpolarized cross sections for the reactions are obtained from transition amplitudes that are composed of mesonic quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions and the transition potential for quark-antiquark annihilation and creation. From a quark-antiquark potential that is equivalent to the transition potential, we prove that the total spin of the two final mesons may not equal the total spin of the two initial mesons. Based on flavor matrix elements, cross sections for some isospin channels of reactions can be obtained from the other isospin channels of reactions. Remarkable temperature dependence of the cross sections is found.

## Key claim

The authors calculate unpolarized cross sections for twelve inelastic 2-to-2 meson-meson scattering reactions among pi, rho, K, and K* mesons using quark-antiquark annihilation and creation in the first Born approximation, finding that the total spin of the two final mesons may not equal the total spin of the two initial mesons, contrary to what might be expected from spin conservation in simpler mechanisms.

## Method

The authors compute transition amplitudes from mesonic quark-antiquark relative-motion wave functions and a transition potential for quark-antiquark annihilation and creation, using the first Born approximation with two Feynman diagrams. The wave functions are obtained from the Schrodinger equation with a temperature-dependent potential that reproduces experimental ground-state meson masses, using up/down quark masses of 0.32 GeV and strange quark mass of 0.5 GeV. Unpolarized cross sections are then calculated from these transition amplitudes at six temperatures from T/Tc = 0 to 0.95.

## Key numbers

- Up and down quark masses: 0.32 GeV; strange quark mass: 0.5 GeV
- Critical temperature Tc = 0.175 GeV used for temperature scaling of the potential
- Gauge coupling constant for quark-antiquark annihilation and creation: 2*sqrt(6)*pi/5
- The cross section for reactions with I=0 is 1.5 times the cross section for I=1, based on flavor matrix elements where the I=0 matrix elements are sqrt(6)/2 times the I=1 values
- For K-bar-K to K-bar-K* at I=0, the peak cross section at a given temperature is more than 4 times the one for I=1, and sqrt(s_z) for I=0 is roughly 2 times that for I=1
- The potential parameters include D = 0.7 GeV, A = 1.5[0.75+0.25(T/Tc)^10]^6 GeV, E = 0.6 GeV, alpha-prime = 1.04 GeV^-2, d1 = 0.15 GeV, d2 = 0.705

## Context

This work extends previous studies of inelastic meson-meson scattering in hadronic matter, complementing reactions studied in Xu et al. (SXW) which included pi-pi to rho-rho, K-bar-K to K*-bar-K*, and other channels. The authors note that elastic pi-pi and pi-K scattering have been studied in the quark interchange mechanism by Barnes and Swanson (BS) and in nonperturbative schemes with chiral perturbation theory, while reactions like pi-pi to rho-rho and K-K to K*-K* have been studied via quark interchange by Li and Xu (LX) and Song and Xu (SX). The present work enables more complete modeling of hadronic matter created in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions by providing cross sections for reactions not previously calculated, particularly those involving K and K* mesons with pi and rho mesons.
