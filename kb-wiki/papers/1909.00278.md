# The Shape of the Correlation Function

> The correlation function measured in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions is non-Gaussian. By making use of models we discuss and assess how much various effects can influence its shape. In particular, we focus on the parametrisations expressed with the help of Lévy-stable distributions. We show that the Lévy index may deviate substantially from 2 due to non-critical effects such as non-spherical shape, resonance decays, event-by-event fluctuations and functional dependence on $Q_{inv}$ or similar.

# The Shape of the Correlation Function

## Key claim
The Lévy index extracted from two-particle correlation functions in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions can deviate substantially from 2 due to non-critical effects such as non-spherical source shape, resonance decays, event-by-event fluctuations, and the choice of one-dimensional projection variable, meaning that a low Lévy index does not uniquely signal critical behavior near the QCD phase diagram endpoint.

## Method
The authors used two independent models to generate pion-emitting sources: a blast-wave model implemented in the DRAGON Monte Carlo event generator (50,000 events per parameter set) and the iEBE-VISHNU 2+1-dimensional hydrodynamic model with Glauber initial conditions (1,000 events of 0-10% Au+Au collisions at 200A GeV). Correlation functions were computed using CRAB for blast-wave events and HoTCoffeeh for hydrodynamic events, then fitted with one-dimensional and three-dimensional Lévy-stable parametrizations to extract the Lévy index as a function of transverse pair momentum K_T.

## Key numbers
- In the hydrodynamic model, inclusion of resonance decays reduces the Lévy index by 0.2-0.3 across the K_T range studied.
- Event averaging and choice between Q_inv and Q_LCMS shift the Lévy index by less than 0.05 at low K_T, with larger but still subdominant shifts at high K_T.
- Blast-wave model parameters: temperature T = 120 MeV, average transverse radius R_0 = 7 fm, freeze-out time tau_fo = 10 fm/c, transverse expansion strength rho_0 = 0.8, second-order spatial and flow anisotropies a_2 and rho_2 each in the range (-0.1; 0.1).
- Hydrodynamic model: freeze-out temperature T_fo = 120 MeV, shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.08.
- Both models show that resonances reduce the Lévy index by approximately 0.2 regardless of the model used.

## Context
This work extends the study of non-Gaussian correlation functions beyond the Gaussian parametrization commonly used in femtoscopy (Heinz 1996, Wiedemann 1999) and addresses the interpretation of Lévy indices observed experimentally (e.g., values near or below 1 reported by the PHENIX and STAR collaborations). The paper shows that non-critical effects, particularly resonance decays and one-dimensional projection of the relative momentum, can produce Lévy indices significantly below 2, complicating the search for the QCD critical endpoint where a Lévy index of 0.5 has been proposed as a signature. The results enable more careful interpretation of future experimental Lévy index measurements by establishing baseline non-critical contributions that must be subtracted or modeled.
