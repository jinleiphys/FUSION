# Shallow Bound States and Hints for Broad Resonances with Quark Content $\bar{b}\bar{c}ud$ in $B$-$\bar{D}$ and $B^*$-$\bar{D}$ Scattering from Lattice QCD

> We present the first determination of the energy dependence of the $B$-$\bar{D}$ and $B^*$-$\bar{D}$ isospin-0, $S$-wave scattering amplitudes both below and above the thresholds using lattice QCD, which allows us to investigate rigorously whether mixed bottom-charm $\bar{b}\bar{c}ud$ tetraquarks exist as bound states or resonances. The scattering phase shifts are obtained using Lüscher's method from the energy spectra in two different volumes. To ensure that no relevant energy level is missed, we use large, symmetric $7 \times 7$ and $8 \times 8$ correlation matrices that include, at both source and sink, $B^{(*)}$-$\bar{D}$ scattering operators with the lowest three or four possible back-to-back momenta in addition to local $\bar{b}\bar{c}ud$ operators. We fit the energy dependence of the extracted scattering phase shifts using effective-range expansions. We observe sharp peaks in the $B^{(*)}$-$\bar{D}$ scattering rates close to the thresholds, which are associated with shallow bound states, either genuine or virtual, a few MeV or less below the $B^{(*)}$-$\bar{D}$ thresholds. In addition, we find hints for resonances with masses of order $100$ MeV above the thresholds and decay widths of order $200$ MeV.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first lattice-QCD determination of the energy-dependent $B$-$\bar{D}$ and $B^*$-$\bar{D}$ isospin-0, $S$-wave scattering amplitudes, finding shallow bound states (either genuine or virtual) a few MeV or less below threshold and hints for broad resonances roughly 100 MeV above threshold with widths around 200 MeV. The bound-state identification is hedged by statistical uncertainty: for $J=0$, 88.5% of random samples yield a genuine bound state and 11.5% a virtual bound state; for $J=1$, the corresponding fractions are 97.7% and 2.3%.

## Method
The authors computed finite-volume energy spectra of the $\bar{b}\bar{c}ud$ system for $I(J^P)=0(0^+)$ and $0(1^+)$ on two lattice volumes ($24^3\times64$ and $32^3\times64$) with a lattice spacing of approximately 0.12 fm and pion mass of approximately 220 MeV. They used large, symmetric $7\times7$ and $8\times8$ correlation matrices including local four-quark operators and $B^{(*)}$-$\bar{D}$ scattering operators with back-to-back momenta up to $\sqrt{3}\cdot2\pi/L$, at both source and sink. The scattering phase shifts were extracted via Luscher's method and fitted with effective-range expansions through order $k^4$ to determine pole locations in the complex energy plane.

## Key numbers
- For $J=0$: $\Delta m_{\rm GBS} = -0.5_{-1.5}^{+0.4}$ MeV (genuine bound-state mass relative to $B$-$\bar{D}$ threshold); resonance parameters $\Delta m_{\rm R} = 138(13)$ MeV and $\Gamma_{\rm R} = 229(35)$ MeV.
- For $J=1$: $\Delta m_{\rm GBS} = -2.4_{-0.7}^{+2.0}$ MeV (relative to $B^*$-$\bar{D}$ threshold); resonance parameters $\Delta m_{\rm R} = 67(24)$ MeV and $\Gamma_{\rm R} = 132(32)$ MeV.
- The left-hand cut from two-pion exchange starts at $\sqrt{s_{\rm cut}}-\sqrt{s_{\rm th}} \approx -18$ MeV for both $J=0$ and $J=1$, corresponding to $(ak)^2_{\rm cut} \approx -0.019$.
- The scattering rate $k\sigma(k)$ reaches near the unitary limit $|T_0|^2=1$ up to several tens of MeV above threshold.

## Context
This work extends previous lattice-QCD studies of $\bar{b}\bar{c}ud$ tetraquarks by Francis et al. (2018, 2020) and Meinel et al. (2022), which found no clear evidence for QCD-stable bound states, and contrasts with a recent lattice result by Padmanath et al. (2023) reporting a bound state 43 MeV below the $B^*$-$\bar{D}$ threshold. The use of multi-operator correlation matrices with nonzero back-to-back momenta at both source and sink is a significant methodological advance over prior calculations. The results enable future lattice studies at additional lattice spacings and pion masses to determine the exact pole locations at the physical point, and provide guidance for experimental searches at the LHC for mixed bottom-charm tetraquarks.
