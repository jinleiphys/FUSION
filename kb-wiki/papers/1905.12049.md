# Linear response theory in asymmetric nuclear matter for Skyrme functionals including spin-orbit and tensor terms II: Charge Exchange

> We present the formalism of linear response theory both at zero and finite temperature in the case of asymmetric nuclear matter excited by an isospin flip probe. The particle-hole interaction is derived from a general Skyrme functional that includes spin-orbit and tensor terms. Response functions are obtained by solving a closed algebraic system of equations. Spin strength functions are analyzed for typical values of density, momentum transfer, asymmetry and temperature.

## Key claim

The paper extends linear response theory for charge-exchange (isospin-flip) excitations in asymmetric nuclear matter to include spin-orbit and tensor terms in the Skyrme functional, showing that tensor terms break the M-degeneracy of the spin-1 response and significantly affect the strength function, especially at higher transferred momenta and asymmetries, contrary to the earlier central-only treatment of Hernandez et al. (1999).

## Method

The particle-hole interaction was derived from a general Skyrme energy density functional including central, spin-orbit, and tensor terms. The Bethe-Salpeter equation for the RPA propagator was solved by reducing it to a closed algebraic system of linear equations for momentum integrals of the propagator, using the formalism of Davesne et al. (2014) adapted to the charge-exchange channel. Response functions were computed at zero and finite temperature for several Skyrme functionals (SLy5, T22, T44, Skxta) at various densities, transferred momenta, and isospin asymmetries.

## Key numbers

*   The asymmetry parameter is defined as \(Y = (\rho_n - \rho_p)/\rho_0\), with \(Y=0\) for symmetric nuclear matter and \(Y=1\) for pure neutron matter; representative values used are \(Y=0.21\) (corresponding to \(^{208}\)Pb) and \(Y=0.5\) (typical of beta-equilibrium in neutron stars).
*   At density \(\rho/\rho_0 = 0.5\) and transferred momentum \(q/k_F = 0.2\), the tensor term breaks the degeneracy of the (1,0) and (1,1) spin channels, an effect that is small at \(Y=0.21\) but significantly enhanced at \(Y=0.5\).
*   At saturation density \(\rho/\rho_0 = 1\) and \(q/k_F = 1\), the tensor interaction produces opposite effects in the two spin-projection channels: strong attraction (accumulation of strength at low energy) in the \(M=0\) channel and repulsion in the \(M=1\) channel.
*   Finite-temperature calculations at \(T = 0.1 e_F\) and \(T = 0.5 e_F\) show that thermal effects wash out the structure of the response function, broadening the peaks and, in some cases, absorbing the collective mode into the continuum.
*   The statistical uncertainty analysis using UNEDF0 and UNEDF1 functionals shows that the main features of the \(S=0\) response function are not strongly impacted by parameter errors, but the height of the peaks is affected within one standard deviation.

## Context

This work extends the linear response formalism of Hernandez et al. (1999), which was limited to the central part of the Skyrme interaction, by including spin-orbit and tensor terms. It builds on previous studies by Davesne et al. (2009, 2012, 2014) and Pastore et al. (2012, 2013) that demonstrated the significant role of tensor terms in non-charge-exchange response functions and neutrino mean free paths in pure neutron matter. The results enable more realistic calculations of neutrino mean free paths in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature, which are crucial for understanding supernova explosions, neutron star mergers, and protoneutron star evolution.
