# An alternate well-founded way to treat the center-of-mass correlations: use of a local center-of-mass correlations potential

> The recently developed "internal" Density Functional Theory provides an existence theorem for a local potential that contains the center-of-mass correlations effects. The knowledge of the corresponding energy functional would provide a much cheaper way than projection techniques to treat these correlations. The aim of this article is to construct such a functional. We propose a well-founded method, suitable for Fermions as well as for Bosons, which does not require any free parameters.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a well-founded local potential that contains center-of-mass (c.m.) correlations effects for self-bound systems, suitable for both Fermions and Bosons, which requires no free parameters; the method is presented as an alternative to projection techniques that is numerically cheaper, but the construction relies on an approximation for the internal wavefunction that may not hold strictly in the general case.

## Method

The author starts from the internal Density Functional Theory (DFT) formalism and splits the interaction energy functional into a "standard" many-body part and a pure c.m. correlations part \(E_{cm}\). To approximate \(E_{cm}\), the exact internal wavefunction \(\psi_{int}\) is replaced by the Kohn-Sham Slater determinant \(\psi^{aux}\) divided by an auxiliary one-body wavefunction \(\Gamma^{aux}(\vec{R})\), which restores the correct dimensionality and subtracts the spurious c.m. vibration present in the Slater determinant. The resulting functional is then expressed in terms of the Kohn-Sham orbitals, and the corresponding local potential is derived by functional differentiation.

## Key numbers

* For the harmonic oscillator case, the separation \(\psi^{aux}(\vec{r}_1,\dots,\vec{r}_{N})=\Gamma^{aux}(\vec{R})\times F(\xi_1,\dots,\xi_{N-1})\) can always be achieved, as noted in reference to Irvine (1980).
* The c.m. correlations are stated to be non-negligible for all nuclei heavier than \(^{16}\)O, citing Schmid (2001), Maruhn (1983), and Schmid (1990).
* The commonly used approximation for the c.m. correlations functional is given as \(E_{cm} = -\sum_{i=1}^N (\varphi^{i}_{int}|\frac{\mathbf{p}^2}{2mN}|\varphi^{i}_{int}) - \frac{1}{2mN} \sum_{i,j=1}^N (\varphi^{i}_{int}|\mathbf{p}|\varphi^{i}_{int})(\varphi^{j}_{int}|\mathbf{p}|\varphi^{j}_{int})\), where the last term is often neglected in practice, citing Bender (2000).

## Context

This work extends the internal DFT formalism developed by Messud (2009, 2011), which provided an existence theorem for a local potential containing c.m. correlations but no constructive method. It contrasts with projection techniques such as the Peierls-Yoccoz method (1957) and the Peierls-Thouless method (1962), which are numerically expensive and have been applied only with the former, not restoring full Galilean invariance. The proposed functional aims to enable numerically manageable calculations for intermediate-sized self-bound systems, where c.m. correlations are non-negligible, and is intended to be added to commonly used functionals (e.g., Skyrme forces in nuclear physics, as described by Ring and Schuck (1980) and Bender (2003)).
