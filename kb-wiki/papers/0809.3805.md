# New fits to the reaction $γp\to K^+Λ$

> The reaction $γp\to K^+Λ$ has been investigated over the center-of-momentum energy, $W$, range from threshold up to 2.2 GeV in a tree-level effective Lagrangian model that incorporates most of the well-established baryon resonances with spins equal to or below 5/2. Four less well-established nucleon resonances of higher mass are also included. The fitted parameters consist, for each resonance included, of the products of the coupling strengths at the electromagnetic and strong interaction vertices and, for the less-established nucleon resonances, the total decay width. For the well-established nucleon resonances, the energy and momentum dependence of the widths is treated within a dynamical model that is normalized to give the empirical decay branching ratios on the resonance mass shells. For the less-established resonances, the total decay width is treated as a single parameter independent of the reaction kinematics. The model is used to fit recent data for the unpolarized differential cross section (CLAS), the induced hyperon polarization asymmetry, $P$ (CLAS, GRAAL, and SAPHIR), the beam spin asymmetry, $Σ$ (LEPS), and the double polarization observables $C_x$ and $C_z$ (CLAS). Two different fits were obtained: one that incorporates SU(3) symmetry constraints on the Born contributions to the reaction amplitude and one in which these constraints are relaxed. Explicit numerical results are given only for the first fit since the two fits gave nearly identical results for the observables and the $χ^2$ per degree of freedom obtained with the second fit was only marginally better than that of the first fit ($<1%$ better). Results are presented for the fitted observables at several different energies and center-of-momentum (c.m.) frame kaon angles.

## Key claim

The authors present a tree-level effective Lagrangian model for the reaction γp → K⁺Λ that incorporates baryon resonances with spins up to 5/2 in both the s- and u-channels, but the fits are restricted to center-of-momentum energies W from threshold to 2.2 GeV because no s-channel resonances with masses above 2.2 GeV are included in the model. Two fits were obtained, one with SU(3) symmetry constraints on the Born terms and one without, but the second fit yielded a χ² per degree of freedom less than 1% better than the first, so only the SU(3)-constrained fit results are presented.

## Method

The authors constructed a tree-level effective Lagrangian model for γp → K⁺Λ including s-channel nucleon resonances, u-channel hyperon resonances (Λ and Σ), and t-channel kaon and kaon-resonance (K*(892) and K1(1270)) exchanges. For well-established resonances (three- or four-star status), the energy and momentum dependence of the decay widths was treated dynamically using a model normalized to empirical on-shell branching ratios; for four less-established nucleon resonances (N(1900), N(2000), N(2080), N(2200)), the total decay width was treated as a single energy-independent fit parameter. The model was fitted to recent unpolarized differential cross section data from CLAS, induced hyperon polarization asymmetry P from CLAS, GRAAL, and SAPHIR, beam spin asymmetry Σ from LEPS, and double polarization observables Cx and Cz from CLAS.

## Key numbers

- The center-of-momentum energy range covered is from threshold up to 2.2 GeV.
- The χ² per degree of freedom for the fit without SU(3) constraints was less than 1% better than the fit with SU(3) constraints.
- The dimensionless electric charge used is e = 0.3029.
- The scaling mass msc used in t-channel vertices is set to 1000 MeV.
- The magnetic moment factors used are κp = -1.79 and κΛ = -0.729.
- The SU(3) parameter α used to relate the ΣKN and ΛKN couplings is α = 0.625.
- On-shell branching ratios for the N(1650) resonance: Nπ 0.77, Nη 0.06, ΛK 0.07, Δ(1232)π 0.03, Nρ 0.07.
- On-shell branching ratios for the N(1710) resonance: Nπ 0.15, Nη 0.06, ΛK 0.14, Nσ 0.25, Δ(1232)π 0.26, Nρ 0.14.

## Context

This work extends the effective Lagrangian models of Maxwell and collaborators (Maxwell 2000, 2001, 2003) by including spin-5/2 baryon resonances in both the s- and u-channels (previously only included in the s-channel by Saclay group analyses), and by fitting to recent polarization data from CLAS, GRAAL, SAPHIR, and LEPS that were not available for earlier fits. The model enables future extraction of electromagnetic form factors of baryon resonances by comparing photoproduction with electroproduction data, and provides a foundation for impulse-approximation studies of strangeness production from deuteron and light nuclear targets to investigate final-state interactions involving Λ and Σ baryons.
