# Tracing the evolution of nuclear forces under the similarity renormalization group

> I examine the evolution of nuclear forces under the similarity renormalization group (SRG) using traces of the many-body configuration-space Hamiltonian. While SRG is often said to "soften" the nuclear interaction, I provide numerical examples which paint a complementary point of view: the primary effect of SRG, using the kinetic energy as the generator of the evolution, is to shift downward the diagonal matrix elements in the model space, while the off-diagonal elements undergo significantly smaller changes. By employing traces, I argue that this is a very natural outcome as one diagonalizes a matrix, and helps one to understand the success of SRG.

## Key claim

The primary effect of the similarity renormalization group (SRG) on nuclear interactions, when using the kinetic energy as the generator, is to shift downward the diagonal matrix elements in the model space, while off-diagonal elements undergo significantly smaller changes; this is presented as a complementary perspective to the common description of SRG as "softening" the interaction.

## Method

The author performs no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for several p-shell nuclei using the Entem-Machleidt chiral effective interaction at N3LO, evolved via SRG in relative momentum space to various values of the evolution parameter s. The many-body Hamiltonians are constructed in a Slater determinant basis of harmonic oscillator states with ℏΩ = 20 MeV and truncated at Nmax = 8, 10, or 12 excitations, using the BIGSTICK code for diagonalization. Spectral distribution theory (SDT) is then applied to analyze the evolution of traces, centroids, and configuration centroids of the many-body Hamiltonian.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ¹²C drops by approximately 90 MeV when evolving from s=0 (bare force) to s=36.3 GeV⁻² (λ=2.0 fm⁻¹) in an Nmax=8 space at ℏΩ=20 MeV.
- For ⁶Li at Nmax=8, the ground state energy change ΔE_g.s. = 25.97 MeV and the centroid change ΔE_centroid = 16.81 MeV under the same SRG evolution.
- For ¹²C at Nmax=8, ΔE_g.s. = 89.55 MeV and ΔE_centroid = 82.65 MeV.
- Overlap of ground state wave functions between s=0 and s=36.3 GeV⁻² for ¹²C at Nmax=8 is 0.860.
- Overlap for ⁶Li at Nmax=8 is 0.948.
- The Nmax=8 data shows ΔE_centroid ≈ 0.6 A(A-1) MeV across the p-shell nuclei studied.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of SRG evolution by applying spectral distribution theory (SDT), developed by French (1967) and others, to analyze the many-body Hamiltonian in a truncated model space. The results are consistent with earlier work by the author (2007) suggesting that induced many-body forces contribute primarily to monopole terms, and with the success of in-medium SRG approaches by Tsukiyama (2011) and Hergert (2016). The analysis enables a clearer interpretation of why SRG-evolved interactions improve convergence in NCSM calculations, and suggests that one might compute the evolution of the centroid separately in terms of number operators.
