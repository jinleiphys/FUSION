# Intrinsic operators for the translationally-invariant many-body problem

> The need to enforce fermionic antisymmetry in the nuclear many-body problem commonly requires use of single-particle coordinates, defined relative to some fixed origin. To obtain physical operators which nonetheless act on the nuclear many-body system in a Galilean-invariant fashion, thereby avoiding spurious center-of-mass contributions to observables, it is necessary to express these operators with respect to the translational intrinsic frame. Several commonly-encountered operators in nuclear many-body calculations, including the magnetic dipole and electric quadrupole operators (in the impulse approximation) and generators of SU(3) and Sp(3,R) symmetry groups, are bilinear in the coordinates and momenta of the nucleons and, when expressed in intrinsic form, become two-body operators. To work with such operators in a second-quantized many-body calculation, it is necessary to relate three distinct forms: the defining intrinsic-frame expression, an explicitly two-body expression in terms of two-particle relative coordinates, and a decomposition into one-body and separable two-body parts. We establish the relations between these forms, for general (non-scalar and non-isoscalar) operators bilinear in coordinates and momenta.

## Key claim

The paper establishes rigorous relations between three distinct forms of intrinsic operators bilinear in nucleon coordinates and momenta, the defining intrinsic-frame expression, an explicitly two-body expression in terms of two-particle relative coordinates, and a decomposition into one-body and separable two-body parts, for general (non-scalar and non-isoscalar) operators, thereby enabling translationally-invariant many-body calculations without spurious center-of-mass contributions.

## Method

The authors derive the transformation properties of bilinear operators (such as \( (\mathbf{x}\times\mathbf{x})_{L_0} \), \( (\mathbf{k}\times\mathbf{k})_{L_0} \), and \( (\mathbf{x}\times\mathbf{k})_{L_0} \)) from single-particle coordinates to the translational intrinsic frame, using spherical tensor coupling and isospin-reduced matrix elements. They then express these intrinsic operators in two equivalent forms: as two-body operators in terms of relative coordinates and momenta, and as a sum of one-body plus separable two-body operators, with explicit scale factors depending on the convention for relative center-of-mass coordinates. The formalism is extended to include the proton-neutron mass difference correction to the kinetic energy and to oscillator ladder operators for both two-body and many-body systems.

## Key numbers

- Isospin-reduced matrix element \( \langle 0 || \tau_1 - \tau_2 || 1 \rangle = -2\sqrt{3} \) (Rose convention) for the isovector relative dipole operator between \( T=0 \) and \( T=1 \) two-body states.
- Isospin-reduced matrix element \( \langle 1 || 2\vec{T} || 1 \rangle = 2\sqrt{2} \) (Rose convention) for the isovector bilinear operator between \( T=1 \) states.
- Relative mass deviation \( \delta_m = (m_p - m_n)/(m_p + m_n) \) used in the proton-neutron mass difference correction.
- Total nuclear mass including mass difference: \( M = [A + (Z-N)\delta_m]m_N \).
- Intrinsic kinetic energy decomposition: \( T' = \left( \frac{1}{1-\delta_m^2} - \frac{1}{A+(Z-N)\delta_m} \right) T_\text{IS} - \frac{\delta_m}{1-\delta_m^2} T_\text{IV} - \frac{1}{A+(Z-N)\delta_m} \sum_{i\neq j} \frac{\hbar^2 \mathbf{k}_i \cdot \mathbf{k}_j}{2m_N} \).
- Relative oscillator length: \( b_\text{rel} = \sqrt{2}\,b \) under the mechanics convention, \( b_\text{rel} = b \) under the symmetric convention.
- Center-of-mass oscillator length: \( b_\text{cm} = b/\sqrt{A} \) under the mechanics convention, \( b_\text{cm} = b \) under the symmetric convention.
- Summed squared intrinsic radius operator: \( \sum_i x_i^{\prime 2} = \frac{2}{A} V\left[ \frac{1}{2} x_\text{rel}^2 \right] \) (mechanics convention) or \( \frac{2}{A} V\left[ x_\text{rel}^2 \right] \) (symmetric convention).
- Intrinsic kinetic energy one-body plus separable two-body form: \( T' = \frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\left(1-\frac{1}{A}\right) U[k^2] - \frac{\hbar^2}{2m}\frac{1}{A} V[2\mathbf{k}_1\cdot\mathbf{k}_2] \).

## Context

This work extends the formalism of translationally-invariant operators in nuclear many-body theory, building on prior developments by Elliott (1955) for center-of-mass corrections in oscillator-basis calculations and by Henley (1969) for isovector corrections to the kinetic energy from the proton-neutron mass difference. The results enable practical implementation of intrinsic operators in no-core configuration interaction (NCCI) calculations, as demonstrated by Cockrell (2012) for lithium isotopes, and provide the necessary operator relations for SU(3) and Sp(3,R) symmetry generators used in algebraic approaches to nuclear structure.
