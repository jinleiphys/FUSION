# Relativistic spectral function of nucleon in hot nuclear matter

> We present a simple calculation of the nucleon self-energy in nuclear matter at finite temperature in a relativistic framework, using the real time thermal field theory. The imaginary parts of one-loop graphs are identified with discontinuities across the unitary and the Landau cuts. We find that in general both the cuts contribute significantly to the spectral function in the region of (virtual) nucleon mass usually considered, even though the unitary cut is ignored in the literature. Also our relativistic spectral function differs from the one in non-relativistic approximation, used in some earlier calculations.

# Relativistic spectral function of nucleon in hot nuclear matter

## Key claim
The unitary cut from the πΔ loop contributes significantly to the nucleon spectral function in the upper region of virtual nucleon mass (p0 ≲ 1.5 GeV), even though this cut is ignored in the literature; the relativistic spectral function differs from the non-relativistic approximation used in some earlier calculations.

## Method
The authors calculate the nucleon self-energy at finite temperature and chemical potential using one-loop graphs with πN and πΔ(1232) intermediate states in the real-time formulation of thermal field theory. The imaginary parts of the self-energy are identified with discontinuities across unitary cuts (p0 ≥ m + mπ) and Landau cuts (0 ≤ p0 ≤ m − mπ), and the spectral function is obtained from the Dyson equation for the complete propagator. The model uses effective Lagrangians from chiral perturbation theory with phenomenological form factors, and the coupling constants are fixed by fitting the πN scattering phase shift in the P33 channel to experimental data.

## Key numbers
- Pion decay constant Fπ = 93 MeV, used in the effective Lagrangians.
- Δ resonance pole mass mΔ = 1210 MeV and width Γ(mΔ) = 100 MeV, used to fit the P33 phase shift.
- Coupling constants gA = 1.26 and gΔ = 2.2, with cutoff Λ = 400 MeV for the form factors.
- The calculation restricts p0 ≲ 1.5 GeV, so only N and Δ contribute to the unitary cut.
- The form factor is F(|k|) = Λ²/(Λ² + k²) in the center-of-mass frame, with Λ = 400 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the study of nucleon self-energy in hot nuclear matter beyond the non-relativistic approximation used by earlier calculations such as Post (year not given) and the virial expansion approach of Leutwyler and Smilga (year not given), which treated on-shell nucleons in pionic medium. The authors compare their results with those of Leutwyler and Smilga, finding good agreement when nucleon and Δ distribution functions are set to zero, validating their model. The calculation also contrasts with Hees and Rapp (year not given), who used imaginary-time formulation and considered only the Landau cut while including higher mass resonances. This work enables more accurate determination of the nucleon spectral function at temperatures and chemical potentials relevant to heavy-ion collisions.
