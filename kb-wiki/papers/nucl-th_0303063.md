# Vector mesons in a relativistic point-form approach

> We apply the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics to develop a Poincare invariant coupled-channel formalism for two-particle systems interacting via one-particle exchange. This approach takes the exchange particle explicitly into account and leads to a generalized eigenvalue equation for the Bakamjian-Thomas type mass operator of the system. The coupling of the exchange particle is derived from quantum field theory. As an illustrative example we consider vector mesons within the chiral constituent quark model in which the hyperfine interaction between the confined quark-antiquark pair is generated by Goldstone-boson exchange. We study the effect of retardation in the Goldstone-boson exchange by comparing with the commonly used instantaneous approximation. As a nice physical feature we find that the problem of a too large $ρ$-$ω$ splitting can nearly be avoided by taking the dynamics of the exchange meson explicitly into account.

## Key claim

The paper claims that taking the dynamics of the exchange meson explicitly into account (rather than using the instantaneous approximation) nearly avoids the problem of a too large ρ-ω mass splitting in the chiral constituent quark model, but this result is presented as a "nice physical feature" of the illustrative example rather than a definitive solution.

## Method

The authors apply the point form of relativistic quantum mechanics to develop a Poincaré invariant coupled-channel formalism for two-particle systems interacting via one-particle exchange, deriving the coupling from quantum field theory. They construct a Bakamjian-Thomas type mass operator acting on a direct-sum Hilbert space of two-particle and two-plus-one-particle states, then reduce the two-channel problem to a one-channel equation with an eigenvalue-dependent optical potential. For the specific application to vector mesons, they expand the quark-antiquark wave functions in a harmonic oscillator basis and solve the resulting coupled algebraic equations using Monte Carlo integration, with the eigenvalues determined by a resonance condition.

## Key numbers

- Constituent quark masses: m_u = m_d = 340 MeV (adopted from the chiral constituent quark model of Gletal98b)
- Statistical errors in Monte Carlo integrations kept smaller than 1 MeV (about one per mille)
- The nonrelativistic reduction of the optical potential yields the standard pseudoscalar meson-exchange potential V_NR with form factor f^2[q^2]/(4 m_1 m_2) times (σ_1·q)(σ_2·q)/(q^2 + m_Mes^2)

## Context

This work extends the point-form relativistic quantum mechanics approach previously applied to baryon electroweak structure by the Graz group (Gletal01, Waetal01, Boetal02) and builds on the velocity-state formalism introduced by Klink (1998) and Karmanov (1998). The paper enables future applications of the coupled-channel formalism to other two-particle systems and provides a fully relativistic comparison to the semirelativistic instantaneous approximation used in earlier vector meson calculations (Th98), showing that retardation effects from the explicit exchange-meson dynamics can resolve the ρ-ω splitting problem.
