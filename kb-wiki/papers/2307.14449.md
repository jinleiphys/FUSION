# Fluid dynamics of charm quarks in the quark--gluon plasma

> A fluid-dynamic approach to charm-quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) is developed for the first time. Results for integrated yields and momentum distributions of charmed hadrons obtained with a fluid-dynamic description for the dynamics of the QGP coupled to an additional heavy-quark-antiquark current are shown. In addition to the thermodynamic Equation of State (EoS), this description uses a heavy-quark diffusion constant which we take from Lattice QCD calculations. The results describe quantitatively experimental data measured at the LHC at the center-of-mass energy of $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$ = 5.02 TeV up to $p_{\rm T}\sim$ 4-5 GeV/$c$, showing that charm quarks undergo a very fast hydrodynamization in the medium created by ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper develops a fluid-dynamic description of charm-quark diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma for the first time, claiming that charm quarks undergo a very fast hydrodynamization. The hedge is that this description is relevant only for the low transverse momentum region (up to pT ~ 4-5 GeV/c), while at higher momentum, path-length-dependent energy loss mechanisms dominate the shape of the pT spectra.

## Method

The authors solve conservation equations for the energy-momentum tensor and an additional conserved current associated with charm-anticharm pairs, using a second-order hydrodynamic formalism in 1+1 dimensions (Bjorken coordinates with boost and azimuthal rotation invariance). Initial conditions for the entropy density are computed using the T_RENTo model for Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV, while charm fields are initialized from hard production using FONLL cross-sections. The heavy-quark spatial diffusion coefficient D_s is taken from Lattice QCD calculations, and charmed-hadron production is computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription on a freeze-out hypersurface at constant temperature.

## Key numbers

- Center-of-mass energy: sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV for Pb-Pb collisions
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamic evolution: tau_0 = 0.4 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: T_fo = 156.5 MeV
- Hard production cross-section at y=0: dsigma^{QbarQ}/dy = 0.463 mb (from FONLL)
- Inelastic proton-proton cross-section: sigma^{in} = 67.6 mb
- Nucleon-nucleon cross-section for Pb-Pb: 67.6 mb
- Woods-Saxon radius for Pb: R = 6.65 fm, surface thickness a = 0.54 fm
- D^0 yield increases by factor 4.3 due to resonance decays; Lambda_c^+ yield increases by factor 5
- A deviation of 2.4 sigma is observed for Lambda_c^+ baryons compared to experimental data
- The model describes experimental data up to pT ~ 4-5 GeV/c for D mesons and up to pT < 3 GeV/c for J/psi

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Capellino 2022) on charm-quark hydrodynamization time in an expanding medium, which motivated the fluid-dynamic approach. It contrasts with traditional Boltzmann/Langevin transport models used by Beraudo (2018), Nahrgang (2014), and others. The approach is supported by Lattice QCD calculations (Altenkort 2023) and the Statistical Hadronization Model for charm (Andronic 2021). The results enable future Bayesian analyses to constrain the spatial diffusion coefficient using both pT-differential spectra and anisotropic flow coefficients, and pave the way for a fluid-dynamic description of beauty quarks.
