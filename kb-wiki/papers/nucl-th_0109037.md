# Transverse Momentum Distribution of J/Psi's in Pb-Pb Collisions and Gluon Rescattering

> The transverse momentum dependence of J/Psi suppression in relativistic heavy ion collisions is calculated from initial state gluon rescattering not only with nucleons but also with prompt gluons produced in nucleon-nucleon collisions in the early phase of the reaction. The second mechanism is new and turns out to be important in a description of the data for central collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the anomalous transverse momentum broadening of J/Psi in central Pb-Pb collisions can be explained by initial-state gluon rescattering not only on nucleons but also on prompt bremsstrahl gluons produced in nucleon-nucleon collisions, a mechanism that had not been considered before. The authors qualify that while this initial-state mechanism can describe the data, a previous final-state explanation (Huefner and Kopeliovich, 2001) also achieves satisfactory agreement after adjusting one parameter in each calculation.

## Method

The authors calculate the mean squared transverse momentum of J/Psi produced in Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV by extending a two-channel model for the evolution of a c-cbar pair. They add a term for gluon-gluon (gg) scattering in the initial state to the standard gluon-nucleon (gN) scattering term, where the prompt gluons are produced in inelastic nucleon-nucleon collisions and their number is proportional to the number of wounded nucleons. The average transverse momentum at fixed transverse energy E_t is obtained by integrating over the collision geometry using a suppression kernel that includes both final-state absorption by nucleons and prompt gluons, and the effect of transverse energy fluctuations is included via a Gaussian distribution.

## Key numbers

- The isolated NN contribution to the mean squared transverse momentum is ⟨p_t^2⟩_NN = 1.11 (GeV/c)^2.
- The gN contribution parameter is ⟨p_t^2⟩_gN/λ_gN = 0.081 (GeV/c)^2/fm, extracted from experiments with light projectiles (NA50, 2000).
- The gg contribution parameter is set to ⟨p_t^2⟩_gg/λ_gg = (1/35) × ⟨p_t^2⟩_gN/λ_gN, determined by fitting the data in the E_t region where prompt gluons become important.
- The inelastic NN cross section used is σ_in^NN = 32 mb, and the nuclear density is ρ_0 = 0.17/fm^3.
- The J/Psi absorption cross section on nucleons is σ_abs^ΨN = 6.7 mb, and the initial (τ→0) absorption cross section is σ_in^pre = 3 mb.
- The mean number of prompt gluons per NN collision is n_g^0 = 0.75, with a formation time t_0 = 0.6 fm/c.
- The ratio of the J/Psi-gluon to J/Psi-nucleon absorption cross sections, weighted by the number of prompt gluons, is n_g^0 · σ_abs^Ψg / σ_abs^ΨN = 0.56.

## Context

This work extends the prompt-gluon model previously developed by Huefner and Kopeliovich (2001) for anomalous J/Psi suppression to also describe the transverse momentum distribution. It contrasts with the final-state explanation proposed by Huefner and Kopeliovich (2001) where high-p_t J/Psi escape absorption in a quark-gluon plasma or comover environment. The calculation uses the same parameters and notation as the earlier suppression model (Huefner and Kopeliovich, 2001) and compares with NA50 collaboration data (2000) on Pb-Pb collisions at 158 A GeV. The approach enables a unified description of both the suppression and the transverse momentum broadening within a single framework, though the authors note that the anomaly in ⟨p_t^2⟩ is only about 10% while the anomaly in suppression is about 50%.
