# Alignment of wave functions for angular momentum projection

> Angular momentum projection is used to obtain eigen states of angular momentum from general wave functions. Multi-configuration mixing calculation with angular momentum projection is an important microscopic method in nuclear physics. For accurate multi-configuration mixing calculation with angular momentum projection, concentrated distribution of $z$ components $K$ of angular momentum in the body-fixed frame ($K$-distribution) is favored. Orientation of wave functions strongly affects $K$-distribution. Minimization of variance of $\hat{J}_z$ is proposed as an alignment method to obtain wave functions that have concentrated $K$-distribution. Benchmark calculations are performed for $α$-$^{24}$Mg cluster structure, triaxially superdeformed states in $^{40}$Ar, and Hartree-Fock states of some nuclei. The proposed alignment method is useful and works well for various wave functions to obtain concentrated $K$-distribution.

## Key claim

Minimization of the variance of the angular momentum operator \(\hat{J}_z\) is proposed as a general alignment method to concentrate the \(K\)-distribution (distribution of the \(z\)-component of angular momentum in the body-fixed frame) of wave functions to fewer components, which improves the accuracy of multi-configuration mixing calculations with angular momentum projection. The method is claimed to work well for spherical, deformed, cluster, and triaxially deformed nuclei, but the abstract glosses over the condition that for some well-deformed nuclei the simpler diagonalization of the moment-of-inertia matrix gives similar results.

## Method

The author defines a variance matrix \(\mathsf{V}^J\) of the angular momentum operator with components \(V_{\sigma\rho}^J = \langle (\hat{J}_\sigma \hat{J}_\rho + \hat{J}_\rho \hat{J}_\sigma)/2 \rangle - \langle \hat{J}_\sigma \rangle \langle \hat{J}_\rho \rangle\). The wave function is rotated by Euler angles \((\alpha, \beta, \gamma)\) such that the variance matrix is diagonalized, thereby minimizing the variance of \(\hat{J}_z\). Benchmark calculations are performed using deformed-basis antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) wave functions for an \(\alpha\)-\(^{24}\)Mg cluster structure in \(^{28}\)Si, a triaxially superdeformed state in \(^{40}\)Ar, and Hartree-Fock (HF) states of several light nuclei, with the Gogny D1S effective interaction.

## Key numbers

- \(^{28}\)Si \(\alpha\)-\(^{24}\)Mg cluster: quadrupole deformation parameters \((\beta_2, \gamma_2) = (0.49, 42.8^\circ)\); the wave function aligned by variance minimization contains only even \(K\)-components, while the moment-of-inertia method gives a wide distribution over even and odd \(K\).
- \(^{40}\)Ar triaxial superdeformed state: \((\beta_2, \gamma_2) = (0.48, 11.2^\circ)\); both alignment methods give similar \(K\)-distribution with \(K = 0\) and \(\pm 2\) components.
- \(^{17}\)O HF state: \(\beta_2 = 0.04\), \(\gamma_2 = 57.0^\circ\); variance minimization yields only \(K = 5/2\) components, whereas moment-of-inertia alignment distributes \(K\) from \(-3/2\) to \(5/2\).
- \(^{17}\)O: overlap of the \(K = 1/2\) and \(3/2\) components after angular momentum projection to \(J = 5/2\) is 0.9999, causing numerical cancellation when using moment-of-inertia alignment.
- \(^{16}\)N HF state: \(\beta_2 = 0.09\), \(\gamma_2 = 59.1^\circ\); variance minimization gives only \(K = 3\) components; moment-of-inertia alignment gives dominant \(K = 3\) but also \(K = 2\) components.
- \(^{12}\)C HF state: \((\beta_2, \gamma_2) = (0.25, 59.8^\circ)\); both methods give only \(K = 0\) components.
- \(^{13}\)C HF state: \((\beta_2, \gamma_2) = (0.00, 19.0^\circ)\); both methods give only \(K = 1/2\) components.

## Context

This work extends the standard practice of aligning deformed wave functions by diagonalizing the moment-of-inertia matrix (used in many AMD and HF-GCM calculations) to a more general method based on angular momentum variance. The proposed method is particularly advantageous for multipole-deformed cluster structures (e.g., \(\alpha\)-\(^{24}\)Mg) and spherical odd or odd-odd nuclei (e.g., \(^{17}\)O, \(^{15}\)N, \(^{16}\)N), where the moment-of-inertia method fails to concentrate the \(K\)-distribution. The results enable more accurate multi-configuration mixing calculations (such as those in Monte Carlo shell model, HF-GCM, FMD, and AMD) by reducing the number of nonorthogonal \(K\)-components that must be mixed, thereby avoiding numerical cancellation.
