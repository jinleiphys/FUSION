# The Coulomb Sum and Proton-Proton Correlations in Few-Body Nuclei

> In simple models of the nuclear charge operator, measurements of the Coulomb sum and the charge form factor of a nucleus directly determine the proton-proton correlations. We examine experimental results obtained for few-body nuclei at Bates and Saclay using models of the charge operator that include both one- and two-body terms. Previous analyses using one-body terms only have failed to reproduce experimental results. However, we find that the same operators which have been used to successfully describe the charge form factors also produce substantial agreement with measurements of the Coulomb sum.

## Key claim

The authors find that including both one- and two-body terms in the nuclear charge operator produces substantial agreement with experimental measurements of the Coulomb sum in few-body nuclei, contradicting earlier analyses that used only one-body terms and failed to reproduce the data; however, the extracted longitudinal-longitudinal distribution function (LLDF) cannot provide direct information on short-range proton-proton repulsion because of the complicated nature of the photon-nucleus coupling.

## Method

The authors calculated the Coulomb sum and longitudinal-longitudinal distribution function (LLDF) for A=3 and A=4 nuclei using exact Faddeev (A=3) and Green's function Monte Carlo (A=4) wave functions from the Argonne v14 two-nucleon and Urbana VIII three-nucleon interactions. They included a charge operator with one-body terms (proton, neutron, Darwin-Foldy, and spin-orbit relativistic corrections) and two-body meson-exchange terms (pion, rho, omega, rho-pi-gamma, and omega-pi-gamma mechanisms), then compared the results to experimental longitudinal response data from Bates and Saclay.

## Key numbers

- The two-body components enhance the energy-weighted sum rule W_L(k) by 10% (6.0%), 8.6% (4.3%), and 7.5% (3.4%) in ^3H (^3He) at k = 300, 400, and 500 MeV/c, respectively
- The two-body components enhance W_L(k) by 9.1%, 7.8%, and 7.4% in ^4He at k = 300, 400, and 500 MeV/c, respectively
- The power alpha(k) for the high-omega tail parameterization is in the range 2.8-3.5 for A=3 nuclei and 2.6-3.1 for A=4 nuclei at k = 200-600 MeV/c
- The diffraction minima in the ^3He and ^4He charge form factors occur at k approximately 630 MeV/c, while the minima in the LLDF occur at significantly lower momentum, k approximately 370 MeV/c
- The experimental ^3H Coulomb sum S_L(k) is larger by approximately 10% in the k = 350-500 MeV/c range than the theoretical prediction

## Context

This work extends earlier analyses by Beck (1990) who found large discrepancies between experimental and calculated proton-proton distribution functions (PPDF) using only one-body charge operators, and by Doyle et al. (1992) who showed that different realistic two-nucleon interactions give similar Faddeev results. The present results indicate that the LLDF extracted from inclusive electron scattering cannot provide direct information on short-range proton-proton repulsion, leaving the experimental evidence for such correlations elusive.
