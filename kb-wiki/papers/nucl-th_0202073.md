# Potential renormalization by tunneling induced intrinsic transition

> The screening effects by bound target electrons in low energy nuclear reactions in laboratory experiments can be well represented in terms of a constant shift of the tunneling potential barrier as postulated in all previous analyses if the electronic cloud were static or structureless and lied outside the tunneling region. In this paper, using a semiclassical mean field analysis of a simple two-level coupled-channels problem, we suggest that there could occur a strong quantum transition of electrons from excited to the ground states in the tunneling region, leading to a spatially dependent screening potential. We thus show the necessity of the dynamical treatment of the tunneling region in order to properly assess the screening effects by bound target electrons in low energy nuclear reactions in laboratories.

# Potential renormalization by tunneling induced intrinsic transition

## Key claim

The paper suggests that, contrary to the standard assumption of a constant potential shift, the screening effects of bound target electrons in low-energy nuclear reactions may involve a strong quantum transition of electrons from excited to ground states within the tunneling region, leading to a spatially dependent screening potential that requires a dynamical treatment of the tunneling region.

## Method

The authors set up a two-level coupled-channels problem with a separable coupling Hamiltonian and analyze the tunneling process using a semiclassical mean field theory, where the macroscopic variable (relative motion between projectile and target) is treated classically and the intrinsic motion (electronic degrees of freedom) is treated quantum mechanically. The tunneling region is described using imaginary time, and the barrier transmission probability is calculated via the WKB formula with a position-dependent potential renormalization derived from the mean field approach. The results are compared against direct numerical integration of the coupled-channels equations.

## Key numbers

- Bare potential barrier height fixed to U0 = 10 MeV
- Mass parameter fixed to μc² = 2000 MeV
- Adiabaticity parameters studied: λ = 0.5 (fast tunneling) and λ = 2.0 (slow tunneling), where λ = ω/Ω with ω = ε2 − ε1 and Ω being the curvature of the bare potential barrier
- Coupling form factor position Xf taken as either 50 fm in front of the barrier center or 0 fm
- Extension parameters (a, af) = (5 fm, 5 fm) for λ = 0.5 and (15 fm, 5 fm) for λ = 2.0
- Incident kinetic energies chosen for detailed analysis: 8.74, 7.64, 9.4, and 6.87 MeV for the Xf = 50 fm case; 6.93, 5.0, 8.96, and 6.43 MeV for the Xf = 0 fm case

## Context

This work extends the study of macroscopic quantum tunneling with intrinsic degrees of freedom, previously applied to heavy-ion fusion reactions below the Coulomb barrier (Brink and Takigawa, 1998), to the problem of electron screening in low-energy nuclear reactions. The paper directly challenges the standard constant-potential-shift analysis used in all previous experimental studies of the screening effect, which has produced screening energies exceeding the adiabatic limit for all systems studied. The results enable future dynamical treatments of the tunneling region for realistic low-energy nuclear reactions, which the authors state they will publish in separate papers (Takigawa et al., 2001; Kimura et al., 2001).
