# Toroidal states in $^{28}$Si with covariant density functional theory in 3D lattice space

> The toroidal states in $^{28}$Si with spin extending to extremely high are investigated with the cranking covariant density functional theory on a 3D lattice. Thirteen toroidal states with spin $I$ ranging from 0 to 56$\hbar$ are obtained, and their stabilities against particle emissions are studied by analyzing the density distributions and potentials. The excitation energies of the toroidal states at $I=28$, 36, 44$\hbar$ reasonably reproduce the observed three resonances extracted from the 7-$α$ de-excitation of $^{28}$Si. The $α$ clustering of these toroidal states is supported by the $α$-localization function.

## Key claim

The cranking covariant density functional theory on a 3D lattice predicts thirteen toroidal states in $^{28}$Si with spins from 0 to 56$\hbar$, and the calculated excitation energies at $I=28$, 36, and 44$\hbar$ reasonably reproduce three observed resonances from the seven-$\alpha$ de-excitation channel, but the effective moment of inertia extracted from the calculated energies (14.5 MeV$^{-1}\hbar^2$) differs from the experimental value (22.2 MeV$^{-1}\hbar^2$), indicating that beyond-mean-field effects or improved single-particle level descriptions may be needed.

## Method

The authors solved the cranking covariant density functional theory (CDFT) with the PC-PK1 density functional on a 3D Cartesian lattice (step size 0.8 fm, grid 34×34×24), using an inverse Hamiltonian method to avoid variational collapse and Fourier spectral methods to avoid Fermion doubling. Toroidal initial states were constructed by placing seven $^{4}$He wavefunction sets along a ring in the $z=0$ plane, and self-consistent calculations were performed at different rotational frequencies with a damped cranking term to suppress unphysical continuum occupations. Fixed toroidal configurations were then constructed from the single-particle "toroidal levels" and calculated self-consistently.

## Key numbers

- Thirteen toroidal states obtained with spin $I$ ranging from 0 to 56$\hbar$.
- For the toroidal state at $I=44\hbar$, the rotational frequency region where both protons and neutrons occupy the lowest levels is 2.31 to 3.31 MeV.
- The width of the cross section of the torus ring is $d \sim 1.3$ fm for all toroidal states, close to the $\alpha$ particle width $d_\alpha = 1.46$ fm in Brink's $\alpha$-cluster model.
- Three observed resonances at excitation energies 112.7, 125.4, and 138.7 MeV are assigned to toroidal states at $I=28$, 36, and 44$\hbar$, respectively.
- The effective centrifugal barrier for the unbound neutron level with $m_z^\pi=11/2^-$ at $I=44\hbar$ is around 9 MeV.
- Effective moments of inertia: 22.2 MeV$^{-1}\hbar^2$ from experimental energies, 14.5 MeV$^{-1}\hbar^2$ from calculated energies.
- The $\alpha$-localization function $\mathcal{C}_\alpha$ values around the maximum density are larger than 0.9 for toroidal states, compared to about 0.5 for the ground state.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of toroidal states in light nuclei using Skyrme DFT (Ichikawa 2012 for $^{40}$Ca; Staszczak 2014, 2015 for nuclei with $28 \leq A \leq 52$) by employing a covariant DFT on a 3D lattice, which provides a self-consistent treatment of time-odd fields and allows examination of stability against nucleon emission. The calculations directly support the interpretation of three resonances observed in deep-inelastic collisions of $^{28}$Si on $^{12}$C (Cao 2019) as high-spin toroidal states, and rule out the linear chain structure of seven-$\alpha$ clusters as an alternative explanation. The results enable future experimental searches for additional toroidal states (e.g., at $I=56\hbar$) and motivate beyond-mean-field studies of $\alpha$ clustering in these exotic configurations.
