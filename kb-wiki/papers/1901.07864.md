# Higher-order corrections to heavy-quark jet quenching

> We calculate higher-order corrections to the quenching factor of heavy-quark jets due to hard, in-medium splittings in the framework of the BDMPS-Z formalism. These corrections turn out to be sensitive to a single mass-scale $m_\ast = (\hat q L)^{1/2}$, where $\hat q$ is the medium transport coefficient and $L$ the path length, and allow to draw a distinction between the way light, with $m < m_\ast$ (in contrast to massless $m=0$), and genuinely heavy, with $m > m_\ast$, quark jets are quenched in the medium. We show that the corrections to the quenching factor at high energies are double-logarithmic and qualitatively of the same order as for the massless quark jet.

## Key claim

Higher-order corrections to the quenching factor of heavy-quark jets are double-logarithmic and qualitatively of the same order as for massless quark jets, but the mass introduces a critical scale \( m_\ast = (\hat q L)^{1/2} \) that distinguishes genuinely heavy quarks (\( m > m_\ast \)) from light quarks (\( m < m_\ast \)) in how they are quenched in the medium.

## Method

The authors calculate higher-order corrections to the jet quenching factor within the BDMPS-Z formalism by expanding the generalized quenching weight in the strong coupling constant. They compute the energy loss of a single massive quark and of a heavy quark-gluon dipole (color-charged antenna) in the multiple-soft scattering approximation, using Laplace-space rate equations and accounting for decoherence times that control interference effects. The next-to-leading correction involves integrating the Altarelli-Parisi splitting function over the phase space of hard, in-medium splittings, with the dead-cone angle regulating collinear divergences.

## Key numbers

- The critical mass scale separating light from genuinely heavy quarks is \( m_\ast = (\hat q L)^{1/2} \), where \( \hat q \) is the medium transport coefficient and \( L \) the path length.
- The dead-cone angle is \( \Theta_{\rm dc} = m/E \), where \( E \equiv p_T \) is the jet energy.
- The characteristic energy scale for the massive quark spectrum is \( \omega_{\rm DC} = (\hat q / \Theta_{\rm dc}^4)^{1/3} = (\hat q \, p_T^4 / m^4)^{1/3} \).
- The critical energy for medium-induced emissions is \( \omega_c \sim \hat q L^2 \).
- The minimal medium resolution angle is \( \theta_c \sim (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2} \).
- The typical angle for soft gluon emissions is \( \theta_s \sim \bar\alpha^{-3/2} \theta_c \), where \( \bar\alpha = \alpha_s C_F/\pi \).
- The quenching factor for a single massive quark is \( \mathcal{Q}^{(0)}(p_T|m) = \exp[-2\bar\alpha L (\pi \hat q n / p_T)^{1/2}] \exp[2\bar\alpha L (\hat q m^2 / p_T^2)^{1/3}] \), where \( n \) is the effective power of the steeply falling spectrum.

## Context

This work extends the BDMPS-Z formalism for radiative energy loss (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1997) to include higher-order corrections for heavy-quark jets, building on the earlier analysis of massless quark/gluon jet quenching by Mehtar-Tani and Tywoniuk (2017). It generalizes the dead-cone effect first discussed by Dokshitzer and Kharzeev (2001) for single partons to the case of jet substructure, and enables predictions for the mass hierarchy of jet suppression at high transverse momenta accessible at the LHC. The results contrast with the behavior of single-inclusive heavy mesons, which show similar suppression to pions at low \( p_T \), and motivate further study of the transition between light and genuinely heavy quark regimes.
