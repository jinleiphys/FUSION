# Time-dependent and quasi-steady features of fast neutrino-flavor conversion

> Despite the theoretical indication that fast neutrino-flavor conversion (FFC) ubiquitously occurs in core-collapse supernova and binary neutron star merger, the lack of global simulations has been the greatest obstacle to study their astrophysical consequences. In this {\it Letter}, we present large-scale ($50 {\rm km}$) simulations of FFC in spherical symmetry by using a novel approach. We effectively rescale the oscillation scale of FFC by reducing the number of injected neutrinos in the simulation box, and then extrapolate back to the case of the target density of neutrinos with a convergence study. We find that FFC in all models achieves quasi-steady state in the non-linear regime, and its saturation property of FFC is universal. We also find that temporal- and spatial variations of FFC are smeared out at large radii due to phase cancellation through neutrino self-interactions. Finally, we provide a new diagnostic quantity, ELN-XLN angular crossing, to assess the non-linear saturation of FFC.

# Time-dependent and quasi-steady features of fast neutrino-flavor conversion

## Key claim
Fast neutrino-flavor conversion (FFC) in core-collapse supernova and binary neutron star merger environments achieves a quasi-steady state with universal saturation properties, but only when time-averaged over the non-linear regime; the system never reaches an exact steady state, and the universality holds specifically for the time-averaged flavor distributions, which are insensitive to the neutrino number density.

## Method
The authors performed large-scale (50 km) spherically-symmetric simulations of FFC using the GRQKNT neutrino transport code, solving the quantum kinetic equation in flat spacetime with vacuum and self-interaction Hamiltonians but neglecting matter interactions. To overcome the computational challenge of sub-centimeter oscillation wavelengths at realistic neutrino densities, they introduced a reduction factor Gamma applied to the neutrino number density at the inner boundary (50 km), ran four models with Gamma from 10^-4 to 8x10^-4, and extrapolated results to the target density (Gamma=1) via convergence study. The simulations covered radial domains up to 100 km with uniform grids of 24576 to 98304 points and 128 angular grid points (256 in one higher-resolution model), evolving for 0.5 ms (0.12 ms for the highest-density model).

## Key numbers
- Neutrino number density at inner boundary (50 km): 6 x 10^32 cm^-3, corresponding to luminosity L_nu ~ 4 x 10^52 erg/s for average energy E_ave ~ 12 MeV
- Reduction factors Gamma: 10^-4 (Model-Gamma1), 2x10^-4 (Model-Gamma2), 4x10^-4 (Model-Gamma4), 8x10^-4 (Model-Gamma8)
- Spatial domain: 50 km <= r <= 100 km (except Model-Gamma8: 50 km <= r <= 60 km)
- Radial grid points: 24576 (Model-Gamma1, Model-Gamma1h), 49152 (Model-Gamma2, Model-Gamma8), 98304 (Model-Gamma4)
- Angular grid points: 128 (all models except Model-Gamma1h with 256)
- Vacuum potential parameters: Delta m^2 = 2.5 x 10^-6 eV^2, mixing angle theta_mix = 10^-6, neutrino energy E_nu = 12 MeV
- Time evolution: 0.5 ms (0.12 ms for Model-Gamma8)
- Time-averaging window for quasi-steady state: 0.3 ms <= t <= 0.5 ms (0.06 ms <= t <= 0.12 ms for Model-Gamma8)
- Diluteness parameter eta for incoming neutrinos at outer boundary: 10^-6

## Context
This work extends local-box FFC simulations (e.g., Duan et al. 2017, Martin et al. 2019, Johns et al. 2020, Bhatt et al. 2021, Richers et al. 2021, Zaizen et al. 2021) by performing the first global simulations on >10 km scales in spherical symmetry, addressing the long-standing obstacle that FFC angular distributions depend on global geometry. The results contradict the expectation from classical neutrino transport that temporal variations from the inner region persist in the free-streaming region; instead, phase cancellation through self-interactions smears out fluctuations at large radii. The new diagnostic quantity, ELN-XLN angular crossing, is proposed as a more natural indicator of non-linear saturation than the standard ELN crossing, since heavy-lepton neutrinos and their antipartners acquire different angular distributions in the non-linear regime. This enables future work incorporating three-flavor treatments, neutrino emission/absorption, and general relativistic multi-dimensional transport.
