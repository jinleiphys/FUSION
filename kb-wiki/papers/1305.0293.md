# Fission properties of the BCPM functional

> We explore the properties of the Barcelona Catania Paris Madrid (BCPM) energy density functional concerning fission dynamics. Potential energy surfaces as well as collective inertias relevant in the fission process are computed for several nuclei where experimental data exists. Inner and outer barrier heights as well as fission isomer excitation energies are reproduced quite well in all the cases. The spontaneous fission half lives $t_{\textrm{\textrm{SF}}}$ are also computed using the standard semiclassical approach and the results are compared with the experimental data. A reasonable agreement with experiment is found over a range of 27 orders of magnitude but the theoretical predictions suffer from large uncertainties associated to the values of the parameters entering the spontaneous fission half life formula. The impact that increasing the pairing correlations strengths has in the spontaneous fission half lives is analyzed and found to be large in all the nuclei considered. Given the satisfactory description of the trend of fission properties with mass number we explore the fission properties of the even-even uranium isotope chain from $^{226}$U to $^{282}$U. Very large half lives are found when getting close to neutron number N=184.

## Key claim

The BCPM energy density functional reproduces the trend of spontaneous fission half-lives over 27 orders of magnitude reasonably well, but the theoretical predictions suffer from large uncertainties (up to 16 orders of magnitude deviation from experiment) when the parameters entering the WKB formula, particularly the pairing strength and the ground-state energy parameter \(E_0\), are varied.

## Method

The authors used the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) theory with axial symmetry and constraining fields (primarily the quadrupole moment \(Q_{20}\)) to compute potential energy surfaces and collective inertias for fission. Spontaneous fission half-lives were calculated using the WKB semiclassical formalism with both the adiabatic time-dependent HFB (ATDHFB) and generator coordinate method (GCM) approximations for the collective inertia. The impact of varying the pairing correlation strength (by scaling factors \(\eta = 1.00, 1.05, 1.10\)) and the ground-state energy parameter \(E_0\) (1.0 and 1.5 MeV) was systematically studied.

## Key numbers

- Inner barrier heights \(B_I\) for BCPM range from 5.87 MeV (\({}^{234}\)U) to 8.65 MeV (\({}^{250}\)Cf), typically 1–2 MeV larger than experimental values (e.g., 4.80 MeV for \({}^{234}\)U).
- Outer barrier heights \(B_{II}\) for BCPM range from 3.84 MeV (\({}^{252}\)Cf) to 6.54 MeV (\({}^{238}\)U), with RMS deviation \(\sigma(B_{II}) = 0.72\) MeV compared to experiment.
- Fission isomer excitation energies \(E_{II}\) for BCPM range from 0.83 MeV (\({}^{252}\)Cf) to 2.47 MeV (\({}^{244}\)Pu), with RMS deviation \(\sigma(E_{II}) = 0.57\) MeV.
- For \({}^{234}\)U, the GCM spontaneous fission half-life with standard parameters (\(\eta=1.0\), \(E_0=1.0\) MeV) is \(t_{\text{sf}} = 2.3 \times 10^{38}\) s; increasing pairing to \(\eta=1.05\) reduces it to \(8.0 \times 10^{27}\) s, and to \(\eta=1.10\) reduces it further to \(6.7 \times 10^{21}\) s.
- For \({}^{234}\)U, the ATDHFB inertia yields \(t_{\text{sf}} = 7.8 \times 10^{52}\) s (standard parameters), compared to \(2.3 \times 10^{38}\) s with GCM inertia.
- The experimental spontaneous fission half-lives span 27 orders of magnitude (from \(4.7 \times 10^{23}\) s for \({}^{234}\)U to values for nuclei up to A=286).

## Context

This work extends the application of the BCPM energy density functional, previously fitted to binding energies of 518 even-even nuclei (Audi and Wapstra 2003 mass table), to fission dynamics. It compares BCPM results with the Gogny D1S and D1M parametrizations (Delaroche 2006; D1M) and with the UNEDF1 Skyrme variant (McDonnell 2013), finding that BCPM reproduces fission barrier trends without using any fission data in its fit. The study demonstrates that fission observables, especially spontaneous fission half-lives, are extremely sensitive to pairing correlations, suggesting that experimental fission data could be used to fine-tune pairing strengths as an alternative to traditional odd-even staggering methods. The work also explores the even-even uranium isotope chain from \({}^{226}\)U to \({}^{282}\)U, finding very large half-lives near neutron number N=184.
