# Gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations in Coulomb gauge

> I will discuss the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations for quantum chromodynamics in Coulomb gauge under a leading order truncation scheme. Within this scheme, the role of charge conservation and the cancellation of infrared divergences becomes particularly clear. The quark gap equation exhibits not only chiral symmetry breaking, but explicitly reproduces the heavy quark limit. The corresponding gluon equation has a massive solution with interesting nonperturbative renormalization properties. I will further discuss various aspects of the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and vector mesons with arbitrary quark masses and present numerical results for the meson masses and leptonic decay constants.

## Key claim

Under a leading-order truncation scheme in Coulomb gauge, the gap and Bethe-Salpeter equations for QCD produce finite, well-defined results for the gluon static propagator dressing, the quark mass function, and meson observables, because the charge constraint cancels infrared divergences; however, the numerical results for leptonic decay constants and chiral condensate are too small compared to experiment, indicating the truncation is qualitatively but not quantitatively accurate.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations in Coulomb gauge by replacing the nonlocal Coulomb kernel with its expectation value (related to the temporal gluon propagator) and restricting to one-loop terms with only the instantaneous Coulomb-like interaction, which includes a charge constraint term that enforces total color charge conservation. The gluon gap equation is derived for the static propagator dressing function G, the quark gap equation (Adler-Davis equation) is solved for the mass function M, and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar and vector mesons is reformulated in terms of finite dressing functions f and h, with all equations solved numerically in units of the string tension.

## Key numbers

- The gluon static propagator dressing function G has the form G_x = x/(x + kappa_x), where kappa_x is an infrared constant mass function that depends logarithmically on the ultraviolet cutoff Lambda; the scaled function bar-kappa(x') = kappa(x=x'a) - a (with a = kappa(x=0)) is independent of Lambda.
- The quark mass function M(p) is plotted for a range of quark masses; in the heavy quark limit, M approaches the bare quark mass m (M -> m).
- Pseudoscalar meson masses M_PS follow M_PS ~ sqrt(m) as the quark mass m -> 0, demonstrating chiral symmetry breaking.
- For one fixed chiral quark, as the other quark mass m -> infinity, f_PS * sqrt(M_PS) and f_V * sqrt(M_V) approach constants, reproducing the heavy quark limit.
- The leptonic decay constants f_PS and f_V are too small when typical values of the string tension are inserted, and the mass-splitting between pseudoscalar and vector states for larger quark masses is also too small.

## Context

This work extends the Coulomb gauge Hamiltonian approach of Szczepaniak and Swanson (2001) and the Adler-Davis gap equation (1984) to a fully covariant Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework, showing that the leading-order truncation qualitatively reproduces both chiral symmetry breaking and the heavy quark limit. The results enable future improvements by including the spatial quark-gluon vertex (as in Pak and Reinhardt, 2011) to increase the chiral condensate and decay constants, and by extending the truncation beyond leading order to improve quantitative agreement with lattice data and experiment.
