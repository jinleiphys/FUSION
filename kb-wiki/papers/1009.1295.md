# Efimov physics in bosonic atom-trimer scattering

> Bosonic atom-trimer scattering is studied in the unitary limit using momentum-space equations for four-particle transition operators. The impact of the Efimov effect on the atom-trimer scattering observables is explored and a number of universal relations is established. Positions and widths of tetramer resonances are determined. Trimer relaxation rate constant is calculated.

## Key claim

The paper establishes universal (interaction-independent) values for atom-trimer scattering observables in the unitary limit, but only for reactions involving highly excited trimers (at least the 2nd excited state); results for lower trimers show significant quantitative deviations from universal behavior.

## Method

The author solves the Alt, Grassberger and Sandhas (AGS) equations for four-particle transition operators in momentum-space partial-wave representation, using rank-1 separable two-boson potentials with Gaussian form factors acting only in S-wave. The equations are solved by direct matrix inversion rather than iterative methods to handle sharp four-boson resonances, and the calculations include higher angular momentum states up to l_y, l_z ≤ 2 for relative motion in the subsystems.

## Key numbers

- Trimer binding energy ratio b_{n-1}/b_n converges to 515.035 for highly excited states (n ≥ 3)
- Atom-trimer scattering length universal limit: a_n/l_n ≈ 22.6 − 1.09i
- Atom-trimer effective range universal limit: r_n/l_n ≈ 3.22 − 0.017i
- Ratio of inelastic cross sections σ(n→n')/σ(n→n'−1) ≈ 43.7 for large n, n'
- Tetramer resonance parameters for N ≥ 3: B_{N,1}/b_N ≈ 4.6108, Γ_{N,1}/2b_N ≈ 0.01484
- Tetramer resonance parameters for N ≥ 3: B_{N,2}/b_N ≈ 1.00228, Γ_{N,2}/2b_N ≈ 2.38×10^{-4}
- At E_n/b_n = 1, elastic cross section contributions: S-wave 31%, P-wave 58%, D-wave 11%

## Context

This work extends the universal Efimov physics framework from three-boson systems (Efimov trimers) to the four-boson continuum, providing the first study of atom-trimer scattering in the unitary limit. It confirms and refines the tetramer resonance positions predicted by Hammer (2007) and Stecher (2009), finding B_{N,1}/b_N ≈ 4.6108 compared to their value of 4.58, while the k=2 resonance is found significantly closer to the atom-trimer threshold than Stecher (2009) predicted. The results enable future experimental measurements of trimer relaxation rate constants in ultracold atom-trimer mixtures and can be extended to dimer-dimer scattering and fermionic systems.
