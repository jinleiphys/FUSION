# Two-flavor color superconductivity in a general Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model with color and charge neutrality

> By using a general NJL model including as many interaction channels as possible, and taking into account the constraints imposed by color and charge neutrality, we analyze how the different interaction channels contribute to the charge-neutral two-flavor color-superconducting matter. After taking the Fierz transformation, the number of parameters in the NJL model is reduced and thereby quark-antiquark condensates and diquark condensates are related. Through a self-consistent solution of the gap equations, we find that in addition to the diquark and vector channels, the scalar-isovector, vector-isovector, and vector-isovector-color-octet channels are also important, while other channels can be neglected. Moreover, between the normal quark matter phase and two-flavor color-superconducting phase, there is a gapless two-flavor color-superconducting phase. The fractions of quarks with different flavors and colors are calculated in both gaped and gapless two-flavor color-superconducting phases. In addition, We illustrate the phase diagrams in the diquark coupling and chemical potential plane, and discuss in detail how the dominant channels influence these phase diagrams.

## Key claim
The authors find that, in addition to the diquark and vector channels already studied in the literature, the scalar-isovector, vector-isovector, and vector-isovector-color-octet interaction channels also play significant roles in determining the phase structure of charge-neutral two-flavor color-superconducting matter, while other channels can be neglected. This conclusion depends on enforcing color and charge neutrality constraints, which enhance the importance of these channels.

## Method
The authors use a general Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model including as many interaction channels as possible, reduce the number of independent parameters via Fierz transformation, and impose color and charge neutrality conditions. They solve the gap equations self-consistently in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov mean-field approximation using the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) model to determine coupling constants, then vary individual channel couplings to study their effects on the phase diagram at zero temperature.

## Key numbers
- The critical endpoint (CEP) is estimated at diquark-to-scalar coupling ratio \(H/G_s^{(0)} = 1.1\) and chemical potential \(\mu = 312\,\text{MeV}\).
- The gapless two-flavor color-superconducting (g2SC) phase is delineated by two phase boundaries at \(H/G_s^{(0)} \approx 0.67\) and \(H/G_s^{(0)} \approx 0.81\).
- The scalar-isovector channel extends the gapless phase region from \(0.67 \lesssim H/G_s^{(0)} \lesssim 0.80\) to \(0.67 \lesssim H/G_s^{(0)} \lesssim 0.85\) in the vicinity of the chiral phase transition.
- The chiral transition occurs at \(\mu \approx 370\,\text{MeV}\) (estimated from the maximum slope of the effective mass).
- The transition from g2SC to gaped 2SC phase occurs at \(\mu \approx 480\,\text{MeV}\).
- In the 2SC phase, the approximate particle density fractions are \(n_{db}:n_{ur}:n_{dr}:n_{ub}:n_e \approx 8:4.5:4.5:1:1\), with electron fraction \(n_e/n \approx 1/27 \approx 0.037\).
- The model parameters are cutoff \(\Lambda = 631\,\text{MeV}\), current quark mass \(m = 5.5\,\text{MeV}\), and scalar coupling \(G_s^{(0)} = 2.188/\Lambda^2\), fitted to \(\langle\bar{u}u\rangle^{1/3} = -246\,\text{MeV}\), \(f_\pi = 93\,\text{MeV}\), and \(m_\pi = 138\,\text{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of two-flavor color superconductivity within the NJL model framework, particularly building on the work of Huang and Shovkovy (2003) and Shovkovy and Huang (2003) who studied the gapless 2SC phase, and Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek (1999) who established the color-flavor locked phase. The authors systematically include interaction channels often overlooked in prior NJL studies, such as those by Kitazawa et al. (2003) and the works on vector and isovector channels by Klahn et al. (2007). The results enable more realistic modeling of neutron star cores, where color and charge neutrality are essential constraints, and provide a basis for studying chromomagnetic instabilities and possible transitions to inhomogeneous phases such as the crystalline phase discussed in reviews by Alford, Schmitt, Rajagopal, and Schafer (2008).
