# Radiative Leptonic Decays of Heavy Mesons

> We compute the photon spectrum and the rate for the decays $B(D)\to lν_lγ$ These photonic modes constitute a potentially large background for the purely leptonic decays which are used to extract the heavy meson decay constants. While the rate for $D\to l\n\g$ is small, the radiative decay in the $B$ meson case could be of comparable magnitude or even larger than $B\to\m\n$. This would affect the determination of $f_B$ if the $τ$ channel cannot be identified. We obtain theoretical estimates for the photonic rates and disscuss their possible experimental implications.

## Key claim

The radiative leptonic decays \(B(D) \to l\nu_l\gamma\) could constitute a significant background to the purely leptonic decays used to extract heavy meson decay constants; while the rate for \(D \to l\nu\gamma\) is small, the radiative decay in the \(B\) meson case could be of comparable magnitude or even larger than \(B \to \mu\nu\), potentially affecting the determination of \(f_B\) if the \(\tau\) channel cannot be identified.

## Method

The authors computed the photon spectrum and decay rate for \(B(D) \to l\nu_l\gamma\) by considering two types of contributions: Internal Bremsstrahlung (IB), which remains helicity suppressed, and Structure Dependent (SD) photon emission, which avoids helicity suppression. The SD contributions were modeled via vector meson (\(B^*\)) and axial-vector meson (\(B_1', B_1\)) intermediate states, with couplings estimated using Heavy Quark Symmetry, the non-relativistic quark model, and Heavy Hadron Chiral Perturbation Theory including one-loop corrections from pion and kaon loops.

## Key numbers

* The purely leptonic decay \(B \to \mu\nu\) has a helicity suppression factor of \(4 \times 10^{-4}\), giving a branching fraction of \(10^{-7} - 10^{-6}\).
* The ratio \(R_B^{\mu} = \Gamma(B \to \mu\nu\gamma) / \Gamma(B \to \mu\nu)\) is approximately \(2 \mu_V^2 (1 + (\gamma_{1/2} + \gamma_{3/2})^2 / 2)\) GeV\(^2\), using mass differences \(\Delta_i = 600\) MeV.
* For \(D\) mesons, \(R_D^{\mu} \approx 4 \times 10^{-2} \mu_V^2 (1 + (\gamma_{1/2} + \gamma_{3/2})^2 / 2)\) GeV\(^2\).
* The \(B^* B \gamma\) coupling \(\mu_V\) is estimated as \(\mu_V \approx -2\) GeV\(^{-1}\) using \(m_b = 5\) GeV and \(\beta = 3\) GeV\(^{-1}\).
* Including form-factor suppression, the final estimate for \(R_B^{\mu}\) is in the range \((1-4) \mu_V^2 (1 + \frac{1}{2}(\gamma_{1/2} + \gamma_{3/2})^2)\) GeV\(^2\), leading to \(Br(B \to l\nu\gamma) \approx 10^{-7} - 10^{-6}\).
* For \(D\) mesons, including form-factor suppression gives \(R_D^{\mu} \approx (1-8) \times 10^{-2} \mu_V^2\) GeV\(^2\), translating to \(Br(D \to \mu\nu\gamma) \approx 10^{-5}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of radiative leptonic decays previously applied to light pseudoscalars like \(\pi \to l\nu\gamma\) and \(K \to l\nu\gamma\) by Goldman and Wilson (1977) to heavy mesons containing charm and beauty quarks. The results indicate that the photonic mode could dominate the purely leptonic decay in the \(B\) meson case, requiring careful subtraction for accurate extraction of \(f_B\) from \(B \to \mu\nu\) measurements. The paper enables future experimental searches for these decays at branching ratios of \(10^{-5} - 10^{-6}\) and suggests similar enhancements could occur in other decays such as \(B_s \to \mu^+ \mu^- \gamma\), which could constrain physics beyond the Standard Model.
