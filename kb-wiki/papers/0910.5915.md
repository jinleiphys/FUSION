# Holographic chiral shear waves from anomaly

> We study dispersion relations of hydrodynamic waves of hot N=4 SYM plasma at strong coupling with a finite U(1) R-charge chemical potential via holography. We first provide complete equations of motion of linearized fluctuations out of a charged AdS black-hole background according to their helicity, and observe that helicity +1 and -1 transverse shear modes receive a new parity-odd contribution from the 5D Chern-Simons term, which is dual to 4D U(1)^3 anomaly. We present a systematic solution of the helicity +1,-1 wave equations in long wave-length expansion, and obtain the corresponding dispersion relations. The results depend on the sign of helicity, which may be called chiral shear waves.

## Key claim

The dispersion relations for helicity +1 and -1 transverse shear modes in hot N=4 SYM plasma at strong coupling with a finite U(1) R-charge chemical potential receive a new parity-odd contribution from the 5D Chern-Simons term, which is dual to the 4D U(1)^3 anomaly; this results in chiral shear waves whose dispersion depends on the sign of helicity, with the first non-trivial anomalous effect appearing at order k^3.

## Method

The authors study linearized fluctuations around a charged AdS black-hole background in 5D gauged supergravity (Einstein gravity plus U(1) Maxwell theory with a Chern-Simons term), using Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates where regularity at the horizon implements the incoming boundary condition. They classify fluctuations by helicity under transverse SO(2) rotation, derive the complete equations of motion for helicity ±1 modes, and solve them systematically in a long wavelength expansion using an iterative integral technique. The dispersion relation is extracted by imposing UV normalizability at the asymptotic boundary, which yields an algebraic condition relating frequency ω and wave number k.

## Key numbers

- Dispersion relation for helicity ±1 modes: ω ≈ -i (r_H^3 / 4m) k^2 ± i (κ Q^3 / (8 m^2 r_H^3)) k^3 + O(k^4), where κ = 1/(3√3) for N=4 SYM, r_H is the horizon radius, m is the black hole mass parameter, and Q is the charge parameter.
- The leading order term ω ∼ -i (η/(ε+p)) k^2 is confirmed, with energy density ε = 3m/(16π G_5), pressure p = m/(16π G_5), and shear viscosity η = r_H^3/(16π G_5) = s/(4π).
- The Chern-Simons coupling κ is given as κ = 1/(3√3) for N=4 SYM.
- The 5D Newton constant is G_5 = π/(2 N_c^2).

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of chiral magnetic effects (Fukushima 2008, Kharzeev 2009, Yee 2009) and hydrodynamic constitutive relations with triangle anomalies (Son 2009, Lublinsky 2009) by computing dynamical dispersion relations for shear modes. The authors note that their results differ from a contemporaneous study by Matsuo (2009) on the same subject, particularly regarding the presence of the O(k^3) term. The computation provides a consistency check between the non-linear fluid/gravity technique (Bhattacharyya 2008, Rangamani 2009) and linearized quasi-normal mode analysis (Policastro 2002, Kovtun 2005, Son 2007), and enables future comparison with perturbative field theory calculations at weak coupling.
