# Distribution amplitudes of radially-excited pi- and K-mesons

> A symmetry-preserving truncation of the two-body bound-state problem in relativistic quantum field theory is used to compute the leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) for the first radial excitations of the $π$- and $K$-mesons. In common with ground states in these channels, the PDAs are found to be dilated with respect to the relevant conformal-limit form and skewed toward the heavier valence-quark in asymmetric systems. In addition, the PDAs of radially-excited pseudoscalar mesons are not positive definite, owing to the fact that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) forces the leptonic decay constant of such states to vanish in the chiral limit. These results highlight that DCSB is expressed visibly in every pseudoscalar meson constituted from light-quarks. Hence, so long as its impact is empirically evident in the pseudoscalar members of a given spectrum level, it is unlikely that chiral symmetry is restored in any of the hadrons that populate this level.

## Key claim

The leading-twist parton distribution amplitudes (PDAs) of radially-excited π- and K-mesons are not positive definite, and in the chiral limit the zeroth Mellin moment of the π₁ PDA vanishes identically because dynamical chiral symmetry breaking forces the leptonic decay constant of such states to zero, a result that holds independent of any and all parameters in a symmetry-preserving truncation.

## Method

The authors used a rainbow-ladder (RL) truncation of QCD's Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) to compute the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for the first radial excitations of π- and K-mesons. They then projected these covariant Bethe-Salpeter wave functions onto the light-front via Mellin moments, using a brute-force integration method with a regulator function to handle oscillatory integrands, and reconstructed the PDAs from the moments using a Gegenbauer polynomial expansion.

## Key numbers

- Chiral-limit π₁ mass: m⁰_π₁ = 1.26 GeV; chiral-limit π₁ leptonic decay constant: f⁰_π₁ = 0 (exact)
- K₁ mass: m_K₁ = 1.39 GeV; K₁ leptonic decay constant: f_K₁ = 6.7 MeV
- Current-quark masses used: hat m_u = hat m_d = 6.8 MeV, hat m_s = 162 MeV (one-loop evolved to ζ₂=2 GeV: m_u=d^ζ₂ = 4.7 MeV, m_s^ζ₂ = 112 MeV)
- π₁ PDA Gegenbauer expansion parameters at ζ₂=2 GeV: α = 1.21, a⁰ = 0, a² = 1.00 GeV, a⁴ = 0.180 GeV
- K₁⁺ PDA Gegenbauer expansion parameters at ζ₂=2 GeV: α = 1.30, a⁰ = 0.0285 GeV, a¹ = -0.0657 GeV, a² = 1.24 GeV, a³ = -0.703 GeV, a⁴ = 0.0813 GeV

## Context

This work extends DSE-based studies of ground-state meson PDAs (Chang 2013, Cloet 2013, Segovia 2013, Gao 2014, Shi 2015) to radial excitations, and directly contradicts the holographic QCD model of Brodsky (2014), which predicts identical PDAs for all pion radial excitations and nonzero decay constants for all n. The results demonstrate that dynamical chiral symmetry breaking (DCSB) is expressed visibly in every pseudoscalar meson constituted from light quarks, and that chiral symmetry is unlikely to be restored in any hadrons that populate a given spectrum level if its impact is empirically evident in the pseudoscalar members. The vanishing of chiral-limit decay constants for radially-excited pseudoscalars follows from the general Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation (Maris 1997, Qin 2014) and was previously established by Dominguez (1976, 1977), Holl (2004, 2005), and others.
