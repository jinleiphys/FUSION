# Nonlinear dynamics of jet quenching

> We present an analytic framework for jet quenching in dense QCD matter that unifies medium-induced branching with vacuum collinear evolution. Energy transported outside the jet region is governed by a non-linear rate equation that resums arbitrary-angle gluon splittings, each enhanced by the medium length $L$. We show that for asymptotically large energies the energy-loss distribution for a single hard parton exhibits an exponential (generalized Poisson) behavior that provides the initial condition for a non-linear, DGLAP-like evolution that resums collinear logarithms associated with early in-vacuum fragmentation and with the angular resolution of the medium. This framework allows a systematic resummation of parton branching contributions to jet energy loss.

## Key claim
The paper presents an analytic framework for jet quenching that unifies medium-induced branching with vacuum collinear evolution, claiming that for asymptotically large energies the energy-loss distribution for a single hard parton exhibits an exponential (generalized Poisson) behavior. The hedge is that this exponential behavior provides the initial condition for a non-linear, DGLAP-like evolution that resums collinear logarithms, but only under the condition that the medium resolution angle \(\theta_c = (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}\) defines the boundary between coherent and decoherent regimes.

## Method
The author derives a non-linear master equation (Eq. 4 in the text) for the quenching factor \(Q_\nu(E)\) in Laplace space, which resums arbitrary-angle gluon splittings enhanced by the medium length \(L\). The evolution is divided into two stages: a medium-induced cascade that resums \((\alpha_s L)^n\) contributions at angles larger than \(\theta_c\), and a vacuum-like collinear cascade that resums logarithms of \(R\) and \(R/\theta_c\). The matching between stages occurs at the medium resolution angle \(\theta_c = (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}\), where color decoherence sets in and partons begin to radiate independently.

## Key numbers
- The medium resolution angle is defined as \(\theta_c = (\hat q L^3)^{-1/2}\), where \(\hat q\) is the jet quenching parameter and \(L\) is the medium length.
- The characteristic scale for LPM suppression is \(\omega_c = \hat q L^2\), introduced by Baier (1996) and Zakharov (1996).
- The formation time for gluon emission is \(t_f \simeq \sqrt{\omega/\hat q}\), where \(\omega\) is the gluon energy.
- The elastic broadening keeps the leading parton inside the cone with high probability when \(E \gg \alpha_s^2 \omega_c\).
- The master equation resums contributions in \(\alpha_s \ln (R/\theta_c)\) and \((\alpha_s L)^n\) through the initial condition.

## Context
This work extends the independent-emission ansatz of Baier (2001) by recovering it as a limiting case (\(R \to 0\)) and generalizes the color coherence and decoherence framework developed by Mehtar-Tani (2010, 2011), Casalderrey-Solana (2011, 2012), and Mehtar-Tani (2017). The framework provides an analytic counterpart to Monte Carlo Event Generators such as JetMed by Caucal (2019), clarifying their approximations, and has recently been recast into a factorized effective field theory framework by Mehtar-Tani (2024). It enables systematic higher-order calculations of jet observables in heavy-ion collisions and provides a bridge to future phenomenology at the Electron-Ion Collider.
