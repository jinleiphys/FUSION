# Comparing pion production models to MiniBooNE data

> Predictions for neutrino-induced charged- and neutral-current single pion production on CH_2 from theoretical models and Monte Carlo event generators are compared with the cross section measurements from the MiniBooNE experiment.

## Key claim

Predictions for charged- and neutral-current single pion production on CH₂ from several theoretical models and Monte Carlo event generators generally fall below the MiniBooNE data in total charged-current cross section, and show shape disagreements in differential cross sections that cannot be fully explained by mismodelling of the neutrino flux shape alone.

## Method

The author compared predictions from three theoretical models (Athar et al., GiBUU, Nieves et al.) and three Monte Carlo generators (GENIE 2.6.2, NEUT 5.1.4.2, NuWro) against MiniBooNE's published absolute cross sections for charged-current single π⁺, charged-current single π⁰, and neutral-current single π⁰ production on CH₂. For each channel, total cross sections as a function of neutrino energy and flux-averaged differential cross sections in Q², muon kinetic energy, pion kinetic energy or momentum, and pion angle were compared, with ratios of prediction to data shown where available.

## Key numbers

- ANL and BNL deuterium bubble chamber measurements of pion production on individual nucleons disagree with one another at the 30–40% level.
- MiniBooNE's neutrino beam has a peak energy of around 600 MeV, with flux small beyond about 2 GeV.
- Predictions for the differential cross section dσ/dQ² differ in overall normalization by up to 50%.
- The region of pion kinetic energy around 0.2 GeV (pion momentum p_π ≈ 0.3 GeV) shows a dip in most models for CC1π⁺ that does not appear in the data.
- MiniBooNE presents CC1π⁰ differential distributions averaged over the neutrino flux from 0.5 to 2 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the comparisons made at NuInt09 (2009) by including MiniBooNE data that were not available at that time, and updates the earlier comparisons between models for charged-current single pion production on carbon. The difficulty of obtaining simultaneous agreement with the three MiniBooNE data sets has led the T2K oscillation analysis to introduce ad hoc tuning parameters with large uncertainties. The next step identified is to compare predictions for neutrino-induced pion production on free nucleons, which are free of nuclear and final state effects.
