# Zero Lattice Sound

> We study the N_f-flavor Gross-Neveu model in 2+1 dimensions with a baryon chemical potential mu, using both analytical and numerical methods. In particular, we study the self-consistent Boltzmann equation in the Fermi liquid framework using the quasiparticle interaction calculated to O(1/N_f), and find solutions for zero sound propagation for almost all mu > mu_c, the critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration. Next we present results of a numerical lattice simulation, examining temporal correlation functions of mesons defined using a point-split interpolating operator, and finding evidence for phonon-like behaviour characterised by a linear dispersion relation in the long wavelength limit. We argue that our results provide the first evidence for a collective excitation in a lattice simulation.

## Key claim
The paper claims to provide the first evidence for a collective excitation (zero sound) in a lattice simulation, but the identification is qualified by a discrepancy: the measured sound speed ratio \(s = \beta_0/\beta_F\) is less than 1 (approximately 0.55–0.65), whereas the analytic Fermi liquid solution predicts \(s > 1\) for undamped zero sound; the authors argue that finite-volume and lattice artifacts may suppress Landau damping and allow a phonon-like pole even with \(s < 1\).

## Method
The authors study the \(N_f\)-flavor Gross-Neveu model in 2+1 dimensions with a baryon chemical potential \(\mu\) using both analytic and numerical methods. Analytically, they solve the self-consistent Boltzmann equation in the Fermi liquid framework using the quasiparticle interaction calculated to \(O(1/N_f)\) and find solutions for zero sound propagation. Numerically, they perform lattice Monte Carlo simulations with staggered fermions on \(32^2 \times 48\) and \(48^3\) lattices, measuring temporal correlation functions of mesons defined using a point-split interpolating operator (spatial components of the conserved vector current) to extract dispersion relations.

## Key numbers
- Critical chemical potential for chiral symmetry restoration: \(\mu_c a \simeq 0.16\) (for the Z\(_2\) model with \(g^{-2}=0.75\)).
- Baryon density at \(\mu = 0.2\): \(n_B = 0.0226(2)\); at \(\mu = 0.6\): \(n_B = 0.2788(2)\) (in lattice units).
- Fermi velocity from fits on \(48^3\): \(B_F = 0.989(1)\) at \(\mu = 0.2\), \(B_F = 0.973(1)\) at \(\mu = 0.6\).
- Sound speed ratio \(s = \beta_0/\beta_F^{\text{bare}}\) extrapolated to \(k \to 0\): \(s = 0.896(8)\) at \(\mu = 0.2\) and \(s = 0.959(2)\) at \(\mu = 0.6\) (Z\(_2\) model, \(48^3\) lattice).
- For the SU(2)\(\otimes\)SU(2) model on \(32^2 \times 48\) at \(\mu = 0.5\): \(s = 0.938(2)\) (isosinglet) and \(s = 0.917(3)\) (isotriplet).

## Context
This work extends the authors’ previous study of the Gross-Neveu model in 2+1 dimensions (Hands, Kogut, and Strouthos, 2004), which found a meson channel with phonon-like dispersion. It applies Landau’s Fermi liquid theory (Landau, 1956) and the zero sound concept (Landau, 1957) to a relativistic lattice field theory for the first time. The results enable future lattice studies of collective excitations in dense fermionic systems, such as two-color QCD or the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model, and highlight the need for larger volumes and finer lattices to resolve the sign of \(s-1\) and distinguish zero sound from first sound.
