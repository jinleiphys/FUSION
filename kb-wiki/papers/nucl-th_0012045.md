# Helicity Amplitudes and Sum Rules for Real and Virtual Photons

> Results of the recently developed unitary isobar model (MAID) are presented for helicity amplitudes, spin asymmetries, structure functions and relevant sum rules for real and virtual photons in the resonance region. Our evaluation of the energy-weighted integrals is in good agreement for the proton but shows big discrepancies for the neutron.

## Key claim

The unitary isobar model (MAID) reproduces the Gerasimov-Drell-Hearn (GDH) sum rule and related integrals well for the proton, but shows "big discrepancies" for the neutron target, particularly violating the sum rule prediction that \(I_3(0)=0\) for the neutron.

## Method

The authors used the MAID unitary isobar model, which describes single-pion photo- and electroproduction data up to a total center-of-mass energy \(W_{\rm max}=2\) GeV and \(Q^2\le 4\) (GeV/c)\(^2\). The model is constructed from effective Lagrangians for Born terms and vector meson exchange (background), with resonance contributions modeled by Breit-Wigner functions; all major resonances below \(W=1700\) MeV are included, and multipoles are built in a gauge-invariant and unitary way for each partial wave. Contributions from eta and multi-pion channels were added using existing total cross section data and a reggeized two-pion photoproduction calculation by Holvoet and Vanderhaeghen (2000).

## Key numbers

*   GDH integral for the proton (full energy range up to \(W_{\rm max}=2\) GeV): model sum = \(-201\ \mu\)b, compared to the sum rule value of \(-205\ \mu\)b and the Mainz experimental value of \(-216\pm 6\ \mu\)b (200-800 MeV range).
*   GDH integral for the neutron (full energy range up to \(W_{\rm max}=2\) GeV): model sum = \(-164\ \mu\)b, compared to the sum rule value of \(-233\ \mu\)b.
*   Zero-crossing of the integral \(I_1(Q^2)\) for the proton: occurs at \(Q^2=0.75\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) with only one-pion contributions, lowered to \(0.52\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) with eta contributions, and to \(0.45\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) with multi-pion contributions.
*   Integral \(I_3(0)\) from the one-pion channel: 0.59 for the proton (sum rule expects 0.45) and 0.78 for the neutron (sum rule expects 0).
*   Forward spin polarizability \(\gamma_0\) for the proton (full energy range): model sum = \(-0.68\times 10^{-4}\ \text{fm}^4\), compared to the experimental value of \(-0.78\pm 0.09\times 10^{-4}\ \text{fm}^4\).

## Context

This work extends the MAID unitary isobar model (Drechsel 1998) to compute generalized GDH integrals and the Burkhardt-Cottingham (BC) sum rule (Burkhardt 1970) for both proton and neutron targets. It enables comparison with experimental data from Mainz (Ahrens 2000, Pedroni 2000) and SLAC (Abe 1998), and highlights the need for improved theoretical treatment of multi-pion channels and new experimental data from ELSA, GRAAL, and JLab to resolve the neutron discrepancies.
