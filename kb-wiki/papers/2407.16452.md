# The Hidden Variables: Harnessing Half-Shell Potentials for Enhanced Precision in Nuclear Reaction Calculations

> We explore the impact of half-shell components on nuclear reaction calculations, focusing on nonelastic breakup cross sections within the Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model. By advocating for the use of a consistent Single Folding Model (SFM) for all optical potentials in IAV calculations, we aim to reduce the uncertainties associated with half-shell components and enhance agreement with experimental data. We present results from deuteron-induced reactions on $^{60}$Ni and $^{208}$Pb, which serve as surrogate targets for neutron-induced reactions on short-lived nuclei. The application of consistent optical potentials derived from the SFM shows improved alignment with experimental data compared to traditional global phenomenological potentials. Furthermore, we investigate the $^{59}$Co($^6$Li,$αX$) reaction, which reveals that the half-shell $T$-matrix plays a pivotal role in accurately modeling nuclear reactions. Our findings suggest that a unified approach to optical potentials, accounting for half-shell effects, is critical for a precise understanding of complex nuclear reactions. This work highlights the significance of the internal dynamics of the wave function, particularly in lighter targets, and underscores the importance of the half-shell $T$-matrix as a previously underappreciated variable in reaction calculations.

## Key claim

Using a consistent Single Folding Model (SFM) for all optical potentials in Ichimura-Austern-Vincent (IAV) model calculations reduces uncertainties from half-shell components and improves agreement with experimental data, but the improvement is significant only for lighter targets where internal wave function dynamics matter; for heavier targets the surface approximation suffices and all potentials agree.

## Method

The authors computed nonelastic breakup (NEB) cross sections within the IAV model for deuteron-induced reactions on ⁶⁰Ni at 23 MeV and ²⁰⁸Pb at 55 MeV, and for the ⁵⁹Co(⁶Li,αX) reaction at 21.5 MeV. They compared results using three different optical potentials for the projectile-target channel: the SFM built from the KD02 nucleon-nucleus interaction, and two global phenomenological potentials (HSS and AC). The half-shell T-matrix and radial scattering wave functions were computed to trace differences in the interior region to the NEB cross section predictions.

## Context

This work extends the IAV model framework (Ichimura, Austern, Vincent, 1985) by addressing systematic errors from inconsistent optical potentials, which previous applications (Potel 2017, Carlson 2016, Jin 2015-2019) did not fully resolve. The half-shell T-matrix effects connect to the Phillips line phenomenon in three-nucleon systems (Phillips 1968, Polyzou and Glöckle 1990) and to phase-equivalent transformations in few-body physics. The results enable more reliable surrogate-reaction method calculations for neutron-induced reactions on short-lived nuclei, and suggest that future work should validate the SFM approach across broader target and projectile ranges.
