# Confinement in Two-Dimensional QCD and the Infinitely Long Pion

> Three current models of QCD in (1+1) dimensions are examined and extended in light-front coordinates. A pion of high momentum is found to have an infinite extent along its direction of motion.

## Key claim

A pion with high momentum has an infinite spatial extent along its direction of motion, but this result holds specifically in the chiral limit where quark masses vanish and the ground-state wave function becomes exactly constant, χ(x)=1, for all three models examined.

## Method

The authors compare three models of QCD in (1+1) dimensions quantized on the light front: the Li-Vary model using a differential longitudinal potential, the 't Hooft model using an integral potential derived from large-N QCD, and the invariant mass wave function (IMWF) model of Brodsky and de Teramond. They analyze the longitudinal wave functions in both momentum-space (x) and coordinate-space (the canonically conjugate variable z̃), compute the ground-state densities ρ(z̃)=|χ(z̃)|², and examine the asymptotic behavior at large |z̃| to determine the spatial extent.

## Key numbers

- For the Li-Vary model with parameters m=15 MeV and σ=620 MeV, the ground-state longitudinal mass squared is M_∥²(LV)=2σm+4m².
- For the 't Hooft model with current quark mass m=3.5 MeV and coupling g=2700 MeV, the ground-state longitudinal mass squared is M_∥²(tH)=2√(π/3) g m + 4m², yielding β=0.00126.
- The Li-Vary model gives excitation energies of approximately 1 GeV, while the 't Hooft model with modern current quark masses gives the first excited state a mass on the order of g, about 3 GeV.
- In the chiral limit, the density falls as 1/z̃² and the mean-square value ⟨χ|z̃²|χ⟩ is infinite.

## Context

This work extends and compares three approaches to two-dimensional QCD: the Li-Vary model (Li and Vary, 2021), the 't Hooft model (t Hooft, 1974) from large-N QCD, and the invariant mass wave function model (de Teramond and Brodsky, 2021). The analysis unifies earlier treatments of light-front holographic QCD (Brodsky et al., 2014) and reveals that the infinite longitudinal extent of the massless pion is consistent with the Ioffe-time argument from deep inelastic scattering (Gribov, 1965; Ioffe, 1969). The results enable future work on the elastic form factor of the pion and clarify which longitudinal potential preserves the successful spectrum of light-front holographic QCD when current quark masses are included.
