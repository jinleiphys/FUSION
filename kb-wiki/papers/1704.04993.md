# Phenomenology of the muon-induced neutron yield

> The cosmogenic neutron yield $Y_n$ characterizes the matter ability to produce neutrons under the effect of cosmic ray muons with spectrum and average energy corresponding to an observation depth. The yield is the basic characteristic of cosmogenic neutrons. The neutron production rate and neutron flux both are derivatives of the yield. The constancy of the exponents $α$ and $β$ in the known dependences of the yield on energy $Y_n {\propto} E_μ^α$ and the atomic weight $Y_n {\propto} A^β$ allows to combine these dependences in a single formula and to connect the yield with muon energy loss in the matter. As a result, the phenomenological formulas for the yield of muon-induced charged pions and neutrons can be obtained. These expressions both are associated with nuclear loss of the ultrarelativistic muons, which provides the main contribution to the total neutron yield. The total yield can be described by a universal formula, which is the best fit of experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the total muon-induced neutron yield can be described by a universal formula \(Y_n^{\mathrm{UF}} = b^{\mathrm{UF}} E_\mu^{\alpha} A^{\beta}\) that is the best fit of experimental data, but this formula is empirical; the underlying physical connection is that the hadronic component of the yield \(Y_n^h\) can be derived from the muon nuclear energy loss \(b_n\) and the characteristics of pion production in hadron showers, with the exponents \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) explained by this approach rather than being purely adjustable parameters.

## Method

The author derives phenomenological expressions for the muon-induced neutron yield by factorizing the dependence on muon energy \(E_\mu\) and atomic weight \(A\) into power laws \(Y_n \propto E_\mu^\alpha A^\beta\), using the constancy of the exponents \(\alpha\) and \(\beta\) over the energy range 40–400 GeV. The yield is decomposed into components from hadron showers (\(Y_n^h\)), electromagnetic showers (\(Y_n^{\mathrm{em}}\)), and virtual photons (\(Y_n^v\)); the hadron component is linked to the muon nuclear energy loss \(b_n = 4.0 \times 10^{-7}\) cm\(^2\)/g and to the charged pion multiplicity, while the electromagnetic component is related to photoneutron production in the giant dipole resonance. The universal formula parameters are fitted to a set of experimental data from multiple underground experiments (Table 1), and the resulting expression is compared with the derived hadron component to determine the fraction of the total yield contributed by hadron showers.

## Key numbers

* Universal formula parameters: \(b^{\mathrm{UF}} = 4.4 \times 10^{-7}\) cm\(^2\)/g, \(\alpha = 0.78\), \(\beta = 0.95\), valid for mean muon energies from \(\sim 40\) GeV to \(\sim 400\) GeV.
* Muon nuclear energy loss: \(b_n = 4.0 \times 10^{-7}\) cm\(^2\)/g, constant in the energy range 10 to \(10^4\) GeV.
* Charged pion yield expression: \(Y_\pi^\pm = b_n E_\mu^{0.75} A^{0.13}\), with \(\alpha_\pi = 0.75\) and \(\beta_\pi = 0.13\).
* Hadron component yield expression: \(Y_n^h = b_n E_\mu^{0.75} A^{0.90}\), with \(\beta_n = 0.74 \pm 0.10\) and \(\beta_\pi + \beta_n \approx 0.90\).
* Deep-inelastic muon-nucleon cross-section: \(\sigma_{\mu N} = 1.1 \times 10^{-28}\) cm\(^2\), constant in the energy range 10–\(10^4\) GeV.
* Fraction of hadron component in total yield at \(E_\mu = 280\) GeV: \(K = 0.91 (E_\mu)^{-0.03} A^{-0.05}\), giving values between 0.68 and 0.59 for atomic numbers \(A\) from 12 to 207.
* Overestimate of yield when using monoenergetic muons with energy \(\overline{E}_\mu\): 12% for \(\overline{E}_\mu \sim 100\) GeV and 5% for \(\overline{E}_\mu \sim 300\) GeV, assuming \(\alpha = 0.75\).

## Context

This work extends the universal formula (UF) approach previously introduced by the same author (Aga13) for the muon-induced neutron yield, which was obtained as a best fit to experimental data without a physical derivation of the exponents. The paper provides a phenomenological derivation of the hadron component \(Y_n^h\) by connecting it to the muon nuclear energy loss \(b_n\) and to the charged pion multiplicity, building on early studies of multiple processes in hadron-nucleus collisions (Bar72, Mey63, Koh67, Bar52). The results enable a more physically motivated interpretation of the universal formula and allow estimation of the relative contributions of hadron and electromagnetic processes to the total neutron yield, which is relevant for background calculations in underground low-background experiments.
