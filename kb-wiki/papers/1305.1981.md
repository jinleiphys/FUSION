# Bulk Viscosity Effects in Event-by-Event Relativistic Hydrodynamics

> Bulk viscosity effects on the collective flow harmonics in heavy ion collisions are investigated, on an event by event basis, using a newly developed 2+1 Lagrangian hydrodynamic code named v-USPhydro which implements the Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) algorithm for viscous hydrodynamics. A new formula for the bulk viscous corrections present in the distribution function at freeze-out is derived starting from the Boltzmann equation for multi-hadron species. Bulk viscosity is shown to enhance the collective flow Fourier coefficients from $v_2(p_T)$ to $v_5(p_T)$ when $% p_{T}\sim 1-3$ GeV even when the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio, $% ζ/s$, is significantly smaller than $1/(4π)$.

## Key claim

Bulk viscosity enhances the differential collective flow Fourier coefficients from v2(pT) to v5(pT) in the pT range of 1-3 GeV, even when the bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio ζ/s is significantly smaller than 1/(4π), and this enhancement is more pronounced in event-by-event simulations than in averaged initial conditions.

## Method

The authors developed a new 2+1 Lagrangian hydrodynamic code called v-USPhydro, which implements the Smoothed Particle Hydrodynamics (SPH) algorithm for viscous hydrodynamics. They derived a new formula for the bulk viscous corrections to the distribution function at freeze-out using the method of moments applied to the Boltzmann equation for multi-hadron species, and performed event-by-event simulations with Monte Carlo Glauber initial conditions for RHIC collisions at √s = 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Bulk viscosity to entropy density ratio ζ/s is parametrized as (1/8π)(1/3 - cs²), which is significantly smaller than 1/(4π) throughout the temperature range
- The freeze-out temperature is set to TFO = 150 MeV
- For pions at TFO = 150 MeV, the δf coefficients from the Moments method are: B0(π) = -65.85 fm⁴, D0(π) = 171.27 fm⁴/GeV, E0(π) = -63.05 fm⁴/GeV²
- The SPH smoothing parameter h = 0.3 fm with NSPH = 27000 particles, yielding approximately 10 minutes per hydro event on a single machine
- Conservation of energy and momentum holds to 0.01%
- For the 0-5% most central collisions, about 300 π⁺ are produced at mid-rapidity, with 41% coming from direct thermal pions (123 π⁺ at TFO = 150 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of bulk viscosity in heavy ion collisions that used averaged initial conditions (Monnai 2009, Song 2009, Bozek 2009, Denicol 2009, Roy 2011, Dusling 2011) and event-by-event simulations (Bozek 2012) by demonstrating that bulk viscosity enhances not only v2 but also higher harmonics v3 through v5. The new v-USPhydro code builds on the Lagrangian SPH approach used in NeXSPheRIO (Aguiar 2000, Hama 2004, Gardim 2011) but includes viscous effects in both the hydrodynamic evolution and freeze-out. The results enable future studies of the interplay between shear and bulk viscosities, and can be extended to include particle decays and hadronic afterburners for direct comparison with experimental data.
