# Anisotropy of thermal dileptons

> The meaningful specific anisotropy in the angle distribution of leptons with respect to the three-momentum of pair is predicted as a feasibility signature of synchrotron-like mechanism resulting from the quarks interacting with a collective confining color field in the heavy ion collisions. The lepton pair production rate and the spectrum of pair invariant mass are presented for this new dilepton source that is apparently not taken into consideration in the available phenomenological estimates.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that a "meaningful specific anisotropy" in the angular distribution of leptons (with respect to the three-momentum of the pair) can serve as a signature of a synchrotron-like radiation mechanism from quarks interacting with a collective confining color field in heavy ion collisions, but this prediction is conditional on the intermediate ("massive") photon having transverse polarization, which the authors estimate at about 20% for a cylindrically symmetric plasma volume.

## Method

The authors calculate the lepton pair production rate and invariant mass spectrum from a "magnetic bremsstrahlung" (synchrotron radiation) mechanism, where quarks at the boundary of a quark-gluon plasma (QGP) volume move along curved trajectories under a constant confining force (sigma ~ 0.2 GeV^2) modeled via the chromoelectric flux tube model. They derive the lepton-pair distribution in invariant mass from the virtual-photon production probability, average over quark paths, and convolute with the quark flux reaching the QGP boundary. The angular distribution of leptons from the decay of polarized massive photons is then computed to extract the azimuthal anisotropy signature.

## Key numbers

- The confining force is sigma ~ 0.2 GeV^2.
- The ratio of the total number of electron-positron pairs from the bremsstrahlung mechanism to that from standard quark-antiquark annihilation is N/N_ann ~ 40 for R = 1 fm and N/N_ann ~ 4 for R = 10 fm at T ~ 200 MeV.
- For muon pairs, the analogous ratio is (N/N_ann)_muon = C_muon / (R T^(1/3) sigma^(1/3)) with C_muon ~ 7.1.
- The degree of polarization delta for intermediate photons from a cylindrically symmetric plasma volume is about 20%.
- The characteristic parameter for the strong-field regime is chi = ((3/2) sigma E / m^3)^(1/3), and intermediate photons up to masses sqrt(k^2) ~ sqrt(sigma) = 0.45 GeV are considered to have small virtuality.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous proposal of a "magnetic bremsstrahlung-like radiation" mechanism for direct photons (Goloviznin 2012) to the case of dileptons, offering an alternative to standard volumetric QGP production mechanisms (Feinberg, Shuryak, Kajantie) and to other recent suggestions such as thermal photons with elliptic flow (Chatterjee 2005), radiation from semi-QGP (Pisarski), and gluon condensate radiation (Chiu). The predicted azimuthal anisotropy in lepton distributions is argued to be absent in the Drell-Yan mechanism and standard volumetric mechanism, and could serve as a probe of QGP as discussed by Baym (2017). The paper enables experimental searches for this anisotropy as a way to resolve discrepancies between observed dilepton spectra and theoretical expectations.
