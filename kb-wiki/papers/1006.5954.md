# Pseudospin Conserving Shell Model Interactions

> Pseudospin symmetry is approximately conserved in nuclei. Normally shell model interactions are written in terms of spin an orbital angular momentum operators, not in terms of pseudospin and pseudo-orbital angular momentum operators. We determine the shell model interactions which conserve pseudospin symmetry and pseudo-orbital angular momentum symmetry and write them in terms of spin and orbital angular momentum operators including the tensor interaction. We show that, although the tensor interaction by itself does not conserve pseudo-orbital angular momentum, certain combinations of the tensor interaction with the two body angular momentum squared interaction and the two body spin orbit interaction do conserve pseudo-orbital angular momentum.

# Pseudospin Conserving Shell Model Interactions

## Key claim
Pseudospin symmetry can be conserved in shell model interactions that include the tensor interaction, but only when the tensor interaction appears in a specific combination with the two-body angular momentum squared interaction and the two-body spin-orbit interaction; the tensor interaction by itself does not conserve pseudo-orbital angular momentum.

## Method
The author derives the general form of a two-nucleon interaction that conserves pseudospin, pseudo-orbital angular momentum, and total angular momentum, expressed in momentum space using pseudospin and pseudo-orbital angular momentum operators. These pseudo-operators are then rewritten in terms of standard spin and orbital angular momentum operators using algebraic relations, yielding an equivalent interaction expressed with conventional shell-model operators including central, spin-spin, orbital angular momentum squared, spin-orbit, and tensor terms.

## Key numbers
- The condition for pseudospin and pseudo-orbital angular momentum conservation is given by the relation: V_t^(T)(p) + V_so^(T)(p) - 2 V_o^(T)(p) = 0, where V_t is the tensor strength, V_so is the two-body spin-orbit strength, and V_o is the two-body orbital angular momentum squared strength.
- When V_so^(T)(p) = 2 V_o^(T)(p), the tensor interaction vanishes and both spin and pseudospin are conserved.
- The pseudospin-pseudospin interaction is shown to be equivalent to the spin-spin interaction: {\tilde s}_1 · {\tilde s}_2 = s_1 · s_2.

## Context
This work extends the understanding of pseudospin symmetry, which was previously established as a relativistic symmetry of the Dirac Hamiltonian by Ginocchio (1997) and shown to approximately hold in nuclear mean fields by Madland and collaborators. The paper provides a formal framework for constructing shell model interactions that preserve pseudospin symmetry, which is relevant for describing shell evolution in exotic nuclei where tensor interactions are important (Otsuka, 2005) and where pseudospin doublets are observed (Sorlin, 2008). The momentum-space formulation is designed to be compatible with modern effective interactions such as effective field theories and low-k interactions.
