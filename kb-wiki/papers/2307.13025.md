# Transverse Momentum Measurements with Jets at Next-to-Leading Power

> In view of the increasing precision of theoretical calculations and experimental measurements, power corrections to transverse-momentum-dependent observables are highly important. We study the next-to-leading power corrections for transverse momentum measurements in $e^+ e^- \rightarrow 2$ jets. We obtain a factorized expression for the cross section, which involve twist-2 and twist-3 operators, and identify the new jet functions that appear in it. We calculate these jet functions at order αs for a family of recoil-free schemes, and provide the corresponding anomalous dimensions at leading order. Additionally, we show that the (endpoint) divergences that typically arise in sub-leading-power factorization can be subtracted and cancel for our case. By working with jets, everything is perturbatively calculable and there are substantial simplifications compared to the general next-to-leading power framework. Importantly, our analysis with jets can be extended to semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, with the future Electron-Ion Collider as key application.

## Key claim

The authors derive a factorized expression for the next-to-leading power (NLP) corrections to transverse momentum measurements in \(e^+e^- \rightarrow 2\) jets, showing that by using jets rather than hadrons, all contributions are perturbatively calculable and the number of independent jet functions reduces to just one new function at NLP, provided the symmetry between the two jet directions is broken (e.g., by using two different recoil-free recombination schemes).

## Method

The authors expand the hadronic tensor for di-jet production in \(e^+e^-\) collisions up to next-to-leading power in \(\lambda = q_T/Q\) using the TMD operator product expansion method of Vladimirov (2021). They identify the twist-2 and twist-3 operator building blocks, express the hadronic tensor in terms of jet functions with open spinor indices, then apply Fierz relations and discrete symmetries to reduce the basis. The jet functions are calculated at order \(\alpha_s\) for a family of recoil-free jet schemes (such as Winner-Takes-All), and the corresponding anomalous dimensions are provided at leading order. The authors also show that endpoint divergences typical of sub-leading-power factorization can be subtracted and cancel for this case.

## Key numbers

* The power expansion parameter is \(\lambda = q_T/Q\), where \(q_T\) is the small transverse momentum and \(Q\) is the hard scale of the process.
* The jet functions are calculated at order \(\alpha_s\) (first order in the strong coupling constant).
* The cross section is differential in \(y\) (related to the jet-lepton angle, \(y = (1 - \cos\theta_2)/2\)) and in the two-dimensional transverse momentum \(\mathbf{q}\).
* The invariant mass \(Q\) of the collision satisfies \(Q^2 = q^2\), and the transverse momentum squared is \(Q_T^2 = -q_T^2\).
* The relation between the angular decorrelation \(\theta_{12}\) and \(Q_T\) is \(Q_T^2 \approx \frac14 \theta_{12}^2 Q^2\).

## Context

This work extends the TMD factorization framework for transverse-momentum-dependent observables, which has been developed at leading power up to N\(^4\)LL order (Moos 2023, Neumann 2022), to next-to-leading power for jet production. It builds on the operator basis and evolution results of Vladimirov (2021), Ebert (2021), and Rodini (2023) for DY and SIDIS processes, but uses jets instead of hadrons to keep everything perturbatively calculable. The results can be extended to semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, with the future Electron-Ion Collider as a key application, and can be tested at existing \(e^+e^-\) colliders such as LEP and Belle.
