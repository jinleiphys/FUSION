# Recent progress on nuclear potentials from lattice QCD

> A new method for nuclear potentials is reviewed. The strategy to extract the potential from the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function in lattice QCD is explained and the first result based on the method is presented in quenched QCD. The recent progress of the method is summarized.

# Recent progress on nuclear potentials from lattice QCD

## Key claim
The Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave function method can extract nuclear potentials directly from lattice QCD, and the leading-order (LO) local approximation is valid because the energy dependence of the LO potentials is very small at low energy, implying weak non-locality of the underlying non-local potential.

## Method
The equal-time Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter wave function for a two-nucleon system is computed on the lattice from QCD eigenstates with baryon number 2. A non-local potential is defined via the relation between the kinetic energy and the NBS wave function, then expanded in a derivative expansion truncated at leading order to obtain local central, tensor, and spin-orbit potentials. The method is applied to both quenched and full QCD simulations, and extended to hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions.

## Key numbers
- Quenched QCD at lattice spacing a ≈ 0.14 fm, lattice size L ≈ 4.4 fm, and pion mass mπ ≈ 530 MeV: the NN central potentials show both the attraction well and the repulsive core.
- Energy dependence test in quenched QCD: LO potentials obtained at εk ≈ 0 MeV compared with those at εk ≈ 46 MeV show very small energy dependence.
- Full QCD at a ≈ 0.1 fm and L ≈ 2.9 fm: larger repulsive core for both central potentials and larger attraction for the tensor force compared to quenched QCD.
- Flavor SU(3) symmetric full QCD at a ≈ 0.12 fm and L ≈ 2 fm: the flavor singlet potential shows an attractive core instead of a repulsive core at mπ = 1014 MeV and 835 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the phenomenological NN potential approach, which uses O(10) parameters to fit O(1000) scattering phase shift data and describes the repulsive core (Jastrow, year not given) and attraction well, by deriving potentials directly from QCD. The method was first demonstrated in quenched QCD (IAH1, AHI1, AHI2) and was selected as one of 21 papers in Nature Research Highlight 2007. It enables ab initio investigations of nuclear and hypernuclear physics once full QCD calculations at the physical pion mass are performed, and has been extended to hyperon interactions (nemura1, nemura2) and flavor SU(3) symmetric baryon interactions (inoue1).
