# Anisotropic Propagator for the Goldstone Modes in Color-flavor Locked Phase in the Presence of a Magnetic Field

> We consider the phase diagram of QCD at very high baryon density and at zero temperature in the presence of a strong magnetic field. The state of matter at such high densities and low temperatures is believed to be a phase known as the color-flavor locked phase which breaks color and electromagnetic gauge invariance leaving a linear combination of them unbroken. Of the 9 quarks (three flavors and three colors), five are neutral under this unbroken generator and four are oppositely charged. In the presence of a magnetic field corresponding to the unbroken generator however, the properties of the condensate changes and a new phase known as the magnetic color flavor locked (MCFL)phase is realized. This phase breaks some of the color-flavor symmetry of the Lagrangian spontaneously, giving rise to 6 Goldstone modes, 5 of which are pseudo Goldstone modes. These Goldstone modes are composed of excitations that correspond to both neutral quarks and charged quarks. Hence it is natural to expect that the propagators of these Goldstone modes get affected in the presence of a magnetic field and their speed becomes considerably anisotropic. Although this anisotropy is self-evident from symmetry arguments, it has not been quantified yet. We calculate this anisotropy in the speed of the Goldstone modes using an NJL model type of interaction between the quarks and comment on the impact of such anisotropic modes on the transport properties of the MCFL phase.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies the anisotropy in the speed of the six neutral Goldstone modes in the magnetic color-flavor locked (MCFL) phase of QCD, which had been predicted from symmetry arguments but not previously calculated. The calculation is performed using an NJL-type four-fermion interaction model and is valid for magnetic fields where the three gaps are equal, specifically at \(\tilde{e}\tilde{B} \sim 2\mu^2\), though the method is intended to demonstrate how similar calculations would work for smaller magnetic fields.

## Method

The author starts from an NJL-type Lagrangian with a four-fermion interaction in the color-flavor locking channel, introduces auxiliary fields for the diquark condensate, and includes a \(U(1)_{\tilde{em}}\) magnetic field via the gauge field \(\tilde{A}^\mu = (0,0,\tilde{B}x,0)\). The action is minimized to obtain gap equations, and the quark propagator is expressed in the Landau level basis using the Ritus method. The action is then expanded to quadratic order in fluctuations about the condensate minimum, and the inverse propagators for the six neutral Goldstone modes are computed by evaluating the resulting integrals at the Fermi surface and expanding in small momenta compared to the gap.

## Key numbers

*   For Goldstone modes \(i = 1, 2, 3\) at \(\tilde{e}\tilde{B} \sim 2\mu^2\): perpendicular speed squared \((v^i)_{\perp}^2 = \frac{2}{9}\) and parallel speed squared \((v^i)_{\parallel}^2 = \frac{4}{9}\).
*   For Goldstone mode \(i = 8\) at \(\tilde{e}\tilde{B} \sim 2\mu^2\): perpendicular speed squared \((v^i)_{\perp}^2 = \frac{8}{27}\) and parallel speed squared \((v^i)_{\parallel}^2 = \frac{11}{27}\).
*   For Goldstone modes \(i = 9, 10\) at \(\tilde{e}\tilde{B} \sim 2\mu^2\): perpendicular speed squared \((v^i)_{\perp}^2 = \frac{5}{27}\) and parallel speed squared \((v^i)_{\parallel}^2 = \frac{17}{27}\).
*   The condition for equal gaps \(\Delta_1 = \Delta_2 = \Delta_3\) is found to be \(\tilde{e}\tilde{B} = 2\mu^2\).
*   The upper bound on magnetic field strength in gravitationally bound stars is around \(10^{18}\) Gauss, and for self-bound quark stars it can be even higher.

## Context

This work extends the study of the MCFL phase, previously explored by Ferrer et al. (2006, 2007) who calculated the gap structure for both extremely large and smaller magnetic fields, and by Ferrer et al. (2007) who discussed the effective theory of the Goldstone modes at a qualitative level. The paper provides the first quantitative calculation of the anisotropic speeds of the neutral Goldstone modes in the MCFL phase, which is necessary for understanding the low-energy transport properties of this phase, such as thermal and electrical conductivity, relevant for the physics of compact stars and magnetars.
