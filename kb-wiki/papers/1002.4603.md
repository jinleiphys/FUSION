# Causality and the effective range expansion

> We derive the generalization of Wigner's causality bounds and Bethe's integral formula for the effective range parameter to arbitrary dimension and arbitrary angular momentum. We also discuss the impact of these constraints on the separation of low- and high-momentum scales and universality in low-energy scattering. Some of our results were summarized earlier in a letter publication. In this work, we present full derivations and several detailed examples.

## Key claim

The paper derives generalized causality bounds (Wigner bounds) and Bethe's integral formula for the effective range parameter that apply to arbitrary spatial dimension \(d\) and arbitrary angular momentum \(L\), not just the standard three-dimensional S-wave case. The key qualifier is that these bounds apply only to finite-range interactions that are energy independent, non-singular, and spin independent, and they show that for \(2L+d \geq 5\) the unitarity limit cannot be reached because the Wigner bound prevents the effective range from being tuned to zero.

## Method

The authors start from the radial Schrodinger equation in \(d\) dimensions with a finite-range interaction kernel \(W(r,r')\), rescale the radial wave function, and derive a Wronskian identity for two solutions at different momenta. Using the effective range expansion for general \(d\) and \(L\), they express the Wronskian in terms of the scattering parameters and the zero-energy wave function, then apply the positivity of an integral of the squared zero-energy wave function to obtain an upper bound on the effective range parameter \(r_{L,d}\). They also extend the bound to nonzero momenta by considering the analytic limit of the Wronskian ratio.

## Key numbers

* For \(2L+d = 4\) (e.g., P-wave in 3D), when \(|a_{L,d}| \to \infty\) the causality bound gives \(\bar{\rho}_{L,d} \leq \frac{R}{2} e^{\gamma}\), where \(R\) is the interaction range and \(\gamma\) is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
* For \(2L+d \geq 5\), when \(|a_{L,d}| \to \infty\) the causality bound gives \(r_{L,d} \leq -\frac{2\Gamma(L+\frac{d}{2}-2)\Gamma(L+\frac{d}{2}-1)}{\pi} \left( \frac{R}{2} \right)^{-2L-d+4}\), which is a fixed negative number, so \(r_{L,d}=0\) is not allowed.
* For the spherical step potential example in \(d\) dimensions, the matching condition at radius \(R\) yields \(\cot\delta_{L,d}(p) = \frac{p R J_{\alpha}(p'R) \dots}{p' R J_{\alpha}(p'R) \dots}\) (the full expression is given in the text but truncated in the provided excerpt).

## Context

This work generalizes the earlier causality analysis of Phillips and Cohen (1996) for S-wave effective range in three dimensions, and extends Bethe's integral formula (1949) for the effective range to arbitrary \(d\) and \(L\) (the \(d=3, L>0\) case was first derived by Madsen (2002)). The results constrain the separation of low- and high-momentum scales in low-energy scattering, showing that for \(2L+d \geq 4\) two relevant parameters (scattering parameter and effective range) are needed to characterize universal behavior, which impacts the description of P-wave Feshbach resonances in ultracold atoms and P-wave alpha-neutron interactions in halo nuclei.
