# Photon Vector Meson Coupling and Vector Meson Properties at Low Temperature Pion Gas

> The vector and axial vector current mixing phenomena at low temperature pion gas by Dey, Eletsky and Ioffe, leads to the low temperature correction of the photon-vector meson coupling ($g_ρ$) at order $ε=T^2/6f_π^2$ and the $ρ$ meson mass at order $ε^2$. We show how this {\it low temperature theorems} involving the photon and vector mesons are satisfied in the chiral models based on hidden gauge symmetry and the massive Yang-Mills approach with an explicit $a_1$ meson. We discuss possible phenomenological consequences of the low temperature corrections in RHIC experiments.

# Photon Vector Meson Coupling and Vector Meson Properties at Low Temperature Pion Gas

## Key claim
The low-temperature mixing between vector and axial-vector current correlators, derived by Dey, Eletsky and Ioffe (DEI) from current algebra and PCAC, implies that the photon-vector meson coupling \(g_\rho\) receives a correction at order \(\epsilon = T^2/6f_\pi^2\) while the \(\rho\) meson mass only changes at order \(\epsilon^2\); the authors show that both the hidden gauge approach and the massive Yang-Mills approach satisfy these low-temperature theorems, but only if the \(a_1\) meson is included in the latter.

## Method
The authors compute the leading thermal pion loop corrections to the vector current correlator and the \(\rho\) meson propagator in two effective chiral Lagrangian frameworks: the hidden gauge approach (HGA) of Bando et al., which introduces the \(\rho\) as a gauge boson of a hidden local symmetry, and the massive Yang-Mills approach (MYMA) with explicit \(\rho\) and \(a_1\) meson fields. They evaluate the temperature-dependent part of the pion propagator using the Bose-Einstein distribution and apply the soft-pion limit to extract the leading-order corrections in \(\epsilon = T^2/6f_\pi^2\).

## Key numbers
- The expansion parameter is \(\epsilon = T^2/6f_\pi^2\) with \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV; the approximation is valid up to \(T \sim 150\) MeV.
- The \(\rho\) meson coupling to the vector current at finite temperature follows \(g_\rho^2(T) = (1-\epsilon)g_\rho^2 + O(\epsilon^2)\).
- In the HGA with \(a=2\), the \(\rho\) meson mass shift appears only at order \(\epsilon^2\) due to the derivative nature of the \(\rho\pi\pi\) coupling.
- The \(\omega\) meson correlator receives no correction at order \(\epsilon\) because two pions cannot couple to the \(\omega\) current.
- For dilepton spectra, the authors use \(m_\rho = 770\) MeV, \(\Gamma_\rho = 150\) MeV, \(f_\rho = 152\) MeV and \(m_\omega = 782\) MeV, \(\Gamma_\omega = 9.8\) MeV, \(f_\omega = 46\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the low-temperature mixing theorem of Dey, Eletsky and Ioffe (DEI, 1990), which showed that vector and axial-vector correlators mix at order \(\epsilon\) with no vector meson mass shift, to explicit chiral models with vector mesons. It demonstrates consistency with the chiral perturbation theory results of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and resolves an apparent discrepancy: the MYMA without the \(a_1\) meson would erroneously produce a \(\rho\) mass shift at order \(\epsilon\), while the HGA automatically satisfies the theorem with only the \(\rho\) included. The results enable quantitative predictions for the relative suppression of the \(\rho\) peak compared to the \(\omega\) peak in thermal dilepton spectra from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, relevant for interpreting NA38 data on \(\phi/(\rho+\omega)\) enhancement and the analysis by Koch, Heinz and Pisut (1990) relating transverse energy to fireball size.
