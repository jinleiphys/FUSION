# The Glasma, Photons and the Implications of Anisotropy

> We introduce distribution functions for quarks and gluons in the Glasma and discuss how they satisfy various relationships of statistical physics. We use these distributions to compute photon production in the early stages of heavy ion collisions. Photon rates satisfy geometric scaling, that is, the emission rate per unit area scales as a function of the saturation momenta divided by the transverse momentum of the photon. Photon distributions from the Glasma are steeper than those computed in the Thermalized Quark Gluon Plasma (TQGP). Both the delayed equilibration of the Glasma and a possible anisotropy in the pressure lead to slower expansion and mean times of photon emission of fixed energy are increased. This delayed emission might allow for larger photon elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The paper argues that introducing a Glasma phase before thermalization in heavy ion collisions, with a possible anisotropy between longitudinal and transverse pressure, leads to a steeper photon spectrum and significantly longer photon emission times compared to a purely thermalized Quark Gluon Plasma (TQGP), which could help explain the experimentally observed large photon elliptic flow.

## Method

The authors construct a model for quark and gluon distribution functions in the Glasma using two energy scales: an ultraviolet scale Λ and an infrared scale Λ_IR, with the gluon distribution enhanced by a factor κΛ_IR/(N_c α_s Λ). They evolve these scales using 1+1 dimensional hydrodynamics with a pressure anisotropy parameter δ (where P_L = δ ε), and compute photon production rates from both the Glasma and the thermalized QGP, matching the two phases at the thermalization time when Λ_IR = N_c α_s Λ/κ.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time for δ=1/3: t_th ≈ 9.7 fm/c
- Thermalization time for δ=1/4: t_th ≈ 8.6 fm/c
- Mean photon emission time at p_T=1 GeV: thermal = 12 fm/c; Glasma+th, δ=1/3 = 16.8 fm/c; Glasma+th, δ=1/4 = 15 fm/c
- Mean photon emission time at p_T=2 GeV: thermal = 2 fm/c; Glasma+th, δ=1/3 = 2.8 fm/c; Glasma+th, δ=1/4 = 3 fm/c
- Mean photon emission time at p_T=3 GeV: thermal = 1 fm/c; Glasma+th, δ=1/3 = 1.3 fm/c; Glasma+th, δ=1/4 = 1.5 fm/c
- Initial temperature for LHC energies: T_0 = 550 MeV
- Coupling constant: α_s = 0.3
- Parameter κ determined to be approximately 2-3 from parton distribution functions
- K-factor of 7 used for δ=1/3 and 5 for δ=1/4 to account for neglected processes

## Context

This work addresses the discrepancy between experimental observations of direct photon spectra and elliptic flow at RHIC and LHC (Adare 2011, Lohner 2012) and theoretical computations from a thermalized QGP (Shen 2013, vanHees 2011, Bratkovskaya 2008, Linnyk 2013). It extends earlier work on photon emission from the Glasma (Chiu 2012) and builds on hydrodynamic descriptions of the Glasma with possible pressure anisotropy (Blaizot 2011, Kurkela 2011). The geometric scaling of photon distributions, previously observed experimentally (Klein-Bosing 2014), emerges naturally from this framework. The results suggest that realistic 3+1 dimensional simulations incorporating a Glasma phase may resolve the photon flow puzzle.
