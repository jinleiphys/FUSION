# Particle-Hole Multiplets Near Closed Shells

> We report here on a shell-model study of nuclei close to doubly magic 132Sn and 100Sn focusing attention on particle-hole multiplets. In our study we make use of realistic effective interactions derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential. We present results for the four nuclei 132Sb, 130Sb, 102In and 98Ag. Comparison shows that the calculated results are in very good agreement with the experimental data available for these nuclei far from stability. This supports confidence in the predictions of our calculations which may stimulate, and be helpful to, future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that shell-model calculations using a realistic effective interaction derived from the CD-Bonn nucleon-nucleon potential, without any adjustable parameters, reproduce experimental particle-hole multiplet energies in nuclei near doubly magic 132Sn and 100Sn to within a few tens of keV for most states, with the largest discrepancies reaching about 300 keV.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for the odd-odd nuclei 132Sb, 130Sb, 102In, and 98Ag, assuming 132Sn and 100Sn as closed cores. They derived a realistic effective interaction from the CD-Bonn free nucleon-nucleon potential by first constructing a low-momentum potential Vlow-k with a cutoff momentum of 2.1 fm-1, then using a folded-diagram method to obtain the particle-hole effective interaction matrix elements. Single-particle and single-hole energies were taken from experimental spectra where available, or from previous analyses of Sn isotopes and N=50 isotones.

## Key numbers

- The calculated 8- state in 132Sb lies 226 keV above the 4+ ground state, consistent with experimental indications that the 8- state is about 200 keV above the 4+ state.
- For the πg7/2 νh-111/2 multiplet in 132Sb, the 9- state is calculated at 1.025 MeV relative to the 8- state.
- The largest discrepancies between theory and experiment in 132Sb are about 300 keV for the 1+ state of the πd5/2 νd-13/2 multiplet and about 200 keV for the 9- state of the πg7/2 νh-111/2 multiplet.
- In 130Sb, the calculated 8- state lies at 92 keV excitation energy above the predicted 4+ ground state.
- For the πg-19/2 νd5/2 multiplet in 98Ag, the largest discrepancy is 130 keV for the 5+ state.

## Context

This work extends the classic shell-model studies of particle-hole multiplets in the 208Pb region, particularly the 208Bi spectrum studied by Kuo (1968) using the Hamada-Johnston potential, to the more exotic 132Sn and 100Sn regions far from stability. The calculations use a modern CD-Bonn potential and a new Vlow-k approach as an alternative to the Brueckner G-matrix method, following the authors' previous studies of nuclei with identical valence particles and holes in these regions (Covello 2001). The results enable future experimental studies with radioactive ion beams by providing reliable predictions for the spectroscopy of these unstable nuclei.
