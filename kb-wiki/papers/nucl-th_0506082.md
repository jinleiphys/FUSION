# Electroweak quasielastic response functions in nuclear matter

> Quasielastic electromagnetic and parity-violating electron scattering response functions of relativistic nuclear matter are reviewed. The roles played by the Hartree-Fock field and by nuclear correlations in the Random Phase Approximation (treated within the continued fraction scheme) are illustrated. The parity-violating responses of nuclei to polarized electrons are also revisited, stressing in particular the crucial role played by the pion in the nuclear dynamics. Finally, some issues surrounding scaling and sum rules are addressed.

## Key claim

The paper reviews how the Hartree-Fock mean field and Random Phase Approximation correlations (treated via a continued fraction scheme) modify the quasielastic electromagnetic and parity-violating response functions of relativistic nuclear matter, but it notes that the validity of the non-relativistic potential expansion at high momenta and the inclusion of short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations in that framework remain to be fully explored.

## Method

The authors employ a non-relativistic one-boson-exchange potential (such as the Bonn potential) as the nucleon-nucleon interaction and compute the polarization propagator in nuclear matter using Green's function techniques. They build a hierarchy of approximations: free Fermi gas, Hartree-Fock (HF), and Random Phase Approximation (RPA) treated via a continued fraction expansion up to second order. Relativistic kinematics are incorporated approximately by using a scaling variable and multiplying each polarization propagator by appropriate powers of \(1+\omega/m_N\).

## Key numbers

- The free relativistic Fermi gas response calculated using the scaling variable \(\psi_{\text{r}}\) reproduces the exact calculation with a discrepancy typically below 1%.
- The real part of the free polarization propagator \(\text{Re}\Pi^{(0)}\) is compared at \(q=500\) MeV/c and \(q=1000\) MeV/c as a function of energy transfer, using \(k_F=195\) MeV/c.
- The free response function is shown at \(q=300\) MeV/c and \(k_F=195\) MeV/c, illustrating the linear dependence on \(\omega\) in the Pauli-blocked region.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of quasielastic electron scattering from nuclei (e.g., Jou96, Ang96, Wil97) and theoretical treatments using Green's function techniques (Fet71) and the Walecka model (Wal95). It builds on prior calculations of nuclear response functions in nuclear matter using the continued fraction method (Alb93, Bar94, Bar96a, Bar96b) and the relativistic Bonn potential expanded to second order (Bar96a). The paper enables future work on finite nuclei by providing a tested semi-analytical framework for incorporating many-body effects beyond the ring approximation.
