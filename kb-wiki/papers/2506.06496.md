# Universal Mass Equation for Equal-Quantum Excited-States Sets II

> We extend our recent study of the universal mass equation for equal-quantum excited-states sets reported by Roper and Strakovsky~\cite{Roper:2024ovj}. The masses of twelve baryon sets and sixteen meson sets, with only two equal-quantum excited states in each set, using Breit-Wigner PDG2024 masses and their uncertainties at fixed $J^P$ for baryons and $J^{PC}$ for mesons, are fitted by a simple one-parameter logarithmic function, $M_n = αLn(n) + M_1$, where $n$ is the level of radial excitation. Two accurate masses that start a set are used to calculate four higher masses in the set accurately. It is noted that $α$ values for $b\bar{b}$ equal-quantum excited-states sets accurately lie on a straight line, whose line parameters can be used to calculate $α$ and predict higher mass states for $b\bar{b}$ sets that have only one known member.

## Key claim

The authors extend their earlier universal mass equation to hadron sets that have only two known excited states (duo sets), showing that the masses of the next four higher states can be calculated with reasonable accuracy using just those two lowest masses, provided the two known states are the first two in the set.

## Method

The authors fit Breit-Wigner masses from the PDG2024 listings for twelve baryon sets and sixteen meson sets, each containing only two equal-quantum excited states at fixed J^P (baryons) or J^PC (mesons), to the one-parameter logarithmic function M_n = alpha * Ln(n) + M_1, where n is the radial excitation level. Using the two lowest masses M_1 and M_2, they determine alpha = (M_2 - M_1)/Ln(2) and propagate uncertainties to predict masses for n = 3 through n = 6. They validate the method against the N(1/2^+) and a_0 data sets from their earlier work (Roper and Strakovsky 2024) which have more than two known members.

## Key numbers

* N(1/2^+) baryon test case: two-state calculation gives alpha = 722.0 +/- 43.3 MeV; predicted m_3 = 1733 +/- 47 MeV compared to data 1710 +/- 30 MeV; predicted m_5 = 2101 +/- 70 MeV compared to data 2100 +/- 50 MeV.
* a_0 meson test case: two-state calculation gives alpha = 662.2 +/- 49.0 MeV; predicted m_3 = 1707 +/- 54 MeV compared to data 1713 +/- 19 MeV; predicted m_4 = 1898 +/- 68 MeV compared to data 1931 +/- 26 MeV.
* Delta 1/2^+ baryon set: alpha = 265.5 +/- 72.1 MeV; predicted m_3 = 2008 +/- 79 MeV, m_4 = 2084 +/- 100 MeV, m_5 = 2143 +/- 116 MeV, m_6 = 2192 +/- 129 MeV.
* Lambda 5/2^- baryon set: alpha = 370.8 +/- 18.7 MeV; predicted m_3 = 2232 +/- 21 MeV, m_4 = 2339 +/- 26 MeV, m_5 = 2422 +/- 30 MeV, m_6 = 2489 +/- 34 MeV.
* Xi_b 3/2^- baryon set: alpha = 11.7 +/- 0.7 MeV; predicted m_3 = 6100.0 +/- 0.8 MeV, m_4 = 6103.4 +/- 1.0 MeV, m_5 = 6106.0 +/- 1.2 MeV, m_6 = 6108.1 +/- 1.3 MeV.
* a_2(2^++) meson set: alpha = 559.5 +/- 20.2 MeV; predicted m_3 = 1933 +/- 22 MeV, m_4 = 2094 +/- 28 MeV, m_5 = 2219 +/- 32 MeV, m_6 = 2321 +/- 36 MeV.
* eta_2(2^-+) meson set: alpha = 324.6 +/- 11.5 MeV; predicted m_3 = 1974 +/- 13 MeV, m_4 = 2067 +/- 16 MeV, m_5 = 2139 +/- 19 MeV, m_6 = 2199 +/- 21 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the universal mass equation approach of Roper and Strakovsky (2024) from hadron sets with three or more known excited states to duo sets with only two known members, using PDG2024 data. The method enables prediction of higher-mass states for baryon families (N, Delta, Lambda, Sigma, charmed and bottom baryons) and meson families (light unflavored, strange, charmed, and bottom mesons) where only two states have been observed, including exotic states such as P_{c\bar{c}s}^0. The authors note that for sets with very small mass uncertainties (such as Lambda 5/2^-, Lambda_c 3/2^-, Xi_b 3/2^-, a_2, and eta_2), many more than four higher states can be accurately predicted.
