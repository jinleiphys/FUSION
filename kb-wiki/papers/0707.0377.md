# Analysis of Emission Shapes

> Shapes of relative emission sources can be accessed by expanding shapes of correlations at low relative velocities in pair center of mass in Cartesian harmonics. Coefficients of expansion for correlations are related to the respective coefficients of expansion for the sources through one dimensional integral transforms involving properties of pair relative wavefunctions. The methodology is illustrated with analyses of NA49 and PHENIX correlation data.

## Key claim

Shapes of relative emission sources in heavy-ion reactions can be accessed by expanding correlation functions at low relative velocities in Cartesian harmonics, where the expansion coefficients for correlations are related to those for sources through one-dimensional integral transforms involving pair relative wavefunctions. The method is not confined to correlations dominated by identity interference, contrary to the common approach of representing sources as a single anisotropic Gaussian.

## Method

The correlation function R(q) is expanded in Cartesian harmonics (real, traceless symmetric tensors) rather than complex spherical harmonics, with the expansion coefficients obtained through angular integration or fitting to data. The source function S(r) is similarly expanded, and the Cartesian coefficients of R and S are related through one-dimensional integral transforms involving the Legendre-expanded kernel K_l(q,r) derived from the pair relative wavefunction. The source coefficients are then extracted either by fitting a parameterized source shape (e.g., single or double anisotropic Gaussian) to the correlation coefficients, or by direct imaging of the source coefficients.

## Key numbers

- NA49 central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=17.3 A GeV: pi- - pi- correlation functions analyzed with l=0 and l=2 Cartesian harmonics; a single Gaussian source fails to describe data at low relative momenta, while a two-Gaussian source provides much better description.
- NA49 central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s)=8.9 A GeV and 17.3 A GeV: imaged and 2-Gaussian sources exhibit tails extending over 30 fm of relative distance in the longitudinal z-direction.
- PHENIX central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 A GeV: pi+ - pi+ correlation functions analyzed with l <= 4 angular moments; imaged source shows tail along z-axis and some tail in outward x-direction not present in single Gaussian source.
- For identical pions around midrapidity of a symmetric system, only even-l components are finite; for l <= 4, the finite Cartesian moments are l=0, x^2, y^2, z^2 (l=2) and x^4, y^4, z^4, x^2 y^2, x^2 z^2, y^2 z^2 (l=4), with only 2 independent l=2 components and 3 independent l=4 components due to tracelessness.

## Context

This work extends the standard HBT interferometry approach (Wiedemann and Heinz, 1999; Lisa et al., 2005) that analyzed source shapes only for charged pions using single anisotropic Gaussians and Coulomb-corrected correlation functions. The Cartesian harmonic expansion method (Applequist, 1989; Danielewicz, 2007) provides an alternative that can handle arbitrary pair types and interactions (Coulomb, strong, identity interference) without requiring Coulomb corrections. The methodology enables detailed comparison of experimental source shapes with model predictions, as demonstrated by comparing PHENIX data to the Therminator model (Kisiel et al., 2006) and the AMPT model (Zhang et al., 1999), revealing which spatial directions each model can or cannot reproduce.
