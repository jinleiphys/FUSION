# Ab initio models of atomic nuclei: challenges and new ideas

> This review presents some of the challenges in constructing models of atomic nuclei starting from theoretical descriptions of the strong interaction between nucleons. The focus is on statistical computing and methods for analyzing the link between bulk properties of atomic nuclei, such as radii and binding energies, and the underlying microscopic description of the nuclear interaction. The importance of careful model calibration and uncertainty quantification of theoretical predictions is highlighted.

# Ab initio models of atomic nuclei: challenges and new ideas

## Key claim
The paper argues that while chiral effective field theory (EFT) provides a promising framework for constructing nuclear interactions with systematic uncertainty estimates, one cannot yet confidently claim that uncertainty estimates in ab initio predictions are linked to missing physics at the level of the effective Lagrangian; the details of the power counting, regularization approach, and chosen maximum chiral order remain unresolved choices that give rise to a rich landscape of different chiral interactions.

## Method
The paper reviews the construction of ab initio nuclear models based on chiral EFT potentials, where the nuclear interaction is expanded order-by-order in powers of Q = max[m_pi, k]/Lambda_b, with m_pi the pion mass, k a typical external momentum, and Lambda_b the hard scale. Model calibration is discussed as a maximum likelihood problem involving minimization of a chi-squared function, with parameters (low-energy constants, LECs) optimized first against nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts and cross sections, then against few-nucleon bound-state observables. The review covers the inclusion of model discrepancy terms (interaction, many-body, numerical) in calibration, and discusses Bayesian inference as an emerging framework for handling these uncertainties.

## Key numbers
- Chiral EFT expansion parameter: Q = max[m_pi, k]/Lambda_b, where m_pi is the pion mass and Lambda_b is the hard scale (e.g. the nucleon mass M_N)
- Total number of LECs in typical chiral potentials: between approximately 10 and 20, sometimes a few more
- The NNLO_sat interaction potential reproduces experimental binding energies and charge radii for nuclei up to mass A ~ 50
- The 1.8/2.0 (EM) interaction potential reproduces binding energies and low-energy spectra up to mass A ~ 100, while radii are underestimated
- Leading-order (LO) chiral potentials consist of one-pion exchange plus a nucleonic contact-potential; higher orders include next-to-next-leading order (NNLO) and next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO)

## Context
This review extends the line of work on chiral EFT descriptions of nuclear interactions (Weinberg 1990; van Kolck 1999; Epelbaum 2009; Machleidt 2011) and statistical methods for model calibration in nuclear theory. It contrasts chiral EFT approaches with purely phenomenological potentials such as Argonne V18 (Wiringa 1995) and CD-Bonn (Machleidt 2001), which lack systematic uncertainty estimates. The paper discusses recent efforts to include model discrepancy terms in calibration (Carlsson 2016) and Bayesian inference methods emerging from the ISNET workshop series, enabling future work on quantifying theoretical uncertainties in ab initio predictions for nuclear structure.
