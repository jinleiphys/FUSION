# Continuous unitary transformations in two-level boson systems

> Two-level boson systems displaying a quantum phase transition from a spherical (symmetric) to a deformed (broken) phase are studied. A formalism to diagonalize Hamiltonians with $O(2L+1)$ symmetry for large number of bosons is worked out. Analytical results beyond the simple mean-field treatment are deduced by using the continuous unitary transformations technique. In this scheme, a 1/N expansion for different observables is proposed and allows one to compute the finite-size scaling exponents at the critical point. Analytical and numerical results are compared and reveal the power of the present approach to compute the finite-size corrections in such a context.

## Key claim

The continuous unitary transformations (CUTs) technique, combined with a 1/N expansion, provides analytical finite-size corrections to observables in two-level boson systems across the entire U(2L+1) to O(2L+2) quantum phase transition, but only when the expansion is performed from the symmetric phase side of the critical point.

## Method

The authors studied a boson pairing Hamiltonian with O(2L+1) symmetry, where the lower level is a scalar s boson and the upper level has multipolarity L. They eliminated the s boson via a Holstein-Primakoff boson expansion, then applied continuous unitary transformations using the quasi-particle conserving generator to diagonalize the Hamiltonian order by order in 1/N. The flow equations for the coupling constants were solved analytically up to order 1/N^2 for the symmetric phase, and numerical diagonalization was performed using a basis constructed from SU(1,1) pair operators.

## Key numbers

- Critical point at x_c = 0.5, where the energy surface becomes a flat β^4 surface near β=0
- Ground-state energy per boson in the symmetric phase: e_0(x ≥ x_c) = (1-x)/4 at mean-field level
- Ground-state energy per boson in the broken phase: e_0(x ≤ x_c) = (x/4)(2-3x)/(1-x) at mean-field level
- Expectation value of n_L in the broken phase: ⟨n_L⟩ = N(1-2x)/(2(1-x)) at mean-field level
- Gap in the symmetric phase at leading order: Δ(N) = Ξ(x)^{1/2} where Ξ(x) = x(2x-1)
- Ground-state energy per particle expansion: e_0(N) = h_{0,0,0}^{(0)}(∞) + (1/N)h_{0,0,0}^{(1)}(∞) + (1/N^2)h_{0,0,0}^{(2)}(∞) + (1/N^3)h_{0,0,0}^{(3)}(∞) + O(1/N^4)

## Context

This work extends the CUTs approach previously applied to the Lipkin-Meshkov-Glick model (L=0) by Dusuel et al. (2004, 2005) to general two-level boson models with arbitrary multipolarity L, including the Vibron Model (L=1) and the Interacting Boson Model (L=2). The formalism enables computation of finite-size scaling exponents at the critical point and provides analytical corrections beyond mean-field and Random Phase Approximation treatments, complementing the exact Richardson solution (1968) and the numerical diagonalization method of Pan et al. (1998) and Rowe et al. (2004).
