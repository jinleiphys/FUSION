# Exact solutions of the Boltzmann equation and optimized hydrodynamic approaches for relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Several recent results are reported from work aiming to improve the quantitative precision of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics for relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The dense matter created in such collisions expands in a highly anisotropic manner. Due to viscous effects this also renders the local momentum distribution anisotropic. Optimized hydrodynamic approaches account for these anisotropies already at leading order in a gradient expansion. Recently discovered exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in anisotropically expanding systems provide a powerful testbed for such improved hydrodynamic approximations. We present the latest status of our quest for a formulation of relativistic viscous fluid dynamics that is optimized for applications to relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper reports that anisotropic hydrodynamics (aHydro) and its viscous extension (vaHydro) reproduce exact solutions of the relativistic Boltzmann equation in the Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA) more accurately than standard second-order viscous hydrodynamics (Israel-Stewart and DNMR theories), especially for systems with large viscosity and pressure anisotropy, but the authors note that vaHydro has not yet been worked out for Gubser flow and its expected improvement remains to be demonstrated.

## Method

The authors compare six hydrodynamic approximation schemes (ideal, Navier-Stokes, Israel-Stewart, DNMR, anisotropic hydrodynamics aHydro, and viscous anisotropic hydrodynamics vaHydro) against exact solutions of the RTA Boltzmann equation for two highly symmetric flow profiles: Bjorken flow (longitudinally boost-invariant, transversally homogeneous) and Gubser flow (simultaneous longitudinal and transverse expansion). For each scheme, they derive the evolution equations for the effective temperature and shear stress in the appropriate coordinate system (Milne coordinates for Bjorken flow, de Sitter coordinates for Gubser flow) and compare the resulting time evolution to the exact solution obtained from integral forms of the Boltzmann equation.

## Key numbers

- For Gubser flow, the ideal hydrodynamics solution gives \(\hat{T}_{\mathrm{ideal}}(\rho) = \hat{T}_0/\cosh^{2/3}(\rho)\).
- For Gubser flow, the Navier-Stokes shear stress is \(\hat{\pi}_{NS}^{\eta\eta} = \frac{4}{3}\hat{\eta} \,\tanh \rho\) with \(\hat{\eta}/\hat{s} = \frac{1}{5}\hat{T}\hat{\tau}_\mathrm{rel}\).
- For Gubser flow, the Israel-Stewart evolution equation for the scaled shear stress is \(d\bar{\pi}_{\eta}^{\eta}/d\rho + \frac{4}{3}\left( \bar{\pi}_{\eta}^{\eta}\right)^{2}\tanh \rho +\bar{\pi}_\eta^\eta/\hat{\tau}_\mathrm{rel} =\frac{4}{15}\tanh\rho\).
- For Gubser flow, the DNMR evolution equation adds a term \(\frac{10}{21}\bar{\pi}_\eta^\eta \tanh\rho\) to the Israel-Stewart equation.
- The exact Gubser solution becomes unphysical for large negative \(\rho-\rho_0\) when starting from thermal equilibrium initial conditions at \(\rho_0\) with \(\hat{T}=0.002\).

## Context

This work extends earlier comparisons for Bjorken flow reported by Florkowski (2013, 2014) and Bazow (2013) to the more realistic Gubser flow profile, which includes simultaneous longitudinal and transverse expansion. The exact solutions of the RTA Boltzmann equation for Gubser flow were discovered by Denicol (2014) and Heinz (2015). The paper establishes a performance hierarchy (vaHydro > aHydro > DNMR ~ IS > NS > ideal fluid) that the authors expect to hold for strongly coupled liquids beyond kinetic theory, and suggests that anisotropic hydrodynamics is robust enough for extreme conditions such as those in small collision systems (p+A or p+p).
