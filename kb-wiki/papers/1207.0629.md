# Collective phenomena in the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> A recently developed framework of highly-anisotropic and strongly-dissipative hydrodynamics -- ADHYDRO -- has been introduced and used to analyze the space-time evolution of matter produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The main goal of this analysis was to study the effect of initial highly-anisotropic stages on the final soft hadronic observables typically measured in the experiment. The study was done in the context of the heavy-ion measurements performed at RHIC (Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider) in Brookhaven National Laboratory. Starting from the general assumption about the form of the phase-space distribution function, thermodynamic properties of locally anisotropic systems of particles have been studied and the form of the generalized equation of state has been formulated. The dynamic equations determining the evolution of a highly-anisotropic fluid have been introduced. The form of the entropy source related to the mechanisms leading to thermalization of the system has been defined. In the simplest case of purely-longitudinal and boost-invariant expansion, different features of the model have been analyzed. Using the ADHYDRO model in the general (3+1)D and boost-invariant (2+1)D versions, different possible scenarios of early stages of heavy-ion collisions have been analyzed. The results of the ADHYDRO model have been compared to the results obtained from the reference, perfect-fluid hydrodynamic model -- LHYQUID. The results of the hydrodynamic models were coupled to the statistical Monte-Carlo model THERMINATOR. The following conclusions have been drawn: a) All studied observables are almost insensitive to the initial anisotropic stage provided the initial conditions of the evolution are properly readjusted, b) Complete thermalization of matter may take place only at the times of about 1 fm/c. In this way the early thermalization puzzle may be circumvented.

# Collective phenomena in the early stages of relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The ADHYDRO framework demonstrates that final soft hadronic observables are almost insensitive to the initial highly-anisotropic stage of the collision, provided the initial conditions (specifically the initial entropy density) are properly readjusted; complete thermalization of matter may take place only at times of about 1 fm/c, which may circumvent the early thermalization puzzle.

## Method

The author developed ADHYDRO, a framework of highly-anisotropic and strongly-dissipative hydrodynamics based on a phase-space distribution function that allows for different transverse and longitudinal temperatures (parameterized by the anisotropy parameter x). The dynamic equations were solved in (3+1)D and boost-invariant (2+1)D versions for Au+Au collisions at RHIC, using a generalized equation of state that interpolates between the anisotropic early stage and the realistic QCD equation of state. The hydrodynamic results were coupled to the statistical Monte-Carlo model THERMINATOR to generate final hadronic observables, and compared to results from the perfect-fluid hydrodynamic model LHYQUID.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density: ε_i = 86.76 GeV/fm^3 for all analyzed cases, corresponding to initial central temperature T_i = 485 MeV for the isotropic case
- Initial central entropy density: σ_i = 227 fm^{-3} for x_0 = 1, and σ_i = 151 fm^{-3} for x_0 = 100 and x_0 = 0.032
- Time-scale parameter τ_eq = 0.25 fm, guaranteeing local thermal equilibrium in the whole fireball volume approximately after 1 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 150 MeV
- Centrality classes analyzed: c = 0-5% (b = 2.26 fm), c = 20-30% (b = 7.16 fm), and c = 20-40% (b = 7.84 fm) for Au+Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV
- For classical Boltzmann statistics, the entropy density to particle density relation is σ = 4 n
- The ratio of classical energy density to Bose-Einstein energy density for fixed σ and x is ε/ε_BE ≈ 1.027

## Context

This work extends the framework of anisotropic hydrodynamics introduced by Florkowski and Ryblewski (2009) and applies it to RHIC data, comparing results with the perfect-fluid LHYQUID model of Chojnacki et al. (2007). The analysis addresses the early thermalization puzzle by showing that complete thermalization may occur only at about 1 fm/c, rather than the much shorter times typically required by standard hydrodynamic models. The results enable future studies of whether initial-state momentum anisotropy can be probed through midrapidity observables, and suggest that the generalized equation of state can describe different stages of heavy-ion collision evolution in a uniform way, including the phase transition from QGP to hadron gas.
