# Measuring neutron skin by grazing isobaric collisions

> Neutron skin thickness ($Δr_{\rm np}$) of nuclei and the inferred nuclear symmetry energy are of critical importance to nuclear physics and astrophysics. It is traditionally measured by nuclear processes with significant theoretical uncertainties. We recently proposed an indirect measurement of the $Δr_{\rm np}$ by charged hadron multiplicities in central isobaric collisions at relativistic energies, which are sensitive to nuclear densities. In this Letter, we propose a direct measurement of the $Δr_{\rm np}$ by using net-charge multiplicities in ultra-peripheral (grazing) collisions of those isobars, under the assumption that they are simple superimposition of nucleon-nucleon interactions. We illustrate this novel approach by the TRENTO and URQMD models.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that the net-charge multiplicity ratio in ultra-peripheral (grazing) collisions of isobars (Ru+Ru over Zr+Zr) can directly measure neutron skin thickness, under the assumption that such collisions are a simple superimposition of nucleon-nucleon interactions, an assumption the authors validate only for collisions with fewer than about 10 charged hadrons at midrapidity.

## Method
The authors use the TRENTO model to calculate the proton fraction among participant nucleons in Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions as a function of charged hadron multiplicity, based on four sets of nuclear densities from energy density functional theory (Skyrme-Hartree-Fock with SLy4 and extended SHF with Lc20, Lc47, Lc70). They then compute the net-charge ratio R_dQ using Eq. (3) with the alpha parameter (ratio of net-charge in nn to pp interactions) obtained from PYTHIA simulations. They validate the simple superimposition assumption by comparing these predictions to full URQMD simulations of peripheral collisions with impact parameters between 7 and 20 fm.

## Key numbers
- PYTHIA (Monash tune) gives alpha = -0.352 for the ratio of net-charge in nn to pp interactions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV, with acceptance cuts |eta|<1 and 0.2 < pT < 2 GeV/c (excluding protons with pT<0.4 GeV/c)
- The overall proton fractions for entire Ru and Zr nuclei are 44/96 and 40/96 respectively, giving R_dQ ≈ 1.267 for the whole nuclei
- URQMD simulations of ~4.5×10^8 events per isobar system yield a statistical uncertainty on the symmetry energy slope parameter L_c of roughly 9 MeV using data points at Nch ≤ 10
- The PREX-II result for 208Pb gives Δr_np = 0.283 ± 0.071 fm, leading to L_c = 71.5 ± 22.6 MeV and L = 105 ± 37 MeV
- The difference between SLy4 and Lc47 parameter sets (similar L_c values) gives an uncertainty of ~6 MeV on L_c
- Nuclear deformation effects change R_dQ by only a couple of percent, leading to possibly less than 5 MeV uncertainty in extracted L_c

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous proposal (Li et al., 2019) to use charged hadron multiplicities in central isobar collisions for indirect neutron skin measurement, which was isospin insensitive. The new net-charge observable is isospin sensitive and directly probes the difference between proton and neutron densities. The method complements traditional measurements from low-energy electron and hadron scattering (Frois, 1987; Lapikas, 1993; RocaMaza, 2011; Tsang, 2012; Tarbert, 2013) and parity-violating electron scattering (PREX-II, Abrahamyan, 2012; Adhikari, 2021), which have either large QCD modeling uncertainties or large statistical uncertainties. Combining both the central multiplicity ratio and the grazing net-charge ratio could yield stringent constraints on neutron skin thickness and the symmetry energy slope parameter L_c.
