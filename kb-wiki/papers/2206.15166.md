# Effective spectral function of vector mesons via lifetime analysis

> Effective spectral functions of the $ρ$ meson are reconstructed by considering the lifetimes inside different media using the hadronic transport SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons). Due to inelastic scatterings, resonance lifetimes are dynamically shortened (collisional broadening), even though the employed approach assumes vacuum resonance properties. Analyzing the $ρ$ meson lifetimes allows to quantify an effective broadening of the decay width and spectral function, which is important in order to distinguish dynamical effects from additional genuine medium modifications to the spectral functions, indicating e.g. an onset of chiral symmetry restoration. The broadening of the spectral function in a thermalized system is shown to be consistent with other theoretical calculations. The effective $ρ$ meson spectral function is also presented for the dynamical evolution of heavy-ion collisions, finding a clear correlation of the broadening to system size, which is explained by an observed dependence of the width on the local hadron density. Furthermore, the difference in the results between the thermal system and full collision dynamics is explored, which may point to non-equilibrium effects.

## Key claim

The paper shows that collisional broadening of the ρ meson, arising purely from inelastic scatterings in a transport approach that assumes vacuum resonance properties, produces an effective spectral function that is qualitatively similar to full in-medium model calculations, but quantitatively different, meaning that collisional broadening alone is not sufficient to reproduce experimental dilepton yields, and genuine medium modifications must be included.

## Method

The hadronic transport code SMASH (Simulating Many Accelerated Strongly-interacting Hadrons) was used to simulate ρ mesons in both thermalized infinite matter (periodic box) and full heavy-ion collision systems. The effective width Γ^eff was computed by extracting the average lifetime of ρ mesons from the simulation and inverting τ = 1/Γ, then the dynamic spectral function A^dyn(m) was constructed by replacing the vacuum decay width with Γ^eff(m) in the relativistic Breit-Wigner formula. Comparisons were made between thermal boxes at various temperatures and baryochemical potentials, and between collision systems of different sizes (p+p, C+C, Ar+KCl, Ag+Ag, Au+Au) at HADES beam energies.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum pole mass of the ρ meson: M0 = 776 MeV; vacuum decay width: Γ0 = 0.149 GeV
- Thermal box calculations performed at temperatures T ∈ {120, 150, 180} MeV, each with baryochemical potentials μB ∈ {0, 330, 450} MeV
- Equilibration in box calculations checked after t = 10^4 fm
- Nuclear ground state density: n0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}
- Central cell of Au+Au collision at E_kin = 1.23 GeV showed stable thermodynamic conditions (T, μB) ≈ (80, 900) MeV between 7 ≤ t ≤ 15 fm
- C+C collisions studied at E_kin = 1 AGeV and 2 AGeV; Au+Au collisions studied with four centrality classes
- Enhanced production peak in collision systems at approximately 0.5 GeV from N*(1520) → N + ρ decay

## Context

This work extends the SMASH transport approach (Weil 2016) by quantifying the dynamical collisional broadening that arises even when vacuum resonance properties are assumed, complementing earlier coarse-graining methods (Staudenmaier 2018) that introduced full in-medium modifications. The results are compared to full in-medium model calculations of the ρ meson propagator (Rapp 1999), showing that collisional broadening from hadronic interactions contributes significantly to medium modifications irrespective of chiral symmetry restoration, but that genuine self-energy modifications are still needed to match experimental dilepton data from CERES/NA45, NA60, and HADES. The density-dependent universal relation found for the effective width is reminiscent of semiclassical calculations used in other transport approaches such as PHSD (Bratkovskaya 2008) and GiBUU (Larionov 2020).
