# Stability and instability of strange dwarfs

> More than 20 years ago, Glendenning, Kettner and Weber proposed the existence of stable white dwarfs with a core of strange quark matter. More recently, by studying radial modes, Alford, Harris and Sachdeva concluded instead that those objects are unstable. We aim to clarify this issue. We investigate the stability of these objects by looking at their radial oscillations while incorporating boundary conditions at the quark-hadron interface, corresponding to either a rapid or a slow conversion of hadrons into quarks. Our analysis shows that objects of this type are stable if the star is not strongly perturbed, and ordinary matter cannot transform into strange quark matter because of the Coulomb barrier separating the two components. On the other hand, ordinary matter can be transformed into strange quark matter if the star undergoes a violent process, as in the preliminary stages of a type Ia supernova, and this causes the system to become unstable and to collapse into a strange quark star. In this way, accretion-induced collapse of strange dwarfs can be facilitated, and km-sized objects with subsolar masses can be produced.

## Key claim

Strange dwarfs (white dwarfs with a strange quark matter core) are stable under small perturbations because a Coulomb barrier prevents ordinary matter from converting into strange quark matter, but they become unstable and collapse into a strange quark star if a violent process (such as the early stages of a type Ia supernova) forces matter above the neutron drip density, allowing rapid conversion.

## Method

The authors solved the Tolman–Oppenheimer–Volkoff (TOV) equation for stellar configurations with a piecewise equation of state: the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) EoS for nuclear matter and an MIT bag model EoS for strange quark matter. They then studied radial oscillations using the Schwarzschild metric, applying two distinct boundary conditions at the quark-hadron interface: a "slow" condition (continuous displacement, fixed quark baryon number) and a "rapid" condition (discontinuous displacement, allowing mass transfer between phases). The eigenvalues of the fundamental radial mode were computed for each case.

## Key numbers

- The neutron drip density is \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{drip} \sim 4 \times 10^{11} \mathrm{\,g/cm^{3}}\); configurations are stable only when the maximum nuclear matter density \(\varepsilon_\mathrm{t}\) is below this value.
- For a slow transition in a star with mass \(M \simeq 0.02 \, \mathrm{M}_\odot\) and \(B_\mathrm{core} \simeq 2.69 \times 10^{55}\), the fundamental mode is stable with \(\omega^2 = 0.788275 \mathrm{\,Hz^2}\).
- For the same star under a rapid transition, the fundamental mode is unstable with \(\omega^2 = -1.62785 \mathrm{\,Hz^2}\).
- For a star at the Chandrasekhar limit with \(B_\mathrm{core} \gtrsim 10^{46}\), the e-folding time of the instability drops below 1 second.
- The rate of strangelets hitting a star is \(F \sim (1.39 \times 10^{30} \mathrm{s}^{-1}) A^{-1}(M/M_\odot)(R/R_\odot)\); a Chandrasekhar-mass WD with radius \(\sim 3000\) km can accumulate \(B_\mathrm{core} \sim 10^{45}\) in 5 Gy.
- The maximum mass of a strange dwarf in the slow scenario is \(M_{\mathrm{max}} \sim 0.996 M_\odot\) for \(B_\mathrm{core} = 10^{55}\).

## Context

This work resolves a 20-year-old disagreement between Glendenning, Kettner and Weber (1995), who claimed strange dwarfs are stable, and Alford, Harris and Sachdeva (2017), who found them unstable via radial mode analysis. The resolution is that the two groups implicitly used different boundary conditions at the quark-hadron interface: Glendenning et al. assumed a continuous eigenfunction (slow conversion, stable), while Alford et al. used a smoothed EoS that effectively allowed rapid conversion (unstable). The results enable a new channel for accretion-induced collapse (AIC) of white dwarfs into kilometre-sized, sub-solar-mass strange quark stars, potentially explaining objects like SAX J1808.4-3658, and connect to dark-matter-as-strangelets scenarios discussed by Madsen (1988) and others.
