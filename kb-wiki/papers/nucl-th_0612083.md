# Isovector deformation and its link to the neutron shell closure

> DWBA analysis of the inelastic $^{30-40}$S$(p,p')$ and $^{18-22}$O$(p,p')$ scattering data measured in the inverse kinematics has been performed to determine the isoscalar ($δ_0$) and isovector ($δ_1$) deformation lengths of the 2$^+_1$ excitations in the Sulfur and Oxygen isotopes using a compact folding approach. A systematic $N$-dependence of $δ_0$ and $δ_1$ has been established which shows a link between $δ_1$ and the neutron-shell closure. Strong isovector deformations were found in several cases, e.g., the 2$^+_1$ state in $^{20}$O where $δ_1$ is nearly three times larger than $δ_0$. These results confirm the relation $δ_1>δ_0$ anticipated from the core polarization by the valence neutrons in the open-shell (neutron rich) nuclei. The effect of neutron shell closure at N=14 or 16 has been discussed based on the folding model analysis of the inelastic $^{22}$O+$p$ scattering data at 46.6 MeV/u measured recently at GANIL.

## Key claim
The isovector deformation length δ₁ of the 2⁺₁ state in neutron-rich Sulfur and Oxygen isotopes is systematically linked to neutron shell closure, with δ₁ reaching a minimum at magic neutron numbers (N=20 in Sulfur, and either N=14 or 16 in Oxygen). The paper hedges that the evidence for shell closure at N=16 in Oxygen is suggestive but not definitive, requiring further measurements on ²⁴O.

## Method
The author performed a folding-model plus DWBA analysis of elastic and inelastic (p,p') scattering data for ³⁰⁻⁴⁰S and ¹⁸⁻²²O isotopes measured in inverse kinematics. Proton deformation lengths δₚ were fixed using experimental B(E2↑) values, while neutron deformation lengths δₙ were adjusted iteratively to fit the measured inelastic cross sections; from δₚ and δₙ, the isoscalar δ₀ and isovector δ₁ deformation lengths were derived using a compact folding approach with the CDM3Y6 interaction and HFB ground-state densities.

## Key numbers
- In ³⁶S (N=20), δ₁ reaches its minimum value, correlated with the minimum of B(E2↑) at the magic number N=20.
- In ³⁸S, δₙ is larger than δₚ by about 30%, and δ₁ is larger than δ₀ by about 64%.
- In ²⁰O, δ₁ is nearly three times larger than δ₀, with the Lane form factor F₁ contributing 40-50% of the total inelastic cross section.
- For ²²O, the upper limit on the neutron deformation length is δₙ ≤ 1.2 δₚ, giving δ₁ ≤ 1.1 fm.
- The (p,p') cross section for the 2⁺₁ state of ²²O at 46.6 MeV/u is about 3 to 4 times smaller than that for ²⁰O at 43 MeV/u over a wide angular range.

## Context
This work extends the earlier folding-model analysis by Khoa (2003) and the QRPA-based analysis by Khan et al. (2002) of the same Sulfur and Oxygen data, confirming the isoscalar rule for ³⁰⁻³⁴S and revealing strong isovector deformation in open-shell neutron-rich nuclei. The results contradict the simple expectation that adding valence neutrons in ²²O would increase collectivity; instead, the sharp drop in δ₁ and cross section supports the neutron shell closure at N=14 or 16, as suggested by the GANIL measurement of Becheva et al. (2006). The method enables determination of δ₁ from (p,p') data alone, which is essential for unstable nuclei where (n,n') measurements are not feasible.
