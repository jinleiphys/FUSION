# Correlations of Conserved Quantities at Finite Baryon Density

> Correlations involving the seven conserved quantities, namely energy, baryon number, electric charge, strangeness, and the three components of momentum, give rise to correlations in heavy-ion collisions. Through the utilization of a simple one-dimensional hydrodynamic model, we calculate the evolution of the entire $7\times7$ matrix of correlations as a function of relative spatial rapidity. This comprehensive analysis accounts for finite baryon density, which results in off-diagonal correlations between the charge-related quantities and the energy-momentum quantities. These correlations in coordinate space are subsequently transformed into correlations in momentum space using statistical weighting. The entire matrix of correlations is revealed to be highly sensitive to the equation of state (EoS), viscosity, and diffusivity.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the full \(7\times 7\) matrix of correlations among energy, baryon number, electric charge, strangeness, and the three momentum components can be calculated using a simple one-dimensional (Bjorken) hydrodynamic model, and that these correlations are highly sensitive to the equation of state, viscosity, and diffusivity, but the authors caution that the neglect of transverse flow and boost-invariance violations means the results are only crude estimates, useful for determining whether further realistic modeling is warranted.

## Method

The authors employ a longitudinally boost-invariant (Bjorken) one-dimensional hydrodynamic model without transverse flow, evolving the entire \(7\times 7\) matrix of correlations as a function of relative spatial rapidity. The equation of state is constructed by adding interaction contributions to a hadron-gas free energy density in the canonical ensemble, with the total free energy expressed as the sum of a non-interacting gas term and an interaction term. Correlations in coordinate space are transformed into momentum-space correlations using statistical weighting (thermal sampling of particles with additional weight factors), and the evolution accounts for finite baryon density which produces off-diagonal correlations between charge-related and energy-momentum quantities.

## Key numbers

* The correlation matrix is \(7\times 7\), involving energy, three components of momentum, baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness.
* The speed of sound is expressed in terms of fluctuation observables: \(c_s^2 = \frac{(P+\epsilon)/T + \rho_a M_a}{\xi + \zeta_a M_a}\), where \(\xi \equiv \partial\epsilon/\partial T = \frac{1}{T^2 V}\langle\delta E\delta E\rangle\), \(\zeta_a \equiv \frac{1}{T}\partial\epsilon/\partial\tilde{\mu}_a = \frac{1}{TV}\langle\delta E\delta Q_a\rangle\), and \(\chi_{ab} \equiv \partial\rho_a/\partial\tilde{\mu}_b = \frac{1}{V}\langle\delta Q_a\delta Q_b\rangle\).
* The charge fluctuation matrix satisfies \(\chi_{ab} = (\chi^{-1}_{\text{free}} + \chi^{-1}_{\text{int}})^{-1}_{ab}\), where the inverse susceptibilities from free and interaction contributions add linearly.
* The energy-charge fluctuation is given by \(\zeta_a = \frac{1}{T}\chi_{ab}\partial\epsilon/\partial\rho_b\).
* The specific heat (energy fluctuation) is \(\xi = \left.\partial\epsilon/\partial T\right|_{\vec{\rho}} + \zeta_a\chi^{-1}_{ab}\zeta_b\).

## Context

This work extends the charge-balance correlation framework previously applied to baryon number, electric charge, and strangeness (Pratt 2015, Pratt 2016, Pratt 2018, Pratt 2021) by including energy and momentum correlations and accounting for finite baryon density. The authors note that energy and momentum correlations do not spread diffusively but instead evolve via viscous hydrodynamics, and that at non-zero average charge density the energy and momentum correlations mix with charge correlations. The paper enables future work to determine whether such correlations are large enough to be observable above competing effects from initial-state inhomogeneities and jets, or whether more realistic three-dimensional modeling is required.
