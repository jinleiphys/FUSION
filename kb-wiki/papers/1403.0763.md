# Revised density of magnetized nuclear matter at the neutron drip line

> We study the onset of neutron drip in high density matter in the presence of magnetic field. It has been found that for systems having only protons and electrons, in the presence of magnetic field >~ 10^{15}G, the neutronization occurs at a density which is atleast an order of magnitude higher compared to that in a nonmagnetic system. In a system with heavier ions, the effect of magnetic field, however, starts arising at a much higher field, >~ 10^{17}G. These results may have important implications in high magnetic neutron stars and white dwarfs and, in general, nuclear astrophysics when the system is embedded with high magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for a system of only protons and electrons, the density at which neutronization (inverse beta decay) begins increases by at least an order of magnitude compared to the non-magnetic case when the magnetic field exceeds approximately 10^15 G. For systems with heavier ions, a significant effect on the neutron drip density only arises at much higher fields, above approximately 10^17 G, and the drip density then increases linearly with the field.

## Method

The authors study the onset of neutronization in a neutron-proton-electron gas by solving the chemical equilibrium condition for Fermi energies, accounting for Landau quantization of electrons and protons in a magnetic field. For a system with heavier ions, they adopt the Harrison-Wheeler formalism to determine the equilibrium composition by minimizing the total energy density of a mixture of ions, electrons, and neutrons, using the semi-empirical mass formula of Green based on the liquid drop nuclear model. The electron Fermi energy at the drip point is computed from the equilibrium conditions, and the drip density is then derived as a function of magnetic field strength.

## Key numbers

*   For a neutron-proton-electron gas, the neutronization density increases linearly with magnetic field, and for the increment to be at least an order of magnitude compared to the nonmagnetic case, the magnetic field must be approximately 10^15 G.
*   For the system with heavier ions, the non-magnetic neutron drip density is 3.25 x 10^11 gm/cc.
*   At a magnetic field of B_D^e = 20000 (corresponding to 8.83 x 10^17 G), the drip density for the ionic system is 4.39 x 10^12 gm/cc.
*   The drip density for the ionic system increases by an order of magnitude compared to the nonmagnetic gas for a field of approximately 6.4 x 10^17 G.
*   The electron Fermi energy at the drip point for the ionic system is 47.2367 times the electron rest mass energy (E^F_e / m_e c^2 = 47.2367).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of magnetic field effects on neutron star matter, such as those by Bandyopadhyay et al. (1997) who used a Thomas-Fermi model, and by Lai (2001) who investigated neutronization conditions. The authors also compare their results to numerical computations by Chamel et al. (2012) on the variation of composition and pressure at the onset of neutron drip with magnetic field, noting that their own analytical approach reveals oscillatory behavior in the drip density at lower field values that was missed in prior work. The findings have implications for models of highly magnetized white dwarfs proposed by Mukhopadhyay et al. (2013), where the modified drip density may affect stability against neutronization.
