# Flavor Decomposition of the Nucleon

> I review some recent developments in the study of quark flavor distributions in the nucleon, including (i) valence quark distributions and the quark-hadron duality prediction for the x -> 1 d/u ratio (ii) sea quark asymmetries and electromagnetic form factors (iii) strange quarks in the nucleon.

# Flavor Decomposition of the Nucleon

## Key claim
The paper reviews evidence that the naive picture of the nucleon as three non-relativistic valence quarks in a perturbative sea is inadequate, and argues that non-perturbative dynamics, such as pion and kaon clouds, are required to simultaneously explain valence quark distributions at large x, light sea quark asymmetries, and electromagnetic form factors. The key hedge is that the quark-hadron duality prediction for the d/u ratio at x → 1 (approximately 0.25) is numerically similar to the perturbative QCD prediction (1/5) above Q² ≈ 5 GeV², but whether this is coincidence or common underlying physics remains unsettled.

## Method
The paper reviews three topics: (1) the valence d/u ratio at large x, using quark-hadron duality to relate the elastic structure function moments to the ratio F₂ⁿ/F₂ᵖ and then to d/u; (2) the light antiquark asymmetry (d̄/ū), modeled via a pion cloud with N and Δ recoil states plus Pauli blocking effects, with parameters taken from measured axial elastic N and NΔ transition form factors; (3) strange quark asymmetries (s − s̄) and strange form factors, modeled via a kaon cloud (KY components with Y = Λ, Σ) using two different KNY vertex form factors (invariant-mass dependent and four-momentum transfer dependent).

## Key numbers
- The quark-hadron duality prediction for the d/u ratio in the x → 1 limit, assuming dipole form factors at large Q², gives d/u → (μₚ² − 4μₙ²)/(μₙ² − 4μₚ²) ≈ 0.25 as Q² → ∞.
- The perturbative QCD prediction for the d/u ratio at x → 1 is 1/5 (0.2).
- The pion cloud model gives an overall pion probability in the proton of approximately 10–15%.
- The kaon cloud contribution to the s − s̄ asymmetry uses a kaon probability of approximately 3%.
- The SAMPLE Collaboration measurement of the strange magnetic form factor G_Mˢ at Q² = 0.1 GeV² gives a value +0.61 ± 0.17 ± 0.21 (statistical and systematic uncertainties).
- The HAPPEX experiment at Jefferson Lab measured a combination of G_Eˢ and G_Mˢ at a value of r ≈ 0.4.

## Context
This review extends the line of work on non-perturbative quark flavor structure initiated by Field and Feynman (Pauli blocking), Thomas (pion cloud model, 1983), and the operator product expansion interpretation of quark-hadron duality by de Rújula, Georgi and Politzer. It directly addresses the controversy over the d/u ratio at large x highlighted by the analyses of Melnitchouk and Thomas (1996) and Bodek (1999), which showed that nuclear corrections in deuterium may cause the neutron structure function to be significantly underestimated. The paper enables future semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering measurements of π⁺/π⁻ ratios at Jefferson Lab's 12 GeV upgrade to cleanly extract the d/u ratio free of nuclear contamination, and motivates improved precision on strange form factors from HAPPEX II and subsequent experiments.
