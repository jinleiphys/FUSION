# Non-perturbative Enhancement of Heavy Quark-Antiquark Pair Annihilation in the Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The annihilation of heavy particles close to thermal equilibrium, which plays a prominent role in the chemical equilibration of heavy quarks in the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) as well as in many classic dark matter scenarios, is reexamined. We derive a scattering amplitude that resums near-threshold attractive interaction of the annihilating particles in terms of the non-relativistic scattering $T$-matrix and Green's function, thereby capturing the pertinent Sommerfeld enhancement from both nonperturbative scattering state and bound state solutions. The derived formula is of such generality that it applies to arbitrary partial wave processes of two-particle annihilation, and enables to incorporate finite widths of the annihilating particles. In a screened potential model, the non-perturbative scattering amplitude is computed and the Sommerfeld enhancement is identified for heavy quark-antiquark annihilation in the QGP.

## Key claim

The paper derives a general formula for the non-perturbative scattering amplitude of heavy quark-antiquark annihilation that resums near-threshold attractive interactions, capturing Sommerfeld enhancement from both nonperturbative scattering state and bound state solutions; the authors show that the bound state contribution dominates the enhancement and agrees qualitatively with lattice QCD estimates, but note that heavy quark chemical equilibration is unlikely in current heavy ion experiments at RHIC and the LHC.

## Method

The authors derive a master formula for the non-perturbative Q\bar{Q} scattering amplitude by expressing it in terms of the non-relativistic Green's function and the elastic scattering T-matrix, which resums the attractive interaction in the color-singlet channel via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. They solve the partial-wave T-matrix equation numerically using a matrix inversion method for a Yukawa (screened Coulomb) potential model, computing the Sommerfeld enhancement factor for both s-wave and p-wave scattering states and bound states. The thermally averaged enhancement factor is then obtained by Boltzmann-weighted averaging over the energy-dependent s-wave enhancement factor.

## Key numbers

* For the s-wave Coulomb potential with coupling constant α=0.47, quark mass m_Q=5 GeV, and Debye screening mass m_D=0.01 GeV, the analytical Sommerfeld enhancement factor is S_0^{Coulomb}(p)=π/ϵ/(1-e^{-π/ϵ}) where ϵ=p/(μα).
* With a Yukawa potential (α=0.47, m_D=0.4 GeV, zero quark widths), the s-wave enhancement factor shows a large enhancement at low energies that becomes stronger for larger coupling constants (α=0.47, 0.35, 0.20).
* The bound state solution for s-wave (α=0.47, m_D=0.4 GeV, Γ=0.1 GeV) yields a binding energy of E=-0.13 GeV.
* For the p-wave case with α=0.47, m_D=0.1 GeV, and Γ=0.05 GeV, the bound state has binding energy E=-0.01 GeV.
* The thermally averaged s-wave enhancement factor from the bound state solution is boosted by one to two orders of magnitude toward low temperatures compared to the perturbative evaluation.

## Context

This work extends the study of Sommerfeld enhancement in heavy quark-antiquark annihilation in the Quark-Gluon Plasma, previously examined within non-relativistic QCD by Bodeker (2012) and Kim (2016) using lattice spectral functions. The derived master formula generalizes existing treatments beyond s-wave scattering to arbitrary partial waves and incorporates finite quark width effects, enabling comparison with lattice NRQCD results from Kim (2016). The findings are relevant for heavy quark chemical equilibration in heavy ion collisions and for dark matter annihilation scenarios studied by Hisano (2002, 2004) and Arkani-Hamed (2008).
