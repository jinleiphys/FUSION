# Electromagnetic transitions in near-threshold resonances

> Near-threshold collectivization of continuum shell model eigenstates is investigated in $^{20}$O on the example of B(E$λ$) decays of 4$^+$ states in the vicinity of elastic and inelastic neutron threshold. Changes of the electromagnetic transition probabilities as a function of the continuum-coupling strength are explained by the corresponding evolution of the double poles of the scattering matrix.

# Electromagnetic transitions in near-threshold resonances

## Key claim

Near-threshold collectivization of shell model eigenstates in $^{20}$O, as seen in B(E$\lambda$) decays of 4$^+$ states, arises from the interplay between internal configuration mixing by nuclear interactions and external configuration mixing via decay channels; the effect is controlled by the proximity of double poles (exceptional points) of the scattering matrix, and the strongest collectivization occurs at a specific neutron energy determined by the balance between the Coulomb-plus-centrifugal barrier and the continuum coupling.

## Method

The authors used the Shell Model Embedded in the Continuum (SMEC) to describe $^{20}$O as an open quantum system, dividing Hilbert space into subspaces with 0 and 1 particle in the scattering continuum. They employed the WBP− interaction in the full $psd$ model space for the internal shell model, with a Wigner-Bartlett contact force for the continuum-coupling interaction (spin-exchange parameter $\alpha = 0.73$), and generated single-particle and scattering wave functions from a Woods-Saxon potential. B(E2) and B(E1) reduced transition probabilities for decays of the $4^+_3$ and $4^+_4$ resonances to bound $2^+_1$ and $3^-_1$ states were computed as a function of the continuum-coupling strength $V_0$, and the results were interpreted via energy trajectories of exceptional points (double poles of the scattering matrix) and the continuum-coupling correlation energy.

## Key numbers

- The elastic threshold crossing of the exceptional point trajectory for the $4^+_3$–$4^+_4$ pair occurs at $V_0 = -129$ MeV fm$^3$, $E = 428$ keV above the elastic reaction channel.
- The elastic threshold crossing of the exceptional point trajectory for the $4^+_3$–$4^+_2$ pair occurs at $V_0 = -417$ MeV fm$^3$, $E = 38$ keV above the elastic reaction channel.
- At $V_0 = -50$ MeV fm$^3$, the minimum of the continuum-coupling correlation energy for both $4^+_3$ and $4^+_4$ appears at $E_n^* \simeq 200$ keV.
- At $V_0 = -50$ MeV fm$^3$ and 200 keV above the elastic threshold, the neutron decay widths are $\Gamma(4^+_4) = 97.9$ keV and $\Gamma(4^+_3) = 44.4$ keV.
- At $V_0 = -200$ MeV fm$^3$, $\Gamma(4^+_3) = 2.25$ MeV and $\Gamma(4^+_4) = 61.3$ keV; at $V_0 = -400$ MeV fm$^3$, $\Gamma(4^+_3) = 9.3$ MeV and $\Gamma(4^+_4) = 10.2$ keV.
- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters used: $R_0 = 1.27 A^{1/3}$ fm, $a = 0.67$ fm, $V_{\rm SO} = 5.97$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the conjecture of Okołowicz et al. (2012) that near-threshold collectivity in nuclei arises from the interplay of internal and external (continuum) configuration mixing, studied previously in light nuclei by Fick (1978), Grancey (2016), Wiescher (2017), Freer (2014, 2018), and others. It uses the SMEC framework of Bennaceur et al. (2000) and Okołowicz et al. (2003) to demonstrate that the evolution of electromagnetic transition probabilities with continuum-coupling strength is governed by exceptional points of the scattering matrix, and that the resulting scale separation of decay times (superradiant $4^+_3$ and trapped $4^+_4$ states) can be observed in both gamma and neutron decay channels. The results enable future experimental verification of unitary shell model predictions for near-threshold resonances and provide a method to extract information about the off-shell behavior of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction from decay properties.
