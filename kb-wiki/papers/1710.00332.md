# Charmonium Coherent Photoproduction and Hadroproduction with Effects of Quark Gluon Plasma

> We study the charmonium coherent photoproduction and hadroproduction consistently with modifications from both cold and hot nuclear matters. The strong electromagnetic fields from fast moving nucleus interact with the other target nucleus, producing abundant charmonium in the extremely low transverse momentum region $p_T<0.1$ GeV/c. This results in significative enhancement of $J/ψ$ nuclear modification factor in semi-central and peripheral collisions. In the middle $p_T$ region such as $p_T<3\sim 5$ GeV/c, $J/ψ$ final yield is dominated by the combination process of single charm and anti-charm quarks moving in the deconfined matter, $c+\bar c\rightarrow J/ψ+g$. In the higher $p_T$ region, $J/ψ$ production are mainly from parton initial hard scatterings at the beginning of nucleus-nucleus collisions and decay of B hadrons. We include all of these production mechanisms and explain the experimental data well in different colliding centralities and transverse momentum regions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed enhancement of the $J/\psi$ nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$ in the extremely low transverse momentum region ($p_T<0.1$ GeV/c) in semi-central and peripheral Pb-Pb collisions at LHC energies can be explained by including coherent photoproduction from strong electromagnetic fields, in addition to standard hadroproduction mechanisms (primordial production, regeneration, and B-hadron decay), with all components subject to hot QGP medium modifications.

## Method

The authors solve a classical Boltzmann transport equation for charmonium phase-space distributions in a (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic QGP background, including loss terms (gluon dissociation with temperature-dependent cross sections) and gain terms (regeneration via $c+\bar c\rightarrow J/\psi+g$ detailed balance). They compute coherent photoproduction using the Equivalent Photon Approximation with realistic nuclear form factors and Glauber-based photon-nucleus cross sections, then propagate photoproduced $J/\psi$ through the same QGP medium. The total yield combines initial hadroproduction (scaled from pp collisions with shadowing and Cronin effects), regeneration, B-hadron decay contributions, and photoproduction, with all components evaluated at forward rapidity $2.5<y<4$ for $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=2.76$ TeV Pb-Pb collisions.

## Key numbers

- Photoproduced $J/\psi$ mean transverse momentum: $\langle p_T\rangle_{J/\psi}\approx 0.055$ GeV/c
- QGP initial maximum temperature for central collisions ($b=0$ fm, $N_p=406$): $T_0^{\rm QGP}/T_c = 2.6$, with QGP lifetime $\tau_f^{\rm QGP}=7.3$ fm/c
- QGP initial maximum temperature for peripheral collisions ($b=12$ fm, $N_p=35$): $T_0^{\rm QGP}/T_c = 1.5$, with QGP lifetime $\tau_f^{\rm QGP}=2.3$ fm/c
- pp $J/\psi$ hadroproduction fit parameters at 2.76 TeV: $n=4.0$, $\langle p_T^2\rangle =7.8\ \rm{(GeV/c)^2}$, $d\sigma_{J/\psi}^{pp}/dy=2.3\ \mu b$ in forward rapidity
- Charm pair production cross section in pp: $d\sigma_{c\bar c}^{pp}/d\eta=0.38$ mb in forward rapidity at 2.76 TeV
- Photon-nucleus cross section parametrization: $b_V=4\ \rm{GeV^{-2}}$, $X=0.00406\ \mu b$, $\epsilon=0.65$
- Non-prompt $J/\psi$ fraction: $f_B=0.04+0.023p_T/(\rm{GeV/c})$
- QGP formation time: $\tau_0\sim 0.6$ fm/c

## Context

This work extends the charmonium transport approach of Chen (2015) and Zhou (2014) by consistently including coherent photoproduction alongside hadroproduction mechanisms (primordial production, regeneration, B-decay) in semi-central collisions where both QGP and strong electromagnetic fields coexist. It builds on the observation by the ALICE Collaboration (Adam, 2015) of enhanced $J/\psi$ production at extremely low $p_T$ in peripheral Pb-Pb collisions, and on the Equivalent Photon Approximation framework developed by Fermi (1924), Weizsacker (1934), and Williams. The results enable a unified description of $J/\psi$ production across all centralities and $p_T$ regions at LHC energies, and motivate further studies of photoproduction as a probe of nuclear gluon densities at small Bjorken-$x$ in the presence of hot medium effects.
