# Glauber theory of initial- and final-state interactions in (p,2p) scattering

> We develop the Glauber theory description of initial- and final-state interactions (IFSI) in quasielastic A(p,2p) scattering. We study the IFSI-distortion effects both for the inclusive and exclusive conditions. In inclusive reaction the important new effect is an interaction between the two sets of the trajectories which enter the calculation of IFSI-distorted one-body density matrix for inclusive (p,2p) scattering and are connected with incoherent elastic rescatterings of the initial and final protons on spectator nucleons. We demonstrate that IFSI-distortions of the missing momentum distribution are large over the whole range of missing momentum both for inclusive and exclusive reactions and affect in a crucial way the interpretation of the BNL data on (p,2p) scattering. Our numerical results show that in the region of missing momentum $p_{m}\lsim 100-150$ MeV/c the incoherent IFSI increase nuclear transparency by 5-10\%. The incoherent IFSI become dominant at $p_{m}\gsim 200$ MeV/c.

## Key claim

The paper develops a Glauber theory description of initial- and final-state interactions (IFSI) in quasielastic A(p,2p) scattering, demonstrating that IFSI distortions of the missing momentum distribution are large over the entire range of missing momentum for both inclusive and exclusive reactions, and that these effects crucially affect the interpretation of BNL data. The authors hedge that their factorized approximation for hard pp scattering and soft IFSI may be questionable due to the strong energy dependence of the hard pp cross section, but argue that IFSI-distortion effects are typically much stronger than the expected magnitude of off-shell effects.

## Method

The authors developed a Glauber model formalism for hard (p,2p) scattering, assuming factorization of hard pp scattering and soft IFSI of the fast protons with spectator nucleons. They calculated the distorted spectral function and missing momentum distribution using the independent particle shell model for the target nucleus, neglecting short-range correlations, and evaluated both coherent IFSI (for exclusive reactions) and incoherent IFSI (for inclusive reactions) using a one-channel Glauber model with a standard high-energy parameterization of the proton-nucleon profile function. The numerical calculations exploited the small scattering angle of the final protons in the laboratory frame to simplify the integration over spectator nucleon coordinates.

## Key numbers

- In the region of missing momentum p_m less than or approximately 100-150 MeV/c, incoherent IFSI increase nuclear transparency by 5-10%.
- Incoherent IFSI become dominant at p_m greater than or approximately 200 MeV/c.
- The analysis covers the region of missing momentum p_m less than or approximately 300 MeV/c.
- At incident beam momentum approximately 10 GeV/c, the laboratory frame scattering angle theta_l.f. is approximately 25 degrees.
- The interaction radius for soft pN-scattering R_int = sqrt(2B(pN)) is approximately 0.8 fm.
- The ratio between the Fermi correlation length l_F approximately 3/k_F and the interaction length l_int approximately 4/(sigma_tot(pN) * average nuclear density) is approximately 0.25.

## Context

This work extends the Glauber analysis previously applied to (e,e'p) scattering by Nikolaev et al. (NSZ) to the (p,2p) reaction, where the relative effect of incoherent rescatterings is enhanced due to the increased number of fast protons propagating through the nuclear medium. The authors criticize the earlier Glauber formalism applied to inclusive (p,2p) reactions by Kohama et al. (KYS) for making unjustified approximations that led to loss of IFSI-distortion effects. The analysis enables a baseline comparison for understanding whether off-diagonal rescatterings or color transparency effects are really important in the BNL experiment on large-angle (p,2p) scattering at beam momenta 6-12 GeV/c.
