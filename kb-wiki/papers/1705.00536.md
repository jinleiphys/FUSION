# Novel self-similar rotating solutions of non-ideal transverse magnetohydrodynamics

> The evolution of electromagnetic and thermodynamic fields in a non-ideal fluid are studied in the framework of ultrarelativistic transverse magnetohydrodynamics (MHD), which is essentially characterized by electric and magnetic fields being transverse to the fluid velocity. Extending the method of self-similar solutions of relativistic hydrodynamics to the case of non-conserved charges, the differential equations of non-ideal transverse MHD are solved, and two novel sets of self-similar solutions are derived. The first set turns out to be a boost-invariant and exact solution, which is characterized by non-rotating electric and magnetic fields. The second set is a non-boost-invariant solution, which is characterized by rotating electric and magnetic fields. The rotation occurs with increasing rapidity $η$, as the angular velocity is defined by $ω_{0}\equiv\frac{\partialζ}{\partialη}=\frac{\partialϕ}{\partialη}$, with $ζ$ and $ϕ$ being the angles of electric and magnetic vectors with respect to a certain axis in the local rest frame of the fluid. For both sets of solutions, the electric and magnetic fields are either parallel or anti-parallel to each other. Performing a complete numerical analysis, the effects of finite electric conductivity as well as electric and magnetic susceptibilities of the medium on the evolution of rotating and non-rotating MHD solutions are explored, and the interplay between the angular velocity $ω_{0}$ and these quantities is scrutinized. The lifetime of electromagnetic fields and the evolution of the temperature of the electromagnetized fluid are shown to be affected by $ω_{0}$.

## Key claim

The paper derives two sets of self-similar solutions for non-ideal transverse magnetohydrodynamics: a boost-invariant, non-rotating exact solution and a non-boost-invariant, rotating approximate solution, where the rotation of electric and magnetic fields is characterized by an angular velocity ω₀ that depends on the electric conductivity and electric and magnetic susceptibilities of the medium.

## Method

The authors extend the method of self-similar solutions from relativistic hydrodynamics to the case of non-conserved charges, solving the coupled differential equations of non-ideal transverse MHD with finite electric conductivity σ and electric and magnetic susceptibilities χₑ and χₘ. They parameterize the electromagnetic fields in terms of their magnitudes E and B and angles ζ and φ relative to an axis in the local rest frame, then derive a second-order nonlinear differential equation for a function ℳ(τ) that describes the deviation from the frozen-flux theorem. Numerical solutions are obtained for this master equation to study the interplay between ω₀, σ, χₑ, and χₘ.

## Key numbers

- Magnetic field strength at RHIC: eB ∼ 1.5 mπ², with mπ = 0.14 GeV
- Magnetic field strength at LHC: eB ∼ 15 mπ²
- Conversion: eB = 1 GeV² corresponds to B ∼ 1.7 × 10²⁰ Gauss
- Magnetic field decay time in heavy ion collisions: τ ∼ 1–2 fm/c
- The boost-invariant solution gives B(τ) ∝ τ⁻¹
- The non-rotating electric field solution gives E ∝ τ⁻¹ exp(−f(σ, χₑ)τ), where f is a positive function of σ and χₑ
- The rotating solution yields B(τ) ∝ τ⁻ᵃ, where the power a is expressed in terms of ω₀

## Context

This work extends the self-similar solution method of Csörgő et al. (2002) from relativistic ideal hydrodynamics to non-ideal transverse MHD, and generalizes the ideal MHD boost-invariant solutions of Rischke et al. (2015, 2016) to include finite conductivity and susceptibilities. The rotating solutions provide a framework for studying the evolution of electromagnetic fields and temperature in the quark-gluon plasma created in non-central heavy ion collisions, where strong magnetic fields are generated and decay over time.
