# On the width of the $K^-$D atom ground state

> Experiments at DA$Φ$NE-Frascati and at J-PARC are scheduled to produce $K^-$D atoms and observe their X-ray cascade down to the 1$S$ ground state (g.s.), thereby measuring its strong-interaction width and shift away from a purely Coulomb state. A width $Γ_{1S}\lesssim 1$ keV will ensure good resolution of the X-ray transitions feeding the 1$S$ g.s. Here we study the expected $K^-$D 1$S$ g.s. width from the perspective of global fits to level shifts and widths in heavier kaonic atoms across the periodic table, using $K^-$ nuclear optical potentials constructed from $\bar K N$ chiral interaction models. Special attention is paid to the subthreshold energy at which the $\bar K N$ subsystem interacts in the $K^-$D atomic g.s. Within this approach we predict strong-interaction upward level shift of close to 700 eV and width of about 1.2 to 1.3 keV for the $K^-$D atom 1$S$ g.s., in fair agreement with genuinely three-body $K^-$D atom calculations. Comparison is made with $π^-$D atom phenomenology.

# On the width of the K⁻D atom ground state

## Key claim

The authors predict a strong-interaction width of about 1.2 to 1.3 keV for the K⁻D atom 1S ground state, which is only modestly larger than the approximately 1 keV width from three-body calculations and contradicts a recent claim of about 2.3 keV; this prediction depends on using a subthreshold energy shift of about 7 MeV derived from few-body considerations rather than the larger shifts used in heavier kaonic atoms.

## Method

The authors solve the Klein-Gordon equation for the K⁻D atom using an optical potential consisting of a single-nucleon term constructed from chiral-model K⁻N subthreshold scattering amplitudes (Kyoto-Munich and Barcelona models) and a phenomenological multinucleon term fitted to global kaonic atom data across the periodic table from ⁷Li to ²³⁸U. The subthreshold energy shift for the K⁻N amplitudes in the deuteron is determined from an expectation value formula appropriate for few-body systems, yielding approximately 7 MeV downward shift. The same methodology is tested on the π⁻D atom, where the calculated 1S level shift agrees with experiment to within 4%.

## Key numbers

- Predicted K⁻D 1S ground state width Γ₁S ≈ 1.2 to 1.3 keV (for subthreshold shift δ√s = -7 MeV)
- Predicted K⁻D 1S upward level shift ε₁S ≈ 700 eV (close to 700 eV)
- Subthreshold energy shift for K⁻D: -⟨δ√s⟩ ≈ 6.0 to 7.7 MeV
- K⁻d scattering length at δ√s = -7 MeV: a_{K⁻d} = -1.260 + i 1.414 fm
- K⁻d scattering length at threshold (δ√s = 0): a_{K⁻d} = -0.978 + i 1.193 fm
- Multinucleon absorption partial width contributes about 80 eV in the KM model
- π⁻D 1S calculated level shift: ε₁S = -2.56 eV (experimental: -2.46 ± 0.05 eV)
- π⁻D 1S calculated width: Γ₁S = 0.62 eV (experimental: 0.86^{+0.03}_{-0.05} eV)

## Context

This work extends the global optical model approach to kaonic atoms developed by Friedman and Gal (2012, 2013, 2017) down to the deuteron, testing the methodology against π⁻D atom phenomenology. The results support the genuinely three-body K⁻D atom calculations of Shevchenko and Revai (2014), Revai (2016), and Hoshino et al. (2017), which all find Γ₁S ≈ 1 keV, and directly contradict the much larger width of about 2.3 keV claimed by Liu et al. (2020) using a fixed-center multiple-scattering expansion. The work enables interpretation of forthcoming K⁻D atom X-ray cascade measurements at DAΦNE-Frascati (SIDDHARTA-2) and J-PARC (E57), where a width below 1 keV would ensure good resolution of the 1S ground state transitions.
