# Large-N_c Relations Among Isgur-Wise Functions

> We investigate the relations that must hold among baryonic Isgur-Wise functions $η_i$ in the large-N_c limit from unitarity constraints, and compare to those found by Chow using the Skyrme model [or SU(4)]. Given the exponential dropoff of the $η_i$ away from threshold, unitarity requires only that the usual normalization conditions hold at w=1, and that $η=η_1$ near threshold. Our results are consistent with, but less powerful than, the Skyrme model relations.

## Key claim

The authors show that unitarity constraints in the large-\(N_c\) limit require only the usual normalization conditions at \(w=1\) and that \(\eta = \eta_1\) near threshold, but cannot reproduce the full set of relations among baryonic Isgur-Wise functions that Chow derived from the Skyrme model or \(SU(4)\) symmetry; the unitarity constraints are consistent with but less powerful than the Skyrme model predictions.

## Method

The authors analyze three weak-decay processes in the chiral, heavy-quark, and large-\(N_c\) limits: one-loop renormalization of \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c W\), single-pion emission \(\Lambda_b \to \Sigma_c \pi W\), and double-pion emission \(\Lambda_b \to \Lambda_c \pi\pi W\). They impose unitarity constraints diagrammatically, requiring that loop and emission amplitudes vanish at appropriate orders in \(1/N_c\), and expand the Isgur-Wise functions in a Taylor series in \(\epsilon^2 = w-1\) to extract relations among the expansion coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The normalization conditions at threshold are \(\eta(1) = \eta_1(1) = 1\), with no prediction for \(\eta_2(1)\) from heavy quark symmetry.
- For \(\epsilon = w-1\) of order \(N_c^{-3/4}\), the constraint \(\eta_{01} - \eta_{11} = \mathcal{O}(N_c^{-1/4})\) is obtained from the one-loop renormalization equation.
- For \(\epsilon\) of order \(N_c^{-1/2}\), the constraint becomes \(\eta_{01} - \eta_{11} = \mathcal{O}(N_c^{-1/2})\).
- The exponential dropoff \(\eta \sim \exp\{-\lambda N_c^{3/2} (w-1)\}\) from Jenkins, Manohar and Wise (1993) implies the Isgur-Wise functions vanish for \(\epsilon > N_c^{-3/4}\).
- Chow's Skyrme model predicts \(\eta_2(1) = -1/2\), which unitarity constraints cannot reproduce.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of baryonic Isgur-Wise functions in the large-\(N_c\) limit, comparing unitarity constraints to the Skyrme model relations derived by Chow (1995). The authors find that perturbative unitarity alone cannot reproduce Chow's full set of relations, providing an explicit counterexample to the conjecture that all large-\(N_c\) predictions follow from unitarity constraints. The results are consistent with the exponential form for \(\eta\) given by Jenkins, Manohar and Wise (1993), and the analysis uses Cho's (1994) Feynman rules for heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.
