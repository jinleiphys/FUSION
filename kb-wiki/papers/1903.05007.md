# The $ss \bar s \bar s$ tetraquark states and the newly observed structure $X(2239)$ by BESIII Collaboration

> We investigate the mass spectrum of the $ss \bar s \bar s$ tetraquark states within the relativized quark model. By solving the Schrödinger-like equation with the relativized potential, the masses of the $S-$ and $P-$wave $ss \bar s \bar s$ tetraquarks are obtained. The screening effects are also taken into account. It is found that the observed resonant structure $X(2239)$ in the $e^+e^- \to K^+K^-$ process by BESIII Collaboration can be assigned as a $P-$wave $1^{--}$ $ss \bar s \bar s$ tetraquark state. Furthermore, the radiative transition and strong decay behaviors of this structure are also estimated, which can provide helpful information for future experimental searches.

## Key claim

The newly observed resonant structure X(2239) in the e+e- → K+K- process by BESIII can be assigned as a P-wave 1-- ss sbar sbar tetraquark state, but only if the lowest predicted mass of 2227 MeV from the relativized quark model with screening effects is taken as the assignment; the authors note that the experimental mass of the phi(2170) is about 50 MeV lower than their calculation and cannot be excluded as a ss sbar sbar tetraquark.

## Method

The authors employed the relativized quark model of Godfrey, Capstick, and Isgur, solving a Schrodinger-like equation with a relativized potential that includes spin-independent linear confinement, Coulomb-like interaction, color contact, tensor, and spin-orbit terms, with screening effects added via a parameter mu = 0.02 GeV. They worked in the diquark-antidiquark picture, first calculating masses and wave functions of axial-vector (1+) and vector (1-) ss diquarks, then obtaining tetraquark masses and wave functions by solving the equation between diquark and antidiquark using the Gaussian expansion method. Radiative transition widths were computed using a nonrelativistic electromagnetic transition operator, and strong decay ratios were estimated via the falling apart mechanism for S-wave and P-wave channels.

## Key numbers

- Mass of X(2239) from BESIII: 2239.2 ± 7.1 ± 11.3 MeV, width 139.8 ± 12.3 ± 20.6 MeV
- Predicted mass of the lowest 1-- ss sbar sbar tetraquark: 2227 MeV (with screening parameter mu = 0.02 GeV)
- Predicted mass of the lowest 0++ ss sbar sbar tetraquark: 1716 MeV (candidate f0(1710))
- Predicted mass of the 2++ ss sbar sbar tetraquark: 2255 MeV (candidate f2(2300))
- Radiative decay width for the lowest 1-- state to the 0++ ground state: 26.6 keV
- Radiative decay width for the 1+- state to the 0++ state: 157 keV
- Radiative decay width for the 2++ state to the 1+- state: 175 keV
- Axial-vector ss diquark mass (with screening mu = 0.02 GeV): 1121 MeV
- Vector ss diquark mass (with screening mu = 0.02 GeV): 1396 MeV

## Context

This work extends the relativized quark model, previously applied to conventional hadrons by Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and to other tetraquark systems by Lu (2016) and Anwar (2018), to the ss sbar sbar tetraquark system. It contradicts QCD sum rule predictions for the 1-- states from Wang (2006), Chen (2008), Chen (2018), and Wang (2019), which give inconsistent masses, and improves upon simple quark model calculations by Drenska (2008) that did not consider internal diquark excitation. The results enable future experimental searches for the predicted higher 1-- states at 2468 and 2574 MeV, and suggest that the X(2500) observed by BESIII in J/psi → gamma phi phi could be a 0-+ ss sbar sbar tetraquark rather than a conventional phi(5^1S0) meson.
