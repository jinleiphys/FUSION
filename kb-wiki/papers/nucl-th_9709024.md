# Kaon Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions and Kaon Condensation in Neutron Stars

> The recent past witnesses the growing interdependence between the physics of hadrons, the physics of relativistic heavy-ion collisions, and the physics of compact objects in astrophysics. A notable example is the kaon which plays special roles in all the three fields. In this talk, we first review the various theoretical investigations of kaon properties in nuclear medium. We then present a detailed transport model study of kaon production and flow in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies. Finally, We discuss the effects of the kaon in-medium properties extracted from heavy-ion data on neutron star properties, especially on the lowering of maximum mass of neutron stars with the onset of kaon condensation around $3ρ_0$.

# Kaon Production in Heavy-Ion Collisions and Kaon Condensation in Neutron Stars

## Key claim
The authors find that experimental data from heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies are consistent with a weak repulsive potential for kaons and a strong attractive potential for antikaons in nuclear medium, and that using these in-medium properties leads to a maximum neutron star mass of about 1.5 solar masses when kaon condensation is included, compared to about 2.0 solar masses without kaons. The hedge is that the scalar attraction parameters \(a_K\) and \(a_{\bar K}\) are treated as free parameters determined phenomenologically from heavy-ion data, and the results depend on the specific choice of nuclear equation of state from Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic transport model including strangeness degrees of freedom to simulate kaon and antikaon production in Ni+Ni collisions at SIS energies (1-2 AGeV). They parameterize the kaon and antikaon energies in nuclear medium using a phenomenological approach with two free parameters \(a_K\) and \(a_{\bar K}\) that determine scalar attractions, and fit these parameters to experimental data from the KaoS collaboration. They then apply the in-medium kaon properties to compute neutron star properties by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equation with an equation of state that includes kaon condensation, using the nucleon energy density from Furnstahl, Tang, and Serot.

## Key numbers
- At normal nuclear matter density \(\rho_0 = 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\), the \(K^+\) feels a repulsive potential of about 20 MeV, using \(a_K = 0.22\) GeV\(^2\)fm\(^3\).
- The antikaon scalar attraction parameter is \(a_{\bar K} = 0.45\) GeV\(^2\)fm\(^3\), which provides a good fit to \(K^-\) data in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies.
- Without kaon medium effects, the calculated \(K^-\) yield in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.8 AGeV is about a factor 3-4 below experimental data; including medium effects increases the yield by about a factor of 3, bringing agreement with data.
- The \(K^+/K^-\) ratio is almost a constant of about 1 in the entire kinetic energy region when medium effects are included, in agreement with KaoS data.
- Without kaons, the maximum neutron star mass in this model is about \(2.0 M_\odot\); with kaon condensation, the maximum mass is about \(1.5 M_\odot\).
- Kaon condensation is predicted to occur around a critical density of \(3\rho_0\) (stated in the abstract).

## Context
This work extends the line of research on kaon condensation in dense matter, originally proposed by Kaplan and Nelson (1986), and connects it to experimental observables in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies. The authors compare their transport model results to experimental data from the KaoS collaboration and to prior calculations by Cassing et al. (1997) and others. The study enables a direct link between heavy-ion collision measurements and neutron star structure, showing that the in-medium kaon properties extracted from laboratory data can constrain the maximum mass of neutron stars, which is relevant for understanding the equation of state of dense matter and the possibility of kaon condensation in astrophysical objects.
