# Two Photon Distribution Amplitudes

> The factorization of the amplitude of the process gamma* gamma -> gamma gamma in the low energy and high photon virtuality region is demonstrated at the Born order and in the leading logarithmic approximation. The leading order two photon (generalized) distribution amplitudes exhibit a characteristic ln Q2 behaviour and obey new inhomogeneous evolution equations.

## Key claim

The factorization of the amplitude for the process gamma* gamma -> gamma gamma is demonstrated at Born order and in the leading logarithmic approximation, but only in the near-threshold kinematics where the squared invariant mass of the two outgoing photons is zero (W^2 = 0) and at high photon virtuality Q^2.

## Method

The authors calculate the six "box" Feynman diagrams with quarks in the loop at leading order in alpha_em and zeroth order in alpha_S, restricting to the threshold kinematics W^2 = 0. They perform the loop integration using the Sudakov representation, integrate over the beta variable using Cauchy's theorem, and identify the partonic fraction z from the pole structure. The ultraviolet divergences are regulated through dimensional regularization and renormalized using a mixing matrix for quark and photon operators, with the renormalization condition that the renormalized quark correlator matrix element vanishes at the renormalization scale M_R = m.

## Key numbers

- The scalar function W_2 is identically zero for the threshold kinematics (W^2 = 0).
- The quark contribution to the diphoton generalized distribution amplitude Phi_1^q contains a factor (N_C * e_q^2 / (2*pi^2)) * log(Q^2 / m^2) multiplying a function F(z, zeta) that depends on the partonic fraction z and the skewedness parameter zeta.
- The polarized diphoton GDA Phi_3^q contains the same prefactor (N_C * e_q^2 / (2*pi^2)) * log(Q^2 / m^2) with a different z- and zeta-dependent function.
- The renormalized quark correlator is set to zero at the renormalization scale M_R = m, fixing the renormalization constant Z_{q gamma}.

## Context

This work extends the concept of generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs), previously developed for two-meson systems by Mueller et al. (1994) and Diehl et al. (1998), to the case of two-photon states. It is related to the study of anomalous parton distributions in the photon initiated by Witten (1977) and to generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in the photon developed by Friot, Pire, and Szymanowski (2007) for deeply virtual Compton scattering on a photon target. The results enable future studies of exclusive hard reactions involving two-photon final states and provide a perturbative laboratory for understanding QCD factorization in photon-photon collisions.
