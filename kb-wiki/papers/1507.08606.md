# Heavy ${\bar Q}Q$ free energy from hadronic states

> Within the spirit of the Hadron Resonance Gas model, we study a representation of the heavy $\bar{Q}Q$ free energy at temperatures below the phase transition in terms of the string and heavy-light hadrons. We discuss the string breaking phenomenon and the relevance of avoided crossings between the fundamental string and the hadron spectrum. Good agreement with lattice data is achieved.

## Key claim

The heavy \(\bar{Q}Q\) free energy in the confined phase can be represented by a hadronic spectral decomposition involving a string state and heavy-light hadrons; the agreement with lattice data depends on whether avoided crossings between the string and the hadron spectrum are negligible or appreciable, and this may explain discrepancies between different lattice groups.

## Method

The authors model the color-averaged heavy \(\bar{Q}Q\) free energy as a ratio of partition functions, approximated by a sum of Boltzmann factors from the fundamental string state and from heavy-light meson and baryon states (the hadronic representation). They compare this representation to lattice data from two groups (Kaczmarek et al. 2005 and Borsanyi et al. 2015), using the heavy-light spectrum from the Isgur model (Godfrey 1985). For the Borsanyi data, they also fit a two-state mixing model with an exponential transition potential to extract the string tension and the mixing strength.

## Key numbers

* String tension \(\sigma = (0.40\ \mathrm{GeV})^2\) reproduces the lattice data of Kaczmarek et al. (2005) for \(N_f=2\) with \(m_\pi \simeq 770\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* String tension \(\sigma = (0.55\ \mathrm{GeV})^2\) is needed to reproduce the lattice data of Borsanyi et al. (2015) for \(N_f=2+1\) with physical quark masses when using the hadronic representation without mixing.
* A fit of the two-state mixing model to the Borsanyi data at \(T = 150\ \mathrm{MeV}\) yields \(W_0 = 1.03(50)\ \mathrm{GeV}\), \(m = 0.85(38)\ \mathrm{GeV}\), and \(\sigma = (0.420(10)\ \mathrm{GeV})^2\) with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 0.036\).
* The critical distance for string breaking is estimated as \(r_c \simeq 1.2\ \mathrm{fm}\).
* The hadronic representation of the Polyakov loop, \(L(T) = \frac{1}{2}\sum_n e^{-\Delta_n / T}\), agrees with lattice data for \(T \lesssim 180\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The heavy-light meson spectrum from the Isgur model is used up to \(\Delta = 3.19\ \mathrm{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model approach to the heavy \(\bar{Q}Q\) free energy, following the authors' earlier hadronic representation of the Polyakov loop (Megias 2012, Megias 2013). It builds on the string-breaking picture and avoided crossings familiar from molecular physics (Landau 1965) and lattice studies of static energies (Bali 2005). The analysis reveals a tension between lattice data from Kaczmarek et al. (2005) and Borsanyi et al. (2015), suggesting that more accurate lattice data and improved modeling of avoided crossings (including more states or string excitations) are needed to resolve the discrepancy.
