# Bare vs effective pairing forces. A microscopic finite-range interaction for HFB calculations in coordinate space

> We propose a microscopic effective interaction to treat pairing correlations in the $^{1}S_0$ channel. It is introduced by recasting the gap equation written in terms of the bare force into a fully equivalent pairing problem. Within this approach, the proposed interaction reproduces the pairing properties provided by the realistic $AV18$ force very accurately. Written in the canonical basis of the actual Bogolyubov transformation, the force takes the form of an off-shell in-medium two-body matrix in the superfluid phase multiplied by a BCS occupation number $2 ρ_{m}$. This interaction is finite ranged, non local, total-momentum dependent and density dependent. The factor $2 ρ_{m}$ emerging from the recast of the gap equation provides a natural cut-off and makes zero-range approximations of the effective vertex meaningful. Performing such an approximation, the roles of the range and of the density dependence of the interaction can be disentangled. The isoscalar and isovector density-dependences derived ab-initio provide the pairing force with a strong predictive power when extrapolated toward the drip-lines. Although finite ranged and non local, the proposed interaction makes HFB calculations of finite nuclei in coordinate space tractable. Through the two-basis method, its computational cost is of the same order as for a zero-range force.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a microscopic effective pairing interaction for the \(^{1}S_0\) channel that is exactly equivalent to the bare \(AV18\) force when used in the gap equation, but the equivalence holds only at the mean-field level and relies on the assumption that beyond-mean-field effects (screening and surface vibrations) largely cancel in finite nuclei.

## Method

The author recasts the BCS gap equation written with the bare \(AV18\) force into a formally equivalent pairing problem using a rank-one separable interaction \(V^{^{1}S_{0}}_{sep}\) with a Gaussian form factor \(v(k) = e^{-\alpha^{2} k^{2}}\). The parameters of this separable force are fitted simultaneously to the neutron-neutron \(^{1}S_0\) scattering phase shifts from \(AV18\) and to the \(^{1}S_0\) pairing gap in infinite nuclear matter computed with the same bare force, using free single-particle energies \(\epsilon_k = k^2/2m\). The resulting effective interaction is then expressed in the canonical basis of the Bogolyubov transformation as an off-shell in-medium two-body matrix multiplied by a BCS occupation number \(2\rho_m\), making it finite-ranged, non-local, total-momentum dependent, and density dependent.

## Key numbers

* The best-fit parameters for the separable force are \(\lambda = -840\ \text{MeV.fm}^{3}\) and \(\alpha = \sqrt{0.52}\ \text{fm}\).
* The maximum \(^{1}S_0\) pairing gap in infinite matter is set to \(3\ \text{MeV}\), a solid prediction from all modern realistic forces.
* The empirical neutron-neutron scattering length in the \(^{1}S_0\) channel is \(a^{^{1}S_0}_{nn} = -18.5 \pm 0.4\ \text{fm}\) (or \(-18.7 \pm 0.6\ \text{fm}\) in a recent experiment).
* The saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter is \(\rho_{sat} = 0.16\ \text{fm}^{-3}\).
* The phase shifts change sign around \(E_{lab} = 250\ \text{MeV}\), corresponding to \(k = 1.73\ \text{fm}^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of using the bare nucleon-nucleon force in the pairing channel at the mean-field level, as established by Gorkov (1958) and others, and contrasts with the use of phenomenological forces such as the Gogny force (Decharge and Gogny, 1980) or the density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) fitted to finite nuclei data by Bertsch and others (1991). The proposed interaction enables tractable Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov (HFB) calculations in coordinate space for finite nuclei, with computational cost comparable to zero-range forces via the two-basis method, and provides a foundation for systematic inclusion of beyond-mean-field effects such as symmetry restoration and the generator coordinate method.
