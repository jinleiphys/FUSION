# The chiral condensate in neutron matter

> We calculate the chiral condensate in neutron matter at zero temperature based on nuclear forces derived within chiral effective field theory. Two-, three- and four-nucleon interactions are included consistently to next-to-next-to-next-to-leading order (N3LO) of the chiral expansion. We find that the interaction contributions lead to a modest increase of the condensate, thus impeding the restoration of chiral symmetry in dense matter and making a chiral phase transition in neutron-rich matter unlikely for densities that are not significantly higher than nuclear saturation density.

## Key claim

The interaction contributions from chiral EFT forces increase the chiral condensate in neutron matter, thereby impeding chiral symmetry restoration and making a chiral phase transition unlikely for densities below about 0.3 fm^-3, though a strong first-order transition cannot be excluded.

## Method

The chiral condensate in neutron matter at zero temperature was calculated using the Hellman-Feynman theorem applied to the energy per particle obtained from chiral EFT interactions at N3LO, including two-, three-, and four-nucleon forces. The explicit pion-mass dependence of nuclear forces was computed by varying the pion mass by 0.5% in the N3LO NN potentials of Epelbaum, Glockle, and Meissner (EGM) with cutoffs 450/500 MeV and 450/700 MeV, and the derivative of the interaction energy with respect to m_pi^2 was evaluated numerically at different densities.

## Key numbers

* At nuclear saturation density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3, the chiral condensate ratio <qbar q>_n / <qbar q>_0 lies in the range 67.3-69.8% for the EGM 450/500 MeV potential and 67.8-69.5% for the EGM 450/700 MeV potential.
* The total interaction contribution yields a 6 +/- 2% enhancement of the chiral condensate at saturation density.
* The leading sigma_pi N term alone would predict chiral symmetry restoration at densities around (2.5-3) n0 based on linear extrapolation.
* Using Delta sigma_pi N = 8 MeV, the uncertainty from the sigma term is on the order of 10% at n = 0.2 fm^-3.
* The kinetic energy contribution to the free energy gives only a 4% correction relative to the leading sigma-term contribution in the density range studied.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT calculations of the neutron matter equation of state by Hebeler et al. (2013) and Tews et al. (2013) to compute the chiral condensate as an order parameter for chiral symmetry restoration. The results agree with those of Kaiser and Weise (2011), who found approximately 5% interaction contributions at saturation density using chiral perturbation theory with explicit Delta resonances, but differ from the NLO calculation by Lacour et al. (2011) which found only small interaction corrections that reduce the condensate. The findings enable future studies of symmetric nuclear matter and provide constraints on the possibility of exotic phases in neutron star interiors.
