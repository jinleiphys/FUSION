# Searching for Axion Dark Matter in Atoms: Oscillating Electric Dipole Moments and Spin-Precession Effects

> We propose to search for axion dark matter via the oscillating electric dipole moments that axions induce in atoms and molecules. These moments are produced through the intrinsic oscillating electric dipole moments of nucleons and through the $P,T$-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction mediated by pion exchange, both of which arise due to the axion-gluon coupling, and also directly through the axion-electron interaction. Axion dark matter may also be sought for through the spin-precession effects that axions produce by directly coupling to fermion spins.

# Searching for Axion Dark Matter in Atoms: Oscillating Electric Dipole Moments and Spin-Precession Effects

## Key claim
Axion dark matter can be detected through oscillating electric dipole moments (EDMs) and spin-precession effects in atoms and molecules, with these observables scaling linearly with the axion-fermion coupling strength (first power in the interaction strength), in contrast to haloscope and helioscope methods which rely on second-power effects.

## Method
The authors calculate the oscillating EDMs induced in atoms and molecules by an axion dark matter field through three mechanisms: the intrinsic oscillating EDM of nucleons arising from the axion-gluon coupling; the P,T-violating nucleon-nucleon interaction mediated by pion exchange with the axion field supplying the oscillating source of P and T violation at one pion-nucleon vertex; and the direct axion-electron interaction via the derivative coupling to the fermion axial-vector current. They also derive the spin-precession potential produced by the coupling of the oscillating axion field to fermion spins through the spatial components of the axial-vector current.

## Key numbers
- Neutron oscillating EDM: \( d_n(t) \approx 1.2 \times 10^{-16} \frac{a_0}{f_a} \cos(m_a t) \, e \cdot \text{cm} \)
- Oscillating EDM of \( ^{199}\text{Hg} \): \( d(^{199}\text{Hg}) \approx -1.8 \times 10^{-19} \frac{a_0}{f_a} \cos(m_a t) \, e \cdot \text{cm} \)
- Oscillating EDM of \( ^{225}\text{Ra} \): \( d(^{225}\text{Ra}) \approx 9.3 \times 10^{-17} \frac{a_0}{f_a} \cos(m_a t) \, e \cdot \text{cm} \)
- Axion-induced P,T-violating pion-nucleon coupling constant: \( \bar{g}_{\pi N N} \approx 0.027 \cos(m_a t) \frac{a_0}{f_a} \)
- Virial velocity of axion dark matter: \( v_{\text{virial}} \sim 10^{-3} \)
- Coherence time: \( \tau_{\text{coh}} \sim 2\pi / m_a v_{\text{virial}}^2 \)
- Coherence length: \( l_{\text{coh}} \sim 2\pi / m_a v_{\text{virial}} \)

## Context
This work extends the proposal of Graham and Rajendran (2011) and Stadnik and Flambaum (2014) for detecting axion-induced oscillating EDMs, and builds on the static EDM measurement framework reviewed by Ginges and Flambaum (2004) and Pospelov and Ritz (2005). It enables experimental searches using existing atomic and molecular EDM setups, including ferroelectric solid-state media as proposed by the CASPER collaboration (2014), and spin-precession measurements with atomic co-magnetometers, ultracold neutrons, and torsion pendula. The nucleon spin content calculations for experimental nuclei were performed by Stadnik and Flambaum (2015) for accurate interpretation of laboratory measurements.
