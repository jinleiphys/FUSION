# Remarks on the study of the X(3872) from Effective Field Theory with Pion-Exchange Interaction

> In a recent paper Phys.Rev.Lett. 111, 042002 (2013) (arXiv:1304.0846), the charmonium state X(3872) is studied in the framework of an effective field theory. In that work it is claimed that (i) the one-pion exchange (OPE) alone provides sufficient binding to produce the X as a shallow bound state at the $D^0\bar{D}^{*0}$ threshold, (ii) short-range dynamics (described by a contact interaction) provides only moderate corrections to the OPE, and (iii) the X-pole disappears as the pion mass is increased slightly and therefore the X should not be seen on the lattice, away from the pion physical mass point, if it were a molecular state. In this paper we demonstrate that the results of Phys.Rev.Lett. 111, 042002 (2013) (arXiv:1304.0846) suffer from technical as well as conceptual problems and therefore do not support the conclusions drawn by the authors.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the results of Wang et al. (2013) claiming that one-pion exchange (OPE) alone provides sufficient binding to produce the X(3872) as a shallow bound state at the D0D*0 threshold suffer from technical and conceptual problems and therefore do not support the conclusions drawn. The authors argue that the OPE potential in the D D* system is well defined as an effective field theory only in connection with a contact operator, contrary to the claim that short-range dynamics provides only moderate corrections.

## Method

The authors re-analyze the one-loop D D* scattering amplitude using sharp cut-off regularization instead of dimensional regularization, making the power-like divergences explicit. They evaluate the tree-level and one-loop amplitudes for the X(3872) system, including the contact interaction and OPE contributions, and examine the [0,1] Pade approximation used by Wang et al. (2013) to construct the physical amplitude. They also study the pole structure of the S-matrix for larger pion masses where mu_pi^2 > 0, corresponding to a stable D* meson, to assess the reliability of the formalism.

## Key numbers

- The X(3872) mass is M_X = (3871.68 +/- 0.17) MeV, residing within less than 1 MeV from the neutral D D* threshold.
- The pion decay constant used is f_pi = 92.2 MeV.
- The dimensionless coupling constant g = 0.3 is used for the D* D pi vertex.
- For physical pion mass, mu_pi = -i sqrt(Delta^2 - m_pi^2) = -i 44.36 MeV, where Delta = M_D* - M_D.
- The pole reported by Wang et al. (2013) for lambda = 0 resides at p_0 = -15.46 + i 24.62 MeV.
- For mu_pi = 44.36 MeV, the authors find multiple near-threshold solutions including p_1 = -11.80 + i 22.47 MeV, which looks similar to the reported pole.
- The one-loop amplitude with sharp cut-off regularization yields a linearly divergent term proportional to Lambda, with the divergent piece for the box diagram being 4 g_pi^4 Lambda / (3 pi^2 f_pi^4 (M_D + M_D*)).

## Context

This work extends the critical discussion of the X(3872) as a molecular state, directly contradicting the conclusions of Wang et al. (2013) in Physical Review Letters. The authors align with earlier effective field theory treatments by Braaten and collaborators (X-EFT with perturbative pions) and Baru et al. (2011) (nonperturbative pions with Faddeev-type integral equations), which include three-body D D pi unitarity cuts. The paper also references lattice QCD calculations by Prelovsek and Leskovec (2013) and the chiral extrapolation studies for the nucleon-nucleon system by Epelbaum, Meissner, and collaborators. The findings enable future lattice QCD calculations to determine the pion mass dependence of the X(3872) pole position, which could reveal information on the structure of the binding potential.
