# Deformed Boson Scheme Stressing Even-Odd Boson Number Difference. III

> A basic idea is proposed for extending the formalism of Part (II) of the series of our paper to the case of the parameter-dependent deformation. It is stressed that, through this extension, the variety of the application increases. Further, an answer for the problem mentioned in Part (II) is presented.

## Key claim

The paper extends the deformed boson scheme from Part (II) to allow the deformation function \( \tilde{f}(k) \) to depend on a real parameter (such as \(|\gamma|^4\)), which removes convergence problems and negative norm coefficients that appeared in the parameter-independent formulation, thereby increasing the variety of possible applications.

## Method

The authors reformulate the boson coherent state formalism of Part (II) by allowing the function \( \tilde{f}(k,\delta) \) to depend on a real parameter \(\delta\) (e.g., \(|\gamma|^4\)), while keeping all relations except (II.2·9a) and (II.2·17) unchanged. They then construct two illustrative classes of parameter-dependent states (one with factorial-like coefficients and one with exponential normalization) and analyze several concrete examples with different choices of the parameter-dependent functions \(g_{ch}\) and \(g_{sh}\), deriving expressions for normalization constants, expectation values, and the quantity \((\tau_-)_{cr}\).

## Key numbers

* The normalization constants for the first illustrative case are \(\Gamma_{ch} = (1 - |\gamma|^4 g_{ch}^2)^{-1/2}\) and \(\Gamma_{sh} = |\gamma|^2 (1 - |\gamma|^4 g_{sh}^2)^{-3/2}\), valid when \(|\gamma|^4 g_{ch}^2 < 1\) and \(|\gamma|^4 g_{sh}^2 < 1\).
* For the second illustrative case, \(\Gamma_{ch} = \exp(|\gamma|^4 g_{ch}^2/2)\) and \(\Gamma_{sh} = |\gamma|^2 \exp(3|\gamma|^4 g_{sh}^2/2)\).
* In the simplest example with \(g_{ch} = \sqrt{\lambda/(1+\lambda|\gamma|^4)}\) and \(g_{sh} = \sqrt{\mu/(1+\mu|\gamma|^4)}\), the relation \(2|c|^2 = |\gamma|^4 (u^2 \lambda + 3|v|^2 \mu)\) holds.
* For the case \(\alpha = \beta\) in the more complicated examples, \(|\gamma|^4 = 2|c|^2 [2\alpha(u^2\lambda + 3|v|^2\mu) + 2|c|^2] / (u^2\lambda + 3|v|^2\mu)^2\).
* In the large \(|\gamma|^2\) limit, \((\tau_-)_{cr} = c\sqrt{|c|^2 + |v|^2 + 1/2}\), independent of the parameters \(\alpha, \beta, \lambda, \mu\).

## Context

This paper is Part (III) of a series by the same authors, extending the formalism of Part (II) (Kuriyama et al., 2003) which used a parameter-independent deformation function \(\tilde{f}(k)\). The work addresses a problem identified in Part (II) where certain normalization constants led to divergent series expansions and negative norm coefficients; the parameter-dependent deformation resolves these issues. The results enable the construction of a wider class of deformed boson coherent states with applications to nuclear physics models, and the authors show that specific parameter choices reproduce the approximate forms for \(\tanh |\gamma|^2\) discussed in Part (II).
