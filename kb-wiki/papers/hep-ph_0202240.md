# HBT interferometry and the parton-hadron phase transition

> We discuss predictions for the pion and kaon interferometry measurements in relativistic heavy ion collisions at SPS and RHIC energies. In particular, we confront relativistic transport model calculations that include explicitly a first-order phase transition from a thermalized quark-gluon plasma to a hadron gas with recent data from the RHIC experiments. We critically examine the "HBT-puzzle" both from the theoretical as well as from the experimental point of view. Alternative scenarios are briefly explained.

# HBT interferometry and the parton-hadron phase transition

## Key claim
The paper argues that even for a first-order phase transition from quark-gluon plasma to hadron gas, the pion interferometry radii are not unusually large (less than 7 fm), and the dissipative hadronic phase dominates the space-time signal, making the specific dependencies on QGP properties rather weak. The "HBT-puzzle", the discrepancy between model predictions and RHIC data for the $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}$ ratio, is confirmed, with models consistently producing ratios larger than unity while data show a decreasing ratio with transverse momentum that can fall below 1.

## Method
The authors use a relativistic hybrid transport model that couples ideal hydrodynamics for the initial dense stage (with a bag model equation of state exhibiting a first-order phase transition) to a microscopic hadronic transport model for the later dilute stages, matched at the hadronization hypersurface. Two-particle correlation functions are computed explicitly and fitted to a Gaussian form $C_2 = 1 + \lambda \exp(-R_{\rm o}^2 q_{\rm o}^2 - R_{\rm s}^2 q_{\rm s}^2 - R_{\rm l}^2 q_{\rm l}^2)$ to extract the HBT radii $R_{\rm out}$, $R_{\rm side}$, $R_{\rm long}$, and the intercept parameter $\lambda$.

## Key numbers
- Pion transverse radii $R_{\rm o}$ and $R_{\rm s}$ from the model are less than 7 fm for Au+Au collisions at RHIC.
- For kaons at RHIC, the fraction of kaons escaping directly from the phase boundary reaches up to approximately 30% at high transverse momentum ($K_T \sim 1$ GeV/c).
- STAR pion data (average of $\pi^-$ and $\pi^+$) at low $K_T$: $R_{\rm out} = 5.9 \pm 0.5$ fm, $R_{\rm side} = 5.7 \pm 0.5$ fm, $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} = 1.04$; model calculations without finite momentum resolution give $R_{\rm out} = 8.2$ fm, $R_{\rm side} = 5.7$ fm, $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} = 1.4$.
- At high $K_T$: STAR data $R_{\rm out} = 4.5 \pm 0.6$ fm, $R_{\rm side} = 5.1 \pm 0.6$ fm, $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} = 0.88$; model gives $R_{\rm out} = 7.7$ fm, $R_{\rm side} = 4.8$ fm, $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side} = 1.6$.
- Critical temperatures considered: $T_c \simeq 160$ MeV and $T_c \simeq 200$ MeV.
- Initial thermalization time of the order of $\tau_i \sim 1$ fm/c.

## Context
This work extends earlier predictions by Pratt (1986) and Schlei et al. that a first-order phase transition should produce enhanced interferometry radii due to larger hadronization times. The paper confronts the hybrid transport model of Bass, Dumitru, and Soff with RHIC data from STAR (STARpreprint) and PHENIX (Johnson, 2001), confirming the "HBT-puzzle" where all dynamical models fail to reproduce the observed $K_T$ dependence of $R_{\rm out}/R_{\rm side}$. The results enable future kaon interferometry measurements at high $K_T$ as a more sensitive probe of the phase boundary, and motivate alternative scenarios such as spinodal decomposition (Zschiesche et al., 2001) or strongly opaque sources to resolve the discrepancy.
