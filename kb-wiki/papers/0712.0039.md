# Dynamical coupled-channels: the key to understanding resonances

> Invited Plenary talk at the 20th European Conference on Few-Body Problems in Physics (EFB20), September 10-14 2007, Pisa, Italy. To appear in the proceedings in Few-Body Systems

## Key claim

The dynamical coupled-channels (MSL) model provides a framework for extracting nucleon resonance parameters from hadronic and electromagnetic production data, but the extracted "dressed" form factors of the resonant amplitude can only be compared with hadron structure calculations that include meson loops, while the "bare" form factors may be compared with quark model or lattice QCD calculations that omit meson clouds.

## Method

The MSL model starts from Lagrangians describing meson-baryon interactions and applies a unitary transformation method to derive an effective Hamiltonian with energy-independent potentials. The full scattering amplitude is separated into a non-resonant part obtained from coupled-channels equations and a resonant part built from bare N* states dressed by the non-resonant interactions. For the Delta(1232) region, the model was simplified to two channels (gamma N and pi N) and the bare N-Delta form factors were extracted by fitting all available pion electroproduction data at each Q^2 value.

## Key numbers

* Bare magnetic form factor G_M(Q^2) for the gamma N to Delta transition was extracted by performing individual chi^2 fits at each Q^2 value to pion electroproduction data from Jefferson Lab
* The full coupled-channels model analyzes experimental electroproduction data at center-of-mass energies below 2 GeV
* The model describes pi N elastic scattering in S, P, D, and F partial waves, with the real part of T_{pi N, pi N} compared to the SAID energy-independent extraction
* Differential cross sections for pi^+ n and pi^0 p photoproduction are shown in the Delta(1232) region and at higher center-of-mass energies at a fixed angle

## Context

This work extends the Sato-Lee model (Sato and Lee, 1996) with the key improvement of extracting N-Delta form factors by fitting all available pion electroproduction data rather than using simple parameterizations. The extracted bare form factors are compared with relativistic quark model calculations by Capstick (2002) and by Bruno (2004), showing reasonable agreement for magnetic and electric form factors but disagreement for the Coulomb form factor G_C. The model enables future consistent studies of meson-baryon scattering, single meson electroproduction, and two-meson photoproduction, as well as connection to lattice QCD simulations of the gamma N to Delta transition.
