# Emission angle dependent pion interferometry at RHIC and beyond

> We use hydrodynamics to generate freeze-out configurations for non-central heavy-ion collisions at present and future collider energies. Such collisions are known to produce strong elliptic flow. The accompanying space-time structure of the source at freeze-out is analyzed using pion interferometry. Between RHIC and LHC energies the source deformation in the transverse plane changes sign. This leaves characteristic signatures in the emission angle dependence of the HBT radii.

## Key claim

The sign of the source deformation in the transverse plane at freeze-out changes from out-of-plane at RHIC energies to in-plane at higher energies (LHC or beyond), and this transition leaves characteristic signatures in the emission angle dependence of the HBT radii. The authors note that their idealized hydrodynamic model does not give a quantitative description of experimental HBT radii for central Au+Au collisions at RHIC, but they expect the qualitative geometric features to be insensitive to details of the decoupling mechanism.

## Method

The authors used ideal hydrodynamics with boost-invariant longitudinal expansion to simulate semicentral Au+Au collisions at impact parameter b=7 fm, with two sets of initial conditions: "RHIC1" (T0=340 MeV, τ0=0.6 fm/c, freeze-out at Tf=130 MeV) and "IPES" (T0=2 GeV, τ0=0.1 fm/c, freeze-out at Tf=100 MeV, using an ideal gas EOS). They computed the pion emission function via the Cooper-Frye prescription and extracted HBT radii from the widths of the K-dependent emission regions, excluding resonance decay pions to preserve the correspondence between homogeneity lengths and measured HBT radii.

## Key numbers

- Impact parameter: b = 7 fm for semicentral Au+Au collisions
- RHIC1 initial conditions: peak initial temperature T0 = 340 MeV at b=0, starting time τ0 = 0.6 fm/c, freeze-out temperature Tf = 130 MeV
- IPES initial conditions: peak initial temperature T0 = 2 GeV at b=0, starting time τ0 = 0.1 fm/c, freeze-out temperature Tf = 100 MeV
- Hadronization temperature in the model: Thad = 164 MeV
- For IPES initial conditions, the sign of the R_os^2 oscillations changes for Kt approximately greater than 100-150 MeV
- The initial entropy density distribution uses 75% weight from wounded nucleons and 25% weight from binary nucleon-nucleon collisions

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic studies of elliptic flow at RHIC (Heinz and Kolb 2002, Kolb et al. 2000) to predict how the spatial deformation of the freeze-out source changes sign between RHIC and LHC energies. The authors note that their predicted oscillation pattern for RHIC energies (out-of-plane elongated source) agrees qualitatively with preliminary RHIC data (R02) and with conclusions from a hydrodynamically motivated model by Lisa and Wells (2002). The work enables experimental searches for the transition to in-plane extended sources at higher collision energies using emission-angle-dependent pion interferometry.
