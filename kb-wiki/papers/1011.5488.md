# Power-law ensembles: fluctuations of volume or temperature ?

> We present two issues here: (i) that in situation when total energy is kept constant recently proposed fluctuations of volume ensemble is equivalent to the approach using Tsallis statistics with fluctuating temperature and (ii) that the later (in which fluctuations are described by the nonextensivity parameter q) leads to the observed experimentally sum rule connecting fluctuations of different physical observables.

# Power-law ensembles: fluctuations of volume or temperature?

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for constant total energy, fluctuations of volume (as proposed by Vfluct) are equivalent to fluctuations of temperature described by Tsallis statistics with nonextensivity parameter q, but the temperature-fluctuation approach has the advantage of being derived from a physical diffusion process rather than being an ad hoc assumption. The main new result is a sum rule connecting fluctuations of different physical observables that explains why q parameters extracted from different distributions in the same experiment can differ.

## Method

The authors compare two approaches to generating power-law distributions: (1) their own previously developed model where temperature fluctuations follow a gamma distribution derived from a Langevin/Fokker-Planck diffusion equation, and (2) the volume-fluctuation ensemble of Vfluct where volume fluctuates following KNO scaling. Using the relation E ~ VT^4 for constant total energy, they show that 1/T and V^(1/4) follow the same gamma distribution, establishing equivalence. They then derive a sum rule by decomposing the variance of the variable z = μ_T/T appearing in rapidity distributions into contributions from transverse mass fluctuations and temperature fluctuations, connecting q values from multiplicity, rapidity, and transverse mass distributions.

## Key numbers

- For p-pbar collisions at 200, 540, and 900 GeV: (T_T; q_T) = (0.134 GeV; 1.095), (0.135 GeV; 1.105), and (0.14 GeV; 1.11) from p_T distributions; (T_L; q_L) = (11.74; 1.2), (20.39; 1.26), and (30.79; 1.29) from rapidity distributions
- The relation q_L - 1 = -0.104 + 0.058 ln sqrt(s) describes the energy dependence of q from rapidity distributions in p-pbar collisions
- For Pb+Pb collisions from NA49, q from multiplicity distributions follows q - 1 = (1/(0.98 N_P))(1 - N_P/A) as function of number of participants N_P
- The sum rule: q - 1 = Var(z)/⟨z⟩^2 - Var(μ_T)/⟨μ_T⟩^2, where z = μ_T/T for rapidity distributions

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous program (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000; Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2009) of deriving Tsallis statistics from temperature fluctuations in a diffusion picture, and compares it with the volume-fluctuation approach of Vfluct. The sum rule resolves an apparent contradiction between q values extracted from different observables in heavy-ion collisions, where q from multiplicity distributions is much smaller than q from rapidity or transverse momentum distributions. The paper uses NA49 data on Pb+Pb collisions and compares with RHIC data from PHENIX and a compilation by qcompilation. The results should be testable with future ALICE data at LHC.
