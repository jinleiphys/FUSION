# Tritium $β$-decay in chiral effective field theory

> We evaluate the Fermi and Gamow-Teller (GT) matrix elements in tritium $β$-decay by including in the charge-changing weak current the corrections up to one loop recently derived in nuclear chiral effective field theory ($χ$ EFT). The trinucleon wave functions are obtained from hyperspherical-harmonics solutions of the Schrodinger equation with two- and three-nucleon potentials corresponding to either $χ$ EFT (the N3LO/N2LO combination) or meson-exchange phenomenology (the AV18/UIX combination). We find that contributions due to loop corrections in the axial current are, in relative terms, as large as (and in some cases, dominate) those from one-pion exchange, which nominally occur at lower order in the power counting. We also provide values for the low-energy constants multiplying the contact axial current and three-nucleon potential, required to reproduce the experimental GT matrix element and trinucleon binding energies in the N3LO/N2LO and AV18/UIX calculations.

## Key claim

The loop corrections to the axial current at N4LO (one order beyond what was previously included) contribute as much as, and in some cases dominate, the one-pion-exchange contributions that occur at nominally lower order in the chiral power counting, contradicting the expectation that higher-order terms should be systematically smaller.

## Method

The authors computed the Fermi and Gamow-Teller matrix elements for tritium beta-decay using trinucleon wave functions obtained from hyperspherical-harmonics solutions of the Schrodinger equation with two- and three-nucleon potentials from either chiral effective field theory (N3LO/N2LO combination) or meson-exchange phenomenology (AV18/UIX combination). They included in the charge-changing weak current the corrections up to one loop recently derived in chiral effective field theory, and evaluated the contributions from each order (LO, N2LO, N3LO, N4LO) separately, then determined the low-energy constant z0 required to reproduce the experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element.

## Key numbers

- Experimental Gamow-Teller matrix element: GT_EXP = 0.9511 ± 0.0013, extracted from the tritium half-life using g_A = 1.2723, outer radiative correction δ_R = 1.9%, and K/G_V^2 = (6144.5 ± 1.9) s
- LO contribution to GT matrix element: 0.9363 (N3LO/N2LO, Λ=500 MeV) and 0.9322 (N3LO/N2LO, Λ=600 MeV); for AV18/UIX: 0.9224
- N4LO(MPE) contribution: -0.430 × 10^{-2} (N3LO/N2LO, Λ=500 MeV) and -0.532 × 10^{-2} (N3LO/N2LO, Λ=600 MeV); for AV18/UIX: -0.565 × 10^{-2} (Λ=500 MeV) and -0.775 × 10^{-2} (Λ=600 MeV)
- N3LO(OPE) contribution: 0.825 × 10^{-2} (N3LO/N2LO, Λ=500 MeV) and 0.043 × 10^{-2} (N3LO/N2LO, Λ=600 MeV); for AV18/UIX: 1.304 × 10^{-2} (Λ=500 MeV) and 7.517 × 10^{-2} (Λ=600 MeV)
- Fermi matrix element: ⟨F⟩ = 0.9998 for all Hamiltonian models considered
- LEC z0 (adimensional, AV18/UIX, Λ=500 MeV): -0.421 at N3LO, -1.607 at N4LO; (Λ=600 MeV): 0.742 at N3LO, -1.048 at N4LO

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory treatment of nuclear axial currents by including N4LO loop corrections that were omitted in earlier studies by Park et al. (1993, 2003) and in calculations of weak fusion processes by Marcucci et al. (2012, 2013). The results provide numerically exact estimates of these loop corrections in the tritium GT matrix element for the first time, and determine the low-energy constant z0 (related to c_D in the three-nucleon potential) required to reproduce the experimental value when including these higher-order terms. This enables more reliable calculations of other weak processes in few-nucleon systems, such as pp and p-3He fusion and muon capture on deuterium and helium-3.
