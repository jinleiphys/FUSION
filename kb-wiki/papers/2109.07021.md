# Pure quantum extension of the semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck equation

> The Boltzmann equation is the traditional framework in which one extends the time-dependent mean field classical description of a many-body system to include the effect of particle-particle collisions in an approximate manner. A semiclassical extension of this approach to quantum many body systems was suggested by Uehling and Uhlenbeck in 1933 for both Fermi and Bose statistics, and many further developments of this approach are known as the BoltzmannUehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equations. Here I introduce a pure quantum version of the BUU type of equations, which is mathematically equivalent to a generalized Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory extended to superfluid systems. As expected, during non-equilibrium processes the quantum Boltzmann one body entropy increases during evolution.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a pure quantum extension of the semiclassical Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) equation, which is mathematically equivalent to a generalized Time-Dependent Density Functional Theory (gTDDFT) extended to superfluid systems, and which can accommodate two-body collisions including neutron-proton (np) pairs, not just nn and pp pairs as in previous TDSLDA implementations.

## Method

The author generalizes the Time-Dependent Superfluid Local Density Approximation (TDSLDA) by introducing generalized Bogoliubov quasiparticle wave functions that mix neutron and proton degrees of freedom, along with four types of anomalous densities: the usual neutron and proton pairing fields, plus two new fields for np-pairs with S_z=0 and S_z=±1. The resulting gTDSLDA equations are 4×4 matrix operators acting on four-component spinor-isospinor wave functions, with the pairing field matrix having the structure of an antisymmetric matrix that couples all spin-isospin channels. The approach is derived via an energy density functional that satisfies isospin invariance, with the mixed neutron-proton densities entering only as specific squared combinations.

## Key numbers

- For a nuclear system in a simulation box L³=50³ fm³ with momentum cutoff p_cut=600 MeV/c, there are approximately 4.56×10⁵ quantum phase-space cells for BUU, while TDHF requires about 1.1×10⁸ quantum phase-space cells for N=500 nucleons with spatial lattice constant l=1 fm
- In TDSLDA simulation of ²³⁶U fission, the quantum Boltzmann one-body entropy changes from S(t_ini)=12.4 to S(t_fin)=23.0
- After neutron and proton particle projection, entropy changes from S(t_ini)=10.1 to S(t_fin)=18.8 for ²³⁶U and from S(t_ini)=10.2 to S(t_fin)=19.9 for ²³⁸Pu
- In ²³⁸U+²³⁸U collisions at 1500 MeV initial center-of-mass energy, final fragments have excitation energies of about 400 and 600 MeV respectively, with a heavy fragment having Z≈123 and N≈198
- The rate of single-particle occupation probability redistribution ∑_k|ṅ_k(t)| becomes approximately constant after t₀≈350 fm/c for fission and t₀≈200 fm/c for heavy-ion collisions

## Context

This work extends the semiclassical BUU equation framework, originally developed by Uehling and Uhlenbeck (1933) and applied to nuclear physics by Bertsch (1988), to a fully quantum treatment. It builds upon the TDSLDA approach developed by Bulgac and collaborators (2007, 2010, 2011, 2013, 2019) for superfluid systems, and addresses the mechanism of avoided level crossings in fission dynamics initially suggested by Bertsch and collaborators (1980, 1990, 1997, 2017). The paper enables a unified quantum description of both mean-field effects and two-body collisions in nuclear fission and heavy-ion collisions, including np-pair transitions that were previously absent from TDSLDA calculations.
