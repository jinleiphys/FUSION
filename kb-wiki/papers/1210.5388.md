# Descriptions of Carbon isotopes within relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov theory

> Within the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) theory, the structure properties of Carbon isotopes are systematically studied. In order to reproduce the experiment data, we take the finite-range Gogny D1S with a strength factor $f$ as the pairing force. The self-consistent RHFB calculations with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings indicate the single-neutron halo structures in both $^{17}$C and $^{19}$C, whereas the two-neutron halo in $^{22}$C is not well supported. It is also found that close to the neutron drip line there exists distinct odd-even staggering on neutron radii, which is tightly related with the blocking effects and correspondingly the blocking effect plays a significant role in halo formation.

## Key claim

The self-consistent RHFB calculations with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings indicate single-neutron halo structures in both ¹⁷C and ¹⁹C, whereas the two-neutron halo in ²²C is not well supported because the last two valence neutrons are bound too deeply (two-neutron separation energy of 1.56 MeV) to spread over a fairly wide range.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (RHFB) and relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) equations for Carbon isotopes from ¹⁰C to ²²C, expanding the quasi-particle spinors on a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with a spherical box-size of 30 fm. The finite-range Gogny force D1S with an adjusted strength factor f was used as the effective pairing interaction in the particle-particle channel, and the quasi-particle spinors were expanded using 48 positive-energy and 12 negative-energy basis states. For odd Carbon isotopes, the blocking effects were taken into account self-consistently within the Bogoliubov scheme.

## Key numbers

* The two-neutron separation energy of ²²C is 1.56 MeV from experimental data (Audi 2011), indicating the last two valence neutrons are bound too deeply for a well-developed two-neutron halo.
* The optimized pairing strength factors were determined as f=1.1 for PKA1, PKO2 and PKO3, and f=1.15 for PKDD, DD-ME2 and PK1, and f=1.25 for NL2, based on the single-neutron separation energy systematics.
* The root mean square deviations from data for binding energies with PKO2 at f=1.10 were 1.78 MeV, for single-neutron separation energies 0.41 MeV, and for two-neutron separation energies 0.84 MeV.
* Matter radii for ¹⁹C calculated by PKO2 were 3.11 fm, compared to experimental values of 2.74(96) fm (Liatard 1990) and 3.13(7) fm or 3.23(8) fm (Ozawa 2001).
* The occupation probability of the canonical 3s₁/₂ orbit was v²=0.034 for ¹⁹C and v²=0.004 for ²⁰C.

## Context

This work extends prior relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) studies of halo structures in ¹¹Li (Meng 1996), Ca isotopes (Meng 2002; Long 2010), and Zr isotopes (Meng 1998; Grasso 2006; Long 2010) by including Fock terms via the RHFB framework with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings (Long 2010). The calculations contradict the interpretation of ²²C as a two-neutron halo nucleus suggested by reaction cross section measurements (Tanaka 2010), while supporting single-neutron halo structures in ¹⁷C and ¹⁹C consistent with breakup measurements (Bazin 1998). The results enable further investigation of how blocking effects and the absence of pairing correlations for unpaired neutrons in low-j orbits contribute to halo formation in odd-mass nuclei near the neutron drip line.
