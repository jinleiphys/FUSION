# Comment on ``Mixing and Decay Constants of Pseudoscalar Mesons''

> The key assumption used recently by Feldmann, Kroll and Stich [Phys.Rev. D58, 114006 (1998)] that the decay constants f_η, and f_η' of the respective eta and eta ' mesons in the quark flavor basis follow the pattern of strange and non--strange quarkonia mixing in their wave functions, is reproduced in identifying the non-isotriplet part of the strong neutral axial current with the genuine axial hypercharge current J_{μ, 5}^Y =\bar q γ_μγ_5 Y/2 q, where Y=C+S+B is defined by the Gell-Mann-Nakano-Nishijima relation as the sum of charm (C), strangeness (S), and baryon (B) quark quantum numbers. The inequivalence between octet and hypercharge axial currents is pointed out.

# Comment on "Mixing and Decay Constants of Pseudoscalar Mesons"

## Key claim

The mixing pattern for η and η' meson decay constants proposed by Feldmann, Kroll and Stich (1998) can be reproduced by identifying the non-isotriplet part of the strong neutral axial current with the genuine axial hypercharge current, rather than the SU(3) octet current. The hypercharge axial current is inequivalent to the octet axial current because it contains the anomalously divergent axial baryon number current, which introduces the QCD anomaly into the decay properties.

## Method

The author calculates the matrix element of the hypercharge axial current J^Y_{μ,5} between the vacuum and the η meson state, using a quark flavor basis representation of the η wave function with a single mixing angle α. The quarkonium-quark couplings κ_j are assumed diagonal in flavor but not necessarily universal in strength, with isospin symmetry imposed for light flavors (κ_u = κ_d). The resulting expressions for the decay constants f^s and f^q are then compared to the phenomenological values from Feldmann, Kroll and Stich (1998).

## Key numbers

- The ratio f^s/f^q = √2 is obtained theoretically when κ_s = κ_u = κ_d (universal quark couplings)
- The phenomenological value from Feldmann, Kroll and Stich (1998) is f^s/f^q = 1.34 ± 0.06, which is compatible with √2

## Context

This comment addresses the work of Feldmann, Kroll and Stich (1998), who introduced a quark flavor basis with a single mixing angle to describe η and η' decay constants, in contrast to the two-angle octet-singlet mixing scheme of Leutwyler (1998). The author shows that the Feldmann et al. ansatz follows naturally from identifying the relevant axial current as the hypercharge current rather than the SU(3) octet current, a distinction previously noted in Kirchbach (1998). This identification explains why the QCD anomaly enters the η and η' decay properties and provides a theoretical basis for the observed f^s/f^q ratio.
