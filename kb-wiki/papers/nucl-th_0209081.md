# Hadrons in Hot and Dense Matter

> The description of excitations in hot and dense (hadronic) matter is discussed with emphasis on the use of correlation functions as a common framework for comparing different model (and QCD lattice) calculations with each other. Typical regimes of applicability of hadronic approaches are assessed, together with possibilities to confront them with experiment. We also elaborate on recent developments to relate baryonic in-medium effects to chiral symmetry restoration.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the description of hadronic excitations in hot and dense matter using correlation functions provides a common framework for comparing different model calculations and lattice QCD results, but emphasizes that the connection between in-medium modifications and chiral symmetry restoration requires evaluating the spectral functions of chiral partners on an equal footing, which remains an open problem.

## Method

The author reviews the use of correlation functions and spectral functions as a unified framework for describing hadronic excitations in hot and dense matter, comparing hadronic model calculations with lattice QCD results. The analysis covers different regimes of applicability from low-density chiral perturbation theory through intermediate-density many-body resummations to the phase transition region where both hadronic and partonic approaches require extrapolations. Specific applications focus on the vector (rho meson) and axialvector (a1 meson) channels, including comparisons with dilepton data from CERN-SPS experiments and quenched lattice QCD results.

## Key numbers

- Normal nuclear matter density n0 = 0.16 fm^-3 (used as reference density in applicability table)
- Temperature regimes for hadronic approaches: T <= 120 MeV (chiral perturbation theory), 120-150 MeV (many-body), 150-Tc (hadronic extrapolation), >= Tc (pQCD extrapolation)
- Energy density regimes: <= 0.05 GeV/fm^3 (low), 0.05-0.3 GeV/fm^3 (intermediate), 0.3-0.8 GeV/fm^3 (high), >= 1 GeV/fm^3 (near Tc)
- Hadron density regimes: <= 0.5 n0, 0.5-2 n0, 2-5 n0
- Mass gaps of Delta E ~ 0.5 GeV for low-lying spin-1/2 and -3/2 baryon states in vacuum spectrum
- Lattice QCD vector correlator analyzed at T = 180 MeV (1.5 Tc) from quenched calculation by Karsch (2002)
- Continuum threshold for perturbative contribution set at q0 ~ 1.3 GeV in hadronic model calculations

## Context

This review extends the line of work on in-medium modifications of hadrons, particularly the rho meson melting description by Rapp and Wambach (1999) that explained dilepton excess observed by CERES/NA45 at CERN-SPS energies. It also builds on earlier work by Shuryak (1993) on correlation functions and Schafer and Shuryak (1996) on instanton-based descriptions of QCD vacuum. The paper enables future comparisons between hadronic models and lattice QCD results, and identifies the need for experimental data on baryon resonance decay modes (e.g., N(1900) to N a1) from the HADES detector at GSI to constrain calculations of axialvector spectral functions.
