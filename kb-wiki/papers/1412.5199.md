# Kinetic freeze out from an anisotropic fluid in high-energy heavy-ion collisions: particle spectra, Hanbury Brown-Twiss radii, and anisotropic flow

> Dissipative relativistic fluid-dynamical descriptions of the extended fireball formed in high-energy heavy-ion collisions are quite successful, yet require a prescription for converting the fluid into particles. We present arguments in favour of using a locally anisotropic momentum distribution for the particles emitted from the fluid, so as to smooth out discontinuities introduced by the usual conversion prescriptions. Building on this ansatz, we investigate the effect of the asymmetry on several observables of heavy ion physics.

## Key claim

Introducing a locally anisotropic momentum distribution for particles emitted from the fluid at freeze-out can smooth out discontinuities inherent in the standard Cooper-Frye prescription and reduce the sensitivity of computed observables to the freeze-out temperature parameter, though the anisotropy is a mere assumption motivated by non-relativistic studies and the actual functional form should emerge from a detailed kinetic description.

## Method

The authors adopt a Romatschke-Strickland-like phase-space distribution with an anisotropy parameter ξ that controls the relative pressure along the radial ("out") direction versus the sideward and longitudinal directions. They compute the Cooper-Frye integral numerically for pions at midrapidity, assuming a constant proper-time freeze-out hypersurface with a generalized blast-wave flow profile, and evaluate the transverse momentum spectra, Hanbury Brown-Twiss (HBT) radii (R_out, R_side, R_long), and anisotropic flow coefficients v_n as functions of ξ and the scale parameter Λ.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass set to m = 140 MeV for all calculations
- Fixed blast-wave parameters: τ_f.o. = 7.5 fm/c, R = 10 fm, ū_max = 1, V_2 = V_3 = 0.05 (except HBT radii where all V_n vanish)
- Λ varied between 130 and 160 MeV; ξ varied from -0.5 to 0.3
- For fixed Λ = 150 MeV, negative ξ (larger radial pressure) yields harder transverse momentum spectra and larger R_out, while R_long remains largely unaffected
- For (Λ = 130 MeV, ξ = -0.5) with ū_max = 0.8, the transverse spectrum nearly collapses onto that for (Λ = 150 MeV, ξ = 0) with at most 15% relative difference over the whole momentum range
- Elliptic flow v_2 decreases as ξ becomes more negative (radial temperature grows), reflecting dilution of collective flow by thermal motion

## Context

This work extends the standard Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription (Cooper 1974) by introducing a locally anisotropic momentum distribution, motivated by non-relativistic freeze-out studies (Hamel 1966) and by the desire to mitigate discontinuities between fluid-dynamical and particle descriptions. The anisotropy differs from that considered by Rybczynski (2012), which was along the beam axis; here the deformation is along the radial direction. The results suggest that introducing the anisotropy parameter opens a wider range for the freeze-out temperature without drastically affecting observables, though the actual form of the distribution and the freeze-out hypersurface remain to be calculated from a more microscopic approach.
