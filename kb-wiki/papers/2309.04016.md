# Fine structure of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance in $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb

> Over the past two decades high energy-resolution inelastic proton scattering studies were used to gain an understanding of the origin of fine structure observed in the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR) and the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR). Recently, the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in $^{58}$Ni, $^{90}$Zr, $^{120}$Sn and $^{208}$Pb was studied at the iThemba Laboratory for Accelerator Based Sciences (iThemba LABS) by means of inelastic $α$-particle scattering at very forward scattering angles (including $0\circ$). The good energy resolution of the measurement revealed significant fine structure of the ISGMR.~To extract scales by means of wavelet analysis characterizing the observed fine structure of the ISGMR in order to investigate the role of different mechanisms contributing to its decay width. Characteristic energy scales are extracted from the fine structure using continuous wavelet transforms. The experimental energy scales are compared to different theoretical approaches performed in the framework of quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) and beyond-QRPA including complex configurations using both non-relativistic and relativistic density functional theory. All models highlight the role of Landau fragmentation for the damping of the ISGMR especially in the medium-mass region. Models which include the coupling between one particle-one hole (1p-1h) and two particle-two hole (2p-2h) configurations modify the strength distributions and wavelet scales indicating the importance of the spreading width. The effect becomes more pronounced with increasing mass number. Wavelet scales remain a sensitive measure of the interplay between Landau fragmentation and the spreading width in the description of the fine structure of giant resonances.

## Key claim

The fine structure of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) in medium-mass and heavy nuclei arises from an interplay between Landau fragmentation and spreading width, with the spreading width becoming more pronounced as mass number increases; this places the ISGMR damping mechanism intermediate between the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR), where Landau damping dominates, and the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (ISGQR), where fine structure originates from coupling to low-lying surface vibrations.

## Method

High energy-resolution inelastic alpha-particle scattering at 196 MeV was performed at the iThemba LABS K600 magnetic spectrometer at laboratory angles of 0 degrees and 4 degrees on self-supporting targets of 58Ni, 90Zr, 120Sn, and 208Pb. Isoscalar monopole strength distributions were extracted using the Difference-of-Spectra technique with excitation energy-dependent corrections based on multipole decomposition analysis of L greater than 0 cross sections from previous experiments. Characteristic energy scales were extracted from the fine structure using continuous wavelet transforms with a Morlet wavelet, and these experimental scales were compared to theoretical calculations at the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) level and beyond-QRPA including phonon-phonon coupling (PPC) and relativistic quasiparticle time blocking approximation (RQTBA) using both non-relativistic Skyrme and relativistic density functional theory.

## Key numbers

- Energy resolution of approximately 70 keV full width at half maximum (FWHM) was achieved for both zero-degree and four-degree measurements
- Spectra were binned to 30 keV for analysis
- The Skyrme-EDF SLy4 interaction used has a nuclear matter incompressibility modulus K_infinity = 229.9 MeV
- The relativistic parameter set NL3* has a compressibility modulus K_infinity = 258 MeV
- For 208Pb, the PPC calculation included 40 monopole phonons, 49 dipole phonons, 74 quadrupole phonons, 109 octupole phonons, 93 hexadecapole phonons, and 104 pentapole phonons when all one- and two-phonon configurations below 25 MeV were included
- The imaginary part of the energy variable in the RQTBA response function was chosen as Delta = 35 keV to match the experimental energy resolution of 70 keV
- The cutoff of the discretized continuous part of the single-particle spectra in the QRPA calculations was at 100 MeV
- Pairing strength was taken as -870 MeV fm^3 with a smooth cutoff at 10 MeV above the Fermi energies

## Context

This work extends previous high energy-resolution studies of fine structure in the ISGQR (Shevchenko 2004, Shevchenko 2009, Usman 2011, Usman 2016, Kureba 2018) and the IVGDR (Poltoratska 2014, Fearick 2018, Donaldson 2020, Carter 2022) to the ISGMR, which had previously only been characterized in terms of gross properties such as centroids and sum-rule strengths. The theoretical framework builds on QRPA calculations with Skyrme interactions (Khan 2009, Severyukhin 2017, Arsenyev 2021) and relativistic approaches (Paar 2003, Litvinova 2008, Litvinova 2023), with the beyond-QRPA calculations incorporating phonon-phonon coupling and 2q-phonon configurations. The results enable systematic understanding of damping mechanisms across different giant resonance types and inform the ongoing debate about nuclear matter incompressibility from finite nuclei versus neutron star data.
