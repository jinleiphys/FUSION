# Small clusters of fermions

> Contributed talk to 17th International IUPAP Conference on Few-Body Problems in Physics, Durham, North Carolina, 5-10 June 2003. Published in the proceedings (Elsevier, 2004) pp. S106-S108

## Key claim

Despite the absence of bound three-neutron (3n) and four-neutron (4n) states in standard nuclear theory, the authors investigate whether small neutron clusters could exist under modified conditions, finding that binding requires such extreme violations of realistic nuclear forces that they produce serious anomalies (e.g., requiring a scaling factor η_c ~ 3 or making P-waves themselves resonant).

## Method

The authors compared the n-n AV18, Reid93, and MT13 potentials with the ³He-³He Aziz potential by rescaling them by the corresponding masses and computing low-energy parameters. They then studied the critical scaling factor η_c needed to bind dimers, trimers, and tetramers for both neutrons and ³He atoms, and also confined N=2,3,4 neutrons in a harmonic oscillator (HO) trap to compute binding energies B_N by solving the internal N-body problem with the pairwise potential V_ij = ½ m (ω/√N)² r_ij² + V_nn(r_ij).

## Key numbers

- n-n scattering length a = -18.59 ± 0.4 fm (experimental), compared to AV18 (-18.49 fm), Reid93 (-17.54 fm), and MT13 (-18.59 fm)
- ³He-³He scattering length a = -7.24 Å (Aziz 91 potential)
- Critical scaling factor for n-n dimer: η_c ≈ 1.08; for ³He-³He dimer: η_c ≈ 1.30
- "Bosonic" neutron trimer binding energy B_n3 ≈ 1 MeV; tetramer B_n4 ≈ 10 MeV
- For N=4 neutrons in an HO trap with b=2 fm: B_4 = 15.30 MeV, B_4/N = 3.58 MeV, B_4/E_HO = 0.23
- For N=4 neutrons in an HO trap with b=4 fm: B_4 = 4.31 MeV, B_4/N = 1.08 MeV, B_4/E_HO = 0.26
- For N=2 neutrons in an HO trap with b=2 fm: B_2 = 6.34 MeV, B_2/N = 3.17 MeV
- For N=3 neutrons in an HO trap with b=2 fm: B_3 = 9.74 MeV, B_3/N = 3.25 MeV

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies by Pieper (2003) indicating no bound 3n or 4n states, and responds to the GANIL experiment by Marques et al. (2002) suggesting a tetraneutron that was not confirmed. The authors draw a parallel with ³He atomic clusters studied by Guardiola and Navarro (2001), showing that while neutron clusters are more favorable than ³He clusters due to different P-wave contributions, realistic n-n forces still prevent binding. The results enable future systematic studies of the N-dependence of the critical scaling factor η_c to determine whether larger neutron clusters could exist, using more powerful computational methods beyond N=4.
