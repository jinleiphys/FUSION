# Revisiting the proton mass decomposition

> Different decompositions (sum rules) for the proton mass have been proposed in the literature. All of them are related to the energy-momentum tensor in quantum chromodynamics. We review and revisit these decompositions by paying special attention to recent developments with regard to the renormalization of the energy-momentum tensor. The connection between the sum rules is discussed as well. We present numerical results for the various terms of the mass decompositions up to 3 loops in the strong coupling, and consider their scheme dependence. We also elaborate on the role played by the trace anomaly and the sigma terms.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, due to the forward limit of the energy-momentum tensor having only two independent form factors, any decomposition of the proton mass can have at most two independent terms; the various four-term decompositions in the literature are therefore not independent, and the trace anomaly operator does not appear in the 00-component of the energy-momentum tensor in dimensional regularization, contrary to some earlier presentations.

## Method

The authors review and revisit four proton mass sum rules (Ji 1994/1995, Lorcé 2017, and Hatta-Rajan-Tanaka 2018) by analyzing the renormalization of the quark and gluon parts of the QCD energy-momentum tensor, including the mixing of four operators under renormalization. They compute numerical results for all terms in these sum rules up to 3 loops in the strong coupling constant, using analytical renormalization constants from the literature, and examine the scheme dependence by comparing four renormalization schemes (MS, MSbar, D1, D2).

## Key numbers

- The trace term in the decomposition of the 00-component contributes exactly 25% of the proton mass (one quarter of M).
- The quark mass (sigma term) contribution to the proton mass receives a sizable contribution from charm quarks and heavier quarks, according to current phenomenology.
- Numerical results for all mass decomposition terms are presented up to 3 loops in the strong coupling constant, with the specific numbers depending on the renormalization scheme chosen (MS, MSbar, D1, D2).
- The two-term decomposition by Hatta, Rajan, and Tanaka (2018) gives quark and gluon contributions as M_q = M (A_q(0) + 4 Cbar_q(0)) and M_g = M (A_g(0) + 4 Cbar_g(0)), with the constraint A_q(0) + A_g(0) = 1 and Cbar_q(0) + Cbar_g(0) = 0.

## Context

This work extends the pioneering mass decompositions of Ji (1994, 1995) and the more recent formulations by Lorcé (2017) and Hatta, Rajan, and Tanaka (2018), by incorporating the full renormalization of the energy-momentum tensor and its scheme dependence. The authors show that the trace anomaly, which was included in Ji's original four-term decomposition, does not appear in the 00-component of the energy-momentum tensor when properly renormalized, and that the four-term sum rules reduce to at most two independent terms. This enables a clearer understanding of how the proton mass is distributed among quark kinetic energy, quark mass, and gluon field energy, and clarifies the role of renormalization scheme choices in interpreting these decompositions.
