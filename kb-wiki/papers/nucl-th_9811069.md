# Probing the deuteron structure at small NN distances by antiproton-deuteron annihilation

> The production of pions by antiproton-deuteron annihilation at rest is analyzed. Assuming the possible existence of two delta-isobars in a deuteron some enhancement in the distribution over the invariant mass of two negative charged pions is predicted.

## Key claim

Assuming the deuteron wave function can contain a Fock component with two delta-isobars (Delta-Delta), an enhancement in the invariant mass distribution of two negative pions in the region 1.4-1.5 GeV/c^2 from antiproton-deuteron annihilation at rest is predicted, which can be used to estimate an upper limit for this exotic deuteron component.

## Method

The authors analyzed the reaction $\bar p d \rightarrow 2\pi^-\pi^+ p$ at rest within the impulse approximation, factorizing the amplitude into the deuteron wave function and the $\bar p n \rightarrow 2\pi^-\pi^+$ annihilation amplitude. The annihilation amplitude was parametrized using a Reggeized meson-exchange model for the $\pi^+ n \rightarrow \pi^+\pi^- p$ process and a one-baryon exchange model, with parameters fitted to OBELIX data for the $\pi^+\pi^-$ invariant mass spectrum. The contribution from a possible $\Delta\Delta$ component in the deuteron was calculated using a quark-model wave function and added incoherently to the conventional contribution, with the joint parameter $s_\Delta \cdot C^2$ fitted to the $\pi^-\pi^-$ invariant mass spectrum from OBELIX.

## Key numbers

- The enhancement in the $\pi^-\pi^-$ invariant mass distribution is predicted in the region $m_{2\pi^-} = 1.4-1.5$ GeV/c^2.
- The upper limit for the yield of the $\bar p (\Delta^-\Delta^{++}) \rightarrow 2\pi^-\pi^+ p$ reaction is $Y \leq 6.5 \times 10^{-5}$ at 90% confidence level.
- The estimated upper limit for the probability of finding the deuteron in a $\Delta\Delta$ configuration is $P_{\Delta\Delta} \leq 1\%$.
- The $\rho$-meson resonance appears at $m_{\pi^+\pi^-} \simeq 0.77$ GeV/c^2 and the $f_2$-meson at $m_{\pi^+\pi^-} = 1.26$ GeV/c^2 in the $\pi^+\pi^-$ spectrum.
- The Breit-Wigner width for the $\pi^-\pi^-$ enhancement was fixed at $\Gamma_{\pi^-\pi^-} = 60$ MeV based on theoretical prediction.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical efforts to detect baryon resonances in the deuteron, such as the $NN^*(1440)$ component studied by Dorodnykh, Lykasov, Rzjanin, and Cassing (1995), and the quark-model calculations of Glozman, Burkova, and Kuchina (1969) and Glozman, Neudatchin, and Obuchovsky (1993). Prior experimental searches by Abramov et al. (1975), Benz and Soeding (1974), Beurtey et al. (1976), and Allasia et al. (1986) had set upper limits for $N^*$ admixtures in the range 0.4-0.9%. The analysis uses new OBELIX data (Bertin et al., 1997) and suggests that higher statistics (a factor of 10) expected from the same collaboration could enable a more definitive test of the $\Delta\Delta$ component hypothesis.
