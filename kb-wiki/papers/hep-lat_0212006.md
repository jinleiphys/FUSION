# Quark-Gluon Mixed Condensate (anti-Q sigma G Q) from Lattice QCD

> We study the quark-gluon mixed condensate g<\bar{q} sigma G q>, which is another chiral order parameter, in SU(3)c lattice QCD with the Kogut-Susskind fermion at the quenched level. Using 100 gauge configurations on the 16^4 lattice with β= 6.0, we measure the mixed condensate at 16 points in each gauge configuration for each current-quark mass of m_q=21, 36, 52 MeV. From the 1600 data for each m_q, we find the ratio between the mixed condensate and the quark condensate, m_0^2 = g<\bar{q} sigma G q> / <\bar{q}q> \simeq 2.5 GeV^2 at the lattice scale of 1/a \simeq 2 GeV in the chiral limit. The large value of the mixed condensate suggests its importance in the operator product expansions in QCD. We also show our preliminary results of the mixed condensate at finite temperature.

## Key claim

The ratio of the quark-gluon mixed condensate to the quark condensate, \(m_0^2 = g\langle\bar{q}\sigma G q\rangle / \langle\bar{q}q\rangle\), is found to be approximately 2.5 GeV\(^2\) at the lattice scale of \(a^{-1} \simeq 2\) GeV in the chiral limit, which is significantly larger than the standard QCD sum rule value of \(0.8 \pm 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) proposed by Belavin et al. This large value suggests the importance of the mixed condensate in operator product expansions, though the authors note that non-perturbative renormalization may be needed for a more precise comparison.

## Method

The authors performed SU(3)\(_c\) lattice QCD simulations at the quenched level using the Kogut-Susskind fermion to preserve chiral symmetry, with the standard Wilson gauge action at \(\beta = 6.0\) on a \(16^4\) lattice. They generated 100 gauge configurations and measured the condensates at 16 space-time points per configuration for three current-quark masses (21, 36, 52 MeV), yielding 1600 data points per mass, and used linear chiral extrapolation to obtain values in the chiral limit.

## Key numbers

- The bare ratio \(m_0^2 = g\langle\bar{q}\sigma G q\rangle / \langle\bar{q}q\rangle \simeq 2.5\) GeV\(^2\) at the lattice scale \(a^{-1} \simeq 2\) GeV in the chiral limit.
- After perturbative rescaling to \(\mu = 1\) GeV with \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} = 200-300\) MeV and \(N_f = 0\), the ratio becomes \(m_0^2 \big|_{\mu=1{\rm GeV}} \sim 3.5-3.7\) GeV\(^2\).
- The bare condensates in the chiral limit are \(a^3\langle\bar{q}q\rangle = -0.008721(17)\) and \(a^5 g\langle\bar{q}\sigma G q\rangle = -0.005652(14)\).
- The lattice spacing is \(a \simeq 0.10\) fm, determined from the string tension \(\sigma = 0.89\) GeV/fm.

## Context

This work extends the only previous lattice QCD study of the mixed condensate by Karsch and Seiler (1984), which used a small \(8^4\) lattice with \(\beta = 5.7\) and very low statistics (only 5 data points). The authors find a value of \(m_0^2\) much larger than the standard QCD sum rule estimate of \(0.8 \pm 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) from Belavin et al., and also larger than the instanton model estimate of about 1.4 GeV\(^2\) at \(\mu \simeq 0.6\) GeV from Polyakov et al. The results enable more reliable inclusion of the mixed condensate in QCD sum rules for baryons, light-heavy mesons, and exotic mesons, and the finite-temperature preliminary results show a drastic change around the critical temperature \(T_c \simeq 280\) MeV, reflecting chiral symmetry restoration relevant for RHIC physics.
