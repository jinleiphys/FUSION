# Next-to-leading order vs. quark off-shellness and intrinsic k_T in the Drell-Yan process

> We calculate the effects of next-to-leading order perturbative QCD as well as of the quark transverse motion and off-shellness on the Drell-Yan process cross section. By studying the s->infinity behaviour of the cross section in these approaches, we find that the effects of quark off-shellness and intrinsic-k_T parametrize those of higher twists. In particular, the off-shellness of partons generates part of the K-factor type corrections to the leading order cross section. Higher twist contributions to the p_T-spectrum of the Drell-Yan pairs are found to be large for presently accessible energies. The evolution of quark off-shellness distribution with the hard scale is also studied.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the effects of quark off-shellness and intrinsic transverse momentum (intrinsic \(k_T\)) in the Drell-Yan process parametrize higher twist contributions, and that these higher twist effects are large at presently accessible energies; the authors show that a phenomenological model incorporating both non-collinearity and off-shellness can reproduce the triple differential cross section without a \(K\)-factor, whereas next-to-leading order (NLO) perturbative QCD alone cannot describe the transverse momentum (\(p_T\)) spectrum.

## Method

The authors calculate the average squared transverse momentum \(\langle p_T^2 \rangle_{pert}\) of Drell-Yan dileptons from the NLO gluon Bremsstrahlung process \(\bar q q \to l^+l^-g\) using collinear perturbative QCD, convoluting the sub-process cross section with parton distributions. They then develop a phenomenological model that includes a Gaussian distribution for the quark intrinsic transverse momentum (with dispersion \(D\)) and a Breit-Wigner distribution for the quark virtuality (with width \(\Gamma\)), using exact off-shell kinematics and the leading-order (LO) sub-process cross section. The model is compared to data from Fermilab experiments E866, E605, and E439 by fitting \(D\) and \(\Gamma\) to the triple differential cross section \(d^3\sigma/dM^2 dx_F dp_T\) and the double differential cross section \(d^2\sigma/dM dx_F\). The authors also expand the model cross section in powers of \(1/s\) in the scaling limit \(s \to \infty\) to identify the twist nature of the corrections.

## Key numbers

- The perturbatively generated \(p_T\) width at NLO is around 0.6 GeV at \(s=1600\) GeV\(^2\) and \(M\approx 7\) GeV, which is about a factor of 2 smaller than the width measured by Fermilab experiment E866.
- For the model fit to E866 data at \(s=1600\) GeV\(^2\), the dispersion of the quark intrinsic transverse momentum is \(D=0.5\pm 0.18\) GeV.
- The off-shellness width \(\Gamma\) fitted to E866 data ranges from 50 to 300 MeV depending on the mass bin.
- The \(K\)-factor needed to scale the LO collinear QCD prediction for the double differential cross section to match E439 data at \(\sqrt{s}=20\) GeV and \(x_F=0.1\) is \(K=1.6\).
- In the model with \(D=500\) MeV and \(\Gamma=200\) MeV, the double differential cross section at \(\sqrt{s}=20\) GeV and \(x_F=0.1\) is reproduced with \(K=1\) (no scaling factor needed).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Altarelli (1979) and Fritzsch on the perturbative generation of \(p_T\) in Drell-Yan processes, and it directly addresses the failure of NLO pQCD to reproduce the \(p_T\) spectrum observed in Fermilab experiments E866 and E605. The authors build on their own prior model (Linnyk, Leupold, Mosel, 2005) that uses a double-unintegrated parton density to account for both intrinsic \(k_T\) and off-shellness, and they compare it to the simplified intrinsic-\(k_T\) approach used in PYTHIA and by Alesio, Murgia (2004). The paper enables future studies of Drell-Yan processes at low energies, such as those planned at GSI-FAIR (PANDA, PAX, ASSIA), by providing a phenomenological framework that can describe both the magnitude and shape of the cross section without a \(K\)-factor, and by clarifying the relationship between intrinsic parton motion and higher twist corrections.
