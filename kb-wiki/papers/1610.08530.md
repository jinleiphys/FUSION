# Tracing the pressure of the gluon plasma

> Being interested in how a strongly coupled system approaches asymptotic freedom, we re-examine existing precision lattice QCD results for thermodynamic properties of the gluon plasma in a large temperature range. We discuss and thoroughly test the applicability of perturbative results, on which grounds we then infer that the pressure and other bulk properties approach the free limit somewhat slower than previously thought. We also revise the value of the first non-perturbative coefficient in the weak-coupling expansion.

## Key claim

The pressure and other bulk properties of the gluon plasma approach the free limit somewhat slower than previously thought, and the first non-perturbative coefficient in the weak-coupling expansion requires a 30% revision; however, these conclusions depend on the assumption that the perturbative expansion is only reliable at much higher temperatures than assumed in prior work, specifically above 40 T_c for order alpha^{5/2} and above 300 T_c for order alpha^3.

## Method

The authors re-examine existing precision lattice QCD results for the gluon plasma from Borsanyi (2012) by matching the lattice interaction measure (the trace of the energy-momentum tensor) directly to perturbative QCD expressions at various orders, using a "thermodynamic renormalization" procedure where the QCD scale parameter lambda = Lambda/T_c is fixed by matching at a chosen renormalization temperature. They then apply the integral method for the pressure by integrating downward from high temperatures, using the perturbative result to fix the integration constant, rather than integrating upward from low temperatures as done in the original lattice analysis.

## Key numbers

- The shift in the scaled pressure p/p_0 for the (5|2)-model is Delta_sigma = -1.5 x 10^{-2}
- The combined shift from the (6|3)-model is Delta_sigma = -1.4 x 10^{-2}, corresponding to a 0.8% reduction of the pressure at large T
- The revised non-perturbative coefficient c_6 = -95 +/- 6, compared to the previous value of -71.8 +/- 2.9 from Borsanyi (2012)
- The QCD scale parameter for the (5|2)-model is lambda_{(5|2)}^* = 0.58 +/- 0.11; for the (5|3)-model it is lambda_{(5|3)}^* = 0.48 +/- 0.09; for the (6|3)-model it is lambda_{(6|3)}^* = 0.44 +/- 0.09
- The perturbative (5|2)-model is reliable for T >= 40 T_c; the (6|3)-model is reliable only for T >= 300 T_c
- The lattice interaction measure integrated from T_min = 0.7 T_c to T_max = 10^3 T_c gives sigma_latt = 1.702(2), while the Stefan-Boltzmann constant is sigma_SB = 1.755

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Borsanyi (2012) on lattice QCD thermodynamics of the gluon plasma, but finds that the perturbative fit used in that study included temperatures too low to be reliable, leading to a biased extraction of the non-perturbative coefficient c_6. The authors demonstrate that the perturbative expansion has characteristics of an asymptotic series, with applicability range decreasing as the perturbative order increases. This revision provides a firmer benchmark for improved analytic approaches like HTL-resummations from Andersen (2014) and suggests that the published lattice results for the pressure need a small but systematic correction, though the effect is likely too small to have immediate phenomenological implications for heavy-ion physics.
