# Nuclear structure and saturation effects from diffractive vector meson production

> We study exclusive vector meson production in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) of a wide range of nuclei, and assess the potential of measurements to constrain the small-$x$ structure of oxygen and neon nuclei. We employ an impact-parameter-dependent color glass condensate framework incorporating JIMWLK evolution, with parameters constrained by a recent global Bayesian analysis of $γ+p$ and $γ+\mathrm{Pb}$ data. We present predictions for coherent and incoherent $\mathrm{J}/ψ$ production in $\mathrm{O}+\mathrm{O}$ and $\mathrm{Ne}+\mathrm{Ne}$ UPCs at LHC energies, and quantify theoretical uncertainties using posterior samples from the calibration. We employ several nuclear structure models and find that $t$-differential observables are sensitive to the chosen model. We further study the mass-number dependence of saturation effects through nuclear suppression factors for coherent and incoherent vector meson production. Saturation-induced suppression increases systematically with both nuclear mass number and energy. Our results provide a unified framework for the systematic study of the onset of gluon saturation and nuclear structure at high energy, accessible in future UPC measurements at the LHC and at the Electron-Ion Collider.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that future measurements of diffractive J/psi production in ultra-peripheral O+O and Ne+Ne collisions at the LHC can discriminate between different nuclear structure models (e.g., PGCM vs. NLEFT) when using cross-section ratios, but the sensitivity is milder than previously claimed in Cepila (2025), and the alpha clustering effect is found to be negligible.

## Method

The authors employ an impact-parameter-dependent Color Glass Condensate (CGC) framework with JIMWLK evolution, where the dipole-target scattering amplitude is computed from Wilson lines evolved from an MV-model initial condition. The non-perturbative parameters (including a K factor) are constrained by a Bayesian global fit to gamma+p and gamma+Pb J/psi data from HERA and the LHC, and the posterior samples are used to propagate uncertainties. Nuclear configurations are generated using several models: Woods-Saxon parametrizations, ab-initio Variational Monte Carlo (VMC), Projected Generator Coordinate Method (PGCM) with and without alpha clustering, Nuclear Lattice Effective Field Theory (NLEFT), and Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC). The coherent and incoherent cross sections are computed from the scattering amplitude averaged over target configurations, and the UPC cross section is obtained by multiplying the photon-nucleus cross section by the photon flux.

## Key numbers

- The difference between realistic nuclear structure models (PGCM and NLEFT) for the t-integrated coherent J/psi cross section is at most 3% in gamma+O and 8% in gamma+Ne.
- The UPC cross sections are computed at LHC energy sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV for O+O and Ne+Ne collisions.
- The meson masses used are M_J/psi = 3.097 GeV and M_rho = 0.776 GeV.
- The minimum impact parameter for the photon flux integration is B_min = 2.608 fm for oxygen and B_min = 2.8 fm for neon; varying B_min by 10% changes the UPC cross section predictions by 2-5%.
- The cross-section ratio between Ne and O targets at small |t| ~ 0.03 GeV^2 is found to be sensitive to differences between PGCM and NLEFT models.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of diffractive vector meson production in photon-nucleus collisions (Mantysaari 2022, 2023) to light nuclei (oxygen and neon), complementing soft QGP probe studies of nuclear deformation at RHIC and LHC (Giacalone 2023, Jia 2022). It provides predictions for future UPC measurements at the LHC and at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), and enables systematic study of the onset of gluon saturation across the nuclear mass number A, from dilute protons to heavy nuclei like Pb and U. The results differ from Cepila (2025) in finding a milder sensitivity to nuclear structure models and a larger incoherent cross section due to inclusion of color charge fluctuations.
