# Excited Hadrons, Heavy Quarks and QCD thermodynamics

> We show how excited states in QCD can be profitably used to build up the Polyakov loop in the fundamental representation at temperatures below the hadron-quark-gluon crossover. The conditions under which a Hagedorn temperature for the Polyakov loop can be defined are analysed.

## Key claim

Excited hadron states can be used to construct the Polyakov loop in the fundamental representation at temperatures below the hadron-quark-gluon crossover, provided a Hagedorn temperature for the Polyakov loop can be defined under specific conditions that the authors analyze.

## Method

The authors compute the cumulative number of hadron states as a function of mass using the Relativized Quark Model (RQM) of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Capstick and Isgur (1986) for light and heavy quarks, and the MIT Bag model for heavy quarks. They then use these spectra to evaluate the trace anomaly via the Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model and the Polyakov loop via a hadronic sum rule, comparing the results to lattice QCD data from the HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations.

## Key numbers

- Hagedorn temperature fit to the cumulative number of light hadrons (u, d, s quarks) in the RQM yields T_H = 260 MeV with A = 0.80.
- For single-charmed hadrons in the RQM, fit to n(Delta) = A exp(Delta/T_H) over the range 1 GeV ≤ Delta ≤ 1.8 GeV gives T_H = 236 MeV and A = 0.216.
- For single-bottom hadrons in the RQM over the same range, fit gives T_H = 207 MeV and A = 0.209.
- The HRG model with the RQM spectrum reproduces lattice trace anomaly data below T = 180 MeV.
- The Polyakov loop results from PDG and RQM require a renormalization shift factor exp(C/T) with C = 25 MeV.
- In the MIT Bag model including tetraquark states, the Hagedorn temperature fit yields T_H ≈ 191 MeV.
- The non-overlapping condition for hadrons is violated at T = 160-170 MeV, where hadrons begin to overlap.

## Context

This work extends the Hadron Resonance Gas approach by using explicit quark-model spectra from the Relativized Quark Model (Godfrey and Isgur, 1985; Capstick and Isgur, 1986) and the MIT Bag model (Johnson, 1975) rather than only PDG-listed resonances. It builds on the authors' previous work (Megias, 2012) showing that a hadronic representation of the Polyakov loop exists in QCD, and addresses the longstanding discrepancy between lattice QCD results from the HotQCD and Wuppertal-Budapest collaborations for the trace anomaly above 200 MeV. The analysis enables systematic study of deviations from the HRG limit and clarifies the role of excited states and finite-size corrections in finite-temperature QCD thermodynamics.
