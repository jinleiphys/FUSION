# Relativistic Chiral Hartree-Fock description of nuclear matter with constraints from nucleon structure and confinement

> We present a relativistic chiral effective theory for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter taken in the Hartree-Fock scheme. The nuclear binding is insured by a background chiral invariant scalar field associated with the radial fluctuations of the chiral quark condensate. Nuclear matter saturation is obtained once the scalar response of the nucleon generating three-body repulsive forces is incorporated. For these parameters related to the scalar sector and quark confinement mechanism inside the nucleon we make use of an analysis of lattice results on the nucleon mass evolution with the quark mass. The other parameters are constrained as most as possible by standard hadron and nuclear phenomenology. Special attention is paid to the treatment of the propagation of the scalar fluctuations. The rearrangement terms associated with in-medium modified mass and coupling constants are explicitly included to satisfy the Hugenholtz -Van Hove theorem. We point out the important role of the tensor piece of the rho exchange Fock term to reproduce the asymmetry energy of nuclear matter. We also discuss the isospin dependence of the Landau nucleon effective mass.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a relativistic chiral effective theory for nuclear matter can achieve saturation only when the scalar response of the nucleon (linked to quark confinement) is incorporated, because the pure chiral sigma model without this response generates attractive three-body forces that cause nuclear matter collapse.

## Method

The authors construct a relativistic chiral Lagrangian in the Hartree-Fock scheme, using a chiral-invariant scalar field associated with radial fluctuations of the chiral quark condensate. They incorporate vector mesons (omega and rho) for short-range repulsion and asymmetry properties, and constrain the scalar sector parameters (sigma mass, scalar nucleon response) by fitting lattice QCD data on the nucleon mass evolution with quark mass. The Hamiltonian is treated in the static approximation, with meson fields decomposed into classical expectation values and quantum fluctuations, and rearrangement terms are explicitly included to satisfy the Hugenholtz-Van Hove theorem.

## Key numbers

- The non-pionic sigma term from lattice analysis is sigma_N^(non-pion) ≈ 29 MeV, and the total sigma term is sigma_N ≈ 50 MeV.
- The lattice-extracted expansion coefficient a_4 ≈ -0.5 GeV^{-3}, while the pure linear sigma model gives a_4^(sigma) = -3.4 GeV^{-3}, a factor of nearly 7 larger.
- The dimensionless confinement parameter C = (f_pi^2 / 2M) * kappa_NS is determined to be C = +1.25 from requiring a_4 = -0.5 GeV^{-3}.
- The sigma mass is fixed to m_sigma = 800 MeV by identifying the model's non-pionic sigma term with the lattice value.
- The rho meson tensor coupling is considered with two scenarios: kappa_rho = 3.7 (Vector Dominance Model) and kappa_rho = 6.6 (strong rho scenario from pion-nucleon scattering).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous chiral effective theory (Chanfray et al., 2002; Chanfray and Ericson, 2005) by incorporating the scalar nucleon response to resolve the long-standing problem of nuclear matter collapse in chiral theories, first noted by Kerman and Miller (1974) and Birse and Tarrant (2001). The approach is constrained by lattice QCD extrapolation methods of Thomas et al. (2004) and connects to the quark-meson coupling model of Guichon (1988). The results enable a framework linking nuclear matter properties to QCD condensates and confinement, with implications for understanding the isospin dependence of the Landau effective mass and the role of the rho meson tensor coupling in reproducing the asymmetry energy.
