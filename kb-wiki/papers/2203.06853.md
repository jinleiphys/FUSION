# Electron Scattering and Neutrino Physics

> A thorough understanding of neutrino-nucleus scattering physics is crucial for the successful execution of the entire US neutrino physics program. Neutrino-nucleus interaction constitutes one of the biggest systematic uncertainties in neutrino experiments - both at intermediate energies affecting long-baseline Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE), as well as at low energies affecting coherent scattering neutrino program - and could well be the difference between achieving or missing discovery level precision. To this end, electron-nucleus scattering experiments provide vital information to test, assess and validate different nuclear models and event generators intended to be used in neutrino experiments. In this white paper, we highlight connections between electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering physics at energies ranging from 10s of MeV to a few GeV, review the status of ongoing and planned electron scattering experiments, identify gaps, and layout a path forward that benefits the neutrino community. We also highlight the systemic challenges with respect to the divide between the nuclear and high-energy physics communities and funding that presents additional hurdle in mobilizing these connections to the benefit of neutrino programs.

## Key claim

Electron-nucleus scattering experiments provide essential, but not sufficient, data to reduce neutrino-nucleus interaction uncertainties that currently limit the discovery potential of the US neutrino program, particularly for the Deep Underground Neutrino Experiment (DUNE) at intermediate energies and for coherent scattering experiments at low energies.

## Method

The white paper reviews the connections between electron- and neutrino-nucleus scattering physics across energies from tens of MeV to a few GeV, surveys the current and planned electron scattering experimental landscape at facilities including Jefferson Lab, and identifies gaps in existing measurements and nuclear models. It assesses how electron scattering data can test, validate, and tune nuclear models and event generators such as GENIE for use in neutrino experiments, while also examining systemic challenges related to the divide between nuclear and high-energy physics communities and funding structures.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino-nucleus interaction uncertainties constitute "one of the biggest systematic uncertainties" in neutrino experiments, affecting both intermediate-energy long-baseline experiments (DUNE) and low-energy coherent scattering programs.
- The energy range considered spans from "10s of MeV to a few GeV."
- The paper cites the COHERENT collaboration's observation of coherent elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering (Science 357, 1123-1126, 2017) and their first measurement on argon (Phys. Rev. Lett. 126, 012002, 2021) as key low-energy results.
- The PREX collaboration's determination of the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb through parity-violating electron scattering (Phys. Rev. Lett. 126, 172502, 2021) is referenced as a relevant precision measurement.

## Context

This white paper was submitted to the Snowmass 2021 community study and extends the work of the NuSTEC white paper (Alvarez-Ruso et al., 2018) on neutrino-nucleus scattering challenges. It builds on prior efforts to use electron scattering data to tune neutrino event generators, including the work of Ankowski and Friedland (2020) assessing GENIE accuracy with electron data, and the "electrons for neutrinos" collaboration (Papadopoulou et al., 2021). The paper identifies the need for new electron scattering measurements on argon and other nuclei relevant to DUNE and coherent scattering experiments, and calls for improved coordination between the nuclear physics and high-energy physics communities to address the systematic uncertainties that could determine whether neutrino oscillation experiments achieve discovery-level precision.
