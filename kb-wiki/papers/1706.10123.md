# A new method to study the number of colors in the final-state interactions of hadrons

> We match the $ππ\toππ$ scattering amplitudes of Chiral Perturbation Theory with those from dispersion relations that respect analyticity and coupled channel unitarity, as well as accurately describing experiment. Their dependence on the number of colors ($N_C$) is obtained. By varying $N_C$ the trajectories of the poles and residues (the couplings to $ππ$) of light mesons, the $σ$, $f_0(980)$, $ρ(770)$ and $f_2(1270)$ are investigated. Our results show that the method proposed is a reliable way to study the $N_C$ dependence in hadron-hadron scattering with final-state interactions.

# A new method to study the number of colors in the final-state interactions of hadrons

## Key claim
The paper presents a method to introduce the number of colors ($N_C$) dependence into dispersive $\pi\pi\to\pi\pi$ scattering amplitudes by matching them to Chiral Perturbation Theory ($\chi$PT) amplitudes up to $\mathcal{O}(N_C^{-1})$, with the caveat that only the leading $N_C$ dependence is included and higher-order $N_C^{-2}$ corrections from $\chi$PT are ignored for simplicity.

## Method
The authors construct $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitudes for the $IJ=00,02,11$ partial waves using an Omnès representation based on the phase of the relevant amplitudes, which respects analyticity and coupled channel unitarity. They match these dispersive amplitudes to one-loop SU(3) $\chi$PT amplitudes up to $\mathcal{O}(p^4)$ to introduce the $N_C$ dependence, implementing it through a scaling of the phase as $\varphi(s,N_C) = \arctan[(3/N_C)\tan\varphi(s)]$ and scaling the polynomial part as $P^I_J(s,N_C) = (3/N_C)P^I_J(s)$. The poles and residues on the second Riemann sheet are then extracted by varying $N_C$ from 3 to large values.

## Key numbers
- $\sigma/f_0(500)$ pole location: $436.2(12.2) - i260.7(6.8)$ MeV, residue $|g_{f\pi\pi}| = 0.45(0.02)$ GeV at $N_C=3$
- $f_0(980)$ pole location: $997.7(1.1) - i21.7(1.9)$ MeV, residue $|g_{f\pi\pi}| = 0.27(0.02)$ GeV at $N_C=3$
- $\rho(770)$ pole location: $762.4(3.9) - i68.7(6.3)$ MeV, residue $|g_{f\pi\pi}| = 0.34(0.01)$ GeV at $N_C=3$
- $f_2(1270)$ pole location: $1278.3(7.0) - i79.3(17.8)$ MeV, residue $|g_{f\pi\pi}| = 0.50(0.05)$ GeV at $N_C=3$
- For $\rho(770)$ and $f_2(1270)$, the residue decreases exactly as $1/\sqrt{N_C}$ and the width decreases exactly as $1/N_C$
- For $\sigma/f_0(500)$, the residue contains $\mathcal{O}(N_C^{1/2})$ and $\mathcal{O}(1)$ components, with a fit $0.47 + 0.013\sqrt{N_C}$ for $N_C \geq 17$
- For $f_0(980)$, the residue contains $\mathcal{O}(N_C^{-1/2})$ components, with a fit $1.10/\sqrt{N_C}$ for $N_C \geq 10$

## Context
This work extends previous large-$N_C$ analyses based on unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory (U$\chi$PT) by Pelaez (2004, 2006) and Dai et al. (2011), which lacked crossing symmetry and could generate spurious poles and cuts. The new method uses dispersion relations that respect analyticity and coupled channel unitarity, building on the Omnès representation developed by Dai et al. (2014, 2015). The results for $\rho(770)$ and $f_2(1270)$ confirm the standard $\bar{q}q$ structure, while the $N_C$ trajectories of the scalar mesons $\sigma/f_0(500)$ and $f_0(980)$ support a mixed structure of hadronic molecule and $\bar{q}q$ components, with a possible tetra-quark component, as discussed in the context of Weinberg (2013) and Knecht (2013). The method enables future studies of $N_C$ dependence in hadron-hadron scattering with final-state interactions, though higher-order $\chi$PT corrections may modify the conclusions for scalar mesons.
