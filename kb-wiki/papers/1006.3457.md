# Stability of 1+1 dimensional causal relativistic viscous hydrodynamics

> The stability of the 1+1 dimensional solution of Israel-Stewart theory is investigated. Firstly, the evolution of the temperature and the ratio of the bulk pressure over the equilibrium pressure of the background is explored. Then the stability with linear perturbations is studied by using the Lyapunov direct method. It shows that the shear viscosity may weaken the instability induced by the large peak of bulk viscosity around the phase transition temperature $T_c$.

## Key claim

The Israel-Stewart theory's 1+1 dimensional solution may become unstable when the bulk viscosity has a large peak near the phase transition temperature and its relaxation time is small, but shear viscosity can weaken this instability; the authors cannot determine stable or unstable regions using the Lyapunov direct method because the largest eigenvalue of the stability matrix is always positive and the smallest always negative.

## Method

The authors study the 1+1 dimensional (boost-invariant longitudinal plus one-dimensional transverse) scaling solution of Israel-Stewart theory. They first evolve the background temperature and bulk pressure ratio using a parametrized bulk viscosity with a Gaussian peak near T_c, then apply the Lyapunov direct method to linear perturbations after performing a Hankel transform to eliminate spatial derivatives. The stability is assessed by computing the eigenvalues of the symmetrized perturbation matrix A + A^T for wave numbers k = 0 and k = 3.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity is set to η/s = 0.02 and 0.2 to study its effects.
- Bulk viscosity parametrization uses a Gaussian peak with width σ = 0.01 T_c and z_pQCD ~ 10^{-3}; z_0 values of 0.0 and 0.1 T_c are used.
- Initial temperature T_0 = 0.34 GeV.
- The relaxation time of shear viscosity is τ_π = (η/s) × (6/T).
- The relaxation time of bulk viscosity is τ_Π = b × ζ/(ε+p), with different b values used to study its effect.

## Context

This work extends the stability analysis of Torri (2008) from Navier-Stokes theory to the causal Israel-Stewart theory, adding shear viscosity which was not considered in that earlier study. It also builds on the Lyapunov direct method stability analysis of Denicol (2008) for the scaling solution with only bulk viscosity. The results suggest that clusterization of the source, which may help resolve the HBT puzzle as argued by Torri (2008), can occur in Israel-Stewart theory when the bulk viscosity peak is large, its relaxation time is small, and shear viscosity is not too large.
