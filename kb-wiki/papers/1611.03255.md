# Heavy Quark Coupled Channel Dynamics from Thermal Shifts

> QCD at finite temperature below the phase transition should be determined in terms of colour singlet states such as hadrons and strings. We show how quark-hadron duality allows extracting sensible information concerning heavy quark and string breaking coupled channel dynamics from Polyakov loop correlators.

## Key claim

Quark-hadron duality allows extracting information about heavy quark and string breaking coupled channel dynamics from Polyakov loop correlators at finite temperature below the QCD phase transition, specifically by showing that the thermodynamic shifts of the free energy when a heavy quark-antiquark pair is placed in the hot medium can be saturated by a coupled channel Hamiltonian involving heavy quarkonium and heavy-light meson-antimeson states.

## Method

The authors compute the heavy quark-antiquark free energy \(F_{\bar Q Q}(r,T)\) from Polyakov loop correlators in the hot QCD medium below the phase transition. They then saturate this free energy using a coupled channel static Hamiltonian that includes the heavy quarkonium potential \(V_{\bar Q Q}(r)\) (Cornell form with a linear confining term and a Lüscher term) and heavy-light meson-antimeson channels \(B\bar B\), with a transition potential \(W(r)=g e^{-m r}\) between them. The parameters of this Hamiltonian are extracted by fitting to lattice QCD data for the free energy with \(N_f=2+1\) flavors at physical pion mass from Borsanyi et al. (2015).

## Key numbers

* String tension: \(\sqrt{\sigma} = 0.424(14) \, {\rm GeV}\) from a fit to the Cornell potential with \(c=0\) and \(\Delta=0.472 \, {\rm GeV}\)
* Transition potential coupling: \(g = 0.98(47) \, {\rm GeV}\) from the fit to the two-mode Hamiltonian
* Transition potential range parameter: \(m = 0.80(38) \, {\rm GeV}\), corresponding to a range \(r_s = 1/m \approx 0.25 \, {\rm fm}\)
* Correlation coefficient for the fit parameters \(g\) and \(m\): \(r_{\rm corr}(g,m) = 0.98\)
* String breaking distance from the SESAM Collaboration (Bali et al., 2005): approximately \(1.2 \, {\rm fm}\) for pion masses of \(m_\pi = 700 \, {\rm MeV}\)
* The constant \(c\) in the Cornell potential takes the phenomenological value \(c = -250 \, {\rm MeV}\) when \(\sigma = (0.42 \, {\rm GeV})^2\) in the Relativized Quark Model

## Context

This work extends the approach of Megias et al. (2012) where single quark free energies were saturated with heavy-light meson states from the Relativized Quark Model of Godfrey and Isgur (1985) and Capstick and Isgur (1986). It builds on the string breaking scenario proposed by Drummond (1998) and observed on the lattice by the SESAM Collaboration (Bali et al., 2005), but extracts the transition potential from finite temperature QCD free energies at physical pion mass rather than from zero temperature calculations with heavier pions. The extracted transition potential \(W(r)=g e^{-m r}\) conforms to Drummond's strong coupling prediction, and the resulting avoided crossing pattern shows a more abrupt transition than assumed in quenched quark models but similar to the generalized screened potential model of Gonzalez et al. (2014, 2015). This enables future extraction of coupled channel dynamics from lattice QCD data and provides a framework for understanding heavy quark energy loss in heavy-ion collisions.
