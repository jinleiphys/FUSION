# Ab initio calculation of the potential bubble nucleus $^{34}$Si

> The possibility that an unconventional depletion in the center of the charge density distribution of certain nuclei occurs due to a purely quantum mechanical effect has attracted theoretical and experimental attention in recent years. We report on ab initio self-consistent Green's function calculations of one of such candidates, $^{34}$Si, together with its Z+2 neighbour $^{36}$S. Binding energies, rms radii and density distributions of the two nuclei as well as low-lying spectroscopy of $^{35}$Si, $^{37}$S, $^{33}$Al and $^{35}$P are discussed. The interpretation of one-nucleon removal and addition spectra in terms of the evolution of the underlying shell structure is also provided. The study is repeated using several chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians as a way to test the robustness of the results with respect to input inter-nucleon interactions. The prediction regarding the (non-)existence of the bubble structure in $^{34}$Si varies significantly with the nuclear Hamiltonian used. However, demanding that the experimental charge density distribution and the root mean square radius of $^{36}$S are well reproduced, along with $^{34}$Si and $^{36}$S binding energies, only leaves the NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ Hamiltonian as a serious candidate to perform this prediction. In this context, a bubble structure, whose fingerprint should be visible in an electron scattering experiment of $^{34}$Si, is predicted. Furthermore, a clear correlation is established between the occurrence of the bubble structure and the weakening of the 1/2$^-$-3/2$^-$ splitting in the spectrum of $^{35}$Si as compared to $^{37}$S.

## Key claim

The existence of a bubble structure in the charge density of $^{34}$Si depends strongly on the chiral Hamiltonian used, but when the NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ Hamiltonian is selected based on its ability to reproduce experimental binding energies and charge radius of $^{36}$S, a bubble structure is predicted.

## Method

Self-consistent Green's function (SCGF) calculations were performed for $^{34}$Si and $^{36}$S using Dyson and Gorkov frameworks at ADC(1), ADC(2), and ADC(3) truncation levels. The calculations employed a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with up to 14 major shells ($N_{\text{max}}=13$) and used three different chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians: NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$, NNLO$_{\text{opt}}$, and NN+3N400 evolved via similarity renormalization group transformation.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of $^{34}$Si: -282.938 MeV at ADC(3) level with NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ Hamiltonian, compared to experimental -283.427 MeV
- Ground-state energy of $^{36}$S: -305.767 MeV at ADC(3) level with NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ Hamiltonian, compared to experimental -308.714 MeV
- Charge rms radius of $^{36}$S: 3.285 fm at ADC(3) level with NNLO$_{\text{sat}}$ Hamiltonian, compared to experimental 3.2985 $\pm$ 0.0024 fm
- Charge rms radius of $^{34}$Si: 3.187 fm at ADC(3) level (no experimental value available)
- Point-proton depletion factor $F_p$ in $^{34}$Si: 0.34 at ADC(3) level, decreasing from 0.49 at ADC(1) level
- Point-proton depletion factor $F_p$ in $^{34}$Si: 0.344 $\pm$ 0.002 for $\hbar\omega\in[16,24]$ MeV at $N_{\text{max}}=13$
- Charge rms radius of $^{36}$S computed with NN+3N400 Hamiltonian: 2.867 fm at ADC(2) level for $\lambda=1.88$ fm$^{-1}$

## Context

This work provides the first ab initio study of the potential bubble nucleus $^{34}$Si, extending previous energy density functional calculations by Richter (2003), Todd-Rutel (2004), Khan (2007), and Grasso (2009), as well as multi-reference EDF calculations by Yao (2012, 2013) and shell model calculations. The results establish a correlation between the bubble structure and the weakening of the $1/2^-$-$3/2^-$ splitting in $^{35}$Si compared to $^{37}$S, and motivate future electron scattering experiments on unstable nuclei at facilities such as ELISe at FAIR or SCRIT at RIKEN.
