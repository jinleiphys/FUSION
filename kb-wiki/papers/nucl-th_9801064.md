# Nuclear forces from chiral Lagrangians using the method of unitary transformation I: Formalism

> We construct the two- and three-nucleon potential based on the most general chiral effective pion-nucleon Lagrangian using the method of unitary transformations. For that, we develop a power counting scheme consistent with this projection formalism. In contrast to previous results obtained in old-fashioned time-ordered perturbation theory, the method employed leads to energy-independent potentials. We discuss in detail the similarities and differences to the existing chiral nucleon-nucleon potentials. We also show that to leading order in the power counting, the three-nucleon forces vanish lending credit to the result obtained by Weinberg using old-fashioned time-ordered perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The authors construct two- and three-nucleon potentials from chiral effective Lagrangians using the method of unitary transformation (Okubo's method) rather than old-fashioned time-ordered perturbation theory, yielding energy-independent potentials; they claim that to leading order in their power counting scheme, the three-nucleon force vanishes, consistent with Weinberg's earlier result.

## Method

The authors start from the most general chiral invariant Hamiltonian for pions and nonrelativistic nucleons, then apply Okubo's unitary transformation to decouple the purely nucleonic subspace from subspaces containing pions. They develop a power counting scheme based on the chiral dimension of the operator A that mediates the transformation, solving the nonlinear decoupling equation perturbatively order by order in the low-energy scale Q. The effective nucleon Hamiltonian is then expanded to leading order (ν = 6-3N) and next-to-leading order (ν = 8-3N), from which the two- and three-nucleon potentials are read off.

## Key numbers

* The chiral symmetry breaking scale Λ_χ is taken as the rho meson mass or 2πF_π ≈ 1.2 GeV, with the weak pion decay constant F_π = 186 MeV.
* The leading-order N-body potential has chiral power ν = 6-3N; the next order is ν = 8-3N (the order ν = 7-3N vanishes due to parity invariance).
* The minimum chiral dimension ν_A for the operator λ^(4k+i) A η is min(ν_A) = 3 - 3N - 2k, where N is the number of nucleons and k a non-negative integer.
* The minimum possible chiral power ν for the decoupling equation (Eq. 25) is min(ν) = 4 - 3N - 2k.

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990, 1991) and developed by van Kolck et al. (1994-1997), who used old-fashioned time-ordered perturbation theory and obtained energy-dependent potentials. The unitary transformation method, originally developed by Okubo (1954) and by Fukuda et al. (1954), produces energy-independent and Hermitian potentials, enabling a cleaner power counting scheme. The formalism enables systematic calculation of nuclear forces to higher orders in chiral perturbation theory without the energy-dependence complications of earlier approaches.
