# Simple non-perturbative resummation schemes beyond mean-field II: thermodynamics of scalar $ϕ^4$ theory in 1+1 dimensions at arbitrary coupling

> Recently, non-perturbative approximate solutions were presented that go beyond the well-known mean-field resummation. In this work, these non-perturbative approximations are used to calculate finite temperature equilibrium properties for scalar $ϕ^4$ theory in two dimensions such as the pressure, entropy density and speed of sound. Unlike traditional approaches, it is found that results are well-behaved for arbitrary temperature/coupling strength, are independent of the choice of the renormalization scale $\barμ^2$, and are apparently converging as the resummation level is increased. Results also suggest the presence of a possible analytic cross-over from the high-temperature to the low-temperature regime based on the change in the thermal entropy density.

# Simple non-perturbative resummation schemes beyond mean-field II: thermodynamics of scalar φ⁴ theory in 1+1 dimensions at arbitrary coupling

## Key claim
The non-perturbative resummation schemes R0 through R3 produce finite-temperature thermodynamic results for scalar φ⁴ theory in 1+1 dimensions that are well-behaved for arbitrary temperature and coupling strength, independent of the renormalization scale choice, and apparently converging as the resummation level increases. The results suggest a possible analytic cross-over between high-temperature and low-temperature regimes based on the change in thermal entropy density, but the author notes this is a cross-over rather than a true phase transition, as there is no confinement mechanism in scalar field theory.

## Method
The author applies a sequence of non-perturbative resummation schemes (R0, R1, R2, R3) developed in Romatschke (2019) to scalar φ⁴ theory in two Euclidean dimensions at finite temperature. The schemes correspond to different approximation levels of the partition function after introducing an auxiliary field: R0 drops the interaction term entirely (mean-field level), R1 includes the next level of resummation, while R2 and R3 incorporate the physics of thermal widths non-perturbatively through self-consistent self-energies Π(K) and Σ(K). The pressure, entropy density, and speed of sound are calculated via saddle-point evaluation of the partition function in the large-volume limit, with numerical evaluation of Matsubara sums for the R2 and R3 schemes.

## Key numbers
- At very high temperature, the R0 pressure reduces to the free scalar field pressure P_free = πT²/6
- For m_F² = 1, the R3 zero-temperature pressure is P_R3(T→0) = -0.021(1), matching the high-precision exact result P_exact(T=0) = -0.0214(3) from Serone (2018) and Elias-Miro (2017)
- The R2 zero-temperature pressure is P_R2(T=0) ≃ -0.0433
- The cross-over transition between low- and high-temperature regimes occurs at T ≃ 0.235 for m_F² = 1
- The author predicts s/s_free > 0.9 for T > 21 and s/s_free < 0.2 for T < 0.2 for m_F² = 1
- Numerical results for R2 and R3 are obtained for T ≳ 0.1 using the first one hundred Matsubara frequencies

## Context
This work extends the zero-temperature study of Romatschke (2019) to finite temperature, providing a systematic improvement over the well-known mean-field (large-N) resummation. The approach addresses three limitations of the Hard-Thermal-Loop (HTL) resummation program of Braaten (1989): HTL does not exhibit a sensible strong-coupling limit, does not easily incorporate transport physics, and exhibits unphysical renormalization scale dependence. The results are compared to high-precision zero-temperature calculations from Serone (2018) and Elias-Miro (2017). The author suggests these methods could be tested against direct Monte Carlo lattice simulations and extended to higher dimensions, where they might provide thermodynamic quantities accurate to better than 20 percent even when thermal widths are ignored.
