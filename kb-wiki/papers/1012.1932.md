# Effect of splitting of the neutron and proton effective mass on nuclear symmetry energy at finite temperature

> We present the temperature and density dependence of symmetry energy for nuclear matter at finite temperature based on the approach of the thermodynamics with Skyrme energy density functional. We first classify the Skyrme interactions into 7 groups according to the range of neutron and proton effective mass in neutron matter limit(99.99 per cent neutron in the matter). We find that there is obvious correlation between the temperature dependence of the symmetry energy and the splitting of the neutron and proton effective mass. For some Skyrme interactions with $m^{*}_{n}>m^{*}_{p}$ and strong splitting of the neutron and proton effective mass in asymmetric nuclear matter, a transition of the temperature dependence of symmetry energy from decreasing with temperature at low densities to increasing with temperature at high densities appears. For other Skyrme interactions, we do not observe such phenomenon. Our study show that the symmetry energy in hot asymmetric matter not only depends on symmetry potential part but also on the splitting of the neutron and proton effective mass to a certain extent.

## Key claim

The temperature dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy is correlated with the splitting of the neutron and proton effective mass; for Skyrme interactions where the neutron effective mass is larger than the proton effective mass and the splitting is strong, the symmetry energy can transition from decreasing with temperature at low densities to increasing with temperature at high densities, a phenomenon not observed for other interactions.

## Method

The authors used the thermodynamic approach with the Skyrme energy density functional to calculate the symmetry energy of nuclear matter at finite temperature. They classified 94 Skyrme interactions into 7 groups based on the range of the neutron and proton effective mass in the neutron matter limit (99.99% neutrons). They then computed the symmetry energy as a function of density and temperature for representative interactions from each group, introducing the ratio \(R_m = m^*_1 / m^*_0\) (effective mass in neutron matter over that in symmetric matter) to quantify the strength of the effective mass splitting.

## Key numbers

- The 94 Skyrme interactions tested were classified into 7 groups based on the neutron and proton effective mass ranges in the neutron matter limit.
- For the Skz-1 interaction (group IV, \(m^*_n > m^*_p\)), the ratio \(R_m^0\) at normal density is 1.84, and the transition of the temperature dependence of the symmetry energy occurs at a density of about \(0.75\rho_0\).
- For the Skz2 interaction (group V, \(m^*_n > m^*_p\) with \(R_m^0 = 1.13\)) and Skz3 (group VI, \(m^*_n < m^*_p\) with \(R_m^0 = 0.91\)), no transition was observed; the symmetry energy decreases with temperature at all densities up to at least \(4\rho_0\).
- The symmetry energy was calculated at temperatures \(T = 0, 5, 10, 20\) MeV for various Skyrme interactions.

## Context

This work extends studies of the symmetry energy for cold nuclear matter, where constraints at subnormal densities have been set by authors such as Liutx (2007), Famiano (2006), Liba (2005), Tsang (2009), and Cent (2009), but where the density dependence at high densities remains model-dependent and divergent (Xiao (2009), Trau (2009)). The paper investigates the previously unclear temperature dependence of the symmetry energy (Xuj (2008), Moustakidis (2007), Liba (2006)) and its correlation with the controversial neutron-proton effective mass splitting (Xuj (2008), Dalen (2005), Rizzo (2004), Persram (2002), Pandharipande (1992), Liba (2004)). The results suggest that experimental extraction of the symmetry energy at high densities from heavy-ion collisions must account for both density and temperature dependence, and that the temperature dependence itself could serve as a probe of the effective mass splitting.
