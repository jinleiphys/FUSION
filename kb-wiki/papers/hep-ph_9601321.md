# Calculation of the Isgur-Wise function from a light-front constituent quark model

> The space-like elastic form factor of heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons is investigated within a light-front constituent quark model in order to evaluate the Isgur-Wise form factor. The relativistic composition of the constituent quark spins is properly taken into account using the Melosh rotations, and various heavy-meson wave function are considered, including the eigenfunctions of an effective light-front mass operator reproducing meson mass spectra. It is shown that in a wide range of values of the recoil the Isgur-Wise form factor exhibits a moderate dependence upon the choice of the heavy-meson wave function and is mainly governed by the effects of the confinement scale.

## Key claim

The Isgur-Wise form factor, calculated within a light-front constituent quark model that includes Melosh rotations, exhibits only a moderate dependence on the choice of heavy-meson wave function over a wide range of recoil values, and is mainly governed by the confinement scale rather than by high-momentum components from one-gluon-exchange interactions.

## Method

The space-like elastic form factor of heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons was computed using a light-front constituent quark model, with the relativistic composition of constituent quark spins treated via Melosh rotations. Three different heavy-meson wave functions were considered: eigenfunctions of the full Godfrey-Isgur effective Hamiltonian (including one-gluon-exchange and linear confinement), eigenfunctions using only the linear-confining term, and a simple gaussian ansatz matched to the same average transverse momentum. The Isgur-Wise function was extracted by taking the infinite heavy-quark mass limit of the body form factor evaluated from the matrix element of the heavy-quark current.

## Key numbers

- Slope of the Isgur-Wise form factor at zero recoil: \(\rho^2 = 1.03\) for spectator quark mass \(m_{sp} = 0.220\) GeV, and \(\rho^2 = 1.14\) for \(m_{sp} = 0.419\) GeV.
- Harmonic oscillator parameters \(\beta_{PS}\) (equal to \(\sqrt{\langle k_\perp^2 \rangle_{conf}}\)): \(\beta_{D} = 0.386\) GeV/c, \(\beta_{D_s} = 0.408\) GeV/c, \(\beta_{B} = 0.417\) GeV/c, \(\beta_{B_s} = 0.445\) GeV/c, \(\beta_{T} = 0.440\) GeV/c, \(\beta_{T_s} = 0.472\) GeV/c.
- Constituent quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 0.220\) GeV, \(m_s = 0.419\) GeV, \(m_c = 1.628\) GeV, \(m_b = 4.977\) GeV, \(m_t = 160\) GeV.
- "Inertia" parameter \(\bar{\Lambda}\) for the \(T\) meson: 0.38 GeV; for the \(T_s\) meson: 0.46 GeV.
- In the infinite heavy-quark mass limit, the choice of wave function affects the slope by only \(\sim 10\) to \(25\%\).

## Context

This work extends the light-front constituent quark model previously applied to leptonic decay constants and electromagnetic form factors of light mesons (Cardarelli et al., 1994; 1995) to the calculation of the Isgur-Wise function for heavy-light pseudoscalar mesons. The results contradict the earlier ISGW model (Isgur, Scora, Grinstein, and Wise, 1989), which predicted a slope \(\rho^2 \sim 0.6\) to 0.8 using an approximate treatment of relativistic effects, and instead agree with QCD sum rule analyses (Bagan and Steinberger, 1993; Narison, 1994) and lattice QCD simulations (LAT1, LAT2), as well as the relativistic flux tube model (RFT). The finding that the Isgur-Wise function is governed primarily by the confinement scale enables future application to the time-like sector relevant to weak decays of heavy mesons.
