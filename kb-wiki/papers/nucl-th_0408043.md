# Inequalities for Light Nuclei in the Wigner Symmetry Limit

> Using effective field theory we derive inequalities for light nuclei in the Wigner symmetry limit. This is the limit where isospin and spin degrees of freedom can be interchanged. We prove that the energy of any three-nucleon state is bounded below by the average energy of the lowest two-nucleon and four-nucleon states. We show how this is modified by lowest-order terms breaking Wigner symmetry and prove general energy convexity results for SU(N). We also discuss the inclusion of Wigner-symmetric three and four-nucleon force terms.

## Key claim

The paper proves that in the Wigner symmetry limit (where isospin and spin degrees of freedom can be interchanged), the energy of any three-nucleon state is bounded below by the average energy of the lowest two-nucleon and four-nucleon states; this inequality is then modified by first-order corrections from the small but nonzero Wigner-symmetry-breaking term, and the resulting physical inequality is satisfied by experimental binding energies.

## Method

The authors use a non-relativistic effective field theory with contact interactions, writing the Lagrangian with a Wigner-symmetric term (coefficient \(C_S\)) and a symmetry-breaking term (coefficient \(C_{odd}\)). They compute \(C_S\) and \(C_{odd}\) on a spatial lattice for various lattice spacings by summing nucleon-nucleon scattering bubble diagrams and comparing with Lüscher's formula. Using Euclidean functional integrals and Hubbard-Stratonovich transformations, they derive correlation function inequalities via the Cauchy-Schwarz inequality, which translate into energy inequalities for states with distinct nucleon numbers. First-order perturbation theory is used to incorporate the effect of the symmetry-breaking term \(H'\).

## Key numbers

* \(C_S = -3.40 \times 10^{-4} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) and \(C_{odd} = -3.8 \times 10^{-5} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) at lattice spacing \(a_{lattice}^{-1} = 20 \text{ MeV}\).
* \(C_S = -1.20 \times 10^{-4} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) and \(C_{odd} = -6. \times 10^{-6} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) at \(a_{lattice}^{-1} = 40 \text{ MeV}\).
* \(C_S = -7.70 \times 10^{-5} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) and \(C_{odd} = -2.4 \times 10^{-6} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) at \(a_{lattice}^{-1} = 60 \text{ MeV}\).
* \(C_S = -5.60 \times 10^{-5} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) and \(C_{odd} = -1.3 \times 10^{-6} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) at \(a_{lattice}^{-1} = 80 \text{ MeV}\).
* \(C_S = -4.40 \times 10^{-5} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) and \(C_{odd} = -8. \times 10^{-6} \text{ MeV}^{-2}\) at \(a_{lattice}^{-1} = 100 \text{ MeV}\).
* Binding energies used for the inequality check: \(^{1}S_0 \sim 0 \text{ MeV}\) (nearly bound), deuteron \(D = -2.224 \text{ MeV}\), \(^{3}\text{He} = -7.718 \text{ MeV}\), triton \(T = -8.481 \text{ MeV}\), \(^{4}\text{He} = -28.296 \text{ MeV}\).
* The inequality \(E_{^{3}\text{He}}, E_T \geq \frac{1}{2}\left[ \frac{1}{2}(E_D + E_{^{1}S_0}) + E_{^{4}\text{He}} \right]\) is satisfied: \(-7.7 \text{ MeV}, -8.5 \text{ MeV} \geq -14.7 \text{ MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the approach of proving rigorous inequalities from Euclidean functional integrals, previously applied to two-nucleon correlations in symmetric nuclear matter by Lee (2004) and to upper bounds on pressure in isospin-asymmetric matter by Lee (2004). It confirms the observation of Mehen (1999) that low-energy symmetric nuclear matter is close to the Wigner limit, where the \(SU(2) \times SU(2)\) spin-isospin symmetry is elevated to \(SU(4)\) symmetry, a limit first studied by Wigner (1939). The results enable rigorous convexity bounds for energies of light nuclei and generalize to \(SU(N)\) systems, which may be relevant for trapped Fermi gases with multiple degenerate fermionic states.
