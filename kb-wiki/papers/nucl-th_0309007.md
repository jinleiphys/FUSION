# Nature of excited 0+ states in 158Gd described by the projected shell model

> Excited 0+ states are studied in the framework of the projected shell model, aiming at understanding the nature of these states in deformed nuclei in general, and the recently observed 13 excited 0+ states in 158Gd in particular. The model, which contains projected two- and four-quasiparticle states as building blocks in the basis, is able to reproduce reasonably well the energies for all the observed 0+ states. The obtained B(E2) values however tend to suggest that these 0+ states might have a mixed nature of quasiparticle excitations coupled to collective vibrations.

## Key claim

The projected shell model (PSM), which includes only two- and four-quasiparticle states and no explicit collective vibrational degrees of freedom, can reproduce the number and approximate energies of the 13 excited 0+ states observed in 158Gd, but the calculated B(E2) values are one to two orders of magnitude too small compared to preliminary measurements, suggesting these states have a mixed nature of quasiparticle excitations coupled to collective vibrations.

## Method

The authors employed the projected shell model (PSM) built on a deformed Nilsson-BCS basis at deformation ε=0.26, constructing the shell model space from projected 0-, 2-, and 4-quasiparticle states using neutron and proton Nilsson orbitals near the Fermi surface. They diagonalized a quadrupole-plus-pairing Hamiltonian (including quadrupole-pairing term) in this projected basis, with interaction strengths taken from previous PSM calculations for rare-earth nuclei, and compared the resulting energies and B(E2) values for transitions from excited 0+ states to the 2+ ground-state band with experimental data from Lesher et al. (2003).

## Key numbers

- 13 excited 0+ states observed in 158Gd by Lesher et al. (2003) within an excitation energy range of 1.2 MeV to 3.1 MeV
- PSM calculation produced 18 calculated 0+ states below 3.25 MeV excitation energy
- Ground-state deformation calculated by Moller et al. (1995) yields ε = 0.25; PSM calculation uses ε = 0.26
- B(E2, 0+_i → 2+_g) values from PSM range from 0 to 1.87 W.u. (Weisskopf units), with the largest value being 1.87 W.u. for the 1.004 MeV state (2-qp: -5/2[642]ν, 5/2[642]ν)
- Experimental B(E2) values from Lesher et al. are a few W.u., which are on average two orders of magnitude smaller than in-band B(E2) values of the ground-state band (typically a few hundred W.u.)
- PSM predicts a backbending in the moment of inertia at spin I=16

## Context

This work extends the projected shell model approach to excited 0+ states in deformed nuclei, directly addressing the 13 excited 0+ states recently observed in 158Gd by Lesher et al. (2003). It contrasts with the collective model approach of Zamfir, Zhang, and Casten (2003), who using the geometric collective model or interacting boson model could obtain at most 5 excited 0+ states up to 3.2 MeV and suggested many states may be of two-phonon octupole character. The PSM results indicate that quasiparticle excitations are essential for producing the observed number of states, but the discrepancy in B(E2) values shows that coupling to collective vibrations (as treated in the quasiparticle-phonon nuclear model) must be included, pointing toward future work incorporating higher-order multipole interactions or the Generator Coordinate Method within the PSM framework.
