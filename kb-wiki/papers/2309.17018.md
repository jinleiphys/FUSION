# Near-threshold resonance in $e^{+}e^{-}\rightarrowΛ_{c}\barΛ_{c}$ process

> We discuss the influence of various contributions of the $Λ_{c}\barΛ_{c}$ potential on the energy dependence of the cross section $e^{+}e^{-}\rightarrowΛ_{c}\barΛ_{c}$ near the threshold. New BESIII experimental data on the cross section and electromagnetic form factors $G_{E}$ and $G_{M}$ are taken into account. Our predictions are in good agreement with experimental data. We predict a bound state of $Λ_{c}\barΛ_{c}$ at energy $\sim 38~\textrm{MeV}$ below the threshold that may manifest itself in anomalous behavior of light meson production cross sections in a given energy region.

## Key claim

The paper predicts a bound state of \(\Lambda_{c}\bar{\Lambda}_{c}\) at approximately 38 MeV below the threshold, but this prediction is conditional on the specific rectangular-well potential parameters that best fit the BESIII data; the bound state may manifest itself only indirectly through anomalous behavior of light meson production cross sections in that energy region.

## Method

The authors model the \(\Lambda_{c}\bar{\Lambda}_{c}\) interaction using a Schrödinger equation with a potential that includes Coulomb, central S-wave and D-wave terms, and a tensor term, all taken as rectangular potential wells with fitted parameters. They solve the coupled radial equations for the S-wave and D-wave components of the wave function, then express the electromagnetic form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) and the production cross section in terms of the wave functions at the origin. The potential parameters are adjusted to simultaneously reproduce the BESIII data on the cross section and the form factors.

## Key numbers

* Bound state energy: \(E_0 = -38\ \text{MeV}\) (below the \(\Lambda_{c}\bar{\Lambda}_{c}\) threshold), corresponding to \(Q = 4545\ \text{MeV}\).
* Potential parameters for the rectangular wells: \(U_S = -1025\ \text{MeV}\), \(R_S = 1.05\ \text{fm}\); \(U_D = -156\ \text{MeV}\), \(R_D = 1.99\ \text{fm}\); \(U_T = -64\ \text{MeV}\), \(R_T = 0.78\ \text{fm}\).
* Goodness of fit: \(\chi^2 / N_{df} = 1.5\) for the combined fit to cross section and form factor data.
* Dipole form factor scale: \(Q_0 = 1\ \text{GeV}\) used in the parametrization of the short-distance amplitude.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous approach to near-threshold hadroproduction in \(e^{+}e^{-}\) annihilation, previously applied to \(p\bar{p}\) and \(n\bar{n}\) pairs (Milstein 2018, Milstein 2022c), to the charmed baryon case \(\Lambda_{c}\bar{\Lambda}_{c}\). It uses the latest BESIII data (Ablikim 2018b, Ablikim 2023a) which provide much higher precision than earlier measurements (Pakhlova 2008). The predicted bound state could be searched for via anomalous behavior in light meson production cross sections, analogous to suggestions in Milstein (2022c).
