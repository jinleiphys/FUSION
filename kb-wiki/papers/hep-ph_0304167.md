# Analysis of the [56,2^+] Baryon Masses in the 1/Nc Expansion

> The mass spectrum of the positive parity [56,2^+] baryons is studied in the 1/Nc expansion up to and including O(1/Nc) effects with SU(3) symmetry breaking implemented to first order. A total of eighteen mass relations result, several of which are tested with the available data. The breaking of spin-flavor symmetry is dominated by the hyperfine interactions, while spin-orbit effects are found to be small.

## Key claim

The mass spectrum of the positive parity [56,2+] baryons can be described by the 1/Nc expansion up to O(1/Nc) with first-order SU(3) breaking, but only because spin-orbit interactions are empirically very small (of order 1/Nc^2 in magnitude), which justifies treating spin-flavor SU(6) as the zeroth-order symmetry despite the fact that no strict spin-flavor symmetry exists in the Nc to infinity limit for excited baryons.

## Method

The authors construct a mass operator as an expansion in 1/Nc using products of generators of SU(6) (spin-flavor) and O(3) (orbital) groups, with SU(3) breaking operators added at first order. They fit the six effective coefficients (three SU(3) singlet operators c_i and three SU(3) breaking operators b_i) to the empirically known masses of [56,2+] baryons from the Particle Data Group listings, using only states with three or more stars in the listings. The fit yields eighteen mass relations, of which four to seven can be tested with available data depending on whether one- and two-star states are included.

## Key numbers

- The spin-flavor singlet mass (operator O1) is approximately 1625 MeV.
- The hyperfine interaction (operator O3) produces a splitting between octet and decuplet states of approximately 240 MeV.
- The spin-orbit operator (O2) induces spin-flavor breaking mass shifts of less than 30 MeV.
- The SU(3) breaking operator B1 gives a shift of about 200 MeV per unit of strangeness.
- The chi-squared per degree of freedom of the fit is 0.7, with four degrees of freedom.
- The systematic error from omitting O(1/Nc^2) operators is roughly estimated at 30 MeV.
- Mixing angles from the fit: theta(Sigma,Sigma')_3/2 = -0.16 rad, theta(Sigma,Sigma')_5/2 = -0.26 rad, theta(Xi,Xi')_3/2 = -0.21 rad, theta(Xi,Xi')_5/2 = -0.19 rad.
- The Lambda-Sigma splitting in the J=5/2 octet is almost 100 MeV, with contributions of 63 MeV from B3 and 40 MeV from B2.

## Context

This work extends the 1/Nc expansion analysis previously applied to ground state baryons (Jenkins 1995), negative parity [70,1-] baryons (Goity 1997, Carlson and Carone 1998, Schat et al.), and the Roper [56',0+] multiplet (Carlson and Carone 2000). The analysis shows that the hyperfine interaction strength increases from about 100 MeV in ground state baryons to about 160 MeV in the [70,1-] multiplet and about 240 MeV in the [56,2+] multiplet, suggesting an underlying dynamical mechanism that specific models might identify. The results provide predictions for fourteen unobserved masses and indicate that further experimental and lattice QCD study of strange states is needed to better establish the structure of SU(3) breaking splittings, particularly the role of the operator B2 which involves orbital angular momentum.
