# Hadronic Correlation Functions in the Random Instanton-dyon Ensemble

> It is known since 1980's that the instanton-induced 't Hooft effective Lagrangian not only can solve the so called $U(1)a$ problem, by making the $η'$ meson heavy etc, but it can also lead to chiral symmetry breaking. In 1990's it was demonstrated that, taken to higher orders, this Lagrangian correctly reproduces effective forces in a large set of hadronic channels, mesonic and baryonic ones. Recent progress in understanding gauge topology at finite temperatures is related with the so called {\em instanton-dyons}, the constituents of the instantons. Some of them, called $L$-dyons, possess the anti-periodic fermionic zero modes, and thus form a new version of the 't Hooft effective Lagrangian. This paper is our first study of a wide set of hadronic correlation function. We found that, at the lowest temperatures at which this approach is expected to be applicable, those may be well compatible with what is known about them based on phenomenological and lattice studies, provided $L$ and $M$ type dyons are strongly correlated.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a random ensemble of instanton-dyons, with strong spatial correlations between L and M type dyons, can reproduce the known features of hadronic correlation functions at the lowest temperatures where the approach is applicable (around T=100 MeV), provided the LM correlation parameter is tuned to match the well-known V-A correlation function.

## Method

The authors construct a Random Instanton-Dyon Model (RIDM) for SU(3) color group, where dyon positions and color orientations are selected randomly. They compute mesonic two-point correlation functions (pseudoscalar, vector, axial vector, and scalar channels) and baryonic correlation functions (nucleon and Delta) by using the fermionic zero modes of L-type dyons as a basis for quark propagators in the Zero Mode Zone. The key parameter controlling the strength of LM correlations, denoted r_LM, is tuned to reproduce the experimentally known V-A correlation function from ALEPH data, after which all other correlators are predicted without further adjustment.

## Key numbers

- Temperature used for the calculation: T=100 MeV, described as the lowest edge of applicability of the instanton-dyon approach
- Meson masses in the four channels studied: pion (134 MeV), rho (770 MeV), a1 (1260 MeV), and a0 (1450 MeV)
- Squared masses of the channels: m²_P ≈ 0.02 GeV², m²_V ≈ 0.5 GeV², m²_A ≈ 1.58 GeV², m²_S ≈ 2.1 GeV²
- The V-A splitting in squared mass: m²_A - m²_V ≈ 1 GeV², indicating the strength of SU(N_f) chiral symmetry breaking
- The P-S splitting in squared mass: m²_S - m²_P ≈ 2 GeV², indicating U(1)_a symmetry breaking
- Mean instanton size in the original instanton liquid model: ρ ≈ 1/3 fm
- Total instanton-antiinstanton density in the original model: n ≈ 1 fm⁻⁴
- The diluteness fraction of the instanton liquid model: (ρ/R)⁴ ≈ 1/3⁴
- The confining holonomy value where the Polyakov line vanishes: ν = 1/3
- The upper boundary of applicability: T ∼ (2-3)T_c ∼ 300-400 MeV
- The lower boundary of applicability: T > 100 MeV, where the dyon action S_M,L ∼ 3ħ
- The integral I_4 (measure of zero mode localization) for calorons: I_4 = 26.6 for ρ=ρ_T, 4.20 for ρ=2ρ_T, 1.55 for ρ=1

## Context

This work extends the instanton-dyon approach beyond its previous applications to deconfinement and chiral restoration phase transitions (studied by Faccioli, Larsen, Shuryak in 2013-2016) to the calculation of hadronic correlation functions. It builds on the earlier Interacting Instanton Liquid Model (IILM) from the 1990s, reviewed by Schafer and Shuryak (1996), which successfully reproduced correlation functions in vector, axial, octet baryon, decuplet baryon, and glueball channels but could not describe confinement. The paper compares its results to experimental data from the ALEPH collaboration (Barate 1997, 1998) and to lattice calculations by Tomii and collaborators (2017), as well as to the original QCD sum rule predictions of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (SVZ). The work enables future studies of whether the instanton-dyon ensemble can correctly reproduce the spin-flavor structure of nonperturbative QCD effects at finite temperature.
