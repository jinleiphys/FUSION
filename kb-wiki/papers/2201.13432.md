# Nuclear transverse momentum imbalance in the color dipole approach at the LHC regime

> Transverse momentum broadening of a parton propagating through a large nucleus is evaluated in the color dipole approach using different models for the dipole cross section or unintegrated gluon distribution, which lead to different values of the coefficient $C_{\mathcal{F}}(0,s)$. Numerical calculations are compared to data extracted from LHCb and ALICE experiments for nuclear broadening of $J/ψ$. We find that different models which describe the small-$x$ data predict values of $Δp_T^2$ that agree reasonably well with experiment, specially for forward rapidity. The centrality dependence was also analysed and the models are consistent with the ALICE measurements.

# Nuclear transverse momentum imbalance in the color dipole approach at the LHC regime

## Key claim

The color dipole approach, using different phenomenological models for the dipole cross section or unintegrated gluon distribution, predicts values of nuclear transverse momentum broadening that agree reasonably well with LHCb and ALICE data for J/psi production, particularly at forward rapidity; however, the experimental uncertainties are not yet able to clearly distinguish between different QCD dynamics, including those with gluon saturation corrections.

## Method

The authors evaluate the coefficient C_F(0,s) for quark broadening (and C_A = 9C_F/4 for gluon broadening) using five distinct models: the Kopeliovich-Schäfer-Tarasov (KST) parametrization, the Schildknecht-Surrow-Tentyukov (SST) model, the Donnachie-Dosch (DD) model, the Moriggi-Peccini-Machado (MPM) model based on geometric scaling, and solutions of the running coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation with two different AAMQS initial conditions (GBW and MV^gamma). Nuclear broadening is computed as Delta p_T^2 = <T_A> C_R(0,s), with gluon shadowing corrections applied, and compared to experimental data extracted from LHCb, ALICE, and PHENIX for J/psi production in pPb and pp collisions.

## Key numbers

- LHCb forward rapidity (2 < y < 4) at sqrt(s_h) = 8.16 TeV: experimental Delta p_T^2 = 2.05 +/- 0.12 GeV^2; KST predicts 3.90 GeV^2, SST predicts 2.81 GeV^2, MPM predicts 3.54 GeV^2, DD predicts 13.23 GeV^2, GBW predicts 2.94 GeV^2, MV^gamma predicts 3.08 GeV^2
- LHCb backward rapidity (-4.5 < y < -2.5) at sqrt(s_h) = 8.16 TeV: experimental Delta p_T^2 = 0.79 +/- 0.12 GeV^2; KST predicts 0.84 GeV^2, SST predicts 0.81 GeV^2, MPM predicts 1.01 GeV^2, DD predicts 1.23 GeV^2, GBW predicts 0.46 GeV^2, MV^gamma predicts 0.48 GeV^2
- ALICE forward rapidity (2.03 < y < 3.53) at sqrt(s_h) = 5.02 TeV: experimental Delta p_T^2 = 1.91 +/- 0.42 GeV^2; KST predicts 2.91 GeV^2, SST predicts 2.22 GeV^2, MPM predicts 2.85 GeV^2, DD predicts 9.14 GeV^2, GBW predicts 2.27 GeV^2, MV^gamma predicts 2.37 GeV^2
- ALICE backward rapidity (-4.46 < y < -2.96) at sqrt(s_h) = 5.02 TeV: experimental Delta p_T^2 = 0.68 +/- 0.33 GeV^2; KST predicts 0.70 GeV^2, SST predicts 0.72 GeV^2, MPM predicts 0.82 GeV^2, DD predicts 0.86 GeV^2, GBW predicts 0.40 GeV^2, MV^gamma predicts 0.42 GeV^2
- For the most central ALICE collisions (2-10% centrality class) at sqrt(s_h) = 5.02 TeV, forward rapidity: experimental Delta p_T^2 = 2.15 +/- 0.51 GeV^2; KST predicts 2.82 GeV^2, MPM predicts 2.75 GeV^2, MV^gamma predicts 2.40 GeV^2
- For the most peripheral ALICE collisions (80-100% centrality class): experimental Delta p_T^2 = 0.72 +/- 0.52 GeV^2; KST predicts 0.51 GeV^2, MPM predicts 0.50 GeV^2, MV^gamma predicts 0.43 GeV^2
- The transport coefficient extracted from data is hat_q_0 = 0.050 GeV^2/fm (from Naim (2020) and Arleo (2020)), compared to 0.02 GeV^2/fm from a global analysis by Ru (2019)

## Context

This work extends the color dipole approach to nuclear broadening, originally developed by Johnson (2001) and Raufeisen (2003), by comparing multiple phenomenological models for the dipole cross section against LHC-era data from LHCb and ALICE. The results are consistent with the KST model predictions of Kopeliovich (2017) and complement CGC-based calculations by Ducloue (2015, 2016) that found strong variation of Delta p_T^2 with N_coll. The study enables systematic comparison of different saturation and non-saturation dynamics against precision LHC data, though the authors note that current experimental uncertainties cannot yet distinguish between models with and without gluon saturation corrections.
