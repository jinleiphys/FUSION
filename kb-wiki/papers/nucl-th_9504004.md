# Dileptons from decay of vector mesons hadronized from a quark-gluon plasma

> We present a simple model for the dimuon production from the decay of vector mesons which are hadronized from a baryon-free quark-gluon plasma. Continuous decay of vector mesons in the medium is known to enhance the dimuon production and in our model we can estimate the total yield of dimuons decayed from vector mesons through the rate equations for the number of vector mesons of interest. Muon pair production critically depends on the time duration of both the mixed and hadronic phases. Results are discussed in view of possible measurements in experiments.

## Key claim

The total yield of dimuons from vector meson decays in a baryon-free quark-gluon plasma depends critically on the time duration of both the mixed phase and the hadronic phase, and the ratio of dimuons from rho to omega mesons can serve as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, but the results are presented as a function of these time durations by controlling the degree of equilibration rather than from a full dynamical calculation.

## Method

The author constructs rate equations for the number of vector mesons (rho, omega, phi) per unit rapidity and transverse area, including terms for hadronization from a quark-gluon plasma, creation and annihilation via secondary two-body collisions, and free decay. The hadronization is modeled assuming a first-order phase transition with chemical equilibrium maintained at constant temperature, leading to a constant hadronization rate during the mixed phase. The rate equations are solved separately for the mixed phase and the hadronic phase, using a parameter R_V to control the degree of balance between gain and loss terms, with the unknown collision parameters estimated from NA38 data on J/Psi suppression and phi/(rho+omega) enhancement.

## Key numbers

- Total decay width of rho meson: Gamma_rho^{tot} = 0.77 fm^{-1}
- Total decay width of omega meson: Gamma_omega^{tot} = 0.043 fm^{-1}
- Branching ratio ratio: B_rho / B_omega = 0.65
- From NA38 data analysis: lambda_{J/Psi}^{A,H} = 0.9, and gamma_rho = gamma_omega ~ 12, giving lambda_rho^{A,H} = lambda_omega^{A,H} ~ 11
- For the ideal case of perfect balance, the ratio X = (dN_rho^{l+l-}/dyds) / (dN_omega^{l+l-}/dyds) = 0.65 * (Gamma_rho^{tot} * Delta_tau_H + 1) for a purely hadronic system
- For the mixed phase contribution to be significant for omega mesons, (1/2 Delta_tau_M + Delta_tau_H) >= 20 fm is required

## Context

This work extends the model of Heinz and Lee (1991) for dilepton production from purely hadronic matter to include the evolution of a quark-gluon plasma through a first-order phase transition. The approach uses rate equations similar to those of Koch, Heinz, and Pisut (1990) which simultaneously described J/Psi suppression and phi/(rho+omega) enhancement measured by NA38. The paper enables future experimental searches for characteristic changes in the rho-to-omega dilepton ratio as a function of beam energy, which could serve as a signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, though the author notes that collisional broadening of the rho peak (as studied by Bi and Rafelski, 1991; Ko and Seibert, 1994; Asakawa et al., 1992; Haglin, 1994) may complicate measurements.
