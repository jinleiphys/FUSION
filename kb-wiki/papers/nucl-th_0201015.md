# On freeze-out problem in hydro-kinetic approach to A+A collisions

> A new method for evaluating spectra and correlations in the hydrodynamic approach is proposed. It is based on an analysis of Boltzmann equations (BE) in terms of probabilities for constituent particles to escape from the interacting system. The conditions of applicability of Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription are considered within the method. The results are illustrated with a non-relativistic exact solution of BE for expanding spherical fireball as well as with approximate solutions for ellipsoidally expanding ones.

# On freeze-out problem in hydro-kinetic approach to A+A collisions

## Key claim

The proposed method of escape probabilities, using local equilibrium approximations for the collision rate and gain term, can describe particle spectra in hydrodynamic models even when finite inhomogeneous systems transition from local equilibrium to free streaming, but the method's accuracy depends on the neglected term (the difference between exact and local-equilibrium gain terms weighted by escape probability) being small along particle trajectories, which the authors argue holds for both peripheral and central regions of the expanding system.

## Method

The authors split the Boltzmann distribution function into interacting and escaping components, then express the escaping function as an integral over the gain term weighted by the escape probability (the probability that a particle with given momentum will not interact again). They approximate the gain term and collision rate using the local equilibrium distribution function, which reduces the Boltzmann equation to a relaxation-time approximation. The method is tested against exact and approximate solutions of the Boltzmann equation for non-relativistic expanding spherical and ellipsoidal fireballs with Gaussian density profiles and self-similar velocity fields.

## Key numbers

- The spherical fireball example uses 400 particles with mass 1 GeV, cross section 40 mb, initial Gaussian radius parameter R = 7 fm, and initial temperature T0 = 0.130 GeV.
- The ellipsoidal fireball example uses initial Gaussian radius parameters X1 = X2 = 7 fm, X3 = 0.7 fm, and initial temperature T0 = 0.300 GeV, with spectra evaluated at t = 8 fm/c.
- The Cooper-Frye comparison for the ellipsoidal case uses the isotherm at T(t) = 0.063 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the continuous emission method of Grassi, Hama and Kodama (1995, 1996) by grounding the escape probability approach in the Boltzmann equation rather than relying on the interacting component approximated by local equilibrium. The method addresses known problems with the Cooper-Frye prescription (Cooper and Frye, 1974), particularly the artificial discontinuities from non-space-like freeze-out hypersurfaces noted by Sinyukov (1989) and others. The exact spherical solution of the Boltzmann equation, previously found by Csizmadia, Csorgo and Lukacs (1998), serves as a test case showing that the emission function is smooth rather than sharp, and the results enable more realistic treatment of dissipative effects in hydrodynamic modeling of SPS and RHIC data.
