# Probing nucleons with photons at the quark level

> The description of electromagnetic interactions with hadrons from the quark level requires knowledge of the underlying quark-gluon ingredients. I discuss some properties of the quark-photon vertex and quark Compton vertex, along with the role of electromagnetic gauge invariance and vector-meson dominance. A simple parametrization for the quark-photon vertex is given.

## Key claim

The quark-photon vertex's transverse dressing functions exhibit only a weak dependence on the angular variables in the spacelike region, allowing a simple multipole parametrization in the symmetric variable \(\mathcal{S}_0\) that is valid for crude modeling purposes, but only within the spacelike domain where \(\mathcal{S}_0 > 0\) and \(a^2+s^2 < 1\).

## Method

The dressed quark-photon vertex was separated into a gauge part (Ball-Chiu vertex) determined by the quark propagator and a transverse part expanded in an eight-element tensor basis. The transverse dressing functions \(f_{1\dots 8}\) were obtained from a numerical solution of the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in rainbow-ladder truncation. Their dependence on the three Lorentz invariants was re-expressed in terms of a symmetric variable \(\mathcal{S}_0\) and two angular variables \(a\) and \(s\) that form the Mandelstam plane, and the numerical results were fitted to simple multipole forms.

## Key numbers

* The scale parameter \(\Lambda = 0.65\) GeV yields the closest simultaneous description of all eight transverse dressing functions in the multipole parametrization.
* The dimensionless coefficients \(c_j\) for the transverse dressing functions are: \(c_1 = -1.0\), \(c_2 = 0.15\), \(c_3 = 0\), \(c_4 = 1.0\), \(c_5 = -1.3\), \(c_6 = -0.3\), \(c_7 = 0.5\), \(c_8 = -0.3\), with corresponding exponents \(n_j\) of 2, 5, 1, 2, 3, 4, 4, and 3 respectively.
* The \(f_3\) component, which encodes the quark anomalous magnetic moment, is practically zero in rainbow-ladder truncation.
* Nucleon magnetic moments calculated from the three-body Faddeev equation are generated by the Ball-Chiu vertex alone, with the transverse part contributing almost nothing.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to QCD, specifically the rainbow-ladder truncation that has been used to describe pseudoscalar and vector-meson spectra, nucleon and \(\Delta\)-baryon observables (Eichmann 2009, 2011; Sanchis-Alepuz 2013), and form factors. The parametrization of the quark-photon vertex provides a practical tool for calculating nucleon form factors and polarizabilities, and the discussion of the quark Compton vertex opens the way to studying two-photon processes such as nucleon polarizabilities and the light-by-light contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment. The work also highlights the need for interactions beyond rainbow-ladder to describe timelike properties of hadrons, such as the \(\rho \to \pi\pi\) decay width.
