# Universal Properties of the Four-Boson System in Two Dimensions

> We consider the nonrelativistic four-boson system in two dimensions interacting via a short-range attractive potential. For a weakly attractive potential with one shallow two-body bound state with binding energy B_2, the binding energies B_N of shallow N-body bound states are universal and thus do not depend on the details of the interaction potential. We compute the four-body binding energies in an effective quantum mechanics approach. There are exactly two bound states: the ground state with B_4^(0)=197.3(1)B_2 and one excited state with B_4^(1)=25.5(1)B_2. We compare our results to recent predictions for N-body bound states with large N>>1.

## Key claim
The four-boson system in two dimensions with a short-range attractive potential and one shallow two-body bound state has exactly two universal bound states, with binding energies that are independent of the details of the interaction potential, provided the two-body binding energy is sufficiently small compared to the scale set by the range of the potential.

## Method
The authors used an effective quantum mechanics approach, replacing the finite-range interaction with a zero-range delta-function potential in momentum space. They solved the Yakubovsky equations, which are a generalization of the Faddeev equations for the four-body system, in two spatial dimensions by discretizing the coupled integral equations and finding eigenvalues corresponding to bound state energies.

## Key numbers
- Ground state four-body binding energy: \(B_4^{(0)} = 197.3(1) \, B_2\)
- Excited state four-body binding energy: \(B_4^{(1)} = 25.5(1) \, B_2\)
- Ratio of ground state four-body to three-body binding energy: \(B_4^{(0)}/B_3^{(0)} \approx 11.94\)
- Asymptotic large-\(N\) prediction for ratio of successive binding energies: \(B_N/B_{N-1} \approx 8.567\) (from Hammer (2004))
- Asymptotic large-\(N\) prediction for ratio of successive droplet sizes: \(R_N/R_{N-1} \approx 0.3417\) (from Hammer (2004))

## Context
This work extends the universal few-body results for two-dimensional bosons from the three-body system, where Bruch and Tjon (1979) first calculated binding energies, to the four-body system. The results approach the asymptotic large-\(N\) predictions of Hammer (2004) for self-bound boson droplets, with the four-body ratio \(B_4^{(0)}/B_3^{(0)} = 11.94\) being closer to the asymptotic value of 8.567 than the three-body ratio \(B_3^{(0)}/B_2 = 16.52\). The calculations enable future tests of universality at larger \(N\) using Monte Carlo methods, and motivate experimental creation of bosonic clusters in anisotropic atom traps.
