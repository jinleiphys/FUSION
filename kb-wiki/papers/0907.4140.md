# Elliptic Flow and Dissipation in Heavy-Ion Collisions at E_{lab} = (1--160)A GeV

> Elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at incident energies $E_{lab}\simeq$ (1--160)A GeV is analyzed within the model of 3-fluid dynamics (3FD). We show that a simple correction factor, taking into account dissipative affects, allows us to adjust the 3FD results to experimental data. This single-parameter fit results in a good reproduction of the elliptic flow as a function of the incident energy, centrality of the collision and rapidity. The experimental scaling of pion eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow versus charged-hadron-multiplicity density per unit transverse area turns out to be also reasonably described. Proceeding from values of the Knudsen number, deduced from this fit, we estimate the upper limit the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio as $Œ∑/s \sim 1-2$ at the SPS incident energies. This value is of the order of minimal $Œ∑/s$ observed in water and liquid nitrogen.

## Context

This work extends earlier 3FD studies (Ivanov et al., 2005‚Äì2007) that found the model overestimates elliptic flow at SPS energies regardless of equation of state stiffness or stopping power, and complements hybrid hydrocascade studies (Bleicher, 2009) that showed afterburner effects reduce but do not fully resolve the discrepancy. The dissipative correction approach follows the method suggested by Dumitru (2007) and Ollitrault (2005, 2008), and the scaling analysis follows the experimental scaling of eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow versus charged-hadron-multiplicity density per unit transverse area proposed by Voloshin (2000, 2008). The results enable future comparisons with data from FAIR and NICA facilities and provide an upper limit for Œ∑/s that can be refined by separating hydrodynamic and afterburner contributions.
