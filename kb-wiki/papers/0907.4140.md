# Elliptic Flow and Dissipation in Heavy-Ion Collisions at E_{lab} = (1--160)A GeV

> Elliptic flow in heavy-ion collisions at incident energies $E_{lab}\simeq$ (1--160)A GeV is analyzed within the model of 3-fluid dynamics (3FD). We show that a simple correction factor, taking into account dissipative affects, allows us to adjust the 3FD results to experimental data. This single-parameter fit results in a good reproduction of the elliptic flow as a function of the incident energy, centrality of the collision and rapidity. The experimental scaling of pion eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow versus charged-hadron-multiplicity density per unit transverse area turns out to be also reasonably described. Proceeding from values of the Knudsen number, deduced from this fit, we estimate the upper limit the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio as $η/s \sim 1-2$ at the SPS incident energies. This value is of the order of minimal $η/s$ observed in water and liquid nitrogen.

# Elliptic Flow and Dissipation in Heavy-Ion Collisions at E_lab = (1–160)A GeV

## Key claim

The 3-fluid dynamics (3FD) model overestimates elliptic flow data at top AGS and SPS energies, but a single-parameter dissipative correction factor based on the Knudsen number can bring the calculations into good agreement with experimental data across incident energies, centralities, and rapidities. The resulting upper limit estimate of the shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio is η/s ∼ 1–2 at SPS energies, which is of the order of minimal values observed in water and liquid nitrogen, but this value should be considered an upper limit because it includes afterburner contributions.

## Method

The authors used the 3-fluid dynamics (3FD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions in the energy range E_lab = (1–160)A GeV, employing a purely hadronic equation of state with incompressibility K = 190 MeV and a refitted inter-fluid friction coefficient β_h = 0.5. To account for dissipative effects that the ideal hydrodynamic 3FD model misses, they applied an empirical reduction formula v_2 = v_2^hydro / (1 + Kn/Kn_0) from Ollitrault (2005), where the Knudsen number Kn is expressed in terms of the charged-hadron rapidity density, transport cross section, sound velocity, and transverse overlap area, with a single fitting parameter c_s σ_tr.

## Key numbers

- The single fitting parameter for the dissipative correction is c_s σ_tr = 2.3 mb, yielding Kn ∼ 0.7 at midrapidity for midcentral collisions.
- The transport cross section is estimated as σ_tr ∼ 6 mb for top AGS and SPS energies, using c_s^2 ∼ 0.15.
- The shear viscosity coefficient is estimated as η ≃ (1.4–1.7) fm^−3 for T = (0.1–0.15) GeV.
- The entropy density is estimated as s ≃ (0.6–0.7) fm^−3 at n = 0.2 fm^−3 and T = (0.1–0.15) GeV, and s ≃ (0.8–1.1) fm^−3 at n = 0.4 fm^−3.
- The shear viscosity-to-entropy ratio is estimated as η/s ∼ 1–2 at SPS incident energies.
- The 3FD calculations were performed at impact parameter b = 6 fm for Au+Au collisions at SIS and AGS energies, and b = 5.6 fm for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies.
- The freeze-out energy density used for the hydrodynamic limit is ε_frz = 0.2 GeV/fm^3.

## Context

This work extends earlier 3FD studies (Ivanov et al., 2005–2007) that found the model overestimates elliptic flow at SPS energies regardless of equation of state stiffness or stopping power, and complements hybrid hydrocascade studies (Bleicher, 2009) that showed afterburner effects reduce but do not fully resolve the discrepancy. The dissipative correction approach follows the method suggested by Dumitru (2007) and Ollitrault (2005, 2008), and the scaling analysis follows the experimental scaling of eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow versus charged-hadron-multiplicity density per unit transverse area proposed by Voloshin (2000, 2008). The results enable future comparisons with data from FAIR and NICA facilities and provide an upper limit for η/s that can be refined by separating hydrodynamic and afterburner contributions.
