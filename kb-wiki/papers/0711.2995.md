# How can we explore the onset of deconfinement by experiment?

> There is little doubt that Quantumchromodynamics (QCD) is the theory which describes strong interaction physics. Lattice gauge simulations of QCD predict that in the $μ,T$ plane there is a line where a transition from confined hadronic matter to deconfined quarks takes place. The transition is either a cross over (at low $μ$) or of first order (at high $μ$). It is the goal of the present and future heavy ion experiment at RHIC and FAIR to study this phase transition at different locations in the $μ,T$ plane and to explore the properties of the deconfined phase. It is the purpose of this contribution to discuss some of the observables which are considered as useful for this purpose.

## Key claim

The paper argues that several proposed experimental observables for detecting the deconfined phase transition in heavy-ion collisions are less reliable than often claimed: dileptons from ρ meson decays are sensitive only to low-density regions, the excitation function of directed flow can be mimicked by hadronic effects without a phase transition, and charmed hadrons at FAIR energies cannot be used until the elementary production cross sections are measured.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD (Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics) transport model to simulate heavy-ion collisions across beam energies from 2 AGeV (SIS) to 200 AGeV (RHIC), tracking the time evolution of density and the fate of ρ mesons. They compared these transport results with hydrodynamical calculations that assume either a hadronic equation of state or one with a quark-gluon plasma phase transition, focusing on the directed flow excitation function. They also compiled world data on charm production cross sections in elementary proton-nucleon and pion-nucleon reactions to assess the feasibility of using charmed hadrons as probes.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out energy density of 1.1 GeV/fm³ for finite chemical potentials, well below the lattice QCD transition energy density.
- For ρ mesons at beam energies from 2 AGeV to 30 AGeV, the average density at the disintegration point is lower at 30 AGeV than at 2 AGeV, due to higher particle multiplicity.
- The threshold for the lowest charm production channel (NN → D⁻(D̅⁰)Λ_c N) is √s_thres = 5.073 (5.069) GeV, corresponding to a beam energy of about 30 AGeV (√s = 7.74 GeV for a nucleon pair).
- The branching ratio for Λ_c decay into electrons is 4.5%, while for D⁻ mesons it is 17.2%.
- No experimental data points exist for the dominant charm production channel NN → D⁻(D̅⁰)Λ_c N at FAIR energies (√s ≈ 7 GeV); only J/ψ production has been measured in that energy range.

## Context

This contribution critically reviews observables proposed for studying the QCD phase transition at RHIC and FAIR, extending earlier work by Rapp (1997) on vector mesons in dense matter and by Korpa (1998) on kaon mass shifts. The authors directly challenge the interpretation of directed flow minima as a signature of deconfinement, as suggested by Rischke (1995) and Schu (1996), showing that the UrQMD model produces a similar excitation function from purely hadronic mechanisms. The paper identifies the lack of measured elementary charm production cross sections as the main obstacle to using charmed hadrons as probes at FAIR, a limitation that must be resolved before the approach used successfully for strange hadrons at SIS energies can be extended.
