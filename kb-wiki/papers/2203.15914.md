# Ab initio prediction of the $^4{\rm He}(d,γ)\,^6\rm Li$ big bang radiative capture

> The rate at which helium ($^4$He) and deuterium ($d$) fuse together to produce lithium-6 ($^6$Li) and a $γ$ ray, $^4$He$(d,γ)^6$Li, is a critical puzzle piece in resolving the roughly three orders of magnitude discrepancy between big bang predictions and astronomical observations for the primordial abundance of $^6$Li. The accurate determination of this radiative capture rate requires the quantitative and predictive description of the fusion probability across the big bang energy window ($30$ keV $\lesssim E\lesssim 400$ keV), where measurements are hindered by low counting rates. We present first-principles (or, ab initio) predictions of the $^4$He$(d,γ)^6$Li astrophysical S-factor using validated nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions derived within the framework of chiral effective field theory. By employing the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum to describe $^4{\rm He}$-$d$ scattering dynamics and bound $^6\rm Li$ product on an equal footing, we accurately and consistently determine the contributions of the main electromagnetic transitions driving the radiative capture process. Our results reveal an enhancement of the capture probability below 100 keV owing to previously neglected magnetic dipole (M1) transitions and reduce by an average factor of 7 the uncertainty of the thermonuclear capture rate between $0.002$ and $2$ GK.

## Key claim

The authors present the first fully *ab initio* prediction of the $^4{\rm He}(d,\gamma)\,^6\rm Li$ astrophysical S-factor that treats bound and scattering states consistently within the same theoretical framework, finding that previously neglected magnetic dipole (M1) transitions enhance the capture probability below 100 keV, contrary to earlier assumptions that M1 contributions were negligible.

## Method

The authors employed the ab initio no-core shell model with continuum (NCSMC) to describe $^4{\rm He}$-$d$ scattering dynamics and the bound $^6\rm Li$ product on an equal footing, starting from nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions derived from chiral effective field theory. The interactions were softened using the similarity renormalization group (SRG) transformation in three-body space with a momentum resolution scale of $\lambda=2$ fm$^{-1}$, and the eigenstates were obtained using a basis of many-body harmonic oscillator wavefunctions with frequency $\hbar \Omega=20$ MeV and a maximum number $N_{\rm max}=11$ of particle excitation quanta. The electromagnetic transition operators (E1, E2, M1) were evaluated microscopically rather than using phenomenological prescriptions.

## Key numbers

- The big bang energy window for the $^4{\rm He}(d,\gamma)\,^6\rm Li$ reaction is 30 keV $\lesssim E\lesssim 400$ keV.
- The uncertainty of the predicted thermonuclear capture rate is reduced by an average factor of 7 compared to previous evaluations (NACRE II) between 0.002 and 2 GK.
- The $^6\rm Li$ ground state is overbound by approximately 310 keV when three-nucleon forces are included (NN+3N$_{\rm loc}$).
- The predicted $^6\rm Li$ ground-state binding energy after phenomenological adjustment (NN+3N$_{\rm loc}$-pheno) is -1.474 MeV, matching the experimental value of -1.4743 MeV.
- The predicted $^6\rm Li$ magnetic moment is 0.84 $\mu_N$ (NN+3N$_{\rm loc}$-pheno), compared to the experimental value of 0.8220473 $\mu_N$.
- The asymptotic normalization constant $\mathcal{C}_0$ is predicted as 2.62(4) fm$^{-1/2}$ (NN+3N$_{\rm loc}$-pheno), compared to the inferred value of 2.28(7) fm$^{-1/2}$ from phase shift analysis.
- The $3^+$ resonance peak is measured by Mohr et al. around $E_{3^+}=0.71$ MeV, and the $2^+$ resonance around $E_{2^+}=2.84$ MeV by Robertson et al.

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical efforts on the $^4{\rm He}(d,\gamma)\,^6\rm Li$ reaction, which were carried out in two-body potential models (Baye et al. 1990, Jaus 1993, Mukhamedzhanov et al. 1995, 2011, 2016, Gnech et al. 2017) or three-body $^4\rm He$+$p$+$n$ models (Rupak et al. 1995, Typel et al. 2016, Bystritsky and Tursunov 2018) with an inert $^4\rm He$ core, and the earlier ab initio treatment by Nollett et al. (2001) that still relied on a phenomenological description of $^4$He-$d$ scattering. The present results contradict the common assumption that M1 transitions are negligible and that E1 transitions dominate below 100 keV, instead finding that M1 transitions drive the low-energy enhancement while E1 transitions remain negligible. The predicted thermonuclear reaction rate is systematically lower than the NACRE II evaluation (Xu et al. 2013) and agrees well with the rate reported by the LUNA collaboration (2017).
