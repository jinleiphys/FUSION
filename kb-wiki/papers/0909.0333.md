# About the sign ambiguity in the evaluation of Grand Canonical traces for quasi-particle statistical density operators

> A simple and general prescription for evaluating unambiguously the sign of the grand-canonical trace of quasi-particle statistical density operators (the so-called sign ambiguity in taking the square root of determinants) is given. Sign ambiguities of this kind appear in the evaluation of the grand-canonical partition function projected to good quantum numbers (angular momentum, parity and particle number) in the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov approximation at finite temperature, since traces are usually expressed as the square root of determinants. A comparison is made with the numerical continuity method.

## Key claim

The sign ambiguity in the grand-canonical trace of quasi-particle statistical density operators can be removed analytically by factorizing the operator into known elementary factors and evaluating each vacuum contribution separately, without relying on a numerical continuity argument that can fail in cases where the phase has a vertical slope.

## Method

The author expresses the statistical density operator as a product of elementary operators of the form exp(½ γ̄ S γ), each with a known matrix S. Using the Lie algebra property that the product of such operators remains of the same form, the grand-canonical trace is expressed in terms of the eigenvalues of S (Eq. 12) when S is known. For the general case where only the product matrix W is known, the trace is reconstructed from the vacuum contributions of each factor using Eq. (35), which involves determinants of submatrices of W and products of eigenvalues of derived matrices D and C, with the square-root sign fixed by Eq. (12) applied to each factor individually.

## Key numbers

- Numerical tests used 12×12 antisymmetric random matrices with elements uniformly distributed in the interval −a to a, with a ≈ 3.
- The numerical continuity argument with N_steps = 200 showed a phase discontinuity, while N_steps = 800 restored continuity for the same test case.
- The maximum phase change threshold was kept fixed at δφ_max = 0.5 for both step sizes.
- Even 400 steps failed to reproduce the continuity of the phase in the presence of a vertical slope.

## Context

This work addresses the sign ambiguity problem that arises in finite-temperature Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations of projected partition functions, previously handled by the continuity argument of the author's earlier work (Puddu, ref. [3]) and by Grassmann algebra methods (ref. [4], [5]) that left the vacuum contribution unresolved. The method enables unambiguous evaluation of grand-canonical partition functions projected to good angular momentum, parity, and particle number, particularly for triaxial shapes at finite temperature where full three-dimensional angular momentum projection is needed.
