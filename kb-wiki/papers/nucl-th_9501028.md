# Pseudo-Spin Symmetry and its Applications

> The pseudo-spin symmetry is reviewed. A mapping that produces the separation of the total angular momentum into pseudo-orbital and pseudo-spin degrees of freedom is discussed, together with the analytic transformations that take us from the normal parity space to the eigenstates of a pseudo-oscillator with one quanta less. The many-particle version of the unitary transformation to the pseudo-SU(3) space is established. As an example, these symmetries are used to describe the double beta decay phenomenon in heavy deformed nuclei.

## Key claim

The pseudo-spin symmetry provides a framework for describing heavy deformed nuclei by separating single-particle Hilbert space into pseudo-orbital and pseudo-spin degrees of freedom, and the paper demonstrates that this formalism can yield double beta decay half-life estimates that are lower bounds consistent with experimental ranges, though the nuclear matrix elements could be reduced by including additional interactions like pairing.

## Method

The authors review the unitary transformation of Bohr, Mottelson, and Hamamoto (BHM) that acts on the angular-spin parts of wave functions, and present an explicit unitary operator mapping normal parity harmonic oscillator eigenstates onto pseudo-oscillator eigenstates with one quantum less. They extend this to a many-particle unitary transformation to the pseudo-SU(3) space, and apply the formalism to calculate the two-neutrino double beta decay matrix elements for 150Nd and 238U using a Hamiltonian that includes spherical Nilsson terms, quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, and rotational scalar operators built from SU(3) generators.

## Key numbers

- For the transition 150Nd → 150Sm: the Gamow-Teller matrix element squared is 1.31, the energy denominator is 12.2 MeV, the theoretical half-life is 6.0 × 10^18 years, and the experimental half-life range is 9–17 × 10^18 years.
- For the transition 238U → 238Pu: the Gamow-Teller matrix element squared is 1.51, the energy denominator is 16.8 MeV, the theoretical half-life is 1.4 × 10^21 years, and the experimental half-life is 2 × 10^21 years.
- The Coulomb energy difference between protons and neutrons is given by Δ_C = (0.70 / A^{1/3}) [2Z + 1 - 0.76 ((Z+1)^{4/3} - Z^{4/3})] MeV.

## Context

This work extends the pseudo-spin symmetry formalism originally introduced by Hecht and Adler (1969) and Arima, Harvey, and Shimizu (1969), and the unitary transformation approach of Bohr, Hamamoto, and Mottelson (1982). The paper provides formal support for the pseudo-SU(3) coupling scheme used in earlier calculations by Draayer and Weeks (1984) and Castanos, Draayer, and Leschber (1988), and demonstrates its applicability to double beta decay in heavy deformed nuclei, enabling future calculations that could include pairing interactions to refine the half-life estimates.
