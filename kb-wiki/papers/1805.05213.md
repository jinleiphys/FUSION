# Hybrid Fluid Models from Mutual Effective Metric Couplings

> Motivated by a semi-holographic approach to the dynamics of quark-gluon plasma which combines holographic and perturbative descriptions of a strongly coupled infrared and a more weakly coupled ultraviolet sector, we construct a hybrid two-fluid model where interactions between its two sectors are encoded by their effective metric backgrounds, which are determined mutually by their energy-momentum tensors. We derive the most general consistent ultralocal interactions such that the full system has a total conserved energy-momentum tensor in flat Minkowski space and study its consequences in and near thermal equilibrium by working out its phase structure and its hydrodynamic modes.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a hybrid two-fluid model where interactions between a strongly coupled infrared sector and a weakly coupled ultraviolet sector are encoded by mutual effective metric couplings, but the resulting composite system only achieves full thermal equilibrium between the two sectors when additional couplings beyond those between energy-momentum tensors are included, as spatially homogeneous density perturbations of the individual subsystems are not attenuated by the metric coupling alone.

## Method

The authors derive the most general consistent ultralocal interactions between two conformal subsystems such that the full system has a total conserved energy-momentum tensor in flat Minkowski space, using a democratic coupling scheme where each subsystem's effective metric depends on the energy-momentum tensor of the other. They then study the equilibrium phase structure by solving the self-consistent metric equations for homogeneous and isotropic configurations, and analyze near-equilibrium dynamics by linearizing first-order conformal fluid equations for each subsystem around the equilibrium solution.

## Key numbers

* The specific heat critical exponent at the second-order endpoint separating crossover from first-order transition is $\alpha = 2/3$.
* Causality requires the tensorial coupling constant $\ga > 0$ and the ratio $r \equiv -\gap/\ga > 1$ to ensure effective lightcone speeds do not exceed the speed of light at arbitrary energy scales.
* In the shear sector, the overall viscosity of the composite system decreases with increasing coupling between the subsystems.
* In the sound sector, both hydrodynamic modes have attenuation that vanishes with the square of momentum, meaning spatially homogeneous density perturbations of the individual subsystems are not attenuated.

## Context

This work extends the semi-holographic framework proposed by Iancu (2014) and Mukhopadhyay (2015), which combined holographic descriptions of strongly coupled sectors with perturbative descriptions of weakly coupled sectors, by introducing a symmetric "democratic" coupling between the two subsystems' energy-momentum tensors via mutual effective metrics as initiated by Banerjee (2017). The results enable systematic construction of two-fluid models for quark-gluon plasma dynamics where both strongly coupled and weakly coupled modes coexist, but also show that metric couplings alone are insufficient for complete thermalization, consistent with the findings of Ecker (2018) that additional scalar couplings are needed.
