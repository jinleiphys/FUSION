# Stable bound states of $N$'s, $Î›$'s, and $Îž$'s

> We review our recent work about the stability of strange few-body systems containing $N$'s, $Î›$'s, and $Îž$'s. We make use of local central Yukawa-type Malfliet-Tjon interactions reproducing the low-energy parameters and phase shifts of the nucleon-nucleon system and the latest updates of the hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon ESC08c Nijmegen potentials. We solve the three- and four-body bound-state problems by means of Faddeev equations and a generalized Gaussian variational method, respectively. The hypertriton, $Î›np$ $(I)J^P=(1/2)1/2^+$, is bound by 144 keV; the recently discussed $Î›nn$ $(I)J^P=(1/2)1/2^+$ system is unbound, as well as the $Î›Î›nn$ $(I)J^P=(1)0^+$system, being just above threshold. Our results indicate that the $ÎžNN$, $ÎžÎžN$ and $ÎžÎžNN$ systems with maximal isospin might be bound.

## Key claim

The paper claims that three- and four-body systems composed of nucleons and Xi hyperons in maximal isospin channels (I=2 for four-body, I=3/2 for three-body) might be bound and stable under the strong interaction, but this conclusion is conditional on the specific ESC08c Nijmegen potentials used and the assumption that the strong decay channels are forbidden by isospin conservation.

## Method

The authors solved the three-body bound-state problem using Faddeev equations in momentum space with S-wave interactions, and the four-body bound-state problem using a generalized Gaussian variational method in coordinate space. Two-body amplitudes for all subsystems (NN, Lambda N, Xi N, Xi Xi, Lambda Lambda) were constructed by fitting local central Yukawa-type Malfliet-Tjon potentials to reproduce the low-energy parameters and phase shifts from the latest ESC08c Nijmegen potentials, with the NN interaction taken from standard Malfliet-Tjon models.

## Key numbers

- The hypertriton (Lambda np) with (I)J^P = (1/2)1/2^+ is bound by 144 keV
- The Lambda nn system with (I)J^P = (1/2)1/2^+ is unbound
- The Lambda Lambda nn system with (I)J^P = (1)0^+ is unbound, being just above threshold
- The Xi NN system with (I)J^P = (3/2)1/2^+ is bound by 1.3 MeV below the threshold 2m_N + m_Xi - B_2, where B_2 is the binding energy of the D* Xi N state
- The Xi NN system with (I)J^P = (1/2)3/2^+ is bound by 17.2 MeV below the Xi NN threshold
- The Xi Xi N system with (I)J^P = (3/2)1/2^+ is bound by 2.9 MeV below the threshold 2m_Xi + m_N - B_2
- The Xi N D* bound state has a binding energy of 1.6 MeV
- The deuteron is bound by 2.225 MeV, the triton by 8.480 MeV, and the alpha particle by 28.295 MeV
- The Lambda N scattering lengths are -2.62 fm for (1/2,0) and -1.72 fm for (1/2,1)
- The Xi N scattering lengths are -5.357 fm for (0,1), 0.579 fm for (1,0), and 4.911 fm for (1,1)
- The Xi Xi scattering lengths are 0.53 fm for (0,1) and -7.25 fm for (1,0)
- The Lambda Lambda scattering length is -0.853 fm with effective range 5.126 fm

## Context

This work extends previous studies of strange few-body systems by Garcilazo, Valcarce, and Vijande, using the most recent updates of the ESC08c Nijmegen potentials that account for the KISO and NAGARA events in the strangeness -2 sector. It complements earlier hypernuclei studies by Yamada (2010), Hiyama (2008), and Nemura (2003) by providing exact calculations for the simplest three- and four-body systems. The results enable experimental searches for stable Xi hypernuclei, particularly at J-PARC where the E07 experiment is expected to record approximately 10^4 Xi- stopping events, and provide benchmarks for lattice QCD calculations of multibaryon systems.
