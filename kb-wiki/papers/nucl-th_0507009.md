# Non-locality in the nucleon-nucleon interaction and nuclear matter saturation

> We study the possible relationship between the saturation properties of nuclear matter and the inclusion of non-locality in the nucleon-nucleon interaction. To this purpose we compute the saturation curve of nuclear matter within the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone theory using a recently proposed realistic non-local potential, and compare it with the corresponding curves obtained with a purely local realistic interaction (Argonne v$_{18}$) and the most recent version of the one-boson exchange potential (CD Bonn). We find that the inclusion of non-locality in the two-nucleon bare interaction strongly affects saturation, but it is unable to provide a consistent description of few-body nuclear systems and nuclear matter.

## Key claim

The inclusion of non-locality in the two-nucleon bare interaction strongly affects nuclear matter saturation, but it is unable to provide a consistent description of few-body nuclear systems and nuclear matter simultaneously; any increase in non-locality improves the fitting of binding for few-body systems but shifts the saturation point to higher density and binding energy, away from the empirical saturation point.

## Method

The authors computed the saturation curve of symmetric nuclear matter within the Bethe-Brueckner-Goldstone (BBG) theory, extended up to three hole-line contributions, using the non-local Doleschall potential (NL) in the \(^1S_0\) channel only (NL1) and in both the \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channels (NL2). They compared these results with the saturation curves obtained from the purely local Argonne v\(_{18}\) potential and the non-local one-boson exchange CD Bonn potential, using the continuous choice for the single-particle potential.

## Key numbers

- For the NL2 potential (non-locality in both \(^1S_0\) and \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) channels), the energy per particle at saturation goes down to about -26 MeV, with an abnormally large saturation density.
- Three hole-line contributions for the NL1 case remain within 2-3.5% of the two hole-line results; for NL2 they remain within 2.5-4%.
- The three hole-line contributions for the non-local potentials are positive, contrary to the v\(_{18}\) case where in the region \(1.4 < k_F < 2\) fm\(^{-1}\) they tend to make the equation of state more attractive.
- The CD Bonn potential at the Brueckner level gives saturation density and energy substantially larger than for v\(_{18}\); three hole-line contributions add repulsion but the binding energy remains too large relative to the empirical value.

## Context

This work extends the long history of studying phase-shift equivalent NN interactions and their predictions of nuclear matter saturation properties, following earlier studies by Coester and Sauer on unitarily equivalent interactions, and more recent work by Engvik (1997) and Machleidt (1996, 1998) on modern NN potentials including CD Bonn and Nijmegen-I. The results contradict the expectation that non-locality might simulate the strong repulsion at short distance and reduce the need for three-body forces; instead, the Doleschall potential, which accurately describes three-nucleon systems without three-body forces (Doleschall 2000, 2003, 2004), produces nuclear matter saturation points that require even larger three-body forces than local potentials, highlighting an inconsistency between few-body and many-body descriptions.
