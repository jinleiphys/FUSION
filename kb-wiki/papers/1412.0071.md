# Nucleon-nucleon scattering with the Complex Scaling Method and realistic interactions

> We demonstrate the validity of the complex scaling method for realistic strong, non-local, nucleon- nucleon interactions by comparing the deuteron bound state and nucleon-nucleon scattering phase shifts with results from other high-precision methods. This opens a pathway for the simultaneous ab initio solutions of the nuclear bound and scattering problems within a unified framework.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Complex Scaling Method (CSM) works for realistic, non-local nucleon-nucleon interactions (not just the local or phenomenological potentials previously assumed), by showing that the deuteron bound state energy remains invariant under complex rotation and that computed phase shifts converge rapidly with the rotation angle, thereby opening a path to unify bound-state and scattering calculations in a single framework.

## Method

The authors apply the Complex Scaling Method to the proton-neutron system by rotating coordinates and momenta as \(r \to re^{i\theta}\) and \(p \to pe^{-i\theta}\), then diagonalizing the resulting non-Hermitian Hamiltonian in a Harmonic Oscillator (HO) basis. For non-local realistic interactions (JISP16, N\(^3\)LO, N\(^2\)LO\(_{opt}\)), they compute the complex-scaled matrix elements by shifting the transformation onto the HO basis states, making the HO length parameter complex (\(b \to be^{i\theta}\)). Scattering phase shifts for uncoupled channels are extracted from the complex-scaled continuum level density (CLD) using the extended completeness relation.

## Key numbers

- Deuteron ground state energy is invariant under complex rotation angles from 0.1 rad to 0.3 rad for JISP16 and N\(^2\)LO\(_{opt}\) interactions at \(b = 1.4399\) fm (\(\hbar\omega = 40\) MeV).
- For phase shifts in the \(^3P_1\), \(^3D_2\), \(^3F_3\), and \(^3G_4\) channels with JISP16, results become practically indistinguishable from exact solutions already at \(\theta = 0.2\) rad.
- For the \(^3P_0\) channel with N\(^3\)LO SRG (\(\lambda = 2.0\) fm\(^{-1}\)) and N\(^2\)LO\(_{opt}\), phase shifts show fast convergence with \(\theta = 0.2\) rad using \(N = 35\) HO basis states.
- HO basis parameters used: \(b = 1.4399\) fm (\(\hbar\omega = 40\) MeV) for JISP16, \(b = 1.5178\) fm (\(\hbar\omega = 36\) MeV) for N\(^3\)LO, and \(b = 1.6099\) fm (\(\hbar\omega = 32\) MeV) for N\(^2\)LO\(_{opt}\).

## Context

This work extends the Complex Scaling Method, previously applied in Quantum Chemistry and to phenomenological local potentials (e.g., Lazauskas (2005) for S-wave interactions), to modern realistic non-local interactions including JISP16 (Shirokov et al.) and chiral potentials (Entem-Machleidt N\(^3\)LO, N\(^2\)LO\(_{opt}\)). It enables future application of CSM to heavier nuclear systems by combining it with existing many-body bound-state techniques such as the No-Core Shell Model (Barrett et al., 2013), Coupled Cluster theory, and In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group, providing a unified framework for structure and scattering observables without imposing scattering boundary conditions.
