# Nuclear magnetization distribution effect in molecules: Ra$^+$ and RaF hyperfine structure

> Recently the first laser spectroscopy measurement of the radioactive RaF molecule has been reported by Ruiz \textit{et al.} [Nature \textbf{581}, 396 (2020)]. This and similar molecules are considered to search for the New Physics effects. The radium nucleus is of interest as it is octupole-deformed and has close levels of opposite parity. The preparation of such experiments can be simplified if there are reliable theoretical predictions. It is shown that the accurate prediction of the hyperfine structure of the RaF molecule requires to take into account the finite magnetization distribution inside the radium nucleus. For atoms, this effect is known as the Bohr-Weisskopf (BW) effect. Its magnitude depends on the model of the nuclear magnetization distribution which is usually not well known. We show that it is possible to express the nuclear magnetization distribution contribution to the hyperfine structure constant in terms of one magnetization distribution dependent parameter: BW matrix element for $1s$-state of the corresponding hydrogen-like ion. This parameter can be extracted from the accurate experimental and theoretical electronic structure data for an ion, atom, or molecule without the explicit treatment of any nuclear magnetization distribution model. This approach can be applied to predict the hyperfine structure of atoms and \textit{molecules} and allows one to separate the nuclear and electronic correlation problems. It is employed to calculate the finite nuclear magnetization distribution contribution to the hyperfine structure of the $^{225}$Ra$^+$ cation and $^{225}$RaF molecule. For the ground state of the $^{225}$RaF molecule, this contribution achieves 4\%.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the finite nuclear magnetization distribution (Bohr-Weisskopf) contribution to the hyperfine structure constant of any system containing a given heavy nucleus can be expressed in terms of a single nucleus-dependent parameter, the BW matrix element for the 1s-state of the corresponding hydrogen-like ion, which can be extracted from accurate experimental and theoretical data without assuming any specific nuclear magnetization distribution model.

## Method

The author develops a formalism that expresses the BW contribution to the hyperfine structure constant in terms of reduced occupation parameters \( \mathcal{P}_s \) and \( \mathcal{P}_p \) for \( s_{1/2} \) and \( p_{1/2} \) reference functions, combined with a single nuclear matrix element \( B_s \). These reduced occupation parameters are calculated from a 4-component relativistic coupled cluster CCSD(T) and CCSDT(Q) electronic structure calculation for the \( ^{225}\mathrm{Ra}^+ \) cation and the \( ^{225}\mathrm{RaF} \) molecule, using uncontracted gaussian-type basis sets and the Dirac-Coulomb Hamiltonian. The nuclear parameter \( B_s \) is then extracted by comparing the calculated point-nucleus hyperfine constant \( A^{(0)} \) for the ground state of \( ^{225}\mathrm{Ra}^+ \) with the experimental value, after including known QED and Breit corrections.

## Key numbers

- The BW contribution to the hyperfine structure constant for the ground 7s \( ^2\mathrm{S}_{1/2} \) state of \( ^{225}\mathrm{Ra}^+ \) is \( A^{\mathrm{BW}} = -1215\ \mathrm{MHz} \), which is about 4% of the total hyperfine constant.
- For the ground state of the \( ^{225}\mathrm{RaF} \) molecule, the finite nuclear magnetization distribution contribution achieves 4% of the hyperfine structure constant.
- The final predicted hyperfine constant for the ground state of \( ^{225}\mathrm{Ra}^+ \) is \( -27731\ \mathrm{MHz} \), compared to the experimental value of \( -27731(13)\ \mathrm{MHz} \).
- For the 7p \( ^2\mathrm{P}_{1/2} \) state of \( ^{225}\mathrm{Ra}^+ \), the BW contribution is \( A^{\mathrm{BW}} = -75\ \mathrm{MHz} \), yielding a final hyperfine constant of \( -5451\ \mathrm{MHz} \) (experiment: \( -5446.0(7)\ \mathrm{MHz} \)).
- The nuclear magnetic moment of \( ^{225}\mathrm{Ra} \) is \( \mu = -0.7338(15) \) (from direct atomic measurement by Arnold (1987)).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Shabaev (2001) for specific differences in highly charged ions to molecules, and generalizes the method of Ginges (2018) for extracting BW corrections in alkali-metal atoms. The paper enables reliable predictions of hyperfine structure for the RaF molecule, which is a candidate for laser cooling and for searches of T,P-violating effects such as the nuclear Schiff moment, previously calculated for \( ^{225}\mathrm{Ra} \) by Engel (2013). The method allows separation of nuclear and electronic correlation problems, so that the BW contribution extracted from atomic data can be applied to any molecular system containing the same nucleus.
