# Low energy onset of nuclear shadowing in photoabsorption

> The early onset of nuclear shadowing in photoabsorption at low photon energies has recently been interpreted as a possible signature of a decrease of the rho meson mass in nuclei. We show that one can understand this early onset within simple Glauber theory if one takes the negative real part of the rho N scattering amplitudes into account, corresponding to a higher effective mass of the rho meson in nuclear medium.

# Low energy onset of nuclear shadowing in photoabsorption

## Key claim
The early onset of nuclear shadowing in photoabsorption at photon energies above 1 GeV can be explained within simple Glauber theory by including the negative real part of the rho-nucleon scattering amplitude, which corresponds to a *higher* effective mass of the rho meson in nuclear medium, contrary to previous interpretations that suggested a decreased rho meson mass.

## Method
The authors applied Glauber multiple scattering theory to compute the total photon-nucleus cross section, expressing it in terms of the nuclear forward Compton amplitude via the optical theorem. They included contributions from vector meson intermediate states (rho, omega, phi) using vector dominance model relations, incorporated the real-to-imaginary ratio alpha_V of the vector meson-nucleon forward scattering amplitudes, and accounted for two-body nucleon correlations using a Bessel function parametrization with a correlation cutoff q_c = 780 MeV. The calculations used Woods-Saxon nuclear density distributions and compared two parametrizations for the rho-nucleon amplitude: one from Model I of Bauer (1978) and one from the dispersion theoretical analysis of Kondratyuk et al. (1998).

## Key numbers
- The correlation cutoff parameter used in the two-body correlation function is q_c = 780 MeV.
- For photon energies below 1.5 GeV (for carbon-12) and below 2 GeV (for lead-208), calculations with alpha_V = 0 produce antishadowing rather than shadowing.
- The rho meson photoproduction amplitude f_{gamma rho} is approximately 3 times larger than that of the omega and phi mesons.
- The effective rho meson mass in medium is given by m* = sqrt(m_rho^2 - 4 pi Re(f_{rho rho}) n_0), where the negative real part of f_{rho rho} yields a larger effective mass.

## Context
This work extends the Glauber-Gribov multiple scattering approach used by Bauer (1978) and Donnelly (1978) for high-energy shadowing, and directly addresses the interpretation by Muehlich et al. (1999) that the early onset of shadowing indicated a decreased rho meson mass in nuclei. The authors show that including the negative real part of the rho-nucleon amplitude, consistent with dispersion theoretical calculations by Eletsky and Ioffe (1997) and Kondratyuk et al. (1998), produces a positive mass shift and explains the data without invoking a lower rho mass. The result enables a consistent understanding of photoabsorption data on C, Al, Cu, Sn, and Pb from Bianchi et al. (1996) and Muehlich et al. (1998) in the 1 to 2.6 GeV energy range.
