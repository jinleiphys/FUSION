# Collective excitations in the Unitary Correlation Operator Method and relativistic QRPA studies of exotic nuclei

> The collective excitation phenomena in atomic nuclei are studied in two different formulations of the Random Phase Approximation (RPA): (i) RPA based on correlated realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions constructed within the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM), and (ii) relativistic RPA (RRPA) derived from effective Lagrangians with density-dependent meson-exchange interactions. The former includes the dominant interaction-induced short-range central and tensor correlations by means of an unitary transformation. It is shown that UCOM-RPA correlations induced by collective nuclear vibrations recover a part of the residual long-range correlations that are not explicitly included in the UCOM Hartree-Fock ground state. Both RPA models are employed in studies of the isoscalar monopole resonance (ISGMR) in closed-shell nuclei across the nuclide chart, with an emphasis on the sensitivity of its properties on the constraints for the range of the UCOM correlation functions. Within the Relativistic Quasiparticle RPA (RQRPA) based on Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model, the occurrence of pronounced low-lying dipole excitations is predicted in nuclei towards the proton drip-line. From the analysis of the transition densities and the structure of the RQRPA amplitudes, it is shown that these states correspond to the proton pygmy dipole resonance.

## Key claim

The UCOM-RPA model, based on a correlated realistic nucleon-nucleon interaction, shows that the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) energy is sensitive to the range of the tensor correlator, and that the RPA correlations from collective vibrations recover a part of the residual long-range correlations not included in the UCOM Hartree-Fock ground state. The relativistic QRPA predicts a proton pygmy dipole resonance in nuclei towards the proton drip-line, where loosely bound protons oscillate against the isospin-saturated core.

## Method

The UCOM-RPA model uses a correlated Argonne V18 interaction, with short-range central and tensor correlations included via a unitary transformation, and solves the RPA equations in a fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock basis. The relativistic QRPA is formulated in the canonical single-nucleon basis of the relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov model, using effective Lagrangians with density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings and the Gogny interaction for pairing, with the RQRPA equations derived from the equation of motion for the generalized nucleon density.

## Key numbers

- The ISGMR excitation energy in closed-shell nuclei (e.g., 16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 90Zr, 132Sn, 208Pb) lowers by approximately 4 MeV when the tensor correlator constraint I_theta^(10) is decreased from 0.09 fm^3 to 0.07 fm^3.
- For the N=20 isotones 44Cr and 46Fe, a pronounced low-energy peak is found at approximately 10 MeV excitation energy (10.15 MeV in 44Cr and 9.44 MeV in 46Fe), compared to the isovector giant dipole resonance at approximately 20 MeV.
- The UCOM-RPA model exhausts the isoscalar energy-weighted sum rules with maximal discrepancies of approximately plus or minus 3 percent.
- The UCOM-HF calculations use a harmonic oscillator basis with maximal major shell quantum number N_max = 12 and maximal orbital angular momentum quantum number l_max = 8.

## Context

This work extends the UCOM approach (Feldmeier 1998, Neff 2003, Roth 2004, 2005) by incorporating RPA to include long-range correlations from collective vibrations, and extends relativistic QRPA studies (Paar 2003) to predict the proton pygmy dipole resonance. The results enable future inclusion of three-body forces and comparison with ab initio methods like no-core shell model calculations (Navratil 2000) and Green's function Monte Carlo (Pieper 2004), and provide a benchmark for the importance of missing correlations in UCOM-HF descriptions.
