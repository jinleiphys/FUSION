# $ T=1 $ Pairing Along the $ N=Z $ Line

> Pairing energies for the addition of two neutrons on even-even nuclei with $ N=Z $ are studied. The $ Z $ dependence is attributed to the number and type of orbitals that are occupied in the valence shell-model space. Properties in the region from $ Z=60-100 $ depend on the location of the $ 0g_{9/2} $ orbital.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the discrepancy between experimental and theoretical T=1 pairing energies for N=Z nuclei in the A=70 region, previously reported by Wang et al., arises because the fp model space used in those calculations is insufficient; the 0g9/2 orbital must be included explicitly to capture the pairing and deformed properties for nuclei with core mass number Ac ≥ 64.

## Method

The authors extracted the T=1 pairing energy Dn from experimental binding energies using the pairing-gap equation for even-even N=Z core nuclei from Zc=4 to Zc=50, using the 2020 mass table and new data from Wang et al. They compared these experimental Dn values to configuration-interaction calculations using empirical shell-model Hamiltonians (USDC in the ds space, GPFX1A in the fp space, JUN45 in the jj44 space) and to valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) calculations in various model spaces.

## Key numbers

- For 74Rb, δVoo_pn = 13.976(40) - 10.682(11) = 3.294(42) MeV from Eq. (1), and Dn = 13.851(7) - 10.682(11) = 3.169(13) MeV from Eq. (4)
- Energies of the lowest 9/2+ states in Ac+1 nuclei: 2.40 MeV (61Zn), 1.22 MeV (65Ge), 0.57 MeV (69Se), and 0.43 MeV (73Kr)
- Energies of 7/2- states in the upper ds shell: 3.62 MeV (29Si), 2.93 MeV (33S), and 1.61 MeV (37Ar)
- The estimated error in one-neutron separation energies from VS-IMSRG is 0.8 MeV, with an additional systematic downward shift of 0.2 to 0.4 MeV
- For Ac=68, the jj44 Dn value is about one MeV larger than the fp model space result, correlated with a change in 0g9/2 occupation from 0.48 in 66Ge to 1.48 in 70Se

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Wang et al. (2023) on δVoo_pn for odd-odd N=Z nuclei by placing those results in the broader context of T=1 pairing along the entire N=Z line from 8Be to 100Sn. The study shows that while empirical shell-model Hamiltonians like USDC, USDA, USDB, and GPFX1A describe the data well in their fitted regions, the VS-IMSRG calculations are not yet accurate enough to draw conclusions about orbital contributions to pairing. The paper identifies the need for future configuration-mixing calculations in the jj44 model space (including the 0g9/2 orbital) for Ac ≥ 72 to explain features such as the sharp drop in Dn at Ac=76.
