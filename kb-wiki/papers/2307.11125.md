# Reexamining constraints on neutron star properties from perturbative QCD

> The implications of perturbative QCD (pQCD) calculations on neutron stars are carefully examined. While pQCD calculations above baryon chemical potentials $μ_B\simeq2.4$ GeV demonstrate the potential of ruling out a wide range of neutron star equations of state (EOSs), such constraints only affect the most massive neutron stars in the vicinity of the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) limit, resulting in constraints that are orthogonal to current or expected astrophysical bounds. In the most constraining scenario, pQCD considerations favor low values of the squared speed sound $C_s$ at high $μ_B$ relevant for the most massive neutron stars, but leave predictions of the radii and tidal deformabilities almost unchanged. Such considerations become irrelevant if the maximum speed of sound squared inside neutron stars does not exceed about $C_{s,\mathrm{max}}\simeq0.5$, or if pQCD breaks down below $μ_B\simeq2.9$ GeV. Furthermore, the large pQCD uncertainties preclude any meaningful bounds on the neutron star EOS at the moment. Interestingly, if pQCD predictions for the pressure at around $μ_B\simeq2.5$ GeV are refined and found to be low ($\lesssim 1.5$ GeV/fm$^3$), evidence for a soft neutron star inner core EOS in combination with the existence of two-solar-mass pulsars would indicate the presence of color superconductivity beyond neutron star densities. We point out that two-solar-mass pulsars place robust upper bounds on this non-perturbative effect and require the pairing gap to be less than $Δ_\mathrm{CFL}\lesssim500$ MeV at $μ_B\simeq2.5$ GeV.

## Key claim

Perturbative QCD constraints on neutron star equations of state only affect the most massive neutron stars near the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff limit and are orthogonal to current or expected astrophysical bounds; the large pQCD uncertainties preclude any meaningful bounds on the neutron star EOS at the moment, and such considerations become irrelevant if the maximum speed of sound squared inside neutron stars does not exceed about 0.5 or if pQCD breaks down below a baryon chemical potential of about 2.9 GeV.

## Method

The author examines the thermodynamic consistency requirements between low-density chiral effective field theory (chiEFT) equations of state and high-density perturbative QCD predictions using the maximally stiff and maximally soft limiting EOS constructions, which are consequences of causality and stability of dense matter. The pQCD pressure at baryon chemical potentials around 2.5-3 GeV is taken as the high-density boundary condition, and the bounds Delta P_min and Delta P_max are applied to constrain the neutron star EOS at lower densities, with matching performed at the density and chemical potential at the center of maximum-mass stars. The analysis uses a parameterization of chiEFT-based EOSs with correlated uncertainties derived from eigenvalue decomposition of the covariance matrix, and employs both limiting EOSs and randomly generated inner core EOSs based on speed of sound parameterizations.

## Key numbers

- pQCD calculations become applicable above baryon chemical potentials mu_B approximately 2.4 GeV
- The maximum speed of sound squared inside neutron stars below which pQCD constraints become irrelevant is C_s,max approximately 0.5
- pQCD constraints become irrelevant if pQCD breaks down below mu_B approximately 2.9 GeV
- If pQCD predictions for pressure at mu_B approximately 2.5 GeV are refined and found to be low (less than or equal to 1.5 GeV/fm^3), evidence for a soft inner core EOS combined with two-solar-mass pulsars would indicate color superconductivity with a pairing gap less than Delta_CFL less than or equal to 500 MeV at mu_B approximately 2.5 GeV
- The chiEFT N3LO calculation shows 1-sigma uncertainties in pure neutron matter pressure of approximately 10% at n_B = n_0 and approximately 40% at 2n_0
- The two most significant eigenvectors from the covariance matrix decomposition account for approximately 90% and approximately 8% of the variances respectively

## Context

This work extends the line of research initiated by Komoltsev (2021) and Gorda (2022) on using pQCD calculations to constrain neutron star equations of state, while clarifying misunderstandings in the subsequent literature including attempts by Somasundaram (2022). The author builds upon the limiting EOS constructions from Rhoades (1974), Koranda (1996), Lattimer (2000), and Drischler (2020), and uses the chiEFT calculations with correlated uncertainties from Drischler (2020) and the pQCD calculations up to N2LO from Freedman (1976) and Kurkela (2009). The work enables a clearer understanding of what pQCD can and cannot say about neutron star properties, and identifies conditions under which refined pQCD predictions could provide evidence for color superconductivity in neutron star cores.
