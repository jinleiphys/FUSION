# Effect of the symmetry energy on nuclear stopping and its relation to the production of light charged fragments

> We present a complete systematics (excitation function, impact parameter, system size, isospin asymmetry, and equations of state dependences) of global stopping and fragment production for heavy-ion reactions in the energy range between 50 and 1000 MeV/nucleon in the presence of symmetry energy and an isospin-dependent cross section. It is observed that the degree of stopping depends weakly on the symmetry energy and strongly on the isospin-dependent cross section. However, the symmetry energy and isospin-dependent cross section has an effect of the order of more than 10% on the emission of light charged particles (LCP's). It means that nuclear stopping and LCP's can be used as a tool to get the information of an isospin-dependent cross section. Interestingly, the LCP's emission in the presence of symmetry energy is found to be highly correlated with the global stopping.

## Key claim

The degree of nuclear stopping depends weakly on the symmetry energy and strongly on the isospin-dependent cross-section, while the symmetry energy and isospin-dependent cross-section have an effect of more than 10% on the emission of light charged particles (LCPs). The authors conclude that nuclear stopping and LCP emission can be used as a tool to obtain information about the isospin-dependent cross-section, but the abstract's claim of a "highly correlated" relationship between LCP emission and global stopping is conditioned by the specific use of the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model and the particular reaction systems studied.

## Method

The authors employed the isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate thousands of events for the neutron-rich reaction of 54Xe131 + 54Xe131 at incident energies between 50 and 1000 MeV/nucleon, using both hard (compressibility 380 MeV) and soft (compressibility 200 MeV) equations of state. They varied the impact parameter from most-central to peripheral collisions and studied the role of symmetry energy by simulating reactions with and without this term, using energy-dependent Cugnon and constant nucleon-nucleon cross-sections. Fragments were constructed using the minimum spanning tree (MST) method with a binding distance of 4 fm, and global stopping was quantified using the anisotropy ratio (R), quadrupole moment (Qzz), and rapidity distributions.

## Key numbers

- The peak value of the Gaussian for LCP rapidity distribution is altered by about 10% when symmetry energy is included or excluded.
- The maximum stopping is observed around 400 MeV/nucleon incident energy for the 54Xe131 + 54Xe131 system.
- The hard and soft equations of state used have compressibilities of 380 MeV and 200 MeV, respectively.
- The symmetry energy term used in the potential is 32 MeV (E_Sym = 32 MeV) when included.
- The isospin-dependent cross-section used in panel (b) of Fig. 3 and Fig. 7 is 55 mb.
- System size dependence was studied for reactions of 20Ca40 + 20Ca40, 28Ni58 + 28Ni58, 41Nb93 + 41Nb93, 54Xe131 + 54Xe131, and 79Au197 + 79Au197.
- Isospin asymmetry was studied for reactions of 20Ca34 + 20Ca34 (N/Z = 0.7), 20Ca40 + 20Ca40 (N/Z = 1), 20Ca48 + 20Ca48 (N/Z = 1.4), and 20Ca57 + 20Ca57 (N/Z = 1.85).

## Context

This work extends the earlier correlation between multifragmentation and global nuclear stopping by Puri and co-workers (Dhaw06), who did not include isospin of the system, by incorporating symmetry energy and isospin-dependent cross-sections. It builds on studies by Bauer (Baur88) showing nuclear stopping is determined by mean field and in-medium NN cross-sections, and by Liu et al. (Liu01) and Feng et al. (Feng02) who investigated isospin dependence of cross-section in nuclear stopping. The findings enable using LCP production as a barometer for nuclear stopping and as a tool to probe the isospin-dependent cross-section in heavy-ion collisions, with the maximum stopping around 400 MeV/nucleon supporting the experimental results of Reisdorf et al. (Reis04a).
