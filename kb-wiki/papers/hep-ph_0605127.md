# ppbar enhancement in B and J/Psi decay

> The near-threshold enhancement in the ppbar invariant mass spectrum from the B^+ -> K^+ ppbar decay reported recently by the BaBar Collaboration is studied within the Jülich NNbar model. We illustrate that the invariant mass dependence of the ppbar spectrum close to the threshold can be reproduced by the final state interactions. This explanation is in line with our previous analysis of the ppbar invariant mass spectrum from the J/Psi -> gamma ppbar decay measured by the BES Collaboration. We also comment on a structure found recently in the pi^+ pi^- eta' mass spectrum of the radiative J/Psi decay by the BES Collaboration. In particular we argue that one should be rather cautions in bringing this structure in connection with the enhancement found in the ppbar invariant mass spectrum or with the existence of NNbar bound states.

## Key claim

The near-threshold enhancement in the \(p\bar p\) invariant mass spectrum from \(B^+\to K^+ p\bar p\) decay can be reproduced by final state interactions (FSI) in the \(p\bar p\) system, using the same Jülich \(N\bar N\) model that previously explained the similar enhancement in \(J/\Psi\to\gamma p\bar p\) decay; the authors caution that this FSI-based explanation does not rule out other interpretations, and they argue against directly connecting the enhancement to the \(X(1835)\) resonance or to \(N\bar N\) bound states.

## Method

The authors extracted the spin-averaged reaction amplitude \(|A|^2\) from the BaBar \(B^+\to K^+ p\bar p\) differential decay rate data using the standard phase-space formula, then compared it to the squared \(p\bar p\) scattering amplitudes \(|T|^2\) predicted by the Jülich \(N\bar N\) model A(OBE) for the \(^1S_0\), \(^3S_1\), \(^3P_0\), and \(^3P_1\) partial waves in both isospin \(I=0\) and \(I=1\) channels, assuming the Watson-Migdal factorization \(A \approx N \cdot T\). For the \(J/\Psi\to\gamma p\bar p\) data, they also divided the extracted amplitude by the \(^1S_0\) scattering amplitude to obtain FSI-corrected spectra and compared them to Breit-Wigner shapes for the \(X(1835)\), \(\eta(1760)\), \(\pi(1800)\), and \(\eta(1800)\) resonances.

## Key numbers

- The \(X(1835)\) resonance reported by BES in \(J/\Psi\to\gamma\pi^+\pi^-\eta'\) has mass \(1833.7\pm6.1\) MeV and width \(67.7\pm20.3\) MeV.
- A fit including a pseudoscalar resonance and \(I=0\) \(^1S_0\) \(p\bar p\) FSI to the \(J/\Psi\to\gamma p\bar p\) spectrum yielded a mass of \(1831\pm7\) MeV and \(\Gamma < 153\) MeV for the same state.
- The \(\eta(1800)\) from Bisello et al. has one solution with mass \(1807\pm10\) MeV and width \(94\pm12\) MeV.
- The Jülich \(N\bar N\) model does not produce a near-threshold bound state in the \(^{31}S_0\) amplitude; the bound state in the \(^{11}S_0\) amplitude lies at \(E \approx -104 - i413\) MeV, too wide to affect the physical region.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Haidenbauer, Meißner, Sibirtsev 2005) of the \(J/\Psi\to\gamma p\bar p\) spectrum measured by BES, applying the same FSI mechanism to the new high-statistics BaBar data on \(B^+\to K^+ p\bar p\). It contradicts interpretations of the near-threshold enhancement as evidence for a \(p\bar p\) bound state or baryonium (Dover, Shapiro) or exotic glueball states (Chua, Rosner), and instead supports the FSI explanation also advocated by Kerbikov, Bugg, Zou, and Loiseau using effective range or Paris \(N\bar N\) model approaches. The analysis enables future searches for the \(X(1835)\) in \(p\bar p\) annihilation reactions at facilities like PANDA at FAIR, and cautions against over-interpreting the connection between the \(X(1835)\) and the \(p\bar p\) threshold enhancement.
