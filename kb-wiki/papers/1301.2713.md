# Dissipative hydrodynamic effects on the quark-gluon plasma at finite baryon density

> The quark-gluon plasma behaves as a relativistic viscous fluid in high-energy heavy ion collisions. I develop a causal dissipative hydrodynamic model at finite baryon density for RHIC and LHC to estimate the net baryon rapidity distribution. The net baryon number is found to be carried to forward rapidity by the flow, effectively enhancing the transparency of the collisions. This suggests that the energy available for the production of a hot medium could be larger than that naively implied from experimental data. Also the distribution can be sensitive to baryon dissipation as much as to shear and bulk viscosity.

# Dissipative hydrodynamic effects on the quark-gluon plasma at finite baryon density

## Key claim

The net baryon number is carried to forward rapidity by hydrodynamic flow, effectively enhancing collision transparency and suggesting that more energy is available for QGP production than naively implied from experimental data; however, the results are dependent on the choice of initial conditions and transport coefficients, and the dissipative effects are more visible at RHIC than at LHC.

## Method

A causal second-order relativistic dissipative hydrodynamic model at finite baryon density was developed, incorporating shear viscosity, bulk viscosity, baryon charge conductivity, and baryon-heat cross conductivity. The longitudinal hydrodynamic expansion was solved by integrating out transverse geometry, using an equation of state from Taylor-expanded lattice QCD results, transport coefficients from AdS/CFT and phenomenological analyses, and initial conditions based on the color glass condensate framework with valence quark distributions for net baryon density.

## Key numbers

- Mean rapidity loss at RHIC: initial value ⟨δy⟩_initial = 2.67, reduced to ⟨δy⟩_hydro = 2.09 (ideal), 2.16 (viscous), and 2.26 (dissipative including baryon diffusion)
- Mean rapidity loss at LHC: initial value ⟨δy⟩_initial = 3.36, reduced to ⟨δy⟩_hydro = 2.92 for the dissipative hydrodynamic case
- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: τ₀ = 1 fm
- Freeze-out temperature: T_f = 0.16 GeV
- Kinetic energy losses after hydrodynamic evolution are about 10-15% less than those of the initial conditions

## Context

This work extends viscous hydrodynamic studies at finite baryon density, which had previously neglected net baryon number in evolving flow due to complexity of causal relativistic formalisms and the boost-invariant ansatz. The results show that the transparency enhancement from hydrodynamic evolution could explain the abrupt deviation of RHIC data from the simple linear extrapolation of lower-energy AGS and SPS results (Bearden 2003). The model enables future quantitative studies of the QCD phase diagram and critical point searches at beam energy scan experiments at RHIC, GSI, NICA, and J-PARC, though transverse dynamics and more realistic transport coefficients are needed for quantitative predictions.
