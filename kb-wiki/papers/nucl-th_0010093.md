# Fission barriers of neutron-rich and superheavy nuclei calculated with the ETFSI method

> Using the ETFSI (extended Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky integral) method, we have calculated the fission barriers of nearly 2000 exotic nuclei, including all the neutron-rich nuclei up to A=318 that are expected to be relevant to the r-process, and all the superheavy nuclei in the vicinity of N=184, with Z<=120. Our calculations were performed with the Skyrme force SkSC4, which was determined in the ETFSI-1 mass fit. For proton-deficient nuclei in the region of N=184 we find the barriers to be much higher than previously believed, which suggests that the r-process path might continue to mass numbers well beyond 300. For the superheavy nuclei we typically find barrier heights of 6-7 MeV.

## Key claim

Using the ETFSI method with the Skyrme force SkSC4, the authors calculate fission barriers for nearly 2000 exotic nuclei and find that for proton-deficient nuclei near N=184 the barriers are much higher than previously believed, suggesting the r-process path might continue to mass numbers well beyond 300; however, the authors note that for primary barrier heights between 10 and 15 MeV they probably overestimate the height by about 1.5 MeV, and for barriers above 15 MeV the overestimate is 2 MeV or more.

## Method

The ETFSI (extended Thomas-Fermi plus Strutinsky integral) method was used as a high-speed approximation to the Skyrme-Hartree-Fock method, with pairing correlations generated by a delta-function force treated in the BCS approach with blocking. Fission paths were optimized with respect to three deformation parameters: elongation c, necking h, and asymmetry tilde-alpha, assuming axial symmetry. The calculations used the SkSC4 force parameters determined entirely by the ETFSI-1 mass fit, with no modification for barrier calculations.

## Context

This work extends the ETFSI method, previously used only for ground-state mass calculations, to fission barrier calculations for the first time, enabling consistent r-process studies using the same microscopic model for both masses and barriers. The results are compared with liquid-drop model calculations of Howard and Moller (1980) and with Thomas-Fermi calculations of Myers and Swiatecki (1999), showing that the ETFSI barriers for proton-deficient nuclei near N=184 are much higher than these earlier predictions. The calculations provide the fission barrier data needed to determine whether the r-process path is terminated by fission before reaching mass numbers beyond 300, a question that previous r-process studies using ETFSI-1 masses had to neglect due to the lack of consistent barrier calculations.
