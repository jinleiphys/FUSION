# Wobbling geometry in simple triaxial rotor

> The spectroscopy properties and angular momentum geometry for the wobbling motion of a simple triaxial rotor are investigated within the triaxial rotor model up to spin $I=40\hbar$. The obtained exact solutions of energy spectra and reduced quadrupole transition probabilities are compared to the approximate analytic solutions by harmonic approximation formula and Holstein-Primakoff formula. It is found that the low lying wobbling bands can be well described by the analytic formulas. The evolution of the angular momentum geometry as well as the $K$-distribution with respect to the rotation and the wobbling phonon excitation are studied in detail. It is demonstrated that with the increasing of wobbling phonon number, the triaxial rotor changes its wobbling motions along the axis with the largest moment of inertia to the axis with the smallest moment of inertia. In this process, a specific evolutionary track that can be used to depict the motion of a triaxial rotating nuclei is proposed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a simple triaxial rotor, the wobbling motion transitions from alignment along the axis with the largest moment of inertia to the axis with the smallest moment of inertia as the wobbling phonon number increases, and that this evolution follows a unique track determined solely by the three moments of inertia, independent of the total spin.

## Method

The triaxial rotor model (TRM) Hamiltonian was exactly diagonalized in the laboratory frame for spins up to I=40ℏ, using a basis of symmetrized Wigner D-functions with D2 symmetry. The exact energy spectra and reduced E2 transition probabilities were compared with approximate analytic solutions from the harmonic approximation (HA) formula and the Holstein-Primakoff (HP) formula. The angular momentum geometry was analyzed through expectation values of squared angular momentum components and K-distributions (probability distributions of the angular momentum projection on the principal axes).

## Key numbers

- The moments of inertia used were J1=8.33 ℏ²/MeV, J2=16.66 ℏ²/MeV, and J3=50.00 ℏ²/MeV, corresponding to A1=2A2=6A3=0.06 MeV/ℏ².
- The HA results deviate from exact TRM results by about 150 keV for the n=2 wobbling band and about 350 keV for the n=3 wobbling band.
- The HP formula reproduces exact TRM results with energy differences less than 30 keV even for the n=3 wobbling band.
- The intrinsic quadrupole moment Q0 was taken as √(16π) e b and the triaxial deformation parameter γ=30°.
- For the n=4 band at spin I=4ℏ, the angular momentum mainly aligns along the 1-axis; at spins 6≤I≤14ℏ it aligns along the 2-axis; and at I>16ℏ it aligns along the 3-axis.
- For I=10ℏ, the azimuthal angle φ≈60° for small n, determined by the ratio of moments of inertia.

## Context

This work extends the foundational triaxial rotor model of Bohr and Mottelson (1975) and the particle rotor model studies of Hamamoto (2002) and Frauendorf (2014) by providing a systematic investigation of angular momentum geometry evolution with both spin and wobbling phonon number. The paper demonstrates that the HA formula works well only for small wobbling phonon numbers and high spins, while the HP formula accurately reproduces exact results across a wider range. The proposed evolutionary track for the angular momentum orientation, which is independent of spin and determined only by the three moments of inertia, provides a characteristic feature for identifying wobbling motion in triaxial nuclei and can be tested against experimental data in nuclei such as 163Lu and 112Ru.
