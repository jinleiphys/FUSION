# Positronium Spectroscopy in a Magnetic Field

> Hyperfine spectroscopy of positronium formed in the presence of a static magnetic field is considered. Generalising the situation hitherto developed in the literature, the magnetic field is not assumed to be parallel to the momentum of incoming polarised positrons, while the possibility of electron polarisation is also included in the analysis. The results are of application to high sensitivity positron polarimeters used in current $β$ decay experiments.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes existing positronium hyperfine spectroscopy calculations to the case where the static magnetic field is *not* assumed to be parallel to the momentum of incoming polarized positrons, and also includes the possibility of electron polarization; the results are applicable to high-sensitivity positron polarimeters used in current beta decay experiments, but the author notes that the magnetic field gradient must be negligible over the spatial extension of the positronium state.

## Method

The author diagonalizes the total Hamiltonian for the 1S positronium ground state in the presence of an arbitrary static magnetic field, starting from the known hyperfine eigenstates (singlet and triplet) in zero field and adding the magnetic coupling term \(H_B = \frac{1}{2}\mu (\vec{\sigma}_- - \vec{\sigma}_+)\cdot\vec{B}\). The resulting four eigenstates (pseudo-singlet, pseudo-triplet, and two degenerate states) are expressed in terms of the magnetic field components, and their decay rates into two and three photons are computed from the known singlet and triplet decay rates. The time evolution of positronium populations is then derived by expanding the initial spin state (tensor product of electron and positron spinors with arbitrary polarizations and phases) in the basis of these eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- Positronium ground state Coulomb energy: \(E_{(C)} = -6.803\ \text{eV}\)
- Positronium radius: \(a = 1.0584\ \text{Å}\)
- Hyperfine splitting between singlet and triplet: \(\Delta E = 8.41 \times 10^{-4}\ \text{eV}\)
- Singlet (2γ) decay rate: \(\lambda_S = 7.98665 \times 10^9\ \text{s}^{-1}\) (lifetime \(0.125209 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{s}\))
- Triplet (3γ) decay rate: \(\lambda_T = 7.03859 \times 10^6\ \text{s}^{-1}\) (lifetime \(142.074 \times 10^{-9}\ \text{s}\))
- Ratio \(\lambda_S / \lambda_T = 1134.695\)
- Characteristic magnetic field scale: \(2\mu / \Delta E = 1 / (3.628575\ \text{Tesla})\), defining \(x = |\vec{B}| / (3.63\ \text{Tesla})\)

## Context

This work extends the standard treatment of positronium hyperfine spectroscopy in magnetic fields found in earlier references (Dick, Mad, Rich) by removing the assumption that the magnetic field is aligned with the positron momentum, and by including electron polarization effects relevant for very-low-temperature experiments. The results enable more accurate analysis of positron polarimeters used in beta decay experiments at the author's institute (F1, F2, M1), which test for deviations from purely right-handed positron polarization that would indicate physics beyond the Standard Model.
