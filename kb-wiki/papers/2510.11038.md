# Microscopic description of the proton halo in $^{12}$N

> The year 2025 marks the 40th anniversary of the discovery of halo nuclei and the 15th anniversary of the development of the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc). In this work, we present the first DRHBc description of the proton halo phenomenon. The available experimental proton separation energies and empirical matter root-mean-square (rms) radii are reasonably well reproduced for the $N=5$ isotones, ranging from the stable nucleus $^{9}$Be to the drip-line nucleus $^{12}$N. In particular, the DRHBc theory captures the abrupt increase in rms radii at $^{12}$N, unambiguously corroborating its proton halo structure. The formation of this halo is attributed to the occupation of a weakly bound orbital with dominant $1p$ components by the valence proton, which contributes approximately $90\%$ to the diffused proton density of $^{12}$N at large distances from the nuclear center. A shape decoupling between the prolate core and the oblate halo in $^{12}$N is predicted.

# Microscopic description of the proton halo in $^{12}$N

## Key claim

The deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) captures the abrupt increase in matter root-mean-square radii at $^{12}$N, corroborating its proton halo structure, but the calculated proton separation energy for $^{12}$N is slightly overestimated relative to experimental data, and the empirical matter radii used for comparison are not fully model independent as they were extracted from reaction cross sections using the Glauber model.

## Method

The authors applied the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) to calculate ground-state properties of the $N=5$ isotones from $^{9}$Be to $^{12}$N. Quasiparticle wave functions were expanded in a Dirac Woods-Saxon basis with a box size of 20 fm, and calculations were performed using four different density functionals (NL3*, NL3, NLSH, PK1) with pairing strength varied from -340 to -380 MeV fm$^3$ to quantify theoretical uncertainties. The proton density distributions were decomposed into core and halo contributions to analyze the shape decoupling between the prolate core and oblate halo in $^{12}$N.

## Key numbers

- The valence proton orbital in $^{12}$N has an rms radius exceeding 3.2 fm, significantly larger than the matter rms radius of the nucleus.
- The valence proton contributes approximately 90% to the diffused proton density of $^{12}$N at large distances from the nuclear center.
- The core of $^{12}$N has a proton rms radius of 2.497 fm and quadrupole deformation $\beta_2 = 0.198$, while the halo has a proton rms radius of 3.259 fm and oblate deformation $\beta_2 = -0.325$.
- The valence proton orbital in $^{12}$N is composed of over 90% $1p_{1/2}$ component.
- The DWS basis was constructed with a box size of 20 fm, mesh size of 0.1 fm, cutoff energy of 100 MeV, and angular momentum cutoff of 19/2 $\hbar$.
- The Legendre expansion was truncated at $l_{\mathrm{max}} = 4$.

## Context

This work presents the first application of the DRHBc theory to a proton halo nucleus, extending previous DRHBc studies that had been limited to neutron halos in nuclei such as $^{17,19}$B (Yang 2021, Sun 2021), $^{15,19,22}$C (Sun 2018, 2020, Wang 2024), $^{31}$Ne (Zhong 2022, Pan 2024), and $^{37}$Mg (Zhang 2023, An 2024). The study addresses the long-standing debate about the proton halo in $^{12}$N, which was first suggested experimentally by Warner et al. (1998) based on enhanced total reaction cross sections and later supported by one-proton removal cross section measurements (Warner 2006) and total reaction cross section remeasurements (Li 2010). The work enables future extensions of the DRHBc framework with angular momentum projection to compute electromagnetic moments and spectroscopic properties for odd-mass and odd-odd nuclei.
