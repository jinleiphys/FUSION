# Chiral perturbation theory in the environment with chiral imbalance

> We discuss the description of chiral imbalance imprint in pion matter. It is based on the Chiral Perturbation Theory supplemented by medium induced chiral chemical potential in the covariant derivative. Such an implementation of chiral imbalance recently was explored in linear sigma model inspired by QCD. The relationship between two sigma models is examined. A possible experimental detection of chiral imbalance in charged pion decays inside the fireball is pointed out.

## Key claim

The paper argues that Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) supplemented with a chiral chemical potential \(\mu_5\) can describe the imprint of chiral imbalance in pion matter, and that the resulting predictions for the distortion of the pion mass shell show a "convincing correspondence" with those from a linear sigma model, provided the low-energy constants are properly handled when the trace of the chiral current \(<j_\mu>\) is nonzero.

## Method

The authors introduce a chiral chemical potential \(\mu_5\) into the covariant derivative of the ChPT Lagrangian for pions, first at dimension-2 and then at dimension-4 (Gasser-Leutwyler) level, for both SU(2) and SU(3) flavor symmetries. They derive the modified dispersion relation for pions and compare the resulting combinations of low-energy constants with those obtained from a linear sigma model for light pions and scalar mesons, whose parameters are fixed from vacuum spectral characteristics.

## Key numbers

* The tree-level neutral pion mass is given by \(M_\pi^2 = 2B_0 \hat m_{u,d}\), with \(F_0 \simeq 92\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The renormalized SU(2) Gasser-Leutwyler constants at scale \(\mu \simeq M_\pi \simeq 140\ \mathrm{MeV}\) are: \(l_1^r = (-0.4 \pm 0.6) \times 10^{-3}\), \(l_2^r = (8.6 \pm 0.2) \times 10^{-3}\), \(l_1^r + l_2^r = (8.2 \pm 0.8) \times 10^{-3}\), and \(l_4^r = (2.64 \pm 0.01) \times 10^{-2}\).
* The change of the pion decay constant squared with \(\mu_5\) from the linear sigma model is \(\Delta F_\pi^2 / \mu_5^2 = 1/(\lambda_1 + \lambda_2) \approx 0.3\), compared to \(32 (l_1 + l_2) \approx 0.26\) from ChPT.
* The linear sigma model estimate for \(l_4\) is \(1/[8(\lambda_1 + \lambda_2)] \approx 3.8 \times 10^{-2}\), compared to the ChPT value \(l_4 \approx 2.64 \times 10^{-2}\).
* The distortion of the mass shell may become detectable when \(\mu_5 \sim F_\pi\), as the effective pion mass approaches the muon mass.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of local parity breaking (LPB) in heavy ion collisions, where a chiral chemical potential was introduced in a linear sigma model inspired by QCD (Andrianov et al., 2016; 2017). The present paper connects those sigma-model results to the more phenomenologically grounded Chiral Perturbation Theory framework of Gasser and Leutwyler (1984). The results enable predictions for observable signatures of chiral imbalance, such as suppression of charged pion decay into muon and neutrino, and photon polarization asymmetry in \(\pi\gamma\) scattering via Wess-Zumino-Witten terms, with lattice computations (Braguta et al.) suggested as an independent check.
