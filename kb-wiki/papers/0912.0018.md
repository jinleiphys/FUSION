# Quark Deconfinement Phase Transition in Neutron Stars

> The hadron-quark phase transition in the interior of compact stars is investigated, when the transition proceeds through a mixed phase. The hadronic phase is described in the framework of relativistic mean-field theory, when also the scalar-isovector delta-meson mean-field is taken into account. The changes of the parameters of phase transition caused by the presence of delta-meson field are explored. The results of calculation of structure of the mixed phase (Glendenning construction) are compared with the results of usual first-order phase transition (Maxwell construction).

## Key claim
The inclusion of the scalar-isovector delta-meson field in the relativistic mean-field description of nuclear matter alters the threshold characteristics of the hadron-quark mixed phase in neutron stars, but the direction of the change depends on the bag constant: for B=60 MeV/fm^3 the lower threshold parameters increase while for B=100 MeV/fm^3 they decrease.

## Method
The hadronic phase is described using relativistic mean-field theory including the scalar-isovector delta-meson field, with model parameters fitted to nuclear saturation properties. The quark phase is described using the MIT bag model with one-gluon exchange corrections for u, d, s quarks. The mixed phase is constructed using the Glendenning construction (global charge neutrality with Gibbs conditions) and compared to the Maxwell construction (separate charge neutrality with a density jump). The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are then integrated to obtain mass-radius relations for hybrid stars.

## Key numbers
- For B=60 MeV/fm^3 with delta-meson field: lower threshold baryon density n_N = 0.0771 fm^-3, upper threshold n_Q = 1.083 fm^-3; lower threshold pressure P_N = 0.434 MeV/fm^3, upper threshold P_Q = 327.745 MeV/fm^3
- For B=100 MeV/fm^3 with delta-meson field: lower threshold n_N = 0.2409 fm^-3, upper threshold n_Q = 1.448 fm^-3; lower threshold P_N = 16.911 MeV/fm^3, upper threshold P_Q = 474.368 MeV/fm^3
- For B=60 MeV/fm^3, the range of masses for stars containing the mixed phase is [0.085 M_sun; 1.853 M_sun] for Glendenning construction and [0.216 M_sun; 1.828 M_sun] for Maxwell construction
- For B=100 MeV/fm^3, the range of masses for stars containing the mixed phase is [0.997 M_sun; 1.780 M_sun] for Glendenning construction; the maximum stable neutron star mass is 1.894 M_sun
- For B=100 MeV/fm^3 with Maxwell construction, the density jump occurs from n_1 = 0.475 fm^-3 to n_2 = 0.650 fm^-3

## Context
This work extends the author's previous study (Alaverdyan 2009) which examined the Maxwell construction for the hadron-quark phase transition with delta-meson fields, finding that the delta field decreases the transition pressure and densities. The present paper compares the Glendenning construction (mixed phase) with the Maxwell construction, following the framework of Glendenning (1992) for two conserved charges. The results show that for B=100 MeV/fm^3, the mixed phase can exist in the center of compact stars but no pure quark matter core forms, which constrains the possible internal composition of neutron stars.
