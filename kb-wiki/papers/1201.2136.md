# Chiral dynamics of few- and many-nucleon systems

> This review gives a brief introduction to the chiral effective field theory of nuclear forces and atomic nuclei. We discuss the status of the nuclear Hamiltonian derived in this framework and some recent applications in few-nucleon systems. Nuclear lattice simulations as a new tool to address the many-body problem are introduced and some first results based on that method are presented.

## Key claim

This review argues that chiral effective field theory provides a systematic and model-independent framework for deriving nuclear forces from QCD, but the convergence of the chiral expansion depends on whether the Delta(1232) isobar is included explicitly; without it, the next-to-next-to-leading order contributions are unnaturally large due to the implicit treatment of the Delta resonance.

## Method

The authors derive nuclear potentials from the most general effective chiral Lagrangian by expanding in powers of the soft scale \(Q\) (pion mass or external momentum) relative to a hard scale \(\Lambda\). They define the nuclear potential by matching Feynman diagram amplitudes to the iterative solution of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation, or alternatively by decoupling pion states via unitary transformations. Long-range two-nucleon potentials are computed up to next-to-next-to-leading order (N\(^2\)LO) from one- and two-pion exchange diagrams, with short-range contact interactions parameterizing unresolved high-momentum physics.

## Key numbers

* The leading-order (LO) one-pion exchange potential is proportional to \((g_A/(2F_\pi))^2\), with \(g_A\) the nucleon axial coupling and \(F_\pi\) the pion decay constant.
* At N\(^2\)LO, the isoscalar-central potential \(V_C^{(3)}\) involves LECs \(c_1, c_3\) with values such as \(c_3 = -3.9\) GeV\(^{-1}\) and \(c_4 = 2.9\) GeV\(^{-1}\) from pion-nucleon subthreshold coefficients at order \(Q^2\).
* In the Delta-full theory, the single Delta-excitation at NLO generates an isoscalar-central potential proportional to \(g_A^2 h_A^2 / (12\pi F_\pi^4 (m_\Delta - m_N))\), with \(m_\Delta - m_N \sim 2 M_\pi\).
* The Nijmegen partial wave analysis shows a reduction in the number of parameters needed to describe proton-proton scattering below pion production threshold from 31 to 28 to 23 when including \(V_{1\pi}\), then \(V_{1\pi} + V_{2\pi}^{(2)}\), then \(V_{1\pi} + V_{2\pi}^{(2)} + V_{2\pi}^{(3)}\) as the long-range potential.

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Weinberg (1990) on applying chiral effective field theory to nuclear forces and summarizes the status of the nuclear Hamiltonian derived in this framework. It contrasts the delta-less approach with the small scale expansion that explicitly includes the Delta(1232) isobar, as developed by Hemmert et al. (1997) and applied to nuclear forces by Kaiser et al. (1998) and Krebs et al. (2007). The paper enables future ab initio calculations of light and medium-heavy nuclei using chiral potentials combined with many-body techniques, and introduces nuclear lattice simulations as a new tool for addressing the many-body problem.
