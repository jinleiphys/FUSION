# Light Flavour and Heavy Quark Spin Symmetry in Heavy Meson Molecules

> We propose an effective field theory incorporating light SU(3)-flavour and heavy quark spin symmetry to describe charmed meson-antimeson bound states. At lowest order the effective field theory entails a remarkable simplification: it only involves contact range interactions among the heavy meson and antimeson fields. We show that the isospin violating decays of the X(3872) can be used to constraint the interaction between the $D$ and a $\bar{D}^*$ mesons in the isovector channel. As a consequence, we can rule out the existence of a isovector partner of the X(3872). If we additionally assume that the X(3915) and Y(4140) are $D^*\bar{D}^*$ and $D_s^*\bar{D}_s^*$ molecular states, we can determine the full spectrum of molecular states with isospin I=0, 1/2 and 1.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the isospin-violating decays of the X(3872) can be used to constrain the interaction between D and D* mesons in the isovector channel, and under the assumption that the X(3915) and Y(4140) are molecular states, the full spectrum of heavy meson-antimeson molecules with isospin I=0, 1/2 and 1 can be determined from only four parameters.

## Method

The authors construct a leading-order effective field theory (EFT) for charmed meson-antimeson bound states that incorporates heavy quark spin symmetry (HQSS) and SU(3) light-flavour symmetry, where the potential reduces to contact-range interactions only. They use the isospin-violating decay branching ratio of the X(3872) into J/Ψ ρ versus J/Ψ ω to extract the isovector contact interaction strength, and then combine this with the assumed molecular nature of the X(3872), X(3915) and Y(4140) to determine the four independent counterterms (C0a, C0b, C1a, C1b) that describe all twenty-four possible S-wave heavy meson-antimeson molecular states.

## Key numbers

- X(3872) mass: 3871.68 ± 0.17 MeV; D0 + D0* threshold: 3871.84 ± 0.20 MeV (binding energy approximately 0.16 MeV)
- Amplitude ratio R_X = M(X → J/Ψ ρ) / M(X → J/Ψ ω) = 0.26^{+0.08}_{-0.05} from the experimental branching ratio B_X = 0.8 ± 0.3
- Mass gap between D*D* and DD thresholds in the 0+ channel: Δ(0+) ≃ 280 MeV; between D*D* and DD* thresholds in the 1+ channel: Δ(1+) ≃ 140 MeV
- Energy gap between D_sD_s and D0D0/D+D- thresholds: approximately 200 MeV
- Energy gap between D0D*0 and D+D*- thresholds: 8 MeV
- Cut-off window used for renormalization: Λ = 0.5-1.0 GeV
- Maximum binding energy for EFT validity in charm sector: B_max ~ 150-300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the heavy meson molecule EFT framework previously developed by the same authors (Valderrama 2012, Nieves 2012) by incorporating SU(3) flavour symmetry alongside HQSS, building on earlier work by AlFiky (2005) on HQSS constraints in heavy meson-antimeson interactions. The analysis of isospin violation in X(3872) decays follows the approach of Gamermann (2009) and uses the amplitude ratio derived by Hanhart (2011). The paper enables predictions for the full spectrum of hidden-charm molecular states, including possible SU(3)-flavour and HQSS partners of the X(3872), X(3915) and Y(4140), which can be tested against future experimental observations of XYZ states.
