# Effects of TMD evolution and partonic flavor on $e^+e^-$ annihilation into hadrons

> We calculate the transverse momentum dependence in the production of two back-to-back hadrons in electron-positron annihilations at the medium/large energy scales of BES-III and BELLE experiments. We use the parameters of the transverse-momentum-dependent (TMD) fragmentation functions that were recently extracted from the semi-inclusive deep-inelastic-scattering multiplicities at low energy from HERMES. TMD evolution is applied according to different approaches and using different parameters for the nonperturbative part of the evolution kernel, thus exploring the sensitivity of our results to these different choices and to the flavor dependence of parton fragmentation functions. We discuss how experimental measurements could discriminate among the various scenarios.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the transverse-momentum spectrum of back-to-back hadron pairs in electron-positron annihilation at BES-III and BELLE energies, using TMD fragmentation functions extracted from low-energy HERMES SIDIS data, and shows that the results are sensitive to both the flavor dependence of the fragmentation functions and to different prescriptions for the nonperturbative part of TMD evolution, potentially allowing experimental measurements to discriminate among these scenarios.

## Method

The authors calculate the differential cross section for two back-to-back unpolarized hadrons in electron-positron annihilation using a factorized formula in impact parameter space at next-to-leading-logarithmic (NLL) accuracy, neglecting the Y-term and higher-twist corrections. They take as input the unpolarized TMD fragmentation functions extracted from HERMES SIDIS multiplicities at a fixed scale of Q0^2 = 2.4 GeV^2, which have a flavor- and z-dependent Gaussian transverse-momentum width, and evolve them to the higher energies of BES-III and BELLE using the Collins-Soper evolution formalism. The evolution is implemented with four different combinations of prescriptions for the b-star function (b* or b-dagger) and the nonperturbative evolution kernel (linear or logarithmic in b_T^2), using anticorrelated pairs of values for the b_max and g_2 parameters.

## Key numbers

- The starting scale for the TMD fragmentation functions is Q0^2 = 2.4 GeV^2, the average scale of the HERMES SIDIS data used for the extraction.
- The hard scales considered are those of the BES-III and BELLE experiments, which are medium/large center-of-mass energies (Q^2 much larger than the HERMES scale).
- The nonperturbative function g_np is parametrized as either g_np^lin(b_T) = (g_2/4) b_T^2 or g_np^log(b_T) = g_2 ln(1 + b_T^2/4), with g_2 anticorrelated to b_max.
- The b-star prescription is b* = b_T / sqrt(1 + b_T^2 / b_max^2), and the alternative prescription is b-dagger = b_max {1 - exp[-b_T^4 / b_max^4]}^{1/4}.

## Context

This work extends the flavor-dependent TMD fragmentation function analysis of Signori et al. (2013), which fitted HERMES SIDIS multiplicities at low energy without evolution effects, to the higher-energy regime of e+e- annihilation where TMD evolution is essential. It enables future experimental measurements at BES-III and BELLE to test different implementations of TMD evolution, such as the b-star prescription of Collins (2011) and Landry et al. (2002) versus alternative prescriptions, and to discriminate between flavor-dependent and flavor-independent transverse-momentum distributions of fragmenting partons.
