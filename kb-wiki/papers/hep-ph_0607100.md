# Meson-baryon scattering lengths in HB$χ$PT

> We calculate the chiral corrections to the s-wave pseudoscalar meson octet-baryon scattering lengths $a_{MB}$ to ${\cal O}(p^3)$ in the SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HB$χ$PT). Hopefully the obtained analytical expressions will be helpful in the chiral extrapolation of these scattering lengths in the future lattice simulation.

# Meson-baryon scattering lengths in HBχPT

## Key claim

The authors calculate chiral corrections to s-wave pseudoscalar meson octet-baryon scattering lengths to O(p^3) in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but note that the chiral expansion may converge slowly for kaon and eta processes because m_K/Λ_χ and m_η/Λ_χ are both approximately 1/2, and that many unknown low-energy constants at O(p^3) are neglected based on the resonance saturation argument that their contributions are much smaller than loop corrections.

## Method

The authors compute threshold T-matrices for all elastic s-wave meson-baryon scattering channels (πN, πΣ, πΞ, πΛ, KN, KΣ, KΞ, KΛ, ηN, ηΣ, ηΞ, ηΛ) to third chiral order in SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, using dimensional regularization with minimal subtraction. Tree-level contributions come from the leading-order (O(p)) and next-to-leading order (O(p^2)) Lagrangians, while the third-order contributions arise from one-loop diagrams with vertices from the leading-order meson-baryon and mesonic Lagrangians; O(p^3) counterterms are omitted following the resonance saturation approach of earlier work. Low-energy constants are determined by fitting to experimental kaon-nucleon and pion-nucleon scattering lengths and to octet baryon masses and the πN sigma term.

## Key numbers

- Extracted low-energy constants: C_1 = 1.786 GeV^{-1}, C_0 = 0.413 GeV^{-1} from experimental kaon-nucleon scattering lengths a_KN^{(1)} = -0.33 fm and a_KN^{(0)} = 0.02 fm
- Extracted C_π = 0.096^{+0.020}_{-0.048} GeV^{-1} from experimental a_πN^+ = -0.0001 ± 0.0021/0.0009 m_π^{-1}
- Baryon mass in chiral limit: M_0 = 820.00 ± 104.24 MeV (Set I, σ_πN = 45 ± 8 MeV) or M_0 = 663.86 ± 104.26 MeV (Set II, σ_πN = 57 ± 8 MeV)
- Low-energy constants b_D = 0.044 ± 0.008 GeV^{-1}, b_F = -0.491 ± 0.003 GeV^{-1} from baryon mass fits
- Meson masses used: m_π = 139.57 MeV, m_K = 493.68 MeV, m_η = 547.75 MeV; decay constants: f_π = 92.4 MeV, f_K = 113 MeV, f_η = 1.2 f_π
- Axial coupling constants: D = 0.8, F = 0.5, with D+F = g_A = 1.26

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of kaon-nucleon scattering lengths by Kaiser to all meson-baryon channels, including pion-hyperon, kaon-hyperon, and eta-baryon scattering lengths for which experimental data are very scarce. The analytical expressions are intended to aid chiral extrapolation of lattice QCD simulations of these scattering lengths. The paper notes that the DEAR experiment on kaonic hydrogen gave a_K^-p, but that direct determination of the two antikaon-nucleon scattering lengths requires further measurements, and that the existence of eta-mesic nuclei remains controversial due to uncertainty in the eta-nucleon scattering length. The authors provide two parameter sets corresponding to different values of the pion-nucleon sigma term (45 MeV and 57 MeV) to illustrate sensitivity.
