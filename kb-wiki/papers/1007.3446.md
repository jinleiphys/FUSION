# Observational Tests of Neutron Star Relativistic Mean Field Equations of State

> Set of neutron star observational results is used to test some selected equations of state of dense nuclear matter. The first observational result comes from the mass--baryon number relation for pulsar B of the double pulsar system J 0737--3039. The second one is based on the mass--radius relation coming from observation of the thermal radiation of the neutron star RX J 1856.35--3754. The third one follows the population analysis of isolated neutron star thermal radiation sources. The last one is the test of maximum mass. The equation of state of asymmetric nuclear matter is given by the parameterized form of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock mean field, and we test selected parameterizations that represent fits of full relativistic mean field calculation. We show that only one of them is capable to pass the observational tests. This equation of state represents the first equation of state that is able to explain all the mentioned observational tests, especially the very accurate test given by the double pulsar even if no mass loss is assumed.

## Key claim

Only one of the three tested parameterizations of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock mean field equation of state (parameterization H) is capable of passing all the observational tests considered, particularly the very accurate double pulsar test, even when no mass loss is assumed; the other parameterizations fail at least one test.

## Method

The authors constructed static, spherically symmetric neutron star models by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation using three parameterized forms (H, L, M) of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock mean field equation of state for asymmetric nuclear matter, with proton fraction determined from beta-equilibrium and charge neutrality including electrons and muons. They then compared the predicted mass-baryon number relation, mass-radius relation, maximum mass, and direct URCA cooling thresholds against four observational constraints: the double pulsar J 0737-3039, the thermal radiation of RX J 1856.35-3754, population analysis of isolated neutron star thermal sources, and the maximum mass limit.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses: parameterization H gives 2.18 solar masses, L gives 1.92 solar masses, M gives 1.62 solar masses.
- Direct URCA threshold densities: parameterization L at 0.457 fm^-3 (corresponding to 1.47 solar masses), parameterization M at 0.571 fm^-3 (corresponding to 1.39 solar masses).
- Pulsar B mass in double pulsar J 0737-3039: 1.2489 +/- 0.0007 solar masses; its baryonic mass must lie in the range 1.366 to 1.375 solar masses under the electron-capture supernova scenario.
- Apparent radius values used for RX J 1856.5-3754: 15.5 km, 16.8 km, and 20.9 km; only parameterization H can explain the 16.8 km estimate.
- PSR J1903+0327 mass: 1.74 +/- 0.04 solar masses (Champion 2008) or 1.67 +/- 0.01 solar masses (Freire 2009), giving a combined lower limit of about 1.66 solar masses at 2 sigma, ruling out parameterization M (maximum 1.62 solar masses).

## Context

This work extends the test suite of Klahn (2006) by applying four astrophysical observations to three parameterizations of the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock mean field equation of state from Kotulic Bunta and Gmuca (2003). The double pulsar test follows the formation scenario of Podsiadlowski (2005), and the RX J 1856.5-3754 constraints use the atmosphere models of Trumper (2004) and Pons (2002). The results enable future work including hyperon degrees of freedom and tests based on quasiperiodic oscillations in low-mass X-ray binaries using rotating neutron star models.
