# Strong suppression of Coulomb corrections to the cross section of e+e- pair production in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> The Coulomb corrections to the cross section of $e^+e^-$ pair production in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions are calculated in the next-to-leading approximation with respect to the parameter $L=\ln γ_Aγ_B$ ($γ_{A,B}$ are the Lorentz factors of colliding nuclei). We found considerable reduction of the Coulomb corrections even for large $γ_Aγ_B$ due to the suppression of the production of $e^+e^-$ pair with the total energy of the order of a few electron masses in the rest frame of one of the nuclei. Our result explains why the deviation from the Born result were not observed in the experiment at SPS.

## Key claim

The Coulomb corrections to the cross section of e+e- pair production in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions are strongly suppressed compared to the leading logarithmic approximation, even for very large Lorentz factors, because the next-to-leading term contains a large negative contribution from the region where the produced pair has total energy of order a few electron masses. This explains why no deviation from the Born result was observed in the SPS experiments.

## Method

The authors calculated the Coulomb correction σ^A (the correction with respect to nucleus A) in the next-to-leading logarithmic approximation with respect to the parameter L = ln(γ_A γ_B). They separated the cross section into an asymptotic part σ^A_as, obtained using the high-energy (ω→∞) limit of the photoproduction Coulomb corrections, and a correction δσ^A that accounts for the exact energy dependence of the Coulomb corrections to real-photon pair production at finite ω. The function G(Zα) was evaluated numerically using known results for the Coulomb corrections to photoproduction, including a bridging formula that interpolates between low-energy and high-energy limits.

## Key numbers

- For Pb-Pb collisions at LHC (γ ≈ 1.8×10^7, Z=82), σ^A/σ^A_LA ≈ 0.66, where σ^A_LA is the leading logarithmic approximation.
- For Au-Au collisions at RHIC (γ ≈ 2.3×10^4, Z=79), σ^A/σ^A_LA ≈ 0.42.
- For the SPS experiments (γ ≈ 200), the Coulomb corrections are much smaller than the leading logarithmic approximation due to strong compensation.
- The function G(Zα) varies slowly from -6.6 for Z=1 to -6.14 for Z=100.
- The ratio σ^A/σ^0 (Coulomb correction to Born cross section) in the next-to-leading approximation is small (≲ 5%), while the leading logarithmic approximation alone gives up to 20% at γ ∼ 1000.

## Context

This work resolves a long-standing contradiction between theoretical predictions and experimental results from Vane (1994, 1997) at SPS, where no Coulomb corrections were observed. Earlier work by Ivanov (1999) and Lee (2000a) had shown that the leading logarithmic Coulomb corrections were large, contradicting the earlier claim by Baltz (1998), Segev (1999), and Eichmann (1999) that the exact cross section coincides with the Born result. The present paper shows that the next-to-leading term, previously conjectured to be negligible by Gevorkyan (2003), is actually large and negative, strongly suppressing the Coulomb corrections. Combined with the σ^AB term from Lee (2001b), this completes the calculation of linear-in-L terms in the number-weighted cross section.
