# Correlated model for quasi-elastic responses in finite nuclear systems

> A model to calculate nuclear responses considering short-range correlation effects is presented. The model is applied to the study of electromagnetic responses induced by one-body operators. We calculate one- and two-nucleon emission responses and cross sections of the 16O and 40Ca nuclei in the quasi-elastic region, and we compare them with experimental data.

# Correlated model for quasi-elastic responses in finite nuclear systems

## Key claim

The authors present a truncated Correlated Basis Function (CBF) model that retains only terms containing a single correlation function, arguing that this simplification is justified by excellent agreement with full CBF/FHNC calculations for nuclear matter charge responses (Ama98). The model treats short-range correlations but explicitly does not consider collective nuclear excitations, restricting its validity to the quasi-elastic region where collective effects are negligible.

## Method

The authors construct nuclear excited states by applying a correlation operator G (built from scalar two-body correlation functions) to particle-hole excitations of a Slater determinant ground state. They calculate electromagnetic response functions for one-body charge and current operators by retaining only linked diagrams containing a single correlation line h = f² - 1, evaluating both one-particle one-hole (1p-1h) and two-particle two-hole (2p-2h) contributions. The responses are computed for ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca using multipole expansions of the correlation function and single-particle wave functions, with the two-nucleon emission treated as a genuine short-range correlation effect while neglecting two-body currents.

## Key numbers

- The model is applied to ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca nuclei in the quasi-elastic region
- The FHNC-SOC approximation provides solutions of the Schrödinger equation with an accuracy of about 1 MeV per nucleon on the binding energy (referenced from prior work)
- In the limit q → 0, the sum over all hole single-particle wave functions should provide the proper number of nucleons A (normalization condition)
- The correlation function h is expanded in Legendre polynomials: h(r_ij) = Σ_{L=0}^∞ h_L(r_i,r_j) P_L(cos θ_ij)

## Context

This work extends the CBF theory, previously well-tested in infinite nuclear matter (Wir88, Akm98), to describe excited states of finite nuclei. The model is inspired by nuclear matter response calculations of Fan87 and Fab89, and the truncation to single correlation lines was validated against full CBF/FHNC calculations in Ama98. The approach enables calculation of inclusive electron scattering cross sections in the quasi-elastic region for medium-mass nuclei, treating two-nucleon emission as a correlation effect, and complements prior work on ground states of ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca within the FHNC-SOC scheme (Fab00).
