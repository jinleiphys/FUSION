# Energy and Centrality Dependence of Rapidity Densities at RHIC

> The energy and centrality dependence of the charged multiplicity per participant nucleon is shown to be able to differentiate between final-state saturation and fixed scale pQCD models of initial entropy production in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The energy dependence is shown to test the nuclear enhancement of the mini-jet component of the initial conditions, while the centrality dependence provides a key test of whether gluon saturation is reached at RHIC energies. HIJING model predicts that the rapidity density per participant increases with centrality, while the saturation model prediction is essentially independent of centrality.

## Key claim

The energy and centrality dependence of the charged multiplicity per participant nucleon can differentiate between final-state saturation models and fixed-scale pQCD models of initial entropy production, but the paper's central claim is conditional: the centrality dependence provides a key test of whether gluon saturation is reached at RHIC energies, and the HIJING model predicts that the rapidity density per participant increases with centrality while the saturation model prediction is essentially independent of centrality.

## Method

The authors used the HIJING Monte Carlo model version 1.35, which incorporates pQCD to compute multiple mini-jet production and uses the Lund string model for soft beam jet fragmentation and hadronization. They compared HIJING predictions for charged particle rapidity density per participant pair as functions of collision energy and centrality (number of participants) against PHOBOS data for Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=65 and 130 AGeV, and against predictions from the EKRT final-state saturation model. The HIJING model parameters p0 and sigma_soft were fixed by fitting pp and pbar-p data, with p0 assumed independent of A or centrality.

## Key numbers

- For central Au+Au (b=0-3 fm) collisions, the averaged number of participants is <N_part> ≈ 350 and the averaged number of binary collisions per participant pair is 2<N_binary>/<N_part> ≈ 4.7 at RHIC energies.
- The soft component in HIJING gives approximately constant <n>_soft ≈ 1.3 charged particles per participant.
- The EKRT saturation model predicts dN_ch/dy(b=0) ≈ (2/3) * 1.16 * A^0.92 * (sqrt(s))^0.4, scaled by a factor 0.9 for comparison to pseudorapidity density.
- In the EKRT model, the saturation scale is p_sat ≈ 0.2 * A^0.13 * (sqrt(s))^0.19, at which dN_g/dy = p_sat^2 * R^2.
- HIJING without jet quenching gives dN_glue(p_T > 2 GeV)/dy ≈ 250 at sqrt(s)=130 AGeV for central collisions.

## Context

This work extends the comparison between pQCD-inspired models (HIJING by Wang and Gyulassy, 1991) and saturation models (EKRT by Eskola, Kajantie, Ruuskanen, Tuominen, 1999) for initial entropy production in heavy-ion collisions, using the first PHOBOS data from RHIC. The paper proposes that measuring the centrality dependence of charged multiplicity per participant can distinguish between these models, and that a threshold for saturation could be identified by looking for a region with negative derivative d(dN_ch/dη N_part)/dN_part ≤ 0 above some N_crit(sqrt(s)). This enables future experimental searches for gluon saturation at RHIC and LHC energies.
