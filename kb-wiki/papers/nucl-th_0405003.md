# Band structure effects for dripped neutrons in neutron star crust

> The outer layers of a neutron star are supposed to be formed of a solid Coulomb lattice of neutron rich nuclei. At densities above neutron drip density (about one thousandth of nuclear saturation density), this lattice is immersed in a neutron fluid. Bragg scattering of those dripped neutrons by the nuclei which has been usually neglected is investigated, within a simple mean field model with Bloch type boundary conditions. The main purpose of this work is to provide some estimates for the entrainment coefficients, as required for hydrodynamical two fluid simulations of neutron star crust, which relate the momentum of one fluid to the particle currents of the other two fluids. The implications for the equilibrium neutron star crust structure are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

Bragg scattering of dripped neutrons by the nuclear lattice in neutron star crust, usually neglected, can significantly enhance the effective neutron mass (up to ~15 times the bare mass) in the outer layers of the inner crust, though this enhancement diminishes at higher densities approaching the crust-core transition.

## Method

The author solved the single-particle Schrödinger equation with a periodic mean-field potential constructed from the Oyamatsu-Yamada model, imposing Bloch-type boundary conditions to account for the crystal lattice. The single-particle wavefunctions were expanded in plane waves with an energy cutoff, and the effective mass was computed from Fermi surface integrals using the Gilat-Raubenheimer method for body-centered cubic, face-centered cubic, and simple cubic lattices of spherical nuclei, as well as for slab, rod, and bubble phases.

## Key numbers

- Effective neutron mass reaches ~15 times the bare neutron mass at baryon density 0.03 fm^-3
- At baryon density 0.03 fm^-3, the Fermi energy is approximately 17.16 MeV (from direct integration) or about 18.02 MeV (from the approximate estimate, ~5% larger)
- The approximate Fermi energy estimate yields an effective mass about 20% lower (mm_star/mm ~ 14) at baryon density 0.03 fm^-3
- Mean Fermi velocity is about one third of the ideal gas Fermi velocity at baryon density 0.03 fm^-3, increasing to one half at 0.055 fm^-3
- Spin-orbit coupling parameters used: lambda_1 = 175.8 MeV.fm^5 and lambda_2 = 16.39 MeV.fm^5 (model I from Oyamatsu and Yamada)

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional band structure calculations of Oyamatsu and Yamada (1994) to three-dimensional cubic lattices, and complements the two-fluid description of neutron star cores based on Fermi liquid theory by Borumand et al (1996) and Comer et al (2003). The results provide the entrainment coefficients needed for hydrodynamical two-fluid simulations of neutron star crust, and suggest that the Wigner-Seitz approximation used in earlier Hartree-Fock calculations by Negele and Vautherin (1973) may be inadequate for describing dripped neutrons. Future work should incorporate spin-orbit coupling and pairing interactions for more realistic calculations.
