# Di-nucleon structures in homogeneous nuclear matter based on two- and three-nucleon interactions

> We investigate homogeneous nuclear matter within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) approach in the limits of isospin-symmetric nuclear matter (SNM) as well as pure neutron matter at zero temperature. The study is based on realistic representations of the internucleon interaction as given by Argonne v18, Paris, Nijmegen I and II potentials, in addition to chiral N$^{3}$LO interactions, including three-nucleon forces up to N$^{2}$LO. Particular attention is paid to the presence of di-nucleon bound states structures in $^1\textrm{S}_0$ and $^3\textrm{SD}_1$ channels, whose explicit account becomes crucial for the stability of self-consistent solutions at low densities. A characterization of these solutions and associated bound states is discussed. We confirm that coexisting BHF single-particle solutions in SNM, at Fermi momenta in the range $0.13-0.3$~fm$^{-1}$, is a robust feature under the choice of realistic internucleon potentials.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the coexistence of two distinct self-consistent single-particle solutions in symmetric nuclear matter at Fermi momenta between 0.13 and 0.3 fm⁻¹ is a robust feature that does not depend on the choice of realistic internucleon potential, extending a previous finding based only on the Argonne v18 interaction.

## Method

The authors performed Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) calculations at zero temperature for both isospin-symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter, using the continuous choice for the single-particle potential. They employed six different internucleon interactions: Argonne v18, Paris, Nijmegen I, Nijmegen II, chiral N³LO two-nucleon forces, and chiral N³LO plus N²LO three-nucleon forces, including partial waves up to total angular momentum J=7. The key methodological innovation was the explicit treatment of singularities in the G-matrix arising from in-medium di-nucleon bound states in the ¹S₀ and ³SD₁ channels during the self-consistency search.

## Key numbers

- Coexisting single-particle solutions in symmetric nuclear matter occur at Fermi momenta in the range 0.13 to 0.3 fm⁻¹, a feature robust across all six interactions.
- For symmetric nuclear matter, N³LO chiral interaction saturates at k_F = 1.85 fm⁻¹ with E/A = -25.7 MeV; N³LO+3N saturates at k_F = 1.30 fm⁻¹ with E/A = -12.1 MeV.
- AV18 and Paris potentials yield saturation energies of -16.8 and -16.3 MeV respectively, both near 1.4 times the empirical saturation density.
- In phase I of symmetric nuclear matter, effective masses reach up to approximately four times the bare nucleon mass near the maximum k_F of phase I.
- In pure neutron matter, the maximum effective mass ratio m*/m is about 1.2, occurring at Fermi momenta between 0.25 and 0.5 fm⁻¹.
- In-medium deuteron binding in phase I reaches about -4.5 MeV at the upper edge of phase I, nearly twice the free-space deuteron binding energy.
- Di-nucleon binding in the ¹S₀ channel for symmetric nuclear matter reaches a maximum of about 600 keV at k_F ≈ 0.28 fm⁻¹ in phase I.
- Di-neutron binding in pure neutron matter reaches a maximum near k_F ≈ 0.6 fm⁻¹, with Paris potential yielding the lowest binding of about -550 keV.
- The Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem is violated in BHF; the weakest violation occurs for N³LO+3N with E/A - e_F = 8.2 MeV at saturation.

## Context

This work extends the investigation of Arellano (2015), which first reported di-nucleon structures and coexisting single-particle solutions in symmetric nuclear matter using only the Argonne v18 potential. The present study demonstrates that these features are robust across multiple realistic interactions, including chiral potentials with three-nucleon forces. The results enable future work on incorporating higher-order correlations beyond BHF to satisfy the Hugenholtz-van Hove theorem, and provide a foundation for connecting in-medium di-nucleon structures to nuclear pairing phenomena and superfluid states as discussed by Broglia (2013) and Sedrakian (2006).
