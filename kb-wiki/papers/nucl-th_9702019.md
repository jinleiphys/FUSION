# Nuclear Polarizabilities and Logarithmic Sum Rules

# Nuclear Polarizabilities and Logarithmic Sum Rules

## Key claim
The electric polarizability and logarithmic mean-excitation energy of the deuteron are calculated using techniques adapted from atomic physics, yielding improved limits on the atomic-deuterium frequency shift due to nuclear polarization in the unretarded dipole approximation, but the authors note this is not a numerically complete result as higher multipoles, retarded dipole contributions, and seagull terms are not included and may decrease the result by up to 1 kHz.

## Method
The electric polarizability is computed by solving the inhomogeneous Schrödinger equation (Podolsky's method) for 14 realistic nucleon-nucleon potential models, including both first-generation and second-generation potentials. The logarithmic mean-excitation energy is obtained via a parametric integration trick originally developed for atomic Bethe logarithms, where a parameter is added to the Hamiltonian and the resulting polarizability as a function of that parameter is integrated numerically. The calculations are performed in the nonrelativistic dipole approximation using the standard electric-dipole operator.

## Key numbers
- Deuteron electric polarizability from second-generation potentials: α_E = 0.6328(17) fm³
- Polarization energy shift for the deuteron 1S-2S transition: ν_pol = 19.26(6) kHz
- Zero-range approximation for polarizability: α_E⁰ = 0.6378(12) fm³, using asymptotic normalization constant A_S = 0.8845(8) fm^{-1/2}
- Correction to zero-range approximation: Δα_E ≅ -0.0044(2) fm³, giving α_E = 0.6334(14) fm³
- Individual second-generation potential results range from α_E = 0.6311 fm³ (Nijmegen full-rel) to 0.6345 fm³ (Reid Soft Core 93), with ν_pol ranging from 19.21 kHz to 19.31 kHz
- Individual first-generation potential results range from α_E = 0.6237 fm³ (Reid Soft Core 68) to 0.6497 fm³ (Super Soft Core C), with ν_pol ranging from 18.99 kHz to 19.77 kHz

## Context
This work extends previous calculations of deuteron electric polarizability by Pachucki (1994) and Leidemann (1995) by using a larger set of potential models and novel numerical techniques from atomic physics. The results confirm the earlier predictions of Friar (1977) that α_E = 0.632(3) fm³, and provide improved theoretical uncertainty for the deuterium-hydrogen isotope shift measurements being performed at the time. The second-generation potentials (Nijmegen group potentials and Argonne V18) are recommended over first-generation models because their fits to nucleon-nucleon scattering data approach χ² per degree of freedom near 1, making them effectively alternative phase-shift analyses.
