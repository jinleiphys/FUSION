# Influence of tunneling on electron screening in low energy nuclear reactions in laboratories

> Using a semiclassical mean field theory, we show that the screening potential exhibits a characteristic radial variation in the tunneling region in sharp contrast to the assumption of the constant shift in all previous works. Also, we show that the explicit treatment of the tunneling region gives a larger screening energy than that in the conventional approach, which studies the time evolution only in the classical region and estimates the screening energy from the screening potential at the external classical turning point. This modification becomes important if the electronic state is not a single adiabatic state at the external turning point either by pre-tunneling transitions of the electronic state or by the symmetry of the system even if there is no essential change with the electronic state in the tunneling region.

## Key claim

Using a semiclassical mean field theory that treats both the classically allowed and tunneling regions consistently, the authors show that the screening potential exhibits a characteristic radial variation in the tunneling region, contrary to the constant-shift assumption of all previous works. The explicit treatment of the tunneling region gives a larger screening energy than the conventional approach that only studies time evolution in the classical region and estimates the screening energy from the screening potential at the external classical turning point.

## Method

The authors describe the time evolution of electrons by a Schrödinger equation and the relative motion between projectile and target nuclei by classical Newtonian equations, coupled through a variational principle leading to a mean field theory. Once the relative velocity becomes zero at the classical turning point, they switch to imaginary time and continue the time evolution in the tunneling region using modified equations. The screening potential is obtained as the total energy of the electrons at each separation distance, and the tunneling probability is calculated using a WKB formula, from which the screening energy is extracted.

## Key numbers

- For the D+d reaction at E_c.m. = 1 keV, the screening potential at the external classical turning point is -34.0 eV, which matches the average of the binding energies in the lowest gerade state (-54.4 eV) and ungerade state (-13.6 eV) of the united atom ^4He^+.
- The adiabatic limit screening energy for the D+d reaction is U_e^(AD) = 20.4 eV, derived from the binding energies of the united atom states.
- The sudden limit screening energy for the D+d reaction is U_e^(S) = 13.6 eV.
- For the D+d reaction, the external classical turning point in the presence of an electron is 0.0142 Å at E_K^∞ = 1.0 keV, compared to 0.0144 Å without an electron.
- The mean square radius of the electron at the external classical turning point for the D+d reaction is 0.688 Å at E_K^∞ = 1.0 keV, 0.518 Å at 10.0 keV, and 0.456 Å at 100.0 keV.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical approach of Shoppa, Koonin, Langanke, and Seki (1993, 1996), who solved coupled equations for electronic and nuclear motions only in the classical region and estimated the screening energy using the electronic wave function at the external classical turning point. The authors show that the conventional method underestimates the screening energy because the fusion cross section is a convex increasing function of incident energy, so averaging the tunneling probability over different electronic states (as in the present method) yields a larger enhancement than using the average potential. The effects demonstrated are too small to explain the large experimental screening energies reported by Rolfs and Somorjai (1995), and the large screening energy found for the D+d reaction is caused by the symmetry special to this system and cannot be generalized to other systems.
