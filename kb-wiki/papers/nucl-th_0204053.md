# Isobaric multiplet yrast energies and isospin non-conserving forces

> The isovector and isotensor energy differences between yrast states of isobaric multiplets in the lower half of the $pf$ region are quantitatively reproduced in a shell model context. The isospin non-conserving nuclear interactions are found to be at least as important as the Coulomb potential. Their isovector and isotensor channels are dominated by J=2 and J=0 pairing terms, respectively. The results are sensitive to the radii of the states, whose evolution along the yrast band can be accurately followed.

# Isobaric multiplet yrast energies and isospin non-conserving forces

## Key claim
The isovector and isotensor energy differences between yrast states of isobaric multiplets in the lower half of the pf shell are quantitatively reproduced in a shell model context, but only when isospin non-conserving nuclear interactions are included at least as strongly as the Coulomb potential. The abstract's claim of quantitative reproduction holds for A=47, 49, 50, and 51, but explicitly fails for A=46, where the full pf shell diagonalizations provide poor spectroscopy.

## Method
The authors perform exact, isospin-conserving shell model calculations in the full pf shell using the KB3G interaction with a single-particle spectrum from 41Ca. Energy differences (MED and TED) are obtained in first-order perturbation theory as sums of expectation values, separating monopole and multipole components of the Coulomb field. The isospin non-conserving nuclear interaction V_B is parameterized using only the leading terms suggested by A=42 data: a J=2 pairing term for the isovector channel and a J=0 pairing term for the isotensor channel, scaled from f_{7/2} matrix elements to the full pf shell via a multiplicative prescription.

## Key numbers
- The monopole radial parameter a_m is taken as 300 keV, estimated from adding the observed Thomas-Ehrman shift (200 keV) to the single-particle splitting (125 keV at A=40, Z_cs=20).
- The isovector and isotensor nuclear interaction parameters are both set to β_1 = β_2 = 100 keV (round numbers).
- In A=42, the bare Coulomb matrix elements V_C^ho in the f_{7/2} subshell are: 81.60 keV (J=0), 24.60 keV (J=2), -6.40 keV (J=4), -11.40 keV (J=6).
- The observed MED minus bare Coulomb contribution in A=42 (isovector channel) gives: 5.38 keV (J=0), 92.55 keV (J=2), 4.57 keV (J=4), -47.95 keV (J=6).
- The observed TED minus bare Coulomb contribution in A=42 (isotensor channel) gives: 116.76 keV (J=0), 80.76 keV (J=2), 2.83 keV (J=4), -42.15 keV (J=6).
- The single-particle splitting from Eq. (5) depresses l=3 orbits relative to l=1 by 125 keV at A=40.

## Context
This work extends the study of mirror energy differences (MED) from earlier work on A=47, 49, 50, and 51 by Zuker (2001) and incorporates new isotensor triplet energy difference (TED) data from 50Mn and 46Cr. It directly addresses the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly, which was previously resolved only to within shell effects by Brown (2000) and Duflo (2002). The results show that isospin non-conserving nuclear forces dominate over the Coulomb potential in these excitation energy differences, contrary to the traditional assumption that Coulomb effects are primary. The work enables future rigorous treatment using charge symmetry breaking potentials (Machleidt, 2001) and charge independence breaking potentials (Machleidt, 2001b) to confirm the dominance of J=2 pairing in the isovector channel and J=0 pairing in the isotensor channel.
