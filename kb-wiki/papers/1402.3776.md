# Charge-exchange scattering to the isobaric analog state at medium energies as a probe of the neutron skin

> The charge-exchange (3He,t) scattering to the isobaric analog state (IAS) of the target can be considered as "elastic" scattering of 3He by the isovector term of the optical potential (OP) that flips the projectile isospin. Therefore, the accurately measured charge-exchange scattering cross- section for the IAS can be a good probe of the isospin dependence of the OP, which is determined exclusively within the folding model by the difference between the neutron and proton densities and isospin dependence of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. Given the neutron skin of the target is related directly to the same density difference, it can be well probed in the analysis of the charge- exchange (3He,t) reactions at medium energies when the two-step processes can be neglected and the t-matrix interaction can be used in the folding calculation. For this purpose, the data of the (3He,t) scattering to the IAS of 90Zr and 208Pb targets at Elab = 420 MeV have been analyzed in the distorted wave Born approximation using the double-folded charge-exchange form factor. The neutron skin deduced for these two nuclei turned out to be in a good agreement with the existing database.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of a target nucleus can be probed by analyzing charge-exchange (³He,t) scattering to the isobaric analog state at medium energies, provided that two-step processes are negligible and a t-matrix interaction is used in the folding calculation; the neutron skin values deduced for ⁹⁰Zr and ²⁰⁸Pb from this analysis agree with the existing database, but the method cannot rule out the larger neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb given by the PREX measurement.

## Method

The differential cross sections for (³He,t) scattering to the isobaric analog state of ⁹⁰Zr and ²⁰⁸Pb at Elab = 420 MeV were analyzed in the distorted wave Born approximation using a double-folded charge-exchange form factor. The form factor was constructed from the isovector term of the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix interaction (Franey-Love parametrization) and the difference between neutron and proton ground-state densities of the projectile and target. The radial parameter of the empirical neutron density from Ray et al. (1978) was adjusted to obtain the best DWBA fit to the data, and the corresponding neutron skin was extracted.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin of ⁹⁰Zr: ΔRnp ≈ 0.09 ± 0.03 fm, deduced from the best DWBA fit to (³He,t) data at Elab = 420 MeV, with uncertainty from the 10% experimental uncertainty in absolute cross sections at forward angles.
- Neutron skin of ²⁰⁸Pb: ΔRnp ≈ 0.16 ± 0.04 fm, deduced from the best DWBA fit to (³He,t) data at Elab = 420 MeV, with uncertainty from the 10% experimental uncertainty in absolute cross sections at forward angles.
- The microscopic HFB density gave ΔRnp = 0.07 fm for ⁹⁰Zr and ΔRnp = 0.17 fm for ²⁰⁸Pb, and the DWBA results using these densities agreed reasonably with the data.
- The PREX measurement gave ΔRnp ≈ 0.33 +0.16 −0.18 fm for ²⁰⁸Pb; the lower edge of this range agreed with the (³He,t) data.

## Context

This work extends the use of charge-exchange reactions to probe the isovector density and neutron skin, building on the Lane model for the optical potential and the Satchler prescription for the charge-exchange form factor. The results for ⁹⁰Zr agree with the neutron skin deduced from spin-dipole sum rule analysis by Sagawa et al. (2006) and from elastic proton scattering by Ray et al. (1978). For ²⁰⁸Pb, the result (ΔRnp ≈ 0.16 fm) agrees with structure studies such as the electric dipole polarizability analysis by Roca-Maza et al. (2013) and is lower than the PREX result; the authors note that a planned new PREX experiment with uncertainty reduced to about 0.06 fm could resolve the discrepancy. The method enables future predictions for (p,n) scattering to the IAS at medium energies as an alternative probe of the neutron skin.
