# Three-loop HTL QCD thermodynamics

> The hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) framework is used to calculate the thermodynamic functions of a quark-gluon plasma to three-loop order. This is the highest order accessible by finite temperature perturbation theory applied to a non-Abelian gauge theory before the high-temperature infrared catastrophe. All ultraviolet divergences are eliminated by renormalization of the vacuum, the HTL mass parameters, and the strong coupling constant. After choosing a prescription for the mass parameters, the three-loop results for the pressure and trace anomaly are found to be in very good agreement with recent lattice data down to $T \sim 2-3\,T_c$, which are temperatures accessible by current and forthcoming heavy-ion collision experiments.

## Key claim

The hard-thermal-loop perturbation theory (HTLpt) framework yields three-loop thermodynamic functions for QCD that agree with lattice data down to temperatures of approximately 2–3 times the critical temperature, but only after a specific prescription is chosen for the mass parameters, because the variational gap equations produce a complex Debye mass at this order.

## Method

The authors computed the thermodynamic potential of QCD to three-loop (NNLO) order in HTLpt by evaluating all contributing Feynman diagrams in the imaginary-time formalism, expanding the resulting sum-integrals in powers of the Debye mass \(m_D\) and thermal quark mass \(m_q\) over temperature, and retaining all terms through order \(g^5\). Ultraviolet divergences were removed by renormalizing the vacuum energy, the HTL mass parameters, and the strong coupling constant, using counterterms derived from the one-loop running of QCD. The final thermodynamic potential was obtained by summing the pure-glue contributions from earlier work with the new quark and quark-gluon interaction diagrams specific to full QCD.

## Key numbers

* The three-loop HTLpt pressure agrees with lattice data for temperatures \(T \sim 2-3\,T_c\), where \(T_c \sim 170\) MeV is the crossover temperature for deconfinement.
* The strong coupling constant at RHIC temperatures is \(\alpha_s(\mu=2\pi\times170\,{\rm MeV}) \sim 0.43\), corresponding to \(g \sim 2\).
* The LHC is expected to yield initial temperatures of \(4-6\,T_c\), driving the running coupling further down.
* The three-loop contribution to the thermodynamic potential includes a term proportional to \(s_{2F}(\alpha_s/\pi)^2\) with a numerical coefficient \((15/64)(35-32\log2)\).
* The running of the coupling constant is given by \(\mu \, d g^2 / d \mu = -(11c_A-4s_F)g^4/(24\pi^2)\).

## Context

This work extends the NNLO HTLpt calculations previously performed for pure-glue QCD by Andersen et al. (2010) and for QED by Andersen et al. (2009) to full QCD with quarks. It builds upon the NLO HTLpt results for QCD by Andersen et al. (2002, 2003) and the weak-coupling expansion through order \(g^6\log g\) by Kajantie et al. (2002). The results demonstrate that lattice data at temperatures around \(2\,T_c\) are consistent with a quasiparticle description of the quark-gluon plasma, providing a test of the quasiparticle picture in the intermediate coupling regime where \(g \sim 2\). The calculation enables comparisons with upcoming LHC data at higher temperatures and with lattice results from Bazavov et al. (2009) and Borsanyi et al. (2010).
