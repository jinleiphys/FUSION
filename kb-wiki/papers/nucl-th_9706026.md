# Lepton pair production in heavy ion collisions and hadronic phenomenology

> We first review the calculations of low invariant mass dilepton production in relativistic heavy ion collisions, using effective hadronic Lagrangians. We go through some of the theoretical techniques used in this kinematical region and we consider some of the appropriate experimental measurements. Moving up to the intermediate invariant mass region, we point out some of the uncertainties that show up in theoretical estimates. Those originate mainly from off-shell effects. Finally, as an application of hadronic chiral Lagrangians at finite temperature, we compute the mass shifts and mixing of the omega and phi mesons due to scattering from thermal pions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that while low-mass dilepton production rates from different theoretical approaches show striking agreement, the intermediate-mass region suffers from large uncertainties (spanning three orders of magnitude) due to off-shell effects in effective chiral Lagrangians, though available phenomenology can help narrow down the possibilities. The abstract glosses over the fact that the low-mass agreement is explicitly shown only for specific calculations and that the intermediate-mass uncertainties are demonstrated through a pedagogical exercise rather than a definitive resolution.

## Method

The author reviews calculations of low invariant mass dilepton production using effective hadronic Lagrangians and Vector Meson Dominance, comparing rates from meson reactions/decays, chiral reduction formulae, and experimentally-extracted spectral functions. For the intermediate mass region, the paper computes dilepton production from the reaction π + a₁ → e⁺ e⁻ using four different effective chiral Lagrangians and compares the results against rates from experimentally-fitted spectral functions. Finally, the mass shifts and mixing of ω and φ mesons due to scattering from thermal pions are computed using one-loop finite-temperature self-energies from effective Lagrangians with ρπ and b₁π interactions.

## Context

This paper extends the line of work on electromagnetic probes of hot hadronic matter, building on earlier dilepton calculations by Gale and Kapusta (1991), and the author's own work with Song and Ko (1994). It addresses the low-mass dilepton excess reported by the CERES collaboration (Drees, 1996) and the intermediate-mass excess, comparing with calculations using experimentally-extracted spectral functions by Huang (1995) and chiral reduction formulae by Steele (1996). The work enables future discrimination between competing theoretical explanations for the CERES data, including dropping ρ mass models (Li, Ko, Brown, 1996) and in-medium baryon effects (Rapp, Chanfray, Wambach, 1997), and suggests that ω mass shifts from thermal pion scattering may be detectable in future experiments.
