# Neutrino quasielastic interaction and nuclear dynamics

> We investigate the double differential neutrino-carbon quasielastic cross sections as measured by the MiniBooNE experiment. Our present treatment incorporates relativistic corrections in the nuclear response functions and includes the multinucleon component. We confirm our previous conclusion that it is possible to account for all the data without any modification of the axial mass. We also introduce the Q^2 distribution for charged and neutral current. The data point at a sizable multinucleon component beside the genuine quasielastic peak. They are also indicative of the collective character of the nuclear response, of interest for hadronic physics.

## Key claim

The authors confirm that the MiniBooNE quasielastic cross section data on carbon can be fully accounted for without any modification of the axial mass (keeping the free value \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV), provided that multinucleon (np-nh) processes and relativistic corrections to the nuclear response functions are included.

## Method

The authors compute the double differential charged-current neutrino-carbon cross section using a random phase approximation (RPA) framework with a particle-hole force, incorporating a multinucleon (np-nh) component parametrized from the work of Alberico et al. (1983) on the 2p-2h transverse response and from Oset (1987) for 3p-3h contributions. Relativistic corrections are implemented via the substitution \(\omega \to \omega (1 + \omega / 2M_N)\) in the nuclear responses and a multiplicative factor \((1 + \omega / M_N)\), following prescriptions from Barbaro et al. (1995) and De Pace et al. (1998). The theoretical cross sections are convolved with the MiniBooNE neutrino flux and compared to the measured double differential cross section as a function of muon kinetic energy and scattering angle, as well as to single differential and \(Q^2\) distributions for both charged and neutral currents.

## Key numbers

* The free axial mass value used is \(M_A = 1.03\) GeV, as measured in deuteron bubble chamber experiments, in contrast to the value \(M_A = 1.35\) GeV required by a relativistic Fermi gas model without multinucleon contributions.
* The MiniBooNE neutrino energy extends to \(\simeq 2\) GeV.
* The double differential cross section is shown for muon kinetic energy bins such as \(0.4\) GeV \(< T_\mu < 0.5\) GeV.
* The single differential cross section as a function of muon scattering angle is integrated over muon kinetic energies \(0.2\) GeV \(< T_\mu < 2.0\) GeV.
* The \(Q^2\) distribution shows a large region \(Q^2 \gtrsim 0.2\) GeV\(^2\) where RPA has practically no influence, allowing the np-nh contribution to be isolated.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Martini et al., 2009, 2010) by incorporating relativistic corrections and analyzing the double differential cross section, which is free from the uncertainty of neutrino energy reconstruction. It addresses criticisms by Amaro et al. (2011) regarding the non-relativistic treatment. The results support the conclusion that multinucleon emission, rather than an increased axial mass, resolves the MiniBooNE quasielastic anomaly, consistent with recent findings by Nieves et al. (2011). The study also suggests that neutrino reactions may provide a signature for the Lorentz-Lorenz quenching effect in the spin-isospin nuclear response, a long-sought effect in hadronic physics that has not been clearly established in electron scattering data.
