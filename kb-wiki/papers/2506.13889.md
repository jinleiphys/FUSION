# Deep-Inelastic Scattering at TeV Energies with LHC Muons

> The LHC far-forward experiments FASER and SND@LHC have pioneered the detection of TeV-energy neutrinos produced in hard-scattering proton-proton collisions at the LHC. In addition to neutrinos, an intense flux of TeV-energy muons reaches these detectors, representing a dominant background for both neutrino studies and beyond the Standard Model searches. Here we demonstrate that this forward muon flux enables a comprehensive neutral-current deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) program at FASER with a strong kinematical overlap with the Electron Ion Collider. For the Run 3 luminosity of $\mathcal{L}_{\rm pp}=250$~fb$^{-1}$, more than $10^5$ inclusive muon DIS events, of which up to $10^4$ from charm production, are expected at FASER$ν$. As a representative application, we demonstrate the sensitivity of muon DIS at FASER$ν$ to probe the (intrinsic) charm content of the proton at large-$x$. We also provide predictions for event yields of muon DIS for future FASER runs and for the proposed Forward Physics Facility.

## Key claim
The forward muon flux at LHC far-forward experiments, previously considered only as a background, can enable a comprehensive neutral-current deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) program at FASERnu with strong kinematical overlap with the Electron Ion Collider, provided that event selection and kinematic reconstruction are optimized to retain sensitivity to large-x physics.

## Method
The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations using POWHEG+Pythia8 to model muon neutral-current DIS events at FASERnu, applying acceptance and selection cuts based on the FASERnu detector geometry and vertex finding algorithms. They computed inclusive and charm-tagged event yields for Run 3 luminosity of 250 fb^-1, comparing predictions from multiple PDF sets (NNPDF4.0 and CT18 families) with both perturbative and fitted charm treatments. The analysis included studies of systematic uncertainties from muon momentum resolution (modeled as Gaussian smearing of 10% and 30%) and dependence on the charged-track multiplicity cut (n_tr >= 1, 3, or 5).

## Key numbers
- More than 10^5 inclusive muon DIS events expected at FASERnu for Run 3 luminosity of 250 fb^-1
- Up to 10^4 charm production events expected at FASERnu for the same luminosity
- For x >= 0.2, NNPDF4.0 predicts 153.2 charm events with fitted charm versus 20.0 with perturbative charm (ratio 7.7)
- For x >= 0.4, NNPDF4.0 predicts 19.3 charm events with fitted charm versus 1.0 with perturbative charm (ratio 19)
- For x >= 0.2, CT18 FC predicts 324.3 charm events with fitted charm versus 40.2 with perturbative charm (ratio 8.1)
- For x >= 0.4, CT18 FC predicts 23.0 charm events with fitted charm versus 2.0 with perturbative charm (ratio 12)
- Charm tagging efficiency adopted as epsilon_c = 0.7
- Semi-leptonic branching ratio for charm hadron decay: BR(c -> l+ + X) = 0.096 +/- 0.004
- With 10% momentum smearing, the ratio for x_reco >= 0.2 reduces to 2.1 (104.6 vs 50.6 events)
- With 30% momentum smearing, the ratio for x_reco >= 0.2 reduces to 1.3 (81.4 vs 60.6 events)

## Context
This work extends the physics program of the LHC far-forward experiments FASER and SND@LHC, which have pioneered TeV-energy neutrino detection, by demonstrating that the intense forward muon flux can be used for DIS measurements rather than treated only as background. The proposed muon DIS program at FASERnu has strong kinematical overlap with the future Electron Ion Collider and provides complementary sensitivity to the charm content of the proton at large x, where previous measurements from HERA (H1 2018) had excellent small-x coverage but could not access large-x. The only existing large-x measurement of the charm structure function F2^c comes from the EMC analysis (Aubert 1982), which claimed evidence for intrinsic charm but has been contested (reviewed by Brodsky 2015). The study shows that FASERnu can discriminate between PDF sets with fitted charm (NNPDF4.0, CT18 FC variants from Guzzi 2022) and perturbative charm, enabling tests of intrinsic charm models, though detector resolution effects significantly impact sensitivity and require optimized event selection.
