# Perturbation theory and non-perturbative renormalization flow in scalar field theory at finite temperature

> We use the non-perturbative renormalization group to clarify some features of perturbation theory in thermal field theory. For the specific case of the scalar field theory with O(N) symmetry, we solve the flow equations within the local potential approximation. This approximation reproduces the perturbative results for the screening mass and the pressure up to order g^3, and starts to differ at order g^4. The method allows a smooth extrapolation to the regime where the coupling is not small, very similar to that obtained from a simple self-consistent approximation.

## Key claim

The non-perturbative renormalization group (NPRG) within the local potential approximation reproduces perturbative results for the screening mass and pressure of O(N) scalar field theory up to order g^3, but begins to differ at order g^4, while providing a smooth extrapolation to strong coupling that closely matches a simple self-consistent 2PI approximation.

## Method

The authors solved the exact NPRG flow equations for the effective potential of O(N) scalar field theory at finite temperature, using the local potential approximation (LPA) with a regulator that depends only on spatial momentum components. They integrated the flow from a microscopic scale Λ down to κ=0, first in vacuum to fix renormalized parameters (vanishing mass, coupling defined at scale μ=2πT), then at finite temperature to obtain the screening mass and pressure. The results were compared to standard perturbation theory expansions up to order g^5 and to a self-consistent Φ-derivable two-loop approximation.

## Key numbers

- Screening mass expansion: m_D² = g²T² {1 - 3g/π - (9/2)[ln(μ̄/2πT) - (4/3)ln(g/π) - 2.415](g/π)² + O(g³)} (Eq. mpert)
- Pressure expansion: P/P₀ = 1 - (15/8)(g/π)² + (15/2)(g/π)³ + (135/16)[ln(μ̄/2πT) + 0.4046](g/π)⁴ - (405/8)[ln(μ̄/2πT) - (4/3)ln(g/π) - 0.9908](g/π)⁵ + O(g⁶ log g) (Eq. Pphi), where P₀ = π²T⁴/90
- The g³ term in the screening mass equals -3g³T²/π (Eq. pertg3)
- The one-loop β-function: μ ∂g²/∂μ = (9/2π²)g⁴ + O(g⁶) (Eq. betafunction)
- The Landau pole scale: Λ_L = 2πT exp(2π²/9g²) (Eq. LandauPole)
- The bound on admissible coupling: g² < 2π²/[9 log(Λ/2πT)] (Eq. couplingbound)

## Context

This work extends the understanding of why thermal perturbation theory converges poorly, even for scalar field theory where the problem is less severe than in QCD. The authors connect the NPRG approach to earlier resummation methods including screened perturbation theory (Karsch 1997), hard thermal loop perturbation theory (Andersen 2000-2004), and the 2PI Φ-derivable approach (Blaizot 1999-2000). The results enable systematic study of how the hierarchy of scales (hard modes at T, soft modes at gT) survives into the strong-coupling regime, and provide a framework for testing whether the LPA captures the same physics as dimensional reduction (Braaten 1995, Kajantie 2000) and self-consistent approximations.
