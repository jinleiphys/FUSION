# Precise determination of the $ηΛ$ scattering length and effective range and relationship to the $Λ(1670)$ resonance

> We use the Belle data on the $K^- p$ mass distribution of the $Λ_c^+ \to p K^- π^+$ reaction near the $ηΛ$ threshold to determine the $ηΛ$ scattering length and effective range. We show that from these data alone we can determine the value of $a$ with better precision than so far determined, and the value of $r_0$ for the first time. The addition of the $K^- p \to ηΛ$ data allows us to improve the precision of these magnitudes, with errors smaller than $15\%$. We also determine with high precision the pole position of the $Λ(1670)$.

## Key claim

The Belle data on the $\Lambda_c^+ \to p K^- \pi^+$ reaction alone can determine the $\eta\Lambda$ scattering length with better precision than previously achieved and determine the effective range for the first time; when combined with $K^- p \to \eta\Lambda$ data, the errors on both quantities become smaller than 15%.

## Method

The authors fit the $K^- p$ invariant mass distribution from Belle (2022) and the $K^- p \to \eta\Lambda$ cross section from Starostin (2000) using a chiral unitary approach in coupled channels (10 channels including $K^- p$, $\bar{K}^0 n$, $\eta\Lambda$, and others). The transition potentials are derived from chiral Lagrangians with flexibility in the meson decay constants $f_i$ and cutoff parameters $q_{\mathrm{max},i}$, and the scattering length $a$ and effective range $r_0$ are extracted from the $\eta\Lambda \to \eta\Lambda$ amplitude at threshold via the effective range expansion. Parameter uncertainties are determined using the resampling (bootstrap) method with about 100 random data sets.

## Key numbers

* $\eta\Lambda$ scattering length from Belle data alone: $a = -(0.70 \pm 0.16) - i(0.75 \pm 0.24)$ fm (errors ~25-30%)
* $\eta\Lambda$ effective range from Belle data alone: $r_0 = -(4.98 \pm 0.96) + i(1.05 \pm 0.35)$ fm (first determination)
* Combined fit (Belle + $K^- p \to \eta\Lambda$): $a = -(0.58 \pm 0.09) - i(0.59 \pm 0.04)$ fm (errors <15%)
* Combined fit effective range: $r_0 = -(6.68 \pm 0.93) + i(2.10 \pm 0.34)$ fm
* $\Lambda(1670)$ pole position: $M = 1669.40 \pm 1.06$ MeV, $\Gamma/2 = 21.46 \pm 1.76$ MeV

## Context

This work extends prior determinations of the $\eta\Lambda$ scattering length from Chiang (1988) and Nefkens (2005), which had large errors and did not extract the effective range. The chiral unitary approach used here builds on the framework of Kaiser (1995), Oller (2000), and Oset (1998) that simultaneously describes the $\Lambda(1405)$ and $\Lambda(1670)$ resonances. The high-resolution cusp from Belle (2022) enables a precision comparable to the classic determination of $\pi\pi$ scattering lengths from kaon decays (Batley 2009, Colangelo 2006). The results enable more precise tests of chiral dynamics in the strangeness $S=-1$ sector and improve constraints on the $\Lambda(1670)$ resonance parameters for future analyses of related reactions such as $\gamma p \to K^+ \eta \Lambda$.
