# Approach to nuclear cross sections via classical Skyrmion scattering: A proposal

> By analogy with heavy ion collisions, which can be modelled by a classical hydrodynamics, it is proposed that the differential cross section for collisions of smaller nuclei can be calculated from the classical, numerical scattering data for Skyrmions. It is also suggested that the numerical data for the outgoing particles should be classified into bins, as is done in nuclear physics experiments.

## Key claim
The paper proposes that differential cross sections for collisions of small nuclei (even nucleon pairs) can be calculated from classical numerical Skyrmion scattering data, provided the collision velocity is sufficiently high that the collision time is much shorter than the Skyrmion rotation time (v ≫ 0.025 in Skyrme units) and the orbital angular momentum is much larger than ℏ.

## Method
The author suggests performing random classical Skyrmion scattering simulations with uniformly distributed initial orientations and impact parameters, then binning the outgoing particle angles into finite-width solid-angle bins as done in nuclear physics experiments. For break-up reactions such as proton-deuteron collisions, the outgoing particles would be classified into bins of solid angle and an additional kinematic variable S (the ratio of outgoing proton energies), with the differential cross section estimated as N(Ω)/(F × ΔΩ) for elastic scattering or N(Ω₁,Ω₂,S)/(F × ΔΩ₁ ΔΩ₂ ΔS) for break-up reactions.

## Key numbers
- ℏ ≈ 30 in Skyrme units (where length unit ≈ 1 fm and energy unit ≈ 6 MeV)
- Rotation time of a B=1 Skyrmion in a spin-half nucleon state is 20 (in Skyrme time units), corresponding to angular velocity ≈ 0.3
- Required collision velocity for classical orientation treatment: v ≫ 0.025, with typical non-relativistic velocities in range 0.2 – 0.5
- Skyrmion mass M ≈ 140 in Skyrme units (M ≈ 1.2 × 12π²)
- Impact parameter cut-off for B=1 collisions: 2.8 fm (≈ 2.8 Skyrme units)
- Space star occurs at lab-frame polar angle ≈ 55° for normal kinematics or ≈ 27° for inverse kinematics
- Recent experiments explore polar angles 13° – 33° for normal kinematics

## Context
This proposal extends the classical hydrodynamical modelling of heavy ion collisions (HeavyIon, year not given) to lower-energy Skyrmion collisions, building on earlier partial implementations by Braaten (year not given) and the author's own previous work (Man, year not given). The approach contrasts with earlier attempts to construct a 2-Skyrmion moduli space with metric and potential (AtMan, year not given) for quantum scattering calculations, which have made little progress. The paper specifically targets the symmetric space star anomaly in proton-deuteron break-up reactions (Wil, year not given; Wit, year not given), where existing nuclear force theories fail to fit the differential cross section, and suggests Skyrmions may provide a new theoretical framework for understanding this phenomenon.
