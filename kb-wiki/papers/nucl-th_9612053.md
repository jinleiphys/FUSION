# Thermal Masses and Equilibrium Rates in the Quark Gluon Phase

> We apply the momentum integrated Boltzmann transport equation to study the time evolution of various quark flavours in the central region of ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions. The effects of thermal masses for quarks and gluons are incorporated to take into account the in-medium properties of these ingredients of the putative quark gluon plasma. We find that even under very optimistic conditions, complete chemical equilibration in the quark gluon plasma appears unlikely.

## Key claim

Even under optimistic conditions where gluons have fully thermalized early, complete chemical equilibration of quark flavors in the quark-gluon plasma appears unlikely when in-medium thermal masses are included; the paper's abstract states this conclusion, but the full text reveals that the claim is conditional on the specific initial conditions used (pQCD or HIJING estimates) and that at SPS energies with complete stopping, u and d quarks may not be too far from equilibrium.

## Method

The authors integrated the momentum-integrated Boltzmann transport equation for number density evolution of quark flavors, coupled with a Bjorken boost-invariant hydrodynamic expansion, to study chemical equilibration from the time of gluon thermalization onward. They included both gluon fusion (gg → qq̅) and gluon decay (g → qq̅) channels, incorporating thermal quark masses m_q^eff(T) = sqrt(m_current^2 + m_th^2) where m_th depends on the non-equilibrium quark density. The coupled equations for quark density and temperature were solved self-consistently using the Runge-Kutta method for initial conditions derived from either perturbative QCD structure functions or the HIJING algorithm.

## Key numbers

- Initial quark densities at RHIC (T_g = 500 MeV, τ_g = 0.3 fm/c): n_q(τ_g) = 1.26 fm⁻³ from pQCD; 0.7 fm⁻³ from HIJING
- Initial quark densities at LHC (T_g = 660 MeV, τ_g = 0.25 fm/c): n_q(τ_g) = 1.33 fm⁻³ from pQCD; 2.8 fm⁻³ from HIJING
- At RHIC, initial r_q (n_q/n_eq) from pQCD is higher than from HIJING, making equilibrium even less likely in the HIJING case
- For SPS with complete stopping, initial r_u = 0.98 and r_d = 1.06 for central Pb+Pb collisions, including valence and sea contributions
- An alternative estimate using production cross sections gives n_q(τ_g) ~ 0.48 fm⁻³ at RHIC, compared to 1.26 fm⁻³ from pQCD

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier kinetic equilibration study (Alam, Raha, Sinha 1994) to chemical equilibration, and contrasts with earlier expectations by Matsui, Svetitsky, McLerran (1986) and Rafelski, Letessier, Tounsi (1995) that s quarks would come to equilibrium at SPS energies. The key difference is the inclusion of thermal masses for quarks and gluons, which the authors show nontrivially affect the conclusions. The paper enables future work on the implications of incomplete chemical equilibration for QGP diagnostic signals such as particle emission from the pre-equilibrium era.
