# Cascade production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies

> Production of the doubly strange $Ξ$ baryon in heavy-ion collisions at \textrm{SIS} energies is studied in a relativistic transport model that includes perturbatively the strangeness-exchange reactions $\bar{K}Λ\to πΞ$ and $\bar{K}Σ\to πΞ$. Taking the cross sections for these reactions from the predictions of a hadronic model, we find that the $Ξ$ yield is about $10^{-4}$ in central collisions of $% ^{58}$Ni + $^{58}$Ni at $E/A=1.93$ \textrm{GeV}. The $Ξ$ yield is further found to be more sensitive to the magnitude of the cross sections for strangeness-exchange reactions than to the medium effects due to modified kaon properties. We have also made predictions for $Ξ$ production in Au+Au collisions at energies from 1 to 2 GeV/nucleon.

# Cascade production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies

## Key claim

Subthreshold Xi baryon production in heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies (1-2 GeV/nucleon) proceeds via strangeness-exchange reactions (anti-K Lambda -> pi Xi and anti-K Sigma -> pi Xi), and the final Xi yield is more sensitive to the magnitude of these reaction cross sections than to medium effects from modified kaon properties, meaning the yield does not reach chemical equilibrium under these conditions.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic Vlasov-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RVUU) transport model treating nucleons, Delta resonances, and pions explicitly, with kaons, antikaons, hyperons, and Xi baryons treated perturbatively. Xi production was included via the strangeness-exchange reactions anti-K Lambda -> pi Xi and anti-K Sigma -> pi Xi, with cross sections taken from a coupled-channel approach based on a flavor SU(3)-invariant hadronic Lagrangian (Chen et al., 2002). Kaon and antikaon mean-field potentials were determined by fitting experimental data on kaon and antikaon yields in Ni+Ni collisions at 1.93 GeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- Xi yield of about 9.8 x 10^-5 per event in central collisions of 58Ni + 58Ni at E/A = 1.93 GeV and impact parameter b = 1 fm
- Xi/K ratio of about 5.2 x 10^-4 in the same system
- Doubling the strangeness-exchange cross sections increased Xi yield to 1.4 x 10^-4 (46% increase); halving them reduced yield to 6.3 x 10^-5 (36% decrease)
- Neglecting kaon medium effects reduced Xi yield to 8.2 x 10^-5 (16% less than with medium effects)
- Xi abundance in central 197Au + 197Au collisions: 5.4 x 10^-6 at E/A = 0.96 GeV, 1.8 x 10^-4 at E/A = 1.48 GeV, and 9.6 x 10^-4 at E/A = 2.0 GeV
- Xi/K ratio in central Au+Au at E/A = 2.0 GeV: about 7.3 x 10^-4
- Central baryon density reached about 2.3 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.15 fm^-3) in Ni+Ni collisions
- Final antikaon/kaon ratio of about 3.3 x 10^-2 in Ni+Ni collisions
- Kaon potential: repulsive about 20 MeV; antikaon potential: attractive about -98 MeV at normal nuclear matter density for zero momentum

## Context

This work extends previous studies of subthreshold kaon and antikaon production at SIS energies (Ko, 1996; Cassing, 1999) and applies the same strangeness-exchange mechanism that had been used to explain enhanced Xi production at SPS and AGS energies (Spalinski, 2001; Spalinski, 2002). The results enable experimental tests of the hadronic model predictions for strangeness-exchange cross sections (Chen et al., 2002) at GSI, and suggest that Xi yields of order 10^-3 per event in Au+Au at 2 GeV/nucleon could be studied at AGS.
