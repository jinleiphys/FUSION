# Covariant Spectator Theory of $np$ scattering: Deuteron form factors

> The deuteron form factors are calculated using two model wave functions obtained from the 2007 CST high precision fits to $np$ scattering data. Included in the calculation are a new class of isoscalar $np$ interaction currents which are automatically generated by the nuclear force model used in these fits. If the nuclear model WJC2 is used, a precision fit ($χ^2$/datum $\eqsim1$) to the Sick Global Analysis (GA) of all $ed$ elastic scattering data can be obtained by adjusting the unknown off-shell nucleon form factors $F_3(Q^2)$ (discussed before) and $F_4(Q^2)$ (introduced in this paper), and predicting the high $Q^2$ behavior of the neutron charge form factor $G_{En}(Q^2)$ well beyond the region where it has been measured directly. Relativistic corrections, isoscalar interaction currents, and off-shell effects are defined, discussed, and their size displayed. A rationale for extending $ed$ elastic scattering measurements to higher $Q^2$ is presented.

# Covariant Spectator Theory of $np$ scattering: Deuteron form factors

## Key claim

Using the WJC2 nuclear model, a precision fit (χ²/datum ∼ 1) to the Sick Global Analysis of all elastic electron-deuteron scattering data can be obtained by adjusting the unknown off-shell nucleon form factors F₃(Q²) and F₄(Q²), but only if the new class of isoscalar np interaction currents automatically generated by the momentum-dependent kernel are included; the fit then predicts the high Q² behavior of the neutron charge form factor GEn(Q²) beyond the region where it has been measured directly.

## Method

The deuteron electromagnetic form factors were calculated using two model wave functions (WJC1 and WJC2) obtained from 2007 Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) high-precision fits to np scattering data, which included momentum-dependent terms in the one-boson-exchange kernel. A new class of isoscalar np interaction currents, required by current conservation due to the momentum-dependent kernel, were incorporated into the calculation, and the off-shell nucleon current was extended to include four form factors: the usual Dirac and Pauli form factors F₁ and F₂, plus two off-shell form factors F₃ (previously discussed) and F₄ (introduced in this paper). The unknown F₃ and F₄ were determined by fitting to the Sick Global Analysis of elastic ed scattering data, using the linear observables G_M (from B) and the ratio y (from T̃₂₀), after which GEn was adjusted to reproduce the structure function A.

## Key numbers

- Model WJC1 has 27 parameters with χ²/datum ≃ 1.06; model WJC2 has 15 parameters with χ²/datum ≃ 1.12
- The momentum-dependent kernel parameter νσ₀ is −15.2 for WJC1 and −2.6 for WJC2
- The normalization of the deuteron wave functions includes a term from the energy dependence of the kernel contributing −5.5% for WJC1 and −2.3% for WJC2
- The structure function B(Q²) = (4/3) η (1+η) G_M²(Q²) where η = Q²/(4M_d²)
- The tensor polarization observable T̃₂₀ = −√2 [y(2+y)]/(1+2y²) where y = (2η G_Q)/(3 G_C)

## Context

This work is the fourth-generation calculation of deuteron form factors using CST, extending the third-generation calculation by Van Orden and collaborators (1995) which used model IIB from a 1991 fit to the np database with χ²/datum ≃ 2.5. The new calculation is required because the 2007 CST fit to the np database achieved χ²/datum ∼ 1 and included momentum-dependent terms in the kernel, which automatically generate new isoscalar interaction currents not present in earlier treatments. This work enables determination of the off-shell nucleon form factors F₃ and F₄ from ed scattering data and provides predictions for GEn at high Q², motivating extension of elastic ed scattering measurements to higher momentum transfers.
