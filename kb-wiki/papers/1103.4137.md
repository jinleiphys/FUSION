# Semi-microscopic model of pairing in nuclei

> A semi-microscopic model for nucleon pairing in nuclei is presented starting from the ab intio BCS gap equation with Argonne v18 force and the self-consistent Energy Density Functional Method basis characterized with the bare nucleon mass. The BCS theory is formulated in terms of the model space S0 with the effective pairing interaction calculated from the first principles in the subsidiary space S0. This effective interaction is supplemented with a small phenomenological addendum containing one phenomenological parameter universal for all medium and heavy atomic nuclei. We consider the latter as a phenomenological way to take into account both the many-body corrections to the BCS theory and the effective mass effects. For protons, the Coulomb interaction is introduced directly. Calculations made for several isotopic and isotonic chains of semi-magic nuclei confirm the validity of the model. The role of the self-consistent basis is stressed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a semi-microscopic model for nucleon pairing that combines an *ab initio* BCS gap equation (using the Argonne v18 force) with a single phenomenological parameter, claiming that this parameter can universally account for many-body corrections and effective mass effects in medium and heavy nuclei, but the model's accuracy depends strongly on the quality of the single-particle spectrum, particularly the positions of high-j levels.

## Method

The authors split the complete Hilbert space into a model subspace S0 (up to 40 MeV) and a complementary subspace S'. They solve the BCS gap equation in S0 using an effective pairing interaction derived from the Argonne v18 potential via the Local Potential Approximation (LPA) in S', with the bare nucleon mass m*=m. A small phenomenological term proportional to the local density, with a single universal parameter gamma, is added to the effective interaction. The self-consistent basis is obtained from the Generalized Energy Density Functional (GEDF) method with the DF3 functional, and the Coulomb interaction is included explicitly for protons.

## Key numbers

- For the lead isotopic chain with gamma=0.06, the rms deviation of theoretical gaps from experimental values is sqrt(mean(delta Delta)^2) approximately 0.045 MeV; the optimal gamma=0.064 gives sqrt(mean(delta Delta)^2) approximately 0.037 MeV
- For the tin isotopic chain with gamma=0.06, the rms deviation is sqrt(mean(delta Delta)^2) approximately 0.165 MeV; with the improved DF3a functional and gamma=0.052, it becomes approximately 0.056 MeV
- For the N=82 isotonic chain (proton pairing) with gamma=0.06, the rms deviation is sqrt(mean(delta Delta)^2)=0.124 MeV
- The overall average rms deviation for all 34 nuclei considered is sqrt(mean(delta Delta)^2) approximately 0.086 MeV at gamma=0.06
- The Coulomb interaction suppresses the proton gap by approximately 0.5 MeV for the N=82 chain
- The model space boundary is E0=40 MeV, the subsidiary space extends to Emax=1000 MeV, with numerical accuracy of 0.01 MeV checked by increasing parameters to E0=60 MeV, Emax=1200 MeV, and box radius R=24 fm

## Context

This work extends the *ab initio* BCS calculations of the Milan group (2005-2008) for 120Sn and the low-k force calculations of Duguet and Losinsky (2008), which gave contradictory results for the pairing gap due to different treatments of the effective mass at high momenta. The model enables systematic calculations of pairing gaps across many semi-magic nuclei with a single adjustable parameter, avoiding the uncertainties in the momentum dependence of the effective mass that plagued earlier Skyrme-Hartree-Fock based approaches. The authors note that the accuracy depends critically on reproducing the positions of high-j levels (such as the 1h11/2 state) in the self-consistent basis, as demonstrated by comparing results using the DF3 and DF3a functionals.
