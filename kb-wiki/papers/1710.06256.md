# Thermal dileptons as QCD matter probes at SIS

> Electromagnetic radiation is emitted during the whole course of a heavy-ion collision and can escape from the collision zone without further interactions. This makes it an ideal tool to study the properties of hot and dense QCD matter. To model the space-time evolution of the collision at SIS energies a coarse-graining approach is used to convert transport simulations into meaningful temperatures and densities. These parameters serve as input for the determination of the pertinent radiation of thermal dileptons based on an in-medium $ρ$ spectral function that describes available spectra at ultrarelativistic collision energies. The resulting excitation function of the thermal excess radiation provides a baseline for future measurements by the HADES and CBM experiments at GSI/FAIR, and experiments proposed at NICA and J-PARC.

## Key claim

The coarse-graining approach, which converts transport simulations into thermodynamic parameters, can successfully model thermal dilepton emission at SIS energies (a few GeV) and reveals that the cumulative low-mass dilepton yield is proportional to the fireball lifetime, with a normalization factor of 1.45 that remains approximately constant from top RHIC down to SIS18 energies.

## Method

The authors used a coarse-graining procedure applied to UrQMD transport simulations of heavy-ion collisions at SIS energies, dividing the space-time evolution into 4-dimensional cells and averaging over many simulated events to extract temperatures, baryon densities, and pion densities from cells meeting thermalization criteria. These thermodynamic parameters served as input for calculating thermal dilepton radiation using an in-medium rho spectral function parametrization from Rapp and Wambach (1999), which was previously validated at ultrarelativistic collision energies. An experimental acceptance filter was applied to compare the calculated invariant-mass spectra with HADES data for Ar+KCl at 1.76A GeV and Au+Au at 1.23A GeV.

## Key numbers

- In central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.4 GeV, temperatures reach up to 90 MeV and densities up to 3 times normal nuclear matter density.
- The interacting fireball lifetime is approximately 13 fm/c in central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=2.4 GeV and about 8 fm/c for the lighter Ar+KCl system.
- The proportionality between thermal low-mass dilepton yield (M_ee=0.3-0.7 GeV/c^2) and fireball lifetime is given by: (N_ll/N_pi±) x 10^6 = 1.45 tau_fb [fm/c] for |y| ≤ 0.5.
- The low-mass thermal dilepton yield normalized to charged pions remains rather flat from top RHIC down to SIS18 energies.

## Context

This work extends the coarse-graining approach previously applied to the SIS18 energy regime by Endres (2015), Galatyuk (2016), and Seck (2017) to provide a baseline for future measurements by HADES and CBM at GSI/FAIR, as well as experiments at NICA and J-PARC. The close correlation between the build-up of collective radial flow and the thermal dilepton emission window consolidates the use of low-mass dilepton yield as a fireball chronometer, as established for ultrarelativistic collisions by Rapp and van Hees (2016). The approach enables studying the impact of different scenarios on the invariant-mass spectrum of thermal lepton pairs in the FAIR energy regime, where no dilepton measurements currently exist, and could help locate the QCD critical point.
