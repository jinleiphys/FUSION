# Large-$N_c$ and renormalization group constraints on parity-violating low-energy coefficients for three-derivative operators in pionless effective field theory

> We extend from operators with one derivative to operators with three derivatives the analysis of two-body hadronic parity violation in a combined pionless effective field theory (EFT$_{π\!/}$) and large-$N_c$ expansion, where $N_c$ is the number of colors in quantum chromodynamics (QCD). In elastic scattering, these operators contribute to $S$-$P$ and $P$-$D$ wave transitions, with five operators and their accompanying low energy coefficients (LECs) characterizing the $S$-$P$ transitions and six operators and LECs those in $P$-$D$ transitions. We show that the large-$N_c$ analysis separates them into leading order in $N_c$, next-to-leading order in $N_c$, etc. Relationships among EFT$_{π\!/}$ LECs emerge in the large-$N_c$ expansion. We also discuss the renormalization scale dependence of these LECs. Our analysis can complement lattice QCD calculations and help prioritize future parity-violating experiments.

## Key claim
The paper extends the combined pionless effective field theory (EFT\(_{\pi\!/}\)) and large-\(N_c\) expansion from one-derivative to three-derivative parity-violating two-nucleon operators, showing that the large-\(N_c\) analysis reduces the eleven independent low-energy coefficients (LECs) in the EFT\(_{\pi\!/}\) to only two independent LECs at leading order in the dual expansion, with corrections of order \(1/N_c^2 \sim 10\%\) channel-by-channel.

## Method
The authors constructed the parity-violating Lagrangian for two-nucleon contact interactions with three derivatives in two bases: a large-\(N_c\) basis where the scaling with the number of colors \(N_c\) is transparent, and a partial-wave basis describing \(S\)-\(P\) and \(P\)-\(D\) wave transitions. They used Fierz transformations to map between the two bases and determine the large-\(N_c\) scaling of the partial-wave LECs, and performed a renormalization group (RG) analysis of the LECs' dependence on the subtraction point \(\mu\).

## Key numbers
- Eleven independent LECs occur at three derivatives in EFT\(_{\pi\!/}\): five for \(S\)-\(P\) transitions and six for \(P\)-\(D\) transitions.
- In the dual EFT\(_{\pi\!/}\) and large-\(N_c\) expansion, these eleven LECs are reduced to two at leading order in \(N_c\).
- The two leading-order LECs, \(C_{G.G}^{[3]}\) and \(C'^{[3]}_{G.G}\), scale as \(N_c\) in the large-\(N_c\) counting.
- Four isovector LECs appear at next-to-leading order in \(N_c\), scaling as \(N_c^0\).
- The relationships among LECs hold up to corrections of \(1/N_c^2 \sim 10\%\) channel-by-channel.
- For the \(J=2\) \(P\)-\(D\) channels, the LECs are \(\mu\)-independent; for the \(J=1\) \(P\)-\(D\) channels, \(\mu\) dependence arises from \(^3S\)-\(^3D\) mixing.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of parity-violating two-nucleon interactions from one-derivative operators (Schindler et al., 2016) to three-derivative operators, using the same dual EFT\(_{\pi\!/}\) and large-\(N_c\) framework previously applied to parity-conserving interactions (Kaplan and Manohar, 1995; Kaplan and Savage, 1996; Schindler et al., 2018). The results can complement lattice QCD calculations (Beane et al.; Wasem; Tiburzi et al.) and help prioritize future parity-violating experiments such as those at the Spallation Neutron Source and the High-Intensity Gamma-ray Source. The isotensor terms, which are dominant in large-\(N_c\) counting, are particularly attractive for lattice QCD because they avoid disconnected (quark-loop) diagrams.
