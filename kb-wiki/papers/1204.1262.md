# Strongly magnetized cold electron degenerate gas: Mass-radius relation of the magnetized white dwarf

> We consider a relativistic, degenerate electron gas at zero-temperature under the influence of a strong, uniform, static magnetic field, neglecting any form of interactions. Since the density of states for the electrons changes due to the presence of the magnetic field (which gives rise to Landau quantization), the corresponding equation of state also gets modified. In order to investigate the effect of very strong magnetic field, we focus only on systems in which a maximum of either one, two or three Landau level(s) is/are occupied. This is important since, if a very large number of Landau levels are filled, it implies a very low magnetic field strength which yields back Chandrasekhar's celebrated non-magnetic results. The maximum number of occupied Landau levels is fixed by the correct choice of two parameters, namely the magnetic field strength and the maximum Fermi energy of the system. We study the equations of state of these one-level, two-level and three-level systems and compare them by taking three different maximum Fermi energies. We also find the effect of the strong magnetic field on the mass-radius relation of the underlying star composed of the gas stated above. We obtain an exciting result that, it is possible to have an electron degenerate static star, namely magnetized white dwarfs, with a mass significantly greater than the Chandrasekhar limit in the range 2.3-2.6M_Sun, provided it has an appropriate magnetic field strength and central density. In fact, recent observations of peculiar Type Ia supernovae - SN 2006gz, SN 2007if, SN 2009dc, SN 2003fg - seem to suggest super-Chandrasekhar-mass white dwarfs with masses up to 2.4-2.8M_Sun, as their most likely progenitors. Interestingly our results seem to lie within the observational limits.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a static, electron-degenerate star (a magnetized white dwarf) can have a mass significantly exceeding the Chandrasekhar limit (1.44 M_Sun), reaching 2.3-2.6 M_Sun, provided the star has an appropriate magnetic field strength and central density, and that only a small number (one, two, or three) of Landau levels are occupied. The hedge is that this result depends on the specific choice of maximum Fermi energy and the corresponding magnetic field that restricts electrons to these few Landau levels.

## Method
The authors considered a relativistic, degenerate electron gas at zero temperature under a strong, uniform, static magnetic field, neglecting any form of interactions. They restricted the system to have at most one, two, or three occupied Landau levels (one-level, two-level, and three-level systems) by fixing the maximum Fermi energy (E_Fmax = 2, 20, or 200 m_e c^2) and the corresponding magnetic field strength from the condition that the maximum Landau level index is an integer. They numerically solved the modified equation of state (pressure vs. density) using the Landau-quantized density of states, then fitted the equation of state with a polytropic relation P = K ρ^Γ in different density ranges, and solved the hydrostatic equilibrium equation (Lane-Emden equation) to obtain the mass-radius relation for each case.

## Key numbers
- Maximum mass of magnetized white dwarfs: 2.3 M_Sun, achieved at a radius of approximately 6.4 × 10^7 cm for the one-level system with E_Fmax = 20 m_e c^2 and B = 8.81 × 10^15 G (B_D = 199.5).
- For the two-level system with E_Fmax = 20 m_e c^2 and B = 4.40 × 10^15 G (B_D = 99.75), a maximum mass of approximately 2.3 M_Sun is reached at a radius of approximately 8.9 × 10^7 cm for a central density of approximately 4.0 × 10^9 gm/cc.
- For the three-level system with E_Fmax = 20 m_e c^2 and B = 2.94 × 10^15 G (B_D = 66.5), the maximum mass of approximately 2.3 M_Sun occurs at a radius of approximately 1.1 × 10^8 cm for a central density of approximately 2.2 × 10^9 gm/cc.
- The critical magnetic field strength B_c is defined as 4.414 × 10^13 G, where the cyclotron energy equals the electron rest-mass energy.
- The scalar virial theorem gives a maximum magnetic field for white dwarfs of approximately 10^12 G.

## Context
This work extends the study of magnetic white dwarfs by Suh et al. (who considered weaker fields B ~ 4.4 × 10^11-13 G and ignored Landau quantization) by explicitly including Landau quantization in the strong-field regime (B ~ 10^15-10^17 G). The results contradict the standard Chandrasekhar limit (1.44 M_Sun) for non-magnetic white dwarfs and provide a possible explanation for the observed super-Chandrasekhar-mass progenitors of peculiar Type Ia supernovae such as SN 2006gz, SN 2007if, SN 2009dc, and SN 2003fg, which suggest white dwarf masses up to 2.4-2.8 M_Sun. The work enables further study of the stability, magnetic field decay timescales, and observational signatures of such highly magnetized white dwarfs.
