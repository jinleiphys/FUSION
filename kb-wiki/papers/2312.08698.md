# Direct solution of Minkowski-space Bethe-Salpeter equation in the massive Wick-Cutkosky model

> In order to solve the Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) in the Minkowski space, we first introduce the Nakanishi integral representations of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (BSA) and the Bethe-Salpeter wave function (BSWF). We then derive the explicit integral equations for the corresponding spectral functions from the BSE for states of $2$ scalar particles bound by a scalar-particle exchange interaction, where the propagators of constituents are allowed to be fully dressed. These integral equations are subsequently solved numerically in the variation of the Wick-Cutkosky model with massive exchange particles, where an algorithm of adaptive mash grid is proposed. The equations and algorithm we develop here serve as the foundation of Minkowski-space formulation of BSE for bound states of fermions.

## Key claim

The paper derives a closed system of integral equations for the Nakanishi spectral functions of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude and wave function, and solves them numerically in the massive Wick-Cutkosky model where constituent and exchange particle propagators are bare, with the caveat that the algorithm is tested only for this simplified case rather than for fully dressed propagators.

## Method

The author introduces Nakanishi integral representations for both the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude (BSA) and the Bethe-Salpeter wave function (BSWF), then converts the momentum-space definition of the BSWF into an integral relation merging the spectral functions of the propagators and the BSA into that of the BSWF. The Bethe-Salpeter equation provides a second integral relation evaluating the Nakanishi spectral function of the BSA in terms of the BSWF, forming a closed system solved iteratively with an adaptive mesh grid for the spectral variables. The equations are tested in the massive variation of the Wick-Cutkosky model where propagators of constituents and exchange particles are bare, using the rainbow-ladder truncation of the BSE kernel.

## Context

This work extends the Nakanishi integral representation approach to Bethe-Salpeter equations previously developed by Kusaka (1995, 1997) for scalar particles, converting the BSE into integral equations of Nakanishi spectral functions with analytic rather than numerical kernels. The equations and algorithm serve as the foundation for a Minkowski-space formulation of BSE for bound states of fermions, with the massive Wick-Cutkosky model providing a test case before generalization to QED or other theories with fermionic constituents.
