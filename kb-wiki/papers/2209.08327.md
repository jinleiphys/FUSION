# Exclusive $J/ψ$ production in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions to NLO pQCD

> We present the first NLO pQCD study of coherent exclusive $J/ψ$ photoproduction in ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions (UPCs) at the LHC. Taking the generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in their forward limit, as parton distribution functions (PDFs), we quantify the NLO contributions in the rapidity-differential cross section, show that the real part of the amplitude must not be neglected, study the gluon and quark contributions, chart the scale-choice and PDF uncertainties, and compare the NLO results with LHC and HERA data. We show that the scale dependence is significant but a scale choice can be found with which we reproduce the 2.76 and 5.02 TeV UPC data. In particular, we show that the process is clearly more sensitive to the nuclear quark PDFs than thought before.

# Exclusive J/ψ production in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions to NLO pQCD

## Key claim

The first NLO pQCD study of coherent exclusive J/ψ photoproduction in ultraperipheral Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC shows that, despite significant scale dependence, an "optimal" scale choice can reproduce the 2.76 and 5.02 TeV data, and that the process is considerably more sensitive to nuclear quark PDFs than previously thought, with quarks dominating the cross section at midrapidity.

## Method

The authors compute the rapidity-differential cross section for coherent exclusive J/ψ photoproduction in Pb+Pb UPCs using the collinearly factorized per-nucleon amplitude at NLO (O(α_s^2)), taking generalized parton distributions (GPDs) in their forward limit as ordinary PDFs. The nuclear cross section is obtained by folding the per-nucleon amplitude with the Weizsäcker-Williams photon fluxes and the nuclear form factor from the Woods-Saxon density distribution. The NLO coefficient functions from Ivanov et al. (2004) are used, with the NRQCD matrix element fixed by the J/ψ leptonic decay width, and the renormalization scale is set equal to the factorization scale.

## Key numbers

- The "optimal" scale that reproduces LHC data is μ = 0.76 M_J/ψ, where M_J/ψ is the J/ψ mass.
- At NLO and at midrapidity (y=0) in Pb+Pb UPCs at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV, the quark contribution dominates the cross section, a result of canceling LO and NLO gluon amplitudes.
- The scale dependence is studied using μ = M_J/ψ/2, 0.76 M_J/ψ, and M_J/ψ, producing a significant envelope of cross section predictions.
- The NLO cross section computed with nNNPDF2.0 deviates considerably from those computed with EPPS16 and nCTEQ15, due to the rapidly growing small-x gluon distributions in nNNPDF2.0.

## Context

This work extends the LO pQCD studies of coherent J/ψ photoproduction in nucleus-nucleus UPCs (Adeluyi 2011, Guzey 2013) to NLO, following earlier NLO calculations for the γ+p case (Ivanov 2004, Jones 2015, Flett 2020). The results enable more precise extraction of nuclear PDFs from LHC UPC data, particularly for quark distributions, and motivate future studies incorporating full GPD modeling beyond the forward limit.
