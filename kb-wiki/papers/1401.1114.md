# Nuclear constraints on the EoS and rotating neutron stars

> In this contribution nuclear constraints on the equation of state for a neutron star are discussed. A combined fit to nuclear masses and charge radii leads to improved values for the symmetry energy and its derivative at nuclear saturation density, $S_{\rm{v}}= 31$ MeV and $L=68\pm 8$ MeV. As an application the sensitivity of some properties of rotating supramassive neutron stars on the EoS is discussed.

# Nuclear constraints on the EoS and rotating neutron stars

## Key claim
A combined fit to nuclear masses and charge radii yields improved values for the symmetry energy and its derivative at nuclear saturation density, specifically $S_{\rm{v}}=31$ MeV and $L=68\pm 8$ MeV, but this phenomenological result favors larger $L$ values than most microscopic models, a trend that is not yet understood.

## Method
The authors performed a combined fit of nuclear masses and charge radii using the liquid drop model (LDM) parameterization of the symmetry energy, including shell corrections. They extracted the volume symmetry energy $S_{\rm{v}}$ and the ratio $y=S_{\rm{v}}/S_{\rm{s}}$ (where $S_{\rm{s}}$ is the surface symmetry energy) from a plot of $1/S$ versus $A^{-1/3}$, then used charge radii data to constrain $y$ independently via the neutron skin expression $R_{\rm{np}} = (2r_0/3)((N-Z)/A)(1/(1+A^{1/3}/y))$. As an application, they computed properties of rotating supramassive neutron stars using a 3-polytrope equation of state with the RNS code.

## Key numbers
- $S_{\rm{v}} = 31$ MeV from the combined fit of masses and charge radii (present work)
- $L = 68 \pm 8$ MeV from the combined fit (present work)
- $y = 2.35 \pm 0.20$ from the combined fit (present work)
- $R_{\rm{np}}(^{208}{\rm Pb}) = 0.185 \pm 0.015$ fm from the combined fit (present work)
- $M_{\rm{max}} = 2.2 M_\odot$ for the 3-polytrope EoS used in the rotating star calculations
- $M(\Omega_K)$ increases by 20% and $R(\Omega_K)$ increases by 50% for rotating supramassive stars relative to static stars
- The empirical relation constant $C = 0.90$ for the minimum rotation period formula $\tau_{\rm{min}} = C (M_\odot/M)^{1/2} (R/10{\rm km})^{3/2}$ ms with the authors' EoS
- The fastest observed pulsar frequency is $\nu_{\rm obs} = 716$ Hz

## Context
This work extends earlier constraints on the symmetry energy and its derivative from nuclear mass fits by Lattimer (2012) and Dieperink (2009), and from charge radius analyses by Danielewicz (2003). The improved $L$ value of $68\pm 8$ MeV is larger than predictions from microscopic approaches such as the Skyrme analysis of Chen (2012) ($L=58\pm 15$ MeV), the quantum Monte Carlo work of Gandolfi ($L=45\pm 15$ MeV), and the effective field theory results of Hebeler ($L=46\pm 10$ MeV). The rotating neutron star application tests the scenario proposed by Falcke and Rezzolla (2013) for interpreting bright millisecond radio pulses as supramassive neutron stars collapsing to Kerr black holes, and shows that the critical rotation frequency $\Omega_K$ is sensitive to the equation of state.
