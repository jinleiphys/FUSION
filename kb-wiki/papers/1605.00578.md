# Neutrinos in Astrophysics and Cosmology

> Neutrinos play a crucial role in many aspects of astrophysics and cosmology. Since they control the electron fraction, or equivalently neutron-to-proton ratio, neutrino properties impact yields of r-process nucleosynthesis. Similarly the weak decoupling temperature in the Big Bang Nucleosynthesis epoch is exponentially dependent on the neutron-to-proton ratio. In these conference proceedings, I briefly summarize some of the recent work exploring the role of neutrinos in astrophysics and cosmology.

## Key claim

Neutrino properties, particularly flavor oscillations and interactions, critically control the electron fraction (neutron-to-proton ratio) in astrophysical environments, thereby determining the yields of r-process nucleosynthesis and the weak decoupling temperature in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis; however, the paper presents this as a summary of recent work rather than a definitive resolution, noting that the r-process astrophysical site remains unknown and that the lithium abundance problem persists.

## Method

The paper reviews the role of neutrinos in astrophysics and cosmology by deriving the differential equation for the electron fraction \(Y_e\) in terms of proton and neutron weak loss rates, and then extending it to include alpha particles. It introduces the many-body Hamiltonian for collective neutrino oscillations using neutrino flavor isospin algebras, discusses the single-angle limit and its analogy to the BCS Hamiltonian, and describes mean-field approximations for neutrino-neutrino, antineutrino-antineutrino, and neutrino-antineutrino interactions. The review also examines the alpha effect, active-sterile neutrino mixing, and the impact of neutrino magnetic moments on Big Bang Nucleosynthesis.

## Key numbers

- Core-collapse supernovae release about \(10^{59}\) MeV of energy, with 99% carried by neutrinos of all flavors; each neutrino carries on average \(1 \sim 10\) MeV, yielding a total of \(10^{57} \sim 10^{58}\) neutrinos.
- Abundances of \(A=7\) nuclei calculated with state-of-the-art nuclear physics are 30% off the observed values.
- The weak decoupling temperature in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis is exponentially dependent on the neutron-to-proton ratio.
- Changes in cosmological parameters from magnetic contributions to neutrino decoupling temperatures are below the level of upcoming precision observations.

## Context

This conference proceedings paper extends the discussion of neutrino roles in astrophysics and cosmology, building on the U.S. National Academy of Sciences report "Connecting Quarks with the Cosmos" and prior work on the alpha effect by Meyer (1992) and McLaughlin (1997). It references active-sterile neutrino mixing studies by McLaughlin (1999), Caldwell (1999), and Fetter (2002), and connects to the LSND results and reactor anomaly as summarized by Abazajian (2012). The paper enables further investigation into collective neutrino oscillations, the r-process astrophysical site (neutron-star mergers and core-collapse supernovae), and the lithium problem in Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, while noting open questions such as the need for exact solutions to the many-body neutrino Hamiltonian and the impact of turbulence and stochastic media on neutrino propagation.
