# Nonperturbative calculations in truncated Fock space in LFD

> A non-perturbative approach based on the Fock decomposition of the state vector and its truncation is discussed. In order the non-perturbative renormalization procedure after truncation could eliminate infinities, it should be the sector dependent. We clarify the meaning of this procedure in a toy model. Then we demonstrate stability, relative to the increasing cutoff, of the anomalous magnetic moment found using the sector dependent renormalization scheme in Yukawa model.

## Key claim

The sector-dependent renormalization scheme, in which counter terms are determined separately for each Fock-sector truncation, produces finite results that converge to the exact solution as the number of included sectors increases, as demonstrated in a zero-dimensional toy model; in the Yukawa model, the scheme yields a stable anomalous magnetic moment that does not depend on the Pauli-Villars regulator mass when that mass becomes large.

## Method

The author constructs a zero-dimensional toy model with a neighbor-only interaction kernel \(V_{ij}=g/(i+j)\) that couples only adjacent Fock sectors (1-body ↔ 2-body, 2-body ↔ 3-body, etc.). The state vector is truncated to include up to \(N\) Fock components, and the mass counter term \(\delta m_N\) is solved from the determinant condition \(\det(M)=0\) for the truncated matrix, first with a single common counter term (the "exact" approach) and then with sector-dependent counter terms where \(\delta m_1=0\) and each \(\delta m_k\) is determined sequentially from the \(k\)-body truncation and then used in the \((k+1)\)-body truncation. In the Yukawa model, the same sector-dependent procedure is applied with Pauli-Villars regularization (one PV fermion and one PV boson) for truncation up to \(N=3\), and the anomalous magnetic moment \(F_2(0)\) is computed as a function of the PV boson mass \(\mu_1\).

- In the zero-dimensional model with \(g=2\), the exact mass counter term converges to \(\delta m = -0.3595160788802980\) for \(N=10\) (table 1).

## Context

This work extends the sector-dependent renormalization approach developed by Karmanov, Mathiot, and Smirnov (2007-2012) for non-perturbative calculations in truncated Fock space on the light front. The zero-dimensional toy model provides the first explicit check that the sector-dependent iterative procedure converges to the exact solution as the truncation number \(N\) increases, which had not been demonstrated previously. The Yukawa model results at \(N=3\) show that the sum of two- and three-body contributions to the anomalous magnetic moment becomes independent of the Pauli-Villars regulator mass, confirming that infinities are cancelled within the scheme; the author suggests that extending these calculations to \(N=4\) truncation would be valuable for testing saturation of the results.
