# Overview of recent UPC measurements

> This contribution presents an overview of recent measurements of photon-induced processes in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPCs) performed with the ALICE experiment at the LHC. Results from Run~2 include detailed studies of exclusive vector meson photoproduction in Pb-Pb collisions. Measurements of incoherent J/$ψ$ photoproduction as a function of energy and the Mandelstam-$|t|$ variable, combined with electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) classification, show a suppression of the energy evolution at large $|t|$, favouring saturation-based descriptions of the gluon structure. Complementary measurements of proton emission in Pb-Pb UPCs constrain nuclear breakup mechanisms and provide a handle on collision geometry through EMD tagging. Coherent $ρ^{0}$ photoproduction exhibits impact-parameter-dependent azimuthal anisotropy consistent with quantum interference effects, while the first polarisation measurement of coherently photoproduced J/$ψ$ confirms $s$-channel helicity conservation. Additional measurements of exclusive multi-hadron photoproduction, including four-pion and charged kaon pair final states, probe resonance contributions and light vector meson couplings to photons and nuclear targets. Run~3 data enable the study of inclusive photonuclear interactions. Measurements of inclusive open charm photoproduction constrain the gluon content of nuclei in the perturbative QCD regime. Studies of identified hadron production and baryon-to-meson ratios in photonuclear collisions provide new information on particle production mechanisms and possible collective effects in photon-induced systems. Photon-photon interactions are also studied through performance and sensitivity analyses for the measurement of the anomalous magnetic moment of the tau lepton, together with projections for future measurements with ALICE~3.

# Overview of recent UPC measurements

## Key claim
Incoherent J/ψ photoproduction in Pb-Pb ultra-peripheral collisions shows a suppression of the energy evolution at large Mandelstam-|t|, favoring saturation-based descriptions of gluon structure over models relying solely on nuclear shadowing, but this conclusion is drawn from multi-differential measurements using electromagnetic dissociation (EMD) classes as a proxy for impact parameter rather than direct geometric determination.

## Method
ALICE measured exclusive vector meson photoproduction (J/ψ, ρ⁰, φ) in Pb-Pb UPCs at √sNN = 5.02 TeV using Run 2 data, separating coherent and incoherent contributions via transverse momentum distributions. For Run 3, inclusive photonuclear interactions were studied using asymmetric vetoing of forward detector activity (FIT and ZDC) to select exclusive, semi-inclusive, and inclusive UPC topologies, with streamed readout replacing dedicated exclusive triggers. Electromagnetic dissociation classes, determined by forward neutron and proton detection in Zero Degree Calorimeters, provided experimental access to collision geometry and impact-parameter dependence.

## Key numbers
- Incoherent J/ψ photoproduction cross section suppression at large |t|: energy evolution shows deviation from shadowing-only predictions, favoring saturation-based models (specific numerical suppression factor not provided in text)
- Gold nucleus production via three-proton emission from ²⁰⁸Pb: measured cross section σ₃ₚ = 6.8 ± 2.2 b, comparable to total inelastic Pb-Pb hadronic cross section at the LHC
- Coherent ρ⁰ photoproduction azimuthal anisotropy: cos(2φ) modulation increases by nearly an order of magnitude from large to small impact parameters
- J/ψ polarization parameters λθ, λφ, λθφ in helicity frame: consistent with transverse polarization and s-channel helicity conservation at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- Inclusive open charm production: measured down to zero pT in midrapidity |y| < 0.9, showing significant deviations from Color Glass Condensate predictions
- Λ/K⁰S ratio enhancement: peaks around pT ≈ 2 GeV/c in inclusive γ-Pb collisions, with magnitude and shape similar to low-multiplicity p-Pb collisions
- Expected τ⁺τ⁻ events for aτ measurement: approximately 3.6×10⁴ reconstructed events in central barrel for e+μ/π topology with 2.3 nb⁻¹ integrated luminosity in Run 3 Pb-Pb
- Bjorken-x values probed by J/ψ photoproduction: down to approximately 10⁻⁵

## Context
This overview extends ALICE's established UPC program (ALICE Collaboration, various years) by presenting new Run 3 results that go beyond the exclusive measurements of Run 2. The incoherent J/ψ results build on prior ALICE measurements of coherent and incoherent photoproduction (PLB 798, midrapjpsi, JHEP 10) and provide constraints on nuclear parton distribution functions and gluon saturation models (EPhJC 82 2022 413, JEskola, EPCJ 75 2015 580). The inclusive open charm and strangeness measurements challenge Color Glass Condensate calculations and extend comparisons to hadronic collision systems (naturestrangeness, collsmallsystem), while the azimuthal anisotropy in ρ⁰ production demonstrates quantum interference effects at femtoscopic scales. These results enable future FoCal measurements in Run 4 targeting Bjorken-x down to 10⁻⁶ and ALICE 3 electroweak precision studies in Run 5.
