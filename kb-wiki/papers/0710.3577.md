# External Momentum, Volume Effects, and the Nucleon Magnetic Moment

> We analyze the determination of volume effects for correlation functions that depend on an external momentum. As a specific example, we consider finite volume nucleon current correlators, and focus on the nucleon magnetic moment. Because the multipole decomposition relies on SO(3) rotational invariance, the structure of such finite volume corrections is unrelated to infinite volume multipole form factors. One can deduce volume corrections to the magnetic moment only when a zero-mode photon coupling vanishes, as occurs at next-to-leading order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory. To deduce such finite volume corrections, however, one must assume continuous momentum transfer. In practice, volume corrections with momentum transfer dependence are required to address the extraction of the magnetic moment, or other observables that arise in momentum dependent correlation functions. Additionally we shed some light on a puzzle concerning differences in lattice form factor data at equal values of momentum transfer squared.

## Key claim

Finite volume corrections to the nucleon magnetic moment can be deduced from the single-particle effective action only when a zero-mode photon coupling vanishes, as occurs at next-to-leading order in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory, but this deduction requires the assumption of continuous momentum transfer, an assumption that is not valid for currently available lattice volumes.

## Method

The author constructs a single-nucleon effective action on a spatial torus of length \(L\) with periodic boundary conditions, writing down all gauge-invariant operators consistent with the reduced cubic symmetry group \(S_4\) and including at most one derivative and one insertion of the Wilson-line operator \(\mathcal{W}_i^{(-)}\). Heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory at \(\mathcal{O}(\varepsilon)\) is then used to compute finite-volume corrections to the isovector Pauli form factor from one-loop diagrams involving pions, nucleons, and deltas, matching these onto the single-particle effective action to extract the volume-dependent couplings \(\mu_N(L)\), \(C_1(L)\), and \(C_2(L)\).

## Key numbers

* The condition for a \(\sim 20\%\) error in keeping only the linear term in the momentum expansion requires \(q^2 / (4 m_\pi^2) = \pi^2 n^2 / (m_\pi^2 L^2) \lesssim 1/5\).
* To approximate a continuous derivative to \(\sim 20\%\) requires \(\delta q = 1/n \lesssim 1/5\).
* Combining these restrictions gives \(m_\pi L \gtrsim \sqrt{5} \pi n \gtrsim 5\sqrt{5}\pi \approx 35\) for the \(n\)-th mode.
* The nucleon-delta mass splitting is \(\Delta \sim 300\ \text{MeV}\).
* The pion decay constant is \(f = 132\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of finite-volume effects in electromagnetic observables begun by Hu et al. (2007), who noted the lack of connection between finite-volume amplitudes and low-energy multipole expansions. It also builds on the finite-volume chiral perturbation theory framework developed by Gasser (1986, 1987) and Leutwyler (1987), and on earlier calculations of finite-volume baryon properties by Beane (2004). The results enable a systematic understanding of why lattice form factor data at equal values of momentum transfer squared can differ due to volume effects, and provide a framework for computing volume corrections to any momentum-dependent correlation function, though practical application requires lattice volumes with \(m_\pi L \gtrsim 35\) that are beyond current computational reach.
