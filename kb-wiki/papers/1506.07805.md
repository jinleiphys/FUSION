# Impact of momentum-space anisotropy on heavy quark dynamics in a QGP medium

> Momentum space anisotropy present in the quark and gluon distribution functions in relativistic heavy ion collisions induces Chromo-Weibel instability in the hot QCD medium created therein. The impact of the Chromo-Weibel instability on the dynamics of a heavy-quark (HQ) traversing in the QGP medium is investigated within the framework of kinetic theory by studying the momentum and temperature behavior of HQ drag and diffusion coefficients. The physics of anisotropy is captured in an effective Vlasov term in the transport equation. The effects of the instability are handled by making a relation with the phenomenologically known jet quenching parameter in RHIC and LHC. Interestingly, the presence of instability significantly affect the temperature and momentum dependences of the HQ drag and diffusion coefficients. These results may have appreciable impact on the experimental observables such as, the nuclear suppression factor, $R_{AA}(p_T)$, and the elliptic flow, $v_2(p_T)$, of heavy mesons in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC energies which is a matter of future investigation.

## Key claim

The presence of Chromo-Weibel instability, induced by momentum-space anisotropy in the quark-gluon plasma, significantly alters the temperature and momentum dependence of heavy-quark drag and diffusion coefficients, but the actual impact on experimental observables such as the nuclear suppression factor \(R_{AA}(p_T)\) and elliptic flow \(v_2(p_T)\) is stated as a matter of future investigation rather than a demonstrated result.

## Method

The authors model the near-equilibrium distribution functions for gluons and quarks in an anisotropically expanding QGP by solving a linearized Dupree-Vlasov transport equation that incorporates the effects of turbulent chromo-fields via an ensemble-averaged force term. The strength of the anisotropy is related to the phenomenologically known jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) from RHIC and LHC data. These anisotropic distribution functions are then used within a kinetic theory framework (Landau's prescription) to compute the heavy-quark drag coefficient \(\gamma\) and diffusion coefficient \(B_0\) for charm quarks as functions of momentum and temperature.

## Key numbers

- At RHIC energy, the initial temperature is taken as \(T_i = 360\) MeV, the initial thermalization time \(\tau_t = 0.6\) fm, and the dimensionless jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}_q/T^3 = 4.6\).
- At LHC energy, the temperature is taken as \(T = 480\) MeV, the initial thermalization time \(\tau_i = 0.3\) fm, and \(\hat{q}_q/T^3 = 3.7\).
- The quark jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}_q\) for a 10 GeV quark jet is \(1.3 \pm 0.3\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at RHIC and \(1.9 \pm 0.7\) GeV\(^2\)/fm at LHC.
- The gluon jet quenching parameter is related to the quark value by \(\hat{q}_g = (9/4) \hat{q}_q\).
- The strength of anisotropy is varied by changing \(\hat{q}/T^3\) from 5 to 15, showing that larger \(\hat{q}/T^3\) (weaker anisotropy) increases the drag coefficient at low momentum (below 4 GeV) and decreases it at high momentum (above 4 GeV).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy-quark transport in an isotropic QGP medium (Moore and Teaney, 2005; Das et al., 2010) by incorporating the effects of momentum-space anisotropy and the resulting Chromo-Weibel instability, which had previously been studied in the context of QGP viscosity (Chandra and others, 2012). The authors connect their formalism to the phenomenologically constrained jet quenching parameter \(\hat{q}\) from multiple model comparisons (Burke et al., 2014), enabling future calculations of \(R_{AA}\) and \(v_2\) for heavy mesons at RHIC and LHC energies.
