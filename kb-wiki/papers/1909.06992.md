# Energy staggering parameters in nuclear magnetic rotational bands

> The systematics of energy staggering for the magnetic rotational bands with $M1$ and $E2$ transition properties strictly consistent with the features of good candidates of magnetic rotational bands in the $A\sim80$, 110, 130 and 190 mass regions are presented. The regularities exhibited by these bands concerning the staggering parameter which increases with increasing spin are in agreement with the semiclassical description of shears mechanism. In addition, the abnormal behaviours in the backbend regions or close to band termination have also been discussed. Taking the magnetic dipole bands with same configuration in three $N=58$ isotones, i.e., $^{103}$Rh, $^{105}$Ag, and~$^{107}$In, as examples, the transition from chiral rotation to magnetic rotation with the proton number approaching $Z=50$ is presented. Moreover, the self-consistent tilted axis cranking and principle axis cranking relativistic mean-field theories are applied to investigate the rotational mechanism in dipole band of $^{105}$Ag.

## Key claim

The paper systematically shows that the energy staggering parameter \(S(I)\) in magnetic rotational bands increases with increasing spin across the \(A\sim80\), 110, 130, and 190 mass regions, consistent with the semiclassical shears mechanism, but notes that abnormal behaviors occur in backbend regions or near band termination, and that the trend may serve as a potential indicator for magnetic rotation rather than a definitive criterion.

## Method

The authors compiled experimental energy staggering parameters \(S(I) = [E(I)-E(I-1)]/2I\) for 50 magnetic rotational bands in 39 nuclides that satisfy strict criteria for good magnetic rotation candidates (decreasing \(B(M1)\) with spin and \(B(M1)/B(E2) \gtrsim 20\ \mu_N^2/(eb)^2\)). They analyzed the systematics across four mass regions and compared the staggering patterns in three \(N=58\) isotones (\(^{103}\)Rh, \(^{105}\)Ag, \(^{107}\)In) with the same configuration \(\pi g_{9/2}^{-1}\otimes \nu h^1_{11/2}(g_{7/2}/d_{5/2})^1\). For \(^{105}\)Ag, they performed self-consistent tilted axis cranking relativistic mean-field (TAC-RMF) calculations for the lower-spin region and principal axis cranking RMF (PAC-RMF) calculations for the higher-spin region using the PC-PK1 interaction.

## Key numbers

- \(S(I)\) values in the \(A\sim80\) region vary from 8 to 42 \(\mathrm{keV}/\hbar\).
- \(S(I)\) values in the \(A\sim110\) region vary from 2 to 25 \(\mathrm{keV}/\hbar\).
- \(S(I)\) values in the \(A\sim130\) region vary from 4 to 16 \(\mathrm{keV}/\hbar\).
- \(S(I)\) values in the \(A\sim190\) region vary from 2 to 12 \(\mathrm{keV}/\hbar\).
- For the negative-parity MR band of \(^{199}\)Pb, \(S(I)\) decreases from 11.5 to 5.3 \(\mathrm{keV}/\hbar\) in the backbend region.
- The quadrupole deformation parameters from previous TAC calculations are 0.26 for \(^{103}\)Rh, 0.19 for \(^{105}\)Ag, and 0.12 for \(^{107}\)In with the same configuration.
- In the TAC-RMF calculations for \(^{105}\)Ag, the shears angle between proton and neutron angular momenta decreases from \(76^\circ\) to \(61^\circ\) as rotational frequency increases from 0.18 to 0.50 \(\mathrm{MeV}/\hbar\).

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of magnetic rotation signatures initiated by Frauendorf (1994) and Clark (2000), and builds on the compilation of candidate MR bands by Amita (2000). It provides the first comprehensive survey of energy staggering in strictly selected MR bands, showing that the increasing \(S(I)\) with spin is a common feature that distinguishes MR from conventional collective rotation. The comparison of three \(N=58\) isotones demonstrates a transition from chiral rotation (in \(^{103}\)Rh, as proposed by Kuti (2014)) to magnetic rotation (in \(^{107}\)In) as proton number approaches \(Z=50\), and the TAC-RMF calculations for \(^{105}\)Ag clarify the competition between shears mechanism and collective motion. This enables future work to use \(S(I)\) systematics as a diagnostic tool for identifying magnetic rotation and studying the interplay between different rotational modes.
