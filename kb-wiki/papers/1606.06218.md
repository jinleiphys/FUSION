# Examination of the sensitivity of the thermal fits to heavy-ion hadron yield data to the modeling of the eigenvolume interactions

> The hadron-resonance gas (HRG) model with the mass-proportional eigenvolume (EV) corrections is employed to fit the hadron yield data of the NA49 collaboration for central Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 6.3, 7.6, 8.8, 12.3,$ and $17.3$ GeV, the hadron midrapidity yield data of the STAR collaboration for Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV, and the hadron midrapidity yield data of the ALICE collaboration for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 2760$ GeV. At given bombarding energy, for a given set of radii, the EV HRG model fits do not just yield a single $T-μ_B$ pair, but a whole range of $T-μ_B$ pairs, each with similarly good fit quality. These pairs form a valley in the $T-μ_B$ plane along a line of nearly constant entropy per baryon, $S/A$, which increases nearly linearly with bombarding energy $E_{\rm lab}$. The entropy per baryon values extracted from the data at the different energies are a robust observable: it is almost independent of the details of the modeling of the eigenvolume interactions and of the specific $T-μ_B$ values obtained. These results show that the extraction of the chemical freeze-out temperature and chemical potential is extremely sensitive to the modeling of the short-range repulsion between the hadrons. This implies that the ideal point-particle HRG values are not unique. The wide range of the extracted $T$ and $μ_B$ values suggested by the eigenvolume HRG fits, as well as the approximately constant $S/A$ at freeze-out, are consistent with a non-equilibrium scenario of continuous freeze-out, where hadrons can be chemically frozen-out throughout the extended space-time regions during the evolution of the system. Even when the EV HRG fits are restricted to modest temperatures suggested by lattice QCD, the strong systematic effects of EV interactions are observed.

## Key claim

The extraction of chemical freeze-out temperature and baryochemical potential from heavy-ion hadron yield data is extremely sensitive to the modeling of short-range repulsive interactions between hadrons via eigenvolume corrections; the ideal point-particle hadron-resonance gas values are not unique, and a wide range of temperature-chemical potential pairs yield similarly good fits, forming a valley of nearly constant entropy per baryon.

## Method

The authors employed the hadron-resonance gas (HRG) model with mass-proportional eigenvolume corrections, using two different multi-component formulations ("Diagonal" and "Crossterms" models), to fit hadron yield data from NA49 (central Pb+Pb at five SPS energies), STAR (central Au+Au at 200 GeV), and ALICE (central Pb+Pb at 2760 GeV). The bag-like constant epsilon_0 was varied to correspond to effective hard-core proton radii between 0.0 and 0.6 fm, and fits were performed in the grand canonical ensemble assuming Boltzmann statistics with full chemical equilibrium.

## Key numbers

- At sqrt(s_NN) = 6.3 GeV (NA49), the point-particle HRG gives T = 138 MeV, mu_B = 486 MeV, chi^2/N_dof = 25.6/6; the "Crossterms" EV model (r_p = 0.5 fm) gives T = 233 MeV, mu_B = 838 MeV, chi^2/N_dof = 9.1/6
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 17.3 GeV (NA49), the point-particle HRG gives T = 148 MeV, mu_B = 254 MeV, chi^2/N_dof = 44.8/10; the "Crossterms" EV model (r_p = 0.5 fm) gives T = 320 MeV, mu_B = 578 MeV, chi^2/N_dof = 7.0/10
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (STAR), the point-particle HRG gives T = 157 MeV, mu_B = 25 MeV, chi^2/N_dof = 9.6/7; the "Crossterms" EV model (r_p = 0.5 fm) gives T = 190 MeV, mu_B = 31 MeV, chi^2/N_dof = 8.4/7
- At sqrt(s_NN) = 2760 GeV (ALICE), the point-particle HRG gives T = 153 MeV, mu_B = 0 (fixed), chi^2/N_dof = 32.9/10; the "Crossterms" EV model (r_p = 0.5 fm) gives T = 330 MeV, mu_B = 0 (fixed), chi^2/N_dof = 8.8/10
- Entropy per baryon S/A at the global minima for the "Crossterms" EV model (r_p = 0.5 fm) ranges from 14.4 (at 6.3 GeV) to 35.0 (at 17.3 GeV), increasing only 5-15% from point-particle values despite massive changes in T and mu_B
- The energy per hadron E/N at the global minima for EV models is typically 1.3-1.4 GeV, compared to approximately 1 GeV for the point-particle case

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Vovchenko and Stoecker (2015) at LHC energies to finite baryochemical potential by fitting NA49 and STAR data, and it contradicts the common assumption from Cleymans and Redlich (1998) that the energy per hadron criterion (E/N ~ 1 GeV) is robust against eigenvolume corrections. The results show that the constant entropy per baryon extracted from fits is robust and consistent with a continuous freeze-out scenario, as previously suggested by transport model simulations (ContFrz1, ContFrz2). The findings enable future studies of chemical freeze-out as a continuous process rather than a single hypersurface event, and motivate further development of eigenvolume HRG models that remain consistent with lattice QCD constraints at moderate temperatures.
