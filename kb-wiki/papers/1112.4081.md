# Effect of temperature-dependent eta/s on flow anisotropies

> We investigate the effects of a temperature-dependent shear viscosity over entropy density ratio eta/s on the flow anisotropy coefficients v_2 and v_4 in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. We find that v_4 is more sensitive to the viscosity at low temperatures than v_2. At RHIC v_2 is mostly affected by the viscosity around the phase transition, but the larger the collision energy, the more the quark-gluon plasma viscosity affects v_2.

## Key claim

The temperature where the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio (η/s) most strongly affects the flow anisotropy coefficients v₂ and v₄ shifts upward with increasing collision energy, and v₄ is generally more sensitive to viscosity at lower temperatures than v₂, but this sensitivity pattern depends on the specific collision system and energy.

## Method

The authors modeled the space-time evolution of matter in heavy-ion collisions using relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance and zero net-baryon number. They employed a lattice-based equation of state with chemical freeze-out at T = 150 MeV, initial energy density proportional to binary nucleon-nucleon collision density at τ₀ = 1.0 fm, and four different temperature-dependent parametrizations of η/s with a fixed minimum of 0.08 at T = 180 MeV. Freeze-out was implemented via the Cooper-Frye formula at T_dec = 100 MeV including dissipative corrections δf, and the shear relaxation time was taken as τ_R = 5η/(e+p).

## Key numbers

- Minimum η/s fixed at 0.08 at T = 180 MeV for all parametrizations
- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T = 150 MeV
- Freeze-out decoupling temperature: T_dec = 100 MeV
- Initial time τ₀ = 1.0 fm
- Peak η/s value of 0.24 at variable T_peak with width 10 MeV used in sensitivity study
- Collision energies studied: √s_NN = 200 GeV (RHIC Au+Au), 2.76 TeV (LHC Pb+Pb), and 5.5 TeV (full LHC Pb+Pb)
- Fictitious boson mass used in time-evolution study: m = 140 MeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies that assumed a constant η/s (e.g., IS formalism) by introducing temperature-dependent parametrizations, building on lattice QCD equation of state parametrizations (Huovinen 2009) and initial-state models (Eskola 2005). The results enable more precise extraction of the temperature dependence of η/s from experimental flow data at RHIC and LHC, and suggest that higher-order flow harmonics (v₄) may provide complementary constraints on viscosity at lower temperatures than v₂.
