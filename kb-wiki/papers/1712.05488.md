# A 4-dimensional Langevin approach to low-energy nuclear fission of $^{236}$U

> We developed a four-dimensional Langevin model which can treat the deformation of each fragment independently and applied it to low energy fission of 236U, the compound system of the reaction n+$^{235}$U. The potential energy is calculated with the deformed two-centerWoods-Saxon (TCWS) and the Nilsson type potential with the microscopic energy corrections following the Strutinsky method and BCS pairing. The transport coefficients are calculated by macroscopic prescriptions. It turned out that the deformation for the light and heavy fragments behaves differently, showing a sawtooth structure similar to that of the neutron multiplicities of the individual fragments $ν$(A). Furthermore, the measured total kinetic energy TKE(A) and its standard deviation are reproduced fairly well by the 4D Langevin model based on the TCWS potential in addition to the fission fragment mass distributions. The developed model allows a multi-parametric correlation analysis among, e.g., the three key fission observables, mass, TKE, and neutron multiplicity, which should be essential to elucidate several long-standing open problems in fission such as the sharing of the excitation energy between the fragments.

## Key claim

The 4D Langevin model, which treats the deformation of each fission fragment independently, reproduces the measured total kinetic energy TKE(A), its standard deviation, and fission fragment mass distributions for low-energy fission of 236U, while the 3D model with identical fragment deformations cannot quantitatively match the experimental TKE values.

## Method

The authors used a four-dimensional Langevin model with collective variables {z0/R0, δ1, δ2, α} representing elongation, independent deformations of light and heavy fragments, and mass asymmetry. The potential energy was calculated using a deformed two-center Woods-Saxon (TCWS) potential with microscopic energy corrections from the Strutinsky method and BCS pairing, while transport coefficients were computed via the Werner-Wheeler approximation for the mass tensor and the wall-and-window model (ks=0.27) for friction. Langevin trajectories started inside the saddle point and evolved until the neck radius reached zero at scission.

## Key numbers

- For 236U at Ex=7 MeV, the 4D model gives σTKE = 9.65 MeV, compared to experimental pre-neutron emission values of 10.65 MeV and post-neutron values of 10.85 MeV (Duke 2015)
- At Ex=20 MeV, the 4D model yields σTKE = 11.72 MeV, versus experimental pre-neutron 11.18 MeV and post-neutron 11.44 MeV
- The mean prescission kinetic energy (PKE) at Ex=7 MeV is 18.56 MeV, approximately 10% of the total kinetic energy
- The damping energy for shell corrections was set to Ed=20 MeV in the Ignatyuk prescription
- The neck parameter ε was fixed at 0.35 throughout the calculations

## Context

This work extends previous 3D Langevin models (Usang 2016) that could not reproduce TKE quantitatively, and contrasts with the random-walk method on a 5D potential energy surface (Moeller 2001, Randrup 2011) which cannot treat energy dissipation or prescission dynamics. The 4D model enables multi-parametric correlation analysis among mass, TKE, and neutron multiplicity, addressing long-standing problems such as excitation energy sharing between fragments. The sawtooth structure of fragment deformation shows remarkable similarity to the prompt neutron multiplicity ν(A) first noted by Wilkins (1976).
