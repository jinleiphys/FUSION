# Relaxation Times for Chiral Transport Phenomena and Spin Polarization in Strongly Coupled Plasma

> We compute the dynamical relaxation times for chiral transport phenomena in strongly coupled regime using the AdS/CFT correspondence. These relaxation times can be a useful proxy for the dynamical time scale for achieving equilibrium spin-polarization of quasi-particles in the presence of magnetic field and fluid vorticity. We identify the Kubo relations for these relaxation times and clarify some previous issues regarding time-dependence of the Chiral Vortical Effect. We study the consequences of imposing time-reversal invariance on parity-odd thermal noise fluctuations that are related to chiral transport coefficients by the fluctuation-dissipation relation. We find that time-reversal invariance dictates the equality between some of the chiral transport coefficients as well as their relaxation times.

## Key claim

The paper computes dynamical relaxation times for chiral transport phenomena in strongly coupled plasma using the AdS/CFT correspondence, but the authors emphasize that these relaxation times should be viewed as a characteristic dynamical time scale rather than a simple Israel-Stewart relaxation time, since the real response of the system may not follow that simple form.

## Method

The authors derive Kubo relations for the relaxation times of four chiral transport coefficients (σ_B, σ_V, σ_ε^B, σ_ε^V) by including next-to-leading order derivative terms in the constitutive relations of chiral hydrodynamics. They then compute these relaxation times numerically using the AdS/CFT correspondence, extracting them from retarded correlation functions of energy-momenta and currents after stripping off shear-diffusion pole structures. The computation also imposes time-reversal invariance on parity-odd thermal noise fluctuations via the fluctuation-dissipation relation.

## Key numbers

- The leading order chiral transport coefficients are given by σ_B = Cμ, σ_V = σ_ε^B = (1/2)Cμ², and σ_ε^V = (2/3)Cμ³, where C = 1/(4π²)
- The shear diffusion constant γ_η = η/(ε+p) is computed using the Kovtun-Son-Starinets result η = s/(4π)
- For the second order coefficient ξ_5 in perturbative QCD, the paper cites a previous result: ξ_5 ≈ -0.5/(α_s² log(1/α_s)) × σ_B/T for two-flavor QCD in leading log

## Context

This work extends previous studies of frequency-dependent chiral conductivities in both weakly coupled regimes (Kharzeev 2009, Son 2012, Kharzeev 2016) and strongly coupled regimes using AdS/CFT (Yee 2009, Amado 2011, Landsteiner 2013). The paper clarifies that the retarded correlation function for the Chiral Vortical Effect computed in Landsteiner (2013) is not directly equal to the frequency-dependent transport coefficient, resolving previous confusion about a vanishing relaxation time. The results provide a proxy for the dynamical time scale of spin polarization relevant to Lambda hyperon spin polarization measurements at RHIC (STAR 2017).
