# The QCD Equation of state and critical end-point estimates at $\mathcal O(μ_B^6)$

> We present results for the QCD Equation of State at non-zero chemical potentials corresponding to the conserved charges in QCD using Taylor expansion upto sixth order in the baryon number, electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials. The latter two are constrained by the strangeness neutrality and a fixed electric charge to baryon number ratio. In our calculations, we use the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) discretization scheme at physical quark masses and at different values of the lattice spacings to control lattice cut-off effects. Furthermore we calculate the pressure along lines of constant energy density, which serve as proxies for the freeze-out conditions and discuss their dependence on $μ_B$ , which is necessary for hydrodynamic modelling near freezeout. We also provide an estimate of the radius of convergence of the Taylor series from the 6th order coefficients which provides a new constraint on the location of the critical end-point in the T-$μ_B$ plane of the QCD phase diagram.

## Key claim

The paper provides continuum-extrapolated lattice QCD results for the equation of state at finite baryon chemical potential up to sixth order in the Taylor expansion, and uses the sixth-order coefficients to place a lower bound on the radius of convergence of the baryon number susceptibility, thereby excluding the existence of a critical end-point for \(\mu_B/T \leq 2\) and temperatures \(145 \leq T \leq 155\) MeV, but only under the condition that the electric charge and strangeness chemical potentials are set to zero for the radius-of-convergence analysis.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD calculations using the Highly Improved Staggered Quark (HISQ) discretization with physical quark masses (pion mass \(m_\pi = 140\) MeV for \(T \leq 175\) MeV, and \(m_\pi = 160\) MeV at higher temperatures) on lattices with temporal extent \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 12, 16\) and spatial extent \(N_s = 4 N_\tau\). They computed the Taylor expansion coefficients of the pressure up to sixth order in the baryon chemical potential \(\mu_B\), imposing strangeness neutrality (\(n_S = 0\)) and a fixed electric charge to baryon number ratio (\(n_Q/n_B = 0.4\)) to match heavy-ion collision conditions. The radius of convergence of the baryon number susceptibility was estimated from ratios of the sixth-order to fourth-order Taylor coefficients, but only for the case \(\mu_S = \mu_Q = 0\).

## Key numbers

* The Taylor expansion for pressure is reported to be convergent for \(\mu_B/T < 2.5\), with the sixth-order contribution becoming important for temperatures \(T > 170\) MeV.
* For the net baryon number density, terms up to \(\mathcal O(\mu_B^6)\) are insufficient for \(\mu_B/T > 2\), as convergence between different truncation orders is not observed.
* The chiral crossover temperature is given as \(T_c = 154(9)\) MeV, cited from Bazavov (2011).
* The radius-of-convergence analysis rules out a critical end-point for \(\mu_B/T \leq 2\) and temperatures in the range \(145 \leq T \leq 155\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD equation-of-state calculations at zero baryon density (Borsanyi 2013, Bazavov 2014) to finite baryon chemical potential using Taylor expansion, building on the methodology of Allton (2002, 2003, 2005) and Gavai (2003). The results provide continuum-extrapolated input for hydrodynamic modeling of heavy-ion collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies, particularly for \(\sqrt{s} \sim 11\) GeV where \(\mu_B/T \leq 2.5\). The radius-of-convergence estimates are compared with calculations using imaginary chemical potential (D'Elia 2016), reweighting (Fodor 2004), and Taylor expansions in 2-flavor QCD (Datta 2016), and the authors note that extending to eighth-order coefficients is needed to provide more robust bounds on the critical end-point location.
