# Baryon Spectra in Deformed Oscillator Quark Model

> We study theoretically the baryon spectra in terms of a deformed oscillator quark (DOQ) model. This model is motivated by confinement of quarks and chiral symmetry breaking, which are the most important non-perturbative phenomena of QCD. The minimization of the DOQ hamiltonian with respect to the deformation for each principal quantum number results in deformations for the intrinsic states of excited baryonic states. We find that the resulting baryon spectra agree very well with the existing experimental data. The spatial deformation of the baryonic excited states carry useful information on the quark confinement and provide a clue to understand the confining mechanism.

## Key claim

The deformed oscillator quark (DOQ) model, with only one free parameter (the oscillator parameter \(\omega_0\)), reproduces the observed baryon excitation spectra very well, provided that the intrinsic states for each principal quantum number are deformed and that the rotational band structure is projected. The abstract's claim of "very good" agreement is hedged by the fact that theoretical masses of \(1/2^-\) states appear systematically lower than experiments, and that the \(\Lambda(1405)\) and \(\Lambda(1520)\) are much lighter than the model prediction.

## Method

The authors construct a non-relativistic quark model with a deformed harmonic oscillator confinement potential, imposing volume conservation (\(\omega_x \omega_y \omega_z = \omega_0^3\)). For each principal quantum number \(N = N_x + N_y + N_z\), the intrinsic energy is minimized with respect to deformation, yielding spherical (\(N=0\)), prolate 2:1 (\(N=1\)), and prolate 3:1 (\(N=2\)) shapes. Angular momentum projection via the Hill-Wheeler method and a cranking moment of inertia then produce rotational bands, which are coupled to the intrinsic spin \(S=1/2\) or \(3/2\) of the three quarks to construct baryon masses.

## Key numbers

- The oscillator parameter \(\omega_0\) is determined to be 607 MeV from the average mass splitting between the first excited \(1/2^+\) (Roper-like) states and the corresponding ground states for flavor SU(3) baryons.
- The lowest three intrinsic states are \(N=0\) (spherical), \(N=1\) (prolate with ratio 2:1), and \(N=2\) (prolate with ratio 3:1).
- The \(N=0\) state is identified with the nucleon; the \(N=1\) state with negative parity states around 1520 MeV; the \(N=2\) state with positive parity excited states.
- The \(5/2^-\) state \(D_{15}(2200)\) (two-star rating) is noted as likely forming a spin doublet with \(G_{17}(2190)\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier suggestion by TDD (1980s) and Bhaduri (1980s) that excited baryons such as the Roper are deformed, and that the baryon spectrum exhibits a rotational band structure. It contrasts with the non-relativistic quark model of Isgur and Karl (1970s-1980s) which includes various perturbative terms, and with the group-theoretical model of Iachello and collaborators. The DOQ model provides a simple, parameter-economical framework that enables future calculations of gamma transitions to distinguish between models, and motivates experimental searches for higher-spin states at facilities such as CEBAF, SPring-8, and the LISS project at Indiana.
