# Theory status of quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions

## Key claim

Conventional nuclear-matter effects (nuclear break-up and shadowing) were expected to affect all $\Upsilon(nS)$ states equally at LHC energies due to the large Lorentz boost, but CMS measurements in $p$Pb collisions at 5 TeV revealed a significant relative suppression of the $\Upsilon(2S)$ and $\Upsilon(3S)$ with respect to the $\Upsilon(1S)$, contradicting these expectations and implying that such effects could account for up to half of the sequential suppression previously attributed to quark-gluon plasma formation in PbPb collisions.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical models for quarkonium production in proton-nucleus collisions, comparing predictions from a Glauber Monte Carlo code (Jin) that implements nuclear parton distribution functions (nPDFs) with impact-parameter dependence and a nuclear break-up survival probability parametrized by $\sigma_{\mathrm{break-up}}$. The comover interaction model (CIM), which accounts for final-state scatterings with comoving hadrons over times long enough for the quarkonium to form, is compared to data from PHENIX, ALICE, and CMS for both charmonia and bottomonia excited states.

## Key numbers

- CMS double ratio $[\Upsilon(2S)/\Upsilon(1S)]_{pPb}/[\Upsilon(2S)/\Upsilon(1S)]_{pp} = 0.83 \pm 0.05\,(\text{stat.}) \pm 0.05\,(\text{syst.})$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ TeV
- CMS double ratio $[\Upsilon(3S)/\Upsilon(1S)]_{pPb}/[\Upsilon(3S)/\Upsilon(1S)]_{pp} = 0.71 \pm 0.08\,(\text{stat.})\pm 0.09\,(\text{syst.})$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5$ TeV
- CMS double ratio $[\Upsilon(2S)/\Upsilon(1S)]_{PbPb}/[\Upsilon(2S)/\Upsilon(1S)]_{pp} = 0.21 \pm 0.07\,(\text{stat.}) \pm 0.02\,(\text{syst.})$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV
- CMS double ratio $[\Upsilon(3S)/\Upsilon(1S)]_{PbPb}/[\Upsilon(3S)/\Upsilon(1S)]_{pp} = 0.06 \pm 0.06\,(\text{stat.}) \pm 0.06\,(\text{syst.})$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV
- PHENIX ratio $[\psi'/J/\psi]_{dAu}/[\psi'/J/\psi]_{pp} = 0.68 \pm 0.14 ^{+0.13}_{-0.08} \pm 0.16$ at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- Quarkonium formation time at LHC (5 TeV, $y=0$): $\gamma \simeq 2660$, formation time $800 \div 1000$ fm/$c$ in the nuclear-matter frame
- Quarkonium formation time at RHIC (200 GeV, $y=0$): $\gamma \simeq 107$, formation time $\simeq 30$ fm/$c$

## Context

This proceedings contribution reviews the theoretical status following the surprising CMS observation (2013) of $\Upsilon(2S,3S)$ suppression in $p$Pb collisions, which challenged the earlier expectation from Vogt (2010), Ferreiro (2011), and Arleo (2012) that excited states would be suppressed equally to the ground state. The work extends the simple nuclear break-up and nPDF framework (EPS09 from Eskola 2009, nDSg from de Florian 2003) to include the comover interaction model (CIM) from Ferreiro (2012) as the only approach that describes the relative suppression without tuning parameters. The results enable improved interpretation of quarkonium suppression in nucleus-nucleus collisions and motivate further measurements of non-prompt $\psi'$ from $b$-hadron decays by LHCb and ATLAS to disentangle initial-state from final-state nuclear effects.
