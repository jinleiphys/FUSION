# Quark Initial State Interaction in Deep Inelastic Scattering and the Drell-Yan process

> We pursue a phenomenological study of higher twist effects in high energy processes by taking into account the off-shellness (virtuality) of partons bound in the nucleon. The effect of parton off-shellness in deep inelastic ep->eX scattering and the Drell-Yan process (pp->l+ l- X) is examined. Assuming factorization and a single-parameter Breit-Wigner form for the parton spectral function, we develop a model to calculate the corresponding off-shell cross sections. Allowing for a finite parton width of the order of 100 MeV, we reproduce the data of both DIS and the triple differential Drell-Yan cross section without an additional K-factor. The results are compared to those from perturbative QCD and the intrinsic-kT approach.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that modeling parton off-shellness (virtuality) via a Breit-Wigner spectral function with a finite width of approximately 100 MeV can reproduce both deep inelastic scattering and Drell-Yan data without an additional K-factor, but the effect is substantial only for the Drell-Yan transverse momentum distribution, not for inclusive DIS.

## Method

The authors assume factorization and model parton virtuality by introducing a single-parameter Breit-Wigner spectral function A(m,Γ) = (1/π) × Γ/(m² + ¼Γ²) for the parton off-shellness distribution. They calculate the off-shell partonic cross sections for electron scattering off a virtual quark (DIS) and for annihilation of an off-shell quark-antiquark pair into dileptons (Drell-Yan), then convolve these with unintegrated parton distributions that include both transverse momentum and off-shellness. The hadronic cross sections are obtained by integrating over parton masses, momentum fractions, and transverse momenta, with the parton width Γ treated as a constant parameter determined by comparison to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Parton spectral function width Γ ≈ 100 MeV reproduces both DIS and Drell-Yan data without an additional K-factor
- At Q² = 1 GeV², the DIS cross section deviation from the parton model reaches 50% when both the parton spectral function width and mean transverse momentum are 300 MeV
- The off-shellness effect in DIS is at most 10% of the cross section when separated from intrinsic transverse momentum effects
- For Q² above 25 GeV², the initial state interaction in DIS gives at most a 5% deviation from the lowest order cross section
- At Q² ≤ 2 GeV², the difference between off-shell and leading order DIS cross section is 30-40%

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological study of higher twist effects in high energy processes, building on the factorization approach of Ji (1998) and the intrinsic-kT approach of several authors (kt1, kt2, kt2a). The paper contrasts with leading order perturbative QCD, which predicts a delta function at zero transverse momentum for Drell-Yan pairs, and with the intrinsic-kT approach, which cannot simultaneously reproduce the magnitude and shape of the experimentally observed transverse momentum distribution. The model enables a consistent treatment of both parton off-shellness and intrinsic transverse momentum to go beyond the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation in these reactions, and the authors note that the triple differential Drell-Yan cross section is more sensitive to initial state interactions than the inclusive DIS cross section.
