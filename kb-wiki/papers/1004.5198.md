# Neutron Stars with Bose-Einstein Condensation of Antikaons as MIT Bags

> We investigate the properties of an antikaon in medium, regarding itas a MIT bag. We first construct the MIT bag model for a kaon with$σ^*$ and $ϕ$ in order to describe the interaction of$s$-quarks in hyperonic matter in the framework of the modifiedquark-meson coupling model. The coupling constant $g'^{B_K}_σ$in the density-dependent bag constant $B(σ)$ is treated as afree parameter to reproduce the optical potential of a kaon in asymmetric matter and all other couplings are determined by usingSU(6) symmetry and the quark counting rule. With various values ofthe kaon potential, we calculate the effective mass of a kaon inmedium to compare it with that of a point-like kaon. We thencalculate the population of octet baryons, leptons and $K^-$ and theequation of state for neutron star matter. The results show thatkaon condensation in hyperonic matter is sensitive to the $s$-quarkinteraction and also to the way of treating the kaon. The mass andthe radius of a neutron star are obtained by solving theTolmann-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation.

# Neutron Stars with Bose-Einstein Condensation of Antikaons as MIT Bags

## Key claim

Treating an antikaon as an extended MIT bag rather than a point-like particle significantly alters the predicted onset density of kaon condensation in neutron star matter, with the effect being particularly sensitive to the inclusion of s-quark interactions via σ* and φ meson exchange; when hyperons are included, kaon condensation becomes a sub-leading correction to the dominant hyperon contribution, and the maximum neutron star mass varies by only about 3% depending on the kaon optical potential value.

## Method

The authors construct a modified quark-meson coupling (MQMC) model in which both baryons and kaons are treated as MIT bags, with quarks interacting through σ, ω, ρ, σ*, and φ meson fields in mean-field approximation. The coupling constant g'^{B_K}_σ in the density-dependent bag constant B(σ) for the kaon is treated as a free parameter to reproduce chosen kaon optical potentials (U_K = -120, -140, -160 MeV), while all other couplings are determined by SU(6) symmetry and the quark counting rule. The equations of state and particle populations for neutron star matter containing octet baryons, leptons, and K^- are computed, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equation is solved to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Kaon bag parameters: B_K^{1/4} = 170.752 MeV, Z_K = 1.152, with bag radius R_K = 0.4 fm and s-quark mass m_s = 150 MeV
- For npK^- matter (no hyperons), kaon condensation onset densities are 3.6, 3.2, and 2.9 times nuclear saturation density ρ_0 (0.17 fm^{-3}) for U_K = -120, -140, and -160 MeV respectively
- For npYK^- matter (with hyperons, no s-quark interactions), onset densities are 6.8, 4.5, and 3.8 ρ_0 for U_K = -120, -140, and -160 MeV respectively
- For npYK^-φ matter (with hyperons and s-quark interactions), kaon condensation does not occur up to 10 ρ_0 for U_K = -120 MeV; onset densities are 5.1 and 3.8 ρ_0 for U_K = -140 and -160 MeV respectively
- Maximum neutron star masses for npK^- matter: 1.78, 1.67, and 1.55 M_⊙ for U_K = -120, -140, and -160 MeV, with radii of 12-13 km
- Maximum masses for npYK^- matter: 1.49, 1.48, and 1.44 M_⊙ for U_K = -120, -140, and -160 MeV
- Maximum masses for npYK^-φ matter: 1.54, 1.54, and 1.50 M_⊙ for U_K = -120, -140, and -160 MeV
- For point-like kaon comparison (npYK^- matter), onset densities are 5.9, 3.8, and 3.0 ρ_0 for U_K = -120, -140, and -160 MeV, with maximum masses in range (1.45-1.61) M_⊙

## Context

This work extends previous QMC model studies of kaon condensation by Saito (1998) and Menezes (2006) to include s-quark interactions through σ* and φ meson exchange, as well as density-dependent bag constants following the MQMC approach of Jin (1994) and Ryu (2005). The results contrast with those from quantum-hadrodynamics (QHD) models by Glendenning (1997) and Banik (2002), where point-like kaons produce much softer equations of state and smaller neutron star radii (about 8 km for U_K = -140 MeV). The finding that hyperon effects dominate over kaon condensation when both are included suggests that future work should focus on constraining hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions, as the role of kaons may be within the uncertainties of current nuclear saturation properties and model parameters.
