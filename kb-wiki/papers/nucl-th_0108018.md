# Photon and Electron induced Particle Production on Nuclei

> We discuss several particle production reactions on nuclei within a BUU transport model. After a short presentation of the model we show our results for the transparency ratio for the nucleon knock-out reaction in different nuclei. The influence of the nucleon spectral function on subthreshold particle production is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a semiclassical BUU transport model can successfully describe photon- and electron-induced particle production on nuclei, but the agreement with data (e.g., transparency ratios for Fe and Au) is limited to the measured momentum transfer range, and the model cannot simulate the coherence effect of shadowing above 1 GeV without a separate Glauber framework.

## Method

The authors use a Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) transport model that solves coupled integral-differential equations for nucleons, nucleon resonances (P33(1232), P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535)), and mesons (pi, eta, rho, K). Photon or virtual photon absorption is treated as an elementary reaction on a single nucleon inside the nucleus, with final-state interactions including scattering, absorption, and side-feeding. For high energies with many unmeasured multiparticle channels, the string fragmentation model FRITIOF is used. The nucleon spectral function is incorporated by modifying the test-particle equations of motion to account for off-shell nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Transparency ratio T for Fe and Au as a function of momentum transfer Q^2: the model shows very good agreement with data from SLAC (O'Neill) and TJNAF (JLab) across the measured Q^2 range.
- For Calcium at E_gamma = 0.8 GeV, the center-of-mass energy distribution for gamma-N pairs shows that the full nucleon spectral function produces contributions at sqrt(s) values larger than the maximum expected from the Thomas-Fermi model (step function at Fermi momentum p_F).
- Short-range correlations move approximately 10% of strength from momenta below p_F to momenta above p_F in the nucleon momentum distribution.

## Context

This work extends the BUU transport model approach previously applied to meson production in the nucleon resonance region (Effenberger et al., 1997) to higher photon energies and electron-induced nucleon knockout. The transparency ratio calculations for (e,e'p) reactions connect to the search for color transparency, with prior calculations by Miller and Golubeva. The inclusion of the nucleon spectral function builds on work by Benhar, Ciofi degli Atti, and others on short-range correlations, and the off-shell transport formalism follows Leupold (2000) and Effenberger (off-shell implementation). Future work will extend calculations to larger Q^2 where an onset of color transparency is expected, and to subthreshold photoproduction using the spectral function.
