# Signatures for flow effects in s**(1/2) = 200 GeV proton-proton collisions

> A simple model based on relativistic geometry and final-state hadronic rescattering is used to predict pion source parameters extracted in two-pion femtoscopy studies of proton-proton collisions at s**(1/2) = 200 GeV. From studying the momentum and particle multiplicity dependences of these parameters in the context of this model and assuming a very short hadronization time, flow-like behavior is seen which resembles the flow behavior commonly observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

Using a simple model based on relativistic geometry and final-state hadronic rescattering with a very short hadronization time (0.1 fm/c), flow-like behavior in the momentum and multiplicity dependences of two-pion femtoscopy radius parameters can be reproduced in proton-proton collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV, resembling the flow behavior observed in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.

## Method

The model proceeds in four steps: (1) generating proton-proton collisions using the PYTHIA event generator (version 6.409) in minimum-bias mode; (2) assigning space-time coordinates to hadronization assuming all particles hadronize at the same proper time tau = 0.1 fm/c from a thin uniform disk of radius 1 fm; (3) simulating final-state hadronic rescattering with a semi-classical Monte Carlo calculation using isospin-averaged cross sections for meson scattering from Prakash et al. (1993) and other cross sections from the Review of Particle Physics; and (4) extracting three-dimensional Gaussian HBT radius parameters (R_side, R_out, R_long) from the two-pion correlation function in the LCMS frame.

## Key numbers

- Hadronization proper time tau set to 0.1 fm/c, consistent with a previous Tevatron study
- Transverse disk radius of 1 fm used for the initial emission geometry
- Pion kinematic cuts: -0.5 < y < 0.5 (rapidity), 0.15 < p_T < 0.8 GeV/c, and 0.15 < k_T < 0.25 GeV/c for the sample correlation function
- Lambda parameter from the sample Gaussian fit is 0.330, far less than the ideal value of unity
- Rescattering calculation carried out to 50 fm/c with time steps of 0.1 fm/c
- 5 x 10^6 events generated with sqrt(s) = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the study of flow-like effects from relativistic heavy-ion collisions, where thousands of particles participate, to proton-proton collisions at the same center-of-mass energy where typically fewer than 100 particles are produced. The model predictions show qualitative agreement with the k_T and rapidity density dependences of radius parameters measured by STAR in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV (Adams et al., 2004), and preliminary STAR measurements in proton-proton collisions at the same energy (Chajecki et al., 2005) show a similarly flat ratio dependence on k_T. The results suggest that hadronic rescattering, sensitively controlled by a short hadronization time, is the main driver of the observed flow-like effects, and that the hadronization time in these collisions appears to be very short.
