# Polarisation-Asymmetry Correlation in Allowed $β$-Decay: a Probe for Right-Handed Currents

> The sensitivity of polarisation-asymmetry correlation experiments to charged currents of right-handed chirality contributing to allowed $β$-decay is considered in the most general context possible, independently of any type of approximation nor of any specific model for physics beyond the Standard Model of the electroweak interactions. Results are then particularised to general Left-Right Symmetric Models, and experimental prospects offered by mirror nuclei are assessed explicitly on general grounds. In order of decreasing interest, the cases of $^{17}$F, $^{41}$Sc and $^{25}$Al are the most attractive, providing sensitivities better or comparable to allowed pure Gamow-Teller transitions, with the advantage however, that recoil order corrections are smaller in the case of super-allowed decays.

## Key claim

The relative longitudinal polarisation-asymmetry correlation in allowed β-decay offers enhanced sensitivity to right-handed charged currents compared to asymmetry measurements alone, but this sensitivity is optimal only when the experimental asymmetry approaches a specific value determined by the β particle velocity and the effective degree of nuclear polarisation.

## Method

The authors derived exact expressions for the relative longitudinal polarisation of β particles emitted from oriented nuclei, considering only vector and axial charged current contributions with arbitrary complex coupling coefficients and ignoring recoil order corrections. They then particularised these general results to general Left-Right Symmetric Models (LRSM) by expressing the four-fermi coupling coefficients in terms of the LRSM parameters: gauge boson masses M₁ and M₂, mixing angle ζ, CP-violating phase ω, gauge coupling ratio r = g_R/g_L, and the quark and lepton CKM matrix elements. The analysis identified mirror nuclei candidates by evaluating the asymmetry parameter A and the new-physics parameter Δ for each case.

## Key numbers

- The experimental lower limit on the mass M₂ of a charged heavy gauge boson W' in Manifest Left-Right Symmetric Models is M₂ > 652 GeV/c² (95% C.L.), from CDF.
- The ordinary W boson mass is M₁ = 80.22 ± 0.26 GeV/c².
- The optimal sensitivity condition is |A 𝒫| = β, where 𝒫 = |J(𝐩̂·𝐉̂)| is the effective degree of nuclear polarisation and β is the β particle velocity normalised to the speed of light.
- The optimal experimental asymmetry values are A_exp⁽⁰⁾(-J,0) = β² for the first polarisation configuration and A_exp⁽⁰⁾(-J,J) = 2β²/(1+β²) for the second configuration.
- The enhancement factor k(-J,0) diverges as A_exp(-J,0) approaches β², and k(-J,J) diverges as A_exp(-J,J) approaches 2β²/(1+β²).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Quin (1993) on polarisation-asymmetry correlations to the most general context of allowed β-decay with vector and axial currents, and particularises the results to general Left-Right Symmetric Models without assuming Manifest LRSM restrictions. The paper builds on the formalism of Jackson, Treiman and Wyld (1957) for allowed β-decay observables, and on the LRSM framework reviewed by Herczeg (1986) and Langacker (1988). The analysis identifies ¹⁷F, ⁴¹Sc and ²⁵Al as the most attractive mirror nuclei candidates, offering sensitivities comparable to or better than pure Gamow-Teller transitions such as ¹⁰⁷In (studied by Bodek et al., 1995) and ¹²N (studied by Quin et al., 1993), with the advantage of smaller recoil order corrections in super-allowed decays.
