# Centrality dependence of electroweak boson production in PbPb collisions at the LHC

> Recent data on the nuclear modification of W and Z boson production measured by the ATLAS collaboration in PbPb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm nn}}=5.02$ TeV show an enhancement in peripheral collisions, seemingly contradicting predictions of the Glauber model. The data were previously explained by arguing that the nucleon-nucleon cross section may be shadowed in nucleus-nucleus collisions, and hence suppressed compared to the proton-proton cross section at the same collision energy. This interpretation has quite significant consequences for the understanding of heavy-ion data, in particular in the context of the Glauber model. Instead, we provide an alternative explanation of the data by assuming that there is a mild bias present in the centrality determination of the measurement; on the size of the related systematic uncertainty. Using this assumption, we show that the data is in agreement with theoretical calculations using nuclear parton distribution functions. Finally, we speculate that the centrality dependence of the W$^-$/W$^{+}$ ratio may point to the relevance of a larger skin thickness of the Pb nucleus, which, if present, would result in a few percent larger PbPb cross section than currently accounted for in the Glauber model and may hence be the root of the centrality bias.

## Key claim

The apparent enhancement of W and Z boson nuclear modification factors in peripheral PbPb collisions, which contradicts Glauber model predictions, can be explained by a mild bias (on the order of 2-4%) in the centrality determination's anchor point, rather than by invoking shadowing of the nucleon-nucleon cross section.

## Method

The authors performed Glauber Monte Carlo simulations using TGlauberMC with σNN = 67.6 mb for PbPb collisions at √sNN = 5.02 TeV, shifting the anchor point by 2-4% to simulate missing peripheral events. They combined this with a multiplicity bias estimated from the HIJING-based HG-PYTHIA model, and constructed a reference model for the nuclear modification factor RAA that includes isospin dependence from NLO cross section calculations using MCFM with NNPDF3.1 parton distribution functions, along with the combined bias factor.

## Key numbers

- The fitted value of σNN = 41.5 +16.2 -12.0 mb proposed by Eskola et al. (2020) is only about 60% of the typical σNN = 67.6 ± 0.6 mb used at √sNN = 5.02 TeV
- An anchor point bias of 2-4% produces a combined bias effect that counteracts the multiplicity bias, leading to a small enhancement of up to about 5% (for 3% bias) or 10% (for 4% bias) in peripheral collisions
- The ATLAS Z-boson yield is approximately 5% higher than the CMS Z-boson yield when comparing integrated data from the two measurements
- The fractions of pp, pn, and nn collisions in central collisions are about f_pp = 0.16, f_pn = 0.48, and f_nn = 0.36, shifting to about 0.05, 0.45, and 0.50 in most peripheral collisions due to the neutron skin
- The theoretical predictions for RAA using nNNPDF2.0 give 0.933 ± 0.030 for Z bosons, 0.725 ± 0.022 for W+, and 1.085 ± 0.035 for W-; using EPPS16 give 0.960 ± 0.012 for Z, 0.753 ± 0.012 for W+, and 1.110 ± 0.017 for W-

## Context

This work provides an alternative to the explanation by Eskola et al. (2020), who argued that the ATLAS data required a shadowed nucleon-nucleon cross section of about 41.5 mb, which would have significant consequences for interpreting heavy-ion data using the Glauber model. The authors extend the multiplicity bias framework developed by Morsch (2017) for high-pT particle production, and show that a 3% anchor point bias brings the data into agreement with standard nuclear parton distribution function calculations (nNNPDF2.0 and EPPS16). The analysis enables future measurements to use electroweak boson production as a probe of the neutron skin thickness in lead nuclei, and suggests that the centrality dependence of the W-/W+ ratio may indicate a larger skin thickness than currently assumed.
