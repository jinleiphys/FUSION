# Reaction Studies of Lepton Number Violation

> Nuclear isotensor spectroscopy as accessible in nuclear double charge exchange (DCE) reactions is indispensable for quantitative studies of lepton number violation as in double beta decay (DBD). For such studies heavy ion double single charge exchange (DSCE) and direct Majorana double charge exchange (MDCE) reactions are discussed. Isotensor two-body transition densities are investigated for the first time. Pion-potentials, mirroring neutrino potentials, and isotensor short range correlations are explored. Lepton DCE (LDCE) reactions on nuclei at accelerators are introduced as a promising new approach to investigate lepton number violation.

## Key claim

Nuclear isotensor spectroscopy via double charge exchange (DCE) reactions is indispensable for quantitative studies of lepton number violation in double beta decay, but the actual utility depends on distinguishing between the sequential double single charge exchange (DSCE) and direct Majorana double charge exchange (MDCE) mechanisms, each probing different aspects of the isotensor response.

## Method

The paper develops theoretical frameworks for heavy-ion DSCE reactions (second-order in the nucleon-nucleon isovector T-matrix) and MDCE reactions (first-order in ion-ion interactions but second-order in the pion-nucleon isovector T-matrix per nucleus). For lepton DCE (LDCE) reactions, the author introduces a phenomenological approach based on the Left-Right Symmetric Model, extracting charged-current matrix elements from existing compilations to evaluate cross sections for (e-,e+) reactions on nuclear targets.

## Key numbers

- The pion potential correlation lengths are of order sqrt(<x^2>) ~ 1 fm, much shorter than the long-range double beta decay correlations from the neutrino potential.
- For the DSCE reaction 76Se(18O,18Ne)76Ge, the momentum-space isotensor two-body transition density for the double-Fermi component extends over momenta of ±400 MeV/c.
- At 10 GeV beam energy on a 208Pb target, the LDCE total cross section is sigma(e-e+) ~ |Gamma_BSM|^2 * 10^(-38) cm^2, with the dimensionless LNV parameter estimated as Gamma_BSM < 10^(-7).
- The LDCE total cross sections increase with energy as ~ E^3 and grow rapidly with target mass.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of heavy-ion DCE reactions developed by Lenske (2021, 2024) and the NUMEN collaboration (Cappuzzello, 2022), and provides the first theoretical exploration of (e-,e+) LDCE reactions on nuclei as a new approach to lepton number violation studies. The isotensor two-body transition densities are investigated for the first time, building on the first-ever study of n^2p^(-2) two-body transition density distributions by Bellone (2025) for the 76Se to 76Ge transition. The proposed LDCE approach bridges the gap between low-energy nuclear double beta decay experiments (such as GERDA, 2023) and high-energy collisional LNV experiments, with cross section estimates indicating feasibility at facilities like Jefferson Laboratory and the planned Electron-Ion Collider.
