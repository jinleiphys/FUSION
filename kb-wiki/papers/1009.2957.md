# Chiral effective field theory for nuclear matter

> We report on the recent developments of a new effective field theory for nuclear matter [1,2,3]. We present first the nuclear matter chiral power counting that takes into account both short-- and long--range inter-nucleon interactions. It also identifies non-perturbative strings of diagrams, related to the iteration of nucleon-nucleon interactions, which have to be re-summed. The methods of unitary chiral perturbation theory has been shown to be a useful tool in order to perform those resummations. Results up to next-to-leading order for the ground state energy per particle of nuclear matter, the in-medium chiral quark condensate and pion self-energy are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper presents a novel chiral effective field theory for nuclear matter that consistently treats both short- and long-range multi-nucleon interactions, with results up to next-to-leading order showing remarkable agreement with experimental saturation properties, though the symmetric nuclear matter case requires some fine-tuning of the subtraction constant \(g_0\) to a value of approximately \(-m_\pi^2\) while neutron matter uses the natural value of approximately \(-0.6\,m_\pi^2\).

## Method

The authors develop an in-medium chiral power counting that identifies non-perturbative strings of diagrams (two-nucleon reducible diagrams) which must be resummed, and apply unitary chiral perturbation theory (U\(\chi\)PT) to perform this resummation partial wave by partial wave. The interaction kernel \(N_{JI}\) is extracted by matching the chiral expansion of nucleon-nucleon partial waves with the geometric series expansion of the U\(\chi\)PT amplitude, using a once-subtracted dispersion relation with the subtraction constant chosen so that the unitarity loop function vanishes at \(k^2 = -m_\pi^2\). The in-medium nucleon propagator is used to extend vacuum results to nuclear matter by replacing the free-space loop function with its full in-medium equivalent.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density for symmetric nuclear matter: obtained in agreement with experiment (exact numerical value not given in text).
- Energy per particle at saturation: obtained in agreement with experiment (exact numerical value not given in text).
- Compression modulus: obtained in agreement with experiment (exact numerical value not given in text).
- Subtraction constant for neutron matter: \(g_0 = \widetilde{g}_0 \simeq -0.6\,m_\pi^2\), close to the natural size \(g_0 \simeq -0.54\,m_\pi^2\).
- Subtraction constant for symmetric nuclear matter: \(g_0 \simeq -m_\pi^2\) (fine-tuned), while \(\widetilde{g}_0\) kept at its natural value.
- The natural size for the subtraction constant from the dispersion relation: \(g_0 \sim -\frac{m m_\pi}{4\pi} = -0.54\,m_\pi^2\).

## Context

This work extends the chiral effective field theory approach to nuclear matter by Lacour, Meissner, and Oller (2009, 2010), building on the in-medium generalized vertex concept from previous work and the unitary chiral perturbation theory methods of Oller and collaborators (1998, 2000). The results enable systematic calculations of nuclear matter properties, the in-medium chiral quark condensate, and pion self-energy from first principles, and explain why pion-nucleon dynamics alone is phenomenologically successful for fitting pionic atom data, because all next-to-leading order contributions from nucleon-nucleon interactions to the pion self-energy mutually cancel. The findings also show that the Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation receives no violations at next-to-leading order in the nuclear medium.
