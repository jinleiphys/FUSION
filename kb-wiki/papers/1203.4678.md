# $dd\to {^3}He n$ reaction at intermediate energies

> The $dd\to ^3He n$ reaction is considered at the energies between 200 MeV and 520 MeV. The Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas equations are iterated up to the lowest order terms over the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix. The parameterized ${^3He}$ wave function including five components is used. The angular dependence of the differential cross section and energy dependence of tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$ at the zero scattering angle are presented in comparison with the experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that iterating the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations to only the lowest-order terms over the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix (including both one-nucleon-exchange and single-scattering diagrams) provides a reasonable description of the $dd\to {^3He} n$ reaction at intermediate energies, but the agreement with experimental differential cross sections degrades above 300 MeV, suggesting that additional mechanisms such as $\Delta$-isobar excitation may be needed.

## Method

The author starts from the AGS equations for the four-nucleon system and iterates them to first order in the nucleon-nucleon t-matrix, retaining only the one-nucleon-exchange (ONE) and single-scattering (SS) terms. The NN interaction is parameterized using the Love-Franey model fitted to the SP07 phase-shift analysis, and the ${^3He}$ wave function is a five-component parameterization fitted to Faddeev calculations with the CD-Bonn potential. No partial-wave decomposition is used; instead, the scattering amplitude is computed directly in momentum space with Lorentz transformations applied to relate the NN t-matrix from the center-of-mass frame to the laboratory frame.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross sections are calculated at three deuteron laboratory momenta: 1.109 GeV/c, 1.387 GeV/c, and 1.493 GeV/c, corresponding to deuteron kinetic energies of 300 MeV, 457 MeV, and 520 MeV, respectively.
- The single-scattering contribution is negligible at small scattering angles (t ~ 0.7–0.8 (GeV/c)^2) but reaches its maximum at 90 degrees.
- The tensor analyzing power $T_{20}$ is calculated as a function of energy at zero scattering angle and compared to RIKEN data from Ourexp (2009).

## Context

This work extends earlier one-nucleon-exchange (ONE) calculations by the same author (Ladygina, 2009) which failed to describe RIKEN polarization data except at very forward and backward angles (0–15 degrees and 165–180 degrees). It also builds on the AGS-based four-body approach previously applied at low energies (below 51.5 MeV) by other groups (PRC77, PRC76, PRC81) and at 231.8 MeV for deuteron-deuteron elastic scattering (dddd). The inclusion of the single-scattering term improves agreement with Saclay differential cross section data (Bizard, 1980) at 300 MeV but underestimates the data at higher energies, pointing toward the need for $\Delta$-isobar contributions as suggested in Bizard (1980).
