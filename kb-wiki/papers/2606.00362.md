# Impact of Future Dihadron Production Measurements on the Transversity Distributions and Tensor Charges of the Nucleon

> We assess the impact of future measurements of dihadron production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering from the CLAS12 and proposed SoLID experiments at Jefferson Lab, as well as from the ePIC experiment at the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC), on the transversity parton distribution functions (PDFs) and the corresponding tensor charges of the nucleon. To this end, we generate pseudo-data for these experiments for a proton target (CLAS12 and ePIC) and a $^3$He target (SoLID and ePIC), and we include these pseudo-data in the JAMDiFF global analysis of existing experimental dihadron data. We find that future data from Jefferson Lab will significantly reduce uncertainties in the transversity PDFs in the region of intermediate-to-large quark momentum fractions $x$, while the EIC will provide strong constraints across the entire range of $x$, allowing for the first experimental test of the predicted small-$x$ behavior of the transversity PDFs. In discussing the reduction of uncertainties in the tensor charges, we also compare the results from the data analyses with those from lattice QCD, highlighting scenarios in which compatibility or tension between the two would arise.

## Key claim

Future dihadron production measurements from CLAS12, SoLID, and ePIC will significantly reduce uncertainties in the nucleon transversity parton distribution functions (PDFs) and tensor charges, but the degree of improvement and potential tension with lattice QCD results depends on the actual experimental outcome, with alternative "low" and "high" scenarios showing qualitatively different compatibility with lattice calculations.

## Method

The authors generated pseudo-data for the transverse single-spin asymmetry (TSSA) in dihadron production from semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) for CLAS12 (proton target at 10.6 GeV), SoLID (³He target at 8.8 and 11 GeV), and ePIC (proton at 10x100 GeV and ³He at 10x166 GeV). These pseudo-data were included in the JAMDiFF global analysis framework, which uses Bayesian inference and Monte-Carlo replica sampling to simultaneously fit transversity PDFs and dihadron fragmentation functions (DiFFs) to existing data from Belle, HERMES, COMPASS, and STAR. The impact was quantified by comparing uncertainty bands and χ² values before and after inclusion of pseudo-data, with and without lattice QCD tensor charge constraints.

## Key numbers

- CLAS12: 9 pseudo-data points on a proton target at 10.6 GeV beam energy, with 5% normalization and 5% uncorrelated systematic errors, integrated luminosity 43.2 fb⁻¹
- SoLID: 715 pseudo-data points (reduced to 570 after cuts) on a ³He target at 8.8 and 11 GeV, with 3% normalization and 6.8% uncorrelated systematic errors, integrated luminosity 6220.8 fb⁻¹
- ePIC: 18 pseudo-data points on proton (10x100 GeV) and 19 on ³He (10x166 GeV), with 2.3% normalization and 5% uncorrelated systematic errors, integrated luminosity 10 fb⁻¹ (proton) and 8.65 fb⁻¹ (³He)
- χ²_red for CLAS12 pseudo-data without LQCD: 0.00; with LQCD: 0.22; with LQCD weighted: 2.24; "low" scenario with LQCD: 2.85
- χ²_red for SoLID pseudo-data without LQCD: 0.03; with LQCD: 0.04; with LQCD weighted: 0.09; "low" scenario with LQCD: 3.20
- χ²_red for ePIC pseudo-data (proton) without LQCD: 0.79; with LQCD: 1.28; with LQCD weighted: 3.49; "high" scenario with LQCD: 3.72
- χ²_red for ePIC pseudo-data (³He) without LQCD: 0.05; with LQCD: 1.19; with LQCD weighted: 4.70; "high" scenario with LQCD: 0.68
- Small-x constraint parameter α = 0.17 ± 0.085 for the transversity PDF behavior at initial scale μ₀ = 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the JAMDiFF global analysis framework (Cocuzza 2023) that previously extracted transversity PDFs and tensor charges from existing dihadron data in electron-positron annihilation (Belle), SIDIS (HERMES, COMPASS), and proton-proton collisions (STAR). The study complements a related impact analysis based on single-hadron production (Gamberg 2021). The results enable the first experimental test of the predicted small-x behavior of transversity PDFs (Kovchegov 2018) using EIC data, and allow comparison with lattice QCD tensor charge calculations from ETMC (Alexandrou 2021) and PNDME (Gupta 2018). The analysis also uses the Soffer positivity bound (Soffer 1994) as a theoretical constraint.
