# Charged-particle optical potentials tested by first direct measurement of 59Cu(p,alpha)56Ni reaction

> Due consideration of proton optical--model potential (OMP) anomalies at sub-Coulomb energies for medium--weight nuclei is shown to be critical for the analysis of the unprecedented measurement of $^{59}$Cu$(p,α)^{56}$Ni reaction cross section at an energy of $\sim$6 MeV [Phys. Rev. C {\bf 104}, L042801 (2021)]. The variation in predicted cross sections from standard statistical--model calculations and the cross--section range corresponding to the anomalous proton imaginary--potential depth, for target nuclei off the line of stability, are distinct and well separated. Consequently, the new measurement provides, under unique conditions, tests of proton isoscalar and isovector real--potential components, the anomalous imaginary potential, as well as previous alpha-particle OMP, for nuclei off the line of stability.

# Charged-particle optical potentials tested by first direct measurement of 59Cu(p,alpha)56Ni reaction

## Key claim

The new direct measurement of the 59Cu(p,α)56Ni reaction cross section at ~6 MeV provides a critical test of proton optical-model potential (OMP) anomalies at sub-Coulomb energies for medium-weight nuclei off the line of stability, but only if the anomalous imaginary-potential depth W(A) dependence near A~61 is properly accounted for rather than standard global or local OMPs.

## Method

Hauser-Feshbach statistical model calculations were performed for the 59Cu(p,α)56Ni reaction at a center-of-mass energy of 6 MeV, using the code TALYS-1.95 and additional calculations with various proton OMPs. The analysis compared results using the global Koning-Delaroche OMP, the local Saini et al. OMP with and without (N-Z)/A dependence adjustments, and the Kailas et al. OMP with the anomalous imaginary-potential depth W(E) derived from 59Co(p,n) data. Direct-interaction collective inelastic scattering within DWBA and pre-equilibrium emission were also considered.

## Key numbers

- The 59Cu(p,α)56Ni reaction Q-value is +2.413 MeV, and the first excited state of 56Ni lies at 2.701 MeV
- Standard Hauser-Feshbach calculations using the Koning-Delaroche OMP overestimate the measured cross section by a factor of ~2 at 6 MeV center-of-mass energy
- Direct-interaction and pre-equilibrium effects account for only ~4.5% decrease in the calculated (p,α) cross section (3.7% from DI, 0.8% from PE)
- Replacing the global OMP with the local Saini et al. OMP gives only ~6% decrease; including (N-Z)/A dependence gives an additional ~13% decrease
- The anomalous imaginary-potential depth W = 3.5 - 0.3E MeV from Kailas et al. (1975) for 59Co, with W values between 1 and 2 MeV for target nuclei with A=55-59, brings calculations into close agreement with the measured cross section
- Feeding of the 2.701 MeV first excited state of 56Ni is only ~4.4% of the calculated (p,α) cross section

## Context

This work extends the analysis of (p,α) reactions on stable Cu isotopes (63,65Cu) by Avrigeanu and Avrigeanu (2021) to the neutron-poor 59Cu nucleus off the stability line. It tests the anomalous imaginary-potential depth W(A) dependence near A~61 originally reported by Kailas et al. (1979), and validates the α-particle OMP of Avrigeanu et al. (2014) which had previously been found to overestimate the new measurement by a factor of 2 when combined with standard proton OMPs. The results complement recent measurements of 54Fe(p,α)51Mn by Lin et al. (2022) and 59Ni(n,p) and 59Ni(n,α) reactions by Sakuta et al. (2022), and enable improved modeling of charged-particle reactions on unstable nuclei for nuclear astrophysics and applications.
