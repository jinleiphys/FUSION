# Linearly polarized gluons and axial charge fluctuations in the Glasma

> We calculate of the one- and two-point correlation functions of the energy density and the divergence of the Chern-Simons current in the nonequilibrium Glasma state formed in a high-energy nuclear collision. We show that the latter depends on the difference of the total and linearly polarized gluon transverse momentum distributions. Since the divergence of the Chern-Simons current provides the source of axial charge, we infer information about the statistical properties of axial charge production at early times. We further develop a simple phenomenological model to characterize axial charge distributions in terms of distributions of the energy density.

## Key claim

The correlation function of the divergence of the Chern-Simons current (which sources axial charge) depends on the *difference* of the total and linearly polarized gluon transverse momentum distributions, whereas energy density correlations depend on their *sum*; this opens a connection between deep inelastic scattering correlation studies and local CP-violating fluctuations in hadronic collisions.

## Method

The authors calculate one- and two-point correlation functions of the energy density and the divergence of the Chern-Simons current in the Glasma state formed immediately after a high-energy nuclear collision, using the Color Glass Condensate framework with a Gaussian (glasma graph) approximation for the field correlators. They express the results in terms of the Weizsacker-Williams gluon distributions (unpolarized \(G^{(1)}\) and linearly polarized \(h^{(1)}_{\bot}\)) of the two nuclei, and evaluate them in both the Golec-Biernat Wusthoff (GBW) and McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) saturation models. They then develop a simple phenomenological model to characterize axial charge distributions in terms of energy density distributions.

## Key numbers

* The expectation value of the energy density is \(\langle \varepsilon(\xt) \rangle = \frac{g^2 N_c (N_c^2-1)}{2} G^{(1)}_{(U)}(\xt,\xt) G^{(1)}_{(V)}(\xt,\xt)\), where \(G^{(1)}\) is the unpolarized Weizsacker-Williams gluon distribution.
* The expectation value of the divergence of the Chern-Simons current vanishes: \(\langle \nudot(\xt) \rangle = 0\).
* In the GBW model, the linearly polarized gluon distribution vanishes identically: \(h^{(1)}_{\bot,\mathrm{GBW}}(\xt,\yt)=0\), and the unpolarized distribution at equal points is \(G^{(1)}_{\mathrm{GBW}}(\xt,\xt)= Q_s^2 / (g^2 C_F)\).
* In the MV model at equal points, the unpolarized distribution is \(\lim_{\yt \to \xt} G^{(1)}_{\mathrm{MV}}(\xt,\yt) = Q_s^2/(g^2 C_F)\) and the linearly polarized distribution vanishes: \(\lim_{\yt \to \xt} h^{(1)}_{\bot,\mathrm{MV}}(\xt,\yt)=0\).
* The local density of axial charge per unit rapidity is estimated as \(\left.\frac{d N_5}{d^2\mathbf{x}_\perp d\eta}\right|_{\tau\lesssim 1/Q_s} \approx \frac{\tau^2}{2} \frac{g^2 N_f}{2\pi^2} \nudot(\xt,\tau=0^+)\).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of Glasma field correlations by Muller and collaborators (2011) and provides an analytic framework connecting the linearly polarized gluon distribution (studied in the context of deep inelastic scattering at a future Electron-Ion Collider) to axial charge fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions. The results enable initial conditions for anomalous hydrodynamical calculations of the Chiral Magnetic Effect, which previously relied on parametric estimates by Hirono and collaborators (2014) or numerical simulations by Kharzeev and collaborators (2001). The paper also relates to two-gluon correlation studies using the glasma graph approximation by Dumitru and collaborators (2008) and Dusling and collaborators (2009-2013).
