# Meson masses in external magnetic fields with HISQ fermions

> We studied the temporal correlation function of mesons in the pseudo-scalar channel in (2+1)-flavor QCD in the presence of external magnetic fields at zero temperature. The simulations were performed on $32^3 \times 96$ lattices using the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) action with $m_π \approx $ 230 MeV. The strength of magnetic fields $|eB|$ ranges from 0 to around 3.3 GeV$^2$ ($\sim 60 m_π^2$). We found that the masses of neutral pseudo-scalar particles, e.g. neutral pion and kaon, monotonouslly decrease as the magnetic field grows and then saturate at a nonzero value. It is observed that heavier neutral pseudo-scalars are less affected by magnetic fields. Moreover, we found a non-monotonous behavior of charged pion and kaon mass in magnetic field for the first time. In the case of small magnetic field (0 $\leq~|eB| \lesssim$ 0.3 GeV$^2~\sim 6m_π^2$ ) the mass of charged pseudo-scalar grows with magnetic field and can be well described by the Lowest Landau Level approximation, while for $|eB|$ larger than 0.3 GeV$^2$ the mass starts to decrease. The possible connection between $|eB|$ dependences of neutral pion mass and the decreasing behavior of pseudo-critical temperature in magnetic field is discussed. Due to the nonzero value of neutral pion mass our simulation indicates that the superconducting phase of QCD does not exist in the current window of magnetic field.

## Key claim

The paper reports that charged pion and kaon masses exhibit a non-monotonous behavior in an external magnetic field for the first time in full QCD, with the mass first increasing and then decreasing beyond |eB| ≈ 0.3 GeV², whereas neutral pseudo-scalar masses monotonously decrease and then saturate at a nonzero value.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD simulations on 32³ × 96 lattices using the Highly Improved Staggered Quarks (HISQ) action with a pion mass of approximately 230 MeV at zero temperature. Temporal correlation functions in the pseudo-scalar channel were measured using multiple corner wall sources to improve signal-to-noise ratio, and meson masses were extracted by fitting correlators with a multi-state ansatz and selecting the best fit via the corrected Akaike Information Criterion (AICc). The magnetic field strength |eB| was varied from 0 to about 3.3 GeV² using quantized flux values N_b = 0 through 64.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass m_π ≈ 230 MeV at zero magnetic field
- Magnetic field range: |eB| from 0 to approximately 3.3 GeV² (∼ 60 m_π²)
- Lattice spacing a⁻¹ = 1.6852 GeV
- For |eB| ≲ 0.3 GeV² (∼ 6 m_π²), charged pion and kaon masses follow the Lowest Landau Level approximation m² = |eB|
- Neutral pion mass reduction of 30–40% at the highest magnetic fields
- Critical magnetic field for ρ-meson condensation: |eB|_c ≈ 0.77 GeV² (based on zero-field ρ mass)
- Statistics: O(10³) configurations per magnetic field value, ranging from 356 to 3432 configurations

## Context

This work extends previous studies of meson masses in magnetic fields from quenched QCD (e.g., Luschevskaya (2015), Bali (2017)) and from full QCD using stout fermions (Bali (2011, 2017)) to the HISQ action with higher magnetic fields up to 60 m_π². The non-monotonous behavior of charged pion and kaon masses is a new finding not seen in earlier quenched or full QCD simulations. The results enable investigation of the connection between decreasing neutral pion mass and the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomenon, and the nonzero neutral pion mass throughout the simulated range indicates that a superconducting phase of QCD does not occur within this magnetic field window.
