# Dileptons from the strongly-interacting Quark-Gluon Plasma within the Parton-Hadron-String-Dynamics (PHSD) approach

> Dilepton production in In+In collisions at 158 AGeV is studied within the microscopic Parton-Hadron-Strings Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium. A comparison to the data of the NA60 Collaboration shows that the low mass dilepton spectra are well described by including a collisional broadening of vector mesons, while the spectra in the intermediate mass range are dominated by off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation in the nonperturbative QGP. In particular, the observed softening of the mT spectra at intermediate masses is reproduced.

## Key claim

The low-mass dilepton spectra in In+In collisions at 158 AGeV are well described by including a collisional broadening of vector mesons, while the intermediate-mass spectra are dominated by off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation in the nonperturbative QGP; the observed softening of the mT spectra at intermediate masses is reproduced, but the comparison relies on the specific PHSD transport approach and the DQPM model matched to lattice QCD results.

## Method

The authors used the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which incorporates off-shell propagation of partonic quasiparticles based on the Dynamical QuasiParticle Model (DQPM) matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium. Dilepton production was calculated from partonic off-shell quasiparticles in an effective field theory, including leading-order Drell-Yan annihilation, gluon Compton scattering, and quark-antiquark annihilation with gluon Bremsstrahlung, with the running coupling depending on local energy density. The results were compared to NA60 Collaboration data for In+In collisions at 158 AGeV after applying the NA60 acceptance filter and mass resolution.

## Key numbers

- In+In collisions at 158 AGeV: the low-mass dilepton spectra are described by including collisional broadening of vector mesons, while the intermediate mass range (above 1 GeV) is dominated by off-shell partonic channels in the QGP.
- The effective temperature of dileptons in the intermediate mass range is lower than the Teff of dileptons from the hadronic phase, as observed by NA60 and reproduced in PHSD.
- The HSD results for the free case and three in-medium scenarios (broadening, mass shift, broadening plus mass shift) were compared to CERES data, showing that broadening of the rho-meson spectral function describes the spectrum better (cf. Fig. 4 in OurDilept08).

## Context

This work extends the HSD transport approach (Cassing and Bratkovskaya, 2008) by incorporating partonic degrees of freedom via PHSD, and builds on the DQPM (Cassing, 2006) which describes QCD properties in terms of single-particle Green's functions. The results contradict the earlier concept of a weakly interacting QGP describable by perturbative QCD, instead supporting a strongly interacting QGP with nonperturbative properties. The approach enables future studies of dilepton production at higher energies (e.g., RHIC and LHC) and provides a framework for extracting in-medium vector meson properties and QGP characteristics from experimental data.
