# An intrinsic state for an extended version of the interacting boson model

> An intrinsic-state formalism for IBM-4 is presented. A basis of deformed bosons is introduced which allows the construction of a general trial wave function which has Wigner's spin-isospin SU(4) symmetry as a particular limit. Intrinsic-state calculations are compared with exact ones showing good agreement.

# Intrinsic State for an Extended Version of the Interacting Boson Model (IBM-4)

## Key claim

The authors present a Hartree-Bose mean-field formalism for IBM-4 that reproduces exact diagonalization results for schematic Hamiltonians, but the agreement is demonstrated only for Hamiltonians with dynamical symmetries or specific limits; the formalism is not yet tested against realistic IBM-4 Hamiltonians that include general quadrupole deformation, spin-orbit coupling, or arbitrary two-body interactions.

## Method

The authors construct deformed boson operators via a unitary Hartree-Bose transformation of the 36 spherical bosons (combining orbital angular momenta ℓ=0,2 with spin-isospin combinations (T,S)=(1,0) and (0,1)). They define trial wave functions for even-even and odd-odd N=Z nuclei that depend on three variational parameters (α_T, α_S, δ) and the deformation parameters λ, then minimize the energy expectation value of a given IBM-4 Hamiltonian by solving six coupled Hartree-Bose equations self-consistently. The formalism is tested against exact calculations for a schematic Hamiltonian composed of Casimir operators of SU_TS(4), SU_S(3), and SU_L(3).

## Key numbers

- For N=5 bosons at b/a = -1.0 (favoring T=0 pairing), exact ground-state energy is -27.8359 a and mean-field energy is -27.8357 a, a difference of 0.0002 a.
- For N=15 bosons at b/a = -1.0, exact energy is -236.126 a and mean-field energy is -236.125 a.
- At b/a = 0.0 (SU(4) symmetry), both exact and mean-field energies are exactly 5.00000 a for both N=5 and N=15.
- For N=5 at b/a = 1.0 (favoring T=1 pairing), exact energy is 10.0763 a and mean-field energy is 10.1187 a.
- For N=15 at b/a = 1.0, exact energy is 20.8479 a and mean-field energy is 21.1652 a.
- In the SU_L(3) limit, the intrinsic state has deformation parameter β_ξ = λ^ξ_20/λ^ξ_00 = √2, with α_T = α_S = -1/2 and δ = -1.

## Context

This work extends the intrinsic-state formalism previously developed for IBM-1 (Ginocchio and Kirson, 1980; Dieperink and Scholten, 1980), IBM-2 (Leviatan and Kirson, 1990; Ginocchio and Leviatan, 1992), and IBM-3 (Ginocchio and Leviatan, 1994; Garcia-Ramos et al., 1998) to the full IBM-4 framework that includes both T=0 and T=1 bosons. The formalism enables geometric visualization of IBM-4 and allows study of the competition between isovector and isoscalar pairing in N≈Z nuclei approaching the proton drip line, a topic of current interest with radioactive nuclear beams. The authors state that work is in progress to apply the formalism to more realistic Hamiltonians including spin-orbit coupling and general quadrupole deformation terms.
