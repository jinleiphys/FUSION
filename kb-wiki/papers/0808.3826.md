# Ground-state energy of the unitary Fermi gas from the epsilon expansion

> We update the ground-state energy ratio of unitary Fermi gas to noninteracting Fermi gas (xi) from the epsilon expansion by including the next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) term near two spatial dimensions. Interpolations of the NNLO epsilon expansions around four and two spatial dimensions with the use of Pade approximants give xi=0.360+/-0.020 in three dimensions with the uncertainty due to different interpolation functions. This value is consistent with the previous interpolations of the NLO epsilon expansions xi=0.377+/-0.014 in spite of the large NNLO corrections.

## Key claim

The ground-state energy ratio of the unitary Fermi gas to the noninteracting Fermi gas in three dimensions is \(\xi = 0.360 \pm 0.020\) when interpolating the next-to-next-to-leading-order (NNLO) epsilon expansions around four and two spatial dimensions using Padé approximants, with the uncertainty arising from different interpolation functions; this result is consistent with the previous next-to-leading-order (NLO) interpolation \(\xi = 0.377 \pm 0.014\) despite large NNLO corrections.

## Method

The author computed the NNLO correction to the universal parameter \(\xi_d\) near two spatial dimensions (\(d = 2 + \bar\eps\)) using a field-theoretic epsilon expansion, evaluating three-loop vacuum diagrams for the pressure of the unitary Fermi gas. The resulting NNLO expansion around \(d=2\) was then combined with the previously known NNLO expansion around \(d=4\) (\(d = 4 - \eps\)), and the two expansions were interpolated to \(d=3\) using Padé approximants of the form \(F_{[M/N]}(\eps)\) applied to the unknown function \(F(\eps)\) in the expression for \(\xi_{4-\eps}\). Four different Padé approximants (\(F_{[5/0]}\), \(F_{[4/1]}\), \(F_{[3/2]}\), and \(F_{[0/5]}\)) were used, and their spread defined the uncertainty.

## Key numbers

*   \(\xi_3 \approx 0.360 \pm 0.020\): the ground-state energy ratio in three dimensions from interpolating the NNLO epsilon expansions, with the uncertainty due to different Padé interpolation functions.
*   Individual Padé interpolation results for \(\xi_3\): 0.340 (\(F_{[5/0]}\)), 0.372 (\(F_{[4/1]}\)), 0.370 (\(F_{[3/2]}\)), and 0.357 (\(F_{[0/5]}\)).
*   Previous NLO interpolation result: \(\xi_3 \approx 0.377 \pm 0.014\).
*   NNLO expansion near two dimensions: \(\xi_{2+\bar\eps} = 1 - \bar\eps + 0.80685\,\bar\eps^2 + O(\bar\eps^3)\).
*   NNLO expansion near four dimensions: \(\xi_{4-\eps} = \frac{\eps^{(6-\eps)/(4-\eps)}}{2} \left[1 - 0.04916\,\eps - 0.95961\,\eps^2 + O(\eps^3)\right]\).

## Context

This work extends the epsilon-expansion approach to the unitary Fermi gas pioneered by Nishida and Son (2006) by including the NNLO term near two spatial dimensions, which had previously only been computed near four dimensions. The interpolated result \(\xi \approx 0.360 \pm 0.020\) is consistent with Monte Carlo simulations (Bulgac 2008, Zhang 2008) that give \(\xi \approx 0.40(5)\) and \(\xi \lesssim 0.40(1)\), and the stability of the interpolation to higher-order corrections suggests the epsilon expansion has predictive power. The method can now be applied to other properties of the unitary Fermi gas, such as the critical temperature, thermodynamic functions, and few-body energies, by incorporating expansions around both \(d=4\) and \(d=2\).
