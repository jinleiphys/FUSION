# Phase transition in compact stars due to a violent shock

> In this letter we study the dynamics of a first order phase transition from nucleonic to quark matter in neutron stars. Using standard equations of state for these two phases we find the density range where such a transition is possible. Then we study the transformation of the star assuming that the quark core is formed via a spherical shock wave. The thermodynamical conditions in the quark core are found from the conservation laws across the transition region. Their dependence on the density and velocity of the incoming nuclear matter are studied. It is found that the shock is especially violent in the beginning of the conversion process when the velocity of the infalling matter is especially high. As the shock propagates further from the center the front velocity first increases and reaches a maximum value when the incoming velocity is around $0.2$. Finally, the front velocity quickly goes to zero when incoming matter velocity approaches zero. We have shown that the density and pressure jumps are especially large in the begining of the transition process.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a first-order phase transition from nucleonic to quark matter in a neutron star, initiated near the center, proceeds via a violent spherical shock wave; the shock is strongest at the onset when incoming matter velocity is near unity, and the front velocity first increases then drops to zero as the incoming velocity falls below about 0.2, with the density and pressure jumps being especially large at the beginning of the transition.

## Method

The authors model the hadronic phase using the relativistic mean-field approach with the NL3 parameter set and the quark phase using the MIT bag model with a bag constant B^(1/4)=140 MeV and interaction parameter a_4=0.5. They apply the conservation laws for energy-momentum and baryon number across the shock front (the Taub adiabat formalism) to relate the thermodynamic quantities and velocities in the nucleonic and quark phases, solving for the front velocity and density/pressure jumps as functions of the incoming nuclear matter velocity at two onset densities (2n_0 and 3n_0).

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium phase transition at T=0 occurs at a baryon chemical potential μ_c ≃ 1150 MeV.
- The maximum mass of pure nucleonic stars is about 2.8 M_⊙, while for hybrid stars the maximum mass is 2.1 M_⊙, still within the 2 M_⊙ constraint.
- The radius at maximum mass for hybrid stars is about 13 km.
- At zero temperature in static matter, the equilibrium PT is predicted at baryon density n_b ≃ 2n_0; at T=20 MeV the PT occurs at lower density n_b ≃ 1.1n_0.
- For strong shocks near the center, the front velocity v_f ≃ 1/3 when the incoming matter velocity is near unity.
- The front velocity reaches a maximum when the incoming velocity is around 0.2, then drops quickly to zero as the incoming velocity approaches zero.
- The density ratio λ = n_q/n_n exceeds the critical value 3/2 for instability when the incoming velocity is high, and falls below 3/2 at incoming velocities ≲ 0.2.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of phase transitions in neutron stars (Mishustin, Haensel 1982, Banik 2001, Gentile 1993, Mallick 2006, Drago 2008) by focusing on the dynamical shock-wave scenario rather than quasi-equilibrium conversion. The analysis uses the Taub adiabat formalism from relativistic shock theory (Taub, Landau, Zeldovich) and the instability criterion λ>3/2 from Ramsey. The results provide a qualitative framework for future time-dependent hydrodynamic simulations coupled to Einstein's equations, and suggest observable signatures such as gamma-ray bursts (Bombaci, Mallick) and changes in cooling rates.
