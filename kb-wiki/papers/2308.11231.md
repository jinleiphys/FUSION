# Emerging γ-soft-like spectrum in 196Pt in the SU3-IBM (I)

> Recently, it has been argued that a spherical-like spectrum emerges in the SU3-IBM, opening up new approaches to understand the γ-softness in realistic nuclei. In a previous paper, γ-softness with degeneracy of the ground and quasi-γ bands was observed. In this paper, another special point connected with the middle degenerate point is discussed, which is found to be related with the properties of 196Pt. This emergent γ-softness has also been shown to be important for understanding the prolate-oblate asymmetric shape phase transition. The low-lying spectra, B(E2) values and quadrupole moments in 196Pt are discussed showing that the new model can account for several observed features. This is the first part of the discussions on the γ-soft-like spectrum of 196Pt.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the nucleus 196Pt, traditionally considered a textbook example of O(6) γ-softness, can be better described by a new γ-soft-like spectrum emerging from SU(3) higher-order interactions in the SU3-IBM, specifically at a special parameter point (point B) that lies near the oblate side of the prolate-oblate shape phase transition, though the authors acknowledge that the fitting precision "needs to be improved."

## Method

The authors diagonalize an extended interacting boson model Hamiltonian (Eq. 3) containing the SU(3) second-order Casimir operator, the SU(3) third-order Casimir operator, and the square of the second-order Casimir operator, using a U(6)⊃SU(3)⊃SO(3) basis with N=6 bosons (corresponding to 196Pt). They identify a special parameter point (point B) at η=0.5, κ=1.404, and ξ=0.05 where accidental degeneracy between the 4₁⁺ and 2₂⁺ states occurs, then compute low-lying energy spectra, B(E2) values, and quadrupole moments to compare with experimental data for 196Pt.

## Key numbers

- Point B is located at κ_B = 1.404 (with η=0.5, ξ=0, N=6), while point A is at κ_A = 1.188
- The SU(3) degenerate point for N=6 is found at κ = 1.2
- For point B with ξ=0, the energy ratio R' = E(0₃⁺)/E(0₂⁺) = 1.845, compared to the experimental value of 1.236
- When ξ=0.144, the energy ratio between 0₃⁺ and 0₂⁺ states reaches a minimum of 1.188
- The overall energy scale parameter c = 0.9753 MeV is used to match the 0₂⁺ state energy to experiment
- An L² term of 0.00803 MeV is added to fit the 2₁⁺ state
- The quadrupole moment Q(2₁⁺) = 0.62(8) eb for 196Pt (experimental value cited from Fewell85)
- For the blue line with ξ=0, the quadrupole moment becomes positive (oblate deformation) when η ≥ 0.372

## Context

This work extends the SU3-IBM framework previously applied to 110Cd (Wang22) and to the prolate-oblate asymmetric shape phase transition in the Hf-Hg region (wang23), and it directly challenges the traditional O(6) description of 196Pt established by Iachello (1978) and Casten (1978). The paper addresses three known deficiencies of the O(6) description for 196Pt identified by Fewell (1985) and Leviatan (2009): the large positive quadrupole moment, the staggering of the γ band intermediate between γ-soft and γ-rigid, and the poor reproduction of 0⁺ state positions. This is the first part of a series on 196Pt, with subsequent papers planned to discuss rotational operators [L×Q×L]⁽⁰⁾ and related terms for describing triaxial rotational spectra.
