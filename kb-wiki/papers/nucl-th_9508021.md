# $πNN$ and $πNΔ$ formfactors determined from a microscopic model for $πN$ scattering

> We determine the $πNN$ and $πNΔ$ formfactors from the $P_{11}$ resp. $P_{33}$ partial wave of $πN$ scattering by dressing corresponding bare vertices with the help of $πN$ non--pole contributions. The underlying model is based on meson exchange, and involves nucleon and delta--isobar pole and crossed--pole terms together with correlated $ππ$--exchange in the $J^P=0^+$ ($σ$) and $1^-$ ($ρ$) channel. The results are very similar for $πNN$ and $πNΔ$ and can be roughly parametrized by a monopole with cutoff mass $>$ 500 MeV, with some variation due to model dependencies. Thus the formfactors are much less soft than derived before for the $πNN$ case by Saito and Afnan using the same procedure but different $πN$ interaction models.

## Key claim

The dressed πNN and πNΔ formfactors determined from πN scattering are much less soft than previously derived by Saito and Afnan (1994) using the same procedure but different πN interaction models, and can be roughly parametrized by a monopole with cutoff mass greater than 500 MeV, though with some variation due to model dependencies.

## Method

The authors determine the πNN and πNΔ formfactors from the P11 and P33 partial waves of πN scattering, respectively, by dressing bare vertices via the non-pole contributions in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation. The underlying meson-exchange πN model includes nucleon and delta-isobar pole and crossed-pole terms together with correlated ππ-exchange in the J^P=0+ (σ) and 1- (ρ) channels. Two different parametrizations of the formfactors in both the pole and non-pole contributions (models 1 and 2) are used, both constrained by simultaneous description of all S and P waves.

## Key numbers

* The dressed πNN and πNΔ formfactors can be roughly parametrized by a monopole with cutoff mass greater than 500 MeV, with some variation due to model dependencies.
* The on-shell momentum for the πNΔ formfactor normalization is p0 = 227.3 MeV; for the πNN formfactor it is p0 = i 137.3 MeV.
* The πNN formfactor is much less soft than the result of Saito and Afnan (1994), who found a monopole cutoff mass much less than 400 MeV, leading to a three-body force contribution to the triton binding energy of about -2 keV.
* The results are still softer than the presently favored monopole with a cutoff mass of 0.8 GeV, and much softer than the hard πNN formfactors used in most NN boson exchange models (e.g., the full Bonn potential uses ΛπNN = 1.3 GeV, as reported in Machleidt (1987)).

## Context

This work extends the approach of Saito and Afnan (1994), who determined the πNN formfactor from the P11 partial wave using phenomenological separable non-pole terms and found an extremely soft formfactor. The present study uses an alternative meson-exchange πN interaction model developed by the Jülich group (Schütz, 1994) that is constrained by all S and P waves simultaneously, yielding a much harder formfactor. The results are more consistent with other information from QCD lattice calculations (Liu, 1995) and other analyses (Coon, 1990) that suggest a monopole cutoff mass around 800 MeV, and enable more realistic calculations of nuclear phenomena such as three-body forces.
