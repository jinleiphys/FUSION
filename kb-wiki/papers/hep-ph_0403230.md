# Vortices and type-I superconductivity in neutron stars

> In a recent paper by Link, it was pointed out that the the standard picture of the neutron star core, composed of a mixture of a neutron superfluid and a proton type-II superconductor, is inconsistent with observations of long period precession in isolated pulsars. In the following we will show that intervortex force between the magnetic flux tubes may be attractive resulting in a type-I (rather than type-II) superconductor. In this case the magnetic field cannot exist in the form of magnetic flux tubes, supporting Link's observation. This behavior of the system is due to the strong interaction between the proton-neutron Cooper pairs, which was previously ignored. We also calculate the critical magnetic fields H_c and H_c2 for type-I/II superconductors. These results also support our claim of type-I superconductivity in the cores of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors argue that when the strong interaction between proton and neutron Cooper pairs is included, the proton superconductor in neutron star cores may be type-I rather than type-II, which would resolve the inconsistency between the standard picture and observations of long period precession in isolated pulsars pointed out by Link. This result depends on the assumption that the interactions between proton and neutron Cooper pairs are approximately equal, leading to an approximate U(2) symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct a two-component Landau-Ginzburg free energy describing coupled proton and neutron condensates, with an interaction potential that is approximately U(2) symmetric. They solve the equations of motion analytically in the asymptotic limit far from a proton vortex core, diagonalizing the mixing matrix that couples the two condensate fields, and verify their results numerically. They then calculate the interaction energy between two widely separated proton vortices using a linearized theory with point sources, following the method of Speight.

## Key numbers

- The London penetration depth is estimated as λ ~ 80 fm and the coherence length as ξ ~ 30 fm, giving a naive Landau-Ginzburg parameter κ = λ/ξ ~ 3
- The ratio of proton Cooper pair density to neutron Cooper pair density in a neutron star core is expected to be γ ≡ n₁/n₂ = 5-15%
- The parameter measuring asymmetry between proton and neutron Cooper pairs is defined as ε = 2 δa/a, with a numerical example using ε = 0.02
- The effective Landau-Ginzburg parameter for the two-component system is κ_np = √ε λ/ξ; for typical parameters this can be much less than 1/√2, indicating type-I behavior
- The correlation length of the concentration mode (the relevant length scale for vortex core size) is ξ/√ε, which can be much larger than ξ when ε ≪ 1

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Link (2003), who pointed out that the standard picture of a type-II proton superconductor in neutron stars is inconsistent with observations of long period precession in isolated pulsars. The authors build on their own earlier letter (Buckley, Metlitski, and Zhitnitsky, 2003) and on prior work by Witten (1985) on two-component condensates in cosmology, as well as studies of nontrivial vortex core structure in systems such as high-Tc superconductors, Bose-Einstein condensates, and superfluid ³He. The results suggest that the neutron star core may exhibit the Meissner effect, with magnetic field expelled from superconducting regions, and that glitch observations may need reinterpretation in this type-I scenario.
