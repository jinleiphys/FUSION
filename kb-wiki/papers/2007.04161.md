# Unitary ambiguity of NN contact interactions and the 3N force

> We identify a redundancy between two- and three-nucleon contact interactions at the fourth and fifth order of the chiral expansion respectively. In particular we show that tensor-type and spin-orbit three-nucleon contact interactions effectively account for that part of the two-nucleon interaction which depends on the total center-of-mass momentum and is unconstrained by relativity. This might give the chiral effective field theory enough flexibility to successfully address $A=3$ scattering observables already at N3LO.

# Unitary ambiguity of NN contact interactions and the 3N force

## Key claim
The paper identifies a redundancy between two-nucleon (NN) contact interactions at fourth order (N3LO) and three-nucleon (3N) contact interactions at fifth order (N4LO) in the chiral expansion, showing that five of the thirteen subleading 3N contact operators can be eliminated by redefining the NN potential through specific unitary transformations. The authors argue that this implies the 3N force is not parameter-free at N3LO as commonly assumed, but depends on five low-energy constants, including two from previously unconstrained center-of-mass momentum-dependent NN interactions.

## Method
The authors classify the most general NN contact unitary transformations at O(p^2) level, identifying five generators T_1 through T_5 (plus isospin-dependent variants related by Fierz identities). They compute the commutators of these generators with the one-body kinetic energy and with the leading NN contact interactions (parametrized by C_S and C_T), then express the resulting induced 3N operators in the basis of thirteen subleading 3N contact operators O_i from Girlanda (2011). The analysis also incorporates constraints from Poincaré symmetry to identify which center-of-mass momentum-dependent NN interactions are unconstrained by relativity.

## Key numbers
- Five unitary transformation parameters (α_1 through α_5) can eliminate five of the thirteen subleading 3N LECs E_i, reducing the basis to operators i=2,...,9.
- Two new LECs D_16 and D_17 parametrize the center-of-mass momentum-dependent NN potential: V_NN(P) = i D_16 (k·Q)(Q×P)·(σ_1 - σ_2) + D_17 (k·Q)(k×P)·(σ_1 × σ_2).
- The transformation parameters relating to these new LECs are α_4 = -(m/8) D_16 and α_5 = -(m/4) D_17.
- The induced 3N potential from D_16 and D_17 involves specific combinations: for D_16, the dominant term in the limit C_S >> C_T is proportional to C_S (O_9 + O_10 - O_11 - O_12), while D_17 contributes as -(m/2) D_17 C_T (O_2 - O_3 - (1/3)O_4 - O_5 - (1/3)O_6 - 2O_9 + 2O_11 + 2O_13).
- The spin-orbit operator O_7 receives a shift δE_7 = 8 α_4 C_T, with large numerical coefficient relative to C_T.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of NN contact unitary transformations from Reinert (2017), who used three transformations (T_1, T_2, T_3) to reduce the number of N3LO NN couplings from fifteen to twelve. The authors identify two additional transformations (T_4, T_5) that generate center-of-mass momentum-dependent NN interactions, which were not considered in that prior work. The results connect to the long-standing A_y puzzle in low-energy N-d scattering, where spin-orbit 3N operators (particularly O_7) have been shown by Girlanda (2016, 2018) and Kievsky (1999) to play an instrumental role. The paper enables future calculations of A=3 scattering observables that include these induced 3N contact interactions, potentially resolving discrepancies at N3LO without requiring N4LO contributions.
