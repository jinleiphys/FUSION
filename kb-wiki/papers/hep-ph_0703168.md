# Pion Interactions in the X(3872)

> We consider pion interactions in an effective field theory of the narrow resonance X(3872), assuming it is a weakly bound molecule of the charm mesons D^{0} \bar D^{*0} and D^{*0} \bar D^{0}. Since the hyperfine splitting of the D^{0} and D^{*0} is only 7 MeV greater than the neutral pion mass, pions can be produced near threshold and are non-relativistic. We show that pion exchange can be treated in perturbation theory and calculate the next-to-leading-order correction to the partial decay width Γ[X \to D^0 \bar D^{0} π^0].

## Key claim

The paper shows that pion exchange in the X(3872) system can be treated using perturbation theory, unlike in conventional nuclear physics, because the expansion parameter is approximately 1/20 to 1/10 rather than 1/2 as in the nucleon-nucleon case. The authors calculate the next-to-leading-order correction to the partial decay width Gamma[X to D0 anti-D0 pi0], finding that non-analytic corrections from pion exchange are negligible and the NLO correction is dominated by contact interaction contributions.

## Method

The authors construct an effective field theory of non-relativistic D0 and D*0 mesons interacting with non-relativistic neutral pions near the D0 anti-D*0 threshold, integrating out the scales m_pi and Delta from heavy-hadron chiral perturbation theory. They treat the leading-order contact interaction C0 non-perturbatively to produce a bound state, while treating pion exchange perturbatively using the power divergence subtraction scheme. The decay width for X(3872) to D0 anti-D0 pi0 is computed by evaluating the two-point function of interpolating fields for the X(3872) and extracting the imaginary part from decay diagrams at leading order and next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

- The X(3872) mass is 3871.2 +/- 0.5 MeV, with a binding energy of 0.6 +/- 0.6 MeV relative to the D0 anti-D*0 threshold at 3871.81 +/- 0.36 MeV
- The hyperfine splitting Delta = 142 MeV is only 7 MeV above the neutral pion mass m_pi = 135 MeV, giving mu = 45 MeV in the pion propagator
- The expansion parameter for perturbative pion exchange is g^2 M_DD* mu / (4 pi f_pi^2) = 1/20 to 1/10, using g = 0.5 to 0.7 and f_pi = 132 MeV
- The maximum pion momentum in the decay X to D0 anti-D0 pi0 is approximately 44 MeV, and the maximum pion energy is 142 MeV
- The upper bound on the typical momentum of D and anti-D* in the bound state is gamma = (2 M_DD* E_X)^(1/2) less than or equal to 48 MeV
- The ratio of branching fractions Br[X to J/psi pi+ pi- pi0] / Br[X to J/psi pi+ pi-] = 1.0 +/- 0.4 +/- 0.3

## Context

This work extends the effective-range theory calculations of Voloshin (2003, 2005) for X(3872) decays by including dynamical pion exchange in a systematic effective field theory framework. The approach is analogous to the KSW theory of nucleon-nucleon interactions by Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), where a leading-order contact interaction is summed to all orders and pion exchange is treated perturbatively. The paper enables future calculations of other X(3872) decays such as X to D0 anti-D0 gamma, and provides a framework that can be extended to include charged D mesons and other nearby thresholds like J/psi rho and J/psi omega.
