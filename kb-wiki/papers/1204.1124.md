# Relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics with extended matching conditions for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> Recently we proposed a novel approach to the formulation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics by extending the so-called matching conditions in the Eckart frame [Phys. Rev. {\bf C 85}, (2012) 14906]. We extend this formalism further to the arbitrary Lorentz frame. We discuss the stability and causality of solutions of fluid equations which are obtained by applying this formulation to the Landau frame, which is more relevant to treat the fluid produced in ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions. We derive equations of motion for a relativistic dissipative fluid with zero baryon chemical potential and show that linearized equations obtained from them are stable against small perturbations. It is found that conditions for a fluid to be stable against infinitesimal perturbations are equivalent to imposing restrictions that the sound wave, $c_s$, propagating in the fluid, must not exceed the speed of light $c$, i.e., $c_s < c$. This conclusion is equivalent to that obtained in the previous paper using the Eckart frame [Phys. Rev. {\bf C 85}, (2012) 14906].

# Relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics with extended matching conditions for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

The paper claims that a relativistic dissipative fluid formulated with extended matching conditions (allowing nonzero off-equilibrium energy density and baryon density corrections) is stable against small perturbations and causal in the Landau frame, provided the sound speed does not exceed the speed of light. This conclusion is equivalent to that previously obtained in the Eckart frame by the same author (Osada, 2012), and the stability condition reduces to a restriction on the phenomenological parameter kappa that ensures 0 ≤ c_s ≤ 1.

## Method

The author generalizes the previously proposed extended matching conditions (u_μ u_ν δT^{μν} = Λ, u_μ δN^μ = δn) to an arbitrary Lorentz frame, then specializes to the Landau frame (W^μ = 0) for a fluid with zero baryon chemical potential (μ_b = 0). Constitutive equations for bulk and shear viscous pressures are derived from the second law of thermodynamics using a generalized entropy current that includes a term χΠ u^μ. Linearized equations of motion are obtained for small perturbations around a homogeneous non-equilibrium background state, and their dispersion relations are analyzed for both transverse and longitudinal modes following the stability analysis method of Hiscock and Lindblom (1983, 1985, 1987).

## Key numbers

- The critical wave number for transverse mode propagation is k_c = sqrt( h_eq / (4 η τ_π) ), where h_eq is the equilibrium enthalpy density, η is shear viscosity, and τ_π is the shear relaxation time.
- The relaxation time for bulk pressure perturbations is τ_Π = ζ (2ξ + κ/Π_0), where ζ is bulk viscosity, κ and ξ are parameters in the extended matching conditions, and Π_0 is the background bulk pressure.
- The relaxation time for shear pressure perturbations is τ_π = 2 ξ'' η, where ξ'' is a parameter in the extended matching conditions.
- The stability condition for longitudinal modes is 1 − κ (∂P_eq/∂ε_eq)|_{n_eq=0} > 0, which is equivalent to c_s^2 + (1/T)(∂P_eq/∂s_eq)|_{ε_eq} < 1/κ.
- The causality condition requires 1/κ to lie in the range (1/T)(∂P_eq/∂s_eq)|_{ε_eq} ≤ 1/κ ≤ 1 + (1/T)(∂P_eq/∂s_eq)|_{ε_eq}, ensuring 0 ≤ c_s ≤ 1.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous formulation of relativistic dissipative hydrodynamics with extended matching conditions in the Eckart frame (Osada, 2012) to the Landau frame, which is more relevant for ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at zero baryon chemical potential. It addresses the known problems of instability and acausality in first-order relativistic Navier-Stokes theories (Eckart, 1940; Landau, 1959) and builds on the causal Israel-Stewart framework (Israel and Stewart, 1976, 1979), whose stability was demonstrated by Hiscock and Lindblom (1983, 1985, 1987). The work enables application of this extended matching condition approach to phenomenological modeling of hot QCD matter produced at RHIC and LHC, where the Israel-Stewart model is currently widely used (Muronga, 2002, 2004).
