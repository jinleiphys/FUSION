# Nature of order from random two-body interactions

> We investigate the origin of order in the low-lying spectra of many-body systems with random two-body interactions. Our study based both on analytical as well as on numerical arguments shows that except for the most $J$-stretched states, the ground states in the higher $J$-sectors are more orderly and develop larger energy gaps than the ones in the J=0-sector. Due to different characteristic energy scales in different $J$-sectors the J=0 ground states may predominate only when all the states are taken together.

## Key claim

The paper argues that, contrary to the common belief that J=0 ground states are the most ordered, the ground states in intermediate total angular momentum sectors (such as J=2 and J=4) are actually more orderly and develop larger energy gaps than those in the J=0 sector, but the J=0 ground states may predominate only when all states from different J-sectors are taken together due to different characteristic energy scales.

## Method

The authors studied a system of 6 identical particles in the sd shell with all single-particle energies set to zero, using both the Two-Body Random Ensemble (TBRE) and the Random Quasiparticle Ensemble (RQE). They analytically derived the probability distributions of off-diagonal matrix elements for different J-sectors, showing that the J=0 sector produces Gaussian-like tails while J≠0 sectors produce exponential tails, and then numerically computed the information entropy and ground state energy gaps for each J-sector to quantify deviations from the Gaussian Orthogonal Ensemble (GOE).

## Key numbers

- The J=0 sector has 14 states (M_0=14), while J=2 has 33 states (M_2=33), J=3 has 29 states (M_3=29), and J=4 has 26 states (M_4=26)
- For J=6, the effective number of terms N_eff = 2.67, indicating a return toward Gaussian-like behavior for the most J-stretched states
- The average level spacing for J=0 is a factor of few larger than for the remaining J-sectors

## Context

This work extends the findings of Johnson, Bertsch, and Dean (1998) who identified a preponderance of J=0 ground states arising from random two-body interactions in strongly interacting Fermi systems such as atomic nuclei. The paper provides a more adequate theoretical framework based on the distribution of matrix elements (equations involving Fourier transforms and Bessel functions) than the multipole expansion approach of Zelevinsky, Brown, Frazier, and Horoi (1996). The results enable a better understanding of deviations from standard random matrix theory in nuclear shell-model calculations and may have broader applications in econophysics and other fields where similar parallels between matrix element distributions and eigenspectra structure can be drawn.
