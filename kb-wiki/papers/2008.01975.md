# Symmetry conserving Coupled Cluster Doubles wave function and the Self-Consistent odd particle number RPA

> Mixing single and triple fermions an exact killing operator of the Coupled Cluster Doubles (CCD) wave function with good symmetry was found in \cite{Tohy13}. Using these operators with the equation of motion (EOM) method the so-called self-consistent odd particle number random phase approximation (odd-RPA) was set up. Together with the stationarity condition of the two body density matrix it is shown that the killing conditions allow to reduce the order of correlation functions contained in the matrix elements of the odd-RPA equations to a fully self consistent equation for the single particle occupation numbers. Excellent results for the latter and the ground state energies are obtained in an exactly solvable model from weak to strong couplings.

# Symmetry conserving Coupled Cluster Doubles wave function and the Self-Consistent odd particle number RPA

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the killing operators of the Coupled Cluster Doubles (CCD) wave function, which mix single and triple fermion operators, can be used within an equation of motion approach to construct a self-consistent odd particle number RPA (odd-RPA) that yields excellent ground state energies and occupation numbers from weak to strong coupling, provided the killing conditions and stationarity of the two-body density matrix are used to reduce higher-order correlation functions to functions of single-particle occupation numbers only.

## Method

The authors construct exact killing operators of the CCD wave function that mix single and triple fermion operators, then use these operators within an equation of motion (EOM) approach to formulate the odd-RPA. The killing conditions are employed to reduce the order of correlation functions appearing in the odd-RPA matrix elements, and the stationarity condition of the two-body density matrix provides the final closure relation. The resulting self-consistent equations for single-particle occupation numbers are solved for the Lipkin model, an exactly solvable N-shell model with a spontaneously broken parity symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the earlier finding by Tohyama (2013) that the CCD wave function is the vacuum to exact killing operators mixing single and triple fermion operators. It addresses the long-standing difficulty of handling the non-linear fermion transformation in CCD approaches, particularly for systems undergoing spontaneous symmetry breaking such as the transition to superconductivity or superfluidity. The method is tested on the Lipkin model (Lipkin 1965), which is exactly solvable and widely used to test many-body techniques in nuclear physics and other fields. The approach enables future applications to pairing problems and broken rotational symmetry, and complements earlier symmetry-projected CCD attempts using BCS quasi-particle bases (Schuck 2013) and parity projection in the Lipkin model (Schuck 2017).
