# Variational calculations of the $Λ$-seperation energy of the $_Λ^{17}$O hypernucleus

> Variational Monte Carlo calculations have been made for the $_{ Λ}^{17}$O hypernucleus using realistic two- and three-baryon interactions. A two pion exchange potential with spin- and space-exchange components is used for the $Λ$N potential. Three-body two-pion exchange and strongly repulsive dispersive $Λ$NN interactions are also included. The trial wave function is constructed from pair- and triplet-correlation operators acting on a single particle determinant. These operators consist of central, spin, isospin, tensor and three- baryon potential components. A cluster Monte Carlo method is developed for noncentral correlations and is used with up to four-baryon clusters in our calculations. The three-baryon $Λ$NN force is discussed.

# Variational calculations of the Λ-separation energy of the Λ¹⁷O hypernucleus

## Key claim
The authors find that even when realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interactions and correlations are used, the Λ-separation energy BΛ of Λ¹⁷O is severely overbound (27.5 MeV) unless a three-body ΛNN potential is included; with a reasonable ΛNN potential (Cp = 0.7 MeV, W0 = 0.015 MeV) the calculated BΛ of 13.5(1.8) MeV becomes consistent with the empirical value of 13.0(0.4) MeV, but this agreement depends on the inclusion of non-central correlations that dramatically alter the expectation value of the ΛNN interaction.

## Method
The authors performed variational Monte Carlo calculations for Λ¹⁷O using realistic two- and three-baryon interactions: an Urbana-type ΛN potential with two-pion-exchange tail and spin- and space-exchange components, the first six operator terms of the Argonne v14 NN potential, and an Urbana-type NNN potential. A cluster Monte Carlo method with up to four-baryon clusters was developed to handle non-central correlations, with the trial wave function constructed from pair- and triplet-correlation operators (central, spin, isospin, tensor) acting on a single-particle determinant. The ΛNN potential included a two-pion-exchange term (strength Cp) and a dispersive term (strength W0), and the variational parameters were optimized for cases with and without this three-body force.

## Key numbers
- BΛ(Λ¹⁷O) = 27.5(2.0) MeV for the calculation with no ΛNN potential, compared to the empirical value of 13.0(0.4) MeV.
- BΛ(Λ¹⁷O) = 13.5(1.8) MeV for the calculation with Cp = 0.7 MeV and W0 = 0.015 MeV, consistent with the empirical value.
- The ¹⁶O core energy used for subtraction was -101.0(9) MeV (with Uijk acting first, only first six Argonne v14 operators, and no extrapolation).
- The optimal Λ Woods-Saxon well parameters were VΛ = -28.3 MeV, RΛ = 3.2 fm, aΛ = 0.5 fm, giving an s-wave separation energy of 15.0 MeV.
- The NN quenching parameter α varied as α = 0.94 - 0.1 Cp for 0 ≤ Cp ≤ 1.2 MeV.
- The expectation value of the two-pion-exchange ΛNN potential changed from +0.9 MeV (Jastrow wave function) to -5.0 MeV (with optimal non-central correlations and UijΛ).
- The empirical BΛ for Λ¹⁷O was estimated as 13.0 ± 0.4 MeV from three phenomenological approaches, with ΔBΛ (difference between Λ¹⁷O and Λ¹⁶O) ranging from 0.30 to 0.76 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the cluster Monte Carlo technique developed by Pieper, Wiringa, and Pandharipande (1992) for ¹⁶O to the hypernucleus Λ¹⁷O, using realistic NN interactions rather than the simplified central NN interactions used in earlier variational studies of s-shell hypernuclei by Dalitz (1972) and Bodmer (1988). The study addresses the long-standing over-binding problem for hypernuclei with A ≥ 5, previously treated with G-matrix approaches by Bando and Shimodaya (1980) and Shinmura et al. (1984) using Reid soft-core and Hamada-Johnston potentials. The results enable future determination of ΛNN three-body force parameters (Cp and W0) by fitting BΛ values across multiple hypernuclei, and show that non-central correlations are essential for correctly evaluating the ΛNN interaction contribution.
