# A Poincaré Covariant Current Operator for Low and High Energy Phenomena

> Within front-form dynamics and in the Breit frame where initial and final three-momenta of the system are directed along the $z$ axis, Poincaré covariance constrains the current operator only through kinematical rotations around the $z$ axis. Therefore, in this frame the current can be taken in the one-body form. Applications to deep inelastic structure functions in an exactly solvable model and to the deuteron magnetic form factor are presented.

## Key claim

Within front-form dynamics and in a specific Breit frame where initial and final three-momenta are directed along the \(z\) axis, Poincaré covariance constrains the current operator only through kinematical rotations around the \(z\) axis, allowing the current to be taken in a one-body form even for interacting systems, provided the constraints from extended Lorentz covariance and Hermiticity are satisfied.

## Method

The authors use a spectral decomposition of the current operator within front-form dynamics, focusing on the Breit frame with momenta along the \(z\) axis to exploit that only rotations around the \(z\) axis are kinematical. They impose extended Poincaré covariance (continuous and discrete transformations), Hermiticity, and current conservation to show that the current can be constructed as a sum of one-body currents. The formalism is applied to deep inelastic scattering in an exactly solvable relativistic harmonic oscillator model for two spin-1/2 particles, and to elastic deuteron form factors using the free current in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- For the exactly solvable harmonic oscillator model, in the Bjorken limit (\(Q^2 \rightarrow \infty\)), the averaged structure functions yield \(\overline{F}_2(x,Q) = 2x \overline{F}_1(x,Q)\) and \(x = \xi\), where \(\xi = p^+/P^+\).
- Deuteron magnetic form factor \(B(Q^2)\) is defined as \(B(Q^2) = 2 \langle m_d 1 1| j_x(K\bm{e}_z;m_d,m_d) | m_d 1 0 \rangle ^2 /(3 m_d^2)\).
- Deuteron magnetic moment \(\mu_d\) is given by \(\mu_d = \lim_{Q \rightarrow 0} 2^{1/2} m_p \langle m_d 1 1| j_x(K\bm{e}_z;m_d,m_d) | m_d 1 0 \rangle /(Q m_d)\).
- Calculated deuteron magnetic moments for different interactions: RSC (6.47% D-state) gives 0.8611; Av14 (6.08% D-state) gives 0.8608; Paris (5.77% D-state) gives 0.8632; Av18 (5.76% D-state) gives 0.8635. Experimental value is 0.8574.

## Context

This work extends the front-form dynamics approach of Dirac (1949) and the authors' previous work (Lev, Pace, Salme, 1998) on Poincaré covariance constraints for current operators. It provides a covariant framework that avoids ambiguities present in the \(q^+=0\) reference frame approach (see, e.g., Grach and Kondratyuk). The method enables consistent calculation of both deep inelastic structure functions and elastic form factors (such as the deuteron magnetic form factor) within the same formalism, and the authors note that calculations for charge and quadrupole form factors are in progress.
