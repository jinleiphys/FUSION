# Unified description of electron-nucleus scattering within the spectral function formalism

> We developed a novel approach based on a generalization of factorization and nuclear spectral functions, allowing for a consistent treatment of the amplitudes involving one- and two-nucleon currents, whose contribution to the nuclear electromagnetic response in the transverse channel is known to be significant. We report the results of calculations of the inclusive electron-carbon cross section, showing that the inclusion of processes involving two-nucleon currents appreciably improves the agreement between theory and data in the dip region, between the quasi elastic and $Δ$-production peaks. The implications for the analysis of neutrino-nucleus cross sections are discussed.

## Key claim
The paper claims that a generalized factorization ansatz combined with nuclear spectral functions can consistently treat both one- and two-nucleon current contributions to electron-nucleus scattering, and that including two-nucleon currents appreciably improves agreement with data in the dip region between the quasi-elastic and Δ-production peaks. The hedge is that this improvement is demonstrated only for carbon at specific kinematics (momentum transfer 300–800 MeV), and that at lower beam energy (680 MeV) the two-body currents play an almost negligible role.

## Method
The authors generalized the spectral function formalism to include transition matrix elements involving two-nucleon currents, using a factorization ansatz that separates nuclear amplitudes from free-nucleon current matrix elements. They computed the inclusive electron-carbon cross section by decomposing the nuclear response tensor into 1p1h (one-particle–one-hole) and 2p2h (two-particle–two-hole) contributions, including interference terms. Final state interactions (FSI) were incorporated via a convolution approach using an optical potential, nuclear transparency, and a folding function derived from NN scattering cross sections.

## Key numbers
- Momentum transfer range: 300 to 800 MeV for the calculations.
- Beam energy 680 MeV, scattering angle 36 deg (panel A); beam energy 1300 MeV, scattering angle 37.5 deg (panel B).
- At E_e = 1.3 GeV and θ_e = 37.5 deg, Q² ~ 0.5 GeV².
- Ratio of integrated strengths in 1p1h and 2p2h sectors at E_e = 1.3 GeV: 4% at θ_e = 10 deg, growing to 46% at θ_e = 60 deg.
- Δ-resonance mass: m_Δ = 1232 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the spectral function formalism previously applied to quasi-elastic scattering (Benhar et al., 2008; Rocco et al., 2015) by including two-nucleon currents and FSI, building on earlier treatments of meson-exchange currents by Van Orden (1984) and Dekker et al. (1994). It enables a unified description of electron-nucleus cross sections that can be extended to neutrino-nucleus scattering, potentially resolving discrepancies between MiniBooNE and NOMAD quasi-elastic cross section measurements. The approach goes beyond the independent particle model by incorporating correlation effects and interference terms that are absent in Fermi gas models.
