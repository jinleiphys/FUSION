# Effects of equation of state on nuclear suppression and the initial entropy density of quark gluon plasma

> We study the effects of the equation of state on the nuclear suppression of heavy flavours in quark gluon plasma and estimate the initial entropy density of the system produced at the highest RHIC energy. For this purpose we have used the experimental data on the charged particle multiplicity and the nuclear suppression of single electron spectra originating from the semi-leptonic decays of open charm and beauty mesons. We have used inputs from lattice QCD to minimize the model dependence of the results. We obtain the value of the initial entropy density which varies from 20 to 59 /fm$^3$ depending on the value of the velocity of sound that one uses for the analysis. Our investigation leads to a conservative value of the initial entropy density $\sim 20/$fm$^3$ with corresponding initial temperature $\sim 210$ MeV well above the value of the transition temperature predicted by lattice QCD.

## Key claim

The initial temperature and entropy density of the quark-gluon plasma formed in Au+Au collisions at RHIC depend strongly on the assumed velocity of sound (equation of state), ranging from 210 to 300 MeV and 20 to 59/fm³ respectively, with a conservative estimate of ~20/fm³ and ~210 MeV that is well above the lattice QCD transition temperature.

## Method

The authors used the Fokker-Planck equation with temperature- and momentum-dependent drag and diffusion coefficients (including both elastic collisions and radiative processes with dead cone and LPM effects) to model heavy quark propagation through an expanding QGP background described by boost-invariant hydrodynamics. They constrained the initial entropy density by simultaneously fitting the measured charged particle multiplicity and the nuclear suppression factor R_AA of single electrons from semi-leptonic decays of charm and beauty mesons in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN)=200 GeV, using lattice QCD inputs for the equation of state and effective degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

- Initial entropy density s_i ranges from 20 to 59 /fm³ depending on the velocity of sound used
- Conservative estimate: s_i ~ 20 /fm³ with corresponding initial temperature T_i ~ 210 MeV
- For c_s² = 1/3: T_i = 300 MeV, s_i ~ 59 /fm³, thermalization time τ_i = 0.6 fm/c
- For c_s² = 1/4: T_i = 240 MeV, s_i ~ 29.34 /fm³, τ_i = 1.17 fm/c
- For c_s² = 1/5: T_i = 210 MeV, s_i ~ 19.66 /fm³, τ_i = 1.7 fm/c
- Transition temperature T_c fixed at 175 MeV from lattice QCD
- Effective degrees of freedom g_eff ~ 38 extracted from lattice QCD variation of s/T³ with T

## Context

This work extends earlier attempts to explain heavy quark R_AA data by including non-perturbative effects (HvH), three-body scattering (Ko), and temperature-dependent coupling (Gossiaux), while minimizing model dependence by using lattice QCD inputs. The obtained initial temperatures (210-300 MeV) are lower than the ~375 MeV from HvH and ~400 MeV from a simultaneous light/heavy quark analysis (Physical Review Letters 105), but consistent with values of 270-310 MeV inferred from initial gluon rapidity distributions (Journal of Physics G 32, Physical Review C 84, Journal of Physics G 34). The results enable future studies incorporating transverse expansion effects, which the authors note may partially cancel due to competing longer path length and diluted density.
