# Description of inclusive scattering of 4.045 GeV electrons from D

> We exploit a relationship between the Structure Functions of nucleons, the physical deuteron and of a deuteron, composed of point-nucleons to compute angular distributions of inclusive cross sections of 4.05 GeV electrons. We report general agreement with data and interpret the remaining discrepancies. We discuss the potential of the data for information on neutron structure functions $F_k^n(x,Q^2)$ and the static form factor $G_M^n(Q^2)$.

## Key claim

The authors compute inclusive electron-deuteron scattering cross sections at 4.045 GeV using a relationship between nucleon structure functions and those of a deuteron composed of point-nucleons, reporting general agreement with data but noting that discrepancies on the inelastic side of the quasielastic peak arise from the parametrization of nucleon structure functions which average over individual nucleon resonance excitations rather than from a failure of the underlying theoretical description.

## Method

The calculation uses a non-perturbative version of the Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) based on the Gersch-Rodriguez-Smith (GRS) theory for structure functions, folding the point-nucleon deuteron structure function with nucleon structure functions via a convolution relation. The deuteron ground state is described by the standard radial L=0 and L=2 components u and w, and final state interactions are included through an eikonal approximation for the off-shell pn scattering amplitude expressed in terms of elastic scattering observables. The structure functions are computed as functions of the scaling variable y and then converted to the (x, Q^2) representation.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy E = 4.045 GeV for inclusive electron scattering from deuterium
- The relation between structure functions is estimated to hold for Q^2 greater than approximately 2-2.5 GeV^2
- Peak values of the normalized point-nucleon structure function f^{PN,A}(x,Q^2) at Q^2 = 3.5 GeV^2 are 6.2 for deuterium, 3.1 for ^4He, and 1.4-1.6 for nuclei with A >= 12
- Cross sections are computed for scattering angles theta = 15°, 23°, 30°, 45°, and 55° as functions of energy loss nu
- The convolution relation is limited to x greater than approximately 0.15-0.20 due to contributions from virtual bosons and anti-screening effects

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous non-perturbative PWIA calculations for nuclei with A >= 12 (Rinat and Taragin, 1999) and a relativistic version of the GRS theory applied to ^4He (Viviani, Kievsky and Rinat, 2001) to the deuteron case, using the first available double-differential cross section data on deuterium at 4.045 GeV (Arrington, private communication; Niculescu et al., 2000). The precise calculation for the deuteron, enabled by the exact single-nucleon density matrix, allows extraction of the neutron magnetic form factor G_M^n and neutron structure functions F_k^n, complementing earlier work on static form factors (Lung et al., 1993) and enabling future improvements using Bethe-Salpeter descriptions.
