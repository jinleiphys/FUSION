# Vector Meson Dominance as a first step in a systematic approximation: the pion vector form factor

> Pade Approximants can be used to go beyond Vector Meson Dominance in a systematic approximation. We illustrate this fact with the case of the pion vector form factor and extract values for the first two coefficients of its Taylor expansion. Pade Approximants are shown to be a useful and simple tool for incorporating high-energy information, allowing an improved determination of these Taylor coefficients.

## Key claim

Padé Approximants can be used to go beyond Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) in a systematic, well-defined approximation for the pion vector form factor, allowing the extraction of low-energy Taylor coefficients with controlled uncertainties; the VMD ansatz is reinterpreted as the first element of a Padé sequence that can be systematically improved, though no mathematical theorem guarantees convergence for the real-world form factor with a cut and non-positive-definite spectral function.

## Method

The authors fit sequences of Padé Approximants (PAs) of type \(P^L_1\) (ratio of a polynomial of degree \(L\) over a polynomial of degree one), \(P^L_2\), Padé-Type approximants (with poles fixed at known physical resonance masses), and Partial-Padé approximants to all available space-like experimental data for the pion vector form factor from \(Q^2 = 0.015\) to 10 GeV\(^2\). The fitted rational functions are expanded around \(Q^2=0\) to extract the Taylor coefficients \(a_1\) and \(a_2\) of the form factor. A warm-up model using an Omnès representation with a realistic rho-meson phase shift is used to estimate systematic uncertainties from the approximation method.

## Key numbers

*   Best-fit Taylor coefficient \(a_1 = 1.92 \pm 0.03\) GeV\(^{-2}\) from the \(P^4_1\) Padé Approximant, with \(\chi^2/\mathrm{dof} = 117/90\).
*   Best-fit Taylor coefficient \(a_2 = 3.49 \pm 0.26\) GeV\(^{-4}\) from the \(P^4_1\) Padé Approximant.
*   Combined average across all rational approximant types: \(a_1 = 1.907 \pm 0.010_{\mathrm{stat}} \pm 0.03_{\mathrm{syst}}\) GeV\(^{-2}\).
*   Combined average for \(a_2 = 3.30 \pm 0.03_{\mathrm{stat}} \pm 0.33_{\mathrm{syst}}\) GeV\(^{-4}\).
*   Quadratic radius from combined average: \(\langle r^2 \rangle = 0.445 \pm 0.002_{\mathrm{stat}} \pm 0.007_{\mathrm{syst}}\) fm\(^2\).
*   In the warm-up model, the \(P^6_1\) Padé achieved relative errors of 0.9% for \(a_1\), 8% for \(a_2\), and 26% for \(a_3\) compared to the exact model values.

## Context

This work extends the traditional Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) approach by placing it as the first term in a systematic Padé sequence, addressing the long-standing question of how to improve upon the VMD monopole ansatz for the pion form factor. The results are compared with prior determinations from Colangelo et al. (CGL), Yndurain (TY), and Portoles (PP), among others, showing competitive precision. The method enables a controlled extraction of low-energy constants from space-like data by incorporating high-energy information through rational approximants, and can be applied to other form factors where similar analytic structures appear.
