# Nucleon pole contribution in the $pp\to ppK^+K^-$ reaction below the $ϕ$ meson threshold

> Nucleon pole contribution in the $pp\to ppK^+K^-$ reaction below the threshold of the production of the $ϕ$ meson is studied within the effective Lagrangian approach. It is assumed that the $K^- p$ final state originates from the decay of the hyperons $Λ(1115)$ and $Λ(1405)$. In addition to the $pp$ final state interaction (FSI) parametrized using the Jost function, we have also considered the $K^+K^-$ FSI with the techniques of the chiral unitary approach, where the scalar mesons $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ were dynamically generated. Hence, the contributions from scalar mesons $f_0(980)$ and $a_0(980)$ occur through the $K^+ K^-$ FSI. It is shown that the available experimental data are well reproduced, especially the total cross sections and the invariant mass distributions of $pp$ and $K^+K^-$. Furthermore, different forms of the couplings (pseudoscalar and pseudovector) for the $πNN$ interaction and different strengths for the proton-proton FSI are also investigated. It is found that the contributions from hyperon $Λ(1115)$ and $Λ(1405)$ are different between these two kinds of couplings. On the other hand, the effects of the proton-proton FSI can be adjusted by the cut off parameters used in the form factors.

## Key claim

The nucleon pole mechanism, combined with hyperon decays of Λ(1115) and Λ(1405) and final state interactions for both the pp and K⁺K⁻ subsystems, can reproduce the available experimental data for the pp → ppK⁺K⁻ reaction below the φ meson threshold, but the model still cannot well describe the K⁻p invariant mass distribution, indicating the need for further study of strong K⁻p final state interactions.

## Method

The authors employed an effective Lagrangian approach with π⁰ exchange as the dominant mechanism, considering both pseudoscalar (PS) and pseudovector (PV) couplings for the πNN and KNΛ vertices. The K⁺K⁻ final state interaction was treated using the chiral unitary approach, where the scalar mesons f₀(980) and a₀(980) are dynamically generated, while the pp final state interaction was parametrized using the Jost function formalism with two different parameter sets.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants used: g_{πNN} = 13.45, g_{KNΛ} = -13.98, g_{KNΛ*} = 1.51
- Cut-off parameter for the exchanged pion meson: Λ_π = 1.3 GeV
- Two sets of pp FSI parameters: Set A with α = 19.7 MeV, β = 98.7 MeV; Set B with α = -20.5 MeV, β = 166.7 MeV
- Cut-off parameters for exchanged hadrons: 1.5 GeV (Sets I and III) or 1.3 GeV (Sets II and IV)
- Excess energy range studied: from threshold to 30 MeV (below the φ threshold at ε = 32 MeV)
- Loop integration cutoff for meson-meson amplitudes: q_max = 1030 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous theoretical studies of pp → ppK⁺K⁻, particularly addressing the underestimation of total cross sections near threshold found in the model of Xie (2010) which used N*(1535) as a doorway state for Λ(1405) production. The study enables further investigation of the controversial Λ(1405) state and the scalar mesons f₀(980) and a₀(980), building on the chiral unitary approach of Oller (1997) and the experimental measurements from the COSY-11 and ANKE collaborations. The authors note that the strong K⁻p final state interaction remains an open question requiring further theoretical work.
