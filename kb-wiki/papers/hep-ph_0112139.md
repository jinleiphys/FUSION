# Production of intermediate-mass dileptons in relativistic heavy ion collisions

> The production of intermediate mass dileptons in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions at SPS energies is studied. The acceptance and detector resolution inherent to measurements by the NA50 experimental collaboration are accurately modeled. The measured centrality dependence of the intermediate mass lepton pair excess is also addressed.

## Key claim

The intermediate-mass dilepton excess observed by NA50 in Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies can be largely explained by thermal hadronic sources plus a modest quark-gluon plasma contribution, without requiring enhanced charm production; the best fit to the data is obtained with a formation time of 0.2 fm/c (initial temperature ~330 MeV), but a formation time of 0.4 fm/c (~265 MeV) remains within two standard deviations of most data points.

## Method

The authors model the space-time evolution of the collision using boost-invariant, azimuthally symmetric hydrodynamics with a quark-gluon plasma phase transitioning to a hadronic gas at T = 180 MeV, with freeze-out at 120 MeV. Dilepton emission rates are calculated from quark-antiquark annihilation in the QGP phase and from a comprehensive set of hadronic channels (ππ, πρ, πω, ηρ, ρρ, πa₁, KK, KK*+c.c.) constrained by e⁺e⁻ → hadrons data, with the initial energy density profile following a wounded-nucleon distribution. The NA50 detector acceptance and resolution are modeled using a numerical subroutine developed by the collaboration, and the centrality dependence is estimated by approximating the nuclear overlap region as a circle with radius proportional to (N_part/2)^(1/3).

## Key numbers

- Average initial temperature T₀ ≈ 330 MeV for formation time τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c, and T₀ ≈ 265 MeV for τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c, for central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS
- Average particle rapidity density dN/dy = 750 for the 10% most central Pb+Pb collisions at SPS
- Average number of participants ≈ 380 for impact parameters 0 ≤ b ≤ 4.5 fm (maximum 416 for head-on)
- Phase transition temperature T_c = 180 MeV; freeze-out temperature T_F = 120 MeV
- Quark matter contribution ≈ 23% of the total dilepton yield at invariant mass 1.5 GeV for τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c, and ≈ 19% for τ₀ = 0.4 fm/c
- Formation time τ₀ = 0.2 fm/c, constrained by the uncertainty principle lower limit τ₀ ~ 1/(3T₀)

## Context

This work extends earlier theoretical studies of the Helios/3 intermediate-mass dilepton excess by Gale and Lichard (1994), which attributed the excess to secondary hadronic reactions, and applies a similar approach to newer NA50 data with improved detector modeling. It contradicts the interpretation of the NA50 collaboration that an overall charm production enhancement is needed, and instead shows that thermal hadronic sources plus a modest QGP contribution can reproduce both the mass spectrum and centrality dependence. The results enable future comparisons with RHIC electromagnetic measurements and provide constraints on the initial temperature of the system formed in heavy ion collisions.
