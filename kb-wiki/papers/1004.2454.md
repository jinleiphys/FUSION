# Noncommutativity and Lorentz Violation in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> The experimental detection of the effects of noncommuting coordinates in electrodynamic phenomena depends on the magnitude of |θB|, where θis the noncommutativity parameter and B a background magnetic field. With the present upper bound on θ, given by θ_{\rm bound} \simeq 1/(10 {\rm TeV})^2, there was no large enough magnetic field in nature, including those observed in magnetars, that could give visible effects or, conversely, that could be used to further improve θ_{\rm bound}. On the other hand, recently it has been proposed that intense enough magnetic fields should be produced at the beginning of relativistic heavy ion collisions. We discuss here lepton pair production by free photons as one kind of signature of noncommutativity and Lorentz violation that could occur at RHIC or LHC. This allows us to obtain a more stringent bound on θ, given by 10^{-3} θ_{\rm bound}, if such "exotic" events do not occur.

## Key claim

If the exotic process of single free photon decay into an electron-positron pair is not observed in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC or LHC, the resulting bound on the noncommutativity parameter \(\theta\) would be \(10^{-3}\) times the current upper bound \(\theta_{\rm bound} \simeq 1/(10\ {\rm TeV})^2\), provided that the intense magnetic fields produced at the beginning of the collisions are indeed present.

## Method

The authors analyze the \(O(\theta)\)-corrected Maxwell action in noncommutative spacetime, which yields a modified dispersion relation for photons propagating transversely to a background magnetic field. They apply this dispersion relation to the kinematical condition for the decay of a single free photon into an electron-positron pair, using the magnetic field strength estimated for peripheral heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC. The time dependence of the magnetic field and the sidereal rotation of the Earth are incorporated to predict an oscillatory signal in the pair production rate.

## Key numbers

- The current upper bound on the noncommutativity parameter is \(\theta_{\rm bound} \simeq 1/(10\ {\rm TeV})^2\).
- For Gold-Gold collisions at RHIC (100 GeV per nucleon, impact parameter \(b = 10\ {\rm fm}\)), the initial magnetic field is \(B_0 \sim 1.9 \times 10^5\ {\rm MeV}^2\) with a decay time \(\tau = 0.05\ {\rm fm}/c\).
- With \(B \sim 2 \times 10^5\ {\rm MeV}^2\) and a photon energy \(E_\gamma = 50\ {\rm GeV}\), the invariant mass of the produced lepton pair is about \(2.5\ {\rm MeV}\); for \(B \sim 2 \times 10^6\ {\rm MeV}^2\) and \(E_\gamma = 100\ {\rm GeV}\), the invariant mass is about \(7.5\ {\rm MeV}\).
- At LHC for Gold-Gold collisions, the magnetic field could reach \(B_0 \simeq 3.2\ {\rm GeV}^2\).
- If a free photon with \(E_\gamma \simeq 100\ {\rm GeV}\) in such a field does not produce a pair, the bound on the noncommutativity parameter becomes \(\theta_{X,Y} < 2 m_e^2 / (B E_\gamma^2) \simeq 1/(10^5\ ({\rm TeV})^2) \simeq 10^{-3}\ \theta_{\rm bound}\).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological study of noncommutative electrodynamics (NCED) beyond previous analyses of synchrotron radiation (Noi et al.), Cerenkov effect in vacuum (Noi et al.), and ultra-high energy gamma rays (Noi et al.), which all concluded that effects were undetectable due to the small bound on \(\theta\) and the lack of sufficiently strong magnetic fields in nature. The authors build on the proposal by Kharzeev et al. (2008) that intense magnetic fields are produced at the beginning of relativistic heavy ion collisions, originally invoked to explain charge-dependent azimuthal correlations observed by the STAR Collaboration. The proposed signal of low-invariant-mass lepton pairs from single photon decay, with a sidereal time dependence, could provide a direct test of spacetime noncommutativity and Lorentz violation, or alternatively improve the bound on \(\theta\) by three orders of magnitude.
