# Scale-Chiral Symmetry, Proton Mass and Sound Velocity in Compact-Star Matter

> With a light dilaton $σ$ and the light-quark vector mesons $V=(ρ,ω)$ incorporated into an effective scale-invariant hidden local symmetric Lagrangian, scale-chiral symmetry -- hidden in QCD -- arises at a high density, $n_{1/2}$, as an "emergent" symmetry, a phenomenon absent in standard chiral perturbative approaches but highly relevant for massive compact stars. What takes place as the density increases beyond $n_{1/2}\sim 2n_0$ in compressed baryonic matter is (1) a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions, (2) parity doubling in the nucleon structure, (3) the maximum neutron star mass $M\simeq 2.01 M_{\odot}$ and the radius $R\simeq 12.0$ km and (4) the sound velocity $v_s^2/c^2\simeq 1/3$ due to the "vector manifestation (VM)" fixed point of $ρ$ and a "walking" dilaton condensate, which is intricately connected to the source of the proton mass.

# Scale-Chiral Symmetry, Proton Mass and Sound Velocity in Compact-Star Matter

## Key claim

The paper argues that at densities above approximately twice nuclear saturation density ($n_{1/2} \sim 2n_0$), an emergent scale-chiral symmetry in dense baryonic matter leads to a sound velocity of $v_s/c \approx 1/\sqrt{3}$ and accounts for most of the proton mass through a mechanism distinct from spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, but this prediction depends on the assumption that the dilaton condensate remains constant above $n_{1/2}$ and that the hidden gauge coupling flows to the vector manifestation fixed point.

## Method

The authors construct an effective scale-invariant hidden local symmetric (sHLS) Lagrangian incorporating a light dilaton $\sigma$ and vector mesons $V=(\rho,\omega)$, with baryons included either explicitly (bsHLS) or as skyrmions. The bare parameters of the Lagrangian are Wilsonian-matched to QCD at a scale $\Lambda_M$, acquiring intrinsic density dependence via QCD condensates. For densities below $2n_0$ (Region I), the density dependence is fixed by experimental data including the in-medium pion decay constant; for densities above $2n_0$ (Region II), the density dependence is extracted from skyrmion crystal simulations that predict a topology change from skyrmions to half-skyrmions at $n_{1/2}$. The equation of state is then computed using the $V_{lowk}$ renormalization-group technique, which is equivalent to a relativistic mean-field calculation.

## Key numbers

- Topology change density: $n_{1/2} \sim 2n_0$ (with $n_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$), estimated to lie in the range $2 \lesssim n/n_0 \lesssim 3$
- Maximum neutron star mass: $M \simeq 2.05 M_\odot$
- Neutron star radius: $R \simeq 12.19$ km
- Central density of maximum-mass star: $n_{cent} \simeq 5.1 n_0$
- Sound velocity in dense matter: $v_s^2/c^2 \simeq 1/3$ for $n > n_{1/2}$
- Symmetry energy at equilibrium: $E_{sym} \simeq 26$ MeV
- Slope parameter: $L \simeq 49$ MeV at equilibrium density
- Nuclear matter equilibrium density: $n_{eq} \simeq 0.15 n_0$
- Binding energy: B.E. $\simeq 16$ MeV
- Compression modulus: $K \simeq 215$ MeV
- Fit parameters for energy per particle in Region II: $B_{\alpha=0} = 570.031$ MeV, $B_{\alpha=1} = 686.4$ MeV, $D_{\alpha=0} = 439.676$ MeV, $D_{\alpha=1} = 252.888$ MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous program (Paeng et al., 2011; Dong et al., PKLR) combining scale-chiral effective field theory with skyrmion crystal simulations to describe dense matter. It builds on the Crewther-Tunstall (2015) scheme for implementing the dilaton and on the hidden local symmetry approach of Harada and Yamawaki (2003). The prediction of a sound velocity approaching $1/\sqrt{3}$ and a topology-driven change in the symmetry energy from soft to hard at $n_{1/2}$ contradicts standard nuclear models that do not incorporate such emergent symmetries. The results enable predictions for massive compact stars with masses near $2 M_\odot$ and suggest that the proton mass may arise primarily from scale symmetry breaking rather than chiral symmetry breaking, challenging the standard Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) paradigm.
