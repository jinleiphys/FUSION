# $Δ$-scaling and Information Entropy in Ultra-Relativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

> The $Δ$-scaling method has been applied to ultra-relativistic p+p, C+C and Pb+Pb collision data simulated using a high energy Monte Carlo package, LUCIAE 3.0. The $Δ$-scaling is found to be valid for some physical variables, such as charged particle multiplicity, strange particle multiplicity and number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions from these simulated nucleus-nucleus collisions over an extended energy ranging from $E_{lab}$ = 20 to 200 A GeV. In addition we derived information entropy from the multiplicity distribution as a function of beam energy for these collisions.

# Δ-scaling and Information Entropy in Ultra-Relativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions

## Key claim
The Δ-scaling method and information entropy analysis, applied to simulated p+p, C+C, and Pb+Pb collisions using the LUCIAE 3.0 Monte Carlo model, show no discontinuity or phase transition signal across beam energies from 20 to 200 A GeV, because the underlying particle production dynamics in the model is a smooth function of beam energy without a phase transition.

## Method
The authors used the LUCIAE 3.0 Monte Carlo model, an extension of FRITIOF that includes re-scattering of final hadrons, the Firecracker Model for collective multi-gluon emission, and energy-dependent strange quark suppression, to simulate head-on collisions for p+p (10,000 events per energy), C+C (5,000 events), and Pb+Pb (1,000 events) at beam energies from 20 to 200 A GeV. They applied the Δ-scaling formalism to probability distributions of charged particle multiplicity, strange particle multiplicity, and number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions, and computed information entropy from the multiplicity distributions using both direct calculation and Gaussian approximation.

## Key numbers
- For p+p collisions, the best Δ-scaling parameter for charged particle multiplicity distributions is Δ = 1.35 across the energy range 20–200 A GeV.
- For C+C collisions, the best Δ-scaling parameter for charged particle multiplicity distributions is Δ = 1.00.
- For Pb+Pb collisions, the best Δ-scaling parameter for charged particle multiplicity distributions is Δ = 0.80.
- For p+p at 20 A GeV, the direct information entropy H_direct = 2.237 and the Gaussian information entropy H_gauss = 2.290, with mean multiplicity ⟨m⟩ = 7.374 and RMS width m_RMS = 2.330.
- For p+p at 200 A GeV, the direct information entropy H_direct = 3.008 and the Gaussian information entropy H_gauss = 3.030, with mean multiplicity ⟨m⟩ = 13.11 and RMS width m_RMS = 4.951.

## Context
This work extends the Δ-scaling method, previously applied by Botet and Ploszajczak (1999) to intermediate-energy heavy ion collisions (Xe+Sn, 25–100 MeV/nucleon) and by Ma et al. to light nuclei multifragmentation data, to the ultra-relativistic regime for the first time. The study uses the LUCIAE model of Sa and Tai (1998), which does not incorporate a QCD phase transition, so the observed monotonic behavior of Δ-scaling parameters and information entropy serves as a baseline for future comparisons with models that include quark-gluon plasma formation. The authors suggest that Δ-scaling and entropy variables could become valuable tools for identifying discontinuities associated with the onset of a QCD phase transition in experimental data.
