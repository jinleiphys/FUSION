# Quark Orbital Angular Momentum and Final State Interactions

> Definitions of orbital angular momentum based on Wigner distributions are used to discuss the connection between the Ji definition of the quark orbital angular momentum and that of Jaffe and Manohar. The difference between these two definitions can be interpreted as the change in the quark orbital angular momentum as it leaves the target in a DIS experiment. The mechanism responsible for that change is similar to the mechanism that causes transverse single-spin asymmetries in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering.

## Key claim

The difference between the Ji definition of quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) and the Jaffe-Manohar definition can be interpreted as the change in the quark's OAM due to the torque from final-state interactions as the quark leaves the target in a deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) experiment, with the Ji definition representing the OAM before the quark is struck and the Jaffe-Manohar definition representing the OAM after it has escaped to infinity.

## Method

The paper uses Wigner distributions with different Wilson-line gauge link paths to define OAM, showing that a straight-line gauge link yields the Ji OAM while a light-cone staple gauge link (extending to light-cone infinity) yields the Jaffe-Manohar OAM. The difference between these two OAM definitions is expressed in terms of an integral over the gluon field strength tensor, which is interpreted semiclassically as the torque from the color Lorentz force acting on the struck quark. The analysis also corrects a spectator model for inclusive single-spin asymmetries by properly accounting for the fact that interactions with quarks of the same flavor as the active quark should be omitted when estimating the relevant correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- The sum rule for the average transverse momentum from final-state interactions in the abelian case: $\frac{2}{3}F_{FT}^u-\frac{1}{3}F_{FT}^d+...=0$, implying $F_{FT}^d=2F_{FT}^u$ if strange and heavier quarks are neglected.
- For the proton, the corrected relation between the QED and QCD correlation functions: $F^{u/p}_{FT}(x,x)=\frac{\alpha_{em}}{3\pi C_F \alpha_s M}gT_F^{u/p}(x,x)$ and $F^{d/p}_{FT}(x,x)=-\frac{2\alpha_{em}}{3\pi C_F \alpha_s M}gT_F^{d/p}(x,x)$.
- For the neutron: $F^{u/n}_{FT}(x,x)=\frac{\alpha_{em}}{3\pi C_F \alpha_s M}gT_F^{d/p}(x,x)$ and $F^{d/n}_{FT}(x,x)=-\frac{2\alpha_{em}}{3\pi C_F \alpha_s M}gT_F^{u,p}(x,x)$.
- The ratio of cross section differences for proton versus neutron: $\frac{\sigma_{UT}^p}{\sigma_{UT}^n}= \frac{2 T_{F}^{u/p} - T_{F}^{d/p}}{2 T_{F}^{d/p} - T_{F}^{u/p}}\approx -1$, assuming a vanishing isoscalar Sivers asymmetry.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of OAM definitions by Ji (1997) and Jaffe and Manohar (1990) using the Wigner distribution framework developed by Lorce and others. It parallels the earlier understanding of the Sivers effect, where the difference between TMDs defined with light-cone staple versus straight-line gauge links corresponds to the average transverse momentum acquired by quarks through final-state interactions. The corrected spectator model for inclusive SSAs resolves a violation of the sum rule in the earlier work by Metz and provides predictions for Jefferson Lab experiments. The interpretation enables lattice calculations of the Jaffe-Manohar OAM using nonforward matrix elements of space-like staples in fast-moving hadrons, as proposed by Engelhardt (2014).
