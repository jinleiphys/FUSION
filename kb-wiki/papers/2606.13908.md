# Anomalies, Topology, and Hadron Structure in QCD

> Quantum Chromodynamics (QCD) provides a remarkable realization of how quantum effects reshape the symmetries of a classical field theory. The axial anomaly links chirality to gauge-field topology and underlies the resolution of the $U(1)_A$ problem, while the trace anomaly generates the intrinsic QCD scale through dimensional transmutation and accounts for most of the mass of hadrons and hence visible matter. Together, these quantum effects reveal the central role of gluonic dynamics and vacuum structure in strong-interaction physics. In this review, we discuss the theoretical foundations and physical consequences of anomalous symmetry breaking in QCD. We examine anomalous Ward identities, the topological structure of gauge fields, instantons, topological susceptibility, and the realization of chiral and scale symmetries in the QCD vacuum. We review their role in the generation of hadron masses, the $η'$ mass, and the resolution of the $U(1)_A$ problem, and discuss their manifestations in polarized deep inelastic scattering, nucleon spin structure, and modern studies of hadron structure. Particular emphasis is placed on recent developments connecting vacuum topology, the flavor-singlet axial charge, topological screening, and the proton spin problem. Our aim is to provide a unified perspective on how quantum anomalies connect vacuum structure, hadron properties, and partonic observables, bridging nonperturbative dynamics and perturbative QCD.

## Key claim

This review argues that the axial anomaly and trace anomaly in QCD provide a unified bridge connecting vacuum topology, hadron mass generation, and nucleon spin structure, with particular emphasis on recent developments linking vacuum topology to the flavor-singlet axial charge, topological screening, and the proton spin problem.

## Method

The paper reviews the theoretical foundations of anomalous symmetry breaking in QCD by examining anomalous Ward identities, the topological structure of gauge fields, instantons, topological susceptibility, and the realization of chiral and scale symmetries in the QCD vacuum. It then connects these nonperturbative dynamics to experimental observables including polarized deep inelastic scattering, nucleon spin decomposition, anomaly-induced operator mixing, and helicity evolution, using the operator-product expansion and perturbative QCD evolution as the framework linking short-distance scattering to nonperturbative matrix elements.

## Key numbers

* The experimentally measured nucleon axial charge is g_A approximately 1.27, governing neutron beta decay and providing a direct probe of nucleon spin structure.
* The Gell-Mann-Oakes-Renner relation m_pi^2 f_pi^2 = -(m_u + m_d) <q qbar> + O(m_q^2) shows the pion mass vanishes linearly with light quark masses, with the coefficient determined by the chiral condensate and pion decay constant f_pi.
* The number of Goldstone bosons for N_f light flavors is N_G = N_f^2 - 1, giving three pions for two light flavors and the pseudoscalar octet for three light flavors.
* The Wess-Zumino-Witten term coefficient is quantized and proportional to N_c (the number of colors), linking low-energy meson dynamics directly to the microscopic color structure of QCD.

## Context

This review extends the theoretical framework established by Adler (1969), Bell and Jackiw (1969), Witten (1979), and Veneziano (1979) on the axial anomaly and U(1)_A problem, as well as the instanton-based approaches of 't Hooft (1976), Diakonov (1985), and Schafer (1996). It connects these foundations to modern developments in polarized deep inelastic scattering, nucleon spin structure, and hadron mass decomposition via gravitational form factors as studied by Ji (1995, 2021), Polyakov (2018, 2019), and others. The review enables a unified perspective bridging nonperturbative vacuum dynamics with perturbative QCD and experimentally accessible observables in hadron structure.
