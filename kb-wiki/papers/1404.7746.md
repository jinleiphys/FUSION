# Partial restoration of chiral symmetry in a confining string

> We attempt to describe the interplay of confinement and chiral symmetry breaking in QCD by using the string model. We argue that in the quasi-abelian picture of confinement based on the condensation of magnetic monopoles and the dual Meissner effect, the worldsheet dynamics of the confining string can be effectively described by the $1+1$ dimensional massless electrodynamics, which is exactly soluble. The transverse plane distribution of the chromoelectric field stretched between the quark and antiquark sources can then be attributed to the fluctuations in the position of the string. The dependence of the chiral condensate in the string on the (chromo-)electric field can be evaluated analytically, and is determined by the chiral anomaly and the $θ$-vacuum structure. Therefore, our picture allows to predict the distribution of the chiral condensate in the plane transverse to the axis connecting the quark and antiquark. This prediction is compared to the lattice QCD results; a good agreement is found.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the partial restoration of chiral symmetry observed around the confining flux tube in lattice QCD can be described by modeling the QCD string as a thin (1+1)-dimensional object whose position fluctuates in the transverse plane, with the dynamics governed by the exactly soluble Schwinger model; the agreement with lattice data depends on the assumption that the effective string radius is comparable to the lattice spacing.

## Method

The authors map the worldsheet dynamics of the confining string, in the quasi-abelian dual Meissner picture, onto the (1+1)-dimensional massless Schwinger model. They use the known analytic expression for the chiral condensate as a function of the background electric field in the Schwinger model, derived via the Feynman-Hellmann theorem. The transverse probability distribution of the thin string is extracted from lattice data on the chromoelectric field profile parameterized by Cea et al. (2012). The transverse distribution of the chiral condensate is then computed as a weighted average of the condensate with and without the string, using the extracted probability distribution and a fitted effective string radius.

## Key numbers

* The effective string radius extracted from the fit to lattice data is \(a = 1.12\ a_{latt}\), where \(a_{latt}\) is the lattice spacing.
* The chiral condensate in the (1+1)-dimensional Schwinger model in the presence of a background electric field \(E^{1+1}\) is given by \(\langle \bar{\psi}\psi \rangle_{E^{1+1}} = -\frac{g e^\gamma}{2\pi^{3/2}} \cos\left(\frac{2\pi E^{1+1}}{g}\right)\), where \(\gamma \approx 0.5772\) is the Euler number.
* The chromoelectric field profile used is parameterized as \(E(x_t)=\frac{\phi}{2\pi}\frac{\mu^2}{\alpha}\frac{K_0[(\mu^2 x_t^2+\alpha^2)^{1/2}]}{K_1(\alpha)}\), with parameters computed at lattice coupling \(\beta=6.01\) with 10 cooling steps.

## Context

This work extends the long-standing proposal by Casher, Kogut, and Susskind (1974) to use the Schwinger model as an effective theory of non-perturbative string fragmentation, which the authors have recently applied to jet fragmentation and multiparticle production (Loshaj and Kharzeev, 2011; Kharzeev and Loshaj, 2012). It directly compares to and finds good agreement with lattice QCD results on the chiral condensate around a static charge by Iritani et al. (2013), and uses the chromoelectric field profile from lattice studies by Cea et al. (2012). The approach enables future applications to nonabelian strings and to the phenomenology of nonperturbative transverse momentum distributions in jet fragmentation.
