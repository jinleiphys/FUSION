# Neutral Pion Production in the Threshold Region

> We give an overview of the physics motivation and evolution of the neutral pion photoproduction measurements in the threshold region conducted in the A2 collaboration at MAMI. The latest two experiments have been performed with the almost 4-pi Crystal Ball detector. The first was with a linearly polarized photon beam and unpolarized liquid-hydrogen target. The data analysis is now complete and the linearly polarized beam asymmetry along with differential cross sections provide the most stringent test to date of the predictions of Chiral Perturbation Theory and its energy region of convergence. More recently a measurement was performed using both circularly polarized photons and a transversely polarized butanol frozen-spin target, with the goal of extracting both the target and beam-target asymmetries. From these we intend to extract pi-N scattering sensitive information for the first time in photo-pion reactions. This will be used to test isospin conservation and further test dynamics of chiral symmetry breaking in QCD as calculated at low energies by Chiral Perturbation Theory.

## Key claim

The latest neutral pion photoproduction measurements at MAMI provide the most stringent test to date of Chiral Perturbation Theory predictions and its energy region of convergence, but the analysis is still preliminary and the maximum energy of convergence remains to be explored.

## Method

The A2 collaboration at MAMI performed two experiments using the almost 4π Crystal Ball and TAPS detector system. The first used a linearly polarized photon beam on an unpolarized liquid-hydrogen target to measure differential cross sections and the photon beam asymmetry Σ. The second used circularly polarized photons on a transversely polarized butanol frozen-spin target to extract the target asymmetry T and the beam-target asymmetry F.

## Key numbers

- The Crystal Ball and TAPS detector system covers approximately 96% of 4π, resulting in a detection efficiency for the π⁰ channel of roughly 90%, compared to the earlier TAPS-only setup which covered about 30% of 4π with roughly 10% efficiency.
- The earlier TAPS experiment provided photon asymmetry data only at the cross-section-weighted energy average of 159.5 MeV; the new CB-TAPS data provides photon asymmetries in 2.4-MeV-wide bins from just above threshold to 200 MeV.
- Preliminary results for the T and F asymmetries are shown at a photon energy of 320 MeV.
- The anticipated statistical uncertainty for the T asymmetry is approximately 1%, with systematic uncertainty ≤ 2% dominated by the degree of target polarization.

## Context

This work extends the long-running program of the A2 collaboration at Mainz studying low-energy γN Compton scattering and pion production to test Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT), building on earlier measurements by Beck, Fuchs, and Schmidt as well as parallel efforts at Saskatoon. The new measurements enable extraction of πN scattering information from photo-pion reactions for the first time, including the charge-exchange scattering length a_cex(π⁺n → π⁰p) from the unitary cusp above the π⁺n threshold, which will test isospin conservation and chiral symmetry breaking in QCD.
