# Holographic Duals of Quark Gluon Plasmas with Unquenched Flavors

> We review the construction of gravitational solutions holographically dual to N=1 quiver gauge theories with dynamical flavor multiplets. We focus on the D3-D7 construction and consider the finite temperature, finite quark chemical potential case where there is a charged black hole in the dual solution. Discussed physical outputs of the model include its thermodynamics (with susceptibilities) and general hydrodynamic properties.

## Key claim
This review paper claims that gravitational solutions holographically dual to N=1 quiver gauge theories with dynamical (unquenched) flavor multiplets can be constructed using the D3-D7 brane system with smeared flavors, but the construction is limited to the regime where the ratio Nf/Nc is treated as a small expansion parameter epsilon.

## Method
The authors construct a holographic dual of a quark-gluon plasma with unquenched flavors by considering D7-branes smeared in the background of D3-branes on a Sasaki-Einstein manifold, incorporating finite temperature and finite quark chemical potential via a charged black hole in the dual solution. They derive equations of motion and solve them perturbatively as an expansion in small epsilon (proportional to Nf/Nc) and small baryon density nb, obtaining explicit solutions up to second order in epsilon and first nontrivial order in nb.

## Key numbers
- The expansion parameter epsilon is proportional to Nf/Nc, treated as a small parameter for the perturbative solution
- The solution is obtained up to second order in epsilon and first nontrivial order in the baryon density nb
- The ratio of viscosity to entropy density in N=4 SYM plasma at strong coupling is mentioned as an almost universal prediction (no specific numerical value given in the text)

## Context
This work extends the holographic approach to quark-gluon plasmas beyond the quenched approximation (where Nf is fixed and Nc goes to infinity, suppressing flavor loops as in the 't Hooft limit described by 't Hooft (1974) and Veneziano (1976)). It builds on the D3-D7 construction previously developed by the same authors in Bigazzi et al. (2009) and Bigazzi et al. (2011), and relates to broader efforts to incorporate dynamical flavors in holography as reviewed by Nunez, Paredes, and Ramallo (2010). The results enable the study of thermodynamics, susceptibilities, and hydrodynamic properties of strongly coupled plasmas with fundamental matter, providing a more realistic holographic model for heavy ion collision physics at RHIC and LHC.
