# Electromagnetic transitions in multiple chiral doublet bands

> Multiple chiral doublet bands (M$χ$D) in the $80$, 130 and $190$ mass regions are studied by the model of $γ$=90$^{\circ}$ triaxial rotor coupled with identical symmetric proton-neutron configurations. By selecting the suitable basis, the calculated wave functions are explicitly exhibited to be symmetric under the operator $\hat{A}$, which is defined as rotation by $90^{\circ}$ about 3-axis with the exchange of valance proton and neutron. We found that both $M1$ and $E2$ transitions are allowed between the levels with different values of $A$, while are forbidden between the levels with same values of $A$. Such a selection rule holds true for M$χ$D in different mass regions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that for multiple chiral doublet bands (MχD) in the 80, 130, and 190 mass regions, both M1 and E2 electromagnetic transitions are allowed between levels with different values of the quantum number A (eigenvalue of the operator representing rotation by 90° about the 3-axis combined with exchange of valence proton and neutron), while forbidden between levels with the same A value. This selection rule holds strictly only for the ideal case of a γ=90° triaxial rotor coupled with identical symmetric proton-neutron configurations; deviations occur when γ deviates from 90° or configurations are asymmetric.

## Method

The authors employed a particle rotor model (PRM) with a γ=90° triaxial rotor core coupled to identical symmetric proton-neutron configurations: πg9/2 ⊗ νg−1 9/2 for A∼80, πh11/2 ⊗ νh−1 11/2 for A∼130, and πi13/2 ⊗ νi−1 13/2 for A∼190. They used a strong coupling basis expressed in terms of spherical harmonic oscillator states |nljk⟩ to explicitly examine wave function symmetry under the operator Â (rotation by 90° about the 3-axis with proton-neutron exchange). The calculated wave functions were systematically analyzed to determine the quantum number A for each level, and B(M1) and B(E2) transition probabilities were computed using standard electromagnetic operators.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameter β = 0.22 and moment of inertia J0 = 30 MeV−1ℏ2 were adopted for all three mass regions.
- The g-factor differences used were gp − gR ≈ 0.82 (−0.70) for g9/2, 0.77 (−0.65) for h11/2, and 0.76 (−0.60) for i13/2 orbits; approximate values gp − gR = 0.7 and gn − gR = −0.7 were taken for all orbits to deduce strictly forbidden M1 transitions.
- The bands were organized based on in-band B(E2; I→I−2) values over degenerate spin ranges: I ≥ 10ℏ for A∼80, I ≥ 12ℏ for A∼130, and I ≥ 14ℏ for A∼190.
- B(M1) and B(E2) values in excited chiral doublet bands have the same order of magnitude as those in lowest chiral doublet bands, while B(M1) and B(E2) values linking excited to lowest chiral doublet bands are two orders of magnitude smaller.
- For the I=17ℏ state of Band 1 in the 130 mass region, the wave function was expanded in a 1260-dimensional basis (∼(2jp+1)(2jn+1)(2I+1)/4).

## Context

This work extends the selection rule for electromagnetic transitions originally introduced by Koike et al. (2004) for the lowest chiral doublet bands in the 130 mass region to excited chiral doublet bands and to the 80 and 190 mass regions with identical symmetric proton-neutron configurations. The study provides the first explicit demonstration that calculated PRM wave functions for MχD satisfy strict symmetry under the operator Â, which was previously assumed but not quantitatively shown. The results enable future experimental identification of MχD with identical intrinsic configurations in odd-odd nuclei, such as those suggested in 103Rh by Kuti et al. (2014), and provide a theoretical benchmark for deviations observed when γ deviates from 90° or configurations are asymmetric.
