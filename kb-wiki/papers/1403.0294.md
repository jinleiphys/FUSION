# Microscopic Origin and Universality Classes of the Efimov Three-Body Parameter

> The low-energy spectrum of three particles interacting via nearly resonant two-body interactions in the Efimov regime is set by the so-called three-body parameter. We show that the three-body parameter is essentially determined by the zero-energy two-body correlation. As a result, we identify two classes of two-body interactions for which the three-body parameter has a universal value in units of their effective range. One class involves the universality of the three-body parameter recently found in ultracold atom systems. The other is relevant to short-range interactions that can be found in nuclear physics and solid-state physics.

## Key claim

The three-body parameter in the Efimov regime is determined by the zero-energy two-body correlation, specifically the suppression of two-body probability at short distances. This leads to two distinct universality classes for the three-body parameter in units of the effective range: one for power-law decaying potentials (relevant to ultracold atoms) and another for very deep short-range potentials decaying faster than any power law (relevant to nuclear physics), but the paper notes that typical nuclear interactions supporting only a few bound states do not reach this second universal limit.

## Method

The authors constructed separable potentials that exactly reproduce the zero-energy two-body s-wave radial wavefunction for various two-body interaction potentials, then solved the three-body problem for these separable interactions using an integral equation in momentum space. They compared the resulting three-body parameters (scattering length at which a trimer appears and binding wave number at unitarity) with exact calculations from prior work for Yukawa, exponential, Gaussian, Morse, Pöschl-Teller, and Lennard-Jones potentials.

## Key numbers

- For van der Waals (power-law) interactions: \(a_{-} = -10.86(1)\, r_{6}\) and \(\kappa = 0.187(1)\, r_{6}^{-1}\), where \(r_{6}\) is the van der Waals length
- The effective range for van der Waals interactions relates to the van der Waals length as \(r_{e} = \frac{4\pi}{3\Gamma(3/4)^{2}}r_{6} \approx 2.78947\, r_{6}\)
- In van der Waals units: \(a_{-} = -7.78(1) \times (\frac{1}{2}r_{e})\) and \(\kappa = 0.261(1) \times (\frac{1}{2}r_{e})^{-1}\)
- For the second universality class (very deep short-range potentials): \(\kappa = 0.2190(1) \times (\frac{1}{2}r_{e})^{-1}\)
- For nuclear interactions supporting at most one bound state: \(\kappa\) is close to \(0.35 \times (\frac{1}{2}r_{e})^{-1}\)
- The discrete scaling factor for identical bosons is \(e^{\pi/s_{0}} \approx 22.7\) with \(s_{0} \approx 1.00624\)

## Context

This work extends the numerical findings of Jia and Wang (2012) and Naidon et al. (2012) on the van der Waals universality of the three-body parameter, providing a microscopic explanation based on pair correlation. It contradicts the suggestion in Chin (2011) that quantum reflection explains the three-body repulsion, instead attributing it to a nonadiabatic deformation effect. The results enable estimation of the three-body parameter for any single-channel two-body interaction from its zero-energy pair correlation, and identify when universality classes apply versus when system-specific calculations are needed.
