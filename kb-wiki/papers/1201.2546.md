# Suppression of high transverse momenta hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC

> Hadron production and their suppression in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC at a center-of-mass energy of s_NN = 2.76 TeV are studied within a multiphase transport (AMPT) model whose initial conditions are obtained from the recently updated HIJING 2.0 model. The centrality dependence of charged hadron multiplicity dN_ch/deta at midrapidity was found quite sensitive to the largely uncertain gluon shadowing parameter s_g that determines the nuclear modification of the gluon distribution. We find final-state parton scatterings reduce considerably hadron yield at midrapidity and enforces a smaller gluon shadowing to be consistent with dN_ch/deta data at LHC. With such a constrained parton shadowing, charged hadron and neutral pion production over a wide transverse momenta range are investigated in AMPT. Relative to nucleon-nucleon collisions, the particle yield in central heavy ion collisions is suppressed due to parton energy loss. While the calculated magnitude and pattern of suppression is found consistent with that measured in Au+Au collisions at s_NN = 0.2 TeV at RHIC, at the LHC energy the suppression is overpredicted which may imply the medium formed at LHC is less opaque than expected from simple RHIC extrapolations. Reduction of the QCD coupling constant alpha_s by ~30% in the higher temperature plasma formed at LHC as compared to that at RHIC was found to reproduce the measured suppression at LHC.

## Key claim

The AMPT model, when using the same strong coupling constant as at RHIC, overpredicts the suppression of high-transverse-momentum hadrons in central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, which may imply the medium formed at LHC is less opaque than expected from simple RHIC extrapolations; a reduction of the QCD coupling constant alpha_s by about 30% in the higher temperature plasma at LHC was found to reproduce the measured suppression.

## Method

The authors used the AMPT (A MultiPhase Transport) model with initial conditions from the HIJING 2.0 model, incorporating parton-parton elastic scatterings via the ZPC parton cascade and final hadron transport via ART. They constrained the gluon shadowing parameter s_g by comparing the centrality dependence of charged hadron pseudorapidity density dN_ch/deta to ALICE data for Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, then calculated the nuclear modification factor R_AA for charged hadrons and neutral pions over a wide transverse momentum range.

## Key numbers

- Charged hadron pseudorapidity density at midrapidity for the 5% most central Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: dN_ch/deta (|eta|<0.5) = 1601 +/- 60 from ALICE data
- HIJING 2.0 model (without final state interactions) predicts dN_ch/deta (|eta|<0.5) = 1775 +/- 3 at LHC and 706 +/- 5 at RHIC
- Gluon shadowing parameter constrained to s_g = 0.16-0.17 for Pb+Pb at LHC and s_g = 0.10-0.17 for Au+Au at RHIC
- In central Pb+Pb collisions at LHC, R_AA reaches a minimum of 0.14 around 6-7 GeV/c in ALICE data; AMPT with alpha_s=0.33 gives a minimum of 0.09 at p_T ~ 6 GeV/c
- With reduced coupling alpha_s = 0.24 at LHC, AMPT reproduces the ALICE suppression data
- Estimated initial temperature T_i ~ 436 MeV for LHC and ~ 320 MeV for RHIC at proper time tau_i = 1 fm/c
- Parton-parton elastic scattering cross section estimated as ~ 1.4 mb at RHIC and ~ 0.76 mb at LHC
- Antibaryon to baryon ratios at LHC: anti-p/p = 0.88, anti-Lambda/Lambda = 0.95, anti-Xi/Xi = 0.99, anti-Omega/Omega = 1.00

## Context

This work extends the AMPT model framework previously applied at RHIC energies to the LHC energy of sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, using the updated HIJING 2.0 model for initial conditions. The study builds on prior work by the HIJING 2.0 collaboration (HIJ2) that constrained gluon shadowing from charged particle density data, and on earlier AMPT studies at RHIC. The finding that the LHC medium appears less opaque than RHIC-based extrapolations suggests a temperature-dependent QCD coupling constant, enabling future studies of energy loss mechanisms including both elastic collisional and radiative parton energy loss in the LHC energy regime.
