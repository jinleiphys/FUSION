# Local Finite Density Theory, Statistical Blocking and Color Superconductivity

> The motivation for the development of a local finite density theory is discussed. One of the problems related to an instability in the baryon number fluctuation of the chiral symmetry breaking phase of the quark system in the local theory is shown to exist. Such an instability problem is removed by taking into account the statistical blocking effects for the quark propagator, which depends on a macroscopic {\em statistical blocking parameter} $ε$. This new frame work is then applied to study color superconducting phase of the light quark system.

## Key claim

The conventional global Grand Canonical Ensemble treatment of the chiral symmetry breaking phase in light quark systems suffers from an instability in baryon number fluctuation that would produce large CP violation in the strong interaction vacuum state; this instability is removed by introducing a macroscopic statistical blocking parameter ε into the quark propagator, which accounts for non-perturbative Pauli blocking effects from the finite density of fermion-antifermion pairs in the vacuum.

## Method

The author constructs a local finite density quantum field theory by replacing the global chemical potential with a Lorentz 4-vector statistical gauge field μ^α(x), then applies the formalism to the half-bosonized Nambu Jona-Lasinio model in 3+1 dimensions and the chiral Gross-Neveu model in 2+1 dimensions. The effective potential is evaluated using a covariant Euclidean momentum cutoff (Λ ~ 1 GeV) rather than doing the p^0 integration first, which reveals an instability in the chiral symmetry breaking phase. A statistical blocking parameter ε is introduced by modifying the causal structure of the theory, filling fermion and antifermion states with energy below ε, which changes the p^0 integration contour and stabilizes the effective potential.

## Key numbers

- The covariant cutoff Λ is set to approximately 1 GeV for the numerical calculations.
- In D=4 with σ/Λ = 1.0, the absolute minimum of the effective potential is located at μ = 0 and ε/Λ = ±0.1.
- A pair of local minima exist at μ/Λ = ±0.1 and ε = 0.
- The effective potential shows ∂²V_eff/∂μ² < 0 at μ = 0 for σ ≠ 0 and D ≥ 2 when the covariant integration procedure is used without statistical blocking.

## Context

This work extends the author's own local finite density theory (Ying, 1999) to address an instability problem that arises when applying the conventional Grand Canonical Ensemble approach to relativistic systems where local measurements are required, such as in high-energy physics and astrophysics. The statistical blocking parameter provides a non-perturbative mechanism to stabilize the chiral symmetry breaking vacuum, and the framework is then applied to study the color superconducting phase of light quark systems, enabling future investigations of how local versus global measurements differ in quantum field theory at finite density.
