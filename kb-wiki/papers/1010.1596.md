# Plans for Hadronic Structure Studies at J-PARC

> Hadron-physics projects at J-PARC are explained. The J-PARC is the most-intense hadron-beam facility in the multi-GeV high-energy region. By using secondary beams of kaons, pions, and others as well as the primary-beam proton, various hadron projects are planned. First, some of approved experiments are introduced on strangeness hadron physics and hadron-mass modifications in nuclear medium. Second, future possibilities are discussed on hadron-structure physics, including structure functions of hadrons, spin physics, and high-energy hadron reactions in nuclear medium. The second part is discussed in more details because this is an article in the hadron-structure session.

## Key claim

The paper outlines future possibilities for hadron-structure studies at J-PARC, but explicitly states that these topics have not been officially approved yet or even a proposal does not exist for some projects, making the discussion somewhat based on the author's personal view.

## Method

The paper reviews the J-PARC facility's capabilities, including its 30-50 GeV primary proton beam and secondary beams (kaons, pions, antiprotons) at the hadron experimental facility. It first describes approved experiments in strangeness hadron physics (hypernuclei, exotic hadrons, kaonic nuclei) and hadron-mass modifications in nuclear medium, then discusses future possibilities for hadron-structure studies including Drell-Yan processes, spin physics with and without beam polarization, and high-energy hadron reactions in nuclear medium.

## Key numbers

- J-PARC beam intensity: 1 MW in the 3-GeV synchrotron and 0.75 MW in the 50 GeV synchrotron
- Proton beam energy: currently 30 GeV instead of the original 50 GeV design
- Center-of-mass energy for proton-proton collisions: sqrt(s) = 10 GeV at 50 GeV beam energy
- K1.8 and K1.1 beamlines for kaon beams with momentum around 1.8 GeV/c and 1.1 GeV/c, respectively
- KEK-PS measurements observed 9% mass shift for omega (rho) and 3% mass shift for phi mesons in nuclear medium
- AGS elastic spin asymmetry measurements up to p_perp^2 = 8 GeV^2
- Proposed J-PARC spin physics would probe medium-x region 0.2 < x < 0.7

## Context

This paper extends the work of the KEK-PS hadron program (KEK-PS, 2009) and complements higher-energy facilities like RHIC and LHC by focusing on the medium-x and large-x region of parton distributions. It builds on results from Fermilab E866 (which found flavor-asymmetric antiquark distributions), HERMES (tensor-polarized PDFs), and AGS (elastic spin asymmetry and color transparency measurements). The proposed studies would enable determination of parton distribution functions at large x, investigation of transverse-momentum-dependent distributions, and tests of perturbative QCD in the transition region between hadronic and quark-gluon degrees of freedom.
