# Comments About the Electromagnetic Field in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> In this short article we discuss the properties of electromagnetic fields in heavy-ion collisions and consequences for observables. We address quantitatively the issue of the magnetic field lifetime in a collision including the electric and chiral magnetic conductivities. We show that for reasonable parameters, the magnetic field created by spectators in a collision is not modified by the presence of matter. Based on this, we draw definite conclusions on observed effects in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The magnetic field created by spectator protons in heavy-ion collisions is not significantly modified by the presence of a conducting medium, contrary to earlier claims; this conclusion holds for realistic values of both Ohmic and chiral magnetic conductivities at top RHIC and LHC energies.

## Method

The authors solved Maxwell's equations numerically using the Yee algorithm, separating the electromagnetic field into external (from the colliding nuclei, treated in the eikonal approximation) and internal (from induced currents in the medium) components. They modeled the medium with Ohmic conductivity from lattice QCD and chiral magnetic conductivity proportional to the axial chemical potential, then compared the time evolution of the magnetic field at the collision origin for Au-Au collisions at fixed impact parameter.

## Key numbers

- Lattice QCD Ohmic conductivity above the transition temperature: sigma_Ohm = (5.8 +/- 2.9) (T/T_c) MeV
- For top RHIC energy, the inverse characteristic time scale is 1/t_c ~ 10^3 MeV, requiring sigma_Ohm >> 10^3 MeV for diffusion effects, which would demand T/T_c >> 10^2 (unrealistic)
- For an optimistic estimate of mu_5 ~ 1 GeV, the chiral conductivity sigma_chi is only 15 MeV, while the inverse characteristic time at top RHIC energy is 10^3 MeV
- At the LHC relative to RHIC, the magnetic field ratio squared (eB_LHC/eB_RHIC)^2 ~ 0.32, using Q_s proportional to (sqrt(s))^0.3
- The ratio of photon v_2 at LHC to RHIC is v_2^LHC/v_2^RHIC = (Q_s^RHIC/Q_s^LHC)^6 * (eB_LHC/eB_RHIC)^2

## Context

This work directly contradicts the magneto-hydrodynamic scenario of Tuchin (2013), which predicted a significant increase of magnetic field lifetime due to Ohmic conductivity under the assumption sigma_Ohm >> 1/t_c. The authors show this condition is never satisfied for realistic conductivities from lattice QCD calculations by Ding (2010) and Aarts (2007). The paper also addresses the apparent discrepancy between the long magnetic field lifetime (4 fm/c) required by the Chiral Magnetic Wave description of elliptic flow data from Burnier (2012) and the short lifetime (few 0.1 fm/c) from photon azimuthal anisotropy measurements at RHIC by Basar (2012). The results enable predictions that photon flow from magnetic field effects should be very small at LHC energies, testable with the method proposed by Bzdak (2012).
