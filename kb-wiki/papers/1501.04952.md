# Early thermalization, hydrodynamics and energy loss in AdS/CFT

> Gauge/gravity duality has provided unprecedented opportunities to study dynamics in certain strongly coupled gauge theories. This review aims to highlight several applications to heavy ion collisions including far-from-equilibrium dynamics, hydrodynamics and jet energy loss at strong coupling.

# Early thermalization, hydrodynamics and energy loss in AdS/CFT

## Key claim

Gauge/gravity duality provides a controlled, systematic framework for studying strongly coupled real-time dynamics in heavy ion collisions, but the dual description of QCD is not known; the results reviewed apply to N=4 super-Yang-Mills theory, which can offer lessons for QCD plasmas at temperatures not too far above the deconfinement transition due to shared features of strongly coupled non-Abelian plasmas.

## Method

The authors review how classical supergravity in asymptotically five-dimensional Anti-de Sitter spacetime (AdS5) is used to model strongly coupled gauge theory dynamics, including solving Einstein's equations for black hole horizons and shock wave collisions. The fluid/gravity correspondence is employed to compute transport coefficients from gradient expansions of Einstein's equations, and numerical relativity techniques are used to simulate far-from-equilibrium dynamics such as colliding gravitational shock waves.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity of strongly coupled N=4 SYM: η = s/(4π) = (π/8) Nc² T³, where s is entropy density
- Finite coupling and finite Nc correction to η/s: η/s = (1/(4π)) (1 + 15 ζ(3)/λ^(3/2) + (5/16) λ^(1/2)/Nc²), with λ = g²Nc the 't Hooft coupling
- Second order transport coefficients in strongly coupled SYM: τπ = (2 - ln(2))/(2πT), λ1 = η/(2πT), λ2 = λ1 ln(2)/2, λ3 = 0
- Lowest quasi-normal mode frequency: Im(ω0) ≈ 8.6 T
- Lower bound on entropy produced in central gold nucleus-nucleus collision: S ≥ S_trapped ≈ 35000 (√sNN / 200 GeV)^(2/3)
- Thermalization time from quasi-normal mode estimate: t_therm < 1/T, which for T = 1000 MeV corresponds to about 0.2 fm/c
- For boost-invariant thermalization, thermalization occurs at τ ≲ 0.5/Teff, where Teff is the temperature associated with the energy density at that time
- For homogeneous thermalization with over 1000 initial states, thermalization always happens within t ≲ 1/T

## Context

This review extends the foundational work of Maldacena (1997) on gauge/gravity duality and the computation of shear viscosity by Policastro, Son and Starinets (2001). It builds on the fluid/gravity correspondence developed by Bhattacharyya et al. (2008) and Baier et al. (2007) to systematically derive hydrodynamic transport coefficients. The shock wave collision studies extend earlier work by Gubser et al. (2008) on trapped surface entropy estimates and by Chesler and Yaffe (2010, 2011) on numerical simulations of thermalization. The review enables future comparisons between holographic predictions and experimental data from RHIC and LHC, particularly for entropy production, thermalization timescales, and jet energy loss at strong coupling.
