# Multiplicity Dependence of Partially Coherent Pion Production in Relativistic Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate two- and three-particle intensity correlation functions of pions in relativistic heavy ion collisions for different colliding energies. Based on three models of particle production, we analyze the degree to which the pion sources are chaotic in the SPS S+Pb, Pb+Pb and the RHIC Au+Au collisions. The ``chaoticity'', $λ$, of the two-particle correlation functions is corrected for long-lived resonance decays. The effect of the partial Coulomb correction is also examined. Although the partially coherent model gives a result which is consistent with that of STAR, the chaotic fraction does not exhibit clear multiplicity dependence if we take into account both the corrected chaoticity and the weight factor of the three-pion correlation function. The result of the partially-multicoherent model indicates an increasing number of coherent sources in higher multiplicity events.

## Key claim

The chaotic fraction of pion sources does not exhibit a clear multiplicity dependence when both the corrected chaoticity and the weight factor of the three-pion correlation function are taken into account, although the partially-multicoherent model indicates an increasing number of coherent sources in higher multiplicity events.

## Method

The authors analyzed two- and three-particle intensity correlation functions of pions from SPS S+Pb, SPS Pb+Pb, and RHIC Au+Au collisions using three models: a partially coherent model (Model I), a multicoherent source model (Model II), and a partially-multicoherent source model (Model III). They corrected the experimental chaoticity for long-lived resonance decays using a statistical model to obtain the true chaoticity, and extracted the weight factor from simultaneous chi-squared fits of the two- and three-particle correlation functions using three different source functions (Gaussian, exponential, and cosh).

## Key numbers

* SPS S+Pb (NA44, minimum bias): true chaoticity lambda_true = 0.94 +/- 0.06; weight factor omega = 0.40 +/- 0.44 (cosh source function)
* SPS Pb+Pb (NA44, central): true chaoticity lambda_true = 0.98 +/- 0.03; weight factor omega = 1.15 +/- 0.67 (cosh source function)
* SPS Pb+Pb (WA98, central): true chaoticity lambda_true = 1.03 +/- 0.04; weight factor omega = 0.78 +/- 0.14 (cosh source function)
* RHIC Au+Au (STAR, central): true chaoticity lambda_true = 0.93 +/- 0.08; weight factor omega = 0.872 +/- 0.097 (cosh source function)
* Thermodynamic parameters for SPS S+Pb: T = 173 +/- 2 MeV, mu_B = 196 +/- 2 MeV; for SPS Pb+Pb: T = 161 +/- 4 MeV, mu_B = 223 +/- 7 MeV; for RHIC Au+Au: T = 158 +/- 9 MeV, mu_B = 36 +/- 6 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of pion interferometry and the HBT effect in relativistic heavy ion collisions, building on the partially coherent model of Heinz (1996) and the multicoherent source model of Nakamura (2000). The study addresses the "HBT puzzle" where hydrodynamic models fail to reproduce experimental two-particle correlation results, and provides a systematic comparison across SPS and RHIC energies. The results enable future investigations into the nature of pion sources and the possible role of coherent sources such as disoriented chiral condensate domains in high-multiplicity events.
