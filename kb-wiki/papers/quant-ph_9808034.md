# Some Aspects of Generalized Contact Interaction in One-Dimensional Quantum Mechanics

> We construct a one-dimensional contact interaction ($ε$ potential) which induces the discontinuity of the wave function while keeping its derivative continuous. By combining the $ε$ potential and the Dirac's $δ$ function, we construct most general one-dimensional contact interactions allowable under the time reversal symmetry. We present some elementary results for the scattering problem which suggest a dual relation between $δ$ and $ε$ potentials.

## Key claim

The paper constructs a one-dimensional contact interaction (the "ε potential") that makes the wave function discontinuous while keeping its derivative continuous, and shows that combining this with the Dirac δ potential generates the full three-parameter family of time-reversal-symmetric contact interactions as zero-range limits of local, self-adjoint potentials.

## Method

The authors consider a potential consisting of three δ functions at positions -a, 0, and +a with strengths v₁, v₀, v₁, and take the limit a → 0 while scaling the strengths as v₀ ~ u/a² and v₁ ~ 2/u - 1/a. This yields the ε-potential connection matrix. They then decompose any SL(2,R) connection matrix into products of δ and ε connection matrices, showing that the general contact interaction can be realized as a zero-range limit of local potentials. Scattering coefficients are computed for both δ and ε potentials, and the two-body scattering problem for identical particles is analyzed.

## Key numbers

- For the δ potential of strength v: transmission coefficient T_δ(k) = k² / [k² + (v/2)²], reflection coefficient R_δ(k) = (v/2)² / [k² + (v/2)²]
- For the ε potential of strength u: transmission coefficient T_ε(k) = (2/u)² / [k² + (2/u)²], reflection coefficient R_ε(k) = k² / [k² + (2/u)²]
- The duality relation between δ and ε coupling constants for identical particles: v u = 4
- For identical fermions with ε potential: C_ε(k) = (2ik + 4/u) / (2ik - 4/u); for identical bosons with ε potential: C_ε(k) = 1
- For identical fermions with δ potential: C_δ(k) = 1; for identical bosons with δ potential: C_δ(k) = (2ik + v) / (2ik - v)

## Context

This work extends the self-adjoint extension theory of Albeverio et al. (1988) for one-dimensional contact interactions, which previously had resisted local self-adjoint realization except for the trivial δ case. Earlier approximations by Seba (1986), Careau (1993), Chernoff and Hughes (1993), and Avron, Exner, and Last (1994) were either non-local or non-Hermitian. The construction enables the design of mesoscopic devices realizing generalized contact interactions and establishes the ε potential as the natural zero-range limit of two-body interactions between identical fermions, analogous to the δ potential for bosons.
