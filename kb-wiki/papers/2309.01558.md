# Ab initio calculation of the alpha-particle monopole transition form factor

> We present a parameter-free ab initio calculation of the $α$-particle monopole transition form factor in the framework of nuclear lattice effective field theory. We use a minimal nuclear interaction that was previously used to reproduce the ground state properties of light nuclei, medium-mass nuclei, and neutron matter simultaneously with no more than a few percent error in the energies and charge radii. The results for the monopole transition form factor are in good agreement with recent precision data from Mainz.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the alpha-particle monopole transition form factor can be accurately reproduced without any parameter adjustment using a minimal SU(4)-invariant nuclear interaction previously fitted to ground-state properties of light and medium-mass nuclei and neutron matter, thereby resolving the "puzzle" suggested by earlier discrepancies between theory and experiment.

## Method

The authors use nuclear lattice effective field theory (NLEFT) with a minimal SU(4)-symmetric Hamiltonian containing two- and three-body contact interactions and Coulomb perturbation theory, with parameters fixed in Lu et al. (2018). They perform auxiliary-field Monte Carlo simulations on periodic cubic boxes of lengths 13.2, 14.5, and 15.7 fm, using Euclidean time projection to extract the ground and first excited 0⁺ states of ⁴He. The transition form factor is computed via the pinhole algorithm, which samples the A-body density in position space.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of ⁴He: −28.30(3) MeV for L = 14.5 fm and L = 15.7 fm, compared to experimental −28.30 MeV.
- First excited state energy of ⁴He: −7.96(9) MeV for L = 15.7 fm, compared to experimental −8.09 MeV.
- Energy difference ΔE = E(0₂⁺) − E(³H) = 0.40(9) MeV for L = 15.7 fm.
- Transition radius squared ⟨r²⟩_tr = 1.49(1) fm² for the SU(4) plus Coulomb interaction, fitted in the range q² = 0.04–0.25 fm⁻².
- Transition radius parameter ℛ_tr = 4.00(4) fm for the SU(4) plus Coulomb interaction, fitted in the range q² = 0.04–0.25 fm⁻².
- Lattice spacing a = 1.32 fm, corresponding to momentum cutoff Λ ≈ 471 MeV.
- Time step size a_t = (1000 MeV)⁻¹ = 0.197 fm⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the NLEFT framework of Lu et al. (2018), which successfully described ground-state properties of nuclei and neutron matter, to the monopole transition in ⁴He. It directly addresses discrepancies between precision data from the Mainz Microtron MAMI (Kegel et al., 2021) and earlier ab initio calculations using phenomenological and chiral potentials. The results are consistent with the coupled-channel no-core Gamow shell model calculations of Michel et al. (2023) and the universal Efimov tetramer relation, and they enable future systematic calculations using high-fidelity chiral interactions and wave function matching.
