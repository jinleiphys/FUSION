# The leading Lyapunov exponent in the glasma

> We show that small perturbations in the boost-invariant color fields of the glasma exhibit an exponential growth with the square root of time. We interpret this growth rate as a Lyapunov exponent, related to entropy production and the thermalization timescale in the earliest stage of heavy-ion collisions. Working in a regime that is linear in this perturbation, we extract the time dependence of this mode as $\sim \exp(0.4\sqrt{g^2μτ})$ for SU($2$), where $g^2μ$ is proportional to the saturation scale and the square-root dependence is caused by the boost-invariant expansion of the system. We show that the growth rate of this mode is, unlike its amplitude, remarkably insensitive to the details of how the perturbations are initialized. In particular, we show that the unstable mode couples to all momentum scales present in the initial perturbation.

## Key claim

Small perturbations in the boost-invariant color fields of the glasma exhibit exponential growth with the square root of proper time, not exponential in time itself, due to the boost-invariant expansion of the system. The growth rate is remarkably insensitive to how the perturbations are initialized, although the amplitude of the unstable mode depends on the initial conditions.

## Method

The authors initialize infinitesimally small fluctuations in the classical color fields at the initial time of a heavy-ion collision within the McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model for SU(2) gauge group, using a real-time lattice gauge theory solver. They evolve the glasma fields forward in proper time using the classical Yang-Mills equations in Milne coordinates with boost invariance, perturbing either the longitudinal electric field \(E_\eta\) or the longitudinal magnetic field \(B_\eta\) separately with white noise or momentum-filtered noise. The growth of the perturbation is monitored by measuring the difference between the perturbed and unperturbed field configurations, and the leading Lyapunov exponent is extracted by fitting the time dependence of this growth at late times.

## Key numbers

* The leading unstable mode grows as \(\sim \exp(0.4\sqrt{g^2\mu\tau})\) for SU(2), where \(g^2\mu\) is proportional to the saturation scale.
* The perturbation amplitude parameter \(\alpha\) for the electric field and \(\beta\) for the magnetic field are chosen in the range \(10^{-5}\) to \(10^{-3}\) to remain in the linear regime.
* The saturation scale \(Q_\mathrm{s}\) is of order \(1\) to \(2\) GeV at the LHC, with an uncertainty of a factor \(\lesssim 2\) from the difference between SU(2) and SU(3).

## Context

This work extends studies of chaotic behavior in classical Yang-Mills systems, previously investigated in thermal equilibrium by Muller (1992), Biro (1993), Heinz (1996), and Kunihiro (2010), to the non-equilibrium glasma state relevant for heavy-ion collisions. It builds on earlier work on isotropization and instabilities in the glasma by Romatschke (2005, 2006) and Epelbaum (2013), and on Lyapunov exponent extraction for non-equilibrium field configurations by Iida (2013). The results provide a timescale for entropy production and thermalization in the glasma, and enable future extraction of the full Lyapunov spectrum and analysis of the structure of the corresponding fluctuations.
