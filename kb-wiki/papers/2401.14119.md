# Pseudoscalar mesons from a PNJL model at zero temperature

> We study pseudoscalar $π$, $K$ and $η$ meson properties, such as masses and couplings, in dense matter at zero temperature. We use a recently proposed phenomenological quark model, known as the PNJL0, which takes into account the confinement/deconfinement phase transition by means of the traced Polyakov loop ($Φ$) which serves as an order parameter at zero temperature. We consider two different scenarios, namely, symmetric quark matter with equal chemical potentials for all the flavors, and the beta equilibrated matter. In the latter case the hadron-quark phase transition is implemented by a two model approach. For the hadron side we use a relativistic mean-field model with density dependent couplings. We show that $Φ$ induces abrupt changes in the mesons properties with gap sizes regulated by the phenomenological gluonic sector of the model.

## Key claim
The paper shows that the traced Polyakov loop (Φ), introduced as an order parameter at zero temperature in the PNJL0 model, induces abrupt changes in pseudoscalar meson masses and couplings at the confinement/deconfinement phase transition, with the size of these gaps regulated by the phenomenological gluonic sector parameters (G_V and a_3).

## Method
The authors use the PNJL0 model, a recently proposed extension of the NJL model that couples quarks to a scalar background field Φ (the traced Polyakov loop) at zero temperature, with coupling constants modified as G_s → G_s(1-Φ²), G_V → G_V(1-Φ²), and K → K(1-Φ²). They compute meson masses and effective couplings for π, K, and η mesons by solving the RPA polarization insertion equations in two scenarios: symmetric flavor matter (equal chemical potentials for u, d, s quarks) and beta-equilibrated stellar matter, where the hadron-quark phase transition is implemented via a Maxwell construction between the PNJL0 model and the DDHδ relativistic mean-field model with density-dependent couplings.

## Key numbers
- For symmetric flavor matter with G_V/G_s=0.2 and a_3=-0.08, the first-order confinement/deconfinement phase transition occurs at chemical potential μ=529.5 MeV.
- For beta-equilibrated stellar matter with G_V/G_s=0.5 and a_3=-0.458, the hadron-quark phase transition coincides with the confinement/deconfinement transition at baryonic chemical potential μ_Bc=1910.4 MeV.
- The PNJL0 model parameters used are: G_s=3.67/Λ², K=-12.36/Λ⁵, m_u=m_d=5.5 MeV, m_s=140.7 MeV, Λ=602.3 MeV, and T_0=190 MeV.
- The DDHδ hadronic model uses normal nuclear density ρ_0=0.16 fm⁻³.
- The kaon model parameters are f_K=122 MeV, d_1=2.5/m_K, and d_2=0.72/m_K.
- The η-nucleon coupling parameters are Σ_ηN=283 MeV and κ=0.4.

## Context
This work extends the PNJL0 model developed by Mattos et al. (2019, 2021) from thermodynamics to meson properties, and continues the approach of Mattos et al. (2021) that coupled the PNJL0 model to the DDHδ hadronic model for hybrid star applications. The study of meson properties in dense matter connects to experimental searches for mesic nuclei (Friedman, Cieply, Nagahiro) and theoretical predictions of kaon condensation in neutron stars (Glendenning, Pons, Vijayan). The results enable future calculations of meson spectral functions and transport properties in the mixed phase region of neutron star interiors.
