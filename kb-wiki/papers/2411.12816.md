# Intertwined Quantum Phase Transitions in Bose and Bose-Fermi Systems

> Pronounced structural changes within individual configurations (Type I QPT), superimposed on an abrupt crossing of these configurations (Type II QPT), define the notion of intertwined quantum phase transitions (QPTs). We discuss and present evidence for such a scenario in finite Bose and Bose-Fermi systems. The analysis is based on algebraic models with explicit configuration mixing, where the two types of QPTs describe shape-phase transitions in-between different dynamical symmetries and shape-coexistence with crossing.

## Key claim

Intertwined quantum phase transitions (QPTs) occur when a Type I QPT (structural change within a single configuration) is superimposed on a Type II QPT (abrupt crossing of two configurations), but only if the mixing between the configurations is weak enough that both types of QPT remain distinguishable.

## Method

The analysis uses the interacting boson model (IBM) for even-even nuclei and the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM) for odd-even nuclei, extended to include configuration mixing (IBM-CM) by associating normal (0p-0h) and intruder (2p-2h) shell-model spaces with boson spaces of N and N+2 bosons, respectively. The Hamiltonian is cast in matrix form with separate Hamiltonians for each configuration and a mixing term, and the energy surfaces are obtained from coherent-state expectation values to enable classical Landau analysis alongside quantum diagonalization.

## Key numbers

- Characteristic energy ratios for the three dynamical symmetry limits of the IBM: R_{4/2}=E(4_1)/E(2_1)=2 (U(5) spherical), 3.33 (SU(3) prolate), 2.5 (SO(6) gamma-unstable)
- In the Zr isotopes, the normal A configuration shows R^{(A)}_{4/2} ≈ 1.6 (spherical) while the intruder B configuration shows R^{(B)}_{4/2} ≈ 2.3 (weakly deformed) for neutron numbers 50-56
- In 104Zr, the intruder B configuration reaches R^{(B)}_{4/2}=3.24, indicating strong prolate deformation
- The energy offset parameter Δ in the IBM-CM Hamiltonian shows a sharp decrease by 1 MeV beyond neutron number 56
- The U(5) n_d=0 probability in the lowest 0^+_B state is approximately 90% for neutron numbers 52-58, then drops drastically at neutron number 60

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach to quantum phase transitions developed by Gilmore (1979) and Dieperink (1980) and the configuration-mixing framework of Duval and Barrett (1981, 1982) for the IBM. It builds on prior studies of shape coexistence and Type II QPTs in nuclei near shell closures by Heyde (2011) and Frank (2006), and applies the intertwined QPT concept introduced by Gavrielov (2019, 2020, 2022) to the zirconium isotope chain. The results enable future studies of coupled shape-phase transitions in both even-even and odd-even nuclei using the IBFM framework.
