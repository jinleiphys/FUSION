# Deuteron electromagnetic properties and the viability of effective field theory methods in the two-nucleon system

> The central tenet of effective theory is that the details of short-distance physics will not have a significant impact on low-energy observables. Here we perform an analysis of electron-deuteron scattering at low momentum transfers which is based on effective field theory. We show that in our approach the deuteron electromagnetic form factors $F_C$ and $F_M$ indeed are largely insensitive to the short-range $NN$ potential at momenta $Q$ up to about 700 MeV. We also find that the effective field theory approach to deuteron electromagnetic structure provides a systematic justification for many features which have been seen in potential model calculations of the same quantities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that deuteron electromagnetic form factors \(F_C\) and \(F_M\) are largely insensitive to the short-range nucleon-nucleon potential for momentum transfers \(Q\) up to about 700 MeV, provided the effective field theory approach (specifically \(\Lambda\)-counting) is used. The hedge is that this insensitivity holds only when the regulator mass is set near the scale of short-distance physics and the asymptotic tail of the deuteron wave function is fitted to experimental values.

## Method

The authors adopt Weinberg's power-counting (\(\Lambda\)-counting) to construct a chiral perturbation theory expansion of the nucleon-nucleon potential and the two-particle-irreducible kernel for electron-deuteron scattering. They generate deuteron wave functions by integrating the Schrodinger equation inward from infinity, using the experimental deuteron binding energy \(B\), asymptotic S-state normalization \(A_S\), and asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta\) as inputs, and including one-pion exchange in the differential equation. A square-well regulator of radius \(R\) is introduced at short distances to render the calculation finite, and the wave functions are then used in the nonrelativistic impulse approximation to compute the deuteron electromagnetic form factors \(F_C\), \(F_Q\), and \(F_M\) up to next-to-leading order in the \(\Lambda\)-expansion.

## Key numbers

* The deuteron binding energy \(B\) is used as an input parameter, with the pole in the two-nucleon T-matrix occurring at momenta of 46 MeV in the \(^3S_1-^3D_1\) channel.
* The asymptotic S-state normalization \(A_S\) and the asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta\) are fitted to experimental values to ensure the correct residue at the deuteron pole.
* The momentum transfer \(Q\) up to which the form factors are insensitive to short-range physics is about 700 MeV.
* The pion mass \(m_\pi\) is approximately 140 MeV, and the scale of chiral symmetry breaking \(\Lambda_\chi\) is the next-lightest scale above \(m_\pi\).
* The pion decay constant \(f_\pi\) is 93 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the application of effective field theory (specifically \(\Lambda\)-counting, as used by Lepage (1999) and Weinberg (1990, 1991)) to deuteron electromagnetic form factors, providing a systematic justification for features seen in potential model calculations (e.g., Nijmegen, Bonn, Paris, Argonne potentials). It contrasts with the Q-counting scheme of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), which treats one-pion exchange perturbatively; the authors suggest that electron-deuteron scattering at momenta \(Q \sim 2 m_\pi\) can test whether one-pion exchange can be treated perturbatively in the two-nucleon system. The results enable reliable error estimates for low-energy deuteron observables and clarify the domain of validity of EFT methods in nuclear physics.
