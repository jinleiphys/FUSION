# `` $풑- 풖$ conversion in $^4_풑$He and $^4_풑$H based on four-body calculation

> Precise four-body calculations for $^4_풑$He and $^4_풑$H have been performed in the framework of the variational method with Jacobian-coordinate Gaussian-basis functions. All the rearrangement channels of both $NNN 풑$ and $NNN 풖$ are explicitly taken into account for the first time with the use of realistic $NN$ and $YN$ interactions. The role of $풑$-$풖$ conversion and the amount of the virtual $풖$-component in $^4_풑$He and $^4_풑$H are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that precise four-body calculations including explicit $NNN\Lambda$ and $NNN\Sigma$ channels can reproduce the ground-state binding energies of $^4_\Lambda$He and $^4_\Lambda$H, but the $1^+$ excited state remains less bound by 0.6 MeV ($^4_\Lambda$He) and 0.4 MeV ($^4_\Lambda$H) compared to experiment, indicating the adopted $YN$ interaction is not sufficient for the excited state.

## Method

The authors performed variational four-body calculations using Jacobian-coordinate Gaussian-basis functions, explicitly including all rearrangement channels of both $NNN\Lambda$ and $NNN\Sigma$ configurations. They employed the AV8 potential for the $NN$ interaction and a simulated version of the NSC97f $YN$ potential with central, spin-orbit, and tensor terms, which reproduces the $\Lambda$ binding energy of $^3_\Lambda$H (0.19 MeV) consistent with the observed value (0.13 췀 0.05 MeV). The total wavefunction was expanded in an $LS$-coupling scheme with angular momenta $l, L, \lambda \leq 2$, and eigenenergies were determined by the Rayleigh-Ritz variational method.

## Key numbers

- $B_\Lambda(^3_\Lambda{\rm H}) = 0.19$ MeV calculated, compared to observed $0.13 \pm 0.05$ MeV
- $B_\Lambda(^4_\Lambda{\rm He}(0^+)) - B_\Lambda(^4_\Lambda{\rm H}(0^+)) = -0.05$ MeV calculated, compared to experimental $+0.35$ MeV
- $1^+$ state less bound by 0.6 MeV for $^4_\Lambda$He and 0.4 MeV for $^4_\Lambda$H relative to observed binding energies
- $\Sigma$-channel admixture probabilities in $^4_\Lambda$He: 2.08% for $0^+$ state, 1.03% for $1^+$ state
- Nucleon r.m.s. radius $\bar{r}_N = 1.65$ fm in $^4_\Lambda$He, shrunk by 8% from 1.79 fm in $^3$He
- With only $\Sigma$-channel amplitude, $\bar{r}_N = 1.49$ fm, a reduction of 17%
- Three-body process (ii) contributes attraction of 0.62 MeV in $0^+$ state and repulsion of 0.09 MeV in $1^+$ state of $^4_\Lambda$He
- Purely central-force coupling in process (ii): $-1.54$ MeV (attractive) in $0^+$, $+0.43$ MeV (repulsive) in $1^+$
- Purely tensor-force coupling in process (ii): $-0.35$ MeV in $0^+$, $-0.06$ MeV in $1^+$

## Context

This work extends earlier Faddeev calculations for $^3_\Lambda$H by Miyagawa et al. and four-body calculations by Gibson et al. (1988) and Carlson using Variational Monte Carlo, by performing the first explicit four-body treatment including all $NNN\Lambda$ and $NNN\Sigma$ rearrangement channels with realistic interactions. The results enable systematic study of $\Lambda$-$\Sigma$ conversion in heavier hypernuclei, though the authors note that the $1^+$ state discrepancy and the charge-symmetry-breaking effect (evidenced by the $0^+$ ground-state binding difference between $^4_\Lambda$He and $^4_\Lambda$H) require more refined $YN$ interactions. The analysis separating two-body and three-body contributions from $\Lambda N$-$\Sigma N$ coupling provides a framework for comparing with the coupled two-body model of Akaishi et al. (2000).
