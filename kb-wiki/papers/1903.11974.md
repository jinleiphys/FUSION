# Effects of Hadron-Quark Phase Transitions in Hybrid Stars within the NJL Model

> We study local and non-local Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models and analyze their respective phase transition diagram. We construct hybrid stars using the zero temperature limit of the local and non-local versions of Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model for quark matter and the modern GM1(L) parametrization of the non-linear relativistic mean field model for hadronic matter. We compare our models with data from PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0343+0432 and also from GW170817 and its electromagnetic counterpart GRB170817A and AT2017gfo. We study observational signatures of the appearance of a mixed phase as a result of modeling a phase transition that mimics the Gibbs formalism and compare the results with the sharp first-order phase transition obtained using the Maxwell construction. We also study in detail the $g$-mode associated with discontinuities in the equation of state, and calculate non-radial oscillation modes using relativistic Cowling approximation.

## Key claim

The paper investigates whether a smooth (Gibbs-like) mixed phase or a sharp (Maxwell) first-order phase transition between hadronic and quark matter better describes hybrid stars, finding that the choice of phase transition construction significantly affects observable properties such as stellar mass-radius relations and g-mode oscillation frequencies, though both local and non-local NJL models can satisfy current observational constraints from pulsar masses and gravitational wave events.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid star equations of state by combining the GM1(L) relativistic mean field model for hadronic matter (including hyperons and Delta isobars) with both local and non-local Polyakov Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models for quark matter at zero temperature, including vector interactions. They model the hadron-quark phase transition using two approaches: a Maxwell construction representing a sharp first-order transition, and a continuous interpolation function that mimics the Gibbs formalism for a mixed phase. They then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar structure and compute non-radial oscillation modes using the relativistic Cowling approximation, focusing on the g-mode associated with discontinuities in the equation of state.

## Key numbers

- Maximum masses of hybrid stars reach approximately 2.0 solar masses, satisfying constraints from PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0343+0432
- The effective tidal deformability constraint from GW170817 gives an upper limit of \(\tilde{\Lambda} < 800\), which translates to a 1.4 solar mass neutron star radius \(R_{1.4}\) not exceeding approximately 13.6 km at 90% confidence level
- Light quark condensates at zero chemical potential are \(\alpha^{1/3} = \beta^{1/3} = -241.9\) MeV for the local model and \(\langle\bar{\psi_u}\psi_u\rangle^{1/3} = \langle\bar{\psi_d}\psi_d\rangle^{1/3} = -243.8\) MeV for the non-local model
- The non-local NJL model parameters reproduce \(f_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, \(m_\pi = 139.0\) MeV, \(m_K = 495.0\) MeV, and \(m_{\eta'} = 958.0\) MeV
- Local NJL model parameters: \(m_u = m_d = 5.5\) MeV, \(m_s = 140.7\) MeV, \(\Lambda = 602.3\) MeV, \(G_s\Lambda^2 = 3.67\), \(H\Lambda^5 = -12.36\)
- Non-local NJL model parameters: \(m_u = m_d = 5.50\) MeV, \(m_s = 127.77\) MeV, \(\Lambda = 780.63\) MeV, \(G_s\Lambda^2 = 14.48\), \(H\Lambda^5 = -267.24\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hybrid stars with quark matter cores by Orsaria (2013, 2014) and others who used NJL models, and builds on calculations by Logoteta (2012, 2013) who focused on finite-size effects in mixed phase structures. The paper also re-examines the universal relationship linked to the Constant Speed of Sound parametrization from Ranea-Sandoval (2018). The results enable future comparisons with gravitational wave data from LIGO's third observing run and provide a framework for distinguishing between different phase transition scenarios in neutron star interiors.
