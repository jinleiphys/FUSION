# On long-range pionic Bose-Einstein correlations -- Including analyses of OPAL, L3 and CMS BECs

> Long-range correlation plays an important role in analyses of pionic Bose-Einstein correlations (BECs). In many cases, such correlations are phenomenologically introduced. In this investigation, we propose an analytic form. By making use of the form, we analyze the OPAL BEC and the L3 BEC at $Z^0$-pole and the CMS BEC at 0.9 and 7 TeV using our formulas and the $τ$-model. The parameters estimated by both approaches are found to be consistent. Utilizing the Fourier transform in four-dimensional Euclidean space, a number of pion-pair density distributions are also studied.

## Key claim

The paper proposes an analytic form for the long-range correlation (LRC) in pionic Bose-Einstein correlations (BECs), given by \( \text{LRC}_{(\alpha, \beta, n)} = C[1 + \alpha Q^n \exp(-\beta Q)] \), which avoids the unphysical asymptotic behavior of the previously used phenomenological quadratic form \( C(1 + \delta Q + \varepsilon Q^2) \) that diverges to negative infinity as \( Q \to \infty \). The authors show that this new form yields consistent parameter estimates when compared to the \( \tau \)-model across multiple datasets, and that negative \( \alpha \) values may indicate the presence of non-Bose-Einstein effects.

## Method

The authors analyzed pionic BEC data from OPAL and L3 at the \( Z^0 \)-pole and from CMS at 0.9 and 7 TeV. They fitted the data using a conventional correlation function \( \text{CF}_I = [1 + \lambda E_{\text{BE}}(R, Q)] \cdot \text{LRC} \) and a two-component form \( \text{CF}_{II} = [1 + \lambda_1 E_{\text{BE}_1}(R_1, Q) + \lambda_2 E_{\text{BE}_2}(R_2, Q)] \cdot \text{LRC} \), employing both their proposed analytic LRC and the \( \tau \)-model. They also performed Fourier transforms in four-dimensional Euclidean space to compute pion-pair density distributions from the correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- OPAL data at \( Z^0 \)-pole: using LRC\(_{(\delta, \varepsilon)}\) gave \( R = 0.95 \pm 0.02 \) fm (Gaussian), \( \lambda = 0.88 \pm 0.04 \), \( \chi^2/\text{ndf} = 113/59 \); using LRC\(_{(\alpha, \beta, n=2)}\) with \( \alpha < 0 \) gave \( R = 1.25 \pm 0.06 \) fm, \( \lambda = 0.46 \pm 0.04 \), \( \chi^2/\text{ndf} = 77.5/59 \).
- L3 data at \( Z^0 \)-pole: for 2-jet events with LRC\(_{(\alpha, \beta, n=2)}\), \( R_1 = 0.87 \pm 0.08 \) fm (exponential), \( \lambda = 0.57 \pm 0.03 \), \( \chi^2/\text{ndf} = 91.6/95 \); for 3-jet events, \( R_1 = 1.19 \pm 0.05 \) fm, \( \lambda = 0.77 \pm 0.03 \), \( \chi^2/\text{ndf} = 88.5/95 \).
- CMS data at 0.9 TeV: using CF\(_{II} \times\) LRC\(_{(\alpha, \beta, n=2)}\) gave \( R_1 = 3.07 \) fm (exponential), \( \lambda_1 = 0.71 \), \( R_2 = 0.13 \) fm (Gaussian), \( \lambda_2 = 0.10 \), \( \chi^2/\text{ndf} = 209/191 \).
- CMS data at 7 TeV: using CF\(_{II} \times\) LRC\(_{(\alpha, \beta, n=2)}\) gave \( R_1 = 3.42 \) fm (exponential), \( \lambda_1 = 0.75 \), \( R_2 = 0.15 \) fm (Gaussian), \( \lambda_2 = 0.08 \), \( \chi^2/\text{ndf} = 207/191 \); the \( \tau \)-model gave \( \chi^2/\text{ndf} = 289/192 \).

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological treatment of long-range correlations in Bose-Einstein correlations, which were previously handled with simple linear or quadratic forms (e.g., LRC\(_{(\delta)}\) and LRC\(_{(\delta, \varepsilon)}\) used by the OPAL collaboration). The proposed analytic form provides a physically motivated alternative that asymptotically approaches a constant value. The results are compared with the \( \tau \)-model of Csorgo (2003, 2008) and collaborators, showing consistency. The Fourier transform analysis enables the extraction of pion-pair density distributions in Euclidean space-time, which can be used to study the size and shape of the particle-emitting source in different collision systems, from \( e^+e^- \) collisions at LEP to \( pp \) collisions at the LHC.
