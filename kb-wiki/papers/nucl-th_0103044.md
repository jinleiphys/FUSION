# Compton Scattering off Relativistic Bound States: Two-Photon Vertices, Ward-Takahashi Identities, Gauge Invariance and Low-Energy Limit

> In a general framework that has been labeled the ``gauging of equations method'', we study the diagrams that contribute to Compton scattering off a relativistic composite system. These contributions can be derived for $N$--particle bound states described by the covariant Bethe--Salpeter equation with a method equivalent to minimal substitution in the one--particle case and yield the correct contributions (including subtraction terms) in the order ${\cal O}(e^2)$. We give the Ward--Takahashi identities for the general two--photon vertex as well as the corresponding constraints for the two--photon irreducible interaction kernel and the Bethe--Salpeter amplitude describing the bound state. From this we can show that gauge invariance holds for the full two--photon vertex. We furthermore study in detail the low--energy limit of the Compton scattering tensor in this approach (including a discussion of the pole terms) and can prove that the full amplitude yields the correct Born--Thomson limit as we shall explicitly show for the spin--0 case. The calculations are completed by the investigation of certain approximations that can be formulated for arbitrary $N$--particle bound states. We neglect for instance contributions from $n$--photon irreducible interaction kernels and show that in this case gauge invariance is only realized if either the interaction kernel in the Bethe--Salpeter equation is independent of the total momentum and additionally is of local type, or if the photon energies vanish; furthermore, we find the correct low--energy limit in this approximation. To clarify our approach, we also give the results in the order ${\cal O} (e)$; as examples, we will quote some resulting lowest order expressions for a $q\bar q$ system explicitly.

## Key claim

The paper provides a complete classification of diagrams contributing to Compton scattering off relativistic bound states described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation, showing that gauge invariance holds for the full two-photon vertex and that the correct Born-Thomson limit is obtained for the spin-0 case, but only when all contributions including subtraction terms are included; neglecting n-photon irreducible interaction kernels preserves gauge invariance only if the interaction kernel is independent of total momentum and of local type, or if photon energies vanish.

## Method

The authors apply the "gauging of equations method" (minimal substitution) to expand Green's functions, Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and interaction kernels to second order in the electromagnetic coupling e, deriving the full two-photon vertex for N-particle bound states described by the covariant Bethe-Salpeter equation. They then derive Ward-Takahashi identities for the two-photon vertex and the n-photon irreducible interaction kernels, and study the low-energy limit of the Compton scattering tensor including pole terms, explicitly verifying the Born-Thomson limit for the spin-0 case.

## Key numbers

- The expansion is carried out to order O(e^2) for the two-photon vertex, with the full vertex given by Γ^{μν}(P',P) = Γ_0^{μν}(P',P) - iK^{μν}(P',P) + Γ^μ(P',P'-q_1)G(P-q_2)Γ^ν(P-q_2,P) - Γ_0^μ(P',P'-q_1)G_0(P-q_2)Γ_0^ν(P-q_2,P) + crossing terms
- The Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in second order is χ^{μν}(P',P) = G(P')Γ^{μν}(P',P)χ(P)
- The Ward-Takahashi identity for the one-photon vertex reads: -iq_μΓ^μ(P+q,P) = G^{-1}(P+q) - G^{-1}(P)
- The Ward-Takahashi identity for the two-photon vertex reads: -iq_{1μ}Γ^{μν}(P+q_1-q_2,P) = G^{-1}(P+q_1-q_2)G(P-q_2)Γ^ν(P-q_2,P) - Γ^ν(P+q_1-q_2,P+q_1)G(P+q_1)G^{-1}(P)

## Context

This work extends the "gauging of equations method" developed by Kvinikhidze and Blankleider (1990s) from first-order electromagnetic processes to second-order Compton scattering, providing a more systematic alternative to the diagram-based analysis of Ito and Gross (1990s) which was applied only to the deuteron system with one charged constituent. The results enable a complete treatment of Compton scattering off any N-particle bound state described by the Bethe-Salpeter equation, including mesons (q\bar q systems) and baryons, and provide a firm basis for studying approximations such as neglecting n-photon irreducible interaction kernels.
