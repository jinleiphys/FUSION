# Investigation of 10Be and its cluster dynamics from nonlocalized clustering concept

> We extend the new concept of nonlocalized clustering to the nucleus 10Be with proton number Z=4 and neutron number N=6 (N=Z+2). The Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function is formulated for the description of different structures of 10Be. Physical properties such as energy spectrum and root-mean-square radii are calculated for the first two 0+ states and corresponding rotational bands. With only one single THSR wave function, the calculated results show good agreement with other models and experimental values. We apply, for the first time, the THSR wave function on the chain orbit (σ-orbit) structure in the 0^+_2 state of 10Be. The ring orbit (π-orbit) and σ-orbit structures are further illustrated by calculating the density distribution of the valence neutrons. We also investigate the dynamics of ff-clusters and the correlations of two valence neutrons in 10Be.

## Key claim

The authors extend the nonlocalized clustering concept, previously applied to N=Z and N=Z+1 nuclei, to the N=Z+2 nucleus 10Be, claiming that a single Tohsaki-Horiuchi-Schuck-Röpke (THSR) wave function can describe both the ground state and the 0+2 chain state, with the caveat that the ground state binding energy from a single THSR wave function (-58.0 to -58.2 MeV) is about 1-2 MeV less bound than other theoretical models, requiring superposition of 8 wave functions to reach -59.0 MeV.

## Method

The authors construct THSR wave functions for 10Be with two formulations: an independent wave function where valence neutrons move freely, and a correlated wave function incorporating dineutron correlations via a pair creation operator. For the 0+2 chain state, they introduce a one-dimensional constrained THSR wave function with a node factor (D-|R_n,z|) to produce the sigma-orbit structure. They apply angular-momentum projection and center-of-mass correction, use the Volkov No. 2 central force and G3RS spin-orbit interaction, and optimize variational beta parameters using Monte Carlo integration.

## Key numbers

- Ground state binding energy: -58.0 MeV (independent THSR), -58.2 MeV (correlated THSR), -59.0 MeV (superposition of 8 THSR wave functions)
- Experimental ground state binding energy: -65.0 MeV
- Excitation energy of 2+1 state: 3.5 MeV (calculated), 3.4 MeV (experimental)
- Root-mean-square radius of 0+1 ground state: 2.57 fm (single THSR), compared to 2.30 fm (experimental)
- Root-mean-square radius of 0+2 state: 3.11 fm (THSR), compared to 2.96 fm from Kobayashi (2011) and 3.4 fm from Kobayashi (2012)
- Overlap between 0+1 and 0+2 states: 1.4%
- Optimum beta parameters for ground state: beta_alpha,xy=0.1 fm, beta_alpha,z=2.0 fm, beta_n,xy=2.0 fm, beta_n,z=3.5 fm (independent); beta_pair,xy=0.8 fm, beta_pair,z=1.8 fm, beta_n,xy=2.0 fm, beta_n,z=2.7 fm (correlated)
- Optimum beta parameters for 0+2 state: beta_alpha=3.5 fm, beta_n=4.0 fm, D=2.0 fm
- Alpha+alpha+n+n threshold: -55.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends the nonlocalized clustering concept from previous applications to 8Be, 12C, 16O, 20Ne, and 9Be (Lyu, 2015) to the neutron-rich nucleus 10Be, which has been studied with various models including AMD (Kobayashi, 2011), AMD+GCM (Suhara, 2010), and molecular orbit models (Oertzen, 1996; Itagaki, 2000). The results enable future application of the THSR approach to more general nuclear structures beyond traditional alpha aggregates, with the Monte Carlo technique requiring less numerical work than traditional GCM treatments.
