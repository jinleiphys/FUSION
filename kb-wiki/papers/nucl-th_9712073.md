# On the Delta-Nucleon and Rho - Pi Splittings: A QCD-inspired Look in Free Hadrons versus Nuclei

> Relationships between mass intervals for free hadrons and in nuclei are studied in two theoretical approaches inspired by QCD: naive quark model and skyrmion model, taking one example each from mesons and baryons, that of pi-rho splitting in mesons, and nucleon-Delta splitting in baryons. Possible deconfinement effects in nuclei are examined.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed increase of the baryonic mass interval (Delta-Nucleon splitting) in nuclei, compared to free hadrons, can be interpreted as evidence for partial deconfinement in the nuclear medium, using two complementary QCD-inspired models. The authors explicitly note that their analysis is qualitative and that traditional nuclear many-body methods (e.g., Delta-hole models) already explain these intervals successfully without invoking QCD.

## Method

The authors analyze the Delta-Nucleon and Rho-Pi mass splittings in free hadrons and in nuclei using two theoretical frameworks: the nonrelativistic naive quark model with color hyperfine interactions, and the Skyrme soliton model with large-N scaling. In the quark model, they assume both the constituent quark mass and the hyperfine constant change in the nuclear medium, and extract these changes from experimental data on nucleon and Delta effective masses in nuclei. In the Skyrme model, they derive scaling relations connecting the baryonic mass interval to the axial coupling constant g_A and the pion decay constant F_pi, then use the Delta-hole model to compute numerical values for the mass and interval ratios as functions of nuclear density.

## Context

This work extends the study of hadron mass splittings in the nuclear medium using QCD-inspired models, contrasting with traditional nuclear many-body approaches such as the particle-hole and Delta-hole methods (Lenz et al., 1970s; Oset et al., 1980s) which successfully describe these intervals without invoking QCD. The paper connects to studies of nucleon swelling and the EMC effect (European Muon Collaboration, 1980s), and to the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model predictions of a sharp drop in quark mass with nuclear Fermi momentum (Ripka and collaborators, 1996). The analysis enables future experimental tests of in-medium hadron property renormalization, including quenching of g_A in nuclei (Mukhopadhyay, 1970s) and changes in electromagnetic and strong vertices, opening a complementary line of QCD exploration in nuclei beyond conventional many-body theory.
