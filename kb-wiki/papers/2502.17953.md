# Stellar weak rates of the $rp$-process waiting points: Effects of strong magnetic fields

> Incorporating microscopic nuclear-structure information into the discussion of bulk properties of astronomical objects such as neutron stars has always been a challenging issue in interdisciplinary nuclear astrophysics. Using the $rp$-process nucleosynthesis as an example, we studied the effective stellar $β^+$ and electron capture (EC) rates of eight waiting-point (WP) nuclei with realistic stellar conditions and presence of strong magnetic fields. The relevant nuclear transition strengths are provided by the projected shell model. We have found that, on average, due to the magnetic field effect, the $β^+$ and EC rates can increase by more than an order of magnitude for all combinations of density and temperature, as well as in each of WPs studied. We relate the onset field strength, at which the weak rates begin to increase, to nuclear structure quantities, $Q$ value or electronic chemical potential $μ_e$. The enhanced weak rates may change considerably the lifetime of WPs, thereby modifying the current understanding of the $rp$-process.

## Key claim

The stellar beta-plus and electron capture rates of eight rp-process waiting-point nuclei can increase by more than an order of magnitude due to strong magnetic fields, with the onset field strength for this enhancement determined by either the Q-value (for beta-plus decay) or the electron chemical potential (for electron capture), depending on the specific nuclear structure and environmental conditions.

## Method

The authors calculated stellar beta-plus and electron capture rates for eight waiting-point nuclei (64Ge, 68Se, 72Kr, 76Sr, 80Zr, 84Mo, 88Ru, and 92Pd) using the projected shell model to provide nuclear transition strengths, including state-to-state transitions from ground and thermally-populated low-lying states of parent nuclei to ground and excited states up to 10 MeV in daughter nuclei. The rates were computed by integrating over Landau levels in the presence of magnetic fields, with electron and positron distribution functions following the Fermi-Dirac distribution and the electron chemical potential determined self-consistently from the electron density. Only allowed Gamow-Teller transitions were considered.

## Key numbers

- The onset field strength for beta-plus rate increase is approximately B_onset ≈ Q_11^2 × 4.4 × 10^12 G, yielding about 3 × 10^14 G for 64Ge (Q_11 ≈ 8) and about 10^15 G for 92Pd (Q_11 ≈ 16)
- The onset field strength for electron capture rate increase is approximately B_onset ≈ μ_e^2 × 4.4 × 10^12 G, giving about 10^12 G for ρY_e = 10^5 mol/cm^3, about 5 × 10^12 G for ρY_e = 10^6 mol/cm^3, and about 2 × 10^13 G for ρY_e = 10^7 mol/cm^3
- The critical magnetic field B_c is approximately 4.414 × 10^13 G
- The constant K = 6144 ± 2 s is adopted from Hardy (2009)
- For 64Ge with ρY_e = 10^6 mol/cm^3, electron capture becomes dominant once B exceeds about 10^13 G

## Context

This work extends previous studies of magnetic field effects on beta-decay rates for r-process neutron-rich nuclei by Famiano (2020), Kajino (2014), and Famiano (2020), but distinguishes itself by treating both beta-plus decay and electron capture channels on an equal footing for proton-rich nuclei near the N=Z line, where electron capture has positive Q-values unlike the negative ones for r-process nuclei. The enhanced weak rates may modify the lifetime of waiting-point nuclei, thereby changing the current understanding of the rp-process path and final element abundances, and the method can be extended to general studies of r- and rp-process problems with magnetic fields.
