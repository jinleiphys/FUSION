# Light-Front projection of spin-1 electromagnetic current and zero-modes

> The issue of the contribution of zero-modes to the light-front projection of the electromagnetic current of phenomenological models of vector particles vertices is addressed in the Drell-Yan frame. Our analytical model of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude of a spin-1 fermion-antifermion composite state gives a physically motivated light-front wave function symmetric by the exchange of the fermion and antifermion, as in the $ρ$-meson case. We found that among the four independent matrix elements of the plus component in the light-front helicity basis only the $0\to 0$ one carries zero mode contributions. Our derivation generalizes to symmetric models, important for applications, the above conclusion found for a simplified non-symmetrical form of the spin-1 Bethe-Salpeter amplitude with photon-fermion point-like coupling and also for a smeared fermion-photon vertex model.

## Key claim

For a symmetric, physically motivated Bethe-Salpeter amplitude model of a spin-1 fermion-antifermion composite state (such as the ρ-meson), the zero-mode contributions to the plus component of the electromagnetic current in the Drell-Yan frame are confined to the 0→0 matrix element in the light-front helicity basis; the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription for extracting form factors eliminates this zero-mode, allowing form factors to be computed using only the valence region.

## Method

The authors constructed an analytical model of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude for a spin-1 composite state with a vertex symmetric under fermion-antifermion exchange, including both γμ and derivative couplings. They computed the plus component of the electromagnetic current in the Breit-frame with q+=0 using the instant-form polarization basis, and isolated zero-mode contributions by applying the pole dislocation method (taking the limit q+→0+). The analysis separated the impulse approximation into three categories of vertex coupling combinations (γμ-γμ, γμ-derivative, and derivative-derivative) to identify which terms produce non-vanishing zero-modes.

## Key numbers

* The zero-mode contribution to the matrix element J+zz[gg] from the direct γμ-γμ term is finite and non-zero in the limit δ+→0+, scaling as O[(δ+)0].
* The zero-mode contributions from the cross-term with γμ and derivative couplings vanish as O[δ+] in the limit δ+→0+.
* The zero-mode contributions from the direct term with two derivative couplings vanish as O[δ+] in the limit δ+→0+.
* The relations J+Zxx + η J+Zzz = 0 and J+Zzx + √η J+Zzz = 0 hold for the zero-mode contributions, where η = -q2/4m2v.
* In the light-front helicity basis, the zero-mode contributions satisfy I+Z11 = 0, I+Z10 = 0, I+Z1-1 = 0, and I+Z00 = (1+η)J+Zzz (non-zero).
* The Grach-Kondratyuk form factors G0GK,Z = G1GK,Z = G2GK,Z = 0 for the zero-mode contributions.

## Context

This work extends previous findings by Pacheco et al. (1997) for a simplified non-symmetrical ρ-meson vertex with point-like quarks, and by Jaus (2002) for a smeared photon vertex model, to symmetric vertex models that are more realistic for phenomenological applications. The results demonstrate that the Grach-Kondratyuk prescription (Inna84) for extracting spin-1 form factors eliminates zero-mode contributions for general symmetric vertex structures, enabling form factor calculations using only the valence component of the light-front wave function. The methods can be applied to study vector meson elastic form factors and extended to transition form factors involving spin-1 composite particles.
