# Charge balancing and the fall off of the ridge

> Two-dimensional correlation functions in $Δη-Δϕ$ for charged hadrons emitted in heavy-ion collisions are calculated in event-by-event hydrodynamics. With the Glauber model for the initial density distributions in the transverse plane and elongated density profiles in the longitudinal direction, the flow patterns in the azimuthal angle of the two-dimensional correlation function are properly reproduced. We show that the additional fall-off of the same-side ridge in the longitudinal direction can be explained as an effect of local charge conservation at a late stage of the evolution. This additional non-flow effect increases the harmonic flow coefficients for the unlike-sign particle pairs.

## Key claim

The observed fall-off of the same-side ridge in the longitudinal (pseudorapidity) direction in two-dimensional correlation functions for charged hadrons can be explained as an effect of local charge conservation at a late stage of the evolution, rather than solely by collective flow patterns. This non-flow effect increases the harmonic flow coefficients for unlike-sign particle pairs.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic calculations with event-by-event initial entropy density distributions generated from the GLISSANDO Glauber model. Particle emission at freeze-out was simulated using the THERMINATOR code, with local charge conservation implemented by emitting opposite-charge particles in pairs from the same fluid element. Two-dimensional correlation functions in Δη-Δφ were computed for like-sign and unlike-sign hadron pairs in Au-Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV in 30-40% centrality class, with freeze-out temperature T_f = 150 MeV and transverse momentum range 0.2 < p_T < 2 GeV for the correlation functions
- Elliptic and triangular flow coefficients plotted as a function of relative pseudorapidity of the pair, with transverse momentum range 0.15 < p_T < 4 GeV
- Elliptic flow coefficient v_2 shown for centrality classes 0-10% and 30-40% with and without local charge conservation

## Context

This work extends event-by-event viscous hydrodynamic calculations (Schenke 2011, Qiu 2011, Bozek 2012) that reproduce the azimuthal dependence of correlation functions via harmonic flow components. It addresses the longitudinal shape of the same-side ridge, which previous work (Gavin 2008, Gelis 2009, Dusling 2012, Gavin 2011, Kapusta 2011) had linked to the mechanism of energy deposition in the early stage. The local charge conservation mechanism explains the magnitude and range in Δη of non-flow correlations in v_n and is consistent with measured differences of flow coefficients for unlike and like-sign pairs (Agakishiev 2011). The charge splitting induced by these effects gives a large contribution to charge parity violation signals (Schlichting 2010, Hori 2012), but reproducing charge-independent correlations (Abelev 2009) requires imposing total transverse momentum conservation (Bzdak 2012).
