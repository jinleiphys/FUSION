# Quantifying fluctuation signatures of the QCD critical point using maximum entropy freeze-out

> A key question about the QCD phase diagram is whether there is a critical point somewhere on the boundary between the hadronic and quark-gluon plasma phases, and if so where. Heavy-ion collisions offer a unique opportunity to search for signatures of such a critical point by analyzing event-by-event fluctuations in particle multiplicities. To draw meaningful conclusions from experimental data, a theoretical framework is needed to link QCD thermodynamics with the particle spectra and correlations observed in detectors. The Equation of State (EoS) of QCD near a critical point can be related to the universal Gibbs free energy of the 3D Ising model using four currently unknown non-universal mapping parameters whose values are determined by the microscopic details of QCD. We utilize the maximum entropy approach to freeze-out the fluctuations in order to make estimates for factorial cumulants of proton multiplicities, assuming thermal equilibrium, for a family of EoS with a 3D Ising-like critical point, varying the microscopic inputs that determine the strength and structure of the critical features. We quantify the effect of the non-universal mapping parameters, and the distance between the critical point and the freeze-out curve, on the factorial cumulants of proton multiplicities.

## Key claim
Using a maximum entropy freeze-out procedure that directly connects thermodynamic fluctuations to particle multiplicity cumulants (eliminating ad hoc coupling parameters used in prior work), the authors show that the factorial cumulants of proton multiplicities depend sensitively on four non-universal mapping parameters between the 3D Ising model and QCD, and on the distance between the freeze-out curve and the critical point, but their calculations assume thermal equilibrium at freezeout and therefore cannot yield quantitative predictions for heavy-ion collisions.

## Method
The authors map the universal Gibbs free energy of the 3D Ising model onto the QCD phase diagram using a quadratic mapping in baryon chemical potential (following Kahangirwe (2024)) that respects charge conjugation symmetry and follows the chiral crossover curve. They then apply the maximum entropy freeze-out procedure (Pradeep (2022)) to translate equilibrium thermodynamic correlation functions of hydrodynamic densities into factorial cumulants of proton multiplicities, varying the four non-universal mapping parameters (mu_c, alpha_2, w, rho) and the temperature offset between the freeze-out curve and the critical point.

## Key numbers
- The chiral crossover temperature at mu_B=0 is T_0 = 158 MeV.
- The freeze-out curves are taken at temperature offsets of Delta T = 4, 6, and 9 MeV below the critical point.
- The 3D Ising critical exponent for the correlation length is nu = 0.63.
- The critical point is considered in the range 500 MeV < mu_B < 700 MeV, consistent with multiple theoretical approaches.
- The STAR BES II measurements probe mu_B <= 420 MeV, showing no evidence of a critical point in that region.
- Lattice QCD calculations exclude a critical point for mu_B < 450 MeV (Borsanyi (2025)).

## Context
This work extends the maximum entropy freeze-out framework (Pradeep (2022)) to incorporate a family of QCD equations of state with a 3D Ising-like critical point, building on the mapping procedure developed by Parotto (2018) and Kahangirwe (2024). It improves upon earlier studies (Stephanov (1999, 2008, 2011), Athanasiou (2010)) that relied on phenomenological couplings between a critical sigma field and hadron masses, by directly relating thermodynamic fluctuations to particle multiplicity cumulants. The framework is designed to be combined with out-of-equilibrium Hydro+ dynamics (Stephanov (2017), Rajagopal (2019), Du (2020), Pradeep (2022)) in future work to enable quantitative interpretation of experimental data from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan and future CBM at FAIR.
