# Deciphering Deconfinement in Correlations of Conserved Charges

> Diagonal and off-diagonal flavor and conserved charge susceptibilities reveal the prevalent degrees of freedom of heated strongly interacting matter. Results obtained from lattice simulations are compared with various model estimates in an effort to weed down various possible pictures of a quark gluon plasma. We argue that the vanishing of the off-diagonal quark flavor susceptibilities and its derivatives with respect to chemical potential, at temperatures above 1.5Tc, can only be understood in a picture of a gas or liquid composed of quasi-particles which carry the same quantum numbers as quarks and antiquarks. A potential new observable, blind to neutral and non-strange particles, is introduced and related via isospin symmetry to the ratio of susceptibilities of baryonic strangeness to strangeness generated in the excited matter created at RHIC.

## Key claim

The vanishing of off-diagonal quark flavor susceptibilities and their derivatives with respect to chemical potential at temperatures above 1.5 Tc can only be understood in a picture of a gas or liquid composed of quasi-particles carrying the same quantum numbers as quarks and antiquarks; bound-state pictures (mesons, diquarks, baryons) are ruled out because the fourth derivative of the off-diagonal susceptibility becomes negative above Tc, inconsistent with any bound-state interpretation.

## Method

The author compares lattice QCD results for diagonal and off-diagonal flavor susceptibilities (χ_ud, χ_us, χ_ds, χ_s) and their derivatives with respect to baryon chemical potential against predictions from a hadronic resonance gas model, a non-interacting quasi-particle gas, and a model including bound states (mesons, diquarks, baryons). For the SU(2)_f case, the off-diagonal susceptibility χ_ud and its second and fourth derivatives with respect to μ_B are decomposed into mesonic and baryonic contributions to test consistency with bound-state pictures. A new observable M = B + 2I_3 is introduced, whose covariance with strangeness S is related by isospin symmetry to the baryon-strangeness covariance C_BS, and its experimental feasibility is tested using HIJING event simulations.

## Key numbers

- The phase transition temperature is T_c ≈ 170 MeV, with an uncertainty of ±10 MeV.
- At T = 1.5 T_c, a model including bound states yields C_BS = 0.62, which differs from lattice results.
- Above T_c, the fourth derivative T ∂⁴χ_ud/∂(μ_B)⁴|_(μ=0) becomes negative, inconsistent with any bound-state interpretation.
- HIJING simulations of central Au+Au collisions at √s = 200 AGeV show that C_MS and C_BS are similar over a range of rapidity acceptance from -|y_max| to |y_max|.

## Context

This work extends lattice susceptibility studies by Gavai (2005) and Allton (2005) and contrasts with the bound-state picture of deconfined matter proposed by Shuryak (2004) and Liao (2005). The analysis rules out models with mesonic, diquark, or baryonic bound states above T_c, favoring a weakly interacting quasi-particle gas. The proposed observable M = B + 2I_3 enables experimental measurement of baryon-strangeness correlations at RHIC, circumventing detector blindness to neutral particles such as neutrons.
