# Isovector properties of the Gogny interaction

> We analyse the properties of the Gogny interaction in homogeneous matter, with special emphasis on the isovector sector. We provide analytical expressions for both the single-particle and the bulk properties of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter. We perform an extensive analysis of these properties using 11 parametrizations extracted from the literature. We find that most Gogny interactions have low values for the slope of the symmetry energy, outside the range of empirically extracted values. As a test of extreme isospin dependence, we also study the mass-radius relations implied by the different Gogny equations of state. Our results call for a more careful fitting procedure of the isovector properties of Gogny functionals.

## Key claim

Most Gogny interactions have symmetry energy slopes that are too low compared to empirically extracted values, and the paper calls for a more careful fitting procedure of the isovector properties of Gogny functionals, noting that the isovector sector is poorly constrained by the existing fitting protocols.

## Method

The authors computed analytical expressions for single-particle and bulk properties of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter within the Hartree-Fock approximation using the Gogny interaction. They analyzed 11 parametrizations (D1, D1S, D250, D260, D280, D300, D1P, D1N, D1AS, GT2, D1M) extracted from the literature, evaluating their isoscalar and isovector single-particle potentials, effective masses, and neutron star mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- At saturation density (rho = 0.16 fm^-3) and zero momentum, the isoscalar single-particle potential U_0(0) is approximately -60 MeV for all functionals
- At half saturation density (rho = 0.08 fm^-3) and zero momentum, U_0(0) is approximately -50 MeV
- The isovector single-particle potential U_1(0) at saturation density ranges from approximately 19 to 35 MeV across functionals
- For a proton impurity in neutron matter at zero momentum, the effective mass m*_p(k=0) falls within 0.48 to 0.62 m_N (excluding D1N)
- For neutrons in neutron matter at the Fermi surface, m*_n(k_F^n) ranges from approximately 0.68 to 1.26 m_N
- The isovector effective mass splitting for typical heavy nuclei (beta = 0.2) is of order (m*_n - m*_p)/m_N approximately 0.04 to 0.06
- The original D1 force has a saturation symmetry energy of S = 30.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends the analysis of isovector properties previously performed for Skyrme functionals by Stone (2003), Steiner (2005), and Dutra (2012) to the Gogny interaction family. The authors find that most Gogny parametrizations produce symmetry energy slopes outside the range of empirically extracted values from Tsang (2009, 2012), and that the neutron star mass-radius relations implied by these functionals struggle to produce sufficiently massive neutron stars. The study enables future improvements to Gogny fitting protocols by identifying specific matrix elements (particularly the isovector zero-range term C_1 and the finite-range exchange terms B_nn and B_np) that need better constraints, potentially from neutron-rich matter data and realistic many-body calculations.
