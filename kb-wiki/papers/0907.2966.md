# Eccentricity Fluctuation in Initial Conditions of Hydrodynamics

> We study effects of eccentricity fluctuations on the elliptic flow coefficient v_2 at mid-rapidity in both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at sqrt{s_{NN}}=200 GeV by using a hybrid model that combines ideal hydrodynamics for space-time evolution of the quark gluon plasma phase and a hadronic transport model for the hadronic matter. We find that the effect of eccentricity fluctuation is modest in semicentral Au+Au collisions but significantly enhances v_2 in Cu+Cu collisions.

# Eccentricity Fluctuation in Initial Conditions of Hydrodynamics

## Key claim
The effect of eccentricity fluctuations on the elliptic flow coefficient v₂ is modest in semicentral Au+Au collisions but significantly enhances v₂ in Cu+Cu collisions at √s_{NN} = 200 GeV, suggesting that simultaneous analysis of both collision systems is necessary to constrain dynamical models and extract transport properties of the produced matter.

## Method
The authors used a hybrid model combining ideal hydrodynamics for the quark-gluon plasma phase with a hadronic transport model for the hadronic matter phase. Initial entropy density profiles were generated on an event-by-event basis using Monte-Carlo versions of both the Glauber model and the factorized Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (fKLN) model, with initial time τ₀ = 0.6 fm/c. The profiles were shifted and rotated to align the apparent reaction plane with the true reaction plane before averaging over many samples, thereby incorporating eccentricity fluctuations into the smooth initial conditions.

## Key numbers
- In semicentral Au+Au collisions at 10-50% centrality, eccentricity fluctuations enhance the eccentricity parameter by 8-11% in the MC-Glauber model and 5-8% in the MC-KLN model.
- At the most central bin (0-5% centrality), the enhancement factor ε_part/ε_RP reaches 1.83 in the MC-Glauber model and 1.53 in the MC-KLN model.
- The elliptic flow v₂ was calculated for charged particles at mid-rapidity (|η| < 1) in both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collisions at √s_{NN} = 200 GeV.
- In Cu+Cu collisions, v₂ is largely enhanced due to fluctuation effects, with the MC-KLN model reasonably reproducing the PHOBOS data when fluctuations are included.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of elliptic flow at RHIC, where ideal hydrodynamics showed reasonable agreement with data only for specific combinations of dynamical modeling (Hirano and Gyulassy, 2005). The authors incorporate initial fluctuation effects previously identified as important (Miller and Snellings, 2003) using Monte-Carlo versions of the Glauber model (Miller et al., 2007) and the fKLN model (Drescher et al., 2006). The results enable future hydrodynamic studies to simultaneously constrain models using both Au+Au and Cu+Cu collision data, and suggest that viscous effects in the QGP phase may need to be included to fully reproduce the data, particularly in Au+Au collisions with MC-KLN initial conditions.
