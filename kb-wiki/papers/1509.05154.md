# Phase space factors and half-life predictions for Majoron emitting $β^-β^-$ decay

> A complete calculation of phase space factors (PSF) for Majoron emitting $0νβ^-β^-$ decay modes is presented. The calculation makes use of exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening and includes life-times, single electron spectra, summed electron spectra, and angular electron correlations. Combining these results with recent interacting boson nuclear matrix elements (NME) we make half-life predictions for the the ordinary Majoron decay (spectral index $n$=1). Furthermore, comparing theoretical predictions with the obtained experimental lower bounds for this decay mode we are able to set limits on the effective Majoron-neutrino coupling constant $\langle g_{ee}^M\rangle$.

# Phase space factors and half-life predictions for Majoron emitting β⁻β⁻ decay

## Key claim
The paper presents a complete calculation of phase space factors for neutrinoless double-beta decay with Majoron emission using exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening, but the half-life predictions for ordinary Majoron decay (spectral index n=1) depend on the assumed effective Majoron-neutrino coupling constant ⟨g_{ee}^M⟩ = 10⁻⁴ and the axial vector coupling constant g_A = 1.269, with the note that if g_A is renormalized to g_{A,eff}, the half-lives and coupling limits scale accordingly.

## Method
The authors computed phase space factors G_{mχ₀n}^{(0)} and G_{mχ₀n}^{(1)} for 20 candidate nuclei by integrating the differential decay rate using exact Dirac electron wave functions that include finite nuclear size and electron screening effects, following the formulation of Doi, Kotani, and Takasugi. They then combined the n=1 phase space factors with interacting boson model (IBM-2) nuclear matrix elements from Bar15 to predict half-lives for ordinary Majoron decay, and compared these predictions with experimental lower bounds to set limits on the effective Majoron-neutrino coupling constant.

## Key numbers
- Phase space factor G_{1χ₀1}^{(0)} for ⁴⁸Ca: 1540 × 10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹
- Phase space factor G_{1χ₀1}^{(0)} for ¹³⁶Xe: 409 × 10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹
- Phase space factor G_{1χ₀1}^{(0)} for ¹⁵⁰Nd: 3100 × 10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹
- Predicted half-life for ¹⁵⁰Nd ordinary Majoron decay: 1.74 × 10²¹ yr (with ⟨g_{ee}^M⟩ = 10⁻⁴)
- Predicted half-life for ¹⁰⁰Mo ordinary Majoron decay: 3.62 × 10²¹ yr (with ⟨g_{ee}^M⟩ = 10⁻⁴)
- Most stringent limit on effective Majoron-neutrino coupling constant: ⟨g_{ee}^M⟩ < 6.2 × 10⁻⁶ eV from ¹³⁶Xe (KamLAND-Zen experiment, Gan12)
- Second most stringent limit on ⟨g_{ee}^M⟩: < 9.2 × 10⁻⁶ eV from ¹³⁶Xe (EXO-200 experiment, Alb14)
- Limit on ⟨g_{ee}^M⟩ from ⁷⁶Ge: < 7.9 × 10⁻⁵ eV (experimental limit > 6.4 × 10²² yr from Kla01)
- Limit on ⟨g_{ee}^M⟩ from ¹⁰⁰Mo: < 3.0 × 10⁻⁵ eV (experimental limit > 3.9 × 10²² yr from Arn14)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous systematic calculations of phase space factors for various double-beta decay modes (Kotila 2012, Kotila 2014) and nuclear matrix elements (Barea 2013, Bar15) to the specific case of Majoron-emitting neutrinoless double-beta decay. The results improve upon earlier approximate calculations by Doi (1988), Tomoda (1991), Suhonen (1993), and Gunther (1996) by using exact Dirac wave functions with finite nuclear size and electron screening, with the authors noting that their results agree within 6% with the calculation by Alb14 for ¹³⁶Xe. The predictions enable experimental collaborations to compare their lower limits on half-lives with theoretical expectations and extract constraints on the effective Majoron-neutrino coupling constant, with the most stringent limits currently coming from ¹³⁶Xe experiments reaching the order of 10⁻⁶ eV.
