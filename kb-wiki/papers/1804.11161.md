# Vector mesons as dynamical resonances in the Bethe-Salpeter framework

> We present the first dynamical calculation of the $ρ$-meson as a resonance in the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework by including explicit two-pion exchange in addition to the t-channel one-gluon exchange of rainbow-ladder in the interaction kernel. The width is determined from the imaginary part of the resonance pole and is generated by the singularity structure of the integrand, treated by means of suitable path deformations. We find the resonant mass and width to be $640$ MeV and $100$ MeV respectively, corresponding to a coupling constant $g_{ρππ}=5.7$; repulsive corrections beyond rainbow-ladder are known to bring these in alignment with experiment. Furthermore we present the dependence of these parameters on the pion mass and compare with results from lattice QCD.

# Vector mesons as dynamical resonances in the Bethe-Salpeter framework

## Key claim

The first dynamical calculation of the ρ-meson as a resonance in the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter framework, including explicit two-pion exchange, yields a resonant mass of 640 MeV and width of 100 MeV; however, repulsive corrections beyond rainbow-ladder are known to be necessary to bring these values into alignment with experimental results.

## Method

The quark Dyson-Schwinger equation and meson Bethe-Salpeter equation were solved with an interaction kernel combining rainbow-ladder t-channel one-gluon exchange with s- and u-channel two-pion exchange, where the pion is treated as a (pseudo-)Goldstone boson using the chiral-limit approximation Γ_π = γ_5 B(k²)/f_π. The resonance pole was determined from the complex eigenvalue of the homogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation and independently from analytic continuation of the inhomogeneous Bethe-Salpeter equation to the second Riemann sheet, with the singularity structure of the integrand handled by path deformation.

## Key numbers

- Resonant mass m_ρ = 640 MeV and width Γ_ρ = 100 MeV for physical pion mass m_π = 140 MeV
- Strong coupling constant g_{ρππ} = 5.7, extracted from the narrow-width approximation formula Γ_R = (p³/M_R²)(g_{ρππ}²/6π) with p = √(M_R²/4 - m_π²)
- The resonant mass is shifted downward by 100 MeV compared to rainbow-ladder results (which give m_ρ = 740 MeV)
- The Maris-Tandy interaction parameter was set to ω = 0.4 GeV
- The leptonic decay constant f_π = 92.4 MeV was used in the pion vertex approximation

## Context

This work extends the hadronic unquenching approach of Fischer (2007, 2008) and Watson (2004) by incorporating two-pion exchange to generate dynamical resonance behavior, going beyond previous DSE studies such as Jarecke (2002) and Mader (2011) that worked in the impulse approximation. The results underestimate experimental values (m_ρ = 780 MeV, Γ_ρ = 150 MeV, g_{ρππ} = 6.0), consistent with the expectation from Fischer (2009) that repulsive corrections beyond rainbow-ladder will push both mass and width upward. The framework enables future calculations of other resonances, notably the f_0(500) in the tetraquark picture and baryon resonances such as the Δ.
