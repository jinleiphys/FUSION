# Hydrodynamics at large baryon densities: Understanding proton vs. anti-proton v_2 and other puzzles

> We study the importance of the initial state, baryon stopping and baryon number transport for the dynamical evolution of a strongly interacting system produced in heavy ion collisions. We employ a hybrid model, which combines the fluid dynamical evolution of the fireball with a transport treatment for the initial state and the final hadronic phase. We present results for collisions at beam energies from sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7 to 200 GeV. We study various observables such as the centrality dependent freeze out parameters, the non-monotonic behavior of effective slope parameter parameter with particle mass as well as the apparent difference in particle and anti-particle elliptic flow. Our results are in reasonable agreement with the available data. We find that the propagation of the baryon-number current in the hydrodynamic evolution as well as the transport treatment of the hadronic phase are essential for reproducing the experimental data.

## Key claim

The observed difference between proton and anti-proton elliptic flow at low beam energies (below √s_{NN} ≈ 60 GeV) can be explained without invoking mean-field potentials, simply because the local baryon chemical potential causes anti-protons to be produced preferentially from the hot, central region of the fireball where flow velocity is smaller, while protons are more abundant in the colder, faster-flowing peripheral region.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD hybrid model, which combines a hadronic transport description for the initial non-equilibrium stage and final hadronic phase with a (3+1)-dimensional ideal fluid dynamical evolution for the hot and dense intermediate stage. The initial state was generated by UrQMD in cascade mode, with energy and baryon number mapped onto a spatial grid at the time when the two Lorentz-contracted nuclei have passed through each other. The hydrodynamic evolution solved conservation of energy-momentum and baryon current using the SHASTA algorithm with a hadron resonance gas equation of state, and particles were converted back to transport via the Cooper-Frye prescription on an isochronous hypersurface when the energy density dropped below approximately 730 MeV/fm³.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: √s_{NN} = 7.7 to 200 GeV
- Spatial grid cell size: δx = 0.2 fm
- Transition energy density threshold: approximately 730 MeV/fm³
- Gaussian width for mapping hadrons to grid: σ = 1 fm
- For top SPS energy (√s_{NN} = 17.3 GeV), thermal model values cited: μ_B = 266 ± 5 MeV, μ_S = 71.1 MeV, μ_I = -5.0 MeV
- Pions and protons are enhanced by a factor of three at low transverse momenta due to resonance decays
- Exponential fits to spectra were performed over the range 1 < m_⊥ - m_0 < 2 GeV
- The difference in elliptic flow between particles and anti-particles becomes significant below √s_{NN} < 60 GeV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model approach of Petersen (2008) and Steinheimer (2009) to address the puzzle of particle vs. anti-particle elliptic flow at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies. It contradicts the interpretation of Xu (2012) that mean-field nuclear potentials are required to explain the proton/anti-proton v₂ difference. The results enable future studies using different freeze-out hypersurface definitions, as a new hypersurface finder by Huovinen (2012) can now be applied to the full 3+1D calculation including local density fluctuations.
