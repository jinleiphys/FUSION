# Formalism of hydrodynamics with spin degrees of freedom

> In this article we review the perfect-fluid hydrodynamics with spin framework proposed recently. This framework generalises the standard relativistic hydrodynamics framework to include spin degrees of freedom and provides a natural method to describe the spin polarization evolution of massive spin 1/2 particles. This formalism is based on the GLW (de Groot - van Leeuwen - van Weert) energy-momentum tensor and spin tensor. We show here using Bjorken model that how this spin hydrodynamics framework may be used for the determination of the observables which describes the particle polarization measured in the experiment.

## Key claim

This paper reviews a perfect-fluid hydrodynamics framework that includes spin degrees of freedom, based on the GLW (de Groot - van Leeuwen - van Weert) energy-momentum and spin tensors, and demonstrates its application to the Bjorken model under the assumption of small spin polarization (|omega_mu_nu| less than 1).

## Method

The formalism is built on conservation laws for charge, energy-momentum, and angular momentum, using the GLW energy-momentum tensor and spin tensor for spin-1/2 particles. The spin polarization tensor is decomposed into four-vectors orthogonal to the fluid flow, and for a boost-invariant, transversely homogeneous Bjorken flow, the scalar coefficients describing spin polarization are projected onto a basis of four-vectors to obtain independent equations of motion. The mean spin polarization per particle at freeze-out is then calculated from the evolved spin polarization tensor using the Pauli-Lubanski vector.

## Key numbers

- Initial conditions used: baryon chemical potential mu_0 = 800 MeV, temperature T_0 = 155 MeV, with initial spin coefficients C_kappa,0 = (0,0,0) and C_omega,0 = (0,0.1,0) for mid-rapidity.
- The longitudinal component of the polarization vector in the particle rest frame, <pi*_z>, is zero at midrapidity.
- The <pi*_y> component is negative, reflecting the initial system's spin angular momentum.
- The <pi*_x> component shows a quadrupole structure.

## Context

This work extends the standard relativistic hydrodynamics framework to include spin degrees of freedom, motivated by STAR Collaboration measurements of Lambda hyperon spin polarization (STAR 2017, Adam 2018, Adam 2019). It builds on the approach of Florkowski (2017, 2018) and uses the Bjorken model (Bjorken 1982, Rezzolla 2013) to demonstrate the formalism. The results do not reproduce the quadrupole structure of longitudinal polarization observed in experiments due to the assumed symmetries, but the framework enables future determination of particle spin polarization at freeze-out from initial polarization conditions.
