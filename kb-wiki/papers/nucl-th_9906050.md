# Parity violation through color superconductivity

> We give a pedagogical discussion of how color superconductivity can produce parity violation in cold quark matter at very high densities.

## Key claim

Color superconductivity can spontaneously break parity in cold quark matter at very high densities, but only if the quark chemical potential is sufficiently large that instanton effects and current quark masses become negligible; otherwise, the true ground state always remains parity-even.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for two-flavor, massless quarks with a spin-zero color-antitriplet condensate, including both a parity-even (\(J^P=0^+\)) and a parity-odd (\(J^P=0^-\)) condensate. They then add explicit symmetry-breaking terms from instantons (proportional to the instanton density) and from current quark masses, and analyze how these terms favor the parity-even condensate over the parity-odd one as a function of quark chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The instanton density and thus the coefficient \(c\) vanish like \(\sim \mu^{-29/3}\) for two flavors as the quark chemical potential \(\mu \rightarrow \infty\).
- The pseudo-Goldstone boson mass from the explicit breaking term is \(m \sim 10\) MeV, taking the up or down quark mass and assuming the proportionality constant is of order 1.
- For chiral symmetry breaking, the characteristic scale is \(1/m_\pi \sim 1.4\) fm; for the color-superconducting case, the region over which a parity-odd condensate forms is \(1/m \sim 20\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the seminal treatment of color superconductivity by Bailin and Love, and is noted by Alford, Rajagopal, and Wilczek, and explicitly discussed by Evans, Hsu, and Schwetz. The authors build on the instanton analysis of Berges and Rajagopal to show that at sufficiently high densities, parity violation becomes possible, and they propose searching for parity-odd color-superconducting condensates in heavy-ion collisions on an event-by-event basis, using a global parity-odd observable discussed in a separate paper.
