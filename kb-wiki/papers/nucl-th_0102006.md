# On radiative muon capture in hydrogen

> We analyze photon spectra in the radiative muon capture in hydrogen using amplitudes derived from chiral Lagrangians. The model can describe the high energy part of the photon spectra, measured recently at TRIUMF, reasonably well.

## Key claim

The chiral Lagrangian model can explain approximately twice as much of the discrepancy between the PCAC-predicted value of the induced pseudoscalar form factor \(g_P\) and the value extracted from the TRIUMF experiment as the standard approach, and by varying the off-shell parameters of the \(\pi N\Delta\) and \(\gamma N\Delta\) vertices independently the discrepancy can be removed completely.

## Method

The authors performed a non-relativistic reduction of radiative muon capture amplitudes derived from a chiral Lagrangian of the \(N\pi\rho\omega a_1\) system, obtaining an effective Hamiltonian with terms up to \(\mathcal{O}(1/M^2)\). They also included \(\Delta(1232)\) isobar excitation effects using chiral Lagrangians with off-shell parameters \(X, Y, Z\) constrained by pion photoproduction data, and estimated contributions from an anomalous Lagrangian.

## Key numbers

- The PCAC-predicted value of the induced pseudoscalar form factor for ordinary muon capture on the proton is \(g_P^{PCAC}(p) = -8.55\) (using the chiral model with \(m_{a_1}\)) or \(g_P^{PCAC}(p) = -8.28\) (using the dipole form with \(m_A = 1.077 \pm 0.039\) GeV).
- The ordinary muon capture value is \(g_P^{OMC}(p) = 6.87\,g_A = -8.71\) at \(q_1^2 = 0.877\,m_\mu^2\).
- The \(\Delta\) excitation enhances the photon spectrum by approximately 7–15% relative to calculations without the \(\Delta\) isobar.
- The hard pion correction enhances the photon spectra by 2–4%.
- The triplet capture rate agrees with the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculation of Bernard, Hemmert, and Meißner to within 10%, with half of that discrepancy attributed to an incorrect phase-space integration in that work.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic formalism of Beder and Fearing (1992) for radiative muon capture on the proton by providing a consistent non-relativistic reduction from chiral Lagrangians, addressing the "\(g_P\) puzzle" where the TRIUMF experiment extracted a value of \(g_P\) enhanced by about 50% over the PCAC prediction. The approach improves upon the Rood-Tolhoek Hamiltonian by including additional \(\mathcal{O}(1/M)\) and \(\mathcal{O}(1/M^2)\) terms and by treating the \(\Delta\) isobar with off-shell parameters from pion photoproduction analyses (Davidson, Mukhopadhyay, and Wittman, 1986; Davidson and Workman, 1991). The results enable more reliable calculations for the upcoming TRIUMF experiment on radiative muon capture in helium-3.
