# Crystalline chiral condensates as a component of compact stars

> We investigate the influence of spatially inhomogeneous chiral symmetry-breaking condensates in a magnetic field background on the equation of state for compact stellar objects. After building a hybrid star composed of nuclear and quark matter using the Maxwell construction, we find, by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for stellar equilibrium, that our equation of state supports stars with masses around 2 $M_\odot$ for values of the magnetic field that are in accordance with those inferred from magnetar data. The inclusion of a weak vector interaction term in the quark part allows one to reach 2 solar masses for relatively small central magnetic fields, making this composition a viable possibility for describing the internal degrees of freedom of this class of astrophysical objects.

## Key claim
The paper claims that hybrid stars with a crystalline (spatially inhomogeneous) chiral condensate quark matter core can reach masses around 2 solar masses, but only when a weak vector interaction term is included in the quark model and for magnetic field values consistent with magnetar data; without the vector term, the equation of state is too soft to support such high masses.

## Method
The authors built hybrid star models using the Maxwell construction to match a nuclear matter equation of state (nonlinear Walecka model with GM1 parametrization, including hyperons) to a quark matter equation of state from a Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a plane-wave "chiral density wave" ansatz for spatially inhomogeneous chiral condensates. They imposed electrical neutrality and beta-equilibrium, included a medium-dependent magnetic field parametrized as a function of baryon chemical potential, and solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations for stellar equilibrium to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers
- Maximum stellar masses around 2 solar masses (2 M_sun) are supported for magnetic field values in accordance with those inferred from magnetar data (surface fields 10^14-10^15 G, central fields up to ~10^18 G).
- The quark matter region of interest for star applications corresponds to baryon chemical potentials between 330 MeV and 550 MeV.
- The nuclear matter parameters reproduce a binding energy of -16.3 MeV and symmetry energy coefficient of 32.5 MeV at saturation, with compression modulus K=300 MeV and effective baryon mass 0.7 times the bare baryon mass.
- The magnetic field profile uses a surface value H_S and a core value H_C with parameters kappa and gamma determining the rise; central field estimates from other work give a range of 0.1-4.2 x 10^18 G for nuclear matter stars.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of inhomogeneous chiral condensates in magnetic fields (Klimenko 2010, Tatsumi 2014) to astrophysical applications, and addresses the longstanding problem that many quark matter equations of state from NJL models produce maximum masses below 2 solar masses (Bordbar, and Fig. 8 in mag3). The results suggest that crystalline chiral condensates with vector interactions provide a viable alternative to purely hadronic or homogeneous quark matter cores for explaining massive compact stars, though the authors note that the exact inner magnetic field profile remains uncertain and that color superconductivity may compete at very high densities.
