# Parton Distribution Functions from Large Momentum Expansion of Current-Current Correlators

> The universality of the large momentum expansion allows computing parton distribution functions (PDFs) starting from any Euclidean correlator with appropriate large momentum Fourier Components. Here we consider current-current correlators which have been used in short-distance expansion to obtain moments of PDFs. The advantage of such correlators is that they have simple renormalization properties and do not have linear power divergences as in quasi-PDF. However, in lattice calculations, four-point functions are needed. Here we present an expansion formula with current-current correlators up to the next-to-leading order, and preliminary numerical calculations with four-point functions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that parton distribution functions (PDFs) can be extracted from current-current correlators using the large momentum expansion of LaMET, avoiding the renormalon ambiguities and linear power divergences present in quasi-PDF calculations, but the method currently requires four-point functions on the lattice and the presented results are a proof of concept with limited precision and hadron momentum.

## Method

The authors compute forward matrix elements of two vector (VV) and axial-vector (AA) currents in the proton on a Euclidean lattice, using the CLS H102 ensemble with \(n_f=2+1\) Wilson-Clover fermions at a pion mass of 355 MeV. They extract the transverse-projected correlator \(W^{\perp\perp}(z,P)\) from four-point correlation functions, perform a Fourier transform to obtain the momentum-space VV distribution \(\tilde{h}(y,P_z)\), and apply a one-loop perturbative matching kernel in the \(\overline{\text{MS}}\) scheme to obtain the isovector PDF \(f_{u-d}(x,\mu)\). The analysis uses a tree-level improvement factor to correct discretization artifacts and an extrapolation ansatz for large \(|z|\) motivated by asymptotic behavior.

## Key numbers

- Maximal proton momentum: \(|\vec{P}|_{\mathrm{max}} = 1.57~\mathrm{GeV}\) (from \(\sqrt{12}\cdot 2\pi/L\) with \(L=32\))
- Lattice spacing: \(a = 0.085~\mathrm{fm}\) on a \(32^3 \times 96\) lattice
- Pion mass: \(m_\pi = 355~\mathrm{MeV}\)
- Largest momentum used in analysis: \(|\vec{P}| = 1.523~\mathrm{GeV}\)
- Perturbative \(z=0\) value in \(\overline{\mathrm{MS}}\): \(W^{\perp\perp}(0,P) = 1 - \frac{\alpha_s C_F}{4\pi}\)
- Real part of \(W^{\perp\perp}_{u-d}\) becomes consistent with zero at \(z \approx 0.42~\mathrm{fm}\)
- Extrapolation starts at \(z = 0.51~\mathrm{fm}\) for real part and \(z = 0.75~\mathrm{fm}\) for imaginary part
- PDF result shown at scale \(\mu = 2~\mathrm{GeV}\)

## Context

This work extends the LaMET framework (Ji 2013, Ji 2014) to current-current correlators, which belong to the same universality class as quasi-PDFs (Ji 2020) but avoid the Wilson-line renormalization issues that challenge quasi-PDF calculations (Chen 2016, Green 2017, Ji 2017, Ishikawa 2017). It differs from short-distance factorization approaches using the same correlators (Chambers 2017, Bali 2018, Liang 2019, Sufian 2020, Bali 2021, Zimmermann 2024) by enabling direct extraction of \(x\)-dependent PDFs through forward matching rather than moment extraction or parametrized fits. The next step is to repeat the calculation at higher momenta (\(|\vec{P}| > 2~\mathrm{GeV}\)) with improved statistics and kinematically enhanced interpolators (Zhang 2025).
