# Gamow-Teller strength distributions for nuclei in pre-supernova stellar cores

> Electron-capture and $β$-decay of nuclei in the core of massive stars play an important role in the stages leading to a type II supernova explosion. Nuclei in the f-p shell are particularly important for these reactions in the post Silicon-burning stage of a presupernova star. In this paper, we characterise the energy distribution of the Gamow-Teller Giant Resonance (GTGR) for mid-fp-shell nuclei in terms of a few shape parameters, using data obtained from high energy, forward scattering (p,n) and (n,p) reactions. The energy of the GTGR centroid $E_{GT}$ is further generalised as function of nuclear properties like mass number, isospin and other shell model properties of the nucleus. Since a large fraction of the GT strength lies in the GTGR region, and the GTGR is accessible for weak transitions taking place at energies relevant to the cores of presupernova and collapsing stars, our results are relevant to the study of important $e^-$-capture and $β$-decay rates of arbitrary, neutron-rich, f-p shell nuclei in stellar cores. Using the observed GTGR and Isobaric Analog States (IAS) energy systematics we compare the coupling coefficients in the Bohr-Mottelson two particle interaction Hamiltonian for different regions of the Isotope Table.

## Key claim

The paper provides empirical formulas for predicting the Gamow-Teller Giant Resonance (GTGR) centroid energies for arbitrary mid-fp-shell nuclei, based on fits to experimental charge-exchange reaction data, with the caveat that these formulas are valid only for nuclei with N > Z and are not applicable near the beginning of the fp-shell or for nuclei occupying the g9/2 shell.

## Method

The authors used experimental data from high-energy forward-scattering (p,n) and (n,p) charge-exchange reactions on mid-fp-shell nuclei to extract Gamow-Teller strength distributions. They fitted these distributions to an Edgeworth expansion (skewed Gaussian) to obtain shape parameters including the energy centroid E_GT, effective half-width sigma, and skewness factor gamma_1. The experimental centroids were then fitted to empirical relations involving mass number A, isospin asymmetry (N-Z)/A, and a pairing term for odd-A nuclei, using linear combinations of spin-orbit and Lane potential terms.

## Key numbers

- The empirical relation for GT- centroid: E_GT- - E_IAS = 44.16 A^(-1/3) - 76.1 (N-Z)/A, with an rms deviation of 0.43 MeV from experimental values
- The empirical relation for GT+ centroid: E_GT+ = 13.10 A^(-1/3) - 11.28 (N-Z)/A + 12 A^(-1/2) delta_{A_odd}, with an rms deviation of 0.31 MeV from experimental values
- GT- centroids are located roughly between 9 to 11 MeV for the nuclei studied
- The observed total Gamow-Teller strength in the GT- direction is approximately 50% of the theoretical 3(N-Z) sum rule value, varying between 45% to 63% for the nuclei studied
- The total GT+ strength in mid-fp-shell nuclei can be fitted by B(GT+) = 0.0429 Z_val (20 - N_val), where Z_val and N_val are valence proton and neutron numbers

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts by Fuller, Fowler and Newman (1982) and Klapdor (1976) to characterize Gamow-Teller strength distributions for astrophysical rate calculations, but uses experimental charge-exchange data rather than purely theoretical single-particle methods. The empirical formulas enable calculation of electron-capture and beta-decay rates for arbitrary neutron-rich f-p shell nuclei in pre-supernova stellar cores, where temperatures around 0.5 MeV and densities near 10^10 g/cm^3 make the GTGR region the dominant contributor to weak interaction rates. The results are compared with the M1 method and the FFN method, and the authors find their empirical relations give values closer to experimental centroids than these earlier theoretical approaches.
