# Spin-polarized neutron matter at different orders of chiral effective field theory

> Spin-polarized neutron matter is studied using chiral two- and three-body forces. We focus, in particular, on predictions of the energy per particle in ferromagnetic neutron matter at different orders of chiral effective field theory and for different choices of the resolution scale. We discuss the convergence pattern of the predictions and their cutoff dependence. We explore to which extent fully polarized neutron matter behaves (nearly) like a free Fermi gas. We also consider the more general case of partial polarization in neutron matter as well as the presence of a small proton fraction. In other words, in our calculations, we vary both spin and isospin asymmetries. Confirming the findings of other microscopic calculations performed with different approaches, we report no evidence for a transition to a polarized phase of neutron matter5

## Key claim

The authors report no evidence for a transition to a polarized (ferromagnetic) phase of neutron matter, confirming findings from other microscopic calculations, but note that this conclusion must be assessed in the context of chiral effective field theory (EFT) uncertainties and that the validity of such a conclusion is limited to densities where chiral perturbation theory is applicable (up to a few times normal density).

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (particle-particle ladder) calculations of the energy per particle in spin-polarized neutron matter using chiral two-nucleon potentials at NLO, N²LO, and N³LO, combined with the leading (N²LO) chiral three-nucleon force (3NF) included as a density-dependent effective two-nucleon interaction. They varied the momentum-space cutoff between 450 MeV and 600 MeV to assess regulator dependence and computed results for fully polarized neutron matter (β_n = 1), partially polarized neutron matter (β_n = 0.5), and neutron-rich matter with a small proton fraction (α = 0.8, corresponding to 10% protons).

## Key numbers

- At N³LO with cutoff Λ = 500 MeV, the energy per neutron in fully polarized neutron matter is close to the free Fermi gas energy up to saturation density (ρ₀ ≈ 0.16 fm⁻³), in good agreement with results from Krueger et al. (2014) that included three- and four-nucleon forces at N³LO.
- The contribution from the N³LO three-nucleon force in neutron matter was found to be about -0.5 MeV at normal density for the potentials considered.
- For partially polarized neutron matter with β_n = 0.5 (75% of neutrons polarized in one direction, 25% in the opposite direction), the energy per neutron shows considerably less repulsion than the fully polarized case.
- With a proton fraction of 10% (α = 0.8), no crossing of the energy bands for fully polarized versus unpolarized neutrons was observed, indicating no phase transition.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous order-by-order chiral EFT calculations of nuclear and neutron matter (Sammarruca et al., 2015) to include spin polarization, and is the first application of chiral forces to systems with both spin and isospin asymmetries. The results confirm earlier microscopic calculations using Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock with meson-theoretic potentials (Sammarruca, 2011) and conventional Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory, which also found no evidence for ferromagnetic transitions, in contrast to predictions from Skyrme forces (such as SLy4 and SLy5) and relativistic Hartree-Fock models with effective meson-nucleon Lagrangians. The work enables systematic uncertainty quantification for polarized neutron matter equations of state relevant to neutron star physics, particularly for understanding the possibility of strong magnetic fields in rotating neutron stars and their impact on neutrino cooling.
