# Thermal fluctuations of gauge fields and first order phase transitions in color superconductivity

> We study the effects of thermal fluctuations of gluons and the diquark pairing field on the superconducting-to-normal state phase transition in a three-flavor color superconductor, using the Ginzburg-Landau free energy. At high baryon densities, where the system is a type I superconductor, gluonic fluctuations, which dominate over diquark fluctuations, induce a cubic term in the Ginzburg-Landau free energy, as well as large corrections to quadratic and quartic terms of the order parameter. The cubic term leads to a relatively strong first order transition, in contrast with the very weak first order transitions in metallic type I superconductors. The strength of the first order transition decreases with increasing baryon density. In addition gluonic fluctuations lower the critical temperature of the first order transition. We derive explicit formulas for the critical temperature and the discontinuity of the order parameter at the critical point. The validity of the first order transition obtained in the one-loop approximation is also examined by estimating the size of the critical region.

## Key claim

Thermal fluctuations of gluons, which dominate over diquark fluctuations in type I color superconductors at high baryon density, induce a cubic term in the Ginzburg-Landau free energy that leads to a relatively strong first order phase transition; however, the strength of this first order transition decreases with increasing baryon density, contrary to earlier claims by Bailin and Love (1984) for two-flavor color superconductors.

## Method

The authors used a Ginzburg-Landau (GL) free energy for a three-flavor color superconductor with color-flavor locking (CFL) and isoscalar (IS) orderings, coupling the diquark order parameter to SU(3)_c gluon gauge fields. They computed the one-loop correction to the free energy from thermal gluon fluctuations in the Coulomb gauge, expanding the momentum integral in powers of the Meissner mass over the ultraviolet cutoff (taken as T_c), and derived the renormalized quadratic, cubic, and quartic terms in the order parameter. The critical temperature of the first order transition and the discontinuity of the order parameter were obtained by solving for degenerate minima of the effective free energy.

## Key numbers

* The Ginzburg-Landau parameter κ (ratio of scalar to gauge fluctuation masses) in weak coupling scales as κ² ∼ (10³/α_s)(T_c/μ)², which is considerably smaller than unity at high baryon density, confirming type I behavior.
* The critical region for gauge fluctuations is defined by t ≫ ε_A ≡ 10 α_s (T_c/μ)²; for scalar fluctuations, t ≫ ε_d ≡ 10² (T_c/μ)⁴, with ε_A ≫ ε_d at high density.
* The renormalized critical temperature T'_c (where the quadratic term vanishes) is given by T'_c/T_c ≃ (1 + 9/(2π²p))⁻¹, and the first order critical temperature T_c* satisfies T_c*/T'_c ≃ 1 + 1/(4p), where p ≡ 3α₀r/q² ≃ 81π/(14 ζ(3) α_s).
* The discontinuity of the order parameter at the critical point is d* ≃ π^(5/2) (6/(7 ζ(3)))¹/² (T_c²)/(μ √α_s).

## Context

This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau analysis of color superconductivity by Iida and Baym (2001-2002) beyond mean-field theory, incorporating thermal fluctuations of gluons and diquark fields. It contradicts the earlier conclusion of Bailin and Love (1984) for two-flavor color superconductors, who predicted the first order transition strengthens with increasing density; the present authors find the opposite trend when the quartic correction from gluon fluctuations is consistently included. The results enable quantitative predictions for the phase transition temperature and condensate jump in dense quark matter, relevant for neutron star interiors, and open the question of the transition order in type II color superconductors at lower densities, where renormalization group methods (Pisarski and Rischke, 2000; Paterson, 1981) may be required.
