# Neutron stars with Bogoliubov quark-meson coupling model

> A quark-meson coupling model based on the quark model proposed by Bogoliubov for the description of the quark dynamics is developed and applied to the description of neutron stars. Starting from a su(3) symmetry approach, it is shown that this symmetry has to be broken in order to satisfy the constraints set by the hypernuclei and by neutron stars. The model is able to describe observations such as two solar mass stars or the radius of canonical neutron stars within the uncertainties presently accepted. If the optical potentials for $Λ$ and $Ξ$ hyperons in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation obtained from laboratory measurements of hypernuclei properties are imposed the model predicts no strangeness inside neutron stars.

## Key claim

The Bogoliubov quark-meson coupling model can describe two-solar-mass neutron stars and canonical-star radii within accepted uncertainties, but only if SU(3) symmetry is broken by making the ω-meson coupling to the s-quark more repulsive than to u,d quarks; when the optical potentials for Λ and Ξ hyperons in symmetric nuclear matter at saturation are constrained to laboratory hypernuclei measurements, the model predicts no strangeness inside neutron stars.

## Method

The authors develop a quark-meson coupling model based on Bogoliubov's independent quark model, where baryons are composed of quarks bound by a linearly raising potential (string tension κ). The quark dynamics are described by a Dirac Hamiltonian with a scalar confining potential, and the baryon masses are obtained from the sum of constituent quark masses, with the s-quark mass including a current-mass contribution. Hadronic matter is described by introducing σ, ω, and b₃ mesons; the ω-meson is given a flavor structure that allows its coupling to the s-quark to differ from that to u,d quarks by a factor (1+δ), breaking SU(3) symmetry. The model parameters are fixed to nuclear matter properties at saturation, and the parameter δ is adjusted to reproduce the Λ-hyperon optical potential while maintaining two-solar-mass stars.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum constituent quark masses: 313 MeV for u,d quarks (with a=0 and κ=37106.93 MeV²), 504 MeV for s quarks (with a_s=-1.2455)
- Nuclear matter at saturation: binding energy -15.7 MeV, incompressibility K=315.0 MeV, saturation density ρ_B=0.145 fm⁻³, symmetry energy a₄=29 MeV, symmetry energy slope L=79.45 MeV, nucleon effective mass M*/M=0.773
- For δ=0: maximum neutron star mass M_max=1.97 M_⊙, radius R=10.91 km, central baryonic density ρ^c=7.674 ρ₀
- For δ=0.25: maximum mass M_max=2.21 M_⊙, radius R=11.84 km, central density ρ^c=6.255 ρ₀, U_Λ(ρ₀)=-36.49 MeV, U_Ξ(ρ₀)=-16.29 MeV
- Canonical 1.4 M_⊙ star radius R_1.4≈13.75 km for all δ≥0.1
- Tidal deformability Λ_1.4=936-954 (depending on hyperon content)
- For δ=0: hyperon onset at ρ=2ρ₀; for δ=0.2: Λ onset at ~4ρ₀, Ξ⁻ onset above 8ρ₀

## Context

This work extends the Bogoliubov-QMC model previously applied to non-strange matter (Bohr, 2010) and strange matter (Panda, 2012) by explicitly including s-quark couplings to vector mesons from a SU(3) symmetry approach. It contrasts with other QMC models: the MIT-bag-based QMC model (Guichon, 1996; Panda, 2012) produces softer equations of state with hyperon onset below 3ρ₀ and maximum masses ~1.8 M_⊙; the improved QMC model with Hartree-Fock and polarizability (Stone, 2007, 2018) predicts hyperon onset near 4ρ₀ and maximum masses 1.9-2.0 M_⊙; the modified QMC model (Barik, 2013) requires quark mass above 200 MeV to reach >1.9 M_⊙ with hyperons. The conclusion that no hyperons appear inside two-solar-mass stars when experimental hyperon potentials are imposed agrees with the auxiliary field diffusion Monte Carlo study (Pederiva, 2015) showing that three-body hyperon-nucleon interactions push hyperon onset above 0.56 fm⁻³. The model enables further study of how non-linear ω-ρ terms could soften the symmetry energy and reduce the tidal deformability to values consistent with GW170817 constraints.
