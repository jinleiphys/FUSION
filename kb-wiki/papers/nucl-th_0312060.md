# Iterated and irreducible pion-photon exchange in nuclei

> We calculate the contribution to the nuclear energy density functional which arises from iterated pion-photon exchange between nucleons. In heavy nuclei, this novel charge symmetry breaking interaction leads to an additional binding of each proton by about 0.2 MeV. Compared to that the analogous effect from irreducible pion-photon exchange is negligibly small. As a possible mechanism to resolve the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly we propose the iteration of one-photon exchange with an attractive short-range NN-interaction. The corresponding energy per proton reads: $\bar E[ρ_p]=(2α/15π^2)(π^2 -3+6 \ln2) {\cal A}_{pp} k_p^2$ with $ρ_p =k_p^3/ 3π^2$ the proton density and ${\cal A}_{pp}\approx 2 $fm an effective (in-medium) scattering length. Hints for such a value of ${\cal A}_{pp}$ come from phenomenological Skyrme forces and from the neutron matter equation of state.

# Iterated and irreducible pion-photon exchange in nuclei

## Key claim

Iterated pion-photon exchange between nucleons contributes an additional binding of each proton in heavy nuclei by about 0.2 MeV, which worsens the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly rather than resolving it; the author proposes instead that iteration of one-photon exchange with an attractive short-range NN interaction, characterized by an effective in-medium scattering length of approximately 2 fm, may resolve the anomaly.

## Method

The author uses chiral perturbation theory to compute three-loop in-medium diagrams of iterated and irreducible pion-photon exchange, evaluating their contributions to the nuclear energy density functional via the density-matrix expansion of Negele and Vautherin (1972). The calculations employ a local proton Fermi momentum and kinetic energy density, with the medium insertion formalism to handle inhomogeneous nuclear systems, and numerical Coulomb energies are obtained for Saxon-Woods proton density profiles.

## Key numbers

- Iterated pion-photon exchange Fock diagram gives Coulomb energy per proton of -0.18 MeV for Ca, -0.18 MeV for Fe, -0.19 MeV for Sn, and -0.20 MeV for Pb (Table 1)
- One-photon exchange Hartree term gives Coulomb energy per proton of 3.94 MeV (Ca), 4.72 MeV (Fe), 7.44 MeV (Sn), and 10.43 MeV (Pb) (Table 1)
- One-photon exchange Fock term gives Coulomb energy per proton of -0.37 MeV (Ca), -0.37 MeV (Fe), -0.38 MeV (Sn), and -0.39 MeV (Pb) (Table 1)
- In the chiral limit (m_pi = 0), iterated pion-photon exchange gives energy per proton approximately -1.50 MeV·fm^2 · rho_p^(2/3) (equation 14)
- The proposed mechanism of iterated one-photon exchange with short-range attraction gives energy per proton = (2*alpha/15*pi^2)*(pi^2 - 3 + 6*ln2)*A_pp*k_p^2, approximately 2.053 MeV·fm · A_pp · rho_p^(2/3) (equation 20)
- Effective pp-scattering length A_pp ≈ 2 fm is required to compensate the Fock contributions
- Skyrme forces SKXce, SKXm, and SKX give A_pp = 1.96 fm, 2.40 fm, and 1.83 fm respectively
- Neutron matter equation of state from the Urbana group implies A_nn ≈ 2.47 fm, and by isospin symmetry A_pp ≈ 2.47 fm

## Context

This work extends the study of charge-symmetry breaking in nuclear structure, specifically addressing the Nolen-Schiffer anomaly where experimental binding energy differences between mirror nuclei are systematically about 7% larger than calculations with charge-symmetric strong interactions. The author builds on the systematic Skyrme-Hartree-Fock study by Brown et al. (2000) who found the anomaly could be resolved by dropping the Coulomb exchange term or introducing a charge-symmetry breaking delta-force, though with strengths 3-4 times larger than expected from high-precision NN potentials such as AV18 or CDBonn. The proposed mechanism of iterated one-photon exchange with short-range attraction provides a microscopic alternative to the quark-based explanation of Henley and Krein (1998) and the quark-meson coupling model of Thomas (1999). The next step would be to perform Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations including all charge-symmetry breaking terms derived here, determining A_pp from a best fit to mirror nuclei binding energy differences.
