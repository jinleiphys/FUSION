# Constraining the density dependence of the symmetry energy using the multiplicity and average $p_T$ ratios of charged pions

> The charged pion multiplicity ratio in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions, a probe of the density dependence of symmetry energy above the saturation point, has been proven in a previous study to be extremely sensitive to the strength of the isovector $Δ$(1232) potential in nuclear matter. As there is no knowledge, either from theory or experiment, about the magnitude of this quantity, the extraction of constraints on the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation by using exclusively the mentioned observable is hindered at present. It is shown that, by including the ratio of average $p_T$ of charged pions $\langle p_T^{(π^+)}\rangle/\langle p_T^{(π^-)}\rangle$ in the list of fitted observables, the noted problem can be circumvented. A realistic description of this observable requires accounting for the interaction of pions with the dense nuclear matter environment by the incorporation of the so called $S$-wave and $P$-wave pion optical potentials. This is performed within the framework of a quantum molecular dynamics transport model that enforces the conservation of the total energy of the system. It is shown that constraints on the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation density and the strength of the $Δ$(1232) potential can be simultaneously extracted. A symmetry energy with a value of the slope parameter $L>$ 50 MeV is favored, at 1$σ$ confidence level, from a comparison with published FOPI experimental data. A precise constraint will require experimental data more accurate than presently available, particularly for the charged pion multiplicity ratio, and better knowledge of the density and momentum dependence of the pion potential for the whole range of these two variables probed in intermediate energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

By including the ratio of average transverse momentum of charged pions ($\langle p_T^{(\pi^+)}\rangle/\langle p_T^{(\pi^-)}\rangle$) alongside the charged pion multiplicity ratio as fitted observables, constraints on the slope of the symmetry energy at saturation density can be extracted independently of the unknown strength of the isovector $\Delta$(1232) potential, though a precise constraint requires more accurate experimental data and better knowledge of the pion potential.

## Method

Heavy-ion collisions were simulated using an upgraded version of the Tübingen quantum molecular dynamics (QMD) transport model that enforces global total energy conservation, including potential energies in the energy conservation constraint for scattering, decay, and absorption processes. The Gogny-inspired MDI interaction parametrization was used for the nuclear mean field, with the stiffness parameter $x$ varied to adjust the symmetry energy density dependence. The pion optical potential, including both $S$-wave and $P$-wave components, was incorporated using parameter sets extracted from pionic atom data, with the $S$-wave energy dependence modeled using experimental constraints from pion-nucleus scattering.

## Key numbers

- The slope parameter $L$ of the symmetry energy is favored to be $L > 50$ MeV at 1$\sigma$ confidence level from comparison with published FOPI experimental data.
- For the MDI parametrization, the slope parameter $L$ takes values: 152 MeV ($x=-2$), 106 MeV ($x=-1$), 61 MeV ($x=0$), 15 MeV ($x=1$), and -31 MeV ($x=2$), with the symmetry energy at saturation fixed at $S_0=30.6$ MeV.
- The compressibility modulus is set to $K=245$ MeV and the effective isoscalar nucleon mass is $0.7 m_N$ with a neutron-proton effective mass splitting of approximately $0.4\beta$ at saturation density.
- The nucleon wave function width is chosen as $L_N=4.33$ fm$^2$, and the pion wave function width is set to $L_\pi=0.5 L_N$.
- The Coulomb interaction strength was decreased by 10% compared to its standard value to reproduce the Coulomb binding energy contribution of approximately 3.72 MeV/nucleon for $^{197}$Au.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Cozma (2014) by adding the average $p_T$ ratio of charged pions to the charged pion multiplicity ratio, resolving the ambiguity caused by the unknown isovector $\Delta$(1232) potential strength that had previously hindered extraction of symmetry energy constraints. The study uses the FOPI experimental data (Reisdorf et al., 2006, 2010, 2015) and addresses the conflicting constraints on the symmetry energy stiffness obtained by different transport models (Xiao et al., 2008; Xie et al., 2013; Feng et al., 2009; Hong et al., 2013). The results enable simultaneous extraction of constraints on both the slope of the symmetry energy and the $\Delta$(1232) potential strength, though more accurate experimental data and better knowledge of the pion optical potential at high densities and momenta are needed for precise constraints.
