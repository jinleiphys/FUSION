# Mixed phase effects on high-mass twin stars

> Recently it has been found that a certain class of hybrid star equations of state with a large latent heat (strong first order phase transition obtained by a Maxwell construction) between stiff hadronic hadronic and stiff quark matter phases allows for the appearance of a third family of compact stars (including "twins") at high mass of $\sim 2 M_\odot$. We investigate how robust this high-mass twin phenomenon is against a smoothing of the transition which would occur, e.g., due to pasta structures in the mixed phase. To this end we propose a simple construction of a pasta-like equation of state with a parameter that quantifies the degree of smoothing of the transition and could eventually be related to the surface tension of the pasta structures. It is interesting to note that the range of energy densities for the transition as well as the pressure at the onset of the transition of this class of hybrid star matter at zero temperature corresponds well to values of the same quantities found in finite temperature lattice QCD simulations for the 1 $σ$ region at the pseudocritical temperature $T_c=154 \pm 9$ MeV. The pattern of the speed of sound as a function of energy density is very different.

# Mixed phase effects on high-mass twin stars

## Key claim

The high-mass twin star phenomenon, previously predicted using a Maxwell construction with a strong first-order phase transition between stiff hadronic and stiff quark matter, is robust against moderate smoothing of the transition caused by pasta-like mixed phase structures; however, the twin sequence disappears when the smoothing parameter exceeds a critical value of \(\Gamma_s = 5.0\) MeV/fm\(^3\), corresponding to a pressure broadening of about \(\pm 10\) MeV/fm\(^3\) around the critical pressure.

## Method

The authors construct a hybrid equation of state by interpolating between hadronic (DD2 with excluded volume) and quark (NJL with higher order interactions) phases using a tanh-based smoothing function controlled by a parameter \(\Gamma_s\) that quantifies the broadening of the transition pressure region. They solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for static, non-rotating stars to obtain mass-radius relations, and vary \(\Gamma_s\) from zero (Maxwell construction) upward to determine the threshold at which the third family of compact stars (twins) merges with the second family.

## Key numbers

- The critical smoothing parameter at which the high-mass twin phenomenon disappears is \(\Gamma_s = 5.0\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- At this critical value, the pressure domain for the phase transition extends to about \(\pm 10\) MeV/fm\(^3\) around the Maxwell critical pressure \(p_c = 40\) MeV/fm\(^3\).
- The high-mass twins occur at masses of approximately \(2 M_\odot\).
- The pseudocritical temperature from lattice QCD used for comparison is \(T_c = 154 \pm 9\) MeV.
- The lattice QCD transition region in energy density is \(180 < \epsilon [\text{MeV/fm}^3] < 500\).

## Context

This work extends the high-mass twin star models of Benic et al. (2014), which used a Maxwell construction to produce a third family of compact stars, by testing their robustness against the inclusion of pasta-like mixed phase structures. The study is motivated by the possibility of detecting such twins with future missions like NICER, NUSTAR, and SKA, and complements microscopic studies by Yasutake et al. (2014) that found pasta structures do not dramatically smooth the Maxwell plateau. The comparison with lattice QCD results from Bazavov et al. (2014) shows that while the pressure and energy density ranges of the cold hybrid star transition match those of hot QCD, the speed of sound behavior differs significantly, suggesting a line of critical endpoints in the three-dimensional QCD phase diagram.
