# Neutron$-^{19}$C scattering near an Efimov state

> The low-energy neutron$-^{19}$C scattering in a neutron-neutron-core model is studied with large scattering lengths near the conditions for the appearance of an Efimov state. We show that the real part of the elastic $s-$wave phase-shift ($δ_0^R$) presents a zero, or a pole in $ k\cotδ_0^{R}$, when the system has an Efimov excited or virtual state. More precisely the pole scales with the energy of the Efimov state (bound or virtual). We perform calculations in the limit of large scattering lengths, disregarding the interaction range, within a renormalized zero-range approach using subtracted equations. It is also presented a brief discussion of these findings in the context of ultracold atom physics with tunable scattering lengths.

## Key claim

The low-energy $s$-wave $n-^{19}$C elastic phase-shift ($\delta_0^R$) exhibits a zero, and $k\cot\delta_0^{R}$ exhibits a pole, when the $n-n-^{18}$C system has an Efimov excited or virtual state; this pole scales with the energy of the Efimov state, and the qualitative behavior is independent of mass ratios, extending the known properties of the neutron-deuteron doublet state to this halo-nucleus system.

## Method

The authors solve a renormalized zero-range three-body model for $n-^{19}$C elastic scattering, using subtracted integral equations derived from the Faddeev formalism in the limit of large scattering lengths. The input parameters are the fixed $n-n$ virtual state energy ($-$143 keV), the fixed $^{20}$C ground-state energy ($-$3.5 MeV), and a variable $^{19}$C neutron separation energy (200 to 850 keV). The $s$-wave phase-shift is extracted from the on-shell scattering amplitude, and the effective range expansion is parametrized with a pole term analogous to the Van Oers-Seagrave formula used for $n-d$ doublet scattering.

## Key numbers

- The $n-n$ virtual state energy is fixed at $E_{nn} = -143$ keV.
- The $^{20}$C ground-state energy is fixed at $-$3.5 MeV.
- The $^{19}$C binding energy is varied from 200 keV to 850 keV.
- For $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}| = 200$ keV, the inverse $n-^{19}$C scattering length is $(a_{n-^{19}C})^{-1} = -0.591 \times 10^{-2}$ fm$^{-1}$ and the pole position $E_0 = 1442.7$ keV.
- For $|E_{^{19}{\rm C}}| = 850$ keV, $(a_{n-^{19}C})^{-1} = -5.510$ fm$^{-1}$ and $E_0 = 28.8$ keV.
- The critical $^{19}$C binding energy for the Efimov excited state to become a virtual state is approximately 170 keV, corresponding to $a_{n-^{18}{\rm C}} \approx 11.347$ fm.
- The ratio of two-body scattering lengths at the zero-scattering-length condition and at the Efimov-state-at-threshold condition is estimated as $a_0/a_B \approx 0.42$ for mass ratio $A=18$, and the numerical result gives $\sqrt{170/850} \simeq 0.45$.

## Context

This work extends the universal three-body physics previously studied in the $n-d$ doublet system (Van Oers and Seagrave, 1967; Whiting and Fuda, 1971; Adhikari and Tomio, 1982) to the halo nucleus $^{20}$C modeled as $n-n-^{18}$C. It builds on the authors' prior finding (Yamashita et al., 2004) that $^{20}$C presents a virtual Efimov state that becomes excited when the $^{19}$C binding is decreased. The results enable experimental searches for Efimov signatures in low-energy neutron scattering from $^{19}$C and suggest analogous phenomena in ultracold atom-dimer mixtures with tunable interactions, where the atom-dimer scattering length can be dialed through zero (Braaten and Hammer, 2006).
