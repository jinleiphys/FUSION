# Study of entropy production in Yang-Mills theory with use of Husimi function

> Understanding the thermalization process in a pure quantum system is a challenge in theoretical physics. In this work, we explore possible thermalization mechanism in Yang-Mills(YM) theory by using a positive semi-definite quantum distribution function called a Husimi function which is given by a coarse graining of the Wigner function within the minimum uncertainty. Then entropy is defined in terms of the Husimi function, which is called the Husimi-Wehrl(HW) entropy. We propose two numerical methods to calculate the HW entropy. We find that it is feasible to apply the semi-classical approximation with the use of classical YM equation. It should be noted that the semi-classical approximation is valid in the systems of physical interest including the early stage of heavy-ion collisions. Using a product ansatz for the Husimi function, which is confirmed to reproduce the HW entropy within 20% error (overestimate) for a few-body quantum system, we succeed in a numerical evaluation of HW entropy of YM fields and show that it surely has a finite value and increases in time.

## Key claim

The authors show that the Husimi-Wehrl (HW) entropy of Yang-Mills fields increases in time under a semiclassical approximation, implying thermal entropy production, but this result relies on a product ansatz for the Husimi function that reproduces the exact HW entropy only within about 10-20% overestimate for a few-body quantum system.

## Method

The authors define entropy using the Husimi function, a Gaussian-smoothed Wigner function that is positive semi-definite. They propose two Monte-Carlo-based numerical methods (two-step Monte Carlo and test particle methods) to compute the HW entropy in the semiclassical approximation, where the Wigner function evolves along classical trajectories. For application to Yang-Mills theory on a lattice, they introduce a product ansatz that factorizes the Husimi function into one-degree-of-freedom components, allowing the HW entropy to be computed as a sum over individual degrees of freedom.

## Key numbers

* The product ansatz reproduces the HW entropy within around 10% error for a two-dimensional quantum mechanical system, and within 20% error (overestimate) for a few-body quantum system.
* The SU(2) Yang-Mills field theory is simulated on \(4^3\) and \(6^3\) lattices, and the HW entropy is shown to increase in time on both lattice sizes.
* At time t=3 on the \(4^3\) lattice, the HW entropy values from the tsMC and TP methods agree when a large number of Monte Carlo samples or test particles is used.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Kunihiro (2010) and Iida (2013), which found a positive Kolmogorov-Sinai entropy production rate in classical Yang-Mills theory and highlighted the role of initial fluctuations. The present paper calculates entropy itself (not just its rate) by including initial quantum fluctuations through the Husimi function, aiming to address the early thermalization problem in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The approach enables future calculations of entropy production in the glasma stage using semiclassical methods, with the product ansatz providing a tractable approximation for systems with many degrees of freedom.
