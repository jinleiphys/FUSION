# New effective interactions in RMF theory with non-linear terms and density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling

> New parameter sets for the Lagrangian density in the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory, PK1 with nonlinear sigma- and omega-meson self-coupling, PK1R with nonlinear sigma-, omega- and rho-meson self-coupling and PKDD with the density-dependent meson-nucleon coupling, are proposed. They are able to provide an excellent description not only for the properties of nuclear matter but also for the nuclei in and far from the valley of beta-stability. For the first time in the parametrization of the RMF Lagrangian density, the center-of-mass correction is treated by a microscopic way, which is essential to unify the description of nuclei from light to heavy regions with one effective interaction.

## Key claim

The paper proposes three new parameter sets for the relativistic mean field (RMF) Lagrangian (PK1, PK1R, and PKDD) that achieve an excellent description of both nuclear matter properties and finite nuclei, including those far from beta-stability. The key advance is that, for the first time in RMF parametrization, the center-of-mass correction is treated microscopically rather than phenomenologically, which the authors claim is essential for unifying the description of nuclei from light to heavy regions with a single effective interaction.

## Method

The authors performed multi-parameter fitting using the Levenberg-Marquardt method to determine the coupling constants and meson masses in the RMF Lagrangian. For PK1, nonlinear self-couplings of the sigma and omega mesons were included; for PK1R, nonlinear self-couplings of sigma, omega, and rho mesons were included; for PKDD, density-dependent meson-nucleon couplings were used instead of nonlinear terms. The masses of ten spherical nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 48Ca, 56Ni, 68Ni, 90Zr, 116Sn, 132Sn, 194Pb, and 208Pb) were fitted together with nuclear matter properties (compression modulus, saturation density, and asymmetry energy), using a microscopic center-of-mass correction from projection-after-variation in first-order approximation.

## Key numbers

* Nuclear matter compression modulus: PK1 gives K = 283.0 MeV, PK1R gives K = 283.0 MeV, PKDD gives K = 262.0 MeV
* Nuclear matter saturation density: PK1 gives rho_sat = 0.148 fm^-3, PK1R gives rho_sat = 0.148 fm^-3, PKDD gives rho_sat = 0.149 fm^-3
* Asymmetry energy at saturation: PK1 gives J = 36.56 MeV, PK1R gives J = 36.56 MeV, PKDD gives J = 36.80 MeV
* For the new effective interactions, only 4 to 5 nuclear masses among the fitted nuclei deviate by more than 1 MeV from experimental values
* The rms deviation for total binding energy of the fitted nuclei is smaller for PK1, PK1R, and PKDD than for TM1, NL3, TW99, and DD-ME1
* For Pb isotopes, the new interactions reproduce the charge radius of 208Pb as 5.501 fm (PK1), 5.501 fm (PK1R), and 5.502 fm (PKDD), compared to the experimental value of 5.501 fm

## Context

This work extends the line of RMF parametrizations including NL1 (Reinhard, 1986), NL-SH (Sharma, 1993), TM1 (Sugahara and Toki, 1994), NL3 (Lalazissis, 1997), and the density-dependent interactions TW99 (Typel, 1999) and DD-ME1 (Niksic, 2002). The new parameter sets improve upon previous ones by providing more reasonable baryonic saturation density and compression modulus while also incorporating a microscopic center-of-mass correction that was previously treated phenomenologically. The new interactions enable more reliable predictions for exotic nuclei far from stability, including neutron-drip line positions (e.g., PK1 predicts neutron-drip number N=50 for Ca, N=70 for Ni, N=96 for Zr, and N=126 for Sn) and improved descriptions of isotope shifts and neutron skin thickness along isotopic chains.
