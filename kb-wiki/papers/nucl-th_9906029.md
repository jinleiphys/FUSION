# Quark Matter '99 --- Theoretical Summary: What Next?

> I review the three broad areas where major progress has been reported: The phase structure of strongly interacting matter, the properties of matter at the instant when it freezes out into individual hadrons in the final stage of the expansion of the hot fireball, and the status of the main signatures of the formation of a quark-gluon plasma. In the final section I present some thoughts about what should be done next, both in the experiemntal and the theoretical arena.

## Key claim

The conference summary argues that while the data from SPS and AGS heavy-ion experiments are consistent with a multi-stage dissolution of hadronic states as energy density increases, the theoretical interpretation of key signatures (especially J/ψ suppression) remains plagued by models that prove nothing, and the field must now focus on confirming or refuting this scenario through careful measurements and serious QCD-based calculations.

## Method

The author reviews theoretical and experimental progress reported at the Quark Matter '99 conference across three areas: the QCD phase structure (including lattice results on monopole condensation, percolation, color superconductivity, and the critical point), the hadronic freeze-out conditions extracted from single-particle spectra, pair correlations, and particle yields, and the status of four robust QGP signatures (flow, strangeness enhancement, J/ψ suppression, and ρ-meson broadening). The final section proposes specific experimental and theoretical next steps.

## Key numbers

- Free quark abundance limit from cosmological evidence: free quarks are at least 10⁻¹⁵ less abundant than expected, implying E_q > 10 GeV
- SPS freeze-out temperature: T_f ≈ 100 MeV, with average transverse flow velocity ⟨β_f⟩ ≈ 0.55 and baryon chemical potential μ_f ≈ 400 MeV
- SPS freeze-out transverse rms radius: approximately 10 fm; average freeze-out time lower limit 6–8 fm/c, spread over 2–3 fm/c
- SPS chemical freeze-out: T_ch = 170 ± 10 MeV, μ_B = 270 ± 25 MeV for Pb+Pb
- AGS chemical freeze-out: T_ch = 125 ± 10 MeV, μ_B = 540 ± 25 MeV for Au+Au
- Pion phase-space occupation at freeze-out: average density ⟨f⟩ = 0.3 from Bose-Einstein correlations
- SPS quark chemical potential: μ ≈ 90 MeV; AGS: μ ≈ 180 MeV
- Predicted ρ-meson broadening under SPS freeze-out conditions: ΔΓ_ρ ≈ 300 MeV, Δm_ρ ≈ 10 MeV
- Energy density required to dissolve J/ψ: predicted around 5 GeV/fm³
- Ratio Λ̄/p̄ for Au+Au at AGS (expt. E864): 3.5
- Ω hyperon enhancement factor in Pb+Pb vs. p+A at SPS (expt. WA97): factor 15
- Critical point location estimate: expected between SPS conditions (μ ≈ 90 MeV) and AGS conditions (μ ≈ 180 MeV); critical fluctuations may persist for |μ − μ_c| < 0.2 μ_c
- Perturbative saturation cut-off momentum for Pb+Pb at RHIC: p₀ ≈ 1 GeV/c

## Context

This theoretical summary of the Quark Matter '99 conference reviews progress in understanding the QCD phase diagram, including lattice evidence for monopole condensation as the cause of confinement (Di Giacomo, DelDebbio 1995), percolation models of the phase transition (Satz 1998), and color superconductivity at high baryon density (Bailin and Love 1984, Alford, Rajagopal, Wilczek 1998). The freeze-out analysis builds on work by Wiedemann and collaborators, showing a consistent picture from SPS data. The paper extends earlier discussions of QGP signatures by identifying four robust signals and calling for a systematic energy scan between AGS and SPS energies, as well as for serious QCD calculations of J/ψ dissociation cross sections, which currently differ by two orders of magnitude. The author proposes that if the SPS program ends, a fixed-target program at RHIC using crystal channeling (Rafelski and Uggerhøj 1999) could fill the gap.
