# A Scientific Analysis of the Preprint arXiv:1301.1828v1 [nucl-th]

> Below we analyze the `critic' statements made in the Preprint arXiv:1301.1828v1 [nucl-th]. The doubtful scientific argumentation of the authors of the Preprint arXiv:1301.1828v1 [nucl-th] is also discussed.

# A Scientific Analysis of the Preprint arXiv:1301.1828v1 [nucl-th]

## Key claim

The authors argue that the critique in arXiv:1301.1828v1 is based on an obsolete and unrealistic version of the hadron resonance gas model (HRGM) that lacks hard-core repulsion, a complete resonance table up to 2.5 GeV, and proper treatment of wide resonances; consequently, the claimed advantages of the alternative freeze-out criterion s/T³ = 7 over the constant entropy per hadron criterion s/ρ_p ≈ 7.18 are invalid because they rely on a model that contradicts QCD at temperatures above the pion mass.

## Method

The authors compare three versions of the HRGM: HRGM1 (the model used in the criticized preprint, lacking hard-core repulsion and other realistic features), HRGM2 (their own model from KABOliinychenko:12), and HRGM3 (the model of Andronic, Braun-Munzinger, and Stachel from 2005 and 2009). They analyze the four main critical statements from the preprint, demonstrating that HRGM1 omits hard-core repulsion (which reduces particle densities by up to 90%), neglects resonances above 2 GeV, and treats wide resonances improperly, making its thermodynamic predictions unreliable. They also present a new parameterization of the chemical freeze-out temperature as a function of baryonic chemical potential, T(μ_b) = T₀ / [1 + 2.44 / (ln(1/a [μ₀/μ_b - 1]))⁴] for μ_b ≤ 750 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The hard-core repulsion reduces particle densities by up to 90% compared to the ideal gas (Begun:12, Fig. 3).
- The parameterization T(μ_b) = a - b μ_b² - c μ_b⁴ has coefficients a = 0.166 ± 0.002 GeV, b = 0.139 ± 0.016 GeV⁻¹, c = 0.053 ± 0.021 GeV⁻³ (Cleymans:06).
- The new freeze-out parameterization uses T₀ ≈ 163 MeV, a ≈ 0.31, and μ₀ ≈ 1407 MeV, valid for μ_b ≤ 750 MeV.
- For μ_b < 750 MeV, the difference between the two freeze-out temperature parameterizations is below 25 MeV.
- The constant entropy per hadron criterion s/ρ_p ≈ 7.18 is valid for center-of-mass energies √s_NN ≥ 2.3 GeV (i.e., μ_b > 500 MeV).
- The σ(600) meson alone provides up to 5% of the total pion yield at energies √s < 6 GeV.
- In the HRGM with hard-core repulsion, s/T³ varies from 3.6 to 6 depending on the set of hard-core radii (Begun:12).
- The ideal gas model gives s/T³ ≈ 7 for lab energies above 4 GeV per nucleon with strangeness suppression factor = 1; with strangeness suppression, s/T³ = 6 for lab energies above 8 GeV per nucleon (Begun:12).

## Context

This paper is a direct rebuttal to the preprint arXiv:1301.1828v1 by Tawfik, Gamal, and Magdy (2013), which criticized the constant entropy per hadron freeze-out criterion proposed by Bugaev, Oliinychenko, and Sorin (2012). The authors defend their work and the earlier HRGM formulations of Andronic, Braun-Munzinger, and Stachel (2005, 2009) against what they characterize as scientifically unsound criticism. The analysis enables future work by clarifying which HRGM features are essential for reliable thermodynamic extraction from heavy-ion data, and by providing an improved parameterization of the chemical freeze-out curve that accounts for hard-core repulsion and a complete resonance spectrum.
