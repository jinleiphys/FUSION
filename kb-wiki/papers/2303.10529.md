# Diproton correlation and two-proton emission from proton-rich nuclei

> Open-print version of dissertation thesis. This thesis focuses on the relation between the diproton correlation and the two-proton emission with quantum entanglement. For this purpose, I developed a time-dependent three-body (proton-proton-core) model, where one Cooper pair of protons move inside the mean-field potential. The time-dependent calculation based on the three-body model provides an intuitive way to describe the quantum dynamics of the entangled fermionic pair. It has been shown that, by considering the Cooper-pairing correlation in the initial state of the two protons, (i) the experimental 2p-decay width of Be-6 is well reproduced, and (ii) the two protons are emitted mainly as a diproton-like cluster with the spin-singlet configuration in the early stage of the emission. Hence, the two-proton emission can provide an access to the diproton correlation. This thesis presented the first step to theoretically investigate the diproton correlation as well as quantum entanglement in the time-dependent radioactive emission. # Wiki Page: Diproton Correlation and Two-Proton Emission from Proton-Rich Nuclei

## Key claim

The thesis demonstrates that by including Cooper-pairing correlation in the initial state of two protons, the experimental two-proton decay width of Be-6 is well reproduced and the two protons are emitted mainly as a diproton-like cluster with spin-singlet configuration in the early emission stage. The author notes that the conclusions do not change even if the nucleon-nucleon scattering length parameter is set to 15.0 fm instead of the empirical value of -18.5 fm, indicating some robustness but also a conditional dependence on the pairing strength.

## Method

The author developed a time-dependent three-body (proton-proton-core) model where one Cooper pair of protons moves inside a mean-field potential, using core-center (V-) coordinates to separate center-of-mass motion from relative motion. The time-dependent calculation based on this three-body model provides an intuitive way to describe the quantum dynamics of the entangled fermionic pair, with the initial state defined using a confining potential procedure. The continuum space is discretized with an energy cutoff, and the time evolution is computed via expansion in eigenstates of the total Hamiltonian, with the survival probability obtained from the Fourier transform of the energy spectrum.

## Key numbers

- The empirical nucleon-nucleon scattering length used is -18.5 fm, with an alternative value of 15.0 fm also tested; the author confirmed that conclusions do not change when using 15.0 fm instead of -18.5 fm.
- The contact potential strength V0 is fitted using the formula V0 = (hbar^2 / 2mu) * (4 pi^2 a_nn / (pi - 2 a_nn k_C)), where a_nn is the scattering length and k_C is the momentum cutoff corresponding to the energy cutoff E_cut.
- The Breit-Wigner distribution for the energy spectrum has center E0 and full width at half maximum Gamma0, with the lifetime tau = hbar / Gamma0 relating to the exponential decay rule P_surv(t) = exp(-t/tau).

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of nuclear pairing correlations beyond the traditional mean-field picture established by Bohr (1958) and Bohr and Mottelson (1969), and builds on the time-dependent framework for quantum meta-stable phenomena described by Krylov and Fock (1947) and Kukulin (1989). The thesis provides a first step toward theoretically investigating diproton correlation and quantum entanglement in time-dependent radioactive emission, enabling future studies of how initial configuration choices affect the dynamics of two-proton emission from proton-rich nuclei.
