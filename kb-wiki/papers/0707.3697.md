# Chromoelectric response functions for quark-gluon plasma

> We determine the chromoelectric response of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) systematically within the framework of classical transport equations. The transport equations are set up in the phase space which includes the SU(3) group space corresponding to color (which is a dynamical degree of freedom), in addition to the position - momentum variables. The distribution functions are defined by projecting the density operators for the quarks and the gluons to their respective coherent states (defined over the extended phase space). The full import of the Yang-Mills(YM) dynamics is shown to manifest through the emergence of an intrinsic nonlinear, nonlocal response, whose behavior we determine in the long wavelength limit. It also manifests as a tensor response which is a characteristic of gluons. The response functions are shown to have a natural interpretation in terms of the renormalizations of the Abelian and the non-Abelian coupling constants. A detailed analysis of the screening of heavy quark potential and of the exact role played by the Debye mass screening in the case of the Cornell potential, is performed. We also discuss the non-Abelian contribution to Landau damping in QGP.

## Key claim

The paper derives chromoelectric response functions for quark-gluon plasma (QGP) using classical transport equations with coherent state projections in color space, showing that the non-Abelian dynamics produces an intrinsic nonlinear, nonlocal response in addition to the standard Abelian screening, but only in the long wavelength limit and for an ideal, collisionless, isotropic plasma close to equilibrium.

## Method

The authors set up classical Vlasov-type transport equations in an extended phase space that includes SU(3) color degrees of freedom, projecting quantum density operators onto coherent states for both quarks (fundamental representation) and gluons (adjoint representation). They solve the linearized transport equation in the temporal gauge, truncating the velocity-moment hierarchy in the long wavelength limit (k/ω ≪ 1), and extract the chromoelectric permittivity tensors by comparing the induced color charge density with Gauss's law in Fourier space.

## Key numbers

- Abelian permittivity for quarks: ε_A = 1 + (2π³Q²T²N_f)/(3k²) { - (ω/k) ln|(ω+k)/(ω-k)| + 2 }
- Non-Abelian permittivity for quarks: ε_N = 1 - (4π³Q²T²N_f)/(9) · 1/(ωω')
- Abelian permittivity for gluons: ε_A = 1 + (4π³Q²T²)/(3k²) { - (ω/k) ln|(ω+k)/(ω-k)| + 2 }
- Non-Abelian permittivity for gluons: ε_N = 1 - (8π³Q²T²)/(9) · 1/(ωω')
- Susceptibility relation between quarks and gluons: A(q) = (N_f/2) A(g) and N(q) = (N_f/2) N(g)

## Context

This work extends the classical transport approach to QGP response pioneered by Blaizot and Iancu (2000s) and the coherent state method of Perelomov (1986), going beyond earlier perturbative QCD calculations by Weldon (1980s) and the SU(2) lattice-based screening analysis by Petreczky (2000s). The new non-Abelian permittivity ε_N, which has no Abelian counterpart, enables future studies of nonlinear, non-Markovian effects such as gluonic bremsstrahlung and Čerenkov radiation of gluons in heavy-ion collisions, and provides a framework for incorporating realistic collision terms (e.g., Bodeker 1998) in subsequent work.
