# Arbitrary l-state solutions of the rotating Morse potential by the asymptotic iteration method

> For non-zero $\ell$ values, we present an analytical solution of the radial Schrödinger equation for the rotating Morse potential using the Pekeris approximation within the framework of the Asymptotic Iteration Method. The bound state energy eigenvalues and corresponding wave functions are obtained for a number of diatomic molecules and the results are compared with the findings of the super-symmetry, the hypervirial perturbation, the Nikiforov-Uvarov, the variational, the shifted 1/N and the modified shifted 1/N expansion methods.

## Key claim
The Asymptotic Iteration Method (AIM) yields analytical energy eigenvalues and wave functions for the rotating Morse potential with non-zero orbital angular momentum, but only when the Pekeris approximation is applied to the centrifugal barrier, which limits validity to low vibrational energy states.

## Method
The authors solved the radial Schrodinger equation for the rotating Morse potential by first applying the Pekeris approximation, which expands the centrifugal term \( \ell(\ell+1)\hbar^2/(2\mu r^2) \) in a series of exponentials up to second order around the equilibrium distance. They then transformed the resulting differential equation into the standard AIM form \( y'' = \lambda_0(x) y' + s_0(x) y \) using the substitution \( y = e^{-\alpha x} \), and obtained energy eigenvalues from the AIM quantization condition \( \delta_k(x) = \lambda_k(x) s_{k-1}(x) - \lambda_{k-1}(x) s_k(x) = 0 \). Wave functions were constructed using the AIM wave function generator and expressed in terms of Laguerre polynomials.

## Key numbers
- Energy eigenvalues for the \( H_2 \) diatomic molecule were calculated and compared with SUSY, hypervirial perturbation, shifted 1/N, and modified shifted 1/N expansion methods (Table 1 in the paper).
- Energy eigenvalues for the \( HCl \) diatomic molecule were calculated and compared with the same methods (Table 2).
- Energy eigenvalues for the \( CO \) and \( LiH \) diatomic molecules were calculated and compared with the Nikiforov-Uvarov method, shifted 1/N, and modified shifted 1/N expansion methods (Tables 3 and 4).
- The general energy eigenvalue formula is \( E_{n\ell} = -\frac{\hbar^2}{2\mu r_e^2} \left[ \frac{\beta_1^2}{2\beta_2} - (n+\frac{1}{2})\alpha \right]^2 + \gamma c_0 \), where \( \gamma = \frac{\ell(\ell+1)\hbar^2}{2\mu r_e^2} \), \( c_0 = 1 - \frac{3}{\alpha} + \frac{3}{\alpha^2} \), \( \beta_1^2 = \frac{2\mu r_e^2}{\hbar^2}(2D - \gamma c_1) \), and \( \beta_2^2 = \frac{2\mu r_e^2}{\hbar^2}(\gamma c_2 + D) \).

## Context
This work extends previous solutions of the rotating Morse potential that used the super-symmetry (SUSY) method of Morales (1990), the Nikiforov-Uvarov method of Ciftci (2003), the shifted 1/N expansion method of Bag (1998), and the hypervirial perturbation method of Killingbeck (1985). The AIM provides a more systematic and practical alternative that yields eigenvalues directly without solving transcendental equations, and the authors suggest it can be extended to other interaction problems.
