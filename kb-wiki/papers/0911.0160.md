# Omega and Eta (Eta-prime) mesons from NN and ND collisions at intermediate energies

> The production of pseudo scalar, Eeta, Eta-prime, and vector, Omega, Rho, Phi, mesons in NN collisions at threshold-near energies is analyzed within a covariant effective meson-nucleon theory. It is shown that a good description of cross sections and angular distributions, for vector meson production, can be accomplished by considering meson and nucleon currents only, while for pseudo scalar production an inclusion of nucleon resonances is needed. The di-electron production from subsequent Dalitz decay of the produced mesons, $η'\to γγ^* \toγe^+e^-$ and $ω\to πγ^*\to πe^+e^-$ is also considered and numerical results are presented for intermediate energies and kinematics of possible experiments with HADES, CLAS and KEK-PS. We argue that the transition form factor $ω\to γ^*π$ as well as $η'\to γ^*γ$ can be defined in a fairly model independent way and the feasibility of an experimental access to transition form factors is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that vector meson (omega, phi) production in NN collisions can be described well using only nucleon and meson currents without nucleon resonances, while pseudoscalar meson (eta, eta-prime) production requires inclusion of nucleon resonances; the authors argue that transition form factors for omega to gamma* pi and eta-prime to gamma* gamma can be defined in a fairly model-independent way, but this claim depends on the validity of the one-boson exchange framework and the specific parameter choices constrained by independent data.

## Method

The authors employ a covariant effective meson-nucleon theory with interaction Lagrangians including scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector mesons, computing Feynman diagrams for nucleon current and internal conversion contributions to meson production in NN collisions. They calculate total cross sections and angular distributions for omega, phi, eta, and eta-prime production, then extend the formalism to Dalitz decays (eta-prime to gamma e+ e- and omega to pi e+ e-) using transition form factors parameterized by vector meson dominance (VMD) and monopole fits. The spectator mechanism is applied to deuteron reactions to access quasi-free neutron targets.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous one-boson exchange calculations for omega and phi production (Kaptari and Kampfer, 2005-2008) and eta/eta-prime production (Kaptari and Kampfer, 2007-2008) to include Dalitz decays and deuteron reactions. The analysis of eta Dalitz decays as a source of di-electrons competing with Delta Dalitz decays and bremsstrahlung builds on HADES data analysis (2007-2008). The approach enables experimental access to transition form factors at HADES, CLAS, and KEK-PS, and provides a framework for testing the OZI rule in phi production without requiring hidden strangeness in nucleons. The spectator technique with deuterons allows extraction of neutron-target cross sections for comparison with proton-target results.
