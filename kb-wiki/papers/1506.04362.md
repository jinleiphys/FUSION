# Ultra-relativistic light-heavy nuclear collisions and collectivity

## Key claim
Ultra-relativistic light-heavy collisions (p-Pb, d-Au, 3He-Au, 12C-Au) display collective evolution with the same characteristic features as in A-A systems, but the applicability of hydrodynamics or transport theory to these small systems is still being tested and differentiated from approaches based on QCD saturation phenomena.

## Method
The authors review experimental signatures of collectivity in light-heavy collisions, including the ridge structure in two-particle correlations, mass ordering of mean transverse momentum and harmonic flow coefficients, near-equality of higher-order cumulants for harmonic flow, and the fall-off of HBT correlation radii with transverse momentum. They present hydrodynamic model results for 3He-Au collisions and Glauber model simulations for 12C-208Pb collisions, using the shape-flow transmutation relation to connect initial-state eccentricities to measurable flow coefficients.

## Key numbers
- For 12C-208Pb collisions at SPS energies, the nucleon-nucleon inelastic cross section used in Glauber model simulations is sigma^{inel}_{NN} = 32 mb.
- The scaled standard deviation of triangular eccentricity sigma(epsilon_n)/langle epsilon_n rangle for 12C-208Pb collisions changes character at centrality c ~ 10%, where clustered and uniform nuclear wave function cases depart from each other.
- The cumulant ratios epsilon_n{4}/epsilon_n{2} for 12C-208Pb collisions also change behavior around c = 10%.

## Context
This work extends the study of collective flow from heavy A-A systems to light-heavy collisions, building on prior observations of the ridge in p-Pb and high-multiplicity p-p collisions by Khachatryan (2010), Adare (2013), Aad (2012), Chatrchyan (2013), and Abelev (2012). The authors propose a new methodology from Broniowski (2013) for studying ground-state correlations in nuclear distributions using shape-flow transmutation, which enables probing low-energy nuclear structure (such as alpha clusterization in 12C) with ultra-relativistic collision techniques. The results set limits on the applicability of hydrodynamics versus QCD saturation approaches as discussed by Dusling (2012).
