# Fock terms in the quark-meson coupling model

> The mean field description of nuclear matter in the quark-meson coupling model is improved by the inclusion of exchange contributions (Fock terms). The inclusion of Fock terms allows us to explore the momentum dependence of meson-nucleon vertices and the role of pionic degrees of freedom in matter. It is found that the Fock terms maintain the previous predictions of the model for the in-medium properties of the nucleon and for the nuclear incompressibility. The Fock terms significantly increase the absolute values of the single-particle, four-component scalar and vector potentials, a feature that is relevant for the spin-orbit splitting in finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The inclusion of Fock (exchange) terms in the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model maintains the previous mean-field predictions for in-medium nucleon properties and nuclear incompressibility, while significantly increasing the absolute values of the single-particle scalar and vector potentials; however, the calculation is not fully self-consistent, as Fock terms are calculated perturbatively using Hartree self-energies.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective meson-nucleon Hamiltonian following the Cloudy Bag Model, projecting the quark-meson Hamiltonian onto single-nucleon states in the presence of constant mean meson fields. They calculated the Fock (exchange) contributions to the nuclear matter energy density for sigma, omega, pion, and rho mesons, using density-dependent form factors derived from MIT bag model wave functions modified by the mean fields. The Fock terms were treated perturbatively on top of the Hartree mean-field solution, with the scalar mean field determined self-consistently including the Fock energy contribution.

## Key numbers

- Saturation binding energy: E/A - M = -15.7 MeV at nuclear matter density rho_0 = 0.15 fm^{-3}
- For free bag radius R = 0.8 fm: in-medium to free nucleon mass ratio M*/M = 0.83; in-medium to free rms radius ratio r*/r = 1.02
- Nuclear matter incompressibility: K = 285 MeV (Hartree value was 280 MeV)
- Symmetry energy: a_4 approximately 30 MeV, independent of free bag radius
- Fock terms increase |V_s| + |V_0| at |p| = k_F by 67 MeV compared to the Hartree value (for R = 0.8 fm)
- When using free-space form factors instead of density-dependent ones (R = 0.8 fm): incompressibility changes to K = 264 MeV; symmetry energy changes to a_4 = 36 MeV
- Hartree coupling constants (R = 0.8 fm): g_sigma^2/4pi = 5.40; g_omega^2/4pi = 5.31
- Pion Fock term repulsive contribution approximately 8 MeV; after subtracting contact term, becomes attractive approximately 5 MeV
- Sigma and omega Fock terms together give repulsive contribution approximately 5 MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, previously applied only in the mean-field approximation, by including exchange (Fock) terms required by the Pauli principle. It follows the approach of Chin (1977) in quantum hadrodynamics (QHD), where Fock terms were calculated perturbatively using Hartree self-energies, and builds on the work of Bouyssy et al. (1987) who showed that momentum-dependent exchange contributions from isovector mesons are essential for spin-orbit splittings in finite nuclei. The results enable future fully self-consistent calculations, application to finite nuclei for spin-orbit splitting studies, and extension to include chiral symmetry and the Delta(1232) resonance.
