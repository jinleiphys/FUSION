# Mini-Proceedings of ECT Workshop "Strangeness in Nuclei"

> This workshop brought together international experts in the research area of strangeness in nuclei physics, working on theory as well as on experiments, to discuss the present status, to develop new methods of analysis and to have the opportunity for brainstorming towards future studies, going towards a deeper understanding of the hot topics in the low-energy QCD in the strangeness sector.

## Key claim
The authors argue that measuring the T21 Sigma-pi invariant-mass spectrum from stopped K- absorption in deuteron can resolve the current debate on whether the Lambda(1405) is a K-p quasi-bound state of mass 1405 MeV/c^2 or 1420 MeV/c^2, which is critical for understanding the formation and structure of kaonic nuclear clusters.

## Method
The authors propose to use an "optical" relation connecting the T21 invariant-mass spectrum to the imaginary part of the antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitude, showing that observing the T21 spectrum is equivalent to observing the K-p quasi-bound-state mass. They compare their own model (AMY) with the chiral dynamics model of Hyodo and Weise (2008), which predicts a higher mass for the Lambda(1405). The stopped K- on deuteron experiment would provide the decisive measurement for mass discrimination.

## Key numbers
- Lambda(1405) mass of 1405 MeV/c^2 and width of 40 MeV, as assumed in the authors' Ansatz that it is a K-p quasi-bound state
- Chiral dynamics prediction of Lambda(1405) mass at 1420 MeV/c^2 or higher, from Hyodo and Weise (2008)

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous predictions of few-body deeply bound kaonic states (Akaishi and Yamazaki, 2002) and addresses a discrepancy with chiral dynamics calculations (Hyodo and Weise, 2008). The proposed measurement would resolve the debate on the K-p quasi-bound state mass, which is essential for understanding the formation of kaonic nuclear clusters. The work enables future experimental discrimination between competing theoretical models of low-energy QCD in the strangeness sector.
