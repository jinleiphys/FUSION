# Structure of the Roper Resonance from Lattice QCD Constraints

> Two different descriptions of the existing pion-nucleon scattering data in the region of the Roper resonance are constructed. Both descriptions fit the experimental data very well. In one scenario the resonance is the result of strong rescattering between coupled meson-baryon channels, while in the other scenario, the resonance has a large bare-baryon (or quark-model like) component. The predictions of these two scenarios are compared with the latest lattice QCD simulation results in this channel. Consideration of the finite volume spectra, the manner in which the states are excited from the vacuum in lattice QCD and the composition of the states in Hamiltonian effective field theory enable a discrimination of these two different descriptions. We find the second scenario is not consistent with lattice QCD results whereas the first agrees with the lattice QCD constraints. In this scenario, the mass of the quark-model like state is approximately 2 GeV and in the finite volume of the lattice is dressed to place the first radial excitation of the nucleon at 1.9 GeV. Within this description, the infinite-volume Roper resonance is best described as a resonance generated dynamically through strongly coupled meson-baryon channels.

## Key claim

The Roper resonance is best described as a resonance generated dynamically through strongly coupled meson-baryon channels, rather than as a traditional three-quark (quark-model-like) state; the quark-model-like radial excitation of the nucleon actually lies at approximately 2 GeV, not at the Roper mass.

## Method

The authors constructed two Hamiltonian effective field theory (HEFT) descriptions of pion-nucleon scattering data in the 1200-1800 MeV region, fitting the coupled $\pi N$, $\pi \Delta$, and $\sigma N$ channels. They then extended these infinite-volume models to finite volume using the Luscher relation to predict the lattice QCD spectrum, and compared both scenarios against lattice QCD results from Lang et al. (2016) and the CSSM collaboration, including analysis of eigenvector compositions from both HEFT and lattice correlation matrices.

## Key numbers

- The Roper resonance pole in Scenario I (dynamical) appears at 1392.92 - 167.13i MeV on the unphysical sheet; in Scenario II (quark-model-like) the pole is at 1362.33 - 100.53i MeV
- The bare baryon mass in Scenario I is 2.000 GeV; in Scenario II it is 1.700 GeV
- Scenario I yields $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 1.18$ (16.5/14) when compared to lattice QCD results; Scenario II yields $\chi^2/\text{dof} = 45.4$ (635.7/14)
- The finite-volume dressed state from the 2 GeV bare state appears at approximately 1.9 GeV for a pion mass of 156 MeV on a 3 fm lattice
- The $\chi^2$ for fits to experimental $\pi N$ scattering data are 241 (Scenario I) and 135 (Scenario II) for 72 data points

## Context

This work extends the HEFT methodology previously applied to the $\Lambda(1405)$ by Liu et al. (2015) and Hall et al. (2014) to the Roper resonance, and contradicts the long-standing constituent quark model expectation (Isgur, 1978) that the Roper is a three-quark radial excitation. The analysis enables a new understanding where the quark-model-like radial excitation of the nucleon sits at approximately 2 GeV (consistent with harmonic oscillator expectations), while the observed $N^*(1440)$ arises from meson-baryon rescattering, similar to how the $\Lambda(1405)$ is understood as a molecular $\bar{K}N$ state.
