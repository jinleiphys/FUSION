# Energy rates due to Fe isotopes during presupernova evolution of massive stars

> This work presents the microscopic calculation of energy rates (γ ray heating and (anti)neutrino cooling rates) due to weak decay of selected Fe isotopes. The isotopes have astrophysical significance during the presupernova evolution of massive stars. The energy rates are calculated using the pn QRPA model and compared with the independent particle model (IPM), large scale shell model (LSSM) and recent shell model calculation (GXPF1J). The reported (anti)neutrino cooling rates are smaller by up to two orders of magnitude at low core temperature values than the IPM rates. The two calculations compare well at T = 30 GK. The comparison of cooling rates with the LSSM is interesting. The pn QRPA cooling rates due to even even Fe isotopes are smaller (up to 2 orders of magnitude). For the odd A isotopes, the reported rates are bigger up to an order of magnitude. The pn QRPA computed cooling rates are, up to 2 orders of magnitude, bigger when compared with the GXPF1J calculation. The γ ray heating rates due to electron capture rates rise with the temperature and density values of the stellar core. On the other hand, the γ ray heating due to \b{eta} decay increases with the core temperature values but decreases by orders of magnitude when the stellar core stiffens. The pn QRPA computed γ heating rates are bigger (up to 3 orders of magnitude) at high temperatures and densities (for the case of 55 56Fe) when compared with the recent shell model results. Owing to the importance of energy rates, this study may contribute to a realistic simulation of presupernova evolution of massive stars.

# Energy rates due to Fe isotopes during presupernova evolution of massive stars

## Key claim
The pn-QRPA model produces (anti)neutrino cooling rates for selected Fe isotopes that are smaller by up to two orders of magnitude than independent particle model (IPM) rates at low core temperatures, but the two calculations compare well at T = 30 GK; the reported rates are also up to two orders of magnitude larger than recent shell model (GXPF1J) results, with the differences attributed to the GXPF1J calculation incorporating quenching of the axial-vector coupling constant and screening effects.

## Method
The authors employed the proton-neutron quasiparticle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) with a Nilsson model mean field and separable particle-hole and particle-particle Gamow-Teller residual interactions, without invoking the Brink-Axel hypothesis for excited state GT strength distributions. Energy rates (gamma-ray heating and (anti)neutrino cooling rates) were computed for the weak decay channels (electron capture, beta decay, positron emission, and positron capture) of the Fe isotopes 55-58 and 61-63, using phase space integrals with Fermi-Dirac distributions for electrons and positrons at given stellar temperatures and densities.

## Key numbers
- (Anti)neutrino cooling rates from pn-QRPA are smaller than IPM rates by up to two orders of magnitude at low core temperatures; the two calculations compare well at T = 30 GK.
- For even-even Fe isotopes (56Fe, 58Fe, 62Fe), pn-QRPA cooling rates are smaller than LSSM rates by up to 2 orders of magnitude.
- For odd-A Fe isotopes, pn-QRPA cooling rates are larger than LSSM rates by up to an order of magnitude.
- pn-QRPA computed (anti)neutrino cooling rates are larger than GXPF1J rates by up to 2 orders of magnitude.
- For 55Fe and 56Fe, pn-QRPA gamma heating rates are larger than GXPF1J rates by up to 3 orders of magnitude at high temperatures and densities.
- Gamma heating rates do not change for core densities in the range rho = 10 to 10^6 g/cm^3; at higher densities they increase by orders of magnitude.
- For 55Fe at T9 = 1 GK and rho*Ye = 10^1 g/cm^3, the pn-QRPA neutrino cooling rate is 4.63E-09 MeV/s, compared to IPM 1.72E-10 MeV/s and LSSM 1.67E-10 MeV/s.
- For 56Fe at T9 = 30 GK and rho*Ye = 10^11 g/cm^3, the pn-QRPA neutrino cooling rate is 8.53E+06 MeV/s, compared to IPM 3.08E+06 MeV/s, LSSM 7.03E+05 MeV/s, and GXPF1J 8.85E+04 MeV/s.

## Context
This work extends previous pn-QRPA calculations of neutrino cooling rates for 54,55,56Fe (Nabi, 2012) to a larger set of Fe isotopes (55-58 and 61-63) and includes gamma-ray heating rates not computed in earlier IPM (Fuller et al., 1982) or LSSM (Langanke et al., 2000) studies. The selected isotopes appear among the top 30 most important weak-interaction nuclei for presupernova evolution according to a recent simulation (Nabi et al., 2021). The results are compared with IPM, LSSM, and a recent shell model calculation using the GXPF1J interaction (Moriya et al., 2020) that included screening effects. The reported energy rates may contribute to more realistic simulations of presupernova evolution of massive stars, particularly for modeling the lepton fraction and entropy evolution during core collapse.
