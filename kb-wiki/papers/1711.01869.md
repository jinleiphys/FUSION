# Observation of deconfinement in a cold dense quark medium

> In this paper we study the confinement/deconfinement transition in lattice $SU(2)$ QCD at finite quark density and zero temperature. The simulations are performed on an $32^4$ lattice with rooted staggered fermions at a lattice spacing $a = 0.044 \mathrm{~fm}$. This small lattice spacing allowed us to reach very large baryon density (up to quark chemical potential $μ_q > 2000 \mathrm{~MeV}$) avoiding strong lattice artifacts. In the region $μ_q\sim 1000 \mathrm{~MeV}$ we observe for the first time the confinement/deconfinement transition which manifests itself in rising of the Polyakov loop and vanishing of the string tension $σ$. After the deconfinement is achieved at $μ_q > 1000 \mathrm{~MeV}$, we observe a monotonous decrease of the spatial string tension $σ_s$ which ends up with $σ_s$ vanishing at $μ_q > 2000 \mathrm{~MeV}$. From this observation we draw the conclusion that the confinement/deconfinement transition at finite density and zero temperature is quite different from that at finite temperature and zero density. Our results indicate that in very dense matter the quark-gluon plasma is in essence a weakly interacting gas of quarks and gluons without a magnetic screening mass in the system, sharply different from a quark-gluon plasma at large temperature.

## Key claim
The paper reports the first observation of a confinement/deconfinement transition in cold dense quark matter, occurring at a quark chemical potential around 1000 MeV, but the exact transition point lies within a range of 850 to 1100 MeV where the data cannot distinguish confinement from deconfinement.

## Method
Lattice simulations of SU(2) QCD with rooted staggered fermions (Nf=2) were performed on a 32^4 lattice at a spacing of a = 0.044 fm, using the Symanzik improved gauge action. The Polyakov loop, static quark-antiquark potential from Wilson loops, and spatial string tension were measured as functions of quark chemical potential up to values exceeding 2000 MeV, with a diquark source term (lambda = 0.00075) included to control finite-volume effects.

## Key numbers
- Lattice spacing a = 0.044 fm, with string tension at zero chemical potential sqrt(sigma_0) = 476(5) MeV
- Pion mass m_pi = 740(40) MeV, with m_pi * L_s approximately 5
- Confinement/deconfinement transition occurs in the range mu_q in (850, 1100) MeV, estimated at mu_q approximately 1000 MeV
- Spatial string tension sigma_s vanishes at mu_q > 2000 MeV
- Quark chemical potential reached up to mu_q > 2000 MeV, the largest value ever simulated in SU(2) QCD

## Context
This work extends previous lattice studies of two-color QCD at finite density by Braguta (2016) which reached mu_q up to 800-1000 MeV but found no deconfinement signatures. The use of a much smaller lattice spacing (0.044 fm versus 0.112 fm) allowed access to higher densities while avoiding saturation artifacts. The results contrast sharply with the finite-temperature deconfinement transition, where spatial string tension rises with temperature rather than vanishing, and suggest that at sufficiently high density the quark-gluon plasma becomes a weakly interacting gas without magnetic screening, unlike the high-temperature plasma. The authors conjecture that the same mechanism would produce deconfinement in SU(3) QCD at mu_q / sqrt(sigma_0) approximately 2.1.
