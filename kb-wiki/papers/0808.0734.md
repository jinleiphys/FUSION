# From Equilibrium to Transport Properties of Strongly Correlated Fermi Liquids

> We summarize recent results regarding the equilibrium and non-equilibrium behavior of cold dilute atomic gases in the limit in which the two body scattering length a goes to infinity. In this limit the system is described by a Galilean invariant (non-relativistic) conformal field theory. We discuss the low energy effective lagrangian appropriate to the limit a->infinity, and compute low energy coefficients using an epsilon-expansion. We also show how to combine the effective lagrangian with kinetic theory in order to compute the shear viscosity, and compare the kinetic theory predictions to experimental results extracted from the damping of collective modes in trapped Fermi gases.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shear viscosity of a cold Fermi gas at unitarity can be computed using a combination of effective field theory and kinetic theory, and that experimental measurements of collective mode damping provide an upper bound on the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio near the conjectured quantum lower bound, though the low-temperature kinetic theory prediction does not match the data because the mean free path exceeds the system size.

## Method

The author develops a low-energy effective Lagrangian for the Goldstone boson (phonon) field in the superfluid phase of a Fermi gas at unitarity, with coefficients determined by an epsilon expansion around d=4-epsilon spatial dimensions. The shear viscosity is then computed using kinetic theory for two regimes: low temperature (phonon-dominated transport, using the effective Lagrangian to obtain phonon scattering amplitudes) and high temperature (atom-dominated transport). Experimental shear viscosity values are extracted from damping rates of the radial breathing mode in trapped Fermi gases measured by the Duke group (Kinast 2005), using hydrodynamic relations that connect the damping rate to the viscosity-to-entropy-density ratio.

## Key numbers

* Universal parameter ξ = μ/E_F computed at NLO in the epsilon expansion: ξ = 0.475 (from matching equation (c0) and the NLO result for ξ)
* Critical temperature for superfluidity: k_B T_c = α E_F with α ≃ 0.15 (from Quantum Monte Carlo calculations and experimental results)
* Low-temperature kinetic theory prediction for shear viscosity: η/s = 7.7 × 10^(-6) ξ^5 (T_F^8 / T^8), where ξ is the universal parameter
* High-temperature kinetic theory prediction: η/s = (45π^(3/2))/(64√2) (T/T_F)^(3/2) [log((3√π/4)(T^(3/2)/T_F^(3/2))) + 5/2]^(-1)
* Experimental minimum of η/s near T/T_F ≃ 0.2: η/s ∼ 1/2 (interpreted as an upper bound)
* Conjectured quantum lower bound: η/s = 1/(4π) (Kovtun 2004)
* Neutron-neutron scattering length: a_nn = -18 fm, effective range r_nn = 2.8 fm

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Son (2005) and the epsilon expansion method of Nishida (2006) to compute transport properties of the unitary Fermi gas. The results are compared to experimental damping measurements from the Duke group (Kinast 2005) and thermodynamic data from Kinast (2005) and Luo (2006). The paper enables future work on holographic descriptions of non-relativistic conformal field theories (Son 2008, Balasubramanian 2008) and suggests further theoretical development using epsilon expansions and large N methods for the regime near T_c.
