# Nuclear Charge Radii and Electric Quadrupole Moments

> Isotope shifts of the mean square radii (MSR) and electric quadrupole moments of even-even nuclei with 20< Z < 98$ are calculated using a dynamical microscopic model. A single particle Nilsson potential with the Seo set of correction term parameters, the pairing forces in the BCS formalism and a long range interaction in the local approximation are used. A collective hamiltonian is obtained using a generator coordinate method (GCM) with the gaussian overlap approximation (GOA). A potential energy of the nucleus consists of a microscopic-macroscopic Strutinsky energy and a zero point vibrational term. A liquid droplet model is used as the macroscopic part of the potential. A BCS wave function is taken as a generator function and two collective variables, quadrupole and hexadecapole deformations, serve as the generator coordinates.

## Key claim

The authors propose that the nuclear charge radius depends less strongly on neutron number than previously assumed, and they derive a new empirical formula for the spherical nuclear radius that reduces the sum of squared errors in mean-square-radius isotope shifts by more than a factor of 10 compared to the traditional \(R_0 = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm formula, but this conclusion is qualified by the exclusion of light nuclei (Z < 36) from the fit and by the use of a macroscopic uniform-charge approximation rather than the full dynamical model.

## Method

The authors used a dynamical microscopic model combining a Nilsson single-particle potential (with the Seo parameter set), BCS pairing, and a long-range interaction in the local approximation. A collective Hamiltonian was obtained via the generator coordinate method with the gaussian overlap approximation, using quadrupole (\(\varepsilon\)) and hexadecapole (\(\varepsilon_4\)) deformations as generator coordinates. The potential energy included a microscopic-macroscopic Strutinsky shell correction with a liquid-droplet macroscopic part and a zero-point vibrational term. For the isospin-dependence analysis, the authors fitted the macroscopic uniform-charge mean-square radii (using microscopic equilibrium deformations) to experimental isotope-shift data for 220 even-even nuclei with Z \(\geq\) 36, minimizing the sum of squared discrepancies to find optimal parameters in the formula \(R_0 = r_0 [1 - \alpha ((N-Z)/A)^p] A^{1/3}\).

## Key numbers

* The optimal spherical nuclear radius formula is \(R_0 = 1.25\,(1 - 0.2 \cdot (N-Z)/A)\, A^{1/3}\) fm, obtained from fitting 220 even-even nuclei with Z \(\geq\) 36.
* The sum of squared errors \(\Sigma^2\) for this formula is 2.795, compared to 29.285 for the traditional formula \(R_0 = 1.2 A^{1/3}\) fm, a reduction by more than a factor of 10.
* The harmonic oscillator frequency used is \(\hbar\omega_0 = 40 \cdot A^{-1/3}\) MeV, with the isospin dependence omitted.
* For the neutron-rich and neutron-deficient region (63 isotopes), the best-fit parameters were \(r_0 = 1.2\) fm, \(\alpha = 0.205\), \(p = 1\); for all three regions combined (220 isotopes) the best fit gave \(r_0 = 1.2\) fm, \(\alpha = 0.203\), \(p = 1\).
* The pairing strengths G (in units of \(\hbar\omega_0\)) for different mass regions are: 0.26 (A\(_{av}\) = 44 and 100), 0.29 (A\(_{av}\) = 126), 0.275 (A\(_{av}\) = 165), and 0.284 (A\(_{av}\) = 217).

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Nerlo-Pomorska, Pomorski, and Skorupska-Mach (1993) for Sr-W isotopes and Nerlo-Pomorska and Pomorski (1993) for the macroscopic isospin dependence, covering all experimentally known even-even nuclei from Z = 20 to Z = 98. The proposed radius formula provides a simple macroscopic correction that can be incorporated into more sophisticated models (e.g., with Woods-Saxon potentials) and is expected to affect predictions for nuclear masses, heavy-ion collisions, and Coulomb interactions in droplet models. The authors note that the model fails to reproduce the kink at N = 82 in Xe and Ba isotopes and the parabolic behavior of Ca isotopes, and they identify the need for including nonaxial \(\gamma\) degrees of freedom (for nuclei near N = 50) and octupole \(\varepsilon_3\) deformations (for Ra-Th nuclei) in future work.
