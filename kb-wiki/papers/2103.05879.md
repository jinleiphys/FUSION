# Parametric resonance of complex scalar field under spacetime oscillations

> In this proceeding, we study time evolution of a complex scalar field, in symmetry broken phase, in presence of oscillating spacetime metric background. We show that spacetime oscillations lead to parametric resonance of the field. This generates excitations in the field for a wide range of frequency of spacetime oscillations which ultimately lead to the formation of topological vortices. The lowest frequency cut-off to induce this phenomena is set by system size due to finite size effects.

## Key claim

Spacetime oscillations can induce parametric resonance in a complex scalar field in the symmetry-broken phase, leading to topological vortex formation, but only if the initial field configuration already contains some fluctuations (e.g., thermal or quantum). The lowest frequency cutoff for this phenomenon is set by the system size due to finite size effects, not by the field mass.

## Method

The authors numerically solved the equation of motion for a complex scalar field on a spacetime with an oscillating inverse metric \(g^{\mu\nu} = \mathrm{diag}(-1, 1-h, 1+h, 1)\), where \(h = \varepsilon \sin(\omega(t-z))\). They used a second-order Leapfrog method on a 2D lattice (assuming no variation along \(z\)) with periodic boundary conditions, starting from an initial configuration containing only transverse fluctuations.

## Key numbers

- The lowest frequency cutoff to induce vortex formation is \(\omega_L \sim 4\pi / L\), where \(L\) is the system size.
- For \(\omega = 100\ \Lambda^{-1}\) and \(\omega = 20\ \Lambda^{-1}\), with \(\varepsilon = 0.4\), \(\Phi_0 = 10\ \Lambda^{-1}\), and \(\lambda = 40\), the field mass is \(m_\Phi = \Phi_0 \sqrt{2\lambda} \approx 56.6\ \Lambda^{-1}\).
- Lattice spacing used: \(\Delta x = \Delta y = 0.01\ \Lambda\); time step: \(\Delta t = 0.005\ \Lambda\).
- For sufficiently large \(L\omega/4\pi\), the number of oscillation cycles before first vortex-antivortex pair formation (NoC) is roughly constant; NoC diverges when \(L\omega/4\pi \sim 1\).

## Context

This work extends the study of parametric resonance as a mechanism for topological defect formation, previously investigated for periodically oscillating heat-bath temperature by Reso (year not given) and Kink (year not given). It builds on the authors' own prior work (Dave and Digal, 2021, ref. [sto]) showing that spacetime oscillations can induce such phenomena. The results enable future studies of vortex formation in early-universe or condensed-matter systems where spacetime or metric oscillations occur, and clarify the role of finite system size in setting the frequency threshold.
