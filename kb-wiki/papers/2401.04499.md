# Jet medium modifications

> Since the start of the heavy-ion collision programs at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and the Large Hadron Collider, the study of jet modifications resulting from their interactions with the produced QCD matter has provided a unique tool to investigate and characterize the properties of the quark-gluon plasma. In this mini-overview, I will present the recent theoretical advancements in describing and understanding the modifications of jets within a QCD medium.

## Key claim

Recent theoretical advancements have enabled evaluation of the BDMPS-Z medium-induced gluon spectrum beyond simplified scenarios (opacity expansion, harmonic oscillator approximation, infinite static medium), but for some energy loss observables the differences among various approaches may be absorbed into fitted values of free parameters such as the strong coupling constant.

## Method

The paper reviews analytical and numerical progress in computing the BDMPS-Z spectrum for medium-induced radiation, including evaluations beyond the first-order opacity expansion and harmonic oscillator approximation for an idealized static QGP. It also discusses extensions to double-differential splitting spectra beyond the soft limit (finite momentum fraction z) using opacity expansion and semi-hard approximations, as well as sub-eikonal corrections for transverse flow and density gradients.

## Key numbers

- For 0-5% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV, fitted strong coupling values for different collision kernel models yield alpha_s = 0.28 (LO), alpha_s = 0.24 (NLO), and alpha_s = 0.26 (matched to infrared lattice), making charged hadron R_AA curves indistinguishable.
- The jet quenching parameter q-hat in pre-hydrodynamization stages (glasma and kinetic theory phases) is found to be comparable to or larger than in the QGP phase, with anisotropy showing larger magnitude along the beam direction.

## Context

This mini-overview extends the BDMPS-Z framework (Baier 1996, Zakharov 1996) and opacity expansion (Gyulassy 1999) by reviewing recent work that quantifies kinematic ranges of validity for idealized static QGP (Andres 2020) and incorporates collision rates with proper infrared matching (Moore 2019, Schlichting 2021). It enables future benchmarking of jet quenching Monte Carlo simulations (e.g., MARTINI) against first-principles QCD calculations, and motivates study of more differential observables beyond single-inclusive hadron R_AA to distinguish between theoretical approaches.
