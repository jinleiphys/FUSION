# One-loop calculations of hyperon polarizabilities under the large N_c consistency condition

> The spin-averaged electromagnetic polarizabilities of the hyperons $Λ$ and $Σ$ are calculated within the one-loop approximation by use of the dispersion theory. The photon and meson couplings to hyperons are determined so as to satisfy the large N_c consistency condition. It is shown that in order for the large N_c consistency condition to hold exotic hyperon states such as $Σ^{**}$ with I=2 and J=3/2 are required in the calculation of the magnetic polarizability of the $Σ$ state.

# One-loop calculations of hyperon polarizabilities under the large N_c consistency condition

## Key claim

The spin-averaged electromagnetic polarizabilities of the hyperons Λ and Σ are calculated within the one-loop approximation using dispersion theory, but the results are "too large" compared to nucleon polarizabilities from the same method, and the magnetic polarizability calculation for the Σ state requires exotic hyperon states such as Σ^{**} with I=2 and J=3/2 to satisfy the large N_c consistency condition.

## Method

The authors compute the spin-averaged electromagnetic polarizabilities of Λ and Σ hyperons by applying dispersion relations to the Compton scattering amplitude, where the imaginary parts are given by the Born terms of pion and kaon photoproduction amplitudes. The photon and meson coupling constants to hyperons are determined using spin-flavor symmetry relations that satisfy the large N_c consistency condition, with the bound kaon-soliton model (BKSM) providing explicit predictions for the exotic state couplings and masses. The magnetic Born terms are constructed from isovector magnetic moment contributions including Σ, Σ^*, and the exotic Σ^{**} states, with the kaon magnetic Born terms additionally requiring exotic Ξ^{**}_1 and Ξ^{**}_3 states.

## Key numbers

- Electric polarizabilities (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3): α_Λ = 18.05, α_{Σ^+} = 22.08, α_{Σ^0} = 13.79, α_{Σ^-} = 18.71
- Magnetic polarizabilities (in units of 10^{-4} fm^3): β_Λ = 3.22, β_{Σ^+} = 6.67, β_{Σ^0} = 5.52, β_{Σ^-} = 7.13
- Pseudovector pion coupling constant: |f_{ΣΛπ}|/√(4π) = 0.22, close to the empirical value 0.20 ± 0.01
- Pseudovector kaon coupling constant: f_{ΛpK}/√(4π) = 0.92, while the empirical value is 0.89 ± 0.10
- Narrow width approximation for magnetic polarizability from Σ^* only: β^M_Λ|_{Σ^*} = 6.13 × 10^{-4} fm^3, β^M_{Σ^±}|_{Σ^*} = 2.15 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Narrow width approximation for magnetic polarizability from Σ^{**} only: β^M_{Σ^±}|_{Σ^{**}} = 3.78 × 10^{-4} fm^3, β^M_{Σ^0}|_{Σ^{**}} = 5.04 × 10^{-4} fm^3
- Exotic state masses from BKSM: Σ^{**}(I=2, J=3/2) = 1517 MeV, Ξ^{**}_1(I=3/2, J=1/2) = 1444 MeV, Ξ^{**}_3(I=3/2, J=3/2) = 1639 MeV
- Electric dipole polarizabilities from Λ^*(1405) contributions: α^D_Λ = 0.18 × 10^{-4} fm^3, α^D_{Σ^0} = 0.24 × 10^{-4} fm^3, using transition dipole moments κ_{Λ^*Λ} = κ_{Λ^*Σ} = 0.41 in Bohr magnetons
- Σ^* decay widths: Γ_{Σ^*}^{Λπ} = 44.8 MeV, Γ_{Σ^*}^{Σπ} = 4.7 MeV; Σ^{**} decay width: Γ_{Σ^{**}}^{Σπ} = 138 MeV

## Context

This work extends the dispersion-theoretic approach to hyperon polarizabilities previously applied to nucleon polarizabilities by Lvov (1998) and Tanushi, Saito, and Uehara (1999), and contrasts with the SU(3) extension of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory (HBChPT) results by Bernard et al. (1992) and the bound-state soliton model calculations by Gobbi et al. (1996). The authors note that their calculated spin-averaged polarizabilities are "too large" compared to nucleon values from the same one-loop method and from HBChPT by Hemmert et al. (1997), indicating that vertex corrections or unitarization beyond the one-loop level are needed. The necessity of exotic hyperon states for the large N_c consistency condition is shared with the large N_c baryon theories of Dashen and Jenkins (1994, 1998), and the mass estimates for these exotic states are consistent with the tree-level mass formula in large N_c chiral perturbation theory by Oh and Weise.
