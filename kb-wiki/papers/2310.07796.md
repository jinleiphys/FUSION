# Light quark mass dependence of nucleon electromagnetic form factors in dispersively modified chiral perturbation theory

> The nucleon isovector electromagnetic form factors are calculated up to next-to-next-to-leading order by combining relativistic chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) of pion, nucleon, and $Δ$(1232) with dispersion theory. We specifically address the light-quark mass dependence of the form factors, achieving a good description of recent Lattice QCD results over a range of $Q^2 < 0.6$ GeV$^2$ and $M_π < 350$ MeV. For the Dirac form factor, the combination of ChPT and dispersion theory outperforms the pure dispersive and pure ChPT descriptions. For the Pauli form factor, the combined calculation leads to results comparable to the purely dispersive ones. The anomalous magnetic moment and the Dirac and Pauli radii are extracted.

## Key claim

The paper claims that combining relativistic chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) with dispersion theory for the two-pion channel provides a good description of lattice QCD results for nucleon isovector electromagnetic form factors over a range of \(Q^2 < 0.6\) GeV\(^2\) and pion masses \(M_\pi < 350\) MeV, but this success is qualified by the fact that for the Pauli form factor the combined calculation yields results comparable to purely dispersive ones rather than outperforming them.

## Method

The authors calculate nucleon isovector electromagnetic form factors up to next-to-next-to-leading order by merging relativistic ChPT (including pion, nucleon, and \(\Delta(1232)\) degrees of freedom) with dispersion theory for the two-pion intermediate state. The dispersive treatment uses a Muskhelishvili-Omnès equation for the \(N\bar{N} \to 2\pi\) p-wave amplitudes, with the pion-pion phase shift and its pion-mass dependence obtained via the inverse amplitude method. The ChPT calculation employs the extended on mass shell (EOMS) renormalisation scheme and the small-scale expansion, treating the baryon mass difference \(\delta = m_\Delta - m_N\) as \(\mathcal{O}(p)\).

## Key numbers

* The calculation achieves a good description of lattice QCD results over a range of \(Q^2 < 0.6\) GeV\(^2\) and \(M_\pi < 350\) MeV.
* The cutoff for the dispersive integral is set to \(\Lambda = 1.8\) GeV.
* The chiral-limit nucleon mass is taken as \(\mathring{m} = 0.855\) GeV, the chiral-limit \(\Delta\) mass as \(\mathring{m}_\Delta = 1.166\) GeV, and the chiral-limit pion decay constant as \(\mathring{F}_\pi = 0.0856\) GeV.
* The axial coupling is set to \(\mathring{g}_A = g_A^{\text{phys}} = 1.2754\), and the \(\pi N\Delta\) coupling is \(h_A \approx 3\mathring{g}_A/(2\sqrt{2}) = 1.35\).
* The baryon mass difference is \(\delta = m_\Delta - m_N \sim 300\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends prior ChPT calculations of nucleon form factors (e.g., Fuchs (2003), Bauer (2012)) by incorporating the \(\rho\)-meson physics via dispersion theory rather than through explicit vector meson degrees of freedom, which avoids phenomenological models like vector-meson dominance. The approach enables a systematic, model-independent treatment of the two-pion channel's contribution to the form factors, and the resulting framework can be used to extrapolate lattice QCD results to the physical point and to study the quark-mass dependence of nucleon electromagnetic structure.
