# Search for delays between unlike particle emissions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

> The possibilities of unlike particle correlations for a study of particle delays and spatial shifts in relativistic heavy ion collisions are demonstrated. This report represents the unpublished Ref. [4] in the paper by S. Voloshin et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf 79} (1977) 4766; the report abstract was submitted to the conference Quark Matter'97.

# Search for delays between unlike particle emissions in relativistic heavy-ion collisions

## Key claim

Unlike particle correlations can directly access information about space-time asymmetries of particle production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions, but the separation of relative time delays from spatial asymmetry is possible in principle only in a model-dependent way due to the velocity dependence of the out asymmetry.

## Method

The authors used the RQMD (v2.3) transport code to simulate 158 A·GeV Pb+Pb central collisions and calculated two-particle correlation functions for unlike particle pairs (π⁺,K⁺), (π⁺,p), and (K⁺,p) using a final state interaction code. They restricted the analysis to the mid-rapidity region |y|<1 and computed correlation functions in the longitudinally co-moving system (LCMS), selecting pairs with q_x positive or negative to probe the asymmetry in the relative emission coordinates.

## Key numbers

- Bohr radii for the three unlike particle systems: 248.5 fm for π⁺p, 222.5 fm for K⁺p, and 83.6 fm for π⁺K⁺
- For the π⁺p system, the ratio R₊/R₋ is less than unity at small q, with the effect determined mainly by the x-asymmetry
- For the π⁺K⁺ system, the ratio R₊/R₋ is less than unity at small q, with both x- and time-asymmetries contributing and the time contribution being somewhat larger
- For the K⁺p system, the ratio R₊/R₋ practically coincides with unity at small q, due to near-complete compensation of space and time asymmetries leading to Δx* ≈ 0

## Context

This work extends the suggestion by Nagamiya (1982) that freeze-out sequence follows photons, K⁺, protons, and pions based on free-space cross-section arguments, and the transport model confirmation that protons appear emitted latest. The method builds on the formalism of Lednicky (1996) for accessing asymmetry information through the scalar product q·r* in the two-particle correlation function. The results enable future studies at SPS, RHIC, and LHC to probe phase transition effects such as quark-gluon plasma production or strangeness distillation through measured freeze-out delays.
