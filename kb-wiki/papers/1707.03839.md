# Hard processes in small systems

> These proceedings from the Quark Matter 2017 conference give an overview of the latest experimental results on hard processes in small collision systems at RHIC and the LHC, discuss their implications, and consider several prospects for future measurements.

## Key claim

These proceedings from the Quark Matter 2017 conference give an overview of the latest experimental results on hard processes in small collision systems at RHIC and the LHC, discuss their implications, and consider several prospects for future measurements.

## Method

The paper reviews experimental measurements of high transverse momentum jets, hadrons, and electroweak probes in small collision systems such as proton-nucleus (p+A) collisions at RHIC and the LHC. It organizes the discussion around three components of such collisions: the initial state of the nucleus, the initial state of the proton, and the final state of the system. The author emphasizes results that are new since the previous Quark Matter conference, places them into context with other experimental data and theoretical ideas, and suggests avenues for future work.

## Key numbers

* Charged particle production rates in 5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions show only a modest enhancement in the anti-shadowing region, pT approximately 40-80 GeV, and are otherwise consistent at lower pT with the initial measurement by ALICE.
* The 8.16 TeV p+Pb dataset collected in late 2016 has a luminosity of 170 nb-1 compared to 31 nb-1 for the 5.02 TeV p+Pb data collected in 2013.
* The systematic uncertainty in existing measurements of the photon production cross-section in 8 TeV pp collisions reaches approximately 2% for 80-300 GeV photons at mid-rapidity, including luminosity uncertainties.
* The hadron RAA measured by CMS is suppressed by 30% in 70-90% Pb+Pb collisions, which have an average of 11 participating nucleons.
* The azimuthal anisotropy v2 for pT = 10 GeV particles in the most central 1% of 5.02 TeV p+Pb events is approximately 0.05.
* The 8.16 TeV p+Pb data is expected to contain more than ten thousand photons with pT-gamma > 40 GeV in the 1% highest-multiplicity events.

## Context

This proceedings paper reviews and synthesizes results presented at the Quark Matter 2017 conference, extending earlier work on hard processes in small systems from previous conferences. The paper discusses how updated measurements using directly-measured 5.02 TeV pp reference data from 2015 have clarified earlier results that relied on extrapolated references with large uncertainties, such as those from ATLAS (2016) and CMS (2016) on charged particle production. The improved data are now being used in the next generation of global nPDF extractions such as the EPPS16 analysis by Eskola (2016), and the paper suggests that the high-luminosity 8.16 TeV p+Pb data will enable precision electroweak measurements including top quark production in heavy ion collisions for the first time.
