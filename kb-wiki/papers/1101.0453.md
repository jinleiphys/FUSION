# Sigma_c Dbar and Lambda_c Dbar states in a chiral quark model

> The S-wave Sigma_c Dbar and Lambda_c Dbar states with isospin I=1/2 and spin S=1/2 are dynamically investigated within the framework of a chiral constituent quark model by solving a resonating group method (RGM) equation. The results show that the interaction between Sigma_c and Dbar is attractive, which consequently results in a Sigma_c Dbar bound state with the binding energy of about 5-42 MeV, unlike the case of Lambda_c Dbar state, which has a repulsive interaction and thus is unbound. The channel coupling effect of Sigma_c Dbar and Lambda_c Dbar is found to be negligible due to the fact that the gap between the Sigma_c Dbar and Lambda_c Dbar thresholds is relatively large and the Sigma_c Dbar and Lambda_c Dbar transition interaction is weak.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a $\Sigma_c \bar D$ bound state exists with a binding energy of about 5-42 MeV, but this result depends on the specific chiral quark model used (Model I, II, or III) and the form of the confinement potential (linear or quadratic), with the binding energy varying significantly across these choices.

## Method

The authors performed a dynamical coupled-channel study of the $S$-wave $\Sigma_c \bar D$ and $\Lambda_c \bar D$ systems with isospin $I=1/2$ and spin $S=1/2$ by solving the resonating group method (RGM) equation within a chiral constituent quark model. The model includes one-gluon exchange, confinement potential, and boson exchanges (scalar, pseudoscalar, and vector nonets) for light quarks, while heavy-light and heavy-heavy quark interactions are described by OGE and confinement only. Parameters for light quarks were fixed by fitting baryon ground state energies, deuteron binding energy, and $NN$ scattering phase shifts, while charm quark parameters were determined by fitting masses of $\Sigma_c$, $\Lambda_c$, $D$, $D^*$, $J/\psi$, and $\eta_c$.

## Key numbers

* $\Sigma_c \bar D$ bound state binding energy: 9.3-41.6 MeV for linear confinement across Models I, II, III with charm quark masses $m_c = 1.43$, $1.55$, and $1.87$ GeV.
* $\Sigma_c \bar D$ bound state binding energy: 4.5-11.3 MeV for quadratic confinement across the same models and charm quark masses.
* $\Sigma_c \bar D$ bound state energy: 4.279-4.316 GeV (corresponding to the binding energies above, with $\Sigma_c \bar D$ threshold at 4.321 GeV).
* $\Sigma_c \bar D$ and $\Lambda_c \bar D$ threshold gap: 166 MeV.
* $\Sigma K$ and $\Lambda K$ threshold gap (for comparison): 78 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the chiral SU(3) quark model, previously applied to light-flavor systems such as $\Sigma K$ and $\Lambda K$ by Huang et al. (2005), to the charm sector by studying $\Sigma_c \bar D$ and $\Lambda_c \bar D$ states. The results are qualitatively similar to those from a coupled-channel unitary approach by Wu et al. (2010), which also found a $\Sigma_c \bar D$ bound state at 4.269 GeV (about 52 MeV below threshold), though with a larger binding energy. The study enables future experimental searches for a hidden-charm baryon state around 4.3 GeV, which would exclude a three-light-quark ($qqq$) interpretation and require either a five-quark ($qqqc\bar c$) or baryon-meson molecular configuration.
