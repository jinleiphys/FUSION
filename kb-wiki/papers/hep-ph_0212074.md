# Drum vortons in high density QCD

> Recently it was shown that high density QCD supports of number of topological defects. In particular, there are U(1)_Y strings that arise due to K^0 condensation that occurs when the strange quark mass is relatively large. The unique feature of these strings is that they possess a nonzero K^+ condensate that is trapped on the core. In the following we will show that these strings (with nontrivial core structure) can form closed loops with conserved charge and currents trapped on the string worldsheet. The presence of conserved charges allows these topological defects, called vortons, to carry angular momentum, which makes them classically stable objects. We also give arguments demonstrating that vortons carry angular momentum very efficiently (in terms of energy per unit angular momentum) such that they might be the important degrees of freedom in the cores of neutron stars.

## Key claim

The authors argue that closed loops of superconducting $K^0$ strings (with a $K^+$ condensate trapped on the core) in the CFL$+K^0$ phase of high-density QCD can form classically stable objects called vortons, provided the time-dependent phase $\omega$ and the spatial winding $k$ satisfy specific constraints that prevent the $K^+$ condensate from being quenched. The stability is guaranteed by conservation of both a topological charge and a Noether charge, rather than by fine-tuning.

## Method

The authors start from the effective Lagrangian for the CFL$+K^0$ phase of high-density QCD, expanded to fourth order in the $K^0$ and $K^+$ fields. They take the known static superconducting string solution (with a $K^0$ winding and a $K^+$ condensate localized on the core) and add a time-dependent phase $e^{-i\omega t}$ and a spatial phase $e^{i k z}$ to the $K^+$ field, where $z$ runs along the string loop. They then derive the effective 2D transverse Lagrangian, compute the constraints on $\omega$ and $k$ that keep the $K^+$ condensate energetically favorable on the core, and estimate the allowed parameter range using variational calculations with the ansatz functions $f(r) \approx 1-e^{-\beta r}$ and $g(r) \approx e^{-\kappa r}(1+\kappa r)$.

## Key numbers

- The strange quark mass is taken as $m_s \simeq 150\ \mathrm{MeV}$, with $m_u \neq m_d \ll m_s$.
- The typical scale for $\omega$ and $k$ is set by $\mu_{\mathrm{eff}} \sim m_s^2/(2\mu) \sim 30\ \mathrm{MeV}$.
- Superconducting strings exist for the effective frequency $\tilde{\omega} = \omega + \mu_{\mathrm{eff}}$ in the range from 25 to 45 MeV.
- The approximate degeneracy condition $\tilde{\omega}^2 - v_\pi^2 k^2 - m_+^2 \approx \mu_{\mathrm{eff}}^2 - m_0^2$ relates $\omega$ and $k$ at the scale of $\mu_{\mathrm{eff}}$.
- The inverse widths of the string core and condensate are $\beta \simeq \sqrt{\mu_{\mathrm{eff}}^2 - m_0^2}$ and $\kappa \simeq \delta m$, where $\delta m^2 = (m_+^2 - m_0^2)/2 = (a/2) m_s (m_d - m_u)$.

## Context

This work extends the earlier construction of superconducting $K$ strings in the CFL$+K^0$ phase of high-density QCD (Buckley, Metlitski, Zhitnitsky, 2002; earlier papers by the same group) by adding time dependence to produce vortons, following the general formalism developed for cosmic strings by Witten (1985) and by Davis and Shellard (1988-1989). The authors note that simple "spring" configurations (with only a spatial current) are unstable in most of parameter space due to quenching of the condensate, as shown by Turner (1988) and Davis and Shellard (1988). The vorton construction avoids this by using a time-dependent phase that counteracts the quenching, making stable loops possible over a much larger region of parameter space. The paper suggests that such vortons could be important degrees of freedom in neutron star cores, carrying angular momentum efficiently, but does not address formation rates, weak/electromagnetic interactions, or finite-temperature effects.
