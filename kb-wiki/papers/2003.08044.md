# Two-neutron halo structure of $^{31}$F and a novel pairing anti-halo effect

> Background: A newly identified dripline nucleus $^{31}$F offers a unique opportunity to study the two-neutron ($2n$) correlation at the east shore of the island of inversion where the $N = 28$ shell closure is lost. Purpose: We aim to present the first three-body theoretical results for the radius and total reaction cross sections of $^{31}$F. This will further help to investigate how the pairing and breakdown of the $N = 28$ shell closure influence the formation of the $2n$-halo structure and the anti-halo effect in this mass region. Methods: A $^{29}$F$+n+n$ three-body system is described by the cluster orbital shell model, and its total reaction cross section is calculated by the Glauber theory. Results: Our three-body calculations predict 3.48-3.70 fm for the root-mean-square radius of $^{31}$F, which corresponds to the total reaction cross section of 1530 (1410)-1640 (1500) mb for a carbon target at 240 (900) MeV/nucleon. The binding mechanism and halo formation in $^{31}$F are discussed. Conclusions: The present study suggests a novel anti-halo effect in this mass region: When the pairing overcome the energy gap between the $p_{3/2}$ and $f_{7/2}$ orbits, the inversion of the occupation number of these orbits takes place, and it diminishes the $2n$-halo structure.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the two-neutron halo structure of $^{31}$F is determined by a delicate balance between the energy gap of the $p_{3/2}$ and $f_{7/2}$ orbits and the pairing interaction; when the pairing correlation overcomes a small energy gap (below about 0.4 MeV), the valence neutrons occupy the higher-degeneracy $f_{7/2}$ orbit instead of the $p_{3/2}$ orbit, which suppresses the halo formation in a novel "pairing anti-halo effect."

## Method

The authors describe $^{31}$F as a $^{29}$F$+n+n$ three-body system using the cluster orbital shell model (COSM) with a spinless $^{29}$F core assuming $N=20$ shell closure. They generate 12 parameter sets for the $^{29}$F-$n$ Woods-Saxon potential by varying the strength, diffuseness, and radius parameters, ensuring $^{30}$F remains unbound as observed experimentally. The valence neutron interaction uses the Minnesota potential, and the total reaction cross sections are calculated via the Glauber theory with nucleon-target profile functions.

## Key numbers

- Root-mean-square matter radius of $^{31}$F: 3.48 to 3.70 fm (range across 12 parameter sets).
- Two-neutron separation energy $S_{2n}$ of $^{31}$F: 0.44 to 1.37 MeV.
- Total reaction cross section on carbon target at 240 MeV/nucleon: 1530 mb (largest radius case) and 1410 mb (smallest radius case).
- Total reaction cross section on carbon target at 900 MeV/nucleon: 1640 mb (largest radius case) and 1500 mb (smallest radius case).
- Total reaction cross section on proton target at 240 MeV/nucleon: 551 mb (largest radius case) and 533 mb (smallest radius case).
- Total reaction cross section on proton target at 900 MeV/nucleon: 574 mb (largest radius case) and 556 mb (smallest radius case).
- Core $^{29}$F rms radius: 3.37 fm.
- Energy gap $\Delta\varepsilon$ between $p_{3/2}$ and $f_{7/2}$ orbits: 0.08 to 1.47 MeV across parameter sets.
- Occupation number inversion between $p_{3/2}$ and $f_{7/2}$ orbits occurs at $\Delta\varepsilon \approx 0.40$ MeV.
- Pearson correlation coefficient between radius and energy gap: 0.93 (strong correlation).
- Pearson correlation coefficient between radius and $S_{2n}$: -0.73 (weak correlation).
- Increase in total reaction cross section from $^{29}$F to $^{31}$F: about 15% for the largest radius case, about 5% for the smallest radius case.

## Context

This work extends three-body studies of two-neutron halo nuclei beyond the well-established cases of $^{11}$Li (Tanihata 1985) and $^{22}$C (Tanaka 2010, Togano 2016) into the island of inversion region where the $N=28$ shell closure is broken. It builds on the identification of $^{31}$F as a dripline nucleus (Ahn 2019) and on earlier theoretical indications of its halo structure (Minomo 2020). The novel pairing anti-halo effect contrasts with the standard pairing anti-halo effect discussed for $^{32}$Ne (Hagino 2011), as here the pairing actively suppresses the halo by promoting neutrons to a higher-angular-momentum orbit ($f_{7/2}$) rather than merely modifying the asymptotic wave function. The results enable experimental tests via total reaction cross section measurements on carbon and proton targets, which could distinguish between the halo and non-halo scenarios.
