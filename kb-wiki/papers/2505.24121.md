# Next-to-leading-order prediction for the neutrinoless double-beta decay

> The neutrinoless double-beta decay ($0νββ$) of two neutrons$nn \rightarrow ppee$ is the elementary subprocess of $0νββ$ decay in nuclei. Accurate knowledge of the $nn \rightarrow ppee$ amplitude is required to pin down the short-range contributions in the nuclear matrix elements of the candidate nuclei for large-scale $0νββ$ searches. In this Letter, we report the first next-to-leading-order prediction of the nn \rightarrow ppee amplitude, with Bayesian uncertainty quantification. This is made possible by the development of the relativistic chiral effective field theory, in which no unknown contact term is required up to next-to-leading order. The theory is validated by reproducing in a parameter-free way the available data on the charge independence and charge symmetry breaking contributions in the two-nucleon scattering. The present work makes an essential step towards addressing the uncertainty in the theoretical calculations of the nuclear matrix elements relevant for $0νββ$ searches.

## Key claim

The first next-to-leading-order (NLO) prediction of the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ amplitude is reported, with Bayesian uncertainty quantification; this is possible because no unknown contact term is required up to NLO in the relativistic chiral effective field theory framework, in contrast to the nonrelativistic heavy baryon approach where a logarithmic divergence would require an additional contact term.

## Method

The authors develop a relativistic chiral effective field theory (EFT) to describe the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ process in the $^1S_0$ channel, which dominates at low energies. The strong potential up to NLO includes a momentum-independent contact term, a pion-exchange Yukawa potential, and a momentum-dependent contact term responsible for the effective range correction, with the NLO corrections treated perturbatively on top of the nonperturbatively iterated LO contributions. The renormalized low-energy constants $C_R$ and $D_R$ are determined by fitting to low-energy $np$ scattering data (scattering length and partial-wave cross sections up to $E_{\rm lab}\leq100$ MeV from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis) using Bayesian inference with the Metropolis-Hastings algorithm, and the theory is validated by predicting charge independence breaking (CIB) and charge symmetry breaking (CSB) contributions in nucleon-nucleon scattering without additional free parameters.

## Key numbers

- The NLO prediction for the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ amplitude at kinematics $|\bm p_i|=25$ MeV, $|\bm p_f|=30$ MeV is $|\mathcal{A}_{0\nu}|=0.0209(7)\ {\rm MeV}^{-2}$.
- The $np$ scattering length used to constrain the LECs is $a_{np}^{1S0}=-23.74(2)$ fm.
- The pion mass used is $m_\pi=138.03$ MeV, the neutral pion mass for CIB/CSB calculations is $m_{\pi^0}=134.98$ MeV, the axial coupling is $g_A=1.27$, the pion decay constant is $f_\pi=92.2$ MeV, and the chiral symmetry breakdown scale is $\Lambda_\chi=m_\rho=770$ MeV.
- At $|\bm p_f|=30$ MeV, the nonrelativistic LO result from the generalized Cottingham model ($0.0195(5)$ MeV$^{-2}$) lies outside the 68% Bayesian confidence interval of the NLO prediction and is smaller by about 10%; at $|\bm p_f|=100$ MeV, the difference between NLO relativistic and LO nonrelativistic results is about 50%.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic chiral EFT approach previously developed by Yang and Zhao (2024) for the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ amplitude from leading order to next-to-leading order, and provides the first Bayesian uncertainty quantification for this amplitude. It contrasts with the nonrelativistic approach using the generalized Cottingham model by Cirigliano et al. (2021), which introduces phenomenological inputs for elastic intermediate states and neglects inelastic contributions, and with the nonrelativistic heavy baryon chiral EFT by Cirigliano et al. (2019) which requires an additional contact term at NLO due to logarithmic divergences. The present results enable a more accurate determination of the contact decay operator in nuclear matrix elements for $0\nu\beta\beta$ searches, and have shown encouraging agreement with the first lattice QCD calculation of the $nn\rightarrow ppee$ process by Davoudi et al. (2024) at somewhat heavy pion mass.
