# Systematics of the chemical freeze-out line in the high baryon density regime explored at SIS100

> The systematic uncertainties of chemical freeze-out fits at SIS100 energies (Au+Au reactions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=3-5$ GeV) are studied using UrQMD simulations. Although hadron production in UrQMD does not occur on a sharp chemical freeze-out hyper-surface, the extracted fit quality is shown to be very good. The extracted chemical parameters depend on the selected hadron species as well as the underlying equation of state (EoS) of the matter. Including light nuclei and anti-protons in the fit increases the expected freeze-out temperature, while a stiffer EoS increases the obtained chemical potential. Similarly, the baryon densities extracted by the thermal fits depend on the choice of hadrons as well as the underlying equation of state. These results are important for the upcoming CBM@FAIR physics program and highlight that a degree of caution is advised when one relates the chemical freeze-out curve to features on the QCD phase diagram like the critical endpoint or a possible phase transition.

## Key claim

The chemical freeze-out parameters extracted from thermal fits to UrQMD simulations depend systematically on both the selection of hadron species included in the fit and the underlying equation of state of the matter, meaning that there is no unique, equation-of-state-independent relation between beam energy and the expected freeze-out point in the QCD phase diagram.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD v4.0 transport model to simulate central (0-10%) Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energies from 2 to 6 GeV, running both a cascade mode (soft equation of state) and a QMD mode with CMF potentials (stiffer equation of state). They then applied the Thermal-FIST hadron resonance gas model in the grand canonical ensemble to fit the simulated hadron yields using three different sets of hadrons: Set 1 (pions, kaons, protons, lambdas, anti-protons, and deuterons), Set 2 (same minus deuterons), and Set 3 (same minus both deuterons and anti-protons). The fit quality was quantified by chi-squared per degree of freedom, and the extracted temperature, baryon chemical potential, and baryon density were compared across the different scenarios.

## Key numbers

- The fit quality (chi-squared per degree of freedom) is mostly below 1 for all scenarios, except when deuterons are included (Set 1) at the lowest and highest beam energies.
- Changes in the underlying equation of state lead to a shift of approximately 10 MeV in the extracted temperature and approximately 50 MeV in the chemical potential.
- The shift in beam energy at which a given chemical freeze-out point is reached, due to using a different equation of state for the fit versus the evolution, is approximately 0.5 GeV in the center-of-mass frame, corresponding to more than 10% of the beam energy.
- The extracted freeze-out points cover the SIS100 energy range of sqrt(s_NN) = 3-5 GeV for Au+Au reactions.

## Context

This work extends previous thermal model fits to experimental data by Cleymans (1998), Becattini (2000, 2016), Andronic (2005), HADES (2010), Lorenz (2014), and Lysenko (2024), which established the chemical freeze-out line across many beam energies. It also builds on the finding by Motornenko (2021) that different sets of hadrons can lead to degenerate minima in thermal fits. The results are directly relevant for the upcoming CBM@FAIR physics program at SIS100 and highlight that caution is needed when relating the chemical freeze-out curve to features of the QCD phase diagram such as the critical endpoint, whose possible location near T=80-120 MeV and mu_B=500-600 MeV has been suggested by Fischer (2014), Fu (2019), Gao (2020), Gunkel (2021), Hippert (2023), Basar (2023), Clarke (2024), Shah (2024), and Ecker (2025).
