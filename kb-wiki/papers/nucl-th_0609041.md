# The statistical hadronization model approach to $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV Au-Au collisions: $p_{T}$-spectra fits and global variable predictions

> Three possible scenarios of the statistical hadronization model are reexamined with the use of the $p_{T}$ spectra of the PHENIX and very low $p_{T}$ PHOBOS measurements at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=200$ GeV. These scenarios are: (\textit{a}) full chemical non-equilibrium, (\textit{b}) strangeness chemical non-equilibrium and (\textit{c}) chemical equilibrium. Fits to the spectra are done within the Cracow single-freeze-out model, which takes into account both the expansion and resonance decays. Predictions for spectra of $ϕ$, $K(892)^{\ast 0}$ and $π^{0}$ are also given. The global variables like the transverse energy at midrapidity, the charged particle multiplicity at midrapidity and the total multiplicity of charged particles are evaluated and their predicted values agree qualitatively well with the experimental data. The thorough analysis within this model suggests that the chemical full non-equilibrium case is the least likely and both other cases are of the similar likelihood. It is also shown that if the full chemical non-equilibrium freeze-out took place it could manifest itself in the enhancement of the $π^{0}$-production at very low transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The Cracow single-freeze-out model, when generalized to include chemical non-equilibrium scenarios, shows that the strangeness chemical non-equilibrium case (gamma_q = 1, gamma_s ≠ 1) provides the best quality fits to the pT spectra of identified hadrons, while the full chemical non-equilibrium case (gamma_q ≠ 1, gamma_s ≠ 1) is the least likely scenario; however, the chemical equilibrium case also fits the spectra better than the full non-equilibrium case, contrary to what might be expected.

## Method

The author generalized the Cracow single-freeze-out model to incorporate chemical non-equilibrium by introducing phase-space occupancy factors gamma_q and gamma_s into the primordial momentum distributions, then used previously determined statistical parameters (T, mu_B, gamma_q, gamma_s) from Rafelski (2004) as fixed inputs to fit the geometric parameters rho_max and tau to the PHENIX pT spectra of pi±, K±, p, and p-bar across multiple centrality bins at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. The invariant distributions were computed using the Cooper-Frye formula on a constant proper-time freeze-out hypersurface with a linear transverse flow profile, including all confirmed resonances up to 2 GeV and full decay cascades.

## Key numbers

- For the 0-5% centrality bin in the full chemical non-equilibrium case: rho_max = 7.24 ± 0.09 fm, tau = 6.61 ± 0.06 fm, chi^2/NDF = 0.74 (NDF = 124)
- For the 0-5% centrality bin in the strangeness chemical non-equilibrium case: rho_max = 8.35 ± 0.10 fm, tau = 8.57 ± 0.08 fm, chi^2/NDF = 0.57 (NDF = 124)
- For the 0-5% centrality bin in the chemical equilibrium case: rho_max = 8.46 ± 0.10 fm, tau = 8.84 ± 0.08 fm, chi^2/NDF = 0.80 (NDF = 124)
- Average chi^2/NDF across all centrality bins: 1.30 for full chemical non-equilibrium, 0.77 for strangeness chemical non-equilibrium, 0.90 for chemical equilibrium
- Maximum transverse flow velocity beta_perp_max ranges from 0.58 to 0.75 across all scenarios and centralities
- For the 0-15% centrality bin including PHOBOS low-pT data: chi^2/NDF = 0.87 (full non-equilibrium), 0.41 (strangeness non-equilibrium), 0.59 (chemical equilibrium), NDF = 132
- Scaling exponents: rho_max ~ N_part^0.36, tau ~ N_part^0.28
- Low-pT pion overestimation in full non-equilibrium case: approximately 33% above data

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model analysis of Rafelski (2004), who determined statistical parameters from particle yield ratios at RHIC, by incorporating the Cracow single-freeze-out model of Broniowski (2001, 2002) to simultaneously describe pT spectra and global variables. The analysis contradicts the expectation that full chemical non-equilibrium might be favored, instead finding that the strangeness chemical non-equilibrium scenario (gamma_q = 1) best reproduces the PHENIX and PHOBOS data. The results enable predictions for phi, K(892)*0, and pi0 spectra, and suggest that low-pT pi0 enhancement could serve as a test for full chemical non-equilibrium freeze-out. The centrality scaling of geometric parameters provides input for future hydrodynamic and transport model comparisons.
