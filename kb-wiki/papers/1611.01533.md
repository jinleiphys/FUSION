# Phenomenological Review on Quark-Gluon Plasma: Concepts vs. Observations

> In this review, we present an up-to-date phenomenological summary of research developments in the physics of the Quark--Gluon Plasma (QGP). A short historical perspective and theoretical motivation for this rapidly developing field of contemporary particle physics is provided. In addition, we introduce and discuss the role of the quantum chromodynamics (QCD) ground state, non-perturbative and lattice QCD results on the QGP properties, as well as the transport models used to make a connection between theory and experiment. The experimental part presents the selected results on bulk observables, hard and penetrating probes obtained in the ultra-relativistic heavy-ion experiments carried out at the Brookhaven National Laboratory Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (BNL RHIC) and CERN Super Proton Synchrotron (SPS) and Large Hadron Collider (LHC) accelerators. We also give a brief overview of new developments related to the ongoing searches of the QCD critical point and to the collectivity in small ($p+p$ and $p+A$) systems.

# Phenomenological Review on Quark-Gluon Plasma: Concepts vs. Observations

## Key claim
This review argues that the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) discovered at RHIC in 2005 has properties that differ markedly from pre-discovery theoretical predictions, and that the QCD phase transition at vanishing baryon chemical potential is a smooth crossover rather than a first-order transition, as established by lattice QCD calculations.

## Method
The authors present a phenomenological summary of QGP research, combining a historical perspective with discussions of the QCD vacuum, non-perturbative and lattice QCD results, and transport models. They review experimental results on bulk observables and hard probes from ultra-relativistic heavy-ion collisions at BNL RHIC, CERN SPS, and CERN LHC, and discuss searches for the QCD critical point and collectivity in small systems (p+p and p+A collisions).

## Key numbers
- Normal nuclear density: ρ₀ = 2 × 10¹⁴ g·cm⁻³ (≈0.16 GeV·fm⁻³)
- Hagedorn temperature: T_H = 170–180 MeV
- Baryon chemical potential at √s_NN = 200 GeV: μ_B ≈ 45 MeV (from Adams et al., 2005)
- QCD vacuum energy density: ε^QCD ≈ -(5 ± 1) × 10⁻³ GeV⁴
- Ratio of cosmological constant to QCD vacuum density: |ε_CC/ε^QCD| ≈ 10⁻⁴⁴
- QCD confinement scale: Λ_QCD ∼ 100 MeV
- Low-p_T pion enhancement at LHC: up to ∼30%–50% compared to hydrodynamic models (from Abelev et al., 2013; Adam et al., 2015)
- Bose-Einstein pion condensate level: 5% can account for missing low-p_T charged pion yields in Pb+Pb collisions at √s = 2.76 TeV (from Begun et al., 2013–2015)

## Context
This review extends the line of work on QGP phenomenology following the discovery of the strongly coupled QGP at RHIC in 2005 (Arsene et al., 2004; Back et al., 2004; Adams et al., 2005; Adcox et al., 2004) and the earlier CERN announcement of circumstantial evidence for a new state of matter in 2000 (Heinz et al., 2000). It builds on lattice QCD results showing the transition is a crossover at μ_B=0 (Aoki et al., 2006; Bhattacharya et al., 2014) and discusses open questions including the nature of the QCD critical point at μ_B ∼ 400 MeV, the role of the QCD vacuum in QGP generation, and the interpretation of low-p_T pion excess at LHC energies.
