# Event Shape Selection Method in Search of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Heavy-ion Collisions

> The search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) in heavy-ion collisions has been impeded by the significant background arising from the anisotropic particle emission pattern, particularly elliptic flow. To alleviate this background, the event shape selection (ESS) technique categorizes collision events according to their shapes and projects the CME observables to a class of events with minimal flow. In this study, we explore two event shape variables to classify events and two elliptic flow variables to regulate the background. Each type of variable can be calculated from either single particles or particle pairs, resulting in four combinations of event shape and elliptic flow variables. By employing a toy model and the realistic event generator, event-by-event anomalous-viscous fluid dynamics (EBE-AVFD), we discover that the elliptic flow of resonances exhibits correlations with both the background and the potential CME signal, making the resonance flow unsuitable for background control. Through the EBE-AVFD simulations of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV with various input scenarios, we ascertain that the optimal ESS strategy for background control entails utilizing the single-particle elliptic flow in conjunction with the event shape variable based on particle pairs.

## Key claim

The optimal event shape selection (ESS) strategy for controlling the elliptic-flow background in chiral magnetic effect (CME) searches uses single-particle elliptic flow ($v_2$) as the background-control variable together with a pair-based event shape variable ($q_{2,\text{pair}}^2$), because resonance $v_2$ and pair $v_2$ both contain CME signal and cause over-subtraction of background.

## Method

The authors performed event-by-event anomalous-viscous fluid dynamics (EBE-AVFD) simulations of Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV with various input chirality charge densities ($n_5/s = 0$, 0.1, and 0.2) and a toy model of flowing $\rho$ meson decays. They categorized events using four combinations of event shape variables ($q_2^2$ from either single particles or particle pairs) and elliptic flow variables ($v_2$ from either single particles, particle pairs, or resonances), then projected the $\Delta\gamma_{112}$ correlator to the zero-flow limit via linear fits to evaluate background subtraction efficacy.

## Key numbers

- EBE-AVFD simulations used $9.6 \times 10^7$, $5.9 \times 10^7$, and $7.7 \times 10^7$ events for $n_5/s = 0$, 0.1, and 0.2 respectively, for 30-40% centrality Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV
- The toy model generated 33 pairs of $\pi^\pm$ per event from $\rho$ meson decays, with multiplicity matching STAR measurements in 30-40% Au+Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV within $|y| < 1$
- The ensemble average of $\Delta\gamma_{112}$ in the pure background scenario ($n_5/s=0$) was $(15.68 \pm 0.09) \times 10^{-5}$
- The unmixed ESS recipes (single $v_2$ with single $q_2^2$, or pair $v_2$ with pair $q_2^2$) achieved approximately eight-fold suppression of background in the $n_5/s=0$ case, but with finite residual backgrounds
- The optimal mixed recipe (single $v_2$ with pair $q_2^2$) recovered the true CME signal within uncertainties for both $n_5/s=0.1$ and $n_5/s=0.2$ scenarios
- Single-particle $v_2$ is typically on the order of 5%, requiring a $(1-\langle v_2\rangle)^2$ scaling factor to restore the ensemble-averaged CME signal

## Context

This work extends the event shape engineering approach previously applied to CME searches (SergeiESE, 1st-ESS) by systematically testing four combinations of event shape and elliptic flow variables. It contradicts the naive expectation that resonance $v_2$ can serve as a clean background-control variable, showing instead that resonance $v_2$ contains CME signal proportional to $a_1^2$ as demonstrated analytically and through EBE-AVFD simulations. The findings enable more reliable extraction of the CME signal from experimental data at RHIC and LHC, with the ultimate validation requiring application to real data such as Au+Au collisions at 3 GeV where the quark-gluon plasma and CME are expected to be absent.
