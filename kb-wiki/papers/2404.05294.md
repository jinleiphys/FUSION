# Reduction of (pseudo-)Critical Temperatures of Chiral Restoration and Deconfinement Phase Transitions in a Magnetized PNJL Model

> We investigate the chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions under external magnetic field in frame of a Pauli-Villars regularized PNJL model. A running Polyakov loop scale parameter $T_0(eB)$ is introduced to mimic the reaction of the gluon sector to the presence of magnetic fields. It is found that a decreasing $T_0(eB)$ with magnetic fields can realize the inverse magnetic catalysis phenomena of chiral condensates of $u$ and $d$ quarks, increase of Polyakov loop and the reduction of (pseudo-)critical temperatures of chiral restoration and deconfinement phase transitions.

## Key claim
By introducing a magnetic-field-dependent Polyakov loop scale parameter \(T_0(eB)\) that decreases rapidly with increasing field strength, the Pauli-Villars regularized PNJL model can reproduce the inverse magnetic catalysis of light-quark condensates and the reduction of (pseudo-)critical temperatures for both chiral restoration and deconfinement, but the model fails to reproduce the lattice QCD result that the strange-quark condensate shows magnetic catalysis in the whole temperature region.

## Method
The author employs two-flavor and three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio models extended with a Polyakov loop (PNJL), using a Pauli-Villars regularization scheme to handle ultraviolet divergences. A running parameter \(T_0(eB)\) is introduced into the Polyakov potential to mimic the gluon sector's response to the magnetic field, and the coupled gap equations for the chiral condensates and Polyakov loop are solved at mean-field level. The (pseudo-)critical temperatures are defined by the vanishing second derivative of the order parameters with respect to temperature.

## Context
This work extends earlier attempts to explain the inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD simulations (Lattice 2011-2016 collaborations) by introducing a magnetic-field-dependent Polyakov scale. It contrasts with prior PNJL model calculations at mean-field level that typically predict increasing critical temperatures with magnetic field, as reviewed by several authors (Review 2010-2016). The approach follows the strategy of using a running \(T_0(eB)\) previously proposed in a two-flavor Polyakov-loop-extended quark-meson model (PQM, 2014) and in a three-flavor entangled PNJL model (PNJL3, 2015), but here a Pauli-Villars regularization is applied instead of other schemes. The model enables qualitative reproduction of lattice results for light quarks and the Polyakov loop, but leaves open the discrepancy for the strange-quark condensate, which in lattice simulations shows magnetic catalysis at all temperatures.
