# Quark matter symmetry energy and quark stars

> We extend the confined-density-dependent-mass (CDDM) model to include isospin dependence of the equivalent quark mass. Within the confined-isospin-density-dependent-mass (CIDDM) model, we study the quark matter symmetry energy, the stability of strange quark matter, and the properties of quark stars. We find that including isospin dependence of the equivalent quark mass can significantly influence the quark matter symmetry energy as well as the properties of strange quark matter and quark stars. While the recently discovered large mass pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 with masses around $2M_{\odot}$ cannot be quark stars within the CDDM model, they can be well described by quark stars in the CIDDM model. In particular, our results indicate that the two-flavor $u$-$d$ quark matter symmetry energy should be at least about twice that of a free quark gas or normal quark matter within conventional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model in order to describe the PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 as quark stars.

# Quark matter symmetry energy and quark stars

## Key claim
The authors claim that the recently discovered large mass pulsars PSR J1614-2230 and PSR J0348+0432 (masses around 2 solar masses) can be described as quark stars, but only if the quark matter symmetry energy is at least about twice that of a free quark gas or normal quark matter within the conventional Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. This requires extending the confined-density-dependent-mass (CDDM) model to include isospin dependence of the equivalent quark mass, forming the confined-isospin-density-dependent-mass (CIDDM) model.

## Method
The authors extend the CDDM model by adding an isospin-dependent term to the equivalent quark mass, parameterized as m_iso = -tau_q * delta * D_I * n_B^alpha * exp(-beta * n_B), where tau_q is the isospin quantum number, delta is the isospin asymmetry, and D_I, alpha, beta are adjustable parameters. They then compute the quark matter symmetry energy analytically, enforce absolute stability conditions for strange quark matter (SQM) following Farhi and Jaffe (1984), and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation to obtain mass-radius relations for static and rotating quark stars.

## Key numbers
- The maximum mass of static quark stars in the CDDM model (parameter set DI-0, with D_I = 0) is 1.65 solar masses with a corresponding radius of 9.60 km.
- The maximum mass of static quark stars with parameter set DI-300 (D_I = 300 MeV*fm^(3*alpha)) is 1.78 solar masses with a corresponding radius of 10.40 km.
- The parameter set DI-2500 (D_I = 2500 MeV*fm^(3*alpha)) yields a maximum mass of 1.93 solar masses for static quark stars.
- The zero-pressure density of SQM is 0.24 fm^(-3) for DI-0, 0.23 fm^(-3) for DI-300, and 0.21 fm^(-3) for DI-2500.
- The measured mass of PSR J1614-2230 is 1.97 +/- 0.04 solar masses (Demorest 2010).
- The two-flavor u-d quark matter symmetry energy predicted by DI-2500 is about 50 times the nuclear matter symmetry energy from the NLrho-delta RMF model.

## Context
This work extends the CDDM model (Fowler 1981, Chakrabarty 1989, Peng 1999) by introducing isospin dependence of the equivalent quark mass, motivated by isospin-dependent in-medium chiral condensates (Kaiser 2009) and QCD sum rules (Dru 2004, Jeong 2012). Prior work by Li A. et al. (2010) showed that the CDDM model cannot produce quark stars with masses above about 1.8 solar masses, ruling out the description of PSR J1614-2230 as a quark star. The CIDDM model enables quark stars to reach the observed 2-solar-mass range, and the results indicate that the quark matter symmetry energy must be significantly larger than previously assumed. This opens the possibility of using quark star observations to constrain the isovector properties of dense quark matter.
