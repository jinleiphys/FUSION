# Survival of heavy flavored mesons in a hot medium

> Hadronization of heavy quarks reveals various unusual features. Gluon radiation by a heavy quark originated from a hard process, ceases shortly on a distance of the order of few fm. Due to the dead-cone effect a heavy quark radiates only a small fraction of its energy. This is why the measured fragmentation function D(z) peaks at large z. Hadronization finishes at very short distances, well shorter than 1 fm, by production of a colorless small-size Qq-bar dipole. This ensures dominance of a perturbative mechanism and makes possible factorization of short and long distances. The latter corresponds to final state interactions of the produced dipole propagating through a dense medium. The results provide good description of data on beauty and charm suppression in heavy ion collisions, fixing the transport coefficient for b-quarks about twice smaller than for charm, and both significantly lower that the values determined from data on suppression of high-pT light hadrons. We relate this to reduction of the QCD coupling at higher scales, and suppression of radiation by the dead-cone effect.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the observed suppression of high-\(p_T\) heavy flavored mesons in heavy ion collisions is not primarily due to radiative energy loss (which is small for heavy quarks due to the dead-cone effect), but rather due to final-state interactions of the produced heavy-light \(Q\bar q\) dipole in the medium, with the key condition that the production length is extremely short (fractions of a fm), allowing factorization of short and long distances.

## Method

The authors relate the measured fragmentation function \(D_{b/B}(z)\) from \(e^+e^-\) annihilation to the production length distribution of the \(b\bar q\) dipole, finding the mean production length to be well below 1 fm. They then model the propagation of this small-size dipole through the hot medium, accounting for its rapid expansion to the large meson size, multiple breakups and recombinations, and both perturbative and nonperturbative (string) energy loss. The nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) is calculated and compared to ATLAS, CMS, and ALICE data for \(B\) and \(D\) mesons, with the transport coefficient \(\hat q_0\) as the only free parameter.

## Key numbers

* The mean production length \(L_p\) for \(B\)-mesons is fractions of a fm, decreasing with rising \(p_T\).
* The transport coefficient for \(b\)-quarks is \(\hat q_0^b = 0.20-0.25\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\).
* The transport coefficient for \(c\)-quarks is \(\hat q_0^c = 0.45-0.55\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\).
* For light hadrons at \(\sqrt{s}=2.76\ \text{TeV}\), the transport coefficient is \(\hat q_0 = 2\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\).
* The string tension in vacuum is \(\kappa \approx 1\ \text{GeV}/\text{fm}\), with temperature dependence \(\kappa(T)=\kappa(1-T/T_c)^{1/3}\) and critical temperature \(T_c=280\ \text{MeV}\).
* The mean free path of a \(B\)-meson in the medium for \(\hat q = 1\ \text{GeV}^2/\text{fm}\) is \(L_{free}=0.04\ \text{fm}\).
* The \(b\to B\) fragmentation function peaks at \(z\sim0.85\).
* The fractional radiated energy for \(b\)-quarks ranges from 10 to 35% for quark energies 10-100 GeV.
* The \(pp\) cross section for \(B\)-mesons is parametrized as \(d\sigma_{pp}/dp_T \propto 1/(p_T^2+m_B^2)^{\alpha_B}\) with \(\alpha_B=2.7\pm0.1\); for \(D\)-mesons \(\alpha_D=2.6\pm0.1\).

## Context

This work extends the color transparency and formation length approach previously applied to light hadron suppression in heavy ion collisions (Kopeliovich et al., "within", "jet-lag", "ct", "ct-eloss") to heavy flavored mesons. It contradicts the expectation from the dead-cone effect (Dokshitzer and Troyan, "dk") that heavy mesons should be much less suppressed than light hadrons, which was not confirmed by ATLAS and ALICE data. The results enable determination of the transport coefficient for heavy quarks, showing it is not universal but depends on the probing parton mass, and relate this to the running QCD coupling and dead-cone suppression. The analysis also connects to earlier \(J/\psi\) suppression studies (Kopeliovich et al., "kps-psi", "k-psi") which found similar \(\hat q_0\) values for charm at RHIC energies.
