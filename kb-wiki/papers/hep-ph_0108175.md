# Collective modes of color-flavor locked phase of dense QCD at finite temperature

> A detailed analysis of collective modes that couple to either vector or axial color currents in color-flavor locked phase of color superconducting dense quark matter at finite temperature is presented. Among the realm of collective modes, including the plasmons and the Nambu-Goldstone bosons, we also reveal the gapless Carlson-Goldman modes, resembling the scalar Nambu-Golstone bosons. These latter exist only in a close vicinity of the critical line. Their presence does not eliminate the Meissner effect, proving that the system remains in the color broken phase. The finite temperature properties of the plasmons and the Nambu-Goldstone bosons are also studied. In addition to the ordinary plasmon, we also reveal a "light" plasmon which has a narrow width and whose mass is of the order of the superconducting gap.

## Key claim

The paper predicts the existence of gapless Carlson-Goldman modes in the color-flavor locked phase of dense QCD, but only in a close vicinity of the critical temperature line; these modes resemble scalar Nambu-Goldstone bosons but their presence does not eliminate the Meissner effect, confirming the system remains in the color broken phase.

## Method

The authors derived the effective action for gluon fields by integrating out quark degrees of freedom in the improved hard dense loop approximation, incorporating finite-temperature effects and nonzero quark gaps. They then analyzed the polarization tensor and current-current correlation function to extract the dispersion relations of collective modes coupling to vector and axial color currents, including plasmons, Nambu-Goldstone bosons, and Carlson-Goldman modes.

## Key numbers

- Plasma frequency: \(\omega_p \equiv g\mu / \sqrt{2}\pi\)
- The superconducting gap (order parameter) at zero temperature is estimated to be of order 10 to 100 MeV
- The "light" plasmon has a mass of the order of the superconducting gap \(|\Delta_T|\)
- The gap in the singlet channel is twice as large as the gap in the octet channel
- The critical temperature dependence follows the standard BCS theory of superconductivity

## Context

This work extends prior studies of collective excitations in the color-flavor locked phase, which were previously analyzed using Bethe-Salpeter equations (bs-cfl, 2000) and effective low-energy actions derived by Casalbuoni and Gatto (1999), Son and Stephanov (2000), and others. The prediction of Carlson-Goldman modes in dense QCD parallels their experimental discovery in ordinary superconductors by Carlson and Goldman (1966). The results enable further investigation of transport properties and observable signatures of color superconducting matter in compact stars.
