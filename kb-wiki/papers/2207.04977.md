# Emergence of mass in the gauge sector of QCD

> It is widely accepted nowadays that gluons, while massless at the level of the fundamental QCD Lagrangian,acquire an effective mass through the non-Abelian implementation of the classic Schwinger mechanism. The key dynamical ingredient that triggers the onset of this mechanism is the formation of composite massless poles inside the fundamental vertices of the theory. These poles enter in the evolution equation of the gluon propagator, and affect nontrivially the way the Slavnov-Taylor identities of the vertices are resolved,inducing a smoking-gun displacement in the corresponding Ward identities. In this article we present a comprehensive review of the pivotal concepts associated with this dynamical scenario, emphasizing the synergy between functional methods and lattice simulations, and highlighting recent advances that corroborate the action of the Schwinger mechanism in QCD.

## Key claim

Gluons acquire an effective mass through the non-Abelian implementation of the Schwinger mechanism, driven by the dynamical formation of composite massless poles inside the fundamental vertices of the theory; the author emphasizes that this mechanism is reconciled with local gauge invariance only when these poles are longitudinally coupled and that their existence is corroborated by a characteristic displacement in the Ward identities of the pole-free vertex parts.

## Method

The paper reviews the Schwinger-Dyson equation (SDE) and Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) framework for pure SU(N) Yang-Mills theory in the Landau gauge, focusing on the three-gluon and ghost-gluon vertices. The vertices are decomposed into pole-free parts and longitudinally coupled pole terms; the pole terms are shown to satisfy homogeneous BSEs whose nontrivial solutions yield the Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes for massless colored bound states. The system of BSEs is solved numerically under a one-particle-exchange approximation for the kernels, using fully dressed propagators and the pole-free vertex parts as inputs.

## Key numbers

* The strong coupling required for nontrivial BSE solutions is found to be \(\alpha_s = 0.63\) at the renormalization point \(\mu = 4.3\) GeV, within the one-particle-exchange approximation.
* The estimated value of \(\alpha_s\) in the asymmetric MOM scheme used for comparison is \(\alpha_s = 0.27\) (Athenodorou 2016, Aguilar 2019), indicating the truncation artifact.
* The gluon mass is defined as \(m^2 = \Delta^{-1}(0)\), where \(\Delta(q^2)\) is the scalar form factor of the gluon propagator; the propagator saturates to a finite nonvanishing value at \(q^2 = 0\) in lattice simulations and SDE analyses.
* The renormalization-group-invariant gluonic scale is about half the proton mass (Binosi 2016, Cui 2019).

## Context

This review extends the classic Schwinger mechanism (Schwinger 1962) to non-Abelian gauge theories, building on the seminal proposal by Cornwall (1979, 1981) and subsequent work by Eichten and Feinberg (1974). It synthesizes continuum functional methods (SDEs and BSEs) with lattice simulations of gluon and ghost propagators (Cucchieri 2007, Bogolubsky 2007, Oliveira 2009) and vertices (Athenodorou 2016, Aguilar 2019). The framework enables the systematic computation of the gluon mass gap and its connection to confinement, the Gribov copy problem, and the definition of a process-independent QCD effective charge.
