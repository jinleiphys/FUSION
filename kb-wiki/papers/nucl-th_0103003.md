# Transition quadrupole moments in gamma-soft nuclei and the triaxial projected shell model

> Transition probabilities of the ground-state bands in $γ$-soft nuclei are studied for the first time using the triaxial projected shell model approach. It is observed that the angular-momentum dependence of the transition quadrupole moment $Q_t$ is related to the triaxial deformation of the nuclear mean-field potential. The introduction of the $γ$-degree of freedom in the shell model basis is shown to have a little influence on the {\it constant} behavior of the low-spin $Q_t$ in a well-deformed nucleus. However, the {\it increasing} collectivity with spin for the low-spin states in a $γ$-soft nucleus can only be explained by considering the triaxial mean-field deformation.

# Transition quadrupole moments in gamma-soft nuclei and the triaxial projected shell model

## Key claim

The angular-momentum dependence of transition quadrupole moments in the low-spin region (I ≤ 10) of ground-state bands can provide a measure of triaxial deformation, but only for transitional (gamma-soft) nuclei; for well-deformed nuclei, the triaxial basis has no significant effect on the constant behavior of Q_t.

## Method

The triaxial projected shell model (TPSM) was used, performing three-dimensional angular-momentum projection on triaxial Nilsson wavefunctions with a pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole Hamiltonian. The basis consisted of angular-momentum projected triaxial quasiparticle vacuum states with even K-values, and the Hamiltonian was diagonalized in this projected basis to obtain wavefunctions for calculating B(E2) transition probabilities and transition quadrupole moments. Calculations were performed for Dy isotopes (154,156,158) and Er isotopes (156,158,160) using standard effective charges of 1.5e for protons and 0.5e for neutrons.

## Key numbers

- For the transitional nucleus 154Dy, the observed increasing Q_t with spin could be reproduced with a triaxial deformation parameter ε' ≈ 0.15
- The monopole pairing strength was taken as G_M = [21.24 ∓ 13.86(N-Z)/A]/A, with the minus sign for neutrons and plus sign for protons
- The quadrupole pairing strength G_Q was set proportional to G_M with a proportionality constant in the range 0.16–0.18
- The model space included three major shells for each nucleon type: N=4,5,6 for neutrons and N=3,4,5 for protons
- For the well-deformed nucleus 166Er (neutron number 98), Q_t showed a nearly constant behavior for low-spin states regardless of ε' value

## Context

This work extends the triaxial projected shell model approach previously developed by Sheikh and Sun (1999) for moments of inertia, and by Sun (2000) for gamma-vibrational bands, to the calculation of transition quadrupole moments. It addresses a long-standing problem in understanding fingerprints of triaxial deformation in electromagnetic transition probabilities, which could not be explained by earlier projected shell model calculations using axially symmetric basis (Sun 1994, Vel 1999). The results enable future application to inter-band transitions between ground-state bands and gamma-bands, and suggest that for well-deformed nuclei, additional physical quantities beyond g-band Q_t are needed to determine triaxiality.
