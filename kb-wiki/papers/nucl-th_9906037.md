# Numerical analysis of two-pion correlation based on a hydrodynamical model

> We will numerically investgate two-particle correlation function of CERN--SPS 158 A GeV Pb+Pb central collisions in detail based on a (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical model with first order phase transition. We use the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretski\uı parametrization as well as the usual Cartesian parametrization and analyze the pair momentum dependence of HBT radii extracted from the parametrizations. We find that the interpretation of the temporal radius parameters as the time duration in YKP parametrization is not available for the hydrodynamical model where the source became opaque naturally because of expansion and surface dominant freeze-out. Finally, effect of the phase transition on the source opacity is also discussed.

# Numerical analysis of two-pion correlation based on a hydrodynamical model

## Key claim

The temporal radius parameter R0 in the Yano-Koonin-Podgoretski parametrization cannot be interpreted as the emission duration (time duration) for a hydrodynamical model where the source becomes opaque due to expansion and surface-dominant freeze-out, even when a first-order phase transition is present. The usual interpretation fails at high transverse momentum because the source opacity term ((Δx)^2 - (Δy)^2)/β⊥^2 makes a large negative contribution to R0^2, not because of any phase transition effect.

## Method

The authors performed (3+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamical simulations of CERN-SPS 158 A GeV Pb+Pb central collisions using a bag model equation of state with a first-order QCD phase transition at Tc = 160 MeV and freeze-out at Tf = 140 MeV. They computed two-pion correlation functions from the Cooper-Frye freeze-out prescription and extracted HBT radii using both Cartesian parametrization (with outward-longitudinal cross term) and Yano-Koonin-Podgoretski (YKP) parametrization, then compared these extracted radii to the actual space-time extensions of the freeze-out hypersurface.

## Key numbers

- Phase transition temperature: Tc = 160 MeV, with bag constant B = 412 MeV/fm^3
- Freeze-out temperature: Tf = 140 MeV
- Initial proper time for hydrodynamics: τ0 = 1 fm
- R0 values from YKP fit: approximately 2-3 fm, smaller than experimental results and decreasing with increasing KT
- R⊥ (YKP transverse radius) difference from space-time extension: about 0.5 fm
- Source opacity measure (Δx)^2 - (Δy)^2 becomes negative throughout the evolution, with the third term in the R0 expression ((Δx)^2 - (Δy)^2)/β⊥^2 providing a large negative contribution at high KT
- For most particles, freeze-out occurs at τ ~ 10-11 fm

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamical modeling approach of Nonaka (1999) for single-particle distributions to two-particle correlations, comparing with NA49 preliminary data (1996) and NA49 data (1998). The authors test the YKP parametrization framework developed by Yano (1978), Chapman (1995), and Wu (1998) against a realistic dynamical model, finding that the simple interpretation of R0 as time duration fails for opaque sources as predicted by Heiselberg (1998). The results contradict the expectation that first-order phase transitions produce large emission durations (Pratt, 1986; Hung, 1997) and show that the source opacity arises from transverse flow rather than phase transition effects. This work enables more careful interpretation of HBT radii from experimental data and motivates inclusion of resonance decays and more realistic source models.
