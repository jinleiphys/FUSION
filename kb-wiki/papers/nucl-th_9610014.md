# Electromagnetic N-$Δ$ transition form factors in a covariant quark-diquark model

> The electromagnetic N-$Δ$ transition form factors are calculated in the framework of a formally covariant constituent diquark model. As a spin-3/2 particle the $Δ$ is assumed to be a bound state of a quark and an axial-vector diquark. The wave function is obtained from a diquark-quark Salpeter equation with an instantaneous quark exchange potential. The three transition form factors are calculated for momentum transfers squared from the pseudothreshold $(M_Δ-M_N)^2$ up to $-2 (GeV/c)^2$. The magnetic form factor is in qualitative agreement with experiment. We find very interesting results for the ratios E2/M1 and C2/M1.

# Electromagnetic N-Δ transition form factors in a covariant quark-diquark model

## Key claim

The electromagnetic N-Δ transition form factors can be calculated in a formally covariant constituent quark-diquark model, but the dominant magnetic transition form factor G_M comes out a factor of 2.8 too low when only the vector-diquark channel is considered; including scalar-to-vector diquark transitions improves the agreement, though G_M remains somewhat too low.

## Method

The Δ resonance (spin-3/2) is treated as a bound state of a quark and an axial-vector diquark, with the wave function obtained from a diquark-quark Salpeter equation using an instantaneous quark-exchange potential. The three transition form factors (magnetic dipole G_M, electric quadrupole G_E, and Coulomb quadrupole G_C) are extracted from the transition currents calculated via the Mandelstam formalism, for momentum transfers squared from the pseudothreshold (M_Δ − M_N)² up to −2 (GeV/c)². The model parameters are fixed by fitting nucleon electromagnetic form factors and the Δ mass at 1232 MeV.

## Key numbers

- The calculated magnetic transition form factor G_M is a factor of 2.8 too low compared to experiment when only the vector-diquark channel contributes.
- At q² = 0, the ratio E2/M1 = −5.5% for parameter Set A; with scalar–vector diquark transitions included (Set B), E2/M1 = −3.4%, compared to the experimental value of −2.5 ± 0.2% and the analysis by Drechsel (1995) giving −3.5% for the dressed Δ resonance.
- The threshold value of C2/M1 is predicted as +2.1% (Set B), with an alternative calculation via current conservation yielding +1.4%.
- The anomalous magnetic moment of the vector diquark is κ = 1.1, close to the point-like diquark value κ₀ = 1.0.
- The nucleon static properties from Set A include: proton charge radius √⟨r²⟩_E^p = 0.79 fm, neutron charge radius ⟨r²⟩_E^n = −0.110 fm², proton magnetic moment μ_p = 2.45 μ_N, neutron magnetic moment μ_n = −1.42 μ_N.

## Context

This work extends the covariant quark-diquark model previously applied to nucleon form factors by Keiner (1995a, 1995b) to the Δ resonance, treating it as a pure vector-diquark–quark bound state. The approach is similar to the light-cone quark-diquark model of Kroll (1995) and the three-quark calculations of Weber (1995) and Cardarelli (1995). The results suggest that strong quark-quark correlations may play a role in nucleon structure, and that scalar–vector diquark transitions are important for describing both the nucleon magnetic form factors and the N-Δ magnetic transition. The model enables predictions for the helicity asymmetry ratio A(q²) and provides a framework for testing the role of diquark correlations in intermediate-energy electromagnetic transitions.
