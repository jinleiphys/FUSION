# The hyperon-nucleon interaction: conventional versus effective field theory approach

> Hyperon-nucleon interactions are presented that are derived either in the conventional meson-exchange picture or within leading order chiral effective field theory. The chiral potential consists of one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms. With regard to meson-exchange hyperon-nucleon models we focus on the new potential of the Juelich group, whose most salient feature is that the contributions in the scalar--isoscalar (σ) and vector--isovector (ρ) exchange channels are constrained by a microscopic model of correlated ππand KKbar exchange.

## Key claim

The paper presents a leading-order chiral effective field theory (EFT) for the hyperon-nucleon interaction, but the authors acknowledge that the five low-energy constants cannot be uniquely determined from scattering data alone, and the \(\Lambda N\) scattering lengths had to be fixed by hand to produce reasonable hypertriton binding energies rather than being constrained by the data.

## Method

The authors constructed a leading-order chiral EFT potential consisting of one-pseudoscalar-meson exchanges (pions, kaons, eta) and non-derivative four-baryon contact terms, with SU(3) flavor symmetry constraining the couplings. They solved a coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation in the particle basis to account for \(\Lambda N\)-\(\Sigma N\) coupling and Coulomb effects, fitting five contact terms to 35 low-energy total cross section data points and the inelastic capture ratio at rest. For comparison, they also presented results from the Juelich meson-exchange model, where the scalar-isoscalar (\(\sigma\)) and vector-isovector (\(\rho\)) exchange contributions are constrained by a microscopic model of correlated \(\pi\pi\) and \(K\bar{K}\) exchange.

## Key numbers

- The chiral EFT potential uses a regulator function \(f^\Lambda(p',p)=e^{-(p'^4+p^4)/\Lambda^4}\) with cutoff \(\Lambda\) varied between 550 and 700 MeV.
- The fit to 35 YN data points yielded \(\chi^2\) values of 29.6, 28.3, 30.3, and 34.6 for cutoffs of 550, 600, 650, and 700 MeV respectively.
- The \(\Lambda N\) S-wave scattering lengths were kept fixed after fitting at \(\Lambda = 600\) MeV, because they are not well determined by scattering data.
- The pseudoscalar meson decay constant used is \(F_\pi = 92.4\) MeV, and the axial-vector coupling satisfies \(F+D = g_A \simeq 1.26\).
- The SU(6) value \(\alpha = 0.4\) was used for the pseudoscalar \(F/(F+D)\) ratio.

## Context

This work extends the chiral EFT approach previously applied to the nucleon-nucleon interaction by Epelbach et al. (2005) to the hyperon-nucleon system, which had seen very few EFT investigations prior. It contrasts with the conventional meson-exchange picture represented by the Juelich group's new potential (Haidenbauer et al., 2005) and the Nijmegen model NSC97f (Rijken et al., 1999). The results enable future systematic improvements to higher orders in chiral EFT for hyperon-nucleon interactions and provide a framework for calculating hypernuclear binding energies and three-body forces consistently.
