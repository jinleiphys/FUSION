# Production of light nuclei in isobaric Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ =7.7-200 GeV from a multiphase transport model

> The production of light nuclei in isobaric $^{96}_{44}$Ru + $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr + $^{96}_{40}$Zr collisions, ranging from $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 7.7 to 200 GeV, are studied using the string melting version of A Multi Phase Transport (AMPT) model in combination with a coalescence approach to light nuclei production. From the calculated yields, transverse momentum $p_{T}$ spectra, and rapidity dependences of light nuclei $p$, $n$, $d$, $t$, ${}^{3}$He, we find that the Ru+Ru/Zr+Zr ratios for the yields of these particles exceed unity with the inclusion of a quadrupole deformation $β_{ 2 }$ and octupole deformation $β_{ 3 }$ as well as the neutron skins. We also find that heavier particles have a larger deviation from unity. Furthermore, we find that as the collision energy increases, the influence of isospin effects on the production of light nuclei in isobar collisions gradually decreases, while the influence of nuclear structure becomes more significant, particularly evident from the energy dependence of the deuteron ratio, which is unaffected by isospin effects.

## Key claim

The Ru+Ru/Zr+Zr yield ratios for light nuclei (proton, neutron, deuteron, triton, helium-3) exceed unity only when the simulation includes a quadrupole deformation β₂, octupole deformation β₃, and neutron skins for the two isobars; the effect of nuclear structure on these ratios becomes more significant with increasing collision energy, while isospin effects weaken.

## Method

The authors used the string melting version of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model to generate nucleon phase-space distributions for isobaric ⁹⁶₄₄Ru + ⁹⁶₄₄Ru and ⁹⁶₄₀Zr + ⁹⁶₄₀Zr collisions at center-of-mass energies per nucleon pair √sNN = 7.7, 27, 62.4, and 200 GeV. Light nuclei (deuteron, triton, helium-3) were formed via a coalescence approach applied to kinetically freeze-out nucleons, with coalescence criteria of maximum relative distance Δr ≤ 3.0 fm and maximum relative momentum Δp ≤ 0.16 GeV/c for deuterons, and Δr_max ≤ 2.0 fm with Δp_max ≤ 0.16 GeV/c for three-body nuclei. Four sets of Woods-Saxon parameters were used to model the initial nucleon distributions, varying the radius R₀, surface diffuseness a, quadrupole deformation β₂, and octupole deformation β₃ for Ru and Zr nuclei.

## Key numbers

- Collision energies studied: √sNN = 7.7, 27, 62.4, and 200 GeV
- Coalescence parameters for deuteron: Δr ≤ 3.0 fm and Δp ≤ 0.16 GeV/c
- Coalescence parameters for triton and helium-3: Δr_max ≤ 2.0 fm and Δp_max ≤ 0.16 GeV/c
- Number of simulated events: 5 million (7.7 GeV), 4 million (27 GeV), 3.5 million (62.4 GeV), and 5 million (200 GeV) for each isobar system
- Ru quadrupole deformation β₂ = 0.13 (Case-2) and β₂ = 0.154 (Case-4); Zr β₂ = 0.06 (Case-2 and Case-4)
- Ru octupole deformation β₃ = 0 (all cases); Zr β₃ = 0.202 (Case-4)
- Ru radius R₀ = 5.065 fm (Case-4); Zr radius R₀ = 4.961 fm (Case-4)
- The t/³He ratio in peripheral collisions is about 1.1, compared to a preliminary STAR result of about 1.8

## Context

This work extends prior studies of isobaric collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC), particularly the STAR experiment's measurements of v₂ and v₃ ratios (Abdallah et al., 2021) and earlier predictions for light nuclei production assuming identical nuclear structures (She et al., 2021). The study uses the AMPT model with coalescence, building on approaches by Deng et al. (2020) and Sombun et al. (2019), and employs Woods-Saxon parameters from recent nuclear structure determinations (Jia et al., 2023; Xu et al., 2021; Nijs et al., 2021). The results enable future comparisons with STAR data on light nuclei yields and ratios, potentially clarifying the formation mechanism of light nuclei and the role of nuclear deformation and neutron skins in isobar collisions.
