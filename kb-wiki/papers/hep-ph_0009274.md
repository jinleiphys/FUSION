# Instantons and Nucleon Magnetism

> We construct improved QCD sum rules for the nucleon magnetic moments by implementing direct-instanton contributions to the operator product expansion of the nucleon correlator in a magnetic background field. The instanton contributions are found to affect only those sum rules which had previously been considered unstable. The new sum rules show a high degree of stability and reproduce the experimental values of the nucleon magnetic moments for values of the magnetic quark condensate susceptibility which are consistent with other estimates.

# Instantons and Nucleon Magnetism

## Key claim

The paper claims that including direct-instanton contributions in the operator product expansion (OPE) of the nucleon correlator in a magnetic background field stabilizes the previously unstable chirally-odd sum rule for the nucleon magnetic moments, provided the magnetic quark condensate susceptibility is taken within a range consistent with other independent estimates.

## Method

The authors construct improved QCD sum rules for nucleon magnetic moments by implementing a generalized operator product expansion that includes direct-instanton contributions (IOPE) to the nucleon correlator in a constant magnetic background field. They evaluate the hard instanton contributions semiclassically using the instanton liquid parameters (average instanton size \(\bar{\rho} \simeq 1/3\) fm, average separation \(\bar{R} \simeq 1\) fm, density \(\bar{n} \simeq 0.5\) fm\(^{-4}\)), treating quark zero modes exactly and continuum modes as plane waves. The sum rule for the chirally-odd amplitude \(\Pi_3\) is then fitted by varying the magnetic susceptibility \(\chi\) and the transition strength parameters \(A_{p,n}\) while keeping the magnetic moments fixed at their experimental values.

## Key numbers

- Average instanton size: \(\bar{\rho} \simeq 1/3\) fm; average instanton separation: \(\bar{R} \simeq 1\) fm; instanton density: \(\bar{n} \simeq 0.5\) fm\(^{-4}\)
- Fiducial Borel mass domain: \(0.8\ \text{GeV} \leq M \leq 1.15\ \text{GeV}\), where highest-dimensional operators contribute at most 10% and continuum does not exceed 50%
- Nucleon coupling: \(\tilde{\lambda}^2 = 2.93\ \text{GeV}^6\); continuum threshold: \(W = 1.66\ \text{GeV}\); mixed quark condensate parameter: \(m_0^2 = 0.8\ \text{GeV}^2\)
- Magnetic susceptibility from proton sum rule: \(\chi \simeq -4.96\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\); from neutron sum rule: \(\chi \simeq -4.73\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (both at renormalization scale \(\nu = 0.5\) GeV)
- Transition strength parameters: \(A_p \simeq 0.28\ \text{GeV}^2\) (proton), \(A_n \simeq -0.27\ \text{GeV}^{-2}\) (neutron)
- Susceptibilities from independent work: \(\kappa = -0.34 \pm 0.1\), \(\xi = -0.74 \pm 0.2\)

## Context

This work extends the instanton-improved operator product expansion (IOPE) approach previously developed by Forkel (2000) and Forkel and Banerjee to the nucleon magnetic moment sum rules originally formulated by Ioffe and Smilga (1984) and Balitsky and Yung. The paper resolves the long-standing instability of the \(\Pi_3\) sum rule noted by Ioffe and Smilga (1984), which had previously been considered flawed. The results reinforce a systematic pattern observed in pion (Forkel, 1995), nucleon (Forkel and Banerjee, 1997), and glueball channels: sum rules that worked without instanton corrections receive little direct-instanton contribution, while previously unstable sum rules are stabilized by large instanton contributions. This enables a third reliable sum rule for nucleon magnetic moments, strengthening the mutual consistency of the background-field sum-rule approach.
