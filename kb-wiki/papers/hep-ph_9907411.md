# Mass shift, width broadening and spectral density of rho-mesons produced in heavy ion collisions

> Our recent work on modifications of rho-mesons formed at the last stage of evolution of hadronic matter produced in heavy ion collisions is summarized. # Mass shift, width broadening and spectral density of rho-mesons produced in heavy ion collisions

## Key claim
The mass shift of rho-mesons in hadronic matter formed at the last stage of heavy ion collisions is on the order of a few tens of MeV, but the width and spectral density become so broad that the rho may lose its identity as a well defined resonance. The authors caution that their results are only semiquantitative because the description of nuclear matter as a weakly interacting gas of pions and nucleons cannot be considered a very good one.

## Method
The authors calculated the in-medium self-energy of rho-mesons using on-shell forward scattering amplitudes and total cross sections for rho-pion and rho-nucleon interactions, obtained by saturating the low-energy part with resonances and combining vector meson dominance with Regge theory at high energy. The mass shift and width were evaluated for temperatures of 100 and 150 MeV and nucleon densities of 0, 1, and 2 times normal nuclear matter density (0.155 nucleons/fm^3), using Fermi-Dirac distributions for nucleons with specified chemical potentials. The dispersion relation and spectral density were computed by summing over all target species and including the vacuum contribution to the self-energy via the Gounaris-Sakurai formula.

## Key numbers
- Mass shift of rho-mesons is on the order of tens of MeV, with the main effect coming from nucleons rather than pions (pion-only effect is negligible, on the order of 1 MeV).
- At 1 times normal nuclear density (0.155 nucleons/fm^3), the rho meson width is about 240 MeV; at 2 times normal density, the width is about 370 MeV.
- At beam energies of a few GeV·A, the width increases by several hundred MeV; at about 100 GeV·A, the width increases by twice that amount.
- The nucleon chemical potentials used were 745 and 820 MeV for densities of 1 and 2 times normal at T = 100 MeV, and 540 and 645 MeV for densities of 1 and 2 times normal at T = 150 MeV.
- The effective mass increases with nucleon density and with momentum but is almost independent of temperature.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Shuryak (1990s) and Eletsky and Ioffe (1990s) on in-medium hadron modifications, using phenomenological information on hadron interactions to calculate mass shifts and width broadenings. The results are roughly consistent with other analyses by Wambach (1990s). The findings are directly relevant to interpreting dilepton production data from heavy ion collisions, such as the Pb-Au data at 160 GeV·A where the rho-peak was found absent at low transverse momentum but reappeared at higher momentum, though the authors note that no conclusion can be drawn without embedding their results in a space-time model of the evolving matter. Applications to thermal and hydrodynamic models of heavy ion collisions are under investigation.
