# Constraining the Symmetry Energy: A Journey in the Isospin Physics from Coulomb Barrier to Deconfinement

> Heavy Ion Collisions (HIC) represent a unique tool to probe the in-medium nuclear interaction in regions away from saturation. In this work we present a selection of reaction observables in dissipative collisions particularly sensitive to the isovector part of the interaction, i.e. to the symmetry term of the nuclear Equation of State (EoS). At low energies the behavior of the symmetry energy around saturation influences dissipation and fragment production mechanisms. We will first discuss the recently observed Dynamical Dipole Radiation, due to a collective neutron-proton oscillation during the charge equilibration in fusion and deep-inelastic collisions. Important Iso-EOS effects are stressed. Reactions induced by unstable 132Sn beams appear to be very promising tools to test the sub-saturation Isovector EoS. New Isospin sensitive observables are also presented for deep-inelastic, fragmentation collisions and Isospin equilibration measurements (Imbalance Ratios). The high density symmetry term can be derived from isospin effects on heavy ion reactions at relativistic energies (few AGeV range), that can even allow a ``direct'' study of the covariant structure of the isovector interaction in the hadron medium. Rather sensitive observables are proposed from collective flows and from pion/kaon production. The possibility of the transition to a mixed hadron-quark phase, at high baryon and isospin density, is finally suggested. Some signatures could come from an expected ``neutron trapping'' effect. The importance of studying violent collisions with radioactive beams from low to relativistic energies is finally stressed.

## Key claim

The paper argues that heavy ion collisions across a wide energy range (from Coulomb barrier to relativistic energies) can systematically constrain the density dependence of the symmetry energy, but the actual constraints depend on the specific observable and the density regime probed, with the stiff versus soft behavior of the symmetry energy producing measurable differences of order 20% in several observables.

## Method

The authors employ semiclassical transport models of Landau-Vlasov type at low to Fermi energies and Relativistic Landau-Vlasov (RLV) transport at relativistic energies, using Skyrme-type effective interactions at low densities and relativistic mean field (RMF) models with different isovector meson fields (NLρ, NLρδ, and DDF) at high densities. Stochastic forces are included to allow growth of dynamical instabilities and fragment formation. The isovector part of the equation of state is tested by comparing two parameterizations: Asysoft (symmetry energy smoothly decreasing at low density) and Asystiff (rapid decrease at low density).

## Key numbers

- Dynamical dipole moment D(t=0) for 132Sn+58Ni at 10 AMeV is approximately 50 fm, about two times the largest values probed before
- Symmetry energy influence on dynamical dipole yields is of order 20%, considered well detectable
- Imbalance ratio analysis for Sn+Sn collisions at 35 and 50 AMeV shows systematic difference between stiff and soft iso-EOS of about 20 percent
- Central collisions studied at 50 AMeV for 112Sn+112Sn, 124Sn+124Sn, and 132Sn+132Sn with (N/Z)in = 1.24, 1.48, and 1.64 respectively
- Relativistic collisions studied for 132Sn+124Sn at 1.5 AGeV (b=6 fm) and 197Au+197Au in the 0.8-1.8 AGeV beam energy range
- Pion yield freezes out at times of order 50 fm/c; kaon production saturates at around 20 fm/c

## Context

This work extends the program of constraining the symmetry energy through heavy ion collisions, building on earlier transport code developments by Baran et al. (2005) and Chomaz et al. (2004). The dynamical dipole radiation mechanism was suggested by Chomaz et al. (1993) and Bortignon et al. (1993), with experimental evidence from Flib et al. (1996) and Pierrou et al. (2004). The isospin transport analysis using imbalance ratios follows the approach of Tsang et al. (1992) and extends it with correlation to kinetic energy loss. The relativistic treatment of isovector interactions with ρ and δ meson fields follows Liu et al. (2002) and Gaitanos et al. (2004). The paper enables future experiments with radioactive beams (particularly 132Sn at SPIRAL2) to test sub-saturation symmetry energy, and suggests that high isospin density collisions may probe the hadron-quark deconfinement transition, with possible "neutron trapping" signatures.
