# New facts about muon production in Extended Air Shower simulations

> Whereas air shower simulations are very valuable tools for interpreting cosmic ray data, there is a long standing problem: is seems to be impossible to accommodate at the same time the longitudinal development of air showers and the number of muons measured at ground. Using a new hadronic interaction model (EPOS) in air shower simulations produces considerably more muons, in agreement with results from the HiRes-MIA experiment. We find that this is mainly due to a better description of baryon-antibaryon production in hadronic interactions. This is a new aspect of air shower physics which has never been considered so far.

## Key claim

Using the EPOS hadronic interaction model in air shower simulations produces considerably more muons than older models, bringing simulations into agreement with HiRes-MIA experimental data for the first time; this increase is mainly due to a better description of baryon-antibaryon production in hadronic interactions, an aspect of air shower physics that had not been considered before.

## Method

The authors introduced the EPOS 1.35 hadronic interaction model into the CORSIKA and CONEX air shower simulation frameworks, using GHEISHA for low-energy interactions below 80 GeV. They simulated proton, iron, and carbon induced showers and compared the resulting shower maximum (Xmax) and muon density at ground with results from the QGSJET01 model and with HiRes-MIA experimental data. To isolate the effect of baryon-antibaryon production, they artificially reduced EPOS baryon-antibaryon production by a factor of two and increased it in SIBYLL by a factor of two, observing the corresponding changes in muon yield.

## Key numbers

* Muon density at ground at 10^18 eV increased by about 40% using EPOS compared to QGSJET01 calculations.
* The EPOS proton line for muon density lies exactly on the QGSJET01 iron line (which was the model giving the highest muon number before).
* At 10^20 eV, EPOS gives a factor of 2 more muons than QGSJET01, and EPOS with proton primaries gives even more muons than QGSJET01 with iron primaries.
* At the lowest energies, the EPOS proton line is at most 25% higher than QGSJET01.
* Reducing EPOS baryon-antibaryon production by a factor of two reduced the muon number by 30%, bringing it close to QGSJET01 values.
* Increasing SIBYLL baryon-antibaryon production by a factor of two resulted in 30% more muons.
* The conversion factor for calorimetric energy measured by fluorescence detectors changes by about 5% when using EPOS.

## Context

This work addresses a long-standing problem in air shower simulations where it seemed impossible to simultaneously reproduce the longitudinal development of air showers and the number of muons measured at ground using models like QGSJET01 (Kalmykov et al., 1997), QGSJET II (Ostapchenko, 2006), and SIBYLL 2.1 (Fletcher et al., 1994). The HiRes-MIA experiment (Abu-Zayyad et al., 2000) had shown inconsistencies between data and simulations, and the Pierre Auger Observatory found a 25% discrepancy in energy reconstruction that could be explained by a lack of muons in simulations. The paper demonstrates that baryon-antibaryon production, previously neglected in air shower physics, is a key mechanism that can increase muon numbers without changing Xmax, enabling consistent interpretation of both observables with a primary composition changing from carbon-like at 10^17 eV to proton-like at 10^18 eV.
