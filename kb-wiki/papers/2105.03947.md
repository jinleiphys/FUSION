# QCD phase transition and equation of state of stellar strong interaction matter via Dyson-Schwinger equation approach

> We study the phase structure and phase transition of cold dense QCD matter via the Dyson-Schwinger equation approach. We take the rainbow approximation and the Gaussian-type gluon model. In order to guarantee that the quark number density begins to appear at the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition chemical potential, we propose a chemical potential dependent modification factor for the gluon model. We find that for the iso-symmetric quark matter, the modification reduces the chemical potential of the phase coexistence region of the first--order phase transition. We also implement the relativistic mean field theory to describe the hadron matter, and make use of the Maxwell and Gibbs construction method to study the phase transition of beta--equilibrium and charge neutral matter in compact stars. The results show that the phase transition will not happen in case of the Gaussian--type gluon model without any modification. The results also indicate that the upper boundary of the coexistence region should be larger than the current Nambu solution existing region. We also calculate the mass-radius relation of the compact stars, and find that the hadron-quark phase transition happens at too high chemical potential so that the maximum mass of the compact star is hardly affected by the hadron-quark phase transition.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a chemical-potential-dependent modification to the Gaussian-type gluon model within the Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach to force the onset of quark number density at the nuclear liquid-gas phase transition chemical potential (μB = 923 MeV). The authors find that this modification reduces the chemical potential of the first-order phase coexistence region for isospin-symmetric matter, but that for beta-equilibrium matter in compact stars, the hadron-quark phase transition occurs at such a high chemical potential that the maximum mass of the star is hardly affected.

## Method
The authors solve the quark Dyson-Schwinger equation at zero temperature and finite chemical potential using the rainbow approximation and a Gaussian-type effective gluon interaction. To enforce the correct nuclear liquid-gas transition point, they introduce a chemical-potential-dependent modification factor h(μ) to the coupling strength D, testing two forms: an exponential damping (α-type) and a function with a polynomial prefactor (β-type). For the hadron phase, they use the relativistic mean field (RMF) theory with the TW-99 parameterization, and they combine the hadron and quark equations of state using both Maxwell and Gibbs construction methods to study phase transitions in beta-equilibrium, charge-neutral matter.

## Key numbers
- The critical coupling strength for the onset of quark number density at μq,c = 307.6 MeV is Dc = 0.716 D0, where D0 = 1.0 GeV².
- The parameters for the modification functions are α = 0.883 and β = 2.714.
- For isospin-symmetric matter, the coexistence region for the unmodified Gaussian model is μq ∈ [0.322, 0.500] GeV; for the α-type modification, it is μq ∈ [0.271, 0.309] GeV; and for the β-type modification, it is μq ∈ [0.276, 0.308] GeV.
- The critical chemical potential for the strange quark Wigner solution to appear is μs,c = 0.461 GeV for the α-type modification and μs,c = 0.420 GeV for the β-type modification.
- The initial pressure difference between the Wigner and Nambu solutions at the left boundary of the coexistence region is 2.58 × 10⁻⁴ GeV⁴ (unmodified), 4.32 × 10⁻⁴ GeV⁴ (α-type), and 4.19 × 10⁻⁴ GeV⁴ (β-type).

## Context
This work extends previous DSE studies of cold dense matter (Chen 2008, Chen 2011, Chen 2012, Chen 2015, Xu 2015, Chen 2016) by introducing a chemical-potential-dependent modification to the gluon model to align the DSE results with the known nuclear liquid-gas phase transition. It contrasts with earlier DSE calculations where the critical chemical potential for the emergence of quark number density did not match the empirical value of 923 MeV. The results enable the construction of hybrid star equations of state and mass-radius relations that can be compared with observations of massive compact stars (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013) and gravitational wave constraints (Abbott 2017).
