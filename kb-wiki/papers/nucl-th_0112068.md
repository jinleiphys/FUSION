# Production of phi mesons in subthreshold heavy-ion collisions

> Within a BUU type transport model we study $ϕ$ meson production in subthreshold Ni+Ni and Ru+Ru reactions. For the first time we included in our model the elementary reaction channels $ρ+N,Δ\to ϕ+N$, $π+N(1520) \to ϕ+N$ and $πρ$ $\to$ $ϕ$. In spite of a substantial increase of the $ϕ$ multiplicities by these channels our results stay significantly below the preliminary experimental data.

## Key claim
Including the new elementary channels ρ+N,Δ → φ+N, π+N(1520) → φ+N, and πρ → φ substantially increases the calculated φ meson multiplicities in subthreshold Ni+Ni and Ru+Ru reactions, but the results still remain significantly below the preliminary experimental data from the FOPI collaboration.

## Method
The authors employed a BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model to simulate φ meson production in Ni+Ni at 1.93 A·GeV and Ru+Ru at 1.69 A·GeV. They calculated cross sections for the new elementary channels using a one-boson exchange model with effective interaction Lagrangians, fitting coupling constants to known decay widths, and included resonance properties up to 2.2 GeV with a soft momentum-dependent potential for nucleons and Δ(1232) baryons.

## Key numbers
- Total φ meson yield per central event in Ni+Ni at 1.93 A·GeV: 1.7 × 10⁻³ (calculated) vs. (8.7 ± 3.6) × 10⁻³ (preliminary experimental)
- Total φ meson yield per central event in Ru+Ru at 1.69 A·GeV: 2.0 × 10⁻³ (calculated) vs. (6.4 ± 2.5) × 10⁻³ (preliminary experimental)
- Contribution from ρ+B channel in Ni+Ni: 8.9 × 10⁻⁴ per central event
- Contribution from ρ+B channel in Ru+Ru: 11.8 × 10⁻⁴ per central event
- φN elastic scattering cross section used in the model: 0.56 mb
- Coupling constants for the ρNN(1520) vertex: f₁ = 10.5 (vector Lagrangian) and f₂ = 68 (tensor Lagrangian)

## Context
This work extends earlier transport model calculations by Chung et al. that only included BB → φBB, πB → φB, and KK → φ channels and substantially underestimated the FOPI data. The new channels increase φ yields by a factor of 2.5, but the persistent discrepancy with the preliminary experimental results of Kotte and the FOPI collaboration raises a "φ puzzle" that the authors suggest requires dedicated measurements with enlarged statistics to resolve.
