# From Chiral quark dynamics with Polyakov loop to the hadron resonance gas model

> Chiral quark models with Polyakov loop at finite temperature have been often used to describe the phase transition. We show how the transition to a hadron resonance gas is realized based on the quantum and local nature of the Polyakov loop.

## Key claim

The authors show that Polyakov-loop-extended Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) models, when treated with a local and quantum Polyakov loop rather than a global mean-field approximation, can be re-expressed as a hadron resonance gas (HRG) at low temperatures, thereby establishing a quark-hadron duality that reproduces lattice QCD results up to about 0.75 T_c.

## Method

The authors start from a PNJL partition function with a local SU(N_c) Polyakov loop field Ω(x) and a gluonic action that yields an exponentially decaying correlation function ⟨tr_c Ω(x) tr_c Ω^†(y)⟩ ∝ exp(-σ|x-y|/T). They expand the partition function to lowest order in the quark fugacity, obtaining a classical partition function for a qq̅ system with Hamiltonian H = √(p²+M²) + √(p²+M²) + σr. They then quantize this two-body Hamiltonian via a Salpeter equation (2√(p²+M²) + σr) ψ_n = M_n ψ_n, which yields discrete meson masses M_α, and similarly quantize the heavy-light Hamiltonian for the Polyakov loop.

## Key numbers

* The chiral-deconfinement crossover occurs at a common critical temperature T_c ∼ 200 MeV, as predicted by lattice QCD.
* The lowest-order approximations reproduce lattice results up to T ∼ 0.75 T_c, with T_c ≈ 250 MeV in the model.
* The ratio Γ/M for mesons and baryons listed in the PDG is 0.12(8), consistent with the large N_c expectation Γ/M = O(N_c^{-1}).
* A temperature shift of T_0 = 10–20 MeV is required to match HRG results to lattice QCD data for the trace anomaly.
* The confinement domain volume is V_σ ≡ 8π T³/σ³, which controls the cross-over between deconfinement and chiral symmetry restoration.

## Context

This work extends the authors’ previous studies of local Polyakov loop models (Megias 2004, 2006) and their HRG representation of the Polyakov loop (Megias 2012). It provides a theoretical bridge between chiral quark models and the HRG approach that has been used to saturate lattice QCD results (Bazavov 2009, Borsanyi 2010). The quantization procedure removes the continuous-spectrum prefactors present in the classical treatment and yields discrete hadron masses, making the independence of deconfinement and chiral restoration evident at low temperatures, as seen in lattice calculations (Bazavov 2011).
