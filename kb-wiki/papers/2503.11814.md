# NNPDFpol2.0: a global determination of polarised PDFs and their uncertainties at next-to-next-to-leading order

> We present NNPDFpol2.0, a new set of collinear helicity parton distribution functions (PDFs) of the proton based on legacy measurements of structure functions in inclusive neutral-current longitudinally polarised deep-inelastic scattering (DIS), and of W -boson, single-inclusive, and di-jet production asymmetries in longitudinally polarised proton-proton collisions. The determination is accurate to next-to-next-to-leading order in the strong coupling, and includes heavy quark mass corrections in the analysis of DIS data. Uncertainties due to missing higher-order corrections are systematically incorporated by means of a covariance matrix determined by scale variations. NNPDFpol2.0 is based on a machine learning methodology, that makes use of Monte Carlo sampling for the representation of uncertainties into PDFs, of a neural network for the parametrisation of PDFs, of stochastic gradient descent for the optimisation of PDF parameters, and of hyperoptimisation for the selection of the best fitting model. We study the impact on PDFs of higher-order corrections, of the positivity constraint, and of the data. We demonstrate two phenomenological applications of NNPDFpol2.0, specifically the determination of the proton spin fraction carried by gluons and quarks, and of theoretical predictions for single-hadron production in longitudinally polarised DIS and proton-proton collisions.

## Key claim

NNPDFpol2.0 provides a new set of collinear helicity parton distribution functions (PDFs) at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) accuracy, but the perturbative dependence of the fit is expected to be mild because the physical observables are spin asymmetries where perturbative corrections mostly cancel in the ratio.

## Method

The determination uses legacy measurements of the polarised structure function g1 in inclusive neutral-current deep-inelastic scattering (DIS) from CERN, SLAC, DESY, and JLab experiments, combined with W-boson longitudinal single-spin asymmetries from STAR at sqrt(s)=510 GeV, and single-inclusive jet and di-jet longitudinal double-spin asymmetries from PHENIX and STAR at sqrt(s)=200 and 510 GeV. Theoretical predictions are computed using yadism for DIS, a modified version of MCFM for W-boson production, and the code of de Florian (1998) and Jager (2004) for jet production, all interfaced to PineAPPL for fast interpolation grids. Missing higher-order uncertainties (MHOUs) are incorporated via a covariance matrix from 7-point renormalisation and factorisation scale variations, with an additional 3-point prescription for jet processes where NNLO matrix elements are unknown.

## Key numbers

- Total data points after kinematic cuts: N_dat = 951, irrespective of perturbative accuracy
- Kinematic cuts applied: Q^2 >= 1.0 GeV^2 and W^2 >= 4.0 GeV^2
- NNLO K-factors for W-boson production are at most of order 3% and relatively independent of lepton rapidity
- Monte Carlo integration errors for W-boson asymmetries are below 1 per mil, except for the leftmost bin in positron pseudorapidity of W+ where they reach 1.5 per mil
- Interpolation uncertainties from PineAPPL grids are at most 0.5 per mil difference between central values
- STAR W-boson data at sqrt(s)=510 GeV covers lepton pseudorapidity range -1.25 <= eta_lpm <= +1.25
- PHENIX and STAR jet data at sqrt(s)=200 GeV and STAR jet data at sqrt(s)=510 GeV

## Context

This work extends the NNPDFpol1.1 determination (Nocera, 2014) by moving from NLO to NNLO accuracy, updating COMPASS data with legacy measurements from 2015 and 2016, adding JLab Hall A and CLAS data, and including MHOUs via scale variations following the methodology developed for unpolarised PDFs (NNPDF, 2019; NNPDF, 2024). The determination enables improved predictions for the EIC era, particularly for the proton spin fraction carried by gluons and quarks, and for single-hadron production in polarised DIS and proton-proton collisions.
