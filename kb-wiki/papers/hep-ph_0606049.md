# Ampere's Law and Energy Loss in AdS/CFT Duality

> We note that the energy loss in ${\cal N}=4$ SYM measures directly the spatial string tension $σ_S=π\sqrtλT^2/2$ which is at the origin of the area law for large spatial Wilson loops. We show that the latter reflects on the nonperturbative nature of Ampere's law in ${\cal N}=4$ SYM both in vacuum and at finite temperature.

# Ampere's Law and Energy Loss in AdS/CFT Duality

## Key claim

The energy loss of a heavy probe in hot N=4 SYM at strong coupling originates from the magnetic sector (Ampere's law) rather than the electric sector, because Coulomb's law is screened while Ampere's law is not; the energy loss directly measures the spatial string tension σ_S = π√λ T²/2, and this interpretation follows from the observation that the string action becomes purely imaginary near the black-hole horizon when the electric part is completely screened.

## Method

The authors analyze spatial Wilson loops in the AdS/CFT correspondence using the AdS black-hole metric with horizon at z₀ = 1/πT, computing the Nambu-Goto action for helicoidal minimal surfaces near the horizon to extract the spatial string tension and the forces between current-carrying wires. They compare the strong-coupling results with leading-order perturbative calculations using thermal Wightman functions for the gluon propagator, and apply an ordered ladder resummation to the magnetic contribution to the Bethe-Salpeter kernel. The energy loss is derived from the imaginary part of the string action for an open string of length L pulled with velocity v near the black-hole horizon.

## Key numbers

- Spatial string tension: σ_S = π√λ T²/2 (Equation 3)
- Color opacity length: 1/πT, independent of frequency/energy content
- For a heavy Q̅Q configuration with velocity v, the string breaks at height z_max/z₀ = (1−v²)^{1/4}
- At zero temperature, the ladder-resummed magnetic contribution gives energy E(L) = √(v²) √(λ/2) / (π L)
- For small v, the drag force yields Stoke's law with effective particle size r ≈ √λ / (N_c² T)
- Diffusion constant for slow particles: D = T/σ_S
- For QCD at RHIC with T ≈ 1.5 T_c, the spatial string tension is σ_S ≈ 1 GeV/fm

## Context

This work extends the analysis of energy loss in hot N=4 SYM reported by Yaffe (2005), Gubser (2006), Herzog (2006), and others, connecting it to the spatial string tension and Ampere's law. The authors build on the observation by Yee (2005) that the static color force is screened in hot SYM, and on the analysis of time-like and space-like Q̅Q configurations by Polchinski (2002) and collaborators. The result that the spatial string tension is measurable through energy loss enables direct comparison with lattice measurements of the spatial string tension in QCD across T_c, as reported by the STRINGT collaboration, and suggests that jet quenching at RHIC may measure this quantity. The work also relates to the suggestion by Gell-Mann (section Vb) that Stoke's law and Einstein's formulae apply to moving particles in AdS/CFT.
