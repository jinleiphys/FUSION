# Hydrodynamic and Non-hydrodynamic Excitations in Kinetic Theory -- A Numerical Analysis in Scalar Field Theory

> Viscous hydrodynamics serves as a successful mesoscopic description of the Quark-Gluon Plasma produced in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. In order to investigate, how such an effective description emerges from the underlying microscopic dynamics we calculate the hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic modes of linear response in the sound channel from a first-principle calculation in kinetic theory. We do this with a new approach wherein we discretize the collision kernel to directly calculate eigenvalues and eigenmodes of the evolution operator. This allows us to study the Green's functions at any point in the complex frequency space. Our study focuses on scalar theory with quartic interaction and we find that the analytic structure of Green's functions in the complex plane is far more complicated than just poles or cuts which is a first step towards an equivalent study in QCD kinetic theory.

# Hydrodynamic and Non-hydrodynamic Excitations in Kinetic Theory -- A Numerical Analysis in Scalar Field Theory

## Key claim
The analytic structure of energy-momentum Green's functions in the complex frequency plane for scalar $\phi^4$ kinetic theory is "far more complicated than just poles or cuts," extending beyond the simple branch-cut-plus-poles structure known from Relaxation Time Approximation (RTA). This claim is conditional on the use of an effective kinetic description derived from a combined weak-coupling and gradient expansion, which restricts the real part of complex eigenvalues to the range ${-k<\rm Re}(\omega)<k$.

## Method
The authors discretize the linearized Boltzmann equation for scalar $\phi^4$ theory with quartic interactions by expanding the distribution function in wedge-function moments over momentum magnitude $p$ and polar angle $\cos\theta$, converting the collision integral and gradient term into finite-dimensional matrices. They compute eigenvalues and eigenvectors of the combined evolution operator $(C+M)$ for nonzero wave number $k$, then reconstruct retarded Green's functions of the energy-momentum tensor as sums over eigenmodes, enabling direct evaluation at any complex frequency. The method is benchmarked against known analytic RTA results before application to $\phi^4$ theory.

## Key numbers
- Viscosity of scalar $\phi^4$ theory: $\eta \approx 3040 \, T^3/\lambda^2$ (Jeon 1995), giving $\eta/s \approx 6930/\lambda^2$ for an ultrarelativistic boson gas.
- Speed of sound: $c_s^2 = 1/3$.
- Second-order transport coefficient: $\bar{\tau}_{\pi} \approx 6.1$ (York 2008).
- Discretization used for production results: $N_p \times N_{\cos\theta} = 64 \times 64$.
- In RTA benchmark, the branch cut is located at $\bar{\omega} = -0.2i$, corresponding to $\omega = -i/\tau_R$ with $\eta/s = \tau_R T/5$.

## Context
This work extends prior zero-gradient ($k=0$) studies of scalar $\phi^4$ kinetic theory by Moore (2018), which found a cut on the imaginary axis, into the finite-$k$ regime. It also generalizes the RTA analysis of hydrodynamic and non-hydrodynamic modes by Romatschke (2015) and Kurkela (2017) to a more realistic collision kernel. The method enables direct calculation of the full complex-frequency structure of Green's functions, which is a step toward equivalent studies in QCD kinetic theory and toward understanding when non-hydrodynamic modes dominate the dynamics, thereby defining the boundaries of hydrodynamic applicability in heavy-ion collisions.
