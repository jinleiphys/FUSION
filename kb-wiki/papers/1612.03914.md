# An $ε'$ improvement from right-handed currents

> Recent lattice QCD calculations of direct CP violation in $K_L \to ππ$ decays indicate tension with the experimental results. Assuming this tension to be real, we investigate a possible beyond-the-Standard Model explanation via right-handed charged currents. By using chiral perturbation theory in combination with lattice QCD results, we accurately calculate the modification of $ε'/ε$ induced by right-handed charged currents and extract values of the couplings that are necessary to explain the discrepancy, pointing to a scale around $10^2$ TeV. We find that couplings of this size are not in conflict with constraints from other precision experiments, but next-generation hadronic electric dipole moment searches (such as neutron and ${}^{225}$Ra) can falsify this scenario. We work out in detail a direct link, based on chiral perturbation theory, between CP violation in the kaon sector and electric dipole moments induced by right-handed currents which can be used in future analyses of left-right symmetric models.

## Key claim

Assuming the tension between lattice QCD calculations of direct CP violation in $K_L \to \pi\pi$ decays and the experimental result is real, the paper investigates whether right-handed charged currents can explain the discrepancy, finding that couplings at a scale around $10^2$ TeV can produce the needed enhancement without conflicting with current precision constraints, but that next-generation electric dipole moment searches can falsify this scenario.

## Method

The authors use chiral perturbation theory (ChPT) in combination with lattice QCD results to calculate the modification of $\varepsilon'/\varepsilon$ induced by a single dimension-six operator parameterizing right-handed charged currents. They match the four-quark operators induced at the weak scale onto the chiral Lagrangian, relate the low-energy constants to the matrix elements of electroweak penguin operators $\mathcal{Q}_7$ and $\mathcal{Q}_8$ using lattice QCD input from Blum et al. (2015), and compute the resulting contributions to both $\varepsilon'/\varepsilon$ and hadronic/nuclear electric dipole moments (EDMs) through the same $(8_L,8_R)$ representation of the chiral group.

## Key numbers

- Experimental world average: $(\varepsilon'/\varepsilon)_{\rm exp} = (16.6 \pm 2.3) \times 10^{-4}$
- Standard Model prediction from lattice QCD: $(\varepsilon'/\varepsilon)_{\rm SM} = (1.4 \pm 6.9) \times 10^{-4}$, indicating a $2\sigma$ discrepancy
- Low-energy constants extracted from lattice QCD: $\mathcal{A}_{1\,LR}(3\ \mathrm{GeV}) = (2.2 \pm 0.13)\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$ and $\mathcal{A}_{2\,LR}(3\ \mathrm{GeV}) = (10.1 \pm 0.6)\ \mathrm{GeV}^2$
- New physics scale required to explain the discrepancy: around $10^2$ TeV
- Current neutron EDM limit: $3.0 \times 10^{-13}\ e\ \mathrm{fm}$; expected sensitivity: $1.0 \times 10^{-15}\ e\ \mathrm{fm}$
- Current mercury EDM limit: $6.2 \times 10^{-17}\ e\ \mathrm{fm}$; expected sensitivity: $6.2 \times 10^{-17}\ e\ \mathrm{fm}$
- Current radium EDM limit: $1.2 \times 10^{-10}\ e\ \mathrm{fm}$; expected sensitivity: $1.0 \times 10^{-14}\ e\ \mathrm{fm}$

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $\varepsilon'/\varepsilon$ in left-right symmetric models by Bertolini et al. (2012, 2014) and EDM calculations by Zhang et al. (2007), An et al. (2009), and Maiezza et al. (2014), but is the first to explicitly correlate the enhancement of $\varepsilon'/\varepsilon$ with EDMs from right-handed currents in the recent literature. The results enable future analyses of left-right symmetric models by providing a direct ChPT-based link between CP violation in the kaon sector and hadronic EDMs, and identify next-generation neutron and radium EDM searches as the most promising probes to falsify the scenario.
