# Medium effects for hadron-tagged jets in proton-proton collisions

> We study the medium modification factor $I_{pp}$ within the light-cone path integral approach to induced gluon emission. We use parametrization of the running coupling $α_s(Q,T)$ which has a plateau around $Q\sim κT$. We calculate $I_{pp}$ with no free parameters using $κ$ fitted to the LHC data on the nuclear modification factor $R_{AA}$. We find that the theoretical multiplicity dependence of the ratio $I_{pp}/\langle I_{pp}\rangle$ for $5.02$ TeV $pp$ collisions agrees reasonably with the recent preliminary data from ALICE [1].

## Key claim

The paper claims that the observed decrease of the medium modification factor \(I_{pp}\) with increasing underlying-event (UE) multiplicity in 5.02 TeV proton-proton collisions is consistent with jet quenching in a mini quark-gluon plasma (mQGP), provided one allows for incomplete thermalization of the mQGP at low multiplicity densities (below \(dN_{ch}/d\eta \sim 10\)).

## Method

The author calculates the medium-modified fragmentation functions for hadron-tagged jets using the light-cone path integral (LCPI) approach to induced gluon emission, with a temperature-dependent running coupling \(\alpha_s(Q,T)\) that has a plateau at \(Q\sim \kappa T\). The parameter \(\kappa=2.55\) is fixed by a \(\chi^2\) fit to LHC data on the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) for Pb+Pb and Xe+Xe collisions. The di-hadron and one-hadron cross sections are computed by convoluting partonic cross sections with medium-modified fragmentation functions, using a Bjorken 1+1D expanding mQGP fireball model with two versions (A and B) for the initial entropy density, where version B includes a multiplicity-dependent thermalization factor.

## Key numbers

- The parameter \(\kappa = 2.55\) is obtained from fitting LHC data on \(R_{AA}\) for 2.76 and 5.02 TeV Pb+Pb, and 5.44 TeV Xe+Xe collisions.
- For the minimum bias UE multiplicity \(dN_{ch}/d\eta \approx 12.5\), the fireball radius is \(R_f \approx 1.48\) fm, and the average initial fireball temperature is \(T_0 \approx 226\) MeV (ideal gas) or \(T_0 \approx 256\) MeV (lattice entropy density).
- The ALICE data show \(I_{pp}\) decreases monotonically by about 15% as the UE multiplicity increases in the range \(5 \lesssim dN_{ch}/d\eta \lesssim 20\).
- The theoretical ratio \(I_{pp}/\langle I_{pp}\rangle\) is computed for 5.02 TeV \(pp\) collisions and compared to ALICE preliminary data, with the trigger hadron momentum \(8 < p_T^t < 15\) GeV and associated hadron momentum \(4 < p_T^a < 6\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\) (Zakharov, 2021) and the earlier study of \(I_{pp}\) for photon-tagged jets (Zakharov, 2013) to hadron-tagged jets in proton-proton collisions. It directly addresses the first measurement of the UE multiplicity dependence of \(I_{pp}\) by the ALICE collaboration (ALICE, 2022). The results support the interpretation that a mini quark-gluon plasma is formed in high-multiplicity \(pp\) collisions, and the observed drop of \(I_{pp}\) with multiplicity, if confirmed, would constitute the first direct evidence for jet quenching in \(pp\) collisions.
