# The NICER data and a $σ$-field dependent stiffness of the hadronic equation of state

> Analyses for the NICER data indicate that there is no significant variation of the compact star radii within the mass range of 1.4 to 2.0 solar masses. Yamamoto et al. [Phys. Rev. C 108, 035811 (2023)] concluded recently that ``this feature cannot be reproduced by the hadronic matter due to the softening of the equation of state (EoS) by hyperon mixing, suggesting the possible existence of quark phases in neutron-star interiors.'' Using a collection of 162 purely nucleonic, hyperonic, and quarkish EoSs from CompOSE database and some other works, we verify that hyperons indeed lead to a significant difference in radii of stars of 1.4 and 2.0 solar masses, which diminishes in the presence of quarks. We compare the shapes of the mass-radius curves and show that hyperons and quarks in the neutron star cores prefer a particular curve shape with backbending. It is argued that the shape {is controlled by the density dependence} of the nuclear symmetry energy. We draw attention to the existence of a class of purely hadronic relativistic mean-field EoSs with scalar-field dependent hadron masses and coupling constants that satisfy the known constraints on the EoSs including the analyses of the new NICER data and the above requirement of no significant variation of the neutron star radii.

# Wiki Page: The NICER data and a σ-field dependent stiffness of the hadronic equation of state

## Key claim

Purely hadronic equations of state with σ-field dependent hadron masses and coupling constants (SHMC models) can satisfy the NICER-data-based constraint that neutron star radii do not vary significantly between 1.4 and 2.0 solar masses, contrary to the claim by Yamamoto et al. (2023) that hyperon softening makes this impossible without quark phases. The authors demonstrate this using a specific class of relativistic mean-field models (KVORcut03 and MKVOR families) that include hyperons and Δ baryons while still meeting the radius-constancy condition.

## Method

The authors collected 162 equations of state from the CompOSE database and other sources, retaining 103 that produce maximum neutron star masses above 2.0 solar masses. They computed radii for 1.4 and 2.0 solar mass neutron stars for each EoS, separating them into purely nucleonic (63 EoSs), hyperonic/Δ-containing (18 EoSs), and hybrid nucleon-quark (22 EoSs) categories. They compared these against NICER data analyses from Miller et al. (2019, 2021) and Riley et al. (2019, 2021), and examined the correlation between the radius difference ΔR(1.4-2.0)M⊙ and the shape of the mass-radius curve characterized by the derivative at the inflection point.

## Key numbers

- 162 EoSs collected from CompOSE database and other works; 103 retained with Mmax > 2.0 M⊙
- 63 purely nucleonic EoSs, 18 EoSs with hyperons and/or Δ baryons, 22 hybrid nucleon-quark EoSs
- Radius difference ΔR(1.4-2.0)M⊙ = 0.12^{+0.85}_{-0.83} km (Miller et al. analysis) and 0.20^{+0.8}_{-0.82} km (Riley et al. analysis)
- Combined analysis constraint: -0.68 km < ΔR(1.4-2.0)M⊙ < 0.35 km
- For MKVOR* model (purely nucleonic SHMC): ΔR(1.4-2.0)M⊙ = -0.1 km
- For KVORcut03 model (purely nucleonic SHMC): ΔR(1.4-2.0)M⊙ = +0.4 km
- For MKVOR*HΔφσ model (with hyperons and Δs): ΔR(1.4-2.0)M⊙ = -0.03 km
- For KVORcut03HΔφσ model (with hyperons and Δs): ΔR(1.4-2.0)M⊙ = 0.5 km
- Symmetry energy slope parameter L = 41 MeV for MKVOR-type models; L = 71 MeV for KVOR-type models
- Maximum mass constraint: 2.1 M⊙ < Mmax < 2.33 M⊙ for non-rotating neutron stars
- PSR J0740+6620 mass: 2.08(7) M⊙; radius: 13.7^{+2.6}_{-1.5} km (Miller et al. 2021) and 12.39^{+1.30}_{-0.98} km (Riley et al. 2021)

## Context

This work extends the SHMC (scaling hadron masses and coupling constants) model framework developed by the authors in prior papers (Kolomeitsev and Voskresensky 2005, 2015, 2017, 2018) and directly addresses the claim by Yamamoto et al. (2023) that hyperon-induced softening of the EoS requires quark phases to explain NICER radius data. The paper contradicts the interpretation that hybrid EoSs are necessary, showing that purely hadronic SHMC models with σ-field dependent masses can satisfy the radius-constancy constraint. The analysis uses the EoS collection compiled by Ofengeim (2023) and compares against NICER analyses by Miller et al. (2019, 2021), Riley et al. (2019, 2021), Legred et al. (2021), and Raaijmakers et al. (2021). The results enable continued use of purely hadronic models for neutron star structure calculations and suggest that future gravitational wave detectors like Cosmic Explorer could verify the radius-constancy prediction with 100-meter precision.
