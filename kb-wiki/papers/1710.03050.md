# Effects of initial-state dynamics on collective flow within a coupled transport and viscous hydrodynamic approach

> We evaluate the effects of preequilibrium dynamics on observables in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions. We simulate the initial nonequilibrium phase within A MultiPhase Transport (AMPT) model, while the subsequent near-equilibrium evolution is modeled using (2+1)-dimensional relativistic viscous hydrodynamics. We match the two stages of evolution carefully by calculating the full energy-momentum tensor from AMPT and using it as input for the hydrodynamic evolution. We find that when the preequilibrium evolution is taken into account, final-state observables are insensitive to the switching time from AMPT to hydrodynamics. Unlike some earlier treatments of preequilibrium dynamics, we do not find the initial shear viscous tensor to be large. With a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of $0.12$, our model describes quantitatively a large set of experimental data on Pb+Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider(LHC) over a wide range of centrality: differential anisotropic flow $v_n(p_T) ~(n=2-6)$, event-plane correlations, correlation between $v_2$ and $v_3$, and cumulant ratio $v_2\{4\}/v_2\{2\}$.

## Key claim

When the full energy-momentum tensor from AMPT preequilibrium dynamics is used as input for viscous hydrodynamics, final-state observables become insensitive to the switching time between the two stages, and the initial shear viscous tensor is not large, contrary to some earlier treatments where preequilibrium dynamics produced large shear tensors that had to be arbitrarily set to zero.

## Method

The authors simulate the initial nonequilibrium phase of Pb+Pb collisions using the AMPT model with string melting and parton-parton elastic cross sections of 1.5 mb, then match the full energy-momentum tensor from AMPT to (2+1)-dimensional second-order viscous hydrodynamics (VISH2+1) on a constant proper-time hypersurface. The energy-momentum tensor is constructed by smearing each parton with a 2D Gaussian of width σ = 0.8 fm, and the Landau matching condition is used to extract energy density, fluid velocity, and shear pressure tensor. The hydrodynamic evolution uses a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.12 with the s95p-PCE equation of state, and hadronic spectra are computed via the Cooper-Frye prescription at a decoupling temperature of 120 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio η/s = 0.12 used throughout the hydrodynamic evolution
- Switching time τ_sw = 0.4 fm/c (and 0.9 fm/c for comparison) between AMPT and hydrodynamics
- Gaussian smearing width σ = 0.8 fm for constructing the energy-momentum tensor from AMPT partons
- Initial shear tensor components (τ²π^{ηη} and Σ = π^{xx} + π^{yy}) normalized by enthalpy density are about 12% at τ_sw = 0.4 fm/c, roughly 40% smaller than Navier-Stokes values
- Initial transverse flow velocity at τ_sw = 0.4 fm/c is about 5% of the speed of light
- Energy density rescaling factor of roughly 1.2 applied to match charged multiplicity density dN_ch/dy at 2.76 TeV in 0-5% centrality
- Decoupling temperature T_dec = 120 MeV for the hydrodynamic freeze-out
- 300 AMPT+Hydro events generated per centrality bin up to 40% centrality, 500 events per bin above 40%
- Parton-parton elastic cross section of 1.5 mb used in AMPT
- Rapidity window -3 < η_s < 3 (width Δη_s = 6) for projecting from 3+1D AMPT to 2+1D hydrodynamics

## Context

This work extends hybrid approaches that couple preequilibrium dynamics to hydrodynamics, improving upon earlier treatments such as IP-Glasma+MUSIC (Gale 2012) which did not conserve the full energy-momentum tensor when switching to hydrodynamics, and superSONIC (Weller 2017) which set the initial shear tensor to zero. It also differs from the free-streaming preequilibrium model of Liu (2015), which produced large initial shear tensors, whereas the AMPT-based approach with realistic parton cross sections drives the system closer to local equilibrium. The model enables quantitative comparison with a broad set of LHC data including differential anisotropic flow v_n(p_T) for n=2-6, event-plane correlations, and cumulant ratios, and demonstrates that preequilibrium dynamics with full T^{μν} matching removes sensitivity to the switching time.
