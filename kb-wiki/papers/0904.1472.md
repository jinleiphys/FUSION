# Quasi-elastic neutrino charged-current scattering off C-12

> The charged-current quasi-elastic scattering of muon neutrino on a carbon target is calculated for neutrino energy up to 2.8 GeV using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation with relativistic optical potential, which was earlier successfully applied to describe electron-nucleus data. We studied both neutrino and electron processes and have shown that the reduced exclusive cross section for neutrino and electron scattering are similar. We have also studied nuclear and axial vector mass effects on the shape of $Q^2$ distribution. The comparison of the (anti)neutrino total cross sections per (proton)neutron, calculated for the carbon and oxygen targets shows the cross sections for oxygen to be lower than those for carbon. We found significant nuclear model dependence of inclusive and total cross sections for energy about 1 GeV.

## Key claim

The reduced exclusive cross sections for electron and neutrino scattering off carbon-12 are similar when calculated using the relativistic distorted-wave impulse approximation (RDWIA), but the relativistic Fermi gas model (RFGM) overestimates cross sections and fails to reproduce exclusive data, particularly at low four-momentum transfer \(Q^2 \leq 0.2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).

## Method

The author calculated charged-current quasi-elastic (anti)neutrino scattering on \(^{12}\)C for energies up to 2.8 GeV using the RDWIA with a relativistic optical potential (EDAD1), comparing results to the plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) and the RFGM. The LEA code was employed to compute exclusive, inclusive, and total cross sections, with bound-state nucleon wave functions from the Hartree-Bogoliubov approximation in the \(\sigma-\omega\) model and final-state interactions treated via the real part of the optical potential. Short-range nucleon-nucleon correlations in the ground state were included using a phenomenological spectral function model.

## Key numbers

- The occupancy of the \(1p_{3/2}\) shell in \(^{12}\)C is 84%, and the \(1s_{1/2}\) shell is 100%, with an average factor of about 89% from RDWIA analysis of \(^{12}\)C\((e,e'p)\) data for \(Q^2 < 2\) (GeV/c)\(^2\).
- At \(Q^2 = 0.1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\), the RFGM overestimates the RDWIA cross section by 12% for neutrinos and 28% for antineutrinos at \(\varepsilon_{\nu} = 0.5\) GeV, decreasing to 7% and 12% respectively at \(\varepsilon_{\nu} = 2.5\) GeV.
- The difference between RDWIA-calculated and measured inclusive electron scattering cross sections at the peak is less than \(\pm 12\%\); for the RFGM, this difference decreases from about 20% at \(|\mathbf{q}| \approx 310\) MeV/c to about 13% at \(|\mathbf{q}| \approx 510\) MeV/c.
- For neutrino energies above 1 GeV, the range \(0.3 \leq Q^2 \leq 1\) (GeV/c)\(^2\) can be used for axial mass \(M_A\) extraction from \(Q^2\) shape-only fits.
- The contribution of \((\nu, \mu N)\) channels to the inclusive cross section is about 60%.

## Context

This work extends earlier RDWIA calculations for neutrino-nucleus scattering (Meu1, Meu2, Meu3, Mair, Ryck1, Ryck2, But2, But3, Kim1, Kim2) by systematically comparing electron and neutrino reduced cross sections and studying nuclear model dependence. The results contradict the simpler RFGM (Moniz, 1969) used in most Monte Carlo event generators, which fails at low \(Q^2\) as seen in K2K (Gran, 2006; Espinal, 2007) and MiniBooNE (MiniA, 2008) data. The findings enable improved modeling of nuclear effects for neutrino oscillation experiments like MINERvA and T2K, particularly for extracting the axial mass \(M_A\) from \(Q^2\) distributions and for comparing cross sections on carbon and oxygen targets.
