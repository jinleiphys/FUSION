# Self-force and synchrotron radiation in odd space-time dimensions

> Classical electrodynamics in flat 3+1 space-time has a very special retarded propagator delta(x^2) localized on the light cone, so that a particle does not interact with its past field. However, this is an exception, and in flat odd-dimensional space-times as well as generic curved spaces this is not so. In this work we show that the so called self-force is not only non-zero, but it matches (in 2+1 dimensions) the radiation reaction force derived from the radiation intensity.

## Key claim

In odd-dimensional flat spacetimes (2+1 and 4+1 dimensions), the retarded electromagnetic propagator has support inside the light cone, so a charged particle experiences a nonzero self-force from its own past field; this self-force matches the radiation reaction force derived from far-field radiation intensity, at least in 2+1 dimensions for ultra-relativistic circular motion.

## Method

The authors compute the covariant Lorentz self-force \(f^\mu = e F^{\mu\nu} \dot{x}_\nu\) using the retarded electromagnetic field sourced by the particle's own past trajectory, with the massless retarded propagator appropriate to 2+1 and 4+1 dimensions. Divergences near the coincidence limit \(\tau' \to \tau\) are regularized by a proper-time cutoff \(\epsilon\) and absorbed into mass renormalization; the finite remainder is evaluated in the ultra-relativistic limit \(\gamma \to \infty\), where the self-force becomes local. The far-field synchrotron radiation power is computed separately by a large-distance flux integral and compared to the local work done by the self-force.

## Key numbers

* In 2+1 dimensions, the leading self-force for ultra-relativistic circular motion is \(f^\mu_{3D} = -\frac{e^2}{\sqrt{3}\pi} \omega \gamma^2 \dot{x}^\mu\), giving a longitudinal drag force \(\vec{\bf f}_L \approx -\frac{e^2}{\sqrt{3}\pi} \omega \gamma^2 \vec{v}\) and a rate of work \(P = \frac{e^2}{\sqrt{3}\pi} \omega \gamma^2\).
* In 4+1 dimensions, the leading longitudinal self-force for the same motion is \(f^\mu_{5D} \sim -\frac{e^2}{10\sqrt{3}\pi^2} \gamma^4 \omega^3 \dot{x}^\mu\), yielding \(P = \frac{e^2}{10\sqrt{3}\pi^2} \gamma^4 \omega^3\).
* The far-field synchrotron radiation power in 2+1 dimensions is \(\frac{dE}{dt} = \frac{e^2}{\sqrt{3}\pi} \omega \gamma^2\), matching the local work done by the self-force.
* The proper-time interval contributing to the self-force in the ultra-relativistic limit scales as \(|\tau' - \tau| \approx 1/(\omega \gamma^2)\) in 2+1 dimensions.

## Context

This work extends the MSTQW approach (Mino, Sasaki, Tanaka 1997; Quinn, Wald 1997) for gravitational self-force to electromagnetic self-force in odd-dimensional flat spacetimes, where the retarded propagator is not confined to the light cone as it is in 3+1 dimensions. The 2+1-dimensional result provides the first explicit local derivation of the radiation reaction force that agrees with the standard far-field radiation calculation, and the 4+1-dimensional result is a step toward applying the self-force method to gravitational radiation in curved backgrounds such as thermal AdS, relevant to jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions (Shuryak, Yee, Zahed 2011).
