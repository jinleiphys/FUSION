# Gluon PDF from Quark dressing in the Nucleon and Pion

> Gluon dressing of the light quarks within hadrons is very strong and extremely important in that it dynamically generates most of the observable mass through the breaking of chiral symmetry. The quark and gluon parton densities, $q(x)$ and $g(x)$, are necessarily interrelated since any gluon emission and absorption process, especially dressing of a quark, contributes to $g(x)$ and modifies $q(x)$. Guided by long-established results for the parton-in-parton distributions from a strict 1-loop perturbative analysis of a quark target, we extend the non-perturbative QCD approach based on the Rainbow-Ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations to describe the interrelated valence $q_{\rm v}(x)$ and the dressing-gluon $g(x)$ for a hadron at its intrinsic model scale. We employ the pion description from previous DSE work that accounted for the gluon-in-quark effect and introduce a simple model of the nucleon for exploratory purposes. We find typically \mbox{$\langle x \rangle_g \sim 0.20$} for both pion and nucleon at the model scale, and the valence quark helicity contributes 52\% of nucleon spin. We deduce both $q_{\rm v}(x)$ and $g(x)$ from 30 calculated Mellin moments, and after adopting existing data analysis results for $q_{\rm sea}(x)$, we find that NLO scale evolution produces $g(x)$ in good agreement with existing data analysis results for the pion at 1.3 GeV and the nucleon at 5 GeV$^2$. At the scale 2 GeV typical of lattice-QCD calculations, we obtain \mbox{$\langle x \rangle_g^{\rm N} = 0.42$} in good agreement with 0.38 from the average of recent lattice-QCD calculations.

## Key claim

The authors extend the non-perturbative Dyson-Schwinger equation (DSE) approach with Rainbow-Ladder truncation to simultaneously describe the valence quark and dressing-gluon parton distribution functions (PDFs) for both the pion and nucleon at their intrinsic model scales, finding that a non-zero gluon PDF at the starting scale is essential for consistency with evolved results and global data analyses.

## Method

The authors compute Mellin moments of the valence quark and gluon PDFs for the pion and nucleon using the Rainbow-Ladder truncation of the Dyson-Schwinger equations, where the gluon-in-quark vertex is obtained from a Bethe-Salpeter equation with an inhomogeneous term that generalizes the 1-loop perturbative result. For the nucleon, they introduce a simple model amplitude based on a generalized SU(6) spin-flavor state with momentum-dependent scalar factors. The PDFs are reconstructed from 30 calculated Mellin moments using a Bernstein polynomial fit, and NLO DGLAP evolution is applied to compare with global data analyses.

## Key numbers

- At the model scale, the gluon momentum fraction is ⟨x⟩_g ∼ 0.20 for both the pion (at μ_0 = 0.78 GeV) and the nucleon (at μ_0 = 0.56 GeV)
- For the nucleon at the model scale, the valence quark helicity contributes 52% of the nucleon spin (ΔΣ_v = 0.518)
- At the scale 2 GeV typical of lattice-QCD calculations, the nucleon gluon momentum fraction is ⟨x⟩_g^N = 0.42, in good agreement with the lattice-QCD average of 0.38
- For the pion at μ = 1.3 GeV, the gluon momentum fraction is ⟨x⟩_g = 0.304, compared to the JAM global analysis value of 0.30 ± 0.02
- For the nucleon at μ = √5 GeV, the gluon momentum fraction is ⟨x⟩_g = 0.430, compared to the NNPDF3.0 value of 0.441
- The 1-loop quark target calculation with g^2 = 5.78 yields ⟨x⟩_q^(1) = 0.766 and ⟨x⟩_g^(1) = 0.234

## Context

This work extends the DSE-Rainbow-Ladder approach previously applied to pion PDFs by Bednar et al. (2018) to include the gluon PDF from quark dressing, and introduces a simple nucleon model for exploratory purposes. The authors emphasize that assuming zero gluon and sea distributions at low model scales, as done in some earlier approaches such as those by Ding et al. (2019), is incompatible with scale evolution and current data, consistent with the findings of Diehl et al. (2019). The results enable future non-perturbative calculations of gluon PDFs in hadrons and provide a framework for connecting DSE-based models to global PDF analyses and lattice-QCD calculations.
