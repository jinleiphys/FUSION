# Practical Considerations for Measuring Global Spin Density Matrix Elements of Vector Mesons in Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The STAR Collaboration has reported a significant $ϕ$-meson global spin alignment ($ρ_{00}$) signal in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}\leq62$ GeV by measuring the polar angle distribution of $ϕ$-meson daughters with respect to the orbital angular momentum (OAM) direction of the collision system. In this paper, a new method is explored for studying vector-meson global spin alignment in heavy-ion collisions by examining the two dimensional polar and azimuthal angle distribution. This method allows simultaneous extraction of $ρ_{00}$ and off-diagonal spin density matrix elements (SDMEs), providing unique access to local quark-antiquark spin correlations and spin hydrodynamics in quark-gluon plasma (QGP). The new 2D method also removes potential biases from non-zero off-diagonal SDMEs on $ρ_{00}$ with the 1D method. A detailed procedure to correct for detector acceptance and resolution effects is also presented and validated by simulation studies.

# Practical Considerations for Measuring Global Spin Density Matrix Elements of Vector Mesons in Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
A two-dimensional (2D) method using both polar and azimuthal angles of vector-meson daughter particles can simultaneously extract the diagonal spin density matrix element ρ₀₀ and off-diagonal elements, removing biases that arise in the standard one-dimensional (1D) method when off-diagonal elements are non-zero and event plane resolution is finite. The method is validated for ϕ → K⁺K⁻ decays but is qualitatively applicable to other two-body decays of vector mesons into pseudoscalar mesons.

## Method
The authors derived analytic event-plane resolution correction formulas relating the spin density matrix elements (SDMEs) measured in the event-plane frame to those in the reaction-plane frame, using a spin-1 rotation operator around the global-frame x-axis. They performed Monte Carlo simulations of ϕ → K⁺K⁻ decays (500M events) with realistic ϕ-meson kinematics from Pythia8 and published STAR data, applying detector acceptance cuts (|η| < 1, pT > 0.1 GeV/c, |p| < 10 GeV/c), STAR detector efficiencies, and event-plane smearing with R₁ = 0.6. The 2D angular distributions in cosθ* and β were fitted to extract all five SDME parameters simultaneously, and the correction procedure was tested both with and without event-plane smearing in the efficiency correction. The effect of finite pT resolution (10% Gaussian smearing) was studied using an iterative correction method.

## Key numbers
- 500 million Monte Carlo events of ϕ → K⁺K⁻ were generated for the simulation studies.
- ϕ-mesons were simulated with 1.2 < pT < 4.2 GeV/c and |y| < 1, matching STAR Collaboration kinematic selections.
- Event plane resolution R₁ = 0.6 was used to roughly match the STAR event plane resolution.
- When true ρ₀₀ is varied, both 1D and 2D methods recover the true value within 1%, with the 2D method having larger uncertainty and slightly better agreement.
- At pT resolutions of 2-3%, the deviations of corrected SDME values from Monte Carlo truth are roughly 10-20% of what is observed for 10% pT resolution.
- The iterative pT correction method converges to the true input values within three iterations.

## Context
This work extends the STAR Collaboration's 1D ρ₀₀ extraction method (Nature, 2023; NST35, 2023) for ϕ-meson global spin alignment in Au+Au collisions at √sNN ≤ 62 GeV. It addresses the potential bias from off-diagonal SDMEs discussed by Tang (2023) and provides the experimental framework to test models predicting nonzero off-diagonal elements, such as the local strange-quark-antiquark spin correlation model (Phys. Rev. D, 2024) and the strong-force field fluctuation model (Sheng et al., 2020-2023). The method enables direct measurement of off-diagonal SDMEs that can influence chiral magnetic effect observables (CME, 2023) and probe hadronization in vortical environments (offdiag1, offdiag2, offdiag3).
