# On the second reference state and complete eigenstates of the open XXZ chain

> The second reference state of the open XXZ spin chain with non-diagonal boundary terms is studied. The associated Bethe states exactly yield the second set of eigenvalues proposed recently by functional Bethe Ansatz. In the quasi-classical limit, two sets of Bethe states give the complete eigenstates of the associated Gaudin model.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the second reference state for the open XXZ spin chain with non-diagonal boundary terms, showing that the associated Bethe states exactly reproduce the second set of eigenvalues previously proposed by functional Bethe Ansatz, and that together with the first set they constitute the complete eigenstates of the model.

## Method

The authors use the vertex-face correspondence to transform the non-diagonal boundary K-matrices into diagonal face-type counterparts, enabling application of the generalized algebraic Bethe Ansatz for SOS-type models. They construct a second reference state using intertwiner vectors with the opposite orientation (labeled by \(\hat{2}\) instead of \(\hat{1}\)), define the associated operators \(\mathcal{A}^{(2)}\) and \(\mathcal{D}^{(2)}\), and derive the corresponding Bethe Ansatz equations and eigenvalues. In the quasi-classical limit \(\eta \to 0\), they expand the transfer matrix to obtain the Gaudin model operators and their complete eigenstates.

## Key numbers

- The second reference state \(|\Omega^{(2)}(\lambda)\rangle\) is constructed from \(N = 2M\) intertwiner vectors \(\phi_{\lambda-(N-1)\eta\hat{2},\lambda-N\eta\hat{2}}(-z_1) \otimes \cdots \otimes \phi_{\lambda,\lambda-\eta\hat{2}}(-z_N)\).
- The second set of Bethe Ansatz equations (Eq. BA-D-2) involves products over \(\alpha=1,\dots,M\) with factors \(\sin(v_\alpha+v_k+2\eta)/\sin(v_\alpha+v_k)\) and \(\sin(v_\alpha-v_k+\eta)/\sin(v_\alpha-v_k-\eta)\).
- The second eigenvalue \(\Lambda^{(2)}(u)\) (Eq. Eigenfuction-D-2) contains prefactors \(\sin(2u+2\eta)/\sin(2u+\eta)\) and \(\sin(2u)/\sin(2u+\eta)\).
- In the quasi-classical limit, the Gaudin model Bethe Ansatz equations (Eq. BAE-1) involve sums over \(1/\sin(x_\alpha+z_k)\sin(x_\alpha-z_k)\) and \(1/\sin(x_\alpha+x_k)\sin(x_\alpha-x_k)\).
- The Gaudin model eigenvalues \(E_j\) (Eq. Eig-1) are given by \(\cot 2z_j + \sum_{j=1}^2 \cot(\lambda_j+\xi-z_j) + \sum_{k=1}^M \frac{\sin 2z_j}{\sin(x_k-z_j)\sin(x_k+z_j)}\).

## Context

This work extends the algebraic Bethe Ansatz treatment of the open XXZ chain with non-diagonal boundaries, where previous constructions (Cao et al. 2003, Yang et al. 2004) had only provided one reference state. The two sets of Bethe states together provide the complete eigenstates, confirming the two-set structure previously found by functional Bethe Ansatz (Nepomechie 2003, Yang et al. 2006). In the quasi-classical limit, the results give the complete eigenstates of the associated Gaudin model, enabling future studies of correlation functions. The paper notes that very recent work (Murgan et al. 2006, Baseilhac 2007) has proposed exact solutions for arbitrary boundary parameters, suggesting an extension of the present algebraic Bethe Ansatz approach.
