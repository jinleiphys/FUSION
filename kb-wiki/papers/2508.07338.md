# Puzzle for the Vector Meson Threshold Photoproduction

> High-statistics total cross sections for the vector meson photoproduction at the threshold: $γp\toωp$ (from A2 at MAMI, ELPH, and CBELSA/TAPS), $γp\to ϕp$ (from CLAS and LEPS), and $γp\to J/ψp$ (from GlueX) allow one to extract the absolute value of vector meson nucleon scattering length using Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model. The ``young'' vector meson hypothesis may explain the fact that the obtained scattering length value for the nucleon $ϕ$-meson compared to the typical hadron size of approximately $\sim1~\mathrm{fm}$ indicates that the proton is more transparent for the $ϕ$-meson compared to the $ω$-meson and is much less transparent than the $J/ψ$-meson. The extended analysis of $Υ$-meson photoproduction using quasi-data from the QCD approach is in perfect agreement with the light-meson findings using experimental data. Future high-quality experiments by EIC and EicC will have the opportunity to evaluate cases for $J/ψ$- and $Υ$-mesons. It allows one to understand the dynamics of $c\bar{c}$ and $b\bar{b}$ production at the threshold. The ability of J-PARC to measure $π^- p\to ϕn$ and $π^-p\to J/ψn$, which are free from the VMD model, is considered.

## Key claim

The absolute value of the vector meson nucleon scattering length extracted from threshold photoproduction data using the Vector Meson Dominance (VMD) model shows that the proton is more transparent for the phi-meson than for the omega-meson and much less transparent than for the J/psi-meson, which the "young" vector meson hypothesis may explain; however, the VMD model itself introduces theoretical uncertainties that could be as large as a factor of 5 for J/psi photoproduction.

## Method

The author extracted the absolute value of the vector meson nucleon scattering length for omega, phi, J/psi, and Upsilon mesons by fitting high-statistics total cross sections of threshold photoproduction reactions (gamma p to V p) using the VMD model, which links the photoproduction cross section to elastic Vp scattering. The scattering length was expressed as a product of a kinematic factor from the V to e+e- decay width and a hadronic factor determined by fitting the total cross sections as a series of odd powers in the center-of-mass momentum q. For the Upsilon meson, quasi-data from a QCD approach were used instead of experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Omega-meson nucleon scattering length: 0.82 +/- 0.03 fm (from A2 at MAMI data), 0.97 +/- 0.16 fm (from ELPH data), and 0.811 +/- 0.019 fm (from Han analysis)
- Phi-meson nucleon scattering length: 0.063 +/- 0.010 fm (from CLAS data) and 0.109 +/- 0.008 fm (from Han analysis)
- J/psi-meson nucleon scattering length: (3.08 +/- 0.55) x 10^-3 fm (from GlueX data)
- Upsilon-meson nucleon scattering length: (0.51 +/- 0.03) x 10^-3 fm (from quasi-data)
- ALICE Collaboration spin-averaged phi N scattering length: (0.85 +/- 0.48) + i(0.16 +/- 0.19) fm from two-particle correlations at W = 13 TeV
- HAL Collaboration LQCD result for phi N spin 3/2 scattering length: [-1.43 +/- 0.23 (+0.36/-0.06)] fm
- HAL Collaboration LQCD result for J/psi N spin 1/2 scattering length: [0.38 +/- 0.04 (+0.00/-0.03)] fm
- HAL Collaboration LQCD result for J/psi N spin 3/2 scattering length: [0.30 +/- 0.02 (+0.00/-0.02)] fm
- Model-dependent analysis for J/psi N scattering length: (0.2 to 3.1) x 10^-3 fm for spin 1/2 and (0.2 to 3.0) x 10^-3 fm for spin 3/2
- Cross section difference between pi- p to phi n and pi- p to J/psi n has a factor of 10^6

## Context

This work extends the author's previous phenomenological analyses of vector meson photoproduction (Strakovsky 2014, 2019, 2020, 2021) and compares with recent LQCD results from the HAL Collaboration (Lyu 2022, 2025) and femtoscopic measurements from the ALICE Collaboration (2021). The results contradict earlier theoretical predictions that gave much larger scattering lengths, which the author attributes to the van der Waals potential tail being suppressed by confinement. Future measurements at EIC, EicC, and J-PARC (which can measure pi- p to phi n and pi- p to J/psi n free from the VMD model) will enable understanding of c-cbar and b-bbar production dynamics at threshold and provide model-independent input for partial wave analysis.
