# Forward particle productions at RHIC and the LHC from CGC within local rcBK evolution

> In order to describe forward hadron productions in high-energy nuclear collisions, we propose a Monte-Carlo implementation of Dumitru-Hayashigaki-Jalilian-Marian formula with the unintegrated gluon distribution obtained numerically from the running-coupling BK equation. We discuss influence of initial conditions for the BK equation by comparing a model constrained by global fit of small-x HERA data and a newly proposed one from the running coupling MV model.

# Forward particle productions at RHIC and the LHC from CGC within local rcBK evolution

## Key claim

The Monte-Carlo implementation of the Dumitru-Hayashigaki-Jalilian-Marian (DHJ) formula with unintegrated gluon distributions from the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation can describe forward hadron production data at RHIC, but the agreement depends on the choice of initial condition for the BK evolution: the AAMQS set h works for both pp and d-Au collisions simultaneously, while the running-coupling McLerran-Venugopalan (rcMV) initial condition only describes d-Au data and fails for pp collisions.

## Method

The authors implement a Monte-Carlo event-by-event sampling of nucleon positions according to the Woods-Saxon density, then apply the DHJ factorized formula locally at each transverse grid point using the unintegrated gluon distribution obtained from numerical solutions of the rcBK equation with different initial conditions. The collinear parton distribution functions (CTEQ6M NLO) and fragmentation functions (DSS NLO) are used, with the factorization scale set to mu^2 = p_T^2, and a K factor is introduced to absorb higher-order contributions.

## Key numbers

- AAMQS parameter set h: Q_{s0}^2 = 0.1597 GeV^2, gamma = 1.118, C = 2.47, Lambda = 0.241 GeV, alpha_fr = 1
- rcMV initial condition parameters: C = 1, Lambda = 0.2 GeV, Q_{s0}^2 = 0.2 GeV^2
- K factor = 1.5 for negatively charged hadrons (h^-) and K = 0.5 for neutral pions (pi^0) in both pp and d-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- Forward hadron spectra compared at pseudorapidities eta = 2.2 and 3.2 (BRAHMS h^- data) and eta = 4 (STAR pi^0 data) in pp and d-Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV
- Test calculation performed at eta = 8.5 for pp collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV (LHCf experiment)

## Context

This work extends the approach of Albacete and Marquet (2009-2010), who applied the rcBK equation to improve the unintegrated gluon distribution in the DHJ formula assuming a homogeneous nucleus. The Monte-Carlo implementation follows the method of Drescher and Nara (2004) for including impact-parameter dependence and initial fluctuations, which had previously been applied to reproduce centrality dependence of hadron multiplicity at RHIC and the LHC. The results enable more quantitative studies of forward particle production at the LHC, particularly for the LHCf experiment, and motivate a global analysis of HERA data using the rcMV initial condition to fix its parameters.
