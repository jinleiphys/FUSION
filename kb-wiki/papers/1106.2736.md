# Oscillations of hot, young neutron stars: Gravitational wave frequencies and damping times

> We study how the frequencies and damping times of oscillations of a newly born, hot proto-neutron star depend on the physical quantities which characterize the star quasi-stationary evolution which follows the bounce. Stellar configurations are modeled using a microscopic equation of state obtained within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock, nuclear many-body approach, extended to the finite-temperature regime. We discuss the mode frequency behaviour as function of the lepton composition, and of the entropy gradients which prevail in the interior of the star. We find that, in the very early stages, gravitational wave emission efficiently competes with neutrino processes in dissipating the star mechanical energy residual of the gravitational collapse.

# Oscillations of hot, young neutron stars: Gravitational wave frequencies and damping times

## Key claim

The frequencies and damping times of proto-neutron star oscillations depend primarily on the entropy profile (core vs. envelope entropy) and lepton composition during the quasi-stationary evolution after core bounce; gravitational wave emission can efficiently compete with neutrino processes in dissipating the star's mechanical energy in the very early stages, but only when the gravitational wave damping time is much shorter than the dissipation timescale from other processes (approximately 10-20 seconds).

## Method

The authors constructed equilibrium stellar models with fixed baryonic mass (1.5 solar masses) using a microscopic equation of state obtained within the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock nuclear many-body approach extended to finite temperature, combined with the Shen equation of state at low densities. They solved the Lindblom-Detweiler formulation of the relativistic stellar perturbation equations to compute complex quasi-normal mode frequencies for configurations with different entropy profiles (core entropy s_c from 1 to 2, envelope entropy s_e from 5 down to 1) and lepton fractions (Y_e from 0.38 down to 0.23), simulating the quasi-stationary evolution from a hot, neutrino-trapped proto-neutron star to a cold neutron star.

## Key numbers

- Baryonic mass fixed at M_B = 1.5 solar masses for all stellar models
- Initial configuration P_{1,5,0.35}: gravitational mass 1.43 solar masses, radius 30.3 km, central temperature 20.2 MeV, central neutrino fraction 0.041
- Intermediate configuration P_{2,3,0.30}: gravitational mass 1.42 solar masses, radius 16.5 km, central temperature 41.2 MeV, central neutrino fraction 0.026
- Late configuration P_{1,1,0.23}: gravitational mass 1.37 solar masses, radius 12.5 km, central temperature 20.2 MeV, central neutrino fraction 0.007
- Cold neutron star (T=0): gravitational mass 1.35 solar masses, radius 11.9 km
- Weak interaction timescales: modified Urca t_react^(1) ≈ 5×10^6 s / (T/10^9 K)^6, direct Urca t_react^(2) ≈ 20 s / (T/10^9 K)^4; at T ≈ (1-4)×10^11 K these are much shorter than the oscillation period t_osc ≈ 10^{-3} s
- Typical f-mode frequency for cold neutron stars: 1-3 kHz, damping time of order a few tenths of seconds
- Dissipation timescale from neutrino processes: τ_diss ∼ 10-20 seconds

## Context

This work extends previous studies of proto-neutron star oscillations by Ferrari et al. (2003) and others (Pons et al., Mezzacappa et al., 2010) by using a microscopic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock equation of state instead of the mean-field models employed in those dynamical simulations. The results enable comparison of gravitational wave damping times with neutrino dissipation timescales, showing that in early stages gravitational wave emission can compete with neutrino processes, which is relevant for detection by next-generation interferometers (LIGO/Virgo and the Einstein Telescope). The study is limited to non-rotating, spherically symmetric models and does not include viscosity, heat transport, or magnetic fields.
