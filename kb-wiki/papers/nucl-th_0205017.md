# Mean-field Based Approaches to Pairing Correlations in Atomic Nuclei

> The evolution of the pairing correlations from closed shell to middle shell nuclei is analyzed with a Finite Range Density Dependent interaction in the Sn isotopes. As theoretical approaches we use the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov, the Lipkin-Nogami, their particle number projected counterparts and the full variation after particle number projection method. We find that whereas all approaches succeed rather well in the description of the total energy they differ significantly in the pairing correlation content of the wave functions. The description of the evolution from the weak to the strong pairing regime is also approach dependent, specially at shell closure.

## Key claim

While all mean-field based approaches (HFB, Lipkin-Nogami, their particle-number projected counterparts, and full variation after projection) succeed rather well in describing total binding energies of Sn isotopes, they differ significantly in the pairing correlation content of the wave functions, and the description of the evolution from weak to strong pairing regimes is approach-dependent, especially at shell closure.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB), Lipkin-Nogami (LN), particle-number projected after variation (PAV and PLN), and full variation after particle number projection (VAP) calculations for the ground states of Sn isotopes (Z=50) from N=50 to N=82, using the finite-range density-dependent Gogny D1 force with all exchange terms included. The particle-particle correlation energy E_pp and the total energy gain relative to Hartree-Fock were computed for each approach, and the fractional occupancies of active neutron orbitals were analyzed to understand the two-hump structure in pairing correlations.

## Key numbers

- The HFB approach deviates from the VAP binding energy by about 2.6 MeV on average, with larger deviations near shell closures and around A=116
- The PAV approach provides an additional lowering of about 0.75 MeV relative to HFB, with relatively uniform behavior across the mass range
- The LN approach gives binding energies about 1.7 MeV higher than VAP on average, with strong deviations only at N=50 and N=82
- The PLN approach differs from VAP by only about 0.75 MeV over a wide range, and at worst (N=50 and N=82) by about 1.25 MeV
- When proton pairing is suppressed, HFB, PAV, LN, and PLN reproduce VAP binding energies within 1, 0.2, plus or minus 0.2, and 0.15 MeV respectively, except at shell closures
- The neutron h_2 parameter in the LN approach is constant and small from A=106 to A=126, then rises to 0.6 at shell closure
- Proton LN h_2 values are large and rather constant, with a small dip around A=110

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of pairing correlations that used schematic or separable forces (such as monopole pairing) by employing the finite-range Gogny force, which provides a self-contained pairing interaction without renormalization. The study systematically compares multiple mean-field based approaches (HFB, LN, PAV, PLN, VAP) for the same isotopic chain, building on the Kamlah expansion framework (Kamlah 1968) and the Lipkin-Nogami recipe (Lipkin 1960, Nogami 1964, Goodfellow and Nogami 1966). The results enable assessment of which approximate methods are reliable for describing pairing correlations in different regimes, particularly for nuclei far from stability where pairing plays a key role, and establish that the PLN approach provides the best approximation to the full VAP method across all mass numbers.
