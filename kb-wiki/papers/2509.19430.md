# Bayesian Constraints on Pre-Equilibrium Jet Quenching and Predictions for Oxygen Collisions

> The contrast between the as-yet unmeasurable energy-loss effects in proton-nucleus collisions and the striking magnitude of the so-called high-momentum flow coefficients challenges our understanding of jet quenching mechanisms in large nucleus-nucleus collisions when applied to smaller systems. Intermediate-sized, light ion collisions will offer key insight into the system-size dependence of the interplay between jet energy loss and jet flow effects. To make quantitative predictions, we extend a semi-analytic jet quenching framework by coupling it to state-of-the-art event-by-event hydrodynamics and, for the first time, incorporate pre-equilibrium energy loss via the hydrodynamic attractor. A Bayesian analysis shows that an early-time onset of energy loss is compatible with RHIC and LHC measurements of jet suppression and jet elliptic flow in large systems, as well as hadron suppression, with the exception of hadron elliptic flow. Using these constraints, we predict both hadron and jet quenching observables in oxygen-oxygen collisions, finding sizable energy loss that exceeds the no-quenching baseline.

## Key claim

The authors show that a Bayesian analysis of jet suppression and jet elliptic flow data from RHIC and LHC is compatible with an early onset of energy loss at approximately 0.24 fm, but this same framework fails to simultaneously describe hadron elliptic flow at low transverse momentum, indicating missing medium effects beyond longitudinal energy loss.

## Method

The authors extended a semi-analytic jet quenching framework by coupling it to event-by-event 2+1D IP-Glasma+JIMWLK+MUSIC+UrQMD hydrodynamic simulations and, for the first time, incorporated pre-equilibrium energy loss via the hydrodynamic attractor extracted from QCD kinetic theory. They performed Bayesian parameter estimation using the JETSCAPE/STAT toolkit to constrain two parameters: the jet-medium strong coupling g_med and the initial quenching onset time tau_min, fitting to jet R_AA and jet v_2 data from ATLAS, ALICE, and CMS across multiple centralities, jet cone sizes, and transverse momenta.

## Key numbers

- The Bayesian posterior constrains the pre-equilibrium quenching onset time to tau_min approximately 0.24 fm when including both R_AA and v_2 data.
- The extracted bare jet transport coefficient is approximately hat_q_0/T^3 approximately 1.7, with effective value hat_q/T^3 approximately 7 after logarithmic corrections.
- The hydrodynamic attractor extrapolation begins at tau_hyd = 0.4 fm, with the specific shear viscosity eta/s = 0.12.
- The flat prior ranges used were 1.2 less than or equal to g_med less than or equal to 1.7 and 0.01 fm less than or equal to tau_min less than or equal to 0.4 fm.
- For oxygen-oxygen collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.36 TeV, the coherence angle is approximately theta_c approximately 0.5 for R = 0.4 jets.

## Context

This work extends the semi-analytic jet quenching framework of Mehtar-Tani (2021), Takacs (2021), and Mehtar-Tani (2024) by incorporating event-by-event fluctuating hydrodynamics and pre-equilibrium energy loss. It builds on prior studies of R_AA and v_2 by Betz (2014), Noronha-Hostler (2016), Andres (2019), and Stojku (2020), but focuses on jets rather than hadrons and uses state-of-the-art hydrodynamics and energy-loss formulas including elastic energy loss and medium response. The predictions for oxygen-oxygen collisions complement baseline analyses by Brewer (2021), Paakkinen (2021), and Gebhard (2024), and hadron quenching predictions by Katz (2019), Huss (2020), and Zakharov (2021). The results enable future experimental confrontation with LHC data on light ion collisions and motivate further theoretical work on energy loss in anisotropic and out-of-equilibrium plasmas.
