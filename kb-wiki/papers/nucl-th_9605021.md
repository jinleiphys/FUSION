# Relativistic heavy ion collisions with realistic non-equilibrium mean fields

> We study the influence of non-equilibrium phase space effects on the dynamics of heavy ion reactions within the relativistic BUU approach. We use realistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) mean fields determined for two-Fermi-ellipsoid configurations, i.e. for colliding nuclear matter, in a local phase space configuration approximation (LCA). We compare to DBHF mean fields in the local density approximation (LDA) and to the non-linear Walecka model. The results are further compared to flow data of the reaction $Au$ on $Au$ at 400 MeV per nucleon measured by the FOPI collaboration. We find that the DBHF fields reproduce the experiment if the configuration dependence is taken into account. This has also implications on the determination of the equation of state from heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper shows that Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) mean fields can reproduce experimental flow data for Au+Au collisions at 400 MeV per nucleon only when non-equilibrium phase space effects are taken into account via the local configuration approximation (LCA); a simple local density approximation (LDA) overestimates the flow by about 30%.

## Method

The authors solved the relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) equation using the relativistic Landau-Vlasov method with covariant Gaussian test particles. They constructed DBHF mean fields for colliding nuclear matter (two-Fermi-ellipsoid configurations) in the local configuration approximation (LCA), where the self-energies depend on the local Fermi momenta and relative velocity of the two currents, and compared these to DBHF fields in the local density approximation (LDA) and to the non-linear Walecka model (NL2). The calculations were filtered through the FOPI detector acceptance and compared to experimental flow data for Au+Au at 400 A.MeV.

## Key numbers

- The directed in-plane transverse flow normalized to the center-of-mass projectile momentum per nucleon for semi-central collisions (3 to 5 fm impact parameter): DBHF LCA gives 0.187, DBHF LDA gives 0.232, NL2 gives 0.103, and the experimental value is 0.173.
- The configuration effect on the flow reaches about 30% of the total amount of flow, comparable to the effect of different equations of state.
- The DBHF equation of state has an incompressibility of 250 MeV and an effective mass ratio m*/m0 of 0.586; NL2 has m*/m0 of 0.83.
- The DBHF nucleon-nucleus optical potential at saturation density and at two times saturation density is shown for laboratory energies up to about 400 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the DBHF approach from ground state nuclear matter calculations by Ter Haar and Malfliet (1987) to non-equilibrium configurations in heavy ion collisions, building on the local configuration approximation developed by Fuchs et al. (1992) and Sehn et al. (1990, 1995). The results show that non-equilibrium effects are essential for extracting the ground state equation of state from flow data, and that the relativistic DBHF approach resolves a shortcoming of non-relativistic G-matrix calculations by Jaeckle et al. (1992) and Khoa et al. (1992), which lacked sufficient density dependence for central collisions. The work enables more reliable determination of the nuclear equation of state from heavy ion collision data and suggests future improvements through inclusion of finite temperature effects and in-medium cross sections.
