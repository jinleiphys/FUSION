# Entanglement in Elastic and Inelastic Two-particle Scatterings at High Energy

> We study the entanglement produced in transverse momentum by two-particle scattering at high energy. Employing the S-matrix framework for the derivation of reduced density matrices, we formulate the entanglement entropy for an inelastic scattering as well as an elastic one. We display the formulas of the entanglement entropy in terms of two-body cross sections. We also derive the entanglement density as a function of the transverse momentum. As an application, we then focus on both forward elastic ($pn \to pn$) and inelastic ($pn \to np$) channels scattering allowing for a fruitful comparison of the two reactions with the same proton-neutron content. We evaluate the elastic and inelastic entanglement entropy by using known parameterizations of experimental data for neutron-proton reactions. Comparing those entanglement entropies, we observe that the inelastic scattering produces more overall entanglement than the elastic one in the $pn$ sector.

## Key claim

The paper claims that inelastic two-particle scattering produces more overall entanglement entropy than elastic scattering in the proton-neutron sector, but this conclusion depends on a volume regularization procedure that removes a divergent factor from the infinite Hilbert space, and the comparison is only valid after this regularization is applied.

## Method

The authors formulate entanglement entropy for both elastic and inelastic two-particle final states using an S-matrix framework with partial wave expansions, expressing the entropy in terms of two-body cross sections and differential cross sections. They then evaluate the regularized entanglement entropy for forward elastic (pn → pn) and inelastic (pn → np) channels using known parameterizations of experimental data for neutron-proton reactions at high energy, including differential cross sections from Refs. [PB], [BouD], and [BizD] and total cross sections from Refs. [BC1] and [BC2].

## Key numbers

- The regularized volume of the Hilbert space is set to V_tilde = (k^2 / pi) * sigma_11^tot, where sigma_11^tot is the total cross section of the incoming particles.
- The entanglement entropy for both elastic and inelastic channels contains a common term ln(V/2) which diverges with the infinite Hilbert space volume V.
- The difference between elastic and inelastic entanglement entropies, Delta S_tilde_{2,1}, is independent of regularization and is given by an integral over cosθ of the difference between the two normalized differential cross section distributions.
- The Mandelstam variable t ranges from -4k^2 to 0 for the integration over scattering angle.

## Context

This work extends the S-matrix formulation of entanglement entropy for elastic scattering developed in Refs. [PS1] and [PS2], which previously evaluated regularized entanglement entropy for proton-proton elastic scattering using Tevatron and LHC data. The paper generalizes this approach to inelastic two-particle channels and applies it to proton-neutron scattering, where both elastic (pn → pn) and inelastic (pn → np) channels are accessible with the same initial and final particle content. The results enable future comparison of entanglement production across different scattering channels and may provide insight into the entanglement flow during the scattering process.
