# Effects of $CP$-violating internucleon interactions in paramagnetic molecules

> We demonstrate that electron electric dipole moment experiments with molecules in paramagnetic state are sensitive to $P,T$-violating nuclear forces and other $CP$-violating parameters in the hadronic sector. These experiments, in particular, measure the coupling constant $C_{SP}$ of the $CP$-odd contact semileptonic interaction. We establish relations between $C_{SP}$ and different $CP$-violating hadronic parameters including strength constants of the $CP$-odd nuclear potentials, $CP$-odd pion-nucleon interactions, quark-chromo EDM and QCD vacuum angle. These relations allow us to find limits on various $CP$-odd hadronic parameters.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that electron electric dipole moment (eEDM) experiments with paramagnetic molecules are sensitive to \(P,T\)-violating nuclear forces, not just to the electron EDM itself, and establishes leading-order relations between the experimentally measured semileptonic coupling constant \(C_{SP}\) and hadronic \(CP\)-violating parameters, with the caveat that the accuracy of the nuclear calculations is no better than a factor of two.

## Method

The authors employ a single-particle nuclear model where a valence nucleon interacts with a heavy nuclear core through a \(P,T\)-odd phenomenological Hamiltonian \(H_{\rm odd} = \xi \boldsymbol{\sigma}\cdot\nabla U({\bf r})\). They compute the atomic EDM arising from this interaction using second-order perturbation theory, combining nuclear matrix elements (estimated within the Nilsson model) with electronic matrix elements calculated numerically using unscreened Dirac-Coulomb wave functions for the \(s_{1/2}\) and \(p_{1/2}\) states. The resulting atomic EDM is then equated to the EDM produced by the contact semileptonic interaction to extract the relation between \(C_{SP}\) and the nuclear coupling constants \(\xi_p, \xi_n\), which are further linked to pion-nucleon couplings, quark chromo-EDMs, and the QCD vacuum angle.

## Key numbers

*   The ACME experiment with \(^{232}\)ThO gives the 90% C.L. limit \(|C_{SP}|_{\rm Th} < 7.3 \times 10^{-10}\).
*   From this limit, the derived constraints on the \(P,T\)-odd nuclear force couplings are \(|\xi_p| < 2.2 \times 10^{-23}\) cm and \(|\xi_n| < 3.0 \times 10^{-23}\) cm.
*   The \(^{180}\)HfF\(^+\) experiment yields weaker limits: \(|\xi_p| < 2.6 \times 10^{-22}\) cm and \(|\xi_n| < 4.5 \times 10^{-22}\) cm.
*   The relation between \(\xi_{p,n}\) and the \(CP\)-odd pion-nucleon couplings is \(\xi_p = -\xi_n = 10^{-14} g (\bar g^{(1)}_{\pi NN} + 0.4 \bar g^{(2)}_{\pi NN} - 0.2 \bar g^{(0)}_{\pi NN})\) cm, with the strong interaction constant \(g \approx 13.6\).
*   Numerical coefficients \(\tilde\lambda_p\) and \(\tilde\lambda_n\) (scaled by 1/100) are tabulated for ten heavy atoms, ranging from 0.65 (for \(^{138}\)Ba) to 15 (for \(^{232}\)Th) for \(\tilde\lambda_p\) and from 0.68 (for \(^{138}\)Ba) to 11 (for \(^{232}\)Th) for \(\tilde\lambda_n\).

## Context

This work extends the results of Flambaum, Pospelov, Ritz, and Samsonov (FPRS) and Flambaum, Samsonov, and Tan (FST2020) by considering \(P,T\)-odd nuclear forces as an independent source of \(CP\) violation in paramagnetic molecules, separate from nucleon EDM contributions. It enables the interpretation of eEDM experiments (such as those by the ACME collaboration with ThO and the HfF\(^+\) experiment) as probes of hadronic \(CP\) violation, including the QCD vacuum angle \(\bar\theta\) and quark chromo-EDMs, with limits comparable to those from nucleon EDM contributions to atomic EDMs.
