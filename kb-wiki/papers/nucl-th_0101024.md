# Coulomb corrections to $e^+ e^-$ production in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions

> The purpose of this paper is to explain the discrepancies existing in the literature relative to $e^+e^-$ pair production in peripheral heavy ion collisions at ultra-relativistic energies. A controversial issue is the possible cancellation of Coulomb corrections to the Born term in the pair production cross-section. Such a cancellation has been observed in a recent approach based on finding retarded solutions of the Dirac equation, but does not seem to hold in a perturbative approach. We show in this paper that the two approaches are in fact calculating different observables: the perturbative approach gives the exclusive cross-section of single pair production, while the other method gives the inclusive cross-section. We have also performed a thorough study of the electron propagator in the non-static background field of the two nuclei, the conclusion of which is that the retarded propagator is in the ultra-relativistic limit a much simpler object than the Feynman propagator, and can be calculated exactly.

## Key claim

The paper resolves the apparent contradiction between two approaches to $e^+e^-$ pair production in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions by showing that the perturbative approach calculates the exclusive cross-section for single pair production, while the Dirac-equation approach calculates the inclusive cross-section; these are different observables and need not agree, contrary to the assumption in prior literature.

## Method

The authors derive reduction formulae relating the exclusive single-pair production amplitude to a time-ordered (Feynman) correlator and the average number of produced pairs to a retarded correlator, using the in- and out-vacuum formalism. They then study the electron propagator in the non-static background field of two ultra-relativistic nuclei, applying a matrix rotation (retarded/advanced basis) to the Keldysh contour formalism to obtain exact expressions for the retarded and advanced propagators in the ultra-relativistic limit, while showing the Feynman propagator does not simplify similarly.

## Key numbers

* The perturbative resummed cross-section at RHIC energy is about 25% smaller than the Born cross-section, according to Ivanov et al. (2000).
* The Coulomb correction factor per photon exchange is of order $Z\alpha$, which may be of order 1 for a nucleus like gold.
* The ultra-relativistic potential approximation is valid only for leptons produced in the mid-rapidity region, where the typical interaction time scales as $1/\gamma$.

## Context

This work extends the exact Dirac-equation solutions of Segev, Wells, Baltz, McLerran, and Eichmann (1998-2000) and the perturbative calculations of Ivanov, Schiller, and Serbo (1999-2000) by clarifying that they address different observables. It also resolves the unitarity puzzle raised by Bertulani, Baur, and Eichler (1990-1995), where perturbative pair production probabilities exceeded 1, by showing that the vacuum-to-vacuum transition probability must be included as a corrective factor. The results enable future approximate calculations of the Feynman propagator for exclusive pair production, and highlight that the multiplicity distribution is not exactly Poissonian.
