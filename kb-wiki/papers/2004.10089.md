# Time-dependent generator coordinate method study of fission: mass parameters

> Collective mass tensors derived in the cranking approximation to the adiabatic time-dependent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (ATDHFB) method are employed in a study of induced fission dynamics. Together with a collective potential determined in deformation-constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations based on nuclear energy density functionals, the mass tensors specify the collective Hamiltonian that governs the time evolution of the nuclear wave function from an initial state at equilibrium deformation, up to scission and the formation of fission fragments. In an illustrative calculation of low-energy induced fission of $^{228}$Th, $^{230}$Th, $^{234}$U, and $^{240}$Pu, we compare the non-perturbative and perturbative cranking ATDHFB mass tensors in the plane of axially-symmetric quadrupole and octupole deformations, as well as the resulting charge yields.

## Key claim

Non-perturbative cranking ATDHFB collective masses, used for the first time in TDGCM+GOA modeling of induced fission, produce charge yields with reduced symmetric fission compared to the perturbative cranking approximation, generally leading to better agreement with experimental data for the four actinide nuclei studied.

## Method

The authors performed deformation-constrained self-consistent relativistic mean-field calculations using the DD-PC1 energy density functional and a separable pairing force to generate potential energy surfaces and single-particle states in the two-dimensional collective space of axially-symmetric quadrupole (\(\beta_2\)) and octupole (\(\beta_3\)) deformations. They computed collective mass tensors using both the non-perturbative cranking formula (Eq. 3, requiring explicit numerical derivatives of densities) and the perturbative cranking formula (Eq. 5, neglecting time-odd mean fields), then used these mass tensors in the TDGCM+GOA framework with the FELIX code to evolve an initial wave packet and calculate fission charge yields for \(^{228}\)Th, \(^{230}\)Th, \(^{234}\)U, and \(^{240}\)Pu.

## Key numbers

- The range for the collective variable \(\beta_2\) is \(0 \le \beta_2 \le 7\) with step \(\Delta \beta_2 = 0.04\); \(\beta_3\) is considered in \(0 \le \beta_3 \le 3.5\) with step \(\Delta \beta_3 = 0.05\).
- The time step for propagation is \(\delta t = 5 \times 10^{-4}\) zs (1 zs = \(10^{-21}\) s), and charge and mass distributions are calculated after \(10^5\) time steps (50 zs).
- The initial wave packet is constructed with a Gaussian width parameter \(\sigma = 0.5\) MeV and the average energy \(E_{\text{coll}}^*\) is chosen approximately 1 MeV above the fission barrier.
- The absorption potential parameters are: absorption rate \(r = 20 \times 10^{22}\) s\(^{-1}\) and width of absorption band \(w = 6.0\); charge yields are convoluted with a Gaussian of width 1.6 charge units.
- The pre-scission domain is defined by \(\langle \hat{Q}_N \rangle > 3\) using a Gaussian neck operator with \(a_N = 1\) fm.
- For \(^{228}\)Th, \(^{230}\)Th, and \(^{234}\)U the scission line starts from an elongated symmetric point with \(\beta_2 \approx 6\); for \(^{240}\)Pu this value is somewhat larger.
- The pairing strength parameters are increased relative to the original values by factors \(G_n/G_0 = 1.12\) for neutrons and \(G_p/G_0 = 1.08\) for protons.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of spontaneous fission half-lives using non-perturbative and perturbative cranking ATDHFB masses (Sadhukhan 2013, Zhao 2015) to the case of induced fission dynamics within the TDGCM+GOA framework, where previously only perturbative cranking masses had been employed (Goutte 2005, Regnier 2016, Zdeb 2017, Regnier 2018, Regnier 2019, Tao 2017, Zhao 2019). The results confirm the finding of Giuliani (2018) that non-perturbative methods predict more complex mass structures with isolated peaks at single-particle level crossings, and demonstrate that using these non-perturbative masses reduces symmetric fission yields, motivating future studies with additional collective degrees of freedom such as non-axial shape deformations and dynamical pairing.
