# A unified approach for the hadronic weak decays of $Λ$ and $Σ^{\pm}\to Nπ$

> We provide a unified approach for the two-body hadronic weak decays of hyperons with $S=-1$, i.e. $Λ$ and $Σ^\pm$, in the framework of the non-relativistic constitute quark model (NRCQM). A combined analysis shows that the branching ratios and asymmetry parameters of the decay channels $Λ\to pπ^-$ and $Σ^\pm\to Nπ$ can be well described in the same framework with the direct pion emission, color suppressed internal $W$ emission, and pole terms included. However, the channel $Λ\to nπ^0$ indicates significant deviations from the experimental data based on these mentioned transition mechanism. We demonstrate that the final state interactions (FSIs) via the coupled-channel rescatterings play a crucial role in $Λ\to nπ^0$. Namely, the dominant decay channel of $Λ\to pπ^-$ can contribute to $Λ\to nπ^0$ via the $pπ^-\to nπ^0$ rescatterings. This is a leading correction effect for $Λ\to nπ^0$ at the one-loop level. We find that such FSIs only become the leading effects in $Λ\to nπ^0$, but contribute as subleading contributions in other channels. We also demonstrate that the pole terms are indispensable in these hyperon decays. In particular, for $Σ^-\to nπ^-$ and $Σ^+\to nπ^+$, it shows that $Λ(1405)$ as the intermediate state in the pole term amplitude is necessary for reproducing the experimental data. We also find that a dynamic selection rule forbids the radial excitation state $N(1710)$ of the quark model multiplet $|70, ^28,2,0^+,1/2^+\rangle$ from contribution. To some extent, the hyperon hadronic weak decays serve as a special probe for the underlying transition mechanisms and can provide some constraints on the intermediate $J^P=1/2^\pm$ baryon resonances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unified non-relativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) framework can simultaneously describe the branching ratios and asymmetry parameters of most hyperon hadronic weak decays (Lambda to p pi- and Sigma+/- to N pi), but the channel Lambda to n pi0 requires additional final-state interactions via coupled-channel rescatterings (specifically p pi- to n pi0) to resolve significant deviations from experimental data.

## Method

The authors computed transition amplitudes for the two-body hadronic weak decays of Lambda and Sigma+/- hyperons using the NRCQM, incorporating three mechanisms: direct pion emission (DPE), color-suppressed internal W emission (CS), and pole terms (type-A and type-B) with intermediate baryon resonances. They then added one-loop final-state interactions (FSIs) via coupled-channel rescatterings to account for the Lambda to n pi0 channel, and calculated partial decay widths and asymmetry parameters for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The branching ratio fraction for Lambda decays was measured as R_Lambda = 0.646 +/- 0.008 (Brookhaven National Laboratory) and earlier as 0.0624 +/- 0.030 (Lawrence Radiation Laboratory)
- The branching ratio fraction for Lambda to n pi0 was reported as R_Lambda = 0.35 +/- 0.05 (Chretien 1963) and 0.291 +/- 0.034 (Brown 1963)
- BR(Sigma+ to p pi0) = 0.5172 +/- 0.0036 (Marraffino 1980)
- The ratio of asymmetry parameters alpha_+(Sigma+ to n pi+)/alpha_0(Sigma+ to p pi0) = -0.073 +/- 0.021 (Marraffino 1980)
- The branching ratio fraction R_Sigma+ = Gamma(Sigma+ to n pi+)/[Gamma(Sigma+ to n pi+)+Gamma(Sigma+ to p pi0)] = 0.488 +/- 0.008 (Nowak 1978)
- BESIII measured alpha_- = 0.757 +/- 0.011 +/- 0.008 for Lambda to p pi- (BESIII 2021)
- BESIII measured alpha_0 = -0.998 +/- 0.037 +/- 0.0009 for Sigma+ to p pi0 (BESIII 2020)
- BESIII measured alpha_+ = 0.0481 +/- 0.0031 +/- 0.0019 for Sigma+ to n pi+ (BESIII 2023)
- The ratio alpha_+/alpha_0 = -0.049 +/- 0.0032 +/- 0.0021 (BESIII 2023)
- Asymmetry parameters from earlier measurements: alpha_- = -0.071 +/- 0.012 for Sigma- to n pi-, alpha_0 = -0.999 +/- 0.022 for Sigma+ to p pi0, alpha_+/alpha_0 = -0.062 +/- 0.016 for Sigma+ to n pi+ (Bangerter 1969)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of hyperon weak decays using the pole model (Nardulli 1987), constituent quark model (LeYaouanc 1977, Wu 1985), and chiral perturbation theory with large-N_c expansion (Flores-Mendieta 2019). It specifically addresses the S/P-wave puzzle and the Delta I = 1/2 selection rule suppression of the Delta I = 3/2 amplitude, which were identified as unresolved issues in earlier theoretical approaches. The unified framework enables future combined analyses of both Lambda and Sigma+/- decays, and the authors note that hyperon hadronic weak decays can serve as a probe for intermediate J^P = 1/2+/- baryon resonances, with the BESIII experiment's correlated hyperon-anti-hyperon production enabling simultaneous measurements of branching ratios and asymmetry parameters for CP violation studies.
