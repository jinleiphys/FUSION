# Search for global f-modes and p-modes in the 8B neutrino flux

> The impact of global acoustic modes on the 8B neutrino flux time series is computed for the first time. It is shown that the time fluctuations of the 8B neutrino flux depend on the amplitude of acoustic eigenfunctions in the region where the 8B neutrino flux is produced: modes with low n (or order) that have eigenfunctions with a relatively large amplitude in the Sun's core, strongly affect the neutrino flux; conversely, modes with high n that have eigenfunctions with a minimal amplitude in the Sun's core have a very small impact on the neutrino flux. It was found that the global modes with a larger impact on the 8B neutrino flux have a frequency of oscillation in the interval 250 μHz to 500 μHz (or a period in the interval 30 minutes to 70 minutes), such as the f-modes (n=0) for the low degrees, radial modes of order n smaller or equal to 3, and the dipole mode of order n=1. Their corresponding neutrino eigenfunctions are very sensitive to the solar inner core and are unaffected by the variability of the external layers of the solar surface. If time variability of neutrinos is observed for these modes, it will lead to new ways of improving the sound speed profile inversion in the central region of the Sun.

## Key claim

The paper computes for the first time the impact of global acoustic modes on the 8B neutrino flux time series, finding that modes with frequencies between 250 μHz and 500 μHz (periods 30 to 70 minutes), specifically f-modes (n=0) for low degrees, radial modes of order n ≤ 3, and the dipole mode of order n=1, produce the largest flux variations because their temperature eigenfunctions have significant amplitude in the solar core, while the exact amplitude of these variations remains uncertain due to unknown excitation and damping mechanisms.

## Method

The author computed temperature eigenfunctions δT_{ln}(r) for acoustic modes of degree l and order n using a standard solar model, then derived the neutrino survival eigenfunction Ψ_{ln} by convolving the temperature perturbation with the 8B neutrino production region Φ(r), density ρ, and radial volume element. The quantity B_{ln} was computed by integrating Ψ_{ln} over the solar radius, representing the fraction of total neutrino flux affected by each mode, and the relative neutrino flux variation was estimated as |Δφ_{ln}/φ_o|_max = 25.5 A_{ln} B_{ln} where A_{ln} is the mode amplitude estimated from surface observations.

## Key numbers

- The relative neutrino flux variation is 25.5 times larger than the temperature perturbation that caused it (ζ_o = 25.5, derived from ϑ = 24 for 8B neutrino production and Γ_3 = 5/3)
- Modes with largest impact have frequencies in the interval 250 μHz to 500 μHz (periods 30 minutes to 70 minutes)
- The p_1 radial mode has observed frequency 258.60 ± 0.030 μHz and B_{ln} = 1.5280 × 10^{-1}
- The f-mode for l=5 has theoretical frequency 415.08 μHz and B_{ln} = 5.7283 × 10^{0}
- The p_1 dipole mode (l=1) has theoretical frequency 283.80 μHz and B_{ln} = 21.674 × 10^{-2}
- The estimated surface amplitude A_{nl} ∼ 10^{-5} leads to |Δφ_{ln}/φ_o|_max ∼ 0.01% for modes with B_{ln} ∼ 1
- The difference between theoretical and observed frequencies is smaller than 0.1%

## Context

This work extends the study of solar neutrino flux time fluctuations, previously focused on solar magnetic cycles and day-night Earth effects (e.g., 2004, 2005, 2006), by computing for the first time the specific impact of global acoustic modes on the 8B neutrino flux. The paper recommends that observers focus on the 30-70 minute period range, following a negative search by Aharmim et al. (2010) who looked for oscillations in the 1-144 day^{-1} range, and suggests that future detectors like LENA (Wurm et al., 2011) could detect temporal variations with amplitudes of order 0.5% covering periods from tens of minutes to hundreds of years.
