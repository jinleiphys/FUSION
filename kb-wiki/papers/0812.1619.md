# Heavy Ions at LHC: A Quest for Quark-Gluon Plasma

> Quantum Chromo Dynamics (QCD), the theory of strong interactions, predicts a transition of the usual matter to a new phase of matter, called Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), at sufficiently high temperatures. The non-perturbative technique of defining a theory on a space-time lattice has been used to obtain this and other predictions about the nature of QGP. Heavy ion collisions at the Large Hadron Collider in CERN can potentially test these predictions and thereby test our theoretical understanding of confinement. This brief review aims at providing a glimpse of both these aspects of QGP.

## Key claim

This review argues that lattice QCD provides a first-principles, parameter-free framework for predicting the properties of Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP) and the QCD phase transition, and that heavy ion collisions at the LHC can test these predictions. The authors hedge that while lattice QCD has produced quantitative results for many observables, the current uncertainty in the transition temperature (170-190 MeV) translates to a roughly 60% difference in energy density estimates at T_c, and that many transport coefficient determinations require extra assumptions not universally accepted.

## Method

The authors review lattice QCD as a non-perturbative technique where space-time is discretized onto a lattice, with quark fields placed on sites and gluon fields on links, enabling numerical evaluation of path integrals via Monte Carlo generation of gauge field configurations. For finite-temperature studies, they use an N_s^3 x N_t lattice with N_t much smaller than N_s, where temperature T = 1/(N_t * a), and compute thermodynamic quantities such as energy density, pressure, and quark number susceptibilities from the partition function. They also discuss extraction of transport coefficients (shear and bulk viscosity) via Kubo relations and spectral functions from Euclidean correlators using the maximum entropy method.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperature T_c range: 170-190 MeV (current lattice QCD results)
- Energy density at T_c: uncertainty of ~60% due to T_c uncertainty (since energy density proportional to T^4)
- Quenched QCD energy density: about 85% of the corresponding ideal gas value
- Wroblewski parameter lambda_s at T_c: 0.4-0.5 from lattice QCD, compared to experimental value 0.47 ± 0.04
- Decay constants from lattice QCD: f_D+ = 201 ± 3 ± 17 MeV, f_Ds = 249 ± 3 ± 16 MeV (experimentally measured as f_D+ = 223 ± 16 ± 7 MeV, f_Ds = 283 ± 17 ± 14 MeV)
- Strong coupling alpha_s(M_Z): lattice determination compared with perturbative results (Figure 3)
- Total proton-proton cross section prediction at 14 TeV: 125 ± 25 mb (from Regge models)
- J/psi and eta_c spectral functions: peaks persist up to 2.25 T_c, gone by 3 T_c; chi_c has no peak by 1.1 T_c
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio: close to T_c, small values consistent with the AdS-CFT bound; perturbation theory suggests larger values

## Context

This review extends the line of work using lattice QCD to study QCD under extreme conditions, building on earlier quenched approximation results and the pioneering finite-temperature studies from the 1980s and 1990s. It connects lattice predictions (such as from the MILC and HPQCD collaborations, and work by Fodor, Cheng, and others) to experimental heavy ion programs at SPS (17 GeV per nucleon), RHIC (62-200 GeV per nucleon), and the upcoming LHC (5.5 TeV per nucleon for Pb-Pb). The review enables future experimental tests of QGP properties including equation of state, transport coefficients, heavy quarkonium suppression, and flavor correlations as signatures of deconfinement.
