# Generalized dipole polarizabilities of the pion

> We discuss the virtual Compton scattering amplitude $γ^\ast+π\to γ+π$ which enters the reaction $π^-+e^-\toπ^-+e^-+γ$. The low-energy scattering amplitude is divided into a model-independent part, involving only the electromagnetic form factor, and a residual part which contains structure information related specifically to (virtual) Compton scattering. In the limit of vanishing final-photon energy, the residual amplitude can be expressed in terms of three generalized dipole polarizabilities which are functions of the squared virtual-photon four-momentum. We study the VCS amplitude in the framework of chiral perturbation theory at ${\cal O}(p^4)$. At the one-loop level the generalized dipole polarizabilities are degenerate $α_L(q^2)=α_T(q^2)=-β(q^2)$.

# Generalized Dipole Polarizabilities of the Pion

## Key claim

At one-loop order in chiral perturbation theory (ChPT at O(p⁴)), the three generalized dipole polarizabilities of the charged pion are degenerate: α_L(q²) = α_T(q²) = -β(q²). This degeneracy is expected to be lifted at two-loop order, and the q²-dependence of the polarizabilities contains no free parameters beyond the pion mass and decay constant.

## Method

The virtual Compton scattering (VCS) amplitude γ* + π → γ + π was analyzed using Low's method to separate a model-independent soft-photon amplitude (depending only on the pion electromagnetic form factor) from a residual structure-dependent part. The residual amplitude was evaluated in the pion Breit frame and parametrized in terms of three generalized dipole polarizabilities. The calculation was performed in chiral perturbation theory at O(p⁴), using the Gasser-Leutwyler Lagrangian with seven low-energy constants l_i, where the combination 2l⁵_r - l⁶_r is determined from the decay π⁺ → e⁺ν_eγ.

## Key numbers

- The combination of low-energy constants is 2l⁵_r - l⁶_r = (2.85 ± 0.42) × 10⁻³, determined from π⁺ → e⁺ν_eγ decay.
- At q² = 0, the real Compton scattering polarizabilities of the charged pion are predicted as ᾱ = -β̄ = (2.68 ± 0.42) × 10⁻⁴ fm³.
- Empirical results for the charged pion electric polarizability from high-energy pion-nucleus bremsstrahlung give ᾱ_{π±} = (6.8 ± 1.4) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Antipov 1983).
- Empirical results for the charged pion magnetic polarizability give β̄_{π±} = (-7.1 ± 4.6) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Antipov 1985).
- Radiative pion photoproduction off the nucleon yields ᾱ_{π±} = (20 ± 12) × 10⁻⁴ fm³ (Aibergenov 1986).
- The pion-decay constant used is F_π = 92.4 MeV.
- O(p⁶) corrections to the O(p⁴) values are 12% for ᾱ and 24% for β̄ (Buergi 1996).

## Context

This work extends the low-energy theorem for real Compton scattering (Low 1954, Gell-Mann and Goldberger 1954) to virtual photons, generalizing the concept of electric and magnetic polarizabilities (Klein 1955) to the spacelike region. The formalism enables extraction of generalized polarizabilities from experiments such as the Fermilab SELEX E781 measurement of π⁻e⁻ → π⁻e⁻γ. The chiral perturbation theory results at O(p⁴) provide a baseline prediction that can be tested against future experimental data, with the degeneracy α_L = α_T = -β expected to be broken at two-loop order, similar to the real Compton scattering case.
