# Kubo formulae for the shear and bulk viscosity relaxation times and the scalar field theory shear $τ_π$ calculation

> In this paper we provide a quantum field theoretical study on the shear and bulk relaxation times. First, we find Kubo formulas for the shear and the bulk relaxation times, respectively. They are found by examining response functions of the stress-energy tensor. We use general properties of correlation functions and the gravitational Ward identity to parametrize analytical structures of the Green functions describing both sound and diffusion mode. We find that the hydrodynamic limits of the real parts of the respective energy-momentum tensor correlation functions provide us with the method of computing both the shear and bulk viscosity relaxation times. Next, we calculate the shear viscosity relaxation time using the diagrammatic approach in the Keldysh basis for the massless $λϕ^4$ theory. We derive a respective integral equation which enables us to compute $ητ_π$ and then we extract the shear relaxation time. The relaxation time is shown to be inversely related to the thermal width as it should be.

## Key claim

The paper derives new Kubo formulas for the shear and bulk viscosity relaxation times from the analytic structure of stress-energy tensor correlation functions, but the shear relaxation time formula contains an additional term \(\kappa/2\) that must be subtracted to isolate \(\eta\tau_\pi\), and the bulk relaxation time formula requires a next-to-leading order calculation that is deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors use the gravitational Ward identity and general properties of retarded Green functions (spectral representation, parity in frequency) to parametrize the analytic structure of the stress-energy tensor correlation functions for both transverse (shear) and longitudinal (sound) hydrodynamic modes. For the shear relaxation time, they then perform a diagrammatic calculation in the Keldysh (closed-time path) formalism for massless \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory, starting from one-loop diagrams and resumming ladder diagrams via a Bethe-Salpeter equation to obtain the leading-order contribution to \(\eta\tau_\pi\). The shear relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) is extracted by numerically solving the integral equation for \(\partial_\omega \text{Re} G_{aarr}\) alongside the integral equation for \(\text{Im} G_{aarr}\) that gives \(\eta\).

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) scales as \(1/\Gamma_p\), where \(\Gamma_p\) is the thermal width (the mean free path scale), while \(\eta\tau_\pi\) scales as \(1/\Gamma_p^2\).
* The product \(\eta\tau_\pi\) is evaluated numerically as a function of the constant coupling \(\lambda\) in massless \(\lambda\phi^4\) theory (specific numerical values are not given in the truncated text).
* The ratio \(\langle\epsilon+P\rangle\tau_\pi/\eta\) is evaluated numerically as a function of the constant coupling (specific numerical values are not given in the truncated text).

## Context

This work extends the field-theoretical approach to second-order transport coefficients introduced in Jeon (2015) by providing explicit Kubo formulas for both shear and bulk relaxation times. It contrasts with kinetic-theory approaches such as the 14-moment approximation of Israel and Stewart and the moment methods of Denicol (2010, 2012, 2014), which require inverting the collision operator and finding its eigenvalues. The results enable first-principles quantum field theory calculations of \(\tau_\pi\) and \(\tau_\Pi\) in weakly coupled theories, with the bulk relaxation time calculation left as an open problem for future next-to-leading order work.
