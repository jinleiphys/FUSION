# Systematical shell-model calculation in the pairing-plus-multipole Hamiltonian with a monopole interaction for the pf5/2g9/2 shell

> The recently-proposed effective shell-model interaction, the pairing-plus-multipole Hamiltonian with the monopole interaction obtained by empirical fits starting from the monopole-based universal force (PMMU), is systematically applied to nuclei of the pf5/2g9/2 shell region. It is demonstrated that the calculation describes reasonably well a wide range of experimental data, including not only the low-lying and high-excitation spectra, E2 transitions, quadrupole moments, and magnetic moments, but also the binding energies, for Ni, Cu, Zn, Ga, Ge, As, and Se isotopes with A=64-80. In particular, a structure of the neutron-rich Ge and Se isotopes is discussed in detail.

## Key claim

The PMMU Hamiltonian, which combines a pairing-plus-multipole interaction with a monopole interaction derived from the monopole-based universal force (VMU) and then refitted to experimental data, can describe a wide range of nuclear properties for nuclei in the pf5/2g9/2 shell region, but the model space may be insufficient to fully capture behavior beyond N=40, particularly for Ni and Zn isotopes, due to missing orbitals such as d5/2 and f7/2.

## Method

The authors performed large-scale shell-model calculations using the PMMU Hamiltonian in the pf5/2g9/2 model space, with 28 parameters (four pairing-plus-multipole force strengths, 14 monopole matrix elements modified from the VMU starting point, and four single-particle energies) determined by fitting to experimental binding energies of 91 nuclei and 186 excited-state energies for nuclei with A=64-80. The monopole matrix elements were scaled by a factor (58/A)^0.3, and calculations were carried out using the MSHELL64 code with M-scheme dimensions up to approximately 3x10^9, with no truncation for Ni, Cu, Zn, Ga, and Ge isotopes, and some truncations for As and Se isotopes.

## Key numbers

* RMS deviations for binding energies: 691 keV across 91 nuclei with A=64-80
* RMS deviations for excitation energies: 256 keV across 186 experimental excited-state energies
* Pairing strength g0 = 18.0/A (MeV), quadrupole strength chi2 = 334.0/A^(5/3) (MeV), octupole strength chi3 = 259.2/A^2 (MeV)
* Single-particle energies: epsilon(p3/2) = -9.40 MeV, epsilon(f5/2) = -8.29 MeV, epsilon(p1/2) = -7.49 MeV, epsilon(g9/2) = -5.70 MeV
* Effective charges for E2 transitions: e_p = 1.5 (protons), e_n = 1.1 (neutrons)
* The first excited 0_2+ state in Ge isotopes reaches its minimum excitation energy at N=40, with 72Ge showing the 0_2+ state lying below the 2_1+ state

## Context

This work extends the PMMU model previously introduced by Kaneko et al. (2014) for the pf and pf5/2g9/2 shells, applying it systematically to a much larger set of nuclei. It builds upon the monopole-based universal force (VMU) developed by Otsuka et al. (2010) and the EPQQM model used by Hasegawa et al. (2001) and Kaneko et al. (2002) for N≈Z nuclei. The results are compared with the JUN45 effective interaction of Honma et al. (2009) and the JJ4B interaction of Padilla et al. (2005) and Lisetskiy et al. (2004). The work enables systematic testing of shell-model predictions for neutron-rich Ge and Se isotopes, particularly for understanding shape coexistence and the behavior of the 0_2+ state at N=40, though the authors note that including the d5/2 and f7/2 orbitals may be necessary for improved description beyond N=40.
