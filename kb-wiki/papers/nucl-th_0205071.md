# Self-consistent antikaon dynamics in isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium

> We investigate properties of antikaons and hyperon resonances in isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium, using a self-consistent, covariant scheme based on vacuum antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitude.

# Self-consistent antikaon dynamics in isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium

## Key claim
The paper extends a previously developed self-consistent, covariant framework for antikaon and hyperon-resonance properties from isospin-symmetric to isospin-asymmetric nuclear medium, finding that the neutral antikaon is more strongly modified than the negative kaon in neutron matter, and that hyperon resonances such as the Σ(1385) exhibit pronounced isospin splitting.

## Method
The authors solve a Bethe-Salpeter equation for in-medium antikaon-nucleon scattering, using vacuum scattering amplitudes for s-, p- and d-waves (up to J=3/2) from Lutz (2002) as input. The antikaon self-energy is computed self-consistently via an iterative procedure (4-5 iterations) that couples the in-medium two-particle propagator, modified by separate neutron and proton Fermi momenta, to the in-medium scattering amplitudes. The formalism accounts for mixing of partial waves in the nuclear medium and treats neutral and charged antikaons separately in the particle basis.

## Key numbers
- Neutron matter density used: ρ = 0.17 fm⁻³
- For the lead-like case: proton Fermi momentum p_{F_p} = 310.8 MeV, neutron Fermi momentum p_{F_n} = 355.8 MeV
- The Σ(1195) in neutron matter at rest: Σ⁺ maximum at 1180 MeV, Σ⁻ maximum at 1192 MeV, with Σ⁰ between the charged states
- Antikaon spectral functions show nonzero support starting from around 90 MeV in the lead-like medium
- The vacuum antikaon-nucleon scattering amplitudes describe data quantitatively up to kaon laboratory momentum of 500 MeV and qualitatively up to 1000 MeV

## Context
This work extends the self-consistent, covariant framework developed by Lutz (2002) for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter to the isospin-asymmetric case relevant for neutron stars and heavy-ion collisions. It builds on earlier studies of kaon condensation in neutron stars (Kaplan, 1986) and antikaon spectral functions in symmetric matter (Lutz, 1998; Lutz, 2002). The results enable improved transport model calculations for subthreshold kaon production in heavy-ion reactions and more realistic descriptions of kaonic atoms and neutron star interiors, where isospin asymmetry is significant.
