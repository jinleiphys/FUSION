# A Fast Algorithm for Lattice Hyperonic Potentials

> We describe an efficient algorithm to compute a large number of baryon-baryon interactions from $NN$ to $ΞΞ$ by means of HAL QCD method, which lays the groundwork for the nearly physical point lattice QCD calculation with volume $(96a)^4\approx$($8.2$fm)$^4$. Preliminary results of $ΛN$ potential calculated with quark masses corresponding to ($m_π$,$m_{K}$)$\approx$(146,525)MeV are presented.

## Key claim

The paper presents an efficient algorithm for computing baryon-baryon interactions from nucleon-nucleon to Xi-Xi using the HAL QCD method, laying groundwork for a nearly physical point lattice QCD calculation. The abstract glosses over the fact that the presented Lambda-N potential results are preliminary, using only about 1/10 of the full scheduled statistics, and that the effects from the Sigma-N channel are only implicitly included rather than explicitly treated.

## Method

The authors employ the HAL QCD method, where interhadron potentials are obtained from lattice QCD measurements of Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter (NBS) wave functions via four-point correlators. They introduce an effective baryon block algorithm that uses Fast-Fourier-Transform (FFT) techniques to reduce the computational cost of evaluating the 52 two-baryon channels simultaneously, compared to the naive contraction approach. The algorithm is implemented on gauge configurations generated with Nf=2+1 dynamical clover fermions on a 96^4 lattice using the K computer.

## Key numbers

- Lattice volume: (96a)^4 approximately (8.2 fm)^4, with lattice spacing a approximately 0.085 fm
- Quark masses correspond to (m_pi, m_K) approximately (146, 525) MeV
- The effective block algorithm reduces the number of iterations for the Lambda-N channel from 3456 (naive counting) to 370, excluding momentum space degrees of freedom
- The preliminary Lambda-N potential results use only about 1/10 of the entire scheduled statistics for fiscal year 2015

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method previously developed by Ishii (2006) and Aoki (2009) for studying hadronic forces from lattice QCD, and builds upon the unified contraction algorithm (UCA) introduced by Doi (2012). The algorithm enables the computation of 52 baryon-baryon channels simultaneously, which is essential for the ongoing large-scale lattice QCD calculation of baryon interactions from NN to XiXi. The preliminary Lambda-N potential results at nearly physical quark masses provide a foundation for future studies that will explicitly include Sigma-N channel effects with larger statistics.
