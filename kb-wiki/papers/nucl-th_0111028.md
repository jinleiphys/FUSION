# A possible quark origin of two-pion emission in $e+N$ and $N+N$ collisions

> The constituent quark model has been taken as a starting point for investigation of one- and two-pion emissions. A wide range of oservables has been evaluated in the framework of the $^3P_0$ model. Comparison of the results with the data (when it is possible) shows an approximate agreement.

# A possible quark origin of two-pion emission in $e+N$ and $N+N$ collisions

## Key claim

The constituent quark model with the $^3P_0$ mechanism can describe one- and two-pion emission in electron-nucleon and nucleon-nucleon collisions without invoking Lagrangian field theory coupling constants, but the predicted cross sections for two-pion processes are too small to be seen over background unless a modified vertex form factor with a shorter-range scale (about 0.2-0.3 fm) is introduced.

## Method

The $^3P_0$ model of pion emission was used to calculate spectroscopic factors and decay widths for baryon resonances ($N$, $\Delta$, $N_{1/2^-}(1535)$, $N_{1/2^+}(1440)$) from quark momentum distributions in the nucleon. For two-pion processes, a triangle diagram with a $\sigma\pi\pi$ scalar coupling was evaluated, summing coherently over all baryon intermediate states up to $2\hbar\omega$ harmonic oscillator configurations. The model was extended to the two-nucleon system by including intermediate dibaryon six-quark states ($d^{\prime}$ and $d^{\prime\prime}$) to construct a coupled-channel model of $NN$ interaction.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factors $S_p^{B_i\pi}$: 0.153 for $N$, 0.065 for $\Delta$, 0.006 for $N_{1/2^-}(1535)$, 0.011 for $N_{1/2^+}(1440)$
- Calculated pion decay widths $\Gamma_{B_i}$: 63 MeV for $\Delta$, 52 MeV for $N_{1/2^-}(1535)$, 47 MeV for $N_{1/2^+}(1440)$
- Total number of pions in the nucleon $N_{\pi}=0.24$ from the model, compared to an "experimental" value of $0.18\pm0.06$ from earlier pion knockout data
- $\sigma NN$ coupling constant $g_{\sigma NN}=0.433$ with standard parameters ($b=0.5$ fm, $b_{\sigma}=0.2$ fm, $g_{\sigma\pi\pi}=4$ GeV), increasing to $g_{\sigma NN}=3.26$ after modifying the Gaussian form factor with $Z=1.9$ (corresponding to $\Lambda=0.9$ GeV/c)
- Dibaryon admixture in the deuteron $P_{DB}=3.66\%$

## Context

This work extends the $^3P_0$ model developed by the Moscow group and the Moscow-Tuebingen group (Obukhovsky and collaborators, late 1990s) from one-pion to two-pion emission processes in both nucleon and two-nucleon systems. The calculated $\sigma NN$ coupling constant is smaller than realistic values (5-10) unless the vertex form factors are modified to include a shorter-range scale, indicating that the standard quark shell model with confinement scale $b\approx 0.5-1$ fm predicts too soft vertices. The coupled-channel model of $NN$ interaction based on this mechanism enables a description of $NN$ scattering at intermediate energies and provides a small but important dibaryon admixture in the deuteron for high-precision deuteron properties.
