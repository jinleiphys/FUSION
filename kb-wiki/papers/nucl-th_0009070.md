# Superdeformed Band in ^{36}Ar Described by Projected Shell Model

> The projected shell model implements shell model configuration mixing in the projected deformed basis. Our analysis on the recently observed superdeformed band in $^{36}$Ar suggests that the neutron and proton 2-quasiparticle and the 4-quasiparticle bands cross the superdeformed ground band at the same angular momentum. This constitutes a picture of band disturbance in which the first and the second band-crossing, commonly seen at separate rotation frequencies in heavy nuclei, occur simultaneously. We also attempt to understand the assumptions of two previous theoretical calculations which interpreted this band. Electromagnetic properties of the band are predicted.

# Superdeformed Band in \(^{36}\)Ar Described by Projected Shell Model

## Key claim
The projected shell model (PSM) reproduces the observed superdeformed band in \(^{36}\)Ar and shows that the neutron and proton 2-quasiparticle bands and the 4-quasiparticle band all cross the superdeformed ground band at the same angular momentum near spin \(I=10\), causing a simultaneous band-crossing rather than the two separate crossings typically seen in heavier nuclei. The authors suggest that this simultaneous crossing explains the observed band disturbance, but note small deviations from data at the crossing region and higher spins, leaving open the question of whether the renormalized like-nucleon pairing interactions are sufficient when both neutron and proton pair alignments occur together.

## Method
The authors performed a projected shell model calculation for \(^{36}\)Ar using a deformed Nilsson single-particle basis with three major shells (\(N=1,2,3\)) for both neutrons and protons at deformation \(\varepsilon_2=0.42\). The model space included 0-quasiparticle (ground band), 2-quasiparticle, and 4-quasiparticle states built from the \(1f_{7/2}\) subshell, with angular momentum projection to restore rotational symmetry and diagonalization of a shell model Hamiltonian containing quadrupole-quadrupole, monopole pairing, and quadrupole pairing interactions. Band diagrams were constructed by plotting the expectation value of the Hamiltonian for each configuration as a function of spin before configuration mixing.

## Key numbers
- The superdeformed band in \(^{36}\)Ar was calculated up to spin \(I=16\), which is the highest spin state achievable given the single-particle content of the mean-field states.
- The band-crossing occurs near spin \(I=10\), where the 0-, 2-, and 4-quasiparticle bands cross simultaneously.
- At spin \(I=0\), both neutron and proton pairing gaps are larger than 1 MeV, falling to 0.3–0.4 MeV after spin \(I=8\).
- The B(E2) values show an approximate 40% drop at the band termination spin \(I=16\) compared to the maximum value at \(I=8\).
- The g-factor increases smoothly from 0.4 at the bandhead to \(Z/A = 0.5\) at \(I=8\), remaining constant thereafter.

## Context
This work extends the projected shell model, previously applied to superdeformed bands in the \(A\sim 190\), \(A\sim 130\), and \(A\sim 60\) mass regions, to the lightest known superdeformed nucleus \(^{36}\)Ar. The paper addresses assumptions in two prior theoretical calculations: the Cranked Nilsson-Strutinsky model by Tod (2000), which neglected pairing correlations entirely, and the spherical shell model by the same experimental group, which excluded the \(1d_{5/2}\) orbital from the model space. The PSM results clarify why the shell model could reproduce the data despite excluding \(1d_{5/2}\) (its contributions can be renormalized) and demonstrate that pairing is non-negligible at low spins. The simultaneous band-crossing picture was suggested earlier by Sheikh (1990) and elaborated by Frauendorf (1999) for \(N=Z\) nuclei. The predictions for B(E2) and g-factor values await experimental verification through lifetime and g-factor measurements.
