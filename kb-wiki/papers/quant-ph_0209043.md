# Singular potentials and annihilation

> We discuss the regularization of attractive singular potentials $-α_{s}/r^{s}$, $s\geq 2$ by infinitesimal imaginary addition to interaction constant $α_{s}=α_{s}\pm i0$. Such a procedure enables unique definition of scattering observables and is equal to an absorption (creation) of particles in the origin. It is shown, that suggested regularization is an analytical continuation of the scattering amplitudes of repulsive singular potential in interaction constant $α_{s}$. The nearthreshold properties of regularized in a mentioned way singular potential are examined. We obtain expressions for the scattering lengths, which turn to be complex even for infinitesimal imaginary part of interaction constant. The problem of perturbation of nearthreshold states of regular potential by a singular one is treated, the expressions for level shifts and widths are obtained. We show, that the physical sense of suggested regularization is that the scattering observables are insensitive to any details of the short range modification of singular potential, if there exists sufficiently strong inelastic short range interaction. In this case the scattering observables are determined by solutions of Schrodinger equation with regularized potential $-(α_{s}\pm i0)/r^{s}$. We point out that the developed formalism can be applied for the description of systems with short range annihilation, in particular low energy nucleon-antinucleon scattering.

## Key claim

Attractive singular potentials of the form $-\alpha_{s}/r^{s}$ with $s \geq 2$ can be uniquely regularized by adding an infinitesimal imaginary part to the interaction constant $\alpha_{s} = \alpha_{s} \pm i0$, which is equivalent to imposing a full absorption (or creation) boundary condition at the origin; this procedure eliminates the collapse problem and yields well-defined scattering observables that are insensitive to any details of short-range modification provided there is sufficiently strong inelastic short-range interaction.

## Method

The author regularizes the attractive singular potential by taking the interaction constant complex, $\alpha_{s} = \alpha_{s} \pm i\omega$ with $\omega > 0$, then taking the limit $\omega \rightarrow +0$ after matching logarithmic derivatives at a cut-off radius $r_0$ that is sent to zero. The solution of the radial Schrodinger equation near the origin is expressed in terms of Hankel functions, and the requirement that only the "incoming" wave survives (full absorption) selects the unique linear combination of independent solutions. The scattering length and phase shifts are then obtained from the asymptotic behavior of the wave function at large distances, and the method is extended to treat the perturbation of near-threshold states of a regular potential by the singular one.

## Key numbers

- For a singular potential $-\alpha_s/r^s$ with $s > 3$, the S-wave scattering length for absorption is $a_S^{abs} = \exp(-i\pi/(s-2)) \left( \frac{\sqrt{\alpha_s}}{s-2} \right)^{2/(s-2)} \Gamma((s-3)/(s-2)) / \Gamma((s-1)/(s-2))$.
- For the case $s=2$ with $\alpha > 1/4$, the scattering phase shift is $\delta = i\pi\sqrt{\alpha - 1/4}/2 - \pi/4$, giving an energy-independent S-matrix magnitude $|S| = \exp(-\pi\sqrt{\alpha_2 - 1/4})$ for absorption.
- For the $^3P_0$, $T=0$ nucleon-antinucleon state, the scattering volume calculated with the regularized singular potential is $a_r = -7.66 - i4.87$ fm$^3$, compared to the Kohno-Weise model value $a_{KW} = -8.83 - i4.45$ fm$^3$.
- For hydrogen-antihydrogen interaction with van der Waals potential $-C_6/r^6$, the S-wave scattering length is $a_S = (M C_6)^{1/4} \frac{\Gamma(3/4)}{2\sqrt{2}\Gamma(5/4)} (1-i)$, where $M$ is the proton mass.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of singular potentials and the collapse problem (Efimov 1971, Landau and Lifshitz) by providing a regularization that links singular potentials to inelastic scattering, building on ideas from KPV and from absorption models (Abs1, Abs2). The formalism enables a parameter-free description of low-energy nucleon-antinucleon scattering (Kohno and Weise 1986, Phil, CPS) and hydrogen-antihydrogen interactions (Froel, Vor), where strong short-range annihilation makes scattering observables independent of the details of the short-range potential.
