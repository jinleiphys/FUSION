# Strange Vector Form Factors from Parity-Violating Electron Scattering

> The simplest models might describe the nucleon as 3 light quarks, but this description would be incomplete without inclusion of the sea of glue and q-qbar pairs which binds it. Early indications of a particularly large contribution from strange quarks in this sea to the spin and mass of the nucleon motivated an experimental program examining the role of these strange quarks in the nucleon vector form factors. The strangeness form factors can be extracted from the well-studied electromagnetic structure of the nucleon using parity-violation in electron-nuclear scattering to isolate the effect of the weak interaction. With high luminosity and polarization, and a very stable beam due to its superconducting RF cavities, CEBAF at Jefferson Lab is a precision instrument uniquely well suited to the challenge of measurements of the small parity-violating asymmetries. The techniques and results of the two major Jefferson Lab experimental efforts in parity-violation studies, HAPPEX and G0, as well as efforts to describe the strange form factors in QCD, will be reviewed.

## Key claim

The strange vector form factors of the nucleon can be extracted from parity-violating electron scattering asymmetries, and the combination of Jefferson Lab experiments HAPPEX and G0, together with lattice QCD calculations, has experimentally defined these form factors with sufficient precision to rule out several earlier theoretical estimates that predicted large strange quark contributions.

## Method

The strange electric and magnetic form factors were extracted from measurements of the left-right asymmetry in elastic scattering of longitudinally polarized electrons from unpolarized proton and helium-4 targets at Jefferson Lab. The HAPPEX experiments used the Hall A High Resolution Spectrometers with total absorption shower counters or Cerenkov detectors to isolate elastic events, while the G0 experiment in Hall C used a different detector configuration. The theoretical calculations employed both an indirect method combining lattice QCD valence quark moments with experimental octet baryon magnetic moments under charge symmetry, and a direct lattice QCD calculation of the disconnected strange quark loop.

## Key numbers

- HAPPEX-I measured the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering at ⟨θ_lab⟩ = 12.3° and ⟨Q²⟩ = 0.477 (GeV/c)², yielding A = -15.05 ± 0.98 ± 0.56 ppm
- From HAPPEX-I, the linear combination of strange form factors was determined as G_E^s + η G_M^s = 0.014 ± 0.020 ± 0.010, where η = 0.392 at the given kinematics
- The indirect lattice QCD calculation gave the strangeness magnetic moment G_M^s = -0.046 ± 0.019 μ_N
- The direct lattice QCD calculation by Doi et al. found μ_s = -(0.017 ± 0.025 ± 0.007) μ_N at pion masses around 500 MeV
- The strange quark charge radius from the indirect method was ⟨r²⟩_s^p = 0.001 ± 0.004 ± 0.004 fm²
- The ratio of valence u-quark contributions from lattice QCD was u^p/u^Σ = 1.092 ± 0.030 and u^n/u^Ξ = 1.254 ± 0.124
- The strange to light sea-quark loop ratio was estimated as ℓR_d^s = 0.139 ± 0.042

## Context

This work reviews the experimental program motivated by the EMC spin crisis finding of Δs = -0.16 ± 0.08 (Jaffe 1989) and the large strange sigma commutator estimate of σ_s = 330 MeV (Kaplan 1988), which suggested a far greater role for strange quarks than anticipated. Modern lattice estimates (Young 2009) indicate the strange sigma commutator is almost an order of magnitude smaller, and the experimental results from HAPPEX and G0 have constrained the strange form factors to be small, ruling out earlier model predictions such as Jaffe's (1989) values of ⟨r²_s⟩ = 0.16 ± 0.06 fm² and μ_s = -0.31 ± 0.09 μ_N. The techniques developed for these measurements, including strained GaAs photocathodes and precision Compton polarimetry, enable future parity-violation experiments at Jefferson Lab.
