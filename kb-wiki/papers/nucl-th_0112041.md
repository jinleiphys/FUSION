# Two-body correlations in N=8 and 10 nuclei and effective neutron-neutron interactions in Tamm-Dancoff and two-particle RPA models

> We apply a particle-particle RPA model to study the properties of the two-neutron valence wave function in nuclei 14-C, 12-Be, 11-Li and 14-Be. The RPA model takes account of two body correlations in the core so that it gives a better description of energies and amplitudes than models which assume a neutron closed shell (or subshell) core. With a Gogny neutron-neutron effective interaction or with the equivalent density dependent delta force we are able to reproduce the two-neutron separation energies in these nuclei and in the corresponding cores, except for 9-Li. These calculations suggest the same 2s-1p1/2 shells inversion in 12-Be_13-Be than in 11-Be.

## Key claim

The particle-particle RPA model, which accounts for two-body correlations in the core, reproduces two-neutron separation energies in N=8 and 10 nuclei (except for 9Li) using either the Gogny finite-range interaction or an equivalent zero-range density-dependent delta force, whereas the simpler Tamm-Dancoff model fails for these systems because it assumes an inert closed-shell core.

## Method

The authors applied the particle-particle RPA (pair vibration) model to describe two neutrons outside a core, expanding the two-particle Green's function in ladder diagrams that include both upward-going and backward-going terms, unlike the Tamm-Dancoff approximation which includes only upward-going diagrams. They used a one-body Woods-Saxon potential with a surface term simulating particle-phonon couplings, discretized continuum states in a 20 fm radial box, and employed either the Gogny D1S finite-range effective interaction or a zero-range density-dependent delta force with parameters fitted by Garrido et al. (2001) to reproduce the Gogny pairing gap in nuclear matter. The RPA equations were solved simultaneously for the A_c+2 and A_c-2 systems, giving the two-neutron separation energies and the RPA amplitudes for the ground states of 14C, 12Be, 11Li, and 14Be.

## Key numbers

- Two-neutron separation energy in 14C: 12.9 MeV (RPA with Gogny force), compared to experimental 13.12 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy in 12Be: 3.69 MeV (RPA with Gogny force), compared to experimental 3.67 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy in 11Li: 0.37 MeV (RPA with Gogny force), compared to experimental 0.34 ± 0.05 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy in 9Li: 3.8 MeV (RPA with Gogny force), compared to experimental 6.1 MeV
- Two-neutron separation energy in 14Be: 1.30 MeV (RPA with Gogny force, assuming 2s-1p1/2 shell inversion in 12Be), compared to experimental 1.34 ± 0.11 MeV
- Strength V0 of the zero-range delta force: 500 MeV.fm^3 for 14C-12C, 510 MeV.fm^3 for 12Be-10Be, and 560 MeV.fm^3 for 11Li-9Li
- For 12Be, the RPA amplitude X_alpha for (1p3/2)^2 is 0.60, indicating strong core correlations
- For 11Li, the RPA amplitude X_alpha for (1p3/2)^2 is 0.45, also indicating strong deviation from closed shell in 9Li

## Context

This work extends the earlier Tamm-Dancoff calculations by Pacheco and Vinh Mau (2000) that assumed a closed 1p3/2 neutron shell core and failed to describe 6He, giving a separation energy of several MeV instead of the experimental 0.97 MeV, a problem also found by Esbensen et al. (1997). The RPA model accounts for core correlations known from Cohen-Kurath (1965) shell model calculations for 12C and from shell model studies of 10Be by Fortune et al. (1978) and others. The results enable a consistent description of neutron-rich N=8 and 10 nuclei and suggest the same 2s-1p1/2 shell inversion in 12Be-13Be as in 11Be, consistent with the recent observation of a low-energy s-wave resonance in 13Be by Thoennessen et al. (2001).
