# Nucleonic resonance excitations with linearly polarized photon in $γp\to ωp$

> In this work, an improved quark model approach to the $ω$ meson photo-production with an effective Lagrangian is presented. The {\it t}-channel {\it natural}-parity exchange is taken into account through the Pomeron exchange, while the {\it unnatural}-parity exchange is described by the $π^0$ exchange. With a very limited number of parameters, the available experimental data in the low energy regime can be consistently accounted for. We find that the beam polarization observables show sensitivities to some {\it s}-channel individual resonances in the $SU(6)\otimes O(3)$ quark model symmetry limit. Especially, the two resonances $P_{13}(1720)$ and $F_{15}(1680)$, which belong to the representation $[{\bf 56, ^2 8}, 2, 2, J]$, have dominant contributions over other excited states. Concerning the essential motivation of searching for "missing resonances" in meson photo-production, this approach provides a feasible framework, on which systematic investigations can be done.

## Key claim

The paper presents an improved quark model approach to ω meson photoproduction that includes both natural-parity (Pomeron) and unnatural-parity (π⁰) t-channel exchanges, and finds that beam polarization observables show sensitivity to specific s-channel nucleonic resonances, particularly P₁₃(1720) and F₁₅(1680), in the SU(6)⊗O(3) quark model symmetry limit, though the authors hedge that the final determination of the two quark-model parameters a and b requires more experimental constraints.

## Method

The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for quark-vector-meson coupling in the SU(6)⊗O(3) quark model symmetry limit, with two universal parameters (a for vector coupling, b for tensor coupling) at the quark level. They include t-channel natural-parity exchange via a Pomeron exchange model (Donnachie and Landshoff, 1980s) and unnatural-parity exchange via π⁰ exchange, then compute helicity amplitudes for s-channel and u-channel resonance excitations for all nucleonic resonances with masses below 2 GeV in the harmonic oscillator basis. Parameters are determined sequentially: the Pomeron coupling β₀=1.27 from high-energy data at ~10 GeV, the π⁰ form factor α_π=290 MeV from interference above 2.2 GeV, and the quark-model parameters a=-2.5 and b=-6.5 from fitting cross section angular distributions and total cross sections.

## Key numbers

* β₀ = 1.27: Pomeron coupling strength to a light constituent quark, determined from high-energy ω photoproduction at ~10 GeV
* α_π = 290 MeV: form factor parameter for π⁰ exchange, determined from t-channel interference above E_γ ≥ 2.2 GeV
* a = -2.5, b = -6.5: vector and tensor coupling parameters for the quark-vector-meson effective Lagrangian
* m_q = 330 MeV, α = 385 MeV: constituent quark mass and harmonic oscillator potential strength
* Γ_{ω→e⁺e⁻} = 0.59 keV: ω radiative decay width used to fix the ω coupling strength f_ω
* ε = 0.08, α′ = 0.25 GeV⁻², μ₀ = 1.2 GeV: Pomeron Regge trajectory parameters
* Eight nucleonic resonances contribute for n ≤ 2: P₁₁(1440), S₁₁(1535), D₁₃(1520), P₁₃(1720), F₁₅(1680), P₁₁(1710), P₁₃(1900), F₁₅(2000)
* The natural-parity exchange becomes necessary to account for missing cross sections when E_γ > ~2.2 GeV

## Context

This work extends the quark model approach of Zhao (1998, 1999) for ω photoproduction by adding t-channel natural-parity exchange via Pomeron exchange, which was absent in the earlier treatment that only included π⁰ exchange. The approach provides a framework for searching for "missing resonances" predicted by the non-relativistic constituent quark model (NRCQM) but not observed in πN→πN scattering, as motivated by facilities at JLAB, ELSA, ERSF, and SPring-8. The results enable systematic investigations of resonance excitations in ω photoproduction using beam polarization observables, and the authors note that Y. Oh et al. (recent, no year given) studied resonance excitations in ω photoproduction using quark model predictions from Capstick (1992, 1994) but only included resonances above the ωN threshold, whereas this work finds low-lying resonances like S₁₁(1535) and D₁₃(1520) still have considerable contributions in beam polarization asymmetries.
