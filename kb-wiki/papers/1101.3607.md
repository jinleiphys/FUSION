# Particle-number conserving analysis of rotational bands in 247,249Cm and 249Cf

> The recently observed high-spin rotational bands in odd-$A$ nuclei $^{247, 249}$Cm and $^{249}$Cf [Tandel \textit{et al.}, Phys. Rev. C 82 (2010) 041301R] are investigated by using the cranked shell model (CSM) with the pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method in which the blocking effects are taken into account exactly. The experimental moments of inertia and alignments and their variations with the rotational frequency $ω$ are reproduced very well by the PNC-CSM calculations. By examining the $ω$-dependence of the occupation probability of each cranked Nilsson orbital near the Fermi surface and the contributions of valence orbitals to the angular momentum alignment in each major shell, the level crossing and upbending mechanism in each nucleus is understood clearly.

## Key claim

The cranked shell model with particle-number conserving (PNC) pairing, which treats blocking effects exactly, reproduces the experimental moments of inertia and alignments for the ground-state rotational bands in 247,249Cm and 249Cf, and reveals that the upbending in these nuclei is mainly caused by proton i13/2 orbitals rather than neutron j15/2 orbitals.

## Method

The authors applied the cranked shell model (CSM) with pairing correlations treated by a particle-number conserving (PNC) method, using a cranked many-particle configuration (CMPC) truncation in a Fock space of about 1000 dimensions for both protons and neutrons. The Nilsson parameters (kappa, mu) were readjusted from a recent systematic fit to odd-A nuclei in the A ≈ 250 region, and deformation parameters (ε2, ε4) were set to (0.242, 0.002) for 247Cm and (0.248, 0.008) for 249Cm and 249Cf. The occupation probability of each cranked Nilsson orbital and the contributions of valence orbitals to angular momentum alignment were examined as functions of rotational frequency to identify the upbending mechanism.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters: ε2 = 0.242, ε4 = 0.002 for 247Cm; ε2 = 0.248, ε4 = 0.008 for 249Cm and 249Cf.
- Effective monopole pairing strengths: G0p = 0.50 MeV for protons, G0n = 0.30 MeV for neutrons.
- Effective quadrupole pairing strengths: G2p = 0.04 MeV for protons, G2n = 0.02 MeV for neutrons.
- Upbending frequency ħωc ≈ 0.20 MeV for the ground-state bands in 247Cm and 249Cm; ħωc ≈ 0.25 MeV for the ground-state band in 249Cf.
- Harris reference parameters used for alignment subtraction: J0 = 65 ħ² MeV⁻¹, J1 = 200 ħ⁴ MeV⁻³, taken from Tandel (2010).
- Rotational bands observed up to spins ≈ 28 ħ (from the experimental work of Tandel et al. 2010).

## Context

This work extends the PNC-CSM treatment, previously applied to normally deformed and superdeformed bands in the A ≈ 160, 190, and 250 mass regions (Zeng 1994, Liu 2002, He 2005, Zhang 2009), to the newly observed high-spin rotational bands in odd-A 247,249Cm and 249Cf reported by Tandel et al. (2010). The calculations reproduce the experimental data well and provide a consistent explanation for the absence of j15/2 neutron alignment in this mass region, which had challenged earlier cranking Woods-Saxon calculations (Tandel 2010). The results enable future PNC-CSM studies of other heavy and superheavy nuclei where pairing and blocking effects are critical.
