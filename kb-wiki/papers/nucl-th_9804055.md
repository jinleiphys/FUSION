# Widths of Isobaric Analog Resonances: a microscopic approach

> A self-consistent particle-phonon coupling model is used to investigate the properties of the isobaric analog resonance in $^{208}$Bi. It is shown that quantitative agreement with experimental data for the energy and the width can be obtained if the effects of isospin-breaking nuclear forces are included, in addition to the Coulomb force effects. A connection between microscopic model predictions and doorway state approaches which make use of the isovector monopole resonance, is established via a phenomenological ansatz for the optical potential.

# Widths of Isobaric Analog Resonances: a microscopic approach

## Key claim
Quantitative agreement with experimental data for the energy and total width of the isobaric analog resonance in $^{208}$Bi can be obtained only if the effects of isospin-breaking nuclear forces (charge-symmetry breaking and charge-independence breaking) are included in addition to the Coulomb force; the improvement from including these nuclear forces is about 15% over calculations with Coulomb alone.

## Method
A self-consistent Hartree-Fock (HF) plus random phase approximation (RPA) framework was extended by coupling to continuum configurations (for escape width) and to 1p-1h plus phonon configurations (for spreading width), using Skyrme interactions SIII and SGII. The isospin-breaking nuclear forces were parametrized as short-range Skyrme-like CSB and CIB interactions from Sagawa (1996), and the calculations were performed for the specific case of $^{208}$Bi. The model space included 96 unoccupied proton single-particle states and all isoscalar vibrations of multipolarity 2$^+$, 3$^-$, and 4$^+$ up to 20 MeV exhausting more than 1% of the energy-weighted sum rule.

## Key numbers
- Total width of the IAR in $^{208}$Bi calculated with force SIII including CSB-CIB forces: 230 keV (experimental value), an improvement of about 15% over the calculation without CSB-CIB forces.
- Spreading width $\Gamma^\downarrow$ increases by about 20 keV when the averaging parameter $\Delta$ is changed from 100 keV to 200 keV, and by another 20 keV when changed from 200 keV to 400 keV; the uncertainty on $\Gamma^\downarrow$ from the choice of $\Delta$ is about $\pm$ 20 keV.
- Without any isospin-breaking force (calculation I), the spurious spreading width obtained with force SGII was only 4 keV; with force SIII it was 24 keV.
- Unperturbed proton particle-neutron hole 0$^+$ configuration energies without Coulomb: between -7.3 MeV and -8.3 MeV for SGII, between -8.6 MeV and -10 MeV for SIII.
- Unperturbed configuration energies with Coulomb included: between 11 and 11.8 MeV for SGII, between 8.8 and 10.3 MeV for SIII.

## Context
This work extends earlier particle-phonon coupling calculations by Colo (1994) which included only Coulomb isospin breaking and showed discrepancies with experiment. It also connects to the second Tamm-Dancoff approximation (TDA) calculation by Adachi (1990) for the IAR in $^{208}$Bi. The paper establishes a formal connection between microscopic particle-phonon coupling models and the isovector monopole resonance (IVMR) doorway state approaches of Mekjian (1970) and Suzuki (1996), showing that the spreading width from the microscopic model can be related to the IVMR doorway result via a phenomenological ansatz for the isospin dependence of the nucleon optical potential.
