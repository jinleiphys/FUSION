# Bubble nuclei with shape coexistence in even-even isotopes of Hf to Hg

> The shape of a nucleus is one of fundamental nuclear properties. We perform a systematic investigation of bubble nuclei that also exhibit shape coexistence in Hf, W, Os, Pt and Hg even-even isotopes using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum. For a systematic study, we first consider nuclear bubble structures and shape coexistence separately. We confirm that deformations and pairing correlations hinder bubble structures by comparing our results with those from relativistic continuum Hartree-Bogoliubov theory that assumes spherical symmetry in nuclei. We then predict candidate isotopes with both bubble structure and shape coexistence. We observe that the depletion fraction factor that characterizes bubble structure is mostly smaller in oblate deformation than in prolate, while some isotopes such as $^{206}$Os have bubble structures both in oblate and prolate deformations. We compare the proton single-particle energy levels for the candidates of shape coexistence both with only prolate bubble structure and with prolate and oblate bubble structures.

## Key claim
The paper claims that certain even-even isotopes in the Hf-to-Hg region can exhibit both bubble structure (central density depletion) and shape coexistence simultaneously, but with the hedge that the bubble parameter is generally smaller in oblate deformation than in prolate, and that both deformation and pairing correlations hinder bubble formation.

## Method
The authors performed systematic calculations using the deformed relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov theory in continuum (DRHBc) with the PC-PK1 density functional and a zero-range pairing force. They first computed bubble parameters for spherical and deformed nuclei, then identified shape-coexisting isotopes by evaluating potential energy curves from constrained calculations, and finally cross-matched candidates that satisfy both criteria.

## Key numbers
- Bubble parameter criterion set at \( {\cal B}_p = 20\% \) for defining a bubble nucleus
- Top bubble candidates: \( ^{256}\text{Hf} \) with \( {\cal B}_p^\star = 29.2\% \) (DRHBc) vs 27.4% (RCHB); \( ^{258}\text{W} \) with 28.3% vs 26.6%; \( ^{260}\text{Os} \) with 27.2% vs 25.5%
- Deformed bubble candidate \( ^{206}\text{Os} \) has \( {\cal B}_p^\star = 24.1\% \) in prolate and 23.5% in oblate, with energy difference \( |\Delta E| = 0.006 \) MeV
- Shape coexistence candidates with \( |\Delta E| \leq 0.5 \) MeV include \( ^{202}\text{Hf} \) (prolate \( {\cal B}_p^\star = 23.7\% \), oblate 22.4%, \( |\Delta E| = 0.214 \) MeV) and \( ^{196}\text{Os} \) (prolate 23.6%, oblate 11.0%, \( |\Delta E| = 0.096 \) MeV)
- Pairing strength in DRHBc is \( -325.0 \) MeV fm\(^3\) vs \( -342.5 \) MeV fm\(^3\) in RCHB; increasing DRHBc pairing to the RCHB value shifted \( ^{256}\text{Hf} \) bubble parameter from 29.2% to 27.2%

## Context
This work extends prior studies of bubble nuclei (e.g., Khan (2007), Grasso (2009), Saxena (2018, 2019)) and shape coexistence (e.g., Wood (1992), Gade (2016)) by systematically searching for nuclei that exhibit both phenomena simultaneously. The DRHBc framework had previously been applied to neutron drip line changes (In 2020), shape decoupling (Sun 2018, 2020, 2021), and bound nuclei beyond the drip line (Pan 2021, He 2021, Zhang 2021). The results enable future experimental searches at rare isotope facilities for nuclei where enhanced \( \pi^-/\pi^+ \) ratios from bubble structure could be modulated by shape coexistence effects.
