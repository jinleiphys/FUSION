# Nuclear exclusive vector meson photoproduction

> We calculate the nuclear cross section for vector meson exclusive photoproduction within the QCD color dipole picture and in the Regge approach. For the former approach, we have considered the phenomenological saturation model, whereas for the latter we use a model based on the dipole Pomeron framework. Theoretical estimates for scattering on both light and heavy nuclei are given for a large range on energy.

## Key claim

The paper calculates nuclear cross sections for exclusive vector meson photoproduction using two distinct theoretical approaches that do not violate unitarity at high energies, in contrast to previous extrapolations of experimental fits from DESY-HERA that would violate unitarity at sufficiently high energies.

## Method

The authors compute cross sections for the process γA → VA (V = ρ, ω, φ, J/Ψ) using two approaches: (1) the QCD color dipole picture with a phenomenological saturation model extended to nuclei via the Glauber-Gribov formalism, using DGKP meson wavefunctions; and (2) a Regge approach based on the dipole Pomeron framework, extended to nuclei using vector meson dominance and the Glauber-Gribov formalism. Both approaches incorporate unitarity corrections and are applied to light and heavy nuclei over a large energy range.

## Key numbers

- Saturation model parameters from fit to small-x HERA data: σ₀ = 23.03 mb (3-flavor) or 29.12 mb (4-flavor), λ = 0.288 (3-flavor) or 0.277 (4-flavor), x₀ = 3.04 × 10⁻⁴ (3-flavor) or 0.41 × 10⁻⁴ (4-flavor)
- Quark masses used: m_{u,d,s} = 0.14 GeV, m_c = 1.5 GeV; for φ meson, m_s = m_{u,d} + 0.15 GeV
- Slope parameters B_V for proton target: ρ (9.00 GeV⁻²), ω (10.14 GeV⁻²), φ (8.92 GeV⁻²), J/Ψ (4.57 GeV⁻²)
- Nuclear radius parameterization: R_A = 1.2 A^{1/3} fm, nuclear density ρ₀ = 0.16 fm⁻³
- Real part correction in photoproduction: about 3% for light mesons, reaching 13% for J/Ψ at high energies
- Dipole Pomeron trajectory slope: α′_Pom(0) = 0.25 GeV⁻²

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of vector meson production on nuclei by Klein (2003) that were based on extrapolation of DESY-HERA experimental fits for the proton case, by using unitarized cross sections from the start. The color dipole approach builds on the saturation model of Golec-Biernat and Wusthoff (1998) and its nuclear extension by Armesto (2002), while the Regge approach follows the dipole Pomeron framework of Martynov and Predazzi (2003). The results enable discrimination between these theoretical approaches using future data from planned lepton-nucleus colliders (eRHIC and HERA-A) and ultraperipheral heavy-ion collisions at LHC.
