# Bremsstrahlung from an Equilibrating Quark-Gluon Plasma

> The photon production rate from a chemically equilibrating quark-gluon plasma likely to be produced at RHIC (BNL) and LHC (CERN) energies is estimated taking into account bremsstrahlung. The plasma is assumed to be in local thermal equilibrium, but with a phase space distribution that deviates from the Fermi or Bose distribution by space-time dependent factors (fugacities). The photon spectrum is obtained by integrating the photon rate over the space-time history of the plasma, adopting a boost invariant cylindrically symmetric transverse expansion of the system with different nuclear profile functions. Initial conditions obtained from a self-screened parton cascade calculation and, for comparison, from the HIJING model are used. Compared to an equilibrated plasma at the same initial energy density, taken from the self-screened parton cascade, a moderate suppression of the photon yield by a factor of one to five depending on the collision energy and the photon momentum is observed. The individual contributions to the photon production, however, are completely different in the both scenarios.

# Bremsstrahlung from an Equilibrating Quark-Gluon Plasma

## Key claim

The photon yield from a chemically equilibrating quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC energies, including bremsstrahlung contributions, is suppressed by a factor of one to five compared to a fully equilibrated plasma at the same initial energy density, but the composition of the photon spectrum by individual processes (bremsstrahlung, annihilation with scattering, Compton scattering, and quark-antiquark annihilation) is completely different between the two scenarios.

## Method

The authors estimated non-equilibrium photon production rates by assigning fugacity factors (describing chemical undersaturation) to the external partons in the matrix elements for Compton scattering, quark-antiquark annihilation, bremsstrahlung, and annihilation with scattering, using the equilibrium rates from Aurenche et al. (1998) as a starting point. These rates were then convolved with a boost-invariant, cylindrically symmetric hydrodynamical model including transverse expansion, using initial conditions for temperature and fugacities from the self-screened parton cascade (SSPC) model and the HIJING model, with two different nuclear profile functions (Fermi-like and wounded-nucleon).

## Key numbers

- For RHIC with SSPC initial conditions, the total photon yield from the equilibrating plasma is suppressed by a factor of five at p_T = 1 GeV and almost identical at p_T = 5 GeV compared to the equilibrated plasma at the same initial energy density (epsilon_i = 61.4 GeV/fm^3).
- For LHC with SSPC initial conditions, the non-equilibrium rate is suppressed by about a factor of three for all photon momenta compared to equilibrium at the same initial energy density (T_i^eq = 0.695 GeV).
- The SSPC initial conditions at RHIC are: tau_i = 0.25 fm/c, T_i = 0.668 GeV, lambda_g = 0.34, lambda_q = 0.064, epsilon_i = 61.4 GeV/fm^3.
- The SSPC initial conditions at LHC are: tau_i = 0.25 fm/c, T_i = 1.02 GeV, lambda_g = 0.43, lambda_q = 0.082, epsilon_i = 425 GeV/fm^3.
- For HIJING-I at RHIC: tau_i = 0.7 fm/c, T_i = 0.55 GeV, lambda_g = 0.05, lambda_q = 0.008, epsilon_i = 4.0 GeV/fm^3.
- For HIJING-II at RHIC: tau_i = 0.7 fm/c, T_i = 0.40 GeV, lambda_g = 0.53, lambda_q = 0.083, epsilon_i = 11.7 GeV/fm^3.
- For HIJING-I at LHC: tau_i = 0.5 fm/c, T_i = 0.82 GeV, lambda_g = 0.124, lambda_q = 0.02, epsilon_i = 48.6 GeV/fm^3.
- For HIJING-II at LHC: tau_i = 0.5 fm/c, T_i = 0.72 GeV, lambda_g = 0.761, lambda_q = 0.118, epsilon_i = 176 GeV/fm^3.
- The photon yield from the Fermi-like profile is less than a factor of two higher than from the wounded-nucleon profile.
- The thermal photon yield at RHIC dominates over prompt photons for p_T less than or equal to 4.5 GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of photon production from a chemically equilibrating QGP by Traxler and Thoma (1996) and Srivastava, Mustafa, and Muller (1997), which did not include bremsstrahlung, by incorporating the enhanced bremsstrahlung and annihilation-with-scattering rates computed by Aurenche et al. (1998) for equilibrium. It also builds on the hydrodynamical treatment of chemical equilibration using rate equations from Biro et al. (1993) and Srivastava et al. (1997). The results enable more realistic predictions for thermal photon spectra at RHIC and LHC, showing that while the total yield is only moderately suppressed relative to equilibrium, the dominance of bremsstrahlung over annihilation-with-scattering in the non-equilibrium case at RHIC is a qualitatively new feature that could be tested experimentally.
