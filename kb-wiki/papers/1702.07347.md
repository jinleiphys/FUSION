# Dispersion relation for hadronic light-by-light scattering: two-pion contributions

> In this third paper of a series dedicated to a dispersive treatment of the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) tensor, we derive a partial-wave formulation for two-pion intermediate states in the HLbL contribution to the anomalous magnetic moment of the muon $(g-2)_Ој$, including a detailed discussion of the unitarity relation for arbitrary partial waves. We show that obtaining a final expression free from unphysical helicity partial waves is a subtle issue, which we thoroughly clarify. As a by-product, we obtain a set of sum rules that could be used to constrain future calculations of $Оі^*Оі^*\toПЂПЂ$. We validate the formalism extensively using the pion-box contribution, defined by two-pion intermediate states with a pion-pole left-hand cut, and demonstrate how the full known result is reproduced when resumming the partial waves. Using dispersive fits to high-statistics data for the pion vector form factor, we provide an evaluation of the full pion box, $a_Ој^{ПЂ\text{-box}}=-15.9(2)\times 10^{-11}$. As an application of the partial-wave formalism, we present a first calculation of $ПЂПЂ$-rescattering effects in HLbL scattering, with $Оі^*Оі^*\toПЂПЂ$ helicity partial waves constructed dispersively using $ПЂПЂ$ phase shifts derived from the inverse-amplitude method. In this way, the isospin-$0$ part of our calculation can be interpreted as the contribution of the $f_0(500)$ to HLbL scattering in $(g-2)_Ој$. We argue that the contribution due to charged-pion rescattering implements corrections related to the corresponding pion polarizability and show that these are moderate. Our final result for the sum of pion-box contribution and its $S$-wave rescattering corrections reads $a_Ој^{ПЂ\text{-box}} + a_{Ој,J=0}^{ПЂПЂ,ПЂ\text{-pole LHC}}=-24(1)\times 10^{-11}$.

## Key claim

The paper derives a partial-wave formulation for two-pion intermediate states in the hadronic light-by-light (HLbL) contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, showing that obtaining a final expression free from unphysical helicity partial waves is a subtle issue that requires careful basis choice, and that the sum of the pion-box contribution and its S-wave rescattering corrections yields a final result of \(-24(1)\times 10^{-11}\).

## Method

The authors developed a dispersive partial-wave formalism for the HLbL tensor, focusing on two-pion intermediate states and using unitarity relations for arbitrary partial waves. They validated the formalism by reproducing the full known pion-box contribution through resummation of partial waves, using dispersive fits to high-statistics data for the pion vector form factor. For the rescattering corrections, they constructed \(\gamma^*\gamma^*\to\pi\pi\) helicity partial waves dispersively using \(\pi\pi\) phase shifts derived from the inverse-amplitude method.

## Key numbers

- Full pion-box contribution: \(a_\mu^{\pi\text{-box}} = -15.9(2) \times 10^{-11}\)
- Sum of pion-box and S-wave rescattering corrections: \(a_\mu^{\pi\text{-box}} + a_{\mu,J=0}^{\pi\pi,\pi\text{-pole LHC}} = -24(1) \times 10^{-11}\)
- Phenomenological value of the low-energy constant combination: \(\bar l_6 - \bar l_5 = 3.0(0.3)\)
- Resonance saturation estimate: \(\bar l_6 - \bar l_5|_\text{sat} = 3.4\)
- One-loop chiral perturbation theory sum rule for \(\bar l_6 - \bar l_5\) yields values in the range \(2.7\ldots 2.9\)

## Context

This work is the third in a series by Colangelo, Hoferichter, Procura, and Stoffer on a dispersive treatment of the HLbL tensor for the muon \((g-2)_\mu\). It extends the formalism of Colangelo (2015) by providing a partial-wave formulation for two-pion intermediate states and clarifies the subtle issue of unphysical helicity partial waves. The paper enables future calculations of \(\pi\pi\)-rescattering effects in HLbL scattering and provides sum rules that could constrain future calculations of \(\gamma^*\gamma^*\to\pi\pi\). The forward-scattering sum rules are compared to those derived by Pascalutsa (2012), with the authors noting that their assumptions about asymptotic behavior are more restrictive for some helicity amplitudes.
