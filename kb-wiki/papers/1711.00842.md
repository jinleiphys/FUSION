# Correlation between heavy flavour production and multiplicity in pp and p-Pb collisions at high energy in the multi-pomeron exchange model

> The multiplicity dependence of heavy flavour production in pp-collisions at LHC energies is studied in the framework of the multi-pomeron exchange model. The model is introducing the string-string interaction collectivity effects in pp collisions, which modifies multiplicity and transverse momenta, leading to the non-trivial mean pt vs. multiplicity (<pt>_Nch - Nch) correlation. The string collectivity strength parameter is fixed by experimental data on multiplicity and transverse momentum correlation in a wide energy range (from ISR to LHC). The particles discrimination is implemented according to Schwinger mechanism taking into account the strong decays of hadron resonances. We demonstrate, that the faster-than-linear growth of the open charm production with the event charged particle multiplicity, observed in experimental pp high energy collisions, can be explained by the modification of the string tension due to the increasing overlap and interaction of quark-gluon strings. The model is extended for p-A interactions and the calculations for p-Pb collisions are performed.

## Key claim

The faster-than-linear growth of open charm production with event charged particle multiplicity in pp collisions can be explained by modification of string tension due to increasing overlap and interaction of quark-gluon strings, without introducing additional parameters for particle differentiation beyond those fixed by data on transverse momentum versus multiplicity correlations.

## Method

The authors use the Extended Multi-Pomeron Exchange Model (EPEM) where multiple pomeron exchanges in a single pp collision correspond to pairs of quark-gluon strings. Particle production probabilities are computed via a modified Schwinger mechanism where the effective string tension increases as n^beta t (with n the number of pomerons and beta a collectivity parameter), and the final hadron spectrum is corrected for strong cascade decays of resonances using a branching matrix from the THERMINATOR 2 Monte Carlo generator. For p-Pb collisions, the model convolves the pp pomeron distribution with the distribution of participating nucleons calculated in the Glauber model.

## Key numbers

- String tension parameter t = 0.566 GeV^2 (fixed constant)
- Collectivity parameter beta described by beta = 1.16 (1 - log(sqrt(s)/(1 GeV)) - 2.52)^0.19, with no extra tuning for particle differentiation
- Pomeron parameters: Delta = 0.139, alpha' = 0.21 GeV^{-2}, gamma = 1.77 GeV^{-2}, R_0^2 = 3.18 GeV^{-2}, C = 1.5
- Model calculations performed for pp and p-Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 7 TeV
- The model reproduces faster-than-linear growth of D meson yield with multiplicity in pp collisions, with the enhancement even stronger than in ALICE experimental data

## Context

This work extends the multi-pomeron exchange model with string collectivity effects previously applied to strange hadron production (Feofilov et al., 2016) to open charm production, following the unexpected faster-than-linear D meson yield versus multiplicity observed by the ALICE Collaboration (2015). The approach is related to the string percolation model of Ferreiro (2015) and the EPOS 3 model with hydrodynamical evolution (Werner, 2016), but uses a simpler parameterization where the collectivity parameter beta is fixed from mean pt versus multiplicity correlations across a wide energy range from ISR to LHC. The model enables predictions for p-Pb collisions that can be compared with future experimental data.
