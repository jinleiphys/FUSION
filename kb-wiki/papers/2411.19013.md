# Elastic properties of nuclear pasta in neutron-star crusts

> Based on the relativistic mean field (RMF) model with Thomas-Fermi approximation, we investigate the elastic properties of neutron star matter. The elastic constants are estimated by introducing deformations on the nuclear pasta structures in $β$-equilibrium, where various crystalline configurations are considered in a fully three-dimensional geometry without the Wigner-Seitz approximation. Two scenarios with different symmetry energy slope ($L = 41.34$ and 89.39 MeV) are examined, where the the elastic constants can vary by ten times. By fitting to the numerical results, we improve the analytic formulae for the elastic properties of nuclear pasta by introducing damping factors.

# Elastic properties of nuclear pasta in neutron-star crusts

## Key claim

The elastic constants of nuclear pasta structures in neutron-star crusts can vary by up to a factor of ten depending on the symmetry energy slope (L = 41.34 vs 89.39 MeV), and the authors introduce damping factors to improve existing analytic formulae, but only for the specific density ranges where the liquid-gas interface becomes less evident and the simple Coulomb crystal picture breaks down.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with Thomas-Fermi approximation in a fully three-dimensional geometry without the Wigner-Seitz approximation, solving the coupled Klein-Gordon and Dirac equations iteratively in a 3D periodic cell. They apply volume-preserving deformations to nuclear pasta structures (droplets in BCC and FCC lattices, rods/tubes in honeycomb lattices, and slabs) under beta-equilibrium conditions, then extract elastic constants from the variation of energy density as a function of deformation. Two parameter sets with different symmetry energy slopes (L = 89.39 MeV for Set 0 and L = 41.34 MeV for Set 1) are examined.

## Key numbers

- Symmetry energy slope values examined: L = 41.34 MeV (Set 1) and L = 89.39 MeV (Set 0)
- Nuclear saturation density: n0 = 0.153 fm^-3
- Binding energy at saturation: B(n0) = -16.3 MeV
- Incompressibility: K(n0) = 240 MeV
- Effective nucleon mass at saturation: m_N^*(n0) = 0.78 m_N
- Symmetry energy at saturation: S(n0) = 32.46 MeV (Set 0) and 31.85 MeV (Set 1)
- Bain deformation parameter for BCC to FCC transition: epsilon_F = 2 - 2^(5/6) ≈ 0.2182
- Elastic constants for droplet phase in BCC lattice: c11 - c12 = 0.04908 mu_0', c44 = 0.1705 mu_0' / 10^(10u^8)
- Elastic constants for droplet phase in FCC lattice: c11 - c12 = 0.03512 mu_0', c44 = 0.1740 mu_0' / 10^(9u^8)
- For rods: c11 + c12 = 3E_C / 10^(3u^8), and c44 = E_C * 10^(2.1(u^2 - 0.3) - 3u^8)
- For slabs: c11 = 6E_C * 10^(0.55u - 10u^8 - 0.19)
- Elastic constants of droplet phase with L = 41.34 MeV are almost ten times larger than those with L = 89.39 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nuclear pasta structures (Xia 2021, Xia 2022, Xia 2023) by calculating elastic properties, and improves upon the analytic formulae of Pethick (1998, 2020) for rod and slab phases, as well as the Monte Carlo results of Ogata (1990) for BCC and FCC Coulomb crystals. The elastic constants for droplets in BCC and FCC lattices were previously estimated by Ogata (1990) using Eq. (El_MC) with mu_0 = alpha n_d Z^2 / R_W, but the authors find these overestimate the elastic constants at higher densities due to dripped neutrons, charge screening, and deviations from spherical droplet shapes. The improved formulae with damping factors enable more realistic modeling of neutron-star crust elasticity for interpreting quasi-periodic oscillations in magnetar flares, continuous gravitational wave emission from rotating neutron stars, and pulsar glitch mechanisms.
