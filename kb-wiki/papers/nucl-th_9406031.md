# Functional approach to the electromagnetic response function: the Longitudinal Channel

> In this paper we address the (charge) longitudinal electromagnetic response for a homogeneous system of nucleons interacting via meson exchanges in the functional framework. This approach warrants consistency if the calculation is carried on order-by-order in the mesonic loop expansion with RPA-dressed mesonic propagators. At the 1-loop order and considering pion, rho and omega exchanges we obtain a quenching of the response, in line with the experimental results.

# Functional approach to the electromagnetic response function: the Longitudinal Channel

## Key claim
The longitudinal electromagnetic response of nuclear matter is quenched at the 1-loop order when including π, ρ, and ω meson exchanges with RPA-dressed propagators, bringing theory closer to experimental data; however, the calculation neglects meson exchange currents (MEC) in the longitudinal channel, and the agreement depends on a specific parametrization of short-range correlations that introduces a free parameter for the ρ-meson correlation cutoff.

## Method
The authors use a functional integral approach with a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation to project the fermionic action onto a bosonic Hilbert space, then apply the Stationary Phase Approximation (SPA) to obtain a loop expansion with RPA-dressed meson propagators as the mean field. The one-loop corrections are evaluated analytically in momentum space using the functions Π⁽³⁾ and Π⁽⁴⁾ for fermionic loops with 3 or 4 external legs, including diagrams with nucleon and Δ-resonance intermediate states, for a non-relativistic system with π, ρ, and ω exchanges and short-range correlations parametrized via momentum-dependent Landau parameters g′_L(q) and g′_T(q).

## Key numbers
- The Landau limit g′₀ is set to approximately 0.65 in both isovector spin-longitudinal and spin-transverse channels, consistent with model-independent analyses.
- The πNN coupling constant is f²_πNN/4π = 0.08; the πNΔ coupling constant is f²_πNΔ/4π = 0.32.
- The ρ-meson coupling constant C_ρ ≈ 2.3, taken from Höhler and Pietarinen (1975); the ω-meson coupling constant C_ω = 1.5, taken from the Bonn potential analysis of Machleidt, Holinde, and Elster (1987).
- Form factor cutoffs are Λ_π = 1.3 GeV/c, Λ_ρ = 2.5 GeV/c, and Λ_ω = 1.5 GeV/c.
- The longitudinal correlation cutoff q_{cL} is chosen in the range 0.8–0.9 GeV/c; the transverse correlation cutoff q_{cT} is treated as a free parameter between approximately 1 GeV/c and 2 GeV/c.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous functional approach to the π-N system (Alberico, Cenni, Molinari, and Saracco, 1987) by including ρ and ω mesons and Δ-resonance excitations, and by applying a non-relativistic approximation. The paper addresses the longstanding discrepancy where the Free Fermi Gas model overestimates the longitudinal response by nearly a factor of two compared to experimental data from Saclay and other groups (e.g., Barreau et al., 1983; Meziani et al., 1984, 1985; Blatchley et al., 1986), while the transverse response is reasonably reproduced. The results enable future calculations of the transverse response and nuclear sum rules in the same framework, and provide a foundation for investigating whether meson exchange currents or relativistic effects can further improve agreement with data.
