# Effects of nuclear symmetry energy and in-medium NN cross section in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies below the pion production threshold

> Based on the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck(IBUU04) transport model, we explored effects of in-medium $NN$ elastic scattering cross section and nuclear symmetry energy on the sub-threshold pion production in $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ reaction. We find that with decrease of incident beam energy, effects of in-medium $NN$ elastic scattering cross section on the $π^{-}/π^{+}$ ratio are larger than that of the symmetry energy although the latter may be also larger. While keeping the effect of symmetry energy, the double ratio of $π^{-}/π^{+}$ from neutron-rich and neutron-poor reaction systems (with the same mass number of system) $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ and $^{128}Pm+^{128}Pm$ almost fully cancels out the effects of in-medium $NN$ elastic scattering cross section.

# Effects of nuclear symmetry energy and in-medium NN cross section in heavy-ion collisions at beam energies below the pion production threshold

## Key claim
At beam energies below the pion production threshold, the in-medium NN elastic scattering cross section has a larger effect on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio than the nuclear symmetry energy, but this contamination can be almost fully cancelled by taking the double ratio of π⁻/π⁺ from two reaction systems that have the same total baryon number but different isospin asymmetry (neutron-rich vs. neutron-poor).

## Method
The authors used the isospin-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU04) transport model to simulate central collisions of ¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn at beam energies from 100 to 300 MeV/nucleon. They compared calculations using either the free-space NN elastic cross section or an in-medium NN elastic cross section derived from a scaling model based on nucleon effective masses, while varying the symmetry energy via the parameter x in the single-nucleon potential. Pion production and absorption were modeled through Δ and N* resonance channels with detailed balance.

## Key numbers
- Maximal baryon densities reached in central ¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn collisions are about 1.5 to 2 times saturation density at beam energies of 100, 200, and 300 MeV/nucleon.
- At 100 MeV/nucleon, effects of in-medium NN elastic cross section on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio can reach about 40%.
- At 100 MeV/nucleon, effects of in-medium NN cross section on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio are about 2 times larger than effects of the symmetry energy.
- At 200 MeV/nucleon, effects of in-medium NN cross section on the π⁻/π⁺ ratio are almost equal to effects of the symmetry energy.
- For the double ratio from ¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn and ¹⁰⁰Sn + ¹⁰⁰Sn at 100 MeV/nucleon, effects of the in-medium NN cross section are about 30% while effects of the symmetry energy are about 42%.
- For the single π⁻/π⁺ ratio at 100 MeV/nucleon, effects of the in-medium NN cross section are about 50% while effects of the symmetry energy are about 25%.

## Context
This work extends earlier studies by Yong (2006) and Zhang (2012) on using the π⁻/π⁺ ratio as a probe of the high-density symmetry energy, but focuses on the lower beam energy regime where in-medium effects become competitive. The authors show that the double-ratio technique proposed by Yong (2006) using same-isotope systems (e.g., ¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn vs. ¹⁰⁰Sn + ¹⁰⁰Sn) does not fully cancel in-medium effects, but a new double ratio using systems with the same total baryon number (¹³²Sn + ¹²⁴Sn vs. ¹²⁸Pm + ¹²⁸Pm) achieves near-complete cancellation. This provides a cleaner experimental observable for constraining the high-density symmetry energy at future radioactive beam facilities such as FRIB and FAIR.
