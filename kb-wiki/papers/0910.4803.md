# Why is the nuclear symmetry energy so uncertain at supra-saturation densities?

> Within the interacting Fermi gas model for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, effects of the in-medium three-body interaction and the two-body short-range tensor force due to the $ρ$ meson exchange as well as the short-range nucleon correlation on the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy are demonstrated respectively in a transparent way. Possible physics origins of the extremely uncertain nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities are discussed.

## Key claim

Within the interacting Fermi gas model, the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy is determined by the competition between the in-medium isosinglet and isotriplet nucleon-nucleon interactions; the strength of the spin-isospin dependence of the three-body force (parameter \(x_0\)) and the in-medium \(\rho\) meson mass in the short-range tensor force are the key parameters controlling this behavior, but the paper does not claim to fully resolve the uncertainty.

## Method

The authors used the interacting Fermi gas model for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter to derive an analytical expression for the nuclear symmetry energy \(E_{sym}(\rho)\) in terms of the isospin-dependent strong nucleon-nucleon interaction. They separately examined the effects of the spin-isospin dependent three-body force (using the Gogny force parameterization with variable \(x_0\) and \(\alpha\) parameters) and the in-medium short-range tensor force (using the Brown-Rho Scaling for the \(\rho\) meson mass and a two-step tensor correlation function with a healing distance \(r_c\)). The symmetry energy was calculated for various values of the three-body force parameters \(x_0\) and \(\alpha\), the Brown-Rho Scaling parameter \(\alpha_{BR}\), and the correlation parameter \(\eta\).

## Key numbers

* The kinetic contribution to the symmetry energy is \(E_{sym}^{kin} = \frac{\hbar^2}{6m} \left(\frac{3\pi^2}{2}\right)^{\frac{2}{3}} \rho^{\frac{2}{3}}\).
* The isoscalar potential used is the GBD parameterization: \(U_{GBD}(\rho,k) = \frac{-75\rho / \rho_0}{1+(k/(\Lambda k_F))^2}\) with \(\Lambda = 1.5\).
* In the 2003 survey of 87 Skyrme interactions by Stone et al., the \(x_0\) parameter ranges between \(-1.56\) and \(1.92\).
* The Brown-Rho Scaling for the in-medium \(\rho\) meson mass is \(m_{\rho}^{\star}/m_{\rho} = 1 - \alpha_{BR} \cdot \rho/\rho_0\), with values \(\alpha_{BR} = 0, 0.05, 0.10, 0.15, 0.20\) used in calculations.
* A recent study indicates that a value of \(\alpha_{BR} \approx 0.15\) is required to reproduce the measured lifetime of \(^{14}C\) (Holt 2008).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the nuclear symmetry energy at supra-saturation densities, which has been identified as a key issue in both nuclear physics and astrophysics (Li 2008, Steiner 2005). The paper builds on the interacting Fermi gas model (Prakash 1988) and the Lane potential (Lane 1962), and it directly addresses the wide diversity of predictions from many-body theories (Stone 2003). The findings are expected to help experimentalists decide what experiments to perform at facilities such as CSR/China, GSI/Germany, MSU/USA, and RIKEN/Japan, and to guide future work that includes both two- and three-body tensor forces simultaneously.
