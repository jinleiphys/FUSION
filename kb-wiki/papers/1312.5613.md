# The evaporation residue in the fission state of Barium nuclei within relativistic mean-field theory

> The evaporation residue of Barium isotopes are investigated in a microscopic study using relativistic mean field theory. The investigation includes the isotopes of Barium from the valley of stability to exotic proton-rich region. The ground as well as neck configurations for these nuclei are generated from their total nucleonic density distributions of the corresponding state. We have estimated the constituents (number of nucleons) in the elongated neck region of the fission state. We found the $α$-particle as the constituent of neck of Ba-isotopes, referred to as the evaporated residue in heavy-ion reaction studies. A strong correlation between the neutron and proton is observed throughout the isotopic chain.

# The evaporation residue in the fission state of Barium nuclei within relativistic mean-field theory

## Key claim
The neck region of the fission state for Ba isotopes (112-134) is composed of an alpha particle (⁴He), which the authors identify as the evaporation residue in heavy-ion reaction studies. This claim is hedged by the fact that the neck ranges are fixed by a graphical method guided by the eye, with an estimated 1-2% uncertainty in the results.

## Method
The authors used an axially deformed relativistic mean-field (RMF) formalism with the NL3 parameter set to calculate ground-state and fission-state properties of ¹¹²⁻¹³⁴Ba isotopes. The total nucleonic density distributions were generated in the ρ-z plane, and the neck region was identified from contour plots of the density. The number of protons and neutrons in the neck was then estimated by integrating the density over the visually determined spatial ranges of the neck.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies for ¹¹²⁻¹³⁴Ba range from 895.33 MeV (¹¹²Ba) to 1127.69 MeV (¹³⁴Ba) in the ground state, and from 857.12 MeV to 1049.79 MeV in the fission state (RMF/NL3).
- Quadrupole deformation parameter β₂ for the fission state ranges from 5.561 (¹¹⁴Ba) to 5.585 (¹³⁰Ba).
- The neck region for all ¹¹²⁻¹³⁴Ba isotopes contains approximately 2.04-2.11 protons and 1.91-2.95 neutrons, yielding a ⁴He nucleus in every case.
- Pairing energy decreases linearly from 21.43 MeV (¹¹²Ba) to 14.38 MeV (¹³⁴Ba) in the ground state, a relative change of approximately 30% across the isotopic chain.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous RMF studies of cluster formation in Ba isotopes (Bhuyan 2011) and addresses the composition of the neck region in fission states, which had not been quantitatively determined before. The study is motivated by experimental measurements of fission fragment charge distributions for ¹¹⁸,¹²²Ba* compound systems (Bonnet 2008, Ademard 2011), where only the total evaporation residue cross-section was measured without identifying the constituents. The results enable future experimental identification of the specific particles (Z or A value) contributing to the evaporation residue cross-section in heavy-ion reactions forming Ba compound nuclei.
