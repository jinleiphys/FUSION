# The application of the Quark-Hadron Chiral Parity-Doublet Model to neutron star matter

> The Quark-Hadron Chiral Parity-Doublet model (Q$χ$P) is applied to calculate compact star properties in the presence of a deconfinement phase transition. Within this model, a consistent description of nuclear matter properties, chiral symmetry restoration, and a transition from hadronic to quark and gluonic degrees of freedom is possible within one unified approach. We find that the equation of state obtained is consistent with recent perturbative quantum chromodynamics (QCD) results and is able to accommodate observational constraints of massive and small neutron stars. Furthermore, we show that important features of the equation of state, such as the symmetry energy and its slope, are well within their observational constraints.

## Key claim

The Quark-Hadron Chiral Parity-Doublet model (QχP) can simultaneously describe nuclear matter properties, chiral symmetry restoration, and a deconfinement phase transition, producing hybrid stars with masses up to 1.98 solar masses and small radii, but the agreement with perturbative QCD pressure worsens at very high chemical potentials because the quark bare mass parameter is kept constant rather than allowed to vanish with the dilaton field.

## Method

The authors apply the SU(3) parity-doublet model, which includes an explicit chiral-symmetry-preserving mass term for baryons, extended to include quark degrees of freedom via a Polyakov-loop potential and an excluded-volume prescription that smoothly suppresses hadrons as deconfinement proceeds. The equation of state is computed in mean-field approximation for charge-neutral, beta-equilibrated matter at zero temperature, and the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations are solved to obtain stellar mass-radius relations, including rotational corrections via monopole and quadrupole metric perturbations.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density: ρ₀ = 0.142 fm⁻³
- Nuclear incompressibility: κ = 267 MeV
- Symmetry energy at saturation: S_v = 30.02 MeV
- Slope parameter of symmetry energy: L = 56.86 MeV
- Maximum neutron star mass: 1.98 M⊙ (solar masses)
- Radius of maximum-mass star: 10.25 km
- Radius of canonical 1.4 M⊙ star: 11.10 km
- Kepler frequency of isolated rotating star: 1606 Hz
- Mass increase of maximally rotating star (fixed baryon number) relative to non-rotating: about 5%
- Quark fraction in most massive star: 35% of baryon mass
- Pseudo-critical temperature for chiral transition at zero chemical potential: T_c = 155 ± 10 MeV (cited from lattice QCD)

## Context

This work extends the QχP model previously applied to isospin-symmetric heavy-ion collision matter (Mukherjee 2016) to the neutron star regime, building on earlier parity-doublet applications to compact stars (Dexheimer 2008, 2007, 2012). The resulting equation of state is consistent with perturbative QCD constraints (Fraga 2013) and observational radius estimates from low-mass X-ray binaries (Guillot 2013, 2010; Ozel 2016, 2015), and enables future consistent studies of both heavy-ion collisions and neutron-star mergers using a single unified equation of state.
