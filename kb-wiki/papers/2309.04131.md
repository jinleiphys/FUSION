# Efimov states in excited nuclear halos

> Universality -- an essential concept in physics -- implies that different systems show the same phenomenon and can be described by a unified theory. A prime example of the universal quantum phenomena is the Efimov effect, which is the appearance of multiples of low-energy three-body bound states with progressively large sizes dictated by the discrete scale invariance. The Efimov effect, originally proposed in the nuclear physics context, has been observed in cold atoms and $^4\mathrm{He}$ molecules. The search for the Efimov effect in nuclear physics, however, has been a long-standing challenge owing to the difficulty in identifying ideal nuclides with a large $s$-wave scattering length; such nuclides can be unambiguously considered as Efimov states. Here, we propose a systematic method to identify nuclides that exhibit Efimov states in their excited states in the vicinity of the neutron separation threshold. These nuclei are characterised by their enormous low-energy neutron capture cross-sections, hence giant $s$-wave scattering length. Using our protocol, we identified $^{90}$Zr and $^{159}$Gd as novel candidate nuclides that show the Efimov states. They are well inside the valley of stability in the nuclear chart, and are suited for experimental realisation of the Efimov states in nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The authors propose that Efimov states can be found not in neutron-rich halo nuclei near the drip-line, but in excited states of stable nuclei that lie very close to the neutron separation threshold, where the s-wave scattering length becomes extremely large. They identify 90Zr and 159Gd as specific candidate nuclides, but note that these are "just a few examples" of a new class they call "excited nuclear halos."

## Method

The authors use a systematic protocol based on thermal neutron capture cross-section data and neutron separation energy data to identify nuclei with extremely large s-wave scattering lengths between a core nucleus and a neutron. They then perform three-body calculations for a system of a core nucleus plus two neutrons, using the scattering lengths estimated from the effective neutron separation energy data, to determine whether the resulting bound states exhibit the discrete scale invariance characteristic of Efimov states.

## Key numbers

- The effective binding energy of captured neutrons in the 157Gd case is reported to be 31.4 meV below the neutron emission threshold
- The effective binding energy of captured neutrons in the 88Zr case is expected to be 25 meV from experimental conditions
- The s-wave scattering length for 88Zr-n and 157Gd-n can be as large as 100 fm
- There exist nuclear excited states with |S1n(GS) - Ex| ~ 10 meV, corresponding to |a| ~ 10^4 fm
- The scale factor for the mass-imbalanced three-body system is e^(pi/|s|) approximately 16 to 20

## Context

This work extends the search for nuclear Efimov states beyond the conventional approach of studying ground-state neutron-rich halo nuclei near the drip-line, such as 11Li (Tanihata 1985), 22C (Tanaka 2010), and 19B (PhysRevLett.121.262502). Previous candidates like 62Ca, 72Ca, and 19B had scattering lengths of at most 10 fm order, with 19B being the best candidate at approximately 100 fm. The proposed method enables systematic identification of candidate nuclei well inside the valley of stability, which are more accessible experimentally than neutron-rich nuclei. The work opens a new frontier for studying universal quantum few-body phenomena in excited nuclear systems.
