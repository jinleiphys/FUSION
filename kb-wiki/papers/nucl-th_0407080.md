# The $J/¤ê-K^*$ dissociation cross section in a meson exchange model

> In this work we study the energy dependence of the $J/¤ê-K^*$ dissociation cross section, using a meson exchange model based on effective hadronic Lagrangian that includes couplings between pseudoscalar and vector mesons. We also consider anomalous parity terms. Off-shell effects at the vertices were handled with QCD sum rule estimates for the running coupling constants, and we compare the results with and without form factors. We also study the $J/¤ê-¤ü$ cross section with form factors. The total $J/¤ê-K^*$ cross section was found to be $1.6 \sim1.9$ mb for $4.2\leq\sqrt{s}\leq5~\GeV$.

## Key claim

The total $J/\psi-K^*$ dissociation cross section is found to be $1.6 \sim 1.9$ mb for $4.2 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 5$ GeV, but this result depends on including QCD sum rule estimates for form factors at the vertices; without form factors the cross section grows much more strongly with energy and can be an order of magnitude larger.

## Method

The authors compute the $J/\psi$ dissociation cross section by $K^*$ mesons using a meson exchange model based on an effective hadronic Lagrangian with SU(4) symmetry, including couplings between pseudoscalar and vector mesons as well as anomalous parity terms. Off-shell effects at the vertices are handled by introducing running coupling constants and form factors estimated from QCD sum rules, and the results are compared with and without these form factors. The calculation includes four classes of processes: $K^* J/\psi \rightarrow D_s \bar{D}$, $K^* J/\psi \rightarrow D_s^* \bar{D}^*$, $K^* J/\psi \rightarrow D_s \bar{D}^*$, and $K^* J/\psi \rightarrow D_s^* \bar{D}$, along with their charge-conjugate counterparts.

## Key numbers

- Total $J/\psi-K^*$ dissociation cross section: $1.6 \sim 1.9$ mb for $4.2 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 5$ GeV (with form factors)
- Coupling constants from QCD sum rules: $g_{\psi DD} = 5.8$, $g_{\psi DD^*} = 4.0$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Derived SU(4) couplings: $g_{K^* D_s D} = g_{K^* D^* D_s^*} = 5.0$, $g_{\psi K^* D_s D} = 58.2$, $g_{\psi K^* D_s^* D^*} = 29.0$, $g_{K^* D_s^* D} = g_{K^* D_s D^*} = 6.9$ GeV$^{-1}$, $g_{\psi K^* D^* D_s} = g_{\psi K^* D D_s^*} = 24.8$ GeV$^{-1}$, $h_{\psi K^* D^* D_s} = h_{\psi K^* D D_s^*} = 8.3$ GeV$^{-1}$
- Form factor cut-off scale: $\sim 3.5$ GeV (monopole form factor for $\rho DD$ vertex)
- $J/\psi-\rho$ total absorption cross section with same form factors: $3.0 \sim 10.0$ mb in the same energy range ($4.2 \leq \sqrt{s} \leq 5$ GeV)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous meson exchange analysis of $J/\psi-K$ dissociation (Azevedo and Nielsen, 2004) to the $J/\psi-K^*$ channel, and also updates the $J/\psi-\rho$ cross section with form factors. The approach follows earlier meson exchange calculations for $J/\psi$ dissociation by pions, rho mesons, and kaons by Haglin (2000), Lin and Ko (2000), and Oh, Song, and Lee (2001), but incorporates QCD sum rule estimates for form factors rather than using simple monopole form factors with smaller cut-offs. The results enable more reliable estimates of $J/\psi$ suppression by comoving hadrons in relativistic heavy ion collisions, helping to distinguish quark-gluon plasma formation from hadronic dissociation mechanisms.
