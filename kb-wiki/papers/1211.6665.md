# Spontaneous parity violation in extreme conditions: an effective lagrangian analysis

> We investigate how large baryon densities (and possibly high temperatures) may induce spontaneous parity violation in the composite meson sector of vector-like gauge theory (presumably QCD or techni-QCD) . The analysis at intermediate energy scales is done by using an extended $σ$-model lagrangian that includes two scalar and two pseudoscalar multiplets and fulfills low-energy constraints for vector-like gauge theories. We elaborate on a novel mechanism of parity breaking based on the interplay between lightest and heavier meson condensates, which therefore cannot be realized in the simplest $σ$ model. The results are relevant for an idealized homogeneous and infinite nuclear (quark or techniquark) matter where the influence of density can be examined with the help of a constant chemical potential. The model is able to describe satisfactorily the first-order phase transition to stable nuclear matter, and predicts a second-order phase transition to a state where parity is spontaneously broken. We argue that the parity breaking phenomenon is quite generic when a large enough chemical potential is present. Current quark masses are explicitly taken into account in this work and shown not to change the general conclusions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that spontaneous parity violation can occur in the composite meson sector of vector-like gauge theories (such as QCD) at sufficiently large baryon chemical potential, but only when the effective model includes at least two scalar and two pseudoscalar multiplets; the simplest one-multiplet sigma model cannot realize this mechanism because it relies on the interplay between lightest and heavier meson condensates.

## Method

The authors construct an extended linear sigma model with two chiral multiplets (two scalar and two pseudoscalar fields) that satisfies low-energy constraints from QCD, and introduce finite baryon density via a constant chemical potential in the one-quark loop free energy. They derive mass-gap equations and second variations of the effective potential to find minima, then analyze the phase structure by studying how the effective potential changes with chemical potential and temperature, including corrections linear in current quark masses.

## Key numbers

- The chiral symmetry breaking scale Lambda is approximately 1 GeV, with constituent quark masses in the range 0.2 to 0.3 GeV.
- The weak pion decay constant F_pi is used as a reference scale, with Lambda ~ 4 pi F_pi.
- The model predicts a first-order phase transition to stable nuclear matter at the saturation point, and a second-order phase transition to a parity-broken state at a higher chemical potential.
- The inequality (lambda_3 - lambda_4) sigma_1^2 + lambda_6 sigma_1 sigma_2 + 2 lambda_2 sigma_2^2 - Delta_22 - (m_q d_2^2)/(d_1 sigma_1 + d_2 sigma_2) > 0 is the condition for the absence of spontaneous parity breaking in normal vacuum.

## Context

This work extends preliminary results reported in Andrianov et al. (2012) and Andrianov et al. (2012) by including corrections beyond the chiral limit linear in current quark masses, and derives new thermodynamic relations for finite temperatures and chemical potentials. It builds on earlier attempts to describe two multiplets of scalar and pseudoscalar mesons by Bars (1970s) and on extended quark models by the same authors, and contrasts with simpler NJL-type models (Buballa, 2005) that suffer from "chiral collapse" and cannot realize the parity-breaking mechanism. The results are relevant for understanding dense matter in compact stars and heavy-ion collisions, where lattice QCD results at large baryon chemical potential are not yet quantitatively available.
