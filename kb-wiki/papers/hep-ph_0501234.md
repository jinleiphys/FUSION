# From Color Glass Condensate to Quark Gluon Plasma through the event horizon

> We propose a new thermalization scenario for heavy ion collisions which at sufficiently high energies implies the phase transition to the quark--gluon plasma. The key ingredient of our approach is the Hawking--Unruh effect: an observer moving with an acceleration $a$ experiences the influence of a thermal bath with an effective temperature $T = a / 2π$, similar to the one present in the vicinity of a black hole horizon. For electric charges moving in external electromagnetic fields of realistic strength, the resulting temperature appears too small to be detected. However for partons in strong color fields the effect should be observable: in the Color Glass Condensate picture, the strength of the color-electric field is $E \sim Q_s^2/g$ ($Q_s$ is the saturation scale, and $g$ is the strong coupling), the typical acceleration is $a \sim Q_s$, and the heat bath temperature is $T = Q_s / 2π\sim 200$ MeV. In nuclear collisions at sufficiently high energies the effect can induce a rapid thermalization over the time period of $τ\simeq 2π/Q_s \simeq 1\ {\rm fm}$ accompanied by phase transitions. We consider a specific example of chiral symmetry restoration induced by a rapid deceleration of the colliding nuclei. We argue that parton saturation in the initial nuclear wave functions is a necessary pre--condition for the formation of quark--gluon plasma. We discuss the implications of our "black hole thermalization" scenario for various observables in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the Hawking–Unruh effect, where an accelerating observer experiences a thermal bath at temperature T = a/2π, can cause rapid thermalization in relativistic heavy ion collisions, provided that the initial parton state is described by the Color Glass Condensate with a saturation scale Q_s large enough to produce decelerations a ∼ Q_s.

## Method

The authors apply the Hawking–Unruh effect to partons decelerating in the strong color fields of the Color Glass Condensate, where the color-electric field strength is E ∼ Q_s^2/g and the typical acceleration is a ∼ Q_s. They derive the resulting thermal distribution from quantum tunneling through the event horizon in Rindler space, and relate the saturation scale Q_s to the Unruh temperature T = Q_s/2π. They also use the dual resonance model to derive a limiting acceleration a_0 related to the Hagedorn temperature, and argue that parton saturation is necessary to exceed this limit and produce deconfined matter.

## Key numbers

* The Unruh temperature for partons in the Color Glass Condensate is T = Q_s/2π ∼ 200 MeV.
* The thermalization time is τ ≃ 2π/Q_s ≃ 1 fm.
* The typical acceleration achieved in a collision is a ∼ Q_s ∼ 1 GeV.
* The limiting acceleration from the dual resonance model is a_0 = √(3/2) b^{-1/2}, where b is the Regge slope, corresponding to the Hagedorn temperature T_Hag = a_0/2π.
* For a short electric field pulse of duration t, the decay rate is Γ ∼ exp(−2π m t), independent of the field strength when a t ≪ 1.

## Context

This work extends the application of the Hawking–Unruh effect, previously considered for electromagnetic fields by Bell and Leinaas (1982), to the strong color fields of the Color Glass Condensate in heavy ion collisions. It provides a mechanism for the rapid thermalization (∼1 fm) observed at RHIC, which is much faster than expected from perturbative calculations by Molnar (2001). The scenario enables a connection between parton saturation in the initial nuclear wave function and the formation of a thermal quark-gluon plasma, and it suggests that the Schwinger pair production mechanism can be reinterpreted as a Hawking–Unruh phenomenon.
