# $Δ$ isobars in hyperon stars in a modified quark meson coupling model

> The possibility of the appearance of $Δ$ isobars in neutron star matter and the so called {\it $Δ$ puzzle} is studied in a modified quark meson coupling model where the confining interaction for quarks inside a baryon is represented by a phenomenological average potential in an equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic form. The hadron-hadron interaction in nuclear matter is then realized by introducing additional quark couplings to $σ$, $ω$, and $ρ$ mesons through mean-field approximations. The couplings of the $Δ$ to the meson fields are fixed from available constraints while the hyperon couplings are fixed from the optical potential values. It is observed that within the constraints of the mass of the precisely measured massive pulsars, PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230, neutron stars with a composition of both $Δ$ isobars and hyperons is possible. It is also observed that with an increase in the vector coupling strength of the $Δ$ isobars there is a decrease in the radius of the neutron stars.

## Key claim

Within the constraints of the precisely measured massive pulsars PSR J0348+0432 and PSR J1614-2230, neutron stars with a composition of both Δ isobars and hyperons are possible in the modified quark meson coupling model, but the model does not produce a lower radius for such stars, indicating the need for a mixed phase of hadrons and quarks.

## Method

The authors used a modified quark meson coupling (MQMC) model where quarks inside baryons are confined by an equally mixed scalar-vector harmonic potential, and baryon-baryon interactions are realized through quark couplings to σ, ω, and ρ mesons in mean-field approximations. They included all four Δ isobar charge states (Δ⁻, Δ⁰, Δ⁺, Δ⁺⁺) together with hyperons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) as degrees of freedom in dense hadronic matter under β-equilibrium and charge neutrality conditions. The hyperon couplings were fixed from optical potential values (U_Λ = -28 MeV, U_Σ = 30 MeV, U_Ξ = -18 MeV), while the Δ vector coupling was set to x_ωΔ = 0.8 with moderate variations studied. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were integrated to obtain mass-radius relations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass with nucleons only: 2.25 M_⊙ at radius 14.0 km (quark mass m_q = 150 MeV)
- Maximum mass with nucleons + Δ isobars: 2.19 M_⊙ at radius 14.4 km (x_ωΔ = 0.8)
- Maximum mass with nucleons + Δ isobars + hyperons: 2.15 M_⊙ at radius 15.4 km (x_ωΔ = 0.8)
- Δ⁻ isobars appear first at baryon density ρ_B = 0.41 fm⁻³
- For nucleons + Δ composition, varying x_ωΔ from 0.70 to 1.00 gives maximum masses from 2.14 M_⊙ to 2.24 M_⊙ and radii from 14.88 km to 14.15 km
- Nuclear matter saturation density ρ_0 = 0.15 fm⁻³, binding energy B_0 = -15.7 MeV, symmetry energy J = 32.0 MeV
- Nuclear matter incompressibility K = 292 MeV, slope of symmetry energy L = 86.39 MeV
- Maximum surface gravitational redshift Z_s^max = 0.17

## Context

This work extends the MQMC model previously applied to symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter (Sahoo et al., 2015; Mishra et al., 2015) by including Δ isobars alongside hyperons to address the "Δ puzzle" and "hyperon puzzle", the problem that exotic baryons soften the equation of state and reduce maximum neutron star masses below observational limits. Earlier work by Glendenning (1985) indicated Δ appearance at much higher densities, while more recent studies by Drago et al. (2014) and others suggested early Δ appearance. The authors show that with appropriate couplings, both Δ isobars and hyperons can coexist in neutron stars while still satisfying the mass constraints from PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 ± 0.04 M_⊙) and PSR J1614-2230 (1.97 ± 0.04 M_⊙), though the resulting radii remain larger than some empirical constraints.
