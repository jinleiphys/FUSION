# DbarN interaction in a color-confining chiral quark model

> We investigate the low-energy elastic DbarN interaction using a quark model that confines color and realizes dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The model is defined by a microscopic Hamiltonian inspired in the QCD Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge. Constituent quark masses are obtained by solving a gap equation and baryon and meson bound-state wave functions are obtained using a variational method. We derive a low energy meson-nucleon potential from a quark-interchange mechanism whose ingredients are the quark-quark and quark-antiquark interactions and baryon and meson wave functions, all derived from the same microscopic Hamiltonian. The model is supplemented with (sigma,rho,omega,a0) single-meson exchanges to describe the long-range part of the interaction. Cross-sections and phase shifts are obtained by iterating the quark-interchange plus meson-exchange potentials in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation. Once model parameters in meson exchange potential are fixed to describe the low-energy experimental phase shifts of the K+N and K0N reactions, predictions for Dbar0N and D-N reactions are obtained without introducing new parameters.

## Key claim
The paper presents predictions for low-energy elastic \(\bar D N\) cross sections and phase shifts using a quark model that simultaneously describes color confinement and dynamical chiral symmetry breaking, with the hedge that these predictions are obtained without introducing new parameters once the model is fixed to experimental \(K^+N\) and \(K^0 N\) phase shifts.

## Method
The authors solve a gap equation derived from a QCD-inspired Hamiltonian in Coulomb gauge to obtain momentum-dependent constituent quark masses, then use variational Gaussian wave functions for baryons and mesons. An effective meson-nucleon potential is derived from a quark-interchange mechanism using the same microscopic quark-quark and quark-antiquark interactions, supplemented by single-meson exchanges (\(\sigma, \rho, \omega, a_0\)) for the long-range part. Phase shifts and cross sections are obtained by iterating the combined potential in a Lippmann-Schwinger equation.

## Key numbers
- Constituent quark mass at zero momentum in the chiral limit: \(M(k=0) \approx 250\) MeV (from solving the gap equation).
- Quark condensate: \((-\langle \bar\Psi\Psi\rangle)^{1/3} = 280\) MeV.
- For the \(\bar D N\) reaction, predicted cross sections are larger by a factor roughly equal to 2 than those of the analogous \(K^+N\) and \(K^0N\) reactions for center-of-mass kinetic energies up to 150 MeV.
- Coulomb string tension parameter: \(\sigma_\text{Coul} = (552\ \text{MeV})^2\) (Model 1).
- Current quark masses used: \(m_u = m_d = 10\) MeV, \(m_s = 150\) MeV, \(m_c = 950\) MeV (Model 1) and \(m_c = 600\) MeV (Model 2).

## Context
This work extends the quark-interchange plus meson-exchange approach of Haidenbauer (2007) for the \(\bar D N\) reaction, which used a nonrelativistic quark model with one-gluon-exchange, by employing a Hamiltonian that self-consistently generates constituent quark masses via dynamical chiral symmetry breaking. The model enables future studies of in-medium modifications of \(\bar D\) meson properties linked to chiral symmetry restoration, overcoming the limitation of earlier models where quark masses and interactions were specified independently.
