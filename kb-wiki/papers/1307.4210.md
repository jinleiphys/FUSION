# Heavy ion collisions in the 1A GeV regime: how well do we join up to astrophysics?

> The derivation of information useful for understanding the physics inside compact stars from HIC observations is a difficult task. Complications due to finite size, different chemistry, non-adiabatic compression, incomplete stopping and structural effects must be overcome. This can only be convincingly achieved by a successful simulation of many experimental observables varying incident energy, system size and isospin content. Using now available systematic FOPI data in the SIS energy range we try to trace the path to take.

## Key claim

The FOPI data in the SIS energy range (0.4A to 1.5A GeV) strongly favor a soft nuclear equation of state (SM) over a stiff version (HM), but this preference is conditional on the use of IQMD simulations that underestimate clusterization and do not yet reproduce the observed isotopic splitting in elliptic flow for mass-3 clusters.

## Method

The FOPI collaboration measured proton and light cluster (deuteron, triton, 3He) yields and anisotropic flow (directed flow v1 and elliptic flow v2) in Au+Au collisions at beam energies from 0.4A to 1.5A GeV. The data were compared to Isospin Quantum Molecular Dynamics (IQMD) simulations using two phenomenological cold nuclear equations of state: a soft version (SM) close to Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations, and a stiff version (HM). A new observable v2n was introduced, defined from a quadratic fit to the elliptic flow rapidity dependence, to quantify the EoS sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- The soft EoS (SM) and stiff EoS (HM) differ by a factor of 1.6 in the v2n observable for Au+Au collisions, exceeding experimental error bars
- The density range probed in SIS experiments extends up to ρ/ρ0 = 2.5
- Beam energies studied span from 0.4A GeV to 1.5A GeV
- The v2n analysis uses scaled rapidity range |y0| < 0.8
- At E/A = 1.5A GeV, a significant isotopic difference in elliptic flow is observed between 3H and 3He, while at 0.4A GeV no significant difference is seen within error bars

## Context

This work extends earlier findings from the FOPI collaboration (Reisdorf 2012) and confirms the preference for a soft EoS previously indicated by comparisons of K+ yield data varying system size or centrality (Foerster 2007). The results are compared to theoretical DBHF calculations (van Dalen 2007) and to recent constraints on EoS parameters L and Esy0 from nuclear masses (Moeller 2012), theoretical efforts (Tews 2012), and neutron star data (Steiner 2012), which show incompatibilities associated with different physics sensitivities. The work enables future constraints on the neutron matter EoS by combining improved cluster formation models with pion yield and flow data.
