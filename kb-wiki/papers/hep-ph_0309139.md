# The Effects of Quantum Entropy on the Bag Constant

> The effects of quantum entropy on the bag constant are studied at low temperatures and small chemical potentials. The inclusion of the quantum entropy of the quarks in the equation of state provides the hadronic bag with an additional heat which causes a decrease in the effective latent heat inside the bag. We have considered two types of baryonic bags, $Δ$ and $Ω^-$. In both cases we have found that the bag constant without the quantum entropy almost does not change with the temperature and the quark chemical potential. The contribution from the quantum entropy to the equation of state clearly decreases the value of the bag constant.

## Key claim

The inclusion of quantum entropy from the SU(3)_c color symmetry in the equation of state introduces a strong, approximately linear temperature dependence for the bag constant B, whereas without this entropy term the bag constant remains nearly constant at low temperatures and small chemical potentials. The effect is demonstrated for two specific baryonic bags, Delta and Omega-minus, but the authors note the quantum entropy term itself depends only on temperature, not on chemical potential.

## Method

The authors start from the trace anomaly of the energy-momentum tensor and write the equation of state for a hadronic bag using the grand canonical partition function at finite temperature T and quark chemical potential mu_q. They numerically solve the resulting equation for the bag constant B, using vacuum condensate values (gluon condensate and light/strange quark condensates) extracted from QCD sum rules and charmonium data. Two baryonic structures are considered: the Delta baryon (three light quarks) and the Omega-minus baryon (three strange quarks), with and without the quantum entropy term S_{q,3}(T) derived from the color-singlet ground state.

## Key numbers

- Vacuum gluon condensate: about 1.95 GeV/fm^3, extracted from the charmonium spectrum
- Light quark condensate (m_{lq}<u\bar{u}+d\bar{d}>_0): -42 MeV/fm^3, using average light quark mass 6 MeV and pion decay constant 92.4 MeV
- Strange quark condensate (m_s<\bar{s}s>_0): -273 MeV/fm^3, using strange quark mass 150 MeV
- Averaged vacuum contribution (gluon + light quark condensates): 1.91 GeV/fm^3
- Averaged vacuum contribution (gluon + strange quark condensates): 1.68 GeV/fm^3
- Proton volume used in the equation of state: 2.76 fm^3, from the charge radius
- Temperature range studied: up to 50 MeV; chemical potential range similarly small

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of quantum entropy in the quark singlet ground state of hadrons (Miller, 2003) and at finite temperatures (Miller and Tawfik, 2003) by incorporating the entropy into the bag model equation of state. The bag model itself follows the formulation of Donoghue, Golowich, and Holstein (1992). The vacuum condensate values rely on the QCD sum rule approach of Shifman, Vainshtein, and Zakharov (1979) and the operator product expansion. The results suggest that the bag constant becomes temperature-dependent when quantum entropy is included, which may affect models of the hadronic phase below the critical temperature T_c and could be connected to glueball condensate studies (Karsch, Redlich, and Tawfik, 2003).
