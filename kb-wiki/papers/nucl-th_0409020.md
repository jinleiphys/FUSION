# Jet quenching and neutral pion production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> In the jet quenching model, we have analysed the PHENIX data on nuclear modification factor of $π^0$, in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s}$=200 GeV, and extracted the initial gluon density of the medium produced. In jet quenching, partons lose energy in the medium, depending on the medium density as well as on the in-medium path length. Systematic analysis indicate that in most central (0-10% centrality) collisions, medium density is very large $dN_g/dy \sim$ 2150. Medium density decreases exponentially as the collision centrality decreases and in very peripheral (70-92% centrality) collisions, $dN_g/dy \sim$ 3. Initial energy density of the medium also decreases smoothly from $ε_0 \sim$ 20 $GeV/fm^3$ in most central collisions to $ε_0 \sim$ 3 $GeV/fm^3$ in most peripheral collisions. Very large $dN_g/dy$ or $ε_0$ indicate very dense matter formation in central Au+Au collisions.

# Jet quenching and neutral pion production in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

## Key claim

The jet quenching model, using the GLV form of partonic energy loss in a one-dimensionally expanding medium, can explain the PHENIX data on neutral pion suppression in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV across all centrality ranges with a single free parameter (the initial transverse gluon density), but the extracted gluon density in central collisions is nearly a factor of 2 larger than previous estimates by Vitev et al. (2002) due to a different (Glauber model) calculation of the in-medium path length.

## Method

The author analyzed PHENIX data on the nuclear modification factor R_AA for neutral pions in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV across nine centrality ranges (0-10% to 70-92%). A leading-order pQCD model with CTEQ5L parton distributions and KKP fragmentation functions was used, with partonic energy loss implemented via the GLV formula for radiative energy loss in an expanding medium. The average in-medium path length for each centrality range was calculated in a Glauber model, and the initial transverse gluon density was extracted as the only free parameter by fitting the pT > 4 GeV region of the R_AA data using the MINUIT minimization program.

## Key numbers

- In 0-10% central collisions: initial gluon rapidity density dN_g/dy ~ 2158, corresponding to initial energy density epsilon_0 ~ 20 GeV/fm^3
- In 70-92% peripheral collisions: dN_g/dy ~ 3, epsilon_0 ~ 3 GeV/fm^3
- Average in-medium path length in 0-10% central collisions: L = 5.17 fm (nearly 1.5 times smaller than the 4/3 R_A value used by Vitev et al.)
- Transverse gluon density in 0-10% central collisions: (1/A_T)(dN_g/dy) = 21.12 +/- 1.00 fm^-2
- Transverse gluon density in 70-92% peripheral collisions: (1/A_T)(dN_g/dy) = 4.58 +/- 1.22 fm^-2
- Debye screening mass fixed at mu_D = 0.8 GeV, obtained from a fit to 0-10% centrality data
- Empirical relation for gluon density vs. path length: dN_g/dy = 0.2 exp(1.8 L) with mu = 1.8 fm^-1
- Empirical relation for energy density vs. participants: epsilon(N_part) = 3.84 + 0.11 (N_part/2) GeV/fm^3
- Even peripheral collisions yield energy density > 1 GeV/fm^3, exceeding the critical energy density for QGP phase transition

## Context

This work extends the jet quenching analysis of PHENIX data by using a Glauber model estimate of the in-medium path length rather than the 4/3 R_A approximation used by Vitev and Gyulassy (2002), resulting in extracted gluon densities roughly twice as large (dN_g/dy ~ 2158 vs. 1000-1200). The analysis enables predictions for LHC energy (sqrt(s)=1.148 TeV), showing that for the same gluon density, suppression is less at LHC than at RHIC, and that 30% larger gluon density is required at LHC to achieve similar suppression (R_AA ~ 0.2). The exponential dependence of gluon density on path length (dN_g/dy = 0.2 exp(1.8 L)) suggests gluon intensity is exponentially attenuated in the medium with attenuation coefficient mu = 1.8 fm^-1.
