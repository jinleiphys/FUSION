# Understanding $X(3872)$ and its decays in the extended Friedrichs scheme

> We present that the $X(3872)$ could be represented as a dynamically generated state in the extended Friedrichs scheme, in which the ratio of "elementariness" and "compositeness" of the different components in the $X(3872)$ is about $Z_{c\bar c}:X_{\bar D^0 D^{0*}}: X_{ D^+ D^{-*}}: X_{\bar D^* D^*}$ $= 1:(2.67\sim 8.85):(0.45\sim 0.46):0.04$. Furthermore, its decays to $π^0$ and a $P$-wave charmonium $χ_{cJ}$ state with $J=0,1$, or $2$, $J/ψπ^+π^-$, and $J/ψπ^+π^-π^0$ could be calculated out with the help of Barnes-Swanson model. The isospin breaking effects is easily understood in this scheme. This calculation also shows that the decay rate of $X(3872)$ to $χ_{c1}π^0$ is much smaller than its decay rate to $J/ψπ^+π^-$.

## Key claim

The X(3872) can be represented as a dynamically generated state from the coupling of a bare chi_c1(2P) state with D Dbar* and D* Dbar* continuum states in the extended Friedrichs scheme, with the claim that its decays to chi_cJ pi0 are about one order of magnitude smaller than decays to J/psi pi+ pi-; this is smaller than the central value measured by BESIII but within the sizable experimental uncertainties.

## Method

The authors solve the eigenvalue problem of the extended Friedrichs model, where a bare discrete chi_c1(2P) state couples to D Dbar* and D* Dbar* continuum states via form factors from the quark pair creation model, using the Godfrey-Isgur model as input. The resulting wave function of X(3872) is then used to compute decay amplitudes to chi_cJ pi0, J/psi pi+ pi-, and J/psi pi+ pi- pi0, with the scattering matrix elements for D Dbar* to chi_cJ pi0 calculated using the Barnes-Swanson constituent interchange model including one-gluon exchange and confinement interactions.

## Key numbers

- Ratio of elementariness and compositeness components in X(3872): Z_{c cbar} : X_{D0 D0*} : X_{D+ D-*} : X_{D* D*} = 1 : (2.67 to 8.85) : (0.45 to 0.46) : 0.04
- Ratio of branching fractions from BESIII: B(X(3872) to chi_c1 pi0) / B(X(3872) to J/psi pi+ pi-) = 0.88 +0.33 -0.27 +/- 0.10
- Upper limit from Belle: B(X(3872) to chi_c1 pi0) / B(X(3872) to J/psi pi+ pi-) < 0.97 at 90% confidence level
- Calculated ratio B(X(3872) to J/psi pi+ pi- pi0) / B(X(3872) to J/psi pi+ pi-) is about 1.6

## Context

This work extends the application of the extended Friedrichs scheme (Xiao 2016, Zhou 2017) to describe X(3872) as a dynamically generated state, and uses the Barnes-Swanson constituent interchange model (Barnes 1991, Barnes 2000) to compute its decays. The calculation addresses the isospin breaking effects observed in X(3872) decays by Belle (delAmoSanchez 2010) and the recent BESIII measurement of X(3872) to chi_c1 pi0 (Ablikim 2019), finding that the decay rates to chi_cJ pi0 are smaller than the BESIII central value but consistent within uncertainties; the result enables future experimental comparisons with higher statistics data to discriminate between molecular and compact tetraquark interpretations.
