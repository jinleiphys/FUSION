# Electric form factors of the octet baryons from lattice QCD and chiral extrapolation

> We apply a formalism inspired by heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory with finite-range regularization to dynamical $2+1-$flavor CSSM/QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration lattice QCD simulation results for the electric form factors of the octet baryons. The electric form factor of each octet baryon is extrapolated to the physical pseudoscalar masses, after finite-volume corrections have been applied, at six fixed values of $Q^2$ in the range 0.2-1.3 GeV$^2$. The extrapolated lattice results accurately reproduce the experimental form factors of the nucleon at the physical point, indicating that omitted disconnected quark loop contributions are small. Furthermore, using the results of a recent lattice study of the magnetic form factors, we determine the ratio $μ_p G^p_E/G^p_M$. This quantity decreases with $Q^2$ in a way qualitatively consistent with recent experimental results.

## Key claim

The extrapolated lattice QCD results for the connected part of the nucleon electric form factors accurately reproduce experimental measurements, indicating that omitted disconnected quark loop contributions are small relative to the uncertainties of the calculation, provided other systematic effects such as excited-state contamination are negligible.

## Method

The authors applied a heavy-baryon chiral perturbation theory formalism with finite-range regularization to dynamical 2+1-flavor CSSM/QCDSF/UKQCD Collaboration lattice QCD simulation results for the octet baryon electric form factors. They performed six independent fits at fixed values of Q² in the range 0.2-1.3 GeV², extrapolating each baryon's form factor to physical pseudoscalar masses after applying finite-volume corrections, using a simultaneous fit to all octet baryon form factors at each Q² value.

## Key numbers

- The six fixed Q² values used for extrapolation span the range 0.2-1.3 GeV²
- The lattice spacing is a = 0.074(2) fm at β = 5.50
- Pion masses for the primary simulation trajectory are 465 MeV, 360 MeV, and 310 MeV, with corresponding kaon masses of 465 MeV, 505 MeV, and 520 MeV
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is taken as f = 0.087 GeV
- The regulator mass Λ is varied in the range 0.7-0.9 GeV
- The proton electric radius from the dipole-like ansatz (Eq. 6) is 0.76(10) fm², compared to the experimental value of 0.878(5) fm²
- The Σ⁻ electric radius from the dipole-like ansatz is 0.45(3) fm², compared to the experimental value of 0.780(10) fm²
- The χ²/d.o.f. for a dipole fit to the isovector nucleon form factor is approximately 3.2, while the more general dipole-like fit yields χ²/d.o.f. ≈ 1

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD study of octet baryon magnetic form factors from the same collaboration (Shanahan et al., 2014, referred to as MagFFs) to the electric form factors, using a larger volume and a lighter pion mass of 220 MeV. The results enable theoretical predictions for hyperon electric form factors that are experimentally challenging to measure, and the comparison with experimental nucleon form factors from Kelly (2004) provides evidence that disconnected quark loop contributions are small, consistent with direct lattice studies at larger pion masses by Abdel-Rehim et al. (2013), Bali et al. (2011), and Alexandrou et al. (2014).
