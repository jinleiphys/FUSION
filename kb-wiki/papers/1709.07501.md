# Reorientation-effect measurement of the first 2$^+$ state in $^{12}$C: confirmation of oblate deformation

> A Coulomb-excitation reorientation-effect measurement using the TIGRESS $γ-$ray spectrometer at the TRIUMF/ISAC II facility has permitted the first determination of the $\langle 2^+_1\mid\mid \hat{E2} \mid\mid 2^+_1\rangle$ diagonal matrix element in $^{12}$C from particle$-γ$ coincidence data. Required state-of-the-art no-core shell model calculations of the nuclear polarizability for the ground and first-excited (2$^+_1$) states in $^{12}$C using chiral NN N$^4$LO500 and NN+3NF350 interactions have been performed. Consistent predictions show a larger polarizability than previously anticipated. The polarizability of the 2$^+_1$ state is introduced into the current and previous Coulomb-excitation reorientation-effect analysis of $^{12}$C. Spectroscopic quadrupole moments of $Q_{_S}(2_1^+)= +0.053(44)$ eb and $Q_{_S}(2_1^+)= +0.08(3)$ eb are determined, respectively, yielding a weighted average of $Q_{_S}(2_1^+)= +0.071(25)$ eb, in agreement with recent ab initio calculations. The present measurement confirms that the 2$^+_1$ state of $^{12}$C is oblate and emphasizes the important role played by the nuclear polarizability in Coulomb-excitation studies of light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that the first 2⁺ state in ¹²C has an oblate deformation through a Coulomb-excitation reorientation-effect measurement, but the result depends critically on incorporating state-of-the-art no-core shell model calculations of the nuclear polarizability for both the ground and excited states, which show a larger polarizability than previously anticipated.

## Method

A beam of ¹²C³⁺ ions at 4.975 AMeV was delivered to the TIGRESS γ-ray spectrometer at TRIUMF/ISAC II and impinged on a ³ mg/cm² thick ¹⁹⁴Pt target. Particle-γ coincidence data were collected using a double-sided CD-type silicon detector (S2) for scattered ions and eight TIGRESS HPGe clover detectors for γ rays, with event selection including time coincidence and an energy-sharing condition of |E_ring − E_sector| ≤ 350 keV. No-core shell model calculations using chiral NN N⁴LO500 and NN+3NF350 interactions were performed to determine the nuclear polarizability parameters κ(g.s.) and κ(2⁺₁), which were then incorporated into the Coulomb-excitation reorientation-effect analysis.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q_S(2⁺₁) = +0.053(44) eb from one analysis
- Spectroscopic quadrupole moment Q_S(2⁺₁) = +0.08(3) eb from another analysis
- Weighted average Q_S(2⁺₁) = +0.071(25) eb, confirming oblate deformation
- Beam energy of 4.975 AMeV was chosen to satisfy Spear's prescription of minimum separation between nuclear surfaces S(θ)_min ≥ 6.5 fm
- The 2⁺₁ state in ¹²C lies at 4.439 MeV excitation energy
- The 4439-keV γ-ray transition depopulating the 2⁺₁ state was observed in coincidence with scattered ¹²C ions

## Context

This work extends the only previous reorientation-effect measurement of the 2⁺₁ state in ¹²C by Vermeer (1983), which used singles particle data and assumed a nominal polarizability parameter κ(g.s.) = 1. The present particle-γ coincidence technique eliminates target contaminants and, combined with modern no-core shell model calculations of the nuclear polarizability, provides a more reliable determination. The results agree with recent ab initio calculations and emphasize that the nuclear polarizability plays a crucial role in Coulomb-excitation studies of light nuclei, where κ > 1 values are generally observed.
