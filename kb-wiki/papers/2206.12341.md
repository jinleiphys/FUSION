# Analysis of one-neutron transfer reaction in $^{18}$O + $^{76}$Se collision at 275 MeV

> Purpose: We want to analyze transitions to low-lying excited states of the residual and ejectile nuclei in the 76Se(18O, 17O) 77Se one-neutron stripping reaction at 275-MeV incident energy and determine the role of single-particle and core excitation in the description of the measured cross sections. In addition, we explore the sensitivity of the calculated cross section to different nuclear structure models. Methods: The excitation energy spectrum and the differential cross-section angular distributions are measured using the MAGNEX large acceptance magnetic spectrometer for the detection of the ejectiles and the missing mass technique for the reconstruction of the reaction kinematics. The data are compared with calculations based on distorted-wave Born approximation, coupled-channels Born approximation, and coupled reaction channels adopting spectroscopic amplitudes for the projectile and target overlaps derived by large-scale shell-model calculations and interacting boson-fermion model. Results: Peaks in the energy spectra corresponding to groups of unresolved transitions to 77Se and 17O are identified. The experimental cross sections are extracted and compared to theoretical calculations. A remarkable agreement is found, without using any scaling factors, demonstrating that the adopted models for nuclear structure and reaction take into account the relevant aspects of the studied processes. The main transitions which contribute to the cross section of each peak are identified.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the measured cross sections for the 76Se(18O,17O)77Se one-neutron stripping reaction at 275 MeV can be reproduced by theoretical calculations without any scaling factors, but only when core excitations in both projectile and target are included via coupled-channel formalisms; the agreement is conditional on using spectroscopic amplitudes from large-scale shell-model or interacting boson-fermion model calculations.

## Method

The experiment used a 275 MeV 18O beam incident on a 76Se target (270 ± 14 μg/cm² on a carbon backing) at INFN-LNS Catania, with the MAGNEX magnetic spectrometer detecting 17O ejectiles and the missing mass technique reconstructing reaction kinematics. Theoretical calculations employed Distorted Wave Born Approximation (DWBA), Coupled Channel Born Approximation (CCBA), and Coupled Reaction Channel (CRC) formalisms, using spectroscopic amplitudes derived from large-scale shell-model calculations (with the ZBM interaction for projectile overlaps and a 56Ni-core Hamiltonian for target overlaps) and from the interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM-2) for target overlaps. The optical potentials were based on the double-folding Sao Paulo potential, and the transfer operator was calculated in the post-representation including full complex remnant terms.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: 275 MeV for the 18O projectile
- Target thickness: 270 ± 14 μg/cm² for 76Se, with a carbon backing of 80 ± 4 μg/cm²
- Energy resolution: δE (FWHM) ≈ 310 keV
- Angular resolution: δθ_LAB (FWHM) ≈ 0.5°
- Angular range covered: 3° < θ_lab < 14° (one spectrometer setting at 8°)
- Optimal Q-value estimated: Q_opt = -5 MeV for the examined reaction
- Grazing angle: θ_c.m. ≈ 9°
- B(E2; 0⁺ → 2⁺) for 18O: experimental 0.0043 e²b², shell-model 0.0030 e²b²
- B(E2; 0⁺ → 2⁺) for 76Se: experimental 0.4320 e²b², shell-model 0.3722 e²b²
- Three regions of interest (ROI) defined: -0.5 < E_x < 0.4 MeV, 0.4 < E_x < 0.9 MeV, and 0.9 < E_x < 1.6 MeV
- Overall systematic uncertainty: about 10% from target thickness and charge collection

## Context

This work is part of the NUMEN project (Cappuzzello 2018, Agodi 2021) which aims to determine nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay via heavy-ion induced double charge exchange reactions. It extends previous systematic studies of one-neutron transfer reactions using 18O beams on targets including 9Be, 11B, 12C, 13C, 16O, 28Si, and 64Ni (Cavallaro 2013, Ermamatov 2016, Carbone 2017, Linares 2018, Carbone 2018, Ferreira 2021, Cavallaro 2021), and is the first study of the 76Se(18O,17O)77Se reaction. The results enable future analyses of single and double charge exchange processes involving 76Se, and indicate that the description of 77Se requires a large model space, which should also be considered for beta-beta decay studies of 76Ge.
