# Global Framework for Emulation of Nuclear Calculations

> We introduce a hierarchical framework that combines ab initio many-body calculations with a Bayesian neural network, developing emulators capable of accurately predicting nuclear properties across isotopic chains simultaneously and being applicable to different regions of the nuclear chart. We benchmark our developments using the oxygen isotopic chain, achieving accurate results for ground-state energies and nuclear charge radii, while providing robust uncertainty quantification. Our framework enables global sensitivity analysis of nuclear binding energies and charge radii with respect to the low-energy constants that describe the nuclear force.

## Key claim
The authors claim that their hierarchical Bayesian neural network framework (BANNANE) can accurately predict nuclear properties across isotopic chains simultaneously, but this claim is qualified by the fact that benchmarking was performed only on the oxygen isotopic chain (12O through 24O), and the framework's applicability to other regions of the nuclear chart remains to be demonstrated in future work.

## Method
The authors combined ab initio many-body calculations using the Valence-Space In-Medium Similarity Renormalization Group (VS-IMSRG) with a hierarchical Bayesian neural network architecture. The network uses learnable embeddings for proton number, neutron number, and fidelity level (emax values of 4, 6, 8, and 10), processes inputs through a shared multi-head self-attention layer, and employs fidelity-specific prediction pathways with additive delta corrections for higher fidelities. Training was performed using stochastic variational inference with 8188 LEC samples from chiral effective field theory at N2LO with explicit Delta-isobars.

## Key numbers
- Ground-state binding energy predictions across the oxygen chain achieved 0.8 MeV Root Mean Squared Error (RMSE) at emax=10
- Charge radius predictions across the oxygen chain achieved 0.01 fm RMSE at emax=10
- For 16O specifically, BANNANE achieved 0.337 MeV RMSE for binding energy and 0.02 fm RMSE for charge radius using 715 total training samples (51 of highest fidelity)
- The Eigenvector Continuation-based approach obtained RMSEs of 3 MeV and 0.02 fm for 16O using 128 training points
- The total number of trainable parameters in the architecture is 2 x 10^5
- Training used 30,000 iterations with a learning rate of 1 x 10^-4

## Context
This work extends the capabilities of nuclear emulators beyond individual isotopes, which was the limitation of prior approaches such as Eigenvector Continuation (Frame 2018, Ekstrom 2019) and multi-output multi-fidelity Deep Gaussian Processes (Belley 2024). The framework enables global sensitivity analysis of binding energies and charge radii with respect to low-energy constants, which was computationally prohibitive using brute-force ab initio methods. The authors anticipate that BANNANE can guide future experiments at the Facility for Rare Isotope Beams and establish connections between measurements of binding energy, charge radii, and specific components of the nuclear force.
