# Enhanced Dilepton production near the color superconducting phase and the QCD critical point

> The dilepton production yields in relativistic heavy ion collisions are investigated along isentropic trajectories in the quark (Wigner) phase within the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model. An enhancement of the ultra-low energy dilepton yield in the vicinity of the color superconducting (CSC) phase and the QCD critical point (QCD-CP) is found, compared to the free quark gas. Furthermore, we have found a nontrivial structure in the beam energy dependence of the ultra-low energy dilepton yield. A local maximum and minimum of the dilepton yield as a function of entropy per baryon emerge when the trajectories are close to both locations of the CSC phase transition line and the QCD-CP. Only the maximum appears in the scenario without the CSC phase but with the QCD-CP. On the other hand, when only the CSC phase is considered, the dilepton yield monotonically increases as the beam energy decreases. These distinctive patterns could potentially serve as signals of the CSC phase and QCD-CP. In addition, it is found that the dilepton production yield and the location of the minimum strongly depend on the value of diquark coupling, suggesting the possibility that the value of the diquark coupling may be extracted from experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors predict that a local maximum and minimum in the beam energy dependence of ultra-low-energy dilepton yields will appear only when the isentropic expansion trajectory passes close to both the color superconducting (CSC) phase transition line and the QCD critical point (QCD-CP); the maximum alone appears without the CSC phase, and a monotonic increase appears without the QCD-CP, but these patterns are model-dependent because the actual beam energy needed to reach the true QCD-CP may differ significantly from the values presented.

## Method

The authors used the two-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation to compute the phase diagram including chiral symmetry breaking and the CSC phase transition. They assumed 1+1 dimensional Bjorken longitudinal expansion with isentropic trajectories (constant entropy per baryon S/A) and integrated the dilepton production rate from the retarded photon self-energy along each trajectory, including contributions from the soft modes of the CSC phase transition and the QCD critical point as well as from a free quark gas.

## Key numbers

* The scalar coupling constant was set to G_S = 5.5 GeV^{-2}, the three-momentum cutoff to Lambda = 631 MeV, and the current quark mass to m_0 = 5.5 MeV, chosen to reproduce the pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV and the pion decay constant f_pi = 93 MeV in vacuum.
* The diquark coupling was varied in the range G_D/G_S = 0.6 to 0.7; the reference value G_D/G_S = 0.75 from Fierz transformation was not used because it places the QCD-CP inside the CSC phase.
* A local maximum in the dilepton yield as a function of S/A was observed at S/A approximately 5.5, and a local minimum at S/A approximately 4.0, for the case with both CSC phase and QCD-CP present (G_D = 0.7 G_S).
* The enhancement of the dilepton yield from soft modes over the free quark gas was several orders of magnitude in the ultra-low energy region (50 MeV < omega < 100 MeV) for S/A values below about 3-4.
* The energy integration ranges used were 50-100 MeV, 100-200 MeV, and 200-300 MeV; the enhancement was most significant in the lowest range.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies (Nishimura 2022, Nishimura 2023) which showed that soft modes near the CSC phase transition and QCD critical point enhance the dilepton production rate in the low-energy/momentum region. It is the first calculation to include both the CSC phase transition and the QCD critical point simultaneously in estimating dilepton yields along isentropic trajectories, building on the approach of Motornenko (2019) and Motta (2020) for isentropic expansion. The results suggest that the distinctive pattern of a local maximum and minimum in the beam energy dependence of ultra-low-energy dilepton yields could serve as a signal for the CSC phase and QCD-CP in experiments such as RHIC BES, HADES, FAIR, J-PARC-HI, and HIAF, and that the diquark coupling value might be extracted from experimental data.
