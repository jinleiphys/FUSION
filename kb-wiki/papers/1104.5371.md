# Elliptic flow of thermal photons in heavy-ion collisions at Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and Large Hadron Collider

> We calculate the thermal photon transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flow in $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC and in $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC, using an ideal-hydrodynamical framework which is constrained by the measured hadron spectra at RHIC and LHC. The sensitivity of the results to the QCD-matter equation of state and to the photon emission rates is studied, and the photon $v_2$ is discussed in the light of the photonic $p_T$ spectrum measured by the PHENIX Collaboration. In particular, we make a prediction for the thermal photon $p_T$ spectra and elliptic flow for the current LHC Pb+Pb collisions.

## Key claim

The authors calculate thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow using an ideal-hydrodynamical framework constrained by measured hadron spectra, but emphasize that the results are sensitive to the choice of equation of state (EoS) and photon emission rates, and that the thermal photon elliptic flow may be significantly "washed away" by other direct photon components.

## Method

The authors solve (2+1)-dimensional ideal hydrodynamic equations with longitudinal boost-invariance using the SHASTA algorithm, with initial conditions from the EKRT saturation model for RHIC and fixed to measured multiplicities for LHC. Thermal photon emission rates are computed using the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe QGP rate with running coupling, and two different hadron gas rates (R92 and TRG), combined with two equations of state (a Bag model EoS "eosQ" with first-order phase transition and a lattice-based EoS "eosL"). The centrality classes are defined via the optical Glauber model, and the initial transverse density profile is chosen as wounded-nucleon-scaled entropy density (sWN).

## Key numbers

* For RHIC Au+Au at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV: initial time tau_0 = 0.17 fm; freeze-out temperature T_dec = 140 MeV (eosQ) or 160 MeV (eosL).
* For LHC Pb+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV: initial time tau_0 = 0.12 fm; same freeze-out temperatures as RHIC.
* In the eosQ case with R92 rates at RHIC, about 50% of thermal photons at p_T = 1 GeV come from the hadron gas; with eosL and TRG rates, only 3% come from the hadron gas at the same p_T.
* At RHIC, the thermal photon elliptic flow peaks around p_T ~ 2 GeV at roughly 5% in the 20-40% centrality class with eosL+TRG.
* The uncertainty in thermal photon yields at LHC from EoS and HG rate choices is at most about 40%.
* The inelastic nucleon-nucleon cross sections used are sigma_NN^in = 42 mb for RHIC and 64 mb for LHC.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamical studies of thermal photon production by Huovinen (2001), Rasanen (2002), and Chatterjee (2005, 2008) to include both RHIC and LHC energies with updated equations of state and emission rates. It provides predictions for thermal photon spectra and elliptic flow at LHC Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, complementing earlier predictions by Arleo (2004) and Dusling (2009) for 5.5 TeV. The results enable future comparisons with direct photon measurements at LHC to identify a possible window where thermal photons dominate, and motivate further studies including event-by-event fluctuations and viscous hydrodynamics.
