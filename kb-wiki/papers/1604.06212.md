# Importance of separated efficiencies between positively and negatively charged particles for cumulant calculations

> We show the importance of separated efficiency corrections between positively and negatively charged particles for cumulant calculations by Monte Carlo toy models and analytical calculations. Our results indicate that Sσ in published net-proton results from the STAR experiment will be suppressed about 5 to 10% in central collisions, and 10 to 20% in peripheral collisions at the beam energy of \sqrt s_{NN} = 62.4 and 200 GeV if the separated efficiencies are used to efficiency correction.

## Key claim

Using an averaged tracking efficiency (instead of separate efficiencies for positively and negatively charged particles) in cumulant calculations introduces systematic deviations that depend on the difference between the two efficiencies and on the particle multiplicities; the published STAR net-proton results for the observable \(S\sigma\) would be suppressed by about 5 to 10% in central collisions and 10 to 20% in peripheral collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) and 200 GeV if separated efficiencies were used.

## Method

The authors derived analytic expressions for cumulants up to fourth order using both an averaged efficiency \(\varepsilon = (\varepsilon_+ + \varepsilon_-)/2\) and separated efficiencies \(\varepsilon_+\) and \(\varepsilon_-\), based on binomial response and factorial-moment recursion relations. They then performed Monte Carlo toy-model simulations assuming independent Poisson distributions for positive and negative particles (Skellam baselines), with parameters taken from published STAR net-proton data and measured tracking efficiencies, to quantify the deviation of efficiency-corrected cumulants from the input values. The analytic deviations \(\Delta K_n\) were obtained by Taylor expansion around \(\Delta\varepsilon = (\varepsilon_+ - \varepsilon_-)/2\) and compared with the toy-model results.

## Key numbers

- For a toy model with \(\mu_+ = 10\), \(\mu_- = 8\), \(\varepsilon_+ = 0.66\), \(\varepsilon_- = 0.65\) (1% difference), the first-order cumulant corrected with averaged efficiency gave \(K_{1,\rm ave} = 2.14 \pm 0.02\), a 7% deviation from the Skellam baseline of 2, while separated efficiency gave \(K_{1,\rm sep} = 2.00 \pm 0.02\).
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV, the toy-model deviations \(\Delta K_n\) (averaged minus separated) were: \(\Delta K_1 = 0.1123 \pm 0.0002\), \(\Delta K_2 = 0.0028 \pm 0.0002\), \(\Delta K_3 = 0.112 \pm 0.010\), \(\Delta K_4 = -0.002 \pm 0.010\); analytic calculations gave 0.112, 0.004, 0.112, 0.004 respectively.
- At \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 7.7\) GeV, the toy-model deviations were: \(\Delta K_1 = 0.0202 \pm 2.4\times10^{-5}\), \(\Delta K_2 = 0.0195 \pm 0.0002\), \(\Delta K_3 = 0.020 \pm 0.002\), \(\Delta K_4 = 0.022 \pm 0.022\); analytic calculations gave 0.021, 0.020, 0.021, 0.020 respectively.
- The published STAR \(S\sigma\) results would be suppressed by about 5 to 10% in central collisions and 10 to 20% in peripheral collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 62.4\) and 200 GeV if separated efficiencies were used.

## Context

This work extends the efficiency-correction formalism developed by Koch (2010) and Kitazawa (2012), which assumed identical efficiency for positive and negative particles and was used in the STAR net-proton and net-charge analyses (STAR Collaboration, 2014). The paper demonstrates that the small (few percent) difference in tracking efficiency between protons and antiprotons in the TPC leads to systematic biases in cumulants, especially odd-order ones, and that the ratio \(S\sigma/\text{Skellam}\) is insensitive to this effect because both numerator and denominator are affected simultaneously. The results enable future experimental analyses to apply separated efficiency corrections and to quantify the associated systematic uncertainties in the search for the QCD critical point.
