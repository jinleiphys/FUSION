# The $(π^-,γγ)$ reaction in nuclei and the $σ$ meson in the medium

> A theoretical analysis of the $(π^-,γγ)$ reaction in nuclei is made in order to find the viability of this reaction to test modifications of the $σ$ meson mass in nuclear matter. The $ππ$ correlation in the scalar-isoscalar channel in nuclear matter could, in principle, manifest itself in this reaction since it plays an important role in the $ππ\toγγ$ mechanism. But we conclude that this effect is hardly visible in this reaction due to the strong background of the pion-Bremsstrahlung terms. Only with some special cuts and for some polarization states are the effect visible at the cost of a strong reduction in the cross section.

## Key claim
The paper concludes that the $(\pi^-,\gamma\gamma)$ reaction in nuclei is **not** a viable test of modifications of the $\sigma$ meson mass in nuclear matter, because the strong background from pion-Bremsstrahlung terms masks the effect of the scalar-isoscalar $\pi\pi$ correlation. Only with special kinematic cuts (photon direction or polarization filters) can the effect become visible, but at the cost of a strong reduction in cross section.

## Method
The authors extended the free $\pi^-p\to\gamma\gamma n$ amplitude to the nuclear medium using many-body techniques, relating the total cross section to the imaginary part of the pion selfenergy via the local density approximation. They included initial-state distortion of the pion through an eikonal factor accounting for absorption and quasielastic collisions, and implemented the in-medium $\pi\pi\to\gamma\gamma$ amplitude by replacing the free $\pi\pi$ scattering amplitude and two-pion loop function with their nuclear-matter values from Chiang et al. (1998). The full calculation was performed for $^{208}$Pb at a pion kinetic energy of 380 MeV with a photon energy cut of 50 MeV.

## Key numbers
- At $T_\pi = 380$ MeV and $\omega_{\rm min}=50$ MeV, the effect of the chiral terms (including the $\sigma$ meson) on the $\pi^-p\to\gamma\gamma n$ invariant mass distribution is only about 1–2%, compared to a ~20% effect in the $\gamma\gamma\to\pi^+\pi^-$ reaction at ~320 MeV.
- The pion-Bremsstrahlung terms ($b$, $c$, $d$, $e$ diagrams) are about 7 times larger than the $a$ term at 320 MeV in the $(\pi^-,\gamma\gamma)$ reaction.
- With a filter selecting photons back-to-back and parallel to the incident pion direction (acceptance angle 5 degrees), the cross section is reduced by nearly five orders of magnitude.
- With a filter on photon polarization orthogonal to the incident pion momentum, the cross section is reduced by a factor of 20.

## Context
This work extends the theoretical framework used for $(\gamma,\pi\pi)$ and $(\pi,\pi\pi)$ reactions in nuclei (Roca 2002, Vicente Vacas 1999) to the $(\pi^-,\gamma\gamma)$ channel, motivated by the preliminary experimental hint from Starostin (2002) of a small medium modification. The authors find that, unlike the $(\gamma,\pi^0\pi^0)$ reaction where a clear shift of strength to low invariant masses was observed (Metag 2002, Roca 2002), the $(\pi^-,\gamma\gamma)$ reaction is dominated by pion-Bremsstrahlung background that cancels the $\sigma$ signal. The paper thus rules out this reaction as a practical probe of in-medium $\sigma$ properties, unless extremely restrictive (and experimentally challenging) polarization or angular filters are applied.
