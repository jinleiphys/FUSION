# Chiral Disorder and Diffusion in The QCD Vacuum

> In this talk, which popularizes some of our recent work, we provide novel insights into the bulk properties of light chiral quarks in a fixed Euclidean volume (e.g. lattice QCD). We show that the spontaneous breakdown of chiral symmetry results into diffusing quarks with a vacuum diffusion constant $D=2F_π^2/|<\bar{q}q>|$ $\approx 0.22$ fm, in striking analogy to diffusing electrons in disordered metals in one-, two- and three-dimensions.

# Chiral Disorder and Diffusion in The QCD Vacuum

## Key claim
The spontaneous breakdown of chiral symmetry in QCD causes light quarks to diffuse through the vacuum with a diffusion constant D = 2F_π^2/|<q̅q>| ≈ 0.22 fm, in analogy to electrons diffusing in disordered metals. This analogy holds for a fixed Euclidean volume (e.g., lattice QCD) and is presented as a popularization of the authors' prior work.

## Method
The authors define a normalized return probability P(τ) for a light quark to return to its starting position after proper time τ, using the Euclidean Dirac operator as a Hamiltonian for evolution in 4+1 dimensions. They perform ensemble averaging over static gauge configurations and derive the large-τ behavior of the return probability as a diffusion equation, with the diffusion constant expressed in terms of the pion decay constant F_π and the chiral condensate |<q̅q>|. They then map the proper-time scales (ergodic, diffusive, ballistic) onto spectral scales of the Dirac operator using Heisenberg's uncertainty relation.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum diffusion constant D = 2F_π^2/|<q̅q>| ≈ 0.22 fm
- The damping factor γ = 2m can be rewritten as γ ≡ D/L_coh^2, where L_coh = 1/m_π (the pion Compton wavelength)
- The ergodic time τ_erg = L^2/D, where L is the linear size of the Euclidean volume V = L^4
- The Thouless virtuality Λ_c = D/L^2 = D/√V, analogous to the Thouless energy in condensed matter
- The elastic time τ_elastic = 1/(2M_q), where M_q is the constituent quark mass
- The average level spacing Δ is related to the quark condensate via the Banks-Casher relation: |<q̅q>| = π/(VΔ)

## Context
This work extends the analogy between QCD and disordered electronic systems, building on Anderson's (1958) concept of localization and Thouless's (1977) energy scale. The authors connect their diffusion picture to chiral random matrix models in the microscopic limit (JACIS, USMAT, USTALK, SEMENOV) and to the GOR relation (GOR, 1968). The paper enables lattice QCD verification of the predicted spectral regimes (ergodic, diffusive, ballistic) and suggests instantons (Negele) as a possible microscopic source of disorder. Numerical confirmations of the Thouless virtuality have appeared in instanton model studies (OJ) and lattice QCD (BB2), though the full diffusion scenario remains to be explored.
