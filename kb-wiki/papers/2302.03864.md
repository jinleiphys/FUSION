# Three-dimensional potential energy surface for fission of $^{236}$U within covariant density functional theory

> We have calculated the three-dimensional potential energy surface (PES) for the fission of compound nucleus $^{236}$U using the covariant density functional theory with constraints on the axial quadrupole and octupole deformations $(β_2, β_3)$ as well as the nucleon number in the neck $q_N$. By considering the additonal degree of freedom $q_N$, coexistence of the elongated and compact fission modes is predicted for $0.9\lesssim β_3 \lesssim 1.3$. Remarkably, the PES becomes very shallow across a large range of quadrupole and octupole deformations for small $q_N$, and consequently, the scission line in $(β_2, β_3)$ plane will extend to a shallow band, which leads to a fluctuation for the estimated total kinetic energies by several to ten MeV and for the fragment masses by several to about ten nucleons.

## Key claim
By adding the nucleon number in the neck \(q_N\) as a third collective coordinate to the standard two-dimensional potential energy surface in \((\beta_2, \beta_3)\), the authors predict that the scission line broadens into a shallow band, causing fluctuations in estimated total kinetic energies by several to ten MeV and in fragment masses by several to about ten nucleons, rather than yielding a unique scission configuration.

## Method
The authors performed constrained covariant density functional theory (CDFT) calculations using the PC-PK1 energy density functional with a \(\delta\)-force pairing interaction. They imposed simultaneous constraints on the axial quadrupole deformation \(\beta_2\) (range \(-1.02\) to \(7.34\), step \(0.08\)), octupole deformation \(\beta_3\) (range \(0.00\) to \(3.76\), step \(0.08\)), and the Gaussian neck operator \(q_N\) (step \(0.5\)) to generate a three-dimensional potential energy surface for the compound nucleus \(^{236}\)U. The Dirac equation was solved by expanding nucleon spinors in a two-center harmonic oscillator basis with 20 major shells.

## Key numbers
- Inner fission barrier height: 6.85 MeV; outer fission barrier height: 6.05 MeV for \(^{236}\)U along the static fission path.
- Coexistence of elongated and compact fission modes predicted for octupole deformations \(0.9 \lesssim \beta_3 \lesssim 1.3\).
- The potential energy surface becomes very shallow for neck nucleon number \(q_N < 6\), forming a wide "estuary" in the \((\beta_2, q_N)\) subspace.
- Estimated total kinetic energy fluctuations: several to ten MeV, with more than ten MeV fluctuation at heavy fragment mass \(A_{\rm H} \sim 132\).
- Fragment mass fluctuations: several to about ten nucleons for asymmetric fission with heavy fragment mass \(A_{\rm H} > 130\).
- For heavy fragment masses \(122 \lesssim A_{\rm H} \lesssim 128\), both lower-than 160 MeV and higher-than 178 MeV total kinetic energies are obtained simultaneously.

## Context
This work extends previous two-dimensional fission calculations using covariant density functional theory (e.g., Tao (2017) for \(^{226}\)Th) by adding the neck degree of freedom \(q_N\), following the approach introduced by Younes (2009) and Schunck (2014) for non-relativistic Skyrme functionals. The results show that the three-dimensional surface resolves discontinuities seen in two-dimensional calculations (as noted by Dubray (2012)) and provides a microscopic explanation for the observed widths of fragment mass and total kinetic energy distributions in actinide fission. The authors note that more precise predictions require dynamical evolution using methods such as the time-dependent generator coordinate method, which is in progress.
