# Spectral functions from the functional renormalization group at finite temperature and density

> We present a viable method to obtain real-time quantities such as spectral functions or transport coefficients at finite temperature and density within a non-perturbative Functional Renormalization Group approach. Our method is based on a thermodynamically consistent truncation of the flow equations for 2-point functions with analytically continued frequency components in the originally Euclidean external momenta. We demonstrate its feasibility by calculating the mesonic spectral functions in the quark-meson model at different temperatures and quark chemical potentials, in particular around the critical endpoint in the phase diagram of the model.

## Key claim

The paper presents a method to obtain real-time spectral functions from the Functional Renormalization Group (FRG) at finite temperature and density by performing analytic continuation on the level of the flow equations for 2-point functions, thereby avoiding the ill-posed inverse problem of numerical reconstruction methods like the maximum entropy method (MEM). The method is demonstrated to be feasible for the quark-meson model, but the results are limited by the zeroth-order derivative expansion truncation where only the effective potential carries scale dependence.

## Method

The authors derive flow equations for the retarded 2-point functions by taking two functional derivatives of the Wetterich equation for the effective average action in the quark-meson model. They perform analytic continuation in two steps: first exploiting the periodicity of bosonic and fermionic occupation numbers in discrete Euclidean external energies, then replacing the discrete imaginary external energy by a continuous real energy via the prescription Gamma^(2),R(omega) = -lim_{epsilon->0} Gamma^(2),E(p_0 = i omega - epsilon). The resulting flow equations are solved numerically with the spectral functions obtained from the imaginary and real parts of the retarded 2-point functions.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant f_pi = 93.5 MeV, pion mass m_pi = 138 MeV, and sigma mass m_sigma = 509 MeV used as vacuum input parameters for the quark-meson model
* Constituent quark mass m_psi approximately 300 MeV used as input
* Ultraviolet cutoff Lambda = 1 GeV used for the flow equations
* Critical endpoint located at mu = 293 MeV and T = 10 MeV for the chosen parameter set
* At mu = 292 MeV near the critical endpoint, the sigma meson becomes light enough to produce a peak near omega = 300 MeV
* At mu = 292.97 MeV, the sigma pole mass moves close to zero, indicating critical fluctuations

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous FRG spectral function calculations for the O(4) linear-sigma model in vacuum (Kamikado 2013) to finite temperature and density in the quark-meson model, which serves as a low-energy effective model for QCD. The method provides an alternative to analytically continued Dyson-Schwinger equations (Strauss 2012) and to using MEM on Euclidean FRG results (Haas 2013) or DSE results (Nickel 2006, Mueller 2010, Qin 2013). The approach enables future computation of transport coefficients and spectral functions for rho and a_1 mesons relevant for electromagnetic probes in heavy-ion collisions.
