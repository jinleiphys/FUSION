# Exactly solvable models of nuclei

> In this paper a review is given of a class of sub-models of both approaches, characterized by the fact that they can be solved exactly, highlighting in the process a number of generic results related to both the nature of pair-correlated systems as well as collective modes of motion in the atomic nucleus.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that exactly solvable sub-models of both self-consistent mean-field methods and the spherical nuclear shell model exist, but these models are necessarily schematic and valid only for specific nuclei, serving primarily as benchmarks rather than as replacements for numerical approaches.

## Method

The paper reviews algebraic and group-theoretical techniques for constructing exactly solvable nuclear many-body Hamiltonians, focusing on dynamical symmetries where the Hamiltonian can be written as a sum of commuting Casimir operators of nested Lie algebras. It examines two specific classes of solvable interactions within the nuclear shell model: pairing interactions (treated via quasi-spin algebras SU(2) for identical nucleons and SO(5) or SO(8) for neutrons and protons) and quadrupole interactions (Wigner's supermultiplet model based on SU(4) symmetry). The review also covers Richardson's Bethe ansatz solution for pairing with non-degenerate single-particle orbits and Talmi's generalized seniority condition.

## Key numbers

* Pairing interaction energy for identical nucleons in a single-\(j\) shell: \(E(n,\upsilon) = -\frac{g}{4}(n-\upsilon)(2j-n-\upsilon+3)\), where \(n\) is nucleon number, \(\upsilon\) is seniority, and \(g\) is the pairing strength.
* For the nucleus \(^{210}\)Pb, the experimental low-energy spectrum is compared to a zero-range delta interaction and a pairing interaction (figure shown but numerical values not given in text).
* Richardson equation for \(n/2\) pairs: \(\sum_j \frac{\Omega_j}{2\epsilon_j-E_p} - \sum_{p'(\neq p)}^{n/2} \frac{2}{E_{p'}-E_p} = \frac{1}{g}\), where \(\Omega_j = j+1/2\) and \(\epsilon_j\) are single-particle energies.
* Generalized seniority ground-state energy: \(E_{\text{GS}}(n) = nE_0 + \frac{1}{2}n(n-1)\Delta\), where \(E_0\) is the lowest two-particle eigenstate energy and \(\Delta\) is a constant.
* For the SO(8) pairing model with equal isoscalar and isovector strengths (\(g=g'\)), the ground state of \(N=Z\) nuclei is a condensate of \(\alpha\)-like quartets: \((\hat S^\ell_+ \cdot \hat S^\ell_+ - \hat P^\ell_+ \cdot \hat P^\ell_+)^{n/4}|{\rm o}\rangle\).

## Context

This review extends the work of Racah (1943, 1949) on seniority in atomic physics and the pairing model introduced by Bardeen, Cooper, and Schrieffer (1957) for superconductivity, later adapted to nuclei by Bohr (1958). It also covers Wigner's supermultiplet model (1937) and the generalized seniority approach of Talmi (1971). The paper enables future work by providing a unified algebraic framework for exactly solvable nuclear models, with Richardson's method (1963) and its generalizations by Dukelsky (2001) offering exact solutions for pairing Hamiltonians with non-degenerate orbits, while noting that no exact solution exists for pairing with both non-degenerate orbits and orbit-dependent strengths except for two orbits (Balantekin, 2007).
