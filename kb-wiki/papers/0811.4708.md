# Transport coefficients near chiral phase transition

> We analyze the transport properties of relativistic fluid composed of constituent quarks at finite temperature and density. We focus on the shear and bulk viscosities and study their behavior near chiral phase transition. We model the constituent quark interactions through the Nambu--Jona Lasinio Lagrangian. The transport coefficients are calculated within kinetic theory under relaxation time approximation including in--medium modification of quasi--particles dispersion relations. We quantify the influence of the order of chiral phase transition and the critical end point on dissipative phenomena in such a medium. Considering the changes of shear and bulk viscosities along the phase boundary we discuss their sensitivity to probe the existence of the critical end point.

## Key claim

Within the Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model under the relaxation-time approximation, neither the shear viscosity nor the bulk viscosity shows singular behavior at the critical end point (CEP), and the ratio η/s is insensitive to the CEP; however, the bulk viscosity ζ/s exhibits a local minimum at the CEP, but the authors conclude that none of these viscosities can serve as a useful probe of the CEP under this approach.

## Method

The authors model a relativistic fluid of constituent quarks using the two-flavor NJL Lagrangian in the mean-field approximation, generating a temperature- and chemical-potential-dependent dynamical quark mass M(T,μ) as the order parameter for chiral symmetry. Transport coefficients (shear viscosity η and bulk viscosity ζ) are computed from relativistic kinetic theory under the relaxation-time approximation, with the relaxation time obtained from thermally averaged elastic scattering cross sections of quarks and antiquarks (including 1/Nc corrections). The viscosities are evaluated along the chiral phase boundary (crossover, first-order line, and CEP) and compared to the entropy density s.

## Key numbers

* The CEP in the NJL model is located at (T, μ) = (80.9, 329) MeV.
* The NJL model parameters used: coupling GSΛ² = 2.44, three-momentum cutoff Λ = 587.9 MeV, and current quark masses mu = md = 5.6 MeV.
* At μ = 0, the collision time τ shows a shallow minimum near the pseudo-critical temperature Tc, with τ large at low T and small at high T.
* The asymptotic high-temperature shear viscosity from perturbative QCD (pQCD) is given by η^(asymptotic) = (4π²/675) * (3.86 / [αs² ln(1/αs)]) T³, with αs computed for Nf = 2 and λ = 30 MeV; the asymptotic bulk viscosity is ζ^(asymptotic) = 0.
* Along the phase boundary, η/s changes monotonically with no sensitivity to the CEP, while ζ/s exhibits a local minimum at the CEP and a visible gap at the first-order transition.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of transport coefficients near the QCD phase transition, particularly those using the NJL model (e.g., the authors’ own Ref. [eta:njl]) and kinetic theory with relaxation-time approximation (e.g., Sasaki and Redlich (2008) in Ref. [sr]). It contrasts with expectations from critical dynamics (Halperin and Hohenberg classification, model H) that η and ζ should diverge at the CEP with dynamical critical exponents zη ≈ 1/19 and zζ ≈ 3 (Onuki (2002)). The results are also compared to lattice gauge theory calculations in pure SU(3) (Refs. [lgts, lgtb]) and SU(2) (Ref. [lgtk]) that show decreasing η/s and increasing ζ/s near the deconfinement transition. The paper enables future work to go beyond the relaxation-time approximation and include dynamical scaling to capture singular behavior at the CEP.
