# Gauge-invariant decomposition of nucleon spin

> Based on gauge-invariant decomposition of covariant angular momentum tensor of QCD in an arbitrary Lorentz frame, we investigate the relation between the known decompositions of the nucleon spin into its constituents, thereby clarifying in what respect they are common and in what respect they are different critically. We argue that the decomposition of Bashinsky and Jaffe, that of Chen et al., and that of Jaffe and Manohar are contained in our more general decomposition, after an appropriate gauge-fixing in a suitable Lorentz frame, which means that they all {\it gauge-equivalent}. We however point out that there is another gauge-invariant decomposition of the nucleon spin, which is closer to the Ji decomposition, while allowing the decomposition of the gluon total angular momentum into its spin and orbital parts. An advantage of the latter decomposition is that each of the four terms corresponds to a definite observable, which can be extracted from high-energy deep-inelastic-scattering measurements.

## Key claim

The paper argues that there exist two distinct gauge-invariant decompositions of the nucleon spin into quark and gluon spin and orbital angular momentum parts, and that the decomposition of Bashinsky and Jaffe, of Chen et al., and of Jaffe and Manohar are all gauge-equivalent to one of these after appropriate gauge-fixing in a suitable Lorentz frame. The author recommends a specific decomposition (decomposition I) because each of its four terms corresponds to a definite observable extractable from generalized parton distributions (GPDs) and polarized deep-inelastic-scattering (DIS) measurements.

## Method

The author constructs a gauge-invariant decomposition of the covariant angular-momentum tensor of QCD in an arbitrary Lorentz frame by splitting the gluon field into physical and pure-gauge parts, imposing a pure-gauge condition on the pure-gauge component and specific gauge transformation properties on both components. Two distinct decompositions are derived: decomposition (II) reduces to the Bashinsky-Jaffe, Chen et al., and Jaffe-Manohar decompositions after gauge-fixing, while decomposition (I) is closer to the Ji decomposition but allows separation of gluon total angular momentum into spin and orbital parts. The author then inserts decomposition (I) into the helicity normalization condition using the Pauli-Lubansky vector to derive a nucleon spin sum rule, and verifies that each term matches the appropriate moment of GPDs or polarized parton distribution functions.

## Key numbers

- The intrinsic quark spin contributes only about 1/3 of the nucleon spin, based on experimental results from EMC (1988), COMPASS (2007), and HERMES (2007).
- The nucleon spin sum rule is given as 1/2 = S_q + L_q + S_g + L_g = J_q + J_g, where S_q = (1/2) Δq, S_g = Δg, L_q = (1/2)[A_{20}^q(0) + B_{20}^q(0)] - (1/2)Δq, and L_g = (1/2)[A_{20}^g(0) + B_{20}^g(0)] - Δg.
- The quark OAM is expressed as L_q = (1/2) ∫_{-1}^{1} x [H^q(x,0,0) + E^q(x,0,0)] dx - (1/2) ∫_{-1}^{1} Δq(x) dx.
- The gluon OAM is expressed as L_g = (1/2) ∫_{-1}^{1} x [H^g(x,0,0) + E^g(x,0,0)] dx - ∫_{-1}^{1} Δg(x) dx.

## Context

This work extends the program of nucleon spin decomposition initiated by Jaffe and Manohar (1990) and Ji (1997), and responds to the recent gauge-invariant decomposition proposed by Chen et al. (2008, 2009). The author clarifies that the quark OAM extracted from combined GPD and polarized PDF analyses is the dynamical OAM (using the full covariant derivative), not the canonical OAM or its gauge-invariant extension advocated by Chen et al. The paper also notes possible discrepancies between lattice QCD predictions from LHPC (2008, 2010) for L^u - L^d and predictions from the refined cloudy-bag model of Myhrer and Thomas (1988), and suggests this may relate to the existence of two kinds of quark orbital angular momenta.
