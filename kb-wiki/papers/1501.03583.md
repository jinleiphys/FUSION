# X(3872), I^G(J^{PC})=0^+(1^{++}), as the χ_{1c}(2P) charmonium

> Contrary to almost standard opinion that the X(3872) resonance is the D^{*0}\bar D^0+c.c. molecule or the qc\bar q\bar c four-quark state, we discuss the scenario where the X(3872)resonance is the c\bar c = χ_{c1}(2P) charmonium which "sits on" the D^{*0}\bar D^0 threshold. We explain the shift of the mass of the X(3872) resonance with respect to the prediction of a potential model for the mass of the χ_{c1}(2P) charmonium by the contribution of the virtual D^*\bar D+c.c. intermediate states into the self energy of the X(3872) resonance. This allows us to estimate the coupling constant of the X(7872) resonance with the D^{*0}\bar D^0 channel, the branching ratio of the X(3872) \to D^{*0}\bar D^0 + c.c. decay, and the branching ratio of the X(3872) decay into all non-D^{*0}\bar D^0 + c.c. states. We predict a significant number of unknown decays of X(3872) via two gluon:X(3872)\to gluon\ gluon\to hadrons. We suggest a physically clear program of experimental researches for verification of our assumption.

## Key claim

The X(3872) resonance is the conventional $\chi_{c1}(2P)$ charmonium state, not a $D^{*0}\bar D^0$ molecule or four-quark state, with its 50 MeV mass shift below potential model predictions explained entirely by the contribution of virtual $D^*\bar D$ intermediate states to its self-energy.

## Method

The authors compute the self-energy of the X(3872) resonance from $\bar D^0 D^{*0}$ and $D^- D^{*+}$ loop diagrams using a phenomenological Lagrangian with coupling constant $g_A$. They impose a mass renormalization condition that shifts the bare $\chi_{c1}(2P)$ mass down to the observed X(3872) mass, then extract the coupling constant and branching ratios from the resulting propagator. They compare radiative decay ratios of X(3872) to those of the bottomonium analogue $\chi_{b1}(2P)$ and predict two-gluon decay modes.

## Key numbers

* The mass shift of X(3872) relative to potential model predictions for $\chi_{c1}(2P)$ is $\Delta \approx 50$ MeV (Eq. 1).
* The binding energy of X(3872) relative to the $D^{*0}\bar D^0$ threshold is $\epsilon_B \lesssim (1 \div 3)$ MeV.
* The molecular radius would be $r_{X(3872)} \gtrsim (3 \div 5)$ fm, while the charmonium radius is $r_{\chi_{c1}(2P)} \approx 0.5$ fm, giving a volume ratio $V_{X(3872)}/V_{\chi_{c1}(2P)} \gtrsim 100 \div 1000$.
* The cross section ratio $\sigma(pp \to X(3872) + anything) / \sigma(pp \to \psi(2S) + anything)$ lies between 0.74 and 2.1 (Eq. 12).
* The coupling extracted is $g_A^2/8\pi \approx 0.2$ GeV$^2$ for a cutoff $\Lambda = 10$ GeV.
* The resulting branching ratio $BR(X \to D^0 \bar D^{*0} + \bar D^0 D^{*0}) \approx 0.3$.
* The radiative decay ratio $BR(X \to \gamma \psi(2S)) / BR(X \to \gamma J/\psi) = 2.46 \pm 0.7$ (LHCb 2014).
* The analogous ratio for $\chi_{b1}(2P)$ is $2.16 \pm 0.28$ (PDG 2014).
* The extracted coefficients are $C_X = 136.78 \pm 38.89$ and $C_{\chi_{b1}(2P)} = 80 \pm 10.37$.
* $BR(\chi_{b1}(2P) \to \omega \Upsilon(1S)) = (1.63 \pm^{0.4}_{0.34})\%$ (PDG 2014).

## Context

This work directly contradicts the dominant molecular interpretation of X(3872) that followed its discovery and the subsequent stream of $D^{*0}\bar D^0$ molecular models. The authors argue against the mixing scenario of giant molecule and compact charmonium proposed by Kalashnikova (2005) and by Badalian et al. (2012), noting that such mixing requires special justification due to the volume ratio. The paper enables experimental tests: searching for two-gluon decays of X(3872) into non-$D^{*0}\bar D^0$ states and measuring $BR(\chi_{b1}(2P) \to \rho \Upsilon(1S))$ to distinguish between one-photon and three-gluon production mechanisms.
