# Spin/Parity Dependent Level Density

> It is shown that the Constant Temperature (CT) model of nuclear level density is a direct consequence of a symmetrized Poisson distribution of nuclear level spacings. The standard CT model describing the total level density is shown to be fatally flawed due to discontinuities at the Yrast energies, the onset of new $J^π$ sequences, that disrupt the exponential formula and cause the back shift parameter to become nonphysically negative. A new CT-JPI level density model is proposed with a constant temperature and separate back shift parameters for each $J^π$ sequence. The CT-JPI model is also constrained to reproduce the spin distribution predicted by Ericson's spin distribution function at the neutron separation energy. A fitting procedure is described for determining the temperature $T$, back shifts $E_0(J^π)$, and spin cutoff parameters $σ_c$ from nuclear structure and resonance data. The CT-JPI model is demonstrated to successfully predict the level densities for a wide range of spins and parities for 46 nuclear with Z=7-92. In variance with earlier predictions the spin cut-off parameters show no mass dependence and instead substantial variation at all mass regions.

## Key claim

The standard Constant Temperature (CT) model of total nuclear level density is fatally flawed because discontinuities at Yrast energies (the onset of new Jπ sequences) disrupt the exponential formula and cause the back shift parameter to become nonphysically negative; the proposed CT-JPI model resolves this by assigning separate back shift parameters for each Jπ sequence while maintaining a single constant temperature.

## Method

The CT-JPI model was derived by showing that a symmetrized (normal) distribution of level spacings, rather than the conventional Poisson or Wigner distributions, yields the exponential level density growth of the CT model with a ground-state sequence number of 1.0 and a physically meaningful temperature. The model was then fit to experimental nuclear structure levels and resonance data for 46 nuclei with Z=7-92 by minimizing the average deviation of the integral level sequence numbers for each Jπ sequence, simultaneously constraining the spin distribution at the neutron separation energy to match Ericson's spin distribution function by varying the spin cutoff parameter σc.

## Key numbers

- For 235U: fitted temperature T = 0.455 MeV, back shift E0(1/2+) = 0.32680 MeV, spin cutoff σc = 4.14, average s-wave spacing D0 = 8.19 eV (compared to RIPL-3 value of 12.0±0.8 eV)
- For 238U: fitted temperature T = 0.420 MeV, spin cutoff σc = 2.81, average deviation of fitted spin distribution from spin distribution function = 0.6%
- For 233-239U: average fit quality ΔN(Jπ) = 0.132(9), temperatures range 7-22% higher than RIPL-3 values, average spin cutoff σc = 3.6(4)
- For 204-209Pb: temperatures range from T = 0.722 MeV (204Pb) to T = 0.795 MeV (209Pb), spin cutoff parameters range from σc = 2.34 (209Pb) to σc = 4.83 (208Pb)
- The fitted back shift for 235U Jπ=1/2+ gave an extremely sharp minimum with FWHM = 3 eV; the temperature fit gave FWHM = 4 keV

## Context

This work extends the Constant Temperature model originally proposed by Ericson (1960) and the unified model of Gilbert and Cameron, who combined CT and Back-Shifted Fermi Gas approaches. It directly addresses deficiencies in the Reference Input Parameter Library (RIPL-3) level density parameters, particularly the nonphysical negative back shifts and the failure to predict spin- and parity-dependent level densities. The CT-JPI model enables improved nuclear reaction calculations and astrophysical modeling by providing level densities for individual Jπ sequences, and contradicts the earlier mass-dependent spin cutoff parameter formula of von Egidy et al. (σc = 0.98A0.29) by finding substantial variation at all mass regions with no mass dependence.
