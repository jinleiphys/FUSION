# Contribution of boundness and motion of nucleons to the EMC effect

> The kinematical corrections to the structure function of nucleon in nucleus due to the boundness and motion of nucleons arise from the excitation of the doorway states for one-nucleon transfer reactions in the deep inelastic scattering on nuclei.

# Contribution of boundness and motion of nucleons to the EMC effect

## Key claim

The kinematical corrections to the nucleon structure function in a nucleus, arising from nucleon boundness and motion, must be calculated using the spectral function of the doorway states for one-nucleon transfer reactions rather than the ground-state one-nucleon spectral function; previous calculations underestimated the nucleon momentum distribution by about 25% because they used the ground-state spectral function.

## Method

The authors computed the deep inelastic scattering structure function ratio \(R_{kin} = (F_{2nA}+F_{2pA})/F_{2D}\) for nuclei \(^{12}\text{C}\), \(^{14}\text{N}\), \(^{27}\text{Al}\), \(^{40}\text{Ca}\), \(^{56}\text{Fe}\), and \(^{63}\text{Cu}\) using the doorway-state spectral function derived from the static nuclear field Hamiltonian. The doorway-state eigenfunctions were obtained by solving the Dirac equation with the Bonn B and OSBEP nucleon-nucleon potentials, using experimental nucleon density distributions and free-space NN forces. The free nucleon structure functions \(F_{2p}\) and \(F_{2n}\) were taken from the MRST2002 NLO parametrization, and the ratio was evaluated assuming no modification of parton distributions inside the nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The root-mean-square radius of the doorway-state density distribution for \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) is \(8.76\ \text{fm}^2\), compared to \(10.88\ \text{fm}^2\) for the ground state, indicating that the nucleon motion was underestimated in prior work by about 25%.
- The "doorway" mass of \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) is \(0.968\) times the ground-state mass, about 3% less.
- For deuteron, the kinematical ratio \(R_{D,kin} = F_{2D}/(F_{2p}+F_{2n})\) reaches 1.07 at \(x=0.75\) and 1.42 at \(x=0.85\) (Bonn B potential).
- For \(^{12}\text{C}\) at \(x=0.125\), \(Q^2=12.0\ \text{GeV}^2\), the calculated \(R_{kin}=0.997\) compared to the experimental ratio \(R_{exp}=1.032\pm0.012\).
- For \(^{56}\text{Fe}\) at \(x=0.100\), \(Q^2=22.0\ \text{GeV}^2\), the calculated \(R_{kin}=0.996\) compared to \(R_{exp}=1.057\pm0.021\).
- For \(^{40}\text{Ca}\) at \(x=0.113\), \(Q^2=4.3\ \text{GeV}^2\), the calculated \(R_{kin}=0.998\) compared to \(R_{exp}=0.994\pm0.010\).
- The difference between results using Bonn B and OSBEP potentials never exceeds 0.5% for \(x<0.6\).

## Context

This work extends prior attempts to account for Fermi motion and nucleon boundness in the EMC effect by Vagradov (using ground-state spectral functions) and by Frankfurt and Strikman (using baryon charge conservation and relativistic flux factors). The authors argue that previous calculations, including those by Birbrair et al. (BLev), underestimated the kinematical effect because they used the ground-state spectral function rather than the doorway-state spectral function. The paper enables a cleaner separation of genuine dynamical modifications of parton distributions in nuclei from purely kinematical effects, showing that at large \(x>0.65\) the pure kinematical ratio exceeds the experimental ratio, implying that in-medium parton distributions become softer. The deuteron Fermi-motion correction was subsequently used by the MRST group to improve fits to high-\(x\) deuteron data, reducing \(\chi^2\) by 20 for 12 data points at \(x=0.75\).
