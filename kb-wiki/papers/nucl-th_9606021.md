# The exclusive (e,e$'$p) reaction at high missing momenta

> The reduced (e,e$'$p) cross section is calculated for kinematics that probe high missing momenta. The final-state interaction is handled within a non-relativistic many-body framework. One- and two-body nuclear currents are included. Electron distortion effects are treated in an exact distorted wave calculation. It is shown that at high missing momenta the calculated (e,e$'$p) cross sections exhibit a pronounced sensitivity to ground-state correlations of the RPA type and two-body currents. The role of these mechanisms is found to be relatively small at low missing momenta.

## Key claim

At high missing momenta, the calculated (e,e$'$p) cross sections exhibit a pronounced sensitivity to ground-state correlations of the RPA type and two-body currents, but the role of these mechanisms is found to be relatively small at low missing momenta; the paper qualifies that these higher-order effects must be carefully examined before conclusions on nucleon-nucleon correlations can be drawn from (e,e$'$p) data.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic many-body calculations of the reduced (e,e$'$p) cross section for $^{16}$O and $^{208}$Pb targets, using a Hartree-Fock (HF) potential generated with a Skyrme-type effective interaction (SkE2). They incorporated long-range correlations via the Random Phase Approximation (RPA), included one- and two-body nuclear currents (with meson-exchange and $\Delta_{33}$-excitation contributions), and treated electron distortion effects exactly within a Coulomb Distorted Wave Born Approximation (CDWBA). The final-state interaction was handled through a phase-shift analysis based on a partial-wave expansion using the same HF potential.

## Key numbers

- For $^{208}$Pb(e,e$'$p) knockout from the $3s1/2$ shell, the HF approach required a spectroscopic factor of $S(3s1/2)=0.15$, while the optical potential model required $S(3s1/2)=0.51$.
- For $^{208}$Pb(e,e$'$p) knockout from the $2d3/2$ shell, the HF approach required a spectroscopic factor of $S(2d3/2)=0.15$, while the optical potential model required $S(2d3/2)=0.54$.
- The optical potential calculation caused a quenching of the PWIA (e,e$'$p) strength by a factor of approximately 2.5 for heavy target nuclei.
- Experimental predictions for nuclear transparency extracted from (e,e$'$p) experiments vary from 0.8 to 0.4 for target nuclei in the range of $A=12$ to $A=181$.
- For $^{208}$Pb, the direct knockout picture underestimated the measured (e,e$'$p) strength at missing momenta $p_m > 300$ MeV$/c$.
- For $^{208}$Pb, at the low missing momentum side the two-body mesonic current strength was at least an order of magnitude smaller than the one-body nucleonic contribution, while at the highest missing momenta the two-body strength could overshoot the one-body contribution.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the (e,e$'$p) reaction that focused on low missing momenta and spectroscopic factors, particularly addressing the inability of the independent particle model (IPM) to explain extracted spectroscopic factors as noted by Fruet (1984), Boffi (1993), and Kelly (1995). The paper directly confronts its $^{208}$Pb results with experimental data from Bobeldijk (1994) taken at NIKHEF, and compares its approach to the CDWIA analysis of the Pavia group (Boffi, 1993) and to fully relativistic analyses by Udias (1995). The findings enable future experiments at new-generation electron facilities to properly account for RPA correlations, two-body currents, and electron distortion when extracting information about short-range correlations and the high-momentum components of the nucleon momentum distribution from (e,e$'$p) data.
