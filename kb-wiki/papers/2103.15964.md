# Heavy+light pseudoscalar meson semileptonic transitions

> A symmetry-preserving regularisation of a vector$\times$vector contact interaction (SCI) is used to deliver a unified treatment of semileptonic transitions involving $π$, $K$, $D_{(s)}$, $B_{(s,c)}$ initial states. The framework is characterised by algebraic simplicity, few parameters, and the ability to simultaneously treat systems from Nambu-Goldstone modes to heavy+heavy mesons. Although the SCI form factors are typically somewhat stiff, the results are comparable with experiment and rigorous theory results. Hence, predictions for the five unmeasured $B_{s,c}$ branching fractions should be a reasonable guide. The analysis provides insights into the effects of Higgs boson couplings via current-quark masses on the transition form factors; and results on $B_{(s)}\to D_{(s)}$ transitions yield a prediction for the Isgur-Wise function in fair agreement with contemporary data.

## Key claim

The symmetry-preserving contact interaction (SCI) framework, despite producing form factors that are typically somewhat stiff, yields results comparable with experiment and rigorous theory, so its predictions for five unmeasured \(B_{s,c}\) branching fractions should be a reasonable guide.

## Method

The authors employed a symmetry-preserving regularisation of a vector\(\times\)vector contact interaction (SCI) to solve the quark gap equation and Bethe-Salpeter equation for pseudoscalar mesons, using a rainbow-ladder truncation of the continuum bound-state problem. They determined the SCI parameters (ultraviolet cutoff \(\Lambda_{\rm uv}\), coupling \(\alpha_{\rm IR}\), and current-quark masses) by fitting to empirical masses and decay constants of the \(\pi\), \(K\), \(D\), and \(B\) mesons, then computed semileptonic transition form factors \(f_+(t)\) and \(f_0(t)\) for all allowed heavy+light pseudoscalar meson decays via the weak vector vertex.

## Key numbers

*   \(f_+^{K\pi}(0) = 0.972(8)\) from an average of continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation studies (no tuning effort made).
*   \(f_+^{D_s K}(0) = 0.673(40)\), \(f_+^{D\pi}(0) = 0.618(31)\), \(f_+^{DK}(0) = 0.756(36)\) from continuum results (Yao 2020).
*   Lattice-QCD results (Lubicz 2017): \(f_+^{D\pi}(0) = 0.612(35)\), \(f_+^{DK}(0) = 0.765(31)\).
*   SCI mean absolute relative difference for meson masses: \(\overline{\rm ard} = 2.8\%\) compared with experiment.
*   SCI mean absolute relative difference for leptonic decay constants: \(\overline{\rm ard} = 13\%\) compared with experiment or lattice QCD.

## Context

This work extends the SCI treatment of semileptonic decays previously applied to \(K_{\ell 3}\) transitions (Chen 2012) to a unified study covering \(\pi\), \(K\), \(D_{(s)}\), and \(B_{(s,c)}\) initial states. It provides a benchmark for future studies using realistic quark+antiquark interactions (Yao 2020) and for checking algorithms employed in high-performance computing studies of heavy-light systems. The results enable predictions for five unmeasured \(B_{s,c}\) branching fractions and yield an Isgur-Wise function from \(B_{(s)}\to D_{(s)}\) transitions that agrees fairly with contemporary data.
