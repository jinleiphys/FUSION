# Evaluation of the hyperon binding energy via statistical production of hypernuclei

> In nuclear reactions of high energy one can simultaneously produce a lot of hypernuclei after the capture of hyperons by nuclear residues. We consider statistical disintegration of such hypernuclear systems and the connection of fragment production with the binding energies of hyperons. It is demonstrated that the hyperon binding energies can be effectively evaluated from the yields of different isotopes of hypernuclei. The double ratio method is suggested for this purpose. The advantage of this procedure is its universality and the possibility to involve many different isotopes. This method can also be applied for multi-strange nuclei, which binding energies were very difficult to measure in previous hypernuclear experiments.

## Key claim
The hyperon binding energy in hypernuclei can be effectively evaluated from the relative yields of different hypernuclei produced in high-energy nuclear reactions, using a double ratio method that does not require precise measurement of all particle momenta. The method is claimed to be universal and applicable to multi-strange nuclei, but its accuracy depends on determining the temperature of the disintegrating hypernuclear system and on corrections for secondary de-excitation processes.

## Method
The authors extend the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) to hypernuclear systems, where the freeze-out volume contains fragments with baryon number A, charge Z, and hyperon number H. They derive a double ratio formula (Eq. 4) that relates the logarithm of the yield ratio of two hypernuclei to the difference in their hyperon binding energies divided by temperature. The method requires identifying hypernuclei and their partner nuclei differing by one Λ hyperon, then measuring their relative yields in the same reaction.

## Key numbers
- The hyperon binding energy parametrization used is: F_{AH}^{hyp} = (H/A)·(-10.68 A + 21.27 A^{2/3}) MeV
- Normal nuclear density ρ₀ ≈ 0.15 fm⁻³ is used for the excluded volume calculation
- The free volume parameter κ ≈ 2 is taken as in previous multifragmentation experiments
- The strangeness population factor S = 0.36 ± 0.26 was obtained by AGS-E864 collaboration for central collisions at 11.5 A GeV/c
- The chemical temperature extracted from the S-factor analysis is T ≈ 5.5 MeV
- Temperatures in multifragmentation are typically around 4–6 MeV for excitation energies above 2–3 MeV per nucleon
- The characteristic time for multifragmentation is around 100 fm/c

## Context
This work extends the statistical multifragmentation model (SMM) previously developed for normal nuclei (Botvina et al., 2007; Buyukcizmeci et al., 2013) to hypernuclear systems, building on earlier theoretical studies of hypernuclei production in relativistic collisions (Botvina et al., 2011, 2013, 2015, 2017). The double ratio method is analogous to the strangeness population factor S introduced by the AGS-E864 collaboration (2004) for light hypernuclei, but generalized to arbitrary hypernuclei and multi-strange systems. This approach enables future experiments at FAIR, NICA, and other facilities to extract hyperon binding energies from deep-inelastic reactions without the expensive full-particle detection required by traditional missing-mass spectroscopy methods (Hasinoff, 2006).
