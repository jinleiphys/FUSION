# Evidence for factorized scattering of composite states in the Gross-Neveu model

> Scattering of two baryons in the large-N Gross-Neveu model via the time-dependent Dirac-Hartree-Fock approach has recently been solved in closed analytical form. Here, we generalize this result to scattering processes involving any number and complexity of the scatterers. The result is extrapolated from the solution of few baryon problems, found via a joint ansatz for the scalar mean field and the Dirac spinors, and presented in analytical form. It has been verified numerically for up to 8-baryon problems so far, but a full mathematical proof is still missing. Examples shown include the analogue of proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering in this toy model. All the parameters of the general result can be fixed by one- and two-baryon input only. We take this finding as evidence for factorized scattering, but on the level of composite multi-fermion states rather than elementary fermions.

## Key claim

The authors claim that scattering of any number of composite baryons and multi-baryon bound states in the large-N Gross-Neveu model can be described by a factorized ansatz whose parameters are fixed entirely by one- and two-baryon input, but they acknowledge that a full mathematical proof is still missing and the result has only been verified numerically for up to 8-baryon problems.

## Method

The authors extend the previously solved two-baryon time-dependent Dirac-Hartree-Fock (TDHF) scattering problem to an arbitrary number of scatterers by constructing a joint ansatz for the scalar mean field S and the Dirac spinors as rational functions of N exponentials U_i, each associated with a single boosted baryon. They impose the Dirac equation on this ansatz, which yields algebraic equations for the polynomial coefficients, and then classify coefficients as reducible (determined by recursion relations from the N-1 baryon problem) or irreducible (the coefficients of the product U_1...U_N in each polynomial). The irreducible coefficients are the only ones that must be determined by solving the Dirac equation, and the authors present an algorithm that expresses all polynomials in terms of these irreducible coefficients, time delay factors, and transmission amplitudes.

## Key numbers

- The ansatz for N baryons involves 4 x 3^N coefficients in the polynomials for S and the spinors, of which all but 4 (the irreducible coefficients a_{11...1}, b_{11...1}, c_{11...1}, d_{11...1}) are determined by normalization and recursion relations.
- The time delay factor between baryons i and j (i less than j) is given by delta_{ij} = (zeta_j Z_i + zeta_i Z_j)(zeta_i Z_i + zeta_j Z_j)(zeta_i Z_i Z_j - zeta_j)(zeta_j Z_i Z_j - zeta_i) / ((zeta_j Z_i - zeta_i Z_j)(zeta_i Z_i - zeta_j Z_j)(zeta_i Z_i Z_j + zeta_j)(zeta_j Z_i Z_j + zeta_i)).
- The transmission amplitude for baryon k is T_k = (zeta_k + Z_k)(zeta_k Z_k - 1) / ((zeta_k - Z_k)(zeta_k Z_k + 1)), which is unitary (|T_k| = 1).
- The result has been verified numerically for up to 8-baryon problems.

## Context

This work extends the exact two-baryon TDHF scattering solution of Fitzner and Thies (2011) to arbitrary numbers of composite scatterers, including multi-baryon bound states (nuclei) in addition to single baryons. It builds on the static multi-fermion bound state solutions of Feinberg and the kink-antikink scattering solutions of Thies and collaborators, which were mapped to sinh-Gordon solitons. The non-relativistic limit of the two-baryon solution recovers the time-dependent solutions of the multi-component nonlinear Schrodinger equation of Nogami and Warke, and the present generalization enables the study of analogue proton-nucleus and nucleus-nucleus scattering in this toy model, with all parameters fixed by one- and two-baryon input alone.
