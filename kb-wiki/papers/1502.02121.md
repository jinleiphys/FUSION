# Techniques for multiboson interferometry

> The quantum statistics (QS) correlations of identical bosons are well known to be sensitive to the space-time extent and dynamics of the particle emitting source in high-energy collisions. While two-pion correlations are most often experimentally measured, the QS correlations of three-pions and higher are rarely explored. We present a set of techniques to isolate and analyze 3- and 4-pion QS correlations. In particular, the technique of built correlation functions allows one to more easily study the effects of quantum coherence at finite relative momenta instead of at the unmeasured intercept of correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper presents a set of techniques to isolate and analyze 3- and 4-pion quantum statistics correlations, with the specific claim that comparing built correlation functions from measured 2-pion correlations to measured higher-order correlations allows a less ambiguous determination of the coherent fraction than 2-pion correlations alone, though the method requires assumptions about the space-time structure of the coherent component.

## Method

The author derives analytic expressions for 2-, 3-, and 4-pion quantum statistics correlation functions in the Wigner function formalism, separating emission into chaotic and coherent components with fraction G. The technique of "built" correlation functions is introduced, where higher-order correlations are constructed from lower-order measurements using the pair exchange magnitudes extracted from 2-pion correlations under various assumptions about the coherent source profile. For experimental application, the paper details how to isolate pure quantum statistics correlations by correcting for final-state interactions using the generalized Riverside method and for dilution from long-lived emitters using the core/halo picture.

## Key numbers

- For a coherent fraction as large as 50%, the intercept of 2-pion correlations is decreased only by 25%
- The threshold triplet energy for applicability of the Omega_0 asymptotic ansatz for 3-body Coulomb wavefunctions is 26 MeV for pp/p-Pb collisions and 4 MeV for central Pb-Pb collisions at the LHC, based on characteristic Gaussian 1D radii of about 1.5 fm and 9 fm respectively
- A single Gaussian fit to a 2-pion correlation function with G=0.5 and a coherent source radius of 1 fm combined with a chaotic source radius of 8 fm yields a radius 25% smaller than the true chaotic source radius
- The pion Bohr radius is +/- 388 fm for like/unlike sign pairs, compared to typical r* values of about 20 fm in high-energy heavy-ion collisions

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of Andreev (1992) and Heinz (1997) on partial coherence in high-energy collisions, providing practical experimental techniques for measuring multi-pion correlations that were previously only theoretically discussed. The techniques enable experimental tests of quantum coherence mechanisms such as the disoriented chiral condensate proposed by Bjorken (1993) and Rajagopal (1997), and build upon the ALICE measurement of r_3 by Abelev (2013) which showed no substantial Q_3 dependence. The paper enables future experimental extraction of the coherent fraction from combined 2-, 3-, and 4-pion correlation measurements at the LHC and RHIC.
