# Neutrons from multiplicity-selected Au-Au collisions at 150, 250, 400, and 650 AMeV

> We measured neutron triple-differential cross sections from multiplicity-selected Au-Au collisions at 150, 250, 400, and 650 \AMeV. The reaction plane for each collision was estimated from the summed transverse velocity vector of the charged fragments emitted in the collision. We examined the azimuthal distribution of the triple-differential cross sections as a function of the polar angle and the neutron rapidity. We extracted the average in--plane transverse momentum $\langle P_x\rangle$ and the normalized observable $\langle P_x/P_\perp\rangle$, where $P_\perp$ is the neutron transverse momentum, as a function of the neutron center-of-mass rapidity, and we examined the dependence of these observables on beam energy. These collective flow observables for neutrons, which are consistent with those of protons plus bound nucleons from the Plastic Ball Group, agree with the Boltzmann--Uehling--Uhlenbeck (BUU) calculations with a momentum--dependent interaction. Also, we calculated the polar-angle-integrated maximum azimuthal anisotropy ratio R from the value of $\langle P_x/P_\perp\rangle$.

## Key claim

Neutron collective flow observables from multiplicity-selected semi-central Au-Au collisions at 150, 250, 400, and 650 AMeV are consistent with those of protons plus bound nucleons measured by the Plastic Ball Group and agree with Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (BUU) calculations using a momentum-dependent interaction, but only after applying a coalescence prescription to remove composite-fragment contributions and with the caveat that BUU overestimates triple-differential cross sections at polar angles above 15 degrees for the two lower beam energies (150 and 250 AMeV).

## Method

Neutron triple-differential cross sections were measured using time-of-flight techniques with 18 NE-102 plastic scintillator detectors at polar angles from 3 to 90 degrees, with flight paths of 5.9 to 8.4 m. The reaction plane for each collision was estimated from the summed transverse velocity vector of charged fragments detected by an array of 184 scintillation detectors, using the transverse-velocity method of Fai et al. (1987). Backgrounds from target-unrelated sources and target-induced neutron scattering were estimated using target-out measurements and shadow-shield techniques, with a correction applied for overestimation of the background at azimuthal angles near 0 degrees. Charged-particle multiplicity cuts (M >= 30, 32, 34, 36 for 150, 250, 400, and 650 AMeV respectively) were applied to select semi-central collisions with maximum impact parameters of about 0.55 to 0.68 times the Au nucleus diameter.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: 150, 250, 400, and 650 AMeV for Au-Au collisions
- Time resolution of typical 25-cm wide neutron detector: 390 +/- 70 ps, corresponding to energy resolutions of about 4 MeV (for 150 MeV neutrons) and 38 MeV (for 650 MeV neutrons) at 8.4 m flight path
- Neutron detection efficiencies at 16 MeVee threshold: 7.4% at 150 AMeV to 6.3% at 650 AMeV
- Dispersions in reaction plane determination (Delta phi_R): 39.4 degrees (150 AMeV), 31.3 degrees (250 AMeV), 29.5 degrees (400 AMeV), and 25.4 degrees (650 AMeV), with uncertainties of about 5%
- Charged multiplicity selection thresholds: M >= 30 (150 AMeV), 32 (250 AMeV), 34 (400 AMeV), 36 (650 AMeV), corresponding to maximum impact parameters of about 0.55, 0.62, 0.63, and 0.68 times the Au nucleus diameter
- Target thicknesses: 0.59 g/cm^2 (150 AMeV), 1.1 g/cm^2 (250 AMeV), 1.7 g/cm^2 (400 and 650 AMeV), with energy spreads of 17%, 14%, 11%, and 6% respectively
- Critical distances for coalescence in BUU calculations: 3.3 fm (150 AMeV), 3.0 fm (250 AMeV), 3.2 fm (400 AMeV), and 2.7 fm (650 AMeV)
- Incompressibility modulus K used in BUU calculations: 215 MeV

## Context

This work extends measurements of collective flow from charged particles (protons and bound nucleons) studied by the Plastic Ball Group (Doss et al., 1986, 1987) to free neutrons, using the same Au-Au system at overlapping beam energies. The neutron flow results are compared with BUU transport calculations using a momentum-dependent mean field parameterized by Gale et al. (1990), and the coalescence prescription for removing composite fragments follows the approach of Aichelin and Bertsch (1985). The polar-angle-integrated maximum azimuthal anisotropy R was previously defined by Madey et al. (1993) for Nb-Nb and Au-Au collisions at 400 AMeV. The weaker dependence of normalized observables (such as <P_x/P_perp>) on beam energy and mass agrees with observations by Lambrecht et al. (1994) for neutrons from Au-Au collisions. The results enable further constraints on the nuclear equation of state through comparison with transport models, though the approximate nature of the coalescence prescription for extracting free neutrons from BUU calculations requires improvement, particularly at lower beam energies.
