# One-loop corrections to omega photoproduction near threshold

> One-loop corrections to $ω$ photoproduction near threshold have been investigated by using the approximation that all relevant transition amplitudes are calculated from the tree diagrams of effective Lagrangians. With the parameters constrained by the data of $γN \to πN$, $γN \to ρN$, and $πN \to ωN$ reactions, it is found that the one-loop effects due to the intermediate $πN$ and $ρN$ states can significantly change the differential cross sections and spin observables. The results from this exploratory investigation suggest strongly that the coupled-channel effects should be taken into account in extracting reliable resonance parameters from the data of vector meson photoproduction in the resonance region.

## Key claim

One-loop corrections from intermediate $\pi N$ and $\rho N$ states can significantly change differential cross sections and spin observables in $\omega$ photoproduction near threshold, suggesting that coupled-channel effects must be included for reliable extraction of resonance parameters. The authors caution that their results for the $\rho N$ channel can only be regarded as an upper bound due to lack of experimental constraints on the $\rho N \to \omega N$ reaction.

## Method

The non-resonant amplitudes for $\gamma N \to \omega N$ were calculated from tree diagrams of effective Lagrangians, with coupling constants constrained by data from $\gamma N \to \pi N$, $\gamma N \to \rho N$, and $\pi N \to \omega N$ reactions. One-loop corrections were computed by evaluating the leading-order terms in a perturbative expansion of a full coupled-channel formulation, where the intermediate meson-baryon propagators were integrated over $\pi N$ and $\rho N$ states. The $\gamma N \to \pi N$ amplitudes were constructed by subtracting resonance contributions from empirical SAID multipole amplitudes, while the $\pi N \to \omega N$ and $\rho N \to \omega N$ amplitudes were generated from tree diagrams with form-factor regularization.

## Key numbers

- $g_{\omega\rho\pi} = 12.9$ GeV$^{-1}$ used for the $\omega\rho\pi$ coupling constant
- $\Gamma^0_\rho = 150$ MeV and $\Lambda_\rho = 0.5$ GeV used for the $\rho$ meson width parametrization
- Cutoff parameters for $\pi N \to \omega N$: $\Lambda_{\omega\rho\pi} = \Lambda_{\rho NN} = 1.3$ GeV (dashed curves) or $1.55$ GeV (solid curves), with $\Lambda_N = 0.5$ GeV
- $g_{\pi NN}^2/4\pi = 14.0$ and $g_{\eta NN}^2/4\pi = 0.99$ used for pseudoscalar meson-nucleon couplings
- $M_\sigma = 0.5$ GeV for the sigma meson mass
- $g_{\omega NN} = 10.35$, $g_{\rho NN} = 6.12$, $\kappa_\rho = 3.1$, $\kappa_\omega = 0.0$ for vector meson-nucleon couplings
- Off-shell extrapolation parameter $\Lambda = 0.5$ GeV used for $\gamma N \to \pi N$ and $\rho N$ loop integrations

## Context

This work extends the tree-diagram approach of Oh, Lee (2001) for $\omega$ photoproduction by adding one-loop corrections, motivated by the demonstration in Sato, Lee (1996) that pion re-scattering effects are essential for correctly extracting the $\gamma N \to \Delta(1232)$ transition amplitude. The calculations follow the dynamical coupled-channel formulation of Sato, Lee (1996) and are similar in spirit to the investigation of coupled-channel effects on kaon photoproduction by Chen, Tabakin, Lee (2001). The results indicate that future full coupled-channel calculations are needed to reliably extract nucleon resonance parameters from vector meson photoproduction data, particularly for polarization observables.
