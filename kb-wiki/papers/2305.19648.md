# Uncertainties in ab initio nuclear structure calculations with chiral interactions

> We present theoretical ground state energies and their uncertainties for p-shell nuclei obtained from chiral effective field theory internucleon interactions as a function of chiral order, fitted to two- and three-body data only. We apply a Similary Renormalization Group transformation to improve the numerical convergence of the many-body calculations, and discuss both the numerical uncertainties arising from basis truncations and those from omitted induced many-body forces, as well as chiral truncation uncertainties. With complete Next-to-Next-to-Leading (N2LO) order two- and three-body interactions, we find significant overbinding for the ground states in the upper p-shell, but using higher-order two-body potentials, in combination with N2LO three-body forces, our predictions agree with experiment throughout the p-shell to within our combined estimated uncertainties. The uncertainties due to chiral order truncation are noticeably larger than the numerical uncertainties, but they are expected to become comparable to the numerical uncertainties at complete N3LO.

## Key claim

The paper finds that with complete Next-to-Next-to-Leading (N2LO) order two- and three-body interactions, p-shell nuclei show significant overbinding, but using higher-order two-body potentials (up to N4LO+) combined with N2LO three-body forces yields predictions that agree with experiment throughout the p-shell within combined estimated uncertainties, with chiral truncation uncertainties noticeably larger than numerical uncertainties.

## Method

The authors performed No-Core Shell Model (NCSM) calculations for ground state energies of stable p-shell nuclei from A=4 to A=16 using semilocal momentum-space (SMS) regulated chiral effective field theory interactions from LO through N4LO+. They applied a Similarity Renormalization Group (SRG) transformation to improve numerical convergence, included induced three-body forces from the SRG evolution, and used exponential extrapolation in the basis truncation parameter Nmax to estimate complete-basis results. Chiral truncation uncertainties were estimated using a Bayesian model with expansion parameter Q ≈ 0.31, and numerical uncertainties from basis truncation and extrapolation were combined empirically.

## Key numbers

- Chiral expansion parameter Q ≈ 0.31, based on effective pion mass Mπ^eff ≈ 200 MeV and breakdown scale ΛB ≈ 650 MeV
- N4LO+ NN potential gives χ²_datum = 1.01 for neutron-proton and proton-proton scattering data below Elab = 300 MeV
- For ⁴He at NLO and N2LO with SRG evolution to α = 0.04 fm⁴, NCSM results at Nmax = 14 agree with Yakubovsky results to within a fraction of an MeV
- NCSM and Jacobi-NCSM codes agree to within 10-20 keV for A=3 and 4, and to within about 30 keV for A=6 (0.1% of eigenvalues)
- Largest calculations (¹⁴N and ¹⁵N at Nmax = 8) had dimensions over one billion with about 76 × 10¹² nonzero matrix elements

## Context

This work extends the LENPIC collaboration's systematic uncertainty quantification of chiral effective field theory interactions from light nuclei (A=3,4) to the full p-shell up to A=16. It builds on the SMS-regulated NN potentials of Reinert (2017) and the Bayesian truncation error model of Melendez (2019), and provides parameter-free predictions for A>3 by fitting the 3N force LECs cD and cE only to A=3 data (triton binding energy and proton-deuteron scattering). The results enable future comparisons with complete N3LO three-body forces once they become available, and establish that chiral truncation uncertainties dominate over numerical uncertainties at current chiral orders.
