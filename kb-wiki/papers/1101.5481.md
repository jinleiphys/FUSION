# Some Comments on Relativistic Hydrodynamics and Fuzzy Bags

> Three subjects are considered here: the relativistic hydrodynamics equations for a boost-invariant expanding fluid; the fuzzy bag model for the pressure which recently appeared in QCD phenomenology; and the early space-time evolution of the QCD matter, drawn from model studies, which can also be expected to arise in realistic fluid dynamics relevant to heavy ion collisions at LHC.

# Some Comments on Relativistic Hydrodynamics and Fuzzy Bags

## Key claim

The fuzzy bag model modification of the QCD equation of state (adding a quadratic temperature term to the pressure) slows the cooling of expanding QCD matter in a way that mimics the effect of shear viscosity, potentially confounding attempts to extract the shear viscosity coefficient from heavy ion collision data at LHC energies.

## Method

The author solves the 1+1-dimensional boost-invariant (Bjorken) relativistic hydrodynamic equations analytically for a perfect fluid with the fuzzy bag equation of state p(T)=a(T^4 - α T^2) - B, obtaining an explicit cubic solution for temperature as a function of proper time. For viscous fluids, the author combines the fuzzy bag equation of state with temperature-dependent shear and bulk viscosity parametrizations from AdS/QCD and lattice fits, then solves the evolution equation numerically and via double series expansions in α and κ.

## Key numbers

- The fuzzy bag parameter α is fixed at 0.09 GeV² from Regge trajectory slopes and the Cornell potential for Nc=Nf=3.
- The bag constant B is set to (0.2 GeV)⁴.
- Initial temperature T₀ = 0.8 GeV and initial proper time τ₀ = 0.08 fm/c, estimated from the uncertainty principle with ⟨E⟩₀ ∼ 3T₀.
- The maximum discrepancy between fuzzy bag and MIT bag temperature evolution at τ = 2 fm/c is 13%.
- For the MIT bag model with constant η/s, the constraint κ ≤ 1/4 is required to avoid unphysical reheating at early times, where κ = η/s.
- The conjectured lower bound on η/s is 1/(4π) ≈ 0.08.
- For the combined viscous fuzzy bag model with κ = 1/(4π), δ ≈ 0.024, and the approximation error for the temperature evolution is less than 0.9%.
- The limiting temperature at large proper time in the fuzzy bag model is √(α/2) ≈ 0.21 GeV, identified as the critical temperature.
- For the polynomial model fit to lattice data, parameters are a=2.8, γ=6.5 MeV, α=0.09 GeV², β=(0.14 GeV)³, B=(0.2 GeV)⁴.

## Context

This work extends the Bjorken (1983) hydrodynamic model for boost-invariant expansion by incorporating the fuzzy bag equation of state proposed by Pisarski, which modifies the MIT bag model with a T² term motivated by lattice QCD data from the Wuppertal-Budapest and HOT-QCD collaborations. The paper shows that the fuzzy bag modification produces temperature evolution effects with the same sign as shear viscosity, complicating extraction of η/s from LHC data. The analysis uses viscosity parametrizations from Kapusta (AdS/QCD shear viscosity) and lattice fits for bulk viscosity, and derives a narrow allowed window 0.08 ≲ η/s ≲ 0.11 from combining the conjectured KSS bound with a bound on ζ/η. The work enables future 3+1-dimensional hydrodynamic simulations to test whether these analytic findings survive in more realistic codes.
