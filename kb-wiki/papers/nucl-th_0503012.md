# Momentum-space treatment of Coulomb interaction in three-nucleon reactions with two protons

> The Coulomb interaction between the two protons is included in the calculation of proton-deuteron elastic scattering, radiative proton-deuteron capture and two-body electromagnetic disintegration of ${}^3\mathrm{He}$. The hadron dynamics is based on the purely nucleonic charge-dependent (CD) Bonn potential and its realistic extension CD Bonn + $Δ$ to a coupled-channel two-baryon potential, allowing for single virtual $Δ$-isobar excitation. Calculations are done using integral equations in momentum space. The screening and renormalization approach is employed for including the Coulomb interaction. Convergence of the procedure is found already at moderate screening radii. The reliability of the method is demonstrated. The Coulomb effect on observables is seen at low energies for the whole kinematic regime. In proton-deuteron elastic scattering at higher energies the Coulomb effect is confined to forward scattering angles; the $Δ$-isobar effect found previously remains unchanged by Coulomb. In electromagnetic reactions Coulomb competes with other effects in a complicated way.

# Momentum-space treatment of Coulomb interaction in three-nucleon reactions with two protons

## Key claim

The screening and renormalization approach for including the Coulomb interaction between two protons in three-nucleon reactions converges at moderate screening radii (R = 10-20 fm) and yields reliable results for proton-deuteron elastic scattering, radiative capture, and two-body electromagnetic disintegration of ³He, but only when the hadron dynamics uses modern two-nucleon potentials (CD Bonn or CD Bonn + Δ) without separable expansion, unlike prior work that relied on low-rank separable potentials.

## Method

The authors solve Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) integral equations in momentum space for three-nucleon scattering, using the CD Bonn potential and its coupled-channel extension CD Bonn + Δ that allows single virtual Δ-isobar excitation. The Coulomb potential between the two protons is screened with a smooth exponential cutoff (w_R(r) = (α/r) exp[-(r/R)^4]), standard short-range scattering theory is applied, and the on-shell amplitudes are renormalized by a diverging phase factor to recover the unscreened Coulomb limit. The method is first validated on proton-proton scattering and then extended to elastic proton-deuteron scattering and electromagnetic reactions.

## Key numbers

- Screening radius R = 20 fm suffices for agreement within 0.01 deg with exact pp ¹S₀ phase shifts at all energies above 5 MeV; R = 10 fm suffices above 25 MeV.
- To reproduce the pp ¹S₀ scattering length a^C_pp = -7.815 fm and effective range r^C_pp = 2.773 fm within 0.010 fm, screening radii larger than R = 100 fm are required.
- At 5 MeV proton lab energy, results for R > 20 fm are indistinguishable from exact Coulomb results for the spin-averaged pp differential cross section.
- At 100 MeV proton lab energy, the proton analyzing power for pp scattering converges with R ≥ 10 fm, while both the "no Coulomb" approximation and the "Coulomb externally corrected" approximation are "pretty poor."

## Context

This work extends the screening and renormalization approach of Taylor (1974) and Alt (1978) from formal scattering theory to practical three-nucleon calculations with realistic potentials, improving upon earlier implementations by Berthold (1990) and Alt (2002) that used low-rank separable potentials. The method enables reliable inclusion of Coulomb effects in proton-deuteron reactions at low energies where Coulomb affects the whole kinematic regime, and confirms that the Δ-isobar effect found previously by Deltuva (2003) remains unchanged by Coulomb at higher energies. Future work could extend this treatment to pd breakup and three-body breakup in electromagnetic reactions with ³He, which are explicitly left out of this paper.
