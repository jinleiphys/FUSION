# Evidence for a missing nucleon resonance in kaon photoproduction

> New SAPHIR p(gamma,K+)Lambda total cross section data show a resonance structure at a total c.m. energy around 1900 MeV. We investigate this feature with an isobar model and find that the structure can be well explained by including a new D_{13} resonance at 1895 MeV. Such a state has been predicted by a relativistic quark model at 1960 MeV with significant gamma-N and K-Lambda branching ratios. We demonstrate how the measurement of the photon asymmetry can be used to further study this resonance. In addition, verification of the predicted large decay widths into the eta-N and eta'-N channels would allow distinguishing between other nearby D_{13} states.

# Evidence for a missing nucleon resonance in kaon photoproduction

## Key claim

The structure observed in new SAPHIR total cross section data for p(gamma,K+)Lambda at a center-of-mass energy around 1900 MeV can be well explained by including a new D13 resonance at 1895 MeV, whose extracted mass, width, and coupling parameters agree with a relativistic quark model prediction for a D13(1960) state, though the quantitative agreement is probably fortuitous.

## Method

The authors performed a tree-level isobar model analysis of the p(gamma,K+)Lambda reaction, including Born terms, t-channel K*(892) and K1(1270) vector meson poles, and three established low-energy resonances (S11(1650), P11(1710), P13(1720)) with energy-dependent widths. They then added candidate missing resonances from the Capstick-Roberts quark model, fitting the mass, width, and coupling constants of each candidate to the new SAPHIR total cross section data while using the gauge method of Haberzettl to include hadronic form factors.

## Key numbers

- The new D13 resonance was fitted with a mass of 1895 MeV and a total width of 372 MeV
- The extracted product of photocoupling and K-Lambda decay width, sqrt(Gamma_{N*N gamma} * Gamma_{N*K Lambda}) / Gamma_{N*}, was 2.292 (+0.722/-0.204) x 10^{-3}, compared to the quark model prediction of -2.722 +/- 0.729 x 10^{-3}
- The chi-squared per data point (chi^2/N) improved from approximately 4.5 to approximately 3 when including any of the four candidate resonances
- The background form factor cutoff was fitted to approximately 800 MeV, while the resonant cutoff was 1900 MeV
- The quark model predicted the D13(1960) state at 1960 MeV with a total width of 535 MeV

## Context

This work extends the isobar model studies of kaon photoproduction pioneered by Thom (1966) and developed by Adelseck, Bennhold, and Wright (1985), Williams, Ji, and Cotanch (1992), and Mart, Bennhold, and Hyde-Wright (1995). It directly addresses the "missing resonance" problem identified by quark model calculations of Capstick and Roberts (1994, 1998), which predict more nucleon resonances than observed in pion-nucleon scattering. The finding enables future searches for this D13 state through measurement of the photon asymmetry in polarized photoproduction and through verification of predicted large decay widths into eta-N and eta'-N channels, which would distinguish it from other nearby D13 states such as the D13(2055) predicted by the quark model.
