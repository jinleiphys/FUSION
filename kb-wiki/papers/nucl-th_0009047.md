# Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov description of sizes and shapes of A=20 isobars

> Ground-state properties of A = 20 nuclei ($^{20}$N, $^{20}$O, $^{20}$F, $^{20}$Ne, $^{20}$Na, $^{20}$Mg) are described in the framework of Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory. The model uses the NL3 effective interaction in the mean-field Lagrangian, and describes pairing correlations by the pairing part of the finite range Gogny interaction D1S. Binding energies, quadrupole deformations, nuclear matter radii, and differences in radii of proton and neutron distributions are compared with recent experimental data.

## Key claim
The Relativistic Hartree-Bogoliubov (RHB) theory with the NL3 effective interaction and Gogny D1S pairing reproduces ground-state properties of A=20 isobars, except for the matter radius of 20O where the calculated value (2.79 fm) exceeds the experimental value (2.69±0.03 fm), a discrepancy shared by several other theoretical models.

## Method
The RHB equations were solved self-consistently by expanding nucleon spinors and meson fields in eigenfunctions of a deformed axially symmetric oscillator potential, using 12 oscillator shells for fermion fields and 20 shells for boson fields. The NL3 effective interaction was used for the mean-field Lagrangian, and pairing correlations were described by the pairing part of the finite-range Gogny interaction D1S. Blocking calculations for odd-proton or odd-neutron systems were performed without breaking time-reversal symmetry.

## Key numbers
- Binding energies for all six A=20 nuclei (20N, 20O, 20F, 20Ne, 20Na, 20Mg) show very good agreement with experimental data from Audi and Wapstra (1995).
- 20Ne ground-state quadrupole deformation: β2 = 0.365 (strongly prolate deformed).
- 20Na ground-state quadrupole deformation: β2 = -0.153 (oblate).
- 20Mg surface thickness: s(p) = 1.81 fm, s(n) = 1.50 fm; diffuseness: α(p) = 0.42 fm, α(n) = 0.35 fm.
- 20N surface thickness: s(p) = 1.60 fm, s(n) = 1.94 fm; diffuseness: α(p) = 0.38 fm, α(n) = 0.46 fm.
- Calculated matter radius of 20O: 2.79 fm; experimental value: 2.69±0.03 fm (Chulkov et al., 1996).
- Difference between proton and neutron radii for 20N: 0.33±0.15 fm (experimental).
- Difference between proton and neutron radii for 20Mg: 0.50±0.28 fm (experimental).

## Context
This work extends previous RHB applications to light nuclei near the drip lines, including halo phenomena (Vretenar et al., 1997) and properties of neutron-rich nuclei (Lalazissis et al., 1998). It addresses the failure of earlier models, the microscopic three-cluster model (Descouvemont, 1998), Hartree-Fock with Skyrme interactions (Brown and Hansen, 1996; Koonin et al., 1997; Sagawa et al., 1999; Suzuki et al., 1999), and shell-model calculations (Suzuki et al., 1999), to reproduce the extremely small matter radius of 20O reported by Chulkov et al. (1996). The results confirm that the isovector channel of the NL3 interaction is correctly parameterized, enabling its use for describing exotic nuclei far from beta-stability.
