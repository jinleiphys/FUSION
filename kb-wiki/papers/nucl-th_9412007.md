# Role of Meson-Meson Correlation Effects in the $\NNb\toρπ$ Annihilation Process

> Meson-meson correlation effects are investigated in the $\NNb\toρπ$ annihilation process using a realistic meson-exchange model for the $ρπ$ interaction determined previously, together with a conventional baryon-exchange transition model and a consistent $\NNb$ interaction. For $\NNb$ $S$-states, they have a drastic effect and bring the relative (${^1S_0}/{^3S_1}$) branching ratio up to the experimental value, thus resolving the long-standing so-called ``$ρπ$'' puzzle. For $\NNb$ $P$-states, their effect is of minor importance, and discrepancies remain for those ratios involving annihilation from the $\NNb({^3P_J})$ state to $ρπ(l'=2)$.

## Key claim

Meson-meson correlation effects in the $\rho\pi$ final state, when included via a realistic meson-exchange model, resolve the long-standing "$\rho\pi$ puzzle" for $\NNb$ $S$-states by bringing the relative ${^1S_0}/{^3S_1}$ branching ratio up to the experimental value, but for $\NNb$ $P$-states the effect is minor and discrepancies remain for ratios involving annihilation from the $\NNb({^3P_J})$ state to $\rho\pi(l'=2)$.

## Method

The authors used a conventional baryon-exchange transition model for $\NNb\to\rho\pi$ together with a consistent $\NNb$ interaction (model D, which includes a microscopic annihilation part for two-meson channels and a phenomenological optical potential for the rest). They incorporated a previously constructed meson-exchange model for the $\rho\pi\to\rho\pi$ interaction, with parameters fixed from other investigations and adjusted to $a_1$, $\omega\to\rho\pi$ decay data, first in a DWBA-type approach in the final state only, and then in a full $\NNb,\rho\pi$ coupled channels calculation where the $\rho\pi$ interaction also modifies the initial $\NNb$ interaction.

## Key numbers

- The ${^1S_0}/{^3S_1}$ branching ratio for $\NNb\to\rho\pi$ at rest, without meson-meson correlations, was far from the experimental value; after including the $\rho\pi$ interaction in a coupled channels calculation, the ratio fell within experimental error bars.
- The inclusion of the $\rho\pi$ interaction changed absolute $P$-state values by only about $10-20\%$, and the $P$-state ratios were similarly little affected.
- The $\rho\pi$ interaction reduced the ${^1S_0}$ contribution strongly while increasing the ${^3S_1}$ contribution, producing the extremely small ${^1S_0}/{^3S_1}$ ratio in agreement with experiment.
- For $\NNb$ $P$-states, annihilation from the ${^3P}$ state into $\rho\pi$ with relative orbital angular momentum $l'=2$ provided the largest contribution of all $P$-states, in sharp contrast to experiment where annihilation from this state appears negligible.

## Context

This work extends the baryon-exchange transition models for $\NNb$ annihilation developed by the same group (Mull, Janssen, Speth, Holinde, 1990s) by adding final-state meson-meson correlations that had previously been neglected. It directly addresses the "$\rho\pi$ puzzle" identified in earlier experimental work by Foster (1960s) and the ASTERIX experiment (May, 1980s), where quark-model calculations by Maruyama et al. using the A2 mechanism had achieved a ${^1S_0}/{^3S_1}$ ratio of 1:10-18, appearing superior to baryon-exchange without correlations. The results show that baryon-exchange models with consistent meson-meson correlations can match the $S$-state data, but discrepancies remain for $P$-state ratios involving $l'=2$, suggesting that either an extension of the $\rho\pi$ interaction model (e.g., adding a pole term or coupling to $\overline{K}K^*\pm\overline{K^*}K$ channels) or improved data is needed.
