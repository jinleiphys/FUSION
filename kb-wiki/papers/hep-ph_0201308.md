# Far-from-equilibrium dynamics with broken symmetries from the 2PI-1/N expansion

> We derive the nonequilibrium real-time evolution of an O(N) - invariant scalar quantum field theory in the presence of a nonvanishing expectation value of the quantum field. Using a systematic 1/N expansion of the 2PI effective action to next-to-leading order, we obtain nonperturbative evolution equations which include scattering and memory effects. The equivalence of the direct method, which requires the resummation of an infinite number of skeleton diagrams, with the auxiliary-field formalism, which involves only one diagram at next-to-leading order, is shown.

## Key claim

The paper derives nonperturbative evolution equations for an O(N)-invariant scalar quantum field theory far from equilibrium with a nonvanishing field expectation value, using a systematic 1/N expansion of the two-particle irreducible (2PI) effective action to next-to-leading order (NLO). The approach provides a controlled description that includes scattering and memory effects, but the derivation is limited to the NLO approximation and assumes the 1/N expansion parameter is sufficiently small.

## Method

The authors perform a 1/N expansion of the 2PI effective action for a real scalar N-component quantum field with O(N) symmetry, classifying diagrams by their scaling with N using irreducible O(N) invariants (phi^2, tr(G^n), and tr(phi phi G^n)). They derive the NLO contribution to the effective action by resumming infinite series of diagrams: one class independent of phi (constructed from quartic vertices) and one class depending on phi (involving field insertions). The resulting equations of motion for the macroscopic field phi and the propagator G are obtained by minimizing the effective action, and the equivalence between the direct diagrammatic resummation and an auxiliary-field formalism is demonstrated.

## Key numbers

- The classical action S[phi] scales proportional to N, with the interaction term written as (lambda/(4! N)) (phi_a phi_a)^2
- The minimum phi_0 of the classical effective potential scales as phi_0^2 = N (-6 m^2/lambda) for negative mass-squared m^2
- Leading order (LO) graphs scale proportional to N; next-to-leading order (NLO) contributions are of order one; next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) scales as 1/N
- The NLO contribution to Gamma_2[phi,G] contains a term (i/2) Tr_C Ln[ B(G) ] and a term (i lambda/(6N)) integral of I(x,y;G) phi_a(x) G_ab(x,y) phi_b(y)
- The self-energy at LO is Sigma^LO_ab(x,y) = -i (lambda/(6N)) G_cc(x,x) delta_ab delta_C(x-y)

## Context

This work extends the 2PI-1/N expansion previously developed by Berges (2001) for the symmetric regime with vanishing field expectation value to the case of nonzero phi, which is important for describing spontaneous symmetry breaking in applications such as heavy-ion collisions (where phi ~ <q-bar q> in the O(4) linear sigma model for two-flavor QCD) and inflationary cosmology (preheating dynamics). The approach improves upon standard 1/N expansion of the 1PI effective action, which is secular in time once direct scattering is included (Bettencourt, 1997), and extends earlier loop-expansion descriptions of large-time behavior (Berges, 2000; Aarts, 2001) to a regime not restricted to small couplings. The availability of these equations enables controlled studies of far-from-equilibrium phenomena including disoriented chiral condensates and the decay of parity-odd metastable states in hot QCD.
