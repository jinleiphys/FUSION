# Direct Neutrino Mass Experiments and Exotic Charged Current Interactions

> We study the effect of exotic charged current interactions on the electron energy spectrum in tritium decay, focussing on the KATRIN experiment and a possible modified setup that has access to the full spectrum. Both sub-eV and keV neutrino masses are considered. We perform a fully relativistic calculation and take all possible new interactions into account, demonstrating the possible sizable distortions in the energy spectrum.

## Key claim

Exotic charged-current interactions beyond the Standard Model can produce sizable distortions in the electron energy spectrum of tritium beta decay, but the observability of these distortions depends critically on whether the emitted neutrino is light (sub-eV) or heavy (keV-scale) and on the specific Lorentz structure of the new interaction; interference terms involving right-handed neutrino fields are suppressed by the neutrino mass and therefore only become relevant for keV-scale sterile neutrinos.

## Method

The authors performed a fully relativistic calculation of the tritium beta-decay spectrum using an effective field theory approach with dimension-six operators, parameterizing ten possible beyond-Standard-Model charged-current contributions (scalar, pseudoscalar, vector, axial-vector, and tensor couplings with left-handed and right-handed chiralities). They computed the 66 real terms in the squared matrix element, extracted the six spectral parameters A, B1, B2, C, D1, D2 for each contribution, and evaluated the relative spectral distortion Δ = (spectrum with new physics)/(Standard Model spectrum) − 1 for both light (0.5 eV) and heavy (5 keV) neutrino emission scenarios, using current 90% CL upper bounds on the new-physics coupling constants.

## Key numbers

- Standard Model spectral parameters for tritium decay (assuming three massless neutrinos, δβ = 0): A_SM = −1.45 × 10⁻¹¹, B1_SM = 2.74 × 10⁻¹¹ MeV⁻¹, B2_SM = 2.71 × 10⁻¹¹ MeV⁻¹, C_SM = 3.98 × 10⁻¹³ MeV⁻², D1_SM = −5.38 × 10⁻¹⁴ MeV⁻², D2_SM = 3.67 × 10⁻¹³ MeV⁻²
- Current 90% CL upper bounds on new-physics couplings used: |Re ε_L| < 5 × 10⁻⁴, |Re ε_R| < 5 × 10⁻⁴, |Re ε_S| < 8 × 10⁻³, |Re ε_T| < 1 × 10⁻³, |Re ε̃_L| < 6 × 10⁻², |Re ε̃_R| < 5 × 10⁻³, |Re ε̃_S| < 1.3 × 10⁻², |Re ε̃_T| < 3 × 10⁻³
- Active-sterile mixing assumed: |S_ej/U_ej|² ∼ 10⁻⁶, with U_ej = V_ej = 1 and T_ej = S_ej = 10⁻³
- Reference light neutrino mass: m_β = 0.5 eV; reference heavy sterile neutrino mass: M_j = 5 keV
- Pseudoscalar coupling suppression factor: g_P × q/M_N ≲ 0.004, making ε_P and ε̃_P effects negligible
- Estimated sensitivity for spectral distortion: Δ(ε̃_j) > 10⁻⁷ assumed observable; current limits on ε_L, ε_R, ε_S, ε_T could be improved by approximately six and five orders of magnitude respectively

## Context

This work extends the effective operator analysis of exotic charged currents in beta decay previously developed by Cirigliano et al. (2013) and applies it specifically to the tritium endpoint spectrum measured by KATRIN and a possible future setup with full spectral access. The authors consider both sub-eV active neutrinos and keV-scale sterile neutrinos, building on the parameterization of active-sterile mixing from Barry et al. (2014) and the tritium sensitivity studies for keV neutrinos from the KATRIN collaboration. The results enable experimental searches for new physics through spectral shape distortions beyond the simple kink signature of a sterile neutrino, and demonstrate that a modified KATRIN-like experiment could provide laboratory limits on exotic charged-current interactions that are competitive with or superior to current bounds from other precision beta-decay measurements.
