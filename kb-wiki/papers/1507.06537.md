# Non-Gaussian particle number fluctuations in vicinity of the critical point for van der Waals equation of state

> The non-Gaussian measures of the particle number fluctuations -- skewness $Sσ$ and kurtosis $κσ^2$ -- are calculated in a vicinity of the critical point. This point corresponds to the end point of the first-order liquid-gas phase transition. The gaseous phase is characterized by the positive values of skewness while the liquid phase has negative skew. The kurtosis appears to be significantly negative at the critical density and supercritical temperatures. The skewness and kurtosis diverge at the critical point. The classical van der Waals equation of state in the grand canonical ensemble formulation is used in our studies. Neglecting effects of the quantum statistics we succeed to obtain the analytical expressions for the rich structures of the skewness and kurtosis in a wide region around the critical point. These results have universal form, i.e., they do not depend on particular values of the van der Waals parameters $a$ and $b$. The strongly intensive measures of particle number and energy fluctuations are also considered and show singular behavior in the vicinity of the critical point.

## Key claim

The skewness and kurtosis of particle number fluctuations diverge at the critical point of the van der Waals equation of state, but this result is obtained only within the Boltzmann approximation (neglecting quantum statistics) and therefore has universal form independent of the specific van der Waals parameters \(a\) and \(b\).

## Method

The authors used the classical van der Waals equation of state formulated in the grand canonical ensemble (GCE) for a Boltzmann gas, obtaining analytical expressions for the scaled variance, skewness \(S\sigma\), and kurtosis \(\kappa\sigma^2\) of particle number fluctuations. They also calculated the strongly intensive measures \(\Delta[E,N]\) and \(\Sigma[E,N]\) for energy and particle number fluctuations. All results were expressed in reduced variables \(\widetilde{T}=T/T_c\) and \(\widetilde{n}=n/n_c\) to demonstrate universality.

## Key numbers

- Critical parameters: \(T_c = 8a/(27b)\), \(n_c = 1/(3b)\), \(p_c = a/(27b^2)\)
- At \(\widetilde{n}\to 0\) (ideal gas limit), skewness \(S\sigma \to 1\) and kurtosis \(\kappa\sigma^2 \to 1\), matching Poisson distribution values
- At the critical point, the scaled variance diverges as \(\omega[N] \cong (4/9)[\tau + (3/4)\rho^2 + \tau\rho]^{-1}\) for \(\tau\ll 1\) and \(\rho\ll 1\), where \(\tau=\widetilde{T}-1\) and \(\rho=\widetilde{n}-1\)
- At \(\tau=0\) and \(\rho\ll 1\), kurtosis diverges as \(\kappa\sigma^2 \propto \rho^{-6}\)
- At \(\widetilde{n}=1\) and \(\widetilde{T}>1\), kurtosis diverges as \(\kappa\sigma^2 \propto -\tau^{-3}\), indicating large negative values in the crossover region
- In the excluded-volume model (\(a=0\)), strongly intensive measures \(\Delta[E,N] = \Sigma[E,N] = 1\)

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior GCE formulation of the van der Waals equation (Vovchenko et al., 2015) to calculate higher-order non-Gaussian fluctuation measures, which are relevant to experimental searches for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions (e.g., STAR collaboration measurements of net-proton and net-charge moments). The results are universal for any system described by the van der Waals equation, from water (\(T_c = 647\) K) to nuclear matter (\(T_c \cong 2\times 10^{12}\) K). The negative kurtosis found in the crossover region above \(T_c\) supports arguments by Stephanov (2009) that this sign is characteristic of critical point proximity. The strongly intensive measures \(\Delta[E,N]\) and \(\Sigma[E,N]\) are proposed as tools to separate critical fluctuations from trivial volume fluctuations in experimental data.
