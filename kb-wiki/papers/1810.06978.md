# Collective Effects in Nuclear Collisions: Experimental Overview

> Final version of proceedings for an invited overview talk in XXVIIth International Conference on Ultrarelativistic Nucleus-Nucleus Collisions (Quark Matter 2018), Venezia, Italy

## Key claim

The paper reviews experimental findings on collective effects in nuclear collisions presented at Quark Matter 2018, concluding that while hydrodynamic models with tuned parametrizations can qualitatively or quantitatively describe many flow observables, further constraints from global Bayesian analysis combining Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe data and more differential measurements are needed to discriminate between different functional forms of the shear viscosity over entropy density ratio.

## Method

The author surveys experimental measurements of anisotropic flow coefficients \(v_n\) using 2- and multi-particle cumulant methods in Pb-Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 2.76\) and 5.02 TeV and Xe-Xe collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 5.02\) TeV from ALICE, ATLAS, and CMS at the LHC, and Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV from STAR at RHIC. The review covers event-by-event flow fluctuations reconstructed via Bayesian unfolding and multi-particle cumulant techniques, flow-vector decorrelation studied through factorization ratios \(r_n\), and flow correlations quantified by symmetric cumulants \(SC(m,n)\) and non-linear flow mode coefficients \(\chi_{n,mk}\). Comparisons are made to hydrodynamic model calculations using various initial conditions including TRENTo, AMPT, EKRT, and IP-Glasma.

## Key numbers

- First anisotropic flow measurements in Xe-Xe collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}} = 5.02\) GeV reported by ALICE show stronger centrality dependence of \(v_2\) compared to \(v_3\) and \(v_4\)
- ATLAS measurements of \(v_n(p_{\rm T})\) for \(n = 2\) to 7 in Pb-Pb collisions show clear ordering \(v_2 > v_3 > ... > v_7\)
- Extracted \(\eta/s\) from \(v_2\) measurements was 0.08 using MC-Glauber initial conditions and 0.16 using MC-KLN initial conditions, giving a 100% uncertainty
- Flow-vector decorrelation effect is larger at 2.76 TeV than at 5.02 TeV, with an even stronger effect observed in 200 GeV Au-Au collisions by STAR
- Symmetric cumulant measurements show positive values of \(SC(4,2)\) and negative values of \(SC(3,2)\) in Pb-Pb collisions
- Non-linear flow mode coefficient \(\chi_{4,22}\) has strong dependence on initial conditions but is almost insensitive to \(\eta/s\), while \(\chi_{5,32}\) and \(\chi_{6,33}\) depend strongly on \(\eta/s\) at freeze-out

## Context

This proceedings paper extends the long line of anisotropic flow measurements at RHIC and LHC that began with early \(v_2\) measurements in Au-Au collisions at 130 GeV (showing first evidence of nearly-perfect fluid behavior) and the development of event-by-event hydrodynamic models. It builds on prior work by Ollitrault (1992), Voloshin (1994, 2008), and Heinz (2013) on flow characterization, and on studies of flow fluctuations by ATLAS (2013) and CMS (2017). The review enables future global Bayesian analyses combining Pb-Pb and Xe-Xe flow data with common parametrizations of transport coefficients, and highlights the emerging use of deep learning as a tool to probe the Quark-Gluon Plasma.
