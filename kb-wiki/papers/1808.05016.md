# $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements, neutrino potentials and $\mathrm{SU}(4)$ symmetry

> Intimate relation between the Gamow-Teller part of the matrix element $M^{0ν}_\mathrm{GT}$ and the $2νββ$ closure matrix element $M^{2ν}_\mathrm{cl}$ is explained and explored. If the corresponding radial dependence $C^{2ν}_\mathrm{cl}(r)$ would be known, $M^{0ν}$ corresponding to any mechanism responsible for the $0νββ$ decay can be obtained as a simple integral. However, the $M^{2ν}_\mathrm{cl}$ values sensitively depend on the properties of higher lying $1^+$ states in the intermediate odd-odd nuclei. We show that the $β^-$ and $β^+$ amplitudes of such states typically have opposite relative signs, and their contributions reduce severally the $M^{2ν}_\mathrm{cl}$ values. Vanishing values of $M^{2ν}_\mathrm{cl}$ are signs of a partial restoration of the spin-isospin $\mathrm{SU}(4)$ symmetry. We suggest that demanding that $M^{2ν}_\mathrm{cl}$ = 0 is a sensible way, within the method of the Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), of determining the amount of renormalization of isoscalar particle-particle interaction strength $g^{T=0}_{pp}$. Using such prescription, the matrix elements $M^{0ν}$ are evaluated; their values are not very different ($\le$ 20\%) from the usual QRPA values when $g^{T=0}_{pp}$ is related to the known $2νββ$ half-lives.

# 0νββ nuclear matrix elements, neutrino potentials and SU(4) symmetry

## Key claim

The Gamow-Teller part of the 0νββ nuclear matrix element (M⁰ν_GT) is intimately related to the 2νββ closure matrix element (M²ν_cl) via a simple radial integral, but the practical utility of this relation is limited because M²ν_cl values depend sensitively on higher-lying 1⁺ states in the intermediate odd-odd nuclei and are typically small due to cancellation between β⁻ and β⁺ amplitudes. The authors propose that demanding M²ν_cl = 0 within QRPA provides a sensible way to fix the isoscalar particle-particle interaction strength g^{T=0}_{pp}, corresponding to partial restoration of SU(4) symmetry, and that this prescription yields 0νββ matrix elements within 20% of standard QRPA values.

## Method

The authors evaluate the radial functions C⁰ν_GT(r) and C²ν_cl(r) for several ββ decay candidate nuclei using the Quasi-particle Random Phase Approximation (QRPA), and decompose the 2νββ closure matrix element into spin S=0 and S=1 components within the LS coupling scheme. They fix the isovector particle-particle coupling g^{T=1}_{pp} by requiring M²ν_Fcl = 0 (isospin conservation) and the isoscalar coupling g^{T=0}_{pp} by requiring M²ν_GTcl = 0 (partial SU(4) symmetry restoration), then compute M⁰ν matrix elements from the relation C⁰ν_GT(r) = H_GT(r, Ē) · C²ν_GTcl(r).

## Key numbers

- The 0νββ matrix elements obtained with the M²ν_cl = 0 prescription differ from standard QRPA values by ≤ 20%.
- For ⁷⁶Ge, the 2νββ matrix element M²ν extracted from experiment is 0.136 MeV⁻¹; the QRPA calculation with quenching factor q = 0.712 gives M²ν_GT × q² = 0.077 MeV⁻¹.
- The effective axial coupling used is g_A^eff = q × g_A^free = 0.904, with q = 0.712 derived from the average ratio of calculated to experimental M²ν values.
- For ⁴⁸Ca, the experimental M²ν is 0.046 MeV⁻¹; the QRPA result with quenching is 0.019 MeV⁻¹.
- The isoscalar coupling g^{T=0}_{pp} values range from 0.733 (⁷⁶Ge) to 0.877 (¹¹⁶Cd) across the nuclei studied.
- The closure approximation for 0νββ matrix elements yields results at most 10% smaller than explicit summation over intermediate states.

## Context

This work extends the earlier study by Šimkovic et al. (2015) on the relation between 0νββ and 2νββ matrix elements, and contrasts with the approach of Xavior et al. (2018) who proposed direct proportionality between M⁰ν_GT and M²ν_GTcl. The authors argue that demanding M²ν_GTcl = 0 better reflects the physics of partial SU(4) symmetry restoration than assuming proportionality. The method enables evaluation of M⁰ν for any neutrino potential (e.g., those listed in Horoi and Neacsu (2017)) once C²ν_GTcl(r) is known, representing a practical simplification for the ~20 different nuclear matrix elements needed for various 0νββ mechanisms. The single-state dominance hypothesis for 2νββ decay (tested e.g. by Ejiri (2012) and Coello et al.) is discussed but shown to miss the cancellations from higher-lying 1⁺ states that are crucial for the closure matrix element.
