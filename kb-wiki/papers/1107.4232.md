# Effects of density dependence of the effective pairing interaction on the first $2^+$ excitations and quadrupole moments of odd nuclei

> Excitation energies and transition probabilities of the first $2^+$ excitations in even tin and lead isotopes as well as the quadrupole moments of odd neighbors of these isotopes are calculated within the self-consistent Theory of Finite Fermi Systems based on the Energy Density Functional by Fayans et al. The effect of the density dependence of the effective pairing interaction is analyzed in detail by comparing results obtained with volume and surface pairing. The effect is found to be noticeable. For example, the $2^+$-energies are systematically higher at 200-300 keV for the volume paring as compared with the surface pairing case. But on the average both models reasonably agree with the data. Quadrupole moments of odd-neutron nuclei are very sensitive to the single-particle energy of the state $λ$ under consideration due to the Bogolyubov factor ($u^2_λ-v^2_λ$). A reasonable agreement with experiment for the quadrupole moments has been obtained for the most part of odd nuclei considered. The method used gives a reliable possibility to predict quadrupole moments of unstable odd nuclei including very neutron rich ones.

## Key claim

The effect of the density dependence of the effective pairing interaction on the first \(2^+\) excitation energies and quadrupole moments of odd nuclei is noticeable but, on average, both volume and surface pairing models reasonably agree with experimental data; the authors note that the quadrupole moments of odd-neutron nuclei are very sensitive to the single-particle energy of the state due to the Bogolyubov factor \((u^2_\lambda - v^2_\lambda)\).

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent calculations within the Theory of Finite Fermi Systems (TFFS) based on the Energy Density Functional (EDF) by Fayans et al., using the DF3-a functional. They solved the QRPA-like TFFS equations for the first \(2^+\) excitations in even tin and lead isotopes and computed quadrupole moments of their odd neighbors, comparing two limiting cases of the effective pairing interaction: volume pairing (density-independent) and surface pairing (with strong density dependence). The pairing problem was treated in a BCS-like scheme with a model space cutoff at \(E_{\text{max}} = 36\) MeV, and the parameters of the pairing force were adjusted to reproduce neutron separation energies for both isotopic chains.

## Key numbers

- The \(2^+\) excitation energies are systematically higher by 200–300 keV for volume pairing compared to surface pairing in both tin and lead isotopes.
- For the lead chain, the difference \(\delta \omega_2 = \omega_2^{\text{vol}} - \omega_2^{\text{surf}}\) is on average about 0.3 MeV.
- For the tin chain, the difference \(\delta \omega_2\) is also approximately 300 keV, with surface predictions closer to experimental data.
- For lighter tin isotopes (below A=114), the volume pairing yields \(B(E2,\text{up})\) values higher by about 25–30% compared to surface pairing.
- The volume pairing parameter \(f^\xi_{\text{ex}}\) is \(-0.31\) for lead and \(-0.33\) for tin; the surface pairing parameters are \(f^\xi_{\text{ex}} = -1.05\) with \(h^\xi = 0.94\) for lead and \(h^\xi = 0.92\) for tin.
- The effective pairing interaction for surface pairing is much stronger (up to ten times) at the surface than inside, as derived from ab initio calculations using the Paris and Argonne v\(_{18}\) NN potentials.

## Context

This work extends the self-consistent TFFS/EDF approach of Fayans et al. (2000) by systematically analyzing the sensitivity of low-lying quadrupole excitations and static quadrupole moments to the density dependence of the pairing interaction, which had not been done in prior QRPA studies with Skyrme forces (Bertsch et al., 2007) or the Generator Coordinate Method with the Gogny force (Bertsch et al., 2009). The results enable reliable predictions of quadrupole moments for unstable odd nuclei, including very neutron-rich ones, and suggest that surface pairing is favored due to weaker A-dependence of its parameters compared to volume pairing. The study also confirms earlier findings by Fayans et al. (2000) that binding energies and separation energies are insensitive to the type of pairing force, but odd-even staggering of charge radii requires strong density dependence.
