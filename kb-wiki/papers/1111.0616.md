# Finite volume effects in pion-kaon scattering and reconstruction of the kappa(800) resonance

> Simulating the kappa(800) on the lattice is a challenging task that starts to become feasible due to the rapid progress in recent-years lattice QCD calculations. As the resonance is broad, special attention to finite-volume effects has to be paid, because no sharp resonance signal as from avoided level crossing can be expected. In the present article, we investigate the finite volume effects in the framework of unitarized chiral perturbation theory using next-to-leading order terms. After a fit to meson-meson partial wave data, lattice levels for piK scattering are predicted. In addition, levels are shown for the quantum numbers in which the sigma(600), f_0(980), a_0(980), phi(1020), K*(892), and rho(770) appear, as well as the repulsive channels. Methods to extract the kappa(800) signal from the lattice spectrum are presented. Using pseudo-data, we estimate the precision that lattice data should have to allow for a clear-cut extraction of this resonance. To put the results into context, in particular the required high precision on the lattice data, the sigma(600), the P-wave resonances K*(892) and rho(770), and the repulsive piK, pipi phases are analyzed as well.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that extracting the kappa(800) resonance from lattice QCD spectra requires high-precision lattice data because the resonance is broad and does not produce a sharp avoided level crossing signal; the authors estimate the required precision using pseudo-data generated from unitarized chiral perturbation theory with next-to-leading order terms.

## Method

The authors employed the inverse amplitude method within unitarized chiral perturbation theory (UCHPT) at next-to-leading order, fitting low-energy constants to meson-meson partial wave data with special weight on the piK channel containing the kappa(800). They then discretized the meson-meson loop function to impose finite volume boundary conditions, solving the determinant equation for lattice energy levels at different box sizes L. Using these predicted levels as pseudo-data, they fitted an energy-expanded potential to reconstruct the infinite-volume amplitude and extract the resonance pole position.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism developed for the Delta(1232) resonance by Bernard (2007) and for the sigma(600) and f0(980) by Doring (2011) to the piK sector with the kappa(800) resonance. It goes beyond prior studies by incorporating next-to-leading order chiral terms, which introduce higher powers of scattering energy in the potential, requiring a systematic expansion in s for resonance extraction. The approach enables model-independent reconstruction of broad resonances from lattice data without assuming Breit-Wigner forms, which are inadequate for the kappa(800) and sigma(600) as noted by Black (1998), Aitala (2002), and Bugg (2005).
