# Charmonium production in hot magnetized hyperonic matter -- effects of baryonic Dirac sea and pseudoscalar-vector meson mixing

> We investigate the medium modifications of the masses of pseudoscalar open charm ($D$ and $\bar D$) mesons and the charmonium state ($ψ(3770)$) in hot isospin asymmetric strange hadronic medium in the presence of an external magnetic field within a chiral effective model. The in-medium partial decay widths of $ψ(3770)$ to $D\bar D$ mesons are computed from the in-medium masses of the initial and final state mesons. These are computed using two light quark pair creation models - (I) the $^3P_0$ model and (II) a field theoretical (FT) model of composite hadrons with quark (and antiquark) constituents. The production cross-sections of $ψ(3770)$, arising from scattering of the $D$ and $\bar D$ mesons, are computed from the relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral function expressed in terms of the in-medium masses and the decay widths of the charmonium state. The effects of the magnetic field are considered due to the Dirac sea (DS) of the baryons, the mixing of the pseudoscalar and vector meson (PV mixing) and the Landau level contributions for the charged hadrons. The production cross-sections of $ψ(3770)$ arising due to scattering of $D^+D^-(D^0\bar{D}^0)$ mesons in the hot magnetized strange hadronic matter are observed to have distinct peak positions, when the magnetic field is large, due to the mass difference of the transverse and longitudinal components of $ψ(3770)$, arising from PV mixing. These can have observable consequences on the dilepton spectra and the production of the charm mesons in ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy ion collision experiments, where the produced magnetic field is huge.

## Key claim

The production cross-sections of the charmonium state ψ(3770) from D and anti-D meson scattering in hot, magnetized, hyperonic matter exhibit distinct peak positions at large magnetic fields because the longitudinal and transverse components of ψ(3770) acquire non-degenerate masses due to pseudoscalar-vector (PV) mixing, a detail the abstract mentions but whose observable consequence is the splitting of the cross-section peaks.

## Method

The authors compute in-medium masses of pseudoscalar D and anti-D mesons and the ψ(3770) charmonium state within a chiral SU(3) effective model extended to SU(4), solved in mean-field approximation for hot, isospin-asymmetric strange hadronic matter under an external magnetic field. The partial decay widths of ψ(3770) to D anti-D pairs are calculated using two light quark pair creation models: the ³P₀ model and a field theoretical (FT) model of composite hadrons. The production cross-sections are then obtained from the relativistic Breit-Wigner spectral function using the in-medium masses and decay widths, with magnetic field effects included via baryonic Dirac sea contributions, PV mixing, Landau level contributions for charged hadrons, and anomalous magnetic moments of baryons.

## Key numbers

- The magnetic field strength in heavy-ion collisions is estimated to be of order 2 m_π² at RHIC and around 15 m_π² at LHC.
- For magnetized nuclear matter at baryon density ρ_B = ρ_0, the Dirac sea effect leads to inverse magnetic catalysis (IMC), a drop in the magnitude of light quark condensates with increasing magnetic field, contrary to magnetic catalysis observed at ρ_B = 0.
- The production cross-sections of ψ(3770) from D⁺D⁻ and D⁰ anti-D⁰ scattering show distinct peak positions when the magnetic field is large, due to the mass difference between transverse and longitudinal components of ψ(3770) arising from PV mixing.

## Context

This work extends prior studies of charmed meson properties in nuclear matter within the chiral SU(3) model, previously generalized to SU(4) by Amarindam et al. and Amarvepja et al., and builds on investigations of magnetic field effects on open charm and charmonium by Reddy (2018), Dhale (2018), and Amal (2018). It incorporates baryonic Dirac sea contributions following the approach of Haber (2014) and Arghya (2018), and PV mixing effects studied for heavy flavored mesons by Charmdw_mag and Open_charm_mag_AM_SPM. The results enable predictions for dilepton spectra and charm meson production in ultra-relativistic peripheral heavy-ion collisions where strong magnetic fields are present.
