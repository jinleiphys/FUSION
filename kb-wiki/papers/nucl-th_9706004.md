# In-medium dependence and Coulomb effects of the pion production in heavy ion collisions

> The properties of the high energy pions observed in heavy ion collisions, in particular in the system Au on Au at 1 GeV/nucleon are investigated. The reaction dynamics is described within the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) approach. It is shown that high energy pions freeze out early and originate from the hot, compressed matter. $N^*$--resonances are found to give an important contribution toward the high energy tail of the pion. Further the role of in-medium effects in the description of charged pion yields and spectra is investigated using a microscopic potential derived from the Brueckner G-matrix which is obtained with the Reid soft-core potential. It is seen that the high energy part of the spectra is relatively more suppressed due to in-medium effects as compared to the low energy part. A comparision to experiments further demonstrates that the present calculations describe reasonably well the neutral (TAPS) and charged (FOPI) pion spectra. The observed energy dependence of the $π^-/π^+$ ratio, i.e. deviations from the isobar model prediction, is due to Coulomb effects and again indicate that high energy pions probe the hot and dense phase of the reaction. These findings are confirmed independently by a simple phase space analysis.

## Key claim
High energy pions in Au+Au collisions at 1 GeV/nucleon freeze out early (around 20 fm/c) and originate from the hot, compressed phase of the reaction, but the empirical determination of the freeze-out radius from measured pi-/pi+ ratios should be performed carefully because the scenario is likely not that of an equilibrated thermalized source but rather of two counterstreaming nuclear matter currents.

## Method
The reaction dynamics was described using the Quantum Molecular Dynamics (QMD) approach, a semiclassical transport model including Fermi motion, stochastic scattering with Pauli blocking, and resonance creation/reabsorption. The nuclear mean field was derived either from a phenomenological Skyrme force (SMD) with a soft equation of state (K=200 MeV) or from a microscopic Brueckner G-matrix potential (GMAT) obtained by solving the Bethe-Goldstone equation with the Reid soft-core interaction. Pions were propagated with Coulomb interactions between baryons and charged pions, and the analysis included Delta(1232) and N*(1440) resonances with their one-pion and two-pion decay channels.

## Key numbers
- For Au+Au at 1 GeV/nucleon, the maximum baryon density achieved in central collisions is about 2 times normal nuclear matter density (rho_0 = 0.16 fm^-3), reached at about 25 fm/c.
- Pions with transverse momentum p_t ~ 200 MeV/c freeze out at t ~ 30 fm/c; pions with p_t ~ 500 MeV/c freeze out at t ~ 20 fm/c.
- The contribution from N* resonances to the total pion multiplicity is about 5%, with a maximum contribution of about 8% at freeze-out time t ~ 20 fm/c.
- The isobar model for Au+Au gives pi-:pi0:pi+ = 1.37:1:0.71, yielding pi-/pi+ = 1.95 for Delta-decay pions and pi-/pi+ = 1.70 for N*-decay pions.
- The experimental pi-/pi+ ratio approaches a constant value of about 0.93 in the high momentum limit.
- From the observed ratio and slope parameter T = 94.6 MeV, the Coulomb energy V_C is estimated to be about 35 MeV, giving a pion source radius R of 4.5 fm (assuming Z=110) and a participant density of about 4 rho_0; using a more realistic density of 2 rho_0 gives R = 6.7 fm.

## Context
This work extends previous QMD and BUU transport model studies of pion production at SIS energies (Bass 1994, Teis 1996, Pelte 1996) by including N*(1440) resonances and comparing phenomenological Skyrme forces with a microscopic Brueckner G-matrix potential. The finding that high energy pions probe the compressed phase supports earlier work by Bass (1994) using a hard equation of state with only Delta resonances. The analysis of the pi-/pi+ ratio and Coulomb effects builds on the isobar model of Stock (1986) and the Coulomb shift formalism of Gyulassy (1981), and addresses the empirical source-size determination method proposed by Oeschler (1996). The results enable more careful interpretation of charged pion ratios as probes of the high-density phase in heavy ion collisions.
