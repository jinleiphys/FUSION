# Universality in Few-Body Systems

> Low-energy universality in atomic few-body systems as a result of a large two-body scattering length has gained a lot of attention recently. Here, I discuss recent progress in describing the three-body recombination of cold atoms in terms of a finite set of universal scaling functions and review results for the recombination length of cesium-133 atoms obtained with these functions. Furthermore, I will consider the inclusion of effective range corrections and the relevance for further calculations in atomic and nuclear physics.

## Key claim

Low-energy universality in three-body systems of identical bosons with large two-body scattering length allows observables to be expressed as functions of the scattering length and a single three-body parameter, but the universal predictions for the recombination length of cesium-133 atoms require an additional parameter eta* to account for non-universal effects from deep dimer recombination channels.

## Method

The author uses an effective field theory (EFT) for short-range interactions, formulated with contact interactions and a three-body force at leading order for proper renormalization. Universal scaling functions from Efimov's radial law are extracted by calculating elastic atom-dimer scattering amplitudes over a range of energies and three-body parameters, then applied to compute the three-body recombination length for cesium-133 atoms. Range corrections are included systematically up to next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) using a subtraction formalism that avoids introducing additional three-body parameters at higher orders.

## Key numbers

- The Efimov scaling factor for identical bosons is s0 ≈ 1.00624, giving the geometric progression of trimer binding energies as B3^(n) = (e^(-2π/s0))^(n-n*) κ*^2/m
- The Innsbruck group observed a resonant enhancement in three-body recombination of cesium-133 atoms at a ≈ -850 a0 (where a0 is the Bohr radius)
- The triton binding energy calculated with the subtraction formalism at LO is Bt^(LO) = 8.08 MeV, at NLO is Bt^(NLO) = 8.19 MeV, and at NNLO is Bt^(NNLO) = 8.54 MeV, using the neutron-deuteron scattering length and = 0.65 fm as input
- The experimental triton binding energy is Bt^exp = 8.48 MeV

## Context

This work extends the Efimov physics framework (Efimov 1970) by using an effective field theory to systematically compute universal scaling functions and range corrections, building on prior EFT developments by Bedaque (1998) and Hammer (2001). The results enable precise comparison with the Innsbruck group's cesium-133 recombination experiments (Grimm 2006) and improve predictions for nuclear three-body observables like the triton binding energy and the Phillips line correlation. Future applications include computing electroweak few-nucleon observables relevant to nuclear astrophysics, such as the pd → γ^3He reaction.
