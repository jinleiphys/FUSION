# A Lagrangian formulation of relativistic Israel-Stewart hydrodynamics

> We rederive relativistic hydrodynamics as a Lagrangian effective theory using the doubled coordinates technique, allowing us to include dissipative terms. We include Navier-Stokes shear and bulk terms, as well as Israel-Stewart relaxation time terms, within this formalism. We show how the inclusion of shear viscosity, and the requirement of a bounded energy-momentum "vacuum", forces the inclusion of the Israel-Stewart term into the theory, thereby providing a justification for the origin and uniqueness of these terms.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the Navier-Stokes Lagrangian is fundamentally unstable because it contains a term that breaks volume-preserving diffeomorphism symmetry and produces only a saddle point rather than a minimum in the action; the Israel-Stewart relaxation-time terms are then required to stabilize the theory by promoting shear stress to independent degrees of freedom, providing a Lagrangian justification for their origin and uniqueness.

## Method

The authors rederive relativistic hydrodynamics as a Lagrangian effective field theory using the "doubled coordinates" (closed-time-path) formalism, which allows dissipative terms to be included in a Lagrangian framework. They construct the ideal fluid Lagrangian from scalar fields \(\phi^I\) representing comoving coordinates, then add first-order dissipative terms for bulk and shear viscosity using doubled degrees of freedom \(\phi_\pm\). For the Israel-Stewart extension, they promote the shear stress tensor \(\pi_{\mu\nu}\) and bulk scalar \(\Pi\) to independent degrees of freedom via a new symmetric matrix \(X_{IJ}\), writing a Lagrangian that includes relaxation-time terms and ensures a bounded action.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity coefficient is given by \(\eta = B^{3/2}(\bar{z}_{003} + \bar{z}_{033} + \bar{z}_{303} + \bar{z}_{333})\), where \(B \equiv \det|\partial_\mu \phi^I \partial^\mu \phi^J|\) and \(\bar{z}_{ijk}\) are coefficients from the CTP Lagrangian.
* The bulk viscosity \(\zeta\) is expressed as a lengthy combination of coefficients \(\bar{z}_{ijk}\) and their derivatives with respect to \(K^l_\gamma K^{m\gamma}\), involving terms proportional to \(B^{3/2}\) and \(B^{5/2}\).
* The Israel-Stewart relaxation-time equations for shear and bulk are \(\tau_\pi^\eta \Delta^{\kappa \mu} \Delta^{\zeta \nu} u^\alpha \partial_\alpha \pi_{\kappa\zeta} + \pi^{\mu\nu} = \sigma^{\mu\nu}_\eta + \mathcal{O}((\partial u)^2)\) and \(\tau_\pi^\zeta u^\alpha \partial_\alpha \Pi + \Pi = \sigma_\zeta + \mathcal{O}((\partial u)^2)\), where \(\sigma^{\mu\nu}_\eta\) and \(\sigma_\zeta\) are the Navier-Stokes shear and bulk terms.
* The linearized dispersion relation for sound waves in Navier-Stokes is \(w - (\partial P/\partial \rho)^{1/2} k + i (4\eta / 3sT) k^2 = 0\), showing that the diffusion speed \(v = w/k \sim k\) becomes superluminal when \(k (4\eta / 3sT) \gg 1\).

## Context

This work extends the Lagrangian effective field theory approach to hydrodynamics developed by Grozdanov and Polonyi (2015) and earlier work by Dubovsky et al. (2012) on ideal fluids, by incorporating dissipative terms via the doubled-coordinate formalism of Galley (2013). It provides a Lagrangian justification for the Israel-Stewart equations (Israel and Stewart, 1979) that resolves the causality and stability problems of relativistic Navier-Stokes hydrodynamics identified by Hiscock and Lindblom (1985). The formalism enables future systematic inclusion of higher-order gradient terms and fluctuations, and clarifies the relationship between the relaxation time \(\tau_\pi\) and the microscopic scale of the theory.
