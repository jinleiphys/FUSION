# Parity and Fermions in Front-Form: An Unexpected Result

> We show that under the operation of parity the {\it front-form} $(1/2,\,0)$ and $(0,\,1/2)$ Weyl spinors (massive or massless) do not get interchanged. This has the important consequence that if a front-form theory containing $(1/2,\,0)\oplus(0,\,1/2)$ representation space has to be parity covariant then one must study the evolution of a physical system not only along $x^+$ but also along the $x^-$ direction. As a result of our analysis, we find an indication that there may be no halving of the degrees of freedom in the front form of field theories.

# Parity and Fermions in Front-Form: An Unexpected Result

## Key claim

Under the operation of parity, front-form (1/2, 0) and (0, 1/2) Weyl spinors (massive or massless) do not get interchanged, contrary to the instant-form behavior. This means that if a front-form theory containing the (1/2, 0) ⊕ (0, 1/2) representation space is to be parity covariant, one must study evolution along both the x⁺ and x⁻ directions simultaneously, and there may be no halving of degrees of freedom in the front form of field theories.

## Method

The authors examine the transformation properties of (1/2, 0) and (0, 1/2) Weyl spinors under parity in the front form, comparing the boost parameters for evolution along x⁺ versus x⁻. They derive the explicit boost transformations for both evolution directions and show that the parity operator maps (1/2, 0)^{[x⁺]} ⊕ (0, 1/2)^{[x⁺]} one-to-one onto (1/2, 0)^{[x⁻]} ⊕ (0, 1/2)^{[x⁻]}, rather than interchanging the two Weyl spaces within a single evolution direction. The analysis is purely kinematical and based on space-time symmetries, using the front-form helicity operators and the Melosh transformation framework previously developed by the authors in Ahluwalia and Sawicki (1993).

## Key numbers

- The boost parameter for front-form evolution along x⁺ is defined as β = η(α vʳ, -i α vʳ, 1), where α = [1 - exp(-η)]⁻¹ and vʳ = v_x + i v_y.
- The hyperbolic functions for the front-form boost parameter satisfy cosh(η/2) = Ω(p⁺ + m) and sinh(η/2) = Ω(p⁺ - m), with Ω = [1/(2m)] √(m/p⁺).
- Under parity, the front-form spinors satisfy u^{[x⁻]}_μ[p'^μ] = +γ⁰ u^{[x⁺]}_h[p^μ] and v^{[x⁻]}_μ[p'^μ] = -γ⁰ v^{[x⁺]}_h[p^μ], where p'^μ is the parity-transformed four-momentum.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of (j,0)⊕(0,j) representation spaces in the front form (Ahluwalia and Sawicki, 1993) and corrects an oversight in that paper regarding parity transformation properties. The result contradicts the widely-favored conjecture that massive front-form theories can maintain parity covariance with evolution along a single light-front direction. The analysis builds on earlier considerations by Soper (1971) and Jacob (1993) regarding parity in the front form, and agrees with McCartor's results (1988) for massless particles that a spin-1/2 system must be specified on both x⁺ and x⁻ surfaces. The work enables further investigation into whether the halving of degrees of freedom in front-form field theories, which has been a source of discomfort since Penrose (1963), may not actually occur.
