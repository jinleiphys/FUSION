# Systematics of fusion probability in "hot" fusion reactions

> The fusion probability in "hot" fusion reactions leading to the synthesis of super-heavy nuclei is investigated systematically. The quasi-fission barrier influences the formation of the super-heavy nucleus around the "island of stability" in addition to the shell correction. Based on the quasi-fission barrier height obtained with the Skyrme energy-density functional, we propose an analytical expression for the description of the fusion probability, with which the measured evaporation residual cross sections can be reproduced acceptably well. Simultaneously, some special fusion reactions for synthesizing new elements 119 and 120 are studied. The predicted evaporation residual cross sections for 50Ti+249Bk are about 10-150fb at energies around the entrance-channel Coulomb barrier. For the fusion reactions synthesizing element 120 with projectiles 54Cr and 58Fe, the cross sections fall to a few femtobarns which seems beyond the limit of the available facilities.

## Key claim

The fusion probability in "hot" fusion reactions leading to super-heavy nuclei can be described by an analytical expression based on the quasi-fission barrier height, but the parameter C in that expression must be re-adjusted if a different liquid-drop formula or different binding energies are used for calculating the fission barrier.

## Method

The authors calculated the quasi-fission barrier height using the Skyrme energy-density functional with the extended Thomas-Fermi approximation (ETF2) and used the Weizsäcker-Skyrme mass model for the masses and shell corrections of super-heavy nuclei. They then proposed an analytical formula for the fusion probability P_CN = (1/C) exp(3 B_qf + 0.3 E*), with C = exp(50 |η|) for "hot" fusion reactions, and combined this with established methods for capture cross sections and survival probability (HIVAP code) to compute evaporation residual cross sections.

## Key numbers

- For the reaction 50Ti + 249Bk leading to element 119, the predicted evaporation residual cross sections are about 10 to 150 femtobarn at energies around the entrance-channel Coulomb barrier.
- For the reactions 54Cr + 248Cm and 58Fe + 244Pu leading to element 120, the predicted cross sections fall to a few femtobarns.
- The estimated uncertainty of the calculated evaporation residual cross section is about a factor of 4.4 at energies above the mean barrier height B_m, with individual uncertainties of about factors of 1.18 for σ_cap, 1.85 for W_sur, and 2.0 for P_CN.
- The optimal evaporation residual cross section predicted in this work for 50Ti+249Bk is about 35 femtobarn, for 50Ti+249Cf about 20 femtobarn, for 54Cr+248Cm about 5 femtobarn, and for 58Fe+244Pu about 3 femtobarn.

## Context

This work extends the systematic study of fusion probabilities in super-heavy element synthesis by Wang et al. (2008) and applies the Weizsäcker-Skyrme mass model of Liu et al. (2011) instead of the finite range droplet model. The results are compared with predictions from the fusion-by-diffusion model of Liu et al. (2006), the dinuclear system model of Nan Wang et al., and the model of Zagrebaev (2008), showing that for 50Ti+249Bk and 50Ti+249Cf the cross sections from this work are in the tens of femtobarn range, an order of magnitude lower than some other predictions. The work enables predictions for the synthesis of elements 119 and 120, indicating that the reactions 54Cr+248Cm and 58Fe+244Pu may be beyond the limit of available facilities.
