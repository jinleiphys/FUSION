# Dilepton production from virtual bremsstrahlung induced by proton capture

> Dilepton production following radiative capture of a proton on a nuclear target is studied in the Impulse Approximation. The cross section is decomposed in terms of four time-like nuclear structure functions through a longitudinal-transverse separation of the nuclear current. Using a simple PWIA model, cross sections and conversion factors are calculated for capture reactions p + n >> d + e^+ e^-, p + p >> 2He + e^+ e^- and p + 11B >> 12C + e^+ e^- at proton energies of 100-200 MeV. The result is compared with a recent measurement of the conversion factor for p + 11B.

## Key claim

The cross section for dilepton production in proton capture can be decomposed into four time-like nuclear structure functions (longitudinal, transverse, and two interference terms) that are experimentally separable through the angular dependence of the emitted electron-positron pair; however, the longitudinal conversion factor is small compared to the transverse one and peaks at larger invariant masses, and the preliminary experimental data for p + 11B do not agree with the PWIA calculations presented.

## Method

The cross section for the reaction p + A → (A+1) + e⁺ + e⁻ was derived in the Impulse Approximation using a longitudinal-transverse (LT) decomposition of the nuclear current, analogous to the (e,e'p) reaction. A non-relativistic one-body current operator with Sachs form factors (G_E, G_M) was employed in a Plane Wave Impulse Approximation (PWIA) model, and the cross section was expressed in terms of four structure functions (W_L, W_T, W_TT, W_LT). Calculations were performed for p + n → d + e⁺ e⁻, p + p → ²He + e⁺ e⁻, and p + ¹¹B → ¹²C + e⁺ e⁻, with the nuclear structure input for ¹²C taken from DWIA momentum distributions from prior (p,γ) calculations.

## Key numbers

- For 200 MeV incident protons, the virtual photon invariant mass M reaches up to about 100 MeV.
- The longitudinal conversion factor R_L for pn capture reaches a maximum at T = 10 MeV incident proton energy before decreasing at lower energies.
- The time-like nucleon form factor variation over the relevant invariant mass range is less than 3% on the current matrix elements.
- The calculated conversion ratios are of order α (the fine-structure constant).
- For p + ¹¹B → ¹²C + e⁺ e⁻ at 98 MeV incident proton energy, the calculated conversion factors show sizeable differences between HF, RPA, and np-nh treatments of initial state interactions.

## Context

This work extends the formalism of virtual bremsstrahlung in nucleon-nucleon systems to proton-nucleus capture, providing a framework analogous to the LT decomposition used in (e,e'p) reactions as developed by Boffi (1980s) and Frullani and Mougey (1984). The approach enables separation of longitudinal and transverse nuclear response in the time-like region, which is relevant for understanding medium effects in heavy-ion reactions and for studying the nucleon form factor in the time-like region. The results are compared with measurements by the Uppsala group (Wiescher et al., 1993) for p + ¹¹B, though the calculations do not reproduce the experimental conversion factors. The work enables future extensions to include initial state interactions, exchange currents, and the more general case of virtual bremsstrahlung in p + A reactions at higher energies.
