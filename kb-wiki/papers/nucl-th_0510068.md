# Phi-Phi Back-to-Back Correlations in Finite Expanding Systems

> Back-to-Back Correlations (BBC) of particle-antiparticle pairs are predicted to appear if hot and dense hadronic matter is formed in high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions. The BBC are related to in-medium mass-modification and squeezing of the quanta involved. Although the suppression of finite emission times were already known, the effects of finite system sizes and of collective phenomena had not been studied yet. Thus, for testing the survival and magnitude of the effect in more realistic situations, we study the BBC when mass-modification occurs in a finite sized, thermalized medium, considering a non-relativistically expanding fireball with finite emission time, and evaluating the width of the back-to-back correlation function. We show that the BBC signal indeed survives the expansion and flow effects, with sufficient magnitude to be observed at RHIC.

## Key claim

The Back-to-Back Correlations (BBC) signal from particle-antiparticle pairs with in-medium mass modification survives expansion and flow effects in a finite, non-relativistically expanding fireball with sufficient magnitude to be observed at RHIC, provided the mass shift occurs in a sufficiently large region and the emission time is not too long.

## Method

The authors model a thermalized, finite-sized fireball that expands non-relativistically with a spherically symmetric flow velocity profile, using a hydrodynamical ensemble approach. They evaluate the squeezed amplitude and the BBC correlation function for φφ boson pairs by applying Bogoliubov-Valatin transformations between in-medium and asymptotic operators, assuming a small mass shift (m⋆ − m)/m ≪ 1 and a weak flow coupling (back-to-back pairs emitted near the system center). The calculation considers two freeze-out scenarios: sudden emission and a smeared emission with a finite duration of Δτ ∼ 2 fm/c, and introduces Gaussian profiles to model the spatial extent of the mass-shifted region.

## Key numbers

- The BBC signal is predicted to be significant for transverse momenta pT < 2 GeV/c.
- A finite emission duration of Δτ ∼ 2 fm/c drastically reduces the BBC magnitude compared to the sudden emission case (dashed curves in Figure 2 decreased by a factor of 400).
- The BBC correlation function is evaluated for back-to-back momenta k = 500 MeV/c and k = 1000 MeV/c, with and without flow, for a system with radius R = 7 fm, temperature T = 140 MeV, and mean expansion velocity ⟨u⟩ = 0.05.
- The maximum BBC signal occurs when the in-medium mass m⋆ is approximately equal to the vacuum mass m.

## Context

This work extends earlier predictions of Back-to-Back Correlations for boson-antiboson pairs (Asakawa, Csörgő, Gyulassy, 1999) and fermion-antifermion pairs (Padula et al., 2001) by incorporating finite system sizes and collective expansion effects that had not been studied previously. The authors use the non-relativistic hydrodynamical model of Csörgő (2004) and the freeze-out formulation of Makhlin and Sinyukov (1988). The results enable experimental searches for the BBC signal at RHIC, though the authors note that future work must incorporate model-based mass shifts, more realistic flow calculations, and experimental acceptance constraints.
