# Entrance-channel Mass-asymmetry Dependence of Compound-nucleus Formation Time in Light Heavy-ion Reactions

> The entrance-channel mass-asymmetry dependence of the compound nucleus formation time in light heavy-ion reactions has been investigated within the framework of semiclassical dissipative collision models. the model calculations have been succesfully applied to the formation of the $^{38}$Ar compound nucleus as populated via the $^{9}$Be+$^{29}$Si, $^{11}$B+$^{27}$Al, $^{12}$C+$^{26}$Mg and $^{19}$F+$^{19}$F entrance channels. The shape evolution of several other light composite systems appears to be consistent with the so-called "Fusion Inhibition Factor" which has been experimentally observed. As found previously in more massive systems for the fusion-evaporation process, the entrance-channel mass-asymmetry degree of freedom appears to determine the competition between the different mechanisms as well as the time scales involved.

## Key claim
The entrance-channel mass-asymmetry degree of freedom determines the compound nucleus formation time in light heavy-ion reactions, with more asymmetric systems reaching an equilibrated shape faster than symmetric ones, consistent with the Fusion Inhibition Factor observed experimentally. This conclusion is drawn from semiclassical dissipative collision model calculations for the $^{38}$Ar compound nucleus, but the authors note that for very light systems the time scales are so compressed that direct measurements may not distinguish between competing processes.

## Method
The authors used the semiclassical dissipative collision model code HICOL, which assumes full one-body dissipation, to follow the shape evolution of dinuclear systems toward equilibrium in collective and thermodynamic degrees of freedom. They calculated the time evolution of the nuclear deformation $\beta$ for four entrance channels leading to $^{38}$Ar at 55 MeV excitation energy, for angular momenta L = 0, 5$\hbar$, 10$\hbar$, and 15$\hbar$. The time needed to reach 80 percent of the final equilibrium deformation was extracted for each entrance channel and compared with the Fusion Inhibition Factor derived from experimental reduced fusion cross sections.

## Key numbers
- The $^{38}$Ar compound nucleus was studied at 55 MeV excitation energy for four entrance channels: $^{9}$Be+$^{29}$Si, $^{11}$B+$^{27}$Al, $^{12}$C+$^{26}$Mg, and $^{19}$F+$^{19}$F.
- For heavier systems (A$_{CN}$ $\leq$ 160), the $^{164}$Yb compound nucleus formed via $^{16}$O + $^{148}$Sm showed a formation time of $\tau$ $\approx$ 10$^{-21}$ sec, while the more symmetric $^{64}$Ni + $^{100}$Mo showed $\tau$ $\approx$ 10$^{-20}$ sec at E$_{CN}^{*}$ = 49 MeV.
- The shape evolution calculations for $^{38}$Ar were performed for L = 0, 5$\hbar$, 10$\hbar$, and 15$\hbar$, with all these partial waves contributing to compound nucleus formation.
- The time scale for shape evolution in the $^{38}$Ar system is indicated in units of 10$^{-22}$ sec.

## Context
This work extends the findings of Thoennessen et al. (1993, 1995) on entrance-channel mass-asymmetry dependence of compound nucleus formation times from heavier systems (A$_{CN}$ $\approx$ 110 and 160) down to the light mass region (A$_{CN}$ $\leq$ 50). It builds on the Fusion Inhibition Factor systematics established by Anjos et al. (1990) for light systems, which showed a correlation between mass asymmetry and the fraction of flux that penetrates the fusion barrier but does not lead to compound nucleus formation. The results enable future experimental attempts to measure fission time scales in this light-mass region, which the authors state will be soon undertaken.
