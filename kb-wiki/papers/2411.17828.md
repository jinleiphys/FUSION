# CP violation in cold dense quark matter and axion effects on the non-radial oscillations of neutron stars

> Charge-conjugation and parity violation in strong interaction for cold dense quark matter is studied with axions of quantum chromodynamic within the three flavor Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model that includes the coupling of axions to quarks. We first calculate the effective potential for axions at finite baryon density and zero temperature including the effects of a first order chiral phase transition. Using the equation of state for quark matter with axions and a hadronic matter equation of state in the ambit of a relativistic mean field theory in quantum hadrodynamics, we discuss the hadron-quark phase transition. Inclusion of axions reduces the critical density for chiral transition. We use a Gibbs construct for the hadron-quark phase transition satisfying the constraints of beta equilibrium and charge neutrality as appropriate for the neutron star matter. The equation of state so obtained is used to investigate the structure of hybrid neutron stars. It is found that with the presence of axions, it is possible to have stable hybrid neutron stars having an inner core of quark matter both in pure quark matter phase as well as in a mixed phase with hyperonic matter along with a outer core of hyperonic matter and is in agreement with modern astrophysical constraints. We also discuss the properties of non-radial oscillations of such hybrid neutron stars. It is observed that the quadrupolar fundamental modes ($f$-modes) for such hybrid neutron stars get substantial enhancements both due to a larger quark core in the presence of axions and from the hyperons as compared to a canonical nucleonic neutron stars.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including axions (the pseudo-Goldstone bosons of the Peccei-Quinn mechanism) in the equation of state for dense quark matter reduces the critical density for the chiral phase transition and enables stable hybrid neutron stars with quark matter cores, but this result depends on the specific choice of the axion parameter \(\theta = \langle a \rangle / f_a\) and the vector coupling strength \(G_v\) within the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model.

## Method

The authors used the three-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model with a Kobayashi-Maskawa-'t Hooft determinant interaction that couples axions to quarks via the parameter \(\theta = \langle a \rangle / f_a\). They computed the thermodynamic potential at zero temperature and finite baryon density, including scalar and pseudoscalar condensates, and constructed the quark matter equation of state. For hadronic matter, they employed a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with hyperonic degrees of freedom using the NL3\(\omega\rho\) parameter set. The hadron-quark phase transition was treated via a Gibbs construction under beta equilibrium and charge neutrality constraints, and the resulting hybrid equations of state were used to solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure and the linearized fluid perturbation equations for non-radial oscillation modes.

## Key numbers

- Critical quark chemical potential for chiral transition: \(\mu_{\rm Q}^c(\theta = 0) = 367.37\ {\rm MeV}\) without axions; \(\mu_{\rm Q}^c(\theta = \pi) = 351.4\ {\rm MeV}\) with axions, showing a reduction.
- At the chiral transition for \(\theta = 0\), baryon density jumps from \(n_{\rm B} = 0\) to \(n_{\rm B} = 0.38\ {\rm fm}^{-3}\); up and down quark masses drop from vacuum values of \(M_{u,d} = 367.65\ {\rm MeV}\) to \(M_u = 81.9\ {\rm MeV}\) and \(M_d = 62.4\ {\rm MeV}\); strange quark mass drops from \(M_s = 549\ {\rm MeV}\) to \(M_s = 465.79\ {\rm MeV}\).
- NJL model parameters: \(\Lambda = 602.3\ {\rm MeV}\), \(G_s\Lambda^2 = 1.835\), \(K\Lambda^5 = 12.36\), \(m_u = m_d = 5.5\ {\rm MeV}\), \(m_s = 140.7\ {\rm MeV}\).
- Hyperon potentials in nuclear matter: \(U_{\Lambda}^{N} = -28\ {\rm MeV}\), \(U_{\Sigma}^{N} = 30\ {\rm MeV}\), \(U_{\Xi}^{N} = -18\ {\rm MeV}\) at saturation density.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of axion effects in dense matter using the NJL model (Chatterjee 2011, Chatterjee 2014) and the Polyakov-loop extended NJL model (Lu 2018, Bandyopadhyay 2019, Ali 2020) by focusing on zero temperature and finite baryon density relevant for neutron stars. It builds on the recent study by Lopes (2022) which showed that axions stabilize massive hybrid neutron stars by weakening the hadron-quark phase transition. The paper also addresses the "masquerade problem" identified by Alford (2004), where different compositions of neutron star cores produce similar mass-radius relations, by showing that non-radial oscillation \(f\)-modes can distinguish hybrid stars with axions from canonical nucleonic neutron stars, which is relevant for interpreting gravitational wave signals from future detectors.
