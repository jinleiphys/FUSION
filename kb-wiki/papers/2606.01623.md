# Quantum dominance of coherent bremsstrahlung in $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn scattering at 25 MeV/u

> We present quantum-mechanical calculations of bremsstrahlung in the $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn at 25 MeV/u reproducing the measured photon spectrum over the full energy range. For the first time, we quantitatively determine the incoherent-to-coherent ratio in the photon spectrum. This ratio is extremely small, ranging from $10^{-11}$ to $10^{-4}$, which demonstrates that coherent emission dominates throughout the measured energy range. This behavior is in sharp contrast to proton-nucleus scattering, where incoherent emission dominates because of the leading role of nucleon magnetic moments. This contrast is illustrated by the TAPS Collaboration data for $p + ^{197}{\rm Au}$ collisions at a proton beam energy of 190 MeV, where the corresponding ratio reaches $10^{3}$-$10^{5}$. We find that incoherent-to-coherent ratios explain the difference between the two spectra in unified picture: (1) In proton--nucleus scattering, the spectrum contains a pronounced hump, (2) In $^{124}$Sn + $^{124}$Sn scattering, the spectrum decreases monotonically and has a nearly logarithmic shape. Our results identify a previously unexplored quantum regime of bremsstrahlung emission in nuclear reactions and open a new route for studying coherent effects in heavy-ion collisions.

# Quantum dominance of coherent bremsstrahlung in \(^{124}\)Sn + \(^{124}\)Sn scattering at 25 MeV/u

## Key claim

The authors claim that coherent bremsstrahlung emission dominates over incoherent emission in \(^{124}\)Sn + \(^{124}\)Sn scattering at 25 MeV/u, with the incoherent-to-coherent ratio ranging from \(10^{-11}\) to \(10^{-4}\) across the measured photon energy range. This claim is conditional on the use of a quantum-mechanical formalism extended from proton-nucleus scattering, and on the specific approximation that the monopole effective charge \(Z_{\rm eff}^{\rm (mon,0)}\) is replaced by a more accurate \(k_{\rm ph}\)-dependent form to avoid the unphysical zero that would otherwise arise for this symmetric reaction.

## Method

The authors extended a previously developed bremsstrahlung formalism for proton-nucleus scattering (Maydanyuk et al., 2015-2023) to nucleus-nucleus collisions by generalizing the Pauli Hamiltonian to include many-nucleon projectiles and targets. They calculated the full bremsstrahlung matrix element as a sum of coherent electric and magnetic terms (\(M_p^{(E)}\), \(M_p^{(M)}\)) and incoherent magnetic and background terms (\(M_{\Delta M}\), \(M_k\)), using relative-motion wave functions for the two nuclei with quantum numbers \(l_i=0\), \(l_f=1\), and photon orbital quantum number \(l_{\rm ph}=1\). The bremsstrahlung probability was obtained by renormalizing the calculated cross section to experimental data from the CSHINE spectrometer at HIRFL, using a single global normalization factor over the full energy range.

## Key numbers

- Incoherent-to-coherent ratio for \(^{124}\)Sn + \(^{124}\)Sn at 25 MeV/u: ranges from \(10^{-11}\) to \(10^{-4}\) across the measured photon energy range.
- For comparison, the incoherent-to-coherent ratio for \(p + ^{197}\)Au at 190 MeV proton beam energy reaches \(10^{3}\) to \(10^{5}\).
- Center-of-mass energy of relative motion: \(E_{\rm scm} = 1550\) MeV, derived from the laboratory beam energy of 3100 MeV (124 nucleons \(\times\) 25 MeV/u).
- The ratio \(m_{\rm p}\bar{\mu}_{\rm pn}^{(A)}/(2\mu) = 0.000310128\), quantifying the suppression of the magnetic coherent term relative to the electric coherent term in the symmetric Sn+Sn system.
- The effective magnetic moment parameter for \(^{124}\)Sn: \(\bar{\mu}_{\rm pn}^{(A)} = -0.03845589578\,\mu_N\).

## Context

This work extends the bremsstrahlung formalism previously developed for proton-nucleus scattering (Maydanyuk et al., 2015; Maydanyuk, 2023) to heavy-ion collisions, and directly reproduces experimental data from the CSHINE collaboration (2026). The results contradict the expectation from proton-nucleus scattering (Goethem, 2002; Maydanyuk, 2023), where incoherent emission dominates due to nucleon magnetic moments, by demonstrating that coherent emission from collective nuclear motion dominates in symmetric heavy-ion collisions. This enables a new route for studying coherent effects in heavy-ion reactions and provides a unified framework to explain the qualitatively different spectral shapes observed in proton-nucleus (hump-shaped) versus nucleus-nucleus (monotonically decreasing, nearly logarithmic) bremsstrahlung spectra.
