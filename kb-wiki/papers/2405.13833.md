# Flavor dependence of unpolarized quark Transverse Momentum Distributions from a global fit

> We present an extraction of the unpolarized transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution and fragmentation functions that takes into account possible differences between quark flavors and final-state hadrons. The extraction is based on experimental measurements from Drell-Yan processes and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering, whose combination is essential to distinguish flavor differences. The analysis is carried out at N$^3$LL accuracy. The extracted flavor-dependent distributions give a very good description of the data ($χ^2/N_{\rm dat} = 1.08$). The resulting uncertainties take fully into account also the uncertainties in the determination of the corresponding collinear distributions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first global extraction of unpolarized transverse-momentum-dependent parton distribution functions (TMD PDFs) and fragmentation functions (TMD FFs) that fully accounts for flavor-dependent differences among quarks and final-state hadrons, achieving a good description of the data with a reduced chi-squared of 1.08, though the results depend on the specific choices of collinear PDF and FF sets and the functional forms adopted for the nonperturbative parametrization.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of unpolarized TMD PDFs and TMD FFs at N$^3$LL accuracy using 2031 experimental data points from Drell-Yan (DY) processes and semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS). They separately parametrized five TMD PDFs (for $u$, $\bar{u}$, $d$, $\bar{d}$, and sea quarks) and five TMD FFs (favored and unfavored pion fragmentation, favored, unfavored and $s$-quark kaon fragmentation), using Monte Carlo replicas of collinear PDFs (NNPDF3.1) and FFs (a variation of MAPFF1.0) to propagate uncertainties. The analysis used the NangaParbat public code and imposed kinematic cuts of $|\mathbf{q}_T| < 0.2 Q$ for DY data and $Q > 1.4$ GeV, $0.2 < z < 0.7$ for SIDIS data.

## Key numbers

- Global fit quality: $\chi^2/N_{\rm dat} = 1.08$ for the flavor-dependent MAPTMD24 FD extraction with 96 free parameters
- Flavor-independent baseline (MAPTMD24 FI): $\chi^2_0/N_{\rm dat} = 1.40$ with 20 free parameters
- Hadron-dependent baseline (MAPTMD24 HD): $\chi^2_0/N_{\rm dat} = 1.19$ with 29 free parameters
- Total dataset: 2031 data points (484 from DY, 1547 from SIDIS)
- Collins-Soper kernel parameter $g_2 = 0.12 \pm 0.0033$ GeV in the flavor-dependent fit
- Initial scale for nonperturbative parametrization: $Q_0 = 1$ GeV
- Kinematic cuts: $Q > 1.4$ GeV, $0.2 < z < 0.7$ for SIDIS; $|\mathbf{q}_T| < 0.2 Q$ for DY

## Context

This work extends the previous MAPTMD22 extraction (Bacchetta et al., 2022) by incorporating flavor-dependent TMD PDFs and FFs, using Monte Carlo replicas of collinear PDFs and FFs as suggested by Bury et al. (2022) for DY, and reaching N$^3$LL accuracy. The flavor-dependent fit significantly improves the description of SIDIS data compared to the flavor-independent baseline, and the extracted Collins-Soper kernel shows a lower slope at large $b_T$ compared to other recent results from Moos et al. (2023), Aslan et al. (2024), and Isaacson et al. (2023). The results enable more refined theoretical predictions for future experiments and improved interpretations of hadron structure in momentum space.
