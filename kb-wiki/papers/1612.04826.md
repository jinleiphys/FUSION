# Threshold effects in P-wave bottom-strange mesons

> Using a nonrelativistic constituent quark model in which the degrees of freedom are quark-antiquark and meson-meson components, we have recently shown that the $D^{(\ast)}K$ thresholds play an important role in lowering the mass of the physical $D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$ and $D_{s1}(2460)$ states. This observation is also supported by other theoretical approaches such as lattice-regularised QCD or chiral unitary theory in coupled channels. Herein, we extend our computation to the lowest $P$-wave $B_{s}$ mesons, taking into account the corresponding $J^{P} = 0^{+}$, $1^{+}$ and $2^{+}$ bottom-strange states predicted by the naive quark model and the $BK$ and $B^{\ast}K$ thresholds. We assume that mixing with $B_{s}^{(\ast)}η$ and isospin-violating decays to $B_{s}^{(\ast)}π$ are negligible. This computation is important because there is no experimental data in the $b\bar{s}$ sector for the equivalent $j_{q}^{P}=1/2^{+}$ ($D_{s0}^{\ast}(2317)$, $D_{s1}(2460)$) heavy-quark multiplet and, as it has been seen in the $c\bar s$ sector, the naive theoretical result can be wrong by more than $100\,{\rm MeV}$. Our calculation allows to introduce the coupling with the $D$-wave $B^{\ast}K$ channel and to compute the probabilities associated with the different Fock components of the physical state.

## Key claim

The authors extend their coupled-channel constituent quark model, previously applied to charm-strange mesons, to predict masses and Fock-component probabilities for the lowest P-wave bottom-strange mesons (J^P = 0+, 1+, 2+), finding that coupling to BK and B*K thresholds lowers the bare quark-model masses by approximately 80 MeV, bringing them into agreement with lattice QCD and effective field theory predictions, though no experimental data yet exist for the j_q^P = 1/2+ doublet in the b-s sector.

## Method

The authors use a nonrelativistic constituent quark model that includes quark-antiquark and meson-meson degrees of freedom, solving coupled-channel equations via the Resonating Group Method. The quark-antiquark interaction incorporates one-gluon exchange with one-loop α_s^2 corrections, a confining potential with screening, and Goldstone-boson exchanges for light quarks; the coupling between quark-antiquark and meson-meson sectors is described by the 3P0 model for light quark pair creation, with a Gaussian form factor (Λ = 0.84 GeV) to soften the vertex at high momenta. The calculation includes BK and B*K thresholds for the 0+ and 1+ sectors, and also couples the D-wave B*K channel for the 1+ states, while neglecting B_s(*)η mixing and isospin-violating B_s(*)π decays.

## Key numbers

- Bare quark-model mass of B_s0* (0+): 5851 MeV with one-gluon exchange only; 5801 MeV after including one-loop α_s^2 corrections
- After coupling to BK threshold, B_s0* mass shifts to 5741.4 MeV, with 61.2% q-qbar and 38.8% BK probability
- BK scattering length for the 0+ sector: a_0^BK = -1.18 fm
- B_s1' (1+) mass after coupling to B*K in S-wave: 5792.5 MeV, with 44.2% B*K(S-wave) probability
- B_s1(5830) mass after coupling to B*K in both S- and D-wave: 5832.9 MeV, with 36.6% B*K(D-wave) probability
- B*K scattering length for the 1+ sector: a_0^B*K = -1.35 fm
- Broad resonance B_s0^R found at 5877.9 MeV with width 303.5 MeV; B_s1^R at 5940.4 MeV with width 271.3 MeV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous coupled-channel study of charm-strange mesons (Ortega et al., 2016) to the bottom-strange sector, where no experimental data exist for the j_q^P = 1/2+ doublet analogous to D_s0*(2317) and D_s1(2460). The results are compared primarily with a lattice QCD study by Lang et al. (2015) that explicitly incorporates B(*)K operators, and with various effective field theory predictions (e.g., Altenbuchinger et al., 2013; Cleven et al., 2010; Guo et al., 2006) and other quark model calculations (Godfrey et al., 2016; Lu et al., 2016). The approach uniquely provides Fock-component probabilities and D-wave coupling, which lattice QCD cannot currently address, and predicts that the B_s0* and B_s1' masses lie near 5720-5770 MeV, consistent with a contemporaneous study by Albaladejo et al. (2016).
