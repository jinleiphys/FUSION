# A new scissors mode on the skin of deformed neutron rich nuclei

> Covariant density functional theory is used to analyze the evolution of low-lying M1 strength in superfluid deformed nuclei in the framework of the self-consistent Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA). In nuclei with a pronounced neutron excess two scissor modes are found. Besides the conventional scissor mode, where the deformed proton and neutron distributions oscillate against each other, a new soft M1 mode is found, where the deformed neutron skin oscillates in a scissor like motion against a deformed proton-neutron core.

# A new scissors mode on the skin of deformed neutron rich nuclei

## Key claim
In deformed neutron-rich nuclei, two distinct low-lying orbital M1 modes exist: the conventional scissors mode (protons oscillating against neutrons) and a new soft mode where the deformed neutron skin oscillates in a scissor-like motion against a deformed proton-neutron core. The new mode has mixed isospin character, closer to T=0, unlike the conventional T=1 scissors mode.

## Method
The authors performed fully self-consistent Relativistic Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (RQRPA) calculations for axially deformed nuclei, using covariant density functional theory with the NL3 parameter set and monopole pairing forces. The relativistic mean field equations were solved in an anisotropic axial harmonic oscillator basis with 20 major shells, and the QRPA equations were solved using a Fourier-Bessel decomposition in cylindrical coordinates. The intrinsic M1 response was transformed to the laboratory frame using the needle approximation for projection onto angular momentum I=1.

## Key numbers
- The conventional scissors mode in 154Sm appears at 3.2 MeV, with orbital character and agreement with experimental data from ZHN (1993)
- The new skin scissors mode appears at 2.5 MeV in 154Sm, approximately 0.7 MeV below the conventional mode
- The spurious Kπ=1+ rotational mode in 154Sm lies below 0.5 MeV and exhausts more than 99% of the total spurious J±1 strength
- The conventional scissors mode at 3.2 MeV has 65% proton quasiparticle contribution and 9% neutron contribution
- The new mode at 2.5 MeV has 58% proton and 20% neutron quasiparticle contributions, with the dominant neutron component coming from the [651] and [642] Nilsson orbitals at 2.75 MeV

## Context
This work extends the study of collective modes in neutron-rich nuclei beyond the Pygmy Dipole Resonance (PDR), which was previously interpreted as a translational oscillation of the neutron skin against the core (Goriely 1998, Adrich 2005). The authors build on earlier group-theoretical predictions by Wambach and Iachello (1997) of possible skin-related scissors modes in a boson model with three degrees of freedom. The new RQRPA code for deformed systems enables microscopic confirmation of these predictions and provides a new understanding of the splitting of low-lying orbital Kπ=1+ modes observed in many deformed nuclei. Further investigations across the periodic table are needed to clarify the dependence of this mode on single-particle structure.
