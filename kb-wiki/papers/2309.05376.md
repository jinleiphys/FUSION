# Strangeness in Neutron Stars

> In this contribution I briefly review the dynamics of strange mesons and baryons with dense nuclear matter, paying a special attention to their presence in the inner core of neutron stars and the consequences for the structure of these compact stars.

## Key claim

This review paper argues that while hyperons are likely to appear in neutron star cores, their presence softens the equation of state and makes it difficult to reach the observed 2 solar mass neutron stars (the "hyperon puzzle"), and that kaon condensation remains controversial because phenomenological models tend to favor it but microscopic unitarized schemes do not support a large enough modification of antikaon mass.

## Method

The author reviews two main lines of theoretical work: (1) studies of hyperon-nucleon and hyperon-hyperon interactions in dense nuclear matter, including the role of three-body forces and alternative degrees of freedom such as Delta baryons or quark matter; (2) calculations of antikaon in-medium masses using both phenomenological relativistic mean-field models and microscopic unitarized approaches based on meson-exchange models or chiral effective Lagrangians. The review connects these microscopic dynamics to neutron star structure through the equation of state, which determines mass, radius, and tidal deformability.

## Key numbers

- Neutron star masses are typically 1-2 solar masses, with radii in the range of 10-12 km, and average density of order 10^14 g/cm^3.
- Observations have determined 2 solar mass neutron stars in the past decade (Demorest 2010, Antoniadis 2013, Cromartie 2020).
- The hyperon puzzle arises because adding hyperons softens the pressure, leading to predicted neutron star masses below the observed 2 solar mass threshold.

## Context

This review extends the discussion of strange hadrons in neutron stars covered in recent reviews by Tolos (2020) and Burgio (2021). It addresses the hyperon puzzle first identified by Chatterjee (2015) and Vidana (2018), and the kaon condensation scenario originally proposed by Kaplan (1986). The paper enables future work by summarizing the tension between phenomenological models that favor kaon condensation (Gupta 2013, Thapa 2021, Malik 2021, Muto 2021) and microscopic unitarized approaches (Lutz 1997, Ramos 1999, Tolos 2000-2008, Cabrera 2014) that do not support it, as well as the five proposed solutions to the hyperon puzzle.
