# Spin entanglement of multinucleons: experimental prospects

> Multiprotons and multineutrons are among the most exotic and mysterious things ever produced on earth. They provide an exceptional opportunity to understand nuclear forces and nuclear dynamics at extreme conditions, as well as neutron stars in the heaven. Quantum entanglement, referred to as ``spooky action at a distance'' by Einstein, is a ubiquitous yet deep property of quantum systems. It not only occupies a central position in quantum information science but also is investigated intensively in high energy physics, condensed matter physics, and quantum gravity. In comparison, the study of nuclear entanglement is still in infancy, and the entanglement properties of multiprotons and multineutrons in free space are generally unknown. Here, we study the crucial problem of how to measure spin entanglement of these multinucleons in nuclear experiments, with special emphases on two- and three-nucleon states. These findings open a freshly new direction for the multinucleon research. They are also useful for understanding entanglement properties of other exotic nuclear objects.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that spin entanglement of multiprotons and multineutrons in free space can be measured experimentally using nuclear state tomography (NST) adapted from quantum information science, but only after the momenta of all nucleons are fixed via momentum selection, and the entanglement properties of indistinguishable particles must be treated using mode entanglement rather than standard definitions.

## Method

The authors develop a theoretical framework mapping multinucleon states with fixed momenta to multi-qubit states using mode entanglement in second quantization, where nucleons with momentum k and helicity lambda are treated as qubits. They then propose nuclear state tomography (NST) to extract one- and two-nucleon reduced density matrices from polarization experiments, from which bipartite concurrences and genuine multipartite entanglement (GME) measures such as genuine multipartite concurrence (GMC), geometric mean of bipartite concurrences (GBC), and concurrence fill (CF) can be computed. A proof-of-concept experimental setup is described using narrow slits and nucleon detectors to fix nucleon momenta for two- and three-nucleon states.

## Key numbers

- Concurrence C_{1|2} for two-qubit states ranges from 0 (no entanglement) to 1 (maximally entangled), defined as C_{1|2} = 2|b_{00}b_{11} - b_{01}b_{10}| for a normalized state.
- The three GME measures for three-qubit states are defined using bipartite concurrences C_{1|23}, C_{2|13}, and C_{3|12}, with GMC = min{C_{1|23}, C_{2|13}, C_{3|12}} and GBC = [C_{1|23} C_{2|13} C_{3|12}]^{1/3}.
- The concurrence fill (CF) for three-qubit states is given by C_CF = [16/3 Q(Q - C_{1|23}^2)(Q - C_{2|13}^2)(Q - C_{3|12}^2)]^{1/4} with Q = 1/2 (C_{1|23}^2 + C_{2|13}^2 + C_{3|12}^2).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study on spin entanglement of proton-neutron pairs (Bai 2023) to multiprotons and multineutrons made of indistinguishable particles, and from two-nucleon to three-nucleon systems. It builds on the mode entanglement definition recommended by Benatti (2020) for identical particles, and relates to prior work on nuclear entanglement by Beane (2018), Bai (2022, 2023), and Liu (2022) showing connections between spin entanglement and symmetry emergence in strong interactions. The paper enables experimental tests of whether nuclear forces favor strong spin entanglement in multinucleon systems, with implications for understanding entanglement properties of other exotic nuclear objects.
