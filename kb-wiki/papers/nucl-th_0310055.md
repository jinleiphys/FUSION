# Light clusters in nuclear matter of finite temperature

> We investigate properties and the distribution of light nuclei (A<4) in symmetric nuclear matter of finite temperature within a microscopic framework. For this purpose we have solved few-body Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas type equations for quasi-nucleons that include self-energy corrections and Pauli blocking in a systematic way. In a statistical model we find a significant influence in the composition of nuclear matter if medium effects are included in the microscopic calculation of nuclei. If multiplicities are frozen out at a certain time (or volume), we expect significant consequences for the formation of light fragments in a heavy ion collision. As a consequence of the systematic inclusion of medium effects the ordering of multiplicities becomes opposite to the law of mass action of ideal components. This is necessary to explain the large abundance of $α$-particles in a heavy ion collision that are otherwise largely suppressed in an ideal equilibrium scenario.

# Light clusters in nuclear matter of finite temperature

## Key claim

The equilibrium composition of nuclear matter at finite temperature is significantly altered when medium effects (self-energy corrections and Pauli blocking) are included in the microscopic calculation of light clusters, to the extent that the ordering of multiplicities becomes opposite to the law of mass action of ideal components. This reversal is necessary to explain the large abundance of alpha particles observed in heavy ion collisions, which are otherwise largely suppressed in an ideal equilibrium scenario.

## Method

The authors solved in-medium Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) type equations for quasi-nucleons in symmetric nuclear matter, incorporating self-energy corrections via Hartree-Fock approximation and Pauli blocking through Fermi functions. The few-body equations were solved using an energy dependent pole expansion (EDPE) for the two-body and three-body subsystems, with a Yamaguchi potential and a Malfliet-Tjon potential as the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The cluster densities were then computed from the in-medium binding energies as functions of density, temperature, and center-of-mass momentum, with bound states only contributing above the dissociation momentum.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the alpha particle decreases with increasing nuclear matter density at T=10 MeV and zero center-of-mass momentum, reaching the continuum threshold (B=0) at approximately 0.04 fm^-3 for the Malfliet-Tjon potential
- For the deuteron at T=10 MeV and zero center-of-mass momentum, the dissociation density (where binding energy reaches zero) is lower than for the alpha particle
- The dissociation momentum for the alpha particle at T=10 MeV is approximately 0.6 fm^-1 per nucleon at a density of about 0.04 fm^-3
- The freeze-out density range considered is 0.085 to 0.034 fm^-3, based on prior work by Borderie (1995)
- Total number of nucleons in the model system is 250
- The expanding fireball model uses a radius of 7.5 fm at 40 fm/c and 20 fm at 140 fm/c after collision

## Context

This work extends microscopic in-medium few-body calculations previously applied to the deuteron (Beyer 1996, 1999) and triton (Beyer 1999) to include the alpha particle, using the AGS formalism. It directly addresses the discrepancy between ideal gas models and experimental data from the INDRA collaboration (2000, 2002) for Xe+Sn collisions at 50A MeV, where alpha particle multiplicities are much larger than predicted by the law of mass action. The results enable improved equilibrium descriptions for heavy ion collision simulations, particularly for BUU and QMD approaches, and suggest that the dissociation (Mott effect) of clusters must be included to reproduce experimental fragment distributions.
