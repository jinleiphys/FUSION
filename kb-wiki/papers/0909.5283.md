# $β$-decay half-lives at finite temperatures for N=82 isotones

> Using the finite temperature quasi-particle random phase approximation (FTQRPA) on the basis of finite temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock + BCS method, we study $β^-$-decay half-lives for even-even neutron magic nuclei with N=82 in a finite temperature environment. We find that the $β^-$-decay half-life first decreases as the temperature increases for all the nuclei we study, although the thermal effect is found to be small at temperatures relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis. Our calculations indicate that the half-life begins to increase at high temperatures for open shell nuclei. We discuss this behavior in connection to the pairing phase transition.

# β-decay half-lives at finite temperatures for N=82 isotones

## Key claim
The β-decay half-life for even-even N=82 isotones first decreases with increasing temperature, but the thermal effect is small at temperatures relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis (below approximately 0.2 MeV). For open-shell nuclei, the half-life begins to increase at high temperatures (above 0.6-0.7 MeV) due to the pairing phase transition.

## Method
The authors used the finite-temperature quasi-particle random phase approximation (FT-QRPA) built on top of the finite-temperature Skyrme-Hartree-Fock + BCS method. They employed the SLy5 and SkM* Skyrme parameter sets, with a zero-range density-dependent force for like-particle pairing and a δ-type interaction for the proton-neutron T=0 pairing channel. The Gamow-Teller strength functions were calculated for even-even N=82 isotones (¹²⁰Sr, ¹²²Zr, ¹²⁴Mo, ¹²⁶Ru, ¹²⁸Pd, and ¹³⁰Cd) at temperatures from 0.0 to 0.8 MeV, and β-decay half-lives were evaluated using the Fermi Golden rule with the calculated strength distributions.

## Key numbers
- The proton pairing strength was set to V_p = -1300 MeV·fm⁻³ to reproduce the empirical pairing gap of ¹³⁰Cd, Δ⁽³⁾_p(Z+1) = 0.92 MeV.
- The proton-neutron pairing strength was adjusted to V_pn⁰ = -360 MeV·fm⁻³ to reproduce the experimental β-decay half-life of ¹³⁰Cd (0.195 sec).
- At T = 0.8 MeV, the ratio T₁/₂/T₁/₂⁰ is approximately 0.2 for ¹³⁰Cd and about 0.9 for ¹²⁰Sr.
- New peaks appearing at T = 0.3 MeV have strengths of order 0.1 on average, approximately 0.1% of the sum rule.
- The critical temperature for the pairing phase transition is approximately T = 0.50-0.65 MeV.

## Context
This work extends previous finite-temperature studies of β-decay using the independent particle model (FFN) and the finite range droplet model plus gross theory (Famiano, 2008). It improves upon earlier finite-temperature pnQRPA calculations by Civitarese (2000, 2001) and Nabi (1999, 2004), which used schematic separable forces and sometimes neglected proton-neutron pairing. The present calculation uses a self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock + BCS basis with a contact force for the proton-neutron particle-particle interaction. The results enable future extension to odd-mass nuclei, where larger thermal effects may be expected due to smaller first-excited-state energies.
