# Analytic Solution of the Pion-Laser Model

> Brooding over bosons, wave packets and Bose - Einstein correlations, we find that a generalization of the pion-laser model for the case of overlapping wave-packets is analytically solvable with complete n-particle symmetrization. The effective radius parameter of the two-particle correlation function is reduced for low values and enlargened for high values of the mean momentum in the rare gas limiting case, as compared to the case when multi-particle symmetrization effects are neglected. These results explicitly depend on the multiplicity, providing a theoretical basis for event-by-event analysis of high energy heavy ion reactions.

## Key claim

Multi-boson symmetrization effects in a generalized pion-laser model with overlapping wave-packets generate momentum-dependent HBT radius and intercept parameters even for static sources, with the effective radius reduced at low mean momentum and enlarged at high mean momentum in the rare gas limiting case, explicitly depending on the event multiplicity.

## Method

The authors generalize the pion-laser model of Pratt (1993) by using properly normalized n-particle wave-packet states with complete symmetrization, and introduce a density matrix that includes stimulated emission controlled by wave-packet overlaps. They reduce the problem to a ring-algebra of permanents and obtain an analytic solution via generating functions and recurrence relations, then evaluate the exclusive two-particle correlation functions in the rare gas limit (large source size or effective temperature, x >> 1) to leading order in 1/x.

## Key numbers

- Critical mean multiplicity for condensation: n_c = [(1 + x + sqrt(1 + 2x))/2]^(3/2), where x = R_e^2 sigma_T^2
- For a fireball with R = 11 fm, T = 120 MeV, sigma_x = 2 fm, and fixed n_pi = 600, the reduction of radius parameters at low momentum is clear while enhancement at high momentum is hardly noticeable
- For a small cold system with R = 4 fm, T = 10 MeV, n_pi = 3, sigma_x = 5 fm, the effective radius R_e = 4.5 fm and effective temperature T_e = 15.6 MeV
- Leading order multiplicity distribution: p_n = (n_0^n / n!) exp(-n_0) [1 + (n(n-1) - n_0^2) / (2 (2x)^(3/2))]

## Context

This work extends the pion-laser model of Pratt (1993) by incorporating overlapping wave-packets rather than plane-wave outgoing states, and provides the first analytic solution with complete n-particle symmetrization. The results enable event-by-event analysis of high energy heavy ion reactions at CERN SPS and RHIC, and connect to studies of Bose-Einstein condensation in atomic systems (Anderson et al., 1995). The momentum-dependent HBT radii provide a theoretical basis for distinguishing multi-particle symmetrization effects from other dynamical effects in experimental correlation functions.
