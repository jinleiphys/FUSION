# Finite amplitude method applied to giant dipole resonance in heavy rare-earth nuclei

> Background: The quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA), within the framework of the nuclear density functional theory (DFT), has been a standard tool to access the collective excitations of the atomic nuclei. Recently, finite amplitude method (FAM) has been developed, in order to perform the QRPA calculations efficiently without any truncation on the two-quasiparticle model space. Purpose: We discuss the nuclear giant dipole resonance (GDR) in heavy rare-earth isotopes, for which the conventional matrix diagonalization of the QRPA is numerically demanding. A role of the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule enhancement factor, connected to the isovector effective mass, is also investigated. Methods: The electric dipole photoabsorption cross section was calculated within a parallelized FAM-QRPA scheme. We employed the Skyrme energy density functional self-consistently in the DFT calculation for the ground states and FAM-QRPA calculation for the excitations. Results: The mean GDR frequency and width are mostly reproduced with the FAM-QRPA, when compared to experimental data, although some deficiency is observed with isotopes heavier than erbium. A role of the TRK enhancement factor in actual GDR strength is clearly shown: its increment leads to a shift of the GDR strength to higher-energy region, without a significant change in the transition amplitudes. Conclusions: The newly developed FAM-QRPA scheme shows a remarkable efficiency, which enables to perform systematic analysis of GDR for heavy rare-earth nuclei. Theoretical deficiency of photoabsorption cross section could not be improved by only adjusting the TRK enhancement factor, suggesting the necessity of beyond the self-consistent QRPA approach, and/or a more systematic optimization of the EDF parameters.

## Key claim

The finite amplitude method (FAM) applied to the quasiparticle random phase approximation (QRPA) can efficiently compute giant dipole resonance (GDR) properties in heavy rare-earth nuclei without truncation of the two-quasiparticle model space, although the theoretical photoabsorption cross sections show a deficiency for isotopes heavier than erbium that cannot be remedied by simply adjusting the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn (TRK) sum rule enhancement factor.

## Method

The authors performed fully self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SkM* Skyrme parameterization for the particle-hole channel and a density-dependent delta pairing interaction for the particle-particle channel, with the HFB solver based on a harmonic oscillator basis of 20 shells under axial symmetry. They then applied the finite amplitude method to solve the QRPA linear response problem iteratively, computing the electric dipole photoabsorption cross section from the isovector dipole strength for even-even rare-earth nuclei from Gd (Z=64) to W (Z=74). The FAM-QRPA calculations were parallelized using MPI, with each part of the strength function distributed on a separate core.

## Key numbers

- For $^{174}$Yb, the energy-weighted sum rule integrated up to $\omega=50$ MeV yields $m_1(\oprt{D}_K)=283.9$ and $283.8$ $e^2$ fm$^2$ MeV for $K=0$ and $K=1$ modes, respectively
- The double commutator procedure gives $m_1(\oprt{D}_{K=0, \pm 1})=287.7$ $e^2$ fm$^2$ MeV with enhancement factor $\kappa^{\rm IVD}=0.397$ for $^{174}$Yb
- The contour integration technique yields $m_1(\oprt{D}_{K=0})=290.1$ $e^2$ fm$^2$ MeV for $^{174}$Yb with an integration contour radius of 200 MeV
- Pairing strengths were adjusted to $V^{\rm pair}_{\rm n}=-282.0$ MeV fm$^3$ and $V^{\rm pair}_{\rm p}=-307.9$ MeV fm$^3$ to reproduce empirical pairing gaps of $^{156}$Dy
- The imaginary part of the energy was set to $\gamma=0.5$ MeV, corresponding to a smearing width of $\Gamma=1.0$ MeV
- A computation of both $K$-modes took about 1500 CPU hours on a multicore Intel Sandy Bridge 2.6-GHz processor system
- The quadrupole deformation of $^{154}$Sm is $\beta=0.317$, while $^{144}$Sm is spherical with $\beta=0$

## Context

This work extends the finite amplitude method for QRPA, originally developed by Nakatsukasa et al. (2007) and implemented in axially symmetric Skyrme-HFB solvers by Kortelainen et al. (2011), to systematic studies of giant dipole resonances in heavy rare-earth nuclei where conventional matrix diagonalization QRPA is numerically demanding. The results are compared with experimental photoabsorption data from Gurevich et al. (1981), Berman et al. (1969), and Goryachev et al. (1976, 1977), and show consistency with matrix QRPA calculations by Yoshida et al. (2011) in the coordinate-space representation. The observed GDR deficiency for heavier isotopes suggests the need for beyond-QRPA approaches or more systematic optimization of energy density functional parameters, and the demonstration that the Thouless theorem provides a reliable approximation for the isovector dipole sum rule enables efficient computation of energy-weighted sum rules using the contour integration technique developed by Hinohara et al. (2015).
