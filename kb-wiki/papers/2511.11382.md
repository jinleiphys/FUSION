# Self-gravitating baryonic tubes supported by $π$- and $ω$-mesons and its flat limit

> In this paper, we construct self-gravitating topological solitons in the $SU(N)$ Einstein non-linear sigma model coupled to $ω$-vector mesons in four space-time dimensions. These solutions represent tube-like configurations free of curvature singularities, carrying a non-vanishing topological charge that is identified as the baryon number. We show that by employing the maximal embedding Ansatz of $SU(2)$ into $SU(N)$ in the exponential representation, these tubes can be constructed for an arbitrary number of flavors, $N$, with the topological charge scaling proportionally to this number. The flat-space limit of the solutions, corresponding to an array of baryonic tubes within a finite volume, is analyzed in detail. Remarkably, while the total energy of the solitons at a finite volume is an increasing function of $N$, the binding energy decreases as the number of flavors increases. This analysis reaffirms that the inclusion of more than two flavors to the model systematically improves the physical predictions.

## Key claim

The paper constructs self-gravitating topological solitons in the SU(N) Einstein non-linear sigma model coupled to ω-vector mesons, claiming that while the total energy of the solitons at finite volume increases with the number of flavors N, the binding energy decreases as N increases. The hedge is that the fully coupled system for arbitrary N > 2 in a curved background ultimately requires numerical integration, though exact analytical expressions exist for the topological charge, energy density, and functional dependence of binding energy on N.

## Method

The authors employ the maximal embedding Ansatz of SU(2) into SU(N) in the exponential representation, reducing the pionic field degrees of freedom to three parameters (α, Θ, Φ) regardless of N. They couple this to a Weyl-Lewis-Papapetrou metric and an ω-meson potential of the form ω_μ = (-S, 0, 0, S), solving the Einstein equations, NLSM equations, and ω-meson equations semi-analytically. The soliton profile α(X) and metric function R(X) are solved exactly, while the ω-meson function S(X,θ) and metric function H(X,θ) require numerical integration.

## Key numbers

- Topological charge per unit length: B/L_z = \bar{N} n p, where \bar{N} = N(N²-1)/6 and n is an integer from boundary conditions α(∞) - α(-∞) = 2π n
- The constant q must equal (2m+1)/2 with m an integer for non-zero topological charge
- Regularity constraint: -(1 - \bar{N}Kκ)|q| < C₂ < -\bar{N}Kκ|q|, generalizing the SU(2) constraint from Barriga (2023)
- For numerical plots: p = 10, q = 1/2, γ = 0.01, C₁ = 0, C₂ = -1/50, C₃ = 1, κ = 0.25, K = 1, M_ω = 0.1 (self-gravitating case)
- For flat-limit binding energy plots: K = 1, γ = 0.04, p = q = 1, M_ω = 0.01

## Context

This work extends the flat-space SU(2) baryonic tube solutions of Barriga (2023) to self-gravitating configurations with arbitrary flavor number N, building on the self-gravitating superconducting tube framework of gravtube1 and gravtube2. The inclusion of ω-mesons follows the mechanism first noticed by Adkins and Nappi (1984), who showed that ω-mesons stabilize solitons and reduce binding energy without the Skyrme term. The results demonstrate that increasing the flavor number systematically reduces binding energy, which the authors argue improves agreement with experimental nuclear data compared to standard two-flavor chiral models.
