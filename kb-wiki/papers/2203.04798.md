# Interacting $ud$ and $uds$ quark matter at finite densities and quark stars

> The stability and equation of state of quark matter are studied within both two-flavor and (2+1)-flavor Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) models including the vector interactions. With a free parameter $α$, the Lagrangian is constructed by two parts, the original NJL Lagrangian and the Fierz transformation of it, as $L=(1-α) L_{\rm{NJL}}+αL_{\rm{Fierz}}$. We find that there is a possibility for both $ud$ nonstrange and $uds$ strange matter being absolute stable, depending on the interplay of the confinement with quark vector interaction and the exchange interaction channels. The calculated quark star properties can reconcile with the recently measured masses and radii of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620, as well as the tidal deformability of GW170817. Furthermore, the more strongly-interacting quark matter in the nonstrange stars allows a stiffer equation of state and consequently a higher maximum mass ($\sim2.7\, M_{\odot}$) than the strange ones ($\sim2.1\, M_{\odot}$). The sound velocities in strange and nonstrange quark star matter are briefly discussed compared to those of neutron star matter.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that both nonstrange ($ud$) and strange ($uds$) quark matter can be absolutely stable under the Bodmer-Witten hypothesis, but only for specific combinations of the mixing parameter $\alpha$ and the vector coupling ratio $R_V$ that control the interplay between confinement, vector repulsion, and exchange interaction channels.

## Method

The authors construct a generalized Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) Lagrangian as a weighted average $\mathcal{L} = (1-\alpha)\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{NJL}} + \alpha\mathcal{L}_{\mathrm{Fierz}}$ for both two-flavor and (2+1)-flavor models, including vector interactions parameterized by $R_V = G_V/G$. They solve the mean-field gap equations and number-density equations at zero temperature using proper-time regularization with a UV cutoff $\Lambda_{\mathrm{UV}} = 1330$ MeV, then compute the equation of state and solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for quark star structure.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of nonstrange ($ud$) quark stars: $\sim 2.7\,M_{\odot}$
- Maximum mass of strange ($uds$) quark stars: $\sim 2.1\,M_{\odot}$
- UV cutoff parameter: $\Lambda_{\mathrm{UV}} = 1330$ MeV
- Two-flavor current quark mass: $m_u = 3.3$ MeV
- (2+1)-flavor current quark masses: $m_u = 3.4$ MeV, $m_s = 104$ MeV
- Two-flavor coupling constant: $G' = 2.028 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$
- (2+1)-flavor coupling constant: $G' = 1.51 \times 10^{-6}$ MeV$^{-2}$, $K = 2.75 \times 10^{-14}$ MeV$^{-5}$
- Threshold chemical potential for $u,d$ quarks in (2+1)-flavor model: $\sim 205$ MeV
- Threshold chemical potential for $s$ quarks in (2+1)-flavor model: larger than $u,d$ threshold due to higher vacuum mass
- Constituent quark mass plateau persists up to $\mu_{u,d} < 207$ MeV and $\mu_s < 210$ MeV in the (2+1)-flavor model

## Context

This work extends the modified NJL approach introduced by Wang et al. (2019) to include vector interactions and applies it systematically to both strange and nonstrange quark matter for the first time. It contradicts the traditional assumption that $uds$ matter is always energetically preferable over $ud$ matter, following the recent finding by Holdom et al. (2018) that $ud$ matter can be more stable when flavor-dependent vacuum feedback is considered. The results enable simultaneous reconciliation with the mass-radius measurements of PSR J0030+0451 and PSR J0740+6620 from NICER, and the tidal deformability constraint from GW170817, providing a framework to constrain the free parameters $\alpha$ and $R_V$ from astrophysical observations.
