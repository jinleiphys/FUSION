# One-point energy correlator for deep inelastic scattering at small $x$

> We derive the expressions for the one-point energy correlator (OPEC) in deep inelastic scattering in the high-energy (small-$x$) limit within the Color Glass Condensate framework. The OPEC is computed as a function of the angle between the energy flow and the target proton or nucleus, enabling a systematic exploration of different momentum scales in the scattering process. Owing to the momentum sum rule, the dependence on fragmentation functions cancels, leaving the dipole amplitude as the only nonperturbative input. As a result, the OPEC provides a clean and direct probe of gluon saturation dynamics at small $x$. We present numerical results for representative kinematic configurations relevant to the future Electron--Ion Collider, demonstrating sizable nuclear suppression effects and highlighting the sensitivity of this observable to saturation phenomena.

## Key claim

The one-point energy correlator (OPEC) in deep inelastic scattering at small $x$ provides a clean probe of gluon saturation because the dependence on fragmentation functions cancels via the momentum-sum rule, leaving the dipole amplitude as the only nonperturbative input; however, the formalism is reliable only for $x \lesssim 0.01$, and the numerical results are restricted to kinematic configurations where this condition holds.

## Method

The authors derive the OPEC expression within the Color Glass Condensate (CGC) effective field theory, working in the Breit frame and using the dipole picture where the virtual photon fluctuates into a quark-antiquark pair that scatters eikonally off the target. They compute the differential cross section for $\gamma^* + p \to h + X$ at leading order, then apply the momentum-sum rule to eliminate fragmentation functions, leaving the dipole amplitude $\mathcal{F}_x(\mathbf{q}_\perp)$ as the sole nonperturbative input. The dipole amplitude is evolved using the running-coupling Balitsky-Kovchegov (rcBK) equation with the Balitsky prescription, with initial conditions from the MVe model fit to proton data and extended to nuclear targets by scaling the saturation momentum and transverse size.

## Key numbers

- For $Q = 1$ GeV and $y = 0.5$ ($x_B = 2.47 \times 10^{-4}$), the nuclear modification factor $R_A$ for the longitudinal OPEC is approximately flat between 0.25 and 0.5 for $\tau < 0.7$, then increases to 0.5–0.75 as $\tau$ approaches 0.9.
- For the transverse OPEC at $Q = 1$ GeV and $y = 0.5$, $R_A$ grows monotonically from roughly 0.2–0.35 at $\tau = 0.1$ to about 0.65–0.8 at $\tau = 0.9$.
- At $Q = 3$ GeV and $y = 0.5$ ($x_B = 2.22 \times 10^{-3}$), $R_A$ approaches unity by $\tau = 0.9$, indicating disappearance of significant nuclear modification.
- The center-of-mass energy of the $e+p/A$ system is taken as $\sqrt{s} = 90$ GeV, with photon virtuality $Q = 1$–$5$ GeV and inelasticity $y = 0.5$ and $0.9$.
- The nuclear saturation scale is modified as $Q_{s0,A}^2 = c A^{1/3} Q_{s0}^2$ with $c$ varied between $0.5 < c < 1.0$ for gold (Au-197) targets.

## Context

This work extends previous analyses of energy correlators in deep inelastic scattering that focused on the back-to-back limit (Li 2021) and the transverse energy-energy correlator (Kang 2025), providing expressions valid at intermediate angles within the CGC framework. It builds on the dipole picture formalism for small-$x$ DIS (Golec-Biernat 1998, Marquet 2009) and the rcBK evolution (Balitsky 1995, Kovchegov 1999). The results enable future measurements at the Electron-Ion Collider to probe gluon saturation dynamics across a wide angular range without contamination from fragmentation function uncertainties.
