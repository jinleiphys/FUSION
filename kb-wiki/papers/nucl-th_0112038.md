# Behavior of the charge fluctuation in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

> Using a hadron and string cascade model, JPCIAE, we investigated the dependence of event-by-event charge fluctuation on the energy, centrality, window size, resonance decay, and the final state interaction for $Pb+Pb$ collisions at SPS and LHC energies and $Au+Au$ collisions at RHIC energies. The JPCIAE results of charge fluctuation as a function of the rapidity window size in $Pb+Pb$ collisions at SPS energies were compared with the preliminary NA49 data. Comparisons with STAR and PHENIX data of $Au+Au$ collisions at $\sqrt{s_{nn}}$=130 GeV were also given. It seems that the final state interaction and resonance decay play a gentle role on the charge fluctuations. The charge fluctuations are slightly decreasing with or nearly independent of the reaction energy and hardly depend on the collision centrality.

# Behavior of the charge fluctuation in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions

## Key claim
The event-by-event charge fluctuation measures (D_R, D_Q, and their corrected versions) are nearly independent of collision centrality and only weakly dependent on reaction energy from SPS through LHC energies, with final-state rescattering and resonance decay playing only a gentle role. However, the JPCIAE model results for Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c show discrepancies in the rapidity-window dependence compared to preliminary NA49 data, indicating that the model does not fully capture the observed behavior at that energy.

## Method
The authors used the JPCIAE hadron and string cascade model, which is based on PYTHIA for hadron-hadron collisions but extended to nucleus-nucleus collisions by sampling nucleon positions from a Woods-Saxon distribution and including rescattering of secondary particles. For each colliding pair with center-of-mass energy above 4 GeV, strings are formed and particle production is handled via the Lund fragmentation scheme; below that threshold, two-body collisions are used. The charge fluctuation measures D_R (scaled variance of the charge ratio) and D_Q (scaled variance of net charge) were computed as functions of rapidity window size, centrality (impact parameter), reaction energy, and with/without rescattering and resonance decays.

## Key numbers
- For Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c, the JPCIAE results for the corrected fluctuation measure $\tilde{D_R}$ as a function of $\Delta y$ (rapidity window) show discrepancies with preliminary NA49 data, while results at 40 and 80A GeV/c are generally compatible.
- In Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{nn}}$=130 GeV, the JPCIAE result for $D_Q$ is approximately 0.9 at $\Delta\eta$=1, about 10% higher than the corresponding STAR datum of 0.8 extracted under the assumption of zero net charge.
- The PHENIX datum for $4D_Q$ in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{nn}}$=130 GeV at $\Delta\eta$=0.7 is about 10% lower than the corresponding JPCIAE result.
- The thermal model predictions for $D_R$ are: approximately 4 for a pion gas, approximately 3 for a resonance pion gas (including $\rho$ and $\omega$ decays), and approximately 0.75 for massless noninteracting quarks and gluons (approximately 1 from lattice calculations).
- The shift in $\tilde{D_R}$ due to resonance decay ($\rho$ and $\omega$) is smaller than 1 over all $\Delta y$ regions studied.

## Context
This work extends prior studies of event-by-event fluctuations as signals of the quark-gluon plasma phase transition, following the proposal by Jeon and Koch (2000) that charge ratio fluctuations could distinguish hadronic from QGP phases. The authors compare their JPCIAE results with the UrQMD model calculations by Jeon and Koch (2000) and with preliminary NA49 data for Pb+Pb collisions at SPS energies, as well as STAR and PHENIX data for Au+Au collisions at RHIC. The study enables further refinement of hadronic transport models and helps establish baseline expectations for charge fluctuation measurements at LHC energies, where the model predicts nearly energy-independent behavior.
