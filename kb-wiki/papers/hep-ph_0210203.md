# Power-like abundance of elements in Universe

> Presented at XXXII International Symposium on Multiparticle Dynamics, Alushta, Crimea, Ukraine, September 7-13, 2002. To be published by World Scientific (2003) (style file ws-procs9x6.cls attached). Some misprints corrected

## Key claim
The observed power-law abundance of elements in the Universe (scaling as approximately \(A^{-7.5}\)) may extend to strangelets (nuggets of strange quark matter), which could populate the "nuclear desert" between heavy elements and neutron stars. The authors propose that strangelets entering Earth's atmosphere are initially large (\(A \sim 1000\)–\(2000\)) and only disintegrate after reaching a critical size \(A_{\text{crit}} = 200\)–\(400\), rather than breaking up immediately like normal nuclei.

## Method
The authors estimated the mass spectrum of strangelets using a neutron-absorption model that yields a power-law distribution \(P(A) \propto A^{-\gamma}\) with \(\gamma = 1 + 1/(nL\sigma)\), where \(n\) is neutron density, \(L\) is path length, and \(\sigma\) is the absorption cross section. They then compared this predicted strangelet flux with the known abundance of elements and with experimental limits from cosmic-ray detectors, including the CosmoLEP experiment at ALEPH. The strangelet flux was tuned to fit the observed muon multiplicity distribution, particularly the high-multiplicity tail (\(N_\mu\) up to 150) that cannot be explained by normal primary cosmic-ray composition.

## Key numbers
- The power-law exponent for strangelet abundance is \(\gamma = 7.5\), corresponding to \(\alpha = nL\sigma = 0.154\).
- The critical mass for strangelet disintegration in the atmosphere is \(A_{\text{crit}} = 200\)–\(400\); initial strangelet masses are \(A = 1000\)–\(2000\).
- The relative flux of strangelets needed to fit CosmoLEP data is \(F_S/F_{\text{tot}} \simeq 2.4 \times 10^{-5}\) at the same energy per particle.
- The neutron-absorption model gives a layer thickness of \(nL = 5.1 \times 10^{24}\) cm\(^{-2}\) = 8.5 g/cm\(^2\); assuming neutron density \(n \approx 10^{20}\) cm\(^{-3}\), this corresponds to a layer thickness \(L = 0.5\) km.
- The absorption cross section used in the model is \(\sigma = 30\) mb.

## Context
This work extends the observed power-law abundance of elements (Zhdanov, year not given) to include strange quark matter nuggets, building on the authors' prior studies (Wilk and Wlodarczyk, 2000; 2001) that proposed strangelets follow an \(A^{-7.5}\) dependence. It also connects to experimental searches for strangelets (Price, year not given) and to the interpretation of Centauro events as strangelet-induced showers. The model enables future searches for strangelets in cosmic-ray data by providing a predicted flux and mass spectrum that can be compared with upper limits from experiments like CosmoLEP.
