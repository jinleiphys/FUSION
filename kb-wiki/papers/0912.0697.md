# Partial-wave contributions to pairing in nuclei

> We present a detailed study of partial-wave contributions of nuclear forces to pairing in nuclei. For T=1, J=0 pairing, partial waves beyond the standard 1S0 channel play an interesting role for the pair formation in nuclei. The additional contributions are dominated by the repulsive 3P1 partial wave. Their effects, and generally spin-triplet nuclear forces between paired nucleons, are influenced by the interplay of spin-orbit partners. We explore the impact of including partial waves beyond the 1S0 channel on neutron-neutron pairing gaps in semi-magic isotopic chains. In addition, we show that nuclear forces favor T=1, J=0 over T=0, J=1 pairing, except in low-j orbitals. This is in contrast to the free-space motivation that suggests the formation of deuteron-like T=0 pairs in N=Z nuclei. The suppression of T=0 pairing is because the 3S1 strength is distributed on spin-orbit partners and because of the effects of the repulsive 1P1 channel and of D waves.

# Partial-wave contributions to pairing in nuclei

## Key claim
Nuclear forces favor T=1, J=0 pairing over T=0, J=1 pairing in nuclei, except in low-j orbitals, contrary to the free-space expectation that deuteron-like T=0 pairs should form in N=Z nuclei. The suppression of T=0 pairing arises because the 3S1 strength is distributed across spin-orbit partners and because of repulsive contributions from the 1P1 channel and D waves.

## Method
The authors use the Skyrme functional SLy4 to solve Hartree-Fock equations on a spherical mesh, then solve Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov equations for neutron-neutron pairing using low-momentum interactions V_lowk evolved from the chiral N3LO two-nucleon potential via renormalization group. Pairing matrix elements in the jj-coupled basis are computed by expanding HF single-particle states on a harmonic oscillator basis and recoupling partial-wave contributions from the V_lowk interaction.

## Key numbers
- The repulsive 3P1 partial wave decreases lowest-quasiparticle-energy canonical state (LCS) gaps by up to 15% compared to gaps obtained from only the 1S0 contribution
- For the tin isotopes, gaps calculated with only the 1S0 contribution agree within 0.5% for most isotopes (with two cases at N=54 and N=56 showing 3% difference) when compared to results of Lesinski using the same pairing interaction
- The ratio R of T=0, J=1 over T=1, J=0 diagonal pairing matrix elements for S-wave contact interactions is R = 1 for j=1/2, R = 1/2 in the limit of large j, and R ≤ 3/5 for j ≥ 3/2
- The 1S0 scattering length is a = -23.768 fm and the 3S1 scattering length is a = 5.420 fm
- Cutoff variation of LCS gaps is small overall, with approximate cutoff independence for Λ = 2.0 to 2.5 fm^{-1}

## Context
This work extends the approach of Duguet (2007) and Lesinski (2009), who used energy density functionals in the particle-hole channel combined with low-momentum interactions in the pairing channel but restricted to the 1S0 partial wave. The analysis provides a microscopic explanation for the suppression of T=0 pairing observed in shell-model calculations using empirical interactions by Satula and Wyss (2003) and by Zerguine and Van Isacker (2008). The results enable future studies that compare directly calculated odd-even mass differences to experimental gaps, and motivate using energy density functionals based on the same nuclear forces with many-body contributions to the pairing interaction as initiated by Hergert (2009).
