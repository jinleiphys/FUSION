# Cascade and Omega productions from Pb+Pb Collisions at LHC energy

> Production of multi strange hadrons like cascade ($╬×$) and omega($╬®$) baryons are studied microscopically using rate equation at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$=2.76 TeV, Large Hadron Collider(LHC) energy. The rate equations for $╬×$, $╬®$ are solved simultaneously with other strange hadrons in an expanding medium. The results for $╬×$ and $╬®$ are compared with the data obtained from Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$= 2.76 TeV from ALICE experiments. The ratio of yields of $╬×$ and $╬®$ to $ŽĆ$ are analysed with various initial conditions and compared with the experimental observations made for various charge particle multiplicities.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the microscopic production and evolution of multi-strange baryons (cascade and omega) in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC energy can be described by solving coupled rate equations in an expanding hadronic medium, but the agreement with experimental data depends strongly on the choice of initial conditions and freeze-out temperatures, with no single set of parameters reproducing all measured multiplicity bins simultaneously.

## Method

The authors solved coupled momentum-integrated Boltzmann (rate) equations for the number densities of kaons, antikaons, lambda, sigma, cascade, and omega baryons simultaneously in an expanding hadronic medium with Bjorken hydrodynamic expansion. Cross sections for the relevant hadronic reactions were calculated using a gauged flavor SU(3) symmetry Lagrangian in the Born approximation, with parametrizations from experimental data and the quark-gluon string model for baryon-antibaryon annihilation channels. The thermal rates for each binary reaction channel were computed as a function of temperature, and the system was evolved from an initial temperature down to various freeze-out temperatures for different charged-particle multiplicity classes.

## Key numbers

- The analysis is performed at center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV for Pb+Pb collisions at the LHC.
- Five multiplicity classes are studied, ranging from dN_ch/deta = 1447.5 (N_part = 356.1) down to dN_ch/deta = 55 (N_part = 22.5).
- Freeze-out temperatures for cascade and omega are varied between 0.116 GeV and 0.154 GeV across different scenarios and multiplicity bins.
- The cross section for the reaction K^- p -> K^+ Xi^- is parametrized as 235.6 (1 - sqrt(s_0)/sqrt(s))^2.4 (sqrt(s_0)/sqrt(s))^16.6 mb.
- The cross section for Lambda Lambda -> N Xi is given by 37.15 (p_N/p_Lambda) (sqrt(s) - sqrt(s_0))^(-0.16) mb, valid for 0 < (sqrt(s) - sqrt(s_0)) < 0.6 GeV.
- The cross section for p-bar p -> Xi-bar^0 Xi^0 is related to the cross section for p-bar p -> Lambda-bar Lambda via sigma = (16/(81 pi)) [sigma(p-bar p -> Lambda-bar Lambda)]^2 / (2 Lambda_1) exp[Lambda_1 t_DC], with Lambda_1 = 9 GeV^(-2).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of strange meson production using rate equations by Nayak (2010) and others, which explained kaon and antikaon production at RHIC and LHC energies but did not address multi-strange baryons. It attempts to explain the multiplicity-dependent enhancement of Xi/pi and Omega/pi ratios observed by the ALICE collaboration (2014) in Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV, which statistical hadronization models by Andronic (2006, 2009) could not reproduce when fitting the p/pi ratio. The results enable future microscopic calculations of multi-strange hadron production across different collision systems and energies, including p-p and p-Pb collisions where similar enhancement trends have been observed by ALICE (2017).
