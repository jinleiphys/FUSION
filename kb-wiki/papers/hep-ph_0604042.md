> We estimate the theoretical uncertainties of the model developed in Phys. Rev. C70 055503 for inclusive quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions at intermediate energies. # Theoretical uncertainties on quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections

## Key claim

The many-body framework of Nieves et al. (2004) predicts quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus cross sections with theoretical uncertainties of 10–15% at intermediate energies; however, the ratio σ(μ)/σ(e) of interest for atmospheric neutrino experiments is affected by uncertainties likely smaller than 5%, with the dominant residual uncertainty coming from deficiencies of the local Fermi gas picture of the nucleus rather than from the model's input parameters.

## Method

The authors performed a Monte Carlo simulation generating 2000 sets of input parameters from an uncorrelated multidimensional Gaussian distribution, with central values and standard deviations assigned to each parameter based on experimental errors or theoretically motivated uncertainties. For each sample set, they computed inclusive quasielastic charged-current cross sections for electron and muon neutrinos on carbon, oxygen, and argon targets using the many-body framework of Nieves et al. (2004), which incorporates a local Fermi gas model with Pauli blocking, correct energy balance using experimental Q-values, Coulomb distortion of charged leptons, RPA correlations including Δ-hole degrees of freedom, and dressed nucleon propagators. Theoretical errors and uncertainty bands were obtained by discarding the highest and lowest 16% of sample values to leave a 68% confidence level interval.

## Key numbers

- Integrated cross section uncertainties are of order 10–15% for both electron and muon neutrino quasielastic reactions on carbon, oxygen, and argon at intermediate energies.
- The ratio σ(μ)/σ(e) shows theoretical uncertainties smaller than 1% from the Monte Carlo simulation of input parameter variations.
- The total uncertainty on σ(μ)/σ(e) is estimated as likely not larger than about 5%, with the dominant contribution from deficiencies of the local Fermi gas picture.
- Relativistic nucleon kinematics corrections to the ratio σ(μ)/σ(e) are smaller than 1% over the whole neutrino energy interval studied.
- For the axial cutoff mass M_A, the authors assume 1.05 ± 0.14 GeV from analysis of the νd → μ⁻ pp reaction.
- The axial coupling constant g_A is taken as 1.26 ± 0.01, with a conservative uncertainty larger than the Particle Data Group average.
- The vector dipole mass M_D is taken as 0.843 ± 0.042 GeV.
- The parameter λ_n is taken as 5.6 ± 0.6.
- For the nucleon self-energy and densities, 10% relative uncertainties are assumed.
- For the nine parameters of the effective baryon-baryon interaction, 10% relative errors are assumed (except c₀).
- The difference between the full model predictions and a simple Pauli-blocked Fermi gas (without RPA, Coulomb, and self-energy effects) for the ratio σ(μ)/σ(e) is at the level of 5%.
- Relativistic kinematics corrections to individual neutrino and antineutrino cross sections range from 4% to 9% at intermediate energies.

## Context

This work extends the many-body framework developed by Nieves et al. (2004) for inclusive quasielastic charged-current neutrino-nucleus reactions by providing the first systematic estimate of theoretical uncertainties, which had been missing in prior studies such as those by Martini et al. and Amaro et al. The model itself is a natural extension of earlier successful descriptions of electron scattering (Garcia-Recio et al., 1997), photon absorption (Carrasco and Oset, 1992), and pion dynamics (Oset et al., 1982) in nuclei. The finding that uncertainties largely cancel in the ratio σ(μ)/σ(e) is consistent with similar analyses by Engel et al. (1993), Martinez et al. (1999), and Marteau et al. (1993). The results enable improved control over systematic uncertainties in atmospheric neutrino oscillation experiments at Super-Kamiokande and future detectors, particularly for sub-leading effects from νₑ flavor oscillations.
