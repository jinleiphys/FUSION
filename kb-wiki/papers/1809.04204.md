# Possible determination of high-lying single particle components with (p,d) reactions

> A detailed feasibility study on deducing the high-lying single-particle components (HLSPCs), which are important but used to be ignored, in the ground and low-lying excited states of even-even light nuclei is performed by analyses of $(p,d)$ reactions with \nuc{12}{C}, \nuc{24}{Mg}, \nuc{28}{Si}, and \nuc{40}{Ca} targets at 51.93 MeV. Coupled reaction channels (CRC) analyses have been made for $(p,d)$ transitions to the $j$-forbidden excited states in \nuc{11}{C} (${\tfrac{5}{2}}^-$, 4.32 MeV), \nuc{23}{Mg} (${\tfrac{7}{2}}^+$, 2.05 MeV), \nuc{27}{Si} (${\tfrac{7}{2}}^+$, 2.16 MeV) and \nuc{39}{Ca} (${\tfrac{9}{2}}^-$, 3.64 MeV), including the major allowed transition components together with direct components of HLSPCs. Spectroscopic amplitudes of the HLSPCs are deduced by fitting the angular distributions of the ground and the $j$-forbidden excited states simultaneously. The present analysis demonstrates for the first time that information about HLSPCs in atomic nuclei can be obtained from analysis of $(p,d)$ reactions.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates for the first time that information about high-lying single-particle components (HLSPCs) in atomic nuclei can be obtained from analysis of (p,d) reactions, but the extracted spectroscopic amplitudes suffer from discrete and continuous ambiguities that limit their uniqueness; the authors suggest that including data from more excited states may resolve these ambiguities.

## Method

The authors performed coupled reaction channels (CRC) analyses of (p,d) reactions on 12C, 24Mg, 28Si, and 40Ca targets at 51.93 MeV, populating both ground states and j-forbidden excited states in the residual nuclei. They included both one-step transfer (OST) processes from high-lying single-particle orbitals and two-step transfer (TST) processes via collective excitations of the target and residual nuclei. Spectroscopic amplitudes for neutrons in normal and high-lying orbitals were determined by simultaneously fitting the angular distributions of the ground and j-forbidden excited states using the code SFRESCO, with over 1000 random initial parameter searches per reaction.

## Key numbers

- Incident proton energy: 51.93 MeV for all four reactions (12C, 24Mg, 28Si, 40Ca targets)
- j-forbidden states analyzed: 11C (5/2-, 4.32 MeV), 23Mg (7/2+, 2.05 MeV), 27Si (7/2+, 2.16 MeV), 39Ca (9/2-, 3.64 MeV)
- For 40Ca, the spectroscopic amplitude for the HLSPC in the ground state (SA4, 1h9/2 orbital) was 0.337 (r0=1.25 fm, group G1) and 0.490 (r0=1.25 fm, group G2), the most stable and largest among the four targets
- For 24Mg, a 9-parameter fit including three residual states gave SA4 = -0.334 (1g7/2 orbital) and SA6 = -0.887 (1g9/2 orbital) in the preferred solution (9P-Set1)
- Relative standard deviations of most spectroscopic amplitudes were smaller than 1%, except for some HLSPC-related amplitudes (SA3, SA4, or SA6) which were around 3%

## Context

This work extends the earlier experimental study of Ohnuma et al. (1980) on (p,d) reactions at 51.93 MeV, which only included two-step transfer processes and did not attempt to extract spectroscopic amplitudes for HLSPCs. Previous analyses by Dehnhard (1967), Nelson (1972), and Abdallah (1973) included both OST and TST processes but either failed to reproduce data with DWBA or only used CRC to test Nilsson model predictions. The present results enable future structure theory development by providing experimental constraints on nucleon population in high-lying orbitals, which are relevant to understanding nucleon-nucleon correlations and the quenching of single-particle strength as discussed by Gade (2008), Jenny (2010), and Atar (2018).
