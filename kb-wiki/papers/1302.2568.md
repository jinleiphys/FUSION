# Neutron-rich Helium isotopes based on hyperspherical harmonics

> We present recent results for neutron-rich Helium isotopes obtained from the hyperspherical harmonics method. Ground-state properties, like the binding energy and the point-proton radius are shown for the two-neutron halo nucleus 6He using two-body low-momentum interactions derived from chiral forces. The applicability of the method to the four-neutron halo nucleus 8He is discussed. As an excited-state observable we present a recent calculation of the nuclear electric polarizability of $^6$He from a semi-realistic potential. A comparison of the calculated quantities to experimental data is performed.

# Neutron-rich Helium isotopes based on hyperspherical harmonics

## Key claim
Ground-state properties of 6He can be precisely calculated using chiral low-momentum two-body forces alone, but the cutoff dependence of the results, combined with comparison to experiment, highlights the importance of including three-nucleon forces to simultaneously reproduce the binding energy and point-proton radius. For 8He, the hyperspherical harmonics method converges too slowly to provide precise results at present.

## Method
The hyperspherical harmonics (HH) method expands the nuclear wave function in a complete basis of hyperspherical harmonics constructed from Jacobi coordinates, using generalized Laguerre polynomials for the hyperradial part with an exponential falling-off function to handle halo tails. For 6He, the effective interaction in hyperspherical harmonics (EIHH) approach accelerates convergence, and the input Hamiltonian uses low-momentum chiral two-nucleon potentials at N3LO with cutoffs of 1.8, 2.0, and 2.4 fm⁻¹. For the electric dipole polarizability of 6He, the calculation employs the Lanczos algorithm with a starting dipole pivot using the semi-realistic Minnesota potential with an adjustable P-wave parameter u.

## Key numbers
- 6He ground-state energy: converged results obtained for three cutoff values Λ = 1.8, 2.0, 2.4 fm⁻¹ of the Vlowk chiral potential, with convergence patterns shown up to Kmax values in the figures.
- 6He point-proton radius: converged results obtained for the same three cutoff values, enabling solid predictions.
- 8He ground-state energy: calculated with Λ = 1.8 fm⁻¹ Vlowk chiral potential, but HH and EIHH results do not merge at Kmax = 10, indicating slow convergence.
- 6He electric dipole polarizability: estimated as αE = 1.00(14) fm³ from the Minnesota potential, compared to the experimental value αE_exp = 1.99(40) fm³.
- 6He skin radius: extrapolated to r_skin = 0.87(5) fm from bound-state calculations at Kmax = 16.
- 6He half-life: t1/2 = 0.8 s; 8He half-life: t1/2 = 0.1 s.

## Context
This work extends ab-initio studies of light neutron-rich halo nuclei using the hyperspherical harmonics method, previously applied to 3- and 4-body systems, to the two-neutron halo 6He and the four-neutron halo 8He. The results for 6He are compared to other ab-initio calculations including Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC), No Core Shell Model (NCSM), Fermionic Molecular Dynamics (FMD), and Microscopic Cluster Model (MCM), as well as to experimental data. The electric dipole polarizability calculation for 6He, motivated by Coulomb dissociation experiments by Aumann et al., shows a factor-of-two disagreement with the experimental value reported by Moro, which the author suggests may be resolved by future calculations with realistic chiral potentials including three-nucleon forces.
