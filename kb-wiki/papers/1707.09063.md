# Proton and neutron electromagnetic form factors and uncertainties

> We determine the nucleon electromagnetic form factors and their uncertainties from world electron scattering data. The analysis incorporates two-photon exchange corrections, constraints on the low-Q2 and high-Q2 behavior, and additional uncertainties to account for tensions between different data sets and uncertainties in radiative corrections.

## Key claim

The paper provides a global fit of nucleon electromagnetic form factors from world electron scattering data that incorporates two-photon exchange corrections and additional systematic uncertainties to account for tensions between data sets, but the fit constrains the proton charge radius to the CODATA consensus value of 0.879 fm rather than extracting it from the scattering data, and the authors note that separate fits with different radius assumptions can be used to estimate sensitivity to this choice.

## Method

The authors performed a simultaneous fit of proton electric and magnetic form factors to 657 cross-section values from the Mainz experiment, 562 cross-section values from other measurements, and 69 polarization extractions of the ratio G_E^p/G_M^p, using a bounded polynomial z-expansion with sum-rule constraints at low and high Q^2. For the neutron, separate fits were performed to 38 data points for G_E^n and 33 data points for G_M^n, with additional constraints from the neutron-electron scattering length for the electric radius. Two-photon exchange corrections were applied to cross-section data using the SIFF Blunden calculation, with an additional phenomenological correction at high Q^2 to resolve remaining differences between polarization and Rosenbluth extractions.

## Key numbers

- Final proton fit yields total chi-squared of 1144.3 for 1306 degrees of freedom
- G_E^n fit yields chi-squared of 24.50 for 45 degrees of freedom
- G_M^n fit yields chi-squared of 29.56 for 40 degrees of freedom
- Proton electric radius constrained to r_E^p = 0.879 fm (CODATA consensus value)
- Neutron electric radius constrained to (r_E^n)^2 = -0.1161(22) fm^2 from neutron-electron scattering length measurements
- Proton magnetic radius constrained to r_M^p = 0.851 fm (PDG consensus)
- Neutron magnetic radius constrained to r_M^n = 0.864 fm (PDG consensus)
- z-expansion parameter t_0 fixed to -0.7 GeV^2 for all four form factors
- Proton fits performed with k_max = 12; neutron fits with k_max = 10
- Additional TPE correction applied for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2: delta_{2gamma} -> delta_{2gamma} + 0.01 [epsilon-1] ln(Q^2)/ln(2.2)
- Dipole form factor defined as G_D = (1 + Q^2/0.71 GeV^2)^{-2}

## Context

This work updates previous global analyses such as Arrington (2007) and Venkat (2011) by incorporating the Mainz high-precision data from Bernauer (2014), applying updated two-photon exchange corrections, and providing full uncertainty parameterizations that earlier fits by Bosted (1994), Kelly (2004), and others lacked. The analysis enables reliable uncertainty estimates for neutrino-nucleus cross sections needed for long-baseline oscillation experiments, Coulomb sum rule studies, and searches for new particles in photon-initiated collider processes, while the unresolved proton radius puzzle means the fit cannot provide new information on the charge radius itself.
