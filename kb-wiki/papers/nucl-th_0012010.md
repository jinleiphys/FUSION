# Neutrinoless double beta decay within Self-consistent Renormalized Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation and inclusion of induced nucleon currents

> The first, to our knowledge, calculation of neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$-decay) matrix elements within the self-consistent renormalised Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (SRQRPA) is presented. The contribution from the momentum-dependent induced nucleon currents to $0νββ$-decay amplitude is taken into account. A detailed nuclear structure study includes the discussion of the sensitivity of the obtained SRQRPA results for $0νββ$-decay of $^{76}$Ge to the parameters of nuclear Hamiltonian, two-nucleon short-range correlations and the truncation of the model space. A comparision with the standard and renormalized QRPA is presented. We have found a considerable reduction of the SRQRPA nuclear matrix elements, resulting in less stringent limits for the effective neutrino mass.

## Key claim

The self-consistent renormalized Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation (pn-SRQRPA) predicts a considerably suppressed neutrinoless double beta decay nuclear matrix element for ⁷⁶Ge compared to the standard and renormalized QRPA, leading to a less stringent (larger) upper limit on the effective Majorana neutrino mass, but this result is sensitive to the particle-particle coupling strength and model space, with the matrix element crossing zero within the physical range of the coupling parameter for the larger model space.

## Method

The authors performed nuclear structure calculations of the 0νββ-decay matrix element for ⁷⁶Ge using three variants of the proton-neutron Quasiparticle Random Phase Approximation: the standard pn-QRPA, the renormalized pn-RQRPA (which approximately restores the Pauli exclusion principle), and the self-consistent pn-SRQRPA (which additionally modifies the mean field by minimizing energy in the correlated ground state). The calculations included momentum-dependent induced nucleon currents (vector, axial-vector, and pseudoscalar form factors) and were performed in two model spaces: a 9-level space (full 3-4ℏω major oscillator shells) and a 12-level space (full 2-5ℏω major shells), using a Bonn one-boson exchange potential with G-matrix elements and a Woods-Saxon single-particle potential.

## Key numbers

- For the pn-SRQRPA with g_pp = 1.0 and g_ph = 1.0, the nuclear matrix element M_⟨m_ee⟩ is 0.66 (model space I) and 0.59 (model space II).
- The corresponding upper limits on the effective neutrino mass ⟨m_ee⟩ from the pn-SRQRPA are 1.2 eV (model space I) and 1.3 eV (model space II), using the experimental half-life lower bound T_{1/2}^{0ν} ≥ 5.7 × 10²⁵ years for ⁷⁶Ge.
- For comparison, the pn-QRPA with g_pp = 1.0 gives M_⟨m_ee⟩ = 2.54 (model space I) and 1.53 (model space II), with ⟨m_ee⟩ limits of 0.30 eV and 0.50 eV respectively.
- The pn-RQRPA with g_pp = 1.0 gives M_⟨m_ee⟩ = 2.95 (model space I) and 2.61 (model space II), with ⟨m_ee⟩ limits of 0.26 eV and 0.29 eV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the line of QRPA-based calculations of 0νββ-decay matrix elements, following earlier studies by Vogel and Zirnbauer (1986) for the standard QRPA and by Toivanen and Suhonen (1995) and Schwieger, Simkovic, and Faessler (1996) for the renormalized QRPA. The authors note that previous shell model calculations by Haxton and Stephenson (1984) gave M_⟨m_ee⟩ = 4.18 (⟨m_ee⟩ ≤ 0.18 eV), while more recent shell model work by Caurier, Nowacki, Poves, and Retamosa (1996) gave M_⟨m_ee⟩ = 1.67 (⟨m_ee⟩ ≤ 0.46 eV), showing significant variation. The SRQRPA results presented here produce even larger upper limits on ⟨m_ee⟩, which would relax constraints on neutrino mass models if confirmed, but the zero-crossing of the matrix element within the physical g_pp range for the larger model space introduces considerable uncertainty.
