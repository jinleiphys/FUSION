# Strongly Intensive Cumulants: Fluctuation Measures for Systems With Incompletely Constrained Volumes

> The cumulants of thermal variables are of general interest in physics due to their extensivity and their correspondence with susceptibilities. They become especially significant near critical points of phase transitions where they diverge along with the correlation length. Cumulant measurements have been used extensively within the field of heavy-ion physics, principally as tools in the search for a hypothetical QCD critical point along the transition between hadronic matter and QGP. The volume of individual heavy-ion collisions can be only partially constrained and, as a result, cumulant measurements are significantly biased by the limited volume resolution. We propose a class of moments called strongly intensive cumulants which can be accurately measured in the presence of unconstrained volume fluctuations. Additionally, they share the same direct relationship with susceptibilities as cumulants in many cases.

## Key claim

Strongly intensive cumulants are a class of fluctuation measures that can be accurately measured in heavy-ion collisions even when the volume of individual collisions is only partially constrained, because they are invariant under both convolution and mixing of distributions sharing the same strongly intensive cumulants. The paper claims that these quantities share the same direct relationship with thermodynamic susceptibilities as standard cumulants in many cases, specifically when the volume-measure variable is independent of the other observables at fixed volume.

## Method

The author defines a generating function for strongly intensive cumulants via a partial differential equation involving the ratio of derivatives of the moment-generating function with respect to two chosen components. Recursion relations are derived to obtain polynomial expressions for the strongly intensive cumulants in terms of moments. The method is tested on 35 million UrQMD-simulated Au+Au events at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV, where net proton fluctuations are analyzed using both standard cumulant ratios and strongly intensive cumulant ratios, with the volume estimated via charged particle multiplicity in a pseudorapidity window (Ref_mult:2) at three different binomial efficiencies (p = 1/3, 2/3, and 1).

## Key numbers

- 35 million Au+Au events at √s_NN = 7.7 GeV were generated using the UrQMD model for the analysis.
- Net proton number was defined as protons minus antiprotons with transverse momenta 0.4 GeV < p_T < 0.8 GeV and pseudorapidities -0.5 < η < 0.5.
- The volume measure (Ref_mult:2) used charged particles with 0.5 < |η| < 1.0 and p_T > 0.15 GeV.
- Standard cumulant ratios were shifted by "well over a factor of two" in some cases due to volume fluctuations, while features observed in STAR data are on order of 20-40%.
- Strongly intensive cumulant ratios suppressed volume fluctuation effects by "well over an order of magnitude" compared to standard cumulant ratios.

## Context

This work extends the concept of strongly intensive observables previously introduced by Merick (1992, 2011, 2013) for second-order fluctuations (the Δ and Σ measures) to all orders via the strongly intensive cumulants. The paper directly addresses the bias in cumulant measurements of conserved charges used in the search for a QCD critical point, as measured by the STAR experiment in the RHIC beam energy scan (Stephanov, 2009; Westfall, 2014). The strongly intensive cumulants enable future analyses to extract fluctuation measures that are independent of volume distribution, allowing direct comparison to lattice QCD calculations of susceptibilities without the systematic uncertainty from volume fluctuations.
