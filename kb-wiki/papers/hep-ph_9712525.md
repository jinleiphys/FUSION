# Chiral Perturbation Theory for Nuclear Physicists

> Chiral perturbation theory is the low energy effective theory of the strong interactions for the light pseudoscalar degrees of freedom. This program is based on effective Lagrangian techniques and is an expansion in the powers of the momenta and the powers of the quark masses, which correct the soft-pion theorems. After briefly reviewing these features and some results, we address the implications of this program to $π-N$ scattering, the $π-N$ sigma term and some recent investigations of the implications of chiral symmetry to nucleon-nucleon forces. We finally look at the implications of chiral perturbation theory to hadron mass relations.

# Chiral Perturbation Theory for Nuclear Physicists

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory provides a systematic low-energy effective framework for the strong interactions based on QCD's chiral symmetry, but its predictive power in the baryonic sector is limited by the proliferation of coupling constants that must be fixed from experiment or from non-perturbative considerations such as large Nc, and the nucleon-nucleon force sector in particular remains theoretically challenging with competing approaches regarding the role of loop effects versus tree-level contributions.

## Method

The paper reviews the effective Lagrangian technique for the light pseudoscalar mesons (pions) as Goldstone bosons of spontaneously broken chiral symmetry, expanding in powers of momenta and quark masses. It describes the construction of the generating functional for QCD Green's functions in the presence of external sources, the non-linear sigma model at leading order O(p^2), and the O(p^4) Lagrangian with its 10 additional coupling constants. For pion-nucleon scattering, the paper discusses both the relativistic framework and the heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory approach that accounts for the nucleon being much heavier than the pion.

## Key numbers

- Pion decay constant: Fπ ≈ 93 MeV
- Leading-order prediction for the S-wave ππ scattering length: a⁰₀ = 7/(32πFπ²) ≈ 0.16 (with mπ = 139 MeV set to unity)
- Next-to-leading order (O(p⁴)) prediction for a⁰₀: 0.20 ± 0.01, compared to the experimental value 0.26 ± 0.05
- Goldberger-Treiman relation: gπN ≈ mN gA / Fπ, with gπN ≈ 13 (from √(4π × 13.5)) and gA = 1.26
- Lowest-order predictions for πN scattering lengths: isospin 3/2 = -0.075, isospin 1/2 = 0.15

## Context

This invited talk (DAE Nuclear Physics Symposium, 1997) reviews the status of chiral perturbation theory as developed by Gasser and Leutwyler (1984) and extended to baryons by Gasser, Sainio, and Svarc (1988), with the heavy baryon formalism of Jenkins and Manohar (1991). It discusses Weinberg's proposals (1990, 1991) for applying chiral symmetry to nucleon-nucleon forces, contrasting the approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1996) with that of Amghar, Hadjimichef, and Szpigel (1997) regarding the dominance of loop effects and the role of explicit chiral symmetry breaking in the scalar-isoscalar channel. The paper enables nuclear physicists to connect modern chiral effective field theory with traditional dispersion relation analyses and current algebra results.
