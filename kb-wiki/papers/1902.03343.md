# Relativistic second order dissipative hydrodynamics from effective fugacity quasi particle model

> In this work a second order relativistic viscous hydrodynamic model has been presented based on the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM). The hydro model has been derived from the effective relativistic second-order transport equation under EQPM for a multi-particle (two component) system and solving it in Grad's 14 moment method. The EQPM model describes the strongly interacting thermal system of QCD interactions through its fugacity parameters extracted from an updated lattice equations of state. The proper time evolution of temperature and pressure anisotropy is observed to be affected significantly due to the inclusion of EQPM model compared to an ideal system.

## Key claim

The paper derives second-order relativistic viscous hydrodynamic equations incorporating the effective fugacity quasi-particle model (EQPM) to describe strongly interacting QCD matter, finding that the proper time evolution of temperature and pressure anisotropy is significantly affected by the EQPM model compared to an ideal system, particularly at lower temperatures where quasi-particle effects are most prominent.

## Method

The author derived second-order hydrodynamic equations from a relativistic transport equation for a multi-component (two-species) system using Grad's 14-moment method, incorporating the EQPM which maps hot QCD effects into non-interacting quasi-quarks and quasi-gluons via temperature-dependent fugacity parameters extracted from updated (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD equations of state. The shear viscosity was first estimated using the Chapman-Enskog method, then the second-order shear viscous flow equation was obtained including relaxation time and additional nonlinear terms. The coupled temperature and shear viscous pressure evolution equations were solved numerically for a 1+1 boost-invariant system with initial conditions corresponding to RHIC and LHC heavy-ion collision parameters.

## Context

This work extends previous second-order relativistic hydrodynamic studies using quasiparticle models, particularly effective mass models such as those by Tinti (2014) and Alqahtani (2015), by employing the EQPM approach where medium effects are encoded in fugacity parameters rather than temperature-dependent masses. The EQPM model was previously developed by Chandra (2012) and extended to finite chemical potential by Mitra (2018). The results are compared with BAMPS transport simulations and other quasiparticle hydrodynamic models (vHydro, QaHydro, QvHydro), showing that the EQPM predictions for pressure anisotropy are consistent with these approaches while exhibiting quantitative differences due to the different equation of state implementation. This work enables more realistic modeling of dissipative QGP dynamics in heavy-ion collisions by incorporating lattice QCD equations of state within a consistently formulated covariant kinetic theory framework.
