# Effects of resonance weak decays and hadronic re-scattering on the proton number fluctuations in Au + Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5$ GeV from JAM model

> Proton number fluctuation is sensitive observable to search for the QCD critical point in heavy-ion collisions. In this paper, we studied rapidity acceptance dependence of the proton cumulants and correlation functions in most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5$ GeV from a microscopic hadronic transport model (JAM). At mid-rapidity, we found the effects of resonance weak decays and hadronic re-scattering on the proton cumulants and correlation functions are small, but those effects get larger when further increasing the rapidity acceptance. On the other hand, we found the baryon number conservation is a dominant background effect on the rapidity acceptance dependence of proton number fluctuations. It leads to a strong suppression of cumulants and cumulant ratios, as well as the negative proton correlation functions. We also studied those two effects on the energy dependence of cumulant ratios of net-proton distributions in most central Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_\mathrm{NN}} = 5-200$ GeV from JAM model. This work can serve as a non-critical baseline for future QCD critical point search in heavy-ion collisions at high baryon density region.

## Key claim

The effects of resonance weak decays and hadronic re-scattering on proton cumulants and correlation functions are small at mid-rapidity in Au+Au collisions at 5 GeV, but these effects become larger when the rapidity acceptance is increased; baryon number conservation is the dominant background effect on the rapidity acceptance dependence of proton number fluctuations.

## Method

The authors used the JAM (Jet AA Microscopic Transportation Model) hadronic transport model to simulate most central (0-5%) Au+Au collisions at 5 GeV. They generated five types of simulation data with mean-field equation of state, systematically turning on or off resonance weak decays, meson-baryon/meson-meson interactions, and hadronic elastic scattering to isolate each effect. Proton cumulants (C1 through C4) and multi-proton correlation functions (kappa2 through kappa4) were calculated as functions of rapidity acceptance, and net-proton cumulant ratios were studied as functions of collision energy from 5 to 200 GeV.

## Key numbers

- At mid-rapidity (Delta y / Delta y_beam < 0.3) in 5 GeV Au+Au collisions, the effects of resonance weak decays and hadronic re-scattering on proton cumulants and correlation functions are small.
- The beam rapidity for sqrt(s_NN) = 5 GeV is y_beam = 1.63 (Delta y_beam = 3.26).
- For C4/C2 (kappa sigma^2) of net-proton distributions at energies from 5 to 200 GeV, the values from all four simulation cases are below the Poisson baseline of unity and show a monotonic decreasing trend with decreasing collision energy, failing to reproduce the non-monotonic energy dependence observed in STAR data.
- The MB/MM scattering suppresses C3/C2 values and enhances C2/C1 values, especially at high energy (e.g., 200 GeV).

## Context

This work extends previous studies of background effects on proton number fluctuations in heavy-ion collisions, including baryon number conservation (Bzdak, 2012), acceptance/efficiency corrections (Bzdak, 2012; Luo, 2014), and light nuclei formation (Feckova, 2015). It provides a non-critical baseline from the JAM model for the QCD critical point search in the high baryon density region, particularly relevant for future FAIR/CBM and NICA/MPD experiments. The results show that the JAM model cannot describe the non-monotonic energy dependence of kappa sigma^2 observed by STAR (Adamczyk, 2014; Luo, 2017), indicating that the model lacks the physics of phase transition and critical point.
