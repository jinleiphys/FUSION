# Study of the proton-proton collisions at 1683 MeV/c

> The new data on the elastic $pp$ and single pion production reaction $pp\to pn π^{+}$ taken at the incident proton momentum 1683 MeV/c are presented. The data on the $pp\to pn π^{+}$ reaction are compared with predictions from the OPE model. To extract contributions of the leading partial waves the single pion production data are analyzed in the framework of the event-by-event maximum likelihood method together with the data measured earlier. The analysis allows us to obtain the assessment with a better precision the contributions from different initial partial waves.

## Key claim

The new data on elastic pp and single pion production at 1683 MeV/c, combined with earlier data in a partial wave analysis, allow a more precise determination of partial wave contributions than previously possible, but the analysis still finds ambiguities for high-spin partial waves (J up to 5) that affect the extracted contributions from specific initial states such as \(^3P_0\) and \(^3F_2\).

## Method

The experiment used the PNPI 1 GeV synchrocyclotron with a hydrogen bubble chamber; events were geometrically reconstructed and kinematically fitted to five reaction hypotheses. The single pion production data were analyzed via an event-by-event maximum likelihood partial wave analysis, combining the new 1683 MeV/c data with earlier \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) data and \(pp\to pn\pi^+\) data at 1628 and 1581 MeV/c. The amplitude was decomposed into spin-orbital partial waves with Blatt-Weisskopf form factors, and the energy dependence of transition amplitudes was parameterized with poles approximating left-hand side singularities.

## Key numbers

* Total elastic cross section at 1683 MeV/c: \(23.96 \pm 0.57\) mb (from Legendre polynomial fit).
* Total cross section for \(pp\to pn\pi^+\) at 1683 MeV/c: \(18.97 \pm 0.57\) mb.
* Total cross section for \(pp\to d\pi^+\) at 1683 MeV/c: \(0.42 \pm 0.05\) mb.
* Total cross section for \(pp\to d\pi^+\pi^0\) at 1683 MeV/c: \(0.05 \pm 0.02\) mb.
* Incident proton momentum spread: 7 MeV/c (FWHM); momentum accuracy: 0.5 MeV/c.
* Double scanning efficiency: 99.95%.
* For the \(^3P_0\) initial wave, only 12% of its contribution comes from \(\Delta(1232)\) production; for all other initial partial waves, the \(\Delta(1232)\) contribution ranges from 65% to 100%.
* The OPE model prediction for the \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) total cross section at 1683 MeV/c is about 30% smaller than the experimental value when the form factor is chosen to reproduce the \(pp\to pn\pi^+\) cross section.

## Context

This work extends the partial wave analysis approach of Anisovich et al. (2007) and the earlier lower-energy analyses by Ermakov et al. (2011, 2014) to a higher incident momentum (1683 MeV/c). It confirms the dominant role of \(\Delta(1232)\) production via one-pion exchange (OPE) as modeled by Suslenko et al. (1984), but shows that the OPE model fails to simultaneously describe the total cross sections of \(pp\to pn\pi^+\) and \(pp\to pp\pi^0\) due to contributions from the Roper resonance \(N(1440)\). The results enable future joint partial wave analyses including HADES data at 1.25 GeV (Agakishiev et al., 2015) and provide a data set for other groups to use in their own analyses.
