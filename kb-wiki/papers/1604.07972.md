# Physical and unphysical solutions of random-phase approximation equation

> Properties of solutions of the RPA equation is reanalyzed mathematically, which is defined as a generalized eigenvalue problem of the stability matrix $\mathsf{S}$ with the norm matrix $\mathsf{N}=\mathrm{diag.}(1,-1)$. As well as physical solutions, unphysical solutions are examined in detail, with taking the possibility of Jordan blocks of the matrix $\mathsf{N\,S}$ into consideration. Two types of duality of eigenvectors and basis vectors of the Jordan blocks are pointed out and explored, which disclose many basic properties of the RPA solutions.

## Key claim

The paper reanalyzes the mathematical properties of solutions to the random-phase approximation (RPA) equation as a generalized eigenvalue problem, showing that unphysical solutions (including those from Jordan blocks) are not merely meaningless but help clarify how physical solutions and Nambu-Goldstone modes emerge, with the caveat that full solvability requires the stability matrix to be positive-definite.

## Method

The RPA equation is reformulated as a generalized eigenvalue problem of the stability matrix S with the norm matrix N = diag(1, -1), and the eigenvalue problem of the matrix N S is analyzed using linear algebra. Two types of duality are introduced: LR-duality connecting left and right basis vectors of the Jordan blocks, and UL-duality connecting eigenvectors via the transformation involving the matrix Σ_x. The vector space is decomposed into subspaces via projectors constructed from the basis vectors, and the properties of each class of eigenvalues (real positive, real negative, pure imaginary, complex quartets, and null) are examined separately, including the possibility of Jordan blocks.

## Key numbers

* The dimension of the A and B matrices is denoted by D, which is finite in many practical calculations.
* The norm matrix N is defined as diag(1, -1), with N² = Σ_x² = 1 and Σ_x N + N Σ_x = 0.
* For a Jordan block of dimension d_ν, the basis vectors satisfy S ξ_{k+1}^{(ν)} = ω_ν N ξ_{k+1}^{(ν)} + i c_k^{(ν)} N ξ_k^{(ν)} with c_k^{(ν)} ∈ C.
* The dimension d_{[ν]} of the subspace W_{[ν]} must be even, as proven from the properties of the UL-duality and Jordan blocks.

## Context

This work extends the foundational analysis of RPA stability by Thouless (1961) and Thouless & Valatin (1962), who did not discuss solutions with unnormalizable eigenvectors or complex eigenvalues (Classes negative and quartet). The paper provides rigorous mathematical treatment for a variety of extensions of the RPA developed to date, including applications to high-spin states and collective motion paths, and is practically significant for coding numerical RPA programs where the structure of the vector space around the mean-field state is not fully known.
