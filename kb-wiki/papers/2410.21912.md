# Chiral perturbation theory

> In the limit of vanishing up, down and strange quark masses, QCD exhibits a chiral symmetry. This symmetry is broken spontaneously to its vector subgroup, giving rise to Goldstone bosons. These acquire a small mass through the explicit chiral symmetry breaking for non-vanishing quark masses. The consequences of these broken symmetries can be investigated in a suitably tailored effective field theory called chiral pertubation theory. It admits a perturbative expansion in the external momenta and the Goldstone boson masses and can be systematically analyzed in terms of a loop expansion. The appearing ultraviolet divergences in loop diagrams can be dealt with order-by-order through the Goldstone boson contact interactions. Matter fields like the lowest-lying baryons can also included, leading to a rich and testable phenomenology of low-energy QCD.

## Key claim

Chiral perturbation theory (CHPT) is the effective field theory of low-energy QCD that systematically exploits the spontaneous and explicit breaking of chiral symmetry, but its predictive power relies on the assumption that the expansion in small momenta and Goldstone boson masses can be mapped onto a loop expansion, with ultraviolet divergences absorbed order-by-order into low-energy constants that must be determined from data.

## Method

The author constructs the chiral effective Lagrangian by collecting the Goldstone bosons (pions, kaons, eta) into a matrix-valued field \(U(x)\) that transforms non-linearly under the chiral symmetry group SU(3)\(_L\) × SU(3)\(_R\). The Lagrangian is organized by chiral dimension (number of derivatives or quark mass insertions), with the leading-order term containing two derivatives and a quark-mass term proportional to the constant \(B\) related to the scalar quark condensate. Loop diagrams are regularized using dimensional regularization, and their divergences are absorbed into the low-energy constants at next-to-leading order.

## Key numbers

* The pion decay constant \(F_\pi \simeq 92\) MeV is the order parameter of spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, extracted from the axial-current matrix element \(\langle 0|A_\mu^i(0)|\pi^k(p)\rangle = i F_\pi p_\mu \delta^{jk}\).
* The scale of spontaneous symmetry breaking is \(\Lambda_\chi \simeq 4\pi F_\pi \simeq 1\) GeV, also identifiable with the \(\rho(770)\) vector meson mass.
* The leading-order Gell-Mann–Oakes–Renner relation gives \(M_\pi^2 = (m_u+m_d)B\), and this leading term provides 94% of the pion mass.
* From the leading-order meson mass relations, the quark mass ratio is \(m_s/(m_u+m_d) \simeq 24\) and \(m_u/m_d = 0.66\).
* The QCD scale is \(\Lambda_{\rm QCD} \simeq 240\) MeV in the \(\overline{\rm MS}\) scheme at \(\mu = 2\) GeV.

## Context

This chapter updates and extends the foundational work of Gasser and Leutwyler (1983, 1984) who first constructed the systematic effective Lagrangian for mesons, and of Weinberg (1978) who proved the effective field theory theorem. The framework enables precision calculations of low-energy hadron properties and reactions, including the role of chiral anomalies as discussed by Fujikawa (1983) and the treatment of baryons as matter fields coupled to the Goldstone bosons. The next steps involve applying CHPT to processes such as pion-pion scattering and form factors, and extending the framework to include the lowest-lying baryon octet.
