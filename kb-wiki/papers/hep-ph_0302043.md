# QCD Form Factors and Hadron Helicity Non-Conservation

> Recent data for the ratio $R(Q)= QF_{2}(Q^{2})/F_{1}(Q^{2})$ shocked the community by disobeying expectations held for 50 years. We examine the status of perturbative QCD predictions for helicity-flip form factors. Contrary to common belief, we find there is no rule of hadron helicity conservation for form factors. Instead the analysis yields an inequality that the leading power of helicity-flip processes may equal or exceed the power of helicity conserving processes. Numerical calculations support the rule, and extend the result to the regime of laboratory momentum transfer $Q^{2}$. Quark orbital angular momentum, an important feature of the helicity flip processes, may play a role in all form factors at large $Q^{2}$, depending on the quark wave functions.

## Key claim

Contrary to the long-standing belief in hadron helicity conservation (HHC) for form factors, perturbative QCD does not enforce a power suppression of helicity-flip processes; instead it yields an inequality that the leading power of helicity-flip processes may equal or exceed that of helicity-conserving processes, depending on the unknown x-dependence of the quark wave functions.

## Method

The authors re-examine the perturbative QCD predictions for helicity-flip form factors using impact-parameter factorization, which retains the transverse coordinate dependence rather than taking the zero-distance limit assumed in the asymptotic short-distance (asd) model of Brodsky and Lepage. They perform a Mellin transform analysis of the form factor integrals to extract the asymptotic Q² power behavior, and complement this with numerical calculations of b-moments for both the pion and proton form factor kernels, using various model wave functions with and without Sudakov form factors.

## Key numbers

- The Jefferson Lab data for the ratio \(R(Q)= QF_{2}(Q^{2})/F_{1}(Q^{2})\) is observed to be approximately constant up to \(Q^{2} \sim 5.8\) GeV\(^{2}\)
- For the pion form factor, the moment \(\langle b(Q)\rangle_\pi\) falls as \(1/Q^{0.42}\) for \(\delta=1\) and as \(1/Q^{0.2}\) for \(\delta=0.2\) at small Q², much slower than the asd prediction of \(1/Q\)
- For the proton form factor using a wave function \(\phi(x_i) \sim (x_1 x_2 x_3)^{0.5}\), the moments \(\langle b_2\rangle_p\) and \(\langle b_1\rangle_p\) fall asymptotically as \(1/Q^{0.2}\) and \(1/Q^{0.14}\) respectively for Q² > 100 GeV²
- With the COZ wave function, the moment \(\langle b_2\rangle_p\) falls as \(1/Q^{0.58}\) at large Q², still much weaker than the asd prediction of \(1/Q\)
- The scaling \(F_1 \sim 1/Q^4\) is seen only for \(Q^2 \ge 6\) GeV², which is larger than the momentum regime explored at JLab

## Context

This work extends the earlier quark-counting model of Brodsky, Farrar, and Matveev, while directly contradicting the asymptotic short-distance (asd) model of Brodsky and Lepage (1980, 1989) which predicted hadron helicity conservation and a \(1/Q^2\) suppression of \(F_2\) relative to \(F_1\). The analysis builds on impact-parameter factorization methods developed by Li and Sterman (1992) and earlier work by Soper (1977) on the Drell-Yan-West relation. The results enable future measurements at higher Q² to determine whether the flat ratio \(QF_2/F_1 \sim\) constant persists, which would indicate the importance of quark orbital angular momentum in the proton wave functions and resolve questions about the proton spin crisis.
