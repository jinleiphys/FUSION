# Isoscalar giant monopole resonance and its overtone in microscopic and macroscopic models

> We calculate the transition density for the overtone of the isosclar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) from the response to an appropriate external field $\hat{f}_ξ(r)$ obtained using the seemiclassical fluid dynamic approximation and the Hartree-Fock (HF) based random phase approximation (RPA). We determine the mixing parameter $ξ$ by maximizing the ratio of the energy weighted sum for the overtone mode to the total energy weighted sum rule and derive a simple expression for the microscopic transition density associated with the overtone mode. This macroscopic transition density agrees well with that obtained from the HF-RPA calculations. We also point out that the ISGMR and its overtone can be clearly identified by considering the response to the electromagnetic external field $\hat{j}_o(qr)$ .

# Isoscalar giant monopole resonance and its overtone in microscopic and macroscopic models

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that the overtone of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) can be isolated using a specifically chosen transition operator with a mixing parameter, and that the resulting macroscopic transition density agrees well with microscopic HF-RPA calculations, provided the mixing parameter is determined by maximizing the fraction of the energy-weighted sum rule exhausted by the overtone mode.

## Method

The authors calculate transition densities for the ISGMR overtone using both the Hartree-Fock based random phase approximation (HF-RPA) with Skyrme force SkM* and the semiclassical Fermi-liquid approach (FLA). They introduce a transition operator of the form f_xi(r) = (r^4 - xi r^2)Y_00 and determine the mixing parameter xi by maximizing the ratio of the energy-weighted sum for the overtone mode to the total energy-weighted sum rule. The macroscopic transition density is then derived from the local sum rule and compared with the microscopic results.

## Key numbers

- For 208Pb, the mixing parameter xi = 68.3 fm^2 in FLA (overtone exhausts about 73% of EWSR) and xi = 78.6 fm^2 in HF-RPA (overtone exhausts about 60% of EWSR in energy range 25-50 MeV)
- The ratio E_02/E_01 for the overtone to main ISGMR centroid energies is approximately 2.2-2.4 in both models
- The mixing parameter scales as xi = 1.89 A^(2/3) fm^2 for FLA and xi = 2.36 A^(2/3) fm^2 for RPA
- For 90Zr, xi = 38.6 fm^2 (FLA) and 48.5 fm^2 (RPA); for 116Sn, xi = 45.9 fm^2 (FLA) and 56.7 fm^2 (RPA); for 144Sm, xi = 53.1 fm^2 (FLA) and 66.1 fm^2 (RPA)
- Cross-section at 0 degrees for the ISGMR overtone peak in 208Pb: 0.5 mb/(sr MeV) for 240 MeV alpha-particles and 3.5 mb/(sr MeV) for 400 MeV alpha-particles
- The distance between nodes of the RPA transition density decreases with energy: 3.1 fm at 30 MeV, 2.7 fm at 40 MeV, and 2.4 fm at 50 MeV

## Context

This work extends the scaling approximation for the ISGMR transition density, originally developed by Blaizot (1980) and described in Bohr and Mottelson (1975), to the overtone mode which has a two-node structure. The authors build on the Fermi-liquid approach of Kolomietz and Shlomo (2000) and the HF-RPA framework of Agrawal, Shlomo, and Sanzhur (2003). The results enable experimental identification of the ISGMR overtone through inelastic alpha-particle scattering at 400 MeV (where cross-sections exceed current experimental sensitivity of about 2 mb/(sr MeV) as reported by Youngblood) and through electron-nucleus scattering by varying momentum transfer to exploit the distinct k-dependence of the overtone response.
