# Demystifying the fusion mechanism in heavy-ion collisions: A six-dimensional Langevin dissipative dynamics approach

> We present an in-depth investigation of heavy-ion fusion dynamics using a six-dimensional Langevin framework that enables unrestricted motion of the asymmetry parameter. The stochastic formalism naturally incorporates friction effects and energy fluctuations, providing a detailed understanding of the fusion process. The dynamics transition into the overdamped regime, facilitating rapid neck stabilization while effectively capturing the interplay between shape and rotational degrees of freedom. This approach achieves excellent agreement with experimental spin distributions and fusion cross-sections, establishing a robust foundation for forthcoming studies on the synthesis of superheavy elements and the exploration of the enigmatic fusion hindrance mechanism.

## Key claim

The six-dimensional Langevin framework, which allows unrestricted motion of the asymmetry parameter and includes all off-diagonal tensor elements, achieves excellent agreement with experimental spin distributions and fusion cross-sections for the systems studied, but only after applying a 2.4 MeV energy shift for the neutron-rich system and separating the fusion process into three distinct stages to handle configuration boundaries and regime changes.

## Method

The authors developed a six-dimensional Langevin dissipative dynamics framework using collective variables for elongation (ρ), neck (λ), asymmetry (Δ), and three rotational angles (θ₀, θ₁, θ₂). The shape friction was calculated using the wall-plus-window friction model with a smooth transition between mononuclear and dinuclear dissipation regimes, incorporating the proximity formalism with a universal proximity distance of 3.2 fm. The potential, mass, and friction tensor elements were precomputed on a 100 × 100 × 100 grid and extrapolated using cubic B-splines, with the Langevin equations numerically integrated using a time step of 10⁻²⁵ s and the Heun method.

## Key numbers

- For ⁶⁴Ni + ⁹²Zr at E_cm = 138.8 MeV: calculated total fusion cross-section σ_fus = 107 mb, compared to experimental value of 100 mb
- For ⁶⁴Ni + ⁹⁶Zr at E_cm = 139.5 MeV: calculated total fusion cross-section σ_fus = 175 mb (with 2.4 MeV energy shift), compared to experimental value of 166 mb; without the shift σ_fus = 145 mb
- Calculated fusion Q-values: Q_fus = -91.7 MeV for ⁶⁴Ni + ⁹²Zr (experimental: -91.4 MeV) and Q_fus = -88.9 MeV for ⁶⁴Ni + ⁹⁶Zr (experimental: -86.5 MeV)
- The quantum-corrected temperature parameter E₀ was set to 2 MeV, with the level density parameter a = A/8 MeV⁻¹
- The proximity separation distance s_prox = 3.2 fm, with contact estimated at d₀ = R₁,₀ + R₂,₀ + 2.0 fm
- The spherical compound nucleus moment of inertia I_tot^sph = 60.2 ℏ²/MeV for the ⁶⁴Ni + ⁹²Zr system

## Context

This work extends the prior Langevin model of Przystupa (1994) which fixed the asymmetry parameter due to computational constraints, and the authors demonstrate that properly treating configuration boundaries, regime changes, and separating the fusion process into three distinct stages is essential for achieving physical asymptotic spin distributions. The approach builds on the shape parametrization of Blocki and Swiatecki (1982) and the wall-plus-window friction model of Blocki (1977, 1978), and the results validate the model for future studies of superheavy element synthesis and the fusion hindrance mechanism at lower excitation energies where shell effects become important.
