# Transversity distributions and Drell-Yan spin asymmetries

> We discuss transversity distributions and Drell-Yan transverse double spin asymmetries. First, the antiquark flavor asymmetry $Δ_{_T} \bar u/Δ_{_T} \bar d$ is discussed by using two different descriptions, a meson-cloud model and a Pauli exclusion model. We find that both calculations produce a significant $Δ_{_T} \bar d$ excess over $Δ_{_T} \bar u$. Next, we study its effects on the transverse spin asymmetry $A_{_{TT}}$ and on the Drell-Yan proton-deuteron asymmetry $Δ_{_T}σ^{pd}/2 Δ_{_T}σ^{pp}$. We find that the ratio $Δ_{_T}σ^{pd}/2 Δ_{_T}σ^{pp}$ is very useful for investigating the flavor asymmetry effect.

## Key claim

Both a meson-cloud model and a Pauli exclusion model predict a significant excess of transversity \(\Delta_{_T} \bar d\) over \(\Delta_{_T} \bar u\), but the meson-cloud contribution appears smaller than that from the Pauli exclusion model; the ratio \(\Delta_{_T}\sigma^{pd}/2 \Delta_{_T}\sigma^{pp}\) is proposed as a cleaner probe of this flavor asymmetry than the transverse double spin asymmetry \(A_{_{TT}}\) alone.

## Method

The author calculated the transversity antiquark flavor asymmetry \(\Delta_{_T} \bar u - \Delta_{_T} \bar d\) using two nonperturbative approaches: a meson-cloud model (considering \(\rho\)-meson–nucleon–baryon splitting processes) and a Pauli exclusion model (based on SU(6) quark-model probabilities). The resulting distributions were evolved to \(Q^2 = 10\) GeV\(^2\) using NLO QCD evolution and then used to compute the transverse double spin asymmetry \(A_{_{TT}}\) for Drell-Yan at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV and the proton-deuteron asymmetry \(\Delta_{_T}\sigma^{pd}/2 \Delta_{_T}\sigma^{pp}\) in the large \(x_F\) region.

## Key numbers

- Pauli exclusion model yields \(\Delta\overline{u}= -0.13\) and \(\Delta\overline{d}= +0.05\) at the starting scale.
- The transverse double spin asymmetry \(A_{_{TT}}\) is about 1% in the dimuon mass region \(100 < M_{\mu\mu}^2 < 500\) GeV\(^2\) at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV.
- The flavor-symmetric prediction for \(\Delta_{_T}\sigma^{pd}/2 \Delta_{_T}\sigma^{pp}\) is approximately one in the large \(x_F\) region, while both asymmetric models give values significantly different from one.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of unpolarized antiquark flavor asymmetry (e.g., from NMC, NA51, E866, and HERMES) into the transversity sector, where no experimental data existed at the time. It builds on prior NLO evolution calculations for transversity by Hayashigaki, Kanazawa, and Morii (1997) and on the Pauli exclusion model for polarized sea by Brodsky and Schmidt (1995). The proposed proton-deuteron asymmetry measurement offers a path to distinguish \(\Delta_{_T} \bar u / \Delta_{_T} \bar d\) at future facilities such as RHIC, FNAL, or HERA, complementing the \(W^\pm\) production method used for longitudinal polarization.
