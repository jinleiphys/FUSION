# Exponential speedup in quantum simulation of Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian via orbifold lattice

> We demonstrate that the orbifold lattice Hamiltonian -- an approach known for its efficiency in simulating SU($N$) Yang-Mills theory and QCD on digital quantum computers -- can reproduce the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian in a controlled limit. While the original Kogut-Susskind approach faces significant implementation challenges on quantum hardware, we show that it emerges naturally as the infinite scalar mass limit of the orbifold lattice formulation, even at finite lattice spacing. Our analysis provides both a general analytical framework applicable to SU($N$) gauge theories in arbitrary dimensions and specific numerical evidence for $(2+1)$-dimensional SU($N$) Yang-Mills theories ($N=2,3$). Using Euclidean path integral methods, we quantify the convergence rate by comparing the standard Wilson action with the orbifold lattice action, matching lattice parameters, and systematically extrapolating results as the bare scalar mass approaches infinity. This reformulation resolves longstanding technical obstacles and offers a straightforward implementation protocol for digital quantum simulation of the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian with exponential speedup compared to classical methods and previously known quantum methods.

## Key claim
The orbifold lattice Hamiltonian reproduces the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian exactly in the limit of infinite bare scalar mass, even at finite lattice spacing, resolving implementation obstacles that have hindered direct quantum simulation of the Kogut-Susskind approach. The claim is conditional: the equivalence holds as a controlled limit, with convergence quantified by comparing the standard Wilson action to the orbifold lattice action and extrapolating as the bare scalar mass approaches infinity.

## Method
The authors provide an analytical framework showing that the orbifold lattice formulation for SU(N) Yang-Mills theory reduces to the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian when the scalar mass parameter is taken to infinity. They perform Euclidean path integral calculations comparing the standard Wilson action with the orbifold lattice action, matching lattice parameters across formulations. Numerical evidence is presented for (2+1)-dimensional SU(2) and SU(3) Yang-Mills theories, where the convergence rate is studied by systematically extrapolating results as the bare scalar mass increases.

## Key numbers
- For SU(3) Yang-Mills theory in (2+1) dimensions, numerical results are shown at bare scalar mass squared values m^2 = 100, 300, and 1000, with temporal lattice spacings a_t = 0.20, 0.25, and 0.30 (in lattice units).
- The analysis covers SU(N) gauge theories with N=2 and N=3, providing specific numerical evidence for these two gauge groups.

## Context
This work extends the orbifold lattice approach previously developed for efficient quantum simulation of SU(N) Yang-Mills theory and QCD, showing it can recover the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian that has faced significant hardware implementation challenges. The result enables a straightforward implementation protocol for digital quantum simulation of the Kogut-Susskind Hamiltonian, offering exponential speedup compared to both classical methods and previously known quantum approaches. The framework is general for SU(N) gauge theories in arbitrary dimensions, opening the door to quantum simulations of lattice gauge theories that were previously considered impractical.
