# Multinucleon transfer reaction in time-dependent Hartree-Fock theory

> Time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory has achieved a remarkable success in describing and understanding nuclear many-body dynamics from nucleons' degrees of freedom. We here report our investigation of multinucleon transfer (MNT) processes employing the TDHF theory. To calculate transfer probabilities for channels specified by the number of protons and neutrons included in reaction products, a particle-number projection (PNP) method has been developed. The PNP method is also used to calculate excitation energies of reaction products. Combined use of the PNP method with a statistical model, we can evaluate MNT cross sections taking account of effects of particle evaporation. Using these methods, we evaluate MNT cross sections for $^{40,48}$Ca+$^{124}$Sn, $^{40}$Ca+$^{208}$Pb, and $^{58}$Ni+$^{208}$Pb reactions. From systematic analyses, we find that cross sections for channels with a large reaction probability are in good agreement with experimental data. However, the agreement becomes less accurate as the number of transferred nucleons increases. Possible directions to improve the description are discussed.

## Key claim

The authors claim that time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) theory combined with a particle-number projection (PNP) method can describe multinucleon transfer (MNT) cross sections, but the agreement with experimental data is good only for channels with large reaction probability and becomes less accurate as the number of transferred nucleons increases.

## Method

The authors performed TDHF calculations for MNT reactions of 40,48Ca+124Sn, 40Ca+208Pb, and 58Ni+208Pb using the Skyrme SLy5 energy density functional on a three-dimensional Cartesian grid with 0.8 fm mesh spacing. They applied a particle-number projection (PNP) method to extract transfer probabilities for channels specified by proton and neutron numbers from the TDHF wave function after collision, and used the PNP method to calculate excitation energies of reaction products. The excitation energies were then used as input to a simple statistical evaporation model by Dostrovsky to account for particle evaporation effects on the final cross sections.

## Key numbers

- 40Ca+124Sn at Elab = 170 MeV, 48Ca+124Sn at Elab = 174 MeV, 40Ca+208Pb at Elab = 235 and 249 MeV, and 58Ni+208Pb at Elab = 328.4 MeV were the systems and energies studied
- Maximum total kinetic energy loss (TKEL) values: 25 MeV and 18 MeV for 40,48Ca+124Sn, 50 MeV and 60 MeV for 40Ca+208Pb at 235 and 249 MeV, and 60 MeV for 58Ni+208Pb
- Charge products ZPZT: 1000 for 40,48Ca+124Sn, 1640 for 40Ca+208Pb, and 2296 for 58Ni+208Pb
- Mesh spacing of 0.8 fm was used for the TDHF calculations
- 200 discrete points (M=200) were used for the trapezoidal rule in the PNP integral
- For 58Ni+208Pb, up to 13 neutrons and 5 protons were transferred at small impact parameters

## Context

This work extends the TDHF approach to MNT reactions, building on the PNP method proposed by Simenel (2011) which reduced computational costs compared to the earlier method by Koonin et al. (1977). The authors compare their results with experimental data from Corradi and collaborators, and find that while semiclassical models like GRAZING have been successful for peripheral collisions, they substantially underestimate cross sections for damped collisions. The TDHF approach with PNP and evaporation corrections aims to provide a more microscopic framework for MNT reactions, particularly for producing neutron-rich nuclei around N=126 as discussed by Zagrebaev (2011) and Watanabe (2015).
