# Universal behavior of multiplicity differences in quark-hadron phase transition

> The scaling behavior of factorial moments of the differences in multiplicities between well separated bins in heavy-ion collisions is proposed as a probe of quark-hadron phase transition. The method takes into account some of the physical features of nuclear collisions that cause some difficulty in the application of the usual method. It is shown in the Ginzburg-Landau theory that a numerical value $γ$ of the scaling exponent can be determined independent of the parameters in the problem. The universality of $γ$ characterizes quark-hadron phase transition, and can be tested directly by appropriately analyzed data.

# Universal behavior of multiplicity differences in quark-hadron phase transition

## Key claim
The scaling exponent γ of factorial moments of multiplicity differences between well-separated bins in heavy-ion collisions is predicted to be approximately 1.09 ± 0.02 if a second-order quark-hadron phase transition occurs, independent of the Ginzburg-Landau parameters a, b, δ, and τ. This universality holds only under the assumption that the two bins are at large separation Δ where short-range correlations are negligible and the Ginzburg-Landau description with c = 0 and constant φ in each bin is valid.

## Method
The author introduces multiplicity difference correlators (MDC) as factorial moments of the distribution Q_m(δ) of absolute multiplicity differences m = |n₁ − n₂| between two bins of size δ at large separation Δ. The dynamical distribution D(s,δ) is taken as the Boltzmann factor exp(−F) from Ginzburg-Landau theory with free energy F = δ²(a|φ|² + b|φ|⁴), and the measured average multiplicity s = δ²τ|φ|² accounts for integration over the hadronization lifetime τ = 𝒯/t_h. The functional integral over the order parameter φ is evaluated numerically to obtain Q_m and the normalized factorial moments ℱ_q, whose scaling ℱ_q ∝ ℱ₂^{β_q} with β_q = (q−1)^γ is extracted from fits.

## Key numbers
- Scaling exponent for quark-hadron phase transition: γ = 1.09 ± 0.02, determined from calculations over the range 3 < τ < 30.
- Scaling exponent for the case without phase transition (uncorrelated bins): γ = 1.33 ± 0.02.
- For comparison, the previously known scaling exponent ν for single-bin factorial moments in Ginzburg-Landau theory is ν = 1.304.
- The exponent β_q satisfies β_q = (q−1)^γ with γ = 1.099 for the specific case τ = 10 shown in the fits.

## Context
This work extends the Ginzburg-Landau approach to factorial moments in phase transitions previously developed by Hwa and collaborators (1990s) for second-order transitions, where the scaling exponent ν was found for single-bin factorial moments. It addresses the experimental difficulty that heavy-ion data have not been analyzed for large multiplicity fluctuations because average multiplicities per bin are too high, as emphasized by Sarcevic and collaborators (1990s). The method introduces multiplicity differences inspired by Haar wavelet analysis, similar to approaches by Fang and Lipa (1990s) in astrophysics, and provides a testable prediction that can distinguish between the presence or absence of a quark-hadron phase transition in heavy-ion collisions at sufficiently high energy and nuclear size.
