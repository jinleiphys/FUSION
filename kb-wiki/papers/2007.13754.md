# Discovering partonic rescattering in light nucleus collisions

> We demonstrate that oxygen-oxygen (OO) collisions at the LHC provide unprecedented sensitivity to parton energy loss in a system whose size is comparable to those created in very peripheral heavy-ion collisions. With leading and next-to-leading order calculations of nuclear modification factors, we show that the baseline in the absence of partonic rescattering is known with up to 2% theoretical accuracy in inclusive OO collisions. Surprisingly, a $Z$-boson normalized nuclear modification factor does not lead to higher theoretical accuracy within current uncertainties of nuclear parton distribution functions. We study a broad range of parton energy loss models and we find that the expected signal of partonic rescattering can be disentangled from the baseline by measuring charged hadron spectra in the range $20\,\text{GeV}<p_T<100\,\text{GeV}$

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that inclusive (centrality-averaged) oxygen-oxygen (OO) collisions at the LHC provide unprecedented sensitivity to parton energy loss in small systems, with the baseline in the absence of partonic rescattering known to up to 2% theoretical accuracy, but only if the measurement uses the minimum-bias nuclear modification factor rather than centrality-dependent measurements that rely on soft-physics modeling.

## Method
The authors performed leading-order (LO) and next-to-leading-order (NLO) perturbative QCD calculations of inclusive jet and charged hadron cross sections in pp and OO collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 7 TeV using the NNLOJET framework with APPLfast interpolation tables. For hadron spectra, parton-level results were convoluted with Binnewies-Kniehl-Kramer (BKK) and Kniehl-Kramer-Potter (KKP) fragmentation functions using the INCNLO program. Nuclear parton distribution function (nPDF) uncertainties were evaluated using EPPS16 sets, with reweighting performed by including additional pPb dijet data from CMS. Parton energy loss model predictions were generated using a modular factorized QCD framework tuned to PbPb data at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV.

## Key numbers
- The baseline nuclear modification factor for OO collisions in the absence of parton energy loss is known with up to 2% theoretical accuracy at p_T = 50 GeV for jets, improving to 1% after reweighting nPDF uncertainties with CMS dijet data.
- nPDF uncertainties for the minimum-bias jet nuclear modification factor increase from approximately 2% at p_T = 50 GeV to approximately 7% for p_T > 200 GeV.
- The expected signal of partonic rescattering can be disentangled from the baseline by measuring charged hadron spectra in the range 20 GeV < p_T < 100 GeV.
- Statistical uncertainties for OO mock data were estimated for an integrated luminosity of L_AA = 0.5 nb^{-1}, corresponding to a few hours of stable beam.
- For Z-boson normalized measurements, an integrated luminosity of L_AA = 0.5 pb^{-1} would yield O(10^5) Z bosons, bringing statistical uncertainties below 1%.

## Context
This work extends the search for parton energy loss from large systems (PbPb, XeXe) and small systems (pPb, pp) where no sign of parton energy loss has been observed within current measurement uncertainties. The paper directly addresses the limitation that centrality-dependent nuclear modification factors in peripheral heavy-ion collisions suffer from 15% uncertainties in the nuclear overlap function T_AA, as documented by Khachatryan (2016) and Morsch (2017). The authors propose that inclusive OO collisions can probe the system size corresponding to highly peripheral PbPb and XeXe collisions (N_part ~ 10) while avoiding these soft-physics uncertainties. This enables testing the robust prediction from all parton energy loss models that some (possibly small) signal should exist in small collision systems, which is identified as one of the most important challenges of the future experimental heavy-ion program as outlined by Citron (2018) and Adolfsson (2020).
