# Unified ab initio approaches to nuclear structure and reactions

> The description of nuclei starting from the constituent nucleons and the realistic interactions among them has been a long-standing goal in nuclear physics. In addition to the complex nature of the nuclear forces, with two-, three- and possibly higher many-nucleon components, one faces the quantum-mechanical many-nucleon problem governed by an interplay between bound and continuum states. In recent years, significant progress has been made in ab initio nuclear structure and reaction calculations based on input from QCD-employing Hamiltonians constructed within chiral effective field theory. After a brief overview of the field, we focus on ab initio many-body approaches - built upon the No-Core Shell Model - that are capable of simultaneously describing both bound and scattering nuclear states, and present results for resonances in light nuclei, reactions important for astrophysics and fusion research. In particular, we review recent calculations of resonances in the $^6$He halo nucleus, of five- and six-nucleon scattering, and an investigation of the role of chiral three-nucleon interactions in the structure of $^9$Be. Further, we discuss applications to the $^7$Be$(p,γ)^8$B radiative capture. Finally, we highlight our efforts to describe transfer reactions including the $^3$H$(d,n)^4$He fusion.

# Unified ab initio approaches to nuclear structure and reactions

## Key claim
The No-Core Shell Model with Continuum (NCSMC) provides a unified ab initio framework capable of simultaneously describing bound and scattering states of light nuclei starting from chiral effective field theory Hamiltonians, but the accuracy of the method is limited by the truncation of the cluster basis (e.g., using only the ground state of ⁴He) which can miss about 1 MeV of binding energy compared to full NCSM results.

## Method
The authors combine the No-Core Shell Model (NCSM), which provides square-integrable eigenstates of the A-nucleon Hamiltonian expanded in harmonic oscillator basis states, with the Resonating-Group Method (RGM), which describes cluster motion with correct asymptotic boundary conditions. The NCSMC wave function is expanded as a superposition of NCSM eigenstates of the compound nucleus and continuous binary-cluster (or multi-cluster) channel states, with the unknown discrete and continuous variational amplitudes obtained by solving a set of coupled equations. The Hamiltonian is softened using similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolution of chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers
- ⁶He ground state energy from NCSM/RGM at Nmax=12: -28.697 MeV, compared to extrapolated NCSM value of -29.84(4) MeV and experimental value of -29.268 MeV
- ⁴He ground state energy from NCSM at Nmax=12: -28.224 MeV, with extrapolated value of -28.23(1) MeV and experimental value of -28.296 MeV
- The NCSM/RGM calculation of ⁶He used the chiral N³LO NN interaction (Entem 2003) with SRG evolution parameter Λ=1.5 fm⁻¹
- Two resonances found in the 2⁺ channel of ⁶He: the narrow 2⁺₁ and the broader 2⁺₂, plus additional resonances in 2⁻, 0⁻ and 1⁺ channels
- No resonance found in the 1⁻ channel of ⁶He

## Context
This work extends the ab initio NCSM approach (Navratil 2000, Barrett 2013) by coupling it with the Resonating-Group Method (Wildermuth 1977, Tang 1978) to include continuum degrees of freedom, building on earlier NCSM/RGM developments (Quaglioni 2008, 2009) and the more recent NCSMC formulation (Baroni 2013, 2013a). The method enables calculations of resonances in halo nuclei like ⁶He, reactions important for astrophysics such as ⁷Be(p,γ)⁸B radiative capture, and fusion reactions including ³H(d,n)⁴He. The results demonstrate that three-nucleon forces from chiral EFT are essential for realistic descriptions of p-shell nuclei, and the framework provides a path toward predictive ab initio calculations for nuclear astrophysics and fusion research.
