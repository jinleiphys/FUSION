# Fixing Two-Nucleon Weak-Axial Coupling L_{1,A} From mu-d Capture

> We calculate the muon capture rate on the deuteron to next-to-next-to-leading order in the pionless effective field theory. The result can be used to constrain the two-nucleon isovector axial coupling L_{1,A} to +/- 2 fm^3 if the muon capture rate is measured to 2% level. From this, one can determine the neutrino-deuteron break up reactions and the pp-fusion cross section in the sun to a same level of accuracy.

## Key claim

The muon capture rate on the deuteron, calculated to next-to-next-to-leading order (N²LO) in pionless effective field theory, can constrain the two-nucleon isovector axial coupling L_{1,A} to ±2 fm³ if the capture rate is measured to 2% precision, but this assumes the theoretical error from neglected higher-order terms is 2-3% and that a neutron energy cut can isolate low-energy events.

## Method

The authors performed a N²LO calculation of the μ⁻d → ν_μ + n + n capture rate using pionless effective field theory (EFT(π̸)) in its dibaryon formulation. They computed the differential and integrated capture rates as functions of the two-neutron relative energy E_nn, including one-nucleon vector and axial currents up to order p²/M_N² and two-nucleon currents at leading and next-to-leading order. The dependence of the integrated rate on L_{1,A} was expressed as Γ(E_nn^th) = a(E_nn^th) + b(E_nn^th)L_{1,A}, with coefficients tabulated for various energy cuts.

## Key numbers

- The integrated capture rate for E_nn^th = 10.0 MeV is Γ = a + b·L_{1,A} with a = 308.0 s⁻¹ and b = 4.2 s⁻¹·fm⁻³.
- For E_nn^th = 15.0 MeV: a = 332.0 s⁻¹, b = 4.7 s⁻¹·fm⁻³.
- For E_nn^th = 20.0 MeV: a = 342.3 s⁻¹, b = 4.9 s⁻¹·fm⁻³.
- The unpolarized capture rate expansion for L_{1,A} = 6 fm³ is Γ_unpol = Γ_unpol^LO (1 + 5.3% + 4.9%), showing NLO and N²LO corrections.
- For L_{1,A} = -6 fm³, the expansion is Γ_unpol = Γ_unpol^LO (1 + 10.9% + 5.2%).
- The N²LO contribution to the polarized (F=1/2) rate is less than 1% of the LO contribution, while NLO is less than 10%.
- Previous experimental results for the total capture rate are Γ^exp = 470 ± 29 s⁻¹ and Γ^exp = 409 ± 40 s⁻¹.
- The hybrid EFT calculation by Ando gave Γ = 386 ± 5 s⁻¹.

## Context

This work extends the program of determining the two-nucleon axial coupling L_{1,A} from weak processes, following earlier attempts by Butler, Chen, and Vogel (2001) using reactor antineutrino-deuteron breakup (L_{1,A}=3.6±5.5 fm³), by Chen, Heeger, and Robertson using SNO data (L_{1,A}=4.0±6.3 fm³), and by Park et al. using tritium beta decay (yielding L_{1,A}=4.2±2.5 fm³). The calculation enables a future high-precision determination of L_{1,A} from the μ⁻d capture experiment under investigation at PSI, which in turn would allow predictions of neutrino-deuteron breakup cross sections and the pp-fusion rate in the sun to 2-3% accuracy.
