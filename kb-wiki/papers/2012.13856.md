# Fun with colours: the standard model with two colour QCD has radically different long distance physics

> In our world the standard model of particle physics contains within it the fairly intractable theory called QCD. A toy version with two colours is often studied as a model confining and chiral symmetry breaking field theory. Here we investigate the cascade of changes at various distance scales if we make this change within the standard model. It is possible to limit the changes at the hadronic scale. However, the minor changes that occur actually cascade down to the far infrared, into nuclear and atomic physics, and chemistry. Through this it also possibly affects the evolution of stars and galaxies. We remark on this unexpected sensitivity of the universe to physics at the scale of quarks.

# Wiki Page: Standard Model with Two-Colour QCD

## Key claim
The authors argue that changing the number of colours in QCD from N_c=3 to N_c=2, while preserving baryon number conservation and keeping the electroweak sector unchanged, produces a cascade of changes that propagate from the hadronic scale down to nuclear, atomic, and cosmological physics. The hedge is that these changes can be limited at the hadronic scale only if one imposes baryon number conservation explicitly via a "baryomagnetic field" term, and even then the long-distance physics is radically different.

## Method
The authors construct a minimal modification of the Standard Model by replacing SU(3) colour with SU(2) colour while keeping the electroweak sector unchanged, imposing baryon number conservation through a symmetry-breaking term (the baryomagnetic field) in the Lagrangian. They then analyse the consequences at each distance scale: quark charges from anomaly cancellation, hadron spectroscopy from chiral symmetry breaking patterns, nuclear physics from the resulting bosonic nucleon with fractional charge, and atomic and cosmological physics from the altered periodic table and recombination history.

## Key numbers
- Quark charges for N_c=2: Q_u = 3/4 and Q_d = -1/4 (from anomaly cancellation equations).
- Baryon charge formula: for a baryon with n u-quarks, charge = n - (N_c - 1)/2, which is integer only for odd N_c.
- For N_f=2, the number of Goldstone bosons with Pauli-Gürsey symmetry is (N_f - 1)(2N_f + 1) = 3; without Pauli-Gürsey symmetry (baryon number conserved), the number is N_f^2 - 1 = 3 pseudoscalar mesons.
- For N_f=3, the scalar baryons form a \overline{3} representation (charges 1/2, 1/2, -1/2) and vector baryons form a sextet.
- The amplitude for π → 2γ contains the coefficient N_c(Q_u^2 - Q_d^2)/f_π, which goes as 1/√N_c.
- The Wess-Zumino-Witten amplitude for K^+K^- → π^+π^-π^0 is proportional to 1/N_c^{3/2}.
- The Rydberg constant for the ^2H atom (Z=1, B=2) is the same as in our world; for the exotic ^1H^- atom (half-integer charge), energy levels are at half the value of ^2H.

## Context
This work extends the long tradition of studying two-colour QCD as a toy model for lattice simulations (Chiral, 1980s) and finite-density studies, but cautions against assuming its long-distance physics mirrors our world. The authors build on the Pauli-Gürsey symmetry analysis (Stern, 1990s) and the large-N_c expansion (Witten, 1979) to show that even with baryon number conservation imposed, the bosonic nature and fractional charge of baryons produce qualitatively different nuclear, atomic, and cosmological physics. The paper enables future lattice studies of the phase diagram of two-colour QCD with the baryomagnetic term, and raises questions about the validity of decoupling theorems in quantum field theory when global symmetries and field statistics are changed.
