# Charge radius of the short-lived $^{68}$Ni and correlation with the dipole polarizability

> We present the first laser spectroscopic measurement of the neutron-rich nucleus $^{68}$Ni at the \mbox{$N=40$} subshell closure and extract its nuclear charge radius. Since this is the only short-lived isotope for which the dipole polarizability $α_{\rm D}$ has been measured, the combination of these observables provides a benchmark for nuclear structure theory. We compare them to novel coupled-cluster calculations based on different chiral two- and three-nucleon interactions, for which a strong correlation between the charge radius and dipole polarizability is observed, similar to the stable nucleus $^{48}$Ca. Three-particle--three-hole correlations in coupled-cluster theory substantially improve the description of the experimental data, which allows to constrain the neutron radius and neutron skin of $^{68}$Ni.

## Key claim

The first laser spectroscopic measurement of the neutron-rich nucleus 68Ni at the N=40 subshell closure yields a charge radius that, when combined with the previously measured dipole polarizability, provides a benchmark for coupled-cluster calculations including three-particle-three-hole correlations; the agreement between theory and experiment depends critically on including these triples corrections, which were not sufficient in earlier calculations for 48Ca.

## Method

Collinear laser spectroscopy was performed at ISOLDE/CERN on 68Ni produced by proton-induced fragmentation, spallation, and fission in a uranium carbide target, followed by resonant laser ionization and mass separation. Isotope shifts relative to 60Ni were measured on the 352.45 nm transition from the metastable 3d9 4s 3D3 level to the 3d9 4p 3P2 level, and a King-plot analysis using known charge radii of stable nickel isotopes from muonic atom and electron scattering data extracted the mean-square charge radius difference. Coupled-cluster calculations with singles, doubles, and leading three-particle-three-hole correlations were performed for five different chiral nucleon-nucleon and three-nucleon interactions.

## Key numbers

* Charge radius of 68Ni: Rc = 3.887(3) fm, extracted from the measured isotope shift of 1992.3(27)(147) MHz relative to 60Ni and the known radius of 60Ni (3.806(2) fm from Fricke (2004)).
* Dipole polarizability of 68Ni: alpha_D = 3.88(31) fm^3, from Rossi et al. (2013) refined by Roca-Maza et al. (2015).
* Coupled-cluster results including triples for the 2.0/2.0 (EM) interaction: alpha_D = 3.83(23) fm^3, Rc = 3.77(1) fm, neutron skin = 0.202(3) fm.
* Coupled-cluster results including triples for the NNLO_sat interaction: alpha_D = 4.65(49) fm^3, Rc = 4.00(4) fm, neutron skin = 0.183(8) fm.
* Predicted range for neutron skin of 68Ni from the best interactions: R_skin = 0.18-0.20 fm, and point-neutron radius Rn = 3.9-4.1 fm.

## Context

This work extends the correlation between charge radius and dipole polarizability previously observed for the stable nucleus 48Ca by Hagen et al. (2015) and measured by Birkhan et al. (2017) to the neutron-rich, short-lived nucleus 68Ni, the only unstable nucleus for which the dipole polarizability has been measured. The coupled-cluster calculations including three-particle-three-hole correlations, following the approach of Miorelli et al. (2018) and Simonis et al., improve agreement with experiment compared to singles-and-doubles calculations and allow constraints on the neutron skin that are consistent with the energy-density-functional predictions of Roca-Maza et al. (2015). This enables first-principles tests of the symmetry energy and neutron matter equation of state in a medium-mass neutron-rich system.
