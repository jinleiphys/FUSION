# Pairing gaps and Fermi energies at scission for 296Lv alpha-decay

> The pairing corrections, the single particle occupation numbers, are investigated within density-dependent delta interaction formalism for pairing residual interactions. The potential barrier is computed in the framework of the macroscopic-microscopic model. The microscopic part is based on the Woods-Saxon two center shell model. The alpha-decay of a superheavy element is treated, by paying a special attention to the region of the scission configurations. The sequence of nuclear shapes follows the superasymmetric fission path for alpha decay. It was found that the pairing gaps of the states that reach asymptotically the potential well of the alpha particle have large values at scission but become zero after scission. The 1s1/2 single particle levels of the nascent alpha particle are fully occupied while the superior levels are empties in the scission region and remains in the same states during the penetration of the Coulomb barrier. The projection of the numbers of particle on the two fragments are obtained naturally. At scission, the nascent alpha particle forms a very bound cluster.

## Key claim

The pairing gaps of single-particle states that will become the alpha particle reach large values (up to ~3.5 MeV) at the scission configuration, suppressing quasiparticle excitations and forming a strongly bound cluster, but these gaps drop to zero after scission when the wave-function overlaps between fragments vanish.

## Method

The alpha decay of 296Lv was treated as a superasymmetric fission process using the macroscopic-microscopic model with a Woods-Saxon two-center shell model for the microscopic part. Pairing corrections were computed using the density-dependent delta interaction (DDDI) formalism, which provides state-dependent pairing interaction strengths, and compared with the constant pairing strength G approximation. The nuclear shape parametrization used two spheroids joined by a neck, with the scission configuration occurring at an elongation R ≈ 10 fm.

## Key numbers

- The theoretical pairing gaps for the ground state of 296Lv are Δp = 0.66 MeV and Δn = 0.56 MeV, taken from theoretical tables (Moller).
- The DDDI interaction strengths for 296Lv were estimated as -1448 MeV fm⁻³ for neutrons and -1528 MeV fm⁻³ for protons.
- The pairing gap associated with the neutron 1s₁/₂ alpha-state increases to ~3.5 MeV at an elongation R ≈ 14 fm, then drops to zero.
- The proton 1s₁/₂ single-particle state gap reaches zero at an elongation close to 19 fm.
- The scission configuration occurs at an elongation R ≈ 10 fm.
- The average pairing gaps of the whole system never exceed 1 MeV for both protons and neutrons.

## Context

This work extends the superasymmetric fission treatment of alpha decay previously applied to 296Lv by Mirea (2013, EPL), using the DDDI formalism to investigate scission properties. It addresses the problem raised by Pomorski (2003) regarding double counting of average pairing energy at scission and the need for deformation-dependent pairing strength. The results support the equivalence between alpha-particle preformation and barrier penetrability discussed by Poenaru (1984) and Zdeb (2013), and provide a mechanism for natural projection of particle numbers onto fragments, which is essential for dynamical fission calculations as noted by Younes (2009).
