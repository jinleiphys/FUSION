# From Low-Density Neutron Matter to the Unitary Limit

> Various quantities of an attractively interacting fermion system at the unitary limit are determined by extrapolating Monte Carlo results of low-density neutron matter. Smooth extrapolation in terms of $1/(k_F a_0)$ ($k_F$ is the Fermi momentum, and $a_0$ is the ${}^1S_0$ scattering length) is found with the quantities examined: the ground-state energy, the pairing gap at $T \approx 0$, and the critical temperature of the normal-to-superfluid phase transition. We emphasize proximity of the physics of low-density neutron matter to that at the unitary limit. The extrapolated quantities are in a reasonable agreement with those in the literature.

## Key claim

The authors extrapolate Monte Carlo results for low-density neutron matter (with densities of about \(10^{-4}\) to \(10^{-2}\) of nuclear matter density) to the unitary limit of an attractively interacting fermion system, finding that the extrapolated ground-state energy, pairing gap, and critical temperature are in reasonable agreement with literature values, but they note that no accurate determination of these quantities is yet available.

## Method

The authors used determinantal quantum Monte Carlo on cubic lattices of sizes \(4^3\), \(6^3\), \(8^3\), and \(10^3\) to simulate low-density neutron matter described by a leading-order effective field theory Hamiltonian that reduces to the attractive Hubbard model. They first applied finite-size scaling to obtain thermodynamic-limit values of the energy parameter \(\xi\), the pairing gap \(\Delta\), and the critical temperature \(T_c\) at three values of \(\eta \equiv 1/(k_F a_0)\) (where \(k_F\) is the Fermi momentum and \(a_0\) is the \(^1S_0\) scattering length), then took the continuum limit by extrapolating in the lattice filling \(n\). Finally, they extrapolated these three quantities as quadratic functions of \(\eta\) to the unitary limit \(\eta = 0\).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy parameter \(\xi = E_{g.s.}/E_{FG}\) at the unitary limit: \(\xi = 0.292(24)\).
- Pairing gap at \(T \approx 0\) at the unitary limit: \(\Delta/\epsilon_F = 0.384(30)\).
- Ratio of pairing gap to ground-state energy at the unitary limit: \(\Delta/E_{g.s.} = 2.19(35)\).
- Critical temperature of the normal-to-superfluid phase transition at the unitary limit: \(T_c/\epsilon_F = 0.189(12)\).
- The three neutron-matter densities used correspond to \(k_F = 15\), 30, and 60 MeV, with \(\eta\) values of \(-0.7997\), \(-0.3999\), and \(-0.1999\), respectively.
- The \(^1S_0\) neutron-neutron scattering length is \(a_0 \approx -16.45\) fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Monte Carlo study of low-density neutron matter (Abe and Seki, 2007) by exploiting the proximity of that system to the unitary limit, where the scattering length diverges and the system exhibits universal behavior. The extrapolation method was originally proposed by Chen and Kaplan (2004). The results are compared with several prior calculations: the \(\epsilon\)-expansion calculations of Chen et al. (2006) and Nishida and Son (2006, 2008), lattice Monte Carlo calculations with the symmetric heavy-light ansatz by Lee (2007), fixed-node Green's function Monte Carlo calculations by Carlson et al. (2003, 2005) and Gezerlis and Carlson (2008), and determinantal quantum Monte Carlo calculations by Bulgac et al. (2008). The work enables a connection between neutron-matter physics and the broader field of ultracold atomic Fermi gases at unitarity, suggesting that low-density neutron matter (densities of about \(10^{-4}\) to \(10^{-2}\) of nuclear matter density) shares much of its physics with the unitary limit.
