# The topological long range order in QCD. Applications to heavy ion collisions and cosmology

> We argue that the local violation of P invariance in heavy ion collisions is a consequence of the long range topological order which is inherent feature of strongly coupled QCD. A similar phenomenon is known to occur in some topologically ordered condensed matter systems with a gap. We also discuss possible cosmological applications of this long range order in strongly coupled gauge theories. In particular, we argue that the de Sitter behaviour might be dynamically generated as a result of the long range order. In this framework the inflaton is an auxiliary field which effectively describes the dynamics of topological sectors in a gauge theory in the expanding universe, rather than a new dynamical degree of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the local violation of P and CP invariance observed in heavy ion collisions arises from a long-range topological order inherent to strongly coupled QCD, rather than from conventional short-range fluctuations. This long-range order is claimed to be a consequence of a non-dispersive contact term in the topological susceptibility that cannot be expressed in terms of any physical propagating degrees of freedom.

## Method

The author analyzes the topological susceptibility in QCD and its decomposition into dispersive (from physical massive states) and non-dispersive (contact term) contributions, following the standard resolution of the U(1)_A problem. The analysis is extended using a weakly coupled "deformed QCD" model that preserves confinement and topological sectors, where semiclassical computations of the vacuum energy and topological susceptibility are performed in terms of fractionally charged monopoles representing tunnelling events between topological sectors. The author then applies this framework to heavy ion collisions and cosmology by considering how the non-dispersive vacuum energy responds to time-dependent backgrounds characterized by system size R or Hubble parameter H.

## Key numbers

- The topological susceptibility in the deformed QCD model is given by χ_YM = (ζ/(NL)) ∫ d³x [δ(x)], where N is the number of colours, ζ is the monopole fugacity, and L is the size of compact dimension in Euclidean time
- The vacuum energy in the deformed QCD model is E_vac(θ) = -(Nζ/L) cos(θ/N) for |θ| ≤ π
- The estimated asymmetry in heavy ion collisions is ∼ (e/N_c²) · (f/(R Λ_QCD)) ∼ 10⁻³, where e ∼ 10⁻¹ is the electric charge, 1/N_c² ∼ 10⁻¹ is a suppression factor, and (R Λ_QCD)⁻¹ ∼ 10⁻¹ with R ∼ 10 fm
- The predicted Hubble constant from the cosmological application is H₀ ∼ Λ_QCD³/(3M²_PL) ∼ 10⁻³³ eV, with dark energy density ρ_DE ∼ H₀ Λ_QCD³ ∼ [(10⁻²–10⁻³) eV]⁴

## Context

This work extends the framework of the chiral magnetic effect (CME) originally proposed by Kharzeev (2007) and developed by Fukushima (2008), which assumes a coherent region with non-zero θ_ind parameter much larger than Λ_QCD⁻¹. The author argues that previous studies did not properly address the origin of this long-range structure, and presents a mechanism based on the non-dispersive contact term in topological susceptibility, following the author's own prior work (Zhitnitsky 2012, 2013, 2014). The cosmological application suggests that the de Sitter phase may emerge dynamically from strongly coupled gauge theories, potentially providing an alternative to conventional inflationary models where the inflaton is a new dynamical degree of freedom.
