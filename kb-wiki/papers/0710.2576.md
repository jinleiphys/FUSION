# Transmission resonances for a Dirac particle in a one-dimensional Hulthén potential

> We have solved exactly the two-component Dirac equation in the presence of a spatially one-dimensional Hulthén potential, and presented the Dirac spinors of scattering states in terms of hypergeometric functions. We have calculated the reflection and transmission coefficients by the matching conditions on the wavefunctions, and investigated the condition for the existence of transmission resonances. Furthermore, we have demonstrated how the transmission-resonance condition depends on the shape of the potential.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that transmission resonances exist for a Dirac particle in a one-dimensional Hulthén potential, with the resonance condition depending on the shape parameters of the potential (diffuseness \(a\) and the parameter \(q\)), but the analysis is limited to the case where the potential parameter \(q\) satisfies \(0<q<1\) to avoid divergence.

## Method
The two-component Dirac equation in 1+1 dimensions was solved exactly for the Hulthén potential by transforming it into hypergeometric differential equations via variable substitutions (\(y=qe^{ax}\) for \(x<0\) and \(z=qe^{-ax}\) for \(x>0\)). The scattering wavefunctions were expressed in terms of hypergeometric functions, and the reflection and transmission coefficients were obtained by matching the wavefunctions and their first derivatives at \(x=0\). The transmission coefficient was then computed numerically as a function of energy \(E\) and potential strength \(V_0\) for various values of the parameters \(a\) and \(q\).

## Key numbers
- The potential is defined as \(V(x)=\Theta(-x)\frac{V_0}{e^{-ax}-q}+\Theta(x)\frac{V_0}{e^{ax}-q}\) with parameters \(V_0\), \(a\), and \(q\) all real and positive, and \(0<q<1\).
- The wave number for scattering states is \(k=\sqrt{E^2-m^2}\), with \(|E|>m\) ensuring \(k\) is real.
- The transmission coefficient \(T\) is given by \(T=|D|^2/|A|^2\), and the reflection coefficient \(R\) by \(R=|B|^2/|A|^2 \cdot (E+k)/(E-k)\), satisfying \(R+T=1\).
- The width of the transmission resonance decreases as the diffuseness parameter \(a\) decreases (e.g., comparing Fig.1 with Fig.2, and Fig.3 with Fig.4).
- As the parameter \(q\) decreases, the height of the potential barrier increases and the width of the transmission resonance increases (e.g., comparing Fig.1 with Fig.3, and Fig.2 with Fig.4).

## Context
This work extends the study of transmission resonances in relativistic quantum mechanics from previously analyzed potentials such as the square potential and Gaussian potential (Dombey, 2000), the Woods-Saxon potential (Kennedy, 2002; Rojas, 2005), and the cusp potential (Villalba, 2003; Villalba, 2007) to the Hulthén potential, which is widely used in nuclear and particle physics. The results show that the transmission resonances in the Hulthén potential exhibit the same rich structure as those observed in the Woods-Saxon potential, with the resonance width depending on the diffuseness parameter \(a\) and the parameter \(q\). This enables further investigation of transmission resonances in other realistic potentials relevant to nuclear physics.
