# Generalised Parton Distributions: A Dyson-Schwinger approach for the pion

> We compute the pion quark Generalised Parton Distribution H and quark Double Distributions in a coupled Bethe-Salpeter and Dyson-Schwinger approach in terms of quark flavors or isospin states. We use analytic expressions inspired by the numerical resolution of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. We obtain an analytic expression for the pion Generalised Parton Distribution at a low scale. Our model fulfils most of the required symmetry properties and compares very well to experimental pion form factor or valence parton distribution function experimental data. In addition, we have highlighted limitations of the so-called impulse approximation, which breaks symmetries when computing the valence parton distribution function. Doing so, we introduced new terms which were neglected before. Finally, we also shed light on a specific property of the pion GPD: Polyakov soft pion theorem.

## Key claim

The paper presents an analytic model for the pion quark Generalised Parton Distribution (GPD) derived from algebraic forms that mimic the numerical solutions of the coupled Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, but the model does not automatically satisfy the Polyakov soft pion theorem unless the Axial-Vector Ward-Takashi identity (AVWTI) is consistently enforced.

## Method

The pion GPD \(H^q(x,\xi,t)\) is computed via its Mellin moments using a triangle diagram approximation with dressed quark propagators and Bethe-Salpeter vertices taken in algebraic forms that fit numerical solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. The GPD is then reconstructed from its Mellin moments using the Double Distribution (DD) formalism via a Radon transform. Additional contributions beyond the triangle approximation are introduced to restore the \(x \leftrightarrow 1-x\) symmetry of the forward parton distribution function (PDF).

## Key numbers

- The algebraic model uses a single free parameter \(M\) (the dressed quark mass) with the parameter \(\nu\) fixed to 1, which gives back the asymptotic pion Distribution Amplitude (DA).
- The triangle-approximation PDF is given by \(q_\pi^{\textrm{Tr}}(x) = \frac{72}{25} \Big( ( 30 - 15 x + 8 x^2 - 2 x^3 ) x^3 \log x + ( 3 + 2 x^2 ) ( 1 - x )^4 \log( 1 - x ) + ( 3 + 15 x + 5 x^2 - 2 x^3 ) x ( 1 - x ) \Big)\).
- The additional PDF component is \(q_\pi^{\textrm{ad}}(x) = \frac{72}{25} \Bigg(-\left(2 x^3+4 x+9\right) (x-1)^3 \log (1-x) + x^3 (2 x ((x-3) x+5)-15) \log (x) - x (x-1)(2x-1) ((x-1) x-9) \Bigg)\).
- The total PDF summing both contributions is \(q_\pi^{\textrm{tot}}(x) = \frac{72}{25} \Bigg(x^3 (x (2 x-5)+15) \log (x)-\left(2 x^2+x+12\right) (x-1)^3 \log (1-x) -2 x (x-1)((x-1) x+6)\Bigg)\), which fulfils the \(x \leftrightarrow 1-x\) symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter approach to GPDs pioneered by Chang (2013) and Mezrag (2014), providing an analytic model that can be checked against experimental data for the pion form factor (Amendolia 1986, Huber 2008) and valence PDF (Aicher 2010). The model enables future studies of pion GPDs that must incorporate the AVWTI and the additional diagrammatic contributions beyond the impulse approximation to satisfy all theoretical constraints, including the Polyakov soft pion theorem (Polyakov 1998).
