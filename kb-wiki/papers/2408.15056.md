# Speed of sound bounds and first-order phase transitions in compact stars

> In the present study, we employ three distinct, physically motivated speed of sound bounds to construct hybrid models, where the high-density phase is described by the maximally stiff equation of state. In particular, we consider the bounds related to special relativity, relativistic kinetic theory and conformality. The low-density hadronic phase is described by a state-of-the-art microscopic relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory. This work aims to access the effect of the different speed of sound constraints on the relevant parameter space of the key parameters of first-order phase transitions by utilizing recent astronomical data. This involves a systematic analysis that also includes two distinct schemes for the construction of hybrid models (abrupt and smooth). Finally, a relevant discussion is conducted on the possible occurrence of a thermodynamic inconsistency that is related to the stability of the high-density phase over hadronic matter at large densities.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that simultaneous reconciliation of the HESS J1731-347 and PSR J0030+0451 mass-radius constraints in hybrid star models is only possible under the special relativity and relativistic kinetic theory speed of sound bounds, while the conformal bound (c_s ≤ c/√3) is incompatible with the existence of 2 solar mass pulsars when a nonzero energy density jump is present.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state (EOS) where the low-density hadronic phase is described by relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory with the Bonn A potential, and the high-density phase is described by a maximally stiff EOS under three different speed of sound bounds: special relativity (c_s ≤ c), relativistic kinetic theory (c_s ≤ [(ε-P/3)/(ε+P)]^(1/2)), and the conformal limit (c_s ≤ c/√3). They employ both abrupt construction (Maxwell construction) and smooth construction (polytropic crossover with varying polytropic index Γ_m) to model the phase transition, then solve the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations to obtain stellar configurations and compare with observational constraints.

## Key numbers

- PSR J0030+0451 mass and radius: M = 1.34^(+0.15)_(-0.16) M_⊙ and R = 12.71^(+1.14)_(-1.19) km (1σ) from Riley et al. (2019)
- HESS J1731-347 mass and radius: M = 0.77^(+0.20)_(-0.17) M_⊙ and R = 10.4^(+0.86)_(-0.78) km (1σ) from Doroshenko et al. (2022)
- For abrupt construction with special relativity or kinetic theory bounds, the transition density is constrained below ~0.28 fm^(-3) and the energy density jump varies in the range ~80-170 MeV fm^(-3)
- Transition density and energy density jump ranges explored: n_tr in [1.5n_0, 3.5n_0] and Δε in [0, 300] MeV fm^(-3) (with n_0 the nuclear saturation density)
- For the conformal bound, maximum considered transition density is ~2.5n_0 (beyond which hadronic speed of sound exceeds the conformal limit)
- Smooth construction with Γ_m = 1.03 results in a maximum mass increase of less than ~0.1 M_⊙ compared to abrupt construction

## Context

This work extends the study of hybrid stars with first-order phase transitions by incorporating three distinct speed of sound bounds, building on prior work by Bedaque and Steiner (2015) on the conformal limit, Olson (2000) on relativistic kinetic theory constraints, and Lattimer (2014) on causality bounds. The analysis enables constraints on the transition density and energy density jump parameters for first-order phase transitions in neutron stars, and demonstrates that the conformal bound is strongly disfavored by the existence of 2 M_⊙ pulsars when a nonzero energy density jump is present, consistent with earlier findings by Tews et al. (2018), Altiparmak et al. (2022), Ecker and Brandes (2022), and Brandes et al. (2024). The results also show that the polytropic index of the crossover region affects the maximum transition density compatible with observations, potentially providing information on the maximal stiffness of the mixed phase.
