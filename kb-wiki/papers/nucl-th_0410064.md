# Configuration mixing in $^{188}$Pb : band structure and electromagnetic properties

> In the present paper, we carry out a detailed analysis of the presence and mixing of various families of collective bands in $^{188}$Pb. Making use of the interacting boson model, we construct a particular intermediate basis that can be associated with the unperturbed bands used in more phenomenological studies. We use the E2 decay to construct a set of collective bands and discuss in detail the B(E2)-values. We also perform an analysis of these theoretical results (Q, B(E2)) to deduce an intrinsic quadrupole moment and the associated quadrupole deformation parameter, using an axially deformed rotor model.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that in $^{188}$Pb, the low-lying $0^+$ and $2^+$ states are so strongly mixed between 2p-2h and 4p-4h configurations that assigning a simple "configuration label" (e.g., '2p-2h' or '4p-4h') to them leads to inconsistencies; instead, the only physically meaningful way to characterise these states is by constructing collective bands using the intensities of E2-transition sequences.

## Method

Using the interacting boson model (IBM) with a three-configuration mixing calculation (0p-0h, 2p-2h, and 4p-4h proton excitations across the $Z=82$ shell gap), the authors first diagonalise the Hamiltonian separately in each subspace to obtain unperturbed bands. They then rotate into an intermediate basis to study mixing matrix elements, and use the calculated E2 decay probabilities starting from high-spin states to define "physical" collective bands, requiring intra-band B(E2) values to be larger than inter-band values. Finally, they extract intrinsic quadrupole moments and deformation parameters from the IBM results using an axially deformed rotor model.

## Key numbers

- Mixing matrix elements between the first three unperturbed $0^+$ states: $0_1^+$-$0_2^+$ = -0.0768 MeV, $0_2^+$-$0_3^+$ = 0.1908 MeV; for the first three $2^+$ states: $2_1^+$-$2_2^+$ = 0.0711 MeV, $2_2^+$-$2_3^+$ = 0.1349 MeV
- IBM parameters for $^{188}$Pb: $\alpha_1 = \beta_1 = 8.5$ keV, $\alpha_2 = \beta_2 = 23.4$ keV, $\Delta_1 = 1923$ keV
- Calculated B(E2; $2_1^+ \rightarrow 0_1^+$) = 0.0195 $e^2b^2$ (3 W.u.), compared to experimental value 5(3) W.u.
- Calculated B(E2; $4_1^+ \rightarrow 2_1^+$) = 0.9747 $e^2b^2$ (152 W.u.), compared to experimental value 160(80) W.u.
- Intrinsic quadrupole moment for the unperturbed 2p-2h (oblate) band: -3.4 to -3.7 $e\cdot b$; for the unperturbed 4p-4h (prolate) band: 5.6 to 6.6 $e\cdot b$
- Deformation parameter $\beta_0$ for the unperturbed 2p-2h band: -0.11 to -0.12; for the unperturbed 4p-4h band: 0.19 to 0.22

## Context

This work extends the three-configuration mixing IBM calculation of Fossion et al. (2003) for neutron-deficient Pb isotopes by introducing an intermediate basis that makes the mixing between unperturbed bands transparent, and by using E2 decay to define collective bands rather than relying on configuration labels. The results are compared with phenomenological band-mixing studies by Dracoulis et al. (2004), Allatt et al. (1998), and Page et al. (2003), and with mean-field calculations by Bender et al. (2004). The method enables future systematic studies of mixing patterns across the Pb isotope chain near neutron mid-shell (N=104) and detailed analysis of electromagnetic properties including E0 transitions and isotopic shifts.
