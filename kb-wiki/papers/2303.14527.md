# Importance of higher orders in opacity in QGP tomography

> We consider the problem of including a finite number of scattering centers in dynamical energy loss and classical DGLV formalism. Previously, either one or an infinite number of scattering centers were considered in energy loss calculations, while attempts to relax such approximations were largely inconclusive or incomplete. In reality, however, the number of scattering centers is generally estimated to be 4-5 at RHIC and the LHC, making the above approximations (a priori) inadequate and this theoretical problem significant for QGP tomography. We derived explicit analytical expressions for dynamical energy loss and DGLV up to the $4^{th}$ order in opacity, resulting in complex mathematical expressions that were, to our knowledge, obtained for the first time. These expressions were then implemented into an appropriately generalized DREENA framework to calculate the effects of higher orders in opacity on a wide range of high-$p_\perp$ light and heavy flavor predictions. Results of extensive numerical analysis, together with interpretations of nonintuitive results, are presented. We find that, for both RHIC and the LHC, higher-order effects on high-$p_\perp$ observables are small, and the approximation of a single scattering center is adequate for dynamical energy loss and DGLV formalisms.

## Key claim

The authors find that for both RHIC and the LHC, higher-order effects (up to 3rd order in opacity) on high-$p_\perp$ observables are small, and the approximation of a single scattering center is adequate for dynamical energy loss and DGLV formalisms, but this conclusion depends on the value of magnetic screening: for $\mu_M/\mu_E=0.6$ higher orders reduce suppression, while for $\mu_M/\mu_E=0.4$ they increase it.

## Method

The authors derived explicit analytical expressions for dynamical energy loss and DGLV up to the 4th order in opacity, starting from a closed-form expression for static medium (Wicks 2007) and generalizing it to a dynamical QCD medium by replacing the static effective potential and mean free path with dynamical ones. These expressions were implemented into an appropriately generalized DREENA-C framework (which assumes a constant temperature medium) to calculate the effects on high-$p_\perp$ $R_{AA}$ and $v_2$ observables for light and heavy flavor hadrons in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=5.02$ TeV.

## Key numbers

* The number of scattering centers in QGP at RHIC and the LHC is generally estimated to be 4-5.
* For the 1st order in opacity, numerical integration required approximately 25 CPU hours; for the 2nd order, approximately 2500 CPU hours; for the 3rd order, approximately 70000 CPU hours.
* For static medium approximation, higher-order effects on $R_{AA}$ are less than 6%.
* For dynamical energy loss with $\mu_M/\mu_E$ in the range 0.58-0.64 (from lattice QCD, Borsanyi 2015), higher-order effects on $R_{AA}$ are less than 5%.
* The effect of higher orders on D meson $R_{AA}$ was tested at temperatures $T=200$, $400$, and $600$ MeV and found to be largely independent of temperature, affecting radiative $R_{AA}$ for less than 5%.

## Context

This work extends the dynamical energy loss formalism (Djordjevic 2009, Djordjevic and Heinz 2008) and the DGLV formalism (Gyulassy, Levai, Vitev 2000, Djordjevic and Gyulassy 2004) beyond the single-scattering approximation, addressing a problem previously tackled with partial or incomplete treatments (Andres 2020, Mehtar-Tani 2020, Sievert 2019, Wicks 2007). The results enable QGP tomography calculations to safely use the single-scattering approximation for both static and dynamical medium models, given current lattice QCD constraints on magnetic screening (Borsanyi 2015).
