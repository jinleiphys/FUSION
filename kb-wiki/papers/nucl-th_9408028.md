# The Axial Charge Renormalization in a Relativistic Description of Finite Nuclei

> Starting from a realistic One-Boson-Exchange model of the nucleon nucleon interaction the relativistic mean field for nucleons is determined within the Dirac Brueckner Hartree Fock approach for finite nuclei. The matrix elements of the axial charge operator evaluated for the solutions of the Dirac equation with this selfenergy are investigated. These matrix elements are enhanced with respect to the equivalent non relativistic ones obtained from the solutions of the Schrödinger equation with the non relativistic equivalent potential. The present results confirm at a qualitative level the results for the axial charge renormalization obtained with perturbative approaches. However, the results obtained differ in size from those of the perturbative approach and are nucleus and state dependent.

## Key claim

The relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) treatment of finite nuclei yields an enhancement of axial charge matrix elements relative to non-relativistic calculations, confirming the qualitative direction of earlier perturbative approaches, but the enhancement factors are significantly smaller (1.2–1.3) than nuclear-matter estimates (1.4–1.7) and are nucleus- and state-dependent.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) calculations for finite nuclei using the relativistic One-Boson-Exchange potential (version A of the Bonn potential). They solved the Dirac equation with the resulting local scalar and vector selfenergies to obtain relativistic single-particle wavefunctions for the nuclei ¹⁶O and ⁴⁰Ca, and separately solved the Schrödinger equation with the equivalent non-relativistic potential (Schrödinger-equivalent potential, SEP) to obtain non-relativistic wavefunctions. Matrix elements of the axial charge operator (γ⁰γ₅) were then evaluated for 0⁺ ↔ 0⁻ first-forbidden β-decay transitions using both sets of wavefunctions, and the ratios of relativistic to non-relativistic matrix elements were computed as a function of momentum transfer.

## Key numbers

- For the 1p₁/₂ → 2s₁/₂ transition in ¹⁶O, the ratio of relativistic to non-relativistic axial charge matrix elements at q=0 is 1.33.
- For the 1p₃/₂ → 1d₃/₂ transition in ¹⁶O, the ratio at q=0 is 1.20.
- For the 1d₃/₂ → 2p₃/₂ transition in ⁴⁰Ca, the ratio is approximately 1.23, nearly independent of momentum transfer.
- The scalar potential Σˢ at r=0 is -384 MeV, close to the nuclear matter saturation value of -400 MeV.
- The nuclear matter saturation density used is ρ₀ = 0.17 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatments of axial charge renormalization in nuclear matter by Towner (1987) and by the same group (refs. 7, 7a) to a fully self-consistent relativistic description of finite nuclei, incorporating both correlations and single-particle wavefunctions consistently. It also contrasts with the finite-nucleus perturbative approach of ref. 8, which used oscillator wavefunctions and a local correlation function. The results show that the enhancement factors for finite nuclei (1.2–1.3) are considerably smaller than the nuclear-matter estimates (1.4 perturbative, 1.7 non-perturbative) due to the dominance of the nuclear surface in the radial integrals, and that the renormalization depends on the specific transition and momentum transfer. The work enables more realistic calculations of first-forbidden β-decay transitions and highlights the need for further constraints on the relativistic potential from NN and anti-NN scattering data.
