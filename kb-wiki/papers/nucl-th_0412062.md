# Collective Paths Connecting the Oblate and Prolate Shapes in 68Se and 72Kr Suggested by the Adiabatic Self-Consistent Collective Coordinate Method

> By means of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method and the pairing-plus-quadrupole interaction, we have obtained the self-consistent collective path connecting the oblate and prolate local minima in 68Se and 72Kr for the first time. The self-consistent collective path is found to run approximately along the valley connecting the oblate and prolate local minima in the collective potential energy landscape. This result of calculation clearly indicates the importance of triaxial deformation dynamics in oblate-prolate shape coexistence phenomena.

## Key claim

The authors obtain, for the first time, self-consistent collective paths connecting oblate and prolate local minima in 68Se and 72Kr using the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method with a pairing-plus-quadrupole interaction, finding that the path runs approximately along the valley between the two minima in the collective potential energy landscape.

## Method

The authors apply the ASCC method to the pairing-plus-quadrupole (P+Q) Hamiltonian, solving the moving-frame Hartree-Bogoliubov (HB) equation and the moving-frame QRPA equations iteratively to determine the state vectors and infinitesimal generators along the collective path. They use a two-major-shell model space with a reduction factor for upper-shell quadrupole matrix elements, employ the Hartree-Bogoliubov approximation ignoring exchange terms, and exploit signature symmetry to decouple the K=1 rotational degrees of freedom from the shape vibrational motion. The collective coordinate scale is fixed by requiring B(q)=1 everywhere on the path.

## Key numbers

- For 68Se and 72Kr, the self-consistent collective path connecting oblate and prolate local minima is obtained for the first time using the ASCC method with the P+Q interaction.
- The collective potential V(q) = ⟨φ(q)|Ĥ|φ(q)⟩ is computed along the path, with the frequency ω(q) = √(B(q)C(q)) determined by the condition det S = 0 from the dispersion equation.
- The canonical variable condition ⟨φ(q)|[Q̂(q),P̂(q)]|φ(q)⟩ = i is enforced, with B(q)=1 chosen to fix the scale of q.

## Context

This work extends the ASCC method previously tested on the multi-O(4) model (Kobayasi 2003) to realistic nuclei, specifically addressing oblate-prolate shape coexistence phenomena recently discovered in 68Se and 72Kr (Fischer 2000, Bouchez 2003). The approach contrasts with the η-expansion method of the original SCC method (Marumori 1980), which assumes a single local minimum, and provides a fully self-consistent microscopic description of large-amplitude collective motion connecting multiple minima. This enables future studies of tunneling dynamics and shape mixing in shape-coexisting nuclei, and the framework can be extended to include rotation for studying how shape vibrational properties change with angular momentum.
