# Damping of the isovector giant dipole resonance in $^{40,48}$Ca

> The fine structure of the IsoVector Giant Dipole Resonance (IVGDR) in the doubly-magic nuclei $^{40,48}$Ca observed in inelastic proton scattering experiments under $0^\circ$ is used to investigate the role of different mechanisms contributing to the IVGDR decay width. Characteristic energy scales are extracted from the fine structure by means of wavelet analysis. The experimental scales are compared to different theoretical approaches allowing for the inclusion of complex configurations beyond the mean-field level. Calculations are performed in the framework of RPA and beyond-RPA in a relativistic approach based on an effective meson-exchange interaction, with the UCOM effective interaction and, for the first time, with realistic two- plus three-nucleon interactions from chiral effective field theory employing the in-medium similarity renormalization group. All models highlight the role of Landau fragmentation for the damping of the IVGDR, while the differences in the coupling strength between one particle-one hole (1p-1h) and two particle-two hole (2p-2h) correlated (relativistic) and non-correlated (non-relativistic) configurations lead to very different pictures of the importance of the spreading width resulting in wavelet scales being a sensitive measure of their interplay. The relativistic approach with particle-vibration coupling, in particular, shows impressive agreement with the number and absolute values of the scales extracted from the experimental data.

## Key claim

The fine structure of the isovector giant dipole resonance (IVGDR) in the doubly-magic nuclei ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca, observed via inelastic proton scattering at 0°, is used to investigate the role of Landau fragmentation versus spreading width in the damping of the resonance; the relativistic time blocking approximation (RTBA) shows impressive agreement with the number and absolute values of wavelet scales extracted from the experimental data, while the other models tested show significant differences in the coupling strength between 1p-1h and 2p-2h configurations.

## Method

High energy-resolution (p,p') scattering experiments were performed at iThemba LABS using a 200 MeV proton beam and the K600 magnetic spectrometer in 0° mode, with an energy resolution of about 40 keV FWHM. The measured spectra were converted to equivalent photo-absorption cross sections via the virtual photon method in the Eikonal approximation, after subtracting nuclear background contributions from the isoscalar giant monopole resonance, isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance, and quasi-free scattering. Characteristic energy scales were extracted from the fine structure of the experimental and theoretical E1 strength functions using a continuous wavelet transform with the complex Morlet mother wavelet, and the resulting power spectra were compared across three theoretical approaches: relativistic RPA and RTBA with an effective meson-exchange interaction (NL3 forces), RPA and second RPA (SRPA) with the UCOM effective interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential, and in-medium similarity renormalization group (IM-SRG) combined with RPA and SRPA using chiral effective field theory two- plus three-nucleon interactions (N2LO_SAT).

## Key numbers

- Energy resolution achieved: ΔE ≈ 40 keV (FWHM) for both ⁴⁰Ca and ⁴⁸Ca spectra.
- Proton beam energy: Ep = 200 MeV; scattering angle acceptance: θLab = 0° ± 1.91°.
- ⁴⁸Ca target: isotopically enriched to 90.0%, areal density 1.5 mg/cm².
- Counting statistics: several hundred counts per 10 keV bin.
- Contributions from ISGMR and ISGQR to measured cross sections: well below 10% at their respective maxima.
- For UCOM-SRPA calculations: 1p-1h configurations account for 90% of the norm in ⁴⁰Ca in the IVGDR energy region.
- For chiral EFT calculations (IMSRPA and IMRPA): the IVGDR centroid is systematically too high by 3-4 MeV relative to experiment.
- RTBA produces a scale at very small values (<200 keV) identified as a generic signature of the spreading width.

## Context

This work extends previous fine-structure studies of the IVGDR across the nuclear chart (Poltoratska 2014, Fearick 2018, Jin 2018, Bass 2020, Donaldson 2020) and of the isoscalar giant quadrupole resonance (Shevchenko 2004, 2009; Usman 2011, 2016; Kureba 2018), where wavelet analysis was identified as a particularly promising method for extracting energy scales (Shevchenko 2008). The present study focuses on doubly-magic ⁴⁰,⁴⁸Ca to minimize ground-state correlations, enabling comparison with three beyond-RPA models: the relativistic time blocking approximation (Litvinova 2007, 2008, 2010), SRPA with UCOM (Papakonstantinou 2009, 2010), and for the first time, IM-SRG with chiral EFT interactions (Trippel 2016). The results demonstrate that high energy-resolution data combined with wavelet analysis can provide unique insight into the interplay of Landau fragmentation and spreading width in IVGDR damping, and the RTBA calculations show remarkable agreement with experiment, particularly for ⁴⁸Ca.
