# Systematic Study of Survival Probability of Excited Superheavy Nuclei

> The stability of excited superheavy nuclei (SHN) with $100 \leq Z \leq 134$ against neutron emission and fission is investigated by using a statistical model. In particular, a systematic study of the survival probability against fission in the 1n-channel of these SHN is made. In present calculations the neutron separation energies and shell correction energies are consistently taken from the calculated results of the finite range droplet model which predicts an island of stability of SHN around Z=115 and N=179. It turns out that this island of stability persists for excited SHN in the sense that the calculated survival probabilities in the 1n-channel of excited SHN at the optimal excitation energy are maximized around Z=115 and N=179. This indicates that the survival probability in the 1n-channel is mainly determined by the nuclear shell effects.

## Key claim

The island of stability of superheavy nuclei around Z=115 and N=179, predicted by the finite range droplet model for ground-state nuclei, persists for excited nuclei in the sense that the calculated survival probabilities in the 1n-channel at the optimal excitation energy are maximized in that same region, indicating that survival probability is mainly determined by nuclear shell effects.

## Method

The authors used a statistical model to calculate neutron emission widths (via a Fermi-gas level density formula) and fission widths (via the Bohr-Wheeler formula with a barrier transmission probability) for superheavy nuclei with atomic numbers 100 ≤ Z ≤ 134. Neutron separation energies and shell correction energies were taken consistently from the finite range droplet model (FRDM) of Moeller (1995). The survival probability in the 1n-channel was computed as the product of the 1n-emission realization probability and the ratio of the neutron width to the sum of neutron and fission widths, evaluated at the optimal excitation energy for each nucleus.

## Key numbers

- The island of stability in the FRDM predictions centers around Z=115 and N=179.
- Deformed sub-shells at Z=108 and N=162–164 produce fission barriers as high as about 7–8 MeV.
- The damping parameter for shell effects with excitation energy was taken as E_D = 5.48A^{1/3} / (1 + 1.3A^{-1/3}) from Ignatyuk (1975).
- The barrier width parameter used was ℏω_s.d. = 2.2 MeV, following Zubov (2005).
- The level density parameter at the saddle point was a_s.d. = 1.1A/12 MeV^{-1}, and a = A/12 MeV^{-1} in other cases.
- The pairing correction δ was taken as 12/√A MeV, 0, and -12/√A MeV for even-even, odd-even, and odd-odd nuclei respectively, following Zubov (2009).
- The nuclear radius parameter was r_0 = 1.20 fm.

## Context

This work extends systematic studies of ground-state stability of superheavy nuclei (e.g., Myers 1966, Sobiczewski 1966, Meldner 1967) to the excited regime, using the FRDM predictions of Moeller (1995). It complements prior calculations of survival probabilities for specific compound nuclei formed in cold and hot fusion reactions (Adamian 2000, Zubov 2002, Zubov 2005, Zubov 2009) by providing a systematic survey across Z=100–134. The results enable future work to compare predictions from different nuclear models (which give different proton magic numbers such as 114, 120, or 126) and to guide experimental searches for new superheavy elements by identifying which mass regions yield the highest survival probabilities.
