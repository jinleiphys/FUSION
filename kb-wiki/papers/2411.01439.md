# Shannon entropy of optimized proton-neutron pair condensates

> Proton-neutron pairing and like-nucleon pairing are two different facets of atomic nuclear configurations. While like-nucleon pair condensates manifest their superfluidic nature in semi magic nuclei, it is not absolutely clear if there exists a T=0 proton-neutron pair condensate phase in $N=Z$ nuclei. With an explicit formalism of general pair condensates with good particle numbers, we optimize proton-neutron pair condensates for all $N=Z$ nuclei between $^{16}$O and $^{100}$Sn, given shell model effective interactions. As comparison, we also optimize like-nucleon pair condensates for their semi-magic isotones. Shannon entanglement entropy is a measurement of mixing among pair configurations, and can signal intrinsic phase transition. It turns out the like-nucleon pair condensates for semi-magic nuclei have large entropies signaling an entangled phase, but the proton-neutron pair condensates end up not far from a Hartree-Fock solution, with small entropy. With artificial pairing interaction strengths, we show that the general proton-neutron pair condensate can transit from an entangled T=1 phase to an entangled T=0 phase, i.e. pairing phase transition driven by external parameters. In the T=0 limit, the proton-neutron pair condensate optimized for $^{24}$Mg turns out to be a purely P pair condensate with large entanglement entropy, although such cases may occur in cold atom systems, unlikely in atomic nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper finds that optimized proton-neutron pair condensates for realistic N=Z nuclei have small Shannon entropy, remaining close to a Hartree-Fock solution, unlike like-nucleon pair condensates in semi-magic nuclei which show large entropy signaling an entangled phase; only under artificially enhanced pairing interaction strengths can a transition from an entangled T=1 phase to an entangled T=0 phase be induced, with the T=0 limit in ²⁴Mg producing a purely P pair condensate with large entanglement entropy, though the authors note such a T=0 condensate phase is unlikely in actual atomic nuclei.

## Method

The authors construct a general proton-neutron pair condensate as a trial wavefunction with good particle number, defined by a collective pair creation operator with variational pair structure coefficients, and optimize it variationally to minimize the expectation of shell model effective interactions (USDB, GX1A, JUN45) for all N=Z nuclei from ¹⁶O to ¹⁰⁰Sn. They then implement triaxial angular momentum projection after variation (PAV) to restore rotational symmetry, and compute the Shannon entropy in the canonical basis of the optimized pair condensate as a measure of mixing among pair configurations.

- Ground state energy from PN-PC is higher than exact shell model ground state by 0.58, 1.58, 1.84, 3.75, 1.09 MeV for even-even N=Z nuclei in the 1s0d major shell (²⁰Ne, ²⁴Mg, ²⁸Si, ³²S, ³⁶Ar respectively)

- 144 free parameters for 1s0d shell, 400 for 1p0f shell, 484 for 1p0f₅/₂0g₉/₂ shell in the variational optimization

## Context

This work extends the formalism of like-nucleon pair condensates from PhysRevC.105.034317 to proton-neutron pair condensates, and applies Shannon entropy analysis previously used in rare earth nuclei by Guan (2016) to detect pairing phase transitions. The results contradict suggestions from Hartree-Fock-Bogolyubov calculations by PhysRevLett.106.252502 that spin-triplet (T=0) condensates prevail in N=Z ≈ 65 nuclei, and instead support the dual description argument of Zerguine (2011), Neergaard (2013), and Fu (2013) that like-nucleon pair truncation and proton-neutron pair schemes can both describe low-lying states in N=Z even-even nuclei. The work enables systematic benchmarking of pair condensate approaches against full shell model calculations in medium-mass nuclei where exact diagonalization becomes intractable.
