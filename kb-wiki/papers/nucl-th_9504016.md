# Boson-fermion mapping of collective fermion-pair algebras

> We construct finite Dyson boson-fermion mappings of general collective algebras extended by single-fermion operators. A key element in the construction is the implementation of a similarity transformation which transforms boson-fermion images obtained directly from the supercoherent state method. In addition to the general construction, we give detailed applications to SO(2N), SU(l+1), SO(5), and SO(8) algebras.

## Key claim

The paper constructs finite Dyson boson-fermion mappings for general collective algebras extended by single-fermion operators, using a similarity transformation derived from supercoherent states; the key improvement over prior work (Dobaczewski, 1993) is that the transformation yields odd states represented by single ideal fermions rather than complicated many-fermion constructs, but this holds only for a wide class of states satisfying the condition that ideal collective fermion pairs vanish.

## Method

The authors start from a Dyson boson-fermion mapping obtained via a supercoherent state method and a collective Usui operator, then apply a similarity transformation X constructed from the invariant operators of the boson and ideal fermion core algebras. The transformation is derived by considering a Hamiltonian-like operator H = R^j B_j + A^j B_j and requiring X^{-1} H X = H_0 = R^j B_j, which leads to an explicit series for X in terms of the ideal fermion invariant C_F and the operator A^j B_j. The transformed images of single-fermion operators are then given as series that truncate when acting on states satisfying A_l |psi) = 0, which includes the physical subspace of interest. Detailed applications are worked out for SO(2N), SU(l+1), SO(5), and SO(8) algebras.

## Key numbers

- For the SO(2N) mapping, the similarity transformation sums to a closed form: X = ((2n-1)!!/(n+n-1)!!) exp[1/2 alpha^mu alpha^nu B_{munu}] with hat notation, where n = alpha^rho alpha_rho is the ideal fermion number operator.
- For the SO(2N) mapping, the single-fermion images are finite and contain terms with denominators (2n-3), (2n-1), and (2n-1)(2n-3), e.g., the transformed annihilation operator includes a term -1/((2n-1)(2n-3)) alpha^sigma alpha^tau alpha_nu B_{sigma tau}.
- For the SU(l+1) mapping, the collective pairs are normalized to g = Omega, where Omega is the number of states per level, and the structure constants are c^{jl}_{ik} = delta^j_i delta^l_k + delta^j_k delta^l_i.
- For the SU(l+1) mapping, the ideal fermion core invariant is C_F = 1/2 n (Omega+1 - 1/2 n), and the transformation is X = ((Omega - 1/2(n+n))!/(Omega - n)!) exp[A^i B_i] with hat notation.

## Context

This work extends the boson-fermion mapping formalism of Dobaczewski (1981, 1993) and the supercoherent state method of Dobaczewski, Geyer, and collaborators (1993), correcting a drawback where prior mappings produced odd states as complicated many-fermion constructs rather than single-fermion states. The construction enables the consistent inclusion of collectivity and the Pauli principle in boson-fermion models for odd-mass nuclei, and it provides a foundation for dynamical supersymmetry applications as already implemented in Navratil, Geyer, and Dobaczewski (1994). The examples for SO(2N), SU(l+1), SO(5), and SO(8) algebras demonstrate the method's applicability to specific fermion models used in nuclear structure theory.
