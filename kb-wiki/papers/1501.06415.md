# Effective model for in-medium $\bar{K}N$ interactions including the $L=1$ partial wave

> Coupled channels model of meson-baryon interactions based on the effective chiral Lagrangian is extended to account explicitly for the $Σ(1385)$ resonance that dominates the $P$-wave $\bar{K}N$ and $πΣ$ interactions at energies below the $\bar{K}N$ threshold. The presented model aims at a uniform treatment of the $Λ(1405)$ and $Σ(1385)$ dynamics in the nuclear medium. We demonstrate the applicability of the model by confronting its predictions with the vacuum scattering data, then we follow with discussing the impact of nuclear matter on the $πΣ$ mass spectrum and on the energy dependence of the $K^{-}p$ branching ratios.

## Key claim

The paper extends a chirally motivated coupled-channels model of meson-baryon interactions, previously restricted to the $S$-wave, to explicitly include the $\Sigma(1385)$ resonance in the $P$-wave ($L=1$) sector, aiming for a uniform treatment of both the $\Lambda(1405)$ and $\Sigma(1385)$ dynamics in nuclear matter. The authors demonstrate that the $P$-wave contribution is small for total cross sections at low kaon momenta (up to 300 MeV) but is essential for reproducing the angular dependence of differential cross sections.

## Method

The authors constructed separable potentials for the $S$-wave ($L=0$, $J=1/2$) and $P$-wave ($L=1$, $J=3/2$) partial waves, using the Tomozawa-Weinberg term for the $S$-wave and a direct Born term with an explicit $\Sigma(1385)$ resonance in the intermediate state for the $P$-wave. The $P$-wave potential was motivated by large $N_c$ consistency relations and used $SU(3)$-flavor Clebsch-Gordan coefficients for $N_c=3$. The model parameters were fixed by adopting the existing TW1 parametrization for the $S$-wave and adjusting the bare $\Sigma(1385)$ mass to reproduce the physical resonance position, then the model was extended to nuclear matter by modifying the Green functions to include Pauli blocking and hadron self-energies.

## Key numbers

- The bare $\Sigma(1385)$ mass was adjusted to $\tilde{M}_{\Sigma^{*0}} = 1590$ MeV to reproduce the physical resonance position.
- The inverse range parameter for all channels was fixed at $\alpha = 701$ MeV, and the meson decay constant at $f_{\pi} = 113$ MeV.
- The $\Lambda(1405)$ poles in the TW1 model are located at complex energies $z_1 = (1371, -54)$ MeV and $z_2 = (1433, -25)$ MeV.
- The nuclear matter density is taken as $\rho_0 = 0.17$ fm$^{-3}$, and the hadron self-energies used are $V_0^{\Lambda} = (-30 - i10)$ MeV, $V_0^{\Sigma} = (30 - i10)$ MeV, $V_0^{N} = (-60 - i10)$ MeV, and $V_0^{\pi} = -i30$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the $S$-wave model of Cieplý and Smejkal (2012) to include $P$-wave interactions, building on earlier attempts by Jido et al. (2002) and Krejčiřík (2012) that did not dynamically generate the $\Sigma(1385)$. The model enables studies of kaon-nuclear clusters such as those measured by the AMADEUS experiment, where disentangling $\Lambda(1405)$ and $\Sigma(1385)$ contributions in nuclear matter is challenging. The in-medium results are consistent with previous findings by Cieplý et al. (2011) and Friedman and Gal (2013) regarding the behavior of the $\Lambda(1405)$ in nuclear matter.
