# Droplets in the coexistence region of the two-dimensional Ising model

> The two-dimensional Ising model with fixed magnetization is studied using Monte Carlo techniques. At the coexistence line, the macroscopic, extensive droplet of minority spins becomes thermally unstable by breaking up into microscopic clusters. Intriguing finite--size effects as well as singularities of thermal and cluster properties associated with the transition are discussed.

## Key claim

The macroscopic droplet of minority spins in the two-dimensional Ising model with fixed magnetization becomes thermally unstable at the coexistence line, losing its extensivity in the thermodynamic limit; the cluster transition temperature appears to be at or below the coexistence temperature \(T_0\), with finite-size effects making a precise extrapolation to the thermodynamic limit subtle.

## Method

The authors performed Monte Carlo simulations of the two-dimensional Ising model on square lattices with \(L^2\) sites (L from 25 to 950) and full periodic boundary conditions, using a nonlocal spin-exchange algorithm and an efficient cluster algorithm. They fixed the magnetization \(M\) (ranging from 0.68 to 0.98) and measured the thermally averaged size of the largest cluster of minority spins, defining the droplet size \(n_c = \langle N_c \rangle / N^2\), as a function of temperature. They analyzed finite-size effects by studying the turning point of \(n_c(T)\) and the scaling of the largest cluster size with system size.

## Key numbers

- Droplet size \(n_c\) vanishes linearly in the reduced temperature \(t = |T_0 - T|/T_0\) as \(T \to T_0\), based on Equation (5) derived from the assumption that \(M_d = -M_s = M_0\).
- For moderate system sizes up to \(L = 750\), the turning point \(T_s(L)\) varies (almost) linearly in \(1/L\) for different values of \(M\).
- At \(M = 0.92\), the effective exponent \(x\) describing the scaling of the largest cluster size \(\langle N_c \rangle \sim L^x\) is around 0.5 to 0.7 for small lattices, but approaches \(x \approx 2\) for larger lattices.
- The crossover length \(L_c\) and the peak height of the effective exponent \(x\) increase as temperature approaches \(T_0\).

## Context

This work extends the study of cluster equilibrium properties in the two-dimensional Ising model, which had been previously examined in the grand-canonical ensemble by Coniglio and Stauffer (Coniglio and Stauffer, 1980), to the fixed-magnetization ensemble. It also relates to recent investigations of adatom islands on crystal surfaces by Selke (Selke, 1999), where the cluster transition was noticed but not placed in the context of the Ising model phase diagram. The results enable future detailed analysis of cluster properties near the transition, analogous to percolation studies, and suggest further work in both two and three dimensions.
