# Fundamental Oscillation Modes in Neutron Stars with Hyperons and Delta Baryons

> For a new parameterization of the modified effective chiral model, developed primarily to regulate the density content of the symmetry energy and its higher order terms, equations of state (EoSs) for hyperon-rich matter ($H$) and delta baryon matter ($Δ$) were obtained. The models were used to investigate the emission of gravitational waves (GWs) through $f$-mode oscillations in the corresponding neutron stars. We obtained the stellar structure, $f$-mode frequency and tidal deformability $Λ$ for our models. We report that the $Δ$ EoS is stiffer compared to the $H$ EoS. We also analyzed the velocity of sound in these media. The corresponding mass--radius relationships were obtained and compared with various observations. We studied the dependence of $f$-mode frequencies on the stellar mass, redshift and tidal deformability. We employed the well known Cowling approximation to obtain the $f$-mode frequencies for $l=2,\,3$ and $4$ modes of oscillation. We found that the $f$-mode frequencies of the $H$ and $Δ$ EoSs were almost the same in the lower mass region, while we observed a substantial difference between them in the high-mass region. We also obtained an empirical relation for the EoSs considered. The various attributes obtained for our models showed close agreement with various observational constraints from pulsars and GW events.

## Key claim
The paper demonstrates that for neutron stars containing hyperons (H EoS) or delta baryons (Δ EoS), the fundamental f-mode oscillation frequencies are nearly indistinguishable at low stellar masses but diverge substantially at high masses, with the delta baryon EoS being stiffer. This difference in the high-mass regime provides a potential observational discriminant between the two compositions, contingent on the detection of gravitational waves from such stars.

## Method
The authors employed a new parameterization of the modified effective chiral model to generate equations of state (EoSs) for hyperon-rich matter and delta baryon matter. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain the stellar structure and mass-radius relationships. The f-mode oscillation frequencies for the quadrupole (l=2), octupole (l=3), and hexadecapole (l=4) modes were computed using the Cowling approximation, which neglects spacetime perturbations. The tidal deformability was also calculated for both EoS models.

## Key numbers
- The delta baryon (Δ) EoS is stiffer than the hyperon (H) EoS, leading to a higher maximum mass for the Δ model.
- The f-mode frequencies for the H and Δ EoSs are almost the same in the lower mass region, but a substantial difference is observed in the high-mass region.
- The study computed f-mode frequencies for l=2, l=3, and l=4 modes of oscillation.
- An empirical relation for the f-mode frequency as a function of stellar properties was obtained for the considered EoSs.
- The models showed close agreement with observational constraints from pulsars and gravitational wave events.

## Context
This work extends studies of neutron star oscillations, such as those by Andersson and Kokkotas (1998), by specifically comparing the gravitational wave signatures of stars with hyperons versus those with delta baryons. It builds on previous efforts to constrain the high-density nuclear equation of state using multi-messenger astronomy, particularly the tidal deformability measurements from GW170817. The results enable future gravitational wave observatories to potentially distinguish between different exotic baryon compositions in the neutron star core, provided a high-mass event is detected.
