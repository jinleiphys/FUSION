# Hadron-Deuteron Correlations and Production of Light Nuclei in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

> The production of light nuclei in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is well described by both the thermal model, where light nuclei are in equilibrium with all other hadron species present in a fireball, and by the coalescence model, where light nuclei are formed due to final state interactions after the fireball decays. A method is proposed to falsify one of the models. We suggest to measure a hadron-deuteron correlation function which carries information about the source of the deuterons and allows one to determine whether a deuteron is directly emitted from the fireball or if it is formed afterwards. The $K^-\!-\!D$ and $p\!-\!D$ correlation functions are computed to illustrate the statement.

# Hadron-Deuteron Correlations and Production of Light Nuclei in Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collisions

## Key claim
The paper proposes that measuring the hadron-deuteron correlation function, specifically the proton-deuteron (p-D) correlation, can distinguish whether deuterons are directly emitted from the fireball (thermal model) or formed afterwards via final-state interactions (coalescence model). The method relies on comparing the source radius extracted from p-D correlations with that from p-p correlations, rather than on absolute source size values.

## Method
The authors compute the hadron-deuteron correlation function under two scenarios: (1) treating the deuteron as an elementary particle directly emitted from the fireball, and (2) treating the deuteron as a neutron-proton bound state formed after nucleon emission. They use the femtoscopic correlation formula with a Gaussian source function and include Coulomb effects via the Gamow factor. The K⁻-D and p-D correlation functions are computed for different source radii (R_s = 1.50, 1.73, and 2.00 fm) using s-wave scattering amplitudes, with the p-D function averaged over spin channels 1/2 and 3/2.

## Key numbers
- The deuteron radius is about 2 fm and the deuteron formation time is roughly 100 fm/c.
- The scattering length for K⁻-D is (1.46 − 1.08 i) fm (Doring, 2011).
- The s-wave scattering lengths for p-D scattering are 4.0 fm (spin 1/2 channel) and 11.0 fm (spin 3/2 channel) (Black, 1999).
- When deuterons are formed as bound states, the source radius is larger by a factor of sqrt(4/3) ≈ 1.15 compared to elementary deuterons.
- The proton source radius in pp collisions at LHC was measured with 7% experimental accuracy (2% statistical) (Acharya, 2018).
- The required accuracy for the proposed measurement is better than 15%.

## Context
This work extends the ongoing debate between the thermal model (Andronic, 2010; Cleymans, 2011; Andronic, 2017) and the coalescence model (Butler, 1963; Schwarzschild, 1963; Sato, 1981) for light nuclei production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions. The authors note that both models predict similar yields (DasGupta, 1981; Zhu, 2015; Mrowczynski, 2016), making discrimination difficult. A prior proposal to distinguish the models involved comparing yields of ⁴He and ⁴Li (Mrowczynski, 2016; Bazak, 2018). The present work enables a new experimental test using femtoscopic correlation measurements, which the ALICE Collaboration is already pursuing (Graczykowski-Janik-Kisiel). The method is designed to be robust against systematic uncertainties by relying on relative comparisons of source radii rather than absolute values.
