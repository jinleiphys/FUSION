# Precise measurement of branching ratios in the beta decay of 38Ca

> We present the full description of a measurement of the branching ratios for the beta-decay of 38Ca. This decay includes five allowed 0+ --> 1+ branches and a superallowed 0+ --> 0+ one. With our new result for the latter, we determine its ft value to be 3062.3(68) s, a result whose precision (0.2%) is comparable to the precision of the thirteen well known 0+ --> 0+ transitions used up till now for the determination of Vud, the up-down quark-mixing element of the CKM matrix. The 38Ca superallowed transition thus becomes the first addition to this set of transitions in nearly a decade and the first for which a precise mirror comparison is possible, thus enabling an improved test of the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections required for the extraction of Vud.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first precise measurement of the branching ratios for the beta decay of 38Ca, determining the superallowed 0+ → 0+ transition ft value to be 3062.3(68) s with 0.2% precision, which is comparable to the thirteen previously known superallowed transitions used for Vud determination; however, this result is achieved by subtracting the sum of measured Gamow-Teller branches from 100%, meaning the superallowed branching ratio precision depends on the 0.6% precision of the Gamow-Teller measurements.

## Method

The experiment produced 38Ca via the p(39K,2n)38Ca reaction using a 30A-MeV 39K beam from the K500 cyclotron at Texas A&M University, separated the ejectiles by charge-to-mass ratio in the MARS recoil spectrometer, and deposited 99.7% pure samples on a fast tape-transport system. Over 60,000 collection-move-count cycles were performed, where beta-gamma coincidences were recorded using a thin BC-404 plastic scintillator for positrons and a calibrated HPGe detector for gamma rays, with the 1567-keV gamma ray from the strongest Gamow-Teller branch serving as the reference for determining all branching ratios via absolute gamma-ray intensity relative to total beta singles.

## Key numbers

- Superallowed 0+ → 0+ ft value: 3062.3 ± 6.8 s (0.2% precision)
- Total beta singles from 38Ca after corrections: 1.2996(40) × 10^8 events
- Number of beta-gamma coincidences recorded: approximately 8.8 million from 60,847 good cycles
- 38Ca fraction of total A=38 beta-decay events in the detector: 0.3510(3)
- Sample purity: 99.7% 38Ca, with total impurity contribution to beta singles of 0.58(30)%
- HPGe detector efficiency at 1567 keV: 0.1777(4)%, with ±0.2% uncertainty from 50-1800 keV and ±0.4% from 1800-3500 keV
- Beta detector absolute efficiency for total 38Ca decay: approximately 40%

## Context

This work adds the 38Ca superallowed transition as the first new addition in nearly a decade to the set of thirteen well-known 0+ → 0+ transitions previously compiled by Hardy and Towner (2009) for Vud determination. The measurement enables the first precise mirror comparison between 38Ca → 38mK and the already-known 38mK → 38Ar superallowed transitions, providing a critical test of isospin-symmetry-breaking calculations required for extracting Vud from the CKM matrix.
