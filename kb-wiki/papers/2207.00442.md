# Emergence of microphysical bulk viscosity in binary neutron star post-merger dynamics

> In nuclear matter in isolated neutron stars, the flavor content (e.g., proton fraction) is subject to weak interactions, establishing flavor ($β$-)equilibrium. However, there can be deviations from this equilibrium during the merger of two neutron stars. We study the resulting out-of-equilibrium dynamics during the collision by incorporating direct and modified Urca processes (in the neutrino-transparent regime) into general-relativistic hydrodynamics simulations with a simplified neutrino transport scheme. We demonstrate how weak-interaction-driven bulk viscosity in post-merger simulations can emerge and assess the bulk viscous dynamics of the resulting flow. We further place limits on the impact on the post-merger gravitational wave strain. Our results show that weak-interaction-driven bulk viscosity can potentially lead to a phase shift of the post-merger gravitational wave spectrum, although the effect is currently on the same level as the numerical errors of our simulation.

# Emergence of microphysical bulk viscosity in binary neutron star post-merger dynamics

## Key claim

Weak-interaction-driven bulk viscosity can emerge in binary neutron star post-merger simulations and potentially cause a phase shift in the post-merger gravitational wave spectrum, but the effect is currently at the same level as the numerical errors of the simulation. The authors demonstrate this for the first time using direct incorporation of direct and modified Urca processes in the neutrino-transparent regime, but caution that the quantitative impact on gravitational wave observables remains uncertain due to resolution limitations and assumptions about neutrino trapping.

## Method

The authors performed general-relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of binary neutron star mergers using the Frankfurt/IllinoisGRMHD code, incorporating direct and modified Urca weak interaction rates (in the Fermi surface approximation) into the evolution of electron fraction via a fully implicit operator-split approach. They used a simplified neutrino leakage scheme to treat neutrino energy losses in the neutrino-transparent regime, and considered two equations of state (TMA and SFHo) that differ in whether they reach the direct Urca threshold. The simulations modeled a system with total mass 2.74 solar masses and mass ratio 0.85, with finest grid resolution of 262 meters (and additional 150 meter resolution runs for error quantification).

## Key numbers

- Total binary mass: M = 2.74 solar masses with mass ratio q = 0.85
- Finest grid resolution: 262 m (with additional simulations at 150 m resolution for error quantification)
- Direct Urca threshold proton fraction: 0.11 (above which direct Urca dominates over modified Urca)
- Nuclear saturation density: n_sat ≈ 0.16 fm^{-3}
- TMA direct Urca threshold: just below 2 n_sat (at beta-equilibrium, T=0)
- Average relaxation time for TMA: approximately 1 ms (exponential decay)
- Average relaxation time for SFHo: approximately 1.7 ms (after plateau of about 4 ms)
- Maximum effective bulk viscosity extracted: zeta_eff less than or approximately 10^28 g/(cm s)
- Entropy increase from dissipation: Delta s ~ 0.1 k_B per baryon
- Gravitational wave frequency shift (neutrino transparent regime): Delta f ≈ 40 Hz for both EoSs
- Numerical resolution error on frequency shift: 70 Hz
- Frequency shift with neutrino trapping above T > 1 MeV: approximately 50 Hz
- Post-merger gravitational wave emission timescale: approximately 20-30 ms

## Context

This work extends previous studies of beta-equilibration in neutron star mergers by Alford (2017), Most (2021), and Hammond (2021), going beyond semi-analytic estimates of bulk viscosity and first-order bulk-viscous approximations to directly simulate the full out-of-equilibrium dynamics of Urca processes. It builds on the framework of bulk viscosity in dense matter developed by Alford (2019) and Gavassino (2020), and connects to the study of viscous effects in heavy-ion collisions (reviewed by Romatschke, 2017). The results enable future work to map out the impact of out-of-equilibrium dynamics on post-merger evolution, including mass ejection, remnant lifetime, and gravitational wave signatures, while highlighting the need for higher-resolution simulations and better treatment of neutrino trapping to reliably quantify these effects.
