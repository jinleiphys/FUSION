# Antiproton physics

> We review the physics of low-energy antiprotons, and its link with the nuclear forces. This includes: antinucleon scattering on nucleons and nuclei, antiprotonic atoms and antinucleon-nucleon annihilation into mesons.

## Key claim
This review paper summarizes the physics of low-energy antiprotons and its connection to nuclear forces, but the abstract glosses over the fact that the field’s early predictions (e.g., a hierarchy of cross sections based on one-pion exchange) were immediately contradicted by the first Berkeley measurements, and that many proposed states (baryonium) were later ruled out by LEAR experiments.

## Method
The paper surveys experimental and theoretical results on antinucleon-nucleon scattering, antiprotonic atoms, and annihilation into mesons, with emphasis on data from the LEAR facility at CERN. It discusses the G-parity rule for relating nucleon-nucleon and antinucleon-nucleon interactions, the quasi-nuclear (molecular) model for bound states, and duality arguments from the 1960s. The review also covers spin observables in elastic and charge-exchange scattering, and compares integrated cross sections for antiproton-proton and antineutron-proton systems.

## Key numbers
- The proton and antiproton masses are equal to less than \(10^{-9}\) precision (Hori, 2011).
- At low energy, the annihilation cross section exceeds the elastic cross section (\(\sigma_\text{ann} > \sigma_\text{el}\)), contrary to the early hierarchy \(\sigma_\text{ce} > \sigma_\text{el} > \sigma_\text{an}\) predicted from one-pion exchange.
- The total \(\bar p p\) cross section and annihilation cross section were measured by the PS172 and PS173 collaborations at LEAR; annihilation accounts for more than half of the total cross section.
- The integrated charge-exchange cross section is small (just a few mb).
- The total \(\bar n p\) cross section was measured between \(p_\text{lab} = 50\) and \(480\ \text{MeV}/c\) by the OBELIX collaboration (Iazzi, 2000).
- The analyzing power \(A_n\) for \(\bar p p \to \bar p p\) at \(679\ \text{MeV}/c\) was found to be sizable (Kunne, 1988).
- A candidate baryonium peak at mass \(2.95\ \text{GeV}/c^2\) was seen in \(\bar p p \pi^-\) (Evangelista, 1977), but not confirmed by LEAR.

## Context
This review extends earlier work on nucleon-nucleon forces (Fermi and Yang, 1959) to the antinucleon-nucleon system via the G-parity rule, and contrasts with the baryonium searches of the 1970s and 1980s (Shapiro, 1978; Dover, 1979) that were largely ruled out by LEAR experiments. It enables future low-energy antiproton experiments at FAIR (Darmstadt) and JPARC (Japan), and notes that the concepts developed for baryonium are now being reused in the study of hidden-charm \(XYZ\) mesons (Brambilla, 2019).
