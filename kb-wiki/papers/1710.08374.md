# Pion production within the hybrid relativistic plane wave impulse approximation model at MiniBooNE and MINERvA kinematics

> The hybrid model for electroweak single-pion production (SPP) off the nucleon, presented in [González-Jiménez et al., Phys. Rev. D 95, 113007 (2017)], is extended here to the case of incoherent pion-production on the nucleus. Combining a low-energy model with a Regge approach, this model provides valid predictions in the entire energy region of interest for current and future accelerator-based neutrino-oscillation experiments. The Relativistic Mean-Field model is used for the description of the bound nucleons while the outgoing hadrons are considered as plane waves. This approach, known as Relativistic Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation (RPWIA), is a first step towards the development of more sophisticated models, it is also a test of our current understanding of the elementary reaction. We focus on the charged-current $ν$($\barν$)-nucleus interaction at MiniBooNE and MINERvA kinematics. The effect on the cross sections of the final-state interactions, which affect the outgoing hadrons on their way out of the nucleus, is judged by comparing our results with those from the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator. We find that the hybrid-RPWIA predictions largely underestimate the MiniBooNE data. In the case of MINERvA, our results fall below the $ν$-induced 1$π^0$ production data, while a better agreement is found for $ν$-induced 1$π^+$ and $\barν$-induced 1$π^0$ production.

## Key claim

The hybrid-RPWIA model predictions largely underestimate the MiniBooNE charged-current single-pion production data, while for MINERvA kinematics the results fall below the neutrino-induced 1π⁰ production data but show better agreement for neutrino-induced 1π⁺ and antineutrino-induced 1π⁰ production, with the caveat that these predictions do not account for elastic or inelastic final-state interactions beyond the Oset and Salcedo medium modifications of the delta width.

## Method

The authors extended their previously developed hybrid model for electroweak single-pion production off the nucleon to incoherent pion production on the nucleus, combining a low-energy model containing s- and u-channel diagrams for the P33(1232), D13(1520), S11(1535), and P11(1440) resonances with tree-level background terms from chiral perturbation theory, and a Regge-based approach for high-energy behavior. Bound nucleons were described using the Relativistic Mean-Field model, while outgoing hadrons were treated as plane waves (Relativistic Plane-Wave Impulse Approximation), with the Oset and Salcedo parametrization used to account for in-medium modifications of the delta-decay width. Results were compared with MiniBooNE and MINERvA data and with predictions from the NuWro Monte Carlo event generator.

## Key numbers

- The difference between the low-energy model with cutoff form factors and the hybrid model reaches approximately 17% at a neutrino energy of 2 GeV for MiniBooNE kinematics
- The MiniBooNE neutrino-induced 1π⁺ sample has an average neutrino energy around 1 GeV, ranging from 0 to 3 GeV, while the π⁰ sample is restricted to 0.5-2 GeV
- The MINERvA neutrino flux for the 1π⁺ sample ranges from 1.5 to 10 GeV with an average energy of approximately 4 GeV, with a cut on experimental invariant mass W_exp < 1.4 GeV
- The MINERvA antineutrino and neutrino 1π⁰ samples have fluxes from 1.5 to 10 GeV with average energy approximately 4 GeV, with a cut W_exp < 1.8 GeV
- The validity of the Oset and Salcedo medium modification formula is limited to pion kinetic energies in the delta rest frame T_π* < 300 MeV

## Context

This work extends the hybrid model for single-pion production off the nucleon presented by Gonzalez-Jimenez et al. (2017) to incoherent pion production on the nucleus, building on previous RPWIA applications to charged-current neutrino-induced incoherent single-pion production by Praet (2009) and Gonzalez-Jimenez et al. (2016). The results highlight the ongoing discrepancy between microscopic model predictions and MiniBooNE data, where predictions from GiBUU by Lalakulich et al. (2013) and Hernandez et al. (2013) do not agree with MiniBooNE data when final-state interactions are included, as well as the apparent inconsistency between MiniBooNE and MINERvA data discussed by Sobczyk (2015) and Mosel (2017). The RPWIA framework serves as a first step toward developing more sophisticated models that include elastic distortion of outgoing hadrons within a relativistic and consistent quantum mechanical approach.
