# Color Transfer Enhancement for Heavy Quarkonium Production

> We study the transfer of color between a heavy quark pair and an unpaired heavy quark or antiquark moving at a nonrelativistic velocity with respect to the pair. We find that the open heavy quark or antiquark can catalyze the transformation of the pair from octet representation at short distances to singlet at long distances. This process is infrared sensitive in general, and we exhibit double poles in dimensional regularization at next-to-next-to-leading order in the transition probability. Because of their dependence on kinematic variables, these poles cannot be matched to the non-perturbative matrix elements of effective field theories based on a single heavy quark pair.

## Key claim

The paper argues that in associated production of heavy quarkonium with open heavy flavor, an additional heavy quark or antiquark can catalyze the transformation of a heavy quark pair from a color-octet state at short distances to a color-singlet state at long distances, but this process is infrared sensitive and produces double poles in dimensional regularization at next-to-next-to-leading order that cannot be absorbed into standard NRQCD matrix elements because of their dependence on kinematic variables.

## Method

The authors compute infrared poles in dimensional regularization for one-loop and two-loop virtual corrections to associated heavy quarkonium production in e+e- annihilation, using the eikonal approximation for soft gluon exchange between the active heavy quark pair and a spectator heavy quark. They expand the resulting expressions in the velocity-ordered region where the relative velocity v of the active pair is much smaller than the relative velocity β̄_S between the active pair and the spectator, and identify the leading contributions in this kinematic limit.

## Key numbers

- The NLO infrared pole residue for the sum of exchanges between the active pair and spectator is proportional to (1/ε)(α_s/2) times a difference of inverse velocities: [1/√(1-P_1²P_3²/(P_1·P_3)²) - 1/√(1-P_2²P_3²/(P_2·P_3)²)]
- In the velocity-ordered region, this difference expands to (2/β̄_S²) v cosφ_S at leading order, where v is the active pair relative velocity and β̄_S is the spectator's velocity relative to the active pair
- The NLO integral for a single eikonal diagram gives an infrared pole of -(1/2ε)(α_s/2π)(1/β̄(P_i,P_j))[ln((1+β̄)/(1-β̄)) - 2iπ], which for β̄ ≪ 1 becomes -(1/ε)(α_s/2π)(1/β̄)(β̄ - iπ)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of NRQCD factorization (Nayak 2005, Nayak 2006) by analyzing the case where the spectator is massive rather than massless, finding that the cancellation of infrared poles that occurs for massless spectators no longer holds. The results challenge the applicability of standard NRQCD factorization (Bodwin 1994) to associated production processes, and connect to potential NRQCD (Brambilla 1999). The mechanism may help explain the large J/ψ production cross section in association with c\bar{c} pairs observed by BELLE and BABAR, which has been partially accounted for by NLO NRQCD corrections (Zhang 2006).
