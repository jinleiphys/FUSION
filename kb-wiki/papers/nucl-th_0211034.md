# Effect of tensor couplings in a relativistic Hartree approach for finite nuclei

> The relativistic Hartree approach describing the bound states of both nucleons and anti-nucleons in finite nuclei has been extended to include tensor couplings for the $ω$- and $ρ$-meson. After readjusting the parameters of the model to the properties of spherical nuclei, the effect of tensor-coupling terms rises the spin-orbit force by a factor of 2, while a large effective nucleon mass $m^{*}/M_{N} \approx 0.8$ sustains. The overall nucleon spectra of shell-model states are improved evidently. The predicted anti-nucleon spectra in the vacuum are deepened about 20 -- 30 MeV.

## Key claim

The inclusion of tensor couplings for the ω- and ρ-meson in the relativistic Hartree approach raises the spin-orbit force by a factor of 2 while maintaining a large effective nucleon mass m*/MN ≈ 0.8, thereby improving the description of nucleon shell-model spectra compared to the earlier RHA model where the spin-orbit splitting was only about 1/3 of the empirical value.

## Method

The authors extended their previous relativistic Hartree approach (RHA) by adding tensor-coupling terms for the ω- and ρ-meson to the interaction Lagrangian, with coupling strengths fω and fρ. They performed a least-square fit of the model parameters to the binding energies, diffraction radii, and surface thicknesses of spherical nuclei (16O, 40Ca, 208Pb), using recent parameterizations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors. The Dirac sea contributions were evaluated via the derivative expansion technique at the one-loop level, and the Schrödinger-equivalent equations for both nucleons and anti-nucleons were solved iteratively in a spherical basis.

## Key numbers

- Effective nucleon mass m*/MN ≈ 0.8 is sustained after including tensor couplings, compared to ≈ 0.6 in relativistic mean-field calculations with the no-sea approximation.
- The spin-orbit force is increased by a factor of 2 relative to the previous RHA model without tensor couplings.
- Predicted anti-nucleon single-particle energies in the vacuum are deepened by about 20–30 MeV compared to the earlier RHA results.
- The truncated basis used for anti-nucleon calculations: n=4, j=9 for 16O; n=5, j=11 for 40Ca; n=9, j=19 for 208Pb.

## Context

This work extends the relativistic Hartree approach of Mao (1999) which included vacuum contributions from the Dirac sea but produced spin-orbit splittings only about 1/3 of empirical values, in contrast to relativistic mean-field models such as the NL1 parameter set of Reinhard et al. (1986) which used the no-sea approximation. The inclusion of tensor couplings for vector mesons, motivated by effective field theory arguments of Weinberg (1995), allows the model to simultaneously achieve a large effective mass (≈ 0.8 MN) and realistic spin-orbit splitting, enabling more reliable predictions for anti-nucleon bound states in the vacuum that could be relevant for anti-matter studies in relativistic heavy-ion collisions.
