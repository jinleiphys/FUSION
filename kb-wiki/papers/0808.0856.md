# Chiral condensate in neutron matter

> A recent chiral perturbation theory calculation of the in-medium quark condensate $<\bar q q>$ is extended to the isospin-asymmetric case of pure neutron matter. In contrast to the behavior in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter we find only small deviations from the linear density approximation. This feature originates primarily from the reduced weight factors (e.g. 1/6 for the dominant contributions) of the $2π$-exchange mechanisms in pure neutron matter. Our result suggests therefore that the tendencies for chiral symmetry restoration are actually favored in systems with large neutron excess (e.g. neutron stars). We also analyze the behavior of the density-dependent quark condensate $<\bar q q>(ρ_n)$ in the chiral limit $m_π\to 0$.

# Chiral condensate in neutron matter

## Key claim
In pure neutron matter, the in-medium quark condensate shows only small deviations from the linear density approximation, in contrast to the strong non-linear behavior found in isospin-symmetric nuclear matter. This result suggests that chiral symmetry restoration is actually favored in systems with large neutron excess, such as neutron stars, primarily due to reduced isospin weight factors (e.g., 1/6 for the dominant 2π-exchange contributions).

## Method
The authors extended a previous in-medium chiral perturbation theory calculation of the quark condensate from isospin-symmetric nuclear matter to the isospin-asymmetric case of pure neutron matter, working up to three-loop order in the energy density. They applied the Feynman-Hellmann theorem to relate the in-medium condensate to the quark mass derivative of the neutron matter energy density, computing contributions from 1π-exchange, iterated 1π-exchange, irreducible 2π-exchange, and 2π-exchange with virtual Δ(1232)-isobar excitations, with the short-distance dynamics taken from lattice QCD NN-potential results.

## Key numbers
- The nucleon sigma-term used is σ_N = 45 MeV (empirical central value).
- The pion decay constant in the chiral limit is f_π = 86.5 MeV, with g_A = 1.224 and c_1 = -0.93 GeV^{-1}.
- The delta-nucleon mass splitting is Δ = 293 MeV.
- The calculation covers neutron densities up to ρ_n ≤ 0.35 fm^{-3}.
- The physical pion mass used is m_π = 135 MeV.
- The adjusted short-distance term in the neutron matter equation of state is: Ē_n(k_n)^{(adj)} = -1.04 GeV^{-2} k_n^3 - 18.4 GeV^{-4} k_n^5.
- In the chiral limit m_π → 0, a singular term remains: D_n(k_n)|_{m_π→0} = (2k_n^3/3(4πf_π)^4)(1+6g_A^2-11g_A^4) ln(m_π/λ).

## Context
This work extends the chiral perturbation theory calculation of the in-medium quark condensate by Kaiser and Weise (2008) from isospin-symmetric nuclear matter to pure neutron matter, following the framework established in their earlier study (Homont et al., 2008). The results contrast with the strong non-linear dependence found in symmetric nuclear matter, where the condensate stabilizes at about 60% of its vacuum value around ρ_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3} for physical pion mass. The findings enable further investigation of possible observable consequences for chiral symmetry restoration in neutron stars and can be interpreted in terms of a density-dependent effective nucleon sigma-term with both isoscalar and isovector components.
