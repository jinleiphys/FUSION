# Superfluid Density of Neutrons in the Inner Crust of Neutron Stars: New Life for Pulsar Glitch Models

> Calculations of the effects of band structure on the neutron superfluid density in the crust of neutron stars made under the assumption that the effects of pairing are small [N. Chamel, Phys. Rev. C 85, 035801 (2012)] lead to moments of inertia of superfluid neutrons so small that the crust alone is insufficient to account for the magnitude of neutron star glitches. Inspired by earlier work on ultracold atomic gases in an optical lattice, we investigate fermions with attractive interactions in a periodic lattice in the mean-field approximation. The effects of band structure are suppressed when the pairing gap is of order or greater than the strength of the lattice potential. By applying the results to the inner crust of neutron stars, we conclude that the reduction of the neutron superfluid density is considerably less than previously estimated and, consequently, it is premature to rule out models of glitches based on neutron superfluidity in the crust.

## Key claim
Band-structure suppression of the neutron superfluid density in the inner crust is greatly reduced when the pairing gap is comparable to or larger than the lattice potential strength; consequently, glitch models based on crustal neutron superfluidity cannot be ruled out, contrary to the conclusion of Chamel (2012) who assumed pairing effects were small.

## Method
The authors solve the Bogoliubov–de Gennes (BdG) equations for fermions with attractive interactions in a one-dimensional sinusoidal periodic potential, extending the mean-field approach previously applied to ultracold atomic gases in optical lattices (Watanabe 2008). They then apply these results to the neutron star inner crust by treating each pair of reciprocal lattice vectors (RLVs) as contributing independently, using Fourier components of the lattice potential from Pearson et al. (2015), and integrating over all RLVs with wave numbers less than twice the neutron Fermi wave number.

## Key numbers
- At a total nucleon density of 0.03 fm⁻³, the neutron density outside nuclei is ≈ 0.024 fm⁻³, corresponding to a neutron Fermi wave number kₙ = 0.89 fm⁻¹ and Fermi energy ≈ 16.4 MeV.
- The pairing gap for ¹S₀ neutrons at these densities is estimated to lie in the range 1–1.5 MeV.
- The lattice potential magnitude has a maximum of 2 MeV at K=0 and falls below 0.1 MeV for K ≳ 0.5 kₙ.
- Without pairing, the estimated superfluid density ratio nˢ/nₙᵒ ≈ 0.20 (a factor of 5 reduction).
- With pairing included: nˢ/nₙᵒ ≈ 0.64 (36% reduction) for a gap of 1 MeV, and nˢ/nₙᵒ ≈ 0.71 (29% reduction) for a gap of 1.5 MeV.

## Context
This work directly addresses the calculations of Chamel (2012), who found the neutron superfluid density reduced by a factor of 10 or more by band structure effects, making crustal glitch models problematic. The authors show that including pairing suppresses band structure effects because the lattice potential is weaker than the pairing gap for most reciprocal lattice vectors. The results enable continued viability of glitch models based on neutron superfluidity in the inner crust and suggest that cold atomic gas experiments in optical lattices could provide direct experimental confirmation of the suppression mechanism.
