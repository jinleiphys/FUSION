# Efimov physics beyond three particles

> Efimov physics originally refers to a system of three particles. Here we review recent theoretical progress seeking for manifestations of Efimov physics in systems composed of more than three particles. Clusters of more than three bosons are tied to each Efimov trimer, but no independent Efimov physics exists there beyond three bosons. The case of a few heavy fermions interacting with a lighter atom is also considered, where the mass ratio of the constituent particles plays a significant role. Following Efimov's study of the (2+1) system, the (3+1) system was shown to have its own critical mass ratio to become Efimovian. We show that the (4+1) system becomes Efimovian at a mass ratio which is smaller than its sub-systems thresholds, giving a pure five-body Efimov effect. The (5+1) and (6+1) systems are also discussed, and we show the absence of 6- and 7-body Efimov physics there.

## Key claim

The paper reviews theoretical progress on Efimov physics in systems with more than three particles, showing that while clusters of more than three identical bosons are tied to each Efimov trimer, no independent Efimov physics exists beyond three bosons; for mass-imbalanced fermionic mixtures, the (4+1) system becomes Efimovian at a mass ratio smaller than its sub-system thresholds, but the (5+1) and (6+1) systems show no Efimov physics.

## Method

The author uses the zero-range limit with Bethe-Peierls boundary conditions and the Skorniakov-Ter-Martirosian (STM) integral equation formalism to study (N+1) systems of N identical heavy fermions interacting with a lighter distinguishable particle. For the (4+1) system, a novel STM-DMC (diffusion Monte Carlo) method is introduced where the wave function is treated as a density probability function for walkers. For the (5+1) and (6+1) systems, the scale factor is extracted from energies in a harmonic trap calculated using a Gaussian potential with finite range, extrapolated to the zero-range limit via the stochastic variational method with correlated Gaussians.

## Key numbers

- For three identical bosons at unitarity, the scale factor is s = 1.00624 i, giving a geometric-series energy spectrum E_n = E_0 e^{-2π n/|s|}.
- The universal (2+1) trimer binding threshold occurs at mass ratio M/m = 8.173; the Efimov threshold for the (2+1) system is at M/m = 13.607.
- The universal (3+1) tetramer threshold is at M/m = 8.862; the Efimov threshold for the (3+1) system is at M/m = 13.384.
- The universal (4+1) pentamer threshold is at M/m = 9.672; the Efimov threshold for the (4+1) system is at M/m = 13.279.
- The (5+1) and (6+1) systems show no Efimov physics (no s=0 crossing) for any mass ratio studied.

## Context

This work extends Efimov's original three-body prediction (Efimov 1970) and the earlier claim by Amado and Greenwood (1973) that no Efimov effect exists for four or more identical particles. It builds on studies of the (2+1) system by Efimov (1973) and the (3+1) system by Castin, Mora, and Pricoupenko (2010) and Blume (2012). The results for the (4+1) system provide a pure five-body Efimov effect, while the absence of Efimov physics in the (5+1) and (6+1) systems sets a boundary on how many heavy fermions can be bound by a single light atom. This enables future experimental searches in ultracold atom mixtures with appropriate mass ratios.
