# Pion inspired by QCD: Nakanishi and Light-Front Integral Representations

> The pion structure in Minkowski space is explored using the Nakanishi integral representation. A general framework is developed for the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude based on the Kallen-Lehmann representation of the dressed quarks with an ansatz for the pseudo-scalar $π-q\bar q$ vertex fulfilling the axial Ward-Takahashi identity. The Nakanishi weight functions are derived for the scalar amplitudes associated with the decomposition of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in different operator bases in the Dirac spinor space in terms of the involved spectral densities. The approach is applied to an analytical model of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, which is combined with Landau gauge lattice QCD results for the quark running mass at space-like momentum. From the Nakanishi integral representation several pion observables were calculated, such as the decay constant, the spin decomposition of the valence probabilities, longitudinal and transverse momentum distributions from the valence component of the light-front wave function.

## Key claim

The paper develops a general framework for the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in Minkowski space using the Nakanishi integral representation combined with the Kallen-Lehmann spectral representation of dressed quark propagators, but the quantitative application is limited to an analytical model of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude that uses Landau gauge lattice QCD results for the quark running mass at space-like momentum, not a full solution of the Bethe-Salpeter equation.

## Method

The authors derive Nakanishi weight functions for the scalar amplitudes of the pion Bethe-Salpeter amplitude in both non-orthogonal and orthogonal Dirac operator bases, expressing them in terms of spectral densities from the Kallen-Lehmann representation of the dressed quark propagator. They apply this framework to an analytical model where the pseudoscalar vertex is constrained by the axial Ward-Takahashi identity in the chiral limit, with the quark mass function fitted to lattice QCD results from ParPRD06. The valence light-front wave function is then obtained from the LF projection of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude, and observables are computed including the decay constant, spin decomposition of valence probabilities, and momentum distributions.

## Key numbers

- The valence component accounts for 70% of the normalization of the pion wave function, as found in a dynamical model where the quark does not have momentum dependent self-energy (cited from Paula_2021, Ydrefors:2021dwa, Paula_2022)
- The number of colors used in the valence wave function expressions is N_c = 3
- The pion mass is taken as m_pi = 0 in the chiral limit for the vertex expression E_pi(k,p) = i B(k^2)/f^0_pi

## Context

This work extends the pion model proposed in Mello (2017mor), which used a simple analytical quark mass function fitted to lattice QCD results from ParPRD06, by further exploring the analytical structure of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude using the Nakanishi integral representation. The approach builds on prior work using the Nakanishi representation to solve Bethe-Salpeter equations in Minkowski space pioneered by Kusaka (1995za) and Karmanov (2005nv), and specifically extends the application to the pion with dressed quarks. The framework enables access to partonic distributions in Minkowski space from Euclidean calculations such as lattice QCD or Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter solutions, as already demonstrated in LeiPRL13 for the pion.
