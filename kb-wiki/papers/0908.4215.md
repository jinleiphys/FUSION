# Non-linear waves in a Quark Gluon Plasma

> Recent measurements at RHIC suggest that a nearly perfect fluid of quarks and gluons is produced in AA collisions. Moreover the passage of supersonic partons through this medium seems to produce waves. These waves might pile up and form Mach cones, which would manifest themselves in the so called away-side jets, forming a broad structure in the angular distribution of the particles recoiling against a trigger jet of moderate energy. In most of the theoretical descriptions of these phenomena, the hydrodynamic equations are linearized for simplicity. We propose an alternative explanation for the observed broadening of the away-side peak. It is based on hydrodynamics but it is a consequence of the non-linearities of the equations, which instead of simple waves may lead to localized waves or even solitons. We investigate in detail the consequences of including the non-linear terms. We use a simple equation of state for the QGP and expand the hydrodynamic equations around equilibrium configurations. The resulting differential equations describe the propagation of perturbations in the energy density. We solve them numerically and find that localized perturbations can propagate for long distances in the plasma. Under certain conditions our solutions mimick the propagation of Korteweg - de Vries solitons.

## Key claim

The observed broadening of the away-side jet in heavy-ion collisions may be explained not by Mach cones but by the non-linear propagation of localized energy-density perturbations (soliton-like pulses) in the quark-gluon plasma; however, the authors' simple equation of state yields a "wave breaking" equation rather than the Korteweg-de Vries (KdV) equation, yet the pulse remains localized over nuclear scales, mimicking soliton behavior.

## Method

The authors start from relativistic Euler and continuity equations for a perfect fluid, using the MIT Bag Model equation of state for the QGP (ideal gas of massless quarks and gluons with bag constant). They expand the hydrodynamic equations around equilibrium in powers of a small parameter σ, using stretched coordinates (ξ, τ) to derive a non-linear wave equation for perturbations in baryon density (at zero temperature) or energy density (at finite temperature). The resulting equation is the "breaking wave equation" (a Burgers-like equation without a third-order dispersive term), which they solve numerically with initial sech² pulses of varying amplitude and width.

## Key numbers

- Speed of sound in the QGP: \(c_S^2 = 1/3\) (from the equation of state).
- At zero temperature, a baryon density pulse with amplitude \(A = 0.075\) and width \(B = 1\) fm remains localized for distances much larger than nuclear size; increasing amplitude to \(A = 0.35\) (same width) causes earlier breaking.
- At finite temperature \(T = 300\) MeV, an energy density pulse with \(A = 0.01\) and \(B = 1\) fm propagates stably; increasing amplitude to \(A = 0.1\) (same width) accelerates breaking.
- The non-linear coefficient in the wave equation varies weakly with temperature: from \(2/3\) (when \(T_0 = T_c\)) to \(1/2\) (when \(T_0 \gg T_c\)), with \(c_S\) fixed at \(1/3\).
- The bag constant is expressed via the critical temperature: \(\mathcal{B} = (37/90) \pi^2 T_c^4\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' prior studies of nuclear solitons in relativistic nuclear matter (Fogaça et al., 2008; earlier papers on the non-linear Walecka model) to the quark-gluon plasma. It contrasts with the standard linearized hydrodynamic treatments of Mach cones (e.g., Casalderrey-Solana et al., 2005; Betz et al., 2008) used to explain RHIC away-side jet data from STAR and PHENIX. The finding that breaking-wave pulses remain localized over nuclear distances enables a future extension to two spatial dimensions (longitudinal and radial) to quantify the angular broadening of the away-side jet.
