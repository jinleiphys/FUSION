# The soft drop momentum sharing fraction $z_g$ beyond leading-logarithmic accuracy

> Grooming techniques, such as soft drop, play a central role in reducing sensitivity of jets to e.g. underlying event and hadronization at current collider experiments. The momentum sharing fraction $z_g$, of the two branches in a jet that pass the soft drop condition, is one of the most important observables characterizing a collinear splitting inside the jet, and directly probes the QCD splitting functions. In this work, we present a factorization framework that enables a systematic calculation of the corresponding cross section beyond leading-logarithmic (LL) accuracy, showing that this measurement is not only sensitive to the QCD charge but also the spin of the parton that initiates the jet. Our results at next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL$'$) accuracy include non-global logarithms, and provide a first meaningful assessment of the perturbative uncertainty. We present a comparison to the available experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, and STAR and find excellent agreement.

## Key claim
The paper presents a factorization framework for the soft drop momentum sharing fraction \(z_g\) that achieves next-to-leading logarithmic (NLL') accuracy, going beyond the previous leading-logarithmic (LL) calculations. The authors show that this observable is sensitive not only to the QCD color charge but also to the spin of the parton initiating the jet, with the caveat that for \(\beta \ge 0\) the observable is Sudakov-safe rather than infrared-collinear safe.

## Method
The authors set up a factorization framework within Soft Collinear Effective Theory (SCET) to calculate the cross section differential in jet transverse momentum \(p_T\), rapidity \(\eta\), the momentum sharing fraction \(z_g\), and the groomed jet radius \(\theta_g \equiv R_g/R\). They perform resummation of large logarithmic corrections of \(z_g\), \(\theta_g\), and \(z_{\rm cut}\) by identifying relevant SCET modes from the Lund diagram, evaluating each ingredient at its natural scale, and using renormalization group evolution to a common scale. The calculation includes non-global logarithms (NGLs) at leading order \(\alpha_s^2\) and matching to fixed-order results at leading order (LO).

## Key numbers
- The leading-order expression for the jet function for quarks is \(\tilde {\cal G}^{(1)}_q = \Theta(1/2>z_g>z_{\rm cut}\theta_g^\beta)\, \Theta(\theta_g<1) \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} \frac{1}{\theta_g} \bigl[P_{qq}(z_g)+P_{gq}(z_g)\bigr]\), where \(P_{ij}\) are the leading-order QCD splitting functions.
- The LL Sudakov exponent is \(\exp\bigg(-\frac{\alpha_s C_i}{\pi}(\beta \ln^2\theta_g+2\ln z_{\rm cut}\ln\theta_g)\bigg)\), where \(C_{i=F,A}\) are the color factors for quarks and gluons.
- The leading NGL contribution at order \(\alpha_s^2\) is \(\tilde{\cal S}_{i,1}'^{{\rm NG},(2)}(z_g \theta_g, z_g) = 2.58 C_i C_A \Big(\frac{\alpha_s}{2 \pi}\Big)^2 \frac{1}{z_g \theta_g} \ln z_g\).
- The spin-dependent corrections (nonsingular part of the splitting function) can be up to order 10%, as shown by the relative difference between quark and gluon-initiated jets.
- The perturbative uncertainty bands are obtained by varying all relevant scales by a factor of 2 around the central scale choice, with scales smoothly frozen at \({\cal O}(1~\text{GeV})\).

## Context
This work extends the earlier LL calculation of \(z_g\) by Larkoski et al. (2015) to NLL' accuracy, providing qualitatively different results for \(\beta<0\) and a smooth transition to \(\beta=0\). The factorization framework within SCET enables systematic extension beyond LL and provides the first meaningful assessment of perturbative uncertainties. The results enable direct comparison to experimental data from ALICE, ATLAS, and STAR, showing excellent agreement, and provide predictions for the future Electron-Ion Collider (EIC). The work also opens the door to future calculations at higher perturbative accuracy and systematic treatment of nonperturbative effects.
