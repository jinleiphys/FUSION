# Isospin dependent effective interaction in nucleon-nucleus scattering

> The isospin-dependent component of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction that causes the $ΔT=1$ (p, p') and (p, n) reactions off nuclei is studied. It is shown that the corrections to the impulse approximation comes from the $g$-matrix type correction and the rearrangement term. They are numerically estimated with the isospin-asymmetric nuclear-matter reaction matrix approach. The analysis of the isobaric analog transitions ${}^{42}$Ca(p,n)${}^{42}$Sc and ${}^{48}$Ca(p,n)${}^{48}$Sc are presented.

## Key claim

The medium corrections to the isospin-dependent component of the effective nucleon-nucleon interaction for \(\Delta T=1\) reactions arise from both the \(g\)-matrix type correction and the rearrangement term; the latter persists even in symmetric nuclear matter where the isovector density vanishes, contrary to what a naive impulse approximation would suggest.

## Method

The authors derived the effective interaction for inelastic scattering by starting from the Bethe-Goldstone equation for the \(g\)-matrix in isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter, separating the \(g\)-matrix into four isospin components. They then obtained the transition potential for inelastic scattering by taking density derivatives of the optical model potential, yielding explicit expressions for the isospin-dependent part of the effective interaction that includes a rearrangement term proportional to \(\rho^{[s]} \partial g^{[\alpha]}/\partial \rho^{[v]}\). Numerical calculations were performed at \(E_p = 150\) MeV using the Reid Soft Core potential, and the resulting density-dependent interaction was applied in DWBA calculations for the isobaric analog transitions \({}^{42}\)Ca(p,n)\({}^{42}\)Sc and \({}^{48}\)Ca(p,n)\({}^{48}\)Sc at \(E_p = 135\) MeV.

## Key numbers

- Volume integrals of the isospin-dependent \(g\)-matrix components at \(E_p = 150\) MeV: \(g^{[\tau]} = [\{22 + 75i\} + \{-24 - 53i\}\rho^{[s]}] + [\{8 + 22i\} + \{17 - 17i\}\rho^{[s]}] \mathbf{L}\cdot\mathbf{S}\), with densities normalized to nuclear matter saturation density.
- Volume integrals of the isovector component: \(g^{[\alpha]} = [\{6+38i\}\rho^{[v]}] + [\{-1+1i\}\rho^{[v]}] \mathbf{L}\cdot\mathbf{S}\).
- The density-dependent corrections in \(g^{[\alpha]}\) and \(g^{[\tau]}\) are of the same order numerically, showing that partial inclusion of medium corrections (e.g., using only the \(g\)-matrix) is not meaningful.
- DWBA calculations for \({}^{42}\)Ca(p,n)\({}^{42}\)Sc and \({}^{48}\)Ca(p,n)\({}^{48}\)Sc at \(E_p = 135\) MeV show that the impulse approximation overestimates the experimental cross section, the \(g\)-matrix alone lowers it but still overshoots the data, and the full medium correction (including the rearrangement term) reproduces the experimental data well.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous theoretical framework for isoscalar natural parity transitions (Cheon, Takayanagi, and Yazaki, 1985) to the isovector (\(\Delta T=1\)) channel, providing the first microscopic identification of medium corrections for the isospin-dependent part of the effective interaction. It contrasts with the empirical density-dependent interactions constructed by Kelly and collaborators (1989-1990) for elastic and isoscalar excitations, and with the free \(t\)-matrix approach of Franey and Love (1985). The results enable systematic microscopic analysis of charge-exchange reactions and isobaric analog transitions at intermediate energies, complementing the existing treatment of isoscalar excitations.
