# A "Domain Wall" Scenario for the AdS/QCD

> We propose a scenario for bottom-up gravity dual picture of QCD-like theories, which consists of two near-AdS$_5$ domains separated by the ``domain wall'' at which the effective coupling relatively rapidly switches from small perturbative value at its UV side to strong at its IR side. Its consequence are corresponding jumps in 5-d masses of the bulk fields, related to different anomalous dimensions of the corresponding operators. Inclusion of both weakly and strongly coupled parts of bulk wave functions allows for consistent inclusion of QCD hard processes. We further discuss how transitions from strong to weak coupling in hard observables should look like, exemplified by pion diffractive dissociation, which seems to show this transition experimentally. Then we propose a dynamical mechanism for this jump related to instantons, which are point-like bulk objects located at/near the domain wall. We further argue that in the limit of large number of colors $N_c\to\infty$ the "domain wall" is becoming a true singularity. Instanton-induced contributions to correlators and partition function of the instanton ensemble is reformulated in the $AdS_5$ language. Among other applications are lowest mesons as collective vibrations of the ``domain wall''.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the QCD coupling does not run smoothly from UV to IR but instead undergoes a rapid jump, a "domain wall", at an intermediate scale, separating a weakly coupled UV domain from a strongly coupled IR domain. This jump is expected to become a true singularity in the large-\(N_c\) limit, and the author suggests that instantons located at or near this wall provide the dynamical mechanism for the transition.

## Method

The author constructs a bottom-up holographic model with two near-AdS\(_5\) domains separated by a domain wall at a specific holographic coordinate \(z_{dw}\). Bulk fields are assigned different 5-dimensional masses \(M_5\) on each side of the wall, corresponding to different anomalous dimensions of the dual operators in the weak and strong coupling regimes. Wave functions are constructed by continuously connecting Whittaker function solutions with different \(M_5\) values at the domain wall, matching the logarithmic derivative to determine the common 4-dimensional mass \(m_4\). The resulting wave functions are then used to compute elastic formfactors via convolution with a bulk photon propagator, and the results are compared to experimental data on pion diffractive dissociation from the E791 experiment.

## Key numbers

* The chiral scale is \(\Lambda_\chi = 4\pi f_\pi \approx 1.2\ \text{GeV}\).
* The t'Hooft coupling in the IR is estimated as \(\lambda_{q<1\ \text{GeV}} = g^2 N_c \approx 80\).
* The domain wall position at large \(N_c\) is estimated as \(z_{dw} \approx 0.4\ \text{fm} = 0.777\) in units where the length unit is \(2/m_\rho = 0.51\ \text{fm}\).
* The instanton diluteness parameter is \(n_{I+A}\bar{\rho}^4 \sim 10^{-2}\).
* The instanton size distribution is suppressed at large \(\rho\) by a factor \(\exp(-2\pi\sigma\rho^2)\) with \(\sigma\) the QCD string tension.
* For the example state with bare dimension 3 jumping to dimension 7, the 4-d mass is \(m_4^2 = 14\) in the strong-coupling domain, shifted by \(-0.00005\) to connect the two solutions.
* The transition from strong to weak coupling in the formfactor occurs at \(Q \sim 6\ \text{GeV}\) for the example shown.
* In the E791 data, the transition between the two regimes in pion diffractive dissociation occurs at \(k_t > 1.9\ \text{GeV}\), corresponding to \(Q = 2k_t \sim 4\ \text{GeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the bottom-up AdS/QCD approach by addressing the running of the coupling at intermediate \(z\), a problem not fully treated in earlier models such as the IR cutoff of Polchinski and Strassler, the "soft wall" of Karch et al (2006), or the smooth interpolation of Gursoy, Kiritsis and Nitti (2007). The paper also connects to older QCD puzzles about the mismatch between \(\Lambda_{QCD} \sim 200\ \text{MeV}\) and the chiral scale \(\Lambda_\chi \sim 1.2\ \text{GeV}\), which were resolved by instanton physics (Shuryak 1981). The proposed domain wall scenario enables a consistent treatment of both spectroscopy and hard exclusive processes within a single holographic framework, and the author claims that the E791 data on pion diffractive dissociation already shows experimental evidence for the predicted weak-strong transition.
