# Flavour decomposition of electromagnetic transition form factors of nucleon resonances

> In Poincaré-covariant continuum treatments of the three valence-quark bound-state problem, the force behind dynamical chiral symmetry breaking also generates nonpointlike, interacting diquark correlations in the nucleon and its resonances. We detail the impact of these correlations on the nucleon's elastic and nucleon-to-Roper transition electromagnetic form factors, providing flavour-separation versions that can be tested at modern facilities.

## Key claim

The presence of strong, nonpointlike diquark correlations inside the nucleon and its first radial excitation (the Roper resonance) is the primary driver of the observed behaviour in both elastic and transition electromagnetic form factors, with meson-cloud contributions only affecting the low-momentum domain below approximately \(x = Q^2/m_N^2 \lesssim 2\).

## Method

The authors employ a Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation framework within a continuum Dyson-Schwinger equation approach, using a quark+diquark picture of baryons. They compute the nucleon's elastic form factors and the \(\gamma^\ast N \to N(1440)\) (Roper) transition form factors using a QCD-kindred interaction with momentum-dependent propagators and vertices, and compare results with those from a momentum-independent contact interaction. Flavour separation of the transition form factors is achieved by combining results for the proton and neutron reactions via isospin relations.

## Key numbers

- The dominant \(u[ud]\) scalar diquark component contributes 62% of the proton's normalisation.
- The predicted Pauli transition form factor at the photoproduction point is \(F_{2}^{\ast}(x=0) = -0.65\), consistent with experimental values \(-0.58 \pm 0.02\) (Dugger 2009) and \(-0.62 \pm 0.04\) (Agashe 2014).
- The flavour-separated Pauli form factors at zero momentum transfer are \(\kappa_u^\ast = F_{2,u}^\ast(0) = -0.91\) and \(\kappa_d^\ast = F_{2,d}^\ast(0) = 0.14\).
- The ratio of rescaling factors satisfies \(|\kappa_d^\ast/\kappa_u^\ast| < 1/6\), compared to approximately \(2/5\) in the elastic case (Cloet 2008).
- The meson-cloud correction to the Dirac transition form factor falls to 20% of its maximum value by \(x=2\) and vanishes rapidly thereafter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of nucleon, Delta, and Roper elastic and transition form factors (Segovia 2013, 2014, 2015) using both QCD-based and contact-interaction frameworks. The results contradict the notion that simple momentum-independent interactions can capture the physics, as the contact-interaction prediction disagrees both quantitatively and qualitatively with experimental data. The findings enable future tests at facilities like Jefferson Lab, particularly for the predicted \(1/x\) suppression of \(d\)-quark contributions at large momentum transfers, which requires measurements at \(x > 4\) for confirmation. The work also builds upon lattice QCD simulations (Alexandrou 2006, Babich 2007) that support the existence of diquark correlations.
