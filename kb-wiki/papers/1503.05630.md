# New measurement of the excited states in $^{11}$B via the elastic resonance scattering of $^{10}$Be + p

> The elastic resonance scattering protons decayed from $^{11}$B to the ground state of $^{10}$Be were measured using the thick-target technique in inverse kinematics at the Heavy Ion Research Facility in Lanzhou (HIRFL). The obtained excitation functions were well described by a multichannel R-matrix procedure under the kinematics process assumption of resonant elastic scattering. The excitation energy of the resonant states ranges from 13.0 to 17.0 MeV, and their resonant parameters such as the resonant energy E$_{x}$, the spin-parity J$^Ď€$, and the proton-decay partial width $Î“_p$ were determined from R-matrix fits to the data. Two of these states around E$_{x}$ = 14.55 MeV [J$^Ď€$ = (3/2$^+$, 5/2$^+$), $Î“_p$ = 475 $\pm$ 80 keV] and E$_{x}$ = 14.74 MeV [J$^Ď€$ = 3/2$^-$, $Î“_p$ = 830 $\pm$ 145 keV], and a probably populated state at E$_x$ = 16.18 MeV [J$^Ď€$ =(1/2$^-$, 3/2$^-$), $Î“_p$ $<$ 60 keV], are respectively assigned to the well-known states in $^{11}$B at 14.34 MeV, 15.29 MeV, and 16.43 MeV. The isospin of these three states were previously determined to be T = 3/2, but discrepancies exist in widths and energies due to the current counting statistics and energy resolution. We have compared these states with previous measurements, and the observation of the possibly populated resonance is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper reports new measurements of excited states in $^{11}$B via elastic resonance scattering of $^{10}$Be + p, but the authors acknowledge that discrepancies exist in widths and energies compared to previous work due to limited counting statistics and energy resolution, and that spin-parity assignments for several states remain tentative rather than definitive.

## Method

The experiment used the thick-target technique in inverse kinematics at the Heavy Ion Research Facility in Lanzhou (HIRFL), where a secondary beam of $^{10}$Be at 7.2$A$ MeV impinged on a polyethylene target. Recoiled protons were detected by three $\Delta$E-E silicon telescopes placed at laboratory angles of approximately +16Â°, -16Â°, and 0Â°. The excitation functions were analyzed using a multichannel R-matrix procedure to extract resonant parameters including excitation energy, spin-parity, and proton-decay partial width.

## Key numbers

- Excitation energy range studied: 13.0 to 17.0 MeV in $^{11}$B
- State at E$_x$ = 14.55 $\pm$ 0.07 MeV: J$^\pi$ = (3/2$^+$, 5/2$^+$), $\Gamma_p$ = 475 $\pm$ 80 keV, assigned to the known 14.34 MeV state
- State at E$_x$ = 14.74 $\pm$ 0.09 MeV: J$^\pi$ = 3/2$^-$, $\Gamma_p$ = 830 $\pm$ 145 keV, assigned to the known 15.29 MeV state
- Probably populated state at E$_x$ = 16.18 $\pm$ 0.21 MeV: J$^\pi$ = (1/2$^-$, 3/2$^-$), $\Gamma_p$ $<$ 60 keV, assigned to the known 16.43 MeV state
- Additional state at E$_x$ = 13.46 $\pm$ 0.13 MeV: J$^\pi$ = 1/2$^+$, $\Gamma_p$ = 608 $\pm$ 242 keV
- Proton separation energy Q$_p$ = 11.2285 MeV for $^{11}$B
- Estimated E$_{c.m.}$ resolution: 40 to 100 keV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of $^{11}$B resonant structure above particle decay thresholds, particularly the isospin T = 3/2 states that are analogs of states in $^{11}$Be. The 14.55 MeV state was previously observed by Goosman (1973) in the $^{10}$Be(p, $\gamma$)$^{11}$B reaction at 14.33 MeV, and the 14.74 MeV state was studied by Aryaeinejad (1985) via $^{14}$C(p, $\alpha$)$^{11}$B. The present results support Aryaeinejad's J$^\pi$ = 3/2$^-$ assignment for the 14.74 MeV state but with a larger particle width, while the spin-parity of the 14.55 MeV state remains ambiguous between 3/2$^+$ and 5/2$^+$. The authors note that high-resolution experiments are needed to draw firm conclusions about the structure of $^{11}$B.
