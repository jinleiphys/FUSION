> Talk given by V.W. at the Helmholtz International Summer School "Dense Matter In Heavy Ion Collisions and Astrophysics", Dubna, August 21 - September 1, 2006; 4 pages, to appear in the proceedings

# Goldstone bosons in the color-flavor locked phase

## Key claim

The authors compute pseudoscalar meson masses in the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase using diquark loops within an NJL-type model, finding that the slope of the meson mass versus quark mass curve is approximately 35% smaller than the low-energy effective field theory (EFT) prediction, with the deviation reducing at weaker coupling.

## Method

The authors use a three-flavor Nambu–Jona-Lasinio model with only scalar and pseudoscalar quark-quark interactions, transforming the Lagrangian into Nambu-Gorkov space to extract diquark interaction vertices. They solve the gap equations and neutrality conditions self-consistently to obtain the gap parameters and color chemical potential, then compute the polarization function via the Bethe-Salpeter equation at zero temperature and zero momentum transfer using the Matsubara formalism. The resulting T-matrix is block-diagonal, with six 2x2 blocks corresponding to flavored mesons (pi+, pi-, K+, K-, K0, anti-K0) and one 6x6 block for unflavored modes (pi0, eta, eta').

## Key numbers

- Chemical potential: mu = 500 MeV
- 3-momentum cutoff: Lambda = 602.3 MeV
- Coupling constant: H * Lambda^2 = 1.37625
- CFL gap: approximately 77 MeV
- Slope of meson mass versus quark mass from the NJL calculation is approximately 35% smaller than the EFT prediction
- For up and down quark masses fixed at 30 MeV, kaon condensation sets in at m_s approximately 142 MeV, where q0(pole) becomes zero for K+ and K0

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of Goldstone bosons in the CFL phase using low-energy effective theory approaches by Son (1999) and Bedaque (2001), which become exact at asymptotically high densities. The NJL-model approach allows explicit construction of CFL Goldstone bosons via diquark loops and confirms the possibility of kaon condensation predicted by the effective theories. The results enable future studies of unflavored mesons (pi0, eta, eta'), inclusion of quark-antiquark interactions and instanton-induced interactions, and calculation of mesonic excitations in the CFL+meson ground state.
