# Exact Dirac equation calculation of ionization and pair production induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ions

> An exact solution of the time-dependent Dirac equation for ionization and pair production induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is presented. Exact transition probabilities, equivalent to those that would be obtained in an untruncated basis coupled channels calculation, are presented. Exact bound-electron positron pair production probabilities are calculated to be mostly smaller than those calculated with the same potential in perturbation theory at impact parameters small enough for differences to occur.

## Key claim

The exact solution of the time-dependent Dirac equation for bound-electron positron pair production yields probabilities that are mostly *smaller* than those calculated in perturbation theory at impact parameters small enough for differences to occur, contrary to earlier coupled channels calculations that had shown a non-perturbative enhancement.

## Method

The author exploits the delta-function form of the ultrarelativistic Coulomb potential in the light-cone gauge to integrate the time-dependent Dirac equation exactly, obtaining closed-form matrix elements for transition amplitudes. The exact amplitudes take the form of perturbation theory but with a universal effective interaction that replaces the perturbative logarithmic potential with a complex exponential factor, thereby including coupling to all orders without basis truncation. Calculations are performed for Pb + Pb collisions at RHIC energies (γ = 23,000) using continuum wave functions and compared with both perturbation theory and earlier truncated coupled channels results.

## Key numbers

- For Pb + Pb at impact parameter b = 125 fm, the exact bound-electron positron pair production probability is 330 × 10⁻⁶, compared to the perturbative value of 487 × 10⁻⁶ (Table I).
- The exact formalism yields a cross section about 3 barns *less* than perturbation theory for a set of final states corresponding to 61% of the perturbation theory cross section, whereas the coupled channels calculation gave about 9 barns *more* than perturbation theory.
- At b = 8000 fm, the exact probability is 1.0348 × 10⁻⁶ versus the perturbative 1.0357 × 10⁻⁶ (Table I).
- For ionization at b = 0 fm, the ground state survival probability is 0.428, the sum of excited bound state probabilities is 0.100, and the sum of continuum electron probabilities is 0.457, giving a total sum of 0.985 (Table II).
- The perturbation theory cross section for continuum-electron positron pair production at RHIC is about 30,000 barns.

## Context

This work extends the ultrarelativistic potential formalism developed by Baltz (1995) and Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser (1991) to provide an exact solution of the Dirac equation, correcting earlier coupled channels calculations (Rumrich et al., 1991; Baltz, Rhoades-Brown, and Weneser, 1993, 1994) that had shown a non-perturbative enhancement of bound-electron positron pair production. The exact method makes tractable the calculation of continuum-electron positron pair production, which had been prohibitive due to the large number of coupled states, and enables ionization calculations without explicit treatment of continuum final states by using the flux lost from the initial bound state.
