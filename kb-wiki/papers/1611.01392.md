# Mirror and triplet displacement energies within nuclear DFT: numerical stability

> Isospin-symmetry-violating class II and III contact terms are introduced into the Skyrme energy density functional to account for charge dependence of the strong nuclear interaction. The two new coupling constants are adjusted to available experimental data on triplet and mirror displacement energies, respectively. We present preliminary results of the fit, focusing on its numerical stability with respect to the basis size.

## Key claim

The paper introduces two new isospin-symmetry-violating contact terms (class II and III) into the Skyrme energy density functional and presents preliminary fits of their coupling constants, showing that the basis-size-related uncertainty (about ±15 keV for optimal basis choices) is small compared to the fitting uncertainty (standard deviations of 100–190 keV).

## Method

The authors extended the Skyrme energy density functional with two contact terms, V^II and V^III, each with a coupling constant (t0^II and t0^III) and a spin-exchange parameter (x0^II and x0^III, later found redundant). They solved the Hartree-Fock equation using the HFODD code in a Cartesian harmonic oscillator basis with varying numbers of spherical shells N (10, 12, 14, 16, 18, 20). Mirror displacement energies (MDEs) for T=1/2 doublets with A=25, 33, 57, 75 and triplet displacement energies (TDEs) for T=1 triplets with A=22, 34, 58 were computed using three Skyrme parametrizations (SV_T, SkM*, SLy4). The coupling constants were then fitted independently via linear regression to all available MDE data (A=10–75) and TDE data (A=10–58).

## Key numbers

- Optimal basis sizes: N=10 shells for light nuclei (10 ≤ A ≤ 30), N=12 for medium-mass (31 ≤ A ≤ 56), N=14 for heavy (A ≥ 57), yielding basis-size uncertainty Δ_basis ≈ ±15 keV
- With a less strict choice (N=10 for A ≤ 56 and 58, N=14 for heaviest), MDE uncertainty increases to Δ_basis ≈ ±30 keV while TDE uncertainty is little affected
- Fitted t0^II values: 17±5 MeV fm^3 (SV_T), 24±8 MeV fm^3 (SkM*), 22±7 MeV fm^3 (SLy4); corresponding σ_fit for MDE: 100 keV, 110 keV, 100 keV
- Fitted t0^III values: -7.3±1.9 MeV fm^3 (SV_T), -5.5±1.3 MeV fm^3 (SkM*), -5.5±1.1 MeV fm^3 (SLy4); corresponding σ_fit for TDE: 190 keV, 150 keV, 120 keV

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous introduction of isospin-symmetry-breaking terms into the Skyrme energy density functional (Baczyk 2015) by systematically testing numerical stability and performing preliminary fits. It addresses the known failure of isospin-invariant Skyrme functionals (e.g., SV_T, SkM*, SLy4) to reproduce experimental mirror and triplet displacement energies, as noted by Nolen (1969) and Satula (2014). The results enable future no-core configuration-interaction calculations of isospin-symmetry-breaking effects in rotational bands and beta decays, as proposed by Sato (2016).
