# Crossover from skin-mode to proton-neutron-mode in $E1$ excitations of neutron-rich nuclei

> Character of the low-energy $E1$ excitations is investigated by analyzing transition densities obtained from the RPA calculations in the doubly-magic nuclei. We propose a decomposition method of the $E1$ excitations into the $pn$ mode (i.e. oscillation between protons and neutrons) and the skin mode (oscillation of the neutron skin against the inner core) via the transition densities, by which their mixing is handled in a straightforward manner. Crossover behavior of the $E1$ excitations is found, from the skin mode at low energy to the $pn$ mode at higher energy. The ratio of the skin-mode strength to the full strength turns out to be insensitive to nuclide and to effective interactions in the energy region of the crossover. Depending on the excitation energy, the observed low-energy $E1$ excitations are not necessarily dominated by the skin mode, as exemplified for $^{90}$Zr. fied for $^{90}$Zr.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that low-energy E1 excitations in neutron-rich nuclei exhibit a gradual crossover from skin-mode character (oscillation of the neutron skin against the inner core) at low energy to proton-neutron mode character (oscillation between protons and neutrons) at higher energy, rather than a sharp transition; the ratio of skin-mode strength to full strength is insensitive to nuclide and effective interaction in the crossover region, but observed low-energy E1 strengths are not necessarily dominated by the skin mode, as exemplified for $^{90}$Zr.

## Method

The authors performed Hartree-Fock plus random-phase approximation (HF+RPA) calculations for a set of doubly-magic nuclei using several non-relativistic effective interactions: Skyrme (SkM*, SkI2), Gogny (D1S, D1M), and M3Y-type (M3Y-P7). They introduced a position-dependent decomposition method that separates the E1 transition density into skin-mode and proton-neutron-mode components based on the ratio of proton to neutron transition densities at each radial point, using a threshold parameter $\lambda_s = 0.05$.

## Key numbers

- The energy at which the skin-mode ratio $R_\mathrm{skin}(\omega)$ decreases to 0.5 is $\omega \approx 10\,\mathrm{MeV}$ for all nuclei from $^{52}$Ca to $^{132}$Sn, with weak nucleus-dependence beyond the Ca region.
- A Lorentzian smearing width of $2\gamma = 1\,\mathrm{MeV}$ was adopted throughout the calculations.
- In $^{132}$Sn, the excitation at $\omega_\alpha = 8.4\,\mathrm{MeV}$ shows typical skin-mode transition densities, while the excitation at $\omega_\alpha = 12.7\,\mathrm{MeV}$ is dominated by the proton-neutron mode.
- In $^{90}$Zr, a shoulder structure in the strength function is found at $\omega \approx 13\,\mathrm{MeV}$, which corresponds to a good proton-neutron mode state.

## Context

This work extends systematic RPA studies of low-energy E1 strengths and their correlation to neutron skin thickness by Inakura, Nakada, and Yabana (2009), and addresses the ambiguity in interpreting pygmy dipole resonances (PDR) that was noted in earlier experimental studies by Savran et al. (2006, 2008) and Endres et al. (2009, 2010) using $(\alpha,\alpha^\prime\gamma)$ and $(\gamma,\gamma^\prime)$ reactions. The decomposition method enables quantitative analysis of mode mixing that was previously only treated qualitatively, and the finding that the crossover energy is nearly nucleus-independent from $^{52}$Ca to $^{132}$Sn provides a benchmark for future experimental measurements of transition densities, particularly for interpreting the character of observed PDRs in nuclei such as $^{90}$Zr.
