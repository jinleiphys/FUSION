# Analysis of the unbound spectrum of $^{12}$Li

> The unbound nucleus $^{12}$Li is evaluated by studying three-neutron one-proton excitations within the multistep shell model in the complex energy plane. It is found that the ground state of this system consists of an antibound $2^-$ state. A number of narrow states at low energy are found which ensue from the coupling of resonances in $^{11}$Li to continuum states close to threshold.

## Key claim

The ground state of the unbound nucleus $^{12}$Li is predicted to be an antibound $2^-$ state, and several narrow low-energy states arise from the coupling of resonances in $^{11}$Li to continuum states near threshold, but these results depend on the specific choice of single-particle representation and the identification of physically meaningful resonances via their localization and imaginary part.

## Method

The authors applied the multistep shell model in the complex energy plane (CXMSM) using a Berggren single-particle representation that included the antibound $1s_{1/2}$ state, the Gamow resonances $0p_{1/2}$ and $0d_{5/2}$, and discretized scattering states along two integration contours. Two-neutron states in $^{11}$Li were first evaluated using a separable interaction fitted to the experimental ground-state energy, then three-neutron states were constructed from the tensor product of one- and two-particle states, and finally one-proton three-neutron states were built to obtain the spectrum of $^{12}$Li.

## Key numbers

* The single-particle antibound state $1s_{1/2}$ was found at -0.050 MeV.
* The Gamow resonance $0p_{1/2}$ was found at (0.195, -0.047) MeV (real part, imaginary part).
* The Gamow resonance $0d_{5/2}$ was found at (2.731, -0.545) MeV.
* The bound state $0p_{3/2}$ was found at -2.589 MeV.
* The bound state $0s_{1/2}$ was found at -23.280 MeV.
* The two-neutron ground state of $^{11}$Li was calculated to have an angular-momentum composition of 46.8% s-states, 49.1% p-states, and 4.2% d-states.
* The experimental ground-state energy of $^{11}$Li used for fitting was -0.369 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier complex shell model (Gamow shell model) calculations by Michel et al. (2004) that successfully described light Li isotopes up to $^9$Li but could not include the crucial $1s_{1/2}$ antibound state for heavier isotopes. The authors build on the continuum shell model approach of Berggren (1991) and the multistep shell model method of Liotta (1982), incorporating both the antibound state and proton degrees of freedom that were neglected in previous treatments of $^{11}$Li. The results provide predictions for the unbound spectrum of $^{12}$Li that can guide future experiments at radioactive beam facilities.
