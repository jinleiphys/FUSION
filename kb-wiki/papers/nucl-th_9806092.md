# Band Crossing studied by GCM with 3D-CHFB

> LaTeX 3 pages, 3 eps figures; Contribution to International Conference, Nuclear Structure at the extreme,Lewes, UK, (1998) Jun.17-19

## Key claim

The authors find that in the improved calculation, band crossing between the g-band, s-band, and high-K bands occurs at a higher angular momentum (J ~ 20 ℏ) than previously thought, and that the tilted axis rotation (TAR) state is confirmed but with a shallower potential depth than in earlier work. The abstract glosses over the fact that the results depend on a specific reduction of the neutron pairing strength to make neutron rotation alignment occur in principal axis rotation (PAR), which is considered the yrast state.

## Method

The authors performed three-dimensional cranked Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (3D-CHFB) calculations for the nucleus under study, using a reduced neutron pairing strength compared to previous work. They then applied the generator coordinate method (GCM) with the north and south latitudes as the generator coordinate, solving a constrained Hill-Wheeler equation that includes terms for the squared angular momentum and particle numbers to improve stability. The rotating axis was tilted along the prime meridian at J = 18 ℏ, and PAR solutions were obtained by cranking up and down along the x-axis perpendicular to the symmetry axis.

## Key numbers

- Band crossing occurs at J ~ 20 ℏ in the present calculation.
- The TAR minimum takes place at θ = 24° with a depth of V_TAR = -0.166 MeV, which is less than half of the previous value.
- Energy differences among the GCM solutions are within the range of 10 keV to 100 keV at J = 18 ℏ.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of wobbling motion and band crossing in 180W, 182Os, and 184Os nuclei, using 3D-CHFB and GCM methods (Horibata and Onishi, 1994; Horibata, Oi, and Onishi, 1995). The improved calculation corrects an earlier parameter set where proton pairing was destroyed before neutron pairing, and now allows neutron rotation alignment to occur in PAR, consistent with experimental evidence that back-bending is due to neutron orbitals (Neskakis et al., 1976). The results enable further investigation of a new type of seesaw vibrational mode of proton and neutron pairing occurring through wobbling motion, and suggest that band mixing is a key concept for understanding multi-band crossing in this mass region.
