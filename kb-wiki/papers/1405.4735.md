# Polynomial fits and the proton radius puzzle

> The Proton Radius Puzzle refers to the ~7σ discrepancy that exists between the proton charge radius determined from muonic hydrogen and that determined from electronic hydrogen spectroscopy and electron-proton scattering. One possible partial resolution to the puzzle includes errors in the extraction of the proton radius from ep elastic scattering data. This possibility is made plausible by certain fits which extract a smaller proton radius from the scattering data consistent with that determined from muonic hydrogen. The reliability of some of these fits that yield a smaller proton radius was studied. We found that fits of form factor data with a truncated polynomial fit are unreliable and systematically give values for the proton radius that are too small. Additionally, a polynomial fit with a χ^2_{reduced} ~ 1 is not a sufficient indication for a reliable result.

## Key claim

Truncated polynomial fits of electron-proton elastic scattering form factor data are unreliable and systematically yield proton charge radii that are too small, even when the fit has a reduced chi-squared near unity.

## Method

The authors generated pseudodata from six known parameterizations of the proton electric form factor (AMT, Arrington, Bernauer, standard dipole, Kelly, and LHM) with 0.2% uncertainties and a data-point density matching the Bernauer dataset. They then fit these pseudodata with polynomials of order 1 through 4 in Q^2 over varying Q^2_max ranges, repeating each fit 5000 times to extract distributions of the extracted radius, its uncertainty, and the reduced chi-squared.

## Key numbers

- The muonic hydrogen result gives r_p = 0.84087 ± 0.00039 fm, while CODATA 2010 gives r_p = 0.8775 ± 0.0051 fm, a ~7σ discrepancy.
- The Bernauer et al. ep scattering data give r_p = 0.879 ± 0.008 fm; the Zhan et al. data give r_p = 0.875 ± 0.010 fm.
- For a cubic fit to AMT pseudodata with Q^2_max = 0.24 GeV^2, the reduced chi-squared is 1.018, but the extracted radius of 0.859 ± 0.002 fm differs from the true 0.878 fm by 0.019 fm, about 10 times the fit uncertainty.
- Even fits with reduced chi-squared < 1.1 can produce a truncation offset equal to the ~0.037 fm difference between the ep and muonic hydrogen radius determinations.
- For the upcoming Jefferson Lab E12-11-106 experiment, a linear fit to pseudodata yields a truncation offset of 0.016 fm (Arrington parameterization) to 0.025 fm (AMT parameterization), with reduced chi-squared ≈ 1.1.

## Context

This work extends earlier criticisms by Sick (2003) and unpublished work by Distler, who argued that higher-order terms in the Taylor expansion prevent a precise radius determination from polynomial fits. The study directly addresses the Proton Radius Puzzle by examining whether the smaller radii extracted by Griffioen and Carlson (using a truncated linear polynomial fit) and others could be artifacts of the fitting method. The results enable a more critical assessment of radius extractions from ep scattering and inform the analysis strategies for upcoming experiments such as Jefferson Lab E12-11-106 and the MUSE experiment at PSI, which will compare muon-proton and electron-proton scattering.
