# Chiral anomaly and the pion properties in the light-front quark model

> We explore the link between the chiral symmetry of QCD and the numerical results of the light-front quark model, analyzing both the two-point and three-point functions of the pion. Including the axial-vector coupling as well as the pseudoscalar coupling in the light-front quark model, we discuss the implication of the chiral anomaly in describing the pion decay constant, the pion-photon transition form factor and the electromagnetic form factor of the pion. In constraining the model parameters, we find that the chiral anomaly plays a critical role and the analysis of $F_{πγ}(Q^2)$ in timelike region is important. Our results indicate that the constituent quark picture is effective for the low and high $Q^2$ ranges implementing the quark mass evolution effect as $Q^2$ grows.

## Key claim

The light-front quark model can describe pion properties consistently with the chiral anomaly only when the axial-vector coupling parameter \(B_\pi\) is negative (\(B_\pi < 0\)), and the probability of the lowest Fock state \(P_{Q\bar{Q}}\) must diminish in the chiral limit to simultaneously reproduce the experimental pion decay constant and the \(\pi^0 \to \gamma\gamma\) decay width.

## Method

The authors extended the standard light-front quark model by including both pseudoscalar (\(A_\pi \gamma_5\)) and axial-vector (\(B_\pi \not{P} \gamma_5\)) couplings in the pion-quark vertex \(\Gamma_\pi = (A_\pi + B_\pi \not{P})\gamma_5\). They computed the pion decay constant \(f_\pi\), the pion-photon transition form factor \(F_{\pi\gamma}(Q^2)\) in both spacelike and timelike regions, and the pion electromagnetic form factor \(F_\pi(Q^2)\) using the valence \(Q\bar{Q}\) light-front wave function with a harmonic oscillator radial wave function. Model parameters were constrained by fitting simultaneously to the experimental \(f_\pi\) and \(F_{\pi\gamma}(0)\) values for various quark masses.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant: \(f^{\rm Exp}_\pi = 130.2(1.7)\) MeV used as experimental input.
* Transition form factor at zero momentum: \(F^{\rm Exp}_{\pi\gamma}(0) = 0.272(3)\) GeV\(^{-1}\) used as experimental input; the chiral anomaly prediction is \(F^{\rm ABJ}_{\pi\gamma}(0) = 0.276\) GeV\(^{-1}\) for \(f_\pi \approx 130\) MeV.
* Reference parameter set: \((M_\pi, m, \beta) = (0.135, 0.255, 0.1980)\) GeV with \((B_\pi, P_{Q\bar{Q}}) = (-0.25, 1)\).
* Chiral limit decay constant: \(f^{\rm chiral}_\pi = \sqrt{P_{Q\bar{Q}}} \frac{\sqrt{3} \beta}{2^{3/4} \pi^{1/4}} \Gamma(\frac{5}{4})\).
* Chiral limit transition form factor at \(Q^2=0\): \(F^{\rm chiral}_{\pi\gamma}(0) = \sqrt{P_{Q\bar{Q}}} \frac{\Gamma(5/4)}{2\sqrt{3}(2\pi)^{1/4}\beta}\).
* Asymptotic scaling: \(Q^2 F_{\pi\gamma}(Q^2) = \sqrt{2} f_\pi \approx 0.185\) GeV in the Brodsky-Lepage limit.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous light-front quark model (Choi 1997, Choi 2007, Choi 2017) by adding an axial-vector coupling to the pion vertex, which was absent in the standard invariant-mass scheme using only \(\Gamma_\pi = A_\pi \gamma_5\). The inclusion of the axial-vector coupling allows the model to reach a well-defined chiral limit and reproduce the Adler-Bell-Jackiw chiral anomaly, which determines \(F_{\pi\gamma}(0)\). The chiral-limit pion distribution amplitude matches the anti-de Sitter/conformal field theory prediction of the asymptotic distribution amplitude (Brodsky and de Teramond 2008). The results enable future spectroscopic analyses of both pseudoscalar and vector meson nonets within the same framework.
