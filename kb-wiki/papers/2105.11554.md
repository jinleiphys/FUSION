# Gauge invariance of meson photo- and electroproduction currents revisited

> An exact expression is derived for the meson production current off the nucleon for real and virtual photons which cleanly separates, in a model-independent manner, a tree-level expression that manifestly preserves local gauge invariance in terms of a generalized Ward-Takahashi identity from transverse final-state-interaction (FSI) terms. As discussed in some detail, this exact formulation is particularly well suited for implementing approximation schemes of exchange-current and FSI contributions (which in practice generally will be necessary) at any level of sophistication without violating local gauge invariance.

## Key claim

The paper derives an exact reformulation of the meson production current that separates manifestly gauge-invariant tree-level terms from transverse final-state-interaction (FSI) terms, such that any approximation made within the transverse part will preserve local gauge invariance without requiring additional repair schemes.

## Method

The production current \(M^\mu\) for meson photo- and electroproduction off the nucleon is decomposed into a gauge-invariant-preserving (GIP) tree-level part (containing \(s\)-, \(u\)-, \(t\)-channel Born terms plus a contact-type GIP current \(C^\mu\)) and a transverse remainder containing exchange currents and FSI. The key step is subtracting the transverse part of \(C^\mu\) inside the transverse bracket \([\cdots]_\Trans\) to avoid double counting, making the formulation model-independent and exact. The resulting expression (Eq. 47) ensures that any approximation of the transverse FSI and exchange-current contributions leaves the generalized Ward-Takahashi identity (gWTI) satisfied.

## Key numbers

* The generalized Ward-Takahashi identity (gWTI) for the production current is given by \(k_\mu M^\mu = -F_s S \hat{Q}_N S^{-1} + S^{-1} \hat{Q}_N S F_u + t_\pi^{-1} \hat{Q}_\pi t_\pi F_t\) (Eq. 27), which reduces to \(k_\mu M^\mu = 0\) for external on-shell hadrons.
* The longitudinal contact current \(C^\mu_\Long\) is defined as \(C^\mu_\Long = \frac{k^\mu}{k^2} \left(-F_s \hat{Q}_N + \hat{Q}_N F_u + \hat{Q}_\pi F_t\right)\) (Eq. 33), containing a \(1/k^2\) singularity.
* The phenomenological GIP current \(C^\mu\) must satisfy \(k_\mu C^\mu = -F_s \hat{Q}_N + \hat{Q}_N F_u + \hat{Q}_\pi F_t\) (Eq. 40) to ensure the gWTI.

## Context

This work extends the field-theory framework of Haberzettl (1997, 1998) for electromagnetic meson production, which generalized earlier gauge-invariance repair schemes by Drell and Lee (1972), Ohta (1989), and Gross and Riska (1987). The new exact formulation shows that the separation of GIP tree-level terms from transverse FSI terms is an exact consequence of gauge invariance, not merely an approximation as suggested in Haberzettl (2006). This enables consistent treatment of off-shell single-meson production currents as input for more complex reactions such as two-meson production (Haberzettl, 2019), strangeness production, and \(NN\) bremsstrahlung, without violating local gauge invariance at any level of approximation.
