# Hadronic molecules with a ${\bar{D}}$ meson in a medium

> We study the effect of a hot and dense medium on the binding energy of hadronic molecules with open-charm mesons. We focus on a recent chiral quark-model-based prediction of a molecular state in the $N \bar D$ system. We analyze how the two-body thresholds and the hadron-hadron interactions are modified when quark and meson masses and quark-meson couplings change in a function of the temperature and baryon density according to predictions of the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model. We find that in some cases the molecular binding is enhanced in medium as compared to their free-space binding. We discuss the consequences of our findings for the search for exotic hadrons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions as well as in the forthcoming facilities FAIR or J-PARC.

## Key claim

The binding energy of the predicted $(I)J^P=(1)5/2^-$ $\Delta \bar D^*$ molecular state is enhanced in a hot and dense medium compared to free space, but this enhancement only becomes significant for temperatures above approximately 0.1 GeV, with almost no variation below that temperature.

## Method

The authors used the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model to compute the temperature and baryon density dependence of constituent quark masses, pion and sigma meson masses, and quark-meson coupling constants. These in-medium quantities were then used as inputs to the chiral constituent quark model (CCQM) of Carames (2012) to calculate the masses of $\bar D$, $\bar D^*$, $N$, and $\Delta$ hadrons and their interactions. The Lippmann-Schwinger equation was solved for the $\Delta \bar D^*$ system by evaluating the Fredholm determinant at zero energy to determine the existence and binding energy of bound states.

## Key numbers

- The free-space binding energy of the $(I)J^P=(1)5/2^-$ $\Delta \bar D^*$ state is 1.35 MeV.
- The binding energy increases up to around 30 MeV for the hardest medium conditions considered (highest temperature and chemical potential).
- The vacuum constituent quark mass obtained from the NJL model is $M_q = 328$ MeV, with vacuum pion mass $m_\pi = 142$ MeV and sigma mass $m_\sigma = 663$ MeV.
- The NJL model parameters used were: current quark mass $m_q = 5$ MeV, $G\Lambda^2 = 2.14$, and cutoff $\Lambda = 653$ MeV, yielding $\langle\bar{u}u\rangle = \langle\bar{d}d\rangle = -(252\ \text{MeV})^3$ and $f_\pi = 94$ MeV.
- Lattice QCD simulations at almost physical pion masses ($m_\pi = 161$ MeV) show a drastic decrease of the quark condensate around $T_{\rm pc} = 154 \pm 9$ MeV at zero baryon density.

## Context

This work extends the chiral constituent quark model prediction of a $\Delta \bar D^*$ molecular state by Carames (2012) to finite temperature and baryon density, using the NJL model framework of Nambu and Jona-Lasinio (1961) to incorporate medium effects. The study is motivated by the possibility of producing such exotic hadrons in high-energy heavy-ion collisions via coalescence mechanisms, as discussed by Cho and collaborators, and by planned experiments at FAIR (PANDA Collaboration) and J-PARC. The results enable future investigations of coalescence dynamics for molecule formation in heavy-ion collisions and provide predictions for D-wave resonances in $D$ meson-nucleon scattering that could be measured at these facilities.
