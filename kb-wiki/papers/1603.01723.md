# Nuclear fusion induced by X-rays in a crystal

> The nuclei that constitute a crystalline lattice, oscillate relative to each other with a very low energy that is not sufficient to penetrate through the Coulomb barriers separating them. An additional energy, which is needed to tunnel through the barrier and fuse, can be supplied by external electromagnetic waves (X-rays or the synchrotron radiation). Exposing to the X-rays the solid compound LiD (lithium-deuteride) for the duration of 111 hours, we have detected 88 events of the nuclear fusion d+Li6 ---> Be8*. Our theoretical estimate agrees with what we observed. One of possible applications of the phenomenon we found, could be the measurements of the rates of various nuclear reactions (not necessarily fusion) at extremely low energies inaccessible in accelerator experiments.

## Key claim

Exposing a LiD crystal to X-rays can excite nuclear oscillations to energies where tunneling through the Coulomb barrier becomes detectable, yielding approximately 88 fusion events over 111 hours of irradiation, though the rate remains very low (roughly one event per half hour).

## Method

A powder sample of lithium deuteride (LiD) with natural lithium isotopic composition and deuterium enrichment was irradiated with X-rays in the energy range of 15–100 keV for 111 hours. The fusion reaction d + ⁶Li → ⁸Be* was identified by detecting alpha particles from the dominant decay branch ⁸Be* → α + α, each with 11.1863 MeV energy. The observed event rate was compared to a theoretical model that treats the crystal as an infinite square well potential, computes stimulated and spontaneous transition rates among nuclear oscillation levels under X-ray exposure, and averages the tunneling probability over the resulting stationary population distribution.

## Key numbers

- 88 fusion events detected over 111 hours of X-ray irradiation of the LiD crystal.
- The fusion reaction d + ⁶Li → ⁸Be* proceeds via a resonance at 22.2 MeV with total width Γ ≈ 0.80 MeV; the partial width for αα decay is Γ_α = 0.77 MeV, giving a branching ratio of 96% into the αα channel.
- Each alpha particle from the fusion carries 11.1863 MeV kinetic energy.
- The Coulomb barrier height between d and ⁶Li in the crystal is estimated as V_max ≈ 0.922 MeV.
- The ground-state oscillation energies in the square well are E₁(d) ≈ 0.6 meV and E₁(⁶Li) ≈ 0.2 meV; at room temperature the average oscillation energies are ⟨E⟩_d ≈ 14.2 meV and ⟨E⟩_Li ≈ 13.6 meV.
- The sample mass was M = 0.61 g, with deuterium fraction f₂ = 0.98 and ⁶Li fraction f₆ = 0.0759.

## Context

This work extends the concept of muon-catalyzed fusion (Jackson, Rafelski; Jones; JINR) by using X-rays instead of muons to enhance tunneling probabilities in a solid-state environment. It directly follows an earlier upper bound on spontaneous fusion in LiD crystals of ~10⁻⁴⁸ s⁻¹ per nuclear pair (Belyaev 2006). The demonstrated technique could enable measurements of nuclear reaction rates at extremely low energies inaccessible to accelerator experiments, though the observed rate is far too low for energy production.
