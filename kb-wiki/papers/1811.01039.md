# Effective density functionals beyond mean field

> I present a review on non relativistic effective energy--density functionals (EDFs). An introductory part is dedicated to traditional phenomenological functionals employed for mean--field--type applications and to several extensions and implementations that have been suggested over the years to generalize such functionals, up to the most recent ideas. The heart of this review is then focused on density functionals designed for beyond--mean--field models. Examples of these studies are discussed. Starting from these investigations, some illustrations of {\it{ab--initio}}--based or {\it{ab--initio}}--inspired functionals are provided. Constructing functionals by building bridges with {\it{ab--initio}} models represents an extremely challenging and timely objective. This will eventually reduce/eliminate the empirical character of EDFs and link them with the underlying theory of QCD. Conclusions are presented in the last part of the review.

## Key claim

This review argues that while traditional phenomenological energy-density functionals (EDFs) have been successful for mean-field calculations, a new generation of functionals specifically designed for beyond-mean-field models is needed, and that constructing ab-initio-based or ab-initio-inspired functionals represents an extremely challenging and timely objective that will eventually reduce or eliminate the empirical character of EDFs and link them with the underlying theory of QCD.

## Method

The paper provides a comprehensive review of non-relativistic effective energy-density functionals (EDFs), beginning with traditional phenomenological functionals (Skyrme and Gogny forces) used in mean-field applications, then examining various extensions and generalizations proposed over the years. The review focuses on density functionals designed for beyond-mean-field models, including discussions of ab-initio-based or ab-initio-inspired functionals, and covers topics such as density-dependent terms, higher-order derivative expansions (N$^l$LO pseudopotentials), DFT-like functionals, and regularization procedures for pathologies encountered in beyond-mean-field calculations.

## Key numbers

- The Skyrme interaction contains 10 adjustable parameters ($t_0$, $x_0$, $t_1$, $x_1$, $t_2$, $x_2$, $t_3$, $x_3$, $\alpha$, and $W_0$)
- The Gogny interaction contains 14 adjustable parameters ($W_i$, $B_i$, $H_i$, $M_i$, $\mu_i$ for $i=1,2$, $t_3$, $x_3$, $\alpha$, and $W_0$)
- The SIII Skyrme EOS for neutron matter strongly deviates from the Akmal et al. results at densities larger than 0.12 fm$^{-3}$
- The N$^3$LO EDF contains derivatives of the density up to sixth order
- The N$^2$LO pseudopotential adds 4 additional parameters ($t_1^{(4)}$, $x_1^{(4)}$, $t_2^{(4)}$, $x_2^{(4)}$) and the N$^3$LO pseudopotential adds 4 more ($t_1^{(6)}$, $x_1^{(6)}$, $t_2^{(6)}$, $x_2^{(6)}$)
- Without a density-dependent term, N$^2$LO and N$^3$LO pseudopotentials gave a very low effective mass of $m^*/m = 0.4$; adding a density-dependent term yielded a more acceptable effective mass of 0.7
- The SeaLL1 functional contains seven adjustable parameters
- The D2 Gogny interaction has 6 parameters for the density-dependent term (instead of 3 for the traditional zero-range form)

## Context

This review extends the extensive body of work on nuclear EDF theory that began in the 1970s with the first mean-field calculations by Vautherin and Brink, and covers developments up to recent optimization efforts such as the UNEDF2 functional by Kortelainen et al. (2014). The paper discusses how traditional functionals like Skyrme and Gogny forces have been generalized through tensor terms (following work by Colo et al., Brink, and others), higher-order derivative expansions using the density-matrix expansion originally introduced by Negele and Vautherin (1972, 1975), and DFT-like approaches such as the Fayans functional and the BCPM functional by Baldo et al. The review enables future work on constructing ab-initio-based functionals that bridge the gap between phenomenological EDFs and the underlying theory of QCD, with particular attention to regularizing pathologies that arise in beyond-mean-field calculations due to density-dependent terms.
