# General relativity effects in precision spin experimental tests of fundamental symmetries

> A search for the $P$- and $CP(T)$-violating electric dipole moments (EDM) of atoms, particles and nuclei with sensitivity up to $10^{-15}$ in units of magnetic dipole moments, allowed by all discrete symmetries, is one of the topical problems of modern physics. According to Sakharov, $CP$-violation is one of the three key criteria of baryogenesis in generally accepted paradigm of the Big Bang cosmology. All three criteria are supported by the Standard Model (SM), but it fails to describe the observed baryon asymmetry of the Universe. This is regarded a strong argument in favor of existence of $CP$-symmetry breaking mechanisms beyond minimal SM, which can lead to measurable EDMs of atoms, particles and nuclei. Direct searches for EDM of charged particles and nuclei are possible only in storage rings (COSY, NICA). After successful studies by the JEDI collaboration at the COSY synchrotron, at the forefront is a search for the proton EDM in an electrostatic storage ring with the proton spin frozen at the magic energy with projected sensitivity $d_p\sim 10^{-29}\,e\cdot$cm. Following a brief introduction to the $CP$-violation physics and the baryogenesis, the review presents a detailed discussion of significant contributions to the spin dynamics from the terrestrial gravity along with the new effects of Earth's rotation in ultrasensitive searches for the EDM of charged particles and neutrons. Quite remarkably, for the projected sensitivity to the proton EDM, these false EDM effects can by one to two orders of magnitude exceed the signal of the proton EDM, and become comparable to an EDM contribution in experiments with ultracold neutrons. We also discuss the role of a precessing spin as a detector of the axion-like dark matter, and consider applications of the quantum gravitational anomalies to the dense matter hydrodynamics and the spin phenomena in the non-central nuclear collisions.

## Key claim

Terrestrial gravity and Earth's rotation produce false electric dipole moment (EDM) signals that, at the projected sensitivity of proton EDM searches (d_p ~ 10^{-29} e·cm), can exceed the true EDM signal by one to two orders of magnitude, and become comparable to EDM contributions in ultracold neutron experiments.

## Method

The authors derive the spin dynamics of charged particles in storage rings using the classical Thomas-Bargmann-Michel-Telegdi (T-BMT) equation extended to include general relativistic corrections from Earth's gravitational field and rotation. They analyze the de Sitter (geodetic) precession, the Lense-Thirring (frame-dragging) effect, and the indirect gravitational effect from focusing electromagnetic fields required to compensate gravity in storage rings. The formalism is applied to the frozen spin mode in electrostatic storage rings and to ultracold neutron experiments.

## Key numbers

- Projected sensitivity for proton EDM search: d_p ~ 10^{-29} e·cm, corresponding to relative sensitivity η_p^edm ~ 10^{-15}
- Relative gravitational radius of Earth: η_g = r_g/R_⊕ = 1.39 × 10^{-9}
- Equatorial velocity of Earth's rotation in units of speed of light: η_⊕ = ω_⊕ R_⊕/c = 1.55 × 10^{-6}
- In a proton storage ring with radius ρ ~ 80 m, the velocity due to Earth's rotation: η_ω = ω_⊕ ρ/c ~ 2 × 10^{-11}
- EDM-induced spin angular velocity in frozen spin electrostatic storage ring: Ω_s ~ 10^{-9} rad/s
- False EDM signal from gravity in all-electric frozen spin proton rings: η_fake^edm ~ 2 × 10^{-14}
- Current neutron EDM upper limit: |d_n| < 1.8 × 10^{-26} e·cm (PSI experiment)
- Mercury atom EDM upper limit: |d_Hg| < 7.4 × 10^{-30} e·cm
- Electron EDM upper bound from ThO molecule: |d_e| < 1.1 × 10^{-29} e·cm
- Observed baryon-to-photon ratio: η_B = n_B/n_γ = (6.12 ± 0.04) × 10^{-10}
- Planck mass: M_P = 1.22 × 10^{19} GeV/c^2

## Context

This review extends the work of Silenko, Teryaev, Obukhov, and collaborators (2006 onward) who first identified the indirect gravitational effect from focusing fields in storage rings, and the work of Vergeles (2019-2020) on geometric magnetic fields from Earth's rotation in electrostatic systems. It builds upon the JEDI collaboration's successful spin dynamics studies at COSY synchrotron and supports the proposed PTR (Prototype Test Ring) storage ring at CERN as a precursor to a dedicated frozen-spin proton EDM ring. The paper also connects to axion dark matter detection via spin precession (Graham 2011, Budker, Sikivie) and to gravitational quantum anomaly applications in heavy-ion collision hydrodynamics relevant to NICA experiments.
