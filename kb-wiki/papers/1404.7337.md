# Efimov spectrum in bosonic systems with increasing number of particles

> It is well-known that three-boson systems show the Efimov effect when the two-body scattering length $a$ is large with respect to the range of the two-body interaction. This effect is a manifestation of a discrete scaling invariance (DSI). In this work we study DSI in the $N$-body system by analysing the spectrum of $N$ identical bosons obtained with a pairwise gaussian interaction close to the unitary limit. We consider different universal ratios such as $E_N^0/E_3^0$ and $E_N^1/E_N^0$, with $E_N^i$ being the energy of the ground ($i=0$) and first-excited ($i=1$) state of the system, for $N\le16$. We discuss the extension of the Efimov radial law, derived by Efimov for $N=3$, to general $N$.

# Efimov spectrum in bosonic systems with increasing number of particles

## Key claim
The discrete scaling invariance (DSI) known from the three-boson Efimov effect persists in systems of up to 16 identical bosons, and the Efimov radial law can be extended to general N, provided the two-body scattering length is large and the system is studied at fixed values of the polar angle ξ in the (1/a, κ) plane.

## Method
The authors solved the Schrödinger equation for N identical bosons (N ≤ 16) using a two-body Gaussian (TBG) potential with fixed range r₀ = 10 a₀, varying the potential strength V₀ to explore the (a⁻¹, κ) plane near the unitary limit. They employed the Hyperspherical Harmonic (HH) method as formulated in Ref. [natasha] and extended earlier calculations up to N=6 (Gattobigio 2012) to N=16. The energy spectrum was analyzed in polar coordinates (H_N, ξ) to extract universal ratios and test the extension of the Efimov radial law.

## Key numbers
- The three-body Efimov ratio E₃^{n+1}/E₃^n equals e^{-2π/s₀} ≈ 1/515.03, with s₀ a universal number.
- For the four-body system at the unitary limit, the ratios are E₄⁰/E₃⁰ ≈ 4.6 and E₄¹/E₃⁰ ≈ 1.001.
- The generalized radial law at ξ = -π gives κ⁰_N a_N⁻ ≈ -1.56(5), using the prediction from the effective field theory universal function Δ(ξ) from Ref. [report].
- The linear relation κ⁰_N/κ⁰_3 = 1 + γ(N-3) has γ = (κ⁰_4/κ⁰_3) - 1, with κ⁰_4/κ⁰_3 ≈ 1.147 extracted from Deltuva (2010), yielding κ⁰_N/κ⁰_3 = 1 + 1.147(N-3).

## Context
This work extends the study of the Efimov effect from three bosons (Efimov 1970) to larger N-body systems, building on earlier calculations for up to six bosons (Gattobigio 2012) and four-body universal ratios (Deltuva 2010). The results enable experimental tests in trapped atomic gases where the scattering length can be tuned via Feshbach resonances (Ferlaino 2011), and they provide a framework for understanding how discrete scaling invariance manifests in systems with more than three particles. The linear relation between κ⁰_N and N also connects to earlier observations of quadratic energy scaling with N (Nicholson 2012), though with different constants.
