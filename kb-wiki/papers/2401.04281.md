# Comments to the paper "Detailed study of the astrophysical direct capture reaction $^6$Li($p,γ$)$^7$Be in a potential model approach" by E. M. Tursunov, S. A. Turakulov, and K. I. Tursunmakhatov, PHYSICAL REVIEW C 108, 065801 (2023)

> We explicitly present the comparison of the results for the astrophysical S-factor and reaction rate for the $^6$Li($p,γ$)$^7$Be capture process at astrophysical energies, presented in Phys. Rev. Phys. Rev. C 105, 065806 (2022) and Phys. Rev. C 108. 065801 (2023) obtained within the famework of potential models. We demonstrate that both potential model approaches describe successfully the astrophysical S-factor and reaction rate simultaneously and reproduce the LUNA Collaboration [Phys. Rev. C 102, 052802(R) (2020)] results.

## Key claim
Both potential model approaches from Dubovichenko et al. (2022) and Tursunov et al. (2023) simultaneously describe the astrophysical S-factor and reaction rate for the 6Li(p,γ)7Be capture process within the uncertainties of the LUNA Collaboration (2020) data, contradicting the claim in Tursunov et al. (2023) that no potential model had achieved this.

## Method
The authors compared the astrophysical S-factor and reaction rate calculations from their own potential model (Dubovichenko et al., 2022) with those from Tursunov et al. (2023) and experimental data including the LUNA Collaboration (2020) measurements. They plotted the S-factor results against seven experimental datasets and compared reaction rates normalized to the NACRE rate (Angulo 1999) over the temperature range T9 = 0.001–1.

## Key numbers
- The deviation between the adopted reaction rate from LUNA (2020) and the calculation from Dubovichenko et al. (2022) does not exceed 5% in the temperature range T9 = 0.001–1.
- The deviation of the results from Tursunov et al. (2023) in the temperature range T9 = 0.001–0.015 is larger than the 5% deviation from Dubovichenko et al. (2022).
- Both potential model results fall within the error bars of the LUNA Collaboration (2020) data for the astrophysical S-factor.

## Context
This comment addresses the claim by Tursunov et al. (2023) that no potential model could simultaneously describe the astrophysical S-factor and reaction rates from the LUNA Collaboration (2020). The authors demonstrate that their own potential model (Dubovichenko et al., 2022) already achieved this, and that Tursunov et al. (2023) used only slight modifications of those potentials. The comparison enables clarification of which potential model approaches are viable for the 6Li(p,γ)7Be reaction, which is important for Big Bang nucleosynthesis and solar neutrino physics.
