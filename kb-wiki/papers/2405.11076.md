# Magnetic Weyl semimetals as a source of circularly polarized THz radiation

> We propose to use the electromagnetic radiation induced by a few MeV electron beam in magnetic Weyl semimetals as a source of the circularly polarized photons in the THz frequency range.

## Key claim

Magnetic Weyl semimetals can serve as a practical source of circularly polarized THz radiation via the Chiral Cherenkov mechanism when irradiated by a few MeV electron beam, provided the electron momentum is aligned with the effective node-separation vector b; the radiation power vanishes for perpendicular incidence.

## Method

The authors compute the photon dispersion relation in a Weyl semimetal with anomalous Hall current j_AH = b x E, finding a gapless branch with negative effective mass squared that enables Chiral Cherenkov radiation. They derive the photon emission rate from the transition amplitude e- -> e- gamma using the standard quantum field theory procedure, then evaluate the radiated power spectrum and angular distribution in two regimes: a high-energy approximation (E >> m, omega >> b, omega_p) and the exact case of electron incidence parallel to b.

## Key numbers

- In Co3Sn2S2, the effective b-parameter is b = 3.8 eV (from Zubkov (2023) and Wang (2018)).
- A typical electron bunch contains N = 10^11 electrons, equivalent to 16 nC charge.
- The total radiated power in the high-energy approximation is P = (alpha b^2 cos^2 beta E^2) / (2 m^2), scaling as b^2.
- The UV cutoff of the spectrum scales as (E^2 f) / m^2, where f = lambda b cos beta.
- For the non-magnetic Weyl semimetal TaAs, the node separation is Delta = 80 eV, giving b = 0.19 eV.
- The plasma frequency used in calculations is omega_p = 0.6 meV.
- The chiral magnetic conductivity is sigma_chi = 4.6 x 10^(-3) mu_5, where mu_5 is the axial chemical potential.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Chiral Cherenkov radiation, previously studied in quark-gluon plasma and general chiral media by Tuchin (2018) and Hansen (2020), to the specific material platform of magnetic Weyl semimetals. It directly compares the predicted radiation intensity to conventional bremsstrahlung in Co3Sn2S2 using the standard bremsstrahlung cross-section from Workman (2022). The results enable a practical source of circularly polarized THz photons, with the magnetic Weyl semimetal Co3Sn2S2 (b = 3.8 eV) offering significantly higher power than non-magnetic Weyl semimetals like TaAs (b = 0.19 eV) or Dirac semimetals with field-induced chiral chemical potential.
