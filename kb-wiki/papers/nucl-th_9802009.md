# Compactness of the set of Faddeev and Lippmann--Schwinger equations for the three-body Coulomb problem

> The set of Faddeev and Lippmann--Schwinger integral equations for three-body systems involving Coulomb interactions deduced from a ``three-potential'' picture are shown to be compact for all energies and a method of solution is given.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the set of Faddeev and Lippmann-Schwinger integral equations for three-body Coulomb scattering, derived from a "three-potential" picture using Kato's chain rule, possess compact kernels and thus have unique solutions for all energies, provided the long-range part of the Coulomb interaction is split so that it does not support bound states (a condition satisfied when all Coulomb interactions are repulsive, or for energies below the three-body breakup threshold when some are attractive).

## Method

The author splits each two-body Coulomb-like subsystem interaction into short-range and long-range parts, then introduces two intermediate Hamiltonians (a channel long-range Hamiltonian and a channel distorted long-range Hamiltonian) to apply Kato's chain rule for wave operators. This yields a three-step factorization of the Moller operator: a two-body scattering in a Coulomb-like potential, a Lippmann-Schwinger equation with a kernel of shorter range than Coulomb (ensuring compactness), and finally Faddeev-Noble integral equations whose compactness was previously shown by Merkuriev (1980). The resulting integral equations are solved numerically using a Coulomb-Sturmian (CS) basis representation, where the three-body Green's operator is expressed as a contour integral convolution of two-body Green's operators, with a contour valid for all complex energies including those above breakup threshold.

## Key numbers

- The auxiliary potential u_alpha^{(l)} has the asymptotic form u_alpha^{(l)} ~ e_alpha(e_beta+e_gamma)/eta_alpha as eta_alpha approaches infinity, where e_alpha, e_beta, e_gamma are the charges of the three particles.
- The CS basis truncation uses indices n, nu = 0, 1, 2, ..., N, with the completeness relation taking the form 1 = lim_{N to infinity} sum_{n,nu=0}^N |widetilde{n nu l lambda}>_alpha {}_alpha<{n nu l lambda}|.
- The contour integral representation of the three-body Green's operator uses a contour C taken counterclockwise around the continuous spectrum of h_{eta_alpha}, with the upper part descending to the second Riemann sheet of g_{eta_alpha} in the epsilon to 0 limit.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous Coulomb-Sturmian (CS) method, which had been applied successfully to bound-state problems with repulsive (Papp and Plessas, 1996) and attractive (Papp, 1997) Coulomb interactions, and to p-d scattering below breakup (Papp, 1997), to energies above the breakup threshold. It provides an alternative to the two existing approaches: the Alt-Grassberger-Sandhas (AGS) equations solved by screening and renormalization (reviewed by Alt and Sandhas, 1996), and the Faddeev-Noble differential equations solved in configuration space with approximate boundary conditions (reviewed by Friar and Payne, 1996), both of which have had only limited solutions available below or above the breakup threshold. The method enables calculations of scattering, bound-state, and resonant-state problems with exact Coulomb asymptotics automatically incorporated.
