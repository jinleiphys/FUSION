# Quantum kinetic theory with interactions for massive vector bosons

> We present a derivation of quantum kinetic theory for massive spin-1 particles from the Wigner-function formalism up to first order in an $\hbar$-expansion, including a general interaction term. Both local and nonlocal contributions are computed in a covariant fashion. It is shown that, up to first order in $\hbar$, the collision term takes the same form as in the case of spin-1/2 particles.

## Key claim

The authors derive a quantum kinetic theory for massive spin-1 particles from the Wigner-function formalism up to first order in an $\hbar$-expansion, showing that the collision term takes the same form as for spin-1/2 particles, but only under the assumption that no initial large vector or tensor polarization is present.

## Method

The authors start from the Lagrangian for a charged vector field and define the Wigner function via field bilinears, then derive equations of motion using Bopp operators. They perform a semiclassical expansion to first order in $\hbar$, decompose the Wigner function and collision terms with respect to the four-momentum, and introduce an extended phase space with a continuous spin variable $\s^\mu$ to define a scalar distribution function. The collision term is expanded in terms of asymptotic "in"-states and evaluated for binary elastic collisions, separating local and nonlocal contributions.

## Key numbers

* The spin variable $\s^\mu$ is normalized with $\sigma^2 = 2$, corresponding to $s(s+1)$ for spin $s=1$
* The phase-space measure for the spin variable satisfies $\int dS(k) = 3$, matching the spin degeneracy of massive spin-1 particles
* The local transition rate in extended phase space is given by $\mathcal{W} = \frac{(2\pi\hbar)^3}{32} M^{\gamma_1\gamma_2\delta_1\delta_2}M^{\zeta_1\zeta_2\eta_1\eta_2} h_{\gamma_1\eta_1}(k_1,\s_1) h_{\gamma_2\eta_2}(k_2,\s_2) h_{\zeta_2\delta_2}(k',\s') \left\{H(k,\s),h(k,\bar{\s})\right\}_{\zeta_1\delta_1}$

## Context

This work extends the Wigner-function approach to quantum kinetic theory previously developed for spin-1/2 particles by Weickgenannt et al. (2021) to the case of massive spin-1 particles, motivated by the need to describe spin alignment of vector mesons such as $\phi$ and $K^{\star 0}$ in heavy-ion collisions. Unlike approaches by Liang et al. (2004), Yang et al. (2017), and Sheng et al. (2019-2022) that determine vector meson properties through quark polarization, this work treats massive spin-1 fields as effective degrees of freedom. The formalism enables the future formulation of spin hydrodynamics for spin-1 particles and provides a foundation for phenomenological applications to spin alignment measurements by the ALICE and STAR collaborations.
