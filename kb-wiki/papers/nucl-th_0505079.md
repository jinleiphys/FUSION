# Target Normal Spin Asymmetry of the Elastic ep-Scattering at Resonance Energy

> We study the target normal spin asymmetry for the reaction $ep \to ep$ at electron energy up to few GeV in the laboratory frame. The asymmetry is proportional to the imaginary part of the reaction scattering amplitude. To estimate the imaginary part of the amplitude we use the unitarity relation and saturate the intermediate hadron states by the proton and resonances from the first, second and third resonance regions. The resonance electromagnetic transition amplitudes, needed to evaluate the asymmetry, are taken from experiment.

## Key claim

The target normal spin asymmetry for elastic ep-scattering at electron energies up to 2 GeV is calculated by saturating the intermediate hadron states with the proton and specific resonances, neglecting the non-resonant continuum contribution which the authors argue is small due to cancellation effects.

## Method

The asymmetry, proportional to the imaginary part of the scattering amplitude, was computed using the unitarity relation with one-photon exchange amplitudes on the right-hand side. The intermediate hadronic states were modeled as the proton plus five resonances (P33(1232), P11(1440), D13(1520), S11(1535), and F15(1680)), with their electromagnetic transition amplitudes taken from experimental data and fitted using a linear least-squares method in a transformed variable. The proton elastic contribution was treated separately using the dipole fit for form factors, while resonances were included via a Breit-Wigner spreading of the delta function.

## Key numbers

* The calculation covers electron lab energies up to 2 GeV, with maximum Q^2 approximately 3 GeV^2 at E_lab = 2 GeV.
* The dipole fit parameters used: Q_0^2 = 0.71 GeV^2, proton magnetic moment mu_p approximately 2.79.
* At E_lab near 0.9 GeV, the Roper resonance P11(1440) contribution becomes comparable to the Delta(1232) contribution to the asymmetry.
* The elastic (proton) contribution dominates at E_lab below 0.3 GeV and above 1.3 GeV.
* Fit coefficients for P33(1232) transition amplitudes: A_tilde_3/2 = -0.929 + 0.264 xi, A_tilde_1/2 = -0.485 + 0.130 xi, A_tilde_S1/2 = 0.069 + 0.022 xi (all in GeV^{-1}).

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Hey (1970s) for the elastic proton contribution and by Pasquini (2000s) who included N and pi N intermediate states, which is reasonable at low energies but becomes worse as energy increases. The authors' resonance-saturation approach is justified at intermediate energies up to 2 GeV, and the results may enable new experiments to access resonance transition form factors through precise asymmetry measurements, as noted by Pasquini. The calculation also relates to the discrepancy between Rosenbluth separation and recoil polarization measurements of proton form factors at Jefferson Lab (Jones, Gayou, Punjabi, Arrington, early 2000s), as the asymmetry provides a handle on two-photon exchange effects.
