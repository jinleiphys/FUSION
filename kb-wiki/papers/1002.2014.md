# Cross sections for neutron capture from surrogate measurements: An examination of Weisskopf-Ewing and ratio approximations

> Motivated by the renewed interest in the surrogate nuclear reactions approach, an indirect method for determining compound-nuclear reaction cross sections, the prospects for determining (n, gamma) cross sections for deformed rare-earth and actinide nuclei are investigated. A nuclear-reaction model is employed to simulate physical quantities that are typically measured in surrogate experiments and used to assess the validity of the Weisskopf-Ewing and ratio approximations, which are typically employed in the analysis of surrogate reactions. The expected accuracy of (n,gamma) cross sections extracted from typical surrogate measurements is discussed and limitations of the approximate methods are illustrated. Suggestions for moving beyond presently-employed approximations are made.

## Key claim
The accuracy of (n,γ) cross sections extracted from surrogate measurements using the Weisskopf-Ewing or ratio approximations is limited by the spin-parity mismatch between the desired and surrogate reactions, particularly at low neutron energies (below about 1 MeV), even for deformed rare-earth and actinide nuclei where higher level densities were expected to reduce this sensitivity.

## Method
The authors employed a nuclear-reaction model using a modified version of the Hauser-Feshbach code Stapre to simulate physical quantities typically measured in surrogate experiments. They calibrated model parameters to fit known neutron resonance spacings, average radiative widths, and (n,γ) and (n,f) cross sections for ²³³U, ²³⁵U, ¹⁵⁵Gd, and ¹⁵⁷Gd, then extracted the γ-decay probabilities G_γ^CN(E,J,π) as functions of spin, parity, and excitation energy. They simulated surrogate coincidence probabilities using several schematic, energy-independent spin-parity distributions and compared the resulting Weisskopf-Ewing and ratio-approximation cross sections to the full Hauser-Feshbach reference calculations.

## Key numbers
- For ²³⁶U at neutron energies above 1.5 MeV, γ-decay probabilities for J = 9,12 differ from those for J = 0,3 by factors ranging from 0.5 to 3; for J = 15,18 the difference is a factor of 3-6.
- At E_ex = 7.55 MeV (E_n = 0 MeV) for ²³⁶U, fission competes with γ emission, resulting in G_γ^CN(E,J,π) < 1.
- The ²³⁵mU first excited state lies at E_ex = 77 eV above the ²³⁵U ground state, with Jπ = 1/2⁺ versus Jπ = 7/2⁻ for the ground state.
- The study covers excitation energies E_ex = 6.55-10.5 MeV for ²³⁶U, corresponding to neutron energies E_n = 0-4 MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study of surrogate reactions for (n,f) cross sections (Escher and Dietrich, 2006) and complements an earlier theoretical study of zirconium isotopes (Forssen, 2007) that demonstrated strong sensitivity of γ-decay probabilities to spin-parity population near closed shells. The paper investigates whether the higher level densities in deformed rare-earth and actinide nuclei reduce this sensitivity, and finds that significant limitations remain. The results enable future development of more sophisticated approaches beyond the Weisskopf-Ewing and ratio approximations, such as using measurements of individual γ-ray yields to constrain the spin-parity distribution of the decaying compound nucleus.
