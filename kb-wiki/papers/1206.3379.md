# Symmetry energy of deformed neutron-rich nuclei

> The symmetry energy, the neutron pressure and the asymmetric compressibility of deformed neutron-rich even-even nuclei are calculated on the examples of Kr and Sm isotopes within the coherent density fluctuation model using the symmetry energy as a function of density within the Brueckner energy-density functional. The correlation between the thickness of the neutron skin and the characteristics related with the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy is investigated for isotopic chains of these nuclei in the framework of the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS method. Results for an extended chain of Pb isotopes are also presented. A remarkable difference is found in the trend followed by the different isotopic chains: the studied correlations reveal a smoother behavior in the Pb case than in the other cases. We also notice that the neutron skin thickness obtained for $^{208}$Pb with SLy4 force is found to be in a good agreement with recent data.

# Symmetry energy of deformed neutron-rich nuclei

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that approximate linear correlations exist between neutron skin thickness and both symmetry energy and neutron pressure in deformed neutron-rich Kr, Sm, and Pb isotopic chains, but these correlations exhibit kinks at shell closures in Kr and Sm that are absent in the Pb chain; the authors note that the neutron skin thickness obtained for $^{208}$Pb with the SLy4 force (0.1452 fm) agrees with recent experimental data, though this agreement is model-dependent and relies on specific Skyrme parametrizations.

## Method

The authors combine the coherent density fluctuation model (CDFM) with the Brueckner energy-density functional to calculate symmetry energy, neutron pressure, and asymmetric compressibility for finite deformed nuclei, using weight functions derived from self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock plus BCS proton and neutron densities. Four Skyrme force parametrizations (SLy4, SGII, Sk3, and LNS) are employed to generate densities for even-even Kr isotopes ($A=82-120$), Sm isotopes ($A=140-156$), and Pb isotopes ($A=202-214$), with the monopole term of the multipole-decomposed density used to obtain the CDFM weight function.

## Key numbers

- Neutron skin thickness $\Delta R$ for $^{208}$Pb calculated with SLy4 force: 0.1452 fm, which agrees with the experimentally extracted value of $0.156^{+0.025}_{-0.021}$ fm from dipole polarizability measurements (Tamii 2011) and with $0.18\pm 0.027$ fm from Tsang (2012)
- Asymmetric compressibility $\Delta K$ values for Sm isotopes fall in the interval between -295 and -315 MeV, comparing favorably with the neutron-asymmetry compressibility $K_{\Sigma}^{\prime}=-320\pm 180$ MeV deduced from breathing mode giant monopole resonance data (Sharma 1988)
- The PREX Collaboration measurement of $\Delta R$ in $^{208}$Pb: $0.33^{+0.16}_{-0.18}$ fm from parity-violating electron scattering (Abrahamyan 2012)
- Symmetry energy values for Pb isotopes calculated with LNS force exceed 30 MeV, larger than results from SLy4, SGII, and Sk3 forces

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous CDFM analysis (Gaidarov 2011) from spherical Ni, Sn, and Pb nuclei to deformed Kr and Sm isotopes, and builds on earlier studies of deformation effects on neutron skin formation in Kr isotopes (Sarriguren 2007). The results confirm that linear correlations between neutron skin thickness and symmetry energy found in spherical nuclei also hold for deformed systems, with kinks at shell closures (e.g., $^{86}$Kr at $N=50$, $^{144}$Sm at $N=82$) that are absent in the Pb chain. The study enables further constraints on nuclear energy-density functionals by providing systematic comparisons across isotopic chains with different deformation properties, and contributes to the ongoing experimental and theoretical effort to determine the neutron skin of $^{208}$Pb (PREX Collaboration, Tamii 2011, Horowitz 2012, Piekarewicz 2012).
