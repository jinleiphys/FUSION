# Scaling laws for near barrier Coulomb and Nuclear Breakup

> We investigate the nuclear and the Coulomb contributions to the breakup cross sections of $^6$Li in collisions with targets in different mass ranges. Comparing cross sections for different targets at collision energies corresponding to the same $E/V_{\mathrm{\scriptscriptstyle B}}$, we obtain interesting scaling laws. First, we derive an approximate linear expression for the nuclear breakup cross section as a function of $A_{\mathrm{% \scriptscriptstyle T}}^{1/3}$. We then confirm the validity of this expression performing CDCC calculations. Scaling laws for the Coulomb breakup cross section are also investigated. In this case, our CDCC calculations indicate that this cross section has a linear dependence on the atomic number of the target. This behavior is explained by qualitative arguments. Our findings, which are consistent with previously obtained results for higher energies, are important when planning for experiments involving exotic weakly bound nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper derives and confirms that, for collisions of the weakly bound projectile \(^{6}\)Li at the same ratio of collision energy to Coulomb barrier height (\(E/V_{\mathrm{B}}\)), the nuclear breakup cross section scales linearly with the target mass number raised to the one-third power (\(A_{\mathrm{T}}^{1/3}\)) and the Coulomb breakup cross section scales linearly with the target atomic number (\(Z_{\mathrm{T}}\)). These scaling laws hold for energies both above and slightly below the barrier, extending earlier high-energy results to the near-barrier regime.

## Method

The authors performed Continuum Discretized Coupled Channels (CDCC) calculations for the \(^{6}\)Li projectile breaking up into a deuteron and an alpha particle, colliding with \(^{59}\)Co, \(^{120}\)Sn, \(^{144}\)Sm, and \(^{208}\)Pb targets. They computed separate nuclear and Coulomb contributions to the breakup cross section at five energies corresponding to \(E/V_{\mathrm{B}} = 0.84\), 1.00, 1.07, 2.07, and 2.99. The nuclear breakup cross section was also derived analytically from a Wong-type fusion formula, yielding an expression linear in \(A_{\mathrm{T}}^{1/3}\) for a fixed \(E/V_{\mathrm{B}}\).

## Key numbers

* Nuclear breakup cross section for \(^{6}\)Li scales linearly with \(A_{\mathrm{T}}^{1/3}\) at \(E/V_{\mathrm{B}} = 0.84\), 1.00, 1.07, 2.07, and 2.99, as confirmed by CDCC calculations (Figure 1).
* Coulomb breakup cross section for \(^{6}\)Li scales linearly with target atomic number \(Z_{\mathrm{T}}\) at the same five \(E/V_{\mathrm{B}}\) values (Figure 2).
* The derived analytic expression for the nuclear breakup cross section is \(\sigma^{\mathrm{bu}}_{\mathrm{N}} = 2\pi a [1 - V_{\mathrm{B}}/E] (R_{\mathrm{P}} + R_{\mathrm{T}})\), where \(a\) is the nuclear surface diffuseness and \(R_{\mathrm{P}}, R_{\mathrm{T}}\) are projectile and target radii (Equation labeled P1P2).
* The linear scaling for nuclear breakup is shown to hold even slightly below the barrier (\(E/V_{\mathrm{B}} = 0.84\)), despite the derivation assuming above-barrier energies.

## Context

This work extends the high-energy scaling laws for nuclear breakup found by Hussein et al. (2006) to near-barrier energies, and complements the authors' own recent study (Otomar et al., 2013) by providing a theoretical derivation and expanding the energy range to \(E/V_{\mathrm{B}} = 0.84\) to 3.0. The results enable experimenters to estimate nuclear and Coulomb breakup contributions for weakly bound projectiles on different targets without full CDCC calculations, aiding the design of experiments studying the influence of breakup on fusion.
