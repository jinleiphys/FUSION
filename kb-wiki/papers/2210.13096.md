# Long-range multiparticle interactions induced by neutrino exchange in neutron-star matter

> Forces with a large radius of interaction can have a significant impact on the equation of state of matter. Low-mass neutrinos generate a long-range potential due to the exchange of neutrino pairs. We discuss a possible relationship between the neutrino masses, which determine the interaction radius of the neutrino-pair exchange potential, and the equation of state of neutron matter. Contrary to previous statements, the thermodynamic potential, when decomposed into the number of neutrino interactions, vanishes in any decomposition order, except for the interaction of two neutrons. In the one-loop approximation, long-range multiparticle neutrino interactions are stable in the infrared region for all neutrino masses and do not affect the equation of state of neutron matter or the stability of neutron stars.

## Key claim

Contrary to earlier claims by Fischbach (1996) and Abada et al. (1996), the thermodynamic potential of neutron matter, when expanded in powers of the neutron number density, vanishes for all multiparticle neutrino interaction terms except the two-neutron interaction, and no infrared divergences appear for any neutrino mass in the one-loop approximation.

## Method

The author computes the change in thermodynamic potential due to neutrino interactions in homogeneous neutron matter using the one-loop approximation of quantum statistics, expanding the neutrino propagator in a power series in the neutron number density (parameterized by the Z-boson mean field U). The effective Hamiltonian is derived from Z-boson exchange between neutrinos and neutrons, and the resulting momentum-space integrals are evaluated analytically using contour integration and symbolic computation with Maple, followed by a Wick rotation to Euclidean space for a general proof.

## Key numbers

- Neutron number density: ρ ∼ 0.4 fm⁻³
- Z-boson mean-field potential: U = -G_F ρ/√2 ∼ -20 eV
- Typical Fermi momentum of neutrons: several hundred MeV
- Neutrino mass threshold for homogeneous matter approximation: m ≳ 1/R_s ∼ 2×10⁻¹¹ eV, where R_s ∼ 10 km
- Number of neutrons in a star: N ∼ M_⊙/m_n = 1.2×10⁵⁷
- The two-neutron interaction potential: U_nn(r) = G_F²/(16π³ r⁵)
- Terms s = 5 through s = 100 in the series expansion vanish identically; terms s = 1, 3, 4 vanish after regularization; term s = 2 is finite and negligible

## Context

This work corrects earlier claims by Fischbach (1996), who argued that multiparticle neutrino interactions cause infrared divergences requiring neutrino masses m ≳ 0.4 eV for neutron star stability, and by Abada et al. (1996), who found the total contribution vanishes but individual terms diverge. The author shows that all multiparticle terms beyond two-body interactions vanish individually in the one-loop approximation, removing the need for a neutrino mass lower bound from neutron star stability and making the equation of state insensitive to neutrino masses. This result is relevant to the KATRIN experiment's upper limit on electron neutrino mass (0.8 eV) and cosmological upper limits on the sum of neutrino masses (0.13 eV).
