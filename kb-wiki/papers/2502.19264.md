# Chiral vortical conductivities and the moment of inertia of a rigidly rotating Fermi gas

> We determine the chiral vortical conductivities, as well as the orbital and spin moment of inertia of a charged, chiral, and rigidly rotating free Fermi gas. To this purpose, we begin by calculating the vacuum expectation values of a vector and axial vector current using the free fermion propagator in this medium. This propagator is derived by employing the Fock-Schwinger method based on the solutions of the Dirac equation in the presence of rotation and finite axial chemical potential. We present a complete derivation of these solutions. We demonstrate that in the first approximation, the chiral vortical conductivity associated with the vector current is proportional to the product of the vector and axial chemical potentials. In contrast, the chiral vortical conductivity related to the axial vector current depends on the temperature, the vector, and the axial vector chemical potential squares. We use the relation between the axial vector current and the angular momentum density associated with spin to determine the spin and the orbital moment of inertia of a rigidly rotating Fermi gas in a charged and chirally imbalanced medium separately. In addition, we compute the total moment of inertia by utilizing the methods presented in the first part of the paper and show that the orbital moment of inertia of a free fermion gas vanishes.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that for a charged, chiral, and rigidly rotating free Fermi gas, the orbital moment of inertia vanishes identically, leaving only the spin contribution to the total moment of inertia, though this decomposition is subject to pseudogauge ambiguities that affect the separation of total angular momentum into orbital and spin parts for massless fermions.

## Method

The authors solve the Dirac equation for a free fermion field in a rigidly rotating medium with finite vector and axial chemical potentials, using cylindrical coordinates and the Fock-Schwinger proper-time method to derive the free fermion propagator in both coordinate and momentum space. They then compute the vacuum expectation values of the vector and axial vector currents from this propagator at finite temperature to extract the chiral vortical conductivities, and use the relation between the axial vector current and spin angular momentum density to separately determine the spin and orbital moments of inertia.

## Key numbers

- The chiral vortical conductivity associated with the vector current is proportional to the product of the vector and axial chemical potentials (no explicit numerical value given in the truncated text)
- The chiral vortical conductivity associated with the axial vector current depends on the temperature, the vector chemical potential squared, and the axial vector chemical potential squared (no explicit numerical value given in the truncated text)
- The orbital moment of inertia of a free fermion gas vanishes: I_L = 0, implying I_tot = I_S

## Context

This work extends the study of anomalous transport coefficients in rotating systems, specifically the chiral vortical effect (CVE), to include separate determination of spin and orbital moments of inertia. The results for the chiral vortical conductivities coincide with those in the literature from Kharzeev (2015), Landsteiner (2011, 2013), and Liao (various years). The method for separately computing spin and orbital moments of inertia is motivated by Chernodub et al. (2023), who studied the total moment of inertia of a gluonic gas and found negative values below a supervortical temperature, but could not separate the spin and orbital contributions. The decomposition is affected by pseudogauge ambiguities as discussed in the review by Speranza (2021), including canonical, Belifante-Rosenfeld, Hilgevoord-Wouthuysen, and de Groot-van Leeuwen-van Wert pseudogauges.
