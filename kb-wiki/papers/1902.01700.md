# Singularity Free Direct Calculation of Spontaneous Mass Generation

> We propose a new iterative method to directly calculate the spontaneous mass generation. It is regarded as a new regularization method resembling the finite volume calculation which assures non-negative fluctuation property at every stage. We work with the Nambu--Jona-Lasinio model and the strong coupling gauge theory where the dynamical chiral symmetry breaking occurs. We are able to conclude the physical mass definitely without encountering any singularity nor recourse to any additional consideration like the free energy comparison. However in special case of the 1st order phase transition, we find that the iterative method has a chance to go wrong.

## Key claim

The authors propose an iterative method that directly sums Feynman diagrams to compute spontaneously generated mass without encountering singularities or requiring free energy comparisons, but they find that this method can fail in cases of first-order phase transitions where the iteration may converge to a metastable rather than the physical vacuum.

## Method

The method defines a sequence of partial sums of diagrams ordered by "node length" (for the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model) or "ladder depth" (for gauge theories), where each successive term is obtained by applying a one-loop integral transformation to the previous term. The iteration starts from the bare mass and proceeds as M^(n+1) = F(M^(n)), where F is the one-loop self-energy integral; the infinite iteration limit yields the total sum of all tree diagrams (NJL) or ladder diagrams (gauge theory). The transformation function F is analyzed graphically by finding fixed points where F(x) = x, and the stability of each fixed point determines which solution the iteration selects.

## Key numbers

- Critical coupling constant for the NJL model with four-dimensional cutoff: g_c = 1 (where g = G/(4π^2))
- Critical coupling constant for the NJL model with three-dimensional cutoff (at μ=0): g_c = 1 (where g = G/(2π^2))
- Critical coupling constant for U(1) gauge theory with fixed coupling: λ_c = 1 (where λ = 3g^2/(4π^2))
- For the NJL model with four-dimensional cutoff, the transformation function near the origin takes the form F(x) ≃ m_0 + kx with k > 1 in the strong coupling regime
- For the finite density system with g=0.85 and μ=0.7, the chiral symmetry broken phase has a stable fixed point at the origin, and the iteration gives vanishing mass in the m_0 → 0 limit despite the system being in the broken phase

## Context

This work extends the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (1961) approach to dynamical chiral symmetry breaking by replacing the standard self-consistent gap equation with an iterative summation method that avoids singularities. The method is applied to both the NJL model and ladder-approximated gauge theories, and the results are compared with the weak solution method of non-perturbative renormalization group analysis from Kumamoto (2014). The failure of the iteration for first-order phase transitions shows that the method cannot completely resolve the indefiniteness of infinite diagram sums, and the authors suggest that shifting the iteration by a fictitious bare mass may select different fixed points.
