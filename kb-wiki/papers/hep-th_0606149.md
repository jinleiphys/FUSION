# Gauge/gravity duality and thermalization of a boost-invariant perfect fluid

> We derive the equation for the quasi-normal modes corresponding to the scalar excitation of a black hole moving away in the fifth dimension. This geometry is the AdS/CFT dual of a boost-invariant expanding perfect fluid in N=4 SUSY Yang-Mills theory at large proper-time. On the gauge-theory side, the dominant solution of the equation describes the decay back to equilibrium of a scalar excitation of the perfect fluid. Its characteristic proper-time can be interpreted as a thermalization time of the perfect fluid, which is a universal (and numerically small) constant in units of the unique scale of the problem. This may provide a new insight on the short thermalization-time puzzle encountered in heavy-ion collision phenomenology. A nontrivial scaling behaviour in proper-time is obtained which can be interpreted in terms of a slowly varying adiabatic approximation.

## Key claim

The dominant quasinormal mode of a scalar excitation in the dual black hole geometry gives a thermalization proper-time for the expanding perfect fluid that is a universal constant in units of the unique scale, but the actual time dependence is not a simple exponential in proper-time τ but rather follows a nontrivial scaling exp(−(3/2)·2.7466·τ^(2/3)), which the authors interpret as an adiabatic approximation where the local temperature T ∼ τ^(−1/3) varies slowly.

## Method

The authors derive the wave equation for a massless scalar field in the Fefferman-Graham metric of a boost-invariant expanding black hole geometry, which is the AdS/CFT dual of an expanding N=4 SUSY Yang-Mills perfect fluid at large proper-time. They factorize the equation into a τ-dependent part solved by Bessel functions and a v-dependent part (v = z/τ^(1/3)) that is formally identical to the static black hole quasinormal mode equation. The quasinormal frequencies are then obtained from the static equation, and the proper-time evolution follows from the Bessel function solutions.

## Key numbers

- The dominant quasinormal frequency for the static planar AdS black hole is ω_static/(πT) ∼ 3.1194 − 2.74667 i, from prior work cited as [5].
- The proper-time damping factor for the expanding perfect fluid is exp( −(3/2)·2.7466·τ^(2/3) ), in units where the horizon is fixed at z_0 = τ^(−1/3).
- The temperature of the expanding perfect fluid scales as T ∼ τ^(−1/3), consistent with the evolving black hole solution.

## Context

This work extends the static black hole quasinormal mode analysis of [4,5,6,6bis] to the moving black hole geometry previously constructed by the same authors (Janik and Peschanski, 2006) as the AdS/CFT dual of an expanding perfect fluid. The results provide a non-perturbative estimate of the thermalization time for a strongly-coupled gauge theory plasma, which the authors suggest may offer insight into the short thermalization-time puzzle in heavy-ion collision phenomenology, where weak-coupling estimates give long thermalization times. The analysis opens the question of extending the method to shear viscosity calculations in the expanding geometry, where Fourier transforms are not directly applicable.
