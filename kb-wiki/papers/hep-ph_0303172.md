# Double meson production in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions

> The double meson production in ultraperipheral heavy ions collisions is addressed, focusing on the particular case of rho-J/Psi from two-photon reactions. The cross section at photon level is obtained using distinct parameterizations for the gluon distribution on the light meson. The resulting estimates for the nuclear case are presented and discussed. As a by product, we estimate the double rho production cross section using the Pomeron-exchange factorization relations.

## Key claim

The cross sections for double meson production (specifically rho-J/Psi and double rho) in ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions are sensitive to the gluon distribution inside the light meson, and future measurements at the LHC could discriminate among different parameterizations of that distribution, but the nuclear cross section is dominated by not-so-high two-photon energies, which reduces the discriminating power compared to the photon-level cross section.

## Method

The authors compute the differential cross section for gamma gamma -> rho J/Psi using a perturbative QCD approach based on the work of Motyka and Ziaja (2000), incorporating the gluon distribution of the rho meson from several parameterizations (GRS LO, GRS NLO, SaS1D, and a Regge-motivated ansatz). The total cross section at the photon level is obtained by integrating over t with a slope parameter B = 5.5 GeV^-2, and correction factors for skewness, NLO effects, and the real part of the amplitude are applied. The nuclear cross section for AA -> AA rho J/Psi is then calculated by folding the photon-level cross section with the two-photon luminosity from the Weizsacker-Williams spectrum of the nuclei. As a byproduct, the double rho production cross section is estimated using the Pomeron-exchange factorization relations, relating it to the rho J/Psi and J/Psi J/Psi cross sections.

## Key numbers

*   For gamma gamma -> rho J/Psi at the photon level, the slope parameter is B = 5.5 +/- 1.0 GeV^-2.
*   The light meson-photon coupling is g_rho^2 = 0.454.
*   For the Regge-motivated gluon distribution, the Pomeron intercept is taken as omega_P = 1.25, with a higher value of 1.4 considered for a BFKL-like scenario.
*   At LHC energies (sqrt(s) = 5500 GeV for Pb-Pb), the nuclear cross section for AA -> AA rho J/Psi takes values between 680 nb and 2.6 mb, depending on the gluon distribution parameterization.
*   The predicted nuclear cross section for AA -> AA rho rho is <= 1250 microbarns.
*   The FELIX collaboration parameterization for gamma gamma -> rho J/Psi uses A = 1.1 nb/GeV^-2, B = 2.5 GeV^-2, C = 0.8, and alpha' = 0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis of diffractive double J/Psi production in gamma gamma collisions at ultraperipheral heavy ion collisions (Goncalves and Machado, 2002) to the rho J/Psi and double rho cases. It follows the pioneering work of Motyka and Ziaja (2000) on the photon-level process and uses the Pomeron-exchange factorization theorem (Gribov, 1970; Block, 1990) to relate cross sections. The results are compared with the FELIX collaboration proposal for the LHC and with estimates of double meson production from photonuclear multiple interactions (Klein, 2001), which are identified as the main background. The analysis enables future experimental discrimination among models for the gluon distribution in light mesons and constrains the partonic content of the photon structure function.
