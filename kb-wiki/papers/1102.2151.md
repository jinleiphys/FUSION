# Technical aspects of the evaluation of the overlap of Hartree- Fock- Bogoliubov wave functions

> Several technical aspects concerning the evaluation of the overlap between two mean field wave functions of the Hartree Fock Bogoliubov type, are discussed. The limit when several orbitals become fully occupied is derived as well as the formula to reduce the dimensionality of the problem when exactly empty orbitals are present. The formalism is also extended to deal with the case where the bases of each of the wave functions are different. Several practical results concerning the evaluation of pfaffians as well as the canonical decomposition of norm overlaps are also discussed in the appendices.

## Key claim

The paper derives regularized formulas for the overlap of Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) wave functions that remain well-defined when the Thouless parametrization becomes ill-defined due to fully occupied or fully empty orbitals, and extends the formalism to cases where the two wave functions are expressed in different single-particle bases.

## Method

The author uses the Bloch-Messiah decomposition of the Bogoliubov amplitudes to write the Thouless matrices in canonical form, then isolates the divergent or vanishing diagonal elements corresponding to fully occupied (v=1) or fully empty (v=0) levels. For the fully occupied case, a regularized matrix is introduced whose diverging determinant cancels when computing the normalized overlap. For the fully empty case, the pfaffian expression is reduced to matrices of dimension equal to the number of non-zero occupancy orbitals. For different single-particle bases, a common basis is constructed from the union of the two subspaces using the overlap matrix of the two bases, and the pfaffian formula is re-expressed in terms of this enlarged basis.

## Key numbers

- The dimension of the Thouless matrices M^(i) is N x N, where N is assumed to be an even number N = 2q as required for fermions.
- In the fully empty limit, the reduced matrices N_C^(i) have dimension 2(N - K_S) x 2(N - K_S), where K_S is the smaller of the number of empty levels in each wave function.
- The matrix U_22 in the empty-level reduction has dimension 2K_S x 2K_S, where K_S is the number of empty levels.
- The overlap matrix N for different bases has dimension (N_(0) + N_(1)) x (N_(0) + N_(1)), where N_(0) and N_(1) are the dimensions of the two single-particle bases.

## Context

This work extends the pfaffian-based formulation of HFB overlaps introduced by Robledo (2009) to handle singular cases where the Thouless parametrization fails due to unit occupancy. It also generalizes earlier treatments of empty-level reduction by Bonche (1990), Haider (1992), Robledo (1994), and Yao (2009) to include the phase of the overlap. The results enable reliable computation of both modulus and phase of HFB overlaps for beyond-mean-field methods in nuclear physics, and address the "pole problem" that plagues such calculations as discussed by Donau (1998), Anguiano (2001), and Lacroix (2009).
