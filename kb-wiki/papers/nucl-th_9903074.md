# A program for coupled-channels calculations with all order couplings for heavy-ion fusion reactions

> A FORTRAN77 program is presented that calculates fusion cross sections and mean angular momenta of the compound nucleus under the influence of couplings between the relative motion and several nuclear collective motions. The no-Coriolis approximation is employed to reduce the dimension of coupled-channels equations. The program takes into account the effects of non-linear couplings to all orders, which have been shown to play an important role in heavy-ion fusion reactions at subbarrier energies.

## Key claim

The paper presents a FORTRAN77 program (CCFULL) that calculates fusion cross sections and mean angular momenta for heavy-ion reactions by solving coupled-channels equations with couplings included to all orders, but the program is restricted to systems where the charge product \(Z_p Z_T\) is less than around 1800 and the sum of charges is larger than about 12, and where multi-nucleon transfer reactions are less important than inelastic channels.

## Method

The program solves the coupled-channels equations using the no-Coriolis (isocentrifugal) approximation, which replaces the angular momentum of relative motion in each channel by the total angular momentum \(J\), thereby reducing the dimension of the equations. The coupled second-order differential equations are integrated outward from the minimum of the Coulomb pocket using a modified Numerov method, with the incoming wave boundary condition (IWBC) imposed at the inner radius. Nuclear coupling matrix elements are evaluated by diagonalising the deformation operator \(\hat{O}\) in the space of intrinsic states, then using the eigenvalues and eigenvectors to compute the full coupling potential without expanding in powers of the deformation parameter.

## Key numbers

- The program is applicable for systems with charge product \(Z_p Z_T\) less than around 1800 and sum of charges \(Z_p + Z_T\) larger than around 12.
- For the test case of \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{144}\text{Sm}\), the uncoupled barrier height is \(V_b = 61.25\) MeV, barrier radius \(R_b = 10.82\) fm, and curvature 4.25 MeV.
- At \(E_{\text{cm}} = 55.00\) MeV, the fusion cross section is \(0.97449 \times 10^{-2}\) mb and the mean angular momentum \(\langle l \rangle = 5.87031\).
- At \(E_{\text{cm}} = 72.00\) MeV, the fusion cross section is 557.73621 mb and \(\langle l \rangle = 19.80734\).
- The radial mesh recommended for asymmetric systems like \(^{16}\text{O} + ^{144}\text{Sm}\) is DR = 0.05 fm with matching radius RMAX = 30 fm; for heavier systems like \(^{64}\text{Ni} + ^{92}\text{Zr}\), RMAX may need to be extended to 50 fm.
- The partial wave summation is truncated at the angular momentum whose contribution to the cross section is less than \(10^{-4}\) times the total cross section.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled-channels calculations that used the linear coupling approximation, which Hagino et al. (1997) showed to be inadequate for reproducing the shape of fusion barrier distributions from high-precision data. The program CCFULL implements the all-order coupling method previously demonstrated for rotational nuclei by Kruppa (1993) and for vibrational nuclei by Hagino et al. (1997), and it employs the no-Coriolis approximation validated by Thompson (1987) for heavy-ion fusion. The code enables systematic analysis of fusion barrier distributions extracted from precise experimental data, such as those discussed by Dasgupta et al. (1998) and Balantekin and Takigawa (1998), and can be used to test the role of higher-order couplings, transfer channels, and mutual excitations in subbarrier fusion.
