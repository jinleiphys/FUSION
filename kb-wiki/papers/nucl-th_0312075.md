# Investigating the parity of the exotic Theta^+ baryon from the kaon photoproduction

> Based on the hadronic model with the gauge prescription suggested by Ohta and Haberzettl, we investigate the possibility of determining the parity state of the $Θ^+$ baryon using photon induced processes, $γn\to K^- Θ^+$ and $γp\to \bar{K}^0 Θ^+$. The total and differential cross sections are simulated in two versions of pseudovector(PV) and pseudoscalar(PS) coupling schemes and the results are reported both on the positive and negative parity states of the $Θ^+$ baryon. It is found that in both coupling schemes the total cross sections from the neutron target are in general larger than those from the proton target, regardless of the $Θ^+$ parities. The cross sections of the $Θ^+$ production however depend largely on the value of the $Θ^+$ decay width which is not yet well established. Moreover, there is a wide theoretical uncertainty associated with the different assumption on the gauge prescription in model calculations. We discuss these points by comparing theoretical predictions with the existing experimental data. Our analysis suggests that the observation of the angular distribution rather than just the total cross section in the photoproduction process may be a useful tool to distinguish the parity of the $Θ^+$ baryon.

## Key claim

The total cross section alone cannot determine the parity of the exotic \(\Theta^+\) baryon due to wide theoretical uncertainty from different gauge prescriptions and the poorly known decay width; instead, the angular distribution of kaon photoproduction near threshold may be a useful tool to distinguish the parity state.

## Method

The authors used a hadronic model with the gauge prescription of Ohta and Haberzettl to compute total and differential cross sections for the reactions \(\gamma n \to K^- \Theta^+\) and \(\gamma p \to \bar{K}^0 \Theta^+\). They simulated both pseudovector (PV) and pseudoscalar (PS) coupling schemes for positive and negative parity states of the \(\Theta^+\), including Born terms, Kroll-Ruderman terms, contact interaction terms, and t-channel \(K^*\) and \(K_1\) meson exchanges with hadron form factors.

## Key numbers

* The coupling constant \(g_{KN\Theta}=2.2\) was adopted, assuming \(\Gamma_\Theta \simeq 5\) MeV for the positive parity \(\Theta^+\).
* For positive parity with \(\Lambda=1.8\) GeV and \(\kappa_\Theta=0\), the cross section for \(\gamma n\to K^-\Theta^+\) is about 150 nb in the PS scheme and 250 nb in the PV scheme near \(E_\gamma=2.5\) GeV.
* For positive parity with \(\Lambda=1.8\) GeV and \(\kappa_\Theta=0\), the cross section for \(\gamma p\to \bar{K}^0\Theta^+\) is about 200 nb in the PS scheme and 80 nb in the PV scheme near \(E_\gamma=2.5\) GeV.
* Including \(K^*\) and \(K_1\) exchanges with \(g_{K^*N\Theta}=1.32\) and \(g_{K_1 N\Theta}=-0.07\) (neutron) or \(-0.1\) (proton), the cross sections at \(E_\gamma=2.5\) GeV are about 230 nb for \(\gamma n\to K^-\Theta^+\) and about 150 nb for \(\gamma p\to \bar{K}^0\Theta^+\).
* The decay width \(\Gamma_\Theta\) is reported in the range \(9\sim 25\) MeV from experiments, but reanalyses suggest \(\Gamma_\Theta < 6\) MeV (Nussinov) or \(\Gamma_\Theta \leq 1\) MeV (Arndt et al.).
* The anomalous magnetic moment \(\kappa_\Theta\) was varied between \(-0.7 \leq \kappa_\Theta \leq 0.7\).
* The cutoff parameter \(\Lambda=1.8\) GeV was chosen; using \(\Lambda=1.2\) GeV lowers the cross section by more than one third.

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic model calculations by Nam et al. (2003), C. M. Ko et al. (2001), and Y. S. Oh et al. (2003) for \(\Theta^+\) photoproduction, but applies the gauge prescription of Ohta (1991) and Haberzettl (1997) instead of the overall form factor method used previously. The authors find that their results differ significantly from those of Y. S. Oh et al. (2003), who concluded that the total cross section determines the parity as positive; the present work shows that the total cross section alone cannot yield a definite conclusion. The analysis enables future experimental distinction of \(\Theta^+\) parity through angular distribution measurements near threshold, where s-wave dominance from parity and angular momentum conservation reduces model dependence.
