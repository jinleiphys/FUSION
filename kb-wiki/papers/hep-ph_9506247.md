# Reason of Large Scale Difference of the Results in the Neutron- antineutron Oscillation Problem

> We expose a grave drawback hidden in the standard model of neutron-antineutron transitions in a medium. Correcting it, one improves a limit on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time by 31 orders of magnitude.

# Reason of Large Scale Difference of the Results in the Neutron-antineutron Oscillation Problem

## Key claim

The standard potential model for neutron-antineutron transitions in a medium uses an erroneous "decay law" for antineutron-nucleus annihilation that violates unitarity, and correcting this error improves the lower limit on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time by 31 orders of magnitude, from previous estimates to approximately 4.7 × 10³¹ years.

## Method

The author identifies that the standard approach solves coupled Schrödinger equations with complex optical potentials for the neutron and antineutron, which leads to a unitarity-violating expression for the antineutron decay probability (2ImT) that gives W = 2 for times much larger than the annihilation lifetime. The correct treatment replaces this with the exponential decay law 1 − exp(−Γτ) for the antineutron-nucleus annihilation stage, and substitutes this into the time-ordered integral formulation of the two-step transition-annihilation process.

## Key numbers

- The corrected lower limit on the free-space neutron-antineutron oscillation time is τ_min = 4.7 × 10³¹ years, an increase of 31 orders of magnitude over previous limits.
- The annihilation width of the antineutron-nucleus state is Γ ∼ 100 MeV, corresponding to a lifetime of ∼ 10⁻²⁴ seconds.
- The experimental bound for the nuclear annihilation lifetime used in the calculation is T ∼ 10³¹ years.
- The ratio of the corrected result to the potential model result scales as W(T)/W_pot(T) ∼ ΓT, where T ∼ 10³¹ years.

## Context

This work extends the author's previous analysis (Nazaruk, 1995) of neutron-antineutron transitions beyond the potential model, and directly contradicts the standard potential model approach used in earlier calculations. The paper identifies an infrared singularity in the S-matrix amplitude for the transition-annihilation process that arises from zero momentum transfer in the epsilon vertex, which makes the result extremely sensitive to the energy gap δU between neutron and antineutron potentials. The corrected formalism enables a proper treatment of neutron-antineutron oscillations in nuclear media, which is relevant for experimental searches for baryon number violation.
