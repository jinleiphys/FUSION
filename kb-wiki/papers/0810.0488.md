# Direct photon emission from hadronic sources: Hydrodynamics vs. Transport theory

> Direct photon emission in heavy-ion collisions is calculated within the relativistic microscopic transport model UrQMD. We compare the results from the pure transport calculation to a hybrid-model calculation, where the high-density part of the evolution is replaced by an ideal 3-dimensional fluiddynamic calculation. The effects of viscosity, present in the transport model but neglected in ideal fluid-dynamics, are examined. We study the contribution of different production channels and non-thermal collisions to the spectrum of direct photons. Detailed comparison to the measurements by the WA~98-collaboration are undertaken.

## Key claim

Direct photon spectra from hadronic sources in heavy-ion collisions, calculated with both a pure transport model (UrQMD) and a hybrid model (transport plus ideal 3D fluid dynamics), both underpredict the WA 98 experimental data below a transverse momentum of 3.5 GeV, indicating that additional production channels or a quark-gluon plasma equation of state may be needed.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD relativistic microscopic transport model to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at 158 AGeV, calculating photon emission perturbatively from hadronic scattering cross-sections (ππ → γρ, πρ → γπ, and related channels from Kapusta (1991) and Xiong (1992)). They compared this to a hybrid model where the high-density phase of the collision was replaced by ideal 3+1-dimensional fluid dynamics using a hadron-gas equation of state, with photon emission from the hydrodynamic cells computed using thermal rates from Turbide, Rapp and Gale (2003). Both models produced fractional photons per scattering or per cell to improve statistics, and results were restricted to midrapidity (|y_cm| < 0.5) and impact parameters b ≤ 4.5 fm to match WA 98 trigger conditions.

## Key numbers

- Incident beam energy: E_Lab = 158 AGeV for Pb+Pb collisions
- Impact parameter selection: b ≤ 4.5 fm for central collisions
- Critical energy density for switching from hydro to transport: ε_crit = 730 MeV/fm³ (≈ 5 ε₀)
- The πρ → γπ channel contributes about one order of magnitude more than the sum of all other channels between p⊥ = 0.5 GeV and p⊥ = 2.5 GeV
- Average emission times for photons in the intermediate p⊥ region of 1 – 2.5 GeV are shifted to late times (5-6 fm)
- Both models underpredict WA 98 data below p⊥ = 3.5 GeV
- pQCD spectrum used for comparison had intrinsic ⟨k⊥²⟩ ≈ 0.9 GeV²

## Context

This work extends earlier direct photon calculations using UrQMD by Dumitru (1998) and compares transport theory to ideal fluid dynamics, finding that both hadronic models underpredict WA 98 data. The results suggest that future work should include an equation of state with a phase transition to a quark-gluon plasma, additional channels (η-meson, baryonic processes), and hard pQCD scatterings, enabling comparisons to RHIC data and predictions for the FAIR facility.
