# Hadronic interactions of the J/psi

> We calculate the cross sections for reactions of the J/psi with light mesons. We also evaluate its finite temperature spectral function. We investigate separately the role of elastic and inelastic channels and we compare their respective importance. We describe J/psi absorption channels that have not been considered previously to our knowledge. The relevance of our study to heavy ion collisions is discussed.

# Hadronic interactions of the J/psi

## Key claim

The cross sections for J/psi interactions with light mesons are all quite different and not constant with respect to energy, contrary to the simplified constant-cross-section assumptions used in many J/psi absorption models; the results are highly sensitive to the choice of hadronic form factor cutoff parameter Lambda, which introduces roughly an order-of-magnitude uncertainty in the cross sections even within the constrained window of 1.25 GeV to 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors construct an effective chiral Lagrangian with SU(4) symmetry for light and heavy pseudoscalar and vector mesons, fixing the gauge coupling from the rho meson decay width into pion pairs (g_rho_pi_pi = 8.54). They evaluate cross sections for both elastic and inelastic J/psi reactions with light mesons (pi, rho, eta, omega, K) using meson-exchange Feynman diagrams, and compare results from the chiral model with a phenomenological model where coupling constants are individually adjusted to empirical constraints. Hadronic form factors of monopole form are introduced at interaction vertices, with the cutoff parameter Lambda constrained by fitting the J/psi radiative decay width (yielding Lambda = 1.25 GeV) and J/psi-nucleon inelastic cross sections from photoproduction data (yielding Lambda up to 2-3 GeV).

## Key numbers

- The gauge coupling constant g_rho_pi_pi = 8.54, determined from Gamma_rho = 151 MeV and m_rho = 770 MeV
- The elastic cross section J/psi + rho -> J/psi + rho reaches approximately 1 mb at sqrt(s) = 6 GeV
- The elastic cross section J/psi + eta -> J/psi + eta is only 6 nb at the same sqrt(s) = 6 GeV
- The form factor cutoff parameter Lambda is constrained to the range 1.25 GeV <= Lambda <= 2 GeV
- The J/psi + N inelastic cross section extracted from photoproduction data is approximately 0.1 mb at sqrt(s) = 6 GeV
- The total inclusive J/psi + pi inelastic cross section flattens at around 4 mb for the larger form factor parameter (Lambda = 2 GeV)
- The cross section for J/psi + pi -> eta_c + rho is approximately 1 mb in the middle of the form factor range
- The cross section for J/psi + pi -> D* + D (plus conjugate) has a mean value of approximately 0.1 mb with form factor corrections
- The cross section for J/psi + eta -> eta_c + phi is approximately 0.06 mb
- The cross section for J/psi + K -> eta_c + K_1 is approximately 0.18 mb
- The inverse reaction eta_c + rho -> J/psi + pi has cross section approximately 0.7 mb
- The inverse reaction eta_c + pi -> J/psi + rho has cross section approximately 2 mb
- The J/psi spectral function shows substantial broadening at T = 150 MeV and T = 200 MeV due to inelastic interactions with pions, kaons, and rho mesons

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations of J/psi hadronic interaction cross sections (Jpsixsect, Jpsixsect2) which were not in agreement with each other (Redlich), and brings the study of charmonium interactions closer to the sophistication level of lighter vector mesons (rho, omega, phi). The authors introduce new J/psi absorption channels involving the eta_c meson that had not been considered previously, and note that Shuryak and Teaney had independently considered the J/psi + pi -> eta_c + rho process using a different model. The results are relevant for interpreting J/psi suppression in heavy ion collisions, where hadronic "comover" absorption models (Jpsit1, Cape2000) have used simple assumptions about cross section sizes; the authors find their comover cross section reaches about 2 mb, compared to the 1 mb used in a recent study by Cape2000. The work enables more realistic simulations of nuclear collisions that include detailed balance partners for the important reaction channels.
