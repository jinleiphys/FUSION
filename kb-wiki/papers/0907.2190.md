# Will jets reduce the elliptic flow at LHC, while decays of resonances restore the constituent quark scaling?

> Formation and evolution of the elliptic flow pattern in Pb+Pb collisions at sqrt{s}=5.5 ATeV and in Au+Au collisions at sqrt{s}=200 AGeV are analyzed for different hadron species within the framework of HYDJET++ Monte-Carlo model. The model contains both hydrodynamic state and jets, thus allowing for a study of the interplay between the soft and hard processes. It is found that jets are terminating the rise of the elliptic flow with increasing transverse momentum. Since jets are more influential at LHC compared to RHIC, the elliptic flow at LHC should be weaker than that at RHIC. The influence of resonance decays on particle elliptic flow is investigated also. These final state interactions enhance the low-p_T part of the v_2 of pions and light baryons, and work towards the fulfilment of idealized constituent quark scaling.

## Key claim

Jets terminate the rise of elliptic flow with increasing transverse momentum, and because jets are more influential at LHC than at RHIC, the elliptic flow at LHC should be weaker than that at RHIC for transverse momenta above about 3 GeV/c; resonance decays enhance the low-pT part of v2 for pions and light baryons and work toward the fulfillment of idealized constituent quark scaling, though at LHC the jet effect is too strong to fully restore the scaling.

## Method

The authors used the HYDJET++ Monte Carlo event generator, which superposes a soft hydro-type state (parametrized relativistic hydrodynamics with given freeze-out conditions) and a hard multiparton state (PYQUEN jets with radiative and collisional energy loss in quark-gluon plasma). They simulated 1,000,000 Au+Au events at sqrt(s)=200 AGeV and approximately 500,000 Pb+Pb events at sqrt(s)=5.5 ATeV at fixed centrality sigma/sigma_geo = 42% (impact parameter b ~ 1.3 R_A), and compared the elliptic flow v2(pT) of pions, kaons, protons, lambdas, and sigmas for direct hadrons versus all hadrons (including resonance decay products).

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow at LHC is almost 50% lower than the flow at RHIC (comparing Pb+Pb at 5.5 ATeV to Au+Au at 200 AGeV at same centrality).
- At LHC, jets dominate over soft processes at lower pT than at RHIC, effectively decreasing v2 for pT >= 3 GeV/c.
- In HYDJET++ calculations, the high-pT flow does not exceed 2-3% (above pT ~ 4 GeV/c).
- At LHC, about 80% of pions, 70% of protons, 60% of Sigma and Lambda hyperons, and more than 50% of kaons are produced from resonance decays.
- For direct hadrons at RHIC, NCQ scaling holds within 20% accuracy in the interval 0.2 <= KET <= 0.8 GeV; after resonance decays, scaling holds at 10% up to KET >= 1.0 GeV.
- The minimum transverse momentum for parton-parton scattering (pT_min) is 3.4 GeV/c at RHIC and 7 GeV/c at LHC.

## Context

This work extends the study of elliptic flow from RHIC to LHC energies using the HYDJET++ model, which combines hydrodynamics and jets in a way not previously elaborated. It contradicts predictions that v2 at LHC will be similar to or larger than at RHIC (e.g., from pure hydrodynamic models), and it shows that the interplay of jets and hydro flow can break the constituent quark scaling observed at RHIC by the STAR and PHENIX collaborations. The effect of resonance decays on v2 was previously studied in coalescence models (Greco, 2004) and parametrized thermal models (Drescher et al., 2004), but those works assumed NCQ scaling for direct particles; here the scaling emerges from the full resonance decay table without such an assumption. The results enable predictions for LHC heavy-ion experiments and highlight the need for a complete resonance table in microscopic models.
