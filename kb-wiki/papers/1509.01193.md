# Two neutron decay of 16Be

> Recently, the first example of two-neutron decay from the ground state of an unbound nucleus, $^{16}$Be, was seen. Three-body methods are ideal for exactly treating the degrees of freedom important for these decays. Using a basis expansion over hyperspherical harmonics and the hyperspherical R-matrix method, we construct a realistic model of $^{16}$Be in order to investigate its decay mode and the role of the two-neutron interaction. The neutron-$^{14}$Be interaction is constrained using shell model predictions. We obtain a ground state for $^{16}$Be that is over-bound by approximately 1 MeV with a width of approximately 0.23 MeV. This suggests, that for such systems, the three-body force needs to be repulsive.

## Key claim

The authors construct a three-body model of 16Be that, without a three-body force, produces a ground state under-bound by approximately 0.7 MeV relative to the experimental value of 1.35 MeV, suggesting that an attractive three-body force must be added to reproduce the experimental ground state energy.

## Method

The authors model 16Be as a three-body system (14Be core plus two neutrons) using hyperspherical coordinates and the hyperspherical R-matrix method. The neutron-14Be interaction is constrained by shell model predictions for 15Be, with Woods-Saxon potentials adjusted to reproduce the shell model levels after shifting them downward by 1 MeV to match the single measured state in 15Be. The neutron-neutron interaction uses the GPT interaction, and the coupled hyperradial equations are solved with scattering boundary conditions to extract resonance energies and widths from phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The calculated ground state resonance energy from the K=0 phase shift is 2.07 MeV, which is under-bound by approximately 0.7 MeV compared to the experimental ground state energy of 1.35 MeV (Spyrou 2012).
- The width extracted from the K=0 phase shift is approximately 0.17 MeV.
- The model required 80 hyperradial basis states, hypermomentum greater than 64, and over 80 hyperangular basis states for convergence.
- Shell model calculations for 15Be give a d5/2 state at 2.8 MeV, an s1/2 state at 4.0 MeV, and a d3/2 state at 7.0 MeV, which were lowered by 1 MeV in this work.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of two-neutron decay from unbound nuclei, following the first experimental observation of two-neutron radioactivity from the ground state of 16Be by Spyrou (2012). The three-body approach using hyperspherical harmonics and the R-matrix method provides a more complete treatment than the two extreme models (dineutron decay and uncorrelated three-body decay) used in the original experimental analysis. The results enable future studies of the decay mechanism and the role of neutron-neutron correlations, with ongoing work to properly extract resonance widths from the coupled-channel phase shifts using methods such as those of Nollett (2007).
