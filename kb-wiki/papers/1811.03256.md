# Lepton angular distributions of fixed-target Drell-Yan experiments in perturbative QCD and a geometric approach

> The lepton angular distributions of the Drell-Yan process in fixed-target experiments are investigated by NLO and NNLO perturbative QCD. We present the calculated angular parameters $λ$, $μ$, $ν$ and the degree of violation of the Lam-Tung relation, $1-λ-2ν$, for the NA10, E615 and E866 experiments. Predictions for the ongoing COMPASS and SeaQuest experiments are also presented. The transverse momentum ($q_T$) distributions of $λ$ and $ν$ show a clear dependence on the dimuon mass ($Q$) while those of $μ$ have a strong rapidity ($x_F$) dependence. Furthermore, $λ$ and $ν$ are found to scale with $q_T/Q$. These salient features could be qualitatively understood by a geometric approach where the lepton angular distribution parameters are expressed in terms of the polar and azimuthal angles of the "natural axis" in the dilepton rest frame.

## Key claim

The paper argues that NLO and NNLO perturbative QCD calculations can describe many features of fixed-target Drell-Yan lepton angular distributions, but the agreement with existing NA10, E615, and E866 data is only qualitative, with notable discrepancies including the sign of the Lam-Tung relation violation being opposite between data (negative) and NNLO pQCD (positive).

## Method

The authors used the DYNNLO (version 1.5) Monte Carlo package to compute differential Drell-Yan cross sections up to NNLO ($\mathcal{O}(\alpha_S^2)$) for pion-induced and proton-induced reactions on proton, neutron, and nuclear targets. Angular parameters $\lambda$, $\mu$, $\nu$ were extracted via harmonic polynomial moments of the lepton angular distributions in the Collins-Soper frame, using CT14nlo/CT14nnlo PDFs for nucleons and GRVPI1 PDFs for pions with factorization and renormalization scales set to the dimuon mass $Q$.

## Key numbers

- NA10 experiment: $\pi^-$ beam on tungsten at 140, 194, and 286 GeV beam energies, with dimuon mass range $4.05 \le Q \le 8.55$ GeV
- E615 experiment: $\pi^-$ beam on tungsten at 252 GeV, with $4.05 \le Q \le 8.55$ GeV and $0.2 \le x_1 \le 1$
- E866 experiment: $p+p$ and $p+d$ at 800 GeV, with $4.5 \le Q \le 15$ GeV (excluding $\Upsilon$ region $9 \le Q \le 10.7$ GeV)
- COMPASS predictions: 190-GeV $\pi^-$ beam, three $Q$ bins from 4.0 to 7.0 GeV, three $x_F$ bins from 0 to 0.6
- SeaQuest predictions: 120-GeV proton beam, three $Q$ bins from 4.0 to 7.0 GeV, three $x_F$ bins from 0 to 0.6
- At $q_T \rightarrow 0$, $\lambda$ approaches 1 and $\mu = \nu = 0$ (collinear parton model limit)
- At large $q_T$, $\lambda$ approaches $-1/3$ and $\nu$ approaches $2/3$ for both $q\bar{q}$ and $qG$ processes
- NLO pQCD expressions: for $q\bar{q}$ process $\lambda = (2Q^2 - q_T^2)/(2Q^2 + 3q_T^2)$ and $\nu = 2q_T^2/(2Q^2 + 3q_T^2)$; for $qG$ process $\lambda = (2Q^2 - 5q_T^2)/(2Q^2 + 15q_T^2)$ and $\nu = 10q_T^2/(2Q^2 + 15q_T^2)$

## Context

This work extends the pQCD comparison of Lambertsen and Vogelsang (2016) by including the $\mu$ parameter, the Lam-Tung violation quantity $1-\lambda-2\nu$, and scaling behavior, as well as providing predictions for the ongoing COMPASS and SeaQuest experiments. The geometric approach developed by Peng, Chang, and Teryaev (2016, 2017) provides an intuitive interpretation where the angular parameters are expressed in terms of the polar and azimuthal angles of the "natural axis" in the dilepton rest frame, explaining why $\lambda$ and $\nu$ scale with $q_T/Q$ while $\mu$ has strong $x_F$ dependence due to cancellation effects among different gluon emission processes.
