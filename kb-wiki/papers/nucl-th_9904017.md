# Baryon Masses in Chiral Perturbation Theory with Infrared Regularization

> The baryon masses are examined in SU(3) chiral perturbation theory to third order using the recently proposed infrared regularization scheme. Fourth order is estimated by evaluating the dominant diagram. With this regularization the magnitude of the loop integrals is reduced so that the convergence of the series appears to be better than in the heavy baryon approach.

## Key claim

The infrared regularization scheme reduces the magnitude of loop integrals in SU(3) chiral perturbation theory for baryon masses, making the chiral series converge better than in the heavy baryon approach, with successive terms decreasing by roughly a factor of a half rather than showing the strong cancellation or overcancellation characteristic of heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory.

## Method

The authors computed baryon octet masses in SU(3) chiral perturbation theory to third order using infrared regularization, where the "soft" part of loop integrals is isolated by extending the Feynman parameter integration range from 0-1 to 0-infinity, and the "hard" part is absorbed into coefficients of the effective Lagrangian. Fourth order was estimated by evaluating only the dominant meson tadpole diagram (Fig. 2) using the three terms of the second-order Lagrangian. Parameters were determined by a least-squares fit to the baryon masses and the pion-nucleon sigma term, with the strange quark contribution to the nucleon mass constrained to 200 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Pion mass taken as m_pi = 0.139 GeV, kaon mass as m_K = 0.494 GeV, yielding m_eta = 0.565 GeV via the Gell-Mann-Okubo relation
- Decay constant f = 0.103 GeV (average of kaon and pion decay constants)
- Axial coupling parameters D = 0.804 and F = 0.463
- In the third-order fit with constrained S = 200 MeV, the ratio of third to second order contributions ranges from 0.54 for the Sigma to 0.30 for the Lambda in the IR scheme, compared to 1.11 for the nucleon to 0.70 for Sigma and Xi in the heavy baryon case
- The fourth order estimate (meson tadpole diagram) gives contributions of 0.17, 0.21, 0.24, and 0.27 GeV for N, Lambda, Sigma, and Xi respectively in the IR scheme
- In the fourth-order fit, the chiral limit baryon mass M_0 = 0.653 GeV in the IR scheme, about 10% smaller than the third-order value
- The pion-nucleon sigma term converges as sigma_piN^IR = 0.074 - 0.036 + 0.008 = 0.045 GeV (order by order)
- The strange quark contribution to the nucleon mass converges as S^IR = 0.254 - 0.059 + 0.005 = 0.200 GeV (order by order)

## Context

This work extends the infrared regularization scheme proposed by Becher and Leutwyler (1999) following earlier work by Ellis and Tang (1998) to the calculation of baryon octet masses in SU(3) chiral perturbation theory. It directly addresses the convergence problems identified in heavy baryon chiral perturbation theory calculations by Borasoy and Meissner (1997), where second, third, and fourth order contributions were of roughly similar magnitude with alternating sign. The approach offers an alternative to the form-factor regularization suggested by Donoghue and Holstein (1993), which was model-dependent and difficult to apply consistently. The improved convergence enables more reliable extraction of low-energy constants and predictions for quantities such as the pion-nucleon sigma term and the strange quark content of the nucleon.
