# Medium modification of the nucleon axial form factor

> We study the modification of the nucleon axial form factor in nuclear matter. The internal quark substructure of the nucleon is self-consistently described by the quark meson coupling model. We find that the axial form factor of the bound nucleon is quenched considerably from that of the free nucleon. The axial vector coupling constant is reduced by roughly 10% at normal nuclear matter density and the axial form factor varies within 8% for moderate momentum transfer.

## Key claim

The axial form factor of a nucleon bound in nuclear matter is quenched considerably from that of a free nucleon, with the axial vector coupling constant reduced by roughly 10% at normal nuclear matter density and the axial form factor varying within 8% for moderate momentum transfer; however, the authors note that conventional mechanisms such as medium polarization also contribute to the quenching of gA, and the 9% reduction they find at the average density of finite nuclei (0.7ρ0) is smaller than the experimental 20% reduction seen in Gamow-Teller transitions.

## Method

The authors use the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, in which nuclear matter is described by non-overlapping MIT bag nucleons whose quarks couple directly to scalar (σ) and vector (ω) meson mean fields. The quark wave functions inside the bag are solved self-consistently with the mean-field equations for symmetric nuclear matter, and the nucleon axial form factor is calculated using the Peierls-Thouless projection method to remove spurious center-of-mass motion, with Lorentz contraction of the bag included via a semi-phenomenological rescaling in the Breit frame.

## Key numbers

- The axial vector coupling constant gA is reduced by roughly 10% at normal nuclear matter density ρ0 (the text also states 12% at ρ0 for a bag radius R0 = 0.95 fm)
- At the average density of finite nuclei (0.7ρ0), gA is reduced by 9% (for R0 = 0.95 fm)
- The axial form factor varies within 8% for moderate momentum transfers (Q² < 2 GeV²)
- The free-space axial radii are 0.587, 0.614, and 0.640 fm for bag radii of 0.90, 0.95, and 1.00 fm respectively, compared to the experimental value of (0.635 ± 0.023) fm
- The free-space axial coupling constant gA after center-of-mass correction is 1.14, compared to the experimental value of 1.26

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous predictions of medium modifications of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Lu et al., 1999), which appeared to be supported by a Mainz experiment measuring polarization transfer in ⁴He(ē,e'p) reactions (Dieterich, 2001). The study is motivated by the nuclear EMC effect, the quenching of gA in nuclear β-decay, and plans for high-intensity neutrino beams; the authors note that conventional interpretations involving polarization effects and meson exchange currents (Mulders, 1990; Alberico et al., 1990) also play a role, but this work explores a new mechanism from changes in the internal quark substructure of the bound nucleon.
