# Sum Rule Approach to the Isoscalar Giant Monopole Resonance in Drip Line Nuclei

> Using the density-dependent Hartree-Fock approximation and Skyrme forces together with the scaling method and constrained Hartree-Fock calculations, we obtain the average energies of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance. The calculations are done along several isotopic chains from the proton to the neutron drip lines. It is found that while approaching the neutron drip line, the scaled and the constrained energies decrease and the resonance width increases. Similar but smaller effects arise near the proton drip line, although only for the lighter isotopic chains. A qualitatively good agreement is found between our sum rule description and the presently existing random phase approximation results. The ability of the semiclassical approximations of the Thomas-Fermi type, which properly describe the average energy of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance for stable nuclei, to predict average properties for nuclei near the drip lines is also analyzed. We show that when hbar corrections are included, the semiclassical estimates reproduce, on average, the quantal excitation energies of the giant monopole resonance for nuclei with extreme isospin values.

## Key claim

The authors show that the sum rule approach (using scaling and constrained Hartree-Fock methods with Skyrme forces) can predict average energies and widths of the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR) for nuclei from stability to the drip lines, with the caveat that the estimated widths represent only the escape width (including Landau damping) and not the full experimental spreading width. The semiclassical Thomas-Fermi type approximations, when including hbar corrections, reproduce the quantal excitation energies on average for nuclei with extreme isospin values.

## Method

The authors performed density-dependent Hartree-Fock calculations using the SkM* Skyrme force for isotopic chains of O, Ca, Ni, Zr, and Pb from the proton to the neutron drip lines. They computed the m3 sum rule via the scaling method and the m-1 sum rule via constrained Hartree-Fock calculations, then derived average energies as E_M^S = sqrt(m3/m1) and E_M^C = sqrt(m1/m-1), with the resonance width estimated from the difference between these two energies. Semiclassical Thomas-Fermi and extended Thomas-Fermi (ETF) calculations with hbar corrections were also performed and compared to the quantal results.

## Key numbers

- For 34Ca: scaled energy E_M^S = 22.0 MeV, constrained energy E_M^C = 17.3 MeV, resonance width = 6.8 MeV (from sum rule approach)
- For 60Ca: scaled energy E_M^S = 18.3 MeV, constrained energy E_M^C = 15.0 MeV, resonance width = 5.3 MeV (from sum rule approach)
- For 34Ca from RPA calculations (Ref. R15): E_M^S = 22.5 MeV, E_M^C = 17.8 MeV
- For 60Ca from RPA calculations (Ref. R15): E_M^S = 18.8 MeV, E_M^C = 15.4 MeV
- The outermost neutron orbit 2d5/2 in 76Ca is bound by only 0.84 MeV
- The 1d3/2 proton orbit in 34Ca has binding energy of -1.1 MeV (compared to -7.5 MeV in 40Ca)
- The last neutron in the 1f5/2 orbit of 60Ca is bound by 3.4 MeV

## Context

This work extends the sum rule approach for ISGMR, previously applied to stable nuclei, to exotic nuclei near both the proton and neutron drip lines. It complements full RPA calculations for specific isotopes such as those by the authors of Ref. R15 for Ca isotopes (34Ca and 60Ca) and Ref. R28 for A=100, 110, and 120 isotopes. The study enables a global understanding of how the ISGMR average energy decreases and width increases near drip lines, and demonstrates that semiclassical ETF methods with hbar corrections can serve as a practical tool for predicting average properties of the ISGMR across the nuclear chart, including regions where full RPA calculations are computationally demanding.
