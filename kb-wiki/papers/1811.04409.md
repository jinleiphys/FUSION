# Relativistic hydrodynamics for spin-polarized fluids

> Recent progress in the formulation of relativistic hydrodynamics for particles with spin one-half is reviewed. We start with general arguments advising introduction of a tensor spin chemical potential that plays a role of the Lagrange multiplier coupled to the spin angular momentum. Then, we turn to a discussion of spin-dependent distribution functions that have been recently proposed to construct a hydrodynamic framework including spin and serve as a tool in phenomenological studies of hadron polarization. Distribution functions of this type are subsequently used to construct the equilibrium Wigner functions that are employed in the semi-classical kinetic equation. The semi-classical expansion elucidates several aspects of the hydrodynamic approach, in particular, shows the ways in which different possible versions of hydrodynamics with spin can be connected by pseudo-gauge transformations. These results point out at using the de~Groot - van~Leeuwen - van~Weert versions of the energy-momentum and spin tensors as the most natural and complete physical variables. Finally, a totally new method is proposed to design hydrodynamics with spin, which is based on the classical treatment of spin degrees of freedom. Interestingly, for small values of the spin chemical potential the new scheme brings the results that coincide with those obtained before. The classical approach also helps us to resolve problems connected with the normalization of the spin polarization three-vector. In addition, it clarifies the role of the Pauli-Lubanski vector and the entropy current conservation. We close our review with several general comments presenting possible future developments of the discussed frameworks.

## Key claim

This review argues that a consistent relativistic hydrodynamic framework for spin-1/2 particles requires introducing an antisymmetric tensor spin chemical potential (or spin polarization tensor) as a Lagrange multiplier coupled to the spin angular momentum, and that the de Groot–van Leeuwen–van Weert (GLW) forms of the energy-momentum and spin tensors represent the most natural and complete physical variables, with a new classical treatment of spin degrees of freedom yielding results that coincide with previous quantum approaches for small values of the spin chemical potential.

## Method

The authors review the construction of relativistic hydrodynamics for spin-polarized fluids by starting from the local conservation laws of energy, linear momentum, and angular momentum, introducing a tensor spin chemical potential as a Lagrange multiplier in the local-equilibrium density operator. They then discuss spin-dependent distribution functions used to construct equilibrium Wigner functions for the semi-classical kinetic equation, and analyze how different versions of hydrodynamics with spin can be connected through pseudo-gauge transformations. Finally, they propose a new method based on a classical treatment of spin degrees of freedom, comparing its results to the quantum GLW framework for small spin chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- The global polarization of Lambda hyperons measured by STAR was in quantitative agreement with theoretical predictions of order 1%, contrasting with earlier perturbative-QCD inspired predictions of order 10% (no explicit numerical value for the STAR measurement is given in the text).
- The paper uses natural units throughout: c = hbar = k_B = 1, except where the Planck constant hbar is displayed explicitly in semi-classical expansions.
- The metric tensor is defined as g_mu_nu = diag(+1, -1, -1, -1) with epsilon^{0123} = -epsilon_{0123} = 1.

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic framework for spin-polarized fluids recently formulated by Florkowski et al. (2017) and Florkowski et al. (2018), which allowed studies of space-time evolution of spin polarization, and clarifies the use of different forms of energy-momentum and spin tensors through pseudo-gauge transformations as discussed by Becattini et al. (2018) and Florkowski et al. (2018). The review builds on earlier studies of global equilibrium with rigid rotation by Becattini et al. (2009, 2015) and kinetic models of spin dynamics by Gao et al. (2012) and Chen et al. (2012), and enables future construction of a dynamic quantum description based on the spin density matrix not restricted to small values of the spin chemical potential.
