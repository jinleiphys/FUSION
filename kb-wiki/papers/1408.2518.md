# Absence of a local rest frame in far from equilibrium quantum matter

> In a collision of strongly coupled quantum matter we find that the dynamics of the collision produces regions where a local rest frame cannot be defined because the energy-momentum tensor does not have a real time-like eigenvector. This effect is purely quantum mechanical, since for classical systems, a local rest frame can always be defined. We study the relation with the null and weak energy condition, which are violated in even larger regions, and compare with previously known examples. While no pathologies or instabilities arise, it is interesting that regions without a rest frame are possibly present in heavy ion collisions.

# Absence of a local rest frame in far from equilibrium quantum matter

## Key claim
In collisions of strongly coupled quantum matter, there exist spacetime regions where a local rest frame cannot be defined because the energy-momentum tensor has no real time-like eigenvector, even though no pathologies or instabilities arise. This effect is purely quantum mechanical and is found in both free field theory and strongly coupled N=4 Super-Yang Mills theory, with the violation occurring in regions close to the light cone and being potentially relevant for heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.

## Method
The authors study the eigenvalue problem for the energy-momentum tensor T^mu_nu and derive the condition |T^00 + T^zz| > 2|T^0z| for existence of a local rest frame in systems with transverse symmetry. They construct a two-particle state in free scalar field theory that violates this condition, and then perform numerical simulations using gauge/gravity duality (holography) to compute the energy-momentum tensor for colliding planar shock waves in N=4 Super-Yang Mills theory at infinite coupling and large N_c, as well as for a boost-invariant radially expanding plasma.

## Key numbers
- For the free scalar field example, the LRF condition at the origin (t=0, x=0) becomes 0 > 2 omega_k |k_z|, which is clearly violated, with the constant alpha chosen as alpha = -(omega_k^2 + k^2)/m^2.
- The colliding shock initial condition has energy-momentum tensor T_{++}(z_+) = (N_c^2/(2 pi^2)) * (mu^3/(sqrt(2 pi) w)) * exp(-z_+^2/(2 w^2)), where mu^3 is the energy per transverse area and w is the shock width.
- The simulations use mu w = 0.05, in the regime where w is less than or approximately 0.1/mu, ensuring results are robust for highly Lorentz contracted shocks.
- For the radially expanding plasma, the effective temperature is defined as T_eff = 12 e_loc^(1/4), where e_loc is the local energy density computed from the energy-momentum tensor.

## Context
This work extends the study of energy condition violations from classical systems (where a local rest frame always exists) to quantum systems far from equilibrium. The authors adapt constructions from Davies and Fulling (1976) and Davies (1977) for free field theory, and compare with the classification of energy-momentum tensors by Hawking and Ellis (1973), who referred to non-diagonalizable tensors as "type IV" and noted no observed fields had this form. The holographic simulations build on prior work by Casalderrey-Solana et al. (2013), Chesler et al. (2013), and van der Schee et al. (2013) on shock collisions in N=4 SYM, and the results are compared with findings by Figueras and Wiseman (2012) who observed similar violations in curved space. The work enables further investigation into whether such regions without a local rest frame are created in real heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC.
