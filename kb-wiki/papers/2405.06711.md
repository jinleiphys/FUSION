# Reconstruction of Bremsstrahlung $γ$-rays Spectrum in Heavy Ion Reactions with Richardson-Lucy Algorithm

> The high momentum tail (HMT) in the momentum distribution of nucleons above the Fermi surface has been regarded as an evidence of short-range correlations (SRCs) in atomic nuclei. It has been showcased recently that the $np$ Bremsstrahlung radiation in heavy ion reactions can be used to extract HMT information. The Richardson-Lucy (RL) algorithm is introduced to the reconstruction of the original Bremsstrahlung $γ$-ray energy spectrum from experimental measurements. By solving the inverse problem of the detector response to the $γ$-rays, the original energy spectrum of the Bremsstrahlung $γ$ in 25 MeV/u $^{86}$Kr + $^{124}$Sn has been reconstructed and compared to the isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) simulations. The analysis based on hypothesis test suggests the existence of the HMT of nucleons in nuclei, in accordance with the previous conclusions. With its effectiveness being demonstrated, it is feasible to apply the RL algorithm in future experiments of measuring the Bremsstrahlung $γ$-rays in heavy ion reactions.

## Key claim

The Richardson-Lucy (RL) algorithm can effectively reconstruct the original Bremsstrahlung gamma-ray energy spectrum from experimentally measured spectra in heavy ion reactions, but the resolution power to quantify the high momentum tail (HMT) ratio is limited by statistics and the upper energy limit of the gamma rays, with the most favored HMT ratio varying across different energy regions of interest.

## Method

The authors applied the Richardson-Lucy iterative algorithm to solve the inverse problem of detector response, using a detector response matrix obtained from Geant4 simulations of the CsI(Tl) hodoscope on the CSHINE spectrometer. The original gamma spectrum from 25 MeV/u 86Kr + 124Sn reactions was reconstructed from experimental measurements and compared to isospin- and momentum-dependent Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (IBUU) transport model simulations that incorporate different ratios of the high momentum tail (R_HMT = 0%, 15%, and 30%). A hypothesis test was performed by generating 10000 pseudo-experimental spectra under the zero-HMT assumption and comparing the chi-square differences to the experimental result.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 25 MeV/u for the 86Kr + 124Sn reaction
- The minimum chi-square value (chi2_c = 1.835) was found at R_HMT = 25% in the energy region of interest from 35 to 80 MeV
- The experimental result excluded the zero HMT hypothesis at 80% confidence level in the 35-80 MeV energy range
- The average confidence level of excluding the zero HMT hypothesis across different energy ROIs was about 74%
- The confidence level varied from 42% to 96% depending on the energy ROI chosen
- Number of events recorded in experiment: N_evt = 51269108
- The normalization factor eta = 1.22 was used to match the summing counts above 20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the previous analysis by Qin et al. (2024) which measured the Bremsstrahlung gamma-ray spectrum up to 80 MeV and suggested the existence of HMT at about 90% confidence level using direct comparison of measured spectra to IBUU simulations. The RL algorithm approach avoids the need to fold detector response into theoretical predictions for each comparison, enabling direct comparison between reconstructed original spectra and model calculations. The results are qualitatively consistent with the earlier conclusions but show variation in the favored HMT ratio depending on the energy region analyzed, and the authors note that higher statistics and extended high-energy coverage are needed for improved resolution, pointing to future experiments by Si et al. (2023).
