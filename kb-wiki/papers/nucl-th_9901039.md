# e+ e- pairs from pi- A reactions

> We calculate dilepton production for the reactions $π^-$C and $π^-$Pb at 1.3 GeV within a semi-classical BUU transport model and compare our results to a previously published calculation. We show that a modified treatment of the $ρ$-meson production and propagation gives substantially different results. We, furthermore, discuss uncertainties related to the electromagnetic decay of the $ρ$-meson and the elementary $π^- N \to e^+ e^- X$ channels.

# e⁺e⁻ pairs from π⁻A reactions

## Key claim

The authors show that a modified treatment of ρ-meson production and propagation within a semi-classical BUU transport model yields dilepton yields up to an order of magnitude larger at intermediate invariant masses than previously published calculations for π⁻C and π⁻Pb at 1.3 GeV; however, they caution that the results remain an "educated guess" until the elementary πN → e⁺e⁻X cross section is measured experimentally.

## Method

The authors employ a semi-classical BUU (Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck) transport model with a perturbative particle scheme that dynamically incorporates finite widths of vector mesons, using resonance parameters from Manley et al. (1993) including the R → Δρ decay channel. The electromagnetic decay width of the ρ-meson to e⁺e⁻ is taken proportional to M⁻³ (from vector meson dominance) rather than proportional to M (from extended VMD), and the production and absorption of ρ-mesons are consistently described within the same resonance model. Calculations are performed for π⁻C and π⁻Pb at a kinetic energy of 1.3 GeV, with various scenarios for in-medium modifications including "dropping masses" and collisional broadening.

## Key numbers

- The dilepton yield at intermediate invariant masses from the new calculation is up to an order of magnitude larger than previous calculations from Weidmann et al. (1998) and Golubeva et al. (1998) for both light (C) and heavy (Pb) systems.
- The modified dilepton decay width introduces a factor (M_ρ/M)⁴, which at M = 0.5 GeV gives a factor of 5.6 enhancement.
- Replacing the Δρ decay channel with Δσ (σ-meson with mass M = 0.8 GeV and width Γ = 0.8 GeV) reduces the dilepton yield at intermediate masses by about a factor of 3.
- Using an e⁺e⁻-width of the ρ-meson proportional to M instead of M⁻³ gives a result more than a factor of 2 different for dilepton masses around 500 MeV.
- About 30% of the ρ-mesons in the calculations are produced via an intermediate D₁₃(1520)-resonance.
- The "dropping masses" scenario reduces the vector meson peak around 770 MeV by about a factor of 2.
- For low momenta (p < 300 MeV), the "dropping mass" scenario leads to a complete disappearance of the vector meson peak around 780 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the BUU transport model previously applied to photoproduction of dileptons in nuclei (Effenberger et al., 1998) to pion-induced reactions, and directly contradicts earlier calculations by Weidmann et al. (1998) and Golubeva et al. (1998) for π⁻A dilepton production. The results highlight the need for experimental measurement of the elementary πN → e⁺e⁻X cross section, which could be performed with the HADES spectrometer at GSI, and demonstrate that momentum cuts on the dilepton pair may help distinguish between different scenarios of in-medium vector meson modifications.
