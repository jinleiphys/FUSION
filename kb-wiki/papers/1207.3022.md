# Streched Magnetic Moments

> We note that for a system of 2 nucleons in a stretched case (J=J1+J2) the magnetic moment of the combined system is the sum of the magnetic moments of the 2 constituents. We compile other simple formulas.

## Key claim

For a stretched two-nucleon configuration (total angular momentum J = j₁ + j₂), the magnetic moment of the combined system is exactly the sum of the magnetic moments of the two constituents, but this simple additivity holds only for the stretched case; for other J values the g-factor relation is more complicated.

## Method

The authors derive the magnetic moment for a stretched two-nucleon configuration by noting that the wave function factorizes into product states with each nucleon at its maximum m-substate (m = j). They then write the general g-factor formula for any J value and show that for the stretched case it reduces to μ(J) = μ(j₁) + μ(j₂). They illustrate the result using the measured g-factor of the lowest 7⁻ state in ¹²⁶Sn, whose leading configuration is [h₁₁/₂ d₃/₂] J=7.

## Key numbers

- The g-factor of the [h₁₁/₂ d₃/₂] J=7 stretched configuration in ¹²⁶Sn is calculated as -0.109 (using g = -2/35 × μ(free neutron)).
- The experimental g-factor for the lowest 7⁻ state of ¹²⁶Sn is -0.098(9), from Ilie et al. (2010).
- For the stretched configuration, the magnetic moment sum uses μ(j₁) = -1.913 (free neutron) and μ(j₂) = -3/5 × (-1.913), giving total μ = 2/5 × (-1.913).

## Context

This work compiles known simple formulas for nuclear magnetic moments, extending the discussion of stretched configurations found in the experimental paper by Ilie et al. (2010) on ¹²⁶Sn. The results are not new but are collected for convenience; the general g-factor formula for two nucleons with arbitrary J is also presented, with simplifications when j₁ = j₂ or g₁ = g₂. The paper enables quick calculation of magnetic moments for stretched two-nucleon configurations without full shell-model diagonalization.
