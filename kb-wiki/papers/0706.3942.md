# Chiral Symmetries and Low Energy Searches for New Physics

> I discuss low energy searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model, identifying the role played by chiral symmetries in these searches and in various new physics scenarios. I focus in particular on electric dipole moment searches; precision studies of weak decays and electron scattering; and neutrino properties and interactions.

## Key claim

Low-energy searches for new physics beyond the Standard Model, particularly electric dipole moment (EDM) experiments, precision weak decay studies, and neutrino property measurements, can complement high-energy collider searches and may detect or rule out CP-violating phases needed to explain the matter-antimatter asymmetry, but the connection between EDM limits and the baryon asymmetry requires careful analysis of electroweak phase transition dynamics and is conditional on specific supersymmetric scenarios.

## Method

The paper reviews the role of chiral symmetries in three categories of low-energy BSM searches: (1) electric dipole moment searches, where the chiral-odd EDM operator is analyzed via SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y dimension-six operators involving couplings to W and B gauge fields; (2) precision studies of weak decays and electron scattering, using an effective four-fermion Lagrangian for semileptonic β-decay and the pion decay rate formula including radiative corrections; (3) neutrino properties and interactions, employing an effective Lagrangian with SM fields and right-handed neutrino fields to derive naturalness bounds on neutrino magnetic moments from loop contributions to neutrino mass operators.

## Key numbers

- Electron EDM bound: |d_e^γ/Λ| < 1.6 × 10^{-27} e-cm at 90% confidence
- Neutron EDM bound: |d_n^γ/Λ| < 3.0 × 10^{-26} e-cm at 90% confidence
- In the MSSM at one-loop order: d_e^γ/Λ ≈ 5 × 10^{-25} (100 GeV/̃m)^2 [tanβ sinφ_μ - 0.05 sinφ_A] e-cm, implying for ̃m = 100 GeV and tanβ of O(1) that φ_μ ≲ 3 × 10^{-3}
- Baryon-to-photon entropy density from BBN: Y_B = (7.3 ± 2.5) × 10^{-11}; from WMAP: Y_B = (9.2 ± 1.1) × 10^{-11}
- Pion decay constant from SM: F_π = 92.4 ± 0.025 (expt) ± 0.25 (theory) MeV
- Ratio of pion leptonic decay rates: R_{e/μ}^{exp} / R_{e/μ}^{SM} = 0.9966 ± 0.0030 ± 0.0004, where R_{e/μ}^{SM} = (1.2352 ± 0.0005) × 10^{-4}
- Naturalness bound on Dirac neutrino magnetic moment: |μ_ν|/μ_B ≲ 10^{-14} × (m_ν / 1 eV)

## Context

This review extends the author's prior work (Ramsey-Musolf 2006) on fundamental symmetries and the early universe, and complements comprehensive reviews by Erler (2004), Pospelov (2005), Severijns (2006), and Herczeg (2001) on low-energy BSM searches. It connects EDM searches to electroweak baryogenesis scenarios in supersymmetric models, showing that future EDM experiments with sensitivity near 10^{-28} e-cm could either detect CP violation consistent with the observed baryon asymmetry or rule out conventional SUSY mechanisms. The neutrino magnetic moment naturalness argument (building on Bell 2005, Davidson 2005, Bell 2006) provides a model-independent framework that could distinguish Dirac from Majorana neutrinos if a non-zero moment is discovered.
