# A Conjecture about Hadrons

> We conjecture that in the chiral limit of QCD the spectrum of hadrons is comprised of decoupled, reducible chiral multiplets. A simple rule is developed which identifies the chiral representations filled out by the ground-state hadrons. Our arguments are based on the algebraic structure of superconvergence relations derived by Weinberg from the high-energy behavior of pion-hadron scattering amplitudes.

## Key claim

The authors conjecture that in the chiral limit of QCD, the hadron spectrum consists of decoupled, reducible chiral multiplets, and that the minimal realistic chiral multiplet for a given hadron is the smallest representation that includes all isospin multiplets appearing in the QCD interpolating field for that hadron, with no degeneracy within the multiplet unless required by an additional symmetry like heavy-quark symmetry.

## Method

The authors construct chiral multiplets for light baryons, heavy baryons, and light mesons by imposing algebraic constraints derived from superconvergence relations (Weinberg, 1966) that relate the high-energy behavior of pion-hadron scattering amplitudes to low-energy axial-current matrix elements. They build effective two-dimensional collinear field theories for each helicity sector, introducing spurion fields that transform under chiral symmetry to generate mass splittings, and diagonalize the resulting mass matrices to obtain axial couplings and mass relations.

## Key numbers

- For light baryons with the inelastic diffraction constraint: predicted axial coupling $g_A = 4/3$, compared to the NCQM value of $5/3$ and the experimental value of $1.26$
- For light baryons with the inelastic diffraction constraint: predicted axial coupling $|{\cal C}_{\Delta N}| = \sqrt{2}$, compared to the NCQM value of $2$ and the experimental value of $1.2 \pm 0.1$
- For light baryons with the inelastic diffraction constraint: predicted Roper resonance mass $M_{N^\prime} = 1467$ MeV, consistent with the observed Roper resonance
- For heavy baryons: predicted axial coupling $|g_3| = 1$ (with maximal mixing), compared to the NCQM value of $\sqrt{2}$ and the experimental value of $0.95 \pm 0.08 \pm 0.08$ from $\Sigma_c^{*++}$ decay
- For heavy baryons: predicted mass of the unobserved $\Lambda_c^\prime$ chiral partner $M_{\Lambda_c^\prime} \sim 2688$ MeV
- For light mesons: predicted $\rho$ decay width $\Gamma(\rho\rightarrow\pi\pi) \sim 283 \cos^2\psi$ MeV, with $\psi = \pi/4$ giving excellent agreement with experiment

## Context

This work extends the algebraic approach of Weinberg (1966, 1968, 1969) and the saturation scheme of Gilman and Harari (1968) by providing a concrete rule for constructing chiral multiplets that goes beyond the naive constituent quark model (NCQM). The authors show that the NCQM chiral multiplet structure is incomplete and not a sensible basis for perturbative expansion, and that including the Roper resonance as a chiral partner of the nucleon and Delta naturally reduces the axial couplings toward experimental values. The results enable systematic computation of quark-mass corrections using chiral perturbation theory, as the Roper-nucleon mass splitting is below the chiral symmetry breaking scale.
