# Two Center Light Cone Calculation of Pair Production Induced by Ultrarelativistic Heavy Ions

> An exact solution of the two center time-dependent Dirac equation for pair production induced by ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions is presented. Cross sections to specific final states approach those of perturbation theory. Multiplicity rates are reduced from perturbation theory.

## Key claim

The exact cross section for pair production to any specific final electron-positron state equals the perturbation theory result, but the rate for processes correlated with low impact parameters (such as pair multiplicity) is reduced from the perturbation theory rate.

## Method

The authors solved the two-center time-dependent Dirac equation for electron-positron pair production in ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions using light cone coordinates and gauge transformations. They constructed the exact wave function in singular gauge (where the potential is proportional to delta functions on the light cone) and verified the result with an independent derivation in light cone gauge. The amplitude was obtained by propagating a negative-energy positron state forward in time and computing the overlap with a positive-energy electron state at late times.

## Key numbers

- The ions at RHIC have \(\gamma = 100\) (Lorentz factor), defining the ultrarelativistic regime considered.
- The Compton wavelength of the electron is large compared to the Lorentz-contracted nuclear radius, allowing the nucleus to be treated as a point charge \(\alpha Z\).
- The transverse integral of the Coulomb field yields the closed form \(\int d^2 y_{\perp} e^{-i k_{\perp} y_{\perp}} e^{i \Lambda^{\pm}(y_{\perp})} = e^{-i k_{\perp} y_{\perp}} (b^2/16)^{i \eta} (4\pi / k_{\perp}^{2-2i\eta}) \Gamma(1-i\eta)/\Gamma(i\eta)\) where \(\eta = +Z\alpha\).
- The cutoff parameter \(\epsilon\) is set to \(\omega / \gamma\), where \(\omega\) is the energy of the produced electron or positron.

## Context

This work extends the exact treatment of bound electron-positron pair production by Baltz (1997) to the case of continuum (free) pair production. The authors note that Segev and Wells (1998) independently obtained an amplitude with the same structure but without the closed form for the non-perturbative transverse momentum integral. The result enables comparison with the perturbative calculations of Bottcher and Strayer (1990) and provides a framework for computing total pair production cross sections at colliders such as RHIC, where high-multiplicity events may show deviations from perturbation theory.
