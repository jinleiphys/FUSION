# Dilepton production in the strongly interacting quark-gluon plasma

> Dilepton production in relativistic heavy-ion collisions is studied within the microscopic Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which is based on a dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium. A comparison to the data of the NA60 Collaboration for In+In collisions at 158 AGeV shows that the dilepton spectra are well described by the sum of hadronic and partonic sources, if a collisional broadening of vector mesons is taken into account as well as the off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation (q+qbar->l^+l^- and q+qbar->gl^+l^-) in the QGP. In particular, the observed softening of the mT spectra at intermediate masses is reproduced. The data of the PHENIX collaboration on dilepton production in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=200 GeV for masses above 1 GeV are found to be dominated by the contributions of the QGP radiation and the charm meson decays, while the measured spectrum is underestimated in the mass range from 0.2 to 0.6 GeV.

## Key claim

The dilepton spectra from heavy-ion collisions can be described by the sum of hadronic and partonic sources within the PHSD transport approach, provided that collisional broadening of vector mesons and off-shell quark-antiquark annihilation in the QGP are both included; however, the low-mass dilepton spectra from Au+Au collisions at RHIC (0.2 to 0.6 GeV) remain underestimated even after including QGP radiation.

## Method

The authors employed the Parton-Hadron-String Dynamics (PHSD) transport approach, which solves generalized transport equations based on off-shell Kadanoff-Baym equations for Green's functions. The partonic phase is described using the dynamical quasiparticle model (DQPM) matched to reproduce lattice QCD results in thermodynamic equilibrium, with off-shell cross sections for \(q\bar q\to\gamma^*\), \(q\bar q\to\gamma^*+g\), and \(qg\to\gamma^*q\) reactions implemented. The hadronic sector uses the Hadron-String-Dynamics (HSD) approach with off-shell dynamics for vector mesons and in-medium spectral functions.

## Key numbers

- In+In collisions at 158 AGeV: the dilepton spectrum at invariant masses below 1 GeV is well reproduced by the \(\rho\) meson yield with in-medium broadening; the spectrum at \(M>1\) GeV is dominated by partonic sources.
- Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV: HSD results underestimate the measured minimum bias spectra in the mass range 0.2 to 0.6 GeV by approximately a factor of 5.
- Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV: between the \(\phi\) and \(J/\Psi\) peaks, HSD results underestimate the PHENIX data by approximately a factor of two.
- Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s}=200\) GeV: for \(M>2.5\) GeV, the partonic yield dominates over the D-meson contribution.

## Context

This work extends earlier hadronic model studies by Linnyk et al. (2009) that showed NA60 data favored the 'melting \(\rho\)' scenario with in-medium broadening of vector mesons. The PHSD approach adds explicit partonic channels that were missing in pure hadronic models, enabling reproduction of the softening of the \(m_T\) spectra at intermediate masses observed by NA60. At RHIC energies, the results are compared to calculations by van Hees and Rapp as well as Dusling and Zahed, showing similar underestimation of low-mass dileptons; the partonic radiation fills the gap between hadronic model results and data for \(M>1\) GeV.
