# The European Muon Collaboration effect from short-range correlated nucleons in a $x$-rescaling model

> In this paper, we examine the hypothesis that the nuclear EMC effect comes merely from the N-N SRC pairs inside the nucleus and that the properties of N-N SRC pair are universal among the various nuclei, using the conventional $x$-rescaling model for the EMC effect. With the previously determined effective mass of the short-range correlated nucleon and the number of N-N SRC pairs estimated, we calculate the EMC effect of various nuclei within the $x$-rescaling approach. From our calculations, the nuclear EMC effect due to the mass deficits of the SRC nucleons is not enough to reproduce the observed EMC effect in experiments. We speculate that the internal structure of the mean-field single nucleon is also obviously modified, or there are more origins of the EMC effect beyond the N-N SRC configuration (such as the $α$ cluster), or the universality of N-N SRC pair is violated noticeably from light to heavy nuclei.

# The European Muon Collaboration effect from short-range correlated nucleons in a $x$-rescaling model

## Key claim

The authors test whether nucleon-nucleon short-range correlated (N-N SRC) pairs are the sole source of the nuclear EMC effect using an $x$-rescaling model, and find that modifications to SRC nucleons alone are insufficient to reproduce the observed EMC effect. They conclude that either mean-field nucleons are also modified, the universality of N-N SRC pairs is violated across nuclei, or additional structures beyond N-N SRC (such as alpha clusters) contribute.

## Method

The authors apply the $x$-rescaling model, where the nuclear structure function is related to the free nucleon structure function by rescaling the Bjorken variable $x$ by a factor $\eta = m/m^*$, with $m^*$ the effective mass of the bound nucleon. They construct two models: Model-A, where only SRC nucleons are modified using a universal rescaling factor $\eta_{\rm SRC}=1.10$ determined from previous analysis of nuclear mass and SRC scaling ratios; and Model-B, where both SRC nucleons (with the same universal factor) and mean-field nucleons (with a nucleus-dependent rescaling factor $\eta_{\rm MF}$ fitted to EMC data) are modified. The number of SRC pairs in each nucleus is estimated from the measured SRC scaling ratio $a_2$ and the number of SRC pairs in deuteron, taken as $n^{\rm d}_{\rm SRC}=0.021$ from prior work.

## Key numbers

* The rescaling factor for SRC nucleons is fixed at $\eta_{\rm SRC}=1.10$, corresponding to an effective mass $m_{\rm SRC}=m/1.10$.
* The number of proton-neutron SRC pairs in deuteron is taken as $n^{\rm d}_{\rm SRC}=0.021$ from prior analysis, with an alternative value of $0.041$ from K. S. Egiyan et al. also tested.
* For Model-B, the fitted rescaling factors for mean-field nucleons range from $\eta_{\rm MF}=1.008\pm0.001$ for $^4$He to $\eta_{\rm MF}=1.027\pm0.001$ for $^{56}$Fe and $1.022\pm0.002$ for $^{208}$Pb.
* Model-A calculations are performed at $Q^2=5.3$ GeV$^2$ for light nuclei and $Q^2=2$ GeV$^2$ for heavy nuclei, matching experimental conditions.
* To explain the EMC effect with only SRC nucleons, $n^{\rm d}_{\rm SRC}$ would need to increase to about 0.1, which is contradictory to experimental analysis.

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether N-N SRC pairs are causally linked to the EMC effect, following the observed linear correlation between the EMC effect and SRC abundance reported by Weinstein (2010) and Hen (2012). The CLAS collaboration (2019) extracted a universal modification function for SRC pairs and argued that the EMC effect is consistent with being due entirely to SRC pairs. However, Wang (2020) analyzed the relationship incorporating nuclear binding and off-shell effects and concluded that SRC nucleons do not give the dominant EMC effect. The present study uses the $x$-rescaling model to test the SRC-driven hypothesis and finds it insufficient, supporting the conclusion of Wang (2020) and suggesting that mean-field nucleon modifications or additional structures beyond N-N SRC (such as 3N SRC or alpha clusters) are needed.
