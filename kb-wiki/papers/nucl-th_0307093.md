# Influence of medium correction of nucleon nucleon cross section on the fragmentation and nucleon emission

> The influence of medium correction from an isospin dependent nucleon nucleon cross section on the fragmentation and nucleon emission in the intermediate energy heavy ion collisions was studied by using an isospin dependent quantum molecular dynamical model (IQMD). We found that the medium correction enhances the dependence of multiplicity of intermediate mass fragment $N_{imf}$ and the number of nucleon emission $N_{n}$ on the isospin effect of the nucleon nucleon cross section,while the momentum dependent interaction (MDI) produces also an important role for enhancing the influence of the medium correction on the isospin dependence of two-body collision in the fragmentation and nucleon emission processes. After considering the medium correction and the role of momentum dependent interaction the increase for the dependence of $N_{imf}$ and $N_{n}$ on the isospin effect of two-body collision is favorable to learn the information about the isospin dependent nucleon nucleon cross section

# Influence of medium correction of nucleon nucleon cross section on the fragmentation and nucleon emission

## Key claim

The medium correction of the isospin-dependent nucleon-nucleon cross section (parameterized as α = -0.2 in σ_NN = (1 + α ρ/ρ₀) σ^free_NN) enhances the sensitivity of both the multiplicity of intermediate mass fragments (N_imf) and the number of nucleon emission (N_n) to the isospin dependence of two-body collisions, but this enhancement is conditional on also including the momentum dependent interaction (MDI), which plays an important role in amplifying the medium correction effect.

## Method

The authors used an isospin-dependent quantum molecular dynamics (IQMD) model to simulate heavy-ion collisions at intermediate energies, with initial density distributions from Skyrme-Hartree-Fock calculations using the SKM* parameter set. They compared results for two colliding systems of equal total mass (A_t + A_p = 152): a mass-symmetric system (⁷⁶Kr + ⁷⁶Kr) and a mass-asymmetric system (¹¹²Sn + ⁴⁰Ca), using both isospin-dependent (σ_iso) and isospin-independent (σ_noiso) in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross sections with medium correction parameter α = -0.2 versus α = 0.0 (free cross section), and with and without momentum dependent interaction (MDI). Clusters were constructed using a modified coalescence model with relative momentum cutoff p₀ = 300 MeV/c and relative distance cutoff R₀ = 3.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- The medium correction parameter used was α = -0.2 in the density-dependent cross section formula σ_NN = (1 + α ρ/ρ₀) σ^free_NN, compared to α = 0.0 for the free nucleon-nucleon collision case.
- The free neutron-proton cross section is approximately a factor of 3 larger than the free proton-proton or neutron-neutron cross section below 400 MeV.
- The beam energy range studied was from 50 MeV/nucleon to 300 MeV/nucleon.
- Impact parameter was fixed at b = 4.0 fm for the main analysis, with impact-parameter-averaged quantities <N_n>_b and <N_imf>_b evaluated at equilibrium time 200 fm/c.
- The intermediate mass fragment charge number range was taken from 3 to 13.
- The coalescence model used relative momentum cutoff p₀ = 300 MeV/c and relative distance cutoff R₀ = 3.5 fm.
- For the reaction ⁷⁶Kr + ⁷⁶Kr at E = 100 MeV/nucleon and b = 4.0 fm, the gap between N_imf lines with MDI was larger than the corresponding gap without MDI (NOMDI) in the medium (α = -0.2).
- For the reaction ⁷⁶Kr + ⁷⁶Kr at E = 150 MeV/nucleon and b = 4.0 fm, the peak nuclear density ratio ρ/ρ₀ was larger for σ_iso than for σ_noiso for both α = -0.2 and α = 0.0, and after about 70 fm/c the density ordering reversed.

## Context

This work extends previous studies by the same group (Liu et al., 2001-2003) showing that nuclear stopping, nucleon emission number, and intermediate mass fragment multiplicity can probe the isospin-dependent in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section. It builds on the proposal by Klakow et al. (1993) that the in-medium cross section depends on nuclear density as σ_NN = (1 + α ρ/ρ₀) σ^free_NN, with α = -0.2 found to reproduce flow data. The results enable more precise extraction of the isospin dependence of the in-medium nucleon-nucleon cross section from experimental measurements of N_imf and N_n, particularly when combined with momentum dependent interaction effects.
