# Inverse magnetic catalysis induced by sphalerons

> The recently discovered inverse magnetic catalysis around the critical temperature indicates that some important information is missing in our current understanding of conventional chiral dynamics of QCD, which is enhanced by the magnetic field. In this work, we provide a mechanism to explain that the inverse magnetic catalysis around the critical temperature is induced by sphalerons. At high temperatures, sphaleron transitions between distinct classical vacua cause an asymmetry between the number of right- and left-handed quarks due to the axial anomaly of QCD. In the presence of a strong magnetic field, the chiral imbalance is enhanced and destroys the right- and left-handed pairings, which naturally induces a decreasing critical temperature of the chiral phase transition for increasing magnetic field. The inverse magnetic catalysis at finite baryon density, and the critical end point in the presence of a strong magnetic field is also explored in this work.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the inverse magnetic catalysis around the critical temperature of the chiral phase transition is induced by sphaleron transitions, which create a chiral imbalance that is enhanced by a strong magnetic field and destroys right- and left-handed quark pairings. The mechanism is presented as happening in a scenario where P and CP symmetries are violated locally, meaning the chiral imbalance is nonzero only in squared expectation value, not as a vacuum expectation value.

## Method

The authors use the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation, incorporating a chiral chemical potential mu_5 to represent the chiral imbalance induced by sphaleron transitions. They model the magnetic field dependence of mu_5 using the sphaleron diffusion rate at strong coupling obtained from holographic AdS/CFT correspondence, finding mu_5 approximately proportional to sqrt(eB). The critical temperature of the chiral phase transition is then computed as a function of the magnetic field for different choices of mu_5, including the case where mu_5 scales with sqrt(eB).

## Key numbers

* Vacuum chiral condensate: <bar u u>^(1/3) = -245.7 MeV, with constituent quark mass M = 339 MeV and pion decay constant f_pi = 92.4 MeV (model parameters: Lambda = 620 MeV, G Lambda^2 = 2.2)
* For mu_5 = 0 and mu_5 = 0.1 GeV, the critical temperature T_c increases with eB (magnetic catalysis)
* For mu_5 = 0.5 sqrt(eB), the critical temperature T_c decreases with eB (inverse magnetic catalysis), reproducing the lattice result of Bali (2011)
* The sphaleron decay time is estimated to be about 80/T for the physical value of alpha_s
* The sphaleron acting window starts around 100 MeV

## Context

This work extends the lattice QCD discovery of inverse magnetic catalysis by Bali (2011), which showed that the chiral transition temperature decreases with increasing magnetic field, contradicting the magnetic catalysis expected from earlier effective model calculations and earlier lattice simulations. The paper provides an alternative mechanism to existing proposals such as those involving the neutral pion (Fukushima, 2012), the large N_c mass gap (Kojo, 2012), or sea quarks (Bruckmann, 2013). The mechanism relies on the enhanced chiral imbalance from sphaleron transitions under strong magnetic fields, supported by lattice evidence from Buividovich (2009) that <n_5^2> increases with eB. The work also explores the critical end point under strong magnetic fields, finding that (T_c, mu_c) for the CEP does not change much at high magnetic field, in contrast to the result of Menezes (2009) where mu_5 = 0 and the CEP moves toward the temperature axis with increasing magnetic field.
