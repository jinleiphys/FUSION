# Microscopic approach to large-amplitude deformation dynamics with local QRPA inertial masses

> We have developed a new method for determining microscopically the fivedimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian, on the basis of the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate method. This method consists of the constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equation and the local QRPA (LQRPA) equations, which are an extension of the usual QRPA (quasiparticle random phase approximation) to non-HFB-equilibrium points, on top of the CHFB states. One of the advantages of our method is that the inertial functions calculated with this method contain the contributions of the time-odd components of the mean field, which are ignored in the widely-used cranking formula. We illustrate usefulness of our method by applying to oblate-prolate shape coexistence in 72Kr and shape phase transition in neutron-rich Cr isotopes around N=40.

## Key claim
The authors develop a method for microscopically determining the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian that includes contributions from the time-odd components of the mean field to the inertial functions, which are ignored in the widely-used Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula. The method is an approximation to the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method, assuming a one-to-one correspondence between the collective manifold and the quadrupole deformation parameter space.

## Method
The method solves constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (CHFB) equations at every point of the (beta, gamma) deformation plane to obtain the collective potential, then solves local quasiparticle random-phase approximation (LQRPA) equations on top of these CHFB states to determine the vibrational and rotational inertial functions. The LQRPA equations are an extension of the usual QRPA to non-HFB-equilibrium points. The resulting five-dimensional collective Hamiltonian is quantized according to Pauli's prescription and solved to obtain excitation spectra, electric quadrupole transitions, and moments.

## Key numbers
- For 72Kr, effective charges were adjusted to (e(n)_eff, e(p)_eff) = (0.658, 1.658) such that the calculated B(E2;2_1+ -> 0_1+) reproduces the experimental value for 74Kr.
- For Cr isotopes, effective charges of (e(n)_eff, e(p)_eff) = (0.5, 1.5) were used.
- For Cr isotopes, the single-particle energies were scaled according to the effective mass of the SkM* functional m*/m = 0.79.
- The calculated excitation energies and R_{4/2} ratios for 58-66Cr reproduce available experimental data, with the largest collectivity at N=40.
- For 72Kr, the calculated 0_2+ excitation energy obtained with LQRPA masses agrees much better with experimental data than results using Inglis-Belyaev cranking masses.

## Context
This work extends the adiabatic self-consistent collective coordinate (ASCC) method developed by Matsuo (2000) and Hinohara (2007) to a practical computational framework for determining the five-dimensional quadrupole collective Hamiltonian. The method overcomes the well-known shortcoming of the Inglis-Belyaev cranking formula (Inglis 1954, Beliaev 1961), which underestimates collective masses by ignoring time-odd mean-field contributions. The approach enables microscopic calculations of shape coexistence phenomena (demonstrated for 72Kr) and shape phase transitions (demonstrated for neutron-rich Cr isotopes around N=40), with future implementation using modern energy density functionals such as Skyrme functionals identified as the next step.
