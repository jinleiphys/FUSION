# Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock versus chiral effective field theory

> We compare nuclear and neutron matter predictions based on two different ab initio approaches to nuclear forces and the nuclear many-body problem. The first consists of a realistic meson-theoretic nucleon-nucleon potential together with the relativistic counterpart of the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock theory of nuclear matter. The second is based on chiral effective field theory, with density-dependent interactions derived from leading order chiral three-nucleon forces. We find the results to be very close and conclude that both approaches contain important features governing the physics of nuclear and neutron matter.

# Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock versus chiral effective field theory

## Key claim

The DBHF method (using the Bonn B meson-exchange potential) and chiral EFT at N³LO plus leading three-nucleon forces produce very similar predictions for symmetric nuclear matter and neutron matter equations of state, symmetry energy, and neutron star properties, despite their fundamentally different theoretical foundations. The authors conclude that both approaches contain important features governing the physics of nuclear and neutron matter, though DBHF energies tend to grow faster at higher densities.

## Method

The authors compare two ab initio many-body approaches: (1) the relativistic Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (DBHF) method using the Bonn B one-boson-exchange potential with pseudovector pion coupling, which incorporates three-body forces through its density dependence; and (2) chiral effective field theory using the Idaho N³LO NN potential (cutoff 500 MeV) together with density-dependent effective two-nucleon interactions derived from leading-order (N²LO) chiral three-nucleon forces. For the chiral side, the low-energy constants c₁, c₃, c₄ are fixed from NN data, while c_D and c_E are fixed from triton and ³He binding energies. The in-medium G-matrix equation is solved self-consistently in both approaches, and the energy per particle is computed for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter across a range of densities.

## Key numbers

- The L parameter (slope of symmetry energy at saturation) is 69 MeV for Bonn B + DBHF and 61 MeV for N³LO + 3NF, compared to an accepted consensus range centered around 70 MeV with typical uncertainty of 20-25 MeV
- The chiral NN potential uses a cutoff Λ = 500 MeV
- Low-energy constants used: c₁ = -0.81 GeV⁻¹, c₃ = -3.2 GeV⁻¹, c₄ = 5.4 GeV⁻¹, c_D = 5.0, c_E = 0.48
- The chiral symmetry breaking scale is Λ_χ ≈ 1 GeV
- The density-dependent repulsive effect from the DBHF Z-diagram scales approximately as (ρ/ρ₀)^(8/3)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous DBHF studies of symmetric and isospin-asymmetric nuclear matter (Sammarruca, 2010) and compares them with the chiral EFT framework reviewed by Machleidt and Entem (2011). The comparison is motivated by the need to understand whether relativistic meson theory (traditionally considered more suitable for dense systems due to high Fermi momenta) and chiral EFT (limited by its low-momentum expansion up to Λ_χ ≈ 1 GeV) produce consistent predictions for applications ranging from rare isotope structure to neutron star properties. The results enable confidence in using either approach for extrapolations to dense astrophysical systems, with the chiral predictions extended to high densities via a fitted three-parameter function e(ρ) = αρ + βρ^γ.
