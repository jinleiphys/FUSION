# Pre-equilibrium dileptons look thermal

> The dilepton mass distribution from pre-equilibrium matter in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions is indistinguishable from a thermally produced distribution.

## Key claim

The dilepton mass distribution from pre-equilibrium matter in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions is indistinguishable from a thermally produced distribution, but this apparent thermal fit may be a coincidence or may indicate that high-energy tails of quark and gluon distributions approach equilibrium faster than the low-energy components.

## Method

The authors calculated the thermal dilepton mass distribution assuming boost-invariant longitudinal expansion with no transverse expansion, using an ideal gas of massless quarks and gluons. They fixed the temperature evolution by matching the final temperature T_f = 300 MeV at proper time tau_f = 2 fm/c from the parton cascade simulation of Geiger and Kapusta (1992). The thermal production rate was integrated over proper time and compared to the non-equilibrium parton cascade results.

## Key numbers

- Final temperature T_f = 300 MeV at proper time tau_f = 2 fm/c, used to fix the constant in the relation tau T^3 = constant
- Initial temperature T_0 = 950 MeV at proper time tau_0 = 0.063 fm/c, giving the best fit to the parton cascade results
- Cross-sectional area A = 150 fm^2 used for the hot matter region
- Transverse momentum cutoff p_perp_cut = 1.5 GeV used in the parton cascade model of Geiger and Kapusta (1992)
- Lowest mode energy at tau_0 is 1.55 GeV, slightly above the transverse momentum cutoff

## Context

This work extends the parton cascade calculations of dilepton production by Geiger and Kapusta (1992) for central Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV/nucleon, showing that the non-equilibrium results match a thermal distribution. The calculation follows the formalism of Kajantie, Kapusta, McLerran and Mekjian (1986) for thermal dilepton production from a quark-gluon plasma. The result demonstrates the difficulty of extracting information about non-equilibrium quark-gluon plasma from high-mass dilepton studies in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions.
