# Giant Resonances based on Unitarily Transformed Two-Nucleon plus Phenomenological Three-Nucleon Interactions

> We investigate giant resonances of spherical nuclei on the basis of the Argonne V18 potential after unitary transformation within the Similarity Renormalization Group or the Unitary Correlation Operator Method supplemented by a phenomenological three-body contact interaction. Such Hamiltonians can provide a good description of ground-state energies and radii within Hartree-Fock plus low-order many-body perturbation theory. The standard Random Phase Approximation is applied here to calculate the isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole excitation modes of the 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb nuclei. Thanks to the inclusion of the three-nucleon interaction and despite the minimal optimization effort, a reasonable agreement with experimental centroid energies of all three modes has been achieved. The role and scope of the Hartree-Fock reference state in RPA methods are discussed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of a simple phenomenological three-body contact interaction, added to unitarily transformed Argonne V18 two-body potentials, yields a reasonable agreement with experimental centroid energies for isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole giant resonances in 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb, despite the minimal optimization effort and the fact that the Hartree-Fock ground-state energies (without many-body perturbation theory corrections) deviate systematically from experimental binding energies.

## Method

The authors start from the Argonne V18 potential, softened via unitary transformations (Similarity Renormalization Group or Unitary Correlation Operator Method), and add a phenomenological three-body contact interaction with a variable strength parameter. They perform Hartree-Fock calculations in a harmonic-oscillator basis (truncated at e_max=14, l_max=10) to obtain the reference state, then apply the standard Random Phase Approximation to compute the response for isoscalar monopole, isovector dipole, and isoscalar quadrupole modes in 40Ca, 90Zr, and 208Pb.

## Key numbers

* For 40Ca, the isoscalar monopole centroid energy ranges from 17.92 MeV (S-SRG) to 19.81 MeV (UCOM(SRG)), compared to an experimental value of 19.18 MeV.
* For 208Pb, the isovector dipole centroid energy ranges from 15.72 MeV (SRG) to 17.88 MeV (UCOM(SRG)), compared to an experimental value of 13.6 MeV.
* For 208Pb, the isoscalar quadrupole centroid energy ranges from 13.43 MeV (SRG) to 14.07 MeV (UCOM(SRG)), compared to an experimental value of 10.9 MeV.
* The exhaustion of the classical isoscalar monopole sum rule lies between 90.5% (40Ca, UCOM(SRG)) and 98.4% (208Pb, S-SRG).
* The smallest percentage found for the Thomas-Reiche-Kuhn sum rule (isovector dipole) was 168.3% for 40Ca based on the SRG interaction.
* The energy of the spurious dipole state never exceeds 20 keV in the present cases.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies that used pure two-body UCOM interactions (Roth 2005) and found poor agreement for giant resonances (Paar 2006). It also builds on a previous paper (Gunther 2010) that showed that adding a phenomenological three-body contact interaction to unitarily transformed Argonne V18 allows a good simultaneous description of ground-state energies and radii. The results are compared to those from the Gogny D1S interaction (Berger 1991) and are found to be of similar quality. The study enables future work on second-order RPA and quasi-particle RPA for open-shell nuclei, and serves as a benchmark before moving to chiral two-plus-three-nucleon interactions.
