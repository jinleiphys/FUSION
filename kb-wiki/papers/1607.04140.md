# Multi-Skyrmions with orientational moduli

> We analyze the mechanism of condensation of orientational moduli (as introduced in [25]) on multi-Skyrmionic configurations of the four-dimensional Skyrme model. The present analysis reveals interesting novel features. First of all, the orientational moduli tend to decrease the repulsive interactions between Skyrmions, the effect decreasing with the increase of the Baryon number. Moreover, in the case of a single Skyrmion, the appearance of moduli is energetically favorable if finite volume effects are present. Otherwise, in the usual flat topologically trivial case, it is not. In the low energy theory these solutions can be interpreted as Skyrmions with additional isospin degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

The paper claims that orientational moduli (Goldstone bosons from breaking a global non-Abelian symmetry on the soliton core) can condense on multi-Skyrmion configurations, and that this condensation decreases the repulsive interactions between Skyrmions in finite-volume geometries, with the effect decreasing as baryon number increases. However, in flat topologically trivial spacetime, the moduli-condensed Skyrmion is only metastable, not energetically preferred.

## Method

The authors generalize the Shifman (2005) model for orientational moduli condensation by coupling the SU(2) Skyrme Lagrangian to a triplet scalar field χ^i with an interaction term designed to make χ condense on the Skyrmion core. They solve the coupled field equations numerically using a second-order finite difference procedure, first in flat Minkowski spacetime with a hedgehog ansatz, then in a tubular geometry (R^(1,1) × S^2) that allows analytic treatment of finite-volume effects and multi-Skyrmion configurations of arbitrary baryon number. The stability of solutions is analyzed via linear perturbation theory, reducing the system to coupled Schrödinger equations.

## Key numbers

- For a single Skyrmion in flat spacetime with parameters γ/κ=1, β=0.05, λ=1/8, Γ=1: energy with moduli is E/(2π√κ)=5.966, while the pure Skyrmion energy is E/(2π√κ)=5.804 (moduli solution is higher in energy).
- The lowest normalizable perturbation mode for the flat-space solution gives ω̃²≈1.1>0, and orthogonal color perturbation modes give ω̃²≈8>0, indicating metastability.
- The moment of inertia for the orientational moduli low-energy action evaluates to I₁√κ/(4π)=∫ρ²χ̃²dρ=0.372.
- In the tubular geometry (√κ R=0.27, λ=1/8, |β|=0.4, Γ=0, γ/κ=1/4): percentage energy differences favoring moduli-condensed solutions over pure Skyrmions are: n=1: 3.05%, n=2: 3.24%, n=3: 4.03%, n=4: 3.88%, n=5: 2.94%.

## Context

This work extends the Shifman (2005) mechanism for orientational moduli condensation, previously applied to vortices and monopoles, to the Skyrme model, using the exact multi-Skyrmion solutions in tubular geometry developed by Canfora et al. (2013, 2014). It contrasts with the flat-space case where moduli condensation is energetically unfavorable, and with the vortex and monopole cases where moduli condensation is energetically preferred. The results enable future studies of Skyrmions with additional isospin degrees of freedom and suggest that moduli can partially screen repulsive Skyrmion-Skyrmion interactions in finite-volume nuclear physics and astrophysics contexts.
