# Analytic $G_0W_0$ gradients based on a double-similarity transformation equation-of-motion coupled-cluster treatment

> The accurate prediction of ionization potentials (IPs) is central to understanding molecular reactivity, redox behavior, and spectroscopic properties. While vertical IPs can be accessed directly from electronic excitations at fixed nuclear geometries, the computation of adiabatic IPs requires nuclear gradients of the ionized states, posing a major theoretical and computational challenge, especially within correlated frameworks. Among the most promising approaches for IP calculations is the many-body Green's function $GW$ method, which provides a balanced compromise between accuracy and computational efficiency. Furthermore, it is applicable to both finite and extended systems. Recent work has established formal connections between $GW$ and coupled-cluster doubles (CCD) theory, leading to the first derivation of analytic $GW$ nuclear gradients via a unitary CCD framework. In this work, we present an alternative, fully analytic formulation of $GW$ nuclear gradients based on a modified version of the traditional equation-of-motion CCD formalism, enabling the inclusion of missing correlation effects in the traditional CCD methods.

## Key claim

This work presents a fully analytic formulation of $G_0W_0$ nuclear gradients using a modified equation-of-motion coupled-cluster doubles (EOM-CCD) formalism, offering an alternative to the recently developed unitary CCD framework for computing adiabatic ionization potentials.

## Method

The authors derive analytic $G_0W_0$ gradients by reformulating the traditional EOM-CCD approach through a double-similarity transformation, enabling the inclusion of correlation effects missing in standard CCD methods. They develop the working equations for the direct-ring CCD (drCCD) approximation, including amplitude equations, $\lambda$-amplitude equations, and one- and two-electron density matrices, along with the EA/IP-EOM-$\lambda$-drCCD eigenvalue equations. The diagrammatic rules for the direct-ring approximation are explicitly defined, including conventions for loop connectivity, symmetrization of non-equivalent external indices, and sign conventions for left and right EOM operators.

## Key numbers

- The drCCD correlation energy diagram is interpreted as $\frac{1}{2}\sum_{ijab}\ERI{ij}{ab}t^{ab}_{ij}$
- For the $\Gamma_{iabj}$ block of the two-body reduced density matrix, the energy contribution is multiplied by a factor of $4$ for each of the combinations $iabj$, $aijb$, $aibj$, and $iajb$, resulting in $4 \cdot \frac{1}{2} \sum_{iabj} \Gamma_{iabj}\ERI{ia}{bj}$
- Within the ring approximation, the $aibj$ and $iajb$ blocks are absent, introducing an additional factor of $\frac{1}{2}$ in the contributions from $\Gamma_{iabj}\ERI{ia}{bj}$

## Context

This work extends recent developments that established formal connections between $GW$ and coupled-cluster doubles (CCD) theory, which led to the first derivation of analytic $GW$ nuclear gradients via a unitary CCD framework. The present formulation provides an alternative approach based on a modified EOM-CCD formalism that can incorporate missing correlation effects not captured by traditional CCD methods. This enables the computation of adiabatic ionization potentials through analytic nuclear gradients of ionized states, which is essential for predicting molecular reactivity, redox behavior, and spectroscopic properties.
