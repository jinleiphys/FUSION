# First saturation correction in high energy proton-nucleus collisions: Part III. Ensemble averaging

> In high energy proton-nucleus collisions, the gluon saturation effects from the nucleus are fully incorporated into the light-like Wilson lines. The gluon saturation effects from the proton, which are anticipated to be important either in the extreme high energy limit or towards the dense-dense (nucleus-nucleus) collision regimes, have been studied perturbatively within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory in previous papers of this series. A configuration-by-configuration expression for the single inclusive semi-hard gluon production including the first saturation correction was obtained. In this paper, we perform ensemble averaging in the McLerran-Venugopalan model and the Dipole Approximation. We find that, in the saturation correction, the effects of the initial state interactions are negligible while the final state interactions play most important role and give a positive-valued contribution to the semi-hard gluon spectrum. Furthermore, we show that the single gluon spectrum scales approximately $1/k_{\perp}^{4}$ at small $k_{\perp}$, suggesting that a resummation of higher order saturation corrections is required to regulate the infrared region of the gluon spectrum.

## Key claim

The first saturation correction to the single inclusive semi-hard gluon spectrum in high energy proton-nucleus collisions is dominated by final state interactions (which give a positive-valued contribution), while initial state interactions are negligible; the resulting gluon spectrum scales approximately as \(1/k_{\perp}^{4}\) at small transverse momentum, indicating that a resummation of higher order saturation corrections is needed to regulate the infrared region.

## Method

The authors start from the configuration-by-configuration expression for single inclusive semi-hard gluon production including the first saturation correction, derived in previous papers of this series using next-to-leading order solutions of the classical Yang-Mills equations in the dilute-dense regime. They perform ensemble averaging over both the projectile (proton) and target (nucleus) color charge densities using the McLerran-Venugopalam (MV) model with Gaussian distributions. For the target, they employ the Dipole Approximation to evaluate adjoint Wilson line correlators involving more than two Wilson lines, factorizing them into products of dipole operators.

## Key numbers

* The leading order gluon spectrum scales as \(1/k_{\perp}^{2}\) at small \(k_{\perp}\) (for \(k_{\perp} \ll Q_{s,T}\)) and as \(1/k_{\perp}^{4}\) at large \(k_{\perp}\) (for \(k_{\perp} \gg Q_{s,T}\)).
* The first saturation correction term scales approximately as \(1/k_{\perp}^{4}\) at small \(k_{\perp}\) and as \(1/k_{\perp}^{6}\) at large \(k_{\perp}\) (for \(k_{\perp} \gg Q_{s,T}\)).
* The parametric expansion of the single inclusive gluon spectrum is given by \(\frac{dN}{d^2\mathbf{k}} = \frac{1}{\alpha_s}\sum_{n = 1}^{\infty} \left[\frac{Q_{s,P}^2}{k_{\perp}^2}\right]^n f_{(n)} \left(\frac{Q_{s,T}^2}{k_{\perp}^2}\right)\), where \(\alpha_s = g^2/4\pi\) is the strong coupling constant.

## Context

This work is Part III of a series (following Li and Skokov (2021) and Li and Skokov (2021)) that studies gluon saturation effects in the proton within the Color Glass Condensate effective theory. It extends the perturbative treatment of saturation corrections to include ensemble averaging, which is necessary to relate theoretical predictions to experimental data from RHIC and LHC. The Dipole Approximation, proposed by Kovner and Rezaeian (2017), is used to handle target Wilson line correlators; the authors provide numerical evidence that this approximation works well for four-Wilson-line correlators. The results enable future all-order resummation studies needed to tame the infrared divergence of the gluon spectrum.
