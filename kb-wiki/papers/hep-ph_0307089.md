# Functional integrals for QCD at nonzero chemical potential and zero density

> In a Euclidean space functional integral treatment of the free energy of QCD, a chemical potential enters only through the functional determinant of the Dirac operator which for any flavor is $\dslash + m - μ_f γ_0$ (where $μ_f$ is the chemical potential for the given flavor). Any nonzero $μ$ alters all of the eigenvalues of the Dirac operator relative to the $μ=0$ value, leading to a naive expectation that the determinant is altered and which thereby alters the free energy. Phenomenologically, this does not occur at T=0 for sufficiently small $μ$, in contradiction to this naive expectation. The problem of how to understand this phenomenological behavior in terms functional integrals is solved for the case of an isospin chemical through the study of the spectrum of the operator $γ_0 (\dslash + m)$.

## Key claim

The paper resolves the "isospin Silver Blaze problem", the apparent contradiction between the naive expectation that any nonzero chemical potential alters the QCD functional determinant and the phenomenological fact that at zero temperature the free energy is unchanged for small isospin chemical potential, by showing that the functional determinant for any gauge configuration remains unchanged from its μ_I=0 value unless |μ_I|/2 exceeds the minimum positive quasi-energy of the operator γ_0(⧸D + m), provided no first-order phase transition occurs for |μ_I| < m_π.

## Method

The author studies the eigenspectrum of the operator γ_0(⧸D + m) (the "quasi-energy" operator) in Euclidean space, showing that the functional determinant of the Dirac operator with isospin chemical potential can be expressed via the trace of this operator's resolvent. The trace is evaluated by summing over eigenvalues grouped into families indexed by an intrinsic state j and a Matsubara-like index n, using anti-periodic boundary conditions in the temporal direction. The zero-temperature limit (β → ∞) is then taken analytically, yielding a form where theta functions enforce that the determinant is unchanged unless |μ_I| exceeds twice the quasi-energy.

## Key numbers

- The critical isospin chemical potential for the phase transition to pion condensation is |μ_I| = m_π (the pion mass), below which the energy density and isospin density are zero at T=0.
- The minimum positive quasi-energy ε_min is shown to equal m_π/2, establishing the threshold condition |μ_I| = 2ε_min = m_π for the functional determinant to change.
- For the baryon chemical potential, the Silver Blaze problem persists for all μ_B < M_N − B ≈ 923 MeV, where M_N is the nucleon mass and B the binding energy per nucleon of nuclear matter.

## Context

This work extends the understanding of how chemical potentials affect QCD functional integrals, building on prior studies of the Dirac operator spectrum in random matrix models (Verbaarschot, 1994) and lattice QCD (Barbour et al., 1997), as well as the Glasgow method for lattice simulations. The resolution of the isospin Silver Blaze problem enables a rigorous functional-integral understanding of why nothing happens below the pion condensation threshold, and it opens the challenge of understanding the baryon Silver Blaze problem, where cancellations from phase factors in the functional determinant must compensate for magnitude changes in the regime m_π < μ_B < M_N − B, which is essential for gaining insight into the first-order phase transition to nuclear matter.
