# Hydrodynamic Description of Heavy Ion Collisions

> We give a short review of hydrodynamic models at heavy ion collisions from the point of view of initial conditions, an equation of states (EoS) and freezeout process. Then we show our latest results of a combined fully three-dimensional macroscopic/microscopic transport approach. In this model for the early, dense, deconfined stage relativistic 3D-hydrodynamics of the reaction and a microscopic non-equilibrium model for the later hadronic stage where the equilibrium assumptions are not valid anymore are employed. Within this approach we study the dynamics of hot, bulk QCD matter, which is being created in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim
The paper argues that while ideal hydrodynamic models successfully describe many bulk observables at RHIC, they fail for certain quantities (e.g., elliptic flow at forward/backward rapidity and peripheral collisions, HBT radii), and a hybrid 3D hydrodynamics + microscopic cascade model (UrQMD) provides a more realistic description by dynamically treating the hadronic freezeout stage where equilibrium assumptions break down.

## Method
The authors combine a relativistic 3+1 dimensional ideal hydrodynamic model for the early, deconfined QGP stage with the microscopic Ultra-relativistic Quantum-Molecular-Dynamics (UrQMD) model for the later hadronic stage. The hydrodynamic evolution is terminated at a switching temperature of 150 MeV (just below the critical temperature of 160 MeV), at which point hadron distributions are calculated via the Cooper-Frye formula and used as initial conditions for the UrQMD cascade. The model uses Glauber-type initial conditions and an equation of state with a strong first-order phase transition (Bag model).

## Key numbers
- Switching temperature from hydrodynamics to UrQMD: 150 MeV; critical temperature: 160 MeV
- At RHIC, collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV and sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV are studied
- For central collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV, the model reproduces P_T spectra of pi+, K, and p without additional normalization corrections
- Impact parameters for centrality bins: b = 2.4 fm (0-6%), 4.5 fm (6-15%), 6.3 fm (15-25%), 7.9 fm (25-35%)
- For elliptic flow v_2 as a function of P_T: at low P_T nearly 100% of elliptic flow is created in the QGP phase; hadronic phase contribution increases to 25% at P_T = 1 GeV/c
- Mean P_T as a function of hadron mass at T_sw = 150 MeV follows a straight line (hydrodynamic expansion), but hadronic rescattering reduces pion mean P_T and increases proton mean P_T, improving agreement with STAR data at sqrt(s_NN) = 130 GeV

## Context
This work extends the pure hydrodynamic calculations of Kolb et al. (2001) and the partial chemical equilibrium approach of Hirano and Kolb, which required additional normalization or initial transverse flow to match data. The hybrid hydro+UrQMD approach builds on earlier hybrid models by Bass and others, and is compared to calculations using CGC initial conditions by Hirano et al. (2006). The results enable quantitative study of dissipative effects in the hadronic phase and provide a framework for investigating jet-medium interactions such as mach cone formation. The paper identifies open questions: the need for more realistic equations of state (lattice QCD suggests crossover rather than first-order transition), the role of viscosity, and the determination of optimal initial conditions and freezeout processes before conclusive statements about the QCD phase transition can be made.
