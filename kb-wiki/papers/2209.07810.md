# Finite particle-number description of symmetric nuclear matter with spin excitations of high-momentum pairs induced by tensor force

> We study the symmetric nuclear matter using bare nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions with finite particle-number approach within finite cubic boxes. Due to the $NN$ correlations originating from bare $NN$ interaction, two nucleons can be excited to the high-momentum region, leading to the increase of the kinetic energy in nuclear matter. We further consider the spin excitations in the nucleon pairs, where the spin of the two nucleons are changed, and this excitation is important for the tensor correlation. The unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) is used to treat the short-range correlation. The tail correction coming from the neighbouring boxes is also included. We demonstrate the contributions of various excitations of nucleon pairs as well as the tail correction to the total energy at the normal density. We also discuss the effects of UCOM and correlated nucleon pairs on the density dependence of the total energy. We calculate the equations of state of symmetric nuclear matter using two kinds of the Argonne potentials and the results agree with those from other many-body theories. The density dependences of the Hamiltonian components are also shown.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that including spin excitation modes (where the total spin of a correlated nucleon pair changes by \(\Delta S_z=1\) or \(\Delta S_z=2\)) in the 2p2h excitation description of high-momentum pairs is essential for capturing the tensor correlation in symmetric nuclear matter, but only when combined with the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM) for short-range correlation and a tail correction from neighboring boxes does the total energy converge to values consistent with auxiliary-field diffusion Monte Carlo (AFDMC) results.

## Method

The authors place symmetric nuclear matter in finite cubic boxes with periodic boundary conditions, using a Slater determinant of plane-wave single-particle states as the 0p0h reference. Short-range correlations are treated via the unitary correlation operator method (UCOM), which transforms the Hamiltonian to incorporate a short-range correlation function \(R_+(r)\) with channel-dependent parameters. Tensor correlations are described by introducing 2p2h excitations of nucleon pairs into high-momentum states, classified into eight modes including pure momentum excitation, spin exchange, isospin exchange, spin-isospin exchange, and spin excitation modes with \(\Delta S_z=1\) and \(\Delta S_z=2\); the tail correction from neighboring boxes is included by allowing 2p2h configurations where the two correlated nucleons reside in different boxes. The total wave function is a linear combination of the 0p0h state and all 2p2h configurations, with amplitudes variationally determined by solving the eigenvalue problem of the transformed Hamiltonian using the power method.

## Key numbers

*   At normal density \(\rho = 0.17\ \text{fm}^{-3}\) with mass number \(A=28\) and the AV6' potential, the 0p0h total energy per particle is about 35.2 MeV; after UCOM treatment this falls to -0.4 MeV; after including all 2p2h excitations and tail correction the converged energy is around -13.8 MeV.
*   At normal density with the AV8' potential and \(A=28\), the 0p0h energy is about 32.4 MeV; after UCOM it is 1.1 MeV; the final converged energy is around -13.3 MeV.
*   With only the central box (\(N_\text{b}=1\)) and no spin excitation mode, the converged energy with AV6' is about -3.6 MeV; including spin excitation modes in the central box lowers it to -9.3 MeV.
*   The tail correction from neighboring boxes (comparing \(N_\text{b}=1\) to \(N_\text{b}=8\)) contributes about 4.2 MeV to the total energy per particle at normal density with AV6'.
*   The total number of 2p2h configurations for \(A=28\) with spin excitation modes and \(N_\text{b}=8\) reaches up to \(10^8\) for the maximum transfer mode considered.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous UCOM+HM approach (Wan et al., 2021, 2022) from neutron matter to symmetric nuclear matter, where the tensor force is stronger for proton-neutron pairs. It introduces spin excitation modes not present in earlier 2p2h treatments of nuclear matter, and shows that these modes are necessary to approach the benchmark AFDMC results of Gandolfi et al. (2014) and the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock benchmark calculations of Baldo et al. (2012). The method enables systematic study of tensor correlation effects in symmetric nuclear matter using bare nucleon-nucleon interactions within a finite particle-number framework, and can be extended to include three-body forces and full Argonne potentials with charge-breaking components.
