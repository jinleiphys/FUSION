# Analysis of the $N_f=2+1$ lattice QCD results on the lowest-lying baryon masses using covariant ChPT

> We review recent progress in the understanding of low-energy baryon structure by means of chiral perturbation theory. In particular, we discuss the application of this formalism to the description of the quark mass dependence of recent Lattice QCD results on the masses. We present the chiral extrapolation of those of the PACS-CS and LHP collaborations and we predict the baryonic sigma-terms.

# Analysis of the $N_f=2+1$ lattice QCD results on the lowest-lying baryon masses using covariant ChPT

## Key claim
The covariant (Lorentz-invariant) formulation of $SU(3)_F$ baryon chiral perturbation theory (B$\chi$PT) up to next-to-leading order (NLO) can successfully describe the quark-mass dependence of recent lattice QCD results on the lowest-lying baryon masses, in contrast to the heavy-baryon (HB) approach which had encountered difficulties. The authors claim that the extrapolated masses at the physical point are manifestly better at NLO than those obtained using the linear extrapolation given by the Gell-Mann–Okubo (GMO) approach at leading order, but note that the consistency between lattice and experimental determinations is only partial for the decuplet sector.

## Method
The authors performed chiral extrapolations of lattice QCD baryon mass data from the PACS-CS and LHP collaborations using Lorentz covariant $SU(3)_F$ baryon chiral perturbation theory up to NLO, with an extended-on-mass-shell (EOMS) power-counting scheme that systematically incorporates the lowest-lying decuplet resonances. They fitted the low-energy constants (LECs) of the theory to the lattice data and compared the resulting values with those obtained from fits to experimental baryon masses. The sigma terms were then predicted via the Hellman-Feynman theorem applied to the fitted mass functions.

## Key numbers
- PACS-CS lattice data used include points at $m_\pi \simeq 156$ MeV (the lightest pion mass in that set).
- LHP lattice data used include points at $m_\pi \gtrsim 293$ MeV.
- The GMO relations (octet and decuplet) are recovered at LO in $\chi$PT and are known to work with an accuracy of $\sim 7$ MeV; leading chiral corrections preserve the GMO equations within $\sim 10$ MeV while corrections to individual baryon masses are of order $\sim 100$–$1000$ MeV.
- For the baryon-octet sector, the effective mass parameter $M_{B0}^{eff}$ from the PACS-CS fit is 1.76(7) GeV and from the LHP fit is 1.85(8) GeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.840(5) GeV.
- For the baryon-decuplet sector, the effective mass $M_{T0}^{eff}$ from the PACS-CS fit is 1.49(8) GeV and from the LHP fit is 1.60(8) GeV, compared to the experimental value of 1.519(2) GeV.
- The predicted pion-nucleon sigma term $\sigma_{\pi N}$ is 59(2)(17) MeV from the PACS-CS fit and 61(2)(21) MeV from the LHP fit.
- The predicted strange-nucleon sigma term $\sigma_{sN}$ is -7(23)(25) MeV from the PACS-CS fit and -4(20)(25) MeV from the LHP fit.

## Context
This work extends the application of Lorentz covariant baryon chiral perturbation theory (previously developed by Fuchs et al. (2003), Pascalutsa (2000), and Geng et al. (2009)) to the analysis of lattice QCD results on baryon masses, specifically addressing the difficulties encountered by the heavy-baryon approach in describing the quark-mass dependence of data from the PACS-CS collaboration (Aoki et al., 2008) and the LHP collaboration (Walker-Loud et al., 2008). The successful description enables reliable chiral extrapolations of lattice data and predictions of baryonic sigma terms, which are relevant for understanding explicit chiral symmetry breaking and for phenomenology of dark matter detection and other areas. The sigma term results agree with those obtained by Young et al. (2009) using cutoff-renormalized B$\chi$PT. The authors note that an analysis of lattice QCD results from other collaborations is in progress.
