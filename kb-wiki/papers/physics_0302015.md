# Limits on temporal variation of quark masses and strong interaction from atomic clock experiments

> We perform calculations of the dependence of nuclear magnetic moments on quark masses and obtain limits on the variation of m_q/Lambda_{QCD} from recent atomic clock experiments with hyperfine transitions in H, Rb, Cs, Hg+ and optical transtion in Hg+.

## Key claim

The paper derives limits on the temporal variation of the dimensionless ratio \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) from atomic clock experiments, but the limits are conditional on the specific model used to relate nuclear magnetic moment variation to quark mass variation, and the results are presented as bounds on combined parameters that mix \(\alpha\) and \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) rather than on \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\) alone.

## Method

The author calculates the dependence of nuclear magnetic moments on the pion mass \(m_\pi\) (and hence on \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\)) using a chiral perturbation theory result from Thomas (2002) that gives nucleon g-factors as functions of \(m_\pi\). These g-factor variations are then inserted into single-particle nuclear magnetic moment formulas for H, \({}^{199}\)Hg, \({}^{133}\)Cs, and \({}^{87}\)Rb to obtain the sensitivity of each nucleus to quark mass variation. The resulting sensitivities are combined with published atomic clock comparison data (hyperfine ratios and an optical transition ratio) to extract limits on the time variation of combined parameters that include both \(\alpha\) and \(m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}\).

## Key numbers

- For the \({}^{199}\)Hg/H hyperfine ratio: limit on the combined parameter \(\tilde{\alpha} = \alpha [m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{-0.01}\) is \(|\frac{1}{\tilde{\alpha}}\frac{d\tilde{\alpha}}{dt}| < 3.6 \times 10^{-14}/\text{year}\).
- For the \({}^{133}\)Cs/\({}^{87}\)Rb hyperfine ratio: \(\frac{1}{X}\frac{dX}{dt} = (0.2 \pm 7) \times 10^{-16}/\text{year}\) where \(X = \alpha^{0.49} [m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{0.17}\).
- For the \({}^{133}\)Cs/H hyperfine ratio: \(|\frac{1}{X_H}\frac{dX_H}{dt}| < 5.5 \times 10^{-14}/\text{year}\) where \(X_H = \alpha^{0.83} [m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{0.2}\).
- For the Cs hyperfine / Hg optical transition ratio: \(|\frac{1}{U}\frac{dU}{dt}| < 7 \times 10^{-15}/\text{year}\) where \(U = \alpha^{6} [m_e/\Lambda_{QCD}][m_q/\Lambda_{QCD}]^{0.1}\).
- The proton g-factor variation is \(\delta g_p/g_p = -0.087 \, \delta m_q/m_q\); the neutron g-factor variation is \(\delta g_n/g_n = -0.107 \, \delta m_q/m_q\).

## Context

This work extends the earlier rough estimates of Flambaum and Shuryak (2002) by providing more detailed calculations of nuclear magnetic moment dependence on quark masses and incorporating newer atomic clock data from Prestage (2001), Marion (2003), and Bize (2003). The results enable tighter laboratory constraints on the variation of the strong interaction scale and quark masses, complementing limits from Big Bang Nucleosynthesis, quasar absorption spectra, and the Oklo natural reactor (Flambaum and Shuryak, 2002; Dmitriev, 2003; Murphy, 2001). The paper also shows that the Hg/H hyperfine ratio is nearly insensitive to quark mass variation, while the Cs/Rb ratio offers the best sensitivity.
