# Exploring universal characteristics of neutron star matter with relativistic \textit{ab initio} equations of state

> Starting from the relativistic realistic nucleon-nucleon ($NN$) interactions, a newly developed relativistic \textit{ab initio} method, i.e., the relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory in the full Dirac space is employed to study the neutron star properties. First, the one-to-one correspondence relation for gravitational redshift and mass is established and used to infer the mass of isolated neutron stars combining the gravitational redshift measurements. Next, the ratio of the moment of inertia $I$ to $MR^2$ as a function of the compactness $M/R$ is obtained, which is consistent with the universal relations in the literature. The moment of inertia for $1.338M_\odot$ pulsar PSR J0737-3039A $I_{1.338M_\odot}$ is predicted to be 1.356$\times10^{45}$, 1.381$\times10^{45}$, and $1.407\times10^{45}\ \mathrm{g~cm^2}$ by the RBHF theory in the full Dirac space with $NN$ interactions Bonn A, B, and C, respectively. Finally, the quadrupole moment of neutron star is calculated under the slow-rotation and small-tidal-deformation approximation. The equation of states constructed by the RBHF theory in the full Dirac space, together with those by the projection method and momentum-independence approximation, conform to universal $I$-Love-$Q$ relations as well. By combing the tidal deformability from GW170817 and the universal relations from relativistic \textit{ab initio} methods, the moment of inertia of neutron star with 1.4 solar mass is also deduced as $I_{1.4M_\odot}=1.22^{+0.40}_{-0.25}\times 10^{45}\mathrm{g\ cm^2}$.

## Key claim

The relativistic Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (RBHF) theory in the full Dirac space, which includes both positive-energy and negative-energy states, produces neutron star equations of state that satisfy universal I-Love-Q relations, but the predicted moment of inertia for a 1.338 solar mass neutron star is systematically lower than predictions from the projection method or momentum-independence approximation, with the Bonn A interaction giving a value very close to the most probable Bayesian estimate.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent RBHF calculations in the full Dirac space for asymmetric nuclear matter using the relativistic Bonn A, B, and C nucleon-nucleon interactions, constructing equations of state for neutron star matter in beta equilibrium with electrons and muons. They solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equations for neutron star structure, computed the moment of inertia under the slow-rotation approximation of Hartle and Thorne, and calculated the quadrupole moment under the slow-rotation and small-tidal-deformation approximation. For densities above 0.57 fm^{-3}, the equation of state was replaced with the maximally stiff causal equation of state, and for the crust the Baym-Bethe-Pethick and Baym-Pethick-Sutherland models were used.

## Key numbers

* Maximum neutron star mass predicted by RBHF in the full Dirac space with Bonn A: 2.43 solar masses
* Radius of a 1.4 solar mass neutron star from RBHF in the full Dirac space: 11.97 km
* Moment of inertia for PSR J0737-3039A (1.338 solar masses) predicted by RBHF in the full Dirac space: 1.356 x 10^45 g cm^2 (Bonn A), 1.381 x 10^45 g cm^2 (Bonn B), and 1.407 x 10^45 g cm^2 (Bonn C)
* Moment of inertia for a 1.4 solar mass neutron star deduced from GW170817 tidal deformability combined with universal relations: I_{1.4M_sun} = 1.22^{+0.40}_{-0.25} x 10^45 g cm^2
* Speed of sound squared at density 0.57 fm^{-3} from RBHF in the full Dirac space: 0.77
* Nuclear matter saturation density from RBHF in the full Dirac space: 0.188 fm^{-3}, with symmetry energy 33.1 MeV and slope L = 65.2 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous RBHF calculations in the full Dirac space for symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter by Wang et al. (2021, 2022) to compute additional neutron star global properties including gravitational redshift, moment of inertia, and quadrupole moment. The results confirm the universal I-Love-Q relations previously established by Yagi and collaborators (2013, 2014) using a variety of nuclear models, and are consistent with the universal I/MR^2 versus compactness relation from Lattimer and Schutz (2005) and the Bayesian analysis by Lim et al. (2019). The predicted masses for isolated neutron stars RBS 1223, RX J1856.5-3754, and RX J0720.4-3125 are obtained by combining gravitational redshift measurements with the theoretical mass-redshift relation, enabling future constraints on neutron star equations of state from redshift observations.
