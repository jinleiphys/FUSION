# Hybrid stars can be self-bound

> Based on the properties of uniform nuclear matter at the nuclear saturation density and basic thermodynamic relations, we first re-study the composition of matter on the surface of normal neutron stars and hybrid stars. It is found that the hybrid stars are composed of uniform hadronic matter on the surface rather than heavy nuclei. Then we use the Walceka model and the self-consistent NJL model to describe the equation of state of low-density hadrons and quark matter at high densities respectively, and the P--interpolation method is employed to connect the equation of state at the extreme densities to study hybrid stars. As a result, we find that the obtained hybrid star mass-radius relation and tidal deformability meet the latest astronomical data. More importantly, we find that the hybrid stars we obtained can be self-bound rather than gravitationally bound, which is completely different from previous related studies.

## Key claim

Hybrid stars can be self-bound rather than gravitationally bound, meaning their surface consists of uniform hadronic matter at nuclear saturation density rather than heavy nuclei or inhomogeneous "pasta" phases, but this conclusion depends on the assumption that for sufficiently large baryon number (as in a neutron star) the bulk effects that stabilize iron nuclei become negligible.

## Method

The authors used the Walecka model to describe hadronic matter at low densities and a self-consistent two-flavor NJL model with vector interactions (controlled by a mixing parameter alpha) for quark matter at high densities. They employed the P-interpolation method on the pressure-chemical potential plane to smoothly connect the hadronic and quark equations of state in the deconfinement region between 2 and 4 times nuclear saturation density. The Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations were then solved to obtain mass-radius relations and tidal deformabilities.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear saturation density from the Walecka model: n0 = 0.155 fm^-3 (mean field theory) versus empirical 0.16 ± 0.01 fm^-3
- Chemical potential at saturation from Walecka model: μ0 = 923 MeV (without beta equilibrium) and μ0 = 937 MeV (with beta equilibrium)
- Binding energy from Walecka model: E0 = 16.317 MeV
- For a 2-solar-mass hybrid star, the quark core radius is 6.38 km (alpha=0.5) and 6.51 km (alpha=0.7)
- Tidal deformability constraint Λ < 800 for 1.4-solar-mass stars is satisfied for alpha values smaller than 0.9
- Deconfinement window set between chemical potentials 1029 MeV (2n0) and 1272 MeV (4n0)

## Context

This work extends the discussion of hybrid star structure by Glendenning (2012) and others, but contradicts the standard picture where neutron star and hybrid star surfaces consist of inhomogeneous "pasta" phases as described by Chamel (2008) and others. The authors argue that for the large baryon numbers in neutron stars, the bulk effect dominates over surface and Coulomb terms that make iron the most stable state for finite nuclei, enabling self-bound hadronic matter at the surface. This enables hybrid star models that can simultaneously satisfy the mass constraint from PSR J0348+0432 (2.01 solar masses), radius measurements from Capano (2020), and tidal deformability constraints from GW170817.
