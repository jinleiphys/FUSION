# Improved microscopic nuclear level densities within the triaxial Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov plus combinatorial method

> New developments have been brought to our energy-, spin- and parity-dependent nuclear level densities based on the microscopic combinatorial method. Our new calculation is based on the BSkG3 mean-field model which relies on a three-dimensional coordinate-space representation of the nucleus, allowing for the spontaneous breaking of ground state rotational, axial and reflection symmetry. In particular, we now account for the impact of possible triaxial deformation of nuclear ground states on the level density. This has two effects on our calculations: the additional freedom of the single-particle levels affects the intrinsic level density while the absence of a rotational symmetry axis results in a larger collective correction. The present model reproduces the experimental s- and p-wave neutron resonance spacings with a degree of accuracy comparable to that of the best global models available. It is also shown that the model gives a reliable extrapolation at low energies where experimental data on the cumulative number of levels can be extracted. The predictions are also in good agreement with the experimental data extracted from the Oslo method. Total level densities for more than 8500 nuclei are made available in a table format for practical applications. For the nuclei for which experimental s-wave spacings and enough low-lying states exist, renormalization factors are provided to reproduce simultaneously both observables. The same combinatorial method is used to estimate the nuclear level densities at the fission saddle points of actinides and at the shape isomer deformation. Finally, the new nuclear level densities are applied to the calculation of radiative neutron capture cross sections and compared with those obtained with our previous combinatorial model.

## Key claim

The authors present an improved microscopic combinatorial nuclear level density (NLD) model based on the BSkG3 mean-field framework that now accounts for triaxial deformation of nuclear ground states, claiming that the model reproduces experimental s- and p-wave neutron resonance spacings with accuracy comparable to the best global models available, though the treatment of triaxiality introduces ad-hoc approximations for angular momentum coupling and parity assignment that are not rigorously derived from first principles.

## Method

The combinatorial method constructs intrinsic level densities by counting particle-hole excitations from the single-particle spectra obtained from triaxial Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the BSkG3 energy density functional for approximately 8500 nuclei. For triaxially deformed ground states, the authors define a rounded angular momentum projection quantum number \(\bar{K}\) from the expectation value of the angular momentum operator along the axis with smallest Belyaev moment of inertia, then build rotational bands using a rigid asymmetric rotor Hamiltonian with Belyaev moments of inertia rescaled by a factor of 1.32, coupling intrinsic and collective angular momenta coherently as \(J = J_{\rm rot} + |\bar{K}|\). For axially symmetric nuclei, the standard rotational enhancement formula is used, while for octupole-deformed nuclei, parity is assumed equally distributed between positive and negative states.

## Context

This work extends the combinatorial NLD approach previously developed by Goriely et al. (2008) and Hilaire et al. (2012), which was based on axially symmetric HFB calculations, by incorporating the BSkG3 model of Grams et al. (2023) that allows spontaneous breaking of rotational, axial, and reflection symmetries. The new model enables global NLD predictions for triaxially deformed nuclei, which are energetically favorable for many nuclei according to BSkG3, and provides NLDs at fission saddle points and shape isomer deformations of actinides, enabling improved calculations of radiative neutron capture cross sections and fission properties.
