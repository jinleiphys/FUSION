# Love-C relations for elastic hybrid stars

> Neutron stars (NSs) provide a unique laboratory to study matter under extreme densities. Recent observations from gravitational and electromagnetic waves have enabled constraints on NS properties, such as tidal deformability (related to the tidal Love number) and stellar compactness. Although each of these two NS observables depends strongly on the stellar internal structure, the relation between them (called the Love-C relation) is known to be equation-of-state insensitive. In this study, we investigate the effects of a possible crystalline phase in the core of hybrid stars (HSs) on the mass-radius and Love-C relations, where HSs are a subclass of NS models with a quark matter core and a nuclear matter envelope with a sharp phase transition in between. We find that both the maximum mass and the corresponding radius increase as one increases the stiffness of the quark matter core controlled by the speed of sound, while the density discontinuity at the nuclear-quark matter transition effectively softens the equations of state. Deviations of the Love-C relation for elastic HSs from that of fluid NSs become more pronounced with a larger shear modulus, lower transition pressure, and larger density gap and can be as large as 60%. These findings suggest a potential method for testing the existence of distinct phases within HSs, though deviations are not large enough to be detected with current measurements of the tidal deformability and compactness.

## Key claim

Deviations of the Love-C relation for elastic hybrid stars from that of fluid neutron stars can be as large as 60%, but these deviations are not large enough to be detected with current measurements of tidal deformability and compactness from LIGO/Virgo and NICER/XMM-Newton.

## Method

The authors constructed static spherically symmetric hybrid star models by solving the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations with a parametrized constant speed of sound (CSS) model for the quark matter core and the APR equation of state for the nuclear matter envelope, assuming a sharp phase transition with a density discontinuity. They then computed the tidal deformability by solving the perturbed Einstein equations including elasticity effects in the solid quark matter core via a power-law shear modulus model, using the slow conversion condition at the nuclear-quark matter interface.

## Key numbers

- Deviations of the Love-C relation for elastic hybrid stars from the fluid neutron star fit can be as large as 60% (35% for fluid hybrid stars) for the low transition pressure case with $P_t = 2\times10^{33}~\text{dyn cm}^{-2}$
- For the high transition pressure case with $P_t = 2\times10^{34}~\text{dyn cm}^{-2}$, deviations reach approximately 50% for elastic and 45% for fluid hybrid stars
- The shear modulus model uses $\mu_\mathrm{ref} = 3.13\times10^{34}~\text{dyn cm}^{-2} = 19.54~\text{MeV fm}^{-3}$ at reference density $\rho_\text{ref} = 2\times10^{15}~\text{g cm}^{-3}$ with index $N = 0.5$
- Current measurement uncertainties for a 1.4 $M_\odot$ star give compactness $C = 0.159^{+0.025}_{-0.022}$ from NICER and dimensionless tidal deformability $\bar\lambda = 190^{+390}_{-120}$ from GW170817

## Context

This work extends previous studies on universal Love-C relations for neutron stars and hybrid stars by Yagi (2017) and Carson (2019), which found fractional deviations within 10% for various equations of state. It builds on the finding by Lau et al. (2017) that solid quark stars can have tidal deformability 60% lower than fluid quark stars, and on Pereira et al. (2020) who showed tidal deformability changes of about 5% for hybrid stars with solid layers. The results suggest that while elasticity in hybrid star cores produces measurable deviations in principle, distinguishing elastic hybrid stars from fluid neutron stars requires future improvements in observational precision beyond current LIGO/Virgo and NICER capabilities.
