# Molecular-orbital structure in neutron-rich C isotopes

> The molecule-like structure of the C isotopes (A=12, 14, 16) is investigated using a microscopic $α+α+α+n+n+\cdot \cdot \cdot$ model. The valence neutrons are classified based on the molecular-orbit (MO) model, and both $π$-orbit and $σ$-orbit are introduced around three $α$-clusters. The valence neutrons which occupy the $π$-orbit increase the binding energy and stabilize the linear-chain of 3$α$ against the breathing-like break-up. However, $^{14}$C with the $π$-orbit does not show clear energy minimum against the bending-like path. The combination of the valence neutrons in the $π$- and the $σ$-orbit is promising to stabilize the linear-chain state against the breathing- and bending- modes, and it is found that the excited states of $^{16}$C with the $(3/2^-_π)^2(1/2^-_σ)^2$ configuration for the four valence neutrons is one of the most promising candidates for such structure.

# Molecular-orbital structure in neutron-rich C isotopes

## Key claim

The linear-chain configuration of three alpha clusters in neutron-rich carbon isotopes can be stabilized against both breathing-like and bending-like breakup modes, but only when four valence neutrons occupy a specific combination of molecular orbitals: the $(3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\sigma)^2$ configuration in $^{16}$C. Configurations with only $\pi$-orbit neutrons stabilize against breathing but not bending, while $\sigma$-orbit neutrons alone do not stabilize against either mode.

## Method

The authors used a microscopic $\alpha+\alpha+\alpha+n+n+\cdots$ model with full antisymmetrization, where each alpha cluster is described by Gaussian wave functions centered on the $z$-axis at positions $-d$, $0$, and $+d$. Valence neutrons are placed in molecular-orbital (MO) states classified as $\pi$-orbit (perpendicular to the $z$-axis, with one node) or $\sigma$-orbit (along the $z$-axis, with three nodes), and the spatial parameters of these orbits are optimized variationally using the Cooling Method from antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD). The total wave function is a superposition of basis states with different $\alpha$-$\alpha$ distances $d$ and bending angles $\theta$, projected onto eigenstates of angular momentum. The Hamiltonian uses the Volkov No.2 central interaction and the G3RS spin-orbit term, with parameters fitted to $\alpha+n$ and $\alpha+\alpha$ scattering phase shifts and the deuteron binding energy.

## Key numbers

- The $^{14}$C$(3/2^-_\pi)^2$ configuration has a minimal $0^+$ energy of approximately $-82$ MeV at $d = 3$ fm, which is 11 MeV lower than the $^{12}$C 3$\alpha$ system and corresponds to an excitation energy of 18 MeV from the ground state calculated with an equilateral-triangle configuration ($-101.2$ MeV).
- The $^{16}$C$((3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\pi)^2)$ configuration is most stable among studied states with an energy pocket of approximately $-86$ MeV at a shorter $\alpha$-$\alpha$ distance of $d = 2.5$ fm.
- The $^{16}$C$((3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\sigma)^2)$ configuration has a minimal $0^+$ energy of approximately $-77$ MeV, which is 5 MeV higher than the $(3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\pi)^2$ configuration.
- The $0^+$ energy of $^{16}$C$((3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\sigma)^2)$ increases by 15.7 MeV from $\theta = 0^\circ$ to $\theta = 30^\circ$, of which 10.3 MeV is kinetic energy.
- The squared overlap between $\theta = 0^\circ$ and $\theta = 30^\circ$ for $^{16}$C$((3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\sigma)^2)$ is 0.602, compared to 0.906 for $^{12}$C, 0.905 for $^{14}$C$((3/2^-_\pi)^2)$, 0.804 for $^{14}$C$((1/2^-_\sigma)^2)$, and 0.865 for $^{16}$C$((3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\pi)^2)$.
- The $^{16}$C$((3/2^-_\pi)^2(1/2^-_\sigma)^2)$ configuration forms a rotational band with $\hbar^2/2I = 150$ keV, and the band head is expected at approximately 25 MeV excitation energy given the experimental two-neutron separation energy of $^{16}$C (5.5 MeV).

## Context

This work extends the molecular-orbital approach previously applied to Be isotopes (Itagaki and Okabe, 2000; Itagaki, Okabe, and Ikeda, 2000) to neutron-rich C isotopes, building on the Ikeda diagram (Ikeda, Takigawa, and Horiuchi, 1968) which predicted multi-alpha cluster states near threshold energies. The study addresses the long-standing question of whether linear-chain configurations of alpha clusters exist, following earlier work on $^{12}$C where the second $0^+$ state was suggested to have a 3$\alpha$ molecular configuration (Morinaga, 1956) but was later described as a triangular or $^8$Be+$\alpha$ configuration (Fujiwara et al., 1980). The results enable experimental searches for rotational bands with large moments of inertia in $^{16}$C via gamma-ray spectroscopy of high-spin states, and suggest extending the analysis to $^{18}$C where all three neutron orbits would be fully occupied.
