# Effective-range corrections to the ground-state energy of the weakly-interacting Bose gas in two dimensions

> Nonuniversal effects due to leading effective-range corrections are computed for the ground-state energy of the weakly-coupled repulsive Bose gas in two spatial dimensions. Using an effective field theory of contact interactions, these corrections are computed first by considering fluctuations around the mean-field free energy of a system of interacting bosons. This result is then confirmed by an exact calculation in which the energy of a finite number of bosons interacting in a square with period boundary conditions is computed and the thermodynamic limit is explicitly taken.

## Key claim

Nonuniversal effective-range corrections to the ground-state energy of the weakly-interacting repulsive Bose gas in two dimensions are computed and found to be suppressed by one power of the weak coupling and one power of the density relative to known universal effects, in disagreement with a prior claim by PhysRevLett.118.130402 which found an enhancement by a power of the coupling.

## Method

The author uses an effective field theory of contact interactions with Galilean invariance, parity, and time-reversal invariance to describe bosons interacting via finite-range potentials in two spatial dimensions. The leading effective-range corrections are computed first by expanding fluctuations around the mean-field free energy, then confirmed by an exact calculation of the energy of a finite number of bosons in a square with periodic boundary conditions, taking the thermodynamic limit explicitly using tractable two-dimensional lattice sums expressed in terms of special functions.

## Key numbers

- The effective-range contribution to the energy density is: E^(sigma_2) = -24 alpha_2(nu)^4 rho^3 sigma_2 / (pi M) [7/6 + ln(4 alpha_2(nu) rho / nu^2)], where alpha_2 is the scale-dependent dimensionless coupling, rho is the density, M is the boson mass, sigma_2 is the effective-range parameter, and nu is the renormalization scale.
- The free energy including effective-range corrections, expanded to leading order in the effective range, is: F = -mu^2 M/(8 alpha_2(nu)) + mu^2 M/(8 pi)[1/2 + ln(mu M / nu^2)] - 3 mu^3 M^2 alpha_2(nu) sigma_2/(8 pi)[7/6 + ln(mu M / nu^2)].
- The universal O(epsilon) contribution from Ref. PhysRevLett.102.180404 is included with I = 1.005 (numerical value), where epsilon is a coupling defined via epsilon(mu)^{-1} = pi alpha_2(sqrt(mu M))^{-1} - 1.
- The effective range is identified as sigma_2 = r_e^2/(2 pi), where r_e is the effective range.

## Context

This work extends the universal ground-state energy calculations for the two-dimensional Bose gas known to three nontrivial orders in the weak coupling expansion (PhysRevA.3.1067, Popov 1972, PhysRevB.37.4936, Lieb 2001, PhysRevE.64.027105, PhysRevLett.102.180404, Andersen 2002) by adding nonuniversal effective-range corrections. The result contradicts PhysRevLett.118.130402, which found an effective-range contribution enhanced by a power of the coupling, attributed to errors in regulating divergent integrals. The calculation enables future Monte Carlo simulations to detect these nonuniversal corrections, though the author notes this may be challenging due to the suppression by both weak coupling and density.
