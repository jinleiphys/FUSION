# Pauli Blocking for a Relativistic Fermi Gas in Quasielastic Lepton Nucleus Scattering

> The expressions for the overall effect of Pauli blocking in quasielastic (QE) lepton scattering from nuclear targets within the framework of the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) given in several publications are incorrect. For example, the expressions published by Bell and Llewelyn Smith in 1972 are incorrect (probably a typographical error). The expressions for Pauli blocking presented in several subsequent publications including the paper of C.H. Llewelyn Smith in 1972 and the paper of Paschos and Yu in 2002 have the same error. Another example is a 1992 paper by Singh and Oset which has a different (probably also a typographical) error. Other papers such as the papers of Tsai in 1974 and Bosted and Mamyan in 2012 use the correct expressions. In this short preprint we review the geometrical derivation of the overall reduction of the QE cross sections due to Pauli blocking for electron and neutrino scattering for targets with equal numbers of neutrons and protons and for targets with unequal numbers of neutrons and protons and derive the correct expressions. However, we note that the effects of Pauli blocking within the framework of the RFG model are much larger than in other more realistic models of QE scattering such as $ψ^\prime$ superscaling or a spectral function approach.

## Key claim

The paper identifies and corrects typographical errors in several published expressions for the overall Pauli blocking factor in quasielastic lepton-nucleus scattering within the Relativistic Fermi Gas (RFG) model, but notes that the RFG model itself predicts much larger Pauli blocking effects than more realistic models such as psi-prime superscaling or spectral function approaches.

## Method

The author reviews the geometrical derivation of the Pauli suppression factor for a Relativistic Fermi Gas, computing the overlap volume between the initial-state Fermi sphere of spectator nucleons and the displaced final-state sphere of struck nucleons after a three-momentum transfer q. For targets with equal numbers of neutrons and protons, the suppression factor is derived from the volume of overlap of two spheres of equal radius K_F separated by distance |q|. For targets with unequal neutron and proton numbers, the derivation uses two spheres of different radii K_F^N and K_F^P, with separate expressions for neutrino scattering on neutrons and antineutrino scattering on protons.

## Key numbers

- For carbon-12, the RFG Pauli blocking factor at Q2 = 0.01 GeV2 (|q| = 0.100 GeV) is 0.334 for K_F = 0.221 GeV and 0.324 for K_F = 0.228 GeV, compared to 0.375 from GENIE v2
- For carbon-12, the RFG Pauli blocking factor reaches 1.000 at Q2 = 0.18 GeV2 (|q| = 0.435 GeV) for K_F = 0.221 GeV
- For argon-40, the RFG Pauli blocking factor for neutrino scattering on neutrons at Q2 = 0.00 GeV2 is 0.131, and for antineutrino scattering on protons at the same Q2 is 0.000
- For lead-208, the RFG Pauli blocking factor for neutrino scattering on neutrons at Q2 = 0.00 GeV2 is 0.309, and for antineutrino scattering on protons at the same Q2 is 0.000
- The correct RFG Pauli blocking factor for equal N and Z is P = (3/4)(|q|/K_F) - (1/16)(|q|/K_F)^3 for |q| less than 2K_F, and P = 1 for |q| greater than 2K_F

## Context

This work corrects typographical errors in the Pauli blocking expressions from Bell and Llewellyn Smith (1972), Llewelyn Smith (1972), Paschos and Yu (2002), and Singh and Oset (1992), while confirming the correct expressions from Gatto (1953), Berman (1961), Tsai (1974), and Bosted and Mamyan (2012). The corrected RFG expressions enable proper implementation of Pauli blocking in event generators like GENIE, though the author emphasizes that the RFG model overestimates Pauli suppression compared to more realistic approaches such as psi-prime superscaling or spectral function models, motivating further experimental investigation of low-q QE electron scattering suppression within the superscaling framework.
