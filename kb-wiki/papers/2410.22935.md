# Spin Polarized Quasi-particle in Off-equilibrium Medium

> It is usually believed that physics in off-equilibrium state characterized by hydrodynamic gradient can be equivalently studied using equilibrium state with suitable metric perturbation. We scrutinize this assumption using chiral kinetic theory in curved space, focusing on spin response to hydrodynamic gradient. Two effects of metric perturbation have been identified: one is to change particle motion by scattering it off the metric fields, which does capture spin response to hydrodynamic gradient, but is limited by kinematic condition in the scattering picture. The other is the genuine effect of off-equilibrium state, which is realizable by mapping the equilibrium state in curved space to flat space through a suitable frame choice. It lifts the kinematic condition in the spin response to hydrodynamic gradient. We classify off-equilibrium effect on spin polarization into modifications of (i) spectral function; (ii) distribution function; (iii) KMS relation. While the last two have been studied using chiral kinetic theory, the first one is usually ignored in kinetic description. We perform a detailed analysis on the first one, finding the radiative correction to spectral function leads to a polarized quasi-particle. The degeneracy of spin responses to different hydrodynamic sources at tree-level is also lifted by radiative correction.

## Key claim

The paper scrutinizes the assumption that off-equilibrium states characterized by hydrodynamic gradients can be equivalently studied using equilibrium states with metric perturbations, finding that while metric perturbation captures some spin response, it is limited by kinematic conditions in the scattering picture; a genuine off-equilibrium effect exists that lifts this kinematic condition and can be accessed through a suitable frame choice mapping curved-space equilibrium to flat-space off-equilibrium.

## Method

The authors use chiral kinetic theory (CKT) in curved space to analyze spin polarization in off-equilibrium media, expanding the metric to linear order in perturbation \(g_{\mu\nu} \simeq \eta_{\mu\nu} + h_{\mu\nu}\). They compute the Wigner function for massless fermions, separating contributions from the gauge link (which modifies the definition of correlation functions) and from particle scattering off metric fields via spin connection. They then classify off-equilibrium effects into three types: modifications of (i) spectral function, (ii) distribution function, and (iii) KMS relation, and perform a detailed analysis of radiative corrections to the spectral function using one-loop quark self-energy diagrams with off-equilibrium propagators.

## Key numbers

* The collision effect from two-loop self-energies is formally suppressed by \(g^4\) but enhanced by a factor of \(1/g^4\) from large deviation of steady state from equilibrium, dominating over the spectral function modification which occurs at \(O(g^2)\).
* The radiative correction to the spectral function arises from one-loop quark self-energy diagrams, with off-equilibrium corrections entering through quark and gluon in the loop.

## Context

This work extends the chiral kinetic theory framework for spin polarization in heavy ion collisions, building on prior studies of spin polarization from vorticity (Liang 2004, STAR 2017) and hydrodynamic gradients (Hidaka 2017, Liu 2021). It contrasts with earlier approaches that mimic off-equilibrium states via metric perturbation (Liu 2021) by identifying limitations of that equivalence and introducing a classification of off-equilibrium effects. The radiative correction to the spectral function, usually ignored in kinetic descriptions, lifts the degeneracy of spin responses to vorticity, shear, and temperature gradient at tree-level, analogous to effects seen for external electromagnetic fields (Lin 2023). This enables more complete theoretical treatment of spin polarization data from heavy ion experiments.
