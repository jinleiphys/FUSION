# LSND, SN1987A, and CPT Violation

> We point out that neutrino events observed at Kamiokande and IMB from SN1987A disfavor the neutrino oscillation parameters preferred by the LSND experiment. For $Δm^2 > 0$ (the light side), the electron neutrinos from the neutronization burst would be lost, while the first event at Kamiokande is quite likely to be due to an electron neutrino. For $Δm^2 < 0$ (the dark side), the average energy of the dominantly $\barν_e$ events is already lower than the theoretical expectations, which would get aggravated by a complete conversion from $\barν_μ$ to $\barν_e$. If taken seriously, the LSND data are disfavored independent of the existence of a sterile neutrino. A possible remedy is CPT violation, which allows different mass spectra for neutrinos and anti-neutrinos and hence can accommodate atmospheric, solar and LSND data without a sterile neutrino. If this is the case, Mini-BooNE must run in $\barν$ rather than the planned $ν$ mode to test the LSND signal. We speculate on a possible origin of CPT violation.

# LSND, SN1987A, and CPT Violation

## Key claim

The neutrino oscillation parameters preferred by the LSND experiment are disfavored by the SN1987A neutrino data from Kamiokande and IMB, independent of whether a sterile neutrino exists, but the authors note that difficulties in supernova theory and low statistics prevent a definite conclusion. A possible remedy is CPT violation, which would allow different mass spectra for neutrinos and antineutrinos and could accommodate atmospheric, solar, and LSND data without a sterile neutrino.

## Method

The authors reexamine three constraints from SN1987A neutrino data on the two-flavor oscillation parameter space: the energy spectrum of dominantly antielectron-neutrino events, the observation of the first Kamiokande event attributed to electron neutrinos from the neutronization burst, and the nuclear r-process constraint from supernova neutrino wind. They apply the MSW effect with a density profile of the supernova envelope and approximate Earth matter effects for neutrinos passing through the Earth to the northern hemisphere detectors, then compare the resulting excluded regions to the LSND-preferred parameter space at 90% and 99% confidence level.

## Key numbers

- Average energies expected from proto-neutron star core: electron neutrinos 10–12 MeV, antielectron neutrinos 14–17 MeV, muon and tau neutrinos and antineutrinos 24–27 MeV
- Observed average energy of antielectron-neutrino-induced events at Kamiokande: 7–14 MeV
- LSND-preferred mass-squared difference range: Δm² = 0.1–1 eV²
- Oscillation probability constraint from antielectron-neutrino spectrum: P_osc < 0.35, based on Smirnov, Spergel and Bahcall (1994)
- Oscillation probability constraint from neutronization burst: P_osc < 0.90, so that observation of one event is possible within 99% CL
- Expected event rate of electron neutrinos from neutronization burst at Kamiokande: about 0.025
- Probability that the first Kamiokande event is due to an isotropically distributed antielectron-neutrino event: about 3%

## Context

This work extends constraints from SN1987A neutrino data previously studied by Smirnov, Spergel and Bahcall (1994) and by Arafune and Minakata (1992), and applies them specifically to the LSND-preferred parameter region. The authors note that the dark side (tan²θ > 1) of the parameter space had not been studied previously for this constraint. The paper enables the possibility that CPT violation could reconcile LSND with other neutrino oscillation data, and predicts that Mini-BooNE must run in antineutrino mode rather than the planned neutrino mode to test the LSND signal. The authors also discuss 3+1 and 2+2 sterile neutrino models, noting that recent reanalysis of LSND data by Kayser (1999) opened a small acceptable region in the 3+1 parameter space.
