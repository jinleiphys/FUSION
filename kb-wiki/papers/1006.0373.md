# A few brief comments to arXiv:1005.3315 and arXiv:1005.3321

> It is discussed the inconsistency of the so-called ladder-rainbow truncation scheme to QCD in the framework of the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the bound state of mesons.

## Key claim

The ladder-rainbow truncation scheme used in the Dyson-Schwinger equation for the quark propagator and the Bethe-Salpeter equation for meson bound states is inconsistent with the color gauge invariance of QCD, because it cannot correctly account for color charge interactions that are always present in QCD (unlike QED, where the approximation works).

## Method

The author analyzes the consistency of the ladder-rainbow truncation scheme by referring to previous work (Gogokhia, 2010) that demonstrated the inconsistency in a general form, and by comparing the approximation’s validity between QCD and QED. The author also rebuts a counter-claim (from another group, 2010) by pointing out a mathematical mistake involving an unjustified change of variables in divergent regularized integrals.

## Key numbers

- No original numerical results are presented in this comment paper.

## Context

This comment extends the author’s prior work (Gogokhia, 2010) showing that the ladder-rainbow truncation is inconsistent with QCD color gauge invariance, and contradicts the claims of the papers arXiv:1005.3315 and arXiv:1005.3321 that used this approximation for meson dynamics. It also responds to a rebuttal (another group, 2010) that attempted to argue the inconsistency proof was incorrect. The comment suggests that all results based on this truncation scheme should be abandoned or re-considered, and that the approximation is definitely wrong for light quarks and trivial for heavy quarks.
