# Coriolis terms in Skyrmion Quantization

> We consider the problem of quantizing a Skyrmion which is allowed to vibrate, rotate and isorotate. Previous approaches have neglected the interactions between vibrations and zero modes (analogous to so-called Coriolis terms in the molecular physics literature). A new formalism incorporating these interactions is introduced, inspired by a principal bundle approach to deformable-body dynamics. We quantize the B=4 and B=7 Skyrmions and compare the results to observed nuclear properties of Helium-4 and the Lithium-7/Beryllium-7 isospin doublet.

## Key claim

The paper introduces a new formalism incorporating Coriolis-type interactions between vibrations and zero modes (rotations and isorotations) in Skyrmion quantization, which previous approaches had neglected. The author claims that these interactions, analogous to Coriolis terms in molecular physics, can explain certain nuclear properties such as the surprisingly low energy of the Lithium-7/Beryllium-7 spin 3/2 ground state, but the quantitative results depend on treating certain parameters (like the Coriolis coupling constant eta) as free parameters rather than computing them from first principles.

## Method

The author develops a principal bundle formalism for the Skyrmion configuration space, treating rotations and isorotations as generating the fibres of a principal SU(2) x SU(2)-bundle over a shape space. The quantum Hamiltonian is derived from the Laplace-Beltrami operator on this bundle, yielding a Schrodinger equation on shape space that couples vibrational motion to rotational and isorotational degrees of freedom through a gauge field A_i. The formalism is applied to the B=4 Skyrmion (with O_h symmetry) and the B=7 Skyrmion, where vibrational modes are classified by irreducible representations of the symmetry group and the Coriolis coupling constants are determined up to a single multiplicative scalar using Jahn's rule from molecular physics.

## Key numbers

- The lowest four vibrational frequencies of the B=4 Skyrmion (for dimensionless pion mass m=1) are: 0.46 (E+ irrep), 0.48 (F2+ irrep), 0.52 (A2- irrep), and 0.62 (F2- irrep), as calculated by Halcrow
- For the equilateral triangle model, the Coriolis coupling constant eta = 1/3, determined from explicit calculation
- The spin 4 excitation from rigid-body quantization of the B=4 Skyrmion is at roughly 40 MeV
- Observed excited states of the alpha-particle in the 20-30 MeV range have spins 0, 1, and 2, which are not captured by rigid-body quantization

## Context

This work extends the rigid-body quantization approach to Skyrmions, which was previously applied to the B=4 Skyrmion by Manko (2015) and others, by incorporating vibrational degrees of freedom that were neglected in those earlier treatments. The formalism is inspired by the principal bundle approach to deformable-body dynamics developed by Littlejohn and Reinsch (1997) in the molecular physics literature, and the symmetry analysis uses Jahn's rule from molecular spectroscopy. The paper enables future calculations of nuclear spectra from the Skyrme model that include rotation-vibration coupling, and suggests that the Carbon-12 work by Rawlinson (2017) and Oxygen-16 work by King (2018) could be extended to include Coriolis effects.
