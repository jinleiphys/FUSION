# 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics at high baryon densities

> We apply a 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic + cascade model to the heavy ion collision reactions with $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=6.3\dots39$ GeV. To accommodate the model for a given collision energy range, the initial conditions for hydrodynamic phase are taken from UrQMD, and the equation of state at finite baryon density is based on Chiral model coupled to the Polyakov loop. We study the collision energy dependence of pion and kaon rapidity distributions and $m_T$-spectra, as well as charged hadron elliptic flow and how shear viscosity affects them. The model calculations are compared to the data for Pb-Pb collisions at CERN SPS, as well as for Au-Au collisions in the Beam Energy Scan (BES) program energies at BNL RHIC. The data favours the value of shear viscosity $η/s\gtrsim0.2$ for this collision energy range.

# 3+1 dimensional viscous hydrodynamics at high baryon densities

## Key claim
The data from SPS and RHIC Beam Energy Scan favor a shear viscosity to entropy density ratio of eta/s greater than or approximately equal to 0.2 for heavy ion collisions at center-of-mass energies between 6.3 and 39 GeV, which is substantially larger than the typical value of 0.08 extracted at top RHIC energy (200 GeV) by Heinz.

## Method
The authors constructed a 3+1D hybrid model coupling a viscous hydrodynamic phase (using Israel-Stewart formalism) to a hadron cascade (UrQMD). Initial conditions for the hydrodynamic phase were taken from UrQMD simulations, and the equation of state at finite baryon density was based on a Chiral model coupled to the Polyakov loop. The model was applied to Pb-Pb collisions at SPS energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 6.3 to 17.3 GeV) and Au-Au collisions at RHIC Beam Energy Scan energies (sqrt(s_NN) = 7.7 to 39 GeV), with shear viscosity as the only free parameter.

## Key numbers
- Starting time for hydro phase at sqrt(s_NN) = 6.3 GeV is 4.1 fm/c, comparable to the pre-hydro stage lifetime
- Transition from fluid to particle description (particlization) fixed at constant energy density epsilon_sw = 0.5 GeV/fm^3
- Shear viscosity values tested: eta/s = 0, 0.1, and 0.2
- Collision energies studied: sqrt(s_NN) = 6.3, 8.8, 12.3, 17.3 GeV (SPS) and 7.7, 27, 39 GeV (RHIC BES)
- The data favor eta/s greater than or approximately equal to 0.2, compared to eta/s = 0.08 at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV from Heinz

## Context
This work extends viscous hydrodynamic modeling, previously successful at top RHIC and LHC energies (Huovinen review), down to lower collision energies where boost-invariant scaling flow is no longer valid. The model uses rapidity-dependent initial conditions from UrQMD and a finite-baryon-density equation of state (Chiral model) to address the Beam Energy Scan regime. The results enable systematic extraction of shear viscosity across a broad energy range and suggest that the effective eta/s increases at lower collision energies, motivating further studies of transport coefficients in high-baryon-density matter.
