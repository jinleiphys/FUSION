# Connecting Euclidean to light-cone correlations: From flavor nonsinglet in forward kinematics to flavor singlet in non-forward kinematics

> We present a unified framework for the perturbative factorization connecting Euclidean correlations to light-cone correlations. Starting from nonlocal quark and gluon bilinear correlators, we derive the relevant hard-matching kernel up to the next-to-leading-order, both for the flavor singlet and non-singlet combinations, in non-forward and forward kinematics, and in coordinate and momentum space. The results for the generalized distribution functions (GPDs), parton distribution functions (PDFs), and distribution amplitudes (DAs) are obtained by choosing appropriate kinematics. The renormalization and matching are done in a state-of-the-art scheme. We also clarify some issues raised on the perturbative matching of GPDs in the literature. Our results provide a complete manual for extracting all leading-twist GPDs, PDFs as well as DAs from lattice simulations of Euclidean correlations in a state-of-the-art strategy, either in coordinate or in momentum space factorization approach.

## Key claim

The paper provides a unified perturbative framework for connecting Euclidean (quasi-light-front) correlations to light-cone correlations for both flavor-singlet and flavor-nonsinglet combinations, valid in non-forward and forward kinematics, and in both coordinate and momentum space; the matching kernels are derived up to next-to-leading order (NLO) in a state-of-the-art renormalization scheme, and the authors clarify discrepancies in the literature regarding the perturbative matching of generalized parton distributions (GPDs).

## Method

The authors start from nonlocal quark and gluon bilinear operators defined on a spatial interval and derive the hard-matching kernel by replacing hadron states with on-shell quark or gluon parton states, expanding the factorization formula to NLO. Dimensional regularization (d=4-2ε) is used to regularize both ultraviolet and infrared divergences, and the matching kernels are computed in the MS-bar, ratio, and hybrid renormalization schemes. The results for GPDs, parton distribution functions (PDFs), and distribution amplitudes (DAs) are obtained by choosing appropriate kinematic limits from the general non-forward matching kernels.

## Key numbers

* The matching kernel for the flavor-nonsinglet quark operator (C_qq) at NLO in the MS-bar scheme contains terms proportional to 2a_s C_F, where a_s = α_s/(4π), with plus-prescription terms [\bar{α}/α]_+ δ(β) and [\bar{β}/β]_+ δ(α) multiplied by (L_z - 1), where L_z = ln(4 e^{-2γ_E} / (-μ² z₁₂²)).
* The matching kernel for the quark-to-gluon mixing (C_qg) at NLO is given by C_qg^{MS-bar}(α,β,μ² z₁₂²) = 4 i a_s T_F N_f z₁₂ B_3 L_z, where B_3,u = \bar{α}\bar{β} + 3αβ for unpolarized and B_3,h = \bar{α}\bar{β} - αβ for helicity.
* The ladder diagram contribution to C_qq yields a factor A_1 = 1 for unpolarized, A_1 = 1-2ε for helicity, and A_1 = 0 for transversity, with the integral over α and β from 0 to 1 and 0 to \bar{α} respectively.
* The vertex diagram contribution to C_qq includes a term -2a_s C_F Γ(-ε) η^ε ∫₀¹ dα/α [(1-ε)/(1-2ε)(α - α^{2ε}) (O_q(z₁₂^α,z₂)+O_q(z₁,z₂₁^α)) + 2α^{2ε} O_q(z₁,z₂)].

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative matching calculations for flavor-nonsinglet PDFs (e.g., Chen (2020), Li (2020) at NNLO) and earlier studies of flavor-singlet quark and gluon PDFs (Wang (2019), Balitsky (2019)) to a unified framework covering GPDs, PDFs, and DAs in both coordinate and momentum space. It resolves discrepancies found in the literature between different groups' calculations of the one-loop matching for quark GPDs (Ji (2015), Xiong (2015), Ma (2022)) and provides a complete manual for lattice QCD extractions of all leading-twist parton distributions using state-of-the-art renormalization schemes, enabling higher-order perturbative calculations for improved accuracy.
