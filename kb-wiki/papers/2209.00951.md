# Phase Shift Analysis of Light Nucleon-Nucleus Elastic Scattering using Reference Potential Approach

> The neutron and proton scattering with either deuteron or stable alpha particle can be modeled as a two particle system. In this paper, using Morse function as reference potential, inverse potentials have been computationally constructed directly from scattering phase shifts (SPS) data for light nucleon-nucleus systems. The phase equation for various l-channels has been numerically solved using 5th order Runge-Kutta (RK-5) method in each iteration within the optimization procedure to obtain best model parameters of Morse function that minimize mean absolute percentage error (MAPE). The inverse potentials for S-wave of neutron-deuteron and proton-deuteron systems have been obtained with MAPE of 1.95 and 2.05% respectively. Those corresponding to various P and D channels of neutron-alpha(n-alpha) and proton-alpha(p-alpha) systems, have been determined to less than 1.53 and 2.17% respectively. The obtained(experimental) resonance energies for p1/2 and p3/2, from their partial cross-sections plots, respectively are 4.1(4) and 0.93(0.89) for n-alpha and 5.21(5) and 1.96(1.96) for p-alpha system. While total cross-section for n-alpha has been found to be matching with values available in literature, that of p-alpha is seen to be following the correct trend.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Morse function can serve as a reference potential to computationally construct inverse potentials directly from experimental scattering phase shift data for light nucleon-nucleus systems, achieving mean absolute percentage errors below 2.17% across all channels studied, though the method's success is explicitly limited to the specific energy ranges and systems examined (nucleon-deuteron up to 10 MeV and nucleon-alpha up to 18 MeV).

## Method

The phase equation (a first-order Riccati-type differential equation) for each orbital angular momentum channel was numerically solved using a 5th-order Runge-Kutta method, with the Morse potential parameters (strength V0, equilibrium distance rm, and shape parameter am) optimized by minimizing the mean absolute percentage error between simulated and experimental phase shifts. For proton-deuteron and proton-alpha systems, a Coulomb interaction with a Gaussian charge distribution (error function form) was added to the nuclear Morse potential. Partial and total cross sections were then computed from the obtained phase shifts using standard formulas.

## Key numbers

- S-wave neutron-deuteron inverse potential obtained with MAPE of 1.95%
- S-wave proton-deuteron inverse potential obtained with MAPE of 2.05%
- All P and D channels for n-alpha and p-alpha systems determined with MAPE less than 1.53% and 2.17% respectively
- For n-alpha: obtained (experimental) resonance energies of 4.1 (4 +/- 1) MeV for p1/2 and 0.93 (0.89) MeV for p3/2
- For p-alpha: obtained (experimental) resonance energies of 5.21 (5-10) MeV for p1/2 and 1.96 (1.96) MeV for p3/2
- For n-alpha p3/2: obtained decay width 0.88 MeV (experimental 0.60 +/- 0.02 MeV)
- For p-alpha p3/2: obtained decay width 1.90 MeV (experimental approximately 1.5 MeV)
- Morse potential parameters for n-alpha s-state: V0 = 37.66 MeV, rm = 1.28 fm, am = 0.90 fm, MAPE = 0.04%
- Morse potential parameters for n-alpha p3/2 state: V0 = 58.30 MeV, rm = 1.63 fm, am = 0.50 fm, MAPE = 0.14%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous phase function method analysis using Gaussian potentials for alpha-nucleon systems (Kumar et al., 2021) and Morse potentials for nucleon-deuteron systems at energies up to 10 MeV (Kumar et al., 2022), now covering all available energies for nucleon-deuteron systems and extending to nucleon-alpha systems up to 18 MeV. The approach contrasts with the resonating group method and generator coordinate method traditionally used for light-nucleus elastic scattering, and with the optical potential model employed by Satchler et al. (1968) for nucleon-alpha scattering below 18 MeV. The computed total cross sections for n-alpha match literature values, while p-alpha total cross sections follow the correct trend, enabling future application of this reference potential approach to other light nuclear systems.
