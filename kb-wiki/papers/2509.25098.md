# New insights from the flavor dependence of quark transverse momentum distributions in the pion

> We update our previous extraction of transverse momentum distributions of unpolarized quarks in the pion by implementing a more comprehensive description of theoretical uncertainties and, for the first time, by exploring possible differences among quark flavors. We extract such distributions from all available data for unpolarized pion-nucleus Drell-Yan processes, where the cross section is differential in the transverse momentum of the final lepton pair. The cross section involves transverse momentum distributions in the nucleon, that we consistently take from our previous studies.

## Key claim

The authors present the first extraction of unpolarized quark transverse momentum distributions (TMDs) in the pion that explores flavor-dependent differences, but the limited Drell-Yan dataset allows separation only of the valence d-quark TMD from a cumulative sea-quark channel, with the flavor-dependent fit achieving only a modest improvement in chi-squared (1.22 vs. 1.28) over the flavor-independent approach.

## Method

The authors performed a global fit of unpolarized pion-nucleus Drell-Yan data from the E615 and E537 experiments, using the TMD factorization formalism at N3LL- accuracy with the Collins-Soper evolution kernel. They parametrized the nonperturbative component of the pion TMD PDF with a flavor-dependent Gaussian model in Fourier (bT) space, using Monte Carlo replicas to propagate uncertainties from both the experimental data and the input collinear PDFs (xFitter for pion, NNPDF for proton). The proton TMD PDFs were taken from the previous MAPTMD24 extraction and kept fixed during the fit.

## Key numbers

- Total chi-squared per data point for the flavor-dependent fit: chi2_0/N_cut = 1.22 (compared to 1.28 for the flavor-independent fit)
- E615 experiment: chi2_0/N_cut = 1.40 (flavor-dependent) vs. 1.52 (flavor-independent), with 41 data points surviving cuts
- E537 experiment: chi2_0/N_cut = 0.94 (flavor-dependent) vs. 0.92 (flavor-independent), with 60 data points surviving cuts
- Fitted parameters for valence d-quark: N_pi^d = 0.16 +/- 0.10, alpha_pi^d = 1.32 +/- 0.42, sigma_pi^d = 2.11 +/- 0.99
- Fitted parameters for sea quarks: N_pi^sea = 62.5 +/- 62, alpha_pi^sea = 21.7 +/- 20.7, sigma_pi^sea = 121.4 +/- 120.6
- Kinematic cut applied: |qT|/Q < 0.2 + 0.6/Q (GeV)
- Center-of-mass energies: E615 at sqrt(s) = 21.8 GeV, E537 at sqrt(s) = 15.3 GeV
- Q range: 4.0 - 13.05 GeV across both experiments

## Context

This work extends the previous MAPTMDPion22 extraction (Cerutti 2022) by implementing a more comprehensive uncertainty analysis and, for the first time, exploring flavor-dependent TMDs in the pion. It uses the same methodology as the MAPTMD24 proton TMD extraction (Bacchetta 2024) and confirms the finding of Barry (2023) that the pion TMD PDF has a larger transverse momentum distribution than the proton. The results enable comparison with lattice QCD calculations (Bollweg 2025) and anticipate improved constraints from upcoming COMPASS Drell-Yan data, particularly in the x_pi ~ 0.2 region.
