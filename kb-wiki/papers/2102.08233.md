# Variational theory combining number-projected BCS and coupled-cluster doubles

> The ground state pairing correlations in finite fermionic systems are described with a high degree of accuracy within a variational approach based on a combined coupled-cluster and particle-number-projected BCS ansatz. The flexibility of this symmetry-preserving wavefunction enables a unified picture valid from weak to strong coupling, both in small and large systems. The present variational approach consistently yields an energy upper bound while operating at the same level of precision of the non-variational particle-number projected Bogoliubov-coupled-cluster theory [Phys. Rev. C 99, 044301 (2019)].

## Key claim

The paper presents a variational wavefunction that combines number-projected BCS (PBCS) and coupled-cluster doubles (CCD) to describe ground state pairing correlations, achieving accuracy comparable to the non-variational particle-number projected Bogoliubov-coupled-cluster theory of Qiu et al. (2019) while consistently providing an energy upper bound due to its Ritz variational character.

## Method

The authors construct a trial wavefunction by applying CCD excitations on top of a particle-number-projected BCS state, with the structure matrix of the CCD operator approximated either as separable (vCCD_sepPBCS) or as a combination of separable excitations plus non-separable excitations restricted to a small window of w levels around the Fermi level (vCCD^(w)_sepPBCS). Expectation values are evaluated using a representation of the PBCS and vCCD_sep terms as disentangled gauge-angle-rotated BCS states, with the non-separable CCD contributions handled via a Hubbard-Stratonovich transformation and Wick contractions. The energy is minimized with respect to all variational parameters using the e04ucf routine of the NAG library.

## Key numbers

- For an L=12 system at half filling, the vCCD_sepPBCS wavefunction yields relative errors in the correlation energy not exceeding 3 x 10^(-4) across all pairing strengths.
- For L=60 at half filling, vCCD_sepPBCS errors at weak coupling are 0.3%; for L=100 at half filling, the corresponding error is 0.5%.
- For L=100 at half filling with G=1.5 G_cr, PBCS errors reach about 20% while vCCD_sepPBCS errors are about 0.5%.
- For L=100 at half filling, pure PBCS errors reach a maximum of about 22% at G/epsilon=0.24 and decrease to 6% at very small G.
- The optimal non-separable structure matrix elements z_ph are typically of order 10^(-2) to 10^(-3) upon full energy minimization.

## Context

This work extends the line of research combining symmetry-restored mean-field theory with coupled-cluster methods, following the particle-number projected Bogoliubov-coupled-cluster theory of Qiu et al. (2019) which was non-variational. The authors benchmark against the exact Richardson solution (Richardson, 1966) for the constant pairing Hamiltonian with equally spaced levels, and compare with earlier approaches including pCCD (Dukelsky et al., 2003; Henderson et al., 2015), BCS-CCD (Henderson et al., 2014), and interpolation schemes between pCCD and PBCS (Degroote et al., 2016; Dukelsky et al., 2016). The method enables accurate variational calculations for systems up to L=100 levels at half filling, with potential applications to finite fermionic systems such as atomic nuclei and ultrasmall superconducting grains.
