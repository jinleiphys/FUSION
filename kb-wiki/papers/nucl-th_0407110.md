# Proton-Neutron Coupling in the Gamow Shell Model: the Lithium Chain

> The shell model in the complex k-plane (the so-called Gamow Shell Model) has recently been formulated and applied to structure of weakly bound, neutron-rich nuclei. The completeness relations of Newton and Berggren, which apply to the neutron case, are strictly valid for finite-range potentials. However, for long-range potentials, such as the Coulomb potential for protons, for which the arguments based on the Mittag-Leffler theory do not hold, the completeness still needs to be demonstrated. This has been done in this paper, both analytically and numerically. The generalized Berggren relations are then used in the first Gamow Shell Model study of nuclei having both valence neutrons and protons, namely the lithium chain. The single-particle basis used is that of the Hartree-Fock-inspired potential generated by a finite-range residual interaction. The effect of isospin mixing in excited unbound states is discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Berggren completeness relation, previously proven only for finite-range potentials and thus applicable to neutrons, can be extended to protons by proving the Newton completeness relation for long-range Coulomb potentials, enabling the first Gamow Shell Model (GSM) study of nuclei containing both valence protons and neutrons.

## Method

The authors first prove analytically that the Newton completeness relation holds for local potentials with Coulomb asymptotic behavior and for nonlocal potentials with localized kernels, then derive the generalized Berggren completeness relation for protons. They perform numerical tests expanding proton states in Woods-Saxon potentials with Coulomb barriers to verify completeness. The GSM is then applied to the lithium chain (⁵Li to ¹¹Li) using a Hartree-Fock-inspired procedure (Gamow-Hartree-Fock) to generate optimal single-particle bases, with a Surface Gaussian Interaction (SGI) as the residual interaction and a valence space of p-shell orbitals plus discretized continua.

## Key numbers

- The 2p₃/₂ proton resonance at E=3.287 MeV with width Γ=931 keV (Woods-Saxon potential depth V₀=65 MeV) was expanded in a basis with V₀=70 MeV, yielding E=3.289 MeV and Γ=934 keV, a discretization error of ~3 keV.
- A bound 2p₃/₂ state at E=-0.0923 MeV (V₀=75 MeV) expanded in a basis with V₀=80 MeV gave E=-2.568 MeV and Γ=1.73 keV after diagonalization.
- A 2p₃/₂ resonance at E=1.905 MeV with Γ=61.89 keV (V₀=70 MeV) expanded in a basis with V₀=75 MeV gave E=1.9065 MeV and Γ=58.9 keV, discretization error ~3 keV.
- Binding energies for ⁷Li: -14.266 MeV (uniform-filling potential) and -13.008 MeV (M-potential); for ⁸Li: -15.521 MeV and -15.094 MeV; for ⁹Li: -20.288 MeV and -20.181 MeV; for ¹⁰Li: -18.082 MeV and -17.749 MeV; for ¹¹Li: -15.649 MeV and -15.634 MeV.
- The discretized continuum used 14 points extending from Re[k]=0 to Re[k]=8 fm⁻¹ for both p₃/₂ and p₁/₂ channels.

## Context

This work extends the Gamow Shell Model formalism, previously applied to neutron-rich helium and oxygen isotopes by Michel et al. (2002, 2003), to include protons by proving the Berggren completeness relation for Coulomb potentials. It enables the first GSM study of nuclei with both valence neutrons and protons, specifically the lithium chain, and introduces the Gamow-Hartree-Fock method for optimizing the single-particle basis. The paper discusses isospin mixing in excited unbound states and notes that T=0 couplings exhibit significant particle-number dependence, opening the door to future studies of proton-neutron correlations in weakly bound and unbound nuclei.
