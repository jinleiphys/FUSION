# Anisotropic pressure of deconfined QCD matter in presence of strong magnetic field within one-loop approximation

> Considering the general structure of the two point functions of quarks and gluons, we compute the free energy and pressure of a strongly magnetized hot and dense QCD matter created in heavy-ion collisions. In presence of strong magnetic field we found that the deconfined QCD matter exhibits a paramagnetic nature. One gets different pressure in a direction parallel and perpendicular to magnetic field due to the magnetization acquired by the system. We obtain both longitudinal and transverse pressure, and magnetization of a hot deconfined QCD matter in presence of magnetic field. We have used hard thermal loop approximation (HTL) for heat bath. We obtained completely analytic expression for pressure and magnetization under certain approximation. Various divergences appearing in free energy are regulated using appropriate counter terms. The obtained anisotropic pressure may be useful for magnetohydrodynamics description of a hot and dense deconfined QCD matter produced in heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors compute the free energy and pressure of deconfined QCD matter in a strong magnetic field using the one-loop hard thermal loop (HTL) approximation, finding that the system exhibits paramagnetic behavior and anisotropic pressure; the analytic expressions are obtained under the condition \(g^2 (q_fB/T^2) < 1\), which requires both strong magnetic field (\(q_fB \gg T^2\)) and weak coupling (\(g \ll 1\)).

## Method

The authors work in the lowest Landau level (LLL) approximation with the scale hierarchy \(q_fB \gg T^2 \gg m_f^2\), using the general structure of quark and gluon two-point functions in a thermomagnetic medium. The quark free energy is computed from the effective fermion propagator via the Dyson-Schwinger equation, while the gluon free energy is obtained from the determinant of the inverse gauge boson propagator with three distinct structure functions \(b, c, d\) arising from the broken rotational symmetry. All calculations are performed within one-loop HTL perturbation theory, with divergences regulated by counterterms.

## Key numbers

- The ideal (free) quark free energy in LLL is \(F_q^{\text{ideal}} = - d_F \sum_f \frac{q_f B T^2}{12}\), where \(d_F = N_c N_f\).
- The ideal gluon free energy is \(F_g^{\text{ideal}} = -d_A \frac{\pi^2 T^4}{45}\), where \(d_A = N_c^2 - 1\).
- The magnetic mass in the strong field approximation is \(M_{\text{sfa}}^2 = \frac{g^2 C_F}{4\pi^2 T^2} \left( \sum_f (q_f B) T^2 \ln 4 - \sum_f (q_f B)^2 \zeta'(-2) \right)\).
- The quark free energy contains divergences of order \(\mathcal{O}[(q_f B)^2 / \epsilon]\) and \(\mathcal{O}[(q_f B)^3 / (T^2 \epsilon)]\).
- The Debye-like screening mass from gluons scales as \(m_D^s \sim g \sqrt{q_f B}\).

## Context

This work extends previous perturbative QCD calculations of the equation of state in a magnetic field, specifically improving upon the approach of Rath (2017) which assumed the only effect of the magnetic field on gluons was a shift in the Debye mass without modifying the general structure of two-point functions. The authors explicitly incorporate the broken rotational symmetry demonstrated by Karmakar (2018), Ayala (2018), Das (2017), and Hattori (2017), and provide analytic expressions for anisotropic pressure that can serve as input for magnetohydrodynamic simulations of the quark-gluon plasma produced in non-central heavy-ion collisions.
