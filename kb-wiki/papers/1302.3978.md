# Nuclear Symmetries of the similarity renormalization group for nuclear forces

> We review the role played by long-distance symmetries within the context of the similarity renormalization group approach. This is based on phase-shift-preserving continuous unitary transformations that evolve Hamiltonians with a cutoff on energy differences. We find that there is a similarity cutoff of 3/fm for which almost perfect fulfillment of Wigner SU(4) symmetry is found at the two body level. This suggests to look for similar symmetry patterns for three- and four-body forces. We also analyze the impact of potentials based on Chiral Perturbation Theory in Nuclear Structure calculations.

## Key claim

The authors find that at a specific similarity renormalization group (SRG) cutoff of approximately 3 fm⁻¹, the evolved two-nucleon potentials in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels become nearly identical, fulfilling Wigner SU(4) symmetry at the two-body level, even though the original phase shifts in those channels are very different.

## Method

The authors apply the SRG method, which uses continuous unitary transformations that preserve phase shifts at all energies while evolving the Hamiltonian with a cutoff on energy differences. They start from high-quality nucleon-nucleon potentials (Argonne AV18, Nijmegen II, and chiral N3LO potentials) and solve the SRG integro-differential equation for coupled partial waves to generate a one-parameter family of phase-equivalent potentials as a function of the SRG cutoff λ. They then compare the diagonal and off-diagonal momentum-space matrix elements of the evolved potentials in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels to test for Wigner symmetry.

## Key numbers

- The SRG cutoff at which Wigner symmetry is approximately realized is λ_Wigner ≈ 3 fm⁻¹.
- For the square well model, the volume integrals are (C₀,¹S₀, C₀,³S₁) = (-1030, -1288) MeV fm³ and (C₂,¹S₀, C₂,³S₁) = (715, 554) MeV fm⁵, using scattering lengths α₀ = -23.74 fm (¹S₀) and α₀ = 5.40 fm (³S₁) with effective ranges r₀ = 2.75 fm (¹S₀) and r₀ = 1.75 fm (³S₁).
- At λ ≈ 1 fm⁻¹, harmonic oscillator estimates give binding energies (B_d^HO, B_³H^HO, B_⁴He^HO) = (-1.5, -7.1, -24.0) MeV.
- At the Wigner scale λ_Wigner = 3 fm⁻¹, the harmonic oscillator estimate gives B_⁴He^HO = -10 MeV, while more accurate calculations from Jurgenson (2009) give (B_³H, B_⁴He) = (-8.1, -26.8) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the long-distance interpretation of Wigner SU(4) symmetry, previously studied by Calle Cordon and Ruiz Arriola (2008, 2009) and by Kaplan and collaborators (1995, 1996) using large-N_c QCD arguments, into the SRG framework. The finding that a specific SRG cutoff reveals Wigner symmetry at the two-body level suggests that three- and four-body forces generated by the SRG evolution may also exhibit SU(4) symmetry, as discussed by Birse (2012). This enables simplified nuclear structure calculations using harmonic oscillator wave functions and raises the question of whether the induced many-body forces are SU(4)-invariant.
