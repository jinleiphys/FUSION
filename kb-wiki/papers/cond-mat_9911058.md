# Superconductivity in Ultrasmall Grains: Introduction to Richardson's Exact Solution

> Studies of pairing correlations in ultrasmall metallic grains have commonly been based on a simple reduced BCS-model describing the scattering of pairs of electrons between discrete energy levels that come in time-reversed pairs. This model has an exact solution, worked out by Richardson in the context of nuclear physics in the 1960s. Here we give a tutorial introduction to his solution, and use it to check the quality of various previous treatments of this model.

## Key claim

The reduced BCS Hamiltonian for ultrasmall metallic grains has an exact solution, worked out by Richardson in the 1960s for nuclear physics, which provides a benchmark to check the quality of various previous approximate treatments; the paper shows that pairing correlations do not vanish abruptly at a critical level spacing but persist smoothly into a fluctuation-dominated regime.

## Method

The authors present a tutorial derivation of Richardson's exact solution for the reduced BCS model, starting from the hard-core boson formulation of pair operators. They then compute the exact ground-state condensation energies and the Matveev-Larkin parity parameter for uniformly spaced levels with a half-filled band, using the Richardson equations, and compare these exact results against those obtained from grand-canonical BCS, projected BCS (PBCS), Lanczos exact diagonalization (Mastellone, Falci, and Fazio), bandwidth-renormalization (Berger and Halperin), and density matrix renormalization group (DMRG) approaches.

## Key numbers

- For even grains, the critical level spacing from grand-canonical BCS is \(d^c_0 \simeq 3.6 \Delta\); for odd grains, \(d^c_1 \simeq 0.89 \Delta\) (these are artifacts of the grand-canonical treatment).
- The bulk condensation energy in the limit \(d \to 0\) is \(E_0^{\rm bulk} = -\Delta^2/(2d)\).
- The dimensionless coupling constant used throughout is \(\lambda = 0.224\).
- The DMRG reproduces the exact results with a relative error of \(< 10^{-4}\) for systems with up to \(n \le 400\) pairs.

## Context

This work extends the earlier grand-canonical BCS studies by von Delft et al. (1996) and canonical studies by Mastellone, Falci, and Fazio (1998) and Berger and Halperin (1998), which had established that pairing correlations persist smoothly across the crossover from the bulk superconducting regime (\(d \ll \Delta\)) to the fluctuation-dominated regime (\(d \gg \Delta\)). The exact solution provides a rigorous benchmark that confirms the DMRG results of Dukelsky and Schuck (1999) are essentially exact for this model, while showing that the PBCS approach is unreliable in the crossover region. The solution enables future studies of correlation functions and finite-temperature properties for which no exact results exist.
