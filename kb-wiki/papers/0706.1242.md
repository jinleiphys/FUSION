# Subtractive renormalization of the NN scattering amplitude at leading order in chiral effective theory

> The leading-order nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential derived from chiral perturbation theory consists of one-pion exchange plus a short-distance contact interaction. We show that in the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels renormalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for this potential can be achieved by performing one subtraction. This subtraction requires as its only input knowledge of the NN scattering lengths. This procedure leads to a set of integral equations for the partial-wave NN t-matrix which give cutoff-independent results for the corresponding NN phase shifts. This reformulation of the NN scattering equation offers practical advantages, because only observable quantities appear in the integral equation. The scattering equation may then be analytically continued to negative energies, where information on bound-state energies and wave functions can be extracted.

## Key claim

The authors show that renormalization of the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for the leading-order chiral perturbation theory nucleon-nucleon potential in the 1S0 and 3S1-3D1 channels can be achieved by performing one subtraction at zero energy, requiring only the NN scattering lengths as input, without needing to fit the contact interactions C_S and C_T.

## Method

The authors start from the leading-order chiral perturbation theory NN potential consisting of one-pion exchange plus a momentum-independent contact interaction. They perform subtractive renormalization on the Lippmann-Schwinger equation at zero energy, first obtaining the half-shell t-matrix t(p,0;0) from the scattering lengths, then using this to obtain the general half-shell t-matrix at arbitrary energies via a second subtraction. The resulting integral equations contain only observable quantities and no reference to the contact interactions C_S and C_T.

## Key numbers

- Singlet scattering length: a_s = -23.7 fm; triplet scattering length: a_t = 5.43 fm (used as input)
- Deuteron binding energy from subtractive renormalization: B_sub = 2.11 MeV (cutoff-independent to better than 0.5% for Lambda > 500 MeV)
- Deuteron binding energy from fitting C_T to a_t: B_fit = 2.13 MeV (for Lambda = 50000 MeV)
- Experimental deuteron binding energy: B_exp = 2.2246 MeV
- Relative difference between subtractive and fitting methods for phase shifts: 0.1-1.3% depending on energy
- Relative difference for mixing parameter epsilon_1: 1-2%
- Subtraction method achieves 4 significant-figure accuracy with 20 mesh points; fitting method requires more than 200 mesh points for the same accuracy
- Cutoffs considered up to Lambda = 50 GeV (factor of 10 larger than previous momentum-space calculations)

## Context

This work extends the subtractive renormalization approach previously developed for pionless effective field theory by Hammer and Mehen (1999) and Afnan and Phillips (2003) to the chiral perturbation theory NN potential. It reproduces the cutoff-independent results of Nogga, Timmermans, and van Kolck (2005) while avoiding the numerical difficulties of fitting contact interactions at large cutoffs. The method differs from an earlier subtractive approach by Timmermans (1999) which assumed the Born approximation at large negative energies; the authors show this assumption fails for the triplet channel due to the tensor potential. The technique enables calculation of NN phase shifts and bound-state properties without fine-tuning of contact interactions.
