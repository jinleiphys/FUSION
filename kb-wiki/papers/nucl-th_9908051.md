# Giant Resonances in Coulomb Excitations of Relativistic Ions

> We propose a soluble model to incorporate the nonlinear effects in the transition probabilities of the multiphonon Giant Dipole Resonances based on the SU(1,1) algebra. Analytical expressions for the multi-phonon transition probabilities are derived. For reasonably small magnitude of nonlinearity $x\simeq 0.1-0.2$ enhancement factor for the Double Giant Resonance excitation probabilities and the cross sections %in relativistic ion collisions reaches values $1.3-2$ compatible with experimental data.

# Giant Resonances in Coulomb Excitations of Relativistic Ions

## Key claim

The paper proposes that nonlinear effects in the transition operators for multiphonon Giant Dipole Resonances, parameterized by a single small nonlinearity parameter x ≈ 0.1–0.2, can produce enhancement factors of 1.3–2 for Double Giant Resonance excitation probabilities and cross sections, consistent with experimental data that disagree with the harmonic phonon picture. The model is presented as a soluble algebraic framework rather than a definitive microscopic theory, and the authors note that detailed numerical analysis and comparison with data are postponed to future publications.

## Method

The authors construct a semiclassical coupled-channels model where the relative motion of the colliding relativistic ions is treated as a classical straight-line trajectory, while the intrinsic excitation of Giant Dipole Resonances is treated quantum mechanically. They introduce nonlinearity into the transition operators E1† by replacing the harmonic boson creation operator d†m with the expression d†m (1 + 2x N̂d)1/2, where x is a single nonlinearity parameter and N̂d is the phonon number operator. This operator structure is shown to realize the SU(1,1) algebra via a Holstein-Primakoff-type representation, allowing the time-ordered evolution operator to be decomposed into ordinary operator exponentials and reducing the dynamics to a single Riccati-type differential equation for a complex amplitude α(t). The excitation probabilities for N-phonon states are then obtained analytically in terms of this amplitude and the nonlinearity parameter.

## Key numbers

- Enhancement factor for Double GDR excitation in 208Pb at high energy (γ ≈ 1.7, bombarding energy ≈ 640 MeV per nucleon): r2(208Pb) ≈ 1.33
- Enhancement factor for Double GDR excitation in 208Pb at low energy (γ ≈ 1.06–1.10, bombarding energy ≈ 60–100 MeV per nucleon): r2(208Pb) ≈ 2
- Nonlinearity parameter extracted for 208Pb: x(208Pb) ≈ 0.16–0.20
- The ratio R2 = (W2/W1) / (Wharm2/Wharm1) = (1 + 2x) |ᾱ(x)|2 / |αharm1|2, where the factor (1 + 2x) gives the kinematic enhancement from nonlinearity
- For x = 0.19, the model reproduces the energy dependence of the enhancement factor from r2 ≈ 2–2.5 at low energies (ε ∼ 100 MeV per nucleon) to r2 ≈ 1.2–1.3 at high energies (ε ∼ 640–700 MeV per nucleon)

## Context

This work addresses a persistent discrepancy between experimental measurements of Double Giant Dipole Resonance excitation cross sections and predictions of the harmonic phonon model, where observed cross sections are enhanced by factors of 1.3–2 relative to harmonic calculations (Emlings, 1990s; experiments on Xe, Pb, Au targets). The model extends prior studies of anharmonic corrections that focused on spectral effects (CCG, 1990s; Bertsch and Feldmeier, 1990s) and provides an exactly solvable alternative to higher-order perturbation theory treatments (BZ, 1993) and microscopic hot-phonon approaches based on the Brink-Axel mechanism (hot-phonon1, hot-phonon2, 1990s). The results enable future work to establish connections between the phenomenological nonlinear parameter and microscopic origins of the nonlinear effects, and to perform detailed numerical comparisons with experimental data across different nuclei and bombarding energies.
