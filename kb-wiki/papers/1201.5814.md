# How Chiral Symmetry Breaking Affects the Spectrum of the Light-Heavy Mesons in the 't Hooft Model

> We find the spectrum and wave functions of the heavy-light mesons in $(1 + 1)$-dimensional QCD in the 't Hooft limit, both in the rest frame, using the Coulomb (axial) gauge, and on the light cone. Our emphasis is on the effects of chiral symmetry breaking on the spectrum. While dynamical equations in both cases look different, the results for the spectrum are identical. The chiral symmetry breaking is clearly seen from the gap and Bethe--Salpeter equations in the laboratory frame. At the same time, while vacuum is trivial on the light cone (no chiral condensate), the effects of the spontaneous breaking of the chiral symmetry manifest themselves in the same way, as it follows from the coincidence of the spectra obtained from the laboratory-frame Bethe--Salpeter equation on the one hand, and the light-cone 't Hooft-type equation on the other.

## Key claim

The spectrum of heavy-light mesons obtained from the laboratory-frame Bethe-Salpeter equation (which manifestly includes chiral symmetry breaking via a nonzero quark condensate) is identical to the spectrum obtained from the light-cone 't Hooft-type equation (where the vacuum is trivial and no chiral condensate is directly visible), demonstrating that the limiting procedures used in the two frames are interchangeable and that chiral symmetry breaking effects are encoded in the light-cone spectrum through the absence of parity doubling.

## Method

The authors solved the heavy-light meson system in (1+1)-dimensional QCD in the 't Hooft large-N limit using two distinct approaches. In the laboratory frame with Coulomb (axial) gauge, they first solved the gap equation for the chiral angle \(\theta_p\) and then solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation for the meson wave function \(\phi(p)\) using a variational expansion in Gaussian basis functions. On the light cone, they solved the 't Hooft-type equation for the rescaled wave function \(\varphi(\xi)\) after taking the limit \(m_2 \to \infty\) and \(m_1 \to 0\), using both principal value and infrared regularization schemes.

## Key numbers

- The ground state (n=0) negative parity heavy-light meson energy is \(1.161\,\sqrt{\lambda}\) and the ground state positive parity energy is \(3.043\,\sqrt{\lambda}\).
- The first excited negative parity level (n=1) is at \(4.300\,\sqrt{\lambda}\) and the first excited positive parity level at \(5.286\,\sqrt{\lambda}\).
- The chiral condensate in the physical vacuum is \(\langle\bar\psi\psi\rangle = -\frac{N}{\sqrt 6}\,\sqrt{\lambda}\).
- The asymptotic behavior of the chiral angle at large momentum is \(\theta_p = \frac{\pi}{2}\,\text{sign}\,p - \frac{\pi}{\sqrt 6}\left(\frac{\sqrt\lambda}{p}\right)^3 + \ldots\) for \(|p|\gg\sqrt\lambda\).

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the 't Hooft model (t'Hooft, 1974) to heavy-light mesons, building on the Bars and Green (1978) formulation of the gap equation in the laboratory frame and the light-cone treatment of heavy-light systems by Burkardt (1995) and Zhitnitsky (1995). The results demonstrate that the spectrum is independent of the quantization scheme and gauge choice, and that chiral symmetry breaking manifests through the absence of parity doubling in both frames. This enables future studies of how chiral symmetry breaking affects meson properties in confining theories where the vacuum structure differs between reference frames.
