# $f$-mode oscillations of hybrid stars with pasta construction

> We investigate nonradial $f$-mode oscillations of hybrid neutron stars in full general relativity, employing hybrid equations of state describing a nuclear outer core and a pasta-phase transition to a quark-matter core. The validity of various universal relations is confirmed for those stars. Prospects of observations are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that hybrid neutron stars with a hadron-quark pasta-phase transition obey the same universal relations (correlating f-mode frequency with compactness, tidal deformability, and moment of inertia) as purely hadronic stars, but only for the specific pasta configurations that remain stable; the maximum-mass configurations never reach a pure quark-matter core, staying within the mixed-phase region near the rod/slab transition.

## Method

The authors construct hybrid equations of state by combining Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) nuclear matter (using Argonne V18 and Bonn-B potentials with three-body forces) with quark matter from Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSM, parameter alpha_DS=1.5). They compute the hadron-quark mixed phase with pasta structures (droplet, rod, slab, tube, bubble) using an energy-minimization method in Wigner-Seitz cells, treating the surface tension sigma as a free parameter (0, 10, and 30 MeV/fm^2). They solve the full general-relativistic TOV equations for stellar structure and the coupled perturbation equations for quadrupole (l=2) f-mode oscillations, using continued fractions to find complex eigenfrequencies.

## Key numbers

- Surface tension values used: sigma = 0 (Gibbs construction), 10, and 30 MeV/fm^2
- Quark matter bag constant: B_DS = 90 MeV/fm^3
- Strange quark current mass: m_s = 115 MeV
- f-mode frequency range quoted: f_f ~ 1.3-3 kHz
- Maximum-mass configurations occur near baryon density ~0.5 fm^-3 (at the rod/slab transition for both V18 and BOB EOSs)
- All EOSs satisfy the two-solar-mass constraint; the heaviest known pulsar PSR J0952-0607 has M = 2.35 +/- 0.17 solar masses

## Context

This work extends previous studies of f-mode oscillations in hybrid stars that used Maxwell construction (Zhao 2022) or quark-hadron crossover (Sotani 2023) by incorporating the more realistic pasta-phase construction with finite surface tension. It builds on the pasta-phase formalism developed by Wu (2019) and Ju (2021) and the BHF nuclear matter approach validated against GW170817 and NICER observations (Wei 2020, Burgio 2021). The results enable future gravitational-wave asteroseismology to distinguish between different phase transition scenarios in neutron star cores, provided next-generation detectors achieve sufficient sensitivity.
