# A symmetry for heavy nuclei: Proxy-SU(3)

> The SU(3) symmetry realized by J. P. Elliott in the sd nuclear shell is destroyed in heavier shells by the strong spin-orbit interaction. However, the SU(3) symmetry has been used for the description of heavy nuclei in terms of bosons in the framework of the Interacting Boson Approximation, as well as in terms of fermions using the pseudo-SU(3) approximation. We introduce a new fermionic approximation, called the proxy-SU(3), and we comment on its similarities and differences with the other approaches.

# A symmetry for heavy nuclei: Proxy-SU(3)

## Key claim
The proxy-SU(3) scheme provides a new fermionic approximation that reveals an approximate SU(3) symmetry in heavy deformed nuclei by replacing invading abnormal-parity orbitals (except the one with highest angular momentum) with their 0[110] counterparts from the shell below, with the expectation that nuclear properties related to angular momentum are little affected because angular momentum projections remain intact.

## Method
The authors identify 0[110] Nilsson orbit pairs (characterized by ΔK[ΔN Δn_z ΔΛ] = 0[110]) between the invading 1h_{11/2} orbitals and the deserting 1g_{9/2} orbitals in the 50-82 major shell. They replace all invading 1h_{11/2} orbitals except the 11/2[505] orbit by their 1g_{9/2} counterparts, which have identical angular momentum projections, thereby reconstructing a full sdg shell with U(15) symmetry and an SU(3) subalgebra. The same procedure is applied to the 28-50, 82-126, and 126-184 shells, which become approximate pf, pfh, and sdgi shells respectively.

## Key numbers
- The 50-82 major shell contains the 3s_{1/2}, 2d_{3/2}, 2d_{5/2}, and 1g_{7/2} orbitals (from the sdg shell) plus the invading 1h_{11/2} orbitals.
- The deserting 1g_{9/2} orbital consists of five Nilsson orbitals: 1/2[440], 3/2[431], 5/2[422], 7/2[413], 9/2[404].
- The invading 1h_{11/2} Nilsson orbitals are: 1/2[550], 3/2[541], 5/2[532], 7/2[523], 9/2[514].
- The 11/2[505] orbit in the 50-82 shell and the 13/2[606] orbit in the 82-126 shell have no 0[110] partners and are excluded from the replacement.
- The resulting shells correspond to U(10) for the 28-50 shell (pf shell), U(15) for the 50-82 shell (sdg shell), U(21) for the 82-126 shell (pfh shell), and U(28) for the 126-184 shell (sdgi shell).

## Context
This work extends the Elliott SU(3) symmetry (Elliott, 1958) from the sd shell to heavy deformed nuclei where the strong spin-orbit interaction destroys the original LS coupling scheme. It differs from the pseudo-SU(3) scheme (pseudo1, pseudo2; Draayer and Weeks, 1983; Bahri, 1968; Blokhin, 1974; Ginocchio, 1978) by using proton-proton and neutron-neutron 0[110] pairs rather than proton-neutron pairs, and by focusing on the replacement of abnormal-parity orbitals rather than a unitary transformation. The proxy-SU(3) scheme enables predictions of nuclear properties in heavy deformed nuclei using algebraic SU(3) methods, complementing existing fermionic descriptions such as the Fermion Dynamical Symmetry Model (FDSM) and bosonic descriptions like the Interacting Boson Model (IBM).
