# Review of quasi-elastic charge-exchange data in the nucleon-deuteron breakup reaction

> The available data on the forward charge exchange of nucleons on the deuteron up to 2GeV per nucleon are reviewed. The value of the inclusive nd->pnn/np->pn cross section ratio is sensitive to the fraction of spin-independent neutron-proton backward scattering. The measurements of the polarisation transfer in d(\vec{n},\vec{p})nn or the deuteron analysing power in p(\vec{d},pp)n in high resolution experiments, where the final nn or pp pair emerge at low excitation energy, depend upon the longitudinal and transverse spin-spin np amplitudes. The relation between these types of experiments is discussed and the results compared with predictions of the impulse approximation model in order to see what new constraints they can bring to the neutron-proton database.

## Key claim

The forward inclusive cross section ratio $R_{np}(0)$ from $nd\to pnn$ and $np\to pn$ is sensitive to the fraction of spin-independent neutron-proton backward scattering, but this interpretation relies on the Dean sum rule converging, which requires sufficiently high energy and negligible longitudinal momentum transfer; at lower energies the sum rule provides at best a qualitative description.

## Method

The authors review all available inclusive and exclusive measurements of forward charge exchange in nucleon-deuteron breakup reactions up to 2 GeV per nucleon, comparing them with predictions from the impulse approximation model. They relate the forward $d(n,p)nn/p(n,p)n$ cross section ratio, the polarisation transfer in $d(\vec{n},\vec{p})nn$, and the deuteron tensor analysing power in $p(\vec{d},pp)n$ to the longitudinal and transverse spin-spin neutron-proton amplitudes evaluated at 180 degrees in the center-of-mass frame, using the SAID SP07 phase shift solution for predictions.

## Key numbers

- $R_{np}(0)$ at 100 MeV: 0.600 (from SAID SP07 phase shift analysis)
- $R_{np}(0)$ at 1.0 GeV: 0.609 (from SAID SP07 phase shift analysis)
- $R_{np}(0)$ at 2.0 GeV: 0.56 $\pm$ 0.05 (JINR VBLHE measurement, 2009)
- $R_{np}(0)$ average from PSI data (300-560 MeV): 0.62 $\pm$ 0.01 (Freiburg group, 1988)
- $R_{np}(0)$ at 794 MeV: 0.56 $\pm$ 0.04 (LAMPF measurement, 1978)
- $R_{pn}(0)$ at 800 MeV: 0.66 $\pm$ 0.08 (LAMPF measurement, 1976)
- $d\sigma/dt$ for $dp\to\{pp\}n$ at $t=0$: 30 $\pm$ 4 mb/(GeV/$c)^2$ (Dubna bubble chamber, 3.35 GeV/$c$ deuteron momentum)
- Deuteron tensor analysing power $A_{NN}(0)$ at 100 MeV: 0.244 (from SAID SP07)
- Deuteron tensor analysing power $A_{NN}(0)$ at 1.0 GeV: -0.305 (from SAID SP07)
- Longitudinal polarisation transfer $K_{LL}(0)$ at 100 MeV: -0.008 (from SAID SP07)
- Longitudinal polarisation transfer $K_{LL}(0)$ at 1.0 GeV: -0.740 (from SAID SP07)

## Context

This review extends the earlier phenomenology of Lehar and Wilkin (2008) on $d(n,p)$ and $d(p,n)$ charge exchange, and builds on the theoretical work of Dean (1972) who derived the closure sum rule relating the deuteron charge-exchange cross section to the spin-flip fraction in $np$ scattering. The paper also discusses the high-resolution approach of Bugg and Wilkin (1985, 1987) for deuteron tensor analysing powers in $p(\vec{d},pp)n$, and compares results from the SAID SP07 phase shift analysis of Arndt et al. with experimental data from SATURNE 2, LAMPF, PSI, TRIUMF, and JINR VBLHE. The consistency between sum-rule and high-resolution approaches in the forward direction contributes positively to the neutron-proton charge exchange phenomenology, and the authors recommend extending measurements up to the highest Nuclotron energy of approximately 3.7 GeV.
