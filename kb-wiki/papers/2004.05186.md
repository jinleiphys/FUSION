# $Z$ plus jets production via double parton scattering in $pA$ collisions at the LHC

> We present results on Zjj production via double parton scattering in pA collisions at the LHC. We perform the analysis at leading and next-leading order accuracy with different sets of cuts on jet transverse momenta and accounting for the single parton scattering background. By exploiting the experimental capability to measure the centrality dependence of the cross section, we discuss the feasibility of DPS observation in already collected data at the LHC and in future runs.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the DPS2 contribution to Zjj production in pA collisions can be separated from the SPS and DPS1 background by exploiting its quadratic dependence on the nuclear thickness function T(B), and that this separation is feasible with data already collected in 2016 LHC pA runs, but only for symmetric jet cuts at LO; at NLO with asymmetric cuts the statistical significance decreases and may require higher luminosity.

## Method

The authors calculate Zjj production via double parton scattering in proton-lead collisions at sqrt(s_pN) = 8.16 TeV, using MCFM for Z and SPS Zjj cross sections and ALPGEN for dijet cross sections at LO, and NLOjet++ for NLO dijet simulations. They separate the DPS2 contribution by binning events in the nuclear thickness function T_A(B) and constructing the ratio R_Z = N(Zjj)/N(Z), which is constant if only SPS and DPS1 contribute (both linear in T) but rises linearly if DPS2 (quadratic in T) is present. The analysis is performed with symmetric jet pT cuts at LO and asymmetric cuts (10 GeV imbalance) at both LO and NLO to avoid instabilities in NLO dijet calculations.

## Key numbers

- For symmetric cuts at LO with pT^j > 20 GeV: DPS2 cross section = 7814 pb, DPS1 = 2971 pb, SPS = 15940 pb, total = 26725 pb; f_DPS2 = 0.292
- For symmetric cuts at LO with pT^j > 30 GeV: DPS2 = 1632 pb, DPS1 = 621 pb, SPS = 8030 pb, total = 10283 pb; f_DPS2 = 0.158
- For asymmetric cuts at NLO with pT^j1,j2 > 30,20 GeV: DPS2 = 3382 pb, DPS1 = 1286 pb, SPS = 14754 pb, total = 19423 pb; f_DPS2 = 0.174
- For asymmetric cuts at NLO with pT^j1,j2 > 40,30 GeV: DPS2 = 1013 pb, DPS1 = 385 pb, SPS = 7714 pb, total = 9112 pb; f_DPS2 = 0.111
- Expected number of Zjj events with symmetric cuts at LO and pT^j > 30 GeV, integrated luminosity 0.1 pb^-1: 152 events in T bin [0.0,0.9], 417 in [0.9,1.7], 459 in [1.7,2.1]
- Expected number of Zjj events with asymmetric cuts at NLO and pT^j1,j2 > 30,20 GeV, integrated luminosity 0.1 pb^-1: 910 events in T bin [0.00,1.60], 1032 in [1.60,2.10]
- The Z fiducial cross section is enhanced by a factor 1.3 at NLO relative to LO; the dijet cross section is enhanced by a factor 1.6; the SPS Zjj background is augmented by only 4%

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analyses of W+jets (Blok 2019) and multijet (Blok 2020) production via DPS in pA collisions to the Zjj final state, using the same technique of exploiting the quadratic T(B) dependence of DPS2 proposed by Alvioli (2019). The results are relevant for interpreting 2016 LHC pA run data and planning future runs, but the NLO analysis with asymmetric cuts shows reduced statistics, motivating future inclusion of soft gluon resummation (Banfi 2003) to recover symmetric-cut NLO predictions.
