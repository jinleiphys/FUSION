# A Nonlinear Realized Approach of SU(2) Chiral Symmetry Spontaneous Breaking and Properties of Nuclear Matter

> A nonlinear realization of SU(2) chiral symmetry spontaneous breaking approach is developed in the composite operator formalism. A Lagrangian including quark, gluon and Goldstone boson degrees of freedom of the chiral quark model is obtained from the QCD Lagrangian. A way to link the chiral symmetry spontaneous breaking formalism at hadron level and that at quark level is predicted. too. The application to nuclear matter shows that the approach is quite successful in describing the properties of nuclear matter and the quark condensate in it.

# A Nonlinear Realized Approach of SU(2) Chiral Symmetry Spontaneous Breaking and Properties of Nuclear Matter

## Key claim

The paper develops a nonlinear realization of SU(2) chiral symmetry spontaneous breaking in the composite operator formalism of QCD, claiming that this approach successfully describes nuclear matter properties and the quark condensate while removing the "upturn" problem present in other hadron-level approaches. The hedge is that the approach is demonstrated only for symmetric nuclear matter and pure neutron matter in the mean-field approximation, with two parameter sets fitted to saturation properties.

## Method

The authors construct four composite operators from current quark fields (u and d) and implement a nonlinear realization of chiral symmetry breaking by separating the composite operators into Goldstone boson and non-Goldstone parts via a transformation involving constituent quarks. They derive an effective QCD Lagrangian expressed in terms of constituent quarks, gluons, and Goldstone bosons, then link this quark-level formalism to a hadron-level formalism by constructing composite nucleon operators. The hadron-level Lagrangian is extended with omega and rho mesons for repulsive interactions and isospin symmetry breaking, and the properties of nuclear matter are computed in the mean-field approximation using the Hellmann-Feynman theorem for the quark condensate.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density: 0.152 fm^-3 (parameter set C1) and 0.151 fm^-3 (parameter set C2) for symmetric nuclear matter
- Binding energy: 15.297 MeV (C1) and 15.040 MeV (C2) for symmetric nuclear matter
- Compression modulus: 349.10 MeV (C1) and 298.88 MeV (C2) for symmetric nuclear matter
- Symmetry energy coefficient: 33.645 MeV (C1) and 32.593 MeV (C2) for symmetric nuclear matter
- Effective nucleon mass: 0.687 M_N (C1) and 0.736 M_N (C2) for symmetric nuclear matter
- Density at which the quark condensate decreasing rate is enhanced: 0.235 fm^-3, coinciding with the onset of pion condensate
- Nucleon sigma term used in quark condensate calculation: sigma_N = 45 MeV
- The nucleon mass constraint: M_N + delta M_N = 938 MeV

## Context

This work extends the composite operator formalism of chiral symmetry breaking (SV92) and the Manohar-Georgi model (MG84) by providing a nonlinear realization that eliminates double counting and spurious states present in that earlier formalism. The approach links the chiral symmetry breaking formalism at hadron level with that at quark level, addressing a gap between these two descriptions. The results for nuclear matter properties are comparable to those from relativistic mean-field models (SW97), while the quark condensate calculation resolves the "upturn" problem identified in other hadron-level approaches (LK94, MD97, Mit97) and shows consistency with the result of Bard90 regarding pion condensate onset.
