# New type of hydrogenlike charm-pion or charm-kaon matter

> Borrowing the structures of the hydrogen atom, molecular ion, and diatomic molecule, we predict the nature of a new type of hydrogenlike charm-pion or charm-kaon matter that could be obtained by replacing the proton and electron in hydrogen matter with a charmed meson and a pion or a kaon, respectively. We find that the spectra of the atom, molecular ion, and diatomic molecule can be obtained simultaneously with the Coulomb potential for the hydrogen, the charm-pion, and the charm-kaon systems. The predicted charm-pion matter also allows us to explore the mass shift mediated by the strong interaction. For the charm-pion and charm-kaon systems, the strong interactions could lead to binding energy shifts. Our calculations suggests that the binding energy shifts in charm-pion systems are in the order of several to tens of eV. For the charm-kaon systems, the results are in the order of tens to hundreds of eV. Exploring hydrogenlike charm-pion matter must lead to new demands for high-precision experiments.

# New type of hydrogenlike charm-pion or charm-kaon matter

## Key claim
The authors predict that hydrogenlike bound states (atom, molecular ion, and diatomic molecule) can be formed by replacing the proton and electron with a charmed meson (D⁺) and a pion (π⁻) or kaon (K⁻), respectively, with the Coulomb potential providing the binding. The strong interaction, treated as a perturbation via one-boson-exchange, produces binding energy shifts on the order of several to tens of eV for charm-pion systems and tens to hundreds of eV for charm-kaon systems, but the authors note that the effect of the strong interaction is small and that experimental detection remains a challenge.

## Method
The authors solve the Schrödinger equation analytically for the two-body atom-type systems using the Coulomb potential. For the three-body molecular ion and four-body diatomic molecule systems, they use the Gaussian expansion method (GEM) to solve the Schrödinger equations numerically, first validating the method by reproducing the known binding energies of the hydrogen molecular ion H₂⁺ (−16.20 eV vs. experimental −16.25 eV) and the hydrogen diatomic molecule H₂ (−31.60 eV vs. experimental −31.65 eV). The strong interaction contribution is computed using a one-boson-exchange (OBE) model with σ, ρ, and ω meson exchange, with a cutoff parameter Λ varied from 1 to 2 GeV.

## Context
This work extends the concept of hadronic atoms, previously studied in systems such as pion atoms (Chatellard 1997, Sigg 1996, Thomas 1979) and the N̄K system observed by the SIDDHARTA Collaboration (2011, 2012), to the charm sector using D⁺ mesons combined with pions or kaons. The authors note that kaonic deuterium has been of great concern to both experimentalists and theorists (Curceanu 2013, Zmeskal 2015, 2019, Meissner 2006, Barrett 1999, Doleschall 2014). The predicted hydrogenlike charm-pion and charm-kaon matter could provide a new platform to study the nonperturbative behavior of strong interactions through the mass shifts induced by the strong force, and the authors suggest that experimental detection will require high-precision measurements that may push current detection limits.
