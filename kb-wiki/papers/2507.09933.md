# Wavefunction-based operator optimization for two-hadron systems in lattice QCD

> A systematic way to constructing optimized interpolating operators for two-hadron systems is developed by incorporating inter-hadron spatial wavefunctions. The wavefunctions can be obtained from an iterative process with an appropriate initial guess. To implement these operators, a novel quark smearing technique utilizing $Z_3$ noise vectors is proposed, which allows for effectively incorporating inter-hadron spatial wavefunctions at the source without using all-to-all quark propagators. Proof-of-principle application to the $Ω_{ccc}Ω_{ccc}$ system using physical-point lattice configurations with a large size $La\simeq8.1$~fm shows that optimized operators enables clear identification of states around $2m_{Ω_{ccc}}\simeq 9700$ MeV with the energy gap as narrow as $\sim 5$ MeV. A comparison on correlation functions, effective energies, and HAL QCD potentials between unoptimized operators and optimized operators is given, with a special emphasis on the effects from nearby elastic scattering states. Potential applicability of the optimized operator to various two-hadron systems and its relation to the variational method are also discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that incorporating inter-hadron spatial wavefunctions into two-hadron interpolating operators enables clear identification of states with energy gaps as narrow as ~5 MeV, but this success is conditional on the iterative construction of dual functions from the HAL QCD potential and the use of a novel Z3-noise-based quark smearing technique to avoid all-to-all propagators.

## Method

The authors first compute the hadronic correlation function R_J(r,t) using a wall source operator, then derive the leading-order local potential V_LO(r) via the time-dependent HAL QCD equation. They solve the finite-volume Schrodinger equation with this potential to obtain eigenfunctions psi_n(r), which approximate the Nambu-Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes, and construct dual functions Psi_n(r) from these eigenfunctions. The optimized two-hadron operators O_n(t) are built by weighting baryon pairs at separation r with Psi*_n(r), and implemented at the source using a novel quark smearing where one baryon uses a local smearing while the other uses multiple supports on a sparse sublattice weighted by Psi^{1/3}_n(r) and multiplied by independent Z3 noise vectors.

## Key numbers

* Lattice size L^4 = 96^4 with lattice spacing a^{-1} = 2338.8 MeV, giving La = 8.1 fm and m_pi L = 5.63
* Pion mass m_pi = 137.1(3) MeV and kaon mass m_K = 501.8(7) MeV at physical point
* Omega_ccc mass m_{Omega_ccc} = 4847.3(1) MeV, with two-particle threshold 2m_{Omega_ccc} = 9694.6 MeV
* Energy gap as narrow as ~5 MeV between nearby elastic scattering states is resolved using optimized operators
* Lattice spacing a = 0.0844(1) fm

## Context

This work extends the HAL QCD method (Ishii 2007, Aoki 2010) by using its derived potentials to construct optimized operators, addressing the long-standing difficulty of dense elastic scattering states in two-hadron systems that plagued early NN calculations (Yamazaki 2012, NPLQCD 2011) where compact operators led to misidentification of states at heavy pion masses. The approach is complementary to the variational method (Luscher 1990, Blossier 2009) which uses linear combinations of low-momentum operators, and enables future applications to various two-hadron systems including those with baryons heavier than nucleons where the energy gap ~4pi^2/(m_B L^2) becomes particularly small.
