# Flavor SU(3) breaking effects in the chiral unitary model for meson-baryon scatterings

> We examine flavor SU(3) breaking effects on meson-baryon scattering amplitudes in the chiral unitary model. It turns out that the SU(3) breaking, which appears in the leading quark mass term in the chiral expansion, can not explain the channel dependence of the subtraction parameters of the model, which are crucial to reproduce the observed scattering amplitudes and resonance properties.

## Key claim

The flavor SU(3) breaking terms from the leading quark mass term in the chiral Lagrangian cannot explain the channel dependence of the subtraction constants in the chiral unitary model, which are necessary to reproduce observed meson-baryon scattering amplitudes and resonance properties.

## Method

The authors used the chiral unitary model with coupled-channel meson-baryon scattering, solving the scattering equation with a loop function regularized by dimensional regularization. They introduced SU(3) breaking via the quark mass term in the chiral Lagrangian (Eq. 4) and attempted to use a single subtraction constant across all channels, fitting it to threshold branching ratios of K⁻p scatterings. They compared results with and without the SU(3) breaking terms for both the S=-1 (K⁻p) and S=0 (πN) channels.

## Key numbers

- Subtraction constants from prior work (Oset 2001, Inoue 2001) with μ=630 MeV: for S=-1 channel, a_i values range from -1.84 (K̄N) to -2.67 (KΞ); for S=0 channel, a_i values range from 0.711 (πN) to -4.09 (KΣ)
- Without SU(3) breaking, optimal single subtraction constant a = -1.96 (case A); with SU(3) breaking, a = -1.59 (case B); with SU(3) breaking and physical decay constants, a = -1.68 (case C)
- Threshold branching ratio γ = Γ(K⁻p→π⁺Σ⁻)/Γ(K⁻p→π⁻Σ⁺): experiment 2.36±0.04; case A 1.80; case B 2.19; case C 2.35
- For S=0 channel, fitted subtraction constants: case A a=0.53; case B a=1.33; case C a=2.24
- Pole positions for Λ(1405) with case B: z₁=1424+1.6i MeV, z₂=1389+135i MeV; with case C: z₁=1424+2.6i MeV, z₂=1363+87i MeV

## Context

This work extends the chiral unitary model developed by Kaiser (1995), Oset (1998), and Inoue (2001), which successfully described Λ(1405) and N(1535) as dynamically generated resonances using channel-dependent subtraction constants. The authors attempted to place the model on firmer theoretical ground by using a single subtraction constant justified by SU(3) symmetry, but found that including the leading SU(3) breaking terms actually worsened agreement with data. This negative result highlights the importance of the subtraction constants as effective parameters and suggests that genuine quark-model resonance components may need to be included alongside the meson-baryon dynamics for a complete description.
