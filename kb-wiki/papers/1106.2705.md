# Sub-barrier capture with quantum diffusion approach: actinide-based reactions

> With the quantum diffusion approach the behavior of capture cross sections and mean-square angular momenta of captured systems are revealed in the reactions with deformed nuclei at subbarrier energies. The calculated results are in a good agreement with existing experimental data. With decreasing bombarding energy under the barrier the external turning point of the nucleusnucleus potential leaves the region of short-range nuclear interaction and action of friction. Because of this change of the regime of interaction, an unexpected enhancement of the capture cross section is expected at bombarding energies far below the Coulomb barrier. This effect is shown its worth in the dependence of mean-square angular momentum of captured system on the bombarding energy. From the comparison of calculated and experimental capture cross sections, the importance of quasifission near the entrance channel is shown for the actinide-based reactions leading to superheavy nuclei.

## Key claim
The quantum diffusion approach predicts an unexpected enhancement of the capture cross section at bombarding energies far below the Coulomb barrier, but this effect is conditional on the external turning point of the nucleus-nucleus potential leaving the region of short-range nuclear interaction and friction. The effect is more pronounced in collisions of spherical nuclei, where the regime change occurs at energies about 3–5 MeV below the barrier, whereas for deformed nuclei the change occurs at 8–20 MeV below the barrier.

## Method
The authors applied the quantum diffusion approach based on a quantum master-equation for the reduced density matrix, which incorporates fluctuation and dissipation effects to model coupling with various channels. They calculated capture cross sections and mean-square angular momenta by integrating over orientations of deformed nuclei and summing over angular momenta, using a double-folding nucleus-nucleus potential with static quadrupole deformation parameters. The capture probability was obtained from a propagator for an inverted oscillator approximating the potential barrier, with two distinct regimes depending on whether the external turning point lies inside or outside the interaction radius \(R_{int}=R_b+1.1\) fm.

## Key numbers
- For the \(^{36}\)S + \(^{238}\)U system, the difference in Coulomb barrier heights between sphere-pole and sphere-side orientations is about 16 MeV; for \(^{16}\)O + \(^{238}\)U, the difference is about 8 MeV.
- The internal-excitation width used in calculations is \(\hbar\gamma = 32\) MeV, and the friction coefficient is \(\hbar\lambda = 2\) MeV.
- For the \(^{16}\)O + \(^{232}\)Th reaction, the minimum in \(\langle J^2\rangle\) occurs about 7 MeV below the Coulomb barrier; for \(^{16}\)O + \(^{238}\)U, about 8 MeV below; for \(^{19}\)F + \(^{232}\)Th, about 12 MeV below; for \(^{48}\)Ca + \(^{232}\)Th, about 15 MeV below.
- The Coulomb barrier for spherical nuclei in the \(^{16}\)O + \(^{238}\)U reaction is 80 MeV; for \(^{4}\)He + \(^{238}\)U, it is 21.2 MeV.
- The pocket in the nucleus-nucleus potential for \(^{16}\)O + \(^{238}\)U is washed out at angular momenta \(J > 65\).

## Context
This work extends the quantum diffusion approach previously applied to spherical nuclei (Sargsyan et al., 2010) to reactions with deformed actinide targets, including systems such as \(^{16}\)O, \(^{48}\)Ca + \(^{232}\)Th, \(^{238}\)U and \(^{48}\)Ca + \(^{246,248}\)Cm. The results contradict the conventional coupled-channel approach, which cannot describe capture cross sections below the Coulomb barrier (Dg, year not given), and show that the Wong formula fails to reproduce the sub-barrier capture cross section even when static deformation is included. The predictions enable experimental searches for the predicted enhancement of the astrophysical S-factor at extreme sub-barrier energies and clarify the role of quasifission near the entrance channel in actinide-based reactions leading to superheavy nuclei.
