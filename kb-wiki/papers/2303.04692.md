# Magnetic enhancement of baryon confinement modeled via a deformed Skyrmion

> We discuss the baryon properties under a strong magnetic field. We adopt the Skyrme model and calculate the magnetic field dependence of the mass and the pressure distribution in the soliton. We elucidate a magnetically induced contribution to the pressure sum rule and interpret it as an extra confining force. We also quantize the soliton to estimate the difference between the proton and the neutron and find a simple relation between the pressure and the mass differences.

## Key claim

The paper argues that a strong magnetic field can enhance baryon confinement by generating an extra confining force, as demonstrated through the pressure sum rule in the Skyrme model; however, this enhancement is conditional on the magnetic field exceeding a threshold value \(B_0 = 496\ \mathrm{MeV}\) (in physical units), below which the magnetic field instead weakens confinement.

## Method

The authors solve the Skyrme model with a generalized hedgehog Ansatz that breaks rotational symmetry due to the external magnetic field, using finite element methods to minimize the energy functional and determine the deformed soliton profile. They derive the energy-momentum tensor via the Noether theorem and compute the pressure distribution inside the soliton, applying the pressure sum rule to quantify the confining force. The soliton is then quantized by introducing a collective coordinate for rotation about the magnetic field axis, yielding the mass splitting between proton and neutron states.

## Key numbers

- The soliton mass \(M(B)\) has a global minimum at \(B_0 = 3.60 f_\pi^2 a^2\) corresponding to \(\sqrt{B_0} = 496\ \mathrm{MeV}\), with minimum mass \(M(B_0) = 61.6 f_\pi a^{-1} = 687\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The model parameters are fixed as \(f_\pi = 54.0\ \mathrm{MeV}\), \(a = 4.84\), and pion mass \(m_\pi = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
- The neutron-proton mass difference is given by \(\Delta m = m_n - m_p = \Phi / \Gamma\), where \(\Phi\) is the effective magnetic flux and \(\Gamma\) the moment of inertia.
- The pressure difference between neutron and proton satisfies \(P_n - P_p = \frac{2}{3} \Delta m\).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous analysis (Chen, 2021) by including finite pion mass effects, which change the deformation pattern: with \(m_\pi = 138\ \mathrm{MeV}\) the soliton always favors prolate deformation, unlike the earlier work where oblate deformation occurred at weak fields. The results connect to lattice-QCD studies of hadron spectra under magnetic fields (Ding, 2020) and to the experimental program at the future Electron-Ion Collider, where pressure distributions inside the proton (Burkert, 2018) are of interest. The formalism also shares technical similarities with studies of self-gravitating Skyrmions in cosmology (Canfora, 2013; Ayon-Beato, 2015).
