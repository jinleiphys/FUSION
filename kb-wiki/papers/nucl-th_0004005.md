# An Effective Approach to VMD at One Loop Order and the Departures from Ideal Mixing for Vector Mesons

> We examine the mechanisms producing departures from ideal mixing for vector mesons within the context of the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model. We show that kaon loop transitions between the ideal combinations of the $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons necessitate a field transformation in order to get the mass eigenstates. It is shown that this transformation is close to a rotation for processes involving, like meson decays, on--shell $ω$ and $ϕ$ mesons. The HLS model predicts a momentum dependent, slowly varying mixing angle between the ideal states. We examine numerically the consequences of this for radiative and leptonic decays of light mesons. The mean $ω-ϕ$ mixing angle is found smaller than its ideal value; this is exhibited separately in radiative and in leptonic decays. Effects of nonet symmetry breaking in the vector sector are compared to those produced by the field rotation implied by the HLS model.

## Key claim

The Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model predicts that the mixing angle between the ideal $\omega$ and $\phi$ states is momentum-dependent and slowly varying, and that the transformation from ideal to physical states is close to a rotation only for on-shell mesons; the mean $\omega$-$\phi$ mixing angle is found smaller than its ideal value, exhibited separately in radiative and leptonic decays.

## Method

The authors used the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) model at one-loop order, incorporating SU(3) breaking via the BKY mechanism in both the pseudoscalar (PS) and vector (V) sectors, and included kaon loop transitions between the ideal $\omega_I$ and $\phi_I$ combinations. They diagonalized the vector mass term at one-loop order using a momentum-dependent transformation matrix $G(\delta_V)$, and numerically examined the consequences for radiative and leptonic decays of light mesons, comparing effects of nonet symmetry breaking to those produced by the field rotation.

## Key numbers

- The SU(3) breaking parameter in the pseudoscalar sector is $\ell_A \equiv [f_K/f_\pi]^2 = 1.5$, with $f_\pi = 92.42$ MeV.
- The $\rho$ meson Higgs-Kibble mass is defined as $m_\rho^2 = a g^2 f_\pi^2$, where $a$ is the standard HLS parameter and $g$ is the universal vector meson coupling.
- The $\omega$-$\phi$ mixing angle $\delta_V(s)$ is given by $\tan{2\delta_V}(s) = \frac{2 \Pi_{\omega_I\phi_I}(s)}{m_\rho^2(1-\ell_V)+(\Pi_{\omega_I\omega_I}(s)-\Pi_{\phi_I \phi_I}(s))}$, where $\ell_V$ is the SU(3) breaking parameter in the vector sector.
- The kaon loop function $\Pi(s)$ near the $\phi$ mass has an imaginary part of order $\simeq 0.05$ GeV$^2$ compared to the polynomial part of $\simeq 0.3$ GeV$^2$.
- The $\phi$ mass is slightly above the 2-kaon thresholds, while the $\omega$ mass is much below the 2-kaon thresholds.

## Context

This work extends the HLS model framework of Bando et al. (1985) and the BKY SU(3) breaking mechanism of Bramon et al. (1992) by incorporating one-loop corrections from kaon loops to generate $\omega$-$\phi$ mixing, building on earlier studies of radiative decays by O'Donnell (1981) and the authors' own previous work (Benayoun et al., 1999). It enables a consistent treatment of momentum-dependent mixing angles in vector meson phenomenology, and provides a foundation for analyzing departures from ideal mixing in both radiative and leptonic decays within an effective Lagrangian approach.
