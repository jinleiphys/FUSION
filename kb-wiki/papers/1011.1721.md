# Short range correlations and wave function factorization in light and finite nuclei

> Recent BNL and Jlab data provided new evidence on two nucleon correlations (2NC) in nuclei. The data confirm the validity of the convolution model, describing the spectral function (SF) of a correlated pair moving in the mean field with high and low relative and center-of-mass (cm) momenta, respectively. The model is built assuming that the wave function (WF) of a nucleus A, describing a configuration where the cm momentum of a correlated pair is low and its relative momentum is high, factorizes into the product of the two-body WF and that of the A-2 system. Such a factorization has been shown to occur in nuclear matter (NM). Here it is shown that few-body systems exhibit factorization, which seems to be therefore a general property, to be reproduced also in studies of the WF of finite nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the factorization of the nuclear wave function into a product of a two-body wave function and the wave function of the A-2 system, previously demonstrated for nuclear matter, also occurs in few-body systems like 3He and 4He, suggesting this factorization is a general property that should be reproduced in studies of finite nuclei.

## Method

The authors analyze the ground-state wave functions of 3He (using the "Pisa" wave function with the AV18 interaction) and 4He (using an ATMS wave function with the RSC interaction) to test whether the wave function factorizes in the region of two-nucleon correlations (2NC), where the relative momentum of a correlated pair is high and its center-of-mass momentum is low. They examine the angular dependence of the squared wave function and ratios of the wave function at different distances to verify factorization, and compare the convolution model spectral function with the exact spectral function for 3He.

## Key numbers

- For 3He, the ratio R1(x,y,y',theta) becomes approximately constant (indicating factorization) when x = 1.5 fm (far from the 2NC region) and when x = 4 fm (in the 2NC region), with the center-of-mass motion assumed in S wave
- The ratio R = |Phi(K_cm, k_rel)|^2 / |Phi(0, k_rel)|^2 approximates n_cm(|K_cm|)/n_cm(0) in the 2NC region where |K_cm| << |k_rel|
- For 4He, preliminary results qualitatively similar to those of 3He are found using an ATMS wave function with the RSC interaction

## Context

This work extends the convolution model for the spectral function, previously validated for nuclear matter by Baldo (year not given) and for 3He by Ciofi degli Atti and Simula (year not given), to finite few-body systems. The model was recently confirmed experimentally at BNL for 12C by Piasetzky (year not given). The results enable phenomenological studies of two-nucleon correlations in finite nuclei using factorized wave functions, with forthcoming experiments expected to measure the center-of-mass and relative momentum distributions n(|K_cm|) and n(|k_rel|).
