# On Single Particle Energies and Nuclear g Factors

> If we add one neutron to doubly magic ^{100}Sn, we can associate the low lying states in^{101} Sn with single particle states.The the J=5/2^{+} and J= 7/2^{+} states are identified as d_{5/2}and g_{7/2}states respectively...They are separated by an energy of 0.18 MeV.Unfortunately there is a disputeas to the ordering of these states.We examine how the 2 scenarios-- J=5/2^{+} ground state or J=7/2^{+}ground state-- affect spectra of higher Sn isotopes .

## Key claim

The ordering of the J=5/2+ and J=7/2+ states in 101Sn (whether the d5/2 or g7/2 single-particle orbital is lower) is disputed, and this ambiguity significantly affects the calculated nuclear g factors of 2+ states in heavier even-even Sn isotopes, though no single choice of ordering and effective g-factor parameters can simultaneously reproduce the measured g factors of both 110Sn and 112Sn.

## Method

The authors performed shell-model calculations for odd-A and even-A Sn isotopes in the g7/2-d5/2 neutron model space using the surface delta interaction (SDI) and the sn100pn effective interaction. For each interaction, they considered three scenarios for the single-particle energy splitting in 101Sn: d5/2 lower by 0.172 MeV, g7/2 lower by 0.172 MeV, and degenerate orbits. They calculated the J=7/2+ - J=5/2+ splitting in 113Sn and the excitation energies of the first 2+ states and their g factors in even Sn isotopes from A=102 to 112, using bare and renormalized g-factor values.

## Key numbers

- The splitting between the J=5/2+ and J=7/2+ states in 101Sn is 0.172 MeV, but the ordering is disputed.
- In 113Sn, the experimental J=7/2+ state lies 77.389 keV above the J=1/2+ ground state, while the J=5/2+ state lies at 409.83 keV, giving a J=7/2+ - J=5/2+ splitting of -332.447 keV (J=7/2+ lower).
- With the SDI interaction and d5/2 lower in 101Sn, the calculated J=7/2+ - J=5/2+ splitting in 113Sn is -0.352 MeV; with g7/2 lower it is -0.008 MeV.
- The experimental g factor of the 2+ state in 112Sn is 0.15 with an error bar of about 0.05.
- The experimental g factor of the 2+ state in 110Sn is 0.29.
- For 112Sn with the SDI interaction and d5/2 lower, the bare g factor is 0.3771; with gL=-0.1 and x=0.75 it is 0.1729; with gL=+0.1 and x=0.75 it is 0.3927.
- For 112Sn with the SDI interaction and g7/2 lower, the bare g factor is 0.0110; with gL=-0.1 and x=0.75 it is -0.0792; with gL=+0.1 and x=0.75 it is 0.1226.

## Context

This work extends the schematic calculations of Yu and Zamick (2000) on g factors in Sn isotopes, who noted that with degenerate single-particle energies and a surface delta interaction the g factors in 112Sn vanish. The paper addresses the experimental dispute between Seweryniak et al. and Darby et al. over the ground-state spin of 101Sn. The results show that the near constancy of 2+ excitation energies across even Sn isotopes, noted by Talmi in developing the Generalized Seniority Model, is reproduced, but the g-factor calculations cannot simultaneously fit the measured values for 110Sn and 112Sn, motivating further measurements in lighter Sn isotopes such as 108Sn.
