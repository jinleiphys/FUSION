# Convergence of Eigenvector Continuation

> Eigenvector continuation is a computational method that finds the extremal eigenvalues and eigenvectors of a Hamiltonian matrix with one or more control parameters. It does this by projection onto a subspace of eigenvectors corresponding to selected training values of the control parameters. The method has proven to be very efficient and accurate for interpolating and extrapolating eigenvectors. However, almost nothing is known about how the method converges, and its rapid convergence properties have remained mysterious. In this letter we present the first study of the convergence of eigenvector continuation. In order to perform the mathematical analysis, we introduce a new variant of eigenvector continuation that we call vector continuation. We first prove that eigenvector continuation and vector continuation have identical convergence properties and then analyze the convergence of vector continuation. Our analysis shows that, in general, eigenvector continuation converges more rapidly than perturbation theory. The faster convergence is achieved by eliminating a phenomenon that we call differential folding, the interference between non-orthogonal vectors appearing at different orders in perturbation theory. From our analysis we can predict how eigenvector continuation converges both inside and outside the radius of convergence of perturbation theory. While eigenvector continuation is a non-perturbative method, we show that its rate of convergence can be deduced from power series expansions of the eigenvectors. Our results also yield new insights into the nature of divergences in perturbation theory.

# Convergence of Eigenvector Continuation

## Key claim

Eigenvector continuation converges more rapidly than perturbation theory both inside and outside the radius of convergence of perturbation theory, with the faster convergence achieved by eliminating "differential folding", the interference between non-orthogonal vectors appearing at different orders in perturbation theory. The authors prove that eigenvector continuation and a diagnostic variant called vector continuation have identical convergence properties, allowing the convergence rate to be deduced from power series expansions of the eigenvectors.

## Method

The authors introduce a new variant called vector continuation (VC), which projects the target eigenvector onto the subspace spanned by training-point eigenvectors, and prove that VC and eigenvector continuation (EC) have identical convergence properties. They analyze convergence by considering a sequence of training points accumulating at a limit point, replacing training vectors with derivative vectors at that point, and computing the norm of the residual vector as a function of order N. The convergence is quantified using convergence ratios μ defined from the ratio of "last term" norms at two widely separated orders, and these ratios are compared for EC, VC, and perturbation theory across several model Hamiltonians including a 262144-dimensional BCS-BEC crossover system.

## Key numbers

- For Models 1A, 1B, and 1C (each with linear space of 800 dimensions), eigenvector continuation and vector continuation converge more rapidly than perturbation theory at all orders N, with nearly identical errors between EC and VC at each order.
- For Model 2 (two-component fermions with attractive zero-range interactions in 3D, 262144-dimensional space), the convergence ratio μ^PT exceeds 1 on the strong-coupling BEC side (c = -3.957 corresponds to the unitary limit), indicating divergence of perturbation theory, while μ^VC and μ^EC remain well below 1 even at very strong coupling.
- For Model 3 (500-dimensional Hamiltonian), the closest branch point to c=0 occurs near c = 0.84; the (1,1) and (2,2) Padé approximations to μ^VC describe the shape of μ^VC well when no nearby branch points are present.
- For the D-parameter case, achieving the same error as N+1 training vectors in the one-parameter case requires (N+D)!/(N!D!) training vectors for D parameters.

## Context

This work presents the first study of convergence properties of eigenvector continuation, a method introduced by Frame (2017) that has been used to extend quantum Monte Carlo to problems with strong sign oscillations (Frame, 2019), as a fast emulator for quantum many-body systems (Konig, 2019; Ekstrom, 2019), and as a resummation method for perturbation theory (Demol, 2019). The analysis reveals that the divergence of perturbation theory arises from large non-orthogonal terms in the series expansion of the wave function, an effect the authors call differential folding, and shows that eigenvector continuation avoids this problem entirely. The results enable prediction of eigenvector continuation convergence outside the radius of convergence of perturbation theory and suggest that superfluid many-body wave functions can potentially be reconstructed by variational methods throughout the entire BEC-BCS crossover region.
