# Quark models of dibaryon resonances in nucleon-nucleon scattering

> We look for $ΔΔ$ and $NΔ$ resonances by calculating $NN$ scattering phase shifts of two interacting baryon clusters of quarks with explicit coupling to these dibaryon channels. Two phenomenological nonrelativistic chiral quark models giving similar low-energy $NN$ properties are found to give significantly different dibaryon resonance structures. In the chiral quark model (ChQM), the dibaryon system does not resonate in the $NN$ $S$-waves, in agreement with the experimental SP07 $NN$ partial-wave scattering amplitudes. In the quark delocalization and color screening model (QDCSM), the $S$-wave NN resonances disappear when the nucleon size $b$ falls below 0.53 fm. Both quark models give an $IJ^P = 03^+$ $ΔΔ$ resonance. At $b=0.52 $fm, the value favored by baryon spectrum, the resonance mass is 2390 (2420) MeV for the ChQM with quadratic (linear) confinement, and 2360 MeV for the QDCSM. Accessible from the $^3D_3^{NN}$ channel, this resonance is a promising candidate for the known isoscalar ABC structure seen more clearly in the $pn$$\to $$dππ$ production cross section at 2410 MeV in the recent preliminary data reported by the CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration. In the isovector dibaryon sector, our quark models give a bound or almost bound $^5S_2^{ΔΔ}$ state that can give rise to a $^1D_2^{NN}$ resonance. None of the quark models used has bound $NΔ$ $P$-states that might generate odd-parity resonances.

## Key claim

The paper claims that two nonrelativistic chiral quark models (ChQM and QDCSM) which give similar low-energy NN properties produce significantly different dibaryon resonance structures, with the QDCSM showing a critical dependence on nucleon size b: S-wave NN resonances disappear when b falls below 0.53 fm, and only the QDCSM with the largest nucleon size (b=0.60 fm) produces isovector resonances.

## Method

The authors calculate NN scattering phase shifts using a coupled-channel resonating group method (RGM) that includes ΔΔ, NΔ, and hidden-color channels, treating Δs as stable particles. Two phenomenological nonrelativistic quark models are used: the Salamanca chiral quark model (ChQM) with scalar meson exchange, and the quark delocalization and color screening model (QDCSM) without scalar exchange, both with parameters fitted to baryon spectra and low-energy NN data. The phase shifts are computed for partial waves up to energies beyond the ΔΔ and NΔ thresholds to identify dibaryon resonances arising from bound states below these thresholds.

## Key numbers

- In the ChQM with quadratic (linear) confinement at b=0.52 fm, the IJ^P = 03^+ ΔΔ resonance mass is 2390 (2420) MeV; in the QDCSM at the same b, it is 2360 MeV
- The critical nucleon size b below which S-wave NN resonances disappear in the QDCSM is approximately 0.53 fm for the ^3S_1^{NN} state and 0.56 fm for the ^1S_0^{NN} state
- The pure ^7S_3^{ΔΔ} bound state mass in the QDCSM1 is 2365 MeV, which is about 60 MeV lower than in ChQM2 (2425 MeV)
- The QDCSM3 (b=0.60 fm) gives a ^3S_1^{NN} resonance at 2393 MeV with width 70 MeV, and a ^1S_0^{NN} resonance at 2433 MeV with width 128 MeV
- The pure ^3S_1^{ΔΔ} bound state mass in QDCSM3 is 2115 MeV, which is 251 MeV lower than in ChQM2 (2366 MeV)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of NN scattering using the same ChQM and QDCSM models (Chen et al., 2007) from low energies into the resonance region near the ΔΔ and NΔ thresholds. The results are compared to the experimental SP07 partial-wave NN scattering amplitudes and to the ABC anomaly observed by the CELSIUS-WASA Collaboration (2007) in pn → dππ production at 2410 MeV. The paper enables future studies of dibaryon resonances with explicit inclusion of the N*(1440) resonance and pionic channels, and suggests that the isoscalar 03^+ ΔΔ resonance is a promising candidate for the ABC structure.
