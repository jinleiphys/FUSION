# Probing $Λ$ potential via its $v_{2}$ flow in hypernuclei-induced reaction

> The hyperon potential, particularly its behavior at high densities, is crucial for resolving the ``hyperon puzzle'' in neutron stars and for advancing our understanding of the strong interactions between strange and non-strange particles in high baryon density environments. Using the hadronic transport model AMPT-HC, hypernucleus-nucleus collision is studied. It is found that at beam energies below the threshold for hyperon production, the hyperon elliptic flow exhibits noticeable asymmetry between the positive and negative rapidity regions and is sensitive to the strength of the hyperon potential, especially in the large negative rapidity region. One can extract the hyperon potential approximately twice the saturation density based on the hyperon elliptic flow in the negative rapidity region, and the hyperon potential around the saturation density based on the hyperon elliptic flow in the positive rapidity region.

# Probing Λ potential via its v₂ flow in hypernuclei-induced reaction

## Key claim

The hyperon elliptic flow v₂ in semi-central hypernucleus-nucleus collisions at beam energies below the threshold for hyperon production is sensitive to the strength of the Λ potential, with the negative-rapidity region probing the potential at approximately twice saturation density and the positive-rapidity region probing it near saturation density. The claim is conditional on using pre-existing Λ hyperons from the projectile hypernucleus rather than secondarily produced hyperons, which avoids uncertainties from inelastic production channels.

## Method

The study uses the hadronic transport model AMPT-HC (A Multi-Phase Transport model with Hadronic Cascade and mean-field potentials) to simulate semi-central collisions of ¹⁹⁷_ΛAu (a heavy hypernucleus) with a normal Au target at a laboratory beam energy of 400 MeV per nucleon. The Λ hyperon elliptic flow v₂ is computed as a function of rapidity and transverse momentum in both positive and negative rapidity regions, with and without a Skyrme-type Λ potential set at two-thirds the strength of the density-dependent single-nucleon potential (approximately -35 MeV at saturation density). The model uses the test-particle method for baryon potentials and assumes free nucleon-nucleon elastic cross sections, with hyperon-nucleon cross sections taken equal to nucleon-nucleon cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Beam energy: 400 MeV per nucleon in the laboratory frame, chosen to be below the threshold for Λ hyperon production.
- Λ potential at saturation density: approximately -35 MeV, based on the quark counting rule giving two-thirds of the nucleon potential strength.
- The Λ elliptic flow v₂ in the positive rapidity region becomes positive and significantly larger than zero when the Λ potential is included, compared to essentially zero without the potential.
- In the negative rapidity region, the Λ elliptic flow v₂ changes from negative (indicating emission primarily in the y direction) to positive when the Λ potential is present, demonstrating sensitivity to high-density potential effects.
- The rapidity distribution shows most Λ hyperons concentrated in the positive rapidity region, with significantly fewer in the negative rapidity region, though exact numerical yields are not provided.

## Context

This work extends the approach of using hypernuclei as probes of the Λ potential in nuclear matter, building on earlier studies of hypernuclei production near saturation density (incl2018) and directed flow of hyperons in heavy-ion collisions (star2023). It addresses the "hyperon puzzle" in neutron stars, where theoretical models predict hyperons soften the equation of state but observations show high-mass neutron stars (lon15). The method avoids uncertainties from secondary hyperon production and the stiffness of the nuclear matter equation of state, which plague studies using typical nucleus-nucleus collisions. The proposed hypernucleus-nucleus collision experiments could be implemented at facilities such as GSI/FAIR using the HYDRA time-projection chamber prototype (gsi23), enabling clean extraction of the high-density hyperon potential relevant to neutron star interiors.
