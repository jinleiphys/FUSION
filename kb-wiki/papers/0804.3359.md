# QCD Splitting/Joining Functions at Finite Temperature in the Deep LPM Regime

> There exist full leading-order-in-alpha_s numerical calculations of the rates for massless quarks and gluons to split and join in the background of a quark-gluon plasma through hard, nearly collinear bremsstrahlung and inverse bremsstrahlung. In the limit of partons with very high energy E, where the physics is dominated by the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal (LPM) effect, there are also analytic leading-log calculations of these rates, where the logarithm is ln(E/T). We extend those analytic calculations to next-to-leading-log order. We find agreement with the full result to within roughly 20% for E(less) >~ 10 T, where E(less) is the energy of the least energetic parton in the splitting/joining process. We also discuss how to account for the running of the coupling constant in the case that E/T is very large. Our results are also applicable to isotropic non-equilibrium plasmas if the plasma does not change significantly over the formation time associated with particle splitting.

## Key claim

The authors extend analytic calculations of QCD splitting/joining rates in the deep LPM regime from leading-log to next-to-leading-log (NLL) order, finding agreement with full leading-order-in-alpha_s numerical results to within roughly 20% for the least energetic parton energy E_< greater than or approximately equal to 10 T, where T is the plasma temperature. The NLL result is derived under the parametric assumption that P is much less than T/[alpha_s^2 ln(alpha_s^{-1})], meaning deep in the LPM regime but not too deep.

## Method

The authors work within the Arnold-Moore-Yaffe (AMY) formalism, solving the LPM integral equation for the splitting functions gamma_{a<->bc} that describe nearly collinear bremsstrahlung and inverse bremsstrahlung in a quark-gluon plasma. They approximate the collision kernel A(q_perp) by its hard-thermal-loop form T m_D^2 / [q_perp^2 (q_perp^2 + m_D^2)], valid when momentum transfers are small compared to T, and solve the resulting differential equation for the vector function F_s(h) by Taylor-expanding the differences of F_sol in the LPM equation. The NLL result is expressed as an implicit algebraic equation for the transverse momentum scale hat_mu_perp, which must be solved numerically.

## Key numbers

* For gluon splitting g<->gg with N_f=3, the NLL result differs from the full leading-order formula by roughly 5% for p_< = 100 T, where p_< is the smaller of the two final momenta.
* For p_< = 10 T, the NLL result differs from the full leading-order formula by roughly 20%.
* The constant xi in the NLL formula is xi = exp(2 - gamma_E + pi/4) approximately equal to 9.09916, where gamma_E is the Euler-Mascheroni constant.
* For QCD with N_f massless fermion flavors, the Debye mass squared is m_D^2 = (1 + N_f/6) g^2 T^2.

## Context

This work extends earlier analytic leading-log calculations of splitting rates in the deep LPM regime, which were based on the work of Zakharov, Baier et al., and the review by Salgado and Wiedemann. The full leading-order-in-alpha_s numerical calculations were carried out by Jeon and Moore (2003) using the AMY formalism. The NLL results enable more accurate analytic comparisons with full numerical calculations for jet energies E_< greater than about 10 T, and are applicable to isotropic non-equilibrium plasmas if the plasma does not change significantly over the formation time. The authors note that for very high energies where alpha_s ln(P/T) is not small, they discuss running coupling effects only to leading-log order, leaving a full NLL calculation for higher energies to future work.
