# The Spinless Relativistic Woods-Saxon Problem

> Motivated by the observation of a recent renewal of rather strong interest in the description of bound states by (semi-) relativistic equations of motion, we revisit, for the example of the Woods-Saxon interactions, the eigenvalue problem posed by the spinless Salpeter equation and recall various elementary knowledge, considerations, and techniques that practitioners seeking solutions to this specific reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter equation may find helpful.

# The Spinless Relativistic Woods-Saxon Problem

## Key claim
The spinless Salpeter equation with a Woods-Saxon potential can be analyzed using standard variational and bounding techniques, but approximate analytical solutions from the literature (particularly Hamzavi et al.) fail basic consistency checks such as the virial theorem and the lower spectral bound, casting doubt on their reliability.

## Context
This paper revisits the spinless Salpeter equation (a three-dimensional reduction of the Bethe-Salpeter formalism) with Woods-Saxon interactions, motivated by renewed interest in semirelativistic bound-state descriptions. It extends the rigorous bounding techniques of Bargmann (nonrelativistic) and Daubechies (relativistic) to the Woods-Saxon potential, and provides variational upper bounds using the Laguerre basis method previously developed by the same authors. The work directly critiques the approximate analytical solutions of Hamzavi et al., Hassanabadi et al., and Feizi et al., showing that the Hamzavi results violate the lower spectral bound and fail the virial theorem, while the Feizi results pass the lower bound check. The paper enables practitioners to test proposed approximate solutions against rigorous constraints before accepting them.
