# Total nuclear reaction cross-section database for radiation protection in space and heavy-ion therapy applications

> Realistic nuclear reaction cross-section models are an essential ingredient of reliable heavy-ion transport codes. Such codes are used for risk evaluation of manned space exploration missions as well as for ion-beam therapy dose calculations and treatment planning. Therefore, in this study, a collection of total nuclear reaction cross-section data has been generated within a GSI-ESA-NASA collaboration. The database includes the experimentally measured total nucleus-nucleus reaction cross-sections. The Tripathi, Kox, Shen, Kox-Shen, and Hybrid-Kurotama models are systematically compared with the collected data. Details about the implementation of the models are given. Literature gaps are pointed out and considerations are made about which models fit best the existing data for the most relevant systems to radiation protection in space and heavy-ion therapy.

## Key claim

The paper compiles a comprehensive database of experimentally measured total nucleus-nucleus reaction cross-sections from 103 publications and systematically compares five semi-empirical models (Tripathi, Kox, Shen, Kox-Shen, and Hybrid-Kurotama) against these data, but the authors note that no single parameterization fits all projectile-target systems well, and charge-changing cross-sections can deviate significantly from true reaction cross-sections for light and neutron-rich projectiles.

## Method

The authors collected 1786 total reaction and charge-changing cross-section data points from 103 peer-reviewed publications, focusing on nucleus-nucleus reactions (excluding hadronic projectiles like protons and neutrons). They re-implemented the Kox, Shen, Kox-Shen, Tripathi (with Horst optimizations for ⁴He), and Hybrid-Kurotama semi-empirical parameterizations in Python, then systematically compared model predictions against the collected experimental data for systems relevant to space radiation protection and heavy-ion therapy. The database includes projectile and target specifications, kinetic energy, cross-section values with uncertainties, measurement methods, and experimental facility information, accessible via a web application at https://www.gsi.de/fragmentation.

## Key numbers

- 1786 cross-section data points from 103 publications are included in the database
- For ⁴He projectiles, neutron-removal reactions contribute up to approximately 20% of the total reaction cross-section at 1 GeV/u, based on Kox-Shen model calculations
- The ratio σ_cc/σ_R for ²⁰Ne + ¹²C at 950 MeV/u was measured as 0.91, while model predictions expected values between 0.95 and 0.97
- For ⁵⁶Fe + ²⁰⁷Pb at 1800 MeV/u, the measured σ_cc/σ_mc ratio was 0.85
- The Kox-Shen model uses a transparency parameter c with separate functional forms for projectile energies below and above 45 MeV/u

## Context

This work extends the earlier data review by Norbury et al. (2012) which analyzed measured cross-section data relevant to space radiation protection but did not report exact energy values or measured cross-section numbers. The database enables systematic validation of the five most commonly used parameterizations in transport codes such as Geant4, PHITS, FLUKA, and HZETRN, and identifies literature gaps where new experimental measurements are needed. The authors recommend that future experiments focus on systems where charge-changing cross-sections are poor estimators of total reaction cross-sections, particularly for light projectiles (A_p ≤ 28) and neutron-rich heavy projectiles (A_p ≥ 56).
