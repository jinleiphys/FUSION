# Electric dipole polarizability of neutron rich nuclei

> Insights into the equation of state of neutron rich matter obtained from the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb are in sharp conflict with earlier measurements of the electric dipole polarizability. We use a set of accurately calibrated energy density functionals to highlight the tension and to articulate how plans for a highly-intense Gamma Factory may alleviate the tension.

## Key claim

The neutron skin thickness of 208Pb measured by the PREX-2 collaboration implies a stiff symmetry energy (L = 106 ± 37 MeV) that is in sharp conflict with the softer symmetry energy (L = 59.8 ± 4.1 MeV) inferred from earlier measurements of the electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb; the overlap between the two probability distributions for the polarizability amounts to less than 25% at the 1σ level.

## Method

The author uses a set of 13 covariant energy density functionals (EDFs) that are accurately calibrated to properties of finite nuclei and neutron stars, then computes both ground-state properties and the linear response using a self-consistent random phase approximation (RPA). The electric dipole polarizability is obtained from the inverse-energy-weighted sum of the dipole response, which is directly related to the photoabsorption cross section. The PREX-2-informed polarizability distribution is derived by exploiting the strong correlation between the product J·αD(208) and the neutron skin thickness Rskin(208), together with the correlation between J and Rskin(208).

## Key numbers

- Recommended value of the slope of the symmetry energy from a compilation of theoretical predictions and experimental measurements: L = (59.8 ± 4.1) MeV.
- Neutron skin thickness of 208Pb reported by the PREX-2 collaboration: Rskin(208) = (0.283 ± 0.071) fm.
- Symmetry energy at saturation density and slope inferred from the PREX-2 result: J = (38.1 ± 4.7) MeV, L = (106 ± 37) MeV.
- Electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb extracted from the RCNP experiment after removal of the quasi-deuteron contribution: αD(208) = (19.6 ± 0.6) fm³.
- PREX-2-informed electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb (median and 68% confidence interval): αD(208) = (21.8⁺¹·¹₋₁·₄) fm³.

## Context

This work highlights the tension between the PREX-2 measurement of the neutron skin thickness of 208Pb (Adhikari 2021) and the earlier RCNP measurement of the electric dipole polarizability of 208Pb (Tamii 2013). The paper extends the analysis of Reed (2021) by directly comparing the probability distributions for αD(208) from the two experimental techniques. The author proposes that a future Gamma Factory, with photon fluxes more than a million times more intense than the HIγS facility at Duke University, could resolve the tension by measuring the photoabsorption cross section of exotic neutron-rich nuclei such as 132Sn with unprecedented precision, thereby constraining the slope of the symmetry energy L and its implications for neutron-star radii.
