# The $S_{E1}$ factor of radiative $Î±$ capture on $^{12}$C in effective field theory

> The $S_{E1}$ factor of radiative $Î±$ capture on $^{12}$C is studied in effective field theory. We briefly discuss the strategy for the calculation of the reaction and report a first result of $S_{E1}$ at the Gamow-peak energy, $E_G=0.3$~MeV.

# The $S_{E1}$ factor of radiative $\alpha$ capture on $^{12}$C in effective field theory

## Key claim
The $S_{E1}$ factor at the Gamow-peak energy $E_G = 0.3$ MeV is reported as approximately 58 keV b, but this is described as a first result with an error estimate still under investigation, meaning the value is preliminary and not yet assigned a formal uncertainty.

## Method
The author employs an effective field theory (EFT) where the ground states of $\alpha$ and $^{12}$C are treated as point-like particles, with the first excited states of these nuclei defining a large momentum scale $\Lambda_H \simeq 150$ MeV and an expansion parameter $Q/\Lambda_H \sim 1/3$. Capture amplitudes are calculated from Feynman diagrams including loop diagrams and a contact vertex; six parameters remain in the amplitudes, of which four are effective range parameters for elastic $\alpha$-$^{12}$C scattering for $l=1$, one is fixed by the binding energy of the subthreshold $1_1^-$ state of $^{16}$O, and the others are fitted to phase shift data and $S_{E1}$ data.

## Key numbers
- Expansion parameter of the EFT: $Q/\Lambda_H \sim 1/3$, where $Q \sim k_G = 41$ MeV (Gamow peak momentum) and $\Lambda_H \simeq 150$ MeV (large momentum scale from first excited energies of $\alpha$ or $^{12}$C).
- Binding energies of subthreshold states in $^{16}$O: $E = -0.045$ MeV for the $1_1^-$ state and $E = -0.24$ MeV for the $2_1^+$ state, relative to the $\alpha$-$^{12}$C breakup threshold.
- Resonant state energies: $E = 2.42$ MeV for the $1_2^-$ state and $E = 2.68$ MeV for the $2_2^+$ state.
- Fitted parameters: $h^{(1)R} = -6.95(11) \times 10^2$ MeV$^3$ and $y^{(0)} = 0.495(18)$ MeV$^{-1/2}$, with $N = 151$ data points and $\chi^2/N \simeq 1.72$.
- Result at Gamow-peak energy $E_G = 0.3$ MeV: $S_{E1} \simeq 58$ keV b (preliminary, error estimate under investigation).

## Context
This work extends previous theoretical approaches to the $^{12}$C($\alpha$,$\gamma$)$^{16}$O reaction, which is critical for determining the C/O ratio in stars. Prior studies are categorized into cluster models using generalized coordinate methods (e.g., Descouvemont et al., 1984) or potential models (e.g., Langanke and Koonin, 1985), and phenomenological models using Breit-Wigner, $R$-matrix (Lane and Thomas, 1958), or $K$-matrix parameterizations (e.g., Humblet et al., 1976). The present EFT approach offers an alternative to intensive numerical analyses that fit many parameters to large experimental datasets (e.g., deBoer et al., 2017; Xu et al., 2013; An et al., 2015). The result enables future work to incorporate $E2$ transitions and to provide a full uncertainty estimate for the $S$-factor at astrophysical energies.
