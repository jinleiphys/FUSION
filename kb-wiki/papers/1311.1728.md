# Acoustic scaling of anisotropic flow in shape-engineered events: implications for extraction of the specific shear viscosity of the quark gluon plasma

> It is shown that the acoustic scaling patterns of anisotropic flow for different event shapes at a fixed collision centrality (shape-engineered events), provide robust constraints for the event-by-event fluctuations in the initial-state density distribution from ultrarelativistic heavy ion collisions. The empirical scaling parameters also provide a dual-path method for extracting the specific shear viscosity $(η/s)_\mathrm{QGP}$ of the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) produced in these collisions. A calibration of these scaling parameters via detailed viscous hydrodynamical model calculations, gives $(η/s)_\mathrm{QGP}$ estimates for the plasma produced in collisions of Au+Au ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 0.2$ TeV) and Pb+Pb ($\sqrt{s_{NN}}= 2.76$ TeV). The estimates are insensitive to the initial-state geometry models considered.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that acoustic scaling patterns of anisotropic flow coefficients $v_n$ hold for events selected by shape (via the $q_2$ flow vector) at fixed collision centrality, and that the slope parameters of these scaling patterns are insensitive to the initial-state geometry model used, providing a robust dual-path method for extracting the specific shear viscosity $(\eta/s)_\mathrm{QGP}$ of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Method

The authors used Monte Carlo Glauber (MC-Glauber) and Monte Carlo Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) models to compute initial-state eccentricities $\varepsilon_n$ and transverse size $\bar{R}_n$ for shape-selected events in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 0.2$ TeV and Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV. They analyzed published $v_2$ data from the ALICE collaboration for shape-engineered events (selected via the $q_2$ flow vector) and $v_n$ data from CMS and STAR collaborations, then validated the predicted linear dependence of $\ln(v_n/\varepsilon_n)$ on $1/\bar{R}$ and on $n^2$ for these data. Viscous hydrodynamical calculations were used to calibrate the slope parameters $\beta'$ and $\beta''$ against $4\pi\eta/s$, allowing extraction of $(\eta/s)_\mathrm{QGP}$.

## Key numbers

- For the plasma produced in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 0.2$ TeV (RHIC), the extracted value is $\langle 4\pi\eta/s \rangle_\mathrm{QGP} \sim 1.3 \pm 0.2$.
- For the plasma produced in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV (LHC), the extracted value is $\langle 4\pi\eta/s \rangle_\mathrm{QGP} \sim 2.2 \pm 0.2$.
- The slope parameter $\beta''$ for the scaling of $\ln[v_2(q_2)/\varepsilon_2(q_2)]$ vs. $1/\bar{R}_2(q_2)$ was found to be $\beta'' \sim 1.3 \pm 0.07$ for each of the three shape selections ($q_{2(\text{Lo})}$, $q_{2(\text{Avg.})}$, $q_{2(\text{Hi})}$) in Pb+Pb collisions.
- The difference between $v_2(q_{2(\text{Hi})})$ and $v_2(q_{2(\text{Lo})})$ can be more than a factor of two for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV.

## Context

This work extends the acoustic scaling framework previously established by Lacey et al. (2013, 2011) for $q_n$-averaged events to shape-engineered events, providing a more stringent constraint on initial-state fluctuations than earlier approaches. It addresses the large (order 100%) theoretical uncertainty in $(\eta/s)_\mathrm{QGP}$ extraction from $v_2$ alone, which had been attributed to incomplete knowledge of initial-state geometry as noted by Luzum (2008) and Song (2010). The method enables extraction of $(\eta/s)_\mathrm{QGP}$ that is insensitive to the choice of MC-Glauber or MC-KLN initial-state models, and the results are in reasonable agreement with estimates from Schenke (2011), Gale (2012), and Qiu (2011).
