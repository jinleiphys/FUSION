# Strongly Intensive Quantities

> Analysis of fluctuations of hadron production properties in collisions of relativistic particles profits from use of measurable intensive quantities which are independent of system size variations. They are referred to as strongly intensive quantities. The first family of such quantities was proposed already in 1992. The second is introduced in this paper. We also present a proof of independence of volume fluctuations for quantities from both families within the framework of statistical mechanics.

# Strongly Intensive Quantities

## Key claim
The paper introduces a second family of strongly intensive quantities (the Δ family) for characterizing fluctuations of hadron production properties, which, together with the previously known Σ family, are strictly independent of volume and volume fluctuations within the grand canonical ensemble, and approximately independent in the canonical and micro-canonical ensembles for large systems. The authors prove this independence explicitly, but note that the property holds exactly only within the grand canonical ensemble framework and approximately in other ensembles when the system is large enough.

## Method
The authors define two families of strongly intensive quantities, Σ^{AB} and Δ^{AB}, constructed from the first and second moments of two extensive random variables A and B (e.g., particle multiplicities). They first demonstrate the independence from source-number fluctuations using a model of independent particle sources, then provide a rigorous proof within the grand canonical ensemble of a quantum gas mixture, showing that Σ^{AB} and Δ^{AB} depend only on intensive quantities (densities and scaled variances) and are strictly independent of average volume and volume fluctuations. The effects of resonance decays and conservation laws in canonical and micro-canonical ensembles are also discussed.

## Key numbers
- The scaled variance ω_A for an extensive variable A is given by ω_A = ω_a^* + ⟨a⟩ ω_s, where ω_a^* is the single-source scaled variance and ω_s is the scaled variance of the source number distribution.
- In the grand canonical ensemble, Σ^{AB} = (1/(n_A + n_B)) [n_B ω_A^* + n_A ω_B^*] and Δ^{AB} = (1/(n_A + n_B)) [n_B ω_A^* − n_A ω_B^*], where n_A and n_B are particle number densities and ω_A^*, ω_B^* are the scaled variances at fixed volume.
- The ν_{dyn}^{AB} measure is related to Σ^{AB} by ν_{dyn}^{AB} = (⟨A+B⟩/(⟨A⟩⟨B⟩)) [Σ^{AB} − 1], showing it decreases as ν_{dyn}^{AB} ∝ ⟨N_s⟩^{-1} and is not intensive.
- For the Φ measure extended to multiplicity fluctuations, Φ = (√(⟨A⟩⟨B⟩)/⟨A+B⟩) [(Σ^{AB})^{1/2} − 1].

## Context
This work extends the concept of strongly intensive quantities first introduced by Gazdzicki and Mrowczynski (1992) for the Φ measure of transverse momentum fluctuations, and later extended for multiplicity fluctuations (1999). The paper introduces the Δ family as a new class of such quantities that require only variances (not correlations) and thus can be measured independently for each variable. The results enable experimental analysis of hadron production fluctuations in relativistic nucleus-nucleus collisions without needing to know or control the fluctuating collision volume, building on prior fluctuation measures such as Φ and ν_{dyn} used in studies by the NA49 collaboration and others.
