# No-core configuration-interaction model for the isospin- and angular-momentum-projected states

> [Background] Single-reference density functional theory is very successful in reproducing bulk nuclear properties like binding energies, radii, or quadrupole moments throughout the entire periodic table. Its extension to the multi-reference level allows for restoring symmetries and, in turn, for calculating transition rates. [Purpose] We propose a new no-core-configuration-interaction (NCCI) model treating properly isospin and rotational symmetries. The model is applicable to any nucleus irrespective of its mass and neutron- and proton-number parity. It properly includes polarization effects caused by an interplay between the long- and short-range forces acting in the atomic nucleus. [Methods] The method is based on solving the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation within a model space built of linearly-dependent states having good angular momentum and properly treated isobaric spin. The states are generated by means of the isospin and angular-momentum projection applied to a set of low-lying (multi)particle-(multi)hole deformed Slater determinants calculated using the self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock approach. [Results] The theory is applied to calculate energy spectra in N~Z nuclei that are relevant from the point of view of a study of superallowed Fermi beta-decays. In particular, a new set of the isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections to these decays is given. [Conclusions] It is demonstrated that the NCCI model is capable to capture main features of low-lying energy spectra in light and medium-mass nuclei using relatively small model space and without any local readjustment of its low-energy coupling constants. Its flexibility and a range of applicability makes it an interesting alternative to the conventional nuclear shell model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a no-core configuration-interaction (NCCI) model that treats isospin and rotational symmetries simultaneously, claiming it can capture main features of low-lying energy spectra in light and medium-mass nuclei using relatively small model space and without local readjustment of low-energy coupling constants; however, the model fails to reproduce the isoscalar pairing correlations needed for the ground state of \(^{6}\)Li, indicating a systematic deficiency in describing \(T=0, I=1\) states.

## Method

The method solves the Hill-Wheeler-Griffin equation within a model space built from linearly-dependent states having good angular momentum and properly treated isobaric spin, generated by isospin and angular-momentum projection applied to a set of low-lying (multi)particle-(multi)hole deformed Slater determinants calculated using self-consistent Skyrme-Hartree-Fock with the SV\(_T\) interaction. The approach mixes states projected from different self-consistent Slater determinants, including those corresponding to different shape-current orientations in odd-odd \(N=Z\) nuclei, and uses a truncation scheme based on eigenvalues of the norm matrix with a cut-off parameter \(\zeta\).

## Key numbers

- The average corrected \(ft\) value \(\overline{\mathcal{F}t} = 3073.7(11)\) s for superallowed Fermi beta decays, leading to \(|V_{\rm ud}| = 0.97396(25)\).
- The CKM matrix first-row unitarity sum \(|V_{\rm ud}|^2 + |V_{\rm us}|^2 + |V_{\rm ub}|^2 = 0.99937(65)\), satisfied with precision better than 0.1%.
- The total \(\chi^2\) per degree of freedom for the confidence-level test is \(\chi_d^2 = 6.3\) for 11 degrees of freedom; excluding \(^{62}\)Ga and \(^{34}\)Cl transitions gives \(\chi_d^2 = 1.7\).
- For \(^{6}\)Li, the calculated energy of the lowest \(I=1^+, T=0\) state is \(-27.037\) MeV, almost 5 MeV higher than the experimental total energy of \(-31.995\) MeV.
- For \(^{8}\)Li, the calculated \(1_2^+\) state underbinds experiment by almost 3 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous static multi-reference density functional theory approach (Satula 2009, 2011, 2012) by introducing a dynamic variant that mixes states projected from different self-consistent Slater determinants, addressing ambiguities in shape-current orientation for odd-odd \(N=Z\) nuclei. The NCCI model is presented as an alternative to conventional nuclear shell model calculations and to perturbative approaches for isospin-symmetry-breaking corrections (Towner and Hardy 2008, 2010), and enables improved calculations of superallowed beta-decay matrix elements for tests of CKM matrix unitarity. The results highlight the need for further theoretical work on isoscalar \(pn\)-pairing correlations, which are missing in the present approach and have been discussed in the context of the Wigner energy by Satula (1997) and others.
