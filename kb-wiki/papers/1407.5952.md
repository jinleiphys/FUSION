# Flow harmonics within an analytically solvable viscous hydrodynamic model

> Based on a viscous hydrodynamic model with anisotropically perturbed Gubser flow and isothermal Cooper-Frye freezeout at early times, we analytically compute the flow harmonics $v_n(p_T)$ and study how they scale with the harmonic number $n$ and transverse momentum, as well as the system size, shear and bulk viscosity coefficients, and collision energy. In particular, we find that the magnitude of shear viscous corrections grows linearly with $n$. The mixing between different harmonics is also discussed. While this model is rather simple as compared to realistic heavy-ion collisions, we argue that the scaling results presented here may be meaningfully compared to experimental data collected over many energies, system sizes, and geometries.

## Key claim

The paper claims that within a simplified, analytically solvable viscous hydrodynamic model with early isothermal freezeout, the shear viscous correction to flow harmonics \(v_n(p_T)\) grows linearly with the harmonic number \(n\), and that these scaling results may be meaningfully compared to experimental data despite the model's simplicity.

## Method

The authors perturb the conformal Gubser flow solution with rapidity-independent anisotropic perturbations proportional to spherical harmonics, linearize the Navier-Stokes equations in the early-time regime \(\tau \ll L\), and compute the flow harmonics \(v_n(p_T)\) via the Cooper-Frye freezeout formula on an isothermal hypersurface, including shear and bulk viscous corrections to the distribution function. The integrals over transverse coordinate and transverse momentum are performed analytically in the small-\(p_T\) region by expanding the Bessel functions, and the scaling with harmonic number \(n\), transverse momentum \(p_T\), system size \(L\), shear viscosity \(\eta_0\), and bulk viscosity is extracted.

## Key numbers

* Shear viscous correction to \(v_n\) scales as \(v_n / v_n^{\text{ideal}} \sim 1 - \mathcal{O}(n \kappa)\), where \(\kappa \equiv \eta_0 B^2 / (\lambda C)\) is the Knudsen number, generalizing the known \(v_2\) behavior to arbitrary \(n\).
* In the small-\(p_T\) region with \(m_T \gg T\) and \(n \sim \mathcal{O}(1)\), \(v_n(p_T) \approx (9 \epsilon_n / 32) [\Gamma(3n)/\Gamma(4n)] (64 p_T / (B^3 T))^n (n-1) \{ 2(3n+2)/(4n+1) - n\kappa/(8(3n-1)) (6n+6+2m_T/T+3-p_T^2/T^2) \}\).
* For large \(n \gg 1\) and \(m_T/T \sim \mathcal{O}(1)\), \(v_n(p_T) \sim \epsilon_n n^2 (27 p_T / (4 B^3 T))^n e^{n - n \ln n} (1 - n\kappa/6)\), showing a faster-than-exponential decrease with \(n\).
* The unperturbed part ratio \(J_2^0 / J_1^0 \sim (T K_0(m_T/T) / (m_T K_1(m_T/T))) (p_T / (B^3 T))^2 \ll 1\).

## Context

This work extends the analytically solvable conformal hydrodynamic framework of Gubser (2010) and Gubser and Yarom (2010) by incorporating anisotropic perturbations and viscous corrections to compute flow harmonics, building on earlier scaling studies of \(v_2\) by Bhalerao et al. (2005) and Hatta et al. (2014). The model enables an analytical understanding of how \(v_n\) scales with harmonic number, system size, and viscosity, which may help interpret experimental scaling trends observed at RHIC and LHC across different collision energies and system sizes, though the early freezeout and conformal assumptions limit direct quantitative comparison.
