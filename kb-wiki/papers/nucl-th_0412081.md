# Isospin dependence of nucleon emission and radial flow in heavy-ion collisions induced by high energy radioactive beams

> Using an isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model we study the emission of free nucleons and the nuclear radial flow in central heavy-ion collisions induced by high energy radioactive beams. The midrapidity neutron/proton ratio and its transverse momentum dependence are found very sensitive to the high density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy. The nuclear radial flow, however, depends only weakly on the symmetry energy.

# Isospin dependence of nucleon emission and radial flow in heavy-ion collisions induced by high energy radioactive beams

## Key claim
The midrapidity neutron/proton ratio and its transverse momentum dependence are very sensitive to the high density behavior of nuclear symmetry energy, but the nuclear radial flow depends only weakly on the symmetry energy, the effect of the symmetry energy on the radial flow is at most about 4% even for the stiffest symmetry energy considered.

## Method
The authors used the isospin- and momentum-dependent transport model IBUU04 to simulate central collisions of $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ at a beam energy of 400 MeV/nucleon. A parameter $x$ was varied in the single nucleon potential to mimic different density dependences of the symmetry energy, ranging from softer ($x=1$) to stiffer ($x=-2$). Free nucleons were identified as those with local baryon densities less than $\rho_0/8$ at 30 fm/c, and radial flow velocities were extracted from the momentum and coordinate distributions of all particles.

## Key numbers
- The reaction system $^{132}Sn+^{124}Sn$ at 400 MeV/nucleon forms compressed matter of about twice normal nuclear matter density that lasts for about 15 fm/c.
- The isospin asymmetry $\delta_{free} \equiv (N_n-N_p)/(N_n+N_p)$ at midrapidity decreases by about a factor of 2 as the parameter $x$ increases from $-2$ to $1$.
- For the stiffest symmetry energy ($x=-2$), the effect of the symmetry energy on the position-averaged radial flow velocity $<\beta>$ is only about 4%.
- With the softest symmetry energy ($x=1$), the Coulomb potential results in a higher radial flow velocity for protons than neutrons by about 3%.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of symmetry energy at subnormal densities, where a softer symmetry energy causes more neutron emission (Li 1997), by showing that at supranormal densities the effect reverses due to the crossover of symmetry energy functions at $\rho_0$. The authors previously identified the $\pi^-/\pi^+$ ratio (Li 2003; Li and Zuo 2004) as a probe of high-density symmetry energy; this paper adds midrapidity nucleon observables. The IBUU04 model with $x=-1$ (corresponding to $E_{sym}(\rho)\approx 31.6(\rho/\rho_0)^{1.05}$) was previously found to reproduce isospin diffusion data from NSCL/MSU (Chen 2004). The results are intended to guide experiments at the planned Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA) using the Time Projection Chamber.
