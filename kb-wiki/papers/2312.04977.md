# Prediction of $P_{cc}$ states in quark model

> Inspired by the observation of hidden-charm pentaquark $P_c$ and $P_{cs}$ states by the LHCb Collaboration, we explore the $qqc\bar{c}c$ ($q~=~u$ or $d$) pentaquark systems in the quark delocalization color screening model. The interaction between baryons and mesons and the influence of channel coupling are studied in this work. Three compact $qqc\bar{c}c$ pentaquark states are obtained, whose masses are 5259 MeV with $I(J^P)$ = $0(1/2^-)$, 5396 MeV with $I(J^P)$ = $1(1/2^-)$, and 5465 MeV with $I(J^P)$ = $1(3/2^-)$. Two molecular states are obtained, which are $I(J^P)$ = $0(1/2^-)$ $Λ_c J/ψ$ with 5367 MeV and $I(J^P)$ = $0(5/2^-)$ $Ξ_{cc}^* \bar{D}^*$ with 5690 MeV. These predicted states may provide important information for future experimental search.

## Key claim

The quark delocalization color screening model predicts three compact pentaquark states and two molecular states in the \(qqc\bar{c}c\) system, but these predictions depend on the model's specific parameter choices and the assumption that only \(S\)-wave configurations are considered, with tensor force coupling and \(D\)-wave decays deferred to future work.

## Method

The authors employed the quark delocalization color screening model (QDCSM), an extension of the quark cluster model, to systematically investigate \(S\)-wave \(qqc\bar{c}c\) pentaquark systems with isospin \(I = 0\) and \(1\) and spin-parity \(J^P = 1/2^-, 3/2^-\), and \(5/2^-\). They calculated effective potentials between baryon and meson clusters, performed dynamic bound-state calculations using the resonating group method for both single channels and coupled channels, and computed scattering phase shifts to examine possible resonance states.

## Key numbers

- Three compact pentaquark states: mass 5259 MeV with \(I(J^P) = 0(1/2^-)\), mass 5396 MeV with \(I(J^P) = 1(1/2^-)\), and mass 5465 MeV with \(I(J^P) = 1(3/2^-)\).
- Two molecular states: \(\Lambda_c J/\psi\) with \(I(J^P) = 0(1/2^-)\) at 5367 MeV and \(\Xi_{cc}^* \bar{D}^*\) with \(I(J^P) = 0(5/2^-)\) at 5690 MeV.
- The \(\Lambda_c J/\psi\) single-channel bound state has a binding energy of \(-15\) MeV relative to its theoretical threshold.
- The \(\Xi_{cc}^* \bar{D}^*\) molecular state has a binding energy of \(-3\) MeV and a root mean square cluster spacing of 1.9 fm.
- The compact state with \(I(J^P) = 0(1/2^-)\) has a root mean square of 1.0 fm; the two compact states with \(I(J^P) = 1(1/2^-)\) and \(I(J^P) = 1(3/2^-)\) each have a root mean square of 0.9 fm.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous QDCSM studies of hidden-charm pentaquark \(P_c\) states (Huang et al., 2015, 2019) to the doubly-charmed \(P_{cc}\) sector, motivated by LHCb's observations of \(P_c\) and \(P_{cs}\) states. It complements molecular picture studies by Chen et al. (2017) and Wang et al. (2019) using the one-boson exchange model, and color-magnetic interaction model predictions by An et al. (2019). The predicted states provide targets for future experimental searches at facilities such as LHCb, and the authors note that \(D\)-wave decay channels (e.g., \(\Lambda_c J/\psi\)) should be investigated in future work to assess the stability of the \(\Xi_{cc}^* \bar{D}^*\) molecular state.
