# Electric, thermal and thermoelectric response of a hot pion gas in a time dependent background magnetic field

> The prime focus of the work is to determine the electric, thermal and thermoelectric transport coefficients of a hot pion gas in the presence of time-dependent background magnetic fields. The thermoelectric effect is analyzed by examining the magneto-Seebeck and Nernst coefficients in the hot pionic medium under such conditions. Furthermore, the phenomenologically relevant elliptic flow coefficient, linked to the Knudsen number, is examined. The analysis reveals the significant impact of both the strength and time dependence of the magnetic field on the transport coefficients of the pionic medium. The results are analyzed in contrast to those obtained under a constant magnetic field.

## Key claim
The paper claims that the electric, thermal, and thermoelectric transport coefficients of a hot pion gas are significantly modified when the background magnetic field is time-dependent (decaying exponentially) compared to the constant-field case, but this analysis is restricted to slowly decaying fields with large decay parameters and assumes the relaxation time approximation.

## Method
The authors solve the relativistic Boltzmann equation under the relaxation time approximation for a hot pion gas composed of π⁺, π⁻, and π⁰, using an ansatz for the non-equilibrium distribution function that includes terms linear in the electromagnetic fields and their first-order space-time derivatives. The coupled differential equations for the transport coefficients are solved analytically for exponentially decaying electric and magnetic fields of the form E = E₀ e^{-t/τ_E} and B = B₀ e^{-t/τ_B}, with the momentum-averaged relaxation time parametrized as a polynomial in m/T. The resulting currents are decomposed into Ohmic and Hall components, and the thermal and thermoelectric responses are obtained from the heat current and the steady-state condition for the induced electric field.

## Key numbers
- Ohmic conductivity σ_e/T and Hall conductivity σ_H/T are computed as functions of temperature (range shown includes T = 0.12 GeV and T = 0.14 GeV) for magnetic field amplitudes up to B₀ values corresponding to cyclotron frequencies, at t = 2.5 fm and chemical potential μ_{π±} = ±0.1 GeV.
- Thermal conductivity κ and Hall-like thermal conductivity are shown at T = 0.14 GeV as functions of time for different magnetic field decay parameters, with μ_{π±} = ±0.1 GeV and μ_{π⁰} = 0 GeV.
- The magneto-Seebeck coefficient S_B and normalized Nernst coefficient NB are presented as functions of magnetic field amplitude and decay parameter at t = 2.5 fm, T = 0.12 GeV, and μ_{π±} = ±0.1 GeV.
- The Knudsen number Kn and elliptic flow coefficient v₂ are computed using the relation v₂ = v₂^h / (1 + Kn/Kn₀) with v₂^h = 0.154 ± 0.014 and Kn₀ = 0.7, with the system size l = 4 fm and relative speed v ≈ 0.98.

## Context
This work extends previous studies of transport in a hot pion gas under constant magnetic fields (Das 2019, Ghosh 2022, Dash 2020) by incorporating time-dependent magnetic fields, which are more realistic for heavy-ion collision conditions at RHIC and LHC. The results are compared with the constant-field results of PhysRevD.102.076007 for electrical conductivity and PhysRevD.89.054013 for thermal conductivity. The analysis enables more accurate modeling of the hadronic phase in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding the elliptic flow coefficient through its connection to thermal conductivity and the Knudsen number, and it opens the door to studying thermoelectric effects such as the magneto-Seebeck and Nernst coefficients in a time-varying magnetic field environment.
