# Fragment Intrinsic Spins and Fragments' Relative Orbital Angular Momentum in Nuclear Fission

> We present the first fully unrestricted microscopic calculations of the primary fission fragment intrinsic spins and of the fission fragments' relative orbital angular momentum for $^{236}$U$^*$, $^{240}$Pu$^*$, and $^{252}$Cf using the time-dependent density functional theory framework. Within this microscopic approach, free of restrictions and unchecked assumptions and which incorporates the relevant physical observables for describing fission, we evaluate the triple distribution of the fission fragment intrinsic spins and of their fission fragments' relative orbital angular momentum and show that their dynamics is dominated by their bending collective modes, in contradistinction to the predictions of the existing phenomenological models and some interpretations of experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors present the first fully unrestricted microscopic calculations of fission fragment intrinsic spins and their relative orbital angular momentum, showing that the dynamics is dominated by bending collective modes, in contrast to existing phenomenological models and some interpretations of experimental data. The key hedge is that the triple projection method used is more precise than single projections, leading to corrections of about 2-3 ℏ for intrinsic spins and about 1 ℏ for orbital angular momentum.

## Method

The authors used time-dependent density functional theory (TDDFT) extended to superfluid systems to simulate fission of 236U*, 240Pu*, and 252Cf. They performed triple angular momentum projections of the overlap of the fissioning nucleus intrinsic wave function to determine the triple probability distribution P(S^L, S^H, Λ) for the light fragment spin, heavy fragment spin, and relative orbital angular momentum. The calculations used two different nuclear energy density functionals (SkM* and SeaLL1) in simulation boxes of 30^2×60 fm with a lattice constant of 1 fm, evolving the initial nuclear wave function from various initial deformations near the outer saddle until fragments were separated by more than 30 fm.

- The sum of yrast bandhead spins for 252Cf from Wilson (2021) is 6.85 ℏ for the heavy FF and 6.44 ℏ for the light FF (averaged over all measured FFs)
- Neutron and gamma emission reduces FF spins by approximately 3.5-5 ℏ by the time the FF decay reaches the yrast bandhead (Stetcu, 2021)
- The probability of having angles φ^LH ≥ π/2 is approximately 0.71 (SeaLL1) and 0.72 (SkM*)
- The sum over the triple distribution minus the uncorrelated product distribution gives a value of 0.35 when the geometrical constraint is taken into account

## Context

This work extends the microscopic TDDFT framework previously developed by Bulgac (2016, 2019b, 2020) to the problem of fission fragment spins and orbital angular momentum, which had been studied phenomenologically since the 1960s by Strutinsky (1960), Ericson (1960), Nix (1965), and Moretto (1980). The results contradict the predictions of the FREYA model (Randrup, 2021) and the interpretation of experimental data by Wilson (2021), which assumed fragment spins are uncorrelated or only weakly correlated. The work enables improved phenomenological models by providing microscopic guidance for the spin distributions, and can extend the Hauser-Feshbach analysis of Stetcu (2021) for neutron and gamma emission effects on observed fragment spins.
