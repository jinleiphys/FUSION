# Nonlocal chiral quark models with wavefunction renormalization: sigma properties and π-πscattering parameters

> We analyze the sigma meson mass and width together with the pion-pion scattering parameters in the context of non-local chiral quark models with wave-function renormalization (WFR). We consider both non-local interactions based on the frequently used exponential form factor, and on fits to the mass and renormalization functions obtained in lattice calculations. In the case of the sigma properties we obtain results which are less dependent on the parameterization than in the standard local NJL model, and which are in reasonable agreement with the recently reported empirical values. We also show that the inclusion of the WFR tend to improve the description of the $π-π$ scattering parameters, with the lattice inspired parameterization providing the best overall results. Finally, we analyze the connection of the non-local quark models discussed here with Chiral Perturbation Theory, and present the model predictions for the low energy constants relevant for $π-π$ scattering to O(4) in the chiral expansion.

## Key claim

Nonlocal chiral quark models that include wavefunction renormalization (WFR) yield sigma meson masses and widths that are less dependent on the specific parameterization than the standard local Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model, and the inclusion of WFR tends to improve the description of pion-pion scattering parameters, with the lattice-inspired parameterization providing the best overall results, though the sigma masses obtained are somewhat larger than recently reported empirical values.

## Method

The authors study nonlocal chiral quark models with two light flavors, extending the standard NJL model by including a momentum-dependent wavefunction renormalization in the quark propagator via an additional nonlocal current-current interaction term. They consider two parameterizations: one using exponential form factors for the nonlocal interactions (scenario S1), and another based on fits to lattice QCD results for the quark mass and renormalization functions (scenario S2), plus a third scenario (S3) without WFR for comparison. The model parameters are fixed by fitting to the empirical values of the pion mass (139 MeV) and pion decay constant (92.4 MeV), with the quark condensate and current quark mass determined self-consistently through gap equations, and the sigma meson mass and width are then computed from the corresponding Bethe-Salpeter equations and three-point functions.

## Key numbers

* Sigma meson mass: obtained values are somewhat larger than the empirical values of 478 (+24/-23) ± 17 MeV (Aitala, 2000) and 390 (+60/-36) MeV (Wu, 2001), with the specific numerical results for each scenario given in Table 1 of the paper.
* Sigma meson width: obtained values are compatible with the empirical values of 324 (+42/-40) ± 21 MeV (Aitala, 2000) and 282 (+77/-50) MeV (Wu, 2001), with specific results in Table 1.
* Pion decay constant: fitted to the empirical value of 92.4 MeV in all scenarios.
* Pion mass: fitted to the empirical value of 139 MeV in all scenarios.
* Quark condensate: for scenario S2, the value is -⟨q̄q⟩^(1/3) ≈ 326 MeV, which is about 10% above the empirical upper limit when rescaled to μ = 1 GeV (giving ≈ 284 MeV).
* Current quark mass: for scenario S2, m_c = 2.37 MeV; for scenario S1, m_c = 5.7 MeV.
* Wavefunction renormalization at zero momentum: Z(0) = 0.7 for both S1 and S2 scenarios.
* Pinch point scale: p_pp ≈ 1 GeV for all three scenarios, marking the limit of validity of the model.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of nonlocal chiral quark models (Bowler, 1994; Scarpettini, 2003; Gomez Dumm, 2006; Golli, 1998; Rezaeian, 2004) that neglected wavefunction renormalization, motivated by lattice QCD calculations (Parappilly, 2005; Bowman, 2003; Furui, 2006) showing that such renormalization can be of order 30% or more at zero momentum. The authors also connect their model to Chiral Perturbation Theory (Gasser, 1983) by extracting low-energy constants for π-π scattering to O(4) in the chiral expansion. The results enable improved modeling of low-energy hadron observables using nonlocal interactions that better reflect the momentum dependence seen in lattice QCD, though the sigma meson masses remain somewhat above the empirical values reported by Aitala (2000) and Wu (2001).
