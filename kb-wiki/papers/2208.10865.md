# $P_{cc}^N$ states in a unitarized coupled-channel approach

> Starting from an effective Lagrangian with heavy quark spin symmetry embedded, the coupled-channel dynamics of the doubly charmed systems $D^{(*)} Σ_c^{(*)}$ is investigated. The potential underlying our investigation includes $t$-channel pseudoscalar and vector meson exchanges. A series of $S$-wave bound states with isospin $I=1/2$ is found by applying the first iterated solution of the $N/D$ method: one state with binding energy $23$ MeV in the $5/2^-$ $D^*Σ_c^*$ channel, three states with binding energy $26$, $30$ and $7$ MeV (relative to the thresholds from low to high, respectively) in the $3/2^-$ $DΣ_c^*$-$D^*Σ_c$-$D^*Σ_c^*$ system and three states with binding energy $32$, $8$ and $16$ MeV in the $1/2^-$ $DΣ_c$-$D^*Σ_c$-$D^*Σ_c^*$ system. Those $P_{cc}^N$ states serve as the open-charm partners of the hidden charm pentaquarks $P_ψ^N$ observed by the LHCb Collaboration.

## Key claim

Using a unitarized coupled-channel approach with the first iterated solution of the $N/D$ method to handle unphysical left-hand cuts, the authors predict a series of $S$-wave bound states and resonances in the $D^{(*)}\Sigma_c^{(*)}$ system with isospin $I=1/2$, which serve as open-charm partners of the hidden-charm pentaquarks $P_{\psi}^N$ observed by LHCb. The results depend on a cutoff $\Lambda$ varied between 0.6 and 0.8 GeV, with binding energies showing sensitivity to this parameter, particularly for the $5/2^-$ $D^*\Sigma_c^*$ state.

## Method

The authors constructed $t$-channel one-boson-exchange potentials including pseudoscalar ($\pi$, $\eta$) and vector ($\rho$, $\omega$) meson exchanges, using effective Lagrangians constrained by heavy quark spin symmetry and hidden local symmetry. They solved the Bethe-Salpeter equation in the on-shell factorization approach, but employed the first iterated solution of the $N/D$ method to avoid the unphysical left-hand-cut problem in the partial-wave projected potentials. The loop function was calculated using dimensional regularization with a subtraction constant matched to a Gaussian form factor with cutoff $\Lambda$.

## Key numbers

- $5/2^-$ $D^*\Sigma_c^*$ bound state: binding energy 23 MeV (with cutoff $\Lambda=0.6$ to 0.8 GeV, varying from 23 to 110 MeV)
- $3/2^-$ system: three states with binding energies 26, 30, and 7 MeV relative to the $D\Sigma_c^*$, $D^*\Sigma_c$, and $D^*\Sigma_c^*$ thresholds respectively
- $1/2^-$ system: three states with binding energies 32, 8, and 16 MeV relative to the $D\Sigma_c$, $D^*\Sigma_c$, and $D^*\Sigma_c^*$ thresholds respectively
- $1/2^-$ $D^*\Sigma_c$ resonance: pole at $(4442,4452)+20i$ MeV (real part range, imaginary part averaged)
- $3/2^-$ $D^*\Sigma_c$ resonance: pole at $(4367,4440)+1i$ MeV (real part range, imaginary part averaged)
- $1/2^-$ $D^*\Sigma_c^*$ resonance: pole at $(4490,4508)+10i$ MeV (real part range, imaginary part averaged)
- $3/2^-$ $D^*\Sigma_c^*$ resonance: pole at $(4496,4518)+20i$ MeV (real part range, imaginary part averaged)

## Context

This work extends previous studies of doubly-charmed pentaquark candidates by Chen (2021), Dong (2021), and Chen (2021), who found similar mass spectra using nonrelativistic Schrodinger equations or single-channel approaches with one-boson-exchange potentials. The present calculation improves upon those by employing a unitarized coupled-channel framework with the $N/D$ method to properly treat left-hand cuts, and by including both pseudoscalar and vector meson exchanges. The predicted $P_{cc}^N$ states around 4 GeV are the open-charm analogues of the hidden-charm pentaquarks $P_{\psi}^N(4312)$, $P_{\psi}^N(4440)$, and $P_{\psi}^N(4457)$ observed by LHCb (2019), and their experimental observation would further support the hadronic molecule interpretation of these exotic states.
