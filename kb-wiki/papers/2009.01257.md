# Left-right symmetry and leading contributions to neutrinoless double beta decay

> We study the impact of the mixing (LR mixing) between the standard model $W$ boson and its hypothetical, heavier right-handed parter $W_R$ on the neutrinoless double beta decay ($0νββ$-decay) rate. Our study is done in the minimal left-right symmetric model assuming type-II dominance scenario with charge conjugation as the left-right symmetry. We then show that the $0νββ$-decay rate may be dominated by the contribution proportional to this LR mixing, which at the hadronic level induces the leading-order contribution to the interaction between two pions and two charged leptons. The resulting long-range pion exchange contribution can significantly enhance the decay rate compared to previously considered short-range contributions. Finally, we find that even if future cosmological experiments rule out the inverted hierarchy for neutrino masses, there are still good prospects for a positive signal in the next generation of $0νββ$-decay experiments.

## Key claim

The paper shows that in the minimal left-right symmetric model (mLRSM) with type-II dominance and charge conjugation as the left-right symmetry, the mixing between the Standard Model W boson and its heavier right-handed partner (LR mixing) can produce a long-range pion-exchange contribution to neutrinoless double beta decay that dominates the decay rate, even if future cosmological experiments rule out the inverted neutrino mass hierarchy.

## Method

The authors compute the neutrinoless double beta decay amplitude in the mLRSM by integrating out the W1, W2 bosons and heavy right-handed neutrinos to obtain Wilson coefficients at the electroweak scale, then evolve them via renormalization group equations to a hadronic scale of 2 GeV. They map the resulting four-quark operators onto an effective hadron-lepton Lagrangian using chiral perturbation theory, including the leading-order two-pion term and next-to-next-to-leading order one-pion and four-nucleon terms, with low-energy constants taken from lattice QCD calculations where available.

## Key numbers

- The W_R boson mass is assumed to be M_WR = 7 TeV, satisfying the direct search bound M_WR > 4.4 TeV from CMS
- The W_L-W_R mixing angle ξ is allowed in the range 0 ≤ ξ ≤ 1.25 × 10^{-3} at 95% C.L. from CKM unitarity constraints
- The lightest heavy neutrino mass is taken as m_Nmax = 500 GeV
- The phase space factor for 136Xe is G_0ν^{-1} = 7.11 × 10^{24} eV^2·yr
- The nuclear matrix elements used are M_ν = 2.91, M_0 = -2.64, M_1 = -5.52, M_2 = -4.20, M_NN = -1.53
- The chiral suppression ratios are |A_NNLO/A_LO| ~ 15-20%, with δ_ππ = 0.046, δ_NNπ = 0.042, δ_NN^ν = -0.0096, and δ_NN = 0.063

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrinoless double beta decay in the mLRSM by Tello et al. (2010) and Chakrabortty et al. (2012) and Barry et al. (2013), which found the LR amplitude was suppressed relative to the RR amplitude due to bounds on the W_L-W_R mixing angle. The key advance is the inclusion of long-range pion-exchange contributions, previously discussed by Prezeau et al. (2003), which significantly enhance the LR amplitude and can compensate for the mixing angle suppression. The results show that even if cosmological data from Planck (2018) exclude the inverted hierarchy, ton-scale experiments searching for neutrinoless double beta decay in 136Xe could still observe a signal from TeV-scale new physics.
