# Nuclear Structure in the UCOM Framework: From Realistic Interactions to Collective Excitations

> The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) provides a means for nuclear structure calculations starting from realistic NN potentials. The dominant short-range central and tensor correlations are described explicitly by a unitary transformation. The application of UCOM in the context of the no-core shell model provides insight into the interplay between dominant short-range and residual long-range correlations in the nuclear many-body problem. The use of the correlated interaction within Hartree-Fock, many-body perturbation theory, and Random Phase Approximation gives access to various nuclear structure observables throughout the nuclear chart.

## Key claim

The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) transforms a realistic NN potential into a phase-shift equivalent correlated interaction that can be used in simple many-body approaches, but the range of the tensor correlator must be artificially restricted to account for screening in the many-body system; the resulting binding energies agree with experiment only because of a cancellation between genuine three-body forces and induced three-body contributions from the cluster expansion.

## Method

The authors constructed a unitary correlation operator consisting of a central correlator and a tensor correlator, each defined by Hermitian two-body generators with analytic forms. They applied this operator to the Argonne V18 potential to produce a correlated interaction V_UCOM, then used this interaction in no-core shell model calculations for light nuclei and in Hartree-Fock plus many-body perturbation theory and Random Phase Approximation for nuclei throughout the nuclear chart.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of 4He converged to approximately -28 MeV using the correlated AV18 potential in no-core shell model calculations, compared to unconverged results with the bare potential
- Tjon-line mapping: energies for 3H and 4He using V_UCOM with tensor correlator range I_theta = 0.09 fm^3 fell close to experimental values
- Hartree-Fock plus second-order perturbation theory gave ground state energies in good agreement with experiment from 4He to 208Pb
- Charge radii from HF for heavier nuclei showed deviations up to 1 fm compared to experiment
- Spurious center-of-mass mode in RPA appeared at excitation energies of order 10 keV
- Energy-weighted sum rules in RPA fulfilled with maximum deviations of plus or minus 3 percent

## Context

This work extends the Unitary Correlation Operator Method previously developed by Feldmeier and Neff (1998) and Roth et al. (2004, 2005) from light nuclei to the full nuclear chart. It demonstrates that the correlated interaction V_UCOM can replace the bare realistic potential in Hartree-Fock and RPA calculations, which previously failed with bare interactions. The approach enables systematic studies of ground-state properties and collective excitations throughout the nuclear chart using a single realistic interaction, while identifying the need for effective three-body forces to resolve discrepancies in radii, single-particle spectra, and giant resonance energies.
