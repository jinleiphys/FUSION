# Model-independent approach to eta -> pi+ pi- gamma and eta' -> pi+ pi- gamma

> We present a new, model-independent method to analyze radiative decays of mesons to a vector, isovector pair of pions of invariant mass square below the first significant pion-pion threshold in the vector channel. It is based on a combination of chiral perturbation theory and dispersion theory. This allows for a controlled inclusion of resonance physics without the necessity to involve vector meson dominance explicitly. As an example, the method is applied to an analysis of the reactions eta -> pi+ pi- gamma and eta'->pi+ pi- gamma.

## Key claim

The paper presents a model-independent method for analyzing radiative decays of mesons to a pion pair and a photon, combining chiral perturbation theory with dispersion theory to include resonance physics without explicit vector meson dominance. The method is applied to eta -> pi+ pi- gamma and eta' -> pi+ pi- gamma, with the key condition that the invariant mass squared of the pion pair must lie below the first significant pi-pi threshold in the vector channel (the omega-pi threshold).

## Method

The decay amplitude is factorized into the well-known pion vector form factor F_V(s_pipi), which contains all non-perturbative pi-pi interactions, and a reaction-specific polynomial P(s_pipi) that is expected to be perturbative in chiral perturbation theory. The spectral decay data are fitted using the form dGamma/ds_pipi = |A * P(s_pipi) * F_V(s_pipi)|^2 * Gamma_0(s_pipi), where P(s_pipi) is expanded as a Taylor series 1 + alpha * s_pipi + O(s_pipi^2). In a second step, the extracted parameters A and alpha are matched to the parameters of U(3)-extended chiral perturbation theory at one-loop order.

## Key numbers

- alpha = (1.96 +/- 0.27 +/- 0.02) GeV^{-2} for eta -> pi+ pi- gamma, extracted from WASA-at-COSY data
- alpha' = (1.80 +/- 0.49 +/- 0.04) GeV^{-2} for eta' -> pi+ pi- gamma, extracted from CRYSTAL BARREL data
- delta = -0.22 +/- 0.04 for eta -> pi+ pi- gamma, extracted from the experimental partial width Gamma_exp = (59.8 +/- 3.8) eV
- delta' = -0.40 +/- 0.09 for eta' -> pi+ pi- gamma, extracted from the experimental partial width Gamma_exp = (57.0 +/- 2.8) keV
- a_2^eta = 9.70 +/- 0.7 and a_2^{eta'} = 9.23 +/- 1.4, the low-energy constant extracted from matching to U(3) chiral perturbation theory
- The pion mean square charge radius used in the form factor is <r^2> = 0.437(3) fm^2
- Quadratic terms in the polynomial P(s_pipi) are compatible with zero: beta = (0.21 +/- 0.67 +/- 0.06) GeV^{-4} and beta' = (0.04 +/- 0.36 +/- 0.03) GeV^{-4}

## Context

This work extends earlier efforts to include final state interactions in eta -> pi pi gamma decays, such as the unitarized extensions by Venugopal and Holstein (2001) and the N/D analysis by Ko and Truong (1990), by providing a fully dispersive treatment that avoids the ambiguity of relative strengths between tree-level and resonance contributions inherent in vector meson dominance models. The method enables a controlled extraction of the low-energy constant a_2 from both eta and eta' decays, and the consistency of the two independent determinations supports the assumption that the pion vector form factor captures all non-perturbative effects. The approach can be generalized to other meson decays with pi pi gamma final states where soft bremsstrahlung does not occur.
