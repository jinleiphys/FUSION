# Double-strangeness production in $Λp\to K^+X$ reaction

> We investigate $S=-2$ production from the $Λp\to K^+X$ reactions within the effective Lagrangian approach. The $Λp\to K^+ΛΛ$ and $Λp\to K^+Ξ^-p$ reactions are considered to find the lightest $S=-2$ system, which is $H$-dibaryon. We assume that the $H(2250)\toΛΛ$, and $H(2270)\toΞ^-p$ decays with the intrinsic decay width of 1 MeV. According to our calculations, the total cross-sections for $Λp\to K^+ΛΛ$ and $Λp\to K^+Ξ^-p$ reactions were found to be of the order of a few $μ$b in the $Λ$ beam momentum range of up to 5 GeV$/c$. Furthermore, the direct access of information regarding the interference patterns between the $H$-dibaryon and non-resonant contributions was demonstrated.

## Key claim

The authors calculate that the total cross-sections for the $\Lambda p\to K^+\Lambda\Lambda$ and $\Lambda p\to K^+\Xi^-p$ reactions are of the order of a few microbarns for $\Lambda$ beam momenta up to 5 GeV/$c$, assuming the $H$-dibaryon exists at masses of 2.25 and 2.27 GeV/$c^2$ with an intrinsic decay width of 1 MeV, and that the interference patterns between the $H$-dibaryon and non-resonant contributions can be directly accessed.

## Method

The authors employ an effective Lagrangian approach at tree level, computing Feynman diagrams for the $\Lambda p\to K^+\Lambda\Lambda$ and $\Lambda p\to K^+\Xi^-p$ reactions including $H$-dibaryon-pole contributions with $\Lambda$ and $\Xi$ exchanges, as well as various baryon-pole contributions with $t$-channel meson exchanges ($\sigma$, $\eta$, $\omega$, $\kappa$, $K$, $K^*$). The coupling constants are taken from the Nijmegen soft-core model and the HAL QCD collaboration results, and final-state interactions are included via an on-shell coupled-channel formalism for the $\Lambda\Lambda$, $N\Xi$, and $\Sigma\Sigma$ scattering states.

## Key numbers

* Total cross-sections for both $\Lambda p\to K^+\Lambda\Lambda$ and $\Lambda p\to K^+\Xi^-p$ reactions are of the order of a few $\mu$b for $\Lambda$ beam momenta up to 5 GeV/$c$.
* The $H$-dibaryon production cross-section is a few tens of nanobarn (approximately 40 nb for $H(2250)\to\Lambda\Lambda$ and 38 nb for $H(2270)\to\Xi^-p$).
* The signal-to-background ratio is approximately 0.3 for the $\Lambda\Lambda$ channel and 1.6 for the $\Xi^-p$ channel at $E_\mathrm{cm}=2.8$ GeV.
* The cutoff mass for the form factor is determined to be $\Lambda=435$ MeV by reproducing the $\Xi^-p\to\Lambda\Lambda$ cross-section $\sigma=4.3^{+6.3}_{-2.7}$ from $^{12}\mathrm{C}(\Xi^-,\Lambda\Lambda)X$ data at $p_{\Xi^-}=0.5$ GeV/$c$.
* The $H$-dibaryon masses are assumed at 2.25 GeV/$c^2$ for the $\Lambda\Lambda$ channel and 2.27 GeV/$c^2$ for the $\Xi^-p$ channel, with an intrinsic decay width of 1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends theoretical studies of $S=-2$ baryon-baryon interactions and the $H$-dibaryon search, building on lattice QCD results from the HAL QCD collaboration (Sasaki et al.) which indicated that the $\Lambda\Lambda$ interaction is not sufficiently attractive to generate a bound state near threshold, while the $\Xi N$ phase shift rises sharply. The calculations are motivated by the J-PARC E42 experiment and the availability of $\Lambda$ beams from photoproduction (CLAS, LEPS2) and $\pi^-$-induced reactions, and they enable experimental searches for the $H$-dibaryon via the $(\Lambda,K^+)$ reaction, particularly favoring the $\Xi^-p$ channel due to its higher signal-to-background ratio.
