# Projected Three-Pion Correlation Functions

> We propose a new procedure for constructing projected three-pion correlation functions which reduces undesirable artificial momentum dependences resulting from the commonly used procedure and facilitates comparison of three-pion correlation data with theoretical models.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the standard procedure for projecting three-pion correlation functions onto a single relative momentum variable \(Q_3\) introduces an artificial momentum dependence into the normalized true three-pion correlator \(r_3\), even for a static Gaussian source where the exact unprojected correlator is completely momentum-independent; the authors propose an alternative projection method that minimizes these artefacts.

## Method

The authors analytically compute the projected two- and three-particle correlation functions \(\bar C_2(Q_{ij})\) and \(\bar C_3(Q_3)\) for a simple spherically symmetric, static Gaussian source with no position-momentum correlations, using the standard projection method employed by the STAR and WA98 collaborations. They then derive the projected normalized true three-pion correlator \(\bar r_3(Q_3)\) from these projected functions and compare it to the exact momentum-independent result \(r_3 = 2\). They propose a new projection method that bins the numerator and denominator of the exact expression for \(r_3\) separately in \(Q_3\) before taking the ratio, rather than projecting the two- and three-particle correlation functions individually.

## Key numbers

* For the simple static Gaussian source, the exact reduced three-pion correlator is \(r_3 = 2\), completely momentum-independent.
* The standard projection method yields \(\bar r_3(Q_3) = 2 - \frac{11}{120} \Sigma_3^2 + \mathcal{O}(\Sigma_3^3)\) at small relative momenta, where \(\Sigma_3 = Q_3^2 (R^2 - 1/(4\Delta^2))\) is a dimensionless variable; the leading deviation is of fourth order in the relative momenta.
* For large \(\Sigma_3\), the standard projection gives \(\bar r_3 \to -\frac{3}{2} \sqrt{\pi e^{\Sigma_3} / \Sigma_3}\), growing exponentially negative.
* The new projection method preserves the exact result \(\langle r_3 \rangle = 2\) for the same static Gaussian source.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for three-pion Bose-Einstein correlations developed by Heinz and Zhang (1997) and by Heiselberg and Vischer (1997), which showed that the full momentum dependence of the reduced three-pion correlator can extract source asymmetries and chaoticity. The paper directly addresses the projection procedure used by the STAR Collaboration (2003) in their analysis of 130 A GeV Au+Au collisions at RHIC and by the WA98 Collaboration in Pb+Pb collisions at the SPS. The new method enables more reliable comparison of projected three-pion correlation data with theoretical models, such as those by Nakamura and Seki (1999), and suggests that the observed \(Q_3\) dependence in existing experimental data may be significantly affected by artificial projection effects.
