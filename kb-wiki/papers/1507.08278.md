# Heavy flavor dibaryons

> We study the two-baryon system with two units of charm looking for the possible existence of a loosely bound state resembling the $H$ dibaryon. We make use of a chiral constituent quark model tuned in the description of the baryon and meson spectra as well as the $NN$ interaction. The presence of the heavy quarks makes the interaction simpler than in light baryon systems. We analyze possible quark-Pauli effects that would be present in spin-isospin saturated channels. Our results point to the non-existence of a charmed $H$-like dibaryon, although it may appear as a resonance above the $Λ_cΛ_c$ threshold.

# Heavy flavor dibaryons

## Key claim

The authors find that a charmed H-like dibaryon (a bound state of two charmed baryons with quantum numbers of the ΛcΛc system) does not exist as a bound state, although it may appear as a resonance above the ΛcΛc threshold. This conclusion depends on the absence of strong transition potentials or strong tensor couplings that other models have reported, and on the large mass gap between the ΛcΛc channel and the next coupled channel (ΣcΣc, 338 MeV above).

## Method

The authors use a chiral constituent quark model (CCQM) previously tuned to describe the nucleon-nucleon interaction and the baryon and meson spectra across all flavor sectors. They construct two-baryon wave functions with explicit quark antisymmetrization, derive baryon-baryon potentials from the underlying quark-quark interaction (one-gluon exchange, Goldstone boson exchange, and confinement) using a Born-Oppenheimer approximation, and solve the Lippmann-Schwinger equation for negative energies by evaluating the Fredholm determinant at zero energy to search for bound states.

## Key numbers

- The mass difference between the ΛcΛc threshold and the next coupled channel ΣcΣc in the (T)J^P=(0)0^+ partial wave is 338 MeV, compared to 25 MeV for the analogous NΞ channel in the strange sector.
- The Σc*Σc* channel lies 468 MeV above the ΛcΛc threshold.
- The strong coupling constant αs takes values of approximately 0.54 for light-quark pairs, 0.43 for uc pairs, and 0.29 for cc pairs.
- The constituent quark masses used are m_u,d = 313 MeV and m_c = 1752 MeV.
- The harmonic oscillator parameter for light quarks is b = 0.518 fm and for the charm quark is b_c = 0.6 fm.

## Context

This work extends the study of dibaryon systems from the strange sector (the H dibaryon proposed by Jaffe (1977)) to the charmed sector, motivated by the reduction of kinetic energy due to larger reduced masses. The authors compare their results with the quark delocalization color screening model of Huang et al. (2014) and hadronic potential models of Lee et al. (2011) and Meguro et al. (2011), noting that those models find more attraction due to different treatments of quark-exchange effects and tensor couplings. The results enable future experimental searches at LHC, J-PARC, and RHIC to test whether a charmed dibaryon resonance exists above threshold.
