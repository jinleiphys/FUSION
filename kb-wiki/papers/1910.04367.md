# Daejeon16 NN Interaction

> We have developed a realistic nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction, dubbed Daejeon16. We start from a SRG (similarity renormalization group) evolved chiral N3LO interaction. We then apply PETs (phase-equivalent transformations) to the SRG-evolved interaction. It turned out that the obtained in such a way Daejeon16 NN interaction provides a good description of various observables in light nuclei without NNN forces. In this contribution, we present our new results for some selected nuclei using the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) with the Daejeon16 interaction. One of the interesting results is that the ab initio NCSM with Daejeon16 clearly demonstrates the phenomenon of parity inversion in Be-11, i.e., the ground state in Be-11 has the spin-parity (1/2, +) in experiments contrary to the expectation from the conventional shell model

## Key claim

The Daejeon16 NN interaction, constructed by applying phase-equivalent transformations (PETs) to a similarity renormalization group (SRG) evolved chiral N3LO interaction, reproduces the parity inversion in Be-11 (ground state spin-parity 1/2+) within the ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) without including three-nucleon (NNN) forces, whereas the JISP16 interaction fails to reproduce this inversion.

## Method

The authors start from the Idaho N3LO NN interaction, evolve it via the similarity renormalization group (SRG), and then apply phase-equivalent transformations (PETs) that preserve two-nucleon scattering phase shifts and the deuteron binding energy. The optimal PET parameters are determined by fitting to the binding energies of H-3, He-4, Li-6, He-8, B-10, C-12, and O-16, plus excitation energies of selected narrow states in Li-6, B-10, and C-12. The resulting Daejeon16 interaction is then used in ab initio no-core shell model (NCSM) calculations for light p-shell nuclei, with results extrapolated to the infinite basis space.

## Key numbers

- Ground state energy of Be-11 (1/2+): -65.22(7) MeV, obtained from NCSM with Daejeon16 and extrapolated to infinite basis space
- First excited state of Be-11 (1/2-): -64.63(2) MeV, obtained from NCSM with Daejeon16 and extrapolated to infinite basis space
- The Daejeon16 interaction was fitted to binding energies of nuclei up to O-16, which is the heaviest nucleus used in the fitting process
- For Li-6, the ground-state energy and point-proton root-mean-square radius were predicted using a feed-forward artificial neural network, with near convergence observed at N_max = 70 for energy and N_max = 90 for radius

## Context

This work extends the approach used to develop the phenomenological JISP16 interaction (Shirokov 2005) by starting from a chiral N3LO interaction rather than from inverse scattering. The Daejeon16 interaction enables ab initio NCSM calculations that reproduce the parity inversion in Be-11, a phenomenon that JISP16 cannot capture (Calci 2016 also studied this parity inversion). The results demonstrate that a two-nucleon interaction alone, without three-nucleon forces, can describe key features of light nuclei, and the interaction is being applied to resonance states such as the tetraneutron and He-5 using the single-state harmonic oscillator representation of scattering equations.
