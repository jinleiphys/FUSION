# Missing resonance decays in thermal models

> Detailed information on decay channel probabilities is absent for many high mass resonances, which are typically included in thermal models. In these cases, the sum over all known decay branching probabilities is smaller than 1. Due to this systematic uncertainty of the model, the exact charge conservation may appear to be violated. We estimate the corresponding number of missing charge states in the canonical ensemble formulation of the hadron resonance gas for p+p reactions at the SPS energy $E_{\rm lab}=158$ GeV: $ΔB \simeq 0.16$ for baryon charge, $ΔQ\simeq 0.12$ for electric charge, and $ΔS=-0.01$ for strangeness. The value of the considered effect is 5-8%, which seems to be important enough to include it as a systematic error in the calculations within a hadron gas.

## Key claim

The paper quantifies a systematic uncertainty in hadron-resonance gas (HRG) thermal models: because many high-mass resonances have incomplete decay-branching information, the sum of known branching probabilities is less than 1, leading to an apparent violation of exact charge conservation. The effect is estimated at 5-8% for baryon and electric charge in p+p collisions at SPS energy, and the authors argue it should be included as a systematic error in HRG calculations.

## Method

The authors used the canonical ensemble formulation of the HRG model to fit available 4π multiplicities from p+p reactions at laboratory momentum 158 GeV/c, extracting thermal parameters (temperature T, volume V, and strangeness undersaturation parameter γ_S). They then compared the total charges calculated from the model (using only known decay branching ratios from the Particle Data Tables) with the exactly known initial charges of the p+p system (B=2, Q=2, S=0). The difference quantifies the missing charge due to unaccounted decay channels.

## Key numbers

- ΔB ≃ 0.16 for baryon charge in p+p at Elab = 158 GeV, corresponding to ΔB/B ≃ 8%
- ΔQ ≃ 0.12 for electric charge in p+p at Elab = 158 GeV, corresponding to ΔQ/Q ≃ 6%
- ΔS = -0.01 for strangeness in p+p at Elab = 158 GeV
- The overall effect is estimated at 5-8% of the total charge

## Context

This work extends the standard HRG thermal model approach (Cleymans 2005, Vovchenko 2015) by identifying a systematic uncertainty from incomplete resonance decay tables. It builds on earlier efforts to handle missing branching ratios, such as normalizing all known branching ratios to 100% (Torrieri 2004, Wheaton 2004) or assigning branching ratios by analogy to known states (Andronic 2008). The results enable future HRG calculations to include this 5-8% effect as a systematic error, particularly relevant for p+p and small-system analyses where exact charge conservation is critical.
