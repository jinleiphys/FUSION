# Stellar Weak Interaction Rates and Energy Losses for fp-Shell Nuclei Calculated in the Proton-Neutron Quasiparticle Random-Phase Approximation (II).(A = 61 to 80)

> Weak interaction rates and the associated energy losses for massive nuclei in the fp-shell are calculated in stellar matter using a modified form of proton-neutron quasiparticle RPA model with separable Gamow-Teller forces. A total of 209 nuclei with mass numbers ranging from A = 61 to 80 are considered here for the calculation of weak rates. These also include many neutron-rich nuclei which play a key role in the dynamics of the core collapse calculations. The stellar weak rates are calculated over a wide range of densities (10 < ρY_{e} (gcm^{-3}) < 10^{11}) and temperatures (10^{7} < T(K) 30 x 10^{9}). This is our third paper in a series starting with the calculation of stellar weak rates for nuclei in the sd-shell. The calculated rates take into consideration the latest experimental energy levels and ft value compilations. The effect of particle emission processes is taken into account and the energies and probabilities of these particle emission processes are also calculated in stellar environment. Our results are presented here on an abbreviated scale of temperature and density.

## Key claim

This paper provides calculated stellar weak interaction rates and associated energy losses for 209 fp-shell nuclei with mass numbers A = 61 to 80, using a modified proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with separable Gamow-Teller forces, but the results are presented on an abbreviated scale of temperature and density rather than as a complete tabulation.

## Method

The authors employed a modified form of the proton-neutron quasiparticle random-phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with separable Gamow-Teller forces to calculate weak interaction rates and energy losses for 209 nuclei in the fp-shell. The calculations incorporated the latest experimental energy levels and ft value compilations, and accounted for particle emission processes and their energies and probabilities in stellar environments. The stellar weak rates were computed over a wide range of densities (10 < rho Y_e (g cm^-3) < 10^11) and temperatures (10^7 < T(K) < 30 x 10^9).

## Context

This paper is the third in a series by Nabi and Klapdor-Kleingrothaus (1999) that began with calculations of stellar weak rates for nuclei in the sd-shell, and it extends the pn-QRPA approach to heavier fp-shell nuclei up to A = 80. The inclusion of many neutron-rich nuclei is intended to support core collapse calculations in supernova dynamics. The results enable future studies of presupernova evolution and nucleosynthesis by providing a more complete set of weak interaction rates for the fp-shell region, though the authors note that the rates are presented on an abbreviated scale of temperature and density.
