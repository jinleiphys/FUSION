# Chemical potentials of light flavor quarks from yield ratios of negative to positive particles in Au+Au collisions at RHIC

> The transverse momentum spectra of $π^{-}$, $π^{+}$, $K^{-}$, $K^{+}$, $\bar{p}$, and $p$ produced in Au+Au collisions at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}}=7.7$, 11.5, 19.6, 27, 39, 62.4, 130, and $200$ GeV are analyzed in the framework of a multisource thermal model. The experimental data measured at midrapidity by the STAR Collaboration are fitted by the (two-component) standard distribution. The effective temperature of emission source increases obviously with the increase of the particle mass and the collision energy. At different collision energies, the chemical potentials of up, down, and strange quarks are obtained from the antiparticle to particle yield ratios in given transverse momentum ranges available in experiments. With the increase of logarithmic collision energy, the chemical potentials of light flavor quarks decrease exponentially.

## Key claim

The chemical potentials of up, down, and strange quarks extracted from antiparticle-to-particle yield ratios in Au+Au collisions at RHIC decrease exponentially with increasing logarithmic collision energy, with the specific functional forms given by power-law fits that are valid only for the transverse momentum ranges available in the experimental data.

## Method

The transverse momentum spectra of π⁻, π⁺, K⁻, K⁺, anti-p, and p measured by the STAR Collaboration at midrapidity (|y|<0.1) for eight collision energies from 7.7 to 200 GeV were fitted using a (two-component) standard distribution based on a multisource thermal model. From the fitted yield ratios of π⁻/π⁺, K⁻/K⁺, and anti-p/p, the chemical potentials of up, down, and strange quarks were derived using relations from the statistical thermal model with a parametrized chemical freeze-out temperature T_ch.

## Key numbers

- Chemical potential of up quark: μ_u = (820.1 ± 0.1) × (√s_NN [GeV])^(-0.914 ± 0.025) MeV, with χ²/dof = 1.67/6
- Chemical potential of down quark: μ_d = (681.1 ± 0.1) × (√s_NN [GeV])^(-0.834 ± 0.031) MeV, with χ²/dof = 2.73/6
- Chemical potential of strange quark: μ_s = (420.7 ± 0.2) × (√s_NN [GeV])^(-1.004 ± 0.063) MeV, with χ²/dof = 1.04/6
- The limiting temperature used for T_ch was T_lim = 0.164 GeV, from the empirical expression T_ch = T_lim / [1 + exp(2.60 - ln(√s_NN)/0.45)]
- At the lowest BES energy (7.7 GeV), the difference between chemical potentials of up/down and strange quarks is dozens of MeV; at the highest RHIC energy (200 GeV), these differences are around a few MeV

## Context

This work extends the statistical thermal model approach used by the STAR Collaboration (2014, 2017) and others to extract chemical freeze-out parameters from particle yield ratios, specifically focusing on light flavor quark chemical potentials rather than the baryon chemical potential alone. The results are consistent with the trend of baryon chemical potential (μ_baryon ~ 100-300 MeV at BES energies and ~1 MeV at LHC) obtained by Cleymans et al. (2006) and others. The extracted quark chemical potentials enable mapping of the QCD phase diagram across the RHIC beam energy scan range and suggest a transition from a liquid-like to a gas-like middle state as collision energy increases, though the critical energy for this change is not determined in this work.
