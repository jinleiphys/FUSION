# Precision nucleon-nucleon potential at fifth order in the chiral expansion

> We present a nucleon-nucleon potential at fifth order in chiral effective field theory. We find a substantial improvement in the description of nucleon-nucleon phase shifts as compared to the fourth-order results of Ref. [E. Epelbaum, H. Krebs, U.-G. Meißner, arXiv:1412.0142 [nucl-th]]. This provides clear evidence of the corresponding two-pion exchange contributions with all low-energy constants being determined from pion-nucleon scattering. The fifth-order corrections to nucleon-nucleon observables appear to be of a natural size which confirms the good convergence of the chiral expansion for nuclear forces. Furthermore, the obtained results provide strong support for the novel way of quantifying the theoretical uncertainty due to the truncation of the chiral expansion proposed in Ref. [E. Epelbaum, H. Krebs, U.-G. Meißner, arXiv:1412.0142 [nucl-th]]. Our work opens up new perspectives for precision ab initio calculations in few- and many-nucleon systems and is especially relevant for ongoing efforts towards a quantitative understanding the structure of the three-nucleon force in the framework of chiral effective field theory.

## Key claim

The authors present a nucleon-nucleon (NN) potential at fifth order (N⁴LO) in chiral effective field theory and find a substantial improvement in the description of NN phase shifts compared to the fourth-order (N³LO) results of Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meißner (2014), with the caveat that the improvement is most pronounced for hard cutoff choices (R = 0.8–1.0 fm) and that the three-pion exchange contributions are not included explicitly, assuming their effects can be absorbed into contact interactions.

## Method

The authors construct the NN potential at N⁴LO by including all fifth-order corrections to the static two-pion exchange potential (TPEP) up to the two-loop level, calculated independently using Cutkosky rules and verified against the expressions of Entem et al. (2014). The potential is regularized in r-space using a cutoff function f(r/R) = [1 – exp(–r²/R²)]⁶ with cutoff R chosen in the range 0.8–1.2 fm, while contact interactions are regularized with a nonlocal Gaussian regulator in momentum space with cutoff Λ = 2R⁻¹. The low-energy constants (LECs) for the TPEP are taken from an order Q⁴ fit to πN scattering (Krebs et al., 2012), and the LECs accompanying contact interactions are determined by fitting to neutron-proton (np) and proton-proton (pp) phase shifts and mixing angles from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (Stoks et al., 1993).

## Key numbers

- χ²/datum for np phase shifts (0–200 MeV lab energy) at N⁴LO is 0.3, compared to 0.7 at N³LO, for cutoff R = 0.9 fm.
- χ²/datum for pp phase shifts (0–200 MeV lab energy) at N⁴LO is 0.6, compared to 0.7 at N³LO, for cutoff R = 0.9 fm.
- Deuteron binding energy B_d at N⁴LO is 2.2246 MeV (input), matching the empirical value of 2.224575(9) MeV.
- Deuteron asymptotic S-state normalization A_S at N⁴LO is 0.8844 fm⁻¹/², compared to the empirical value of 0.8846(9) fm⁻¹/².
- Deuteron asymptotic D/S ratio η at N⁴LO is 0.0255, compared to the empirical value of 0.0256(4).
- Deuteron radius r_d at N⁴LO ranges from 1.970 to 1.981 fm for cutoff variation R = 0.8–1.2 fm, compared to the empirical value of 1.97535(85) fm.
- Deuteron quadrupole moment Q at N⁴LO ranges from 0.270 to 0.281 fm² for cutoff variation R = 0.8–1.2 fm, compared to the empirical value of 0.2859(3) fm².
- The breakdown scale Λ_b is estimated as 600 MeV for R = 0.8–1.0 fm, 500 MeV for R = 1.1 fm, and 400 MeV for R = 1.2 fm.

## Context

This work extends the N³LO NN potential of Epelbaum, Krebs, and Meißner (2014) to N⁴LO, providing the first complete NN potential at fifth order in chiral EFT. The results confirm the good convergence of the chiral expansion for nuclear forces and strongly support the novel approach for estimating theoretical uncertainty due to truncation of the chiral expansion proposed in that same prior work. The N⁴LO potential opens new perspectives for precision ab initio calculations in few- and many-nucleon systems and is especially relevant for ongoing efforts to understand the structure of the three-nucleon force in chiral EFT, where large N⁴LO corrections associated with intermediate Δ(1232) excitations (Krebs et al., 2012, 2013; Epelbaum et al., 2014) are expected to be important for resolving long-standing discrepancies in the three-nucleon continuum.
