# Nonlinear Supersymmetry for Spectral Design in Quantum Mechanics

> Nonlinear (Polynomial, N-fold) SUSY approach to preparation of quantum systems with pre-planned spectral properties is reviewed. The full classification of ladder-reducible and irreducible chains of SUSY algebras in one-dimensional QM is given. Possible extensions of SUSY in one dimension are described. They include (no more than) ${\cal N} =2$ extended SUSY with two nilpotent SUSY charges which generate the hidden symmetry acting as a central charge. Embedding stationary quantum systems into a non-stationary SUSY QM is shown to yield new insight on quantum orbits and on spectrum generating algebras.

## Key claim

Nonlinear (Polynomial, N-fold) supersymmetry provides a framework for spectral design in quantum mechanics that goes beyond the standard linear SUSY QM, but the classification of ladder-reducible versus irreducible chains of SUSY algebras reveals that certain higher-order Darboux transformations cannot be decomposed into a sequence of first-order steps without introducing singular intermediate Hamiltonians or breaking positivity conditions.

## Method

The authors review the construction of Polynomial SUSY algebras by assembling chains of first-order Darboux (intertwining) operators into higher-order supercharges, and classify the resulting algebraic structures according to the disposition of polynomial roots relative to the energy spectrum. They analyze the matrix representations of the Hamiltonian on the subspaces of supercharge zero-modes, distinguishing reducible chains (where roots lie at or below the ground state) from irreducible types I (complex conjugate root pairs), II (real roots between adjacent energy levels), and III (confluent degenerate roots with Jordan cell structure). The Strip-off theorem is presented to identify and remove redundant polynomial factors in supercharges.

## Key numbers

* The polynomial closure for N-th order SUSY is given by {Q_N, Q_N^†} = ∏_{l=1}^N (H - λ_l), where the λ_l are the eigenvalues of the Hamiltonian projected onto the N-dimensional zero-mode subspace.
* For second-order SUSY, the polynomial takes the form P_2(H) = (H - λ_1)(H - λ_2), with the confluent case λ_1 = λ_2 producing a Jordan cell matrix S^- = [[λ, 1], [0, λ]].
* The general polynomial closure allows complex conjugate roots: P_N(H) = ∏_{l=1}^n (H - λ_l)^{ν_l} ∏_{j=1}^m [(H + a_j)^2 + d_j]^{μ_j}, with d_j > 0 and N = ∑ ν_l + 2∑ μ_j.
* Type II irreducible blocks involve 2k real roots placed between successive energy levels E_j < λ_l < ... < λ_{l+2k-1} < E_{j+r}, with r-1 ≤ 2k.
* Type III irreducible blocks involve 2ν_l confluent roots: P_{2ν_l,j}^{(III)}(H) = (H - λ_l)^{2ν_l}, with E_j < λ_l ≤ E_{j+1}.

## Context

This review extends the earlier work on linear SUSY QM (Witten, 1981; Cooper et al., 1995) and Parasupersymmetry (Rubakov and Spiridonov, 1988) by providing a complete classification of nonlinear SUSY algebras in one-dimensional quantum mechanics. The classification of irreducible types I, II, and III builds on prior studies of second-order Darboux transformations (Andrianov et al., 1995; Fernandez et al., 2002) and the confluent case (Fernandez et al., 2003). The work enables systematic spectral design, the construction of quantum potentials with pre-planned energy spectra, by clarifying which higher-order intertwining operations are elementary and which can be decomposed into simpler steps.
