# Neutrinos, WMAP, and BBN

> New data from WMAP have appeared, related to both the fractional energy density in relativistic species at decoupling and also the primordial helium abundance, at the same time as other independent observational estimates suggest a higher value of the latter than previously estimated. All the data are consistent with the possibility that the effective number of relativistic species in the radiation gas at the time of Big Bang Nucleosynthesis may exceed the value of 3, as expected from a CP-symmetric population of the known neutrino species. Here we explore the possibility that new neutrino physics accounts for such an excess. We explore different realizations, including neutrino asymmetry and new neutrino species, as well as their combination, and describe how existing constraints on neutrino physics would need to be relaxed as a result of the new data, as well as possible experimental tests of these possibilities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that recent WMAP 7-year data and independent measurements of the primordial helium abundance (Y_p) are consistent with the effective number of relativistic neutrino species (N_eff) exceeding the standard value of 3, but the authors emphasize that the uncertainties in these estimates remain large and the results are only tentative; if confirmed, new neutrino physics could account for the excess.

## Method

The authors performed a model-independent analysis using a modified version of the Kawano/Wagoner BBN code that allows full numerical integration of each weak reaction rate, enabling simultaneous treatment of neutrino asymmetries and extra relativistic degrees of freedom. They calculated Y_p isocontours in the N_eff versus electron neutrino degeneracy parameter (ξ_νe) parameter space, and separately analyzed constraints on sterile neutrino oscillation parameters (Δm²_LSND and sin²2θ_LSND) using results from Chu et al. (2006) combined with the cosmological bound on neutrino mass density from WMAP 7.

## Key numbers

- WMAP 7 measurement: N_eff = 4.34 +0.86 -0.88, about 1.4σ higher than the standard value of 3
- Izotov and Thuan primordial helium mass fraction: Y_p = 0.2565 ± 0.0010 (stat.) ± 0.0050 (syst.)
- New relaxed constraint on electron neutrino asymmetry: -0.14 ≲ ξ_νe ≲ 0.12 (allowing N_eff in the 1σ WMAP7 interval and Y_p in the 3σ Izotov and Thuan interval), compared to the previous constraint of -0.04 ≤ ξ_νe ≤ 0.07 from Serpico (2005)
- For N_eff = 4, the cosmological bound on neutrino mass gives: Δm²_41 ≲ 0.28 eV² and m_4 ≲ 0.53 eV (assuming normal mass hierarchy with m_1 ≃ m_2 ≪ m_3 ≃ 0.05 eV)
- The high Y_p region (Y_p ≥ 0.258) for sterile neutrinos corresponds to N_eff = 3.8 - 4 and extends down to sin²2θ_LSND ∼ 10⁻⁵

## Context

This work extends previous studies of neutrino physics in the early universe by Krauss et al. and others, responding to new WMAP 7-year data (Komatsu et al., 2010) and the higher helium abundance measurement from Izotov and Thuan (2010). The paper revisits constraints that were previously considered to exclude certain sterile neutrino parameters (e.g., from Chu et al., 2006) and demonstrates that the new data reopens parameter space for both neutrino asymmetries and sterile neutrinos. The analysis enables future experimental tests at reactor experiments, neutrino beams, supernova detectors, and cosmological probes like the Planck satellite and South Pole telescope.
