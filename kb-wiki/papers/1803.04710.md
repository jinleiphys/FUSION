# Effect of direct reaction channels on deep sub-barrier fusion in asymmetric systems

> A steeper fall of fusion excitation function, compared to the predictions of coupled-channels models, at energies below the lowest barrier between the reaction partners, is termed as deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance. This phenomenon has been observed in many symmetric and nearly-symmetric systems. Different physical origins of the hindrance have been proposed. This work aims to study the probable effects of direct reactions on deep sub-barrier fusion cross sections. Fusion (evaporation residue) cross sections have been measured for the system $^{19}$F+$^{181}$Ta, from above the barrier down to the energies where fusion hindrance is expected to come into play. Coupled-channels calculation with standard Woods-Saxon potential gives a fair description of the fusion excitation function down to energies $\simeq 14\%$ below the barrier for the present system. This is in contrast with the observation of increasing fusion hindrance in asymmetric reactions induced by increasingly heavier projectiles, \textit{viz.} $^{6,7}$Li, $^{11}$B, $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O. The asymmetric reactions, which have not shown any signature of fusion hindrance within the measured energy range, are found to be induced by projectiles with lower $α$ break-up threshold, compared to the reactions which have shown signatures of fusion hindrance. In addition, most of the $Q$-values for light particles pick-up channels are negative for the reactions which have exhibited strong signatures of fusion hindrance, \textit{viz.} $^{12}$C+$^{198}$Pt and $^{16}$O+$^{204,208}$Pb. Thus, break-up of projectile and particle transfer channels with positive $Q$-values seem to compensate for the hindrance in fusion deep below the barrier. Inclusion of break-up and transfer channels within the framework of coupled-channels calculation would be of interest.

## Key claim

For the asymmetric system \(^{19}\)F+\(^{181}\)Ta, no signature of deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance is observed down to energies \(\simeq 14\%\) below the Coulomb barrier, in contrast to the trend of increasing hindrance with heavier projectiles. The authors propose that the absence of hindrance is due to compensation by projectile break-up and light-particle transfer channels with positive \(Q\)-values, which are specific to this reaction.

## Method

Fusion (evaporation residue) cross sections were measured for \(^{19}\)F+\(^{181}\)Ta using the Heavy Ion Reaction Analyzer (HIRA) at the 15UD Pelletron accelerator at IUAC, New Delhi, over a beam energy range of 73.7–123.8 MeV. Evaporation residues were separated from background using time-of-flight and energy-loss coincidence, and the transmission efficiency of HIRA was calculated with the semi-microscopic Monte Carlo code TERS. The measured excitation function was compared to coupled-channels calculations using the code CCFULL with a standard Woods-Saxon potential, and the logarithmic derivative and astrophysical \(S\)-factor were examined for signs of hindrance.

## Key numbers

- Fusion cross sections were measured for \(^{19}\)F+\(^{181}\)Ta down to \(E_{\text{lab}} = 73.7\) MeV, corresponding to \(E_{\text{c.m.}}/V_{\text{B}} \simeq 0.86\) (approximately 12 MeV below the Coulomb barrier of 77.9 MeV).
- The coupled-channels calculation with Woods-Saxon potential (depth 104.5 MeV, radius 1.12 fm, diffuseness 0.70 fm) reproduces the fusion excitation function down to energies \(\simeq 14\%\) below the barrier.
- The \(\alpha\)-particle break-up threshold for \(^{19}\)F is 4.013 MeV, compared to 7.162 MeV for \(^{16}\)O and 8.665 MeV for \(^{11}\)B.
- For \(^{19}\)F+\(^{181}\)Ta, the \(Q\)-values for light-particle pick-up channels include: \(Q_n = -0.98\) MeV, \(Q_{2n} = 0.48\) MeV, \(Q_p = 6.90\) MeV, \(Q_{2p} = 1.32\) MeV, \(Q_{^2\text{H}} = 6.27\) MeV, \(Q_{^3\text{H}} = 10.53\) MeV, \(Q_{^3\text{He}} = 5.59\) MeV, and \(Q_{^4\text{He}} = 11.99\) MeV.
- For \(^{16}\)O+\(^{208}\)Pb, which shows clear hindrance, the \(Q\)-values are mostly negative: \(Q_n = -3.22\) MeV, \(Q_{2n} = -1.92\) MeV, \(Q_p = -7.40\) MeV, \(Q_{2p} = -10.86\) MeV, \(Q_{^2\text{H}} = -5.11\) MeV, \(Q_{^3\text{H}} = -1.18\) MeV, \(Q_{^3\text{He}} = -5.95\) MeV, and only \(Q_{^4\text{He}} = 5.25\) MeV is positive.

## Context

This work extends the study of deep sub-barrier fusion hindrance to the asymmetric system \(^{19}\)F+\(^{181}\)Ta, contradicting the trend observed in earlier work by Dasgupta (2007) and Shrivastava (2016) that hindrance becomes stronger with increasing projectile mass and charge. The results are compared with systems such as \(^{6,7}\)Li+\(^{198}\)Pt (Shrivastava, 2009, 2016), which also showed no hindrance, and \(^{12}\)C+\(^{198}\)Pt (Shrivastava, 2016) and \(^{16}\)O+\(^{204,208}\)Pb (Dasgupta, 2007), which exhibited clear hindrance. The findings suggest that future coupled-channels calculations should include break-up and transfer channels to reproduce fusion excitation functions deep below the barrier, and that further measurements for more asymmetric systems are needed to confirm the proposed compensation mechanism.
