# Chiral four-body interactions in nuclear matter

> An exploratory study of chiral four-nucleon interactions in nuclear and neutron matter is performed. The leading-order terms arising from pion-exchange in combination with the chiral $4π$-vertex and the chiral NN$3π$-vertex are found to be very small. Their attractive contribution to the energy per particle stays below $0.6\,$MeV in magnitude for densities up to $ρ=0.4\,$fm$^{-3}$. We consider also the four-nucleon interaction induced by pion-exchange and twofold $Δ$-isobar excitation of nucleons. For most of the closed four-loop diagrams the occurring integrals over four Fermi spheres can either be solved analytically or reduced to easily manageable one- or two-parameter integrals. After summing the individually large contributions from 3-ring, 2-ring and 1-ring diagrams of alternating signs, one obtains at nuclear matter saturation density $ρ_0=0.16\,$fm$^{-3}$ a moderate contribution of $2.35\,$MeV to the energy per particle. The curve $\bar E(ρ)$ rises rapidly with density, approximately with the third power of $ρ$. In pure neutron matter the analogous chiral four-body interactions lead, at the same density $ρ_n$, to a repulsive contribution that is about half as strong. The present calculation indicates that long-range multi-nucleon forces, in particular those provided by the strongly coupled $πN Δ$-system with its small mass-gap of $293\,$MeV, can still play an appreciable role for the equation of state of nuclear and neutron matter.

## Key claim

The leading-order chiral four-nucleon interactions from pion-exchange with the chiral 4π-vertex and NN3π-vertex contribute negligibly (below 0.6 MeV) to the energy per particle up to ρ=0.4 fm⁻³, but the Δ-isobar-induced four-body interactions mediated by triple pion-exchange produce a moderate contribution of 2.35 MeV at saturation density that rises rapidly with density, indicating that long-range multi-nucleon forces from the πNΔ-system can still play an appreciable role for the equation of state.

## Method

The author evaluates four-loop in-medium diagrams for homogeneous nuclear and neutron matter, computing contributions to the energy per particle from chiral four-nucleon interactions. The leading-order terms from the chiral 4π-vertex and NN3π-vertex are treated via 2-ring (Hartree) and 1-ring (Fock) diagrams, while the Δ-induced four-nucleon interactions from pion-exchange and twofold Δ-isobar excitation are evaluated through 3-ring, 2-ring, and 1-ring diagrams using a contact-vertex approximation that neglects the energy dependence of the Δ-propagator. The integrals over four Fermi spheres are solved analytically or reduced to one- or two-parameter integrals.

## Key numbers

- Attractive contribution from leading-order chiral four-body interactions stays below 0.6 MeV in magnitude for densities up to ρ=0.4 fm⁻³.
- At nuclear matter saturation density ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³, the summed Δ-induced four-body contribution to the energy per particle is 2.35 MeV.
- The 3-ring Δ-induced diagram alone gives 7.42 MeV at ρ₀=0.16 fm⁻³ and 74 MeV at 2ρ₀=0.32 fm⁻³ for isospin-symmetric nuclear matter.
- In pure neutron matter at the same density ρₙ, the chiral four-body contribution is about half as strong as in nuclear matter.
- The ΔN mass-splitting used is 293 MeV.
- The 1-ring Δ-induced contributions reach 14.4 MeV (nuclear matter) and 21.2 MeV (neutron matter) at 2ρ₀=0.32 fm⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the study of chiral four-body contributions to nuclear matter initiated in Section 5 of Kaiser (2012, ref. [salvatore]), where only 2-ring Hartree diagrams were evaluated and found negligible. It contrasts with the method of unitary transformations used by Epelbaum (refs. [evgeni4na, evgeni4nb]) and the recent neutron matter calculation by the Darmstadt-Ohio group (ref. [achim]), which includes induced four-body forces. The results indicate that Δ-induced long-range four-nucleon interactions are of considerable size and may partially cancel with the relatively large attractive contributions from subleading chiral three-nucleon forces found in that same neutron matter study; quantitative demonstration of such cancellations remains a future task.
