# Beam Energy Dependence of the Linear and Mode-Coupled Flow Harmonics Using the A Multi-Phase Transport Model

> In the framework of the A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model, the multi-particle azimuthal cumulant method is used to calculate the linear and mode-coupled contributions to the quadrangular flow harmonic ($v_{4}$), mode-coupled response coefficient as a function of centrality in Au+Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\mathrm{NN}}}$ = 200, 39, 27 and 19.6 GeV. This study indicated that the linear and mode-coupled contributions to the $v_{4}$ are sensitive to the beam energy change. Nevertheless, the correlations between different order flow symmetry planes and the mode-coupled response coefficients show a weak beam energy dependence. In addition, the presented results suggest that the experimental measurements that span a broad range of beam energies can be an additional constraint for the theoretical models' calculations.

## Key claim

The linear and mode-coupled contributions to the quadrangular flow harmonic v4 are sensitive to beam energy changes in Au+Au collisions, but the correlations between different order flow symmetry planes and the mode-coupled response coefficients show a weak beam energy dependence, suggesting initial-state effects dominate these correlations.

## Method

The A Multi-Phase Transport (AMPT) model (version ampt-v2.26t9b) with string-melting mechanism and hadronic cascade was used to simulate Au+Au collisions at four beam energies. The multi-particle azimuthal cumulant method with two sub-events (separated by |Δη| > 0.7) was applied to particles with pseudorapidity |η| < 1 and transverse momentum 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c, using two-, three-, and four-particle correlations to extract the linear and mode-coupled contributions to v4.

## Key numbers

- Beam energies studied: √sNN = 200, 39, 27, and 19.6 GeV for Au+Au collisions
- Parton-scattering cross section σpp fixed to 1.5 mb
- Pseudorapidity acceptance: |η| < 1; transverse momentum range: 0.2 < pT < 2.0 GeV/c
- The AMPT model overestimates C4,22 for centrality larger than 30% and C5,23 for mid-central (10-50%) region compared to experimental data at 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends prior studies of anisotropic flow as a probe of the Quark-Gluon Plasma's shear viscosity to entropy density ratio (η/s), following the framework of Teaney (2012) and Bhalerao (2014) on linear and mode-coupled flow harmonics. The results enable experimental measurements across a broad range of beam energies to serve as additional constraints for theoretical models, particularly for differentiating between initial-state models and extracting the temperature and baryon chemical potential dependence of η/s, as previously suggested by STAR (2018) and ALICE (2020).
