# Towards a Pion Generalized Parton Distribution Model from Dyson-Schwinger Equations

> We compute the pion quark Generalized Parton Distribution H and quark Double Distributions F and G in a coupled Bethe-Salpeter and Dyson-Schwinger approach in terms of quark flavors or isospin states. We use simple analytic expressions inspired by the numerical resolution of Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations. We explicitly check the support and polynomiality properties, and the behavior under charge conjugation or time invariance of our model. We obtain analytic expressions for the pion Double Distributions and Generalized Parton Distribution at vanishing pion momentum transfer at a low scale. Our model compares very well to experimental pion form factor or Parton Distribution Function data. This paper is the first stage of a GPD-modeling program which will be pursued by incorporating more realistic solutions of the Bethe-Salpeter and Dyson-Schwinger equations.

## Key claim

The authors construct an analytic model of the pion quark Generalized Parton Distribution (GPD) \(H\) and Double Distributions (DDs) \(F\) and \(G\) using simplified algebraic expressions inspired by numerical solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations, rather than solving the full coupled equations; this is presented as the first stage of a program that will later incorporate more realistic solutions.

## Method

The Mellin moments of the pion GPD \(H^u\) are computed from a triangle diagram (Eq. 13) using a Euclidean-space algebraic model for the fully dressed quark propagator \(S(p) = [-i\gamma\cdot p + M]/(p^2 + M^2)\) and a smeared Bethe-Salpeter vertex \(\Gamma_\pi(k,p) = i\gamma_5 (M/f_\pi) M^{2\nu} \int_{-1}^{+1} dz\, \rho_\nu(z) [\Delta_M(k_{+z}^2)]^\nu\) with \(\rho_\nu(z) = R_\nu (1-z^2)^\nu\). The Double Distributions \(F^q\) and \(G^q\) are then identified from the parameterization of the twist-2 quark operator matrix elements (Eq. 12), and the GPD is reconstructed via the Radon transform relation \(H^q(x,\xi,t) = \int_\Omega d\beta d\alpha\, \delta(x-\beta-\alpha\xi)(F^q + \xi G^q)\).

## Key numbers

* The model contains a single dimensionful parameter \(M\) (effective quark mass) and a shape parameter \(\nu\); \(\nu=0\) corresponds to a flat pion distribution amplitude (DA) and \(\nu=1\) to an asymptotic DA.
* The isovector sum rule at \(t=0\) is normalized to \(\int_{-1}^{+1} dx\, H^{I=1}(x,\xi,0) = 2\), enforcing canonical normalization of the Bethe-Salpeter amplitude.
* The support of the Double Distributions is the rhombus \(|\alpha| + |\beta| \leq 1\).
* The pion form factor decomposition yields \(F_\pi(t) = F_\pi^u(t) = -F_\pi^d(t)\) from isospin and charge conjugation constraints.

## Context

This work extends the Dyson-Schwinger/Bethe-Salpeter modeling strategy previously applied to the pion distribution amplitude by Chang et al. (2013) to the case of Generalized Parton Distributions. It contrasts with earlier pion GPD models based on the Radyushkin Double Distribution Ansatz (Amrath et al., 2008), Nambu-Jona-Lasinio approaches (Broniowski et al., 2003, 2007), and light-front calculations with Gaussian or power-law wavefunctions (Choi et al., 2001; Mukherjee and Radyushkin, 2002). The present algebraic model enables analytic checks of polynomiality, support, and discrete symmetry properties, and will be superseded by computations using numerical solutions of the Dyson-Schwinger and Bethe-Salpeter equations.
