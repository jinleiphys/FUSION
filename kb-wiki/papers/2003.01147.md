# Momentum-dependent flow fluctuations as a hydrodynamic response to initial geometry

> We propose a redefinition of the principal component analysis (PCA) of anisotropic flow that makes it more directly connected to fluctuations of the initial geometry of the system. Then, using state-of-the-art hydrodynamic simulations, we make an explicit connection between flow fluctuations and a cumulant expansion of the initial transverse geometry. In particular, we show that the second principal component of elliptic flow is generated by higher-order cumulants, and therefore probes smaller length scales of the initial state. With this information, it will be possible to put new constraints on properties of the early-time dynamics of a heavy-ion collision, including small-scale structure, as well as properties of the quark-gluon plasma.

## Key claim
The paper proposes a redefinition of principal component analysis (PCA) of anisotropic flow that removes sensitivity to particle-number fluctuations, making the subleading principal component of elliptic flow a direct probe of smaller-scale structure in the initial geometry of heavy-ion collisions, specifically generated by higher-order cumulants of the initial transverse energy-density profile.

## Method
The authors redefine the flow covariance matrix by dividing the original PCA observable (which included particle-number fluctuations) by the average product of particle multiplicities at two transverse momenta, \(\langle N(p_{T1}) N(p_{T2})\rangle\). They then perform event-by-event hydrodynamic simulations using the TrENTO+MUSIC+iSS+UrQMD framework with parameters optimized to LHC data, and map the resulting flow harmonics onto a cumulant expansion of the initial transverse geometry, testing which eccentricity terms (such as \(\epsilon_{2,2}\), \(\epsilon_{2,4}\), and nonlinear combinations) reproduce each principal component.

## Key numbers
- The second principal component of elliptic flow is largely sensitive to the subleading eccentricity \(\epsilon_{2,4}\), which probes smaller length scales of the initial state.
- The expansion of elliptic flow \(V_2(p_T)\) includes linear terms up to \(\epsilon_{2,8}\) and nonlinear terms such as \(|\epsilon_{2,2}|^2\epsilon_{2,2}\), \(\epsilon_{4}\epsilon_{2}^*\), and \(\epsilon_{1,3}^2\), with truncation at order \(m=10\) and \(\mathcal{O}(\epsilon^3)\).
- The toy model tests four scenarios: global particle-number fluctuations, event-plane fluctuations, mean transverse momentum fluctuations (\(\overline{p_T} \to \overline{p_T} + \delta \overline{p_T}\)), and combinations thereof.

## Context
This work extends the principal component analysis of anisotropic flow originally proposed by Bhalerao et al. (2014) and measured by the CMS Collaboration (2017), which was sensitive to both flow fluctuations and particle-number fluctuations. The redefined PCA builds on the hydrodynamic response mapping developed by Teaney (2010), Gardim et al. (2011), and Gardim et al. (2014), which connects final flow harmonics to cumulants of the initial transverse geometry. This enables new constraints on early-time dynamics of heavy-ion collisions, including small-scale structure of the initial state and properties of the quark-gluon plasma.
