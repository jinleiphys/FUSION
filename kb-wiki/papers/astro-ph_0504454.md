# Magnetic Fields in High-Density Stellar Matter

> Invited review, conference "Magnetic Fields in the Universe: from Laboratory and Stars to Primordial Structures", Angra dos Reis, Brazil, Nov 28 - Dec 3, 2004, eds. E. M. de Gouveia dal Pino, G. Lugones & A. Lazarian, to be published in the AIP COnference Proceedings. 10 pages, no figures

## Key claim

Magnetic fields in high-density stellar matter can significantly affect combustion processes in neutron stars by quenching Rayleigh-Taylor instabilities in the equatorial direction, leading to asymmetric flame propagation that may enable bipolar gamma-ray burst emission, though the magnetic field's effect on the bulk equation of state becomes negligible at the highest densities.

## Method

The paper reviews the effects of magnetic fields on neutron star structure, composition, superfluidity, and combustion processes, using the virial theorem to estimate maximum field strengths, Landau quantization to analyze equation-of-state modifications, and a fractal model of turbulent deflagration with magnetic-field-modified Rayleigh-Taylor dispersion relations to compute anisotropic flame velocities. The author also examines observational tests of the dipole magnetic field model for pulsars by comparing characteristic ages with supernova remnant ages and braking indices.

## Key numbers

- Maximum magnetic field from virial theorem: \(B_{max} = 1.4 \times 10^{18} \, \mathrm{G}\) for a star of mass \(M/M_\odot\) and radius \(R/10 \, \mathrm{km}\)
- Quantum critical field: \(B_{ce} = 4.41 \times 10^{13} \, \mathrm{G}\)
- Critical density for Landau quantization effects: \(\rho_B = 7.09 \times 10^3 Y_e^{-1} B_{12}^{3/2} \, \mathrm{g \, cm^{-3}}\)
- PSR J1846-0258 characteristic age \(\tau = 700\) yrs while associated SNR Kes75 age \(t_{SNR} > 2000\) yrs
- 1E1207.4-5209 magnetic field from cyclotron lines: \(B \sim 8 \times 10^{10}\) G, while dipole model predicts \(B_\perp = 2-3 \times 10^{12}\) G
- 1E1207.4-5209 characteristic age \(\tau \approx 4.5 \times 10^5\) yrs, associated SNR age \(t_{SNR} < 10^4\) yrs
- Nuclear saturation density: \(\rho_0 = 2.7 \times 10^{14} \, \mathrm{g \, cm^{-3}}\)
- Maximum average fields within stable neutron stars: \(B \sim 1-3 \times 10^{18}\) G
- Total energy released in quark matter transition: \(\sim 10^{53}\) ergs
- Timescale of gamma emission in quark matter transition model: \(\sim 0.2\) s

## Context

This invited review synthesizes work on magnetic fields in neutron stars presented at the 2004 conference "Magnetic Fields in the Universe." It extends earlier analyses of pulsar spin-down by Link and Epstein (1996), Weber and Glendenning (1992), and Allen and Horvath (1997), and discusses contradictions between dipole model predictions and observations such as the braking index discrepancies noted by Lyne (1998). The paper enables future work on anisotropic combustion in neutron star conversions to quark matter, building on prior combustion models by Benvenuto and Lugones (1989) and Lugones (1994, 1999), and connects to gamma-ray burst models by Bombaci (2004) and Ouyed (2005).
