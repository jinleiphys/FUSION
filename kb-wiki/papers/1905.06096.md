# Statistical properties of thermal neutron capture cross section calculated with randomly generated resonance parameters

> We investigated the probability distribution of the thermal neutron capture cross section ($σ_{th}$) deduced stochastically with the resonance parameters randomly sampled from Wigner and Porter-Thomas distributions. We found that the typical probability distribution has an asymmetric shape. While there is a long tail on the large $σ_{th}$ side due to a resonance happening to be close to the thermal energy, the multi-resonance contribution considerably reduces the probability on the small $σ_{th}$ side. We also found that the probability distributions have a similar shape if nuclei have an average resonance spacing sufficiently larger than an average radiation width. We compared the typical probability distribution with the distribution of the experimental values of 193 nuclei, and found a good agreement between them.

# Statistical properties of thermal neutron capture cross section calculated with randomly generated resonance parameters

## Key claim

The probability distribution of the thermal neutron capture cross section ($\sigma_{th}$) derived from stochastically sampled resonance parameters has an asymmetric shape with a long tail toward large cross sections, but this shape is nearly universal across nuclei provided the average resonance spacing $D_0$ is sufficiently larger than the average radiation width $\langle \Gamma_{\gamma 0} \rangle$ (specifically $D_0 / \langle \Gamma_{\gamma 0} \rangle > 100$). The agreement with experimental data from 193 nuclei supports the validity of the stochastic approach, but only for the typical distribution shape, not for individual nucleus predictions.

## Method

The authors calculated $\sigma_{th}$ using the Breit-Wigner formula with resonance parameters randomly sampled from Wigner distributions for resonance energy spacings and Porter-Thomas distributions for reduced neutron widths, using experimental averages of resonance spacing $D_0$, radiation width $\langle \Gamma_{\gamma 0} \rangle$, and strength function $S_0$ from Mughabghab's compilation. They generated $10^7$ samples per nucleus to obtain numerical probability distributions $P_{\sigma}^{M}$, and also derived an analytical expression $P_{\sigma}^{S}$ using a single-resonance approximation that yields a hyperbolic secant distribution. The study examined 193 nuclei from $^{27}$Al to $^{209}$Bi, excluding fissionable nuclei heavier than bismuth.

## Key numbers

- The median of the multi-resonance distribution $\sigma_{md}^{M}$ is approximately 1.8 times the single-resonance median $\sigma_{md}^{S}$ for $I=0$ nuclei, and 1.7 times for $I\neq0$ nuclei, in the region $D_0 / \langle \Gamma_{\gamma 0} \rangle > 100$.
- The dispersion $V$ (defined on $\log \sigma_{th}$) converges to approximately 0.8 for nuclei with $D_0 / \langle \Gamma_{\gamma 0} \rangle > 100$.
- For $^{120}$Sn, the single-resonance median $\sigma_{md}^{S}$ is 0.01 b.
- $^{152}$Eu has the smallest $D_0 / \langle \Gamma_{\gamma 0} \rangle$ ratio among the 193 nuclei studied, at 1.56 ($D_0 = 0.25$ eV, $\langle \Gamma_{\gamma 0} \rangle = 0.16$ eV).
- $^{135}$Xe has an experimental $\sigma_{th}$ of $2.6 \times 10^6$ b and $^{157}$Gd has $2.5 \times 10^5$ b, cited as examples of extremely large cross sections that systematics cannot predict.
- The variation of $V$ from statistical fluctuation was less than 0.5% with $10^7$ trials.
- Using $N=100$ resonances instead of $N=50$ changed $V$ by less than 1% for $^{120}$Sn.

## Context

This work extends the stochastic approach of Rochman et al. (2005) for generating resonance parameters from $\chi^2$ and Wigner distributions, and connects to the width fluctuation treatment of Moldauer (1975). The authors show that the probability distribution of $\sigma_{th}$ has a universal shape for most stable nuclei, enabling estimation of uncertainty when applying the stochastic method to nuclei with no experimental data. The analytical single-resonance approximation provides a simple formula (hyperbolic secant distribution) that captures the essential shape, while the multi-resonance contribution is shown to suppress the probability of extremely small cross sections. This enables future application to unstable nuclei where experimental data are scarce, particularly for transmutation system evaluations.
