# The Time-Delay Signature of Quark-Gluon-Plasma Formation in Relativistic Nuclear Collisions

> The hydrodynamic expansion of quark-gluon plasmas with spherical and longitudinally boost-invariant geometries is studied as a function of the initial energy density. The sensitivity of the collective flow pattern to uncertainties in the nuclear matter equation of state is explored. We concentrate on the effect of a possible finite width, $ΔT \sim 0.1 T_c$, of the transition region between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phase. Although slow deflagration solutions that act to stall the expansion do not exist for $ΔT > 0.08 T_c$, we find, nevertheless, that the equation of state remains sufficiently soft in the transition region to delay the propagation of ordinary rarefaction waves for a considerable time. We compute the dependence of the pion-interferometry correlation function on $ΔT$, since this is the most promising observable for time-delayed expansion. The signature of time delay, proposed by Pratt and Bertsch, is an enhancement of the ratio of the inverse width of the pion correlation function in out-direction to that in side-direction. One of our main results is that this generic signature of quark-gluon plasma formation is rather robust to the uncertainties in the width of the transition region. Furthermore, for longitudinal boost-invariant geometries, the signal is likely to be maximized around RHIC energies, $\sqrt{s} \sim 200$ AGeV.

## Key claim

The time-delay signature of quark-gluon plasma formation, proposed by Pratt and Bertsch as an enhancement of the ratio of inverse widths of pion correlation functions in out-direction to side-direction, is robust to uncertainties in the width of the transition region between quark-gluon plasma and hadronic phase, holding for a finite width up to ΔT = 0.1 Tc even though slow deflagration solutions that stall expansion do not exist for ΔT > 0.08 Tc.

## Method

The authors solved relativistic ideal hydrodynamics for spherical (fireball) and longitudinally boost-invariant (Bjorken cylinder) geometries using Sod's operator splitting method with a relativistic HLLE scheme, employing a parametrized equation of state that approximates lattice QCD data with variable transition width ΔT and ratio of degrees of freedom dQ/dH. They computed the time evolution of temperature and energy density profiles for different initial energy densities and transition widths, then calculated two-pion correlation functions to extract the out- and side-direction inverse widths R_out and R_side.

## Key numbers

- The critical temperature is Tc ~ 160 MeV, with the transition width known to be in the range 0 ≤ ΔT < 0.1 Tc ~ 16 MeV
- For ΔT < ΔT* ≃ 0.07676 Tc, the expansion solution changes from a rarefaction wave to a rarefaction discontinuity (deflagration front)
- For dQ/dH = 37/3 with ΔT = 0, the latent heat is 4B = 1.7 Tc sc ≃ 1.272 GeV fm^-3
- For dQ/dH = 3, the latent heat is reduced to εQ - εH = Tc sc, corresponding to about 400 MeV fm^-3 in physical units
- For the Bjorken cylinder geometry with ΔT = 0 and dQ/dH = 37/3, the freeze-out time is prolonged by a factor [dQ/dH]^(1/4) ≈ 1.87 (87% delay) relative to ideal gas; for dQ/dH = 3 the delay is about 32%
- The signal is likely to be maximized around RHIC energies, √s ~ 200 AGeV
- For the fireball geometry with ε0 = 1.875 Tc sc (near εQ) and ΔT = 0, the mixed phase lifetime is about 18 R (where R is the initial radius)
- For the Bjorken cylinder with ε0 = 18.75 Tc sc ~ 10 εQ and τ0 = 0.1 R, a transverse deflagration front forms for ΔT = 0, with the system entering the mixed phase at τQ ~ 3 fm (for τ0 = 0.5 fm)

## Context

This work extends the one-dimensional Landau expansion studies of Rischke and Gyulassy (1995) to more realistic three-dimensional geometries (fireball and Bjorken cylinder), and builds on the time-delay signature first proposed by Pratt and by Bertsch et al. for sharp first-order transitions (ΔT = 0). The authors show that the signature remains robust for a rapid cross-over transition with finite width up to ΔT = 0.1 Tc, and that the maximum time delay may occur at AGS or RHIC energies depending on whether matter is initially stopped in a fireball or expands as in Bjorken's longitudinally boost-invariant scenario. This enables experimental tests of the QCD equation of state via pion interferometry at RHIC.
