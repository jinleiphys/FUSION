# General theory of constructing potential with bound states in the continuum

> We present a general theory of potentials that support bound states at positive energies (bound states in the continuum). On the theoretical side, we prove that, for systems described by nonlocal potentials of the form $V(r,r')$, bound states at positive energies are as common as those at negative energies. At the same time, we show that a local potential of the form $V(r)$ rarely supports a positive energy bound state. On the practical side, we show how to construct a (naturally nonlocal) potential which supports an arbitrary normalizable state at an arbitrary positive energy. We demonstrate our theory with numerical examples both in momentum and coordinate spaces with emphasis on the important role played by nonlocal potentials. Finally, we discuss how to observe bound states at positive energies, and where to search for nonlocal potentials which may support them.

## Key claim

The paper proves that bound states in the continuum (BICs) are as common as negative-energy bound states for systems described by nonlocal potentials of the form $V(r,r')$, while local potentials of the form $V(r)$ can rarely support a BIC. The authors present a general solution to the inverse problem for an arbitrary BIC, but the practical construction requires nonlocal potentials.

## Method

The authors use the SB-decomposition of a Hermitian potential $V = V_{\rm S} + V_{\rm B}$, where $V_{\rm S}$ operates on scattering states and $V_{\rm B}$ operates on bound states. They construct a BIC-supporting potential by starting with an arbitrary Hermitian potential $V_0$ that has a bound state at negative energy, then applying an energy-shift transformation $\Delta V_{\rm B} = (K^2 + \gamma_0^2) |\phi\rangle\langle\phi|$ to shift that bound state to a positive energy $K^2$ while leaving all eigenstates unchanged. The method is demonstrated numerically in momentum space for an S-wave Gaussian potential.

## Key numbers

* The Gaussian potential $V_0(r) = \lambda \exp(-r^2/b^2)$ with $\lambda = -30.0 \,{\rm fm}^{-2}$ and $b = 0.5 \,{\rm fm}$ supports a single bound state at $-\gamma_0^2 = -5.373\, {\rm fm}^{-2}$ (equivalent to $-222.8\, {\rm MeV}$).
* The unit system uses $\hbar = 2m = 1$, giving $1 = 41.47\,{\rm MeV\,fm}^2$ (proton-neutron reduced mass).
* The bound state wave function $\phi(k)$ has a momentum range approximately $\sim 1/b = 2.0 \,{\rm fm}^{-1}$.

## Context

This work extends the von Neumann and Wigner (1929) pioneering study of BICs and the SB-decomposition framework of Takayanagi (2023). The authors contrast their general nonlocal potential approach with earlier theoretical treatments that described BICs as resonances with vanishing width occurring only accidentally (Stillinger, 1975; Pursey, 1995; Weber, 1998). The paper enables systematic construction of potentials supporting BICs at arbitrary positive energies, and provides theoretical guidance for searching BICs in physical systems with nonlocal potentials, such as those described by the Hartree-Fock Fock potential.
