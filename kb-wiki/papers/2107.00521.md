# Eccentricities, fluctuations and A-dependence of elliptic and triangular flows in heavy-ion collisions

> A simple geometrical model with event-by-event fluctuations is suggested to study elliptical and triangular eccentricities in the initial state of relativistic heavy-ion collisions. This model describes rather well the ALICE and ATLAS data for Pb+Pb collisions at center-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 5.02$~TeV per nucleon pair, assuming that the second, $v_2$, and third, $v_3$, harmonics of the anisotropic flow are simply linearly proportional to the eccentricities $\varepsilon_2$ and $\varepsilon_3$, respectively. We show that the eccentricity $\varepsilon_3$ has a pure fluctuation origin and is substantially dependent on the size of the overlap area only, while the eccentricity $\varepsilon_2$ is mainly related to the average collision geometry. Elliptic flow, therefore, is weakly dependent on the event-by-event fluctuations everywhere except of the very central collisions 0--2%, whereas triangular flow is mostly determined by the fluctuations. The scaling dependence of the magnitude of the flow harmonics on atomic number, $v_n \propto A^{-1/3}$, is predicted for this centrality interval.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a simple geometrical model with event-by-event fluctuations can describe ALICE and ATLAS data for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but only under the assumption that the second and third flow harmonics are linearly proportional to the corresponding eccentricities; the model predicts an A^{-1/3} scaling of flow harmonics in very central collisions (0-2%), though this scaling is only partially confirmed by existing data.

## Method

The authors compare three Monte Carlo approaches: a hard sphere model with uniformly distributed nucleons, the Magma model based on Color Glass Condensate energy density deposition, and an improved Monte Carlo Glauber model with separated transverse profiles for neutrons and protons and hard-core repulsion. Eccentricities ε₂ and ε₃ are calculated from the transverse overlap geometry using different weighting schemes (binary collisions, energy density, or number of participants), and flow harmonics v₂ and v₃ are obtained via linear response proportionality coefficients k₂=0.321 and k₃=0.314 fitted to ATLAS data.

## Key numbers

- Saturation momentum at nucleus center: Q_{s0} = 1.24 GeV, used in Magma model calculations
- Proportionality coefficients: k₂ = 0.321 and k₃ = 0.314, adjusted to ATLAS data for Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 5.02 TeV
- Inelastic NN cross section: σ_NN = 67.6 mb, used in Monte Carlo Glauber model
- Triangular flow fluctuation formula: v₃(C) = K₃ / √(1 - C^{1/2}) with K₃ = 0.0183, describing data for centralities below 50%
- Predicted scaling factor for Xe+Xe relative to Pb+Pb in central collisions: 1.17 (from (208/A)^{1/3} with A=129)
- Predicted scaling factor for Cu+Cu relative to Pb+Pb in central collisions: 1.5 (from (208/A)^{1/3} with A=63)
- Observed v₃ enhancement in Xe+Xe at 3-7% centrality: factor 1.17, in agreement with prediction
- Observed v₂ enhancement in Xe+Xe at 3-7% centrality: factor 1.35, larger than the predicted 1.17

## Context

This work extends the geometrical fluctuation approach of Alver and Roland (2010) who first showed that fluctuations in nucleon positions cause triangular anisotropy, and builds on the Magma model of Gelis and collaborators which successfully described ATLAS v₂ and v₃ data. The paper addresses the nontrivial centrality dependence of the v₂/v₃ ratio that is typically missed in hydrodynamic calculations (Alba et al., 2017) and in the HYDJET++ model (Lokhtin et al., 2008; Bravina et al., 2013). The predicted A^{-1/3} scaling for central collisions provides a testable benchmark for future measurements at NICA and FAIR, and the calculated eccentricities can serve as input to phenomenological models to improve description of flow azimuthal characteristics.
