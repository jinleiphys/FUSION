# The Phase Diagram of QC2D from Functional Methods

> We study the phase diagram of two-color Quantum Chromodynamics at finite temperature and chemical potential. This is done within an effective low-energy description in terms of quarks, mesons and diquarks. Quantum, thermal and density fluctuations are taken into account with the functional renormalisation group approach. In particular, we establish the phenomenon of pre-condensation, affecting the location of the phase boundary to Bose-Einstein condensation. We also discuss the Silver Blaze property in the context of the functional renormalisation group.

## Key claim

The paper establishes that including non-perturbative corrections to the classical dispersion relations of quarks, mesons, and diquarks via running wave function renormalisations and running Yukawa couplings significantly modifies the phase diagram of two-color QCD compared to earlier functional renormalisation group studies, particularly affecting the location of the phase boundary to Bose-Einstein condensation through the phenomenon of pre-condensation.

## Method

The authors employ the functional renormalisation group (FRG) approach to the quark-meson-diquark (QMD) model, an effective low-energy description of two-color QCD valid at momentum scales below approximately 1 GeV. They extend previous truncation schemes by including running wave function renormalisations for quarks, mesons, and diquarks, as well as running Yukawa couplings for quark-meson and quark-diquark interactions, and solve the resulting flow equations for the effective potential and the scale-dependent couplings.

## Key numbers

* The ultraviolet cutoff of the effective model is chosen as Lambda approximately 1 GeV, corresponding to the mass gap of the gluon propagator in Landau gauge.
* The onset chemical potential for finite density at vanishing temperature is given by mu_c = m_Delta_pol / 2, where m_Delta_pol is the pole mass of the diquark, the lowest-lying state with non-vanishing baryon number.
* The effective potential is expanded in two invariants: rho_phi = (Z_phi/2)(pi^2 + sigma^2) for the chiral symmetry and rho_Delta = (Z_Delta/2)(Delta_1^2 + Delta_2^2) for the baryon number symmetry.

## Context

This work extends the FRG phase diagram for two-color QCD previously established by Strodthoff et al. (2011, 2013) by including non-perturbative corrections to dispersion relations and running couplings, which were shown to play a large quantitative role in QCD and in the non-relativistic BCS-BEC crossover. The study also provides a proof of the Silver Blaze property within the FRG framework and establishes the basic requirements that approximations of n-point functions must fulfil to maintain this property, enabling more reliable continuum studies of dense QCD-like theories.
