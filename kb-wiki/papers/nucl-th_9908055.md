# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering and Effective Field Theory: Including Pions Non-perturbatively

> Next to leading order effective field theory calculations are performed for $ {}^1S_0$ NN scattering using subtractive renormalization procedure. One pion exchange and contact interaction potentials are iterated using Lippman-Schwinger equation. Satisfactory fit to the Nijmegen data is obtained for the momenta up to 300 MeV in the centre of mass frame. Phase shifts are also compared with the results of KSW approach where pions are included perturbatively.

# Nucleon-Nucleon Scattering and Effective Field Theory: Including Pions Non-perturbatively

## Key claim
Next-to-leading order effective field theory calculations for \( {}^1S_0 \) nucleon-nucleon scattering, using subtractive renormalization with non-perturbative one-pion exchange, yield a satisfactory fit to Nijmegen phase shift data up to 300 MeV centre-of-mass momentum, but only when pions are included non-perturbatively; perturbative inclusion of pions (as in the KSW approach) produces negative phase shifts, questioning the consistency of that expansion.

## Method
The leading-order potential (contact interaction plus one-pion exchange) and a next-to-leading order momentum-dependent contact term were iterated via the Lippmann-Schwinger equation. Divergences were handled by introducing a cut-off regularization, then performing subtractive renormalization at subtraction points \(\mu = 240\) MeV and \(\nu = 800\) MeV. The coupling constants \(C\) and \(C_2\) were fitted to reproduce low-energy phase shifts, and the resulting phase shifts were compared to Nijmegen data and to results from the perturbative-pion KSW approach.

## Key numbers
- Fit to Nijmegen phase shift data was satisfactory for centre-of-mass momenta up to **300 MeV**.
- Leading-order contact coupling constant: \(C = -1/(17348\ \text{MeV}^2)\).
- Next-to-leading order coupling constant: \(C_2 = 1/(8.5 \times 10^9\ \text{MeV}^4)\).
- Subtraction points: \(\mu = 240\ \text{MeV}\) and \(\nu = 800\ \text{MeV}\).
- Cut-off values tested for cut-off independence: \(\Lambda = 1\times 10^5,\ 2\times 10^5,\ 3\times 10^5,\ 4\times 10^5\ \text{MeV}\).

## Context
This work extends the Weinberg power-counting approach to nucleon-nucleon scattering by applying subtractive renormalization, following earlier cut-off EFT work by Lepage (1997) and Ordonez (1996). It directly compares with the KSW approach of Kaplan, Savage, and Wise (1998), where pions are treated perturbatively, and finds that perturbative pions yield negative phase shifts, supporting the criticism by Gegelia (1999) that perturbative inclusion of pions in EFT for NN scattering is questionable. The results enable further development of non-perturbative chiral EFT treatments of nuclear forces.
