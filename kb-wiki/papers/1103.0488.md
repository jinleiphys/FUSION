# Augmentation of nucleon-nucleus scattering by information entropy

> Quantum information entropy is calculated from the nucleon nucleus forward scattering amplitudes. Using a representative set of nuclei, from $^4$He to $^{208}$Pb, and energies, $T_{lab} < 1$\,[GeV], we establish a linear dependence of quantum information entropy as functions of logarithm nuclear mass $A$ and logarithm projectile energy $T_{lab}$.

## Key claim
The paper establishes a linear dependence of quantum information entropy (IE) on the logarithm of target mass number A and the logarithm of projectile kinetic energy T_lab for nucleon-nucleus scattering, but this linearity is an approximation based on a uniform probability distribution for partial wave amplitudes and shows deviations of a few percent due to shell effects and target spectroscopy.

## Method
The authors calculated quantum information entropy from nucleon-nucleus forward scattering amplitudes using microscopic optical model potentials based on the Argonne AV18 NN potential, generalized above pion production threshold. They computed partial-wave elastic and reaction cross sections for neutron and proton scattering on a set of nuclei from ⁴He to ²⁰⁸Pb at energies 20 < T_lab < 1000 MeV, then normalized the partial-wave cross sections by the total cross section to obtain probabilities and evaluated the Shannon information entropy S = -Σ[p_l^(e) ln p_l^(e) + p_l^(r) ln p_l^(r)]. A χ² fit was performed to determine the coefficients a, b, c in the linear relation S = a + b ln T + c ln A.

## Key numbers
- The uniform model predicts coefficients a = -1.25 ± 0.25, b = 1/2, c = 1/3
- For nA scattering at 20-1000 MeV: a = -1.0796, b = 0.7168, c = 0.3787
- For pA scattering at 20-1000 MeV: a = -1.1866, b = 0.7412, c = 0.3634
- For nA scattering at 50-500 MeV: a = -1.1267, b = 0.7348, c = 0.3598
- For pA scattering at 50-500 MeV: a = -1.2638, b = 0.7638, c = 0.3485
- The grazing radius parameterization uses r_0 = 1.15 fm and a = 0.55 fm for the Fermi distribution
- The study covers targets: ⁴He, ¹²C, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca, ⁵⁸Ni, ⁹⁰Zr, and ²⁰⁸Pb

## Context
This work extends prior studies of Shannon information entropy in nuclear systems by Massen and collaborators, who previously applied IE to nuclei, atomic clusters, and boson traps. The paper connects nuclear reaction phenomenology with quantum information theory, using microscopic optical potentials from the Argonne AV18 NN potential (Wiringa, 1995) and scattering codes developed by Raynal (ECIS). The results enable future studies of shell effects in information entropy, as demonstrated by the authors' analysis of isotope chains at T_lab = 400 MeV showing sensitivity to magic numbers (N=8, 20, 28, 50, 82, 126).
