# Weak Field Collapse in AdS: Introducing a Charge Density

> We study the effect of a non-vanishing chemical potential on the thermalization time of a strongly coupled large $N_c$ gauge theory in $(2+1)$-dimensions, using a specific bottom-up gravity model in asymptotically AdS space. We first construct a perturbative solution to the gravity-equations, which dynamically interpolates between two AdS black hole backgrounds with different temperatures and chemical potentials, in a perturbative expansion of a bulk neutral scalar field. In the dual field theory, this corresponds to a quench dynamics by a marginal operator, where the corresponding coupling serves as the small parameter in which the perturbation is carried out. The evolution of non-local observables, such as the entanglement entropy, suggests that thermalization time decreases with increasing chemical potential. We also comment on the validity of our perturbative analysis.

## Key claim

The paper claims that, contrary to naive expectations from integrable systems, introducing a small chemical potential (mu/T << 1) in a strongly coupled large N_c gauge theory speeds up thermalization rather than slowing it down, based on a perturbative holographic analysis of a quench driven by a marginal operator.

## Method

The authors construct a perturbative solution to the Einstein-Maxwell-dilaton equations in asymptotically AdS4 space, expanding in the small amplitude epsilon of a neutral scalar field whose boundary value acts as a time-dependent source for a marginal operator. The initial state is an AdS-Reissner-Nordstrom black hole with small mass and charge, and the perturbative solution dynamically interpolates between this initial state and a final black hole with different temperature and chemical potential. The evolution of non-local observables, specifically entanglement entropy, is then used to extract the thermalization time as a function of the chemical potential.

## Key numbers

* The perturbative expansion parameter epsilon is related to the quench parameters as epsilon = kappa_quench^(1/2), where kappa_quench = (Delta E)(delta t)^3 and Delta E is the injected energy, delta t is the quench duration.
* The perturbative solution is valid up to a time scale t_pert = O(delta t / epsilon^(2/3)), which can be made parametrically larger than the pulse duration by taking epsilon << 1.
* The initial state is characterized by kappa_mu^(initial) = mu/T << 1 and kappa_E^(initial) ~ O(1), while the final state has kappa_mu^(final) ~ kappa_mu^(initial) << 1 and kappa_E^(final) ~ O(1).
* The initial black hole mass and charge are written as M = epsilon_1^2 m and Q = epsilon_1^2 q with epsilon_1 = kappa_mu^3, where m and q are O(1).

## Context

This work extends the weak-field collapse construction of Bhattacharyya (2009), which validated the AdS-Vaidya phenomenology from first-principles gravity for neutral black hole formation, to include a conserved U(1) charge. It generalizes the earlier holographic studies of thermalization in AdS-Vaidya backgrounds by Caceres (2012) and Galante (2012) to a setup with a chemical potential, providing analytical control in the small chemical potential regime. The results enable future studies of how finite density affects thermalization in strongly coupled systems, potentially relevant for understanding the quark-gluon plasma at RHIC and LHC where the baryon chemical potential is small, and suggest that the scaling of thermalization time may separate into two qualitatively different regimes: mu/T << 1 and mu/T >> 1.
