# Leading Order Calculation of Shear Viscosity in Hot Quantum Electrodynamics from Diagrammatic Methods

> We compute the shear viscosity at leading order in hot Quantum Electrodynamics. Starting from the Kubo relation for shear viscosity, we use diagrammatic methods to write down the appropriate integral equations for bosonic and fermionic effective vertices. We also show how Ward identities can be used to put constraints on these integral equations. One of our main results is an equation relating the kernels of the integral equations with functional derivatives of the full self-energy; it is similar to what is obtained with two-particle-irreducible effective action methods. However, since we use Ward identities as our starting point, gauge invariance is preserved. Using these constraints obtained from Ward identities and also power counting arguments, we select the necessary diagrams that must be resummed at leading order. This includes all non-collinear (corresponding to 2 to 2 scatterings) and collinear (corresponding to 1+N to 2+N collinear scatterings) rungs responsible for the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect. We also show the equivalence between our integral equations obtained from quantum field theory and the linearized Boltzmann equations of Arnold, Moore and Yaffe obtained using effective kinetic theory.

## Key claim

The paper computes the shear viscosity at leading order in hot Quantum Electrodynamics using purely diagrammatic methods, showing equivalence with the kinetic theory results of Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (2003). The authors claim that their approach preserves gauge invariance by using Ward identities as the starting point, unlike previous two-particle-irreducible effective action methods.

## Method

Starting from the Kubo relation for shear viscosity, the authors write coupled integral equations for bosonic and fermionic effective vertices that resum an infinite number of ladder diagrams. They use Ward identities to constrain the kernels of these integral equations, relating them to functional derivatives of the full self-energy. Power counting arguments are applied to select which diagrams must be resummed at leading order, including both non-collinear (2 to 2 scatterings) and collinear (1+N to 2+N scatterings) rungs responsible for the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect.

## Key numbers

- Shear viscosity in hot QED parametrically scales as eta = C * T^3 / (e^4 * ln(e^{-1})), where T is temperature, e is the electromagnetic coupling constant, and C is a numerical coefficient obtainable from detailed analysis (Arnold, Moore and Yaffe 2000, 2003a, 2003b)
- The shear viscosity diverges as eta ~ integral dp^0 G(p)G(p) ~ 1/epsilon due to pinch singularities, where epsilon is the usual infinitesimal i epsilon prescription
- Collinear singularities appear when the angle between quasiparticles is parametrically small, theta_{pq} ~ O(e), causing the expression to diverge as 1/theta_{pq}^2

## Context

This work extends the diagrammatic calculation of transport coefficients from scalar theory (Jeon 1995) to gauge theories, where collinear singularities and the Landau-Pomeranchuk-Migdal effect require additional resummation beyond the pinch singularity resummation needed in scalar theories. The paper provides a first-principles quantum field theory verification of the kinetic theory results of Arnold, Moore and Yaffe (2003a, 2003b), which are often quoted in the heavy ion community. A similar calculation for electric conductivity was performed by Gagnon and Jeon (2007).
