# Large Q2 electrodisintegration of the deuteron

> The break up of the deuteron is studied at high $Q^2$ kinematics, with main motivation to probe the deuteron at small internucleon distances. For this, two main issues are studied: electromagnetic interaction of the virtual photon with the bound nucleon and the strong interactions of the produced baryons in the final state of the reaction. Within virtual nucleon approximation we developed a new prescription to account for the bound nucleon effects in the electromagnetic interaction. The final state interaction at high $Q^2$ kinematics is calculated within generalized eikonal approximation ~ (GEA). Comparison with the first experimental data confirm GEA's early prediction that the rescattering is maximal at $\sim 70^0$ of recoil nucleon production relative to the momentum of the virtual photon. Also the forward recoil nucleon angles are best suited for studies of the deuteron at small distances.

## Key claim

The paper develops a theoretical model for deuteron electrodisintegration at high momentum transfer that accounts for both bound-nucleon effects in the electromagnetic interaction and final-state interactions, and confirms that the maximum of final-state rescattering occurs at recoil nucleon angles of approximately 70 degrees relative to the virtual photon momentum, contradicting the conventional Glauber theory prediction of 90 degrees.

## Method

The calculation uses the virtual nucleon approximation, treating the struck nucleon as an off-shell particle, and the generalized eikonal approximation (GEA) to compute final-state interactions of the two outgoing nucleons. The plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) amplitude includes both on-shell and off-shell contributions to the nucleon electromagnetic current, ensuring gauge invariance. Forward elastic and charge-exchange final-state interaction amplitudes are computed using effective Feynman diagram rules, with the on-shell NN scattering amplitude parameterized by total cross section, slope parameter, and ratio of real to imaginary parts fitted to experimental pn scattering data.

## Key numbers

- The kinetic energy of the recoil nucleon is restricted to T_N < 500 MeV to neglect inelastic initial state transitions involving baryonic resonances
- The calculation applies for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, where virtual photon interactions with exchanged mesons are neglected
- Experimental data cover Q^2 range from 2 to 5 GeV^2, with bins: Q^2 = 2 ± 0.25, 3 ± 0.5, 4 ± 0.5, 5 ± 0.5 GeV^2
- Recoil neutron momenta are restricted to 200 < p_r < 300 MeV/c and 400 < p_r < 600 MeV/c in the two data sets compared
- The maximum of final-state rescattering occurs at recoil angles of approximately 70 degrees, in agreement with GEA prediction

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework for high-Q^2 deuteron electrodisintegration developed since the mid-1990s by multiple groups (including work by Frankfurt and Strikman (1988), and later by Ciofi degli Atti and collaborators). It provides a self-consistent treatment of off-shell effects in the electromagnetic current and final-state interactions without requiring stationary approximations for recoil nucleons. The comparison with experimental data from Jefferson Lab (Kim et al., 2007) confirms that forward recoil nucleon angles are best suited for probing short-range NN interactions in the deuteron, while the region near 70 degrees shows maximal sensitivity to final-state interactions. The approach enables future studies with the 12 GeV upgrade at Jefferson Lab.
