# Statistical hadronization model description for rapidity densities at RHIC

> The rapidity densities in Au-Au collisions at center-of-mass energies 200 and 130 A GeV measured at Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider by STAR and PHENIX collaborations are analyzed within the statistical hadronization model at chemical freeze-out. We find that the model can describe the experimental rapidity densities well. The corresponding chemical freeze-out parameters are determined and they are seen to be in agreement with what we expect from our previous analyzes at lower beam energies at AGS and SPS.

## Key claim

The statistical hadronization model can describe the experimental rapidity densities at RHIC well, but the analysis at 130 GeV yields an unexpectedly large strangeness under-saturation parameter (γ_S significantly over unity) that is not yet well understood.

## Method

The author analyzed rapidity densities at mid-rapidity in central Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 and 130 GeV using the statistical hadronization model in the grand canonical ensemble with a supplementary γ_S parameter. Instead of fitting ratios of rapidity densities, the author fit a scaling parameter V' common for all particle species, characterizing the size of the hadron emitting source in the limited rapidity window around y=0. For the 130 GeV STAR data, rapidity densities measured in different centrality windows were extrapolated to the 0-5% most central collisions by assuming linear scaling with the negative hadron pseudorapidity density.

## Key numbers

- At √s_NN = 200 GeV (STAR 0-5% central): temperature T = 161.0 ± 3.9 MeV, baryon chemical potential μ_B = 30.0 ± 9.8 MeV, γ_S = 1.02 ± 0.05, χ²/dof = 12.5/8
- At √s_NN = 130 GeV (STAR 0-5% central): temperature T = 160.5 ± 4.3 MeV, baryon chemical potential μ_B = 35.6 ± 12.8 MeV, γ_S = 1.20 ± 0.08, χ²/dof = 7.0/8
- At √s_NN = 130 GeV (PHENIX central): temperature T = 158.0 ± 5.9 MeV, baryon chemical potential μ_B = 33.5 ± 17.8 MeV, γ_S = 1.24 ± 0.22, χ²/dof = 0.5/4
- Mean energy per particle at all three fits: approximately 1.0 GeV (0.99 ± 0.04, 1.00 ± 0.04, 0.97 ± 0.06 GeV respectively)
- Negative hadron pseudorapidity density in 0-5% most central collisions at 130 GeV: dN_h-/dη = 297 ± 18

## Context

This work extends the author's previous statistical hadronization model analyses at lower beam energies at AGS and SPS (Becattini 2005) to RHIC energies, finding that the chemical freeze-out parameters at RHIC agree with interpolating curves determined from those lower energies. The analysis differs from other statistical model analyses at RHIC (Braun-Munzinger 2001, Cleymans 2004, Andronic 2005) by fitting rapidity densities directly rather than ratios, avoiding potential bias from ratio fitting. The results enable comparison of strangeness production across beam energies, showing that strangeness reaches absolute chemical equilibrium only at RHIC energies, and that the RHIC systems follow the chemical freeze-out curve of mean energy per particle = 1 GeV (Cleymans 1998).
