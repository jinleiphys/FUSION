# Virtual Pion Scattering

> We propose a theory which exploits the charge-exchange reactions ($^3$He,$^3$H$π^+$) and (p,n$π^+$) as effective sources of virtual pions. We consider processes in which the creation of virtual pions is followed by conventional coupled-channel pion scattering to discrete nuclear states. This picture allows us to incorporate successful theories of pion scattering and utilize virtual pions as probes of the nuclear matter. For coherent pion production we clearly demonstrate that the shift of the coherent peak position in the excitation function of $^3$He-A relative to $^3$He-N scattering is determined entirely by the pion nucleus rescattering.

# Virtual Pion Scattering

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the downward energy shift observed in coherent pion production spectra from nuclear targets (relative to nucleon targets) is determined entirely by resonant pion absorption and subsequent incoherent Δ decay in the pion-nucleus rescattering, rather than by Δ-nucleus dynamics as previous models had argued.

## Method

The authors formulate coherent pion production as a two-step process: creation of a virtual pion via a charge-exchange reaction (³He,³Hπ⁺) or (p,nπ⁺), followed by conventional coupled-channel pion scattering through a pion-nucleus optical potential. They solve inhomogeneous coupled-channel Klein-Gordon equations in coordinate space, with the source term for pion production derived from a phenomenological t-matrix for NN→NNπ via Δ excitation, and the pion-nucleus optical potential including both resonant p-wave and s-wave contributions with medium corrections from pion absorption, Lorentz-Lorenz effect, and Pauli correlations. Calculations are performed for ¹²C and ⁴⁰Ca targets using shell-model transition densities and eikonal distortion for the projectile.

## Key numbers

- The Δ resonance corresponds to T_π ≈ 160 MeV, ω ≈ 300 MeV, where p-wave pion scattering dominates and resonant absorption reduces coherent pion yield.
- The phenomenological t-matrix uses t'_NΔ = 0.6 and Λ'_π = 650 MeV, chosen to match previous calculations by Oltmanns, Osterfeld, and Udagawa (1993) and Dmitriev (1993).
- Calculations are performed for ³He probe at T_³He = 2 GeV and θ_³H = 0 deg, and for p probe at T_p = 800 MeV and θ_n = 0 deg.
- The s-wave contribution to the pion-nucleus optical potential is important at pion c.m. kinetic energy below 50 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the theoretical treatment of coherent pion production beyond the Δ-hole model calculations of Oltmanns, Osterfeld, and Udagawa (1993) and Fernandez de Cordoba et al. (1993), which focused on Δ-nucleus dynamics, by instead emphasizing the role of pion-nucleus rescattering via an optical potential. The approach is similar in principle to that of Oset et al. (1994) but provides a quantitative connection between coherent pion production and elastic/inelastic pion scattering, and sets limits on the sensitivity required for future experiments by the Saclay-Orsay-Niels Bohr Institute collaboration.
