# $Θ^+$ pentaquark in the nuclear medium

> We study the interaction of the $Θ^+$ pentaquark with nuclear matter associated to the $KN$ decay channels and to the two meson cloud. We find that the potential is attractive and could be strong enough to lead to the existence of $Θ^+$ nuclear bound states.

## Key claim

The paper finds that the attractive potential from the two-meson cloud could be strong enough (50–100 MeV at normal nuclear density) to bind the Θ⁺ pentaquark in nuclei, but this conclusion depends on assumptions about the Θ⁺ quantum numbers (Jᴾ = 1/2⁺, SU(3) antidecuplet) and on large experimental uncertainties in the coupling constants extracted from N*(1710) decay data.

## Method

The authors calculate the Θ⁺ selfenergy in nuclear matter from two sources: the KN decay channel (using a one-loop diagram with in-medium nucleon and kaon propagators) and the two-meson cloud (coupling the Θ⁺ to a nucleon and two mesons via an SU(3)-symmetric Lagrangian). The coupling constants for the two-meson terms are determined from the PDG branching ratios of the N*(1710) resonance into Nππ channels. Medium effects are incorporated by including the kaon selfenergy (s-wave and p-wave parts), the pion selfenergy (p-wave with ph and Δh excitations), and Pauli blocking on the nucleon propagator.

## Key numbers

- The s-wave part of the kaon selfenergy is Π_K^(s)(ρ) = 0.13 m_K² ρ/ρ₀, where ρ₀ is normal nuclear density.
- For the KN decay channel with L=1 and a free Θ⁺ width of 15 MeV, the in-medium width for a bound state with ~20 MeV binding and Θ⁺ momentum of 200 MeV is around 5 MeV or smaller.
- The real part of the Θ⁺ selfenergy from the KN channel is of order 1 MeV or less.
- The coupling constants from N*(1710) decays are ḡ₁₀ = 0.72 (for p-wave ππ) and g̃̄₁₀ = 1.9 (for s-wave ππ).
- The two-meson contribution to the real part of the Θ⁺ selfenergy at ρ = ρ₀ is attractive with magnitude 50–100 MeV.
- The imaginary part of the two-meson contribution gives a width smaller than 5 MeV for bound states with ~20 MeV binding, and negligible for binding energies of ~40 MeV or higher.
- The total Θ⁺ width in nuclei is below 8 MeV assuming a free width of 15 MeV, and much lower if the free width is ~1 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies by Miller (2004) suggesting Θ⁺ hypernuclei might exist, and by Kim (2004) who found the KN coupling alone too weak to bind the pentaquark. It builds on the authors' own prior work (Cabrera 2004) showing that two-meson cloud contributions could produce sizable attraction. The results contradict the conclusion of Kim (2004) that binding is impossible, and support the quark mean-field model calculation by Shen and Toki (2004) which also found strong attraction. The paper enables experimental searches for Θ⁺ hypernuclei in light and intermediate nuclei, where the predicted narrow widths would make bound states observable.
