# Elastic pp-scattering at \sqrt s=7 TeV with the genuine Orear regime and the dip

> The unitarity condition unambigously requires the Orear region to appear in between the diffraction cone at low transferred momenta and hard parton scattering regime at high transferred momenta in hadron elastic scattering. It originates from rescattering of the diffraction cone processes. It is shown that such region has been observed in the differential cross section of the elastic pp-scattering at \sqrt s=7 TeV. The Orear region is described by exponential decrease with the scattering angle and imposed on it damped oscillations. They explain the steepening at the end of the diffraction cone as well as the dip and the subsequent maximum observed in TOTEM data. The failure of several models to describe the data in this region can be understood as improper account of the unitarity condition. It is shown that the real part of the amplitude can be as large as the imaginary part in this region. The overlap function is calculated and shown to be small outside the diffraction peak. Its negative sign there indicates the important role of phases in the amplitudes of inelastic processes.

# Elastic pp-scattering at √s = 7 TeV with the genuine Orear regime and the dip

## Key claim

The unitarity condition of the scattering amplitude requires an Orear regime (exponential decrease with scattering angle) to appear between the diffraction cone and the hard parton scattering regime; this regime, with superimposed damped oscillations, explains the steepening of the diffraction cone near |t| = 0.3 GeV², the dip at |t| = 0.53 GeV², and the subsequent maximum at |t| = 0.7 GeV² observed by TOTEM, but the fit depends on the ratio of real to imaginary parts of the amplitude outside the cone (ρ_l ≈ -2), which is not predicted from first principles.

## Method

The authors solved the unitarity equation for elastic pp-scattering using a Fourier transform method, assuming the overlap function (inelastic shadowing) is negligible outside the diffraction cone. They inserted the experimentally measured Gaussian diffraction peak amplitude into the two-particle intermediate state integral, obtaining a linear integral equation whose analytic solution yields an exponentially decreasing Orear term plus a series of damped oscillatory terms. The resulting formula for the differential cross section was then fitted to TOTEM data at √s = 7 TeV using the experimental values B = 20.1 GeV⁻² and σ_t = 98.3 mb, with the ratio of real to imaginary parts outside the cone (ρ_l) as a free parameter.

## Key numbers

- Diffraction slope: B = 20.1 GeV⁻², used as input from experiment.
- Total cross section: σ_t = 98.3 mb, used as input from experiment.
- Dip position: |t| = 0.53 GeV², where the cosine in the oscillating term is close to -1.
- Maximum position: |t| ≈ 0.7 GeV², where the oscillating term becomes positive.
- Steepened slope region: 0.3 < |t| < 0.36 GeV², explained by negative contribution of the oscillating term.
- Oscillation period: Δt = 2π/B, predicted from unitarity and depending only on the measured slope B.
- Fitted parameters: p₁ = 18.71 (overall normalization), p₂ = 115.6 (oscillation amplitude), φ = -0.845 (phase), ρ_l ≈ -2 (ratio of real to imaginary parts outside the cone).
- The ratio 4πB/σ_t is very close to 1, making the Orear exponent extremely sensitive to ρ_l.
- The overlap function crosses zero at |t| ≈ 0.3 GeV² and becomes negative in the Orear region.

## Context

This work extends the model-independent unitarity-based approach of Andreev and Dremin (1970s) and Andreev, Dremin, and Ginzburg (1970s), which successfully described the Orear regime at laboratory energies of 8–20 GeV, to the much higher LHC energy of √s = 7 TeV. It contradicts several reggeon-based models cited by TOTEM that fail to describe the data beyond the diffraction peak, and also differs from the non-damped oscillations predicted by Adya (1970s) in the reggeon exchange model. The results enable model-independent extraction of the ratio of real to imaginary parts of the elastic amplitude at large |t| for the first time, and provide a constraint on the phases of inelastic amplitudes through the negative sign of the overlap function in the Orear region.
