# Half-Skyrmions and the Equation of State for Compact-Star Matter

> The half-skyrmions that appear in dense baryonic matter when skyrmions are put on crystals modify drastically hadron properties in dense medium and affect strongly the nuclear tensor forces, thereby influencing the equation of state (EoS) of dense nuclear and asymmetric nuclear matter. The matter comprised of half skyrmions has vanishing quark condensate but non-vanishing pion decay constant and could be interpreted as a hadronic dual of strong-coupled quark matter. We infer from this observation combined with certain predictions of hidden local symmetry in low-energy hadronic interactionsa a set of new scaling laws -- called "new-BR" -- for the parameters in nuclear effective field theory controlled by renormalization-group flow. They are subjected to the EoS of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter, and are then applied to nuclear symmetry energies and properties of compact stars. The changeover from the skyrmion matter to a half-skyrmion matter that takes place after the cross-over density $n_{1/2}$ provides a simple and natural field theoretic explanation for the change of the EoS from soft to stiff at a density above that of nuclear matter required for compact stars as massive as $\sim 2.4M_\odot$. Cross-over density in the range $1.5n_0 \lsim n_{1/2} \lsim 2.0 n_0$ has been employed, and the possible skyrmion half-skyrmion coexistence {or cross-over} near $n_{1/2}$ is discussed. The novel structure of {the tensor forces and} the EoS obtained with the new-BR scaling is relevant for neutron-rich nuclei and compact star matter and could be studied in RIB (rare isotope beam) machines.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that a crossover from skyrmion matter to half-skyrmion matter at a density \(n_{1/2}\) (estimated between \(1.5n_0\) and \(2.0n_0\)) provides a field-theoretic explanation for the equation of state (EoS) changing from soft to stiff at supra-nuclear densities, which is required to support compact stars as massive as \(\sim 2.4 M_\odot\). The claim is hedged by noting that the crystal structure valid in the large \(N_c\) limit may not be applicable at the few-times-normal-density regime, and the arguments are "neither rigorous nor completely unambiguous."

## Method

The authors translate classical skyrmion-crystal results for the symmetry energy into a hidden local symmetry (HLS) effective Lagrangian with density-dependent parameters, introducing "new-BR" scaling laws for nucleon and meson masses and couplings that differ below and above the half-skyrmion onset density \(n_{1/2}\). They then perform all-order \(pphh\) ring-diagram calculations using a renormalization-group-derived low-momentum potential \(V_{low-k}\) (from the BonnS potential) with these scaling parameters to compute the EoS of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter up to \(\sim 5n_0\).

## Key numbers

- The crossover density for the skyrmion-to-half-skyrmion transition is estimated in the range \(1.5n_0 \lesssim n_{1/2} \lesssim 2.0n_0\), where \(n_0 \approx 0.16\) fm\(^{-3}\) is normal nuclear matter density.
- The new-BR EoS for symmetric nuclear matter yields saturation density \(n_{sat} = 0.93 n_0\), energy per nucleon \(E_0/A = -15\) MeV, and compression modulus \(K = 208\) MeV.
- The Ericson scaling parameter \(D = n_0 \Sigma_{\pi N} / (f_\pi^2 m_\pi^2)\) is given as \(0.35 \pm 0.06\), using \(\Sigma_{\pi N} = 45 \pm 7\) MeV.
- The scaling parameter \(c_I\) in Region I is chosen as 0.130 for nucleon and \(\rho\)-meson, 0.121 for \(\sigma\)-meson, and 0.139 for \(\omega\)-meson.
- The smoothness parameter \(\delta\) for joining scaling functions is taken as \(\delta / n_0 \approx 0.05\)–0.10.
- The decimation scale for \(V_{low-k}\) is \(\Lambda = 3\) fm\(^{-1}\).

## Context

This work extends the skyrmion-crystal approach of Lee, Park, and Rho (LPR) and Lee and Rho (LR12), who predicted a cusp in the symmetry energy at the half-skyrmion onset density, by translating those classical results into a quantum effective field theory with renormalization-group flow. It contrasts with the "old-BR" scaling of Brown and Rho (1991) and the Ericson scaling based on the quark condensate, which do not capture the change in tensor-force behavior above \(n_{1/2}\). The results enable predictions for neutron-rich nuclei and compact-star properties, including maximum masses up to \(\sim 2.4 M_\odot\), and could be tested at rare isotope beam (RIB) facilities.
