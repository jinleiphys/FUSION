# Neutrino Scattering: Connections Across Theory and Experiment

> In this document drafted by the Neutrino Scattering Theory Experiment Collaboration (NuSTEC), we provide input on the synergies between theoretical and experimental efforts that can provide critical input to the prediction accuracy needed for the forthcoming high-precision neutrino measurements. These efforts involve a wide range of energies and interaction processes, as well as target nuclei and interaction probes. The challenges discussed will be overcome only through the active support of integrated collaboration across strong and electroweak physics from both the nuclear and high energy physics communities.

## Key claim

The paper argues that achieving the percent-level systematic uncertainties required by next-generation neutrino oscillation experiments (DUNE, Hyper-Kamiokande) demands integrated collaboration across strong and electroweak physics from both nuclear and high-energy physics communities, but that current efforts remain fragmented with no clear leadership or sufficient institutional support.

## Method

The Neutrino Scattering Theory Experiment Collaboration (NuSTEC) surveyed the current state of neutrino event generators (GENIE, GiBUU, NuWro, NEUT, ACHILLES, FLUKA/NUNDIS) and the experimental landscape spanning energies from 0.1 GeV to 1 TeV. They assessed the modeling challenges across energy regimes, the impact of cross-section uncertainties on oscillation analyses, and the need for improved numerical methods including machine learning techniques and common event-generator formats like NuHepMC.

## Key numbers

* Oscillation analyses in T2K and NOvA see cross-section uncertainties impacting results at the 3-10% level, with statistical uncertainties of 5-10%
* For forthcoming experiments, statistical uncertainties will decrease to 1-2%, requiring systematic uncertainties to scale accordingly
* The range of neutrino energies needing modeling spans from 0.1 GeV to 1 TeV
* Nuclear targets vary from hydrogen and simple hydrocarbons to argon and heavier materials like iron

## Context

This white paper extends the work of the NuSTEC collaboration and builds on prior community efforts to address neutrino-nucleus interaction uncertainties. It specifically references the need for a CERN Neutrino Physics Center modeled on Fermilab's LHC Physics Center, and discusses the integration of the NUISANCE tuning framework with HepData. The paper enables future coordinated development of neutrino event generators across energy scales, particularly addressing the gap in MeV-scale generator support exemplified by MARLEY, which has been adopted for all official DUNE studies on supernova and solar neutrinos but currently lacks dedicated development support.
