# High density behaviour of nuclear symmetry energy

> Role of the isospin asymmetry in nuclei and neutron stars, with an emphasis on the density dependence of the nuclear symmetry energy, is discussed. The symmetry energy is obtained using the isoscalar as well as isovector components of the density dependent M3Y effective interaction. The constants of density dependence of the effective interaction are obtained by reproducing the saturation energy per nucleon and the saturation density of spin and isospin symmetric cold infinite nuclear matter. Implications for the density dependence of the symmetry energy in case of a neutron star are discussed, and also possible constraints on the density dependence obtained from finite nuclei are compared.

## Key claim

The nuclear symmetry energy (NSE) calculated using the density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective interaction yields a "soft" density dependence that peaks near 1.8 times saturation density and becomes negative above about 4 times saturation density, implying that pure neutron matter becomes the most stable state at very high densities, a condition the abstract does not explicitly state.

## Method

The authors compute the nuclear symmetry energy from the isoscalar and isovector components of the M3Y-Reid-Elliott effective interaction supplemented by a zero-range pseudo-potential, with a density dependence of the form \( g(\rho, \epsilon) = C (1 - \beta \rho^n) \). The parameters \( C \) and \( \beta \) are fixed by reproducing the saturation energy per nucleon and saturation density of symmetric nuclear matter (SNM). The symmetry energy is then obtained as the difference in energy per nucleon between pure neutron matter and SNM, and the \(\beta\)-equilibrium proton fraction in neutron-star matter is derived from it.

## Key numbers

- Saturation density of SNM: \(\rho_0 = 0.1533 \, \text{fm}^{-3}\).
- Saturation energy per nucleon of SNM: \(\epsilon_0 = -15.26 \, \text{MeV}\).
- For \(n=2/3\), the density-dependence parameters are \(C = 2.07\) and \(\beta = 1.668 \, \text{fm}^2\), yielding an SNM incompressibility \(K_0 = 293.4 \, \text{MeV}\).
- For \(n=1/3\), the incompressibility is \(K_0 = 226.1 \, \text{MeV}\).
- The NSE peaks at \(\rho \approx 1.8 \rho_0\) and becomes negative at \(\rho \approx 4 \rho_0\) (for \(n=2/3\)).
- The \(\beta\)-equilibrium proton fraction reaches a maximum of \(x_\beta \approx 0.048\) at \(\rho \approx 1.4 \rho_0\) and goes to zero at \(\rho \approx 3.9 \rho_0\) (for \(n=2/3\)); for \(n=1/3\) it goes to zero at \(\rho \approx 5.1 \rho_0\).
- The low-density NSE extracted from isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions has the approximate form \(31.6 [\rho/\rho_0]^{1.05} \, \text{MeV}\).

## Context

This work extends the use of the DDM3Y effective interaction, previously applied to nuclear scattering and radioactivity (Satchler 1979, Khoa 1984, Gu 2005, Basu 2005), to the high-density behavior of the nuclear symmetry energy. It contrasts with "stiff" density dependences (Chen 2005) that predict monotonic increases and with "soft" dependences that peak near saturation density (Basu 2002). The calculated symmetry energy is consistent at subsaturation densities with constraints from isospin diffusion in heavy-ion collisions (Chen 2005), and the resulting \(\beta\)-equilibrium proton fraction forbids the direct URCA process in neutron stars, consistent with the absence of fast cooling. The results can be tested by experimental probes such as the \(\pi^-\) to \(\pi^+\) ratio, transverse collective flow, and neutron-proton differential flow.
