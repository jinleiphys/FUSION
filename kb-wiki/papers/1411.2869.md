# Single-inclusive particle production in proton-nucleus collisions at next-to-leading order in the hybrid formalism

> We reconsider the perturbative next-to-leading calculation of the single inclusive hadron production in the framework of the hybrid formalism, applied to hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions. Our analysis, performed in the wave function approach, differs from the previous works in three points. First, we are careful to specify unambiguously the rapidity interval that has to be included in the evolution of the leading-order eikonal scattering amplitude. This is important, since varying this interval by a number of order unity changes the next-to-leading order correction that the calculation is meant to determine. Second, we introduce the explicit requirement that fast fluctuations in the projectile wave function which only exist for a short time are not resolved by the target. This Ioffe time cutoff also strongly affects the next-to-leading order terms. Third, our result does not employ the approximation of a large number of colors. Our final expressions are unambiguous and do not coincide at next-to-leading order with the results available in the literature.

## Key claim

The paper reanalyzes the next-to-leading order (NLO) calculation of single-inclusive hadron production in proton-nucleus collisions within the hybrid formalism, finding that previous calculations were incomplete because they omitted the Ioffe time restriction on the phase space of emissions and did not unambiguously specify the rapidity interval for the evolution of the leading-order eikonal scattering amplitude; the authors provide corrected expressions that differ from those in the literature and may lead to different numerical results in some kinematic regions.

## Method

The authors performed a perturbative NLO calculation using the wave function approach, treating the projectile proton via collinear factorization (parton distribution functions and fragmentation functions) and the target as a distribution of strong color fields. They introduced an explicit Ioffe time cutoff to ensure that only quark-gluon fluctuations with coherence time longer than the target's longitudinal size scatter eikonally, and they specified the target evolution rapidity as \(Y_T = \ln(s/s_0)\) rather than depending on the final-state transverse momentum. The calculation was done without the large-\(N_c\) approximation, and collinear divergences were absorbed into DGLAP-evolved parton densities and fragmentation functions.

## Key numbers

- The Ioffe time restriction is given by \(\frac{2(1-\xi)\xi x_B P^+}{k_\perp^2} > \tau\), where \(\tau\) is the target size at initial energy \(s_0\), leading to the condition \(\frac{(1-\xi)\xi x_B}{k_\perp^2} > s_0^{-1}\).
- The target evolution rapidity is defined as \(Y_T = \ln(s/s_0)\), with \(s_0\) being an arbitrary initial energy high enough for eikonal validity.
- The modified Weizsäcker-Williams field is \(A_{\xi,x_B}^i(y-z) = -\frac{1}{2\pi} \frac{(y-z)^i}{(y-z)^2} \left[1 - J_0\left(|y-z| \sqrt{2\xi(1-\xi)\frac{x_B P^+}{\tau}}\right)\right]\), which reduces to the standard form \(-\frac{1}{2\pi}\frac{(y-z)^i}{(y-z)^2}\) in the limit \(P^+/\tau \to \infty\).
- The natural factorization scale for the parton distribution and fragmentation functions is \(\mu = |p_{h\perp}|\).

## Context

This work extends and corrects the NLO calculations of single-inclusive hadron production in the hybrid formalism by Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2012) and by Kang, Vitev, and Xing (2014), which did not include the Ioffe time constraint and used different prescriptions for the target evolution rapidity. The authors show that the prescription in Kang, Vitev, and Xing (2014) using \(Y_g = \ln(1/x_g)\) with \(x_g = e^{-\eta} p_\perp/\sqrt{s}\) is not consistent with NLO accuracy, and that the Ioffe time restriction leads to the leading-order Balitsky-Kovchegov evolution of the dipole scattering amplitude with a well-defined scale. The results enable future numerical studies to determine whether the corrected NLO expressions stabilize the cross section, which previously became negative at moderate transverse momenta in calculations by Chirilli, Xiao, and Yuan (2012) and by Stasto, Xiao, and Yuan (2014).
