# Open charm mesons at nonzero temperature: results in the hadronic phase from lattice QCD

> We study what happens to D and D_s mesons as the temperature increases, using lattice QCD simulations with N_f=2+1 dynamical flavours on anistropic lattices. We have access to five temperatures in the hadronic phase. Using the determined groundstate mass at the lowest temperature, we investigate the effect of rising temperature by analysing ratios of mesonic correlators, without the need for further fitting or spectral reconstruction. In the pseudoscalar and vector channels, we demonstrate that temperature effects are at the percent level and can be captured by a reduction of the groundstate mass as the thermal crossover is approached. In the axial-vector and scalar channels on the other hand, temperature effects are prominent throughout the hadronic phase.

## Key claim

Temperature effects on open charm mesons in the hadronic phase are at the percent level for pseudoscalar and vector channels, where a reduction of the groundstate mass is observed as the thermal crossover is approached; in contrast, axial-vector and scalar channels show prominent temperature effects throughout the hadronic phase, though the analysis is complicated by larger noise and the absence of clear plateaus in the correlator ratios.

## Method

The authors performed lattice QCD simulations with \(N_f=2+1\) dynamical quark flavours on anisotropic lattices, using a fixed-scale approach where temperature is varied by changing the temporal lattice extent \(N_\tau\). They extracted groundstate masses at the lowest temperature (\(T=47\) MeV) by fitting the exponential decay of mesonic correlators, then analysed temperature dependence by constructing ratios of correlators and model correlators (single-state propagator functions) without explicit spectral reconstruction. A double ratio was used to eliminate trivial temperature effects from the kernel, allowing changes in spectral content to be identified.

## Key numbers

- Groundstate masses at \(T=47\) MeV: \(D\) meson \(1876(4)\) MeV, \(D^*\) meson \(2001(4)\) MeV, \(D_s\) meson \(1972(5)\) MeV, \(D_s^*\) meson \(2092(4)\) MeV (pseudoscalar and vector channels)
- Mass reduction between \(T=47\) and \(152\) MeV: \(D\) meson \(20(7)\) MeV, \(D^*\) meson \(43(10)\) MeV, \(D_s\) meson \(24(7)\) MeV, \(D_s^*\) meson \(32(7)\) MeV
- Pseudocritical temperature \(T_{\rm pc}=167(2)(1)\) MeV determined from the inflection point of the renormalised chiral condensate
- Inverse temporal lattice spacing \(a_\tau^{-1}=6.079(13)\) GeV; pion mass \(m_\pi=239(1)\) MeV
- Double ratio deviations from 1 at \(T=152\) MeV are on the order of 5% in pseudoscalar and vector channels, rising to 20% in the QGP phase

## Context

This work extends previous lattice QCD studies of open charm mesons at nonzero temperature, notably the analysis of spectral functions by Kelly et al. (2018) which used heavier light quarks (\(m_\pi=390\) MeV) and found no significant mass shift, whereas here smeared sources enable extraction of mass shifts in the hadronic phase. The results are compared with effective field theory predictions by Montana et al. (2020) based on chiral and heavy-quark spin-flavour symmetries, which found similar mass reductions of about 40 MeV for \(D^{(*)}\) and 20 MeV for \(D_s^{(*)}\) between \(T=0\) and 150 MeV. The study provides a first-principle benchmark for effective models that aim to describe hadrons under extreme conditions, particularly those that can be extended to regions of the phase diagram where Monte Carlo simulations are not directly applicable, such as at larger baryon chemical potential.
