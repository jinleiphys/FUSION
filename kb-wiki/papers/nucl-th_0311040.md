# Testing $^{6,8}$He density distributions by calculations of total reaction cross-sections of $^{6,8}$He+$^{28}$Si

> Calculations of the $^{6,8}$He + $^{28}$Si total reaction cross sections at intermediate energies are performed on the basis of the Glauber-Sitenko microscopic optical-limit model. The target-nucleus density distribution is taken from the electron-nucleus scattering data, and the $^{6,8}$He densities are used as they are derived in different models. The results of the calculations are compared with the existing experimental data. The effects of the density tails of the projectile nuclei as well as the role of shell admixtures and short-range correlations are analyzed.

## Key claim

The total reaction cross sections of \(^{6,8}\)He + \(^{28}\)Si at intermediate energies (10–50 MeV/nucleon) are largely insensitive to the detailed shape of the neutron density tail beyond about two root-mean-square (rms) radii, meaning that such cross-section data alone cannot distinguish between density models that differ mainly in the far outer region.

## Method

The authors used the Glauber-Sitenko microscopic optical-limit model to calculate total reaction cross sections for \(^{6,8}\)He projectiles on a \(^{28}\)Si target. The target density was taken as a symmetrized Fermi distribution fitted to electron-scattering data, while the projectile densities were taken from four different models: the M-model (based on a cluster expansion with Jastrow-type short-range correlations), the T-model (Tanihata et al., fitted to 800 MeV/nucleon data), the COSMA model (cluster-orbital shell-model approximation), and the LSSM model (large-space shell-model). The calculated cross sections were compared with experimental data from Swedish and Penionzhkevich (2003) at energies of about 10–50 MeV/nucleon.

## Key numbers

- The rms matter radius of \(^{6}\)He is 2.331 fm for both the M-model and the T-model.
- The neutron rms radius for \(^{6}\)He in the M-model is 2.5865 fm (from Table I, with \(B = 1.8249\) fm, \(\beta = 6.0001\) fm\(^{-2}\)).
- The neutron rms radius for \(^{8}\)He in the M-model is 2.6587 fm (from Table I, with \(B = 1.8075\) fm, \(\beta = 5.1558\) fm\(^{-2}\)).
- The proton rms radius for \(^{6}\)He is 1.72 fm, and for \(^{8}\)He is 1.76 fm (both from Table I).
- The target \(^{28}\)Si density parameters are \(R = 3.085\) fm and \(a = 0.563\) fm.
- The COSMA-model matter rms radius for \(^{6}\)He is 2.560 fm; the LSSM-model matter rms radius for \(^{6}\)He is 2.956 fm.
- The cross sections from the COSMA and LSSM models exceed the experimental data by about 200–500 mb.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of \(^{6,8}\)He density distributions by Tanihata et al. (1992) and Zhukov et al. (1993), who used total reaction cross sections at 800 MeV/nucleon to infer halo structures. It also contrasts with elastic scattering studies (e.g., Alkhazov et al., 2002) that are more sensitive to the density tail. The paper shows that total reaction cross sections at lower energies (10–50 MeV/nucleon) are dominated by the nuclear interior up to about two rms radii, so they cannot discriminate between models with different tail shapes. This finding enables future work to focus on combining total reaction data with elastic scattering or breakup measurements to constrain the full density profile.
