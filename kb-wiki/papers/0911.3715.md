# Topologically induced local P and CP violation in QCD x QED

> The existence of topological solutions and axial anomaly open a possibility of P and CP violation in QCD. For a reason that has not yet been established conclusively, this possibility is not realized in strong interactions - the experimental data indicate that a global P and CP violation in QCD is absent. Nevertheless, the fluctuations of topological charge in QCD vacuum, although not observable directly, are expected to play an important role in the breaking of $U_A(1)$ symmetry and in the mass spectrum and other properties of hadrons. Moreover, in the presence of very intense external electromagnetic fields topological solutions of QCD can induce local P- and CP-odd effects in the $SU_c(3)\times U_{em}(1)$ gauge theory that can be observed in experiment directly. Here I show how these local parity-violating phenomena can be described by using the Maxwell-Chern-Simons, or axion, electrodynamics as an effective theory. Local P- and CP- violation in hot QCD matter can be observed in experiment through the "chiral magnetic effect" - the separation of electric charge along the axis of magnetic field. Very recently, STAR Collaboration presented an observation of the electric charge asymmetry with respect to reaction plane in relativistic heavy ion collisions at RHIC.

## Key claim

Topological fluctuations in QCD, while not producing global P and CP violation in strong interactions, can induce local P- and CP-odd effects observable in experiment when very intense external electromagnetic fields are present, specifically through the chiral magnetic effect -- the separation of electric charge along the axis of a magnetic field.

## Method

The paper derives an effective Maxwell-Chern-Simons (axion) electrodynamics by coupling QCD to QED, introducing a pseudo-scalar field theta that varies in space and time to represent fluctuations of topological charge. The resulting modified Maxwell equations are then used to analyze charge separation phenomena in the presence of external magnetic fields, with the topological charge fluctuations driving the dynamics through the axial anomaly.

## Key numbers

- The CP-violating phase theta is constrained to be less than 3 x 10^{-10} from experimental bounds on the electric dipole moment of the neutron (Baker 2006)
- The sphaleron transition rate in weakly coupled Yang-Mills theory with N colors is proportional to (g^2 N)^5 ln(1/g^2 N) T^4
- In strongly coupled N=4 SYM via AdS/CFT, the topological charge diffusion rate is (g^2 N)^2/(256 pi^3) T^4
- The coupling constant c in the Maxwell-Chern-Simons Lagrangian equals sum_f q_f^2 e^2 / (2 pi^2)
- The electric dipole moment induced by a magnetic field B across a domain of size L and area S is d_e = c theta (B dot S) L = sum_f q_f^2 (e theta/pi) (eB dot S / 2pi) L
- The induced current from a time-varying theta field is J = - (e^2 / 2 pi^2) d(theta)/dt B
- The rate of chirality generation is d^4 Q_5 / dt dV = (e^2 / 2 pi^2) E dot B

## Context

This work extends the theoretical framework of topological effects in QCD, building on instanton solutions (Belavin 1975), the strong CP problem, and the Witten effect (Witten 1979) where magnetic monopoles acquire electric charge at finite theta angle. The paper formalizes the chiral magnetic effect previously proposed by Kharzeev (2004, 2007) and connects it to recent lattice QCD calculations (Buividovich 2009) and experimental observations by the STAR Collaboration (Abelev 2009) of charge-dependent azimuthal correlations in Au-Au collisions at RHIC. The framework enables direct experimental tests of topological charge fluctuations in QCD through heavy ion collision measurements.
