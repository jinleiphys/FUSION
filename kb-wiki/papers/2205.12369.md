# One-loop matching for gluon lattice TMDs

> Transverse-momentum-dependent parton distributions (TMDs) can be calculated from first principles by computing a related set of Euclidean lattice observables and connecting them via a factorization formula. This work focuses on the leading-power factorization formula connecting the lattice quasi-TMD and continuum Collins TMD for gluons. We calculate the one-loop gluon matching coefficient, which is known to be independent of spin and exhibits no mixing with quarks. We demonstrate that this coefficient satisfies Casimir scaling with respect to the quark matching coefficient at one-loop order. Our result facilitates reliable lattice QCD calculations of gluon TMDs.

## Key claim

The one-loop gluon matching coefficient connecting lattice quasi-TMDs to continuum Collins TMDs is identical in form to the quark matching coefficient, differing only by the replacement of the quark Casimir $C_F$ with the gluon Casimir $C_A$, and this Casimir scaling holds for the full one-loop result and implies it extends to the full tower of next-to-leading-logarithmic terms.

## Method

The authors computed the one-loop matching coefficient $C_g$ by evaluating the difference between the Large Rapidity (LR) scheme and Collins scheme beam functions for a free external gluon state with transverse polarization, working in Feynman gauge and dimensional regularization with $d=4-2\epsilon$. They identified that only diagrams containing rapidity divergences contribute to the matching, specifically those with Wilson line attachments, and evaluated the relevant integrals under two different orders of limits: $y_B\to -\infty$ before $\epsilon\to0$ (Collins scheme) and $\epsilon\to0$ before $y_B\to -\infty$ (LR scheme). The matching coefficient was then extracted from the difference of the renormalized results, with the soft function contribution cancelling between the two schemes.

## Key numbers

- The one-loop gluon matching coefficient is: $C_g(x\tilde P^z,\mu) = 1-\frac{\alpha_s C_A}{4\pi}\left[ \ln^2 \frac{\mu^2}{(2x\tilde P^z)^2} + 2\ln \frac{\mu^2}{(2x\tilde P^z)^2} + 4 - \frac{\pi^2}{6}\right]$
- The result differs from the quark matching coefficient only by the Casimir replacement $C_F\to C_A$, confirming Casimir scaling at one-loop order
- The one-loop Collins soft function for gluons is $S_C^{g,(1)}(b_T,\epsilon,2y_n,2y_B) = \frac{\alpha_s C_A}{2\pi} \iota^\epsilon \frac{\Gamma(-\epsilon)}{4^\epsilon} (b_T^2\mu^2)^\epsilon\left[2(y_n-y_B)\frac{1+e^{2(y_B-y_n)}}{1-e^{2(y_B-y_n)}} - 2\right]$
- The matching coefficient is independent of gluon spin structure and exhibits no mixing with quarks

## Context

This work extends the quasi-TMD factorization program, previously developed for quarks by Ji (2014), Ebert (2018, 2019), and Ebert (2022), to gluons by providing the missing one-loop matching coefficient. The result enables first-principles lattice QCD calculations of all eight leading-twist gluon TMDs, which are important for understanding proton internal dynamics at planned facilities like the Electron-Ion Collider. The Casimir scaling observed here, which is known to hold for the cusp anomalous dimension and Collins-Soper kernel to four-loop order from work by Korchemsky (1987), Moch (2004), and Henn (2019), suggests a systematic relationship for quasi-TMD matching coefficients at higher orders.
