# Nucleon Electromagnetic Form Factors in a chiral constituent-quark model

> The electromagnetic form factors of the nucleon are calculated in an extended chiral constituent-quark model where the effective interaction is described by the exchange of pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar mesons. Two-body current-density operators, constructed consistently with the extended model Hamiltonian in order to preserve gauge invariance and current conservation, are found to give a significant contribution to the nucleon magnetic form factors and improve the estimates of the nucleon magnetic moments.

## Key claim

The two-body current operators derived from the full pion- and rho-exchange interaction (including tensor forces) give a sizeable contribution to the nucleon magnetic form factors, unlike the case where only the spin-spin component of pseudoscalar exchange was considered, where the two-body current contribution was found to be zero.

## Method

The authors used the wavefunctions from an extended Goldstone-boson exchange (GBE) constituent-quark model with a semirelativistic Hamiltonian containing linear confinement and a chiral interaction from pseudoscalar, vector, and scalar meson exchanges. Two-body current-density operators were derived from the continuity equation consistently with the model Hamiltonian, yielding pion-pair, pion-in-flight, rho-pair, and rho-in-flight currents, and their matrix elements were calculated for the nucleon elastic electromagnetic form factors.

## Key numbers

- Proton magnetic moment from one-body currents only: 1.516 n.m.; with two-body currents included: 2.218 n.m.
- Neutron magnetic moment from one-body currents only: -0.993 n.m.; with two-body currents included: -1.695 n.m.
- Individual two-body current contributions to the proton magnetic moment: pion-pair -0.126 n.m., pion-in-flight +0.735 n.m., rho-pair -0.109 n.m., rho-in-flight +0.202 n.m.
- Individual two-body current contributions to the neutron magnetic moment: pion-pair +0.126 n.m., pion-in-flight -0.735 n.m., rho-pair +0.109 n.m., rho-in-flight -0.202 n.m.
- With extended quarks (anomalous magnetic moment kappa = 0.379 for quark mass 340 MeV), the neutron magnetic moment becomes -2.073 n.m.
- Extracted quark charge radius: 0.7 fm.

## Context

This work extends the earlier calculation by Boffi et al. (1999) which used only the spin-spin component of pseudoscalar exchange and found zero two-body current contributions, by including the full tensor forces from pion and rho exchange. The results show that two-body currents significantly improve the magnetic moments compared to the one-body-only results, but the pointlike quark assumption leads to overestimated form factors at non-zero Q^2. The authors then show that treating constituent quarks as extended particles with a dipole form factor and anomalous magnetic moment yields satisfactory agreement with experimental nucleon form factors, with the extracted quark charge radius consistent with the Vector Meson Dominance model.
