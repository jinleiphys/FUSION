# Islands of shape coexistence in proxy-SU(3) symmetry and in covariant density functional theory

> Shape coexistence in even-even nuclei is observed when the ground state band of a nucleus is accompanied by another K=0 band at similar energy but with radically different structure. We attempt to predict regions of shape coexistence throughout the nuclear chart using the parameter-free proxy-SU(3) symmetry and standard covariant density functional theory. Within the proxy-SU(3) symmetry the interplay of shell model magic numbers, formed by the spin-orbit interaction, and the 3-dimensional isotropic harmonic oscillator magic numbers, leads to the prediction of specific horizontal and vertical stripes on the nuclear chart in which shape coexistence should be possible. Within covariant density functional theory, specific islands on the nuclear chart are found, in which particle-hole excitations leading to shape coexistence are observed. The role played by particle-hole excitations across magic numbers as well as the collapse of magic numbers as deformation sets in is clarified.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that shape coexistence in even-even nuclei is not uniformly distributed across the nuclear chart but is confined to specific islands, arising from an interplay between the harmonic oscillator (HO) magic numbers and the spin-orbit (SO) magic numbers, with the condition that shape coexistence requires the second-order Casimir eigenvalue of the SO irrep to exceed that of the HO irrep, and that particle-hole excitations serve as a sufficient condition for its appearance.

## Method

The authors used the parameter-free proxy-SU(3) symmetry to identify horizontal and vertical stripes on the nuclear chart where shape coexistence is possible, based on the condition that the SU(3) Casimir eigenvalue for the spin-orbit irrep exceeds that of the harmonic oscillator irrep. They then performed covariant density functional theory calculations using the DDME2 functional to compute single-particle energy levels labeled by Nilsson quantum numbers, searching for proton and neutron particle-hole excitations in specific isotopes and isotones across different mass regions.

## Key numbers

* The condition for shape coexistence is fulfilled within regions of 7-8, 17-20, 34-40, 59-70, 96-112, and 146-168 nucleons, defining the stripes where shape coexistence is possible.
* In the Z=82 region, shape coexistence in Hg isotopes is observed only between N=96 and N=110, with a minimum at the neutron midshell N=104.
* In the Z=50 region, shape coexistence in Sn isotopes appears only between N=60 and N=70, with a minimum at the neutron midshell N=64.
* Proton particle-hole excitations were found in isotopes around Z=82 (e.g., Pt-176,188, Hg-176,190, Pb-180,190, Po-182,192) and around Z=50 (e.g., Te-116,120).
* Neutron particle-hole excitations were found in isotones around N=90 (e.g., Nd-150,152, Sm-152,154, Gd-154,156) and around N=60 (e.g., Zr-98,100, Mo-102).
* Both proton and neutron particle-hole excitations appear in an island around Z=N=40 (e.g., Sr-78, Zr-78,80).

## Context

This work extends the schematic regions of shape coexistence identified in the review by Heyde (2011) by providing a microscopic mechanism based on the interplay of HO and SO magic numbers, and by using covariant density functional theory to identify specific islands where particle-hole excitations occur. The proxy-SU(3) approach builds on the Elliott model and the Nilsson model, while the density functional calculations use the DDME2 functional from Lalazissis (2005). The results enable future experimental searches for shape coexistence in the predicted islands, particularly at radioactive ion beam facilities, and call for further investigation of four possible proton-neutron cases (SO-SO, SO-HO, HO-SO, HO-HO) that might lead to predictions of multiple coexistence.
