# Constraining nuclear symmetry energy with the charge radii of mirror-pair nuclei

> The nuclear charge radius plays a vital role in determining the equation of state of isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. Based on the correlation between the differences in charge radii of mirror-partner nuclei and the slope parameter ($L$) of symmetry energy at the nuclear saturation density, an analysis of the calibrated slope parameter $L$ was performed in finite nuclei. In this study, relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functionals were employed to constrain the nuclear symmetry energy through the available databases of the mirror-pair nuclei $^{36}$Ca-$^{36}$S, $^{38}$Ca-$^{38}$Ar, and $^{54}$Ni-$^{54}$Fe. The deduced nuclear symmetry energy was located in the range 29.89-31.85 MeV, and $L$ of the symmetry energy essentially covered the range 22.50-51.55 MeV at the saturation density. Moreover, the extracted $L_s$ at the sensitivity density $ρ_{s}=0.10~\mathrm{fm}^{-3}$ was located in the interval range 30.52-39.76 MeV.

## Key claim

The slope parameter \(L\) of the nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density is constrained to the range 22.50–51.55 MeV by analyzing the differences in charge radii of three mirror-pair nuclei using both relativistic and non-relativistic energy density functionals, but the authors note that deformation effects and model uncertainties were not incorporated and may change the present results.

## Method

The authors employed two families of energy density functionals: Skyrme (non-relativistic) and covariant density functional theory (relativistic). For each family, parameter sets were constructed with fixed symmetry energy around a sensitivity density of 0.10 fm\(^{-3}\) but varying symmetry energy at saturation density, yielding different slope parameters \(L\). The differences in root-mean-square charge radii (\(\Delta R_{\mathrm{ch}}\)) for the mirror pairs \(^{36}\)Ca-\(^{36}\)S, \(^{38}\)Ca-\(^{38}\)Ar, and \(^{54}\)Ni-\(^{54}\)Fe were calculated and compared to experimental data to extract linear correlations with \(L\).

## Key numbers

* The deduced nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density was located in the range 29.89–31.85 MeV.
* The slope parameter \(L\) of the symmetry energy at saturation density covered the range 22.50–51.55 MeV.
* The slope parameter \(L_s\) at the sensitivity density \(\rho_s = 0.10\ \mathrm{fm}^{-3}\) was located in the interval 30.52–39.76 MeV.
* For the \(A=54\) mirror pair, the extracted \(L\) range was 17.99–62.43 MeV; for \(A=38\), 6.83–52.49 MeV; for \(A=36\), 22.50–51.55 MeV.
* The experimental \(\Delta R_{\mathrm{ch}}\) values used were: \(^{36}\)Ca-\(^{36}\)S = 0.150(4) fm, \(^{38}\)Ca-\(^{38}\)Ar = 0.063(3) fm, \(^{54}\)Ni-\(^{54}\)Fe = 0.049(4) fm.

## Context

This work extends the approach of Brown (2020) and Pineda (2021), who used mirror-pair charge radii to constrain \(L\) with Skyrme functionals, by simultaneously analyzing three mirror pairs (\(A=36, 38, 54\)) with both Skyrme and covariant density functionals. The resulting \(L\) range (22.50–51.55 MeV) provides a tighter constraint than those earlier studies and overlaps significantly with constraints from electric dipole polarizability (Roca-Maza, 2013), the CREX neutron skin measurement on \(^{48}\)Ca, and the gravitational-wave event GW170817 (Raithel, 2019), but does not overlap with the larger \(L\) values from the PREX-II measurement on \(^{208}\)Pb.
