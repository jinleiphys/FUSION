# (Hybrid) Baryons in the Flux-Tube Model

> We construct baryons and hybrid baryons in the non-relativistic flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton. The motion of the flux-tube with the three quark positions fixed, except for centre of mass corrections, is discussed. It is shown that the problem can to an excellent approximation be reduced to the independent motion of a junction and strings.

# (Hybrid) Baryons in the Flux-Tube Model

## Key claim
The non-relativistic flux-tube model of Isgur and Paton can be applied to baryons and hybrid baryons, and the full multibead Hamiltonian can, to an excellent approximation, be reduced to independent junction and string motions, with the interaction term giving a minor contribution of at most 5-6% for the lowest parallel frequencies and up to 40% for the perpendicular frequency.

## Method
The authors construct baryons and hybrid baryons in the non-relativistic flux-tube model, motivated by the strong coupling limit of Hamiltonian lattice gauge theory. They model the flux-tube as beads connected by linear potentials, with a junction where three flux-lines meet, and work in the quasi-adiabatic approximation with fixed quark positions. The Hamiltonian is separated into a junction part H_J, a string part H_S, and an interaction term H_int, and the small oscillation approximation is applied to compare the exact numerical solution with the simplified Hamiltonian where H_int is set to zero.

## Key numbers
- String tension: 0.18 GeV²
- Quark mass: 0.33 GeV
- Ratio of junction mass to bead mass: 1
- Triad length: 2.5 GeV⁻¹
- Exact parallel frequencies (lowest): 0.607 GeV and 0.607 GeV
- Exact perpendicular frequency (lowest): 0.828 GeV
- Junction frequencies with H_int=0, parallel: 0.614 GeV and 0.614 GeV
- Junction frequency with H_int=0, perpendicular: 0.869 GeV
- Deviation ε for low parallel frequency: 1%
- Deviation ε for high parallel frequency: 1%
- Deviation ε for perpendicular frequency: 5%
- Maximum ε for parallel frequencies over parameter variations: ≤ 5% and ≤ 6%
- Maximum ε for perpendicular frequency over parameter variations: ≤ 40%
- Quark mass varied up to: 1.5 GeV (charm quark mass)
- Ratio of junction to bead mass varied up to: 10
- Triad lengths varied from: 0.5 to 5 GeV⁻¹

## Context
This work extends the Isgur-Paton flux-tube model (Isgur and Paton, 1985), which was previously applied to hybrid mesons and shown consistent with lattice gauge theory predictions, to the baryon sector. Prior constructions of hybrid baryons existed only in the MIT bag model (Barnes and Close, 1983; Golowich, Haqq and Karl, 1983; Carlson and Hansson, 1983). The results enable predictions for three low-lying hybrid baryon states corresponding to junction vibrations along three perpendicular axes, which are relevant to the excited baryon resonance program at TJNAF (Jefferson Lab) below 2.2 GeV.
