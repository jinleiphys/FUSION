# Interference between Meson Exchange and One-Body Currents in Quasielastic Electron Scattering

> In this work, we present a detailed analysis of the interference between meson exchange currents (MEC) and one-body currents in quasielastic electron scattering, with a focus on the sign of this interference in the transverse response for one-particle emission. We prove that the interference of both the Delta and pion-in-flight currents with the one-body current is negative, leading to a partial cancellation with the seagull current. This is mathematically demonstrated within the framework of the Fermi gas model. By comparing these interferences across various independent particle models, both relativistic and non-relativistic, our results indicate that all studied models display the same behavior. This consistency suggests that the interference is negative in models that do not incorporate tensor correlations in the nuclear wave function.

## Key claim

The paper proves that the interference between the Delta and pion-in-flight meson exchange currents with the one-body current in the transverse response for one-particle-one-hole excitations is negative, leading to partial cancellation with the positive seagull current, but this result holds specifically for models that do not incorporate tensor correlations in the nuclear wave function.

## Method

The authors perform a non-relativistic reduction of a relativistic meson exchange current model derived from pion production Lagrangians, obtaining standard Riska-type operators for seagull, pion-in-flight, and Delta currents. They then compute the one-body-two-body interference transverse response in the Fermi gas model by evaluating 1p1h matrix elements with explicit spin sums, and compare these results across multiple independent particle models including relativistic Fermi gas, mean field, plane wave approximation, spectral function, relativistic mean field, and Dirac-equation based shell model.

## Key numbers

- The low momentum transfer regime considered is around q ~ 500 MeV/c or lower
- The pion-nucleon coupling constant used is f^2 = 1, and the pion-nucleon-Delta coupling constant is f* = 2.13 f
- The Delta width at rest is Gamma_0 = 120 MeV
- The cutoff parameter Lambda for the pion-nucleon and pion-nucleon-Delta form factors is typically chosen around 1300 MeV
- The Delta vector form factor uses C_3^V = 2.13/(1 - Q^2/M_V^2)^2 * 1/(1 - Q^2/4M_V^2)

## Context

This work extends previous calculations by Amaro et al. (1994, 2002, 2003) and Casale et al. (2023) that found small negative MEC effects in independent particle models, and directly addresses recent contradictory findings by Fraga et al. (2023, 2025) and Lovato et al. (2023) that reported large positive interference without tensor correlations. The paper also connects to Fabrocini (1997) who showed positive effects in correlated basis function theory due to tensor correlations, and to Carlson et al. (2002) who found enhancement in Green's Function Monte Carlo calculations that include complete sets of final states. The results enable clarification of whether discrepancies arise from fundamental differences in nuclear dynamics modeling or from specific approximations used in recent calculations.
