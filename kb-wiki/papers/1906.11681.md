# Hyperon-nucleon interaction within chiral effective field theory revisited

> The $螞N$ and $危N$ interactions are considered at next-to-leading order in SU(3) chiral effective field theory. Different options for the low-energy constants that determine the strength of the contact interactions are explored. Two variants are analysed in detail which yield equivalent results for $螞N$ and $危N$ scattering observables but differ in the strength of the $螞N \to 危N$ transition potential. The influence of this difference on predictions for light hypernuclei and on the properties of the $螞$ and $危$ hyperons in nuclear matter is investigated and discussed. The effect of the variation in the potential strength of the $螞N$-$危N$ coupling (also called $螞-危$ conversion) is found to be moderate for the considered $^3_螞\rm H$ and $^4_螞\rm He$ hypernuclei but sizable in case of the matter properties. Further, the size of three-body forces and their relation to different approaches to hypernuclear interactions is discussed.

# Hyperon-nucleon interaction within chiral effective field theory revisited

## Key claim
The authors demonstrate that an alternative set of low-energy constants (LECs) for the $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N$ interactions, constrained by SU(3) symmetry relations to the $NN$ sector for $S$-waves, yields scattering observables practically indistinguishable from the original NLO13 fit, but produces qualitatively different predictions for hyperon in-medium properties, with the new NLO19 potential being considerably more attractive for the $\Lambda$ in nuclear matter.

## Method
The $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N$ interactions were constructed at next-to-leading order (NLO) in SU(3) chiral effective field theory, with contact terms fixed by imposing SU(3) symmetry relations between $YN$ and $NN$ systems for $S$-wave LECs, using $NN$ values from Petschauer (2016). The resulting potential was inserted into a regularized coupled-channels Lippmann-Schwinger equation, and binding energies for light hypernuclei were computed via Faddeev and Yakubovsky equations in momentum space, while in-medium properties were evaluated within the Brueckner reaction-matrix formalism using the Bethe-Goldstone equation.

## Key numbers
- $\chi^2$ for the 36 low-energy $\Lambda N$ and $\Sigma N$ data points: 16.0 to 18.1 for NLO19 (cutoff range 500-650 MeV), compared to 15.7 to 16.8 for NLO13
- $\Lambda p$ singlet scattering length $a_s^{\Lambda p}$: -2.90 to -2.91 fm for both NLO13 and NLO19 across cutoff values
- $\Lambda p$ triplet scattering length $a_t^{\Lambda p}$: -1.40 to -1.52 fm for NLO19, compared to -1.51 to -1.61 fm for NLO13
- $\Sigma^+ p$ singlet scattering length $a_s^{\Sigma^+ p}$: -3.43 to -3.90 fm for NLO19, compared to -3.46 to -3.60 fm for NLO13
- $\Sigma N$ triplet scattering length (isospin 1/2) real part: 0.95 to 2.29 fm for NLO19, compared to 2.06 to 2.61 fm for NLO13
- $\Sigma N$ triplet scattering length (isospin 1/2) imaginary part: -4.77 to -3.39 fm for NLO19, compared to -3.59 to -2.89 fm for NLO13
- $^3S_1$ LEC $C^{10^*}$ from $np$ phase shifts more than a factor 5 smaller than those determined in the 2013 $YN$ study
- $^1S_0$ LEC $C^{27}$ values from $NN$ and $YN$ fits coincide within 5-10%

## Context
This work revisits the chiral EFT hyperon-nucleon potential of Haidenbauer (2013) (NLO13) by imposing stricter SU(3) symmetry constraints on $S$-wave contact terms, following the scheme demonstrated for the $^1S_0$ partial wave in Haidenbauer (2014). The new NLO19 potential yields scattering observables equivalent to NLO13 but predicts a $\Lambda$ single-particle potential in nuclear matter that is considerably more attractive and becomes repulsive at much higher densities, in contrast to the earlier EFT interaction. This has implications for the hyperon puzzle in neutron stars, where the original NLO13 potential showed repulsion at densities of two-to-three times normal nuclear matter, a feature promising for reconciling hyperon formation with observed neutron star masses.
