# Heavy baryon spectroscopy in a quark-diquark approach

> We report progress on calculations of the heavy-light baryons $Σ_c$ and $Λ_c$ and their excitations with $J^P = 1/2^+$ using functional methods. We employ a covariant quark-diquark approach, where the interaction amounts to a quark exchange between quarks and diquarks and the ingredients are determined from the quark level. A partial-wave analysis reveals the presence of orbital angular momentum components in terms of p waves, which are non-relativistically suppressed.

# Heavy baryon spectroscopy in a quark-diquark approach

## Key claim
The quark-diquark Bethe-Salpeter approach, previously successful for light and strange baryons, can be extended to singly-charmed baryons $\Sigma_c$ and $\Lambda_c$ with $J^P = 1/2^+$, but all masses above approximately 2.4 GeV are extrapolated rather than directly calculated, and the heavy-light diquark masses require readjustment of the diquark normalization fitted to the $\Sigma_c$ ground state.

## Method
The authors solve a covariant quark-diquark Bethe-Salpeter equation (BSE) where the interaction kernel is a quark exchange between quarks and diquarks. The quark propagators, diquark propagators, and diquark amplitudes are precomputed from their Dyson-Schwinger equations (DSEs) and BSEs in a rainbow-ladder truncation using the Maris-Tandy interaction. For the heavy-light diquarks $[nc]$ and $\{nc\}$, complex conjugate eigenvalues in the BSE spectrum are resolved via spectral reconstruction, and the diquark masses are extrapolated to their onshell values by altering the coupling, with the diquark normalization then readjusted by fitting to the $\Sigma_c$ ground state.

## Key numbers
- The $\Sigma_c(2455)$ appears as the nucleon-like ground state in the $1/2^+$ channel, and the $\Lambda_c(2286)$ appears as the nucleon-like ground state in its channel.
- Both $\Lambda_c$ and $\Sigma_c$ ground states feature close to 20% $p$-wave contributions (relativistic orbital angular momentum components), compared to approximately 33% $p$-wave admixture in the nucleon ground state.
- All baryon masses above approximately 2.4 GeV are extrapolated results, with error bars shown in the spectrum figure.
- The $\Lambda_c$ has similarly strong scalar-diquark $[nn]c$ and axialvector $\{nc\}n$ components, while the $\Sigma_c$ is dominated by the scalar $[nc]n$ component with smaller axialvector contributions.

## Context
This work extends the quark-diquark approach of Eichmann (2016) from light baryons to the heavy-light sector, following earlier studies of strange baryons by Fischer (2017) and Eichmann (2018). It complements three-quark Faddeev equation studies of charm and bottom baryons by Qin (2018, 2019) using an equal spacing scheme and by Yin (2021) using a contact-interaction approach, but provides the first direct covariant calculation of singly-charmed baryons with the same microscopic ingredients. The results enable future extension to baryons with different quantum numbers and quark content ($nsc$, $ssc$, $ncc$, $scc$, $ccc$), generalization to the three-quark Faddeev equation, and systematic implementation of kernels beyond rainbow-ladder truncation.
