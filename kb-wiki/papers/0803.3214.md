# Robustness and Infrared Sensitivity of Various Observables in the Application of AdS/CFT to Heavy Ion Collisions

> We investigate the robustness with respect to nonconformality of five properties of strongly coupled plasmas that have been calculated in N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) theory at nonzero temperature, motivated by the goal of understanding phenomena in heavy ion collisions. (The properties are the jet quenching parameter, the velocity dependence of screening, and the drag and transverse and longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficients for a heavy quark pulled through the plasma.) We use a toy model in which nonconformality is introduced via a one-parameter deformation of the AdS black hole dual to the hot N=4 SYM plasma. Upon introducing a degree of nonconformality comparable to that seen in lattice calculations of QCD at temperatures a few times that of the crossover to quark-gluon plasma, we find that the jet quenching parameter is affected by the nonconformality by at most 30%, the screening length is affected by at most 20%, but the drag and diffusion coefficients for a slowly moving heavy quark can be modified by as much as 80%. However, four of the five properties that we investigate become completely insensitive to the nonconformality in the limit v -> 1. The exception is the jet quenching parameter, which is "infrared sensitive" even at v=1, where it is defined. It is the only high-velocity observable that we investigate which is sensitive to properties of the medium at infrared energy scales proportional to T, namely the scales where the quark-gluon plasma of QCD can be strongly coupled. The other four quantities all probe only scales that are larger than T by a factor that diverges as v -> 1, namely scales where the N=4 SYM plasma can be strongly coupled but the quark-gluon plasma of QCD is not.

## Key claim

The paper investigates how five observables computed in the conformal N=4 supersymmetric Yang-Mills (SYM) plasma change when nonconformality is introduced via a toy model, finding that the jet quenching parameter is affected by at most 30% and the screening length by at most 20%, while the drag and diffusion coefficients for a slowly moving heavy quark can change by as much as 80%; however, in the high-velocity limit v approaches 1, all quantities except the jet quenching parameter become completely insensitive to the nonconformality.

## Method

The authors deform the AdS black hole metric dual to the hot N=4 SYM plasma by a one-parameter exponential factor exp(29 c z^2 / 20) in the string frame metric, where c is a dimensionful parameter controlling the degree of nonconformality. They then compute five observables (jet quenching parameter, screening length, drag coefficient, and transverse and longitudinal momentum diffusion coefficients) using the standard AdS/CFT prescription of evaluating extremal string worldsheets and Wilson loops in the deformed background. The results are expressed as functions of the dimensionless ratio c/T^2, and the range 0 less than or equal to c/T^2 less than or equal to 4 is explored, with c approximately 0.13 GeV^2 corresponding to the degree of nonconformality seen in lattice QCD thermodynamics at T = 300 MeV.

## Key numbers

* Jet quenching parameter increases by about 14% for c/T^2 = 2 and about 28% for c/T^2 = 4, relative to the conformal value.
* Screening length increases by about 9% for c/T^2 = 2 and about 20% for c/T^2 = 4.
* Drag and diffusion coefficients for a slowly moving heavy quark increase by about 34% for c/T^2 = 2 and about 80% for c/T^2 = 4.
* The toy model parameter c = 0.127 GeV^2 best reproduces QCD thermodynamics, putting the transition temperature at T_c = 170 MeV as in QCD.
* At T = 300 MeV, the range 0.11 GeV^2 less than c less than 0.18 GeV^2 corresponds to 1.2 less than c/T^2 less than 2.0, matching the degree of nonconformality from lattice QCD.

## Context

This work extends the program of using AdS/CFT to compute properties of strongly coupled plasmas relevant to heavy ion collisions, following calculations in conformal N=4 SYM by Liu, Rajagopal, and Wiedemann (2006) for the jet quenching parameter and by Herzog, Gubser, and others (2006) for drag and diffusion coefficients. The toy model metric was introduced by Kajantie, Tahkokallio, and Yee (2006) to mimic QCD thermodynamics. The results show that the jet quenching parameter is uniquely infrared sensitive among the five observables studied, meaning it probes the plasma at energy scales proportional to T where QCD is strongly coupled, while the other four quantities become ultraviolet-dominated at high velocity and thus less reliable for quantitative QCD predictions.
