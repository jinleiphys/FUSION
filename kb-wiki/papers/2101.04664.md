# Simultaneous Monte Carlo analysis of parton densities and fragmentation functions

> We perform a comprehensive new Monte Carlo analysis of high-energy lepton-lepton, lepton-hadron and hadron-hadron scattering data to simultaneously determine parton distribution functions (PDFs) in the proton and parton to hadron fragmentation functions (FFs). The analysis includes all available semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering and single-inclusive $e^+ e^-$ annihilation data for pions, kaons and unidentified charged hadrons, which allows the flavor dependence of the fragmentation functions to be constrained. Employing a new multi-step fitting strategy and more flexible parametrizations for both PDFs and FFs, we assess the impact of different data sets on sea quark densities, and confirm the previously observed suppression of the strange quark distribution. The new fit, which we refer to as "JAM20-SIDIS", will allow for improved studies of universality of parton correlation functions, including transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distributions, across a wide variety of process, and the matching of collinear to TMD factorization descriptions.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first simultaneous and overconstrained fit of unpolarized parton distribution functions (PDFs) and fragmentation functions (FFs) that utilizes both charged hadron production in semi-inclusive deep-inelastic scattering (SIDIS) and single-inclusive e+e- annihilation (SIA), but the authors hedge that the analysis is performed consistently only to order αs in the QCD coupling and that the universality of collinear factorization at moderate Q2 in SIDIS has had few direct tests.

## Method

The authors performed a global Monte Carlo analysis using Bayesian inference with a multi-step fitting strategy, fitting 87 free parameters for the nonperturbative functions plus 42 normalization parameters across datasets. They solved DGLAP evolution equations analytically in Mellin space at next-to-leading logarithmic accuracy, using the zero-mass variable flavor scheme with input scale μ0 = mc = 1.28 GeV, and computed all hard coefficients at fixed next-to-leading order in perturbative QCD. The analysis included inclusive DIS, Drell-Yan lepton-pair production, SIA, and SIDIS data for pions, kaons, and unidentified charged hadrons, with the SIDIS data from COMPASS on deuterium targets being the critical addition over the previous JAM19 analysis.

## Key numbers

- Total of 129 free parameters fitted: 28 for PDFs, 16 for residual charged hadron FFs, 19 for pion FFs, 24 for kaon FFs, and 42 for data set normalizations
- Input scale for PDFs and FFs set at μ0 = mc = 1.28 GeV, with bottom quark mass mb = 4.18 GeV
- Strong coupling boundary condition αs(MZ) = 0.118 at the Z boson mass MZ = 91.18 GeV
- SIA data restricted to range 0.2 < zh < 0.9 to ensure applicability of leading power formalism
- Kinematic cuts of W2 > 10 GeV2 and Q2 > mc2 applied for inclusive DIS data
- Drell-Yan data included for Q2 > 36 GeV2
- Belle and BaBar SIA data have Q ≈ 10.5 GeV, while approximately half of SIA data points have Q ≈ MZ

## Context

This work extends the JAM Collaboration's previous analyses (JAM17 for helicity PDFs, JAM19 for unpolarized PDFs and FFs, and JAM3D20 for TMD observables) by performing the first simultaneous overconstrained fit of unpolarized PDFs and FFs using both SIDIS and SIA data. The analysis was motivated by recent tensions found between standard PDF/FF sets and fixed-order perturbative QCD calculations in processes including SIDIS (Gonzalez-Hernandez, 2018; Wang, 2019), Drell-Yan (Bacchetta, 2019), and SIA into wide-angle hadron pairs (Moffat, 2019). The new JAM20-SIDIS fit enables improved studies of universality of parton correlation functions, including transverse momentum dependent distributions, and the matching of collinear to TMD factorization descriptions.
