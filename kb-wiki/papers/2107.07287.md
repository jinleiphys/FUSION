# Checking Non-Flow Assumptions and Results via PHENIX Published Correlations in $p$$+$$p$, $p$$+$Au, $d$$+$Au, $^3$He$+$Au at $\sqrt{s_{NN}}$ = 200 GeV

> Recently the PHENIX Collaboration has made available two-particle correlation Fourier coefficients for multiple detector combinations in minimum bias p+p and 0-5% central p+Au, d+Au, 3He+Au collisions at 200 GeV [1]. Using these coefficients for three sets of two-particle correlations, azimuthal anisotropy coefficients $v_2$ and $v_3$ are extracted for midrapidity charged hadrons as a function of transverse momentum. In this paper, we use the available coefficients to explore various non-flow hypotheses as well as compare the results with theoretical model calculations. The non-flow methods fail basic closure tests with AMPT and PYTHIA/ANGANTYR, particularly when including correlations with particles in the low multiplicity light-projectile going direction. In data, the non-flow adjusted $v_2$ results are modestly lower in p+Au and the adjusted $v_3$ results are more significantly higher in p+Au and d+Au. However, the resulting higher values for the ratio $v_3/v_2$ in p+Au at RHIC compared to p+Pb at the LHC is additional evidence for a significant over-correction. Incorporating these additional checks, the conclusion that these flow coefficients are dominated by initial geometry coupled with final-state interactions (e.g.~hydrodynamic expansion of quark-gluon plasma) remains true, and explanations based on initial-state glasma are ruled out. The detailed balance between intrinsic and fluctuation-driven geometry and the exact role of weakly versus strongly-coupled pre-hydrodynamic evolution remains an open question for triangular flow, requiring further theoretical and experimental investigation.

## Key claim

The non-flow subtraction methods (template, c1-method, ZYAM) fail basic closure tests with AMPT and PYTHIA/ANGANTYR Monte Carlo calculations, particularly when including correlations with particles in the low multiplicity light-projectile going direction; the resulting higher values for the ratio v3/v2 in p+Au at RHIC compared to p+Pb at the LHC is additional evidence for a significant over-correction from these methods.

## Method

The authors used the PHENIX Collaboration's published two-particle correlation Fourier coefficients (c_n) from three sets of detector combinations (BBCS-FVTXS-CNT, FVTXS-CNT-FVTXN, BBCS-CNT-FVTXN) to extract v2 and v3 for midrapidity charged hadrons as a function of transverse momentum in minimum bias p+p and 0-5% central p+Au, d+Au, and 3He+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV. They applied three non-flow subtraction methods (template fit method from ATLAS, c1-method, and ZYAM method) using p+p collisions as the low-multiplicity reference, and tested these methods for closure using AMPT and PYTHIA/ANGANTYR Monte Carlo simulations with the same kinematic selections.

## Key numbers

* Raw v2 in p+Au from BBCS-FVTXS-CNT combination is modestly lower than template-adjusted v2; template-adjusted p+Au results are consistent with raw results within original non-flow estimated uncertainty for pT < 2 GeV
* Template-adjusted v3 in p+Au and d+Au are higher than raw v3 beyond the original non-flow estimated uncertainties
* Raw v2 in d+Au relative to 3He+Au is 5-10% lower, independent of pT regardless of template adjustment
* Raw v3 in p+Au and d+Au are consistently 60% lower than 3He+Au and flat with pT within uncertainties; template-adjusted v3 are only 15-30% lower than 3He+Au
* Template-adjusted results for different detector combinations show 15-25% relative difference remaining in p+Au v2
* Raw p+Au v3/v2 ratio at RHIC is lower by a factor of 2-3 compared to ATLAS p+Pb at sqrt(s_NN) = 5 TeV
* AMPT raw v2 values are almost a factor of two higher than the PHENIX data

## Context

This work extends the PHENIX Collaboration's published results on v2 and v3 in p+Au, d+Au, and 3He+Au collisions (PHENIX, 2018) by using the newly available Fourier coefficients from three sets of two-particle correlations (PHENIX, 2021) to test non-flow subtraction methods. The study contradicts the assumption that standard non-flow methods (template fit from ATLAS, 2019; c1-method; ZYAM from Adare, 2008) reliably correct for non-flow contributions, as demonstrated by closure test failures in AMPT (Lin, 2004) and PYTHIA/ANGANTYR (Bierlich, 2018). The results enable further investigation into the role of pre-hydrodynamic evolution and the balance between intrinsic and fluctuation-driven geometry in small collision systems, with the conclusion that initial-state glasma correlations are ruled out while hydrodynamic explanations remain viable.
