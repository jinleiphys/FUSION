# Partial-wave decomposition of the Faddeev amplitude of a Poincaré-invariant three-fermion bound state

> A partial-wave decomposition of the Faddeev amplitude of a Poincare-invariant three-fermion bound state is presented. Some implications for the properties of the nucleon as relativistic three-quark bound state are discussed. E.g. it is demonstrated that the nucleon is not spherically symmetric.

## Key claim

The nucleon, as a Poincaré-invariant three-quark bound state described by a Faddeev amplitude, is not spherically symmetric due to the highly relativistic motion of quarks within it; this is demonstrated through a partial-wave decomposition in the nucleon's rest frame, without referring to a specific dynamical model.

## Method

The authors perform a partial-wave decomposition of the Faddeev amplitude for a Poincaré-invariant three-fermion bound state in the rest frame, using the Pauli-Lubanski vector to separate orbital and spin angular momentum. They reduce the problem by approximating two-body quark-quark correlations as separable scalar (spin 0) and axialvector (spin 1) diquark correlations, mapping the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev equation to a set of coupled Bethe-Salpeter equations with quark exchange as the interaction. The resulting Bethe-Salpeter wave functions are decomposed into partial waves by applying the squared orbital and spin angular momentum operators.

## Key numbers

- The nucleon's Faddeev amplitude requires a minimum of eight components (reduced from 64) after projection onto positive energy states, grouped into four bispinors with upper and lower components.
- The upper components contain three s-waves (eigenvalue 0 for L²) and one d-wave (eigenvalue 6 for L²); the lower components consist of four p-waves (eigenvalue 2 for L²).
- The scalar diquark correlation contributes two components: one s-wave (L² eigenvalue 0, S² eigenvalue 3/4) and one p-wave (L² eigenvalue 2, S² eigenvalue 3/4).
- The axialvector diquark correlation contributes six components: two s-waves (L² eigenvalue 0, S² eigenvalue 3/4), two p-waves (L² eigenvalue 2, S² eigenvalue 3/4), one d-wave (L² eigenvalue 6, S² eigenvalue 15/4), and one p-wave (L² eigenvalue 2, S² eigenvalue 15/4).
- A prediction from related calculations (Holl 2005) is a zero of the proton's electric form factor at Q² ≈ 8 GeV².

## Context

This work extends the Poincaré-covariant Faddeev approach developed by Oettel (1998, 1999, 2000) for three-quark bound states, providing a systematic partial-wave decomposition of the nucleon's Faddeev amplitude. The analysis demonstrates that the nucleon's wave function contains s-, p-, and d-wave components even in the rest frame, contradicting a naive spherical picture. This decomposition enables calculations of nucleon electromagnetic form factors (Oettel 2002, Alkofer 2004, Holl 2005) that agree with experimental data at larger momentum transfers, while the failure at lower Q² is attributed to missing mesonic contributions.
