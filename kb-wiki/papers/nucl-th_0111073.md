# Hypercentral constituent quark model and isospin dependence

> The constituent quark model based on a hypercentral approach takes into account three-body force effects and standard two-body potential contributions. The quark potential contains a hypercentral interaction, to which a hyperfine term is added. While the hypercentral potential supplies good values for the centroid energies of the resonance multiplets and a realistic set of quark wave functions, the hyperfine splittings are sometimes not sufficient to account for the observed masses. In this work we have introduced an improved form of the hyperfine interaction and an isospin dependent quark potential. The resulting description of the baryon spectrum is very good, also for the Roper resonance, specially thanks to the flavour dependent interaction.

# Hypercentral constituent quark model and isospin dependence

## Key claim

The hypercentral constituent quark model, which previously gave good centroid energies but insufficient hyperfine splittings for the Roper resonance and higher states, can reproduce the baryon spectrum much more accurately when an isospin-dependent quark potential is added as a perturbative term, with the spin-isospin interaction alone accounting for about 50% of the N-Δ mass difference.

## Method

The authors start from the hypercentral constituent quark model (hCQM), in which the quark potential depends only on the hyperradius x and contains a Coulomb-like term plus a linear confining term, with a standard hyperfine interaction treated as a perturbation. They replace the δ-like hyperfine interaction with a Gaussian-smeared two-body form, then add two new perturbative terms: a pure isospin-isospin interaction and a spin-isospin interaction, both also with Gaussian smearing. The complete Hamiltonian is diagonalized to compute the baryon spectrum, and the parameters are fitted to the observed 3-star and 4-star resonances.

## Key numbers

- The hypercentral potential parameters fitted to the spectrum are τ = 4.59 and α = 1.61 fm⁻² (for the model without isospin dependence).
- For the smeared hyperfine interaction alone (Eq. 9), the fitted parameters are α = 1.58 fm⁻², τ = 4.98, A_S = 38.4 fm², σ_S = 0.8 fm.
- For the smeared hyperfine interaction depending on hyperradius (Eq. 10), the fitted parameters are α = 1.49 fm⁻², τ = 5.01, B_S = 196.4 fm², Λ_S = 1.6 fm.
- The N-Δ mass difference in the full model receives contributions of about 35% from the hyperfine term, 50% from the spin-isospin term, and 15% from the isospin term.
- The model predicts the first three P₁₁ states at 1463 MeV, 1752 MeV, and 1828 MeV, compared to the PDG values of N(1440) at 1439 ± 19 MeV and N(1710) at 1729 ± 16 MeV.
- The nucleon r.m.s. radius from the original hCQM is 0.46 fm.

## Context

This work extends the hypercentral constituent quark model of Giannini, Santopinto, and Vassallo (previous work) by adding isospin-dependent terms motivated by the Gursey-Radicati mass formula, the algebraic approach of Bijker, Iachello, and Leviatan, and the chiral constituent quark model of Glozman, Riska, and collaborators. The improved description of the Roper resonance and higher states addresses a known shortcoming of the hCQM, and the authors note that the different predictions for 1-star and 2-star states among various constituent quark models can be tested against new data expected from TJNAF experiments.
