# A Review of Pentaquark Calculations on the Lattice

> We review lattice calculations of pentaquarks and discuss issues pertaining to interpolation fields, distinguishing the signal of pentaquarks from those of the KN scattering states, chiral symmetry, and ghost state contaminations.

## Key claim

Despite some positive claims in the literature, after examining interpolation fields, scattering state contamination, chiral symmetry, and ghost state issues, there is no convincing evidence that any lattice calculation has observed a pentaquark state; the authors note that "absence of evidence is not evidence of absence."

## Method

The authors review the dozen or so lattice pentaquark calculations performed up to 2005, analyzing the choice of interpolation fields (including the nucleon-kaon product operator, the diquark-diquark-antiquark operator, and the Jaffe-Wilczek derivative operator) and showing they are linearly related through Fiertz transformations. They examine methods to distinguish pentaquark signals from KN scattering states, including volume dependence of spectral weights, hybrid boundary conditions, and variational approaches with multiple operators, as well as the contamination from quenched ghost states (KNη') in the 1/2+ channel.

## Key numbers

- On a 16^3 × 28 lattice with overlap fermions at lattice spacing 0.2 fm and pion mass 633 MeV, the three interpolation fields (χ1, χ2, χ3) in the 1/2- channel do not give the same mass at short time separation (t ≤ 7, or 1.4 fm); only at t ~ 12 (2.4 fm) do they reach the KN threshold.
- The spectral weight of χ3 in the 1/2- channel is somewhat smaller than those of χ1 and χ2, but not orders of magnitude smaller; all three give the same weights in the 1/2+ channel.
- The spectral weight of the S11 state from the lower component of the nucleon correlation function decreases like m^2 in the pion mass range from 250 MeV to 350 MeV.
- Volume dependence studies with overlap fermions on 16^3 × 28 and 12^3 × 28 lattices with pion mass as low as 180 MeV showed that ground states of both 1/2- and 1/2+ channels are scattering states.
- One calculation (Ch05) claimed a pentaquark in the 1/2+ channel that approaches the KN threshold when extrapolated to the chiral limit, but this contradicts all other calculations; the overlap fermion results were found to be higher than Wilson fermion results by up to ~300 MeV for mπ heavier than ~550 MeV in the positive parity channel.
- Ground state masses in the 1/2- channel from 6 different calculations with both overlap and Wilson fermions lay on top of each other in the pion mass range from 420 MeV to 890 MeV.

## Context

This review extends the discussion of pentaquark lattice calculations following the experimental discovery of Θ+(1540) by Nakano (2005). It addresses the challenge that pentaquark interpolation fields inevitably couple to KN scattering states, a problem not confronted in prior lattice calculations of stable particles like the nucleon, pion, and kaon. The paper builds on the diquark-diquark-antiquark picture of Jaffe and Wilczek (2004) and the chiral Skyrme model prediction of the anti-decuplet pentaquark by Diakonov, Petrov, and Polyakov (1997). It enables future work by specifying that realistic dynamical fermion calculations at physical quark masses with variational methods and ghost state removal are needed to settle the question.
