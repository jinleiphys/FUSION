# Phenomenological model of the Kaonic Nuclear Cluster K^-pnn in the ground state

> A phenomenological model, proposed for the Kaonic Nuclear Cluster (KNC) K^-pp, is applied to the theoretical analysis of the KNC K^-pnn (or S^0). The theoretical values of the binding energy and the width are equal to epsilon_(S^0) = - 197 MeV and Gamma_(S^0) = 16 MeV. They agree well with the experimental data epsilon^(exp)_(S^0) = - 194.0(+ 1.5/- 4.4) MeV and Gamma^(exp)_(S^0) < 21 MeV} (PLB 597, 263 (2004)) and the theoretical values epsilon_(S^0) = - 191 MeV and Gamma_(S^0) = 13 MeV, obtained by Akaishi and Yamazaki within the potential model approach.

## Key claim

The phenomenological model previously applied to the KNC K^-pp is extended to the KNC K^-pnn (S^0), yielding a binding energy of -197 MeV and width of 16 MeV that agree with experimental data and with the potential model predictions of Akaishi and Yamazaki, though the model predicts a nuclear matter density three times smaller than their result.

## Method

The authors use a phenomenological quantum field theoretic model with a chiral Lagrangian employing non-linear realization of chiral SU(3)×SU(3) symmetry and derivative meson-baryon couplings. They calculate the binding energy from the Weinberg-Tomozawa term for K^-pnn scattering in the tree approximation to leading order in the large N_C and chiral expansion, and compute partial widths for decay channels using the same framework with oscillator-form wave functions for the K^-pnn system.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of S^0: epsilon_{S^0} = -197 MeV (theoretical), compared to experimental epsilon^{exp}_{S^0} = -194.0(+1.5/-4.4) MeV
- Total width of S^0: Gamma_{S^0} = 16 MeV (theoretical), compared to experimental Gamma^{exp}_{S^0} < 21 MeV
- Partial width for S^0 → Σ^- p n: Gamma = 15 MeV
- Partial width for S^0 → Σ^- d: Gamma = 1 MeV
- Energy excess for S^0 → Σ^- p n decay: epsilon = 44 MeV (using m_N = 940 MeV, m_Sigma = 1193 MeV)
- Nuclear matter density at center of K^-pnn system: n_{S^0}(0) = 0.53 fm^{-3}, compared to 1.56 fm^{-3} from Akaishi and Yamazaki
- Pion decay constant used: F_pi = 92.4 MeV
- piNN coupling constant: g_{piNN} = 13.3
- Oscillator frequency from fit to Lambda(1405) width: omega = 64 MeV

## Context

This work extends the phenomenological model of Ivanov et al. (2005) for the KNC K^-pp to the three-body KNC K^-pnn (S^0), which was experimentally discovered by Suzuki et al. (2004) with binding energy -194 MeV and width below 21 MeV. The results agree with the potential model predictions of Akaishi and Yamazaki (epsilon = -191 MeV, Gamma = 13 MeV) but yield a significantly lower nuclear matter density (0.53 fm^{-3} versus 1.56 fm^{-3}), and the approach can be extended to heavier kaonic nuclear clusters such as K^-ppn and K^-ppp.
