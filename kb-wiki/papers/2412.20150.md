# The Spectra of $p\barΛ$ and $p\barΣ$ Hexaquark States

> Motivated by the observation of the $J^P = 1^+$ resonance $X(2085)$ in the $p\barΛ$ system by the BESIII collaboration, we studied the molecular states of hexaquarks $p\barΛ$ and $p\barΣ$ with baryon-antibaryon structures within the framework of the QCD sum rules. Non-perturbative contributions up to dimension 13 were considered in our analysis. The results indicate the existence of six possible molecular states $p\barΛ$ and $p\barΣ$, with quantum numbers $J^{P}=0^{-}, 0^{+}, 1^{-}$. Consequently, the current sum rule results do not support the interpretation of $X(2085)$ as a $p\barΛ$ or $p\barΣ$ molecular state. On the other hand, we find that the masses of the proposed $p\barΛ$ and $p\barΣ$ structures with $J^{P} = 1^{-}$ are in the vicinity of observed $X(2075)$, which implies that the nature of this state needs more invistigations. Moreover, the possible decay modes of the concerned hexaquark states are analyzed.

## Key claim

The QCD sum rule analysis, including non-perturbative contributions up to dimension 13, finds six possible p-bar-Lambda and p-bar-Sigma molecular states with J^P = 0^-, 0^+, 1^- but does not support interpreting the J^P = 1^+ resonance X(2085) as a p-bar-Lambda or p-bar-Sigma molecular state, or at least not as its main component; however, the masses of the J^P = 1^- p-bar-Lambda and p-bar-Sigma structures lie near the observed X(2075), suggesting its nature may involve a mixture of tetraquark and hexaquark states.

## Method

The authors used QCD sum rules (QCDSR) to study baryon-antibaryon type hexaquark molecular states, constructing interpolating currents for p-bar-Lambda and p-bar-Sigma systems with quantum numbers J^P = 0^-, 0^+, 1^-, 1^+. They evaluated two-point correlation functions including non-perturbative contributions up to dimension 13 in the operator product expansion (OPE), using two independent types of baryonic interpolating currents for the baryon octet. The mass spectra were extracted by matching the OPE side to the phenomenological side via Borel transform and sum rule analysis.

## Key numbers

- For the p-bar-Lambda system with Type-1 current, the masses obtained are: 2.14 +/- 0.13 GeV for J^P = 0^-, 2.12 +/- 0.12 GeV for J^P = 0^+, and 2.07 +/- 0.11 GeV for J^P = 1^-.
- For the p-bar-Lambda system with Type-2 current, the masses obtained are: 2.13 +/- 0.12 GeV for J^P = 0^-, 2.11 +/- 0.12 GeV for J^P = 0^+, and 2.07 +/- 0.11 GeV for J^P = 1^-.
- For the p-bar-Sigma system with Type-1 current, the masses obtained are: 2.16 +/- 0.12 GeV for J^P = 0^-, 2.14 +/- 0.12 GeV for J^P = 0^+, and 2.09 +/- 0.11 GeV for J^P = 1^-.
- For the p-bar-Sigma system with Type-2 current, the masses obtained are: 2.15 +/- 0.12 GeV for J^P = 0^-, 2.13 +/- 0.12 GeV for J^P = 0^+, and 2.09 +/- 0.11 GeV for J^P = 1^-.
- The experimentally observed X(2085) has mass 2085 MeV and J^P = 1^+, while X(2075) has mass 2075 MeV.

## Context

This work extends previous QCD sum rule studies of baryonium states (Wan 2021, Wang 2006) that successfully explained structures like X(1835) and X(1840), and follows quark model calculations of p-bar-Lambda and p-bar-Sigma mass spectra (Ding 2005, Huang 2011). The results contradict the interpretation of X(2085) as a p-bar-Lambda or p-bar-Sigma molecular state, while suggesting X(2075) may have a mixed tetraquark-hexaquark nature, motivating further experimental and theoretical investigation of these near-threshold structures.
