# Proca equation and vector field quantization in rotating system

> A strong background field will change the vacuum structure and the proper basis of a system drastically in both classical and quantum mechanics, e.g. the Landau levels in a background magnetic field. The situation is the same for the rotating case. In such a system the usual set of plane-wave states would no longer be suitable as a starting point of perturbation. Alternatively and straightforwardly in a rapidly and globally rotating system, it is better to reformulate the perturbation computation in principle. In this work we will complete the first step for the spin-1 field, which includes solving the Proca equation in present of a background rotation and complete its canonical quantization. It will be shown that because of the symmetry the eigen states are actually the same as the ones of Maxwell equations in cylindrical coordinate. The propagator as well as the near-central approximation will be obtained by considering the vorticity areas are so small in the relativistic QGP.

# Proca equation and vector field quantization in rotating system

## Key claim
The authors solve the Proca equation for a massive spin-1 vector field in a uniformly rotating background and complete its canonical quantization, showing that the eigenstates are the same as those of Maxwell equations in cylindrical coordinates. The rotation effects manifest as an energy shift of \(E_{n k_t k_z} = E_k - n\omega\) and serve as an additional chemical potential in the propagator poles, rather than a straightforward energy modification.

## Method
The authors introduce a rotating metric and tetrads to transform the Proca Lagrangian density into a local inertial frame, where rotation effects appear as additional interaction terms. They solve the resulting equations of motion in cylindrical coordinates with the angular velocity along the \(z\)-axis, obtaining transverse electric (TE), transverse magnetic (TM), and longitudinal (L) mode solutions. Canonical quantization is performed, and the propagator is constructed from the mode expansions, with a near-central expansion performed by setting one spacetime point at the origin.

## Key numbers
- The eigen energies for the vector field in the rotating background are \(E_{n k_t k_z} = E_k - n\omega\), where \(E_k = \sqrt{k_t^2 + k_z^2}\) and \(n\) is the azimuthal quantum number.
- The propagator poles are located at \((k_0 - n\omega)^2 - E_k^2 + i\eta\), indicating rotation acts as a chemical potential.
- In the near-central expansion (\(r=0\), \(\rho \rightarrow 0\)), only the \(n = 0, \pm 1\) modes contribute because only \(J_0(0) \neq 0\).
- The normalization condition for the eigenstates is \(\int \rho d\rho d\phi dz A^\mu_{\lambda} A_{\mu, \lambda'} = -(2\pi)^2 \delta_{mn} \delta(k_z - p_z) \frac{1}{k_t} \delta(k_t - p_t)\).

## Context
This work extends previous studies of quantum fields in rotating systems, which have been motivated by measurements of vector meson polarization at RHIC and LHC (Bedangadas Mohanty for the ALICE Collaboration, 2017; Zhou, 2019; Mohanty, 2020). The authors note that while fermionic fields in rotating backgrounds have been studied extensively (Yang, 2017; Sheng, 2019), the gluonic (spin-1) sector has been neglected due to technical difficulties. The approach parallels the treatment of Landau levels in magnetic fields (Fukushima, 2018) and uses cylindrical wave solutions similar to those in Maxwell equations (Chernodub, 2018). This work enables future studies of non-abelian gauge fields and interaction terms in rotating QCD systems, particularly for understanding gluon polarization effects in heavy-ion collisions.
