# The role of nuclear form factors in Dark Matter calculations

> The momentum transfer dependence of the total cross section for elastic scattering of cold dark matter candidates, i.e. lightest supermymmetric particle (LSP), with nuclei is examined. We find that even though the energy transfer is small ($\le 100 KeV$) the momentum transfer can be quite big for large mass of the LSP and heavy nuclei. The total cross section can in such instances be reduced by a factor of about five.

# The role of nuclear form factors in Dark Matter calculations

## Key claim
The total cross section for elastic scattering of the lightest supersymmetric particle (LSP) with nuclei can be reduced by a factor of about five when the momentum transfer dependence of nuclear form factors is included, but this reduction occurs only for heavy nuclei and large LSP masses (around 100 GeV/c²); for light nuclei the modification is small.

## Method
The authors computed the differential and total cross sections for elastic LSP-nucleus scattering using an effective four-fermion interaction Lagrangian that includes vector, axial-vector, and scalar currents. They modeled the nuclear form factor within the harmonic oscillator model, with coefficients computed for light, medium, and heavy nuclei, and evaluated the momentum-transfer-dependent integrals I_ρ(q²₀) and I_ρρ′(q²₀) that modify the cross section. For the spin-dependent contributions, they performed detailed shell-model calculations for ²⁰⁷Pb (described as a 2s₁/₂ neutron hole in ²⁰⁸Pb with configuration mixing from Kuo-Brown G-matrix diagonalization) and for ¹⁹F (assuming a pure SU(3) state with largest symmetry).

## Key numbers
- The total cross section can be reduced by a factor of about five for heavy nuclei and massive LSP.
- The LSP mass is expected in the 10–100 GeV/c² region, with non-relativistic velocities β ≃ 10⁻³.
- The energy transfer to the recoiling nucleus is ≤ 100 keV.
- The reference cross section σ₀ = 0.77 × 10⁻³⁸ cm², defined as (1/2π)(G_F m_p)².
- For ²⁰⁷Pb, the ground state wave function coefficients from Kuo-Brown diagonalization are: C₀ = 0.973350, C₁ = 0.005295, C₂ = −0.006984.
- For ²⁰⁷Pb, the zero-momentum spin matrix elements are Ω₀(0) = −(1/√3)(0.95659) and Ω₁(0) = −(1/√3)(0.83296), showing small and sizable retardation respectively.
- The harmonic oscillator size parameter is b = 1.0 A¹/³ fm.

## Context
This work extends the authors' previous study (Kosmas and Vergados, 1995) on nuclear form factors in the harmonic oscillator model to the context of dark matter detection, providing explicit form factor coefficients for heavy nuclei. It addresses the momentum transfer dependence that had been neglected in earlier four-fermion interaction treatments of LSP-nucleus scattering (references 10–16 in the paper). The results enable more accurate predictions for cryostatic detectors and superconducting granule detectors with targets of about 1 kg, particularly for heavy nuclei such as ²⁰⁷Pb, ⁷²Ge, and ²⁰⁸Pb, and show that spin-dependent form factors cannot be ignored for heavy nuclei and high LSP mass.
