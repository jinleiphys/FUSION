# A reggeized model for eta and eta-prime photoproduction

> A reggeized model for ηand η' photoproduction on the nucleon is presented. In this model, t-channel vector meson exchanges are described in terms of Regge trajectories to comply with the correct high energy behavior. We compare this reggeized model with an isobar model (η-MAID), where the t-channel exchanges are described by ρand ωpoles. Both models contain the same resonance contributions, and describe current γp \to ηp data up to E_γ^{lab} = 2 GeV quite well, but only the reggeized model can be successfully extended to higher energies. For the γp \to η' p reaction, the reggeized model is found to be able to give a satisfactory description. For the differential cross section data from SAPHIR, we find that the observed linear forward rise in cos(theta) near E_γ^{lab} = 1.6 GeV can be well described by the interference of an S11 resonance and the Regge trajectory exchanges without any need for an additional P-wave resonance.

# A reggeized model for eta and eta-prime photoproduction

## Key claim

The reggeized model, which replaces t-channel vector meson poles with Regge trajectories, can describe both η and η' photoproduction data, but only the reggeized model can be successfully extended to higher energies beyond the resonance region (W > 2 GeV). For η' photoproduction specifically, the Regge trajectories are required even at energies near the η' production threshold, unlike for η photoproduction where reggeization is not required up to W ~ 2 GeV.

## Method

The authors constructed a reggeized model for γp → ηp and γp → η'p where t-channel ρ and ω meson exchanges are described by Regge trajectories of the form α(t) = α₀ + α't, with a rotating phase e^(-iπα(t)). The model includes the same nucleon resonance contributions as the isobar model η-MAID, using Breit-Wigner parameterizations for multipoles, but omits Born terms due to the small ηNN and η'NN coupling constants. The hadronic couplings g_{VNN} and κ_{VNN} for the vector mesons were determined by fitting high-energy differential cross section data at Eγ^lab = 4 and 6 GeV from DESY.

## Key numbers

- The Regge trajectory for ρ exchange: α_ρ(t) = 0.55 + 0.8 t/GeV², with signature S = -1
- The Regge trajectory for ω exchange: α_ω(t) = 0.44 + 0.9 t/GeV², with signature S = -1
- Radiative decay widths used: Γ(ρ → ηγ) = 36 keV, Γ(ω → ηγ) = 5.5 keV, Γ(η' → ργ) = 89 keV, Γ(η' → ωγ) = 9.1 keV
- χ²/N_dof = 2.0 for the η-MAID model and 3.9 for the reggeized model fit to η photoproduction data up to Eγ^lab = 2 GeV
- The S11(1535) resonance parameters from the reggeized fit: mass = 1521 MeV, total width = 118 MeV, β_ηN = 50%, A^p_{1/2} = +80 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2}
- The S11(1650) resonance parameters from the reggeized fit: mass = 1635 MeV, total width = 120 MeV, β_ηN = 16%, A^p_{1/2} = +46 × 10⁻³ GeV^{-1/2}
- Damping parameter X = 450 MeV in the reggeized model (compared to 500 MeV in η-MAID)
- Hadronic couplings from fit to high-energy data: g_ρNN = 2.4, κ_ρNN = 3.7; g_ωNN = 9, κ_ωNN = 0

## Context

This work extends the η-MAID isobar model of Chiang (2001) by replacing t-channel ρ and ω pole exchanges with Regge trajectories, following the approach of Guidal (1997) for pion and kaon photoproduction. The reggeized model successfully describes η' photoproduction data from SAPHIR (Plotzke, 1998) that could not be adequately described by the pole model, and shows that the observed linear forward rise in cosθ near Eγ^lab = 1.6 GeV arises from interference between an S11 resonance and Regge trajectory exchanges without requiring an additional P-wave resonance. This enables future analyses of higher-energy η and η' photoproduction data expected from CLAS, GRAAL, and CB-ELSA collaborations.
