# Short-range three-nucleon interaction from A=3 data and its hierarchical structure

> We construct accurate models of three-nucleon (3N) interaction by fitting, in a hybrid phenomenological approach, the low-energy constants parametrizing the subleading 3N contact operators to the triton binding energy, n-d scattering lengths, cross section and polarization observables of p-d scattering at 2 MeV center-of-mass energy. These models lead to a satisfactory description of polarized p-d scattering data in the whole energy range below the deuteron breakup threshold. In particular, the long-standing $A_y$ puzzle seems to be solved thanks to the new terms considered in the 3N force. Two types of hierarchies among the subleading contact operators are also derived, based on the large-$N_c$ counting and on a recently proposed relativistic counting. We test these hierarchies against the same experimental data and show that they are respected at a reasonable level.

# Short-range three-nucleon interaction from A=3 data and its hierarchical structure

## Key claim
The long-standing $A_y$ puzzle in low-energy nucleon-deuteron scattering appears to be resolved by including subleading three-nucleon contact operators (at N4LO in chiral effective field theory), whose low-energy constants are fitted to triton binding energy, n-d scattering lengths, and p-d scattering observables at 2 MeV center-of-mass energy. The authors hedge that this conclusion is based on a hybrid phenomenological approach using the AV18 NN potential plus Urbana IX TNI as the leading Hamiltonian, rather than a fully consistent chiral framework.

## Method
The authors solve the three-nucleon Schrödinger equation using the Hyperspherical Harmonics (HH) expansion method with the Kohn variational principle, computing scattering observables from the resulting S-matrix. They fit the 10 subleading contact LECs (subject to a T=1/2 isospin constraint that reduces the effective number to 9) to the triton binding energy, doublet and quartet n-d scattering lengths, and six p-d scattering observables at 3 MeV proton energy, using the POUNDerS algorithm for minimization. The contact potential is constructed with a Gaussian-like cutoff function and tested for cutoff values between 200 and 500 MeV.

## Key numbers
- $\chi^2$/d.o.f. = 1.7 for all cutoff values (200, 300, 400, 500 MeV) when using AV18+UIX as the leading Hamiltonian
- Triton binding energy fixed to 8.482 MeV in all fits
- Doublet n-d scattering length $a_2$ predicted between 0.635 fm and 0.650 fm across cutoffs (experimental value: $0.645 \pm 0.003 \pm 0.007$ fm)
- Quartet n-d scattering length $a_4$ predicted as 6.32 fm for all cutoffs (experimental value: $6.35 \pm 0.02$ fm)
- Leading contact LEC $e_0$ ranges from -6.193 to -1.174 across cutoffs; subleading LEC $e_7$ (spin-orbit term) ranges from 7.450 at $\Lambda=200$ MeV to 0.815 at $\Lambda=500$ MeV
- Large-$N_c$ constrained fits yield $\chi^2$/d.o.f. between 2.0 and 2.1 across cutoffs
- Relativistic counting constrained fits yield $\chi^2$/d.o.f. between 1.7 and 1.8 across cutoffs

## Context
This work extends the preliminary study of Girlanda (2016) which showed that subleading contact operators could improve description of vector analyzing powers $A_y$ and $iT_{11}$. It addresses the long-standing discrepancy where chiral potentials at N2LO (Epelbaum 2002, Bernard 2008) produced $\chi^2$/d.o.f. values of several hundreds for low-energy N-d observables (Kievsky 2001, Marcucci 2009). The paper also tests two proposed hierarchies for the contact operators: the large-$N_c$ counting of 't Hooft (1974) and Witten (1979) as applied to 3N forces by Phillips (2013), and a relativistic counting proposed by Ren (2018). The results enable future energy-dependent fits and extension to energies above deuteron breakup threshold, as well as determination of the T=3/2 component of the three-nucleon force from additional observables.
