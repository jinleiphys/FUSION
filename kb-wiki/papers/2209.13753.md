# Light-Front Holography Model of the EMC Effect

> A new two-component model of the EMC effect based on Light-Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD) is presented. The model suggests the EMC effect is the result of the nuclear potential breaking SU(6) symmetry. The model separates the $F_2^A$ nuclear structure function into two parts: a free contribution, involving the addition of proton and neutron structure functions weighted by the number of protons and neutrons respectively, and a nuclear/medium modified contribution that involves a universal function for all nuceli. Further, the model displays a connection with the correlation between the size of the EMC effect and the SRC pair density, $a_2$ - extracted from kinematic plateaus at around $x$ > 1 in inclusive quasi-elastic (QE) scattering.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the EMC effect arises from the nuclear potential breaking SU(6) symmetry, which modifies the probabilities of finding spin-up or spin-down quarks in nucleons; this modification is parameterized by two free parameters, δr_p and δr_n, that depend on mass number A and atomic number Z, and the model explicitly connects these parameters to the virtuality of bound nucleons.

## Method

The authors construct a two-component model of the nucleon (a large blob-like configuration and a small point-like configuration) and use Light-Front Holographic QCD (LFHQCD) to obtain free nucleon valence parton distribution functions (PDFs) from nucleon form factors, expressed as superpositions of twist-3 and twist-4 PDFs (q_3 and q_4). They introduce medium effects by modifying the effective charges in the form factor expressions through the parameters δr_p and δr_n, which suppress the point-like configuration contribution, and then construct nuclear PDFs as f^A = Z f̃^p + N f̃^n, where f̃^p and f̃^n are the modified proton and neutron PDFs.

## Key numbers

- The free parameter r multiplying neutron effective charges is set to r = 1.5, as done in deTeramond (2018)
- The flavor-independent parameter a = 0.531 ± 0.037 appears in the profile function w(x) = x^(1-x) e^(-a(1-x)^2)
- The matching scale between LFHQCD and pQCD is μ_0 = 1.06 ± 0.15 GeV
- The probability of the point-like configuration (PLC) for free nucleons is P_PLC = 1/2, corresponding to ε = -1
- The modified PLC probability is given by P̃_PLC = 1/2 - 2δr_p, with δr_(n,p) = (1/4)(|U_(n,p)|/Δ̄) where Δ̄ = √(Δ² + 4V²)

## Context

This work extends the LFHQCD framework of Sufian (2017) and deTeramond (2018) for free nucleon PDFs to the nuclear medium, and builds on the two-component nucleon model originally motivated by color transparency physics from Frankfurt (1985) and Brodsky (1987). The model explicitly connects the EMC effect to short-range correlations (SRCs) through the relationship between virtuality and the nuclear potential derived from Ciofi degli Atti (2007), and shows a correlation with the SRC pair density a_2 extracted from kinematic plateaus at x > 1 in inclusive quasi-elastic scattering.
