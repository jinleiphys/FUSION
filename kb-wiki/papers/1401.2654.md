# Limits on TMD Evolution From Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering at Moderate $Q$

> In the QCD evolution of transverse momentum dependent parton distribution and fragmentation functions, the Collins-Soper evolution kernel includes both a perturbative short-distance contribution as well as a large-distance non-perturbative, but strongly universal, contribution. In the past, global fits, based mainly on larger $Q$ Drell-Yan-like processes, have found substantial contributions from non-perturbative regions in the Collins-Soper evolution kernel. In this article, we investigate semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering measurements in the region of relatively small $Q$, of the order of a few GeV, where sensitivity to non-perturbative transverse momentum dependence may become more important or even dominate the evolution. Using recently available deep inelastic scattering data from the COMPASS experiment, we provide estimates of the regions of coordinate space that dominate in TMD processes when the hard scale is of the order of only a few GeV. We find that distance scales that are much larger than those commonly probed in large $Q$ measurements become important, suggesting that the details of non-perturbative effects in TMD evolution are especially significant in the region of intermediate $Q$. We highlight the strongly universal nature of the non-perturbative component of evolution, and its potential to be tightly constrained by fits from a wide variety of observables that include both large and moderate $Q$. On this basis, we recommend detailed treatments of the non-perturbative component of the Collins-Soper evolution kernel for future TMD studies.

## Key claim

The paper argues that at moderate hard scales (Q of order a few GeV), the non-perturbative component of the Collins-Soper evolution kernel becomes especially significant, contrary to claims in alternative formalisms that such a component is unnecessary for studying evolution down to Q ~ 2.0 GeV. The authors hedge that while their results are robust enough to guide future global fitting, the TMD factorization formalism itself may require refinements and extensions when Q approaches ~1.0 GeV.

## Method

The authors analyze semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) data from the COMPASS experiment for charged hadrons on a lithium-deuterium target, focusing on the region of moderate Q (a few GeV) where sensitivity to non-perturbative transverse momentum dependence may dominate. They estimate the regions of transverse coordinate space (b_T) that dominate TMD processes at these scales by examining the Fourier transform of the product of TMD parton distribution and fragmentation functions in the Collins TMD factorization formalism, and compare the observed Q-dependence of the transverse momentum distributions with expectations from extrapolations of large-Q fits.

## Key numbers

- The COMPASS SIDIS data cover a range of Q values of the order of a few GeV, with the paper noting that the range in Q is "too small to allow for reasonably accurate fits to the nonperturbative evolution" but "significant enough" to rule out dramatic variations with Q.
- The paper compares proton-tungsten Drell-Yan data at beam energy 400 GeV (27.4 GeV center-of-mass energy) from Oliver (1978) with antiproton-tungsten data at beam energy 125 GeV (15.4 GeV center-of-mass energy) from Anassontzis (1987), noting the proton-tungsten data are cut off at Q = 7 GeV while the antiproton-tungsten data are cut off at Q = 4 GeV.
- The paper references a fit from Signori (2013) that finds sensitivity to sea versus valence quark distributions in SIDIS data with Q^2 > 1.4 GeV^2, but notes the trend reverses when using a cut of Q^2 > 1.6 GeV^2.
- The paper notes that previous global fits within the CSS formalism found substantial non-perturbative effects even at relatively large Q (Q ≳ 10 GeV), citing Landry (2002), Konychev (2005), and Nadolsky (1999, 2000).

## Context

This work extends the CSS formalism and the Collins TMD factorization framework (Collins 2011) to the moderate-Q regime, directly addressing the tension between large-Q global fits that find substantial non-perturbative evolution effects and recent claims by Sun and Yuan (2013) that such effects are unnecessary down to Q ~ 2.0 GeV. The paper uses COMPASS SIDIS data (2013) to demonstrate that distance scales much larger than those probed in high-Q Drell-Yan measurements become important at moderate Q, suggesting that detailed treatments of the non-perturbative Collins-Soper kernel are essential for future TMD studies. The analysis is intended to guide global fitting efforts that incorporate questions about intrinsic non-perturbative structure, such as the difference between valence and sea quark transverse momentum distributions.
