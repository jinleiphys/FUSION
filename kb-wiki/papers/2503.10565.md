# Apparent teleportation of indistinguishable particles

> Teleportation, introduced in science fiction literature, is an instantaneous change of the position of a macroscopic object. Two teleportation-like phenomena have been predicted by quantum mechanics: quantum teleportation and, more recently, quantum particle teleportation. Here, we introduce the third teleportation-like phenomenon - apparent teleportation. It seems to be a natural consequence of the Standard Model's indistinguishable elementary particles and antiparticles. We illustrate the idea within a 1+1D toy model of particle-antiparticle creation and space-time evolution obeying transport locality. Furthermore, we propose a novel method to observe apparent teleportation driven by strong interactions through measurements of correlations between the momenta of charm and anticharm hadrons in nuclear collisions. Observing the apparent teleportation would uncover the basic transport properties of indistinguishable particles.

## Key claim

The authors introduce "apparent teleportation" as a third teleportation-like phenomenon that follows naturally from the Standard Model's postulate that elementary particles and antiparticles are indistinguishable, but they hedge that this is an illusion arising because particle trajectories and velocities are undefined for indistinguishable particles, and the phenomenon cannot be used for superluminal signalling.

## Method

The authors construct a 1+1D discrete-time Markov chain "Cell Model" on a ring of cells, where particle-antiparticle creation, annihilation, and redistribution occur under a transport-locality condition expressed as inequalities that limit how particle numbers can change between adjacent cell intervals. They then propose observing apparent teleportation in heavy-ion collisions by measuring momentum correlations between charm and anticharm hadrons (D and anti-D mesons) from single charm-anticharm pairs, using the condition that if the difference in scaled flow velocities exceeds 1, the process cannot be explained by local distinguishable particles moving subluminally.

## Key numbers

- The hadronisation temperature for quark-gluon plasma is approximately 150 MeV (T_HAD ≈ 150 MeV)
- The threshold for quark-gluon plasma creation in heavy-ion collisions is located at sqrt(s_NN) ≈ 10 GeV
- The mean number of charm quarks produced in central Pb+Pb collisions at top SPS energy is approximately 1 (≈ 1)
- The hard-scattering scale for charm quark separation is hbar c/(2 m_c) ≈ 0.1 fm, with m_c ≈ 1.3 GeV
- The thermal scale for charm quark separation in quark-gluon plasma is hbar c/T ≈ 1 fm, with T ≈ 0.2 GeV
- The spatial extent of the system created in central Pb+Pb collisions is approximately 10 fm (the diameter of the lead nucleus)
- The Schwinger limit for spontaneous electron-positron pair creation is about 1.3 × 10^18 V/m, corresponding to energy density ≈ 10^18 J/m^3 (≈ 10^6 GeV/fm^3)
- The mean number of bottom-antibottom pairs produced in central Pb+Pb collisions at top LHC energy is expected to be on the order of 10 (⟨b anti-b⟩ ≈ 10)

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Cell Model framework (Gazdzicki 2017, Gazdzicki 2022) and their proposal to use charm-anticharm momentum correlations to extract spatial information (Gazdzicki 2023). It contrasts with quantum teleportation (Bennett 1992, Bouwmeester 1997) and quantum particle teleportation (Porras 2023), which are distinct phenomena. The apparent teleportation mechanism may explain the puzzle of fast equilibration in heavy-ion collisions (Schlichting 2019) and the quark-gluon paradox (Miskowiec 2007) regarding local colour neutrality during hadronisation. The proposed measurement method enables experimental tests at existing facilities like the CERN LHC and SPS, and potentially at future experiments like CBM at FAIR.
