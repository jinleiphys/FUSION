# Effective Mass of $α$-Cluster in $^{12}$C Nucleus

> Based on the effective-mass concept, we perform the Faddeev calculations for a low-lying spectrum of 3$α$ states in $^{12}$C nucleus. A three-body potential is used to describe the known breaking of the 3$α$-cluster structure in the nucleus. We show that the contribution of the three-body potential to the Hamiltonian can be compensated by increasing/decreasing the $α$-particle free mass. The effective-mass values are adjusted so that to reproduce the experimental data for the $^{12}$C nucleus. The energy dependence of the effective mass and the correlation to a three-body potential are discussed. We show that the coupling between the $0^+$ ($2^+$) levels forms a specific picture of anti-crossing on the energy/effective-mass plane.

## Key claim

The contribution of a three-body potential to the 3α Hamiltonian can be compensated by adjusting the free mass of the α-particle, but the effective mass must be defined separately for each energy level rather than depending solely on total orbital momentum as assumed in prior work.

## Method

The authors solved the Faddeev equations in configuration space for the 3α system using the Ali-Bodmer α-α potential with s, d, and g partial components. They treated the effective α-particle mass as a variable parameter, adjusting it to reproduce experimental energies of low-lying 12C states without including an explicit three-body potential. For resonance states, they used a continuation-in-constant method, extrapolating bound-state energies calculated with different effective masses to positive energies.

## Key numbers

- For the 3H test case with the AV14 potential, the nucleon effective mass ratio m*/m = 1.017 reproduces the experimental ground state energy of about -8.48 MeV.
- The 0+1 ground state of 12C is calculated at -7.275 MeV with effective mass ratio m*α/mα = 1.290.
- The 0+2 state is at 0.286 MeV with m*α/mα = 1.176.
- The 2+1 state is at -2.93 MeV with m*α/mα = 1.15.
- The 2+2 state is at 2.25 MeV with m*α/mα = 1.11.
- The 4+1 state is at 6.05 MeV with m*α/mα = 0.92.
- The 4+2 state is at 6.81 MeV with m*α/mα = 1.17.
- The 3-1 state is at 2.4 MeV with m*α/mα = 1.05.
- The 1-1 state is at 3.6 MeV with m*α/mα = 1.19.

## Context

This work extends the effective-mass concept previously applied to 3N systems (Filikhin et al. 2021) to the 3α cluster model of 12C. It addresses the long-standing problem that cluster models using only α-α potentials fail to reproduce the 12C spectrum, requiring three-body potentials that differ between levels (Hiyama 2013; Kurokawa and Kato 2005). The authors show that the coupling between 0+ and 2+ levels produces anti-crossing behavior on the energy/effective-mass plane, and conclude that three-body forces must be defined separately for each energy level as earlier suggested by Fedorov.
