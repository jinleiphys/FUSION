# CP Violation in the Semileptonic $B_{l4}$ ($B->D πl ν$) Decays: Multi-Higgs Doublet Model and Scalar-Leptoquark Models

> CP violation from physics beyond the Standard Model is investigated in $B_{l4}$ decays: $B\to Dπl\barν_l$. In the decay process, we include various excited states as intermediate states decaying to the final hadrons, $D+π$. We consider the semileptonic decay to a tau lepton family as well. The CP violation is implemented through complex scalar--fermion couplings in the multi-Higgs doublet model and scalar-leptoquark models beyond the Standard Model. With these complex couplings, we calculate the CP-odd rate asymmetry and the optimal asymmetry. We find that for $B_{τ4}$ decays the optimal asymmetry is sizable and can be detected at $1σ$ level with about $10^6$-$10^7$ $B$-meson pairs, for maximally-allowed values of CP-odd parameters in those extended models.

## Key claim
CP violation from multi-Higgs doublet models and scalar-leptoquark models can produce a detectable optimal asymmetry in $B_{\tau 4}$ decays ($B \to D \pi \tau \bar{\nu}_\tau$) at the $1\sigma$ level with $10^6$–$10^7$ $B$-meson pairs, but only for the maximally-allowed values of the CP-odd parameters in those extended models; the rate asymmetry alone is much smaller and requires more events.

## Method
The authors computed the decay amplitude for $B^- \to D^+ \pi^- l \bar{\nu}_l$ and its charge-conjugate process including intermediate excited $D$ and $B$ meson states ($D^*$, $D_0$, $D_1$, $D'^*$ and their $B$ counterparts) within heavy quark effective theory and chiral perturbation theory. They introduced complex scalar-fermion couplings from the multi-Higgs doublet model and three scalar-leptoquark models, parametrized the new physics contributions by a complex parameter $\zeta$, and calculated both the CP-odd rate asymmetry $A$ and the optimal asymmetry $\varepsilon_{\rm opt}$ using a weight function $w_{\rm opt} = \mathcal{D}/\mathcal{S}$.

## Key numbers
- For the multi-Higgs doublet model with $\tau$ leptons: $|\Im(\zeta_{\rm MHD})| < 1.88$; for $\mu$ leptons: $|\Im(\zeta_{\rm MHD})| < 0.11$.
- For scalar-leptoquark model I with $\tau$ family: $|\Im(\zeta^I_{\rm SLQ})| < 0.38$; with $\mu$ family: $|\Im(\zeta^I_{\rm SLQ})| < 0.03$.
- For scalar-leptoquark model II with $\tau$ family: $|\Im(\zeta^{II}_{\rm SLQ})| < 1.52$; with $\mu$ family: $|\Im(\zeta^{II}_{\rm SLQ})| < 1.52$.
- The optimal asymmetry for $B_{\tau 4}$ decays can be detected at $1\sigma$ level with about $10^6$–$10^7$ $B$-meson pairs for maximally-allowed CP-odd parameters.
- The soft-pion limit restricts the analysis to $s_M \le 6.5$ GeV$^2$, corresponding to pion momentum less than about 0.6 GeV.
- Quark masses used: $m_c = 1628$ MeV, $m_b = 4977$ MeV.

## Context
This work extends the model-independent analysis of direct CP violation in $B \to D \pi l \nu$ decays by Kim, Lee, and Namgung (1998, Ref. [bcp]) to specific beyond-Standard-Model scenarios: the multi-Higgs doublet model and scalar-leptoquark models. It builds on the formalism of Goity and Roberts (Ref. [goity]), who studied $B \to D \pi l \nu$ decays including higher excited intermediate states. The results enable experimental searches at $B$ factories, where about $10^8$ $B$-meson pairs per year are expected, allowing constraints on the CP-odd parameters of these models.
