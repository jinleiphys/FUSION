# Renormalization group analysis of graphene with a supercritical Coulomb impurity

> We develop a field-theoretic approach to massless Dirac fermions in a supercritical Coulomb potential. By introducing an Aharonov--Bohm solenoid at the potential center, the critical Coulomb charge can be made arbitrarily small for one partial-wave sector, where a perturbative renormalization group analysis becomes possible. We show that a scattering amplitude for reflection of particle at the potential center exhibits the renormalization group limit cycle, i.e., log-periodic revolutions as a function of the scattering energy, revealing the emergence of discrete scale invariance. This outcome is further incorporated in computing the induced charge and current densities, which turn out to have power-law tails with coefficients log-periodic with respect to the distance from the potential center. Our findings are consistent with the previous prediction obtained by directly solving the Dirac equation and can in principle be realized by graphene experiments with charged impurities.

## Key claim

By introducing an Aharonov–Bohm solenoid at the potential center, the critical Coulomb charge for massless Dirac fermions in two dimensions can be made arbitrarily small for one partial-wave sector, enabling a perturbative renormalization group analysis that reveals discrete scale invariance through log-periodic behavior in scattering amplitudes and induced densities.

## Method

The author formulated an effective one-dimensional action for a single partial-wave sector of massless Dirac fermions in a Coulomb potential with an Aharonov–Bohm solenoid, introducing contact terms at the potential center to ensure cutoff independence. A perturbative renormalization group analysis was performed to lowest order in the Coulomb coupling \(g_0\) and the centrifugal coupling \(g_1\), deriving the beta function for the complex scattering amplitude \(v_1 - i_\eps v_2\) representing reflection at the potential center. The resulting renormalization group flow was solved analytically for both subcritical (\(|g_0| < |g_1|\)) and supercritical (\(|g_0| > |g_1|\)) regimes, and the solutions were used to compute the induced charge and current densities via Feynman diagrams.

## Key numbers

* The critical Coulomb coupling becomes \(|j+\phi|\), which can be made arbitrarily small by controlling the magnetic flux \(\phi\) (the Aharonov–Bohm flux in units of \(h/e\)).
* In the supercritical case, the scattering amplitude exhibits log-periodic revolutions with period \(e^{n\pi/\gamma}\) for integer \(n\), where \(\gamma = \sqrt{g_0^2 - g_1^2}\).
* The atomic collapse resonances appear at poles \(E_n = -\frac{i}{2}\Lambda_* e^{-(\frac12 + n)\pi/\gamma}\).
* The induced charge density \(\rho(r)\) has a power-law tail \(\sim 1/r^2\) with a log-periodic coefficient, given explicitly by \(\rho(r) = -\frac{e}{2\pi^2 r^2}\left\{\gamma\int_0^\infty dz\,\Im[\tan(\gamma\ln\frac{r\Lambda_*}{z})]\Gamma(0,z)\right\}\).
* The induced angular current density \(J_\theta(r)\) also has a \(\sim 1/r^2\) power-law tail with a log-periodic coefficient, given by \(J_\theta(r) = -\frac{e\vF}{2\pi^2 r^2}\left\{\# - \frac{\gamma}{2g_1}\int_0^\infty dz\,\Re[\tan(\gamma\ln\frac{r\Lambda_*}{z})]e^{-z}\right\}\), where \(\#\) is an unknown constant from subcritical sectors.

## Context

This work extends the previous prediction of discrete scale invariance and log-periodic oscillations in the vacuum polarization obtained by directly solving the Dirac equation (Nishida, 2014) to a field-theoretic renormalization group framework. It also connects to earlier studies of atomic collapse resonances in graphene (Pereira, 2007; Shytov, 2007a; Shytov, 2007b) and the proposal to control the critical charge via the Aharonov–Bohm effect (Levitov, 2015). The field-theoretic approach developed here enables future incorporation of electron-electron interactions, which were neglected in this work, and provides a systematic framework for analyzing supercritical Coulomb problems in graphene experiments with charged impurities (Wang, 2012; Wang, 2013; Luican-Mayer, 2014; Mao, 2015).
