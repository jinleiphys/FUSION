# Meson-exchange currents and superscaling analysis with relativistic effective mass of quasielastic electron scattering from $^{12}$C

> We reanalyze the scaling properties of inclusive quasielastic electron scattering from $^{12}$C by subtracting from the data the effects of two-particle emission. A model of relativistic meson-exchange currents (MEC) is employed within the mean field theory of nuclear matter, with scalar and vector potentials that induce an effective mass and a vector energy to the nucleons. A new phenomenological quasielastic scaling function is extracted from a selection of the data after the subtraction of the 2p-2h contribution. The resulting superscaling approach with relativistic effective mass (SuSAM*) can be used to compute the genuine quasielastic cross section without contamination of the 2p-2h channel that can then be added separately to obtain the total quasielastic plus two-nucleon emission response.

## Key claim

The paper claims that subtracting the two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) contribution, computed using a relativistic mean field (RMF) model with effective mass \(M^* = 0.8\) and vector energy \(E_v = 141\) MeV, from inclusive quasielastic electron scattering data on \(^{12}\)C yields a cleaner phenomenological scaling function \(f^*(\psi^*)\) that avoids double counting when the 2p-2h channel is later added separately.

## Method

The authors used a relativistic meson-exchange current (MEC) model within the mean field theory of nuclear matter, incorporating scalar and vector potentials that produce an effective nucleon mass \(m_N^*\) and a vector energy \(E_v\). They computed the 2p-2h electromagnetic response functions for \(^{12}\)C using Feynman diagrams for seagull, pion-in-flight, and \(\Delta\) (1232) isobar currents, modifying the \(\Delta\) propagator and nucleon spinors to include the RMF effects. The 2p-2h cross section was then subtracted from experimental \((e,e')\) data, and the resulting subtracted data were used to extract a new phenomenological scaling function \(f^*(\psi^*)\) via a fit to a double-Gaussian parametrization, employing a density criterion to select data points that form a narrow band in the scaling plot.

## Key numbers

* The effective mass ratio used is \(M^* = m_N^*/m_N = 0.8\), corresponding to \(m_N^* = 0.8 \times m_N\).
* The vector energy is fitted to \(E_v = 141\) MeV, obtained by matching the \(\Delta\) peak position in the 2p-2h cross section to experimental data.
* The Fermi momentum is taken as \(k_F = 225\) MeV/c.
* The depth of the nucleon potential energy in \(^{12}\)C from the 2p-2h model is \(U_0 \simeq -47\) MeV.
* The reduction of the 2p-2h peak due to the relativistic mean field amounts to approximately 25% of the relativistic Fermi gas (RFG) model.
* The total database contains 2969 entries for \(^{12}\)C; after subtraction and density selection, 1546 data points survive for the RFG MEC model and 1453 for the RMF MEC model.
* The new scaling function parameters (central values) are: \(a_1 = -0.0971\), \(a_2 = 0.422\), \(a_3 = 0.477\), \(b_1 = 0.299\), \(b_2 = 0.855\), \(b_3 = 0.330\).

## Context

This work extends the SuSAM* (superscaling approach with relativistic effective mass) framework developed by the same group in earlier studies (Martinez-Consentino et al., 2015, 2017, 2018) by explicitly subtracting the 2p-2h MEC contribution before extracting the scaling function, avoiding double counting. It contrasts with the SuSA-v2 model (Gonzalez-Jimenez et al., 2014; Megias et al., 2016), which uses a different treatment of transverse enhancement and MEC, and with the RFG-based MEC model of Simo et al. (2016) that employs a separation energy shift. The resulting SuSAM*+MEC model provides a consistent framework for computing quasielastic plus two-nucleon emission responses, with potential application to neutrino-nucleus scattering for experiments such as MiniBooNE, MINERvA, and T2K.
