# Split of the pseudo-critical temperatures of chiral and confine/deconfine transitions by temperature gradient

> Searching of the critical endpoint of the phase transition of Quantum Chromodynamics~(QCD) matter in experiments is of great interest. The temperature in the fireball of a collider is location dependent, however, most theoretical studies address the scenario of uniform temperature. In this work, the effect of temperature gradients is investigated using lattice QCD approach. We find that the temperature gradient catalyzes chiral symmetry breaking, meanwhile the temperature gradient increases the Polyakov loop in the confined phase but suppresses the Polyakov loop in the deconfined phase. Furthermore, the temperature gradient decreases the pseudo-critical temperature of chiral transition but increases the pseudo-critical temperature of the confine/deconfine transition.

# Split of the pseudo-critical temperatures of chiral and confine/deconfine transitions by temperature gradient

## Key claim

Using lattice QCD with $N_f=2$ dynamic staggered fermions, a temperature gradient decreases the pseudo-critical temperature of the chiral transition while increasing the pseudo-critical temperature of the confine/deconfine transition, causing the two pseudo-critical temperatures to move away from each other. This effect is demonstrated for zero baryon density and for temperature gradients introduced via non-uniform lattice spacings, with the caveat that the fermion mass used ($am=0.1$) is heavier than the physical mass.

## Method

The authors introduce a temperature gradient along the $z$-axis by making the gauge coupling $\beta$ a periodic function of $n_z$ (the spatial lattice coordinate), so that different $z$-slices have different lattice spacings and thus different temperatures. They perform lattice simulations on a $12^3 \times 6$ lattice using both the quenched approximation and $N_f=2$ Kogut-Susskind staggered fermions with the rational hybrid Monte Carlo algorithm, measuring the Polyakov loop, chiral condensation, and their susceptibilities at $n_z=0$ and $n_z=6$ where the temperature gradient is maximal. For the quenched case, $\Delta\beta=0.5$ is studied; for $N_f=2$, four gradient strengths are considered with $\Delta\beta=0.05, 0.1, 0.15, 0.2$.

## Key numbers

- For $N_f=2$ dynamic fermions with $\Delta\beta=0.05$, the temperature gradient at $n_z=0,6$ ranges from $(135\;\mathrm{MeV})^2$ (at $\beta=5.29$) to $(149\;\mathrm{MeV})^2$ (at $\beta=5.36$); gradients for $\Delta\beta=0.1,0.15,0.2$ are two, three, and four times these values respectively.
- For the quenched approximation with $\Delta\beta=0.5$, the temperature gradient ranges from $(385\;\mathrm{MeV})^2$ (at $\beta=5.7$) to $(611\;\mathrm{MeV})^2$ (at $\beta=6.05$).
- The lattice spacing matching uses the Sommer scale $r_0=0.5\;\mathrm{fm}$; for $N_f=2$, $a^{-1}$ ranges from $1129(13)\;\mathrm{MeV}$ at $\beta=5.28$ to $1415(10)\;\mathrm{MeV}$ at $\beta=5.36$.
- The fermion mass is set to $am=0.1$, which is heavier than the physical mass but lighter for computational feasibility.

## Context

This work extends lattice QCD studies of the QCD phase diagram (Fukushima 2013, Bzdak 2019) by addressing the realistic condition of non-uniform temperature in heavy-ion collision fireballs, which most theoretical studies treat as uniform. The finding that temperature gradients split the pseudo-critical temperatures of chiral and confine/deconfine transitions contrasts with the near-coincidence of these temperatures in uniform-temperature lattice studies (Borsanyi 2010, Aoki 2006). The result that temperature gradients push the system away from singularity suggests that the critical endpoint in the $T-\mu$ plane may shift toward larger chemical potentials and lower temperatures compared to uniform-temperature predictions, which has implications for experimental searches at RHIC and LHC. The work also confirms the earlier model prediction using an Ising-like effective potential (Zheng 2021) that temperature gradients increase the critical temperature of the confine/deconfine transition.
