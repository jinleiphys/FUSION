# Strong decays of the low-lying bottom strange baryons

> In this work, the strong decay behaviors of the $őĽ-$mode low-lying $őě_{b}$ and $őě_{b}^\prime$ baryons are investigated within the $^3P_0$ model. Our results suggest that all of the low-lying $\bar 3_F$ states $őě_b(2S)$, $őě_b(1P)$, and $őě_b(1D)$ have small decay widths of less than 20 MeV, and these states have good potentials to be observed in the $őě_b^\prime ŌÄ$ and $őě_b^{\prime *} ŌÄ$ invariant masses. Further, most of the $6_F$ multiplets are relatively narrow and may decay into the $őě_b ŌÄ$, $őě_b^\prime ŌÄ$, $őě_b^{\prime *} ŌÄ$, and $őõ_b \bar K$ final states. Considering the masses and strong decay behaviors, we can assign the newly observed $őě_{b}(6100)$ resonance as the $őě_b(1P)$ state with $J^P=3/2^-$ and interpret the $őě_{b}(6227)$ structure as the $J^P=3/2^-$ $őě_b^\prime(1P)$ state with a proper mixing angle. We expect that our predictions for these excited bottom strange baryons will provide helpful information for future experimental research.

# Strong decays of the low-lying bottom strange baryons

## Key claim
The newly observed őě_b(6100) resonance can be assigned as the őě_b(1P) state with J^P = 3/2^- and the őě_b(6227) structure can be interpreted as the J^P = 3/2^- őě_b'(1P) state with a mixing angle in the range 12¬į to 29¬į (or -12¬į to -29¬į), but the authors caution that the ^3P_0 model cannot give precise results due to relatively small phase space and that predictions contain uncertainties of about 30%.

## Method
The strong decay behaviors of őĽ-mode low-lying őě_b and őě_b' baryons were calculated using the ^3P_0 model, where a quark-antiquark pair with quantum numbers J^PC = 0^{++} is created from the vacuum and the initial baryon rearranges into two final hadrons. The masses of the initial states were taken from the relativistic quark model predictions of Ebert (2011), while final ground state masses were taken from the Review of Particle Physics. The same model parameters previously used successfully for őõ_c, ő£_c, őě_c, őě_c', őõ_b, ő£_b, and ő©_b systems were adopted.

## Key numbers
- All low-lying őě_b(2S), őě_b(1P), and őě_b(1D) states have total decay widths less than 20 MeV.
- The őě_b(2S) state (predicted mass 6266 MeV) has a total decay width of 6.99 MeV, with branching ratios Br(őě_b' ŌÄ, őě_b'* ŌÄ) = 37.9%, 62.1%.
- The őě_b(6100) assigned as the J^P = 3/2^- őě_b(1P) state has a total decay width of 6.76 MeV, dominated by the őě_b'* ŌÄ mode.
- The pure őě_b0'(1/2^-) state has a total decay width of 370.75 MeV, while the pure őě_b2'(3/2^-) and őě_b2'(5/2^-) states have widths of 6.93 MeV and 7.12 MeV respectively.
- For the őě_b(6227) interpreted as a mixed J^P = 3/2^- őě_b'(1P) state with mixing angle őł = 22¬į, the branching ratios are Br(őě_b ŌÄ, őě_b' ŌÄ, őě_b'* ŌÄ, őõ_b KŐÖ) = 21.8%, 2.3%, 65.1%, 10.8%.
- The őě_b'(2S) and őě_b'*(2S) states have total decay widths of 33.89 MeV and 35.64 MeV respectively.
- The six őě_b'(1D) states have total widths ranging from 2.23 MeV (J^P = 7/2^+) to 39.82 MeV (J^P = 3/2^+).

## Context
This work extends the systematic ^3P_0 model studies of singly heavy baryons previously applied to őõ_c, ő£_c, őě_c, őě_c', őõ_b, ő£_b, and ő©_b systems by Liang (2019, 2020) and Lu (2018, 2019, 2020). It addresses the fact that unlike the őõ_b and ő©_b systems, the spectra of bottom strange baryons are far from established, with only two excited structures (őě_b(6227) and őě_b(6100)) observed experimentally. The results provide assignments for these two observed resonances and predict narrow widths and specific decay channels for most low-lying őě_b and őě_b' states, enabling future experimental searches in the őě_b' ŌÄ, őě_b'* ŌÄ, őě_b ŌÄ, and őõ_b KŐÖ final states. The authors note that the mixing angle obtained for őě_b(6227) is close to that found for the non-strange partner ő£_b(6097) in their previous work (Liang 2019).
