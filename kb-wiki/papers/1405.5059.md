# Sivers Effect in Dihadron Semi-Inclusive Deep Inelastic Scattering

> The Sivers effect describes the correlation of the unpolarized parton's transverse momentum with the transverse spin of the nucleon. It manifests as a sine modulation of the cross section for single hadron semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) on a transversely polarized nucleon with the azimuthal angle between the produced hadron's transverse momentum and the nucleon spin ($φ_h$ and $φ_S$, respectively). It has been recently suggested that the Sivers effect can also be measured in two hadron SIDIS process as sine modulations involving the azimuthal angles $φ_T$ and $φ_R$ of both the total and the relative transverse momenta of the hadron pair. Here we present the detailed derivation of the two hadron SIDIS cross section using simple parton-model inspired functional forms for both the parton distribution and the fragmentation functions. We show explicitly that the terms corresponding to the $\sin(φ_R-φ_S)$ and $\sin(φ_T-φ_S)$ modulations are non-zero. Further, we derive the cross section expressions for single hadron production in the two hadron sample. Finally, we employ a modified version of the $\tt{LEPTO}$ Monte Carlo event generator that includes the Sivers effect to estimate the size of single spin asymmetries corresponding to these modulations independent of the formalism developed.

## Key claim

The Sivers effect in dihadron semi-inclusive deep inelastic scattering (SIDIS) produces non-zero single spin asymmetries (SSAs) with both \(\sin(\varphi_R-\varphi_S)\) and \(\sin(\varphi_T-\varphi_S)\) modulations, contrary to earlier leading-twist calculations that found only the \(\sin(\varphi_T-\varphi_S)\) term. The \(\sin(\varphi_R-\varphi_S)\) modulation is absent in prior work because those studies used a different definition of the relative transverse momentum \(\mathbf{R}^P\) that is disconnected from the fragmenting quark's transverse momentum at leading order.

## Method

The authors derived the two-hadron SIDIS cross section using a factorized parton-model approach with Gaussian parametrizations for the unpolarized and Sivers parton distribution functions (PDFs) and for the unpolarized dihadron fragmentation function (DiFF), including a correlation term \(c\,\mathbf{P}_{1\perp}\cdot\mathbf{P}_{2\perp}\). They then employed a modified version of the LEPTO Monte Carlo event generator (mLEPTO) that incorporates the Sivers effect to simulate \(10^{11}\) DIS events in the COMPASS kinematic region (\(E_\mu = 160\) GeV muons on an \(NH_3\) target), extracting SSAs for both single-hadron and dihadron production.

## Key numbers

- COMPASS kinematic cuts used in mLEPTO simulations: \(Q^2 > 1\) GeV\(^2\), \(0.1 < y < 0.9\), \(0.03 < x < 0.7\), \(W > 5\) GeV.
- Additional kinematic cuts for single-hadron analysis: \(P_T > 0.1\) GeV and \(z > 0.2\), following COMPASS collaboration procedure.
- Additional kinematic cuts for dihadron analysis: \(P_{1(2)T} > 0.1\) GeV, \(z_{1(2)} > 0.1\).
- Asymmetric cuts to enhance dihadron SSAs: \(z_1 > 0.3\) and \(P_{T1} > 0.3\) GeV for the positively charged hadron.
- \(10^{11}\) DIS events generated in mLEPTO simulations.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier proposal (Kotzinian et al., 2014) for measuring the Sivers function in two-hadron SIDIS, providing detailed derivations and Monte Carlo validation. It contradicts the leading-twist results of Bianconi et al. (1999) and the subleading-twist results of Bacchetta et al. (2003), which found no \(\sin(\varphi_R-\varphi_S)\) modulation, by showing that the discrepancy arises from different definitions of the relative transverse momentum. The dihadron method offers advantages over single-hadron SIDIS: for \(n\) detected hadron types there are \(n(n+1)/2\) dihadron SSAs versus only \(n\) single-hadron SSAs, providing better flavor sensitivity, and statistical uncertainties for dihadron SSAs scale as \(1/\sqrt{N_i N_j}\) versus \(1/\sqrt{N_i}\) for single-hadron SSAs, potentially yielding more precise measurements in high-multiplicity experiments at JLab12 and the planned EIC.
