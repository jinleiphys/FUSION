# On the nucleation of hadronic domains in the quark-hadron transition

> We present numerical results on bubble profiles, nucleation rates and time evolution for a weakly first-order quark-hadron phase transition in different expansion scenarios. We confirm the standard picture of a cosmological first-order phase transition, in which the phase transition is entirely dominated by nucleation. We also show that, even for expansion rates much lower than those expected in heavy-ion collisions nucleation is very unlikely, indicating that the main phase conversion mechanism is spinodal decomposition.

# On the nucleation of hadronic domains in the quark-hadron transition

## Key claim

The authors show that for a weakly first-order quark-hadron phase transition, nucleation is very unlikely to be the main phase conversion mechanism in heavy-ion collisions even for expansion rates much lower than those expected in such collisions, indicating that spinodal decomposition dominates instead; however, for cosmological expansion rates, nucleation completely dominates the conversion dynamics.

## Method

The authors numerically solve the 3-d Euclidean action for critical bubble profiles to obtain the critical radius and surface tension as functions of temperature, using two different equations of state for the quark phase (one from lattice QCD and one from the Bag Model) and a hadronic gas of over 250 free massive resonances. They then compute the fraction of plasma hadronized via nucleation and the temperature as functions of time under a homogeneous, isotropic Hubble-like expansion with constant expansion rate, including homogeneous reheating from latent heat release.

## Key numbers

- For very fast expansion (inverse expansion rate H0^{-1} = 100 fm/c), the plasma quenches into the spinodal region without nucleating a non-negligible number of bubbles.
- For intermediate expansion (H0^{-1} = 600 fm/c) with the Hadron+Lattice equation of state, the plasma reaches considerable supercooling but reheats before reaching the spinodal temperature.
- For very slow expansion (H0^{-1} = 10^7 fm/c), the system presents very slight supercooling quickly washed away by reheating, following equilibrium closely.
- The Hadron+Lattice equation of state leads to stronger supercooling and shorter coexistence time compared to the Hadron+Bag equation of state, which has a larger latent heat.

## Context

This work extends the study of first-order quark-hadron phase transition dynamics by using realistic equations of state from lattice QCD (Cheng 2007) and the Bag Model (Csernai and Kapusta 1992), going beyond the thin-wall approximation for bubble profiles. The results contradict the assumption that nucleation dominates in heavy-ion collision scenarios, instead supporting the spinodal decomposition picture proposed by Mishustin (1999) and Scavenius et al. (2001). The findings enable quantitative modeling of the phase transition in both heavy-ion collisions and early Universe cosmology, showing that the dominant mechanism depends critically on the expansion rate.
