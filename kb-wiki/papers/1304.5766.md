# Comparison of shell model results for even-even Se isotopes

> Comprehensive set of shell model calculations for $^{78-84}$Se isotopes have been performed with recently derived interactions, namely JUN45 and jj44b for ${f_{5/2}pg_{9/2}}$ space. To study the importance of the proton excitations across Z=28 shell in this region mentioned by Cheal {\it et al.} [Phys. Rev. Lett. {\bf104}, 252502 (2010)], calculation for ${fpg_{9/2}}$ valence space using an ${fpg}$ effective interaction with $^{48}$Ca as core and imposing a truncation has also been performed. Comparison of the calculations with experimental data show that the predicted results of jj44b interaction are in good agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The jj44b interaction provides the best overall agreement with experimental data for the energy levels, B(E2) transition strengths, and quadrupole moments of even-even Se isotopes, but the fpg interaction (which includes proton excitations across Z=28) gives better B(E2) values for the lighter isotopes 78Se and 80Se.

## Method

Large-scale shell-model calculations were performed for 78-84Se isotopes in two valence spaces: the full f5/2pg9/2 space with a 56Ni core using the JUN45 and jj44b interactions, and the fpg9/2 space with a 48Ca core using the fpg interaction with truncation allowing up to four particle-hole excitations. The calculations were carried out with the ANTOINE shell-model code, and the results were compared to experimental data for excitation energies, E2 transition probabilities, and quadrupole moments.

## Key numbers

- For 78Se, the jj44b interaction predicts the 2_1+ level at only 60 keV higher than the experimental value of 614 keV, while JUN45 predicts it 134 keV higher and fpg predicts it much lower
- The rms deviation between experiment and theory for the jj44b interaction fit was 250 keV
- For 78Se, the fpg interaction predicts B(E2; 2_1+ -> 0_1+) = 30.87 W.u., closest to the experimental value of 32.8(4.5) W.u., compared to JUN45 (18.83 W.u.) and jj44b (20.74 W.u.)
- For 80Se, the fpg interaction predicts B(E2; 2_1+ -> 0_1+) = 24.08 W.u., very close to the experimental value of 24.2(0.4) W.u.
- For 82Se, the fpg interaction predicts B(E2; 2_1+ -> 0_1+) = 16.33 W.u., close to the experimental value of 16.7(0.3) W.u.
- For 82Se, the fpg interaction predicts B(E2; 8_1+ -> 6_1+) = 0.57 W.u., very close to the experimental value of 0.56(0.03) W.u.
- For 78Se, the experimental quadrupole moment Q(2_1+) = -0.20(7) eb is best reproduced by jj44b with -0.32 eb
- The maximal dimension for 78Se in the f5/2pg9/2 space reached approximately 165 million (1.65 x 10^8) for positive parity
- Computing time for 78Se was approximately 21 days for both parities

## Context

This work extends earlier shell model calculations by Yoshinaga et al. using pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interactions in the 28-50 model space, and by Honma et al. (2009) using the JUN45 interaction for Se isotopes. The study directly addresses the importance of proton excitations across the Z=28 shell gap, as highlighted by Cheal et al. (2010) for Ga isotopes and Astier et al. (2011) for As isotopes. The results for 82Se may be used for calculating nuclear transition matrix elements relevant to neutrinoless double beta decay, and the authors suggest further theoretical development by including the nu d5/2 orbital to study excitations across both Z=28 and Z=50 shells.
