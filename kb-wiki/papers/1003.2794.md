# Critical-like behavior in a lattice gas model

> ALADIN multifragmentation data show features characteristic of a critical behavior, which are very well reproduced by a bond percolation model. This suggests, in the context of the lattice gas model, that fragments are formed at nearly normal nuclear densities and temperatures corresponding to the Kertesz line. Calculations performed with a lattice gas model have shown that similarly good reproduction of the data can also be achieved at lower densities, particularly in the liquid-gas coexistence region.

## Key claim

Critical-like features (signaled by skewness zero and minimum kurtosis excess of the largest fragment distribution) appear in the lattice gas model not only along the Kertesz line but also at subcritical densities and temperatures inside the liquid-gas coexistence region, meaning that first-order phase transitions can mimic critical behavior in finite nuclear systems.

## Method

The authors performed Metropolis Monte Carlo simulations of an isospin-dependent lattice gas model (LGM) with Coulomb interactions on a simple cubic lattice, using the Pan-Das Gupta cluster definition with free boundary conditions. They computed cumulants (skewness K3 and kurtosis excess K4) of the largest fragment charge distribution as functions of temperature and system density, and compared the results to ALADIN S114 data on fragmentation of 197Au projectiles at 600-1000 MeV per nucleon. The critical-like point was identified by K3=0 and a minimum of K4, following the procedure established in percolation studies by Brzychczyk (2006).

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature in LGM at density ρ = 0.5 ρ0 is about 4.8 MeV for systems with Z0 = 36 to 68
- The percolation transition signal appears at Zbound/Z0 = 0.84
- In ALADIN data, the criticality conditions are reached at Zbound = 54
- For LGM at ρ = 0.5 ρ0, the system charge required to match the experimental signal is Z0 ≈ 54/0.67 ≈ 80
- The experimental mean value of Z0 is estimated as 64.5 ± 4.5 (Pochodzalla 1995)
- LGM calculations were performed for densities in the range (0.3 to 1.0) ρ0
- The lattice spacing is r0 = 1.8 fm, with nuclear coupling constant ε1-1 = ε-11 = 5.33 MeV and Coulomb constant Ic = 1.44 MeV fm

## Context

This work extends the criticality analysis of Brzychczyk (2006) from percolation models to a more realistic isospin-dependent lattice gas model with Coulomb interactions, and confronts the results with ALADIN multifragmentation data previously analyzed by Brzychczyk (2009). The finding that critical-like signals appear at subcritical densities contradicts the simpler interpretation that the data necessarily reflect the Kertesz line, as suggested by earlier lattice gas studies (Gulminelli 1999, Carmona 2002, Gulminelli 2005). The results enable future investigations into how the multiplicity of Z=1 fragments depends on model parameters and cluster definitions, and suggest that isotopic distributions may help resolve the freeze-out density question.
