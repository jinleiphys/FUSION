# Colored Dark Matter

> We explore the possibility that Dark Matter is the lightest hadron made of two stable color octet Dirac fermions ${\cal Q}$. The cosmological DM abundance is reproduced for $M_{\cal Q}\approx 12.5$ TeV, compatibly with direct searches (the Rayleigh cross section, suppressed by $1/M_{\cal Q}^6$, is close to present bounds), indirect searches (enhanced by ${\cal Q}{\cal Q}+\bar{\cal Q}\bar{\cal Q}\to {\cal Q}\bar{\cal Q}+{\cal Q}\bar{\cal Q}$ recombination), and with collider searches (where ${\cal Q}$ manifests as tracks, pair produced via QCD). Hybrid hadrons, made of $\cal Q$ and of SM quarks and gluons, have large QCD cross sections, and do not reach underground detectors. Their cosmological abundance is $10^5$ times smaller than DM, such that their unusual signals seem compatible with bounds. Those in the Earth and stars sank to their centers; the Earth crust and meteorites later accumulate a secondary abundance, although their present abundance depends on nuclear and geological properties that we cannot compute from first principles.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that Dark Matter (DM) could be the lightest hadron made of two stable color octet Dirac fermions, with the cosmological DM abundance reproduced for a mass of approximately 12.5 TeV, but this scenario is only viable because the abundance of hybrid hadrons (containing both the new fermion and Standard Model quarks or gluons) is cosmologically suppressed to about 10^5 times smaller than the DM abundance, rather than being dominant as one might naively expect.

## Method

The authors add a single new Dirac fermion in the (8,1)_0 representation of SU(3)_c × SU(2)_L × U(1)_Y (a neutral color octet) to the Standard Model. They compute the thermal relic abundance of the fermion and its bound states through the QCD phase transition, modeling the formation of deep Coulombic bound states (quorn-onlyum) versus loose hybrid hadrons using classical Larmor radiation approximations and quantum decay rates for bound states with large principal and angular momentum quantum numbers.

## Key numbers

- DM cosmological abundance is reproduced for M_Q ≈ 12.5 TeV
- The binding energy of the quorn-onlyum ground state is E_B ≈ 200 GeV for M_Q ∼ 10 TeV
- The cosmological abundance of hybrid hadrons is approximately 10^5 times smaller than the DM abundance
- The QCD string tension for adjoint representation is σ ≈ (0.67 GeV)^2
- The QCD confinement scale is taken as Λ_QCD ≈ 0.27 GeV
- The number density ratio of quorns to quarks and gluons when DM abundance is reproduced is n_Q ∼ 10^{-14} n_{q,g}
- The recombination rate parameter N ∼ n_{q,g} σ_QCD / H ∼ M_Pl / Λ_QCD ∼ 10^{19} at temperature T ∼ Λ_QCD

## Context

This work extends the Minimal Dark Matter framework of Cirelli et al. (2005) by considering colored DM candidates, which were previously believed to be excluded due to strong bounds on hybrid hadrons containing both the new particle and Standard Model quarks or gluons. The paper shows that cosmological evolution naturally suppresses hybrid hadron abundance to levels compatible with experimental bounds, analogous to how Big Bang Nucleosynthesis produces mostly Helium with only traces of deuterium and tritium. The scenario enables new experimental searches at colliders (where the new fermion manifests as tracks pair-produced via QCD) and in direct detection experiments (where the Rayleigh cross section is close to present bounds).
