# Cooper pair sizes in 11Li and in superfluid nuclei: a puzzle?

> We point out a strong influence of the pairing force on the size of the two neutron Cooper pair in $^{11}$Li, and to a lesser extent also in $^6$He. It seems that these are quite unique situations, since Cooper pair sizes of stable superfluid nuclei are very little influenced by the intensity of pairing, as recently reported. We explore the difference between $^{11}$Li and heavier superfulid nuclei, and discuss reasons for the exceptional situation in $^{11}$Li.

# Cooper pair sizes in $^{11}$Li and in superfluid nuclei: a puzzle?

## Key claim
The very small Cooper pair size (about 2 fm) observed on the surface of many nuclei is generally not caused by pairing correlations, but is instead a consequence of the finiteness of single-particle wave functions; however, in the halo nuclei $^{11}$Li and (to a lesser extent) $^6$He, the pairing interaction does strongly influence the Cooper pair size, making these cases exceptional.

## Method
The authors performed three-body model calculations ($^{9}$Li+$n$+$n$ for $^{11}$Li, $^{4}$He+$n$+$n$ for $^6$He) using a density-dependent contact pairing interaction (surface-type pairing), and compared the local root-mean-square distance between valence neutrons (the local coherence length) as a function of nuclear radius $R$ for correlated (interacting) and uncorrelated (non-interacting) pairs. They also examined uncorrelated pairs in $^{176}$Sn and $^{18}$O to contrast the behavior across different mass regions and single-particle configurations.

## Key numbers
- The local coherence length for a Cooper pair in $^{120}$Sn takes a minimum of about **2 fm** on the surface, even in a situation of negligible pairing gap.
- For $^{11}$Li, uncorrelated pairs in the $(1p_{1/2})^2$ and $(2s_{1/2})^2$ configurations were calculated with single-particle energy set to **-0.15 MeV**.
- For $^6$He, the uncorrelated $(1p_{3/2})^2$ pair was calculated at single-particle energy **-0.49 MeV**.
- For $^{176}$Sn, the uncorrelated $(3p_{1/2})^2$ configuration at **-0.032 MeV** binding gave an rms distance that did not get lower than **10 fm**.
- The three-body model calculation for $^{18}$O included single-particle levels up to $e_{\rm max}=$**30 MeV** and $l_{\rm max}=$**7**.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier three-body model study of $^{11}$Li (Hagino et al., 2007) and Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov calculations with the Gogny interaction (Pillet et al., 2007) that showed Cooper pair size shrinking on nuclear surfaces. It directly contrasts with the recent finding for $^{120}$Sn (Pillet et al., 2009) that the coherence length is nearly independent of pairing strength in stable superfluid nuclei. The paper resolves this apparent puzzle by identifying the low angular momentum ($l\le1$) and very weak binding of the valence neutrons in $^{11}$Li and $^6$He as the key factors that make pairing dominate over the single-particle size effect. It calls for further experimental study of $^{11}$Li (Nakamura, 2009) and searches for similar exceptional cases in heavier nuclei.
