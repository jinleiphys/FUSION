# Investigation of the fundamental constants stability based on the reactor Oklo burn-up analysis

> The burn-up for SC56-1472 sample of the natural Oklo reactor zone 3 was calculated using the modern Monte Carlo codes. We reconstructed the neutron spectrum in the core by means of the isotope ratios: $^{147}$Sm/$^{148}$Sm and $^{176}$Lu/$^{175}$Lu. These ratios unambiguously determine the spectrum index and core temperature. The effective neutron absorption cross section of $^{149}$Sm calculated using this spectrum was compared with experimental one. The disagreement between these two values allows to limit a possible shift of the low laying resonance of $^{149}$Sm even more . Then, these limits were converted to the limits for the change of the fine structure constant $α$. We found that for the rate of $α$ change the inequality $|δ\dotα/α| \le 5\cdot 10^{-18}$ is fulfilled, which is of the next higher order than our previous limit.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the variation of the fine structure constant α during the past 2 billion years is not larger than 1.0×10⁻⁸, with a bound on its time derivative of |δ\dot{α}/α| ≤ 5×10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹ at 99% C.L., but this result depends on the specific sample SC56-1472 from Oklo Zone 3 and on the assumption that the resonance shift of ¹⁴⁹Sm is entirely due to changes in α, with the zero resonance shift not excluded by the data.

## Method

The author performed Monte Carlo burn-up calculations for the SC56-1472 sample of the natural Oklo reactor Zone 3 using the MCNP-4C and MCU-REA codes, reconstructing the neutron spectrum from the ¹⁴⁷Sm/¹⁴⁸Sm and ¹⁷⁶Lu/¹⁷⁵Lu isotope ratios to determine the spectrum index and core temperature independently. The theoretical effective neutron absorption cross section of ¹⁴⁹Sm calculated from the reconstructed spectrum was compared with the experimental cross section derived from the ¹⁴⁹Sm concentration in the sample after burn-up, and the disagreement was converted into a limit on the shift of the 97.3 meV resonance of ¹⁴⁹Sm, which was then translated into a limit on the variation of the fine structure constant.

## Key numbers

- The experimental effective absorption cross section of ¹⁴⁹Sm for sample SC56-1472 was determined to be 62.91 kb at 367 K.
- The theoretical effective cross section of ¹⁴⁹Sm at the temperature of 455 K (determined from the lutetium ratio) was 69.78 kb, giving a disagreement of 6.9 kb.
- The shift of the ¹⁴⁹Sm low-lying resonance was constrained to the range -11.3 meV < ΔE_r < 0.8 meV at 99% C.L.
- The variation of the fine structure constant over the past 2×10⁹ years was bounded as -0.7×10⁻⁹ ≤ Δα/α ≤ 1.0×10⁻⁸.
- The time derivative bound was -0.35×10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹ ≤ δ\dot{α}/α ≤ 5×10⁻¹⁸ yr⁻¹ at 99% C.L.
- The core temperature determined from the ¹⁷⁶Lu/¹⁷⁵Lu ratio was 455 K, with a 99% C.L. range of 364 K < T < 525 K.
- The uranium content in the sample was 0.32 ± 0.01 wt.%, with a neutron fluence of 0.228 kb⁻¹ and a reactor operation duration of 300,000 years.

## Context

This work extends the neutronics analysis of the Oklo reactor begun in Petrov (2005) by performing a realistic burn-up calculation for a specific sample rather than a fresh core, and by using the lutetium isotope ratio to independently determine the core temperature, unlike the approach of Lamoreaux (2006) which accepted close limits on temperature. The obtained constraints on α variation are of the next higher order than the previous limit from Petrov (2005) and serve as an additional rigid boundary condition for cosmon field models of dark energy and for unification scenarios analyzed by Olive (2002), Wetterich (2003), and Dent (2004). The paper enables further investigation of other Oklo samples to confirm the result and refine limits on fundamental constant variation.
