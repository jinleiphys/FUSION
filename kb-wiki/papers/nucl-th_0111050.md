# Reconstruction of Hadronization Stage in Pb+Pb Collisions at 158A GeV/c

> Recent data on hadron multiplicities in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c at mid-rapidity are analyzed within the concept of chemical freeze-out. A non-uniformity of the baryon chemical potential along the beam axis is taken into account. An approximate analytical solution of the hydrodynamic equations for a chemically frozen Boltzmann-like gas is found. The Cauchy conditions for hydrodynamic evolution of the hadron resonance gas are fixed at the thermal freeze-out hypersurface from analysis of one-particle momentum spectra and HBT correlations. The proper time of chemical freeze-out and physical conditions at the hadronization stage, such as energy density and averaged transverse velocity, are found.

## Key claim

The paper reconstructs the hadronization stage in central Pb+Pb collisions at 158A GeV/c by solving hydrodynamic equations backwards in time from thermal freeze-out to chemical freeze-out, under the assumption that chemical composition is frozen after hadronization and that the baryon chemical potential is non-uniform along the beam axis.

## Method

The authors analyze hadron multiplicity ratios at mid-rapidity from WA97 and NA49 experiments using a chemically equilibrated hadron resonance gas model with Boltzmann and quantum statistics, extracting temperature and baryon and strangeness chemical potentials at chemical freeze-out. They then fix Cauchy conditions at the thermal freeze-out hypersurface by analyzing one-particle transverse momentum spectra and HBT correlations for different hadron species. Using an approximate analytical solution of the relativistic hydrodynamic equations for a chemically frozen Boltzmann-like gas with non-relativistic transverse flow, they evolve the system backwards in time from thermal freeze-out to the chemical freeze-out temperature to reconstruct the space-time extension, energy density, and averaged transverse velocity at hadronization.

## Key numbers

- Chemical freeze-out temperature: T_ch ≈ 164 MeV (specifically 163.37 MeV for Boltzmann statistics, 163.91 MeV for quantum statistics)
- Baryochemical potential at chemical freeze-out: μ_B ≈ 224 MeV (223.6 MeV for Boltzmann statistics, 224.0 MeV for quantum statistics)
- Strangeness chemical potential at chemical freeze-out: μ_S ≈ 55 MeV (55.2 MeV for Boltzmann statistics, 55.1 MeV for quantum statistics)
- The model overpredicts the (p−p̄)/π⁻ ratio by about 30% (model gives 0.245 vs data 0.184 for Boltzmann statistics)
- The correction factor δ_i for non-uniform baryochemical potential effects at mid-rapidity is estimated to contribute about 10% to pion transport from peripheral to central rapidity regions

## Context

This work extends the chemical freeze-out analysis of Braun-Munzinger et al. (2001) by accounting for non-uniformity of baryon chemical potential along the beam axis at SPS energies, where incomplete baryon stopping is indicated by NA49 data. The method differs from the approach of Shuryak (1990s) by solving hydrodynamic equations backwards in time from thermal freeze-out rather than forward from an equilibrated QGP initial condition. The results enable reconstruction of the hadronization stage properties and address the debate about applicability of hydrodynamics to the post-hadronization stage, supporting the chemically frozen hydrodynamic approach as argued by Kolb et al. (2000) against the criticism of Bass et al. (2000) and Teaney et al. (2000).
