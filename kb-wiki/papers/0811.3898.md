# Study of shape transitions in $N\sim90$ isotopes with beyond mean field calculations

> We study the spherical to prolate-deformed shape transition in $^{144-158}$Sm and $^{146-160}$Gd isotopes with modern calculations beyond the mean field with the Gogny D1S force. We compare the results with the shape-phase transition predicted by the collective hamiltonian model and with the experimental data. Our calculations do not support the existence of a first order phase transition in these isotopic chains in the viewpoint of the Bohr hamiltonian neither the interpretation of the nuclei N=90 as critical points.

## Key claim

The authors argue that beyond-mean-field calculations with the Gogny D1S force do not support the interpretation of the spherical-to-prolate shape transition in Sm and Gd isotopes as a first-order quantum phase transition, nor do they support the identification of N=90 nuclei as critical points of the X(5) type, contrary to what the collective Bohr Hamiltonian model predicts.

## Method

The authors performed particle-number-projected Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny D1S interaction to obtain potential energy surfaces (PES) in the (β,γ) deformation plane for 144-158Sm and 146-160Gd isotopes. They then carried out Generator Coordinate Method (GCM) calculations with angular-momentum-projected axially symmetric wave functions to compute excitation spectra and energy ratios, and compared these results to the predictions of the collective Bohr Hamiltonian model and to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- The ratio r4,2 = E(4+1)/E(2+1) varies from approximately 2 (vibrational spectrum) to approximately 3.3 (rotational spectrum) across the isotopic chains for both Sm and Gd.
- For the collective Bohr Hamiltonian model, the critical point occurs at a control parameter ζ where the spherical and prolate-deformed minima are degenerate; this degeneracy appears for ζ values around 0.025.
- The PES for N=86, 88, 90 transitional nuclei show softness in the γ direction, in contrast to the collective model where degeneracy occurs only along γ=0.
- The spherical minimum is obtained only for the shell closure N=82 nuclei; for all other isotopes, only oblate and prolate deformed minima are found.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of shape transitions previously performed for Nd isotopes by Rodríguez and Egido (2008) to the Sm and Gd isotopic chains. The results contradict the interpretation of 152Sm and 154Gd as empirical realizations of the X(5) critical point proposed by Iachello (2001) and supported by Casten and Zamfir (2001) and Tonev et al. (2004). The calculations also contrast with the relativistic mean-field GCM calculations of Nikšić et al. (2007) for 150Nd, which also found good agreement with experiment but with a different intrinsic potential. The work enables future inclusion of triaxial and octupole degrees of freedom, which are identified as important for improving agreement in the transitional region.
