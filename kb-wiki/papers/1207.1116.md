# Production of Prompt Photons: Holographic Duality and Thermalization

> We study the production of prompt photons in strongly coupled out-of-equilibrium Super Yang-Mills plasma using the AdS/CFT correspondence. Our goal is to determine the photon emission spectrum at different stages of a thermalization process, which is modeled via the gravitational collapse of a thin spherical shell in AdS$_5$ space. Particular emphasis is placed on the limit of large frequencies, which we are able to treat analytically.

## Key claim

The paper extends the equilibrium photon spectral function calculation of Caron-Huot et al. (2006) to an out-of-equilibrium setting modeled by a collapsing thin shell in AdS$_5$, but the results are valid only in the quasistatic approximation where the shell moves slowly compared to the photon frequency, and the large-frequency limit is treated analytically.

## Method

The authors model thermalization of strongly coupled $\mathcal{N}=4$ Super Yang-Mills plasma via the gravitational collapse of a thin spherical shell in AdS$_5$ spacetime, with the shell location $r_s$ parameterizing the degree of thermalization. They compute the photon spectral function by solving linearized U(1) gauge field fluctuations in the bulk, matching solutions across the shell using the quasistatic approximation, and extracting the retarded correlator via the AdS/CFT prescription of Son and Starinets (2002). The large-frequency limit is treated analytically using Airy function expansions of the gauge field equation of motion.

## Key numbers

- The final equilibrium temperature is given by $T = r_h / (\pi L^2)$, where $r_h$ is the Schwarzschild radius of the shell.
- The dimensionless frequency variable is $\hat{\omega} \equiv k^0 / (2\pi T)$.
- The ratio $c_-/c_+$ vanishes as $(1-u_s)^{1/2 - i\hat{\omega}} / (4\sqrt{2}\hat{\omega})$ in the limit $u_s \to 1$ (shell approaching horizon).
- The asymptotic large-$\hat{\omega}$ spectral function is $\chi_\text{asym}(\hat{\omega},u_s) = (N_c^2 T^2 / 4) (3^{5/6} \Gamma(2/3) / \Gamma(1/3)) \times [1 + (\sqrt{1-u_s^2}(1+\sqrt{1-u_s^2})/(8 u_s^{3/2})) (\cos(\hat{\omega} g(u_s)) + \sqrt{3}\sin(\hat{\omega} g(u_s)))/\hat{\omega} + \mathcal{O}(1/\hat{\omega}^2)]$.
- The thermal equilibrium spectral function is $\chi_\text{thermal}(\hat{\omega}) = N_c^2 T^2 \hat{\omega} / (8 |F|^2)$, where $F$ is a hypergeometric function.

## Context

This work extends the equilibrium photon production calculation of Caron-Huot et al. (2006) to a specific holographic thermalization model involving gravitational collapse of a thin shell, following the same approach previously applied to dileptons by the same authors (Baier et al., 2012). The results show a top-down thermalization pattern similar to the dilepton case, with oscillations in the spectral function that are dampened in the photon emission spectrum by the Bose-Einstein distribution, and contrast with the behavior of $1/\lambda^{3/2}$ corrections to equilibrium photon emission studied by Hassanain and Schvellinger (2012).
