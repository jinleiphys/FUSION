# Contributions of box diagrams and $Δ(1940)$ resonance to $K^0Σ^+$ photoproduction

> We study first the box-diagram contribution to the $γp\,\rightarrow \,K^0Σ^+$ process to understand the anomaly of the kaon photoproduction cross section from CBELSA/TAPS experiment at Electron Stretcher Accelerator (ELSA), where the imaginary part of the scattering amplitude from the box-diagrams is calculated by using Cutkosky's rules in the on-shell approximation while the real part of the amplitude is derived by dispersion relation calculations. Together with the results of the K-MAID model, the contribution of the box-diagrams fails to provide the sudden drop of the differential cross-section between the $K^*\,Λ$ and $K^*\,Σ$ thresholds. In addition, we include the $Δ(1940)$ resonance in the process to complete the description of the differential cross-section. Combining the contributions from the K-MAID model, the box-diagrams and the $Δ(1940)$ resonance, we have obtained the theoretical differential cross-section the $γp\,\rightarrow \,K^0Σ^+$ process, which is compatible with the CBELSA/TAPS experimental data.

## Key claim

The box-diagram contribution alone, calculated via Cutkosky's rules in the on-shell approximation, fails to reproduce the sudden drop in the differential cross-section between the \(K^*\Lambda\) and \(K^*\Sigma\) thresholds; only by adding the \(\Delta(1940)\) resonance to the modified K-MAID model and the box-diagrams can the theoretical differential cross-section be made compatible with the CBELSA/TAPS experimental data.

## Method

The authors calculated the imaginary part of the box-diagram scattering amplitude for \(\gamma p \rightarrow K^0\Sigma^+\) using Cutkosky's rules in the on-shell approximation, with intermediate \(K^*Y\) (\(Y = \Lambda, \Sigma\)) states in the \(t\)-channel, and derived the real part via dispersion relations. They combined these box-diagram amplitudes with the CGLN amplitudes from the K-MAID model (with modified parameters \(G1_{S_{31}}(1900)=0.8\), \(G1_{P_{31}}(1910)=0.6\), \(G2_{S_{31}}(1900)=0.8\), \(G2_{P_{31}}(1910)=0.6\)) and added the \(\Delta(1940)\) resonance contribution, using electromagnetic coupling constants fixed from PDG helicity amplitudes and a hadronic coupling constant \(f_{KYR}\) determined from the decay width.

## Key numbers

* The cut-off momenta for the form factors are \(\Lambda_K = 900\) MeV and \(\Lambda_\pi = 450\) MeV.
* The electromagnetic coupling constants for the \(\Delta(1940)\) resonance are estimated as \(e\kappa_1 = 0.667_{-0.328}^{+0.406}\) and \(e\kappa_2 = 1.281_{-0.576}^{+0.877}\); the values used for the final calculation are \(e\kappa_1 = 0.34\) and \(e\kappa_2 = 0.82\).
* The strong decay width of \(\Delta(1940) \rightarrow K\Sigma\) is constrained to \(\Gamma_{\Delta(1940)\rightarrow K\Sigma} < 3.4\) MeV, and the value used in the fit is \(\Gamma_{\Delta(1940)\rightarrow K\Sigma} = 2.3\) MeV.
* The hadronic coupling constant for the \(\Delta(1940)\) resonance is \(f_{KYR} = \varepsilon\,0.28\) with \(\varepsilon = -1\).
* The cut-off momentum for the \(\Delta(1940)\) hadronic form factor is \(\Lambda_\Delta = 500\) MeV.

## Context

This work addresses the anomaly in the \(\gamma p \rightarrow K^0\Sigma^+\) differential cross-section observed by the CBELSA/TAPS experiment (Ewald, 2011), where the K-MAID model (Lee, 1999) and its modified version fail to describe the data above 1920 MeV center-of-mass energy. The study extends the chiral unitary coupled-channel approach of Ramos (2013), which successfully reproduced the sudden drop using hidden gauge formalism, by providing a detailed one-loop box-diagram calculation. The results enable a more complete description of kaon photoproduction in the \(K^*\Lambda\) and \(K^*\Sigma\) threshold region and suggest the importance of the \(\Delta(1940)\) resonance in this energy range.
