# Neutron-triton elastic scattering

> The Kohn variational principle and the hyperspherical harmonics technique are applied to study n-3H elastic scattering at low energies. In this contribution the first results obtained using a non-local realistic interaction derived from the chiral perturbation theory are reported. They are found to be in good agreement with those obtained solving the Faddeev-Yakubovsky equations. The calculated total and differential cross sections are compared with the available experimental data. The effect of including a three-nucleon interaction is also discussed.

# Neutron-triton elastic scattering

## Key claim
The Kohn variational principle combined with hyperspherical harmonics can be extended to treat non-local chiral potentials for four-body scattering, and the resulting phase shifts and cross sections agree well with Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations, though some discrepancies with experimental data remain in the peak region that may be due to missing parts of the three-nucleon interaction.

## Method
The authors solved the four-body scattering problem using the Kohn variational method with an expansion of the internal wave function in hyperspherical harmonics (HH). They applied this to neutron-triton elastic scattering using the non-local N3LO-Idaho chiral potential (cutoff 500 MeV) and also included a N2LO three-nucleon interaction with parameters fit to A=3,4 binding energies. The HH results were compared to Faddeev-Yakubovsky (FY) calculations in momentum space.

## Key numbers
- Phase shift for \(^1S_0\) at \(E_n = 4\) MeV: \(-69.3^\circ\) (HH) vs \(-69.1^\circ\) (FY)
- Phase shift for \(^3S_1\) at \(E_n = 4\) MeV: \(-61.4^\circ\) (HH) vs \(-61.2^\circ\) (FY)
- Phase shift for \(^3P_0\) at \(E_n = 4\) MeV: \(23.2^\circ\) (HH) vs \(23.3^\circ\) (FY)
- Phase shift for \(^3P_2\) at \(E_n = 4\) MeV: \(48.4^\circ\) (HH) vs \(48.7^\circ\) (FY)
- Mixing angle \(\epsilon\) for \(^3S_1\)-\(^3D_1\) at \(E_n = 4\) MeV: \(0.77^\circ\) (HH) vs \(0.80^\circ\) (FY)
- The N3LO-Idaho/N2LO model gives perfect agreement with experimental total cross section in the minimum around \(E_n = 1\) MeV

## Context
This work extends the hyperspherical harmonics method, previously applied only to local potentials like Argonne V18 (Viviani et al., 1998; 2001; 2005), to non-local chiral potentials for four-body scattering. It complements the Faddeev-Yakubovsky calculations of Deltuva and Fonseca (2007) which used momentum-space methods with screening-renormalization for Coulomb. The results enable systematic testing of chiral three-nucleon forces in the A=4 system, with the remaining discrepancy in the peak region motivating development of N3LO three-nucleon interactions currently in progress.
