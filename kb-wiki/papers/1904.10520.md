# Measurement of the mass difference and the binding energy of the hypertriton and antihypertriton

> According to the CPT theorem, which states that the combined operation of charge conjugation, parity transformation and time reversal must be conserved, particles and their antiparticles should have the same mass and lifetime but opposite charge and magnetic moment. Here, we test CPT symmetry in a nucleus containing a strange quark, more specifically in the hypertriton. This hypernucleus is the lightest one yet discovered and consists of a proton, a neutron, and a $Λ$ hyperon. With data recorded by the STAR detector{\cite{TPC,HFT,TOF}} at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, we measure the $Λ$ hyperon binding energy $B_Λ$ for the hypertriton, and find that it differs from the widely used value{\cite{B_1973}} and from predictions{\cite{2019_weak, 1995_weak, 2002_weak, 2014_weak}}, where the hypertriton is treated as a weakly bound system. Our results place stringent constraints on the hyperon-nucleon interaction{\cite{Hammer2002, STAR-antiH3L}}, and have implications for understanding neutron star interiors, where strange matter may be present{\cite{Chatterjee2016}}. A precise comparison of the masses of the hypertriton and the antihypertriton allows us to test CPT symmetry in a nucleus with strangeness for the first time, and we observe no deviation from the expected exact symmetry.

## Key claim

The STAR Collaboration reports a measurement of the hypertriton mass and its \(\Lambda\) hyperon binding energy that differs from the widely used 1973 value and from theoretical predictions that treat the hypertriton as a weakly bound system; the observed binding energy is larger than zero with a statistical significance of 3.4\(\sigma\), and the mass difference between hypertriton and antihypertriton is consistent with zero, providing the first CPT symmetry test in a nucleus containing strangeness.

## Method

The hypertriton (\(^3_\Lambda\)H) and antihypertriton (\(^3_{\bar{\Lambda}}\overline{\mathrm{H}}\)) were reconstructed from gold-gold collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200\) GeV recorded by the STAR detector at RHIC, using both the 2-body decay (\(^3_\Lambda\)H \(\rightarrow\) \(^3\)He + \(\pi^-\)) and the 3-body decay (\(^3_\Lambda\)H \(\rightarrow\) d + p + \(\pi^-\)). Charged particle identification was performed via the mean energy loss per unit track length (\(\langle dE/dx\rangle\)) in the Time Projection Chamber and the speed (\(\beta\)) from the Time Of Flight detector, while high-precision tracking was provided by the TPC and the Heavy Flavor Tracker. Invariant mass distributions were fitted with a Gaussian plus linear background using the unbinned maximum likelihood method, and the final masses were obtained as the weighted average of the 2-body and 3-body decay channels.

## Key numbers

- Hypertriton mass: \(m_{^3_\Lambda\mathrm{H}} = 2990.95 \pm 0.13\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.11\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\)
- Antihypertriton mass: \(m_{^3_{\bar{\Lambda}}\overline{\mathrm{H}}} = 2990.60 \pm 0.28\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.11\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\)
- Combined average mass (hypertriton and antihypertriton): \(m = 2990.89 \pm 0.12\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.11\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}/c^2\)
- Relative mass difference between hypertriton and antihypertriton: \(\Delta m/m = [0.1 \pm 2.0\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 1.0\ (\mathrm{syst.})] \times 10^{-4}\)
- \(\Lambda\) binding energy for the hypertriton: \(B_\Lambda = 0.41 \pm 0.12\ (\mathrm{stat.}) \pm 0.11\ (\mathrm{syst.})\ \mathrm{MeV}\)
- Signal significance: 11.4 for \(^3_\Lambda\)H and 6.4 for \(^3_{\bar{\Lambda}}\overline{\mathrm{H}}\) in the invariant mass window \(2.986 - 2.996\ \mathrm{GeV}/c^2\)
- Signal counts: approximately 121/35 (2-body/3-body) for \(^3_\Lambda\)H and 36/21 for \(^3_{\bar{\Lambda}}\overline{\mathrm{H}}\)

## Context

This measurement extends the validation of CPT invariance to a nucleus containing a strange quark for the first time, improving precision by an order of magnitude over earlier data with the same mass number from ALICE (2015). The measured \(\Lambda\) binding energy of 0.41 MeV differs from the commonly used value of 0.13 \(\pm\) 0.05 MeV from 1973 emulsion experiments (Bohm 1973) and from recent theoretical calculations using SU(3) chiral effective field theory (2019) that predict values ranging from 0.046 to 0.135 MeV; the larger binding energy and correspondingly shorter effective scattering length suggest a stronger hyperon-nucleon interaction, which has implications for the equation of state of neutron star interiors where strange matter may be present (Chatterjee 2016).
