# Gluon Shadowing and Hadron Production at RHIC

> Hadron multiplicity in the central rapidity region of high-energy heavy-ion collisions is investigated within a two-component mini-jet model which consists of soft and semi-hard particle production. The hard contribution from mini-jets is reevaluated using the latest parameterization of parton distributions and nuclear shadowing. The energy dependence of the experimental data from RHIC requires a strong nuclear shadowing of the gluon distribution in this model. The centrality dependence of the hadron multiplicity at $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV is reproduced well with the impact-parameter dependent parton shadowing. However, energy variation of the centrality dependence is needed to distinguish different particle production mechanisms such as the parton saturation model.

# Gluon Shadowing and Hadron Production at RHIC

## Key claim

The two-component mini-jet model can reproduce the centrality dependence of hadron multiplicity at RHIC's $\sqrt{s}=130$ GeV only if a strong nuclear shadowing of the gluon distribution (parameter $s_g=0.24$–0.28) is assumed; however, the authors note that this constraint is indirect and model-dependent, and that energy variation of the centrality dependence is needed to distinguish this mechanism from the parton saturation model.

## Method

The authors use a two-component mini-jet model in which soft particle production is proportional to the number of participant nucleons and semi-hard (mini-jet) production is proportional to the number of binary collisions, with the jet cross section calculated from leading-order pQCD using GRV parton distributions and an energy-dependent cut-off scale $p_0(\sqrt{s})$ fitted to $p+p(\bar{p})$ data. Nuclear effects are incorporated via a new impact-parameter-dependent parameterization of parton shadowing, with the gluon shadowing strength $s_g$ adjusted to match RHIC data on charged hadron multiplicity in central Au+Au collisions.

## Key numbers

- The energy-dependent cut-off scale $p_0(\sqrt{s})$ ranges from 1.7 GeV/$c$ at $\sqrt{s}=20$ GeV to 3.5 GeV/$c$ at $\sqrt{s}=5$ TeV, as fitted to $p+p(\bar{p})$ data using GRV parton distributions.
- The soft cross section $\sigma_{\rm soft}=57$ mb is used in the eikonalized total inelastic cross section.
- Soft interaction produces $\langle n\rangle_{\rm s}=1.6$ particles and jet hadronization produces $\langle n\rangle_{\rm h}=2.2$ particles per event.
- The gluon shadowing parameter required by RHIC data is $s_g=0.24$–0.28 in the parameterization $R_g^A(x)$ given by Eq. (6).
- The quark shadowing parameter is fixed at $s_q=0.1$ for all quark distributions.
- The parton saturation model uses $\Lambda_{\rm QCD}=0.2$ GeV, $\lambda=0.25$, and $c\approx 0.82$ for its centrality-dependent predictions.

## Context

This work extends the HIJING two-component model (Wang and Gyulassy, 1991; Wang, 1997) by updating the parton distributions from Duke-Owens to GRV parameterization and introducing a new nuclear shadowing parameterization constrained by NMC data (1996) and RHIC results from PHOBOS, PHENIX, STAR, and BRAHMS. The authors show that the old HIJING shadowing is too strong for heavy nuclei, while the HKM (Hirai, Kumano, Miyama, 2001) and EKS (Eskola, Kolhinen, Salgado, 1999) parameterizations produce gluon anti-shadowing that overpredicts multiplicity. The results enable future discrimination between conventional mini-jet production and the parton saturation model (Kharzeev and Nardi, 2001; Kharzeev and Levin, 2001) through more precise measurements of centrality dependence at different energies, particularly the ratio of multiplicities at $\sqrt{s}=200$ and 56 GeV.
