# Resonance photoproduction of pionic atoms at the Gamma Factory

> We present a possibility of direct resonance production of pionic atoms (Coulomb bound states of a negative pion and a nucleus) with a rate of up to $\sim10^{10}$ per second using the gamma-ray beams from the Gamma Factory.

## Key claim

Direct resonance photoproduction of pionic atoms (Coulomb bound states of a negative pion and a nucleus) using the gamma-ray beams from the proposed Gamma Factory could achieve production rates up to approximately 10^10 per second, but this estimate relies on order-of-magnitude approximations for the free-pion photoproduction cross-section near threshold and assumes that the photon-distribution width can be matched to the total width of the pionic state.

## Method

The authors relate the integrated cross-section for bound-pion production to the measured cross-section for free-pion photoproduction near threshold using the proportionality of bound and free Coulomb wavefunctions at the nucleus, which gives a factor (Zα)^2/n^3. They then estimate the resonant cross-section σ0 and the ratio Γγ/Γtot using Eq. (4) from the paper, and compute the maximal production rate at the Gamma Factory by combining the effective photon flux after collimation with the effective absorption cross-section, accounting for competing processes such as pair production and Compton scattering.

## Key numbers

- Production rate of pionic atoms at the Gamma Factory could reach up to approximately 10^10 per second, compared to approximately 10^5 pionic helium atoms per second at existing 590 MeV proton facilities (Hori 2020).
- For light nuclei, the resonant cross-section σ0 for 1s state production is 1200 microbarn for 7Li, 260 microbarn for 11B, 200 microbarn for 12C, and 8 microbarn for 14N (Table 1).
- The free-pion photoproduction cross-section at threshold σp=0 is 8 microbarn for 7Li, 4 microbarn for 11B, 4 microbarn for 12C, and 0.2 microbarn for 14N (Table 1).
- For nuclei with atomic number Z between 9 and 92, the authors estimate σp=0 ≈ 1 microbarn as an order-of-magnitude value.
- The maximal production rate for 1s states is approximately 5.6×10^8 s^-1 using the approximation σp=0 ≈ 1 microbarn for Z between 9 and 92.
- The photon energy required is approximately 140 MeV, corresponding to the rest energy of the negative pion (139.57 MeV).
- The total photon attenuation cross-section for the target is approximately 0.6×10^-26 (Z_i^2 + Z_i) cm^2.

## Context

This work extends the concept of direct photoproduction of pionic atoms originally proposed by Tzara (1970) to the specific capabilities of the Gamma Factory, a proposed facility under study within the CERN Physics Beyond Colliders program (Krasny 2015; Budker 2020). The approach contrasts with conventional production methods that create free negative pions which are then captured by nuclei, and with transfer reactions used to produce deeply bound pionic states (Yamazaki 1996; Gilg 2000; Geissel 2002). The paper enables future experimental investigation of pionic atoms, including deeply bound states that cannot be populated via X-ray cascades from free-pion capture, and could provide information on pion behavior inside the nucleus.
