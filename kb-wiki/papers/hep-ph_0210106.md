# Single color and single flavor color superconductivity

> We survey the non-locked color-flavor-spin channels for quark-quark (color superconducting) condensates in QCD, using an NJL model. We also study isotropic quark-antiquark (mesonic) condensates. We make mean-field estimates of the strength and sign of the self-interaction of each condensate, using four-fermion interaction vertices based on known QCD interactions. For the attractive quark pairing channels, we solve the mean-field gap equations to obtain the size of the gap as a function of quark density. We also calculate the dispersion relations for the quasiquarks, in order to see how fully gapped the spectrum of fermionic excitations will be. We use our results to specify the likely pairing patterns in neutral quark matter, and comment on possible phenomenological consequences.

## Key claim

The paper surveys non-locked color-flavor-spin channels for quark-quark pairing in QCD using an NJL model, finding that several attractive channels exist for "orphaned" quarks that cannot participate in the standard 2SC or CFL pairing patterns, but the actual gaps are often small (ranging from eV to MeV) and many channels lose their BCS enhancement or become ineffective when charge neutrality constraints are imposed.

## Method

The authors performed mean-field calculations of the binding energy for a wide range of translationally invariant diquark condensates using an NJL Hamiltonian with four-fermion interaction vertices based on instanton, electric gluon exchange, and magnetic gluon exchange interactions. For attractive channels, they solved the mean-field gap equations to obtain the gap parameter as a function of quark chemical potential, and calculated the quasiquark dispersion relations to determine how fully gapped the fermionic excitation spectrum would be.

## Key numbers

- The dominant (color-antisymmetric, flavor-antisymmetric, spin-zero) channel (2SC/CFL pattern) has a maximum gap calibrated to 100 MeV
- The (color-sextet, flavor-antisymmetric, j=1, parity +) channel with Dirac structure Cσ₀₃ has a gap of order 1 MeV for instanton interaction and order 1 eV for magnetic gluon interaction
- The (color-antisymmetric, flavor-triplet, j=1, parity +) channel with Dirac structure Cγ₃ (single-flavor pairing) has gaps in the range 2-10 MeV for magnetic gluon interaction
- The (color-sextet, flavor-triplet, j=0, parity +) channel with Dirac structure Cγ₀γ₅ (single-color, single-flavor) reaches about 10 keV for strange quarks with mass 350 MeV
- The (color-antisymmetric, flavor-antisymmetric, j=1, parity -) channel with Dirac structure Cγ₃γ₅ has a gap smaller than the dominant channel by a factor of 10 to 100
- All calculations used a 3-momentum cutoff Λ = 800 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies of color superconductivity (Barrois 1977, Bailin and Love 1984, Alford et al. 1998, Rapp et al. 2000) by systematically surveying the non-locked pairing channels that become relevant when electric and color neutrality constraints disfavor the standard 2SC+s phase, as shown in model-independent calculations by Alford and Rajagopal (2002) and NJL model calculations by Steiner et al. (2002). The results enable future work on the phase diagram of neutral dense quark matter in the chirally restored region at high strange quark mass, where single-color or single-flavor pairing may occur, and are directly relevant to compact star physics where the smallest gap controls transport properties such as specific heat and neutrino emission rates.
