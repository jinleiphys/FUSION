# Chiral Dynamics and Heavy-Fermion Formalism in Nuclei: I. Exchange Axial Currents

> Chiral perturbation theory in heavy-fermion formalism is developed for meson-exchange currents in nuclei and applied to nuclear axial- charge transitions. Calculation is performed to the next-to-leading order in chiral expansion which involves graphs up to one loop. The result turns out to be very simple. The previously conjectured notion of "chiral filter mechanism" in the time component of the nuclear axial current and the space component of the nuclear electromagnetic current is verified to that order. As a consequence, the phenomenologically observed soft-pion dominance in the nuclear process is given a simple interpretation in terms of chiral symmetry in nuclei. In this paper, we focus on the axial current, relegating the EM current which can be treated in a similar way to a separate paper. We discuss the implication of our result on the enhanced axial-charge transitions observed in heavy nuclei and clarify the relationship between the phenomenological meson-exchange description and the chiral Lagrangian description.

## Key claim

The authors verify the previously conjectured "chiral filter mechanism" for the time component of the nuclear axial current to next-to-leading order in chiral perturbation theory, showing that short-range correlations in nuclei suppress all four-fermion counterterm contributions, leaving only long-range pion-exchange contributions as the dominant effect.

## Method

The authors develop chiral perturbation theory in the heavy-fermion formalism for meson-exchange currents in nuclei, calculating irreducible one- and two-body axial current operators to next-to-leading order (one-loop level). They employ the heavy-fermion formalism to avoid the problem of time derivatives on baryon fields breaking the chiral expansion, and they include 1/m corrections systematically. The calculation is restricted to one-pion exchange and two-pion exchange diagrams, with the argument that short-range correlations in nuclear wavefunctions suppress zero-range operators from counterterms.

## Key numbers

* Pion decay constant F ≈ 93 MeV, used as the chiral expansion scale parameter
* Nucleon mass m ≈ 939 MeV, used as the heavy-fermion mass parameter
* Axial coupling constant g_A ≈ 1.25, the leading-order axial coupling
* Pion mass m_π ≈ 140 MeV, the explicit chiral symmetry breaking scale
* Chiral expansion scale Λ_χ ≈ 1 GeV, the cutoff for the derivative expansion
* Short-range correlation cutoff distance ≤ 0.6 fm, below which nuclear wavefunctions suppress interactions

## Context

This work extends the chiral perturbation theory approach to nuclear forces pioneered by Weinberg (1990) and applies it to nuclear response functions rather than nuclear forces, following earlier work on the "chiral filter phenomenon" by Rho (1991) and Kim, Dong, and Rho. The authors build on the heavy-fermion formalism developed by Jenkins and Manohar (1991) for baryon chiral perturbation theory. The paper enables a systematic chiral-symmetry-based understanding of the experimentally observed enhanced axial-charge transitions in heavy nuclei and clarifies the relationship between phenomenological meson-exchange descriptions and chiral Lagrangian descriptions.
