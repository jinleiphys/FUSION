# Open Charm Production and its Lepton Pair Signal in High Energy Nuclear Collisions

> Open charm production at the initial and the pre-equilibrium stages in nuclear collisions is studied. The pre-equilibrium contribution to open charm production in $A+A$ collisions at $\sqrt s=200 AGeV$ is shown to be very sensitive to correlations between momenta and space-time coordinates of the minijets produced after the initial collision. A minimal correlation model based on the uncertainty principle is introduced, and leads only to a small pre-equilibrium charm yield as compared with initial yields. Opposite-sign lepton pairs $ee, eμ$ and $μμ$ from open charm decay in $p+A$ reactions at $\sqrt s=200$ AGeV are proposed as a measure of nuclear shadowing effects. Via an approximate scaling, the ratio of the spectra from $p+A$ collisions to those from $pp$ collisions is shown to reflect the shadowing function well. Finally it is shown that the required measurements are feasible at RHIC.

## Key claim

Open charm production in the pre-equilibrium stage of A+A collisions at √s = 200 AGeV is very sensitive to correlations between momenta and space-time coordinates of minijets, but a minimal correlation model based on the uncertainty principle leads only to a small pre-equilibrium charm yield compared to initial yields.

## Method

The author studied open charm production at initial and pre-equilibrium stages in nuclear collisions using a minimal correlation model based on the uncertainty principle to relate momenta and space-time coordinates of minijets. For p+A reactions, opposite-sign lepton pairs (ee, eμ, μμ) from open charm decay were proposed as a measure of nuclear shadowing effects, with an approximate scaling relation connecting the ratio of spectra from p+A to pp collisions to the shadowing function.

## Key numbers

- Pre-equilibrium charm yield in A+A collisions at √s = 200 AGeV is shown to be small compared to initial yields (no explicit numerical ratio given in the text)
- The shadowing parameterization includes x₀ = 0.1 and x_L = 0.7 for the HIJING model
- For central p+Au collisions, the overall shadowing factor is S_Au(x,0) for HIJING's case and [4/3 S_{b/Au}(x, Q²) - 1/3] for Eskola's case
- The transverse density function for a uniform-sphere nucleus is T_A(r) = (3A/2πR_A²)√(1 - r²/R_A²)

## Context

This work extends studies of open charm production as a probe of the earliest moments of heavy ion reactions at RHIC energies, building on the HIJING model framework. The author compares two shadowing parameterizations: one from the HIJING model and one from Eskola (1990s), showing how the impact-parameter-dependent shadowing functions differ for central p+Au collisions. The results enable using dilepton spectra from open charm decay as a diagnostic tool for nuclear shadowing effects at RHIC, with the required measurements shown to be feasible at that facility.
