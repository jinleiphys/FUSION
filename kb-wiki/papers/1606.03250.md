# HYDRO + JETS (HYDJET++) event generator for Pb+Pb collisions at LHC

> The Monte Carlo event generator HYDJET++ is one of the few generators, designed for the calculations of heavy-ion collisions at ultrarelativistic energies, which combine treatment of soft hydro-like processes with the description of jets traversing the hot and dense partonic medium. The model is employed to study the azimuthal anisotropy phenomena, dihadron angular correlations and event-by-event (EbyE) fluctuations of the anisotropic flow in Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV. The interplay of soft and hard processes describes the violation of the mass hierarchy of meson and baryon elliptic and triangular flows at p_T > 2 GeV/c, the fall-off of the flow harmonics at intermediate transverse momenta, and the worsening of the number-of-constituent-quark (NCQ) scaling of elliptic/triangular flow at LHC compared to RHIC energies. The cross-talk of v_2 and v_3 leads to emergence of higher order harmonics in the model and to appearance of the ridge structure in dihadron angular correlations in a broad pseudorapidity range. HYDJET++ possesses also the dynamical EbyE fluctuations of the anisotropic flow. The model results agree well with the experimental data.

## Key claim
HYDJET++ combines a parameterized ideal hydrodynamic description of soft processes with a jet-quenching routine (PYQUEN) to reproduce the violation of mass ordering of elliptic and triangular flow, the worsening of NCQ scaling at LHC compared to RHIC, and the emergence of a ridge structure in dihadron correlations, all in Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV. The agreement with experimental data is described as "good," but the model underpredicts higher harmonic flow magnitudes (v₄–v₆) in the most central (0–5%) collisions.

## Method
The soft component (FASTMC) uses parameterized ideal hydrodynamics with chemical and thermal freeze-out, a table of over 360 meson and baryon states including charmed ones, and final-state resonance decays. The hard component (PYQUEN) propagates partons through a quark-gluon plasma with collisional and radiative energy losses, followed by Lund-model hadronization; the number of jets is proportional to the number of binary nucleon-nucleon collisions. Elliptic and triangular flow are generated by spatial eccentricities of the fireball controlled by three free parameters (ε(b), ε₃(b), δ(b)), and event-by-event fluctuations are introduced by smearing these parameters around their fixed values.

## Key numbers
- Pb+Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV are the primary system studied.
- Triangular flow v₃ of charged hadrons at centrality 20–30%: resonance decays increase the maximum of the v₃(p_T) distribution by about 20% and shift its position to higher p_T.
- 90% of hadrons have transverse momenta less than 1 GeV/c; therefore p_T-integrated v₃ values change insignificantly.
- NCQ scaling for v₂ is approximately fulfilled within the 20% accuracy limit in the interval 0.1 ≤ K E_T ≤ 0.7 GeV.
- The ratio v₆^(1/6)/v₂^(1/2) is independent of centrality at p_T ≥ 1 GeV/c; jets increase it by about 10% and lead to a rise of high-p_T tails at p_T ≥ 3 GeV/c.
- Event-by-event fluctuations are studied for charged particles with p_T ≥ 0.5 GeV/c and |η| < 2.5, matching ATLAS kinematic cuts.
- Centrality intervals for EbyE flow distributions: 5–10%, 20–25%, and 35–40%.

## Context
This work extends the HYDJET++ model (first described in the HYDJET++ paper) by incorporating triangular flow (following the triangular flow extension) and event-by-event fluctuations (following the EbyE fluctuations paper). It directly compares with ALICE data on NCQ scaling (ALICE collaboration, 2013) and ATLAS data on EbyE flow fluctuations (ATLAS Collaboration, 2013). The model's approach to the ridge phenomenon builds on the suggestion by Alver and Roland (2010) that triangular flow is important for understanding this signal. The results enable further study of the interplay between soft hydrodynamics and jet quenching in heavy-ion collisions, particularly for understanding the dynamical origin of flow fluctuations and the nonlinear contributions of v₂ and v₃ to higher harmonics.
