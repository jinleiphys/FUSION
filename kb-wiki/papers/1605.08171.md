# Effects of level inversion on the Coulomb displacement energies of the Ar-K isobaric analog state pairs

> The Coulomb displacement energies of the neutron-rich Ar-K isobaric analog state pairs with mass number $A=35-47$ are calculated within the relativistic mean field model and the effects of level inversion on the Coulomb displacement energies of the Ar-K isobaric analog state pairs are studied. The calculations are carried out in two cases, with and without consideration of the possible $2s_{1/2}$ and $1d_{3/2}$ proton level inversion of the neutron-rich Ar isotopes. Results show that the $2s_{1/2}$ and $1d_{3/2}$ level inversion of the neutron-rich Ar isotopes may reduce the Coulomb displacement energy by 0.06$\sim$0.17 MeV for the Ar-K isobaric analog state pairs. The results may provide a reference for experimental investigations of nuclear level inversion and a new test of the relativistic mean field model.

## Key claim

The possible inversion of the proton \(2s_{1/2}\) and \(1d_{3/2}\) orbitals in neutron-rich Ar isotopes may reduce the Coulomb displacement energy of Ar-K isobaric analog state pairs by 0.06 to 0.17 MeV, with the largest reduction occurring near mass number \(A=41\). The authors present this as a possible signature of level inversion, not a definitive measurement.

## Method

The Coulomb displacement energies for Ar-K isobaric analog state pairs with mass numbers \(A=35-47\) were calculated using the relativistic mean field model with NL-SH and TM1/TM2 parameter sets. The Coulomb potential from core protons and the distribution of excess neutrons were computed from single-particle wave functions. Calculations were performed in two cases: without level inversion (excess neutrons in the \(1d_{3/2}\) orbital) and with level inversion (excess neutrons in the \(2s_{1/2}\) orbital), and the differences between the two cases were extracted.

## Key numbers

- The \(2s_{1/2}\) and \(1d_{3/2}\) level inversion reduces the Coulomb displacement energy by 0.06 to 0.17 MeV for the Ar-K isobaric pairs.
- The largest reduction, about 0.17 MeV, occurs for the Ar-K pairs near \(A=41\) (specifically \(^{41}\)Ar-\(^{41}\)K gives 0.13602 MeV with NL-SH and 0.16741 MeV with TM1/TM2).
- For the \(^{37}\)Ar-\(^{37}\)K pair, the reduction is 0.05927 MeV (NL-SH) and 0.13083 MeV (TM1/TM2).
- For the \(^{46}\)Ar-\(^{46}\)K pair, the reduction is 0.1245 MeV (NL-SH) and 0.11743 MeV (TM1/TM2).
- In test calculations on 20 isobaric pairs from \(A=27\) to \(A=65\), the mean absolute deviation from experiment was 0.069 MeV (NL-SH) and 0.11 MeV (TM1/TM2), with maximum deviations of 0.17 MeV and 0.31 MeV respectively.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study of possible \(2s_{1/2}\) and \(1d_{3/2}\) level inversion in neutron-rich Ar isotopes using the relativistic mean field model (Wang 2014), which had proposed elastic electron-nucleus scattering as a probe. Here they instead examine Coulomb displacement energies as an alternative signature, following the suggestion of W. Mittig. The results are compared to experimental data from Antony (1997) and updated mass data from Audi and Wapstra (2012). The reduction in Coulomb displacement energy from level inversion is analogous to the 0.100-0.200 MeV reduction attributed to the halo in \(^{11}\)Li reported by Suzuki (1998). The work provides a reference for experimental searches for level inversion and a test of the relativistic mean field model.
