# On differences between even and odd anisotropic-flow harmonics in non-equilibrated systems

> To assess how anisotropic transverse flow is created in a system out of equilibrium, we compare several kinetic-theoretical models in the few-rescatterings regime. We compare the flow harmonics $v_n$ from three types of transport simulations, with either $2\to 2$ or $2\to 0$ collision kernels and in the former case allowing the particles to rescatter several times or not, and from analytical calculations neglecting the gain term of the Boltzmann equation. We find that the even flow harmonics are similar in all approaches, while the odd ones differ significantly. This suggests that while even $v_n$ harmonics may to a large extent be due to the anisotropic escape probability of particles, this is not the predominant mechanism underlying the odd $v_n$ coefficients.

## Key claim

Even flow harmonics ($v_2$, $v_4$, $v_6$) in the few-rescatterings regime can be largely attributed to the anisotropic escape probability of particles (the "escape mechanism"), but this mechanism is not the predominant source of odd flow harmonics ($v_3$), which instead arise from higher-order contributions in the cross section and depend significantly on the collision kernel used.

## Method

The authors performed four types of calculations for massless particles in a two-dimensional transverse plane: (1) numerical transport simulations with elastic $2\to 2$ binary collisions; (2) a "single-hit" version where particles scatter at most once with the $2\to 2$ kernel; (3) a $2\to 0$ collision kernel where particles become inactive after one collision; and (4) analytical calculations using only the loss term of the Boltzmann equation at linear order in the cross section. All calculations used the same initial distorted Gaussian density profile with controlled eccentricities $\varepsilon_n$, and results were compared at fixed mean number of rescatterings per particle $\Nresc$ rather than fixed Knudsen number.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow $v_2$ in the $2\to 0$ model differs at most by 20% from that in the full $2\to 2$ case for systems with $\Nresc \approx 0.02$ or $0.14$ and initial $\varepsilon_2 = 0.15$.
- Triangular flow $v_3$ is an order of magnitude smaller than $v_2$ in all simulations with initial $\varepsilon_3 = 0.15$.
- For $\Nresc \approx 0.02$, the $2\to 0$ results for $v_3$ lie about a factor 1.5 below the $2\to 2$ results; for $\Nresc \approx 0.08$ and $0.14$, the $2\to 0$ results are larger than the $2\to 2$ results.
- The analytical calculation yields $v_2(t) \propto t^3$ at early times $t \ll R$ for systems with initial $\varepsilon_2 = 0.15$.
- In the $2\to 2$ scenario, $v_2(t) \propto \Nresc$ holds to a very good approximation; in the $2\to 0$ scenario, $v_3 \propto \Nresc^2$ is suggested by the data for $\Nresc \approx 0.08$ and $0.14$.

## Context

This work extends recent studies on anisotropic flow generation in low-opacity systems, particularly addressing the "escape mechanism" proposed by He et al. (2015) in the AMPT framework, which claimed that most anisotropic flow arises from particles escaping the asymmetric geometry without scattering. The results contradict the finding of Kurkela et al. (2021) that triangular flow (energy-weighted) depends significantly on the collision kernel, showing instead that even harmonics are robust across scenarios while odd harmonics are not. The work enables systematic separation of escape and rescattering contributions to flow harmonics in small systems where fluid dynamics may not apply.
