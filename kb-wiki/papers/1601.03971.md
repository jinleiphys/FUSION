# Initial conditions from the shadowed Glauber model for Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV

> We study the initial conditions for Pb+Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=2.76$ TeV using the two component Monte-Carlo Glauber model with shadowing of the nucleons in the interior by the leading ones. The model parameters are fixed by comparing to the multiplicity data of p+Pb and Pb+Pb at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=5.02$ and $2.76$ TeV respectively. We then compute the centrality dependence of the eccentricities upto the fourth order as well as their event by event distributions. The inclusion of shadowing brings the Monte-Carlo Glauber model predictions in agreement with data as well as with results from other dynamical models of initial conditions based on gluon saturation at high energy nuclear collisions. Further, we find that the shadowed Glauber model provides the desired relative magnitude between the ellipticity and triangularity of the initial energy distribution required to explain the data on the even and odd flow harmonics $v_2$ and $v_3$ respectively at the LHC.

## Key claim

The shadowed Monte-Carlo Glauber model (shMCGM), which introduces a shadowing effect from leading nucleons on those located deeper inside the nucleus, brings the geometric Glauber model predictions for Pb+Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV into agreement with both LHC data and results from dynamical models based on gluon saturation (IP-Glasma), specifically for the relative magnitude of ellipticity and triangularity and for event-by-event distributions of eccentricities.

## Method

The authors extend the standard two-component Monte-Carlo Glauber model by introducing a shadowing factor S(n,λ) = e^{-nλ} that reduces the energy contribution of a participant nucleon based on the number n of other nucleons from the same nucleus that are in front of it. Model parameters (n₀, f, k, λ) are fixed by fitting to the charged-particle multiplicity distribution in p+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV and the centrality dependence of multiplicity in Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV, with λ determined from the event-by-event distribution of scaled elliptic flow v₂ assuming linear hydrodynamic response. The nucleon-nucleon cross-section is taken as 64 mb, and energy deposition is smeared with a Gaussian of width σ = 0.5 fm.

## Key numbers

- Nucleon-nucleon cross-section σ_NN = 64 mb for Pb+Pb at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV (compared to 42 mb at RHIC).
- Woods-Saxon parameters for Pb: radius R = 6.7 fm, surface diffusion δ = 0.54 fm.
- shMCGM parameters: n₀ = 4.05, f = 0.32, k = 1, λ = 0.08; MCGM parameters: n₀ = 4.05, f = 0.11, k = 1.
- Shadow parameter λ drops by approximately 25% from top RHIC energy to LHC energy (2.76 TeV).
- n₀ nearly doubles from √s_NN = 200 GeV to 2.76 TeV in both models.

## Context

This work extends the shadowed Glauber model previously applied to Au+Au and U+U collisions at top RHIC energy (Chatterjee, 2015) to LHC energies, addressing the known failure of standard MCGM to describe event-by-event flow data for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV. The results are compared with the IP-Glasma model (Gale, 2012) and ATLAS data on flow harmonics (Aad, 2013; Aad, 2014). The approach enables a simple geometric framework to reproduce observables that previously required dynamical saturation models, and the authors note that at lower energies (e.g., GSI-FAIR) the shadowing effect will be even more significant.
