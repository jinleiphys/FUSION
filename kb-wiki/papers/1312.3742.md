# Alternative way to understand the unexpected results of the JLab polarization experiments to measure the Sachs form factors ratio

> In the one-photon exchange approximation we discuss questions related to the interpretation of unexpected results of the JLab polarization experiments to measure the Sachs form factors ratio $G_E/G_M$ in the region $1. 0 \leq Q^2 \leq 8.5$ GeV$^2$. For this purpose, we developed an approach which essentially is a generalization of the constituent-counting rules of the perturbative QCD (pQCD) for the case of massive quarks. We assume that at the lower boundary of the considered region the hard-scattering mechanism of pQCD is realized. Within the framework of the developed approach we calculated the hard kernel of the proton current matrix elements $J^{\pm δ, δ}_{p}$ for the full set of spin combinations corresponding to the number of the spin-flipped quarks, which contribute to the proton transition without spin-flip ($J^{δ, δ}_{p}$) and with the spin-flip ($J^{-δ, δ}_{p}$). This allows us to state that (i) around the lower boundary of the considered region, the leading scaling behavior of the Sachs form factors has the form $G_E, G_M \sim 1/Q^6$, (ii) the dipole dependence ($G_E, G_M \sim 1/Q^4$) is realized in the asymptotic regime of pQCD when $τ\gg 1$ ($τ=Q^2/4M^2$) in the case when the quark transitions with spin-flip dominate, (iii) the asymptotic regime of pQCD in the JLab experiments has not yet been achieved, and (iv) the linear decrease of the ratio $G_{E}/G_{M}$ at $τ< 1$ is due to additional contributions to $J^{δ, δ}_{p}$ by spin-flip transitions of two quarks and an additional contribution to $J^{-δ, δ}_{p}$ by spin-flip transitions of three quarks.

## Key claim

In the one-photon exchange approximation, the unexpected linear decrease of the proton Sachs form factors ratio \(G_E/G_M\) observed in JLab polarization experiments can be explained by generalizing the constituent-counting rules of perturbative QCD to massive quarks, without invoking two-photon exchange contributions. The authors assume that the hard-scattering mechanism of pQCD starts at the lower boundary of the considered region, around \(Q_0 \sim 1\) GeV.

## Method

The authors developed a generalization of the constituent-counting rules of perturbative QCD for massive quarks, using the diagonal spin basis (DSB) formalism to calculate the hard kernel of the proton current matrix elements \(J^{\pm \delta, \delta}_{p}\) for all spin combinations. They considered four sets of quark spin-flip configurations contributing to proton non-spin-flip (\(J^{\delta, \delta}_{p}\)) and spin-flip (\(J^{-\delta, \delta}_{p}\)) transitions, assuming three quarks of mass \(m_q = M/3\) and equal momentum transfer fractions, and derived the scaling behavior of the Sachs form factors \(G_E\) and \(G_M\) from the product of three point-quark current amplitudes divided by \(Q^6\).

## Key numbers

* The JLab polarization experiments measured the Sachs form factors ratio \(G_E/G_M\) in the region \(1.0 \leq Q^2 \leq 8.5\) GeV\(^2\).
* The linear fit to the ratio \(R = \mu G_E/G_M\) from JLab data (Jones 2000, Gayou 2002) is \(R = 1 - 0.13\,(Q^2 - 0.04)\) for \(0.5 < Q^2 < 5.6\) GeV\(^2\).
* Around the lower boundary of the considered region, the leading scaling behavior is \(G_E, G_M \sim 1/Q^6\).
* The dipole dependence \(G_E, G_M \sim 1/Q^4\) is realized in the asymptotic pQCD regime when \(\tau = Q^2/4M^2 \gg 1\) and quark spin-flip transitions dominate.
* The Brodsky saturation law \(Q^2\, G_E/G_M \sim 4M^2 = \text{const}\) arises from the set (0,3) where three quarks undergo spin-flip transitions.

## Context

This work extends the constituent-counting rules of Brodsky (1973) and the pQCD framework of Brodsky (1980) and Chernyak (1984) to massive quarks, providing an alternative to the two-photon exchange explanation (Guichon 2003) for the discrepancy between JLab polarization experiments (Jones 2000, Gayou 2002, Puckett 2010, Puckett 2012) and Rosenbluth separation data (Qattan). The approach enables an analytical derivation of the linear decrease law for \(G_E/G_M\) at \(\tau < 1\) and predicts that the asymptotic pQCD regime has not yet been reached in JLab experiments, with the dipole dependence expected to be restored at higher \(Q^2\).
