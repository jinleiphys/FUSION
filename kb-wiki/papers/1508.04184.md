# Composite Fermions and the First-Landau-Level Fine Structure of the Fractional Quantum Hall Effect

> A set of scalar operators are employed to generate explicit representations of both hierarchy states (e.g., the series of fillings 1/3, 2/5, 3/7, ... ) and their conjugates (fillings 1, 2/3, 3/5, ...) as non-interacting quasi-electrons filling fine-structure sub-shells within the FLL. This yields, for planar and spherical geometries, a quasi-electron representation of the incompressible FLL state of filling p/(2p +1) in a magnetic field of strength B that is algebraically identical to the IQHE state of filling p in a magnetic field of strength B/(2p+1). The construction provides a precise definition of the quasi-electron/composite fermion that differs in some respects from common descriptions: they are eigenstates of L,Lz; they and the FLL subshells they occupy carry a third index I that is associated with breaking of scalar pairs; they absorb in their internal wave functions one, not two, units of magnetic flux; and they share a common, simple structure as vector products of a spinor creating an electron and one creating magnetic flux. We argue that these properties are a consequence of the breaking of the degeneracy of noninteracting electrons within the FLL by the scale-invariant Coulomb potential. We discuss the sense in which the wave function construction supports basic ideas of both composite fermion and hierarchical descriptions of the FQHE. We describe symmetries of the quasi-electrons at half filling, where a deep Fermi sea of quasi-electrons forms, and the quasi-electrons take on Majorana and pseudo-Dirac characters. Finally, we show that the wave functions can be viewed as fermionic excitations of the bosonic half-filled shell, producing at half filling an operator that differs from but plays the same role as the Pfaffian.

## Key claim

The paper provides an explicit analytic construction of fractional quantum Hall effect (FQHE) hierarchy states (fillings \(p/(2p+1)\) and their conjugates \(p/(2p-1)\)) as non-interacting quasi-electrons occupying fine-structure sub-shells within the first Landau level (FLL), where the quasi-electrons absorb one unit of magnetic flux (not two, as in common composite fermion descriptions) and carry a third index \(\mathcal{I}\) associated with breaking of scalar pairs.

## Method

The authors employ scalar operators originally introduced by Ginocchio and Haxton (1996) to generate explicit representations of hierarchy and conjugate states on both the sphere and the plane. These operators create quasi-electrons as vector products of a spinor creating an electron and one creating a single unit of magnetic flux, yielding wave functions that are eigenstates of angular momentum \(L, L_z\) and that fill fine-structure sub-shells induced by the Coulomb interaction. The construction eliminates all derivatives from the original GH operators, allowing wave functions to be expressed in closed form for both spherical and planar geometries.

## Key numbers

* The quasi-electron representation of the incompressible FLL state at filling \(\nu = p/(2p+1)\) in a magnetic field of strength \(B\) is algebraically identical to the IQHE state of filling \(\nu = p\) in a magnetic field of strength \(B/(2p+1)\).
* The Laughlin \(\nu = 1/3\) state corresponds to \(m = 3\) in the wave function \(\prod_{j>k} (z_j - z_k)^m e^{-\sum_i |z_i|^2/2}\), with \(m\) odd to ensure antisymmetry.
* The number of FLL single-particle states on the sphere is \(2S+1\), where \(S\) is the monopole strength, and the fractional filling \(\nu\) is defined as the large-\(N\) limit of \(N/(2S+1)\).
* The GH operators generate hierarchy states when \(s = 0, 1/2, 1, \ldots\) with \(s \le \ell\), and the electron number is given by \(N = (2s+1)(2\ell+1)\).

## Context

This work extends the analytic construction of Ginocchio and Haxton (1996) for FQHE wave functions on the sphere, which itself generalized Laughlin's (1983) variational wave functions by introducing operators that create higher-density defects beyond \(\nu = 1/3\). The paper addresses criticisms by Dyakonov (2002) regarding the lack of a simple FLL formulation of hierarchy states, the absence of explicit conjugate state representations, and the inadequate theoretical foundation for composite fermions as introduced by Jain (1989). The construction enables systematic improvements of wave functions in the spirit of an effective theory and provides a framework for understanding the \(\nu = 1/2\) state, including connections to the Pfaffian operator and Majorana/pseudo-Dirac descriptions.
