# A Primer on Instantons in QCD

> These are the (twice) extended notes of a set of lectures given at the ``12th Workshop on Hadronic Interactions'' at the IF/UERJ, Rio de Janeiro (31. 5. - 2. 6. 2000). The lectures aim at introducing essential concepts of instanton physics, with emphasis on the role of instantons in generating tunneling amplitudes, vacuum structure, and the induced quark interactions associated with the axial anomaly. A few examples for the impact of instantons on the physics of hadrons are also mentioned.

## Key claim

This lecture note argues that instantons provide the semiclassical description of tunneling processes in quantum chromodynamics, but the treatment is limited to the saddle-point approximation of the Euclidean path integral, which requires analytical continuation to imaginary time and assumes that the quantum fluctuations around the instanton solutions are Gaussian and that all eigenvalues of the fluctuation operator are positive (an assumption that fails for "bounce" solutions).

## Method

The paper develops the semiclassical approximation (SCA) for tunneling by first reviewing the WKB method in quantum mechanics, then introducing the path integral representation and analytically continuing to imaginary time (Wick rotation) where the potential is inverted. It expands the Euclidean action around classical saddle-point solutions (instantons) to second order in fluctuations, diagonalizes the fluctuation operator, and performs Gaussian integrations to obtain the propagator as the exponential of the classical action times the inverse square root of the fluctuation determinant.

## Key numbers

- For a macroscopic particle with mass 1 g and action difference δS ≈ 1.6 × 10²⁶ ħ between two paths, the phase difference δφ ≈ 1.6 × 10²⁶ rad ≫ π, making the alternative path irrelevant
- For an electron with mass 10⁻²⁷ g, the same action difference δS ≈ (1/6) ħ gives δφ ≈ (1/6) rad ≪ π, keeping the alternative path within the coherence region
- The ground state energy is obtained as E₀ = -ħ lim_{T→∞} (1/T) ln Z(x_f, x_i) from the Euclidean propagator

## Context

This paper extends the instanton formalism introduced by Polyakov (1977) and Coleman (1980) from quantum mechanics to QCD, building on the earlier WKB treatment of tunneling by Wentzel, Kramers, and Brillouin. It provides a pedagogical foundation for understanding instanton-liquid vacuum models (Schäfer and Shuryak, 1998), instanton-induced hadron interactions (Horn and Mandula, 1978), and the instanton-based operator product expansion (Forkel, 2000). The work enables further study of instanton contributions to spontaneous chiral symmetry breaking, hadron phenomenology, and lattice simulations of QCD.
