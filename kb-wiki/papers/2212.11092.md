# Entanglement generation in few-nucleon scattering

> Inspired by a recent Letter [S. R. Beane et al., Phys. Rev. Lett. 122, 102001(2019)], the entanglement generated in the elastic $S$-wave scattering of $p+{}^{3}\text{He}$ and $n+{}^3\text{H}$ is studied, where the proton, neutron, ${}^{3}$He, and ${}^3$H are all regarded as qubits. To deal with the Coulomb interaction between the proton and ${}^{3}$He, we derive the entanglement power, a physical quantity that measures the average entanglement generated by a scattering process, for charged qubits within the screening method. The entanglement power in the aforementioned two few-nucleon scatterings is found to be generally much smaller than that in the $S$-wave $n+p$ scattering at low energies, with the corresponding cluster effective field theories possessing an enhanced approximate $\text{SU}(2)_1\otimes\text{SU}(2)_{2}$ symmetry at leading order. Our study suggests that the entanglement generation capacities of effective interactions between nucleons and light nuclei could be more suppressed than realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions at low energies.

## Key claim

The entanglement power in low-energy S-wave p+³He and n+³H scattering is generally much smaller than in S-wave n+p scattering, suggesting that the entanglement generation capacities of effective interactions between nucleons and light nuclei could be more suppressed than realistic nucleon-nucleon interactions at low energies, with the corresponding cluster effective field theories possessing an enhanced approximate SU(2)₁⊗SU(2)₂ symmetry at leading order.

## Method

The authors treat proton, neutron, ³He, and ³H as spin-1/2 qubits and compute the entanglement power, defined as the average 2-entropy over all unentangled spin in-states, for elastic S-wave scattering. For the p+³He system, they derive the entanglement power for charged qubits using the screening method to handle the Coulomb interaction, cutting off the Coulomb potential at a large screening radius r_S. The nuclear phase shifts in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels are obtained from Coulomb-modified effective range expansions (EREs) using experimentally extracted effective range parameters up to O(p⁴), with the entanglement power depending only on the difference between the singlet and triplet nuclear phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- The scattering length for the ¹S₀ channel in p+³He is a₀^C = 11.1 ± 0.4 fm, with effective range r₀^C = 1.58 ± 0.12 fm and shape parameter P₀^C = -4 ± 0.8 fm³
- The scattering length for the ³S₁ channel in p+³He is a₁^C = 9.04 ± 0.14 fm, with effective range r₁^C = 1.50 ± 0.06 fm and shape parameter P₁^C = 0.36 ± 0.32 fm³
- The entanglement power is given analytically as E(S) = (1/6) sin²[2(δ₀^N - δ₁^N)], where δ₀^N and δ₁^N are the nuclear phase shifts in the ¹S₀ and ³S₁ channels
- Phase shifts are calculated for relative momenta p ≤ 80 MeV, corresponding to relative energies ≤ 6 MeV, with experimental phase shift values tabulated at p = 48.71, 57.63, 64.95, and 76.20 MeV

## Context

This work extends the study of entanglement generation in hadron-hadron scattering by Beane et al. (2019) to scatterings involving light nuclei, specifically p+³He and n+³H. The authors note that previous knowledge on entanglement generation capacities of nuclear interactions was limited to hadron-hadron interactions (Beane 2019, Beane 2021, Liu 2022). The p+³He and n+³H scatterings have been studied in ab initio calculations (Lazauskas 2004, Deltuva 2006, Viviani 2011) and in pionless effective field theories (Kirscher 2011, Contessi 2022, Schafer 2022). The results enable understanding of how entanglement structures evolve from nucleons to nuclei and provide information on the entanglement generation capacities of effective interactions between nucleons and light nuclei.
