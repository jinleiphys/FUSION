# Intense X-ray laser-induced proton emission from halo nuclei

> We investigate the intense X-ray laser-induced proton emission from halo nuclei within the framework of a nonperturbative quantum $S$-matrix approach. We have analytically deduced the angular differential as well as the total multi-photon rates of the proton emissions. For a linearly polarized X-ray laser field, we find that the angular distributions of proton emission sensitively depend on the laser frequency and show an interesting petal structure with increasing the laser frequency as well as the number of absorbed photons. Meanwhile, we find the Coulomb repulsion potential between the proton and the remainder nucleus has a strong hindering effect on the total multi-photon rates of the proton emissions and leads to the blue shifts of the multi-photon transition frequency. Moreover, the polarization effects of laser fields on total rates of proton emission have also been addressed. We find that the polarized ellipticity corresponding to the maximum of the total rates depend on the laser frequency showing a transition from perturbative to nonperturbative proton emission. The underlying mechanism of the above findings is uncovered, and some implications are discussed.

# Intense X-ray laser-induced proton emission from halo nuclei

## Key claim

The paper claims that intense X-ray lasers can induce multiphoton proton emission from one-proton halo nuclei, with angular distributions and total rates that depend sensitively on laser frequency, intensity, and polarization; however, the Coulomb repulsion between the emitted proton and the remainder nucleus strongly suppresses the total emission rate and shifts the multiphoton transition frequencies to higher values (blue shift), an effect that is more significant than in atomic ionization due to the femtometer-scale distances involved.

## Method

The authors employ a nonperturbative quantum S-matrix approach within the strong field approximation (SFA), using Coulomb-Volkov states to describe the final continuum state of the emitted proton and a Yukawa-type wavefunction for the initial bound halo proton state. They analytically derive the angular differential and total multiphoton emission rates for an arbitrarily polarized monochromatic X-ray laser field, then numerically evaluate these rates for the one-proton halo nucleus ⁸B, using a proton separation energy of 137 keV and an rms radius of 4.73 fm.

## Key numbers

- Proton separation energy of ⁸B: E_b = 137 keV
- Root-mean-square radius of the proton halo in ⁸B: R_rms ≈ 4.73 fm
- Laser intensity used for angular distribution calculations: I = 10²³ W/cm²
- Laser frequencies studied for angular distributions: ω = 100 keV, 80 keV, 70 keV, 66 keV (two-photon and three-photon absorption regimes)
- Total rate calculations performed at intensities up to 10²⁵ W/cm² and frequencies from 40 keV to 100 keV
- Transition from perturbative to nonperturbative behavior identified at ω/E_b ≈ 0.45 (low frequency) and ω/E_b ≈ 0.63 (high frequency) for intensity I = 10²² W/cm²

## Context

This work extends prior theoretical studies of laser-assisted nuclear photoeffect in deuterons (Li 2021) and proton emission from halo nuclei assisted by combined gamma-ray and low-frequency laser fields (Anis, Anis2, Peter). It differs by considering intense X-ray lasers alone (without a separate gamma-ray source) and by including arbitrary laser polarization. The results are relevant for upcoming experiments at ultra-intense laser facilities such as the Extreme Light Infrastructure (ELI) and the superintense ultrafast laser facility of Shanghai (Guo 2018). The paper suggests that the approach could be extended to other halo nuclei such as ²⁶P (Loureiro) and ¹⁰⁰Sn (Bielich), and that more realistic initial states from halo effective field theory (Phillips, Hammer) should be considered in future work.
