# Multi-quasiparticle gamma-band structure in neutron-deficient Ce and Nd isotopes

> The newly developed multi-quasiparticle triaxial projected shell-model approach is employed to study the high-spin band structures in neutron-deficient even-even Ce- and Nd-isotopes. It is observed that gamma-bands are built on each intrinsic configuration of the triaxial mean-field deformation. Due to the fact that a triaxial configuration is a superposition of several K-states, the projection from these states results in several low-lying bands originating from the same intrinsic configuration. This generalizes the well-known concept of the surface gamma-oscillation in deformed nuclei based on the ground-state to gamma-bands built on multi-quasiparticle configurations. This new feature provides an alternative explanation on the observation of two I=10 aligning states in 134Ce and both exhibiting a neutron character.

# Multi-quasiparticle gamma-band structure in neutron-deficient Ce and Nd isotopes

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that gamma-vibrational bands can be built not only on the ground state of deformed nuclei, but also on multi-quasiparticle configurations in triaxially deformed nuclei. This provides an alternative explanation for the observation of two I=10 aligning states in 134Ce that both exhibit a neutron character, where the second state arises from a gamma-vibration built on the two-quasineutron configuration rather than from a two-quasiproton configuration.

## Method

The authors employ the extended triaxial projected shell-model (TPSM) approach, which performs three-dimensional angular-momentum projection from a triaxially deformed quasiparticle vacuum and includes 0-quasiparticle, two-proton, two-neutron, and 4-quasiparticle configurations. The shell-model Hamiltonian includes pairing plus quadrupole-quadrupole interactions with a quadrupole-pairing term, and the basis is constructed from Nilsson single-particle states with three major shells (N=3,4,5) for both neutrons and protons. Calculations are performed for four even-even isotopic chains of 128-134Ce and 132-138Nd, with deformation parameters taken from Möller and Nix (1995) and triaxiality parameters determined from projected potential energy surfaces.

## Key numbers

- Deformation parameters for 128Ce: epsilon = 0.250, epsilon' = 0.120, gamma = 26 degrees
- Deformation parameters for 134Ce: epsilon = 0.150, epsilon' = 0.100, gamma = 34 degrees
- Deformation parameters for 132Nd: epsilon = 0.267, epsilon' = 0.120, gamma = 24 degrees
- Deformation parameters for 138Nd: epsilon = 0.170, epsilon' = 0.110, gamma = 33 degrees
- Monopole pairing strength parameters: G1 = 20.82, G2 = 13.58
- Quadrupole pairing strength proportionality constant: 0.18 relative to monopole pairing
- In 134Ce, the two lowest 10+ states are observed at excitation energies 3.2086 MeV and 3.7193 MeV
- The (2,0) bandhead energy is approximately 0.8 MeV for 128,130Ce
- The (4,0) bandhead is slightly below 2 MeV for 128,130Ce
- In 132Nd, the (2,0) and (4,0) bandheads are at approximately 1 MeV and 2 MeV excitation energy, respectively

## Context

This work extends the earlier triaxial projected shell-model approach of Sheikh and Hara (1999) and Sun et al. (2002), which used only the triaxially deformed quasiparticle vacuum configuration and could not describe quasiparticle excitations. The present extension includes 2- and 4-quasiparticle configurations as developed by Javid et al. (2008), enabling the study of gamma-bands built on multi-quasiparticle states. The results address the long-standing puzzle from the g-factor measurements by Zell et al. (1982) showing that both lowest I=10 states in 134Ce have neutron character, contrary to the expectation that one should be a two-quasiproton state. The paper predicts that in 136Nd and 138Nd, the two lowest I=10 states should both have proton character with positive g-factors, which can be tested experimentally.
