# The compressibility of quark matter under strong magnetic field in the NJL model

> The compressibility of magnetized quark matter is investigated in the SU(2) NJL model. The increases of the chemical potential and the temperature can reduce the compressibility, and lead to the much stiffer equation of state. The variation of the compressibility with the magnetic field will depend on the phase region. Due to the anisotropic structure, the compressibility is different in the directions parallel and perpendicular to the field. The discontinuity of longitudinal compressibility with the chemical potential and the temperature captures the signature of a first-order chiral phase transition and the crossover at high temperature. Moreover, the magnetic-field-and-temperature running coupling would have an important effect on the position of the phase transition. Under the lowest landau level approximation at zero temperature, the longitudinal compressibility has a direct inverse proportional relation to the magnetic field strength and the chemical potential square as $κ^\parallel_{\mathrm{LLL},χ}\propto1/(eB μ^2)$.

## Key claim

The compressibility of magnetized quark matter is anisotropic and depends on the phase region; the longitudinal compressibility shows a discontinuity at the first-order chiral phase transition and a peak at the chiral crossover, while under the lowest Landau level approximation at zero temperature it follows an inverse proportional relation to the magnetic field strength and the chemical potential squared as \(\kappa^\parallel_{\mathrm{LLL},\chi}\propto 1/(eB \mu^2)\).

## Method

The authors use the SU(2) Nambu–Jona-Lasinio (NJL) model in the mean-field approximation with a magnetic-field-and-temperature-dependent running coupling constant. The thermodynamic potential is computed including vacuum, magnetic field, and medium contributions, with ultraviolet divergences handled by a momentum cutoff and the Magnetic Field Independent Regularization (MFIR) scheme. The longitudinal and transverse compressibilities are derived from the quark number density and its derivative with respect to chemical potential, and the anisotropic pressures are obtained from the thermodynamic potential and magnetization.

## Key numbers

- At fixed temperature \(T = 150\) MeV and chemical potential \(\mu = 260\) MeV, the critical magnetic field for the chiral crossover is \(B_c = 8.1 \times 10^{19}\) Gauss.
- The quark dynamical mass is solved using parameters \(m_u = m_d = 5.6\) MeV, \(\Lambda = 587.9\) MeV, and \(G = 2.44/\Lambda^2\).
- At \(T = 50\) MeV, first-order phase transitions occur at magnetic fields \(B = 5 \times 10^{19}\) Gauss and \(B = 1 \times 10^{20}\) Gauss, while crossovers occur at \(B = 2 \times 10^{18}\) Gauss and \(B = 2 \times 10^{19}\) Gauss.
- Under the lowest Landau level approximation at zero temperature in the chiral limit, the longitudinal compressibility is \(\kappa^\parallel_{\mathrm{LLL},\chi} = \frac{2\pi^2}{N_c(|q_u|+|q_d|)eB \mu^2}\).
- The transverse compressibility \(\kappa^\perp\) is much larger than the longitudinal compressibility \(\kappa^\parallel\) at \(B = 1.0 \times 10^{20}\) Gauss and \(T = 50\) MeV, but the difference becomes unobvious at larger chemical potentials.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of compressibility in the NJL and PNJL models under zero magnetic field by Bhattacharyya and Iwasaki, and incorporates the magnetic-field-and-temperature-dependent coupling from lattice simulations by Farias (2014) to capture inverse magnetic catalysis. The results enable a deeper understanding of the equation of state of quark matter in compact stars and heavy-ion collisions, and provide a complementary probe of the QCD phase diagram at finite density, temperature, and strong magnetic fields.
