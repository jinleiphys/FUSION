# Effect of thermal gluon absorption and medium fluctuations on heavy flavour nuclear modification factor at RHIC and LHC energies

> The presence of thermal gluons reduces the stimulated gluon emission off a heavy quark propagating in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) while absorption causes reduction of the radiative energy loss. On the other hand, the chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations present in the QGP lead to collisional energy gain of the heavy quark. The net effect of the thermal gluon absorption and field fluctuations is a reduction of the total energy loss of the heavy quark, prominent at the lower momenta. We consider both kind of the energy gains along with the usual losses, and compute the nuclear modification factor ($R_{AA}$) of heavy mesons, viz., $D$ and $B$ mesons. The calculations have been compared with the experimental measurements in Au-Au collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 200$ GeV from STAR and PHENIX experiments at the RHIC and Pb-Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{NN}} = 2.76$ TeV and $5.02$ TeV from CMS and ALICE experiments at the LHC. We find a significant effect of the total energy gain due to thermal gluon absorption and field fluctuations on heavy flavour suppression, especially at the lower transverse momenta.

## Key claim

The net effect of thermal gluon absorption and chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations is a reduction of the total energy loss of heavy quarks, prominent at lower momenta; this reduction significantly affects the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) of D and B mesons, especially at lower transverse momenta, but the calculations for D mesons at 5.02 TeV still show larger suppression compared to experimental data at high p_T.

## Method

The author computed the nuclear modification factor (R_AA) of D and B mesons by combining radiative energy loss (using the generalized dead cone approach) and collisional energy loss (using the Thoma-Gyulassy formalism) with energy gains from thermal gluon absorption and chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations. Heavy quark production cross sections were calculated at leading order and next-to-leading order with CT10 parton distribution functions, including nuclear shadowing via EPS09, and fragmentation was performed using the Peterson fragmentation function. The QGP medium evolution was modeled as an isentropic cylindrical expansion with parameters (initial temperature, freeze-out time, path length) determined for each centrality class at RHIC and LHC energies.

## Key numbers

- Initial temperature T_0 = 0.303 GeV for 0-10% centrality Au-Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV
- Initial temperature T_0 = 0.467 GeV for 0-10% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV
- Initial temperature T_0 = 0.469 GeV for 0-100% centrality Pb-Pb collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.02 TeV
- Average path length <L> = 5.63 fm for 0-10% centrality Au-Au at 200 GeV; 5.73 fm for 0-10% Pb-Pb at 2.76 TeV; 4.18 fm for 0-100% Pb-Pb at 5.02 TeV
- Charm quark mass m_c = 1.25 GeV and bottom quark mass m_b = 4.2 GeV used in calculations
- Strong coupling constant alpha_s = 0.3 (constant, momentum and temperature independent)
- Peterson fragmentation parameters: epsilon_c = 0.016 for c quarks, epsilon_b = 0.0012 for b quarks

## Context

This work extends previous heavy quark energy loss studies by incorporating thermal gluon absorption (as argued by Wang and Wang) and chromo-electromagnetic field fluctuations (as calculated by Florkowski and collaborators) which were ignored in earlier calculations such as those by the author's group (Sheikh, previous work). The calculations are compared with experimental measurements from STAR and PHENIX at RHIC and from CMS and ALICE at LHC, showing that these energy gain mechanisms reduce the suppression of heavy flavour mesons at lower momenta. Future precise measurements and better constrained model calculations are needed to understand the different energy gain processes and their consequences on heavy flavour observables.
