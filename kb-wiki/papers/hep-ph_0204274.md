# Classical gluodynamics in curved space--time and the soft Pomeron

> QCD at the classical level possesses scale invariance which is broken by quantum effects. This ``dimensional transmutation'' phenomenon can be mathematically described by formulating classical gluodynamics in a curved, conformally flat, space-time with non-vanishing cosmological constant. We study QCD high-energy scattering in this theory. We find that the properties of the scattering amplitude at small momentum transfer are determined by the energy density of vacuum fluctuations. The approach gives rise to the power growth of the total hadron-hadron cross section with energy, i.e. the Pomeron. The intercept of the Pomeron and the multiplicity of produced particles are evaluated. We also speculate about a possible link between conformal anomaly and TeV scale quantum gravity.

## Key claim
The paper derives the soft Pomeron intercept from classical gluodynamics formulated in a curved, conformally flat spacetime with a non-vanishing cosmological constant, but the result depends on the identification of the dilaton with the broad scalar sigma resonance and on the specific numerical values of the vacuum energy density and dilaton mass, yielding an intercept range rather than a single value.

## Method
The authors construct an effective Lagrangian for gluodynamics that includes a dilaton field, derived from coupling QCD to conformally flat gravity via the Einstein-Hilbert action with a cosmological constant term representing the vacuum energy density. They then compute high-energy scattering amplitudes in the leading logarithmic approximation by summing ladder diagrams where gluons exchanged in the t-channel produce dilatons in each rung, using the Weizsacker-Williams approximation for the gluon propagator. The ultraviolet cutoff M0 is fixed by requiring vacuum stability, i.e., cancellation of dilaton and gluon contributions to the vacuum energy density.

## Key numbers
- Vacuum energy density in pure gluodynamics: vac = 0.012 GeV^4, derived from the QCD vacuum energy density vac_QCD = (0.24 GeV)^4 via the relation vac ≈ (11/9) * 3 * vac_QCD.
- Dilaton mass: m = 0.4 to 0.5 GeV, identified with the broad physical sigma resonance.
- Ultraviolet cutoff: M0 = 3.0 to 2.8 GeV, obtained from Eq. (CONJ) using the above vac and m values.
- Soft Pomeron intercept: alpha_P = 1.047 to 1.085, from Eq. (CHTOTO).
- Gravitational constant: G = 0.2 GeV^{-2}, from Eq. (MASS).
- Average number of charged pions per unit of rapidity: <dN_pi^ch/dy> = 0.14 to 0.26.

## Context
This work extends the approach of Kharzeev and Levin (2001) by replacing the scalar glueball coupling to two gluons with a dilaton field derived from coupling QCD to conformally flat gravity, which changes the dependence of the intercept on the scalar particle mass. The result agrees with the phenomenological analysis of Donnachie and Landshoff (1992) for the soft Pomeron intercept. The paper also speculates on a possible link between the conformal anomaly and TeV-scale quantum gravity, as proposed by Arkani-Hamed, Dimopoulos, and Dvali (1998).
