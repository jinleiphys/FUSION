# Electromagnetic form factors of hyperons in the timelike region: A short review

## Key claim
The review argues that while cross section data for hyperon electromagnetic form factors in the timelike region have increased dramatically, the separation of electric and magnetic form factors and their relative phase remains experimentally unresolved, with tensions between different experiments and between data and theoretical expectations in several channels.

## Method
The authors review experimental cross section data from e+e- collisions producing hyperon-antihyperon pairs (Lambda, Sigma, Xi, Omega, Lambda_c) collected by BESIII, BaBar, Belle, DM2, and CLEO collaborations. They compare these data to theoretical calculations based on final-state interactions (FSI) in the baryon-antibaryon system using potentials constrained by antiproton-proton scattering data from the PS185 experiment at LEAR, and to vector-meson dominance (VMD) model calculations that include vector mesons such as rho, omega, phi, and higher-mass states.

## Key numbers
- The Lambda-antilambda threshold is at 2M_Lambda = 2231.37 MeV; a BESIII data point at 2232.4 MeV (about 1 MeV above threshold) deviates from the overall trend and is not reproduced by FSI calculations
- The Lambda-Sigma0 transition cross section is noticeably smaller than that for Lambda-antilambda; the normalization in Haidenbauer (2020) fixed the cross section to 0.04 nb at 2.4 GeV
- For the Sigma+ antichannel, BESIII data very close to threshold show large values for |G_E/G_M| and Delta-phi that are difficult to achieve with a regular hyperon-antihyperon interaction
- For the Xi channels, the empirical cross section ratio between neutral and charged channels is practically compatible with unity over a larger energy range
- The Omega-antilambda cross section is practically compatible with zero within the achieved accuracy
- For Lambda_c, the latest BESIII measurement (2023) practically excludes the presence of a resonance near threshold, showing a more or less constant cross section

## Context
This review extends the line of work on baryon electromagnetic form factors from the spacelike region (probed by electron-proton scattering, reviewed by Denig (2012), Pacetti (2014), and others) to the timelike region accessible only through e+e- annihilation. It builds on earlier hyperon form factor measurements by DM2 (1990) and BaBar (2007), and on theoretical frameworks for final-state interactions developed by Haidenbauer (2014, 2016, 2020) and Kang (2015) that were previously applied to nucleon-antinucleon systems. The review enables future experimental programs at BESIII and Belle II to resolve tensions between data sets and to measure differential cross sections and polarization observables needed to separate electric and magnetic form factors.
