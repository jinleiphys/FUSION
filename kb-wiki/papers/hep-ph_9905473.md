# Skyrmions and Bags in the 2D-O(3) model

> Localized static solutions of the 2D-O(3) model are investigated in a representation with the 3-vector field $\vec Phi$ split into the unit vector $\hat Phi$ and the modulus $Φ$. As in the nonlinear version of the model this allows for the definition of a topological winding number $B$, and for the separation of the complete configuration space into distinct $B$-sectors. For small values of the $Φ^4$-coupling strength the stable energy minima in these sectors are characterized by bag formation in the modulus field which in the standard cartesian representation of the linear O(3) model would be unstable towards decay into the trivial B=0 vacuum. Stabilized by $B$-conservation they exhibit a surprising variety of very appealing features for multiply charged systems. With the total charge bound into one common deep bag opposite ways of distributing the topological charge density inside the bag can be realized: Pointlike structures which retain the individuality of single constituents (or doubly charged pairs), or a deconfined charge density spread uniformly throughout the interior of the bag. It is suggested that this extension supplies a crucial link to overcome the unsatisfactory existing mismatch between multiskyrmion configurations and nuclear structure.

## Key claim
For small values of the Φ⁴-coupling strength λ, stable energy minima in sectors of fixed topological winding number B are characterized by bag formation in the modulus field; these configurations would be unstable toward decay into the trivial B=0 vacuum in the standard cartesian representation of the linear O(3) model, but are stabilized by B-conservation in the radial-angular representation.

## Method
The 3-vector field Φ is split into a unit vector ĥ and a modulus Φ, allowing definition of a conserved topological winding number B. Static solutions are obtained by numerical relaxation on an 80×80 lattice with ℓ=10 using a Metropolis cooling algorithm, for a Lagrangian containing a Φ⁴ potential, a Zeeman term with strength α, and a Skyrme current-current coupling. Two versions of the Skyrme term are compared: one using the topological current ρμ defined from the angular fields, and one using Tμ = Φ³ρμ defined from the full vector field.

## Key numbers
- For α=1, λ=10, the B=1 bag has total classical energy E₁=6.85.
- For α=1, λ=10, the central winding density for B=1 is ρ₀=0.025, converging toward ρ₀≈0.017 for large B.
- The analytic estimate for the asymptotic central density in the deconfined case (λ=10, α=1) is ρ₀ = √3/ℓ² = 0.0173.
- For the alternative Skyrme term (Φ⁶ factor) with α=1, λ=10, the B=1 energy is E₁=1.152; the B=2 sector has a '1+1' configuration at E₂/2=0.943 and a lower '2' configuration at E₂/2=0.663; the B=3 '1+2' configuration gives E₃/3=0.697; the B=4 '2+2' configuration gives E₄/4=0.543.
- For α=0.1, the transition from skyrmion to bag occurs near log λ ≈ 3.2 for B=1.

## Context
This work extends the nonlinear 2D-O(3) model (Belavin-Polyakov monopoles, 1975) by including a dynamical modulus field, bridging the linear and nonlinear versions. It contrasts with the standard cartesian representation where B≠0 configurations are unstable for small λ. The results suggest that the constraint Φ≡1 is too restrictive for describing nuclei as multiskyrmion configurations, and that the 3D-O(4) model (relevant for nuclear structure via the Skyrme-Witten conjecture) should be investigated with a similar radial-angular decomposition. The pair formation observed for the alternative Skyrme term resembles the pair formation found for skyrmions in the λ→∞ limit (Durham group).
