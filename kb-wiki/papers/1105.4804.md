# Gauge links for transverse momentum dependent correlators at tree-level

> In this paper we discuss the incorporation of gauge links in hadronic matrix elements that describe the soft hadronic physics in high energy scattering processes. In this description the matrix elements appear in soft correlators and they contain non-local combinations of quark and gluon fields. In our description we go beyond the collinear approach in which case also the dependence on transverse momenta of partons is taken into consideration. The non-locality in the transverse direction leads to a complex gauge link structure for the full process, in which color is entangled, even at tree-level. We show that at tree-level in a 1-parton unintegrated (1PU) situation, in which only the transverse momentum of one of the initial state hadrons is relevant, one can get a factorized expression involving transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distribution functions. We point out problems at the level of two initial state hadrons, even for relatively simple processes such as Drell-Yan scattering.

## Key claim

The paper shows that at tree-level, in the "1-parton unintegrated (1PU)" situation where only the transverse momentum of one initial-state hadron is relevant, a factorized expression involving transverse momentum dependent (TMD) distribution functions can be obtained, but this factorization breaks down when two initial-state hadrons are involved, even for simple processes such as Drell-Yan scattering.

## Method

The authors use a diagrammatic approach to include all gluon fields that contribute at leading order and tree-level, resumming collinear gluon fields into path-ordered exponentials (Wilson lines) via Ward identities. They analyze the color flow in hard scattering processes by constructing correlators of quark and gluon fields, then systematically include multi-gluon interactions from different correlators coupling to the same parton line, which produces intertwined Wilson lines. The analysis is performed for a generic two-to-two hard process with truncated amplitudes, working in the Sudakov decomposition for parton momenta.

## Key numbers

- The squared hadronic mass scale is taken as \(M^2 \sim 1\) GeV\(^2\), while the hard scale is \(Q^2 \gg M^2\)
- The momentum fraction \(x = p \cdot n\) is of order \(\mathscr O(1)\), with transverse momenta \(p_\st \sim M\) and the light-like vector \(n \sim 1/Q\)
- The expansion parameter for power corrections is \(M^2/Q^2\), with contributions at this order explicitly excluded from the tree-level treatment

## Context

This work extends the collinear treatment of parton distribution functions to include transverse momentum dependence, building on the foundational work of Collins (1981) and Jaffe (1991) on correlators. It addresses the gauge link structure previously studied by Belitsky (2002), Boer (2003), and Bomhof (2004-2007), and connects to the Efremov-Teryaev-Qiu-Sterman (ETQS) functions studied by Efremov (1981, 1984), Qiu (1991, 1998), and others. The paper enables the analysis of weighted asymmetries in terms of transverse moments of TMD functions, and identifies the gluonic pole matrix elements as the source of non-universality and factorization complications.
