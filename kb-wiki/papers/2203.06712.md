# Memory effects on energy loss and diffusion of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma

> We study the dynamics of heavy quarks in a thermalized quark-gluon plasma with a time-correlated thermal noise, $η$. In this case it is said that $η$ has memory. We use an integro-differential Langevin equation in which the memory enters via the thermal noise and the dissipative force. We assume that the time correlations of the noise decay exponentially on a time scale, $τ$, that we treat as a free parameter. We compute the effects of $τ\neq 0$ on the thermalization time of the heavy quarks, on their momentum broadening and on the nuclear modification factor. We find that overall memory slows down the momentum evolution of heavy quarks: in fact, transverse momentum broadening and the formation of $R_{AA}$ are slowed down by memory and the thermalization time of the heavy quarks become larger. The potential impact on other observables is discussed briefly.

## Key claim

Memory effects from a time-correlated thermal noise slow down the momentum evolution of heavy quarks in the quark-gluon plasma, increasing the thermalization time and delaying both transverse momentum broadening and the formation of the nuclear modification factor \(R_{AA}\), with the memory time \(\tau\) treated as a free parameter.

## Method

The authors solve an integro-differential Langevin equation for heavy quark momentum, where the dissipative force and thermal noise both incorporate memory via an exponential time correlation with decay constant \(\tau\). The colored noise is generated numerically using an ancillary stochastic process, and the heavy quark evolution is simulated in a thermalized quark-gluon plasma at fixed temperature, using diffusion coefficients from perturbative QCD (pQCD) at high temperature and from a quasiparticle model (QPM) at low temperature.

## Key numbers

- Thermalization time increases by approximately 20% for \(\gamma\tau \approx 0.5\) (where \(1/\gamma\) is the memoryless thermalization time), as shown in Fig. 1.
- For charm quarks in the nonrelativistic limit with \(\mathcal{D}=0.2\) GeV\(^2\)/fm, \(\sigma_p\) (momentum broadening) evolves as \(\sigma_p \approx \mathcal{D}t^2/\tau\) for \(t\ll\tau\), compared to \(\sigma_p \approx 2\mathcal{D}t\) for \(\tau\ll t\ll 1/\gamma\) (Eqs. 31-32).
- At \(T=1\) GeV using pQCD, \(R_{AA}\) at \(t=1\) fm/c and \(t=3\) fm/c shows slower evolution for \(\tau=1\) fm/c compared to \(\tau=0\) (Fig. 6).
- At \(T=0.25\) GeV using QPM, \(R_{AA}\) at \(t=3\) fm/c for \(\tau=1\) fm/c stays above the \(\tau=0\) case at high \(p_T\) and below at low \(p_T\), indicating slower energy loss and diffusion (Fig. 8).

## Context

This work extends prior studies of heavy quark dynamics in the quark-gluon plasma using Langevin equations (Rapp 2018, Cao 2018, Dong 2019) and kinetic theory (Uphoff 2011, Song 2015, Plumari 2017), which typically treat the thermal noise as a Markov process with no time correlations. The authors build on analytical results for memory effects in Brownian motion (Ruggieri 2019) and apply them to heavy quark observables for the first time. The findings suggest that memory could alter the \(R_{AA}\)-\(v_2\) correlation in heavy quark phenomenology, potentially requiring larger diffusion coefficients to match experimental data and motivating future quantitative studies.
