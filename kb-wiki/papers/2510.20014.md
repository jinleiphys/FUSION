# Nuclear Fragmentation at the Future Electron-Ion Collider

> We investigate aspects of low-energy nuclear reactions that could be explored at the forthcoming Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) at Brookhaven National Laboratory and compare them with analogous measurements performed in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) at CERN. The estimated fragmentation cross sections at the EIC are roughly three orders of magnitude smaller than those observed at the LHC. At the LHC, uranium nucleus fragmentation exhibits a distinctive double-peaked mass spectrum arising from fission processes, whereas at the EIC, the breakup pattern is mainly characterized by neutron evaporation and a vastly reduced yield of fission fragments, about four orders of magnitude fewer events in comparison.

# Nuclear Fragmentation at the Future Electron-Ion Collider

## Key claim
The estimated fragmentation cross sections at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) are roughly three orders of magnitude smaller than those observed in ultraperipheral collisions (UPCs) at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC), and the breakup pattern at the EIC is mainly characterized by neutron evaporation with a vastly reduced yield of fission fragments, about four orders of magnitude fewer events compared to the LHC. The analysis is limited to electromagnetic interactions at sub-50 MeV photon energies, primarily through giant dipole resonance excitation, and does not account for strong-interaction processes.

## Method
The author calculated electromagnetic dissociation cross sections for 18 GeV electrons on 110 GeV/nucleon 238U and 208Pb beams at the EIC, and compared them with 208Pb+208Pb and 238U+238U UPCs at the LHC at sqrt(s_NN) = 5.5 TeV/nucleon. Giant resonance excitation (isovector giant dipole, isoscalar and isovector giant quadrupole) was modeled using Lorentzian distributions with centroid energies from standard parameterizations, and decay branching ratios were evaluated using the Ewing-Weisskopf model with separation energies from the 2017 atomic mass evaluation and Bass potential for Coulomb barriers. Fission yields were obtained via a dynamical approach from Jurado (2003, 2005).

## Key numbers
- Fragmentation cross sections at the EIC are roughly 10^3 times smaller than those at the LHC
- For e-Pb at the EIC: sigma_{-1n} = 20.24 mb, sigma_{-2n} = 11.45 mb, sigma_{-3n} = 1.416 mb, sigma_{-4n} = 0.5933 mb, sigma_fission = 0 mb, sigma_total = 33.90 mb
- For e-U at the EIC: sigma_{-1n} = 15.58 mb, sigma_{-2n} = 14.88 mb, sigma_{-3n} = 1.591 mb, sigma_{-4n} = 0.2934 mb, sigma_fission = 8.867 mb, sigma_total = 41.32 mb
- For Pb+Pb at the LHC: sigma_{-1n} = 33.93 b, sigma_{-2n} = 18.89 b, sigma_{-3n} = 2.546 b, sigma_{-4n} = 1.091 b, sigma_fission = 0 b, sigma_total = 55.74 b
- For U+U at the LHC: sigma_{-1n} = 33.20 b, sigma_{-2n} = 30.59 b, sigma_{-3n} = 3.537 b, sigma_{-4n} = 0.784 b, sigma_fission = 18.24 b, sigma_total = 85.48 b
- Fission branching ratio for U at the EIC: 21.45%; at the LHC: 19.54%
- The dominant energy window for photon-induced nuclear fragmentation corresponds to the GDR region, typically around 10-20 MeV for heavy systems
- The LHC Lorentz factor gamma_lab = 2941 for Pb+Pb at 2.76 TeV/nucleon

## Context
This work extends the study of electromagnetic dissociation from ultraperipheral collisions at the LHC, as previously investigated by Bertulani (1988) and the Schmidt (2002) and Jurado (2003, 2005) collaborations on fission residues, to the future EIC environment. The analysis contrasts the EIC's clean electron probe, which isolates purely electromagnetic excitation without pomeron exchange contributions present in UPCs, with the higher cross sections available at the LHC. The results enable planning for nuclear structure and fission studies at the EIC, particularly for neutron-rich isotope production and symmetry energy constraints via the giant dipole resonance, while also highlighting that UPCs at the LHC remain more favorable for nuclear-structure investigations under appropriate detection conditions.
