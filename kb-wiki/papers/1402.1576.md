# Quantum Monte Carlo calculations of neutron matter with non-local chiral interactions

> We present fully non-perturbative quantum Monte Carlo calculations with non-local chiral effective field theory (EFT) interactions for the ground state properties of neutron matter. The equation of state, the nucleon chemical potentials and the momentum distribution in pure neutron matter up to one and a half times the nuclear saturation density are computed with a newly optimized chiral EFT interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order. This work opens the way to systematic order by order benchmarking of chiral EFT interactions, and \emph{ab initio} prediction of nuclear properties while respecting the symmetries of quantum chromodynamics.

## Key claim

The paper presents the first fully non-perturbative quantum Monte Carlo calculations that incorporate the full non-local chiral effective field theory interactions, rather than trading non-localities for additional spin-isospin operator dependence as done in prior work (Gezerlis 2013). The calculations use a newly optimized chiral interaction at next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO$_{\rm opt}$) and are limited to pure neutron matter up to one and a half times nuclear saturation density.

## Method

The authors use the configuration interaction Monte Carlo (CIMC) method, which performs a random walk in Fock space (occupation number basis) rather than in real space, allowing non-local interactions to be incorporated directly. The ground state wave function is obtained by repeatedly applying the imaginary-time propagator on an initial state, with a coupled cluster double (CCD) type wave function serving as the guiding wave function to control the sign problem via a fixed-phase approximation. Calculations are performed in a periodic box containing 66 neutrons (or 14 neutrons plus 1 proton for chemical potentials), with a finite single-particle basis cutoff $k_{\rm max}$ that is increased until convergence is reached.

## Key numbers

- Energy per particle in pure neutron matter computed at densities up to $\rho = 0.24$ fm$^{-3}$ (one and a half times nuclear saturation density, where saturation density $\rho_0 \approx 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$)
- Statistical error in QMC energies is $1-2\%$ of the correlation energy (measured with respect to Hartree-Fock energy)
- Renormalization factor $Z$ at $\rho = 0.08$ fm$^{-3}$: $Z = 0.9579(8)$
- Renormalization factor $Z$ at $\rho = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$: $Z = 0.9484(8)$
- Renormalization factor $Z$ at $\rho = 0.24$ fm$^{-3}$: $Z = 0.9378(8)$
- Occupation number at zero momentum $n(0)$ at $\rho = 0.08$ fm$^{-3}$: $n(0) = 0.9913(5)$
- Occupation number at zero momentum $n(0)$ at $\rho = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$: $n(0) = 0.9909(5)$
- Occupation number at zero momentum $n(0)$ at $\rho = 0.24$ fm$^{-3}$: $n(0) = 0.9906(5)$
- CCD energy estimate differs from QMC estimate by at most about $3\%$ at $\rho = 0.04$ fm$^{-3}$
- Second-order perturbation theory (PT-2) overestimates correlation energies by $24-36\%$ compared to QMC results
- Estimated fixed-phase bias is about $5-6\%$ of the correlation energy (approximately $1\%$ of total energy)

## Context

This work extends the CIMC method, previously developed for the homogeneous electron gas (Mukherjee 2013, Roggero 2013), to nuclear matter with non-local chiral interactions. It provides an alternative to the approach of Gezerlis (2013), who traded non-localities for additional spin-isospin operator dependence and treated residual non-localities perturbatively. The results enable systematic order-by-order benchmarking of chiral EFT interactions and provide unbiased QMC estimates of the momentum distribution, showing that the commonly used mixed estimator overestimates depletion at the Fermi energy by more than 50%. The method is general enough to be extended to finite nuclei and three-body forces.
