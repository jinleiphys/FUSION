# Signals of quark substructure in hadron reactions at intermediate energies

> Exotic hadrons of a few GeV/c^2 mass, exhibiting a spectrum determined by the perturbative interaction of a non-minimal number of valence quarks, have long been predicted as signals of the underlying QCD structure. Models that include the confinement region, in addition to the region of asymptotic freedom, predict exotic masses 0.2--0.8 GeV higher than the others. The R-matrix hybrid model has made the most detailed predictions, some of which have been experimentally observed. Here we will give an introduction to the model and review the status of its predictions and new applications.

# Signals of Quark Substructure in Hadron Reactions at Intermediate Energies

## Key claim
Exotic hadron resonances predicted by the R-matrix hybrid model, which incorporates both the confinement region and the asymptotic freedom region of QCD, have masses 0.2–0.8 GeV higher than those predicted by models that omit confinement, and some of these predictions have been experimentally observed. The model postulates a short-range valence quark and perturbative gluon exchange region connected to a long-range hadronic region by an R-matrix boundary condition, enabling predictions for exotic spectra while also describing background reactions.

## Method
The R-matrix hybrid model divides the two-hadron interaction into an interior region (radius ~1 fm) described by perturbative QCD with multi-quark configurations, and an exterior region described by hadron exchange. The two regions are connected by an R-matrix boundary condition at the separation radius, where the logarithmic derivative of the external wave function is matched to a meromorphic function with poles at the energies of internal quark states, weighted by fractional parentage coefficients. The model fits low-energy polarized and unpolarized differential cross sections and then predicts exotic resonance masses and widths from the pole structure of the internal quark configurations.

## Key numbers
- The lowest mass I=1 di-nucleon state, a ¹S₀, is predicted at 2.7 GeV/c² and has been observed in Ann (90°) of pp elastic scattering; the resonance position was decreased by 20 MeV from the original prediction.
- The lowest mass I=0 di-nucleon, a ³S₁ at 2.63 GeV/c², is consistent with recent Δσ_L(np) data in that energy region.
- The R-matrix calculation predicts a ΛΛ–ΞN resonance at 2.35 GeV/c² in the S=-2 di-hyperon sector.
- Several other observable di-nucleons are predicted with masses ≤ 3.0 GeV/c², including a lowest-mass odd parity ³P₁ resonant structure with a width of about 70 MeV (c.m. width 80 MeV).
- The sum of the squares of the fractional parentage coefficients for six S-state quarks is 0.2, resulting in resonance widths of order 50 MeV.
- The d* quasi-bound Δ-Δ state predicted near 2.1 GeV/c² in a cluster model (Goldman, 1989) would appear at neutron beam energy T(lab) = 425–550 MeV; the R-matrix model produces a width of 4 MeV, too large to agree with phase shift analyses that require width ≤ 1 MeV for smooth energy dependence.
- The d' (2065) dibaryon resonance at 2.065 GeV is conjectured to be in a 0⁻ state; the corresponding exotic configuration is more than 0.5 GeV too massive in the R-matrix model.

## Context
This work extends the R-matrix hybrid model developed by Lomon and collaborators (1986, 1987) and applied to multi-quark configurations by Mineo (1988) and Glozman (1993). It contrasts with models that omit adequate confinement treatment (e.g., Aerts et al. 1978, Goldman 1989, Oka and Yazaki 1980, and Jaffe 1977), which predict exotic masses 0.2–0.8 GeV lower. The paper reviews experimental corroboration of predicted di-nucleon states and applies the model to the di-hyperon sector, where it predicts a resonance at 2.35 GeV/c² rather than the bound H particle predicted by Jaffe (1977). The work enables future searches for specific exotic resonances in pp, np, ΞN, and KN scattering, and tests of the d' interpretation as a bound N-S₁₁ system.
