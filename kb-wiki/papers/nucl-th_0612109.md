# Negative parity states of $^{11}$B and $^{11}$C and the similarity with $^{12}C

> The negative parity states of $^{11}$B and $^{11}$C were studied based on the calculations of antisymmetrized molecular dynamics(AMD). The calculations well reproduced the experimental strengths of Gamov-Teller(GT), $M1$ and monopole transitions. We, especially, focused on the $3/2^-_3$ and $5/2^-_2$ states, for which GT transition strengths were recently measured. The weak $M1$ and GT transitions for the $3/2^-_3$ in $^{11}$B and $^{11}$C are described by a well-developed cluster structure of $2α$+$t$ and $2α$+$^3$He, respectively, while the strong transitions for the $5/2^-_2$ is characterized by an intrinsic spin excitation with no cluster structure. It was found that the $3/2^-_3$ state is a dilute cluster state, and its features are similar to those of the $^{12}$C$(0^+_2)$ which is considered to be a gas state of three $α$ clusters.

# Negative parity states of $^{11}$B and $^{11}$C and the similarity with $^{12}$C

## Key claim

The $3/2^-_3$ states in $^{11}$B (8.56 MeV) and $^{11}$C (8.10 MeV) are dilute cluster states with $2\alpha+t$ and $2\alpha+^3$He structure respectively, similar to the $^{12}$C($0^+_2$) three-alpha gas state, but the cluster gas-like nature in $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$) is less remarkable than in $^{12}$C($0^+_2$) due to more attractive inter-cluster potentials and the lack of identical boson symmetry.

## Method

Antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) calculations with variation after spin-parity projection (VAP) were performed for negative-parity states of $^{11}$B and $^{11}$C, using 17 base AMD wave functions superposed after diagonalization of the Hamiltonian and norm matrices. The effective nuclear interaction consisted of the MV1 central force (with Bartlett, Heisenberg and Majorana parameters $b=h=0.25$, $m=0.62$) and a spin-orbit force with strengths $u_{I}=-u_{II}=2800$ MeV, with mirror symmetry assumed for the $^{11}$C base wave functions.

## Key numbers

- $B({\rm GT};^{11}{\rm B}\rightarrow ^{11}{\rm C}(3/2^-_3)) = 0.005(2)$ experimentally, reproduced by AMD as 0.02 (extremely weak compared to other transitions)
- $B({\rm GT};^{11}{\rm B}\rightarrow ^{11}{\rm C}(5/2^-_2)) = 0.461(23)$ experimentally, reproduced by AMD as 0.56 (strong transition)
- $B(E0;IS)$ for $3/2^-_1\rightarrow 3/2^-_3$ in $^{11}$B: experimental $94\pm 16$ fm$^4$, AMD calculation 94 fm$^4$
- Matter root-mean-square radius of $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$): 3.0 fm, compared to 2.5 fm for the ground state and 3.3 fm for $^{12}$C($0^+_2$)
- $B(M1;3/2^-_3\rightarrow 3/2^-_1)$ in $^{11}$B: experimental 0.072(0.007) $\mu_N^2$, AMD 0.07 $\mu_N^2$ (weak)
- $B(M1;5/2^-_2\rightarrow 3/2^-_1)$ in $^{11}$B: experimental 0.50(0.02) $\mu_N^2$, AMD 0.45 $\mu_N^2$ (strong)
- Excitation energies: $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$) at 8.56 MeV, $^{11}$C($3/2^-_3$) at 8.10 MeV, $^{11}$B($5/2^-_2$) at 8.92 MeV, $^{11}$C($5/2^-_2$) at 8.42 MeV

## Context

This work extends the AMD studies of cluster structures in light nuclei previously applied to $^{12}$C (Kanada-En'yo, 2005) and $^{10}$Be, and directly addresses the puzzle that the $^{11}$B($3/2^-_3$) state could not be described by shell model calculations (Cohen, 1965; Navratil, 2003) nor by earlier cluster model calculations (Nishioka, 1979). The calculations were motivated by recent high-resolution GT transition measurements (Kawabata, 2004; Fujita, 2004) and inelastic $(d,d')$ scattering experiments (Kawabata, 2005) that suggested similarities with $^{12}$C($0^+_2$). The results enable systematic study of dilute cluster states in A=11 systems and their relationship to the three-alpha gas state in $^{12}$C, while also demonstrating the coexistence of cluster and non-cluster states at similar excitation energies.
