# alpha+28Si and 16O+16O molecular states, and their isoscalar monopole strengths

> The properties of the alpha+28Si and 16O+16O molecular states which are embedded in the excited states of 32S and can have an impact on the stellar reactions are investigated using the antisymmetrized molecular dynamics. From the analysis of the cluster spectroscopic factors, the candidates of alpha+28Si and 16O+16O molecular states are identified close to and above the cluster threshold energies. The calculated properties of the alpha+28Si molecular states are consistent with those reported by the alpha+28Siresonant scattering experiments. On the other hand, the 16O+16O molecular state, which is predicted to be identical to the superdeformation of 32S, is inconsistent with the assignment proposed by an alpha inelastic scattering experiment. Our calculation suggests that the monopole transition from the ground state to the 16O+16O molecular state is rather weak and is not strongly excited by the alpha inelastic scattering.

## Key claim

The isoscalar monopole transition has strong selectivity for alpha+28Si molecular states but is inactive to 16O+16O molecular states, contradicting the assignment by Itoh et al. (2013) that the 10-12 MeV states observed in alpha inelastic scattering are 16O+16O molecular states and superdeformed states.

## Method

The authors used antisymmetrized molecular dynamics (AMD) with the Gogny D1S effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, applying both a quadrupole deformation constraint (beta-constraint) and an inter-cluster distance constraint (d-constraint) to generate wave functions for alpha+28Si and 16O+16O configurations. After angular momentum projection, the wave functions were superposed using the generator coordinate method (GCM) to diagonalize the Hamiltonian. Reduced width amplitudes and cluster spectroscopic factors were calculated to identify molecular states, and isoscalar monopole transition strengths were computed from the ground state to excited 0+ states.

## Key numbers

- The alpha+28Si and 16O+16O threshold energies measured from the 32S ground state are calculated as 7.56 MeV and 16.25 MeV, respectively (experimental: 6.95 MeV and 16.54 MeV)
- The ground state has alpha spectroscopic factors of 0.09 in the alpha+28Si(0+1) channel and 0.04 in the alpha+28Si(2+1) channel
- The 0+2 state has a monopole transition matrix of 5.7 fm^2 (experimental: 4.0 fm^2 at 3.78 MeV excitation)
- The 0+3 state has a monopole transition matrix of 6.5 fm^2 (experimental: 6.3 fm^2 at 6.59 MeV excitation)
- The 0+4 state (superdeformed/16O+16O molecular state at 11.0 MeV) has a monopole transition matrix of 0.0 fm^2
- The 0+5 state (11.6 MeV) has a monopole transition matrix of 2.8 fm^2 and alpha spectroscopic factor of 0.29 in the alpha+28Si(0+1) channel
- The 0+6 state (13.1 MeV) has a monopole transition matrix of 4.8 fm^2 and alpha spectroscopic factor of 0.34 in the alpha+28Si(0+1) channel
- The squared overlap between the superdeformed state and the 16O+16O configuration is 0.92
- The lowest 16O+16O band built on the 0+4 state has a moment-of-inertia parameter of 68 keV

## Context

This work extends previous AMD studies of molecular states and superdeformation in sd-pf nuclei by Kimura (2004) and Taniguchi (2007, 2009), and follows the method developed for 12C+16O molecular states by Taniguchi (2020). The results contradict the assignment by Itoh et al. (2013), who proposed that states at 10-12 MeV observed in alpha inelastic scattering are 16O+16O molecular states and superdeformed states; instead, the calculation supports the alpha+28Si molecular band assignment by Lonnroth et al. (2010) for these states. The work enables future experimental re-evaluation of the 16O+16O molecular state and superdeformation in 32S using probes other than isoscalar monopole transitions.
