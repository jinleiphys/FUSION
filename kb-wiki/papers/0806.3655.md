# Correlating radii and electric monopole transitions of atomic nuclei

> A systematic analysis of the spherical-to-deformed shape phase transition in even-even rare-earth nuclei from $_{58}$Ce to $_{74}$W is carried out in the framework of the interacting boson model. These results are then used to calculate nuclear radii and electric monopole (E0) transitions with the same effective operator. The influence of the hexadecapole degree of freedom ($g$ boson) on the correlation between radii and E0 transitions thus established, is discussed.

# Correlating radii and electric monopole transitions of atomic nuclei

## Key claim

The correlation between nuclear charge radii and electric monopole (E0) transition strengths can be tested in the interacting boson model (IBM) under the assumption that the same effective nucleon charges appear in both the radius and E0 operators; however, the hexadecapole degree of freedom (g boson) may significantly perturb this correlation in deformed nuclei, even though it has only a weak effect on the radii themselves.

## Method

The authors performed a systematic analysis of even-even rare-earth nuclei from cerium (Z=58) to tungsten (Z=74) using the interacting boson model (IBM), first fitting Hamiltonian parameters to reproduce observed energy spectra across the spherical-to-deformed shape phase transition. With wave functions fixed from the energy fit, they computed nuclear radii and isotope shifts using a radius operator containing a constant core term, a linear boson-number term, and a deformation-dependent term proportional to the d-boson number operator; E0 transition strengths were then computed using the same deformation-dependent operator scaled by the effective charges. The influence of hexadecapole deformation was explored by extending the model to include g bosons (sdg-IBM) and comparing matrix elements of the d- and g-boson number operators across the phase transition.

## Key numbers

- The parameter eta (quadrupole deformation contribution to radius) was fixed to 0.5 fm^2 for all isotopic chains, determined from peaks in isotope shifts in the spherical-deformed transitional region.
- The parameter |alpha| (isotope shift background) was adjusted per isotopic chain, taking values 0.22, 0.24, 0.26, 0.13, 0.15, 0.15, 0.11, 0.10, and 0.11 fm^2 for even Z between 58 and 74.
- Estimated |alpha| from the average nuclear charge distribution for A~150 gives approximately 0.2 fm^2.
- Estimated eta from geometric model considerations gives a range between 0.25 and 0.75 fm^2 for typical N_b~10 and A~150.
- Effective charges used for E0 calculations: e_n = 0.5e and e_p = e.
- In the classical limit of SU(3) in sdg-IBM, the ground-state expectation values are: <n_s> = N_b/5, <n_d> = 4N_b/7, <n_g> = 8N_b/35.
- The critical point for the spherical-to-deformed transition in the sdg-IBM Hamiltonian occurs at zeta_c approximately 0.5.
- In the sdg-IBM, the g-boson matrix element <0^+_1|n_g|0^+_2>^2 dominates over the d-boson matrix element for zeta >= 0.7.

## Context

This work extends the systematic IBM analysis of shape phase transitions in rare-earth nuclei previously carried out by Garcia-Ramos et al. (2003), who achieved root-mean-square deviations of approximately 100 keV for entire isotopic chains. The correlation between radii and E0 transitions builds on the geometric picture of E0 strength advocated by Reiner (1961) and on the observation by von Brentano et al. (2004) that the sd-IBM predicts sizable E0 strength in all deformed nuclei. The study enables empirical determination of effective nucleon charges from combined radius and E0 data, and identifies hexadecapole deformation as a possible explanation for the order-of-magnitude discrepancies observed in tungsten isotopes, where the measured E0 strength is consistently smaller than the sd-IBM calculation.
