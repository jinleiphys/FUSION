# Electron-positron pairs from thermal resonances in ultrarelativistic nuclear collisions

> We use a boost-invariant one-dimensional (cylindrically symmetric) fluid dynamics code to calculate $e^+e^-$ production from $ρ^0$ and $ω$ decay in the central rapidity region of a central S+Au collision at $\sqrt{s}=20$ GeV/nucleon. We use equations of state with a first-order phase transition between a massless pion gas and quark gluon plasma, with transition temperatures in the range $150-200$ MeV. The production cross section at the $ρ$ mass loosely constrains the transition and freeze-out temperatures, and we find that the $m_T$ spectrum is a good thermometer for sufficiently high $T_c$.

## Key claim

The transverse mass spectrum of electron-positron pairs from rho and omega decays retains a correlation with the transition temperature even when transverse expansion is included, but the spectrum is dominated by the freeze-out contribution at sufficiently high transverse mass, making the interpretation more complicated than in models without transverse expansion.

## Method

The authors used a boost-invariant one-dimensional (cylindrically symmetric) fluid dynamics code to model central S+Au collisions at sqrt(s)=20 GeV/nucleon, with equations of state featuring a first-order phase transition between a massless pion gas and quark-gluon plasma. They calculated the e+e- production rate from rho0 and omega decays in the central rapidity region, separating contributions from the interacting hot matter and from mesons that cross a dynamically determined freeze-out surface. The calculated spectra were then fit to extract an apparent temperature T_fit in the transverse mass window 1.155 to 1.755 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Transition temperatures studied: 150 to 200 MeV
- Standard freeze-out temperature: 120 MeV
- Standard equilibration time: 0.2 fm/c, giving initial temperature T_0 = 327 MeV
- Partial widths used: Gamma(rho0 -> e+e-) = 6.77 keV, Gamma(omega -> e+e-) = 0.60 keV
- Average rho/omega mass used: 0.775 GeV
- Total widths: Gamma(rho) = 151 MeV, Gamma(omega) = 8.43 MeV
- Calculated production at the rho-omega peak for standard parameters with T_c=150 MeV: d^2N/dm d_eta = 5.9 x 10^-4 GeV^-1
- Calculated production with T_c=200 MeV: d^2N/dm d_eta = 1.2 x 10^-3 GeV^-1
- Preliminary NA45 data value in the bin 0.7 < m < 0.8 GeV: d^2N/dm d_eta = 1.7 x 10^-3 GeV^-1
- T_fit is 10-20 MeV lower for tau_0 = 1 fm (T_0 = 192 MeV) compared to the standard tau_0 = 0.2 fm/c
- For x=0.7 (reduced quark density), T_fit is about 15 MeV higher than for x=1

## Context

This work extends earlier calculations by Seibert (1992) that predicted sensitivity of the e+e- transverse mass spectrum to the transition temperature but neglected transverse expansion. The authors had previously shown that the photon transverse momentum spectrum lost its sensitivity to T_c when transverse expansion was included (Neumann, Seibert, and Fai, 1995). The present paper demonstrates that the correlation between T_c and the dilepton spectrum persists with transverse expansion, though the freeze-out contribution dominates at high transverse mass. The results are compared to preliminary NA45 data from the SPS, and the authors note that in-medium modifications of vector meson properties (chiral restoration effects) are left for future work.
