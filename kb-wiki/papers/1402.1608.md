# A comparison of Monte Carlo generators

> A comparison of GENIE, NEUT, NUANCE, and NuWro Monte Carlo neutrino event generators is presented using a set of four observables: protons multiplicity, total visible energy, most energetic proton momentum, and $π^+$ two-dimensional energy vs cosine distribution.

## Key claim

The predictions of four Monte Carlo neutrino event generators (GENIE, NEUT, NUANCE, and NuWro) for four observables are not consistent, despite sharing the same underlying physics and similar construction, indicating that closer investigation of the final state interaction models is needed.

## Method

The author compared GENIE, NEUT, NUANCE, and NuWro by simulating neutrino-argon charged-current interactions at incident energies of 1 GeV and 3 GeV for proton multiplicity and total visible energy, and at 1 GeV for most energetic proton momentum; additionally, neutrino-carbon charged-current interactions at 5 GeV were simulated for the π⁺ two-dimensional energy vs cosine distribution. The comparisons used the intra-nuclear cascade model for final state interactions, with NuWro employing a formation zone effect that reduces re-interaction probability.

## Key numbers

- Proton multiplicity in neutrino-argon CC interactions was compared at incident neutrino energies of Eν = 1 GeV and Eν = 3 GeV, with and without a cut on proton kinetic energy Tk > 50 MeV.
- Total visible energy (sum of kinetic energies of protons and total energies of mesons and charged leptons) was compared for neutrino-argon CC interactions at Eν = 3 GeV.
- Most energetic proton momentum in neutrino-argon CC interactions at Eν = 1 GeV was compared for events with one, two, three, or more protons in the final state, normalized to 100 events, with a cut Tk > 50 MeV.
- π⁺ two-dimensional energy vs cosine of scattering angle distribution was compared for neutrino-carbon CC interactions at Eν = 5 GeV, for events with a single π⁺ and no other pions.

## Context

This work extends prior comparisons of Monte Carlo neutrino event generators (Antonello 2009) by focusing on four specific observables sensitive to final state interactions, particularly nucleon cascade models which have been less studied than pion cascade models. The results enable future confrontation with experimental data from the ArgoNeuT experiment (Partyka 2012) and motivate systematic studies starting from the primary interaction vertex to separate uncertainties from final state interactions.
