# Dynamical nature of the nuclear pseudospin and its isospin asymmetry

> Pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is investigated by solving the Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector radial potentials. We relate the pseudospin interaction with a pseudospin-orbit term in a Schroedinger-like equation for the lower component of the Dirac spinor. We show that this term gives a large contribution to the energy splittings of pseudospin partners, so that the near pseudospin degeneracy arises from a significant cancellation among the different terms in that equation. This is a manifestation of the dynamical character of this symmetry in the nucleus. We analyze the isospin dependence of the pseudospin symmetry in a nuclear isotope chain by including a vector-isovector potential V_rho and a Coulomb potential and conclude that V_rho gives the main contribution to the observed pseudospin isospin asymmetry.

# Dynamical nature of the nuclear pseudospin and its isospin asymmetry

## Key claim

Pseudospin symmetry in nuclei is not an exact symmetry arising from a cancellation between scalar and vector potentials, but rather a dynamical effect: the near degeneracy of pseudospin partners results from a significant cancellation among several terms in a Schroedinger-like equation for the lower Dirac component, particularly between the pseudospin-orbit term and the kinetic and mean-field terms. The isospin asymmetry of pseudospin splittings is primarily driven by the vector-isovector rho meson potential, not the Coulomb potential.

## Method

The authors solved the Dirac equation with Woods-Saxon scalar and vector radial potentials, fitting the potential parameters to the neutron spectra of 208Pb. They then rewrote the Dirac equation as a Schroedinger-like equation for the lower component of the Dirac spinor and computed expectation values of the kinetic term, pseudospin-orbit term, Darwin-like term, and mean-field term for each member of pseudospin doublets. To study isospin asymmetry, they added a vector-isovector rho potential and a Coulomb potential and examined pseudospin splittings along a tin isotopic chain from A=130 to A=170.

## Key numbers

- The Woods-Saxon potential parameters fitted to 208Pb neutron spectra: radius R = 7 fm, diffuseness a = 0.6 fm, Delta_0 = 650 MeV, Sigma_0 = -66 MeV
- The magnitude of the rho potential is around 4-8 MeV
- The tin isotopic chain studied spans from A=130 to A=170

## Context

This work extends the relativistic understanding of pseudospin symmetry originally uncovered by Ginocchio (1997), who showed it would be an exact SU(2) symmetry when the sum of scalar and vector potentials Sigma = 0. The authors demonstrate that this condition cannot hold in real nuclei (since bound states would not exist) and instead show the symmetry is dynamical, dependent on the shape of the Sigma potential. This explains the isospin asymmetry observed by Meng (1998) in proton versus neutron pseudospin splittings, and the analysis of the pseudospin-orbit potential as non-perturbative follows the approach of Marcos et al. (2001). The findings enable future studies of pseudospin symmetry across isotopic chains and in exotic neutron-rich nuclei.
