# Inverse magnetic catalysis in holographic models of QCD

> We study the effect of magnetic field $B$ on the critical temperature $T_{c}$ of the confinement-deconfinement phase transition in hard-wall AdS/QCD, and holographic duals of flavored and unflavored $\mathcal{N}=4$ super-Yang Mills theories on $\mathbb{R}^3\times \rm S^1$. For all of the holographic models, we find that $T_{c}(B)$ decreases with increasing magnetic field $B\ll T^2$, consistent with the inverse magnetic catalysis recently observed in lattice QCD for $B\lesssim 1~GeV^2$. We also predict that, for large magnetic field $B\gg T^2$, the critical temperature $T_{c}(B)$, eventually, starts to increase with increasing magnetic field $B\gg T^2$ and asymptotes to a constant value.

## Key claim

The paper directly computes the critical temperature \(T_{c}(B)\) of the confinement-deconfinement phase transition in several holographic models and finds that \(T_{c}(B)\) decreases with increasing magnetic field \(B\) for \(B \ll T^{2}\), consistent with the inverse magnetic catalysis observed in lattice QCD for \(B \lesssim 1~\text{GeV}^{2}\); it also predicts that for very large fields \(B \gg T^{2}\), \(T_{c}(B)\) eventually increases and asymptotes to a constant value.

## Method

The authors use the 5-dimensional Einstein-Maxwell action with a negative cosmological constant to model the dual gravity theory. For the hard-wall AdS/QCD model, the critical temperature is found by equating the on-shell Euclidean actions of a black hole (deconfined phase) and a thermal-\(AdS_{5}\) solution (confined phase) with a hard-wall IR cut-off. For unflavored and flavored \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super-Yang Mills theories on \(\mathbb{R}^{3}\times S^{1}\), the confined phase is represented by an \(AdS_{5}\)-soliton geometry, and the critical temperature is determined by comparing its free energy with that of a black hole solution, using perturbative black hole metrics in the presence of a constant magnetic field \(B \ll T^{2}\) from D'Hoker (2009) and a backreacted D3/D7 model from Ammon (2012).

## Key numbers

*   For the hard-wall AdS/QCD model, the IR cut-off is set to \(r_{0} = m_{\rho} / 2.405 = 0.323~\text{GeV}\).
*   For unflavored \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM on \(\mathbb{R}^{3}\times S^{1}\), the critical temperature at zero field is \(T_{c}^{0} = r_{0} / \pi\), with \(r_{0} = \pi \times 0.175~\text{GeV} = 0.55~\text{GeV}\).
*   The magnetic field range studied is \(0-0.35~\text{GeV}^{2}\) for the hard-wall AdS/QCD model and \(0-4.2~\text{GeV}^{2}\) for the flavored \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM model.
*   For unflavored \(\mathcal{N}=4\) SYM, the critical temperature as a function of field is given by \(T_{c} = T_{c}^{0} \left(1 - (B / B_{c})^{2}\right) + \mathcal{O}(B^{4})\), where the critical magnetic field is defined as \(B_{c} = \sqrt{6} \pi^{2} (T_{c}^{0})^{2} / (1 - 2 \ln (L T_{c}^{0} \pi))\).

## Context

This work extends holographic studies of the QCD phase diagram under magnetic fields, which had previously focused on chiral symmetry breaking and predicted magnetic catalysis (increasing \(T_{c}\) with \(B\)) as in Filev (2007) and Albash (2007). It directly computes the confinement-deconfinement transition temperature, motivated by the lattice QCD results of Bali (2011) which showed inverse magnetic catalysis for \(B \lesssim 1~\text{GeV}^{2}\). The results enable further comparison between holographic models and lattice QCD, and provide a prediction for the behavior of \(T_{c}(B)\) at large magnetic fields beyond current lattice reach.
