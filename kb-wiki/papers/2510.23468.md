# Model-independent mass determination of near-threshold states from short-range production

> We propose a novel observable for the precision measurements of a wide class of near-threshold dimer states: the short-range production rate of a dimer--spectator two-body system, composed of the given near-threshold state and one of its constituents. Within the framework of nonrelativistic effective field theory, these production rates exhibit characteristic line shapes for the specific partial wave and reach a model-independent minimum. This feature enables a precise extraction of their masses from experimental data, provided that the line shape can be resolved with sufficient accuracy. Applying this novel method to both the $T_{b\bar{b}1}(10610)B$ and $T_{b\bar{b}1}(10650)B^*$ systems allows for a precise determination of the binding energy $δ$ of the $T_{b\bar{b}1}(10610)$ and $T_{b\bar{b}1}(10650)$ via the relation of $δ=-{E_{\text{dip}}^{\text{exp}}}/{0.1983}$ once the respective dip position $E_{\text{dip}}^{\text{exp}}$ is experimentally identified.

## Key claim

The short-range production rate of a dimer–spectator two-body system exhibits a universal, model-independent dip whose position is linearly related to the binding energy of the near-threshold dimer state, provided that the effective range of the two-body subsystem is small (|γρ| ≲ 0.1) and no shallow three-body bound states exist.

## Method

The authors formulate a nonrelativistic effective field theory (NREFT) for a dimer–spectator system, where the dimer is a near-threshold bound state of two constituent particles and the spectator is one of those constituents. They derive an integral equation for the partial-wave short-range production amplitude ΓL and compute the point production rate R(x) as a function of the scaled energy x = E/|δ| + 1. By numerically solving the integral equation for S-wave production in the ZbB, Zb′B*, and XD systems, they identify a characteristic peak–dip structure and extract the dip position xdip as a function of the mass ratio r and the quantum-number factor CSI.

## Key numbers

- For the ZbB system with (S,I) = (1,3/2) and r = 0.99148(4), the dip position is xdip = 0.8022, giving the binding energy relation δZb = −Edip^exp / 0.1983.
- For the XD system with (S,I) = (1,1/2) and rX = 0.92924, the binding energy relation is δX = −Edip^exp / 0.2126.
- The characteristic peak–dip structure appears only within the quantum-number factor window 4/12 < CSI < 7/12 for r = 1, shifting to 5/12 < CSI < 5/8 for r = 0.5.
- The linear fit for the zero-range case (ργ = 0) gives xdip = 0.2308 r + 0.5729.
- The method is applicable only for |γρ| ≲ 0.3, and the dip can be resolved experimentally only with an energy resolution better than approximately 0.4 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the universal few-body dynamics of near-threshold states studied in Hammer (2021) and Braaten (2021, 2023) to a new observable: the short-range production rate of a dimer–spectator system. It differs fundamentally from the method proposed by Guo (2019) for the X(3872) mass via X(3872)γ line shapes. The approach enables model-independent mass extraction for exotic hadrons such as the Zb(10610), Zb′(10650), and X(3872), and is promising for future facilities like PANDA at FAIR and STCF, which are expected to achieve the required energy resolution of about 0.3 MeV.
