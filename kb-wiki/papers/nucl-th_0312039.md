# Dilepton emission rates from hot hadronic matter

> The vacuum polarisation effects from the nucleon sector lead to large medium modifications of the vector meson masses in the Walecka model. With a quantum hadrodynamic framework including the quantum effects, and using a quasiparticle description for quark gluon plasma(QGP), the dilepton emission rate from the hot and dense matter resulting from relativistic nuclear collisions is calculated. Using a model for the fireball evolution which has been shown to reproduce other observables such as charmonium suppression, photon emission and the abundance of hadronic species, we compare with the dilepton invariant mass spectrum measured by the CERES collaboration at CERN SPS. We also compare the results to a previous calculation where the spectral function of a virtual photon (a key ingredient for the emission rate) has been calculated in a chiral SU(3) model.

## Key claim

The paper calculates dilepton emission rates from hot and dense matter formed in relativistic nuclear collisions using a quantum hadrodynamic framework that includes vacuum polarization effects from the nucleon sector, and compares these rates to CERES experimental data, finding that the model can reproduce the measured dilepton invariant mass spectrum when including medium modifications of vector meson masses and widths.

## Method

The authors compute the in-medium properties of vector mesons (ρ and ω) in hot nuclear matter using a nonperturbative variational framework based on the Walecka model with quantum fluctuation effects from baryon and scalar meson sectors, employing thermofield dynamics at finite temperature and density. The hadronic phase is described by a Breit-Wigner parametrization of the photon spectral function with medium-modified masses and energy-dependent decay widths, while the quark-gluon plasma phase is treated using a quasiparticle picture. The space-time evolution of the fireball is modeled assuming local thermal equilibrium and isentropic expansion, with parameters fitted to reproduce experimental data on transverse momentum spectra, HBT correlations, and hadron multiplicities.

- Pion chemical potential μ_π ≈ 120 MeV from resonance decays

## Context

This work extends previous calculations of dilepton emission from hot hadronic matter by including vacuum polarization effects from the nucleon sector in the Walecka model, which lead to significant medium modifications of vector meson masses. The results are compared to calculations using a chiral SU(3) model for the photon spectral function and to experimental data from the CERES collaboration at CERN SPS. The fireball evolution model has been previously shown to reproduce other observables including charmonium suppression (Renk and Mishra, Charm), photon emission (Renk and Mishra, Photons), and hadronic species abundances (Renk and Mishra, Hadrochemistry), and the present work enables further testing of the equilibration time assumption τ₀ through dilepton observables.
