# Electromagnetic response of quark-gluon plasma in heavy-ion collisions

> We study the electromagnetic response of the quark-gluon plasma in AA-collisions at RHIC and LHC energies for a realistic space-time evolution of the plasma fireball. We demonstrate that for a realistic electric conductivity the electromagnetic response of the plasma is in a quantum regime when the induced electric current does not generate a classical electromagnetic field, and can only lead to a rare emission of single photons.

## Key claim

For a realistic electric conductivity of the quark-gluon plasma and a realistic space-time evolution of the fireball, the induced electromagnetic field is too weak to be described classically; the electromagnetic response consists only of rare single-photon emission, not a classical field.

## Method

The author solved the Maxwell equations in Milne coordinates using the Yee algorithm, with the induced current given by Ohm's law in the fireball. The fireball evolution was modeled with Bjorken 1+1D longitudinal expansion and a Gaussian initial entropy density profile tuned to reproduce experimental charged-particle pseudorapidity densities from STAR and ALICE. The electric conductivity was taken from recent lattice QCD calculations (Amato et al.) and parametrized as a function of temperature.

## Key numbers

- For Au+Au at RHIC ($\sqrt{s}=200$ GeV, $b=6$ fm), the induced magnetic field at $t=1$ fm and $\rb=0$ is $eB_y/m_\pi^2 \approx 0.0013$.
- For Pb+Pb at LHC ($\sqrt{s}=2.76$ TeV, $b=6$ fm), the induced magnetic field at $t=1$ fm and $\rb=0$ is smaller than the RHIC value by a factor of roughly 3-4 (from Fig. 2).
- The initial external magnetic field at $t=0$ and $\rb=0$ is $eB_y/m_\pi^2 \approx 2.6$ for Au+Au at RHIC (Woods-Saxon density).
- The photon attenuation length in the fireball is $l_a \approx 2/\sigma \sim 100$ fm for $T\sim 250$ MeV.
- The conductivity from lattice QCD (Amato et al.) gives $\sigma/C_{em}T$ rising from $\sim 0.07$ at $T=150$ MeV to $\sim 0.32$ at $T=350$ MeV.
- The temperature at the fireball center at $\tau=0.5$ fm is $T\approx 400$ MeV for RHIC and $T\approx 520$ MeV for LHC.

## Context

This work extends the study of magnetic field evolution in heavy-ion collisions beyond the uniform-static-matter approximations used by Tuchin (2013) and Kharzeev et al. (2013), which predicted strong enhancement of the magnetic field lifetime. It contradicts those results by showing that for a realistic expanding fireball the induced field is orders of magnitude smaller. The results also disagree with the model of McLerran and Skokov (2014), which overestimated the external field by a factor of 2.7 at $t=0$. The finding that the electromagnetic response is in a quantum regime implies that it cannot contribute to magnetohydrodynamics effects or magnetic-field-induced photon production as previously assumed.
