# Qudit circuits with SU(d) symmetry: Locality imposes additional conservation laws

> Local symmetric quantum circuits provide a simple framework to study the dynamics and phases of complex quantum systems with conserved charges. However, some of their basic properties have not yet been understood. Recently, it has been shown that such quantum circuits only generate a restricted subset of symmetric unitary transformations [I. Marvian, Nature Physics, 2022]. In this paper, we consider circuits with 2-local SU(d)-invariant unitaries acting on qudits, i.e., d-dimensional quantum systems. Our results reveal a significant distinction between the cases of d = 2 and d>2. For qubits with SU(2) symmetry, arbitrary global rotationally-invariant unitaries can be generated with 2-local ones, up to relative phases between the subspaces corresponding to inequivalent irreducible representations (irreps) of the symmetry, i.e., sectors with different angular momenta. On the other hand, for d>2, in addition to similar constraints on the relative phases between the irreps, locality also restricts the generated unitaries inside these conserved subspaces. These constraints impose conservation laws that hold for dynamics under 2-local SU(d)-invariant unitaries, but are violated under general SU(d)-invariant unitaries. Based on this result, we show that the distribution of unitaries generated by random 2-local SU(d)-invariant unitaries does not converge to the Haar measure over the group of all SU(d)-invariant unitaries, and in fact, for d>2, is not even a 2-design for the Haar distribution.

## Key claim

For qudits with SU(d) symmetry where d is greater than 2, 2-local symmetric unitaries impose additional conservation laws inside individual irreducible representation sectors that are not present for general SU(d)-invariant unitaries, whereas for qubits with SU(2) symmetry, 2-local unitaries can generate arbitrary rotationally-invariant unitaries up to relative phases between sectors with different angular momenta.

## Method

The authors study the group V_2 generated by composing 2-local SU(d)-invariant unitaries, which up to a global phase take the form e^{i theta P_{ab}} where P_{ab} is the swap operator between qudits a and b. They analyze the Lie algebra generated by these swap operators and decompose the total Hilbert space into irreducible representations of SU(d) using Schur-Weyl duality. They identify two classes of additional constraints: a Z_2 symmetry related to the parity of permutations that applies for systems with n less than or equal to d^2 qudits, and constraints derived from a mapping between the qudit dynamics and free fermionic systems that apply for arbitrarily many qudits.

## Key numbers

* For d=2 (qubits with SU(2) symmetry), arbitrary global rotationally-invariant unitaries can be generated with 2-local unitaries up to relative phases between sectors with different angular momenta.
* For d greater than or equal to 3, 2-local SU(d)-invariant unitaries do not form a 2-design for the Haar distribution over all SU(d)-invariant unitaries.
* The Z_2 symmetry constraint applies for systems with n less than or equal to d^2 qudits for d greater than or equal to 3.
* The Hamiltonian for 2-local SU(d)-invariant interactions can be written as H(t) = sum_{i less than j} h_{ij}(t) P_{ij}, where P_{ij} is the swap operator.

## Context

This work extends the recent result of Marvian (2022) showing that local symmetric quantum circuits generate only a restricted subset of symmetric unitary transformations. It contrasts with the well-known universality of 2-local unitary transformations in the absence of symmetries established by Lloyd (1995), DiVincenzo (1995), and Deutsch (1995). The results connect to mathematical work by Marin (2007) on the Lie algebra generated by transpositions in the group algebra of the symmetric group. The findings have implications for quantum chaos, quantum complexity in systems with conserved charges, and the classification of symmetry-protected topological phases.
