# Anisotropic hydrodynamics for conformal Gubser flow

> In this proceedings contribution, we review the exact solution of the anisotropic hydrodynamics equations for a system subject to Gubser flow. For this purpose, we use the leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics equations which assume that the distribution function is ellipsoidally symmetric in local-rest-frame momentum. We then prove that the SO(3)_q symmetry in de Sitter space constrains the anisotropy tensor to be of spheroidal form with only one independent anisotropy parameter remaining. As a consequence, the exact solution reduces to the problem of solving two coupled non-linear differential equations. We show that, in the limit that the relaxation time goes to zero, one obtains Gubser's ideal hydrodynamic solution and, in the limit that the relaxation time goes to infinity, one obtains the exact free streaming solution obtained originally by Denicol et al. For finite relaxation time, we solve the equations numerically and compare to the exact solution of the relaxation-time-approximation Boltzmann equation subject to Gubser flow. Using this as our standard, we find that anisotropic hydrodynamics describes the spatio-temporal evolution of the system better than all currently known dissipative hydrodynamics approaches.

## Key claim

Anisotropic hydrodynamics provides a better description of the spatio-temporal evolution of a system subject to Gubser flow than all currently known dissipative hydrodynamics approaches, when compared to the exact solution of the relaxation-time-approximation Boltzmann equation. This conclusion holds for finite relaxation time, with the exact solution reducing to Gubser's ideal hydrodynamic solution in the zero relaxation time limit and to the exact free streaming solution of Denicol et al. in the infinite relaxation time limit.

## Method

The authors used the leading-order anisotropic hydrodynamics equations, which assume the distribution function is ellipsoidally symmetric in the local-rest-frame momentum. They proved that the SO(3)_q symmetry in de Sitter space constrains the anisotropy tensor to spheroidal form with only one independent anisotropy parameter, reducing the exact solution to two coupled non-linear ordinary differential equations derived from the first and second moments of the Boltzmann equation in relaxation-time approximation. For finite relaxation time, these equations were solved numerically and compared to the exact solution of the relaxation-time-approximation Boltzmann equation subject to Gubser flow.

## Key numbers

- For three different shear viscosity to entropy density ratios, the comparison used \(4 \pi \eta/s \in \{1,3,10\}\).
- The initial effective temperature in de Sitter space was fixed to \(\hat{T}_0 = 0.002\) at \(\rho = \rho_0 = -10\).
- The initial anisotropy parameter was set to \(\hat{\alpha}_{\varsigma,0}=1\), corresponding to an isotropic initial condition in de Sitter space.
- The relaxation time in de Sitter space is given by \(\hat{\tau}_{\rm eq} = 5\hat{\bar{\eta}}/\hat{T}\), where \(\hat{\bar{\eta}} = \eta/s\).

## Context

This work extends the exact solutions of anisotropic hydrodynamics obtained for (0+1)-dimensional boost-invariant flow by Florkowski et al. (2013) and Bazow et al. (2013) to the case of Gubser flow, which includes both longitudinal and transverse expansion. It provides a test case for code validation in future anisotropic hydrodynamics codes, as demonstrated by Nopoush et al. (2015) who showed that the (1+1)-dimensional ellipsoidal anisotropic hydrodynamics framework of Tinti and Florkowski (2013) could numerically reproduce the exact solution without transforming to de Sitter space. The results enable comparison of different dissipative hydrodynamics approaches against an exact kinetic theory benchmark.
