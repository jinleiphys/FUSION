# Electromagnetic probes

> We introduce the seminal developments in the theory and experiments of electromagnetic probes for the study of the dynamics of relativistic heavy ion collisions and quark gluon plasma.

## Key claim

Electromagnetic probes (photons and dileptons) are the most promising and efficient tool to characterize the initial state of relativistic heavy ion collisions because they interact only weakly with the medium and carry undistorted information about their production circumstances, though in practice the different sources (prompt, pre-equilibrium, thermal, and jet-conversion) cannot be distinguished experimentally and must be separated theoretically.

## Method

The authors review the theoretical and experimental framework for electromagnetic probes in relativistic heavy ion collisions, covering: (1) the invariant mass analysis and mixed-event techniques used to subtract decay-photon backgrounds from inclusive photon spectra; (2) perturbative QCD calculations at next-to-leading order for prompt photon production from partonic processes (Compton scattering, annihilation, and fragmentation); (3) thermal photon emission rates from quark-gluon plasma derived from relativistic kinetic theory with Braaten-Pisarski resummation to handle infrared divergences; and (4) the jet-photon conversion mechanism where high-energy quark jets annihilate with thermal antiquarks or scatter with thermal gluons in the plasma.

## Key numbers

- Critical temperature for deconfinement: \(T_c \sim 180\) MeV
- Typical system size (mean free path of photons): \(\sim 10\) fm
- Equilibration time for photons at \(T = 200\) MeV: \(\tau_{\rm eq} \approx 270\) fm/\(c\) for \(E = 0.5\) GeV, 356 fm/\(c\) for \(E = 1\) GeV, 505 fm/\(c\) for \(E = 2\) GeV, and 639 fm/\(c\) for \(E = 3\) GeV
- More than 90% of photons in the inclusive spectrum come from hadron decay
- The factor \(5/9\) appears in the thermal photon rate from QGP, arising from the sum of squares of electric charges of \(u\) and \(d\) quarks
- The constant \(C_F = 0.0460\) and \(C_B = -2.1472\) appear in the infrared-regulated Compton and annihilation rates respectively
- For jet-photon conversion, the constant \(C = -1.916\) appears in the rate expression

## Context

This review extends the foundational work on electromagnetic probes from relativistic heavy ion collisions, building on the theoretical framework of Kapusta, Lichard, and Seibert (KLS) for thermal photon production and the Braaten-Pisarski resummation method for handling infrared divergences in finite-temperature QCD. The paper discusses experimental techniques developed by the WA98 and PHENIX Collaborations for decay-photon subtraction, and presents NLO pQCD results for prompt photons at RHIC, SPS, and LHC energies including isospin, shadowing, and energy-loss corrections. The work enables future studies at the LHC and provides the theoretical basis for using direct photons to probe the early-time dynamics of the quark-gluon plasma, including jet quenching and momentum anisotropy.
