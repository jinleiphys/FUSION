# Baryon flow from SIS to AGS energies

> We analyze the baryon sideward and elliptic flow from SIS (0.25 $\sim$ 2 A GeV) to AGS (2 $\sim 11.0A$GeV) energies for Au + Au collisions in the relativistic transport model RBUU that includes all baryon resonances up to a mass of 2 GeV as well as string degrees of freedom for the higher mass continuum. There are two factors which dominantly determine the baryon flow at these energies: the momentum dependence of the scalar and vector potentials and the resonance-string degrees of freedom. We fix the explicit momentum dependence of the nucleon-meson couplings within the NL3 parameter set by the nucleon optical potential up to 1 GeV of kinetic energy. When assuming the optical potential to vanish identically for $E_{kin} \geq 3.5$ GeV we simultaneously reproduce the sideward flow data of the FOPI, EOS, E895 and E877 collaborations, the elliptic flow data of the EOS, E895 and E877 collaborations, and approximately the rapidity and transverse mass distribution of protons at AGS energies. The gradual change from hadronic to string degrees of freedom with increasing bombarding energy can be viewed as a transition from {\it hadronic} to {\it string} matter, i.e. a dissolution of hadrons.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the simultaneous reproduction of sideward and elliptic flow data from SIS to AGS energies requires both a momentum-dependent reduction of the vector potential at high relative momenta (with the optical potential set to zero above 3.5 GeV kinetic energy) and a transition from resonance to string degrees of freedom at invariant collision energies around 3.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed relativistic Boltzmann-Uehling-Uhlenbeck (RBUU) transport calculations for Au + Au collisions from 0.25 to 11.0 A GeV, incorporating all baryon resonances up to 2 GeV mass and Lund string degrees of freedom for higher excitations. The momentum dependence of the scalar and vector self-energies was fixed using the NL3 parameter set with form factors fitted to the nucleon optical potential up to 1 GeV, and the optical potential was set to vanish for kinetic energies above 3.5 GeV. The transition energy between resonance and string excitation was set to \(\sqrt{s_{sw}} \approx 3.5\) GeV by fitting proton transverse mass spectra at AGS energies.

## Key numbers

* Sideward flow maximum at approximately 2 A GeV for Au + Au collisions at impact parameter \(b = 6\) fm.
* Transition energy between resonance and string degrees of freedom: \(\sqrt{s_{sw}} \approx 3.5 \pm 0.3\) GeV, corresponding to a proton laboratory energy of about 3.6 – 5.8 GeV.
* String lifetime in its rest frame: \(t_F \approx 0.8\) fm/c.
* At 11 A GeV, the initial high-density string matter reaches up to 10 \(\rho_0\) (normal nuclear density).
* Cut-off parameters for momentum-dependent form factors: \(\Lambda_s = 1.0\) GeV and \(\Lambda_v = 0.9\) GeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier RBUU studies by Sahu et al. (1998) on directed flow at SIS energies to the full SIS-to-AGS range, and addresses the failure of simple relativistic mean-field models (which produce a linearly growing optical potential) to describe flow at higher energies. The results are compared with experimental data from the FOPI, EOS, E895, and E877 collaborations, and with hydrodynamical calculations by Rischke that predicted a minimum in sideward flow from a first-order phase transition. The study enables refined constraints on the momentum dependence of nuclear mean fields at high momenta and densities, and indicates a smooth transition from hadronic to string matter starting around 3 A GeV.
