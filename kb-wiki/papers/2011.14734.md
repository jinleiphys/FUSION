# Present Status of Nuclear Shell-Model Calculations of Neutrinoless Double-Beta Decay Matrix Elements

> Neutrinoless double beta decay searches are currently among the major foci of experimental physics. The observation of such a decay will have important implications in our understanding of the intrinsic nature of neutrinos and shed light on the limitations of the Standard Model. The rate of this process depends on both the unknown neutrino effective mass and the nuclear matrix element associated with the given neutrinoless double-beta decay transition. The latter can only be provided by theoretical calculations, hence the need of accurate theoretical predictions of the nuclear matrix element for the success of the experimental programs. This need drives the theoretical nuclear physics community to provide the most reliable calculations of the nuclear matrix elements. Among the various computational models adopted to solve the many-body nuclear problem, the shell model is widely considered as the basic framework of the microscopic description of the nucleus. Here, we review the most recent and advanced shell-model calculations of the nuclear matrix elements considering the light-neutrino-exchange channel for nuclei of experimental interest. We report the sensitivity of the theoretical calculations with respect to variations in the model spaces and the shell-model nuclear Hamiltonians.

## Key claim
The paper reviews the most recent and advanced shell-model calculations of nuclear matrix elements for neutrinoless double-beta decay (0νββ) via the light-neutrino-exchange channel, but emphasizes that the results are sensitive to variations in the model spaces and the shell-model nuclear Hamiltonians used, meaning no single definitive value is provided.

## Method
The authors survey shell-model calculations of 0νββ nuclear matrix elements for nuclei of experimental interest, focusing on the light-neutrino-exchange mechanism. They examine how the computed matrix elements change when the model space (the set of single-particle orbitals included) and the effective nuclear Hamiltonian are varied. The review covers multiple candidate isotopes and compares results from different shell-model implementations.

## Key numbers
- Review-type paper: no original numerics.

## Context
This review extends the ongoing effort to provide reliable nuclear matrix elements for 0νββ searches, a field where experimental programs (such as those for 76Ge, 136Xe, and 130Te) depend on theoretical input. It builds upon earlier shell-model calculations by various groups (e.g., Caurier et al., Menendez et al., and Horoi et al.) and contrasts with other many-body approaches like the quasiparticle random-phase approximation and the interacting boson model. The paper enables experimental collaborations to better understand the theoretical uncertainties in matrix elements, which directly affect the extraction of the neutrino effective mass from decay-rate limits.
