# A Quantum Computational Determination of the Weak Mixing Angle in the Standard Model

> The weak mixing angle $s_W$ is a fundamental constant in the Standard Model (SM) and measured at the $Z$ boson mass to be $\widehat{s}^2_W(m_Z) = 0.23129 \pm 0.00004$ in the $\overline{\rm MS}$ renormalization scheme, where $m_Z=91.2\ \text{GeV}$. On the other hand, non-stabilizerness - the magic - characterizes the computational advantage of a quantum system over classical computers. We consider the production of magic from stabilizer initial states, which carry zero magic, in the 2-to-2 scattering of charged leptons in the SM at the tree level, which is mediated by the photon and the $Z$ boson. Using the second order stabilizer Rényi entropy, and averaging over all 60 initial stabilizer states and the scattering angle, we compute and minimize the magic production as a function of $s^2_W$ in the Møller scattering $e^-e^-\to e^-e^-$, which is free of kinematic thresholds. At the centre-of-mass energy $\sqrt{s}=m_Z$, there is a unique minimum in magic production at $\mathbf{s}^{2}_W(m_Z)=0.2317$, which agrees with the measured $\widehat{s}^2_W(m_Z)$ at the sub-percent level. At higher energies, the magic-minimizing $\mathbf{s}^{2}_W$ continues to agree with the empirical value at the percent level or better, up to 10 TeV. The finding suggests the electroweak sector of the SM tends to generate minimal quantum resources from the computational viewpoint.

# A Quantum Computational Determination of the Weak Mixing Angle in the Standard Model

## Key claim
The weak mixing angle that minimizes magic production (non-stabilizerness) in Møller scattering at the Z boson mass is s²_W(m_Z) = 0.2317, which agrees with the experimentally measured value of 0.23129 ± 0.00004 at the sub-percent level. This agreement persists at the percent level or better up to 10 TeV, suggesting the electroweak sector of the Standard Model tends to generate minimal quantum resources from a computational viewpoint.

## Method
The authors computed the second-order stabilizer Rényi entropy (SRE) for the final spin states of 2-to-2 charged lepton scattering at tree level in the Standard Model, mediated by photon and Z boson exchange. They averaged the SRE over all 60 two-qubit stabilizer initial states (which carry zero magic) and over the scattering solid angle for Møller scattering e⁻e⁻ → e⁻e⁻, which lacks s-channel contributions and is free of kinematic thresholds at the Z pole. The magic production was then minimized as a function of s²_W at various center-of-mass energies, with the renormalization group running of the weak mixing angle included for comparison.

## Key numbers
- The magic-minimizing weak mixing angle at √s = m_Z = 91.2 GeV is s²_W(m_Z) = 0.2317, compared to the experimental value ŝ²_W(m_Z) = 0.23129 ± 0.00004 (sub-percent agreement).
- For tau lepton Møller scattering, the magic-minimizing value becomes s²_W = 0.2316.
- The maximum possible second-order SRE for two-qubit states is M₂ ≤ log(16/7) ≈ 0.827.
- The agreement between magic-minimizing s²_W and the empirical value holds up to √s = 10 TeV, never deviating by more than a few percent.
- At s²_W = 1/4, the Z coupling to charged leptons becomes purely axial, which maximizes entanglement in e⁺e⁻ → μ⁺μ⁻ scattering (Cervera-Lierta, 2017), but the magic minimum does not occur at this value.

## Context
This work extends recent efforts to understand fundamental interactions from quantum information perspectives, including studies of enhanced symmetries (Beane, 2018; Low, 2021; Liu, 2022), area laws in particle scattering (Aoude, 2024; Low, 2024), and magic state production in nuclear and particle physics (Robin, 2024; Liu, 2025; Gargalionis, 2025). It contrasts with prior work by Cervera-Lierta (2017) that obtained s²_W = 1/4 from maximal entanglement in e⁺e⁻ → μ⁺μ⁻, and with Thaler (2024) on entanglement minimization for flavor mixing. The finding that the Standard Model minimizes quantum computational resources opens a new frontier for understanding the electroweak sector from quantum computation perspectives, and suggests that fundamental forces are not efficient generators of the magic needed for quantum speedup, consistent with Liu (2025) and Gargalionis (2025).
