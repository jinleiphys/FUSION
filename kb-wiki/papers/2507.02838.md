# Early time hydrodynamic attractor in a nearly-unitary Fermi gas

> The hydrodynamic attractors paradigm aims to explain the applicability of hydrodynamics after a very short timescale in ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC in terms of the emergence of universal behavior across different initial states. The attractors observed in theoretical models are first driven by a rapid expansion of matter, which later gives way to the decay of exponentially damped transient excitations into the viscous fluid regime. Recently, a theoretical proposal was made how to realize hydrodynamic attractors in cold atomic gases focusing on the decay of transients and opening prospects for a future experimental realization in table top experiments. Motivated by this, in the present paper we show how the far-from-equilibrium, expansion-driven part of the hydrodynamic attractor emerges in a model of a nearly-unitary Fermi gas.

# Early time hydrodynamic attractor in a nearly-unitary Fermi gas

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the expansion-driven stage (STAGE E) of a hydrodynamic attractor can emerge in a realistic model of a nearly-unitary Fermi gas, provided one tracks both the bulk pressure and the energy density simultaneously. This goes beyond the prior proposal by Fujii (2024) which focused only on the decay of transient excitations (STAGE T) and the hydrodynamic tail (STAGE H).

## Method

The authors model a Fermi gas close to unitarity (k_F a >> 1) in the normal phase (T > 0.2 T_F) using coupled evolution equations for the energy density and the non-equilibrium bulk pressure. The scattering length is driven as a^{-1}(t) = a_0^{-1} t^{-alpha} with alpha > 0, mimicking the Bjorken expansion geometry. Transport coefficients (bulk viscosity zeta, relaxation time tau_Pi, and equilibrium contact density C_eq) are taken from equilibrium Luttinger-Ward model calculations by Enss (2010, 2019). The authors analyze the convergence of solution sets in the (energy density, bulk pressure) plane using principal component analysis.

## Key numbers

- The system is studied in the regime k_F a >> 1 and temperature T > 0.2 T_F, where T_F = E_F = k_F^2/(2m) is the Fermi temperature
- The equilibrium contact density at high temperature (theta >> 1) is C_eq = 8 pi^2 n^2 / (m T) = 4 pi m * (8/(3 pi theta)) * E_F n k_F^{-1}
- The bulk viscosity scales as zeta ~ a^{-2} close to unitarity
- The drive exponent alpha takes values 1/8, 3/8, and 1 in the numerical simulations
- Initial conditions are set at t_0 = 10^{-2} (in units of E_F^{-1}) and t_0 = 1
- The energy density grows by about an order of magnitude during the early time evolution
- The equilibrium shift parameter xi_n is approximately 0.45 (Ku, 2012)

## Context

This work extends the hydrodynamic attractor paradigm from ultra-relativistic nuclear collisions at RHIC and LHC to cold atomic gases, building on the theoretical proposal by Fujii (2024) for realizing attractors in Fermi gases with time-dependent scattering length. The authors show that the expansion-driven information loss mechanism (STAGE E), previously studied in Bjorken flow models by Blaizot (2017) and Heller (2015), can also appear in near-unitary Fermi gases, though with the important difference that the attractor requires tracking both energy density and bulk pressure rather than a single observable. This enables future experimental realization in table-top cold atom experiments, complementing the existing observation of non-thermal fixed points by Prufer (2018) and Erne (2018) and elliptic flow by Brandstetter (2023) in such systems.
