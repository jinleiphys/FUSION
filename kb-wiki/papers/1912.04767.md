# Dynamic Structure of Hadrons in ChPT

> The Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) has been very successful in describing low-energy hadronic properties in the non-perturbative regime of Quantum Chromodynamics. The results of ChPT, many of which are currently under active experimental investigation, provide stringent predictions of many fundamental properties of hadrons, including quantities such as electromagnetic polarizabilities. The paper outlines our semi-automated calculations in ChPT, the corresponding results for the electric and magnetic polarizabilities of the proton and our predictions for Compton differential cross sections.

## Key claim

The paper presents semi-automated Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) calculations of proton electric and magnetic polarizabilities and Compton differential cross sections, but the results explicitly depend on including the Delta-pole resonance contribution from the small-scale expansion (SSE) approach to shift the magnetic polarizability from negative (diamagnetic) to positive (paramagnetic) values.

## Method

The authors computed the Compton scattering amplitude using the Chiral Hadronic Model (CHM) in a basis of nine Dirac-chain structure functions, then connected these to the standard Compton structure functions R_i which relate directly to electric, magnetic, and spin-dependent polarizabilities via multipole expansion. The calculations included one-loop pion-nucleon contributions at O(p^3), the Wess-Zumino-Witten (WZW) anomalous interaction, and tree-level Delta-pole resonance contributions borrowed from the small-scale expansion (SSE) approach.

## Key numbers

- Proton electric polarizability: alpha_p = 10.63 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3), composed of 7.38 from pi-loop, -0.95 from Delta-pole, and 4.2 from SSE contributions
- Proton magnetic polarizability: beta_p = 1.49 (in units of 10^-4 fm^3), composed of -2.20 from pi-loop, 3.0 from Delta-pole, and 0.7 from SSE contributions
- The proton polarizabilities show almost no energy dependence below 50 MeV photon energy in the center-of-mass frame
- The Delta-pole contribution shifts beta_p from negative to positive values for energies up to 250 MeV

## Context

This work extends ChPT calculations of nucleon polarizabilities by incorporating Delta-pole resonance contributions from the small-scale expansion (SSE) approach, building on prior work by Babusci and collaborators on Compton structure functions and by Wess, Zumino, and Witten on anomalous interactions. The results enable comparison with ongoing experimental measurements of Compton scattering and nucleon polarizabilities, with future work planned to include Delta-type resonances in the loops for both polarizabilities and differential cross sections.
