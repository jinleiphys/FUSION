# The Muon Anomalous Magnetic Moment and the Pion Polarizability

> We compute the charged pion loop contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment $a_μ$, taking into account the effect of the charged pion polarizability, $(α_1-β_1)_{π^+}$. We evaluate this contribution using two different models that are consistent with the requirements of chiral symmetry in the low-momentum regime and perturbative quantum chromodynamics in the asymptotic region. The result increases the disagreement between the present experimental value for $a_μ$ and the theoretical, Standard Model prediction by as much as $\sim 60\times 10^{-11}$, depending on the value of $(α_1-β_1)_{π^+}$ and the choice of the model. The planned determination of $(α_1-β_1)_{π^+}$ at Jefferson Laboratory will eliminate the dominant parametric error, leaving a theoretical model uncertainty commensurate with the error expected from planned Fermilab measurement of $a_μ$.

## Key claim

The charged pion loop contribution to the muon anomalous magnetic moment, when computed with models that include the pion polarizability and are consistent with both chiral symmetry at low momentum and perturbative QCD at high momentum, can increase the discrepancy between the experimental value and the Standard Model prediction by as much as approximately 60 × 10⁻¹¹, depending on the value of the polarizability and the choice of model.

## Method

The authors computed the charged pion loop contribution to the hadronic light-by-light (HLBL) scattering amplitude for the muon anomalous magnetic moment, incorporating the effect of the charged pion polarizability (α₁ − β₁)π⁺. They constructed two phenomenological models (Model I and Model II) for the γγππ vertex that reproduce the low-energy chiral perturbation theory (χPT) results for the polarizability and charge radius, and that also satisfy the asymptotic 1/k² falloff of the virtual Compton amplitude required by perturbative QCD. The resulting Feynman parameter integrals for aμ were evaluated numerically using a Monte Carlo routine.

## Key numbers

*   The LO (scalar QED) charged pion loop contribution to aμHLBL is −44 × 10⁻¹¹.
*   The HLS model result for the charged pion loop contribution is −4.4(2) × 10⁻¹¹.
*   The ENJL model result for the charged pion loop contribution is −19(13) × 10⁻¹¹.
*   For the polarizability LEC value (α₉ʳ + α₁₀ʳ) = (1.32 ± 1.4) × 10⁻³ (from radiative pion decay), Model I gives a contribution of −11 × 10⁻¹¹ and Model II gives −40 × 10⁻¹¹.
*   For the polarizability LEC value (α₉ʳ + α₁₀ʳ) = (3.1 ± 0.9) × 10⁻³ (from radiative pion photoproduction), Model I gives a contribution of −34 × 10⁻¹¹ and Model II gives −71 × 10⁻¹¹.
*   The planned JLab experiment will determine the polarizability LECs with an uncertainty of δ(α₉ʳ + α₁₀ʳ) = 0.16 × 10⁻³.

## Context

This work extends the analysis of the charged pion loop contribution to aμHLBL, which was previously computed using leading-order scalar QED (Kinoshita, 1984), the Hidden Local Symmetry (HLS) approach (Hayakawa, 1995; Hayakawa, 1996), and the Extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (ENJL) model (Bijnens, 1995). The authors show that these earlier models fail to reproduce the low-momentum structure of the HLBL amplitude implied by QCD, specifically omitting terms proportional to the pion polarizability. The results contradict the smaller shift found by Bijnens (2012) and Abyaneh (2012) using a cutoff regulator, and they motivate the planned JLab measurement of the pion polarizability to reduce parametric uncertainty, while further experimental tests of the momentum-dependence of the polarizability are needed to reduce model dependence.
