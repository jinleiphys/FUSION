# S-wave Meson-Meson Scattering from Unitarized U(3) Chiral Lagrangians

> An investigation of the s-wave channels in meson-meson scattering is performed within a U(3) chiral unitary approach. Our calculations are based on a chiral effective Lagrangian which includes the eta' as an explicit degree of freedom and incorporates important features of the underlying QCD Lagrangian such as the axial U(1) anomaly. We employ a coupled channel Bethe-Salpeter equation to generate poles from composed states of two pseudoscalar mesons. Our results are compared with experimental phase shifts up to 1.5 GeV and effects of the eta' within this scheme are discussed.

## Key claim

The inclusion of the eta' meson as an explicit degree of freedom in a U(3) chiral unitary approach does not destroy the agreement with experimental meson-meson scattering data that was previously achieved with SU(3) chiral Lagrangians, provided the same low-energy constants are used and the additional U(3) couplings are set to zero.

## Method

The authors derived s-wave interaction kernels from a U(3) chiral effective Lagrangian up to fourth chiral order, including eta-eta' mixing with a two-angle scheme. They solved a coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter equation with on-shell amplitudes and dimensional regularization to generate poles corresponding to scalar resonances, fitting the free parameters (regularization scale and low-energy constants beta_0, beta_3, beta_5, beta_8) to experimental phase shift data up to 1.5 GeV.

## Key numbers

- Pole position of the sigma/f0(400-1200) resonance: (448 - 263i) MeV in SU(3), (459 - 233i) MeV in U(3)
- Pole position of the f0(980) resonance: (983 - 14i) MeV in SU(3), (994 - 10i) MeV in U(3)
- Pole position of the kappa/K0*(900) resonance: (740 - 246i) MeV in SU(3), (737 - 248i) MeV in U(3)
- Pole positions of the a0(980) cusp resonance: (1081 - 36i) MeV and (791 - 75i) MeV in SU(3); (1061 - 55i) MeV and (761 - 62i) MeV in U(3)
- Low-energy constant values used: beta_0 = (0.6 +/- 0.1) x 10^-3, beta_3 = (-0.5 +/- 0.1) x 10^-3, beta_5 = (1.4 +/- 0.2) x 10^-3, beta_8 = (0.2 +/- 0.2) x 10^-3
- Regularization scale mu = 1 GeV for all channels except I=1/2 where mu = 0.8 GeV

## Context

This work extends the SU(3) coupled-channel Bethe-Salpeter approach of Oller and Oset (1997) and Oller (1999) by including the eta' meson explicitly, motivated by the axial U(1) anomaly and the need for a consistent treatment of eta8-eta0 mixing as developed in Beisert (2001). The authors find that, unlike in the meson-baryon sector where Bass (2001) observed substantial changes when including the eta', the purely mesonic s-wave scattering up to 1.5 GeV is largely unaffected by eta' channels. This enables future extension to p-wave scattering and further study of the role of the axial anomaly in scalar meson structure.
