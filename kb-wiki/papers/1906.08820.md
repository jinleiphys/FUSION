# Non-relativistic expansion of single-nucleon Dirac equation: Comparison between Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation and similarity renormalization group

> By following the Foldy-Wouthuysen (FW) transformation of the Dirac equation, we work out the exact analytic expressions up to the $1/M^4$ order for the general cases in the covariant density functional theory. These results are further compared with the corresponding ones derived from another novel non-relativistic expansion method, the similarity renormalization group (SRG). Based on that, the origin of the difference between the results obtained by the FW transformation and SRG method is explored.

## Key claim

The Foldy-Wouthuysen (FW) transformation and the similarity renormalization group (SRG) method produce different analytic expressions for the non-relativistic expansion of the single-nucleon Dirac equation starting at the 1/M^3 order, but these differences arise from an additional unitary transformation that does not affect the single-particle spectra, so both methods yield identical energy eigenvalues.

## Method

The authors performed the Foldy-Wouthuysen transformation of the Dirac Hamiltonian for nucleons in covariant density functional theory, including both scalar and vector potentials, deriving exact analytic expressions up to 1/M^4 order. They compared these results with the corresponding expansion obtained from the similarity renormalization group method, which uses a flow equation to gradually diagonalize the Hamiltonian. The difference between the two sets of expressions was traced to an additional unitary transformation acting on the already-decoupled Hamiltonian, and the explicit form of this transformation was identified.

## Key numbers

- Typical values of the strong vector potential V(r) ~ 300 MeV and scalar potential S(r) ~ -350 MeV in covariant density functional theory, with nucleon mass M = 939 MeV
- To achieve single-particle energy accuracy at the 0.1 MeV level, terms up to 1/M^4 order are needed
- The FW and SRG results agree up to 1/M^2 order, with differences appearing at 1/M^3 and 1/M^4 orders
- The difference at 1/M^3 order is given by (beta/32M^3)[[O^2, epsilon], epsilon]
- The difference at 1/M^4 order is given by (1/64M^4)[[O^2, epsilon], O^2] + (1/128M^4)[([epsilon^2, O^2] - 2[epsilon, O epsilon O]), epsilon]

## Context

This work extends the non-relativistic expansion of the Dirac equation for nuclear systems beyond the leading-order terms typically treated in earlier FW applications, such as those by Greiner (1990) which covered only up to 1/M^2 or 1/M^3 order. It builds on the SRG expansion up to 1/M^3 order by Guo (2012) for nuclear pseudospin symmetry, and the recent extension to 1/M^4 order by Guo and Liang (2019). The results enable a rigorous connection between relativistic covariant density functional theory and non-relativistic density functional approaches, with the explicit FW transformation forms allowing straightforward transformation of other operators like the one-body density operator.
