# Effects of Nuclear Structure on Quasi-fission

> The quasi-fission mechanism hinders fusion of heavy systems because of a mass flow between the reactants, leading to a re-separation of more symmetric fragments in the exit channel. A good understanding of the competition between fusion and quasi-fission mechanisms is expected to be of great help to optimize the formation and study of heavy and superheavy nuclei. Quantum microscopic models, such as the time-dependent Hartree-Fock approach, allow for a treatment of all degrees of freedom associated to the dynamics of each nucleon. This provides a description of the complex reaction mechanisms, such as quasi-fission, with no parameter adjusted on reaction mechanisms. In particular, the role of the deformation and orientation of a heavy target, as well as the entrance channel magicity and isospin are investigated with theoretical and experimental approaches.

## Key claim

The paper argues that shell effects in the entrance channel hinder quasi-fission and favor fusion only for systems with small N/Z asymmetry; the apparent exception of the 40Ca+208Pb system is explained by rapid charge equilibration that destroys the magic character of the di-nuclear system before quasi-fission occurs.

## Method

The study combines experimental measurements of mass-angle distributions (MAD) for several Ca,Ti+Pb,Hg reactions using two Multi-Wire Proportional Chambers at the Australian National University with time-dependent Hartree-Fock (TDHF) calculations using the tdhf3d code. The width of the fission fragment mass distribution is used to quantify the amount of fast re-separation associated with quasi-fission events, and TDHF calculations provide contact times, fragment masses, and scattering angles for comparison.

## Key numbers

- Contact time for 70Zn+208Pb (Z1Z2=2460) reaches a maximum of approximately 4 zs at about 1.15 times the proximity barrier, with no fusion observed.
- An extra-push energy of E_X^{TDHF} ~ 23 MeV is predicted for 90Zr+124Sn, compared to the extra-push model value E_X^{Swi.} ~ 15 MeV from Swiatecki (1982).
- The initial N/Z difference is 0.54 for 40Ca+208Pb and 0.14 for 48Ca+208Pb; charge equilibration in the 40Ca+208Pb system occurs within about 2 zs, which is shorter than the quasi-fission time of longer than 10 zs.
- In 40Ca+238U at E_c.m.=206 MeV, collisions with the tip of 238U produce quasi-fission with light fragment masses in the 55Mn region at L=80 hbar, while collisions with the side produce more symmetric fragments such as 111Rh+167Ho at L=20 hbar.
- The scattering angle for 40Ca+238U collisions with the tip ranges from 180 degrees at L=0 hbar down to about 17 degrees at L=80 hbar.

## Context

This work extends experimental studies of quasi-fission in heavy systems such as 16O,32S+238U (Hinde 1996, Itkis 2011, Nishio 2007) and 32S+208Pb (Nishio 2007), and theoretical investigations using macroscopic approaches (Swiatecki 1982, Zagrebaev 2001, Adamian 2003). The results enable future comparisons between TDHF predictions and experimental data, and motivate the application of beyond-mean-field approaches such as time-dependent RPA (Simenel 2011) or stochastic mean-field (Washiyama 2009) to obtain complete mass-angle distributions.
