# Global Strings in High Density QCD

> We show that several types of global strings occur in colour superconducting quark matter due to the spontaneous violation of relevant U(1) symmetries. These include the baryon U(1)_B, and approximate axial U(1)_A symmetries as well as an approximate U(1)_S arising from kaon condensation. We discuss some general properties of these strings and their interactions. In particular, we demonstrate that the U(1)_A strings behave as superconducting strings. We draw some parallels between these strings and global cosmological strings and discuss some possible implications of these strings to the physics in neutron star cores.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that several types of global strings occur in colour superconducting quark matter due to spontaneous violation of U(1) symmetries, but the existence of U(1)_A strings is conditional on the instanton density being small at large chemical potential, which suppresses the chiral anomaly and makes the U(1)_A symmetry approximate rather than exact.

## Method

The authors construct effective Lagrangians for the Goldstone modes associated with spontaneously broken U(1) symmetries in both the N_f=2 (2SC) and N_f=3 (CFL) colour-superconducting phases of QCD at high baryon density. They solve the classical field equations for static string configurations using a complex scalar field description, where the phase corresponds to the Goldstone boson and the magnitude describes the condensate. For the U(1)_A strings, they also solve the relevant Maxwell equations in the string background to determine electromagnetic properties.

## Key numbers

- Condensate magnitude: |X| = |Y| = (3/(2√2 π)) (μ² Δ / g) for the 2SC phase
- Decay constant for η' in 2SC: f² = μ²/(8π²), with velocity u² = 1/3
- η' mass: m_{η'} = 2π Δ √a, where a ~ (Λ_QCD/μ)^{29/3} vanishes at large μ
- Domain wall tension: σ = 8√(2a) u f μ Δ ~ √a μ² Δ
- Decay constant for axial Goldstone in CFL: f²_A = 9μ²/π², with u²_A = 1/3
- Kaon condensate pseudo-Goldstone mass: m_{φ_S} ~ 50 KeV
- String core size: of order (2√3 Δ)^{-1} in dimensionless units
- String tension diverges logarithmically with outer cutoff radius R

## Context

This work extends the discovery of QCD domain walls in high-density quark matter by Son (2000) to include topological string configurations, and draws parallels between these QCD strings and global cosmological strings studied by Vilenkin (1994) and others. The U(1)_B strings in the CFL phase are identified with superfluid vortices likely to form in rotating neutron star cores, following the formalism of Davis (1989) that connects static global strings to superfluid vortices in a nonzero-density background. The paper enables future study of how these strings may affect electromagnetic properties of neutron star cores, particularly through the superconducting behaviour of U(1)_A strings.
