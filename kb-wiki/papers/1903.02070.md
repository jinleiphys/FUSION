# Electromagnetic couplings of pentaquarks

> In this contribution, we discuss the electromagnetic couplings of pentaquark states with hidden charm. This work is motivated by recent experiments at CERN by the LHCb Collaboraton and current experiments at JLab to confirm the existence of hidden-charm pentaquarks in photoproduction experiments.

# Electromagnetic couplings of pentaquarks

## Key claim
The electromagnetic couplings of ground-state $uudc\bar{c}$ pentaquark states with $J^P=3/2^-$ are computed, but the authors note that the photon momentum is large and therefore the couplings are expected to be strongly suppressed by the form factor from the orbital part of the wave function, which is not evaluated in this work.

## Method
The authors construct the color-spin-flavor wave functions for $uudc\bar{c}$ pentaquark states using permutation symmetry under $S_4$ (or the isomorphic tetrahedral group ${\cal T}_d$), requiring the four-quark subsystem to be antisymmetric and the total wave function to be a color singlet. They then compute the helicity amplitudes for the radiative decay $P_c \rightarrow N + \gamma$ using a nonrelativistic form of the electromagnetic interaction, focusing on the $c\bar{c}$ annihilation term and extracting the color-spin-flavor contribution $\beta_\nu$ common to all quark models.

## Key numbers
- Seven total $uudc\bar{c}$ ground-state pentaquark configurations exist with $J^P=3/2^-$: three belonging to a flavor decuplet and four to a flavor octet.
- For the octet configuration with $\phi_{F_2} \times \chi_{F_2}$, the helicity amplitude coefficients are $\beta_{1/2} = \frac{1}{6\sqrt{2}} e_c$ and $\beta_{3/2} = \frac{1}{2\sqrt{6}} e_c$, where $e_c = 2/3$ is the charm quark electric charge.
- For the octet configuration with $\phi_{E} \times \chi_{F_2}$, the coefficients are $\beta_{1/2} = -\frac{1}{6} e_c$ and $\beta_{3/2} = -\frac{1}{2\sqrt{3}} e_c$.
- For the octet configuration with $\phi_{F_1} \times \chi_{F_2}$, the coefficients are $\beta_{1/2} = -\frac{1}{2\sqrt{6}} e_c$ and $\beta_{3/2} = -\frac{1}{2\sqrt{2}} e_c$.
- The couplings to the octet configuration with $\phi_{A_1}$ and all three decuplet configurations vanish (coefficients are zero).
- The helicity amplitudes satisfy the relation $\beta_{1/2} = \beta_{3/2}/\sqrt{3}$ for all cases considered.

## Context
This work is motivated by the observation of hidden-charm pentaquark states by the LHCb Collaboration (LHCb, 2015-2019) and by ongoing photoproduction experiments at JLab. It extends the classification scheme of $uudc\bar{c}$ pentaquark states developed in JPG2019 and Cocoyoc2017, and uses methods similar to those applied to baryon photocouplings by Copley (1969) and to quark-antiquark annihilation by Le Yaouanc (1973). The results provide the color-spin-flavor part of the electromagnetic couplings needed to interpret near-threshold $J/\psi$ photoproduction experiments, though the authors note that the full radiative decay widths require inclusion of the orbital form factor, which is model-dependent and expected to cause strong suppression.
