# Photon structure functions at small $x$ in holographic QCD

> We investigate the photon structure functions at small Bjorken variable $x$ in the framework of the holographic QCD, assuming dominance of the Pomeron exchange. The quasi-real photon structure functions are expressed as convolution of the Brower-Polchinski-Strassler-Tan (BPST) Pomeron kernel and the known wave functions of the U(1) vector field in the five-dimensional AdS space, in which the involved parameters in the BPST kernel have been fixed in previous studies of the nucleon structure functions. The predicted photon structure functions, as confronted with data, provide a clean test of the BPST kernel. The agreement between theoretical predictions and data is demonstrated, which supports applications of holographic QCD to hadronic processes in the nonperturbative region. Our results are also consistent with those derived from the parton distribution functions of the photon proposed by Glück, Reya, and Schienbein, implying realization of the vector meson dominance in the present model setup.

# Photon structure functions at small $x$ in holographic QCD

## Key claim

The photon structure functions at small Bjorken variable $x$ can be described by the BPST Pomeron kernel in holographic QCD with only a single free parameter (the overall coefficient $g_0^2$), and the resulting predictions agree with LEP data and with the GRS parton distribution functions, suggesting that vector meson dominance is realized in this model setup.

## Method

The authors compute the quasi-real photon structure functions $F_2^\gamma$ and $F_L^\gamma$ by convolving the BPST Pomeron kernel (both conformal and modified versions) with the known wave functions of the U(1) vector field in five-dimensional AdS space. Two of the three adjustable parameters in the BPST kernel were fixed from previous studies of nucleon structure functions (Watanabe 2013), leaving only the overall coefficient $g_0^2$ to be tuned to fit the absolute magnitude of the OPAL data. The authors consider two schemes for handling the conformal invariant $\tau$ to avoid negative values in the integration region.

## Key numbers

- The parameter $z_0$ (governing confinement strength) is taken as $z_0 = 4.25$ GeV$^{-1}$ or $z_0 = 6$ GeV$^{-1}$ for the modified kernel, values fixed from nucleon structure function studies (Watanabe 2013).
- The overall coefficient $g_0^2$ is determined as $g_0^2 = 17.51$ for the conformal kernel and $g_0^2 = 49.01$ for the modified kernel, using 9 OPAL data points with $x \leq 0.025$.
- The target photon virtuality is assumed to be $P^2 = 0.01$ GeV$^2$, consistent with typical LEP measurements.
- The analysis covers the kinematic region $10^{-5} \leq x \leq 10^{-2}$ and $1$ GeV$^2 \leq Q^2 \leq 10$ GeV$^2$.
- The ratio $R_{L/T} = F_L^\gamma / F_T^\gamma$ is found to be around 0.4 in the considered kinematic region, indicating the longitudinal structure function is not negligible.
- The hadronic component in the GRS photon PDFs dominates at small $x$, contributing about 90% (80%) of the total $F_2^\gamma$ for $Q^2 = 1$ (10) GeV$^2$.

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach previously applied to nucleon structure functions (Brower 2010, Watanabe 2012, Watanabe 2013) to the case of photon structure functions, providing a clean test of the BPST Pomeron kernel since the photon wave functions are known from the 5D U(1) vector field without additional modeling. The results are consistent with the GRS photon PDFs (Gluck, Reya, and Schienbein 1999), which include hadronic components via pion PDF parameterizations, and differ from the GRV PDFs (Gluck, Reya, and Vogt 1991) at small $x$. The predictions at very low $x$ (below $10^{-3}$) can be tested at future linear colliders such as the International Linear Collider.
