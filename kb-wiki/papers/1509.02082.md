# Baryon effects on the location of QCD's critical end point

> The location of the critical end point of QCD has been determined in previous studies of $N_f=2+1$ and $N_f=2+1+1$ dynamical quark flavors using a (truncated) set of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark and gluon propagators of Landau-gauge QCD. A source for systematic errors in these calculations has been the omission of terms in the quark-gluon interaction that can be parametrized in terms of baryonic degrees of freedom. These have a potentially large dependence on chemical potential and therefore may affect the location of the critical end point. In this exploratory study we estimate the effects of these contributions, both in the vacuum and at finite temperature and chemical potential. We find only a small influence of baryonic contributions on the location of the critical end point. We estimate the robustness of this result by parameterizing further dependencies on chemical potential.

## Key claim
The authors estimate that baryonic back-reaction effects on the quark-gluon vertex have only a small influence on the location of QCD's critical end point, but this result is exploratory and depends on parameterizations of further chemical-potential dependencies that have not been fully determined.

## Method
The authors use a truncated set of Dyson-Schwinger equations for the quark and gluon propagators in Landau-gauge QCD with two degenerate quark flavors, extending previous work by including diagrams in the quark-gluon vertex that can be parametrized in terms of baryonic degrees of freedom. They approximate the baryon's three-body Faddeev amplitude using a quark-diquark ansatz with scalar diquarks only, and evaluate the resulting diquark and baryon loop contributions to the quark self-energy at finite temperature and chemical potential using vacuum expressions for the bound-state amplitudes and masses.

## Key numbers
- The quark mass function at zero momentum in the vacuum decreases from M(0)=640 MeV (without baryonic loops) to M(0)=545 MeV when both diquark and baryon loops are included, a reduction of approximately 15%.
- The nucleon mass used is m_N=0.938 GeV and the scalar diquark mass is m_dq=0.810 GeV, taken from bound-state calculations.
- The critical end point from previous work without baryonic effects was located at (T^c, mu_q^c) = (115, 168) MeV for N_f=2+1 flavors.

## Context
This work extends previous Dyson-Schwinger studies by Fischer et al. (2012, 2013, 2014) that located the critical end point for N_f=2, 2+1, and 2+1+1 flavors but omitted baryonic vertex corrections. It contrasts with two-color QCD studies by Strodthoff et al. (2011, 2013) where baryonic effects were found to be crucial for the existence of a critical end point. The results enable future work that should determine the temperature and chemical-potential dependence of diquark and baryon masses and Bethe-Salpeter amplitudes from their bound-state equations.
