## Context
This work extends the approach of Flambaum (2006) for calculating nuclear sensitivity to fundamental constant variation, applying it systematically to M√∂ssbauer transitions. It builds on prior calculations for 229Th by Fadeev (2020) and provides the first comprehensive tabulation of enhancement factors for dozens of M√∂ssbauer transitions. The paper notes that atomic optical clocks (e.g., Lange 2021) are sensitive to őĪ variation but not to quark mass variation, whereas M√∂ssbauer transitions offer complementary sensitivity to m_q/őõ_QCD. The results enable searches for dark matter fields (as proposed by Stadnik and Arvanitaki) and Yukawa-type interactions (as in Gratta 2020) using M√∂ssbauer spectroscopy, potentially reaching sensitivities orders of magnitude beyond current atomic limits.
