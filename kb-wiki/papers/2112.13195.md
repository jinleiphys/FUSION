# Effects of variation of the fine structure constant $α$ and quark mass $m_q$ in Mössbauer nuclear transitions

> High accuracy measurements in Mössbauer transitions open up the possibility to use them in the search for temporal and spatial variation of the fine-structure constant $α$, quark mass $m_q$, and dark matter field which may lead to the variation of $α$ and $m_q$. We calculate the sensitivity of nuclear transitions to variation of $α$ and $m_q$. Mössbauer transitions have high sensitivity to variation of quark mass $m_q$ and the strong interaction scale $Λ_{QCD}$, to which atomic optical clocks are not sensitive. The enhancement factors $K$, defined by $\frac{δf}{f} =K_{α}\frac{δα}α$ and $\frac{δf}{f} =K_{q }\frac{δm_q}{m_q}$ where $f$ is the transition energy, may be large in some transitions. The 8~eV nuclear clock transition in $^{229}$Th ($K_q \approx 10^4$) and 76~eV transition in $^{235}$U ($K_α\approx K_q \approx 10^3$) may be investigated using laser spectroscopy methods.

# Effects of variation of the fine structure constant α and quark mass m_q in Mössbauer nuclear transitions

## Key claim
Mössbauer nuclear transitions can serve as sensitive probes for temporal and spatial variation of the fine structure constant α and quark mass m_q, with enhancement factors K that may reach 10^4 in the 8 eV 229Th transition and 10^3 in the 76 eV 235U transition; however, the actual sensitivity depends on the availability of accurate measurements of changes in mean square charge radius and quadrupole moment between nuclear states, and for many transitions the errors remain large (25% or more).

## Method
The authors calculate the Coulomb energy difference ΔE_C between ground and excited nuclear states using measured changes in mean square charge radius Δ⟨r²⟩ and intrinsic quadrupole moment ΔQ₀, modeling the nucleus as a spheroid. For transitions where ΔQ₀ measurements are unavailable, they employ a constant density (constant volume) ansatz to estimate ΔQ₀ from Δ⟨r²⟩. The enhancement factors K_α and K_q are then derived from K_α = ΔE_C/f (where f is the transition energy) and K_q = 1.45(K_α − 1), using the relation δE_S/E_S = −1.45 δm_q/m_q for the strong-interaction contribution to nuclear binding.

## Key numbers
- 229Th 8 eV transition: K_q ≈ 1.19 × 10^4 (13% error), K_α ≈ −0.82 × 10^4 (13% error)
- 235U 76 eV transition: K_α ≈ K_q ≈ 10^3 (conservative estimate from Nilsson model)
- 181Ta 6 keV transition: K_α = 30 (25% error), K_q = 43 (26% error)
- 151Eu 22 keV transition: K_α = −4.6 (29% error), K_q = −8.1 (24% error)
- 57Fe 14.4 keV transition: K_α = 2.7 (9% error), K_q = 2.4 (14% error)
- 119Sn 23.9 keV transition: K_α = −1.053 (3% error), K_q = −2.98 (1% error)
- Average |ΔE_C| in medium and heavy deformed nuclei: ∼70 keV; in light and spherical nuclei: ∼30 keV
- Estimated sensitivity from Mössbauer experiments: δα/α ∼ δm_q/m_q ∼ 10^−20 to 10^−23, compared to current atomic clock limits of δα/α ∼ 10^−17 to 10^−18 and δm_q/m_q ∼ 10^−14

## Context
This work extends the approach of Flambaum (2006) for calculating nuclear sensitivity to fundamental constant variation, applying it systematically to Mössbauer transitions. It builds on prior calculations for 229Th by Fadeev (2020) and provides the first comprehensive tabulation of enhancement factors for dozens of Mössbauer transitions. The paper notes that atomic optical clocks (e.g., Lange 2021) are sensitive to α variation but not to quark mass variation, whereas Mössbauer transitions offer complementary sensitivity to m_q/Λ_QCD. The results enable searches for dark matter fields (as proposed by Stadnik and Arvanitaki) and Yukawa-type interactions (as in Gratta 2020) using Mössbauer spectroscopy, potentially reaching sensitivities orders of magnitude beyond current atomic limits.
