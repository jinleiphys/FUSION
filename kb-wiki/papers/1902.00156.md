# Quark-Hadron Crossover with Vortices

> The quark-hadron crossover conjecture was proposed as a continuity between hadronic matter and quark matter with no phase transition. It is based on matching of symmetry and excitations in both the phases. It connects hyperon matter and color-flavor locked (CFL) phase of color superconductivity in the limit of light strange quark mass. We study generalization of this conjecture in the presence of topological vortices. We propose a picture where hadronic superfluid vortices in hyperon matter could be connected to non-Abelian vortices (color magnetic flux tubes) in the CFL phase during this crossover. We propose that three hadronic superfluid vortices must join together to three non-Abelian vortices with different color fluxes with the total color magnetic fluxes canceled out, where the junction is called a colorful boojum.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the quark-hadron crossover conjecture, which posits a smooth continuity between hyperon matter and the color-flavor locked (CFL) phase with no phase transition, can be extended to include topological vortices only if a junction (a "colorful boojum") forms where three hadronic superfluid vortices join together into three non-Abelian vortices via a single Abelian CFL vortex, because the phase changes of quark wave functions around a single vortex do not match between the two phases.

## Method

The authors compared the Onsager-Feynman circulation and the generalized Aharonov-Bohm phases acquired by quark wave functions after one encirclement of vortices in both the hadronic phase (using a Ginzburg-Landau description of \(\Lambda\Lambda\) pairing in hyperon matter) and the CFL phase (using Ginzburg-Landau theory for Abelian and non-Abelian vortices). They derived the phase-change matrices for quarks around a single \(\Lambda\Lambda\) vortex, a single Abelian CFL vortex, and three types of non-Abelian vortices (up-red, down-green, strange-blue), and then imposed the condition that these phases must match for a smooth crossover to occur.

## Key numbers

* The Onsager-Feynman circulation for a single \(\Lambda\Lambda\) vortex is \(C_{\Lambda\Lambda} = \frac{2\pi}{2\mu_\B}\), where \(\mu_\B\) is the chemical potential for a single baryon.
* The Onsager-Feynman circulation for an Abelian CFL vortex is \(C_{\rm A} = \frac{3\pi}{\mu_\B}\).
* The Onsager-Feynman circulation for a non-Abelian CFL vortex is \(C_{\rm N\!A} = \frac{\pi}{\mu_\B}\).
* The phase change of quarks after one encirclement of a single \(\Lambda\Lambda\) vortex is given by the matrix \(Q_{\Lambda\Lambda} = \frac{\pi}{3}\) times a \(3\times3\) matrix of all \(+1\) entries, corresponding to a \(\mathbb{Z}_6\) group.
* The phase change of quarks after one encirclement of a single non-Abelian vortex (e.g., the up-red vortex) is \(Q_{ur} = \pi\) times a matrix with \(-1\) entries for the first row and \(+1\) entries for the other two rows, corresponding to a \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) group.

## Context

This work extends the quark-hadron continuity conjecture of Schafer (1998) and the study of vortices in neutron stars by Alford (2018) by incorporating the detailed phase structure of quark wave functions. It proposes a specific topological configuration (a boojum) that resolves the mismatch between the \(\mathbb{Z}_6\) phase group of hadronic vortices and the \(\mathbb{Z}_2\) phase group of non-Abelian CFL vortices, building on prior work on non-Abelian vortices by Nakano (2007) and boojum structures by Eto (2013). This enables future studies of non-Abelian statistics of vortices, monopole confinement, and topological order in the context of neutron star interiors.
