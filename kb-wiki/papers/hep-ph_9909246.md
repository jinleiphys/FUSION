# On the Convergence of the Expansion of Renormalization Group Flow Equation

> We compare and discuss the dependence of a polynomial truncation of the effective potential used to solve exact renormalization group flow equation for a model with fermionic interaction (linear sigma model) with a grid solution. The sensitivity of the results on the underlying cutoff function is discussed. We explore the validity of the expansion method for second and first-order phase transitions.

# On the Convergence of the Expansion of Renormalization Group Flow Equation

## Key claim

The polynomial expansion of the effective potential used to solve exact renormalization group flow equations converges reliably only when combined with sufficiently smooth cutoff functions (order n >= 2), while the simplest cutoff function (I) yields poor or even worsening convergence as the expansion order increases, despite the abstract's general claim of comparing truncation with grid solutions.

## Method

The authors solve renormalization group flow equations for the linear sigma model coupled to N_f = 2 constituent quarks, using the local potential approximation (LPA) with a proper-time (heat-kernel) regularization. They compare two approaches: a grid discretization of the potential on 80 points in the range 0 < phi^2 < 0.05 GeV^2, and a polynomial expansion of the potential around its minimum up to order M (with M = 2, 4, 6, 8), deriving coupled flow equations for the expansion coefficients a_{2n} (symmetric phase) and b_{2n} (broken phase). Three different cutoff functions f_k^{(n)} are tested, corresponding to n = 1 (exponential times linear term), n = 2, and n = 3 (higher-order exponential series).

## Key numbers

- For cutoff function (I), the lowest-order M=2 truncation overestimates the pion decay constant f_pi by 70% compared to the grid solution.
- For cutoff function (II), the M=2 truncation overestimates f_pi by 25%; for cutoff function (III), the M=2 truncation overestimates f_pi by only 8%.
- The grid value of the critical temperature T_c is approximately 150 MeV for all three cutoff functions.
- For cutoff function (II), the M=2 truncation overshoots the asymptotic critical temperature by 25%, stabilizing at M >= 6; for cutoff function (III), the M=2 deviation is only 9%.

## Context

This work extends the renormalization group flow equation approach of Schaefer (1999) and earlier proper-time regularization studies by Liao (1996) and Oleszczuk (1994), specifically examining the convergence properties of polynomial truncations of the effective potential. The authors compare their results to the grid-based solution as the exact reference, and find that the cutoff function choice critically affects truncation convergence, with higher-order cutoff functions (n >= 2) providing much better behavior. The results enable practical guidance for future finite-density calculations using expansion techniques, showing that cutoff (III) with M=4 already gives reliable results, while cutoff (I) is unsuitable for polynomial expansion methods. The asymptotic divergence of higher expansion coefficients (b_6, b_8, etc.) in the infrared limit limits the radius of convergence of the polynomial approximation around the minimum.
