# $\bar K$ nuclear bound states in a dynamical model

> A comprehensive data base of K- atom level shifts and widths is re-analyzed in order to study the density dependence of the Kbar-nuclear optical potential. Significant departure from a t*rho form is found only for nuclear densities about and less than 20% of nuclear-matter density, and extrapolation to nuclear-matter density yields an attractive potential, about 170 MeV deep. Partial restoration of chiral symmetry compatible with pionic atoms and low-energy pion-nuclear data plays no role at the relevant low-density regime, but this effect is not ruled out at high densities. Kbar-nuclear bound states are generated across the periodic table self consistently, using a relativistic mean-field model Lagrangian which couples the Kbar to the scalar and vector meson fields mediating the nuclear interactions. The reduced phase space available for Kbar absorption from these bound states is taken into account by adding an energy-dependent imaginary term which underlies the corresponding Kbar-nuclear level widths, with a strength required by fits to the atomic data. Substantial polarization of the core nucleus is found for light nuclei, and the binding energies and widths calculated in this dynamical model differ appreciably from those calculated for a static nucleus. A wide range of binding energies is spanned by varying the Kbar couplings to the meson fields. Our calculations provide a lower limit of Gamma(Kbar) = 50 +/- 10 MeV on the width of nuclear bound states for Kbar binding energy in the range B(Kbar) = 100 - 200 MeV. Comments are made on the interpretation of the FINUDA experiment at DAFNE, Frascati, which claimed evidence for deeply bound (K- pp) states in light nuclei.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that a "deep" attractive $\bar K$-nucleus optical potential (about 170 MeV deep at nuclear-matter density) is required to fit kaonic atom data, but this density dependence departs from a simple $t\rho$ form only at nuclear densities below about 20% of nuclear-matter density; partial restoration of chiral symmetry plays no role in this low-density regime but is not ruled out at higher densities.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed a comprehensive database of $K^-$ atom level shifts and widths across the periodic table using a Klein-Gordon equation with an optical potential, testing three parameterizations: a simple $t\rho$ form, a density-dependent (DD) form with a power-law correction, and a novel "F" form that separates internal and external nuclear regions via a Fermi function. They then performed self-consistent relativistic mean-field (RMF) calculations for $\bar K$-nuclear bound states across the periodic table, coupling the $\bar K$ to scalar ($\sigma$) and vector ($\omega$) meson fields, and included an energy-dependent imaginary term with phase-space suppression factors for the absorption channels $\bar K N \rightarrow \pi Y$ and $\bar K NN \rightarrow YN$.

## Key numbers

- The best fit to kaonic atom data (65 data points) using the "F" potential gave $\chi^2 = 84.4$, with Re$B_0 = 1.42 \pm 0.05$ fm and Im$B_0 = 0.66 \pm 0.05$ fm, using $R_{x0} = 1.30 \pm 0.05$ fm, $\delta_x = 0.8 \pm 0.3$ fm, and $a_x = 0.4$ fm.
- The extrapolated attractive potential at nuclear-matter density $\rho_0 = 0.16$ fm$^{-3}$ is about 170 MeV deep.
- The dynamical calculation for the $1s$ state in $^{12}$C gave $B_{K^-} = 172$ MeV, compared to $B_{K^-} = 132$ MeV from the static calculation.
- The lower limit on the width of $\bar K$ nuclear bound states is $\Gamma_{\bar K} = 50 \pm 10$ MeV for binding energies in the range $B_{\bar K} = 100 - 200$ MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier RMF calculations for light nuclei (Mares, Friedman, Gal 2005) to the full periodic table, and contrasts with "shallow" potentials from self-consistent coupled-channel approaches (Ramos and Oset 2000; Cieply, Friedman, Gal 2001) that give depths of 40-60 MeV and cannot produce deeply bound states. The density-dependent analysis tests the chiral-motivated form successful in pionic atoms (Friedman 2002; Friedman and Gal 2003) but finds it does not improve fits for kaonic atoms, though it is not ruled out at higher densities. The results provide a framework for interpreting the FINUDA experiment at DA$\Phi$NE, which claimed evidence for deeply bound $K^- pp$ states in light nuclei.
