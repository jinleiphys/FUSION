# Chemical and mechanical instability in warm and dense nuclear matter

> We investigate the possible thermodynamic instability in a warm and dense nuclear medium (T<50 MeV and ρ_0<ρ_B< 3ρ_0) where a phase transition from nucleonic matter to resonance-dominated Delta-matter can take place. The analysis is performed by requiring the global conservation of baryon and electric charge numbers in the framework of a relativistic equation of state. Similarly to the liquid-gas phase transition, we show that the nucleon-Delta matter phase transition is characterized by both mechanical instability (fluctuations on the baryon density) and by chemical-diffusive instability (fluctuations on the charge concentration) in asymmetric nuclear matter. We then perform an investigation and a comparative study on the different nature of such instabilities and phase transitions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a phase transition from nucleonic matter to resonance-dominated Delta-matter can occur in warm and dense nuclear matter (T ≤ 50 MeV, ρ₀ ≤ ρ_B ≤ 3ρ₀), and that this transition is characterized by both mechanical instability (baryon density fluctuations) and chemical-diffusive instability (charge concentration fluctuations) in asymmetric nuclear matter, similarly to the liquid-gas phase transition. The existence of this transition depends sensitively on the meson-Delta coupling constants, particularly the ratio x_{σΔ} = g_{σΔ}/g_{σN}.

## Method

The authors use a relativistic mean-field (RMF) model with the TM1 parameter set, incorporating nucleons, Delta-isobars (Δ⁺⁺, Δ⁺, Δ⁰, Δ⁻), and pions as degrees of freedom. They enforce global conservation of baryon number and electric charge, requiring two independent chemical potentials (μ_B and μ_C), and apply Gibbs conditions for phase coexistence. The stability of the system is analyzed by examining mechanical stability (∂P/∂ρ_B > 0) and chemical stability conditions on the chemical potential derivatives, with the binodal surfaces constructed from isobaric chemical potential curves.

## Key numbers

- Temperature range explored: T ≤ 50 MeV; baryon density range: ρ₀ ≤ ρ_B ≤ 3ρ₀ (where ρ₀ is saturation nuclear density)
- For the TM1 parameter set, the maximum scalar coupling ratio for Delta-isobars is x_{σΔ}^{max} = 1.33; the value x_{σΔ}^{II} = 1.27 corresponds to the appearance of a second minimum in the energy per baryon (metastable state)
- At T = 50 MeV and x_{σΔ} = 1.3, the system becomes mechanically stable but chemically unstable for electric charge fraction y < 0.3
- The binodal section at T = 50 MeV and x_{σΔ} = 1.3 extends to negative electric charge fraction, ending at a point of maximum asymmetry with y = -1 (corresponding to a system dominated by Δ⁻ particles)
- For the liquid-gas phase transition at T = 10 MeV, mechanical instability is present for proton fraction y > 0.2, while chemical instability dominates for y < 0.2

## Context

This work extends the study of phase transitions in asymmetric nuclear matter from the well-known liquid-gas transition (studied by Müller and Serot (1995) using relativistic mean-field models) to higher temperatures and densities where Delta-resonance degrees of freedom become relevant. Previous investigations by Greiner and collaborators (1987, 1997) predicted a nucleon-to-Delta phase transition in symmetric nuclear matter but considered only one conserved charge (baryon number), finding only mechanical instabilities. The present work introduces the two conserved charge formalism (baryon and electric charge) to the Delta-matter transition, enabling the study of chemical-diffusive instabilities that can produce very different electric charge fractions in the coexisting phases. This is relevant for interpreting heavy-ion collision experiments (such as those planned at the CBM experiment at FAIR) and for understanding the equation of state of compact stars.
