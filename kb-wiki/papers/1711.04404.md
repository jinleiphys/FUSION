# Meson spectral function and screening masses in magnetized quark gluon plasma

> We calculate the spectral function in pseudoscalar (scalar) channel in the high temperature phase of QCD in presence of a background magnetic field. Spatial and temporal screening masses are determined from the long distance behavior of the corresponding correlation functions.

## Key claim

The paper derives analytic expressions for the pseudoscalar (scalar) channel spectral function in the deconfined and magnetized phase of QCD without any approximation regarding the kinematics or strength of the magnetic field, but notes that QCD corrections from strong gluon interactions will inevitably mix Landau levels and likely wash out the sawtooth pattern in the spectral functions.

## Method

The author calculates the one-loop self-energy in a background magnetic field using Landau level quantization, evaluating the imaginary part of the self-energy via the discontinuity of the loop integrals. Spatial and temporal screening masses are extracted from the long-distance behavior of the corresponding correlation functions. The evaluation of the transverse momentum integrals (T integrals) is performed using Laguerre-Fourier expansions and Bateman's addition formula, with the angular integration handled via Sommerfeld's representation of Bessel functions.

## Key numbers

- The transverse mass in the r-th Landau level is given by ε_r² = m² + 2r|q_f B|, where |q_f B| is the absolute value of the product of quark charge and magnetic field strength.
- The T integrals are expressed as T_{m,n}^{α,β,γ} = (-1)^{m+n} (|q_f B|/(16π²)) (|q_f B|/2)^γ e^{-ξ/2} χ_{m,n}^{α,β,γ}, where ξ = 2p_⊥²/|q_f B|.
- For ξ = 0, the normalization integral yields χ_{m,n}^{α,α,α} = 2π Γ(α+n+1)/Γ(n+1) δ_{m,n}.
- The analytic expression for χ⁰_{m,n} is given by χ⁰_{m,n} = (-1)^m (2π e^{ξ/4}/Γ(m+1)) U(-m, -m+n+1, ξ/4) L_n^{m-n}(ξ/4), where U(a,b,z) is Tricomi's confluent hypergeometric function.

## Context

This work extends calculations of meson spectral functions in hot QCD to include a background magnetic field, which is relevant for heavy ion collision phenomenology where strong transient magnetic fields are produced. The author notes that a recent lattice study by Bruckmann (2017) found that the lowest Landau level can be defined even for strongly interacting quarks, and that work by Hattori (2015) argued that quark self-energy in the lowest Landau level does not receive p_⊥-dependent radiative corrections. The results enable future analysis of observables in general, inhomogeneous, and time-dependent electromagnetic fields.
