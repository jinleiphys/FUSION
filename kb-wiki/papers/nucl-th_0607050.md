# Isospin dependent kaon and antikaon optical potentials in dense hadronic matter

> Isospin effects on the optical potentials of kaons and antikaons in dense hadronic matter are investigated using a chiral SU(3) model. These effects are important for asymmetric heavy ion collision experiments. In the present work the dispersion relations are derived for kaons and antikaons, compatible with the low energy scattering data, within our model approach. The relations result from the kaonic interactions with the nucleons, vector mesons and scalar mesons in the asymmetric nuclear matter. The isospin asymmetry effects arising from the interactions with the vector-isovector $ρ$- meson as well as the scalar isovector $δ$ mesons are considered. The density dependence of the isospin asymmetry is seen to be appreciable for the kaon and antikaon optical potentials. This can be particularly relevant for the future accelerator facility FAIR at GSI, where experiments using neutron rich beams are planned to be used in the study of compressed baryonic matter.

## Key claim

The isospin asymmetry of the nuclear medium produces appreciable differences in the optical potentials of kaons and antikaons, with the vector-isovector rho meson contribution dominating over the scalar-isovector delta meson contribution; however, the actual magnitude and sign of the effect depend on the specific kaon or antikaon species, the baryon density, and the momentum.

## Method

The authors used a chiral SU(3) model in the mean-field approximation to describe dense hadronic matter, deriving dispersion relations for kaons and antikaons from interactions with nucleons, scalar mesons (sigma, zeta, delta), and vector mesons (omega, rho). The self-energies were computed including the Weinberg-Tomozawa term, scalar exchange from explicit chiral symmetry breaking, vector meson exchange, and a range term, with the parameter d1 fixed to reproduce the empirical isospin-averaged KN scattering length. The optical potentials were then obtained as the difference between the in-medium energy and the free-space energy at a given momentum.

## Key numbers

* Symmetry energy a4 = 28.4 MeV at saturation nuclear matter density rho0 = 0.15 fm^-3.
* At rho_B = 0.16 fm^-3 and zero momentum, the K+ energy drops by about 15 MeV when the isospin asymmetry parameter t changes from 0 to 1.
* At rho_B = 0.16 fm^-3 and zero momentum, the K0 energy increases by a similar amount (about 15 MeV) for t = 1 compared to the isospin symmetric case t = 0.
* The isospin-averaged KN scattering length used to fix the parameter d1 is approximately -0.255 fm.
* The vacuum kaon mass m_K = 498 MeV and pion mass m_pi = 139 MeV are used in the explicit symmetry breaking term.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of kaon and antikaon in-medium properties within the chiral SU(3) model by Mishra and Schramm (2006) to include isospin asymmetry effects from both the rho meson and delta meson, which were not considered in earlier symmetric matter calculations. The results are relevant for interpreting future heavy-ion collision experiments at FAIR (GSI) and the Rare Isotope Accelerator (RIA), where neutron-rich beams will probe compressed baryonic matter, and can be incorporated into transport simulations that include mean fields for meson propagation.
