# Viscous Hydrodynamic Evolution with Non-Boost Invariant Flow for the Color Glass Condensate

> The Large Hadron Collider (LHC) experiments have revealed that the predictions of the color glass condensate (CGC) tend to underestimate the multiplicity at mid-rapidity. We develop and estimate a full second-order viscous hydrodynamic model for the longitudinal expansion to find that the CGC rapidity distributions are visibly deformed during the hydrodynamic stage due to the interplay between the entropy production and the entropy flux to forward rapidity. The results indicate the importance of viscous hydrodynamic evolution with non-boost invariant flow for understanding the CGC in terms of the heavy ion collisions.

# Viscous Hydrodynamic Evolution with Non-Boost Invariant Flow for the Color Glass Condensate

## Key claim
The CGC rapidity distributions are visibly deformed during the hydrodynamic stage due to the interplay between entropy production from non-equilibrium processes and entropy flux to forward rapidity caused by non-boost invariance, suggesting that readjustment of CGC parameters (specifically the parameter λ) may be necessary to explain the gap between CGC predictions and LHC multiplicity data.

## Method
The authors developed a full second-order extended Israel-Stewart viscous hydrodynamic model for (1+1)-dimensional longitudinal expansion, solving the constitutive equations for bulk pressure and shear stress tensor with both shear and bulk viscosity. Initial energy distributions were calculated from the Monte-Carlo version of the Kharzeev-Levin-Nardi (MC-KLN) model, with the saturation scale parameter λ fixed at 0.28, and the initial time set to τ₀ = 1 fm/c. The equation of state was taken from lattice QCD calculations, shear viscosity was set to η = s/4π, and bulk viscosity to ζ = (5/2)(1/3 − cₛ²)η.

## Key numbers
- Shear viscosity set to η = s/4π (the conjectured lower bound)
- Bulk viscosity set to ζ = (5/2)(1/3 − cₛ²)η, where cₛ is the sound velocity
- Initial time τ₀ = 1 fm/c
- Saturation scale parameter λ = 0.28
- Saturation scale formula: Qₛ,ₐ²(x; x⊥) = 2 GeV² × [Tₐ(x⊥)/1.53 fm⁻²] × (0.01/x)^λ
- RHIC case: Au-Au collisions at √s_NN = 200 GeV with final time τ_f = 30 fm/c
- LHC case: Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN = 2.76 TeV with final time τ_f = 50 fm/c
- 5% most central events used for smooth initial conditions
- Final times chosen so temperatures at mid-rapidity are near the QCD pseudo-critical temperature; distributions do not change much after τ ∼ 20 fm/c

## Context
This work extends prior CGC modeling by Kharzeev, Levin, and Nardi (2001, 2002) and the MC-KLN model by Drescher (2006, 2007), which successfully reproduced RHIC rapidity distributions but underestimated LHC mid-rapidity multiplicity as reported by Aamodt (2010). The authors note that previous (2+1)-dimensional viscous hydrodynamic models used boost-invariant flow, whereas this study incorporates non-boost invariant longitudinal expansion for the first time. The results enable future parameter readjustment of CGC models and indicate that non-boost invariant viscous hydrodynamics is indispensable for an integrated picture of heavy ion collisions.
