# Improving target neutron momentum reconstruction using MINER$ν$A $π^0$ data

> With the neutrino experiments advancing toward high-precision measurements and greater emphasis on reducing systematic uncertainties, improving the single-pion production models, a major component of the hadronic activity observed in the neutrino oscillation experiments, into the Monte Carlo simulations is crucial. This work presents the predictions of the struck nucleon's Fermi motion by analyzing the charged-current neutral pion production on carbon nucleus in MINER$ν$A. A minimal variation of GENIE and NuWro based on their default models shows an improvement in the prediction of single $π^0$ production. The prediction describes the data more accurately in the higher-momentum tail; however, discrepancies between the predictions and data below the Fermi peak highlight the limitations in current nuclear models used in the Monte Carlo generators.

# Improving target neutron momentum reconstruction using MINERνA π⁰ data

## Key claim
The authors show that minimal variations of the GENIE and NuWro Monte Carlo generators, specifically adjusting the short-range correlation fraction and resonance axial mass in GENIE, and disabling the formation zone effect for primary particles in NuWro, improve predictions of single π⁰ production in charged-current neutrino-carbon interactions, but discrepancies remain below the Fermi peak, indicating limitations in current nuclear models.

## Method
The authors simulated charged-current νμ interactions on carbon using GENIE v3.01.00 (with G18_10a_02_11a tune) and NuWro v21.09.2, applying the MINERνA low-energy flux peaking around 3 GeV. They reconstructed the initial neutron momentum pN using transverse kinematic imbalance (TKI) from events with final states of 1μ⁻1p1π⁰ plus any number of neutrons, applying detector thresholds of 1.5 ≤ pμ ≤ 20.0 GeV/c, θμ < 25°, and pp ≥ 0.45 GeV/c. Predictions were compared to deconvoluted cross section data from MINERνA (2020).

## Key numbers
- Fermi peak in data occurs at approximately 200 MeV/c for both GENIE spectral function and NuWro local Fermi gas and spectral function models.
- χ² per bin for GENIE default spectral function: 5.37/12; for GENIE default local Fermi gas: 19.89/12.
- χ² per bin for NuWro default local Fermi gas: 38.16/12; for NuWro default spectral function: 44.4/12.
- After tuning, GENIE χ² per bin reduced to 19.57/12 with SRC fraction set to 0.15 and RES axial mass set to 1.088962 GeV.
- After tuning, NuWro χ² per bin reduced to 8.24/12 with formation zone effect disabled for primary particles.
- Excitation energy b for carbon taken as 28.7 MeV.
- Momentum cutoff for local Fermi gas in GENIE: 0.7 GeV; removal energy for carbon: 0.025 GeV.
- RES axial mass default: 1.065 GeV; RES-DIS threshold: 1.8 GeV.

## Context
This work extends previous studies by MINERνA (2020) that showed discrepancies in NuWro and GiBUU predictions of p-π⁰ cross sections using Fermi gas and spectral function models, despite success in quasielastic-like results (MINERνA 2018, T2K 2018). It also builds on recent tuning efforts in NuWro (Yan 2024) and GENIE (GENIE 2024) that incorporated hybrid models. The improved predictions could benefit future MINERνA measurements with higher statistics and cross section measurements involving pion production in accelerator experiments like DUNE and atmospheric experiments like JUNO, Hyper-Kamiokande, and Super-Kamiokande.
