# Energy-momentum tensor form factors of the nucleon within a $π$-$ρ$-$ω$ soliton model

> We investigate the energy-momentum tensor form factors of the nucleon within the famework of a chiral soliton model, including the $ρ$ and $ω$ vector mesons. We examine the role of each meson degrees of freedom in these form factors. It is explicitly shown that the pion provides strong attraction whereas the $ρ$ and $ω$ yield repulsion in such a way that the soliton becomes stabilized. The results are discussed in comparison with those of other models.

# Energy-momentum tensor form factors of the nucleon within a π-ρ-ω soliton model

## Key claim
The pion provides strong attraction while the ρ and ω mesons yield repulsion in such a way that the soliton becomes stabilized, analogous to the one-boson exchange picture of the nucleon-nucleon interaction. The model satisfies the stability condition that the integral of the pressure density over volume is zero, and the results are generally more shifted to the inner part of the nucleon compared to the Skyrme model and the chiral quark-soliton model.

## Method
The authors construct a chiral soliton model with π, ρ, and ω meson degrees of freedom based on a hidden local gauge symmetry SU(2)_V ⊗ U(1), where the ρ meson is identified as a gauge boson and the ω meson is also treated as a gauge boson. The nucleon arises as a topological soliton solution, and the energy-momentum tensor form factors M₂(t), J(t), and d₁(t) are computed in the large N_c limit from the densities of energy, angular momentum, and pressure, using spherical Bessel transforms. The model parameters are fixed entirely in the mesonic sector using experimental values for f_π = 93 MeV, m_π = 135 MeV, and the KSRF relation with g = 5.85.

## Key numbers
- Hedgehog mass M_sol ≃ 1473 MeV; nucleon mass M_N ≃ 1562 MeV
- Mean square radius for energy density ⟨r_00²⟩ = 0.78 fm²
- Mean square radius for angular momentum distribution ⟨r_J²⟩ = 0.74 fm²
- Position where pressure changes sign r_0 = 0.55 fm
- D-term at zero momentum transfer d₁(0) = -5.03
- Pressure at origin p₀(0) = 0.58 GeV/fm³
- Energy density at origin T₀₀(0) = 3.56 GeV/fm³
- p-pole mass for mass form factor M_{M₂} = 0.724 GeV with p = 2.17
- p-pole mass for angular momentum form factor M_J = 0.786 GeV with p ≈ 1
- p-pole mass for D-term form factor M_{d₁} = 0.510 GeV with p = 1.57

## Context
This work extends studies of nucleon energy-momentum tensor form factors from the Skyrme model (Cebulla 2007) and the chiral quark-soliton model (Goeke 2007) to a model with explicit vector meson degrees of freedom. The results are compared with those from lattice QCD (Mathur 1999, Hagler 2003, Gockeler 2003), chiral perturbation theory (Chen 2001, Belitsky 2002), and the χQSM (Petrov 1998, Schweitzer 2002). The approach enables future study of medium-modified nucleon properties in nuclear matter by connecting changes in vector meson properties to medium modifications of the nucleon, as well as investigation of quantum corrections to the form factors.
