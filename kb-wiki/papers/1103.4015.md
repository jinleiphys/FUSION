# Lattice QCD Study for Confinement in Hadrons

> We study three subjects on quark confinement in hadrons in SU(3)$_{\rm c}$ lattice QCD. From the accurate lattice calculation for more than 300 different patterns of three-quark (3Q) systems, we find that the static 3Q potential is well described by Y-Ansatz, i.e., the Coulomb plus Y-type linear potential. We also study the multi-quark (4Q, 5Q) potentials in lattice QCD, and find that they are well described by the one-gluon-exchange (OGE) Coulomb plus string-theoretical linear potential, which supports the {\it infrared string picture} even for the multi-quarks. The second subject is a lattice-QCD determination of the relevant gluonic momentum component for confinement. The string tension (confining force) is found to be almost unchanged even after cutting off the high-momentum gluon component above 1.5GeV in the Landau gauge. In fact, {\it quark confinement originates from the low-momentum gluon below about 1.5GeV.} Finally, we consider a possible gauge of QCD for the quark potential model, by investigating "instantaneous inter-quark potential" in generalized Landau gauge, which describes a continuous change from the Landau gauge to the Coulomb gauge.

## Key claim

The static three-quark (3Q) potential is well described by the Y-Ansatz (Coulomb plus Y-type linear potential) based on accurate lattice calculations for more than 300 different 3Q patterns, and the string tension (confining force) is found to be almost unchanged even after cutting off high-momentum gluon components above 1.5 GeV in the Landau gauge, indicating that quark confinement originates from low-momentum gluons below about 1.5 GeV.

## Method

The authors performed SU(3) quenched lattice QCD Monte Carlo simulations on multiple lattices (beta = 5.7, 5.8, 6.0, 6.2) to compute static 3Q, 4Q, and 5Q potentials using Wilson loop techniques. To identify the relevant gluon momentum component for confinement, they applied a four-dimensional discrete Fourier transformation to gauge configurations in the Landau gauge, introduced momentum-space cuts (UV or IR), and then inverse-Fourier-transformed back to coordinate space to compute the Q anti-Q potential with the modified link variables. They also investigated the "instantaneous inter-quark potential" in a generalized Landau gauge (lambda-gauge) that continuously interpolates between the Landau gauge (lambda=1) and the Coulomb gauge (lambda=0).

## Key numbers

- The string tension (confining force) is almost unchanged after cutting off high-momentum gluon components above 1.5 GeV, but is significantly reduced when the UV cutoff is smaller than about 1.5 GeV.
- The gluonic-excitation energy for baryons (Delta E_3Q) is about 1 GeV, as measured from the difference between the first excited-state and ground-state 3Q potentials at beta=5.8.
- The string tension for multi-quark systems (n=3,4,5) shows universality: sigma_nQ is approximately equal to sigma_Q anti-Q, and the OGE coefficient A_nQ is approximately A_Q anti-Q / 2.
- The instantaneous potential in the lambda_C-gauge with lambda_C approximately 0.02 approximately reproduces the physical static inter-quark potential.
- Lattice calculations were performed on 16^4 lattices at beta=5.7, 5.8, and 6.0, with lattice spacing a approximately 0.10 fm and momentum-space lattice spacing a_p approximately 0.77 GeV at beta=6.0.

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier lattice QCD studies of the 3Q potential (TS0102, OST05, TS0304) and multi-quark potentials (OST05) to include a quantitative determination of the gluon momentum scale responsible for confinement, building on the method of Yamamoto and Suganuma (YS0809). The finding that confinement originates from gluon momenta below about 1.5 GeV provides a direct QCD-based energy scale for the infrared string picture, and the investigation of the generalized Landau gauge (IS11) attempts to bridge lattice QCD with the quark potential model, which has been successful in describing low-lying hadrons due to the large gluonic-excitation energy of about 1 GeV (JKM03, TS0304). This work enables future studies to construct multi-quark Hamiltonians based on lattice QCD results and to better understand the gauge dependence of the instantaneous inter-quark potential.
