# Pion, kaon, and proton femtoscopy in Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}$=2.76 TeV modeled in 3+1D hydrodynamics

> Femtoscopy is providing information on system size and its dynamics in heavy-ion collisions. At ultra-relativistic energies, such as those obtained at the LHC, significant production of pions, kaons and protons enables femtoscopic measurements for these particles. In particular the dependence of system size on pair momentum and particle type is interpreted as evidence for strong collective flow. Such phenomena are naturally modeled by hydrodynamics. We present calculations within the 3+1D hydrodynamic model coupled to statistical hadronization code THERMINATOR 2, corresponding to Pb--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=$2.76 TeV. We obtain femtoscopic radii for pions, kaons, and protons, as a function of pair transverse momentum and collision centrality. We find that an approximate universal scaling of radii with pair transverse mass and final state event multiplicity is observed, and discuss the consequences for the interpretation of experimental measurements.

## Key claim

The 3+1D hydrodynamic model coupled to THERMINATOR 2 predicts an approximate universal scaling of three-dimensional femtoscopic radii with pair transverse mass and final-state event multiplicity for pions, kaons, and protons, but this scaling is realized only at the 5% level (with some outliers reaching 10-20%), not as an exact relation.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1D viscous hydrodynamic calculations with smooth Glauber initial conditions, using an equation of state interpolating between lattice QCD and hadron gas, with shear viscosity η/s=0.08 and bulk viscosity ζ/s=0.04. The resulting freeze-out hypersurfaces at T_f=140 MeV were fed into the THERMINATOR 2 statistical hadronization code, which generates particles via the Cooper-Frye formula including nonequilibrium corrections from shear and bulk viscosity, followed by resonance propagation and decay but without hadronic rescattering. Femtoscopic correlation functions were computed from the simulated particle emission points and momenta using only quantum statistics (no final-state interactions), and fitted with Gaussian source functions to extract R_out, R_side, and R_long in the Longitudinally Co-Moving System (LCMS) and R_inv in the Pair Rest Frame (PRF).

## Key numbers

- Initial time for hydrodynamic evolution: 0.6 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature: 140 MeV
- Shear viscosity η/s = 0.08, bulk viscosity ζ/s = 0.04
- Impact parameters (in fm) for seven centrality ranges: 3.1, 5.7, 7.4, 8.7, 9.9, 10.9, and 11.9, corresponding to 0-10% through 60-70% centrality
- Pion transverse size reaches 7 fm at lowest m_T and largest multiplicity; longitudinal size reaches over 11 fm in the same range
- Lowest radii observed: approximately 2 fm for high m_T at largest centrality
- Power-law exponent β for pions: approximately -0.45 for R_out and R_side, -0.75 for R_long
- Global scaling parameters for all three particle types (R_out): α=2.11, β=-0.40, a=0.32, d=0.128
- Global scaling parameters (R_side): α=2.04, β=-0.43, a=0.42, d=0.117
- Global scaling parameters (R_long): α=2.12, β=-0.68, a=0.28, d=0.133
- Average absolute deviation of combined pion, kaon, and proton radii from common power-law fit: 2-6% depending on direction and centrality
- R_inv values determined from LCMS radii via Lorentz boost agree with direct PRF fits to within 3%

## Context

This work extends hydrodynamic femtoscopy calculations from RHIC energies (Adams 2005, Adcox 2004) to LHC Pb-Pb collisions at √s_NN=2.76 TeV, providing predictions for pion, kaon, and proton femtoscopic radii. The results contradict a recent calculation by Shapoval (2014) using the HKM model, which predicted that kaon radii would be higher than the pion trend; the present study suggests that such scaling breaking arises from hadronic rescattering rather than from the hydrodynamic phase itself. The work enables experimental tests of collectivity via three-dimensional radii in LCMS rather than one-dimensional R_inv, and provides a calibration for searching for the signature of hadronic rescattering at LHC energies.
