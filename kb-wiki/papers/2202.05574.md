# Resummed lattice QCD equation of state at finite baryon density: strangeness neutrality and beyond

> We calculate a resummed equation of state with lattice QCD simulations at imaginary chemical potentials. This work presents a generalization of the scheme introduced in 2102.06660 to the case of non-zero $μ_S$, focusing on the line of strangeness neutrality. We present results up to $μ_B/T \leq 3.5$ on the strangeness neutral line $\left\langle S \right\rangle = 0$ in the temperature range $130 \rm{MeV} \leq T \leq 280 \rm{MeV}$. We also extrapolate the finite baryon density equation of state to small non-zero values of the strangeness-to-baryon ratio $R=\left\langle S \right\rangle / \left\langle B \right\rangle$. We perform a continuum extrapolation using lattice simulations of the 4stout-improved staggered action with 8, 10, 12 and 16 timeslices.

## Key claim

The paper generalizes a previously introduced resummation scheme (Borsanyi 2021) for the QCD equation of state from the case of zero strangeness chemical potential to the strangeness neutral line, achieving results up to \(\mu_B/T \leq 3.5\) while noting that the approximate scaling variable used for extrapolation works well in the crossover region but requires a Stefan-Boltzmann correction to remain valid at high temperatures.

## Method

The authors perform lattice QCD simulations with \(N_f=2+1+1\) dynamical quark flavors using the 4stout-improved staggered action on lattices with temporal extents \(N_\tau = 8, 10, 12, 16\) for continuum extrapolation. They simulate at imaginary baryochemical potentials (values of \(\operatorname{Im} \hat{\mu}_B \frac{8}{\pi} = 0,3,4,5,6,6.5\)) while enforcing strangeness neutrality, then use a resummation ansatz that shifts the temperature by a factor \(T(1 + \lambda_2(T) \hat{\mu}_B^2 + \lambda_4(T) \hat{\mu}_B^4 + \dots)\) applied to Stefan-Boltzmann-corrected observables to extrapolate to real chemical potentials.

## Key numbers

* Baryon density, pressure, entropy density, energy density, strangeness chemical potential ratio, and strangeness susceptibility are presented for \(\mu_B/T \leq 3.5\) on the strangeness neutral line in the temperature range \(130\ \mathrm{MeV} \leq T \leq 280\ \mathrm{MeV}\).
* The Stefan-Boltzmann limit for \(c^B_2(0)\) is \(\frac{2}{9}\) and for \(c^B_4(0)\) is \(\frac{4}{27\pi^2}\).
* Continuum extrapolation uses lattices with \(N_\tau = 8, 10, 12, 16\) for most quantities, and \(N_\tau = 12, 16, 20\) for \(\chi^S_2\).
* The \(\lambda_2^{BB}\) coefficient is approximately constant in the crossover region and goes to zero within error bars at high temperatures; \(\lambda_2^{SS}\) remains non-zero at high temperatures due to the larger strange quark mass.

## Context

This work extends the resummation scheme introduced by Borsanyi (2021) for \(\mu_S = 0\) to the phenomenologically relevant strangeness neutral line, where previous results were limited to fixed-order Taylor expansions that exhibit unphysical oscillations above \(\mu_B/T \approx 2-2.5\). The resulting equation of state can be used in relativistic hydrodynamic simulations of heavy ion collisions that enforce local strangeness neutrality, and the extension to small non-zero strangeness-to-baryon ratio \(R = \langle S \rangle / \langle B \rangle\) allows relaxation of this condition to account for local charge fluctuations.
