# The kinetic energy and and the geometric structure in the $B=2$ sector of the Skyrme model: A study using the Atiyah-Manton Ansatz

> We study the construction of the collective-coordinate manifold in the baryon number two sector of the Skyrme model. To that end we use techniques of adiabatic large amplitude collective motion, which treat potential and kinetic energy on an equal footing. In this paper the starting point is the Ansatz proposed by Atiyah and Manton (Phys.~Lett.~{\bf 438B}, 222 (1989)), which allows a study of the dynamics using a finite and small number of variables. From these variables we choose a subset of collective ones. We then study the behavior of inertial parameters along parts of the collective manifold, and study the dynamical parts of the interaction.

## Key claim

The Atiyah-Manton Ansatz, combined with adiabatic large amplitude collective motion (ALACM), can be used to study the geometric structure and kinetic energy of the baryon number two sector of the Skyrme model, but only for short and intermediate distances; the long-distance behavior is better described by the product approximation.

## Method

The author uses the Atiyah-Manton Ansatz, which derives Skyrme fields from instanton fields via holonomy, to parametrize the B=2 configuration space with 18 parameters. From these, a subset of collective coordinates is selected using the local harmonic approximation (LHA) of adiabatic large amplitude collective motion, which solves coupled equations for the force and the covariant second derivative of the potential to identify approximately decoupled collective modes. The inertial parameters (mass tensor) are computed along the collective manifold, and the decoupling quality is assessed by comparing the metric-based and tangent-based definitions of the collective mass.

## Key numbers

* The Skyrme unit of energy is 11.18 MeV and the Skyrme unit of length is 0.754 fm, for the standard values f_pi = 54.1 MeV and g = 4.84.
* The baryon number was found to be 2 to at least six significant digits in the numerical integration.
* The energy at constant separation in the attractive channel was studied for values of R (separation) of 1.2, 1.3, 1.4, 1.5, 1.7, 2.0, 2.5, and 3.0 Skyrme units.
* In the repulsive channel, the energy at constant separation was studied for R = 1.3 Skyrme units.

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of the B=2 Skyrme model that used the product approximation (which ignores deformation of the two Skyrmions) and the numerical grid calculations of Walhout and Wambach (1991, 1992), which imposed constraints on the fields. It also builds on the Atiyah-Manton Ansatz as used by Hosaka et al. (1991) for static potential calculations. The approach enables the study of the dynamical (velocity-dependent) parts of the nucleon-nucleon interaction, particularly the spin-orbit force, which previous calculations using the product Ansatz (Riska and Dannbom, 1988; Amado et al., 1993, 1994; Abada, 1994) found to have the wrong sign compared to phenomenology.
