# New Observables in Break-up Reactions of Three-body Halos

> Momentum distributions of particles from fast three-body halo fragmentation reactions with polarized beams and outgoing fragments are analyzed by use of the sudden approximation. The final state interaction between the two non-disturbed particles is considered. We first give a general and detailed description of the method. We introduce observable quantities that emphasize the two-body correlations in the initial three-body structure as well as in the final two-body system. Using neutron removal from 11Li as an example we investigate the dependence of the polarization observables on the properties of the low-lying resonances and virtual states of the 10Li subsystem. These observables are very sensitive to the l > 0-waves in the wave functions, and they provide detailed information on the resonance structure of the neutron-core subsystem.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that polarization observables (asymmetries) in fast three-body halo fragmentation reactions are highly sensitive to non-zero orbital angular momentum components (ℓ > 0) in the wave function and to the resonance structure of the neutron-core subsystem, but only when the beam is polarized and/or final-state spin projections are measured, unpolarized momentum distributions alone cannot distinguish these features.

## Method

The authors use the sudden approximation to describe neutron removal from a polarized ¹¹Li projectile, where one neutron is instantaneously captured by the target without disturbing the remaining neutron-⁹Li system. The final-state interaction between the two undisturbed particles (neutron and ⁹Li core) is included by replacing the plane wave with distorted two-body wave functions obtained by solving the Schrödinger equation with appropriate Gaussian potentials. The transition matrix element is computed as the overlap of the initial three-body wave function (expanded in hyperspherical harmonics) and the final-state wave function, and the resulting momentum distributions are integrated to define polarization asymmetries A₁ and A₂ for longitudinal and transverse momentum components.

## Key numbers

- The ¹¹Li projectile has a binding energy of 295 ± 35 keV and a root mean square radius of 3.1 ± 0.3 fm.
- The neutron-⁹Li subsystem is modeled with a p-resonance at 0.5 MeV and a low-lying virtual s-state or p-resonance at energies of 50 keV, 100 keV, or 150 keV.
- The radial wave function f₁(ρ) contains more than 95% of the norm of the total three-body wave function.
- The p-resonance at 0.5 MeV corresponds to a relative neutron-core momentum p_r ≈ 30 MeV/c; low-lying resonances at 50, 100, and 150 keV correspond to p_r values of 10, 14, and 17 MeV/c, respectively.
- For the neutron-core subsystem, more than 98% of the probability in the neutron-neutron subsystem is found in s-waves.

## Context

This work extends the sudden-approximation framework for halo fragmentation reactions previously developed by the same group (Garrido et al., 1996) and others (Zhukov et al., 1994; Korsheninnikov et al., 1994; Thompson et al., 1994) by introducing polarization observables as a new tool. The method is applied to ¹¹Li, building on three-body structure calculations by Fedorov et al. (1994) and Garrido et al. (1995). The results enable experimental discrimination between low-lying virtual s-states and p-resonances in the ¹⁰Li subsystem, which is not possible with unpolarized momentum distributions alone, and can guide future experiments with polarized radioactive beams.
