# Charged scalar bosons under rotation and acceleration

> Creation of charged spinless bosons from vacuum in the rigidly rotating frame is studied in presence of the external static electromagnetic fields, being formed either in the resting, or in the general rotating or in the local-flat frames. It is shown that the description remains the same in the resting and the local-flat frames. More specifically, the case of a solenoid (magnetic flux tube) embedded into the rotating empty cylinder (rotation frame) is studied in presence or absence of a static square electric potential well $eA_0$. A supervortex of a spinless-boson field can be created from vacuum when the rotation frequency exceeds a critical value $Ω_c$. It is shown that $Ω_c$ is smaller provided the solenoid rests in the resting frame. Then the case is considered when the rotating frame additionally moves with acceleration $\vec{w}(\vec{r})\neq 0$. A specific case $w(r)=G/r$ for $G=const$ is treated explicitly. It is shown that for $G<0$ the charged spinless-boson vortex can be created in rapidly rotating system even in the limit when the magnetic and electric fields tend to zero.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the description of charged spinless bosons in a rigidly rotating frame remains the same whether formulated in the resting frame or the local-flat frame, and that a supervortex can be created from vacuum when rotation frequency exceeds a critical value \(\Omega_c\), which is smaller when the solenoid rests in the resting frame rather than the rotating frame.

## Method

The authors study the nonlinear Klein-Gordon equation for charged spinless bosons in a rigidly rotating cylindrical coordinate system, with external static electromagnetic fields from a solenoid (magnetic flux tube) embedded in a rotating empty cylinder. They consider two cases: (i) the solenoid rests in the resting frame, and (ii) the solenoid rests in the rotation frame, using transformation matrices between frames and tetrads for the local-flat frame. For the case with acceleration, they introduce a specific acceleration profile \(w(r)=G/r\) and solve the modified Klein-Gordon equation with the metric from Nelson (1987).

## Key numbers

- Rotation frequency in peripheral heavy-ion collisions: \(\Omega \simeq (9\pm 1) \cdot 10^{21}\) Hz \(\simeq 0.05 m_\pi\), where \(m_\pi \simeq 140\) MeV is the pion mass, for Au + Au at \(\sqrt{s} = 200\) GeV, corresponding to angular momenta \(l \lesssim 10^6\)
- Magnetic fields expected in heavy-ion collisions and compact stars: \(H \lesssim 10^{18}\) G
- Critical rotation frequency for thin flux tube with \(l = \delta_\Phi\): \(\Omega_c \simeq (m - V_0)/\delta_\Phi\) for \(mR \gg 1\)
- For \(l \gg \delta_\Phi \gg mR \gg 1\) and \(\Omega = (1-\delta)/R\) with \(\delta \ll 1\): instability occurs for \(0 < \delta < (\delta_\Phi - c l^{1/3})/l\) where \(c \simeq 1.856\)
- With acceleration \(w(r)=G/r\) and \(l \gg mR \gg 1\): \(\Omega_c \approx (1+G)/R\)
- Typical fireball size at compression stage: \(R_0 \sim 5 m_\pi^{-1}\)
- Freeze-out radius: \(R_{\text{f.o.}} \lesssim 10 m_\pi^{-1}\) fm
- Expansion velocity: \(v_r < 0.5 c\) on expansion stage, with deceleration \(-G \lesssim 0.5\)

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Zahed, Guo, Voskresensky (2023-2024), and Bordag and Voskresensky (2025) on charged pion vacuum instability under rotation with external magnetic fields, and by Nelson (1987) on accelerated reference frames. The paper resolves ambiguities in the literature regarding the equivalence of descriptions in resting and local-flat frames, and demonstrates that even weak deceleration (\(G<0\)) of a rapidly rotating cylinder can trigger spinless-boson vortex creation even without external electromagnetic fields. The results suggest that in heavy-ion collisions, the combination of rapid rotation, strong magnetic fields, and deceleration at the compression stage may stimulate formation of a charged pion vortex field.
