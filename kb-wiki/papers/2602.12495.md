# Dark matter effects on the properties of hybrid neutron stars

> We study the effects of dark matter on the properties of hybrid neutron stars, in particular the influence on the mass-radius relation, the value of the maximum mass, and the hadron-quark phase transition. To single out the equilibrium configurations of dark-matter-admixed hybrid neutron stars (DHSs), we also study their radial oscillations. Both the stellar structure equations and the radial oscillation equations are solved for the two-fluid system, where the ordinary matter component and dark matter component couple only through gravity. For the ordinary matter components, we adopt the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock method for nuclear matter, and the Dyson-Schwinger or the field-correlator model for quark matter. For the dark matter component, we use a non-self-annihilating self-interacting fermionic model. We find that the presence of dark matter in DHSs leads to a decrease of the critical mass of the hadron-quark phase transition, a related possible onset of quark matter in dark-matter accreting stars, and a significant reduction of radial oscillation frequencies.

## Key claim

The presence of dark matter in hybrid neutron stars lowers the critical mass for the onset of the hadron-quark phase transition, potentially triggering quark deconfinement in stars that would otherwise remain purely hadronic, but this scenario requires very large dark matter fractions (f ~ O(1)) that demand exotic formation mechanisms beyond standard dark matter capture.

## Method

The authors solve two-fluid Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations and coupled radial oscillation equations for a system where ordinary matter and dark matter interact only gravitationally. For nuclear matter they use the Brueckner-Hartree-Fock (BHF) method with the Argonne V18 potential plus three-nucleon force; for quark matter they employ either the Dyson-Schwinger model (DSM) or the field-correlator model (FCM), joined to the hadronic phase via Gibbs construction. The dark matter is modeled as a non-self-annihilating self-interacting fermionic gas with particle mass μ = 1 GeV and a repulsive Yukawa interaction constrained by σ/μ = 1 cm²/g from galaxy cluster observations.

## Key numbers

- Maximum mass of pure nucleonic star (V18 EOS): M_max = 2.34 M⊙
- Maximum mass of hybrid stars (both DSM and FCM models): M_max = 2.1 M⊙
- Radius of pure nucleonic star at 1.4 M⊙: R_1.4 = 12.32 km
- Radius of pure nucleonic star at 2.0 M⊙: R_2.0 = 11.93 km
- Tidal deformability of pure nucleonic star at 1.4 M⊙: Λ_1.4 = 430
- Dark matter particle mass used: μ = 1 GeV
- Dark matter self-interaction parameter: y = 10.94 (for μ = 1 GeV)
- Maximum mass of pure dark star: M_max = 3.11 M⊙
- Onset of quark matter in V18+DS1 model: reduced from M_G = 1.45 M⊙ at f = 0 to about 1.31 M⊙ at f = 0.19
- Critical DM fraction for quark matter onset in a star with initial M_0 = 1.4 M⊙: f_pt = 0.02
- Critical DM fraction for quark matter onset in a star with initial M_0 = 1.2 M⊙: f_pt = 0.11

## Context

This work extends previous studies by Liu et al. (2023, 2024) and Zhou et al. (2025) on dark-matter-admixed neutron stars, now incorporating hybrid star configurations with a hadron-quark phase transition. The results show that dark matter accumulation could trigger quark deconfinement in neutron stars that would otherwise remain purely hadronic, potentially producing observable pulsar timing signatures (as discussed by Haensel et al., 2016; Wei et al., 2017; Prasad et al., 2022) and altering thermal evolution through the quark direct Urca process (Iwamoto, 1982; Yakovlev et al., 2001). The study also enables future work on distinguishing dark matter effects from nuclear equation of state uncertainties through radial oscillation frequency measurements.
