# Effective interaction: From nuclear reactions to neutron stars

> An equation of state (EoS) for symmetric nuclear matter is constructed using the density dependent M3Y effective interaction and extended for isospin asymmetric nuclear matter. Theoretically obtained values of symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility, isobaric incompressibility, symmetry energy and its slope agree well with experimentally extracted values. Folded microscopic potentials using this effective interaction, whose density dependence is determined from nuclear matter calculations, provide excellent descriptions for proton, alpha and cluster radioactivities, elastic and inelastic scattering. The nuclear deformation parameters extracted from inelastic scattering of protons agree well with other available results. The high density behavior of symmetric and asymmetric nuclear matter satisfies the constraints from the observed flow data of heavy-ion collisions. The neutron star properties studied using $β$-equilibrated neutron star matter obtained from this effective interaction reconcile with the recent observations of the massive compact stars.

# Effective interaction: From nuclear reactions to neutron stars

## Key claim

The density-dependent M3Y (DDM3Y) effective nucleon-nucleon interaction, with its density dependence determined entirely from nuclear matter saturation conditions, provides a unified description spanning nuclear reactions, radioactivities, and neutron star properties, but the neutron star masses obtained depend on whether a quark core is present, and the symmetry energy is "super-soft" (increasing only up to about twice normal density then decreasing and becoming negative at higher densities), which is consistent with FOPI/GSI experimental data on pion ratios.

## Method

The DDM3Y effective interaction was constructed using isoscalar and isovector components of the M3Y-Reid-Elliott interaction with a density dependence of the form g(ρ) = C(1 − βρ^n), where the constants C and β were determined by solving the saturation condition for symmetric nuclear matter at ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³ and ε₀ = −15.26 MeV. The equation of state was then extended to isospin asymmetric nuclear matter and used to generate folded microscopic potentials for elastic and inelastic scattering calculations (via DWBA with DWUCK4), proton/alpha/cluster radioactivity half-lives (via WKB barrier penetration), and β-equilibrated neutron star matter properties (solving Einstein's field equations for rotating axisymmetric configurations using the rns code).

## Key numbers

- Symmetric nuclear matter incompressibility K∞ = 274.7 ± 7.4 MeV
- Nuclear symmetry energy at saturation density Esym(ρ₀) = 30.71 ± 0.26 MeV (or 30.03 ± 0.26 MeV using the alternative definition)
- Slope parameter L = 45.11 ± 0.02 MeV
- Curvature parameter Ksym = −183.7 ± 3.6 MeV
- Isospin-dependent part of isobaric incompressibility Kτ = −408.97 ± 3.01 MeV
- Saturation density ρ₀ = 0.1533 fm⁻³ and saturation energy ε₀ = −15.26 MeV used as input
- Density dependence constants: C = 2.2497 ± 0.0420, β = 1.5934 ± 0.0085 fm²
- Maximum mass for static neutron star with pure nuclear matter: 1.92 M⊙ with radius ~9.7 km
- Maximum mass for neutron star rotating at Keplerian frequency (pure nuclear matter): 2.27 M⊙ with equatorial radius ~13.1 km
- Maximum mass for static hybrid star (with quark core, B¹/⁴ = 110 MeV): 1.68 M⊙ with radius ~10.4 km
- Maximum mass for hybrid star rotating at Keplerian frequency: 2.02 M⊙ with equatorial radius ~14.3 km
- Maximum mass for neutron star with r-mode instability limit at 1.5 ms period: 1.95 M⊙ with radius ~9.9 km
- Deformation parameter δ for ¹⁸O: 0.33 (from B(E2): 0.355(8))
- Deformation parameter δ for ²⁰O: 0.46 (from B(E2): 0.261(9) or 0.50(4))
- Deformation parameter δ for ²²O: 0.26 (from B(E2): 0.208(41))

## Context

This work extends the DDM3Y interaction framework previously applied to nuclear reactions and radioactivities (Basu 2003, Basu et al. 2008) to a comprehensive study of neutron star properties. The calculated incompressibility and symmetry energy are compared with relativistic mean-field models including NL3, FSUGold, and Hybrid (Piekarewicz 2009), as well as Skyrme interactions (SkM*, SLy4, SkI3) and the accepted range K∞ = 250–270 MeV (Shlomo et al. 2009) and Kτ = −370 ± 120 MeV (Chen et al. 2009). The "super-soft" symmetry energy behavior is consistent with evidence from FOPI/GSI data on π⁺/π⁻ ratios (Zhang et al. 2009) and enables neutron star masses up to ~2 M⊙ that reconcile with observations of massive compact stars (Demorest et al. 2010). The results enable future studies of the nuclear-to-quark matter deconfinement transition and its effect on neutron star structure.
