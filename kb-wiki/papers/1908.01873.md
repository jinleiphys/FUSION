# The union of rotational and vibrational modes in generator-coordinate-type calculations, with application to neutrinoless double-beta decay

> Good many-body methods for medium and heavy nuclei are important. Here we combine ideas from standard generator-coordinate methods (GCM) and the so-called Monte Carlo shell model, and set forth a novel approach: starting from a mean-field solution (Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov), we create a set of non-orthogonal basis states, by using low-lying vibrational quasiparticleTamm-Dancoff modes, and then project onto states of good angular momentum and particle number. The results we benchmark against full shell model calculations. Even with just a few such modes we find improvement over standard GCM calculations in excitation spectra. We also find significant improvement in $0νββ$ nuclear matrix elements.

## Key claim

The authors propose a novel generator-coordinate method (GCM) that uses low-lying quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff (QTDA) vibrational modes, rather than manually chosen constraining fields, to generate non-orthogonal reference states via Thouless' theorem, and find that even with just a few such modes the approach improves excitation spectra and neutrinoless double-beta decay matrix elements over standard GCM calculations, though ground-state energies remain higher than exact shell-model results.

## Method

Starting from a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) mean-field solution, the authors solve the quasiparticle Tamm-Dancoff approximation (QTDA) equations to identify low-lying collective vibrational modes. They then apply Thouless' theorem to generate new quasiparticle vacua from these QTDA operators, project the resulting non-orthogonal basis states onto good angular momentum and particle number, and diagonalize the Hamiltonian in this basis via the Hill-Wheeler equation. The method is benchmarked against full shell-model diagonalization and against standard GCM calculations that use constrained quadrupole and proton-neutron pairing fields (CHFB-GCM).

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies from QTDA-driven GCM are 50 to 350 keV higher than CHFB-GCM, and 40 keV to 1.4 MeV above exact shell-model energies (Table I, for 6 nuclei: 124Sn, 124Te, 130Te, 130Xe, 136Xe, 136Ba)
- QTDA-driven GCM uses about 15 QTDA operators built on HFB states, compared to 50–90 basis states for CHFB-GCM (for 124Te, 130Te, 130Xe, 136Ba) and 13 basis states for CHFB-GCM (for 124Sn, 136Xe)
- B(E2:0_1+ → 2_1+) values from QTDA-driven GCM range from 0.137 e²b² (124Sn) to 0.547 e²b² (124Te), compared to shell-model values of 0.146 e²b² and 0.579 e²b² respectively (Table II)
- The 0νββ matrix elements from QTDA-driven GCM are smaller than CHFB-GCM values and in closer agreement with shell-model results; CHFB-GCM overestimates the Gamow-Teller contribution by about 30% relative to shell model

## Context

This work extends standard GCM approaches that use quadrupole deformation and proton-neutron pairing as generator coordinates (Hinohara 2014, Javier 2016, Jiao 2017, Jiao 2018), which had shown about 30% overestimation of 0νββ matrix elements compared to shell-model predictions. The method is related to previous work building reference states from two-quasiparticle excitations (PhysRevC.79.014311, PhysRevC.95.024307), but uses collective superpositions from QTDA rather than individual excitations. The approach enables systematic inclusion of vibrational and non-collective correlations beyond what constrained-field GCM captures, though the full triaxial angular momentum projection required is computationally more expensive than axially-symmetric GCM calculations.
