# Nucleation of Quark--Gluon Plasma from Hadronic Matter

> The energy densities achieved during central collisions of large nuclei at Brookhaven's AGS may be high enough to allow the formation of quark--gluon plasma. Calculations based on relativistic nucleation theory suggest that rare events, perhaps one in every 10$^2$ or 10$^3$, undergo the phase transition. Experimental ramifications may include an enhancement in the ratio of pions to baryons, a reduction in the ratio of deuterons to protons, and a larger source size as seen by hadron interferometry.

## Key claim

Relativistic nucleation theory applied to central heavy-ion collisions at AGS energies suggests that quark-gluon plasma formation occurs only in rare events, perhaps one in every 10^2 or 10^3 central collisions, rather than as a bulk phenomenon, because the extreme superheating of hadronic matter pushes the system beyond the regime where standard homogeneous nucleation theory is reliable.

## Method

The authors model a central nucleus-nucleus collision as two Lorentz-contracted disks that interpenetrate and stop completely, then expand radially with entropy-conserving hydrodynamics. They compute the time-dependent baryon density, temperature, and chemical potential using a hadronic equation of state (including mesons, baryons, and a repulsive mean field) and a quark-gluon plasma equation of state (free quarks and gluons with a bag constant B = (220 MeV)^4). The nucleation rate of plasma droplets in superheated hadronic matter is calculated via Langer's formalism, including dynamical prefactors from thermal conductivity and shear viscosity, surface tension σ = 50 MeV/fm^2, and a shape entropy term, then integrated over the collision evolution to obtain the volume fraction converted to plasma.

## Key numbers

- Initial energy density for gold-gold at 11.6 GeV/A: 1.95 GeV/fm^3 (set 1)
- Initial energy density for lead-lead at 25 GeV/A: 3.90 GeV/fm^3 (set 2)
- Initial baryon density: 0.78 fm^{-3} (set 1) and 1.10 fm^{-3} (set 2)
- Interpenetration time t_0: 2.56 fm (set 1) and 1.85 fm (set 2)
- Maximum volume fraction converted to quark-gluon plasma q(t_f): 9.2 × 10^{-3} (set 1) and 3.2 × 10^{-3} (set 2)
- Maximum average droplet radius: 5.0 fm (set 1) and 4.3 fm (set 2)
- Average droplet density at t_f: 2 × 10^{-5} fm^{-3} (set 1) and 1 × 10^{-5} fm^{-3} (set 2)
- Average number of droplets nucleated per collision: roughly 1/50 (AGS) and 1/100 (SPS)
- Bag constant B = (220 MeV)^4; surface tension σ = 50 MeV/fm^2; correlation length ξ_had = 0.7 fm; critical exponent τ_crit = 2.2

## Context

This work extends the relativistic nucleation theory previously applied to quark-gluon plasma formation at RHIC energies (Csernai, 1992) to the lower-energy, baryon-rich environment of the AGS and SPS. The authors use a simple fireball expansion model consistent with ARC cascade simulations and direct experimental measurements. The results suggest that at AGS energies the phase transition proceeds via rare nucleation events rather than bulk conversion, in contrast to the expected behavior at RHIC and LHC where nearly instantaneous plasma formation is anticipated. The paper enables event-by-event experimental searches for signatures such as enhanced pion-to-baryon ratios, reduced deuteron-to-proton ratios, and larger source sizes from hadron interferometry.
