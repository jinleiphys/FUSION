# Collective non-Abelian instabilities in a melting Color Glass Condensate

> We present first results for 3+1-D simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills equations for matter expanding into the vacuum after a heavy ion collision. Violations of boost invariance cause a Weibel instability leading soft modes to grow with proper time $τ$ as $\exp(Γ\sqrt{g^2μτ})$, where $g^2μ$ is a scale arising from the saturation of gluons in the nuclear wavefunction. The scale for the growth rate $Γ$ is set by a plasmon mass, defined as $ω_{\rm pl}= κ_0 \sqrt{\frac{g^2μ}τ}$, generated dynamically in the collision. We compare the numerical ratio $Γ/κ_0$ to the corresponding value predicted by the Hard Thermal Loop formalism for anisotropic plasmas.

## Key claim

Violations of boost invariance in the expanding Color Glass Condensate cause a non-Abelian Weibel instability, leading soft modes to grow as exp(Γ√(g²μτ)) rather than the exponential-in-time growth seen in stationary plasmas; the growth rate Γ extracted from simulations agrees with Hard Thermal Loop motivated predictions to within 4–6% accuracy, but only if a factor-of-2 ambiguity in the HTL extrapolation is ignored.

## Method

The authors performed 3+1-dimensional numerical simulations of SU(2) Yang-Mills equations on a discretized space-time lattice, using Color Glass Condensate initial conditions that include small rapidity-dependent fluctuations (parameterized by Δ) superimposed on the boost-invariant classical fields. They evolved Hamilton’s equations for the gauge fields and their conjugate momenta using an adaptive leap-frog algorithm, averaging results over many configurations of the color charge sources. The growth of unstable modes was extracted from the Fourier transform of the longitudinal pressure τ²Tηη, and the dynamically generated plasmon mass ωpl was computed from the zero-momentum gluon dispersion relation.

## Key numbers

- Soft-mode amplitudes grow as exp(Γ√(g²μτ)); for g²μL = 67.9, the best-fit exponent is Γ_fit = 0.427 ± 0.01 with the functional form c₀ + c₁ exp(c₂ τ^0.5).
- The plasmon mass scales as ωpl(τ) = κ₀ √(g²μ/τ); for g²μL = 22.6, κ₀ = 0.304 ± 0.002, and for g²μL = 67.9, κ₀ = 0.258 ± 0.002.
- The HTL-motivated prediction Γ_th = √3 κ₀ gives 0.526 ± 0.003 for g²μL = 22.6 and 0.447 ± 0.003 for g²μL = 67.9, compared to fitted values of 0.502 ± 0.01 and 0.427 ± 0.01 respectively.
- The saturation scale Qs is estimated as ≈ 1.4 GeV for RHIC energies and ≈ 2.2 GeV for LHC energies (√s = 5.5 TeV/nucleon).
- The initial time for simulations is τ₀ = 0.05 a⊥, and the rapidity extent is Lη = 1.6 units; raising Lη to 3.2 lowers the onset time by about 20% while the growth rate remains unchanged.

## Context

This work extends the 2+1-dimensional boost-invariant simulations of Krasnitz, Nara, and Venugopalan (2001) by relaxing strict boost invariance, and it directly tests the non-Abelian Weibel instability predicted by Mrowczynski (1993) and further developed by Arnold, Lenaghan, and Moore (2003) and by Romatschke and Strickland (2003). The results enable quantitative comparison with the Hard Thermal Loop formalism of Braaten and Pisarski (1990) extended to anisotropic plasmas, and they open the path to studying whether collective instabilities can accelerate thermalization beyond the “bottom-up” scenario of Baier, Mueller, Schiff, and Son (2001), which predicted thermalization times of order 1/(α_S^{13/5} Q_s).
