# Effective Field Theory and Matching in Non-Relativistic Gauge Theories

> The effective Lagrangian and power counting rules for non-relativistic gauge theories are derived via an expansion in $1/c$. It is shown that the $1/c$ expansion leads to an effective field theory which incorporates a multipole expansion. Failure to use this expansion in the low energy theory leads to a loss of predictive power as the matching procedure becomes intractable. It is emphasized that there is not a one-to-one correspondence between operators in the modified low energy theory and those in the heavy quark effective theory.We also discuss the Lamb shift as a pedagogical example of the power counting scheme.

## Key claim

The paper derives an effective field theory for non-relativistic gauge theories via a systematic expansion in the dimensionful parameter \(1/c\), showing that this expansion naturally incorporates a multipole expansion and that failure to use this multipole expansion in the low-energy theory leads to a loss of predictive power because the matching procedure becomes intractable.

## Method

The authors start from the large-\(c\) limit of a non-Abelian gauge theory and write the Lagrangian as an expansion in powers of \(1/c\), with explicit powers of \(c\) in the coefficients. They then rescale the transverse gauge field \(\tilde A_i(\vec y=\vec x/c,t) = \sqrt c A_i(\vec x,t)\) to remove \(c\) dependence from the lowest-order Lagrangian, leaving the states of the effective theory independent of \(c\). The \(1/c\) expansion forces a multipole expansion of the gauge fields about the center of mass; the matching procedure is performed by expanding full-theory amplitudes in \(1/c\) and adjusting the coefficients of the effective Lagrangian to reproduce that expansion.

## Key numbers

* The lowest-order Lagrangian retains a \(1/c\) term in the kinetic energy of transverse gluons, which is necessary because the Hamiltonian coefficient of that kinetic energy is \(c^2\).
* The one-loop correction to the two-point function from two insertions of the magnetic moment operator, when computed using the unexpanded Lagrangian, gives \(\Gamma^{(2)} = \frac{c_F^2 C(R)\alpha}{\pi} \Gamma(2\epsilon)\left[ 2(E-\frac{\vec{p}^2}{2m})+4mc^2+\frac{4}{3}\frac{\vec{p}^2}{2m} \right] + \text{finite}\), showing mixing of operators of different orders in \(1/c\).
* When the same calculation is performed using the multipole-expanded Lagrangian, the result is \(\Gamma^{(2)} = \frac{c_F^2 C(R)\alpha}{2\pi} \Gamma(2\epsilon)\frac{(E-\frac{\vec{p}^2}{2m})^3}{m^2 c^4} + \text{finite}\), which renormalizes only a higher-order operator.
* The Lamb shift in hydrogen is cited as a pedagogical example: the leading \((v/c)^2\) corrections come from the \(\psi^\dagger \bfpartial^4/(8m^3c^2)\psi\) term, the Darwin term, and the spin-orbit term; the transverse photon self-energy correction is of relative size \((v/c)^3\) due to a factor of \(c^{-3/2}\) from each transverse photon vertex.

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach of Caswell and Lepage (1986) for non-relativistic bound states, which differs from Heavy Quark Effective Theory (HQET) because in heavy-heavy systems the quark velocity is not fixed and Coulombic velocity-altering exchanges build up the Schroedinger kernel. The paper shows that previous velocity-scaling rules derived via heuristic arguments (e.g., by Lepage and others) can be replaced by a systematic \(1/c\) expansion that makes power counting as simple as in HQET. The formalism enables clean matching calculations for non-relativistic bound states in both Abelian and non-Abelian gauge theories, and it clarifies how to incorporate confining effects in the non-Abelian case by including the full non-perturbative gluondynamics in the zeroth-order states.
