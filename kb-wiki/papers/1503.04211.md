# A Modest Revision of the Standard Model

> With a modest revision of the mass sector of the Standard Model, the systematics of the fermion masses and mixings can be fully described and interpreted as providing information on matrix elements of physics beyond the Standard Model. A by-product is a reduction of the largest Higgs Yukawa fine structure constant by an order of magnitude. The extension to leptons provides for insight on the difference between quark mixing and lepton mixing as evidenced in neutrino oscillations. The large difference between the scale for up-quark and down-quark masses is not addressed. In this approach, improved detail and accuracy of the elements of the current mixing matrices can extend our knowledge and understanding of physics beyond the Standard Model.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that the fermion mass sector of the Standard Model can be revised by assuming the Higgs boson couples identically to all right-chiral fermions of a given electric charge, with the observed mass spectrum and mixing arising from small (order 1%) perturbative corrections from physics beyond the Standard Model (BSM), rather than from arbitrary Yukawa couplings. The authors explicitly note that the substantial difference between the scale for up-quark and down-quark masses is not addressed by this approach.

## Method

The authors replace the generation-dependent Yukawa couplings in the Standard Model with a single Yukawa coupling for all up-type quarks and another for all down-type quarks, producing a "democratic" mass matrix where all entries are equal. They then introduce the most general Hermitian 3×3 perturbation matrix parameterized by Gell-Mann SU(3) basis coefficients (y₀ through y₈), scaled by a small parameter ε, to represent BSM loop corrections. The resulting mass matrix is diagonalized using a tri-bi-maximal (TBM) transformation followed by block diagonalization, and the CKM matrix is constructed from the separate diagonalization matrices for up-quarks (U) and down-quarks (V).

## Key numbers

- Up-quark masses used: 2.3 MeV/c², 1275 MeV/c², 173500 MeV/c² (from PDG)
- Down-quark masses used: 3.8 MeV/c², 95 MeV/c², 4150 MeV/c² (from PDG)
- ε_u = 7.35 × 10⁻³ (ratio of intermediate to heaviest up-quark mass)
- δ_u = 1.8 × 10⁻³ (ratio of lightest to intermediate up-quark mass)
- ε_d = 2.29 × 10⁻² (ratio of intermediate to heaviest down-quark mass)
- δ_d = 4.0 × 10⁻² (ratio of lightest to intermediate down-quark mass)
- Deviations from perfect democracy are less than 1% in the up-quark sector and less than 4% in the down-quark sector
- The largest Higgs Yukawa fine structure constant is reduced by an order of magnitude compared to the Standard Model

## Context

This work extends the "democratic" mass matrix approach previously described by Jarlskog (1980s) and contrasts with "top-down" approaches that start from symmetry assumptions, such as those by Fritzsch (1970s) and others. The authors explicitly distinguish their "bottom-up" method from prior work that reduces the number of unknown BSM parameters by assuming limited forms for initial mass matrices. The approach enables extraction of information on matrix elements of BSM physics from improved measurements of CKM matrix elements, and provides insight into why quark mixing (CKM) differs from lepton mixing (PMNS) as seen in neutrino oscillations.
