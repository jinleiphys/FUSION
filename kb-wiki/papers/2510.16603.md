# Flux-Conservative BDNK Hydrodynamics and Shock Regularization

> We present a new, first-order, flux-conservative formulation of relativistic viscous hydrodynamics in the BDNK framework, applicable to conformal and nonconformal fluids at zero chemical potential. Focusing on the conformal case in 1+1 dimensions, we numerically solve the equations of motion for two classes of consistent initial data and assess the robustness of the resulting solutions with respect to changing the hydrodynamic frame. Our flux-conservative formulation does not exhibit spurious oscillatory structures within the regime of validity of BDNK theory (the hydrodynamic-frame-robust regime), corresponding to sufficiently small Knudsen numbers. Using this flux-conservative approach, we numerically investigate the potential formation of shocks and their fate in BDNK in 1+1D using smooth initial data known to produce shocks in the relativistic Euler equations. For the type of initial data we consider, we show that the sharp features formed in Euler are prevented by the hyperbolic viscous BDNK equations. The prevention of shock formation we observe occurs in the frame-robust regime of BDNK. This, however, does not preclude the formation of shocks in BDNK for different smooth initial data. The reliability of our results is supported by systematic numerical convergence testing, which is used to assess shock indicators, simulation performance, and the robustness of solutions for different hydrodynamic frames.

## Key claim

The paper presents a new first-order flux-conservative formulation of BDNK relativistic viscous hydrodynamics that, for smooth initial data known to produce shocks in the relativistic Euler equations, prevents the formation of sharp features in the frame-robust regime (sufficiently small Knudsen numbers), though this does not preclude shock formation for different smooth initial data.

## Method

The authors derive a flux-conservative formulation of BDNK hydrodynamics by expressing the energy-momentum tensor in terms of deviations from the Killing condition using the vector \(C_\mu = T u_\mu\) and its derivatives \(X_{\mu\nu} = \partial_\mu C_\nu\). They numerically solve the resulting first-order PDE system in 1+1 dimensions for conformal fluids using smooth Gaussian energy profiles and semi-discontinuous stair-step initial conditions, systematically varying the hydrodynamic frame parameters \(\tau_\varepsilon\) and \(\tau_\mathcal{Q}\) while monitoring Knudsen numbers and performing numerical convergence testing.

## Key numbers

* For Gaussian initial conditions at low viscosities and sufficiently large background energy density, simulations recover second-order numerical convergence (\(p \sim 2\)).
* For stair-step initial conditions, the simulations exhibit lower-order convergence (\(p \sim 1\)), consistent with the eventual formation of shock-like structures.
* The prevention of shock formation is observed in the frame-robust regime of BDNK, corresponding to sufficiently small Knudsen numbers (Kn \(\ll 1\)).

## Context

This work extends the BDNK framework (Bemfica, Disconzi, Noronha, Kovtun, 2017-2020) by providing a flux-conservative formulation suitable for numerical shock studies, complementing prior numerical simulations by Pandya et al. (2021) and Bea et al. (2023). The results contrast with Israel-Stewart theory, where finite-time gradient blow-up and shock formation have been shown to occur (2025arXiv250804717B), and establish a framework for systematically studying shock emergence in BDNK theory, with numerical convergence testing serving as a diagnostic for simulation reliability.
