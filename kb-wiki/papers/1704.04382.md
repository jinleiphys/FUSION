# Chiral magnetic effect in isobaric collisions

> We give a numerical simulation of the generation of the magnetic field and the charge-separation signal due to the chiral magnetic effect (CME) --- the induction of an electric current by the magnetic field in a parity-odd matter --- in the collisions of isobaric nuclei, $^{96}_{44}$Ru + $^{96}_{44}$Ru and $^{96}_{40}$Zr + $^{96}_{40}$Zr, at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200$ GeV. We show that such collisions provide an ideal tool to disentangle the CME signal from the possible elliptic-flow driven background effects. We also discuss some other effects that can be tested by using the isobaric collisions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that comparing charge-separation signals in collisions of isobaric nuclei (Ru+Ru vs. Zr+Zr) at 200 GeV can disentangle the chiral magnetic effect (CME) from elliptic-flow-driven background effects, because the two systems produce nearly identical elliptic flow but a roughly 10% difference in magnetic field strength. The claim is conditional on the background level being sufficiently low; the authors show that with 400 million events, the relative difference in the CME-sensitive observable can reach 5% and be 5 sigma above the flow-driven baseline.

## Method

The authors performed a numerical simulation using the HIJING model to compute the event-averaged projected magnetic field squared (B_sq) at the center of mass of the overlapping region for Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions at 200 GeV. They also used a Monte Carlo Glauber simulation to compute the initial eccentricity (epsilon_2) for both systems. The charge-separation observable S = N_part * Delta_gamma was modeled via a two-component perturbative approach that combines the magnetic-field-driven CME contribution (proportional to B_sq) and the flow-driven background contribution (proportional to epsilon_2), with a free parameter "bg" representing the background fraction.

## Key numbers

- The relative difference in B_sq between Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr collisions, R_Bsq, approaches 15% (case 1) or 18% (case 2) for peripheral events, and reduces to about 13% (both cases) for central events.
- The relative difference in initial eccentricity, R_epsilon2, is always much smaller than R_Bsq, particularly for centrality bins above 20%.
- For the centrality range 20-60% with bg=2/3 and 400 million events, the relative difference in S (R_S) is about 5% for both case 1 and case 2.
- With 400 million events combined over 20-60% centrality, R_S is 5 sigma above R_epsilon2 for both cases.

## Context

This work extends the proposal by Voloshin (2010) to use nuclear shape and magnetic field variations to isolate the CME signal, following earlier experimental studies by the STAR Collaboration on Au+Au, Cu+Cu, and U+U collisions. The paper directly builds on the authors' prior simulation framework (Deng 2016) and provides a concrete numerical prediction for the upcoming isobaric collision program at RHIC. The results enable experimental determination of the background level in the charge-separation observable and also allow tests of nuclear deformation parameters and the magnetic-field contribution to Lambda/anti-Lambda spin polarization splitting.
