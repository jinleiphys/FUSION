# Can QFT be UV finite as well as effective ?

> A new attempt is demonstrated that QFTs can be UV finite if they are viewed as the low energy effective theories of a fundamental underlying theory (complete and well-defined in all respects) according to the modern standard point of view. This approach is much simpler in principle and in technology comparing to any known renormalization program. Some subtle and difficult issues can be easily resolved. The importance of the procedure for defining the ambiguities is fully appreciated in the new approach. Some simple but important nonperturbative examples are discussed to show the power and plausibility of the new approach.

## Key claim
Quantum field theories can be rendered UV finite if they are treated as low-energy effective theories of a well-defined underlying fundamental theory, but the resulting finite amplitudes contain unavoidable polynomial ambiguities that must be fixed by the underlying theory through the low-energy limit operation.

## Method
The author proposes a procedure where ill-defined Feynman amplitudes are first differentiated with respect to external momenta a sufficient number of times (determined by the superficial divergence degree) to produce convergent integrals, then the low-energy limit operator is applied, and finally the result is integrated indefinitely with respect to external momenta. For multi-loop graphs, the procedure is applied recursively to divergent subgraphs, with the low-energy limit operator crossing progressively more parts of the graph until all subgraphs are convergent, after which loop integrations are performed from the smallest subgraphs upward.

## Key numbers
- The superficial divergence degree of a graph is denoted by ω-1, where ω is the number of differentiations applied to render the integral convergent.
- The indefinite integration after differentiation introduces an ω-1 order polynomial in external momenta with arbitrary constant coefficients (denoted c_ω and C_ω).

## Context
This work extends the modern standard viewpoint that quantum field theories are low-energy effective theories of a fundamental underlying theory, as discussed by Jackiw (cited as Jac) and further developed by the author in prior work (cited as YYY). The approach directly addresses the non-commutativity of the low-energy limit operation and path integration, which the author identifies as the origin of UV ill-definedness. The method is applied to critique the inequivalence of regularization schemes in nonperturbative contexts, specifically referencing the work of Phillips et al. (cited as QMDR) which showed that cutoff and dimensional regularization schemes yield different results, and aims to revive the effective field theory approach to low-energy nuclear physics initiated by Weinberg (cited as WeinEFT) from recent criticism by Cohen (cited as Cohen).
