# The Use of Nuclear Beta-decay as a Test of Bulk Neutrinos in Extra Dimensions

> Theories which include neutrinos in large extra dimensions can be constrained by nuclear beta decay experiments. We examine universality of beta decay strengths of Fermi transitions. From this we find that the extra dimensional Yukawa coupling for a higher dimensional scale of 10 TeV, and two extra dimensions can be constrained to y < 1. Kinematic implications are also discussed. In particular, an extra dimensional scenario will produce a tritium decay endpoint spectrum with a different shape than that for just one massive state.

## Key claim

Nuclear beta-decay universality tests can constrain the Yukawa coupling of bulk neutrinos in extra dimensions, but the constraint is always less stringent than limits from direct neutrino mass measurements and reactor neutrino experiments; for two extra dimensions with a higher-dimensional scale of 10 TeV, the Yukawa coupling can be constrained to y < 1, but only if the precision of beta-decay data improves by two orders of magnitude to probe higher scales.

## Method

The authors compared the apparent ft-values for pure Fermi 0+ → 0+ transitions in 14O (Q-value 1.81 MeV) and 54Co (Q-value 7.22 MeV), using the combined experimental uncertainty of 0.22% from published Ft-value data. They calculated the modification to the beta-decay phase space factor arising from a tower of Kaluza-Klein neutrino states, summing over all kinematically accessible KK modes up to kmax = Q R, and converted the sums to integrals to derive limits on the Yukawa coupling y and the compactification radius R for both one and two extra dimensions.

## Key numbers

- For one extra dimension with M* = 10 TeV, the limit on the Yukawa coupling from universality is y < 10^-3, which is less stringent than the limit from neutrino mass (y < 3.2 × 10^-3 × (M*/10 TeV)^(1/2) × (R/1 mm)^(1/2) × (m_D/1 eV)) and from CHOOZ reactor data (y < 4.4 × 10^-8 × (1 mm/R)^(1/2) × (M*/10 TeV)^(1/2)).
- For two extra dimensions with M* = 10 TeV and R1 = R2, the universality constraint on y is insensitive to R and does not constrain y below order 1 for scales above 10 TeV.
- The tritium beta-decay endpoint spectrum for one extra dimension with ζ = 0.13 and 1/R = 25 eV gives m_D = 2.3 eV, with the first three mass eigenstates at 2.3 eV, 25 eV, and 50 eV having mixings |U_ke|^2 of 0.974, 0.017, and 0.004 respectively.
- For a 17 keV neutrino in the extra-dimensional scenario, the mixing is ζ^2 ~ 5 × 10^-9, far below the experimental limit of sin^2 2θ ~ 10^-3.
- For a 1 MeV KK state, the mixing is ζ^2 ~ 3 × 10^-13, many orders below current constraints of sin^2 2θ ~ 10^-3.

## Context

This work extends the study of bulk neutrinos in large extra dimensions, following the framework of Arkani-Hamed, Dimopoulos, and Dvali (1998) and Dienes, Dudas, and Gherghetta (1999), who proposed that right-handed neutrinos propagating in extra dimensions could produce small neutrino masses. The authors apply nuclear beta-decay universality tests, previously used to test the Standard Model and unitarity of the CKM matrix, as a new probe of extra-dimensional parameters. The results show that current beta-decay data cannot compete with constraints from tritium endpoint measurements and reactor neutrino experiments (CHOOZ), but improved precision could probe higher-dimensional scales beyond 10 TeV, and the distinctive multi-bump shape of the tritium endpoint spectrum from a KK tower differs from single or two-neutrino mixing scenarios.
