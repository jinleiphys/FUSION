# A New State of Matter between the Hadronic Phase and the Quark-Gluon Plasma?

> Lattice-QCD simulations and theoretical arguments hint at the existence of an intermediate phase of strongly interacting matter between a confined hadron gas and a deconfined Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP). We qualitatively and semi-quantitatively explore and differentiate the phase structures in the temperature window from the QCD pseudo-critical temperature $T_c\simeq 160\;\text{MeV}$ to the pure-gluonic deconfinement temperature $T_d\simeq 285\;\text{MeV}$. We propose a three-regime picture using a hadron resonance gas (HRG) description augmented with the glueball spectrum based on the analysis of a large number, $N_c$, of colors. We estimate the entropy density from our model to confirm that the lattice-QCD data are bracketed with three regimes, i.e., a hadron gas, a QGP, and a new phase for $T_c \lesssim T \lesssim T_d$. In this new phase that we name a Spaghetti of Quarks with Glueballs (SQGB), thermal degrees of freedom of quarks are deconfined, yet gluons remain confined in glueballs. Since the Hagedorn temperature, $T_H\sim 285\;\text{MeV}$, is universal in the meson and the glueball sectors, in the infinite $N_c$ limit, the phase diagram in the plane of the baryon chemical potential and the temperature is reduced to the one with the confined and deconfined phases and Quarkyonic Matter at high density. At large but finite $N_c$, an SQGB window may open between these phases. We point out that the SQGB has interesting similarities with Quarkyonic Matter and that this matter in the large $N_c$ limit is confined as measured by the interaction between heavy quarks, but behaves in other respects like a quasi-free gas of quarks. As a result of the extrapolation to $N_c=3$, we present a revised phase diagram with the SQGB phase bounded by thermal crossovers. Finally, we give a quantitative analysis of chiral symmetry restoration in the SQGB phase.

## Key claim

The paper proposes that between the hadronic phase and the Quark-Gluon Plasma (QGP), there exists an intermediate phase called "Spaghetti of Quarks with Glueballs" (SQGB) where quarks are deconfined but gluons remain confined in glueballs; this phase exists in the temperature window from the QCD pseudo-critical temperature \(T_c\simeq 160\;\text{MeV}\) to the pure-gluonic deconfinement temperature \(T_d\simeq 285\;\text{MeV}\), but the authors note that this window opens only at finite \(N_c\) and may be suppressed as \(\mathcal{O}(N_c^{-1})\) in the large-\(N_c\) limit.

## Method

The authors construct a hadron resonance gas (HRG) model augmented with Hagedorn spectra for open strings (mesons) and closed strings (glueballs), using large-\(N_c\) counting arguments to classify thermodynamic scaling regimes. They compute the entropy density from these spectra and compare it to lattice-QCD data, varying quark masses between bare and constituent values and gluon masses between zero and half the lightest glueball mass. The model is extended to finite baryon chemical potential using Debye screening arguments to determine the deconfinement boundary.

## Key numbers

* The QCD pseudo-critical temperature is \(T_c\simeq 160\;\text{MeV}\) and the pure-gluonic deconfinement temperature is \(T_d\simeq 285\;\text{MeV}\), defining the temperature window for the proposed SQGB phase.
* The Hagedorn temperature is estimated as \(T_H\simeq 285\;\text{MeV}\) using the string tension \(\sqrt{\sigma}\simeq 440\;\text{MeV}\) and the relation \(T_H^2 = 3\sigma/(2\pi)\).
* The entropy density jump at the deconfinement transition in pure gluonic theory is \(\Delta s/T^3 \simeq 1.45\).
* The lightest glueball mass is \(M_\mathrm{GB}\simeq 1.65\;\text{GeV}\), and the effective gluon mass at the critical temperature is taken as \(m_\mathrm{gluon}=0.8\;\text{GeV}\) (half the glueball mass).
* The ratio \(T_H/T_d\simeq 1.06\) for \(N_c=3\), with a direct lattice fit giving \(T_H/T_d\simeq 1.024(3)\).

## Context

This work extends the speculative scenario of Cohen and Glozman (2023) regarding a new phase between hadronic matter and QGP, and builds on the concept of Quarkyonic Matter introduced by McLerran and collaborators (2007). The paper provides a concrete thermodynamic model that explains lattice-QCD data from the HotQCD collaboration (2014) and Borsanyi et al. (2013), and revises the QCD phase diagram by introducing a temperature window where quarks are deconfined but gluons remain confined, with implications for understanding the strongly-coupled QGP (sQGP) and chiral symmetry restoration.
