# Pions valence quark distributions in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature

> We have calculated the valence quark distributions of the lightest pseudoscalar meson, pions, in the isospin asymmetric nuclear matter at zero and finite temperature employing a light-cone quark model. The medium modifications in the pion properties have been stimulated through the effective quark masses computed using the chiral SU($3$) quark mean field model. We have primarily focused on the impact of isospin asymmetric medium on the distribution amplitudes (DAs) and parton distribution functions (PDFs) of a valence quark for different baryon density and temperature values. Also, the DAs and PDFs have been evolved to $Q^2=10$ GeV$^2$ and $Q^2=16$ GeV$^2$ for different densities of nuclear medium and results have been compared with the available experimental data. The DAs and PDFs are found to modify substantially as a function of baryon density as compared to temperature and isospin asymmetry of the medium.

## Key claim

The valence quark distributions and distribution amplitudes of pions are modified substantially as a function of baryon density in isospin asymmetric nuclear matter, with density having a stronger effect than temperature or isospin asymmetry, though the abstract does not quantify the relative magnitude of these effects.

## Method

The authors combined the light-cone quark model (LCQM) with the chiral SU(3) quark mean field (CQMF) model to compute in-medium pion properties. The CQMF model provided effective quark masses for u and d quarks in asymmetric nuclear matter at finite temperature and density, which were then used as inputs to the LCQM to calculate the distribution amplitudes (DAs) and valence quark parton distribution functions (PDFs) of pions. The DAs and PDFs were evolved to Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 and Q^2 = 16 GeV^2 for comparison with experimental data.

## Key numbers

* Vacuum constituent quark masses: m_u^0 = m_d^0 = 256 MeV at zero baryon density and zero temperature.
* At nuclear saturation density (rho_B/rho_0 = 1) and T = 0 GeV with isospin asymmetry eta = 0, effective quark masses m_u^* = m_d^* = 0.1625 GeV.
* At rho_B/rho_0 = 1 and T = 0 GeV with eta = 0.5, m_u^* = 0.1676 GeV and m_d^* = 0.1627 GeV, showing isospin-induced mass splitting.
* At rho_B/rho_0 = 1 and T = 0.1 GeV with eta = 0, m_u^* = m_d^* = 0.1769 GeV, showing temperature-induced increase.
* DAs and PDFs were evolved to Q^2 = 10 GeV^2 and Q^2 = 16 GeV^2 for different nuclear medium densities.
* Nuclear saturation density: rho_0 = 0.16 fm^{-3}.

## Context

This work extends previous studies of pion structure in nuclear matter, which were limited to isospin symmetric matter at zero temperature, such as the Nambu-Jona-Lasinio model calculations by Suzuki (1995) and the combined NJL and quark meson coupling model approach by Hutauruk et al. (2018, 2019). The present calculation enables future investigations of pion structure in neutron-rich environments relevant to experiments at J-PARC, NICA, and the Electron-Ion Collider, and provides a framework for studying other mesons like kaons in asymmetric matter at finite temperature.
