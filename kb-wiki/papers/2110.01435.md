# Utilization of Event Shape in Search of the Chiral Magnetic Effect in Heavy-ion Collisions

> The search for the chiral magnetic effect (CME) has been a subject of great interest in the field of high-energy heavy-ion collision physics, and various observables have been proposed to probe the CME. Experimental observables are often contaminated with background contributions arising from collective motions (specifically elliptic flow) of the collision system. We present a method study of event-shape engineering (ESE) that projects the CME-sensitive $γ_{112}$ correlator and its variations ($γ_{132}$ and $γ_{123}$) to a class of events with minimal flow. We discuss the realization of the zero-flow mode, the sensitivity on the CME signal, and the corresponding statistical significance for Au+Au, Ru+Ru, and Zr+Zr collisions at $\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200$ GeV with a multiphase transport (AMPT) model, as well as a new event generator, Event-By-Event Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (EBE-AVFD).

## Key claim

Event-shape engineering (ESE) can project the CME-sensitive \(\gamma_{112}\) correlator and its variations to a class of events with minimal flow, but the zero-flow mode does not completely remove the flow-related background in the EBE-AVFD model; a residual background remains even at zero \(v_2\), and the intercept must be corrected by a factor of \((1-2v_2)\) to restore the ensemble average of the CME signal.

## Method

The authors used the multiphase transport (AMPT) model (string melting v2.25t4cu) to simulate pure-background events and the Event-By-Event Anomalous-Viscous Fluid Dynamics (EBE-AVFD) model to simulate events with controlled CME signal strength via the ratio \(n_5/s\) (axial charge density to entropy density). They applied event-shape engineering by binning events according to \(q_n^2\) (the squared flow vector magnitude of the particles of interest), then measured \(v_n\), \(\Delta\delta\), and the \(\gamma\) correlators (\(\gamma_{112}\), \(\gamma_{132}\), \(\gamma_{123}\)) as functions of \(q_n^2\), \(v_n\), or \(v_n\Delta\delta\), and extrapolated to the zero-flow mode using polynomial or linear fits. The analysis was performed for Au+Au, Ru+Ru, and Zr+Zr collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}} = 200\) GeV, with particles selected at mid-rapidity (\(|\eta|<1\)) and \(0.15 < p_T < 2\) GeV/\(c\).

## Key numbers

- For 30–40% Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV in AMPT, the \(q_2^2\) and \(q_3^2\) ranges under study covered 86.2% and 98.5% of the whole event sample, respectively.
- In EBE-AVFD Au+Au collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV, the numbers of events were \(9.6 \times 10^7\) for \(n_5/s=0\), \(5.9 \times 10^7\) for \(n_5/s=0.1\), and \(7.7 \times 10^7\) for \(n_5/s=0.2\).
- In the pure-background EBE-AVFD scenario (\(n_5/s=0\)), the ESE intercepts suppressed the background contribution roughly by a factor of 6 relative to the ensemble average for both \(\Delta\gamma_{112}\{\rm RP\}\) and \(\Delta\gamma_{112}\{\rm PP\}\).
- For \(\Delta\gamma_{132}\) in EBE-AVFD, the intercept of the linear fit was always consistent with zero for all \(n_5/s\) values under study.
- The isobaric systems Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr have roughly a 15% difference in the magnetic field squared, leading to a similar magnitude difference in the CME signal.

## Context

This work extends the ESE method previously applied to AMPT pure-background events in Ref. [ESE1] by also studying \(\gamma_{132}\) and \(\gamma_{123}\) correlators, and by employing the EBE-AVFD model (Shi et al., 2017–2019) which implements a realistic CME signal on top of flow-related backgrounds. The study enables future experimental application of ESE to STAR isobar collision data (Ru+Ru and Zr+Zr at \(\sqrt{s_{\rm NN}}=200\) GeV) to search for the CME, and shows that \(\Delta\gamma_{123}\) is not a good background estimate for \(\Delta\gamma_{112}\), consistent with the conclusion of Subikash (2020). The results also indicate that \(v_n\) and \(v_n\Delta\delta\) are technically preferred over \(q_n^2\) for projecting \(\Delta\gamma\) correlators to the zero-flow mode.
