# Hadron resonance probes of QGP

> We discuss the indirect and direct role of the short-lived resonances as probes of QGP freeze-out process. The indirect effect is the distortion of stable single particle yields and spectra by contributions of decaying resonances, which alter significantly the parameters obtained in fits to experimental data. We than discuss the direct observation of short-lived resonances as a probe of post-hadronization dynamics allowing to distinguish between different hadronization models.

## Key claim

Short-lived hadron resonances can serve as direct probes of the freeze-out dynamics of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) because their lifetimes are comparable to the hadronization timescale, allowing measurement of both the hadronization temperature and the duration of the post-hadronization interacting hadron gas phase, provided that two or more resonances with different masses and lifetimes are measured simultaneously.

## Method

The authors model resonance production using the Fermi statistical hadronization model with a Boltzmann spectrum, then compute the feed-down contribution of decaying resonances to stable particle spectra via Lorentz-invariant phase space integrals, including both two-body and N-body decays. To extract freeze-out dynamics, they evolve resonance abundances over time using coupled differential equations that include resonance decay with width Gamma, dilution from collective expansion at constant velocity v, and rescattering of decay products parameterized by an average cross-section and hadron gas density. The method is applied to SPS and RHIC data for K*, Lambda(1520), and Sigma* resonances.

## Key numbers

- At SPS energies (158 GeV per nucleon), including resonance decays in spectral fits to WA97 data for K_S, Lambda, anti-Lambda, Xi, anti-Xi, Omega, anti-Omega improves the chi-squared by a factor of 3 compared to fits without resonance contributions.
- The Lambda(1520) resonance is strongly suppressed at both SPS and RHIC energies relative to statistical model predictions, while the K*/K ratio is consistent with statistical model predictions within error.
- The resonance-to-daughter ratio N*/N for a purely thermal source follows a step function: zero for transverse mass m_T below the resonance mass m*, and equal to the ratio of degeneracies g*/g for m_T greater than m*.

## Context

This work extends the statistical hadronization model approach previously applied to SPS data by Becattini (2001) and to RHIC data by Rafelski (2002), and addresses the puzzle that HBT measurements, traditionally used to probe freeze-out time, are not well described by hydrodynamic models as noted by Heinz and Kolb (2002). The paper enables a new experimental program where simultaneous measurement of two resonance ratios (e.g., K*/K and Lambda(1520)/Lambda) can determine both the hadronization temperature and the timescale of the interacting hadron gas phase, distinguishing between sudden explosive hadronization and models with significant post-hadronization rescattering.
