# Three-body decay of $Λ_c^{*} (2765)$ and determination of its spin-parity

> We study three-body decays of $Λ_c^{*}(2765) \to Λ_c^+π^+π^-$ by using effective Lagrangians in a non-relativistic framework. We consider the sequential decays through $Σ_c(2455)π$ and $Σ_c^*(2520)π$ in intermediate states which are dominant contributions. The coupling constants in the effective Lagrangians are computed in the quark model. We demonstrate that the ratio $R= Γ(Λ_c^*\toΣ_c^*(2520)π)/Γ(Λ_c^*\to Σ_c(2455)π)$ and angular correlations are sensitive to the spin and parity of $Λ_c^{*}(2765)$. Thus, the measurement of these observables in experimental facilities such as Belle and LHCb can provide useful constraints to determine the spin and parity of $Λ_c^{*}(2765)$.

## Key claim

The ratio \(R = \Gamma(\Lambda_c^*\to\Sigma_c^*(2520)\pi)/\Gamma(\Lambda_c^*\to \Sigma_c(2455)\pi)\) and angular correlations in the three-body decay \(\Lambda_c^*(2765) \to \Lambda_c^+\pi^+\pi^-\) are sensitive to the spin and parity of \(\Lambda_c^*(2765)\), but the paper does not definitively assign a spin-parity; instead it shows that measurement of these observables at Belle or LHCb can provide useful constraints, with the caveat that the direct decay process is assumed negligible based on experimental hints.

## Method

The authors computed two-body decay amplitudes for \(\Lambda_c^* \to \Sigma_c^{(*)}\pi\) and \(\Sigma_c^{(*)} \to \Lambda_c\pi\) using effective Lagrangians in a non-relativistic framework, with coupling constants determined from the quark model via helicity amplitude matching. They then constructed three-body decay amplitudes for \(\Lambda_c^*(2765) \to \Lambda_c^+\pi^+\pi^-\) as a coherent sum of sequential processes through intermediate \(\Sigma_c(2455)\) and \(\Sigma_c^*(2520)\) states, including both charge channels, and evaluated the Dalitz plots and angular correlations for all possible spin-parity assignments up to \(2\hbar\omega\) excitation (\(J^P = 1/2^\pm, 3/2^\pm, 5/2^\pm, 7/2^+\)).

## Key numbers

- The total decay width of \(\Lambda_c^*(1P_\lambda, 1/2(1)^-)\) into \(\Sigma_c\pi\) and \(\Sigma_c^*\pi\) is 65.1–146 MeV, with a ratio \(R = 0.04\)–0.06.
- The total decay width of \(\Lambda_c^*(1P_\lambda, 3/2(1)^-)\) is 52.2–104 MeV, with a ratio \(R = 5.60\)–7.80.
- The total decay width of \(\Lambda_c^*(1P_\rho, 5/2(2)^-)\) is 6.30–8.80 MeV, with a ratio \(R = 0.87\)–0.90.
- The total decay width of \(\Lambda_c^*(2S_{\lambda\lambda}, 1/2(0)^+)\) is 1.60–4.50 MeV, with a ratio \(R = 0.79\)–0.91.
- The total decay width of \(\Lambda_c^*(1D_{\lambda\lambda}, 5/2(2)^+)\) is 1.90–4.40 MeV, with a ratio \(R = 12.8\)–13.8.
- The total decay width of \(\Lambda_c^*(1D_{\lambda\rho}, 7/2(3)^+_2)\) is 0.17–0.37 MeV, with a ratio \(R = 0.41\)–0.43.
- The coupling constant for \(\Sigma_c(1S,1/2(1)^+) \to \Lambda_c\pi(p)\) is \(g_3^p = -\frac{1}{\sqrt{3}} C_1\), and for \(\Sigma_c^*(1S,3/2(1)^+) \to \Lambda_c\pi(p)\) it is \(g_4^p = -C_1\), where \(C_1 = G \left[ 2 + \frac{E}{2m} \left(1- \frac{M}{2m+M}\right) \right] F(p)\) and \(G = g_A^q/(2 f_\pi)\) with \(f_\pi = 93\) MeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous studies of three-body decays of \(\Lambda_c^*(2595)\) and \(\Lambda_c^*(2625)\) (Arifi 2017, Arifi 2018) to the poorly understood \(\Lambda_c^*(2765)\) resonance, which has a one-star rating in the Particle Data Group (Tanabashi 2018) with unknown spin and parity. The isospin was recently determined to be \(I=0\) by Belle (Abdesselam 2019). The paper enables experimental determination of the spin-parity of \(\Lambda_c^*(2765)\) through comparison of measured ratios and angular correlations with the computed predictions for all possible quark model configurations up to \(2\hbar\omega\) excitation, and tests whether this state shares the flavor-independent nature of the Roper-like excitation seen in other baryon sectors.
