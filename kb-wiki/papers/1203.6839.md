# A new Parameterization for the Pion Vector Form Factor

> A new approach to the parameterization of pion form factors is presented and for illustration applied to the pion vector form factor. It has the correct analytic structure, is at low energies consistent with recent high accuracy analyses of $ππ$ scattering phase shifts and, at high energies, maps smoothly onto the well--known, successful isobar model.

## Key claim
The paper presents a new parameterization for the pion vector form factor that has the correct analytic structure, is consistent with high-accuracy ππ scattering phase shifts at low energies, and maps smoothly onto the isobar model at high energies, but the approach relies on the model-dependent assumption that all long-range forces are contained in the elastic ππ interactions while all other channel interactions are short-ranged.

## Method
The formalism splits the full interaction potential into an elastic part (described by a phase shift from dispersive analyses) and a resonance part, then uses the two-potential formalism to derive closed-form expressions for the scattering T-matrix and form factor. The resonance potential is written as a sum of pole terms with subtraction at s=0, and the form factor is expressed as F = Γ_out [1 - V_R Σ]^{-1} M, where Γ_out contains the Omnès function for the elastic channel, Σ are self-energies computed from subtracted dispersion integrals, and M contains direct photon-continuum couplings and photon-resonance couplings linear in s.

## Key numbers
- The elastic ππ phase shift input uses the central values from Ref. [madrid_new] for energies below s_cut = (1.4)^2 GeV^2, with a smooth extrapolation to π at high energies using Λ = 10 GeV.
- For the pion vector form factor alone, fits #1 and #2 give χ^2/d.o.f = 1.2 and 1.4 respectively.
- The ρ-ω mixing strength parameter κ_1 = -2.2(1)×10^{-3} and ρ-φ mixing strength κ_2 = -0.6(1)×10^{-3} for fit #2A.
- The bare masses of the two explicit resonances in fit #2A are m_(1) = 1.57(1) GeV and m_(2) = 2.0(1) GeV.
- At s = 2 GeV^2, Ref. [Simon] reports a value of r ~ 26, indicating the 2π channel provides only a small fraction of e+e- annihilation in the I=1 channel.
- The mean square charge radius of the pion increases by nearly 10% compared to the Omnès function alone when high-lying resonances are included.

## Context
This work extends the Omnès representation (Omnes, 1958) for form factors beyond the elastic region by providing an analytically improved version of the K-matrix approach. It is consistent with high-accuracy Roy equation analyses of ππ scattering from the Madrid group (2011) and the Bern group, and contrasts with standard isobar models (e.g., Gounaris-Sakurai, 1968) that violate unitarity for overlapping resonances or violate analyticity through incomplete dispersive corrections. The formalism enables simultaneous, consistent analysis of multiple channels and observables, which could clarify the experimental situation for ρ resonances beyond ρ(770), where the PDG notes three additional resonance candidates beyond the established ρ(1450) and ρ(1700).
