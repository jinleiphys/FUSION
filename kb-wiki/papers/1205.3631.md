# Two-particle interferometry for the sources undergoing first-order QCD phase transition in high energy heavy ion collisions

> We investigate the two-particle interferometry for the particle-emitting sources which undergo the first-order phase transition from the quark-gluon plasma with a finite baryon chemical potential to hadron resonance gas. The effects of source expansion, lifetime, and particle absorption on the transverse interferometry radii $R_{\rm out}$ and $R_{\rm side}$ are examined. We find that the emission durations of the particles become large when the system is initially located at the boundary between the mixed phase and the quark-gluon plasma. In this case, the difference between the radii $R_{\rm out}$ and $R_{\rm side}$ increases with the transverse momentum of the particle pair significantly. The ratio of $\sqrt{R_{\rm out}^2 -R_{\rm side}^2}$ to the transverse velocity of the pair is an observable for the enhancement of the emission duration.

# Two-particle interferometry for the sources undergoing first-order QCD phase transition in high energy heavy ion collisions

## Key claim

The ratio of sqrt(R_out^2 - R_side^2) to the transverse velocity of the particle pair is an observable for the enhancement of the emission duration, but this quantity reliably reflects the source lifetime only when the system is initially located at the boundary between the mixed phase and the quark-gluon plasma (the soft point); for systems initially in the QGP phase, the large transverse expansion and positive correlation between r_o and t cause this ratio to underestimate the true lifetime.

## Method

The authors solved (2+1)-dimensional relativistic hydrodynamic equations with an equation of state for a first-order QCD phase transition, using a bag-model QGP phase and an excluded-volume hadron gas model with a baryon chemical potential corresponding to incident energy of about 30 AGeV. They then applied two-particle Hanbury-Brown-Twiss (HBT) interferometry with the quantum transport of the interfering pair (QTIP) technique to compute the transverse HBT radii R_out and R_side for both pions and kaons emitted from hydrodynamic sources initialized either in the QGP phase or at the soft point (the boundary between the mixed phase and QGP).

## Key numbers

- The soft point occurs at energy density epsilon^MQ = 1.83 GeV/fm^3, with temperature T_0^SP = 142 MeV and baryon chemical potential mu_B0^SP = 780 MeV.
- The QGP initial condition has energy density epsilon_0^QGP = 4.12 GeV/fm^3, temperature T_0^QGP = 180 MeV, and baryon chemical potential mu_B0^QGP = 990 MeV.
- The ratio p/epsilon retains values smaller than 0.075 in the energy density range 0.6 to 2.1 GeV/fm^3.
- The initial source radius for the soft-point case is 5.2 fm, scaled from the QGP case radius of 4.0 fm by the factor (epsilon_0^QGP / epsilon^MQ)^(1/3).
- For the simple Gaussian source model with lifetime tau = 12 fm/c, the standard deviation of time sigma_t = sqrt((pi-2)/pi) * tau is approximately 0.363 * tau^2.
- The average emission duration for the pion source for ICQGP is 8.4 fm/c at large K_T.
- The correlation <r_o t> for pions is 0.16 fm^2/c for ICQGP and -8.30 fm^2/c for ICSP.

## Context

This work extends the HBT analysis technique with quantum transport of the interfering pair (QTIP) developed by Yu et al. (2008) and its application to spherical systems with first-order phase transitions by Yu et al. (2010). It uses relativistic hydrodynamics in (2+1) dimensions rather than spherical symmetry, and examines both pion and kaon interferometry to disentangle the effects of source expansion, lifetime, and particle absorption. The results enable experimental searches for the first-order QCD phase transition and its critical end point, particularly relevant to the RHIC and SPS low energy programs and the future FAIR facility at GSI.
