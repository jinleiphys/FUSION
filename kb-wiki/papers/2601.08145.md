# Light Nuclei embedded in a Nuclear Medium: Clustering and Mott Transitions

> The clustering of nucleons is a fundamental phenomenon with broad implications for nuclear physics and astrophysics. In this work, we employ a microscopic in-medium few-body approach to systematically investigate the formation and dissolution of light clusters (deuteron, $\rm{^3H}$, $\rm{^3He}$, $α$-particle) embedded in nuclear medium. The medium-modified cluster structures under the Pauli blocking and the picture of Mott transitions in nuclear medium are discussed in detail. We find that the weakly bound deuteron survives to higher densities as compared with the more compact $α$-particle in symmetric nuclear matter, with its r.m.s. radius expanding markedly prior to its dissolution. Moreover, the Mott density of $α$-particle is slightly lower in neutron-rich matter than in symmetric matter. These results may provide useful constraints for the formation of light clusters at nuclear surface and the cluster yields in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions.

## Key claim

The authors find that the weakly bound deuteron survives to higher densities than the more compact α-particle in symmetric nuclear matter, with its r.m.s. radius expanding markedly prior to dissolution; however, this conclusion is conditional on the specific variational approach and the choice of the Volkov nucleon-nucleon interaction, and the authors note that a sharp transition from bound state to continuum may be an artifact of the method.

## Method

The authors solve the in-medium Schrödinger equation for A=2-4 clusters (deuteron, ³H, ³He, α-particle) in momentum space using a Rayleigh-Ritz variational method with trial wave functions expanded in multi-Gaussian basis functions whose range parameters follow a geometric progression. The Pauli blocking effect from the surrounding nuclear medium is incorporated through occupation factors [1-f(p_i)] that exclude single-nucleon states below the Fermi surface, and through a modified nucleon-nucleon interaction that includes the isospin-dependent Volkov potential and Coulomb interaction between protons. For α-particles in asymmetric nuclear matter, the authors treat neutron and proton Fermi surfaces separately using isospin projection operators.

## Key numbers

- The Mott density of the α-particle in symmetric nuclear matter is n_α^Mott = 0.0321 fm⁻³, corresponding to approximately 0.2 n₀ (where n₀ is nuclear matter saturation density)
- The Mott density of ³He in symmetric nuclear matter is n_³He^Mott = 0.0126 fm⁻³
- The Mott density of ³H in symmetric nuclear matter is n_³H^Mott = 0.0131 fm⁻³
- The Mott density of the deuteron in symmetric nuclear matter is n_d^Mott = 0.0956 fm⁻³
- Prior to the Mott transition, the deuteron r.m.s. radius expands to about 4.80 fm, roughly 2.3 times its free-space value of 2.07 fm
- The Mott density of the α-particle in pure neutron matter (δ=1) is n_α,δ=1^Mott = 0.0293 fm⁻³, slightly lower than in symmetric matter
- The first-order Pauli blocking shifts Δ^Pauli at zero density are: deuteron 1161.13 MeV·fm³, ³H 3682.32 MeV·fm³, ³He 3501.23 MeV·fm³, α-particle 4733.94 MeV·fm³

## Context

This work extends the microscopic in-medium few-body approach previously applied to light clusters in free space by Meng (2023) and builds on the quantum statistical approach of Röpke (1982, 1983) and the quartetting wave function approach of Xu (2006, 2017) and Yang (2021, 2023) for α-clustering in finite nuclei. The results provide systematic Mott densities for A=2-4 clusters in a unified framework, which can be used to constrain cluster yields in intermediate-energy heavy-ion collisions as studied by Wang (2023) and to understand α-cluster formation at nuclear surfaces, particularly in neutron-rich environments relevant to neutron star crusts.
