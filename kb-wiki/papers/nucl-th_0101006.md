# Vacuum polarization effects in hyperon rich dense matter -- a nonperturbative treatment

> We derive the equation of state (EOS) for electrically charge neutral dense matter using Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model. This is carried out in a nonperturbative manner including quantum corrections for baryons through a realignment of vacuum with baryon-antibaryon condensates. This yields the results of relativistic Hartree approximation of summing over baryonic tadpole diagrams. The quantum corrections from the scalar meson is also taken into account in a similar way. This leads to a softening of the equation of state for the hyperonic matter. The formalism also allows us to do a self consistent calculation of the in-medium sigma meson mass. The effects of such quantum corrections on the composition of charge neutral dense matter is considered. The effect of the resulting EOS on the structure of the neutron stars is also studied.

# Vacuum polarization effects in hyperon rich dense matter, a nonperturbative treatment

## Key claim

The paper claims that including quantum corrections from both baryon-antibaryon condensates and scalar meson condensates in a nonperturbative framework softens the equation of state for hyperon-rich dense matter, but this softening and the resulting neutron star properties depend sensitively on the poorly known hyperon-meson coupling constants and on the value of the quartic scalar self-coupling λ_R.

## Method

The authors construct a trial ground state with baryon-antibaryon condensates (generalized to include hyperons) and scalar meson condensates within the Quantum Hadrodynamics (QHD) model, using thermofield dynamics to extend to finite density. They extremize the thermodynamic potential to determine condensate functions self-consistently, renormalize the divergent integrals, and solve the coupled equations for meson fields, chemical potentials, and Fermi momenta under baryon number conservation and electric charge neutrality. The resulting equation of state is then used to integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff equations for neutron star structure.

## Key numbers

- Nuclear matter saturation density ρ₀ = 0.193 fm⁻³ with binding energy −15.75 MeV
- Compressibility of nuclear matter: 401 MeV for λ_R = 1.8, 329 MeV for λ_R = 5, compared to the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) value
- Nucleon-rho meson couplings: g_{ρN} = 6.82 (RHA), 7.0 (λ_R = 1.8), 7.25 (λ_R = 5), from symmetry energy a_{sym} = 32.5 MeV
- Hyperon potential depths: U^N_Λ = U^N_Σ = −30 MeV, U^N_Ξ = −28 MeV
- Maximum neutron star masses: 1.717 M_⊙ (RHA), 1.6 M_⊙ (λ_R = 1.8), 1.42 M_⊙ (λ_R = 5)
- Corresponding radii at maximum mass: 12.0 km (RHA), 11.62 km (λ_R = 1.8), 11.1 km (λ_R = 5)
- Threshold for hyperon appearance: first Λ appears at ρ/ρ₀ ∼ 2
- Maximum isovector vector field ρ₀₃ reaches ∼ 15–20 MeV throughout the density regime

## Context

This work extends the nonperturbative treatment of vacuum polarization effects previously applied to nuclear matter by Mishra et al. (1999) to include strange baryons (Λ, Σ, Ξ) in dense matter relevant for neutron stars. It builds on the relativistic Hartree approximation (RHA) of Chin (1977) and the work of Prakash et al. (1997) on hyperonic matter, while also incorporating scalar meson quantum corrections that were not included in earlier studies of hyperon stars within Brueckner-Bethe-Goldstone theory by Baldo et al. (1998). The results enable self-consistent calculation of the in-medium sigma meson mass and show that quantum corrections reduce the maximum neutron star mass, which has implications for the possible presence of kaon condensed phases in neutron star cores.
