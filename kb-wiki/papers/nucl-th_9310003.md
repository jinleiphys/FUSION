# Experimental Nuclear Masses and the Ground State of Cold Dense Matter

> We study the consequences of recent progress in the experimental determination of masses of neutron rich nuclei for our knowledge of the ground state of cold dense matter. The most recent experimental data determine the ground state of cold dense matter up to $ ρ\simeq 10^{11}~{\rm g~cm^{-3}} $. The composition and the equation of state of the ground state of matter, in this density interval, are calculated.

# Experimental Nuclear Masses and the Ground State of Cold Dense Matter

## Key claim

The most recent experimental data on neutron-rich nuclear masses determine the ground state of cold dense matter up to approximately 10^11 g cm^-3, but the persistence of the N=50 closed shell effect at densities above 5×10^10 g cm^-3 is somewhat weakened when experimental uncertainties in the masses of 76Ni, 78Ni, 80Zn, and 82Zn are taken into account.

## Method

The authors use the Baym-Pethick-Sutherland (BPS) model of cold catalyzed matter, assuming a perfect crystal with a single nuclear species at lattice sites immersed in an electron gas, and minimize the Gibbs energy per nucleon using the Wigner-Seitz approximation. Experimental atomic masses from the evaluation of Audi (1992, 1993) are used for even-even nuclei, supplemented by theoretical masses from the mass formula of Möller (1992) for isotopes beyond experimental reach. The calculation includes improved treatment of electron screening effects and the exchange term in the electron energy density compared to the original BPS work.

## Key numbers

- Maximum density determined by experimental masses: ρ_max^exp = 9.64×10^10 g cm^-3 using the Möller (1992) mass formula, and ρ_max^exp = 1.08×10^11 g cm^-3 using the mass formula of Pearson and collaborators (1992)
- Neutron drip density: ρ_ND = 4.3×10^11 g cm^-3 (Möller 1992) and ρ_ND = 4.1×10^11 g cm^-3 (Pearson and collaborators 1992)
- Experimental mass uncertainties: ΔW(82Zn) = 400 keV, ΔW(76Ni) = 900 keV, ΔW(80Zn) = 170 keV, ΔW(78Ni) = 1100 keV
- At ρ = 2×10^9 g cm^-3, the ground state nucleus is 86Kr with experimental mass uncertainty (ΔW)_exp = 5 keV, giving (Δg)_exp = 60 eV
- At ρ ≈ 2×10^10 g cm^-3, the ground state nucleus is 82Ge with (ΔW)_exp = 155 keV, giving (Δg)_exp = 2 keV
- The last experimentally studied nucleus in the BPS paper (1971) was 84Se (Z/A=0.405) at ρ_max^exp = 8.2×10^9 g cm^-3

## Context

This work extends the classical BPS model of Baym, Pethick, and Sutherland (1971) by incorporating two decades of progress in experimental nuclear mass measurements of neutron-rich isotopes, particularly the evaluations of Wapstra and Audi (1985) and Audi (1992, 1993). The results enable determination of the ground state composition and equation of state of cold dense matter up to densities an order of magnitude higher than previously possible with experimental data. The paper also highlights that the strong N=82 shell effect dominating densities between ρ_max^exp and the neutron drip point may be an artifact of extrapolation via semiempirical mass formulae, as many-body calculations by Haensel, Zdunik, and Dobaczewski (1989) suggest the Z=40 subshell closure might be more important at these densities.
