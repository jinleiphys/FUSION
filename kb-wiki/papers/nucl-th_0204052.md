# Effect of transients in nuclear fission on multiplicity of prescission neutrons

> Transients in the fission of highly excited nuclei are studied in the framework of the Langevin equation. Time-dependent fission widths are calculated which show that after the initial transients, a steady flow towards the scission point is established not only for nuclei which have fission barriers but also for nuclei which have no fission barrier. It is shown from a comparison of the transient time and the fission life time that fission changes from a diffusive to a transient dominated process over a certain transition region as a function of the spin of the fissioning nucleus. Multiplicities of prescission neutrons are calculated in a statistical model with as well as without a single swoop description of fission and they are found to differ in the transition region. We however find that the difference is marginal and hence a single swoop picture of fission though not strictly valid in the transition region can still be used in the statistical model calculations.

## Key claim

The authors show that a diffusive (stationary) fission flow persists even for nuclei with no fission barrier, and that the transition from diffusive to transient-dominated fission occurs over a specific spin window; however, the difference in prescission neutron multiplicity between a full time-dependent treatment and a single-swoop approximation in this transition region is marginal, so the simpler single-swoop picture can still be used in statistical model calculations without significant error.

## Method

The authors solved the one-dimensional Langevin equation for the fission of \(^{200}\)Pb, using the chaos-weighted wall friction (with a shape-dependent chaoticity parameter) and a wall-and-window friction model. They extracted time-dependent fission widths from ensembles of Langevin trajectories and fitted them to a parametric form. They then performed statistical model calculations of prescission neutron multiplicity using either the full time-dependent fission widths or a single-swoop (fixed swooping-down time) description, and compared these results to a full dynamical Langevin calculation that included neutron and gamma evaporation.

## Key numbers

- The compound nucleus studied was \(^{200}\)Pb, formed in the \(^{19}\)F + \(^{181}\)Ta reaction at excitation energies of 78 MeV and 132 MeV.
- The critical angular momentum \(l_c\) at which the fission barrier vanishes was identified; the transition region where \(\tau / \tau_f\) changes from less than 1 to greater than 1 lies beyond \(l_c\).
- At an excitation energy of 78 MeV, the transition region lies beyond the range of the spin distribution; at 132 MeV, it lies well within the populated spin range.
- The difference in prescission neutron multiplicity between the statistical model with and without the single-swoop assumption was found to be marginal (a few percent) at higher excitation energies.
- The statistical model calculations were continued for a period of 300 \(\hbar\)/MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier analytical studies of fission transients by Grange (1984) and Weidenmuller and Zhang (1984), who derived expressions for time-dependent fission widths under simplifying assumptions. It also builds on the authors' own prior work (Chaudhuri and Pal, 2001) on fission widths using chaos-weighted wall friction for nuclei with fission barriers. The present paper extends that analysis to nuclei without fission barriers and quantifies the effect of the single-swoop approximation on prescission neutron multiplicities. The results enable the use of simpler statistical model calculations (with time-dependent widths or single-swoop descriptions) in place of more computationally expensive full dynamical Langevin simulations for predicting prescission neutron yields.
