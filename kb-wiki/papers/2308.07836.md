# $η$ and $η'$ mesons in nuclear matter and nuclei

> We present updated and extended results for the $η$- and $η'$-nucleus bound state energies, obtained by solving the Schrödinger and Klein-Gordon equations with complex optical potentials, for a wide range of nuclei. The $η$ and $η'$ nuclear potentials are obtained in the local density approximation from the mass shift of these mesons in nuclear matter, which is calculated within the quark-meson coupling model. Our results show that the $η$ and $η'$ mesons are expected to form mesic nuclei with all the nuclei considered. However, the signal for the formation of the $η$- and $η'$-mesic nuclei may be difficult to identify experimentally due to possible large widths.

## Key claim

The authors predict that both η and η' mesons are expected to form bound states (mesic nuclei) with all nuclei considered, but caution that experimental identification may be difficult due to possibly large absorption widths, which depend sensitively on the phenomenological parameter γ used to model the imaginary part of the optical potential.

## Method

The mass shifts of η and η' mesons in symmetric nuclear matter were calculated using the quark-meson coupling (QMC) model, which describes nucleons as MIT bags with quarks coupled to scalar σ and vector ω, ρ mean fields. These mass shifts were then used via the local density approximation to construct complex optical potentials for finite nuclei, and the Schrödinger and Klein-Gordon equations were solved numerically in momentum space to obtain bound state energies and absorption widths for seven nuclei ranging from ⁴He to ²⁰⁸Pb.

## Key numbers

- η mass shift at nuclear saturation density ρ₀: Δm_η(ρ₀) = -63.6 MeV
- η' mass shift at nuclear saturation density ρ₀: Δm_η'(ρ₀) = -61.3 MeV
- η-⁴He 1s bound state energy (Schrödinger equation, γ=0): -13.24 MeV
- η-²⁰⁸Pb 1s bound state energy (Schrödinger equation, γ=0): -57.59 MeV
- η'-⁴He 1s bound state energy (Schrödinger equation, γ=0): -23.72 MeV
- η'-²⁰⁸Pb 1s bound state energy (Schrödinger equation, γ=0): -57.90 MeV
- η-⁴He 1s width with γ=1.0 (Schrödinger equation): 41.45 MeV
- η-²⁰⁸Pb 1s width with γ=1.0 (Schrödinger equation): not listed in truncated text but ground state remains bound
- η' vacuum decay width: Γ_η'^vac = 0.188 MeV
- η vacuum decay width: Γ_η^vac = 1.31 keV
- Pseudoscalar mixing angle used: θ_P = -11.3 degrees
- Quark-meson coupling constants: (g_σ^q, g_ω^q, g_ρ^q) = (5.69, 2.72, 9.33)

## Context

This work extends previous QMC model calculations by Tsushima (1998) and the authors' own earlier studies (Cobos-Martinez 2017, 2020, 2022) by using an updated pseudoscalar mixing angle from the Particle Data Group and systematically including complex optical potentials to account for meson absorption. The results are compared with chiral unitary approach calculations by Inoue (2002) and Garcia-Recio (2002), where the present binding energies are more than twice those from energy-dependent potentials but closer to energy-independent results. The paper enables future experimental searches for η- and η'-mesic nuclei by providing predicted binding energies and widths across a wide mass range, though the authors note that overbinding may occur for light nuclei like ⁴He based on the analysis of Xie (2018) of the dd → η⁴He reaction.
