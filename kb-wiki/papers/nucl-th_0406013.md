# Consequences of self-consistency violations in Hartree-Fock random-phase approximation calculations of the nuclear breathing mode energy

> We provide for the first time accurate assessments of the consequences of violations of self-consistency in the Hartree-Fock based random phase approximation (RPA) as commonly used to calculate the energy $E_c$ of the nuclear breathing mode. Using several Skyrme interactions we find that the self-consistency violated by ignoring the spin-orbit interaction in the RPA calculation causes a spurious enhancement of the breathing mode energy for spin unsaturated systems. Contrarily, neglecting the Coulomb interaction in the RPA or performing the RPA calculations in the TJ scheme underestimates the breathing mode energy. Surprisingly, our results for the $^{90}$Zr and $^{208}$Pb nuclei for several Skyrme type effective nucleon-nucleon interactions having a wide range of nuclear matter incompressibility ($K_{nm} \sim 215 - 275$ MeV) and symmetry energy ($J \sim 27 - 37$ MeV) indicate that the net uncertainty ($δE_c \sim 0.3$ MeV) is comparable to the experimental one.

## Key claim
The net uncertainty in the calculated breathing mode centroid energy due to violations of self-consistency in HF-RPA is about 0.3 MeV for \(^{90}\)Zr and \(^{208}\)Pb, comparable to experimental uncertainties, but only because the spurious enhancement from neglecting the spin-orbit interaction and the underestimation from neglecting Coulomb or using the TJ scheme partially cancel each other.

## Method
The authors performed Hartree-Fock (HF) calculations with Skyrme interactions and compared fully self-consistent values of the constrained energy \(E_{con}\) and scaling energy \(E_s\) (obtained from energy moments \(m_{-1}\), \(m_1\), and \(m_3\) via CHF, double commutator, and generalized scaling approaches) with values from discretized RPA (DRPA) and continuum RPA (CRPA) calculations. They isolated each source of self-consistency violation by selectively turning off the spin-orbit interaction, the Coulomb interaction, or by using the TJ (isospin) scheme in the RPA particle-hole interaction, for symmetric and asymmetric nuclei.

## Key numbers
- For \(^{90}\)Zr, the ISGMR energy \(E_{con}\) from DRPA agrees with self-consistent values to within 0.3 MeV.
- For \(^{208}\)Pb, \(|\delta E_{con}| \leq 0.4\) MeV.
- The net uncertainty \(\delta E_c \sim 0.3\) MeV is comparable to experimental uncertainties of \(0.1 - 0.3\) MeV.
- The study used Skyrme interactions covering nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_{nm} \sim 215 - 275\) MeV and symmetry energy \(J \sim 27 - 37\) MeV.
- For spin-unsaturated nuclei (e.g., \(^{56}\)Ni, \(^{100}\)Sn), neglecting the spin-orbit interaction in RPA causes spurious enhancement equivalent to overestimating \(K_{nm}\) by 30-40 MeV.
- Numerical calculations used a box of 18 fm, mesh size \(\delta r = 0.02\) fm, smearing parameter \(\Gamma/2 = 5\times 10^{-2}\) MeV, and maximum particle-hole excitation energy \(E_{ph}^{max} = 950\) MeV.

## Context
This work extends the authors' earlier investigation of numerical approximations in HF-RPA (Agrawal 2003) and provides the first systematic separation of each source of self-consistency violation (spin-orbit, Coulomb, TJ scheme) for the isoscalar giant monopole resonance (ISGMR). It contrasts with earlier work by Colo and collaborators, who did not treat the components separately and did not consider the scaling energy \(E_s\). The results validate the use of HF-RPA for extracting the nuclear matter incompressibility \(K_{nm}\) from \(^{90}\)Zr and \(^{208}\)Pb, but caution that for spin-unsaturated or drip-line nuclei the cancellation may not hold.
