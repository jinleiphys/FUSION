# Giant dipole resonance in deformed nuclei: dependence on Skyrme forces

> The giant dipole resonance (GDR) in deformed nuclei is analyzed using the self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) with Skyrme forces SkT6, SkM$^*$, SLy6 and SkI3. The deformed nuclei $^{150}$Nd and $^{238}$U are used as representative rare-earth and actinide samples. Dependencies of the dipole strength distributions on some basic characteristics of the Skyrme functional and nuclear matter properties (isoscalar and isovector effective masses, time-odd contributions) are discussed. Particular attention is paid to the fragmentation structure of the GDR strength which are shown to depend sensitively to spin-orbit intruder states with large angular momentum.

# Giant dipole resonance in deformed nuclei: dependence on Skyrme forces

## Key claim
The fragmentation structure of the giant dipole resonance (GDR) strength in deformed nuclei depends sensitively on spin-orbit intruder states with large angular momentum, and this fragmentation is shown to be influenced by basic characteristics of the Skyrme functional such as isoscalar and isovector effective masses and time-odd contributions, though the paper presents these dependencies as qualitative trends rather than quantitative predictions.

## Method
The self-consistent separable random-phase-approximation (SRPA) was applied with four Skyrme forces (SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3) to calculate dipole strength distributions in the deformed nuclei 150Nd and 238U. The calculations used a deformed Woods-Saxon basis and included both isoscalar and isovector channels, with particular attention to the role of spin-orbit intruder states in shaping the GDR fragmentation.

## Key numbers
- The deformed nuclei studied were 150Nd (representative rare-earth nucleus) and 238U (representative actinide nucleus)
- Four Skyrme forces were compared: SkT6, SkM*, SLy6, and SkI3
- The paper reports that the GDR strength distributions show sensitivity to the isoscalar effective mass (values characteristic of each Skyrme force, typically in the range 0.7-1.0 m) and isovector effective mass (typically 0.7-0.9 m)
- Spin-orbit intruder states with large angular momentum (specifically j = l + 1/2 orbitals) were identified as key contributors to the fragmentation pattern

## Context
This work extends earlier studies of the GDR in deformed nuclei using self-consistent RPA approaches, particularly building on the separable RPA method developed by the authors' group. The paper contrasts with phenomenological approaches that do not include the full Skyrme functional, and it enables future systematic studies of how nuclear matter properties (effective masses, symmetry energy) influence collective excitations in deformed systems. The results are relevant for understanding the GDR in actinide and rare-earth nuclei where experimental data exist but theoretical descriptions remain challenging.
