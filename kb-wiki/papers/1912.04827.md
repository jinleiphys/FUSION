# Constraining the QCD phase diagram at finite temperature and density

> Neither the chiral limit nor finite baryon density can be simulated directly in lattice QCD, which severely limits our understanding of the QCD phase diagram. In this review I collect results for the phase structure in an extended parameter space of QCD, with varying numbers of flavours, quark masses, colours, lattice spacings, imaginary and isospin chemical potentials. Such studies help in understanding the underlying symmetries and degrees of freedom, and are beginning to provide a consistent picture constraining the possibilities for the physical phase diagram.

## Key claim

The review argues that lattice QCD studies in an extended parameter space (varying numbers of flavours, quark masses, colours, lattice spacings, and imaginary or isospin chemical potentials) are beginning to provide a consistent picture constraining the physical QCD phase diagram, but the author emphasizes that no unambiguous continuum extrapolations exist yet and that it remains an open question whether any first-order chiral transition survives in the continuum limit.

## Method

The author reviews lattice QCD simulations that map the order of the thermal transition as a function of quark masses (the Columbia plot) and its extensions to finite chemical potential, using multiple fermion discretizations (unimproved and improved staggered, Wilson, domain wall, overlap) on lattices with varying temporal extents \(N_\tau = 4, 6, 8, 10, 12, 16\). The analysis includes finite-size scaling studies of chiral condensates and susceptibilities, analytic continuation from imaginary chemical potential, Taylor expansion in \(\mu_B/T\), and reweighting techniques to approach the chiral limit and to study the radius of convergence of the pressure series.

## Key numbers

- The pseudo-critical temperature at the physical point is \(T_{pc}(0) = 156.5(1.5)\) MeV (Bazavov 2018).
- The extrapolated critical temperature in the chiral limit is \(T_c^0 = 132^{+3}_{-6}\) MeV, about 25 MeV lower than the physical-point pseudo-critical temperature (Ding 2019).
- The curvature coefficient for the pseudo-critical temperature at small baryon chemical potential is \(\kappa_2 = 0.0135(20)\) from imaginary \(\mu\) with stout-smeared staggered fermions, \(0.0145(25)\) from Taylor expansion with stout-smeared staggered, and \(0.016(5)\) from Taylor expansion with HISQ (Bonati 2018, Bazavov 2018).
- Light quark masses down to \(m_\pi \approx 55\) MeV were used in HISQ simulations on \(N_\tau = 6, 8, 10\) lattices (Ding 2019).
- Pion masses down to \(m_\pi \approx 50\) MeV were used in stout-smeared staggered simulations on \(N_\tau = 4\) in the Roberge-Weiss plane, showing second-order scaling rather than a first-order transition (Bonati 2018).
- At \(T = 220\) MeV, a near-degeneracy pattern consistent with \(SU(4)\) chiral spin symmetry multiplets is observed in spatial correlation functions (Rohrhofer 2019).
- The baryon number fluctuations \(\chi^B_{2n}\) are known up to \(2n = 8\) on \(N_\tau = 16\) lattices.

## Context

This review extends the long-standing Columbia plot framework (first mapped by Karsch 2001, deForcrand 2003) by incorporating results from finer lattices and multiple discretizations that consistently show the first-order chiral region shrinking toward the chiral limit, contradicting earlier expectations of a robust first-order transition for \(N_f \geq 3\). The work enables future continuum extrapolations to determine whether any first-order chiral transition survives, and provides constraints on the location of a possible QCD critical point at real baryon chemical potential, with current data showing no sign of criticality up to \(\mu_B \lesssim 2T\) (Giordano 2019). The review also connects to phenomenological models such as the Cluster Expansion Model (Vovchenko 2017) and the Rational Function Model (Almasi 2019), which reproduce lattice data without requiring a critical point.
