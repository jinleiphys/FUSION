# Constraining Transfer Cross Sections Using Bayes' Theorem

> Being able to rigorously quantify the uncertainties in reaction models is crucial to moving this field forward. Even though Bayesian methods are becoming increasingly popular in nuclear theory, they are yet to be implemented and applied in reaction theory problems. The purpose of this work is to investigate, using Bayesian methods, the uncertainties in the optical potentials generated from fits to elastic scattering data and the subsequent uncertainties in the transfer predictions. We also study the differences in two reaction models where the parameters are constrained in a similar manner, as well as the impact of reducing the experimental error bars on the data used to constrain the parameters. We use Bayes' Theorem combined with a Markov Chain Monte Carlo to determine posterior distributions for the parameters of the optical model, constrained by neutron-, proton-, and/or deuteron-target elastic scattering. These potentials are then used to predict transfer cross sections within the adiabatic wave approximation or the distorted-wave Born approximation. We study a number of reactions involving deuteron projectiles with energies in the range of $10-25$ MeV/u on targets with mass $A=48-208$. The case of $^{48}$Ca(d,p)$^{49}$Ca transfer to the ground state is described in detail. A comparative study of the effect of the size of experimental errors is also performed. Five transfer reactions are studied, and their results compiled in order to systematically identify trends. Uncertainties in transfer cross sections can vary significantly (25-100\%) depending on the reaction. While these uncertainties are reduced when smaller experimental error bars are used to constrain the potentials, this reduction is not trivially related to the error reduction. We also find smaller uncertainties when using the adiabatic formulation than when using distorted-wave Born approximation.

## Key claim

Bayesian methods can quantify uncertainties in transfer cross sections predicted from optical potentials fitted to elastic-scattering data, but the resulting uncertainties vary significantly (25–100%) depending on the reaction, and reducing experimental error bars on the elastic data does not produce a straightforward reduction in transfer cross-section uncertainties.

## Method

The authors used Bayes' Theorem with a Metropolis-Hastings Markov Chain Monte Carlo (MCMC) to sample posterior distributions for the nine parameters of the optical model (real volume, imaginary volume, and imaginary surface Woods-Saxon terms), constrained by neutron-, proton-, and/or deuteron-target elastic-scattering data. These parameter posteriors were then propagated through either the adiabatic wave approximation (ADWA) or the distorted-wave Born approximation (DWBA) to predict (d,p) and (d,n) transfer cross sections and their 95% confidence intervals.

## Key numbers

- Transfer cross-section uncertainties ranged from 25% to 100% depending on the reaction studied.

- Deuterons had energies in the range 10–25 MeV/u on targets with mass A = 48–208.
- The MCMC used a step-size scaling factor ε = 0.005, a burn-in of N_burn-in = 500 accepted steps, and a thinning of N_jump = 10 between recorded parameter sets.
- Experimental errors were initially set to 10% of the experimental data values for all data sets.

## Context

This work extends the frequentist uncertainty quantification of Lovell (2017) to a Bayesian framework, which allows systematic incorporation of prior knowledge and comparison of different reaction models. It is among the first applications of Bayesian methods to few-body reaction theory, following their successful use in effective field theories (e.g., Furnstahl 2015, Wesolowski 2016) and heavy-ion collisions (e.g., Bernhard 2016). The results enable rigorous uncertainty quantification for transfer reactions used to extract nuclear structure information from experiments.
