# Observation of New Resonance Structure in the Invariant Mass Spectrum of Two gamma-Quanta in dC-Interactions at Momentum 2.75 GeV/c per Nucleon

> A new resonance structure at M = 355 \pm 6 \pm 9 MeV is observed in the invariant mass spectrum of two gamma-quanta produced in the reaction d + C => gamma + gamma + x at momentum 2.75 GeV/c per nucleon. Preliminary estimates of its width and cross section are Gamma = 41 \pm 12 MeV and sigma_{gamma-gamma} = 0.6 mkb. The collected statistics is 2680 \pm 310 events of 1.5 10^6 triggered interactions of a total number 3 10^{12} dC-interactions.

## Key claim
A new resonance structure is observed in the invariant mass spectrum of two gamma-quanta from deuteron-carbon collisions at 2.75 GeV/c per nucleon, with the authors explicitly stating that the mass, width, and cross section are preliminary estimates requiring further experiments for confirmation and more accurate determination.

## Method
The experiment used an internal deuteron beam from the Nuclotron incident on a carbon target, with two arms of a 90-channel Cherenkov gamma-spectrometer (lead glass modules) measuring energies and emission directions of gamma-quanta. Events were selected requiring exactly two gamma-quanta with energies above 100 MeV, and combinatorial background was subtracted using event mixing (random sampling of gamma-quanta from different events). The invariant mass distribution of gamma-gamma pairs was fitted with Gaussian and Breit-Wigner functions to extract the resonance parameters.

## Key numbers
- Resonance mass: M = 355 ± 6 (stat) ± 9 (syst) MeV
- Resonance width: Γ = 41 ± 12 MeV
- Production cross section: σ_γγ ≈ 0.6 μb
- Collected statistics: 2680 ± 310 events from 1.5 × 10^6 triggered interactions out of 3 × 10^12 total dC-interactions
- Signal-to-background ratio in the 300-420 MeV invariant mass region: 2.7 × 10^(-2)
- Mean gamma-quanta energies in the resonance region (340-360 MeV): E1 ≈ 380 MeV, E2 ≈ 520 MeV
- Instrumental mass resolution (w_app) in the 340-360 MeV region: 52.6 MeV

## Context
This work extends the JINR physics program (initiated in 1997) searching for nontrivial structures in two-photon invariant mass spectra from nucleus-nucleus collisions at the Nuclotron, serving as a pilot study for future experiments at SIS-100/300 (GSI). The observed peak had not been seen in analogous analyses of other experiments (referenced as prior work from 1997-2005), possibly due to the significantly larger statistics in this dataset. The authors note that verification of the conclusions, measurement of quantum numbers, and more accurate determination of the resonance parameters require new dedicated experiments optimized for registration of gamma-gamma pairs with invariant masses 300-400 MeV.
