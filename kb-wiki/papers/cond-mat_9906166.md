# The Crossover from the Bulk to the Few-Electron limit in Ultrasmall Metallic Grains

> We study the properties of ultrasmall metallic grains with sizes in the range of 20 up to 400 electrons. Using a particle-hole version of the DMRG method we compute condensation energies, spectroscopic gaps, pairing parameters and particle-hole probabilities of the ground state wave function. The results presented in this paper confirm that the bulk superconducting regime (large grains) and the fluctuation dominated regime (small grains) are qualitative different, but show that the crossover between them is very smooth with no signs of critical level spacings separating them. We compare our DMRG results with the exact ones obtained with the Richardson solution finding complete agreement. We also propose a simplified version of the DMRG wave function, called the Particle-Hole BCS ansatz, which agrees qualitatively with the DMRG solution and illustrates what is lacking in the PBCS wave function in order to describe correctly the crossover. Finally we present a new recursive method to compute norms and expectation values with the PBCS wave function.

## Key claim

The crossover between the bulk superconducting regime (large grains) and the fluctuation-dominated regime (small grains) is very smooth with no signs of critical level spacings separating them, contradicting previous BCS and PBCS studies that predicted sharp crossovers or critical values of the level spacing.

## Method

The authors used a particle-hole version of the Density Matrix Renormalization Group (PHDMRG) method to study the BCS pairing Hamiltonian for ultrasmall metallic grains with sizes ranging from 20 to 400 electrons at half filling. They computed condensation energies, spectroscopic gaps, pairing parameters, and particle-hole probabilities of the ground state wave function, and compared these DMRG results with exact solutions from the Richardson method and with the projected BCS (PBCS) ansatz. They also proposed a simplified variational ansatz called the Particle-Hole BCS (PHBCS) state, which sits between PBCS and DMRG in accuracy.

## Key numbers

* The BCS coupling constant used for Al grains is λ = 0.224, as given by Braun and von Delft (1999).
* The DMRG calculations used m = 60 states kept, which was sufficient to study system sizes up to 400 energy levels with a relative error of 10⁻⁴ in condensation energies.
* For the PHBCS ansatz, it was sufficient to include ℓ values from 0 up to ℓ_max = 11 for system sizes Ω ≤ 400.
* The PBCS study by Braun and von Delft (1999) found critical level spacings of d_c⁰ ≈ 0.5Δ for even grains and d_c¹ ≈ 0.25Δ for odd grains, which the present work contradicts by finding no such critical values.
* The grand canonical BCS analysis predicted critical level spacings of d_c⁰ = 3.56Δ for even grains and d_c¹ = d_c⁰/4 for odd grains, as given by Dukelsky et al. (1999).

## Context

This work extends the study of ultrasmall metallic grains initiated by Anderson (1959) and revived by the experiments of Ralph, Black, and Tinkham (1997) on Al grains. It contradicts the grand canonical BCS results of Dukelsky et al. (1999) and the PBCS results of Braun and von Delft (1999), both of which predicted critical level spacings separating different regimes. The DMRG results agree completely with the exact Richardson solution (1963-1977), which had been overlooked in the condensed matter community until the recent developments in ultrasmall metallic grains. The work enables future studies using the PHBCS ansatz as a simplified variational approach that captures the essential physics of the crossover without requiring the full DMRG machinery.
