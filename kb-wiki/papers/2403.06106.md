# Global sensitivity analysis in Monte Carlo radiation transport

> We consider Global Sensitivity Analysis for Monte Carlo radiation transport applications. GSA is usually combined with Uncertainty Quantification, where the latter quantifies the variability of a model output in the presence of uncertain inputs and the former attributes this variability to the inputs. The additional noise inherent to MC RT solvers due to the finite number of particle histories presents an additional challenge to GSA and UQ, which are well-established for deterministic solvers. In this contribution, we apply variance deconvolution to the Saltelli method to address MC RT solver noise without having to over-resolve the MC RT simulation.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that variance deconvolution can be applied to Saltelli's sampling method for global sensitivity analysis to correctly estimate first-order and total Sobol' indices from Monte Carlo radiation transport solvers, without requiring the solver to be over-resolved to suppress its inherent stochastic noise.

## Method

The authors modified Saltelli's sampling method by incorporating variance deconvolution to separate parametric variance from solver-induced noise. For each sample point in the Saltelli matrices, they collected not only the Monte Carlo radiation transport estimate of the quantity of interest but also an estimate of the solver variance from the particle histories. They then derived modified estimators for first-order and total sensitivity indices that remove the contribution of solver noise from the conditional variance terms. The approach was tested on a one-dimensional radiation transport problem with three independent uncertain input parameters.

## Key numbers

- The test problem used three independent sources of parametric variance (d = 3 uncertain input parameters)
- The Saltelli method required N sampling realizations for each of the A and B matrices, plus N realizations for each of the d = 3 C_i matrices
- The variance deconvolution approach required collecting solver variance estimates from Neta particle histories at each sample point
- The modified approach accurately estimated first-order and total sensitivity indices for significantly less computational cost than the unmodified Saltelli method

## Context

This work extends the variance deconvolution approach previously developed by Clements et al. (2022) for uncertainty quantification to the global sensitivity analysis context. It addresses a gap in the literature where GSA methods are well-established for deterministic solvers but have not been adapted for stochastic solvers like Monte Carlo radiation transport codes. The approach enables practitioners to perform meaningful sensitivity analysis on Monte Carlo radiation transport simulations without needing to run excessively large numbers of particle histories, and a companion paper by Geraci et al. (2023) introduces an alternative polynomial chaos strategy for the same problem.
