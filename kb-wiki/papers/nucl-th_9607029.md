# Hydrodynamical Description of 200 A GeV/c S+Au Collisions: Hadron and Electromagnetic Spectra

> We study relativistic S+Au collisions at 200 A GeV/c using a hydrodynamical approach. We test various equations of state (EOSs), which are used to describe the strongly interacting matter at densities attainable in the CERN-SPS heavy ion experiments. For each EOS, suitable initial conditions can be determined to reproduce the experimental hadron spectra; this emphasizes the ambiguity between the initial conditions and the EOS in such an approach. Simultaneously, we calculate the resulting thermal photon and dielectron spectra, and compare with experiments. If one allows the excitation of resonance states with increasing temperature, the electro-magnetic signals from scenarios with and without phase transition are very similar and are not resolvable within the current experimental resolution. With regard to the CERES dilepton data, none of the EOSs considered, in conjunction with the standard leading order dilepton rates, succeed in reproducing the observed excess of dileptons below the rho peak. Our work, however, suggests that an improved measurement of the photon and dilepton spectra has the potential to strongly constrain the EOS.

## Key claim

The ambiguity between initial conditions and the equation of state (EOS) in hydrodynamical simulations of S+Au collisions at 200 A GeV/c is so severe that, for any EOS tested, suitable initial conditions can be found to reproduce the experimental hadron spectra; furthermore, when resonance excitation with increasing temperature is allowed, the electromagnetic signals from scenarios with and without a phase transition become very similar and are not resolvable with current experimental resolution.

## Method

The authors performed two-dimensional (radial and longitudinal) ideal-fluid hydrodynamical simulations of central S+Au collisions at 200 A GeV/c, using the SHASTA algorithm with a finite-difference method. They tested four equations of state: a pure hadronic resonance gas with a repulsive mean field (EOS H), an ideal gas of massless pions (EOS I), and two parametrizations of a hadron-to-quark-gluon-plasma phase transition (EOS A and EOS B) constructed via Maxwell construction with a bag constant and a mean-field repulsion parameter. Initial conditions were parametrized to span the range between full stopping (Landau) and scaling expansion (Bjorken), and were tuned separately for each EOS to fit the measured hadron spectra. Thermal photon and dielectron spectra were then computed from the hydrodynamical evolution and compared to WA80 and CERES data.

## Key numbers

- The total center-of-mass energy of the participant nucleons in the S+Au fireball is 970 GeV, with a total baryon number of 104 participants.
- For EOS A, the critical temperature is 165 MeV and the latent heat is 1.4 GeV/fm³; for EOS B, the critical temperature is 140 MeV and the latent heat is 0.8 GeV/fm³.
- An upper bound of about 250 MeV is deduced for the initial temperature from the single photon spectra of WA80.
- The freeze-out surface is defined at a constant energy density chosen so that the mean freeze-out temperature is approximately 140 MeV.

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamical studies of S+Au collisions at CERN-SPS energies (Venugopalan 1994, Katscher 1993) by simultaneously analyzing hadron, photon, and dilepton spectra to constrain the equation of state. The authors find that none of the EOSs considered, combined with standard leading-order dilepton rates, can reproduce the CERES dilepton excess below the rho peak, a result that contradicts the expectation that a phase transition would clearly modify the dilepton yield. The study enables future work by showing that improved measurements of photon and dilepton spectra have the potential to strongly constrain the EOS, and it highlights the need for more precise data to break the degeneracy between initial conditions and the EOS.
