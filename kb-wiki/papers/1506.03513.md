# Ab initio alpha-alpha scattering

> Processes involving alpha particles and alpha-like nuclei comprise a major part of stellar nucleosynthesis and hypothesized mechanisms for thermonuclear supernovae. In an effort towards understanding alpha processes from first principles, we describe in this letter the first ab initio calculation of alpha-alpha scattering. We use lattice effective field theory to describe the low-energy interactions of nucleons and apply a technique called the adiabatic projection method to reduce the eight-body system to an effective two-cluster system. We find good agreement between lattice results and experimental phase shifts for S-wave and D-wave scattering. The computational scaling with particle number suggests that alpha processes involving heavier nuclei are also within reach in the near future.

## Key claim

The paper reports the first *ab initio* calculation of alpha-alpha scattering, finding good agreement with experimental S-wave and D-wave phase shifts at next-to-leading order (NLO) and next-to-next-to-leading order (NNLO) in chiral effective field theory, but the results carry systematic uncertainties from the finite number of projection time steps and the use of a single lattice spacing.

## Method

The authors used lattice effective field theory with a spatial lattice spacing of 1.97 fm and a temporal lattice spacing of 1.32 fm, applying the adiabatic projection method to reduce the eight-body alpha-alpha system to an effective two-cluster system. They performed projection Monte Carlo simulations with auxiliary fields on a periodic cubic lattice of length 16 fm, computed the radial adiabatic Hamiltonian, and then extended it to a larger volume of 120 fm to extract scattering phase shifts by solving for standing wave modes with a hard spherical wall boundary.

## Key numbers

- Spatial lattice spacing: a = 1.97 fm; temporal lattice spacing: a_t = 1.32 fm.
- Periodic cubic lattice length: L = 16 fm; extended volume length: 120 fm.
- The calculated 8Be ground state at NNLO is bound, though only a small fraction of an MeV away from threshold.
- S-wave and D-wave phase shifts at NLO and NNLO show good agreement with experimental data from Heydenburg (1956), Nilson (1958), Tombrello (1963), and Afzal (1969).
- The computational scaling for the A1-body + A2-body problem is roughly (A1+A2)^2.

## Context

This work extends prior *ab initio* nuclear scattering calculations for light nuclei (Nollett 2006, Quaglioni 2008, Navratil 2011) and medium-mass nuclei (Hagen 2012) by addressing the challenge of projectile nuclei with more than a few nucleons. It uses the same lattice action previously applied to the Hoyle state of 12C (Epelbaum 2012) and the structure of 16O (Epelbaum 2013), and the adiabatic projection method developed in earlier work (Rupak 2013, Pine 2013, Elhatisari 2014). The results enable future *ab initio* studies of alpha processes involving heavier nuclei, such as 12C + 4He scattering and radiative capture, as well as carbon and oxygen burning in stellar evolution.
