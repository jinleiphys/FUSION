# $β$-decay properties of some astrophysically important Sc-isotopes

> In the late progressive stages of heavy stars, electron capture and $β^\pm$-decay are the governing processes. The weak rates are essential inputs for modeling the stages of high-mass stars before supernova explosions. As per results obtained from previous simulations, weak rates of Scandium isotopes contribute substantially in changing the lepton-to-baryon ratio ($Y_e$) of the nuclear matter in the core. In the present analysis, we report important $β^-$-decay properties of crucial Sc isotopes in an astrophysical environment with mass numbers $49 \leq A \leq 54$. The investigation includes Gamow-Teller (GT) strength distributions, terrestrial half-lives, and stellar rates of electron capture (EC) and $β^-$-decay reactions. The calculations are performed using the proton-neutron (pn) quasi-particle random phase approximation (QRPA) model over a wide temperature range ($10^7$-$3 \times 10^{10}$ K) and density range ($10^1 - 10^{11}$ g/cm$^3$). Additionally, we compare our calculated results with available experimental and theoretical data. A good agreement is observed between our calculated half-lives and experimentally measured values. Our weak $β^-$-decay and EC rates are compared with those from the Independent-Particle Model (IPM) and Large-Scale Shell Model (LSSM). At high stellar temperatures and densities, our calculated $β^-$-decay rates are smaller than those from the other models.

# Beta-decay properties of some astrophysically important Sc-isotopes

## Key claim

The pn-QRPA model produces beta-decay half-lives for Sc isotopes (A=49-54) that agree well with experimental data, but at high stellar temperatures and densities the calculated beta-decay rates are 1-3 orders of magnitude smaller than those from the Independent Particle Model (IPM) and Large-Scale Shell Model (LSSM), while the electron capture rates are 1-2 orders of magnitude larger than those models at high temperature.

## Method

The authors used the proton-neutron quasi-particle random phase approximation (pn-QRPA) model with a Nilsson single-particle basis, BCS pairing, and particle-particle and particle-hole Gamow-Teller residual interactions to compute GT strength distributions, terrestrial half-lives, and stellar beta-decay and electron capture rates for Sc isotopes with mass numbers 49 through 54. The calculations were performed over a temperature range of 10^7 to 3x10^10 K and a density range of 10 to 10^11 g/cm^3, with a standard quenching factor of 0.6 applied to the GT strengths.

## Key numbers

- Terrestrial half-life of 49Sc: 3522.29 s (pn-QRPA) vs. 3430.80 +/- 7.8 s (experimental)
- Terrestrial half-life of 50Sc: 102.52 s (pn-QRPA) vs. 102.50 +/- 0.5 s (experimental)
- Terrestrial half-life of 51Sc: 12.41 s (pn-QRPA) vs. 12.40 +/- 0.1 s (experimental)
- Terrestrial half-life of 52Sc: 8.53 s (pn-QRPA) vs. 8.20 +/- 0.2 s (experimental)
- Terrestrial half-life of 53Sc: 2.44 s (pn-QRPA) vs. 2.40 +/- 0.6 s (experimental)
- Terrestrial half-life of 54Sc: 0.54 s (pn-QRPA) vs. 0.53 +/- 0.015 s (experimental)
- For 49Sc at rhoYe=10^2 g/cm^3 and T9=3 K, the ratio of pn-QRPA beta-decay rate to LSSM rate is 1.49x10^1 and to IPM rate is 1.30x10^1
- For 50Sc at rhoYe=10^2 g/cm^3 and T9=10 K, the ratio of pn-QRPA EC rate to LSSM rate is 9.27x10^1 and to IPM rate is 1.34x10^2
- The pp and ph GT-interaction parameters chi and kappa were parameterized as chi = 8.54/A^0.7 MeV and kappa = 1.525/A^0.7 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous pn-QRPA studies of weak rates for iron-peak nuclei by Nabi and collaborators (Nabi 2005, 2007, 2008, 2017, 2019; Shehzadi 2020) and specifically follows a recent study by Nabi (2021) that identified Sc isotopes as important for changing the lepton-to-baryon ratio in presupernova cores. The results are compared with the earlier IPM calculations of Fuller (FFN) and LSSM calculations of Langanke and Martinez-Pinedo (2000, 2003), showing systematic differences attributed to the use of Brink's hypothesis in shell-model calculations and unquenched GT strengths in IPM. The calculated rates are intended to improve simulations of late stellar evolution stages before supernova explosions.
