# Comparative analysis of formalisms and performances of three different beyond mean-field approaches

> We investigate the differences and analogies between the equation of motion phonon method (EMPM) and second Tamm-Dancoff and random-phase approximations (STDA and SRPA) paying special attention to the problem of spurious center-of-mass (c.m.) admixtures. In order to compare them on an equal footing, we perform self-consistent calculations of the multipole strength distributions in selected doubly magic nuclei within a space including up to two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) basis states using the UCOM two-body intrinsic Hamiltonian and we explore the tools each approach supplies for removing the spurious c.m. admixtures. We find that the EMPM and STDA yield exactly the same results when the same intrinsic Hamiltonian is used and the coupling of the Hartree-Fock state with the 2p-2h space is neglected, but, unlike STDA and SRPA, the EMPM offers the possibility to completely remove c.m. admixtures.

## Key claim

The Equation of Motion Phonon Method (EMPM) and the Second Tamm-Dancoff Approximation (STDA) yield exactly the same results when the same intrinsic Hamiltonian is used and the coupling of the Hartree-Fock state with the 2p-2h space is neglected, but the EMPM uniquely offers the possibility to completely remove spurious center-of-mass admixtures, unlike STDA and SRPA.

## Method

The authors performed self-consistent calculations of multipole strength distributions in the doubly magic nuclei 16O, 40Ca, and 48Ca within a configuration space including up to two-particle-two-hole (2p-2h) basis states, using the UCOM two-body intrinsic Hamiltonian. They compared the EMPM, STDA, and SRPA formalisms on an equal footing by neglecting the coupling between the HF state and two-phonon states in the EMPM, and explored the tools each approach provides for removing spurious center-of-mass admixtures, including Gram-Schmidt orthogonalization and singular value decomposition (SVD).

## Key numbers

- In 40Ca RPA calculations with the intrinsic two-body kinetic energy operator T_int, the spurious center-of-mass state occurs at 0.09 MeV in a basis of N_max=6 (60 p-h states), while with the effective one-body operator T_int^(1) it appears at 0.86 MeV; in a basis of N_max=10, the respective values are 0.006 MeV and 0.322 MeV.
- A Lorentzian width of Gamma = 0.5 MeV was used for presenting strength functions.
- The TDA running sum accounts for 90% to 95% of the RPA energy weighted sum for all multipoles, except for the isovector E1 where it overestimates the RPA sum by approximately 10%.
- In 40Ca, the RPA low-lying octupole peak is more than 5 MeV below the corresponding TDA peak in energy.

## Context

This work extends the comparison of beyond-mean-field approaches initiated by earlier studies of SRPA (Gamba 2015, Gamba 2012) and the EMPM (AndLo, Bianco), and addresses the center-of-mass problem previously noted in SRPA (Papa14a). The finding that EMPM and STDA are equivalent within the 2p-2h space, but that only EMPM can fully remove spurious c.m. admixtures via SVD (DeGregorio 2021, DeGreg 2022), enables future calculations with correlated ground states and extension to three- and four-phonon spaces without spurious contamination.
