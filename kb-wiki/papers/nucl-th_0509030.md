# Signals of spinodal hadronization: strangeness trapping

> If the deconfinement phase transformation of strongly interacting matter is of first-order and the expanding chromodynamic matter created in a high-energy nuclear collision enters the corresponding region of phase coexistence, a spinodal phase separation might occur. The matter would then condense into a number of separate blobs, each having a particular net strangeness that would remain approximately conserved during the further evolution. We investigate the effect that such `strangeness trapping' may have on strangeness-related hadronic observables. The kaon multiplicity fluctuations are significantly enhanced and thus provide a possible tool for probing the nature of the phase transition experimentally.

## Key claim

If the deconfinement phase transition of strongly interacting matter is first-order and the expanding matter enters the region of phase coexistence, spinodal decomposition may cause the system to break into separate blobs with approximately conserved net strangeness; this "strangeness trapping" significantly enhances kaon multiplicity fluctuations, providing a possible experimental probe of the phase transition nature, though the authors note that several conditions must be met for this scenario to occur and that the existence of a first-order phase transition is not yet well established theoretically.

## Method

The authors adopt a schematic scenario where an expanding quark-gluon plasma enters the phase coexistence region and undergoes spinodal breakup into blobs of equal size (typically 50 fm³). The net strangeness of each blob is sampled from a Poisson distribution of strange quarks and antiquarks at the plasma temperature, then canonically conserved through hadronization. The resulting hadron gas is described by a canonical ensemble with the fixed blob strangeness, using 124 hadronic species from pions through the Omega baryon, with baryon number and charge treated grand-canonically using biased chemical potentials adjusted for each blob's strangeness. Results are compared against a standard grand-canonical reference scenario where the system remains macroscopically uniform.

## Key numbers

- At μB=0 with Tq=170 MeV, the width of the strangeness distribution in a 50 fm³ blob is σS0=5.74
- At μB=400 MeV with Tq=150 MeV, the width decreases to σS0=4.65
- The average K+ multiplicity enhancement from strangeness trapping amounts to 2% at μB=400 MeV
- In the restricted canonical scenario (S0=0 only), the variance suppression is 8-10% compared to grand-canonical
- The expansion factor χ=Vh/Vq is taken as χ≈3, with a crossover where plasma and hadron gas degeneracies balance occurring at χ≈3.3
- At μB=300 MeV, doubling χ from 2 to 4 reduces the normalized K+/π+ variance by less than 10%

## Context

This work extends studies of spinodal decomposition in nuclear collisions, following earlier work on baryon fluctuations by Bower and Gavin and on N-body kinematic correlations by Randrup. The authors specifically address the enhancement of the K/π ratio reported at beam energies of 20-30 AGeV at the SPS by NA49, which represents the only non-monotonic behavior seen so far in high-energy heavy-ion collisions. The results suggest that fluctuations in the K+/π+ ratio may offer a suitable observable for probing clumping-induced strangeness trapping, though the authors note that subsequent electroweak decays must be addressed before quantitative comparison with data can be made.
