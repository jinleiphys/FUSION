# Radiative decay of the Delta(1700)

> Electromagnetic properties provide information about the structure of strongly interacting systems and allow for independent tests of hadronic models. The radiative decay of the Delta(1700) is studied, which appears dynamically generated in a coupled channel approach from the rescattering of the (3/2^+) decuplet of baryons with the (0^-) octet of pseudoscalar mesons. The radiative decay is predicted from the well-known couplings of the photon to the mesons and hadrons which constitute this resonance in the dynamical picture.

## Key claim

The radiative decay of the Delta(1700) is predicted from the well-known couplings of the photon to the mesons and baryons that constitute this resonance in a dynamical coupled-channel picture, without introducing new free parameters for the electromagnetic transition; the prediction relies on the hypothesis that the Delta(1700) is dynamically generated from the rescattering of the (3/2+) decuplet of baryons with the (0-) octet of pseudoscalar mesons.

## Method

The coupled-channel model from Sarkar (2004) for the Delta(1700) in the channels Delta(1232)pi, Sigma*(1385)K, and Delta(1232)eta was extended by including the (pi N) channel in d-wave, with transition strengths fitted to the partial wave analysis of Arndt (2006). The phototransition amplitude for Delta(1700) -> gamma N was then calculated from one-loop diagrams where the photon couples to the meson or baryon constituents of the resonance, using gauge-invariant loop functions derived from the same coupled-channel T-matrix.

## Key numbers

- Pole position of the Delta(1700) from fit 1: 1707 - i 160 MeV (compared to PDB range 1620-1680 - i (160-240) MeV)
- Coupling strengths from fit 1: |g_Delta pi| = 1.4, |g_Sigma*K| = 4.0, |g_Delta eta| = 2.6, |g_(pi N)_d| = 0.84
- The original model without (pi N)_d gave pole position 1827 - i 108 MeV and couplings |g_Delta pi| = 1.0, |g_Sigma*K| = 3.4, |g_Delta eta| = 2.2
- The cross section for gamma p -> K0 pi+ Lambda increases by a factor of 1.4 with the new couplings from fit 1
- The cross section for gamma p -> pi0 K0 Sigma+ also changes by a factor close to 1.4
- The cross section for gamma p -> pi0 eta p stays practically the same (10% decrease)

## Context

This work extends the dynamical generation approach of Sarkar (2004) for the Delta(1700) by including the (pi N)_d channel and by calculating the radiative decay from first principles, following the method previously applied to the Lambda*(1520) by Doring (2006). The predictions for strong couplings to Delta eta and Sigma*K had already shown good agreement with photoproduction data from ELSA/Bonn (Doring 2005, 2006), and the present radiative decay calculation provides an independent test of the dynamical generation hypothesis. The results enable future studies of photoproduction reactions where the Delta(1700) contributes, now using a fully theoretical electromagnetic transition instead of relying on experimental helicity amplitudes.
