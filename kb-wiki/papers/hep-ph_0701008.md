# Microscopic 8-quark study of the antikaon nucleon nucleon systems

> We study the possibility to bind eight quarks in a molecular hadronic system composed of two nucleons and an antikaon, with the quantum numbers of a hexaquark flavour, in particular with strangeness -1, isospin 1/2, parity -, baryonic number 2 and two possible spins, 0 or 1. These exotic hadrons are motivated by the deuteron, a proton-neutron boundstate, and by the model of the Lambda(1405) as an antikaon proton boundstate. We discuss the possible production of this hadron in the experiments which are presently investigating hot topics like the Theta+ pentaquark or the K- deeply bound in nuclei. The K- N interactions and the coupling to other channels are computed microscopically from a confining and chiral invariant quark model resulting in local plus separable Gaussian potentials. The N N interactions used here are the state of the art Nijmegen potentials. The binding energy and the decay rate of the K- N and K- N N systems are computed with configuration space variational methods. The only systems that bind with our microscopic interaction are the K- N in the I=0 channel and the K- N N in the S=0 channel.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a bound state of an antikaon and two nucleons (K-N-N) exists only in the spin S=0 channel, not in the S=1 channel, based on a microscopic quark-model calculation; the abstract's statement that "the only systems that bind" is qualified by the full text's finding that the S=1 system almost binds but requires an arbitrary increase of the K-N attraction by a factor of 1.14 to 1.35 to achieve binding.

## Method

The author computes the K-N interaction microscopically from a confining and chiral-invariant quark model using the Resonating Group Method (RGM), producing local plus separable Gaussian potentials. The N-N interaction is taken from the state-of-the-art Nijmegen REID93 potential. Binding energies and decay widths for the K-N and K-N-N systems are computed by diagonalizing the Hamiltonian in configuration space using variational methods with Laguerre polynomial bases and finite difference methods, including coupled-channel effects to pi-Sigma and pi-Lambda channels via the substitution method.

## Key numbers

- K-N I=0 (Lambda-like) resonance binding energy: M - m_K - m_N in the range [-23.8, -1.7] MeV, with width Gamma in [3.5, 7.0] MeV (using PCAC ratio c_pi/c_K = 0.56)
- With increased chiral coupling c_pi/c_K = 1, K-N I=0 binding energy: M - m_K - m_N in [-42.2, -7.7] MeV, width Gamma in [11.2, 23.0] MeV
- K-N-N S=0 binding energy: M - m_K - 2m_N in [-53.0, -14.2] MeV, with decay width Gamma in [13.6, 28.3] MeV to pi-Sigma-N and pi-Lambda-N channels
- Hyperfine splitting parameter: <V_hyp> = 390 MeV from baryon spectroscopy
- Hadronic size parameter alpha in [0.34, 0.39] fm, determined from K-N S=1 repulsive channel phase shifts
- c_K^2 <V_hyp> in the interval [200, 300] MeV
- For the S=1 K-N-N system, binding would require increasing the K-N attraction by a factor in [1.14, 1.35], yielding a vanishing binding energy and width Gamma in [8.0, 9.8] MeV

## Context

This work extends the quark-model approach to antikaon-nucleon interactions previously developed by Bicudo and collaborators (Bicudo 1987, 1995, 2004), applying it to the three-body K-N-N system motivated by the deuteron and the Lambda(1405) as a K-N bound state. The results contradict recent theoretical calculations by Ivanov (2005), Yamazaki (2006), and Shevchenko (2006) that found deeper and wider K-N-N resonances using effective local or separable potentials; the author attributes this difference to the shorter range and weaker K-N potential in the microscopic quark model. The work enables experimental searches for the K-N-N system at facilities like RCNP, JLab, KEK, and DAFNE, where the FINUDA collaboration has already reported evidence for such a state.
