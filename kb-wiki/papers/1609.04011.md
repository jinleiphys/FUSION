# The Analytic Structure of Non-Global Logarithms: Convergence of the Dressed Gluon Expansion

> Non-global logarithms (NGLs) are the leading manifestation of correlations between distinct phase space regions in QCD and gauge theories and have proven a challenge to understand using traditional resummation techniques. Recently, the dressed gluon expansion was introduced that enables an expansion of the NGL series in terms of a "dressed gluon" building block, defined by an all-orders factorization theorem. Here, we clarify the nature of the dressed gluon expansion, and prove that it has an infinite radius of convergence as a solution to the leading logarithmic and large-$N_c$ master equation for NGLs, the Banfi-Marchesini-Smye (BMS) equation. The dressed gluon expansion therefore provides an expansion of the NGL series that can be truncated at any order, with reliable uncertainty estimates. In contrast, manifest in the results of the fixed-order expansion of the BMS equation up to 12-loops is a breakdown of convergence at a finite value of $α_s$log. We explain this finite radius of convergence using the dressed gluon expansion, showing how the dynamics of the buffer region, a region of phase space near the boundary of the jet that was identified in early studies of NGLs, leads to large contributions to the fixed order expansion. We also use the dressed gluon expansion to discuss the convergence of the next-to-leading NGL series, and the role of collinear logarithms that appear at this order. Finally, we show how an understanding of the analytic behavior obtained from the dressed gluon expansion allows us to improve the fixed order NGL series using conformal transformations to extend the domain of analyticity. This allows us to calculate the NGL distribution for all values of $α_s$log from the coefficients of the fixed order expansion.

## Key claim

The dressed gluon expansion of the Banfi-Marchesini-Smye (BMS) equation for non-global logarithms (NGLs) has an infinite radius of convergence as a solution to the leading logarithmic and large-\(N_c\) master equation, in contrast to the fixed-order expansion in \(\alpha_s\) which breaks down at a finite value of \(\alpha_s \log\). This convergence is proven by relating the dressed gluon expansion to the method of successive approximations for solving differential equations, and the finite radius of convergence of the fixed-order expansion is explained by the dynamics of the buffer region near the jet boundary.

## Method

The authors prove that the dressed gluon expansion converges absolutely to the full solution of the BMS equation by relating it to the method of successive approximations, a technique for solving ordinary differential equations that produces convergent series. They analyze the analytic structure of the dressed gluon itself, showing it has a radius of convergence \(|L| \leq 1\) due to a singularity at \(L = -1\) in the complex plane, which arises from the buffer region where radiation near the jet boundary is suppressed. Using this understanding, they apply conformal transformations to reorganize the fixed-order perturbative series, extending its domain of analyticity to calculate the NGL distribution for all values of \(L\) from the fixed-order coefficients.

## Key numbers

* The dressed gluon has a radius of convergence of \(|L| \leq 1\) when expanded in \(\alpha_s\), where \(L = \frac{\alpha_s}{\pi} N_c \log \frac{m_H}{m_L}\) for the heavy and light hemisphere masses \(m_H\) and \(m_L\).
* The fixed-order expansion of the BMS equation up to 12-loop order shows a breakdown of convergence at a finite value of \(\alpha_s \log\), with a barrier to continuation beyond \(L = 1\).
* The resummation factor \(U_{abj}(L)\) in the BMS equation is bounded as \(U_{abj}(L) \leq 2^L\) for all dipoles.
* The buffer region \(B\) is defined as \(\{j \in J: U_{abj}(L) < 1\}\), where \(J\) is the jet region, and its complement \(\bar{B}\) is the active jet region.

## Context

This work extends the dressed gluon expansion introduced by Larkoski, Moult, and Neill (2015) by proving its convergence and clarifying its relation to the method of successive approximations, which was not formalized in the original paper. It contrasts with the fixed-order expansion of the BMS equation studied by Dasgupta and Salam (2001) and Banfi, Marchesini, and Smye (2002), which has a finite radius of convergence due to the buffer region identified in early studies of NGLs by Dasgupta and Salam (2002). The results enable reliable truncation of the dressed gluon expansion with well-defined uncertainty estimates and allow calculation of the NGL distribution for all values of \(\alpha_s \log\) using conformal transformations on the fixed-order series.
