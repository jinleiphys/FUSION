# Inclusive breakup of three-body projectiles: A unified four-body framework for pair-detected and single-particle observables

> Inclusive breakup of three-body projectiles $a=i+j+k$ on a target $A$ admits two distinct inclusive observables: detection of a correlated pair $b=(ij)$ with $k+A$ unresolved, and detection of a single particle $i$ with $jk+A$ unresolved. A four-body DWBA sum-rule framework is derived for both channels from a common Hamiltonian. For the pair-detected channel, the unresolved propagator remains the two-body $k+A$ Green function and all three-body projectile effects enter through a pair-projected source built from $Φ_a$; a reference pair-target optical interaction splits this source into a target-elastic reference part and an explicit pair-target coupling part, yielding a state-resolved semi-inclusive coincidence observable and an amplitude-level diagnostic of the two-body cluster approximation. For the single-particle channel, the unresolved propagator is the three-body $jk+A$ resolvent, whose reference-channel Feshbach reduction reproduces the Carlson-Frederico-Hussein (CFH) absorptive kernel $W_j+W_k+W_{3B}$; the additional source $V_{iA}-U_{iA}$ drives target excitations, with its direct $g_Q$ component yielding target-excited CFH-like kernels under a diagonal-intermediate-states approximation. Prior forms are derived for both partitions, with reduced post-prior identities at the single-channel level (pair-detected) and at the CFH-optical level (single-particle). For $^{6}$Li$=α+n+p$, the explicit deuteron-target coupling has an E1/E2/monopole tidal structure evaluated on the full three-body wave function. The framework is validated by recovery of the two-body IAV, CFH, and detected-cluster limits, and separates exact DWBA identities from later optical and diagonal-target approximations.

## Key claim

The paper derives a unified four-body DWBA sum-rule framework for inclusive breakup of three-body projectiles that treats both pair-detected and single-particle observables from a common Hamiltonian, but the derivation relies on a diagonal-intermediate-states approximation for target-excited contributions in the single-particle channel, and the exact DWBA sum rule is separated from later optical and diagonal-target approximations.

## Method

The author starts from the four-body Hamiltonian for a projectile \(a = i + j + k\) on target \(A\) and constructs post-form DWBA transition amplitudes for two distinct exit-channel partitions: (i) detection of a correlated pair \(b = (ij)\) with the \(k + A\) system unresolved, and (ii) detection of a single particle \(i\) with the \(jk + A\) system unresolved. For the pair-detected channel, the unresolved propagator remains the two-body \(k + A\) Green's function, and the source is built by projecting the three-body projectile wave function onto the pair internal state \(\phi_\alpha\); a reference pair-target optical interaction splits the source into a target-elastic reference part and an explicit coupling part. For the single-particle channel, the unresolved propagator is the three-body \(jk + A\) resolvent, whose Feshbach reduction reproduces the Carlson-Frederico-Hussein (CFH) absorptive kernel \(W_j + W_k + W_{3B}\), with additional target-excited CFH-like kernels obtained under the diagonal-intermediate-states approximation.

## Key numbers

* For \(^{6}\mathrm{Li} = \alpha + n + p\), the explicit deuteron-target coupling \(V_{dA} - U_{dA}\) has an E1/E2/monopole tidal structure, with pair moments evaluated on the full three-body projectile wave function.
* The Jacobi transformation between the two coordinate sets has unit determinant (\(\det\mathcal J = 1\)), preserving the internal volume element.
* The projectile binding energy \(\epsilon_a > 0\) enters the total energy as \(E = E_a - \epsilon_a\) (with target ground-state energy set to zero).

## Context

This work extends the two-body IAV (Ichimura-Austern-Vincent) sum-rule formalism, previously limited to two-body projectiles like \((d,p)\) and \((^{6}\mathrm{Li}, \alpha)\) reactions (Potel 2015, Lei 2015), to three-body projectiles such as Borromean nuclei \(^{6}\mathrm{He}\) and \(^{11}\mathrm{Li}\) or \(^{9}\mathrm{Be}\). It generalizes the CFH framework (Carlson, Frederico, Hussein 2017) for single-particle inclusive observables by adding target-excited CFH-like kernels and a reduced post-prior identity, and provides the first IAV treatment of the pair-detected channel, which prior work (Villanueva 2024) handled only with frozen-pair or two-body cluster approximations. The framework enables quantitative assessment of the two-body cluster approximation's validity and provides a tractable complement to exact four-body Yakubovsky calculations.
