# Nucleon isovector structure functions in (2+1)-flavor QCD with domain wall fermions

> We report on numerical lattice QCD calculations of some of the low moments of the nucleon structure functions. The calculations are carried out with gauge configurations generated by the RBC and UKQCD collaborations with (2+1)-flavors of dynamical domain wall fermions and the Iwasaki gauge action ($β= 2.13$). The inverse lattice spacing is $a^{-1} = 1.73$ GeV, and two spatial volumes of ((2.7{\rm fm})^3) and ((1.8 {\rm fm})^3) are used. The up and down quark masses are varied so the pion mass lies between 0.33 and 0.67 GeV while the strange mass is about 12 % heavier than the physical one. The structure function moments we present include fully non-perturbatively renormalized iso-vector quark momentum fraction, (< x >_{u-d}), helicity fraction, (< x >_{Δu - Δd}), and transversity, (< 1 >_{δu - δd}), as well as an unrenormalized twist-3 coefficient, (d_1). The ratio of the momentum to helicity fractions, (< x >_{u-d}/< x >_{Δu - Δd}), does not show dependence on the light quark mass and agrees well with the value obtained from experiment. Their respective absolute values, fully renormalized, show interesting trends toward their respective experimental values at the lightest quark mass. A prediction for the transversity, (0.7 < < 1 >_{δu -δd} < 1.1), in the (\bar{\rm MS}) scheme at 2 GeV is obtained. The twist-3 coefficient, (d_1), though yet to be renormalized, supports the perturbative Wandzura-Wilczek relation.

## Key claim

The ratio of the isovector quark momentum fraction to helicity fraction, ⟨x⟩_{u-d}/⟨x⟩_{Δu-Δd}, shows no dependence on light quark mass and agrees with experiment, but the absolute values of these moments only show trends toward experimental values at the lightest quark mass studied (m_π ≈ 0.33 GeV), not a definitive agreement.

## Method

The authors performed (2+1)-flavor lattice QCD calculations using dynamical domain wall fermions with the Iwasaki gauge action at β=2.13, corresponding to inverse lattice spacing a^{-1}=1.73 GeV. They computed nucleon three-point correlation functions at zero momentum transfer to extract bare matrix elements for the isovector quark momentum fraction, helicity fraction, transversity, and twist-3 coefficient d_1, then non-perturbatively renormalized the first three quantities using the Rome-Southampton RI-MOM scheme with conversion to the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV.

## Key numbers

* Inverse lattice spacing: a^{-1} = 1.73(3) GeV
* Pion mass range: 0.33 to 0.67 GeV (four values: 0.33, 0.42, 0.56, 0.67 GeV)
* Two spatial volumes: (2.7 fm)^3 and (1.8 fm)^3
* Strange quark mass: about 12% heavier than physical value
* Renormalization factor for ⟨x⟩_{u-d}: Z^{MS-bar}_{⟨x⟩_q}(2 GeV) = 1.15(4)
* Renormalization factor for ⟨x⟩_{Δu-Δd}: Z^{MS-bar}_{⟨x⟩_{Δq}}(2 GeV) = 1.15(3)
* Prediction for transversity: 0.7 < ⟨1⟩_{δu-δd} < 1.1 in the MS-bar scheme at 2 GeV
* Heavier quark mass points for ⟨x⟩_{u-d}: roughly constant at ~0.26, about 70% higher than the experimental value of ~0.15

## Context

This work extends previous lattice calculations of nucleon structure function moments by the RBC and UKQCD collaborations, using improved statistics and larger volumes compared to earlier studies such as Orginos et al. (2005) and Lin et al. (2008). The results enable direct comparison with experimental determinations of nucleon structure from deep inelastic scattering, and the transversity prediction provides a target for upcoming measurements at RHIC and other facilities. The study also investigates finite-volume effects that were previously found to be severe for elastic form factors like the axial charge (Yamazaki et al., 2008, 2009), finding that the inelastic moments may be less affected.
