# Unitary Transformations in Quantum Field Theory and Bound States

> Finding the eigenstates of the total Hamiltonian H or its diagonalization is the important problem of quantum physics. However, in relativistic quantum field theory (RQFT) its complete and exact solution is possible for a few simple models only. Unitary transformations (UT's) considered in this survey do not diagonalize H, but convert H into a form which enables us to find approximately some H eigenstates. During the last years there have appeared many papers devoted to physical applications of such UT's. Our aim is to present a systematic and self-sufficient exposition of the UT method. The two general kinds of UT's are pointed out, distinct variations of each kind being possible. We consider in detail the problem of finding the simplest H eigenstates for interacting mesons and nucleons using the so-called ``clothing'' UT and Okubo's UT. These UT's allow us to suggest definite approaches to the problem of two-particle (deuteron-like) bound states in RQFT. The approaches are shown to yield the same two-nucleon quasipotentials in the first nonvanishing approximation. We demonstrate how the particle mass renormalization can be fulfilled in the framework of the ``clothing'' procedure. Besides the UT of the Hamiltonian we discuss the accompanying UT of the Lorentz boost generators.

## Key claim

The paper argues that unitary transformations (UTs) which do not fully diagonalize the Hamiltonian can still be used to find approximate eigenstates of the total Hamiltonian in relativistic quantum field theory, specifically by converting the Hamiltonian into a form where certain "bad" terms are eliminated order by order in the coupling constant. The key hedge is that the clothing procedure described yields only approximate eigenstates for the physical vacuum and one-particle states, and that the two-nucleon quasipotentials derived from the clothing UT and Okubo's UT coincide only in the first nonvanishing approximation.

## Method

The authors develop two unitary transformation approaches for interacting meson and nucleon fields with Yukawa coupling: the "clothing" procedure and Okubo's block-diagonalization method. In the clothing procedure, new creation and destruction operators (clothed operators) are introduced via a unitary transformation \(W = \exp R\), where the antihermitian operator \(R\) is chosen to eliminate "bad" terms (those that prevent the no-particle and one-particle states from being Hamiltonian eigenstates) order by order in the coupling constant \(g\). The Okubo UT is applied to reduce the Hamiltonian matrix to block-diagonal form, decoupling the nucleon subspace from its complement in Fock space. Both methods are used to derive effective two-nucleon quasipotentials for bound state problems.

## Key numbers

- The condition for solvability of the clothing problem is \(\mu < 2m\), where \(\mu\) is the meson physical mass and \(m\) is the fermion physical mass; this ensures the one-meson state is stable against decay into a fermion-antifermion pair.
- The meson mass counterterm condition is \(\mu^2 = \mu_0^2 + 4t_{\mathbf{k}}\), where \(t_{\mathbf{k}}\) is a quantity determined by the commutator \([R,V]\) (Eq. A.20), though the integral for \(t_{\mathbf{k}}\) is quadratically divergent.
- The mass counterterms \(\delta\mu^2 \equiv \mu_0^2 - \mu^2\) and \(\delta m \equiv m_0 - m\) are expanded in series: \(\delta\mu^2 = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} g^{2n}(\delta\mu^2)_{2n}\) and \(\delta m = \sum_{n=1}^{\infty} g^{2n}(\delta m)_{2n}\).
- The interaction \(V\) is proportional to the coupling constant \(g\) (first order), while the mass counterterm \(M_{ren}\) is of order \(g^2\).

## Context

This review extends the unitary transformation method first applied in quantum field theory by Wentzel (1946) and Heitler (1954), and later developed by Gresch (1958), Schweber (1961), and Faddeev (1963). It builds on the Okubo approach (1954) for block-diagonalizing Hamiltonians and the clothing formalism for defining physical particles. The work enables the derivation of effective nucleon-nucleon and meson-nucleon quasipotentials for bound state problems such as the deuteron, providing a framework that connects field-theoretic Hamiltonians to Schroedinger-like equations. The approach is contrasted with the Bethe-Salpeter formalism and its three-dimensional versions, offering a more direct link between Hamiltonian eigenvectors and bound state wavefunctions.
