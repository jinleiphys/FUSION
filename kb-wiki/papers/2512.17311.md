# From closed shells to open shells: Coupled-cluster calculations of atomic nuclei

> Coupled-cluster theory is a powerful tool for first-principles calculations of atomic nuclei, enabling accurate predictions of nuclear observables across the Segrè chart. While coupled-cluster computations are especially efficient at shell closures, extensions have been developed to tackle open-shell nuclei, by exploiting the equation-of-motion method or by expanding the coupled-cluster wave function on top of a symmetry-breaking (either deformed or superfluid) reference state. In this study, we provide a comprehensive comparison of these different formulations applied to the calcium and nickel isotopes using nuclear two- and three-body interactions from chiral effective field theory. Based on ground-state energies, two-neutron separation energies, and two-neutron shell gaps, different coupled-cluster computations - based on symmetry-broken reference states and equation-of-motion techniques - offer consistent descriptions of bulk properties across medium-mass isotopic chains.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that different coupled-cluster formulations, based on symmetry-broken reference states (deformed Hartree-Fock or spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov) and equation-of-motion techniques, provide consistent descriptions of bulk properties across medium-mass isotopic chains, but the comparison is limited to ground-state energies, two-neutron separation energies, and two-neutron shell gaps, with the caveat that symmetry restoration (angular momentum and particle number projection) is not included in the calculations.

## Method

The authors performed coupled-cluster calculations with singles and doubles (CCSD) on calcium and nickel isotopes using two chiral effective field theory interactions: EM 1.8/2.0 and ΔNNLO_GO(394). Three variants were compared: equation-of-motion coupled-cluster (EOM-CC) with up to 3p-1h or 1p-3h configurations from a spherical closed-shell reference, Bogoliubov coupled-cluster (BCCSD) using a spherical Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov reference breaking particle-number conservation, and deformed coupled-cluster (deformed CCSD) using an axially-symmetric deformed Hartree-Fock reference breaking rotational symmetry. Three-body forces were included via the normal-ordered two-body approximation, and all calculations used a harmonic oscillator basis with 13 major shells (emax=12) and oscillator frequency ℏω=16 MeV.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energies of even-even Ca isotopes from A=34 to A=56 and Ni isotopes from A=50 to A=80 were computed
- For calcium chain, three-body matrix elements used a cut of E^(3)_max = 16; for nickel chain, E^(3)_max = 24
- A conservative 2% symmetric error bar was attributed to the correlation energy due to model-space truncation
- The uncertainty from omitted triple excitations was estimated as 10% of the BCCSD correlation energy, applied as an asymmetric band centered on the mean of BCCSD and deformed CCSD calculations
- The oscillator frequency was set to ℏω = 16 MeV for all calculations

## Context

This work extends previous coupled-cluster studies of closed-shell nuclei (Hagen 2014) to open-shell systems by comparing three complementary approaches: EOM-CC (Jansen 2011, Marino 2025), Bogoliubov CC (Signoracci 2015, Tichai 2024), and deformed CC (Novario 2020, Sun 2025). The paper enables future systematic comparisons of these methods for spectroscopic observables and provides a benchmark for assessing the reliability of symmetry-broken approaches without symmetry restoration for bulk properties of medium-mass nuclei.
