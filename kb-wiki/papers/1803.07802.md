# Prolate-to-oblate shape phase transitions in neutron-rich odd-mass nuclei

> We investigate the prolate-to-oblate shape phase transitions in the neutron-rich Pt, Os and Ir nuclei in the mass $A\approx 190$ region. The Hamiltonian of the interacting boson-fermion model, used to describe the odd-mass $^{185-199}$Pt, $^{185-193}$Os and $^{185-195}$Ir isotopes, is partially constructed by using as a microscopic input the results of constrained self-consistent mean-field calculations within the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov method with the Gogny force. The remaining few parameters are adjusted to experimental data in the odd systems. In this way the calculations reasonably describe the spectroscopic properties of the odd-mass systems considered. Several calculated observables for the odd-mass nuclei, especially the low-energy excitation spectra and the effective deformation parameters, point to a prolate-oblate shape transition as a function of the neutron number for all the isotopic chains considered and similar to the one already observed in the neighboring even-even systems.

## Key claim

The prolate-to-oblate shape phase transition observed in neighboring even-even Pt, Os, and Ir nuclei also occurs in the odd-mass isotopes \(^{185-199}\)Pt, \(^{185-193}\)Os, and \(^{185-195}\)Ir, as indicated by calculated low-energy excitation spectra and effective deformation parameters, though the description relies on fitting a few free parameters of the boson-fermion interaction to experimental data for each odd-mass nucleus.

## Method

The authors constructed an interacting boson-fermion model (IBFM-2) Hamiltonian for odd-mass nuclei by taking the IBM-2 Hamiltonian parameters for the even-even cores from constrained Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations with the Gogny-D1M energy density functional, using the mapping procedure of Nomura et al. (2008). Single-particle energies and occupation probabilities for the odd nucleon were also obtained from Gogny-D1M HFB calculations constrained to zero quadrupole moment. The coupling constants of the boson-fermion interaction (quadrupole dynamic, exchange, and monopole terms) were treated as free parameters and fitted to reproduce the low-lying excitation spectrum for each odd-mass nucleus separately. The IBFM-2 Hamiltonian was then diagonalized to obtain excitation spectra and electromagnetic transition rates.

## Key numbers

- The effective bosonic charge was taken as \(e_{\rm B}^\nu = e_{\rm B}^\pi = 0.15\,e\)b, fitted to reproduce the experimental \(B(E2;2^+_1\rightarrow 0^+_1)\) in \(^{196}\)Pt.
- Fermion effective charges used were \(e_{\rm F}^\nu = 0.3\,e\)b and \(e_{\rm F}^{\pi} = 0.5\,e\)b.
- Bosonic \(g\)-factor was taken as \(g_{\rm B}^\nu = g_{\rm B}^\pi = 0.3\,\mu_N\), fitted to the magnetic moment of the \(2^+_1\) state in \(^{196}\)Pt.
- For the even-even Pt isotopes, the theoretical \(R_{42} = E(4^+_1)/E(2^+_1)\) ratios are located around the O(6) limit value of 2.5.
- The theoretical spectroscopic quadrupole moment \(Q_{2^+_1}\) is negative for \(^{186,188}\)Pt and positive for \(^{190-200}\)Pt, consistent with a prolate-to-oblate transition.
- For \(^{195}\)Pt, the predicted \(B(E2; {3/2}^-_1 \rightarrow {1/2}^-_1)\) is 24 W.u. versus the experimental 11.5(15) W.u., and the magnetic moment of the \({1/2}^-_1\) state is predicted as +0.46 \(\mu_N\) versus the experimental +0.60952(6) \(\mu_N\).

## Context

This work extends the method of Nomura et al. (2016) based on nuclear energy density functional theory and the particle-core coupling scheme, previously applied to spherical-to-axially-deformed and spherical-to-\(\gamma\)-soft shape phase transitions, to the prolate-to-oblate shape transition in the \(A \approx 190\) region. It builds on prior IBM-2 studies of even-even Pt and Os nuclei by Nomura et al. (2011) and on earlier IBFM-2 phenomenology in this region by Arias (1986). The results enable future comparisons with computationally more demanding approaches such as the symmetry-projected generator coordinate method for odd-mass systems (Bally, 2014; Borrajo, 2016) and large-scale shell model calculations (Caurier, 2005; Shimizu, 2017).
