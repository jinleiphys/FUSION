# Bogoliubov Theory and Lee-Huang-Yang Corrections in Spin-1 and Spin-2 Bose-Einstein Condensates in the Presence of the Quadratic Zeeman Effect

> We develop Bogoliubov theory of spin-1 and spin-2 Bose-Einstein condensates (BECs) in the presence of a quadratic Zeeman effect, and derive the Lee-Huang-Yang (LHY) corrections to the ground-state energy, sound velocity, and quantum depletion. We investigate all the phases of spin-1 and spin-2 BECs that can be realized experimentally. We also examine the stability of each phase against quantum fluctuations and the quadratic Zeeman effect. Furthermore, we discuss a relationship between the number of symmetry generators that are spontaneously broken and that of Nambu-Goldstone (NG) modes. It is found that in the spin-2 nematic phase there are special Bogoliubov modes that have gapless linear dispersion relations but do not belong to the NG modes.

## Key claim

The paper develops Bogoliubov theory for spin-1 and spin-2 Bose-Einstein condensates including the quadratic Zeeman effect, deriving Lee-Huang-Yang corrections to ground-state energy, sound velocity, and quantum depletion across all experimentally realizable phases, with the hedge that the quadratic Zeeman term is treated perturbatively assuming it is at most of the order of the interaction potential.

## Method

The authors start from the low-energy effective Hamiltonian for spin-\(f\) bosons with contact interactions, expand the field operator in plane waves, and apply a Hartree-Fock mean-field approximation to classify ground-state phases. They then construct the Bogoliubov Hamiltonian by replacing condensate operators with c-numbers and keeping terms up to second order in the non-condensate operators, diagonalizing via Bogoliubov transformations. Ultraviolet divergences in the ground-state energy are removed by renormalizing the bare coupling constants using the \(T\)-matrix up to second order, relating them to the \(s\)-wave scattering lengths \(a_F\).

## Key numbers

- For the spin-1 ferromagnetic phase, the ground-state energy per volume is \(\frac{E_0^F}{V} = qn + \frac{2\pi\hbar^2 n^2}{M} a_2 \left(1 + \frac{128}{15} \sqrt{\frac{n a_2^3}{\pi}}\right)\), where the LHY correction is \(\frac{128}{15} \sqrt{\frac{n a_2^3}{\pi}}\).
- The sound velocity in the ferromagnetic phase is \(c = \sqrt{\frac{4\pi\hbar^2 n a_2}{M^2}} \left(1 + 8 \sqrt{\frac{n a_2^3}{\pi}}\right)\).
- The quantum depletion in the ferromagnetic phase is \(\frac{N-N_0}{N} = \frac{8}{3} \sqrt{\frac{n a_2^3}{\pi}}\).
- For the spin-1 polar phase with \(q>0\), the ground-state energy per volume is \(\frac{E_0^P}{V} = \frac{n^2 c_0^{(1)}}{2} \left[1 + \frac{16\sqrt{M^3}}{15\pi^2\hbar^3} \sqrt{n(c_0^{(1)})^3}\right] + \frac{16\sqrt{M^3} n^2 |c_1^{(1)}|}{15\pi^2\hbar^3} \sqrt{n(|c_1^{(1)}|)^3} \phi_1(t_1 + \text{sgn}(c_1^{(1)}))\), where \(t_1 = q/(n|c_1^{(1)}|) - 1\) and \(\phi_1(0)=1\).
- The quantum depletion in the polar phase with \(q>0\) is \(\frac{N-N_0}{N} = \frac{\sqrt{M^3}}{3\pi^2\hbar^3} \left( \sqrt{n(c_0^{(1)})^3} + 2\sqrt{n(|c_1^{(1)}|)^3} \phi_3(t_1 + \text{sgn}(c_1^{(1)}))\right)\).

## Context

This work extends the Bogoliubov theory of spinor BECs developed by Ho (1998), Ohmi and Machida (1998), and Ueda and Koashi (2002) by incorporating the quadratic Zeeman effect and deriving LHY corrections, which had previously been studied only for scalar BECs by Lee, Huang, and Yang (1957). The results enable experimental measurement of quantum fluctuations in spinor BECs using Feshbach resonances or optical lattices, as demonstrated for scalar BECs by Xu (2006) and Papp (2008), and provide a framework for understanding the relationship between spontaneously broken symmetries and Nambu-Goldstone modes in spin-2 nematic phases.
