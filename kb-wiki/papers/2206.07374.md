# Wave function asymptotics for scattering of three-particles with Coulomb interaction

> The coordinate asymptotics of the wave function for the problem of scattering of three particles with Coulomb interaction is constructed. Representation of hyperspherical functions is used to reduce the Schrödinger equation to a system of partial wave one-dimensional equations. Asymptotic solutions of this system are constructed by direct asymptotic methods.

## Key claim

The paper constructs the coordinate asymptotics of the wave function for three-particle scattering with Coulomb interactions, using a representation of hyperspherical functions to reduce the Schrödinger equation to a system of one-dimensional partial wave equations, and then solving these asymptotically by direct methods. The author notes that the asymptotic representation is valid with an error of \(O(R^{-3})\) for the full equation, but the \(S\)-matrix \(\mathbb{S}_c\) that appears in the asymptotics remains an undetermined quantity that must be found by solving the full scattering problem over all hyperradii.

## Method

The Schrödinger equation for three particles with Coulomb potentials \(V_\beta(\bm{x}_\beta)=c_\beta/x_\beta\) is expressed in hyperspherical coordinates, yielding a system of coupled one-dimensional equations for the partial components \(\Psi_{\mathcal{K}\mathcal{N}}(R,P)\) with a charge matrix \(\mathbb{C}\) and an angular-momentum matrix \(\mathbb{L}\). Asymptotic solutions are constructed in two stages: first, the leading-order term is obtained by diagonalizing the charge matrix \(\mathbb{C}\) and using incoming/outgoing Coulomb functions \(u_0^\pm(\eta,z)\); second, the centrifugal term is included by diagonalizing the \(R\)-dependent matrix \(\mathbb{C}+(1/R)\mathbb{L}\) via an adiabatic expansion, leading to asymptotic solutions expressed in terms of Coulomb functions \(u_{L_\mathcal{K}}^\mp(\eta_\mathcal{K},PR)\) with effective angular momenta \(L_\mathcal{K}\). The final asymptotic form of the wave function is given as a superposition of these incoming and outgoing waves, with the \(S\)-matrix \(\mathbb{S}_c\) left to be determined by matching to the solution at \(R=0\).

## Key numbers

* The short-range potential condition is given as \(V(\bm{x})\sim O(x^{-3-\rho})\) with \(\rho>0\) as \(x\to\infty\).
* The eigenvalues of the squared hypermomentum operator are \(\ell_k(\ell_k+1)\) with \(\ell_k=k+3/2\) for integers \(k=0,1,2,\dots\).
* The effective angular momentum \(L_\mathcal{K}\) is defined by \(L_\mathcal{K}(L_\mathcal{K}+1)=d^{(1)}_\mathcal{K}\), where \(d^{(1)}_\mathcal{K}\) are the diagonal elements of \(\mathbb{V}^\dagger\mathbb{L}\mathbb{V}\), and \(L_\mathcal{K}\ge 0\).
* The asymptotic error of the final constructed solution is \(O(R^{-3})\) as \(R\to\infty\).
* The leading-order asymptotic form \(\mathbb{F}_0(R,P)\) satisfies the full equation with error \(O(R^{-2})\).

## Context

This work extends the author's previous rigorous approach for short-range interactions (Yakovlev, 2021) to the Coulomb case, which had remained unexplored in that formalism. It directly addresses the long-standing difficulty noted by Merkuriev (1977) and others (Klar, Alt) in constructing three-particle Coulomb wave function asymptotics beyond leading terms. The result provides the correct asymptotic boundary conditions for solving the three-particle Schrödinger equation with Coulomb potentials over all hyperradii, enabling future calculations of the \(S\)-matrix and scattering cross sections for processes such as electron-impact ionization of atoms, as previously approached using hyperspherical coordinates in the monograph by Peterkop.
