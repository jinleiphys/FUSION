# Electron wave functions in beta-decay formulas revisited (II): Completion including recoil-order and induced currents

> We present complete formulas of the allowed and first-forbidden transitions of the nuclear beta decay taking into account the recoil-order and induced currents up to the next-to-leading order (NLO). The longitudinal part of the vector current is cleared away by the use of the conservation of the vector current for the multipole operators of the natural-parity transitions, which makes the effect of the meson exchange current for the vector current as small as possible. The formula is transparent enough to be applied to various beta-decay processes. As a numerical demonstration, we apply our formulas to the beta decay of a neutron-rich nucleus $^{160}$Sn. We find that the NLO corrections amount to 10--20\% of the total decay rate, whereas the induced currents alter the rate at most 5\%.

## Key claim

The paper provides complete formulas for allowed and first-forbidden nuclear beta decay transitions that include recoil-order and induced currents up to next-to-leading order (NLO), with the key advance being the elimination of the longitudinal part of the vector current via current conservation to minimize meson-exchange current effects; the authors find that NLO corrections amount to 10–20% of the total decay rate for the test case of ¹⁶⁰Sn, while induced currents alter the rate at most 5%.

## Method

The authors reformulate the multipole expansion of the beta-decay effective Hamiltonian using Fourier–Bessel transformation and the conservation of the vector current (CVC) to rewrite the longitudinal component of the spatial vector current in terms of the Coulomb multipole operator, thereby reducing sensitivity to meson-exchange currents. They then express the nuclear multipole operators in the non-relativistic impulse approximation, including the velocity-dependent terms (convection current, time component of axial-vector current) and induced currents (weak-magnetism and induced pseudo-scalar terms). As a numerical demonstration, they apply the formulas to the beta decay of ¹⁶⁰Sn using transition densities obtained from a nuclear energy-density-functional method with Skyrme SLy4 functional and the proton–neutron quasiparticle-random-phase approximation (pnQRPA).

## Key numbers

- NLO corrections to the total decay rate of ¹⁶⁰Sn amount to 10–20% of the total decay rate
- Induced currents alter the total decay rate of ¹⁶⁰Sn at most 5%
- The Fermi coupling constant used is G_F = 1.166 × 10⁻⁵ GeV⁻²
- The up-down Cabibbo–Kobayashi–Maskawa matrix element is V_ud = 0.9737
- The axial-vector coupling constant at q²=0 is g_A(0) = 1.27
- The weak-magnetism combination is g_V(0) + g_M(0) = μ_p − μ_n = 4.7
- The induced pseudo-scalar coupling at q²=0 is g_P(0) = g_A(0) × 2M/m_π² (assuming pion-pole dominance)

## Context

This work extends the formalism of Horiuchi et al. (2021) [paper (I)], which provided NLO formulas for beta-decay rates using electron wave functions but only gave explicit impulse-approximation formulas for the leading σ τ operator of the axial-vector current. The present paper completes the formalism by including velocity-dependent terms (such as A₀ ∼ σ·p/M τ and V ∼ p/M τ) and induced currents, which are particularly important for first-forbidden transitions. This enables more precise calculations of beta-decay rates for neutron-rich nuclei relevant to r-process nucleosynthesis and neutrino physics, including the reactor neutrino anomaly and searches for beyond-standard-model physics.
