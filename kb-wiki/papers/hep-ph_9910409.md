# Neutralino Inelastic Scattering with Subsequent Detection of Nuclear Gamma Rays

> We consider the potential benefits of searching for supersymmetric dark-matter through its inelastic excitation, via the "scalar current", of low-lying collective nuclear states in a detector. If such states live long enough so that the gamma radiation from their decay can be separated from the signal due to nuclear recoil, then background can be dramatically reduced. We show how the kinematics of neutralino-nucleus scattering is modified when the nucleus is excited and derive expressions for the form factors associated with exciting collective states. We apply these results to two specific cases: 1) the 5/2^+ state at 13 keV in 73Ge, and 2) the rotational and hence very collective state 3/2^+ at 8 keV in 169Tm (even though observing the transition down from that state will be difficult). In both cases we compare the form factors for inelastic scattering with those for elastic scattering. The inelastic cross section is considerably smaller than its elastic counterpart, though perhaps not always prohibitively so.

# Neutralino Inelastic Scattering with Subsequent Detection of Nuclear Gamma Rays

## Key claim

Inelastic scattering of neutralinos via the scalar current to low-lying collective nuclear states could dramatically reduce background through detection of the subsequent gamma-ray decay, but the inelastic cross section is considerably smaller than its elastic counterpart, though perhaps not always prohibitively so, as the ratio of inelastic to elastic cross sections in 169Tm reaches approximately 1.5 × 10^{-3} (integrated from the lowest possible momentum transfer).

## Method

The authors derived expressions for the form factors associated with exciting collective nuclear states via the scalar current, focusing on E2 multipole transitions. They applied these results to two specific cases: the 5/2^+ state at 13 keV in 73Ge and the rotational 3/2^+ state at 8.4 keV in 169Tm, comparing the inelastic form factors to elastic form factors as functions of recoil energy. For 73Ge they used a vibrational model with the transition density concentrated at the nuclear surface, while for 169Tm they employed a rotational model based on the deformation parameter β.

## Key numbers

- For 73Ge with a 60 GeV neutralino and Maxwellian velocity distribution (mean 220 km/s, escape 650 km/s): ratio of total inelastic to elastic cross sections = 2.8 × 10^{-5}; with a 10 keV threshold, ratio = 5.7 × 10^{-5}
- For 169Tm with the same neutralino parameters: ratio of total inelastic to elastic cross sections = 1.5 × 10^{-3}; with a 10 keV threshold, ratio = 5.9 × 10^{-3}
- The 5/2^+ state in 73Ge at 13 keV has a half-life of 2.95 μs and B(E2) of 23 Weisskopf units
- The 3/2^+ state in 169Tm at 8.4 keV has a half-life of 4.1 ns and B(E2) of 226 Weisskopf units, with deformation parameter β ≈ 0.3
- Previous work by Ejiri reported an upper limit of 9.8 × 10^{-2} counts/kg/day (at 90% CL) for inelastic excitation of the 7/2^+ state at 57.6 keV in 127I

## Context

This work extends earlier considerations of inelastic neutralino scattering by Ellis, which focused on spin-dependent M1 transitions, to the scalar current and E2 excitations of collective nuclear states. The authors note that spin-independent scattering (scalar current) will almost always occur with greater probability than spin-dependent scattering, as established in prior work. The paper enables future experimental searches for supersymmetric dark matter using inelastic excitation of low-lying collective states, particularly in 169Tm, where the suppression factor relative to elastic scattering in 73Ge is less than 100 with a 10 keV threshold.
