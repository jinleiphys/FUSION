# Renormalization and Universality of Van der Waals forces

> Renormalization ideas can profitably be exploited in conjunction with the superposition principle of boundary conditions in the description of model independent and universal scaling features of the singular and long range Van der Waals force between neutral atoms. The dominance of the leading power law is highlighted both in the scattering as well as in the bound state problem. The role of off-shell two-body unitarity and causality within the Effective Field Theory framework on the light of universality and scaling at low energies is analyzed.

# Renormalization and Universality of Van der Waals forces

## Key claim
The paper argues that for singular, attractive Van der Waals potentials (dominated by the leading \(-1/r^6\) term), the short-distance phase shift is energy-independent and can be absorbed into a single parameter (the scattering length), making the effective range and phase shifts universal functions of that scattering length alone, provided the potential is truncated to a single scale \(R_6\). This universality holds despite the potential being singular at the origin, and the neglected higher-order dispersion coefficients (\(C_8\), \(C_{10}\), etc.) produce only tiny corrections even at energies where \(k R_6 \sim 10\).

## Method
The authors solve the radial Schrödinger equation for s-waves with a singular attractive potential of the form \(U(r) = -R_6^4/r^6\) (plus optional higher-order terms). They impose a short-distance cut-off \(r_c\) and use the superposition principle of boundary conditions to relate the short-distance phase \(\varphi\) to the scattering length \(\alpha_0\). By demanding energy independence of \(\varphi\), they derive a bilinear rational mapping (Moebius transformation) that expresses \(k \cot \delta_0(k)\) explicitly in terms of \(\alpha_0\) and potential-dependent integrals. The effective range \(r_0\) is then obtained from the zero-energy solution and shown to follow a universal quadratic form in \(1/\alpha_0\). The analysis is extended to bound states via orthogonality to the zero-energy state.

## Key numbers
- The Van der Waals length scale \(R_6 = (2\mu C_6)^{1/4}\) ranges from about 10.2 a.u. (H-H) to 215 a.u. (Fr-Fr) for the diatomic systems listed.
- For the pure \(-1/r^6\) potential, the universal effective range coefficients are \(\bar A = 16\,\Gamma(5/4)^2/(3\pi) \approx 1.39473\), \(\bar B = -4/3 \approx -1.33333\), and \(\bar C = 4\,\Gamma(3/4)^2/(3\pi) \approx 0.637318\).
- The dimensionless correction parameters \(g_1\) (from \(C_8\)) are of order \(10^{-2}\) (e.g., 0.0175 for H-H, 0.00365 for Cs-Cs), and \(g_2\) (from \(C_{10}\)) are of order \(10^{-4}\) (e.g., 0.0423 for H-H, 0.000140 for Cs-Cs).
- Finite cut-off corrections to the phase shift scale as \(\mathcal{O}(k^3 r_c^4 / R_6)\), so universality is robust when \(r_c \ll R_6\).
- For the Lennard-Jones potential, the WKB number of bound states is \(N_{\text{WKB}} = 0.1339 / g^2\), giving e.g. \(N_{\text{WKB}} = 100\) for \(g = 0.0365\).

## Context
This work extends the renormalization approach previously applied to nuclear physics potentials (Pavon Valderrama and Ruiz Arriola, 2005, 2007) to the atomic-molecular Van der Waals problem. It builds on quantum defect theory treatments by Gao (1998) and others, which also showed a reduction of parameters for VdW forces, and on Effective Field Theory approaches (Braaten, 2004, 2007; Platter, 2009) that use pure contact interactions. The paper demonstrates that the leading \(-1/r^6\) term dominates scattering and bound states over a wide energy range, making higher-order dispersion coefficients (\(C_8\), \(C_{10}\)) largely irrelevant, a result that enables model-independent extraction of scattering lengths from bound-state data and provides a universal correlation between effective range and scattering length that can be tested against ab initio calculations for many atomic pairs.
