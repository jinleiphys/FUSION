# Large Amplitude Dynamics of the Pairing Correlations in a Unitary Fermi Gas

> A unitary Fermi gas has a surprisingly rich spectrum of large amplitude modes of the pairing field alone, which defies a description within a formalism involving only a reduced set of degrees of freedom, such as quantum hydrodynamics or a Landau-Ginzburg-like description. These modes are very slow, with oscillation frequencies well below the pairing gap, which makes their damping through quasiparticle excitations quite ineffective. In atomic traps these modes couple naturally with the density oscillations, and the corresponding oscillations of the atomic cloud are an example of a new type of collective mode in superfluid Fermi systems. They have lower frequencies than the compressional collective hydrodynamic oscillations, have a non-spherical momentum distribution, and could be excited by a quick time variation of the scattering length.

## Key claim

The paper claims that a unitary Fermi gas supports a rich spectrum of large-amplitude, very slow collective modes of the pairing field alone, with oscillation frequencies well below the pairing gap, which cannot be described by simplified formalisms such as quantum hydrodynamics or a Landau-Ginzburg-like description; the authors hedge that these modes are demonstrated within a specific time-dependent density functional theory (TD-SLDA) framework, and that their experimental excitation would require a quick time variation of the scattering length.

## Method

The authors used the Time-Dependent Superfluid Local Density Approximation (TD-SLDA), an extension of density functional theory to superfluid systems, to simulate the dynamics of both homogeneous and trapped unitary Fermi gases. For the homogeneous system, they prepared the ground state and then abruptly changed the coupling constant γ (which controls the pairing field magnitude) back to its unitary value after a slow reduction, inducing oscillations of the pairing field without density variations. For the trapped system, they simulated a gas initially in a very weakly interacting state in a harmonic potential, then quickly brought the scattering length to its unitary value, exciting coupled density and pairing field oscillations.

## Key numbers

- The equilibrium pairing gap at unitarity is Δ₀ ≈ 0.50 ε_F, where ε_F = k_F²/2 and n = k_F³/(3π²).
- For the homogeneous system, the switching time intervals used were t₀ ε_F = 160 and 10, with γ_s/γ values of 0.005 and 0.5.
- The abrupt change in coupling constant occurred over a time interval δt ≈ 0.005/ε_F, much less than 1/ε_F.
- For the trapped system (ω/ε_F ≈ 0.0683, N = 20,000 particles), the rms cloud size oscillation frequency was Ω_H/ω = 1.74, with damping rate η_H/ω = 0.082.
- The quantum hydrodynamic frequency for a unitary gas in the small amplitude limit is Ω_QHD/ω = 4/√3 ≈ 2.31.
- The speed of the running waves v_H/v_F was within 10% of the ground state speed of sound c/v_F = √(ξ/3) ≈ 0.37, where ξ is the Bertsch parameter.

## Context

This work extends studies of pairing dynamics in the weak-coupling regime by Spivak, Levitov, and others (early 2000s) to the unitary regime, where experimental realization is feasible. It contradicts the expectation that a simplified Landau-Ginzburg-like description (as used by Babaev and others) or quantum hydrodynamics (as used by Ketterle and others) can capture the full spectrum of collective modes. The results enable the identification of a new type of collective mode in superfluid Fermi systems that couples to density oscillations in traps, with a non-spherical momentum distribution, and suggest that such modes could be excited experimentally by rapidly varying the scattering length using magnetic fields, as demonstrated in a related MIT experiment.
