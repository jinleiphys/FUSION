# The CJT calculation in studying nuclear matter beyond mean field approximation

> We have introduced a CJT calculation in studying nuclear matter beyond mean field approximation. Based on the CJT formalism and using Walecka model, we have derived a set of coupled Dyson equations of nucleons and mesons. Neglecting the medium effects of the mesons, the usual MFT results could be reproduced. The beyond MFT calculations have been performed by thermodynamic consistently determining the meson effective masses and solving the coupled gap equations for nucleons and mesons. The numerical results for the nucleon and meson effective masses at finite temperature and chemical potential in nuclear matter are discussed.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the Cornwall-Jackiw-Tomboulis (CJT) formalism, applied to the Walecka model at Hartree-Fock approximation, can produce beyond-mean-field results for nuclear matter while preserving thermodynamic consistency, provided the meson effective masses are determined by minimizing the thermodynamic potential rather than by directly solving the full momentum-dependent Dyson equations.

## Method

The authors start from the Walecka model (QHD-I) Lagrangian and use the CJT formalism to derive a thermodynamic potential and a set of coupled Dyson equations for the full propagators of nucleons, sigma mesons, and omega mesons at the Hartree-Fock level. To make the calculation tractable, they neglect vacuum fluctuations and the momentum dependence in the nucleon self-energy, define the nucleon effective mass as the pole mass in the zero-momentum limit, and determine the meson effective masses by minimizing the thermodynamic potential with respect to those masses rather than solving the full meson Dyson equations. The resulting closed set of equations for the nucleon self-energy components and the meson effective masses is solved numerically at finite temperature and chemical potential.

## Key numbers

- Coupling constants fixed from reproducing saturation properties: \(g_\sigma^2 = 155.6\) and \(g_\omega^2 = 521.5\).
- At zero chemical potential, the nucleon effective mass decreases with increasing temperature; at high temperature, the decrease is more rapid in the CJT case than in the MFT case (qualitative comparison shown in figures, no explicit numerical values given for specific temperatures).
- The sigma meson effective mass increases with temperature at zero chemical potential, while the omega meson effective mass decreases with temperature (qualitative trends shown in figures, no explicit numerical values given).
- At given temperatures, the nucleon effective mass decreases with increasing chemical potential, more rapidly in the CJT case than in MFT at high temperatures (qualitative trends shown in figures, no explicit numerical values given).
- The sigma meson effective mass increases with chemical potential at given temperatures; the omega meson effective mass decreases with chemical potential; both change slightly at low temperatures but rapidly at high temperatures (qualitative trends shown in figures, no explicit numerical values given).

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous application of the CJT formalism to nuclear matter (Shu and Li, 2007), where they reproduced mean-field theory (MFT) results by neglecting meson medium effects. It contrasts with the one-particle-irreducible effective action approach, which becomes very involved at two loops and yields results inconsistent with nuclear matter properties after certain approximations (Author, year not specified in text). The paper enables future studies of nuclear matter beyond MFT that preserve thermodynamic consistency without solving the full momentum-dependent Dyson equations, and it provides a framework for investigating effective masses of nucleons and mesons in hot and dense nuclear matter.
