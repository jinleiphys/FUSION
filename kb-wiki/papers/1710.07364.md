# Constraints on the hadronic spectrum from Lattice QCD

> The spectrum of hadronic resonances continually receives updates from the Particle Data Group, which lists every state with a status representing how established the state is. Moreover, the existence of additional states is predicted by relativistic quark models. It has been suggested that further states might need to be included in the hadronic spectrum in order to improve the agreement between the hadron resonance gas model predictions and lattice QCD data. Such an inclusion would also affect the results of many areas of heavy-ion collision physics that make use of hadronic degrees of freedom, such as hydrodynamical simulations afterburners. However, for some selected observables, the inclusion of further states worsens the agreement with the lattice results. We propose new observables, sensitive to the spectrum content divided by quantum numbers, which allow us to gauge the contribution of additional states. The comparison of Lattice QCD results and predictions from the Hadron Resonance Gas model for these observables, helps to clarify the situation and determine how many, and which new states are needed.

# Constraints on the hadronic spectrum from Lattice QCD

## Key claim
Comparing Lattice QCD results with Hadron Resonance Gas (HRG) model predictions for partial pressures grouped by baryon number and strangeness shows that the PDG list of established states is insufficient in the baryonic sector, but that including all quark-model-predicted states overestimates the data for some strangeness sectors, meaning only a selective addition of new states is needed.

## Method
The author separates the total pressure of the hadron gas into partial pressures grouped by absolute baryon number and strangeness quantum numbers. These partial pressures are computed from Lattice QCD using an imaginary chemical potential approach and compared to HRG model predictions using four different hadron lists: PDG2016 (established states), PDG2016+ (including one-star states), the original Quark Model (QM), and the hypercentral Quark Model (hQM). The comparison identifies which sectors are under- or over-estimated by each list.

## Key numbers
- The PDG2016 list contains 608 states (including particles, anti-particles, and isospin multiplicity) for hadrons containing only u, d, and s quarks; PDG2016+ contains 738 states; the QM list contains 1517 states; the hQM list contains 985 states.
- For |S|=1 and |S|=2 baryons, including PDG one-star states (PDG2016+) "sensibly improves" agreement with Lattice data; for |S|=1 and |S|=3 baryons, states from the original QM list appear needed to reproduce the data.
- For |S|=2 baryons, the QM list "clearly" contains too many states, overestimating the Lattice data.
- For |S|=1 mesons, all lists underpredict the partial pressure, and no continuum extrapolation was achievable because the data were not in a clear scaling regime.

## Context
This work extends the analysis of Alba (2017) by using partial pressures grouped by quantum numbers to systematically test which additional states are needed beyond the PDG list. It addresses the discrepancy noted by Bazavov (2014) between Lattice QCD and HRG model predictions, where adding all quark-model states (Capstick 1986, Ebert 2009, Ferretti 2015) sometimes worsens agreement. The results enable more precise thermal fits for freeze-out parameters in heavy-ion collisions (Andronic 2011, Cleymans 2005) and studies of strangeness freeze-out (Bellwied 2013, Noronha-Hostler 2016).
