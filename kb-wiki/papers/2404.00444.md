# New method for the solution of the two-body Dirac equation for the positronium bound states

> A new theoretical method is developed to solve the two-body bound-state Dirac equation for positronium. Only Coulomb potential was included in the Dirac Hamiltonian. It is shown that the two-body Dirac Hamiltonian can be written in the Hermitian matrix form of the 4$\times$4 size and diagonalized in the momentum-state representation. Numerical results for the energy spectrum of the para- and ortho-positronium ground states performed within the variational method using the harmonic oscillator basis functions are in good agreement with a high-precision finite-element method of T.C. Scott et al. After the Fourier transformation into the coordinate-state representation the bound state wave functions of the para-Ps and ortho-Ps do not contain any singularity at the origin in contrast to the method mentioned above. The weights of the large-small and small-large components of the ground state wave functions are estimated to be of order 10$^{-6}$, while the weight of the small-small component is of order 10$^{-12}$.

## Key claim

The authors present a new variational method for solving the two-body Dirac equation for positronium that yields bound-state wave functions free of the singularity at the origin found in the earlier finite-element method of Scott et al. (1992), while achieving binding energies accurate to about 0.003 eV.

## Method

The two-body Dirac Hamiltonian with only the Coulomb potential is rewritten as a Hermitian 4x4 matrix in momentum-state representation, where all matrix elements become real numbers. The wave function is expanded in a harmonic oscillator basis, and the variational method is applied to diagonalize the Hamiltonian. After solving in momentum space, an inverse Fourier transformation yields coordinate-space wave functions that are free of singularities at the origin.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of para-positronium ground state: 6.803075 eV (compared to 6.806403 eV from Scott et al. (1992))
- Binding energy of ortho-positronium ground state: 6.803074984 eV (compared to 6.806354 eV from Scott et al. (1992))
- Weight of the sum of large-small and small-large components (W): 6.656E-6 for both para- and ortho-positronium ground states
- Weight of the small-small component: of order 10^{-12} for both para- and ortho-positronium ground states
- Optimal oscillator length parameter: a0 = 3.3 x 10^5 GeV^{-1}
- Fine-structure constant used: alpha = 1/137

## Context

This work extends the two-body Dirac equation formalism for positronium originally developed by Scott et al. (1992) using a finite-element method, which produced wave functions with a singularity at the origin due to mixing with anomalous bound states. The new Hermitian formulation avoids this singularity, addressing questions raised by Patkos (2019, 2023) about the structure of positronium bound states. The method enables calculation of positronium wave functions suitable for studying processes relevant to precision tests of discrete symmetries and medical applications in positron emission tomography.
