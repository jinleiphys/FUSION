# Interplay between $Δ$ Particles and Hyperons in Neutron Stars

> We analyze the effects of including $Δ(1232)$ isobars in an equation of state (EoS) for cold, $β$-stable neutron star matter, employing relativistic nuclear mean field theory. The selected EoS reproduces the properties of nuclear matter and finite nuclei and, in the astrophysical context, allows for the presence of hyperons in neutron stars having masses larger than 2$M_{\odot}$. We find that the composition and structure of neutron stars is critically influenced by the addition of the $Δ$ isobars, which allows us to constrain their interaction with the meson fields taking into account astrophysical information. Imposing that the EoS is stable and ensures the existence of 2$M_{\odot}$ neutron stars, as well as requiring agreement with data of $Δ$ excitation in nuclei, we find that, in the absence of other mechanisms stiffening the EoS at high densities, the interaction of the $Δ$ isobars with the sigma and omega meson fields must be at least 10\% stronger than that of the nucleons. Moreover, the neutron star moment of inertia turns out to be sensitive to the presence of $Δ$ isobars, whereas the inclusion of $Δ$ isobars in the EoS allows for smaller stellar radii and for a lower value of the tidal deformability consistent with the analysis of the GW170817 merger event.

## Key claim

The paper finds that including Delta(1232) isobars in neutron star matter, alongside hyperons, does not prevent the existence of 2 solar mass neutron stars, provided the Delta coupling to the sigma and omega meson fields is at least 10% stronger than that of nucleons, and that this condition is necessary only in the absence of other mechanisms that stiffen the equation of state at high densities.

## Method

The authors employ a relativistic mean field (RMF) model, specifically the FSU2H parametrization, to describe cold, beta-stable neutron star matter containing the full baryon octet (n, p, Lambda, Sigma, Xi) and the Delta(1232) isobar quartet. They solve the equations of motion for the meson fields and particle populations self-consistently under charge neutrality and beta-equilibrium conditions, then integrate the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkoff (TOV) equations to obtain stellar mass, radius, and tidal deformability, varying the unknown Delta-meson coupling ratios x_sigmaDelta, x_omegaDelta, and x_rhoDelta.

## Key numbers

* The FSU2H model without Delta isobars predicts a maximum neutron star mass of 2.02 solar masses with a radius of 12.1 km, and for a 1.4 solar mass star a radius of 13.3 km.
* The onset density for the Lambda hyperon in the FSU2H model without Delta isobars is 0.336 fm^-3.
* For the case x_sigmaDelta = x_omegaDelta = x_rhoDelta = 1, the onset density for the Delta- isobar is 0.27 fm^-3.
* The nuclear matter saturation density n0 of the FSU2H model is 0.1505 fm^-3, with saturation energy per particle E/A = -16.28 MeV, incompressibility K = 238 MeV, symmetry energy E_sym = 30.5 MeV, and symmetry energy slope L = 44.5 MeV.
* The constraint from electron-nucleus scattering data is given as 0 less than or approximately equal to (x_sigmaDelta - x_omegaDelta) less than or approximately equal to 0.2.

## Context

This work extends the FSU2H RMF model of Ramos et al. (2018) and Tolos et al. (2017) by adding Delta isobar degrees of freedom, building on earlier studies of Delta isobars in neutron stars by Drago et al. (2014), Cai et al. (2015), Zhu et al. (2016), and Li et al. (2018). It contrasts with the quark-meson-coupling model study by Motta et al. (2019) which predicts a strongly repulsive Delta potential that prevents Delta isobars from appearing in neutron stars. The results enable improved constraints on the Delta-meson couplings by requiring simultaneous agreement with the 2 solar mass pulsar observations, the GW170817 tidal deformability measurement, and laboratory data on Delta excitation in nuclei.
