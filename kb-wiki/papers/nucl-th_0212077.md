# On the NN-final-state-interaction in the $^{16}{O}(e,e' pp)$ reaction

> The influence of the mutual interaction between the two outgoing nucleons (NN-FSI) in the $^{16}{O}(e,e' pp)$ reaction has been investigated. Results for various kinematics are discussed. In general, the effect of NN-FSI depends on kinematics and the chosen final state in the excitation spectrum of $^{14}{C}$.

# On the NN-final-state-interaction in the $^{16}{O}(e,e' pp)$ reaction

## Key claim
The mutual interaction between the two outgoing protons (NN-FSI) in the $^{16}{O}(e,e' pp)$ reaction can produce an enhancement of about one order of magnitude in the cross section at medium and large recoil momenta, even in back-to-back superparallel kinematics where NN-FSI was previously expected to be minimal. However, the effect is strongly kinematics-dependent and no general statement about its relevance can be drawn.

## Method
The authors extended the unfactorized approach of Giusti et al. (1997) for two-nucleon knockout on finite nuclei by incorporating, for the first time, the complete NN-scattering amplitude $T^{NN}$ from the Bonn OBEPQ-A potential up to first order in the final state, while still neglecting multiscattering processes. The final state was constructed as a product of two uncoupled single-particle distorted wave functions from a complex optical potential plus a perturbative term $G_0 T^{NN}$ acting on plane waves, with the NN-interaction evaluated in momentum space using all isospin 1 partial waves up to orbital angular momentum 3.

## Key numbers
- In superparallel kinematics (Mainz experiment: $E_0 = 855$ MeV, $\theta_e = 18^\circ$, $\omega = 215$ MeV, $q = 316$ MeV/$c$, $\gamma_1 = 0^\circ$, $\gamma_2 = 180^\circ$), NN-FSI produces about one order of magnitude enhancement at recoil momentum $p_B = 300$ MeV/$c$.
- In NIKHEF kinematics ($E_0 = 584$ MeV, $\theta_e = 26.5^\circ$, $\omega = 210$ MeV, $q = 300$ MeV/$c$, $\gamma_1 = 30^\circ$, fixed proton kinetic energy $T_1 = 137$ MeV), NN-FSI is maximal near $\gamma_2 \approx 120^\circ$, corresponding to $p_B \approx 0$ MeV/$c$.
- The $^1S_0$ initial relative partial wave gives almost the full contribution of NN-FSI; the $^3P_1$ state shows negligible effect in NIKHEF kinematics and only a small effect in superparallel kinematics.

## Context
This work extends the treatment of final state interactions in electromagnetic two-nucleon knockout beyond the approach of Giusti et al. (1997, 1998, 1999), which included only the optical potential for nucleon-nucleus scattering while neglecting the mutual NN interaction. The study was motivated by calculations on nuclear matter by Knoepfle et al. (2000) indicating that NN-FSI are non-negligible even in back-to-back kinematics. The results show that the full three-body problem of the final state must be tackled in future studies, and that systematic investigation of different kinematics may identify situations where FSI effects are minimized for direct access to short-range correlations.
