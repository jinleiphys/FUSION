# Potential for measurement of the tensor magnetic polarizability of the deuteron in storage ring experiments

> General formulas describing deuteron spin dynamics in storage rings with allowance for the tensor electric and magnetic polarizabilities are derived. It is found that an initially tensor-polarized deuteron beam can acquire a final horizontal vector polarization of the order of 1%. This effect allows one to measure the tensor magnetic polarizability of the deuteron in storage ring experiments. We also confirm an existence of the effect found by Baryshevsky and Gurinovich, hep-ph/0506135 and Baryshevsky, hep-ph/0510158; hep-ph/0603191 that the tensor magnetic polarizability of the deuteron causes the spin rotation with two frequencies and experiences beating for polarized deuteron beams in storage rings.

## Key claim

An initially tensor-polarized deuteron beam in a storage ring can acquire a final horizontal vector polarization of the order of 1%, which would allow measurement of the tensor magnetic polarizability of the deuteron, provided that systematic errors from betatron oscillations, field defects, and magnet misalignments can be controlled.

## Method

The author derived general formulas for deuteron spin dynamics in storage rings using a matrix Hamiltonian approach with three-component spinors and 3×3 matrices, incorporating corrections from tensor electric and magnetic polarizabilities. The evolution of the polarization vector and polarization tensor was calculated for both vector-polarized and tensor-polarized deuteron beams in a uniform vertical magnetic field, with the tensor polarizabilities causing spin rotation at two frequencies ω₀ ± b instead of a single frequency ω₀.

## Key numbers

- The tensor magnetic polarizability of the deuteron is theoretically estimated as β_T = 1.95×10⁻⁴⁰ cm³ (CGS, JL).
- Three theoretical predictions for the tensor electric polarizability of the deuteron are given: α_T = -6.2×10⁻⁴¹ cm³ (CGS), -6.8×10⁻⁴¹ cm³ (JL), and 3.2×10⁻⁴¹ cm³ (FP).
- For the planned deuteron electric-dipole-moment experiment parameters (γ = 1.28, β = 0.625, τ ~ 1000 s, B_z = 3 T), the beat frequency parameter b ~ 10⁻⁵ s⁻¹ and bt ≲ 10⁻².
- The ratio of the g−2 frequency to the cyclotron frequency is ω₀/ω_c = γ(g−2)/2 = -0.143γ in semicircular sections.

## Context

This work confirms and extends the effects predicted by Baryshevsky and Gurinovich (2005) and Baryshevsky (2005, 2006), who found that the tensor magnetic polarizability of the deuteron causes spin rotation with two frequencies and beating for polarized deuteron beams in storage rings. The paper proposes using a tensor-polarized deuteron beam (rather than vector-polarized) to eliminate systematic errors from interactions linear in the spin, enabling measurement of the tensor magnetic polarizability. This approach could also allow determination of the tensor electric polarizability by performing measurements at two different beam momenta on the same ring, complementing a method proposed in an earlier paper (PRC).
