# High Density Effective Theory Confronts the Fermi Liquid

> The high density effective theory recently introduced by Hong and Hsu to describe ultradense relativistic fermionic matter is used to calculate the tree-level forward scattering amplitude between two particles at the Fermi surface. While the direct term correctly reproduces that of the underlying gauge theory, the exchange term has the wrong sign. The physical consequences are discussed in the context of Landau's theoretical description of the Fermi liquid.

# High Density Effective Theory Confronts the Fermi Liquid

## Key claim

The high density effective theory (HDET) introduced by Hong and Hsu correctly reproduces the direct term of the tree-level forward scattering amplitude between two particles at the Fermi surface, but the exchange term has the wrong sign compared to the underlying gauge theory; this mismatch cannot be resolved at higher order in the derivative expansion and requires either a four-fermi contact term or the retention of the integrated-out $\psi_-$ degrees of freedom to restore agreement.

## Method

The author calculates the tree-level forward scattering amplitude for two particles at the Fermi surface in both QED and HDET, averaging over spins to obtain the spin-symmetric part. The HDET calculation uses the leading-order quark-gluon vertex derived from the projection operators $P_\pm(p) = \frac12(1 \pm \vec\alpha\cdot\hat{\bf p})$ and the phase factor $X \equiv \mu({\bf x}\cdot\hat{\bf p})(\vec\alpha\cdot\hat{\bf p})$ that ensures gluons scatter off physical rather than residual momenta. The author then compares the resulting direct and exchange amplitudes and traces the discrepancy to the elimination of the $\psi_-$ (fast) degrees of freedom, showing that including mixed $\psi_+$–$\psi_-$ vertices restores the full QED result.

## Key numbers

- Direct QED amplitude: ${\cal A}_{QED}^{dir}(k,k^\prime) = -e^2/\lambda^2 (1-\cos\theta)$, where $\lambda$ is an infrared regulator mass and $\theta$ is the angle between particle momenta.
- Exchange QED amplitude: ${\cal A}_{QED}^{ex}(k,k^\prime) = -e^2/(4\mu^2)$ in the limit $m,\lambda\to0$ with $\varepsilon_{\bf k}=|{\bf k}|=\mu$.
- HDET exchange amplitude: ${\cal A}_{HDET}^{ex}(p,p^\prime) = e^2/(8\mu^2)(1+\cos\theta)$, which has the opposite sign to the QED result.
- For QCD with $N_c$ colors and $N_f$ flavors, the HDET exchange interaction yields Landau parameters $f_0^S = f_1^S = -g^2 (N_c^2-1)/(16 N_c^2 N_f \mu^2)$, leading to a predicted Fermi velocity $\beta_F = 1 + g^2 5(N_c^2-1)/(144 N_c \pi^2)$, which is superluminal.
- The correct QCD result gives $\beta_F = 1 - g^2 (N_c^2-1)/(24 N_c \pi^2)$.

## Context

This work extends the HDET programme of Hong and Hsu (2000–2002), which was derived analogously to heavy quark effective theory (HQET) and had previously succeeded in calculating the screening mass of electric gluons and the BCS gap in the color-flavor locked channel. The paper contradicts the implicit assumption that HDET faithfully reproduces all tree-level scattering at the Fermi surface, showing specifically that exchange interactions are misrepresented. This enables the correction of HDET via a four-fermi contact term as outlined by Schäfer (2003), or alternatively motivates the retention of $\psi_-$ states in loop calculations. The result is particularly relevant for applications to Fermi liquid theory (Landau, 1956–1959) where the forward scattering amplitude determines the Fermi liquid interaction ${\cal F}_{{\bf k},{\bf k}^\prime}$ and thus collective excitations such as zero sound.
