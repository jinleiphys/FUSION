# Elliptic flows of light nuclei

> Using the coalescence model based on nucleons from a blast-wave model with its parameters fitted to the measured proton transverse momentum spectrum and elliptic flow in heavy ion collisions at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider, we study the elliptic flows of light nuclei in these collisions. We find that to describe the measured elliptic flows of deuterons (anti-deuterons) and tritons (helium-3) requires that the emission source for nucleons of high transverse momentum is more elongated along the reaction plane than in the perpendicular direction. Our results thus suggest that the elliptic flows of light nuclei can be used to study the nucleon emission source in relativistic heavy ion collisions.

## Key claim

The paper claims that the coalescence model based on a blast-wave model can describe the measured elliptic flows of light nuclei (deuterons, anti-deuterons, tritons, helium-3) only if the nucleon emission source includes a space-momentum correlation where high-transverse-momentum nucleons are more elongated along the reaction plane than in the perpendicular direction; without this correlation, the model fails to reproduce the experimental data at high transverse momenta.

## Method

The authors used a blast-wave model with parameters fitted to reproduce the measured proton transverse momentum spectrum and elliptic flow in Au+Au collisions at sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV and 0-80% centrality. They then applied a coalescence model, using harmonic oscillator Wigner functions with widths determined from empirical charge radii, to compute the transverse momentum spectra and elliptic flows of deuterons (anti-deuterons) and tritons (helium-3) from the nucleon phase-space distributions. To improve agreement with data, they introduced a space-momentum correlation by making the transverse radius parameter R_0 dependent on transverse momentum for nucleons with p_T > 0.9 GeV and with |p_Tx| > |p_Ty|.

## Key numbers

- Kinetic freeze-out temperature: T_K = 130 MeV
- Radial flow parameter: beta_0 = 0.67
- Spatial parameters: R_0 = 10.0 fm, s_2 = -0.04, tau_0 = 9.0 fm/c
- Flow anisotropy parameters: c_1 = 0.148, c_2 = 2.12 GeV/c
- Fugacity for protons: xi = 1.76; for deuterons: xi_d = 2.1; for tritons: xi_t = 3.9
- Deuteron charge radius: R_ch = 2.1421 fm, oscillator frequency omega = 0.1739 sec^-1, width parameter sigma_rho = 2.473 fm
- Triton charge radius: R_ch = 1.7591 fm, omega = 0.3438 sec^-1, sigma_rho = 1.759 fm
- Helium-3 charge radius: R_ch = 1.9661 fm, omega = 0.5504 sec^-1, sigma_rho = 1.390 fm
- Space-momentum correlation introduced for nucleons with p_T > 0.9 GeV using R_0 = 10 * exp(0.23 * (p_T - 0.9)) fm

## Context

This work extends prior studies of light nuclei production at RHIC using coalescence models (Chen 2012, Sun 2015, Zhu 2015) and addresses the failure of a simple blast-wave model to describe the elliptic flows of light nuclei, as noted in the STAR Collaboration measurement (Adamczyk 2016). The study contrasts with the approach of Oh (2009), who used a relativistic transport (ART) model, and with the AMPT model used by the STAR Collaboration. The results suggest that elliptic flows of light nuclei can serve as a probe of nucleon emission source geometry, complementing Hanbury-Brown Twiss (HBT) interferometry methods (Bertsch 1988, Pratt 1990, Mrowczynski 1992).
