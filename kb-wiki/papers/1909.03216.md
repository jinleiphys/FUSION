# Aspects of isentropic trajectories in chiral effective models

> The evolution of the fireball in heavy ion collisions is an isentropic process, meaning that it follows a trajectory of constant entropy per baryon in the phase diagram of the strong interaction. Responsible for the collective acceleration of the fireball is the speed of sound of the system, while fluctuations of conserved charges are encoded in quark-number susceptibilities: together, they leave their imprint in final observables. Here, this isentropic evolution will be analysed within chiral effective models that account for both chiral and center symmetry breaking, two central aspects of QCD. Our discussion focusses on the impact on the isentropic trajectories of the treatment of high-momentum modes, of the meson contribution to thermodynamics and of the number of quark flavours.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the shape of isentropic trajectories (constant entropy per baryon) in the phase diagram of strongly interacting matter depends significantly on model-specific choices, such as whether a UV cutoff is applied to convergent thermal integrals, whether mesonic contributions to thermodynamics are included, and the number of quark flavours, rather than being a robust prediction of chiral effective models alone.

## Method

The authors compare isentropic trajectories computed in the Polyakov-loop extended Nambu-Jona-Lasinio (PNJL) model and the Polyakov-loop extended Quark-Meson (PQM) model in mean-field approximation. They systematically vary the treatment of the UV cutoff in the quark contribution to the grand canonical potential, add thermal meson gas contributions (pions and sigma mesons) to the thermodynamics, and compare two-flavour versus (2+1)-flavour calculations.

## Key numbers

* For AGS, SPS, and RHIC, the values of entropy per baryon (s/n_B) are 30, 45, and 300 respectively (from Bluhm (2007)).
* In the two-flavour Quark-Meson model, isentropic lines are shown for s/n_B values from 2 to 28 (left panel of Fig. 9 of Scavenius (2000)).
* In the two-flavour NJL model, isentropic lines are shown for s/n_B values from 1 to 22 (right panel of Fig. 9 of Scavenius (2000)).
* The meson pressure contribution for each meson species is computed using the integral p_phi_i = (1/(2pi)^3) integral d^3k (k^2/(3 E_phi_i)) * 1/(exp((E_phi_i - mu_phi_i)/T) - 1) with E_phi_i = sqrt(k^2 + m_phi_i^2).

## Context

This work extends earlier studies of isentropic trajectories in chiral effective models by Scavenius (2000) and Kahara (2008), and compares with lattice QCD results for the (2+1)-flavour equation of state by DeTar (2010) and Borsanyi (2012). The analysis enables future systematic comparisons between PNJL and PQM frameworks (Pereira (2019), Motta (2019)), and opens the door to studying the impact of improved Polyakov-loop potentials (Baillot (2019)) and finite-size effects (Palhares (2009)) on isentropic trajectories.
