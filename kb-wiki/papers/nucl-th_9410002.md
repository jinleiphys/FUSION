# Few-Body Physics -- Then and Now

> A summary of the XIV\underline{th} International Conference on Few-body Problems In Physics is given, with an emphasis on the important problems solved recently and the prognosis for the future of the field. Personal remarks and ``homework'' problem assignments are made.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the few-nucleon systems (currently defined as those calculable with less than 1% error) have reached a stage where accurate calculations and experiments can be compared to test dynamics, but that fully relativistic three-nucleon calculations and complete p-d scattering calculations above breakup threshold with Coulomb interaction remain unfinished priorities.

## Method

The author summarizes the XIVth International Conference on Few-body Problems in Physics by reviewing recent Green's Function Monte Carlo (GFMC) calculations for light nuclei, comparing theoretical binding energies and charge radii with experimental values, and assessing the status of complete three-nucleon scattering calculations across four energy regions. The talk also evaluates the potential of Chiral Perturbation Theory as a framework for constructing nuclear forces, using power counting to estimate the relative sizes of two-nucleon, three-nucleon, and four-nucleon forces.

## Key numbers

- GFMC calculation of triton binding energy: 8.47(2) MeV (experiment: 8.48 MeV) using Argonne V18 potential plus Urbana model-9 three-nucleon force
- GFMC calculation of 4He binding energy: 28.35(8) MeV (experiment: 28.30 MeV)
- GFMC calculation of 6Li binding energy: 31.5(4) MeV (experiment: 32.0 MeV)
- Charge radius of 3He extracted from atomic isotope shift: 1.9500(14) fm
- Wave-function charge radius of 3He (experimental, after removing nucleon intrinsic radii): 1.765(6) fm
- Wave-function charge radius of 3He (theoretical, from scaling plot): 1.769(5) fm
- Deuteron wave-function rms radius from electron scattering: 1.953(3) fm
- Deuteron wave-function rms radius from Nijmegen potentials: 1.968(1) fm
- Deuteron wave-function rms radius from hydrogen-deuterium 1S-2S isotope shift: 1.973(7) fm
- Local potentials produce triton binding energy of 7.62(3) MeV; additional binding from relativistic momentum dependence is about 100 keV
- Bonn B potential gives triton binding roughly 300 keV higher than local potential result after correcting 1S0 strength
- Pion mass extracted from NN data: charged pions 139.4(10) MeV, neutral pions 135.6(13) MeV

## Context

This summary talk reviews progress since the 1974 Laval conference (Few Body Problems in Nuclear and Particle Physics, 1975) and extends the line of work by the Urbana-Argonne group (Pieper, Wiringa, Pandharipande 1992) on variational calculations of light nuclei, and the Nijmegen group (Friar, Payne, Stoks, de Swart 1993) on NN potentials and triton binding benchmarks. The paper identifies as open problems the need for complete p-d scattering calculations above breakup threshold with Coulomb, fully relativistic three-nucleon calculations, and determining whether Chiral Perturbation Theory can provide a sound economical framework for few-nucleon systems.
