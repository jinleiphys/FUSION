# Neutrino-Nucleus Quasi-Elastic Scattering in a Relativistic Model

> A relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation model is applied to neutral-current and charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering. The effects of final state interactions are investigated and the sensitivity of the results to the strange nucleon form factors is discussed in view of their possible experimental determination

## Key claim

A relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) model, previously tested on exclusive (e,e'p) reactions, is applied to neutral-current and charged-current quasi-elastic neutrino-nucleus scattering; the authors find that final-state interactions (FSI) produce large reductions in cross sections but are almost negligible in cross-section ratios, which are proposed as a more robust observable for extracting the strange axial form factor.

## Method

The authors compute semi-inclusive cross sections for one-nucleon knockout in quasi-elastic neutrino and antineutrino scattering on 12C, using a relativistic distorted-wave impulse-approximation (RDWIA) model. Bound nucleon states are Dirac-Hartree solutions from relativistic mean-field theory, and scattering states are obtained from an energy- and A-dependent optical potential (EDAD1) via a direct Pauli reduction scheme. The hadronic weak current includes vector, axial-vector, and induced pseudoscalar terms, with strange-quark contributions parameterized by the constants g_s_A, mu_s, and rho_s.

## Key numbers

- Fermi constant G_F = 1.16639 x 10^-11 MeV^-2; for charged-current processes, multiplied by cos^2(theta_C) = 0.9749.
- Weinberg angle sin^2(theta_W) = 0.23143.
- Axial mass M_A = (1.026 +/- 0.021) GeV.
- Axial coupling constant g_A = 1.26.
- Vector mass M_V = 0.843 GeV.
- FSI reduce cross sections by approximately 50% when comparing RPWIA to RDWIA results at incident neutrino energies of 500 and 1000 MeV.
- With g_s_A = -0.10, proton knockout cross sections are enhanced and neutron knockout cross sections reduced by about 10% relative to calculations with no strangeness.

## Context

This work extends the RDWIA approach previously applied to exclusive (e,e'p) reactions (Meucci et al., 2001) to neutrino-nucleus scattering, and complements earlier inclusive charged-current calculations using a relativistic Green's function approach (Giusti et al., 2004). The study investigates the sensitivity of neutral-current cross sections and their ratios to strange-quark form factors, building on prior work by Alberico et al. (1997) and Barbaro et al. (1998) on nuclear structure effects in strange-quark determinations, and by Horowitz et al. (1992) and Martinez et al. (2005) on FSI effects in proton-to-neutron ratios. The results enable experimental extraction of the strange axial form factor g_s_A from cross-section ratios at facilities such as FINeSSE, with complementary parity-violating electron scattering needed to resolve vector strange form factors.
