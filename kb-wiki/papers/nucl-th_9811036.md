# The Delta-Nucleon Interaction in Hadronic Reactions on a Deuteron Target

> The pi+ d scattering and the d(p,n) charge exchange reaction are used to study the Delta-Nucleon interaction in the Delta resonance energy region. Various observables, such as inclusive and exclusive cross sections, angular distributions, and analyzing powers, are calculated in a coupled channel approach and shown to be influenced by the Delta-Nucleon potential.

# The Delta-Nucleon Interaction in Hadronic Reactions on a Deuteron Target

## Key claim

The Delta-Nucleon (ΔN) interaction influences observables in π⁺d scattering and the d(p,n) charge exchange reaction, but the effect is demonstrated only in a coupled channel model where the ΔN potential is constructed from meson exchange; the paper does not claim direct experimental extraction of the interaction strength.

## Method

The authors constructed a coupled channel model for the correlated ΔN wave function using the source function formalism and solved it with the Lanczos method. The ΔN potential was built from π, ρ, ω, and σ meson exchange following the meson exchange model of Machleidt (1987). The Δ resonance was treated as a quasi-particle with an energy-dependent width, and after Δ decay the system evolved into final states πNN, πd, or 2p.

## Key numbers

- Differential cross section dσ/dΩ and analyzing power A_y for π⁺d → 2p were calculated at T_π = 142 MeV, showing sensitivity to V_ΔN.
- Total cross section for inclusive d(p,n) and exclusive d(p,n)π⁺d coherent pion production was calculated at T_p = 789 MeV and θ_n = 0°, plotted as a function of energy transfer ω = E_p − E_n.
- The missing inclusive cross section in the dip region was observed between 50 MeV < ω < 250 MeV, attributed to meson exchange currents and projectile excitation.

## Context

This work extends earlier coupled channel models for π⁺d scattering by Maxwell (1980) and Garcilazo, and applies the same formalism to the d(p,n) charge exchange reaction previously studied by Mosbacher (1997). The use of virtual π and ρ meson fields in the (p,n) reaction allows probing the Δ excitation in a region inaccessible to real pion scattering (where ω² < q² rather than ω² = q² + m_π²). The exclusive d(p,n)π⁺d reaction is identified as a tool for directly measuring the attractive spin-longitudinal part of the ΔN interaction.
