# Signatures of the chiral critical endpoint of QCD in heavy-ion collisions: the role of finite-size effects

## Key claim

Finite-size effects in heavy-ion collisions may shift pseudocritical quantities significantly from the genuine critical endpoint in the thermodynamic limit, but the finite-size scaling behavior of correlation functions across different system sizes (centralities) can be turned into a complementary tool for identifying the QCD critical endpoint, rather than only an obstacle.

## Method

The authors review proposed signatures for the QCD critical endpoint in heavy-ion collisions, focusing on non-monotonic behavior in cumulants of particle number fluctuations (two-particle correlations scaling as ξ², skewness as ξ⁹/², kurtosis as ξ⁷) and discuss how finite system size limits correlation length growth and shifts pseudocritical quantities. They then describe how finite-size scaling analysis, applied to correlation functions from systems of different sizes (different centralities), can be used to test for criticality via curve collapsing in scaling plots, as previously proposed in Palhares (2009) and applied to RHIC and SPS data.

## Key numbers

- Two-particle correlations near the CEP scale as ⟨δn_p δn_k⟩ ∼ ξ², where ξ is the correlation length
- Skewness critical contribution scales as ω₃ ∼ ξ⁹/²
- Kurtosis critical contribution scales as ω₄ ∼ ξ⁷
- Finite-size scaling analysis of RHIC and SPS data suggests exclusion of a critical point below chemical potential μ ∼ 450 MeV
- Extrapolations from the same analysis point to criticality slightly above μ ∼ 500 MeV

## Context

This work extends the finite-size scaling approach to the QCD critical endpoint search introduced by Palhares (2009) and applies it to data from RHIC and SPS experiments. It contrasts with the original proposal by Stephanov (1998) for CEP signatures based on correlation length divergence, and addresses the recent observation from the RHIC Beam Energy Scan that kurtosis shows a decreasing trend rather than the expected increase, which Stephanov (2011) interprets as a signature from the crossover side. The analysis enables predictions for new data at lower center-of-mass energies currently being investigated in the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC.
