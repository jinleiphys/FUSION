# Fluctuations of the number of participants and binary collisions in AA interactions at fixed centrality in the Glauber approach

> In the framework of the classical Glauber approach, the analytical expressions for the variance of the number of wounded nucleons and binary collisions in AA interactions at a given centrality are presented. Along with the optical approximation term, they contain additional contact terms arising only in the case of nucleus-nucleus collisions. The magnitude of the additional contributions, e.g., for PbPb collisions at SPS energies, is larger than the contribution of the optical approximation at some values of the impact parameter. The sum of the additional contributions is in good agreement with the results of independent Monte Carlo simulations of this process. Due to these additional terms, the variance of the total number of participants for peripheral PbPb collisions and the variance of the number of collisions at all values of the impact parameter exceed several multiples of the Poisson variances. The correlator between the numbers of participants in colliding nuclei at fixed centrality is also analytically calculated.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the variance of the number of wounded nucleons and binary collisions in AA interactions at fixed centrality contains additional "contact terms" beyond the optical approximation, which arise only in nucleus-nucleus collisions and depend on the detailed shape of the NN interaction probability in the impact parameter plane, not just the total inelastic cross-section.

## Method

The authors derived analytical expressions for the variance of the number of wounded nucleons and binary collisions in AA collisions at fixed impact parameter within the classical Glauber approach, using probabilistic averaging over nucleon positions in both nuclei with Woods-Saxon profile functions. They compared their analytical results with independent Monte Carlo simulations of the same process, using both black disk and Gaussian approximations for the NN interaction probability, for PbPb collisions at SPS energies.

## Key numbers

- For PbPb collisions at SPS energies (σ_NN = 31 mb), the variance of the number of wounded nucleons in one nucleus at intermediate and large impact parameters receives approximately equal contributions from the optical term and the contact term (Fig. 1)
- The variance of the total number of participants for peripheral PbPb collisions (impact parameter 8-12 fm) is a few times larger than the Poisson variance (Fig. 5, Fig. 6)
- The variance of the number of binary collisions at all values of the impact parameter is a few times larger than the Poisson variance, while the optical approximation variance practically coincides with the Poisson one (Fig. 8, Fig. 9)
- The mean number of wounded nucleons in one nucleus depends only on σ_NN in the limit r_N << R_A, R_B, while the variance depends on the shape of σ(b) through the integral I(a) = ∫ db σ(b) σ(b+a)

## Context

This work extends the classical Glauber approach of Bialas (1976) beyond the optical approximation used in earlier treatments by Wong and Vogt, which gave correct mean values but incorrect variances for nucleus-nucleus collisions. The analytical results enable understanding of why multiplicity fluctuations in AA collisions at SPS and RHIC energies exceed Poisson expectations, as observed by NA49 and RHIC experiments. The contact terms correspond to loop diagrams in the quantum Glauber approach discussed by Boreskov (1988), Pak (1979), and Braun (1987-1990), and the paper enables future work incorporating nucleon-nucleon correlations within one nucleus, which would increase the contact term contributions further.
