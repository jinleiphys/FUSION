# Towards high-order calculations of three-nucleon scattering in chiral effective field theory

> We discuss the current status of chiral effective field theory in the three-nucleon sector and present selected results for nucleon-deuteron scattering observables based on semilocal momentum-space-regularized chiral two-nucleon potentials together with consistently regularized three-nucleon forces up to third chiral order. Using a Bayesian model for estimating truncation errors, the obtained results are found to provide a good description of the experimental data. We confirm our earlier findings that a high-precision description of nucleon-deuteron scattering data below pion production threshold will require the theory to be pushed to fifth chiral order. This conclusion is substantiated by an exploratory study of selected short-range contributions to the three-nucleon force at that order, which, as expected, are found to have significant effects on polarization observables at intermediate and high energies. We also outline the challenges that will need to be addressed in order to push the chiral expansion of three-nucleon scattering observables to higher orders.

## Key claim

The paper confirms that a high-precision description of nucleon-deuteron scattering data below pion production threshold will require pushing chiral effective field theory to fifth order (N4LO), but this conclusion is hedged by the fact that the presented N2LO results already provide a good description of experimental data when using a Bayesian model for estimating truncation errors.

## Method

The authors computed nucleon-deuteron scattering observables using semilocal momentum-space-regularized (SMS) chiral two-nucleon potentials from Reinert (2017) combined with consistently regularized three-nucleon forces at N2LO. They employed a Bayesian model for estimating truncation errors, using a modified version of the approach from Furnstahl (2015) and Melendez (2017), with the expansion parameter defined using a breakdown scale of 650 MeV and an effective pion mass of 200 MeV. The low-energy constants c_D and c_E were determined by fitting to the triton binding energy and the nucleon-deuteron differential cross section minimum at 70 MeV laboratory energy.

## Key numbers

* The three-nucleon force low-energy constants c_1, c_3, and c_4 were taken from the Roy-Steiner-equation analysis of Hoferichter (2015) with values c_1 = -0.74 GeV^{-1}, c_3 = -3.61 GeV^{-1}, and c_4 = 2.44 GeV^{-1}
* The fitted c_D values ranged from 8.9 (for cutoff Lambda = 400 MeV) to -5.4 (for cutoff Lambda = 550 MeV), while c_E ranged from 1.15 to -0.25 over the same cutoff range
* The breakdown scale was set to Lambda_b = 650 MeV, with the expansion parameter Q defined using an effective pion mass of 200 MeV
* The pion production threshold in the nucleon-deuteron system corresponds to E_lab^N approximately 215 MeV, with p_CM approximately 425 MeV and expansion parameter Q = 0.65
* The success rate for next-higher order results lying within the estimated 68% degree-of-belief intervals was 62.5-75%, and 100% for the 95% intervals

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study Epelbaum (2018) by replacing semilocal coordinate-space regularized (SCS) NN forces with the newer SMS chiral NN potentials from Reinert (2017), and by replacing the earlier EKM algorithm for truncation error estimation with a Bayesian approach from Furnstahl (2015) and Melendez (2017). The results are compared to calculations using the CD Bonn NN potential from Machleidt (2000) and the Tucson-Melbourne 3NF from Coon (2001). The paper outlines the challenges that must be addressed to push chiral expansion of three-nucleon scattering to higher orders, particularly the need for consistent regularization of many-body forces and exchange operators at N3LO and beyond.
