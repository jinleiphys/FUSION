# Implementation of Cluster expansion for hot QCD matter

> We present a cluster expansion EoS model for strongly-interacting matter based on the generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formalism to describe hadrons as bound clusters of quarks. This formalism can describe both confined and deconfined phases. Our emphasis is on the region of vanishing baryon densities, where numerical solutions available from Lattice QCD predict a smooth crossover transition from hadron to quark matter. Medium effects are taken into account as self energies, which are motivated from both perturbative QCD calculations and phenomenological models. Parameters are tuned to Lattice QCD data and result in a good agreement of the thermodynamics.

## Key claim

The cluster expansion model, using a generalized Beth-Uhlenbeck formalism with simple step-function phase shifts and self-energies motivated by perturbative QCD and a linear string potential, can qualitatively reproduce Lattice QCD thermodynamics at vanishing baryon density, but shows quantitative deviations in the transition region around T ≈ 150 MeV.

## Method

The authors implement a cluster expansion equation of state for strongly-interacting matter, treating hadrons as bound clusters of quarks and partons as quasi-particles. Medium effects are incorporated via scalar self-energies for quarks, gluons, and hadrons, with a generating scalar density that counts color charges. The self-energy parameters are first fitted to effective masses from a separate quasi-particle model at high temperatures (T ≥ 500 MeV), then the remaining confinement and hadronic correction parameters are fitted directly to Lattice QCD entropy density data from Bazavov (2014).

## Key numbers

*   High-temperature fit parameters: σ₂,l = 226.7 MeV, σ₂,s = 182.2 MeV, σ₂,g = 342.2 MeV, σ₄ = 0.1414 MeV fm³, C = 35.02 MeV fm.
*   Transition-region fit parameters: A = 7.0 MeV fm³, B = 2.8 fm⁻³, B₀ = 7.6 fm⁻³, D = 200.0 MeV fm, D_g = 100.0 MeV fm.
*   The Mott transition for pions (crossing of effective pion mass and two-light-quark threshold mass) occurs at T ∼ 600 MeV.
*   Partons begin to populate the system at temperatures as low as T ≈ 80 MeV, with mesons dominating up to that point.

## Context

This work extends the cluster expansion model of Bastian (2018) from high baryon density and low temperature to the high-temperature, low-density regime accessible to Lattice QCD. It provides a framework that can describe both confined and deconfined phases, enabling future construction of a "coast-to-coast" equation of state covering the entire QCD phase diagram. The approach contrasts with Dyson-Schwinger and functional renormalization group methods by including baryons and using a phenomenological treatment of confinement via a linear string potential.
