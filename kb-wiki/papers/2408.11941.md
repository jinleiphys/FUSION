# $ϕ$-$p$ bound state and completeness of quantum states

> The existence of a bound state of a $ϕ$ meson and proton has been recently indicated on the basis of a combined analysis of the measured $ϕ$-$p$ correlation function and the QCD lattice calculations of $ϕ$-$p$ interaction in the spin 1/2 and 3/2 channels. We check whether the correlation functions and bound state formation rate satisfy the sum rule which follows from the completeness of quantum states. The sum rule is not satisfied but it is close to it, suggesting that the inferred scattering parameters, which determine the correlation functions, are not fully consistent with the potential responsible for the bound state existence.

# $\phi$-$p$ bound state and completeness of quantum states

## Key claim

The correlation functions and bound state formation rate derived from the combined ALICE data and HAL QCD lattice calculations do not satisfy the quantum completeness sum rule, but they are close to it, suggesting that the inferred scattering parameters are not fully consistent with the potential responsible for the bound state existence.

## Method

The authors computed the $\phi$-$p$ correlation functions in the spin 1/2 and 3/2 channels using the asymptotic scattering wave function with the Lednicky-Lyuboshitz formalism, using scattering parameters from Chizzali et al. (2022). They solved the Schrodinger equation with the potential from Chizzali et al. (2022) using the Rayleigh-Ritz variational method with two different basis sets (trigonometric and spherical harmonic oscillator) to obtain the bound state wave function and binding energy. The bound state formation rate was then computed from the wave function and compared with the left-hand side of the sum rule integral over the difference of the two spin-channel correlation functions.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy of the $\phi$-$p$ bound state in the spin 1/2 channel: $E_B = -28.309$ MeV (face values of potential parameters), with an imaginary part of $-0.112i$ MeV when including the maximal imaginary potential parameter $\gamma = -5.4$
- Scattering length in spin 1/2 channel: $\Re a_{1/2} = 1.54^{+0.62}_{-0.69}$ fm, $\Im a_{1/2} = 0.00^{+0.00}_{-0.51}$ fm
- Scattering length in spin 3/2 channel: $a_{3/2} = -1.43^{+0.59}_{-0.29}$ fm (purely real)
- Effective range in spin 1/2 channel: $\Re d_{1/2} = 0.39^{+0.11}_{-0.12}$ fm, $\Im d_{1/2} = 0.00^{+0.00}_{-0.06}$ fm
- Effective range in spin 3/2 channel: $d_{3/2} = 2.36^{+0.12}_{-0.58}$ fm (purely real)
- Reduced mass of $\phi$ and $p$: $\mu = 488.6$ MeV
- Source size parameter used for correlation function calculations: $r_0 = 2$ fm
- The sum rule is satisfied for $r_0 \lesssim 2$ fm but not for $r_0 \gtrsim 2$ fm where the computation is reliable

## Context

This work extends the analysis of Chizzali et al. (2022), who combined the ALICE collaboration (2021) measurement of the $\phi$-$p$ femtoscopic correlation function with HAL QCD lattice calculations (Lyu et al., 2022) to infer spin-dependent scattering parameters and a potential that implies a $\phi$-$p$ bound state. The authors apply the sum rule formalism developed by Mrowczynski (1994), Maj (2004), and Maj (2019) for femtoscopic correlation functions, which follows from the completeness of quantum states. The sum rule was previously shown to relate the integrated neutron-proton correlation function to the deuteron formation rate. The present work checks consistency between the scattering parameters and the bound state potential from Chizzali et al. (2022), finding that the sum rule is not satisfied but close, and suggests that increasing the spin 3/2 scattering length to 6 fm or explicitly including inelastic channels (such as $\phi p \leftrightarrow K^+ \Lambda$) could resolve the discrepancy.
