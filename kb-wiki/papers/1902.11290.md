# Examination of Flow and Non-Flow Factorization Methods in Small Collision Systems

> Two particle correlations have been used extensively to study hydrodynamic flow patterns in heavy-ion collisions. In small collision systems, such as $p$$+$$p$ and $p$$+$$A$, where particle multiplicities are much smaller than in $A$$+$$A$ collisions, non-flow effects from jet correlations, momentum conservation, particle decays, etc. can be significant, even when imposing a large pseudorapidity gap between the particles. A number of techniques to subtract the non-flow contribution in two particle correlations have been developed by experiments at the Large Hadron Collider (LHC) and then used to measure particle flow in $p$$+$$p$ and $p$$+$Pb collisions. Recently, experiments at the Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider (RHIC) have explored the possibility of adopting these techniques for small collision systems at lower energies. In this paper, we test these techniques using Monte Carlo generators PYTHIA and HIJING, which do not include any collective flow, and AMPT, which does. We find that it is crucial to examine the results of such tests both for correlations integrated over particle transverse momentum $p_T$ and differentially as a function of $p_T$. Our results indicate reasonable non-flow subtraction for $p$$+$$p$ collisions at the highest LHC energies, while failing if applied to $p$$+$$p$ collisions at RHIC. In the case of $p$$+$Au collisions at RHIC, both HIJING and AMPT results indicate a substantial over-subtraction of non-flow for $p_{T}\gtrsim1~{\rm GeV}/c$ and hence an underestimate of elliptic flow.

## Key claim

The paper tests non-flow subtraction methods (template fitting and near-side jet yield scaling) using Monte Carlo generators that lack collective flow (PYTHIA and HIJING) and one that includes it (AMPT), finding that the methods work reasonably for proton-proton collisions at LHC energies but fail for proton-proton and proton-gold collisions at RHIC energies, with substantial over-subtraction of non-flow for transverse momentum above 1 GeV/c.

## Method

The authors applied two non-flow subtraction methods, Method 1 (ATLAS template fitting) and Method 2 (CMS near-side jet yield scaling), to two-particle correlation functions with a pseudorapidity gap of 2 < |Δη| < 5 in Monte Carlo events from PYTHIA8, HIJING, and AMPT. They compared the extracted elliptic flow coefficients (v₂) before and after subtraction across different multiplicity classes and as a function of transverse momentum, using the absence of flow in PYTHIA and HIJING as a closure test to quantify residual non-flow.

## Key numbers

- In PYTHIA proton-proton collisions at √s = 13 TeV, the subtracted v₂ (v₂^sub) remained within |v₂| < 0.03 over the full multiplicity range when using low multiplicity reference selections of 10 ≤ Nch < 20 or 20 ≤ Nch < 30.
- For proton-proton collisions at √s = 13 TeV with Nch ≥ 85, v₂^sub values were smaller than 0.001 for trigger pT between 0.5 and 5 GeV/c when using reference events with 10 ≤ Nch < 20 or 20 ≤ Nch < 30.
- The ratio j_n = d_n^HM / d_n^LM in PYTHIA at √s = 13 TeV showed violations of the assumption that j_n is independent of harmonic n, with j_1 deviating from j_2 by -20% and j_3 deviating by +40% at higher multiplicities.
- For proton-gold collisions at RHIC, both HIJING and AMPT results indicated substantial over-subtraction of non-flow for pT ≳ 1 GeV/c.

## Context

This work extends the testing of non-flow subtraction methods originally developed by the ATLAS Collaboration (Aad et al., 2015) and CMS Collaboration (Khachatryan et al., 2016) for LHC proton-proton and proton-lead collisions, applying them to lower-energy RHIC systems. The results indicate that these methods cannot be straightforwardly adopted for RHIC proton-proton and proton-gold collisions without significant systematic uncertainties, and that future work should develop improved subtraction techniques that account for the multiplicity dependence of non-flow harmonic coefficients.
