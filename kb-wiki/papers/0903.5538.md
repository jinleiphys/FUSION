# Universal Correlations in Pion-less EFT with the Resonating Group Model: Three and Four Nucleons

> The Effective Field Theory "without pions" at next-to-leading order is used to analyze universal bound state and scattering properties of the 3- and 4-nucleon system. Results of a variety of phase shift equivalent nuclear potentials are presented for bound state properties of 3H and 4He, and for the singlet S-wave 3He-neutron scattering length a_0(3He-n). The calculations are performed with the Refined Resonating Group Method and include a full treatment of the Coulomb interaction and the leading-order 3-nucleon interaction. The results compare favorably with data and values from AV18(+UIX) model calculations. A new correlation between a_0(3He-n) and the 3H binding energy is found. Furthermore, we confirm at next-to-leading order the correlations, already found at leading-order, between the 3H binding energy and the 3H charge radius, and the Tjon line. With the 3H binding energy as input, we get predictions of the Effective Field Theory "without pions" at next-to-leading order for the root mean square charge radius of 3H of (1.6\pm 0.2) fm, for the 4He binding energy of (28\pm 2.5) MeV, and for Re(a_0(3He-n)) of (7.5\pm 0.6)fm. Including the Coulomb interaction, the splitting in binding energy between 3H and 3He is found to be (0.66\pm 0.03) MeV. The discrepancy to data of (0.10\mp 0.03) MeV is model independently attributed to higher order charge independence breaking interactions. We also demonstrate that different results for the same observable stem from higher order effects, and carefully assess that numerical uncertainties are negligible. Our results demonstrate the convergence and usefulness of the pion-less theory at next-to-leading order in the 4He channel. We conclude that no 4-nucleon interaction is needed to renormalize the theory at next-to-leading order in the 4-nucleon sector.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that pion-less Effective Field Theory at next-to-leading order converges for the four-nucleon system and that no four-nucleon interaction is required to renormalize the theory at this order, provided the three-nucleon interaction strength is fitted to the triton binding energy.

## Method

The authors used the Refined Resonating Group Method (RRGM), a variational approach in coordinate space with a Gaussian basis, to solve the Schrödinger equation for three- and four-nucleon systems. They employed the pion-less Effective Field Theory (EFT\(_{\not{\pi}}\)) potential at next-to-leading order, including the leading-order three-nucleon contact interaction and a full treatment of the Coulomb interaction, with the low-energy constants fitted to reproduce the deuteron binding energy and Nijmegen phase shifts.

## Key numbers

- Triton (\(^3\)H) root mean square charge radius: \((1.6 \pm 0.2)\) fm, predicted with the \(^3\)H binding energy as input
- \(^4\)He binding energy: \((28 \pm 2.5)\) MeV, predicted with the \(^3\)H binding energy as input
- Real part of the singlet S-wave \(^3\)He-neutron scattering length: \(\text{Re}\{a_0(^3\text{He-n})\} = (7.5 \pm 0.6)\) fm, predicted with the \(^3\)H binding energy as input
- Binding energy splitting between \(^3\)H and \(^3\)He including Coulomb interaction: \((0.66 \pm 0.03)\) MeV, with a discrepancy to data of \((0.10 \mp 0.03)\) MeV attributed to higher-order charge independence breaking interactions

## Context

This work extends leading-order calculations of the Tjon line by Platter et al. (2005) and the bound four-nucleon system to next-to-leading order, confirming universal correlations between three- and four-nucleon observables. It introduces a new correlation between the \(^3\)He-neutron scattering length and the triton binding energy, and demonstrates that the RRGM is a feasible numerical tool for EFT\(_{\not{\pi}}\) calculations in heavier systems, enabling future studies beyond the four-nucleon sector.
