# Gravitational form factors in the perturbative limit

> Generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) have attracted significant attention in recent years due to their connection with the energy-momentum tensor (EMT) form factors (FFs). The GDAs can be experimentally accessed through the study of amplitudes in $γ^{\ast} γ\to M_1 M_2$ and $γ^{\ast} \to M_1 M_2 γ$, where $M_1M_2$ is a pseudoscalar meson pair such as $πη$ and $ηη^{\prime}$. In this work, we calculate these amplitudes in the perturbative limit and express the extracted $M_1M_2$ GDAs in terms of meson distribution amplitudes that have been constrained by the previous experiments. Our explicit calculation verifies the existence of a new EMT FF that violates the conservation law of EMT when the hadronic matrix element of the EMT operator is considered separately for each quark flavor. In addition, our result shows that the $M_1M_2$ GDAs are identical in $γ^{\ast} γ\to M_1 M_2$ and $γ^{\ast} \to M_1 M_2 γ$, which confirms the universality of GDAs in the perturbative limit. In the future, the GDAs and the EMT FFs studied in this paper can be probed at Belle II. Our study enhances the accessibility to the $P$-wave GDAs in $γ^{\ast} γ\to M_1 M_2$ and $γ^{\ast} \to M_1 M_2 γ$, and provides a promising approach for searching for exotic hybrid mesons in future experiments.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the shear viscosity term \(\Theta_3^q\) of the energy-momentum tensor form factors, which violates conservation of the EMT for a single quark flavor, exists and is nonzero for \(\pi\eta\) and \(\pi\eta^{\prime}\) pairs in the perturbative limit, but cancels when summing over all quark flavors; this result is conditional on the meson distribution amplitudes differing between \(\pi\) and \(\eta\) or on the gluon distribution amplitude being nonzero.

## Method

The authors calculate the leading-twist helicity amplitudes for \(\gamma^{\ast}\gamma \to M_1 M_2\) and \(\gamma^{\ast} \to M_1 M_2 \gamma\) in the perturbative limit \(Q^2 \gg s \gg \Lambda_{\text{QCD}}^2\), using QCD factorization to express the amplitudes in terms of meson distribution amplitudes (DAs) expanded in Gegenbauer polynomials. They extract the \(M_1 M_2\) generalized distribution amplitudes (GDAs) from these amplitudes, then take the first moment of the GDAs to obtain the transition EMT form factors \(\Theta_1^q\), \(\Theta_2^q\), and \(\Theta_3^q\) in terms of the meson DAs and their Gegenbauer coefficients.

## Key numbers

- The Gegenbauer coefficient for the pion DA at scale \(\mu_F = \sqrt{30}\) GeV is \(a_2^{\pi} \sim 0.16\), evolved from \(a_2^{\pi} \sim 0.25\) at \(\mu_0 = 1\) GeV.
- For the \(\eta\) meson, the octet Gegenbauer coefficient is \(\hat{a}_2(\mu_F) = -0.032\), the singlet coefficient is \(\bar{a}_2(\mu_F) = -0.027\), and the gluon DA coefficient is \(b_2(\mu_F) = 7.6\), evolved from \(\hat{a}_2(\mu_0) = -0.05\), \(\bar{a}_2(\mu_0) = -0.12\), and \(b_2(\mu_0) = 19\) at \(\mu_0 = 1\) GeV.
- The resulting \(\eta\) DA Gegenbauer coefficient is \(a_2^{\eta}(\mu_F) \sim -0.03\) and similarly \(a_2^{\eta^{\prime}}(\mu_F) \sim -0.03\).
- The ratio \(\Theta_3^q/\Theta_2^q\) is estimated to be about \(0.055\) under model assumptions, slightly smaller than the holographic bound of \(1/4\pi \approx 0.08\) from Kovtun (2004).
- The kinematic reach at Belle II is \(Q^2 \sim 100\) GeV\(^2\) and \(s \sim 10\) GeV\(^2\), satisfying the perturbative limit.

## Context

This work extends the perturbative treatment of \(\pi\pi\) GDAs by Diehl (1999) to the case of two different pseudoscalar mesons (\(\pi\eta\) and \(\eta\eta^{\prime}\)), enabling access to \(P\)-wave GDAs and the new EMT form factor \(\Theta_3\) proposed by Teryaev (2022). The results confirm the universality of GDAs between spacelike (\(\gamma^{\ast}\gamma \to M_1 M_2\)) and timelike (\(\gamma^{\ast} \to M_1 M_2 \gamma\)) processes, analogous to GPD universality discussed by Mueller (2012). The study enables future experimental searches for exotic hybrid mesons with \(J^{PC}=1^{-+}\) (such as \(\eta_1(1855)\) reported by BESIII in 2022) at Belle II and BESIII, and provides a connection between the shear viscosity term and the holographic viscosity-to-entropy ratio bound from Kovtun (2004).
