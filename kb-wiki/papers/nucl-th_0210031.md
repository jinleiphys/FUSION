# Weakly screened thermonuclear reactions in astrophysical plasmas: Improving Salpeter's model

> This paper presents a detailed study of the electron degeneracy and nonlinear screening effects which play a crucial role in the validity of Salpeter's weak-screening model. The limitations of that model are investigated and an improved one is proposed which can take into account nonlinear screening effects. Its application to the solar pp reaction derives an accurate screening enhancement factor and provides a very reliable estimation of the associated neutrino flux uncertanties.

## Key claim

Salpeter's weak-screening model for thermonuclear reactions in astrophysical plasmas is shown to be invalid across the entire Coulomb barrier under essentially all stellar conditions, because the Debye-Huckel potential on which it relies cannot be used inside the tunneling region where thermonuclear reactions actually occur; the paper proposes an improved model that accounts for nonlinear screening effects and provides analytic degeneracy factors for partially degenerate plasmas.

## Method

The author derives Salpeter's screening enhancement factor (SEF) from first principles using Thomas-Fermi theory, then extends the formalism to partially degenerate environments by expanding the Fermi-Dirac function of one-half in the appropriate degeneracy regimes. A tuning equation is defined to determine the radial region where the Debye-Huckel potential is valid, and a new model is constructed that incorporates both electron degeneracy effects (via a novel analytic degeneracy factor) and nonlinear screening corrections. The model is applied to the solar pp reaction to compute the screening enhancement factor and associated neutrino flux uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- For the solar pp reaction at the center of the sun: Debye radius \(R_D = 24819\) fm, Salpeter SEF \(f_s^{pp} = 1.049\), most effective energy \(E_0 = 5.6\) keV, thermal energy \(kT = 1.2\) keV
- The classical turning point for the solar pp reaction is \(r_{tp} = 10^{-2} R_D\) (approximately 248 fm)
- The Debye-Huckel potential is only valid for \(r \gg 8.7339 \times 10^{-2} R_D\) (approximately 2168 fm) for the solar case with \(Z_{\max}=2\)
- The weak-screening condition for the pp reaction would require \(T_6 \gg 29274\) to be obeyed across the entire Coulomb barrier
- Non-degenerate (ND) limit defined at degeneracy parameter \(a^* = 2\), corresponding to \(\rho/\mu_e \leq T_6^{3/2}\)
- Strong degeneracy (SD) limit defined at \(a \leq -5\), corresponding to \(\rho/\mu_e \geq 71 T_6^{3/2}\)
- Complete degeneracy (CD) limit defined at \(a \leq -10\), corresponding to \(\rho/\mu_e \geq 193 T_6^{3/2}\)
- Intermediate degeneracy (ID) limit at \(\rho/\mu_e = 24 T_6^{3/2}\) (degeneracy parameter \(a = -2\))
- For central solar conditions: \(\rho/\mu_e \simeq 148\), \(T_6 \simeq 15.6\), degeneracy parameter \(a = 1.1\)
- For the solar region of maximum energy production (0.075 solar radii): \(T_6 = 14\), \(\rho = 105\) g/cm³, degeneracy parameter \(a = 1.3\)

## Context

This work extends and corrects Salpeter's original weak-screening model (Salpeter, 1954) and Mitler's model (Mitler, 1977) for screening in stellar plasmas, addressing the need identified in the Adelberger (1998) solar fusion cross section study for a satisfactory analytical investigation of electron degeneracy and nonlinear screening effects. The paper improves upon the thermal corrections to dielectric screening derived by Shaviv and Shaviv (1996), replacing their approximation \((1 - \pi^2/24a^2)\) with a more accurate expression. The new analytic degeneracy factor avoids the computationally expensive improper integrals currently used in stellar evolution codes and enables reliable estimation of neutrino flux uncertainties from screening effects in the solar pp reaction.
