# Analytic approach to nuclear rotational states: The role of spin - A minimal model -

> We use a simple field theory model to investigate the role of the nucleon spin for the magnetic sum rules associated with the low-lying collective scissors mode in deformed nuclei. Various constraints from rotational symmetry are elucidated and discussed. We put special emphasis on the coupling of the spin part of the M1 operator to the low lying collective modes, and investigate how this coupling changes the sum rules.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the low-lying scissors mode, generated by the isovector combination of proton and neutron total angular momenta, remains the dominant contributor to the inverse energy weighted M1 sum rule even when the nucleon spin is included via the spin-orbit interaction, but the sum rule receives non-trivial modifications from interference terms that the abstract does not detail.

## Method

The authors apply mean-field approximation and random-phase approximation (RPA) to a model Hamiltonian with a separable quadrupole-quadrupole interaction, including a one-body spin-orbit potential but no spin-dependent two-body interactions. They use Ward-Takahashi identities derived from angular momentum conservation to constrain the correlation functions and derive the M1 sum rules analytically.

## Key numbers

- Free proton g-factors: \(g_{\ell p}^{\rm free}=1\), \(g_{s p}^{\rm free}=5.58\); free neutron g-factors: \(g_{\ell n}^{\rm free}=0\), \(g_{s n}^{\rm free}=-3.82\)
- Derived spin-orbit coupling parameters: \(h_p^{\rm free}=4.58\) and \(h_n^{\rm free}=-3.82\) (from \(h_\tau = g_{s\tau} - g_{\ell\tau}\))
- The Goldstone mode vertex normalization constant is \(N = \sqrt{3/I^J}\), where \(I^J\) is the total moment of inertia from the Inglis formula

## Context

This work extends the authors' earlier pure-orbital model (Bentz et al., 2010) by adding the nucleon spin via the spin-orbit interaction, while keeping the same quadrupole-quadrupole interaction framework. It addresses the known discrepancy between theoretical and experimental values of the inverse energy weighted M1 sum rule noted in prior work (PRC71, 2005). The minimal model deliberately excludes spin-spin interactions and pairing correlations, which the authors state will be treated in a separate work, and thus provides a baseline for understanding how the spin-orbit term alone modifies the scissors-mode sum rules.
