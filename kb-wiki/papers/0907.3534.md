# Parton Propagation and Fragmentation in QCD Matter

> We review recent progress in the study of parton propagation, interaction and fragmentation in both cold and hot strongly interacting matter. Experimental highlights on high-energy hadron production in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering, proton-nucleus and heavy-ion collisions, as well as Drell-Yan processes in hadron-nucleus collisions are presented. The existing theoretical frameworks for describing the in-medium interaction of energetic partons and the space-time evolution of their fragmentation into hadrons are discussed and confronted to experimental data. We conclude with a list of theoretical and experimental open issues, and a brief description of future relevant experiments and facilities.

# Parton Propagation and Fragmentation in QCD Matter

## Key claim

The multiplicity ratio $R_M^h$ alone cannot disentangle parton energy loss from prehadron absorption mechanisms; however, a scaling analysis of $R_M^h$ with a variable $\tau = C\, z_h^\lambda (1-z_h) \nu$ reveals that pion data from HERMES exhibit $\lambda_{\rm best} \approx 0.5 > 0$, indicating dominance of prehadron absorption over energy loss, with prehadron production times $\langle t_{preh}\rangle \lesssim R_A$.

## Method

The authors perform a $\chi^2$ minimisation fit of the scaling relation $R_M = R_M[\tau(z_h,\nu)]$ to combined $z_h$- and $\nu$-distributions of hadron multiplicity ratios measured by HERMES in deep inelastic lepton-nucleus scattering on N, Ne, and Kr targets at $E_{lab}=27$ GeV. The scaling exponent $\lambda$ is extracted as a free parameter, with positive values characteristic of absorption models and negative values characteristic of energy loss models. Additionally, the prehadron production time scale is estimated from preliminary HERMES $p_T$-broadening data by matching the observed $A^{1/3}$ scaling and $z$-dependence to the nuclear radius.

## Key numbers

- Scaling exponent for pions: $\lambda_{\rm best} \approx 0.5 \gneqq 0$, extracted from HERMES data at $E_{lab}=27$ GeV on Kr targets, indicating prehadron absorption dominance.
- Power-law fit of $1-R_M^h$ yields $\alpha = 0.61 \pm 0.14$ for HERMES data on {He, N, Ne, Kr} targets at $z=0.65$, compatible with $A^{2/3}$ but excluding $A^{1/3}$.
- Estimated prehadron production time scale: $\langle t_{preh}\rangle \approx 0.8\, z^{0.5} (1-z) \,\nu$ fm/GeV, derived from HERMES $p_T$-broadening data with $\bar z=0.4$, $\bar\nu=14$ GeV, and $\langle L_{\rm Xe}\rangle \approx (3/4)R_A$.
- Average prehadron production times from HERMES kinematics range from 0.7 fm (at $z=0.92$) to 5.8 fm (at $\nu=18.6$ GeV) for Kr and Xe targets.
- Perturbative estimate of hadron formation times at HERMES: $\pi$: 36 fm, K: 11 fm, p: 3.8 fm, D: 1.3 fm, B: 0.2 fm (for $z\approx0.5$, $\nu\approx14$ GeV).
- Perturbative estimate of hadron formation times at RHIC mid-rapidity: $\pi$: 26 fm, K: 10 fm, p: 3.8 fm, D: 1.5 fm, B: 0.2 fm (for $p_T^h \approx 7$ GeV).

## Context

This review extends the theoretical and experimental work on parton energy loss models (Baier 1996, Wang 2002, Arleo 2003) and prehadron absorption models (Bialas 1986, Kopeliovich 2003, Falter 2004, Gallmeister 2005) by proposing a scaling analysis to distinguish between these mechanisms in deep inelastic scattering. The scaling variable approach builds on earlier work by Accardi (2006) and provides a method to extract the prehadron production time from existing HERMES data. The paper identifies open questions regarding the $Q^2$ dependence of $p_T$-broadening, which challenges the colour dipole model prediction of Kopeliovich (2003), and outlines future experimental investigations needed at the Electron-Ion Collider (EIC) to resolve the discrepancy between HERMES and CLAS production time estimates.
