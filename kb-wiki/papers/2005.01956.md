# Finite-volume formalism in the $2 \xrightarrow[]{H_I+H_I} 2$ transition: an application to the lattice QCD calculation of double beta decays

> We present the formalism for connecting a second-order electroweak $2\xrightarrow[]{H_I+H_I}2$ transition amplitudes in the finite volume (with two hadrons in the initial and final states) to the physical amplitudes in the infinite volume. Our study mainly focus on the case where the low-lying intermediate state consists of two scattering hadrons. As a side product we also reproduce the finite-volume formula for $2\xrightarrow[]{H_I}2$ transition, originally obtained by Briceño and Hansen. With the available finite-volume formalism, we further discuss how to treat with the finite-volume problem in the double beta decays $nn\to pp ee\barν\barν$ and $nn\to pp ee$.

# Finite-volume formalism in the $2 \xrightarrow[]{H_I+H_I} 2$ transition: an application to the lattice QCD calculation of double beta decays

## Key claim

The paper derives a finite-volume formalism connecting second-order electroweak $2\xrightarrow[]{H_I+H_I}2$ transition amplitudes computed on the lattice to infinite-volume physical amplitudes, specifically for the case where the low-lying intermediate state consists of two scattering hadrons. The derivation relies on a perturbative approach and the coupled-channel quantization condition, and the authors note that the formalism applies when the initial and final states are not exactly degenerate; when $E_\alpha^{(0)} \to E_\beta^{(0)}$, the formula becomes singular and must be treated by equating pole residues.

## Method

The authors use second-order degenerate perturbation theory within a finite-volume quantum field theory framework, starting from a Hamiltonian $H^L = H_0^L + H_I^L$ where $H_0^L$ is the strong interaction and $H_I^L$ represents non-QCD (electroweak) interactions. They construct a two-state subspace of low-lying eigenstates $|\alpha\rangle^L$ and $|\beta\rangle^L$ of $H_0^L$, then derive the energy shift $\Delta E_\alpha$ due to the $2\xrightarrow[]{H_I+H_I}2$ transition. This finite-volume energy shift is related to the infinite-volume scattering amplitude via the coupled-channel quantization condition of He, Liu, and collaborators (2005), yielding the master formula Eq. (1). The derivation follows the Lellouch-Luscher approach (2000) rather than the Kim-Sachrajda-Sharpe (KSS) Poisson summation method.

## Key numbers

- The energy shift $\Delta E_\alpha$ in finite volume is given by $\Delta E_\alpha = \frac{|{^L}\langle\beta|H_I^L|\alpha\rangle^L|^2}{E_\alpha^{(0)}-E_{\beta}^{(0)}} + \sum_{n_\beta\neq\beta}\frac{|{^L}\langle n_\beta|H_I^L|\alpha\rangle^L|^2}{E_\alpha^{(0)}-E_{n_\beta}^{(0)}}$, where $E_\alpha^{(0)}$ and $E_\beta^{(0)}$ are the discrete energies of the two strong-interaction eigenstates.
- The master formula: $\frac{d\left(\phi+\delta_\alpha^{(0)}\right)}{dE}\Delta E_\alpha + \Delta\delta_\alpha = \frac{1}{4}\cot\left(\phi+\delta_\beta^{(0)}\right)|\langle E,\mathrm{in},\beta|H_I|E,\mathrm{in},\alpha\rangle|^2$, evaluated at $E=E_\alpha^{(0)}$, where $\phi$ is Luscher's kinematic function, $\delta_\alpha^{(0)}$ and $\delta_\beta^{(0)}$ are strong scattering phases, and $\Delta\delta_\alpha$ is the infinite-volume $2\xrightarrow[]{H_I+H_I}2$ matrix element.
- In the degenerate limit $E_\beta^{(0)}\to E_\alpha^{(0)}$, the finite-volume formula reduces to $h_\alpha' |{^L}\langle\beta|H_I^L|\alpha\rangle^L|^2 h_\beta' = \frac{1}{4}|\langle E,\mathrm{in},\beta|H_I|E,\mathrm{in},\alpha\rangle|^2$, where $h_i = \phi + \delta_i^{(0)}$ and $h_i' = dh_i/dE$ for $i=\alpha,\beta$, reproducing the $2\xrightarrow[]{J}2$ transition formula of Briceno and Hansen (2015).
- For the $2\xrightarrow[]{H_I}1\xrightarrow[]{H_I}2$ transition with a bound intermediate state, the formula is $\frac{d\left(\phi+\delta_\alpha^{(0)}\right)}{dE}\Delta E_\alpha + \Delta\delta_\alpha = 0$.

## Context

This work extends the finite-volume formalism for long-distance electroweak amplitudes, previously developed for $K_L$-$K_S$ mixing by Christ et al. (2010, 2014, 2015) and for rare kaon decays, to the case with two hadrons in both initial and final states. It reproduces the $2\xrightarrow[]{J}2$ transition formula of Briceno and Hansen (2015) as a special case. The formalism enables lattice QCD calculations of double beta decay processes $nn\to pp ee\bar{\nu}\bar{\nu}$ and $nn\to pp ee$, building on prior lattice studies in the pion sector by Feng et al. (2018) and Tuo et al. (2019), and on chiral effective field theory work by Cirigliano et al. (2017, 2018, 2019). For the neutrinoless double beta decay $nn\to pp ee$, the authors propose introducing a massive neutrino as an infrared regulator to avoid power-law finite-volume effects from massless neutrinos, allowing application of the $2\xrightarrow[]{J}2$ formula.
