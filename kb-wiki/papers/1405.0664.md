# Flow in pPb collisions at 5 TeV?

> There is little doubt that hydrodynamic flow has been observed in heavy ion collisions at the LHC and RHIC, mainly based on results on azimuthal anisotropies, but also on particle spectra of identified particles, perfectly compatible with hydrodynamic expansions. Surprisingly, in p-Pb collisions one observes a very similar behavior. So do we see flow even in p-Pb? We will try to answer this question.

## Key claim

Hydrodynamic flow appears to play an important role in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, but the paper hedges that the conclusion is based on comparing experimental data to multiple Monte Carlo generators, with the EPOS3 model (which includes full viscous hydrodynamics) providing the best description of identified particle spectra and their multiplicity dependence.

## Method

The authors employed the EPOS3 approach, which combines a Gribov-Regge multiple scattering framework for initial conditions with a 3D+1 viscous hydrodynamical evolution and a core-corona procedure that separates initial energy into a hydrodynamically evolving core and particles that escape the matter. They compared their simulations to experimental data from CMS and ALICE on transverse momentum spectra and particle ratios for pions, kaons, protons, and lambdas in p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV, and also compared to results from QGSJETII (no flow), AMPT (partonic cascade with some collectivity), and EPOS LHC (parametrized flow).

## Key numbers

- p-Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV center-of-mass energy per nucleon pair
- Multiplicity classes with mean values of 8, 84, 160, and 235 charged tracks (CMS definition, |eta| < 2.4)
- For the Lambda/Ks ratio, a peak structure appears with maximum around 2-3 GeV/c, more pronounced for higher multiplicities
- Elliptic flow v2 shows mass splitting: in the pt range of 1-1.5 GeV/c, proton v2 is clearly below pion v2

## Context

This work extends earlier hydrodynamic calculations in heavy ion collisions at RHIC and LHC (EPOS2, EPOS1.99) to the p-Pb system, and contrasts with prior approaches that treat p-A collisions as simple reference systems without flow. The paper directly compares with the Monte Carlo Glauber-based viscous hydro calculations of Bozek (2012), the Glauber and Glasma initial condition studies of Schenke (2012), and the ideal hydro with smooth Glauber initial conditions by Qin (2012). The results enable further investigation of whether hydrodynamic flow is universal across collision systems, and suggest that the core-corona mechanism is crucial for understanding the multiplicity dependence of flow in small systems.
