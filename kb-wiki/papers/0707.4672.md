# Effects of non-uniform acceptance in anisotropic flow measurement

> Applicability of anisotropic flow measurement techniques and their extension for detectors with non-uniform azimuthal acceptance are discussed. Considering anisotropic flow measurement with two and three (mixed harmonic) azimuthal correlations we introduce a set of observables based on the x and y components of the event flow vector. These observables provide independent measures of anisotropic flow, and can be used to test self-consistency of the analysis. Based on these observables we propose a technique that explicitly takes into account the effects of non-uniform detector acceptance. Within this approach the acceptance corrections, as well as parameters which define the method applicability, can be determined directly from experimental data. For practical purposes a brief summary of the method is provided at the end.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a technique for measuring anisotropic flow that explicitly corrects for non-uniform detector azimuthal acceptance, with the hedge that the method is valid when contributions from harmonics other than the one being analyzed can be neglected (i.e., when the n-th harmonic flow is dominant or acceptance effects for higher harmonics are small).

## Method

The authors introduce observables based on the x and y components of the event flow vector Q_n and the single-particle vector u_n, which provide independent measures of anisotropic flow. They derive acceptance corrections in three steps: re-centering (subtracting mean values to correct for shifts), diagonalization (rotating to correct for twists), and rescaling (dividing by acceptance coefficients a^±_{2n}) for both u_n and Q_n vectors. The corrected vectors are then used in standard two-particle and three-particle (mixed harmonic) correlation formulae.

## Key numbers

- Anisotropic flow at RHIC is v_m ≤ 10%, leading to multiplicity correlation deviations |[ρ_a ρ_b] - 1| ≤ 2% in the most pessimistic estimate.
- In practice, mean acceptance coefficients ⟨u_m⟩_{a,b} ≤ 0.2 to 0.3, reducing acceptance effects on multiplicity correlations to the level of a tenth of a percent.
- The acceptance coefficient a^±_{2n} = 1 ± ⟨cos 2nφ⟩ defines the rescaling correction for u_n-vector components.
- The resolution for two-particle correlations uses the relation ⟨Q_n^a Q_n^{b*}⟩ = (1/4) V_n^2, where V_n = ⟨M v_n⟩_{EP}.

## Context

This work extends the scalar product method of Adler (2002) and the event plane method of Poskanzer (1998) to detectors with non-uniform azimuthal acceptance, such as the PHENIX central arms and NA49 TPC. It provides an alternative to the cumulant and Lee-Yang zeros approaches of Borghini (2001) and Bhalerao (2003), with the advantage that acceptance corrections can be determined directly from experimental data without Monte Carlo simulations. The method enables precise anisotropic flow measurements from detectors with complicated acceptance patterns, allowing comprehensive comparison of theoretical predictions with experimental data from a wider range of experimental setups.
