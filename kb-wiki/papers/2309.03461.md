# Covariant density functional theory for nuclear fission based on two-center harmonic oscillator basis

> Nowdays, modern microscopic approaches for fission are generally based on the framework of nuclear density functional theory (DFT), which has enabled a self-consistent treatment of both static and dynamic aspects of fission. The key issue is a DFT solver with high precision and efficiency especially for the large elongated configurations. Purpose: To develope a DFT solver with high precision and efficiency based on the point coupling covariant density functional theory (CDFT), which has achieved great success in describing properties of nuclei for the whole nuclear chart. Method: We have extended the point-coupling CDFT to be based on the two-center harmonic oscillator (TCHO) basis, which matches well with the large elongated configurations during the fission process. Multi-dimensional constraint and time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) have been used to analyze the fission potential energy surface and fission dynamics, respectively. To simulate the splitting process of the nascent fragments beyond scission, we also introduce a density constraint into the new CDFT framework. Results: Illustrative calculations have been done for the PESs and induced fission dynamics of two typical examples: $^{226}$Th and $^{240}$Pu. A more reasonable PES is obtained in the new framework compared to that based on the once-center harmonic oscillator (OCHO) with the same basis space. An optimization of about $0.2\sim0.3$ MeV has been achieved for the outer fission barriers and large elongated configurations. The dynamical simulations based on TCHO basis presents a trend to improve the description for fission yields. Conclusions: The new developed CDFT solver optimizes the elongated configurations, improves the calculation efficiency, and provides a basis for large-scale multi-dimensional constraint calculations and dynamical simulations.

## Key claim

The paper claims that extending point-coupling covariant density functional theory (CDFT) to a two-center harmonic oscillator (TCHO) basis improves both accuracy and efficiency for describing large elongated fission configurations compared to the standard one-center harmonic oscillator (OCHO) basis, with the caveat that the improvement is demonstrated only for two illustrative nuclei (²²⁶Th and ²⁴⁰Pu) and the dynamical simulations show only a "trend" toward better fission yield descriptions rather than definitive quantitative agreement with experiment.

## Method

The authors extended the point-coupling CDFT framework by expanding the Dirac spinor in a two-center harmonic oscillator (TCHO) basis, which consists of two off-center harmonic oscillators connected at z=0. They performed multi-dimensionally constrained calculations for the quadrupole moment (β₂), octupole moment (β₃), and neck nucleon number (qN) to generate potential energy surfaces (PESs) and mass tensors, then used the time-dependent generator coordinate method (TDGCM) with Gaussian overlap approximation to simulate induced fission dynamics for ²²⁶Th and ²⁴⁰Pu. A density constraint was also introduced to handle post-scission configurations.

## Key numbers

- For ²²⁶Th along the asymmetric fission path, the inner, middle, and outer barrier heights calculated with TCHO (OCHO) are 5.92 (6.11), 6.05 (6.25), and 4.32 (4.51) MeV, respectively, showing an optimization of about 0.2-0.3 MeV for the outer barriers.
- For ²²⁶Th along the symmetric fission path, the barrier heights with TCHO (OCHO) are 5.92 (6.11), 10.99 (11.30), and 2.37 (2.52) MeV from inner to outer.
- For ²⁴⁰Pu, the first fission barrier height is 6.83 MeV (TCHO) versus 6.79 MeV (OCHO), and the outer barrier is 3.76 MeV (TCHO) versus 3.62 MeV (OCHO).
- The computing time for TCHO with Nf=20 is similar to OCHO with Nf=20 (less than 300 seconds per configuration), but to match TCHO accuracy, OCHO requires Nf=24, which takes about twice the computing time.
- For ²²⁶Th charge yields, the asymmetric fission peak value decreased from 15.07% (OCHO) to 13.76% (TCHO), and the dip at Z~40,50 increased from 1.66% to 1.96%.

## Context

This work extends the constrained CDFT framework previously implemented in an axially deformed one-center harmonic oscillator basis by Lu (2014) and Tao (2017), which had decreasing accuracy and efficiency for highly elongated fission configurations. The TCHO basis approach was inspired by earlier work in nonrelativistic frameworks, such as the two-center shell model used by Berger (1985) and Regnier (2016, 2019), and the two-center harmonic oscillator basis developed by Geng (2007). The new CDFT-TCHO solver enables more reliable multi-dimensional constraint calculations and dynamical simulations for fission, particularly for configurations beyond scission, and provides a basis for future large-scale studies incorporating more collective degrees of freedom.
