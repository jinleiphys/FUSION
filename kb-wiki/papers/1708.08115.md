# From Strangeness Enhancement to Quark-Gluon Plasma Discovery

> This is a short survey of signatures and characteristics of the quark-gluon plasma in the light of experimental results that have been obtained over the past three decades. In particular, we present an in-depth discussion of the strangeness observable, including a chronology of the experimental effort to detect QGP at CERN-SPS, BNL-RHIC, and CERN-LHC.

## Key claim

This review argues that the enhancement of strangeness, particularly multi-strange antibaryons, in relativistic heavy-ion collisions constitutes the pivotal signature of quark-gluon plasma (QGP) formation, and that this signature enabled the first clear observation of QGP at CERN-SPS in the early 1990s, though the discovery was not universally accepted at the time and required subsequent confirmation at BNL-RHIC and CERN-LHC.

## Method

The authors present a historical survey of theoretical and experimental work on strangeness as a QGP signature, covering the period from 1980 to 2017. They describe the development of the strangeness observable based on three criteria: operational on the collision time scale of 10^{-23} s, sensitive to local color deconfinement, and dependent on the gluon degree of freedom. The review traces the chronology of experiments at CERN-SPS (NA35, NA36, WA85, WA94, WA97, NA49, NA57), BNL-RHIC (STAR), and CERN-LHC (ALICE), and discusses how the statistical hadronization model was used to interpret hadron yields in terms of chemical freeze-out conditions.

## Key numbers

* Initial temperature in Au+Au collisions at RHIC (sqrt(s_NN) = 200 GeV) exceeds 300 MeV
* Initial temperature in Pb+Pb collisions at LHC (sqrt(s_NN) = 2.76 TeV) is at least 450 MeV
* Quasi-critical temperature from lattice QCD: T_c(mu_B=0) approximately 150 MeV (Borsanyi 2012)
* NA35 measured Lambda-bar/p-bar ratio approximately 1.4 near mid-rapidity in S-A collisions at 200 A GeV, showing three-to-fivefold enhancement compared to elementary reactions (Alber 1996)
* In Pb+Pb collisions at CERN-SPS (sqrt(s_NN) = 17.2 GeV, equivalent to 158 A GeV), the enhancement for Omega + anti-Omega baryons reaches a factor of 20 relative to elementary collisions (ALICE 2013)

## Context

This review extends the authors' foundational work on strangeness as a QGP signature, beginning with their 1986 review (Koch 1986) that completed the theoretical foundations, and their earlier papers establishing strangeness equilibration in QGP (Rafelski 1980, 1982). The paper contrasts the strangeness observable with other proposed QGP signatures such as fluid dynamics, jet quenching, quarkonium production, and electromagnetic signals, arguing that only strangeness uniquely probes local color deconfinement. The review enables future work by documenting the chronology of experimental discovery and the development of the statistical hadronization model (SHARE) for analyzing chemical freeze-out conditions.
