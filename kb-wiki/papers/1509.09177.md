# Compton Scattering and Nucleon Polarisabilities in Chiral EFT: Update and Future

> 6 pages LaTeX2e (pdflatex) including 11 figures as .pdf files. First presented by Griesshammer at the 12th Conference on the Intersections of Nuclear and Particle Physics CIPANP2015, 19-24 May 2015, Vail (CO), USA; updated for 22nd International Spin Symposium (SPIN 2016), University of Illinois, Urbana (USA), 26-30 September 2016. Corrected 2 figures, added clarifying text

## Key claim

Chiral Effective Field Theory (ChiEFT) can extract nucleon static dipole polarisabilities from Compton scattering data with reproducible theoretical uncertainties, but the extraction requires careful statistical consistency checks and is most reliable for photon energies well below the pion-production threshold; data around and above the Delta(1232) resonance show increased sensitivity to spin polarisabilities but all theoretical approaches resort to well-motivated but not fully controlled approximations in that region.

## Method

The authors performed ChiEFT calculations of single-nucleon Compton amplitudes from zero energy up to about 350 MeV, including all contributions at order e^2 delta^4 (accuracy delta^5 less than 2%) for omega less than the pion mass, and at order e^2 delta^0 (accuracy delta^2 less than 20%) for omega near the Delta resonance. They fitted static polarisabilities to data at omega less than 170 MeV and Delta(1232) parameters above that, iterating until convergence, and applied Bayesian statistical methods to estimate theoretical truncation uncertainties. For the deuteron, they extended the analysis to include 22 new data points from MAXlab, assessing theoretical uncertainty as plus or minus 0.8 times 10^{-4} fm^3.

## Context

This work extends the ChiEFT framework for Compton scattering developed by Griesshammer, McGovern, and Phillips (2012) and McGovern et al. (2012), and incorporates new deuteron data from Myers et al. (2014) at MAXlab. The results update the Particle Data Group values for nucleon polarisabilities and provide predictions for spin polarisabilities that were subsequently tested by MAMI data from Martel et al. (2014). The framework enables extraction of neutron polarisabilities from few-nucleon targets (deuteron, helium-3) with quantified theoretical uncertainties, and connects to lattice QCD computations at heavier pion masses from the NPLQCD collaboration (Chang et al., 2015) and others.
