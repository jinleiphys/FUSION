# Calculations of scattering lengths in four-nucleon system on the basis of cluster reduction method for Yakubovsky equations

> The cluster reduction method for the Yakubovsky equations in configuration space is used for calculations of zero-energy scattering in four-nucleon system. The main idea of the method consists in making use of expansions for the Yakubovsky amplitudes onto the basis of the Faddeev components for the two-cluster sub-Hamiltonian eigenfunctions. The expansions reduce the original equations to ones for the functions depending on the relative coordinates between the clusters. On the basis of the resulting equations the N-(NNN) zero-energy scattering problems are solved numerically with the MT I-III model for N-N forces and neglecting the Coulomb interaction between protons.

## Key claim

The cluster reduction method for Yakubovsky equations in configuration space can produce scattering lengths for the four-nucleon system that agree reasonably with direct solutions of the full Yakubovsky differential equations, but only when the MT I–III model for N–N forces is used and Coulomb interaction between protons is neglected.

## Method

The authors expand the Yakubovsky amplitudes onto a basis of Faddeev components for two-cluster sub-Hamiltonian eigenfunctions, then project the original Yakubovsky equations onto a biorthogonal basis to obtain coupled-channel equations for functions depending only on the relative coordinates between clusters. These reduced equations are solved numerically for zero-energy N–(NNN) scattering after partial-wave analysis, using the MT I–III model for nucleon-nucleon forces and the isotopic spin formalism with Coulomb interaction neglected.

## Key numbers

- n–³H scattering length (T=1, S=0): 3.70 fm (from the cluster reduction method, Tab. 2)

- n–³He scattering length (T=0, S=0): 5.10 fm (from the cluster reduction method, Tab. 3, with spurious basis functions removed)
- n–³He scattering length (T=0, S=1): 3.70 fm (from the cluster reduction method, Tab. 3, with spurious basis functions removed)

## Context

This work extends the cluster reduction method previously developed by Yakovlev and Filikhin (1995–1996) for the Yakubovsky equations in configuration space, which was originally applied to bound-state problems. The results are compared with direct solutions of the Yakubovsky differential equations from Carbonell (1995) and with other approaches for n–³H scattering lengths. The method enables practical four-nucleon scattering calculations without requiring supercomputer facilities, and the authors note that removing spurious basis functions from the expansion affects the calculated scattering lengths in the T=0 channel.
