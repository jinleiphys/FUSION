# Lepton pair production in ultra-peripheral collisions

> The lepton pair production in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) is studied in the classical field approximation. We derive a general form of the cross section in terms of photon distributions which depend on the transverse momentum and coordinate based on the wave packet form of nuclear wave functions. Such a general form of the cross section in the classical field approximation contains the results of the generalized equivalent photon approximation (EPA) as well as the corrections beyond EPA in the Born approximation. By rewriting the general form of the cross section in light-cone coordinates, we find a good connection with the transverse momentum dependent distribution (TMD) factorization formalism in the Born approximation. Our numerical results are consistent with current experimental data.

## Key claim

The authors derive a general form of the lepton pair production cross section in ultra-peripheral collisions (UPC) using the classical field approximation, which unifies the generalized equivalent photon approximation (gEPA) and the transverse momentum dependent (TMD) factorization formalism; the general form contains both the gEPA results and corrections beyond EPA in the Born approximation, and the numerical results are consistent with current experimental data.

## Method

The authors start from a wave-packet description of nuclear wave functions and derive a general cross section expression in terms of photon Wigner functions that depend on transverse momentum and coordinate. They then implement the classical field approximation for the photon fields, solving Maxwell equations in the Lorentz gauge to obtain classical photon fields from nuclear charge currents. The general cross section is rewritten in light-cone coordinates to establish a connection with the TMD factorization formalism, and numerical calculations are performed for comparison with experimental data and with results from gEPA and TMD approaches.

## Key numbers

- The original EPA calculations predict that the peak in the transverse momentum spectrum is at less than 20 MeV, which is inconsistent with experimental data (Baltz 2009, Klein 2016, Zha 2018).
- The transverse magnetic field in ideal magnetohydrodynamics with longitudinal boost invariance decays as approximately 1/τ, where τ is proper time (Pu 2016, Roy 2015).
- The total cross section of γγ → l⁻l⁺ has been calculated in EPA in the classical field approximation of QED (Vidovic 1992).

## Context

This work extends the generalized equivalent photon approximation (gEPA) developed by Vidovic (1992), Hencken (1994, 2004), and Zha (2018, 2021) and the TMD factorization formalism for linearly polarized photons in UPC studied by Li (2019) and Klein (2020, 2018). The unified description enables systematic inclusion of corrections beyond EPA and provides a baseline for future studies of medium effects in peripheral collisions, where the STAR Collaboration (Adam 2018) and ATLAS Collaboration (Aaboud 2018) have observed significantly broader transverse momentum spectra compared to UPC.
