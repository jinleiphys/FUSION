# Singularity-free two-body equation with confining interactions in momentum space

> We are developing a covariant model for all mesons that can be described as quark-antiquark bound states in the framework of the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) in Minkowski space. The kernel of the bound-state equation contains a relativistic generalization of a linear confining potential which is singular in momentum space and makes its numerical solution more difficult. The same type of singularity is present in the momentum-space Schrödinger equation, which is obtained in the nonrelativistic limit. We present an alternative, singularity-free form of the momentum-space Schrödinger equation which is much easier to solve numerically and which yields accurate and stable results. The same method will be applied to the numerical solution of the CST bound-state equations.

## Key claim

The authors present a singularity-free form of the momentum-space Schrödinger equation with a linear confining potential, which eliminates the principal-value singularity that makes numerical solution cumbersome, while acknowledging that the method is first demonstrated in the nonrelativistic limit and will subsequently be applied to the Covariant Spectator Theory (CST) bound-state equations.

## Method

The authors start from a screened linear potential in coordinate space, compute its Fourier transform, and take the unscreened limit to obtain the singular momentum-space kernel \(V_A(\mathbf{q}) = -8\pi\sigma/\mathbf{q}^4\). They then derive the partial-wave projected Schrödinger equation with this kernel and perform an additional subtraction (proportional to \(\psi'_\ell(p)\)) that eliminates the Cauchy principal-value singularity at \(k=p\) in all partial waves. The resulting singularity-free equation is solved numerically by expanding the wave function in a basis of B-splines.

## Context

This work extends the Covariant Spectator Theory approach to mesons as quark-antiquark bound states, improving on earlier work by Gross, Milana, and Savkli (1990s) by incorporating quark self-interaction self-consistently. The singularity-free method enables stable numerical solution of the momentum-space bound-state equation with confining interactions, and the authors state that the same technique will be applied to the CST bound-state equations for \(q\bar{q}\) systems, with the nonrelativistic results serving as benchmarks for heavy quarkonia such as charmonium and bottomonium.
