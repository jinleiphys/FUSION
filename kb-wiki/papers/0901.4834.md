# Consistent Anti-de Sitter-Space/Conformal-Field-Theory Dual for a Time-Dependent Finite Temperature System

> We propose a consistent setup for the holographic dual of the strongly coupled large-Nc N=4 super Yang-Mills theory plasma which undergoes the Bjorken flow relevant to the quark-gluon plasma at BNL Relativistic Heavy Ion Collider and CERN LHC. The dual geometry is constructed order by order in a well-defined late-time expansion. The transport coefficients are determined by the regularity of the geometry. We prove, for the first time, that the dual geometry has an apparent horizon hence an event horizon, which covers a singularity at the origin. Further we prove that the dual geometry is regular to all orders in the late-time expansion under an appropriate choice of the transport coefficients. This choice is also shown to be unique. Our model serves as a concrete well-defined example of a time-dependent AdS/CFT dual.

## Key claim

The paper proposes a holographic dual for the strongly coupled large-\(N_c\) \(\mathcal{N}=4\) super Yang-Mills plasma undergoing Bjorken flow, and proves for the first time that the dual geometry possesses an apparent horizon (hence an event horizon) and is regular to all orders in a late-time expansion, provided the transport coefficients are chosen uniquely. The claim is conditional on the assumption that the static relation between energy density, entropy density, and temperature holds at sufficiently large proper time due to local thermal equilibrium.

## Method

The authors construct a five-dimensional dual metric in Eddington-Finkelstein coordinates, assuming boost invariance and a diagonal 4d stress tensor. They solve the vacuum Einstein equation with a negative cosmological constant (\(\Lambda = -6\)) order by order in a \(\tau^{-2/3}\) late-time expansion, introducing a spatial variable \(u \equiv r \tau^{1/3}\). The integration constants in the solutions are related to the energy density and transport coefficients of the 4d fluid via the standard AdS/CFT dictionary. Regularity of the geometry at \(u = w\) (where \(w\) is a constant related to the energy density) is enforced by fixing these constants, and the existence of an apparent horizon is demonstrated by solving for its location in the expansion.

## Key numbers

* The shear viscosity to entropy density ratio is fixed by the regularity condition \(\zeta_1 = 1/(3w)\) to be \(\eta(\tau)/s(\tau) = 1/(4\pi)\).
* The second-order transport coefficient combination is fixed by \(\zeta_2 = (1 + 2\log 2)/(18 w^2)\), yielding \(\lambda_1(\tau) - \eta(\tau) \tau_\Pi(\tau) = -\frac{1 - \log 2}{9} N_c^2 (T_{\tau\tau}(\tau)/s)^2\).
* The apparent horizon is located at \(u_0 = w\), \(u_1 = -1/6 + \xi_1/3\), and \(u_2 = (8 - 3\pi - 4\log 2)/(72 w) + \xi_2/3\) in the expansion \(u_H(\tau) = u_0 + u_1 \tau^{-2/3} + u_2 \tau^{-4/3} + O(\tau^{-2})\).
* The area element of the apparent horizon is \(A_H = w^3 - (w^2/2) \tau^{-2/3} + (w(2 + \pi + 6\log 2)/24) \tau^{-4/3} + O(\tau^{-2})\).
* The entropy production rate matches the expansion rate of the apparent horizon area at leading order: \(A_H^{-1} \partial_\tau A_H = (\tau s)^{-1} \partial_\tau (\tau s) = \eta_0 \tau^{-5/3}\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic construction of the Bjorken flow initiated by Janik and Peschanski (2006) and further developed by Nakamura and Sin (2006), Bak and Janik (2007), and others. It directly addresses the claim by Benincasa, Buchel, Heller, and Janik (2007) of a logarithmic singularity at third order in the late-time expansion, showing that such a singularity is absent in the proposed setup. The paper provides the first proof of an event horizon in a time-dependent dual geometry and enables future studies of dynamical black hole thermodynamics in asymptotically AdS\(_5\) spacetimes, as well as connections between horizon regularity and the validity of fluid dynamics.
