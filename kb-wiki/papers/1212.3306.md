# Introductory Overview of Quark Matter 2012

> The two arguably most generic phenomena seen in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions are the flow of essentially all soft hadronic observables and the quenching of essentially all hard hadronic observables. Limiting the discussion to these two classes of phenomena, I review what can be said so far about the properties of hot and dense QCD matter from the heavy ion programs at RHIC and at the LHC, and I discuss the opportunities for further progress in the coming years.

## Key claim

The two most generic phenomena in ultra-relativistic heavy ion collisions are the flow of essentially all soft hadronic observables and the quenching of essentially all hard hadronic observables; the author limits the discussion to these two classes to review what can be said about the properties of hot and dense QCD matter from RHIC and LHC programs, while noting that a detailed understanding of how hard-probe sensitivity translates into statements about specific properties of matter is not yet achieved.

## Method

The author reviews the fluid-dynamical characterization of soft physics via Fourier coefficients v_n of azimuthal particle spectra, and the use of hard probes (jet quenching) to study medium properties. For soft physics, the review covers the transition from qualitative to quantitative fluid dynamics at RHIC, the constraint on shear viscosity over entropy density (eta/s) from elliptic flow data, and the second fluid-dynamical revolution based on event-by-event fluctuations of harmonic coefficients. For hard probes, the review contrasts results from gauge/gravity duality (strong coupling) with perturbative QCD approaches, highlighting the need for medium-modified final-state parton showers with controlled uncertainties.

## Key numbers

- The KSS bound for shear viscosity over entropy density in the strong coupling limit is eta/s -> 1/(4 pi) ~ 0.08 (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2004).
- Fluid dynamic simulations require early initialization times tau_0 < 1 fm/c for phenomenological validity.
- For light-flavored quarks in a strongly coupled plasma, the maximal penetration depth is Delta x_max(E) ~ (1/2T) (E/(T sqrt(lambda)))^(1/3) (Chesler, 2008).
- The sound attenuation length is Gamma_s = eta/(s T), and fluid dynamic excitations decay as delta v(tau,k) = delta v(tau_0,k) (tau_0/tau)^n exp[-Gamma_s k^2 (tau - tau_0)].
- In a HTL-resummed analysis, the probability distribution P(k_perp) of transverse momentum broadening acquires a powerlaw tail proportional to 1/k_perp^4 at large transverse momenta (D'Eramo, 2012).

## Context

This review extends the qualitative fluid-dynamical picture established by earlier work (Ollitrault, 1992; Appelshauser, 1997) and the quantitative fluid-dynamical revolution at RHIC (Teaney, 2001; Kolb, 2001) that constrained eta/s to be close to the KSS bound (Kovtun, Son, and Starinets, 2004). It builds on the second fluid-dynamical revolution initiated by Alver and Roland (2010) regarding event-by-event fluctuations, and parallels the development of CMB fluctuation analysis in cosmology. For hard probes, it contrasts results from gauge/gravity duality (Casalderrey-Solana, 2011) with perturbative approaches (D'Eramo, 2012) and highlights the formulation of jet quenching by Zapp (2011, 2012) as a path toward quantitatively controlled medium-modified parton showers.
