# Photon Rates for Heavy-Ion Collisions from Hidden Local Symmetry

> We study photon production from the hidden local symmetry approach that includes pions, rho and a1 mesons and compute the corresponding photon emission rates from a hadronic gas in thermal equilibrium. Together with experimental radiative decay widths of the background, these rates are used in a relativistic transport model to calculate single photon spectra in heavy-ion collisions at SPS energies. We then employ this effective theory to test three scenarios for the chiral phase transition in high-temperature nuclear matter including decreasing vector meson masses. Although all calculations respect the upper bound set by the WA80 Collaboration, we find the scenarios could be distinguished with more detailed data.

# Photon Rates for Heavy-Ion Collisions from Hidden Local Symmetry

## Key claim

The hidden local symmetry (HLS) approach, extended to include pions, rho, and a1 mesons, produces photon emission rates that respect the WA80 upper bound for all three tested scenarios of chiral symmetry restoration; however, the three scenarios (Brown-Rho scaling with equal mass reduction, Georgi vector limit with constant a1 mass, and lattice-inspired degenerate-mass scenario) could be distinguished only with more detailed experimental data than were available at the time.

## Method

The authors computed photon production cross sections and thermal emission rates from the processes ππ → ργ, πρ → πγ, and ρ → ππγ using the extended HLS Lagrangian with parameters a and g, including a momentum-dependent a1 width. These rates were then implemented in a relativistic transport model (based on RQMD initial conditions with string fragmentation) to calculate single photon spectra for central S+Au collisions at 200 AGeV, testing three scenarios for the density dependence of vector meson masses.

## Key numbers

- Free-space ρ → ππγ decay width: 1.6 MeV for a=2, 1.8 MeV for a=1.64 (experimental value 1.5 ± 0.3 MeV)
- Isospin-averaged ρ → ππγ width: 0.88 MeV (a=2), 0.98 MeV (a=1.64)
- a1 → ρπ width: 360 MeV for a=2, 430 MeV for a=1.64 (experimental value 400 MeV)
- a1 → γπ width: 320 keV for a=2, 50 keV for a=1.64 (experimental value 630 keV)
- Pion vector radius: 0.39 fm² for a=2, 0.46 fm² for a=1.64 (experimental value 0.44 ± 0.03 fm²)
- ρ mass from second KSRF relation: 800 MeV for a=2, 760 MeV for a=1.64
- a1 mass from Weinberg sum rules: 1090 MeV for a=2 (experimental value ~1230 MeV)
- Thermal emission rate for πρ → πγ increases by factor of ~6 when ρ mass reduced to 500 MeV at T=150 MeV
- Photon spectra from all scenarios lie below the WA80 upper bound for transverse momenta well below 1 GeV

## Context

This work extends the transport calculations of Li, Brown, and collaborators (1995-1997) by including a1 meson effects that were treated incompletely in prior work, and by testing three distinct scenarios for chiral symmetry restoration that had not been addressed for photon rates. The HLS approach provides a unified framework that includes the massive Yang-Mills Lagrangian of Song (1993) as a special case. The results enable future experimental discrimination between scenarios where vector meson masses drop together (Brown-Rho scaling), where the a1 mass remains constant (Georgi vector limit), or where masses approach a common nonzero value (inspired by lattice results of Gottlieb et al., 1996).
