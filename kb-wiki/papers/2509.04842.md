# Ab initio study in the island of inversion within the two-major-shell valence space

> We present an \textit{ab initio} study of nuclear structure in the island of inversion around neutron number $N=20$, using multishell effective Hamiltonians derived from the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group approach combined with the quantum-number projected generator coordinate method. By progressively expanding the valence space from the \textit{sd} shell to the intermediate $sdf_{7/2}p_{3/2}$ space and, for the first time, to the full \textit{sdfp} shell, we investigate low-lying spectra, $E2$ transition strengths, deformation properties, and neutron occupancies in even-even Ne, Mg, and Si isotopes around $N=20$. Our results show that enlarging the valence space significantly improves the description of quadrupole collectivity, yielding better agreement with experimental data for key observables such as the lowered $2^+$ energies and the enhanced $B(E2;0^+_1\rightarrow2_1^+)$ values. The analysis reveals the critical role of cross-shell multi-particle multi-hole excitations in breaking the $N=20$ shell closure and establishing intruder-dominated ground states. It also demonstrates the ability of the VS-IMSRG+PGCM framework to capture both dynamical (short range) and static (long range) correlations across multiple major-oscillator shells.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that expanding the valence space to the full two-major-shell *sdfp* space, for the first time within the VS-IMSRG+PGCM framework, significantly improves the description of quadrupole collectivity in the island of inversion around N=20, but the calculated B(E2) values remain systematically underestimated compared to experimental data when the IMSRG is truncated at the normal-ordered two-body level.

## Method

The authors derived *ab initio* multishell effective Hamiltonians using the valence-space in-medium similarity renormalization group (VS-IMSRG) approach starting from the 1.8/2.0 (EM) chiral interaction, with the IMSRG evolution carried out in a single-particle basis with e_max=12 and 3N matrix elements truncated at E_3max=16. These Hamiltonians were decoupled in three progressively larger valence spaces (sd, sdf_7/2p_3/2, and full sdfp) above an 16O core, then used as input for the quantum-number projected generator coordinate method (PGCM) with constraints on the quadrupole moment operator to compute low-lying spectra, E2 transition strengths, deformation properties, and neutron occupancies.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of 32Mg: -245.2 MeV within the sdfp space at e_max=12, compared to -224.7 MeV at e_max=6
- B(E2;0+_1→2+_1) for 32Mg: 341.7 e^2fm^4 within the full sdfp space
- Neutron occupancy in 32Mg: approximately 1.5 neutrons occupy the fp shell within the sdf_7/2p_3/2 space
- Ground-state energy of 30Ne: -206.2 MeV at e_max=12, compared to -189.7 MeV at e_max=6
- Ground-state energy of 34Si: -276.0 MeV at e_max=12, compared to -256.2 MeV at e_max=6
- The 1.8/2.0 (EM) chiral interaction reproduces ground-state energies of nuclei up to A≈100 with a root-mean-square deviation from experiment of approximately 3.5 MeV

## Context

This work extends previous VS-IMSRG calculations that were limited to the πsd+νsdf_7/2p_3/2 valence space (PhysRevC.102.034320), which failed to reproduce the lowered 2+_1 state and strong electric quadrupole transitions for 32Mg. The results are compared with the extended Kuo-Krenciglowa method (PhysRevC.95.021304) that empirically fits single-particle energies, and with the in-medium generator-coordinate method (ZHOU2025139464) that combines PGCM with multi-reference IMSRG. The VS-IMSRG+PGCM framework offers a scalable path for future *ab initio* studies of heavier open-shell nuclei, though expanding to the sdfpg_9/2 space presents new challenges in the decoupling procedure.
