# Kondo phase diagram of quark matter

> We discuss the ground state of a quark matter containing heavy quarks as impurities in a simple model which exhibits the QCD Kondo effect. The model includes a current-current interaction with the color exchange between a light quark ($ψ$) and a heavy quark ($Ψ$). We introduce a gap function $Δ\sim \langle \bar ψΨ\rangle $ which represents the correlation between $ψ$ and $Ψ$, and perform the mean-field approximation assuming that heavy quarks are uniformly distributed. Values of the gap $Δ$ measure the strength of mixing between $ψ$ and $Ψ$. The gap equation obtained from the minimum of the thermodynamical potential together with the condition for the heavy-quark number conservation turns out to allow for nonzero values of the gap as the most stable state. We draw a phase diagram in $μ$ (the light-quark chemical potential) and $λ$ (an analog of the heavy-quark chemical potential) plane, and identify the region where the QCD Kondo effect occurs.

## Key claim

The paper identifies a novel "Kondo phase" in quark matter containing uniformly distributed heavy quark impurities, characterized by a nonzero heavy-light condensate $\langle \bar{\psi}\Psi \rangle$. The existence of this phase is conditional on the heavy-quark density being sufficiently high so that the averaged inter-heavy-quark distance is smaller than the coherence length $\sim 1/|\Delta|$, and the phase diagram is drawn in the plane of light-quark chemical potential $\mu$ and an analog of heavy-quark chemical potential $\lambda$.

## Method

The authors construct a model Lagrangian with a current-current color-exchange interaction between light quarks ($\psi$) and heavy quarks ($\Psi$), simplified in the heavy-quark limit using heavy-quark effective theory. They introduce a heavy-light condensate gap $\Delta \sim \langle \bar{\psi}\Psi \rangle$, perform a mean-field approximation assuming uniformly distributed static heavy quarks, and solve the gap equation obtained from minimizing the thermodynamic potential together with a constraint for heavy-quark number conservation via a Lagrange multiplier $\lambda$.

## Key numbers

- At $\mu = 0.5$ GeV and $\lambda = 0$ GeV, the gap is $|\Delta| = 0.028$ GeV from full numerical calculation (analytic approximation gives $|\Delta| = 0.026$ GeV).
- At $\mu = 0.5$ GeV and $\lambda = 0.01$ GeV, the light-quark number density is $n_q = 3.5$ fm$^{-3}$ and the heavy-quark number density is $n_Q = 1.8$ fm$^{-3}$.
- At $\mu = 0.5$ GeV and $\lambda = -0.01$ GeV, $n_q = 3.4$ fm$^{-3}$ and $n_Q = 2.0$ fm$^{-3}$.
- At $\mu = 0.5$ GeV and $\lambda = 0$ GeV, the gap decreases with temperature: $|\Delta| = 0.024$ GeV at $T = 0.01$ GeV, and becomes zero at $T = 0.017$ GeV.
- The energy gain per heavy quark at $\mu = 0.5$ GeV, $\lambda = 0$ GeV, and $T = 0$ GeV is $\delta\Omega/n_Q = -0.016$ GeV.

## Context

This work extends the QCD Kondo effect, previously studied for single heavy quark impurities by Yasui (2013) and Hattori (2015), to the case of a finite density of uniformly distributed heavy quarks. It introduces a heavy-light condensate $\langle \bar{\psi}\Psi \rangle$ as a new order parameter, contrasting with the light quark-antiquark condensate for chiral symmetry breaking (Nambu, 1961) and the light quark-quark condensate for color superconductivity (Fukushima, 2010; Alford, 2007). The results enable future studies of competition with color superconductivity (Kanazawa, 2016) and coexistence with chiral condensates (Suzuki, 2017), and can be applied to estimate the energy gain of heavy-quark droplets in dense environments such as neutron star cores or heavy-ion collisions.
