# Coexistence of Regular Undamped Nuclear Dynamics with Intrinsic Chaoticity

> We study the conditions under which the nucleons inside a deformed nucleus can undergo chaotic motion. To do this we perform self-consistent calculations in semiclassical approximation utilizing a multipole-multipole interaction of the Bohr-Mottelson type for quadrupole and octupole deformations. For the case of harmonic and non-harmonic static potentials, we find that both multipole deformations lead to regular motion of the collective coordinate, the multipole moment of deformation. However, despite this regular collective motion, we observe chaotic single particle dynamics.

# Coexistence of Regular Undamped Nuclear Dynamics with Intrinsic Chaoticity

## Key claim
The authors show that in a self-consistent semiclassical model of a deformed nucleus with quadrupole or octupole multipole-multipole interactions, the collective multipole moment oscillates regularly and without damping, even though the underlying single-particle dynamics are chaotic. This coexistence of ordered collective motion and chaotic microscopic motion depends on the self-consistent treatment and the averaging of random single-particle fluctuations over many particles.

## Method
The authors perform self-consistent semiclassical calculations using a Wigner transformation of the von Neumann equation to obtain a Vlasov equation, which is solved numerically via the test particle method with a fourth-order Runge-Kutta algorithm. They use a Bohr-Mottelson type multipole-multipole interaction for quadrupole and octupole deformations, with both harmonic (r^2) and non-harmonic (r^6) static potentials. Collective motion is studied by tracking the time evolution of the multipole moment, while single-particle chaoticity is analyzed using Poincaré surfaces of section.

## Key numbers
- The giant quadrupole oscillation period is stretched from the 0-coupling value of 88.4 fm/c to 128 fm/c, consistent with the analytic result T = 125 fm/c for ω₀ = 0.0355 c/fm.
- The observed octupole oscillation period is T₃ ≈ 66 fm/c, in good agreement with the analytic result T₃ ≈ 66.8 fm/c for ω₀ = 0.0355 c/fm.
- The numerical simulation conserves total energy to better than 0.1%.
- The Poincaré section calculations ran for 10⁵ time steps of 1 fm/c each, yielding approximately 550 crossings of the r_x = 0 plane from below.

## Context
This work extends the classical billiard model of Blocki et al. (1992, 1993), who studied particle motion in a deformed container and found that quadrupole (ℓ=2) deformations yield non-chaotic motion while ℓ≥3 deformations produce chaotic single-particle dynamics. The present paper introduces self-consistency into the problem, which was absent in those earlier studies. The results enable further investigation of how ordered macroscopic motion can emerge from underlying chaotic microscopic dynamics, with potential applications to other physical systems such as plasmas in tokamaks.
