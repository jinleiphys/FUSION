# Fate of Multiparticle Resonances: From $Q$-Balls to $^3$He Droplets

> We consider a system of $N$ nonrelativistic particles which form a near-threshold resonance. Assuming no subset of these particles can form a bound state, the resonance can only decay through an "explosion" into $N$ particles. We show that the decay width of the resonance scales as $E^{Δ-5/2}$ in the limit when the energy $E$ of the resonance goes to zero, where $Δ$ is the ground state energy of a system of $N$ particles in a spherical harmonic trap with unit frequency. The formula remains valid when some pairs of final particles have zero-energy $s$-wave resonance, but the Efimov effect is not present. In the limit of large $N$, we show that the final particles follow a Maxwell-Boltzmann distribution if they are bosons, and a semicircle-like law if they are fermions. We argue that metastable $^3$He droplets exist with the lifetime varying over many orders of magnitudes ranging from a fraction of a nanosecond to values greatly exceeding the age of the Universe.

## Key claim
The decay width of a near-threshold N-particle resonance (where no subset of particles forms a bound state) scales universally as Γ ∼ E^{Δ−5/2} as the resonance energy E approaches zero, where Δ is the ground-state energy of N surrogate particles in a spherical harmonic trap with unit frequency, provided that Δ > 7/2 and the Efimov effect is absent.

## Method
The authors use a low-energy effective field theory in which the resonance field Ψ couples to a composite operator O of conformal dimension Δ built from the constituent particle fields ψ_a. The self-energy of Ψ is computed by integrating out the ψ fields, yielding an imaginary part proportional to E^{Δ−5/2} that determines the decay width. For large N, the decay is reinterpreted as tunneling through a centrifugal 1/R² barrier in hyperradial coordinates, and the momentum distribution of final particles is derived from the vertex structure and microcanonical ensemble arguments.

## Key numbers
- For a three-boson resonance with no resonant interaction, Δ = 9/2, giving Γ ∼ E², consistent with Matsuyama (1991).
- For two spin-up and one spin-down fermions at unitarity (ℓ=1): Δ = 4.27272, so Γ ∼ E^{1.773}.
- For two spin-up and one spin-down fermions at unitarity (ℓ=0): Δ = 4.66622, so Γ ∼ E^{2.166}.
- For a four-neutron resonance in the regime E_a ≪ E ≪ E_{r₀}: Δ ≈ 5.0, giving Γ ∼ E^{2.5}.
- For a metastable ³He droplet with N = 28 atoms (N₀ − 1), Δ = 96, leading to Γ ∼ (E/E₀)^{93.5} with E₀ ∼ 40 K; for E ∼ 1 K the suppression factor is 10^{−150}.
- For weakly coupled bosonic droplets, the metastability window lies between N₁ ≈ 189.4 G^{1/2}/g² and N₀ ≈ 240.4 G^{1/2}/g².

## Context
This work extends the study of Borromean and Brunnian states (Zhukov, 1993) and universal properties of few-body resonances (Hammer, 2006; Stecher, 2008) to the many-body regime. It generalizes the known two-body Wigner threshold law Γ ∼ E^{ℓ+1/2} and the three-boson result Γ ∼ E² (Matsuyama, 1991) to arbitrary N. The results enable predictions for the lifetimes of metastable ³He nanodroplets (previously unestimated) and for near-threshold resonances in ultracold atomic gases and nuclear systems such as the trineutron and tetraneutron.
