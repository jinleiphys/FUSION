# Examination and experimental constraints of the stellar reaction rate factor $N_A < σv >$ of the $^{18}$Ne($α$,$p$)$^{21}$Na reaction at temperatures of X-Ray Bursts

> The $^{18}$Ne($α$,$p$)$^{21}$Na reaction is one key for the break-out from the hot CNO-cycles to the $rp$-process. Recent papers have provided reaction rate factors $N_A < σv >$ which are discrepant by at least one order of magnitude. The compatibility of the latest experimental results is tested, and a partial explanation for the discrepant $N_A<σv>$ is given. A new rate factor is derived from the combined analysis of all available data. The new rate factor is located slightly below the higher rate factor by Matic {\it et al.}\ at low temperatures and significantly below at higher temperatures whereas it is about a factor of five higher than the lower rate factor recently published by Salter {\it et al.}

## Key claim

The stellar reaction rate factor \(N_A\langle\sigma v\rangle\) for the \(^{18}\text{Ne}(\alpha,p)^{21}\text{Na}\) reaction, which is key for breakout from the hot CNO cycles to the rp-process in X-ray bursts, has been discrepant by at least one order of magnitude between recent publications; this paper provides a partial explanation for the discrepancy and derives a new rate factor from a combined analysis of all available data, finding it lies slightly below the higher rate of Matic et al. at low temperatures and significantly below at higher temperatures, while being about a factor of five higher than the lower rate of Salter et al.

## Method

The authors compared experimental results from four studies: direct \(^{18}\text{Ne}(\alpha,p)^{21}\text{Na}\) measurements by Groombridge et al. (GRO), transfer reaction data from Matic et al. (MAT) using the \(^{24}\text{Mg}(p,t)^{22}\text{Mg}\) reaction with high energy resolution, transfer data from Chae et al. (CHA) with improved spin assignments, and inverse \(^{21}\text{Na}(p,\alpha)^{18}\text{Ne}\) measurements by Salter et al. (SAL) and unpublished Argonne National Laboratory (ANL) data. They tested compatibility of resonance energies, widths, and strengths, and recalculated resonance strengths for states where spin assignments were revised, using the narrow-resonance formula to derive a reference reaction rate factor.

## Key numbers

* The \(^{18}\text{Ne}\) half-life is 1.67 s.
* The \(\alpha\)-particle separation energy in \(^{22}\text{Mg}\) is \(S_\alpha = 8.142\) MeV (from the Audi and Meng compilation).
* The MAT experiment achieved an energy resolution of about 13 keV for excitation energies.
* The deviation between numerical integration of the cross section and the narrow-resonance formula remains below 10% over \(T_9 = 0.25 - 3\) and far below 5% for \(T_9 = 1 - 2\).
* The summed \(p_0\) resonance strength to the ground state is about 42% of the total summed strength in the GRO data.
* For a hypothetical \(0^+\) state in \(^{22}\text{Mg}\) at \(E^\ast = 9.942\) MeV (\(E = 1.8\) MeV), proton decay proceeds by 31% to the ground state of \(^{21}\text{Na}\) and by 69% to excited states.
* The recommended rate factor is approximately \(0.55 \times N_A\langle\sigma v\rangle_{\text{ref.}}\), where the reference rate is derived from MAT data with all revisions.

## Context

This work resolves a discrepancy between the higher rate factor of Matic et al. (2009) and the lower rate factor of Salter et al. (2012) for the \(^{18}\text{Ne}(\alpha,p)^{21}\text{Na}\) reaction, which is critical for breakout from the hot CNO cycles to the rp-process in X-ray bursts at temperatures around \(T_9 = 1 - 2\). The analysis extends earlier direct measurements by Groombridge et al. (2002) and transfer studies by Chae et al. (2009), and enables more reliable nucleosynthesis modeling in explosive astrophysical environments by providing a rate factor constrained by all available experimental data.
