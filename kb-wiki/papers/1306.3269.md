# Nuclear Polarization Corrections to mu-d Atoms in Zero-Range Approximation

> Nuclear polarization corrections to the 2P-2S Lamb shift in mu-d atoms are developed in order alpha^5 and are shown to agree with a recent calculation. The nuclear physics in the resulting corrections is then evaluated in zero-range approximation. The dominant part of the correction is very simple in form and differs from a recent potential model calculation by less than 1%. It is also demonstrated how the third-Zemach-moment contribution largely cancels against part of the polarization correction, as it did in e-d atoms and does so exactly for point-like nucleons. This suggests that it may be possible to reduce the uncertainty in the theory (of which nuclear polarization is the largest contributor) to less than 1%.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that nuclear polarization corrections to the muon-deuteron 2P-2S Lamb shift can be accurately calculated using the zero-range approximation, with the dominant term differing from a full potential model calculation by less than 1%, suggesting that theoretical uncertainties (of which nuclear polarization is the largest contributor) may be reducible to less than 1%.

## Method

The author derives nuclear polarization corrections to order alpha^5 using non-relativistic second-order perturbation theory expressed as energy-weighted photonuclear sum rules, then evaluates the nuclear physics in the zero-range approximation developed by Bethe and Peierls (1935). This approximation assumes the asymptotic s-wave deuteron wave function holds everywhere, allowing all matrix elements to be expressed analytically in terms of the deuteron binding energy, asymptotic normalization constant, and nucleon masses. The results are compared to the numerical potential model calculation of Ref. [KP] using the Argonne V18 potential.

## Key numbers

- The leading C1 (electric dipole) polarization contribution to the 2P-2S Lamb shift in muon-deuteron atoms is 1.925 meV in zero-range approximation, compared to 1.910 meV from the potential model calculation of Ref. [KP], a difference of 0.015 meV (0.8%)
- The sub-leading C1 term contributes -0.037 meV (zero-range) versus -0.035 meV (Ref. [KP]), a 7.0% difference
- The C0 (monopole) term contributes -0.042 meV (zero-range) versus -0.045 meV (Ref. [KP])
- The retarded C1 term contributes 0.137 meV (zero-range) versus 0.151 meV (Ref. [KP])
- The C2 (quadrupole) term contributes -0.061 meV (zero-range) versus -0.066 meV (Ref. [KP])
- The M1 (magnetic dipole) term contributes -0.011 meV (zero-range) versus -0.016 meV (Ref. [KP])
- The deuteron binding energy is E_B = 2.224575(9) MeV, with corresponding momentum parameter kappa = 45.7022 MeV (0.23161 fm^{-1})
- The prefactor for 2S state polarization corrections is 0.088636 meV fm^{-2}
- The asymptotic s-wave normalization constant A_S = 0.8845(8) fm^{-1/2} with 0.2% uncertainty

## Context

This work extends the approach of Friar (1977) for electron-deuteron atoms to muon-deuteron atoms, providing an independent framework for treating nuclear polarization that complements the numerical potential model calculation of Ref. [KP] using the Argonne V18 potential. The zero-range approximation, originally developed by Bethe and Peierls (1935), exploits the weak binding of the deuteron to produce simple analytic formulae based only on one- and two-nucleon observables. The paper demonstrates that the third Zemach moment contribution largely cancels against part of the polarization correction, as previously shown for electron-deuteron atoms by Friar (1979), and shows this cancellation is exact for point-like nucleons. This work enables uncertainty estimates for the ongoing PSI muon-deuteron Lamb shift experiment and provides a framework for treating nuclear polarization in muon-helium atoms.
