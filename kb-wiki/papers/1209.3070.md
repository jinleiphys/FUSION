# Effective theory with dibaryons and perturbative pions

> 5 pages, contribution to The 20th International IUPAP Conference on Few-Body Problems in Physics (FB20), Fukuoka International Congress Center, Japan, August 20-25, 2012

## Key claim

The paper argues that the unitary limit (where S-wave scattering lengths become infinite) is a more convenient theoretical starting point than the chiral limit for studying pion cloud corrections in few-nucleon systems, particularly when crossing the critical pion mass in lattice QCD simulations. The claim is hedged by noting that results depend significantly on detailed specifications of the calculation, such as which physical quantities and renormalization scheme are used.

## Method

The author constructs an effective field theory with dibaryon fields and perturbative pions, calculating S-wave NN scattering amplitudes for the 1S0 and 3S1 channels. Pions are treated perturbatively with only leading one-pion-exchange diagrams around the leading order amplitude from dibaryon fields, using dimensional regularization with the PDS scheme. Renormalization is performed by fixing two effective range parameters (scattering length a and effective range r) for each channel, and the scale parameter μ is treated as a fine-tuning parameter for high-energy contributions.

## Key numbers

- Critical pion mass at the unitary limit: mπcrit. = 198 MeV, where S-wave scattering lengths become infinite and deuteron binding energy goes to zero
- Lattice QCD pion mass: mπlatt. = 354 MeV, with scattering lengths a0 = 0.63 ± 0.50 fm (1S0) and a1 = 0.63 ± 0.74 fm (3S1)
- Physical scattering lengths: a0 = -23.7 fm (1S0) and a1 = 5.42 fm (3S1) at physical pion mass mπphys. = 140 MeV
- Fine-tuned μ values: μ = 178 and 330 MeV for the 1S0 channel; μ = 167 and 246 MeV for the 3S1 channel
- Phase shifts agree with accurate potential model calculations up to p ∼ 50 MeV with μ-independence requirement, and up to p ∼ mπ with fine-tuned μ values

## Context

This work extends the approach of Cohen and Hansen (1999) by including effective range corrections from the dibaryon fields, obtaining new contributions to higher-order effective range parameters v2, v3, and v4. The paper addresses the difficulty of nonperturbative renormalization in few-body EFTs, as discussed by previous authors (e.g., Phillips, Beane, and Cohen, 1998; Yang and Huang, 2005), and provides a framework for connecting lattice QCD results at large pion mass (from the NPLQCD collaboration, 2006) to physical pion mass by crossing the unitary limit. The approach enables systematic study of QCD dynamics in few-nucleon systems through deviations from the universal behavior at the unitary limit.
