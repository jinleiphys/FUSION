# DIS on a Large Nucleus in AdS/CFT

> We calculate the total cross section for deep inelastic scattering (DIS) on a nucleus at high energy for a strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills theory using AdS/CFT correspondence. In analogy to the small coupling case we argue that at high energy the total DIS cross section is related to the expectation value of the Wilson loop formed by the quark-antiquark dipole. We model the nucleus by a metric of a shock wave in AdS_5. We then calculate the expectation value of the Wilson loop by finding the extrema of the Nambu-Goto action for an open string attached to the quark and antiquark lines of the loop in the background of an AdS_5 shock wave. We find three extrema of the Nambu-Goto action: the string coordinates at the extrema are complex-valued and are given by three different branches of the solution of a cubic equation. The physically meaningful solutions for the total DIS cross section are given either by the only branch with a purely imaginary string coordinate in the bulk or by a superposition of the two other branches. For both solutions we obtain the forward scattering amplitude N for the quark dipole-nucleus scattering. We study the onset of unitarity with increasing center-of-mass energy and transverse size of the dipole: we observe that for both solutions the saturation scale, while energy-dependent at lower energies, at very high energy becomes independent of energy/Bjorken-x. The saturation scale depends very strongly on the atomic number of the nucleus as Q_s ~ A^{1/3}.

## Key claim

The authors calculate the total DIS cross section on a nucleus at high energy for strongly coupled N=4 super Yang-Mills theory using AdS/CFT, finding that the saturation scale becomes independent of energy at very high energies (contrary to perturbative behavior), but the abstract's claim of a saturation scale scaling as Q_s ~ A^{1/3} applies only under the specific static approximation valid for very large nuclei.

## Method

The authors model the nucleus as a shock wave metric in AdS_5 and relate the total DIS cross section to the expectation value of a Wilson loop formed by the quark-antiquark dipole. They calculate this expectation value by extremizing the Nambu-Goto action for an open string attached to the quark and antiquark lines in the shock wave background, finding three complex-valued extremal solutions from a cubic equation. The physically meaningful forward scattering amplitude N for quark dipole-nucleus scattering is obtained either from the branch with purely imaginary string coordinate or from a superposition of the other two branches, with the static approximation justified for sufficiently large atomic number A.

## Context

This work extends the strongly-coupled AdS/CFT approach to DIS initiated by Janik and Peschanski (1999), who showed that at large 't Hooft coupling the single-pomeron exchange cross section grows as sigma_tot ~ s, and by Hatta (2007) and Hatta (2007) who studied DIS on static N=4 SYM via R-current correlators. The authors incorporate saturation effects that unitarize the cross section, addressing the puzzle that perturbative QCD cross sections grow faster with energy at lower Q^2 while phenomenology shows the opposite trend. This enables future studies of how saturation modifies the energy dependence of cross sections at strong coupling, with the result that the saturation scale becomes energy-independent at very high energies, reminiscent of the "saturation of saturation" conjectured by Kharzeev (2007).
