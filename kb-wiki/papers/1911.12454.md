# Toward Initial Conditions of Conserved Charges Part II: The ICCING Monte Carlo Algorithm

> At top collider energies where baryon stopping is negligible, the initial state of heavy ion collisions is overall charge neutral and predominantly composed of gluons. Nevertheless, there can also be significant local fluctuations of the baryon number, strangeness, and electric charge densities about zero, perturbatively corresponding to the production of quark/antiquark pairs. These previously ignored local charge fluctuations can permit the study of charge diffusion in the quark-gluon plasma (QGP), even at top collider energies. In this paper we present a new model denoted ICCING (Initial Conserved Charges in Nuclear Geometry) which can reconstruct the initial conditions of conserved charges in the QGP by sampling a ($g \rightarrow q\bar{q}$) splitting probability over the initial energy density. We find that the new charge distributions generally differ from the bulk energy density; in particular, the strangeness distribution is significantly more eccentric than standard bulk observables and appears to be associated with the geometry of hot spots in the initial state. The new information provided by these conserved charges opens the door to studying a wealth of new charge- and flavor-dependent correlations in the initial state and ultimately the charge transport parameters of the QGP.

## Key claim

The ICCING algorithm can reconstruct initial conditions of conserved charges (baryon number, strangeness, electric charge) in the quark-gluon plasma at top collider energies by sampling a \(g \rightarrow q\bar{q}\) splitting probability over the initial energy density, even though the total charge in the plasma is zero; the charge distributions generally differ from the bulk energy density, with the strangeness distribution being significantly more eccentric than standard bulk observables.

## Method

The algorithm takes an externally-provided initial 2D energy density profile (here from the Trento model for Pb Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV) and treats it as composed entirely of gluons. It performs Monte Carlo sampling of the total splitting probabilities for gluons to split into up, down, strange, or charm quark-antiquark pairs, using theoretical distributions from the color-glass condensate (CGC) effective theory in either the Golec-Biernat-Wusthoff (GBW) or McLerran-Venugopalan (MV) model. For each splitting, the algorithm samples the quark-antiquark separation distance \(r_\bot\) and light-front momentum fraction \(\alpha\), then redistributes the gluon energy to the quark and antiquark positions while depositing the corresponding conserved charge densities.

## Key numbers

- The entropy density is related to the reduced thickness function by \(s(\vec{x}_\bot) = a \, T_R(\vec{x}_\bot)\) with \(a = 119 \, \mathrm{fm}^{-1}\) for Pb Pb collisions at \(5.02 \, \mathrm{TeV}\).
- The default initialization time is \(\tau_0 = 0.6 \, \mathrm{fm}\) from the Trento Bayesian analysis.
- The default gluon energy blob radius is \(r = 0.5 \, \mathrm{fm}\).
- The minimum light-front momentum fraction cutoff is \(\alpha_\mathrm{min} = 0.01\).
- The maximum quark-antiquark separation cutoff is \(d_\mathrm{max} = 1 \, \mathrm{fm}\).
- The default saturation scale proportionality constant is \(\kappa = 1 \, \mathrm{GeV} \, \mathrm{fm}\), roughly a factor of 5 larger than the perturbative QCD estimates of \(0.140 \, \mathrm{GeV \, fm}\) (nucleon as quark) or \(0.210 \, \mathrm{GeV \, fm}\) (nucleon as gluon) for \(\alpha_s \approx 0.3\).

## Context

This work extends the standard paradigm of heavy-ion collisions where only the energy density is initialized and conserved currents are set homogeneously to zero, as done in models such as Trento (Moreland, 2014) and Bayesian analyses (Bernhard, 2016). It provides the initial condition ingredient needed to study charge diffusion and dissipation in the QGP at top collider energies where baryon chemical potential is zero, complementing recent progress in charge-diffusive hydrodynamics (Denicol, 2018; Du, 2019). The algorithm enables future extraction of charge transport parameters at top energies where theory and systematics are best controlled, serving as a necessary baseline for the Beam Energy Scan program at RHIC.
