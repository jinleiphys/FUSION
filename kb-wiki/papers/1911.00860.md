# Neutron Shell Closure at N=32 and N=40 in Ar and Ca Isotopes

> In this paper, we investigate features of the ground state of some nuclei far from the stability for isotope chains with proton numbers Z=18 and 20. Our aim is to predict the eventual existence of magic numbers in these exotic nuclei. For this purpose, we use two methods: the non relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) approach based on SLy4 Skyrme functional and the relativistic (so-called covariant) density functional theory (CDFT) by using the DD-ME2 force parametrization. We compare our results with the available experimental data and with the predictions of other models such as Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM). Our present investigation predicts that N=32 and N=40 are magic numbers for Ar and Ca isotopes.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that N=32 and N=40 are magic numbers for both argon (Z=18) and calcium (Z=20) isotopes, based on calculations using two mean-field approaches, but the authors note that the microscopic theories fail to reproduce the experimental observation that N=20 is not a magic number in argon isotopes, suggesting a limitation of these models.

## Method

The authors performed non-relativistic Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) calculations using the SLy4 Skyrme functional with the HFBTHO code, and relativistic density functional theory (CDFT) calculations using the DD-ME2 force parametrization with the DIRHBZ code. They computed one-neutron and two-neutron separation energies, two-neutron shell gaps, neutron pairing gaps, and neutron pairing energies for even-even calcium and argon isotope chains, comparing their results with the Finite Range Droplet Model (FRDM) and available experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Binding energy and neutron rms radii for $^{60}Ca$ and $^{50}Ar$ converge at $N_{sh}=14$ oscillator shells (680 basis states) in HFB+SLy4 calculations
- Binding energy and neutron rms radii for $^{60}Ca$ and $^{50}Ar$ converge at $N_F=12$ fermion shells and $N_B=20$ boson shells in CDFT+DD-ME2 calculations
- The pairing strength for protons and neutrons was set to $V_0^{p,n}=270$ for Ar isotopes and $V_0^{p,n}=5N-30$ for Ca isotopes, where N is the neutron number
- The energy cut-off parameter for the pairing space was set to 60 MeV
- The oscillator frequency was fixed at $1.2 \hbar \omega$ with $\hbar \omega_0= 41/A^{1/3}$ MeV

## Context

This work extends previous studies on the emergence of new magic numbers in exotic nuclei, specifically following observations of N=16 in Z=8 by Ozawa and N=32 in Z=20 by Tendeur and Huck, as well as the quenching of N=20 in magnesium and neon by Kanungo and Grawe, and N=28 in sulfur and silicon by Jurado. The predictions enable future experimental investigations of neutron-rich argon and calcium isotopes near the drip line, though the authors note that the continuum coupling may affect results for drip-line Ar isotopes near N=40, and that the microscopic models fail to reproduce the experimental finding that N=20 is not magic in argon isotopes.
