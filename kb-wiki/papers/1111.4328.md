# Coarse graining Nuclear Interactions

> We consider a coarse graining of NN interactions in coordinate space very much in the spirit of the well known Vlowk approach. To this end we sample the interaction at about the minimal de Broglie wavelength probed by NN scattering below pion production threshold. This amounts to provide a simple delta-shell potential plus the standard OPE potential above 2 fm. The possible simplifications in the Nuclear many body problem are discussed.

## Key claim
The authors propose that the nucleon-nucleon (NN) interaction can be coarse-grained in coordinate space by sampling it at intervals set by the minimal de Broglie wavelength of NN scattering below pion production threshold (about 0.5 fm), yielding a simple delta-shell potential plus the standard one-pion-exchange (OPE) tail above 2 fm. This representation is presented as an alternative to the momentum-space \(V_{\text{low k}}\) approach, with the hedge that while the diagonal matrix elements resemble those of \(V_{\text{low k}}\), the off-diagonal elements do not necessarily match.

## Method
The authors construct a neutron-proton (np) potential as a sum of delta functions at discrete radii, with strengths treated as fitting parameters to reproduce phase shifts from the Nijmegen partial wave analysis (PWA). For S-waves, they fit one or two delta-shells to the scattering length and effective range, and for higher partial waves (J ≤ 4) they include an OPE tail above 2 fm and fit about 40 parameters to the full np database. The resulting potential is then used in harmonic oscillator shell model calculations for closed-shell nuclei (⁴He, ¹⁶O, ⁴⁰Ca) with exact kinetic energy and a Slater determinant wave function.

## Key numbers
- Minimal de Broglie wavelength at pion production threshold: \(\lambda = \hbar / \sqrt{M_N m_\pi} \sim 0.5\) fm.
- For the ¹S₀ channel, scattering length \(\alpha_{0,^1S_0} = -23.74\) fm and effective range \(r_{0,^1S_0} = 2.77\) fm yield a single delta-shell strength \(\lambda_{^1S_0} = -0.4626\) at radius \(r_{c,^1S_0} = 1.99\) fm.
- For the ³S₁ channel, scattering length \(\alpha_{0,^3S_1} = 5.42\) fm and effective range \(r_{^3S_1} = 1.75\) fm yield \(\lambda_{^3S_1} = -0.911\) at \(r_{c,^3S_1} = 1.53\) fm.
- A two-delta fit to S-waves gives parameters: for ¹S₀, \((\lambda_i, r_i) = (-0.568, 1.56\ \text{fm})\) and \((-0.023, 3.47\ \text{fm})\); for ³S₁, \((-0.951, 1.35\ \text{fm})\) and \((-0.052, 2.60\ \text{fm})\).
- Preliminary fits to all partial waves with J ≤ 4 require about 40 parameters, yielding \(\chi^2/\text{d.o.f.} \lesssim 2\).
- Deuteron binding momentum from the delta-shell model: \(\gamma = 0.230348\ \text{fm}^{-1}\) (empirical: 0.231605 fm⁻¹); asymptotic D/S ratio \(\eta = 0.02488\) (empirical: 0.0256(5)); quadrupole moment \(Q_D = 0.2693\ \text{fm}^2\) (empirical: 0.2859(3) fm²); D-state probability \(P_D = 5.498\%\) (empirical: 5.67(4)%).
- For ⁴He, a single delta-shell fit to S-waves (reproducing phase shifts up to \(E_{\text{LAB}} = 50\) MeV) gives binding energy \(B(^4\text{He}) = 20\) MeV; adding more deltas and fitting up to \(E_{\text{LAB}} \le 100\) MeV gives \(B(^4\text{He}) = 24\) MeV.
- Harmonic oscillator shell model calculations of nuclear binding energies yield results at 20–30% accuracy.

## Context
This work extends the \(V_{\text{low k}}\) approach of Bogner (2003) to coordinate space, using delta-shell sampling inspired by the Nyquist theorem as discussed by Entem (2007) and earlier work by Aviles (1973). The coarse-grained potential reproduces phase shifts from the Nijmegen PWA of Stoks (1993) and yields deuteron properties comparable to high-quality potentials like AV18 (Wiringa, 1994) and Nijmegen potentials (Stoks, 1994). The approach enables simplified many-body calculations using product wave functions (Slater determinants) without strong short-range correlations, similar to the UCOM method of Neff (2002), and provides a direct path to fitting NN interactions from scattering data rather than transforming existing potentials.
