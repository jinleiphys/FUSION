# Field Theory of the d+t -> n+alpha Reaction Dominated by a 5He* Unstable Particle

> An effective, non-relativistic field theory for low-energy d+t -> n+alpha reaction is presented. The theory assumes that the reaction is dominated by an intermediate 5He* unstable spin 3/2+ resonance. It involves two parameters in the coupling of the d+t and n+alpha particles to the unstable resonant state, and the resonance energy level -- only three real parameters in all. All Coulomb corrections to this process are computed. The resultant field theory is exactly solvable and provides an excellent description of the d+t fusion process.

## Key claim

The d+t → n+α reaction can be described by an effective non-relativistic field theory using only three real parameters, assuming the process is dominated by a single intermediate ⁵He* unstable spin 3/2⁺ resonance, but the theory requires a "wrong-sign" free-particle Lagrangian for the unstable field to achieve a positive effective range parameter, limiting its validity to low energies.

## Method

The authors constructed an effective field theory with fields for deuteron, triton, neutron, alpha, and the unstable ⁵He* resonance, using a Rarita-Schwinger-type constraint for the spin 3/2 field. They computed the full Green's function for the unstable particle including self-energy contributions from both dt and nα loops, incorporated all Coulomb corrections through the squared Coulomb wave function at the origin and a Coulomb-modified loop function expressed via the digamma function, and fitted the resulting cross section formula to experimental data.

## Key numbers

- Q-value of the reaction: Q ≃ 17.59 MeV (energy release)
- Fitted resonance energy: E* = -154 ± 8 keV
- Fitted dt coupling: g²_dt/(4π) = 199 ± 8 fm³ MeV²
- Fitted nα coupling: g²_nα/(4π) = 16.4 ± 1.0 fm⁷ MeV²
- Best fit χ² per degree of freedom: 0.784 for the effective field theory with 3 parameters
- Comparison: multilevel R-matrix analysis used 117 free parameters with χ² per degree of freedom of 1.56
- Coulomb scale parameter: b₀ = 24.04 fm
- Energy range of interest: 0 < E < 300 keV, with resonance at E ≃ 50 keV

## Context

This work extends the effective field theory approach pioneered by Fermi (1936) for low-energy neutron scattering and by Schwinger (1973) for deuteron description, following the modern nuclear field theory methods advocated by Weinberg (1990). The authors show that their three-parameter field theory is precisely the zero-channel-radii limit of one-level R-matrix theory described in a companion paper (Brown, Hale, and collaborators). The result enables field-theoretic calculations of plasma screening effects in fusion reactions, which was the initial motivation for the work.
