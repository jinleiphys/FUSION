# On the Infinite Variance Problem in Fermion Models

> Monte Carlo calculations of fermionic systems with continuous auxiliary fields frequently suffer from a diverging variance. If a system has the infinite variance problem, one cannot estimate observables reliably even with an infinite number of samples. In this paper, we explore a method to deal with this problem based on sampling according to the distribution of a system with an extra time-slice. The necessary reweighting factor is computed both perturbatively and through a secondary Monte Carlo. We show that the Monte Carlo reweigthing coupled to the use of a non-biased estimator of the reweigthing factor leads to a method that eliminates the infinite variance problem at a very small extra cost. We compute the double occupancy in the Hubbard model at half-filling to demonstrate the method and compare the results to well established results obtained by other methods.

## Key claim

The paper demonstrates that the infinite variance problem in fermion Monte Carlo simulations with continuous auxiliary fields can be eliminated by sampling from a distribution with an extra time-slice and using an unbiased estimator for the reweighting factor, but only when the reweighting factor is computed non-perturbatively via a secondary Monte Carlo rather than through a perturbative expansion in the Trotter step size.

## Method

The authors adapt the extra time-slice method of Shi et al. (2016) to Euclidean time path integrals over compact auxiliary fields. They multiply and divide the partition function by a function F(phi) defined as an integral over an extra time-slice field, then sample configurations from the (N+1)-time-slice distribution and reweight by R(phi) = det M_N(phi) / F(phi). The reweighting factor F(phi) is computed either through a perturbative expansion of exp(-epsilon H) to order epsilon or through a secondary Monte Carlo estimate using an unbiased estimator for 1/F based on the method of Moka et al. (2019), which uses a geometrically distributed random number of samples.

## Key numbers

- Double occupancy computed on a 4x4 lattice with U/kappa = 8, mu = 0, and T/kappa = 0.5
- Trotter step sizes epsilon = 0.5 and epsilon = 2.0 used for comparing biased and unbiased estimators
- The unbiased estimator requires approximately 2k samples per evaluation on average, where k is the number of pilot samples used to tune parameters w and p
- The perturbative expansion of exp(-epsilon H) truncated at order epsilon introduces systematic errors that shift the epsilon^2 coefficient substantially compared to the sub-Monte Carlo method
- The improved action is accurate to order epsilon^2, while the conventional action is accurate only to order epsilon

## Context

This work extends the method of Shi et al. (2016), which proposed the extra time-slice reweighting for auxiliary-field quantum Monte Carlo with noncompact fields at zero temperature, adapting it to standard Monte Carlo simulations of Euclidean time path integrals over compact auxiliary fields. The infinite variance problem also appears in lattice QCD for discretizations with exceptional gauge configurations (Luscher, 1996) and in dynamical simulations (Del Debbio, 2005), where similar reweighting solutions have been explored (Luscher, 2008; Bruno, 2014). The method enables reliable estimation of fermionic observables in the Hubbard model and related systems where continuous auxiliary fields are desirable for complex Langevin or hybrid Monte Carlo applications.
