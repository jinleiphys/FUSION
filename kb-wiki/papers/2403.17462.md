# Rapid neutron star equation of state inference with Normalising Flows

> The first direct detection of gravitational waves from binary neutron stars on the 17th of August, 2017, (GW170817) heralded the arrival of a new messenger for probing neutron star astrophysics and provided the first constraints on neutron star equation of state from gravitational wave observations. Significant computational effort was expended to obtain these first results and therefore, as observations of binary neutron star coalescence become more routine in the coming observing runs, there is a need to improve the analysis speed and flexibility. Here, we present a rapid approach for inferring the neutron star equation of state based on Normalising Flows. As a demonstration, using the same input data, our approach, ASTREOS, produces results consistent with those presented by the LIGO-Virgo collaboration but requires < 1 sec to generate neutron star equation of state confidence intervals. Furthermore, ASTREOS allows for non-parametric equation of state inference. This rapid analysis will not only facilitate neutron star equation of state studies but can potentially enhance future alerts for electromagnetic follow-up observations of binary neutron star mergers.

## Key claim

The authors demonstrate that a Normalising Flow neural network (ASTREOS) can produce neutron star equation of state posterior distributions consistent with the LIGO-Virgo collaboration's spectral parametrisation analysis of GW170817, but requiring less than 1 second to generate confidence intervals; however, the results show some discrepancy at higher energy densities that the authors attribute to differences between the training prior distribution of equations of state and that assumed in the LIGO-Virgo analysis.

## Method

The authors train a conditional Real NVP Normalising Flow on 10^5 phenomenological 3-piece polytropic neutron star equations of state, where each equation of state is represented by 7 principal component analysis coefficients (capturing 99.975% of variation) plus three auxiliary parameters (central densities of each star and maximum allowed density). The flow learns the mapping from gravitational wave observables (component masses m1, m2 and tidal deformabilities Lambda1, Lambda2) to equation of state parameters, and inference is performed by drawing latent space samples for each posterior sample from a gravitational wave parameter estimation analysis and applying the inverse transform.

## Key numbers

- Training set contains 10^5 phenomenological neutron star equations of state
- Equation of state data represented on 105 grid points spanning pressure range from 2.024 x 10^32 dyn/cm^2 to 2.517 x 10^36 dyn/cm^2
- 7 principal component analysis components account for 99.975% of variation in the prior training set
- Data space reduced to 10 dimensions (7 PCA components plus 3 auxiliary parameters)
- Training requires approximately 3 hours on a NVIDIA Tesla V100 with approximately 2 GB GPU memory
- Network generates approximately 25000 samples per second from the equation of state posterior
- Generation and conversion of 2500 posterior samples to equation of state curves takes approximately 0.1 seconds
- Combined p-value of 0.7301 from the probability-probability plot test on 100 validation instances
- Training uses 5000 epochs with batch size 4096 and initial learning rate 0.005
- Component mass prior lower bound of 0.5 solar masses for all equations of state
- Piece-wise polytrope transition densities at 5 x 10^14 g/cm^3 and 10^15 g/cm^3

## Context

This work extends Bayesian approaches to neutron star equation of state inference from gravitational wave data, which were first demonstrated on GW170817 by the LIGO-Virgo collaboration using spectral parametrisation and equation-of-state-insensitive methods. The authors build on prior machine learning applications to gravitational wave analysis including parameter estimation work by Gabbard (2022), Dax (2021, 2023), and others, while addressing the computational expense of traditional Markov chain Monte Carlo sampling methods as used by Lackey (2015) and the LIGO-Virgo collaboration. The approach enables rapid equation of state inference that can be combined with low-latency parameter estimation pipelines to inform electromagnetic follow-up observations, and the Normalising Flow architecture is naturally suited for combining results from multiple binary neutron star detections expected in future observing runs.
