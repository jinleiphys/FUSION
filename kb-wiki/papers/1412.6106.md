# Universality of plasmon excitations in Dirac semimetals

> The recent experimental discovery of ${\rm Cd_3 As_2}$ and ${\rm Na_3 Bi}$ Dirac semimetals enables the study of the properties of chiral quasi-particles in three spatial dimensions. As demonstrated by photoemission, Dirac semimetals are characterized by a linear dispersion relation for fermion quasi-particles, and thus represent three dimensional analogs of graphene. While the distinctive behavior of chiral fermions (e.g. Klein tunneling) is already evident in two dimensional graphene, the physics of chirality in three dimensions opens a number of new possibilities. In this paper we investigate the properties of the collective plasmon excitations in Dirac semimetals by using the methods of relativistic field theory. We find a strong and narrow plasmon excitation whose frequency is in the terahertz (THz) range which may be important for practical applications. The properties of the plasmon appear universal for all Dirac semimetals, due to the large degeneracy of the quasi-particles and the small Fermi velocity, $v_F \ll c$. This universality is closely analogous to the phenomenon of "dimensional transmutation", that is responsible for the emergence of dimensionful scales in relativistic field theories such as Quantum Chromodynamics, the modern theory of nuclear physics.

## Key claim

The plasmon frequency in three-dimensional Dirac semimetals is universal, depending only on the ratio of temperature to a single dimensionful scale \(\Lambda_L\) (and on chemical potential), and not on the coupling constant, the fermion degeneracy \(N\), or the Fermi velocity \(v\), provided the effective coupling is strong (\(\lambda(\Lambda_c)/6\pi^2 \approx 2\)).

## Method

The authors used relativistic field theory methods, specifically a large-\(N\) expansion (with \(N=4\) for the fermion degeneracy) to compute the longitudinal retarded photon self-energy at one-loop order in the static limit (\(\mathbf{p} \to 0\)). The plasmon frequency is obtained from the pole of the longitudinal photon propagator in Coulomb gauge, combining a vacuum contribution (which encodes the running coupling via dimensional transmutation) and a thermal contribution from finite temperature and chemical potential. The resulting equation depends only on the single scale \(\Lambda_L \approx 1.65\,\Lambda_c\), where \(\Lambda_c \approx 5\ \mathrm{eV}\) is the ultraviolet cutoff from the quasiparticle spectrum.

## Key numbers

* The effective coupling constant for Dirac semimetals is \(\alpha \simeq 2.2\), comparable to the strong coupling constant in the Quark-Gluon Plasma.
* The Landau pole scale is \(\Lambda_L \approx 1.65\,\Lambda_c\), with \(\Lambda_c \simeq 5\ \mathrm{eV} \simeq 5.8 \times 10^4\ \mathrm{K}\) for \(\mathrm{Cd_3As_2}\).
* At room temperature \(T \simeq 300\ \mathrm{K}\), the plasmon frequency is \(\omega_{pl} \simeq T \simeq 6\ \mathrm{THz} \simeq 0.5\ \mathrm{mm}^{-1}\).
* At \(T = 888\ \mathrm{K}\) (the phase change temperature of \(\mathrm{Cd_3As_2}\)), the plasmon frequency is \(\omega_{pl} \simeq 18\ \mathrm{THz}\).
* At \(T = 3\ \mathrm{K}\), the plasmon frequency is \(\omega_{pl} \simeq 60\ \mathrm{GHz} \simeq 1\ \mathrm{cm}^{-1}\).
* The damping ratio is \(\gamma/\omega_{pl} < 0.05\) for \(\log(\Lambda_L/T) \gtrsim 5\), so the plasmon peak is very narrow.
* The real part of the plasmon frequency ceases to exist for \(x > x_c \approx 0.13\) (where \(x = T/\Lambda_L\)), with a maximum value around 3.5 in units of \(T\).

## Context

This work extends the study of plasmons from two-dimensional graphene (where a plasmon appears only after doping or including interactions, as in Grigorenko (2012)) to three-dimensional Dirac semimetals like \(\mathrm{Cd_3As_2}\) and \(\mathrm{Na_3Bi}\), which were recently discovered experimentally by Borisenko (2014), Neupane (2014), and Liu (2014). The authors use the Hard Thermal Loop (HTL) approach, previously applied to the Quark-Gluon Plasma by Pisarski (1989) and Braaten (1990), to compute the plasmon spectrum at strong coupling. The universality they find is analogous to dimensional transmutation in Quantum Chromodynamics, and it enables the prediction that Dirac semimetals can serve as tunable sources or sensors of electromagnetic radiation from radio waves (\(\sim 100\ \mathrm{GHz}\)) to near infrared (\(50\ \mathrm{THz}\)), depending on temperature and chemical potential.
