# The Transparency of 12C for Protons

> Existing cross-section data for 1p-shell knockout in the reaction 12C(e,e'p) - as obtained under different kinematic conditions - are shown to be mutually consistent, apart from a recent measurement performed in Mainz. New data have been collected at the Amsterdam Pulse Stretcher that confirm the normalization of the older measurements. An analysis of the world's 12C(e,e'p) data has yielded precise values of the spectroscopic factor for 1p-shell and 1s-shell knockout from 12C. These values have been used to evaluate the transparency of the 12C nucleus for 1p-shell and 1s-shell protons separately on the basis of recent high-energy 12C(e,e'p) data taken at a four-momentum transfer squared of 1.1 GeV/c^2. As the resulting average value of the nuclear transparency, 0.81(0.04), is considerably higher than the value obtained from previous analyses and theoretical estimates, the high Q^2 data were used instead for an independent determination of the spectroscopic strength for 1p + 1s knockout. Combining these results with the low Q^2 data the spectroscopic factors appear to be momentum-transfer dependent. Possible explanations of these surprising results in terms of reaction-mechanism effects or a possible breakdown of the quasi-particle concept at high Q^2 are discussed as well.

## Key claim

Existing low-energy 12C(e,e'p) data are mutually consistent and yield precise spectroscopic factors, but when these are used to extract the nuclear transparency from high-energy (Q² = 1.1 (GeV/c)²) data, the resulting average transparency of 0.81 ± 0.04 is considerably higher than previous analyses and theoretical estimates, suggesting either reaction-mechanism effects or a possible breakdown of the quasi-particle concept at high momentum transfer.

## Method

The authors re-analyzed all existing 12C(e,e'p) data for 1p-shell and 1s-shell knockout at Q² < 0.4 (GeV/c)² using a consistent Complete Distorted-Wave Impulse Approximation (CDWIA) framework with Woods-Saxon bound-state wave functions and optical-potential parameters from elastic proton scattering. New measurements were performed at the Amsterdam Pulse Stretcher (AmPS) to resolve a normalization discrepancy with Mainz data. The spectroscopic factors obtained from the low-Q² data were then used to extract the nuclear transparency from SLAC NE18 data at Q² = 1.1 (GeV/c)² by fitting a plane-wave impulse approximation (PWIA) calculation to the reduced cross sections.

## Key numbers

- Spectroscopic factor for 1p-shell knockout from 12C: S₁ₚ = 2.23 ± 0.07 (summed over ground state and first two excited states), which is 44% below the independent-particle shell-model prediction of 4.
- Spectroscopic factor for 1s-shell knockout: n₁ₛ = 1.32 ± 0.08 (asymptotic occupation), with centroid energy E₁ₛ = 39 ± 1 MeV and spreading width Γ(E₁ₛ) = 12 ± 3 MeV.
- Nuclear transparency for 1p-shell protons at Q² = 1.1 (GeV/c)²: T₁ₚ = 0.86 ± 0.05.
- Nuclear transparency for 1s-shell protons at Q² = 1.1 (GeV/c)²: T₁ₛ = 0.71 ± 0.06.
- Average nuclear transparency of 12C for protons at Q² = 1.1 (GeV/c)²: T₁₂C = 0.81 ± 0.04, compared to the previously published value of 0.65 ± 0.05 from Makins et al. (1994).

## Context

This work resolves a normalization discrepancy between Mainz data (Blomqvist et al., 1995) and all other existing 12C(e,e'p) measurements, confirming the older data sets from Tokyo (Nakamura et al., 1976), Saclay (Mougey et al., 1976; Bernheim et al., 1982), and NIKHEF (Steenhoven et al., 1988). The precise spectroscopic factors enable an experimentally-based extraction of nuclear transparency from the SLAC NE18 data (Makins et al., 1994), which were previously analyzed using theoretical estimates. The resulting transparency values are significantly higher than standard Glauber calculations, raising questions about the applicability of low-Q² spectroscopic factors at high momentum transfer and motivating further investigation of possible Q²-dependent effects or a breakdown of the quasi-particle concept.
