# Simulation of the traveling wave burning on epithermal neutrons on the year time scale

> We present the newly obtained results of two computer simulations of the epithermal neutron-nuclear burning in natural uranium. Each of them modeled the period of six months of the traveling wave reactor (TWR) operation -- for two different flux densities of an external neutron source. The simulation results confirm the existence of a nuclear burning wave at longer time scales, and reveal the dependence of the wave burning modes on the parameters of an external neutron source.

## Key claim
The paper reports that two computer simulations of epithermal neutron-nuclear burning in natural uranium, each covering 150 days of reactor operation, confirm the existence of a traveling wave burning mode at longer time scales than previously modeled, but the wave burning speed and wave half-width depend on the flux density of the external neutron source.

## Method
The authors solved a kinetic system of 20 equations for neutron and isotope densities using the Mathematica 8 software package, with the same initial and boundary conditions as in their prior work (Rusov 2015). Two simulations were run for 150 days each, one with an external neutron flux density of 10^23 cm^-2 s^-1 and a time step of 10 minutes, and another with a flux density of 10^15 cm^-2 s^-1 and a time step of 5 minutes, both in a 1000 cm long fissile medium of natural uranium.

## Key numbers
- For the simulation with external neutron flux density 10^23 cm^-2 s^-1, the steady-state wave burning speed was estimated as approximately 0.39 x 10^-5 cm/s, based on the 239Pu concentration maximum shifting by 20 cm over 60 days.
- For the simulation with external neutron flux density 10^15 cm^-2 s^-1, the steady-state wave burning speed was approximately 0.77 x 10^-5 cm/s, based on the 239Pu concentration maximum shifting by 60 cm over 90 days.
- The wave half-width (evaluated using 239Pu) increased from approximately 10 cm for the high-flux case to approximately 100 cm for the low-flux case.
- From the low-flux simulation, the equilibrium 239Pu concentration was approximately 0.19 x 4.8 x 10^22 cm^-3 and the critical 239Pu concentration was approximately 0.1 x 4.8 x 10^22 cm^-3.
- The estimated phase velocity from the Wigner distribution formalism was approximately 0.94 x 10^-5 cm/s, in good agreement with the simulated value of 0.77 x 10^-5 cm/s.
- The estimated specific heat power for the low-flux case was approximately 1.24 MW/cm^3, and for a cylindrical active zone with 50 cm diameter, the resulting total heat power was approximately 10.8 GW.

## Context
This work extends the numerical modeling of wave nuclear burning in natural uranium on epithermal neutrons previously reported by Rusov (2015), which only simulated 48 days of reactor operation and could not estimate the steady-state wave speed. The results are interpreted within the theoretical framework of a soliton-like neutron wave of slow nuclear burning based on quantum chaos theory, developed by Feoktistov (1989), Ershov (2003), and Rusov (2011, 2015), which uses a Wigner quantum-statistical distribution for phase velocities. The findings enable further study of how external neutron source parameters (flux density and energy spectrum) affect the burning regime in a traveling wave reactor, and provide estimates for the heat power production of an epithermal wave nuclear reactor with a natural uranium core.
