# The effect of deformation and vibration on the alpha decay half-life

> In this work, we expand upon our previous study, the effect of surface vibrations (low-lying vibrational states) on the calculation of penetration probability in alpha decay of spherical isotopes [Phys.Rev.C91,034611(2015)]. To this aim, the Coulomb and proximity potential model, taking in to account the ground state deformations of the involved nuclei along with the surface vibrations in the daughter nucleus, is used to evaluate the alpha decay probability. The results are compared with those obtained by spherical potential barrier, which shows the dramatic effect of employing the ground state deformations in case of deformed nuclei. As well, including of surface vibrations give rise to an increase in the value of tunneling probability in better agreement with experimental data.

## Key claim

The paper claims that including both ground-state deformations (β₂ and β₄) and coupling to low-lying 2⁺ vibrational states of the daughter nucleus in the Coulomb and proximity potential model significantly improves agreement with experimental alpha decay half-lives compared to spherical-barrier calculations, but the improvement is conditional on the specific deformed or vibrational character of each isotope.

## Method

The authors used the Coulomb and proximity potential model for deformed nuclei (CPPMDN), incorporating ground-state quadrupole (β₂) and hexadecapole (β₄) deformations of parent and daughter nuclei, to compute the alpha decay penetration probability via the WKB approximation. They then applied the coupled-channel formalism using the CCFULL code, including one-phonon excitations of the 2⁺ states of daughter nuclei, to account for surface vibrations. The half-life was calculated from the penetration probability and assault frequency, with results compared to spherical-barrier calculations and to experimental data.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous study (Ghodsi and Gholami, 2015) on surface vibrations in spherical alpha emitters by now incorporating ground-state deformations of both parent and daughter nuclei. The results are compared with calculations from Denisov and Khudenko (using the UMADAC model), Xu and Ren (using the density-dependent cluster model), and Bao et al. (using the generalized liquid drop model), showing that the CPPMDN plus coupled-channel approach yields the best overall agreement with experimental half-lives. The study enables more accurate predictions of alpha decay half-lives for deformed and transitional nuclei, and opens the way to including couplings to additional excited states beyond the 2⁺ phonon.
