# Neutron-Proton Interaction in Doubly Odd Deformed Nuclei

> In this paper, we present some results of a particle-rotor model study of 176Lu. In particular, we consider the two lowest K=1+ balds, which present a rather large odd-even staggering. This effect, which may be traced to direct Coriolis coupling with Newby-shifted K=0+ bands, is of great interest since it gives information on the proton-neutron interaction. We use both zero-range and finite-range interactions with particular attention focused on the role of the tensor force. Comparison of the calculated results with experimental data evidences the importance of the tensor-force effects in the description of the odd-even staggering in K not-equal-to zero bands.

## Key claim

The odd-even staggering observed in the two lowest Kπ=1+ bands of 176Lu is a manifestation of tensor-force effects in the neutron-proton interaction, mediated by Coriolis coupling with Newby-shifted Kπ=0+ bands; only calculations including the tensor terms reproduce the correct experimental staggering, while pure central forces fail.

## Method

A particle-rotor model was used to perform Coriolis band-mixing calculations for 176Lu, with the unpaired neutron and proton coupled to an axially symmetric core. The effective neutron-proton interaction was implemented both as a finite-range Gaussian force (with and without tensor terms) and as a zero-range delta force, using parameters from Boisson et al. (1976). Single-particle energies and the rotational parameter were derived from the experimental spectra of neighboring odd-mass nuclei 175Yb and 175Lu, and the Nilsson potential with deformation β2=0.26 was employed.

## Key numbers

- The two lowest Kπ=1+ bands in 176Lu have bandheads at 197 keV (p 9/2[514] n 7/2[514]) and 339 keV (p 9/2[624] n 7/2[404]).
- Wave functions of the first Kπ=1+ band contain 5–10% components with Kπ=0+ p 7/2[523] n 7/2[514] in all three calculations.
- Wave functions of the second Kπ=1+ band contain 5–15% components with Kπ=0+ p 7/2[404] n 7/2[633] in all three calculations.
- For the Kπ=0+ p 7/2[404] n 7/2[633] band (observed in 174Lu with a Newby shift of -44 keV), the calculated Newby shifts are -26 keV (Gaussian with tensor), 1 keV (pure central Gaussian), and 6 keV (delta force).
- The main discrepancy for the second Kπ=1+ band is a downshift of the calculated value by about 2 keV relative to experiment.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous particle-rotor model study of 176Lu (Covello, 1997), which focused on Gallagher-Moszkowski splitting and Newby shifts in K=0 bands, by now addressing odd-even staggering in K≠0 bands. The results contradict the adequacy of pure central forces (zero-range or finite-range) for describing this staggering, and instead support the tensor-force parameterization of Boisson et al. (1976). The findings enable future studies of other K≠0 bands exhibiting large odd-even staggering, as the authors state work in this direction is in progress.
