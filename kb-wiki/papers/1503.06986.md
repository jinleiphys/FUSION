# Hadronic "flow" in p--Pb collisions at the Large Hadron Collider?

> Using the Ultra-relativistic Quantum Molecular Dynamics (UrQMD) model, we investigate azimuthal correlations in p--Pb collisions at $\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=5.02$ TeV. Comparison with the experimental data shows that UrQMD can not reproduce the multiplicity dependence of 2- and 4-particle cumulants, especially the transition from positive to negative values of $c_{2}\{4\}$ in high multiplicity events, which has been taken as experimental evidence of collectivity in p--Pb collisions. Meanwhile, UrQMD can not qualitatively describe the differential elliptic flow, $v_{2}(p_{\rm T})$, of all charged hadrons at various multiplicity classes. These discrepancies show that the simulated hadronic p--Pb systems can not generate enough collective flow as observed in experiment, the associated hadron emissions are largely influenced by non-flow effects. However, the characteristic $v_{2}(p_{\rm T})$ mass-ordering of pions, kaons and protons is observed in UrQMD, which is the consequence of hadronic interactions and not necessarily associated with strong fluid-like expansions.

## Key claim

Using the UrQMD hadron cascade model, the authors show that pure hadronic interactions alone cannot reproduce the experimentally observed transition from positive to negative values of the 4-particle cumulant \(c_{2}\{4\}\) in high-multiplicity p--Pb collisions, nor the differential elliptic flow \(v_{2}(p_{\rm T})\) of charged hadrons; however, the characteristic mass-ordering of \(v_{2}(p_{\rm T})\) among pions, kaons, and protons is still generated by hadronic interactions and is not necessarily a signature of strong fluid-like collective expansion.

## Method

The authors used the UrQMD version 3.4 hadron cascade model to simulate p--Pb collisions at \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=5.02\) TeV, assuming no quark-gluon plasma formation. They calculated 2- and 4-particle azimuthal cumulants (\(c_{2}\{2\}\), \(c_{2}\{4\}\), \(c_{3}\{2\}\)) and differential elliptic flow \(v_{2}(p_{\rm T})\) for all charged hadrons and for identified pions, kaons, and protons, using the Q-Cumulant method with various pseudorapidity gap cuts. Results were compared to ALICE experimental data for several multiplicity classes defined by charged-particle multiplicity in \(|\eta|<1.0\) or \(2.8<\eta<5.1\).

## Key numbers

- At \(\sqrt{s_{_{\rm NN}}}=5.02\) TeV, UrQMD gives \(dN_{ch}/d\eta\) at midrapidity about 5% lower than the ALICE measurement.
- For the most central collisions (0-5% multiplicity class, \(N_{ch}>72\) in \(|\eta|<1.0\)), ALICE observes a transition of \(c_{2}\{4\}\) from positive to negative values; UrQMD keeps \(c_{2}\{4\}\) positive for all multiplicity classes, preventing extraction of a real \(v_{2}\{4\}\).
- For \(|\Delta\eta|>1.0\), \(c_{3}\{2\}\) from UrQMD turns negative, failing to produce a real triangular flow \(v_{3}\{2\}\), while ALICE measures positive \(c_{3}\{2\}\) for all pseudorapidity gaps.
- UrQMD cannot reproduce the shape of the experimental \(v_{2}(p_{\rm T})\) curve for all charged hadrons with \(|\Delta\eta|>1.0\): it underpredicts data at lower \(p_{\rm T}\) and overpredicts above 1 GeV.

## Context

This work extends the debate on whether observed azimuthal correlations in small p--Pb systems at the LHC arise from collective flow or from non-flow effects. It directly tests the hypothesis that pure hadronic interactions (without QGP formation) could explain the data, contradicting hydrodynamic interpretations by Bozek (2013), Bzdak (2013), Qin (2013), and Werner (2013) who reproduced the flow signatures with (3+1)-d hydrodynamics. The finding that UrQMD generates a \(v_2\) mass-ordering among pions, kaons, and protons via hadronic rescattering (meson-baryon cross sections about 50% larger than meson-meson cross sections) shows that mass-ordering alone is not a unique signature of collective flow. This enables future work to focus on observables that can distinguish between hadronic rescattering and true hydrodynamic expansion in small systems.
