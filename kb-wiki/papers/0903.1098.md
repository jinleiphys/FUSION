# Partonic calculation of $γZ$ Exchange Corrections to Parity-Violating Elastic Electron-Proton Scattering

> We calculate $γZ$ exchange corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry of the elastic electron-proton scattering in a parton model using the formalism of generalized parton distributions(GPDs). We also examine the validity of the zero-momentum-transfer approximation adopted in the literatures and find that it overestimates the $γZ$ exchange effect significantly at the forward scattering angles.

## Key claim

The paper calculates the $\gamma Z$ exchange correction to the parity-violating asymmetry in elastic electron-proton scattering using a parton model with generalized parton distributions (GPDs), and finds that the widely used zero-momentum-transfer approximation of Marciano and Sirlin (1983) significantly overestimates the $\gamma Z$ exchange effect at forward scattering angles, by up to 45% even at the lowest $Q^2$ considered.

## Method

The authors compute the parity-violating $\gamma Z$ exchange amplitude at the quark level using the handbag approximation, where the electron scatters from a single quark within the proton. The quark-level amplitudes are then convoluted with the nucleon's generalized parton distributions (GPDs) for $u$ and $d$ quarks, using an unfactorized GPD model with a forward parton distribution and a Gaussian factor in $x$ and $Q^2$, following the approach of Chen et al. (2004). The resulting $\gamma Z$ exchange correction $\delta_{\gamma Z}$ to the parity-violating asymmetry is evaluated at four $Q^2$ values (1, 2, 5, and 9 GeV$^2$) and compared with the zero-momentum-transfer approximation of Marciano and Sirlin (1983).

## Key numbers

- $\delta_{\gamma Z}$ triples when $Q^2$ decreases from 5 GeV$^2$ to 1 GeV$^2$ (e.g., from approximately 1% to 3% at forward angles).
- $\delta_{2\gamma}$ (the two-photon-exchange correction) is between 1% and 2% at all $Q^2$ values studied (1, 2, 5, and 9 GeV$^2$).
- At $Q^2 = 0.03$ GeV$^2$ and $\epsilon = 0.99$, the ratio $\Delta_{MS} = A_{PV}^{Parton}(\gamma Z) / A_{PV}^{MS}(\gamma Z) \approx 0.55$, meaning the Marciano-Sirlin approximation overestimates the $\gamma Z$ exchange effect by 45%.
- The $\gamma Z$ exchange correction is evaluated for $Q^2$ values of 1, 2, 5, and 9 GeV$^2$ and for $\epsilon$ values of 0.95, 0.97, and 0.99 in the low-$Q^2$ range (0.03 to 1 GeV$^2$).

## Context

This work extends the parton-model GPD approach used by Chen et al. (2004) for two-photon-exchange (TPE) corrections and by Afanasev et al. (2005) for TPE corrections to the parity-violating asymmetry, now applying it to the $\gamma Z$ exchange diagrams. The results contradict the zero-momentum-transfer approximation of Marciano and Sirlin (1983), which is widely used in experimental analyses of parity-violating electron scattering (SAMPLE, HAPPEX, A4, G0) to extract the strange form factors of the proton. The paper corroborates the claim by Zhou et al. (2007) that the zero-momentum-transfer approximation overestimates the $\gamma Z$ exchange effect, and notes that combining the partonic and hadronic model results remains an open issue.
