# Breaking of factorization of two-particle correlations in hydrodynamics

> The system formed in ultrarelativistic heavy-ion collisions behaves as a nearly-perfect fluid. This collective behavior is probed experimentally by two-particle azimuthal correlations, which are typically averaged over the properties of one particle in each pair. In this Letter, we argue that much additional information is contained in the detailed structure of the correlation. In particular, the correlation matrix exhibits an approximate factorization in transverse momentum, which is taken as a strong evidence for the hydrodynamic picture, while deviations from the factorized form are taken as a signal of intrinsic, "nonflow" correlations. We show that hydrodynamics in fact predicts factorization breaking as a natural consequence of initial state fluctuations and averaging over events. We derive the general inequality relations that hold if flow dominates, and which are saturated if the matrix factorizes. For transverse momenta up to 5 GeV, these inequalities are satisfied in data, but not saturated. We find at least as large factorization breaking in event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic calculations as in data, and argue that this phenomenon opens a new window on the study of initial fluctuations.

# Breaking of factorization of two-particle correlations in hydrodynamics

## Key claim
Hydrodynamic flow alone, without any nonflow correlations, predicts breaking of factorization of two-particle azimuthal correlations as a natural consequence of event-by-event initial state fluctuations and averaging over events; the observed deviations from factorization in data do not necessarily indicate nonflow contributions but can be explained by ideal hydrodynamics.

## Method
The authors derive general Cauchy-Schwarz inequality relations that must hold if flow dominates two-particle correlations, with saturation of the inequality corresponding to perfect factorization. They test these inequalities against ALICE data for Pb-Pb collisions at 2.76 TeV and compare with event-by-event ideal hydrodynamic calculations using the NeXSpheRIO model with fluctuating initial conditions from the NeXuS event generator for Au-Au collisions at 200 GeV.

## Key numbers
- For n=3, diagonal elements V_{3Δ}(p_T^a,p_T^a) become negative above 5 GeV for 0-10% centrality and above 4 GeV for 40-50% centrality in ALICE data
- For n=2, r_2 equals 1 within errors when both particles are below 1.5 GeV, but deviates from unity when one particle exceeds 1.5 GeV
- 30,000 NeXuS events were generated and sorted into 10% centrality bins for the hydrodynamic calculations
- The observed factorization breaking in ideal hydrodynamics is at least as large as in data for transverse momenta up to 5 GeV

## Context
This work extends the understanding of two-particle correlations beyond the traditional view that approximate factorization supports the hydrodynamic picture while deviations signal nonflow correlations, as previously stated by ATLAS (2012) and ALICE (2011). The authors show that factorization breaking arises from the same initial state fluctuations studied by Miller (2003), Alver (2006), and Alver (2010). This opens a new window for studying initial fluctuations and potentially constraining viscosity, since shear viscosity is expected to damp fluctuations and restore factorization.
