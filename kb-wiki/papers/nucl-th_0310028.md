# Solving Three-Cluster OCM Equations in the Faddeev Formalism

> Two different types of orthogonality condition models (OCM) are equivalently formulated in the Faddeev formalism. One is the OCM which uses pairwise orthogonality conditions for the relative motion of clusters, and the other is the one which uses the orthogonalizing pseudo-potential method. By constructing a redundancy-free T-matrix, one can exactly eliminate the redundant components of the total wave function for the harmonic-oscillator Pauli-forbidden states, without introducing any limiting procedure. As an example, a three-alpha-particle model interacting via the deep alpha alpha potential by Buck, Friedrich and Wheatley is investigated.

## Key claim

The paper proves that two formulations of the three-cluster orthogonality condition model (OCM), Horiuchi's pairwise orthogonality conditions and Kukulin's orthogonalizing pseudo-potential method, are equivalent in the Faddeev formalism, and that the redundancy-free T-matrix constructed from the OCM potential eliminates Pauli-forbidden components without requiring the limiting procedure of taking the pseudo-potential strength parameter to infinity.

## Method

The authors start from the two-cluster resonating-group method (RGM) equation and separate the interaction kernel into a part containing the energy parameter ε and a part containing the exchange normalization kernel. They construct a formal T-matrix solution that contains a singular term, then subtract this divergent term to obtain a modified, non-singular "RGM T-matrix" suitable for use in the three-body Faddeev equation. This procedure is then applied to the ordinary OCM with energy-independent local potentials, yielding an "OCM T-matrix" that no longer depends on ε. The equivalence between the two OCM formulations is demonstrated both for the case where the Pauli-forbidden state is a bound state of the local potential and for the general case of harmonic-oscillator Pauli-forbidden states.

## Key numbers

- The three-alpha ground state energy calculated with the Buck-Friedrich-Wheatley (BFW) potential is E₃α = -17 MeV (without Coulomb force), which is far below the experimental value.
- The ground-state energy from the work of Oryu and Kamada using microscopic two-alpha cluster kernels is E₃α ∼ -20 MeV.
- The bound-state energy εB of the two-alpha system (the Pauli-forbidden state) is negative (εB < 0), with the specific value determined by the BFW potential.
- The parameter λ in Kukulin's pseudo-potential is defined as λ = ε - εB, where ε is an arbitrary positive energy parameter.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous Faddeev formalism for three-cluster systems using two-cluster RGM kernels (Fujiwara et al., 2003) to the standard OCM with local potentials. It directly addresses the insufficient treatment of the Pauli principle in earlier three-alpha Faddeev studies by Fujiwara (1967) and by Oryu and Kamada (1986, 1989), where spurious states or large overbinding occurred. The formalism enables exact elimination of redundant components in three-cluster calculations without numerical instability from taking the pseudo-potential strength to infinity, and it establishes the formal equivalence between the OCM approaches of Horiuchi (1974, 1975) and Kukulin (1978).
