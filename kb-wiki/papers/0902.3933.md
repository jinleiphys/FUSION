# Pairing in the Framework of the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM): Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov Calculations

> In this first in a series of articles, we apply effective interactions derived by the Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) to the description of open-shell nuclei, using a self-consistent Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework to account for pairing correlations. To disentangle the particle-hole and particle-particle channels and assess the pairing properties of $\VUCOM$, we consider hybrid calculations using the phenomenological Gogny D1S interaction to derive the particle-hole mean field. In the main part of this article, we perform calculations of the tin isotopic chain using $\VUCOM$ in both the particle-hole and particle-particle channels. We study the interplay of both channels, and discuss the impact of non-central and non-local terms in realistic interactions as well as the frequently used restriction of pairing interactions to the ${}^1S_0$ partial wave. The treatment of the center-of-mass motion and its effect on theoretical pairing gaps is assessed independently of the used interactions.

## Key claim

The Unitary Correlation Operator Method (UCOM) effective interactions can be applied to open-shell nuclei within a Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov framework, but the resulting pairing gaps depend sensitively on whether the particle-hole and particle-particle channels are treated consistently with the same interaction, and on the inclusion of non-central and non-local terms beyond the usual \(^1S_0\) partial wave restriction.

## Method

The authors solve the Hartree-Fock-Bogoliubov (HFB) equations in a spherical harmonic oscillator basis with truncation in the oscillator quantum number \(e = 2n + l\), using the UCOM effective interaction derived from the Argonne V18 potential via unitary transformations with central and tensor correlation operators. To isolate the pairing properties, hybrid calculations are performed where the Gogny D1S interaction generates the particle-hole mean field while UCOM provides the pairing interaction, followed by fully self-consistent HFB calculations using UCOM in both channels for the tin isotopic chain.

## Key numbers

- Ground-state energy of \(^{120}\)Sn calculated with SRG-optimized UCOM (\(\bar\alpha = 0.4\)) yields less than half the experimental binding energy, while Gogny D1S provides the bulk of the binding energy already at mean-field level.
- Basis truncation at \(e_{\text{max}} = 12\) (13 major oscillator shells) gives residual energy dependence of \(1-2\) MeV for Gogny D1S and even smaller for UCOM when increasing to \(e_{\text{max}} = 14\).
- The SRG flow parameter \(\bar\alpha = 0.04\ \text{fm}^4\) produces ground-state energies of \(^3\)H and \(^4\)He close to experimental values without explicit three-nucleon forces.
- The energy gain from increasing basis size from \(e_{\text{max}} = 12\) to \(14\) is merely \(1-2\) MeV for Gogny D1S.

## Context

This work extends previous UCOM applications to closed-shell nuclei using Hartree-Fock and many-body perturbation theory (Roth 2005, Guenther 2009) by incorporating pairing correlations for open-shell systems via HFB. It contrasts with phenomenological density functional theory approaches like Skyrme and Gogny functionals (Bender 2003) which treat particle-hole and particle-particle channels separately, and addresses the inconsistency highlighted by Anguiano (2001) and Dobaczewski (2007) regarding cancellations between channels in beyond-mean-field methods. The results enable future calculations of odd nuclei and systematic studies of pairing along isotopic chains using a Hamiltonian-based framework.
