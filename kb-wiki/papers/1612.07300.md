# Internal conversion from excited electronic states of $^{229}{\mathrm Th}$ ions

> The process of internal conversion from excited electronic states is investigated theoretically for the case of the vacuum-ultraviolet nuclear transition of $^{229}{\mathrm Th}$. Due to the very low transition energy, the $^{229}{\mathrm Th}$ nucleus offers the unique possibility to open the otherwise forbidden internal conversion nuclear decay channel for thorium ions via optical laser excitation of the electronic shell. We show that this feature can be exploited to investigate the isomeric state properties via observation of internal conversion from excited electronic configurations of ${\mathrm Th}^+$ and ${\mathrm Th}^{2+}$ ions. A possible experimental realization of the proposed scenario at the nuclear laser spectroscopy facility IGISOL in Jyväskylä, Finland is discussed.

# Internal conversion from excited electronic states of $^{229}$Th ions

## Key claim
The authors propose that internal conversion (IC) from optically excited electronic states of Th$^+$ and Th$^{2+}$ ions can be used to detect the $^{229}$Th isomeric nuclear state, but only if the isomeric energy lies in specific ranges: approximately 9.0 to 12.0 eV for Th$^+$ and 19.1 to 20.0 eV for Th$^{2+}$, the latter being considered highly unlikely based on current knowledge.

## Method
The authors calculated IC rates for selected excited electronic configurations of Th$^+$ and Th$^{2+}$ using relativistic Dirac-Hartree-Fock wave functions from the grasp2K package for bound electrons and the Ratip package for continuum electron wave functions, with experimental electronic energy levels from laser spectroscopy databases. The IC rates include both M1 and E2 multipole contributions, with nuclear transition strengths taken from theoretical estimates (B$\downarrow$(M1) = 0.048 Weisskopf units and B$\downarrow$(E2) = 29 Weisskopf units). The method was validated by comparing calculated IC coefficients with tabulated values from Pauli (1970) and Trzhaskowskaya (1980) for test cases.

## Key numbers
- Isomeric state energy currently accepted as $E_{\mathrm{m}} = 7.8 \pm 0.5$ eV (Beck, 2007)
- Ratio of radiative width to transition energy estimated at $\gamma_{\gamma}/E_{\mathrm{m}} = 10^{-19}$
- IC coefficient for neutral Th ground state: $\alpha = \gamma_{IC}/\gamma_{\gamma} \sim 10^9$
- For Th$^+$ state $5f6d_2$ at 30223 cm$^{-1}$ (J=15/2): IC rate ranges from 253 to 432 s$^{-1}$ (M1+E2) for $E_{\mathrm{m}}$ between 9.0 and 11.5 eV; radiative lifetime of this state is 0.4 s
- For Th$^+$ state $7s_27p$ at 31626 cm$^{-1}$ (J=1/2): IC rate ranges from 60717 to 61824 s$^{-1}$ (M1+E2) for $E_{\mathrm{m}}$ between 9.7 and 11.5 eV; radiative lifetime is 40 ns
- For Th$^{2+}$ state $5f7s$ at 7501 cm$^{-1}$ (J=3): IC rate is approximately $7 \times 10^5$ s$^{-1}$ at $E_{\mathrm{m}} = 19.1$ eV; radiative lifetime is 20 $\mu$s
- For Th$^{2+}$ state $6d7s$ at 16038 cm$^{-1}$ (J=2): IC rate is approximately $2 \times 10^5$ s$^{-1}$ at $E_{\mathrm{m}} = 19.2$ eV; radiative lifetime is 100 ns
- Ionization thresholds: Th neutral 6.3 eV, Th$^+$ 12.1 eV, Th$^{2+}$ 20.0 eV, Th$^{3+}$ 28.7 eV, Th$^{4+}$ 58 eV

## Context
This work extends the theoretical understanding of internal conversion in $^{229}$Th, which was first directly observed in neutral atoms by Larsen (2016). The authors build on prior IC calculations by Karpeshin (2007) and Tkalya (2015), and address the unresolved experimental challenge of directly exciting the isomeric state, which has not been achieved despite efforts by Campbell (2011) using trapped Th ions and by Stellmer (2015) using VUV-transparent crystals. The proposed scheme enables a new detection method for the isomeric state via charge-state identification of ions produced by IC from excited electronic configurations, potentially allowing estimation of the isomeric energy and comparison with theoretical IC rates.
