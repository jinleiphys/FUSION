# Nuclear effects in neutrino-nucleus DIS

> We explain the current status of nuclear parton distribution functions in connection with neutrino-nucleus interactions. Neutrino deep inelastic scattering (DIS) measurements have been done for heavy nuclear targets such as iron and lead. In order to extract structure functions of the nucleon, one needs to remove nuclear effects from the data. However, recent studies indicate that there are inconsistencies in nuclear modifications between charged-lepton and neutrino scattering measurements. Nuclear medium effects could be also an origin for the NuTeV anomaly in the weak-mixing angle. In addition, the modifications could affect neutrino-oscillation experiments because some DIS events of neutrino-oxygen nucleus interactions are contained. On the other hand, the nuclear medium effects themselves are interesting and important for describing nuclei in terms of quark and gluon degrees of freedom.

## Key claim

Nuclear modifications of parton distribution functions (PDFs) in neutrino deep inelastic scattering (DIS) may differ from those in charged-lepton DIS, and this discrepancy could affect the extraction of nucleonic structure functions, the interpretation of the NuTeV anomaly in the weak-mixing angle, and the analysis of neutrino-oscillation experiments that include DIS events on oxygen nuclei.

## Method

The authors review the current status of nuclear parton distribution functions (NPDFs) by comparing several global chi-squared analyses (DS04, HKN07, SYKMOO08, EPS09) that fit world data on nuclear structure function ratios, Drell-Yan cross section ratios, and pion-production ratios. They also discuss the alternative approach of Kulagin and Petti (2007), which calculates nuclear corrections using conventional nuclear models and attributes remaining effects to off-shell modifications of bound nucleons.

## Key numbers

- Neutrino DIS experiments (CCFR, CDHSW, CHORUS, NuTeV) used iron and lead targets with neutrino beam energies ranging from 20 to 500 GeV (Table 1).
- The minimum Bjorken x reached in fixed-target neutrino DIS is about 0.01 for Q^2 > 1 GeV^2, compared to x_min ~ 10^-4 at HERA.
- Total chi-squared per degree of freedom for the global NPDF analyses: 0.76 (DS04), 1.2 (HKN07), 1.4 (SYKMOO08), 0.80 (EPS09).
- Nuclear modifications of F_2 in charged-lepton scattering on carbon show suppression at medium x (0.2 < x < 0.6) and enhancement at large x (>0.7) due to Fermi motion, with shadowing at x < 0.1 and antishadowing at x ~ 0.15 (Figure 3).
- The SYKMOO08 analysis found that the medium-x depletion of F_2 in neutrino scattering off iron is smaller than in charged-lepton scattering, and the antishadowing region is shifted toward smaller x (Figure 7).

## Context

This paper reviews and compares the global analyses of nuclear PDFs by de Florian and Sassot (2004), Hirai, Kumano, and Nagai (2007), Schienbein et al. (2008), and Eskola, Paukkunen, and Salgado (2009), as well as the nuclear model approach of Kulagin and Petti (2007). It extends the discussion of the EMC effect (European Muon Collaboration, 1988) to neutrino-nucleus interactions, highlighting inconsistencies between charged-lepton and neutrino nuclear modifications that were also noted by the MSTW collaboration (2009). The work enables future investigations of the NuTeV anomaly and supports the need for dedicated measurements by the MINERvA collaboration and proposed experiments at J-PARC and an electron-ion collider.
