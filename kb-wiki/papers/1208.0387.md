# Novel cluster states in $^{10}$Be

> Cluster structures of excited $^{10}$Be states are investigated with a hybrid model of dineutron condensate wave functions and $^6$He+$α$ cluster wave functions. Two kinds of cluster states are theoretically suggested a few MeV above the $α+α+n+n$ threshold energy. They have quite distinct cluster structure; ones have gas-like structures of $α+α$+dineutron, and the others have $^6$He+$α$ with extremely extended an $α$ cluster. Although these cluster states have not been confirmed experimentally yet, theoretically suggested properties of these states such as monopole transition strengths and $α$-decay widths may be helpful for possible experimental observation.

## Key claim

The paper theoretically predicts two distinct types of cluster states in \(^{10}\)Be a few MeV above the \(\alpha+\alpha+n+n\) threshold energy, but these states have not been confirmed experimentally; the authors suggest that monopole transition strengths and \(\alpha\)-decay widths may be helpful for future experimental observation.

## Method

The authors superpose two kinds of cluster wave functions: \(^{6}\)He+\(\alpha\) cluster wave functions (with cluster separation distance \(d_c\) varied from 1 to 8 fm) and dineutron condensate (DC) wave functions describing a 2\(\alpha\) core plus a dineutron (with parameters for dineutron size \(b_n\), extension \(\beta\) from 2 to 9 fm, and \(\alpha\)-\(\alpha\) distance \(d_n\) from 1 to 6 fm). The wave functions are projected to parity and angular momentum eigenstates, and the Hamiltonian matrix is diagonalized using the Volkov No.2 central force and G3RS spin-orbit force. To identify resonance states among continuum states, the authors apply a pseudo-potential method where a short-range attractive potential is artificially increased to separate resonance states from continuum states.

## Key numbers

- The \(0^+_3\) state has a main peak in overlap with DC wave functions at \((\beta, b_n) \sim (4.0, 2.3)\) fm, indicating a compact dineutron (size \(\sim 2.3\) fm) far from the 2\(\alpha\) core (extension \(\sim 4.0\) fm)
- The \(0^+_4\) state has a peak in overlap with \(^{6}\)He+\(\alpha\) cluster wave functions at a cluster distance of about 7 fm, indicating an extremely developed \(\alpha\) cluster
- The pseudo-potential parameters used are \(v_0 = -100\) MeV and \(a_0 = 1.0\) fm
- The \(0^+_2\) state is experimentally at \(E_x = 6.2\) MeV, which is 2.2 MeV below the \(\alpha+\alpha+n+n\) threshold
- The ground \(0^+\) state is 8.4 MeV below the \(\alpha+\alpha+n+n\) threshold

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous dineutron condensate model applied to \(^{10}\)Be (Kobayashi, 2011) and the \(^{6}\)He+\(\alpha\) cluster model (Kanada-En'yo, 2012). It also responds to an experimental suggestion by Kuchera (2011) of a new \(0^+\) state in \(^{10}\)Be located about 3 MeV above the \(^{6}\)He+\(\alpha\) threshold with a large \(\alpha\) spectroscopic factor. The predicted \(0^+_3\) state (gas-like \(\alpha+\alpha+\)dineutron) is associated with the Hoyle state concept in \(^{12}\)C, while the \(0^+_4\) state (\(^{6}\)He+\(\alpha\) with extremely extended \(\alpha\)) is described as a possible "\(\alpha\)-halo" state. The work enables experimental searches for these states via \(\alpha\)-decay measurements and monopole transition strengths in inelastic scattering.
