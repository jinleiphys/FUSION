# The nature of the lightest scalar meson, its Nc behaviour and semi-local duality

> One-loop unitarized Chiral Perturbation Theory (UChPT) calculations, suggest a different Nc behaviour for the sigma or f0(600) and rho(770) mesons: while the rho meson becomes narrower with Nc, as expected for a qqbar meson, the f0(600) contribution to the total cross section below 1 GeV becomes less and less important. Here we review our recent work where we have shown, by means of finite energy sum rules, that a different Nc behaviour for these resonances may lead to a conflict with semi-local duality for large Nc, since local duality requires a cancellation between the f0(600) and rho(770) amplitudes. However, UChPT calculations also suggest a subdominant qqbar component for the f0(600) with a mass above 1 GeV and this can restore semi-local duality, as we show.

## Key claim

The paper argues that the different $N_c$ behavior of the $f_0(600)$ (sigma) and $\rho(770)$ mesons, where the sigma contribution to the total cross section below 1 GeV becomes less important with increasing $N_c$, unlike the rho, would conflict with semi-local duality for large $N_c$ unless a subdominant $\bar{q}q$ component of the $f_0(600)$ with a mass above 1 GeV restores the required cancellation.

## Method

The authors use the Inverse Amplitude Method (IAM) applied to one-loop and two-loop SU(2) Chiral Perturbation Theory (ChPT) to generate the $\rho(770)$ and $f_0(600)$ poles and study their $N_c$ dependence by rescaling the low-energy constants according to their known leading $1/N_c$ behavior. They then test semi-local duality by computing Finite Energy Sum Rules (FESR) ratios $F^{21}_n$ for $\pi\pi$ scattering, comparing the low-energy resonance contributions (from the IAM) with the Regge expectation that the $t$-channel $I=2$ amplitude must be suppressed.

## Key numbers

* The $f_0(600)$ subdominant $\bar{q}q$ component emerges at a mass of $\simeq$ 1.2 GeV, approximately twice the physical sigma mass at $N_c=3$.
* For $N_c=3$, the FESR ratio $F^{21}_1$ with a cutoff $\nu_{max}=400$ GeV$^2$ is $0.021 \pm 0.016$, indicating strong cancellation (ratio $\sim$ 1:50).
* For $N_c=3$, the FESR ratio $F^{21}_2$ with a cutoff $\nu_{max}=1$ GeV$^2$ is $-0.131 \pm 0.042$ using the KPY data parametrization, and $-0.172$ using two-loop UChPT.
* For $N_c=3$, the FESR ratio $F^{21}_3$ with a cutoff $\nu_{max}=1$ GeV$^2$ is $0.215 \pm 0.027$ using the KPY data parametrization, and $0.145$ using two-loop UChPT.

## Context

This work extends the earlier studies of Pelaez (2003) and Pelaez (2006) on the $N_c$ behavior of light mesons from unitarized ChPT, which showed that the $\rho(770)$ behaves as a $\bar{q}q$ state while the $f_0(600)$ does not. The paper addresses a potential conflict with semi-local duality, a concept established in earlier Regge theory and finite-energy sum rule analyses, and shows that the subdominant $\bar{q}q$ component of the sigma, also suggested by models such as van Beveren (1986), resolves this tension. This enables future studies of the nature of light scalar mesons within the QCD $1/N_c$ expansion framework.
