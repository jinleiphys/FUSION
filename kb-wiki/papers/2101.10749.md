# Weak Kaon Production off the nucleon and Watson's theorem

> We have improved the tree-level model of Ref arXiv:1004.5484 [hep-ph] for weak production of kaons off nucleons by partially restoring unitarity. This is achieved by imposing Watson's theorem to the dominant vector and axial-vector contributions in appropriate angular momentum and isospin quantum number sectors. The observable consequences of this procedure are investigated.

## Key claim

The authors partially restore unitarity in a tree-level model for weak kaon production off nucleons by imposing Watson's theorem on the dominant vector and axial-vector contributions, but the resulting corrections to total cross sections are small (within existing model uncertainties) because of the weakness of kaon-nucleon strong interactions.

## Method

The authors start from the tree-level SU(3) chiral perturbation theory model of Rafi Alam et al. (2010) for the reactions ν_l p → l^- p K^+, ν_l n → l^- p K^0, and ν_l n → l^- n K^+. They introduce complex phases (Olsson phases) into the contact-term contributions of the hadronic current, separately for vector and axial-vector parts, and determine these phases by requiring that the amplitudes satisfy Watson's theorem in the dominant s-wave (L=0) partial wave for isospin I=0 (axial) and I=1 (vector) channels, using KN scattering phase shifts from the SAID database. The phases are applied only to the contact term, which is the largest contribution, while the remaining diagrams (kaon pole, pion/eta in flight, hyperon exchange) are left unchanged.

## Key numbers

* The Fermi coupling constant G_F = 1.166 × 10^{-5} GeV^{-2} and the CKM matrix element |V_us| = 0.2243 are used in the cross section calculation.
* A global dipole form factor with dipole mass 1 ± 0.1 GeV is applied to extend the kinematic range of the calculation.
* The largest change from unitarization is an approximately 18% increase in the total cross section for ν_μ n → μ^- p K^0 at E_ν = 2 GeV compared to the reference tree-level model.
* The model is restricted to KN invariant masses W below the K K Y threshold, and KN partial wave inelasticities are close to one for W below 2.1 GeV.
* Structure functions are shown for fixed kinematics: E_ν = 2 GeV, W = 1.5 GeV, and Q^2 = 0.2 GeV^2.

## Context

This work extends the tree-level model of Rafi Alam et al. (2010) for weak kaon production by partially restoring unitarity, following the same Olsson-phase strategy previously applied to weak pion production by Alvarez-Ruso et al. (2015) and Hernandez et al. (2016). The improved model is applicable below the KKY threshold and enables more reliable predictions for neutrino oscillation experiments such as MINERvA and the future Short Baseline Near Detector (SBND) at Fermilab, where single kaon production measurements could test the underlying strangeness-changing weak interaction mechanisms.
