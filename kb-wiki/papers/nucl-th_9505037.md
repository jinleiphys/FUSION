# Neutron Stars and Massive Quark Matter

> Using the Color-Dielectric model to describe quark confinement, including strange quarks and accounting for beta--equilibrium, we get masses for a static neutron star in the range $1.3\leq M/M_{\odot}\leq 1.54 $ for a radius $R\approx 9$ km. Rapid cooling through the direct URCA mechanism is obtained. The implications for the composition of neutron stars is discussed.

## Key claim

Using the Color-Dielectric model (CDM) to describe quark confinement with strange quarks and beta-equilibrium, the authors obtain static neutron star masses in the range 1.3 to 1.54 solar masses with a radius of about 9 km, but this result depends on the specific parameter set that reproduces nucleon mass and radius, and the equation of state is much softer than those from the Walecka model or Dirac-Brueckner-Hartree-Fock calculations.

## Method

The authors employed the Color-Dielectric model (CDM) in the mean-field approximation to describe quark matter, including u, d, and s quarks, with coupling constants fixed to reproduce experimental nucleon mass and radius. They constructed an equation of state valid at all densities by joining the Friedman-Pandharipande equation of state for low densities with the CDM equation of state through a Maxwell construction, then solved the Tolman-Oppenheimer-Volkov equation to obtain static neutron star masses and radii. The neutrino and antineutrino luminosity from direct URCA processes was computed using the ultrarelativistic expansion for chemical potentials to estimate cooling times.

## Key numbers

- Maximum neutron star mass: M_max ≈ 1.54 M_sun, with radius 9 km at central density approximately 9 times nuclear matter saturation density (n_0 = 0.17 fm^-3)
- Minimum neutron star mass: 1.3 M_sun
- Minimum rotation period: P_m = 0.55 ms
- Quark masses in the CDM are of order 100 MeV up to densities about 10 times n_0
- Fermi momenta of quarks in the relevant region are of order 400 MeV
- Strange quarks appear at density approximately 1/3 n_0
- Direct URCA mechanism involving u and d quarks starts at densities larger than 0.53 fm^-3
- Direct URCA mechanism involving strange quarks starts at densities larger than 1.4 fm^-3
- Cooling time: tau = C * 1 day / T_9^4, where C ranges from 0.5 to approximately 10 going from heavy to light neutron stars

## Context

This work extends the application of the Color-Dielectric model, previously successful for single nucleon properties (Birse 1990, Pirner 1992), to neutron star observables, contrasting with the MIT bag model where quarks have masses of a few MeV. The results support the possibility that neutron star cores contain quark matter, and the soft equation of state obtained is relevant for supernova simulations (Bethe 1990) and for interpreting observations of young neutron star cooling (Umeda), where temperatures lie below those predicted by indirect URCA processes.
