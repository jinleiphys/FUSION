# Alpha-induced inelastic scattering and alpha-transfer reactions in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O within the Algebraic Cluster Model

> The molecular algebraic model based on three and four alpha clusters is used to describe the inelastic scattering of alpha particles populating low-lying states in $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O. Optical potentials and inelastic formfactors are obtained by folding densities and transition densities obtained within the molecular model. One-step and multi-step processes can be included in the reaction mechanism calculation. In spite of the simplicity of the approach the molecular model with rotations and vibrations provides a reliable description of reactions where $α$-cluster degrees of freedom are involved and good results are obtained for the excitation of several low-lying states. Within the same model we briefly discuss the expected selection rules for the $α$-transfer reactions from $^{12}$C and $^{16}$O.

## Key claim

The Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM), despite its simplicity in assuming preformed alpha particles in triangular (for 12C) or tetrahedral (for 16O) configurations, provides a reliable description of alpha-induced inelastic scattering cross sections for several low-lying states, even within a first-order DWBA treatment, though the agreement depends on the choice of the imaginary part of the optical potential and may require coupled-channel calculations for some transitions.

## Method

The authors constructed ground-state densities for 12C and 16O by summing Gaussian densities of individual alpha particles placed at the vertices of an equilateral triangle (12C, with beta = 1.82 fm) or a tetrahedron (16O, with beta = 2 fm). Transition densities for vibrational bands (e.g., the Hoyle band in 12C) were obtained by expanding the intrinsic density in spherical harmonics and, for excited bands, by taking derivatives with respect to the size parameter beta. These densities were then used in a double-folding procedure with the M3Y nucleon-nucleon interaction to generate optical potentials and inelastic form factors, which were input into DWBA calculations for alpha-induced inelastic scattering at 240 MeV (12C) and 130 MeV (16O).

## Key numbers

- For 12C, the alpha particle density width parameter alpha = 0.56(2) fm^-2.
- For 12C, the distance of each alpha from the center of the equilateral triangle is beta = 1.82 fm, chosen to reproduce the ground state radius and B(E2) to the first excited 2+ state.
- For 12C, the intrinsic strength parameter chi_1 for the transition to the Hoyle band was set to chi_1 = 0.247255 to reproduce the monopole matrix element M(E0) connecting the Hoyle and ground 0+ states.
- For 16O, the distance of each alpha from the center of the tetrahedron is beta = 2 fm.
- For 16O, the strength parameter chi for the transition to the first excited A band was set to chi = 0.22, based on the M(0) matrix element between the ground 0+ and the excited 0+ state.
- The bombarding energy for the alpha + 12C reaction was 240 MeV.
- The bombarding energy for the alpha + 16O reaction was 130 MeV.

## Context

This work extends the Algebraic Cluster Model (ACM), developed by Iachello, Bijker and collaborators (Bijker 2002, Bijker 2014), from structure calculations of spectra and electromagnetic transitions to the description of hadron scattering reactions. The paper builds on the authors' previous work on 12C (Casal 2019) and presents new preliminary results for 16O. The approach enables the calculation of inelastic scattering cross sections for alpha-cluster nuclei using a simple geometrical model, and it predicts selection rules for alpha-transfer reactions between 12C and 16O based on the symmetry properties of the underlying discrete groups (D3h and Td), which can be tested against future experimental data.
