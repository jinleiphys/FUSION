# Collective effects in light-heavy ion collisions

> We present results for the azimuthal anisotropy of charged hadron distributions in A+A, p+A, d+A, and He3+A collisions within the IP-Glasma+MUSIC model. Obtained anisotropies are due to the fluid dynamic response of the system to the fluctuating initial geometry of the interaction region. While the elliptic and triangular anisotropies in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions at root-s=2.76 TeV are well described by the model, the same quantities in root-s=5.02 TeV p+Pb collisions underestimate the experimental data. This disagreement can be due to neglected initial state correlations or the lack of a detailed description of the fluctuating spatial structure of the proton, or both. We further present predictions for azimuthal anisotropies in p+Au, d+Au, and He3+Au collisions at root-s=200 GeV. For d+Au and 3He+Au collisions we expect the detailed substructure of the nucleon to become less important.

## Key claim

The IP-Glasma+MUSIC model, which successfully describes elliptic and triangular flow in peripheral Pb+Pb collisions, underestimates the same quantities in p+Pb collisions by up to a factor of four, suggesting that either neglected initial state correlations or an oversimplified description of the proton's fluctuating spatial structure (or both) are responsible for the discrepancy.

## Method

The authors used the IP-Glasma model to generate event-by-event initial conditions for the energy density and flow velocity from classical Yang-Mills fields, then evolved the system with the 2+1 dimensional viscous relativistic fluid dynamics code MUSIC (with shear viscosity to entropy density ratio eta/s = 0.12) until freeze-out at T = 135 MeV. For deuteron-gold collisions, the nucleon distribution in the deuteron was computed using the Hulthen form of its wave function; for helium-3-gold collisions, nucleon configurations from Green's function Monte Carlo calculations with the AV18 + UIX model interaction were employed. The impact parameter b was sampled between 0 and 2 fm for all systems.

## Key numbers

- Elliptic flow v2 in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV is under-predicted by approximately a factor of 4 compared to CMS data
- Triangular flow v3 in p+Pb collisions at sqrt(s) = 5.02 TeV agrees with CMS data at lower multiplicities but underestimates data at higher multiplicities
- Initial time for fluid dynamic evolution was set to tau0 = 0.5 fm/c
- Freeze-out temperature was set to T = 135 MeV
- Shear viscosity to entropy density ratio was set to eta/s = 0.12
- Impact parameter b was sampled between 0 and 2 fm for p+Au, d+Au, and He3+Au collisions at sqrt(s) = 200 GeV

## Context

This work extends the IP-Glasma+MUSIC framework from heavy ion collisions (Pb+Pb at sqrt(s) = 2.76 TeV) to small collision systems including p+Pb, p+Au, d+Au, and He3+Au. The results differ significantly from those in Bozek (2012), Werner (2013), Kozlov (2014), and Bzdak (2014), indicating that the details of the initial shape in small systems are critically important. The predictions for He3+Au collisions at RHIC energies enable experimental tests of whether final state collective effects dominate the measured azimuthal anisotropy, with the expectation that the detailed substructure of the nucleon becomes less important for d+Au and He3+Au compared to p+A collisions.
