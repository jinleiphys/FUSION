# QGP viscosity at RHIC and the LHC - a 2012 status report

> In this article, we briefly review the recent progress related to extracting the quark-gluon plasma (QGP) specific shear viscosity from the flow data measured at Relativistic Heavy-Ion Collider (RHIC) and the Large Hadron Collider (LHC).

## Key claim
The paper reviews the status of extracting the QGP specific shear viscosity from flow data at RHIC and LHC, concluding that the averaged value lies between 1/(4π) and 2.5/(4π) at RHIC, but noting that the extracted value depends strongly on the choice of initial condition model (MC-Glauber vs. MC-KLN), which introduces a factor-of-two uncertainty.

## Method
The author reviews viscous hydrodynamic simulations (2+1-d and 3+1-d) and hybrid models that couple viscous hydrodynamics for the QGP phase to a hadron cascade (UrQMD) for the late hadronic stage. The key observable is the eccentricity-scaled elliptic flow v₂/ε_x as a function of charged multiplicity per unit overlap area, which is claimed to be universal and depends only on the QGP shear viscosity, not on initialization models. Single-shot event-averaged simulations are compared to experimental data after correcting for non-flow and fluctuation effects.

## Key numbers
- The extracted averaged specific shear viscosity at top RHIC energies (200 A GeV Au+Au) is: 1/(4π) < (η/s)_QGP < 2.5/(4π), where the uncertainty is dominated by the difference between MC-Glauber and MC-KLN initial conditions.
- An earlier conservative upper limit from Luzum and Romatschke (2008) gave (η/s)_QGP < 5 × 1/(4π).
- For LHC (2.76 A TeV Pb+Pb), the extracted (η/s)_QGP increases from ~2/(4π) to ~2.5/(4π) for MC-KLN initial conditions to better describe the elliptic flow data.
- A simultaneous fit of elliptic and triangular flow at LHC using pure viscous hydrodynamics favors a smaller value around 1/(4π), with MC-Glauber initial conditions describing the centrality-dependent integrated v₂ and v₃, while MC-KLN under-predicts triangular flow.
- The combined analysis of all measured v_n (n=1,…,5) with IP-Glasma initial conditions gives η/s ~ 0.20 for LHC collisions and η/s ~ 0.12 for RHIC collisions.

## Context
This work extends the earlier extraction by Luzum and Romatschke (2008) that used pure viscous hydrodynamics and gave an upper limit of (η/s)_QGP < 5/(4π), by incorporating the VISHNU hybrid model (Song 2010) that includes a hadronic afterburner (UrQMD) to control uncertainties from the hadronic phase. The review also discusses how the IP-Glasma model (Schenke, Tribedy, Venugopalan) has recently reduced uncertainties by eliminating both MC-Glauber and MC-KLN as viable initial-state fluctuation models. The results enable future systematic χ² fitting of multiple observables (spectra, flow harmonics, HBT radii) to determine temperature-dependent (η/s)_QGP(T) and constrain initial conditions.
