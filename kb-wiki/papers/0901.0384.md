# Break-up fragment topology in statistical multifragmentation models

> Break-up fragmentation patterns together with kinetic and configurational energy fluctuations are investigated in the framework of a microcanonical model with fragment degrees of freedom over a broad excitation energy range. As far as fragment partitioning is approximately preserved, energy fluctuations are found to be rather insensitive to both the way in which the freeze-out volume is constrained and the trajectory followed by the system in the excitation energy - freeze-out volume space. Due to hard-core repulsion, the freeze-out volume is found to be populated un-uniformly, its highly depleted core giving the source a bubble-like structure. The most probable localization of the largest fragments in the freeze-out volume may be inferred experimentally from their kinematic properties, largely dictated by Coulomb repulsion.

## Key claim

Kinetic and configurational energy fluctuations in statistical multifragmentation are rather insensitive to both the freeze-out volume constraint and the trajectory in the excitation energy–freeze-out volume plane, provided that the fragment partitioning is approximately preserved; this holds even when the two considered paths explore different regions of the phase diagram (one supra-critical, one crossing phase coexistence).

## Method

The author employed the Microcanonical Model of Multifragmentation (MMM) for a medium-size nucleus (130,60) over an excitation energy range of 2 to 14 MeV/nucleon, comparing two paths: a constant freeze-out volume at V=6V0 and a path where the average freeze-out volume increases from 3.5V0 at 2 MeV/nucleon to 10.4V0 at 14 MeV/nucleon. The statistical weight of each break-up configuration was computed in the microcanonical ensemble with fixed total energy, proton number, and neutron number, and the resulting average and RMS values of binding energy, internal excitation, Coulomb interaction, and thermal kinetic energy were analyzed alongside fragment charge and radial distributions.

## Key numbers

- The two considered paths differ in average freeze-out volume from 3.5V0 at 2 MeV/nucleon to 10.4V0 at 14 MeV/nucleon for the increasing-volume path.
- At a given excitation energy, the total binding energy and Coulomb interaction values along the two paths differ by at most 20% over the considered energy domain.
- The relative difference in fragment internal excitation between the two paths reaches 50% at Eex = 14 MeV/nucleon.
- The maximum average kinetic energy of primary fragments at 4 MeV/nucleon is 49 MeV, exceeding by 25% the maximum at 6 MeV/nucleon.
- The heaviest fragments (Z=50) are concentrated in a region representing only 15% of the total freeze-out volume.
- At 8 MeV/nucleon, the matter in the inner regions of the freeze-out volume is 10 times denser than at 6 MeV/nucleon.

## Context

This work extends the statistical multifragmentation studies using the Microcanonical Model of Multifragmentation (MMM) previously developed by Raduta and Raduta (1997) and builds on comparisons with the MMMC model of Gross (1990) and the SMM model of Bondorf et al. (1995). The finding of bubble-like nuclear matter distributions at break-up agrees with results from stochastic mean-field approaches reported by Colonna et al. (2004) and Parlog et al. (2005). The systematic shift in average kinetic energies of the largest, second-largest, and third-largest fragments confirms experimental observations by the INDRA collaboration for Xe+Sn at 32 MeV/nucleon and Gd+U at 36 MeV/nucleon reactions (Tabacaru et al., 2006). The work enables experimental inference of fragment spatial localization at freeze-out from kinetic energy distributions, and highlights the need to account for post-break-up dynamics and multiparticle correlations when nuclear proximity potentials are included.
