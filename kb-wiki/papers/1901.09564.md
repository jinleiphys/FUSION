# Elastic proton-proton scattering at LHC energies in holographic QCD

> The cross sections of the high energy proton-proton scattering are studied in a holographic QCD model, focusing on the Regge regime. In our model setup, the involved nonperturbative partonic dynamics is described by the Pomeron exchange, which is realized by applying the Reggeized spin-2 particle propagator together with the proton gravitational form factor obtained from the bottom-up AdS/QCD model. Our model includes three adjustable parameters which are to be fitted by experimental data. We show that both the resulting differential and total cross sections are consistent with data, including the ones recently measured at $\sqrt{s}=13$ TeV by the TOTEM collaboration at the LHC. Our results imply that the present framework works well in the considered TeV scale, and further applications to other high energy scattering processes, in which the involved strong interaction can be approximated by the Pomeron exchange, are possible.

## Key claim

The holographic QCD model, using the proton gravitational form factor from the bottom-up AdS/QCD model (hard-wall or soft-wall) and a Reggeized spin-2 Pomeron propagator with only three adjustable parameters, can simultaneously describe both the differential and total cross sections of elastic proton-proton scattering at LHC energies from 2.76 to 13 TeV, provided the kinematic region satisfies the Regge condition \(s \gg |t|\) with \(|t| < 0.45\) GeV\(^2\).

## Method

The authors model elastic proton-proton scattering in the Regge regime by describing the nonperturbative partonic dynamics via Pomeron exchange, realized by combining a Reggeized spin-2 particle propagator with the proton gravitational form factor \(A(t)\) obtained from the bottom-up AdS/QCD model. The three adjustable parameters (Pomeron intercept \(\alpha_c(0)\), Pomeron slope \(\alpha_c'\), and proton-glueball coupling \(\lambda\)) are fitted simultaneously to experimental differential and total cross section data using a log-linear least squares fit with the MINUIT package. Two versions of the AdS/QCD model are considered: the hard-wall model with an IR cutoff and the soft-wall model with a background dilaton field.

## Key numbers

- Pomeron intercept \(\alpha_c(0) = 1.084 \pm 0.005\) (soft-wall) and \(1.086 \pm 0.003\) (hard-wall) when fitting all data from 546 GeV to 13 TeV.
- Pomeron slope \(\alpha_c' = 0.368 \pm 0.008\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (soft-wall) and \(0.377 \pm 0.007\) GeV\(^{-2}\) (hard-wall) for the full data set.
- Proton-glueball coupling \(\lambda = 9.59 \pm 0.44\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (soft-wall) and \(9.70 \pm 0.32\) GeV\(^{-1}\) (hard-wall) for the full data set.
- \(\chi^2 / d.o.f. = 1.304\) (soft-wall) and \(1.352\) (hard-wall) for the full data set, calculated using the 8 TeV TOTEM differential cross section data.
- The soft-wall model parameter \(\kappa = 0.350\) GeV gives a proton mass of 0.990 GeV and a \(\rho\)-meson mass of 0.700 GeV.
- The hard-wall model IR cutoff is \(z_0 = (0.245\ \text{GeV})^{-1}\).
- The kinematic region used for fitting is \(\sqrt{s} \ge 546\) GeV and \(|t| < 0.45\) GeV\(^2\), giving \(|t|/s < 1.5 \times 10^{-6}\).

## Context

This work extends the holographic QCD approach of Domokos (2009) and Hu (2017) by replacing the phenomenological dipole form factor for the proton-Pomeron vertex with the gravitational form factor \(A(t)\) calculated from the bottom-up AdS/QCD model of Abidin (2009), thereby reducing the number of free parameters from four to three. The model successfully describes the new TOTEM data at \(\sqrt{s} = 13\) TeV (Antchev 2017, 2018) which previous phenomenological models could not simultaneously fit across the full range from 2.76 to 13 TeV. This enables future applications to other high-energy scattering processes where the strong interaction can be approximated by Pomeron exchange, such as proton-antiproton scattering or central production processes.
