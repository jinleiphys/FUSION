# The cosmological constant and the use of cutoffs

> Of the contributions to the cosmological constant, zero-point energy and self energy contributions scale as $Λ^4$ where $Λ$ is an ultraviolet cutoff used to regulate the calculations. I show that such contributions vanish when calculated in perturbation theory. This demonstration uses a little-known modification to perturbation theory found by Honerkamp and Meetz and by Gerstein, Jackiw, Lee and Weinberg which comes into play when using cutoffs and interactions with multiple derivatives, as found in chiral theories and gravity. In a path integral treatment, the new interaction arises from the path integral measure. This reduces the sensitivity of the cosmological constant to the high energy cutoff significantly, although it does not resolve the cosmological constant problem. The feature removes one of the common motivations for supersymmetry. It also calls into question some of the results of the Asymptotic Safety program. Covariance and quadratic cutoff dependence are also briefly discussed.

## Key claim

The paper shows that the naive quartic cutoff dependence (Λ⁴) of zero-point energy contributions to the cosmological constant vanishes when calculated in perturbation theory, but this demonstration does not resolve the cosmological constant problem and leaves quadratic cutoff dependence (m²Λ²) intact.

## Method

The author applies a modification to perturbation theory originally developed by Honerkamp and Meetz and by Gerstein, Jackiw, Lee and Weinberg (GJLW) for theories with derivative interactions, showing that when using cutoff regularization, a new interaction term arising from the path integral measure cancels the δ⁴(0) ∼ Λ⁴ contributions. The analysis is performed for scalar fields, fermions, photons, and gravitons coupled to gravity, using both canonical quantization and covariant path integral methods with a simplified conformal metric gμν = (1+h(x))ημν and a general metric decomposition into conformal and unimodular parts.

## Key numbers

- The measured cosmological constant is Λcc ∼ (10⁻³ eV)⁴
- The naive zero-point energy calculation yields a contribution ∼ 1/(16π²) Λ⁴, which would fail by "120 orders of magnitude" if Λ is taken at the Planck scale
- The covariant tadpole diagram calculation gives Λcc ∼ 1/(64π²) Λ⁴
- The new interaction term in the Lagrangian is Δℒ = -½ i δ⁴(0) log(1+h(x)) for the simplified metric, where δ⁴(0) = ∫ d⁴p/(2π)⁴ = i/(32π²) Λ⁴
- For fermions, the new interaction term is Δℒ = -i (3/8) δ⁴(0) log(-g)
- For gravitons, the new interaction term is Δℒ = i (1/8) δ⁴(0) log(-g̃)
- The quadratic cutoff dependence from massive particles gives δΛcc ∼ m²Λ²

## Context

This work extends the results of Honerkamp and Meetz (1970s) and Gerstein, Jackiw, Lee and Weinberg (1971) on derivative interactions in chiral theories to gravitational interactions, and also builds on Fradkin and Vilkovisky (1974) who showed a similar cancellation in pure gravity. The paper removes one of the common motivations for supersymmetry (cancellation of Λ⁴ zero-point contributions) and calls into question some results of the Asymptotic Safety program. It enables a more careful treatment of cutoff regularization in effective field theories of gravity, while noting that quadratic cutoff dependence and other radiative corrections to the cosmological constant remain.
