# Cherenkov and Fano effects at the origin of asymmetric vector mesons in nuclear media

> It is argued that the experimentally observed phenomenon of asymmetric vector mesons produced in nuclear media during high energy nucleus-nucleus collisions can be explained as Cherenkov and Fano effects. The mass distributions of lepton pairs created at meson decays decline from the traditional Breit-Wigner shape in the low-mass wing of the resonance. That is explained by the positive real part of the amplitude in this wing for classic Cherenkov treatment and further detalized in quantum mechanics as the interference of direct and continuum states in Fano effect. The corresponding parameters are found from the comparison with rho-meson data and admit reasonable explanation.

# Cherenkov and Fano effects at the origin of asymmetric vector mesons in nuclear media

## Key claim

The experimentally observed asymmetric low-mass wing of vector meson resonances produced in high-energy nucleus-nucleus collisions can be explained by the combination of Cherenkov gluon effects (classical treatment) and Fano interference between continuum and discrete states (quantum treatment), with the condition that the refractivity index of the nuclear medium exceeds 1 due to a positive real part of the forward scattering amplitude in the low-mass wing.

## Method

The author fits the dilepton mass spectrum from semi-central In(158 AGeV)-In collisions measured by the NA60 Collaboration using a modified Breit-Wigner formula that includes an additional term proportional to the real part of the forward scattering amplitude, which is nonzero only for masses below the resonance mass. The asymmetry parameter \(w_r\) is extracted from the fit and then related to the Fano parameter \(q\) by equating the asymmetric components of the two formalisms.

## Key numbers

- The fitted width of the in-medium \(\rho\)-meson peak is \(\Gamma = 354\) MeV, approximately twice the in-vacuum width of 150 MeV.
- The fitted asymmetry parameter for the \(\rho\)-meson is \(w_{\rho} = 0.19\).
- The normalization constant from the fit is \(A = 104\) GeV\(^3\).
- The Fano parameter derived from the relation \(q = -w_{\rho}/2\) is \(q \approx -0.1\).
- The admixture of direct-state contributions relative to continuum contributions in the Fano effect is estimated as \(\pi q^2/2 \approx 0.014\).
- The observed ratio of resonance strengths is \(\rho : \omega : \phi = 10 : 1 : 2\).

## Context

This work extends the author's earlier proposal of Cherenkov gluon effects in nuclear media (Dremin, 2014) and provides a quantum-mechanical foundation via the Fano effect, originally developed for atomic resonances (Fano, 1961). The approach offers an alternative to several prior explanations of the low-mass dilepton excess in heavy-ion collisions, including models by Rapp and collaborators (2000), Brown and Rho (2002), and others reviewed by Hayashi and collaborators (2013). The connection to Feshbach resonances in nuclear physics and the Efimov effect for three-body bound states is noted as a possible direction for identifying the unknown third component interacting with \(\rho\)-meson quarks. The work enables further experimental searches for universal low-mass asymmetry in other vector meson resonances and motivates theoretical models of collective excitations in the nuclear medium.
