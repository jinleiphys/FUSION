# Wigner distributions and quark orbital angular momentum

> We discuss the quark phase-space or Wigner distributions of the nucleon which combine in a single picture all the information contained in the generalized parton distributions and the transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions. In particular, we present results for the distribution of unpolarized quarks in a longitudinally polarized nucleon obtained in a light-front constituent quark model. We show how the quark orbital angular momentum can be extracted from the Wigner distributions and compare it with alternative definitions.

## Key claim

The paper argues that quark orbital angular momentum (OAM) can be extracted from Wigner distributions via an intuitive phase-space integral, but the resulting value depends on the choice of gauge link (straight vs. staple-shaped), giving either the kinetic OAM (Ji decomposition) or the canonical OAM (Jaffe-Manohar decomposition); in pure quark models, all definitions agree on the total quark OAM but differ for separate quark-flavor contributions.

## Method

The authors compute quark Wigner distributions by taking the Fourier transform of generalized transverse-momentum dependent parton distributions (GTMDs) to impact-parameter space, using the light-front constituent quark model (LFCQM) and the light-front version of the chiral quark-soliton model (LFχQSM). They then extract the quark OAM from the Wigner distributions via the integral \(l_z^q = \int dx\, d^2k_\perp\, d^2b_\perp (\vec{b}_\perp \times \vec{k}_\perp)_z \, \rho^{[\gamma^+]q}_{\Lambda\Lambda}(x,\vec{k}_\perp,\vec{b}_\perp,n)\) and compare it with alternative definitions based on GPDs (Ji sum rule) and TMDs.

## Key numbers

* In the LFCQM, the total quark OAM (u+d) is 0.126 for all three definitions (Ji, Jaffe-Manohar, TMD-based).
* In the LFCQM, the Ji OAM for u quarks is 0.071 and for d quarks is 0.055.
* In the LFCQM, the Jaffe-Manohar OAM for u quarks is 0.131 and for d quarks is -0.005.
* In the LFCQM, the TMD-based OAM for u quarks is 0.169 and for d quarks is -0.042.
* In the LFχQSM, the total quark OAM (u+d) is 0.069 for all three definitions.
* In the LFχQSM, the Ji OAM for u quarks is -0.008 and for d quarks is 0.077.
* In the LFχQSM, the Jaffe-Manohar OAM for u quarks is 0.073 and for d quarks is -0.004.
* In the LFχQSM, the TMD-based OAM for u quarks is 0.093 and for d quarks is -0.023.
* The distortion \(\rho^q_{LU}\) of the Wigner distribution is shown for a fixed transverse momentum \(k_\perp = 0.3\) GeV.
* The d-quark OAM changes sign around 0.25 fm away from the transverse center of momentum.

## Context

This work extends the concept of Wigner distributions in QCD, first explored by Ji (2003) and Belitsky (2003) where relativistic effects were neglected, by using the impact-parameter representation of GTMDs identified by Lorce (2011) as relativistic Wigner distributions. The results are obtained in two quark models (LFCQM and LFχQSM) because direct experimental access to Wigner distributions is not yet known. The paper enables future connections between Wigner distributions, GPDs, and TMDs for extracting quark OAM, and notes that the LFχQSM predicts a negative sign for u-quark OAM in agreement with lattice calculations by Hagler (2007), unlike the LFCQM.
