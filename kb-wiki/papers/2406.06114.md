# Unraveling alignment pattern in high-energy particles via transverse momentum disbalance analysis

> The hypothesis of the relation between strong azimuthal correlations, known as alignment, in families of hadrons and photons observed in cosmic ray experiments and the selection procedure of the highest-energy particles together with the transverse momentum conservation are tested in the framework of the HYDJET++ model. The results show that the high degree of alignment can appear in nucleus-nucleus interactions at reasonable values of the transverse momentum disbalance of most energetic detected particles.

## Key claim

The paper shows that a high degree of alignment (strong azimuthal correlations) in high-energy particle families can arise from the selection procedure for the highest-energy particles combined with event-by-event transverse momentum conservation, but only for three particles; for four and five particles the model cannot reproduce the central values of experimental data from the Pamir collaboration, leaving room for other explanations.

## Method

The authors used the HYDJET++ event generator to simulate Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV per nucleon pair, transforming particle momenta from the center-of-mass system to the laboratory frame with a rapidity shift to mimic cosmic-ray interactions in the atmosphere. They imposed a transverse momentum disbalance condition on the selected highest-energy particles, requiring the vector sum of their transverse momenta to be less than a variable threshold Delta, and computed the alignment parameter lambda_N for N=3,4,5 particles as a function of Delta.

## Key numbers

* Degree of alignment for three particles without disbalance condition: P_3 approximately 0.2 at lambda_N > 0.8 for Pb+Pb collisions at 5.02 TeV.
* Degree of alignment for four particles without disbalance condition: P_4 approximately 0.04 at lambda_N > 0.8.
* Degree of alignment for five particles without disbalance condition: P_5 approximately 0.008 at lambda_N > 0.8.
* Best alignment values achieved with disbalance Delta in the range 0 to 1 GeV: P_3 = 1, P_4 approximately 0.07, P_5 approximately 0.04.
* Experimental values from Pamir collaboration: P_3_exp = 0.83 plus or minus 0.27, P_4_exp = 0.67 plus or minus 0.33, P_5_exp = 0.33 plus or minus 0.23.
* Fraction of selected events at Delta = 0.05 GeV for N=3: L^{[Delta]} / L^{[tot]} = 2 times 10^{-4} out of 10^5 total events.
* Fraction of selected events at Delta = 9.0 GeV for N=3: L^{[Delta]} / L^{[tot]} = 0.9.
* Simulation parameters: r_min = r_res = 1 mm, r_max = 15 mm, height h = 1 km, energy threshold E_thr = 4 TeV.

## Context

This work extends the authors' previous geometrical and kinematical studies (Lokhtin 2023, Lokhtin 2024) by implementing the alignment analysis in the realistic HYDJET++ heavy-ion collision model, which successfully describes RHIC and LHC data. The results contradict the hypothesis that alignment is merely a fluctuation tail, as suggested by Mukhamedshin (2019), but show that transverse momentum conservation alone cannot explain the high alignment degrees for four and five particles observed by the Pamir collaboration, motivating searches for additional dynamical mechanisms.
