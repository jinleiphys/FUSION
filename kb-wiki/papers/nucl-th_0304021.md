# Hartree-Fock approach to nuclear matter and finite nuclei with M3Y-type nucleon-nucleon interactions

> By introducing a density-dependent contact term, M3Y-type interactions applicable to the Hartree-Fock calculations are developed. In order to view basic characters of the interactions, we carry out calculations on the uniform nuclear matter as well as on several doubly magic nuclei. It is shown that a parameter-set called M3Y-P2 describes various properties similarly well to the Skyrme SLy5 and/or the Gogny D1S interactions. A remarkable difference from the SLy5 and the D1S interactions is found in the spin-isospin properties in the nuclear matter, to which the one-pion-exchange potential gives a significant contribution. Affecting the single-particle energies, this difference may play a certain role in the new magic numbers in unstable nuclei.

## Key claim

By introducing a density-dependent contact term into M3Y-type interactions, the parameter-set M3Y-P2 describes various nuclear properties similarly well to the Skyrme SLy5 and Gogny D1S interactions, but a remarkable difference appears in the spin-isospin properties of nuclear matter due to the one-pion-exchange potential contribution, which may affect single-particle energies and play a role in new magic numbers in unstable nuclei.

## Method

The authors modified the Paris-potential version of the M3Y interaction (called M3Y-P0) by adding a density-dependent contact term to achieve saturation properties, while keeping the Yukawa function ranges fixed and the longest-range part fixed to the one-pion-exchange potential. They performed Hartree-Fock calculations on uniform nuclear matter and on several doubly magic nuclei using a hybrid basis-set of Kamimura-Gauss and harmonic-oscillator bases, with two parameter-sets (M3Y-P1 and M3Y-P2) to test sensitivity.

## Key numbers

- Saturation Fermi momentum for M3Y-P1: k_F0 approximately 1.36 fm; saturation energy per nucleon approximately 16 MeV
- Incompressibility K for M3Y-type interactions: approximately 210 MeV with alpha approximately 1/3
- Volume asymmetry energy a_t for M3Y-type interactions: approximately 30 MeV
- Enhancement factor for the LS force in M3Y-P2: 1.8; reduction factor for the tensor force in M3Y-P2: 0.12
- Binding energies of doubly magic nuclei reproduced by M3Y-P2 within about 5 MeV accuracy
- The OPEP part increases the a_st coefficient by about 11 MeV

## Context

This work extends the M3Y interaction approach (Bertsch et al., 1977) by adding density dependence to make it applicable to Hartree-Fock calculations, following the strategy used in Skyrme and Gogny interactions. The M3Y-P2 set is compared with the Skyrme SLy5 set (Chabanat et al., 1998) and the Gogny D1S set (Berger et al., 1991), showing similar performance for stable nuclei but different spin-isospin properties that may be relevant for understanding new magic numbers in unstable nuclei as discussed by Ozawa et al. (2000).
