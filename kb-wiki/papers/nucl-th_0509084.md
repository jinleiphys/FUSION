# Number of states for nucleons in a single-$j$ shell

> In this paper we obtain number of states with a given spin $I$ and a given isospin $T$ for systems with three and four nucleons in a single-$j$ orbit, by using sum rules of six-$j$ and nine-$j$ symbols obtained in earlier works.

## Key claim

The paper derives, for the first time, algebraic formulas for the number of nuclear states with a given spin I and isospin T for three and four nucleons confined to a single-j shell, using sum rules of six-j and nine-j symbols previously obtained for identical particles. The formulas are conditional on the shell angular momentum j being a half-integer and are derived under the assumption that all two-body pairing interaction strengths G_JT are set to 1.

## Method

The authors use the trace of the J-pairing interaction Hamiltonian H_JT (with all strengths set to 1) over states of total spin I and isospin T for three and four nucleons in a single-j orbit. By equating this trace to n(n-1)/2 times the number of states D_IT, they derive algebraic expressions for D_IT from sum rules of six-j and nine-j symbols. The method is applied separately for each allowed isospin value (T=2, 1, 0 for four nucleons; T=1/2 and 3/2 for three nucleons), with the summations over intermediate angular momenta J and K restricted by the Pauli principle and the triangle relations for vector coupling.

## Key numbers

* For four nucleons with T=2 and I=12k+4: D_IT = (2k+1)j - (1/2)(34k+18k(k-1)+3)
* For four nucleons with T=2 and I=12k+10: D_IT = (2k+2)j - (1/2)(26k+9k(k-1)+8)
* For four nucleons with T=1 and I >= 2j: D_IT = ( [I_0/2] + 1 ) ( [I_0/2] + 1 + (I_0 mod 2) ), where I_0 = (I_max - I)/2 for odd I and (I_max - I - 1)/2 for even I
* For four nucleons with T=1 and I <= 2j: D_IT = (I_0+1)j - (1 + 4[I_0/2] + 6[I_0/2]^2 + (I_0 mod 2)(6[I_0/2]+3))/2, where I_0 = (I-1)/2
* For three nucleons with T=1/2 and I <= j: D_IT = 1 + 2[(I-1/2)/3] + delta_{((I-1/2) mod 3), 2}
* For three nucleons with T=1/2 and I >= j: D_IT = 1 + [(I_max - I)/3], where I_max = 3j-1
* For three nucleons with T=3/2 and I <= j: D_IT = [(2I+3)/6]
* For three nucleons with T=3/2 and I >= j: D_IT = [(3j-3-I)/6] + delta_I, where delta_I = 0 if ((3j-3)-I) mod 6 = 1, otherwise 1

## Context

This work extends earlier algebraic formulas for the number of spin-I states for identical particles in a single-j shell (Zhao and Arima, 2004; Zhao and Arima, 2005) to include both protons and neutrons by incorporating isospin. The results are consistent with a modular relation between D_I(T=0) and D_I(T=2) recently found by Zamick and Escuderos (2005). The method can be applied to obtain similar formulas for three and four bosons with F-spin in the interacting boson models IBM-II, IBM-III, and IBM-IV.
