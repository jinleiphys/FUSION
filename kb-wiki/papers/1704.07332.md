# Spin partners of the $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ revisited

> We study the implications of the heavy-quark spin symmetry for the possible spin partners of the exotic states $Z_b(10610)$ and $Z_b(10650)$ in the spectrum of bottomonium. We formulate and solve numerically the coupled-channel equations for the $Z_b$ states that allow for a dynamical generation of these states as hadronic molecules. The force includes short-range contact terms and the one-pion exchange potential, both treated fully nonperturbatively. The strength of the potential at leading order is fixed completely by the pole positions of the $Z_b$ states such that the mass and the most prominent contributions to the width of the isovector heavy-quark spin partner states $W_{bJ}$ with the quantum numbers $J^{++}$ ($J=0,1,2$) come out as predictions. Since the accuracy of the present experimental data does not allow one to fix the pole positions of the $Z_b$'s reliably enough, we also study the pole trajectories of their spin partner states as functions of the $Z_b$ binding energies. It is shown that, once the heavy-quark spin symmetry is broken by means of the physical $B$ and $B^*$ masses, especially the pion tensor force has a significant impact on the location of the partner states clearly demonstrating the need of a coupled-channel treatment of pion dynamics to understand the spin multiplet pattern of hadronic molecules.

## Key claim

The paper predicts that the tensor spin partner \(W_{b2}\) with quantum numbers \(2^{++}\) survives as a bound state even when the \(Z_b\) states become virtual, and that the one-pion exchange interaction, treated nonperturbatively, has a significant impact on the location of the spin partner states, producing binding energies as large as 20 MeV that are of the order of the heavy-quark spin symmetry violating parameter \(\delta = 45\) MeV.

## Method

The authors formulate and solve numerically coupled-channel Lippmann-Schwinger equations for the \(Z_b\) states and their spin partners \(W_{bJ}\) (\(J=0,1,2\)) in the bottomonium sector. The potential includes short-range contact terms and the one-pion exchange potential (and optionally one-eta exchange), all treated fully nonperturbatively, with the strength of the contact interaction fixed by the assumed pole positions of the \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) states. The binding energies of the spin partners are extracted from calculated production rates in elastic channels as functions of the \(B^*\)-\(B\) mass splitting \(\delta\), which is varied from zero to its physical value of 45 MeV.

## Key numbers

* The physical \(B^*\)-\(B\) mass splitting is \(\delta = 45\) MeV (Eq. \ref{deldef}).
* The \(B^*B\pi\) coupling constant used is \(g_b = g_c \approx 0.57\) (Eq. \ref{gc}), extracted from the \(D^*\to D\pi\) decay width.
* The pion decay constant is taken as \(f_\pi = 92.2\) MeV.
* As input, the binding energies of the \(Z_b\) states are fixed to \(E_B(B^*\bar B)[Z_b] = 5\) MeV and \(E_B(B^*\bar B^*)[Z_b'] = 1\) MeV (Eq. \ref{input1}).
* In the strict heavy-quark limit (\(\delta=0\)), the spin partners \(W_{b0}\), \(W_{b1}\), and \(W_{b2}\) all have the same binding energy of 5 MeV, while the other \(0^{++}\) partner has 1 MeV.
* The tensor spin partner \(W_{b2}\) with \(2^{++}\) is predicted to lie a few MeV below the \(B^*\bar{B}^*\) threshold and should be detectable in experiment.

## Context

This work extends the molecular interpretation of the \(Z_b(10610)\) and \(Z_b(10650)\) states, originally proposed by Bondar et al. (2011), by including the nonperturbative one-pion exchange interaction that was previously omitted in studies of the spin partners. It builds on the coupled-channel analysis of Hanhart et al. (2015) and Guo et al. (2016), which treated the \(Z_b\) states as virtual states near threshold, and on the work of Baru et al. (2016) that demonstrated the importance of OPE for the spin partners of the \(X(3872)\) in the charmonium sector. The results enable future refined analyses of experimental line shapes that include one-pion exchange interactions to directly predict the partner state properties.
